 Western India v Cantonment Board

Supreme Court of India

16 January 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhi Ranjan Das, S.K. Das, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Mohammad Hidayatullah

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 130; AIR 1959 SC 582; [1959] 2 S.C.R. 63

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax

Keywords: Cantonments Act

Summary: Indirect Tax - Cantonments Act, 1924, s. 60 - Bombay Municipal Boroughs Act, 1925, s. 73 - Constitution of India, 1950 arts. 14 and 276(2) and Sch. 7, list 2, entries 60 and 62 - Taxation Laws - (A) Cantonments Act, 1924, s. 60 - Tax on entertainments - On person who provides entertainment - Power to levy tax vest in - (B) Imposition of higher tax on certain cinema houses in light of art. 114 of Constitution - Municipalities - Held, it may not be unreasonable or improper if a higher tax is imposed on cinema shows which contains large seating accommodation in fashionable or busy localities and in more affluent circumstances than tax that may be imposed on shows in a smaller cinema house containing less accommodation and in locality where visitors are less numerous or financially in less affluent circumstances, for two cannot in those circumstances be said to be similarly situated - Appeal dismissed.
