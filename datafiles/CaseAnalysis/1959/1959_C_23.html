Commissioner of Income Tax, Bombay City Ii v Ranchhoddas Karsondas

Supreme Court of India

08 May 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 200; AIR 1959 SC 1154; [1959] 36 ITR 569; [1960] 1 S.C.R. 114

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922 - Assessment - Notice - Limitation - Books of account - Public notice u/s.22(1) of Act issued, requiring every person whose total income exceeded maximum amount not chargeable to income-tax to furnish, within such period not being less than sixty days - Assessee did not make a return of his income - Income-tax Officer while examining books of account of a partnership of which assessee was a partner, found there were six cash credits in name of assessee's wife - Assessee submitted a voluntary return of his income - Income-tax Officer then issued and served upon the assessee notices after limitation period - On appeal, both Commissioner and Tribunal rejected assessee's contentions - HC upheld the contentions of assessee - Hence instant appeal by Commissioner - Whether Income-tax Officer remained inactive without taking any steps until expiry period - Held, yes - Income-tax Officer could have avoided the result by issuing a notice u/s.23(2) of Act and not remaining inactive until the period was about to expire - Further, all laws of limitation lead to some inconvenience and hard cases - Remedy is for the legislature to amend the law suitably - Courts can administer the laws as they find them, and they are seldom required to be astute to defeat the law of limitation - Return was not a true one, and fell within the mischief of cl. (c) of sub-s.(1) of s.28 of Act, and that, therefore, period during which action could be taken was extended one of 8 years - Appeal dismissed.
