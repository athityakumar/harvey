 Chaturbhai M. Patel v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

02 December 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

J.L. Kapur, Syed Jaffer Imam, S.K. Das, A.K. Sarkar, Mohammad Hidayatullah

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 58; AIR 1960 SC 424; 1960 ALJ 262; 1978 (2) E.L.T. 297; 1960 SCJ 224; [1960] 2 S.C.R. 362

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Excise

Keywords: Government of India Act

Summary: Excise - Constitution - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944, ss. 6, 8, 9 and 10 - Govt. of India Act - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 19(6) - Central Excise Rules, 1944, rr. 140-148, 150, 171-181, 215 and 226 - Whether Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944 falls within item. 45 of List 1 r/w s. 100 of Govt. of India Act? - Held, various provisions of Act and rules made thereunder were essentially connected with levying and collection of excise duty and in its true nature and character Act remains one that falls under item 45 of List 1 - Incidental trenching upon provincial field of items 27 or 29 would not affect its constitutionality because extent of invasion of provincial field may be a circumstance to determine true pith and substance - Petition dismissed.
