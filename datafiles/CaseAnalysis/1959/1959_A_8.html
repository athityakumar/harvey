Union Of India v Amar Singh

Supreme Court of India

28 October 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 189; AIR 1960 SC 233; 1960 SCJ 543; [1960] 2 S.C.R. 75

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Bailment, Quasi-Contract

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Railways Act, 1890, ss. 72 and 80 - Contract Act, 1872, ss. 71, 151, 152, 161 and 194 - Limitation Act, 1908, art. 30 - Liability of forwarding railway in regard to consignment from Pakistan which was supposed to be delivered in India and carried by both Pakistan and Indian Railway - Determination of liability for loss of goods in such transit in absence of treaty when consigneee sued Dominion of India - Held, liability under implied contract of bailment - And court declined to give opinion on question whether there was a subsequent acknowledgment of appellant's liability within meaning of art. 19 of Limitation Act - Appeal dismissed.
