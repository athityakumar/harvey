British India General Insurance Co., Ltd v Captain Itbar Singh And Others

Supreme Court of India

11 May 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sarkar

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 114; AIR 1959 SC 1331; [1959] 29 Comp Cas 60; 1960 (1) MLJ(SC) 6; 1960 SCJ 44; [1960] 1 S.C.R. 168

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Indian Penal Code,1860- Motor Vehicles Act, 1939 - Whether s. 96(2) limits right to defend on grounds stated in that sub-section? - Held,s. 96(2), clearly specifies defences open to insurer, it is not permissible to add to those defences - This is put beyond doubt by sub-s(6) - It prevents insurer from avoiding liability in a 'manner' other than that stated in sub-s (2) - Language of sub-s(2) seems to us to be perfectly plain and admit no doubt or confusion - It is that an insurer to whom requisite notice of action has been given 'shall be entitled to be made a party thereto and to defend action on any of following grounds, namely, after which comes an enumeration of grounds - It would follow that an insurer is entitled to defend on any of grounds enumerated and no others - If it were not so, then of course no grounds need have been enumerated - When grounds of defence have been specified, they cannot be added to - To do that would be adding words to statute - Appeals dismissed.
