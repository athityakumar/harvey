 K. Satwant Singh v State of Punjab(and connected petition)

Supreme Court of India

28 October 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

Syed Jaffer Imam, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, K.C. Das Gupta, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 113; AIR 1960 SC 266; 1960 CRLJ 410; 1960 SCJ 863; [1960] 2 S.C.R. 89

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Joint Trial, Distinct Offence, Joinder Of Charges, Code of Criminal Procedure 1898, Prevention of Corruption Act, misa, Criminal Law (Amendment) Act, Criminal Law Amendment Ordinance 1943

Summary: Criminal - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 20 - Criminal Procedure Code, ss. 179, 180, 188, 197 and 233 to 239 - Govt. of India Act, 1935, s. 270 - Indian Penal Code, ss. 109 and 420 - Whether absence of a sanction u/s. 197 CrPC vitiated trial? - If act complained of is directly concerned with his official duties so that, if questioned, it could be claimed to have been done by virtue of office, then sanction would be necessary; and that would be so, irrespective of whether it was, in fact, a proper discharge of his duties, because that would really be a matter of defence on merits, which would have to be investigated at trial, and could not arise at stage of grant of sanction, which must precede institution of prosecution - Held, offence was not one committed by petitioner while acting or purporting to act in discharge of his official duty, provisions of s. 197 are inapplicable - Conviction of petitioner upheld - Petition dismissed.
