Waman Shriniwas Kini v Ratilal Bhagwandas and Company

Supreme Court of India

16 February 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

J.L. Kapur, Syed Jaffer Imam, S.K. Das

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 183; AIR 1959 SC 689; [1959] 2 S.C.R. 217

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Contract Act

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Land and Property - Rent Control - Contract & Commercial - Bombay Hotel and Lodging Houses Rates Control Act, 1947, ss. 9, 10, 13(1)(e) and 15 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 108 - Indian Contract Act, 1872, s. 23 - Whether court would aid tenant in enforcing a term of agreement which s.15 of Bombay Rents, Hotel and Lodging House Rates Control Act, 1947 declare to be illegal - Held, s. 15 prohibits sub-letting - An agreement entered into after Act has come into force, contrary to provisions of s. 15 would be unenforceable - A right founded upon an illegal transaction cannot be asserted - Main aim of s. 15 is to serve public policy - Enforcing an illegal contract will amount to enforcement of an illegality and infraction of a statutory provision which cannot be condoned by any conduct or agreement of parties - Appeal dismissed.
