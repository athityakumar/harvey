Union of India v Kishorilal Gupta and Bros

Supreme Court of India

21 May 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, A.K. Sarkar, Syed Jaffer Imam

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 185; AIR 1959 SC 1362; 1960 SCJ 1101; [1960] 1 S.C.R. 493

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Constitution; Criminal; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Contract & Commercial - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Arbitration Act, 1940 - Indian Contract Act, 1872 - (A) What is legal effect of parties of original contract entering into a subsequent contract in substitution of old one ? - Held, one of the modes by which a contract can be discharged is by same process which created it, i.e., by mutual agreement, parties to original contract may enter into a new contract in substitution of old one - (B) Does arbitration clause of original contracts survive after execution of settlement contract ? - Fact that new contract not only did not provide for survival of arbitration clause but also circumstance that it contained both substantive and procedural terms indicates that parties gave up terms of old contracts, including arbitration clause - Further held, arbitration clause perished with original contract - Appeal dismissed.
