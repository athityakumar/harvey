The Indian Molasses Co. (Private) Ltd. v The Commissioner Of Income-Tax, Westbengal.

Supreme Court of India

05 May 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 82; AIR 1959 SC 1049; [1959] 37 ITR 66; [1959] 2 S.C.R. 964

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Annuity

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s 10(2) (xv) - Indian Stamp Act, 1899, s. 98 - Purchase Annuities - Expenditure - Contingent liability - Exemption - Trustees of appellant company took out a policy and money was set out for that purpose - Respondent charged Income Tax without providing exemptions for money sort to purchase policy and on appeal to Tribunal order was confirmed and further order confirmed by HC - Hence, instant appeal - Whether amounts paid to purchase policy involved an expenditure in accounting years which could be exempted from Tax - Held, no - Payment not merely contingent but liability itself was also contingent - Expenditure which was deductible for income-tax purposes was one which was towards a liability actually existing at time - Money which may become expenditure on happening of an event was not expenditure - Hence, money with trustees or insurance company to purchase annuities of different kinds if required but to be returned if annuities were not bought and setting a part of money was not a paying out or away of these sums irretrievably - Appeal dismissed.
