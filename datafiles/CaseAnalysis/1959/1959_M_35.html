Messrs. Shalimar Works Limited v Their Workmen

Supreme Court of India

08 May 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 102; AIR 1959 SC 1217; 1959 (2) LLJ 26; [1960] 1 S.C.R. 150

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 33 and 33A - (A) Whether any remedy is available to workmen discharged in breach of s. 33 of Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Held, s. 33A provides remedy to workmen wrongfully discharged u/s. 33 - It may be availed individually or collectively - Although there is no limitation prescribed for reference of disputes to an industrial tribunal, it must be done within reasonable time - Vague and delayed reference indicated that workmen were not interested in reinstatement - Defect, in order of discharge, in such a case can be ignored - Appeal No 318 dismissed; Appeal No 317 allowed.
