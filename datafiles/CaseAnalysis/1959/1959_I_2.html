India General Navigation and Railway Company Limited v Their Workmen

Supreme Court of India

14 October 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, K. Subba Rao

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 111; AIR 1960 SC 219; 1960 (1) LLJ 13; 1960 SCJ 1031; [1960] 2 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Treating, Unjustified, Board Of Conciliation, Code of Criminal Procedure 1898

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1898, s. 144 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 188 and 143 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 22, 15, 17(2), 17 A, 10, 24(3), 22(2)(d) and 33 - Appellants carry on business of inland water transport in north east India and in Pakistan, in association with each other - Workmen were organized under two labour unions, Dhubri Transhipment Labour Union and Dhubri Local Ghat Transhipment Labour Union - Appellant's on five different occasions dismissed eight of their employees - (A) Whether no appeal lies from decision of tribunal? - Held, provisions of Act are subject to paramount law as laid down in Constitution - Art. 136, Constitution, under which this court grants special leave to appeal which cannot be read as subject to provisions of Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Whatever finality may be claimed under provisions of ID Act must necessarily be subject to result of determination of appeal by special leave - (B) Whether appellants did not exhaust their statutory remedies u/s. 17A, Industrial Disputes Act, 1947? - Further held, making award final and enforceable, must always be read subject to decision of this court, in event of special leave being granted against such determination by tribunal - State Govt. was not a party to industrial dispute, nor was it an award given by a national tribunal - Appellants did not exhaust their statutory remedies u/s. 17A, Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - (C) Whether appeal is not competent as Govt. of Assam has not been impleaded as party-respondent to appeal? - Further held, State Govt. does not play any part in proceedings, except referring dispute to Tribunal u/s. 10, Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Publication of award u/s. 17, is automatic on receipt of same by Govt. - Its coming into operation is also not subject to any action on part of State Govt. - No merit in preliminary objection - (D) Whether dismissal of eight workmen, was justified, and if not, to what relief they were entitled? - Further held, Tribunal misdirected itself in looking into sufficiency of proof led before inquiring officer, as if it was sitting in appeal on decision of employers - Dismissal order made by appellants on a proper inquiry, after giving workmen concerned sufficient opportunity of explaining their conduct, must be upheld - Appeal in respect of two workmen, allowed, and order of tribunal in respect of them, accordingly, set aside - (E) Whether strike was illegal ? - Further held, conciliation proceedings lasted between July 26 and August 8, 1955 - Strike, having commenced on August 11, was clearly illegal in view of provisions of s. 22 Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - (F) Whether strike was justified? - Further held, strike in respect of a public utility service, which is clearly, illegal, could not at same time be characterized as 'perfectly justified' - Every one participating in an illegal strike, is liable to be dealt with departmentally, but it is not permissible to characterize an illegal strike as justifiable - (G) Whether appellant's attitude in refusing to grant leave to five representatives of Union, shows that appellants were not inclined to give facilities for conciliation proceedings? - Further held, Leave should have been applied for before date fixed for commencement of conciliation proceedings - Application should have been made by workmen concerned, either direct or through Union - Application not in time on behalf of workmen - Appellants were not to blame for attitude they took in matter of procedure for application for leave to particular workmen - Conciliation proceedings stopped abruptly not because management was to blame for not granting leave but because of insistence that leave application would not be made by individual workmen but only by Union - (H) Whether lockout was illegal? - Further held, lock-out was clearly not illegal - Continuance of lock-out after strike had been called of, may be unjustified; but that does not make lock-out itself illegal - (I) What punishment should be meted out to those workmen who took part in illegal strike? - Further held, distinction has to be made between those workmen who not only joined in such a strike, but also took part in obstructing loyal workmen from carrying on their work, or took part in violent demonstrations, or acted in defiance of law and order, and those workmen who were more or less silent participators in such a strike - It is not in interest of Industry that there should be a wholesale dismissal of all workmen who merely participated in such a strike - Punishment of dismissal or termination of services, has, therefore, to be imposed on such workmen as had not only participated in illegal strike, but had fomented it, and had been guilty of violence or doing acts detrimental to maintenance of law and order in locality where work had to be carried on - Degree and kind of punishment had to be modulated according to gravity of their guilt - Appeal partly allowed.
