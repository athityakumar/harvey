 Messrs Titaghur Paper Mills Company Limited v Its Workmen

Supreme Court of India

05 May 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, S.K. Das, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 84; AIR 1959 SC 1095; 1959 (2) LLJ 9; [1959] 2 S.C.R. 1012

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Rehabilitation, Labour Appellate Tribunal, Available Surplus, Full Bench Formula

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Practice & Procedure - Service - Indirect Tax - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 2, cl. k - (A) Whether nature of production bonus depends upon production and not profit under Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 2, cl. k? - Held, payment of production bonus depends upon production and is in addition to wages - Extra payment depends not on extra profits but on extra production - This extra payment calculated on basis of extra production is in a case where payment is made after annual production is known in nature of emoluments paid at end of year - Therefore payment of production bonus is nothing more nor less than a payment of further emoluments depending upon production as an incentive to workmen to put in more than standard performance - (B) Whether tribunal has a jurisdiction to deal with dispute related to production bonus scheme by employers? - Further held, tribunal has jurisdiction to deal with dispute - In order that tribunal may have jurisdiction all that is necessary is that an industrial dispute within meaning of s. 2(k) of Act should exist or be apprehended and there should be a reference of such dispute by appropriate Govt. to tribunal u/s. 10 - Employer introduces a production bonus scheme and same is put in operation and workmen accept it, it becomes a term of employment for workmen and any dispute with respect to such a term of employment is an industrial dispute and if it is referred to a tribunal u/s 10, it has jurisdiction u/s 15 to deal with it - (C) Whether there must be progressive intensification of labour when production rose more than minimum production? - Further held, scheme was to pay a production bonus of 13 days' basic wage on a minimum production of 30,000 tons - There must have been progressive intensification of labour as production rose beyond 36,000 tons and in premises that was a circumstance which appellate tribunal was properly entitled to take into account when considering a change in rate for production over 36,000 tons - (D) Whether production bonus scheme can be calculated according to Full bench formula?- Further held, this bonus scheme is not same as profit bonus worked out under Full Bench formula and it cannot be called a profit bonus scheme even otherwise - This is nothing more nor less than a pure production bonus scheme based on tonnage, depending on certain conditions one of which is related to profits also - Under Full Bench formula, what they get is something out of profits, if there is an available surplus on ground that both capital and labour contribute to accrual of profits and it is only fair that labour should get a part of it - (E) Whether clause in a production bonus scheme showed that scheme was one for profit sharing or profit bonus? - Further held, clause did not show that scheme was one for profit sharing or profit bonus - Govt. enforced by legislation any scheme or provision for bonus or profit sharing company may decide to cancel or modify scheme in its entirety - But scheme was not mentioned - (F) Whether to receive higher multiplier greater rise in price must be proved? - Further held, if an employer is able to prove that in fact there has been a greater rise in price, he should be given a higher multiplier which is used to find out current prices of machinery to be replaced - But there has to be good proof tendered by employer for multiplier which he claims - (G) Whether surplus has to be divided under Industrial Disputes Act, s. 2, cl. k? - Further held, available surplus of profits has to be divided in fair manner between industry, shareholders and workmen u/s. 2, cl. k of Act - Appeals Nos. 450 and 451 allowed in part - Appeal No. 514 dismissed.
