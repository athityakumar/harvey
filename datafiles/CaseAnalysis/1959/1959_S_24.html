Shiva Jute Baling Limited v Hindley and Company Limited

Supreme Court of India

21 August 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 105; AIR 1959 SC 1357; 1960 (1) MLJ(SC) 1; 1960 SCJ 35; [1960] 1 S.C.R. 569

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration (Protocol and Convention) Act, 1937, ss. 7(e), 33 and 35 - Contract Act, 1872, ss. 73 and 74 - Contract - Arbitration cl. - Liquidated damages - Arbitration in London - Arbitrators awarding maximum amount named in contract - Aplication u/s. 33 of Act - Prayer, implied no contract, no arbitration agreement - Dealing with other matters - Purposes for which s. 33 of Arbitratin Act, contemplates application - Applicability of s. 35 of Act - Award if bad on face of it as being contrary to laws of India in light of provisions of Contract Act - Held, s. 33 is concerned only with term relating to arbitration - And s. 35 has no application - Award not bad - Appeal dismissed.
