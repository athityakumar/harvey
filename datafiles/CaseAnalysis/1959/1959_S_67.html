 State of Jammu and Kashmir and Others v Thakur Ganga Singh and Another

Supreme Court of India

26 November 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, K.C. Das Gupta, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 206; AIR 1960 SC 356; 1960 (1) MLJ(SC) 67; 1960 SCJ 231; [1960] 2 S.C.R. 346

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Whether a substantial question of law exists for a certificate of appeal u/art. 132(2) by High Court? - Held, not a substantial question of law to grant certificate - Law already laid down - Budhan Choudhry v State of Bihar ([1955] 1 SCR 10451955 (1) SCR 10451954 Indlaw SC 4, 1, relied on - Art.14 forbids class legislation, it does not forbid reasonable classification for purposes of legislation - To pass test of permissible classification two conditions must be fulfilled, namely, (i) that classification must be founded on an intelligible differentia which distinguishes persons or things that are grouped together from others left out of group and (ii) that differentia must have a rational relation to object sought to be achieved by statute in question explained true meaning and scope of that Article - Reiterated in Chiranjit Lal Chowdhuri v Union of India ([1950] SCR 8691950 SCR 869) and in other subsequent decisions -Rama Krishna Dalmia v Justice Tendolkar ([1959] SCR 2791959 SCR 279) and in Mohammed Haneef Qureshi v State of Bihar ([1959] SCR 6291959 SCR 629)- A substantial question of law cannot arise when law has been finally and authoritatively decided by Supreme Court - Appeal dismissed.
