 Y. Mahaboob Sheriff and Others v Mysore State Transport Authority and Others

Supreme Court of India

06 November 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, K.C. Das Gupta, Syed Jaffer Imam, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 208; AIR 1960 SC 321; 1960 SCJ 402; [1960] 2 S.C.R. 146

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Constitution; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1939 - Constitution Of India, 1950 - Prohibition Act - (A) Whether a renewal should also be for same period as provided in s. 58(1)(a)? - Held,s. 58(2) when it says that an application for renewal shall be made, disposed of as if it were an application for a permit must mean that all incidents which apply to a permit shall also apply to a renewal application - Duration of a permit u/s. 58(1)(a) being not less than three years and more than five years, same applies to a renewal - Reading ss. 58(1)(a) and 58(2) together, a renewal must also be governed by same provision which governs duration of a permit - (B) What order should be passed in circumstances? - Further held, where it is a case of discretion of an authority, this court will only quash order and ask authority to reconsider matter if discretion has not been properly exercised - But in this case, discretion is not absolute, it is circumscribed by provision of s. 58(1)(a) - It is open to this court to direct authority to carry out duty laid on it by s. 58(1)(a) r/w s. 58(2), when it has granted renewal - Petitions allowed.
