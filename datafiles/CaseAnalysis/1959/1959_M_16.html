Lilachand Tuljaram Gujar and Others v Mallappa Tukaram Borgavi and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 September 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhi Ranjan Das, Mohammad Hidayatullah, K.C. Das Gupta

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 224; AIR 1960 SC 85; 1960 (62) BomLR 134; 1960 SCJ 96; [1960] 1 S.C.R. 693

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Bombay Land Revenue Code, s .74 - Suit filed by respondent - Plaintiff,Tukaram and his father Shidappa, for redemption of mortgaged property and possession - Shidappa, who was registered occupant, gave a Rajinama u/s. 74 of Bombay Revenue Code recording his desire to; submit an unconditional surrender of above mentioned khata of R S No 307 from end of then current year, which was sanctioned by Mamlatdar on 05-05-1900 - In 1907 Shiddappa's son Tukaram (the original first plaintiff herein) and Annappa, brother of Shiddappa, filed suits against mortgagees for accounts to be taken under Deccan Agriculturists' Relief Act - Issues arise - (A) Whether u/s 74 of Bombay Land Revenue Code, as Shiddappa was registered occupant, Rajinama filed by him operated upon entire occupancy and amounted to a relinquishment of rights of both brothers Shiddappa and Annappa? - Held, it is true that principal one of such persons must enter into a written agreement to become registered occupant - This is for facilitating purpose of Code but it does not mean that other persons in whose favour occupancy is relinquished cease to have any right - It is clear that Shiddappa did not purport to file same on behalf of Annappa nor had he any right to do so, by reason only of his being registered occupant - (B) Whether Annappa's interest in property was extinguished by reason of adverse possession exercised by mortgagees since at least 1905 when claim of Annappa to get mortgaged property registered in his name failed? - Further held, it should be remembered that mortgagees came into possession of property pursuant to usufructuary mortgage - Therefore their possession had a lawful origin - A mere assertion of an adverse title on the part of appellants cannot affect subsisting equity of redemption of mortgagors or operate to shorten period of limitation prescribed for a suit for redemption - Appeal dismissed.
