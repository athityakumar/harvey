 Okara Electric Supply Company Limited, and Another v State of Punjab and Another

Supreme Court of India

13 November 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, K. Subba Rao, K.C. Das Gupta, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 116; AIR 1960 SC 284; 1960 SCJ 502; [1960] 2 S.C.R. 239

Case Digest

Subject: Electricity & Energy

Keywords: Supply Of Energy, Electricity Act, 1910, Bombay Prohibition Act 1949

Summary: Electricity & Energy - Electricity Act, 1910, s. 28 (as it stood prior to its amendment by Act 32 of 1959) - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 19(1)(g) and 31(2) - Taking over of possession of property of petitioner/company - In pursuance of Govt. Notification issued under provisions of Act - Notice and action taken thereunder challenged - (A) Whether notice ultra vires Act? - (B) Whether s. 28 under which notification was issued is ultra vires Constitution on touchstone of art.19 and 31? - (C) Whether conditions for acquisition of supplier's property were treated as a part of conditions on which supplier was allowed to carry on business of supplying electricity? - Court deduced as to that statutory provision which deals with grant of sanction to a person to engage in business of supplying energy must, having regard to special features of business necessarily deal with position which would arise on termination of sanction; and so it would not be unreasonable to assume that statutory provisions which deals with this question would think of making adequate provision empowering State Govt. to provide for compulsory acquisition of assets of supplier on payment of proper compensation - It is in light of this special feature of business of supplying energy that we must construe s. 28(1) of Act - Expression used therein 'such conditions in this behalf' - Expression 'such conditions in this behalf' in context should take in conditions dealing with position which would inevitably arise when business comes to an end - Context requires that said words should receive a wider and liberal construction - Held, a condition for acquisition of property of petitioners like in instant case would, therefore, fall within scope of s. 28(1) and challenge to validity of this condition on ground that it is ultra vires s. 28(1) must accordingly fail - Provision of s. 28 not ultra vires Constitution u/arts. 31 and 19 - Petition dismissed.
