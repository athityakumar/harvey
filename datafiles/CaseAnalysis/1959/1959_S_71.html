 Kavalappara Kottarathil Kochunni Moopil Nayar v State of Madras and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 March 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

S.K. Das, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, K. Subba Rao, Kailas Nath Wanchoo

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 249; AIR 1959 SC 725; 1959 KerLJ 464; 1959 (2) MLJ(SC) 70; [1959] 2 S.C.R. 316

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Declaratory Suit

Summary: Family & Personal - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 12 and 32 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1908, O. 7, r. 11(d) - Madras Marumakkattayam Act, 1932, s. 42 - Madras Murumakkathayam (Removal of Doubts) Act, 1955,s. 2 - (A) Whether Petition u/art. 32 for a writ of Mandamus, maintainable where alternative remedy available? - Held, mere existence of an adequate alternative legal remedy cannot per se be a good and sufficient ground for throwing out a petition u/art. 32, if existence of a fundamental right and a breach, actual or threatened, of such right is alleged and is prima facie established on petition - In instant cases as grievance was primarily against impugned Act passed by Madras Legislature, which was a state u/art. 12 of Constitution and dispute was between petitioners and state, art. 32 must apply - (B) Whether application u/art. 32 can be maintained until state has taken or threatens to take any action under impugned law which action, if permitted to be taken, will infringe petitioners' fundamental rights? - Further held, where an enactment such as impugned Act, unlike others that contemplated some further action to be taken by State after enactment had come into force, automatically took away or abridged a person's fundamental rights immediately it came into force, there was no reason why aggrieved person should not immediately be entitled to seek the remedy under art 32 of the Constitution - In view of language used in s. 2 of Madras Murumakkathayam (Removal of Doubts) Act, and its effect, there could be no doubt that petitioners could legitimately complain that their fundamental right to hold and dispose of properties have been violated by action of Legislature - Court's powers u/art. 32 are wide enough to make even a declaratory order where there is proper relief to be given to aggrieved party - Petition dismissed.
