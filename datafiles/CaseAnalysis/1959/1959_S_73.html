State of Madras v C. P. Agencies and Another

Supreme Court of India

25 August 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

K.C. Das Gupta, Mohammad Hidayatullah

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 281; AIR 1960 SC 1309

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 80 - Validity of notice of suit given u/s. 80 - Appellant contention that claim is not specificially stated in notice - Whether cause of action has been stated in notice in manner as provided u/s. 80? - On a fair reading of notice it may be said that fact of contract for payment of godown rent, quantity of goods stored, rate at which and period for which claim was made and failure of first defendant to pay the same are sufficiently stated so as to enable first defendant, which is appellant before this court, to know what plaintiff's claim was about and whether claim should be conceded or resisted - Held, claim in notice prima facie appears to be founded on contract - Appeal dismissed.
