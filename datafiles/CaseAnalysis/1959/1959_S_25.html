State of Ajmer (now Rajasthan) v Shivji Lal

Supreme Court of India

22 April 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 72; AIR 1959 SC 847; 1959 CRLJ 3327; 1960 (1) LLJ 239; [1959] 2 S.C.R. 739

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Illegal Gratification, Prevention of Corruption Act

Summary: Criminal- Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 2 and 161 - Bribe - Accused, a public servant taking money for procuring job somewhere - Charged of misconduct in discharge of duty - Held, that there is no misconduct as accused was taking money in order to get job for another person somewhere and not in his discharge of duty - Appeal allowed.
