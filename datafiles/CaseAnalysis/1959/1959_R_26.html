State of Bombay v Rusy Mistry and Another

Supreme Court of India

24 September 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 263; AIR 1960 SC 391; 1960 CRLJ 532

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Summary: Criminal - IPC - ss. 120B, 467 r/w 34 - Whether acquittal of the respondents by the HC justified - was at least one clear misdirection in the charge to the jury - Most of the statements contained entirely irrelevant to the charges made against the accused - Any jury would be prejudiced if the document was read to them - graphic picture of the alleged past misdeeds and the fraudulent acts of the accused must have unconsciously prejudiced the jury against the accused - Two questions arise in connection with this document, viz., (i) whether it is a first information report; (ii) if it be a first information report, was the learned Sessions Judge justified in reading to the jury those portions of it which were not connected with the subject-matter of the charge? - Held, Ex. Z. 26 was not a first information report, as it was not the first complaint made to the police - If it was not the first information report, it was hit by Ss. 161 and 162 of the Cr. P. C. and the learned Judge should not have relied upon it - learned Sessions Judge therefore, clearly misdirected himself in reading the so-called "relevant portions" of the said document to the jury - agree with the HC that this was a misdirection and the accused must have been prejudiced - Whether The third charge relates to the offence of forgery of Ex. D and the user thereof, and the fourth to the offence of cheating the Government of India - Held, must be held that the attempt to cheat the Government of India in the manner stated in the charge has not been established - In the result, agree with the conclusion arrived at by the learned Judges of the HC - Appeal dismissed.
