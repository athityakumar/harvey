Satyanarayan Laxminarayan Hegde and Others v Millikarjun Bhavanappa Tirumale

Supreme Court of India

25 September 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

K.C. Das Gupta, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Mohammad Hidayatullah

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 219; AIR 1960 SC 137; 1960 (62) BomLR 146; 1960 SCJ 1065; [1960] 1 S.C.R. 890

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Administrative - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, ss. 3, 111(g), 63 -Possession of property - Mulegeni tenure - Notice - Validity - Respondent/landlord made an application in Mamltadar Court praying for delivery of possession of property which appellant/tenant was on that date possessing as tenant under him, on basis of a 'Mulegeni' deed executed by respondent's predecessor-in-interest in favour of appellant's predecessor-in-interest - Mamlatdar made an order for possession in favour of respondent subject to condition that tenancy of sub-tenants would not be disturbed - On appeal, Collector allowed appeal and held that respondent was not entitled to an order for possession as tenancy had not been terminated by due notice - On further appeal, Tribunal dismissed appeal holding that the Act was applicable to lands held on Mulegeni tenure but landlord failed to terminate tenancy by notice before instituting action for ejectment - Respondent filed writ petition before HC challenging impugned order of Tribunal - HC allowed holding that an order of possession could not be made unless a notice terminating tenancy had been given before institution of proceedings and quashed order of Tribunal and restored order of Mamlatdar - Hence, instant appeal - Whether there was any perversity or illegality in judgment and order of HC - Held, requirement of a notice in writing being given by lessor to lessee of lessor's intention to determine lease became a part of Cl.(g) only on amendment of the Act by the 1929 Act - S. 3 of the 1882 Act makes no distinction whatsoever as between tenancies and leases made before 1-4-1930, and those made after but instead it says generally that provisions of Chapter V of the Act, should in so far as they were not inconsistent with provisions of this Act, apply to tenancies and leases of lands to which this Act applies - Thus, proper way of approaching was to read as a proviso to s. 111(g) as well as other sections mentioned in s. 63 of the Amending Act - Order accordingly.
