Charan Singh v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

14 May 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

S.K. Das, A.K. Sarkar, K. Subba Rao

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 292; AIR 1967 SC 520; 1967 CRLJ 525

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Murder - Circumstantial evidence - Whether circumstantial evidence in case is sufficient to establish guilt of appellant? - In cases where evidence is of a circumstantial nature, circumstances from which conclusion of guilt is to be drawn should, in first instance, be fully established, and circumstances so established should be consistent only with hypothesis of guilt of accused person, that is, circumstances should be of such a nature as to reasonably exclude every hypothesis but one proposed to be proved - Held, circumstances alleged against appellant have been fully established - No grounds for interference either with conviction or sentence of appellant - Murder was a coldblooded murder of a defenseless child, there are no mitigating circumstances - Appeal dismissed.
