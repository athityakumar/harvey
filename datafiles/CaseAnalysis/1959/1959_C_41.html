Ct. A. Ct. Nachiappa Chettiar and Others v Ct. A. Ct. Subramaniam Chettiar

Supreme Court of India

13 November 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, K. Subba Rao, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 258; AIR 1960 SC 307; 1960 (1) AnWR 101; 1960 (1) MLJ(SC) 101; [1960] 2 S.C.R. 209

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Civil Procedure; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration Act, 1940, ss. 30 and 31 - (A) Whether challenge as to validity of reference and award maintainable, being based on assumption that reference involved determination of title to immovable properties situated in Burma and/or that award has actually determined said question of title? - In none of three appeals pending before High Court it was urged by any party that immoveable properties in Burma should be brought within scope of partition suit - Respondent's claim for a share in properties outside India was negatived by Trial Court and decision of Trial Court become final not being challenged by respondent, hence, there can be no doubt that said claim was outside purview of dispute which was then pending between parties in High Court - Held, thus HC was right in coming to conclusion that reference did not include any claim with regard to immoveable properties in Burma - Appeals dismissed.
