 Narendra Kumar And Others v The Union Of India And Others

Supreme Court of India

03 December 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

K.C. Das Gupta, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Syed Jaffer Imam, J.L. Kapur, Kailas Nath Wanchoo

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 61; AIR 1960 SC 430; [1960] 2 S.C.R. 375

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Trade

Keywords: Non-Ferrous Metal Central Order 1958

Summary: Trade - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950,arts. 13,14,19,19(1)(f)&(g),19(5), 19(6), 21,32 - Essential Commodities Act, 1955,ss.3, 3(5), 3(6) - Non-ferrous Metal Control Order,1958, cls.3&4 - Legislation - Challenged - Effect - Petitioners were dealers in importing copper - UOI passed Non-ferrous Metal Control Order,1958 to regulate imported copper and its price etc and published in Gazette of India on dt.2-4-1958 - Petitioners challenged validity of this Act by instant petition as against Constitution - Whether petition was maintainable - Held, s.3 of 1955 Act confers power on UOI to provide for regulation or prohibition of production of any essential commodities which should not violate any fundamental rights granted by Constitution - UOI restricted price of copper to be sold at 3 ½ % above landed cost was in interests of general public to restrain monopoly of copper traders as they were selling at high prices - Middleman's profits increase goods price and consumer had to pay axiomatic - To conserve foreign exchange only, copper was excluded from open General List w.e.f. 1-7-1957 - Introduction of permit system was in interests of general public - Without principles, cl. 4 of the 1958 Order was not effective - As long as principles were not specified, Controller could not issue permits - To make cl. 4 of 1958 Order effective, some principles should be specified and notified in Gazette and laid before Parliament - As was not done so cl. 4 of the Order 1958 was struck down as void - Respondents/UOI was restricted from enforcing cl. 4 of 1958 Order until published in Gazette and placed before parliament as per s. 3(5)(6) of the 1955 Act - Petition partly allowed.
