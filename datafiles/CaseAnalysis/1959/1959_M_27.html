Messrs Sarupchand Hukamchand and Company v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

05 May 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 83; AIR 1959 SC 1207; [1959] 37 ITR 81; 1960 SCJ 376; [1959] 2 S.C.R. 986

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Unregistered Firm

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 23(5)(b), 26(A) , 34, 35 - A/ys. 1940 to 43 - Unregistered firm - Assessed as registered firm - Disputed - Validity - Appellant/ unregistered partnership firm was assessed by ITO as ' resident and ordinarily resident' by treating as registered firm by applying s. 23(5)(b) of the 1922 Act - For A/ys. 1940 to 42 on total income and for A/ys 1942-43 computed by taking its British Indian income - Appeal filed by appellant was allowed by appellate Assistant Commissioner and as per his Orders ITO modified assessments and carried loss to partners in A/y.1940-41 and granted refund - Appellant sought rectification of assessments by claiming that it was entitled to carry forward losses to subsequent years as unregistered firm but respondent authorities declined to interfere stating that orders u/s. 23(5)(b) of the 1922 Act became final - Appellant filed writ petition in HC and was dismissed by Single Judge and DB - Hence instant appeal - Whether appellants claim was justified - Held, assessment becomes final subject to powers exercisable u/ss. 34 and 35 of the 1922 Act when properly exercised - ITO had wrongly assessed unregistered firm as registered firm u/s. 23(5)(b) of the 1922 Act - Assessment did not become final as assessment itself was disputed and was allowed in appeal who converted profits into losses and directed ITO to modify assessment - Appellate Assistant Commissioner exercised powers u/s. 31 of the 1922 Act and annulled assessment of first year and converted profits into losses - Respondent authorities under wrong apprehension failed to give effect to Orders of Appellate Assistant Commissioner - Impugned judgment of HC was set aside - Directions issued to ITO to hear and determine assessment as per SC observations - Appeal allowed.
