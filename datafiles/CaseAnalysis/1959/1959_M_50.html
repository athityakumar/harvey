Commissioner of Income Tax, Hyderabad-Deccan v Vazir Sultan Tobacco Company Limited

Supreme Court of India

20 March 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 146; AIR 1959 SC 814; [1959] 36 ITR 175; 1960 (2) MLJ(SC) 73; 1960 SCJ 1176; [1959] 2 S.C.R. 375

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 66(1) - Capital receipt - Revenue receipt - Compensation - Exemption - Assessee a registered firm received certain sums Income-tax Officer included this sum in total income and taxed it as a revenue receipt - Appellate Assistant Commissioner found that sum was not a revenue receipt but a capital receipt being compensation for loss of the agency - Tribunal found that sum received by assessee was a revenue receipt and liable to tax and on appeal HC answered in favor of assessee but in different form - Hence, instant appeal - Whether particular payment received by assessee was income or was to be regarded as a capital receipt - Held, termination did not affect trading activities of assessees and therefore termination of contract viewed against background of assessees' business and profit-making structure appears to be no more than compensation for loss of future profit and commission - Compensation therefore was in nature of surrogatum and in this view of the matter it was revenue and not capital - Appeal dismissed.
