 Mineral Development Limited v State of Bihar and Another

Supreme Court of India

15 December 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, K.C. Das Gupta, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 125; AIR 1960 SC 468; 1960 (2) MLJ(SC) 16; 1960 SCJ 643; [1960] 2 S.C.R. 609

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Mines & Minerals - Constitution - Ajmer Tenancy and Land Records Act - Bihar Mica Act, 1947 - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Constitution of India, 1950 arts. 19(1) and 32 - Bihar Mica Act, 1947, ss. 6, 10, 12 and 14 - Ajmer Govt. Wards Regulation, 1888 - Whether cancellation of license was invalid ? - Held, u/s. 25(1)(c) of Act, repeated failure to comply with any of provisions of Act is a necessary condition for cancellation of a licence - Unless there is repeated failure within meaning of that clause state Govt. has no power to cancel licence under said clause - Respondents failed to prove that petitioner was guilty of repeated failure to comply with provisions of Act - On basis of said finding, respondents would have no power to take action u/s. 25(1)(c) of Act - Neither state Govt. had afforded reasonable opportunity to petitioner within meaning of second proviso to s. 25(1) - Petition allowed.
