Messrs. Howrah Trading Company, Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax, Calcutta

Supreme Court of India

26 March 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 139; AIR 1959 SC 775; [1959] 29 Comp Cas 282; [1959] 36 ITR 215; [1959] 2 S.C.R. 448

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Corporate - Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 16(2), 18(5)and 49 B - Companies Act, 1956, s.110, Table A, regn. 19 - Companies Act, 1913, s.34, Table A, Regn. 18 - Blank transfer of shares - Shareholders of Co. get benefit of payment of income tax paid by Co. though Co. is not agent of shareholders - Whether meaning given to expression 'shareholder' used in s. 18(5) of Act by impugned cases is correct?- Held, 'Member', 'shareholder' and 'holder of a share' are interchangeable words and shareholder holding by blank transfer is not entitled to benefit u/s. 18(5) - Words of s. 18(5) must accordingly be read in light in which word 'shareholder' has been used in subsequent sections, and read in that manner, present assessee, notwithstanding equitable right to dividend, was not entitled to be regarded as a 'shareholder' for purpose of s. 18(5) of Act - That benefit can only go to person who, both in law and in equity, is to be regarded as owner of shares and between whom and Co. exists bond of membership and ownership of a share in share capital of Co. - Appeal dismissed.
