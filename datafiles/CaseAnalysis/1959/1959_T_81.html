Tiruvenibai and Another v Smt. Lilabai

Supreme Court of India

21 January 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Mohammad Hidayatullah

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 198; AIR 1959 SC 620; [1959] 2 S.C.R. 107

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Ex-Parte, Attorney-General

Summary: Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Registration Act, 1908, ss. 2(7) and 17(1)(d) - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, ss. 8, 49 and 54 - Central Provinces Land Revenue Act, 1917, s. 2 - (A) Whether expression 'an agreement to lease' u/ss. 2(7) and 17(1)(d) of Registration Act,1908 covers only such agreements as to create a present demise? - Held, leases are instruments which transfer lessees' rights in property immediately and in praesenti - All four instruments which are treated as leases satisfy test of immediate and present demise in respect of immovable property covered by them - Expression 'an agreement to lease' covers only such agreements as create a present demise - (B) Whether a document executed by laymen without legal assistance operates as present demise and an agreement to lease - Further held, such documents must be liberally construed without recourse to technical considerations - An agreement would operate as a present demise although its terms may commence at a future date-effect of document should be considered after enquiring whether it contains unqualified and unconditional words of present demise and includes essential terms of a lease - Another relevant test is intention to deliver possession - Regard must be had to relevant and material terms and an attempt must be made to reconcile relevant terms - Document was not an agreement to lease and so did not require registration - Whether precedents have any value in construing documents - Further held, usefulness of precedents is of a limited character - Courts have to consider material and relevant terms of document with which they are concerned - Nature and character of transaction evidenced by document are determined by fair and reasonable construction of said terms - Appeal dismissed.
