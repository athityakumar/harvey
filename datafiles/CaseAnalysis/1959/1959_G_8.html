Gohar Begam v Suggi Alias Nazma Begam and Others

Supreme Court of India

27 August 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sarkar, Syed Jaffer Imam, Kailas Nath Wanchoo

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 228; AIR 1960 SC 93; 1960 CRLJ 164; 1960 SCJ 31; [1960] 1 S.C.R. 597

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Family & Personal

Summary: Criminal - Family & Personal - Mohammedan Law - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 491 - Custody of illegitimate minor child - Respondent had custody of illegitimate minor child of appellant, and appellant feared that respondent may take child to Pakistan - Both parties belong to community of singing girls - Whether respondent is entitled to retain custody of appellants minor child? - Held, no - Under Mohammedan law, appellant is entitled to custody of her illegitimate daughter, irrespective of paternity of child - Respondent has no legal right to custody of child and her refusal to hand over child to appellant has resulted in an illegal detention of child - Child to be handed over to appellant - Appeal allowed.
