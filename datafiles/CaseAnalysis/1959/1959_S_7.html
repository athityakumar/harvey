SHRI BHASKAR WAMAN JOSHI (deceased)AND OTHERS v SHRI NARAYAN RAMBILAS AGARWAL(deceased) and OTHERS

Supreme Court of India

03 November 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, K. Subba Rao

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 188; AIR 1960 SC 301; 1960 (1) MLJ(SC) 87; 1960 SCJ 327; [1960] 2 S.C.R. 117

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Mortgage By Conditional Sale, Capitalising

Summary: Land & Property - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 58 - Whether by incorporation of a condition, a transaction ostensibly of sale may be regarded as a mortgage? - Decree for redemption - Transaction shall not be deemed to be a mortgage unless condition referred to in clause is embodied in document which effects or purports to effect sale - If words are plain and unambiguous they must in light of evidence of surrounding circumstances be given their true legal effect and if there is ambiguity in language employed, intention may be ascertained from contents of deed with such extrinsic evidence as may by law be permitted to be adduced to show in what manner language of deed was related to existing facts - Oral evidence of intention is not admissible in interpreting covenants of deed but evidence to explain or even to contradict recitals as distinguished from terms of documents may ofcourse be given - Held, respondent agreed to pay in interest on price paid till reconveyance strongly indicates that transaction is mortgage, not sale - Real transaction incorporated was a mortgage and not a sale - Appeal dismissed.
