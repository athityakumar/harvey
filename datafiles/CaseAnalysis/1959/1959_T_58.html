 Tata Oil Mills Company Limited v Its Workmen and Others

Supreme Court of India

05 May 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, S.K. Das, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 85; AIR 1959 SC 1065; 1959 (2) LLJ 250; [1960] 1 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Depreciation Reserve

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Demand of bonus - Award - Maintainability - Respondents/workmen of appellant/company demanded 4 months wages as bonus for year 1955-56 for employees drawing wages less than Rs. 500 - Govt. referred dispute to Tribunal - Tribunal made calculations and based on surplus, granted bonus of 3 ½ months - Hence, instant appeal - Whether award of Tribunal was justified - Held, company had rightly paid 2 ½ months bonus to respondents based on available surplus - 3 sharers to available surplus were industry, share-holders and labour and half of surplus was already paid to respondents by appellant - Respondents demand could not be accepted as company had not claimed rehabilitation charges for year in question - Respondents were entitled to bonus equivalent to 2 ½ months wages which was already paid by appellant - Impugned Award of Tribunal was set aside - Appeal allowed.
