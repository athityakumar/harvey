 Sardar Gurmej Singh v Sardar Partap Singh Kairon

Supreme Court of India

30 September 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, Sudhi Ranjan Das, S.K. Das, A.K. Sarkar, Mohammad Hidayatullah

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 217; AIR 1960 SC 122; 1960 SCJ 386; [1960] 1 S.C.R. 909

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Summary: Election - Representation of People Act, 1951, s. 123(7) - Construction of provisions of s. 123(7) - Is Lambardar a person in service of Govt. or is it covered by any of clause of s. 123(7) of Representation of People Act, 1951 ? - Held, Respondent was guilty of corrupt practice within meaning of s. 123 of the Act - Obtaining assistance from any person serving under Govt. was a corrupt practice, and all village officers were declared to be persons serving under Govt. - It is said that there cannot be any logical basis for disqualifying a patwari and qualifying a headman in matter of elections, for, argument proceeds, a headman has greater influence on electorate than a patwari and there is relevant difference between a lambardar and village accountants - Parliament in its latest amendment has prima facie sustained distinction, may be an indication that in its view there is relevant difference between a lambardar and village accountants - A village headman cannot be brought within words 'the like' in cl. (f) of s. 123(7) -

Appeal dismissed.
