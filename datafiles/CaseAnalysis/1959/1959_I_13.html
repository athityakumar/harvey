Phulbari Tea Estate v Its Workmen

Supreme Court of India

06 May 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 35; AIR 1959 SC 1111; 1959 (2) LLJ 663; [1960] 1 S.C.R. 32

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Constitution - Practice & Procedure - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 2(k)and 15 - Constitution of India, 1950 art.311 -(A) Whether enquiry held without providing employee with a fair chance to examine witnesses flouts principles of natural justice? - Held, rules of natural justice require that a party should have opportunity of adducing all relevant evidence on which he relies - That evidence of opponent should be taken in his presence - That he should be given opportunity of cross-examining witnesses - That he should be given an opportunity of explaining all materials relied on against him - Not providing a party with all such opportunities contravenes principles of a fair trial - Tribunal's decision that dismissal of employee was not justified on ground of proper procedure not having been followed was correct and must stand - (B) Whether a defect in an enquiry by an employer u/s. 15 of Act can be made good ? - Further held, he could make good that defect by producing necessary evidence before Tribunal - Appeal dismissed.
