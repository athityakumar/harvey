Godrej and Company v Commissioner of Income Tax, Bombay City

Supreme Court of India

04 August 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhi Ranjan Das, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, Mohammad Hidayatullah

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 150; AIR 1959 SC 1352; [1959] 37 ITR 381; 1960 (1) MLJ(SC) 55; 1960 SCJ 166; [1960] 1 S.C.R. 527

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 3, 4 - Consideration for payment - Remuneration - Revenue Receipt - Liability to Tax - Asseessee/appellant received sum of Rs. 7,50,000 from managed company - Respondent charged Income Tax as real object and consideration for payment was reduction of remuneration and was a revenue receipt liable to tax - Appellant contended that sums received were for compensation and not to be taxed - Tribunal found in favor of respondent and confirmed by HC - Hence, instant appeal - Whether on facts and in circumstances of case sum of Rs. 7,50,000 was a revenue receipt liable to tax - Held, no - Sum of Rs. 7,50,000 was paid and received not to make up difference between higher remuneration and reduced remuneration but was in reality paid and received as compensation for releasing company from onerous terms as to remuneration as it was in terms expressed to be - Assessee firm received as compensation for deterioration or injury to managing agency by reason of release of its rights to get higher remuneration and therefore a capital receipt and not subjected to tax - Appeal allowed.
