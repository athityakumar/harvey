Orissa Cement, Limited v Adikanda Sahu

Supreme Court of India

30 April 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 20; 1960 (1) LLJ 518

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 33 - Appellant alleged that respondent abused secretary and labour officer in filthy language - Appellant made application against respondent u/s. 33 for permission to dismiss him - Appellate Tribunal took view that no enquiry had been made by appellant before it applied u/s. 33, and so it confirmed finding of tribunal that respondent should not be dismissed having regard to mitigating circumstances mentioned by tribunal in its award - Appeal - Tribunal agreed that respondent used filthy language in abusing labour officer - Respondent has not offered an unconditional apology as has been assumed by tribunals - Filthy words used by respondent not once but twice without any provocation were absolutely indecent and vulgar - This is a clear case where appellant's prayer for permission to dismiss respondent should have been granted - If no enquiry was held by appellant, it has produced evidence before tribunal to support its case - Appeal allowed.
