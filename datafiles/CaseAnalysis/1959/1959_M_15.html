Mallappa Girimallappa Betgeri and Others v R. Yellappagouda Patil and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 April 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sarkar, S.K. Das, K. Subba Rao

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 288; AIR 1959 SC 906

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Summary: Family & Personal - Constitution Of India, 1950 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872 - Whether courts below were right in holding that properties and those that were acquired between 19l4 and 1929 were joint properties? - Held, both courts below disbelieved case of appellant that properties had been transferred to him without any consideration - The courts below came to conclusion that appellant had not been able to discharge that onus and, therefore, properties were joint family properties - Since subsequently acquired properties had been admittedly purchased with income of properties, it followed that they were also joint properties - It is not practice of this court to set aside findings of fact concurrently arrived at by courts below, court have to proceed on basis that appellant has failed to prove that properties had been transferred to him by I entirely without any consideration - This finding was based on proper evidence - Appellant did not lead any evidence to show that no consideration was paid for sale deed except his own bare statement - Thus appellant was a manager of a joint family and had acquired properties in his own name for a consideration - Courts below held that Belhode properties provided a sufficient nucleus of joint family property out of which properties might have been acquired - Sufficiency of nucleus is again a question of fact and it is not for court to interfere with findings of courts below on that question - Presumption remains unrebutted - There is no reason why a presumption should not arise in this case that properties were joint family properties - That being so, held on appellant's own case, that properties acquired between 1914 and 1929 were also joint family properties - Appeal dismissed.
