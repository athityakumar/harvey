 State of Bihar v Rai Bahadur Hurdut Roy Moti Lall Jute Mills and Others

Supreme Court of India

26 November 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, K. Subba Rao, K.C. Das Gupta, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 54; AIR 1960 SC 378; 1960 SCJ 255; [1960] 2 S.C.R. 331; 1960 (11) STC 17

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Sales Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Bihar Sales Tax Act, 1947, ss. 14 A and 33(1)(a)(i) - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 20(1), 31(2), 132(1) and 286 - Bihar Sales Tax Rules, r. 19 - Whether High Court had erred in holding that proviso to s. 14A violates either art. 20(1) or art. 31(2) of Constitution? - Relevant provisions impose a serious penalty on registered dealer - A dealer is authorised to collect amounts by way of tax from purchasers only in accordance with provision of s. 14 A and conditions and restrictions prescribed thereunder - Held, proviso to s. 14 A cannot be invoked against first respondent and so order of forfeiture passed against him by second respondent is unjustified and illegal - In view of this conclusion, it is unnecessary to consider objections raised by first respondent against validity of proviso on ground that it contravenes arts. 20(1) and 31(2) of Constitution - Appeal dismissed.
