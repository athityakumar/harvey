Saroj Kumar Mazumdar v Commissioner of Income Tax, West Bengal

Supreme Court of India

04 May 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

J.L. Kapur, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 149; AIR 1959 SC 1252; [1959] 37 ITR 242; 1960 SCJ 464; [1959] 2 S.C.R. 846

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Constitution Of India, 1950 - Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 2(4), 4, 10 and 66 - Constitution, art. 136 - (A)Whether Supreme Court can examine whether a particular transaction is an adventure in nature of trade? - Held, yes, such question is a question of law or a mixed question of law and fact, hence open to examination by Supreme Court in appeal - (B) Whether an isolated or a single instance of a transaction can be regarded as an adventure in nature of trade? - Further held, no general principles or universal tests can be laid down in this regard - Court to consider all facts and circumstances surrounding case - Burden lies on Revenue to bring case within words of statute - Appeal allowed.
