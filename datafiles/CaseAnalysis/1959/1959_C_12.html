Bhinka and Others v Charan Singh

Supreme Court of India

24 April 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, S.K. Das, A.K. Sarkar

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 76; AIR 1959 SC 960; 1959 CRLJ 1223; 1960 SCJ 892; [1959] 2 S.C.R. 798

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898, ss. 145, 145(1) and (6) - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 4 and 79 - Uttar Pradesh Tenancy Act, 1939, ss. 10, 16, 20, 180, 183 and 242, sch. 4 - Uttar Pradesh Zamindari Abolition and Land Reforms Act, 1950, s. 16 - Uttar Pradesh Zamindari Abolition and Land Reforms Rules, rr. 4 (2) V (a), (1) and 5 (2), (3) - Issue - (A) Whether the possession by virtue of an order of a Magistrate under the provisions of s. 145 of CrPC, 1898 is one in accordance with the provisions of the law for the time being in force? - The words "possession in accordance with the law for the time being in force" in the context can only mean possession with title - Unless, the person sought to be evicted has title or right to possession, it cannot be said, that his possession is in accordance with the provisions of the law for the time being in force - (B) - What is the legal effect of the order made by the Magistrate u/s. 145 of the CrPC, 1898? - A provisional order of a Magistrate in regard to possession irrespective of the rights of the parties cannot enable a person to resist the suit u/s. 180 of the Act - The life of the said order is conterminous with the passing of a decree by a Civil Court and the moment a Civil Court makes an order of eviction, it displaces the order of the Criminal Court - (C) Whether r. 5 of the amended rules would apply to a case which was restarted under the provisions of the original rules? - There was neither a subsisting stay under the old rules nor any stay order made under the new rules, therefore ,r. 5 of the amended rules cannot be invoked in the present case - Held, appeals dismissed.
