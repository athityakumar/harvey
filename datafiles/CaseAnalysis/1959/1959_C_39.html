 Crompton Parkinson (Works) Private, Limited, Bombay v Its Workmen and Others

Supreme Court of India

05 May 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhi Ranjan Das, S.K. Das, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 27; AIR 1959 SC 1089; 1959 (2) LLJ 382; [1959] 2 S.C.R. 936

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Uttar Pradesh Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 10(2)(xv) - Service fees - Determination of - Whether there was correctness of tribunal's award as to service fees? - Bonus formula enjoins tribunals to arrive at available surplus after providing for certain prior charges mentioned therein and then to determine, after taking into consideration all material circumstances, how that available surplus should be distributed between three interests, namely, industry, shareholders and workmen - Such a process does not give effect to bonus formula but amounts to ad hoc determination which may vary and lead to chaos and industrial unrest - Income tax authorities are entitled to apply test of commercial necessity by reason of express provisions of s. 10(2)(xv) which authorises them to arrive at taxable income, profits and gains after making allowance for expenditures laid out and expended wholly and exclusively for purpose of business - Held, a conclusion drawn by tribunal without adverting to evidence before it amounts to an error of law and cannot possibly be sustained - Appeal partly allowed.
