Narayandas Bhagwandas Madhavdas v State of West Bengal

Supreme Court of India

07 May 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

Syed Jaffer Imam

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 1; AIR 1959 SC 1118; 1959 CRLJ 1368; 1983 ECR 1718; 1999 (110) E.L.T. 85; [1960] 1 S.C.R. 93

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Criminal - Customs - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Foreign Exchange Regulation Act, 1947, ss. 19(3), 23(3), 8(2) - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988 - Sea Customs Act, s. 19 - Customs Officers, found suspicious pouch with appellant and was subjected to personal search and found Indian currency notes in two secret pockets - Additional District, Magistrate issued search warrant and arrest warrant and appellant was arrested and released on bail - RBI authorized Inspector to file a complaint against appellant charging with offence under s. 8(2) of the 1947 Act r/w s. 19 of Sea Customs Act and notification No. FERA 105/55 RB - Appellant was tried and was acquitted under s. 258 of CrPC - Currency notes which had been seized by Customs Officials were directed to be released - State preferred appeal to HC, which allowed appeal and convicted appellant - Hence instant appeal - Whether Additional District Magistrate had jurisdiction to take cognizance - Held, that requisite authority had been granted by Reserve Bank, to file complaint, complaint filed on was one which complied with provisions of s. 23 of FERA and Additional District Magistrate could take cognizance of offence - As to when cognizance was taken of offence will depend upon facts and circumstances of each case and it was impossible to attempt to define what was meant by taking cognizance - Issuing of search warrant for purpose of an investigation or of a warrant of arrest for that purpose could not by themselves be regarded as acts by which cognizance was taken of offence, it was only when Magistrate applied his mind for purpose of proceeding u/s. 200 and subsequent sections of Chapter XVI of CrPC or u/s. 204 of Chapter XVII of CrPC that it could be positively stated that he had applied his mind and therefore had taken cognizance - SC opined that proceedings before Additional District Magistrate and trying Magistrate were with jurisdiction and trial of appellant was legal - Appeal dismissed.
