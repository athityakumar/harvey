Faguna Kanta Nath v State of Assam

Supreme Court of India

13 January 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

J.L. Kapur, Kailas Nath Wanchoo

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 129; AIR 1959 SC 673; 1959 CRLJ 917; 1959 (2) MLJ(SC) 18; [1959] 2 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Indirect Tax; Service; Socio-Economic

Keywords: Prevention of Corruption Act

Summary: Criminal - Service - Socio-Economic - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 107, 165 A, 161 and 302 r/w 149 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947, s. 5(2) - Whether an abettor can be convicted in a case of abetment by aid where accused has been acquitted - Held, in case of abetment by instigation or engaging with one or more other persons in any conspiracy, it is immaterial for conviction of abettor whether person instigated commits offence or not - However, where person charged with offence is acquitted, question of intentionally aiding, by any act or omission, commission of that offence does arise - Conviction of abettor cannot be allowed to stand if accused has been acquitted - Appeal allowed.
