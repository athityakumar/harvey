Rm. Nl. Ramaswami Chettiar and Others v Official Receiver, Ramanathapuram At Madurai and Others

Supreme Court of India

28 August 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sarkar, S.K. Das, K. Subba Rao

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 226; AIR 1960 SC 70; 1960 (1) MLJ(SC) 41; 1960 SCJ 128; [1960] 1 S.C.R. 616

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Building - Provincial Insolvency Act, ss. 28(2),(7) and 54 - Limitation Act, art. 182 - Application in insolvency proceedings - Maintainability - Whether application for annulling the transfer made by the insolvent was made within time prescribed by Limitation Act? - Held, on 26-01-1942, receiver made an application in insolvency proceedings for an order annulling transfer of decree by insolvent to assignee and on this application an order was made on 09-04-1943, u/s. 54 of Act annulling that transfer - Art. 182 of Limitation Act, that article provides a period of three years within which application must be made - Application was within time as it had been made within three years of date on which final order on assignee's last application was made - When an order is made u/s. 54 of Act annulling a transfer, transfer stands annulled as from date it was made - But even so, transfer stands till it is annulled and therefore, till then, transferee has all rights in property transferred - So long as transferee had such rights he was competent to exercise them and such exercise would be legal and fully in accordance with law - It is obvious that a transfer liable to be annulled under this section remains a perfectly valid transfer till it is annulled - If it had become void automatically on an order for adjudication being made, there would be no need to provide for its annulment by court - Nothing in it to lead to view that an order of adjudication founded on an act of insolvency constituted by a transfer of property amounting to a fraudulent preference - Transfer amounted to a fraudulent preference and thereupon made an order annulling it - Appeal dismissed.
