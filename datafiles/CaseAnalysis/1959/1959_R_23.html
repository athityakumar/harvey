State of Rajasthan v Rehman

Supreme Court of India

14 October 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, K.C. Das Gupta

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 210; AIR 1960 SC 210; 1960 CRLJ 286; 1978 (2) E.L.T. 294; 1960 SCJ 521; [1960] 1 S.C.R. 991

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax

Summary: Customs - Criminal Procedure Code, ss. 103 and 165 - Central Excise and Salt Act, 1944, s. 18 - Central Excise Rules, 1944, rr. 201 and 210 - Whether s. 103 or s. 165 of CrPC would be applicable when Deputy Superintendent of Central Excise made a search only to gather information about quantity of tobacco stored in house of respondent for imposing excise duty on said article and not to make any investigation with a view to prosecute respondent? - Provisions of s. 18 of Act makes it clear that searches under Act and Rules shall be carried out in accordance with provisions of CrPC relating to searches - S. 165 of CrPC lays down various steps to be followed in making a search - Recording of reasons does not confer on officer jurisdiction to make a search, though it is a necessary condition for making a search - Recording of reasons is an importing step in matter of search and to ignore it is to ignore material part of provisions governing searches - If that can be ignored, it cannot be said that search is carried out in accordance with provisions of CrPC, it would be a search made in contravention of provisions of CrPC - Held, search made by Deputy Superintendent in present case in contravention of provisions of s. 165 of CrPC was illegal - Appeal dismissed.
