Calcutta Company Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax, West Bengal

Supreme Court of India

12 May 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, Mohammad Hidayatullah, Sudhi Ranjan Das

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 169; AIR 1959 SC 1165; 2000 (162) CTR 325; [1959] 37 ITR 1; [1960] 1 S.C.R. 185

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 10(1) - A/y 1948-49 - Mercantile method - Deduction - Estimated expenditure - Appellant deals in land and property and carries on land developing business and in the course of the said business, it buys land, develops it so as to make it fit for building purposes and sells it at a profit in plot - In a/y 1948-49, the appellant sold a number of plots and received a portion of the sale price from the purchasers but as it maintained its accounts in mercantile method, it entered the whole price receivable, viz., Rs. 4,36,92,119/- in credit side and claimed a deduction of the sum of Rs. 24,809/- as estimated expenditure in computation of the profits and gains of its business during the a/y 1948-49 - Income Tax Officer, while accepting the method of accounting adopted by the appellant, disallowed said claim on the ground that the expenditure was not actually incurred in the year of account and it was by no means certain what the actual cost would be when the developments were carried out and that there was as yet no accrued liability but only a contingent liability undertaken by the appellant - Appellate Asst. Commissioner and Tribunal confirmed the order of the Income Tax Officer - HC also dismissed appeal filed against said order - Hence, the instant appeal - Whether the appellant was entitled to a deduction of Rs. 24,809/- as estimated expenditure in the computation of its profits and gains for the a/y 1948-49? - Held, the liability which was undertaken by the appellant under the deeds of sale was an accrued liability and not a contingent one - The undertaking appellant had given was unconditional and absolute in terms and the liability must be held to have accrued on the execution of the deeds of sale though it was to be discharged at a future date - The difficulty in estimating such a liability for purposes of debit under the mercantile system of accounting could be no ground for treating an accrued liability as a conditional one, since it was always open to the Income Tax Authorities to arrive a proper estimate thereof - Hence, Rs. 24,809/- which was to be incurred by appellant was accrued liability which according to mercantile system of accounting appellant entitled to debit into bank account - Estimated expenditure to be allowed as deduction - Impugned order of the HC set aside - Appeal allowed.
