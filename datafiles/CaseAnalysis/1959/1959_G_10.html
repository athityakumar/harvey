 Gopi Chand v Delhi Administration

Supreme Court of India

20 January 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, S.K. Das, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Mohammad Hidayatullah, Sudhi Ranjan Das

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 199; AIR 1959 SC 609; 1959 CRLJ 782; [1959] 2 S.C.R. 87

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14 and 134(1)(c) - General Clauses Act, 1897, s. 6 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 408, 409 and 120(B) - East Punjab Public Safety Act, 1949, ss. 36, 11 and 20 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 263, 264, 254 and 265 - (A) Whether in treating dangerously disturbed areas as a class by themselves and in providing for one uniform procedure for trial of all specified offences in such areas impugned provision has violated art. 14? - Held, classification must be based on an intelligible differentia which distinguishes persons or things grouped together in one class from others left out of it, and differentia must have a reasonable or rational nexus with object sought to be achieved by said impugned provision - There is no doubt that procedure prescribed for trial of summons cases is simpler, shorter and speedier, and so, when dangerously disturbed areas were facing problem of unusual civil commotion and strife, legislature was justified in enacting first part of s. 36 so that cases against persons charged with commission of specified offences could be speedily tried and disposed of - (B) Whether third and fourth notifications are invalid? - Held, power to cancel or modify must be exercised in reference to areas of Province which it is competent for Provincial Govt. to specify as dangerously disturbed - Power to modify cannot obviously include power to treat same area as dangerously disturbed for persons accused of crimes committed in past and not disturbed for others accused of same or similar offences committed later - Appeal allowed.
