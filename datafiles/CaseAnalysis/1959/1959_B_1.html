BHARWAD MEPA DANA and ANOTHER v STATE of BOMBAY

Supreme Court of India

10 November 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

S.K. Das, A.K. Sarkar, Mohammad Hidayatullah

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 187; AIR 1960 SC 289; 1960 CRLJ 424; 1960 SCJ 478; [1960] 2 S.C.R. 172

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure 1898, Children Act

Summary: Criminal - Children Act - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898, s. 374 - Juvenile Justice Act - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 141, 147, 148, 149, 34 and 120 A - Murder - Unlawful assembly - Total number of persons constituting unlawful assembly was ten to thirteen and all persons had common object and common intention of killing and that killing was done in prosecution of common object of unlawful assembly and in furtherance of common intention of all, and appellants took a major part in assault on two of brothers - Though identity of all persons except those four who were convicted was not established - Whether can it be said that High Court wrongly applied s.149, because number of convicted persons was only four? - Held, it is a clear finding with certainty that number of persons who constituted unlawful assembly was more than five, though identity of four only has been established,and killing was done in prosecution of common object of entire unlawful assembly - Therefore, s. 149 is applicable in present case - Further held, that principle which s. 34 embodies is participation in some action with common intention of committing a crime, once such participation is established,s. 34 is at once attracted - No difficulty in applying s. 34 in present case - Appeal dismissed.
