R. C. Mitter and Sons v Commissioner of Income Tax, Calcutta

Supreme Court of India

15 April 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 153; AIR 1959 SC 868; [1959] 36 ITR 194; 1959 (2) MLJ(SC) 192; [1959] 2 S.C.R. 641

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 23, 26, 26A and 28 - Income tax Rules, rr. 2 to 6B - Whether the assessee firm which is alleged to have come into existence by a verbal agreement, where the instrument of partnership was drawn up only after the expiry of the relevant previous year, is entitled to be registered u/s. 26A for the purpose of assessment relevant to which the verbal agreement took place? - The firm to be registered should have been in existence during the relevant accounting year - The assessee partnership business was not carried on in accordance with the terms of an instrument of partnership which was operative during the accounting year - Held, High Court answered rightly by refusing the registration - Appeal dismissed.
