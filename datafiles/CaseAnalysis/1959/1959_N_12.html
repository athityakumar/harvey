Dolgobinda Paricha v Nimai Charan Misra, and Others

Supreme Court of India

27 April 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

S.K. Das, A.K. Sarkar, K. Subba Rao

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 237; AIR 1959 SC 914; 1967 CLT 740; 1960 SCJ 879; [1959] 2 S.C.R. 814

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Family & Personal Law - Land & Property - Existence of relationship - Facts in issue and Relevant Fact - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 5 and 50 - Suit filed for declaration of title and recovery of possession - Whether testimony u/s 50 of Indian Evidence Act can be relied upon to determine existence of relationship between parties? - S. 50 deals with relevancy of a particular fact - When court has to form an opinion as to relationship of one person to another, opinion of person expressed by conduct by a person who has special knowledge as to existence of such relationship is a relevant fact - Opinion is something more than gossip or hearsay - Opinion means judgment or belief, resulting from what one thinks on a particular question which may be expressed by his conduct - Such conduct or outward behavior as evidence of opinion is relevant and may, be proved - Held, yes - Relationship of plaintiff-respondent existed - No merits in appeal - Appeal dismissed.
