 State of Bihar and Others v Sm. Charusila Dasi

Supreme Court of India

15 April 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

S.K. Das, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Mohammad Hidayatullah

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 241; AIR 1959 SC 1002; [1959] 2 S.C.R. 601

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Prohibition Act

Summary: Trust & Association - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 19 - (A) Whether provision of Bihar Hindu Religious Trust Act to file return applied to private trust created for worship of family idol? - (B) Whether Act was ultra vires Constitution and interfered with right as a citizen guaranteed u/art. 19 of Constitution? - Held, cardinal point to be decided is whether it was intention of founder that specified individuals are to have right of worship at shrine, or general public or any specified portion thereof - When property is dedicated for worship of a family idol, it is a private and not a public endowment, as persons who are entitled to worship at shrine of deity can only be members of family, and that is an ascertained group of individuals - It cannot be disputed that if religious endowment is itself situated in Bihar and trustees function there, connection between religious institution and property appertaining thereto is real and not illusory, indeed, religious institution and property appertaining thereto form one integrated whole and one cannot be dissociated from other - If, therefore, any liability is imposed on trustees, such liability must affect trust property - On the basis of the submission of the State, it appeared that there was no intention on the part of the Govt of Bombay or the Charity Commissioner to apply the provisions of the Bombay Act to any math or religious institution situated outside the State territory - It is further submitted that the Bombay Act could be made applicable, if at all, to any place of religious instruction or worship which is appurtenant to the math and is actually within the State territory - In view of the submissions no decision was given on the point urged - The case cannot, therefore be taken as a final decision of the question in issue before Supreme Court - For the reasons, the Act applies to the Charusila Trust which is in Bihar and its provisions cannot be struck down on the ground of extra-territoriality - Appeal allowed.
