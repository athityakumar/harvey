 Hamdard Dawakhana (Wakf) Lal Kuan, Delhi and Another v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

18 December 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

J.L. Kapur, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, K.C. Das Gupta, Syed Jaffer Imam

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 203; AIR 1960 SC 554; 1960 CRLJ 735; 1960 (2) MLJ(SC) 1; 1960 SCJ 611; [1960] 2 S.C.R. 671

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Excise Regulation

Summary: Media & Entertainment - Health & Drug - Constitution - Drug and Magic Remedies (Objectionable Advertisement) Act, 1954, ss. 2, 3 and 8 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 19, 21 and 31 - Prohibition Act - Excise Regulation - Whether Drug and Magic Remedies (Objectionable Advertisement) Act, 1954 contravened the provisions of arts. 14, 21 and 31? - Court held, absence of safeguards which the legislature has thought it necessary and expedient in other statutes, e.g. Indian Drugs Act, is an unreasonable restriction on the fundamental rights of the petitioners and therefore portion of cl. (d) of ss. 3 and 8 unconstitutional - Petition partly allowed.
