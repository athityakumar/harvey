 Kangsari Haldar and Another v State of West Bengal

Supreme Court of India

18 December 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, A.K. Sarkar, K. Subba Rao, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 128; AIR 1960 SC 457; 1960 CRLJ 654; 1960 SCJ 629; [1960] 2 S.C.R. 646

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Tribunals of Criminal Jurisdiction Act, 1952 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1898 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120 B, 302 and 436 - West Bengal Act - Constitution of India, 1950 - Appellants were alleged that with some other they commited offence u/s. 302, 436 and 120 B - High Court dismissed their petition and special leave of Court was taken - Whether impugned provisions are based on ground that they violate fundamental right guaranteed by art. 14 of Constitution, 1950? - Held, it is quite plain that object of Act is not simply to provide a speedy trial - Indeed, all offences require speedy trial - Object of Act is expressly to provide speedy trial of certain offences committed in a specified area and during a specified period because it is 'expedient in interests of security of State, maintenance of public peace and tranquility', to do so classification by areas is based on disturbance in an area and necessity of restoring peace there - Such being object, a distinction made between cases, where trials had been concluded and cases where trials had not been concluded, is not a distinction which has any rational relation to that object - In my view, s. 2(b) of Act in so far as, it permits an area which was a disturbed area in past to be declared a disturbed area for purposes of Act, offends art. 14 of Constitution, 1950 is therefore unconstitutional, void - In result, I would allow appeal - Order passed by HC is confirmed - Appeal dismissed.
