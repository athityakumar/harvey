Bengal Chemical and Pharmaceuticalworks Limited v Their Workmen

Supreme Court of India

28 January 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, A.K. Sarkar

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 132; AIR 1959 SC 633; 1959 (1) LLJ 413; [1959] 2 S.C.R. 136

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Cost Of Living, Industrial Disputes (Appellate Tribunal) Act, 1950, Industrial Disputes (Amendment And Miscellaneous Provisions) Act, 1956, Industrial Disputes (Amendment) Act, 1957, Industrial Disputes (Amendment) Act

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Practice & Procedure - Bombay Industrial Disputes Act, 1938 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Industrial Disputes (Amendment and Miscellaneous Provisions) Act, 1956 - Industrial Disputes (Amendment) Act, 1957, s. 2(r) - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 10 and 33 B (as amended by Act of 1956) - Whether art. 136 confers upon Supreme Court jurisdiction vis-a -vis awards of tribunals - Held, art. 136 confers a discretionary power on Supreme Court to grant special leave to appeal from order of any tribunal in territory of India - No party can claim an appeal as a matter of right - Undue interference by court will affect smooth and expeditious functioning of tribunals - Supreme Court should exercise its discretionary jurisdiction only in cases where awards are made in violation of principles of natural justice, causing substantial and grave injustice to parties or if case raises an important principle of industrial Law requiring elucidation and final decision by Supreme Court or discloses such other exceptional or special circumstances which merit consideration of Supreme Court - Appeal dismissed.
