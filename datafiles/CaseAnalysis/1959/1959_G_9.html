Narayan Bhagwantrao Gosavi Balajiwale v Gopal Vinayak Gosavi and Others

Supreme Court of India

22 September 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, Sudhi Ranjan Das, S.K. Das

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 221; AIR 1960 SC 100; 1960 SCJ 263; [1960] 1 S.C.R. 773; 1990 (76) STC 183

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Trusts & Associations - Charitable and Religious Trusts Act 1920, ss. 3 and 5 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, ss. 109 and 110 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 133 - Whether the suit was bad due to absence of deity? - A trustee must not mix private property with trust property, because if he does so, he undertakes a heavy burden of proving that any particular property is his, as distinct from the trust - Held, no useful purpose will be served in examining in detail the evidence relating to these properties in the absence of the deity - The appellant neglected to bring necessary evidence - This matter suffers from defects, namely, the failure to join the deity as a party and no distinction between one kind of property and another - No further directions - Appeal dismissed.
