 Rehman Shagoo and Others v State of Jammu and Kashmir

Supreme Court of India

10 September 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Sudhi Ranjan Das, S.K. Das, A.K. Sarkar, Mohammad Hidayatullah

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 109; AIR 1960 SC 1; 1960 CRLJ 126; 1960 SCJ 172; [1960] 1 S.C.R. 680

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Miscellaneous; Petroleum & Gas

Keywords: Competence Of The Legislature, Emergency Provisions

Summary: Constitution - Criminal - Miscellaneous - Petroleum & Gas - Jammu and Kashmir Constitution Act, 1996 - Jammu and Kashmir Constitution (Amendment) Act (JK XVII of s.2005) - Jammu and Kashmir General Clauses Act, 1897, ss. 6 and 4 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898, s. 491 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 120-B - Explosive Substances Act, 1908, ss. 3, 4 and 5 - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 14, 35, 132 and 352 - Issue - (A) Whether ordinance u/s. 3 of Act was unconstitutional and void by reason of violation of art. 14 of Constitution? - There can be no doubt that classification is reasonable and is founded on an intelligible differentia which distinguished persons brought under ordinance from others - No doubt that differentia had a rational relation to object sought to be achieved by ordinance - Ordinance cannot be said to be discriminatory and, therefore, violative of art. 14 of Constitution - (B) Whether there was no legislative competence in His Highness to issue ordinance u/s. 5 of Constitution Act, as His Highness had executed instrument of accession on 26-10-1947 surrendering his powers Govt. of India? - There is no force in contention that ordinance was beyond legislative competence of His Highness because certain matters were ceded in instrument of accession D/-26-10-1947, to Govt. of India - Fact that it was within competence of legislature when it was passed and being permanent would continue till amended or repealed under amended Constitution Act - Held, Appeal dismissed.
