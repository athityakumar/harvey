Gullapalli Nageswara Rao v State of Andhra Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

21 August 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 104; AIR 1959 SC 1376; 1960 (1) MLJ(SC) 13; 1960 SCJ 53; [1960] 1 S.C.R. 580

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Keywords: Transport Department, Nationalisation

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Constitution of India, 1950,arts. 166, 226, 32 - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 - Road Transport Corporation Act, 1950 - Motor transport business - Permits for carriages - Principles of natural justice - Violation - Appellants were carrying on motor transport business for several years - General Manager of State Transport Undertaking of Andhra Pradesh Road Transport, published scheme for nationalization of motor transport in State from date to be notified by State Govt. - Objections to proposed scheme were invited by State Govt. and appellant filed their objections - Approved scheme was published in Andhra Pradesh Gazette scheme was duly published by Govt. in official Gazette - Corporation applied to Road Transport Authority for issue of permits for plying stage carriages and for eliminating permits granted to private bus operators - Appellants aggrieved by orders of Govt. filed petitions was dismissed - Hence instant appeals - Whether State Govt. acted in violation of principles - Held, an authority for proposition that unless legislature clearly and expressly ordained to contrary, principles of natural justice cannot be violated - Relevant provisions of Act do not sanction any dereliction of the principles of natural justice - Undertaking prepares scheme providing for road transport service in relation to an area to be run or operated - Act does not authorize State Govt. to act in derogation of principles of natural justice - Appellants failed to interfere on technical point - HC did not rightly exercise its discretion - Appeal disposed of.
