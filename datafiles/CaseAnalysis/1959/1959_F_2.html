 Fedco Private Limited v S. N. Bilgrami

Supreme Court of India

09 December 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

K.C. Das Gupta, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, K. Subba Rao, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 2; AIR 1960 SC 415; 1960 (62) BomLR 293; 1999 (110) E.L.T. 92; 1960 (1) MLJ(SC) 71; 1960 SCJ 235; [1960] 2 S.C.R. 408

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Constitution - Trade - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 19 and 31 - Cancellation of five import licenses - Alleged that import licences fraudulently obtained - Whether licensee (petitioner) was given a reasonable opportunity of being heard before order of cancellation was made? - Besides stating that licences had been obtained fraudulently, respondent informed petitioners' representatives that though issue of licences had been purported to be authorised by him - Such issue not actually authorised by him - Held, petitioners' representatives more concerned to show that Co. was not a party to fraud than to show that there was no fraud practised at all - Omission to give further particulars or inspection of papers did not deprive petitioners of a fair chance of convincing respondent - Therefore, opportunity given to petitioners amounted to a reasonable opportunity of being heard - Petition dismissed.
