Associated Hotels of India Limited v R. N. Kapoor

Supreme Court of India

19 May 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

S.K. Das, A.K. Sarkar, K. Subba Rao

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 90; AIR 1959 SC 1262; 1960 SCJ 453; [1960] 1 S.C.R. 368

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Delhi and Ajmer-Merwara Rent Control Act, 1947, Bombay Rent, Hotel and lodging House Rates Control Act, 1947

Summary: Rent Control - Land & Property - Delhi and Ajmer Merwara Rent Control Act, 1947, s. 2(b) - Indian Easements Act, 1882, s. 52 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 105 - (A) What are principles to be applied for distinguishing between lease and license? - Held, Supreme Court has adopted 4 principles - Substance of document must be preferred to form - Real test is whether parties intended to create a lease or a licence - If document creates an interest in property, it is a lease - But if it only permits another to make use of property, of which legal possession continues with owner, it is a licence - If under document a party gets exclusive possession of property, prima facie, he is considered to be a tenant; but circumstances may be established which negative intention to create a lease - (B) What conditions are to be fulfilled for determining whether a room should be regarded as a 'room in a hotel' within meaning of s. 2(b) of Delhi and Ajmer Merwara Rent Control Act, 1947? - Further held, Supreme Court laid down 2 conditions - Room must be part of hotel in physical sense - Its user must be connected with general purpose of hotel of which it is a part - Appeal allowed.
