Ranipur Colliery v Bhuban Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

21 April 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 12; AIR 1959 SC 833; 1959 (2) LLJ 231; 1959 (2) MLJ(SC) 232; [1959] 2 S.C.R. 719

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 33 - (A) Whether workmen being an appellant on getting no relief in Appellate court can join as respondent in Supreme Court? - Held, workmen to whom no relief was granted by Appellate Tribunal cannot be joined as respondents - (B) Whether suspension without pay pending permission of Industrial Tribunal u/s. 33 of Act amounted to a breach of standing order? - Further held, (i) proceedings u/s. 33 is not an enquiry by industrial tribunal into rights or wrongs of dismissal - (ii) enquiry which is contemplated by standing order is an enquiry into conduct of employee, that enquiry can only be by employer - Therefore 'pending enquiry' only refers to enquiry by employer into conduct of employee - When an enquiry is mentioned in standing order it can in context only refer to enquiry by employer and not to a proceeding u/s. 33 before tribunal - (iii) Where suspension without pay took place after application u/s. 33 had been made and was pending permission under section and industrial tribunal accorded permission, there was no question of employee being paid during period of suspension without pay - Appeal allowed.
