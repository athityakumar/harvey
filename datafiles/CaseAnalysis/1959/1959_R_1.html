R. Abdulla Rowther v State Transport Appellate Tribunal, Madras and Others

Supreme Court of India

18 February 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, A.K. Sarkar, K. Subba Rao

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 286; AIR 1959 SC 896

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1939, s. 48A(1) - G.O. No. 1298 by Govt. of Madras D/- 28-04-1956 - Permit of stage carriages - System of allotment of marks under several heads vide G.O. - Construction of relevant G.O. - Construction done by State Transport Authority (STA) which lead to decision in favour of appellants - But such construction was rejected by State Transport Appellate Authority (STAA) and upheld by High Court - Whether directions contained in G.O. executive or administrative orders, or, are they statutory rules having force of law? - Distinction between statutory rules having force of law and administrative or executive directions - Executive direction though valid, and in a sense binding on subordinate authorities is not a statutory rule and has not force of law and so its misconstruction cannot be said to be an error of law, to sustain exercise of writ jurisdiction and issuance of writ - G.O. with its paragraphs perused - Held, directions in question are merely executive directions - Thus no question of issuance of writ of 'certiorari' arises - Appeals dismissed.
