Guest, Keen, Williams Private, Limited v Sterling P. J. and Others

Supreme Court of India

15 May 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 29; AIR 1959 SC 1279; 1959 (2) LLJ 405; [1960] 1 S.C.R. 348

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes (Appellate Tribunal) Act, 1950, s. 7(1)(a) - Industrial Employment (Standing Order ) Act, 1946, ss. 7 and 10 - (A) Whether a reference can be made against a standing order introducing a system of forced retirement? - Held, yes, it is open to employees to raise a dispute and ask standing orders to be amended - (B)Whether question of justification of system of forced retirement of workmen can be challenged in appeal? - Further held, yes, such question involves a substantial question of law as it involves question of industrial policy and principle affecting a large number of employees - Appeal partly allowed.
