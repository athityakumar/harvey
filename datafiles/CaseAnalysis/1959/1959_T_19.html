Graham Trading Company (India) Limited v Its Workmen

Supreme Court of India

07 May 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 99; AIR 1959 SC 1151; 1959 (2) LLJ 393; 1961 (1) SCJ 246; [1960] 1 S.C.R. 107

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Implied Term

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Whether tribunals order of granting one month's basic wages as customary puja bonus to workmen is justified and whether puja bonus is an implied term of employment or on custom? - Right from 1948 to 1952, Co. whenever it paid this bonus, made it clear that it was an ex gratia payment and would not constitute any precedent for future years - There has been unbroken payment and period has been sufficiently long to justify an inference of customary and traditional bonus - Payments were made in at least two years of loss - Puja bonus has always remained uniform at one month's basic wage - Held, all conditions, therefore, of a customary and traditional bonus are satisfied in this case and there is no reason to interfere with order of appellate tribunal - Appeal dismissed.
