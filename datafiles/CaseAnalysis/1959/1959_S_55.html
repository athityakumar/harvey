Shambhu Nath Singh and Others v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

30 October 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, K. Subba Rao

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 274; AIR 1960 SC 725; 1960 BLJR 236; 1960 CRLJ 1144

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Murder & unlawful assembly - Constitution - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 147, 148, 149, 302 and 326 - Arms Act, s. 19 - Appellants convicted for murder by Trial Court and confirmed by High Court - Whether conviction of accused no. 2 to 8 and 14 u/s. 326 IPC, 1860 proper? - By s. 149 vicarious liability of members for offence committed in prosecution of common object or for offences which were known to be likely to be committed by members of unlawful assembly is declared - Offence of murder being in its nature an aggravated form of offence of grievous hurt - Held, common object to cause grievous hurt of unlawful assembly proved, while one person caused death - Conviction proper - Appeal dismissed.
