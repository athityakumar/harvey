Mst. Rukhmabai v Lala Laxminarayan and Others

Supreme Court of India

17 November 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 207; AIR 1960 SC 335; 1960 SCJ 433; [1960] 2 S.C.R. 253

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: (A) Land & Property - Family & Personal - Practice & Procedure - Limitation Act, 1963, art. 120 - Suit for partition - Relinquishment deed - Fictious - Validity - Trustee executed a trust deed in favour of beneficiaries of suit property i. e. appellant and respondent - Appellant filed a suit against respondent for partition of said property - Trial Court decreed suit against respondent for partition and mesne profits - On appeal, District Court held, suit building was put up with trust amount and additional amounts given by Trustee - Aggrieved, respondent preferred an appeal against that decree to HC - HC held that two relinquishment deeds were sham documents brought into 'existence to shield liquid assets of family and suit was within time u/art. 120 of the Act - Hence, instant appeal - Whether plaint-schedule house was property of joint family or whether it was built out of self-acquisitions of Trustee in respect whereof he executed trust deed - Held, there was no separation of members of family all members of family continued to be joint and family was doing business in different places - Various attempts were made to salvage properties of family and to keep both movable and immovable properties not mortgaged from reach of creditors - However, relinquishment deeds, innumerable mortgages, sale deeds and trust deed were all executed as parts of same scheme - Therefore, suit property was joint family property and respondent was entitled to declaration that trust deed was a colourable and fictitious document and could not affect respondent's right to ownership of property in suit - Appeal dismissed.

(B) Land & Property - Family & Personal - Practice & Procedure - Specific Relief Act, 1963, s. 42 - Possession of property - Validity - Whether suit should have been dismissed in limine as plaintiff asked for a bare declaration though he was in a position to ask for further relief u/s. 42 of the Act - Held, it was well-settled rule of practice not to dismiss suits automatically but to allow plaintiff to make necessary amendment if he seeks to do so - However, plaintiff, being in possession of joint family property, was not bound to ask for partition if he did not have intention to separate himself from other members of family - Thus, plaintiff could have asked for necessary amendment to comply with provisions of s. 42 of the Act - Appeal dismissed.

(C) Land & Property - Family & Personal - Practice & Procedure - Limitation Act, 1963, art. 120 - Whether suit was barred by limitation - Suit for partition - Barred by limitation - Maintainability - Held, right to sue u/art. 120 of the Act accrues when defendant had clearly and unequivocally threatened to infringe right asserted by plaintiff in suit - Every threat by a party to such a right, however ineffective and innocuous it might be, could not be considered to be a clear and unequivocal threat so as to compel him to file a suit - Thus, plaintiff's right to property was not effectively threatened by appellant till Commissioner came to divide property - It was only then there was an effectual threat to his right to suit property and suit was filed within 6 years thereafter - Hence, suit was filed within time - Appeal dismissed.
