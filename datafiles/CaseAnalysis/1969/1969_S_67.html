Rani Bai v Shri Yadunandan Ram & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

19 February 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Grover, J.C. Shah, V. Ramaswami

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 243; (1969) 1 SCC 604; AIR 1969 SC 1118; 1969 ALJ 988; [1969] 3 S.C.R. 789

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Family & Personal; Practice & Procedure; Women & Children

Keywords: Marriage & Divorce, Minority, Adoption & Guardianship

Summary: Family & Personal - Women & Children - Civil Procedure - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 23, rr. 1 and 3 - Hindu Adoptions And Maintenance Act, 1956, s. 19 - Hindu Succession Act, 1956, s. 14 - Application of withdrawal of suit by a co. - Plaintiff in respect of a suit filed for declaration of right and possession against trespasser - Whether will affect maintainability of suit by remaining plaintiff? - Held, suit cannot be dismissed on basis of application - A trespasser could not deprive her of possession of properties - Plaintiff - Widow - Who had a possessory title was entitled to restoration of possession as against trespasser as if he had no right, title or interest and was a mere trespasser and she was further entitled to remain in possession by virtue of her claim or right to maintenance until trespasser who claimed estate of made some proper arrangement for her maintenance - Appeal allowed.
