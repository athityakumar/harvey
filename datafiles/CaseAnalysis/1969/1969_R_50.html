Giani Ram and Others v Ramji Lal and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 March 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 194; (1969) 1 SCC 813; AIR 1969 SC 1144; 1969 (71) PLR 996; [1969] 3 S.C.R. 944

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Family & Personal; Practice & Procedure; Women & Children

Keywords: Female Heir, Declaratory Suit, Succession & Inheritance, Ancestral Immovable Property

Summary: Land & Property - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Hindu Succession Act 1955 - Hindu Succession Act, 1956 - Punjab Custom (Power to Contest) Act 1920 - Suit for possession of land - Decreed - Justifiability - 'J' a Hindu Jat governed by customary law of Punjab sold land to 'S' without legal necessity, which was ancestral in his hands - 'J' died leaving his surviving three sons, two daughters and wife/plaintiffs/appellants - Plaintiffs filed petition before Trial Court for decree for possession of lands and declaration that sale of ancestral lands of 'J' in favor of 'S' was null and void and was ineffective against his reversionary rights - Trial Court decreed suit for half share in property claimed by plaintiffs - Aggrieved plaintiff appealed before Appellate Court - Appellate Court decreed claim in its entirety, but only in favor of three sons - Aggrieved respondents filed appeal against said decree before HC - HC set aside decree passed by Appellate Court and restored decree of Trial Court - Hence instant appeal - Whether order passed by HC was just and proper or does it call for interference - Held, expression 'which ought to have been passed' means 'which ought in law to have been passed' - If Appellate Court was of view that any decree which ought in law to have been passed, but was in fact not passed by subordinate court, it might pass or make such further or other decree or order as justice of case might require if claim of respondents to retain any part of property after death of 'J' was negatived, it would, be perpetrating gave injustice to deny to widow and two daughters, their share in property to which they were in law entitled - Case was one in which power u/o. 41 r. 33, CPC ought to have been exercised and claim not only of three sons but also of widow and two, daughters ought to have been decreed - Decree passed by HC was modified - There would be decree for possession of lands in suit in favor of three sons, widow and two daughters of 'J' - Interest three sons was one-half in Lands in suit and interest of widow and two daughters was other half in lands - Plaintiffs would be entitled to mesne profits from date of the suit Appeal allowed.
