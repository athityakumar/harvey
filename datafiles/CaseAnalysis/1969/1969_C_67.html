 Commissioner of Income Tax, Central Calcutta v Gold Mohore Investment Company Limited

Supreme Court of India

03 April 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, G.K. Mitter, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, A.N. Grover, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 289; (1969) 2 SCC 460; AIR 1969 SC 1183; [1969] 74 ITR 62; [1970] 1 S.C.R. 199

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922 - Whether assessee can add face value of bonus shares issued to it free of cost on basis of its old holdings, and, secondly, whether closing stock of bonus shares can be valued at face value of these shares? - Decided in accordance with decision in Commissioner of Income-tax v Gold Mohore Investment Co. Ltd (Civil Appeals Nos 1236-1237 of 1967) - ITAT method of spreading price of old shares over old and new shares and calculating profit or loss accordingly was held correct - Appeal allowed.
