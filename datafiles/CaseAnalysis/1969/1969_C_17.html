 Chandra Prakash Agarwal v Chaturbhuj Das Parikh & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

18 December 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Shelat, S.M. Sikri, Vishishtha Bhargava, G.K. Mitter, C.A. Vaidyialingam

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 373; (1970) 1 SCC 182; AIR 1970 SC 1061; [1970] 3 S.C.R. 354; 1970 SLR 383

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure; Women & Children

Keywords: Right To Practise

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Constitution - Advocates Act, 1961, s. 16 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 217(2) and 233(2) - Legal Practitioners Act, 1879 - Appointment of Judge - Appellant filed writ in High Court for issue of quo warranto against appointment of respondent as judge - Appellant contended that respondent did not practice in HC within meaning of art. 217(2)(b) but in other courts - Held, language of art. 217 meant an advocate enrolled as advocate of a HC not necessarily practicing in HC - HC order upheld - Appeal dismissed.
