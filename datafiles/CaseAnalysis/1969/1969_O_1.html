State of Uttar Pradesh v Om Prakash Gupta

Supreme Court of India

28 October 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 448; (1969) 3 SCC 775; AIR 1970 SC 679; 1970 LabIC 568; 1969 SLR 890; 1970 UJ 181

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Government of India Act, 1935, ss. 241 and 59 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 166(1), 166(2) and 311(2) - Order of dismissal of respondent - High Court set aside order of dismissal on sole ground that a second show-cause notice was required by s. 240 of Government of India Act, 1935 had not been given - Hence, present appeal - (A) Reasonable opportunity contemplated by s. 240 - Held, reasonable opportunity contemplated by s. 240 of Government of India Act, 1935 as u/art. 311(2) of Constitution primarily consist of : - (i) opportunity to concerned officer to deny his guilt and establish his innocence which means he must be told what charges against him are and allegations on which such charges are based -(ii) he must be given reasonable opportunity to cross-examine witnesses produced against him and examine himself or other witnesses on his behalf - (iii) he must be given opportunity to show cause that proposed punishment would not be proper punishment to inflict which means, that tentative determination of competent authority to inflict one of three punishments must be communicated to him - Held, all these requirements have been substantially complied with in present case - (B) Principle of natural justice - Compliance of - Held, it is true that an enquiry u/s. 240 must be conducted in accordance with principles of natural justice - But these principles are not embodied principles - What principle of natural justice should be applied in a particular case depends on facts and circumstances of that case - All that courts have to see is whether non-observance of any of those principles in a given case is likely to have resulted in deflecting course of justice - Held, provisions of 166(1) and 166(2) [similar to sub-ss. (1) and (2) of s. 59 of Govt. of India Act, 1935], are directory and substantial compliance with those provisions is sufficient - Appeal allowed.
