State of Kerala v P. P. Joseph and others

Supreme Court of India

14 August 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, S.M. Sikri, Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 72; 1970 (25) STC 483

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Inter-State trade - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956, ss. 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9 and 10 - Kerala General Sales Tax Act, 1963 - Central Sales Tax (Second Amendment) Act, 1958 - Respondents are dealers in eggs - They purchase eggs in State of Kerala and sell them to merchants outside State - Whether High Court erred in discharging order of assessment of turnover arising out of sale of eggs in course of inter-state trade? - Even if, no tax was leviable under general sales tax law of State in respect of intra-State transaction of sale, tax will be leviable on sale of goods effect by a dealer in course of inter-state trade according to sale tax law of appropriate State - Held, view of HC cannot be sustained in view of provisions of s.6 of principal Act as amended by ordinance, with retrospective effect - Proceeding remanded to Sales Tax Officer to be dealt with and assessment to sales tax on turnover of respondents under provisions of Central Sales Tax Act as amended by Ordinance 4 of 1969 - Order accordingly.
