M. L. Abdul Jabhar Sahib v H. V. Venkata Sastri & Sons & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

04 February 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Bachawat, S.M. Sikri, K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 410; (1969) 1 SCC 573; AIR 1969 SC 1147; 1969 (2) MLJ 107; 1969 (2) MLJ(SC) 107; [1969] 3 S.C.R. 513

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: (A) Land & Property - Transfer of Property Act, 1882 - Registration Act, 1908 - Security bond - Attestation - Intention - Decree was passed in favour of appellant against Hajee Ahmed Batcha for amount due under promissory notes - Hajee executed security bond - Respondents were money creditors of Hajee - Respondents filed applications to get order for rateable distribution of assets realized in execution of decree passed in favour of appellant - Appellant contended that properties were charged for payment of his decretal amount and sale proceeds were not available for rateable distribution amongst simple money creditors - Divisional Bench hearing appeals referred to Full Bench - Later, Divisional Bench allowed appeals and declared that respondents were entitled to rateable distribution along with appellant - Whether security bond was attested by two witnesses - Held, evidence did not show that registering officer put his signature on document with intention of attesting it - It was not proved that document was signed in presence of executant - Officer was not regarded as attesting witness - HC rightly rejected contention - SC decision in Giria Datt v. Gangotri, 1955 Indlaw SC 124, 351, followed - Decision in Abinash Chandra Bidvanidhi Bhattacharya v. Dasarath Malo, I.L.R. 56 Cal 598, followed - Appeals allowed.

(B) Land & Property - Transfer of Property Act, 1882 - Registration Act, 1908 - Security bond - Attestation by witnesses - Validity - Whether security bond was valid - Held, security bond was not required to be attested by witnesses - It was duly registered and was valid and operative - Appeals allowed.

(C) Land & Property - Transfer of Property Act, 1882 - Registration Act, 1908 - Sale of property - Discharge of encumbrance - Whether the decree in C.S. No. 56 of 1953 directed sale of properties for discharge of a charge thereon - Held, security bond created a charge over properties in favour of appellant for payment of decretal amount and gave them liberty to apply for sale of properties for discharge of encumbrance - Court did not agree with HC - Appeals allowed.

(D) Land & Property - Transfer of Property Act, 1882 - Registration Act, 1908 - Sale proceeds - Distribution - Money creditors - Whether respondents were entitled to rateable distribution of sale proceeds of assets - Held, proceeds of sale after defraying expenses of sale must be applied in first instance in discharging amount due to appellant, only balance left after discharging this amount could be distributed amongst respondents - Court rejected HC decision - Appeals allowed.
