Hindustan Steel Limited v Messrs Dilip Construction Company

Supreme Court of India

18 February 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 370; (1969) 1 SCC 597; AIR 1969 SC 1238; 1969 ALJ 1006; [1969] 3 S.C.R. 736

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Arbitration Act, Stamp Act, Indian Stamp Act

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Practice & Procedure - Stamp Act, 1899, s. 35 - Indian Arbitration Act, 1940 , ss. 30, 33 - Award by umpire - Validity - Respondents entered into contract - Dispute which arose between parties was referred to arbitration-Arbitrators differed and dispute was referred to umpire who made and published his award - Umpire filed award in Court of District Judge - appellant filed application for setting aside award u/ss. 30 , 33 of the 1940 Act claiming that award was unstamped and on that account 'invalid and illegal and liable to be set aside' - respondents then applied to District Court that award be impounded and validated by levy of stamp duty and penalty - By order District Judge directed that award be impounded - He then called on respondents to pay appropriate stamp duty on award and penalty and directed that authenticated copy of instrument be sent to Collector together with certificate in writing stating receipt of amount of duty and penalty - Against that order appellant moved HC in exercise of its revisional jurisdiction - HC rejected petition - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order passed by HC was justified - Held, the ??Act was fiscal measure enacted to secure revenue for State on certain classes of instruments it was not enacted to arm litigant with weapon of technicality to meet case of his opponent - Stringent provisions of the 1899 Act were conceived in interest of revenue - Once that object was secured according to law, party staking his claim on instrument would not be defeated on ground of initial defect in instrument - Court had not considered s. 42(2) of the 1899 Act which expressly rendered instrument, when certified by endorsement that proper duty and penalty had been levied in respect thereof, capable of being acted upon as if it had 'been duly stamped - Appeal dismissed.
