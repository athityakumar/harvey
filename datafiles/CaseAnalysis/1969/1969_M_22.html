State of Orissa v Maharaja Shri B. P. Singh Deo

Supreme Court of India

01 December 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 328; (1971) 3 SCC 52; AIR 1970 SC 670; [1970] 76 ITR 690

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Assessable income - Enhancement of - Assessing authority, namely, the Agricultural Income-tax Officer, being unable to rely on the books of account produced by the assessee, assessed him on the basis of best of judgment - On appeal, Assistant Collector of Agricultural Income-tax enhanced the assessable income for the year 1952-1953 by Rs. 30, 000 and for the year 1953-1954 by Rs. 20, 000, after giving notice to the assessee to show cause against the proposed enhancement - Tribunal affirmed that decision - Whether the Assistant Collector of Agricultural Income-tax was justified in enhancing the assessment made by the Agricultural Income-tax Officer.

Held, mere fact that the material placed by the assessee before the assessing authorities is unreliable does not empower those authorities to make an arbitrary order. Assessment must be based on some relevant material. The Agricultural Income-tax Tribunal gave no reasons in its order for affirming the decision of the Assistant Collector. Therefore the Tribunal was clearly in error in confirming the decision of the Assistant Collector. Hence, the HC was justified in interfering with the order of the Tribunal. Appeal dismissed.
