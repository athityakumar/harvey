 Maganbhai Ishwarbhai Patel v Union of India and Another

Supreme Court of India

09 January 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, G.K. Mitter, A.N. Grover, J.C. Shah, V. Ramaswami

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 269; (1970) 3 SCC 400; AIR 1969 SC 783; [1969] 3 S.C.R. 254

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Defence & Security Forces; Indirect Tax; Service

Keywords: Attorney-General

Summary: Parliament & Legislature - Constitution - Constitution (Ninth Amendment) Act, 1960 - Government Of India Act, 1935 - Indian Independence Act, 1947 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 2, 3 and 32 - Challenged act of Government of India of coding without approval of Parliament the areas in the Rann of Kutch known as Kanjarkot, Chhadbet, Dharabanni, Priol Valo Kun and two inlets on either side of Tharparkar to Pakistan as awarded to it in the award, 19-2-1968, of the Indo-Pakistan Western Bombay case Tribunal - Held, it is difficult to accept that at any time effective sovereign authority could have been exercised over these inlets by the Maharao of Kutch and there is no evidence of exercise of any such right, before or after occupation of Sind - Therefore there being no evidence of exercise of sovereign authority by Maharao of Kutch, this Court cannot treat it as part of Indian territory - Appeals and petitions dismissed.
