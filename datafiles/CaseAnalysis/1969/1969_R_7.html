R. Obliswami Naidu v Additional State Transport Appellate Tribunal, Madras and Others

Supreme Court of India

17 February 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, S.M. Sikri, R.S. Bachawat

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 407; (1969) 1 SCC 733; AIR 1969 SC 1130; [1969] 3 S.C.R. 730

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1939, ss. 47, 48 and 57 - Whether determination as to number of stage carriages required on a route should be done at a stage anterior to that of entertaining applications for stage carriage permits or that it could be done at time it considers applications made by operators for stage carriage permits in that route? - Held, on an examination of relevant provisions of Act and purpose behind ss. 47 and 57, court convinced that before granting a stage carriage permit two independent steps have to be taken - Firstly, there should a determination by RTA u/s. 47(3) of number of stage carriages for which stage carriage permits may be granted in that route - Thereafter applications for stage carriage permits in that route should be entertained - RTA is not competent to grant stage carriage permits for more carriages than fixed u/s. 47(3) - S. 48(1) which empowers authority to grant or refuse to grant permit starts with words 'subject to provisions of s. 47 - It is, therefore, clear that authority has first to fix limit and after having done so, consider application or representations in connection therewith in accordance with procedure laid down in s. 57 - Appeal dismissed.
