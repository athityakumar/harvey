 Ram Kirpal Bhagat and Others v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

13 November 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Ray, Mohammad Hidayatullah, S.M. Sikri, G.K. Mitter, P. Jaganmohan Reddy

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 378; (1969) 3 SCC 471; (1970) SCC (Cr) 154; AIR 1970 SC 951; 1970 CRLJ 875; [1970] 3 S.C.R. 233

Case Digest

Subject: Excise

Keywords: Enactment, Tariff Act, 1934, Santhal Parganas Settlement Regulation, 1872

Summary: Excise - Seizure - Imports and Exports Control Act, 1947 - S. 3 - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Ss. 147, 149, 333 and 379 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Ss. 173 and 178 - Appellant was arrested with bags of cloves on which no duty was paid - Appellant was rescued by his other associates when they entered into a scuffle with the officers of Excise Department who apprehended appellant - Officers of the Excise Department received injuries - Trial Court and High Court held that officers acted within their authority and powers - Whether the officers had power to seize the goods and arrest the appellants - Held, yes - Reasonable suspicion arose against appellant - Officers were justified in arresting the appellant - Inspectors of Central Excise were Land Customs Officers and Officers of Customs under the Sea Customs Act, 1878 - Authorized to make arrest and seizure - Appeal dismissed.
