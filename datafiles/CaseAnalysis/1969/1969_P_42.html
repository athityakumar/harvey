V. P. Gopala Rao v Public Prosecutor, Andhra Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

07 March 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Bachawat, S.M. Sikri, K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 401; (1969) 1 SCC 704; AIR 1970 SC 66; 1970 CRLJ 22; 1969 (18) FLR 397; 1970 LabIC 56; 1970 (2) LLJ 59; 1969 (2) MLJ(SC) 114; [1969] 3 S.C.R. 875

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Factories Act, 1948 - Andhra Pradesh Factory Rules, 1950 - Licence - Factory - Manufacturing process - Master and servant relationship - Appellant was prosecuted and tried for operating factory without obtaining licence from Chief Inspector and his previous permission for getting approved plans of building - Second Addl. Munsif Magistrate accepted defence contention and acquitted appellant - HC allowed convicted appellant - Appellant contended that premises did not constitute factory and it was not necessary for him to obtain licence or permission - Whether appellant contravene s. 16(1) of the Act and rr. 3 and 5(3) of the Rules by operating factory without obtaining licence or permission from Chief Inspector - Held, company's premises were a factory - Work processes followed in appellant's company were manufacturing processes - Prosecution proved that more than twenty persons worked in premises every day - Relationship of master and servant existed between work-men and management - HC decision was right - SC decision in State of Kerala v. V. M. Patel, 1960 Indlaw SC 31, followed - Appeal dismissed.
