Messrs V. O. Tractoroexport, Moscow v Messrs Tarapore and Company and Another

Supreme Court of India

28 October 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Grover, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 362; (1969) 3 SCC 562; AIR 1971 SC 1; 1971 (2) MLJ(SC) 44; [1970] 3 S.C.R. 53

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Foreign Award

Summary: CPC, 1908, Pre - Foreign Awards (Recognition and Enforcement) Act, 1961, s. 3(1) - Arbitration Act, 1940, s. 35 - (A) Interpretation of Statutes - Need for consistency with International law - (B) Provisons of 1961 Act - (1) Expression 'submission' in s. 3(1) means actual submission or completed reference and does not mean an agreement to refer or an arbitral clause - 'Agreement' does not mean a commercial contract but an agreement to refer or an arbitral clause - (2) Suit in India not liable to be stayed when there is absence of actual submission of dispute to arbitration in foreign country - Foreign firm, a party to suit will be restrained from proceeding with arbitration in foreign country - Held, interpretation should not be inconsistent with established International Law and domestic legislation to carry out International conventions - Genesis and the background of the Act considered - Principles of s. 35 of 1940 Act will be applied - Appeal Allowed.
