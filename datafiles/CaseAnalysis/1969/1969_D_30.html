Dhian Singh v Municipal Board, Saharanpur

Supreme Court of India

31 July 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, S.M. Sikri, G.K. Mitter

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 508; (1969) 2 SCC 371; AIR 1970 SC 318; 1970 CRLJ 492; [1970] 1 S.C.R. 736

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection; Health & Drug; Socio-Economic

Summary: CrPC, 1898, s. 417(3) - Prevention of Food Adulteration Act, 1954, s. 20 - Constitution, art. 136 - (A) Complaint against offence under 1954 Act - By Municipal Board - But signed by its food Inspector - Acquittal - Competency to file - (B) Report of Public Analyst - Need not contain mode or particulars of analysis nor the test applied - But should contain result of analysis namely, data from which it can be inferred whether the article of food was or was not adulterated - Relevant data given in report - Conviction on its basis - (C) Permission u/s. 20 - No question of applying one's mind to the facts of case before institution of complaint arises - Authority under section can be conferred long before a particular offence takes place - (D) Appealby special leave u/art. 136 - Proceedings under 1954 Act - Food Inspector's authority to file complaint neither challenged before trial court nor before HC in appeal - But contented for first time before SC - Permissibility - Municipal Board hel, was competent to file complaint or to authorise its Food Inspector on its behalf - Accused can be convicted on basis of such report - Authority u/s. 20 can be conferred long before a particular offence takes place - Accused cannot be permitted to take up contention for first time before the SC after disposal of appeal by the HC - Appeal Dismissed.
