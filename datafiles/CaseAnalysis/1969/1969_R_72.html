Ruston and Hornsby Limited v Zamindara Engineering Company

Supreme Court of India

09 September 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 494; (1969) 2 SCC 727; AIR 1970 SC 1649; 22 PTC 175; 1977 (1) SCJ 34; [1970] 2 S.C.R. 222

Case Digest

Subject: Intellectual Property

Keywords: Registered User

Summary: Intellectual Property - Companies Act, 1956 - Trade Marks Act, 1940, ss. 21, 22, 25 and 26 - Whether use of the word 'RUSTAM INDIA' by the respondent constituted infringement of the appellant's trade Mark 'RUSTON'? - Court held, if respondent's trade mark is deceptively similar to that of the appellant the fact that the word 'INDIA' is added to the respondent's trade mark is of no consequence and the appellant is entitled succeed in its action for infringement of its trade mark - Appeal allowed.
