State of Mysore and Another v P. N. Nanjundiah and Another

Supreme Court of India

07 March 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 550; (1969) 3 SCC 633; 1969 SLR 346; 1969 UJ 380

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Service

Summary: Service - Bombay Civil Service Rules, r. 50(b) - Civil Services Rules - Mysore Jail Service Rules, r. 53(b) (i) - Whether respondent fulfilled qualifications for being promoted as a overseer? - Held, it is clear that services of an officer on deputation to another department is treated as equivalent to service in parent department and it is this equation between service in two departments that forms basis of r. 53(b)(i) of Mysore Jail Service Rules - So long as service of employee in new department is satisfactory and he is obtaining increments and promotions in that department, it stands to reason that satisfactory service and manner of its discharge in post he actually fills, should be deemed to be rendered in parent department - Also, so as to entitle him to promotion which are seniority-cum-merit basis - Appeal dismissed.
