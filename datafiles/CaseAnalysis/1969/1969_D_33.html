 Rayala Corporation (P) Ltd. & Ors. v Director Of Enforcement, New Delhi

Supreme Court of India

23 July 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

Vishishtha Bhargava, J.M. Shelat, C.A. Vaidyialingam, K.S. Hegde, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 146; (1969) 2 SCC 412; AIR 1970 SC 494; 1970 CRLJ 588; [1970] 1 S.C.R. 639

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax

Keywords: Bombay Prohibition Act 1949

Summary: FERA, 1947, ss. 23(1)(b), 23(1)(a) and 23D, 4(1), 5(1), 9, 23D(1) and proviso and 23(3) - Defence of India Rules, 1962, rr. 132A(2) and 132A(4) - Limitation Act, 1908 - Constitution, art. 14 - (A) FERA , 1947 - (1) Vires - Provision of s.23(1)(b) - Constitutionality of - (2) Contravention of ss. 4(1), 5(1) and 9 - Enquiry u/s. 23-D (1) - Show cause notice - Complaint to the Court without having any material which could lead to the opinion that Director of Enforcement will not be in a position to impose adequate penalty - Validity - (B) Violation of r. 132-A(2) of 1962 Rules - Prosecution launched on 17-03-1968 after rule was omitted by Defence of India Amendment Rules 1965 - Legality - Held, provision does not violate art.14 of Constitution - Complaint, held was filed without complying with the proviso and was invalid - Prosecution is illegal - Appeal Allowed.
