Arjun Kanoji Tankar v Santaram Kanoji Tankar

Supreme Court of India

18 April 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 531; (1969) 3 SCC 555; AIR 1969 SC 77; 1969 UJ 405

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Summary: Trusts & Associations - Indian Partnership Act, 1932 - Agreement - Termination - Maintainability - Plaintiff started business of Printing Press in 1937 - Defendant joined as partner by agreement 16-03-1953 to share profits and losses of business, but without any interest in machinery, goodwill and premises - Dispute arose between partners and plaintiff terminated partnership by notice dated 19-04-1957 - Plaintiff filed suit against defendant in Trial Court which was decreed - Aggrieved by said order defendant preferred appeal before HC which was dismissed - Hence instant appeal - Whether order passed by lower Court was maintainable - Held, Courts below were right in holding that business of Press since commencement in year 1937 was exclusive business of plaintiff till he admitted defendant as a partner in year 1953 - Further, there was no evidence that plaintiff had, while entering into a partnership with defendant, surrendered his individual interest in assets brought by him into business, or had admitted that defendant was to be owner in equal share with him in all assets brought into partnership - Furthermore, right of defendant to a share in assets brought into business depended upon terms of agreement of partnership - Thus, there was no rule that whatever was brought by a partner in partnership and was continued to be used by members was presumed to have become property of partnership - Appeal dismissed.
