Navalkha and Sons v Sri Ramanya Das and Others

Supreme Court of India

27 October 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, I.D. Dua

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 379; (1969) 3 SCC 537; AIR 1970 SC 2037; [1970] 40 Comp Cas 936; 1970 (2) CompLJ 8; 1970 (2) MLJ(SC) 46; 1970 (2) SCJ 96; [1970] 3 S.C.R. 1; 1970 UJ 176

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Corporate - Companies (Court) Rules, 1959, r. 273 - CPC, 1908, O. 21, r. 92 - Company - Auction sale is subject to confirmation of court - Must fetch reasonable price - Property does not vest in auction purchaser (unless sale is confirmed) - Prejudice is inherent - Sale confirmed without being so satisfied - Confirmation challenged and, thereby, sale also - Held, confirmation is not proper exercise of jurisdiction - Sale held not by public auction and without due publicity, confirmation set aside - Appeals dismissed.
