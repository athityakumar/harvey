In The Matter of Durga Show and Others

Supreme Court of India

02 September 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

Vishishtha Bhargava

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 497; (1970) 3 SCC 696; 1970 (6) DLT 494; 1970 (72) PLR 910

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 22(5) - Preventive Detention - Duty of State Govt. arising out of provisions of art. 22(5) - Considerable delay between receipt of representation made by petitioners and its consideration and rejection by Govt. - In none of cases any attempt has been made in counter affidavit to explain delay - A delay of 16 days which is minimum out of three cases is a long delay where a person is being detained without trial under a special law relating to preventive detention - Held, petitioners were entitled to receive a consideration of their representations by State Govt. as expeditiously as possible which Govt. failed to do, and consequently their continuance in detention thereafter is illegal - Petitions allowed.
