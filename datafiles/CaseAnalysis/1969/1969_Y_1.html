Yogendra Nath Naskar v Commissioner of Income Tax, Calcutta

Supreme Court of India

18 February 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, J.C. Shah, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 281; (1969) 1 SCC 555; AIR 1969 SC 1089; [1969] 74 ITR 33; [1969] 3 S.C.R. 742; 1969 UJ 171

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Artificial Juridical Person

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income-tax Act, 1961, s. 2(31) - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922 - Assessment - Income - Trust - Hindu undivided family - R left a will by which he left certain properties as debuttar to two deities in land adjoining his residential house - R appointed his two adopted sons H /deceased and Y as shebaits - For a long time income from property was assessed in hands of shebaits as trustees - In respect of A/Y 1950-51 and 1951-52, two shebaits contended that there was no trust executed and as such income from property did not attract liability to tax and particularly assessments made in name of H and his brother Y as trustees of debuttar estate could not be sustained - Appellate Commissioner set aside assessments - Claim for exemption under proviso to s.4(3)(i) of Act was rejected - On appeal Appellate Commissioner upheld assessment orders of Income Tax Officer - Hence instant appeal - Whether a Hindu deity could be treated as a unit of assessment u/ss. 2(31) and 3 of 1922Act - Held, Hindu deity falls within meaning of word 'individual u/s. 3 of Act and could be treated as a unit of assessment - Hindu idol was, according to long established authority, founded upon religious customs of Hindus and recognition by Courts of law a 'juristic entity'- Hindu idol had a juridical status with power of suing and being sued - Dieties interests were attended to by person who had deity in his charge and who was in law its manager with all powers which would, in such circumstances, on analogy, be given to manager of estate of an infant heir - Quote authorities was unnecessary for that doctrine stated, was firmly established - Appeals disposed of.
