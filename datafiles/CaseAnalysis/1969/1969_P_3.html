P. M. Mohammed Meerakhan v Commissioner of Income Tax, Ernakulam

Supreme Court of India

12 February 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, A.N. Grover, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 324; (1969) 2 SCC 25; AIR 1969 SC 1053; [1969] 73 ITR 735; [1969] 3 S.C.R. 659

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922 - Whether transactions constituted a venture in the nature of trade and the surplus thereon was assessable to tax? - Recital of the agreement clearly indicates that the intention of the assessee in purchasing the estate was to re-sell it at a profit - Profit was ascertained by including the land purchased in the stock in trade and valuing the stock in trade at the beginning and at the end of the relevant accounting year - Held, transactions of the assessee constituted an adventure in the nature of trade and were, in the course of a profit-making scheme - Decision of High Court was correct - Appeal dismissed.
