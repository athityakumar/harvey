Somnath Burman v Dr. S. P. Raju and Another

Supreme Court of India

16 October 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 459; (1969) 3 SCC 129; AIR 1970 SC 846; 1970 (2) MLJ(SC) 29; [1970] 2 S.C.R. 869

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Specific Relief Act, 1963, s. 9 - Purchase of land by plaintiff - Trespass and possession of same by second defendant - Suit filed by plaintiff for possession of suit property - Trial Court held that plaintiff has not established his title to suit property and also not satisfactorily proved that he was in possession of suit property at any time - (A) Whether plaintiff was in possession of suit property? - Held, oral evidence coupled with documentary evidences satisfactorily establishes possession of plaintiff - Possession of plaintiff was a sufficient evidence of title as owner against defendant - (B) Whether except in a suit u/s. 9 of Specific Relief Act, 1963, plaintiff for succeeding in suit, has to prove both existing title to suit property and its possession within twelve years? - Held, possession of plaintiff prior to 1945 is a good title against all but true owner - Defendants who are mere trespassers cannot defeat plaintiff's lawful possession by ousting him from suit property - Possessory title is a goods title as against everybody other than lawful owner - Appeal dismissed.
