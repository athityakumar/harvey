Mohd. Husain Umar Kochra etc. v K. S. Dalipsinghji and another

Supreme Court of India

31 March 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Bachawat, S.M. Sikri, K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 1; (1969) 3 SCC 429; (1970) SCC (Cr) 99; AIR 1970 SC 45; 1970 CRLJ 9; 1999 (110) E.L.T. 347; [1970] 1 S.C.R. 130; 1969 UJ 356

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Customs - Criminal - Customs Act, 1962, s. 11 - Sea Customs Act, 1878, s. 167(81) - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 120B - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 124, 133 and 114 - (A) Was import of gold in contravention of s. 8(1) of Foreign Exchange Regulation Act, 1973 punishable u/s. 167(81) of Sea Customs Act, 1878? - Held, a fraudulent evasion of restriction imposed by s. 8(1) of FERA was punishable u/s. 167(81) - (B) Did prosecution establish general conspiracy laid in charge no. 1 and which is punishable u/s. 120 B(1)? - Held, there was a single general conspiracy embracing all activities since each conspirator profited from general scheme and each one of them played his own part in general conspiracy - (C) Did learned Magistrate wrongly allow a claim of privilege in respect of disclosure of certain addresses and cables and if so, with what effect? - Held, privilege not properly claimed u/s. 124 - Other cables and cable addresses were not relevant to defence, and their non-disclosure has not occasioned any failure of justice - (D) Did Lower Court wrongly refuse to issue commission for examination of 1st accused? - Held, application was misconceived and proper grounds for issue of commission u/s. 503 of Code Of Criminal Procedure had not been made out - (E) Did Lower Court wrongly refuse to recall PW 50 for cross-examination ? - Held, no ground was made out for recalling PW 50 - Appeal partly allowed.
