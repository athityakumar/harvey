K.M. Viswanatha Pillai v K.M. Shanmugham Pillai

Supreme Court of India

25 November 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri, K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 377; (1969) 1 SCC 188; AIR 1969 SC 493; 1969 (1) MLJ 86; [1969] 2 S.C.R. 896

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles (Amendment) Act, 1956 - Motor Vehicles Act 1939, ss. 2, 3, 19, 20 and 42(1) - Whether benamidar of vehicles, representing himself to be owner, falsely obtained permits in his name, and allowed true owner, who had no permit, to conduct actual business? - Held, no - S. 42(1) does not require that owner himself should obtain permit - It only requires owner, that transport vehicle shall not be used except in accordance with conditions of permit - Definition of 'permit' itself shows that all permits need not be in name of owner because latter part of definition shows that it is only in case of a private carrier or a public carrier that a permit has to be in owner's name - There may be permit holders who own vehicle covered by permit and there may be permit holders who do not own vehicle - This clause appears to apply only to former case and not to latter - On its basis, therefore, it cannot be held to be a requirement of Act that in each case person in whose favour a permit has been issued should necessarily be owner of vehicle covered by it - Appeal allowed.
