Praduman Kumar v Virendra Goyal (Dead) By L. Rs.

Supreme Court of India

11 March 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 398; (1969) 1 SCC 714; AIR 1969 SC 1349; 1970 RCR(Rent) 304; [1969] 3 S.C.R. 950

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Transfer of Property Act, 1882 - Non payment of rent - Ejectment - Appeal - Respondent-tenant failed to pay rent accrued for two years - Appellant served notice for terminating tenancy and instituted a suit to obtain decree of ejectment and for payment of rent and compensation - Trial Court gave an opportunity to the tenants to pay the amount of rent due together with interest and costs, but the tenants failed to avail themselves of the opportunity - Appeal to HC was dismissed since HC had granted another opportunity to the tenants to make the requisite payment and grant relief against forfeiture of the tenancy - Hence the instant appeal - Whether, the decision of HC was liable to be set aside - Held, SC could not interfere with an order made in exercise of the discretion of the Courts below, specially when there was no evidence that the tenant was guilty of conduct thus, disentitled appellant to claim relief against forfeiture for non-payment of rent - According to s. 114 of 1882 Act if tenant made payment of rent at the hearing of the suit in ejectment a condition of the exercise of the Courts jurisdiction but an appeal being a rehearing of the suit, in appropriate cases it was open to the HC at the hearing of the appeal to relieve the tenant in default against forfeiture - Decree in ejectment against the tenant passed by the Court of First Instance had not taken away the jurisdiction of the HC to grant equitable relief - Appeal dismissed.
