 Assistant Commissioner Of Urban Land Taxand Others v The Buckingham & Carnatic Co. Ltd., Etc.

Supreme Court of India

11 April 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Ramaswami, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.C. Shah, G.K. Mitter, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 188; (1969) 2 SCC 55; AIR 1970 SC 169; [1970] 75 ITR 603; 1970 (1) MLJ(SC) 55; [1970] 1 S.C.R. 268

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Bihar Taxation On Passengers And Goods (Carried By Public Service Motor Vehicles)

Summary: Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 19(5), 19(6), 39(c) and 304(b) - Madras Urban Land Tax Act, 1966 - Constitutionally valid - In 1963 Madras Legislature enacted Madras Urban Land Tax Act, 1963 which came into force in city of Madras on 1-07-1963 - Whether Madras Legislature was competent to enact legislation under Entry 49 of List 11 of Schedule VII of Constitution of India, 1950? - Directive principle embodied in art. 39(c) applies both to Parliament and to State Legislature and Entries 86 to 88 of List I do not exclude any power of State Legislature to implement same principle - Legislative entries must be given a large and liberal interpretation, reason being that allocation of subjects to lists is not by way of scientific or logical definition but by way of a mere simplex enumeration of broad categories - There is no conflict between Entry 86 of List I and Entry 49 of List II - Basis of taxation under two entries is quite distinct - As regards Entry 86 of List I, basis of taxation is capital value of asset - For purpose of levying tax under Entry 49, List 11 State Legislature may adopt for determining incidence of tax annual or capital value of lands and buildings - But adoption of annual or capital value of lands and buildings for determining tax liability will not make fields of legislation under two entries overlapping - Held, two taxes are entirely different in their basic concept and fall on different subject matters - (B) Whether tax imposed valid? - Held, as a general rule it may be said that so long as a tax retains its character as a tax and is not confiscatory, or extortionate reasonableness of tax cannot be questioned - It is not possible to put test of reasonableness into straight jacket of a narrow formula - Objects to be taxed, quantum of tax to be levied, conditions subject to which it is levied and social and economic policies which a tax is designed to subserve are all matters of political character and these matters have been entrusted to Legislature and not to Courts - In applying test of reasonableness it is also essential to notice that power of taxation is generally regarded as an essential attribute of sovereignty and constitutional provisions relating to power of taxation are regarded not as grant of power but as limitation upon power which would otherwise be practically without - (C) Retrospective operation of Act, whether reasonable? - Held, retrospective operation of Act does not make it unreasonable - Restrictions imposed by the retrospective operation was reasonable in public interest - Madras Urban Land Tax Act, 1966 must be upheld as constitutionally valid - Order accordingly.
