Lala Mata Din v A. Narayanan

Supreme Court of India

25 August 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 353; (1969) 2 SCC 770; AIR 1970 SC 1953; [1970] 2 S.C.R. 90

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Limitation Act, 1963, s. 5 - Punjab Courts Act, s. 39(1) - Suit for ejectment - Extending time u/s. 5 - On ground of mistake - Whether mistake was bona fide or was merely device to cover an ulterior purpose such as laches on part of litigant or an attempt to save limitation in an underhand way? - Mistake of counsel may in certain circumstances be taken into account in condoning delay although there is no general proposition that mistake of counsel by itself is always a sufficient ground - Held, High Court has never considered matter in which mistake is bona fide one - If it had, it would have seen quite clearly that there was no attempt to avoid Limitation Act but rather to follow it albeit on a wrong reading of situation - Matter remitted to HC.
