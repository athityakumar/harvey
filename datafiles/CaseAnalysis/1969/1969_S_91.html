State Bank Of India v Ghamandi Ram (Dead) Through Shri Gurbax Rai

Supreme Court of India

13 February 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, J.C. Shah, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 593; (1969) 2 SCC 33; AIR 1969 SC 1330; [1969] 3 S.C.R. 681

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Corporate Body

Summary: Banking & Finance - Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Family & Personal - Displaced Persons (Debts Adjustment) Act, 1951, s. 13 - Pakistan (Administration of Evacuee Property) Ordinance, 1949, s. 13 - Pakistan (Administration Of Evacuee Property) Act, 1951, ss. 2(5), 7 and 11 - (A) Joint hindu family firm, if 'person' or 'body of individuals not incorporated' in Pakistan notification - Having regard to juristic nature of hindu joint family under Mitakshara law, family firm could not be treated as a 'person' within meaning of Pakistan notification - Held, firm was 'a body of individuals not incorporated' and hence amount in bank was evacuee property and had become vested in custodian of evacuee property, Pakistan, w.e.f. 01-03-1947 - (B) Priority between voluntary and involuntary assignments of debts in private international law - Rule of private international law is that where an involuntary assignment occurs after a voluntary assignment has already been made, question whether rights of voluntary assignee have been postponed or defeated would be governed by lex situs of debt - Further held, in present case, since involuntary assignment in favor of custodian in Pakistan occurred in 1949, when ordinance was promulgated after voluntary assignment by bank in favor of firm in 1948 and situs of debt was Pakistan (the bank having garnishable assets of respondent in Pakistan which could be attached by custodian in Pakistan), question of priority would be governed by law in Pakistan, namely, provisions of ordinance and under ordinance, amount vested in custodian in Pakistan w.e.f. 01-03-1947 - Therefore, liability of bank to firm must be deemed to have been extinguished - Appeal allowed.
