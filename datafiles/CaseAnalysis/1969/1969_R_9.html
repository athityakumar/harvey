R. V. M. Neeladri Rao and Another v Board of Revenue

Supreme Court of India

23 September 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 480; (1969) 3 SCC 49; 1970 (2) MLJ(SC) 39; [1970] 2 S.C.R. 538

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Land & Property; Service

Summary: Land & Property - Board of Revenue Act, 1913, s. 20 - Life Insurance Corporation of India - Andhra Pradesh (Andhra Area) Estates (Abolition and Conversion into Ryotwari) Act, 1948 - Andhra Pradesh (Andhra Area) Estate Land (Reduction of Rent) Act, 1947, s. 55(1) - Land acquisition - Compensation of - (A) At what rate should compensation for termination of leasehold rights be payable to appellants? - Held, as a ryotwari settlement was brought into force provisions of Act XXX of 1947, ceased to be applicable owing to provisions of s. 23 of Act - Therefore, for purpose of compensation it was settlement rate which should have been taken into account and not rent payable under Act XXX 1947 - Only rent as fixed under Act XXX of 1947, in three preceding years could be taken into account - Settlement rates represent what is payable to Govt. as revenue in respect of land granted on patta by Govt. in ryotwari settlement - They do not represent what is due to persons like appellants from their tenants - It is not possible to equate rents payable by tenants to appellants with land revenue payable to Govt. - No exception could thus be taken to manner and measure of computing compensation - (B) Whether in determining compensation payable to appellants it is unexpired portion of period for which lease was created that should have formed basis and not period provided by r. 1(ii)? - Further held, s. 20(2) of Act simply says that rules must be framed having regard to value of right, unexpired portion period for which right was created - Did not, in any way, fetter power of rule making authority to frame r. 1(ii) in manner in which it has been done - (C) Whether appellants are entitled to interest on amount of unpaid rents in hands of Govt. for period 1950 to 1961 as u/s. 55 of Act after notified date land-holder is not entitled to collect any rent which accrued due to him from any ryotwari before and is outstanding on that date? - Further held, on language of s. 55 as well as under general principles of law, it submitted, appellants should have been held to be entitled to payment of interest on amounts withheld by Govt. - Appeal allowed only to extent that it is declared that appellants should have been paid interest at rate of 6% per annum on amount of rents collected by manager on behalf of Govt.
