Hira H. Advani v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

13 August 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

G.K. Mitter, K.S. Hegde, S.M. Sikri

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 6; (1969) 2 SCC 662; AIR 1971 SC 44; 1971 CRLJ 5; 1983 (13) E.L.T. 1413; 1970 MLJ 490; [1970] 1 S.C.R. 821

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Customs - Criminal - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 1, 114 illustration(f) and 132 - Customs Act, 1962, ss. 171-A and 171(3) - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 192 - Oaths Act, 1873, s. 4 - (a) Presumption as to common course of business-Marine Insurance -Insurance of export cargo - Marine premium debit note - When prepared - Practice of insurance company - Held, in usual course of business of insurance company, marine premium debit note always prepared at time when policy is issued and a copy thereof attached to copy of policy kept in office records - Court entailed to rely on presumption afforded by illustration (f) of s. 114 of Evidence Act that practice of insurance company of attaching such a note to policy had been followed in a particular case and proof of preparation of original premium note not required- (B) - Power of Customs Officers to summon persons to give evidence and produce documents - Whether admissible in evidence in criminal proceedings against him? - Held, a Customs Officer is given power to interrogate any person in connection with smuggling of any goods which it is his duty to prevent - Not a witness giving evidence in a court and testimony makes liable u/s. 193, Indian Penal Code only because of express provision of law in Sub-s. 4 of s. 171-A of Customs Act, 1962 - (C) Double jeopardy Sea Customs Authorities are merely constituted administrative machinery for purpose of adjudging confiscation, increased rates of duty and penalty prescribed in Act and are not a Judicial Tribunal - No double jeopardy u/s. 171-A, Customs Act, 1962, - (D) Power of Custom Officer for interrogation - Witness not excused from answering on ground that answer will incriminate him - S. 132 of Indian Evidence Act not attracted in interrogations by Customs Officer u/s. 171-A, Sea Customs Act - No scope for introduction of a rule of evidence in criminal cases unless it is within four corners of s.132 of Indian Evidence Act - Nothing in s. 171-A of Customs Act, 1962 which limits right of interrogation to question answers whereto may not incriminate person interrogated Authority to administer oaths etc. - Power of Customs Officers to summon persons, etc. - Appeal dismissed.
