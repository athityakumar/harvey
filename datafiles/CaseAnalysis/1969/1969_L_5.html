Jamshed Jahan Begam & Ors. v Lakhan Lal & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

25 September 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

C.A. Vaidyialingam, I.D. Dua, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 88; (1969) 3 SCC 1; AIR 1971 SC 1678; 1970 ALJ 1104; [1970] 2 S.C.R. 566

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments; Agriculture & Agri. Products

Keywords: Debt Laws

Summary: U.P. Encumbered Estates Act, 1934, ss. 18 and 24 - U.P.Z.A.& L.R. Act, 1951, ss. 6(a) and 18 - (A) Encumbered Estates - (1) 'Debtor's property other than proprietary rights in land ' - Bhumidhari rights, whether proprietary - (2) Scope of s.19(2)(b) - Interest or rights of debtor need not be mentioned in the decree - (B) Zamindari Abolition - Tenancy - Trees constituting grove do not vest in States - If can proceeded against u/s. 24 of 1934 Act - Held, bhumidhari rights are not proprietary rights - Nature and extent of property as being liable to attachment and sale is only to be mentioned - They can be proceeded against u/s. 24 - Appeal Dismissed.
