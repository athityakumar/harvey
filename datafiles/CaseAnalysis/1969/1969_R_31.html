Mudi Gowda Gowdappa Sankh v Ram Chandra Ravagowda Sankh

Supreme Court of India

07 January 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, J.C. Shah, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 575; (1969) 1 SCC 386; AIR 1969 SC 1076; [1969] 3 S.C.R. 245

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Family & Personal Law - Partition - Joint family property - Mitakshara law - Suit instituted by adopted son alleging partition deed as fraudulent - Both Trial Court and High Court reached a concurrent finding after an elaborate examination of evidence that partition deed was not genuine, and that it was effected for an ulterior purpose in order to defeat rights of widows in joint family - Whether partition deed executed was a sham transaction and not intended to be effective? - Held, it is manifest that finding of Lower Courts upon this question is essentially a finding upon a question of fact, and in an appeal by special leave it is normal practice of Supreme Court to accept such a concurrent finding of fact as correct - Properties subject to sale found to be purchased from joint family funds, those properties will be joint family property and cannot be transferred before proper partition - There is no presumption that a Hindu family merely because it is joint, possesses any joint property - Burden of proof - Partition is invalid - Appeal dismissed.
