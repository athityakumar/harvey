Beohar Rajendra Sinha and Others v State of Madhya Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 March 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, Mohammad Hidayatullah, G.K. Mitter

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 397; (1969) 1 SCC 796; AIR 1969 SC 1256; [1969] 3 S.C.R. 955

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Possession of land - Ownership - Dispute - Plaintiffs claimed that they were owners of plot in dispute and they and their predecessors were in possession of said plot from immemorial times - State Govt., defendant refused to recognize possession of plaintiffs - On an appeal to Trial Court by plaintiffs it was held that there was no evidence regarding the user of the property by the plaintiffs and in the subsequent years a part of the property was found in possession of the Municipal Committee - Further, appeal HC held that order of defendant refusing to recognize share of plaintiffs was wrong and illegal - Whether, the decision of HC was justified - Held, that there was no evidence to show that the plaintiffs were in possession of the land from 1909 to 1932, and the plaintiffs had not established their title by prescription for the statutory period of sixty years - HC had no justification to hold that the plaintiffs had established the title of 'Raiyat Sarkar' and the finding of the HC was not based upon any proper evidence - There was no reliable oral or documentary evidence to prove that the plaintiffs or their ancestors had any possession over the disputed land - Since, the ownership of the land was unknown and where possession was proved for a long time, but its original title could not be traced the land would be recorded as Govt. land - Appeal dismissed.
