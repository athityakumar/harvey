Kanbi Nanji Virji and Others v State of Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

06 October 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, Vishishtha Bhargava

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 476; (1970) 3 SCC 103; (1970) SCC (Cr) 520; AIR 1970 SC 219; 1970 CRLJ 363; 1969 UJ 730

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: CrPC, 1898, s. 423 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 3 - IPC, 1860, ss. 34, 149, 304 , 323 and 304, Part I - (A) Criminal Procedure - Appreciation of evidence of prosecution witness by appellate court - Truth and falsehood not separable - (B) IPC, 1860 - (1) Free fight between two groups - Injuries sustained by persons of both groups in course of such fight - Death of two persons - (C) Bona fide assertion of right of way through uncultivated portion of private land by villagers - Does not amount to common intention to commit a criminal act - Conviction u/ss. 304 Part I / 34 - Legality of - Held, entire evidence has to be rejected - Only persons found to have inflicted injuries can be convicted for the injuries caused by them - Conviction u/ss. 304 Part I/34, held, illegal - Conviction, held, illegal - Appeal Allowed.
