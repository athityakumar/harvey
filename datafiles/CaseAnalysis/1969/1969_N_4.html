Bhudan Singh and Another v Nabi Bux and Another

Supreme Court of India

20 August 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, A.N. Ray

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 502; (1969) 2 SCC 481; AIR 1970 SC 1880; 1970 ALJ 903; [1970] 2 S.C.R. 10

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Possession Of Land

Summary: Land & Property - Possession of Land - Zamindari Abolition and Land Reforms Act, 1950, ss. 4, 6 and 9 - Whether appellants who had demolished buildings put up by respondents and put up some other building in their place can claim any legal rights in property? - In demolishing buildings appellants committed offence of mischief - No material on record to find out value of buildings demolished by and value of building put up by appellant - It is not satisfied that newly put up building is worth more than buildings that had been demolished - Old buildings have been substituted by new buildings and owners of old buildings continue to be owners of new building - True owner of land is entitled to recover land with building on it - When respondents came back, they were entitled to recover not only site but also building constructed on it by appellants - Held, on date of vesting, respondents were owners of building in question - In law respondents are owners - Appeal dismissed.
