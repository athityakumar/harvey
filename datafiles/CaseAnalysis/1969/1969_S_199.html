Municipal Committee, Amritsar and Another v State of Punjab and Others

Supreme Court of India

30 January 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 413; (1969) 1 SCC 475; AIR 1969 SC 1100; [1969] 3 S.C.R. 447

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Environment, Wildlife & Animal

Summary: Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 13, 32, 246 and 226 - Punjab Cattle Fairs (Regulation) Act, 1968, s. 3 - (A) Cattle Fair - Violation of art. 19(1)(b) and (d) - Held, definition cl. 2(bb) does not prohibit the concourse of twenty five persons - Act does not place restrictions upon freedom of assembly or of free movement either u/cls. (b) or (d) of art. 19(1) - The Act only prohibits an individual or local authority from arranging a gathering of more than twenty-five persons for purpose specified in definition of 'cattle fair' - The restriction for purpose of making monopoly effective must be regarded as reasonable within meaning of cls. (3) and (5) of art. 19 - (B) Directions of Fair Officer - In respect of providing assistance to organizing fair - Authority of - Held, demand made by Fair Officer for assistance of staff, articles and offices of Municipality for holding fair and demand for supply of water and electricity and making suitable sanitary arrangements is also uncalled for and unauthorized - Directions invalid - (C) Declaration of Fair Area u/s. 4 (2)(i) r/w s. 2(d) of the Act - Calling upon the petitioner to stop his cattle business at Fair Area - Validity of - Held, Govt. of Punjab is not competent to declare a particular land as a fair area - Notification declaring particular land as fair area is, therefore, invalid - The petitioner's land as fair area and intimation calling upon him to stop his business of cattle market is unauthorized - Petitioners are not prohibited from carrying on business of cattle market on their own lands - Petition disposed of.
