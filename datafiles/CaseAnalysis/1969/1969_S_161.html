Municipal Council Raipur and Another v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

18 August 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri, G.K. Mitter, P. Jaganmohan Reddy

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 108; (1969) 2 SCC 582; AIR 1970 SC 1923; 1970 CRLJ 1656; 1970 (2) LLJ 40; [1970] 1 S.C.R. 915

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Motor Transport Workers Act, 1961, ss. 2(g), 3, 38, 38(1) - Maintenance of public order - Sustainability - Inspector inspected Municipal Council under the Act and found that 50 transport workers had been employed by Council but Council had not been registered as required u/s. 3(1) of the Act - Inspector filed complaint before Special Magistrate and Presiding Officer, Labour Court, who issued summons to accused persons- Accused persons appeared by counsel and filed preliminary objections - Magistrate dismissed complaint and discharged accused persons - State filed revision before Sessions Court and sessions court dismissed revision - State then filed revision u/s. 439, CrPC - HC held that revision lay u/s. 439, CrPC because order passed by Magistrate was order of discharge and not of acquittal - Hence, instant appeal - Whether revision lay u/s. 439, CrPC and if Municipal Council does not fall within definition of expression 'motor transport undertaking' u/s. 2(g) and (3) that transport vehicles owned by Municipal Council were exempt u/s. 38(1) of the Act - Held, it was true that it was summons case and no formal charge was necessary to be framed u/s. 242, CrPC but even so when accused appeared, before anything was done accused filed preliminary objection and no particulars of offence of which accused was charged were even stated to him - HC was right in holding that Municipal Council was motor transport undertaking as defined in the Act - First, the Act provided for welfare of motor transport workers and regulated conditions of their work - Such beneficial acts were not, as rule, construed strictly - Secondly, words of definition were plain and not susceptible of any reasonable limitation - It seemed that by using word 'includes' Legislature undoubtedly intended to enlarge meaning of expression 'motor transport undertaking' - Words 'private carrier' had been given specific meaning in the Act, 1939, and it was difficult to limit this specific meaning on any reasonable basis - Further, s. 38 of the Act, which exempts certain transport vehicles, also proceeds on basis that private carrier who was carrying on activities which were not commercial would be included within expression 'motor transport undertaking' - Appeal dismissed.
