Malini Ayyappa Naicker (Now Dead) Through L.R. v Seth Manghraj Udhavdas Firm By Managing Partner Chathurbhuj

Supreme Court of India

13 February 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, S.M. Sikri, R.S. Bachawat

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 237; (1969) 1 SCC 688; AIR 1969 SC 1344; 1970 (1) MLJ(SC) 1; [1969] 3 S.C.R. 698

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Civil Procedure Code, ss. 100(1) and 4 - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881 - Provincial Insolvency Act, 1920, ss. 53 and 75(1) - Whether mortgages are supported by consideration and whether they are executed with a view to screen some of properties of insolvents' from their creditors? - Ordinarily burden of proving that a document impeached u/s. 53 of Act is not supported by consideration is on party who challenges its validity - Evidence of mortgagees as regards payment of consideration is strongly corroborated by entries in insolvents' account books - Hence mortgagees finding as regards validity of mortgage under Exh. A-2 must be held to be final - After going through evidence bearing on point, Court is not satisfied that payment of amount is satisfactorily proved - Appeal allowed.
