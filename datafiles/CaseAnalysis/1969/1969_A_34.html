Arati Paul v Registrar, Original Side, High Court, Calcutta

Supreme Court of India

10 March 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

Vishishtha Bhargava, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 369; (1969) 2 SCC 756; AIR 1969 SC 1133; [1969] 3 S.C.R. 926

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Practice & Procedure - Arbitration Act, 1940 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, ss. 521, 623 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 133(1), 226 - Arbitration Agreement - Revocation - Appellant presented Letter of Demand to Registrar of Original Side of HC to recall, cancel and withdraw filing of order from record of suit and to take it off file of that suit - Failing to get any response - Appellant 4-9-1963, presented petition under art. 226 of Constitution praying for issue of writ in nature of mandamus directing Registrar of HC on Original Side to forthwith recall, cancel and withdraw filing of said pretended Award dated 1-4-1963 as judgment in said Suit No. 1045/1957 as part of records of said suit, and another writ of mandamus directing Registrar of HC to forthwith take it off said, pretended Award from file and/or records of said Suit No. 1045/1957 - Notice of Motion taken out by K and B for revocation of Letters of Administration was partly heard by J - It appears that, simultaneously with these proceedings application for taking proceedings for Contempt of Court were also pending before him in this connection - Hearing in Matter No. 366/1963 was concluded on and then order was made that this Matter as well as proceedings relating to Notice of Motion for revocation of Letters of Administration and application for taking proceedings for contempt should appear in list for judgment one after the other - J. passed order discharging Rule as well as dismissing other two applications - Subsequently, on preliminary decree drawn up on basis of order of J. in Partition Suit No. 1045/1957 was signed by him, and on 3-9-1964, decree was filed - Appellant filed appeal challenging order passed by, J. dismissing Matter No. 366 of 1963 - Appeal was dismissed by Appellate Bench of HC and order of HC in appeal was filed - Appellant then applied for certificate under art. 133(1) of Constitution for leave to appeal to this Court - That having been refused, she obtained special leave from this Court and has now come up in this appeal challenging confirmation by Appellate Bench of order of dismissal of Matter No. 366 of 1963 - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order of HC in partition suit amounted to award or judgment in suit - Held, on face of it when this order was passed by J he acted as Judge - Seized of suit who alone was competent to pass preliminary decree in suit - Court could not accept that order made J should be held to be award of arbitrator pure and simple and not decree by Court - Court further held that sole relief claimed before HC was issue of writ of mandamus directing Registrar on Original Side to recall, cancel and withdraw order and to take it off record, on ground that it was award and not judgment of Court - Since Court had held that it was judgment of Court, Registrar on Original Side, under Rules of Sate HC, was bound to file it on record and retain it - Appellant could have sought appropriate remedy for having that judgment vacated and, if such remedy had been sought against that judgment directly, question whether it was good judgment and should be retained on record or not could have been appropriately decided - Remedy sought by appellant of seeking writ to restrain Registrar from keeping judgment on record of suit could 'not possibly be allowed, while judgment stood and was not vacated - Appeal dismissed.
