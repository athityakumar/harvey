Narendrajit Singh Another v State of Uttar Pradesh and Another

Supreme Court of India

21 November 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

G.K. Mitter, S.M. Sikri, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 233; (1970) 1 SCC 125; AIR 1971 SC 306; 1970 ALJ 70; [1970] 3 S.C.R. 278; 1970 UJ 196

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4(1) and 6(1) - Whether acquisition proceeding valid in view of non compliance of provisions of s. 4? - Issuance of a notification u/s. 4(1) is a condition precedent to exercise of any further powers under Act and a notification which does not comply with essential requirement of that provision of law must be held to be bad - It enjoins upon Govt. duty to specify locality in which land is needed - Any notification which is first step towards depriving a man of his property must be strictly construed and courts ought not to tolerate any lapse on part of acquiring authority in issue of such notification if it be of a serious nature - Held, notifications suffered from a very serious defect in that locality where lands were needed was not specified and defect in a notification u/s. 4(1) cannot be cured by giving full particulars in notification u/s. 6(1) - Appeals allowed.
