 Vrajlal Manilal & Co. & Ors. v State Of Madhya Pradesh. & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

25 April 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Shelat, Mohammad Hidayatullah, Vishishtha Bhargava, K.S. Hegde, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 180; (1969) 2 SCC 248; AIR 1970 SC 129; [1970] 1 S.C.R. 400

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Summary: M.P. Tendu Patta (Vyapar Viniyaman) Adhiniyam, 1964, ss. 5 and 5(2)(b) - M.P. Tendu Patta (Vyapar Viniyaman) Niyamawali, 1965, rr. 4, 5, 6, 7, 8 and 9 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Constitution, Pre., arts. 19(1)(f), 19(1)(g) , 19(5), 19(6) and 304(b) - (A) Provisions of s. 5(2)(b) of 1964 Act and rr. 4, 5, 6, 7, 8 and 9 of 1965 Rules - Intend behind - (B) Constitutional validity - Of s. 5 and r. 9 - Act creating State monopoly in trade of tendu leaves - Transport of leaves do not form integral part of trade - Restrictions imposed on transport affects fundamental rights u/art.19(1)(f) and (g) and have to satisfy tests of reasonableness u/art. 19, cl. (5) and first part of cl.(6) and also art. 304(b) - (C) Act infringing rights u/cl. (1)of art.19 Constitution - (D) Constitutionality of Act - (A) Held, provisions were intended to restrict not only movement of tendu leaves from purchasing units to place of storage but also their subsequent movement - (B) Held, s. 5 and r. 9 as also order D/- 04-06-1965 cannot be held unreasonable except to the extent of its requiring a permit for distribution by sattedars to mazdoors and cannot be challenged - Order D/- 12-10-1965 being a mere cancellation of concession is not a restriction - (C) Act is invalid unless it is protected by cl. (2) to (6) and burden of proof lies on those who seek its protection - (D) Rules of construction indicated - Appeal Dismissed.
