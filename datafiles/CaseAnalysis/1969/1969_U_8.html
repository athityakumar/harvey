 A. K. K. Nambiar v Union of India and Another

Supreme Court of India

28 October 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Ray, K.S. Hegde, J.C. Shah, J.M. Shelat, C.A. Vaidyialingam

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 220; (1969) 3 SCC 864; AIR 1970 SC 652; 1970 LabIC 566; 1969 RCR(Rent) 786; [1970] 3 S.C.R. 121; 1970 UJ 189

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service; Socio-Economic

Summary: Service - All India Service (Appeal and Discipline) Rules 1955, r. 7 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947 - (A) Whether order of suspension was warranted by rule? - Appellant was charged with offences under Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947 - There was an investigation and trial is awaiting relating to a criminal charge - Order of suspension has to be read in context of entire case and combination of circumstances - Held, order of suspension was made u/r. 7(3) and does not suffer from any vice of infringement of r. 7(1) - (B) Whether act of Govt. was mala fide? - No allegation against any particular officer of Govt. of India about acting mala fide - Chief Minister nor any other person was made a party - Held, appellant neither alleged nor established that order of suspension was made mala fide or that order was made for collateral purposes - (C) Affidavits - Admissibility - Neither petition nor affidavit was verified - Held, affidavits of all parties suffer from mischief of lack of proper verification with result that affidavits should not be admissible in evidence - Appeal dismissed.
