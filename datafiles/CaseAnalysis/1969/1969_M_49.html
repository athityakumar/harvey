Tayabali Jaferbhai Tankiwala v Messrs Asha and Company and Another

Supreme Court of India

24 September 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Grover, J.M. Shelat, V. Ramaswami

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 87; (1970) 1 SCC 46; AIR 1971 SC 102; [1970] 3 S.C.R. 554

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Transfer of Property Act 1872

Summary: Land & Property - Bombay Rents, Hotels and Lodging House Rate Control Act, 1947, s. 12(2) - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 113 - Suit for ejection - Whether, in a case where a second notice to quit is served, it means expiration of tenancy and a new tenancy being created and for waiver u/s. 113 of Transfer of Property Act, 1882 a new tenancy, by express or implied agreement is required? - Held, it is wholly unnecessary to decide whether for bringing about a waiver u/s. 113 of Transfer of Property Act, 1882 a new tenancy by an express or implied agreement must come into existence - All that need be observed is that s. 113 in terms does not appear to indicate any such requirement and all that has to be seen is whether any act has been proved on part of present appellant which shows an intention to treat lease as subsisting provided there is an express or implied consent of person to whom notice is given - There is no doubt that serving of second notice and what was stated therein together with claim as laid and amplified in plaint showed that landlord waived first notice by showing an intention to treat tenancy as subsisting and, that this was with express or implied consent of tenant to whom first notice had been given because he had even made payment of rent which had been demanded though it was after expiration of period of one month given in notice - Further, in suit requirement of personal necessity was made one of main grounds on which eviction was sought - In first notice which was sent in June 1956 no such requirement or ground had been mentioned - It was not open, therefore to landlord to say that he did not want to rely on second notice and should be allowed to base his action for eviction only on first notice containing ground of default in payment of arrears of rent - Appeal dismissed.
