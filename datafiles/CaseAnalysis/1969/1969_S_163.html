Ram Gopal Chaturvedi v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

29 April 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Bachawat, S.M. Sikri, Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 133; (1969) 2 SCC 240; AIR 1970 SC 158; 1970 (1) LLJ 367; 1969 MPLJ 706; [1970] 1 S.C.R. 472; 1969 SLR 429

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Madhya Pradesh Govt. Servants (Temporary and Quasi-permanent Service) Rules, 1960, r. 12 - (A) Whether r. 12 being framed without consulting State Public Service Commission and High Court was unconstitutional? - Held, not allowed to be raised for first time in Supreme Court - (B) Whether r. 12 was violative of art. 14 and 16 of Constitution of India, 1950? - Held, not violative of arts. 14 and 16 of Constitution of India, 1950 - R. 12 applies to all temporary Govt. servants who are not in quasi-permanent service - All such Govt. servants are treated alike - (C) Whether appellant's services could be terminated on one month's notice as his confirmation was recommended by HC after expiry of probationary period? - Not allowed to be raised for first time - Further held that it is immaterial that advertisement inviting applications for temporary posts of Civil Judges did not specifically mention that their services could be so terminated - (D)Whether order passed without consulting State Public Service Commission u/art. 320(2)(c) of Constitution of India, 1950 was invalid? - State of U P v M L Srivastava([1958] S C R 533) decided that provisions of art. 320(3)(c) were not mandatory and did not confer any rights on public servant and that absence of consultation with State Public Service Commission did not afford him a cause of action - In present case, impugned order did not deprive appellant of any vested right - Appellant was a temporary Govt. servant and had no right to hold office - The State Govt. had right to terminate his services u/r. 12 without issuing any notice to appellant to show cause against proposed action - Appeal dismissed.
