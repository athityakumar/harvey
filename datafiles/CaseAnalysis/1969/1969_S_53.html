Shivashankar Prasad Shah and Others v Baikunth Nath Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 March 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, S.M. Sikri, R.S. Bachawat

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 400; (1969) 1 SCC 718; AIR 1969 SC 971; 1970 (1) SCJ 101; [1969] 3 S.C.R. 908

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, s. 47 - Bihar Land Reforms Act, 1950, ss. 3, 4(d) and 14 - Appeal arises from a proceeding u/s. 47, CPC, 1908 - In execution of a mortgage decree, the decree-holders (DH) sought to proceed against Bakasht lands of Judgment Debtor (JD) - Decision of the execution court was affirmed in appeal but reversed in second appeal by High Court - (A) Whether the objection as regards the executability of the decree pleaded by JD is barred by principles of res judicata and (B) Whether mortgage decree has become unexecutable in view of the provisions of the Act? - Held - (A) Courts in India have generally taken the view that an execution petition which has been dismissed for the default of the DH though by the time that petition came to be dismissed, the JD had resisted the execution on one or more grounds, does not bar the further execution of the decree in pursuance of fresh execution petition filed in accordance with law - Hence, court agree with the HC that the plea of res judicata advanced by the appellant is unsustainable - Further held, (B) There is no dispute that the property mortgaged was an Estate within the meaning of s. 2(1) and the notification issued u/s. 3 covered the entirety of the Estate - But what was urged on behalf of the appellants is that what had vested in the State was the non-bakasht lands as well as the proprietory interest in the Bakasht lands and hence the Bakasht lands do not have the protection of s. 4(d); Consequently it is not necessary for them to exclusively proceed u/s. 14 - Appeal dismissed.
