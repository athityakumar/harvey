New Savan Sugar and Gur Refining Company Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax, Calcutta

Supreme Court of India

19 February 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, A.N. Grover, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 405; (1969) 1 SCC 621; AIR 1969 SC 1062; [1969] 74 ITR 7; [1969] 3 S.C.R. 761; 1969 UJ 199

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 10, 66(1) and 12(3) - Excess Profits Tax Act, 1940 - Finance Act, 1955 - Whether on facts and in circumstances of case, income of assessee-company was liable to be assessed u/s. 12 of Indian Income Tax Act, 1922 and not u/s. 10 of said Act? - Held, income was liable to be assessed u/s. 12 and that no additional depreciation and development rebate could be allowed - S. 12 was to following effect - Tax shall be payable by an assessee under head 'Income from other sources' in respect of income, profits and gains of every kind which may be included in his total income - Lease as contemplated in indenture D/- 15-03-1948 was a lease of a commercial asset, and, therefore, income arising from lease should be assessed u/s. 10(1) of Act and not u/s. 12(1) - When an assessee does not carry on business at all, s. 10 cannot be applicable and income that he receives cannot bear character of profits of business - A part of assets did not cease to be commercial assets of that business merely because it was temporarily put to a different use or let out to another and accordingly income from assets would be profits of business irrespective of manner in which assets were exploited by company - Appeal dismissed.
