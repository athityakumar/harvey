 Baijnath Kedia v State Of Bihar & Ors

Supreme Court of India

28 August 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.M. Shelat, Vishishtha Bhargava, K.S. Hegde, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 126; (1969) 3 SCC 838; AIR 1970 SC 1436; [1970] 2 S.C.R. 100

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Indian Independence Act, 1947

Summary: Constitution, sch. 7, List 2, entry 23 , art. 254, sch.7, List 1, entry 54 - Bihar Land Reforms Act, 1950, s. 10(2), Second proviso - Bihar Minor Mineral Concession Rules, 1964, r. 20(2) - Bihar Land Reforms Act, 1950 - (A) Constitution - Mines and minerals - Regulation and development - Minor minerals - Declaration by Parliament in statute under entry 54 of Union list that control should vest in Central Govt. - Effect - (B) Second proviso to s.10 (2) of 1950 Act after 1957 Amendment Act - Validity - (E) Validity of r. 20(2) of 1964 Rules - Held, State Legislature not competent to enact legislation under entry 23 of State list trenching upon field disclosed in the declaration - Amendment is without jurisdiction and ultra vires the Constitution - Second sub-rule ineffective - .Appeal Allowed
