Praga Tools Corporation v Shri C. A. Imanual and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 February 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Shelat, Vishishtha Bhargava

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 404; (1969) 1 SCC 585; AIR 1969 SC 1306; [1969] 39 Comp Cas 889; 1969 (19) FLR 140; 1969 (2) LLJ 749; 1970 (1) MLJ(SC) 29; [1969] 3 S.C.R. 773; 1969 (36) SLR 791

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Companies Act, 1913

Summary: Constitution, art. 226 - Companies Act, 1956), ss. 2(18) and 617 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 2 - Mandamus - Non statutory company - Agreement among Union and Company - There must be legal right to performance of legal duty - (A) Writ against such company which is not having any staturory or public duty etc., restrain company from enforcing agreement an also to declare the agrement as illegal - (B) Shares - Provision of ss. 2(18), 617 of Companies Act and s. 2 of Industrial Dispute Act - Shares held by govt. and private individuals - Union Govt. being largest shareholder nominated company's directors - Whether the company is a Govt. Corporation running under authority of Union Govt. - Writ cannot issue to restrain company from enforcing agreement - Court cannot also grant declaration about illegality of agreement - The Company held not to be a govt. corporation or an industry run by or under the authority of the Union Govt. - Appeal allowed.
