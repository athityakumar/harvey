Yuvraj Digvijay Sinhji v Yuvrani Pratap Kumari

Supreme Court of India

02 May 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

C.A. Vaidyialingam, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 519; (1969) 2 SCC 279; AIR 1970 SC 137; [1970] 1 S.C.R. 559

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Summary: Hindu Marriage Act, 1955, s. 12(1)(a) - Divorce Act, 1869, s. 19(1) - Constitution, art. 136 -Nullity of marriage for want of consumation of marriage due to alleged impotency - (A) Consummation of marriage - Practical impossibility - Burden of proof - (B) Constitution, art. 136 - Concurrent findings of fact based on proper consideration of entire evidence - Supreme Court do not interference with - Finding courts below that, petitioner's case about respondent's impotency could not be believed and that respondent's evidence that she had always been ready and willing to allow her husband to consummate marriage should be believed - Held, petitioner must prove that mental or physical condition of respondent from time of marriage till institution of proceedings was such as to make consummation of marriage a practical impossibility - SC refused to interfere and annul the marriage u/s.12(1)(a) Hindu Marriage Act - Appeal Dismissed.
