Padrauna Rajkrishna Sugar Works Limited and Others v Land Reforms Commissioner, U.P. and Others

Supreme Court of India

31 January 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, A.N. Grover, V. Ramaswami

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 261; (1969) 1 SCC 485; AIR 1969 SC 897; [1970] 75 ITR 358; [1969] 3 S.C.R. 468; 1969 UJ 98

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Artificial Juridical Person

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Land & property - Practice & Procedure - Uttar Pradesh Sugar Factories Control Act, 1938 - Uttar Pradesh Zamindari Abolition And Land Reforms Act, 1951, ss. 279, 285H and 286 - Indian Income-tax Act, 1922 - Whether sale in question suffered from material irregularity and was liable to be set aside for that reason? - S. 286(2) does not require that Collector must resort, firstly, to processes in cls. (a) to (e) before he resorts to cl.(f), of s. 279 - Restrictions on power of Collector operate only when land revenue is in arrears - Restrictions, if any, to recover dues under other statutes, as arrears of land revenue arise from statute which is source of liability - To hold otherwise is to amend substantive provisions of Acts under which liability for money due is recoverable as land revenue - Held, Collector is entitled to exercise all powers of a Civil Court for purpose of recovery of an amount due under a decree under Code of Civil Procedure - Sale is not proved to be vitiated on ground of any material irregularity or mistake in publishing or conducting it, and it is therefore not liable to be set aside - Appeal dismissed.
