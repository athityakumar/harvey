Jagad Bandhu Chatterjee v Nilima Rani and Others

Supreme Court of India

17 October 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 456; (1969) 3 SCC 445; [1970] 2 S.C.R. 925

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - Bengal Tenancy Act 1885, s. 26F -The Indian Contract Act, s. 63 - Whether waiver could be brought about only, by a contract and since no consideration had passed, it could not be said that there had been any waiver in present case? - Held, waiver could not be proved by estoppel - Waiver is contractual, and may constitute a cause of action it is an agreement to release or not to assert a right (Dawson's Bank Limited v Nippon Menkwa Kabushiki Kaisha) - Waiver of existing obligations does not appear to require presence of detriment in order to make it effective - Neither consideration nor an agreement would be necessary to constitute waiver - Appeal dismissed.
