Hindustan Steel Ltd. v State Of Orissa

Supreme Court of India

04 August 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, A.N. Grover, V. Ramaswami

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 131; (1969) 2 SCC 627; AIR 1970 SC 253; 1978 (2) E.L.T. 159; [1972] 83 ITR 26; [1970] 1 S.C.R. 753; 1970 (25) STC 211

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Keywords: Statement Of Case, Companies Act, 1913

Summary: Sales Tax - Orissa Sales Tax Act, 1947, ss. 2(g), 2(c), 9(1), 12(5) and 25(1)(a) - Penalty - Between 1954 and 1959, Appellant-Company was erecting factory buildings for the steel plant, residential buildings for its employees and ancillary works such as roads, water supply, drainage - Some constructions were done departmentally and the rest through contractors - Appellant supplied bricks, coal, cement, steel etc. for consideration to the contractors and adjusted the value of the goods supplied at the rates specified in the tender - In proceedings for assessment of tax, the Sales Tax Officer held that the appellant was a dealer in building material, and had sold the material to contractors and was on that account liable to pay tax at the appropriate rates under the Orissa Sales Tax Act - Sales Tax Officer also imposed penalty on the appellant for failure to register itself as a dealer - Appellate Assistant Commissioner confirmed the order of the Sales Tax Officer - On appeal, Tribunal agreed with the tax authorities and held that the appellant was liable to pay tax on its turnover from bricks, cement and steel supplied to the contractors - Tribunal however substantially reduced the penalty imposed upon the appellant - HC confirmed the order of the Tribunal - Hence, present appeal - (A) Whether appellant can be held to be a 'dealer' within the meaning of s. 2(c) of the Orissa Sales Tax Act, 1947? - Held, dealer' means any person who executes any contract or carries on the business of selling or supplying goods in Orissa whether for commission, remuneration or otherwise and includes any firm or Hindu Joint family, and any society, club or association which sells or supplies goods to its members - In the present case, it is the Stores Department of the appellant as a whole which deals with the purchase, storage and sale of all the goods required both for acquisition and issue of materials to be used for the construction and operation work of the appellant - There is nothing in the evidence to show that the appellant had at any time even contemplated the allocation of the total expenditure incurred for the maintenance of its Stores Department between the expenditure incurred in respect of the goods namely bricks, cement, steel etc. and other goods - If such allocation was not even contemplated, it will be unreasonable to say that when these goods were sold to the building contractors at the prices mentioned above, the intention of the appellant was merely to utilise the difference in price to meet the overhead charges in respect of these articles and that there was no profit making motive - Hence, appellant is not a dealer within the meaning of s. 2(c) of the Orissa Sales Tax Act, 1947 - (B) Whether the imposition of penalty for failure to register as a 'dealer' was justified? - Held, under ss. 9(1) and 25(1)(a) of the 1947 Act a penalty may be imposed for failure to register as a dealer - But the discretion to impose a penalty must be exercised judicially - A penalty will ordinarily be imposed in the cases where the party acts deliberately in defiance of law, or is guilty of contumacious or dishonest conduct, or acts in conscious disregard of its obligation; but not, in cases where there is a technical or; venial breach of the provisions of the Act or where the breach flows from a bona fide belief that the offender is not liable to act in the manner prescribed by the statute - In the present case, those in charge of the affairs of the appellant, in failing to. register it as a dealer, acted in the honest and genuine belief that the appellant was not a dealer; and therefore, assuming the appellant to be a dealer' no case for imposing penalty was made out - Appeal disposed of.
