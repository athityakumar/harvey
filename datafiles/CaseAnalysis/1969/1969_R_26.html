Sidramappa v Rajashetty and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 December 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 202; (1970) 1 SCC 186; AIR 1970 SC 1059; [1970] 3 S.C.R. 319

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O.2 r. 2 - Suit for possession of lands - Maintainability - Plaintiff filed suit for declaration in respect of lands in Schedule I of plaint - Trial Court dismissed suit on the ground that relief in question was barred by O. 2 r. 2 of CPC and decreed suit for possession of lands mentioned in Schedule II except items 3 and 9 - Trial Court also decreed plaintiff's claim in respect of cash amount mentioned in plaint - On appeals, HC affirmed decision of Trial Court - Hence instant appeal - Whether order of HC and Trial Court were right in their conclusions that plaintiff's claim in respect of lands mentioned in Schedule I of plaint was barred by O. 2 r. 2 of CPC - Held, in instant case, SC was in opinion that Trial Court and HC erred in holding that plaintiff's suit in respect of lands, mentioned in plaint Schedule I was barred by O. 2 r. 2 of CPC - Plaintiff remedy was to file a suit for possession of concerned properties on basis of his title - HC and Trial Court proceeded on erroneous basis that former suit was a suit for a declaration of plaintiff's title to the lands mentioned in Schedule I of plaint - Requirement of O. 2 r. 2 of CPC was that every suit should include whole of the claim which the plaintiff was entitled to make in respect of a cause of action - It could not be Said that cause of action on which suit was brought was the same as that in previous suit - Trial court's decree was modified by including lands mentioned in Schedule I of the plaint - Appeal allowed.
