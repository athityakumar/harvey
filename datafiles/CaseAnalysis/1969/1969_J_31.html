Jullunder Rubber Goods Manufacturers' Association v Union of India and another

Supreme Court of India

25 August 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Grover, J.C. Shah, V. Ramaswami

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 2; (1969) 2 SCC 644; AIR 1970 SC 1589; 1999 (110) E.L.T. 339; [1970] 2 S.C.R. 68

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - Rubber Act, 1947, s. 12(2) - Whether the provision empowering collection of excise duty from users rather than producers was beyond the legislative competence of the Parliament? - Whether the delegation in the provision is excessive and hence unconstitutional? - Method of collection of a tax is separate from its incidence and a collection of excise from user rather than producer will not invalidate the excise duty - So long as the law has provided the method by which the local body can be controlled and there is a provision to see that reasonable rates are fixed, it can be said that there is guidance in the matter of fixing the rates for local taxation - Held, excise can be collected from users of rubber in manufacturing units - The Legislature has not abdicated its functions u/s. 12(2) since it required legislative review of the rules as well - Appeals dismissed.
