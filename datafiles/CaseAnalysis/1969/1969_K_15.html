Kanaiyalal Maneklal Chinai and Another v State of Gujarat and Others

Supreme Court of India

17 October 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 380; (1969) 3 SCC 456; AIR 1970 SC 1188; [1970] 2 S.C.R. 908

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894. ss. 3(4), 4 and 6 - Commissioners of Divisions Act, 1958, ss. 3(3) and 3(4) - (A) Whether Commissioners of Divisions Act 8 of 1958 pursuant to which Commrs. of Divisions were vested with authority to discharge statutory functions vested in State Commr. was ultra vires legislature? - Held, powers conferred by s. 3(4) on State Govts. are not unguided and that State Legislature has by enacting s. 3(4) not abdicated its powers in favour a executive, for it has laid down legislative policy and has left it to State Govt. to reorganise administration, consequent on setting up of Commrs. Divisions - Challenge to vires of Commissioners of Divisions Act 8 of 1958 must fail - (B) Whether in any event Commr., Baroda Division, State of Gujarat was incompetent to issue notification u/s. 6 without issuing a fresh notification u/s. 4? - Held, Authority of Commr. of State of Gujarat to issue notification u/s. 6 not being open to challenge - There is nothing in Land Acquisition Act or Commissioners of Divisions Act, 1958 which requires that to invest notification u/s. 6 with validity - Commr. of State of Gujarat had in first instance to issue a notification u/s. 4 of Act declaring that land was needed or was likely to be needed for any public purpose - (C) Whether 'instrumentality' which was to carry out purpose not having been set out in notifications u/ss. 4 and 6 notifications were illegal and on that account unenforceable? - Held, Land Acquisition Act, 1894 does not provide that instrumentality which is to carry out purpose must be set out in notifications u/ss. 4 and 6 of Act - If public purpose for which land is notified for acquisition is to be executed through an instrumentality other than State Govt. failure to specifically mention 'the instrumentality' in notifications renders notification invalid - Further held, failure to specify instrumentality which is to execute public purpose by state Govt. does not affect validity of notification either u/s. 4 or u/s. 6 of Land Acquisition Act - (D) Whether Commr., Baroda Division, in issuing notification u/s. 6 did not apply his mind to evidence before him and on that account notification was liable to be struck down? - Held, there is no substance in argument that Commr., Baroda Division, did not apply his mind in issuing notification u/s. 6 - Land notified for acquisition u/s. 4 was 3428 square yards 3 square feet out of Survey No. 348-B, and 494 square yards 5 square feet out of Survey No. 349 - Area of land notified u/s. 6 was stated to be 3562 square yards out of Survey No. 348 B and 387 square yards out of Survey No. 349 - Even though area of land out of Survey No. 348-B exceeded area originally mentioned in notification u/s. 4, Commr. stated in impugned notification that' remaining area of said lands notified u/s. 4 is hereby abandoned - Appeal dismissed.
