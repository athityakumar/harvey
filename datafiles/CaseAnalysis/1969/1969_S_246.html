Amritsagar Gupta and Others v Sudesh Beharilal and Others

Supreme Court of India

13 March 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, S.M. Sikri, R.S. Bachawat

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 545; (1969) 1 SCC 810; AIR 1970 SC 5; 1969 (5) DLT 639; 1969 (71) PLR 371; [1969] 3 S.C.R. 1002

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Whether suit is barred by res judicata? - Joint family property - Suit for partition - Abuse of judicial process - Held, properties that were in dispute in former suit as well as in present suit are identical properties - It is not necessary in order that a decree against manager may operate as res judicata against coparceners who were not parties to suit that plaint or written statement should state in express terms that he is suing as manager or is being sued as a manager, it is sufficient if manager was in fact suing or being sued as representing whole family - It is not necessary, where manager is plaintiff, that plaint should state in distinct terms that he is suing as manager or where he is defendant that he is being sued as manager - A Karta can represent family effectively in a proceeding though he is not named as such - Appeal allowed.
