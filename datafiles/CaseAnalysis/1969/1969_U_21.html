Travancore Rayons Limited v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

28 October 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 580; (1969) 3 SCC 868; AIR 1971 SC 862; 1978 (2) E.L.T. 378; [1970] 3 S.C.R. 40

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944, s. 36 - Central Excise Rules, 1944, r. 55 - Constitution - Disclosure of reasons in support of order - Whether Central Govt. is obliged to give reasons while dismissing petition u/ r. 55? - Party aggrieved in a proceeding before High Court or Supreme Court should have opportunity to demonstrate that reasons which persuaded authority to reject his case were erroneous - Obligation to record reasons operates as a deterrent against possible arbitrary action by executive authority invested with judicial power - Held, order passed by Central Govt. set aside - Case is remanded to Central Govt. - Appeal allowed.
