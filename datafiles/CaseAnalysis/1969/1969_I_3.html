Mackinnon Mackenzie and Company Private Limited v Ibrahim Mahommed Issak

Supreme Court of India

14 August 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, J.C. Shah, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 14; (1969) 2 SCC 607; 1969 ACJ 422; AIR 1970 SC 1906; 1969 (19) FLR 281; 1970 LabIC 1413; 1970 (1) LLJ 16; [1970] 1 S.C.R. 869

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: University

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Workmen's Compensation Act, 1923 s.3 - Missing seaman - Compensation for death - Course of employment - Validity of - Missing seaman was employed as deck-hand, seaman of category II on ship ss - 'Dwarka' which was owned by British India Steam Navigation Company Limited in which appellant was Agent - Respondent filed application u/s. 3 of the Act for compensation for death of his son, missing seaman, occurred personal injury caused by accident arising out of and in course of his employment - Additional Commissioner held that there was no evidence to show that seaman was dead and there was no evidence to justify inference that death of missing seaman was caused by accident which arose out of employment - Respondent filed appeal before HC - Appeal was allowed reversing judgment of Additional Commissioner and granted application for compensation and death of seaman could be held on account of accident which arose out of his employment - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether accident arose in course of employment and it arose out of employment within meaning of s. 3 of the Act and order of HC was justified - Held, official log book of ship showed that when ship was in Persian Gulf, respondent was seen near bridge of ship - Respondent was sent back but later on he was seen on Tween Deck when he told seaman on duty that he was going to bed - After that respondent was found missing and search was undertaken - Dead body was not found either on that day or later on - However, evidence did not show that it was stormy night - Commissioner made local inspection of ship and saw position of bridge and deck and found that there was bulwark more than 31/2 feet - Nobody saw missing seaman at so-called place of accident - Further, Additional Commissioner held that there was no material for holding that death of seaman took place on account of accident which arose out of his employment - Thus, Additional Commissioner did not commit any error of law in finding and HC was not justified in reversing it - Therefore, judgment of HC was set be aside - Appeal allowed.
