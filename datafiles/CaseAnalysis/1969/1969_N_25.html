Netherlands Steam Navigation Company Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax, West Bengal

Supreme Court of India

14 March 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, V. Ramaswami

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 189; (1969) 2 SCC 84; AIR 1969 SC 1262; [1969] 74 ITR 72; [1970] 1 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s.10 - Taxable profits - Additional depreciation - Taxable income - Non-resident Company - Netherlands Steam Navigation Company Ltd. assessee was a Non-resident Company engaged in shipping business - For assessment years 1952-53 to 1956-57 assessee filed its return of income for relevant accounting years disclosing taxable income computed on basis of its annual turnover in its round voyages to and from Indian Ports - Assessee did not furnish particulars of its world income - While computing profits of assessee in India Income-tax Officer allowed normal depreciation and other trade allowances admissible under Indian Income-tax Act,1922 and did not allowed initial depreciation and additional depreciation of ships of assessee in any of assessment years because ships acquired by assessee were not introduced into Indian business in years which they were newly acquired and orders of assessments were confirmed by Appellate Assistant Commissioner - In appeal to Income tax Appellate Tribunal assessee claimed Additional depreciation on four ships - Assessee and Commissioner were agreed taxable income of assessee had to be determined by application of second method and Tribunal also observed Commissioner and assessee agreed formula adopted by Income-tax Officer was correct method of assessment - Whether assessee Company was entitled to additional depreciation for four ships - Held, HC answered question in negative Clause - Assessee is a non-resident Company maintains a Branch Office in Calcutta - while computing profits or gains of business carried on by an assessee normal depreciation u/s.10(2)(vi) and additional depreciation u/s.10(2)(vi-a) are undoubtedly admissible in conditions and to extent allowed under two clauses - Profits and gains of business of a non-resident received or deemed to be received in taxable territories by or on behalf of assessee are taxable under Indian Income Tax Act 1922 and profits and gains of business which accrue are deemed to accrue in taxable territories are also taxable under Act but profits and gains which accrue or arise or are deemed to accrue or arise to a non-resident without taxable territories are not taxable under Act - Additional depreciation was a statutory allowance in determination of taxable profit u/s10 of Act and in case of a non-resident where actual income cannot be determined - Source of income of assessee charged to tax was business and it was not income from any other source - Additional depreciation is not admissible as an allowance in computation of taxable income by special formula adopted by Income tax Officer - Appeal dismissed.
