Hoogly Trust (Private) Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax, West Bengal and Andaman and Nicob

Supreme Court of India

04 February 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Grover, J.C. Shah, Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 293; (1969) 1 SCC 535; AIR 1969 SC 946; [1969] 73 ITR 685; [1969] 2 S.C.R. 557

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Vocation

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Finance Act, 1955 - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 24(1)

- Assessment - Loss of profits - Set off - Carry forward - Assessee/ Private limited company owned shares and securities - In assessment losses were determined for purposes of s.24(2) of 1922 Act - Income-tax Officer(I.T.O.) refused to allow carry forward losses and their set off against business profits of years on ground that losses determined in preceding years arose out of business was different from other business - Appellate Assistant Commissioner agreed with conclusion of ITO - Hence instant appeal - Whether assessee entitled to amount of loss set off against his income - Held, s. 24(1) of Act provided that where any assessee sustained loss of profits or gains u/s.6, was entitled to amount of loss set off against his income - Businesses were same sought to be substantiated on ground that only one set of accounts maintained that both businesses were carried on in same premises - Appeals allowed.
