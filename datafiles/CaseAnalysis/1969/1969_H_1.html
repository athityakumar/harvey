Swaran Lata Ghosh v H. K. Banerjee and Another

Supreme Court of India

12 March 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 196; (1969) 1 SCC 709; AIR 1969 SC 1167; [1969] 3 S.C.R. 976

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Suit for recovery of money - Decreed - Justifiability - 'G' was practicing as an attorney in HC of Calcutta died on August 1950 - Respondent No. 1 filed suit before Trial Court claiming that he had deposited some money with 'G' for 'specific purpose of investing amount' and later agreed to pay interest and to repay same or any portion thereof when demanded - 'G' paid diverse sums of money as interest and balance amount with interest was due thereon were repayable by appellants/defendants to plaintiff - Trial Court passed order in favor of plaintiff in said order a decree was drawn up - Appellant appealed before HC - HC disposed of appeal in favor of plaintiff - Hence instant appeal - Whether order of decree passed by HC was justified - Held, extracts however do not evidence agreement under which money passed from plaintiff to 'G' - Plaintiff had to prove not only that money passed from him to 'G' he had to prove, that money passed under agreement pleaded by him - Oral testimony of plaintiff had to be examined in context of several weighty circumstances - In reaching conclusion SC had to consider probabilities and circumstances in which plaintiff alleged that he had deposited two sums of money with 'G' - It was essentially case in which there should have been full record of reasons which persuaded Trial Court to reach conclusion he did - A mere order directing payment of money, not supported by reasons, does not do duty for judgment according to law - That there had been no real trial of defendants' case - It was very unfortunate state of affairs that eighteen years after date on which suit was instituted, SC remanded suit for trial according to law, as there was no other satisfactory alternative - Decree passed by HC was set aside - It would be open to Judge who tries suit to proceed on evidence already on record - If parties desired to lead any additional evidence, he would give them opportunity in that behalf - If Judge was of opinion that witnesses should be examined over again before him, he might adopt that course - Observations made by us in course of this judgment were not intended to express any opinion by SC on merits of dispute - Order accordingly.
