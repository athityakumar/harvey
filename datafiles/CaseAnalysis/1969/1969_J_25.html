 Coffee Board, Bangalore v Joint Commercial Tax Officer, Madras & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

29 October 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, S.M. Sikri, G.K. Mitter, P. Jaganmohan Reddy, A.N. Ray

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 225; (1969) 3 SCC 349; AIR 1971 SC 870; [1970] 3 S.C.R. 147; 1970 (25) STC 528; 1971 TAXLR 391

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Sales Tax

Keywords: Intermediary, Coffee Act, 1942, Sale In The Course Of Export, Transfer Of Documents Of Title

Summary: Constitution, arts. 31(1) and 32 - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956, s. 5(1) - (A) Right to move SC for enforcement of fundamental right - (1) Conditions for - (2) Demand of tax not backed by valid law - Is a threat to property - Gives right to move Supreme Court by petition u/art. 32 - (B) Demand of tax from corporation not leviable under a valid law - Breach of fundamental right guaranteed u/art. 31(1) is involved - (C) The art. 286(1)(b) of Constitution r/w s. 5(1) of CST Act, 1956 - Restrictions as to imposition of tax on sale or purchase of goods - Sale in the course of export - Essentials - Tests to determine it - When first sale of coffee conducted by Coffee Board to its registered exporter within Madras State - Amnd second sale by registered exporter to foreign buyer having no connection with first sale by Coffee Board - To foreign buyer having no connection with first sale by Coffee Board - (C) Word 'Occasions' does not necessarily mean to cause or to be the immediate cause as it is bearing two meanings - Context in which it is used must determine appropriate meaning - Held, where action is taken under an ultra vires statute, or where, although the statute is intra vires, the action is without jurisdiction or the principles of natural justice are violated, a right to move SC u/art. 32 for enforcement of fundamental right exists; Petitioner will be limited only to establishing fundamental right and existence of other remedies no bar - Held, Corporation being a person within art. 31(1) can maintain a petition u/art. 32 -- Sale by Coffee Board held not in course of export within art. 286(1)(b) r/w s. 5(1) of CST Act and was liable to sales tax under the Madras Act - So construed word 'occasions' must be given wider meaning viz., to bring about especially in an incidental or subsidiary manner - Petition Dismissed.
