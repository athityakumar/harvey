Sheo Sadan Singh v Mohan Lal Gautam

Supreme Court of India

24 January 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, S.M. Sikri, R.S. Bachawat

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 567; (1969) 1 SCC 408; AIR 1969 SC 1024; [1969] 3 S.C.R. 417

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Election - Representation of the People Act, 1951, s. 116-A - Validity of election - (A) Election petition - Not properly presented - Maintainability - Held, High Court held that petition presented by the advocate's clerk in the immediate presence of petitioner to the registry - So, in substance, it was presented by the petitioner himself - Requirement of law fully satisfied - (B) Abatement of Election Petition - Contended that it would have become infructuous in view of the dissolution of Assembly - Held, election petitions solely regulated by statutory provisions - Unless some statutory provisions directly or impliedly prescribed the abatement, the election petition would never become abated on the reason of dissolution of assembly - To decide the question of abatement, Supreme Court cannot travel outside the provisions contained in Chap. IV of Part VI - No other provisions in the Act provided for dropping the election petition - No reliance can be placed on CPC for abatement or withdrawal of election petition - HC rightly held that election petition has not been abated - (C)Corrupt practice - Publication of false statement - Conviction of - Held, reliability of PW 16, a signatory to the pamphlet, is unreliable - PW 16 can be a stooge, he walked over to the appellant side from the side of respondent - No evidence which shows voucher produced is relating to the printing of pamphlets in question - HC was rightly rejected the ocular evidence of large number of witness as unreliable - (D) Finding of fact by HC - Acceptability - Held, Supreme Court accepts the finding of fact by the HC - Election petitions are tried by the experienced Judges of HC - They had the benefit of observing witness - Hence, appreciation of evidence by HC is entitled to great weight - No reason for departing that rule - (E) Corrupt practice - Allegation of using vehicles to carry electorates - Held, evidences of the PW 45, worker who transported electorates, is wholly false - Evidence of PW 33, who witnessed a tractor carrying the voters, is extremely vague - Owner of the vehicle has not been examined - Agreeing with the HC for not relying the evidences - (F) Corrupt practice - Allegation of election expenses exceeded the prescribed limit - Held, evidence relating to expenses incurred for hiring vehicles, already rejected in view of the earlier findings - Trial Court did not believe the ocular evidences of large number of witness purport to prove the feeding expenses of workers - It is unworthy - Documentary evidences are unreliable - Appeal dismissed.
