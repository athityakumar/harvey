Union of India v A. V. Narasimhalu

Supreme Court of India

01 September 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 4; (1969) 2 SCC 658; AIR 1970 SC 145; 1983 (13) E.L.T. 1534; 1970 (2) MLJ(SC) 34; [1970] 2 S.C.R. 145

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Customs - Sea Customs Act, 1878, ss. 188 and 191 - Whether Civil Court has jurisdiction to entertain a suit challenging validity of imposition of duty of customs? - In case of a right which depends upon a statute, jurisdiction of Civil Court to grant relief may by express provision or by clear implication of statute be excluded - Where a statute re-enacts a right or a liability existing at common law, and statute provides a special form of remedy, exclusion of jurisdiction of Civil Court to grant relief in absence of an express provision, will not be readily inferred - However, where a statute creates a new right or liability and it provides a complete machinery for obtaining redress against erroneous exercise of authority, jurisdiction of Civil Court to grant relief is barred - Liability to pay duty of customs is not a common law liability - It arises by virtue of Sea Customs Act - Jurisdiction of Civil Court is by clear implication of statute excluded - However, finality which may be declared by statute qua certain liability either by express exclusion of jurisdiction of Civil Court or by clear implication does not affect jurisdiction of High Court to issue high prerogative writs - Held, jurisdiction of Civil Court to entertain a suit challenging validity of imposition of duty of customs being excluded, respondent suit must fail - Appeal allowed.
