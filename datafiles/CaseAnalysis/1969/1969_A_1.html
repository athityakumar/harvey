Commissioner Of Income-Tax, Andhra Pradesh,Hyderabad v A. Dharma Reddy, Morthad

Supreme Court of India

19 February 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Grover, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 284; (1969) 1 SCC 580; AIR 1969 SC 940; [1969] 73 ITR 751; 1969 (2) MLJ(SC) 95; [1969] 3 S.C.R. 782

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Vocation

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Finance Act, 1955 - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 24(2)(ii) - Partnership - Assessment - Loss - Dissolution - Assessee was an individual whose only sources of income were his shares in several partnership concerns - Apart from firms which carried on other businesses there were two firms which carried on business in Bidi leaves - First was styled as A - Second firm was called B - First partnership was dissolved but second one continued during A/y 1956-57 - During A/Y 1955-56 assessee sustained a loss of Rs. 30,255 in first firm - During A/y 1956-57 assessee's profit in second firm was estimated at Rs. 11,853 and his total taxable income was assessed at Rs. 28,758 for that A/Y - Assessee claimed that loss sustained by him in previous year viz., A/y 1955-56 should be carried forward and set off against his profit in subsequent year 1956-57 u/s. 24(2)(ii) of 1922Act - Income tax Officer rejected claim - Appellate Assistant Commissioner in appeal considered constitution of two firms - First consisted of two partners in which originally loss had occurred and which had ceased to exist in relevant assessment year - Second firm against whose income loss was sought to be set off consisted of four partners - Both firms had filed separate returns and were assessed separately for A/y 1955-56 - Assessee took matter to Appellate Tribunal which upheld contention of assessee that same business continued during A/y - HC disposed of matter - Hence instant appeal - Whether assessee was entitled under provisions of s. 24(2) of Act to set off his share of unabsorbed loss from dissolved firm - Held, computation of a partner's share in firm's profits was dealt with by s.16(1)(b) of 1922 Act - Proviso laid down that if his share was computed as a loss such loss may be set off or carried forward and set off in accordance with provision of s. 24 of 1922 Act - There was a proviso which said that if such share of any partner was a loss it should be set off against his other income or carried forward and set off in accordance with provisions of s. 24 of 1922 Act - HC was right that when profits of a registered firm were ascertained assessee for purpose of paying tax was not registered firm but each Partner of that firm - s. 24(2)(ii) did not change by reason of change in persons who carried on that business since it continued to be carried on by same individual - Appeal dismissed.
