Commissioner of Income Tax (Central), Calcutta v Bikaner Trading Company Limited

Supreme Court of India

12 February 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Grover, K.S. Hegde, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 326; (1969) 3 SCC 510; [1970] 78 ITR 12

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act,1922,s.16(2) - Total income - Dividends - Assessment - Payment - For a/y 1956-57 an amount of Rs.1,843 was assessed as income on account of dividends in hands of company no income was assessed on account of dividends - Income Tax Officer found that dividends amounting to Rs.40,562 had been declared and dividends amounting to Rs.5,000 - Similarly, dividends amounting to Rs. 7, 500 net had been declared by the M Ltd in favour of the assessee - Two companies had declared dividends of Rs. 20,281 and Rs. 5, 000 during year - Whether dividends were to be included during relevant A/Y and whether mere declaration of dividends amounted to a payment u/s.16(2) of 1922 Act - Held, s.16(2) of 1922 Act, prescribes a special rule relating to determination of previous year in which a dividend was liable to be included in total income of assessee - Purpose of inclusion in total income of an assessee any dividend shall be deemed to be income of previous year in which it was paid - S.16(2) of Act, when the company discharged its liability and made amount of dividend unconditionally available to member entitled - Dividend warrants were delivered to assessee - Assessee was liable to pay Income Tax on dividends received by it - Amount was taxable because it was received in year of account lay upon department and in the absence of the information were not liable to be taxed - Appeals disposed of.
