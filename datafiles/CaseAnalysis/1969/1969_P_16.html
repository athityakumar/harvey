State Of Gujarat v Patel Raghav Natha & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

21 April 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri, R.S. Bachawat, K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 176; (1969) 2 SCC 187; AIR 1969 SC 1297; 1969 (10) G.L.R. 992; [1970] 1 S.C.R. 335

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Bombay Provincial Municipal Corporation Act, 1949

Summary: Municipalities & Local Governments - Land Revenue Rules, 1921, rr. 81, 82, 82 A, 82 AA, 84 and 85 - Bombay Provincial Municipal Corporation Act, 1949 - Bombay Land Revenue Code, 1879, ss. 48, 65 and 211 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Respondent at an auction effected by State, acquired agricultural land - Respondent applied to Collector for permission to convert this land to non-agricultural use - Granted permission - Pursuant to this order a sanad was issued by Collector - Municipal Committee had objected to grant of permission before Collector when a sketch of land was sent to Municipality - Collector had overruled these objections - Order of Collector set aside by Commr. - Quashed by High Court - Whether Commr. had jurisdiction to pass order ? - Application is made u/s. 65 of Bombay Land Revenue Code and Collector either grants or refuses permission applied for - Collector having given permission u/s. 65 he can prescribe conditions u/s. 67 of Code - S. 65 indicates length of reasonable time within which Commr. must act u/s. 211 - U/s. 65 if Collector does not inform applicant of his decision on application within a period of three months permission applied for shall be deemed to have been granted - Reading s. 211 and s. 65 together Commr. must exercise his revisional powers within a few months of order of Collector - This is reasonable time - Held, Commr. set aside order of Collector more than a year after order - Thus this order was passed too late - That order of Commr. should be quashed on ground that he did not give any reasons for his conclusions - Further held, Commr. should not have gone into question of title - Appeal dismissed.
