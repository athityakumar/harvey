Md. Hanif v State of Assam

Supreme Court of India

03 September 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Ramaswami

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 352; (1969) 2 SCC 782; [1970] 2 S.C.R. 197

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Constitution - Land & Property - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Land was leased out for 99 years to British Govt. - Right and title in land sold to appellants is successor - Appellants rented land to Govt. of Assam and offices of Electricity Board - Cl. V of lease provided that if site, or any part of it, is required for public purposes it may be resumed by Govt. - Deputy Commr. served a notice of resumption on appellant purporting to exercise right of Govt. u/cl. V of lease - Writ petition filed challenging validity of order of resumption - High Court dismissed writ petition holding that rights and obligations springing from contract of lease should be decided by ordinary civil courts and it is not proper for HC to exercise its power u/art. 226 of Constitution, 1950 - Whether writ petition u/art. 226 of Constitution, 1950 challenging validity of order of resumption maintainable? - Held, it cannot be urged that appellant was merely attempting to enforce a contractual right by taking recourse to machinery provided by art. 226 of Constitution, 1950 - It is true that jurisdiction of HC u/art. 226 is an extraordinary jurisdiction vested in HC not for purpose of declaring private rights of parties but for purpose of ensuring that law of land is implicitly obeyed and that various tribunals and public authorities are kept within limits of their jurisdiction - Jurisdiction of HC u/art. 226 is a supervisory jurisdiction, a jurisdiction meant to supervise work of tribunals and public authorities and to see that they act within limits of their respective jurisdiction - In a proceeding u/art. 226, HC is not concerned merely with determination of private rights of parties and only object of such a proceeding u/art. 226 is to ensure that law of land is implicitly obeyed and that various authorities and tribunals act within limits of their respective jurisdiction - Further held, appellant is not merely attempting to enforce his contractual right but important constitutional issues - Appeal allowed.
