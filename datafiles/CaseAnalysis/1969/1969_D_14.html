Delhi Administration v Chanan Shah

Supreme Court of India

12 February 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Bachawat, S.M. Sikri, K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 408; (1969) 1 SCC 737; AIR 1969 SC 1108; 1969 MLJ 739; [1969] 3 S.C.R. 653; 1969 SLR 217; 1969 UJ 136

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Uttar Pradesh Police Regulations

Summary: Service - Criminal - Punjab Police Rules, 1934, rr. 28 and 38 - Complaint against Assistant Sub-Inspector of Police for receiving illegal gratification - Order of censure was passed by Superintendent of Police - D.I.G u/r. 28 ordered to deal the matter departmentally - D.M was not informed of the former order of S.P and its setting aside by D.I.G - Departmental action taken against the Assistant Sub-Inspector is invalid as there has been no substantial compliance with the provisions of r. 38 - It is for the District Magistrate to decide who should investigate the case - No investigation of any kind was made under his direction - Held, inquiry held by the Superintendent of Police (City) was not authorised by the District Magistrate nor did it receive his approval - Appeal dismissed.
