Lnanak Chand v Shri Chandra Kishore Agarwala And Others

Supreme Court of India

20 May 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri, Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 393; (1969) 3 SCC 802; (1970) SCC (Cr) 127; AIR 1970 SC 446; 1970 CRLJ 522; 1974 (2) ILR(Del) 59; [1970] 1 S.C.R. 565

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Keywords: Hindu Adoptions And Maintenance Act, 1956.

Summary: CrPC, 1898, ss. 488, 488(1) - Hindu Adoptions And Maintenance Act, 1956 - (A) Effect of s. 4(b) of 1956 Act on s. 488 of the Code - (B) Meaning of 'Child' u/s.488(1) - Limitation as regards to maintatinance - (C) Maintenance grant to child - Court consideration the existing situation, such as one of the child was a student of M. Com., and the other was of MBBS Course, at the time of passing order - Held, s. 4(b) does not repeal or affect in any manner the provisions of s. 488 of tht Code - Does not mean a minor son or daughter and the real limitation is contained in expression 'unable to maintain itself' - Court held rightly taken into consideration the existing situation - Appeal dismissed.
