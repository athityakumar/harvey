Ganesh Prasad Dixit v Commissioner of Sales Tax, Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

03 February 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 64; (1969) 1 SCC 492; AIR 1969 SC 1276; 1969 MPLJ 421; 1970 (1) SCJ 68; [1969] 3 S.C.R. 490; 1969 (24) STC 343

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Keywords: Sale Price, Bombay Co-operative Societies Act, 1925, Madhya Pradesh General Sales Tax Rules, 1959, Hyderabad General Sales Tax Act, 1950, Income-tax (Amendment) Act, 1939

Summary: Sales Tax - Income-tax (Amendment) Act, 1939 - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 22(2) - Madhya Pradesh General Sales Tax Act, 1958, s. 18(5) - Madhya Pradesh General Sales Tax Rules, 1959 - Appellants were a firm of building contractors, and were registered under the 1958 Act - Sales Tax Officer served notices under s. 18(5) of the 1958 Act calling upon the appellants to show cause why 'best judgment' assessments should not be made, and by order dt. 30-11-1961, he assessed the appellants to tax and imposed a penalty - Hence, the present appeal - Whether the HC rightly decreed - Held, that the terms of r. 33 of the 1959 Rules, were plainly not mandatory, so the answer given by the HC on the first question should be accepted - Mere buying for personal consumption, i.e. without a profit motive, would not make a person, a dealer within, the meaning of the Act, but a person who consumed, a commodity bought by him in the course of his trade, or used in manufacturing another commodity for sale would be regarded as a dealer, so the answer given by the HC in the second question should also be accepted - Legislature had not made sale the very article bought by a person a condition for treating in as a dealer; the definition merely required that the buying, of the commodity mentioned u/r. 5 (2), of the 1959 Rules, should be in the course of business. i.e. should be for sale or, use with a view to make profit, out of the integrated activity of buying and, disposal - Appeals dismissed.
