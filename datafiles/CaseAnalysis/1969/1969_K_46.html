 Kumari Chitra Ghosh and Another v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

25 April 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Grover, Vishishtha Bhargava, K.S. Hegde, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 525; (1969) 2 SCC 228; AIR 1970 SC 35; [1970] 1 S.C.R. 413

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Constitution, arts. 14, 15, 29 and 133 - CPC, 1908 - Maulana Azad Medical College Delhi Prospectus, r. 4 - Eduation - Admission - Reservation of seats for some categorie - (A) Categories (c) to (h) of r. 4 of prospectus - Constitutionality - (B) Reservation - Nomination by Central Govt. - Some reserved seats fall vacant due to non-compliance with rules - Whether Govt. can be compelled to release these seats to general pool - (C) Contention that nominations were illegal as candidates who had been nominated had not applied in time - Maintainability - Reservations held were not violative of Articles - Government cannot be compelled - Held, contention could not be entertained on two grounds, namely, that it was not raised before the High Court and that the petitioners did not compete for the reserved seats and had no locus standi in the matter of nomination to such seats - Appeal Dismissed.
