State of Andhra Pradesh v I. B. S. Prasada Rao and Others

Supreme Court of India

27 October 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, I.D. Dua

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 451; (1969) 3 SCC 896; (1970) SCC (Cr) 151; AIR 1970 SC 648; 1970 CRLJ 733; 1970 UJ 162

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Circumstantial evidence - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 417 and 467 - Accused employed by bank - Forged signature of superior to issue letter of authority for payment - Bank lodged FIR - One accused arrested and further recoveries made on his admission - Convicted by Trial Court - Acquitted by High Court - (A) Sufficiency of circumstantial evidence for purpose of conviction - Whether a conviction can be based solely on circumstantial evidence? - Before conviction based solely on such evidence can be sustained, it must be such as to be conclusive of guilt of accused and must be incapable of explanation on any hypothesis consistent with innocence of accused - Before an accused can contend that a particular hypothesis to his innocence has remained unexcluded by facts proved against him, court must be satisfied that suggested hypothesis is reasonable and not far-fetched - When deciding question of sufficiency, what court has to consider is total cumulative effect of all proved facts each one of which reinforces conclusion of guilt, and if combined effect of all those facts taken together, is conclusive in establishing guilt of accused, conviction would be justified even though it may be that any one or more of those facts by itself is not decisive - Held, applying principle to present case charges u/ss. 120-B and 420, IPC, 1860 have been established against all four respondents and u/s. 416, IPC, 1860 against respondent No. 3 - (B) Extraordinary jurisdiction of Supreme Court u/art. 136 - When to be exercised - Held, it will be exercised by it only when it finds: (a) substantial and grave injustice has been done, and (b) exceptional and special circumstances exist in case - Judgment of HC in present case is perverse and guilt of respondents has been established beyond all reasonable doubt and facts proved against them are of such a nature that only conclusion which any court would legitimately reach on those fact is that offences charged have been committed by each one of respondents - Appeal allowed.
