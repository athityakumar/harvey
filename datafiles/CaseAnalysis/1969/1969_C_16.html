Executive Committee, U.P. Warehousing Corporation v Chandra Kiran Tyagi

Supreme Court of India

08 September 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

C.A. Vaidyialingam, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 365; (1969) 2 SCC 838; AIR 1970 SC 1244; 1970 (20) FLR 17; 1970 LabIC 1044; 1970 (1) LLJ 32; 1970 (1) SCJ 790; [1970] 2 S.C.R. 250; 1969 SLR 799

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Contract Of Personal Service, Agricultural Produce (Development and Warehousing) Corporations Act, 1956

Summary: Service - Specific Relief Act, 1963, ss. 21, s. cl. B, 21, 21(b) - Reinstatement - Suspension - Validity - Respondent/plaintiff entered service with appellant as Technical Assistant in November 1958 and later was promoted to post of Warehouseman - He was confirmed in 1962 - Certain charges were framed against respondent and pending enquiry into those charges he was placed under suspension - Trial Court held that plaintiff was not entitled to protection under art. 311 of Constitution - But it held that in conducting enquiry EO had not complied with provisions of sub cl. (3) of cl. 16 of Regulations framed by appellant and that there had been violation of rules of natural justice - Trial Court held that order dismissing plaintiff was illegal and opined that in view of s. 21 of the 1877 Act plaintiff was not entitled to relief of for reinstatement with full pay - Aggrieved, appellant challenged decision in appeal - Respondent filed Memorandum of Cross Objections challenging decree of Trial Court declining his relief - Civil Judge, dismissed appeal and allowed Memorandum of Cross Objections filed by respondent - Appellant challenged decrees of both lower Courts before HC - HC had dismissed appeal and agreed with findings recorded by Subordinate Courts - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order passed by HC was justified - Held, in instant case breach had been committed by appellant of regulation 16(3) when passing order of dismissal - As procedure indicated had not been followed Act does not guarantee any statutory status to respondent, nor does it impose any obligation on appellant in such matters - It was not in dispute that, authority who could pass order of dismissal had passed same under those circumstances violation of regulation 16(3), as alleged and established could only result in order of dismissal being held to be wrongful and, in consequence, making appellant liable for damages - But order could not be held to be one which had not terminated service, albeit wrongfully, or which entitled respondent to ignore it and ask for being treated as still in service - Hence, decree of HC as declared that order was null and void and that respondent continued to be in service of appellant was set aside - Appeal allowed.
