Gita Devi Aggarwal v Commissioner of Income Tax, West Bengal and Others

Supreme Court of India

31 July 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, A.N. Grover, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 314; [1970] 76 ITR 496

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Constitution of India, 1950 - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s.33B - Income - Assessment - Opportunity - Jurisdiction - Appellant /assessee was married started business under name and style of Union Trading Co - Assessee submitted voluntarily returns of income for assessment years 1953-54 to 1960-61 to Income-tax Officer - Income Tax officer made assessment orders for A/y under provisions of 1922 Act - Respondent No.1 issued a notice to assessee u/s. 33B of 1922 Act for cancelling assessments and was fixed as date of hearing - On date of hearing no one attended on behalf of assessee and an order was made by respondent in exercise of powers conferred u/s. 33B of 1922 Act cancelling assessment orders and directed I.T.O. to make fresh assessments according to law - Being aggrieved by order appellant moved HC u/art. 226 of Constitution for grant of a writ - No notice was issued in connection with proceedings u/s. 33B of Act - Hence instant appeal - Whether appellant was entitled to invoke special jurisdiction of HC u/art. 226 of Constitution - Held, no explanation had been given by appellant in petition for not preferring an appeal under Act and justified her recourse to special jurisdiction of HC u/art. 226 of Constitution - HC found after examination of evidence that an opportunity was given to appellant to be heard before respondent No.1 made order u/s. 33B of 1922 Act - Appeal dismissed.
