State of Rajasthan and Others v Nenu Ram

Supreme Court of India

31 July 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Grover, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 576; 1970 (26) STC 268

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Sales Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Constitution - Rajasthan Sales Tax Act - Position taken up by respondent was that making of doors and windows as also frames was a part of a single and indivisible works contract and as such no tax was payable - High Court came to conclusion that 'the contract in question was a single and indivisible contract for supply and fixing of wooden doors on spot and that this contract was given and taken for an inclusive price' - Appeal - Test in each case is whether object of party sought to be taxed is that chattel as chattel passes to other party and services rendered in connection with installation are under a separate contract or are incidental to execution of contract of sale - Held, judgment of HC upheld - It was only upon fixing of windows-leaves and when window-leaves had become a part of building construction that property in goods passed under terms of contract - Appeal dismissed.
