Somnath Sahu v State of Orissa and Others

Supreme Court of India

21 March 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 540; (1969) 3 SCC 384; 1969 (35) CutLT 606; 1969 (19) FLR 135; 1981 (2) SLR 550; 1969 UJ 351

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 32 - Orissa Welfare Officers' (Recruitment and Conditions of Service) Rules, 1961, r. 6(2) - Appellant was appointed as Welfare Officer by respondent No. 4 - Appellant dismissed from service - Challenged - Held, respondent No. 4 had a contractual right to terminate service of appellant without assigning any reason by giving one month's notice or one months's salary in lieu of notice - No element of punitive action in order of respondent No. 4. - Respondent No. 4 was not required to issue notice to appellant or to make an enquiry and there was no violation of principle of natural justice - Appeal dismissed.
