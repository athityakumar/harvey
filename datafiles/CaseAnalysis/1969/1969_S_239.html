Nazul Ali Molla, Etc v State of West Bengal

Supreme Court of India

16 September 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

Vishishtha Bhargava

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 486; (1969) 3 SCC 698

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Preventive Detention Act, s. 3(2) - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 32 and 226 - Whether if a petition u/art. 226 of Constitution is presented in High Court and is dismissed on merits and not on grounds of limitation or without a speaking order and no appeal is brought up against order of HC, a petition on similar grounds in Supreme Court should be held to be barred by res judicata? - Held, there is no decision that a petition u/art. 32 of Constitution for issue of a writ of habeas corpus is to be held to be barred on principle of res judicata if a petition for a similar writ u/art. 226 of Constitution before a HC has been decided and no appeal is brought up to this court against that decision - Material supplied on records of these two petitions on behalf of respondent is totally insufficient to invoke principle of res judicata even if such a principle could be applied - Pleadings and judgments in two petitions before HC not filed - Further held, detention of petitioners held to be illegal as representations were considered and decided by Govt. after expiry of two months after Govt. had already confirmed order of detention - No explanation given for delay - Petitioners directed to be released - Petition allowed.
