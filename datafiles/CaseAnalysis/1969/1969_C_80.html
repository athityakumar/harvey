Commissioner of Income Tax, West Bengal Iii v Kamal Singh Rampuria

Supreme Court of India

12 February 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, A.N. Grover, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 323; (1969) 3 SCC 644; [1970] 75 ITR 157; 1969 UJ 212

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, ss.34(1)(a),66 - Will - Earned interest - Assessment - Evidence - Assessee's mother executed a will by which she disposed of Rs.5,00,000 received by her as a gift previously from her husband - Sum of Rs.3,00,000 was invested by assessee in firm and earned interest - Assessee's mother bequeathed to assessee 1/6th share held by her in Trading Company - Tribunal assumed that assessee had not discharged his duty of returning his income at the proper time and provisions of s.34(1)(a) of Act applied - Whether assessment made was justified in law - Held, fact made by Tribunal cannot be interfered with by court - Matter was open to attack u/s. 66 of 1922 Act as erroneous in law - There was no evidence to support it and it was perverse - HC was in error in reappraising the evidence before Appellate Tribunal - Income Tax Officer had no reason to believe that there was an omission on part of assessee to disclose fully and truly all tile material facts necessary for assessment - Appellate Tribunal answered in the affirmative and against assessee - Appeal allowed.
