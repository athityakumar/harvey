Ouseph Varghese v Joseph Aley and Others

Supreme Court of India

18 August 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 109; (1969) 2 SCC 539; [1970] 1 S.C.R. 921

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Contract & Commercial - Oral contract - Specific Performance - Reconveyance - Whether decree for specific performance can be granted for an oral agreement? - Burden of proving oral agreement was on plaintiff - Suit for specific performance has to conform to requirements prescribed in Forms 47 and 48 of 1st sch. in Civil Procedure Code, 1908 - In a suit for specific performance it is incumbent on plaintiff not only to set out agreement on basis of which he sues in all its details, he must go further and plead that he has applied to defendant specifically to perform agreement pleaded by him but defendant has not done so - He must further plead that he has been and is still ready and willing to specifically perform his part of agreement - Neither in plaint nor at any subsequent stage of suit plaintiff has taken those pleas - Held, no evidence for proving terms of oral contract - Plaintiff failed to prove agreement pleaded in plaint - Relied on Pt Prem Raj v D L F Housing and Construction (Private) Ltd and anr, 1968 Indlaw SC 118 - In suit for specific performance plaintiff should allege that he is ready and willing to perform his part of contract and in absence of such an allegation suit is not maintainable - Order accordingly.
