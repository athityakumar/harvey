Messrs Silver Screen Enterprises v Devki Nandan Nagpal

Supreme Court of India

28 November 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 438; (1970) 3 SCC 878; AIR 1970 SC 669; 1970 (6) DLT 311; 1970 (72) PLR 583; 1970 RCJ 98

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - Practice & Procedure - East Punjab Urban Rent Restriction Act, 1949, s. 13 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Dismissal of appeal - Compromised - Respondent was the owner of a Cinema House - That Cinema House had been leased to the appellant - Respondent filed an application u/s. 13(2)(i) of the Act for the ejectment of the tenant on the ground of non-payment of rent - Application was dismissed by the Rent Controller - Subsequently, by an agreement, appellant and the respondent settled all their pending disputes excepting one Regular First pending in the HC - One of the disputes pending at that time was an application made by the appellant to the Rent Controller for fixing a fair rent for the Cinema House in question - Appellant moved the Court to dismiss the appeal on the strength of the compromise - Appellate Court dismissed the appeal - On revision, HC set aside the order of the Appellate Court holding that there was no provision in the CPC under which an appellant can be compelled to withdraw his appeal - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, appellate court could always dismiss an appeal on the ground that it has been settled out of Court. On the basis of that compromise both the appellant and the respondent were required to withdraw all the pending proceedings excepting the one. Once a dispute is validity settled out of Court, it is open to a party to a litigation to move the Court to pass a decree in accordance with the compromise. Hence the appellate court was bound to accept the same. That Court acted in accordance with law in dismissing the appeal. Hence the HC was clearly wrong in interfering with the judgment of the Appellate Court. Appeal allowed.
