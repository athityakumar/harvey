Bennett Coleman and Company Private Limited v Punya Priya Das Gupta

Supreme Court of India

02 April 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Shelat, Vishishtha Bhargava, C.A. Vaidyialingam

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 37; (1969) 2 SCC 1; AIR 1970 SC 426; 1969 (19) FLR 32; 1970 LabIC 512; 1969 (2) LLJ 554; [1970] 1 S.C.R. 181

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Keywords: Fair Wage

Summary: Minimum Wages Act, 1948 - Working Journalists and other Newspaper Employees (Conditions of Service) and Miscellaneous Provisions Act, 1955, ss. 2(c), 2(f), 2(g), 5 and 17 - Evidence Act, 1872 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 2(rr) - (A) Expression 'who is employed' in definition of 'working journalist' in s. 2(f) of 1955 Act - It includes employee whose service has come to an end by resignation - Maintainability of claim for gratuity from such employee - (B) Evidence - Burden of proving ingredients of s. 115 of 1872 act lies on party claiming estoppel - Representation - (C) Term 'wages' not defined in 1955 Act - Definition of that term in s. 2 (rr) of 1947 act can be applied - Car allowance and benefit of free telephone and newspapers given to employee not only for his personal use but for use connected with his employment - (A) Held, claim for gratuity from such employee is maintainable - (B) Representation must be clear - (C) Held, that both the items were relevant in fixation of fair wages as they were allowed to him to directly reduce the expenditure which would otherwise have gone into his family budget - These items could properly be regarded as part of the wages and had to be taken into account into calculation of gratuity payable to him - Appeal Dismissed.
