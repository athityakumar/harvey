Punjab National Bank Limited v Bikram Cotton Mills & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

17 September 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 83; (1970) 1 SCC 60; AIR 1970 SC 1973; [1970] 40 Comp Cas 927; [1970] 2 S.C.R. 462

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Corporate

Keywords: Debt Laws

Summary: Corporate - Companies Act, 1956, s. 391 - Contract Act, 1872, ss. 124 and 126 - Contract of indemnity and guarantee - Loan by Bank to company - Promissory note by managing agent of company in favour of Bank - MD guaranteeing payment of final balance - Suit against MD - But ultimate balance not found due - (A) Whether suit is premature - (B) Whether binding obligation created under a composition u/s. 391 of 1956 Act, between co. and its creditors affect liability of surety - Held, suit not premature but should be stayed till ultimate balance is arrived at - Surety is not affected unless contract of suretyship otherwise provides - Matter remitted to High Court
