O.P. Bhandari v Indian Tourism Development Corporation Limited

Supreme Court of India

26 September 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

M.P. Thakkar, S. Natarajan

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 200; (1986) 4 SCC 337; (1986) SCC (L&S) 769; AIR 1987 SC 111; 1986 ATC 541; 1986 (2) CCC 1040; [1987] 61 Comp Cas 291; 1986 (3) CompLJ 283; 1986 (53) FLR 752; JT 1986 SC 586; 1987 LabIC 25; 1986 (2) LLJ 509; 1986 (2) LLN 1051; 1986(2) SCALE 523; [1986] 3 S.C.R. 923; 1987 (2) SLJ 268; 1986 (3) SLR 710; 1987 (1) UJ 317

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Indian Tourism Development Corporation (Conduct, Discipline and Appeal) Rules 1978

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950 - Industrial Dispute Act, 1947 - Indian Tourism Development Corporation (Conduct, Discipline and Appeal) Rules 1978, r.31(v) - Termination of service - (A) Constitutionality of the r.31(v) - (B) Order of termiantion passed under regulation - Which found to be unconstitutional - Relations between employee and employer, strained - Trade union threat strike - Determination of proper relief ? (C) Determination of compensation in lieu of reinstatement - When full salary and allowances till date of superannuation (around 8 years) claimed - Held, is violative of arts. 14 and 16 - Held, in circumstances that compensation in lieu of reinstatement, and not reinstatement, was warranted - Claim, held, not reasonable, award of compensation to equivalent to 3.33 years salary with allowances found to be reasonable, as it would yield 50% of annual salary and allowances to employee if it is invested at prevailing rate of interest - Appeal allowed.
