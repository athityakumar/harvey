Bakul Cashew Company and Others v Sales Tax Officer Quilon and Another

Supreme Court of India

12 March 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

E.S. Venkataramiah, M.P. Thakkar

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 419; (1986) 2 SCC 365; (1986) SCC (Tax) 385; AIR 1987 SC 2239; [1986] 159 ITR 565; 1986(1) SCALE 380; [1986] 1 S.C.R. 610; 1986 (62) STC 122; 1987 TAXLR 2000; [1986] 28 TAXMAN 121; 1986 (2) UJ 121

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Land & Property; Sales Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Kerala General Sales Tax Act, 1963 - Evidence Act, 1872, s.115 - Sales Tax - (A) Withdrawal of exemption - Promissory estoppel - Absence of clear and unambiguous representation by Government to grant exemption - (B) Government's power to grant exemption from tax - Piece of subordinate legislation - Exemption could not be granted retrospectively in absence of legislative authorisation - Notification No. G. O. Ms. 127/73/ID, D/- 12-10-1973 granting exemption on imported cashewnuts retrospectively - Withdrawal not illegal and promissory estoppel would not operate - Held, ineffective - Appeal dismissed.
