Chaitanya Kumar v State Of Karnataka And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

09 April 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy, K.N. Singh

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 418; (1986) 2 SCC 594; AIR 1986 SC 825; 1986 (2) CCC 327; 1986(1) SCALE 1099; [1986] 2 S.C.R. 409

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Karnataka Excise (Bottling of Liquor) Rules 1967 (as stood before its amendment on November 30 1984)

Summary: Excise - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 32, 226 and 14 - Karnataka Excise Act, 1966, s. 13(1)(e) - Karnataka Excise (Bottling of liquor) Rules, 1967, r. 3 - Excise Duty - Grant of contract for bottling arrack - (A) Challenged by way of Public interest litigation - Grant established as arbitrary - But High Court refuses to set aside contract - (B) Arrack meant for sale - Need of licence - Held, HC could not refuse to set aside contract - Grantee must have license contemplated by rules - Appeal dismissed.
