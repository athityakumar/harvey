Commissioner Of Income-Tax, U.P. v Laxmi Sugar And Oil Mills Ltd

Supreme Court of India

16 July 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Pathak, S. Mukharji

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 170; (1986) 3 SCC 528; AIR 1986 SC 1746; 1986 (58) CTR 105; [1986] 161 ITR 168; JT 1986 SC 239; 1986(2) SCALE 33; [1986] 3 S.C.R. 214; 1986 TAXLR 1141; [1986] 27 TAXMAN 284; 1986 (2) TLR 1141; 1986 (2) UJ 502

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax and Direct Taxes - Super Profits Tax Act, 1963, ss. 2(q), 4, and sch.2, r. 1- Appeal by special leave against the judgment of the High Court pronouncing on the meaning of the expression 'reserves' in the 1963 Act - Determination of 'Reserves and provision' - Assessee debiting certain amount under the head 'current liabilities and provisions' - Representing additional cane price payable to growers - Charge against profits - Evidence disclosing no liability requiring it to set apart such a sum - In fact payment never made - Held, amount is reserve and not provision - Appeal dismissed.
