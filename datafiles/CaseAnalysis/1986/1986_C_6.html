S.P. Gramophone Company v C.I.T., Patiala

Supreme Court of India

29 January 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

V.D. Tulzapurkar, S. Mukharji

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 266; (1986) 2 SCC 1; (1986) SCC (Tax) 346; AIR 1986 SC 1152; 1986 (51) CTR 104; 1986 (9) ECR 28; [1986] 158 ITR 313; 1986(1) SCALE 137; [1986] 1 S.C.R. 164; 1986 TAXLR 570; [1986] 24 TAXMAN 507; 1986 (2) TLR 570; 1986 (2) UJ 169

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Indirect Tax; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Income Tax and Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 26A and 66 - Partnership Act , 1872 - Income Tax - Registration of partnership firm - (A) Whether a valid partnership came into existence? - Held, no genuine partnership had come into existence - (B) Whether assessee is entitled to registration under s.26A of the Income Tax Act, 1922 r/w r. 6 of the Income Tax Rules, 1922? - Held, there was sufficient material on record on the basis of which the taxing authorities as well as the Tribunal could record an adverse finding on the genuineness of the firm against the assessee and registration in our view was rightly refused - (C) Whether fact that one of the partners is benamidar of another bars grant of registration?Puts no bar , if the partnership was otherwise genuine and valid - Appeal dismissed.
