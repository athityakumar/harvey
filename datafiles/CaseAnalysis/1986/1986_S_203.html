State of Himachal Pradesh and Anr v Umed Ram Sharma and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 February 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, V.D. Tulzapurkar, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 659; (1986) 2 SCC 68; AIR 1986 SC 847; 1986(1) SCALE 182; [1986] 1 S.C.R. 251; 1986 (1) UJ 478

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Constitution - Constitution, arts. 19(1)(d), 21, 38(2), 226, 202 to 207 - HP Budget Manual, chap. 12 - Residents of hilly areas affected by denial of proper roads and non-availability of roads (A) Scope of right of life - (B) Additional or excess grant for any Project - Executive can make recommendation to legislature - Whether High Court can impinge upon judgment of executive - (D) PIL - Scope of affirmative action by HC in form of remedial measure - In the light of Constitutional aspiration u/art. 38 r/w arts.19 land 21 - (A) In the context of constitutional provisions, existence of roads in reasonable conditions is embraced in their right to life - (B) HC cannot impinge upon - (C) Directions in cases of executive inaction or slow action is permissible within the limits - By the process of judicial review, if the HC activises or energises executive action, it should do so cautiously - Remedial action in public interest must be with caution and within limits - Order accordingly
