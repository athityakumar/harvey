Central Inland Water Transport Corporation Limited and Another v Brojo Nath Ganguly and Another

Supreme Court of India

06 April 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Madon, A.P. Sen

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 645; (1986) 3 SCC 156; (1986) SCC (L&S) 429; AIR 1986 SC 1571; 1986 ATC 103; 1986 (2) CCC 335; [1986] 60 Comp Cas 797; 1986 (3) CompLJ 1; 1986 (53) FLR 523; 1986 LabIC 1312; 1986 (2) LLJ 171; 1986 (2) LLN 382; 1986(1) SCALE 799; [1986] 2 S.C.R. 278; 1986 (2) UJ 171

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Negligence in maintenance of PF accounts, Termination order

Summary: (a) Constitution - Service - Companies Act, 1956, s. 617 - Constitution of India, 1950, art.12 - Indian Contract Act, 1872, s. 23 - Service, Discipline and Appeal Rules, 1979, r. 9(i) - Negligence in maintenance of PF accounts - Termination order - Reinstatement - Entitled to - Appellant/Corporation issued termination notice to respondents in allegation of negligence in maintaining of PF accounts - Aggrieved respondents filed petitions before Single Judge challenging termination order - Single Judge allowed the petition - Appellants appealed before DB - DB held that Corporation was State within meaning of art. 12 of Constitution and that said r. 9(i) was ultra vires art. 14 of Constitution and struck down r. 9(i) as being void and quashed impugned orders of termination - Hence instant appeal - Whether Government company as defined in s. 617 of 1956 Act, was 'State' within meaning of art. 12 of the Constitution - Held, if there was an instrumentality or agency of state which had assumed garb of Government company as defined in s. 617 of 1956 Act it did not follow that it thereby ceased to be an instrumentality or agency of State - For purposes of art. 12 one should necessarily see through corporate veil to ascertain whether behind that veil was face of an instrumentality or agency of State - Respondent/Corporation, which was appellant in these two appeals, squarely fell within these observations and it also satisfied various tests which had been laid down - Merely because it had so far not monopoly of inland water transportation was not sufficient to divest it of its character of an instrumentality or agency of State - It was nothing but Government operating behind corporate veil, carrying out governmental activity and governmental functions of vital public importance - Corporation was 'the State' within meaning of art. 12 of Constitution - Appeal dismissed.

(b) Constitution - Service - Companies Act, 1956, s. 617 - Service, Discipline & Appeal Rules, 1979, r. 9(i) - Constitution of India, 1950, art.12 - Indian Contract Act, 1872, s. 23 - Negligence in maintenance of PF accounts - Termination order - Reinstatement - Entitled to - Whether an unconscionable term in contract of employment was void u/s. 23 of 1872 Act as being opposed to public policy and, when such term was contained in contract of employment entered into with Government company, was also void as infringing art. 14 of Constitution in case a Government company was 'the State' under art. 12 of Constitution - Held, clause such as r. 9(i) in a contract of employment affecting large sections of public was harmful and injurious to public interest for it tends to create a sense of insecurity in minds of those to whom it applied and consequently it was against public good - Such a clause, therefore, was opposed to public policy and being opposed to public policy, it was void u/s. 23 of 1872, Act - Order passed by HC was modified by substituting for declaration given by it a declaration that r. 9(1) of 1979, Rules of Central Inland Water Transport Corporation Limited was void u/s. 23 of 1872 Act as being opposed to public policy and was also ultra vires art. 14 of Constitution to extent that it confers upon Corporation right to terminate employment of permanent employee by giving him three months' notice in writing or by paying him equivalent of three months' basic pay and dearness allowance in lieu of such notice - SC vacated stay order of reinstatement and direct Corporation forthwith to reinstate Respondent No. 1 in each of these Appeals and to pay to him within six weeks from today all arrears of salary and allowances payable, if any still unpaid - Appeal dismissed.
