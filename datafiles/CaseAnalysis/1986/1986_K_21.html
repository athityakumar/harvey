Union of India v Kanti Lal Chunilal and Others

Supreme Court of India

03 April 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Bhagwati, V. Khalid, G.L. Oza

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 761; (1986) Supp SCC 345; AIR 1987 SC 1410; [1987] 62 Comp Cas 401; 1986 CRLR 479; 1986 (9) ECR 273; 1986 (26) E.L.T. 289; 1986(1) SCALE 1355

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Customs; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Wholesale Cash Price

Summary: Excise - Constitution, sch. 7, list 1, entry 84 - Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944, s. 4(4)(c) - Excisable goods - Levy of duty - Value of goods - (A) Whether the definition of related person u/s. 4 (4) (c) of the Central Excise Act is unconstitutional or void - (B) Value of the goods - Whether the value of the excisable goods for the purpose of levy of excise duty in the hands of the respondents could be taken to be the wholesale cash price at which the excisable goods were sold by the firm on the basis that the firm was a related person - (C) Whether post manufacturing expenses and profits can be excluded - (D) Whether the firm could be said to be related person within the meaning of that expression as defined in s.4(4)(c) of the Act - Held, cannot be said to be ultra vires or void - The value fixed at the wholesale cash price - Such expenses and profits cannot be excluded - Could not be said to fall within the category of 'related person' as it could not be said that the respondents had any interest, direct or indirect, in the business - Appeal Partly Allowed.
