C.I.T. Bombay City v Bombay Burmah Trading Corporation, Bombay

Supreme Court of India

16 July 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, K.N. Singh

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 356; (1986) 3 SCC 709; AIR 1987 SC 500; 1986 (58) CTR 144; [1986] 161 ITR 386; 1986(2) SCALE 277B; [1986] 3 S.C.R. 269; [1986] 27 TAXMAN 314

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax and Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1922, s.10(c)(vii) - Assessee-company enter into contracts ('forest leases') with the Government of Burma - (A) Whether particular receipt is capital or revenue - (1) Nature of payments received by assessee for sterilisation of rights under such leases - (2) Sale proceeds of few tons of logs handed over in lieu of logs extracted but not removed by assessee - When lease cancelled due to nationalisation and there was an agreement to receive certain tons of logs in lieu of it - (B) Whether liable to levy of balancing charge - When agreement does not involve any sale transaction but the sale of depreciable assets is absolutely necessary - (C) Taxability - Assessee obtaining decree for more logs from forest taken over earlier by Burma Govt. - Held, are capital receipts and the sale proceeds were capital receipts - Balancing charge cannot be levied against assessee - Held, logs received under the decree were also in lieu of surrender of rights under forest leases , so sale proceeds thereof could not be taxed - Appeal dismissed.

.
