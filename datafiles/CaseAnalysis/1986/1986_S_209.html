Bijoe Emmanuel and Others v State of Kerala and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 August 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy, M.M. Dutt

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 626; (1986) 3 SCC 615; AIR 1987 SC 748; 1986 (2) CCC 744; JT 1986 SC 115; 1986 KLT 1037; 1986(2) SCALE 217; [1986] 3 S.C.R. 518

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution

Keywords: Freedom Of Speech And Expression, Right To Freedom, Sovereignty And Integrity Of India, Defamation, Defamatory Material, Defamatory Articles

Summary: Constitution - Constitution, 1950, arts. 19(1)(a), 25(1) and 51 A(a) - Expulsion - Disrespect to national anthem - Petitioner were student who didn't join singing but stood up respectully when national anthem was sung - Contended that it is against the tenets of their religious faith-not the words or the thoughts of the Anthem but the singing of it - Held, that there is no provision of law which obliges anyone to sing the National Anthem nor it is disrespectful to the National Anthem if a person who stands up respectfully when the National Anthem is sung does not join the singing - No disrespect is shown by not joining in the singing - Appeal allowed.
