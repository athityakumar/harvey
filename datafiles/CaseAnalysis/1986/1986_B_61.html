Brooke Bond And Company Limited(Now Known Asbrooke Bond Leibig Li v C.I.T., West Bengal-Ii, Calcutta

Supreme Court of India

30 September 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Pathak, S. Mukharji

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 204; (1986) 4 SCC 689; 1986 (57) CTR 25; JT 1986 SC 613; 1986(2) SCALE 573; [1986] 3 S.C.R. 980; [1986] 28 TAXMAN 426

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Depreciation Allowance, Business Income or Loss

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1922, ss.24(2), 33A(2) - Depreciation allowance - Assessment - Relevant financial year - Taxable territories - Dividend income - Appellant/ Company was a sterling company carrying on business - Appellant invested in shares of other tea companies in different parts of world and hundred per cent share holding in an Indian subsidiary - Appellant was assessed and relevant financial year was previous year in relation to the corresponding assessment year - For assessment year 1955-56 appellant was assessed on its total world income by an assessment order on the basis of provisional figures of its business loss including depreciation, and its income from dividends - On the basis of those provisional figures it was assessed to a net loss of Rs.31,33,647 - Assessment for assessment year 1957-58 was completed in November 1957 as a non- resident, determining an income of Rs.51,85,836 received by way of dividends on its share holdings - An appeal was taken to Appellate Assistant Commissioner of Income Tax claiming that loss for assessment year 1955-56 should be carried forward and set off against income for assessment year 1957-58 - Assistant Commissioner dismissed appeal holding that there would be no loss if the loss for the assessment year 1955-56 was set off against income for a/y 1956-57 and loss could not be legally set off directly in assessment year 1957-58 - Appellant appealed to Income-tax Appellate Tribunal and Appellate Tribunal set the order of Appellate Assistant Commissioner and directed to dispose of appeal afresh after determining - Whether appellant was entitled to set off a business loss arising outside taxable territories for assessment year 1955-56 against dividend income arising in taxable territories for assessment year 1957-58 - Held, order of Commissioner disposing of revision application for assessment year 1956-57 should have been set aside by Division Bench and Commissioner should have been directed to consider claim on its merits - Revenue to contend on merits that appellant was not entitled to the carry forward of unabsorbed depreciation - Division Bench and Single Judge as well as the order of the Commissioner of Income Tax on revision application the a/y 1956-57 are set aside - Claim of appellant to carry forward of unabsorbed depreciation and Commissioner was directed to dispose of the revision application in respect of that claim afresh - Appeal partly allowed.
