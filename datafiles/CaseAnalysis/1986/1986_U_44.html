Delhi Police Non-Gazettedkarmchari Sangh and Others v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

20 November 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Khalid, G.L. Oza

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 229; (1987) 1 SCC 115; AIR 1987 SC 379; 1987 (2) ATC 194; 1987 (1) CCC 540; JT 1986 SC 920; 1987 (1) LLJ 121; 1987 (1) LLN 430; 1986(2) SCALE 872; [1987] 1 S.C.R. 347; 1987 (1) SLJ 213; 1987 (1) UJ 234

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Trusts & Associations

Keywords: Police Forces (Restriction of Rights) rules 1966, Police Forces (Restriction of Rights) Amendment Rules 1970, Police Forces (Restriction of Rights) Act, 1966

Summary: Constitution - Trusts & Associations - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 33, 19(4) and 19(1)(c) - Police Forces (Restriction of Rights) Amendment Rules, 1970 - Police Forces (Restriction of Rights) Rules, 1966 - Whether impugned statute, rules and orders are violative of rights of appellants guaranteed u/art. 19(1)(c) of Constitution of India, 1950? - It is true that rules impose a restriction on right to form association - It virtually compels a Govt. servant to withdraw his membership of association as soon as recognition accorded to said association is withdrawn or if, after association is formed, no recognition is accorded to it within six months - Right to form an association is conditioned by existence of recognition of said association by Govt. - If association affairs recognition and continues to enjoy it, Govt. servants can become members of said association; if said association does not secure recognition from Govt. or recognition granted to it is withdrawn, Govt. servants must cease to be members of said association - That is plain effect of impugned rule - These rules are protected by arts. 33 and 19(4) of Constitution, 1950 - Besides, it is settled law that right guaranteed by art. 19(1)(c) to form associations does not involve a guaranteed right to recognition also - Held, there has been violation of any law - Police Force (Restriction of Rights) Act 1966, the Police Force (Restriction of Rights) Rules 1966 and circular D/- 1-4-1971 are all constitutionally valid - Appeal dismissed.
