Ujagar Prints and another v Union of India and another

Supreme Court of India

19 December 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Bhagwati, G.L. Oza

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 130; (1986) Supp SCC 652; AIR 1987 SC 874; 1987 (1) CCC 537; [1987] 62 Comp Cas 548; 1987 (10) ECR 640; 1987 (27) E.L.T. 567; [1987] 167 ITR 904; JT 1987 (1) SC 54B; 1986(2) SCALE 1218; 1987 (66) STC 403; 1987 (1) UJ 209

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Trader, Processing, Central Excises and Salt and Additional Duties of Excise (Amendment) Act, 1980

Summary: Excise - Indirect Tax - Central Excise Act, 1944 - Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944, s. 4 - Central Excises and Salt and Additional Duties of Excise Act, 1980 - Central Excise Rules, 1944, rr. 56-A, 96-D - Assessment - Raw material - Petitioners and appellants were carrying business of processed cotton fabrics of grey cloth and manmade fabrics contended that process on job work basis was amounted to levy of excise duty - Petitioners and appellants contended observations did not represent correct law and to be reconsidered - Whether processed fabrics were assessable to excise duty in hands of processor - Held, it could not accept that value of grey cloth processed by processor should not be included in assessable value of processed fabric - Grey cloth was one of raw materials goes into manufacture of processed fabric and value of processed fabric could not be computed without including value of raw material - Assessable value of processed fabric could not be limited to value of work done but it must be determined by reference to wholesale cash price of processed fabric - Element of selling profit of trader would be entirely extraneous element and it could not be taken into account for purpose of determination of assessable value of processed fabric - Order accordingly.
