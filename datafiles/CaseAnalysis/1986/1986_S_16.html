Kerala State Electricity Board and Others v S.N. Govinda Prabhu and Brothers and Others

Supreme Court of India

26 August 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy, M.M. Dutt

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 623; (1986) 4 SCC 198; AIR 1986 SC 1999; 1986 (2) CCC 1047; JT 1986 SC 261; 1986(2) SCALE 313; [1986] 3 S.C.R. 628

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Electricity - Tariff - Electricity (Supply) Act, 1948, ss. 49, 59, 67 and 67-A - Constitution of India, arts. 12 and 14 - Kerala State Electricity Board raised/increased the electricity tariff - Whether the Kerala State Electricity Board is competent to increase the tariff under the Act? - Held, yes - Surplus left from total revenues after meeting all expenses properly chargeable to revenues - Revision of tariff was based on fair principles of accounting and not arbitrary in any manner - Committee constituted for revision of tariff advocated revision of tariff keeping in mind the rising cost of production - Appeals allowed.
