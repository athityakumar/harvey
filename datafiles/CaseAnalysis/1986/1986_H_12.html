Hari Bhanu Maharaj of Baroda v Charity Commissioner, Ahmedabad

Supreme Court of India

28 August 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Natarajan, Ranganath Misra

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 579; (1986) 4 SCC 162; AIR 1986 SC 2139; JT 1986 SC 280; 1986(2) SCALE 306; 1987 (1) UJ 300

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Bombay Public Trust Act, 1950 - Math constructed from family funds - Math formed small area of family property and was also located within its residential area - Public visited math only on specified occasion - Admittedly, other extent of property was being enjoyed and always had been enjoyed by appellant and his ancestors for their private use and occupation - Whether property known as Laxman Maharaj Math is a public trust or private property of appellant? - Held, Laxman Maharaj Math also called Ramji Mandir was private property of family and it was not a public trust - Evidence in case stood unchallenged - No evidence to contrary showing that Samadhis and Mandir were built by public or out of public funds - Order accordingly.
