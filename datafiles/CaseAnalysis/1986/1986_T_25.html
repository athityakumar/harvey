Sterling Foods, A Partnership Firm Represented By v The State Of Karnataka And Anr

Supreme Court of India

21 July 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Bhagwati, V. Khalid, G.L. Oza

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 177; (1986) 3 SCC 469; (1986) SCC (Tax) 609; AIR 1986 SC 1809; 1987 (11) ECR 641; 1986 (26) E.L.T. 3; JT 1986 SC 155; 1986(2) SCALE 106; [1986] 3 S.C.R. 367; 1986 (63) STC 239; 1986 (2) UJ 545

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Keywords: Processing, Karnataka Sales Tax Act 1957, Karnataka Taxation and Certain Other Laws (Amendment) Act 1978, Purchase of sea food products

Summary: Sales Tax - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956 - Karnataka Sales Tax 1957, s. 12B(2) - Purchase of sea food products - Purchase tax - Exemption from tax - Entitled to - Appellants were partnership firm carrying on business as dealers in shrimps, prawns and lobsters and other sea food products - Appellants in course of their business purchase shrimps, prawns and lobsters locally for purpose of complying with orders for export and they cut heads and tails of shrimps, prawns and lobsters purchased by them, peel, devein and clean them and after freezing and packing them in cartons, they export them to foreign buyers - Appellants claimed total exemption from tax in respect of purchase turn-over of shrimps, prawns and lobsters on ground that same had purchased in course of export - Asst. Commissioner of Commercial Taxes(ACCT) rejected the appellants claim - ACCT made an order u/s. 12B(2) of the 1957 Act assessing the appellants to purchase-tax and other incidental taxes for the month of April 1982 in respect of purchases of shrimps, prawns and lobsters made by them - ACCT also passed another order assessing appellants to purchase tax and other incidental taxes in respect of purchases of shrimps, prawns and lobsters made by them during month of May, 1982 - Appellants filed writ petition before HC challenging the assessment orders - HC dismissed the petitions - Hence, instant appeal - Whether shrimps, prawns and lobsters subjected to processing like cutting of heads and tails, peeling, deveining, cleaning and freezing cease to be same commodity and become a different commodity for purpose of the 1956 Act - Held, it was clear that on application of test that processed or frozen shrimps, prawns and lobsters were commercially regarded same commodity as raw shrimps, prawns and lobsters - When raw shrimps, prawns and lobsters were subjected to process of cutting of heads and tails, peeling, deveining, cleaning and freezing, they did not cease to be shrimps, prawns and lobsters and become another distinct commodity - They were in common parlance known as shrimps, prawns and lobsters - There was no essential difference between raw shrimps, prawns and lobsters and processed or frozen shrimps, prawns and lobsters - In instant case, it was not disputed on behalf of Revenue that purchases of raw shrimps, prawns and lobsters were made by appellants for purpose of fulfilling existing contracts for export and after making such purchases appellants subjected raw shrimps, prawns and lobsters purchased by them to process of cutting of heads and tails, peeling, deveining, cleaning and freezing and exported such processed and frozen shrimps, prawns and lobsters in fulfillment of contracts for export - However, shrimps, prawns and lobsters purchased by appellants did not lose their original character and identity when they were subjected to processing for purpose of export - So far as commercial parlance or popular usage was concerned, they remained same goods and hence purchases of raw shrimps, prawns and lobsters by appellants should be held to be purchases in course of export and hence exempt from liability to tax under the 1957 Act - Impugned order passed by HC was set aside as also orders passed by ACCT and directed that purchases of raw shrimps, prawns and lobsters made by appellants for purpose of fulfilling existing contracts for export should not be included in tax-able turnover of appellants - Appeal allowed.
