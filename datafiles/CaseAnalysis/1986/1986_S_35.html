Niranjan Patnaik v Sashibhusan Kar and Another

Supreme Court of India

11 April 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Natarajan, V. Balakrishna Eradi

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 60; (1986) SCC (Cr) 196; (1986) 2 SCC 569; AIR 1986 SC 819; 1986 (12) AWC 737; 1986 BBCJ 647; 1986 (3) Crimes 5; 1986 CRLJ 911; 1986 CrLR(SC) 230; 1986(1) SCALE 877; [1986] 2 S.C.R. 470; 1986 (1) UJ 667

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 436, 395, 397 and 160 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, ss. 378 and 482 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947, ss. 5 and 8 - Appeal by special leave preferred by a prosecution witness for expunction of several highly derogatory remarks made against him by a learned Judge of High Court of Orissa while allowing criminal appeal - Held, remarks are unjustified and need to be expunged - Appeal allowed.
