Om Prakash Shukla v Akhilesh Kumar Shukla and Others

Supreme Court of India

18 March 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

E.S. Venkataramiah, A.P. Sen, B.C. Ray

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 280; (1986) Supp SCC 285; (1986) SCC (L&S) 644; AIR 1986 SC 1043; 1986 ALJ 662; 1986 LabIC 796; 1986 (2) LLN 38; 1986(1) SCALE 475; [1986] 1 S.C.R. 855; 1986 (3) SLJ 235; 1986 (1) SLR 699; 1986 (2) UJ 184

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950 - Uttar Pradesh Subordinate Civil Courts Ministerial Establishment Rules, 1947 - Uttar Pradesh Subordinate Offices Ministerial Staff (Direct Recruitment) rules, 1975 - Selection for Grade III Ministerial Staff in subordinate courts - Competitive examination conducted by District Judge - Legality - Whether examinations held in 1981 according to 1950 Rules are valid? - Held, 1975 Rules did not apply to subordinate courts under control and superintendence of High Court - Hence, 1950 Rules insofar as they applied to subordinate courts continued to be in force - Finding of HC is erroneous and is liable to be set aside - Appeal allowed.
