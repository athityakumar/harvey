Sushil Kumar Yadunath Jha v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

06 May 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 81; (1986) 3 SCC 325; (1986) SCC (L&S) 600; AIR 1986 SC 1636; 1986 (12) ALR 536; 1986 (53) FLR 278; 1986 LabIC 1105; 1987 (1) LLJ 7; 1986 (2) LLN 435; 1986(1) SCALE 1262; 1986 (3) SLR 32; 1986 (2) SLR 204; 1986 (2) UJ 387

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 226 and 311 - Appointment as temporary PGT - Termination - Re-appointment (without benefit of previous service) - (A) Laches - Representations, requesting condonation of break in service - Communication by Commissioner of Kendriya Vidhyalya Sangathan that question of condonation could be considered after 3 years - Request for condonation, rejected - Writ petition, if suffer from laches - (B) Condonation of break in service - Teacher's performance in subsequent years highly praised - Petition does not suffer from laches - Held, he was entitled to benefit of continuous service notwithstanding term to contrary in his fresh appointment - Appeal allowed.
