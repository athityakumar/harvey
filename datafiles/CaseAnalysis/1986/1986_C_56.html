Anchor Pressings (P) Limited v Commissioner Of Income Tax, U.P. And Ors

Supreme Court of India

16 July 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Pathak, S. Mukharji

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 175; (1986) 3 SCC 439; AIR 1987 SC 575; 1986 (58) CTR 126; 1987 (27) E.L.T. 590; [1986] 161 ITR 159; JT 1986 SC 235; 1986(2) SCALE 86; [1986] 3 S.C.R. 223; 1986 TAXLR 1168; [1986] 27 TAXMAN 295; 1986 (2) TLR 1168; 1986 (2) UJ 519

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Code of Civil Procedure, Industrial Undertaking, Chargeable Profits, Super Profits Tax, Super Profit Tax Act, 1963

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income-tax Act, 1961, s.84 - Super Profit Tax Act, 1963 - Manufacture and sale of locks - Rebate - Assessment - Rectification - Appellant was a private limited company carrying on business of manufacture and sale of locks - No claim was made by assessee for rebate u/s. 84 of 1961 Act - There was an appeal by assessee to Appellate Assistant Commissioner of Income Tax but no claim was made for rebate - Appellant made an application u/s. 154 of 1961 Act praying for rectification of assessment order by grant of relief u/s. 84 of 1961 Act - Application was rejected by Income Tax Officer - HC dismissed - Whether there was material on record to sustain claim of assessee entitle to relief -- Held, there must be material to support claim to relief u/s.84 and unless such material can be referred to no grievance can be made if the Income Tax Officer refuses to rectify the assessment and refuses relief u/s. 84 - Assessee was entitled to relief u/ s. 84of 1961 Act - Material required for satisfying conditions requisite for grant of relief u/s. 84 existed on Super Profits Tax record at time when income-tax assessment was completed - Omitting to grant relief u/s. 84 when making assessment order Income Tax Officer committed a mistake apparent from record - An order refusing rectification and not to an order directly assailing the assessment - Appeal dismissed.
