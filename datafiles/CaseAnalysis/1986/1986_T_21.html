Dilharshankar C. Bhachecha v The Controller Of Estate Duty, Ahmedabad

Supreme Court of India

08 January 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, V.D. Tulzapurkar

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 365; (1986) 1 SCC 701; AIR 1986 SC 1707; 1986 (50) CTR 321; [1986] 158 ITR 97; [1986] 158 ITR 238; 1986(1) SCALE 6; [1986] 1 S.C.R. 94; 1986 TAXLR 916; [1986] 24 TAXMAN 318; 1986 (2) TLR 916

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Estate, Estate Duty, Estate Duty Act, 1953, Settled Property, Joint Will

Summary: Land & Property - Succession Act, 1925, s. 74 - Benefit u/s. 29 of Estate Duty Act, 1953 Whether will was joint or joint and mutual? - Husband and wife executing joint will and demarcated portions of house bequeathed to grandsons - Held, survivor did not become sole owner - Will being joint and mutual, on survivor's death accountable person entitled to benefit of s. 29 - Appeal allowed.
