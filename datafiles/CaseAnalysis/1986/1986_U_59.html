Ram Chander v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

02 May 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

A.P. Sen, G.L. Oza, O. Chinnappa Reddy, K.N. Singh

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 69; (1986) 3 SCC 103; (1986) SCC (L&S) 383; AIR 1986 SC 1173; 1986 ATC 47; 1986 (30) DLT 282; 1986 (53) FLR 191; 1986 LabIC 885; 1986 (2) LLJ 334; 1986 (2) LLN 81; 1986(1) SCALE 998; [1986] 2 S.C.R. 980; 1986 (2) SLJ 249; 1986 (2) SLR 608

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Trade; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Service - Labour & Industrial Law - Industries (Development And Regulation) Act, 1951 - Railway Servants (Discipline and Appeal) Rules, 1968, r. 22(2) - Whether Division Bench rightly dismissed the appeal in limine -The Railway Board agreed with the findings of the General Manager - There was no duty cast on the Railway Board to record reasons for its decision to dismiss the services of the appellant? - Considerations of fair play and justice also require that such a personal hearing should be given - Opportunity must be given to the delinquent Govt. servant not only at the first stage to be heard in respect of the charges but also at the second stage i.e. after the disciplinary authority had come to a tentative conclusion of guilt at the conclusion of the inquiry and had decided upon the punishment proposed to be inflicted - No decision must be taken which will effect the rights of any person without first giving him an opportunity of putting forward his case - Held, an objective consideration is possible only if the delinquent servant is heard and given a chance to satisfy the authority regarding the final orders that may be passed on his appeal - Considerations of fair play and justice also require that such a personal hearing should be given - Held, the judgment and order of a Learned Single Judge and that of the Division Bench dismissing the Letters Patent Appeal filed by the appellant are both set aside - Held, appeal is allowed.
