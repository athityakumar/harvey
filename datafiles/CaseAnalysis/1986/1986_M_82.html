Dinesh Kumar and Others v Motilal Nehru Medical College, Allahabad and Others

Supreme Court of India

21 July 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Bhagwati, Ranganath Misra

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 178; (1986) 3 SCC 727; AIR 1986 SC 1877; 1987 ALJ 189; JT 1986 SC 97; 1986(2) SCALE 188; [1986] 3 S.C.R. 345

Case Digest

Subject: Education; Health & Drug; Indirect Tax; Service

Summary: Education - Indian Medical Council Act, 1956 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - All India Medical Admission - Scheme formulated by Govt. of India - Confirmation by Supreme Court - (A) Reservation by States - On the basis of domicile and institution - Validity of - Held, wholesale reservation by the state is unconstitutional and void as offending the equality clause of the Constitution of India, 1950 - Very mandate of the equality clause viewed in the perspective of social justice, would justify some extent of reservation based on residence requirement within the State or on institutional preference for students passing the qualifying examination held by the University or the State - (B) Language of the All India Entrance Examination - States contention that it would be in regional language - Held, Medical Council of India in revised scheme suggested that All-India Entrance Examination should be held in English language - (C) State reservation - Held, 30% of open seats for admission to MBBS/BDS courses were to be reserved provides enough scope to State Govts. to increase number of reserved categories - (D) Exemption from the operation of scheme - Held, since States of AP and J and K have been exempted from operation and these two States would not be liable to set apart seats for admission on basis of All India Entrance Examination - Students from other States would not be entitled to compete for admission to MBBS/BDS and post graduate courses in medical colleges and institutions in these two States - Appeal pending for confirmation.
