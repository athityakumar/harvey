Surinder Singh v Central Government and Others

Supreme Court of India

26 September 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

K.N. Singh, M.P. Thakkar

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 199; (1986) 4 SCC 667; AIR 1986 SC 2166; JT 1986 SC 578; 1986(2) SCALE 550; [1986] 3 S.C.R. 946

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Displaced Persons (Compensation and Rehabilitation) Act, 1954, ss. 2, 4, 7, 8, 14, 20, 33 and 40 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Whether in absence of rules Central Govt. had authority in law to provide for disposal of urban agricultural land by auction sale? - Held, where a statute confers powers on an authority to do certain acts or exercise power in respect of certain matters, subject to rules, exercise of power conferred by statute does not depend on existence of rules unless statute expressly provides for same - (B) Whether order of officer to whom power u/s. 33 was delegated, extending time to enable appellant to deposit auction sale money? - Held, while exercising powers of Central Govt. u/s. 33 of Act, officer had ample jurisdiction to set aside orders of subordinate authorities canceling auction held on August, 1959 and to permit appellant to deposit balance amount of purchase money and he further had jurisdiction to extend time initially granted by him - Appeal partly allowed.
