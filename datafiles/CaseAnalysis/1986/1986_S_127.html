Shivaji Atmaji Sawant And Anr. v State Of Maharashtra And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

14 February 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Madon, A.P. Sen

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 273; (1986) 2 SCC 112; (1986) SCC (L&S) 209; AIR 1986 SC 617; 1986 (1) CCC 1028; 1986 (52) FLR 474; 1986 LabIC 585; 1986 (1) LLN 700; 1986(1) SCALE 193; [1986] 1 S.C.R. 300; 1986 (1) SLJ 358; 1986 (1) SLR 495

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Police Forces (Restriction of Rights) Act, 1966

Summary: Service - Constitution - Constitution, art. 311- Bombay Police Act, 1951, s. 25(1) - Dismissal without inquiry - Appellant, a head constable in Maharashtra Police - No charge-sheet issued - No inquiry held with respect to misconduct alleged against him -Dismissal order passed u/s. 25(1) of the Bombay Police Act read with clause (b) of the second proviso to art. 311(2) of the Constitution - Reasons for dispensing inquiry served separately after order of dismissal - Whether valid and proper? - Held, in view of these facts and circumstances it was not practicable to hold an inquiry, there was no error in applying art. 311(2), Second Proviso, Cl.(b) to the case - The appellants should file an application for revision to the Inspector-General of Police who shall condone the delay and hear and dispose of the said application on the merits - Appeal dismissed.
