A.P. Christians Medical Educational Society Etc. v Government Of Andhra Pradesh And Anr.

Supreme Court of India

24 April 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy, G.L. Oza, K.N. Singh

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 304; (1986) 2 SCC 667; AIR 1986 SC 1490; 1986(1) SCALE 895; [1986] 2 S.C.R. 749

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Education - Constitution, arts.30, 32 and 136 - The matter deals with : brazen and bizarre exploitation of the naive and foolish, eager and ready-to-be-duped, aspirants for admission to professional collegiate courses, behind the smoke screen of the right of the minorities to establish and administer educational institutions of their choice - Minority veil can be pierced by authorities as also the Court to find out if it is minority institution - Permissibility to establish the institution - Held on facts professional College could not be permitted to be established as minority institution - Appeal dismissed.
