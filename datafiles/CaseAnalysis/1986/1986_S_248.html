Gian Singh v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

14 October 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

K.N. Singh, M.P. Thakkar

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 487; (1986) Supp SCC 676; (1987) SCC (Cr) 223; AIR 1987 SC 1921; 1987 CRLJ 1918; 1987 CRLR 293

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 300 - Appellant, accused of murder of his uncle, challenged his conviction awarded by High Court - Murder proved by circumstantial evidence - Witness saw accused and two others having a quarrel with deceased - Accused was later seen with cycle which belonged to deceased, recovered at instance of accused - Whether Trial Court was justified in convicting accused on circumstantial evidence? - Held, evidence relating to recovery of cycle belonging to deceased lends strength to prosecution case and same is reinforced by evidence showing that accused was absconding for 13 days and therefore, findings of guilt against accused of Trial Court and HC is not liable to be disturbed - Appeal dismissed.
