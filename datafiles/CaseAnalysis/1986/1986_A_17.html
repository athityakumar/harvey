Smt. Madhu Khanna v Administrator, Union Territory of Delhi and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 September 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 27; (1986) 4 SCC 240; (1986) SCC (Cr) 426; AIR 1987 SC 48; 1987 CRLJ 318; 1986 CRLR 401; 1986 (30) DLT 382; 1986 (26) E.L.T. 197; JT 1986 SC 401; 1986(2) SCALE 394

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Criminal - COFEPOSA Act, 1973 - Preventive Detention - Petitioner praying for quashing the detention of her husband u/s. 3(1) r/w s.2(f) - A rule nisi has been issued - Smuggling of gold by detenu, a clearing agent in Embassy - Challenged on the grounds: (A) Declaration u/s. 3(1) is bad because of the failure of authority to consider the representation - Non-reference to the representation in the declaration shows that the representation was not considered by the declaring authority before he made the declaration - (B) Whether continued detention of the detenu is illegal and invalid as the Advisory Board did not at all consider the same - (C ) Whether undue delaying in receiving required information means a denial of right u/art. 22(5) of the Constitution, making the order of detention is illegal - (D) Letter sent from one officer to another as a routine practice for their comments and not because the detaining authority required such comments for supplying the information to the detenu - Validity - (E) Contended that that there was non consideration of deposition of the defence witness, father of detenu by the confirming authority - But Advisory Committee has in its report referred to the evidence - Held, non-mention of the representation in the declaration seems to be a mere omission - Advisory Board in its report has categorically observed that there is sufficient cause for the continued detention - Held, there was no unjustified delay - Act of calling for the comments from the Customs Deptt. for the purpose of supplying the authentic information was justified - the confirming authority considered the report - And constitutional validity of s.9 of the COFEPOSA Act has been challenged but not - Order accordingly.
