Kameshwar Singh Srivastava v Iv Addl. Dist. Judge Lucknow And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

14 November 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

K.N. Singh, S. Mukharji

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 221; (1986) 4 SCC 661; AIR 1987 SC 138; 1986 (12) ALR 688; 1987 (1) CCC 192; JT 1986 SC 842; 1987 (1) RCJ 94; 1987 (1) RCR(Rent) 231; 1987 (1) RentLR 627; 1986(2) SCALE 797; [1987] 1 S.C.R. 224; 1987 (1) UJ 268

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Rent Control - UP Urban Buildings (Regulation of Letting, Rent and Eviction) Act, 1972, Pre. ss. 20(4), 30(1), 30(6), 39 and 40 - Eviction of tenant - Appellant, tenant on monthly rent - Death of landlord - Appellant ready to pay to heirs - Heirs not ready, rent paid in court - Landlord asking for eviction and payment of arrears - Appellant stated that he was willing to pay, provided if one accepts to take - Paid the arrears in court - Held, the appellant is not liable to ejectment as appellant was ready to pay and since the landlords did not give any reply to his notice he was justified in depositing the arrear in the Munsif's Court - Since the deposit was made it must be deemed that the appellant had tendered rent to the landlords - Appeal allowed.
