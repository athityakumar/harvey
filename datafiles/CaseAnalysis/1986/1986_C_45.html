Maharaj Purshottamlalji Maharaj v Collector of Junagad Distt.

Supreme Court of India

09 September 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranganath Misra, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 620; (1986) 4 SCC 287; AIR 1986 SC 2094; 1986 (2) CCC 910; JT 1986 SC 387; 1986(2) SCALE 400B; [1986] 3 S.C.R. 705

Case Digest

Subject: Trusts & Associations

Summary: Trusts & Associations - Bombay Public Trusts Act, 1950, s.18 - Nature of property - Constitution of trust - Legality of - Appellant filed application to Assistant Charity Commissioner u/s.18 of Act contending that Haveli and its properties did not constitute public trust - Charity Commissioner found that institution was public trust and all forty items of property belonged to that trust - HC affirmed appellate decision of Charity Commissioner that property in dispute and thirty eight items of its properties constitute public trust and found that property in dispute also were part of source of income and according to it these constitute important source of income of trust - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether property in dispute is public or private.

Held, single or few features would not provide conclusive basis for decision to be arrived at. Entire material has to be scanned and ultimate conclusion has to rest on sum total view. nothing has been shown from record to justify the conclusion that Guru is only conduit pipe between devotee at one end and Lord on other so as to lead to conclusion that whatever is offered at feet of the Guru Belongs to Lord. Preceptor has his position and if he is not conduit pipe in sense stated above, what is laid at his feet out of reverence by the devotee must belong to him. In position to uphold finding of HC on this score and would conclude that proceeds of Guruseva and Pradesh Seva do not constitute part of public trust. HC has said that these two sources contribute seventy per cent of the income of the trust. No argument was raised on aspect by either side. Ajanta Estate Agency, appellants in connected appeal entered into agreement with trustee to purchase certain properties during pendency of litigation arising out of enquiry under the Act. Thus, once properties are held to belong to public trust, appellants would have no claim to enforce. Appeal dismissed.
