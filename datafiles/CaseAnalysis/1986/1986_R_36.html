Azhar Hussain v Rajiv Gandhi

Supreme Court of India

25 April 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

M.P. Thakkar, E.S. Venkataramiah

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 305; (1986) Supp SCC 315; AIR 1986 SC 1253; 1986 ALJ 625; 1986(1) SCALE 573; [1986] 2 S.C.R. 782; 1986 (2) UJ 63

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Criminal; Election; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Foreign Power

Summary: Election - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872 - Representation of the People Act, 1951, ss. 83, 86 and 116-A - Dismissal of election petition - On the ground that failure to furnish material facts, particulars enjoined by s. 83 and cause of action - Maintainability of - Held, an election petition can be summarily dismissed if it does not furnish cause of action in exercise of powers under CPC, 1908 -Appropriate orders in exercise of powers under CPC, 1908 can be passed if mandatory requirements enjoined by s. 83 of Act to incorporate material facts in election petition are not complied with - The pleading in regard to matters where there is scope for ascribing an alleged corrupt practice to a returned candidate in context of a meeting of which dates and particulars are not given would tantamount to failure to incorporate essential particulars - If an averment of fact is an essential part of pleading, it must be considered to be an integral part of petition - If such an averment is not actually put in election petition, petition suffers from lack of material facts and therefore, statement of cause of action would be incomplete - HC was perfectly justified in taking view that no cause of action was made out,for, in absence of material particulars as to who had printed, published or circulated pamphlet, when, where and how it was circulated and which facts went to indicate respondent's consent to such distribution - Pleading would not disclose a cause of action - Appeal dismissed.
