Shitla Prasad Shukla v State of Uttar Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 May 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

M.P. Thakkar, E.S. Venkataramiah

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 408; (1986) Supp SCC 185; (1986) SCC (L&S) 584; AIR 1986 SC 1859; 1986 ALJ 793; 1986 ATC 236; 1986 (12) AWC 1147; 1986 (53) FLR 349; JT 1986 SC 357; 1986 (2) LLJ 298; 1986 (2) LLN 800; 1986(1) SCALE 1311; [1986] 3 S.C.R. 106; 1986 (2) SLR 628

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Uttar Pradesh Intermediate Education Act, 1921

Summary: Service - Uttar Pradesh Intermediate Education Act, 1921, ss. 16E and 16F - Inter se seniority - District Inspector of Schools hold Respondents Nos. 5 and 6 were senior to appellant in lecturer's grade - Appellant Writ Petition to High Court, which affirmed decision District Inspector - Judgment of HC challenged - Held, in a matter where there is no rule or regulation governing the situation or where there is one, but is not violated, the court will not overturn the determination unless it would be unfair not to do so - In any view of the matter the appellant who did not even belong to the stream of regularly (he was allowed to teach only in an irregular and unauthorized manner) and lawfully appointed lecturers, cannot claim seniority against any one already in the stream before he joined the stream himself - Appeal dismissed.
