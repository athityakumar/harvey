Commissioner of Wealth Tax, Calcutta v O.M.M. Kinnison (Dead) Through Her Executors and Trustees

Supreme Court of India

29 August 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Pathak, S. Mukharji

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 621; (1986) 4 SCC 297; AIR 1986 SC 2019; 1986 (58) CTR 193; [1986] 161 ITR 824; JT 1986 SC 295; 1986(2) SCALE 355; [1986] 3 S.C.R. 674; [1986] 28 TAXMAN 8; 1986 (2) TLR 1335

Case Digest

Subject: Foreigners; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: (A) Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Foreigners - Wealth-tax Act, 1957 - Estate - Beneficiary - Net Wealth - Sub-Partnership - There were managing agents of two Indian companies for several years - They entered into a sub-partnership from time to time under which the two shared equally the emoluments from the managing agency - After the death of one of the partner, all his property was given to his wife who executed two deeds of assignment assigning her share of the emoluments under the sub-partnership in favour of her son - Thereafter the son received the half share of emoluments from the managing agency - Son made a will and appointed his wife and a solicitor as executors and trustees - HC of Justice in England granted probate of the will - Letters of Administration were obtained in India and the stamp duty paid at the time of obtaining the Letters of Administration - The widow was non-resident and not a citizen of India - Assessee then appealed for all the six A/Ys to the Appellate Tribunal - She contended that the assets held by her were situated outside India, and being a non-resident she was not taxable thereon - Alternatively, she urged that she was entitled to exemption under sub-clause (iv) of clause (e) of s. 2 of the Act - Whether on the facts and in the circumstances of the case, the Tribunal was right in holding that the assessee who had a life interest in the testamentary trust estate of late C.H. Kinnison comprising inter alia of the shares in an Indian company and commission from the managing agency of an Indian Company can be said to have an interest in such shares and commission and that such interest was property located in India so as to be taxable under the Wealth Tax Act - Held, the assessee was entitled to on the valuation dates was the right to have the trust administered and, as the HC had observed, having regard to the several considerations patent in this case that the settlement was an English settlement, created by an Englishman who was resident in England, that it was an English will proved in England and the trustees were residents in England and moreover that the assessee, the beneficiary, was an English woman who was also residing in England, therefore the proper forum for the enforcement of the rights of the beneficiary under the will was the appropriate Court in England - The right of the assessee was a right enforceable in that Court and, therefore, must be regarded as a foreign asset, an asset not located in India - Thus the Tribunal had no right in holding that the assessee who had a life interest in the testamentary trust estate of the deceased comprising inter alia of the shares in an Indian company and commission from the managing agency of an Indian Company can be said to have an interest in such shares and commission and that such interest was property located in India so as to be taxable under the Act - Appeals dismissed.

(B) Foreigners - Wealth-tax Act, 1957 - Estate - Beneficiary - Net Wealth - Sub-Partnership - Whether on the facts and in the circumstances of the case, the Tribunal was right in holding that the life interest of the assessee in the testamentary trust estate of late C.H.Kinnison was not an annuity which was exempt u/s. 2(e)(iv) of the Act - Held, in the present case, it did not appear that on the relevant valuation dates the estate of the testator had been completely and finally administered and that the trustees had proceeded to the point where it could be said that there was a clear and ascertained residue from which the income payable to the assesee as a beneficiary under the will could be known, and whether the assessee was entitled to income arising from the Indian shares and the managing agency of the Indian company - The right of the assessee was a right enforceable in that Court and, therefore, must be regarded as a foreign asset, an asset not located in India - Thus the Tribunal was not right in holding that the life interest of the assessee in the testamentary trust estate of late C.H.Kinnison was not an annuity which was exempt u/s. 2(e)(iv) of the Act - Appeals dismissed.
