Shivajirao Nilangekar Patil v Dr. Mahesh Madhav Gosavi & Ors. And Vice Versa

Supreme Court of India

09 December 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, R.S. Pathak, S. Natarajan

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 606; (1987) 1 SCC 227; AIR 1987 SC 294; JT 1986 SC 1071; 1986(2) SCALE 977; [1987] 1 S.C.R. 458; 1987 (1) UJ 88

Case Digest

Subject: Education; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, art.136 - CPC, 1908, O.41, r.27 - Evidence Act, 1872 - Application for adducing additional evidence - Alleged tampering with examination results at behest of Chief Minister for his relatives - No direct evidence - High Court rejected SLP to set aside examination result - Appeal u/art. 136 filed against it - Application for adducing additional evidence - As to instances when CM had used his influence to drop criminal proceedings sought to be adduced - Admissibility of additional evidence - Held, SC declined to consider the additional evidence as Chief Minister had no opportunity to deal with it - Additional evidence refused to be admitted at such belated stage - Appeals and application disposed of.
