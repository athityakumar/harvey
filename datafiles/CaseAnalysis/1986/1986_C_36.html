Capt. Virendra Kumar Through His Wife v Chief of The Army Staff, New Delhi and another

Supreme Court of India

13 February 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy, V. Khalid

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 775; (1986) SCC (Cr) 149; (1986) 2 SCC 217; (1986) SCC (L&S) 248; AIR 1986 SC 1060; 1986 (2) Crimes 353; 1986 CRLJ 848; 1986 CRLR 131; 1986 (1) LLN 688; 1986(1) SCALE 219; 1986 (1) SLR 422

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Comments

Summary: Defence and Security Forces - Army Act, 1950, ss. 10, 18, 38, 39, 104, 105 - Artillery Records Instructions, 1981, para. 418 - Constitution of India, 1950 - (A) Service - (1) Whether recommissioning of officer is required when revocation of commission by notification of president was declared invalid by the Supreme Court - (2) Whether the Captain was a deserter - While drawing distinction between desertion and absence without leave - (B) Habeas Corpus - Validity of arrest and detention of Army Officer on ground of desertion and not deemed desertion - (C) Advocates Act - Validity of cancellation of license to practice of military officer discharged from service and admitted to bar - On ground that he has been restored in Army - Show cause notice for withheld information regarding invalidation of his discharge and subsequent arrest -(D) Contempt application - Whether there has been any non-compliance with the directions given by this Court - Held: (A) recommissioning not necessary - Distinction explained as: If there is animus dedicandi the absence is straightway desertion - (B) Its legality cannot be challenged on ground of non-compliance of s.106 - (C) Not proper - (D) No willful non-compliance hence no further action in the contempt application - Appeal allowed.
