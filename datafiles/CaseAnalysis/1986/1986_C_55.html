State Bank Of Travancore v Commissioner Of Income Tax, Kerala

Supreme Court of India

08 January 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

V.D. Tulzapurkar, Ranganath Misra, S. Mukharji

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 796; (1986) 2 SCC 11; (1986) SCC (Tax) 289; AIR 1986 SC 757; 1986 (2) CompLJ 126; 1986 (50) CTR 290; [1986] 158 ITR 102; 1986(1) SCALE 34; [1986] 1 S.C.R. 25; 1986 TAXLR 521; [1986] 24 TAXMAN 337; 1986 (2) TLR 521; 1986 (2) UJ 208

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: (Majority View)

Mukharji and Ranganath Misra, JJ.

Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 28, 36(1)(vii), and 145 - A/ys 1965-68 - Assessment - Mercantile system of accounting - Interest on advances doubtful of recovery - Assessee, a subsidiary bank of the State Bank of India, used to maintains accounts on mercantile system making entries on accrual basis and adopted calendar year as its previous year - Assessee, in the course of its banking business, used to charge interest on advances considered doubtful of recovery termed as 'sticky advances' by debiting the concerned parties but instead of carrying the same to its 'Profit & Loss Account', credited the same to a separate account called 'Interest Suspense Account' as the principal amounts of these 'sticky advances' themselves had become not bad or irrecoverable, but extremely doubtful of recovery - However, in its returns, assessee disclosed such interest separately and claimed that the same was not taxable in its hands as income for the concerned years - The business of assessee also included buying and selling of foreign exchange and before devaluation of the Indian Rupee, assessee held exchange by way of cash balances available with their foreign correspondents, to U.S. Dollars and in sterling, which on devaluation of the Indian Rupee when converted back to rupees at the post devaluation rates gave rise to a profit of 57.5% in the transaction - Assessee credited this surplus to an account designated 'Provision for Contingencies' and claimed that profit by way of exchange difference on devaluation should not be taxed as it was of a casual and non-recurring nature - Said claims of the assessee was rejected by the ITO - On appeal, Tribunal affirmed the order of the ITO - HC held that since assessee was following the mercantile system of accounting, such interest had accrued to it at the end of each a/y - HC also held that assessee had itself shown the accrual of such interest by charging the same to the concerned parties by making debit entries in their accounts - HC further held that if any part of the debts later became irrecoverable in any year the assessee could in that year treat it as such and claim deduction u/s. 36(1)(vii) of the Act - Hence, the instant appeals - Whether the addition of amount representing interest on 'sticky' advances, as income for the relevant a/ys was justified in law? - Held, the concept of real income would apply where there had been a surrender of the income which in theory may have accrued but in the reality of the situation no income had resulted because the income did not really accrue - Mere improbability of recovery, where the conduct of the assessee was unequivocal cannot be treated as evidence of the fact that income had not resulted or accrued to the assessee - After debiting the debtor's account and not reversing that entry, but taking the interest merely in suspense account cannot be such evidence to show that no real income has accrued to the assessee or treated as such by the assessee - In the instant case, assessee after the close of the a/y without giving up the interest which the assessee could have as a bad debt, did not offer it for taxation but carried it to 'interest suspense account' - Carrying certain amount which had accrued as interest without treating it as bad debt or irrecoverable interest but keeping in suspense account would be repugnant to s. 36(1)(vii) r/w. s. 36(2) of the Act - Hence, the addition of amount representing interest on 'sticky' advances, as income for the relevant a/ys was justified in law - Appeal dismissed.

Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 28, 36(1)(vii), 145 and 5 - A/ys 1965-68 - Devaluation of Rupee - Whether, exchange difference of amount arising on devaluation of Indian rupee was rightly treated as income for a/y? - Held, if the stock-in-trade remains unused or unsold the mere book appreciation in the value thereof cannot be brought to tax but on the facts requisite to sustain the proposition the assessee does not seem to stand on any firm footing - In the first place by carrying the surplus resulting from the devaluation of the Indian rupee to an account designated 'Provision for Contingencies' the assessee itself could be said to have clearly treated such surplus as its business income - Secondly, the Asst. Commissioner in his appellate order had recorded a categorical finding that the stock in trade in terms of foreign currency was sold and used by the assessee in its normal banking business - Therefore, exchange difference of amount arising on devaluation of Indian Rupee was rightly treated as income for a/y - Appeal dismissed.

(Minority View)

V. D. Tulzapurkar, JJ.

Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 28, 36(1)(vii), 145 and 5 - A/ys 1965-68 - Mercantile system of accounting - Interest on advances doubtful of recovery - Whether the addition of amount representing interest on 'sticky' advances, as income for the relevant a/ys was justified in law? - Held, method of accounting regularly employed by an Assessee was relevant only for purpose of computation of income, profits and gains u/s. 28 of the Act - Under s.145 of the Act, assessee's regular method of accounting determined mode of computing taxable income but it did not determine or even effect range of taxable income or ambit of taxation - Hence, theory of real income could be and should be extended to interest on sticky loans and that on principle such interest being hypothetical cannot be brought to tax - Amounts representing interest on sticky advances in the instant case being hypothetical and not real income of the assessee could not be brought to tax for the relevant a/ys - Appeal allowed.
