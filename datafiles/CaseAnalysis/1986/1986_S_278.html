 Messrs Kasturi Lal Harilal v State of Uttar Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

29 October 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Bhagwati, G.L. Oza, M.M. Dutt, Ranganath Misra, V. Khalid

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 458; (1986) SCC (Tax) 62; (1986) 4 SCC 704; AIR 1987 SC 27; JT 1986 SC 749; 1986(2) SCALE 708; [1987] 1 S.C.R. 86; 1987 (64) STC 1; 1987 (1) UJ 54

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Sales Tax

Keywords: Collected

Summary: Sales Tax - Constitution - U P Sales Tax Act, 1948, s. 29-A - Appellants carry on business as dealers in coal and are registered under U P Sales Tax Act, 1948 - Coal became taxable for first time in 1965 - Appellants passed burden on their purchasers - It was found by Sales Tax Officer that no sales tax was payable by appellants on sales of coal - Appellants claimed refund but Sales Tax Officer rejected claim on ground that no refund was to be claimed by appellants u/s. 29-A of Act and refund could only be claimed by their purchasers - Whether s. 29-A of U P Sales Tax Act, 1948 is valid? - Held, yes - S. 29-A provides procedure for disbursement of amounts wrongly realised by dealer as tax - S. 29 A falls within legislative competence of state legislature and be upheld - Taking over of sums collected by dealers from public under guise of tax solely to return them to buyers so deprived is incidental to tax on sale and purchase of goods - Such a provision is a consumer protection measure - Suits against dealers to recover paltry sums by a large number of customers would lead to endless litigation - Simpler process of returning those to relevant purchasers would protect common buyer and prevent dealers from making unjust gains - Appeal dismissed.
