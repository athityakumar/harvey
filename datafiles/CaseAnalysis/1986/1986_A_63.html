Sham Lal (Dead) By Lrs v Atme Nand Jain Sabha (Regd.) Dal Bazar

Supreme Court of India

10 December 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

B.C. Ray, M.P. Thakkar

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 604; (1987) 1 SCC 222; AIR 1987 SC 197; JT 1986 SC 1016; 1987 (1) RCJ 49; 1987 (1) RCR(Rent) 181; 1987 (1) RentLR 24; 1986(2) SCALE 1032; [1987] 1 S.C.R. 509; 1987 (1) UJ 119

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Framing Of Issues

Summary: Women and Children - Constitution - Immoral Traffic (Prevention) Act, 1956 - U.P. Suppression of Immoral Traffic in Women and Girls Rule, 1961, r.40 - Protective Home - Writ petition that the conditions in which girls were living in the Govt.Protective Home were abominable and they were being denied their right to live with basic human dignity by the State Govt. - Matter pending before Supreme Court - When everything which had been done by the court in order to improve the living conditions - It was set at naught by the State Govt.by shifting the Protective Home from its location without Court's permission - There also conditions unsatisfactory - And also danger of sliding back into prostitution of the immates releases from such homes - Held, directions given to authorities for improving conditions in the home and to State Govt. for constituting Board of Visitors and for formulating programme of rehabilitation of inmates - Other concerned writs dealt with - Order accordingly.
