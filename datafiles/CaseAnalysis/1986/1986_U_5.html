Umaji Keshao Meshram and Others v Radhikabai W/O Anandrao Banapurkar and Another

Supreme Court of India

14 March 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy, D.P. Madon

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 651; (1986) Supp SCC 401; AIR 1986 SC 1272; 1986 (2) CCC 273; 1986(1) SCALE 681; [1986] 1 S.C.R. 731; 1986 (2) UJ 319

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Court Of Record, Indian Independence Act, 1947, Karnataka High Court Act, 1961, State of Himachal Pradesh Act, 1970, Bombay Reorganisation Act, 1960, Assam Revenue Tribunal (Transfer Of Powers) Act, 1948, Andhra State Act, 1953, Constitution (Tenth Amendment) Act, 1961, Constitution (Forty-Third Amendment) Act, 1977, Bombay Tenancy and Agricultural Lands (Vidharbha Region) Act, 1958, High Court of Bombay (Extension of Jurisdiction to Goa, Daman and Diu) Act, 1981, Dadra and Nagar Haveli Act, 1961

Summary: Constitution - Practice and Procedure - Constitution, arts. 226, 227, 228 - States Reorganisation Act, 1956 - Maintainability of appeal against judgment of single Judge in petition under arts. 226 or 227 to Division Bench - Held, appeal is maintainable in petition under art. 226 but not under art. 227 as it is barred under Cl. 15 of the Letters Patent of the Chartered High Courts - Appeals dismissed.
