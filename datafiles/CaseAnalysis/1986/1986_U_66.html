Sheela Barse and Others v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

13 August 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Bhagwati, Ranganath Misra

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 184; (1986) SCC (Cr) 337; (1986) SCC (Cr) 352; (1986) 3 SCC 632; AIR 1986 SC 1773; 1986 CRLJ 1736; 1986 CrLR(SC) 405; JT 1986 SC 136; 1986(2) SCALE 230; [1986] 3 S.C.R. 562

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Summary: Women and Children - Human Rights - Constitution of India, 1950 - PIL - Condition of physically and mentally retarded children - Abandoned or destitute children - Lodged in various jails in country for 'safe custody' - Held, if a child is a national asset, it is duty of State to look after child with a view to ensuring full development of its personality - The State Govts. must set up necessary remand homes and observation homes where children accused of an offence can be lodged pending investigation and trial or otherwise children should be released on bail - Set up by Juvenile Courts, one in each district, and there must be special cadre of Magistrates who must be suitably trained for dealing with cases against children - State Govt. to bring into force and to implement vigorously provisions of Children's Acts enacted in various States and that instead of each State having its own Children's Act - It would be desirable if Central Govt. initiates parliamentary legislation on subject, so that there is complete uniformity in regard to various provisions relating to children in entire territory of country - Order accordingly.
