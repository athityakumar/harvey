State of Uttar Pradesh v Man Mohan and Others

Supreme Court of India

23 April 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

M.P. Thakkar, G.L. Oza

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 52; (1986) SCC (Cr) 214; (1986) 2 SCC 587; AIR 1986 SC 1652; 1986 (12) AWC 990; 1986 (3) Crimes 84; 1986 CRLJ 1245; 1986 CRLR 329; 1986(1) SCALE 1252; 1986 (2) UJ 247

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Practice & Procedure -Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, ss. 438 and 439 -Reference and Revisionary power of High Court - Magistrate formed an opinion that accused had committed an offence - Prosecution not false Whether HC can quash charge on an accused in exercise of revisional and reference powers where magistrate had formed an opinion that accused had committed offence? -Held, where Magistrate has formed opinion that there were grounds to presume that accused had committed offence punishable u/s. 3 along with s. 7 of Essential Commodities Act 1955 and that prosecution was not false, frivolous or vexatious or one which was by way of abuse of process of law, HC could not quash charge in exercise of revisional and reference powers - Appeals allowed.
