Pournami Oil Mills, Etc. v State Of Kerala And Anr.

Supreme Court of India

19 December 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranganath Misra, P.N. Bhagwati

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 13; (1986) Supp SCC 728; (1987) SCC (Tax) 134; AIR 1987 SC 590; 1987 (27) E.L.T. 594; [1987] 165 ITR 57; JT 1986 SC 1112; 1986(2) SCALE 1225; [1987] 1 S.C.R. 654; 1987 (65) STC 1; 1987 (1) UJ 249

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Keywords: Exemptions, Entitilement, Notifications

Summary: Sales Tax - Indirect Tax - Notifications dt. 11-4-1979 and dt 29-9-1980 - Exemption -Entitlement -Whether the appellant industries were entitled to the exemption in question - Held, it was not disputed that the first order dt. 11-4-1979 gave more of tax exemption than second order - Second notification withdrew exemption related to purchase tax and confined exemption from sales tax to the limit specified in proviso of notification - Appellants who in response to the order of 11-4-1979 set up their industries prior to 21-10-1980 within State, would thus-be entitled to exemption extended and/or promised under that order - Such exemption would continue for full period of 5 years from the date appellants start production - New industries set up after 21-10-1980 would not be entitled to that benefit as they had noticed of curtailment in exemption before they came to set up their industries - State contended that question as to which of appellants were entitled to benefit should be left to Sales Tax Authorities to decide - Law was settled and that part of decision should be left to the Departmental authorities and they might decide the question on merits in appropriate proceeding in accordance with the law laid down - Appeals allowed.
