Central Provinces Manganese Ore Co. Ltd v Commissioner Of Income Tax

Supreme Court of India

15 July 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Pathak, S. Mukharji

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 166; (1986) 3 SCC 461; (1986) SCC (Tax) 607; AIR 1987 SC 438; 1986 (58) CTR 112; [1986] 160 ITR 961; JT 1986 SC 35; 1986(2) SCALE 41; [1986] 3 S.C.R. 140; 1986 TAXLR 1187; [1986] 27 TAXMAN 275; 1986 (2) TLR 1187; 1986 (2) UJ 676

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Waiver Or Reduction, Reduction Or Waiver

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income-tax Act, 1961, ss. 139(8), 215, 246 and 264 - Income Tax Rules, 1962, rr. 117A(v), 40(1) and 40 (5) - A/y 1967-68 - Interest - Interest was levied against the assessee u/s. 139(8) of 1961 Act for delay in furnishing its return and also u/s. 215 of 1961 Act for making payment of advance tax at a figure less than 75% of the assessed tax - Assessee preferred an appeal u/s. 246 of 1961 Act before Asst. Commissioner raising objection to the total income assessed and also including grounds objecting to the interest charged u/ss. 139 and 215 of the 1961 Act - On being advised thereafter that the grounds objecting to the charge of interest were infructuous inasmuch as orders u/ss. 139 and 215 of the 1961 Act were not appealable, the appellant filed two revision petitions before the Commissioner u/s. 264 of the 1961Act, one objecting to the levy of interest u/s. 139(8) of 1961 Act and the other u/s. 215 of 1961 Act - Commissioner dismissed said petitions and held that in order to maintain revision petitions u/s. 264 of the 1961Act challenging the levy of interest the assessee would have been obliged to abandon the challenge to the assessment of its income - HC dismissed appeal filed against said order - Hence, present appeal - Whether orders levying interest u/s. 139(8) of 1961 Act and u/s. 215 of 1961 Act are appealable u/s. 246 of the 1961 Act? - Held, s. 246 (c) of 1961 Act provides an appeal against an order where the assessee denies his liability to be assessed under the Act or against any assessment order u/ss. 143(3) or 144 , where the assessee objects to the amount of income assessed or to the amount of tax determined or to the amount of loss computed or to the status under which he is assessed - Inasmuch as the levy of interest is a part of the process of assessment, it is open to an assessee to dispute the levy in appeal provided he limits himself to the ground that he is not liable to the levy at all - In the present case, the assessee having made no application to the Income Tax Officer for reduction or waiver of interest u/s. 139(8) of 1961 Act and u/s. 215 of 1961 Act, no question arises of the relevant authority having denied improperly a reduction or waiver of the interest and that being so, no revision petition can be maintained in that regard by the assessee before the Commissioner - Order of the Commissioner affirmed - Appeal dismissed.
