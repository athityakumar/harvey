Assistant Collector of Central Excise and Others v Madras Rubber Factory Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

20 December 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Bhagwati, V. Khalid

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 682; (1986) Supp SCC 751; (1987) SCC (Tax) 115; AIR 1987 SC 701; 1987 (10) ECR 625; 1987 (27) E.L.T. 553; JT 1987 (1) SC 41; 1986(2) SCALE 1239; [1987] 1 S.C.R. 846

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Discount, Trade Discount, Wholesale Cash Price

Summary: Excise - Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944, ss. 4, 4(4)(d)(ii) - Central Excise (Valuation) Rules, 1975, r.6A - Excise Duty - Post-manufacturing expenses under the new s. 4 - Sale of tyres and other rubber products through depots throughout India as wholesale sales - (A) Determination of entitlement to deductions under r. 6A of the 1975 Rules - Whether the following deductions arising for consideration of Court are allowable: (1) TAC/Warranty discount - (2) Product discounts - (3) Interest on Finished goods and stocks carried by the manufacturer after clearance - (4) Overriding commission to Hindustan Petroleum Corporation - (5) Cost of distribution incurred at duty paid Sales Depots - (6) Interest on receivables (sundry debtors for sales) - (7) 1 per cent turnover discount allowed to RCS dealers - (8) Secondary packing cost on tread rubber - (9) Discount to govt. and other departments - (B) Whether excise duty should be first deducted or the permissible deduction should be first deducted from the selling price for the re-assessments before the Assistant Collectors - (A) Held, (1) Claim for deduction in respect of TAC / Warranty is not allowable - (2) Deduction in respect of year ending discount and campaign bonus are not allowable - (3) Claim is allowable - (4) Claim in respect of cannot be allowed - (5) - Claim allowable - (6) Allowable deduction - (7) - (8) No deduction allowable - (9) Not allowable - (B) Ad valorem excise duty can only be computed after reducing the assessable value by permissible deductions and then applying the tariff rate to the assessable value and to reverse this sequence is to misinterpret the scheme and mode of levy of excise duty on the assessable value - Appeal disposed of.
