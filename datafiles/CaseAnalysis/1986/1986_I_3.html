Indian Oil Corporation v Income Tax Officer, Central Circle V,Calcutta

Supreme Court of India

08 May 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 368; (1986) 3 SCC 409; AIR 1987 SC 1897; 1986 (58) CTR 83; [1986] 159 ITR 956; 1986(1) SCALE 1022; [1986] 2 S.C.R. 1107; [1986] 26 TAXMAN 336; 1986 (2) TLR 928; 1986 (2) UJ 249

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Failure To Disclose, Failure To Disclose Fully And Truly, Management Expenses

Summary: Income Tax and Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961 - Claim for deduction of charges paid by assessee to foreign Company for management and secretarial work carried on on behalf of assesses in London - Reopening of assessment u/s.147(1)(a) for non production of London Auditors' report regarding reasonableness of claim - Whether the reopening of the assessments of the assessee u/s.147(a) of the Indian Income Tax Act, 1961 is valid - Held, not proper - Appeal allowed.
