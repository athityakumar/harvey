Superintendent and Remembrancer of Legal Affairs, West Bengal v Usha Ranjan Roy Choudhury and Another

Supreme Court of India

21 May 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

M.P. Thakkar, V. Balakrishna Eradi

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 54; (1986) Supp SCC 190; (1986) SCC (Cr) 311; (1986) SCC 390; AIR 1986 SC 1655; 1986 (3) Crimes 11; 1986 CRLJ 1248; 1986 CrLR(SC) 309; JT 1986 SC 363; 1986(3) SCALE 931; [1986] 3 S.C.R. 113

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Court Martial

Summary: Criminal - Concurrent Jurisdiction - Army Act of 1950, ss. 52 and 70 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1898,s. 549 - Appellants were army officers and were tried for offences in respect of which both the ordinary criminal court and the court martial had concurrent jurisdiction u/s. 52 of Army Act - Whether the High Court was justified in taking the view that the Special Judge has acted without jurisdiction and as a result of which proceedings are liable to be quashed - Held, yes - The initial lack of jurisdiction to take cognizance and try the case will vitiate the trial and the order of conviction and sentence would be liable to be quashed as a result thereof - No jurisdiction to try the case on merits - provisions of s. 549 are attracted in the present case - Appeal dismissed.
