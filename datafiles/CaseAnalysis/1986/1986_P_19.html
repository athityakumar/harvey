Arya Samaj, Sagar and Others v Pinjamal and Another

Supreme Court of India

08 August 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, K.N. Singh

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 182; (1986) 4 SCC 3; AIR 1986 SC 1789; JT 1986 SC 143; 1986 MPLJ 579; 1987 (1) RCJ 102; 1986 (2) RCR(Rent) 629; 1986 (2) RentLR 442; 1986(2) SCALE 238; [1986] 3 S.C.R. 514; 1986 (2) UJ 483

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - Madhya Pradesh Accommodation Control Act, 1961, s. 12(f) - Dilapidated condition of building - Eviction - Justifiability - Appellant was running a girls' school, being covered by one of its objects - Respondents were in occupation of an old shed as tenant in said house - Appellant needed additional accommodation for said purpose - Building was also in dilapidated condition - Landlord/appellant filed eviction suit against respondents before Trial Court - Trial Court passed eviction order u/s. 12(f) of the Act - On appeal before Appellate Court - Appellate Court upheld order of Trial Court - Aggrieved by said order respondent appealed before HC and same was allowed - Hence instant appeal - Whether order passed by HC was justified - Held, in order to avoid any apprehension, though SC allowed appeal and restored order of Appellate Court as well as Trial Court, SC made it clear that in case if any part of building was used for shops or let out as shops, first option should be given to respondents - Building must be constructed on basis of plan sanctioned and as appended in records of this case - In view of fact that respondents were carrying on business for quite long time, they should had some time to vacate - Respondents were directed to vacate premises in their occupation and handover same to appellant to enable appellant to proceed with construction - Respondent would file an undertaking on usual terms within month - Order of HC was set aside - Orders of Appellate Court and Trial Court were restored subject to modifications indicated above - After obtaining possession of premises from respondents, appellant should proceed to construct as quickly as possible - Appeal allowed.
