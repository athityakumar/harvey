Ashok Gulati And Ors. v B.S. Jain And Ors

Supreme Court of India

17 December 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

A.P. Sen, S. Natarajan

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 243; (1986) Supp SCC 597; AIR 1987 SC 424; 1987 (2) ATC 608; 1987 (1) CCC 378; JT 1987 (1) SC 92; 1987 LabIC 247; 1987 (1) LLN 456; 1986(2) SCALE 1062; [1987] 1 S.C.R. 603; 1987 (1) SLJ 169; 1986 (1) SLR 1

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Punjab Reorganisation Act, 1966, Haryana Service of Engineers, Class I, PWD (Irrigation Branch) Rules 1964 (as amended in 1975), Haryana Service of Engineers Class II (Public Works Department, Irrigation Branch) Rules 1970

Summary: Service - Haryana Service of Engineers, Class I, Public Works Department (Irrigation Branch) Rules, 1964 (as amended in 1975), r. 2(5) - Service Rules - Promotion of some Asst. Engineers on ad hoc basis to post of Executive Engineer - (A) Whether benefits of the period of service rendered as Temporary Engineers on an ad-hoc basis i.e. prior to their appointment as AEs on regular basis can be considered for purposes of promotion to the post of Executive Engineer u/r.6(b) r/w the Expln. thereto, as amended in 1975, as also for purposes of their seniority in the cadre of AAs - (B) Legality of quashing of order of promotion by recourse to r.22 - (C) Interpretation of Statutes - Meaning of word 'as' in r.6(b), Expln. of the Rules - Held, service rendered as temporary Engineers purely as stopgap arrangement cannot be considered - Illegal - In collocation of words any service rendered as a temporary Engineer word 'as' means in the capacity of - Appeal allowed.
