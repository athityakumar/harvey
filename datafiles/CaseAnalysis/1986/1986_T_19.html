Jagdish Sugar Mills Ltd v The C.I.T Lucknow

Supreme Court of India

16 July 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Pathak, S. Mukharji

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 174; (1986) 3 SCC 578; AIR 1986 SC 1742; 1986 (58) CTR 95; [1986] 161 ITR 209; JT 1986 SC 214; 1986(2) SCALE 90; [1986] 3 S.C.R. 198; 1986 TAXLR 1137

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Uttar Pradesh Zamindar Abolition and Land reforms Act 1950 (Uttar Pradesh 1 of 1951), Uttar Pradesh Zamindari Abolition and Land reforms Rules 1950

Summary: Income Taxes & Direct Taxes - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922 - Uttar Pradesh Zamindar Abolition and Land reforms Act 1950 - Auction sale - Double taxation - Liquidation - Written Down Value - Capital Gains - Auction Sale - Public limited company put into liquidation and assessees mills put to auction sale - Income-tax Officer questioned excess amount for which assessee replied that computation of income under cl. (vii) of ss.10(2) and of capital gains u/s.12B amounted to double taxation and was against principles of natural justice - It was claimed value of mills as was much higher than determined and assessee not liable to tax on capital gain - Claim rejected - On appeal , Appellate Assistant Commissioner, by his order agreed with Income-tax Officer that sale attracted cl. (vii) of s.10(2), and assessee liable to capital gains u/s.12B but reduced the capital gains to certain extent - Both Revenue and assessee filed appeals - Appellate Tribunal by its order allowed assessee's appeal and dismissed Revenue appeal - Hence, instant appeal - Whether the transaction amounts to sale - Held, transactions must be treated as sales because element of mutual assent was not excluded altogether from transactions - Transaction constitutes a sale for purposes of cl. (vii) of ss.10(2)- It was apparent that only after sale was confirmed and a certificate was granted that property stands transferred and purchaser becomes owner of property - R. 285-M was explicit - Certificate operates as a transfer of property, assessee relies on s.65 of CPC - properties of assessee fall within scope of cl.(vii) of ss.10 (2)- Appeal dismissed.
