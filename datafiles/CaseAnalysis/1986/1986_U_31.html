Suk Das and Another v Union Territory of Arunachal Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

10 March 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Bhagwati, D.P. Madon, G.L. Oza

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 57; (1986) SCC (Cr) 166; (1986) 2 SCC 401; AIR 1986 SC 991; 1986 ALJ 774; 1986 (12) AWC 567; 1986 BLJR 394; 1986 (2) Crimes 40; 1986 CRLJ 1084; 1986 CrLR(SC) 188; 1986(1) SCALE 368; [1986] 1 S.C.R. 590

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Keywords: Child Abuse

Summary: Criminal - Legal aid - Administration of criminal justice - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 21 - Whether an indigent accused is entitled to free legal aid at expense of state and it is duty of Court to inform him about this right? - Held, yes - Right to free legal service is an essential ingredient of reasonable, fair and just procedure - Implicit in guarantee of art. 21 - It is constitutional right of every accused unable to engage a lawyer due to reasons such as poverty, indigence and State is under a mandate to provide a lawyer to such person - Appeal disposed of.
