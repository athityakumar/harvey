Sodhi Transport Co. And Anr. Etc. Etc. v State of Uttar Pradesh and Another (And Others

Supreme Court of India

20 March 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

E.S. Venkataramiah, M.P. Thakkar

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 136; (1986) SCC (Tax) 410; (1986) 2 SCC 486; AIR 1986 SC 1099; 1986(1) SCALE 489; [1986] 1 S.C.R. 939; 1986 (62) STC 381; 1986 TAXLR 2347; 1986 (2) UJ 147

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Sales Tax

Keywords: Freedom of Trade and Commerce

Summary: Sales Tax - Uttar Pradesh Sales Tax Act, 1948, ss.21, 28, 28A, 28B, 28C - Income Tax Act, 1922, s.23A - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 32, 19(1)(g), 301 - U.P. Sales Tax Rules, 1948, r.87 - Constitutionality of s.28B the and r.87 of the Sales tax Act and Rules respectively - And whether transporter is a dealer -Provisions are introduced to check evasion of tax and to provide a machinery for levying tax from persons (transporters) who dispose of goods inside the State and avoid tax which they are otherwise liable to pay - Held, not unconstitutional and held that transporter is dealer, if he sells goods inside the State - Petition disposed of.
