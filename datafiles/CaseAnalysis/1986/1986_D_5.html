Smt. Dhanwanti v D.D. Gupta

Supreme Court of India

09 May 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Pathak, S. Mukharji

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 320; (1986) 3 SCC 1; AIR 1986 SC 1184; 1986 (30) DLT 179; 1987 (1) RCJ 40; 1986 (2) RCR(Rent) 290; 1986 (2) RentLR 320; 1986(1) SCALE 1109; [1986] 3 S.C.R. 18; 1986 (2) UJ 92

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - Delhi Rent Control Act, 1958, s. 21 - Evidence Act, 1872, s.114 - Constitution, art.136 - Tenancy - Limited lease with genuine permisssion u/s. 21 of the Act - Expiry - Tenant, not vacating - Genuine requirement - Proceedings u/s21 of Delhi Rent Control Act - Gross misconstruction of facts and circumstances, ignorance of material evidence on record and inferences drawn, unreasonable and illegal - (A) Application for possession - (B) Again permission sought for another limited period if infer that premises were available for letting out indefinitely - (C) Powers of SC to interfere, art. 136 of Constitution, findings of fact - Held, tenant must deliver possession to landlady - No inference on that count could be drawn that from the very beginning the premises were available for letting out indefinitely - SC, in such a case must interfere - Appeal allowed.
