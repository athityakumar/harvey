Indian Oil Corporation Limited v State of Bihar and Others

Supreme Court of India

13 August 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Balakrishna Eradi, M.M. Dutt

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 10; (1986) 4 SCC 146; (1986) SCC (L&S) 740; AIR 1986 SC 1780; 1986 (2) CCC 898; [1987] 62 Comp Cas 541; 1987 (27) E.L.T. 578; 1986 (53) FLR 518; [1987] 167 ITR 897; JT 1986 SC 132; 1987 (1) LLJ 17; 1986 (2) LLN 1047; 1986 (2) RCR(Rent) 442; 1986(2) SCALE 233; [1986] 3 S.C.R. 553; 1987 (1) SLJ 94; 1987 (66) STC 408; 1986 (2) UJ 704

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Sales Tax - Constitution of India 1950, art. 136 and 226 - Whether dismissal in limine of a Special Leave Petition (SLP) filed before this Court by a party challenging award of a Labour Court would preclude said party from subsequently approaching High Court u/art. 226 of Constitution seeking to set aside said award? - Held, that dismissal of SLP, by a non-speaking order, in limine, by Supreme Court, will not preclude a party from seeking same relief u/art. 226 on identical grounds - Further held, HC also cannot presume that issues involved must have been decided by implication by Supreme Court - Appeal allowed.
