Mrs. Elizabeth Dinshaw v Arvand M. Dinshaw And Anr.

Supreme Court of India

11 November 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Balakrishna Eradi, G.L. Oza

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 219; (1987) SCC (Cr) 13; (1987) 1 SCC 42; AIR 1987 SC 3; 1987 (1) Crimes 71; 1987 CRLR 117; 1987 CrLR(SC) 117; 1987 (1) HLR 416; JT 1986 SC 795; 1986(2) SCALE 745; [1987] 1 S.C.R. 175

Case Digest

Subject: Women & Children

Summary: Constitution of India,1950; Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s.13; Guardians And Wards Act, 1890,s.7 - A minor child born of Indian father and American mother was an American citizen - Divorce of the parents - Custody and guardianship entrusted to the mother by a competent Court in U.S.A - Father given visitation rights - Father abducted the minor child illegally in India - Writ petition filed by the mother for custody of the minor - Whether entitled - Held, that the mother loved the Child and she could be safely trusted to look after him, educate him and give him proper up-bringing - The Child has not taken root in India as he was born in U.S.A. - The Child's presence in India is the result of an illegal act of abduction - father is guilty - Cannot claim any advantage- It is the duty of Courts in all countries to see that a parent doing wrong by removing Children out of their country did not gain any advantage by his or her wrong doing.
