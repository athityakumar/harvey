J.B. Chopra and Others v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 December 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

A.P. Sen

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 12; (1987) 1 SCC 422; AIR 1987 SC 357; 1987 (2) ATC 344; 1987 (28) E.L.T. 3; 1987 (54) FLR 297; JT 1986 SC 1104; 1987 LabIC 466; 1987 (1) LLJ 255; 1987 (1) LLN 307; 1986(2) SCALE 1097; 1987 (1) SLR 203; 1987 (1) UJ 128

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Constitution - Central Hindi Directorate (Class III and Class IV) Posts Recruitment Rules, 1961 - Administrative Tribunals Act, 1985 - Determination of authority and jurisdiction of the Central Administrative Tribunal constituted under the Administrative Tribunals Act, 1985 to strike down as constitutionally invalid a rule (amended r. 4 of 1961 Rules) framed by the President of India under the proviso to art. 309 of the Constitution as being violative of arts.14 and 16(1) of the Constitution - Held, it is competent - Petition dismissed.
