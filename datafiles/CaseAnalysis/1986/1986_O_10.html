Commissioner of Income Tax, Orissa v Orissa Corporation Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

19 March 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 281; (1986) Supp SCC 110; AIR 1986 SC 1849; 1986 (2) CCC 78; 1986 (52) CTR 138; [1986] 159 ITR 78; 1986(1) SCALE 446; [1986] 1 S.C.R. 979; 1986 TAXLR 864; [1986] 25 TAXMAN 80; 1986 (2) UJ 132

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1961, 256(2) - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 271(1)(c) r/w 274(2) - Assessment - Undisclosed Income - Endorsement - Imposition of penalty - Appeal was related to an assessment order whereby additions were made to quantum of income disclosed by assessee and other was in respect of imposition of penalty u/s. 271(1)(c) r/w 274(2) of 1922 Act - Assessee/private limited company and maintained accounts according to the calendar year - For the accounting year ending, corresponding to assessment year 1962-63, Income-tax Officer did not accept assessee's accounts showing cash credit of Rs. 1,50,000 - Three accounts were shown to have been received by way of loans from three individual creditors of Hundis - Assessee produced before Income- tax Officer letters of confirmation, discharged Hundis and particulars of different creditors general index numbers were with Income-tax Department - Attempts made to bring those creditors - Income-tax Officer by issue of notices u/s. 131 of the Act, but notices were returned with endorsement left - Income-tax Officer treated the entire amount of R. 1,50,000 as unproved cash credit and added same to income of assessee - Assessment order proceeding was taken u/s. 271(1)(c) of the Act and Inspecting Assistant Commissioner imposed a penalty of Rs. 50,000 - An appeal against imposition of penalty was also filed before Tribunal could not sustain imposition of penalty and deleted such imposition - Revenue sought for statement of case on both these aspects of addition of Rs. 1,50,000 to the total income of the assessee and also on the imposition of penalty - Tribunal refused to refer any statement of case to HC - Hence instant appeals - Whether Tribunal was right in shifting onus assessee to Revenue in deleting the penalty - Held, assessee had given names and addresses of the alleged creditors - Revenue said creditors were income-tax assessees - Apart from issuing notices u/s. 131 at the instance of assessee, did not pursue matter - Revenue did not examine the source of income of alleged creditors to find out the alleged loans - There was no effort made to pursue the so called alleged creditors - Hundis racket in which assessee was alleged to have been involved was not proved - That was only suspicion of Revenue - Penalty aspect depended on quantum aspect - Appeals dismissed.
