Manmohan Singh Jaitla and another v The Commissioner. Union Territory Chandigarh And Ors. Etc.

Supreme Court of India

19 December 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

D.A. Desai, V. Khalid

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 251; (1984) Supp SCC 540; (1985) SCC (L&S) 269; AIR 1985 SC 364; 1985 LabIC 672; 1985 (1) LLJ 514; 1985 (1) LLN 954; 1984(2) SCALE 991; [1985] 2 S.C.R. 479; 1985 (1) SLJ 90; 1985 (1) SLR 505; 1985 UJ 411

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Service - Punjab Aided (Schools Security of Service) Act, 1969, s. 3 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 12, 32, 226 and 227 - Aided High School - (A) Headmaster and Drawing Teacher - Terminated without enquiry - In violation of s. 3, by taking recourse to a clause in service agreement - Ground, they were appointed by outgoing management - Validity - (B) Maintainabiility of writ petition challenging approval of termination of services of teacher by statutory authorities u/s. 3 of Act as extended to Chandigarh - (C) Aided School receiving grant up to 95% of its expenditure - Teachers receiving protection under Act - Whether school subject to regulations made by Education Department comes under jurisdiction of High Court - Held, action mala fide and order liable to be quashed, clause in agreement liable to be ignored - Is maintainable - It is other authority u/art. 12 and amenable to HC's writ jurisdiction - Appeal allowed.
