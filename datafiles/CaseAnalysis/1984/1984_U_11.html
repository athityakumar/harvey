Manick Chand Paul And Others Etc. v Union Of India And Others

Supreme Court of India

17 April 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

V.D. Tulzapurkar, V. Balakrishna Eradi, D.P. Madon

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 296; (1984) 3 SCC 65; (1984) SCC (Cr) 389; AIR 1984 SC 1249; 1984 CRLR 475; 1984 CrLR(SC) 475; 1985 ECR 514; 1984 (18) E.L.T. 185; 1984(1) SCALE 772; [1984] 3 S.C.R. 461; 1984 UJ 1072

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Customs; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Custom

Summary: Customs - Gold (Control) Act, 1968, s. 14, 16(7), 27(6), 27(7)(b), 52, 55, 56, 79, 80, 100 and 114 - Gold Control (Licensing of Dealers) Rules, 1969, rr. 2 and 3 - Gold Control (Identification of Customs) Rules, 1969, r. 3 - Gold Control (Forms, Fees and Miscellaneous Matters) Rules, 1968, r. 11 - Constitution, art. 14, 19(1)(g), 301, 302 and 245 - Licensed dealers, are challenging the constitutional validity of the Gold (Control) Act, 1968 and in particular the provisions contained in ss. 2(p), 16, 27 (as amended), 44, 48, 52, 79 and 100 (as amended) and the Gold Control (Forms, Fees and Miscellaneous Matters) Rules, 1968 (as amended in 1975/1976) and the Gold Control (Identification of Customers) Rules, 1969 as being violative of their fundamental rights u/arts.14 and 19(l)(g) and are seeking suitable directions restraining the respondents from giving effect to any of those provisions - Specifically dealing : (1) The first provision that has been challenged is s. 16(7) of the Act - Requirement of making declaration u/s.16 (7) by licensed dealer or refiner in respect of gold - Held, provision is not violative of art. 14 or 19 (1) (g) of Constitution; challenge to the constitutionality of s.16(7) must fail - (2) The next provision challenged is s.52 of the Act which provides for licence issued to a firm becoming invalid if there is any change in the partnership of the firm - It is difficult to accept that s.52 suffers from the vice of excessive delegation of legislative power or for that reason the said provision is unconstitutional - (3) The next provision that has been challenged is s.79 r/w second proviso thereto - Whether power to extend of period by Collector is arbitrary or suffers from lack of guidelines - Held, power to extend the initial period or the extended period must be exercised subject to the observance of the aforesaid two safeguards; challenge to s.79 and the second proviso thereto has to fail - (4) The next provision challenged is s.100 of the Act (as amended) r/w r.3(1) of the Gold Control (Identification of Customers) Rules, 1969, on the ground that the said provision is incapable of compliance in a practical sense and from a commercial point of view and has the effect of ruining the business of the petitioners and since the said Rule 3(1) unreasonably restricts the right of the petitioners to carry on their business including their inter-state trade the same is violative of arts.19(l)(g), 301 and 302 of the Constitution - Obligation cast on Licensed dealer to satisfy about identity of person from whom gold is purchased - Validity of the amended s.100 r/w r.3(1) upheld; requirement is not violative of arts. 19 (1) (g), 301 or 302 - (5) lastly, grievance against the amended prescribed Forms Nos. G.S. 11 and G.S. 12 required to be maintained u/s.55 of the Act r/w r.11 of 1968 Rules - Contention has been that the old Forms were better but the new Forms lack in providing adequate or proper columns - Held, either the new Forms will have to be suitably revised or the old Forms could again be revived and meanwhile no action penal or otherwise would be taken against licensed dealers for failure to maintain accounts in the amended Forms G.S. 11 and G.S. 12 - (B) Petitioners challenging the GOI's Letter of Instructions and the Trade Notices withdrawing the facility of permitting licensed dealers to send ornaments for sale through their traveling salesmen as being violative of the constitutional guarantee u/art.301 as also their fundamental rights u/arts. 14 and 19(l)(g) of the Constitution - Held, s.27 (7) (b), which confines a licensed dealer to carry on business as such dealer to the premises specified in his licence, being regulatory in character does not violate any of his rights under the Constitution - Petitions dismissed.
