Deepak Pahwa and Others v Lt. Governer of Delhi and Others

Supreme Court of India

22 August 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy, A.P. Sen, E.S. Venkataramiah

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 316; (1984) 4 SCC 308; AIR 1984 SC 1721; 1985 (1) CCC 173; 1984(2) SCALE 239; [1985] 1 S.C.R. 588; 1985 (9) ShimLC 240; 1985 UJ 237

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Keywords: Simultaneous

Summary: Administrative - Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Land Acquisition Act, 1894 ss. 4, 5A, 6, 17 - Land acquisition notification - Dismissal of appeal - Maintainability - Respondent/Govt. issued combined notification u/ss. 4, 17 and declaration u/s. 6 of the Act were published in Delhi Extraordinary Gazette on 18-6-1984 in regard to acquisition of certain lands of petitioners/land owners - Public notice of substance of notification u/s. 4 was given in locality on 17-7-1984 - Writ Petition was filed by petitioners in HC impugning notification and declaration on 2 grounds, 1st was that delay of 29 days in giving public notice of substance of notification in locality after publication of notification u/s. 4 in Gazette was fatal to notification itself - Ground no. 2 was that very circumstance that period of 8 years was spent in interdepartmental discussion showed that there was no urgency necessitating invocation of s. 17(4) of the Act to dispense with enquiry u/s. 5A of the Act - HC dismissed Writ Petition in limine - Hence instant Special Leave petitions - Whether rejection of grounds pleaded in appeal by HC was justified - Held, if there was publication in Gazette and if there was public notice in locality, requirements of s. 4 (1) of the Act should be held to be satisfied unless two were unlinked from each other by gap of time so large as might lead one to prima facie conclusion of lack of bona fides in proceedings for acquisition - Further, period of 30 days might be reckoned from either date of publication in gazette or date of public notice of substance of notification in locality, whichever was later, that was only reasonable and practical way of construing s. 5-A of the Act so as to advance object of that provision, which was to provide reasonable opportunity to interested persons to oppose acquisition - Thus, s. 5A of the Act did not refer either to date of publication in official Gazette or date of public notice of substance of notification in locality, it speaks of 'the issue of the notification' - Words 'the issue of the notification' could only signify completion of prescribed process, rather, twin process, of notifying interested public of proposed acquisition in manner provided for by s. 4(1) of the Act, that was by publication in official Gazette and giving public notice in locality - Therefore, both submissions of petitioners were rejected and special leave petitions were dismissed- Petitions dismissed.
