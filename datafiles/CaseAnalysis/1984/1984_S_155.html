Union of India v State of Rajasthan

Supreme Court of India

04 September 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

E.S. Venkataramiah, A.P. Sen, O. Chinnappa Reddy

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 137; (1984) 4 SCC 238; AIR 1984 SC 1675; 1984 (10) ALR 678; [1985] 1 S.C.R. 700

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Civil Procedure; Defence & Security Forces; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Animals, Railway Act 1890

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Civil Procedure - Railway Act 1890, s. 80 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 131 - Whether suit filed by State of Rajasthan against Union of India for compensation, for loss on account of damage caused to goods despatched through Indian Railways in a civil court at Rajasthan, is maintainable or whether it should be filed in Supreme Court u/art. 131? - Art. 131 is attracted only when a dispute is between or amongst states and Union in context of constitutional relationship that exists between them and powers, rights, duties, immunities, liabilities and disabilities etc. flowing therefrom - A dispute arising between a state in capacity of a consumer of railway services etc. like any other private party on one hand and Union of India on other cannot be construed as a dispute arising between state and Union in discharge of their respective executive powers attracting art. 131 - Held, State Govt. has made a claim, like any other consignee of goods despatched through railway, for compensation and its success or failure depends on proof of facts to be established as a private person would do - Claim involved is one based on s. 80 - Union of India is impleaded as a party as being owner of Indian Railways by virtue of art. 300 - Act treats dispute as one between Railway Administration and person instituting suit - Petition dismissed.
