M. P. Mittal v State Of Haryana And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

10 October 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Pathak, M.P. Thakkar

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 218; (1984) 4 SCC 371; AIR 1984 SC 1888; 1985 (2) CCC 874; 1985 (1) CompLJ 190; 1984(2) SCALE 555; [1985] 1 S.C.R. 940; 1985 UJ 22

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Corporate

Summary: Corporate - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Writ petition u/art. 226 against order of recovery - Shares of the Company underwritten by Industrial Corporation - Managing director gauranteed the payment of dividend in personal capacity - But failed to do so - Corporation try to recover it as land revenue - Opportunity to discharge liability provided by Assistant Collector - Before adopting coercive measures - Petition filed - Dismissal in limine - Whether the dismissal of petition is proper in the absence of any reason - Dismissal held proper - But HC should set forth brief statement of reasons for its order - Especially when the controversy is the subject of judicial examination for the first time - Appeal dismissed.
