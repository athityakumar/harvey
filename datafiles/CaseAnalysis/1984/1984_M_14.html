Chingleput Bottlers v Majestic Bottling Company

Supreme Court of India

15 March 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

A.P. Sen, A. Varadarajan

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 333; (1984) 3 SCC 258; AIR 1984 SC 1030; 1984 CRLR 617; 1984 CrLR(SC) 617; 1984(1) SCALE 832; [1984] 3 S.C.R. 190; 1984 UJ 570

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 266 - Prohibition Act, 1937, ss. 17B, 17C, 54 - T.N. Arrack (Manufacture) Rules, 1981, r. 7 -Commission refused to grant liquor licence ( a discretionary matter) u/r.7 of 1981 Rules on the basis of some information - HC quashing order of rejection of one of rival applications for - Issues arised as to: (A) Jurisdiction of High Court to issue writ of mandamus and certiorari - Mandamus ordaining the Commissioner to grant a licence u/ r. 7 of the Rules without the prior approval of the State Govt. which was a condition pre-requisite for the grant of such privilege - (B)Whether commissioner is bound to disclose source of information on basis o which licence refused - Held- High Court exceeded its jurisdiction in issuing a writ of mandamus as absence of a specific plea in nature of demurrer would not invest the High Court with jurisdiction - Commissioner is not bound to disclose sources of information or gist of information on which refusal was based - Appeal no. C.As. 11970-71/83 dismissed; Appeal no. C.A.11972/83 allowed.
