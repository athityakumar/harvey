Indian Express Newspapers (Bombay) Private Limited v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

06 December 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

E.S. Venkataramiah, O. Chinnappa Reddy, A.P. Sen

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 1; (1985) SCC (Tax) 121; (1985) 1 SCC 641; AIR 1986 SC 515; 1985 (3) CompLJ 115; 1985 CrLR(SC) 79; 1999 (110) E.L.T. 3; [1986] 159 ITR 556; 1984(2) SCALE 853; [1985] 2 S.C.R. 287; 1985 TAXLR 2451

Case Digest

Subject: Customs

Keywords: Auxiliary Duty, Finance Act, 1972, Constitutionality, Finance Act, 1981, Finance Act, 1973

Summary: Customs - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 19(1)(a), 19(1)(g), 19(2), 19(6), 226, 285 and 245, Sch.7, List 1, entry 97 - Customs Act, 1962, s. 25(1) - Customs Tariff Act, 1975 - (A) Constitution - (1) Freedom of speech expression - Includes freedom of Press - Duty of court - (2) Determination of validity of law alleged to contravene art. 19(1)(a) - Consideration of American decisions - (3) Pattern of cls. (a) and (g) is different from that of First Amendment to American Constitution - First Amendment is almost absolute - (4) Newspaper industry - Sale and purchase of newspaper and advertisements therein - Exemption from taxation - (5) Object of freedom of expression - Test to determine ,validity of Statute taxing newsprint - (B) Customs Act - (1) Notification u/s. 25 of Act - A piece of subordinate legislation - Test, on question of its being unreasonable i.e. manifestly arbitrary - (2)Issue of notification granting, modifying or withdrawing exemption from duty on newspapers (3) Levy of customs duty on newspaper - Wherther classification as small, medium and big newspaper for purpose of levy is violative of art. 14 - (A) Court's duty is to uphold it and, invalidate all laws and administrative actions interfering with it - American decisions though not sole guides can be taken into consideration for understanding basic principles of freedom of speech and expression - Rights u/cls. (a) and (g) are to be r/w. cls. (2) and (6) of art. 19 of Constitution - Held, not granted in express terms by Constitution - Reconciliation of power to impose tax in public interest and protection of free press - (B) Further held, subject to certain exceptions, notification is not beyond reach of administrative law - Relevant factors to be considered by Govt. - Not violative of art. 14 - Petition allowed.
