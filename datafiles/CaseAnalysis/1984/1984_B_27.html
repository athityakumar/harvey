Gramophone Company of India Ltd. v Birendra Bahadur Pandey and Others

Supreme Court of India

21 February 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy, E.S. Venkataramiah, R.B. Misra

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 336; (1984) SCC (Cr) 313; (1984) 2 SCC 534; AIR 1984 SC 667; 1985 (1) ARBLR 361; 1984 (1) CompLJ 362; 1984 (2) ECC 142; 22 PTC 547; 1984(1) SCALE 338; [1984] 2 S.C.R. 664; 1984 UJ 475

Case Digest

Subject: Intellectual Property; International

Keywords: Unfair Competition

Summary: Intellectual Property - International - Copyright Act, 1957, s. 53 - Customs Act, 1962, s. 11 - Convention on Transit Trade of Land-locked States, 1965 - Conflicts of law - Private international law - Whether international law is, of its own force, drawn into law of land without aid of a municipal statute and, if, so drawn, it overrides municipal law in case of conflict? - Held, Convention on Transit Trade of Land-locked States and Treaties between two countries, leave either country free to impose necessary restrictions for purpose of protecting industrial, literary or artistic property and preventing false marks, false indications of origin or other methods of unfair competitions in order to further other general conventions therefore it is not necessary that land-locked country should be a party to general Convention along with transit country - (B) What does word 'import' mean in s. 53 of Copyright Act? - Held, 'import' in ss. 51 and 53 of Copyright Act means 'bringing into India from outside India', that it is not limited to importation for commerce only but includes importation for transit across country - Appeal allowed.
