Ajoy Kumar Banerjee and Others v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

21 March 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, Y.V. Chandrachud, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 332; (1984) 3 SCC 127; (1984) SCC (L&S) 355; AIR 1984 SC 1130; 1984 (48) FLR 448; 1984 LabIC 691; 1984 (1) LLJ 368; 1984 (2) LLN 1; 1984(1) SCALE 539; [1984] 3 S.C.R. 252; 1984 (2) SLR 50; 1984 UJ 500

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Sick Textile Undertakings (Nationalisation) Act, 1974, Life Insurance Corporation (Modification of Settlements) Act, 1976, Life Insurance Corporation (Amendment) Act, 1981, New India Assurance Company Limited (Merger) Scheme, 1973

Summary: Constitution - Insurance - Constitution of India, 1950, arts.14 and 246 - General Insurance Business (Nationalisation) Act, 1972, Pre. and ss. 16(1)(g)(j), 16(2), 16(6), 16(1), 16(5) and 16(7) - General Insurance (Rationalization and Revision of Pay Scales and other Conditions of Service of Supervisory, Clerical and Subordinate Staff) Second Amendment Scheme, 1980 - On Nationalization of general insurance business scheme introduced by govt. regarding pay scales for employees - (A) Validity of Scheme Challenge on the basis of Constitutionality: arbitrary, discriminatory, violative of fundamental rights u/arts. 14, 19(1)(g) and 31 and beyond legislative authority and other ancillary grounds - Supreme Court held that impugned scheme of 1980 as bad as beyond the scope of the authority of the Central Govt. under 1972 Act - (B) General Insurance Business (Nationalisation) Act, 1972 placed under Ninth sch. of Constitution - Whether rights conferred on employees before placing in sch. 9, or making scheme are affected - Rights of employees are not affected or abridged by s.16 (5), (7) - Petition allowed.
