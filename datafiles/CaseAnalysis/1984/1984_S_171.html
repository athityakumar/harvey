Ram Sumer Puri Mahant v State of Uttar Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

17 December 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

D.A. Desai, Ranganath Misra

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 15; (1985) SCC (Cr) 98; (1985) 1 SCC 427; 1985 ACC 45; AIR 1985 SC 472; 1985 ALJ 240; 1985 (2) AllCriC 45; 1985 BBCJ 37; 1985 BLJ 409; 1985 CRLJ 752; 1985 CRLR 1; 1994 (3) RCR(Rent) 217; 1984(2) SCALE 1014

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Practice and Procedure - Criminal Procedure Code, 1974, s. 145 - Whether initiation of criminal proceedings while civil litigation is pending, justified? - Held, no - Initiation of a parallel criminal proceeding under s. 145 of Code, would not be justified - Particularly when possession is being examined by civil Court and parties are in a position to approach civil court for interim orders such as injunction or appointment of receiver for adequate protection of property during pendency of dispute - Appeal allowed.
