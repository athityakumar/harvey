Koshal Kumar Gupta & Ors. v State Of J. & K. And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

05 April 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

D.A. Desai, A.P. Sen, V. Balakrishna Eradi

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 152; (1984) SCC (L&S) 337; (1984) 2 SCC 652; AIR 1984 SC 1056; 1984 (10) ALR 616; 1984(1) SCALE 661; [1984] 3 S.C.R. 407

Case Digest

Subject: Education

Summary: Education - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Admission to Engineering College - Challenge is to the manner, the method and the number of marks assigned for the viva- voce test - Reservation of 15 marks for viva-voce test, if arbitrary - Viva splited up into four heads - Only 3 marks assiged to personality test - Held, merit had been ascertained by the most scientific method that can be applied for selecting candidates on merits leaving room for any arbitrary choice - Petition dismissed.
