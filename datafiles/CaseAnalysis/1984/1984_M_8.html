Commissioner Of Income Tax, Andhra Pradesh v M:. Chandra Sekhar

Supreme Court of India

04 December 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Pathak, V.D. Tulzapurkar

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 266; (1985) SCC (Tax) 85; (1985) 1 SCC 283; AIR 1985 SC 114; 1985 (44) CTR 110; [1985] 151 ITR 433; 1984(2) SCALE 973; [1985] 2 S.C.R. 215; 1985 TAXLR 497; [1985] 20 TAXMAN 3; 1985 UJ 262

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income-tax Act, 1961 - Finance Act, 1963 - Charging of interest - Penalty - Levy of interest - Waive the interest - Respondent/assessee was a partner in a firm 'M' filed voluntary returns for assessment years - On account of delay in filing returns Income Tax officer treated assessee as being in default and imposed penalties - Assessee contended that as returns had been furnished before end of four years from the end of relevant assessment years and that he was not liable to any penalty - Appellate Tribunal allowed appeals and cancelled penalties - HC answered reference in favour of assessee - Hence, instant appeal - Whether Tribunal was justified in concluding that charging of interest and sufficient cause for delay in filing return of income - Held, that HC was right in answering in favour of assessee - Penalty was attracted if Income Tax officer was satisfied that assessee had without reasonable cause failed to furnish returns within time allowed - Time allowed for furnishing a voluntary return was time specified in s.139(1) - When Income Tax officer extends date, he does so in exercise of authority conferred by statute and additional time available to assessee consequent upon such extension was for all relevant purposes of same character and as effective as statutory period specifically enacted by Parliament - For purpose of furnishing a return it constitutes an integral part of allowed for furnishing a return - Therefore, Income Tax Officer extends date and time upto that date was time allowed for furnishing return - Additional period falls within expression the time allowed u/s. 271 of 1961 Act - Appeals disposed of.
