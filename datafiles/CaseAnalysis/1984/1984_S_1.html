(1) Bhagirath Kanoria and Others; (2) Bahadur Singh; (3) Raja Bahadur Singh v (1) State of Madhya Pradesh and Others; (2) Provident Fund Inspector and Others

Supreme Court of India

24 August 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.V. Chandrachud, O. Chinnappa Reddy

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 315; (1984) 4 SCC 222; (1984) SCC (Cr) 590; AIR 1984 SC 1688; 1984 (3) CompLJ 49; 1984 (2) Crimes 441; 1984 CRLR 391; 1984 (49) FLR 382; 1984 LabIC 1578; 1994 (3) LLJ 765; 1984 (2) LLN 731; 1984(2) SCALE 218; [1985] 1 S.C.R. 626

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Sick Textile Undertakings (Nationalisation) Act, 1974

Summary: Labour and Industrial Law - Employees' Provident Funds Act, 1952 - CrPC, 1973 ss. 468, 472 and 473 - Nature of offence - Whether failure to pay the employers' contribution to the Provident Fund is continuing offence - Limitation u/s. 468 inapplicable - Held, is a continuing offence and, therefore, the period of limitation prescribed by s.468 of Code cannot have any application - Appeals dismissed.
