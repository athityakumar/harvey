Union Of India v H.R. Patankar And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

14 August 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Bhagwati, R.S. Pathak, Amarendra Nath Sen

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 182; (1984) Supp SCC 359; (1985) SCC (L&S) 19; AIR 1984 SC 1587; 1985 (1) CCC 232; 1985 (51) FLR 186; 1984 LabIC 1553; 1984(2) SCALE 172; [1985] 1 S.C.R. 400; 1984 (2) SLJ 358; 1984 (3) SLR 136; 1984 UJ 1118

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: (Regulation of Seniority) Rules 1954 (as amended on April 11 and August 13 1958)

Summary: Service - Indian Administrative Service (Regulations of Seniority) Rules, 1954, r. 4(3) - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 73, 309, Proviso and 311 - Notifications D/- 11-04-1958 and 13-08-1958 - Service Rules - High Court striking down the seniority list - (A) Determination of extent of executive power of Union when statutory rules are silent on particular subject - (B) What principle must be followed determining inter s e seniority between IAS officers directly recruited through a competitive examination (before amendment notification D/- 11-04-1958 ) and officers promoted from the State Civil Service and having the same year of allotment - Held, govt. can fill in lacuna by making executive order - Only fair and just principle could be applied was to regard direct recruits through a competitive examination as senior to promotees from the State Civil Service - Appeal dismissed.
