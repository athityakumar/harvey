Union Of India And Another v G.M. Kokil And Others

Supreme Court of India

21 March 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

V.D. Tulzapurkar, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 130; (1984) Supp SCC 196; (1984) SCC (L&S) 631; AIR 1984 SC 1022; 1984 (48) FLR 405; 1984 LabIC 663; 1984 (2) LLJ 20; 1984 (2) LLN 240; 1984(1) SCALE 521; [1984] 3 S.C.R. 292; 1984 (1) SLJ 675; 1984 UJ 534

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour and Industrial Law - Maharashtra Factories Rules, 1963, r. 100 - Factories Act, 1948, ss. 2(1), 59 and 64 - Bombay Shops and Establishments Act, 1948, s. 70 - Overtime - Whether employees were entitled to over-time wages at twice the normal rate of their wages u/s. 59 of the Factories Act 1948 r/w s. 70 of the Bombay Shops and Establishments Act, 1948 - Labour Court taking the view that because of the non-obstante clause s. 64 r/w r.100 itself would not apply and they would be entitled to claim overtime wages - Held, entitled at twice of normal rate of wages in view of s. 70 - Held, s. 64 and r. 100 framed thereunder do not apply - Appeal dismissed.
