Union Of India v Bidhubhushan Malik And Others

Supreme Court of India

24 April 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy, A.P. Sen, E.S. Venkataramiah

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 143; (1984) 3 SCC 95; (1984) SCC (L&S) 354; AIR 1984 SC 1177; 1984(1) SCALE 643A; [1984] 3 S.C.R. 550

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Service

Keywords: High Court Judges (Conditions of Service) Act, 1954, High Court Judges (Conditions Of Service) Amendment Act, 1976

Summary: Advocates and Judges - Service - High Court Judges (Conditions of Service) Act, 1954, Sch.I, Para 10 - High Court Judges (Conditions of Service) (Amendment) Act, 1976 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Pension - Constitutionality of words 'and who has retired on or after 1st day of October 1974' under para 10 sch.I of the Act - High Court hold it unconstitutional and are struck down - Special leave petition - Omitting the unconstitutional part Supreme Court declared that the Judges (including t he Chief Justices) of the High Court are entitled to pension as computed under the Act, (as amended) irrespective of the date of retirement - And no question of payment of arrears of pension for the period preceding 1-10-1974 i.e. date from which the liberalised pension became operative under the Act, irrespective of the date of retirement - Petition dismissed.
