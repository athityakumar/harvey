Jiwani Devi Paraki v First Land Acquisition Collector, Calcutta and Others

Supreme Court of India

30 August 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, V.D. Tulzapurkar, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 200; (1984) 4 SCC 612; AIR 1984 SC 1707; 1984(2) SCALE 274; [1985] 1 S.C.R. 686; 1985 UJ 251

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, s. 49 - West Bengal Premises Requisition and Control (Temporary Provisions) Act, 1947 - Whether order of requisition has been passed for extraneous purpose and is arbitrary and malafide? - Held, exercise of power was not bad initially, nor would continuance of requisition become mala fide or colourable by mere lapse of time - Therefore Govt. is directed to take steps to acquire premises in question by procedure prescribed in s. 49(1) of Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - Application disposed of.
