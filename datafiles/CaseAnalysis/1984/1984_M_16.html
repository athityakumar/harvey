N. Sivammal and Others v Managing Director, Pandian Roadways Corporation and Another

Supreme Court of India

24 September 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

D.A. Desai, Ranganath Misra

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 372; (1985) 1 SCC 18; (1986) SCC (Cr) 235; 1985 ACJ 75; AIR 1985 SC 106; 1985 CRLR 24; 1986 (10) ShimLC 254

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1939, s. 110 - Rash and negligent driving by the driver of passenger bus of a public sector corporation - Victim, process server in the District Court died - LRs of deceased claim compensation against the owner - High Court found the victim died due to the rash and negligent driving of the driver but compensation reduced - Whether HC was right in reducing compensation especially item included under heading mental agony? - Held, deceased had suffered serious injuries because of which he had to suffer from continuous pain - Hence amount of Rs. 5000 which HC held inadmissible, is legitimately admissible under another head and therefore by changing head amount of Rs. 5000 awarded by Tribunal was restored - Further, HC overlooked most obvious fact that monthly pay packet of deceased was Rs. 355 - Victim's receipt per year would be Rs. 4260 - Keeping in view age of deceased at time of his death and taking modicum of 10 years' purchase amount of compensation would work out at Rs. 42,600 to which customary figure of Rs. 5000 for loss to estate has to be added - Thus under these two heads corporation would be liable to pay Rs. 47,600 apart from pain and sufferings - Therefore, HC was clearly in error in further reducing compensation as awarded by Tribunal - Appeal allowed.
