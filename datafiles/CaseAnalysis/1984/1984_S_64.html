Chandra Bansi Singh And Ors. Etc. v State Of Bihar And Ors. Etc.

Supreme Court of India

22 August 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, A. Varadarajan, S. Mukharji

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 189; (1984) 4 SCC 316; AIR 1984 SC 1767; 1984(2) SCALE 235; [1985] 1 S.C.R. 579; 1985 UJ 78

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property

Summary: Constitution - Land and Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4, 16, 23 and 48 - Constitution of India, 1950 , art. 14 - State Govt. acquired a vast tract of land but chose to exempt certain persons from the statutory action on purely unreasonable and illusory grounds - (A) Validity of order of release u/s. 48 of the Act - (B) Delay between date of notification and taking possession - Collector not at fault for causing delay - Land owners enjoying usufruct - Determination of compensation - Held, is non est and violative of art. 14 - Compensation could not be paid according to value of land prevailing on date of actual taking over of possession; value prevalent on date of s. 4 notifications is only relevant; however additional compensation granted on ground of equity - Appeal dismissed.
