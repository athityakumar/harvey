Rohtas Industries Limited and Another Etc v Chairman, Bihar State Electricity Board and Others

Supreme Court of India

02 March 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Balakrishna Eradi, D.A. Desai

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 103; (1984) Supp SCC 161; AIR 1984 SC 657; 1984 BLJR 232; 1984(1) SCALE 465; [1984] 3 S.C.R. 59; 1984 UJ 329

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Electricity & Energy

Keywords: Revised tariff

Summary: Electricity & Energy - Administrative - Notification - Revised tariff - Fuel surcharge - Legality - Respondents/ Board by its tariff notification 1979 published in Gazette Extraordinary No.355 dt. 6-4-1979 issued a revised tariff for all categories of consumers served by it which was to take effect from 1-4-1979 - Appellant/companies filed writ petitions before HC challenging provision contained in Para 16.7 in Tariff Notification of 1979 for levy fuel surcharge was devoid of legal sanction and was arbitrary and void - DB dismissed writ petitions - Hence, instant appeals - Whether supplementary bills issued to appellants by respondents for 'fuel surcharge' as per revised tariff was valid - Held, Board was under a statutory obligation to carry on its operations and adjust its tariffs in such a way as to ensure that total revenues earned in any year of account should after meeting all expenses properly chargeable to revenue, leave such surplus as State Govt. might from time to time specify - However, tariff fixation had therefore, to be so made as to raise sufficient revenue which would not merely avoid any net loss being incurred during financial year but would ensure a profit being earned, rate of minimum profit to be earned being such as might be specified by State Govt. - Further, Board was selling energy at rates which were lower than of Actual cost incurred by it per unit of production - Hence, no basis for contention of appellants that tariff fixation effected by Board suffers from voice of arbitrariness and was liable to be interfered with by Court on ground - In Prag Ice & Oil Mills and another v. Union of India 1978 Indlaw SC 472, SC held that mechanics of price fixation was necessarily to be left to judgment of executive and unless it was patent that there was hostile discrimination against a class of persons, processual basis of price fixation was to be accepted in generality of cases as valid - Appeals dismissed.
