State of Uttar Pradesh v Zavad Zama Khan

Supreme Court of India

04 May 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

A.P. Sen, D.A. Desai, V. Balakrishna Eradi

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 136; (1984) SCC (Cr) 425; (1984) 3 SCC 505; AIR 1984 SC 1095; 1984 (2) Crimes 1; 1984 CRLJ 922; 1984(1) SCALE 938; [1984] 3 S.C.R. 789; 1984 UJ 964

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Defence & Security Forces; Service

Keywords: Defence Regulations, communal harmony, communal violence, communalism

Summary: Criminal - Maintenance of Internal Security Act, 1971 - National Security Act, 1980, ss. 3(3), 11, 8(1)and 14 - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Detention - Due compliance with requirements of art. 22 (5) of Constitution and s. 8(1) of National Security Act - Representation by detenu was duly considered - Rejected by Central Govt. - Held, detention cannot be declared invalid merely because Central Govt. did not apply its mind to subsequent representation.
