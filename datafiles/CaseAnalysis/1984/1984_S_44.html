Jagdisih Prasad v Smt. Angoori Devi

Supreme Court of India

15 March 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranganath Misra, D.A. Desai

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 126; (1984) 2 SCC 590; AIR 1984 SC 1447; 1984 ALJ 379; 1984 (10) ALR 281; 1984 (1) RCJ 485; 1984 (1) RCR(Rent) 524; 1984 (1) RentLR 538; 1984(1) SCALE 502; [1984] 3 S.C.R. 216; 1984 UJ 641

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Civil Procedure; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Rent control - Constitution of India, 1950 - Provincial Small Cause Courts Act, 1887 - Tenancy - Eviction - Appeal against order of eviction - Application for eviction was based on allegation of sub-letting - Whether tenant has allowed shop to be occupied by someone not a member of his family is indisputably a pure question of fact? - Rent Controller looked for evidence from the tenant against sub-letting by assuming from the presence of the son of the proprietor of Pavan Trading Company that there was a sub-tenancy, held against the tenant and directed his eviction from the premises - The Addl. District Judge before whom the tenant's revision petition came for disposal took note of the erroneous approach of the Trial Court and set aside the Order of Rent Controller - The appellate authority, therefore, allowed the revision and reversed the order of eviction and directed dismissal of the petition of the landlord - The landlord filed an application u/art. 227 of the Constitution, 1950 - HC held, that sub-tenancy is a pure question of fact u/s. 25 of the Provincial Small Cause Court Act, not to be disturbed - HC was clearly wrong in reversing the decision of the Addl. District Judge - The application for eviction was based on the allegation of sub-tenancy - The allegation that the premises had been sub-let to Pavan Trading Co. had to be proved as a fact by the landlord and merely on the basis of photograph showing the presence of the son - Merely from presence of person other than tenant in shop sub-letting cannot be presumed - Order passed by HC liable to be set aside - Appeal allowed.
