Sadhuram Bansal v Pulin Behari Sarkar and Others

Supreme Court of India

26 April 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, A. Varadarajan, S. Mukharji

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 327; (1984) 3 SCC 410; AIR 1984 SC 1471; 1984(1) SCALE 997; [1984] 3 S.C.R. 582

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land and Property - Practice and Procedure - CPC, 1908, O. 40, r. 1 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 and Pre. - Dispute over property conveyed through will to various claimants pending over three decades - Legal heirs interested in monetary value - Receiver selling property but higher price offered by 38 dwellers on the property - There rehabilitation is also to be considered - Contract between first offerer and Receiver not concluded - (A) Division bench ordered to convey property to dwellers on payment of certain amount - Validity - Held, in the interest of social justice higher offer of hut-dwellers should be accepted -(B) While administering justice-social or legal, Division Bench of the High Court has shifted away from fine spun technicalities and abstract rules to recognition of human being as human beings and as human needs - Without deprivation of existing legal rights of any party - Interference u/art. 136 of Constitution is justifiable - Held, it is improper for this Court in exercise of the discretion vested under art. 136 of the Constitution to interfere with that decision - Appeal dismissed.
