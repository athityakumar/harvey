Javid Rasool Bhat & Ors. Etc. v State Of Jammu & Kashmir And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

16 February 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy, E.S. Venkataramiah, R.B. Misra

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 105; (1984) SCC (L&S) 309; (1984) 2 SCC 631; AIR 1984 SC 873; 1984(1) SCALE 358; [1984] 2 S.C.R. 582; 1984 (1) SLR 543; 1984 UJ 445

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Tort

Keywords: Criticism

Summary: Education - Jammu and Kashmir Government Medical Colleges (Selection of Candidates for Admission to First Year MBBS Course) Procedure Order, 1983 - Writ petitions questioning admissions to medical colleges at Srinagar and Jammu - Held, fact that marks obtained by candidates in written test were not even available to Selection Committee when they conducted the viva voce test, this circumstance is sufficient to repudiate the broad allegation that viva voce test was designed to facilitate selection of candidates who had fared badly in written test - There is no genuine basis for any complaint in regard to nature of the questions which candidates were asked in the viva voce test - It is now well recognised that while a written examination assesses a candidate's knowledge and intellectual ability, an interview test is valuable to assess a candidate's overall intellectual and personal qualities - Further held, procedure adopted by Selection Committee and member concerned was in accord with quite well-known and generally accepted procedure adopted by Public Service Commissions every where it is not unusual for candidates related to members of Service Commission or other Selection Committee to seek employment - Petitions dismissed.
