Jaspal Singh v Additional District Judge, Bulandshahr And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

28 September 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

R.B. Misra, E.S. Venkataramiah

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 309; (1984) 4 SCC 434; AIR 1984 SC 1880; 1984 (2) RCJ 540; 1985 (2) RCR(Rent) 363; 1985 (1) RentLR 52; 1984(2) SCALE 538; [1985] 1 S.C.R. 889; 1985 UJ 33

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - U.P. Urban Buildings (Regulation of Letting, Rent and Eviction) Act, 1972, ss.14, 3(a) - Shop - Tenancy - Death of tenant without male issue and landlord filed application u/s. 14 r/w s. 16 for declaration of shop as vacant - The application was resisted by his nephew, the present appellant, on the ground that: (A) He was heir and was in occupation - (B) Tenant's will conveying tenancy right in disputed shop - Original tenant alive on commencement of Act - Revision petition by landlord challenging claim of tenancy by heir, pending when 1976 Act came into force - Held, benefit of s. 14 ( both before and after its amendment in 1976 ) not available to heir - And nephew could not be said to be heir of original tenant - Appeal dismissed.
