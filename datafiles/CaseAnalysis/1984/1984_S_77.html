Manchegowda Etc. v State Of Karnataka Etc.

Supreme Court of India

17 April 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

Amarendra Nath Sen, P.N. Bhagwati, Ranganath Misra

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 139; (1984) 3 SCC 301; AIR 1984 SC 1151; 1984(1) SCALE 632; [1984] 3 S.C.R. 502; 1984 UJ 1061

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Evacuee Property & Displaced Persons

Summary: Land and Property - Karnataka Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes (Prohibition of Transfer of Certain Lands) Act, 1979 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 13, 14, 245, 246, 19(1)(f), 31 and 31A - (A) Whether the Karnataka Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes (Prohibition of Transfer of Certain Lands) Act, 1978 is constitutionally valid or not - Constitutionality of ss. 4 and 5 of the Act - (B) Scope of Act as regard to transfer of lands granted before commencement of Act , in absence of contravention of prohibition Held, valid and they are not violative of arts.14,19(1)(f), 31 and 31A - Held, ss.4 and 5, not attracted; Granted lands which had been transferred after the expiry of the period of prohibition do not come within the purview of the Act, and cannot be proceeded against under the provisions of this Act - Appeals dismissed.
