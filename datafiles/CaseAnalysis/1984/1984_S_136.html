Javed Ahmed Abdul Hamid Pawala v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

09 November 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy, E.S. Venkataramiah

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 348; (1984) SCC (Cr) 653; (1985) 1 SCC 275; AIR 1985 SC 231; 1985 (1) Crimes 913; 1984 CRLJ 1909; 1985 CrLR(SC) 35; 1984(2) SCALE 697; [1985] 2 S.C.R. 8

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Constitution - Constitution, art. 21 - IPC, 1860, ss. 302 and 53 - Accused invoked aid of art. 21 - Whether death sentence should be commuted to life imprisonment? Held, execution of death sentence was delayed for more than 2 years - He was only 22 years of age - Genuinely repentant - His conduct and behavior in the jail which was evident from the report of the jail authorities was to the effect that there was nothing adverse against him during the period of his incarceration - He appeared to be genuinely repentant and he desired to atone for the grievous wrong that has been done by him - The death sentence was quashed and in its place life imprisonment was imposed - Petition allowed.
