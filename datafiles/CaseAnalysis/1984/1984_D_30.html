Smt. M.M. Amonkar and Others v Dr.S.A.Johari

Supreme Court of India

21 February 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

V.D. Tulzapurkar, S. Mukharji

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 111; (1984) 2 SCC 354; AIR 1984 SC 931; 1984 (2) Bom.C.R. 441; 1984 (1) RCR(Rent) 389; 1984 (3) RentLR 720; 1984(1) SCALE 381; [1984] 2 S.C.R. 646; 1984 UJ 455

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 227 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872 - Bombay Rent Act , 1947, s. 15A - (A) Whether High Court in exercise of its powers of superintendence u/art. 227 was justified in interfering with a concurrent finding of fact recorded by both Lower Courts in favour of appellants? - (B) Whether respondent was a protected licensee in respect of suit premises under Bombay Rent Act (No 57 of 1947) as amended by Maharashtra Act XVII of 1973? - Held, unless reasons given by Trial Judge could be said to be moon-shine, flimsy or irrational rejection of application cannot be dubbed as suggestive of non-judicial approach or bias or partiality on his part - There was no justification for HC to undertake a re-appreciation of evidence and it ought not to have interfered with concurrent finding of fact recorded by two Courts below on factual issue arising in case - Appeal allowed.
