Ram Nath and Others v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

25 April 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

D.A. Desai, Amarendra Nath Sen, R.B. Misra

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 405; (1984) Supp SCC 96; AIR 1984 SC 1178; 1984(1) SCALE 644; [1984] 3 S.C.R. 572; 1984 UJ 692

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Constitution - Resettlement of Displaced Person (Land Acquisition) Act, 1948, s. 7(1)(e) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 31-B, Whether first and second proviso to s. 7 (1) (e) of 1948 Act is violative of s. 299(2) of Government of India Act, 1935 as it then stood and compensation awarded by arbitrator in consonance with provisos is illusory and therefore both provisos are constitutionally invalid? - Held, protection u/art. 31-B against violation of fundamental rights mentioned therein must extend to rights u/s. 299 of Government of India Act, 1935 also - It is thus satisfactorily established that in view of insertion of 1948 Act in Ninth Sch. it enjoys umbrella of protection of art. 31-B and therefore it is immune from challenged as violating any of provisions in Part III of Constitution of India, 1950 - Once impugned statue or impugned provisions of statue enjoy protection of art. 31-B, it is not open to Court to examine whether principles for valuation therein prescribed are relevant to land valuation because that question arises where a complaint as to contravention of fundamental rights enacted in repealed art. 31 can be entertained and examined - That complaint has to be rejected at threshold as soon as is it pointed out that impugned statute or impugned provisions of statue enjoy protection of art. 31-B - Relied on Union of India v. Mohinder Kaur (1969 Del 1154) wherein it was upheld the constitutional validity of two provisos to s. 7(1)(e) of 1948 Act - No merit - Appeal dismissed.
