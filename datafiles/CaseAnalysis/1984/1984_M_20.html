Manmohan Kalia v Yash & Others

Supreme Court of India

02 April 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, A. Varadarajan, Ranganath Misra

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 104; (1984) 3 SCC 499; AIR 1984 SC 1161; 1984(1) SCALE 621; [1984] 3 S.C.R. 383; 1984 UJ 622

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Election; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Election - Representation of the People Act, 1951, s. 123(4) - IPC, 1860 - (A) Appellant sought the application of the doctrine of inuendo - What is the nature of proof required for applicability of doctrine of inuendo - (B) What is the nature of proof required for alleged corrupt practice - Held, inuendo cannot be proved merely by inferential evidence having two possibilities - And allegation must be proved as strictly as a criminal charge and the principle of preponderance of probabilities would not apply; evidence-both oral and documentary-led by the appellant falls short of the standard of proof required to bring his case within the four corners of s. 123 (4) of the Act ; news item published in newspaper is at best a secondhand secondary evidence - Appeal dismissed.
