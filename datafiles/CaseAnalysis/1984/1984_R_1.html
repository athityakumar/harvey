Prem Chand Jain and Another v R. K. Chhabra

Supreme Court of India

13 February 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranganath Misra, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, A. Varadarajan

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 340; (1984) SCC (Cr) 233; (1984) 2 SCC 302; AIR 1984 SC 981; 1984 CRLJ 668; 1984 CRLR 122; 1984(1) SCALE 279; [1984] 2 S.C.R. 883

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Education; Indirect Tax; Service

Keywords: Education Norms

Summary: Education - University Grants Commission Act, 1956, ss. 2(f), 23 and 24 - Constitution of India, 1950, sch.7 list 1 entry 66 - ' University' is an institution incorporated under Genral Statute is not included u/s. 23 of the 1956 Act - Educational institution incorporated under Companies Act using the word 'University' (despite prohibition of use) under bona fide impression that the company satisfied requirements of Act - Conviction u/s. 24 of the UGC, Act - issues arise : (A) Whether s. 2(f) or s. 23 of the Act are ultra vires the legislative competence of Parliament - (B) Whether conviction can be set aside - Held, provisions not ultravirus - Held, Conviction of accused persons connected with Company u/s. 24 was liable to be set aside - Appeal partly allowed.
