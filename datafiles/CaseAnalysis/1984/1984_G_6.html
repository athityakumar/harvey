Krishi Utpadan Mandi Samiti Kanpur v Ganga Dal Mill and Company and Others

Supreme Court of India

25 September 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

D.A. Desai, V. Balakrishna Eradi, V. Khalid

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 312; (1984) 4 SCC 516; AIR 1984 SC 1870; 1985 (11) ALR 23; 1985 (1) CCC 83; 1984(2) SCALE 518; [1985] 1 S.C.R. 787; 1985 (58) STC 23; 1985 UJ 445

Case Digest

Subject: Trade; Agriculture & Agri. Products

Summary: Agriculture & Agri. Products - Trade - Uttar Pradesh Krishi Utpadan Mandi Adhiniyam, 1964, ss. 2(t), 17 - Agricultural produce - Legume in split form - Legality - Appellant/Market Committee levied market fee on transaction of sale of dal of various legumes by respondents, asserting that they were specified agricultural produce and transactions of sale in respect of them by respondents in Market Area would be exigible to levy of market fee - Respondents filed writ petitions before HC - HC held that legume in its split form was not same thing as legume specified in Schedule and therefore, in absence of a specification, dal of any of legumes enumerated in Schedule could not be said to be specified agricultural produce and any transaction of sale in respect of them was not exigible to levy of market fee - Hence, instant appeals - Whether the whole includes the parts is the core question. Whether legume, whole grain, when notified as a 'specified agricultural produce' u/s. 2(t) of the 'Act would also comprehend its split folds or parts, commercially called dal so as to enable Market Committee to levy market fee u/s. 17 of the Act on transaction of sale of dal of legumes specified in schedule to the Act - Held, analysing definition of expression 'agricultural produce', it would mean not only those items of produce of agriculture as were specified in Schedule, but would also include admixture of two or more of such items as also any such item in its processed form - Therefore, not only Gram was an agricultural produce but Gram in its processed form was equally an agricultural produce - HC was in error in holding that legume whole grain as set out in Schedule did not include its split form i,e. dal and therefore, no market fee was leviable on transaction of sale of legume in split form - Thus, all matters were remitted to HC - Appeals allowed.
