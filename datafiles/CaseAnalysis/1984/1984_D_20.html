Vijay Kumar Rampal and Others v Diwan Devi and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 May 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

D.A. Desai, V. Balakrishna Eradi, R.B. Misra

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 440; AIR 1985 SC 1669; 1984 SLJ 19

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Court fees Act, s. 7(v)(b) - Limitation Act, 1963, s. 14 - Plaintiffs filed two suits for possession of land particulars - District Judge directed that plaint on each of suits be returned to plaintiff for presentation to proper court - Pursuant to this order plaint in each suit was returned and on same date both plaints were presented in Court of Sub Judge at Jammu - Suits were dismissed as being barred by limitation - (A) Whether plaintiffs are entitled to benefit of s. 14? - S. 14 of Limitation Act, 1963 provides for exclusion of time of proceeding bona fide in court without jurisdiction - In computing period of limitation for any suit time during which plaintiff has been prosecuting with due diligence another civil proceeding against defendant shall be excluded where proceeding relates to same matter in issue and is prosecuted in a good faith in a court which from a defect of jurisdiction is unable to entertain it - Held, there was not slightest negligence nor any delay in acting upon order of District Judge - S. 14 of Limitation Act, 1963 must come to rescue of plaintiffs - (B) Expression good faith s. 14 of Limitation Act, 1963 - Held, it qualifies prosecuting proceeding in Court which ultimately is found to have no jurisdiction - Failure to pay requisite court-fee found deficient on a contention being raised or error of judgment in valuing a suit filed before a Court which was ultimately found to have no jurisdiction has absolutely nothing to do with question of good faith in prosecuting suit as provided in s. 14 of Limitation Act, 1963 - Held, HC was in error in holding that defective valuation and improper computation of court-fees discloses lack of good faith on part of plaintiff - Order accordingly.
