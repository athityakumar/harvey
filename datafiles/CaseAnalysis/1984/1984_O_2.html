O.P. Kathpalia v Lakhmir Singh (Dead) and Others

Supreme Court of India

23 July 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

D.A. Desai, V. Balakrishna Eradi, R.B. Misra

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 447; (1984) 4 SCC 66; AIR 1984 SC 1744

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - Delhi and Ajmer Rent Control Act, 1952, ss. 8 and 13(5) - Limitation Act, 1963 - (A) Whether petition for determination of standard rent filed by tenant u/s. 8 of Act was barred by limitation? - Held, s. 8 prescribes circumstances in which Court can fix standard rent of premises to which Act applies - S. 11(b) contends that petition for determination of standard rent made by tenant was barred by limitation - S. 11 enables either a landlord or a tenant to make an application to Court for fixing standard rent of premises - Sub-cl. (b) provides that such a petition has to be filed within 6 months from date on which premises have been let if they have been so let after commencement of Act - Expression 'within 6 months from date on which it is so let' has been construed to mean letting or leasing premises for first time to anyone after commencement of Act - (B) Whether tenant has made out sufficient cause for condoning delay in preferring appeal against interpolated order D/- 6-11-1956? - Held, a just and convincing cause wholly sufficient to condone delay was thus made out - More so because failure of District Judge and Single Judge of HC to condone delay resulted in gross miscarriage of justice inasmuch as tenant lost protection of Act for his failure to comply with a non est injudicious order, which was a nullity in eye of law - Justice demands that order D/- 6-11-1956 must be set aside as it is an interpolated and therefore legally non-existent order - Appeals allowed.
