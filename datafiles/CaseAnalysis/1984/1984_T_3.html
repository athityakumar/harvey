Managing Board of The Milli Talimi Mission, Bihar, Ranchi v State of Bihar and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 August 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, A. Varadarajan, S. Mukharji

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 181; (1984) 4 SCC 500; AIR 1984 SC 1757; 1985 (1) CCC 163; [1985] 1 S.C.R. 410; 1985 UJ 307

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution

Summary: Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 30 - Affiliation - Refusal - Violation of art. 30 of Constitution - Validity - In instant case SC was mainly concerned with the rights, privileges and status of minority institutions - Right of the minority institutions to get aid from the Govt., - Right to get affiliation from the Universities - Nature and extent of the autonomy which such institutions enjoy in their internal discipline and administration, and right to be protected from undue or repeated interference in the independence of the institutions in the garb of achieving excellence in the standard of education - Whether the minority institutions have a fundamental right to get aid from Govt. or affiliation from the whether the right to affiliation or to not so as to violate art. 30 of Constitution - Held, State Govt. in its counter-affidavit had stated that it was prepared to grant affiliation to the appellants' college on fulfilling certain conditions - SC satisfied that it was nothing but a pretext or a smoke-screen to cloud the real issue - Indeed, if the Govt. meant business it should have the courage to produce the report which was deliberately suppressed despite our orders to produce the same - SC compelled to draw an adverse inference against the State Govt. to the effect that if the materials on which the report was based had been produced it would have exploded the case of the Govt. and disclosed the real state of affairs, viz, that appellants Institute did fulfill all the conditions imposed by the State - Thus, the position was that the State had refused to grant affiliation on purely illusory grounds which do not exist and failed to consider the recommendation of the Education Commissioner which was made after full inspection for grant of affiliation - Affiliation was refused without giving any sufficient reasons and such a refusal contravenes the provisions of art. 30 of Constitution - Heart of the matter was that as the Govt. did not like the recommendation of the Education Commissioner and was not prepared to grant affiliation for undisclosed reasons, the act of the Government was a colourable exercise of jurisdiction which deprived the appellants' Institution of its constitutional rights - Impugned order of HC was set aside dismissing the petition - Appeal allowed.
