Khazan Chand and Others v State Of Jammu And Kashmir And Others

Supreme Court of India

13 February 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Madon

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 93; (1984) 2 SCC 456; AIR 1984 SC 762; 1984(1) SCALE 264; [1984] 2 S.C.R. 858; 1984 (56) STC 214; 1984 TAXLR 2841; 1984 UJ 394

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Keywords: Sale Price, Recovery Of Tax, Tax On The Sale Or Purchase Of Goods, Collected, Rates Of Interest, Indian Sale of Goods (Amendment) Act, 1963

Summary: Sales Tax - J. and K. General Sales Tax Act, 1962, ss. 8(1), 8(2), 8(3), 7(2), 10B - Constitution of India, 1950 , arts. 265, 14 - Constitution of J and K., s. 11S - (A) Constitutional validity of sub-ss.(1), (2) and (3) of s.8 of the 1962 Act - Held, State legislature is competent to so provide and provision is not discriminatory, arbitrary or unreasonable - (B) Writ seeks to quash orders directing petitioner and appellants to pay interest on the amount of tax due according to the quarterly returns filed by them but not paid within the prescribed time - Assessee would be liable to pay interest - (C) Interest imposition at uniform rate for full period of default , not as per scale of rates u/s. 8 (2) - Held, proper - (D) Whether interest more than amount default can be imposed - Assessing authority can impose interest for late payment of tax though its amount exceeded the amount of tax in respect of which default was committed - Appeals and petitions partly allowed.
