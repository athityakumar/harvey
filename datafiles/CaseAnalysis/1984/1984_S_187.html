Smt. Saroj Rani v Sudarshan Kumar Chadha

Supreme Court of India

08 August 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 319; (1984) 4 SCC 90; AIR 1984 SC 1562; 1985 (1) CCC 127; 1984 (2) DMC 325; 1984 HLR 713; 1984(2) SCALE 118; [1985] 1 S.C.R. 303; 1985 (9) ShimLC 265

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Family & Personal; Practice & Procedure; Women & Children

Keywords: Attachment Of Property, Decree For Restitution Of Conjugal Rights

Summary: Family and Personal - Hindu Marriage Act, 1955, ss. 9, 13, 13B and 23, 28 - CPC, 1908, O. 21, r. 32.O. 6, r. 17 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 133, 14 and 21 - (A) Determination of husband's entitlement to decree of divorce when petition for restitution of conjugal rights filed by wife - Consent decree - And no cohabitation for one year - (B) Permissibility to amend pleading in appeal before Supreme Court to the effect that husband got consent decree with ulterior motive - In the light of the fact that petition for divorce was allowed by rejecting wife's plea of cohabitation - (C) Constitutionality of remedy of restitution of conjugal rights provided u/s. 9 of the Act - (D) Maintenance - Determination of - Held, husband is not disentitled to get decree of divorce - Cannot be allowed - Held, s. 9 is not violative of arts. 14 and 21 of Constitution - Separate maintenance should be paid for the wife and the living daughter - Appeal dismissed.
