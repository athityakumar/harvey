Union of India and Others v Atic Industries Limited

Supreme Court of India

22 June 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Bhagwati, R.S. Pathak, Amarendra Nath Sen

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 162; (1984) SCC (Tax) 217; (1984) 3 SCC 575; AIR 1984 SC 1495; [1986] 59 Comp Cas 500; 1984 (3) CompLJ 1; 1984 (3) ECC 90; 1984 ECR 1437; 1984 (17) E.L.T. 323; 1984(1) SCALE 931; [1984] 3 S.C.R. 930; 1984 TAXLR 2559; 1984 UJ 824

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Customs; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - Excises and Salt Act, 1944, ss. 4(4)(c), 4(1)(a), Proviso (iii) and 4(4)(c) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 246, sch. 7, List 1, entry 84 - Related person - Assessee assailed the validity of the demand made by the Assistant Collector for differential duty - (A) Question relating to the applicability of the definition of 'related person' contained in s.4(4)(c) of the amended 1944 Act (effective from 1-10-1975) - Applicability of first part of definition - Held, first part of the definition not satisfied in relation to both companies (Atul Products Limited and Crescent Dyes and Chemicals Limited) and neither of them can be said to be a 'related person' vis-à-vis the assessee under amended Act - (B) Whether concept of 'related person' occurring in s.4(4)(c) of the amended 1944 Act was outside the legislative competence of Parliament u/art. 246 r/w entry 84 in the Union List and was, therefore, unconstitutional and void - Held, the definition of 'related person' is not unduly wide and does not suffer from any constitutional infirmity; it is within the legislative competence of Parliament - Appeal dismissed.
