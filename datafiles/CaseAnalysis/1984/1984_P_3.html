State Of Uttar Pradesh v P.A. Madhu

Supreme Court of India

17 July 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, A. Varadarajan, S. Mukharji

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 166; (1984) 4 SCC 83; (1984) SCC (Cr) 598; AIR 1984 SC 1523; 1984 (10) ALR 565; 1984 (1) Crimes 651; 1984 CRLJ 1438; 1984 CrLR(SC) 117; 1984 (2) ECC 137; [1985] 1 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - COFEPOSA Act, 1974, s. 3 - SAFEMA, 1976, ss. 2, 6, 7 - Indian Arms Act, 1959, s.25(1)(a) - IPC, 1860, s.302 - Detention Order - Proceedings for forfeiture of property - Failure to supply copies of documents which were relied upon in grounds of detention - Effect - Held, vitiates detention and consequently subsequent forfeiture of property - However, appellant is convicted u/s.302 of IPC, 1860 to imprisonment for life as also u/s.25(1)(a) of the Indian Arms Act to one year's R.I. because he was undoubtedly found using a country made pistol which was recovered from the place of occurrence - Appeal allowed.
