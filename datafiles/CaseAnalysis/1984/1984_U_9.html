M/S. Msco. Pvt. Limited v Union Of India And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

31 October 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

E.S. Venkataramiah, R.B. Misra

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 293; (1985) 1 SCC 51; AIR 1985 SC 76; 1985 (1) CCC 260; 1985 (1) CompLJ 184; 1985 (4) ECC 247; 1985 ECR 110; 1985 (19) E.L.T. 15; 1984(2) SCALE 676; [1985] 1 S.C.R. 1146; 1985 UJ 341

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Industrial Disputes (Amendment) Act, 1982

Summary: Customs - Customs Act, 1962, ss.11N, 12, 131 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s.2(j) - Industrial Disputes (Amendment) Act, 1982 - Constitution of India, 1950, s.19(6)(ii) - Interpretation of word 'industry' - Sale of certain articles to 'Industry' is exempted from duty under Customs Act - Whether sale to hospitals and nursing homes is exempted, by considering them as 'Industry' under notification D/-15-7-1977 of the Act and as per Industrial Disputes Act - Held, Industrial Disputes Act cannot be depended upon - Exemption cannot be claimed as new definition given to the word industry by Parliament in the Industrial Disputes (Amendment) Act, 1982 specifically excludes hospitals or dispensaries from the category of industry - Appeal rejected.
