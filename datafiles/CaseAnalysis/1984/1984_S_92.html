Nepal Singh v State Of U. P. Ors.

Supreme Court of India

09 November 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Pathak, D.P. Madon, M.P. Thakkar

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 286; (1985) SCC (L&S) 1; (1985) 1 SCC 56; AIR 1985 SC 84; 1985 (1) CCC 258; 1985 (52) FLR 77; 1985 LabIC 587; 1986 (2) LLJ 343; 1986 (1) LLN 683; 1984(2) SCALE 693B; [1985] 2 S.C.R. 1; 1984 (2) SLR 126; 1985 UJ 287

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Uttar Pradesh Government Servants Conduct Rules 1956

Summary: Service - Police Act s. 7 - U.P. Government Servants' Conduct Rules, 1956 r.29 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 311(2) - Service - Termination - Appellant/petitioner was temporarily working as SI in police department and disciplinary proceedings u/s. 7 of Police Act were initiated on charge that appellant had contracted second marriage while his first wife was alive and not obtained permission from Govt. thereby violating r.29 of 1956 Rules - Subsequently DIG dropped disciplinary proceedings against appellant on ground of jurisdiction - Later on IG of police issued circular to SI to furnish list of officers who fall under category specified in circular and appellant's name appeared in said list - DIG made order of termination of services of appellant - Aggrieved appellant filed writ before HC , SB dismissed said petition - Appeal was filed before DB of HC which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, it was not open to the SP to mention in his report, as a statement of fact, that the appellant had married a second time against the Rules and with the dropping of the enquiry the allegation remained unverified. Therefore, court observe that where allegations of misconduct are levelled against a Govt. servant, and it is a case where the provisions u/art. 311(2) of Constitution should be applied, it is not open to the competent authority to take the view that holding the enquiry contemplated by that clause would be a bother or a nuisance and that therefore it is entitled to avoid the mandate of that provision and resort to the guise of an ex facie innocuous termination order. Thus, Court will view with great disfavor any attempt to circumvent the Constitutional provision u/art.311(2) in a case where that provision comes into play. Hence, unable to uphold the judgment and order of the HC, and in the result the appeal allowed and order of the DIG of Police, is quashed and appellant is entitled to be treated as continuing in service without interruption and it will be open to the authorities to take fresh proceedings against the appellant in accordance with law. Appeal allowed.
