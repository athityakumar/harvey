Commissioner Of Wealth Tax, Kanpur v M/S. J.K. Cotton Manufacturers Ltd.

Supreme Court of India

28 February 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

V.D. Tulzapurkar, S. Mukharji

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 116; (1984) 3 SCC 393; AIR 1984 SC 946; 1984 (2) CompLJ 48; 1984 (38) CTR 148; 1984 (39) CTR 158; [1984] 146 ITR 552; 1984(1) SCALE 445; [1984] 3 S.C.R. 37; 1984 TAXLR 470; [1984] 16 TAXMAN 18; 1984 (2) TLR 470; 1984 UJ 318

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Corporate; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Exempted Assets, Debt Laws

Summary: Income Tax and Direct Taxes - Wealth Tax Act, 1957, ss. 2(m), 4, 5 and 6 - Taxation on Income (Investigation Commission) Act, 1947 - Net wealth - Debts qualifying for deduction - Whether the balance of the demands payable as a result of the findings and orders of the Income Tax Investigation Commission in the settlements made under the 1947 Act are deductible as debts owed by them in determining the net-wealth of these companies - Held, need not be relatable or referable, for qualifying deduction - Deductions claimed do not fall within the exclusionary part contained in s. 2 (m) (iii) of the Act - Appeal allowed.
