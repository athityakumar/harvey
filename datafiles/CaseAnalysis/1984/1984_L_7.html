State of Haryana and Others v Lal Chand and Others

Supreme Court of India

02 May 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

A.P. Sen, D.A. Desai, V. Balakrishna Eradi

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 325; (1984) 3 SCC 634; AIR 1984 SC 1326; 1984(1) SCALE 690; [1984] 3 S.C.R. 715; 1984 TAXLR 2551; 1984 UJ 722

Case Digest

Subject: Environment, Wildlife & Animal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: The Writ Jurisdiction, Forest Contract Rules

Summary: Excise - Punjab Excise Act, 1914, ss. 60, 27, 34, 58 and 59 - Punjab Liquor Licence Rules, 1956, r. 36 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 226 and 299 - Excise Duty - Auction of right to sell country liquor - Appeal against High Court order quashing the impugned notices of demand for recovery of the difference between the amount agreed to pay under the terms of auction of a liquor vend and the amount realized on re-auction of the vend as also the defaulted installments of the licence fee payable in respect of a liquor vend issued u/s. 60 of the Punjab Excise Act 1914 - Held, difference can be recovered by Govt. and petitioner cannot wriggle out of contractual obligations - He was bound to pay defaulted installments - Appeals allowed.
