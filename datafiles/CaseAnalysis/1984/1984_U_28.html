Coromandel Fertilizers Limited v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

17 August 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

Amarendra Nath Sen, P.N. Bhagwati, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 184; (1984) SCC (Tax) 225; (1984) Supp SCC 457; AIR 1984 SC 1772; 1984 (3) CompLJ 289; 1985 CRLR 132; 1985 CrLR(SC) 132; 1984 (3) ECC 98; 1984 ECR 1853; 1984 (17) E.L.T. 607; 1984(2) SCALE 282; [1985] 1 S.C.R. 523; 1984 TAXLR 2585; 1985 UJ 133

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Trade Discount, Payment Of Commission, Central Excise Rules 1944 (contd), Denied

Summary: Excise - Indirect Tax - Central Excise and Salt Act, 1944 - Payment of commission - Excise duty - Exemption - Denied - Justifiability - Appellant (company) carried on its business as manufacturers of diverse kinds of fertilizers - Appellant appointed two companies as their selling agents and entered into agreements with them for sale of fertilizers manufactured by appellant on terms and conditions mentioned in agreements entered into by appellant with selling agents - Commission was the remuneration paid by appellant to the selling agents for discharging the obligation of selling agents under the agreement of selling fertilisers - Appellant claimed exemption from imposition of excise duty on fertilizers which, according to appellant, were mixed fertilizers manufactured by appellant - Appellant further claimed that amount of commission paid by it to its selling agents should be deducted as trade allowance in computing the value of goods for assessment of excise duty - Both claims of appellant were disallowed by Asst. Collector whose decisions were upheld by Appellate Collector - Aggrieved appellant filed petitions before HC - HC by impugned common judgment dismissed petitions - Hence, instant appeals - Whether appellant was entitled to claim exemption from imposition of excise duty and whether amount of commission paid should be deducted as trade allowance in computing the value of goods for assessment of excise duty - Held, HC elaborately and very carefully considered case made for exemption under Notification no. 25/70 dt.1-3-1970 issued by GOI, and rightly disallowed the claim for exemption - Question as to whether Explanation seeks to control the operation or the effect of Notification was indeed immaterial, as the Explanation purports neither to control nor to alter but only seeks to explain - What Explanation provided was not in any way in conflict with or contrary to what the Notification provided - Fertiliser manufactured by appellant in respect of which claim for exemption under said Notification, was made, was not a mixed fertiliser within the meaning and scope of Notification and there was no hesitation in rejecting case of appellant - Commission which was paid by appellant to the selling agents was for services rendered by them as such agents - Such commission paid to agents for services rendered could not be considered to be in the nature of any trade discount which might qualify for deduction in determining the assessable value of goods for the purpose of imposition of excise duty under the Act - HC was clearly justified in rejecting claim of appellant - Claim of appellant was failed - Appeals dismissed.
