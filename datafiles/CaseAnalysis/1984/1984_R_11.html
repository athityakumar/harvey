Life Insurance Corporation Of India v Raja Vasireddy Komallavalli Kamba & Others

Supreme Court of India

27 March 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, V. Balakrishna Eradi

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 131; (1984) 2 SCC 719; 1984 ACJ 345; AIR 1984 SC 1014; [1984] 56 Comp Cas 174; 1984(1) SCALE 561; [1984] 3 S.C.R. 350; 1984 UJ 1040

Case Digest

Subject: Insurance

Keywords: Retention, Life Insurance Corporation of India standing Order 1960 (Financial Powers)

Summary: Contract and Commercial - Contract Act, 1872, ss. 7, 8, 10 - Life Insurance Corporation of India standing Order 1960, chap. 3 - Acceptance when complete - Acceptance of insurance proposal is complete only when it is communicated to the offeror - High Court coming to the conclusion that there was a concluded contract of insurance between the deceased and the Life Insurance Corporation - Whether silence or receipt and retention of premium also amounts to acceptence - Held, cannot be construed as acceptance - Appeal allowed.
