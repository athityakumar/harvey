Samarias Trading Co. Pvt. Limited v S. Samuel And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

09 November 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy, A.P. Sen, E.S. Venkataramiah

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 234; (1984) 4 SCC 666; AIR 1985 SC 61; 1984(2) SCALE 741; [1985] 2 S.C.R. 24; 1985 UJ 349

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative

Summary: Administrative - Grant of auction - Entitlement - An auction of right to sell liquor at 'R' was held on 15-2-84 by Deputy Commissioner - One 'H' was the highest bidder - Appellant/company was one of participants in auction - As 'H' did not deposit fifty per cent of license fee as cl. 14 of the terms and conditions of auction, sale was not confirmed and shop had to be auctioned again - Second auction was held on 28-3-1984 - At this auction appellant/company was highest bidder - Bid was for a sum of Rupees 25 lakhs - 'S' participated in auction but his bid was just over Rupees 17 Lakhs only - Highest bidder deposited sum of Rs.10,000, 2,50,000 and 9,90,000 - Sale was confirmed and shop was awarded to appellant and license was to enure for the period 1-4-1984 to 31-3-1985 - In meanwhile, 'S' filed oral application before HC and obtained an ex-parte order - Hence instant appeal - Held, facts and circumstances pertaining to proceedings that took place in HC, their affidavits which confirming the facts, disclosed that none of advocates personally knew 'S', as indeed one might not expect an advocate to know every client of his personally - They were like others, taken for a ride, if one might be permitted to use so common an expression - Their affidavits only emphasise what SC had already said about undesirability of making oral applications of consequence before courts with nothing placed in court's record to vouch for authenticity of facts forming basis of representations made to court, etc. - Further, SC sanctioned lease of liquor shop in favour of appellants for year 1-4-1984 to 31-3-1985 - However, informed by petitioner that though lease was confirmed in their favour from 1-4-1984 to 31-3-1985 for a sum of Rs. 30 lakhs, administration of Andaman Nicobar Islands, was demanding from them a sum of Rs. One lakh and odd towards lease for few days that they ran liquor shop after 1-4-1984 under orders of HC, calculated at the rate of Rs. 25 lakhs per year - SC was unable to see any justification for demand since lease as sanctioned and as confirmed was admittedly for entire period 1-4-1984 to 31-3-1985 for Rs. 30 lakhs - Appeal partly allowed.
