Jeewanlal (1929) Limitedetc. Etc. v The Appellate Authority Under The Payment Of Gratuity Act Ando

Supreme Court of India

29 August 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

A.P. Sen, E.S. Venkataramiah, O. Chinnappa Reddy

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 198; (1984) 4 SCC 356; (1984) SCC (L&S) 753; AIR 1984 SC 1842; 1985 (1) CompLJ 318; 1984 (49) FLR 313; 1984 LabIC 1458; 1984 (2) LLJ 464; 1984 (2) LLN 459; 1985 (1) MLJ(SC) 25; 1984(2) SCALE 303; [1985] 1 S.C.R. 664; 1985 (1) SLJ 38; 1985 UJ 106

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Labour and Industrial Law - Payment of Gratuity Act, 1972, ss. 2(a), 3, 4(2) and 4(3) - Interpretation of Statutes - Intention of legislature - Wages - Fifteen days' wages - Monthly rated employee - Establishment having factories in different State - Controlling Authority declining to entertain claim of employees at the office attached to factories - The questions arise - (A) How the monthly wages last drawn should be treated as , wages of 26 working days or of 30 days - (B) Jurisdiction of the controlling authority challenged on the ground that Central Govt. is the 'Appropriate Government' in relation to s. 3 of the Act - Wages to be treated of 26 days - Jurisdiction of the controlling authority can't be challenged on this ground - Appeal dismissed.
