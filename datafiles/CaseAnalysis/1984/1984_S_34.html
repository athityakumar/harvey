Jltentdra Singh Rathor v Shri Baidyanath Ayurved Bhawan Ltd. & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

15 March 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranganath Misra, D.A. Desai

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 127; (1984) 3 SCC 5; (1984) SCC (L&S) 333; AIR 1984 SC 976; 1984 (48) FLR 396; 1984 LabIC 554; 1984 (2) LLJ 10; 1984 (1) LLN 649; 1984(1) SCALE 506; [1984] 3 S.C.R. 223; 1984 (1) SLJ 664; 1984 UJ 651

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 11A - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 227 - Librarian filed an appeal by special leave u/art. 136 of Constitution - Services were terminated on payment of a month's salary - Charge of misconduct established, termination not warranted - Tribunal directing reinstatement - But withholding payment of half of back wages in view of misconduct -(A) Whether penal in nature - (B) High Court interfere with award of Tribunal in exercise of powers of Superintendent u/art. 227 of Constitution - Limits of - Held, withholding is by way of penalty - Wide discretion has been vested in the Tribunal by s. 11 of 1947 Act but HC u/art. 227 of Constitution does not enjoy such power, it can scrutinise an remand appropriate cases but not entitled to exercise the powers of the Tribunal and substitute an award in an appeal where it lies to it - Appeal allowed.
