Balmer Lawrie Workers Union, Bombay and Another v Balmer Lawrie and Company Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

21 December 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

D.A. Desai, V. Khalid

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 298; (1984) Supp SCC 663; (1985) SCC (L&S) 331; AIR 1985 SC 311; 1985 LabIC 242; 1985 (1) LLJ 314; 1985 (1) LLN 564; 1984(2) SCALE 1000; [1985] 2 S.C.R. 492; 1985 (1) SLJ 209

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Keywords: Eligibility - Experience

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950 art.14, 19(1)(a) and 19(1)(c) - Maharashtra Recognition of Trade Unions and Prevention of Unfair Labour Practices Act, 1972, s. 20(2), sch. 1 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 2(p), 2A and 18 - Industrial Dispute - Trade Unions - (A) Right to represent workmen granted to recognised union u/s. 20(2) of 1972 Act -Whether denial of fundamental freedom granted to workmen u/art. 19 - (B) Settlement among management and union - Arrears granted - Legality of clausr therein for deduction of 15% of amount of arrears payable to workmen towards union fund - Held, freedom not denied, s. 20(2) is not violative of art.19 or art. 14 (Question of constitutionality left open) - Further held, is legal - Appeal dismissed.
