Messrs Bhojraj Kunwarji Oil Mill and Ginning v Yograjsinha Shankarsinha Parihar and Others

Supreme Court of India

24 September 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

D.A. Desai, Ranganath Misra

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 370; (1985) 1 SCC 149; AIR 1984 SC 1894; 1984(2) SCALE 511; 1985 UJ 61

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - CPC, 1908, s. 115 - Revisional jurisdiction of Court - Assistant Judge reversed the findings of the trial court and held that no case was made out for condoning the delay - Whether interference on ground possibility of different view on facts is permissible - Held, not permissible in exercise of revisional jurisdiction - Both the learned Assistant Judge and the High Court were not justified in interfering with the order of the trial court, in exercise of the revisional jurisdiction on the only ground that a different view on facts elicited was possible - Appeal allowed.
