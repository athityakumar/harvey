Siliguri Municipality & Others v Amalendu Das & Others

Supreme Court of India

06 January 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

A.P. Sen, M.P. Thakkar

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 158; (1984) 2 SCC 436; AIR 1984 SC 653; [1984] 55 Comp Cas 506; 1984 (40) CTR 189; [1984] 146 ITR 624; 1984(1) SCALE 278; [1984] 2 S.C.R. 344; 1984 UJ 303

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Bengal Municipal Act, 1932

Summary: Practice and Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Bengal Municipal Act, 1932, ss.123, 124 - Appeal by special leave against an interlocutory order of High Court restraining the Municipality from recovering a graduated consolidated rate on the annual value of the holdings in terms of the amended provisions in ss. 123 and 124 of the Bengal Municipal Act, 1932 - Whether the grant of stay of recovery of such tax is proper - Held, not proper - Main purpose of passing an interim order is to evolve a workable formula or a workable arrangement to the extent called for by the demands of the situation keeping in mind the presumption regarding the constitutionality of the legislation and the vulnerability of the challenge, only in order that no irreparable injury in occasioned - Appeal allowed.
