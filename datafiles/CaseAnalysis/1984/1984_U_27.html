 Arjun Chaubey v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

23 March 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.V. Chandrachud, V.D. Tulzapurkar, R.S. Pathak, D.P. Madon, M.P. Thakkar

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 282; (1984) SCC (L&S) 290; (1984) 2 SCC 578; AIR 1984 SC 1356; 1984 (48) FLR 393; 1984 LabIC 915; 1984 (2) LLJ 17; 1984 (2) LLN 32; 1984(1) SCALE 612; [1984] 3 S.C.R. 302; 1984 (1) SLJ 654; 1984 (2) SLR 16; 1984 UJ 593

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Railway Servants (Discipline and Appeal) Rules 1968

Summary: Service - Railway Servants (Discipline and Appeal) Rules, 1968, r. 14(ii) - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 311(2) Proviso (b), 309 Proviso and 226 - Principles of natural justice - Appeal against order of dismissal on charge of misconduct - By senior himself considering employee's explanation - Order held, illegal and violative of principles of natural justice - And to hold him guilty of habitual acts of indiscipline is to assume something which remains unproved and secondly, the illegality from which the order of dismissal is of a character so grave and fundamental that the alleged habitual misbehavior on the part of the appellant cannot cure or condone it - Appeal allowed.
