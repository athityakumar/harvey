P. Jayappan v S.K. Perumal, First Income Tax Officer, Tuticorin

Supreme Court of India

17 August 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

E.S. Venkataramiah, A.P. Sen, O. Chinnappa Reddy

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 185; (1984) Supp SCC 437; AIR 1984 SC 1693; 1984 (2) Crimes 436; 1984 CRLR 380; 1984 CrLR(SC) 380; 1984 (42) CTR 180; [1984] 149 ITR 696; [1985] 1 S.C.R. 536; 1984 TAXLR 1197; [1984] 19 TAXMAN 1; 1984 (2) TLR 1197

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Criminal - Income Tax and Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 276C, 277 and 147 - IPC, 1860, ss. 193 and 196 - CrPC, 1973, ss. 309, 482 - Maintainability - Whether prosecutions for offences punishable u/ss. 276C and 277 of the Act and u/ss. 193 and 196 of IPC, 1860 instituted by the Department while the reassessment proceedings under the Act are pending are liable to be quashed on the ground that they were not maintainable - Held, prosecutions are maintainable and cannot be quashed - Pendency of the reassessment proceedings cannot act as a bar to the institution of the criminal prosecution for offences punishable u/s. 276C or s.277 of the Act - Petition dismissed.
