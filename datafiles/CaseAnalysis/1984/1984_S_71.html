 Election Commission Of India v State Of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

25 April 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.V. Chandrachud, V.D. Tulzapurkar, R.S. Pathak, D.P. Madon, M.P. Thakkar

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 145; (1984) Supp SCC 104; AIR 1984 SC 1406; 1984(1) SCALE 670; [1984] 3 S.C.R. 554; 1984 UJ 955

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Election; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Election - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art.226 and 324 - Bye-elections in one of constituency of Haryana - Date fixed by Election Commission - Govt. of Haryana challenged order before HighCourt and obtained a stay order - Ex parte - Election Commission approached this Court without informing state of Haryana - Held, order issued by High Court is set aside as state Govt. was supposed to approach first Election Commission and then HC - Appeal allowed.
