Amar Nath Om Parkash and Others v State of Punjab and Others

Supreme Court of India

29 November 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy, A.P. Sen, E.S. Venkataramiah

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 91; (1985) SCC (Tax) 92; (1985) 1 SCC 345; AIR 1985 SC 218; AIR 1996 SCW 4020; 1985 (1) CCC 547; 1984(2) SCALE 769; [1985] 2 S.C.R. 72; 1986 (62) STC 130

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Practice & Procedure; Sales Tax

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Constitution Of India, 1950, sch. 7, List 2, entry 54 - (A) Precedents - Whether judgments can be constructed as statutes - (B) Punjab Agricultural Produce Markets Act, 1961 - Proviso of s. 23 A - (1) Whether validating an illegal levy - (2) Lesislative competency of state legislature as to s. 23 A - (A) Held, cannot be construde so - (B) Further held, cannot be characterised as, it only prevents unjust enrichment of dealers, who have already passed on burden of fee on next purchaser - It is within legislative competence of state legislature - Appeals dismissed.
