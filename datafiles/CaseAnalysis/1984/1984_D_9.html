Rajinder Kumar Kindra v Delhi Administration Through Secretary (Labour) and Others

Supreme Court of India

27 September 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

D.A. Desai, D.P. Madon

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 210; (1984) 4 SCC 635; (1985) SCC (L&S) 131; AIR 1984 SC 1805; 1984 (49) FLR 424; 1986 LabIC 374; 1984 (2) LLJ 517; 1985 (1) LLN 11; 1984(2) SCALE 428; [1985] 1 S.C.R. 866; 1984 (2) SLJ 492; 1985 UJ 465

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 136 and 226 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 10(A)(1) and 11 A - Termination - On charge of negligence or embezzlement of funds - Validity of - Whether appellant can be accused of embezzlement of funds? - Held, there is no evidence in support of charge of embezzlement and misappropriation of funds simultaneously conceding that charges Nos. 1 and 2 are not proved - Only conducts of which appellant is guilty is that appellant had so deliberately left his cheque book unattended as to be accessible to anyone who may misuse it and this constitutes negligence in performance of duty - Keeping one's private cheque book in any manner is not part of performance of duty of employee - Charge apart from being frivolous is ludicrous and could not have even framed -Therefore, the appellant would be entitled to full back-wages and all consequential benefits - Appeal allowed.
