Mahant Salig Ram v Musammat Maya Devi

Supreme Court of India

21 January 1955

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhi Ranjan Das, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, Syed Jaffer Imam

Where Reported

1955 Indlaw SC 94; AIR 1955 SC 266; 1955 SCJ 248; [1955] 1 S.C.R. 1191

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Family & Personal - Land & Property - Hindu Law vis-a-vis Customary Law - Rattigan's Digest of Customary Law, para 23 - Self acquired property and ancestral property - Whether there is a custom in Gurdaspur district governing parties under which a collateral within fourth degree excludes daughter of last male holder from succession to self acquired property of her father? - Customary rights of succession of daughters as against collaterals of father with reference to ancestral and non-ancestral lands stated in para 23 of Digest - General custom of Punjab being that a daughter excludes collaterals from succession to self-acquired property of her father initial onus, therefore, must, on principle, be on collaterals to show that general custom in favour of daughter's succession to self-acquired property of her father has been varied by a special local custom excluding daughter which is binding on parties - Held, statements in a Riwaj-am truth of which is doubted by compiler himself in preface and which stand contradicted by instances collected and set out in Riwaj-am and which have been discredited in judicial proceedings and held to be incorrect cannot be regarded as a reliable or trustworthy document and cannot displace initial presumption of general custom recorded in Rattigan's book so as to shift onus to daughter who is respondent - Appellant failed to discharge onus that was initially on him - Further held, general customs recorded in Rattigan's book must prevail - Impugned judgment upheld - Appeal dismissed.
