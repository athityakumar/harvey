 Pradyat Kumar Bose v Hon'Ble The Chief Justice of Calcutta High Court

Supreme Court of India

23 December 1955

Case Analysis

Bench

B. Jagannadhadas, Vivian Bose, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Syed Jaffer Imam

Where Reported

1955 Indlaw SC 69; AIR 1956 SC 285; 1956 SCJ 259; [1955] 2 S.C.R. 1331

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Indirect Tax; Service

Keywords: Constitutional Safeguards

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 132(1), 229 and 320(3) - Civil Services (Classification, Control and Appeal) Rules, 1930 - Whether a person belonging to the staff of a High Court is within the scope of the phrase "a person serving under the Govt. of India or the Govt. of a State in a civil capacity"? - It may be noticed that while the constitutional safeguards under art. 311 are available to every person in the civil service, the safeguard in art. 320(3)(c) is one capable of being taken away by regulations to be made by the President or Governor - The Constitution itself appears, therefore, to have classed this safeguard on a different footing - This may well have been intended not to apply to the HCs - Therefore, both on the ground that art. 320(3)(c) would be contrary to the implication of art. 229 and on the ground that the language thereof is not applicable to the HC staff, we are of the opinion that for the dismissal of the appellant by the Chief Justice, prior consultation with the Public Service Commission was not necessary - Held, appeal dismissed.
