 V. O. Vakkan and Others v State of Madras

Supreme Court of India

19 September 1955

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Chandrasekhara Aiyar, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, Syed Jaffer Imam, B. Jagannadhadas

Where Reported

1955 Indlaw SC 96; AIR 1956 SC 76; 1956 (1) MLJ 4; 1956 (1) MLJ(SC) 4; 1955 (6) STC 647

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Keywords: Madras General Sales Tax Act

Summary: Sales Tax - Madras General Sales Tax Act, Explanation 2 to s. 2 - Appellant was a resident of Palluruthy in Cochin State - He was assessed by Deputy Commercial Tax Officer, Chowghat (Madras State) to a sales tax under Act - Appellant claimed that he was non-resident foreigner who had no place of business in Madras State and that he was not therefore, liable to sales tax - Whether appellant is a 'dealer' within the meaning of the Act? - Held, question of residence is immaterial - As appellant is a person who carried on business of selling goods within State of Madras - Explanation (2) to s. 2 marks agent (if any) and also a 'dealer' and is not intended to take principal outside scope of liability - Appeal dismissed.
