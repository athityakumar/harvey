D. P. Joshi v State of Madhya Bharat and Another

Supreme Court of India

27 January 1955

Case Analysis

Bench

T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, Bijan Kumar Mukherjea, Vivian Bose, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha

Where Reported

1955 Indlaw SC 123; AIR 1955 SC 334; 1955 MWN 406; [1955] 1 S.C.R. 1215

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 15(1) - The appellant who was resident of Delhi, was the medical student in Mahatma Gandhi Memorial Medical College run by the State of Madhya Bharat - Through the instant petition appellant alleged that rules in force in the said institution discriminated in the matter of fees between students who were residents of Madhya Bharat and those who were not, that the latter had to pay in addition to the tuition fees and charges payable by all the students, a sum of Rs. 1,500 per annum as capitation fee, and that was in contravention of arts. 14, 15(1) of the Constitution - Respondent contested that the institution in question had its origin in private enterprise, and was under the management of a Committee, that it was the Committee that had made the rule imposing capitation fee on students who did not belong to Madhya Bharat, that the respondent took over the College subject to the conditions relating to reservation of seats under which it was being run, and that the requirement of a capitation fee from non-residents did not offend either art. 14 or art. 15(1) of the Constitution - Whether, the capitation fees so collected by the institution was unconstitutional as alleged by the appellant - Held, on the examining the facts and circumstances of the case, instant court was of the view that there was the considerable force in the contention of the respondent and the contention of the appellant that the rule was repugnant to art. 15(1) or art. 14 of the Constitution was not sustainable - Petition dismissed.
