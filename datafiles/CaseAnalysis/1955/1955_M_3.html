M. K. Ranganathan and Another v Government of Madras and Others

Supreme Court of India

20 April 1955

Case Analysis

Bench

Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Sudhi Ranjan Das

Where Reported

1955 Indlaw SC 552; AIR 1955 SC 604; [1955] 25 Comp Cas 344; 1955 (2) MLJ(SC) 68; 1955 SCJ 515; [1955] 2 S.C.R. 374

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate

Keywords: Floating Charge, Mortgage Deed, Indian Companies Act, Solvent Company, Debenture-Holder, Tramways

Summary: Companies Act, 1913, ss. 171, 229 and 232 - CPC, 1908, Pre., s. 100 - Winding up of company - Sale of property of corporation - Interpretation of Statutes - Reference to object and reasons - Provisions of s. 232(1) of 1913 Act - Rights of secured creditor - Second appeal - Question of law - Held, reference not admissible as an aid to the construction of a statute except for ascertaining the conditions then prevailing and evil which he sought to remedy - Refer only to sales held through the intervention of the Court - He can realise his security without the intervention of the Court by effecting a sale of the mortgaged premises by private treaty or by public auction - Pure question of law can be allowed even at apeallent stage - Appeal dismissed.
