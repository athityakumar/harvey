 Union of India v Commercial Tax Officer, West Bengal and Others (With Connected Appeal)

Supreme Court of India

19 December 1955

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhi Ranjan Das, Vivian Bose, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, B. Jagannadhadas

Where Reported

1955 Indlaw SC 71; AIR 1956 SC 202; 1956 SCJ 223; [1955] 2 S.C.R. 1076; 1956 (7) STC 113

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Sales Tax - Constitution of India, 1950 - Bengal Finance (Sales Tax) Act, 1941, s. 5(2)(a)(iii) - Exemption from sales tax liability - In form of deductions from 'taxable turnover' inter alia of sales to Indian Stores Department, Supply Department of Govt. of India, and any railway or water transport administration - Whether sales of goods made by (Mills) to Govt. of India, Ministry of Industry and Supplies are to be deducted from taxable turnover of Mills so as to be exempt from sales tax demanded by Commercial Tax officer of State of West Bengal? - Sales to other departments of Govt. of India were not so exempted - In 1945 Govt. of India by its resolution announced creation of Department of Industries and Supplies in place of existing Department of Supply and Department of Industries and Civil Supplies - This newly created department had wider powers and was a new department altogether - Exemption granted by Bengal Act was given to two Departments by name, it was not given to sales to Govt. of India in all its departments - Reference to these two particular departments in s. cannot possibly be read as a reference to Govt. of India generally - Bengal Act by providing for deduction of sales to two named departments from taxable turnover certainly treated those two departments as distinct entities - This exemption is creation of statute and must be construed strictly and cannot be extended to sales to other departments - Held, no sales tax exemption on transactions in question, sales tax payable - By Majority, appeal dismissed.
