Anil Behari Ghosh v Smt. Latika Bala Dassi and Others

Supreme Court of India

15 April 1955

Case Analysis

Bench

Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Vivian Bose, B. Jagannadhadas

Where Reported

1955 Indlaw SC 59; AIR 1955 SC 566; 1955 (2) MLJ(SC) 84; 1955 SCJ 578; [1955] 2 S.C.R. 270

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Keywords: Succession Act 1925

Summary: Family & Personal - Probate and Administration Act, 1881 - Indian Succession Act, 1925, ss. 70 and 263 - Expression 'defective in substance' - Meaning of - Probate proceedings - (A) Whether case came directly within purview of cl. (a) of explanation and illustration (ii) of s. 263? - Omission to issue citation to persons who should have been appraised - Legal effect thereof - Revocation of grant - Whether, an absolute right irrespective of other considerations arising in case - Judicial discretion vested in courts - Held, expression 'defective in substance' in explanation cl. (a) to s. 263 of Indian Succession Act, 1925 means that defect was of such a character so as to substantially affect regularity and correctness of previous proceedings - Omission to issue citations to persons who should have been apprised of probate proceedings may well be in a normal case a ground by itself for revocation of grant - But this is not an absolute right irrespective of other considerations arising from proved facts of a case - Law has vested a judicial discretion in court to revoke a grant where court may have prima facie reasons to believe that it was necessary to have will proved afresh in presence of interested parties - Hence, in the present case just cause for annulment of grant of probate within meaning of s. 263 of Act has been made out - Whereas, in the present case, validity or genuineness of will has not been challenged, it would serve no useful purpose to revoke grant and to make parties go through mere formality of proving will again - Appeal dismissed.
