Chattanatha Karayalar v Ramachandra Iyer and Another

Supreme Court of India

19 September 1955

Case Analysis

Bench

T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, Vivian Bose, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha

Where Reported

1955 Indlaw SC 50; AIR 1955 SC 799; 1956 KLT 371; 1956 (1) MLJ(SC) 1; 1956 (58) PLR 314; 1956 SCJ 1; [1955] 2 S.C.R. 477

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance

Keywords: Debt Laws

Summary: Election - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 7(d) and 9(2) - Whether, if contract has been entered into by a member of an undivided family carrying on a separate business in course of such business, other members of family having no share or interest in that business shall be liable to be disqualified u/s. 7(d)? - Held, finding of fact cannot be interfered with in special appeal - No presumption under Hindu law that a business standing in name of any member is a joint family business even when that member is manager of family - Order of tribunal be set aside and matter be remitted for rehearing by reconstitute tribunal - Appeal allowed.
