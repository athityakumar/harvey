K C Mathew and Others v State of Travancore-Cochin

Supreme Court of India

15 December 1955

Case Analysis

Bench

Vivian Bose, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, N. Chandrasekhara Aiyar

Where Reported

1955 Indlaw SC 40; AIR 1956 SC 241; 1956 CRLJ 444; 1956 (1) MLJ(SC) 130; 1956 SCJ 213; [1955] 2 S.C.R. 1057

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure 1898, Travancore Penal Code

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898, ss. 225, 342 and 537 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302 and 149 - Travancore Indian Penal Code - Rioting with murder - High Court allowed appeal against acquittals and hence, this special leave petition - Scope of s. 342 CrPC, 1898 discussed - Now s. 149 applies not only to offences actually committed in pursuance of common objective but also to offences that members of assembly know are likely to be committed - Held, Court declines to interfere with concurrent findings of fact, where there is ample evidence which, if believed, can be used in support of findings - Sentences imposed on each accused to run concurrently and not consecutively - Appeal dismissed.
