State of Madras v Gurviah Naidu and Company Limited

Supreme Court of India

28 October 1955

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhi Ranjan Das, Vivian Bose, Syed Jaffer Imam, B. Jagannadhadas

Where Reported

1955 Indlaw SC 1; AIR 1956 SC 158; 1956 CRLJ 331; 1999 (110) E.L.T. 53; 1955 (6) STC 717

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 286(1)(b) and 134 - Tamil Nadu General Sales Tax Act (9 of 1939), ss. 3 and 15 b - Criminal Procedure Code,1898, s. 417 - Exemption on sale tax - Purchases made for export - (A) Whether purchasing requisite kind and quantity of skins to implement orders for supply of skins will fall within exemption of art. 286(1)(b) of Constitution? - After securing orders for supply of skins to buyers, where assessees go about purchasing requisite kind and quantity of skins to implement such orders, then there are purchases for purpose of export but as such purchases do not themselves occasion export, they do not fall within exemption of art. 286(1)(b) of Constitution - Nor do such purchases in State by exporter for purpose of export come within the ambit of art. 286 (1)(b) - Held,the purchases are liable to be included in turnover and assessed to sales tax, under Madras General Sales Tax Act - (B) Whether any provision in Constitution corresponds to s. 417, CrPC indicating that Supreme Court shall not, in an appeal by special leave, interfere with an order of acquittal passed by High Court? - Held, that observation that there was no provision in Constitution corresponding to s. 417 of CrPC was obviously made to emphasise that this Court shall not, in an appeal by special leave, interfere with an order of acquittal passed by HC merely for correcting errors of facts or law - Appeal allowed.
