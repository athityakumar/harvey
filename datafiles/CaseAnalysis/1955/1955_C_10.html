 ndira Sohanlal v Custodian of Evacuee Property, Delhi and Others

Supreme Court of India

28 October 1955

Case Analysis

Bench

B. Jagannadhadas, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Vivian Bose, Syed Jaffer Imam, N. Chandrasekhara Aiyar

Where Reported

1955 Indlaw SC 36; AIR 1956 SC 77; 1956 SCJ 171; [1955] 2 S.C.R. 1117

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Evacuee Property & Displaced Persons

Summary: Land & Property - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Delhi Laws Act, 1915 - General Clauses Act, 1897 - East Punjab Evacuees (Administration of Property) Act, 1947, ss. 24 and 27 - Administration of Evacuee Property Act, 1950 - Administration of Evacuee Property Ordinance, 1949 - Revisional power of Custodian-General - (A) Whether Custodian-General had revisional power which he purported to exercise? - Held, provision for appeal u/s. 24 thereof was virtually same as before, but as regards revision, s. 27 of Act provided for revisional powers of Custodian - U/s. 27, Custodian-General can exercise revisional powers for the purpose of satisfying himself as to legality or propriety of any order of Custodian - (B) Was order of Custodian-General on its merits such as to call for interference by this court? - Held, order under appeal is one passed by virtue of wide powers of revision vested in Custodian-General u/s. 27 of the Act - Jurisdiction which had been challenged found in favour of Custodian - But with respect to learned Custodian-General, his order is such that it is difficult to maintain it - Case is remanded to Custodian-General - Appeal allowed.
