Brajnandan Sinha v Jyoti Narain

Supreme Court of India

08 November 1955

Case Analysis

Bench

Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Syed Jaffer Imam

Where Reported

1955 Indlaw SC 78; AIR 1956 SC 66; 1956 ALJ 164; 1956 CRLJ 156; 1956 SCJ 155; [1955] 2 S.C.R. 955

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Contempt Of Courts Act, 1952, Public Servants Inquiries Act, 1850

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Public Servants (Inquiries) Act, 1850, ss. 2, 8, 21 and 22 - Contempt of Courts Act, 1952 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 227 - Whether Commr. appointed under 1850 Act is a court within meaning of term as used in Contempt Act? - Word 'court' was not defined in Act and expression 'courts subordinate to High Court' would prima facie mean courts of law subordinate to HC in hierarchy of courts established for purpose of administration of justice throughout Union - Tests - In order to constitute a court in strict sense of term, an essential condition is that court should have, apart from having some of trappings of a Judicial Tribunal, power to give a decision or a definitive judgment which has finality and authoritativeness which are essential tests of a judicial pronouncement - S. 8 inter alia provides for that Commrs. shall have same power of punishing contempt and obstructions to their proceedings, as is given to Civil and Criminal Courts by CrPC, 1973 - Commr. is merely a fact finding authority - Held, Commr. appointed under Act is not a court and his report or findings are not a definitive judgment or a judicial pronouncement in as much as they were not binding and authoritative and lacked finality - Impugned order set aside - Appeal allowed.
