Chatturam Horilram Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax, Bihar and Orissa

Supreme Court of India

18 April 1955

Case Analysis

Bench

B. Jagannadhadas, Vivian Bose, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha

Where Reported

1955 Indlaw SC 87; AIR 1955 SC 619; [1955] 27 ITR 709; [1955] 2 S.C.R. 290

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Definite Information

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 34 - Assessment Proceedings - Total Income - Chargeable To Tax - Assessee was appellant a private limited company exporting mica for sale to foreign countries - Proceedings were initiated on a notice issued to assessee u/s. 34 of Act, 1922 matter for consideration circumstances - Appellant had previously been assessed to tax on an income which was reduced on appeal - Assessment was set aside by Income-tax Appellate Tribunal - HC of Patna and confirming setting aside of assessment and Governor of Bihar promulgated Bihar Regulation IV of 1942 - Regulation, Indian Finance Act of 1939 was brought into force - Income-tax Officer discovered chargeable income escaped assessment in relevant year - Hence instant appeal - Whether income chargeable to income-tax has escaped assessment in relevant year - Held , regn. 4 of 1942 purported to retrospective, it cannot have effect of effacing result brought about by decision of Income-tax Appellate Tribunal - There were clear and express words to that effect - It might had been quite a different matter Regulation assessment proceedings themselves were still by date of relevant Regulation continued up to their termination - Income of assessee chargeable to income-tax had escaped assessment in relevant year - Appeal dismissed.
