Prabhu Babaji Navle v State of Bombay

Supreme Court of India

19 September 1955

Case Analysis

Bench

Vivian Bose, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha

Where Reported

1955 Indlaw SC 97; AIR 1956 SC 51; 1956 CRLJ 147

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34 and 302 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898, s. 31 - Appellant and four others person were charged u/s. 302 r/w s.34 - All others were acquitted but appellant convicted u/s. 302 r/w s.34 - Conviction challenged - Appellant not charged for having committed murder himself nor charged for having shared common intention of four named persons and for having participated in crime - If four persons were acquitted, element of sharing a common intention with them disappears, appellant cannot be convicted with aid of s. 31 - Held, no evidence on which a finding of prior concert that s. 34 requires can be based nor is there a fact to such a finding - Conviction cannot stand without aid of s. 34 and it must be set aside - Appeal is allowed.
