Sahu Madho Das and Others v Pandit Mukand Ram and Another

Supreme Court of India

22 March 1955

Case Analysis

Bench

Vivian Bose, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, B. Jagannadhadas

Where Reported

1955 Indlaw SC 90; AIR 1955 SC 481; 1955 (2) MLJ(SC) 1; 1955 SCJ 417; [1955] 2 S.C.R. 22

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - On Nanak Chand's death, estate went to his widow, and on her death, to their three daughters, Durga Devi, Maha Devi and Har Devi - On daughter's death, plaintiff's right as reversioner accrued - Mst. Pato, Nanak Chand's widow, had executed a so-called will wherein she had given her property to her daughters and grandsons - Whether Mst. Pato, widow, by way of a family arragement, gave properties separately to each of her three daughters and to daughter's sons and put them in proprietary possessions and they remained absolute owners of their properties and what is its effect on case? - Held, Mst. Pato in her will stated that property belonged exclusively to her without participation of anyone else - That assertion, coupled with fact that she purported to dispose of property after her death (which she could not have done as a limited owner), and taken in conjunction with subsequent conduct of daughters and that of grandsons, imports, admissions by them that was her claim and leaves us in little doubt about what she meant - Agree with Judicial Committee as Mst. Pato claimed an absolute estate in 1864 - Conduct of various members of family is relevant to show that their acting so, viewed as a whole, suggest existence of family arrangement on which defendants-appellants rely - Documents on record disclose a long line of conduct on part of various members of family and show that from 1877 down to 1922 each dealt with properties in his or her possession as absolute owner and set up exclusive proprietary title to properties transferred - There is only one way in which they could have got these exclusive titles and that is by a family arrangement - Whether property belonged to Pato or to Nanak Chand, Pato claimed an absolute right which daughters acknowledged, and in return they and their sons were given separate and absolute estates in separate portions of property immediately - Appeals allowed.
