Shivnandan Sharma v Punjab National Bank Limited

Supreme Court of India

15 March 1955

Case Analysis

Bench

Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Vivian Bose, B. Jagannadhadas

Where Reported

1955 Indlaw SC 92; AIR 1955 SC 404; 1955 (1) LLJ 688; 1955 (1) MLJ(SC) 247; 1955 SCJ 407; [1955] 1 S.C.R. 1427

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Service - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 7 and 10 - National bank - Industrial Dispute - Whether services of an employee of cash department can be terminated on a change made in services of contractor cashier? - To ascertain who is the employer is to ask who is entitled to tell the employee the way in which he is to do work upon which he is engaged - Tribunal had jurisdiction to make direction it did in respect of appellant - It is Bank which had undertaken responsibility in matter of their pay and prospects in service - Held, Treasurers are servants of Bank - Appeal allowed.
