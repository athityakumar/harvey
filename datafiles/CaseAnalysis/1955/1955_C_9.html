Corporation of Calcutta v Mulchand Agarwalla

Supreme Court of India

17 November 1955

Case Analysis

Bench

T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, Syed Jaffer Imam

Where Reported

1955 Indlaw SC 39; AIR 1956 SC 110; 1956 CRLJ 285; 1956 SCJ 128; [1955] 2 S.C.R. 995

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure 1898, Calcutta Municipal Act, 1923, Calcutta Municipal Act, misa

Summary: Municipalities & Local Governments - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Calcutta Municipal Act - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898, Schedule XVII, rr. 3, 14, 25, 32, 62 and 63 - Calcutta Municipal Act, 1923, ss. 363, 364, 365, 488, 493 and 536 - Appellant lodged a complaint before Magistrate charging respondent with constructing two rooms in fifth storey of his house without obtaining permission - Respondent pleaded guilty, and was fined - Corporation on examining nature of constructions put up by respondent, found that they contravened rr. 3, 14, 25 and 32 of Schedule XVII, and decided to take action - Issued notice to respondent - Magistrate, however, held this was not a fit case in which an order should be made for demolition - High Court dismissed revision petition - (A) Whether Corporation was precluded from taking proceedings u/s. 363 of Calcutta Municipal Act by reason of its having taken proceedings u/s. 488? - Held, nothing express in statute enacting such a bar - U/s. 493, if erection of any new building is commenced without obtaining written permission of Corporation, owner of building shall be liable to a fine - There is a proviso that where an application had been made u/s. 363, no proceeding shall be instituted under this section - Proceedings under two sections are mutually exclusive - When Legislature provides that on same facts proceedings could be taken under two different sections and penalties provided in those sections are not same, it obviously intends to treat them as distinct, and, therefore, where no question u/s. 403 of CrPC arises, proceedings taken under one section cannot be treated as falling within other - Prosecution under s. 488 for is, not in essence, a prosecution under s. 493(a) - If intention of Legislature was that proceedings taken u/s. 488 should bar proceedings u/s 363, it could have said so expressly - (B) Whether it was open to Corporation to have asked for demolition of building in proceedings taken by it under s. 488, and as it did not ask for it, it was precluded from claiming that relief in present proceedings? - Held, a question of demolition cannot arise when charge is commencement or construction without permission and at that stage no question of demolition of, a building necessarily arises - Further held, where, as in present case, application was based solely and exclusively on a breach of r. 62 of Schedule XVII, no order could have been passed for demolition under s. 536 - Even if Magistrate had power under s. 536 to order demolition of building appellant is not precluded from asking for an order u/s 363 merely by reason of fact that Magistrate had failed to pass such an order, or even that Corporation did not ask for it in prior proceedings - (C) Whether order passed by Municipal Magistrate and affirmed by HC is open to attack on merits? - Held, s. 363 does not require that when a building is shown to have been erected without permission an order for its demolition should be made as a matter of course - Word 'may' in s. 363 does not mean 'shall', and Magistrate has a discretion whether he should pass an order for demolition or not - Proceedings under s. 363 had been commenced when notice was issued to respondent - Proceedings under s. 488 were taken for erecting a building without permission whereas, present proceedings are taken substantially for breaches of building rules - Orders of courts below are based on mistakes and misdirections, and cannot be supported - Conduct of respondent in adopting a hide-and-seek attitude in completing constructions in deliberate defiance of law calls for severe action - Lapse of time, an order for demolition cannot be called for in interests of public - Appeal dismissed.
