 Messrs Mohanlal Hargovind Das, Bidi Merchants, Jabalpur (M.P.) v State of Madhya Pradesh and Another

Supreme Court of India

20 September 1955

Case Analysis

Bench

Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Syed Jaffer Imam, B. Jagannadhadas, N. Chandrasekhara Aiyar

Where Reported

1955 Indlaw SC 22; AIR 1955 SC 786; 1956 (1) MLJ(SC) 5; 1955 SCJ 6; [1955] 2 S.C.R. 509; 1955 (6) STC 687

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Sales Tax

Keywords: Sales Tax Authorities, Central Provinces and Berar Sales Tax Act

Summary: Constitution - Indirect Tax & Sales Tax - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 286 - Central Provinces and Berar Sales Tax Act, 1947 - ss. 8 and 27 A - (A) Whether liability can be imposed on registered dealers either for sales tax or purchase tax on transactions in course of inter-state commerce? - Held, when transactions are in course of inter-state trade and commerce, registered dealers under C.P. and Berar Sales Tax Act will be exempted from liability to pay sales tax or purchase tax within terms of C.P. and Berar Sales Tax Act - Bans regarding exemption has been imposed on powers of State Legislature to tax under art. 286 of Constitution - Appeal allowed.
