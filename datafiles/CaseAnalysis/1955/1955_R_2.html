Rai Bahadur Kanwar Raj Nathand Others v Pramod C. Bhatt, Custodian Ofevacuee Property

Supreme Court of India

10 November 1955

Case Analysis

Bench

T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, Bijan Kumar Mukherjea, Syed Jaffer Imam

Where Reported

1955 Indlaw SC 32; AIR 1956 SC 105; 1956 (58) BomLR 507; 1956 SCJ 134; [1955] 2 S.C.R. 977

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Evacuee Property & Displaced Persons

Summary: Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Administration of Evacuee Property Act, 1950, s. 12 - Supreme Court Rules, 1950, O. 16, r. 4 and O. 19, r. 4 - M/S. Abdul Karim and Brothers having migrated to Pakistan, their properties were declared by a notification D/-12-9-1951 issued u/s 7 of Act of 1950 as evacuee property and u/s 8 (1) of Act and they became vested in respondent as custodian for state - Appellants were displaced persons, who entered into contract with custodian for possession of property - (A) Whether u/s 12, of the Act of 1950, custodian has a power to cancel lease? - Held, custodian has u/s 12 of Act of 1950, power and always had power, to cancel leases created not merely by evacuees but also by himself - (B) Whether contention of a party not put forward in lower Court can be entertained in Supreme Court in proceedings under O. 16 r. 4 of Supreme Court Rules (1950)? - Held, the fact that a contention which was open to appellants on terms of s. 12 of Administration of Evacuee Property Act, 1950, but was not put forward at any stage prior to hearing, appeal in Supreme Court will not be entertained for it when it is sought to be raised by a supplemental proceeding under O. 16 r. 4 of Supreme Court Rules - (B1) Whether under O. 41, r. 1 of CPC, 1908, a point which was not raised in lower court can be entertained in appeal in Supreme Court? - Held, that where contention raised in lower Court was only with respect to one matter forming part of an agreement of a composite character consisting of three distinct matters, Supreme Court declined to consider question of rights of appellant with respect to other matters when raised for first time argument before Supreme Court - Appeal dismissed.
