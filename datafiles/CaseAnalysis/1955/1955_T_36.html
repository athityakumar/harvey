Tirath Singh v Bachittar Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

15 September 1955

Case Analysis

Bench

T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, Sudhi Ranjan Das

Where Reported

1955 Indlaw SC 85; AIR 1955 SC 830; 1955 (2) MLJ(SC) 292; 1955 SCJ 797; [1955] 2 S.C.R. 457

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Keywords: Dismissed, Parties To The Petition, Representation of People (Amendment) Act, 1951

Summary: Election - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 136 and 227 - Representation of Peoples Act, 1951, ss. 83, 99(1)(a), 100(2)(b) and 123(1) - Whether it is obligatory on part of tribunal to issue notice to parties to election petition before recording a finding u/s. 99(1)(a)? - Held, no - Proviso contemplates notice only to persons who are not parties to petition - Object of giving notice to a person under proviso is to give him an opportunity to be heard before a finding is given u/s. 99(1)(a)(i) that he has committed a corrupt or illegal practice - Person to whom notice is to be given should have an opportunity of cross-examining witnesses who had been examined before and given evidence against him, of calling his own evidence and of being heard - Persons to be named u/s. 99(1)(a)(ii) would include both parties to petition as well as non-parties, proviso thereto applies only to persons who had no opportunity of taking part in trial - Appeal dismissed.
