 M. Ct. Muthiah and 2 Others v Commissioner of Income Tax, Madras and Another

Supreme Court of India

20 December 1955

Case Analysis

Bench

Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Vivian Bose, B. Jagannadhadas

Where Reported

1955 Indlaw SC 61; AIR 1956 SC 269; [1956] 29 ITR 390; 1956 SCJ 349; [1955] 2 S.C.R. 1247

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Defence Of India Act, 1971, Taxation on Income (Investigation Commission) Act, 1947, Taxation of Income (Investigation Commission) Act, 1947, misa, Bombay Prohibition Act 1949

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1922 - Taxation on Income (Investigation Commission) Act, 1947, ss.3,7(2),7(4),8(2) - Enquiry - Income - Assessment - Inspection - 'M' carried on a flourishing banking business and died by an accident leaving behind him two sons 'X' and 'Y', petitioner No.3 and his widow/ 'D' - 'X' continued ancestral banking business and started several commercial enterprises - Govt. in exercise of its powers u/s. 5(1) of 1947 Act, referred to Income-Tax Investigation Commission relating to petitioners - Commission after holding recorded their findings and assumed that an aggregate sum of Rs. 10, 07, 322-4-3 represented undisclosed income during investigation period and directed distribution of that sum over several years in manner indicated by them in Schedule A to their report - Appropriate action under 1922 Act, taken against assessees with a view to assess or re-assess income which had escaped assessment for years 1940-41 to 1948-49 - Whether assessee was entitled to inspection in course of Investigation - Held, s.7(2) of 1947 Act, preclude possibility of Commission pushing in into final record on which report was to be based any ex parte material to which assessee has had no access - S.7(4) of 1947 Act, implied that assessee was not entitled to a roving inspection of material gathered by the Investigation Commission in the course of Investigation, which might relate to affairs of various other persons - Such a provision was not opposed to natural justice for even in matter of criminal judicial trials accused was not entitled to a roving inspection of material gathered by police during investigation - Petition allowed.
