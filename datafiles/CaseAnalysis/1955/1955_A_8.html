 State of Bombay v Ali Gulshan

Supreme Court of India

04 October 1955

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Chandrasekhara Aiyar, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, Syed Jaffer Imam

Where Reported

1955 Indlaw SC 28; AIR 1955 SC 810; 1956 (58) BomLR 490; 1955 SCJ 822; [1955] 2 S.C.R. 867

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property

Keywords: Land Acquisition

Summary: Land & Property - Constitution Of India, 1950 - Bombay Land Requisition Act, 1948 - Was Govt. of Bombay entitled u/s. 6 (4)(a) of Bombay Act to requisition, as for a public purpose, certain premises for 'housing a member of staff of a foreign consulate'? - Held, reading item 33 in Union Legislative List (List I), item 36 in State List (List II), item 42 of Concurrent Legislative List (List III) together, categories of 'purpose' contemplated are three in number namely Union purpose, State purpose, and any other public purpose - Even if it is conceded that law contemplates only two purposes, namely, State purpose and Union purpose, it is difficult to see how finding accommodation for staff of a foreign consulate is a Union purpose and not a State purpose - Item 11 in Union List specifies 'diplomatic, consular and trade representation' as one of subjects within legislative competence of Parliament, and u/art. 73 of Constitution, executive power of Union shall extend to all such matters - It can hardly be said that securing a room for a member of staff of a foreign consulate amounts to providing for consular representation, and that therefore it is a purpose of Union for which State cannot legislate - Appeal allowed.
