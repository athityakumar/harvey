U.J.S. Chopra v State of Bombay

Supreme Court of India

25 March 1955

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhi Ranjan Das, Syed Jaffer Imam, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati

Where Reported

1955 Indlaw SC 13; AIR 1955 SC 633; 1955 CRLJ 1410; 1955 (2) MLJ(SC) 99; 1955 SCJ 603; [1955] 2 S.C.R. 94

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Constructive

Summary: Criminal - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 134(1) (c) - Criminal Procedure Code, ss. 369, 430 and 439 - Whether summary dismissal of appeal preferred by appellant precluded him from taking advantage of provisions of s. 439(6) CrPC? - Right which is thus conferred upon accused u/s. 439(6) cannot be taken away by having resort to principle of finality of judgments incorporated in s. 369 of CrPC - Held, right which is conferred on accused of showing cause against his conviction u/s. 439(6) of CrPC is a right which accrues to him on a notice for enhancement of sentence being served upon him and he is entitled to exercise same irrespective of what has happened in past unless and until there is a judgment of High Court already pronounced against his conviction after a full hearing in presence of both parties on notice being issued by HC in that behalf - This right of his is not curtailed by anything contained in earlier provisions of s. 439 nor by anything contained in either s. 369 or s. 430 of CrPC - Order of HC is set aside, and matter is sent back to HC directing to allow appellant an opportunity to show cause against his conviction - Appeal allowed.
