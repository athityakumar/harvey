Merla Ramanna v Nallaparaju and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 November 1955

Case Analysis

Bench

T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha

Where Reported

1955 Indlaw SC 79; AIR 1956 SC 87; 1956 (1) MLJ 71; 1956 (1) MLJ(SC) 71; 1956 SCJ 101; [1955] 2 S.C.R. 938

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 47, O. 21, rr. 89,90 and 91 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908, arts. 165, 166 and 181 - (A) Whether suit is liable to be dismissed as barred by s. 47 of Code? - Held, it is well settled that when a sale in execution of a decree is impugned on grounds that it is not warranted by terms thereof, that question could be agitated, when it arises between parties to decree, only by an application u/s. 47 of the Code and not in a separate suit - Present suit is barred u/s. 47 of Code - That, however, does not conclude matter - U/s. 47, cl.(2), court has power to treat plaint presented on 07-08-1939 as an application u/s. 47 provided that on that date an application for relief claimed was not barred by limitation and that court in which it was filed was competent to execute decree - (B) Whether plaint was barred by limitation either u/arts. 165 or 166 of Indian Limitation Act, if it is treated as an execution application presented on 07-08-1939, or whether it was in time u/art. 181? - Held, u/art. 165, an application by a person dispossessed of immovable property and disputing right of decree-holder or purchaser at an execution sale to be put in possession must be filed within 30 days of dispossession - If this article is applicable to the present proceedings, then it must be held that plaint treated as an execution application was filed out of time - But then, there is abundant authority that art. 166 applies only when sale is one which has under law to be set aside as for example, under O. XXI, rr. 89, 90 and 91, but that it has no application when sale is inoperative and void - When a sale in execution is inoperative and void, an application by a judgment-debtor to have it declared void and for appropriate reliefs is governed by art. 181 and not art. 166 - An application by a party to suit to recover possession of properties which had been taken under a void execution sale would be in time u/art. 181, if it was filed within three years of his dispossession - Therefore, there is no legal impediment to plaint filed on 07-08-1939 being treated as an application u/s. 47, on ground that it is barred by limitation - Appeal dismissed.
