Atley v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

16 September 1955

Case Analysis

Bench

Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Vivian Bose, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar

Where Reported

1955 Indlaw SC 135; AIR 1955 SC 807; 1955 CRLJ 1653

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Demeanour Of Witness

Summary: Criminal - Criminal Procedure Code, 1898, ss. 342 and 417 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302 and 34 - Appellant acquitted by Trial Court and convicted to life by High Court on appeal by Govt. - Present appeal - Powers of HC in reviewing entire evidence - HC had very carefully examined evidence led on behalf of prosecution and came to a conclusion that circumstances proved beyond any doubt that appellant was responsible for murder - Where there is clear proof of motive for crime, that lends additional support to finding of court that accused was guilty but absence of clear proof of motive does not necessarily lead to contrary conclusion - Held, HC has wide powers of appreciation of evidence in appeal against an order of acquittal - No ground for interference - Appeal dismissed.
