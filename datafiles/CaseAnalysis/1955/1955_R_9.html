Raj Kali Kuer v Ram Rattan Pandey

Supreme Court of India

07 April 1955

Case Analysis

Bench

B. Jagannadhadas, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Vivian Bose

Where Reported

1955 Indlaw SC 60; AIR 1955 SC 493; 1955 (2) MLJ(SC) 49; 1955 SCJ 493; [1955] 2 S.C.R. 186

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Sacred, Bombay Prohibition Act 1949, Hindu Woman's Right to Property Act

Summary: Family & Personal - Hindu Woman's Right to Property Act - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 (1) - Whether a Hindu female is entitled to succeed the hereditary priestly office of a Pujari and Panda held by her husband in a temple and to receive emoluments thereof? - Held, usage of a female succeeding to a priestly office and getting same performed through a competent deputy is one that has been fairly well recognized - There is nothing in textual Hindu law to the contrary - Nor can it be said that recognition of such a usage is opposed to public policy in Hindu law sense - Consideration of public policy can only be given effect in present state of law to the extent required for enforcing adequate discharge of duties appurtenant to office - Subject to proper and efficient discharge of duties of office, there can be no reason either on principle or on authority to refuse to accord to a female right to succeed to hereditary office held by her husband and to get duties of office performed by a substitute excepting in cases where usage to contrary is pleaded and established - Appeal allowed.
