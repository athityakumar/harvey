Divisional Engineer, G.I.P. Railway v Mahadeo Raghoo and Another

Supreme Court of India

02 March 1955

Case Analysis

Bench

Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Vivian Bose, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, B. Jagannadhadas

Where Reported

1955 Indlaw SC 7; AIR 1955 SC 295; 1955 (1) LLJ 359; 1955 (1) MLJ(SC) 209; 1955 SCJ 358; [1955] 1 S.C.R. 1345

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Attorney-General

Summary: Service - Payment of Wages Act, 1936, ss. 15(1), 4 - Allowance - Wages - Sustainability - Respondent no. 1 was gangman in employ of Central Railway - Railway Board introduced scheme of grant of compensatory (city) allowance and house rent allowance - As result of this scheme certain railway employees stationed at specified headquarters were eligible for allowance - Respondent no. 1 became entitled to allowance - Respondent no. 1 was offered by Govt., quarters suitable to his post, but he refused to occupy same - On refusal house rent allowance was stopped - Respondent no. 1 put in his claim before Authority for Rs. 290 - Authority held that house rent allowance was 'wages' as defined in the Act that as matter of fact, accommodation was offered to respondent no. 1 and he refused it; but that even so, appellant was not entitled to withhold house rent allowance - Accordingly claim for Rs. 290 was allowed by Authority - Hence, instant appeal - Whether house rent allowance claimed by respondent no. 1 came within purview of definition of 'wages' contained in the Act - Held, legislature had used expression 'value of any house accommodation' in definition of 'wages' as denoting something which could be deducted from 'wages' - It was clear that definition of 'wages' under the Act could not include value of any house accommodation supplied by employer to employee; otherwise it would not be legally permissible deduction from wages - It was equally clear that house rent allowance which may in certain circumstances be included in 'wages' was not same thing as value of any house accommodation referred to in the Act - That being so, there was no validity in argument advanced on behalf of respondent no. 1 that r. 3(i)?? was inconsistent with provisions of ss. 7 and 11 of the Act - House rent allowance which was right conferred on employee was absolute right - Act read along with rules does not create any absolute right in employee to house rent allowance - Hence, there was no question of employee relinquishing right as was contemplated by s. 23 of the Act - Appeal allowed.
