In The Matter of D An Advocate of The Supreme Court

Supreme Court of India

23 November 1955

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhi Ranjan Das, Bijan Kumar Mukherjea, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar

Where Reported

1955 Indlaw SC 77; AIR 1956 SC 102; 1956 (58) BomLR 510; 1956 CRLJ 280; 1956 (1) MLJ 85; 1956 (1) MLJ(SC) 85; 1956 SCJ 124; [1955] 2 S.C.R. 1006

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Prohibition Act

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Prohibition Act - Bar Councils Act, 1926, s. 10 - Supreme Court Rules, 1950, O. 4, r. 30 - Legal Practitioners Act, 1879, s. 13 - Respondent was prosecuted before Presidency Magistrates at Bombay, on a charge of having committed an offence under Bombay Prohibition Act - Magistrate convicted Advocate - Appeal to High Court - Conviction upheld - Conduct of advocate examined by Tribunal constituted by Bombay High Court -(A) Whether s. 10 of Bar Councils, 1926 confers disciplinary jurisdiction on court to take actions in cases of professional or other misconduct? - Held, Indian Legislature by using the words 'professional or other misconduct' in s. 10, Indian Bar Councils Act intended to confer on Court disciplinary jurisdiction to take action in all cases of misconduct whether in a professional or other capacity leaving it to discretion of court to take action only in suitable cases - (B) Whether suspension of advocate for misconduct in criminal trial against him should be co-terminous with period of suspension fixed by Bombay HC? - Held, in present case Advocate should, by reason of his having indulged in conduct unworthy of a member of honourable profession to which he belonged, be suspended from practice for some time - He was an advocate of Supreme Court and was entitled to exercise his profession in all courts throughout UOI - Any suspension for a period less than period fixed by Bombay HC would obviously lead to serious anomaly and inconvenience - Hence, Advocate concerned be suspended from practice for a period co-terminous with period of suspension fixed by Bombay HC - (C) Whether in present case enquiry asked by advocate is justified under Supreme Court Rules, 1950, O. 4, r. 30? - Held, Advocate admitted correctness of report against him by Magistrate, confessed his guilt and tendered an unconditional apology to Tribunal evidently in hope that he would get away with it merely by tendering an apology - He asked for an enquiry which he had himself avoided by means of his admission and apology - a person holding responsible position of an Advocate of a HC and of Supreme Court cannot be permitted to play with Court in such a manner - (D) Whether in present case application for special leave was rejected on being affected by decision of HC ? - Held, application of Advocate for special leave to appeal from judgment and order of HC having been dismissed by Supreme Court was not concerned with proceedings in Bombay HC or before Tribunal of Bar Council - Appeal allowed.
