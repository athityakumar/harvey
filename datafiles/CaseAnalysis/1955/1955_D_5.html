Deonandan Mishra v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

28 September 1955

Case Analysis

Bench

B. Jagannadhadas, Vivian Bose, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha

Where Reported

1955 Indlaw SC 25; AIR 1955 SC 801; 1955 CRLJ 1647; 1956 (1) MLJ(SC) 31; 1956 SCJ 41; [1955] 2 S.C.R. 570

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure 1898

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898, s. 342 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Murder - No eye-witness - Circumstantial evidence - Last seen with deceased - Chain of evidence should be so far complete so as not to leave any reasonable ground for a conclusion and consistent with the innocence of accused - Ample evidence against appellant - Held, this is a case which satisfies standards requisite for conviction on basis of circumstantial evidence - Appeal dismissed.
