 Ananda Bejera And Another v State Of Orissa

Supreme Court of India

27 October 1955

Case Analysis

Bench

Vivian Bose, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Syed Jaffer Imam, N. Chandrasekhara Aiyar, B. Jagannadhadas

Where Reported

1955 Indlaw SC 34; AIR 1956 SC 17; 1956 CRLJ 24; 1956 (22) CutLT 101; 1956 (1) MLJ 69; 1956 (1) MLJ(SC) 69; 1956 SCJ 96; [1955] 2 S.C.R. 919

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Orissa Estate Abolition Act, 1951

Summary: Land & Property - General Clauses Act, 1897, s. 3 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 3 - Easements Act, 1882, s. 52 - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 19 and 21 - Right on surplus out of immovable property - (A) Whether right to carry surplus out of an immovable property sold constitute a license? - Benefit or surplus that arises out of land or immovable property is regarded as profit a prendre - Held, right acquired on an immovable property by virtue of sale over a specified future period would constitute a license - (B) Whether sale of profit a prendre is subject to writing and registration to give title or interest? - Sale of profit a prendre which is an immovable property by nature and values more than hundred rupees is required to be written and registered u/s 54 of Transfer of Property Act, 1882 - Further held, oral sale as in this particular case does not give any title or interest - (C) Whether refusal to recognise licenses towards an immovable property by Govt. infringes fundamental rights u/art. 19 of Constitution? - Fundamental rights can only be infringed if immovable property is confiscated or acquired or taken possession of - Held, simply refusing to recognise existence of a license shall not amount to an infringement - Appeal dismissed.
