Sri S Sita Manharani and Others v Chhedi Mahto and Others

Supreme Court of India

03 February 1955

Case Analysis

Bench

Syed Jaffer Imam, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati

Where Reported

1955 Indlaw SC 121; AIR 1955 SC 328

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 40 - Registration Act, 1908 - Bihar Tenancy Act, ss. 21 and 40 - Criminal Penal Code, 1973, s. 144 - This is an appeal against decree of Patna High Court setting aside decree passed by Addl. District Judge of Motihari in favour of Raja Mohan Bikram Shah - After his death appellants were substituted in his place and stead - Raja executed a registered Sadaua Pataua deed in favour of Naraipur Concern - Raja instituted suit out of which this appeal arises for a declaration that land in suit was his Bakasht and respondents were unlawfully in possession of it - Respondents resisted suit on ground that land in suit had been permanently settled with him by Naraipur Concern as 'Raiyats' under a 'hukumnama' and that they were settled Raiyats of village Ratni and had been in possession of land for more than 20 years and that they had acquired occupancy rights in land in suit - High Court relied upon an alleged acknowledgment in written statement filed by Naraipur Concern in a commutation proceeding u/s. 40, Bihar Tenancy Act instituted at instance of respondents and held that possession of respondents over disputed land was in capacity of Raiyats - Whether statement is an acknowledgment that respondents held land in suit as 'Raiyats', whether it was binding on Raja and whether it could create a 'Raiyati' interest when creation of such an interest under 'Hukumnama' has been rejected by concurrent findings of Courts below? - Held, it is to be observed that in their written statement respondents did not plead that a 'Raiyat' interest had been created in land because neither Raja nor Naraipur Concern nor both had acknowledged that land held by them was as 'Raiyats' - All that was pleaded in para 9 of written statement was that Raja and Naraipur Concern had admitted in commutation proceedings possession of respondents as well as settlement with them - Case of settlement has been rejected by courts below and a mere admission of possession would not lead to result respondents held land as 'Raiyats' - Respondents although settled 'Raiyats' of village Ratni did not, therefore, acquire occupancy rights in land in suit by virtue of provisions of s. 21, Bihar Tenancy Act - Appeal allowed.
