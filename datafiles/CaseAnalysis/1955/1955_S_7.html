Seth Thawardas Pherumal v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

24 March 1955

Case Analysis

Bench

Vivian Bose

Where Reported

1955 Indlaw SC 11; AIR 1955 SC 468; 1955 (2) MLJ(SC) 23; 1955 SCJ 445; [1955] 2 S.C.R. 48

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Arbitration Act, misa, Interest Act

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Indian Contract Act, 1872, s. 9 - Arbitration Act, 1940, ss. 16(1)(c), 20(2) and 30 - Interest Act, 1839 - Contractor, entered into a contract on 1-11-1945 for the supply of bricks to the C.P.W.D - Dispute referred to Superintendent Engineer u/cl. 14 - Objections u/s. 30 of the Arbitration Act, 1940 for award to be set aside - Whether there is an error of law apparent on the face of the award? - Held, the arbitrator awarded Rs. 40,000 as compensation, the error is apparent - Facts must be based either on evidence or on admissions; they cannot be found to exist from a mere contention by one side especially when they are expressly denied by the other - As the arbitrator based solely on the contractor's contentions, it is evident that he failed to appreciate the fact that the Dominion Govt. and the Provincial Rationing Authorities were separate entities distinct from one another - It is an error in law to hold that any contractual obligation can be inferred or implied from these circumstances - The High Court was right in dismissing the claims made under the heads in dispute here - Appeals dismissed.
