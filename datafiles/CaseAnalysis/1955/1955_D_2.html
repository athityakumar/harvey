 Dajisaheb Mane And Others v Shankar Rao Vithal Rao Maneand Another

Supreme Court of India

11 October 1955

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Chandrasekhara Aiyar, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Vivian Bose, Syed Jaffer Imam, B. Jagannadhadas

Where Reported

1955 Indlaw SC 37; AIR 1956 SC 29; 1956 SCJ 70; [1955] 2 S.C.R. 872

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Defence & Security Forces; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Constitution Of India, 1950 - Civil Procedure Code, 1908 - Government of India Act, 1935 - Whether Constitution which brought Supreme Court into being makes any provision for an appeal from a reversing decree of High Court prior to date of Constitution respecting properties of value of Rs. 10,000 and more being entertained and heard by Supreme Court? - Held, as soon as decree of HC came into existence, jurisdiction of Federal Court to hear an appeal from that decree became exercisable - With reference to para 20 of Adaptation of Laws Order, 1950, as amended in 1951, what is provided is that this adaptation will not affect right of appeal already accrued - If Court accedes to argument urged by respondents, Court shall be shutting out altogether a large number of appeals, where parties had an automatic right to go before Federal Court before Constitution and which Court must hold was taken away from them for no fault of their own, merely because Supreme Court came into existence in place of Federal Court - Appeal allowed.
