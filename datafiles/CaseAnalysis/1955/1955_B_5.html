 Bengal Immunity Co. Ltd v State Of Bihar & Ors

Supreme Court of India

06 September 1955

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhi Ranjan Das, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, B. Jagannadhadas, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Vivian Bose, Syed Jaffer Imam, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar

Where Reported

1955 Indlaw SC 553; AIR 1955 SC 661; 1955 (2) MLJ(SC) 168; [1955] 2 S.C.R. 603

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Sales Tax

Keywords: Bengal Finance (Sales Tax) Act, 1941, Sales Tax Authorities, Tax On The Sale Or Purchase Of Goods, Tax Levied, Collected, Tax On Sale Or Purchase, Essential Goods, Indian Companies Act, Practice, Bihar Sales Tax Act, 1947, Impugned Act, Air Force Act, 1950, Situs Of Sale, Local Goods, Bombay Sales Tax Act, 1952, Constitutionality, British North America Act, 1867, Central Provinces and Berar Sales of Motor Spirit and Lubricants Taxation Act, 1938, Charter Act, 1833, Indian Independence Act, English Sale of Goods Act, Assam Sales-Tax Act, 1947, Travancore-Cochin General Sales Tax Act, 1124, Adaptation of Laws (Third Amendment) Order, 1951, Bihar Finance Act, 1950, Central Provinces And Berar Sales-Tax Act, 1947, East Punjab General Sales-Tax Act, 1948, United Provinces Sales-Tax Act, 1948, Bombay Sales-Tax Act, 1946, Madras Sales-Tax Act, 1939, Mysore Sales-Tax Act, 1948, Sale of Goods Act, 1932

Summary: (A) Sales Tax - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 286 - Bihar Sales Tax Act, 1947, s. 13 (5) - Tax liability - Appellant company is an incorporated company carrying on the business of manufacturing and selling various sera, vaccines, biological Products and medicines - Its registered head office is at Calcutta - Goods are dispatched from Calcutta by rail, steamer or air against orders accepted by the appellant company in Calcutta - Appellant company has neither any agent or manager in Bihar nor any office, godown or laboratory in that State - Superintendent, Commercial Taxes, Patna issued show cause notice u/s. 13(5) of Bihar Sales Tax Act, 1947 alleging that appellant company was liable to pay tax - Appellant company denied its liability on the ground, that it was not resident in Bihar, it carried on no business there, none of its sales took place in Bihar and that it did not collect any sales tax from any person of that State - Appellant further submitted that notice issued u/s. 13(5) of Act is ultra vires and entirely illegal and called upon the Superintendent to forthwith rescind and cancel the same - Bihar Sales Tax authorities maintained that all sales in West Bengal or in any other State under which goods had been delivered in the State of Bihar as a direct result of the sale for the purposes of consumption in that State were liable to Bihar Sales Tax - Appellant company filed petition before Patna HC submitting that Act which authorises the assessment, levying and collection of sales tax on inter-State trade contravenes and constitutes an infringement of article 286 and is, therefore, ultra vires, void and unenforceable - HC dismissed said petition holding that it was not maintainable - Hence, present appeal - Whether the application for a writ of prohibition is maintainable? - Held, yes - Writ of prohibition is issued whenever a subordinate Court or Tribunal usurps jurisdiction which does not belong to it, and when that has been shown, the issue of the writ, though not of course, is of right and not discretionary - Point to be determined, therefore, is whether in taking proceedings u/s. 13(5) of the Act, respondents 2 and 3 acted without jurisdiction or in excess of it - Writ of prohibition should issue, if the appellant establishes that the proceedings taken against it u/s. 13(5) of the Act are without jurisdiction - Appeal allowed.

(B) Sales Tax - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 286 - Bihar Sales Tax Act, 1947, s. 13 (5) - Tax liability - Whether the Explanation to art. 286(1)(a) confers authority on the State Legislatures to impose tax on sales falling within its purview? - Held, object of art. 286(1)(a) is to avoid multiple taxation and that, was sought to be achieved by fixing the situs of sale in one State in accordance with the Explanation - Scheme of the enactment is to fix, what had not been done under the Government of India Act, 1935, the situs of the sale, and for that purpose, to classify it into two categories, sale inside the State and sale outside the State - On what principle the situs was fixed will presently be considered - But when once that is done, the problem is solved - If a sale is inside a State, the power of that State to tax it under Entry 54 remains unaffected - But if the sale is outside a State, article 286(1) (a) prohibits that State from taxing it - Appeal allowed.

(C) Sales Tax - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 286 - Bihar Sales Tax Act, 1947, s. 13 (5) - Tax liability - Whether the sales covered by the Explanation to art. 286(1) (a) are subject to the prohibition contained in article 286(2)? - Held, art. 286(1) (a) confers on States power to tax sales inside their territory - Article 286(2) prohibits them from taxing sales in the course of inter-State trade - Inter-State commerce consists in a flow of goods not merely from one State to another but in its continuous flow through several States, and art. 286(2) is designed to protect such a flow without being burdened by State taxes - Effect of the combined operation of both art. 286(2) and art. 286(1) (a) read with the Explanation is that the only State which can tax the sale is the one in which the goods are sold for local consumption - Hence, u/art. 286 (2), respondents have the power to tax - Appeal allowed.

(D) Sales Tax - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 286 - Bihar Sales Tax Act, 1947, s. 13 (5) - Tax liability - Whether the Bihar Sales Tax Act, 1947 is invalid on the ground that it is extra-territorial in its operation, and ultra vires the power of the State Legislature? - Held, explanation to article 286(1) (a) of the Constitution has created a legal fiction as a result of which a transaction of sale or a purchase partaking of an inter-State character has been treated as a domestic transaction - Fiction has localized sales or purchases contemplated by the Explanation, by converting such transactions as would otherwise have' been inter-State sales or purchases into sales or purchases inside one State in a sense in which it is placed in a class distinct and separate from what is referred to as sales or purchases 'outside the state' in the main body of art. 286(1) (a) which prohibits imposition of tax by any State - Imposition of multiple sales tax on transactions of sale or purchase may be an obstacle to the free flow of inland trade and commerce, the imposition of sales tax by a single State in which the sale, is deemed to have taken place by virtue of the Explanation cannot be predicated as having such an effect - Hence, directions issued that until parliament by law provides otherwise, the State of Bihar do forbear and abstain from imposing Sales Tax on out-of State dealers in respect of sales or purchases that have taken place in the course of inter-State trade or commerce even though the goods have been delivered as a direct result of such sales or purchases for consumption in Bihar - Appeal allowed.
