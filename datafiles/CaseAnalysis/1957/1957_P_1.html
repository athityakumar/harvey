 P. Balakotaiah v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

03 December 1957

Case Analysis

Bench

T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, Sudhi Ranjan Das, S.K. Das, A.K. Sarkar, Vivian Bose

Where Reported

1957 Indlaw SC 138; AIR 1958 SC 232; 1958 (1) MLJ(SC) 162; 1958 SCJ 451; [1958] 1 S.C.R. 1052

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Power To Terminate

Summary: Service - Government of India Act, 1935, ss. 241(2), 247 and 266(3) - Railway Services (Safeguarding of National Security) Rules, 1949, rr. 3, 4, 5 and 7 - The appellant, employed in 1939 in Bengal Nagpur railway as a clerk - State took over administration of Railway and gave option to employees to continue in service on terms set out in a document D/- 05-07-1946 - Appellant accepted those terms and continued in service on conditions mentioned in that document - (A) Whether orders terminating services of appellants can be upheld u/r. 148 of Railway Establishment Code? - The notice D/- 06-07-1950, was avowedly issued u/r. 3 of Railway Service Rules - Orders terminating services of appellants, in terms, recite that they were made u/r. 3 of the Rules - Held, orders were passed u/r. 3 of Security Rules, without reference to r. 148 - (B) Whether classification of persons on basis of subversive activities is too vague to be foundation of a valid classification? - It is also true that it is not unlawful to be either a Communist or a trade unionist - It is not necessary attribute either of a Communist or a trade unionist that he should indulge in subversive activities - When action was taken against appellant under rules, it was not because he was a Communist or a trade unionist, but because he was engaged in subversive activities - Further held, Security Rules are not vague and illegal as being repugnant to art. 14 of Constitution - (C) Whether impugned orders are in contravention of art. 19(1)(c) of constitution? - Appellants have no doubt a fundamental right to form associations u/art. 19(1)(c), but they have no fundamental right to be continued in employment by State, and when their services are terminated by State they cannot complain of infringement of any of their Constitutional rights, when no question of violation of art. 311 arises - Further held, right of appellants u/art. 19(1)(c) has been not infringed - (D) Whether procedure prescribed by Security Rules for hearing of charges does not satisfy requirements of art. 311 of Constitution? - Art. 311 has application only when there is an order of dismissal or removal - Held, the order terminating services u/r. 3 of Security Rules stands on same footing as an order of discharge u/r. 148, and it is neither one of dismissal nor of removal within meaning of art. 311 - (E) Whether impugned orders were not passed by competent authorities under Security Rules rendering them void? - Further held, orders had been actually passed by General Manager, a competent authority under Rules - Appeal dismissed.
