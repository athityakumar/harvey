 F. N. Roy v Collector of Customs, Calcutta

Supreme Court of India

16 May 1957

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sarkar, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Syed Jaffer Imam, S.K. Das, P. Govinda Menon

Where Reported

1957 Indlaw SC 33; AIR 1957 SC 648; 1957 CRLJ 1026; 1983 ECR 1667; 1983 (13) E.L.T. 1296; 1957 SCJ 734; [1957] 1 S.C.R. 1151

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Customs Act, Sea Customs Act 1878, Bombay Prohibition Act 1949, Import and Export (Control) Act. 1947

Summary: Customs - Bombay Prohibition Act, 1949 - Import and Export (Control) Act, 1947, s. 3(2) - Customs Tariff Act, 1975 - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Sea Customs Act, 1878, ss. 19, 167 and 183 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 32 and 14 - Whether order of confiscation by authoritied invalid in law? - By its own force no part of s. 3(2) purports to give any discretion to Customs-authorities at all - S. 183 with or without modification really contains one statutory provision and therefore it must go out of statute book as a whole or not at all; s. 183 does not give uncontrolled discretion - Petitioner had been asked before order was made whether he wanted a personal hearing and he had stated in reply that he did not and had ample confidence in authorities - Held, order of confiscation is valid - Application dismissed.
