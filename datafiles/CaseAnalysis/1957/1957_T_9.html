Commissioner of Income Tax, Madhya Pradesh and Bhopal v Sodra Devi

Supreme Court of India

17 May 1957

Case Analysis

Bench

Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, S.K. Das, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1957 Indlaw SC 35; AIR 1957 SC 832; [1957] 32 ITR 615; 1958 (1) MLJ(SC) 1; 1958 SCJ 1; [1958] 1 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 31 - General Clauses Act, 1897, s. 3(42) - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 16(3)(a)(ii), 66 (1), 66a (2) - Indian Income-tax (Amendment) Act, 1937 - Share - Partnership - Enquiry report - Undivided Hindu family - Recommendation - Those two appeals with certificates u/s. 66A (2) of Act raise a common question of law and would be governed by that common judgment - Prior to one R/deceased, his wife/assessee and his three major and three minor sons constituted a joint and undivided Hindu family - There was a severance of joint status between erstwhile members of joint family and joint family properties were accordingly partitioned - On such partition, business and agency shop fell to share of assessee and her three major and three minor sons - Partnership entered between assessee and her three major sons for purpose of carrying on business - Three minor sons of assessee admitted to benefits of partnership - Genuineness of partnership not disputed - Whether income falling to share of three minor sons liable to be included in total income of assessee - Held, legislature was not confined itself to recommendations made in Income Tax Enquiry Report - Clause covered share of profits of firm received by wife in her capacity as a partner or by minor child in his or her capacity as one admitted to benefits of partnership - But income received from firm by wife or minor child under any other contract with firm or in any other capacity, did not fall within clause and was not included in husband's or parent's total income - From what was stated above, it was clear that Legislature did not confine itself strictly or solely to recommendations made by Income Tax Enquiry Committee but provided for all such aspects of evil or mischief as it thought fit to remedy by 1937Act - Appeal allowed.
