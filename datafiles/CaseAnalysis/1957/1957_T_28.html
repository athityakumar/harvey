 Bombay Dyeing and Manufacturing Company, Limited v State of Bombay and Others

Supreme Court of India

20 December 1957

Case Analysis

Bench

T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, S.K. Das, A.K. Sarkar, Vivian Bose

Where Reported

1957 Indlaw SC 73; AIR 1958 SC 328; 1958 (1) LLJ 778; 1958 SCJ 620; [1958] 1 S.C.R. 1122

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Factories Act, Indian Contract Act, Constitution (Fourth Amendment) Act, 1955, Constitution (Fifth Amendment) Act,1955

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Constitution - Labour & Industrial Law - Municipalities & Local Govts - Practice & Procedure - Bombay Labour Welfare Fund Act, 1953, ss.3(1) and 3(2)(b) - Constitution of India, 1950, art.19(1)(f), 31(1), 31(2) 31(2 A) - Limitation Act, 1908, s. 28 - Payment of Wages Act, 1936, s. 15 - Indian Contract Act, 1872, ss. 56 and 65 - (A) Whether a debt barred by limitation ceases to be a debt? - Held, limitation only bars remedy but doen not extinguish debt itself - A debt that is time barred can be subject of transfer - (B) Whether transfer of title to state is an essential requisite of acquisition u/art.31(2)? - Held, no, transfer of title is not an pre requisite of acquisition u/art.31(2) - Deprivation of property and substantial abridgement of rights of owner also come under scope of art.31(2) - (C)Whether expression 'property' as used under art. 19(1)(f) includes money? - Held, yes - Citizens have right to hold money subject only to such law as is saved by art. 19(5) - Appeal partly allowed.
