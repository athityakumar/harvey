S. A. Venkataraman v State. (And Connected Appeal)

Supreme Court of India

03 December 1957

Case Analysis

Bench

Syed Jaffer Imam, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1957 Indlaw SC 139; AIR 1958 SC 107; 1958 CRLJ 254; 1958 (2) LLJ 1; 1958 (2) MLJ(SC) 45; 1958 SCJ 594; [1958] S.C.R. 1040

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Labour & Industrial Law; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure 1898, Prohibition Act

Summary: Criminal -Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947, s. 6 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898, s. 197 - Bribery - Accused, a public servant at time of commission of offence but not when court was asked to take cognizance - Prosecution under Prevention of Corruption Act - Validity of same - Whether s. 6 of Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947 applies if accused have ceased to be public servants at time court takes cognizance of offence? - At time a court is asked to take cognizance not only offence must have been committed by a public servant but person accused is still a public servant removable form his office by a competent authority before provisions of s. 6 of Act of 1947 can apply - Held, where accused have ceased to be public servants at time court takes cognizance of offence alleged to have been committed by them as public servants, provisions of s. 6 do not apply and prosecution against them is not vitiated by lack of a previous sanction by a competent authority - Appeal dismissed.
