 S. Rm. Ar. S. Sp. Sathappa Chettiar v S. Rm. Ar. Rm. Ramanathan Chettiar

Supreme Court of India

28 November 1957

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Vivian Bose, Sudhi Ranjan Das, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, S.K. Das, A.K. Sarkar

Where Reported

1957 Indlaw SC 83; AIR 1958 SC 245; 1958 (1) MLJ(SC) 148; 1958 SCJ 407; [1958] S.C.R. 1024

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Suits Valuation Act, 1887, s. 8 - Court Fees Act, 1870, ss. 5, 7 and 12 - Whether Court fee payable in suits covered u/s. 7(iv) of Court Fee Act? - Basis of claim is that property in respect of which a share is claimed is joint family property - S. 7, sub-s (iv)(b) deals with suits to enforce right to share in any property on ground that it is joint family property and amount of fees payable on plaints in such suits is 'according to amount at which relief sought is valued in plaint or memorandum of appeal' - S. 7 further provides that in all suits falling u/s. 7(iv) plaintiff shall state amount at which value of relief is sought - If scheme laid down for computation of fees payable in suits covered by several sub-sections of s. 7 is considered, it would be clear that, in respect of suits falling under sub-s (iv), a departure has been made and liberty has been given to plaintiff to value his claim for purposes of court fees - Held, plaintiff should be allowed to amend the plaint as well as memorandum of appeal in which he values relief sought by him for purpose of s. 7(iv) (b) of Act - Appeal allowed.
