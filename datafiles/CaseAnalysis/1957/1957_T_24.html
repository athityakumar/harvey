State of Bihar v Ram Naresh Pandey. (With Connected Appeal)

Supreme Court of India

31 January 1957

Case Analysis

Bench

B. Jagannadhadas

Where Reported

1957 Indlaw SC 161; AIR 1957 SC 389; 1957 CRLJ 567; 1957 SCJ 336; [1957] 1 S.C.R. 279

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure 1898

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898, ss. 202, 417, 435, 436 and 494 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 109 and 302 - Withdrawal of Public prosecutor from prosecution - Appeals against order of discharge passed by Subordinate Judge u/s. 494 of CrPC on his consent to withdrawal of Public Prosecutor from a prosecution - Whether Court is justified in consenting to withdrawal of prosecution on ground of insufficiency of evidence, even before evidence is taken? - Held, evidence should be recorded prior to withdrawal from prosecution - Order of High Court set aside as no interference with order of Magistrate was called for - Order of trial court restored - Appeal allowed.
