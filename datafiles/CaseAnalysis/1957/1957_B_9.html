G. X. Francis and Others v Banke Bihari Singh and Another

Supreme Court of India

04 December 1957

Case Analysis

Bench

Syed Jaffer Imam, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1957 Indlaw SC 175; AIR 1958 SC 309; [1958] 28 Comp Cas 23; 1958 CRLJ 569; 1958 MPLJ 81

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure,1898, s.527 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss.34, 501 and 502 - Application u/s. 527 for transfer of a criminal case from Jashpurnagar in State of Madhya Pradesh to some other State - Case is a prosecution for defamation u/ss. 501 and 502 r/w s.34 - Complainant is a member of royal family of Jashpur - Held, good grounds for transfer from Jashpurnagar are made out because of bitterness of local communal feeling and tenseness of atmosphere there - In view of unanimity of testimony from both sides about nature of surcharged tension in this area, local atmosphere is not conducive to a fair and impartial trial and so, there must be a transfer - Therefore direct that this case be transferred to such Magistrate in Sambalpur in State of Orissa competent to try it as District Magistrate of that place shall direct - Order accordingly.
