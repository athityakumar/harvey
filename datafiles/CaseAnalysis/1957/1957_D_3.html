Dhian Singh Sobha Singh and Another v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

29 October 1957

Case Analysis

Bench

Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Syed Jaffer Imam

Where Reported

1957 Indlaw SC 106; AIR 1958 SC 274; 1958 (1) MLJ(SC) 93; 1958 SCJ 363; [1958] 1 S.C.R. 781

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Attorney-General

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Contract Act, 1872, s. 161 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 80 - (A) Whether compliance of s. 80 of CPC demands strict interpretation of its provisions? - Held,no - Terms of s. 80 must be construed with due regard to common sense - Object of s. 80 is to give sufficient information as to nature of claim so as to enable recipient to avert litigation - (B)In a claim of damages for wrongful detention what date is to be regarded for valuation of property? - Held, relevant date would be date of judgment -(C) What is criterion for deciding measure of damages for conversion? - Held, it is well settled that in an action for wrongful detention plaintiff is entitled, besides re-delivery of chattel or payment of its value in alternative, also to damages for such wrongful detention - Unlawful keeping back of what belongs to another person is of itself a ground for real damages and not for nominal damages at all - Appeal allowed.
