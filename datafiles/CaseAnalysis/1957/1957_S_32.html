Kailash Nath and Another v State of Uttar Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

22 February 1957

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Govinda Menon, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, Syed Jaffer Imam, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1957 Indlaw SC 163; AIR 1957 SC 790; 1957 (8) STC 358

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Sales Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Bihar Sales Tax Act, 1947 - Constitution of India, 1950 arts. 19(1)(g), 32 and 286 - Uttar Pradesh Sales Tax Act, 1948, ss. 2(c), 3 and 4 - Whether first respondent, illegally and unauthorisedly levied sales tax on second petitioner-firm, under Act, 1948 with regard to cloth manufactured by second petitioner, with a view to exporting such cloth outside territories of India, by customers of second petitioner called 'indentors'? - It is not necessary for application of exemption cls. that there should be an initial manufacture with a view to make a sale for export but that exemption will be satisfied even if already existing manufactured cloth is sold with a view to export - Exemption will be attracted if sale is made on or after particular date for specific purpose - Three conditions are necessary to be fulfilled in order to attract exemption under notification; (1) are cloth must be manufactured in UP; (2) sale should be on or after 1-12-1949, with a view to export, and (3) there should be actual export of such cloth - Words 'such cloth or yarn' would mean cloth or yarn manufactured in U.P. and sold - Held, cloth exported is same as cloth sold by petitioners, there can be no question about exemption cls. not applying to it and if exemption applies, then tax has been levied without jurisdiction - Disputed levy of sales tax for year 1953-54 is not according to law - Appeal allowed.
