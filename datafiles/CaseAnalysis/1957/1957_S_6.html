 State of Bombay v Salat Pragji Karamsi

Supreme Court of India

07 March 1957

Case Analysis

Bench

J.L. Kapur, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, Syed Jaffer Imam, P. Govinda Menon, B. Jagannadhadas

Where Reported

1957 Indlaw SC 45; AIR 1957 SC 517; 1957 ALJ 682; 1957 CRLJ 884; 1957 SCJ 493; [1957] 1 S.C.R. 745

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Media & Entertainment

Summary: Criminal - General Clauses Act, 1897 - Extra Provincial Jurisdiction Act, 1947 - Bombay Prevention Of Gambling Act, 1887 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 132, 134 and 239 - Whether Bombaby Act extends to Kutch and whether respondent can be convicted under Act? - Held, Chief Commr. of Kutch has power to issue notifications making Act operative in Kutch or any part of Kutch and those powers were not affected by art. 239 of Constitution particularly because of cl. 15 of Adaptation of Laws Order, 1950, which preserved these powers of Chief Commr. - Act applies to state of Kutch and hence respondent can be convicted under Act - Judgment of learned Judicial Commr. acquitting respondent is set aside - Appeal allowed.
