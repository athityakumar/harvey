Mst. Kirpal Kaur v Bachan Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

15 November 1957

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sarkar, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Syed Jaffer Imam

Where Reported

1957 Indlaw SC 99; AIR 1958 SC 199; [1958] S.C.R. 950

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - On 06-02-1932, a document was executed by Harnam Kaur whereby she agreed that lands would belong to her for her life and after her death to Kirpal Kaur for latter's life - This document was never registered - Whether Harnam Kaur was customary heir and whether she had acquired a right to lands by adverse possession at time of gift to Kirpal Kaur? - Held, it is unable to hold that Patwari's report or order thereon proves that Harnam Kaur was customary heir and had got into possession in 1920, as such heir and, therefore, could not have been in adverse possession - In present case, Harnam Kaur had been in possession before date of document and to admit it in evidence to show nature of her possession subsequent to it would be to treat it as operating to destroy nature of previous possession and to convert what had started as adverse possession into a permissive possession and, therefore, to give effect to agreement contained in it which admittedly cannot be done for want of registration - Special custom under which alone Harnam Kuar could have become a customary heir has not been proved - It was impossible for her to have acquired by adverse possession title to property as his heir or to make such property good to his estate - As there is no evidence of any arrangement with respondents under which Harnam Kaur can be said to have taken possession of lands, her possession must be taken to have been adverse to collaterals - So at date of mortgage and gift, Harnam Kaur had acquired a title to lands by adverse possession - Appeal allowed.
