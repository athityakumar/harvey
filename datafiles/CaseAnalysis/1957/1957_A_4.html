Al. Pr. Ranganathan Chettiar v Al. Pr. Al. Periakaruppan Chettiar (and connected

Supreme Court of India

24 May 1957

Case Analysis

Bench

B. Jagannadhadas, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, P. Govinda Menon

Where Reported

1957 Indlaw SC 39; AIR 1957 SC 815; 1958 (1) MLJ(SC) 36; 1958 SCJ 58; [1958] 1 S.C.R. 214

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Keywords: Negotiable Instruments Act, Second Adoption, Charity

Summary: Family & Personal - Indian Limitation Act,1908,s. 7 - Negotiable Instruments Act,1881 - Constitution Of India, 1950 - Whether disposition of residue in favour of R contained in will was to him as a persona designata or is dependent on his being a duly and validly adopted son? - Held, question as to whether a disposition in such terms is to person intended therein as a persona designata or by reason of his filling a particular legal status which turns out to be invalid depend on its own facts and terms of particular document containing disposition - In instant case, in view of exclusion of validly adopted son and his heirs from succession and conduct of parties for over 14 years in allowing first appellant to retain property, taking an overall picture of various provisions of will, it was clear that first appellant was intended by testator to take property as persona designata and that will was therefore effective to convey title to him - Appeal allowed.
