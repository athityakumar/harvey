 Leo Roy Frey v Superintendent, District Jail, Amritsar, and Another. (And Connected Petition)

Supreme Court of India

31 October 1957

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhi Ranjan Das, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, S.K. Das, A.K. Sarkar, Vivian Bose

Where Reported

1957 Indlaw SC 104; AIR 1958 SC 119; 1958 CRLJ 260; 1983 ECR 1674; 1983 (13) E.L.T. 1302; 1958 SCJ 301; [1958] 1 S.C.R. 822

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Customs

Keywords: Indian Arms Act, Sea Customs Act 1878, Bombay Prohibition Act 1949, Customs (Amendment) Act

Summary: Constitution - Customs - Sea Customs Act,1878, s.167(8) - Sea Customs (Amendment ) Act, 1955 - Arms Act, 1959 - Indian Penal Code,1860, 120-B - Constitution of India, 1950, art.20 and 20(2) - (A) Whether art.20(2) of Constitution bar subsequent trial for conspiracy and conviction for offence under s. 167 (8), Sea Customs Act - Held, art.20(2) protects a person from being 'prosecuted and punished for same offence more than once' - Award of any confiscation, penalty or increased rate of duty under s.186 of Sea Customs Act by an officer of Customs does not prevent infliction of any punishment to which person affected thereby is liable under any other law - Thus, where offence of criminal conspiracy was not subject matter of proceedings under s.167(8), Sea Customs Act, it cannot be said that accused has already been prosecuted and punished for 'same offence' as conspiracy under s.120B, IPC r/w s.167 (81), Sea Customs Act - Petitions dismissed.
