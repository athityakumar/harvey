 State of Bombay v Saubhagchand M. Doshi

Supreme Court of India

25 September 1957

Case Analysis

Bench

T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, J.L. Kapur, A.K. Sarkar

Where Reported

1957 Indlaw SC 108; AIR 1957 SC 892; 1958 SCJ 161; [1958] 1 S.C.R. 571

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Indirect Tax; Service

Summary: Service - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 311(2) - Civil Services (Classification, Control and Appeal Rules), 1930, r. 55 - Bombay Civil Services Rules, r. 161 - Whether order of termination was valid? - Held, Govt. retains an absolute right to retire any Govt. servant after he has completed 25 years qualifying service or 50 years of age, whatever service without giving any reason, and no claim to special compensation on this account will be entertained - This right will not be exercised except when it is in public interest to dispense with further services of a Govt. servant such as on account of inefficiency or dishonesty - R. 165-A is not violative of art. 311(2) and is intra vires, and that impugned order D/- 30-10-1952, passed in exercise of power conferred thereby is valid - Appeal allowed.
