S. S. Shetty v Bharat Nidhi Limited

Supreme Court of India

17 September 1957

Case Analysis

Bench

Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, S.K. Das, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar

Where Reported

1957 Indlaw SC 112; AIR 1958 SC 12; 1957 (2) LLJ 696; 1958 SCJ 187; [1958] 1 S.C.R. 442

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Master And Servant Relationship

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 95 - Industrial Disputes (Appellate Tribunal) Act, 1950, s. 20(2) - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Computation of money value for reinstatement award - Held, benefit of reinstatement awarded cannot be treated as part of contract between employer and employee - Monetary value cannot be computed not on basis of breach of contract of employment - Industrial Tribunal would have to take into account terms and conditions of employment, tenure of service, present value of his salary till he attained the age of superannuation in computing money value - Appellant shall recover from respondent a sum of Rs. 12,500 being computation of money value of benefit of reinstatement awarded to him - Appeal allowed.
