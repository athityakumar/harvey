 Commissioner of Income Tax, Bombay v Provident Investment Company, Limited

Supreme Court of India

15 May 1957

Case Analysis

Bench

S.K. Das, A.K. Sarkar, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, P. Govinda Menon, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1957 Indlaw SC 32; AIR 1957 SC 664; [1957] 32 ITR 190; 1957 SCJ 679; [1957] 1 S.C.R. 1141

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Finance Act, 1956 - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 12B - Capital Asset - Excess Profits Tax - Capital Gains - Earnest Money - Assessee company is a private limited company and shares of were held by M and his nominees - Assessee company was managing agent of firm/D - Assessee company held all conversion shares of D and a substantial majority of conversion shares of S - Company E wrote two letters to assessee company - In these two letters, E offered to purchase conversion shares of D and S together with managing agency - Board considered offers made by E and resolved to accept offers - E received acceptance of offers made by it and sent two drafts - Assessee company accepted modified arrangement suggested by E and wrote to D and S that it had decided to resign office of managing agency and tendered its resignation - Balance of consideration money was paid to assessee company and it was not disputed that value of managing agency was computed nor was there any dispute that managing agency was a capital asset - Income-tax Officer computed capital gains and asked assessee company to pay tax - Lower Courts assumed that assessee company was owner of shares and managing agencies - It sold shares to E and handed back managing agencies to managed companies - HC assumed in negative on ground that there was neither a sale nor a transfer of managing agency within meaning u/s. 12B of Act, 1922 - Whether assessee company made a capital gain within meaning u/s. 12B of Act, 1922 - Held that it is obvious that entire assessment proceedings proceeded on basis that sum was consideration for sale or relinquishment of managing agencies and dispute between parties was whether transaction with regard to managing agencies in its true legal character was a sale or transfer or relinquishment - SC opined answer was correctly given by HC - Transaction in its true legal character was a relinquishment of managing agency and was neither a sale nor a transfer - Tax shall be payable by an assessee under head Capital gains in respect of any profits or gains arising from sale, exchange or transfer of a capital asset effected after 31-3-1946 and such profits and gains shall be deemed to be income of previous year in which sale, exchange or transfer took place - Two conditions must be fulfilled before transaction took place and first condition was that profits or gains must arise from sale, exchange or transfer of a capital asset and second condition was sale, exchange or transfer must be effected after 31-3-1946 - Appeal dismissed.
