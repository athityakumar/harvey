 Virendra v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

06 September 1957

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhi Ranjan Das, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, J.L. Kapur, A.K. Sarkar

Where Reported

1957 Indlaw SC 42; AIR 1957 SC 896; 1958 SCJ 88; [1958] 1 S.C.R. 308

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Freedom Of Speech And Expression, Fair Comment

Summary: Media & Entertainment - Constitution - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Punjab Special Powers (Press) Act, 1956, ss. 2 and 3 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 32 - Whether Punjab Special Powers (Press) Act, 1956 is constitutionally valid - Held, exercise of powers u/s. 3(1) is subject to the same condition of the satisfaction of the State Govt. or its delegate as is mentioned in s. 2(1)(a) - There is, however, no time limit for the operation of an order made under this section nor is there any provision made for any representation being made to the State Govt., therefore, absence of these safeguards in s. 3 clearly makes its provisions unreasonable - Petition No. 96 of 1957 allowed.
