 State of Bombay v R. M. D. Chamarbaugwala

Supreme Court of India

09 April 1957

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhi Ranjan Das, S.K. Das, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar

Where Reported

1957 Indlaw SC 153; AIR 1957 SC 699; 1957 SCJ 607; [1957] 1 S.C.R. 874

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Pith And Substance, Betting

Summary: Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14, 19(1)(g), 19(6) and 301 - Prize Competition Act, 1955, ss. 2(1)(d), 10 and 12 - Bombay Prize Competitions Tax Act, 1939 - Specific Relief Act, s. 45 - Whether 1955 Act is constitutionally valid under Entries 33, 34 and 62 of State list and opposed to public policy? - First petitioner is an individual who claims to be a citizen of India and founder and Managing Director of second petitioner, which is a company incorporated in State of Mysore and having its registered head office at 2, Residency Road, Bangalore in that State - Impugned law is a law with respect to betting and gambling u/entry 34 and impugned taxing section is a law with respect to tax on betting and gambling u/entry 62 and that it was within legislative competence of State Legislature to have enacted it - There is sufficient territorial nexus to entitle State Legislature to collect tax from petitioners who carry on prize competitions through medium of a newspaper printed and published outside State - Held, prize competitions being of a gambling nature, they cannot be regarded as trade or commerce and as such petitioners cannot claim any fundamental right u/art. 19(1)(g) in respect of such competitions, nor are they entitled to protection of art. 301 - Therefore, order of lower court was set aside - Appeal allowed and Petition dismissed.
