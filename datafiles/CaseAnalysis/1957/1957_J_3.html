L. J. Leach and Company Limited v Jardine Skinner and Company

Supreme Court of India

22 January 1957

Case Analysis

Bench

T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, S.K. Das

Where Reported

1957 Indlaw SC 15; AIR 1957 SC 357; 1957 ALJ 794; 1957 (59) BomLR 395; 1957 MPLJ 497; 1957 SCJ 313; [1957] 1 S.C.R. 438

Case Digest

Subject: Tort; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Tort - Contract & Commercial - Trade - Practice & Procedure - Corporate - Constitution - Indian Limitation Act, 1908, s. 48 - Indian Companies Act, 1956 - Constitution of India, 1950 art.133(1)(a) - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 101, 102, 103 and 104 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, - (A) Whether claim for damages on ground of conversion is maintainable? - Held, claimant must establish that they had title to goods in question - Further, it should be shown that property in goods passed to them in accordance with provisions of Sale of Goods Act - If claimant is a purchaser, title to goods would pass to them only when other party appropriates them to contract - If not so done, claim for damages on ground of conversion will be misconceived - (B) Whether power of courts to order amendments is affected by limitation? - Held, generally courts would not allow amendments if a fresh suit on amended claim would be barred by limitation - Courts discretion to order an amendment is not barred by limitation if that is required in interests of justice - (C) What was 'Lease and Lend' scheme? - Held, this scheme was introduced by Govt. of India somewhere about March - April, 1942 - It was a war measure - Govt. prohibited direct import of oil and lubricants from America through private agencies - All imports were carried out by Govt. - Govt. authorized agents in America shipped requisite goods to India - Dealers became entitled to goods only on shipping documents being endorsed to them by Govt. - Appeal allowed.
