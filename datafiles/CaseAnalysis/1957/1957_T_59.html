 A. V. Fernandez v The State Of Kerala

Supreme Court of India

02 April 1957

Case Analysis

Bench

Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, Syed Jaffer Imam, P. Govinda Menon, J.L. Kapur, B. Jagannadhadas

Where Reported

1957 Indlaw SC 18; AIR 1957 SC 657; 1957 SCJ 689; [1957] 1 S.C.R. 837; 1957 (8) STC 561

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Health & Drug; Practice & Procedure; Sales Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Travancore -Cochin General Sales Tax (Amendment) Act, 1957 - Travancore Cochin General Sales Tax Act, 1125, ss. 2, 24 and 26 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 286 - Cochin General Sales Tax Rules, 1950, rr. 7 and 20(i) - Assessment - Gross and net turnover - Purchase of copra - In the year 1951-52, the appellant purchased copra of the value of Rs. 7, 16,04814 and after manufacturing oil therefrom he sold the oil partly in the State and partly outside the State and the cake entirely within the State - The total gross turnover of the appellant was thus Rs. 14, 59, 92318 and he claimed to deduct therefrom the whole of the purchase price of the copra u/rr. 7 (1) (k) r/w. r. 20 of 1950 Rules as per 1125 Act - Subsequently, in 1951 the 1125 Act was amended and s. 26 was added - Sales Tax Officer assessed the appellant to sales tax on a net assessable turnover by taking the value of the whole of the copra purchased by him, adding thereto the respective values of the oil and the cake sold inside the State and deducting only the value of the copra corresponding to the oil sold inside the State - Appellant contended that in the calculation of the net turnover he was entitled to include the total value of the oil sold by him, both inside and outside the State, and deduct therefrom the total value of the copra purchased by him, and further that, under the overriding provision of the Act u/s. 26, he was entitled to have the value of the oil sold outside the State deducted - Whether appellant was entitled to exemption claimed by him? - Held, non-obstante provision contained in s. 26 of Act has effect of taking these transactions out of purview of Act with result that dealer is not required nor is he entitled to include them in calculations of his turnover liable to tax thereunder - Held, calculations of net turnover made by Sales Tax Authorities were also correct - Appeal dismissed.
