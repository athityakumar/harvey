 R. M. D. Chamarbaugwalla v Union of India(with connected petitions)

Supreme Court of India

09 April 1957

Case Analysis

Bench

T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, S.K. Das, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar

Where Reported

1957 Indlaw SC 49; AIR 1957 SC 628; 1957 (59) BomLR 973; 1957 SCJ 593; [1957] 1 S.C.R. 930

Case Digest

Subject: Competition; Constitution

Keywords: Severability, Bombay Lotteries and Prize Competitions Control and Tax Act, 1948, Prize Competitions Act, 1955, Bombay Prohibition Act 1949

Summary: Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 19(6), 19(1)(g), 32, 252(1) and 301 - Prize Competitions Act, 1955, ss. 2(d), 4, 5 and 20, rr.11 and 12 - Validity of provisions of 1955 Prize Act and rules framed under this Act - Bombay Lotteries and Prize Competitions Control and Tax Act, 1948 - Constitution of India, 1950,arts. 19(1)(g) and 301 - (A) Whether valid parts of a statute are separable from invalid parts? - Held, it is intention of legislature that is determining factor - Separability of valid and invalid provisions of a statute does not depend on whether law is enacted in same section or different sections - It is not form, but substance of matter that is material, and that has to be ascertained on an examination of Act as a whole and of setting of relevant provisions therein - If after invalid portion is expunged from statute what remains cannot be enforced without making alterations and modifications therein, then whole of it must be struck down as void, as otherwise it will amount to judicial legislation - (B)Whether impugned provisions are severable in their application to competitions of a gambling character, assuming definition of 1 prize competition' in s. 2(d) is wide enough to include also competitions involving skill to a substantial degree? - Held, difference between two classes of competitions is as clear-cut as that between commercial and wagering contracts - On facts, there might be difficulty in deciding whether a given competition falls within one category or not , but when its true character is determined, it must fall either under one or other - Distinction between two classes of competitions has long been recognised in legislative practice of both United Kingdom and this country, and courts have, pointed out characteristic features which differentiate them - Restriction of impugned provisions to competitions of a gambling character affect either texture or colour of Act, nor do provisions require to be touched and re-written before they could be applied to them - They will squarely apply to them on their own terms and in their true spirit, and form a code complete in themselves with reference to subject - Conclusion is, therefore, inescapable that impugned provisions, assuming that they apply by virtue of definition in s. 2(d) to all kinds of competitions, are severable in their application to competitions in which success does not depend to any substantial extent on skill - Petition dismissed.
