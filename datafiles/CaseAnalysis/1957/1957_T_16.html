Oriental Investment Company, Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax, Bombay

Supreme Court of India

22 May 1957

Case Analysis

Bench

J.L. Kapur, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, S.K. Das

Where Reported

1957 Indlaw SC 36; AIR 1957 SC 852; [1957] 32 ITR 664; 1958 (1) MLJ(SC) 18; 1958 SCJ 37; [1958] 1 S.C.R. 49

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Excess Profits Tax Act, 1940, s. 26(1) - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922 - Investment and properties - Profits and losses - Purchase or sale of shares - Memorandum of association - Petitioner company was incorporated as investment company objects of which are set out in memorandum of association - Any business in purchase or sale of shares, securities or properties and in view of order of Central Board of Revenue made on its application u/s. 26(1) of 1940 Act it should be assessed for income tax purpose as investor and not as dealer - Income Tax Officer rejected plea and alleged that investments as stock-in-trade of its business carried during previous year - Company filed appeal to Appellate Assistant Commissioner which was also dismissed and order of Income Tax Officer upheld - Assessee company made application u/s. 66(2) of 1922 Act requiring Appellate Tribunal to refer to HC but application was dismissed and company obtained special leave to appeal to this Court - Tribunal ordered that company had dealt in investments and properties throughout and it was dealer in investments and properties - Hence instant appeal - Whether assessee company could be treated as dealer in investments and properties and whether profits and losses arising from sale of shares, securities and immoveable properties of assessee company can be taxed as business profits - Held, Appellate Tribunal in its appellate order set out amount of profits made by assessee company in years of assessment 1943-1944 to 1948-1949 - Inconsistent positions taken up by assessee in first claiming to be dealer and then to be investor according to Tribunal was due to fact that it was incurring losses in earlier years and begun making profits when claim of being investor was put forward - Objects set out in memorandum of association of assessee company and previous assertion by assessee company that it was dealer in investments and not merely an investor - Appeal allowed.
