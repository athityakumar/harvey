Prem Singh and Others v Deputy Custodian General, Evacuee Property and Others

Supreme Court of India

24 May 1957

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhi Ranjan Das, Syed Jaffer Imam, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, A.K. Sarkar

Where Reported

1957 Indlaw SC 186; AIR 1957 SC 804

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Administration of Evacuee Property Act, 1950, ss.27 and 56 - Administration of Evacuee Property Rules, r.14 (6) - Deputy Custodian General passed an order on 18-08-1953, whereby he cancelled allotment of lands in village of Ratauli, Tehsil Jagadhari, District Ambala, made to appellants - Appellants thereupon moved Punjab High Court u/art. 226 of Constitution praying for a writ quashing above order of Deputy Custodian General - (A) Whether a writ of certiorari can be issued, when decision of inferior Court or tribunal is erroneous in law? - This is on principle that a Court which has jurisdiction over a subject-matter has jurisdiction to decide wrong as well right and when Legislature does not choose to confer a right of appeal against that decision, it would be defeating its purpose and policy if a superior Court were to rehear case on evidence, and substitute its own findings in certiorari - It may be taken as settled that a writ of certiorari could be issued to correct an error of law - But it is essential that it should be something more than a mere error; it must be one which must be manifest on face of record - In present case, errors pointed out, if they are errors at all are mostly errors of fact and even if errors may by any stretch of argument be said to constitute errors of law, they are nothing more than mere errors of law, which may be corrected by a court of appeal but which do not render order a 'speaking order' showing a clear ignorance or disregard of provisions of law, so as to be amenable to correction by a writ of certiorari - (B) Whether allotment in favour of appellant could be cancelled? - Held, allotment of land in Ratauli in favour of appellants were made by Director General and were never sanctioned by Financial Commr. - Further Rehabilitation authorities charged with duty of making allotments have exercised their discretion and for cogent reasons stated in Deputy Custodian General's order, allotment to respondents No. 3 and 4 were not disturbed -There is no reason to interfere with that decision on an application u/art. 226 - None of pre-requisites for issue of a writ of certiorari exists and claim of appellants as against respondents No. 3 and 4 was therefore rightly rejected - It was not a proper matter to be decided on a petition u/art. 226 - Appeal dismissed.
