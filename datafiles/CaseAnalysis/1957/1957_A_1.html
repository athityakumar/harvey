The State Of Assam v A. N. Kidwai, Commissioner Of Hillsdivision And Appeals,Sh

Supreme Court of India

31 January 1957

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhi Ranjan Das

Where Reported

1957 Indlaw SC 16; AIR 1957 SC 414; 1957 (1) MLJ 66; [1957] 1 S.C.R. 295

Case Digest

Subject: Excise

Keywords: Indian Boilers Act, 1923, Enactment, Factories Act, Government of India Act 1935, Eastern Bengal And Assam Excise Act, 1910, Assam Revenue Tribunal (Transfer Of Powers) Act, 1948, Delhi Laws Act, 1915, misa, Bombay Prohibition Act 1949, Assam Revenue Tribunal (Transfer of Payments) Act 1948 (Assam 4 of 1948), Central Board of Revenue Act

Summary: Excise - Govt. of India Act, 1935, s.296(2) - Assam Revenue Tribunal (Transfer of Powers) Act, 1948, s. 3(3) - Validity of notification issued by Governor of Assam in exercise of powers conferred on him by s.3(3) appointing Commissioner of Hills Division and Appeals, as appellate authority under Act, 1948 challenged - Provisions of s. 296(2) of Act, 1935, itself, which authorised Governor to constitute a tribunal did not indicate any qualification for eligibility of persons to be appointed as members of tribunal - Possibility of an appeal from decision of Commr. of a Division coming up before authority referred to in s. 3(3) cannot affect validity of said Notification - Appeals allowed.
