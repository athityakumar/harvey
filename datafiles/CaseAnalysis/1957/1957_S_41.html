Subramania Goundan v State of Madras

Supreme Court of India

17 September 1957

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Govinda Menon, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1957 Indlaw SC 93; AIR 1958 SC 66; 1958 CRLJ 238; 1958 (1) MLJ(SC) 130; 1958 SCJ 321; [1958] 1 S.C.R. 428

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure 1898

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898, ss. 164, 364, 207-A, cl.(3), 342 - Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 24 and 133 - (A)Whether absence of question regarding confession in committal Court will effect voluntary nature of confession - Held, absence of question under s. 342, CrPC with regard to confession before committing Magistrate would not in any way show that confession was involuntary - That appellant had no opportunity to put forward his complaint about confession until case came before Sessions Court will not affect voluntary nature of confession - (B) Whether absence of details in confession has any effect on its evidentiary value - Held, confession cannot be said to be untrue where there is nothing in statement in confession which is contrary to oral evidence - Thus, absence of details doesn't hamper truthfulness of a confession - (C) Whether corroboration is necessary in case of a retracted confession? - Held, u/s. 133 of Act, a conviction is not illegal merely because it proceeds on uncorroborated testimony of witnesses - In case of an unworthy accomplice, court may mandate his corroboration in material particulars - General corroboration is sufficient in retracted confessions - An accomplice's evidence should be corroborated in material particulars - Appeal dismissed.
