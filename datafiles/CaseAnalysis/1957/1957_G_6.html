 Gulabdas and Company and Another v Assistant Collector of Customs and Others

Supreme Court of India

25 April 1957

Case Analysis

Bench

S.K. Das, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Syed Jaffer Imam, P. Govinda Menon, A.K. Sarkar

Where Reported

1957 Indlaw SC 182; AIR 1957 SC 733

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Customs - Constitution of India, 1950 - Indian Customs Tariff - Petitioners is a registered partnership firm - Firm is an established importer of stationary articles and has quota rights for importing stationary items including goods known as 'Artists Materials' - License given to import goods known as 'Artists' Material' - Asst. Collector of Customs for Appraisement wrote to petitioner to effect that 'Lyra' brand crayons were classifiable under item No. 45 (a) of Indian Customs Tariff - Petitioners imported 'Lyra' brand coloured Crayons - Asst. Collector of Customs instead of assessing duty under item 45 (a) was assessing duty under item 45 (4) of Indian Customs Tariff - Petitioners felt aggrieved by this order - Asst. Collector of Customs and Central Board of Revenue dismissed appeal - Present writ application - (A) Question of enforcement of fundamental rights - If provision of law under which impugned orders have been passed are good provisions and orders passed are with jurisdiction, whether they be right or wrong on facts, there is really no question of infraction of a fundamental right - If a particular decision is erroneous on facts or merits, proper remedy is by way of an appeal - Petitioners did not choose to do so nor do they challenge any of provisions of law under which impugned orders were made; nor is it contended that orders were without jurisdiction - Held, no question of enforcement of a fundamental right in any of petitions - (B) Unequal treatment - Petitioners relied on letter in which Asst. Collector of Customs said that 'Lyra' brand crayons were classifiable under item 45(a) - This letter, however, itself stated that classification was provisional and liable to revision in light of examination of goods at time of importation - Moreover, tentative classification made by Asst. Collector, of Customs was not binding on Collector and petitioners cannot found any claim on ground of estoppel - Applications liable to be dismissed - (C) Reasonable opportunity - Held, petitioners have had a full hearing before two successive Collectors of Customs, Calcutta and their grievance as to violation of principles of natural justice is totally devoid of merit - Petitioners became aware of decision of Collector, through Customs officers, that assessment was made under item 45(4) - There has been a violation of principles of natural justice - Applications dismissed.
