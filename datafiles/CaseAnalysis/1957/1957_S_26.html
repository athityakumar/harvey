 Sri Venkataramana Devaru and Others v State of Mysore and Others (with connected petition)

Supreme Court of India

08 November 1957

Case Analysis

Bench

T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Syed Jaffer Imam, A.K. Sarkar, Vivian Bose

Where Reported

1957 Indlaw SC 120; AIR 1958 SC 255; 1988 (34) E.L.T. 521; 1958 (1) MLJ(SC) 109; 1958 SCJ 382; [1958] 1 S.C.R. 895

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Trusts & Associations

Keywords: Actionable Wrong

Summary: Trusts & Associations - Constitution - Madras Temple Entry Authorisation Act (Madras V of 1947), s. 3 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 25(2)(b), 26(b) - The appellants were the members of a sect known as Gowda Saraswath Brahmins who were the worshipers of Sri Venkataramana (god renowned for its sanctity) - Temple in the dispute was the temple of Sri Venkataramana - It was alleged by the appellants that as the temple was a denominational one, they were entitled to the protection of art. 26 of the Constitution, that it was a matter of religion as to who were entitled to take part in worship in a temple, and that s. 3 of the Act, in so far as it provided for the institution (disputed temple) being thrown open to communities other than Gowda Saraswath Brahmins, was repugnant to art. 26(b) of the Constitution and was, in consequence void - Whether, the right of a religious denomination to manage its own affairs in matters of religion guaranteed u/art. 26(b) of the Constitution, was subject to, and could be controlled by, a law protected by art. 25(2)(b) of the Constitution, throwing open a Hindu public temple to all classes and sects of Hindus - Held, considering all the facts of the cases and the provisions of the Constitution and impugned provisions of the Act with regard to the question before the court, instant court was of the view that the decree of the HC was a proper decree recognising the rights of all sects of the public that, as a denominational institution (disputed temple) would also be a public institution, art. 25(2)(b) of the Constitution was applied, and that, thereunder, all classes of Hindus were entitled to enter into the temple for worship, however, there were certain religious ceremonies and occasions during which the Gowda Saraswath Brahmins alone were entitled to participate, and that right was protected by art. 26(b) of the Constitution - Appeals dismissed.
