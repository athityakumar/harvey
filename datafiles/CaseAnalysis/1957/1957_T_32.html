Kushal Rao v State of Bombay

Supreme Court of India

25 September 1957

Case Analysis

Bench

Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, P. Govinda Menon, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1957 Indlaw SC 109; AIR 1958 SC 22; 1958 ALJ 91; 1958 CRLJ 106; 1958 SCJ 198; [1958] 1 S.C.R. 552

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 134(1)(c) - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Appellant was charged and convicted for charge of murder, and sentenced to death - Hence, the present appeal - Whether the conviction could be based on the dying declaration alone - Held, that it was a case of a deliberate cold-blooded murder, so the conviction should be upheld - In order to be found on a dying declaration alone, a judgment of conviction of an accused person, the Court should be fully satisfied that the dying declaration had the impress of truth on it, after examining all the circumstances in which the dying person made his statement ex-parte and without the accused having the opportunity of cross-examining him - If, on such an examination, the Court was satisfied that the dying declaration was the true version of the occurrence, conviction could be based solely upon it - Court should, of course, be fully convinced of the truth of the statement and, naturally, it could not be fully convinced if there was anything in the other evidence or in the surrounding circumstances to raise suspicion as to its credibility - Appeal dismissed.
