Indian Iron and Steel Company, Limited and Another v Their Workmen (and connected appeals)

Supreme Court of India

15 October 1957

Case Analysis

Bench

S.K. Das, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1957 Indlaw SC 57; AIR 1958 SC 130; 1958 (11) LLJ 260; 1958 SCJ 285; [1958] 1 S.C.R. 667

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure 1898, misa

Summary: Constitution - Labour & Industrial Law - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 2(1), 2(ss.)and 15 - (A)Whether Tribunal can reverse order of dismissal for misconduct passed by management? - Held, tribunal is not a court of appeal - Order of dismissal can be reversed only on want of good faith, victimization or unfair labour practice on part of management - (B)Whether Supreme Court can conduct fresh scrutiny of evidence in an appeal against decision of Industrial Tribunal? - Held, no fresh review of evidence unless exceptional or special circumstances exist or that substantial or grave injustice has been caused - Appeal No. 336 partly allowed - Others dismissed.
