Nasar Ali v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

14 February 1957

Case Analysis

Bench

J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1957 Indlaw SC 19; AIR 1957 SC 366; 1957 CRLJ 550; 1957 SCJ 392; [1957] 1 S.C.R. 657

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Criminal Procedure Code, 1898, ss.154, 145, 157, 252 and 286 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 5, 101, 102, 103, 104, 367, 157 and 145 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 114 and 302 - (A) Whether an FIR can be submitted as evidence? - Held, F.I.R can only be used to corroborate statement of maker u/s. 157 of Evidence Act or to contradict it u/s. 145 of that Act - Cannot be used as evidence against maker or to corroborate or contradict other witnesses -(B) Whether onus of proving guilt lies with prosecution? - Held, a person is presumed to be innocent until proved guilty - It is duty of prosecution to prove prisoner's guilt subject to any statutory exception -(C) Whether maxim 'Falsus in uno falsus in omnibus' is merely a rule of caution ? - Held, this maxim has not assumed status of a rule of law - It merely involves question of weight of evidence - Cannot be called 'a mandatory rule of evidence' - It is merely a rule of caution - Appeal dismissed.
