Ramgopal Ganpatrai Ruia and Another v State of Bombay

Supreme Court of India

08 October 1957

Case Analysis

Bench

Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, P. Govinda Menon, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1957 Indlaw SC 47; AIR 1958 SC 97; 1958 CRLJ 244; 1958 SCJ 266; [1958] 1 S.C.R. 618

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Committing Court, Code of Criminal Procedure 1898, misa

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898, ss. 423, 435, 436, 437 and 439 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 109 and 409 - Whether High Court has power to revise an order of discharge passed by Presidency Magistrate? - Magistrate trying case himself exclusively triable by Sessions Judge - Held, both courts below travelled beyond their proper limits for decision at stage at which case was before them - HC, in circumstances of case, had been taken into matters which should have been left to be determined at trial - They had to cover ground which had been so elaborately discussed in order of learned Presidency Magistrate - Court of Session will conduct trial and conclude proceedings with all reasonable speed and without any avoidable delay - No merits in appeal - Appeal dismissed accordingly.
