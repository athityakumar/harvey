Commissioner of Income Tax v Patiala Cement Company Limited

Supreme Court of India

17 May 1957

Case Analysis

Bench

J.L. Kapur, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, S.K. Das

Where Reported

1957 Indlaw SC 34; AIR 1957 SC 692; [1957] 32 ITR 333; 1957 SCJ 685; [1957] 1 S.C.R. 1161

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Patiala Income Tax Act

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Finance Act, 1950, s. 13 - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Patiala Income Tax Act , ss. 18(3a),18(7),33 - Incorporation - Auditing - Deduction - Notice - Super tax - Appeal under certificate of HC and question for decision related to applicability of 1922 Act for assessment years - Assessee company failed to deduct from out of remuneration paid to its managing agents, income-tax and supertax which, it under law was required to do - Company paid to its auditors auditing fees and from out of that sum it did not deduct income-tax and super-tax under provisions of Patiala Income tax Act - Two sums in dispute and for particular assessment year also assessee company failed to make deduction from remuneration paid to its managing agents - Income-tax Officer issued two demand notices for amounts u/ss. 18(3A) and 18(7) of the Patiala Income-tax Act - On appeal, Appellate Assistant Commissioner reduced the amount demanded but did not decide the question whether the assessee company was bound to make the deductions or not - Assessee/company appealed to Income-tax Appellate Tribunal and it held that u/s. 18(7) of the Patiala Income tax Act no order was required to be passed by Income-tax Officer and that no appeal lay to Appellate Assistant Commissioner against the order u/ s. 18(3A) as there was no provision for it under the Patiala Income-tax Act - High Court decided that in regard to the assessment year 1948-49, the law applicable was Patiala Income-tax Act and therefore no appeal lay to Appellate Assistant Commissioner but in regard to the assessment year 1949-50 the Indian law became applicable and therefore the order of the Income-tax Officer was appealable - Hence instant appeal - Whether assessee company was bound to make deductions - Held , a close reading of that provision will show that it saves the operation of the State law only in respect of 1948-49 or - any earlier period which is the period not included in the previous year (1949-50) for the purposes of assessment for the year 1950-51 - In other words, there remained no State- law of income-tax in operation, in any Part B State in the year 1949-50 - It appears that the error which has crept in the judgment of HC has been due to misreading the year 1949-50 as being assessment year and not accounting year - Therefore both for the assessment years 1948- 49 and 1949-50 the law applicable would be the Patiala Income-tax law and not the Indian Income-tax Act and consequently no appeal against the order of the' Income-tax Officer was competent - Order accordingly .
