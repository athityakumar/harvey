Rajvi Amar Singh v State of Rajasthan

Supreme Court of India

28 November 1957

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhi Ranjan Das, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, S.K. Das, A.K. Sarkar

Where Reported

1957 Indlaw SC 127; AIR 1958 SC 228; 1958 SCJ 420; [1958] S.C.R. 1015

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Constitution - Service - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 311- (A) Whether upon integration of old state of service with new state after adoption of Constitution, continuation of judge who served in old state amounts to absorbing them impliedly in permanent cadre by new state or only from date of fresh appointment? - Held, when one state absorbs into another state, all contracts of service between prior Govt. and its servants automatically terminate - To work under new conditions of new state - Orders interpreted - Order of transfer not to be interpreted as an order of appointment - Determination to post appellant permanently cannot be gathered from orders passed - (B) Whether new appointment as Civil Judge disregarding prior service amounts to demotion violating art. 311 of Constitution? - Held, new appointment is first substative appointment in new state for appellant - No reduction in rank - Art. 311 was not attracted - Prior postings were all transitional and temporary - Appeal dismissed.
