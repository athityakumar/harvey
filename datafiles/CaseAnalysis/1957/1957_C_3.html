 Babulal Amthalal Mehta v Collector of Customs, Calcutta

Supreme Court of India

08 May 1957

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Govinda Menon, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Syed Jaffer Imam, S.K. Das, A.K. Sarkar

Where Reported

1957 Indlaw SC 148; AIR 1957 SC 877; 1983 ECR 1657; 1957 SCJ 828; [1957] 1 S.C.R. 1110

Case Digest

Subject: Customs

Keywords: Prohibition Act, Sea Customs Act 1878, Import and Export (Control) Act. 1947, Board Of Revenue Act, 1913, Amending Act XXI of 1955

Summary: Customs - Board of Revenue Act, 1913 - Prohibition Act - Import and Export (Control) Act, 1947 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14 and 32 - Sea Customs Act 1878, ss. 19, 20, 86, 87, 89, 167, 169, 170, 172,178-A, 179, 181, 188 and 191 - Whether s. 178-A of Sea Customs Act, 1878 is violative of art. 14 of Constitution? - S. 178-A gives a well defined classification of goods based on an intelligible differentia It applies only to certain goods described in sub-s.(2) which are or can be easily smuggled - Section applies only to those goods of specified kind which have been seized under Act and in reasonable belief that they are smuggled goods - Differentia on basis of which goods have been classified and presumption raised by section obviously have a rational relation to object sought to be achieved by Act - Held, section is not hit by art. 14 and cannot be struck down on infirmity either of discrimination or illegal classification - Petition dismissed.
