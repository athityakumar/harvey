Asa Ram and Another v Mst. Ram Kali and Another

Supreme Court of India

21 November 1957

Case Analysis

Bench

T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, S.K. Das, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar

Where Reported

1957 Indlaw SC 65; AIR 1958 SC 183; 1958 (2) MLJ(SC) 32; 1958 SCJ 575; [1958] S.C.R. 988

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Municipalities & Local Govts - Transfer of Property Act (4 of 1882), ss. 105 and 76(a) - Evidence Act (1 of 1872), ss. 101, 102, 103 and 104 - Uttar Pradesh Tenancy Act (17 of 1939), s. 29(a) - (A) Whether accepting kabuliat by lessor gives status of tenant to lessee? - Held, that where no lease deed had been executed by landlords in favour of lessee, but latter had executed a Kabuliat, in favour of former and lessors accepted Kabuliat and received rent as provided therein, lessee cannot be denied status of tenant u/s. 105 of Transfer of Property Act (B) Does transfer effected by mortgagee cease on redemption of mortgage ?- Held, that law undoubtedly is that no person can transfer property so as to confer on transferee a title better than what he possesses therefore, any transfer of property mortgaged, by mortgagee must cease, when mortgage is redeemed - S. 76(a), T.P.Act, provides that a mortgagee in possession must manage property as a person of ordinary prudence would manage it if it were his own - An agricultural lease created by him would be binding on mortgagor even though mortgage has been redeemed, provided it is of such a character that a prudent owner of property would enter into it in usual course of management - Action of mortgagees in leasing lands to tenants on terms set out in Kabuliat was neither prudent nor bona fide, and on a consideration of entire evidence that lease evidenced by Kabuliat was not binding on mortgagors - Appeal allowed.
