Messrs. Crown Aluminium Works v Their Workmen

Supreme Court of India

15 October 1957

Case Analysis

Bench

Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, S.K. Das, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar

Where Reported

1957 Indlaw SC 142; AIR 1958 SC 30; 1958 (1) LLJ 1; [1958] 1 S.C.R. 651

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Fair Wage, Convention, Cost Of Living, Labour Appellate Tribunal, Indian Factories Act, 1948

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 10 - Indian Factories Act, 1948 - Constitution of wage structure - Whether payments constituted by appellant was purely concessional payments or constituents of wage structure? - Because these additional payments were made by appellant to its workmen, they did not raise any dispute - These payments have been made for some years and that also is a relevant factor to consider in dealing with true character of these payments - Held, payments are not matters of bounty but in essence and in substance form part of basic wage and dearness allowance payable to workmen - Appeal dissmissed.
