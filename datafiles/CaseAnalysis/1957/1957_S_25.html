State of Madras v Vaidyanatha Ayyar A

Supreme Court of India

26 September 1957

Case Analysis

Bench

J.L. Kapur, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, P. Govinda Menon

Where Reported

1957 Indlaw SC 9; AIR 1958 SC 61; 1958 CRLJ 232; 1958 (2) LLJ 653; 1958 (1) MLJ(SC) 136; 1958 SCJ 335; [1958] 1 S.C.R. 580

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes (Appellate Tribunal) Act, 1950, ss. 33 and 7(1)(a) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947, s. 4 - Whether scope of power of Supreme Court u/art. 136 extends to interfering with a judgment of acquittal? - Held, Supreme Court should not in appeal by special leave interfere with order of acquittal passed by High Court merely for correcting errors of fact of law - Phrasing of art. 136 shows that in criminal matters no distinction can be made as a matter of construction between a judgment of conviction or acquittal - Supreme Court should ideally interfere with findings of fact given by High Court only if High Court acts perversely or otherwise improperly - In this case, certain salient features of case were not properly appreciated by High Court - Judgment of acquittal passed by High Court should be set aside - (B) Whether nature of presumption u/s. 4 of Prevention of corruption Act is same as that under Indian Evidence Act? - Held, presumption u/s. 4 of Prevention of Corruption Act is a presumption of law - It is obligatory on court to raise this presumption in every case brought under this provision - Prevention of Corruption Act is in pari materia with Evidence Act - It deals with a branch of law of evidence - Therefore, it should have same meaning - Appeal allowed.
