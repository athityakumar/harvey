 Amarsingh v Custodian, Evacuee Property, Punjab

Supreme Court of India

29 March 1957

Case Analysis

Bench

B. Jagannadhadas, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, Syed Jaffer Imam, P. Govinda Menon, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1957 Indlaw SC 117; AIR 1957 SC 599; 1957 SCJ 574; [1957] 1 S.C.R. 801

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property

Keywords: Evacuee Property & Displaced Persons

Summary: Land & Property - Administration of Evacuee Property Act, 1950 - Administration of Evacuee Property Ordinance, 1949 - Displaced Persons (Claims) Act, 1950 - Displaced Persons (Compensation and Rehabilitation) Act, 1954 - Punjab Refugees (Registration of Land Claims) Act, 1948 - Punjab Land Revenue Act,1887 - Constitution Of India, 1950 - Administration of Evacuee Property (Central) Rules, 1950 - Displaced Persons Compensation and Rehabilitation Rules, 1955 - Whether interest of a quasi-permanent allottee is 'property' within concept of that word so as to attract protection of fundamental right? - Held, notwithstanding privilege of quasi-permanent allottee to continue in possession u/s. 10 and scope he has for obtaining, a transfer under same section and r. 72(2) of rules made thereunder, his allotment itself is liable to be cancelled u/s. 19 and r. 102 - He does not, therefore, appear to have an indefeasible right to obtain transfer of very land of which he is quasi-permanent allottee, if such land is acquired u/s. 12 of Act - Property, to fall within scope of art. 19(1)(f), must be capable of being subject-matter of 'acquisition and disposal' - Interest of quasi-permanent allottee arises by statutory grant to a specified class of persons and is not capable of acquisition by ordinary citizen in any of normal modes - Nor is it capable of disposal by allottee himself in normal modes by way of sale, mortgage, gift or will - Neither is interest of quasi-permanent allottee such as can be brought within scope of art. 31(2) - Petition dismissed.
