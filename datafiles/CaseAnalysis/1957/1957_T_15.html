Newspapers Limited v State Industrial Tribunal, U.P

Supreme Court of India

20 March 1957

Case Analysis

Bench

J.L. Kapur, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha

Where Reported

1957 Indlaw SC 24; AIR 1957 SC 532; 1957 (2) LLJ 1; 1957 SCJ 566; [1957] 1 S.C.R. 754

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Individual Dispute, misa, United Provinces Industrial Disputes Act 1947 (UP 28 of 1947) (ENG), Trade Disputes Act

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - United Provinces Industrial Disputes Act 1947 (UP 28 of 1947) (ENG) - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947,r. 12 (ii), ss. 2, 2 (k) - Settlement - Conciliation - Maintainability - Respondent No. 3 was dismissed on, allegations of incompetence - It was alleged that dismissal of respondent No. 3 was welcomed by his co-workers and other workmen in employ of appellant company and they made no grievance of it, nor did they espouse his cause - Case of respondent No. 3 was not taken up by any union of workers of appellant company - U.P. Working Journalists Union with which respondent No. 3 had no connection took matter to Conciliation Board - State Govt. made reference to Tribunal - Tribunal decided in favour of respondent No. 3 and ordered reinstatement - Appeal was filed by appellant company to Appellate Tribunal, which by order affirmed order of Tribunal - Appellant company further moved petition in HC u/art. 226 of Constitution but was dismissed - Special appeal against judgment was also dismissed - Appellant company filed appeal with certificate u/art. 133 (1) (c) of Constitution - Hence, instant appeal - Whether dispute between employer and single workman falls within definition of 'industrial dispute' as used in the 1947 Act - Held, dispute raised by individual workman as to his personal grievance was within industrial dispute, it could not be said to have been correctly decided - As held in State of Madras v. C. P.Sarathy ( 1952 Indlaw SC 83, 347.) in spite of making reference by Govt. under the 1947 Act was exercise of its administrative powers, that was not destructive of rights of aggrieved party to show that what was referred was not 'industrial dispute' at all and therefore jurisdiction of Tribunal to make award could be questioned, even though factual existence of dispute may not be subject to party's challenge - Points of dispute in reference comprised wrongful termination of service of only respondent no. 3 - Words used in first part of notification show that Govt. was labouring under misapprehension that this dispute was between employer and workmen which, in fact was not - Respondent no. 3 could not be termed workmen (in plural) nor could U. P. Working Journalists Union be called 'his workmen' nor was there any indication that individual dispute had got transformed into industrial dispute - Appeal allowed.
