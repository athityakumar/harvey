Indu Bhusan Chatterjee v State of West Bengal

Supreme Court of India

26 November 1957

Case Analysis

Bench

Syed Jaffer Imam, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1957 Indlaw SC 129; AIR 1958 SC 148; 1958 CRLJ 279; 1958 SCJ 581; [1958] S.C.R. 1001

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Keywords: Illegal Gratification

Summary: Criminal - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947, s. 6 - Whether sanction granted u/s. 6 of Prevention of Corruption Act was valid?- Held, sanction granted under s. 6 was perfectly valid - Statement of sanctioning authority did not prove that he merely put his signature on readymade sanction presented by police without applying his mind to facts of case - It was not for him to judge truth of allegations made against accused by calling for records of connected claim cases or other records in connection with matter from his office - Papers which were placed before him apparently gave him necessary material upon which he decided that it was necessary in ends of justice to accord his sanction - Appeal allowed.
