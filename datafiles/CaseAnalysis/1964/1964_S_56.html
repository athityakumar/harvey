Municipal Board, Manglaur v Sri Mahadeoji Maharaj

Supreme Court of India

24 November 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, Raghubar Dayal, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 367; AIR 1965 SC 1147; [1965] 2 S.C.R. 242

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Plaintiff is owner of plot, through which runs a public road and two nalis on north and south of said road - There is also a water pipe running through said plot which belongs to defendant Municipality - There is a vacant site lying in between nalis and road - Whether site was part of road which vested in Municipality? - Held, admittedly, public have bean using road for decades - Municipal Board has been maintaining road and drains - It is, therefore, reasonable to hold that entire pathway between two drains was dedicated to public - Such a public pathway vests in Municipality, but Municipality does not own soil - Subject to rights of Municipality and public to pass and repass on highway, owner of soil in general remains occupier of it and, therefore, he can maintain an action for trespass against any member of public who acts in excess of his rights - But plaintiff cannot ask for possession of any part of public pathway, as it continues to vest in Municipality - Plaintiff would be entitled to a decree for permanent injunction restraining Municipality from putting up said structures on a part of said public pathway, and suit in so far as it asked for a decree for possession would be liable to be dismissed - Appeal partly allowed.
