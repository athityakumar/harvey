Sant Ram and Others v Labh Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

15 April 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, K.C. Das Gupta

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 145; AIR 1965 SC 314; 1964 ALJ 852; [1964] 7 S.C.R. 756

Case Digest

Subject: Contract & Commercial

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Constitution - Rewa State Pre-emption Act, 1946, s. 10 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 19(1)(f) and 19(5) - (A) Whether law of pre-emption is contrary to provisions of art. 19(1)(f) r/w art. 13 of Constitution, or is it saved by cl. (5) of art. 19?. - (B) Whether expression 'all laws in force' includes customs and usage- Held, (A) law of pre-emption imposed unreasonable restrictions on the vendor and vendee in terms of right to acquire, hold and to dispose of property contrary to art. 19(1)(f) of Constitution and was void - Restrictions was placed on both vendor and on vendee and there was no advantage to general public and only reason given in support of it, that it prevented persons belonging to different religions, races or castes from acquiring property in any area peopled by persons of other religious, races or castes, could not be considered reasonable in view of art. 15 of Constitution - Further held, in view of above ruling, present appeal succeeds - Respondent No. 1 cannot now sustain decree in view of prescriptions of Constitution and decision in Bhau Ram's case (1962 Indlaw SC 540) has been follwed - (B) Expression 'all laws in force' includes custom and usage - Appeal allowed.
