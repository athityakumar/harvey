Uttamchand v S. M. Lalwani

Supreme Court of India

28 August 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 446; AIR 1965 SC 716

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Rent Control

Keywords: Fixtures, Reasonable Annual Rent, Madhya Pradesh Accommodation Control Act 1955

Summary: Land & Property - Rent Control - Madhya Pradesh Accommodation Control Act 1955, ss. 2, 3, 4 and 9 - Whether lease executed by respondent in favour of appellant can be said to be a lease of an accommodation within meaning of s. 3 of Act ? - S. 3(a)(y)(3) takes within definition of accommodation any building or part of a building, including any fittings affixed to such building or part of a building for more beneficial enjoyment thereof and fittings to which s. 3(a)(y)(3) refers are obviously fittings made in building to afford incidental amenities for person occupying building - Held, it is mill which is subject-matter of lease, and it was because mill was intended to be let out that building had inevitably to be let out along with mill and this is a case where appellant entered into lease for purpose of running Dal mill which was located in building, a mill, of this kind will have to be located in some building or another, and so, mere fact that lease purports to be in respect of building will not make it a lease in respect of an accommodation as defined by s. 3(a)(y)(3), fixtures described in schedule to lease are in no sense intended for more beneficial enjoyment of building and fixtures are primary object which lease was intended to cover and building in which fixtures are located comes in incidentally, therefore High Court was right in coming to conclusion that rent which appellant had agreed to pay to respondent under document in question cannot be said to be rent payable for any accommodation to which Act applies, fact that a mill situated in a building is not expressly exempted by s. 2(1) would hardly make any difference, because no lease can attract provisions of Act unless it is shown that it is in respect of accommodation as defined by s. 3(a)- Appeal dismissed.
