Management of Delhi Transport Undertaking v Industrial Tribunal, Delhi

Supreme Court of India

30 October 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 214; AIR 1965 SC 1503; 1965 (10) FLR 236; 1965 (1) LLJ 458; [1965] 1 S.C.R. 998

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Delhi Road Transport Authority Act, 1950

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Labour & Industrial Law - Delhi Road Transport Authority Act, 1950 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 33 - Delhi Road Transport Authority (Conditions of Appointment and Service) Regulations, 1952, Para 15(2), No 2, No 19(m) - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 33 - (A) Whether proviso to s. 33 indicates that wages for one month would have to be actually paid?- Held, it does not mean so - Since in many cases employer can only tender amount before dismissal but cannot force employee to receive payment before dismissal becomes effective - Making of tender of amount before order of dismissal becomes effective would be sufficient compliance in this respect -(B)Whether promotion of employee dismissed can affect dismissal order?- Promotion of employee in due course during period after passing of order of dismissal can not be reason review order of dismissal -(C) Delhi Road Transport Authority Act - Whether conductor charged for a breach of certain executive instruction brings in application of Standing Order No 19 (m) r/w Standing Order No. 2 and para 15 (2) of Regulations? - Further held, Executive Instructions issued to conductors under Standing Order No. 2 are a code of principles and practice which every conductor has to follow rigidly and invariably and there is a warning that a breach of any instruction would expose conductor to disciplinary action as laid down in para 15 (2) of Regulation - Cl. (m) of Standing order No. 19 is sufficiently wide to cover a breach of instructions issued, under Standing Order No - When facts are quite sufficient, omission to mention appropriate Standing Order, Regulation and Sections of Act does not amount to a flaw in charge - Appeal allowed.
