Sarju Prasad v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

20 August 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

J.R. Madholkar, A.K. Sarkar, Raghubar Dayal

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 449; AIR 1965 SC 843; 1965 CRLJ 766

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 307 - Probation of Offenders Act, 1958, s. 6 - Whether act of appellant amounts to an offence u/s. 307 - Witnesses deposed that appellant used a chhura, injury was inflicted on a vital part of body but no vital organ of body was injured - It cannot be ascertained that chura was sufficiently long to cause injury to a vital organ - Evidence as to size could not be elicited, further no evidence that appellant inflicted or even tried to inflict any further injury - Held, prosecution has not established that offence committed by appellant falls squarely u/s. 307, it amounts only to an offence u/s. 324 - Matter remanded to High Court to pass appropriate order applying provisions of s. 6 - Appeal partly allowed.
