 Bishun Narain Mishra v State of Uttar Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 October 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Mohammad Hidayatullah, Raghubar Dayal, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 162; AIR 1965 SC 1567; 1966 (1) LLJ 45; [1965] 1 S.C.R. 693

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Age Of Superannuation

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Labour & Industrial Law - Delhi Road Transport Authority Act, 1950 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 33 - Delhi Road Transport Authority (Conditions of Appointment and Service) Regulations, 1952, para. 15(2), No 2, No 19(m) - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 33 - (A) Whether proviso to s. 33 indicate that wages for one month would have to be actually paid?- Held, it does not mean so - Since in many cases employer can only tender amount before dismissal but cannot force employee to receive payment before dismissal becomes effective - Making of tender of amount before order of dismissal becomes effective would be sufficient compliance in this respect - (B)Whether promotion of employee dismissed can affect dismissal order? - Further held, promotion of employee in due course during period after passing of order of dismissal can not be reason review order of dismissal - (C) Delhi Road Transport Authority Act - Whether conductor charged for a breach of certain executive instruction brings in application of Standing Order No.19 (m) r/w Standing Order No. 2 and para. 15 (2) of Regulations?- Further held, executive Instructions issued to conductors under Standing Order No 2 are a code of principles and practice which every conductor has to follow rigidly and invariably and there is a warning that a breach of any instruction would expose conductor to disciplinary action as laid down in para. 15 (2) of Regn. - Cl. (m) of Standing order No 19 is sufficiently wide to cover a breach of instructions issued, under Standing Order No - When facts are quite sufficient, omission to mention appropriate Standing Order, Regulation and Sections of Act do not amount to a flaw in charge - Appeal allowed.
