 A. P. Krishnasami Naidu v State of Madras(With connected Petitions

Supreme Court of India

09 March 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, S.M. Sikri, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 175; AIR 1964 SC 1515; 1965 (1) MLJ(SC) 14; [1964] 7 S.C.R. 82

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Constitutionality, Madras Land Reforms (Fixation of Ceiling On Land) Act, 1961

Summary: Constitution - Land & Property - Socio-Economic - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - T N Land Reforms (Fixation of Ceiling on Land) Act, 1961, ss. 5(1) and 50, Chaps. II and III - (A) Whether only provisions contained only in s. 50 r/w Sch. III is violative of Constitution or whole Act is violative? - Held, provision of s. 5(1) results in discrimination between persons equally circumstanced, violative of art. 14 of Constitution - Provisions contained in s. 50 r/w Sch III of Act with respect to compensation are discriminatory and violate art. 14 of Constitution - Ss. 5 and 50 are pivotal provisions of Act, and if they fall then whole Act must be struck down as unconstitutional - Working of entire Act depends on s. 5 which provides for a ceiling and s. 50 which provides for compensation - If these sections are unconstitutional, whole Act must fall - Petition allowed.
