Commissioner of Income Tax, Bihar v Dalmia Investment Company Limited

Supreme Court of India

13 March 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sarkar, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 305; AIR 1964 SC 1464; [1964] 52 ITR 567; 1964 (2) SCJ 546; [1964] 7 S.C.R. 210

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income-Tax Act, 1961 - Bonus shares - Determination of value - How to determine the cost of acquisition of bonus shares for ascertaining the profits made on a sale of them? - The assessee had acquired bonus shares at their face value - Referred Bai Shirinbai Kooka's case - Bonus shares must have been acquired at the market value of the date of their issue - The face value of the bonus shares cannot be taken to be dividend - Held, the bonus shares can be valued by spreading the cost of the old shares over the old shares and the new issues taken together, if the shares rank pari passu - Appeal allowed.
