Shyamlal Mohanlal v State of Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

14 December 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 361; AIR 1965 SC 1251; 1965 CRLJ 256; 1965 (2) SCJ 18; [1965] 2 S.C.R. 457

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: S. M. Sikri, J.

(A) Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, s.94 - Moneylenders' Act - Production of document - Applicability of provision - Respondent was registered moneylender doing business as moneylender and he was required to maintain books according to provisions of the Moneylenders' Act and the Rules but he was prosecuted for failing to maintain books by Judicial First Class Magistrate - Police Prosecutor presented application praying that Court be pleased to order respondent to produce daily account book and ledger to Magistrate but Magistrate refused to accede to prayer - State filed revision before Sessions Judge in which matter would have to be referred back to Magistrate to ascertain - Reference was made to HC and HC agreeing with Sessions Judge held that s. 94 of CrPC did not apply to accused person - Hence, instant Appeals - Whether s. 94 of CrPC was applicable to accused person - Held, it was true that words of s. 94 of CrPC were wide enough to include accused person but it was well-recognized that in some cases limitation could be put on construction of wide terms of statute - Thus, if it construed s. 94 of CrPC to include accused person, that construction was likely to lead to grave hardship for accused and make investigation unfair to him - Therefore, agreeing with HC, s. 94 of CrPC on its true construction, did not apply to accused person - Appeals dismissed.

J.C. Shah J.

(B) Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, ss.94, 96 - Production of document - Applicability of provisions - Whether s. 94 of CrPC was applicable to accused person - Held, s. 94 of CrPC authorized any Court, or any officer in charge of police-station to issue summons or written order to person in whose possession or power such document or thing was believed to be, requiring such person to attend and produce it, at time and place indicated in summons or order - S.94 of CrPC conferred on Court power to issue summons and on police officer to make order to any person demanding production of document or thing believed to be in possession of that person - Indisputably person referred to in s. 94(2) of CrPC was same person who was summoned or ordered to produce document or thing - However, if u/s. 94(1) of CrPC summons could not be issued against person accused of offence, search warrant u/s. 96(1) of CrPC could evidently not be issued in respect of document or thing in his possession - It was open to State to call for information which could incriminate person giving information and under certain statutes obligation was imposed upon person even if he stands in danger of being subsequently arraigned as accused, to give information in respect of transaction with which he was concerned - Therefore, person accused of offence could not in exercise of power u/s. 94(1) of CrPC be called upon to produce documents or things in his possession, on assumption that rule of protection against self-incrimination was introduced - Appeals allowed.
