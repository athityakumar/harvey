Saran Motors Private, Limited, New Delhi v Vishwanath and Another

Supreme Court of India

31 March 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 27; 1964 (9) FLR 77; 1964 (2) LLJ 139

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 33 - Domestic enquiries conducted by enquiry officer, a lawyer - Whether biased? - Domestic enquiries in industrial relations must be fairly conducted and should be supported by evidence - If enquiry is conducted in a fair manner and conclusions by enquiry officer is supported by an evidence then tribunal is bound by findings of enquiry officer - Held, domestic enquiry was fairly conducted, a reasonable opportunity was given to respondents to prove their case and conclusions recorded at enquiry are supported by evidence which was believed by enquiry officer - Appeals allowed.
