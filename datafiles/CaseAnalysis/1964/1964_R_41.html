 Raja Birakishore v State of Orissa

Supreme Court of India

05 March 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, S.M. Sikri, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 149; AIR 1964 SC 1501; 1964 (2) SCJ 682; [1964] 7 S.C.R. 32

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Constitutionality, Shri Jagannath Temple Act, 1954

Summary: Trust & Association - Constitution - Shri Jagannath Temple Act, 1954, s. 6 - Orissa Hindu Religious Endowments Act, 1952 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 19(1)(f)/(5), 14 and 31(2) - Constitutionality of Act, challenged - Appellant claimed Shri Jagannath Temple(Temple) to be private property of petitioner, his father - Further he claimed that he had sole right of superintendence and management of Temple - Thus he contended violation of various arts of Constitution - High Court repelled all contentions and submissions of appellant and upheld validity of Act - Attack on Constitutionality of Act, whether acceptable? - Object and scheme of Act looked into, provisions perused - Held, Act provides for management of secular affairs of Temple and does not interfere, with religious affairs thereof, which have to be performed according to record of rights prepared under Act of 1952 and where there is no such record of rights in accordance with custom and usage obtaining in Temple - Temple occupies a unique position in State of Orissa and is a temple of national importance and no other temple in that State can compare with it, stands in a class by itself and considering fact that it attracts pilgrims from all over India in large numbers it must be a subject of special consideration by State Govt., thus finding given by court that temple stands in a class by itself in State of Orissa and therefore requires special treatment, thus no violation of art. 14 (Tilkayat Shri Govindlalji v. State of Rajasthan, 1964) - On attack on ground of taking away of sole right of management, court noted preamble of Act, history of temple, its importance, concerns taken for it by Britishers, successive rulers till stage when Raja of Puri was entrusted therewith - Preamble says that administration under Superintendent has further deteriorated and a situation has arisen rendering it expedient to reorganise scheme of management of affairs of Temple and its properties and provide better administration and governance therefore in supersession of all previous laws, regulations and arrangements, having regard to ancient customs and usages and unique and traditional nitis and rituals contained in record of rights prepared under 1952 Act - So for all these reasons appellant was removed from sole superintendence of Temple and a committee was appointed by s. 6 of Act for its management - Thus secular management of Temple was taken away from sole control of appellant and vested with Committee - Provisions are not hit by arts. 31(2) or 19(f) - Another constitutional issue as to rights of appellant as 'adya sevak' being taken away by provisions which are unreasonable and thus cannot be protected u/art. 19(5), raised - Rights as 'adya sevak' left intact other than those of superintendence - Further, s. 8 of Act 1954, preserves by clearest implication rights of appellant as adya sevak in connection with sevapuja of Lord Jagannath - Further, challenge to vires of Act, 1954 on ground that it takes away rights, privileges and perquisites of appellant as adya sevak, to be rejected - Further contentions and submissions, dismissed - Appeal dismissed.
