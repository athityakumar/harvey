Official Receiver, Kanpur And Another. v Abdul Shakur And Others

Supreme Court of India

11 September 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 216; AIR 1965 SC 920; 1965 ALJ 482; 1965 (2) MLJ(SC) 152; [1965] 1 S.C.R. 254

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance

Summary: Banking & Finance - Provincial Insolvency Act, 1920, ss. 33, 80 and 49 - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, s. 118 - (A) What is nature of proceedings u/s. 33 of Provincial Insolvency Act and how are promissory notes falling under same section examined by courts?- Held, proceeding u/s. 33 of Provincial Insolvency Act is between creditors (represented by Official Receiver) and insolvent - There is a presumption that promissory note was made for consideration u/s. II 8 of Negotiable Instruments Act therefore, unless that presumption is rebutted by promissory or by other creditors or by receiver that amount for which promissory note is executed is included in schedule - (B) Whether s. 188 of Negotiable Instruments Act works independent of rules u/s. 144 of evidence act? - Further held, u/s. 118 of Negotiable Instruments Act a rule of evidence operates between parties to instruments or persons claiming for suit or proceeding relating to Bill of Exchange which operates independent of rules u/s. 114 of Evidence act - Appeal allowed.
