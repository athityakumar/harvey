Sunil Kumar Paul v State of West Bengal

Supreme Court of India

06 March 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

Raghubar Dayal, K. Subba Rao, K.C. Das Gupta

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 155; AIR 1965 SC 706; 1965 CRLJ 630; [1964] 7 S.C.R. 70

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure 1898, West Bengal Criminal Law Amendment (Special Courts) Act, 1949

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 420 and 409 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898 - West Bengal Criminal Law Amendment (Special Courts) Act, 1949 - Appellant was a clerk in office of Sub-Divisional Health Officer at Barrackpore in 1955-56 - He used to prepare bills of establishment, to present them at, sub-treasury and later present them at State Bank at Barrackpore, to receive payment in cash and to make over that amount to Sub Divisional Health Officer - This bill was duly passed by sub-treasury and was subsequently presented to Bank - 'The Bank paid this amount to appellant amount was not paid to Sub-Divisional Health Officer - In fact, records of Office of Sub-Divisional Health Officer did not refer to any such bill being prepared and submitted to sub-treasury and bank for payment - Fact of presentation of a bill for its encashment on 06-10-1956 came to notice of Sub-Divisional Health Officer at instance of Accountant General and on enquiry it was found that no such bill had been actually presented by his office for encashment and that no such amount was received by him - This led to a complaint and further enquiries and investigation which ended in prosecution of appellant - Special Judge tried appellant for that offence and convicted him and sentenced him to rigorous imprisonment for two years and to pay a fine of Rs. 2,000 - Appellant went in appeal to High Court of Calcutta HC agreed with appellant's contenion that no offence u/s. 409 IPC, 1860 had been made out, but held that he was proved to have committed an offence u/s. 420 IPC, 1860 - It accordingly altered his conviction from an offence u/s. 409 IPC, 1860 to one u/s. 420 IPC, 1860 for cheating employees of State Bank - Whether Special Court could try appellant for offence u/s. 420 IPC, 1860? - Held, it has also been urged for appellant that proviso to s. 4 does not give any power to Special Court to try an offence which be independent of offence mentioned in allotment order - That is to say, Special Court, in this case, could have tried appellant only for such offences which will be in some way related to offence u/s. 409 IPC, 1860 - It is further urged that ingredients of offence u/s. 409 IPC, 1860 are absolutely different from ingredients of offence u/s. 409 IPC, 1860 - Ingredients of two must be different from one another and it is therefore not necessary to consider whether ingredients of two offences are in any way related - Court has to see, for purpose of proviso, whether accused could be charged with any offence, other than one referred to in allotment order, in view of provisions of Code - There is nothing in proviso which could lead to construction that any limitations other than those laid down by provisions of CrPC, 1898 were to affect nature of offence which could be tried by Special Court - Therefore, Special Court could try appellant for offence u/s. 409 IPC, 1860 and that therefore HC was right in altering his conviction from that u/ss. 409 - 420 IPC, 1860 - Appeal dismissed.
