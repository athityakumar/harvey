Suraj Ratan Thirani and Others v Azamabad Tea Company and Others

Supreme Court of India

13 January 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 430; AIR 1965 SC 295; 1965 (1) ALT(SC) 82; [1964] 6 S.C.R. 192

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Ex-Parte

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 9, r. 9 - Whether High Court was right in holding that suit by plaintiff-appellant was barred by O. 9, r. 9? - Appellant claimed title and recovery of possession of property known as Azambad tea estate - Appellant claimed title of under purchasers for heirs of Azam Ali who was full owner of entire property - Property covered by tea estate was granted by Govt. by way of lease for 30 years - After death of actual owner his eldest son was entered in official records as next in succession - Considerable sum was taken from National Co. Ltd - Amount under mortgage not paid so property went to respondent - Held, suit substantially barred by O. 9, r. 9 - Facts on which plaintiff based their right and their right to relief was identical in two suits and property sought to be recovered in two suits was same - Matter remitted back to Trial Court for recording finding as regards reality of sale - Appeal dismissed.
