Kurban Hussein Mohammedali Rangwalla v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

15 December 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 362; AIR 1965 SC 1616; 1965 CRLJ 550; [1965] 2 S.C.R. 622

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 304 A and 285 - (A) Whether death u/s. 304A must be causa causans? - Held, death must be direct result of rash or negligent act of accused and act must be efficient cause without intervention of another's negligence - It must be causa causans - It is not enough that it may have been causa sine qua non - (B) Whether negligent omission u/s. 285 means probable danger or possible danger - Further held, act may be 'probable danger' to human life, viz, factory-workers, and not simply a 'possible danger' on ground that no fire broke out earlier - Appeal partly allowed.
