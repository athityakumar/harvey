 Suganmal v State of Madhya Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 November 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

Raghubar Dayal, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 235; AIR 1965 SC 1740; 1965 (1) ITJ 472; [1965] 56 ITR 84; 1965 (16) STC 398

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Constitution - Indirect Taxes - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Indore Industrial Tax Rules, 1927, r. 8(a) - (A) Whether mandamus can be issued solely for refund of tax to Appellate Court setting aside the assessment? - Held, mandamus cannot be issued to Appellate Court solely for refund of tax illegally collected by the state - Reason being that a claim for such a refund can always be made in a suit against the authority which had illegally collected the money as a tax where state can raise defenses - (B) Whether order of the appellate authority that tax collected was not authorized by any law means that the state is to return the amount? - Held, order of the appellate authority that the tax collected was not authorized by any law is not a decision to the effect that the state is to return the amount to the assessee - It cannot be taken to amount to a law making it incumbent on the state to refund the amount to the assessee - Appeal dismissed.
