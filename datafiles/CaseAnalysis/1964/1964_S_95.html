Faddi v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

24 January 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

Raghubar Dayal, Mohammad Hidayatullah

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 426; AIR 1964 SC 1850; 1964 CRLJ 744; [1964] 6 S.C.R. 312

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 21 and 25 - Code of Criminal Procedure, s. 162 - Whether a FIR which is not a confession cannot be used as an admission u/s 21 of Evidence Act, 1872 or as a relevant statement under any other provision of that Act? - S. 25 of Evidence Act, 1872 and s. 162 of CrPC do not bar admissibility of FIR? - A confessional FIR cannot be used against maker when he be an accused and necessarily cannot be used against a co-accused - Held, FIR is not a confession of appellant - It is not a statement made to a police officer during course of investigation - FIR is an admission by accused of certain facts which have a bearing on question to be determined by court - Objection to admissibility of first information report lodged by appellant is not sound and that courts below have rightly admitted it in evidence and have made proper use of it - Appeal dismissed.
