 Poona City Municipal Corporation v Dattatraya Nagesh Deodher

Supreme Court of India

05 May 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

K.C. Das Gupta, J.C. Shah, Mohammad Hidayatullah, P. Govinda Menon, Raghubar Dayal

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 350; AIR 1965 SC 555; [1964] 8 S.C.R. 178

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax

Keywords: Bombay District Municipal Act, 1901, Bombay Prohibition Act 1949

Summary: Indirect Tax - Bombay Municipal Boroughs Act, 1925 - Bombay District Municipal Act, 1901 - Bombay Provincial Municipal Corporation Act, 1949 - Bombay Prohibition Act 1949 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Octroi Rules, r. 18(3) Issue arise - (A) Whether deduction of 10 percent on refund of octroi as provided for in r. 18(3) is invalid from 15-02-1950? - Held, a tax on octroi refund cannot be imposed by Bombay Municipal Corporation it not being a specified taxes - Nor is it a tax which State Legislature has power under Constitution to impose in state - Further, earlier parts of s. 127, categorically prohibits in sub-section 4 imposition of such tax - (B) Whether plaintiff was not entitled to bring a suit for refund of amount? - Plaintiff was not person who paid amount was claiming same on behalf of his prinicpals - Further held, plaintiff is 'a claimant' u/r. 2(g) - Hence, can institute suit - (C) Whether suit brought by plaintiff was barred by limitation? - Held suit not barred by limitation - Plea of limitation can only be raised for an act done or purported to be done in pursuance or execution or intended execution of this Act - Appeal dismissed.
