Commissioner of Income Tax, Madras v Express Newspapers Limited, Madras

Supreme Court of India

07 May 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, J.C. Shah, S.M. Sikri

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 349; AIR 1965 SC 33; [1964] 53 ITR 250; 1964 (2) SCJ 405; [1964] 8 S.C.R. 189

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s 10(2) (vii)(proviso II) - Winding up of company - Sale of machinery - Assessment of capital gains - Liability to pay tax - Free Press Company carried on business as printers and publishers of certain newspapers - Free Press Company passed a resolution transferred to assessee company, the right to print and publish newspapers from 1-9-1946 letting out its machinery and assets and authorized assessee company to collect book debts and pay off liabilities of Free Press Company - Free Press Company resolved at a General Body Meeting to wind up company voluntarily - Profit of Free Press Company worked out in two items that profit under proviso to s.10(2)(vii) was Rs.2,14,090 and capital gains u/s.12B was Rs. 3,94,576 and assessed each to tax - Income Tax Officer included two items in total income of assessee company - Income Tax Appellate Tribunal uphold validity of inclusion of item under capital gains in total income of assessee but decided against inclusion of first item - Appellate Tribunal referred questions to HC that whether Free Press Company made a business profit of Rs. 2,14,090 under proviso to s.10(2)(vii) of 1922 Act and whether capital gain made by Free Press Company was liable to assessed in the hands of assessee company u/s. 26(2) of Act - HC answered questions in negative and against department - Hence instant appeal - Whether two items were liable to tax on part of assessee company - Held, second proviso would only apply to sale of such machinery which was used for purpose of business during accounting year - It brought in to charge escaped profits under guise of superfluous allowances if machinery sold was used for business during accounting year when business was carried on - First item was not liable to tax and HC gave correct answer to first question submitted to it - Second item related to capital gains represented excess of price obtained on sale of machinery over its original cost price - Scope of s.26(2) only limited to income from business - Agree with HC in regard to answer given in respect of second question - Appeal dismissed.
