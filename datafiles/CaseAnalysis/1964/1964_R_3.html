Commissioner of Income Tax, Punjab v R. D. Aggarwal and Company and Another

Supreme Court of India

06 October 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, S.M. Sikri, K. Subba Rao

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 254; AIR 1965 SC 1526; [1965] 56 ITR 20; [1965] 1 S.C.R. 660

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income tax & Direct taxes - Income tax Act, ss.4,29 and 42 - Taxable income of non- Residents - 'the assessees' are a registered firm, place of business at Amritsar - Assessees carry on business as importers and as commission agent of non-resident exporters -(A)Whether there was between non-resident companies and assessees such a relation as may be called 'business connection' in taxable territories ? - U/s 42 of Income Tax Act income, profit or gain which accrues or arises to a non-resident outside taxable territories is sought to be brought within net of income-tax law, and not income, profit or gain which accrues or arises or is deemed to accrue or arise within taxable territories -Held, no business connection of assessees with non-residents within taxable territories resulted - Activity of assessees in procuring orders was not as agents of non-residents in matter of sale of goods manufactured by latter, nor of procuring raw materials in taxable territories for their manufacturing process - (B) Whether Income not taxable u/s 4 Income Tax Act is taxable later? - Further held, Income not taxable u/s 4 of Act of a non-resident becomes taxable u/s 42(1) of Income Tax Act if there subsists a connection between activity in taxable territories and business of nonresident, and if through or from that connection income directly or indirectly arises - Petition dismissed.
