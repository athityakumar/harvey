Afrahim Sheikh and Others v State of West Bengal

Supreme Court of India

07 January 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, Raghubar Dayal

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 432; AIR 1964 SC 1263; 1964 ALJ 437; 1964 CRLJ 350; [1964] 6 S.C.R. 172

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 304 Part II r/w s. 34 - Whether s. 34 could be read in conjunction with Part II of s. 304? - S. 304 is in two parts, first part is concerned with culpable homicide committed with two types of intention and second part with culpable homicide committed with a particular knowledge - Whether there was common intention between appellants or not? - Common intention which is requisite for application of s. 34 is common intention of perpetrating a particular act - Previous concert which is insisted upon is meeting of minds regarding achievement of a criminal act - Held, that circumstance is completely fulfilled in a case like present where a large number of persons attack an individual, chase him, throw him on ground and beat him till he dies - Further held, common intention is with regard to criminal act, i.e., the act of beating - If result of beating is death of victim, and if each of assailants possesses knowledge that death is likely consequence of criminal act, i.e, beating,ss. 34 or 35 should be r/w Part II of s. 304 to make each liable individually - Conviction of appellants upheld - Appeal dismissed.
