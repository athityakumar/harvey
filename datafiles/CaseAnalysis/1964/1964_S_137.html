 State of Rajasthan v Mukanchand and Others

Supreme Court of India

26 February 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 110; AIR 1964 SC 1633; 1964 (2) SCJ 586; [1964] 6 S.C.R. 903

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Civil Procedure; Constitution; Land & Property

Keywords: Debt Laws

Summary: Land & Property - Constitution - Rajasthan Jagirdars' Debt Reduction Act, 1957, s. 2(e) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - (A) Whether portion of s. 2(e) of Rajasthan Jagirdars' Debt Reduction Act, excluding certain debts due to creditors mentioned in cls. (i) to (vi) infringes art. 14 of Constitution? - Held, yes it does and therefore is invalid - Rest of section excluding this part is valid - Portion does not satisfy test of permissible classification - Fact that debts are owed to a Govt. or local authority or other bodies mentioned in impugned part of s. 2(e) has no rational relationship with object sought to be achieved by Act - (B) Whether s. 7(2) of Rajasthan Jagirdars' Debt Reduction Act is valid? - Further held, it is valid - It imposes reasonable restrictions in interest of general public on rights of secured creditor - Appeal partly allowed.
