 Jyoti Prokash Mitter v Hon'ble Justice Himansu Kumar Bose, Chief Justice, High Court, Calcutta and Another

Supreme Court of India

09 November 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Mohammad Hidayatullah, Raghubar Dayal, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 374; AIR 1965 SC 961; [1965] 2 S.C.R. 53

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Service

Summary: Advocates & judges - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 217, 217(3) and 226(1) - Constitution (Fifth Amendment) Act, 1963, s. 4 - (A) Who has power to take decision regarding age of Judges? - Held, retrospective operation of art. 217(3) postulates that this provision must be read in Constitution as from 26-01-1950 and so, it will apply even in regard to determination of ages of judges of High Courts who had been appointed to their office before actual provision was inserted in Constitution by Amendment Act of 1963 - It vests jurisdiction to determine question about judge's age exclusively in President - No court can claim jurisdiction - Procedural questions and other allied questions pertaining to decision, are entirely within discretion of President - (B) Whether decision reached by Home Minister after consulting Chief Justice of India could be taken as decision of President - Further held, mandatory requirement of cl. 3 that before President reaches his decision, he has to consult Chief Justice of India - Judge must be given a reasonable opportunity to give his version in support of age stated by him at time of his appointment and produce his evidence in that behalf - President's decision is final, and its propriety, correctness, or validity is beyond reach of jurisdiction of courts - Decision reached by Home Minister after consulting Chief Justice of India is not decision of President - Procedure followed by Govt. does not fit with formal consultation made mandatory u/art. 217(3) - Union of India will place matter before president to decide question about age u/art. 217(3) - (C) Should a judge be made to step down during period when dispute is raised ? - Further held, a judge cannot cease to be a judge merely because a dispute has been raised about his age and same is being considered by President - Judge should not be required to step down from his office - But in dealing with this legal position, considerations of prudence and expediency cannot be ignored - If a dispute arises about age of a judge any prudent and wise Chief Justice would naturally think of avoiding unnecessary complications by refusing to assign any work to sitting judge if, at time when dispute had been raised, it appears that allegation is that at relevant time judge in question has reached age of superannuation - Appeal disposed of.
