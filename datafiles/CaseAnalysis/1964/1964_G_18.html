 Union Of India And Others v Gwalior Rayon Silk Manufacturing (Weav-Ing) Co. Ltd. And A

Supreme Court of India

28 April 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Mohammad Hidayatullah, K.C. Das Gupta, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 72; AIR 1964 SC 1903; 1964 (9) FLR 124; [1964] 53 ITR 466; [1964] 7 S.C.R. 892

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Land & Property; Parliament & Legislature

Keywords: Devolution, Extravagant, Bombay Prohibition Act 1949

Summary: Parliament & Legislature - Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Constitution - Land & Property - Municipalities & Local Governments - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 372, 278, 294 and 295 - Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 60 A - Finance Act, 1950, s. 13 - (A) What are the factors which must be considered when an order passed by an absolute monarch be considered as an legislative act? - Held, relevant factors are : nature of order, scope and effect of its provisions, its general setting and context, method adopted by Ruler in promulgating legislative as distinguished from executive orders, these and other allied matters would have to be examined before character of order is judicially determined - (B) Whether Gwalior Darbar order (GDO) of January 18, 1947 is a law u/art. 372? - Further held, as a law jurisprudential distinction between legislative, judicial and executive acts must always be kept in mind and only those orders of Ruler which are jurisprudentially legislative acts will continue as laws u/art. 372 of Constitution - GDO was not a law by which Ruler of Gwalior granted exemption from Income Tax to company to be established - (2) Order of 18-01-1947 cannot be read independently of agreement of 7-04-1947 and must be read in context of entire set of circumstances - (3) form and content of order are against it being a law - Finally fact that it was never published and remained only on file concerned has also a bearing on question and shows that it was not a law but a mere signification of Ruler's acceptance of request made by Birla Brothers Limited - Agreement having force as a contract is wholly irreconcilable with a law operating side by side simultaneously and de hors contract - (C) Whether Govt. of India is under an obligation to fulfill law made by Ruler of Gwalior? - Further held, that agreement D/- 7-4-1947 by former Ruler of Gwalior State involving Contractual obligation devolving on Part B State of MP due to constitutional changes lies ultimately on Govt. of India by virtue of art. 295(1)(b) - U/art. 295(1)(b) there is no constitutional obligation on Govt. of India to fulfill it and it can be affected by law validly passed by Parliament - There is therefore no question of any constitutional obligation being cast by provisions contained in art. 295(1)(b) on Govt. of India to fulfill contracts irrespective of whether they were binding on original State which entered into them and whether they can affected by law validly passed after Constitution came into force - (D) Whether art. 295 prohibits parliament to enact a law as to Income Tax in territories which became Part B States and which are formerly Indian states? - Further held, there is nothing in art. 295 which expressly prohibits Parliament from enacting a law as to Income Tax in territories which became Part B States and which are formerly Indian states and such a prohibition cannot be read into art. 295 by virtue of some contract that might have been made by then Ruler of an Indian State with any person - Appeal allowed.
