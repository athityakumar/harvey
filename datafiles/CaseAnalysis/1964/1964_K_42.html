Chitturi Subbanna v Kudapa Subbanna and Others

Supreme Court of India

18 December 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

Raghubar Dayal, S.M. Sikri, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 231; AIR 1965 SC 1325; 1966 (1) AnWR 67; 1966 (1) MLJ(SC) 67; [1965] 2 S.C.R. 661

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 20, r.12 - (A) Whether High Court was in error in not allowing appellant to have raised objection based on provisions of O. 20, r.12, CPC ? - Held, question sought to be raised was a pure question of law and was not dependent on determination of any question of fact - First Appellate Court ought to have allowed it - Both parties, decree-holder and judgment-debtor, were under impression that mesne profits could be awarded till date of delivery of possession as directed by decree of HC - Fact that appellant raised no such objection before Commissioner or Trial Court, does not mean that he had given his consent for determination of mesne profits for period subsequent to expiry of 3 years from date of HC decree and that order of trial Court for payment of mesne profits up to date of delivery of possession is an order based on consent of parties - In circumstances of case, it is not that omission of appellant to raise point before Trial Court amounts to his waiving his right to raise objection on basis of O. 20, r.12, CPC - (B) Whether mesne profits could be awarded to decree-holder for a period subsequent to expiry of three years from date of HC's decree? - Further held, there is no provision of law other than provision of r. 12, O. 20, CPC which empowers Court to decree mesne profits subsequent to institution of a suit for recovery of possession of immovable property and mesne profits - Law with respect to decree for mesne profits had been changing from time to time and had been construed in accordance with law at relevant time - Period was, however, restricted to three years in order to discourage decree-holders from making delays in taking possession - It is open to Court to construe direction in preliminary decree about inquiry with respect to future mesne profits when such direction is not so fully expressed as to cover all alternatives mentioned in O. 20, r.12 (1) (c), CPC and to hold that decree be construed in accordance with those provisions - Judgment-debtor appellant is not precluded from contending that mesne profits could not be awarded for a period exceeding three years from date of decree - (C) Whether HC was in error in arbitrarily fixing a higher amount of mesne profits than what had been adjudged by trial Court which had itself arbitrarily increased mesne profits suggested by Commissioner? - Further held, HC has given no good reasons for enhancing amount - In dealing with various items it seems to have proceeded on assumptions or raised rates of profits to be allowed without referring to basis for enhancement - Appeal allowed.
