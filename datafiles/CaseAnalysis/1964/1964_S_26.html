Shankarlal Kachrabhai and Others v State of Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

21 September 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, S.M. Sikri, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 185; AIR 1965 SC 1260; 1965 CRLJ 266; [1965] 1 S.C.R. 287

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 301 and 302 - Common Intention - Murder - Issue: Whether conviction of accused 1 to 4 u/s. 302, r/w ss. 34 and 301 correct? - Criminal act mentioned in s. 34 result of concerted action of more than one person; if said result was reached in furtherance of common intention, each person is liable for result as if he had done it himself - Killing of another under a mistaken impression of identity is in furtherance of common intention to kill intended victim - Held, accused shot at a particular person with intention of killing him though under a misapprehension of his identity, all ingredients of ss. 299 and 300 are complied with, aid of s. 301 not called for and s. 301 has no application to present case - All accused are liable u/s. 302 r/w s. 34 - Appeal dismissed.
