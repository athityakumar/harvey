Mahendra Manilal Nanavati v Sushila Mahendra Nanavati

Supreme Court of India

18 March 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

Raghubar Dayal, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 129; AIR 1965 SC 364; 1964 (66) BomLR 681; 1965 MahLJ 365; 1965 MPLJ 509; [1964] 7 S.C.R. 267

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Family & Personal

Keywords: Matrimonial Offence, Chastity, Betrothal

Summary: Family & Personal - Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Hindu Marriage Act, 1955 - Indian Divorce Act, 1869 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872 - Pregnancy - Illegitimate - Annulment - Evidence - Respondent/S married to appellant/M - M went abroad and daughter was born to S - M returned but not live together with S, and suspected that S was pregnant by someone at time o marriage - M contended that child was born in five months and seventeen days after their marriage and instituted suit for declaration of nullity of marriage was dismissed by Trial Court - M filed petition for annulment of his marriage was allowed by Trial Court - HC dismissed petition for annulment - Hence instant appeal - Whether annulment petition dismissed by HC was justifiable - Held, delivery was sudden and S was in poor state of health , hence illegitimate stands disproved - Medical evidence adduced by M ,could be no sense regarded as of conclusive nature - Illegitimacy of child and un-chastity of its mother on assumption that birth appeared to be in normal period of gestation - Additional evidence was not proved - Decision of HC could not be regarded as judgment of affirmance as Trial Court had granted decree for annulment of marriage to appellant - Unless it was shown that concurrent finding was vitiated by error of law it would not be in consonance to re-examine - Hence, findings were based upon appreciation of evidence - Appeal dismissed.
