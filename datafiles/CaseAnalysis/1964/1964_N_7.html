Hiralal Prabhubhai and Others v Nagindas Atmaram Khatri

Supreme Court of India

14 February 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 422; AIR 1966 SC 367; 1964 MahLJ 532; [1964] 6 S.C.R. 773

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Bombay Tenancy Act, 1939 - Bombay Tenancy and Agricultural Lands Act, 1948, ss. 88(1)(c) and 89(2) - On 07-05-1946, respondent herein, who was owner of said land, gave a lease of same in favour of appellants for a period of six years - On 28-07-1948, 1939 Act was made applicable to said land - Suit filed by respondent for eviction of lessee-appellant - High Court held that 1939 Act was repealed by 1948 Act and that latter Act did not apply to suit land, as it was within two miles of limits of Surat Borough Municipality - Whether right of appellants under 1939 Act were preserved u/s. 89(2) of 1948 Act? - Held, 1939 Act was repealed by 1948 Act - U/s. 88(1)(c) of 1948 Act, provisions of that Act were not applicable to any area within municipal limits of said borough of Surat and within a distance of two miles of limits of said borough; but right, title and interest of a lessee in such area was preserved u/s. 89(2)(b)(i) of said Act - S. 88(1) of 1948 Act, among other things, was amended by 1952 Act, under which 1948 Act was made applicable to land in question which is within two miles of limits of Surat Borough Municipality - With result, interest of appellants could be terminated only u/s. 14 of 1948 Act - HC, therefore, was wrong in holding that appellants could not claim benefit of provisions of 1948 Act - Appeal allowed.
