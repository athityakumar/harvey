 Kasturilal Ralia Ram Jain v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

29 September 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Mohammad Hidayatullah, Raghubar Dayal, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 10; AIR 1965 SC 1039; 1965 CRLJ 144; 1966 (2) LLJ 583; 1965 (2) MLJ(SC) 15; 1965 (2) SCJ 318; [1965] 1 S.C.R. 375

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Constitution - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Indian Partnership Act, 1932 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, Ss. 409 - Seizure - Recovery - Alternative Claim - Guilty of Gross Negligence - Appellant/partner of a firm dealt in bullion and other goods under the Act, 1932 and while appellant was trying to sell gold, silver and other goods, he was taken into custody by police officials - Appellant was searched, detained and belongings were seized from him - Appellant made repeated demands for return of gold seized from him, by respondents, he could not recover same - So, he filed suit against respondent and claimed a decree for said recovery, which respondents hold that they could not be made liable for the loss resulting from negligence - Trial Court found in favor of appellant and respondent challenged same by an appeal, which was upheld by HC - Hence instant appeal - Whether respondent/State, was liable to compensate appellant, for the loss caused to it by negligence - Held, Lower Court was right in concluding, that seizure and non returnable of gold was based on negligence of respondent was incorrect, as, there could be no escape in dealing with appellant's property - HC had erroneously assumed that there was no obligation on respondents to deposit appellant's property in Treasury - Respondents, not only had kept property of appellant, in safe custody or in treasury, but the manner which it was dealt showed gross negligence on part of respondent - Appeal allowed.
