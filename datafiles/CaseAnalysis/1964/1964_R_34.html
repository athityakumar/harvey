Commissioner of Income Tax, Delhi and Rajasthan v Rao Thakur Narayan Singh

Supreme Court of India

30 October 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, S.M. Sikri, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 284; AIR 1965 SC 1421; [1965] 56 ITR 234; [1965] 1 S.C.R. 990

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1922, ss.31, 33, 34, 66, 66 A - Whether ITO can initiate proceedings all over again under s.34 of Act in derogation of finding given by Appellate Tribunal that ITO did not discover that income had escaped assessment?- Held, no ITO cannot - Under s.31 right of appeal against some orders of ITO lies to Appellate Asst Commr - U/s 33 right to appeal lies to ITAT - Sub-s (6) of s.33 says that save as provided in s.66 orders passed by Appellate Tribunal on appeal shall be final - S. 66 provides for reference to High COurt on a question of law and s.66 A provides for appeals in certain cases to SC - If a mistake is committed by Appellate Tribunal in setting aside an order of reassessment and Income Tax Department does not resort to remedy allowed by Act and allows mistake to remain uncorrected - Then decision of Appellate Tribunal becomes final - Appeal dismissed.
