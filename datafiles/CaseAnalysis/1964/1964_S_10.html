 Sajjan Singh v State of Rajasthan

Supreme Court of India

30 October 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Mohammad Hidayatullah, Raghubar Dayal, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 213; AIR 1965 SC 845; 1965 (1) MLJ 57; 1965 (1) MLJ(SC) 57; [1965] 1 S.C.R. 933

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property

Keywords: Indian Independence Act, 1947, Constitution (Seventeenth Amendment) Act, 1964, Bombay Prohibition Act 1949, Constitution (Fifth Amendment) Act,1955

Summary: Land & Property - Constitution - Constitution (First Amendment) Act, 1951 - Constitution (Fifth Amendment) Act, 1955 - Constitution (Seventeenth Amendment) Act, 1964 - Govt. of India Act, 1935 - Bombay Prohibition Act 1949 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Indian Independence Act, 1947 - Constitution of India, arts. 31 A, 31 B, 226 and 368 - Whether Constitution (17 th Amendment) Act, 1964 is constitutionally invalid and validity of Acts by which they are affected cannot be saved? - Held, if pith and substance test is applied to amendment made by impugned Act, it would be clear that Parliament is seeking to amend fundamental rights solely with object of removing any possible obstacle in fulfilment of socioeconomic policy in which party in power believes - If that be so, effect of amendment on area over which High Court has powers prescribed by art. 226 operate, is incidental and in present case can be described as of an insignificant order - Impugned Act does not purport to change provisions of art. 226 and it cannot be said even to have that effect directly or in any appreciable measure - It is an Act object of which is to amend relevant Articles in Part III which confer fundamental rights on citizens and as such it falls under substantive part of art. 368 and does not attract provisions of cl. (b) of proviso - What impugned Act purports to do is not to make any land legislation but to protect and validate legislative measures in respect of agrarian reforms passed by different State Legislatures in country by granting them immunity from attack based on plea that they contravene fundamental rights - What impugned Act purports to do is to amend art.31 A, and art. 31 A itself is included in Part III - If Parliament thought that instead of adopting cumbersome process of amending each relevant Article in Part III, it would be more appropriate to add arts. 31 A and 31 B, and on that basis, it passed material provisions of Constitution (First Amendment) Act, it would not be reasonable to suggest that this method brings amendment within proviso - When art. 368 confers on Parliament right to amend Constitution power in question can be exercised over all provisions of Constitution - How power should be exercised, has to be determined by reference to question as to whether proposed amendment falls under substantive part of art. 368, or attracts provisions of proviso - Impugned Act is constitutionality valid - Petitions dismissed.
