Commissioner of Income Tax, Madras v A. Krishnaswami Mudaliar and Others

Supreme Court of India

16 April 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, K. Subba Rao, S.M. Sikri

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 147; AIR 1964 SC 1843; [1964] 53 ITR 122; 1964 (2) MLJ 99; 1964 (2) MLJ(SC) 99; 1964 (2) SCJ 491; [1964] 7 S.C.R. 776

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Land & Property

Keywords: Debt Laws

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act , 1922, ss. 4, 10 and 13 - (A)Whether the expression "in the opinion of the ITO" in the proviso to s. 13 of Income Tax Act confers a mere discretionary power? - Held, it does not - It imposes a statutory duty on the ITO to examine in every case the method of accounting employed by the assessee and to see whether or not it has been regularly employed and to determine whether the income profits and gains of the assessee could properly be deduced therefrom - (B) Whether ITO is bound to adopt the method of accounting to calculate the profit? - Held, he is not - There is a system of accounting regularly employed and by appropriate adjustments from the accounts maintained taxable profit may properly be deduced, the ITO is bound to compute the profits in accordance with the method of accounting - But where in the opinion of Income Tax Officer the profits cannot properly be deduced from the system of accounting adopted by the assessee it is open to him to adopt a more suitable basis for computation of the true profits - (C) Whether computing profits by including value of assets by ITO amounts to forcing another method of accounting on assessee? - Held, it does not - Where accounts are maintained in cash system and out goings for acquiring that asset posted as debits but its value at end of year were ignored and also accounts do not show profits properly - ITO computing profits by including value of assets in stock - Procedure held proper - Does not amount to forcing another method of accounting on assessee - (D) Whether deducing out of the receipts the cost of the film in the absence of an entry crediting the value of the asset at the end of the year will give a false picture of the partnership? - Held,yes it will - The assessee firm paid an amount of Rs. 1,00,000/- for acquiring a cinema film, a wasting asset, which was to be exploited for four years for the benefit of the partnership - The price paid for acquiring the assets was debited as an outgoing - At the end of the year there was a total collection of Rs. 1,46,849/- by the exploitation of the asset - The expenses for carrying on the business amounted to Rs. 18,206 - The result according to the firm which maintained its accounts on cash basis, was a net profit of Rs. 28,647 - This was arrived at by posting the outgoing for acquiring its stock in trade as a proper debit, and ignoring the value of that asset at the end of the year altogether - Appeal allowed.
