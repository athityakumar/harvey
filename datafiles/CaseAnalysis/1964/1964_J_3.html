 Jagdev Singh Sidhanti v Pratap Singh Daulta

Supreme Court of India

12 February 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, K.C. Das Gupta, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 226; AIR 1965 SC 183; 1964 (2) SCJ 633; [1964] 6 S.C.R. 750

Case Digest

Subject: Election; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Electorate, Religious Symbol

Summary: Election - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 100 and 123 - Whether appeals were made to electorate by appellants, his agents or other persons with his consent to vote in his favour on account of his language and to refrain from voting in favour of respondent on ground of his language? - It is open to a candidate in course of his election campaign to criticise policies of Govt. including its language policy and to make promises to electorate that if elected he will secure a reversal of that policy or will take measures in Legislature to undo danger, real, apprehended or even fancied, to language of people - Political speeches espousing cause of a particular language and making promises or asking people to protest against Govt. of day in respect of its language policy is not a corrupt practice within description of corrupt practice u/s. 123(3) of Act - Appeal allowed.
