 Sardar Govindrao and Others v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

06 October 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Raghubar Dayal, J.R. Madholkar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 329; AIR 1965 SC 1222; 1964 JabLJ 613; 1965 MahLJ 502; 1965 MPLJ 566; 1966 (1) SCJ 480; [1965] 1 S.C.R. 678

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Central Provinces and Berar Revocation of Land Revenue Exemptions Act, 1948, s. 5 - (A) Whether provisions of s. 5(3) make it obligatory upon State Govt. to make a suitable grant of money or pension in case it is proved that applicant has lost exemption under Act and is a descendant from a former ruling chief ? - Word 'may' is often read as 'shall' or 'must' when there is something in nature of thing to be done which makes it duty of person on whom power is conferred to exercise power - Held, u/s. 5(3), Govt. must, if it is satisfied that an institution or service must be continued or that there is a descendant of a former ruling chief, grant money or pension to institution or service or to descendant of former ruling chief, as case may be, though it need not make a grant if person claiming is not a descendant of a former ruling chief or there is other reasonable ground not to grant money or pension, but except in those cases where there are good grounds for not granting pension, Govt. is bound to make a grant to those who fulfill required condition and word 'may' in third sub-section though apparently discretionary has to be read as 'must' - (B) Whether Govt. justified in making order D/- 26-04-1955? - Act bars a suit and there is all more reason that Govt. must deal with such case in a quasi-judicial manner giving an opportunity to claimants to state their case in light of Deputy Commr.'s report - Further held, appellants do not seem to have been heard at all and as order of Govt. does not fulfil elementary requirements of a quasi-judicial process, is set aside - Appeal allowed.
