Mukund Deo (Dead) Represented By His Legal Representatives Kasibai and Others v Mahadu and Others

Supreme Court of India

31 August 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 445; AIR 1965 SC 703; 1965 (2) MLJ(SC) 116

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Declaratory Suit

Summary: Family & Personal - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Hyderabad Civil Procedure Code, 1323, s. 602 - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, s. 100 - B died leaving his surviving his wife R and his daughter V, and his property devolved upon R as a limited owner - By a deed she sold lands to one M - Respondents to this appeal who were sons of V an action against transferee in court of Munsiff, for a decree for possession of lands sold by R - They alleged in their plaint that they were governed by Mayukha school of Hindu Law and sale of lands by R was not supported by legal necessity and that on death of R they became entitled to lands as nearest reversioners to estate of B - Before Supreme Court question of law arose that under Code a second appeal lay to High Court on questions of fact as well as of law - Appellant contended that Hyderabad CPC, 1323 was repealed, and CPC (Act 5 of 1908) was extended on 1-04-1951 to State of Hyderabad after it became part of Indian Union and power to hear and decide second appeal before HC was derived from s. 100 of CPC, 1908 and HC could not set aside findings of fact recorded by First Appellate Court - Whether such contention is justifiable in law? - Held, it is unable to agree with this contention - It is true that as a general rule, alterations in law of procedure are retrospective, but a right of appeal to a particular forum is a substantive right and is not lost by alteration in law, unless provision is made expressly in that behalf, or a necessary implication arises - Supreme Court further held that it is unable to see how absence of any statement made by plaintiffs' witnesses that V did not inherit property was a circumstance from which an inference could be drawn in favour of defendant - In any event HC has preferred view taken by Trial Court and has refused to accept view taken by First Appellate Court and in an appeal with special leave under art. 136 of Constitution of India, 1950 Supreme Court will not ordinarily discard findings of HC on what is essentially a question of fact - Appeal dismissed.
