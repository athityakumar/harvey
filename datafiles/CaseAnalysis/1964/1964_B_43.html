Burhanpur Tapti Mills, Limited v Burhanpur Tapti Mills Mazdoor Sangh

Supreme Court of India

05 November 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 32; AIR 1965 SC 839; 1965 (1) LLJ 453

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Madhya Pradesh Industrial Relations Act, 1960, ss. 31(2) and 52 - Gratuity - Determination - Whether industrial court, erred in introducing by its award scheme of gratuity? - Held, in awarding gratuity scheme industrial court was not going against principle of an industry-cum-region basis - A scheme may impose a burden on finances of concern but pressure is ex facie distributed over years for it is limited to number of retirements each year - The employer is not required to provide whole amount at once, it may create a fund, and pay from interest which accrues on a capitalized sum - Burden of gratuity is not likely to be too much - Nothing in scheme which merits alteration -Appeal dismissed.
