Rup Chand Gupta v Raghuvanshi Private Limited and Another

Supreme Court of India

15 April 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

K.C. Das Gupta, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 399; AIR 1964 SC 1889; [1964] 7 S.C.R. 760

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Possession Of Land

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 9, O. 9, r. 13 - (A) Whether a suit without impleading sub-lessee amounts to collusive ex partee decree?- Held, no, it does not - Mere fact that sub-lessee was not impleaded or that lessee did not actually contest suit did not render decree passed in suit as collusive especially when it was not suggested by sub-lessee that lessor had even a plausible defence to claim for ejectment - Collusion in judicial proceedings is a secret arrangement between two persons that one should institute a suit against other in order to obtain decision of a judicial tribunal for some sinister purpose - Law allows this and so omission cannot be said to be an improper act - Mere fact that defendant agrees with plaintiff that if a suit is brought he would not defend it, would not necessarily prove collusion - It is only if this agreement is done improperly in sense that a dishonest purpose is intended to be achieved that they can be said to have colluded - Appeal dismissed.
