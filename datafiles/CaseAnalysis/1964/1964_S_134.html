Roshan Lal and Others v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

03 December 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sarkar, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, R.S. Bachawat

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 124; AIR 1965 SC 1413; 1965 CRLJ 426; [1965] 2 S.C.R. 316

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Tort

Keywords: Wrongful Confinement

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 201, 304, 330 and 348 - Appellant convicted u/s. 330 and 348 also u/s. 201 - Question as to legality of term of imprisonment imposed u/s. 201 - Conviction and punishment u/s. 201 depend not so much on what offence was in fact, committed but on what accused knew or had reason to believe to have been committed, and whose a major offence was committed but accused knew or believed that only a minor offence had been committed - Held, High Court did not even find that appellants believed that an offence u/s. 304 had been committed - Maximum sentence for offence u/s. 330 is imprisonment for seven years, and under fourth paragraph to s. 201, appellants are liable to be sentenced to a maximum of one-fourth of seven years of imprisonment - Sentence passed on appellants for offence u/s. 201 should be reduced to a sentence of one year and nine months - Appeal partly allowed.
