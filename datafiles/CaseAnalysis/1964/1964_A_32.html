Associated Banking Corporation of India Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax, Bombay-1

Supreme Court of India

22 October 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, K. Subba Rao, S.M. Sikri

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 205; AIR 1965 SC 1188; [1965] 35 Comp Cas 308; [1965] 56 ITR 1; [1965] 1 S.C.R. 788

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Debt Laws

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income-tax Act, 1961 - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922 - Liquidator - Business loss - Gross profits - Irrecoverable debts - Suspension - J was appointed Secretary to Associated Banking Corporation of India Ltd and under a power of attorney he was entrusted with powers, amongst others, to supervise, manage and conduct business, to lend with or without security to any person - Order of HC was ordered to be compulsorily wound up and an Official Liquidator was appointed to liquidate the business of the Bank - Liquidator submitted a return for the assessment year 1948-49 disclosing for previous year ending June 30, 1947 business loss computed at Rs.9,71,664, after debiting against gross profits in profit & loss account an amount exceeding Rs.12,00,000 as debts which became irrecoverable - Liquidator informed the Income-tax Officer that in the course of investigations it was found that the bad debts of the Bank including the amounts embezzled by Secretary amounted to Rs.48,50,952 - Whether assessee was entitled to claim bad debts amounting to Rs. 38,35,654 or any lesser sum - Held, no evidence that in year of account J Secretary could not have met the obligations either wholly or partially if he was called upon to refund amounts embezzled - Embezzled amounts did not come to the knowledge of the liquidator who were appointed auditors to investigate the affairs of the Bank by Registrar of the Joint Stock Companies - Tribunal found in its supplementary report that the withdrawals and misapplication of funds by the Secretary came to the knowledge of liquidator after accounting year under reference, because no one suspected that entries posted in the books of account were false entries to cover up his dealings by the Secretary - Agree with HC that the amount of Rs.10,15,000 was not a permissible deduction under S. 10(1) - First question recorded by HC discharged, and it will be recorded that Bank was entitled to claim u/s.10 (2 )(xi) Rs.1 5,00,000 as bad debts in the year of account ending June 30, 1947 - On second question, answer in negative - Appeal partly allowed.
