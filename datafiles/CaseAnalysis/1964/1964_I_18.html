Murugan Mills, Limited v Industrial Tribunal, Madras, and Another

Supreme Court of India

11 November 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Mohammad Hidayatullah

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 217; AIR 1965 SC 1496; 1965 (10) FLR 162; 1965 (1) LLJ 422; 1966 (2) MLJ(SC) 9; [1965] 2 S.C.R. 148

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 33(2)(a), 33(2)(b), 33 A and 38(2) - (A) Whether dismissal in manner done is sustainable? - Held, one should not be dismissed without assigning proper reason - Form used in present case for terminating respondent's services u/cl. 17(a) was not conclusive and tribunal was justified in enquiring into reasons which led to such termination; even Standing Orders provide that an employee can ask for reasons in such a case - Since appellant's case that respondent was guilty of dereliction of duty and goes slow in his work had not been proved, case was clearly covered by cl. (b) of s. 33(2) of Act and as services of respondent were dispensed with during pendency of a dispute by meting out punishment of discharge to him for misconduct without complying with proviso, termination of service was rightly set aside -(B)Whether in given case Supreme Court can interfere in order of tribunal? - Further held, even though tribunal was in error in holding that proviso to s. 33(2) applied to cl. (a) thereof also, there was no reason for Supreme Court to interfere with order passed by tribunal - Appeal dismissed.
