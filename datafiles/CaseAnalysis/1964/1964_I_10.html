Commissioner of Income Tax, Madras v Indian Bank Limited

Supreme Court of India

26 October 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri, J.C. Shah, K. Subba Rao

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 321; AIR 1965 SC 1473; [1965] 35 Comp Cas 321; [1965] 56 ITR 77; [1965] 1 S.C.R. 833; 1974 TAXLR 124

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, s.10(2) (Iii) - Fixed deposits - Income - Tax free securities - Taxable territories - Respondent carried on business of banking it received deposits from constituents and paid interest to them and invested a large sum in securities both of Central and State Govt. - Interest on Govt. securities was exempted from income tax and super tax under provisions of a notification issued u/s.60 of Act - For a/y 1951-52 respondent Bank claimed a deduction of Rs.25,91,565 as interest paid to various depositors, u/s.10(2) (iii) of 1961 Act - Income Tax Appellate Tribunal disallowed interest amounting to Rs.2,80,194 - Whether respondent was entitled to claim deduction of entire interest paid by it on fixed deposits u/s.10(2) of 1961 Act - Held, investments in question were part of business of Respondents' trade, and expense connected with them was wholly and exclusively laid out for purposes of trade - Expenditure in course of trade which was unremunerative was none less a proper deduction if wholly and exclusively made for purposes of trade - If a part of income of a business was tax free, expenditure incurred for purpose of earning income was outside purview of s.10 of Act - Appeal dismissed.
