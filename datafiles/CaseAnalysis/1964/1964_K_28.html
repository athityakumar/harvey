Karan Singh v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

04 November 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sarkar, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, R.S. Bachawat

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 376; AIR 1965 SC 1037; 1965 CRLJ 142; [1965] 2 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 148, 149, 302 and 307 - Whether conviction of accomplice in a separate trial was justified, while murderer was acquitted from case? - Held, in spite of acquittal of a person in one case it is open to court in another case to proceed - If evidence warrants it - That acquitted person was guilty of offence of which he had been tried in other case and to find in later case that person tried in it was guilty of an offence u/s. 34 by virtue of having committed offence along with acquitted person - Nothing in principle to prevent this being done - Court had power to proceed on matter on basis of his common intention with murderer - Each case had to be decided on evidence irrespective of any view of same act that was taken on different evidence led in another case - Appeal dismissed.
