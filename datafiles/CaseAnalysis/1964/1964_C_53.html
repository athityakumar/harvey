Commissioner of Income Tax, Madras v Kumbakonam Mutual Benefit Fund Limited

Supreme Court of India

07 May 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri, K. Subba Rao, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 326; AIR 1965 SC 96; [1964] 53 ITR 241; 1964 (2) MLJ(SC) 89; 1964 (2) SCJ 473; [1964] 8 S.C.R. 204

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Companies Act. 1882 - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, ss.10,66(A)(2) - Banking business - Recurring deposit scheme - Contribution of funds - Loans on security - Assessment years - Assessee was a company incorporated under 1882 Act limited by shares - Assessee carried on banking business restricted to its shareholders i.e.shareholders are entitled to participate in various recurring deposit schemes of assessee or to obtain loans on security - Income Tax Officer(IO) assessed entire profits for eight years - IO observed that “Style's” case did not apply to facts of case that a shareholder might not hold any deposit with fund and might not utilize borrowing facilities afforded by fund but may be content to receive such dividend as declared - Appellate Assistant Commissioner uphold order of IO - Appellate Tribunal stated a consolidated case in respect of assessment years 1946-47 to 1953-54 and referred questions to HC that whether there were materials for Tribunal to hold that assessee was a banking concern assessable u/s.10 for all assessment years and not exempt - HC answered question in negative and awarded costs of Rs.250 and further ordered refund to assessee of institution fee of Rs.100 - HC certified cases as fit for appeal u/s.66(A)(2) of 1922 Act - Hence instant appeal - Whether “Styles” case applied to facts of present case - Held, test applied by HC was not sound - It was not consistent with true decision in 'Styles' case - Difficult to hold that “Style's” case applied to facts of case - Shareholder in assessee company entitled to participate in profits without contributing to funds of company by taking loans and not to fulfil any other condition - Position of assessee was no different from an ordinary bank except that it lends money to and received deposits from its shareholders - Position of shareholder in assessee company was in no way different from a shareholder in a banking company limited by shares - It did not by itself make its income any the less income from business within s.10 of 1922 Act - Answered the question referred to HC should be in affirmative - Appeal allowed.
