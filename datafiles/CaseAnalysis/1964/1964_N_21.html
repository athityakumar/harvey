 Tansukh Rai Jain v Nilratan Prasad Shaw and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 November 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

Raghubar Dayal, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 375; AIR 1966 SC 1780; 1965 (44) ILR(Pat) 482; 1965 (2) SCJ 732; [1965] 2 S.C.R. 6

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1939, s. 64A - Motor Vehicles (Bihar Amendment) Act, 1949, s. 64A - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Whether s. 64A of Motor Vehicles Act, 1939 as introduced by Motor Vehicles (Bihar Amendment) Act, 1949 void or has been repealed by Central s. 64A? - Language of Bihar s. 64A is very general and empowers State Govt. to revise any order made in course of any proceedings taken under Chap. IV and pass such orders as it thinks fit - It is not in conflict with provisions of Central Act - Power of revision vested in State Govt. under its provisions are to come into play only when Central Act does not provide any remedy against orders proposed to be revised - It will be available only against non-appealable orders after aggrieved person has taken action under Central s. 64A - There is nothing in Central s. 64A or in any other provision of Act which expressly states that Bihar s. 64A is repealed - Mere fact that Central s. 64A deals with revisions against non-appealable orders of Regional Transport Authority is not sufficient to conclude that Parliament intended to repeal Bihar s. 64A - Language of Bihar s. 64A is very wide and covers all orders made by any authority or officer in course of any proceedings taken u/Chap. IV of Act - Only limitation on exercise of revisional power conferred on State by Bihar s. 64A is that State cannot suo motu exercise that power - Central s. 64A can preclude State Govt. from entertaining revisions against non-appealable orders of Regional Transport Authority, but cannot preclude operation of Bihar s. 64A in regard to other orders - Bihar s. 64A is neither void nor has been repealed by Central s. 64A and that its scope has been limited only to this extent that revisions against such orders of Regional Transport Authority which are not appealable have to be preferred to State Transport Authority - In present case State Govt. of Bihar revised order made by appellate authority - It was competent to do so - Appeal allowed.
