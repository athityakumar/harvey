 C. Rajagopalachari v Corporation of Madras

Supreme Court of India

03 March 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, S.M. Sikri, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, J.C. Shah, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 119; AIR 1964 SC 1172; [1964] 53 ITR 454; 1964 (2) MLJ(SC) 59; 1964 (2) SCJ 234; [1964] 6 S.C.R. 962

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax

Keywords: Profession Tax, Bombay Prohibition Act 1949

Summary: Indirect Tax - Government of India Act, 1935, s. 143(2) - Madras City Municipal Act, 1919, s.111 - Madras City Municipal Amendment Act, 1936, ss. 3, 98 A - Demand of Profession tax from pensioner - Disputed - Maintainability - Respondent/corporation demanded profession tax for year 1958-59 from appellant/pensioner holding that pensioners were also liable to pay profession tax - Appellant filed writ petition and was dismissed by HC - Hence, instant appeal - Whether appellant was liable to profession tax as held by HC - Held, levy of profession tax on pensions could not be sustained as was not sanctioned by s. 143(2) of the 1935 Act - Statutory charge to profession tax imposed on pensioners by the 1919 Act was lifted by the 1936 Act and later once again came into operation w.e.f. 1-4-1937 - No charge of tax was levied between 1-4-1936 to 31-3-1937 - By imposing new conditions for existing law, it became new one and not one existed prior to 1-4-1937 - Tax was not lawfully levied prior to 1-4-1937 and was levied after the 1935 Act came into force and on reality w.e.f.1-4-1942 and hence could not sustain as it was not in continuity of pre existing levy u/s. 143 (2) of the 1935 Act - Tax rates were enhanced w.e.f. 1-4-1950, 1-4-1958 and 1-4-1961 - Entire tax would become new tax and so unconstitutional and unenforceable - Respondents demand was not justified and writ of Prohibition was issued against respondent/ corporation from enforcing demand - Judgment of HC was set aside - Appeal allowed.
