 Lalji Haridas v State of Maharashtra and Another

Supreme Court of India

07 February 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, A.K. Sarkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, K.C. Das Gupta, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 257; AIR 1964 SC 1154; 1964 (66) BomLR 460; 1964 CRLJ 249; [1964] 52 ITR 423; 1965 MLJ 21; 1964 (1) SCJ 543; [1964] 6 S.C.R. 700

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 37(2) and 37(4) - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 193 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898, s. 195 - Lalji Haridas and respondent No. 2, Mulji Manilal Kamdar, are businessmen and they carry on their business in Jamnagar and Bombay respectively - They know each other - Respondent No. 2 said that he does not have a son in the name Nihal and never did business in his son's name - According to appellant, said statements were false to knowledge of respondent No. 2 and were made by him to mislead ITO and to avoid incidence of income tax on himself - As a result of said false statements, appellant was heavily taxed - Appellant filed a criminal complaint against respondent No. 2 u/s. 193 of IPC, 1860 - Respondent No. 2 raised a preliminary objection that Magistrate could not take cognizance of said complaint, because proceedings in which he was alleged to have made a false statement on oath were proceedings before a court within meaning of s. 195(1)(b) of CrPC, 1973 and since no complaint in writing had been made by court of ITO before which said proceedings were conducted - Provisions of s. 195(1)(b) created a bar against competence of appellant's complaint - Division Bench of said High Court held that ITO was a court within meaning of s. 195(1)(b) of CrPC, 1973 and so it upheld preliminary objection raised by respondent No. 2 - Whether proceedings before an ITO are proceedings in any court u/s. 195(1)(b) of CrPC, 1973? - Held, words used in s. 37(4) of Income Tax Act furnish no reason to alter legal position that is inescapable on a consideration of functions of ITO that he is not a court within meaning of s. 195 of CrPC, 1973 - Set aside order passed by HC and direct that Presidency Magistrate, Bombay, should dispose of case in accordance with law - Appeal allowed.
