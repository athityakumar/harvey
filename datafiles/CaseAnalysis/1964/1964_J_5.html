 Indo-China Steam Navigation Company Limited v Jasjit Singh, Additional Collector of Customs and Others

Supreme Court of India

03 February 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, K.C. Das Gupta, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, J.C. Shah, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 2; AIR 1964 SC 1140; [1964] 34 Comp Cas 435; 1964 CRLJ 234; 1984 ECR 467; 1983 (13) E.L.T. 1392; [1964] 6 S.C.R. 594

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Customs - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 19, 31(1) and 136(1) - Sea Customs Act, 1878), ss. 52 A, 167, 167(3), 167(12 A), 167(8),167(81), 167(14), 167(61)and 167(78) - Customs officer - Special Leave - Grant at ex parte hearing - (A) Whether preliminary objection that decision etc has not been pronounced by a 'Tribunal' or a 'Court' can be raised at final hearing? - (B) Is customs Officer acting u/s. 167 of Sea Customs Act is a 'court' or a 'tribunal'? - (C) Contravention of s. 52 A - Whether Mens rea is relevent? - (D) Nature of liability u/s. 167(12 A) - Whether customs Officer can refuse to confiscate vessel on grounds of extenuating circumstances and whether penalties prescribed u/cl. 12 A of s. 167 are independent or alternative - (E) Whether a foreign Company whose vessel has been confiscated is entitled to challenge validity of s. 52 A of Act as being ultra vires arts. 14, 19 and 31 (1) of Constitution - Can be raised - Not a court of tribunal - Mens rea need not be proved - Officer cannot refuse and penalties are independent, not alternative - Foreign company not entitled - Appeal dismissed.
