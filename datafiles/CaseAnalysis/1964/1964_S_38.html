 Shri Roshanlal Gautam v State of Uttar Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

26 October 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Raghubar Dayal, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 207; AIR 1965 SC 991; [1965] 1 S.C.R. 841

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles (Amendment) Act, 1956 - Uttar Pradesh Road Transport Services (Development) Act, 1955, s. 3 - Motor Vehicles Act, 1939, s. 68C - (A) Whether requirements of s. 3 of U P Act which were conditions precedent for action are not same as requirements of s. 68 C? - Held, it is no doubt true that while s. 68C makes a mention of an 'efficient, adequate, economical and properly coordinated road transport service in public interest', U P Act merely mentioned 'the interest of general public subserving common good or for maintaining and developing efficient road transport system' - Change of verbiage, however, does not make a change in requirements - It would be wrong to think that even under U P Act Govt. would not think of an 'adequate', 'economical' or 'properly coordinated' road transport service when it chose to provide road transport services for common good and for maintaining and developing efficient road transport system - (B) Whether scheme is not properly framed because it provides for operation of contract carriages on certain routes to exclusion of appellant who holds a permit for an area irrespective of any route or routes? - Further held, if u/s. 51(2)(1) a permit for a contract carriage could be limited to specified route or routes notwithstanding that petition for such a permit must be for an area there is no difficulty in accepting a scheme which cuts down area by subtracting a few routes - By taking over of routes area is as effectively cut down as when an area is included in permit but routes are indicated on which alone contract carriages can ply - Appeal dismissed.
