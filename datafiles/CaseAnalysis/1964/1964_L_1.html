Commissioner of Income Tax, Kerala and Coimbatore v L. W. Russel

Supreme Court of India

01 April 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, S.M. Sikri, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 290; AIR 1965 SC 49; [1964] 53 ITR 91; 1964 (2) SCJ 332; [1964] 7 S.C.R. 569

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Annuity

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s.7(1) - Interpretation of - Whether contributions paid by employer to assessee in respect of a contract for a deferred annuity on life of assessee is a 'perquisite' as contemplated by s. 7(1) of Indian Income-tax Act and whether deferred annuity aforesaid is an annuity hit by s. 7(1) and paragraph (v) of Explanation 1 thereto? - Only where a contribution by employer creates a vested interest to an employee, it is taxable - Held, in present case it is only a contingent interest depending upon assessee employed lived upto age of super annuation - Contribution paid by employer to supen annuation is not a perquisite - Appeal dismissed.
