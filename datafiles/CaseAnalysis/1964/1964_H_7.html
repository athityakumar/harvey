Harishchandra v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

24 September 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, A.K. Sarkar, R.S. Bachawat

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 187; AIR 1965 SC 932; 1965 CRLJ 24; [1965] 1 S.C.R. 323

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Unauthorized sale of scrap

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Iron & Steel (Scrap Control) Order, 1943, s. 8(4) - Madhya Bharat Iron, Steel and Scrap (Production, Procurement and Distribution) Control Order, 1949, s. 5(1) - Unauthorized sale of scrap - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant accused was President of Scrap Dealers Association and was prosecuted before Trial Court in 7 sets of criminal cases filed by respondent - Respondent alleged that appellant was committed offence u/s. 8(4) of the 1943 Order by selling or causing to be sold scrap iron to different customers on different dates at a rate higher than was authorized by notification dt. 30-9-1952 issued by Steel Controller under the 1943 Order - Trial Court acquitted appellant - Respondent preferred appeal before Appellate Court, which was convicted the appellant and set aside the order of Trial Court - Aggrieved, appellant preferred appeal before HC, which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether impugned sale as per price fixed under column III rates u/s. 5(1) of the 1949 Order, which was directed by Advisory Committee of State Govt., was in violation of the 1943 Order - Held, 2 orders, though achieving substantially the same object, were not identical in their provisions - If that was so, it was obvious that on the extension to the 1943 Order, the 1949 Order should stand repealed and be replaced by Indian law - When Indian Scrap Order was extended to Madhya Bharat, the result was that it effectively replaced the 1949 Order on same topic - When the 1943 Order was extended it carried with it notifications issued by Controller from time to time and that after extension of Scrap Order to the 1949 Order, all sales of scrap should have to be effected only in conformity with prices fixed by 'notifications issued under the Scrap Order was not contested - Nor was it disputed that on terms of notifications issued fixing prices at which several classes of dealers might effect sales tinder 1943 Order, Association of which appellant was President should have fallen under column 11 and should have bound to sell scrap only at prices fixed in that column - Appeal dismissed.
