 Messrs Modi Spinning and Weaving Mills Company, Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax, Punjab and Another

Supreme Court of India

05 October 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Raghubar Dayal, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 151; AIR 1965 SC 957; [1965] 1 S.C.R. 592; 1965 (16) STC 310

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Sales Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Constitution of India, 1950 - Punjab General Sales Tax Act, 1948, ss. 2(1), 4, 5, and 5(2)(a)(ii) - Punjab General Sales Tax Rules, 1949, r. 26 - Whether appellant was entitled to deduction on account of unginned-cotton purchased by it on a certificate of registration granted to it? - Held, goods which Co. manufactured in State of Punjab were bales of ginned cotton and they were admittedly not for sale because they were sent to its spinning and weaving mills in Uttar Pradesh - All conditions of s. 5(2)(a)(ii) were not complied with - Registration certificate was only evidence that Company was a registered dealer for purposes of certain commodities to be used in manufacture, one of them being cotton - Omission to prescribe new Form or to issue it did not render s. 5 and Rules ineffective - Appellant was not entitled to deduction - Appeal dismissed.
