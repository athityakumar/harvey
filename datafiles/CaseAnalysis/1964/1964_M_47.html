Raja Mohammad Amir Ahmad Khan v Municipal Board of Sitapur and Another

Supreme Court of India

03 December 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, K. Subba Rao, Raghubar Dayal

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 458; AIR 1965 SC 1923

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 111(g) - Oudh Estates Act, 1869, s. 32 A - Rehabilitation of Refugees Act, 1948, s. 9 - Whether appellant incurred forfeiture of his lease-hold interests by denying title of Govt. so as to justify latter in terminating lease? - S. 111(g) are equally applicable to tenancies to which Act does not apply on ground of same being in consonance with justice, equity and good conscience - It was also clear law that permanent tenancies are within rule and are liable to forfeiture if there is a disclaimer of tenancy or a denial of landlord's title - Held, rent was being continuously paid right up to March, 1947 and appellant never raised a dispute as regards his liability to pay rent - Do not consider that any inference adverse to appellant could be drawn from his not tendering rent for period up to date on which possession was taken - Further held, learned judges were in error in holding that appellant had incurred a forfeiture of his tenancy, assuming it was a permanent tenancy - Matter remanded to High Court for being dealt with in accordance with law - Appeal allowed.
