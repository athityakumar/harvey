Bharat Fire and General Insurance Company Limited New Delhi v Commissioner of Income Tax, New Delhi

Supreme Court of India

02 April 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri, K. Subba Rao, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 104; AIR 1964 SC 1800; [1964] 34 Comp Cas 683; 1964 (2) CompLJ 100; 1964 (2) ITJ 138; [1964] 53 ITR 108; 1965 (67) PLR 1; 1964 (2) SCJ 434; [1964] 7 S.C.R. 626

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 2(6 A)(a) and 12 - Companies Act, 1956, s. 78 - Company - Declaring dividend in 1953 out of capital reserve having share premium on issue of shares in 1945 - Assesse taxed on such dividend in Assesment Years: 1954-55 - (A) Plea raised for first time before Supreme Court - Whether premiums received on issue of share capital are gains within explaination to s. 2(6 A) of 1922 Act - (B) Effect of s. 8 of Companies Act, 1956 on taxability of such dividend - Held, question not allowed to be developed by Supreme Court - Held, s. 78 of 1956 Act did not affect taxability of such dividend - Appeal dismissed.
