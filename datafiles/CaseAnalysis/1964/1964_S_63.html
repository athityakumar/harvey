K. Kankarathnamma and Others v State of Andhra Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

23 January 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

J.R. Madholkar, K. Subba Rao

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 358; AIR 1965 SC 304; [1964] 6 S.C.R. 294

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 30, 18 and 19 - Land Acquisition Officer had fixed Rs. 3, 500 per acre for a certain land -The learned Subordinate Judge granted a uniform rate of Rs. 10,000 per acre for lands comprised in all survey numbers- There were some disputes with regard to entitlement to compensation for survey No. 339/1 to 3 and Land Acquisition Officer, therefore, made a reference to court for apportionment of compensation amount among various claimants - Whether High Court had jurisdiction to question quantum of compensation by re-opening award of Land Acquisition Officer? - S. 12(1) of Land Acquisition Act, 1894 provides that after an award is filed in Collector's office it shall, except as provided in Act, be final and conclusive evidence as between Collector and persons interested of true area and value of land and apportionment of compensation among persons interested as matter goes to court only upon a reference made by Collector - Held, the fact that reference u/s. 30 of Act of 1894 was made by Land Acquisition Officer would not invest court with jurisdiction to consider question of quantum of compensation which is not directly connected with reference regarding apportionment of compensation amongst various claimants, view of HC is not correct - Appeal dismissed.
