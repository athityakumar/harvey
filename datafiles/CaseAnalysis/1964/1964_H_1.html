K. Narasimhah v H. C. Singri Gowda

Supreme Court of India

01 April 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

K.C. Das Gupta, K. Subba Rao, Raghubar Dayal

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 88; AIR 1966 SC 330; 1965 (1) MLJ 105; 1965 (1) MLJ(SC) 105; [1964] 7 S.C.R. 618

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Mysore Town Municipalities Act, 1951

Summary: Municipalities & Local Government - Mysore Town Municipalities Act, 1951, ss. 27(2), 27(3), 24(1)(a), 23(9) and 36 - Appellant was elected as President of Holenarsipur Municipality - Council passed no confidence motion against him in a meeting - (A) Whether meeting was held in accordance with law as petitioner was not allowed to preside? - It was only after President left meeting that Vice President took chair and thereafter no confidence resolution was moved and passed - Held, there can therefore be no question of any contravention of requirement that President shall preside (B) Whether requirement of three clear days' notice for holding of a special general meeting as embodied in s. 27(3) of Mysore Town Municipalities Act 1951, a mandatory provision? - Very fact that while three clear days' notice is not to be given of all special general meetings and for some such meetings notice only of such shorter period as is reasonable has to be given justifies conclusion that 'three clear days' mentioned in section was given by legislature as only a measure of what it considered reasonable - Further held, requirement of three clear days' notice is not mandatory - (C) Whether fact that some of Councillors received less than three clear days' notice invalidates proceedings of meeting? - It is reasonable to think that service of notice mentioned in this provision refers to giving of notice to Councillors - Further held, fact that some of Councillors received less than three clear days' notice of the meeting did not by itself make proceedings of meeting or resolution passed there invalid - These would be invalid only if proceedings were prejudicially affected by any irregularity - Appeal dismissed.
