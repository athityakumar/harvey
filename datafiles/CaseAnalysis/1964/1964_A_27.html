 C. S. Rowjee and Others v Andhra Pradesh State Road Transport Corporation

Supreme Court of India

27 January 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, K. Subba Rao, Raghubar Dayal, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 100; AIR 1964 SC 962; [1964] 6 S.C.R. 330

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Carriers &Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1939, ss. 68 C and 68 D - Andhra Pradesh Motor Vehicles Rules, 1957 - (A) Whether scheme is invalid as draft scheme in s. 68-D really did not originate from Corporation, State Transport Undertaking, but that it was done under direction of Chief Minister of Andhra Pradesh who acted mala fide in directing Transport Undertaking to frame impugned schemes for areas for which they were purported to be framed? - Held, allegations that Chief Minister was motivated by bias and personal ill-will against appellants, stands unrebutted - On evidence placed in case we are satisfied that it was as a result of conference of 19-04-1962, and in order to give effect to wishes of Chief Minister expressed there, that schemes now impugned were formulated by Corporation - (B) Whether scheme framed by Corporation could still not be assailed as not in conformity with requirements of s. 68-C of Act so long as choice of 'area' in which and routes in it to be run by Corporation was made by them alone? - Further held, if choice of district was that of Chief Minister, fact that within area of district pointed out to them, Corporation selected some area within district and routes within that area, cannot on any reasonable construction of s. 68-C be a sufficient compliance with statute - Appeal allowed.
