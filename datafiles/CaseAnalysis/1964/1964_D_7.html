Dahyabhai Chhaganbhai Thakker v State of Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

19 March 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, K.C. Das Gupta, Raghubar Dayal

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 409; AIR 1964 SC 1563; 1964 CRLJ 472; [1964] 7 S.C.R. 361

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Evidence Act, 1872, ss.137, 154 and 162 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 84 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 134 - Evidence - (A) Whether Court can permit a party calling a witness to put questions in nature of cross-examination at stage of re examination u/s. 154 ?- Held, yes, Court can - But adverse party must be given further opportunity to cross examine witness in such case - S. 154 does not in terms, or by necessary implication, confine exercise of power by Court before examination in chief is concluded or to any particular stage of examination of witness - (B) Whether omission in earlier statement can be relied upon to contradict subsequent statement in court u/s. 145 ? - Further held, in circumstances it was necessarily implied in previous statements of witnesses before police that accused was not insane at time he committed murder - In this view previous statements of witnesses before police could be used to contradict their version in court - (C) When can a plea of legal insanity be set up u/s. 84 of IPC, 1860?- Held, court has to consider whether at time of commission of offence accused, by reason of unsoundness of mind, was incapable of knowing nature of act or that he was doing what was either wrong or contrary to law- Plea can only be established from circumstances which preceded, attended and followed crime - Number of blows does not necessarily prove that accused was doing act under some hallucination - (D) Whether u/art. 136 of Constitution Supreme Court can interfere with a finding of fact? - Held, yes, it can but only in exceptional cases - Appeal dismissed.
