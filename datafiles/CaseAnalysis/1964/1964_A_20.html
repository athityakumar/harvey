Amba Prasad v Abdul Noor Khan and Others

Supreme Court of India

17 April 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 397; AIR 1965 SC 54; 1965 (1) SCJ 559; [1964] 7 S.C.R. 800

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Board Of Revenue Act- 1913 - U.P. Zamindari Abolition and Land Reforms Act, 1950, s. 20(b)(i) - U P Land revenue Act, 1901, ss. 28 and 33 - U P Tenancy Act, 1939, ss. 180, 61 and 183 - Protection of lands under Abolition Act to occupants - (A) Whether answering respondents were recorded as 'qabiz' in 1356F? - There is nothing on record of this case on strength of which it can be said that entry in 1356F was not regularly made - Held, there is thus no doubt that answering respondents were recorded as 'qabiz' in 1356 F - There is also no doubt that if they were and Land revenue Act they were dispossessed after 30-06-1948 - (B) Whether a person who is recorded as 'qabiz' but not as a tenant or a sub-tenant can get advantage of s. 20 of Abolition Act and claim rights as an adhivasi? - Cls. (b)(i) and (b)(ii) of s. 20 do not require proof of actual possession in year 1356 F What they require merely is entry of a person's name as an occupant in Khasra or Khatauni of 1356 F words of section are clear- Further held, a person who is recorded as 'qabiz' can get advantage of s. 20 of Abolition Act and claim rights as an adhivasi - Appeal dismissed.
