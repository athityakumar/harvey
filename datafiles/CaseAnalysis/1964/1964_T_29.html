 Manipur Administration v Thokchom, Bira Singh

Supreme Court of India

11 March 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, K.C. Das Gupta, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 413; AIR 1965 SC 87; 1965 CRLJ 120; [1964] 7 S.C.R. 123

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Autre Fois Acquit

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 141 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898, ss. 144, 403, 403(2) and 5(1) - Indian Penal Code, 1860 ss. 188, 333, 323, 440 and 149 - Estoppel in a criminal trial - (A) Whether there had been a specific finding on an issue of fact ? - S. 403 of CrPC, 1898 not preclude applicability of rule of issue estoppel - Rule is one which is in accord with sound principle and supported by high authority and there being a decision of this court which has accepted it as a proper one to be adopted, there is no reason for discarding it - Held, s. 403 of CrPC, 1898 does not preclude applicability of this rule of issue-estoppel- Appeal dismissed.
