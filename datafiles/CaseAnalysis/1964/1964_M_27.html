 Ch. Subbarao v Member, Election Tribunal, Hyderabad

Supreme Court of India

13 January 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, K. Subba Rao, Raghubar Dayal, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 429; AIR 1964 SC 1027; [1964] 6 S.C.R. 213

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Representation of People, Act, 1951, ss. 81 and 117 - Election petititon filed by appellant was dismissed - No formal defect in petition - Election was sought to be set aside on grounds of allegations of corrupt practices - Election petition was referred to election tribunal - Respondent No. 3 filed statement of objections to petition of technical nature - Tribunal decided in favour of appellant - Respondent No. 3 moved to High Court for quashing order of tribunal - HC held, petition does not comply with requirements of s. 81(3) of 1951 Act - Election petition should be accompanied with number of copies with signature of petitioner and it should be attested as being true copy - Election petition of appellant was not attested - Held, there had been substantial compliance with requirement of s. 81(3) of 1951 Act - Absence of writing in copy indicating signature in original would not detract copy from being true copy - Appeal allowed.
