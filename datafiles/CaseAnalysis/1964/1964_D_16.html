 Malayalam Plantations Limited, Quilon v Deputy Commissioner of Agricultural Income Tax and Sales Tax, South Zone, Quilon

Supreme Court of India

20 March 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, S.M. Sikri, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 50; AIR 1965 SC 161; [1964] 7 S.C.R. 391; 1964 (15) STC 665

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Keywords: Bihar Sales Tax Act, 1959, General Sales Tax Act, Travancore-Cochin General Sales Tax Act, 1125

Summary: Sales Tax - Indirect Tax - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 286(1)(a) - General Sales Tax Act - Bihar Sales Tax Act, 1959 - Travancore-Cochin General Sales Tax Act, 1125 - Indian Sale of Goods Act, 1930 - Constitution (Sixth Amendment) Act, 1956 - Govt. of India Act, 1935 - Doctrine of territorial nexus - Outside sale - Appellant claimed that certain sales of its tea which were conducted by auction at Fort Cochin - A place which at relevant date was in Madras State - Were sales 'outside' Travancore-Cochin State and that consequently these sales were exempt from taxation by State of Travancore-Cochin u/art. 286(1)(a) of Constitution? - Sales Tax Officer rejected this contention and included sum involved in these sales in taxable turnover - Whether sale of tea effected by appellant by auction at Fort Cochin and which was included in its taxable turnover be said to be 'outside' State? - Held, sale was an 'outside' sale, because title passed at Fort Cochin in State of Madras - On this reasoning, this Court reversed decision in case of High Court and held that sale there in question being as 'outside' sale was not taxable by reason of prohibition contained in art. 286(1)(a) - Appeal allowed.
