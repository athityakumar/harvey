 State of Uttar Pradesh and Another v Audh Narain Singh and Another

Supreme Court of India

09 March 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, S.M. Sikri

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 176; AIR 1965 SC 360; 1964 (9) FLR 238; 1964 (2) SCJ 590; [1964] 7 S.C.R. 89

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Constitution of India, 1950, art. 311(2) - Audh Narain Singh was appointed in 1949 as Tahvildar in District of Azamgarh in State of Uttar Pradesh and worked in Cash Department of Govt. treasury of that District - Appointment of Singh was made by Govt. Treasurer, with approval of District Magistrate - By an order, Singh was informed that he was, under instructions from Collector, removed from service - (A) Whether a Tahvildar appointed in Cash Department in State of Uttar Pradesh is a civil servant of State of Uttar Pradesh or holds a civil post in state? - Even after posts of Tanvildar were abolished Govt. of Uttar Pradesh did not adopt a consistent attitude and from time to time issued orders which indicated that a considerable degree of control was maintained by . upon Tahvildars in matter of appointment, removal from service, suspension and transfers and in matter of payment of remuneration, dearness allowance and making available certain medical benefits - Held,Tahvildars were treated on a par with other civil servants of State - (B) Whether Tahvildar is entitled to protection of art. 311 of Constitution? - Held, Tahvildar is entitled to protection of art. 311 of Constitution and an order, removing him from service must conform to requirements of Article - Appeal dismissed.
