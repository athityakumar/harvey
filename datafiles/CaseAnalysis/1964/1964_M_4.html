Commissioner of Income Tax, Madras v M. K. Stremann

Supreme Court of India

09 November 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri, K. Subba Rao, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 181; AIR 1965 SC 1494; [1965] 56 ITR 62; [1965] 2 S.C.R. 106

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 16 (3)(a)(iv) and 25 A, - (A) Whether various assets in question belonged only to assessee in his individual capacity? (B) Whether averment in relation to blending of properties in past was supported by other evidence or not? (C) Whether partition deed effect transfer of assets to minor children within meaning of s. 16(3)(a)(iv)(D) whether new plea can be allowed in an appeal? - Held (A), it did not - Partition proceeded on basis that self acquired properties were made available for partition along with only item of joint family property - That itself constituted proof that antecedent to partition, however, short interval, there was blending of self acquired properties of assessee with his ancestral joint family property (B) it certainly was - Properties dealt with at partition were treated by volition of assessee as properties available for partition between members of joint family (C) that partition deed did not effect a direct or indirect transfer of assets to minor children - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 (D) it cannot - Appeal by special leave against order of High Court on reference u/s. 66, Income tax Act, 1922 raising a particular question which neither arise out of order of Appellate Tribunal nor taken before HC or in statement of case before Supreme Court cannot be allowed -Appeal dismissed.
