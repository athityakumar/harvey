 N. B. Jeejeebhoy v Assistant Collector, Thana Prant, Thana

Supreme Court of India

05 October 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Mohammad Hidayatullah, Raghubar Dayal, S.M. Sikri

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 379; AIR 1965 SC 1096; 1965 (67) BomLR 575; [1965] 1 S.C.R. 636

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Market Value - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 3 and 4 - Govt. of India Act, 1935, s. 299 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 31(5)(a), 31A and 31B - Whether Amending Act was void on ground that it did not comply with provisions of s. 299 of Govt. of India Act, 1935? - Bombay Act, 1958 - Notification - Public purpose in act includes housing scheme - Amending Act provides for acquiring lands for housing schemes on payment of compensation which is likely to less than that payable if land is acquired - No property shall be taken possession of or acquired for a public purpose unless law provides for compensation for property taken possession of or acquired and either fixes amount of compensation or specifies principles on which and manner in which compensation is to be determined and given - Held, art. 31-B is not governed by art. 31-A and that art. 31-B is a constitutional device to place specified statutes beyond any attack on ground that they infringe Part III of Constitution - Bombay Amending Act being void at inception, was not an exercising law within meaning of art. 31(5)(a) or art. 31A at date of commencement of Constitution and could not therefore, be saved by either of these provisions -The lands acquired should be valued in accordance with provisions of Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - Appeals remanded.
