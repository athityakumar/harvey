Additional Income Tax Officer, Cuddapah v A. Thimmayyaand Others

Supreme Court of India

09 November 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, K. Subba Rao, S.M. Sikri

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 180; AIR 1965 SC 1238; [1965] 55 ITR 666; [1965] 2 S.C.R. 91

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 3, 14(1) and 25A - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Impugned order of ITO for recovery of assessed tax u/s. 25 - Whether liable to be quashed? - Hindu undivided family was disrupted prior to assessment - U/s. 25-A a Hindu undivided family being assessed in respect of its income will continue to be assessed in that status notwithstanding partition of property among its members - If a claim is raised at time of making an assessment that a partition has been effected, ITO must make an order that family property has been partitioned in definite portions - Held, ITO proceeded to assess income of Hindu undivided family as if property of family had not been partitioned and did not make any order at time of making assessments - Liability for payment of tax is on property of Hindu undivided family and not personally on members - ITO seeks to reach personal income of two respondents - Impugned order of ITO not valid - Appeals dismissed.
