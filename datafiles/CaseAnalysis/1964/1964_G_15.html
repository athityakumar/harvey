 Gurbux Singh v Bhooralal

Supreme Court of India

22 April 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Mohammad Hidayatullah, K.C. Das Gupta

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 67; AIR 1964 SC 1810; 1965 (1) ALT(SC) 107; [1964] 7 S.C.R. 831

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Civil Procedure Code, 1908 - Plaintiff filed suit for recovery of possession and mesne profits - It was found that in previous suit a decree for said profits was passed in respect of same land - Whether cause of action was same in both suits and so subsequent suit would be barred? - A plea of a bar u/O. 2, r. 2, CPC can be established only if defendant files in evidence pleadings in previous suit and thereby proves to Court identity of cause of action in two suits - Held, plea of bar under O. 2, r. 2 should not have been entertained by Trial Court because appellant had not filed pleadings in civil suit in support of his plea - Appeal dismissed.
