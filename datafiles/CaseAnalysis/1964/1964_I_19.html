 G. S. Ramaswamy and Others v Inspector-General of Police, Mysore

Supreme Court of India

21 January 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, K.C. Das Gupta, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 98; AIR 1966 SC 175; 1965 (10) FLR 65; 1970 (1) LLJ 649; [1964] 6 S.C.R. 279

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Right To Promotion, Eligibility - Seniority - Cadre

Summary: Service - States Reorganisation Act, 1956 - Mysore Seniority Rules 1957, r. 2 (c) - Hyderabad District Police Manual, r. 399 - Right to promotion - Seniority - Petitioners were sub-inspectors in former Hyderabad State - Posts of circle inspectors were to be filled by promotion from rank of sub-inspectors as per procedure - Eligibility list was to be considered for promotions - Petitioners names were included in eligibility list published in prior to the Act coming into force - They therefore contended that as per eligibility list they were entitled as of right to promotion to post of circle inspector as and when vacancies occurred - However, pursuant to the Act coming into force, transfers of public servants transpired - U/s. 115 of the Act, public servants so transferred were deemed to serve in connection with affairs of principal successor State - Petitioners continued to serve in new State and were promoted to circle inspectors - Promotion was done on ad hoc basis - Thereafter, petitioners were ordered to be reverted when certain confirmed circle inspectors who were on leave or on deputation outside State returned to new State - Aggrieved, petitioner filed writs which claimed that they were in eligibility list of former Hyderabad State, thus were entitled as of right to promotion as circle inspectors and to continue as such thereafter and order of their reversion amounted to reduction in rank and prayed for quashing of orders - Petitioners relied on r. 2(c) of the Rules - Respondents stated that petitioners were never confirmed by them as inspectors and therefore there was no question of any reduction in rank - HC accepted contentions raised on behalf of respondents and dismissed petitions - Hence, instant appeal - Whether sub-inspector's name that was on eligibility list had indefeasible right to promotion, and whether after such promotion on temporary or officiating basis he had right not to be reverted under any circumstances - Held, fact that sub inspector's name was in eligibility list gave him no right of kind urged on behalf of petitioners - R. 401 of Manual made it clear that even after sub-inspector's name was put in eligibility list, his fitness for promotion was to be decided year by year based on report that concerned his fitness for promotion or not - Hence the Rules had not contemplated automatic confirmation after probationary period - Petitioners were not entitled to claim that they were treated as confirmed circle inspectors because they had worked for more than two years on probation - Hence, petitioners contention that they had indefeasible right to promotion once their names were put in eligibility list and that they were entitled to continue as circle inspectors thereafter if they were promoted, on temporary or officiating basis, was not sustained - Petitioners could not be treated as confirmed circle inspectors - Reversion was made because senior circle inspectors had come back to State either from deputation or from leave and they had to be accommodated - Such reversion therefore had not amounted to reduction in rank - R. 2(c) of the Rules was not strictly applicable in instant case - Charge of discrimination based on violation of r. 2(c) of the Rules in special circumstances of instant case were not sustained, for it was not in dispute that petitioners were most junior according to provisional list, when orders of reversion were made - No order as to costs - Appeals dismissed.
