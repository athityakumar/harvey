Commissioner of Income Tax, Madras v Sivakasi Match Export Company

Supreme Court of India

29 April 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, S.M. Sikri, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 75; AIR 1964 SC 1813; [1964] 53 ITR 204; 1964 (2) MLJ(SC) 73; 1964 (2) SCJ 373; [1964] 8 S.C.R. 18

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Bombay Prohibition Act 1949 - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922 - Indian Partnership Act, 1932 - Partnership - Registration - Manufacturing of matches - Decline to register - 5 partnership firms manufacturing matches R is the sole proprietor and for other 4 firms there are more than one partner - When firm applied for registration, it was refused on the ground that different firms could not constitute a valid partnership - Thereafter, S , A , Aa , P and R the first partners of their respective firms and the last being the sole proprietor of his firm, in their individual capacity entered into a partnership - They presented the said deed of partnership to Income-tax Officer for registration - Income-tax Officer registered the same u/s. 26A but Commissioner cancelled the registration - On appeal, Appellate Tribunal opined that it was not genuine deed - HC, concluded that Match Works were not the real parties to partnership but parties of the document were the real partners - Hence instant appeal - Whether Income tax Officer can decline to register the deed if the partners failed sign in the deed - Held, yes - If all sons who have in truth agreed to be partners have not signed the deed or their shares are not truly set out in the deed of partnership, it would be open to the Income tax Officer to decline to register the deed, even if under the general law of partnership the rights and obligations of the partners so nomine thereto may otherwise be adjusted - When application for registration of the firm is made, Income tax Officer is entitled to ascertain whether the names of the partners in the instrument are of sons who have agreed to be partners, whether the shares are properly specified and whether the statement about the shares is real or is merely a cloak for distributing the profits in a different manner - HC was in error in holding on the question submitted that the registration of the assessee u/s. 26-A of Act was wrongly refused - Appeal dismissed.
