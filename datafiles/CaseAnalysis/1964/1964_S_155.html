State of W.B. and Others v P. N.Talukdar and Others

Supreme Court of India

13 August 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, A.K. Sarkar, S.M. Sikri

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 444; AIR 1965 SC 646; 1966 (1) SCJ 28

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4, 5-A, 6 and 38 - (A) Whether acquisition was for a public purpose or for a Company ? - Notification u/s. 6 specify that land is needed for construction of staff, quarters, hostel buildings and playground of Mission which is a Company - Agreement provides thereof that 'all and every compensation in respect of said land shall be paid by Mission' - Held, land is required for a Company - Therefore, it was necessary to comply with Part VII of Act - (B) Land and Property - Land Acquisition (Amendment) Act, 1962, ss. 40 and 41 - Whether acquisition is valid and terms of ss. 40 and 41 were complied with? - Question as to which clause of s. 40(1) was acted upon by state Govt. to give consent is important because nature of agreement u/s. 41 depends upon it - Construction of hostel buildings and playground comes within cl. b of s. 40(1)- construction of staff-quarters cannot come under that clause but may very well come within cl. a of s. 40(1) - However, preamble to agreement puts all three purposes together and makes no distinction between them indicating that it was cl. b which was under consideration of Govt. - Further held, land required for construction of staff-quarters cannot be brought u/cl. b - Whole of notification must be struck down - Appeal dismissed.
