Brahma Nand Puri v Neki Puri

Supreme Court of India

24 November 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, K. Subba Rao, Raghubar Dayal

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 368; AIR 1965 SC 1506; [1965] 2 S.C.R. 233

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Miscellaneous - Land & Property - Trusts & Associations - (A) Whether in a suit for ejectment plaintiff has to establish his title? - Held, custom must be established in favor of plaintiff to avoid ejectment - She must succeed or fail on title that she establishes and if she cannot succeed on strength of her title, her suit must fail notwithstanding that defendant in possession has no title to property - (B) Whether usage should be established by legal evidence? - Further held, party who lays on strength of any usage must establish it affirmatively by proper legal evidence - (C) Whether election is mandatory for Mahantship?- Further held, Mahantship Succession is by election by Bhekh in Punjab, unless there is usage of hereditary succession - Usage of hereditary succession without reference to election- Not established - Appeal dismissed.
