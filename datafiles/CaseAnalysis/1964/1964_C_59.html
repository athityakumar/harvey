Commissioner of Income Tax, Madras v V. Mr. P. Firm, Muar

Supreme Court of India

26 October 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri, J.C. Shah, K. Subba Rao

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 208; AIR 1965 SC 1216; [1965] 56 ITR 67; [1965] 1 S.C.R. 815

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Land & Property

Keywords: Equitable Doctrine, Debt Laws

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Debtor & Creditor (Occupation Period) Ordinance No XLII of 1948 of Malaya - Income-tax Act - Question of effect of Ordinance on liability of assessee to pay income-tax in respect of pre-occupation debts revived thereunder - Held, legal relationship of creditor and debtor was not created by Ordinance but it was regulated on basis of pre-existing relationship - Under Ordinance discharged debts became enforceable to extent of balance of amount due after scaling down of payments - ITO could only impose tax on income recovered by assessees thereafter towards their debts if such income was taxable under provisions of Act - In regard to payment made by assessees towards such debts they could claim relief by way of deductions only if such deductions were permissible under Act - Appeal dismissed.
