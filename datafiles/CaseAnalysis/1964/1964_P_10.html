Pentakota Srirakulu v Co-Operative Marketing Society Limited

Supreme Court of India

28 August 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 383; AIR 1965 SC 621; 1965 (2) MLJ(SC) 111; [1965] 1 S.C.R. 186

Case Digest

Subject: Trusts & Associations; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Prohibition Act

Summary: (A) Trusts & Associations - Madras Co-operative Societies Act , 1932, s. 51 - Madras Co-operative Societies Act Conduct of enquiry - Show cause notice - Maintainability - There were complaints regarding working of Society and accordingly enquiry was instituted into its affairs by Registrar of Co-operative Societies - As result of facts disclosed in inquiry Committee then in management of Society was, after due notice to show cause and hearing, superseded by order of Registrar such supersession being authorized by s. 43 of the Act - Special officer was appointed to take charge of affairs of Society and that officer filed claim before Registrar against appellant - Registrar appointed, u/s. 51(2) of the Act, Deputy Registrar of Co-operative Societies to act as arbitrator to adjudicate claim - Immediately order was passed and appellant filed petition in HC for prohibiting Deputy Registrar from dealing with claim which he was directed to try - Single Judge allowed petition and granted appellant relief he sought - Co-operative Society made appeal to Division Bench of HC which allowed appeal and dismissed petition - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether Registrar should had proceeded u/s. 49 of the Act and not u/s. 51 of the Act - Held, it was true that there was enquiry conducted affairs of Society u/s. 38 of the Act but that by itself was not sufficient - However, it had further to be proved that facts alleged in claim, and on which it was based, were disclosed at that enquiry that could be proved or established only if enquiry report which was submitted to Registrar was placed before Court and facts disclosed corresponded with facts alleged in statement of claim - Further, it was not possible to say that there was correspondence between facts disclosed in that report as result of enquiry u/s. 38 of the Act and those found in Statement of Claim which was referred by Registrar to Deputy Registrar for arbitration u/s. 51 of the Act - Therefore, case did not fell u/s. 49 of the Act - Appeal dismissed.

(B) Trusts & Associations - Contract & Commercial - Madras Co-operative Societies Act , 1932, s. 51 - Sale transaction - Legality - Whether transaction of sale which gave rise to commission alleged to be improperly retained was illegal and that Society could not made claim on basis of such illegal transaction - Held, no illegality was attached to contract between appellant and Society so that was perfectly legal - that arose out of his position as President of Society and he was, in law, bound to account for moneys he received on behalf of Society - Further, fact that he entered into illegal transactions would have no bearing on right of Society to make claim for account of commission due to Society which he unjustly withheld - Further, it was clear that Judges did not intend to prejudice appellant in his defence before Deputy Registrar in arbitration proceedings u/s. 51 of the Act but it was possible that it could had such effect - Appeal dismissed.
