Kaviraj Pandit Durga Dutt Sharma v Navaratna Pharmaceutical Laboratories

Supreme Court of India

20 October 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 464; AIR 1965 SC 980; 1965 KerLJ 690; [1965] 1 S.C.R. 737

Case Digest

Subject: Intellectual Property

Summary: Intellectual Property - Indian Trade Marks Act, 1940, ss. 2, 6, 21 and 82-A - Cochin Trade Marks Act, 1944, s. 78-A - Trade Marks (Amendment) Act, 1946 - U.K. Trade Marks Act, 1938 (ENG) - Whether appellant's mark is deceptively similar to that of the respondents? - Court held, trade mark to be compared with that used by the appellant is entire registered mark including the word 'Navaratna', therefore, conclusion reached by courts below that appellant's mark is deceptively similar to that of respondents are correct - Appeal dismissed.
