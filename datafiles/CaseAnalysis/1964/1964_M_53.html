 Income Tax Officer, A-Ward, Sitapur v Murlidhar Bhagwan Das

Supreme Court of India

29 January 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Raghubar Dayal, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 296; AIR 1965 SC 342; 1964 (1) ITJ 599; [1964] 52 ITR 335; 1964 (1) SCJ 583; [1964] 6 S.C.R. 411

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, ss.31,34(3) - Income - Impugned notice - Assessment - Classification - Respondent was a firm carrying on business in different lines assessed to Income Tax u/s. 23 (4) of Act - Assessment was cancelled u/s.27 of Act - Before cancellation it was found that interest income of Rs. 88,737 received by him in discharge of debts due from third parties had escaped assessment as assessee failed to disclose - Income Tax Officer issued a notice u/s.34(1)(a) of Act for a/y 1949-50 on ground that sum of Rs.88,737 had escaped assessment in assessment year - Appellate Assistant Commissioner found that bonds were received by assessee in previous accounting year and directed sum representing interest on bonds should be deleted from assessment for year ending 1949-50 and included in assessment for year ending 1948-49 - HC quashed proceedings initiated by Income Tax Officer - Hence instant appeal - Whether escaped assessment has been discovered - Held, persons whose income have escaped assessment and fact that they had escaped assessment had not been discovered till after lapse of eight years from year in which they could had been assessed to tax on such income can be placed in one class - Absence of a stay of proceedings by HC Income Tax Officer had actually made an assessment in pursuance of impugned notice - Assessment will stand unless it was modified or annulled in any proceeding permitted by law - Appeal dismissed.
