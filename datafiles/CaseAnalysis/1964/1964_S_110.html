State of Maharashtra v Mayer Hans George

Supreme Court of India

24 August 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 5; AIR 1965 SC 722; [1965] 35 Comp Cas 557; 1965 CRLJ 641; 1999 (110) E.L.T. 236; [1965] 1 S.C.R. 123

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Exchange Control & FDI - Foreign Exchange Regulation Act, 1947, ss. 8(i), 23(1A) and 24(1) - Notifications D/- 08-11-1962 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 134 and 136 - Objects - Notifications u/s. 8(1) by Central Govt. and RBI - Contravention - Foreigner - Prosecution - Criminal appeal - Sentence - Interference - Practice of Supreme Court - Case presenting unusual features - Questions arise - (A) Is mens rea, an essential ingredient of offence u/s. 23 (lA) r/w s. 8(l) of Act - (B) Is absence of - Knowledge or statutory requirement as to mode of publication of notification, valid defence - (C) Meaning of 'Cargo' under second proviso of notification - (D) Whether to interfere with sentence - Mens Rea held to be an essential ingredient and mere voluntary act of bringing gold into India without permission of Reserve Bank constitutes offence - Ignorance of law not excused as publication in Gazette of India is effective to give notice of it to persons concerned - Gold bars carried on person by air passenger is cargo and not personal luggage and has to be entered in manifest - Sentence reduced to that already undergone - Appeal allowed.
