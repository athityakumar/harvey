 Ranjit D. Udeshi v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

19 August 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 167; AIR 1965 SC 881; 1965 CRLJ 8; [1965] 1 S.C.R. 65

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Keywords: Freedom Of Speech And Expression, Speech And Expression, Obscene

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 292 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Appellant partner of a firm which owns a book-stal - Appellant found in possession of obscene books - Prosecuted along with other partners u/s. 292 - Revision petition dismissed by High Court - Whether offence u/s. 292 IPC, 1860 made out and can appellant be convicted? - Prosecution need not prove something which law does not burden it with if knowledge were made a part of guilty act and law required prosecution to prove it would place an almost impenetrable defence in hands of offenders Something much less than actual knowledge must therefore suffice - Held, no social gain from said book - Books were for sale and that comes with meaning of s. 292 IPC, 1860 - Appeal dismissed.
