 Brij Mohan Singh v Priya Brat Narain Sinha and Others

Supreme Court of India

05 February 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

K.C. Das Gupta, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 148; AIR 1965 SC 282; 1964 (2) MLJ(SC) 140; 1964 (2) SCJ 644; [1965] 3 S.C.R. 861

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Election - Constitution of India, 1950, art.137 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 30 and 35 - Election of Bihar Legislative Assembly - Appellant declared elected - Respondent challenging validity of appellant's election on ground that appellant was under 25 years of age on date of filing nomination papers and therefore was disqualified u/art. 137 from being member of Bihar Legislative Assembly - HighCourt, set aside election of appellant - Present appeal - Book was maintained by Chowkidar for noting births in his Ilaka during years 1934 to 1936 - Issue: whether an entry made in an official record maintained by an illiterate public servant or by some one else at his request is relevant u/s. 35 of Evidence Act - Reason why an entry made by a public servant in a public or other official book, register, or record stating a fact in issue or a relevant fact has been made relevant is that when a public servant makes it himself in discharge of his official duty, probability of its being truly and correctly recorded is high - That probability is reduced to a minimum when public servant himself is illiterate and has to depend on somebody else to make entry - Held, High Court is right in holding that entry made in an official record maintained by illiterate Chowkidar, by somebody else at his request does not come within s. 35 - Entry in hath-chitha has therefore to be left out of consideration in coming to a conclusion about appellant's age - Appeal allowed.
