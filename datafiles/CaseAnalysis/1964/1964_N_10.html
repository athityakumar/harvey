Patel Chunibhai Dajibhai Etc v Narayanrao Khanderao Jambekar and Another

Supreme Court of India

03 December 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sarkar, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, R.S. Bachawat

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 355; AIR 1965 SC 1457; [1965] 2 S.C.R. 328

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Rent Control - Bombay Tenancy and Agricultural Lands Act, 1948, ss. 32 (1)(a), (b) (i),(ii),(iii) and 76 A - (A) Whether legislature had made law so retrospective as to affect a pending proceeding? - Held, s. 32 after amendment provided that a tenant personally cultivating land would on date of amending Act be entitled to claim to have become a purchaser of land held by him with effect from 01-04-1957, if no application for his ejectment on strength of a notice u/s. 14 or u/s. 31 had been filed on or before 31-03-1957 - (B) Whether orders passed by Collector in present case is valid? - Further held, orders of Collector pass before arrival of record do not affect decision after its arrival - But having called for record, Collector should properly have waited for its arrival before passing any orders - Collector orders are final in dismissing revision petition and could not be reviewed and reopened by him subsequently - Appeal Nos. 791 to 798 of 1964 dismissed.
