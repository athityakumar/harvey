State of Gujarat v Kansara Manilal Bhikhalal

Supreme Court of India

07 April 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 34; AIR 1964 SC 1893; 1965 CRLJ 90; 1964 (9) FLR 147; 1964 (5) G.L.R. 819; 1964 (2) LLJ 456; [1964] 7 S.C.R. 656

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Simple imprisonment

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Factories Act, 1948, ss. 101 and 117 - (A) Whether s. 117 of Factories Act, 1948 applies only to officers?- Held, language of s. 117 is not limited to officers but is made wide to include any person - It thus gives protection not only to an officer doing or intending to do something in pursuance or execution of this Act but also to any person - (B) Whether protection u/s. 117 of Act be claimed for acts done in violation of Act?- Further held, protection conferred by s. 117 can only be claimed by a person who can plead that he was required to do or omit to do something under Act or that he intended to comply with any of its provisions - It cannot confer immunity in respect of actions which are not done under Act but are done contrary to it - Even assuming that an act includes an omission as stated in General clauses Act, omission also must be one which is enjoined by Act - It is not sufficient to say that act was honest - That would bring it only within words 'good faith' - (C) Whether mens rea is necessary for offence committed u/s. 101 of Act?- Further held, it is not necessary that means rea must always be established - Responsibility exists without a guilty mind - An adequate safeguard exists in s. 101 and occupier and manager can save themselves if they prove that they are not real offenders but who, in fact, is - (D)Whether departure from hours of work as laid down in s. 61 (2) be made in cases covered by exempting provisions?- Further held, s. 59 to be r/w s. 64 - Departure from hours of work as laid down in s. 61(2) can only be made in cases covered by exempting provisions - S. 59 cannot be considered in isolation - It has to be r/w s. 64, where State Govt. has been given power to make exempting rules - (E) Whether changes mentioned in s. 61(10) speaks of over all change affecting whole group or an individual worker?- Further held, provision contemplates over all change affecting whole group and not individual worker - Order accordingly
