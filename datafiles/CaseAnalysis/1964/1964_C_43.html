Commissioner of Income Tax, Kerala v Malayalam Plantations Limited

Supreme Court of India

10 April 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, S.M. Sikri, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 274; AIR 1964 SC 1722; [1964] 53 ITR 140; 1964 (2) SCJ 338; [1964] 7 S.C.R. 693

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Indirect Tax

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act,1922, s. 10(2)(xv) - Estate Duty Act, 1953, s. 84 - (A) Whether expression 'for purpose of business' is same as expression 'for purpose of earning profits' ? - Held, expression 'for purpose of business' is wider in scope than expression 'for purpose of earning profits' - Its range is wide - But however wide meaning of expression may be, its limits are implicit in it - Purpose shall be for purpose of business, expenditure incurred shall be carrying on of business and assessee shall incur it in his capacity as a person carrying on businesses and assessee shall incur it in his capacity as a person carrying on business - (B) Whether payment of duty by Resident Company incorporated outside India and paying estate duty u/s. 84 of Estate Duty Act is deductible u/s. 10(2) (xv) of Income Tax Act?- Further held, estate duty paid by assessee was not an allowable deduction u/s 10(2)(xv) of Income Tax Act - Appeals allowed.
