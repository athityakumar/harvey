Isher Singh v Sarwan Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

30 September 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, A.K. Sarkar, R.S. Bachawat

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 451; AIR 1965 SC 948

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s.11 - Res-judicata - Respondents initiated suit for possession and title against appellant - High Court hold that plea regarding title of respondents to property as next heirs of deceased was barred by res judicata - Whether finding in a previous suit between same parties on issue relating to relationship of respondent to deceased owner of suit property is or is not res judicata in suit? - In former suit heirship of respondents to the deceased (a) was in terms raised by pleadings, (b) that an issue was framed in regard to it by trial judge, (c) that evidence was led by parties on that point directed towards this issue, (d) a finding was recorded on it by appellate court, and (e) that on proper construction of pleadings it would have been necessary to decide issue in order to properly and completely decide all points arising in case to grant relief to plaintiff - Every one of conditions necessary to satisfy test as to applicability of s.11, CPC, 1908 is satisfied - Held, matter brought up by an independent appeal would not affect jurisdiction of court which is seized of suit to enter into correctness of this finding and give its decision thereon - Every necessary condition of s.11 of CPC, 1908 has been satisfied - Appeal dismissed.
