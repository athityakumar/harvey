Radha Kishan Bhatia v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

23 November 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

Raghubar Dayal, K. Subba Rao, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 335; AIR 1965 SC 1072; 1965 CRLJ 154; 1984 ECR 497; 2004 (178) E.L.T. 8; [1965] 2 S.C.R. 213

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Importation

Summary: Customs - Sea Customs Act, 1878, ss.18, 19 and 167(8) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - (A) Whether recovery of gold from person of appellant is sufficient to conclude that appellant was concerned in committing offence of importing gold illegally? - Appellant cannot be said to be so concerned in commission of this offence if he is not responsible in this manner and if he got possession of gold after it had been brought into country - (B) Whether it was necessary for Collector of Customs to record a formal finding to effect that person proceeded against was concerned in importing of smuggled gold? - Mere finding of fact that a person is in possession of smuggled goods does not imply that Collector of Customs had considered question of person's being concerned in the commission of offence of illegal importation of goods - Held, order of appellant bench of High Court set aside - Appeal allowed.
