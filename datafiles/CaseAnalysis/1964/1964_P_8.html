 Mahjibhai Mohanbhai Barot v Patel Manibhai Gokalbhai and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 December 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sarkar, K. Subba Rao, Raghubar Dayal, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 218; AIR 1965 SC 1477; 1965 BLJR 542; 1965 (6) G.L.R. 901; [1965] 2 S.C.R. 436

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Ex-Parte

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, ss. 144, 47, 39, 38, 37, 2(2) - Limitation Act, 1908, arts. 181 and 182 - Operation of s.114 of CPC when court deals with powers for ordering restitution? - Held, whenever an Appellate Court provides for restitution, application goes for execution of decree which is guided by powers and procedure laid down in s.114 of CPC - (1)What does a court do when decree is reversed or varied?- Further held, for a decree being reversed or varied,the 'first instance court makes a restitution and other necessary orders related to it to execute restitution' - (B) How does 'court of first instance' execute a decree u/ss. 144, 37 and 38 of CPC ? - Further held, an application u/s. 144 can be filed before court of first instance but u/s. 38 decree is executed by court passing or executing it - U/s. 37 of CPC expression 'courts which passed a decree' includes - (a) Court of Appellate Jurisdiction of court of first instance is used (b)when court of first instance ceases its jurisdiction another court wherein decree was passed was instituted at time of making application for execution of decree, would have jurisdiction to try such suit - When s. 37 is r/w s. 38 its clearly lays down court can execute a decree u/s. 38 and thus an application for execution can be filed under it once established as court, passing decree this decree can be passed u/s. 39 - (C) How does limitation on appeal operate once an appellant does not execute a decree in his favour? - Further held, when a respondent in appeal does not execute a decree in his favour and appeal is dismissed, limitation for execution of duty is governed by s. 182 of Limitation Act - If respondent has taken property from appellant even if appeal is allowed limitation is governed by s. 181 of Limitation Act - When an appellant is under some disability and is not covered by extension periods u/s. 6 and 7 of Limitation Act though appeal is allowed in his favour - Restitutions get barred when it is stayed by a second Appellate Court u/s. 15 of Limitation act while party had been prevented from applying for restitution - Appeal dismissed.
