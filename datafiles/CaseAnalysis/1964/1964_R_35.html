Rattan Lal v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

10 April 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, K.C. Das Gupta, Raghubar Dayal

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 196; AIR 1965 SC 444; 1965 CRLJ 360; [1964] 7 S.C.R. 676

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Defence & Security Forces; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Probation of Offenders Act, 1958,ss. 3, 4, 6 and 11 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 20 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 307 and 326 - Whether Appellate Court can exercise its powers u/s. 6 of Probation of Offenders Act, 1958 in respect of accused convicted before Act? - Appellant 16 years old at time of offence - Held, if Act is not given retrospective effect, it will lead to various anomolies and it cannot be intention of legislature - Appellate court can validly exercise its powers u/s. 6 of Probation of Offenders Act - Appeal allowed by majority - Matter remanded back to High Court for making order in compliance with s.6 of Probation of Offenders Act - Matter remitted back to HC - Order accordingly.
