Hindustan Commercial Bank, Limited, Kanpur v Bhagwan Dass

Supreme Court of India

26 November 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Bachawat

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 46; AIR 1965 SC 1142; 1965 (10) FLR 168; 1965 (1) LLJ 466; [1965] 2 S.C.R. 265

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Constitution - Labour & Industrial Law - Practice & Procedure - Uttar Pradesh Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Supreme Court Rules, 1950 - O. XIII, r. 2 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 408 and 420 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 22, 132(1) and 133 - Special leave granted ex parte - Non-compliance with O. 13, r. 2 brought to notice of Supreme Court - Exemption from compliance not obtained - Whether an application for special leave under art. 136 of Constitution can be entertained by Supreme Court where High Court has not been first moved for issue of a certificate under art. 132 and had refused to grant certificate under O. 45, r. 1 of Supreme Court Rules? - Held, O. 13, r. 2 of Supreme Court Rules is mandatory - Supreme Court can excuse appellant from compliance with requirements of O. 13, r.2 for sufficient reasons - As appeal involves substantial question of law obtaining fitness certificate is mandatory - Violation of O. 13, r. 2 - No exemption was granted - Supreme Court revoked special leave to appeal as obtained in contravention of rules - No proof showing appellant prejudiced due to delay in filing objection - Appellant had applied to High Court for certificate under art. 132, but without pursuing application further applied to Supreme Court for special leave and Supreme Court ex parte granted same, and subsequently it had been brought to notice of Supreme Court that appellant had not applied to Supreme Court for exemption from compliance with O. 13, r. 2 and had not obtained exemption, special leave granted ex parte was revoked - Leave revoked.
