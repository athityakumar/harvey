State of Mysore v Yaddalam Lakshminarasimhaiah Setty and Sons

Supreme Court of India

10 November 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri, J.C. Shah, K. Subba Rao

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 198; AIR 1965 SC 1510; [1965] 2 S.C.R. 129; 1965 (16) STC 231

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Keywords: Tax On The Sale Or Purchase Of Goods, Collected, Mysore Sales Tax Act, 1957

Summary: Sales Tax - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956, ss.6, 8 and 9 - Karnataka Sales Tax Act (25 of 1957), s. 5(3)(a) and Sch. II, Entry 7 - Tax liability under State and Central Act - Assessee is a dealer in powerloom and handloom textiles - Assessed and taxed turnover relating to powerloom textiles u/s.9 of Central Sales Tax Act - Assessee contended that its turnover consisting of sales of textiles manufactured by means of powerlooms in course of inter-state trade is liable to be taxed at same rate and exactly in same manner as they would have been taxed if they had been intra- state transactions - (A) Whether sale of powerloom cloth is taxable under Central Act when in course of inter state trade ? - Held, Sales of powerloom cloth which are not 'first sales' within meaning of s.5(3)(a) of Mysore Sales Tax Act are not exigible to tax under State act - They would not also be liable to tax under Central Act when they are effected in course of inter state trade or commerce - (B) Whether dealer is liable to pay any tax u/s. 6 of Central Sales Tax Act,1956 if he comes within proviso to s.8(1) of Act of 1956 ? - If effect of another provision is to take away liability effect will have to be given to it - Further held, notwithstanding s.6 of Act of 1956, the dealer may not be liable to pay any tax if he comes within proviso to s.8(1) of same - It follows that scheme of Act is not that every transaction in inter-state trade must bear some tax - (C) Whether expression 'Levied' in s.9(1) of Central Act refers to expression 'levied' in s.5(3)(a) of State Act? - S 9(1) says that under Central Act tax shall be levied in same manner as tax on sale or purchase of goods under general sales tax law of State is assessed, paid and collected - There is no reason why Central Act made a departure in manner of levy of tax on specified goods, which are taxed only at a single point under State Act- If any such radical departure was intended, Central Act would have expressly stated so - Further held, expression 'levied' in s.9(1) of Central Act refers to expression 'Levied' in s.5(3)(a) of State Act - Appeal dismissed.
