 Shree Bajarang Jute Mills Limited, Guntur v State of Andhra Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

06 February 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, K.C. Das Gupta

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 55; AIR 1966 SC 376; 1964 (2) MLJ(SC) 122; [1964] 6 S.C.R. 691; 1964 (15) STC 430

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Keywords: Madras General Sales Tax Act

Summary: Sales Tax - Indirect Tax - Indian Sale of Goods Act, 1930, s. 39 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 286(1) - Government of India Act, 1935 - Constitution (Sixth Amendment) Act, 1956 - Non-explanation sales - Property in goods despatched by appellant passed to A.C.C. within State of Andhra when railway receipts were handed over to agent of A.C.C. against payment of price - Whether sales to A.C.C. by appellant may be regarded as 'non-Explanation sales i.e., sales falling outside Explanation to art. 286(1)? - Goods had to be actually delivered as a direct result of sale, for purpose of consumption in State in which they were delivered - Held, High Court was therefore in error in inferring from fact that property had passed within State of Andhra against delivery of railway receipts, that goods were actually delivered within State - Appeal allowed.
