Dr. Sham Lal Narula v Commissioner of Income Tax, Punjab, Jammu and others

Supreme Court of India

09 April 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, S.M. Sikri, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 263; AIR 1964 SC 1878; [1964] 53 ITR 151; [1964] 7 S.C.R. 668

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Land & Property

Keywords: Compulsory Acquisition of Land and Property

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Land & Property - Income Tax Act 1922, s. 4 - Land Acquisition Act, ss. 23 and 34 - (A) Whether interest paid u/s 23 of Act amounts to compensation?- Held, s. 34 of Land Acquisition Act itself makes a distinction between amount awarded as compensation, interest payable on amount so awarded - Interest has to be paid on amount awarded from time Collector takes possession until amount is paid or deposited - A perusal of provisions of s. 23 shows that interest is not an item included in compensation for any of matters mentioned therein, nor it is mentioned as a consideration for acquisition of land u/s. 23(2) - Interest pertains to domain of payment after compensation has been ascertained- It is a consideration paid either for use of money or for forbearance from demanding it after it has fallen due - Act itself makes a clear distinction between compensation payable for land acquired and interest payable on compensation awarded - Legislature expressly used word 'interest' with its well known connotation u/s. 34 of Act - It is reasonable to give expression natural meaning it bears - Interest, whether it is statutory or contractual, represents profit creditor might have made if he had use of money or loss he suffered because he had not that use - It is something in addition to capital amount, though it arises out of it - Scheme of Land Acquisition Act and express provisions thereof thus establish that statutory interest payable u/s 34 is not compensation paid to owner for depriving him of his right to possession of land acquired, but is given to him for deprivation of use of money representing compensation for land acquired - Therefore, statutory interest paid u/s 34 is a revenue receipt liable to tax under Income Tax Act - Appeal dismissed.
