Bondada Gajapathy Rao v State of Andhra Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

16 March 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sarkar, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 410; AIR 1964 SC 1645; 1964 CRLJ 598; [1964] 7 S.C.R. 251

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 136 and 134 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898, s. 431 - (A) Whether principles of s.431 of CrPC, 1898 can be applied to appeals under Constitution against sentence of fine and not against sentence of imprisonment?-Held, s. 431 CrPC, 1898 does not apply proprio vigore to a case of appeal filed with special leave of Supreme Court granted u/art.136, when appellant accused dies pending appeal - Where appeal is against sentence of fine, appeal may be permitted to be continued by LRs of deceased appellant accused - No provision making such appeals abate - If they can be continued when arising under Code, there is no reason why they should not be continued when arising under Constitution - Where sentence is not one of fine but of imprisonment, which on death of accused becomes in fructuous, sentence does not affect property of deceased accused in hands of his LRs - Appeal, in such a case, would abate, upon death of accused - (B) Whether setting aside of sentence would entitle heirs to salary? - Held, fact that accused was a Govt. servant and was under suspension during trial and fact that if conviction and sentence were set aside, his estate would be entitled to receive full pay for period of suspension, cannot be said to affect his estate, because, setting aside of sentence would not automatically entitle LRs to salary - (C) Whether English decisions are binding on Indian courts? - Held, no - They have only persuasive value in India - Appeal refused.
