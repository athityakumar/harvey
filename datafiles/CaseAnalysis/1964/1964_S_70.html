 State of Assam and Another v Ajit Kumar Sarma and Others

Supreme Court of India

27 October 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, J.R. Madholkar, Mohammad Hidayatullah, Raghubar Dayal

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 11; AIR 1965 SC 1196; 1966 (1) LLJ 451; [1965] 1 S.C.R. 890

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service

Summary: Service - Constitution - Constitution of India, art. 226 - Grant of Leave - Approval of Resolution - Leave without Pay - Enforcement of Rules - Respondent/Teacher applied for leave with pay, in order to contest a seat for Parliament and leave was granted by respondent No. 2/Governing Body of College, by a resolution - Appellant, informed that he was unable to approve resolution permitting respondent and others to rejoin their duties - Hence, respondent was granted compulsory leave without pay - Respondent filed petition before HC, which was accepted - Appellant was aggrieved by said order - Hence instant appeal - Whether appellant's grant of compulsory leave without pay to respondents was justifiable and resolution passed by respondent No. 2 was ultra vires and void - Held, Court could not interfere with order of HC, as it was against respondent No. 2/Governing Body of College - Statutory rules did not confer right on respondent /teachers of private colleges, which would entitle them to maintain a petition u/art. 226 of Constitution for enforcement or non-enforcement of any provision of Rules - Court opined that HC had committed error, in granting a writ against respondent/State through its Director, by which Director was asked not to give effect to its letter against Governing Body of College and HC's order was set-aside - Appeal allowed.
