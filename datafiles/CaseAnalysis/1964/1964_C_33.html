 Kylasa Sarabhiah, Bombay Cloth Shop, Secunderabad v Commissioner of Income Tax, Andhra Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

01 December 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Mohammad Hidayatullah, S.M. Sikri, R.S. Bachawat

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 93; AIR 1965 SC 1411; [1965] 56 ITR 219; [1965] 2 S.C.R. 310

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 26A - Whether assessee is entitled to registration u/s. 26A? - If statutory conditions which qualify appellants for registration are fulfilled, an arrangement between some of partners of appellants which binds them to distribute profits under a stipulation which is not a part of partnership agreement does not affect right to claim registration of partnership agreement - Held, partnership deed clearly recited in its preamble equal share of minor partners in profits, and losses to be shared equally by and between major partners - Tribunal erred in holding that shares of partners were not specified in deed - Partnership agreement was signed by major partners, application for registration was in conformity with rules framed under Act- Statutory conditions were fulfilled - Assessee was entitled to registration u/s. 26A - Appeal allowed.
