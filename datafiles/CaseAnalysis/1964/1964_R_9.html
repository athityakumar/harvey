 Rafiquennessa v Lal Bahadur Chetri (Dead) Through His

Supreme Court of India

24 February 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, S.M. Sikri, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 108; AIR 1964 SC 1511; 1964 (2) SCJ 562; [1964] 6 S.C.R. 876

Case Digest

Subject: Parliament & Legislature

Keywords: Assam Non-Agricultural Urban Areas Tenancy Act, 1955

Summary: Land & Property - Rent Control - Assam non-agricultural Urban Areas Tenancy Act, 1955, s. 5(1)(a) - Leases executed after Act came into force and leases executed prior to operation of Act - Whether a legislature is competent to take away vested rights by means of retrospective legislation ? - Held, where vested rights are affected by any statutory provision, said provision should normally be construed to be prospective in operation and not retrospective unless provision in question relates merely to a procedural matter - Legislature is undoubtedly competent to make laws which override and materially affect terms of contracts between parties - Express provisions essential in statute to so - S. 5 itself gives an unmistakable indication of legislative intention to make its provisions retrospective - A suit which was pending when Act came into force would be governed by s. 5(1)(a) and an appeal arising from a suit which had been decided before Act came into force would likewise be governed by s. 5(1)(a), provided it is pending after date when Act came into force - Appeal dismissed.
