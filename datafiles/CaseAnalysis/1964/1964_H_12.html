 Hindustan Construction Company Limited v Income Tax Officer (Companies Circle) Bombay and Another

Supreme Court of India

10 December 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, J.C. Shah, Mohammad Hidayatullah, R.S. Bachawat

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 200; AIR 1965 SC 1316; [1965] 56 ITR 241; [1965] 2 S.C.R. 414

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Set Off, Bombay Prohibition Act 1949

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Bombay Prohibition Act 1949 - Constitution of India, 1950

- Indian Income Tax Act, 1922 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Appellant carried on business not only in India but also outside India - Controversy between appellant and Revenue that facts relating to Ceylon income and Kolhapur income are substantially similar - Appellant wrote a letter to Income Tax Officer stating that for the assessment year 1949-50, it was entitled to refund on the income taxed in Kapurthala State - Attached an original certificate for tax showing payment of Rs. 37,828/11/-, and requested that a refund order be passed at an early date - Income Tax Officer rejected the claim on ground that claim filed by appellant was not within the time limit of four years laid down in r.5 of 1939 Rules - Appellant filed revision, u/s.3A of Act, against order of Commissioner of Income-Tax that "unfortunately the Company's assessment for the year in question was completed by the Income-Tax Officer on the last day of the financial year 1953-54 - Commissioner rejected the petition and observed that "the assessment in the Kapurthala State was made on 20-3-1950 much before the assessment was completed by the Bombay Income-tax Officer - Appellant then approached Central Board of Revenue who inturn declined to interfere - Appellant failed to take steps to apply to HC for quashing the above orders of Commissioner of Income-Tax or Central Board of Revenue - HC assumed that s.49E of Act not give any assistance to appellant since prior adjudication in favour of appellant - Appellant entitled to refund u/r.3 of 1939Rules and Income-Tax Officer has only to calculate the relief due and then set it off - Whether refund was due or not when an application for refund was made to him - Whether there was any condition necessary to be fulfilled before s.49E of Act becomes applicable - Held, prior adjudication before a claim can be allowed u/s.49E of Act and there was nothing to debar the Income-Tax Officer from determining the question - If a claim to refund is barred by a final order, it cannot be said that there. is a subsisting obligation to make a payment - S.49E of Act does not applies - Therefore, agreeing with HC assumed that s.49E of Act was of no assistance to the appellant and that petition was rightly dismissed - Appeal dismissed.
