 State of Madras v G. Sundaram

Supreme Court of India

09 October 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

Raghubar Dayal, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 454; AIR 1965 SC 1103; 1965 (2) MLJ(SC) 140

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226, 309 and 311 - Madras Civil Services (Disciplinary Proceedings Tribunal) Rules, 1948, r. 8 - Madras District Police Act, 1859, s. 10 - Madras Police Subordinate Service (Discipline and Appeal) Rules, 1950, r. 3(b) - Respondent, an inspector of Police - Corruption charges - Enquiry ordered by Govt. - Tribunal under Rules, 1948 conducted enquiry - Submission of enquiry report - Dismissal recommended - Govt. instead ordered compulsory retirement - Petition u/art. 226 preferred - Dismissed by Single Judge - Letters Patent Appeal - Accepted - Division Bench found evidence to be not sufficient - (A) Whether High Court, in a petition u/art. 226, could examine findings of a Disciplinary Tribunal and come to conclusion whether Govt. servant could be held to be guilty of any of charges laid against him' to be a matter of general public importance? - Held, position well settled vide decisions of court that in exercise of its jurisdiction u/art. 226 of Constitution of India, 1950 cannot sit in appeal over findings of fact recorded by a Competent Tribunal in a properly conducted departmental enquiry except when it be shown that impugned findings were not supported by any evidence - Re-appreciation of evidence before Tribunal to be erroneous - (B) Contention by respondent that he is governed by Police Act, 1859 and that an order of compulsory retirement amounts to an order of dismissal which could be passed only by one of officers specified in s. 10 of Police Act, 1859 and not by State Govt. which is not given any power to pass such order, whether acceptable? - Held, order of compulsory retirement not amounted to an order of dismissal and thus did not come within language of s. 10 of Police Act, 1859 - Provisions of s. 10 are subject to art. 311 and if order of compulsory retirement is order of dismissal, than also requirement of art. 311 are satisfied which is that there could not be any dismissal by an authority subordinate to that by which he was appointed - Respondent must have been appointed to Police Service in 1929 by an authority subordinate to State Govt. and therefore, State Govt. was competent to dismiss him - (C) Whether referring of case to Tribunal by Govt. and acting on its report when there is existent Police Rules, proper? - Held, Tribunal Rules are applicable to respondent - Provision of r. 8 of Tribunal Rules compared with r. 3(b) of Police Rules - Procedures substantially same in both provisions - Impugned judgment set aside - Appeal allowed.
