 Godavari Shamrao Parulekar v State of Maharashtra and Others

Supreme Court of India

29 January 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, A.K. Sarkar, K.C. Das Gupta, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 101; AIR 1964 SC 1128; 1964 (66) BomLR 453; 1964 CRLJ 222; 1965 MPLJ 134; [1964] 6 S.C.R. 446

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Defence & Security Forces; Land & Property

Keywords: Defence Regulations

Summary: Criminal - Detention - Defence of India Act, 1962, ss. 40 and 44 - Defence of India Rules, 1962, r. 30(1)(b) - Preventive Detention Act - Code of Criminal Procedure ,s. 491 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 226 and 32 - Habeas Corpus - Impugned detention order revoked by Govt. and on same day fresh detention order was passed - Whether habeas corpus petition maintainable in regard to previous detention order and whether second detention order legal? - It is true that s. 44 provides that there should be as little interference with ordinary avocations of life as possible when orders are made under Act or Rules, but that does not mean that a detention order must show on face of it that State Govt. had considered various clauses of r. 30(1) and had come to conclusion that only way in which purposes of Act and Rules could be carried out was by use of cl. (b) of r. 30(1) - Held, fresh detention order made in view of public safety and nothing illegal committed by Govt. by passing fresh detention order - Appeal dismissed.
