Commissioner of Income Tax, Madras v M. Ganapathi Mudaliar

Supreme Court of India

27 April 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri, K. Subba Rao, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 241; [1964] 53 ITR 623

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s.66 - Agreement - Income of assessee - Supplementary Statement - No Evidence - Remuneration - Two appeals by way of special leave against judgment and order of HC relating to the assessment years 1947-48 and 1948-49 - Respondent/Assessee, joined the cloth business started by A in Singapore, in the name of T Company - Assessee had married A's sister - As per agreement between them, assessee was engaged as a manager for a period of three years from September 23, 1940 - Remuneration was to be a fixed salary at the rate of $ 18 per month and a commission of 10% on the net profits of the business, to be calculated at the end of the said three years provided with free board and lodging - On same day, a power of attorney was executed by A in favour of assessee, enabling him to carry on the business of a cloth and general merchant, being carried on - Later A sold the said business and goodwill thereof, with the other assets of the business, at the price of $ 4,441.78 -Income-tax Officer estimated the remittance at Rs.1, 58, 501 and found that enough profits out of the current year Rs.17, 461, and out of the un assessed but unremitted profits of earlier years Rs. 1,41,040 existed - Assessee appealed to Assistant Commissioner against both the orders who inturn observed that all credits in A's account could not be treated as unexplained and suspicious - Estimated the remittance at Rs. 30, 000, instead of the sum of Rs. 61 538 adopted by the Income-tax Officer - Regarding the assessment year 1948-49, Appellate Assistant Commissioner estimated the income of the assessee from business in Singapore - Assessee filed two appeals to the Income-tax Appellate Tribunal - Whether Tribunal was right in holding that 87,500 dollars was the income of assessee - Held, that Tribunal entitled to hold that if the accounts of shop exist, and if they had been produced, it would have shown that assessee made not 10% during the whole period of five years for which the business of old T and Company was carried on, but much more - A lend 87,500 dollars without even a Pronote and seeing it all being sent to Pudukkottai in the assessee's name - Once it was held that 86, 500 dollars was the income of the assessee, it was not necessary for the revenue to locate its exact source - No evidence in support of the finding of the Tribunal With great respect, HC did not appreciate the findings of the Tribunal, recorded in the supplementary statement - Tribunal accepted the earlier findings of the court, recorded in the order - No other conclusion except that 87,500 dollars was the income of the assessee was possible - Finding of Tribunal that 87,500 dollars represented the income of the assessee, no other issue survives - Appeal allowed.
