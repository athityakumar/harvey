 B. Rajagopala Naidu v State Transport Appellate Tribunal and Others

Supreme Court of India

05 March 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, S.M. Sikri, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 417; AIR 1964 SC 1573; 1964 (2) AnWR 131; 1964 (2) MLJ(SC) 131; 1964 (2) SCJ 570; [1964] 7 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Keywords: Writ Proceedings

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1939, s. 43-A - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Validity of Government Order no. 1298 challenged on ground of violation of s. 43 A of Act - Held, on a fair and reasonable construction of s. 43A, it ought to be held that said section authorises State Govt. to issue orders and directions of a general character only in respect of administrative matters which fall to be dealt with by State Transport Authority or Regional Transport Authority under relevant provisions of Act in their administrative capacity - S. 43A itself provides that effect shall be given to orders issued under it, and so, if an order issued u/s. 43A itself were to prescribe that it shall be followed, it will have to be followed by Tribunal and no exception can be made in that behalf - Impugned order is outside purview of s. 43A in as much as it purports to give directions in respect of matters which have been entrusted to Tribunals constituted under Act and which have to be dealt with by these Tribunals in a quasi-judicial manner - Appeal allowed.
