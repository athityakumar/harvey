Thurukanni Pompiah and Another v State of Mysore

Supreme Court of India

25 September 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Bachawat, A.K. Sarkar, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 450; AIR 1965 SC 939; 1965 CRLJ 31; 1965 (2) SCJ 157

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 32(1) - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34 and 326 - Dying declaration - Conviction u/s 326/34 -Whether conviction of appellant can be based on dying declaration - Truthful and reliable dying declaration may form sole basis of conviction, even though it is not corroborated - Reliability of declaration should be subjected to close scrutiny, considering that it was made in absence of accused who had no opportunity to test its veracity by cross-examination - If court finds declaration not wholly reliable and material and integral portion of deceased's version of entire occurrence is untrue, court may, in all circumstances of case, consider it unsafe to convict accused on basis of declaration alone without further corroboration - Held, material and integral portion of deceased's version of entire occurrence is unreliable - Truthfulness of dying declarations as whole is not free from doubt - Prosecution case does not inspire confidence, eye-witnesses found to be unreliable - Dying declarations suffer from infirmity and are not reliable by themselves and cannot safely form basis of conviction of appellants without further corroboration - Convictions and sentences of appellants set aside - Appeal allowed.
