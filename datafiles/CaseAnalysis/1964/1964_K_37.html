Kettlewell Bullen and Company v Commissioner of Income Tax, Calcutta

Supreme Court of India

01 May 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, K. Subba Rao, S.M. Sikri

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 352; AIR 1965 SC 65; [1964] 53 ITR 261; 1964 (2) SCJ 381; [1964] 8 S.C.R. 93

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Compensation On Termination

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s.66(1) - Termination of agency - Compensation for loss of office - Taxable income - Capital receipt - Appellant was a public limited company - By an agreement dt.1-5-1925 F/Company appointed appellant its managing agent upon certain terms and conditions - Appellant was to receive as managing agent remuneration at Rs.3,000 per month - But by virtue of s.87-A(2) of 1913 Act appointment of appellant would expire on 14-1-1957 - Appellant advanced Rs.12,50,000 to Company on security of stocks, raw materials and manufactured goods of that company - Appellant entered into an agreement with M&Co - M&Co agreed to procure that Company would pay to appellant Rs.3,50,000 and that M&Co would reimburse company for payment, it being anticipated that they would in line course be appointed managing agents of company - Appellant tendered its resignation with effect from 1-7-1952 in pursuance of terms of agreement and M&Co were appointed as managing agent of company - Sum of Rs.3,50,000 received by appellant from company was credited in profit and loss account of appellant on account of compensation for loss of office - In arriving at net profit in return for income Tax for year 1953-54 that amount was deleted - Income Tax Officer(IO) included that amount in appellant's taxable income - Appellate Assistant Commissioner observed that Rs.3,50,000 received by appellant as compensation for surrendering managing agency was a capital receipt - Appellate Tribunal confirmed order of Appellate Assistant Commissioner - Tribunal referred question u/s.66(1) of 1922 Act to HC that whether Rs.3,50,000 received by assessee to relinquish managing agency was a revenue receipt assessable under Act - HC answered question in affirmative - Hence instant appeal - Whether payment of compensation received by appellant for termination of an agency was a capital or a revenue receipt - Held, unable to agree with HC that managing agency of Company was an asset of character of stock in trade of company - No evidence available that Company was formed for purpose of acquiring and selling managing agencies and making profit by those transactions of sale and purchase - Amount not received by assessee as price of any capital assets sold or surrendered or destroyed, but amount was simply received by assessee in course of its going distributing agency business and it was an income receipt - Assessee was paid to compensate him for loss of a capital asset - Transaction was not in nature of a trading transaction, but assessee parted with an asset of an enduring value - Unable to agree with HC that amount received by appellant was in nature of a revenue receipt - Record the answer on question submitted by Tribunal in negative - Appeal allowed.
