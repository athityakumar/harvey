Kumaranand v Brij Mohan Lal

Supreme Court of India

24 August 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 169; AIR 1965 SC 628; [1965] 1 S.C.R. 116

Case Digest

Subject: Election; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Election - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 119 A and 123(4) - Whether in case of failure to comply with requirements of s. 119 A High Court had jurisdiction to rectify mistake committed in making deposit for costs? - Held, when there is default in complying with requirement, it is for court in each case to consider whether it will exercise its discretion to proceed with appeal after rectifying mistake committed or it will decline to proceed with appeal - Court is by statute not obliged to dismiss appeal for failure to comply with requirements of s. 119 A ; it has therefore jurisdiction having regard to circumstances, either to permit rectification of mistake, or to decline to proceed with appeal which does not comply with statutory requirements - There was defective presentation of appeal which contributed to irregularity of procedure adopted by appellant - Appeal allowed.
