 Gurdev Singh Sidhu v State of Punjab and Another

Supreme Court of India

01 April 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Mohammad Hidayatullah, K.C. Das Gupta, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 327; AIR 1964 SC 1585; 1964 CRLJ 481; 1965 (10) FLR 231; 1965 (1) LLJ 323; [1964] 7 S.C.R. 587

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Public Servants (Inquiries) Act, 1850

Summary: Constitution - Service - Pepsu Service Regulations, Vol 1, art. 9.1, 2nd Proviso, Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 32, 226 and 311(2) - (A) Pepsu Service Regulations - (A) Whether proviso providing for compulsory retirement after 10 years service is valid?- Held, it is invalid - For efficient administration of State, it is absolutely essential that permanent public servants should enjoy a sense of security of tenure - Safeguard which art. 311 (2) affords to permanent public servants - Intended to dismiss, remove or reduce them in rank, a reasonable opportunity should be given to them of showing cause against action proposed to be taken in regard to them - Retirement is exception - It might be on reaching age of superannuation or a reasonable period prescribed - Reserving power to State to compulsorily retire a permanent public servant, a rule is framed prescribing a proper age of superannuation, and another rule is added giving power to State to compulsorily retire a permanent public servant at the end of 10 years of his service - That cannot be treated as falling outside art. 311 (2) - Termination of service of a permanent public servant under such a rule, though called compulsory retirement - Is in substance, removal under art. 311 (2) of Constitution - So provision is invalid - (B) Whether Govt. servant can challenge validity of a service rule in Supreme Court u/art. 32? - Further held, here proper remedy is available through writ petition to HC u/art. 226 and so writ petition directly to Supreme Court is not competent - Petition allowed.
