Babbar Sewing Machine Co. v Trilok Nath Mahajan

Supreme Court of India

07 August 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

A.P. Sen, Jaswant Singh, D.A. Desai

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 509; (1978) 4 SCC 188; AIR 1978 SC 1436; 1978 (4) ALR 747; [1979] 1 S.C.R. 57

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, O. 11, r. 21, s. 151 - Striking out of defense - Legality - Whether trial court was justified in striking out defense of defendant under O. 11, r. 21, CPC, 1908? - Held, the principle governing the court's exercise of its discretion under O. 11, r. 21 is that it is only when the default is willful and as a last resort that the court should dismiss the suit or strike out the defense, when the party is guilty of such contumacious conduct or there is a willful attempt to disregard the order of court that the trial of the suit is arrested - Applying this test, it is quite clear that there was no willful default on the part of the defendant of the court's order under O. 11, r. 18 (2) for the production of documents for inspection, and consequently, the order passed by the trial court on 23-05-1967, striking out the defense of the defendant must be vacated, and the trial must proceed afresh from the stage where the defendant was not permitted to participate - Therefore, the order passed by the trial court dated 23-05-1967 striking out the defense of the defendant under O. 11, r. 21 r/w. s. 151 of the CPC, and its subsequent order dated 21-07-1967 are both set aside and it is directed to proceed with the trial according to law - Appeal allowed.
