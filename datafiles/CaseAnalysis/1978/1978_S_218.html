Sandhya Rani Sarkar v Sudha Rani Debi and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 February 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

D.A. Desai, M. Hameedullah Beg

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 329; (1978) 2 SCC 116; AIR 1978 SC 537; [1978] 2 S.C.R. 839

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Limitation Act, 1963, 5 - Agreement of Sale - Specific Performance - Breach of Contract - Disentitlement - Appellant entered into contract of agreement of sale with respondent/vendor for sale of property - Respondent received earnest money from appellant/intending purchaser, who filed a suit for specific performance of contract, as she was ready and willing to perform her part of contract but, respondent No. 1 had not completed transaction - Trial Court directed respondent No. 1 to execute and register sale deed in favor of appellant - Respondent preferred appeal before HC, and an application u/s. 5 of Act, 1963 - Hence instant appeal - Whether relief of specific performance of contract for purchase of immoveable property could be granted to appellant - Held, Court opined that appellant was not entitled to decree of specific performance of contract, as stages in which contract was to be completed clearly demarcated that transaction was completed - Respondent/vendor put appellant in possession of substantial portion of property even when she had not paid a major part of consideration, was clear evidence which shows contract was successfully completed within stipulated time - Both Courts below concurrently found that, respondent never agreed for t land to be sold to appellant, but she persisted respondent to perform an agreement which was not entered between them -Therefore, appellant was disentitled to decree for specific performance - Appeal dismissed.
