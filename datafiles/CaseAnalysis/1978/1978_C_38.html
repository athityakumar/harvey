Gestetner Duplicators Private Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax, West Bengal

Supreme Court of India

14 December 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

V.D. Tulzapurkar, P.N. Bhagwati, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 262; (1979) SCC (L&S) 183; (1979) 2 SCC 354; AIR 1979 SC 607; 1979 (8) CTR 371; 1979 (38) FLR 315; [1979] 117 ITR 1; [1979] 2 S.C.R. 788; 1979 TAXLR 451; [1979] 1 TAXMAN 1

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Recognition, Recognised Provident Fund

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, rr. 2(h), 4(C), Part A, sch. IV, ss. 36, (1)(iv) - Whether expression 'salary' as defined in r. 2(h) of Part A of Fourth Schedule to Act includes commission payable by an assessee to his or its employees in terms of their contracts of employment - Held, r. 4 (b) was to effect that contribution of an employee in any year shall be a definite proportion of his 'salary' for that year and shall be deducted by employer from employee's 'salary' in that proportion at each periodical payment of such salary in that year, and credited to employee's individual account in Fund - It appears that conceptually 'salary' and 'wages' connote one and same thing, namely, remuneration or payment for work done or services rendered - Under contracts of employment assessee has been paying and did pay during previous years relevant to three assessment years to its salesmen, in addition to fixed monthly salary, commission at a fixed percentage of turnover achieved by each salesman, rate of percentage varying according to class of article sold and category to which each salesman belonged - It is clear that entire remuneration so determined clearly partakes of character of salary - Appeal allowed.
