 Madan Mohan Pathak and Another v Union Of India & Ors. Etc

Supreme Court of India

21 February 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Bhagwati, Y.V. Chandrachud, V.R. Krishna Iyer, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, P.N. Shingal, D.A. Desai, M. Hameedullah Beg

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 152; (1978) 2 SCC 50; (1978) SCC (L&S) 103; AIR 1978 SC 803; 1978 LabIC 612; 1978 (1) LLJ 406; 1978 (1) LLN 516; [1978] 3 S.C.R. 334

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service

Keywords: Controlled, Requisitioning Of Property, Life Insurance Corporation (Modification of Settlements) Act, 1976, Constitution (Fourth Amendment) Act, 1955, Life Insurance Corporation (Staff) Regulations, 1960, Life Insurance Corporation Modification Of Settlement Act 1976, Gujarat Imposition Of Taxes By Municipalities (Validation) Act, 1963, Payment Of Bonus (Amendment) Act, 1976, Constitution (Fifth Amendment) Act,1955

Summary: Service - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 31(2), 31(2A) - Life Insurance Corporation (Modification of Settlements) Act, 1976 - Annual cash bonus - Extinguishment of debts - Compensation - Validity - Respondents/ Life Insurance Corporation (LIC) issued a circular stating that payment of bonus for year 1975-76 was to be withheld - Petitioner/employees filed writ petitions challenging impugned circular passed by respondents - HC passed an order recognising right of petitioners to payment of bonus for year 1975-76 which had become payable along with salary and ordered that it should be paid to employees -Modification of the 1976 Act was enacted by Parliament denying to petitioners right which recognised by settlements approved by Central Govt. and acted on by actual payment of bonus to employees and converted into right under decision of HC - Hence, instant writ petitions - Whether right of Class III and Class IV employees to 'annual cash bonus' for years 1975 to 1976 and 1976 to 1977 under Settlement was property so as to attract inhibition of art. 31(2) of Constitution - Held, there was no doubt that in instant case impugned Act extinguished or put an end to debts due and owing from LIC to Class III and Class IV employees - However, that was direct effect of the impugned Act and it could therefore, be legitimately said that in substance the impugned Act provided for extinguishment of those debts - Hence, direct effect of the impugned Act was to transfer ownership of debts due and owing to Class III and Class IV employees in respect of annual cash bonus to LIC - Since, LIC was a corporation owned by State, the impugned Act was a law providing for compulsory acquisition of those debts by State within meaning of art. 31(2A) of Constitution - Thus, the impugned Act should be held to be violative of art. 31(2) of Constitution since it did not provide for payment of any compensation at all for compulsory acquisition of those debts - Petitions allowed.
