Moti Ram and Others v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

24 August 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

V.R. Krishna Iyer, D.A. Desai

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 194; (1978) 4 SCC 47; (1978) SCC (Cr) 485; AIR 1978 SC 1594; 1978 CRLJ 1703; [1979] 1 S.C.R. 335; 1978 UJ 747

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Debt Laws

Summary: Practice & Procedure -Indian Limitation Act, 1908- Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 436, 437 and 441 - Appellant a poor man, from Madhya Pradesh - Appeal pending in Supreme Court - Obtained an order for bail in his favour 'to satisfaction of Chief Judicial Magistrate' not spelling out details of bail - Magistrate ordered surety in a sum of Rs. 10,000/- Further demanded sureties from his own district - Refused to accept suretyship of brother for reason that his assets were in another district - Whether magistrate was justified in akin for surety from same district and specified amount of money? - Bearing in mind need for liberal interpretation in areas of social justice, individual freedom and indigent's rights, bail covers both release on one's own bond, with or without sureties - When sureties should be demanded and what sum should be insisted on are dependent on variables -Held, it was not within power of Court to reject a surety because he or his estate was situated in a a different district or State - There was no law prescribing geographical discrimination implicit in asking for sureties from court district - So Demand by Magistrate, of sureties from his own district, was discriminatory and illegal - Even so, poor men, young persons, infirm individuals and women are weak categories, and courts should be liberal in releasing them on their own recognisance put whatever reasonable conditions they may - Order accordingly.
