Sant Ram v Rajinder Lal and Others

Supreme Court of India

22 September 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

V.R. Krishna Iyer, D.A. Desai, A.P. Sen

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 89; (1979) 2 SCC 274; AIR 1978 SC 1601; 1979 (1) RCJ 13; 1978 (2) RCR(Rent) 601; 1978 (2) RentLR 621; [1979] 1 S.C.R. 900; 1978 UJ 858

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Keywords: East Punjab Urban Rent Restrictions Act, 1949

Summary: Rent Control - East Punjab Urban Rent Restriction Act, 1949, s. 13 (2) (ii) (b) - Purpose of the lease - Determination of - Whether using demised premised for anything apart from commercial use as required amounts to using premise for anything other than that for which it was leased?- Held, law, not being an abstraction but a pragmatic exercise, legal inference to be drawn from a lease deed is conditional by prevailing circumstances intention of parties from which the purpose of lease is to be garnered from social milieu - It is difficult to hold, especially when lease has not spelt it out precisely, that purpose was exclusively commercial and incompatible with any residential use, even of a portion - Two rules for interpreting deeds and statutes: 1.In drafting it is not enough to gain a degree of precision which a person reading in good faith can understand, but it is necessary to attain if possible to a degree of precision which a person reading in bad faith cannot misunderstand; 2.The second one is more important for Third World countries Statutory construction, so long as law is at service of life, cannot be divorced from social setting - The welfare legislation like one in instant case must be interpreted in a Third World perspective - It is impossible to hold that if a tenant who takes out petty premises for carrying on a small trade also stays in rear portion, cooks and eats, he so disastrously perverts purpose of lease - A different 'purpose' in context is not minor variations but majuscule in mode of enjoyment - Appeal allowed.
