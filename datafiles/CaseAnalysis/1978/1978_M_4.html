 State of Kerala and another v M. K. Krishnan Nair & Ors.Andk. Sukumaran Nair & Anr.V.M.

Supreme Court of India

14 February 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

V.D. Tulzapurkar, P.N. Bhagwati, V.R. Krishna Iyer, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, P.N. Shingal, Jaswant Singh, M. Hameedullah Beg

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 134; (1978) SCC (L&S) 76; (1978) 1 SCC 552; AIR 1978 SC 747; 1978 LabIC 723; [1978] 2 S.C.R. 864; 1978 (1) SLR 499

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service

Keywords: Sources Of Recruitment, Kerala State Higher Judicial Service Rules 1961, Kerala Civil Judicial Services Rules 1973

Summary: Constitution - Service - Bifurcation of State Judicial Service into two wings i.e Civil and Criminal - Separate Rules governing recruitment and conditions of service (A) Whether bifurcation violates art. 14 and 16 of Constitution - (B) Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 309 and 234 - Ambit of powers of State Govt. - (C) Interpretation of Statutes -Rule of construction for scenario where two constructions are possible - Held, do not violate art. 14 and 16 -It is open to State Govt. to constitute as many cadres in any particular service as it may choose according to administrative convenience and expediency - Constructions which leads to unconstitutionality must be avoided and other adopted, even if straining of language is necessary - Appeals Allowed.
