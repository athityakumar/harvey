State Of Kerala v Mathew ( M. M. ) And Anr

Supreme Court of India

18 August 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

Jaswant Singh, P.S. Kailasam

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 193; (1978) 4 SCC 65; (1978) SCC (Cr) 503; AIR 1978 SC 1571; 1978 CRLJ 1690; 1978 CrLR(SC) 410; [1979] 1 S.C.R. 264; 1978 (42) STC 348; 1978 UJ 701

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Sales Tax

Summary: Criminal - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 73 - Kerala General Sales Tax Act, 1963, s. 46 - Respondents filed returns for turnover well below actual turnover - Inspection conducted by State authorities - Charges u/s. 46(1)(a) of Act for submission of untrue returns, u/s. 46(1)(c) of Act for failure to keep true and complete accounts and u/s. 6(2)(c) of Act for fraudulent evasion of tax - Acquitted - Present appeal - To establish charges against respondents, it was essential for prosecution to establish that secret books of account related to business transactions carried on by respondents and none - Held, offences against respondents have not been proved beyond reasonable doubt - No evidence on record to prove that secret books of account seized were recovered from business premises of respondents or was in their exclusive possession and control - No evidence to prove that these books of accounts were maintained by respondents - Appeal dismissed.
