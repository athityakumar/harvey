Bega Begum and Others v Abdul Ahad Khan and Others

Supreme Court of India

06 October 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, P.N. Shingal

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 277; (1979) 1 SCC 273; AIR 1979 SC 272; 1979 (1) RCJ 344; 1979 (1) RCR(Rent) 170; 1979 (1) RentLR 93; [1979] 2 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - Civil Procedure - Jammu & Kashmir Houses and Shops Rent Control Act, 1966, ss. 2(3), 11, 11(h) - Expiry of Lease - Demand of Possession - Personal Necessity - Genuine Need - Suit property/four-storey building belonging to ancestor of appellants was leased to respondents by registered lease deed for period of time for purpose of running hotel - On expiry of period of lease, appellants demanded possession of building from respondents - In spite of several notices, respondents failed to give possession and appellant filed suit - Trial Court dismissed appellant's suit - On appeal before HC, ordered appellants had not proved their personal necessity and dismissed same - Hence instant appeal - Whether appellants/landlords need premises for their use or occupation - Held, Court disagreed with finding of HC, as appellants proved their requirement for house for starting hotel business was both genuine and reasonable, as their scanty income was not sufficient to maintain them or to afford them decent or comfortable living - HC not discussed distinction between desire and need and its findings established that appellants had genuine requirement to possess house property - Being owners of house appellants could not be denied eviction and compelled to live below poverty line merely to enable respondents to carry their flourishing hotel business, at cost of appellants - Respondents lease was only for limited period, but was extended for long period, thus no equity in favor of respondents for continuing in possession further - Respondents had not proved that they will not be able to get any accommodation anywhere in city to set up hotel - Judgment and decree for ejectment of respondents of HC were set aside - Appeal allowed.
