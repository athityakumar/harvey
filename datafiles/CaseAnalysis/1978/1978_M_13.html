State of Gujarat and Another v Maharaj Shri Amarshinhji Himatsinhji

Supreme Court of India

14 April 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

V.D. Tulzapurkar, V.R. Krishna Iyer, Jaswant Singh

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 169; (1978) 2 SCC 619; AIR 1978 SC 1167; 1978 (19) GLR 811; [1978] 3 S.C.R. 675; 1978 UJ 422

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Bombay Merged Territories and Areas (Jagirs Abolition) Act, 1953, s. 11 - Daljitgarh Jagir - Enquiry order - Quashing of - Respondent was granted Daljitgarh Jagir comprising of few villages for maintenance - According to respondent these grants read together he was given full proprietary rights in soil of said villages, it was proprietary Jagir that was granted to him - Admittedly, on coming into force of 1953 Act with effect from respondent's Daljitgarh Jagir stood abolished and all his rights in Jagir villages, save as expressly provided by or under the Act, were extinguished and respondent became entitled to compensation u/s. 11 of the Act - Competent authority/collector held an inquiry into question whether respondent's Jigir was proprietary or non-proprietary u/s. 2 (4) (i) of the Act - Competent authority by its order held that Daljitgarh Jagir of respondent was proprietary jagir - Aggrieved by order passed by Collector respondent preferred writ petition before HC for quashing the order - HC negatived contentions urged by appellants and quashed the order passed by collector - Hence instant appeal challenging order passed by HC - Whether HC was right in quashing inquiry initiated by Collector u/s. 37(2) of the Code - Held, it was clear that by these two applications respondent had not put forward any claim as such to excavating gravel and stones for first time' but, had merely requested making of appropriate entry with regard to his said right which had already been recognized by Govt. previously - There was no occasion for Collector to initiate inquiry u/s. 37(2) of the Code - In fact, he had no jurisdiction to do so, condition precedent not being satisfied - Moreover, regard to statement made by respondent before us it would be unfair to subject respondent to further inquiry u/s. 37(2) of the Code - Respondent categorically stated before SC that he was confining his right to excavating only one type of mineral product, namely, gravel and stones, and that too from only two villages, comprised in his Jagir, in regard to which Mamlatdar's order was quite clear and, therefore, he urged that further inquiry u/s. 37(2) of the Code into that very right was misconceived and uncalled for - SC found considerable force in this contention - Besides, while determining proprietary nature of grant under s. 2(4)(1) of the Act competent authority had, on evidence led before it, alluded among others to respondent's right to excavate and sell gravel and stones and enjoyment thereof by respondent - It would be fair and proper that respondent was not subjected to further inquiry u/s. 37(2) of Code so far as his right to excavating gravel and stones from two villages was concerned - If and when he prefers claim to this particular mineral product from other villages comprised in his grant or to other mines or mineral products in all villages including this two villages inquiry into such claim u/s. 37(2) could be held, but even decision at such inquiry would be subject to adjudication by Civil Court in appropriate proceedings, for final pronouncement on such rights must, as was clear from scheme of Bombay Land Revenue Code, always rest with Civil Court - HC was right in its final conclusion whereby it had quashed inquiry initiated by Collector u/s. 37(2) of the Code and issued necessary injunction prayed for by respondent - Appeal dismissed.
