S. P. Kohli, Civil Surgeon, Ferozepur v High Court Of Punjab & Haryana

Supreme Court of India

12 September 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

Jaswant Singh, P.S. Kailasam, A.D. Koshal

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 115; (1979) 1 SCC 212; (1979) SCC (Cr) 252; 1978 (15) ACC 381; AIR 1978 SC 1753; 1978 CRLJ 1804; 1978 CrLR(SC) 417; 1979 MLJ 378; [1979] 1 S.C.R. 722; 1978 UJ 870

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: IPC, 1860, ss. 191, 193 and 376 - (A) Offence of rape - Delay in examination of smegma - Effect - (B) Offence of perjury - Initiation of proceedings - Matter to be considered - High Court tried to find out whether there was no prima facie case against the appellant - (C) Perjury - Show cause notice - Contents of - Held, it is well known in the medical world that the examination of smegma loses all importance after 24 hours of the performance of the sexual intercourse - Held, HC was in error as instead of trying to find out whether there was a prima facie case justifying the issue of notice to the appellant, it adopted a negative approach and tried to find out whether there was no prima facie case against the appellant - Portions of the witness's statement in regard to which the accused has, in the opinion of the Court, perjured himself should be specifically set out in or form annexure to the notice issued to the accused so that he is in a position to furnish an adequate and proper reply in regard thereto and be able to meet the charge - Order accordingly.
