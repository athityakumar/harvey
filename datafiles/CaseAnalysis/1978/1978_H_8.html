U.P. State Electricity Board v Hari Shanker Jain and Others

Supreme Court of India

28 August 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy, V.R. Krishna Iyer, D.A. Desai

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 290; (1978) 4 SCC 16; (1978) SCC (L&S) 481; AIR 1978 SC 65; 1978 (37) FLR 280; 1978 LabIC 1657; 1978 (2) LLJ 399; 1978 (2) LLN 514; [1979] 1 S.C.R. 355; 1978 UJ 659

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Civil Services (Classification, Control and Appeal) Rules, Industrial Employment (Standing Order ) Act, 1946, Indian Railway Establishment Code, 1951

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Indian Railway Establishment Code, 1951 - Industrial Employment (Standing Orders) Act, 1946, ss. 5 and 13 B - Indian Electricity Act, 1910, s. 79 (C) - Civil Services (Classification, Control and Appeal) Rules - Superannuation - Determination u/s. 13 B - Age of 58 as date of compulsory retirement - Validity - Industrial Employment (Standing orders) Act is a special law in regard to matters enumerated in schedule - Regulations made by Electricity Board with respect to any of those matters are of no effect unless such regulations are either notified by Govt. u/s. 13-B or certified by certifying officer u/s. 5 of Industrial Employment (Standing orders) Act - Held, in present case regulation made by Board with regard to age of superannuation having been duly notified by Govt., regulation shall have effect under Industrial Employment (Standing orders) Act - Respondents were therefore, properly retired when they attained age of 58 years - Appeal allowed.
