 Chief Justice Of A.P. & Anr v L.V.A. Dikshitulu & Ors

Supreme Court of India

12 September 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Sarkaria, Y.V. Chandrachud, N.L. Untwalia, A.D. Koshal, A.P. Sen

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 498; (1979) 2 SCC 34; (1979) SCC (L&S) 99; AIR 1979 SC 193; 1978 LabIC 1672; [1979] 1 S.C.R. 26

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Constitutional Safeguards

Summary: Constitution - Service - Judiciary - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 371 D cls. 3 and 4, 226, 229 and 235 - Andhra Pradesh High Court Service Rules, r. 19 r/w art. 229 of Constitution - Andhra Pradesh Liberalised Pension Rules, 1961, r. 3(2)(a) - Hyderabad Civil Service Rules, r. 292 - Andhra Pradesh Government Servants Premature Retirement Rules, 1975, r. 2(1) - Andhra Pradesh Administrative Tribunal Order, 1975 (made by President) u/art. 371 D - Special provisions with respect to State of Andhra Pradesh as to public employment and education u/art. 371 D - (A) What is interpretation, scope and impact of art.371-D on arts. 226, 229 and 235 of Constitution? - Respondent No. 1 was deputy registrar of HC at relevant time when he was sought to be retired prematurely as per Premature Retirement Rules after reviewing service records and meeting requirements contained therefor under Rules, by a Committee constituted by HC under order of Chief Justice of said HC - Order passed by acting Chief Justice u/art. 229 and under relevant service Rules - Review petition against such order, rejected by HC in its administrative jurisdiction - Writ petition filed u/art. 226 on judicial side of HC against order - Dismissed on preliminary ground of non-maintainability, reason assigned being that jurisdiction of HC which was earlier exercised u/art. 226 to correct orders of Chief Justice on administrative side with regard to conditions of service of officers of HC, now stands vested in Administrative Tribunal under cl. 6(1) of Administrative Tribunal Order passed u/art. 371 D - Tribunal quashed such order of premature retirement - (i) Whether officers and servants of Andhra Pradesh HC and persons holding posts in judicial service of State, including 'District Judges' are subject to jurisdiction of Administrative Tribunal Order, 1975 made by President in exercise of his powers under cls. (3) and (4) of art.371-D? - Chap. V and VI of Constitution of India, 1953 arts. 229 and 235 looked into, debated and precedents deliberated upon - Position on art. 235 - -control over subordinate judiciary vested in HC under article is exclusive in nature, comprehensive in extent and effective in operation - Precedents summed up - Scheme of Chapters V and VI in Part VI epitomised in arts. 229 and 235, had been assiduously designed by founding fathers to insure independence of HC and subordinate judiciary - Historical background of provisions of art. 371 D and reasons for it, looked into and deliberated upon - Principles of interpretation of constitutional provisions, deliberated upon - On reasons of inserting art. 371 D Court summed up as to that evil which was sought to be remedied, (viz, inequitable opportunities and facilities for people belonging to different parts of State of AP in matters of public employment and education) had no causal nexus, whatever, with independence of HC and subordinate judiciary which founding fathers had with solemn concern vouchsafed in arts. 229 and 235, nor did public agitation which led to enactment of art. 371-D make any grievance against basic scheme of chap. V and VI in part VI of Constitution - On principles of interpretation Court summed up as to that in our quest for true intention of Parliament, therefore, we must eschew this wide literal interpretation which will defeat or render otiose scheme of chap. IV and V, part VI particularised in arts. 229 and 235, and instead, choose alternative interpretation according to which members of HC staff and subordinate judiciary will not fall within purview of phrase 'civil services of State' - Such a restricted construction will ensure smooth working of Constitution and harmony amongst its various provisions - (i) Held, no, nothing in order of President constituting Administrative Tribunal confers jurisdiction on Tribunal to entertain, deal with or decide representation by a member of staff of HC or of Subordinate Judiciary - Interpretation, scope and impact of art. 371 D on arts. 226, 229 and 235 of Constitution thus answered in above terms - Impugned order of Administrative Tribunal held to be a nullity being without jurisdiction - Appeal allowed.
