Ram Chander Singh Sagar And Anr v State Of Tamil Nadu And Anr

Supreme Court of India

23 January 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

V.R. Krishna Iyer, Jaswant Singh

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 123; (1978) 2 SCC 35; (1978) SCC (Cr) 171; AIR 1978 SC 475; 1978 CRLJ 640; 1978 CRLR 94; 1978 CrLR(SC) 94; 1978 MLJ 341; [1978] 2 S.C.R. 604

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s.406 - Transfer of investigations - Sustainability - Whether SC has power u/s.406 of CrPC to transfer investigations from one police station to another in country.

Held, CrPC clothes this Court with power u/s. 406 of CrPC to transfer a case or appeal from one HC or a Court subordinate to one HC to another HC or to a Court subordinate thereto. But it does not clothe this Court with power to transfer investigations from one police station to another in country simply because first information or a remand report is forwarded to a Court. There is as yet no case pending before any Court as has been made clear in counter affidavit of State. In light of this counter affidavit, nothing can be done except to dismiss this petition. If petitioners are being directed to appeal in a far-off Court during investigatory stage it is for them to move that Court for appropriate orders so that they may not be tormented by long travel or otherwise teased by judicial process. If justice is denied there are other redresses, not u/s. 406 of CrPC, though it is unfortunate that petitioners have not chosen to move that Court to be absolved from appearance until necessitated by circumstances or progress of investigation. To come to this Court directly seeking an order of transfer is travelling wrong street. If petitioner no.2 is ailing, as is represented, and this fact is brought to notice of Court which has directed her appearance, just orders will be passed in case there is veracity behind representation. Petition dismissed.

Ratio - Courts should use their processes to purpose of advancing justice, not to harass parties.
