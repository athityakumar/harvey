 Oriental Gas Company Limited and Others v State of West Bengal

Supreme Court of India

12 September 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy, A.P. Sen, Y.V. Chandrachud, R.S. Sarkaria, N.L. Untwalia

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 117; (1979) 1 SCC 171; AIR 1979 SC 248; [1979] 1 S.C.R. 617

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Constitution; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Debt Laws, Gilt-Edged Securities

Summary: Corporate - Constitution (Fifth Amendment) Act, 1955 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 31 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Oriental Gas Company Act, 1960 - Company acquisition by the State - Valuation of the assets - (A)'Compensation' Meaning of - Held, the word 'compensation' was not qualified by any adjective such as just or fair - In Bela Banerjee's case, Supreme Court introduced the concept of a 'just equivalent' - Compensation were to be subjected to the 'basic requirement of full indemnification of the expropriated owner' - What was to be given was full compensation on the basis of the market value of the property acquired - (B) Amendment of art. 31 'Effect of' - Adequacy of the compensation provided by the law - non-justiciable - (C) Public utility undertaking - Valuation of Property - Mode of - Held, tangible and intangible property need not be valued separately - Treat them as indivisible - Capitalisation of net profit as one of the recognised principles of valuation although it is not advantageous to the owner of undertaking - (D) Valuation of land near the gas plant - Claim of enhancement of compensation - Sustainability - Held, nothing in the petition shows the land purchased by way of investment - Nor capable of being sold independently - No one come forward to purchase land next to gas works - Other factors make the land unsaleable - Caused to depreciate the value of land - (E) Amendment of Writ Petition - For incorporating the fact that land purchased by way of investment - Admissibility - Held, acquisition was made in 1962 - Impugned Act was passed in 1970 - Writ Petition was filed in 1971 and pending for seven years - If there was any substance in the present assertion, the petitioner would surely have mentioned it prominently in the Writ Petition - Permitting amendment at this stage is unjustifiable - (F) Method of valuation - Choice of five years immediately preceding the take over of the management and control of the Company - Arbitrariness - Held, valuation is not on the basis of value at the date of take over but the average of hypothetical anterior date - Legislature did not take the average of three years but took five years - No arbitrariness in valuation - Petition dismissed.
