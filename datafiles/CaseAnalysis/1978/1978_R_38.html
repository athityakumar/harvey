Syed Abdulkhader v Rami Reddy and Others

Supreme Court of India

29 November 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

D.A. Desai, P.N. Shingal

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 111; (1979) 2 SCC 601; AIR 1979 SC 553; [1979] 2 S.C.R. 424

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Indian Regulation Act 1908

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 133(1)(a) - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Indian Contract Act, 1872 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872 - Indian Regulation Act 1908 - Indian Trusts Act, 1882, s. 82 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 41 - Whether it is impermissible in law for three persons to jointly grant a Power of Attorney in favour of defendant 34? - Held, three co-principals jointly constituted an agent then unless contrary is indicated by deed of Power of Attorney, necessary inference would be that agent can act in respect of those affairs in which all co-principles are jointly interested - A General Power of Attorney is not a compulsorily registrable document - No rule or regulation was pointed out to court in support of submission that it was obligatory for Sub-Registrar to make endorsement that those who have executed deed were either personally known to him or were identified by someone known to him - There is no merit in contention and it must be rejected - Appeal dismissed.
