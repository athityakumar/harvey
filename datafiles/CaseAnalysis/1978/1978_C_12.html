G. T. Lad and Others v Chemicals and Fibres of India

Supreme Court of India

06 December 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

Jaswant Singh, V.R. Krishna Iyer, A.D. Koshal

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 5; (1979) SCC (L&S) 76; (1979) 1 SCC 590; AIR 1979 SC 582; 1979 (38) FLR 95; 1979 LabIC 290; 1979 (1) LLJ 257; 1979 (1) LLN 331; [1979] 2 S.C.R. 613; 1979 SLJ 318; 1979 UJ 225

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 33(A) - (A) What is true meaning of expression 'abandonment' of service? - Abandonment or relinquishment of service is always a question of intention, and normally, such an intention cannot be attributed to an employee without adequate evidence in that behalf - (B) Whether in circumstances of case it could be said that appellants had voluntarily abandoned service of company? - Held, appellants' absence from duty was because of their peaceful strike to enforce their demands - There is no abandonment of service on part of appellants - (C) Whether action of company in removing names of appellants from its rolls on presumption that they had abandoned service would constitute a change in conditions of service of appellants? - Held, unable to uphold impugned action of company and award under appeal which are manifestly illegal, reinstatement has been directed by Court, entire back wages must follow as a matter of course - Appeal allowed.
