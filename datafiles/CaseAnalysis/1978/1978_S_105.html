Bishan Lal Gupta v State of Haryana and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 January 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Hameedullah Beg, P.N. Bhagwati, D.A. Desai

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 46; (1978) SCC (L&S) 55; (1978) 1 SCC 202; AIR 1978 SC 363; 1978 LabIC 312; 1978 (1) LLJ 316; [1978] 2 S.C.R. 513

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 311, 311(2) and 136 - Termination of probationer - Validity of - Whether termination of services of petitioner as a probationer by an innocuously worded order is justified? - Held, no abstract proposition can be laid down that where services of a probationer are terminated without saying anything more in order of termination than that services are terminated - It can never amount to a punishment - If a probationer discharged on ground of misconduct, or inefficiency or for a similar reason without a proper enquiry and without his getting a reasonable opportunity of showing cause against his discharge, it may in a given case amount to removal from service within meaning of art. 311(2) of Constitution, 1950 - Notices issued usually mention that explanation was being called for taking action u/r. 7(2) appearing in part D of Haryana Civil Service (Judicial Branch) Rules, 1951, r/w r. 9 of Punjab Civil Services (Punishment and Appeal) Rules, 1952 - Such rules gives a clear indication to judicial officer concerned that no action to impose a punishment on him was envisaged and in case appellant cannot contend with any justification that his rights under art. 311(2) of Constitution, 1950, have been violated - It is only if patent facts disclose a serious enough infringement of law as well as indubitably damaging and undeserved consequences upon a petitioner that Court's conscience could be so moved as to induce it to interfere under art. 136 of Constitution, 1950 - On this ground alone this case could not be one in which Court can grant Special Leave to Appeal - Petition dismissed.
