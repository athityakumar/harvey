Manager, Government Branch Press v D. B. Belliappa

Supreme Court of India

30 November 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Sarkaria, A.P. Sen, V.D. Tulzapurkar

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 458; (1979) SCC (L&S) 39; (1979) 1 SCC 477; AIR 1979 SC 429; 1979 (38) FLR 337; 1979 LabIC 146; 1979 (1) LLJ 156; 1979 (1) LLN 324; [1979] 2 S.C.R. 458; 1979 SLJ 233; 1979 (1) SLR 351

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950 - Mysore Civil Services (Classification Control and Appeal) Rules, 1957 - Disciplinary proceeding - Equal opportunity - Respondent was appointed as temporary junior compositor in the Govt. Branch press - Post was non gazetted class 4 as defined in r. 5(3) cl. (4) of the 1957 Rules - It was a temporary employment and was to continue until further orders - Appellant served notice on the respondent stating that he had taken outside the press some copies of ballot papers relating to the Director's election - Respondent was required to show cause why disciplinary action be not taken against him - It was further stated in the notice that failure to comply with the notice would result in the respondent's suspension and further disciplinary action against him - An order was served on the respondent, terminating his service - Respondent submitted to appellant a representation but without success - Respondent instituted a W.P. in the HC of Mysore for quashing the order terminating his service - HC allowed the W.P. holding that the impugned order contravened the guarantee of equal treatment embodied in art. 16 of the Constitution - The impugned order by which the respondent's services were terminated was set aside - Hence this appeal - Whether HC was right in setting aside the termination order passed against the respondent by the appellant - Held, the termination of respondent's service was made arbitrarily and not on the ground of unsuitability or other reason - In the absence of any information from the appellant indicating that the respondent was marked off for discharge on the basis of an intelligible differentia having a reasonable nexus with the object of maintaining the efficiency and integrity of the public service - The impugned order suffers from the vice of unfair discrimination and is violative of arts. 14 and 16(1) of the Constitution - Appeal dismissed.
