B. J. Shelat v State of Gujarat and Another

Supreme Court of India

28 March 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

P.S. Kailasam, R.S. Sarkaria, N.L. Untwalia

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 165; (1978) 2 SCC 202; (1978) SCC (L&S) 208; AIR 1978 SC 1109; 1978 (37) FLR 157; 1978 LabIC 824; 1978 (2) LLJ 34; 1978 (2) LLN 45; [1978] 3 S.C.R. 553; 1978 SLJ 503; 1978 (2) SLR 88; 1978 UJ 303

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service; Socio-Economic

Keywords: Food Adulteration

Summary: Service - Bombay Civil Services (Conduct, Discipline and Appeal) Rules, 1932, r. 161(2)(ii), Proviso - Service Rules - Civil Services - Scope and applicability of Proviso to s. 161(2)(ii) of Rules - Permissibility of disciplinary action after retirement - Held, appointing authority has no jurisdiction to take disciplinary proceedings against a Govt. servant, Magistrate, who had effectively retired - Appeal allowed.
