Pottery Mazdoor Panchayat v The Perfect Pottery Co. Ltd. & Anr

Supreme Court of India

19 October 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.V. Chandrachud, P.S. Kailasam, A.D. Koshal

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 97; (1979) SCC (L&S) 340; (1979) 3 SCC 762; AIR 1979 SC 1356; 1978 (38) FLR 38; 1979 LabIC 827; 1983 (1) LLJ 232; 1979 (1) LLN 336; [1979] 2 S.C.R. 126; 1978 UJ 930

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Closure

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 s. 25FFF - Closure - Retrenchment - Justifiability - Respondent was engaged in manufacture of stoneware pipes and other refractory material and employed 900 workmen - Respondent took lease of mines for purpose of its factory and employed 81 workmen - Later on respondent issued notice of closure of factory and mines - Aggrieved appellants applied for initiation of conciliation proceedings to Deputy Labour Commissioner and Regional Labour Commissioner, which were failed - State Govt. referred said dispute to arbitration and Tribunal made interim award and declined to issue injunction - State Govt. made another reference before Tribunal which held that it had no jurisdiction to enquire into propriety of closure - Appellants and respondent filed writ petitions before HC against said order which dismissed petition of appellants and allowed petition filed by respondent - Hence, instant appeal - Whether Tribunal had jurisdiction to question the propriety or justification of the closure and they had jurisdiction to question retrenchment compensation.

Held, HC was right that the two Tribunals had no jurisdiction to go behind the references and inquire into the question whether the closure of business, which was in fact effected, was decided upon for reasons which were proper and justifiable. Therefore, propriety of or justification for the closure of a business, in fact truly effected and the parties have arrived at a settlement on that question under which, the respondent agrees to fix within a period of six months the retrenchment compensation payable to the retrenched workers in accordance with the provisions u/s.25FFF of the Act. Hence, respondents were entitled to set off the amounts of retrenchment compensation already paid to the workers against the amounts due to them under this settlement. Appeals dismissed.
