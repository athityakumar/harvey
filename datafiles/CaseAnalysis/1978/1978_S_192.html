Babu Singh and Others v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

31 January 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

V.R. Krishna Iyer, D.A. Desai

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 128; (1978) SCC (Cr) 133; (1978) 1 SCC 579; AIR 1978 SC 527; 1978 CRLJ 651; 1978 CrLR(SC) 86; 1978 MLJ 465; [1978] 2 S.C.R. 777

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure 1898, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Constitution -Constitution of India,1950, art. 21- Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 389 and 437 - CrPC,1898, s. 496 - s. C (Enlargement of Criminal Appellate Jurisdiction) Act, 1970, s. 2(a) - Murder case- Six appellants acquitted by Sessions judge - Appeal by State against acquittal - Conviction by High Court - Sentenced to life imprisonment - Appeal before Supreme Court - Appellants entire male members of their family and all of them in jail - If entitled to bail - (A) Whether order refusing bail on earlier application bar fresh application on later occasion? - Held,order refusing bail does not bar fresh application on later occasion giving more details, further developments and different considerations - Defence of appellants likely to be jeopardised for them being entire male members of their family and all of them in jail - (B)Whether deprivation of bail is violative of art. 21 of Constitution? - Personal liberty deprived when bail is refused, is too precious a value of our constitutional system recognised under art. 21 of Constitution of India - Subject to certain safeguards appellants entitled to bail -Order Accordingly.
