Harishankar Rastogi v Girdhari Sharma and Another

Supreme Court of India

13 March 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

V.R. Krishna Iyer

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 160; (1978) 2 SCC 165; (1978) SCC (Cr) 168; AIR 1978 SC 1019; 1978 (4) ALR 353; 1978 CRLJ 778; 1978 CrLR(SC) 161; [1978] 3 S.C.R. 493; 1978 UJ 301

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Criminal

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Advocates Act, s. 32 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 2(B) and 2(Q) - Whether private person, not an advocate, has right to argue for a party? - Held, person, not an advocate has no right to barge into court and claim to argue for a party - Person must take permission from court - Motion for permission must come from party - It is discretionary on part of court to grant permission - Antecedents, relationship, reason for requisitioning services of private person and a variety of other circumstances must be gathered before grant or refusal of permission - Petition allowed.
