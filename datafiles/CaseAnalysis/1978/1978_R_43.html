Maneka Sanjay Gandhi and Another v Rani Jethmalani

Supreme Court of India

23 November 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

V.R. Krishna Iyer, P.S. Kailasam, A.D. Koshal

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 109; (1979) 4 SCC 167; (1979) SCC (Cr) 934; AIR 1979 SC 468; 1979 CRLJ 458; 1979 MLJ 541; [1979] 2 S.C.R. 378; 1979 UJ 120

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure,1973, s. 406 - Mrs Maneka Gandhi, accused -Prosecution launched against her and others by Miss Rani Jethmalani for an offence of defamation in Court of Metropolitan Magistrate, Bombay- Transfer petition - Grounds of substantial prejudice, non-availability of competent legal service, and absence of congenial atmosphere for a fair and impartial trial - Whether grounds claimed by petitioner is sustainable for transfer of case of defamation? - Complainant has right to choose any court having jurisdiction and accused cannot dictate where case against him should be tried - Held, petitioner's case of great insecurity or molestation to point of threat to life is not accepted - Mere word of an interested party is insufficient to convince Court that she is in jeopardy or court may not be able to conduct case under conditions of detachment, neutrality or uninterrupted progress - Petition dismissed.
