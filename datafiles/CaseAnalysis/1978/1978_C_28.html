Y. L. Agarwalla and Others v Commissioner of Income Tax, Central Calcutta

Supreme Court of India

27 July 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

V.D. Tulzapurkar, P.N. Bhagwati

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 240; (1978) 3 SCC 426; AIR 1978 SC 1412; 1978 (7) CTR 124; [1978] 114 ITR 471; [1978] 3 S.C.R. 105; [1978] 3 S.C.R. 1059; 1978 TAXLR 995; 1978 UJ 584

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income tax Act, 1961 - Whether sum of Rs. 3,08,187, being share income of three minor sons from firm of M-s Grand Smithy Works for period from 19-12-1967, to 31-08-1968, is liable to be assessed as income of HUF, M-s Y L Agarwalla and Co., for assessment year 1969-70? - Held, admittedly, deceased, represented HUF as its karta in firm and his share of 36% in profits of firm was always assessed as income of HUF - On his death, family continued to be joint, and as per cl. 13 of partnership deed dated 20-09-1961, heirs of deceased were given option of joining partnership firm - Three minor sons were admitted to benefits of partnership, each one getting 14% share in profits and a new deed of partnership dated 11-01-1968, was executed by surviving partners having retrospective effect as from 19-12-1967 - Widow and daughters declined to become partners in firm presumably - None of heirs disclaimed or relinquished his or her right to claim share, right, title and interest of deceased in partnership firm and its assets - There can be no doubt that sharp income that was received by three minor sons during relevant period was earned with aid and assistance of HUF funds and was directly related to utilization of such funds by firm and further that HUF had suffered detriment in process of realisation of such income inasmuch as capital amount lying to credit of deceased, was utilized by firm free of interest - Further, in this case, there was no question of any services being rendered by three minors and, therefore, share income received by them must, in substance, be regarded as a return made to family because of investment of family funds in business - Therefore, taxing authorities as also Tribunal and High Court were right in assessing said income in hands of HUF-assessee - Appeal dismissed.
