G. Appaswami Chettiar and Another v R. Sarangapani Chettiar and Others

Supreme Court of India

22 March 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

P.S. Kailasam, R.S. Sarkaria

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 503; (1978) 3 SCC 55; AIR 1978 SC 1051; [1978] 3 S.C.R. 520

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Women & Children

Keywords: Female Heirs

Summary: Family & Personal - Women & Children - Hindu Adoptions and Maintenance Act, 1956 - Hindu Succession Act, s. 14(2) - Suit filed by appellants for declaration that adoption by first respondent is invalid - Adoption of son challenged on basis of motive - Contended that adoption made for purpose of depriving sapindas of their right to property and consent of sapindas of deceased husband not taken before adoption - Whether adoption by widow is invalid? - The power of a Hindu widow to adopt a son to her husband is well recognised in Hindu Law - The widow is the surviving half of the husband and the widow adopts according to the texts in her own right though the later view appears to be that she acts as a delegate or representative of her husband - When the adoption is authorised by the husband the widow's power is co-extensive with that of her husband - Equally, when the consent of the husband's kinsmen is obtained the widow's power is co-extensive with that of her husband - The power of adoption can be exercised by the widow alone and nobody can compel her to adopt - When there is no specific authority by the husband her authority is co-terminus with that of her husband subject only to the assent of the sapindas - It is not disputed that the consent of the sapindas is necessary in the absence of the husband's authorisation under the school of Hindu law to which the parties belong - The assent of the sapindas cannot be equated with the authority of the husband as the role of the sapindas is only advisory in nature - Held, consent of sapindas necessary in absence of husband authorization under school of Hindu Law - It was found by Courts that widow consulted all sapindas which was withheld by sapindas - Motive of widow is irreverent for purpose of validating adoption - Refusal of consent of sapindas on ground that motive of widow was improper and would amount to improperly withholding consent - Adoption was valid as widow consulted sapindas - Appeal dismissed.
