Tuka Ram and Another v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

15 September 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

A.D. Koshal, P.S. Kailasam, Jaswant Singh

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 86; (1979) 2 SCC 143; (1979) SCC (Cr) 381; 1979 (16) ACC 7; AIR 1979 SC 185; 1978 CRLJ 1864; 1978 CrLR(SC) 479; [1979] 1 S.C.R. 810

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 375, Thirdly - Appeal against judgment of High Court reversing a judgment of acquittal of two appellants of an offence u/s. 376 r/w s. 34 - Sexual intercourse - Consent - Circumstances negativing existence of 'fear' and story of 'passive submission' - Alleged intercourse by one and fondling of private part by other constable at police station - Charges unproved against later - Circumstance is not inculpatory and is cable of more explanations than one - Supreme Court did not take girl at her word in relation to appellant / accused Tukaram - Accused, held, entitled to be acquitted - Appeal allowed Note: case become a land mark case in criminal jurisprudence for rape cases and known as Mathura Rape Case.
