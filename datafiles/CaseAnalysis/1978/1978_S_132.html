Rama Dayal Markarha v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

14 March 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

D.A. Desai, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 321; (1978) SCC (Cr) 327; (1978) 2 SCC 630; AIR 1978 SC 921; 1978 CRLJ 917; 1978 CrLR(SC) 167; [1978] 3 S.C.R. 497

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Freedom Of Speech And Expression, Fair Comment

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Practice & Procedure - Contempt of Courts Act, 1971, ss. 3, 5 and 13 - Appellant advocate convicted by High Court for committing criminal contempt for lowering the authority of Court - Whether HC right in convicting appellant for criminal contempt? - Held, appellant published offending pamphlet which was mischievous - Appellant contended that he had published fair comment so he is entitled to benefit u/s. 5 of 1971 Act - Fair and reasonable criticism of a judgment which is a public document or act of judge would not constitute contempt - Contemnor cannot be given benefit of s. 18 - Contemnor did not recant before Court - Appellant found guilty of criminal contempt and order of conviction passed by HC is confirmed - Token fine is imposed on appellant - Appeal partly allowed
