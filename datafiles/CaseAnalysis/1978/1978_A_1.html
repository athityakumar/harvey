M. C. Gupta v A. K. Gupta & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

15 December 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

D.A. Desai

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 208; (1979) SCC (L&S) 168; (1979) 2 SCC 339; 1979 ALJ 251; 1979 LabIC 296; [1979] 2 S.C.R. 853; 1979 (2) SLR 1

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Education - Service - Indian Medical Council Act, 1956, s. 33 - Qualification - Medical lecturer - (A) Whether requisite teaching or research experience must be acquired while holding post set out in regulation in that subject? - Held, teaching/research experience must be acquired while holding post of Reader/Assistant Professor in Medicine for five years in a Medical College - As per regn. 4, 2 1/2 years' experience shall be, specifically teaching experience and credit can be given to extent of 50% of time spent in recognised research as prescribed in regulation; which experience can be in same subject, viz, subject for which recruitment is being made or in allied subject - (B) Whether Lecturer in Cardiology could be said to be a Lecturer in one of subjects under general medicine? - Further held, cardiology is a specialist branch under medicine and, therefore, a Lecturer in Cardiology could be said to be a Lecturer in one of subjects under general medicine and hence he had requisite experience as Lecturer in Medicine - Appeal allowed.
