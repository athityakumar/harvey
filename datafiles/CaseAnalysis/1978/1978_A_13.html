 State of Kerala and Others v Alasserry Mohammed and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 February 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

N.L. Untwalia, M. Hameedullah Beg, Y.V. Chandrachud, P.S. Kailasam, V.D. Tulzapurkar

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 330; (1978) SCC (Cr) 198; (1978) 2 SCC 386; 1978 (15) ACC 109; AIR 1978 SC 933; 1978 (4) ALR 223; 1978 CRLJ 925; 1978 CrLR(SC) 100; 1978 (1) FAC 145; [1978] 2 S.C.R. 820

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection; Criminal; Health & Drug; Socio-Economic

Keywords: Food Adulteration

Summary: Criminal - Health & Drug - Prevention of Food Adulteration Act, 1954, ss. 11 and 16(1)(a)(i) - Prevention of Food Adulteration Rules, 1955, r. 22 - Whether non-compliance with requirement of r. 22 of Prevention of Food Adulteration Rules, 1955 vitiates trial or conviction recorded u/s. 16(1)(a)(i) of Act? - R. 22 is directory and not mandatory - Use of word 'shall' in sub-s. (3) of s. 11 and in r. 22 indicates that an imperative duty has been cast upon Food Inspector to send a sample in accordance with prescribed rules - But mere use of word 'shall' does not invariably lead to this result - Use of word 'approximate' in r. 22 does indicate directory nature of rule but does not necessarily militate against view that rule is mandatory - Expression 'approximate quantity' is meant to convey that quantity to be supplied must be in close vicinity of quantity specified - Whole object of s. 11 and r. 22 is to find out by a correct analysis, subject to further verifications and tests by Director of Central Laboratory or otherwise, as to whether sample of food is adulterated or not - If quantity sent to Public Analyst, even though it is less than that prescribed, is sufficient and enables public analyst to make a correct analysis, then merely because quantity sent was not in strict compliance with rule will not result in nullification of report and obliterate its evidentiary value - If object is frustrated by sending of short quantity by Food Inspector to Public Analyst, it is obvious, that case may end in acquittal - But if object is not frustrated and is squarely and justifiably achieved without any shadow of doubt, then it will endanger public health to acquit offenders on technical grounds which have no substance - Fact that rule is directory and not mandatory does not, however, mean that it is open to Food Inspector to violate rule - If quantity supplied is sufficient and enables Public Analyst to do his duty of making a correct analysis, it should be inferred that rule has been substantially complied with, as purpose of rule has been achieved - Order accordingly.
