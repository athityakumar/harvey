Ram Rattan (Dead) By Legal Representatives v Bajrang Lal and Others

Supreme Court of India

05 May 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

D.A. Desai, Y.V. Chandrachud, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 303; (1978) 3 SCC 236; AIR 1978 SC 1393; 1978 (4) ALR 516; [1978] 3 S.C.R. 963; 1978 UJ 509

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land & Property - General Clauses Act, 1897, s. 2 (6) - Transfer of Property Act, 1882 - Registration Act, 1908 - Stamp Act, 1765, ss. 33, 36 - Declaration - Transfer of Interest - Inadmissible Evidence - Not Duly Stamped - Want of Registration - Plaintiff/appellant sought declaration that he was entitled to right of worship by turn in temple under will executed by deceased - Defendants/respondents submitted that document was inadmissible in evidence as being not duly stamped and for want of registration - Trial Court rejected same u/s. 36 of Act as document was not compulsorily registrable - On appeal by respondents, Lower Court reversed same holding document was gift, so inadmissible - Appellant's appeal to HC was dismissed - Hence instant appeal - Whether document purporting to be will of deceased was will or gift - Held, document purports to be gift of turn of worship as Shebait-cum-Pujari in Hindu temple, therefore, document was compulsorily registrable - Court held that document was inadmissible in evidence, as it was not duly stamped and for want of registration - Trial Court admitted document inadvertently without applying its mind, same could not be admitted in evidence as appellant had neither paid duty nor penalty u/s. 36 of Act - Courts with very few exceptions had recognized hereditary office of Shebait as immovable property, and it was treated as immovable property - Appeal dismissed.
