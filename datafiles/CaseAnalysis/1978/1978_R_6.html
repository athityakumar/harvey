Board Of Muslim Wakfs, Rajasthan v Radha Krishna & Ors

Supreme Court of India

24 October 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

A.P. Sen, R.S. Pathak, Jaswant Singh

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 99; (1979) 2 SCC 468; AIR 1979 SC 289; [1979] 2 S.C.R. 148

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Family & Personal; Practice & Procedure; Trusts & Associations

Summary: Land & Property - Trusts & Associations - Wakf Act, 1954, ss. 5(2), 6(1), 6(4), 27 and 36B - Publication of Wakf property - Entry in Wakf property list - Legality - Appellants filed instant appeal against judgment of HC holding that inclusion of disputed property in list of wakfs published by Board of Muslim Wakfs, u/s. 5(2) of the Act was not binding on respondents Nos. 1 and 2, mortgagee purchasers and restraining Board from taking only sleeps u/s. 36B of the Act for evicting them from same - Whether respondents Nos. 1 and 2 could be dispossessed, or their possession could be threatened by Board of Wakfs by proceeding u/s. 36B of the Act without filing a suit in Civil Court of competent jurisdiction did not arose - Held, Commissioner of Wakfs acted within jurisdiction in holding disputed property to be wakf property - It should follow that Board of Muslim Wakfs, was justified in including property in list of wakfs published u/s. 5(2) of the Act - However, on construction of s. 6(1) of the Act that list of wakfs so published by Board was not final and conclusive u/s. 6(4) of the Act against respondents Nos. 1 and 2 due to their failure to bring a suit within 1 year as contemplated by s. 6(1) of the Act - Right of respondent Nos. 1 and 2 in respect of disputed property, if at all they had any, would remain unaffected by impugned notification - Respondents were at liberty to bring a suit for establishment of their right and title, if any, to property - Order of HC allowing the petition and declaring that inclusion of property in dispute in list of wakf published by Board of Muslim Wakfs, u/s. 5(2) of the Act was not binding on respondents Nos. 1 and 2 was upheld - But direction of HC, restraining Board of Muslim Wakfs from entering disputed property in register of wakfs and from dispossessing respondents Nos. 1 and 2, except by way of a suit in a Civil Court of competent jurisdiction was set aside as it proceeds on assumption that ss. 27 and 36B of the Act ware not applicable, which question did not arose - Appeal dismissed.
