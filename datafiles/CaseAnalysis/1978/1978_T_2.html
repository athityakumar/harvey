Inder Singh and Another v State (Delhi Admn.)

Supreme Court of India

24 February 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

V.R. Krishna Iyer, Jaswant Singh

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 155; (1978) 4 SCC 161; (1978) SCC (Cr) 564; AIR 1978 SC 1091; 1978 CRLJ 766; 1978 CrLR(SC) 154; 1979 SCJ 10; [1978] 3 S.C.R. 393

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal -Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 302 and 307 - Constitution art. 21 - Murder - Criminal trial - What is 'standard' for proof of guilt beyond reasonable doubt -Conviction under s. 302 r/w s. 34 and s. 307,IPC - Whether sentence of accused of tender age hit under protection of art. 21, Constitution?- Credibility of testimony, oral and circumstantial, depends considerably on a judicial evaluation of totality, not isolated scrutiny - Proof beyond reasonable doubt is a guideline, not a fetish and guilty man cannot get away with it because truth suffers some infirmity when projected through human processes - Held,instead of bolting young men behind high walls of a prison and forgetting about them, humanising influences must be brought to bear upon them - Jurisdictional root for legal liberalism is art. 21 of Constitution of India,1950 - Petition dismissed.
