Udai Chand v Shanker Lal and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 February 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Hameedullah Beg, P.N. Bhagwati, D.A. Desai

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 144; (1978) 2 SCC 209; AIR 1978 SC 765; 1978 (1) RCJ 576; 1978 (2) RCR(Rent) 476; 1978 (1) RentLR 484; [1978] 2 S.C.R. 809; 1978 UJ 171

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950 art. 136 - Validity of revocation of special leave obtained by making false and misleading assertion in a petition - Held, Supreme Court would be justified in revoking leave to appeal if same was obtained by making misstatement of a material fact which was of decisive importance in case - Revoke special leave granted and reject special leave petition - Application allowed - However, application for urging additional grounds is rejected.
