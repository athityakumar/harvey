Muthu Naicker and Others v State of Tamil Nadu

Supreme Court of India

10 August 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

D.A. Desai, P.N. Shingal

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 405; (1978) 4 SCC 385; (1979) SCC (Cr) 14; AIR 1978 SC 1647; 1978 CRLJ 1713; 1978 CRLR 378; 1979 (1) MLJ(SC) 10

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Pelting Stones, Waylaid

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 147, 149, 302, 323, 324, 325 and 427 - Supreme Court (Enlargement of Criminal Appellate Jurisdiction) Act, 1971, s. 2(a) - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 313 - (A) Whether mere presence in an unlawful assembly is sufficient to attach vicarious liability to very member of the unlawful assembly? - Held, whenever in uneventful rural society something unusual occurs, a good number of people appear on the scene not with a view to participating in the occurrence but as curious spectators, in such an event mere presence in the unlawful assembly should not be treated as leading to conclusion that the person concerned was present in the unlawful assembly as a member of the unlawful assembly - Vicarious liability would attach to every member of the unlawful assembly if that member either participates in the commission of the offence by overt act or knows that the offence which is committed was likely to be committed by any member of the unlawful assembly in prosecution of the common object of the unlawful assembly and becomes or continues to remain a member of the unlawful assembly - If one becomes a member of the unlawful assembly and his association in the unlawful assembly is clearly established, his participation in commission of the offence by overt act is not required to be provided if it could be shown that he knew that such offence was likely to be committed in prosecution of the common object of the unlawful assembly - (B) When was the unlawful assembly formed, what was its common object and who were its members who committed the offence of rioting? - PW 16's evidence show that she had seen certain persons collecting at Casuarina Thope and some of them were armed with sticks and knives and when Gajarajan alighted from the bus, from Casuarina Thope, a group of persons emerged and rushed towards him who was encircled and attacked near the well, PW 1 stated that the deceased was first encircled and attacked near the well, PW 17 saw a mob consisting of 40 to 50 persons running in the street and identified accused 2, 3, 5, 9 and 15 in the crowd, PW 9 saw Gajarajan alighting from the bus and a crowd running from Casuarina Thope and raising alarm 'Pidida' - Held, if some persons had collected at one place and chased him, and some of them belaboured him, it can be in concert, if some persons combined to attack Gajarajan and if they emerged together one can say that those who were the members of this assembly shared the common object of the assembly, viz., to assault and even to cause hurt to Gajarajan and that at that stage an unlawful assembly was formed is unmistakably established - (C) Whether the accused persons can be held guilty of various charges u/ ss. 147, 148, 302/34, 326/149, 323, 324, 427/34? - (i) The dispute about taking water and laying pipeline had divided the local village community into two rival factions, one faction was led by PW 31 and Gajarajan was his brother; accused belonged to opposite faction; there was enmity and bitterness between the two factions and this water dispute would establish some motive, (ii) PW1 saw accused 1 inflicting an injury with the stick on Gajarajan's shoulder, PW 2 claimed to identify him by his voice, PW 9 also says that he beat deceased near the well but he did not know whether the blow landed on him or not, at least three persons identified him in the unlawful assembly and he was armed with weapon and was seen by two witnesses wielding the weapon, and the medical evidence corroborates that a stick blow had landed on the right hand of the deceased, thus his presence in the unlawful assembly and participation in the assault on the deceased in established (iii) PW1 saw accused 2 armed with patta kathi and inflicting an injury on the right side of the deceased's head; he is shown to have caused an injury on PW6's leg who identified his assailant; PW 9 refers to his presence in the crowd with a knife which he has described as patta knife, thus his presence in the unlawful assembly and participation in the assault is affirmatively established, (iv) Accused 3's presence is referred to by at least five witnesses, however, manifest error emanates from the fact that the copy of the F.I.R. used by the High Court materially differs in respect of this accused from the copy supplied to this Court where name of accused 3 is not to be found, PW1's evidence is not corroborated by the PW 6's evidence and PW 9 is not clear about his role, thus he is entitled to benefit of doubt with regard to charge u/s. 302/34, (v) Accused 4 was seen in the unlawful assembly by PWs 1, 2, 6, 7, 8, 18 and 22, evidence shows that he gave two blows with patta kathi to PW 2 and his presence and causing injury to PW 7 is spoken by PW 7, hence evidence establishes his presence in the unlawful assembly and his participation in the assault on the deceased, (vi) Injury attributed to accused 5 has been corroborated in the form of medical evidence; there is PW1's evidence that this accused tried to give him a stab blow with his spear but he warded of the blow and in the process his shirt was torn which fact is clearly stated in the F.I.R. and also that he gave a blow with patta kathi to the deceased, on the identity and presence of this accused, evidence of PW 1 is corroborated by FIR, medical evidence and evidence of PWs 18, 19 and 22 as having seen him in the unlawful assembly, thus his presence and participation is clearly established, (vii) Accused 6 and 7 are alleged to have pelted stones at deceased for which there is evidence of PW 1 alone in respect of which the learned Session Judge has noted that after PW 1 described the entire incident, he volunteered that accused 6 and 7 pelted stones which is an infirmity in the evidence of this sole witness; PW 19 stated that he had seen accused 6 brandishing an iron pipe but in the police statement he referred to a stick, PWs 20, 22 and 24 referred to his presence in the crowd, similarly, PWs 19, 20 and 22 referred to accused 7's presence in the crowd, PW1 had stated that when deceased fell down near PW 10's house, he was encircled by members of the unlawful assembly and if thereafter accused 6 and 7 pelted stones at Gajarajan it is difficult to believe that they would hit Gajarajan, thus accused 6 and 7 cannot be stated to have participated in assault, (viii) as to the accused 19, the doctor had admitted in cross-examination that if a man stood near deceased and dropped a stone about 4 kg. in weigh, the skull would be broken into multiple bits, and if the stone is thrown with great force, the skull would be crushed; if this part evidence is correlated to the external injuries found on the person of deceased, PW1's evidence is contradicted by the medical evidence, also that part of his evidence was added by him volunteering the information after he had described the whole incident, therefore in this state of evidence, he is entitled to be acquitted of the charge u/ss. 302/34 - Held, the conviction of accused 1, 6, 8, 11, 15, 19, 20 and 23 u/s. 147 is confirmed and that of accused 7, 12, 13, 14, 22, 26 and 27 is set aside and accused acquitted - Conviction u/s. 148 of accused 2, 3, 4, 5, 9, 10, 17, 18, 21, 24 and 25 is confirmed and that of accused 16 is set aside - Conviction u/s. 302/34 of accused 1, 2, 4 and 5 is confirmed and that of accused 3, 6, 7 and 19 is set aside - Conviction u/s. 326/149 of accused 11, 12, 13, 14, 15, 18, 21, 22, 23, 24, 25, 26 and 27 is set aside - Conviction of accused 1 u/s. 323 and of accused 2, 4 and 24 u/s. 324 are confirmed - Conviction of accused 5 and 23 u/s. 323 is set aside - Accused 27's conviction u/s. 323 is set aside - Conviction and sentence of accused 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9, 10, 15, 16, 17, 19, 20, 21 and 23 u/s. 427/34 are set aside and that of accused 2, 3, 8 and 9 are confirmed - Criminal Appeals 230 and 238 of 1972, and 97 of 1973 partly allowed.
