Bajaya v Gopikabai and Another

Supreme Court of India

04 April 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Sarkaria, N.L. Untwalia, P.S. Kailasam

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 319; (1978) 2 SCC 542; AIR 1978 SC 793; [1978] 3 S.C.R. 561; 1978 UJ 463

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Women & Children

Keywords: Succession & Inheritance

Summary: Family & Personal - Hindu Succession Act, 1956, s. 4(2) - Madhya Pradesh Land Revenue Code, 1954, s. 151 - Whether Chap. XII in general and s. 151 in particular, is a law 'for devolution of tenancy rights in respect of agricultural holdings' within saving clause in s. 4(2) of Hindu Succession Act, 1956? - Held, the suit property being an agricultural holding, in view of s. 4(2) of Hindu Succession Act, 1956, inheritance to estate will not be governed by provisions of that Act, but by Mitakshra School of Hindu Law - S. 151 of Madhya Pradesh Land Revenue Code, 1954 was a law for devolution of tenancy rights in agricultural holdings - Devolution of agricultural holding will be governed by s. 151 of Code - S. 151, which is in Chap. XII, says that subject to his personal law, interest of a tenure holder shall on his death pass by inheritance, survivorship or bequest, as case may be - Plaintiff would exclude defendants-agnates from inheritance even according to 'personal law' which, within contemplation of s. 151 of Code, will include Hindu Succession Act, 1956 in force at time when plaintiff,s husband died and succession opened out - Appeal allowed.
