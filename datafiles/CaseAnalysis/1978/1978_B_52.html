Workmen of Cochin Port Trust v Board of Trustees of The Cochin Port Trust and

Supreme Court of India

05 May 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

N.L. Untwalia, R.S. Pathak, Jaswant Singh

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 302; (1978) 3 SCC 119; (1978) SCC (L&S) 438; AIR 1978 SC 1283; 1978 (37) FLR 85; 1978 LabIC 1111; 1978 (2) LLJ 161; 1978 (2) LLN 298; [1978] 3 S.C.R. 971; 1978 UJ 489

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Constructive Res Judicata, Quo Warranto

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Constitution - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 11 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Constructive Res Judicator - Work by Rotation - Extra Wages - Non-speaking Order - Two categories of appellant/workmen were made to work on Sundays by rotation and get another day off in week - They did not got extra wages for half day, which were given to other categories of workers - Industrial dispute between appellants and respondent was referred to Industrial Tribunal/respondent No. 2, which gave an award in favor of appellant/workmen which was set aside by HC - Hence instant appeal - Whether Roster off system should be discontinued and appellants should be given half day's additional wages and Whether demand of appellants were justifiable - Held, applying principles of constructive res judicature, non-speaking order of dismissal without indicating grounds or reasons for its dismissal was void - Court thought it proper to indicate that dismissal of a petition in liming by a non speaking order could certainly create a bar in entertainment of another writ petition filed by same party on same cause of action - Court declined to exercise its discretion in favor of petitioner would affect jurisdiction vested in HC u/art. 226 of Constitution - Demand for changing Roster off system in respect of two categories of appellant/workmen not justified - Tribunal did not find that Roster off system was not necessary for successful working of Port work as deposed by respondent and no discrimination could be found in Roster off system - Tribunal exceeded its jurisdiction in saying that categories of appellant could be always made to work on Sundays, but they should be given additional half day's wages, besides a day off for working on Sundays - Relief granted by Tribunal was beyond scope of reference - Appeal dismissed.
