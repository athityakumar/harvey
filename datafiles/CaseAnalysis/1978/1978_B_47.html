Commissioner of Income Tax (Central), New Delhi v Bijli Cotton Mills Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

07 November 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

V.D. Tulzapurkar, P.N. Bhagwati

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 222; (1979) 1 SCC 496; AIR 1979 SC 346; 1979 (8) CTR 1; [1979] 116 ITR 60; [1979] 2 S.C.R. 241; 1979 TAXLR 146; 1979 UJ 23

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 4(3)(i), 10(1) - Exemption from tax u/s.4(3)(i) of 1922 Act - Company carrying on business of manufacturing and selling yarn - Realisations by assessee from customers for 'Dharmada' - (A) Validity - (B) Whether realisations can be regarded as assessee's income - Held, must be regarded as a gift or payment for religious or charitable purposes and such a gift or payment would not be invalid for vagueness or uncertainty - Realisations as such could not be regarded as trading receipts of the assessee - Appeals dismissed.
