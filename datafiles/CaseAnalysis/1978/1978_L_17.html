Northern India Caterers (India) Limited v Lt. Governor of Delhi

Supreme Court of India

07 September 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Pathak, P.N. Bhagwati, V.D. Tulzapurkar

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 286; (1978) 4 SCC 36; (1978) SCC (Tax) 198; AIR 1978 SC 1591; 1978 (7) CTR 130; [1979] 1 S.C.R. 557; 1978 (42) STC 386; 1978 TAXLR 2316

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Bengal Finance (Sales Tax) Act, 1941, ss. 2(g), 4 and 21(3) - Whether in case of non-residents service of meals by appellant in restaurant constitutes a sale of foodstuffs? - Supply of meals was essentially in nature of a service provided to them - A number of services are concomitantly provided by way of hospitality, supply of meals must be regarded as ministering to a bodily want or to satisfaction of a human need - Held, service of meals to visitors in restaurant of appellant is not taxable under Bengal Finance (Sales Tax) Act, as extended to Union Territory of Delhi - Order accordingly.
