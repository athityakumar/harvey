Raghunath Pradhani v Damodra Mahapatra and Others

Supreme Court of India

02 November 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.V. Chandrachud, P.S. Kailasam, A.D. Koshal

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 59

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, ss. 47 and 151, O. 21 rr. 89 and 90 - Auction sale - Entitled to - Respondent No.1 obtained money decree against respondent No.3 and his mother respondent No.4 - Respondent No.1 filed an execution petition for recovering the decretal amount and prayed therein for attachment of the Immovable property belonging to respondent No.3 - Property was attached by an order passed by the Executing Court - Respondent No.1 filed an application in the Executing Court praying that permission be obtained of the Revenue Divisional officer for sale of the property since respondent No.3 - Appellant filed an application under O. 21 rr. 89 and 90 and ss. 47 and 151 of CPC praying that the auction sale should be set aside on the ground that the attachment and the auction sale were void since they were effected without obtaining the permission of the competent authority u/Regn. No. 2 of 1956 - District Munsif who dealt with the matter accepted the contention of the appellant and set aside the auction sale - Before HC respondent 1 raised two contentions only viz., (1) that the judgment-debtor, respondent No.3, was not a member of the Scheduled Tribe and therefore the attachment and the court sale were not void; and (2) that the judgment-debtors, having failed to take objection to the attachment on the ground that they belonged to a Scheduled Tribe, were debarred from objecting to the sale of the property on the principle of constructive res judicata - HC rejected the first contention relying mainly on the circumstance that respondent 1, the decree holder, had accepted the position that respondent 3 whose property was being put to sale was a member of the Scheduled Tribe - HC, however, accepted the second contention on the ground that neither respondent No.3 nor the appellant had taken any objection in the execution proceedings that since the former had no saleable interest in the property the auction sale could not be held or that the permission given by the, R.D.O. did not authorise the sale - Hence instant appeal - Whether prayer for setting aside of auction sale was sustainable - Held, it was elementary that what could be brought to sale in a court sale was the right, title and interest of the judgment-debtor and therefore, the auction purchaser could get nothing more than that right, title and interest - Judgment-debtor not having any saleable interest in the property at all on the date of the auction sale, there was nothing that respondent No.2 could get in the auction sale which was held in execution of the money decree obtained by his father, respondent No.1 - Auction sale therefore could not displace the title of the appellant which was the same thing as saying that as between the title of the appellant and the so called title of the auction purchaser, the appellant's title should prevail - It should follow that the auction sale was bad and must be set aside - There was an additional reason why the auction sale was not valid By the permission granted by the R.D.O for sale of the property, one of the conditions imposed on the judgment-debtor was that the property should be sold for a sum of Rs 4,000/- - In the private sale, the appellant purchased the property for Rs. 4,000/- and therefore the condition of the permission was complied with - But the auction sale was held in satisfaction of the decretal dues which were far less than Rs. 4,000/-, the decree itself being in the sum of Rs. 1,000 odd and the highest bid at the auction being of Rs. 3,000/- only - As the condition imposed by the R.D.O. regarding the price was violated by the auction sale, the auction purchaser could not get a valid title to the property under that sale - In this view, no question of res judicata could arise because the basic issue of the appeal was as regards the validity of the auction sale in favour of respondent No.2 - Appellant claimed through the judgment-debtor and neither the latter nor the decree-holder ever disputed that he, the judgment-debtor, was a member of the Scheduled Tribe - On the other hand both of them were conscious of the situation that the property could not be sold without the sanction of the R.D.O. - Decree-holder himself, apprised the Executing Court of that position - Failure, there, of the judgment-debtor to raise any particular contention could not operate as res judicata, actually or constructively, either against him or against the appellant - Appeal allowed.
