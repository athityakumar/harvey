Kamlapati Trivedi v State of West Bengal

Supreme Court of India

13 December 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

P.S. Kailasam, Jaswant Singh, A.D. Koshal

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 263; (1980) 2 SCC 91; (1980) SCC (Cr) 347; AIR 1979 SC 777; 1979 CRLJ 679; 1979 CRLR 128; [1979] 2 S.C.R. 717

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Election; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Election - Representation Of People Act, 1951 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 6, 169, 173, 190 and 195(1)(b) - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 211 - Meaning of expression 'in relation to any proceeding' u/s. 195 (1)(b), CrPC, 1973 - Whether Magistrate is debarred from taking cognizance of offence under s. 211, IPC in absence of a complaint in writing in view of provisions of cl. (b) of s. 195(1), CrPC? - Magistrate releasing accused of offence committed under s. 211, IPC, 1860 in relation to a proceeding in Court, on bail and ultimately discharging him on submission of final report by police that complaint made to it was false - Whether acts judicially and therefore a 'Court'- (B) Whether taking of cognizance of offence by a Magistrate u/s. 190, CrPC is a condition precedent to be regarded as a 'Court'? - While deciding question of bail a Magistrate must be held to be acting as a Court and not in any other capacity, irrespective of stage which case has reached by then, that is, whether it is still under investigation by police or has progressed to stage of an inquiry or trial by Magistrate - Held, taking of a cognizance of any offence by a Magistrate under Section 190 is not a condition precedent for him to be regarded as a Court - Appeal allowed.
