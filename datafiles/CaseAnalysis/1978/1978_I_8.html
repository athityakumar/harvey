Avon Services (Production Agencies) Pvt. Ltd v Industrial Tribunal, Haryana Farldabad Ors

Supreme Court of India

06 October 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

D.A. Desai, V.R. Krishna Iyer

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 68; (1979) 1 SCC 1; (1979) SCC (L&S) 15; AIR 1979 SC 170; 1978 (37) FLR 363; 1979 LabIC 1; 1979 (1) LLJ 1; 1978 (2) LLN 503; [1979] 2 S.C.R. 45; 1979 SLJ 265; 1978 UJ 833

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Closure

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Indian Companies Act, 1956 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 2(j), 10(i), 25F and 25FFF - Reference - Legality of - (A) Whether the present reference is bad in law for the reasons given in para No. 1 of the preliminary objection in the written statement? - Held, it is not possible to accept the submission that if the Govt. had on an earlier occasion declined to make a reference unless it be shown that there was some fresh or additional material before the Govt. the second reference would be incompetent - It has not been shown that the dispute had ceased to exist and the very existence of the dispute enables the Govt. to exercise the power u/s. 10(l) and it has been rightly exercised - (B) Retrenchment - Validity of - Whether retrenchment of respondents was justified? If not, to what relief they are entitled? - Held, tribunal found that alleged retrenchment notice was not served upon workmen and that finding was not controverted by pointing out some evidence which may point to contrary notice expressly states that workmen are retrenched though it simultaneously states that action is taken u/s. 25 FFF - Workmen appear not to have been employed initially as painters - They were doing some other work from which they were brought to painting section - They could have as well been absorbed in some other work from which they were capable of doing as observed by tribunal - Once container making section was closed down, three painters became part and parcel of manufacturing process and if painting work was not available for them they could have been assigned some other work and even if they had to be retrenched as surplus, case would squarely fall in s. 25F and not be covered by s. 25 FFF, on a specious plea of closure of an undertaking - Appeal dismissed.
