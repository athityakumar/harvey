Chief Commissioner, Delhi, and Another v Delhi Cloth and General Mills Company Limited and

Supreme Court of India

07 April 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, Jaswant Singh

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 248; (1978) 2 SCC 367; AIR 1978 SC 1181; [1978] 48 Comp Cas 588; [1978] 3 S.C.R. 657; 1978 UJ 352

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Administrative - Valid Fee - General revenues - Respondent-company executed debenture trust deeds in favour of trustees, and when said documents were presented for registration, Sub-Registrar under notification issued by Chief Commissioner of Delhi, demanded registration fee of Rs.1,25,157.50 - Hence, trustees filed petition before HC challenging validity of notification and exorbitant amount realized as registration fee - HC quashed impugned notification and directed refund of fee - Hence, instant appeal - Whether fee charged under notification issued by Chief Commissioner was legal impost justified by provisions of Constitution.

Held, it was not disputed before HC that fee realized by Registration Department under the impugned notification was to form part of general revenues of State. Hence, second element of valid fee was not present and so, HC was right in striking down the impugned notification. Appeal dismissed.

Ratio - Fee in order to be legal fee, should satisfy two conditions, viz., the authority levying the fee renders some service for fee levied however remote the service may be and the fee realized is being spent for purposes of imposition and not form part of general revenues of the State.
