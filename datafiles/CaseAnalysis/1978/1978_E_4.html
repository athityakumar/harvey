Hindustan Tin Works Private Limited v Employees of Hindustan Tin Works Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

07 September 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

D.A. Desai, V.R. Krishna Iyer, O. Chinnappa Reddy

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 70; (1979) SCC (L&S) 53; (1979) 2 SCC 80; 1979 ACJ 58; AIR 1979 SC 75; 1978 (37) FLR 240; 1978 LabIC 1667; 1978 (2) LLJ 474; 1979 (1) LLN 6; [1979] 1 S.C.R. 563; 1978 UJ 712

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - UP Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 4 K and 6 - Illegal termination of workmen - Labour Court holding retrenchment illegal and directing reinstatement with full back wages - (A) Appeal against award - Effect of grant of special leave upto extent of grant of full back wages - (B) Scope of jurisdiction of Supreme Court u/art. 136 of Constitution - Interference with award of Labour Court, when allowed - (A) Held, question as to relief of reinstatement is no more open to challenge - Compelling necessity for retrenchment of workmen cannot be gone into by Court - (B) Scope and ambit of this wide constitutional discretionary power cannot be exhaustively defined -Interference only where awards are made in violation of principles of nature justice causing substantial and gave injustice to parties or raises an important principle of Industrials law require elucidation and final decision by Supreme Court or discloses such other exceptional or special circumstances which merit consideration of Supreme Court - 75 per cent of back wages allowed - Order accordingly.
