State of Orissa v Ram Chander Agarwala and Others

Supreme Court of India

05 October 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

P.S. Kailasam, Jaswant Singh

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 278; (1979) 2 SCC 305; (1979) SCC (Cr) 462; (2001) SCC (L&S) 1; AIR 1979 SC 87; 1979 CRLJ 33; 1978 CrLR(SC) 604; 2001 LabIC 70; [1979] 1 S.C.R. 1114; 1979 UJ 172

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898, s. 369, 424, 430 and 561A - High Court's power to review its judgment - (A) Whether restriction u/s. 369 of CrPC, 1898 apply to trial dourt alone? - Held, ss. 369 and 424 do not restrict prohibition u/s. 369 to trial Court alone - The purpose of s. 424 is to prescribe mode of delivering judgment, language and contents of judgment, while s. 369 is general in its application and prohibits all courts from altering or reviewing its judgment when once it has signed it - (B) Whether High Court can review its own jusgements? - Further held, Once a judgment has been pronounced by a HC either in exercise of its appellate or its revisional jurisdiction, no review or revision can be entertained against that judgment as there is no provision in Code which would enable HC to review same or to exercise revisional jurisdiction - Appeal allowed.
