 Excel Wear Etc v Union Of India & Ors

Supreme Court of India

29 September 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

N.L. Untwalia, Y.V. Chandrachud, R.S. Sarkaria, A.D. Koshal, A.P. Sen

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 93; (1978) 4 SCC 224; (1978) SCC (L&S) 509; AIR 1979 SC 25; 1978 (37) FLR 314; 1978 LabIC 1537; 1978 (2) LLJ 527; 1978 (2) LLN 482; [1979] 1 S.C.R. 1009

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Closure, General Public, Industrial Disputes (Amendment) Act, 1976

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Constitution - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 25-O and 25-R - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 19, 19(1)(g), 31C, 358 - SS. 25-O, 25-R of the Act - Constitutional validity - Petitioner/employers filed instant writ petitions challenging Constitutional validity of ss. 25-o and 25-R of the Act - Whether restrictions imposed by ss. 25-O and 25-R of the Act were reasonable or not u/art. 19(1)(g) of Constitution - Held, in case of art. 19 of Constitution contravention was of fundamental rights guaranteed to a citizen - Further, art. 358 of Constitution postulates that law which was incompetently made and bad for violation of art. 19 of Constitution should not be taken to be so during period of Emergency - However, as soon as Emergency was lifted law becomes bad because it was bad when it was enacted, although it could not be taken to be so during period of Emergency - Thus, law which was enacted in March, 1976 could by no stretch of imagination be said to be a law giving effect to policy of State towards securing any of principles laid down in Part-IV within meaning of amended art. 31C of Constitution which came into force in January, 1977 - Legislature could not have thought of enacting a law within meaning of amended art. 31C of Constitution at a point of time when art. 31C of Constitution stood unamended - Therefore, purport, content and principles underlying in two articles were so different that it was difficult to tag effects of two together and make it a continuous effect - Hence, law was bad for violation of art. 19(1)(g) of Constitution when it was enacted, but it was not to be taken to be so during period of Emergency, its invalidity sprouted out with full vigour on lifting of Emergency and amended art. 31C of Constitution did not save it - Thus, content of arts. 358 and 31C of Constitution was entirely different -Former did not lift ban in way of State to enact a law in violation of art. 19 of Constitution and it puts ban under suspension during period of Emergency and suspension comes to an end on its lifting art. 31C of Constitution had no words to indicate that ban was removed by it which merely saves law enacted after coming into force of said article - Therefore, ss. 25-O and s. 25-R of the Act as a whole in so far as it relates to awarding of punishment for infraction of provisions of s. 25-O of the Act were Constitutionally bad and invalid for violation of art. 19(1)(g) of Constitution - Impugned orders passed u/s. 25-O of the Act were void - Petitions allowed.
