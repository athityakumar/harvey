Bai Tahira A v Ali Hussain Fissalli Chothia And Anr

Supreme Court of India

06 October 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

V.R. Krishna Iyer, V.D. Tulzapurkar, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 67; (1979) 2 SCC 316; (1979) SCC (Cr) 473; AIR 1979 SC 362; 1979 CRLJ 151; 1978 CrLR(SC) 616; 1980 HLR 766; 1979 MahLJ 95; [1979] 2 S.C.R. 75; 1979 UJ 150

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Summary: Constitution - Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 125 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Maintenance - Scope and ambit of s. 125 CrPC - Held, every divorcee is entitled to benefit of maintenance allowance and dissolution of marriage makes no difference to this right under current Code and in normal course, an order for maintenance must follow, quantum - S. 125 requires, as a sine qua non for its application, neglect by husband or father - The absence of mutual consent to live separately must be made out if hurdle of s. 125(4) is to be over come - Where husband, by customary payment at time of divorce, has adequately provided for divorce, a subsequent series of recurrent does is contra-indicated and husband liberated - Payment of illusory amounts by way of customary or personal law requirement will be considered in reduction of maintenance rate but cannot annihilate that rate unless it is a reasonable substitute legal sanctity of payment is certified by fulfillment of social obligation, not by a ritual exercise rooted in custom - No construction which leads to frustration of statutory project can secure validation if court is to pay true homage to Constitution - The only just construction of section is that Parliament intended divorcees should not derive a double benefit - The purpose of payment 'under any customary or personal law' must be to obviate destitution of divorcee and to provide her with wherewithal to maintain herself - Appeal allowed.
