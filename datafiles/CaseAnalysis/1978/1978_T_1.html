In Re The Special Courts Bill, 1978

Supreme Court of India

01 December 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.V. Chandrachud

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 352; (1979) 1 SCC 380; AIR 1979 SC 478; [1979] 35 ITR 190; 1979 (2) SCJ 35; [1979] 2 S.C.R. 476

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Parliament & Legislature

Keywords: Constitutional Safeguards, Emergency Provisions, Equal Justice, Equal protection of law, Equality before law

Summary: Constitution - Scope of the art. 143 (1) of Constitution - Held, article empowers the President to make a reference even on questions of fact provided the other conditions of the article are satisfied - Supreme Court u/art. 143 (1) is to consider the question on which the President has made the reference and report to the President its opinion, provided of course the question is capable of being pronounced upon and falls within the power of the Court to decidein matters arising - And u/cls. (2), SC may be justified in returning the reference unanswered if it finds for a valid reason that the question is incapable of being answered - Order Accordingly.
