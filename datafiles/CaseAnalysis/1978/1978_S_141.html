Madhav Hayawadanrao Hoskot v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

17 August 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

V.R. Krishna Iyer, D.A. Desai, O. Chinnappa Reddy

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 192; (1978) SCC (Cr) 468; (1978) 3 SCC 544; AIR 1978 SC 1548; 1978 CRLJ 1678; 1978 CrLR(SC) 362; [1979] 1 S.C.R. 192; 1978 UJ 847

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; International; Tort

Keywords: Equal Justice, Universal Declaration of Human Rights

Summary: Criminal - Fair Trial - Legal aid - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 21, 39A, 136 and 142 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 417, 467, 468, 471 and 511 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 304 and 363 - Petitioner, a Reader in Saurashtra University - Petitioner was charged for fabrication of documents - Convicted - Undue delay caused in preferring appeal as petitioner was provided copy of decision after undue delay - Whether petitioner can be allowed to defend himself? - One component of fair procedure is natural justice - Held yes - State has duty u/arts. 21, 39A and 142 of Constitution to provide legal aid to every accused - Right of petitioner to procure legal aid reserved - Right to counsel in its wider amplitude in peremptory sense of art. 21 confined to prison situations - Further held, courts shall forthwith furnish a free transcript of the judgment when sentencing a person to prison term - In event of any such copy being sent to the jail authorities for delivery to prisoner, by appellate, revisional or other court, the official concerned shall, with quick dispatch, get it delivered to the sentence and obtain written acknowledgment thereof from him - Where prisoner seeks to file an appeal or revision, every facility for exercise of that right shall be made available by Jail Administration - State which prosecuted prisoner and set in motion the process which deprived him of his liberty shall pay to assigned counsel such sum as the court may equitably fix - Petition dismissed.
