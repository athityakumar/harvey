Ganesh Bhavan Patel and Another v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

18 October 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Sarkaria, Y.V. Chandrachud, O. Chinnappa Reddy

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 95; (1978) 4 SCC 371; (1979) SCC (Cr) 1; AIR 1979 SC 135; 1979 CRLJ 51; 1978 CrLR(SC) 632; [1979] 2 S.C.R. 94

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Tort

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 34 - Appreciation of evidence - Power of High Court - Whether in an appeal HC has power to reassess evidence and reach its own conclusion? - Held, although in an appeal from an order of acquittal powers of HC to reassess evidence and reach its own conclusions are as extensive as in an appeal against an order of conviction, yet, as a rule of prudence, it should always give proper weight and consideration to such matters as (1) views of Trial Judge as to credibility of witnesses (2) presumption of innocence in favour of accused; (3) right of accused to benefit of any doubt; and (4) slowness of an appellate Court in disturbing a finding of fact arrived at by a Judge who had advantage of seeing witnesses - Where two reasonable conclusions can be drawn on evidence on record, HC should refrain from interfering with order of acquittal recorded by Court - Circumstances inevitably lead to conclusion, that prosecution story was conceived and constructed after a good deal of deliberation and delay in a shady setting, highly redolent of doubt and suspicion - Appeal allowed.
