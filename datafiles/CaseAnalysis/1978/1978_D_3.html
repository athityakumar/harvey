V. C. Rangadurai v D. Gopalan and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 October 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

V.R. Krishna Iyer, D.A. Desai, A.P. Sen

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 62; (1979) 1 SCC 308; AIR 1979 SC 281; 1979 ALJ 144; 1979 (2) MLJ(SC) 1; [1979] 1 S.C.R. 1054; 1978 UJ 838

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Attorney-General

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Advocates Act, 1961, s. 35(3) - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Appellant found guilty of misconduct and suspended from practice for one year - Whether disciplinary committee of Bar Council was right in reducing period of suspension from practice to one year from six years? - Held, appellant contended that finding of disciplinary committee on part of appellant is not based on any legal evidence but proceeds on mere conjectures - Purpose of disciplinary proceedings is not punitive but to inquire - Findings in disciplinary proceedings must be sustained by a higher degree of proof than that required in civil suits - There should be convincing preponderance of evidence - There is no reason for this court to interfere in appeal with finding in such a domestic enquiry merely because on a reappraisal of evidence a different view is possible - No ground for interference with finding of Disciplinary Committee of Bar Council of India - Burden is on applicant to establish that he is entitled to resume privilege of practicing law without restrictions and it is not case in present appeal - Appeal dismissed.
