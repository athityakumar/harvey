Supreme Court of India

21 February 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Hameedullah Beg, Y.V. Chandrachud, P.N. Bhagwati, V.D. Tulzapurkar, D.A. Desai, V.R. Krishna Iyer, Jaswant Singh

Where Reported

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 2(a), 10 and 22 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 12, 19(1)(g), 38, 39, 43, 46, 298, 310 and 311 - What is meaning of industry? - Held, phrase 'analogous to trade or business' could reasonably mean only activity which result in goods made or manufactured or services rendered which are capable of being converted into saleable ones - They must be capable of entering world of res commercium although they may be kept out of market for some reason - It is not motive of an activity in making goods or rendering service, but possibility of making them marketable if one who makes goods or renders services so desires, that should determine whether activity lies within domain or circle of industry - Appeal dismissed.
