Smt. S. R. Venkataraman v Union Of India & Anr

Supreme Court of India

02 November 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Shingal, O. Chinnappa Reddy

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 251; (1979) SCC (L&S) 216; (1979) 2 SCC 491; AIR 1979 SC 49; 1978 (37) FLR 425; 1978 LabIC 1641; 1979 (1) LLJ 25; 1978 (2) LLN 479; [1979] 2 S.C.R. 202; 1979 SLJ 1; 1979 (1) SLR 130; 1979 UJ 14

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Constitution, arts. 226 and 311 - Fundamental Rules, r. 56(j)(i) - (A) Tort - Meaning of malice - (B) Maintainability of plea of malice in compulsory retirement - (C) Fundamental Rules - Compulsory retirement in public interest - Legality of Order based on non-existent fact - Held, malice in its legal sense means malice such as may be assumed from the doing of a wrongful act intentionally but without just cause or excuse, or for want or reasonable or probable cause - Held, there was no actual malicious intention on the part of the Government in passing the wrongful order of premature retirement so as to amount to malice in fact - Held, illegal - Appeal Allowed.
