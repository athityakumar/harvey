Ganpat Ladha v Sashikant Vishnu Shinde

Supreme Court of India

21 February 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Hameedullah Beg, P.N. Bhagwati, Jaswant Singh

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 322; (1978) 2 SCC 573; AIR 1978 SC 955; 1978 (19) GLR 502; 1978 (1) RCJ 511; [1978] 3 S.C.R. 198; 1978 UJ 218

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - Constitution - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 115 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Bombay Rents, Hotel and Lodging House Rates Control Act, 1947, ss. 5 (11) (c), 11, 12(3), 12(3) (b) - Predecessor-in-interest - Rent in Arrears - Possession of Business Premises - Tenancy Claim - Predecessor-in-interest of defendant/respondent did not pay rent for the shop, rented to him - Therefore, appellant/landlord sent registered notice to quit, with a claim for arrears of rent - Respondent filed an appeal before Trial Court challenging validity of notice, which appeal was allowed - After fixation of standard rent, Respondent/tenant filed Revision application u/s. 115 of the CPC, which was dismissed by HC and case was sent back to Appellate Court for decision - Respondent's application u/art. 227 of Constitution, succeeded before Division Bench - Appellant/landlord was aggrieved by this - Hence instant appeal - Whether respondent can claim to be tenant in respect of shop, which constituted business premises u/s. 5 (11) (c) of Act - Held, s. 5 (11)(c) of Act apply, not only to residential premises, but also to business premises and therefore, on death of tenant of business premises, any member of tenant's family residing with him at time of his death would become tenant, was not correct - Legislative intent, never was to confer protection in respect of business premises on member of tenant's family residing with him at time of his death - Object was not to deprive altogether of appellant/landlord's rights in property u/s. 12 (3) (a) of Act was unwarranted - HC, without even considering or setting aside findings of Court, committed gross error in interfering, and exercising discretion, upon an application u/art. 227 of Constitution - Judgment and decree of HC set aside - Appeal allowed.
