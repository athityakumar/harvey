Rameshwar Dayal and Others v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

15 February 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, P.N. Shingal

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 326; (1978) SCC (Cr) 311; (1978) 2 SCC 518; 1978 (15) ACC 276; AIR 1978 SC 1558; 1978 ALJ 1011; 1978 CRLR 182; 1978 CrLR(SC) 182; [1978] 3 S.C.R. 59

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Constitution - Criminal - Practice and Procedure - Constitution, art. 136 - CrPC, 1973, ss. 162 , 286, 540 - Evidence Act, 1872 ,s. 60 - (A) Right of the party to contest question of fact in judgment - Held, SC would not allow the parties to contest any statement of fact mentioned in the judgment unless unerring and cogent evidence is produced to draw a converse conclusion - Opportunity of call evidence in rebuttal - (B) Whether Judges should be allowed to become witnesses in cases which they decide? - Held, Judges should not be allowed to become witnesses in cases which they decide otherwise that would lead to most anomalous results and would undermine the confidence of the people in the judiciary - Under s. 540, CrPC, the HC has got very wide powers to examine any witness it likes for the just decision of the case, but this power has to be exercised sparingly and only when the ends of justice so demands - If the HC is justified in exercising its discretion under s. 540, CrPC, the HC commits a serious error of law in not allowing the accused an opportunity to rebut the statement of the witnesses examined by the HC which caused a serious prejudice to the accused - A refusal of the same amounts not only to an infraction of the provisions of the CrPC but also of the principles of natural justice and offends the famous maxim audi alteram partem - (C) Admissibility of statements - Held, those under s. 162 CrPC are inadmissible - Appeal dismissed.
