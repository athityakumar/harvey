Shiveshwar Prasad Narain Singh & Anr. v Gharahu & Anr. Etc.

Supreme Court of India

15 November 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

D.A. Desai, P.S. Kailasam

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 104; (1979) 3 SCC 23; AIR 1979 SC 413; 1979 ALJ 49; [1979] 2 S.C.R. 296

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Uttar Pradesh Zamindar Abolition and Land reforms Act 1950 (Uttar Pradesh 1 of 1951)

Summary: Land & Property - Uttar Pradesh Zamindar Abolition and Land reforms Act, 1950, ss. 202, 18, 157, 20(a)(i), 20(b)(i) and 21(h) - Suits filed by plaintiff for possession of certain plots of land from defendants alleging that on relevant date she was holding land involved in suit as an intermediary and by become bhumidhar and she being a disabled person defendant in each case being an occupant, has become an asami and, therefore, she is entitled to recover possession from defendant in each suit - Present appeals by LRs. of original plaintiff - Whether defendant in each case would become an adhivasi u/s. 20(a)(i) or an asami by combined operation of s. 20(b)(i) and s. 21(1)(h) of Abolition Act? - Held, s. 20(a)(i) seeks to confer on a tenant of sir status of adhivasi, subject to certain exceptions enacted in section - Analysing s. 20(a)(i) it would appear that every person who is a tenant of sir on date immediately preceding date of vesting shall, unless he has become a bhumidhar u/s. 18(2) or asami u/s. 21(h) would become an adhivasi - S. 20(b)(i) provides that every person who was recorded as occupant of any land in khasra or khatauni shall, except in cases therein mentioned, become adivasi - Once in respect of a land there is not shown to be any person as envisaged by s. 20(b)(i) on land of which there is a tenant of sir or a sub-tenant as envisaged by s.20(a)(i) and (ii) respectively - A tenant of sir was more favourably placed than a mere occupant whose possession may not be referable to a valid title before enactment of Abolition Act, 1950 - Expression 'occupant' in Abolition Act, 1950 is not defined but it has been interpreted to mean a person holding land in possession or actual enjoyment - If a person is not only a tenant of sir and is also recorded as an occupant A in khasra or Khatauni of 1356 F meaning thereby that if on relevant date he was in possession and actual enjoyment of land he would become adhivasi u/s. 20(b)(i) and would be exposed to further exception enacted in s. 21(h) which if attracted, would make him asami - A tenant of sir or a sub-tenant would become u/s. 20(a)(i) or (ii), as case may be, an adhivasi - Obviously tenant of sir or sub-tenant is intermediary and by conferring adhivasi status on occupant in such circumstances intermediaries are being done away with - Held, respondent in each case was a tenant of sir in respect of land of which possession is sought by appellant and no one else was shown as occupant of such land, obviously defendant in each case would become adhivasi u/s. 20(a)(i) - Therefore, s. 21(h) would not be attracted because third clause of s. 21(h) refers to an occupant as envisaged in s. 20(b)(i) and, therefore, defendant would not become an asami - Appeal dismissed.
