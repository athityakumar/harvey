Jagir Singh v Ranbir Singh and Another

Supreme Court of India

09 November 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy, Jaswant Singh

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 468; (1979) 1 SCC 560; AIR 1979 SC 381; 1979 CRLJ 318; 1979 (15) DLT 74

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Family & Personal; Practice & Procedure; Women & Children

Summary: Criminal - Family & Personal - Women & Children - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898, s. 488 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1974, ss. 125, 127, 484(2), 484(2)(b) - General Clauses Act, 1897, s. 24 - Claim for maintenance - Sustainability - Appellant was living separately from respondents (His wife and son) and respondents filed an application for maintenance u/s. 488, CrPC, old code, before Family Court and the same was awarded - Appellant filed revision petition before Sessions Court and Sessions Judge made reference to HC to reduce award of maintenance in favour of wife, and reference was accepted by HC - Further, old code was repealed and new code was enacted in its place as CrPC, 1974, and appellant made an application before Magistrate, u/s. 127, of new Code, for cancellation of maintenance order in favour of son on ground that son had attained majority and did not suffer from any infirmity or abnormality which prevented him from maintaining himself and the same was allowed - Aggrieved son filed Revision Application before Sessions Court which was dismissed - Aggrieved son filed Revision Application before HC which was allowed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order passed by HC was sustainable - Held, there were no such limiting words as may be found as for example in s. 24 of the Act, which limits its application to an order, rule, etc; 'so far as it was not inconsistent with provisions re-enacted' and did not mean that statutory instruments made under old Code and which were inconsistent with provisions of new Code continued to be effective - - SC, therefore, hold that order for maintenance made in favour of son should be deemed to be an order made u/s. 125 of new Code and that it did not automatically ceased to be effective on coming into force of new Code - HC arrived at that conclusion and thought that it was sufficient to hold in favour of son and allowed Revision Application - SC did not think that HC was right in stopping there - HC should had further considered question whether order for maintenance which was deemed to be an order u/s. 125 of new Code could not be cancelled under provisions of s. 127 of new Code - Once order u/s. 488, was deemed to be an order u/s. 125 of new Code, it must be so deemed for all purposes including application of s. 127 of new Code - S. 127 provides for consequential orders upon proof of change in circumstance of any person receiving, u/s. 125, monthly allowance, or ordered under same section to pay monthly allowance to his wife, child, father or mother, as case may be and admitted attainment of majority of son and changed of law were surely circumstances which entitled appellant to had order in favour of son cancelled - Thus, appeal was allowed and judgment of HC was set aside - Appeal allowed.
