Rambai Manjanath Nayak & Ors. v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

17 November 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

J.S. Verma, A.S. Anand, Yogeshwar Dayal, M.H. Kania, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 1164; (1992) 4 SCC 742; AIR 1993 SC 342; 1992 (108) CTR 294; 1993 (49) DLT 1; [1993] 201 ITR 422; JT 1992 (Supp) SC 562; 1993 (2) RCR(Rent) 78; 1992(3) SCALE 166; [1992] Supp3 S.C.R. 56; [1993] 66 TAXMAN 486; 1993 (2) TLR 50

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 269 F (6), 269 K, 269 D(2)(9) and 269 AB, chap. XXA - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, - Acquisition of property - (A) Pleas that, whether right to continue in possession of acquired property as tenant of Govt. subsists ? - As acquisition is not of tenancy right and it is not an encumbrance - (B) Nature of proceedings u/s. 269 K of 1961 Act - Held, not sustainable, right surviving with occupant is only to claim share in compensation that too against transferee - Are akin to acquisition proceedings under 1894 Act - Appeal dismissed.
