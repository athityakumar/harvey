Satrucharla Chandrasekhar Raju v Vyricherla Pradeep Kumar Dev and another

Supreme Court of India

04 September 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

G.N. Ray, K. Jayachandra Reddy

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 1255; (1992) 4 SCC 404; AIR 1992 SC 1959; JT 1992 (5) SC 417; 1992(2) SCALE 458; [1992] Supp1 S.C.R. 408; 1993 (1) UJ 51

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Durgah Khawaja Saheb Act, 1955, Civil Services (Classification, Control and Appeal) Rules

Summary: Constitution - Parliament & Legislature - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 191(1)(a) - Disqualification of M L A - Accused of holding 'office of profit' - Appellant, teacher in a primary school supported by a registered Society - Govt. had control over appointing authority but not over teachers - Held, that appellant cannot be said to be holding an office of profit under Govt. for purpose of contesting election for legislative assembly as he wasn't appointed by Govt. - Question of any conflict between his duties and interests as an elected members does not arise since it cannot be said that he, as a teacher, can be subjected to any kind of pressure by Govt. which has neither power to appoint him nor to remove him from service - Appeal allowed.
