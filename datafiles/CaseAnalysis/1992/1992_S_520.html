Commissioner of Income Tax v Sun Engineering Works Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

17 September 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

A.S. Anand, Yogeshwar Dayal

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 611; (1992) 4 SCC 363; AIR 1993 SC 43; AIR 1992 SCW 2600; 1992 (107) CTR 209; [1992] 198 ITR 297; JT 1992 (5) SC 543; 1992(2) SCALE 591; [1992] Supp1 S.C.R. 732; 1993 TAXLR 58; [1992] 64 TAXMAN 442

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Constitution - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 147 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 141 - A/ys 1960-61 and 1961-62 - Reassessment - Scope - Assessee filed return for a/y 1960-61 on 17.11.1960 showing a loss of Rs. 36,418/- and for a/y 1961-62 on 4.12.1961 declaring a loss of Rs. 24,314/- - Income Tax Officer (ITO) held that return filed beyond time - Assessee challenged said order of ITO before Appellate Asst. Commissioner who held dismissed said appeals - Assessee did not prefer any further appeal from the order of the Appellate Asst. Commissioner and thus the orders of the ITO became final - Subsequently, assessee filed a disclosure petition in respect of some Hundi loans and a settlement was arrived at between the assessee and the Revenue as a result whereof, the assessee became assessable for the disclosed sum of Rs. 27,000 for the a/y 1960-61 and for the sum of Rs. 9,000 for the a/y 1961-62 - ITO considered the said amounts as 'escaped income' - Appellate Asst. Commissioner allowed the appeal filed against said order and directed the ITO that in the reassessment proceedings not only the redetermine the loss as per the original loss returns and set it off against the escaped income from other sources but also that the un-absorbed loss, if any, should be carried forward and set off against the income in the subsequent years - On appeal, Tribunal held that since the earlier decision of the ITO had been upheld in appeal by the Appellate Asst. Commissioner and the assessee had not taken up the matter in any further appeal or revision, the order of the ITO had been acquired finality - HC held that in the proceedings u/s. 147 of the Act relating to the income which had 'escaped assessment' viz., Rs. 27,000 and Rs. 9,000 the original 'loss returns' in the original assessment proceedings could not be wholly ignored and in order to determine what income had 'escaped taxation', the ITO could not ignore losses which the assessee had suffered in the relevant years in question as reflected in the 'loss returns' and the same were required to be computed for the purpose of determining the 'income which had escaped assessment' - Hence, the instant appeal - Where an item unconnected with the escapement of income has been concluded finally against the assessee how for in reassessment on an escaped item of income it was open to the assessee to seek a review of the concluded item for the purpose of computation of the escaped income? - Held, the tribunal rightly found that the loss which the assessee wanted to be set off against the 'escaped income' could not be allowed to be so set off because in the original assessment proceedings, no 'set off' was claimed or permitted and the original assessment had acquired finality when the appeal against the order of assessment failed before the Appellate Asst. Commissioner and the assessee took no further steps to agitate the issue - The Tribunal was also right in concluding that the item which the assessee wanted to be taken into account in the proceedings u/s. 147 of the Act were unconnected with the escapement of income - The HC clearly fell in error in holding otherwise - Since the original assessment had been concluded finally against the assessee, it was not permissible for the assessee in the reassessment proceedings to seek a review/revision of the concluded assessment for the purpose of computation of the escaped income - The HC clearly fell in error by permitting the assessee to reagitate, in the reassessment proceedings u/s. 147(a) of the Act, the finally concluded assessment proceedings and to grant to him relief in respect of items not only earlier rejected, but also unconnected with the escapement of income by assuming as if the original assessment had not been concluded or was 'still open' - Therefore, in the reassessment proceedings it was not open to an assessee to seek a review of the concluded item, unconnected with the escapement of income, for the purpose of computation of the escaped income - Impugned orders of the HC set aside and order of the tribunal restored - Appeals allowed.
