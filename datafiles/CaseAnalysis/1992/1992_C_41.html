Baikuntha Nath Das And Anr. v Chief District Medical Officer, Baripada and

Supreme Court of India

19 February 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, L.M. Sharma, Veeraswami Ramaswami

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 1228; (1992) 2 SCC 299; (1993) SCC (L&S) 521; AIR 1992 SC 1020; 1992 (21) ATC 649; 1992 (73) CLT 665; 1992 (64) FLR 1090; JT 1992 (2) SC 1; 1992 LabIC 942; 1992 (1) LLJ 784; 1992 (1) LLN 902; 1992(1) SCALE 428; [1992] 1 S.C.R. 836; 1992 (1) SLJ 177; 1992 (2) SLR 2; 1992 (2) UPLBEC 816

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service

Keywords: Fundamental Rule

Summary: Service - Constitution - Orissa Service Code, 1939, r.71, arts. 14, 226, 311 and 309 - Fundamental Rules, r. 56(j) - Compulsory retirement - (A) Nature of order, punitive - (B) Subjective satisfaction - Formulation - (C) Un-communicated adverse remarks - Consideration of, while passing order - Held, (A) Not a punishment, it implies no stigma nor suggestion of misbehavior - (B) Govt. ought to consider entire service record, more importance however to be given to record of later years - (C) Can be considered, principles of natural justice do not apply to order of compulsory retirement -

Appeal dismissed.
