Life Insurance Corpn. Of India And Ors. Etc. v Prof. Manubhai D. Shah Etc.

Supreme Court of India

22 July 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, M.M. Punchhi

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 1106; (1992) 3 SCC 637; AIR 1993 SC 171; AIR 1992 SCW 3099; JT 1992 (4) SC 181; 1992(2) SCALE 60; [1992] 3 S.C.R. 595; 1992 (2) UJ 481

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Insurance

Keywords: Freedom Of Speech And Expression

Summary: Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 19(1)(a) and 19(2) - Life Insurance Corporation Act, 1956, s. 6(1) - Cinematograph Act, 1952, s. 5 B - Freedom of speech and expression - Exhibition of film - Duty to publish - Views of a person criticised in a magazine - Refusal to publish - His counter view on criticism - Held, person has right to get his counter - In magazine where critics published - Broadly construed freedom of speech and expression - Onus to show reasonability of restrictions lies on authorities.
