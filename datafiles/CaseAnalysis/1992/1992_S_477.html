Ram Krishna Verma and Others v State of Uttar Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

31 March 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, N.M. Kasliwal

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 949; (1992) 2 SCC 620; 1992 (2) ACC 122; AIR 1992 SC 1888; AIR 1992 SCW 2141; 1992 ALJ 1; JT 1992 (2) SC 545; 1992(1) SCALE 762; [1992] 2 S.C.R. 378; 1992 (2) UJ 249

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Constitution; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1939, ss. 68-C and 68-D - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, ss. 80, 98, 100(4) and 217(2)(e) - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 13, 19(6) and 142(1) - Whether grant of permits u/s. 80 of Act on Muzaffarnagar-Chausana; Ghaziabad to Shahdara; Saharanpur to Ghaziabad covered and partly overlapping nationalised routes to respondents valid in law? - Supreme Court has consistently held that draft scheme u/s. 68-C and approved u/s. 68-D of Chap. IV-A of repealed Act (Chap. VI of Act) is a law and has overriding effect over Chap. IV of repealed Act; right of private operators to apply for and to obtain permits u/chap. IV of repealed Act has been frozen and prohibited, hence, nationalisation of Saharanpur-Shahdara-Delhi route approved and published on 29-09-1959 became final and to that extent cannot be said to have been quashed in Shri Chand's case [1985 Indlaw SC 254] - Fresh draft scheme u/s. 68-C, D/- 13-02-1986 must, therefore, be construed to be only in relation to 50 existing operators as per directions ultimately emerged in Jeewan Nath Wahal case - On harmonious construction of ss. 217(2)(e) and 100(4) of Act, draft scheme published u/s. 68-C of repealed Act would stand lapsed only if it is not approved within 1 year from date when Act came into force i.e. 01-07-1989, by which date it was pending before hearing authority and 1 year had not expired, therefore, hearing authority, wrongly concluded that draft scheme stood lapsed and High Court also equally committed illegality following its earlier view which now stood overruled by this court in Krishna Kumar case [1991 Indlaw SC 802]; by operation of s. 98, Chap. VI overrides Chap. V and other law and shall have effect notwithstanding anything inconsistent therewith contained in Chap. V or any other law for time being in force or any instrument having effect by virtue of such law, result is that even under Act existing scheme under repealed Act or made under Chap. VI of Act shall have overriding effect on Chap. V notwithstanding any right given to private operators in Chap. V of Act; no corridor protection to private operators is permissible - Held, approved scheme D/- 29/09/1959 on Saharanpur-Delhi route shall continue to be valid scheme under Act; U.P. State Road Transport Corporation alone shall have exclusive right to ply their stage carriages on said route and Bulandshahar-Delhi route/areas or portions thereof; fresh draft scheme D/- 13-02-1986 had not been lapsed and would continue to be in operation and it would be confined only to 50 operators; nationalisation of Saharanpur-Delhi route by publication of approved scheme on 29-09-1959 is operating total exclusion of every private operator except U.P. State Road Transport Corporation and 50 operators including appellants herein objections were upheld by HC in first instance and merged in judgment of Supreme Court in Jeewan Nath Wahal case [Civil Appeal No. 1616 of 1968]; equally of Bulandshahar to Delhi route u/s. 80, no pvt. operator has right to apply for and obtain permits to ply stage carriages on approved or notified route/routes or areas or portion thereof; grant of permit to all respondents 7 to 285 pvt. operators in C A No. 1198 of 1192 or any others u/s. 80 on respective routes or part, or portion thereof provide transport service is clearly illegal and without jurisdiction - Appeals allowed.
