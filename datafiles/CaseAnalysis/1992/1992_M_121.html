Indian Oil Corporation v Municipal Corporation, Jullundhar and Others

Supreme Court of India

20 October 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

A.S. Anand, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 686; (1993) 1 SCC 333; AIR 1993 SC 844; 1993 (1) ALT(SC) 44; 1993 (1) AnWR 123; JT 1992 (6) SC 71; 1992(2) SCALE 839; [1992] Supp2 S.C.R. 62; 1993 (1) UJ 160

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Punjab Municipal Corporation Act, 1976

Summary: Constitution - Municipalities & Local Governments - Punjab Municipal Corporation Act, 1976, s. 113 - Constitution of India, 1950, Sch. VII, List II, Entry 52 - (A) Whether s. 113 of Municipal Act, 1976 is constitutionally valid? - Municipality derives its power to tax from State Legislature and cannot have any authority more extensive than authority of State Legislature, since State Legislature, in view of Entry 52 of List II, Sch. VII is competent to levy a tax only on entry of goods for 'consumption, use or sale' into a local area, Municipality cannot, under a legislation enacted in exercise of power conferred by Entry 52 of List II, have power to levy tax in respect of goods brought into local area for purposes other than consumption, use or sale - Held, expression 'imported into city' in s. 113 has to be interpreted as meaning 'imported into municipal limits for purposes of consumption, use or sale' only, thus, construed in limited sense, s. 113 is not ultra vires Entry 52 of List II of Sch VII - (B) Whether, in present case, transaction within municipal limits reflected in category (4) can be treated to be sale to dealers at depot or it is only in nature of re-export? - Terms of agreement executed between IOC and its dealers show that in respect of goods which were re-exported by IOC to its dealers outside municipal limits, risk, till delivery of goods at premises of dealers, continue to remain with IOC which was also obliged to make good any loss during transit and therefore transaction by IOC with its dealers or agents as reflected in category four did not amount to any sale at depot within municipal limits of Municipal Corporation - Held, applying observations made in Burmah-Shell Oil Storage case [1962 Indlaw SC 295] and in Parekh Automobiles Ltd's case [1989 Indlaw SC 135] and on basis of material on record, transaction covered by category (4), viz, where petroleum products transported to depot of IOC are meant for export from its depot inside octroi limits to outside municipal limits to its dealers for sale, use and consumption by persons other than IOC, outside octroi limits, is a transaction of re-export and that appropriation of goods does not take place at depot but at outlets of dealers or agents outside municipal limits octroi duty is, therefore, not chargeable on such a transaction - Appeal allowed.
