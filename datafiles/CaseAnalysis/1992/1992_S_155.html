K.P.M. Basheer Etc. v State Of Karnataka And Anr. Etc.

Supreme Court of India

28 February 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Ratnavel Pandian, Kuldip Singh, R.M. Sahai

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 346; (1992) 2 SCC 295; (1992) SCC (Cr) 387; AIR 1992 SC 1353; 1992 (1) CCR 1031; 1992 (1) Crimes 996; 1992 CRLJ 1927; 1992 CrLR(SC) 274; 1992 (38) ECC 260; JT 1992 (3) SC 610; 1992(1) SCALE 525; [1992] 1 S.C.R. 1075

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Summary: Constitution - Criminal - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - COFEPOSA, 1974, ss. 3, 7 - (A) Detention order - Execution after five months - Action not taken u/s. 7 - Sufficient cause for not taking action not shown - Address given by detenu alleged to be fictitious by detaining authority but same not disclosed in their counter - Detenu appearing before customs authority twice - No attempt to arrest him made - Whether order liable to be set aside? - (B) New plea - Legal plea - Not taken in memorandum of appeal - Whether can be raised before Supreme Court? - Held, (A) Live and proximate link between grounds of detention and purpose thereof snapped by delay -Liable to be set aside - Can be raised even though not specifically taken in memorandum of appeal - Appeal allowed.
