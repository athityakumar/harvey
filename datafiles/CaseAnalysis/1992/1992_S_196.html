Hotel Balaji and Others v State of Andhra Pradesh Others

Supreme Court of India

22 October 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Ranganathan, B.P. Jeevan Reddy, Veeraswami Ramaswami

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 695; (1993) Supp4 SCC 536; AIR 1993 SC 1048; JT 1992 (2) SC 182; 1992(2) SCALE 924; 1993 (88) STC 98; [1992] Supp2 S.C.R. 182

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Indirect Tax; Parliament & Legislature; Sales Tax

Keywords: Competence Of The Legislature

Summary: (A) Sales Tax - Indirect Tax - Parliament & Legislature - Constitution - Gujarat Sales Tax Act, 1969 - Uttar Pradesh Sales Tax Act, 1948 - Andhra Pradesh General Sales Tax Act, 1957 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Levy of purchase tax - Ultra vires - Appellants filed instant appeals taking a cue from decision of SC in Goodyear India Ltd. v. State of Haryana 1989 Indlaw SC 269, that corresponding provisions of the 1969 Act, the 1948 Act and the 1957 Act were ultra vires powers of State Legislature as they seek to levy a purchase tax in certain circumstances - Whether conclusion in Goodyear India Ltd. v. State of Haryana 1989 Indlaw SC 269 was rightly reached in context of provisions of statutes there considered or would need a fresh consideration - Held, attempt of appellants to undertake an analysis of what would eventually happen to purchased goods where purchaser was consumer and on basis to suggest that legislature really intends to tax consumption, production or consignment was ingenious but farfetched, artificial and unrealistic - Hence, marginal title to provisions indicates that their direct purpose was to levy a tax on purchases effected in State in certain circumstances and tax was couched as a tax on all goods and on raw or processing materials and consumable stores which was designated as a purchase tax - Thus, ambit of power to levy a tax in respect of sale of goods was very wide and would cover any tax which had a nexus with sale or purchase of goods including a last purchase in State - Hence, levy was only of a purchase tax and such a levy was within domain of State Legislature - Appeals dismissed.

(B) Sales Tax - Indirect Tax - Parliament & Legislature - Constitution - Gujarat Sales Tax Act, 1969, s. 15B - Gujarat Sales Tax, Rules, 1970, r. 14E - Constitution of India, 1950 - S. 15B - Constitutionality - Whether substitution of s. 15B of the 1969 Act with that of the 1990 Act was constitutionally valid - Held, s. 15B of the 1969 Act did not speak of nor does it refer in any manner to movement sale or disposal of manufactured goods - However, it was equally immaterial whether manufacturer consigns them to his own depots or depots of his agents outside State - Therefore, tax imposed by s. 15B of the 1969 Act could not be characterised as a consignment tax - However, r. 14E of the Rules provides for set off etc. in case manufactured goods were sold within State which was not available if manufactured goods were disposed of otherwise than by way of sale or were consigned to manufacturer's own depots or outside State - Thus, it was difficult to see how could it be said that by reading r. 42E of the Rules into s. 15B of the 1969 Act levy becomes a consignment tax - Appeals dismissed.

(C) Sales Tax - Indirect Tax - Parliament & Legislature - Constitution - Uttar Pradesh Sales Tax (Amendment) Act, 1992, s. 3-AAAA - Constitution of India, 1950 - S. 3-AAAA -Retrospective effect - Whether decision of HC that s. 3-AAAA substituted by the 1992 Act with retrospective effect was valid - Held, only two conditions stipulated in 3-AAAA of the 1992 Act were that if purchased goods were sold within State or sold in course of inter-State trade or commerce, tax under it was not payable - Policy of legislature was not to tax same goods twice over - However, fact that purchased goods were consigned by purchaser to his own depots or agents outside State makes no difference to nature and character of tax - By doing so, he could not escape even one-time tax upon goods purchased, which was policy of Legislature - Constitutionality of s. 3-AAAA of the 1992 Act ought to be judged on its own language and both before and after the 1992 Act represents a perfectly valid piece of legislation - It was relatable to and fully warranted by Entry 54 of List II of Constitution - Appeals dismissed.

(D) Sales Tax - Indirect Tax - Parliament & Legislature - Constitution - Andhra Pradesh General Sales Tax Act, 1957, s. 6A - Constitution of India, 1950 - Purchase of milk -Purchase tax - Liability - Whether milk purchased by appellant from dealers exclusively dealing in milk, would be liable to pay purchase tax u/s. 6A of the Act - Held, while exempting sale or purchase of any specified class of goods Govt. was empowered to specify whether exemption operates at all points or any specified point or points in series of sales or purchases of successive dealers - Several notifications were issued by Govt. from time to time exempting certain dealers or exempting certain goods at the point of sale or purchase - It was not a general exemption but a qualified one - Thus, exemption at point of sale by a particular category of persons could not be construed as operating to exempt purchase tax u/s. 6A as well in all cases - Appeals dismissed.

(E) Sales Tax - Indirect Tax - Parliament & Legislature - Constitution - Gujarat Sales Tax Act, 1969 - Uttar Pradesh Sales Tax Act, 1948 - Andhra Pradesh General Sales Tax Act, 1957 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Decision in Goodyear India Ltd. v. State of Haryana 1989 Indlaw SC 269 - Applicability - Whether decision in Goodyear India Ltd. v. State of Haryana 1989 Indlaw SC 269 was applicable to instant case - Held, tax purports to be and was in truth a purchase tax levied on purchase price of raw material purchased by a manufacturer - In certain situations sale of manufactured goods within State, inter-State sale and export sale of manufactured goods was waived and in other cases, it was not - Thus, ratio of Goodyear India Ltd. v. State of Haryana 1989 Indlaw SC 269 governs said case and it was so found - It was clear that correctness of decision in Goodyear India Ltd. v. State of Haryana 1989 Indlaw SC 269 was not questioned before Bench and that was why Bench took care to specifically advert to and record said circumstance - Appeals dismissed.
