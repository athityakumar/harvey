Ramvir Singh v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

13 November 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Venkatachala, K. Ramaswamy, L.M. Sharma

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 729; (1993) Supp2 SCC 262; (1993) SCC (L&S) 436; (2009) 3 SCC 97; (2009) 1 SCC (L&S) 581; 1993 (24) ATC 255; JT 1992 (Supp) SC 378; 1992(3) SCALE 160; 1993 (1) UJ 119

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Service - Administrative Tribunals Act, 1985 - Whether Central Administrative Tribunal was unjustified in upholding action of Hospital authorities in matter of disturbing inter se seniority of appellant and respondent 4? - Held, when non-selection of respondent 4 by Hospital authorities for any of three newly created posts of Medico Social Workers in Hospital in select list prepared by them on basis of interview held for all candidates including respondent 4 on 01-02-1979 and appointment of appellant and two others made thereon was not questioned by respondent 4, it is difficult to think of basis on which respondent 4, who was appointed subsequently in one of those posts on a fresh application made by him in that regard, could claim seniority over appellant, earlier appointee in one of those posts - Therefore, Order of Hospital authorities by which date of appointment of respondent 4 is preponed from 11-04-1980 to 25-09-1979, date of appointment of appellant, so as to enable them to treat former as senior to latter in their cadre has to be regarded as an arbitrary one if not a whimsical one - CAT was also fully unjustified - Appeal allowed.
