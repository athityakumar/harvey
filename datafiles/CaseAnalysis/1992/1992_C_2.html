K.A. Anthappai v C. Ahammed

Supreme Court of India

05 May 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Agrawal, T.K. Thommen

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 1193; (1992) 3 SCC 277; AIR 1992 SC 1696; JT 1992 (4) SC 65; 1992 (2) KerLJ 376; 1992 (2) KLT 284; 1992 (1) RCJ 515; 1992 (2) RCR(Rent) 270; 1992 (2) RentLR 259; 1992(1) SCALE 1055; [1992] 3 S.C.R. 70; 1992 (2) UJ 112

Case Digest

Subject: Environment, Wildlife & Animal; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Code of Civil Procedure 1908

Summary: Rent Control - Kerala Buildings (Lease and Rent Control) Act, 1965, ss. 11, 11(3) second provision and 20 - Eviction - Bona fide need - Claim of landlord that he needs building bona fide for his personnel occupation - Can it be negatived on ground that building require repairs and alterations before landlord can occupy same or that he was living with his son - Tenant depending for his livelihood mainly from trade or business carried on in disputed premises - Passing of decree of eviction by appellate authority without considering whether tenant was entitled to invoke protection of second proviso to s. 11(3) - No proper - Held, claim cannot be said to be false and negatived on such grounds - Decree of eviction, not proper - Matter remanded - Appeal allowed.
