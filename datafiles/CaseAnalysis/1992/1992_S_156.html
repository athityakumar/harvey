Karnataka State Private College Stop-Gaplecturers Associatio v State Of Karnataka And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

29 January 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Sahai, S. Ratnavel Pandian, Kuldip Singh

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 1250; (1992) 2 SCC 29; (1992) SCC (L&S) 394; AIR 1992 SC 677; 1992 (20) ATC 190; 1992 (3) CCC 785; 1992 (1) CLR 402; 1992 (64) FLR 757; JT 1992 (1) SC 373; 1992 LabIC 575; 1993 (2) LLJ 831; 1992 (2) LLN 434; 1992(1) SCALE 198; [1992] 1 S.C.R. 397; 1992 (1) SLJ 215; 1992 (1) SLR 643; 1992 (1) UJ 521; 1992 (2) UPLBEC 1110

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 32 and 39(d) - Order issued by Education and Youth Services Department of State of Karnataka for Ad hoc appointment - (A) Teachers appointed temporarily for years with break of day or two every three months by privately managed degree colleges - Colleges receiving cent per cent grants-in-aid from State Govt. - Regulation of teachers - Directions issued to State Govt. by Supreme Court - Equal pay for equal work - Temporary teacher - Legality of payment of fixed salary ten rupees less than minimum payable to regular employee - Whether fixation of such salary violative of art. 14 ? - Held, (A) Provision of one day's break in service is struck down as ultra vires - (B) Orders for payment of fixed salary, declared invalid - (C) Any teacher appointed temporarily shall be continued till purpose of appointment exhausts, etc. - Is arbitrary and violative of art. 14 - Practice of private colleges - Deprecated - Petition allowed.
