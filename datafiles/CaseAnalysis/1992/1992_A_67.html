State Of U.P. v Ashok Kumar Srivastava

Supreme Court of India

14 January 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 107; (1992) 2 SCC 86; (1992) SCC (Cr) 241; AIR 1992 SC 840; 1992 (1) CCR 602; 1992 (1) Crimes 576; 1992 CRLJ 1104; 1992 CRLR 144; JT 1992 (1) SC 340; 1992(1) SCALE 149; [1992] 1 S.C.R. 37

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 3 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 34 and 300 - (A) Appreciation of circumstantial evidence - Meaning of rule that cumulative effect of all facts established must be consistent only with hypothesis of guilt - (B) Murder - Bride burning case - Accused, husband and in-laws of deceased unhappy over dowry - Ill treatment and torture to deceased before and on fateful day proved - None of accused helping deceased while she was on fire - All accused absconding after incident - Presence of eye-witnesses natural - No reason for them to falsely implicate accused - Whether want of their name in F.I.R be doubted as an evidence - (C) Whether accused be liable to be convicted u/ss. 34 and 300 - Held, (A) does not mean that prosecution must meet any and every hypothesis put by accused - (B)Their evidence cannot be doubtful for want of their name in First Information Report - (C) Accused held liable to be convicted - Appeal allowed.
