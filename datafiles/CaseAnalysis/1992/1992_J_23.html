Union of India Others v Jain Spinners Limited and Another

Supreme Court of India

10 September 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

P.B. Sawant, M.N. Venkatachaliah, N.P. Singh

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 1209; (1992) 4 SCC 389; AIR 1992 SC 1993; 1992 (43) ECR 1; 1992 (61) E.L.T. 321; JT 1992 (5) SC 386; 1992(2) SCALE 541; [1992] Supp1 S.C.R. 484

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax; Land & Property

Summary: Excise - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 133 and 226 - Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944, s. 11 B - Constitution - Excise Duty - (A) Refund - Duty deposited in High Court - Withdrawal by Govt. - Permitted, suject to refund if assessee succeeds - Subsequent retrospective amendment of Act prohibiting refund in case duty has been passed on to others by assessee - (B) Powers of HC - Levy of excise duty - Passing of interlocutory orders injuncting statutory authorities from recovering duty - Ignoring amended provisions of statute - Held, (A) Assessee cannot get refund only on basis of order of HC without fulfilling condition under amended provisions - (B) Supreme Court had given note of caution to courts - Appeal allowed.
