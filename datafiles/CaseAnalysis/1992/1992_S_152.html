Paras Ram v State Of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

20 October 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Bharucha, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 894; (1992) 4 SCC 662; (1993) SCC (Cr) 13; AIR 1993 SC 1212; AIR 1993 SCW 169; 1993 CRLJ 416; JT 1992 (Supp) SC 472; [1992] Supp2 S.C.R. 55

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Defence & Security Forces; Socio-Economic

Summary: Criminal - Terrorist and Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Act, 1987, ss. 5, 11 and 12 - Arms Act, 1959, s. 25 - Arms Rules, 1962, sch. I, category III(a) - (A) Designated Court under TADA Act - Jurisdiction - Offence u/s. 25 of Arms Act - (B) Term 'revolvers and pistols' in category III of sch. 1 of Arms Rules - If include country made pistol - (C) TADA Act, s. 5 - Applicability - Words 'arms and ammunition' - Person found in possession of only country made pistol and not any ammunition- Can provisions of s. 5 be applied to him - (D) TADA Act, s. 12 - Charge for offence u/s. 25(1 B)(a) of Arms Act - Evidence - Police officials giving evidence not shown to be hostile to accused - Evidence not shaken in cross-examination - Whether doubt can be raised about its credibility ? - Confirmation of conviction - Held, (A) Is exclusively triable by Designated Court - (B) Include country made pistol - (C) Words Should be read conjunctively - Cannot be applied to him - (D) Cannot be ipso facto doubted - Conviction confirmed - Appeal dismissed.
