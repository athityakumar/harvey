Dhiraj Lal R. Vohra v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

09 December 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 26; (1993) Supp3 SCC 453; 1993 (49) DLT 343; 1997 (68) ECR 778; 1993 (66) E.L.T. 551; JT 1993 (Supp) SC 375; 1992(3) SCALE 375; [1992] Supp3 S.C.R. 494; 1993 (1) UJ 231

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Customs Act, 1962, ss. 15(1), 31, 46 and 58 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 19(1)(g), 21, 32, 265 and 300A - (A) Whether rate of duty levied should be one applicable when ship entered Indian water or one applicable when bill of entry is presented u/s. 46 ? - Held, relevant provisions make it clear that due date to calculate rate of duty applicable to any imported goods shall be rate and valuation in force on date on which bill of entry in respect of such goods is presented - (B) Whether s. 15(1) of Act is ultra vires of provisions of Constitution ? - Further held, issue is no longer res integra since court has previously upheld validity of s. 15(1) - Respondent may consider case of petitioner sympathetically and pass an appropriate order - Petitions dismissed.
