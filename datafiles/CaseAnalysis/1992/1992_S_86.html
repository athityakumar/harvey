Shyam Singh v Collector, District Hamirpur, U. P. and Others

Supreme Court of India

25 September 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

N.P. Singh, M.N. Venkatachaliah, P.B. Sawant

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 645; (1993) Supp1 SCC 693; [1993] 76 Comp Cas 525; 1994 RD 30; 1992(2) SCALE 692; [1992] Supp1 S.C.R. 862; 1993 (1) UJ 179

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance

Keywords: Uttar Pradesh Agricultural Credit Act, 1973

Summary: Banking & Finance - Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Uttar Pradesh Agricultural Credit Act, 1973, ss. 2(a)(ii), 12, 11, 10-B and 11-A - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908, ss. 51, O. 21 rr.21, 30 - Limitation Act, 1963 - U.P. Zamindari Abolition And Land Reforms Act, 1950, s. 279 - Proceedings initiated by Bank for sale of 22 acres of land, mortgaged in favour of Bank in connection with a loan for purchase of a tractor, hypothecated with Bank - Bank approached concerned Tehsildar for recovery outstanding amount till that date u/s. 10-B of U.P. Agricultural Credit Act, 1973 - Tractor attached and was taken away by Tehsildar - Other proceeding initiated u/s. 11-A of U.P. Agricultural Credit Act, 1973 for recovery of same amount - Notice issued to sell 22 acres of lands u/s. 279 of U.P. Zamindari Abolition and Land Reforms Act, 1950 - Appellant saying that first sale proceeds of tractor be adjusted and steps for sale of agricultural land be taken only thereafter - Plea was rejected by Revenue Authorities as well as by High Court - Whether three different modes for realisation of dues of Bank under provisions of Act can proceed simultaneously? - Court held, power of decree-holder is, however, subject to exercise by court of a judicial discretion vested in it under O. 21, r.21 of CPC, 1908 - Right of Bank to follow one or other modes, separately or simultaneously be recognised, but that right does not extend to extent of selling different moveable or immovable properties of debtor under different provisions and through different procedures without ascertaining whether amount due has already been realised by sale of property already attached in proceedings which were initiated for purpose - Court should, on facts and circumstances of a particular case, decide as to whether simultaneous proceedings should be permitted against debtor for realisation of same amount - Appeal allowed.
