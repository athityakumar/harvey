M.J. Exports Limited And Anr. v Customs, Excise And Gold (Control) Appellatetribunal, Bombay

Supreme Court of India

14 May 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Ranganathan, Veeraswami Ramaswami, Yogeshwar Dayal

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 1087; (1993) Supp1 SCC 169; AIR 1992 SC 2014; 1992 (41) ECR 203; 1992 (60) E.L.T. 163; JT 1992 (3) SC 398; 1992(1) SCALE 1145; [1992] 3 S.C.R. 300

Case Digest

Subject: Customs

Keywords: Warehouse, Bombay Prohibition Act 1949

Summary: Customs - Indirect Tax - Customs Act, 1962, ss. 2(33), 51, 68, 69, 74, 113(d) and 114 - Imports and Exports (Control) Act, 1947, s. 3 - Export Control Order, 1988, cl. 15(g) - Constitution, art. 133 - (A) Customs - Penalty - Import of Life Saving Equipments - Re-export to foreign country is prohibited as under terms of Open General Licence - Import-export policy does not envisage re-export of goods belonging to said category - Goods are 'prohibited' goods within meaning of s. 2(33) - Confiscation and levy of penalty - Legality - (B) Goods imported for 'home consumption' - Can be re-exported - Whether s. 69 make warehousing an imperative pre-condition for exporting the imported goods - (C) Export Control Order, 1988, cl. 15(g) - Scope - (D) Constitution - New Plea - That goods sought to be exported do not conform to description in the bill of entry for export - Raised for first time before SC as it involves investigation of facts - Held, was legal - Does not make - Sub-clause (g) cannot be interpreted to mean that imported goods cannot be exported unless they are cleared, at the time of import, under a bond for re-export - Cannot be allowed to be raised - Appeal dismissed.
