Arbind Singh and another v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

03 November 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, K. Ramaswamy, M.M. Punchhi

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 168; (1994) SCC (Cr) 1418; (1995) Supp4 SCC 416; AIR 1994 SC 1068; 1994 (2) BLJ 332; 1994 CRLJ 1227

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 300 and 201 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 3 - Murder - Accused husband convicted for causing death of his wife and other family members convicted under s. 201 for destroying her dead body - Conviction based only on basis of evidence of child aged 5 years, daughter of deceased - Held, accused should be acquitted from charge of murder by giving him benefit of doubt - Other family members also acquitted of charge under s. 201 in absence of knowledge that death was not natural.
