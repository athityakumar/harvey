Management Of M/S. Puri Urban Cooperative Bank v Madhusudan Sahu And Anr.

Supreme Court of India

29 April 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

M.M. Punchhi, L.M. Sharma

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 419; (1992) 3 SCC 323; (1992) SCC (L&S) 675; AIR 1992 SC 1452; 1992 (20) ATC 753; 1992 (74) CLT 50; JT 1992 (3) SC 290; 1992 (2) LLJ 6; [1992] 2 S.C.R. 977; 1992 (4) SLR 460

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Termination, Challenged

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Termination - Reinstatement - Challenged - Respondent employee was engaged as appraiser appellant Bank- Respondent was terminated from services - Respondent made a reference to Labour Court - Labour Court set aside the order of termination and held disentitled to back wages and ordered respondents reinstatement in service - HC affirmed the orders of Labour Court - Hence instant appeal - Whether the order recorded by HC was just and proper.

Held, respondent claims to be workman as commonly understood, he was not 'employed' as such, so as to establish master and servant relationship, which could warrant re-union in the event of disruption, by intervention of Labour Court. The allegation of the Bank before Labour Court, as well as here, that respondent is a reputed goldsmith and remained gainfully employed so as to disentitle him any back wages, which appealed to the Labour Court, has remained uncontroverted. It also remains uncontroverted that Bank has, on its approved list, other such like appraisers and it is not obligatory for Bank to allot work to respondent or any other, at all. Additionally, in no event can he ask for work, or periodic remuneration or idling wages. These particulars, not by themselves, but in the totality or circumstances indicate lack of master and servant relationship. Thus, in view of these jurisdictional facts, it is difficult to uphold the view of HC and that of the Labour Court that any master and servant relationship stood established in engaging respondent as an appraiser of ornaments. Hence, orders of HC and that of Labour Court are set aside. Appeal allowed.
