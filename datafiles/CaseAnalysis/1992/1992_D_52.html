Mohan Lal Tripathi v District Magistrate, Rae Bareilly And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

15 May 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Sahai, N.M. Kasliwal

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 1264; (1992) 4 SCC 80; AIR 1993 SC 2042; 1993 ALJ 994; JT 1992 (4) SC 363; 1992(1) SCALE 1191; [1992] 3 S.C.R. 338; 1992 (2) UJ 125; 1993 (1) UPLBEC 35

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Constitution - Municipalities & Local Governments - Election - Contitution of India, arts. 136, 226 and 326 - Uttar Pradesh Municipalities Act, 1916, ss. 87 A and 47 A - (A) Right to elect representatives to govern - Whether 'Fundamental' or 'Common law' right ? - If it is a statutory right - Removal/recall of elected representatives - Legislature, if competent to enact law - (B) President - Removal by vote of no confidence - Power vested in Board - It is in public interest - Whether President elected directly by electors or indirectly by Board, if material - Provisions relating to election, qualification, resignation and removal of President - Whether opposed to concept of democracy ? - (C) S. 87 A(14) of 1990 Act - No confidence motion against Municipalities President - Reduction of period of tabling it from two to one year - Held, (A) It is statutory and not fundamental or common law right - Is competent to enact law ? - (B) Is immaterial ? - Not opposed to democracy -

(C) It is a matter of legislative policy ? - Court cannot scan its wisdom - Petition dismissed.
