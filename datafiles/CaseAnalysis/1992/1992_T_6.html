Oriental Insurance Company, Limited v T. Mohammed Raisulihassan

Supreme Court of India

18 December 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Venkatachala, Yogeshwar Dayal, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 799; (1993) SCC (L&S) 297; (1993) 1 SCC 553; 1993 (23) ATC 809; 1993 (1) CLR 905; JT 1992 (Supp) SC 767; 1993 (2) LLN 24; 1992(3) SCALE 568; 1993 (1) SLJ 157; 1993 (1) SLR 431; 1993 (1) UPLBEC 265

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Termination - Two stipulations governing conditions of service by which respondent was appointed that continuation in service of respondent as probationer unless confirmed, other related to termination of his service at any time by giving one month's notice or on payment of one month's salary in lieu of such notice - By a notice service was terminated - Instituted a suit before Civil Judge Court, affirmed finding of Munsif Court as regards respondent being on probation at time of termination of his service - Second appeal before High Court, affirmed decree of Civil Judge Court - Whether termination of service effected by appellant was illegal? - Nothing to indicate or suggest, even remotely, that non-service of one month's notice as a condition precedent for termination of respondent's service would result in vitiation or invalidation of termination - Second part of term - Breach of this condition will entitle company to recover one month's salary in lieu of notice and same would be consequence in there was a breach of condition on part of company - Held, non-service of one month's notice in writing by appellant to respondent before terminating latter's service did not invalidate or vitiate such termination - Appeal allowed.
