Swaraj Mazda Ltd v Collector Of Central Excise, Chandigarh

Supreme Court of India

27 August 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

A.S. Anand, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 1302; 1995 (77) E.L.T. 505

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Exclusion From Assessable Value

Summary: Excise - Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944 - Appellants claim for deduction of Rs. 5,000 from retail selling price as mentioned in part V of price list - Findings of Collector on appeal about nature of amount of Rs. 5,000 claimed as deduction by appellant and justification of that deduction were at variance with findings rendered by Asst. Collector - Tribunal dismissing appeal without going to merits on ground that it is bound by an earlier decision of Tribunal - Whether tribunal was right in dismissing appeal without going into facts on which point of law involved had to be decided? - In present situation it was incumbent upon tribunal to first determine nature of this amount and then to proceed for deciding justification for granting that deduction - Held, where tribunal has not recorded its findings on disputed facts and these being at variance in findings rendered by Asst. Collector and Collector, only appropriate course to adopt is to remit matter to tribunal for a fresh decision - Appeal allowed.
