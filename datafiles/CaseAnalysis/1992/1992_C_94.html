Sk. Ar. K. Ar. Somasundaram Chettiar and Company v Commissioner of Income Tax

Supreme Court of India

15 January 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 1125; (1992) Supp2 SCC 54; 1992 (102) CTR 182; [1992] 194 ITR 1; JT 1992 (1) SC 93; 1992(1) SCALE 12; [1992] 1 S.C.R. 49; [1992] 60 TAXMAN 406

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income-tax Act, 1961 - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 24(1)(a)(iii) - Assessee was a registered firm carrying on the business in cloth and yarn - Income Tax Officer (ITO) held that the losses sustained by the assessee in the A/Y's, 1960-61 and 1961-62, constituted losses in speculative transaction in the nature of business and, therefore, could not be set off except against profits from speculation - Appeal to the Tribunal was upheld - HC decreed against the assessee - Hence, the present appeal - Whether the contract of purchase entered by assessee fell within cl.(a), of the third proviso, to s. 24(1), of the 1922 Act - Held, that there need not be co-relation between contract to contract but there ought to be a co-relation between the contracts spoken to in the first part of cl. (a) and the contracts spoken to in the latter part, of s. 24(1), of the 1922 Act, in the absence of which, the Clause was not attracted - For a transaction to come u/cl. (a), of the s. 24(1), of the 1922 Act, it should be one entered into by an assessee to guard against loss through future price fluctuations in respect of his sale contracts - Appeal dismissed.
