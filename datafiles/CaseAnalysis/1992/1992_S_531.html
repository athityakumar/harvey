Punjab National Bank and Others v Surendra Prasad Sinha

Supreme Court of India

20 April 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 374; (1993) SCC (Cr) 149; (1993) Supp1 SCC 499; AIR 1992 SC 1815; AIR 1992 SCW 2046; 1993 (1) AnWR 104; 1992 (2) BankCLR 123; 1992 (1) BC 579; 1992 (2) CCR 103; [1992] 75 Comp Cas 699; 1992 (2) Crimes 297; 1992 CRLJ 2916; JT 1992 (3) SC 46; 1992(1) SCALE 926; [1992] 2 S.C.R. 528

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance

Summary: Banking & Finance - Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Limitation Act, 1908, ss. 3 and 27 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 409 - Embezzlement - Complaint impleading Chairman, the Managing Director of the Bank by name and a host of officers - Held, Responsibility and duty on Magistrate to find whether concerned accused should be legally responsible for offence against juristic person or persons impleaded and then only process would be issued - Further held, respondent had abused the process and laid complaint against all the appellants without any prima facie case of harass them for vendetta - Appeal allowed.
