Dr. L.P. Agarwal v Union Of India And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

21 July 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

Kuldip Singh, A.S. Anand

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 1103; (1992) 3 SCC 526; (1992) SCC (L&S) 731; AIR 1992 SC 1872; 1992 (21) ATC 249; 1992 (65) FLR 758; JT 1992 (4) SC 220; 1992 LabIC 1807; 1993 (2) LLJ 825; 1992 (2) LLN 400; 1992(2) SCALE 54; [1992] 3 S.C.R. 567; 1992 (3) SLJ 137; 1992 (4) SLR 583; 1992 (2) UJ 266; 1992 (2) UPLBEC 1409

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Constitution - Service - Constitution, art. 311 - All India Institute of Medical Sciences Act, 1956, s. 11 - All India Institute of Medical Sciences Regulations ,1958, Regn. 20 - Tenure post - Superannuation - Appellant director of AIIMS - Was prematurely retired on grounds of Superannuation - Appointment order said that he is appointed for period of 5 years or till he attains age of 62 years - Held, premature retirement is illegal as under the ' Recruitment Rules ' the post of Director of the AIIMS is a tenure post and tenure means a term during which an office is held and it comes to an end on the completion of the tenure - Appeal allowed.
