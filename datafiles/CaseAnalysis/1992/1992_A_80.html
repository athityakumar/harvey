Guro Smt v Atma Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

05 March 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Agrawal, M. Fathima Beevi

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 1007; (1992) 2 SCC 507; 1992 (19) ALR 392; 1992 (2) AWC 1076; 1992 (1) CCC 564; 1993 (1) DMC 394; 1992 (1) HLR 300; JT 1992 (2) SC 325; 1993 LabIC 844; 1992 (2) MLJ(SC) 52; 1992(1) SCALE 552; [1992] 2 S.C.R. 30; 1992 (2) UJ 21

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Code of Civil Procedure 1908, Succession Act 1925

Summary: Family & Personal - Civil Procedure -Genuineness of a will - Indian Succession Act, 1925, s. 63 - Will executed in favour of respondent but Asst. Collector sanctioned mutation in favour of appellant on ground that it is not beyond suspicion - Respondent filed a suit for declaration of will - Sub-Judge, I Class, decreed suit on view that execution of will was duly proved - On appeal, Addl. District Judge, found suspicious circumstances, reversed decree and dismissed suit - High Court restored judgment of Trial Court on view that will had been validly executed - Whether will is genuine? - With regard to proof of a will law is well settled that mode of proving a will does not ordinarily differ from that of proving any other document except as to special requirement prescribed in case of a will by s. 63 of Indian Succession Act, 1925 - Onus of proving will is on propounder - Held, HC was not justified in reversing findings of fact recorded by Appellate Court which were based on a proper appreciation of evidence on record - In doing so, HC failed to attach sufficient importance to various suspicious features relating to execution of the will that were pointed out by Appellate Court - Testator had died within eight days of execution of will and recital in will that testator had been ill for a long time and was seriously ill at time of execution of will - In view of said recital, it was necessary for holder of will to adduce satisfactory evidence with regard to nature of illness of testator and about his mental capacity to execute will - Incorrect statements in will have to be considered in this background - Circumstance, testator had not put his signature and had put only his thumb impression on will, when there is no evidence on record with regard to literacy of testator creates suspicion and will is not proved to be a genuine document - Appeal allowed.
