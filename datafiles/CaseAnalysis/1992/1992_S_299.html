S. Rajan v State of Kerala and Another

Supreme Court of India

29 July 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, P.B. Sawant

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 524; (1992) 3 SCC 608; AIR 1992 SC 1918; 1992 (2) AnWR 33; 1992 (2) ARBLR 281; JT 1992 (4) SC 312; 1992 (2) KLT 435; 1992(2) SCALE 86; [1992] 3 S.C.R. 649; 1992 (2) UJ 436

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Banking & Finance; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Debt Laws

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Practice & Procedure - Indian Limitation Act, 1908, art. 181 - Arbitration Act, 1940, s. 20 - Limitation Act, 1963, ss. 2(a) and 2(b), arts. 131 and 137 - (A) Application u/s. 20 of Arbitration Act - Period of limitation - Held, under Limitation Act, 1908, art. 181 does not govern an application u/s. 20 but as pointed out in Kerala State Electricity Board case [1976 Indlaw SC 268], new Act governs all petition and applications under special laws so long as they are filed in a civil court - It was held in Inder Singh's case [1988 Indlaw SC 417] that art. 137 governs application u/s. 20, therefore, it cannot be said that no period of limitation is prescribed for making an application u/s. 20 - Right to apply accrues when differences arises or differences arise, as case may be, between parties - (B) Arbitrator - Appointment - Power of Court - Whether a different person could be appointed as arbitrator where agreement itself specifies and names arbitrator? - Only in cases where agreement does not specify arbitrator and parties cannot also agree upon an arbitrator, does court get jurisdiction to appoint an arbitrator - Held, it was not open to Court to appoint another person as an arbitrator; only if arbitrator specified and named in agreement refuses or fails to act does court get jurisdiction to appoint another person or persons as arbitrator which is clear purport of sub-section (4) of s. 20 - Appeal dismissed.
