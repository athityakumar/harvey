P.V. Mohammad Barmay Sons v Director Of Enforcement

Supreme Court of India

20 August 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, S. Ratnavel Pandian

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 822; (1993) Supp2 SCC 724; (1993) SCC (Cr) 777; AIR 1993 SC 1188; AIR 1992 SCW 3609; 1992 (3) CCR 265; [1993] 76 Comp Cas 27; 1993 CRLJ 197; 1992 CrLR(SC) 620; 1992 (42) ECR 625; 1992 (61) E.L.T. 337; JT 1992 (4) SC 565; 1992(2) SCALE 227; [1992] 3 S.C.R. 960

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Foreign Exchange Regulation Act , 1973, ss. 9(1)(a)(c), 51 and 81(2) - General clauses Act, 1897, ss. 6 and 26 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, s. 300 - (A) Repealed Act, 1997 - Penalty incurred under repealed Act - Whether authorities entitled under new Act to institute proceedings, conduct investigation and impose such penalty? - (B) Penalty proceedings for evasion of excise duty under Sea Customs Act ending in favour of accused - Jurisdiction of authorities under Act to impose penalty for contravention of Act - Whether taken away? - Doctrine of double jeopardy - Applicability - (C) Payments by persons to agents outside India proved - No express permission granted by RBI for such payments - Show cause notice - Contravention brought to party's notice - Explanation also given by party - Imposition of penalty as contravention was proved - Whether based on no material? - Held, (A) Entitled under new Act - (B) Not taken away on that ground - Not applicable - (B) Cannot be held to be based on no material - Appeal dismissed.
