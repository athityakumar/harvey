Banwari and Others v Income Tax Officer and Another

Supreme Court of India

25 March 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, K. Jayachandra Reddy

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 1135; (1993) Supp1 SCC 619; [1992] 195 ITR 651; [1992] 63 TAXMAN 516

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Criminal - Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 3 - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 240 and 277 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 245(2) - Income Tax Officer, however, filed three complaints in respect of statements made by one of partners alleging that accused No. 2, a partner of firm, had made false statements on verification which was punishable u/s. 277 of Income Tax Act and s. 420 of IPC - Whether order of High Court allowing revision applications, setting aside order of Magistrate and remitting complaints to Magistrate to be dealt within accordance with law, is correct? - Held, no reason for continuance of proceedings of 1977 - No useful purpose will be served by proceeding with complaint after such a long lapse of time - Having regard to facts and circumstances of case, Magistrate could not be said to have been grossly wrong in inferring that it was merely a bona fide mistake - Order of HC set aside - Appeals allowed.
