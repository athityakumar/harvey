Bhoop Singh v Union Of India And Ors

Supreme Court of India

29 April 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

J.S. Verma, L.M. Sharma, A.S. Anand

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 410; (1992) 3 SCC 136; AIR 1992 SC 1414; 1992 (21) ATC 675; JT 1992 (3) SC 322; 1992 LabIC 1464; 1993 (1) LLJ 260; 1992 (2) LLN 397; 1992(1) SCALE 954; [1992] 2 S.C.R. 969; 1992 (2) SLJ 103; 1992 (4) SLR 761; 1992 (2) UJ 238

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Constitution - Service - Constitution, 1950, arts. 14 and 136 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Mass agitation - termination from service - Later some dismissed constables were taken back in service as fresh entrants - WP by others in HC, allowed - Administration preferred appeals which dismissed - Whether rejection of application of petitioner by Tribunal on the ground that it is highly belated and there is no cogent explanation for the inordinate delay of twenty-two years in filing the application, was legally correct? - Court held, lapse of a much longer unexplained period of several years in the case of the petitioner is a strong reason to not classify him with the other dismissed constables who approached the Court earlier and got reinstatement, so argument of discrimination is, therefore, not available to the petitioner - Petition dismissed.
