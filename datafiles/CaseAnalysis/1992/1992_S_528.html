Poolpandi Etc v Superintendent, Central Excise Andothers

Supreme Court of India

14 May 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

L.M. Sharma, Yogeshwar Dayal, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 474; (1992) 3 SCC 259; (1992) SCC (Cr) 620; 1992 (29) ACC 550; AIR 1992 SC 1795; 1992 (2) CCR 130; [1992] 75 Comp Cas 504; 1992 (2) Crimes 648; 1992 CRLJ 2761; 1992 CrLR(SC) 545; 1992 (42) ECR 7; 1992 (60) E.L.T. 24; JT 1992 (4) SC 119; 1992(1) SCALE 1114; [1992] 3 S.C.R. 247

Case Digest

Subject: Customs

Keywords: Accused Of An Offence, Criminal Appeals, Sea Customs Act 1962

Summary: Constitution - Exchange Control & FDI - Customs - Exchange Regulation Act, 1973, s. 40 - Customs Act, Whether petitioners are entitled to presence of their lawyers when they are questioned during investigation under provisions of FERA and Customs Act? - Held, a person called for questioning during investigation by authorities under provisions of Customs Act or Foreign Exchange Regulation Act, 1973 is not an accused and therefore he cannot claim that in view of possibility of his being made an accused in future he is entitled to presence of his lawyer when he is questioned - Refusal to allow presence of lawyer in such case would not be violative of protection u/art. 20 (3) nor can it be violative u/art. 21 - Appeal Nos. 301-302 of 1987, dismissed - Appeal No. 476 of 1986, allowed.
