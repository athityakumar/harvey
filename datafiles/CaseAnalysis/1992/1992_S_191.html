State Of U.P. v Dr. Ravindra Prakash Mittal

Supreme Court of India

28 April 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Ratnavel Pandian, M. Fathima Beevi

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 210; (1992) 3 SCC 300; (1992) SCC (Cr) 642; AIR 1992 SC 2045; 1992 (2) AWC 1105; 1992 (2) CCR 71; 1992 (2) Crimes 664; 1992 CRLJ 3693; JT 1992 (3) SC 114; 1992(1) SCALE 937; [1992] 2 S.C.R. 815

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Constitution - Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 3 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 134 - Essential ingredients for circumstantial evidence to prove guilt - (1) circumstances from which conclusion is drawn should be fully proved - (2) circumstances should be conclusive in nature - (3) All facts so established should be consistent only with hypothesis of guilt and inconsistent with innocence - (4) circumstances should, to a moral certainty, exclude possibility of guilt of any person other than accused - Accused of murdering his wife - Medical evidence as well as circumstantial evidence corroborated the fact that victim died due strangulation and later her body was set on fire - Held, evidence established that both of them were in one room throughout night - Letters written by deceased wife to her relatives from time to time showed that husband was an alcohol addict, ill-treated her and their relations were strained - Court convicted respondent u/ss. 302 and 201, IPC, 1860 and sentenced him to imprisonment for life and rigorous imprisonment for 3 years respectively - Delay in disposal of appeal by Supreme Court could not be a ground for not disturbing the finding of acquittal by High Court when its judgment suffers from illegality or manifest error or perversity - Appeal allowed.
