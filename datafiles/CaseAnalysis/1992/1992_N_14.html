Nandu Mal Girdhari Lal Etc v State of Uttar Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

03 April 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mohan, M.M. Punchhi, G.N. Ray

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 347; (1993) Supp1 SCC 338; AIR 1992 SC 2084; JT 1992 (2) SC 537; 1992 RD 310; 1992(1) SCALE 778; [1992] 2 S.C.R. 446; 1992 (2) UJ 151; 1992 (2) UPLBEC 913

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments; Agriculture & Agri. Products

Summary: Agriculture & Agri Products - Uttar Pradesh Krishi Utpadan Mandi Adhiniyam, 1964, ss. 5, 7, 9, 10, 40, 2(a), 8 and 17 - Object of UPKUMA Act was to regulate sale and purchase of agricultural produce and for establishment, superintendence and control of markets in U.P. - Notice D/- 13-09-73 issued u/s. 8 of Act was suspended in so far as it related to khandsari - Appellants, commission agents were carrying on trade in notified market area - Attack is as to levy of market fee on them in relation to business of Khandsari sugar - Whether fee is validly imposed with regard to Khandsari? - Held, fee has been validly imposed and no exception could be taken to same - It is incorrect to submit that notification u/s. 10 has not been issued - Merely because there was a stay, it does not mean liability disappears - Notification D/- 13/09/1973 stood suspended at instance of other traders - That cannot ensure to benefit of appellants herein - They were neither petitioners nor respondents - Therefore, they cannot take advantage of stay order and plead inability to pay - In as much as Act itself has been retrospectively amended, appellants cannot disown liability - Effect of last notification is khandsari gets included to list of agricultural produce to notification issued u/s. 6 D/- 27-10-1965 - Therefore, for dealing in this commodity commission agents will be liable to pay at rate prescribed - Though stay was granted on 11-10-1973, it was not at instance of Appellants herein - Therefore, they cannot take advantage of same - Appeal dismissed.
