Aslam Babalal Desai v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

15 September 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, K. Ramaswamy, M.M. Punchhi

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 218; (1992) 4 SCC 272; (1992) SCC (Cr) 870; AIR 1993 SC 1; AIR 1992 SCW 2621; 1993 (3) Bom.C.R. 166; 1992 CRLJ 3712; JT 1992 (6) SC 21; [1992] Supp1 S.C.R. 545

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Bail - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 167, 439, 437, 41, 57, 190, 167 and 344 - Whether bail granted under proviso to s. 167(2) CrPC for failure to complete investigation within time prescribed can be canceled afterwards when charge sheet is filed? - Held, provisions of CrPC, in particular ss. 57 and 167, manifest legislative anxiety that once a person's liberty has been interfered with by police arresting him without a court's order or a warrant, the investigation must be carried out with utmost urgency and completed within the maximum period allowed by the proviso (a) to s. 167(2) of CrPC - Since s. 167 does not empower cancellation of bail, power to cancel bail can only be traced to s. 437(5) or 439(2) of CrPC - Bail can then be canceled on considerations which are valid for cancelation of bail granted u/s. 437(1) or (2) or 439(1) of CrPC - Fact that the bail was earlier rejected or that it was secured by the thrust of proviso (a) to s. 167(2) of CrPC then recedes in background - Once accused is released on bail u/s. 167(2) he cannot be taken back in custody merely on the filing of charge-sheet but there must exist special reasons for so doing besides fact that charge-sheet reveals commission of a non-bailable crime - Unless there are strong grounds for cancelation of bail, bail once granted cannot be canceled on mere production of charge-sheet - Matter remitted to High Court - 'Bashir v. State of Haryana' 1977 Indlaw SC 304 followed - Appeal allowed.
