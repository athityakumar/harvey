Sat Pal @ Sadhu v State Of Haryana And Anr.

Supreme Court of India

19 August 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

Kuldip Singh, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 570; (1992) 4 SCC 172; (1992) SCC (Cr) 866; AIR 1993 SC 1218; 1992 (3) CCR 257; 1992 (3) Crimes 576; 1993 CRLJ 314; 1992 CrLR(SC) 628; JT 1992 (4) SC 530; 1992(2) SCALE 203; [1992] 3 S.C.R. 898; 1992 (2) UJ 634

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Criminal; Human Rights

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 53 A, 55 and 60 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 433(b) - Appellant has challenged his continued detention in jail and is seeking an order in nature of habeas corpus claiming that he has served more than maximum sentence of imprisonment prescribed under law and should, therefore, be released - Accused also alleged that his sentence has not been remitted fully nor commuted for imprisonment for a term not exceeding 14 years either u/s. 55 IPC, 1860 or u/s. 433(b) CrPC, 1973 - Whether a sentence can be deemed to be commuted by state Govt. u/s. 55, IPC, 1860 and u/s. 433(b), CrPC, 1973 ? - Held, absence of any proper authority of law warranting detention and execution in such jail custody will have to be regarded as illegal and unlawful - Nature of sentence of life imprisonment is undefined and it is not necessarily rigorous - But because petitioners was made to undergo his sentence of life imprisonment in jail and that too in rigorous manner for more than 14 years (inclusive of remissions) his sentence should be deemed to have been commuted by State Govt. either u/s. 55, IPC, 1860 or u/s. 433(b) CrPC, 1973 without a formal order in that behalf - Petition dismissed.
