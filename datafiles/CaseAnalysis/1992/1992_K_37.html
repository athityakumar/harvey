 Karnataka Public Service Commission and Others v B.M. Vijaya Shankar and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 February 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Sahai, S. Ratnavel Pandian, Kuldip Singh, N.D. Ojha, K. Jagannatha Shetty

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 335; (1992) 2 SCC 206; (1992) SCC (L&S) 362; AIR 1992 SC 952; 1992 (20) ATC 215; 1992 (58) E.L.T. 558; JT 1992 (4) SC 348; 1992(1) SCALE 451; [1992] 1 S.C.R. 668; 1992 (2) SLR 77; 1992 (2) UJ 242

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Customs; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 226 and 320 - Examination of civil service - Conduct and regulation - Instruction not to write roll no on answer book at any place except in space provided on first page - Breach of - Commission not subjecting such answer books to evaluation - (A) If arbitrary - (B) Opportunity of hearing - Does rule of natural justice has no exception - Held, action not arbitrary - Exception, opportunity of hearing to candidates need not be given - Appeal allowed.
