Indian Airlines Corporation v Capt. K. C. Shukla and Others

Supreme Court of India

23 September 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Sahai, S. Ratnavel Pandian

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 636; (1993) 1 SCC 17; (1993) SCC (L&S) 114; 1993 (23) ATC 407; 1992 (65) FLR 934; JT 1992 (5) SC 565; 1993 (1) LLJ 215; 1992 (2) LLN 866; 1992(2) SCALE 587; 1993 (2) SLJ 2; 1992 (5) SLR 519; [1992] Supp1 S.C.R. 811; 1992 (2) UJ 796

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Personality

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Post of Chief Pilot Officer was redesignated and was placed in higher category and it was provided that posts shall be filled by selection and promotion - Letter issued by Managing Director that while making selection to post of Operations Manager it was advised that normally seniority should be adhered to - High Court quashed the order promoting respondents 2 to 25 as illegal and in alternative directing Corporation to promote appellant to post of Dy Operations Manager - Selection was found to be vitiated as committee was not constituted in accordance with rules and regulation - Method of evaluation based 50 per cent on ACR and 50 per cent on interview was held to be arbitrary - Whether HC was right in quashing promotions? - Court cannot substitute its opinion and devise its own method of evaluating fitness of a candidate for a particular post - Marks to be allotted by Committee were on professional ability and management capacity - Appears Committee was neither guilty of arbitrariness nor it violated any rule or regulation in allotting marks which of course were very low in interview - Post of Chief Pilot or Deputy Operations Manager were considered posts of Officer's category - Held, whether a post should be filled by promotion or by selection is a matter which is governed by Promotion and Recruitment Rules - So long as rules are not violative either of regulations or Act or arbitrary the courts will have little jurisdiction to interfere with it - Since in this case promotion or selection to post of Deputy Operations Manager is governed by Rules they appear to be valid and do not suffer from any infirmity - Nor it can be said that Corporation in limiting filed of eligibility to Commander Pilots acted contrary to any rules - Moreover Committee was not illegally constituted - Appeal allowed.
