M. Sankaranarayanan Ias. v State Of Karnataka And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

11 November 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

G.N. Ray, M.H. Kania

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 1215; (1993) 1 SCC 54; (1993) SCC (L&S) 122; AIR 1993 SC 763; 1993 (23) ATC 412; 1993 (1) CLR 532; JT 1992 (Supp) SC 232; 1992 LabIC 2537; 1993 (1) LLN 215; 1992(3) SCALE 90; 1993 (1) SLR 437; [1992] Supp2 S.C.R. 368

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Challenged, Transfer Order

Summary: Service - Transfer order - Challenged - Appellant filed instant appeal challenging the order of Tribunal, whereby appellant filed application before Tribunal for quashing the order of transfer and posting him as Secretary High Power Committee for development of Hyderabad, and to allow the applicant to continue as Chief Secretary, Government of Karnataka - Application was dismissed - Hence instant appeal. Whether the order recorded by Tribunal was justifiability.

Held, there are sufficient materials from which reasonable inference of malice in fact for passing the impugned order of transfer can be drawn. It is an admitted position that the Chief Secretary and Chief Minister had differences of opinion on number of sensitive matters. If on that score, the Cabinet and the Chief Minister had taken decision to relieve appellant from the post of Chief Secretary and post very senior officer of their confidence to the post of Chief Secretary, it cannot be held that such decision is per se illegal or beyond the administrative authority. Decision to declare equivalence was taken prior to impugned order of transfer and the formal publication by way of statutory requirement was made in order to give effect to the decision to declare equivalence already taken. In such circumstances, SC do not think that the formal declaration made invalidates the impugned order of transfer. The Tribunal is justified in holding that infraction, even if any, in making publication formally, is only technical violation for which no interference is called for. In the result, there is no reason to interfere with impugned decision of Tribunal. Appeal dismissed.
