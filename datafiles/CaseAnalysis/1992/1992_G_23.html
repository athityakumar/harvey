State of Rajasthan v Gopi Kishan Sen

Supreme Court of India

07 April 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

A.S. Anand, L.M. Sharma

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 1039; (1993) SCC (L&S) 295; (1993) Supp1 SCC 522; AIR 1992 SC 1754; 1993 (23) ATC 665; 1992 LabIC 1798; 1992 (6) SLR 726

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Constitution - Service - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 16 and 39(d) -Rajasthan Civil Services (New Pay Scales) Rules (1969) - Rajasthan Educational Subordinate Service Rules, 1971, r. 1 - Equal pay for equal work - Respondent an untrained teacher, was paid less compared to trained - Rule making distinction in their pay scales on basis of their qualifications - Held, that rule is not discretionary as additional qualification of being trained is of such a nature as to enable teacher to do his duty better and for that reason distinction made in rules must be upheld as valid - Appeal allowed.
