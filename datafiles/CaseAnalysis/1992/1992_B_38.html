Basanagouda v Dr. S. B. Amarkhed And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

31 March 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, Kuldip Singh

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 943; (1992) 2 SCC 612; AIR 1992 SC 1163; JT 1992 (2) SC 484; 1992(1) SCALE 770; [1992] 2 S.C.R. 397; 1992 (1) UJ 773

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Election; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Code of Civil Procedure 1908

Summary: Election - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 37, 100 and 135 A - Election Petition - Production of documents - Ordering of - Making out of cast-iron case in pleadings - Condition precedent - Allegation of booth capturing - Production of packets of unused ballot papers with counter foils, packets of used ballot papers, valid, tendered or rejected - Packets of used ballot and packet of declarations by electors and declarations of attestation of signatures directed - No factual foundation laid - Held, order is erroneous - Appeal partly allowed.
