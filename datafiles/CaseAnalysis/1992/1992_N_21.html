National Thermal Power Corporation v Singer Company And Ors

Supreme Court of India

07 May 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

T.K. Thommen, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 497; (1992) 3 SCC 551; AIR 1993 SC 998; 1992 (2) ARBLR 154; 1992 (2) CCC 380; 1992 (2) CompLJ 256; JT 1992 (3) SC 198; 1992(1) SCALE 1034; [1992] 3 S.C.R. 106; 1992 (2) UJ 201

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; International

Keywords: Foreign State, Private International

Summary: Foreign Awards Recognition & Enforcement Act, 1961 - Constitution of India, 1950 - New York Convention on Recognition and Enforcement of Foreign Arbitral Awards, 1958 - International law - Commercial contract between parties of two different countries - Proper law by which contract is to be governed - Depends upon intention of parties - Contract between Indian and foreign companies - Intention of parties would decide as to law which country would govern contract and which Court would have jurisdiction - Whether an arbitration award made in london but award governed by indian laws makes it a Foreign Award - Held, award made on arbitration agreement governed by law of India not foreign award even if rendered outside India.
