Kuldip Singh v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

19 August 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Jayachandra Reddy, G.N. Ray

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 568; (1992) Supp3 SCC 1; (1992) SCC (Cr) 946; AIR 1992 SC 1944; 1992 (3) Crimes 575; 1992 CRLJ 3592; 1992 CRLR 626; JT 1992 (4) SC 528; 1992(2) SCALE 193; 1992 (2) UJ 628

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Tort

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 174 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 300 - Murder of wife - Plea that gun fired accidentally - (A) Inquest report - Evidence of panch witness - Supporting accident theory - Through version in inquest report was that accused fired deliberately - Inquest report drafted in language known to witness - (B) Medical evidence - Not even suggested to doctor that it was accident due to grappling - Evidence of eye-witness who was mother-in-law of accused stating that accused deliberately shot - Evidence of witnesses on behalf of accused not reliable - Conviction - Held, (A) Evidence not reliable, inquest report though cannot be treated as evidence but can be looked to test veracity of witness - (B) Theory of accident found artificial, conviction upheld - Appeal dismissed.
