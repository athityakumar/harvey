Syed Khalid Rizvi and Others v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

20 November 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, A.M. Ahmadi, M.M. Punchhi

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 758; (1993) Supp3 SCC 575; (1994) SCC (L&S) 84; 1994 (26) ATC 192; JT 1992 (Supp) SC 169; 1993 (1) LLJ 887; 1993 (1) SLR 89; [1992] Supp3 S.C.R. 180

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service; Tort

Keywords: Right To Promotion

Summary: Service - All India Services Act, 1951 - Indian Police Service (Regulation Of Seniority) Rules 1954, rr. 3(3)(b) and 2(g) - Indian Police Service (Probation) Rules, 1954 - Indian Police Service (Appointment by Promotion) Regulations, 1955, r. 5 - Whether failure to prepare select list could give rise to an inference that rules have been collapsed and State Govt.'s local arrangement shall be given legitimacy as regular appointments? - Held, appointment by promotion to Indian Police Service and as a fact to any All India Service and determination of inter se seniority bear vital effect at higher echelons of super time scale of pay and above - State Govt. and Central Govt. should strictly comply with provisions in making recruitment by promotion from State Service to All India Services - Concept of All India Services introduced to effectuate national integration by drawing persons from different regions by direct recruitment into concerned States cadres would be defeated by manipulation - Junior most and unqualified or unfit would be pushed in from back door and pumped up into higher echelons, eroding efficiency and honesty - Majority members committed gravest of errors in holding that Explanation 1 to r. 3(3)(b) of Seniority Rules and relevant rules were deemed to have been relaxed and directions given to Central Govt. to refix seniority is illegal - Appeal allowed.
