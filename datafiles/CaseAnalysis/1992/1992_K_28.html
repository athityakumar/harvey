 Kanoria Chemicals and Industries Limited and Another v State of Uttar Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

16 January 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Ranganathan, M. Fathima Beevi, N.D. Ojha, K. Jayachandra Reddy, S.C. Agrawal, R.M. Sahai

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 829; (1992) 2 SCC 124; JT 1992 (3) SC 199; 1992(1) SCALE 107; [1992] 1 S.C.R. 151

Case Digest

Subject: Electricity & Energy

Keywords: Official Languages Act, 1963, Supply Of Energy, Electricity Laws (Uttar Pradesh Amendment) Act, 1983

Summary: Electricity & Energy - Electricity (Supply) Act, 1948, ss. 49, 59 and 60 - Electricity Laws (Uttar Pradesh Amendment) Act, 1983, s. 7 - (A) Whether State Govt. was obliged to give a hearing to consumer before revising rates u/s. 60(5) and whether factors relevant u/s. 60(5) have been taken into account? - Held, yes - Section does not require Board or State Govt. to explain each and every step in its calculation; it is in order to ensure that these recommendation take into account all relevant factors that an opportunity has been provided to consumer to satisfy Board as well as State Govt. that fixation has not taken into account certain relevant factors - Rates recommended by Electricity Board and approved by State Govt. were within knowledge of appellant - Copy of recommendations were also available to appellant and appellant also had full opportunity to meet various points set out in recommendations of Board; comments of both Board and appellant have been taken into account by State Govt. before finally approving of rates proposed by Board - Held, appellant has been given a fair opportunity u/s. 60(5)(a) - Board has taken into account purpose for which supply was required by petitioner along with factor of recurring losses incurred by Board year after year and its statutory requirements to maintain a minimum surplus of 3% as required u/s. 59 of Act, therefore, fixation of rates on 31-08-1991 is not vitiated - (B) Whether Board (i) can fix rates higher than HV-2 rates in respect of bulk consumers like company for whom a concessional rate had been granted on special considerations (ii) can fix rates in 1991 and make them retrospective w.e.f 1983? - Held, (i) s. 60(5)(a) is intended to enable Board and State to cut off shackles cast by an ancient contract entered into at a time when condition were totally different and in doing this, only limitation is factors mentioned in section; whether revised rates for governed earlier by contract should be higher or lower than, or equal to, tariff rates would depend on a large number of considerations, in particular, basis on which, and point of time at which, those general rates were fixed; with regard to legality of fixing such higher rates, as such there is no difficulty either on language of statute or on other considerations; (ii) a retrospective effect to revision also seems to be clearly envisaged by section; if section were interpreted as conferring a power of revision only prospectively, a consumer affected can easily frustrate effect of provisions by initiating proceedings seeking an injunction restraining Board and State from revising rates, on one ground or other, and thus getting revision deferred indefinitely; further, clause provides that revised rates fixed by Board shall be rates to be charged by Board for energy supplied by it to any consumer for which payment will be due for first time on or after 20-05-1983; Board is at complete liberty to fix different rates from different dates and that scheme of fixation will be r/w contract, only Board cannot revise rates in respect of supplies for which payment under contract, fell due before 20-05-1983; also though power u/s. 60 is exercisable more than once, however, while making a subsequent revision, authorities will not normally tamper with an earlier revision(s) or alter dates of effectiveness fixed for earlier revision(s) without a valid reason to do so - (C) Whether rates to be fixed in present case should, be less than, equal to or higher than general HV-2 rates? - Held, there is no material to justify any departure time from HV-2 rates in appellant's case; appellant has no valid justification for staking a claim to less than HV-2 rates and authorities have no case for seeking to raise rates beyond HV-2 rates - Appeal partly allowed.
