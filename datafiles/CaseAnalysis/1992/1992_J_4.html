Union of India and Others v J. S. Brar

Supreme Court of India

09 October 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

T.K. Thommen, S.P. Bharucha, Veeraswami Ramaswami

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 593; (1993) 1 SCC 176; (1993) SCC (Cr) 318; AIR 1993 SC 773; 1993 ALJ 807; JT 1992 (6) SC 266; 1992(2) SCALE 749; 1993 (2) SLJ 77; [1992] Supp1 S.C.R. 916; 1992 (2) UJ 789

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces; Service

Keywords: Court Martial

Summary: Service - Defence & Security Forces - Army Act 1950, ss. 160, 164 (2) - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 379 - Stolen of articles - Reversal of conviction - Sustainability - Accused/respondent was charged with offence punishable u/s. 69 of 1950 Act r/w s. 379 of IPC - Accused alleged to have been stolen articles on 27-9-1983 and 9-2-1984 from Department of Salvage - Accused was found guilty relating to incident of 9-2-1984 and was cashiered and sentenced to one year of rigorous imprisonment - In respect of incident of 27-9-1983 accused was acquitted - Confirming authority, however, ordered, u/s. 160 r/w r. 68, revision of findings of General Court-martial (GCM) for recording fresh evidence and reconsideration - Subsequently, GCM found accused guilty of both charges and sentenced him to be cashiered and to suffer rigorous imprisonment for one year - Confirming authority confirmed findings and sentences rendered by GCM by order dt.29-8-1986 - Accused filed petition before Govt. of India u/s. 164(2) of the Act - Govt. of India rejected petition vide order dt.25-1-1988 - Aggrieved by that, accused filed petition before HC - HC set aside revised findings of GCM and sentences imposed, as confirmed by order of competent authority - Hence instant appeal - Whether impugned judgment was sustainable - Held, SC found that a number of witnesses were examined in respect of offences alleged against respondent - They speak to present of accused at the time and place of offence and his active participation in commission of crime as well as attempt made by him to suppress evidence by threatening concerned witnesses or tampering with relevant document - Act and Rules did not require reasons to be given for findings and sentences rendered by court-martial - In absence of reasons, it was not possible to say whether GCM had placed any reliance at all on testimony of so-called accomplices or retracted confession of accused in coming to its findings against accused - General Officer Commanding, U.P., being so empowered to reserve confirmation for superior authority, did reserve by his order dt.31-7-1985 the findings and sentences for such confirmation - That authority, being Chief of Army Staff, confirmed findings and sentences of GCM vide his order dt.29-8-1986 - Revised findings and sentences of GCM thus stand confirmed by competent authority - These orders were made in compliance with relevant provisions of Act and Rules and were reasonably based on evidence - Thus, HC was not justified in interfering with findings and sentences rendered by GCM and confirmed by Chief of Army Staff - Appeal Allowed.
