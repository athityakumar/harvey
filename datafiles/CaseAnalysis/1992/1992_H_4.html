R. Banerjee And Ors. v H.D. Dubey And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

13 March 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 348; (1992) SCC (Cr) 412; (1992) 2 SCC 552; AIR 1992 SC 1168; 1992 (2) CCR 3; [1992] 75 Comp Cas 722; 1992 (2) CompLJ 195; 1992 (1) Crimes 1215; 1992 CRLJ 1523; 1992 CrLR(SC) 323; 1992 (104) CTR 33; 1992 (1) FAC 128; JT 1992 (2) SC 436; 1992(1) SCALE 690; [1992] 2 S.C.R. 221; 1992 (2) UJ 57

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Health & Drug; Socio-Economic

Keywords: Vanaspati, Misbranded, Prevention of Food Adulteration Rules 1955

Summary: Criminal - Prevention of Food Adulteration Act, 1954, s. 17 - Offence by Co. - Prosecution of Directors - (A) When permissible? - (B) Nomination u/s. 17(2) made by Co. not acted upon - Ground, it was invalid and Directors prosecuted - No allegation in complaint that offence was committed by consent of Directors - Held, (A) Scheme of s. 17 explained - (B) Provisions of s. 17(4) is not attracted, prosecution unjustified as their prosecution u/s. 17(1)(a)(ii) depends on question whether nomination was invalid, so matter remanded for determination of validity of nomination as preliminary issue - Appeal allowed.
