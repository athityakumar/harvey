B.N. Shankarappa v Uthanur Srinivas And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

21 January 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, K. Ramaswamy, R.M. Sahai

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 844; (1992) 2 SCC 61; AIR 1992 SC 836; AIR 1992 SCW 635; 1992 (1) AnWR 54; JT 1992 (1) SC 389; 1992(1) SCALE 213; [1992] 1 S.C.R. 286

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Karnataka General Clauses Act 1897, Karnataka Zila Parishads, Taluk Panchayat Samithis, Mandal Panchayat and Nyaya Panchayats Act, 1983

Summary: Municipalities & Local Governments - Administrative - Karnataka Zila Parishads, Taluk, Panchayat Samithis, Mandal Panchayats and Nyaya Panchayats Act, 1983, s. 4(2) - Change of headquarter - Power of Deputy Commissioner - Deputy Commissioner in exercise of power u/s. 4(1) of the Act named Mandal and located its headquarter - Divisional Commissioner changed headquarter - Decision of Divisional Commissioner was challenged - HC dismissed petition - DB allowed appeal - Whether s. 4(2)of the Act conferred any power in Deputy Commissioner to change the headquarter of any Mandal - Held, absence of power in s. 4(2) of the Act to specify headquarter afresh did not mean that once initial constitution of Mandal took place and headquarter was specified the power got exhausted - Such view would create vacuum and even when genuine need for specifying any other headquarter would arise, authorities would not be able to exercise power - SC decision in J.R. Raghupathy & Ors. v. State of A.P. others, 1988 Indlaw SC 536, followed - Appeal allowed.
