Binod Bihari Singh v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

08 December 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

G.N. Ray, B.P. Jeevan Reddy

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 556; (1993) 1 SCC 572; AIR 1993 SC 1245; AIR 1993 SCW 475; 1993 (1) ARBLR 313; 1992 (3) CCC 791; 1993 (49) DLT 498; JT 1992 (Supp) SC 496; 1992(3) SCALE 386; [1992] Supp3 S.C.R. 468

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Arbitration Act, 1940 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872 - (1) Whether arbitration award delivered by registered post creates presumption that it reached in time? - (2) Whether award given for information provides authority to file it in court? - (3) Whether delay in filing case can be condoned? - Held, presumption arises that registered cover had reached applicant within time - No, implied authority for filing award can be inferred - Delay in filing suit can not be condoned.
