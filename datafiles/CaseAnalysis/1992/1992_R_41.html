Crescent Dyes and Chemicals Limited v Ram Naresh Tripathi

Supreme Court of India

16 December 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, K. Ramaswamy, M.M. Punchhi

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 788; (1993) 2 SCC 115; (1993) SCC (L&S) 360; AIR 1993 SCW 1106; 1993 (49) DLT 444; 1993 (66) FLR 537; JT 1992 (Supp) SC 539; 1993 (1) LLJ 907; 1993 (1) LLN 761; 1992(3) SCALE 518; 1993 (1) SLR 408; [1992] Supp3 S.C.R. 559; 1993 (1) UJ 374

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Industrial Employment (Standing Order ) Act, 1946, Bombay Prohibition Act 1949

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Maharashtra Recognition of Trade Unions and Prevention of Unfair Labour Practices Act, 1971, ss. 3(5), 3(13), 3(17), 20, 21, 22(ii) and 30, sch. IV (Items 1(f), 2 and 6) - Bombay Industrial Relation Act, 1946, s. 30 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 2(j) - Industrial Employment (Standing Orders) Act, 1946 - (A) Whether a delinquent is entitled to be represented by an office-bearer of other Trade Union, who is not a member of either a recognised union or a non-recognised union functioning within undertaking in which delinquent is employed, notwithstanding statutory limitation contained in certified Standing Orders and cls. (ii) of s. 22 of Maharashtra Act? - Held, while s. 20(1)(d) confers a right on authorised officers, staff-members and members of recognised union to represent a delinquent in any domestic or departmental enquiry held by employer, in case of unfair labour practices falling within Items 2 and 6, right is further restricted, in that, in case of employees governed under Bombay Act and only those representatives mentioned in s. 30 of Bombay Act will be entitled to represent while employees governed by I D Act would have to appear in person and conduct proceeding relating to such unfair labour practices in absence of a recognised union - In case of an unrecognised union, officers staff-members and members of union authorised by State Govt. shall have a right to appear on behalf of any employee in domestic or departmental enquiry held by employer; hence in case of an employee facing a domestic or departmental enquiry, officers, office staff members or members of a recognised or unrecognised union may appear and act on behalf of delinquent provided they are authorised by State Govt.; further standing Order No. 26(2) entitles a delinquent to be defended by a clerk or a workman working in same department as himself and there is no such statute which has effect of overriding Standing Orders except Act, s. 22(ii) whereof enlarges scope and permits authorised officer, staff-member or member of an unrecognised union to represent delinquent - (B) Whether principal of fair hearing extends to right to be represented through counsel or agent? - Held, right to be represented through counsel or agent can be restricted, controlled or regulated by statute, rules, regulations or Standing Orders; a delinquent has no right to be represented through counsel or agent unless law specifically confers such a right and requirement of rule of natural justice insofar as delinquent's right of hearing is concerned, cannot and does not extend to a right to be represented through counsel or agent - Further held, here delinquent's right of representation was regulated by Standing Orders which permitted a clerk or a workman working with him in same department to represent his and this right stood expanded on ss. 21 and 22(ii) permitting representation through an officer, staff-member or a member of union, albeit on being authorised by State Govt. and High Court was in error in holding that proceedings before Enquiry Officer were vitiated as violative of principles of natural justice - Appeal allowed.
