Jarnail Singh v Secretary, Ministry of Home Affairs and Others

Supreme Court of India

03 December 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

J.S. Verma, M.N. Venkatachaliah, Yogeshwar Dayal

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 428; (1993) 1 SCC 47; (1993) SCC (L&S) 119; AIR 1994 SC 1484; 1993 (23) ATC 642; 1993 (1) CLR 511; JT 1992 (Supp) SC 489; 1993 (1) LLJ 962; 1993 (1) LLN 207; 1992(3) SCALE 313; 1993 (1) SLR 23; [1992] Supp3 S.C.R. 318; 1993 (1) UJ 205; 1993 (1) UPLBEC 133

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Challenged, Retirement benefits, With-held

Summary: Service - Central Civil Services (Pension) Rules, 1972, rr. 69(1)(c), 73(3), 3(1)(a) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 366 - Retirement benefits - With-held - Challenged - Appellant retired from post of Under Secretary in the Ministry of Home Affairs, Govt. of India - By order President of India directed that full monthly pension and entire amount of death-cum-retirement gratuity, otherwise admissible to the appellant, be with-held on permanent basis - This direction was given on account of serious irregularities found to have been committed by appellant in inquiry held for purpose and in consultation with Union Public Service Commission - Appellant challenged the order before Tribunal which was dismissed - Hence instant appeal - Whether the order recorded by Tribunal was sustainable.

Held, reference to some other provisions in the Rules supports the view that SC have taken. R. 69(1)(c) of the Rules provides that no gratuity shall be paid to the Govt. servant until the conclusion of departmental or judicial proceedings and issue of final orders thereon. This provision is indicative of the power to withhold payment of gratuity and its payment being subject to the final outcome of any pending departmental or judicial proceeding against the Government servant. Rr.71 and 73 of the Rules relating to recovery and adjustment of Govt. dues and the express provision in r. 73(3) of the Rules for adjustment of dues against the amount of death-cum- retirement gratuity payable to the Govt. servant also reinforce this conclusion. Art. 366 of the Constitution contains the definitions for the purpose of the Constitution and there in cl. (17) is defined 'pension' to include gratuity as well. This definition of 'pension' in the Constitution also indicates that conceptually the term 'pension' includes gratuity. In r. 3(1)(a) of the Rules, the term 'pension' is defined to include gratuity except when the term 'pension' is used in contradistinction to gratuity, in consonance with the basic concept. The contention of the appellant was, therefore, rightly rejected by the Tribunal - Appeal Dismissed.
