Mohan Rawale v Damodar Tatyaba Alias Dadasaheb and Others

Supreme Court of India

06 August 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

M.N. Venkatachaliah, P.B. Sawant

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 493; (1994) 2 SCC 392; AIR 1994 SCW 2028; 1995 (1) Bom.C.R. 322; JT 1993 (Supp) SC 611; 1994(1) SCALE 481; [1992] Supp3 S.C.R. 850

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Election - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 79(b), 81, 123(2), 123(3) and 123(3-A) - First respondent, called in question election of returned candidate, appellant - Corrupt practices u/ss. 123(2), 123(3) and 123(3-A) of Act are alleged - Appellant took put Chamber Summons for an order of dismissal of election petition on ground that memorandum of election petition did not disclose a cause of action that copies were not supplied and pleadings were frivolous and vexatious - High Court dismissed this motion - (A) Whether a person is responsible for his act as a candidate only from day he duly nominates as a candidate at election? - Held, all sub-sections of s. 123 of Act refer to acts of a 'candidate' or his election agent or any other person with consent of candidate or his election agent, substituted definition completely excludes acts by a candidate up to date he is nominated as a candidate - (B) Content of pleadings and of documents - Whether constitutes an integral part of pleadings? - Held, question whether a particular document produced by election petitioner partakes of nature of and constitutes an integral part of pleadings or whether it is merely material by which allegations in election petition are intended to be proved depends upon facts and circumstances of each case and nature and content of pleadings and of documents - No hard and fast test applicable to all cases can, or is possible to be laid down - Appeal disposed of.
