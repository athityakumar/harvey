Dr. A.K. Sabhapathy v State Of Kerala And Ors

Supreme Court of India

22 April 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Agrawal, M. Fathima Beevi

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 380; (1992) Supp3 SCC 147; AIR 1992 SC 1310; JT 1992 (3) SC 66; 1992 (1) KLT 784; 1992(1) SCALE 843; [1992] 2 S.C.R. 653; 1992 (2) UJ 159

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Health & Drug

Keywords: Ayurvedic, Siddha, Repugnancy, Indian Medicine Central Council Act, 1970, Homoeopathy Central Council Act, 1973, Travancore Cochin Medical Practitioners Act 1953

Summary: Travancore-Cochin Medical Practitioners Act, 1953, s. 38, first proviso -Notification D/-13-04-1981 - Govt. order D/-20-9-1978 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 245 - Medicine Central Council Act, 1970, ss. 11, 12, 13, 14, 15 and 21 - Medical practice - Allopathic system of medicine - Recognised medical qualifications - Govt. power to permit for practice - Consistency with - All India Medicine Central Council Act - Held, suffers from vice of repugnancy - Notification set aside.
