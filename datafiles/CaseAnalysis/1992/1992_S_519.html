State of Maharashtra v Sukhdev Singh Alias Sukha and another

Supreme Court of India

15 July 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 44; (1992) 3 SCC 700; (1992) SCC (Cr) 705; AIR 1992 SC 2100; AIR 1992 SCW 2486; 1993 (1) Bom.C.R. 233; 1992 (2) CCR 195; 1992 (3) Crimes 5; 1992 CRLJ 3454; 1992 CrLR(SC) 678; JT 1992 (4) SC 73; 1992(2) SCALE 9; [1992] 3 S.C.R. 480

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Fingerprints, Ballistics

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1898 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 307, 34 - Deceased General A. S. Vaidya followed the order of the then Prime Minister Indira Gandhi and flushed out the militants who had taken refuge in the Golden Temple at Amritsar and while following the order some militants were killed and a part of the Golden Temple was damaged - Prime Minister Indira Gandhi and deceased had, therefore, incurred the wrath of the Punjab militants - In the instant case respondents were charged for the offence of the murder of deceased General A. S. Vaidya - But respondents were acquitted - Hence, instant appeal by the appellant-State against their acquittal - Whether, the acquittal of the respondents was maintainable - Held, after detailed consideration of the facts and the circumstances of the case, instant court was of the view that, respondent no. 1's and respondent no. 5's attitude revealed that they had resolved to kill the deceased as they considered deceased an enemy of sheikh community since he had desecrated the Temple and respondent no. 1 and respondent no. 5 also told the trial court that they were proud of act alleged against them, which proved the charges against them - Instant court held, that conviction of respondent no. 1 and respondent no. 5 u/ss. 302, 307, IPC both read with s. 34, IPC should be confirmed and the sentence of death was awarded to both of them - However, instant court found no merit in the appellant-State's appeal against the acquittal of the other three respondents - Appeal disposed of.
