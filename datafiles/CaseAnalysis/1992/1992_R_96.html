Roshan Lal Ahuja (In Re)

Supreme Court of India

26 November 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

A.S. Anand, M.N. Venkatachaliah, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 1221; (1993) Supp4 SCC 446; (1994) SCC (Cr) 75; 1994 (26) ATC 243; JT 1992 (Supp) SC 66; [1992] Supp3 S.C.R. 257

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Service

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Contempt of Court Act, 1971 -Respondent/contemner-in-person - Circulated 'note for direction' - Casted unfounded and unwarranted aspersions and make scurrilous and indecent attacks against court and its Judges in wild, intemperate and even abusive language - Whether contemner statement in writ petition amounts to contempt of court? - Yes - Tend to scandalise court in relation to judicial matters and to interfere with administration of justice - Ordinarily, courts of law do not initiate proceedings to commit a person for contempt of court where there is mere technical contempt or where contemner satisfies court that he was truly repentant for his action - Judgments of court are open to criticism - Held, no litigant can be permitted to overstep limits of fair, bona fide and reasonable criticism of a judgment and bring courts generally in disrepute or attribute motives to Judges rendering judgment - Contemner found guilty of having committed gross criminal contempt of court - Order accordingly.
