Pine Chemicals Limited and Others v Assessing Authority and Others

Supreme Court of India

16 January 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

Veeraswami Ramaswami, S. Ranganathan, N.D. Ojha

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 830; (1992) 2 SCC 683; AIR 1992 SCW 3431; 1993 (41) ECC 75; 1993 (67) E.L.T. 25; JT 1992 (1) SC 220; 1992(1) SCALE 46; [1992] 1 S.C.R. 179; 1992 (85) STC 432

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Keywords: Declared Goods, Government Orders

Summary: Sales Tax -- Indirect Tax - General Sales Tax Act, 1962 - Government orders - Exemption - Applicability - Appellants in the W.P. had prayed for quashing the assessment order made by Assessing Authority - Assessment orders were regular assessment orders made on the findings that they were not entitled to exemption from pay of sales tax based on the findings of two Govt. Orders - Appellants also prayed for declaration that they were entitled to exemption from payment of tax under the Act on the finished goods produced by them for a period of five years when their company went into commercial production - Whether Govt. Order No. 159 Ind dt. 26-3-1971 and the amending Govt. Order No. 414 Ind dt. 25-8-1971 were orders of exemption referable to s. 5 of the Act - Held, that there was material on record to show that the Govt. made representations to industry that they would give tax exemptions and other incentives and invited entrepreneurs to establish their industries in that State - Appellants acting on those representations had set up their industries - HC was in error in thinking that the exemption order should be specific in favour of the appellant - Exemption as could be seen from the provisions of s. 5 of the Act could be in respect of any class of dealers of any goods or class or description of goods - There could be an exemption in an individual also but the power of exemption was not restricted to such cases alone - Hence, appellants were entitled to claim the benefit of the exemption for the entire period of five years calculated as per the terms of the Govt. orders - Appeals allowed.
