Mrs. Harpreet Kaurharvinder Singh Bedi v State Of Maharashtra And Anr.

Supreme Court of India

21 January 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

A.S. Anand, S. Ratnavel Pandian

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 848; (1992) 2 SCC 177; (1992) SCC (Cr) 370; AIR 1992 SC 979; AIR 1992 SCW 835; 1992 (1) CCR 626; 1992 (1) Crimes 589; 1992 CRLJ 769; 1992 CrLR(SC) 156; JT 1992 (1) SC 502; 1992(1) SCALE 142; [1992] 1 S.C.R. 234

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Maharashtra Prevention of Dangerous Activities of Slumlords, Bootleggers and Drug Offenders Act, 1981, ss. 3, 2(a), Explanation, 3(2), Proviso, 13 and 12 Preventive detention - (A) Detenue - Transporting illicit liquor - Keeping arms - Bootlegging - His activities creating fear psychosis - Whether falls within explanation to s. 2(a) - (B) Proviso to s. 3(2) has nothing to do with period of detention of detenue - Order of detention for more than three months - Whether vitiated ? - Held, (A) Falls within, not merely prejudicial to maintenance of 'law and order' but prejudicial to 'public order' - Not vitiated - Appeal dismissed.
