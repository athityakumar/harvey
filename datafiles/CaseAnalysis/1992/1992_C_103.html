Controller of Examinations and Others v G. S. Sunder and Another

Supreme Court of India

17 July 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, S. Mohan

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 510; (1993) Supp3 SCC 82; JT 1992 (4) SC 204; 1993(1) SCALE 604

Case Digest

Subject: Education

Summary: Education - Malpractice in B E examinations - Discretion of University - (A)Whether court must interfere in matters of enforcement of discipline? - Held, authorities in charge of education whose duty it is to conduct examinations fairly and properly, know best how to deal with situations of this character (B) Whether there is violation of principles of natural justice where charge of malpractice is admitted by accused? - Further held, there is no question of violation of principles of natural justice - First respondent knew charges fully and he had admitted his guilt -Appeal disposed of.
