Sri Chand v Inder and Others

Supreme Court of India

15 May 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

G.N. Ray, S. Mohan

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 480; (1993) Supp2 SCC 560; JT 1992 (4) SC 445; 1992(1) SCALE 1209; 1992 (1) UJ 725

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Punjab Tenancy Act, 1887 - Suit for title - Dismissal order - Maintainability - Appellant/plaintiff filed suit for Title before Trial Court against respondents/defendants no. 1,2 and 3, for declaration in respect of some agricultural lands - Trial Court dismissed the suit - Appellant filed appeal before Appellate Court - Appellate Court reversed judgment and decree of Trial Court and decreed suit in favor of appellant - Respondents filed appeal before HC - HC held that respondents no. 1 and 2 cultivated said land by exercising rights as subtenant and they had also got declaration from competent authority about their occupancy tenancy rights, accordingly, suit for appellant was liable to be dismissed - Hence instant appeal - Whether impugned order passed by HC was justified - Held, it was established by appellant that at inception of tenancy in favor of 'N' (maternal grandfather of appellant) a right of an occupancy tenant was given by landlord - In record of rights, such occupancy tenancy was not recorded for over long stretch of time either in favor of 'N' or in favor of 'B' (widow of 'N') - Admittedly 'B' had not got any declaration of occupancy tenancy right under the Act and it was not established for how many years actually 'B' had occupied and cultivated said land and when she had fulfilled conditions for getting status of an occupancy tenant - Respondents had possessed and cultivated said land even during life time of 'B' and such fact was reflected in Girdawari records - It was not necessary to investigate as to whether or not respondents had lawfully acquired any sub-tenancy right under 'B' because even if it was held that respondents had not acquired such sub-tenancy right, weakness of respondents case, could not strengthen case of appellant who must succeed by establishing his own case - It was an admitted position that respondents obtained declaration as occupancy tenants by competent Court under the Act and admittedly, respondents were occupying and cultivating disputed land - HC did not find any reason to interfere with decree of dismissal of suit of appellant - Appeal dismissed.
