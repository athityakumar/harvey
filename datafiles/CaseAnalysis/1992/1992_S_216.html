Bhupendra Nath Prasad v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

31 July 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Jayachandra Reddy, G.N. Ray

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 530; (1992) 3 SCC 547; (1992) SCC (Cr) 701; AIR 1993 SC 300; 1992 (2) CCR 222; 1992 (2) Crimes 165B; 1993 CRLJ 55; 1992 CRLR 581; JT 1992 (4) SC 309; 1992(2) SCALE 101; 1992 (2) UJ 573

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302 and 328 - Appellant convicted u/ss. 302 and 328 IPC,1860 - The proof of murder as together with authenticity of dying declaration in question - Deceased after coming from home of accused started vomiting and later died in hopital - Doctor said that death was due to cardio respiratory failure either due to taking excess wine or due to poison, none of reasons were concrete - Whether accused is to be convicted u/s. 302, IPC, 1860 based on evidence available? - Reasonable doubt arises about cause of death - No satisfactory evidence in this case that it was accused who gave betel containing poison to deceased - It is well settled that in a case depending upon circumstantial evidence all circumstances should conclusively point towards guilt of accused - Held, conviction and sentence awarded against appellant are set aside - Appeal allowed.
