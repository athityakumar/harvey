Janata Dal and Others v H. S. Chowdhary and Others

Supreme Court of India

28 August 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Jayachandra Reddy, S. Ratnavel Pandian

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 1257; (1992) 4 SCC 305; (1993) SCC (Cr) 36; AIR 1993 SC 892; AIR 1993 SCW 248; 1992 (3) CCR 326; 1992 (3) Crimes 199; 1993 CRLJ 600; 1993 CRLR 10; JT 1992 (5) SC 213; 1992(2) SCALE 338; [1992] Supp1 S.C.R. 226

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 32 and 226 -Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 397, 401 and 482 - Requirements of locus standi to file a P I L u/art. 32 of Constitution and inherent power conferred to High Courts u/s. 482 of CrPC, 1973 - CBI filed FIR filed persons involved in BOFORS - (A) Whether locus standi of a party in a public interest litigation is manadatory ? - Held, requirement of locus standi of a party to a litigation is mandatory, because legal capacity of party to any litigation whether in private or public action in relation to any specific remedy sought for has to be primarily ascertained at threshold - Strict rule of locus standi applicable to private litigation is relaxed in public interest litigation (PIL) and a broad rule is evolved which gives right of locus standi to any member of public acting bona fide and having sufficient interst in instituting an action for redressal of public wrong or public injury, but who is not a mere busy body or a meddlesome interloper - (B) Whether in suo motu exercise of inherent power single Judge of High Court is competent to direct office to issue notice for quashing prosecution ? - Powers possessed by HC u/s. 482 of CrPC, 1973 are very wide and very plenitude of power requires great caution in its exercise - No hard and fast rule can be laid down in regard to cases in which HC will exercise its extraordinary jurisdiction of quashing proceedings at any stage - Held, in instant case Judge had taken an extreme view that 'the court can take judicial notice of any illegality being committed by any court, with a view to prevent injury being caused to known or unknown aggrieved party' - Even when investigation is at its threshold - He overstepped his jurisdiction and made statement which was unwarranted and uncalled for, thus order of Judge taking suo motu cognizance u/ss. 397 and 401 of CrPC, 1973 r/w s. 482 CrPC, 1973 issuing show cause notice to CBI and State was quashed - Appeal partly allowed.
