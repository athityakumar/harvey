Dr. A. R. Sircar v State of Uttar Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

30 July 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, M.M. Punchhi

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 1197; (1993) Supp2 SCC 734; (1993) SCC (L&S) 896; 1993 (24) ATC 832; 1992 LabIC 2090; 1993 (1) SLR 457; 1992 (2) UPLBEC 1393

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Service - Uttar Pradesh State Colleges Medical Teachers Service Rules, 1990, r. 20 - Whether regularisation of respondent 4 and 5 entitle them to claim seniority over appellant ? - Held, r. 20(1) clearly states that if appointment order specifies a particular back date with effect from which a person is substantively appointed, that date will be date of this substantive appointment, otherwise relevant date will be date of order of appointment - Though appointment order was made on 31-10-1989, it related to vacancy of 1982-83 - There can, therefore, be no doubt whatsoever that appointment of appellant was for vacancy of 1982 - Had it not been for intervening stay order granted by High Court in Writ Petition No. 1545 of 1986, appellant would have been appointed long before regularisation of promotion of respondents 4 and 5 under 1988 Rules - Appeal allowed.
