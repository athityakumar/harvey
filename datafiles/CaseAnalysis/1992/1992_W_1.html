Union of India and Another v W. N. Chadha

Supreme Court of India

17 December 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Ratnavel Pandian, K. Jayachandra Reddy

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 207; (1993) Supp4 SCC 260; (1993) SCC (Cr) 1171; AIR 1993 SC 1082; 1993 CRLJ 859; JT 1992 (Supp) SC 255; [1992] Supp3 S.C.R. 594

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Non-bailable Warrant

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 41(a), 157, 166A, 41(g), 354 and 482 - Criminal Law Amendment Ordinance, 1944 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 21, 22 and 226 - Bofors case - Issue of letter rogatory with request to authorities in Switzerland for freezing certain bank accounts - (A) Meaning of letter rogatory - (B) Whether accused is entitled to opportunity of hearing at stage when investigating agency collecting material for ascertaining whether prima facie case is made out -(C) Duty of judge to not to rely on extraneous matter - Extensive use of secret document handed over in sealed over to judge for concluding that no prima facie case existed - (D) Constitution - Applicability and exclusion of rule audi alteram partem - Held, it is request of foreign court/judge for furnishing testimony of witness residing within its jurisdiction to issuing court - No, CrPC, 1973 itself provides for giving hearing to accused under specified circumstances, but not under Chap. XII of Code - Cannot be said that accused was deprived of his property, he is not entitled to prior notice and opportunity of being heard - Not sustainable - Administrative decision to be followed by full-fledged enquiry, it is a factor in deciding whether hearing should be afforded - Appeal allowed.
