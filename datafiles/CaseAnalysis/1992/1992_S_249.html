Thakore Dolji Vanvirji and Others v State of Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

15 September 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Jayachandra Reddy, G.N. Ray

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 70; (1993) Supp2 SCC 534; (1993) SCC (Cr) 704; AIR 1993 SC 209; 1992 CRLJ 3953; 1992 CRLR 674; 1993 (1) GLH 1040; JT 1992 (6) SC 66; 1992(2) SCALE 774

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Supreme Court (Enlargement of Criminal Appellate Jurisdiction) Act, s. 2 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 323, 147, 148, 149 and 34 - Appellants acquitted by Trial Court but on appeal by Govt. convicted by HC - Hence, present appeal - When accused themselves do not want to rely on injuries found on them and do not any manner connect receipt of injuries to present occurrence, question of explaining same by prosecution does not arise - Held, evidence of eye-witnesses natural as they live in vicinity - Evidence of eye-witnesses consistent with medical evidence - On a careful examination of entire prosecution case and surrounding circumstances, common object of unlawful assembly was only to cause grievous hurt - A-1 acted in his own individual manner and proved fatal - No doubt he inflicted only one blow but having regard to nature of weapon and vital organ on which blow was inflicted and medical evidence regarding result of injury proving fatal, he must be held responsible for offence of murder - Order accordingly.
