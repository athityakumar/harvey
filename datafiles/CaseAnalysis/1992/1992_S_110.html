Sewaram v Sobaran Singh

Supreme Court of India

15 September 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

N.M. Kasliwal, Kuldip Singh

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 601; (1993) Supp2 SCC 46; AIR 1993 SC 212; JT 1992 (5) SC 447; 1992(2) SCALE 674; [1992] Supp1 S.C.R. 512; 1993 (1) UJ 67

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Election; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Election - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 9-A, 80, 84, 100, 103 and 116-A - Whether election of appellant was void on ground that work contract taken by appellant with PWD of Govt. of Madhya Pradesh was subsisting on date of filing nominations? - Question of subsistence of a contract with appropriate Govt. making it a disqualification u/s. 9-A is a question of fact - Held, in present case, tender was accepted on 16-04-1987; cls. (25) of contract provide that contract shall not be assigned or sub-let without written approval of Divisional Officer - Nothing on record to show that at any time during subsistence of this contract appellant had ever intimated PWD that contract may be transferred or assigned in favour of firm - Till 01-02-1990 date of filing nomination paper, contract with PWD was continuing and dealt with appellant in his individual capacity and not with firm - Contract had not come to an end, and was subsisting, thereby incurring a disqualification u/s. 9-A, therefore, as held by High Court, contract with PWD was subsisting on date of filing nomination and date of scrutiny of nomination papers and appellant thus incurred a disqualification for contesting election of Morena Assembly Constituency in State of M P - Appeal dismissed.
