Indore Municipal Corporation And Another v Dr. Hemalata And Others

Supreme Court of India

24 February 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 447; (2010) 4 SCC 435; AIR 2010 SC 1758; JT 2010 (2) SC 380; 2010(2) SCALE 599

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Madhya Pradesh Municipal Corporation Act, 1956, Madhya Pradesh Bhumi Vikas Rules, 1984

Summary: Land & Property - Municipalities & Local Governments - Conversion of permitted user - Order of local government - Legality - Respondent/Company sought permission from appellant/Municipal Corporation for the development of a land for residential-cum-commercial purpose - Appellant granted permission and thereby, respondent commenced construction - Joint Inspection team of the appellant approved the construction - Respondent applied for the service certificate to enable them to apply for service connections, however, appellant issued show-cause notice alleging that the respondent had made the actual construction which exceeded the sanctioned area - Respondent replied to the show-cause notice - Appellant passed the impugned order of demolition - Respondent challenged the demolition order before the HC - HC allowed respondent's writ petition and quashed the impugned order - (A) Whether respondent's construction for commercial use as against the sanction plan for residential-cum-commercial use, amounted to violation of building rules - Held, even before the completion of the construction and obtaining of occupation certificate, without issuing a show-cause notice alleging misuse of permitted user, an order has been issued by the appellant alleging violation of the permitted user - No occasion for the appellant to assume that the respondent is likely to violate the sanctioned user - After the issue of occupancy certificate, if there is any violation of sanctioned use, it is always open to the Municipal Corporation to take appropriate action in accordance with law at that stage - Therefore, findings in the impugned order is unwarranted and premature - (B) Construction beyond sanctioned area - Legality - Whether respondents have made construction beyond the sanctioned area - Held, HC after examining the records has concluded that there was no excess construction - Joint Inspection report of the appellant confirmed that the area constructed was less than what was sanctioned and that there was no violation - Allegation of the appellant in the impugned order that there was an excess construction, is without basis - Appellant sanctioned construction of building and the construction is less than the sanctioned area, the question of appellants holding that there was excess construction, does not arise - No interference with the findings of HC - Appeal dismissed.
