Mohd. Yunus Khan v State Of U.P. & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

28 September 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 796; (2010) 10 SCC 539; 2011 (1) AWC 958; JT 2010 (10) SC 341; 2011 (3) MLJ(SC) 480; 2010(10) SCALE 286; [2010] 12 S.C.R. 448; 2010 (6) SLR 88; 2011 (1) UPLBEC 81

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Armed Forces, Uttar Pradesh Police Officers of the Subordinate Ranks (Punishment and Appeal) Rules, 1991, Guard and Escort Rules

Summary: Service - Uttar Pradesh Police Officers of the Subordinate Ranks (Punishment and Appeal) Rules, 1991 - Disciplinary Proceedings - Violation of principles of natural justice - Commandant imposed punishment of 10 days punishment drill on appellant (Head Constable in Provincial Armed Constabulary) on the ground that the appellant remained absent from duty for 25 minutes - Upon protest by appellant, Commandant enhanced punishment to 10 days confinement in a cell - However, as appellant refused to serve the punishment, enquiry was initiated against the appellant by the Commandant, for disobedience of the order of punishment - Inquiry officer conducted the enquiry and held that the appellant was guilty of negligence and disobedience and recommended his removal from service - Thereafter, Commandant passed the order imposing the punishment of termination from service - Appeal filed thereagainst was dismissed by Appellate Authority - In appeal Tribunal held that absence of appellant from duty as Guard Commander for 25 minutes was bona fide and permissible under the statutory rules, however, not obeying the order of punishment was a case of indiscipline and thus, order of termination was justified - On appeal, HC held that charge of disobedience of the orders of the higher authority stood proved and the enquiry was conducted in accordance with law - Hence, present appeal - (A) Whether imposition of punishment for absence from duty for 25 minutes was warranted? - Held, absence of appellant from duty as Guard Commander for 25 minutes was bona fide and permissible under the statutory rules - Hence, imposition of punishment of punishment drill for 10 days for the said absence was unwarranted - (B) Whether enhancement of punishment of the appellant for confinement in cell for ten days was justified? - Held, a protest/disobedience against an illegal order may not be termed as misconduct in every case - Hence, protest by the appellant against the imposition of the punishment could not warrant enhancement of punishment of the appellant for confinement for ten days - (C) Whether disciplinary proceedings against appellant was in violation of the principles of natural justice and impermissible in law? - Held, disobedience of enhanced punishment could not, in present case, warrant initiation of disciplinary proceedings by Commandant against appellant - Hence, initiation of disciplinary proceedings against the appellant and the conclusion thereof by the imposition of the punishment by the Commandant, who had himself been a witness, was in violation of the principles of natural justice and thus, stood vitiated - (D) Whether order of punishment stood vitiated as Authority who initiated disciplinary proceedings against appellant became a witness before the inquiry officer appointed by him, who was subordinate to him in his office and also accepted enquiry report and passed the order of punishment? - Held, if a person appears as a witness in disciplinary proceedings, he cannot be an inquiry officer nor can he pass the order of punishment as a disciplinary authority - As an apprehension of bias operates as a disqualification for a person to act as adjudicator - Further, no person can be a Judge in his own cause and no witness can certify that his own testimony is true - Hence, Commandant could not himself become the Judge of his own cause - Also, Commandant could not appoint his own subordinate as the inquiry officer - Further, once the Commandant concerned appeared as a witness himself in the enquiry, he could not pass the order of punishment - Thus, the order of punishment stood vitiated - (E) Whether Appellate Authority could consider past conduct of appellant? - Held, Appellate Authority could not consider the past conduct of the appellant to justify the order of punishment passed by the disciplinary authority without bringing it to the notice of the appellant - Moreover, error of violating the principles of natural justice by the Disciplinary Authority was of such a grave nature that under no circumstance can the past conduct of the appellant, even if not satisfactory, be taken into consideration - Hence, order of punishment was null and void and therefore, cannot be given effect to - Appeal allowed.
