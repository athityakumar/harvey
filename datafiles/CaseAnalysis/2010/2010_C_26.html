Centre For Public Interest Litigation And Others v The Union Of India And Others

Supreme Court of India

16 December 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 1086; (2011) 1 SCC 560; JT 2010 (13) SC 619; 2011 (3) MLJ(SC) 1084; 2010(13) SCALE 501

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure; Telecom

Summary: Telecom - Practice & Procedure - Criminal - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 136 and 151 - Telecom Regulatory Authority of India Act, 1997 - Indian Telegraph Act, 1885 - 2G Spectrum Scam - Court monitored investigation - Writ petition filed by appellant for a court monitored investigation by the Central Bureau of Investigation (CBI) or a Special Investigating Team into '2G Spectrum Scam' for unearthing the role of respondent No.5 Shri A. Raja, the then Union Minister for the Department of Telecommunications (DoT), senior officers of that department, middlemen, businessmen and others was dismissed - Hence, present appeal - Parties filed affidavits and large number of documents including performance audit report (draft and final) prepared by the Comptroller and Auditor General of India (CAG) on the issue of licences and allocation of 2G Spectrum by the Department of Telecommunications, Ministry of Communications and Information Technology, which has been submitted to the President of India a compact disc allegedly containing conversation of Ms. Niira Radia with some public representatives, businessmen, journalists and alleged middlemen and written submissions - Held, HC committed a serious error by dismissing the writ petition at the threshold ignoring that the issues raised by the appellants, whose bonafides have not been doubted, are of great public importance - Allegations contained in the writ petition and the affidavits which are supported by the documents produced by them and report of the Central Vigilance Commission and the findings recorded by the CAG in the Performance Audit Report, need a thorough and impartial investigation - However, at this stage, it is not necessary to appoint a Special Team to investigate 2G Spectrum Scam because the Government of India has, agreed for a Court monitored investigation - Directions issued to ensure that in comprehensive and coordinated investigation is conducted by the CBI and the Enforcement Directorate without any hindrance - Order accordingly.
