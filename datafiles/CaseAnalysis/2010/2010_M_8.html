M. Chandra v M. Thangamuthu and another

Supreme Court of India

07 September 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Dattu, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 765; (2010) 9 SCC 712; AIR 2011 SC 146; 2011 (1) CLT(SC) 98; JT 2010 (10) SC 315; 2010 (8) MLJ(SC) 739; 2010 (4) RCR(Civil) 696; 2010(9) SCALE 145; [2010] 11 S.C.R. 38

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Keywords: Scheduled Caste, Reserved Constituency

Summary: Election - Representation of the Peoples Act, 1951, s. 81 r/w. ss. 5(a), 100 (1)(a) and 125-A - Appellant (Candidate of the AIADMK party) and Respondent no. 1 contested election of Tamil Nadu Legislature Assembly - Result of the election was declared and appellant was declared as the successful candidate - Respondent no.1 challenged the result submitting that impugned assembly constituency was a reserved constituency and only candidates belonging to the Scheduled Caste were eligible to contest the elections from such constituency - Respondent no. 1 further submitted that appellant filed false declaration by claiming to be a member of a Scheduled Caste while she was a Christian - Appellant contended that she was born to a Christian father and Hindu mother and her father deserted her and her mother when she was a child - Appellant further claims that she converted to Hinduism in the Arya Samaj in the year 1994 and on 23.01.1995 she married one 'X' (who had converted to Hinduism in the year 1975) who belonged to Pallan caste - HC held that appellant belongs to Pallan Christian Community and she could not have contested the Assembly elections from reserved constituency and, therefore, declared her election as void - Hence, present appeal - (A) Burden of proof - Held, for an election result to be annulled, there must be positive evidence to prove illegality of the election - The natural corollary is that the person who files an election petition, must have a clear and definite case, to prove that the election was illegal - Therefore the burden of proof shall lie on the petitioner filing the election petition - In present case, the evidence put forward by the appellant is consistent and reliable - (B) Discrepancy in school record - Held, it was recorded that the appellant belonged to Indian Christian Pallan community and studied in CSI School while in the declaration filed along with nomination papers, it is stated that she studied in Govt. High School and, therefore, as she had made a false declaration she was ineligible to contest from the reserved constituency - Appellant in her counter affidavit and also in her evidence has explained that the discrepancy in the declaration form filled by her brother, who was assisting her in filing the nomination papers - Discrepancy pointed out by the election petitioner has been properly explained by the appellant and this by itself cannot be a ground to hold that the appellant was ineligible to contest from the reserved constituency - (C) Reliance on birth records, entries in the telephone application and voters list - Held, reliance placed on the birth records, entries in the telephone application and voters list cannot be the sole ground for proving that the appellant is professing Christianity - Records could have been made by people other than the appellant or her husband - As far as the birth register is concerned, it is clear that the birth was reported not by the parents, but the Village Head Nurse - Similarly, it is very likely that after her conversion, the appellant never went ahead and changed the name appearing in the voter's list - Also it is not mentioned in the voter's list as to what religion the appellant professes - (D) Validity of community Certificate and Evidence Act - Held, there is nothing on record to show that the community certificate was issued illegally or in contravention of the valid procedure - Validity of the issuance of the community certificate was presumed unless shown otherwise by the respondent no.1, who clearly failed to do so - It is also pertinent to mention that no one raised any objection to the appellant filing her nomination for the Assembly elections in 2006 from the reserved constituency - All the issues have been raised after the appellant won the election from the impugned constituency - (E) Testimony of witnesses - Held, all the witnesses of the respondent are affiliated to the rival party DMK in some capacity or the other as opposed to the appellant who represented the AIADMK party - Moreover, the testimony of all the three witnesses are highly contradictory and hearsay as all the three witnesses have come to know about the religion of the appellant and her husband from other people - Pleadings and the evidence adduced in support of the same requires to be read conjointly and not by applying the hypertechnical approach of reading between the lines to arrive at a finding against a candidate in an election petition who has support of the majority of the people in the constituency - Hence, impugned order of the HC set aside - Appeal allowed.
