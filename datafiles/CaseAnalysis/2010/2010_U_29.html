Construction of Park at Noida Near Okhla Bird Sanctuary Anand Arya and another v Union Of India & Ors

Supreme Court of India

03 December 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Aftab Alam, S.H. Kapadia, K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 1045; (2011) 1 SCC 744; 2011 (3) AWC 2551; JT 2010 (13) SC 403; 2010(13) SCALE 50

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Environment, Wildlife & Animal

Keywords: Andhra Pradesh Forest Act, 1967, Andhra Pradesh Scheduled Areas Land Transfer Regulation, 1959

Summary: Environment, Wildlife & Animal - Constitution - Forest (Conservation) Act, 1980 - Environment Protection Act, 1986 - Construction of Park at Noida Near Okhla Bird Sanctuary - Project of the Uttar Pradesh government at NOIDA - Applicants filed objections to construction of Park at Noida Near Okhla Bird Sanctuary - It was stated that project, undertaken at the instance of Uttar Pradesh Government was a "huge unauthorized construction" and a very large number of trees were cut down for clearing the ground for the project - (A) Restriction on de-reservation of forests or use of forest land for non-forest purpose - Whether there was violation of s. 2 (ii) of the Act, 1980? - It was contended that trees that were felled down for the project formed a "forest" as the term was construed by SC in its order T.N. Godavarman Thirumulkpad v. Union of India & Ors., 1996 INDLAW SC 2014 - Held, restriction imposed by s. 2(ii) is in respect of forest land - In the present case, CEC on a consideration of all the materials made available to it, including the report of the FSI held that the project site was not a forest or a deemed forest or a forest-like area in terms of the order of SC - Hence, project site is not forest land and the construction of the project without the prior permission from the Central Government does not in any way contravene s. 2 of the Act, 1980 - (B) Project and EIA Notification 2006 - Power of Central Government to take measures to protect and improve environment - It was contended that the construction of the project was started by the U.P. Government without obtaining the prior environmental clearance from the Central Government or the State Level Environment Impact Assessment Authority in complete violation of the notification issued by the Central Government on September 14, 2006 u/s. 3 (3) of the Act, 1986 - EIA notification provides that all projects and activities enumerated in its Schedule would require prior environmental clearance before any construction work or preparation of land for the project is started on the project or activity - Held, for the project under consideration, the relevant entries in the schedule are 8(a) and 8(b) - Item 8(a) deals with Building and Construction projects and Item 8(b) deals with Townships and Area Development projects - In the facts and circumstances, applying the test of 'Dominant Purpose or Dominant Nature' of the project or the "Common Parlance" test, i.e. how a common person using it and enjoying its facilities would view it, the project can only be categorized under item 8(b) of the schedule as a Township and Area Development project" - But under that category it does not come up to the threshold marker inasmuch as the total area of the project (33.43 hectares) is less than 50 hectares and its built-up area even if the hard landscaped area and the covered areas are put together comes to 1,05,544.49 square metres, i.e., much below the threshold marker of 1,50,000 square metres - Hence, project does not fall within the ambit of the EIA notification dated September 14, 2006 - (C) Project and Okhla Bird Sanctuary - Project being located virtually adjoining the Okhla Bird Sanctuary - It was contended that very close proximity of the project site to the bird sanctuary raises issues of serious concern and poses a dilemma - Held, environment is a matter directly under the Constitution and if the Court perceives any project or activity as harmful or injurious to the environment it would feel obliged to step in - In the present case, none of the expert bodies has taken the view that the project is so calamitous or ruinous for the bird sanctuary that it needs to be altogether scrapped in order to save the Sanctuary - Expert bodies have given recommendations which allow the completion of the project subject to certain conditions - There was no justification for directing the demolition of the constructions made in the project, as prayed for on behalf of the applicants - Project allowed to be completed, subject, to the conditions suggested by the expert bodies and further subject to the directions - Order accordingly.
