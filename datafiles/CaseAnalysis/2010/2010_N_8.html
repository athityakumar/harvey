Nagbhai Najbhai Khackar v State of Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

09 September 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan, Swatanter Kumar

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 736; (2010) 10 SCC 594; JT 2010 (10) SC 76; 2010(9) SCALE 242; [2010] 11 S.C.R. 414

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Constitution (Thirty-Fourth Amendment) Act, 1974, Grass, Gujarat Agricultural Lands Ceiling Act, 1960, Saurashtra Land Reforms Act, 1951, Saurashtra Barkhali Abolition Act, 1951, Saurashtra Estates Acquisition Act, 1952, Gujarat Agricultural Lands Ceiling Amendment Act, 1972

Summary: Land & Property - Gujarat Agricultural Lands Ceiling Act, 1960, ss. 2(17) and 5 - Gujarat Agricultural Lands Ceiling Amendment Act, 1972 - Applicability of the Gujarat Agricultural Lands Ceiling Amendment Act, 1972 which came into force w.e.f. 1.4.1976 to the 'bid lands' - Amendment Act was challenged only for the reason that u/s. 5(1)(a) of Saurashtra Estates Acquisition Act, 1952 no bid lands which were uncultivable waste land vested in the State Government, which bid lands are now sought to be covered by 1960 Act on account of the Amendment Act - Appellant contended that once such "bid lands" stood excluded from vesting under the 1952 Act, the same could not be included for calculating the total holding to determine the ceiling limit under the 1960 Act, as amended - (A) Whether bid lands were required to be taken into consideration for the purpose of land ceiling under the Gujarat Agricultural Lands Ceiling Act, 1960, as amended which amendment came into force from 1.4.1976? - Held, definition of 'land' is specifically amended by the Amendment Act to include 'bid lands' of Girasdars or Barkhalidars in s. 2(17)(ii)(c) - Also, statement of objects and reasons of the Amending Act also makes it clear that there was a specific legislative intent of including 'bid lands' of Girasdars or Barkhalidars within the definition of 'land' - This inclusion does not make any distinction between cultivable and uncultivable bid lands - Insertion of bid lands in s. 2(17) is without any such qualification - Therefore, this specific intent of the Legislature must be given its full meaning - Under the present Act, s. 2(1) defines the use of land for the purpose of grazing cattle as an agricultural purpose - Thus, 'bid lands' fall u/s. 2(1) of the Act - (B) Interpretation of the definition of the words 'dry crop land' in Explanation I(e) to s. 2(6) - It was contended that bid lands were excluded from the definition of dry crop land and they do not fall within any of the categories of classes of land under the Act and, therefore, cannot be subjected to ceiling under 1960 Act - Held, definition has two parts, namely, (i) 'land other than the land specified in paragraphs (a) to (c)' and (ii) 'grass land' - Thus, the first part includes all lands other than those specified in paragraphs (a) to (c) - Therefore, once the subject land falls in the first part of definition of the word 'dry crop land' which land comes u/s. 2(17) and which falls outside paragraphs (a) to (c) then such lands would fall within the definition of the words 'dry crop land' - Further, proviso to s. 5 itself makes it clear that by the Amending Act the Legislature was placing a ceiling even on desert and hill areas - Legislature intended to include even desert and hills in drought-prone areas within the definition of "dry crop land" - Once such lands are used for grazing of cattle, s. 2(1) of the Act would kick in and consequently the "bid lands" would stand covered by the Act - - Appeals dismissed.
