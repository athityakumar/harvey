K. K. Poonacha v State of Karnataka and others

Supreme Court of India

07 September 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 716; (2010) 9 SCC 671; JT 2010 (9) SC 517; 2011 (1) KarLJ 177; 2010(9) SCALE 119; [2010] 10 S.C.R. 1022

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property

Keywords: Competence Of The Legislature, Madras General Sales Tax Act, 1939, Constitutionality, Unconstitutionality, Bombay Prohibition Act, State Sales Tax Act, Constitution (Fourth Amendment) Act, 1955, Constitution Act, Indian Press (Emergency Powers) Act, 1931, Sales Tax Laws Validation Act, 1956, C.P. & Berar Motor Vehicles (Amendment) Act, 1947, Transfer Act, U.P. Sugar Undertakings (Acquisition) Act, 1971, Financial Act, Rajasthan Passengers and Goods Taxation (Amendment and Validation) Act, 1964, U.P. Land Tenures (Regulation of Transfers) Act, 1952, U.P. Transport Service (Development) Act, 1955, Orissa Estates Abolition (Amendment) Act, 1954, Indian Forest (U.P. Amendment) Act, 1956, Rajasthan Finance Act, 1961, U.P. State Transport Act, 1951, State Finance Act, 1961, Private Forests Act, 1948, Forest Amendment Act

Summary: Constitution - Land & Property - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 13(2), 31(3) and 255 - Bangalore Development Authority Act, 1976 - Non-compliance of art. 31(3) - Bangalore Development Authority Act, 1976 - Whether Act is liable to be declared void on the ground that the same was not reserved for the consideration of the President and did not receive Presidential assent as per the requirement of art. 31(3) of the Constitution? - Held, a post-Constitution law is void ab initio if it is not within the domain of the Legislature or is violative of the rights conferred by Part III of the Constitution - If the law is within the legislative competence of the Union or State and does not infringe any of the rights conferred by Part III of the Constitution, then the same cannot be declared void on the ground of non-compliance of the procedural requirement of prior recommendation or sanction, if assent is given in the manner provided u/art. 255 of the Constitution - Such law remains on the statute book but cannot be enforced till the assent is given by the President - Once the assent is given, the law becomes effective and enforceable - If the provision requiring pre-enactment sanction or post-enactment assent of the President is repealed, then the law becomes effective and enforceable from the date of repeal and such law cannot be declared unconstitutional only on the ground that the same was not reserved for consideration of the President and did not receive his assent - Further, procedural provision contained in clause (3) of art. 31 did not create any substantive right in favour of any citizen or non citizen like those conferred by other Articles of Part III including clauses (1) and (2) of art. 31 - Therefore, the Act cannot be declared unconstitutional or void only on the ground that the same was not reserved for consideration of the President and did not receive his assent - The only consequence of non-compliance of clause (3) of art. 31 was that the same did not become effective and the State Government or the BDA could not have taken action for implementation of the provisions contained therein - Further, once art. 31 was repealed, the necessity of reserving the Act for consideration of the President and his assent disappeared and the provisions contained therein automatically became effective - (B) Whether the Act is a law enacted by the Legislature of the State with reference to Entry 5 of List II or it is a law enacted under Entry 42 of List III? - Held, Act was enacted by the Legislature of the State of Karnataka to provide for the establishment of a Development Authority for the development of the city of Bangalore and the area adjacent thereto and for matters connected therewith - It is not a law enacted for acquisition or requisitioning of property - Provisions relating to acquisition of land contained in Chapter 4 (Sections 35 and 36) are only incidental to the main object of enactment, namely development of the city of Bangalore and area adjacent thereto - Appeals dismissed.
