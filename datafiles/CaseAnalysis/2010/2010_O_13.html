Om Prakash Singh v Union Of India & Others

Supreme Court of India

20 July 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Dalveer Bhandari, Deepak Verma

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 515; (2010) 12 SCC 667; AIR 2010 SC 3557; 2010 (6) AWC 5926; 2010 (126) FLR 897; JT 2010 (7) SC 188; 2010(7) SCALE 99; [2010] 8 S.C.R. 490; 2011 (1) SLR 62

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces; Service

Keywords: Disability Pension, Pension Regulations for the Army, 1961, Entitlement Rules for Casualty Pensionary Awards, 1982

Summary: Defence & Security Forces - Service - Pension Regulations for the Army 1961 - Part I, regn. 173 - Entitlement Rules for Casualty Pensionary Awards 1982, rr. 5 and 14(b) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Entitlement for disability pension - Appellant was enrolled in the Territorial Army as a Sepoy - At the time of joining service the appellant was put through the medical test and was found medically fit - While serving in the Army, appellant contacted the disease known as 'Unspecified Psychosis' which is a psychiatric disorder - On the recommendations of the Medical Board which assessed the appellant's disability as 40%, he was invalided out from the service - Appellant claimed for grant of disability pension which was rejected by the competent authority - Appellant filed writ petition against said order which was dismissed by HC - Appellant challenged said order submitting that whether a disability is attributable to or aggravated by the military service, has to be determined under the Entitlement Rules for Casualty Pensionary Awards 1982 and opinion of the medical board in respect of attributability does not get supremacy and it is to be treated only of recommendatory nature - Whether appellant is entitled to disability pension? - Held, no - Medical Board is an expert body and they take into consideration all relevant factors and essential practice before arriving at any opinion and its opinion is entitled to be given due weight, merit credence and value - In the present case, the Medical Board has given a unanimous opinion that the disease of the appellant was neither attributable to nor aggravated by the military service - The findings of the Medical Board has been accepted by the DB - Thus, no interference is called for and appellant is not entitled to the disability pension - Appeal dismissed.
