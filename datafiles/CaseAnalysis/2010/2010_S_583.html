Utpal Das and another v State of West Bengal

Supreme Court of India

07 May 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

B. SUDARSHAN REDDY, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 355; (2010) 6 SCC 493; AIR 2010 SC 1894; 2010 (3) CalLT 86; 2010 CRLJ 2867; JT 2010 (4) SC 587; 2010 (3) RCR(Criminal) 144; 2010(5) SCALE 328; [2010] 6 S.C.R. 495; 2010 (2) UC 741

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 366, 368 and 376 - Rape - Conviction - Challenged - A case u/ss. 366, 368 and 376 r/w s. 34 of the IPC filed against the accused persons - Trial Court held that prosecution failed to prove its case beyond reasonable doubt and accordingly acquitted all the accused persons - On appeal, HC upon re-appreciation of the evidence and the totality of circumstances held that the Trial Court extended benefit of doubt to the appellants under misconception of facts and wrong appreciation of evidence and accordingly came to the conclusion that the appellants were guilty of the offence punishable u/s. 376 r/w 34 of the IPC - However, the HC confirmed the acquittal of the other accused - Hence, present appeal - Whether prosecution established the case against the appellants beyond reasonable doubt? - Held, no reason to disbelieve the evidence of prosecutrix who meticulously narrated the sequence of events - There are no material contradictions in PW-14 and PW-6 evidence so as to disbelieve their evidence - Version given by prosecutrix receives complete corroboration from the evidence of PW-6 - Further, mere fact that no injuries were found on private parts of her body cannot be the ground to hold that she was not subjected to any sexual assault - HC rightly observed that the prosecutrix made no mistake in identifying the two appellants, and that, based on the evidence of PW-1, PW-6 and the Prosecutrix herself, it was satisfactorily proved that the two appellants were actually the persons were guilty of offence punishable u/s. 376 r/w 34 of the IPC - Appeal dismissed.
