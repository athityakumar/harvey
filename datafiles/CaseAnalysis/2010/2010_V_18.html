Trimex International Fze Limited. Dubai v Vedanta Aluminium Limited., India

Supreme Court of India

22 January 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 57; (2010) 3 SCC 1; AIR 2010 SC (Supp) 455; 2010 (2) AWC 1170; 2010 (2) CTC 581; JT 2010 (1) SC 474; 2010 (1) MLJ(SC) 1322; 2010 (1) RCR(Civil) 887; 2010(1) SCALE 574; [2010] 1 S.C.R. 820

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration & Conciliation Act, 1996, s. 11(6) - Indian Contract Act 1872, s. 4 - Appointment of arbitrator - Petitioner submitted a commercial offer through e-mail for the supply of bauxite to the respondent - Respondent conveyed their acceptance of offer through e-mail confirming the supply of 5 shipments of bauxite - On the basis of said acceptance by the respondent, petitioner concluded the deal with the bauxite supplier and entered into a binding Charter Party Agreement with the ship owner - A meeting was held between the representatives of the parties and acceptance of the offer was acknowledged by the respondent in the minutes of the meeting - A formal contract containing a detailed arbitration clause was also sent by the respondent to the petitioner which was accepted by the petitioner with some changes - Respondent requested the petitioner to hold the next consignment until further notice - Petitioner informed the respondent of its inability to postpone the cargo and requested them to sign the purchase agreement - In the meantime, ship owners also nominated the ship for loading the material - Petitioner terminated the contract reserving the right to claim for damages which was informed by petitioner to ship owners also - Ship owners made a claim of 1 million US$ towards commercial settlement - Petitioner informed the respondent to pay a sum of 1 million US$ towards compensation for loss on account of the estimated loss for 5 shipments and 0.8 million towards compensation for loss of profit and other costs and expenses for cancellation of the order which was rejected by respondent - After negotiations, a settlement was arrived at between the ship owners and the petitioner to pay a lump-sum of 600,000 US$ which was paid by petitioner - Petitioner served a notice on the respondent to make payment or treat the notice for referring the dispute to arbitration and informed the respondent about nominating a retired Judge as the arbitrator from their side - Respondent rejected the arbitration notice stating that there was no concluded contract between the parties - Petitioner filed the present petition for appointment of an Arbitrator - Whether there exists a valid contract with the arbitration clause? - Held, minute to minute correspondence exchanged between the parties, all the conditions prescribed which had been laid down, awareness of urgency of accepting the offer without any further delay to avoid variation in the freight or other factors, coupled with the e-mail sent under the subject 're: offer for imported bauxite' stated in unequivocal terms, would clearly show that after understanding all the details and the confirmation by the respondent, the petitioner sent a reply stating that 'thanks for the confirmation, just in time to go to the ship owners' - All the above details clearly establish that both the parties were aware of various conditions and understood the terms and finally the charter was entered into a contract by the parties - Once the offer of the petitioner was accepted following a very strict time schedule, the respondent could not escape from the obligations that flowed from such an action - Petitioner has made out a case for appointment of an arbitrator - Accordingly, former Judge of SC is appointed as an Arbitrator to resolve the dispute between the parties - Petition allowed
