Tassadiq Hussain v Mohd. Rashid Qureshi & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

23 November 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Panchal, Gyan Sudha Misra

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 987; (2010) 13 SCC 787; JT 2010 (13) SC 175; 2011 (1) RCR(Civil) 942; 2010(12) SCALE 340

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Election; Parliament & Legislature

Keywords: Delimitation, Jammu and Kashmir Representation of the People Act, 1957, Jammu and Kashmir Land Revenue Act, 1996, Constitution of Jammu and Kashmir, 1957, J & K Revenue Department Reorganisation Act, 2008

Summary: (A) Election - Parliament & Legislature - Jammu and Kashmir Representation of the People Act, 1957 - Constitution of Jammu & Kashmir, 1956, s. 50(3) - Legislative Council Election - Validity - Election Commission of India issued notification for election to six vacant seats in Legislative Council including one seat reserved for the residents of Poonch District - Appellant and respondents filed their nomination papers to the reserved seat - 1st respondent raised objections to the candidature of appellant before the Returning Officer on the ground that appellant was not the resident of Poonch District but the resident of Rajouri District - Returning Officer rejected 1st respondent's objection on the basis of a clarification provided by the Election Commission - Consequently, election held and appellant was declared as returned candidate - 1st respondent challenged the election of appellant before the HC HC - HC allowed 1st respondent's election petition and election of the appellant declared to be void whereas in his place the 1st respondent was declared as elected - Whether the expression 'Poonch District' used in the proviso to s. 50(3) of the Constitution, reserving a seat in the Legislative Council for the resident of Poonch District means Poonch District as it existed when the election notification was published or it includes Rajouri also - Held, if the expression 'Poonch District', appearing in the proviso to s. 50(3) of the Constitution, is interpreted in its ordinary and grammatical meaning, it means District Poonch as was constituted at the time when election notification was published - Word 'resident of Poonch District' in its ordinary sense would mean a resident of Poonch District and resident of Rajouri cannot be regarded as resident of Poonch District - Expression 'Poonch District' must be understood in its natural, ordinary or popular meaning - As per the definition of the term 'District' mentioned in the provisions of Revenue Department Reorganization Act, 2008, the word 'District' means a 'Revenue District' and hence the expression 'District Poonch' appearing in the proviso to s. 50(3) of the Constitution, does not include Rajouri - General Department of the State issued Government Order No. 137-C of 1967 dated 30-9-1967 splitting Poonch District into two Districts, i.e., (1) Poonch District comprising Haveli and Mendhar Tehsils with Head Quarters at Poonch and (2) Rajouri District comprising Rajouri and Nowshera Tehsils with Head Quarters at Rajouri, in the interest of revenue and law and order - By the said order Tehsils Rajouri and Nowshera which were excluded from the Poonch District, constituted a separate district known as District Rajouri - Further, Revenue Department of the State issued notification SRO 444 dated 21-10-1967 u/s. 5 of the Jammu and Kashmir Land Revenue Act, 1996, directing that the territorial limits of Tehsil Rajouri and Nowshera should be excluded from the existing Poonch-Rajouri District and should constitute a separate District to be known as District Rajouri - No fresh notification u/s. 5 of the Jammu and Kashmir Land Revenue Act was required for creation of the District Rajouri as an independent District, yet the same was issued again to emphasis that Tehsil Rajouri and Nowshera excluded from administratively known as Poonch-Rajouri District and constituted into a separate District known as District Rajouri - Therefore, expression 'Poonch District' used in the proviso to s. 50(3) of the Constitution does not include Rajouri District - Appeal dismissed.

(B) Election - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of Jammu & Kashmir, 1956, s. 50(3) - Election Petition - Burden of Proof - Whether burden to prove that the expression 'Resident of Poonch District' does not include a resident of Rajouri is on 1st respondent, who has filed petition challenging the election of the appellant as a Member of the Legislative Council and as the said burden was not discharged, the petition should have been dismissed - Held, impugned HC order made it evident that both the parties had accepted before the HC that issue in the case was purely a legal issue to be decided on the interpretation of the provisions of the Constitution - In such circumstances question of burden of proof never arose before the HC - Appellant had a specific case that the expression 'a resident of District Poonch' includes a resident of District Rajouri and, therefore, the appellant could have brought the evidence on record to substantiate the said plea - However, no material was brought on record by the appellant to indicate, even remotely, that a resident of Rajouri was called or known as resident of Poonch District for the purposes of the proviso to s. 50(3) of the Constitution - Therefore, HC was justified in not dismissing the petition filed by the 1st respondent questioning the election of the appellant as a Member of the Legislative Council on the ground that the 1st respondent had failed to discharge the burden of proof - Appeal dismissed.

(C) Administrative - Election - Constitution of Jammu & Kashmir, 1956, s. 138 - Alteration of boundaries of Districts - Jurisdiction of Election Commission - Whether Election Commission of India has the power to fix the constituency since it has the power to superintend and control elections in the State u/s. 138 of the Constitution - Held, u/s. 138 of the Constitution, the superintendence, direction and control of elections to either House of the State Legislature held under the Constitution vest in the Election Commission of India, however, in exercise of powers u/s. 138 of the Constitution, the Election Commission of India cannot define boundaries of the constituencies or territorial limits either of State Legislature or of Legislative Council - SC, in Laxmi Kant Bajpai vs. Haji Yaqoob and others 2009 INDLAW SC 1962, held that the Election Commission of India had no power to change the boundaries or areas or extend the boundaries or areas of any constituency - Appeal dismissed.
