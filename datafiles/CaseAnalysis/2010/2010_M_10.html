M. Jagdish Vyas And Ors. v Union Of India And Ors

Supreme Court of India

29 March 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Surinder Singh Nijjar, B. SUDARSHAN REDDY

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 228; (2010) 4 SCC 150; AIR 2010 SC 1596; 2010 (3) AWC 2701; 2010 (125) FLR 583; JT 2010 (3) SC 380; 2010(3) SCALE 400; 2010 (4) SLR 590; 2010 (2) UPLBEC 912

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service

Keywords: Deputationist

Summary: Service - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 and 16 - Appointment - Appellant-deputationists alongwith departmental candidates appeared in the examination for Junior Accounts Officer (JAO) - Result of same was declared through letter dt. 23-07-2002 which also mentioned that the result in the case of candidates on deputation from other departments, would be declared separately - Thereafter, the results of deputationist candidates were declared on 29-08-2002 - Appellants who would have been declared successful under the criteria contained in the letter dt. 23-07-2002 were not included in the list of successful candidates - Appellants filed application before Tribunal - Tribunal allowed said application and directed respondents to include the names of the appellants in the list of successful candidates - On appeal, HC held that instructions vide letter dt. 23-07-2002 had not superseded the qualifications laid down by Central Government in its letter dt. 24-06-2003 wherein onetime concession was given to department candidates - Appellant contended that all the candidates appearing in the examinations formed one class - Therefore, deputationists cannot be discriminated by providing higher qualifying marks in comparison to the marks required by departmental candidates - Hence, present appeal - Whether the deputationists are justified in claiming the parity with the departmental candidates? - Held, no - No material was placed on the record either before the Tribunal or before the HC to show that there has been any relaxation in the standard or the minimum marks required to be obtained by the deputationists - Final decision has been taken by Government of India for relaxing the minimum qualifying marks for the departmental candidates as a onetime measure in order to facilitate the departmental candidates to get promotion to the posts of JAO - Deputationists, on the other hand, had been provisionally allowed to sit in the examination subject to the final decision of the competent authority whether to absorb them or not - These conditions were made known to the deputationists in the policy decision dt. 30-9-2000 before appearing in the examination - Categorization of deputationists and the departmental candidates into the two categories, has been rightly upheld by the HC - Moreover, law has been well settled for many years that members of one homogenous group have to be treated equally - At the same time arts. 14 and 16 do not mandate that un-equals are to be treated as equals - In the present case, the classification cannot be said to be either irrational or arbitrary - Impugned order of HC upheld - Appeals dismissed.
