Stanes Higher Secondary School v Special Tahsildar, (Land Acquisition) Coimbatore, Tamil Nadu

Supreme Court of India

09 March 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Dalveer Bhandari, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 188; (2010) 4 SCC 565; 2010 (4) ADJ 202; AIR 2010 SC 1323; 2010(4) ALL MR 450; 2010 (79) ALR 713; 2010 (3) AWC 2218; JT 2010 (3) SC 489; 2010 (5) MLJ(SC) 338; 2010 (2) RCR(Civil) 319; 2010 (110) RD 33; 2010(2) SCALE 774; 2010 (2) UPLBEC 1176

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 18, 25, 4(1) - Acquisition of land - Compensation - Succeed - Land belonging to appellant-school was acquired by respondent by notification u/s. 4(1) of the Act for purpose of expansion of road - Land Acquisition Officer by his award dt. 31-12-1981 fixed compensation at rate of Rs. 4/- per sq. ft. and 15/- solatium - Appellant aggrieved by award made reference to Trial Court and claimed compensation at rate of Rs. 30/- per sq. ft. - Trial Court fixed compensation at rate of Rs.20/- per sq. ft. along with solatium of 15 per cent and also awarded interest at rate of 4 per cent per annum from date possession was taken till payment was made - Respondents filed appeal before HC, which was allowed and modified decree reducing compensation to Rs. 10/- per sq. ft. and HC fixed interest at rate of 9 per cent and solatium at 30 per cent of market value - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order of HC was justified - Held, relied on in Gobardhan Mahto v. State of Bihar 1979 Indlaw SC 534, s. 25 of the Act, mandated Court not to award compensation exceeding amount so claimed by landowners and not to be less than amount awarded by Collector - Hence, facts of this case it was unamended s. 25 to be applicable and not amended section - In view of this peripheral limitation on court awarding compensation, would equally apply to HC exercising its power as first appellate court - Therefore, in the peculiar facts and circumstances of the case, the judgment of HC was modified and in interest of justice, SC deem it appropriate to direct respondent not to recover amount of compensation already paid to appellant - Appeal disposed of.
