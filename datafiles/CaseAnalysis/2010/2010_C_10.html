C.M. Sharma v State Of A.P. Th. I.P.

Supreme Court of India

25 November 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Chandramauli Kumar Prasad, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 997; (2010) 15 SCC 1; 2011 CRLJ 975; 2011 (1) JhrCR(SC) 30; JT 2010 (12) SC 546; 2011 (1) RCR(Criminal) 183; 2010(12) SCALE 381

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Illegal Gratification

Summary: Criminal - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 7, 13(1)(d) and 13(2) - Demand and acceptance of illegal gratification - Conviction - Challenged - Appellant was convicted for commission of the offences u/s. 7 and 13(1)(d) r/w s. 13(2) of the Act - It was alleged that the appellant, Deputy Chief Engineer, Railway Electrifications, demanded illegal gratification from contractor - Conviction and sentence of the appellant affirmed by HC - Hence, present appeal - (A) Whether conviction of the appellant was not fit to be sustained only on the evidence of the contractor without any corroboration? - It was submitted that the contractor was an accomplice and, therefore, before sustaining the appellant's conviction it was essential that his evidence was corroborated by evidence of other witnesses? - Held, a person concerned in the commission of crime, a partner in crime and associate in guilt is an accomplice - Hence, a witness forced to pay on promise of doing or forbearing to do any official act by a public servant, is not a partner in crime and associate in guilt and therefore cannot be said to be accomplice - In the present case, contractor who gave bribe, therefore, cannot be said to an accomplice as the same was extorted from him - Further corroboration of evidence of a witness is required when his evidence is not wholly reliable - In the present case, evidence of the contractor was corroborated in material particulars by the shadow-witness - (B) Whether demand and acceptance was proved? - Held, demand of illegal gratification is sine qua non to constitute the offence under the Act - Further mere recovery of currency notes itself does not constitute the offence under the Act, unless it is proved beyond all reasonable doubt that the accused voluntarily accepted the money knowing it to be bribe - In present case, from the evidence it was evident that the appellant demanded the money from the contractor - There was further evidence demand for payment of illegal gratification was made and paid - Positive sodium carbonate test vis-'-vis the fingers and right trousers pocket of the appellant go to show that he voluntarily accepted the bribe - Thus there was evidence of demand of illegal gratification and the voluntary acceptance thereof - Hence, both the ingredients to bring the act within the mischief of ss. 7 and 13(1)(d)(ii) of the Act were satisfied - Appeal dismissed.
