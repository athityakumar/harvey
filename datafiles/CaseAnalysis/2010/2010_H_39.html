Union of India and Another v Hemraj Singh Chauhan and Others

Supreme Court of India

23 March 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Ganguly, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 199; (2010) 4 SCC 290; AIR 2010 SC 1682; 2010(3) ALL MR 942; 2010 (3) CalLT 45; 2010 (2) ESC 220; 2010 (125) FLR 282; JT 2010 (3) SC 240; 2010(3) SCALE 272; 2010 (3) SLR 1; 2010 (2) UPLBEC 921

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Constitution; Service

Keywords: Indian Police Service (Appointment by Promotion) Regulations, 1955, Indian Administrative Service (Cadre) Rules, 1954, Indian Administrative Service (Fixation of Cadre Strength) Regulations, 1956

Summary: Service - Constitution - Administrative - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14, 16 and 142 - Indian Administrative Service (Appointment by Promotion) Regulations, 1955, regn. 5(3) - Indian Administrative Service (Fixation of Cadre Strength) Regulations, 1956, r. 4(2) - Promotion - Respondents, members of State Civil Service (S.C.S) of State of UP submitted that in terms of regn. 5(3) of 1955 Regulations, members of the S.C.S. who have attained the age of 54 years shall be considered by the Committee for promotion, provided they were eligible for such consideration on the 1st day of the year or of any of the years immediately preceding the year in which such meeting is held, but could not be considered as no meeting of the Committee was held during such preceding year or years - Two notifications were issued on 21-10-2000 after bifurcation of the State of UP, determining the number of senior posts in State of UP - Proposal was received from the Government of UP only in the month of January 2005 - Respondents challenged said notifications submitting that the last cadre review of the I.A.S. in UP cadre was conducted in 1998 and the next cadre review was therefore due in April, 2003 - Hence, cadre review which was conducted in August, 2005 should have been given effect from April, 2003 so that the respondents could be considered for promotion against the promotion quota - Petitioner contended that with the issuance of notification dt. 21-10-2000, cadre review has already taken place and as such the next review was due in 2005 only - CAT upheld the contention of the petitioner and held that the cadre review carried out in 2005 cannot be given retrospective effect - On appeal, HC set aside the judgement of CAT and impugned notifications - HC also directed State Government and Central Government that cadre review exercise should be undertaken as if it was taking place on 30-04-2003 - Hence, present appeal - Whether HC's order could be upheld? - Held, both the authorities u/r. 4(2) of the Cadre Rules accepted on principle that cadre review in UP was due in 2003 - Neither the appellants nor the State Government has given any plausible explanation justifying the delay in Cadre review - Only reason for the delay in review, is that there was total in-action on the part of the UP Government and lackadaisical attitude in discharging its statutory responsibility - Statutory duty which is cast on the State Government and the Central Government to undertake the cadre review exercise every 5 years is ordinarily mandatory subject to exceptions - Lethargy, in-action, an absence of a sense of responsibility cannot fall within category of just exceptions - Respondents cannot be made in any way responsible for the delay - Hence, directions given by HC cannot be said to be unreasonable - SC reiterates those very directions in exercise of its power u/art. 142 of the Constitution of India subject to the only rider that in normal cases the provision of r. 4(2) of the said Cadre Rules cannot be construed retrospectively - Appeal disposed of.
