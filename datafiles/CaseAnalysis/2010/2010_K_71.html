Amrit Lal Kapoor and another v Kusum Lata Kapoor and others

Supreme Court of India

06 May 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 350; (2010) 6 SCC 583; AIR 2010 SC 2733; 2010 (80) ALR 930; 2010 (3) AWC 2819; 2010 (3) CTC 887; JT 2010 (5) SC 276; 2010 (4) MPLJ 290; 2010 (159) PLR 177; 2010 (3) RCR(Civil) 135; 2010 (1) RCR(Rent) 504; 2010 (110) RD 553; 2010(5) SCALE 372

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Closure of evidence - Suit for declaration of title and consequential relief in the nature of permanent prohibitory injunction was filed - Defendants-appellants set up a Will allegedly executed in their favour - After the plaintiff had led her evidence in the suit, the defendants-appellants were called upon to adduce evidence in support of their case - One of the witnesses which the defendants-appellants had cited was 'S' who was the attesting witness to the will propounded by the defendants-appellants - Trial Court ordered closing the evidence of the defendants-appellants as the witness was not present for getting his deposition recorded - Order was affirmed by HC - Present appeal - Appellants argued that the Trial Court and HC failed to appreciate the circumstances in which said witness was prevented from appearing as an attesting witness to the will - Held, said witness was an attesting witness to the Will which the defendants-appellants have set up in their defence - Non-examination of the said witness would, therefore, seriously affect their case - Further, fact that the witness could not be produced not because of any deliberate neglect or inaction on the part of the defendants-appellants but on account of the refusal of leave to him - In the circumstances, interest of justice would be substantially served if a final opportunity is given to the defendants-appellants to produce the witness - Impugned order set aside - Matter remitted back to the Trial Court with the direction that the defendants-appellants shall be permitted to produce said witness as a witness in support of their case - Appeal allowed.
