Yogeshwar Prasad & Ors. v National Inst.,Edu.Planning & Admn.&Ors.

Supreme Court of India

21 October 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Dalveer Bhandari, Deepak Verma

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 972; 2011 (1) AWC 841; 2010 (5) ESC 639; 2010 (127) FLR 823; JT 2010 (12) SC 278; 2010(11) SCALE 379; 2011 (7) SLR 1; 2010 (4) UPLBEC 2936

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - National Institute of Educational Planning and Administration Service Regulations, regn. 4(2) - Parity in pay scale - Entitlement - Appellant/Assistants/Stenographers in the 1st respondent/Institute were getting the same pay scale as of their counterparts in Central Government service - Fourth Pay Commission revised the pay scale of Central Government employees and subsequently, Anomalies Removal Committee increased the scale further - Although appellants were given the revised scale in accordance with the recommendation of Pay Commission, they were not given the further increment effected by the Anomalies Removal Committee - Appellants filed writ petition before the HC - Single Judge directed the respondent/Institute to consider the revision of appellants' pay scale - 1st respondent challenged the Single Judge order before the DB - DB quashed the Single Judge order - Hence the present appeals - Whether the appellants are entitled to get the revised pay scale from the date when their counterparts have been given that pay scale in the Central Government service - Held, DB was not justified in setting aside the judgment of the Single Judge - DB did not consider the service regulations of the 1st respondent/Institute and the case of the appellants - Further, 1st respondent/Institute recommended itself to the Union of India for revising the pay scale of appellants at par with the Central Government employees - Union of India, at later, accepted the recommendations of Fifth and Sixth Pay Commissions and were giving the appellants the same pay scale which their counterparts in the Central Government were getting - If the appellants are entitled to get the same pay scale as of their counterparts in Central Government during the Fifth and Sixth Pay Commissions, appellants are entitled to get the same scale in Fourth Commission also - Further, if any amount paid to the appellants pursuant to the recommendations of Pay Commission, such amount should not be recovered unless it was a case of misrepresentation or fraud - Impugned HC order is set aside - Appeals allowed.
