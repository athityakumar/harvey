Omprakash Verma And Others v State Of Andhra Pradesh And Others

Supreme Court of India

08 October 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 1059; (2010) 13 SCC 158; 2011 (1) ALD(SC) 75; JT 2010 (11) SC 366; 2010(10) SCALE 707

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: A.P. Land Reforms Act, 1975

Summary: Land & Property - A.P. Land Reforms (Ceiling on Agricultural Holdings) Act, 1973 - Urban Land (Ceiling and Regulation) Act, 1976 - Owners of land in question executed registered General Power of Attorney (GPA) in favour of a partnership firm - Thereafter, A.P. Land Reforms Act, 1975 came into force - Since the land in Survey was agricultural land, the owners filed declarations under the A.P. Land Reforms (Ceiling on Agricultural Holdings) Act, 1973 (Land Reforms Act) - Meanwhile, Urban Land (Ceiling and Regulation) Act, 1976 (ULC Act) came into force - Owners, through their GPA, filed declarations u/s. 6(1) of the ULC Act under a mistaken impression that the ULC Act was applicable to their land, though the same was inapplicable for the reason that the land in question was agricultural land and the same was not included in the Master Plan as on the date of commencement of the ULC Act - Later on, Master Plan was amended as a result of which re-computation of the land in the said Survey had to be carried out in accordance with the ULC Act - Competent Authority issued a notice for surrendering possession, however, the possession was not surrendered - By virtue of the exemption granted by State Government u/s. 20(1) of the ULC Act, each holder of excess land was entitled to hold 5 acres instead of 1000 sq meters - A number of persons including the appellants purchased small extents of land in said Survey by registered sale deeds - However, District Registrar ordered cancellation of the sale deeds - Writ petitions were filed thereagainst - Single Judge set aside the orders of the District Registrar nullifying the sale deeds regarding the land in question - Appeals filed thereagainst was dismissed by DB - SLP was filed before SC - SC disposed of all the appeals, i.e. State of Andhra Pradesh and Others vs. N. Audikesava Reddy and Others 2001 INDLAW SC 20388 holding that the master plan prepared as per law in force even subsequent to enforcement of the Act is to be taken into consideration to determine whether a particular piece of land is vacant land or not - By way of a representation, the owners requested the Competent Authority to compute the holdings afresh in terms of the law declared by SC in Audikesava Reddy's case and also to compute the compensation amounts to be paid for regularization - Without taking any action on the representations, the State Government, in exercise of its powers u/s. 23 of the ULC Act issued G.O.Ms.No. 161 dated 13.02.2006 purporting to allot the excess land to respondent No. 4 - Writ petitions filed thereagainst were dismissed - Hence, present appeal - Whether the proceedings of the Competent Authority u/ss. 8, 9 and 10 of the ULC Act in relation to the land in question declared by the DB of HC as void, stood restored by virtue of judgment of SC in Audikesava Reddy's case as claimed in G.O. Ms. No. 161 dated 13.02.2006? - Appellants contended that in view of the ratio in Audikesava Reddy's case the State was liable to re-compute the excess land holding of the appellants under the provisions of the ULC Act with reference to the date on which the Master Plan for the City of Hyderabad came to be extended to the appellants land - Appellants further submitted that any "land" though situated in urban agglomeration is not a "Vacant Land" if the same is used mainly for the purpose of agriculture and not referred to in the Master Plan existing as on 17.02.1976 - Held, once the appeal of the State was allowed in Audikeshava Reddy's case the net result was that the HC judgment which held that the proceedings under the ULC Act were vitiated stood merged in the decision of SC in Audikeshava Reddy - Logical sequitor of this is that the writ petitions filed by the appellants were deemed to be dismissed - Inasmuch as the writ petitions having been dismissed, the orders passed under the ULC Act have attained finality and declarations which had been made and statements filed on 06.09.1976 and 25.07.1977 stand till today and these declarations are not even sought to be withdrawn - Further, DB finding with respect to nature of land in a writ petition filed by purchasers does not survive after appeals of the State were allowed and after SC refused to go into the question of filing of statements by owners under a wrong impression - Hence, no part of the judgment of the HC would survive after the appeal is allowed unless and until it is expressly and specifically preserved - In view of the same, the contrary contention of the appellants in this context unacceptable and unsustainable - In any case, the owners were bound by the determination of surplus land by the Competent Authority on the basis of their own declaration and the various orders passed under the ULC Act - They cannot be permitted to re-open the chapter after about 25 years - Appeals dismissed.
