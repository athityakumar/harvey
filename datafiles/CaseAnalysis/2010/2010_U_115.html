J. S. Sekhon v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

10 August 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Mukundakam Sharma, A.R. Dave

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 598; (2010) 11 SCC 586; AIR 2010 SC 2887; 2010 (6) AWC 5878; 2010 (4) ESC 535; 2010 (127) FLR 141; JT 2010 (8) SC 429; 2010(8) SCALE 15; [2010] 9 S.C.R. 1025; 2010 (5) SLR 199

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces

Keywords: Army Regulations, Commander

Summary: Defence & Security Forces - Army Act, 1950, ss. 122 and 130 - Army Rules, 1954, r. 44 - Appellant (Commissioned Officer in the Indian Army) was convicted by the General Court Martial (GCM) for fraud - Writ petition filed thereagainst was dismissed - On appeal, DB of HC held that there was no 'procedural irregularity or illegality in the GCM proceedings' - Hence, present appeal - (A) Period of limitation for trial - Whether convening of the General Court Martial was barred under the provisions of s. 122? - Held, s. 122 of the Army Act provides the period of limitation for trial by court martial of any person shall not be commenced after the expiration of a period of three years (a) from the date of the offence or, (b) where the commission of the offence was not known to the person aggrieved by the offence or to the authority competent to initiate action, the first day on which such offence comes to the knowledge of such person or authority, whichever is earlier or (c) where it is not known by whom the offence was committed, the first day on which the identity of the offender is known to the person aggrieved by the offence or by the authority competent to initiate action, whichever is earlier - In the present case, acts were committed against the Government and not a natural person - In the facts of the present case no single person can be said to be aggrieved person individually due to the act of defrauding the Army and therefore cl. (b) of s.122(1) of the Army Act is applicable - Hence, as competent authority to convene a general court martial against the appellant was the general officer commanding, therefore, his date of knowledge of the commission of offence was material - Competent authority initiated action within the limitation period - Trial was not barred by limitation - (B) Scope and applicability of paragraph 449(b) of the Army Regulation - Whether there was violation of paragraph 449(b) of Army Regulation? - Held, the General Court Martial, which was held against the appellant was convened by the general officer commanding who was of the rank of a Major General - General officer commanding in the case of the appellant was a Major General who was much higher in rank than the commanding officer and therefore, there was no violation of paragraph 449(b) of the Army Regulation - Appeal dismissed.
