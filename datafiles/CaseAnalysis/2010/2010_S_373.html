Kootha Perumal v State Tr. Inspector Of Police, Vigilance And Anti Corruption

Supreme Court of India

15 December 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Surinder Singh Nijjar, B. Sudarshan Reddy

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 1073; (2011) 1 SCC 491; 2011 ALL MR (Cri) 670; 2011 (1) JCC 32; JT 2011 (6) SC 321; 2011 (1) RCR(Criminal) 278; 2010(13) SCALE 350; 2011 (1) WLN(SC) 29

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 7, 13(1)(d), 13(2) and 19 - Demand and acceptance of illegal gratification - Sanction - Conviction - Challenged - Special Judge convicted the appellant for offences punishable u/ss. 7 and 13(2) r/w s. 13(1)(d) of the Act - On appeal, HC affirmed the findings recorded by the Trial Court - Hence, present appeal - Appellant submitted that the entire proceedings were vitiated, as previous sanction to prosecute the appellant was not legally obtained as required u/s. 19 of the Act - Whether sanction was duly obtained prior to the prosecution of the appellant? - Held, sanctioning authority adverted to all the necessary facts which were proved by the prosecution in the trial - Upon examination of the material facts, the sanctioning authority certified that it was the authority competent to remove the appellant from the office - Upon examination of the statements of the witnesses as also the material on record, the sanctioning authority duly recorded its satisfaction that the appellant should be prosecuted for the offences - Hence, no merit in the submissions of the appellant that the sanctioning order to prosecute the appellant was not legal - Further, Trial Court as well as the HC analyzed the entire evidence and clearly held that a demand was made by the and entire trap was meticulously orchestrated by the prosecution authority - Appeal dismissed.
