Kalabharati Advertising v Hemant Vimalnath Narichania and others

Supreme Court of India

06 September 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 891; (2010) 9 SCC 437; AIR 2010 SC 3745; 2011 (1) Bom.C.R. 391; JT 2010 (9) SC 382; 2010 (7) MLJ(SC) 1043; 2011 (1) RCR(Civil) 216; 2010(9) SCALE 60; [2010] 10 S.C.R. 971

Case Digest

Subject: Media & Entertainment; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Municipalities & Local Governments - Media & Entertainment - Bombay Municipal Corporation - Guidelines of the hoardings - Violation of - Appellant approached the society in 2001 for grant of permission to erect a hoarding admeasuring 40' x 20' in its compound - Society passed a Resolution in the year 2001, permitting the appellant to erect a hoarding of the aforesaid measurement - Thereafter, appellant applied to the Municipal Corporation for grant of necessary permission for erecting the same - Said application was allowed by the Corporation - Some of the members of the Society filed writ petition for cancellation of the permission granted in favour of the appellant - HC constituted a Committee to find out violations of the guidelines of the hoardings in Mumbai - Committee came to the conclusion that the said hoarding had been in violation of condition Nos.16(f) and 16(c), i.e., obstructing the air, light and ventilation and situated in the compulsory open space - HC directed the Municipal Corporation to withdraw its earlier order of granting permission to appellant to erect a hoarding without giving an opportunity of hearing to the appellant or the Society - In pursuance of the said order, Municipal Corporation withdraw its earlier order - Hence, present appeal - (A) Whether HC rightly directed to the Municipal Corporation to withdraw its earlier order of granting permission to appellant to erect a hoarding? - Held, HC could not have allowed the Corporation to recall its earlier order and pass a fresh order, that too, without giving an opportunity of hearing to the appellant and the Society - Review is a statutory remedy - The court cannot confer a jurisdiction upon any authority as conferring jurisdiction upon a Court/Tribunal/Authority is a legislative function and the same cannot be conferred either by the court or by the consent of the parties - Such an order passed by the HC is without jurisdiction and, therefore, a nullity - Any order passed in pursuance thereof, also remains unenforceable and inexecutable - Thus, the order passed by the Corporation stood vitiated for not recording reasons and violating the principles of natural justice - It establishes the allegations of legal malice made by the appellant against the Corporation - (B) Review in absence of statutory provisions - Held, unless the statute/rules so permit, the review application is not maintainable in case of judicial/quasi-judicial orders - In absence of any provision in the Act granting an express power of review, it is manifest that a review could not be made and the order in review, if passed is ultra-vires, illegal and without jurisdiction - (C) Case dismissed/withdrawn- effect on interim relief - Held, no litigant can derive any benefit from the mere pendency of a case in a Court of Law, as the interim order always merges into the final order to be passed in the case and if the case is ultimately dismissed, the interim order stands nullified automatically - A party cannot be allowed to take any benefit of his own wrongs by getting an interim order and thereafter blame the Court - Court is under an obligation to undo the wrong done to a party by the act of the Court - Thus, any undeserved or unfair advantage gained by a party invoking the jurisdiction of the Court must be neutralised, as the institution of litigation cannot be permitted to confer any advantage on a party by the delayed action of the Court - (D) Court-cannot be used only for interim relief - Held, forum of the writ court cannot be used for the purpose of giving interim relief as the only and the final relief to any litigant - If the Court comes to the conclusion that the matter requires adjudication by some other appropriate forum and relegates the said party to that forum, it should not grant any interim relief in favour of such a litigant for an interregnum period till the said party approaches the alternative forum and obtains interim relief - Impugned order of the HC set aside - Appeal allowed.
