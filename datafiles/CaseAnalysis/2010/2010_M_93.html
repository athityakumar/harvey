Commr. Of Income Tax-V, New Delhi v M/S. Oracle Software India Limited

Supreme Court of India

13 January 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, H.L. Dattu, Surinder Singh Nijjar

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 34; (2010) 2 SCC 677; AIR 2010 SC (Supp) 500; 2010 (1) CTC 728; 2010 (250) E.L.T. 161; [2010] 320 ITR 546; JT 2010 (6) SC 369; [2010] 1 S.C.R. 543; [2010] 187 TAXMAN 275

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 80IA(1) r/w. s. 80IA(12)(b) - Manufacture - Compact Disc - Software loaded disc - Whether the process by which a blank Compact Disc (CD) is transformed into software loaded disc constitutes 'manufacture or processing of goods' in terms of s. 80IA(1) r/w. s. 80IA(12)(b) of the Income Tax Act, 1961? - Held, yes - Blank CD is an input - By the duplicating process undertaken by the assessee, the recordable media which is unfit for any specific use gets converted into the programme which is embedded in the Master Media and, thus, blank CD gets converted into recorded CD by the intricate process - Duplicating process changes the basic character of a blank CD, dedicating it to a specific use - Without such processing, blank CDs would be unfit for their intended purpose - Therefore, processing of blank CDs, dedicating them to a specific use, constitutes a manufacture in terms of s. 80IA(12)(b) r/w. s. 33B of the Income Tax Act - Revenue's appeal dismissed.
