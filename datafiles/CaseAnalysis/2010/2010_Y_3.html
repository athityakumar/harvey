Dinesh Kumar v Yusuf Ali

Supreme Court of India

26 May 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, Swatanter Kumar

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 421; (2010) 12 SCC 740; AIR 2010 SC 2679; 2010(5) ALL MR 483; 2010 (82) ALR 258; 2011 (2) AWC 1383; 2010 (3) JhrCR(SC) 148; JT 2010 (5) SC 543; 2010 (8) MLJ(SC) 786; 2010 (3) RCR(Civil) 243; 2010 (1) RCR(Rent) 543; 2010(6) SCALE 41; [2010] 7 S.C.R. 222

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Rent Control

Keywords: U.P. Urban Buildings (Regulation of Letting, Rent and Eviction) Act, 1972, U.P. Urban Buildings (Regulation of Letting, Rent and Eviction) Rules, 1972

Summary: (A) Rent Control - M.P. Accommodation Control Act, 1961, s. 12(1)(f) - Eviction - Bona fide requirement - Appellant-tenant was inducted by the respondent-landlord in a shop for a non-residential purpose on a monthly rent - Respondent-landlord filed suit for eviction of the appellant on the grounds of nuisance and bone fide requirement for himself - Trial Court decreed the suit for eviction u/s. 12(1)(f) of the Act on the ground of bona fide need - On appeal, First Appellate Court reversed the order of the Trial Court on ground that the bona fide need was a pretext to enhance the rent or evict the tenant - On second appeal, HC restored the order of the Trial Court and ordered the eviction of the appellant-tenant - Hence, present appeal - Held, landlord is the best Judge of his need, however, it should be real, genuine and the need may not be a pretext to evict the tenant only for increasing the rent - In the present case, HC concluded that the landlord, in spite of the fact that he was owner of the suit premises could not be forced to continue his business in a shop of negligible area - Further, fact that rent was not enhanced since 1995, the First Appellate Court erred in drawing the inference that need of the respondent-landlord may not be bona fide and it might be a pretext for increasing the rent or to evict the tenant - However, in the facts and circumstances of the case, the HC did not consider the relevant factors i.e. as what would be the magnitude of his business, partial eviction of the appellant could serve the purpose of both the parties - Directed that respondent-landlord shall recover possession of half of the area of the premises - Appeal disposed of.

(B) Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 100 - Second appeal - Maintainability of - Held, second appeal u/s. 100 of CPC is maintainable basically on a substantial question of law and not on facts - However, if the HC comes to the conclusion that the evidence on record recorded by the courts below are perverse being based on no evidence or based on irrelevant material, the appeal can be entertained and it is permissible for the Court to re-appreciate the evidence - Appeal disposed of.
