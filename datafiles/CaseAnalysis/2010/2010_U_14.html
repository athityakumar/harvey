Uma Shankar Singh v State of Bihar and another

Supreme Court of India

09 September 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 723; (2010) 9 SCC 479; 2012 ALL MR (Cri) 1778; 2010 (4) JCC 3002; 2010 (4) JhrCR(SC) 118; JT 2010 (9) SC 512; 2010 (4) RCR(Criminal) 363; 2010(9) SCALE 213; [2010] 10 S.C.R. 1132

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 291 and 302 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 173(2) and 190 - Magistrate's power of enquiry if he disagreed with Final Report submitted by investigating authorities - FIR was lodged u/s. 364/34 of IPC and ss. 302, 291/34 of IPC and s. 27 of the Arms Act - Investigation was transferred to the CID - Writ petition was filed thereagainst wherein HC directed CID and Superintendent of Police to submit their reports to the Chief Judicial Magistrate and upon such report being submitted, Chief Judicial Magistrate was directed to proceed according to law after considering both the reports and case diary - Both the CID and the local police had submitted their reports in final form exonerating the petitioner of the allegations made against him in the FIR - However, after examining the materials in the case diary, Chief Judicial Magistrate differed with the Final Report submitted by the investigating agency exonerating petitioner of allegations made against him in the FIR - Petitioner filed application u/s. 227 of CrPC for discharge from case which was rejected and a date was fixed for framing of charge - Order was upheld by HC - Hence, present special leave petition - Whether in case Magistrate disagreed with the Final Report filed by the investigating agency, Magistrate was at liberty to hold a separate enquiry and to take cognizance thereafter? - Held, even if the investigating authority is of the view that no case has been made out against an accused, the Magistrate can apply his mind independently to the materials contained in the police report and take cognizance thereupon in exercise of his powers u/s. 190(1)(b) of CrPC - Present is not a case where the Magistrate took recourse to any further inquiry but took cognizance on the police report itself, which he was entitled to do u/s. 190(1)(b) of CrPC - Special Leave Petition dismissed.
