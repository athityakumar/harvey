Ravindra Tukaram Hiwale v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

02 August 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

H.S. Bedi, Chandramauli Kumar Prasad

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 608; (2010) 13 SCC 253; AIR 2010 SC 3492; 2010 ALL MR (Cri) 3359; JT 2010 (9) SC 87; 2011 (1) OLR 160; 2010 (4) RCR(Criminal) 115; 2010(8) SCALE 436; [2010] 9 S.C.R. 454; 2010 (3) UC 1485

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 306 and 498A - Deceased suffered serious burn injuries and ultimately died of those injuries - Trial Court on a consideration of the evidence convicted the appellant for offences punishable u/s. 498-A and to a sentence of one year and u/s. 306 to a sentence of 4 years - Appellant thereafter filed an appeal against said order submitting that deceased made a dying declaration in which she stated that she had a quarrel with her husband over the house-hold chores and over the feeding of the children and she had thereafter poured kerosene on herself and then burnt herself whereas the State also filed an appeal pleading for a higher sentence - State also filed appeal pleading for a higher sentence which was allowed and the sentence awarded by the Trial Court u/s. 306 IPC was enhanced from 4 to 6 years - Hence, present appeal - Whether order of the HC could be allowed? - Held, trial court had given a positive finding that there was no misbehaviour on the part of the appellant over a period of time and the incident was a spontaneous one arising out of a family quarrel in the morning - The finding of the HC based on two letters written about a year before the incident would therefore have little value in the light of the dying declaration of the deceased - Hence, sentence awarded by the HC to be quashed and judgment of the trial court to be restored - Appeal allowed.

Legislation Cited

Indian Penal Code, 1860

Indian Penal Code, 1860 s. 306

Indian Penal Code, 1860 s. 498A
