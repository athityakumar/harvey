Govindappa and others v State of Karnataka

Supreme Court of India

11 May 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, H.L. Dattu

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 376; (2010) 6 SCC 533; 2010 CRLJ 3439; JT 2010 (4) SC 645; 2010 (5) KarLJ 369; 2010(5) SCALE 587; [2010] 6 S.C.R. 962; 2010 (2) UC 717

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 302 and 498-A - Murder - Cruelty - Conviction - Challenged - Trial Court convicted all the five accused for offences punishable u/s. 498-A and 302 r/w s. 34 of IPC - On appeal, HC acquitted A-3 and A-5 of all the charges levelled against them and affirmed the sentence passed by the Trial Court on A-1, A-2 and A-4 (appellants)- Hence, present appeal - (A) Dying declaration - It was argued that Tahsildar has not obtained the certificate from the Medical Officer regarding condition of the deceased - Held, what is essentially required is that the person who recorded the dying declaration must be satisfied that the deceased was in a fit state of mind - Certification by the doctor is essentially a rule of caution and, therefore, the voluntary and truthful nature of the declaration can be established otherwise - In the present case, evidence of doctor clearly shows that the deceased was in a sound state of mind while giving the statement before the Tahsildar - Dying declaration fully corroborates the evidence of doctor and Tahsildar who recorded it - (B) Conviction - Held, prosecution witnesses and Taluk Executive Magistrate who recorded the dying declaration of deceased clearly proves the involvement of appellants in the commission of offence as charged and they were rightly awarded sentence of life imprisonment - Prosecution fully established its case against the appellants - Appeal dismissed.
