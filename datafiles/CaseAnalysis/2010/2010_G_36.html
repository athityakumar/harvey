Goa Glass Fibre Limited and another v State of Goa and Another

Supreme Court of India

03 May 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Dattu, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 331; (2010) 6 SCC 499; 2010 (4) Bom.C.R. 73; JT 2010 (4) SC 565; 2010(4) SCALE 616; [2010] 5 S.C.R. 970

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Constitutionality, Goa (Prohibition of Further Payments and Recovery of Rebate Benefits) Bill, 2002, Goa (Prohibition of Further Payment and Recovery of Rebate Benefits) Act, 2002

Summary: Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14, 19(1)(g) and 32 - The Goa (Prohibition of Further Payment and Recovery of Rebate Benefits) Act, 2002 - Constitutional validity - Petitioners filed writ petition seeking a declaration that the Goa (Prohibition of Further Payment and Recovery of Rebate Benefits) Act, 2002) is ultra vires of the Constitution of India on the ground that it nullifies a judgment of SC dt. 13.02.2001 affirming the view taken by Bombay HC, Goa Bench, in its judgment dt. 21.01.1999 and that it seeks to give effect to the decision of the Bombay HC dt. 19/24th April 2001- On the other hand State contends that the impugned Act is constitutionally valid and the impugned Act is not aimed at giving effect to the Order of the Bombay HC dt. 19/24.04.2001 nor is it passed because the abovementioned Special Leave Petition is pending before SC, but has been passed and aimed to save the coffers of the State and to prevent further abuse and payment out of the State Funds which the State can ill afford - (A) Whether Goa (Prohibition of Further Payment and Recovery of Rebate Benefits) Act, 2002 is ultra vires of the Constitution of India? - Held, no - Statute can be invalidated or held unconstitutional on limited grounds viz., on the ground of the incompetence of the Legislature which enacts it and on the ground that it breaches or violates any of the fundamental rights or other Constitutional Rights - By impugned Act, the Legislature has imposed prohibition of further payments under the Notifications dt. 15.5.1996 and 1.8.1996, provides for recovery of rebate benefits from the beneficiaries and extinguishes the State's Liability under the said Notifications - This exercise by the Legislature is independent of and it was well within the Legislative power of the State to respond to the undisputed and disturbing facts which had enormous financial implication on the State's Finances to enact the Law with an object of remedying the unsatisfactory state of affairs which were known to the Legislature - Moreover, impugned Act is not aimed at giving effect to the Order of the Bombay HC dt. 19/24.04.2001 nor is it passed because the abovementioned Special Leave Petition is pending before this Court, but has been passed with an object or aim to sustain the State Coffers and to prevent further abuse and payment out of the State Funds - (B) Whether impugned Act is unconstitutional, because it seeks to take away the fundamental rights guaranteed to the petitioners under Article 14 and 19(1)(g) of the Constitution of India? - Held, no - There is nothing in the Act which suggests invidious discrimination, unreasonable classification or manifest violation of equality clause - Writ petitions not maintainable, hence dismissed.
