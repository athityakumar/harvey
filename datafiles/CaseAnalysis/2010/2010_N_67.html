Shanti Budhiya Vesta Patel And Others v Nirmala Jayprakash Tiwari And Others

Supreme Court of India

21 April 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Mukundakam Sharma, R.M. Lodha

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 297; (2010) 5 SCC 104; AIR 2010 SC 2132; 2010 (5) Bom.C.R. 418; 2010 (2) CLT(SC) 184; JT 2010 (4) SC 196; 2010 (6) MLJ(SC) 616; 2010 (3) MPLJ 625; 2010 (2) OLR 126; 2010 (3) RCR(Civil) 262; 2010 (111) RD 312; 2010(4) SCALE 182; [2010] 4 S.C.R. 958; 2010 (2) UC 1021

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, o. 23 r. 3 - Predecessor-in-interest - 'X' was appointed as a watchman by one 'Y' to take care of the suit property and for this a Kachcha shed on the impugned property was provided to him - In due course of time, 'X' extended the shed to construct a chawl consisting of 38 rooms, which were let out by him - After the death of the real owner of suit property, suit property stood recorded in the name of the respondent no. 7 and by means of a consent between respondent no. 7 and respondent no. 8, latter became the owner of the suit property - 'X' filed a suit against respondent no. 7 seeking a declaration that he is the owner of the suit property by adverse possession - Against this, a counter-claim seeking eviction of 'X' and his tenants from suit property was filed by respondent nos. 7 and 8 - Trial court dismissed the suit filed by 'X' and allowed the counter claim filed by respondent nos. 7 and 8 - Trial court also negated 'X's' claim of ownership of the suit property by adverse possession since his initial possession of the suit property was a permissive possession - Appeals filed against said order - During the pendency of aforesaid appeals, 'X' died and each of the present appellants executed an irrevocable Power of Attorney in favour of respondent no. 9 - Later, present appellants filed applications before HC alleging that respondent no. 9 colluded with respondent no. 8 and coerced them to enter into a compromise with respondent nos. 7 and 8, which was strongly objected to by the appellants - It is also alleged that despite objection, respondent no. 9 for himself and for and on behalf of the appellants as their Power of Attorney holder entered into consent terms with respondent nos. 7 and 8 and thereby submitted to the decree of eviction - HC dismissed said applications - Hence, present appeal - Whether HC's order could be upheld? - Held, burden to prove that a compromise arrived at u/o. 23 r. 3 of the CPC was tainted by coercion or fraud lies upon the party who alleges the same - Appellants, on whom the burden lay, have failed to do so - Although, the application for recall did allege some coercion, it could not be said to be a case of established coercion - Three criminal complaints were filed, but the appellants did not pursue the said criminal complaints to their logical end - Hence, appellants have failed to furnish the full and precise particulars with regard to the alleged fraud - Rather the Affidavits-cum- Declarations executed by the appellants indicate that no coercion or fraud was exercised upon the appellants by respondent no. 8 or 9 at any point of time and thus the consent decree cannot be said to be anything but valid - Order of HC upheld - Appeals dismissed.
