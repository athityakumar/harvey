Shaukat and another v State of Uttaranchal and another

Supreme Court of India

22 April 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Panchal, Deepak Verma

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 400; (2010) 5 SCC 68; 2010 CRLJ 4310; JT 2010 (4) SC 314; 2010(4) SCALE 299; [2010] 4 S.C.R. 873; 2010 (2) UC 1011

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 302, 304, 307 and 308 - Murder or culpable homicide - Appellant and his father were charged for commission of offences punishable u/s. 302 r/w s. 34 of IPC and s. 307 r/w s. 34 of IPC - Appellant was convicted u/ss. 302 and 307 of IPC - On appeal, HC held appellant guilty for commission of offence of culpable homicide not amounting to murder punishable u/s. 304 Part-I of IPC and u/s. 308 of IPC - Appellant was acquitted of the offences punishable u/ss. 302 and 307 of IPC - HC found that there was no enmity between the parties nor there was premeditation between the appellant and his father for committing the crime - HC was of the view that the quarrel had taken place on account of sudden provocation in which the appellant had caused injuries to the deceased with knife - HC concluded that the appellant had, in fact, no intention to make an attempt to commit murder and had committed offence punishable u/s. 308 of IPC - Present appeal - (A) Evidence of eye-witnesses - Held, there were no major contradictions or omissions in the evidence of the eye-witnesses - Neither the Trial Court nor the HC committed any error in placing reliance on the testimony of the three eye-witnesses for the purpose of coming to the conclusion that the appellant was the author of the injuries sustained by the deceased and injured victim - (B) Plea of self defence - Held, testimony of the medical officer makes it abundantly clear that the appellant's father had sustained two superficial injuries whereas the injury sustained by the appellant was self-inflicted one - Evidence on record does not indicate that any assault was mounted either on the appellant or his father by the deceased or injured - On facts and in the circumstances of the case, plea of self-defence not made out by the appellant - (C) Murder or culpable homicide - Whether Exception 4 to S. 300 IPC would be applicable to the facts of present case? - Held, exception 4 to s. 300 of IPC would be attracted only if four requirements are satisfied, namely, (1) it was a sudden fight; (2) there was no premeditation; (3) the act was not done in a heat of passion; and (4) the assailant had not taken any undue advantage or acted in a cruel manner - In the present case, evidence on record, shows that before reaching the place of incident, the appellant had armed himself with a dangerous weapon and had caused injury by using that weapon with such a great force on vital part of the body of the deceased that it had resulted in instant death of the deceased on the spot - Eye-witness account of assault on the deceased by the appellant read with medical evidence makes it clear that the act of the appellant, by which the death of the deceased was caused, was done with the intention of causing such bodily injury to the deceased as found by medical evidence and that the bodily injury intended to be inflicted was sufficient in the ordinary course of nature to cause death of the deceased - There was premeditation between the appellant and his father to cause the death of the deceased - Thus the facts proved bring the case of the appellant within four corners of clause Thirdly of s. 300 of IPC - Appellant committed murder of the deceased punishable u/s. 302 of IPC - (D) Conviction of the appellant u/s. 308 IPC for attempting to commit culpable homicide by causing injuries on the person of injured - Held, finding recorded by the HC that the appellant had caused injuries to injured in an attempt to escape, was not borne out from the record of the case at all but are contrary to the positive evidence on record - Appellant could not have been convicted u/s. 308 for causing injuries to injured and was liable to be convicted u/s. 307 of IPC - Appellant held guilty u/s. 302 of IPC for commission of murder of deceased and u/s. 307 for attempting to commit murder of injured - Appeals disposed of.
