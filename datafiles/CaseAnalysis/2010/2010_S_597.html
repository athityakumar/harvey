Indian Oil Corporation Ltd. & Ors v Subrata Borah Chowlek , Etc

Supreme Court of India

12 November 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

D.K. Jain, H.L. Dattu

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 995; 2011 (84) ALR 462; 2011 (3) AWC 2745; 2011 (2) CalLT 91; 2010 (262) E.L.T. 3; 2011 (130) FLR 324; JT 2011 (1) SC 535; 2011 (1) MLJ(SC) 1010; 2011 (112) RD 249; 2010(12) SCALE 209

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Service - Limitation Act, 1963, s. 5 - Condonation of delay of 59 days - Respondents filed writ petition seeking regularization of their services from the dates of their initial appointment with consequential benefits - Single Judge allowed said writ petition and directed appellant no. 2 to treat the respondents as having been regularly appointed from the date of their initial appointment, and to give them all the consequential service benefits - Appellants preferred an appeal against said order along with an application for condonation of delay of 59 days submitting that the delay of 59 days had occasioned because of the time taken by the company's consultant at Delhi, mainly on account of summer vacation - DB dismissed said appeal on ground of limitation - Hence, present appeal - Whether delay of 59 days in filing the appeal could be condoned on the ground of that the delay was unintentional and bona fide? - Held, the conduct of the appellants does not indicate inaction, negligence or mala fides - The explanation furnished for the marginal delay of 59 days, constitutes a sufficient cause and therefore, deserves to be accepted - Hence, DB erred in declining to condone the delay of 59 days - Impugned judgment of DB set aside - Appeals allowed.
