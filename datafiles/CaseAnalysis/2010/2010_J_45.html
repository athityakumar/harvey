John Pandian and others v State Rep. By Inspector Of Police, T. Nadu

Supreme Court of India

03 December 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

V.S. Sirpurkar, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 1057; 2011 (1) JCC 193; JT 2010 (13) SC 284; 2011 (1) MLJ(Crl) 636; 2010(13) SCALE 13

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 109, 302, 120B - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellants allegedly hatched a conspiracy and consequently, murdered the deceased - Trial Court convicted appellant nos. 1, 9, 10 u/s. 302 r/w 34 IPC and convicted appellant nos. 2, 4, 5, 6, 7 u/s. 120B IPC - Appellants filed appeal, however, 1st appellant expired during the pendency of appeal - HC dismissed appeals - Hence the present appeal - Whether conviction of appellants is sustainable - Held, murder took place on the busy road in daylight and as such, the eye-witnesses had the full opportunity to witness the incident - Complaint was registered immediately after the incident and Investigating Officer had visited the spot without delay - Both the Courts below relied upon evidence of eye-witness holding them to be credible witnesses - Although evidence of one eye-witness was recorded after 15 days of incident, that witness appeared to be truthful and hence, lower Courts rightly relied upon his evidence - Further, eye-witness identified the appellant nos. 1, 9, 10 in Identification Parade -Weapon used for the crime was recovered pursuant to the confession of 9th appellant in the case and said confessional statement was undoubtedly made before a witness who entered the witness box and offered himself for cross-examination - Recovered weapon was sent to Forensic Science Laboratory (FSL) and it was found stained with human blood - Taxi driver who took the appellants to the place of occurrence also identified the appellants - Prosecution could prove the motive behind the crime - Further, prosecution proved that there was a conspiracy for murdering the deceased, however, there was no evidence suggested appellant nos. 2, 4, 5,6, 7 had some nexus with the other appellants - Lower courts did not appreciate the evidence properly - Courts below committed an error in holding the existence of conspiracy and proceeded on that basis and then taking pieces of evidence to suggest that those pieces would connect the appellants with the conspiracy as the conspirators - Therefore, conviction of appellant nos. 9, 10 u/s. 302 r/w 34 IPC is upheld, however, appellant nos. 2, 4, 5, 6, 7 were given benefit of doubt and hence acquitted - Appeals disposed of.
