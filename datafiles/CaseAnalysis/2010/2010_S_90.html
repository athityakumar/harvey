Damodar S. Prabhu v Sayed Babalal H.

Supreme Court of India

03 May 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

K.G. Balakrishnan, P. Sathasivam, J.M. Panchal

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 334; (2010) 5 SCC 663; 2010 (4) ADJ 464; AIR 2010 SC 1907; 2010 ALL MR (Cri) 1967; 2010 (3) BC 110; 2010 (4) Bom.C.R. 45; [2010] 159 Comp Cas 1; 2011 (6) CompLJ 1; 2010 CRLJ 2860; 2010 (3) CTC 441; 2010 (169) DLT 1; 2010 (3) G.L.R. 2042; JT 2010 (4) SC 457; 2010 (2) KLT 587; 2010 (3) MLJ(Crl) 390; 2010(4) M.P.H.T. 1; 2010 (4) MPLJ 257; 2010 (2) OLR 336; 2010 (158) PLR 689; 2010 (2) RCR(Criminal) 851; 2010(4) SCALE 568; [2010] 5 S.C.R. 678; 2010 (2) UC 707; 2010 (3) WLN(SC) 9

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Banking, Public Financial Institutions and Negotiable Instruments Laws (amendment) Act, 1988

Summary: (A) Criminal - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, ss. 138 and 147 - Compounding of offences - Appellant and respondent parties were involved in commercial transactions - Disputes had arisen on account of the dishonour of cheques issued by the appellant - Thereafter, the parties went through the several stages of litigation and appellant was convicted by HC - Present appeal - Appellant pleading for the setting aside of his conviction in these matters by relying on the consent terms that have been arrived at between the parties - Respondent has not opposed this plea - Held, compounding of the offence allowed and appellant's conviction in each of the impugned judgments set aside - Appeals disposed of.

(B) Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, ss. 138, 142 and 147 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 320 - Compounding of offences under the Act - Held, s. 147 of the Act is in the nature of an enabling provision which provides for the compounding of offences prescribed under the Act, thereby serving as an exception to the general rule incorporated in sub-section (9) of s. 320 of the CrPC which states that 'No offence shall be compounded except as provided by this Section' - Hence, in view of the non-obstante clause, the compounding of offences under the Act is controlled by s. 147 of the Act and the scheme contemplated by s. 320 of the CrPC will not be applicable in the strict sense since the latter is meant for the specified offences under the IPC - Further, since s. 147 of the Act was inserted by way of an amendment to a special law, the same will override the effect of s. 320(9) of the CrPC, especially keeping in mind that s. 147 carries a non-obstante clause - Appeals disposed of.

(C) Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, ss. 138, 142 and 147 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 320 - Compounding of offences under the Act - Dishonour of cheques - With regard to the progression of litigation in cheque bouncing cases, Attorney General urged SC to frame guidelines for a graded scheme of imposing costs on parties who unduly delay compounding of the offence - It was submitted that application for compounding made after several years not only results in the system being burdened but the complainant is also deprived of effective justice - Held, s. 147 of the Act does not carry any guidance on how to proceed with the compounding of offences under the Act - Even, scheme contemplated u/s. 320 of the CrPC cannot be followed in the strict sense - Therefore, in view of the legislative vacuum, there is no hurdle to the endorsement of some suggestions which have been designed to discourage litigants from unduly delaying the composition of the offence in cases involving s. 138 of the Act - Further, graded scheme for imposing costs is a means to encourage compounding at an early stage of litigation - Guidelines framed - Appeals disposed of.

(D) Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 200 - Filing of multiple complaints relatable to the same transaction - Submitted that complaints are being increasingly filed in multiple jurisdictions in a vexatious manner which causes tremendous harassment and prejudice to the drawers of the cheque - Held, directed that it should be mandatory for the complainant to disclose that no other complaint has been filed in any other court in respect of the same transaction - Such a disclosure should be made on a sworn affidavit which should accompany the complaint filed u/s. 200 of the CrPC - If it is found that such multiple complaints have been filed, orders for transfer of the complaint to the first court should be given, generally speaking, by the HC after imposing heavy costs on the complainant for resorting to such a practice - Directions should be given effect prospectively - Appeals disposed of.
