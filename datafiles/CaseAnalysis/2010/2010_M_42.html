M/S Dozco India P. Ltd. v M/S Doosan Infracore Co. Ltd.

Supreme Court of India

08 October 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

V.S. Sirpurkar

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 855; (2011) 6 SCC 179; 2011 (1) AWC 564; JT 2010 (12) SC 198; 2011 (4) MLJ(SC) 854; 2011 (3) RCR(Civil) 592; 2010(10) SCALE 691; [2010] 13 S.C.R. 259; 2010 (4) WLN(SC) 20

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Constitution

Keywords: Curial Law

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Constitution - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, s. 11(6) - Companies Act, 1956 - Jurisdiction of Indian Courts - International arbitration - A Distributorship Agreement which was entered between the petitioner, a company registered under the Companies Act, 1956 and respondent, a company incorporated in Seoul, South Korea - Art. 23 of the Distributorship Agreement provides for the resolution of disputes by arbitration - Disputes arose between parties - In pursuance of the disputes, the petitioner issued notice for appointment of an Arbitrator to resolve the disputes that arose between the parties - However, that was not done and hence the present petition filed - Whether SC would be justified and would have the jurisdiction to appoint an Arbitrator u/s. 11(6) of the Act? - Held, a clear language of Arts. 22 and 23 of the Distributorship Agreement between the parties in present case spell out a clear agreement between the parties excluding Part I of the Act - Also, since the interpretation of art. 23.1 suggests that the law governing the arbitration will be Korean law and the seat of arbitration will be Seoul in Korea, there will be no question of applicability of s. 11(6) of the Act and the appointment of Arbitrator in terms of that provision - Petition dismissed.
