Ranjit Singh v State Of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

04 January 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Dalveer Bhandari, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 41; (2010) 12 SCC 506; 2010 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1158; 2011 (5) RCR(Criminal) 94; 2010(1) SCALE 554

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss.302, 307 and 333 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s.428 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant was sentenced to imprisonment for life u/s. 302 of IPC, 10 years' rigorous imprisonment u/s. 307 IPC and 10 years' rigorous imprisonment u/s. 333 IPC - While sentencing the appellant, the Additional Sessions Judge did not grant him benefit of set off u/s. 428 of CrPC - Whether appellant was entitled to benefit u/s.428 CrPC -

Held, appellant was entitled to the benefit of set off u/s. 428 of CrPC on basis of judgment of SC - Bhagirath vs. Delhi Administration, 1985 Indlaw SC 427, relied on - Appeals partly allowed.
