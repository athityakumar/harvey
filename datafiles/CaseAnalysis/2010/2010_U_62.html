Prem Chand And Others v Union Of India

Supreme Court of India

30 March 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

V.S. Sirpurkar, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 253; (2010) 4 SCC 551; AIR 2010 SC 1308; 2010 (3) ALD(SC) 98; 2010 (80) ALR 256; 2010 (3) AWC 2500; JT 2010 (3) SC 365; 2010 (2) RCR(Civil) 622; 2010 (110) RD 636; 2010(3) SCALE 440; 2010 (2) WLN(SC) 17

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land acquisition Act, 1894 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 6 r. 17 and s. 151 - Land acquisition compensation - Land in question were acquired - Claimants filed application under O. 6 r. 17 r/w s. 151, CPC for enhancement of compensation - HC enhanced the amount and granted compensation at Rs.39,300 per bigha - HC, however, ordered that the claimants would not be entitled to the benefit u/s. 23(1-A) of the Act - Hence, present appeal - (A) Whether claimants entitled to benefit u/s. 23(1-A) of the Act? - Held, relied on SC judgment in K.S. Paripoornan v. State of Kerala & Ors. 1994 INDLAW SC 1227 wherein it was held that the claimants would be entitled to the compensation u/s. 23(1-A) r/w s.30(1)(b) since the award was not made on or before 30.04.1982 - In the present case, award was passed on 25.02.1983 and it was pending on 24.09.1984, therefore, claimants would, be entitled to the benefit u/s. 23(1-A) of the Act - Direction of the HC set aside - (B) Whether HC erred in fixing the compensation at Rs.39,300 per bigha? - It was submitted that in Delhi Development Authority v. Bali Ram Sharma & Others 2004 INDLAW SC 573, SC awarded compensation at Rs.76,550 per bigha therefore in the present case also claimants would be would be entitled to the compensation at Rs.76,550 per bigha as lands in question have been held to be identically circumstanced with the land in said judgment - Held, the lands are identically circumstanced, therefore, it would not be proper to grant the compensation at lesser rate of Rs.39,300 per bigha - However, rate of Rs.76,550 was in respect of the Notification dated 17.11.1980 and the Notification in the present case was published on 22.03.1978, therefore, compensation scale down by deducting 10 per cent - Compensation to be paid at Rs.69,550 plus the benefit u/s. 23(1-A) r/w s. 30(1)(b) of the Act - Appeal partly allowed.
