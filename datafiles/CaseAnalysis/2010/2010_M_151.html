D.V. Paul v Manisha Lalwani

Supreme Court of India

18 August 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 778; (2010) 8 SCC 546; AIR 2010 SC 3356; 2010 (6) ALD(SC) 145; 2011 (1) AWC 280; 2011 (1) JabLJ 126; JT 2010 (9) SC 434; 2010 (7) MLJ(SC) 840; 2011(3) M.P.H.T. 1; 2010 (4) MPLJ 610; 2010 (4) RCR(Civil) 190; 2010 (2) RCR(Rent) 251; 2010(8) SCALE 337; [2010] 10 S.C.R. 425

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 148 - M.P. Accommodation Control Act, 1961, ss. 12(1)(a), 12(1)(c) 12(1)(k), 12(1)(m) - Extension of time for making of deposit in terms of decree - Respondent filed a suit for eviction of the appellant, which was dismissed by Trial Court - On appeal, HC directed deposit of Rs.10,000/- towards compensation payable to the respondent for the damages caused to the premises in the occupation of the appellant - It was further directed that failure to deposit would result in a decree of eviction against the appellant - However, appellant instead of the deposit of the said amount before the Trial Court as directed by the HC, committed a bonafide mistake in getting the bank draft for the said amount prepared in the name of the respondent and sending the same to her under Registered A.D - Since respondent denied the receipt of such a draft, the question whether or not the draft had been sent and if so received by the respondent through her domestic help fell in issue for purposes of determining whether the appellant had complied with the directions contained in the decree - HC, by an interim order, directed an inquiry into the matter - On appeal, SC held that such an inquiry to be unnecessary - SC further held that since no deposit had been made in terms of the decree passed by the HC, the Executing Court could proceed with the execution of decree - In view of this, HC held no further action or direction was necessary in the said application - Thereafter, appellant filed an application, seeking an inquiry into the question whether the bank draft sent by the appellant had been received by the respondent - Executing Court held that there was no need for any inquiry for the execution of the decree - Application seeking extension of time for deposit of a sum of Rs.10,000/- by the appellant was also dismissed - Present appeals challenging correctness of the said orders - (A) Enlargement of time - Whether extension can be legally granted in a case where non making of the deposit would result in a civil consequence like a decree of eviction against the appellant? - Held, as per s. 148 of CPC when any time period is granted by the Court for doing any act, the Court has the discretion from time to time to enlarge such period even if the time originally fixed or granted by the Court has expired - Hence, power to fix the time for doing of an act carry with it the power to extend such period, depending upon whether the party in default makes out a case to the satisfaction of the Court who has fixed the time - There is no distinction depending on whether the prayer for extension is in regard to a conditional order or a conditional decree - (B) Whether in the facts and circumstances of the case, extension of time was justified for making of the deposit, and if so, on what terms? - Held, fact that appellant prepared bank draft of Rs.10,000/- in the name of the respondent and dispatched the same under Registered AD cover to the respondent established the bona fides of the appellant - Also fact that the appellant offered to deposit the amount of Rs.10,000/- afresh also shows that there was no deliberate inaction on his part so as to disentitle him to the relief of extension of time - Hence, this was a fit case where extension of time for making of the deposit by the Trial Court ought to be granted - Extension of time granted - Appeals disposed of.
