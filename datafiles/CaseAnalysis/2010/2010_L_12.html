Shyam Gopal Bindal And Ors. v Land Acquisition Officer And Anr.

Supreme Court of India

11 January 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Surinder Singh Nijjar, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 14; (2010) 2 SCC 316; AIR 2010 SC 690; 2010(2) ALL MR 465; 2010 (78) ALR 931; 2010 (1) AWC 598; JT 2010 (1) SC 323; 2010 (3) MPLJ 74; 2010 (2) RCR(Civil) 845; 2010 (109) RD 502; 2010(1) SCALE 241; 2010 (1) UC 500

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Rajasthan Biswedari & Abolition of Jamindari Act

Summary: Land & Property - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 41 r. 27 - Possession of land - Adduce of additional evidence - Remand of matter - Appellants claimed to be owners in possession of suit land on basis of order passed by Additional District Collector - Another order was passed in Revenue Suit by Sub Divisional Officer and direction was issued on basis of averments made in application that appellants were cultivators of land in dispute - Predecessors of appellants filed civil suit in Court of Additional Civil Judge (A.B.) & Judicial Magistrate First Class, seeking injunction and declaration and suit of plaintiff was dismissed - Further, Appellants filed Civil Appeal in Court of Additional District Judge, along with appeal application under O. 41 r. 27 of CPC was filed to adduce additional evidence - Appellate Court dismissed appeal and rejected application for additional evidence - Against orders of lower Courts appellants moved to HC by way of second appeal which was dismissed - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether Courts below erred in not accepting application for additional evidence and not remanding matter back to Trial Court - Held, documents which were sought to be produced by appellants formed very basis of claim made by appellants in civil suit - However, it could not be said that appellants was not given any reason as to why documents was not produced in trial Court - Therefore, it appeared that dismissal of suit by Trial Court for non-production of evidence by plaintiff was mechanically affirmed by Appellate Court - Thus, application was not examined keeping in view principles laid down in O. 41 r. 27 of the CPC - Documents sought to be produced were Judicial Orders declaring ownership rights of appellants, that have crucial bearing on merits of claim put forward by appellants and prayer was duly made before Appellate Court which was repeated before HC for remanding matter with opportunity to adduce additional evidence - Hence, Appellate Court as also HC erred in not accepting application for additional evidence and not remanding matter back to trial court - Appeal allowed.
