Pillayar P. K. V. K. N. Trust, Thru Ramanathan v Karpaga N. N. U. S., Rep. by Secretary and others

Supreme Court of India

01 September 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

V.S. Sirpurkar, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 714; (2010) 9 SCC 344; AIR 2010 SC 3266; JT 2010 (9) SC 393; 2010 (8) MLJ(SC) 808; 2010(9) SCALE 1; [2010] 11 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Trusts & Associations

Keywords: Tamil Nadu District Municipalities Act, 1920, Madurai City Municipal Corporation Act, 1971, Tamil Nadu Panchayats Building Rules, 1970, Tamil Nadu Town & Country Planning Act, 1971, Madras Panchayat Act, 1958, Madras Corporation Act, Preparation, Publication and Sanction of Detailed Development Plan Rules

Summary: Land & Property - Trusts & Associations - Tamil Nadu Town and Country Planning Act, 1971 - Development plan of local government - De-reservation of private lands - Legality - Appellant/Trust acquired properties and divided the same into different residential plots - Appellant submitted lay out plan to the concerned Panchayat and Panchayat approved the plan - Appellant sold the plots to individuals retaining 40 plots for its own use - Panchayat, later, merged with the City Corporation and appellants submitted the plan to Corporation for revalidating the same - Corporation, in the mean time, prepared a development plan wherein appellant's 40 plots were shown as residential areas and thereupon de-reserved the same from development plan - Appellant proposed to make some construction on its plot and thereby submitted its application to Corporation for approval - Corporation rejected appellant's application on the ground that appellant's plots were demarcated for public purposes and not for residential purposes - Appellant challenged the Corporation's rejection order before the HC - HC quashed Corporation's rejection order - Appellant filed another application to the Corporation for approval and the Corporation, again, rejected appellant's application - Appellant made a representation to the Director of Town and Country Planning and consequently, State Government, by G.O.Ms. No.244 dated 23-9-1994, de-reserved appellant's plot from development plan - Respondent/Residential Association challenged G.O.Ms. No.244 before the HC - HC, by impugned order, quashed G.O.Ms. No.244 and directed the Corporation to utilize appellant's plot only for public purposes - Hence the present appeal - Whether G.O. Ms. 244 passed by the State Government de-reserving appellant's land was justifiable - Held, nothing to suggest that the G.O.Ms. 244 was hit by mala fides or was in excess of the power of the State Government and there was no violation of principles of natural justice - Earlier HC order was studied by the Director of Town and Country Planning who recommended the case for de-reservation of appellant's plots and there was no reason to doubt the correctness of the said recommendation since the said Director was aware of the earlier position - Director consulted the earlier correspondence on the subject and, therefore, the HC was completely in error in deducing that the order was in excess of the power of the State Government or was hit by mala fides or was in violation of the principles of natural justice - Even if it is presumed that the appellant's plots were private lands, yet the said lands were not acquired either by agreement or by acquisition, therefore, they would be deemed to be released from reservation - HC completely ignored the fact that the appellant had already parted with more than 21 acres of its land while getting approval from the concerned erstwhile Panchayat for lay out plan - Therefore, State Government took a right stance in passing the G.O.Ms. 244, hence impugned HC order is set aside - Appeal dismissed.
