Daya Singh And Anr. v Gurdev Singh (Dead) By L.Rs. And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

07 January 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Tarun Chatterjee, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 22; (2010) 2 SCC 194; AIR 2010 SC 3240; 2010(2) ALL MR 461; 2010 (78) ALR 889; 2010 (1) AWC 680; 2010 (1) CLT(SC) 31; 2010 (1) CTC 843; JT 2010 (1) SC 54; 2010 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 329; 2010 (2) MLJ(SC) 815; 2010 (1) RCR(Civil) 654; 2010 (109) RD 498; 2010 (1) RLW 909; 2010(1) SCALE 127; [2010] 1 S.C.R. 194; 2010 (1) UC 585

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Land & Property - Limitation Act, 1963 - Barred by limitation - Appellants were owners and in joint possession of 1/9th share in entire land - Two co-owners sold their respective shares to respondents - Respondents got their names mutated in relevant record of rights as owners of area purchased by them - Appellants filed a pre-emption suit against respondents for possession of 2/3rd share sold to them and got it decreed in their favour by trial court - Appellate court dismissed appeal filed against said order - Subsequently, appellants and respondents compromised their dispute and such compromise was reduced into writing on 26-10-1972 - As per compromise, appellants were entitled to retain half of 2/3rd share of impugned land and respondents were to retain other half - After such compromise was effected, appellants filed a suit for declaration that they were in possession as owner of 1/9th share and in joint possession of half of 2/3rd share (thus totalling of 4/9th shares) - Respondents contended that suit was barred by limitation in view of art. 58 of Act because suit having been filed after about 18 years of entering into compromise by parties - Whether suit for declaration and injunction could be held to be barred by limitation? - Held, right to sue accrued when such right was infringed by respondents about a week back when appellants had for first time come to know about wrong entries in record of rights and when respondents had refused to admit claim of appellants - Suit was filed on 21-10-1990 - Therefore, within three years from date of infringement suit was filed - Suit cannot be held to be barred by limitation - Appeal allowed.
