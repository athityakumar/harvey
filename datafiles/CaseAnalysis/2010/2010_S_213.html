Md. Shahabuddin v State Of Bihar And Others

Supreme Court of India

25 March 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Dalveer Bhandari, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 232; (2010) 4 SCC 653; JT 2010 (3) SC 266; 2010 (2) MLJ(Crl) 779; 2010 (2) RCR(Criminal) 615; 2010(3) SCALE 204

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Keywords: Power Of The Court, Special Courts, West Bengal Special Courts Act, 1950, Public Trial, Open Trial

Summary: Dalveer Bhandari, J.

Constitution - Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 9(6), 465 - Bengal, Agra and Assam Civil Courts Act, 1887, ss. 13(1) and 14(1) - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14 and 16 - Trial inside jail - Notification No.184A dated 20.05.2006 was issued whereby Patna HC in exercise of administrative powers conferred u/s. 9(6) of CrPC decided that premises of District Jail, Siwan will be place of sitting of Court of Sessions for Sessions Division of Siwan for expeditious trial of Sessions cases pending against appellant - Another Notification dated 7.6.2006 was issued by Governor of Bihar, in exercise of powers conferred by s.13(1) and s. 14(1) of Act and s. 9(6) of CrPC and in light of Notification No. 184A - Appellant challenging legality of notifications - (A) Appellant submitted that notification dated 20.05.2006 stated 'other proceedings before and during trial' and therefore, s. 465 would not apply - Held, a notification empowering a Sessions Court to sit and hold a trial inside jail is not outside purview of s. 465 of CrPC - It would come within meaning of other proceedings - Firstly, as per admission of appellant himself, judicial proceedings against him had started in several cases and trials were going on, and therefore, it would come within purview of words 'before or during trial' - Emphasis of State is on 'during trial' - Secondly, words 'other proceedings before and during trial' would include notification issued by HC and given effect to by State Government by virtue of constitutional provisions in Chapter-VI of Constitution relating to Subordinate Courts and notification is in nature of a sanction to prosecute appellant within Siwan Jail premises in courts of Sessions Division and Judicial Magistrate - Notification issued, therefore, in other proceedings during trial would clearly come within purview of s. 465 of CrPC - It would also come within words 'irregularities in any sanction for prosecution' - (B) Appellant further submitted that exercise of power for transfer of proceedings could only be done u/s. 407 of CrPC after giving adequate opportunity of hearing to appellant - Held, it has been answered against appellant by SC in Ranbir Singh v. State of Bihar (1995) 4 SCC page 392 - (C) Violation of principles of natural justice - Held, holding of trials of appellant in open court may affect trials of other civil and criminal cases that are going on in same court building - Therefore, there is no violation of principles of natural justice in shifting trials of cases of appellant from a regular court to a special court - (D) Whether present case would fall in category of extraordinary or exceptional cases where universal rule of open trial can be given a go-bye? - Held, though trials are taking place in jail but in fact no real prejudice has been caused to appellant - Present case would fall in category of those extraordinary and exceptional cases where in interest of justice it became imperative to shift venue of trials - (E) Notifications - Legality - Held, three notifications, one issued by HC dated 20.5.2006 and two issued by State Government on 7.6.2006 are valid and were issued in consonance with provisions of law - (F) Power of HC to change venue of proceedings - Held, HC in view of extraordinary facts and circumstances of a particular case is empowered to change venue of pending case/trial without hearing appellant and this would not violate appellant's fundamental rights u/arts. 14 and 21 or any other provision of Constitution - Relied on Kehar Singh & Others v State (Delhi Administration) 1988 INDLAW SC 403 - (H) Compliance of rule of 'audi alteram partem' - Held, it is clear from the wording of s. 9 of the CrPC that there is no need for the High Court to give a hearing while deciding the venue of the trial - It is only if the Sessions Court is moving the place of trial that the parties have a right to a hearing - Appeal dismissed.

Dr. Mukundakam Sharma, J.

Constitution - Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 9(6), 465 - Bengal, Agra and Assam Civil Courts Act, 1887, ss. 13(1) and 14(1) - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14 and 16 - Trial inside jail - Appeal challenging legality and validity of three notifications one of which was issued by Patna HC on 20.05.2006 and other two notifications dated 07.06.2006 were issued by Government of Bihar - Impugned notifications issued by respondents was for holding trial of cases pending against appellant in one Sessions Division and for constituting and establishing two Special Courts i.e. Court of Additional District and Sessions Judge to try sessions cases pending against appellant and Court of Judicial Magistrate, First Class to try cases pending against appellant in Court of Judicial Magistrate, First Class, within premises of District Jail - Appellant, a Member of Parliament, being aggrieved by issuance of aforesaid notifications filed a writ petition in HC of Patna, which was dismissed - Hence, present appeal - (A) Scope and ambit of power u/s. 9(6) and s. 11 of CrPC - Held, s. 11 of CrPC makes it explicitly clear that a Court of Judicial Magistrate could be established by State Government after consultation with HC - By virtue of provision contained in second part of S. 9(6), Court of Sessions is endowed with power to hold its sittings at any place in sessions division other than that notified by HC - (B) Non-publication of notification in official Gazette - Held, issue is a mixed question of law and fact and said facts were required to be urged evidentially before courts below - Therefore, such a mixed question of law and fact should not be allowed to be raised at time of final hearing of appeal before SC - However, HC took all necessary steps to get notification issued and published in official gazette - Impugned notification dated 20.05.2006 satisfies all requirements as envisaged u/s. 11 of CrPC and, therefore, said notification legal and valid inasmuch as, same was issued by competent authority and also in full compliance with requirements and safeguards provided in said provisions - (C) Compliance of rule of 'audi alteram partem' - Whether rule of audi alteram partem should have been complied with when HC notified a shift in venue of trial? - Held, power of HC u/s. 9(6) to notify a particular place or places where Court of Sessions shall ordinarily hold its sitting is an administrative power unlike power of Court of Sessions under second part of s. 9(6) of CrPC which is judicial in nature - Being so, HC was under no obligation to observe rule of audi alteram partem - Furthermore, second part of s. 9(6) of CrPC expressly requires Court of Sessions to afford prosecution and accused an opportunity of hearing and to obtain their consent beforehand whereas there is no such stipulation under first part of s. 9(6) of CrPC - Omission of such a requirement in case of HC pertaining to first part of s. 9(6) of CrPC is to be construed as a conscious decision on part of legislature for, it intended to exclude such a requirement when such power is to be exercised by HC - It was not necessary for HC to observe or comply with rule of audi alteram partem before issuing notification dated 20.05.2006 - (D) Transfer of pending case - Held, there were 40 cases pending against appellant and they were being tried in different courts creating difficulties for conducting cases at various courts both for prosecution as also to appellant - Since venue of trial of a group or a class of cases was shifted by establishing and constituting a Court within District Jail, Siwan, same cannot be said to be void or invalid in any manner - (E) Violation of art. 14 and 21 of Constitution and s. 327 of CrPC - Held, trial inside a jail does not stand vitiated solely because it is conducted inside jail premises - However, at the same time, there must be compliance of provisions contained in s. 327 of CrPC which guarantees certain safeguards to ensure that a trial is an open trial - In the present case, there was sufficient compliance with s. 327 of CrPC - No prejudice was caused to appellant while shifting cases to Special Courts situated inside premises of District Jail, Siwan - Therefore, there was no violation either of s. 327 or of arts. 14 and 21 of Constitution - (E) Applicability of s. 407 of CrPC - Held, s. 407 of CrPC deals with power of HC to 'transfer' cases and appeals - Every transfer involves two different courts - By issuing said notification, HC cannot be said to have transferred cases pending against appellant, for said notification simply notified premises of District Jail, Siwan, to be place of sitting for holding trial of cases pending against appellant - Notifications did not, in any manner, affect or abridge jurisdiction of Court of Sessions, Siwan, to try those cases - Thus, there was a shift simpliciter in venue of trial, without there being anything more - In such circumstances, present case cannot be said to be a case of 'transfer' to which provisions of s. 407 of CrPC are attracted - (F) Whether State Government has any power to issue such a direction u/s. 9(6) and S. 11 of CrPC? - Held, competent authority as envisaged under law having issued a notification for constituting and establishing a Sessions Court within District Jail, Siwan, any further notification by State Government making jail premises available for said purposes cannot be said to be illegal and void - Legality and validity of all three notifications upheld - Appeal dismissed.
