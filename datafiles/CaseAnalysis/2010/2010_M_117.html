East Coast Railway and another v Mahadev Appa Rao and others

Supreme Court of India

07 July 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Aftab Alam, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 490; (2010) 7 SCC 678; AIR 2010 SC 2794; 2010 (5) ALD(SC) 16; 2010 (5) AWC 4572; 2010 (3) ESC 393; 2010 (127) FLR 880; JT 2010 (6) SC 522; 2010 (8) MLJ(SC) 194; 2010(6) SCALE 432; [2010] 7 S.C.R. 908; 2010 (5) SLR 563

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Selection process - Arbitrary order - A notification was issued proposing to conduct a written/practical typewriting test for filling up the vacant posts of Chief Typists - 12 candidates appeared in the test and the result was announced - Some of the unsuccessful candidates made a representation complaining about the manner in which the test was conducted - Successful candidates filed application before the CAT for a direction to respondent to proceed with the selection - Meanwhile, typewriting test was cancelled and another notification was issued for conducting fresh typewriting test - By interim order Tribunal allowed to conduct the proposed second test - Pursuant to the said notification and the order passed by the Tribunal a fresh test was indeed conducted - Tribunal dismissed application holding that the test earlier conducted was rightly cancelled - A Writ Petition was filed before the HC - HC set aside the order passed by the Tribunal - HC further directed the respondent to proceed with the selection process pursuant to earlier notification - Present appeals - Whether decision of the competent authority in the present case be described as arbitrary? - Held, arbitrariness in the making of an order by an authority can manifest itself in different forms - Non-application of mind by the authority making the order is one of them - Every order passed by a public authority must disclose due and proper application of mind by the person making the order - Absence of reasons either in the order passed by the authority or in the record contemporaneously maintained is clearly suggestive of the order being arbitrary hence legally unsustainable - In the present case, order passed by the competent authority does not state any reasons whatsoever for the cancellation of the typing test - In the absence of reasons in support of the order it is difficult to assume that the authority had properly applied its mind before passing the order cancelling the test - Order of cancellation passed by the competent authority was not preceded even by a prima facie satisfaction about the correctness of the allegations made by the unsuccessful candidates leave alone an inquiry into the same - Competent authority directed to pass an appropriate order - Order accordingly.
