State of Haryana And Others v Jagdish

Supreme Court of India

22 March 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, J.M. Panchal

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 196; (2010) 4 SCC 216; AIR 2010 SC 1690; 2010 (80) ALR 258; 2010 CRLJ 2398; 2010 (3) JhrCR(SC) 109; JT 2010 (3) SC 341; 2010 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1121; 2010 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1346; 2010 (2) MLJ(Crl) 996; 2010 (2) RCR(Criminal) 464; 2010(3) SCALE 285

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Keywords: Life Convict, West Bengal Correctional Services Act, 1992, Rajasthan Prisons (Shortening of Sentences) Rules, 1958

Summary: Criminal - Constitution - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 432 (1) r/w. s. 433 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 72 and 161 - Life convict - Pre-mature release - Respondent was convicted and sentenced for life imprisonment vide judgment and order dt. 20-05-1999 - Respondent, having served more than 10 years imprisonment, approached the HC that in spite of having undergone the sentence as per the policy decision dt. 04-02-1993, his case for pre-mature release was not being considered in view of the new policy of short sentencing, introduced on 13-08-2008 - HC relied on SC decision in State of Haryana vs. Mahender Singh & Ors 2007 INDLAW SC 1087 and State of Haryana vs. Bhup Singh 2008 INDLAW SC 2020 held that the case of the respondent for pre-mature release was to be considered in the light of the short sentencing policy existing on the date of his conviction and thus, a direction was issued to the State Authorities to consider his case for pre- mature release in view of the policy dt. 04-2-1993 existing on the date of his conviction i.e. 20-5-1999 - Petitioner submitted that SC in State of Haryana & Ors. vs. Balwan 1999 INDLAW SC 1704 held that law prevailing on date on which convict's case was required to be put up before Governor is relevant and not law prevailing on date of their conviction - Hence, present appeal - Whether the policy which makes a provision for remission of sentence, should be that which was existing on the date of the conviction of the accused or it should be the policy that exists on the date of consideration of his case for pre-mature release by the appropriate authority? - Held, policy dt. 04-02-1993 refers to the exercise of powers u/art. 161 of the Constitution whereas the policy dt. 13-08-2008 is in exercise of the powers u/s. 432 r/w. ss. 433 and 433-A of Cr.P.C - Restriction u/s. 433-A is only to the extent of the powers to be exercised in respect of offences as referred to u/s. 432 Cr.P.C - Notification dt. 13-08-2008 is, therefore, under a rule of procedure, which is subordinate to the Constitution - Power exercised u/art. 161 of the Constitution is obviously a mandate of the Constitution and, therefore, the policy dt. 13-08-2008 cannot override the policy dt. 04-02-1993 - Therefore, right of the respondent prisoner, to get his case considered at par with such of his inmates, who were entitled to the benefit of the said policy, cannot be taken away by the policy dt. 13-08-2008 - HC, was absolutely justified in arriving at the conclusion that the case of the respondent was to be considered on the strength of the policy that was existing on the date of his conviction - State authority is under an obligation to at least exercise its discretion in relation to an honest expectation perceived by the convict, at the time of his conviction that his case for pre-mature release would be considered after serving the sentence, prescribed in the short sentencing policy existing on that date - No reason to interfere with the judgment of the HC - Appeal dismissed.
