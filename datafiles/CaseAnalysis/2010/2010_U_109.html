Soham Mayankkumar Vyas and another v Union of India and others

Supreme Court of India

28 September 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, H.L. Gokhale

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 924; (2010) 13 SCC 137; 2010(6) ALL MR 935; JT 2010 (12) SC 481; 2010(11) SCALE 362; [2010] 11 S.C.R. 818

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Education

Keywords: Dental Council of India Screening Test Regulations, 2009

Summary: Education - Constitution - Dentists Act, 1948, s. 2(j) - Dental Council of India Screening Test Regulations, 2009 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Foreign College sought affiliation with Bhavnagar University which was granted by Government for the academic year 2004-05 and renewed the affiliation for 2004-05 and 2005-06 - Subsequently, Dental Council of India framed the Dental Council of India Screening Test Regulations 2009, providing for conduct of a screening test to determine the eligibility of candidates for registration with any State Dental Council or any other purpose - Said Regulations were applicable only to those Indian citizens possessing a primary dental qualification/PG Diploma/Post Graduate Dental qualification, awarded by any dental institution outside India, who were desirous of getting registration with any State Dental Council or of any other, they shall have to qualify in a screening test conducted by the prescribed authority for that purpose - Government also issued notification dt. 6.3.2009 adding the said qualification in Part III of the Schedule to the 1948 Act - Petitioners filed writ petitions against said notification dt. 6.3.2009 and Regulations, 2009 and sought quashing of the notification dt. 6.3.2009 and a declaration that the communications of the Government of India requiring the students of the foreign College to appear in the screening test is illegal and contrary to art. 14 of the Constitution of India and that the DCI Screening Test Regulations, 2009 are inapplicable to students who have been conferred BDS degree by Bhavnagar University - Respondents resisted the said petitions contending that the dental qualifications possessed by the Indian students of foreign College are 'foreign dental qualifications obtained by citizens of India' and they are therefore required to undergo a screening test as provided by the DCI Screening Test Regulations, 2009 - (A) Whether the notification dt. 6.3.2009, entering the Foreign College and its BDS degree, in the III Schedule to the 1948 Act is valid? - Held, the Government does not dispute the fact that the BDS examinations for the students of Foreign College are held by Bhavnagar University and the degrees are also granted by Bhavnagar University and not by any authority or institution outside India - So long as the Foreign College is affiliated to Bhavnagar University and the said University is the examining body and is the authority which grants the BDS degree dental qualification to the students of Foreign College, s. 10(4) and Part-III of the Schedule to the Act will not apply - As Bhavnagar University is not an authority outside India, the dental qualifications granted by it cannot be included in Part III of the Schedule to the Act even if the college/institution affiliated to the said University is outside India - Therefore the notification dt. 6.3.2009 is illegal being contrary to and violative of s. 10(1) and (4) of the 1948 Act and liable to be struck down - (B) Whether the BDS graduates from Foreign College affiliated to Bhavnagar University have to take the screening test under the DCI Screening Regulations, 2009, for recognition of their BDS degrees in India? - Held, the dental qualification of BDS obtained by undergoing a course of study at foreign College is not listed in Part I of 1948 Act - Though the recognition of the dental qualification by the Central Government is with reference to the University (Authority) which grants it, Part I of the Schedule makes it clear that in regard to dental qualification granted by an Indian University, the affiliated College/Institution where the course of study is undergone should have prior permission of the Central Government - There was no direction to Central Government to recognize the Foreign College or treat it as having the 'prior permission' of the Central Government - As BDS degrees granted by the Bhavnagar University is recognized only with reference to a College in Gujarat and as Foreign College has not been included in Part I of the Schedule to the 1948 Act, foreign College will have to take steps to get its name entered in Part I, as an institution affiliated to Bhavnagar University for getting the benefit of 'recognized dental qualification - Hence, unless and until foreign College is included as an affiliated institution in Part-I of the Schedule to the 1948 Act, the Indian students of foreign College will have to undergo a screening test as per the first proviso to regn. (4) of DCI Screening Test Regulations 2009 - Writ petitions partly allowed.
