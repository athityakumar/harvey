Ajmer Singh v State of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

15 February 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Dattu, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 394; AIR 2010 SC (Supp) 582; 2010 ALL MR (Cri) 1323; JT 2010 (2) SC 175; 2010 (2) MLJ(Crl) 292; 2010 (2) RCR(Criminal) 132; 2010(2) SCALE 362

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985, s. 20 and 50 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 313 - Possession of narcotic substance without license - Conviction - Challenged - 500 gms of charas wrapped in wax paper recovered from possession of the appellant without licence - Trial court convicted appellant and sentenced him to rigorous imprisonment of 10 years with 1 lakh as fine - On appeal, HC confirmed the order of trial court - Appellant contended that prosecution has not joined any independent witnesses to prove the fact of recovery of 'Charas' from the possession of the accused - Appellant also contended that the principle of parity requires the awarding of lesser punishment as has been done in the case of co-accused 'X' - Hence, present appeal - Whether order of HC could be upheld? - Held, for applying the principle of parity both the accused must be involved in same crime and must be convicted in single trial, and consequently, accused is one who is awarded punishment along with the other accused in the same proceedings - Principle of parity does not apply to the present case as the record show that the accused 'X' was convicted vide a separate trial arising out of a separately registered F.I.R. - Merely because the accused 'X' happened to be searched on 24-1-1996 before the same Gazetted Officer, he cannot be said to be a co-accused in the present case - Order of HC upheld - Appeal dismissed.
