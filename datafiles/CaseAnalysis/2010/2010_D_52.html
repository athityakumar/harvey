Steel Authority Of India Ltd. & Ors v Dibyendu Bhattacharya

Supreme Court of India

29 October 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, J.M. Panchal, Gyan Sudha Misra

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 989; (2011) 11 SCC 122; AIR 2011 SC 897; 2011(2) ALL MR 981; 2011 (1) AWC 871; JT 2010 (12) SC 462; 2010(11) SCALE 647; 2011 (2) SLR 243; 2010 (4) UPLBEC 2945

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 39(d) - Parity in pay - Entitlement - Respondent was appointed as Therapist/Audiologist in appellant/Company - Respondent sought parity with another employee who was employed at another unit of the Company holding the post in Grade E-1 executive cadre though designated as Therapist/Audiologist - Respondent, subsequently, made a representation seeking change of cadre and for upgrading his post to executive cadre from the date of joining - Appellant rejected the representation - Respondent filed writ petition before the HC - Singe Judge dismissed writ petition, however, DB, in intra court appeal, allowed respondent's appeal and directed the appellant to grant the higher pay scale with arrears from the date of initial appointment - Hence the present appeal - Whether the respondent is entitled to the parity of pay and post - Held, it is the duty of an employee seeking parity of pay u/art. 39(d) of the Constitution to prove and establish that he had been discriminated against, as the question of parity has to be decided on consideration of various facts and statutory rules etc - Respondent, in representation, sought a relaxation in the eligibility criteria for the post in Grade E-1 - Representation itself evidenced that the respondent never possessed the eligibility for the post of Grade E-1 - Law does not prohibit an employer to have different grade of posts in two different units owned by him - Every unit is an independent entity for the purpose of making recruitment of most of its employees - Respondent had not been appointed in centralised services of the appellant/Company whereas the alleged similarly situated employee had been appointed in E-1 Grade, in another unit, considering his past services for about two decades prior to the recruitment of the respondent - As every unit may make appointments taking into consideration the local needs and requirement, such parity claimed by the respondent cannot be held to be tenable - Reliefs sought by the respondent for up-gradation of the post and waiving the eligibility criteria had rightly been refused by the appellants and by the Single Judge - In such a fact-situation, there was no justification for the DB to allow the writ petition, granting the benefit to respondent from the date of his initial appointment - Further, respondent did not produce any tangible material to substantiate his claim, thus, he could not discharge the onus of proof to establish that he had made some justifiable claim - Respondent failed to make out a case for pay parity to the post of E-1 Grade in executive cadre - Impugned judgment and order of the DB, therefore, is set aside and Single Judge order is restored - Appeal allowed.
