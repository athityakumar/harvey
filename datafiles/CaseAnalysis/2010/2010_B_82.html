Collector, Phagwara and others v Brahm Dutt and another

Supreme Court of India

25 October 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 908; (2010) 13 SCC 78; JT 2010 (12) SC 274; 2011 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 3697; 2011 (2) RCR(Civil) 299; 2010(11) SCALE 297

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Keywords: Punjab Public Premises and Land (Eviction and Rent Recovery) Act, 1973

Summary: Rent Control - Punjab Public Premises and Land (Eviction and Rent Recovery) Act, 1973, ss. 4 and 7 - Eviction petition - Appellant no. 3 filed two eviction petitions u/ss. 4 and 7 of 1973 Act against respondent alleging that impugned land belongs to the Government and has been unauthorizedly occupied by the respondents - Collector ordered the eviction of both the respondents - Commissioner dismissed appeal filed against said order and confirmed the orders passed by the Collector - On appeal, HC quashed the orders passed by the Collector and the Commissioner and held that the respondents had entered into possession of impugned land in an authorized manner and had become owners since they had been in possession for more than 30 years - Hence, present appeal - Whether order of the HC could be upheld? - Held, HC without adverting to the factual details, particularly, the assertions of the respondents as well as the entries in the Jamabandi for the relevant years and without assigning valid reasons, set aside the orders of the original and the appellate Authority - Ends of justice would be met by directing the original authority - the Collector to pass fresh order after considering the above materials and after affording opportunity to all the parties concerned - Impugned orders of the HC as well as the orders of the original authority set aside - Appeals allowed.
