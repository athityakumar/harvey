Secretary, Cannanore District Muslim Educational Association, Kanpur v State of Kerala and others

Supreme Court of India

07 May 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Ganguly, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 358; (2010) 6 SCC 373; AIR 2010 SC 1955; JT 2010 (6) SC 147; 2010 (2) KLT 725; 2010 (5) MLJ(SC) 731; 2010(5) SCALE 184; [2010] 6 S.C.R. 556; 2010 (5) SLR 226

Case Digest

Subject: Education

Keywords: Pre-degree Course (Abolition) Act, 1997, Judicial Review of administrative Act

Summary: Education - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 30 - Minority educational institution - Sanction to start new course - Entitlement - Appellant/Educational Society applied for sanctioning Higher Secondary Course to its school - Respondents accorded sanction to appellant as a special case, however, said decision for sanction was not implemented in the light of a decision of the HC wherein HC had directed the respondents that newer Higher Secondary schools were not to be sanctioned without further orders from the Court - Respondents, in the meantime, issued G.O.(P) No.107/07/G.Edn dated 13-6-2007, restraining any further sanctioning of Higher Secondary Course to any school - Appellant challenged respondent's refusal to sanction before HC - Appellant contended that respondents had afforded sanctions to number of other similar situated schools - HC dismissed appellant's writ petition - Held, since appellant is a religious minority, it has a fundamental right to establish and administer educational institutions of its choice in view of the clear mandate of art. 30 of the Constitution - Respondent committed itself to give the appellant the required sanction, however, it could not be implemented due to the existence of an earlier HC order - Before the procedural dispute in the court could be cleared, new policy of the Government had come into effect - Therefore, it cannot be denied that the appellant has a right or at least a legitimate expectation to get the permission to hold Higher Secondary classes and hence HC order is set aside - Direction issued to the respondents to sanction Higher Secondary course in the appellant's institution from the next academic session with the rider that the appellant must follow the extant statutory procedures for the appointment of teachers in the Higher Secondary section - Appeal allowed.
