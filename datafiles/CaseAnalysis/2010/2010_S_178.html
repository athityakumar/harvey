A. Natesam Pillai v Spl. Tahsildar, Land Acquisition, Tiruchy

Supreme Court of India

11 August 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Mukundakam Sharma, A.R. Dave

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 623; (2010) 9 SCC 118; 2011 (1) ALD(SC) 34; 2010(5) ALL MR 420; 2010 (6) AWC 5653; 2010 (4) CalLT 82; 2010 (6 ) CTC 327; JT 2010 (9) SC 39; 2010 (8) MLJ(SC) 352; 2011 (1) MPLJ 4; 2010 (4) RCR(Civil) 76; 2011 (112) RD 332; 2010(8) SCALE 152; [2010] 10 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4(1), 6 and 18 - Land acquisition proceedings - Quantum of compensation - Land acquired by Government of Tamil Nadu - Land Acquisition Officer awarded a sum of Rs. 1.72 per sq. ft. for the acquired land - Appellant filed a reference u/s. 18 of the Act for enhancement of compensation - Reference Court fixed the market value of the acquired land at Rs. 17/- per sq. Feet - On appeal, HC reduced the amount of compensation granted by Reference Court from Rs. 17/- per sq. feet to Rs. 9/- per sq. feet - Hence, present appeal - (A) Whether sale deed executed subsequent to date of notification u/s. 4(1) relevant for purpose of determining compensation? - Held, first clause of s. 23 of the Act clearly provides that the amount of compensation awarded for land acquired is required to be determined on the basis of market value of the land at the time of publication of the notification u/s. 4 of the Act - Any sale deed which is post notification date is generally ignored, unless evidence is led to show that there was no increase in price despite such acquisition - In the present case, no evidence adduced to show that the market value of adjacent land has not increased in the interregnum - Hence, Reference Court and HC were justified in rejecting these sale deeds from consideration - (B) Whether HC correct and justified in reducing the compensation to be given to the appellant? - Held, potentiality of acquired land, in so far as it relates to use to which it is reasonably capable of being put in the immediate or near future, must be given due consideration while determining compensation - Present acquired land has all the potentiality to be used as building sites, even in the immediate future - Further, its correct that acquisition of a large tract of land merits a discount, however, in the present circumstance, it is significant to note that the compensation granted by the HC does not match the potentiality of the land, even after the discount has been taken into consideration - Rate of Rs. 11/- would be adequate and just compensation for same - Appeal allowed.
