Banatwala & Company v L.I.C Of India & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

19 September 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Gokhale, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 1508

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Keywords: Prevention of Corruption Act 1947, Maharashtra Housing and Area Development Act, 1976, Liabilities, Criminal Law Amendment Act 1952, Bombay Housing Board Act, 1948, A.P. Shops and Establishments Act, 1966, Rajasthan Shops and Establishment Act, 1958, UP Sugarcane (Regulation of Supply and Purchase) Act, 1953, Tamil Nadu Public Men (Criminal Misconduct) Act 1973, Karnataka Rent (Amendment) Act, 2008

Summary: Rent Control - Maharashtra Rent Control Act, 1999 - Public Premises (Eviction of Unauthorised Occupants) Act, 1971 - Eviction - Public premises - Provisions of statutes - Applicability - Appellant was tenant in possession of fifth floor of building owned by respondent no.1,/LIC, which was statutory corporation - Petitioner was tenant of premises under agreement of lease which was extended from time to time - Respondent no. 2 revised monthly rent of premises suddenly by his letter including municipal taxes and miscellaneous charges - Appellant filed petition in HC to challenge increasing of rent as arbitrary but petition was withdrawn - Appellant filed application in Small Causes Court for restoration of lift services and Single Judge of Court directed respondents by to repair lift - Revision petition was filed by respondents to challenge order before Division Bench, which was dismissed - Appellant filed application in Court of Small Causes for fixation of standard rent and also filed application for fixing interim rent - Respondent filed application seeking decision on that issue as preliminary issue which was rejected - Respondents filed petition before HC Single Judge which was allowed and dismissed standard rent application of appellant - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether provisions for fixation of standard rent and provisions prescribing other obligations under 1999 Act for landlord were applicable in respect of public premises owned by corporation which was otherwise covered by provisions of 1971 Act - Held, there was no conflict between 1999 Act and 1971 Act when it comes to provisions in 1999 Act, with respect to fixation of standard rent and requiring landlord to maintain essential services and supplies - Provisions of 1999 Act in that behalf could not in any way be said to be repugnant to 1971 Act - Provisions of 1971 Act should govern relationship between public undertakings covered under 1971 Act and their occupants to extent they provide for eviction of unauthorized occupants from public premises, recovery of arrears of rent or damages for such unauthorized occupation, and other incidental matters specified under 1971 Act - Provisions of 1999Act should govern relationship between public undertakings and their occupants to extent that Act covers other aspects of relationship between landlord and tenants, not covered under 1971 Act - Application of appellant and similar applications of tenants for fixation of standard rent or for restoration of essential supplies and services when necessary, should be maintainable under 1999 Act - Provisions of 1999 Act with respect to fixation of standard rent for premises, and requiring landlord not to cut off or withhold essential supply or service, and to restore same when necessary, were not in conflict with or repugnant to any of provisions of 1971 Act - Hence, order passed by Single Judge, in Writ Petition filed by respondents was set aside and said Writ Petition shall stand dismissed - HC filed by respondents was set aside - Therefore, order passed by Court of Small Causes rejecting respondents application objecting to maintainability of appellant's application for fixation of standard rent was upheld - Appeal allowed.
