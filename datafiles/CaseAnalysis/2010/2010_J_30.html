Javed Masood and Another v State of Rajasthan

Supreme Court of India

09 March 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

B. SUDARSHAN REDDY, Surinder Singh Nijjar

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 153; (2010) 3 SCC 538; AIR 2010 SC 979; 2010 CRLJ 2020; 2010 (2) JhrCR(SC) 15; JT 2010 (2) SC 473; 2010 (4) MLJ(Crl) 356; 2010 (2) RCR(Criminal) 285; 2010(2) SCALE 717; [2010] 3 S.C.R. 236; 2010 (2) WLN(SC) 5

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 147, 201, 302, 323 and 324 - Conviction - Challenged - Trial Court convicted appellants for the offences punishable u/ss. 148, 201 and 302, IPC - On appeal, the HC, confirmed conviction u/s. 302, IPC but set aside the conviction of the appellants of the charges u/s. 201 and 148, IPC - Hence, present appeal - Entire prosecution case rests upon the Parcha Bayan lodged by PW-5 (brother of deceased) - Whether the courts below committed any manifest error in relying on the evidence of PW-5, PW-13 and PW-14 to convict the appellants for the charge u/s. 302, IPC? - Held, in evidence of PWs 6, 18, 29 and 30 it was specifically stated that PW-5 was not present at the place of occurrence - However, the prosecution never declared PWs 6,18, 29 and 30 'hostile' - Their evidence did not support the prosecution - Instead, it supported the defence - Evidence of PW-6 completely rules out the presence of PW-5 at the scene of offence - It was thus clear that PW-5 being interested witness made an attempt to implicate the appellant in the case due to previous enmity - In addition, the evidence of PWs 18, 29 and 30, all independent witnesses, also cast a serious doubt on the evidence of PWs 5, 13 and 14 as regards their presence at the scene of offence - Therefore, no reliance can be placed on the evidence of PW-5 to convict the appellants of the charge u/s. 302, IPC - Evidence of PWs 13 and 14 also discarded - No other acceptable evidence on record based on which the charge could be held proved against the appellants - Conviction of the appellants and the sentence imposed on them set aside - Appeal allowed.
