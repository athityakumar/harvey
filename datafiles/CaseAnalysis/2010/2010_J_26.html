Jeevan Diesels and Electricals Limited v Jasbir Singh Chadha (Huf) and another

Supreme Court of India

07 May 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Ganguly, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 345; (2010) 6 SCC 601; AIR 2010 SC 1890; 2010 (6) ALD(SC) 119; 2010 (81) ALR 222; 2010 (4) AWC 3427; JT 2010 (4) SC 574; 2010 (5) MLJ(SC) 311; 2010 (3) RCR(Civil) 217; 2010 (1) RCR(Rent) 532; 2010(5) SCALE 367; [2010] 6 S.C.R. 546

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 12 r. 6 - Decree of dispossession - Respondent-landlord, claiming to be the landlord/owner of the suit premises filed a suit against the appellant for recovery of possession and mesne profit - Respondent-landlord submitted that the case of termination of tenancy was admitted by the appellant in its written statement - A decree of dispossession was passed by the Additional District Judge - On appeal, HC held that a case of ejectment was made out against the appellant on the basis of admission of the case of the plaintiff-landlord in the written statement filed by appellant - HC affirmed the judgment and decree of dispossession passed by the Additional District Judge - Held, whether or not there is a clear, unambiguous admission by one party of the case of the other party is essentially a question of fact and the decision of this question depends on the facts of the case - This question, namely, whether there is a clear admission or not cannot be decided on the basis of a judicial precedent - Further, it has been held in number of judgments, a Court can act under O. 12 r. 6 of CPC the admission must be clear and unambiguous - In the present case, it cannot be said that there was a clear admission of the case of the respondent-landlord about termination of tenancy by the appellant in its written statement or in its reply to the petition of the respondent-landlord under O. 12 r. 6 of the CPC - In view of the facts of present case, judgment of the HC as well as of the Additional District Judge cannot be upheld - Matter remanded to the Trial Court for expeditious disposal - Order accordingly.
