Ajmer Singh v State Of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

15 February 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Dattu, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 1516; (2010) 3 SCC 746; 2010 CRLJ 1899; 2010 (1) JCC (Narcotics) 28; 2010 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1459; [2010] 2 S.C.R. 785

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Health & Drug; Practice & Procedure

Summary: (A) Criminal - Health & Drugs - Narcotics Drugs & Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985, s. 20 - Possession of contraband articles - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant allegedly possessed the contraband articles in his bag - Trial Court convicted the appellant u/s. 20 of the Act - Appellant challenged the Trial Court order before HC - HC dismissed appeal - Whether prosecution has complied with the requirements of s. 50 of the Act whilst in search and seizure - Held, question of compliance or non-compliance of s. 50 of the Act is relevant only where search of a person is involved and the said Section is not applicable nor attracted where no search of a person is involved - Search and recovery from a bag, brief case, container, etc., does not come within the ambit of s. 50 of the Act - Bag carried by the appellant on his shoulder was searched in front of the gazetted officer and the contraband article found in that bag was seized - Therefore, the search conducted by the investigation officer and the evidence collected thereby, is not illegal and hence compliance of s. 50 is not required in the present case- Appeal dismissed.

(B) Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Seizure - Absence of independent witness - Validity - Held, testimony of the prosecution witnesses revealed that efforts were made by the investigating party to include independent witness at the time of recovery, but none was willing - It may not be possible to find independent witness at all places, at all times and obligation to take public witnesses is not absolute - If after making efforts which the court considered in the circumstances of the case reasonable, the police officer is not able to get public witnesses to associate with the raid or arrest of the culprit, the arrest and the recovery made would not be necessarily vitiated - Therefore, it would be travesty of justice, if the appellant is acquitted merely because no independent witness has been produced - Appeal dismissed.

(C) Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Principle of parity in sentence - Held, where the case of the accused is similar in all respects as that of the co-accused then the benefit extended to one accused should be extended to the co-accused - However, for applying the principle of parity both the accused must be involved in same crime and must be convicted in single trial, and consequently, a co-accused is one who is awarded punishment along with the other accused in the same proceedings - In the instant case, record showed that the co-accused was convicted vide a separate trial arising out of a separately registered F.I.R. - Merely because the co-accused happened to be searched on the same date before the same gazetted officer, he cannot be said to be a co-accused - Further, the sentence of co-accused was altered by the HC vide a separate judgment arising out of a separate appeal - Therefore, appellant is not entitled the benefit of parity of sentence to the co-accused - Appeal dismissed.
