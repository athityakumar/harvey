Association Of Leasing &Financial Service Companies v Union Of India And Others

Supreme Court of India

26 October 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan, Swatanter Kumar

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 911; (2011) 2 SCC 352; 2010(6) ALL MR 953; 2010 (4) CompLJ 520; JT 2010 (12) SC 49; 2010(11) SCALE 461; 2010 (20) S.T.R. 417; [2010] 35 VST 549

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Parliament & Legislature; Service Tax

Keywords: Madras General Sales Tax (Amendment) Act, 1947

Summary: Service Tax - Constitution - Parliament & Legislature - Finance Act, 1994 - Finance Act, 2001 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 248, 366 - 2001 Act (amended 1994 Act) sought to levy service tax on 'banking and other financial services' - Appellant/Association of leasing and financial companies challenged the levy of service tax imposed by s. 65(12)(a)(i) of 2001 Act before the HC - However, in the meantime, respondent/Union of India issued a Notification ST dated 1-3-2006 exempting 90% of the amount repayable under hire- purchase/equipment leasing agreement(s) from service tax on the ground that the said 90% represented interest income earned by the service provider - Appellant contended that the concept of 'service tax' was first introduced by the 1994 Act, however, no service tax was levied by the said Act or by its subsequent amendments till 2001 - Consequently, service tax was imposed on 'banking and other financial services' in 2001 Act and vide s. 137(a) of the said Act, s. 65 of the 1994 Act was replaced by a new s. 65 which defined 'banking and other financial services' vide cl. (10) - By virtue of the said definition u/s. 65(10)(i) of 2001 Act, Parliament sought to bring within the service tax net, transactions in the nature of financial leasing, equipment leasing and hire-purchase - Being aggrieved by the inclusion of hire-purchase and leasing services within the service tax net, the appellant challenged the amendment of 2001 as ultra vires the legislative competence of the Parliament u/art. 366 (29A) of the Constitution - HC, subsequently, dismissed appellant's writ petition - Hence the present appeal - Whether the State Legislature has the exclusive competence to levy tax on 'financial leasing services' under Entry 54, List II - Held, impugned levy relates to or is with respect to the particular topic of 'banking and other financial services' which includes several financial leasing services includes long time financing by banks and other financial institutions (including NBFCs) - Said services are rendered to appellant's customers which comes within the meaning of the expression 'taxable services' as defined in s. 65(105)(zm) of 2001 Act - Taxable event under the impugned law is the rendition of service and not that on material or sale - Therefore, tax is on activity/service rendered by the service provider to its customer - Equipment Leasing/Hire-Purchase finance are long term financing activities undertaken as their business by NBFCs - As far as the taxable value in case of financial leasing including equipment leasing and hire-purchase is concerned, the amount received as principal is not the consideration for services rendered - Such amount is credited to the capital account of the lessor/hire-purchase service provider and the interest/finance charge thereof which is treated as income or revenue and which is credited to the revenue account - Such interest or finance charges together with the lease management fee/processing fee/documentation charges are treated as considerations for the services rendered and accordingly they constitute the value of taxable services on which service tax is made payable - Further, Government has given exemption from payment of service tax to financial leasing services including equipment leasing and hire-purchase on that portion of taxable value comprising of 90% of the amount representing as interest, i.e., the difference between the instalment paid towards repayment of the lease amount and the principal amount in such instalments paid (Notification No. 4/2006 - Service Tax dated 1-3-2006) - Merely because for valuation purposes inter alia 'finance/ interest charges' are taken into account and merely because service tax is imposed on financial services with reference to 'hiring/ interest' charges, the impugned tax does not cease to be service tax and nor does it become tax on hire-purchase/leasing transactions u/art. 366(29A) r/w Entry 54, List II - Thus, while State Legislature is competent to impose tax on 'sale' by legislation relatable to Entry 54 of List II of Seventh Schedule, tax on the aspect of the 'services', however, vendor not being relatable to any entry in the State List, would be within the legislative competence of the Parliament u/art. 248 read with Entry 97 of List I of Seventh Schedule to the Constitution - Further, appellants moved the HC without exhausting the statutory remedy, therefore, direction issued to the competent authority under the 1994 Act to decide the matter in accordance with the law laid down in the present judgement - Appeals dismissed.
