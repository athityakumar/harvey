Sadaram Suryanarayana and another v Kalla Surya Kantham and another

Supreme Court of India

22 October 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 887; (2010) 13 SCC 147; AIR 2011 SC 294; 2011 (3) ALD(SC) 25; 2011 (84) ALR 705; 2011 (1) AWC 553; JT 2010 (11) SC 308; 2010 (4) RCR(Civil) 876; 2011 (112) RD 382; 2010(11) SCALE 247; [2010] 12 S.C.R. 808

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Family & Personal - Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Conditional Will - Validity - Testator executed a Will in favour of legatee (Testatrix) with a condition that female off-springs of the legatee would devolve upon the suit property after the demise of testatrix - 1st plaintiff/daughter of testatrix, subsequently, inherited the suit property by virtue of the said Will after the demise of testatrix, however, defendant/sons of testatrix took possession of the suit property - Therefore, plaintiffs filed a suit for declaration of title and for recovery of possession - Defendants contended that testatrix had bequeathed the suit property to the defendants by virtue of a subsequent Will - Trial Court dismissed plaintiffs' suit holding that testatrix inherited the suit property by virtue of the earlier Will as absolute owner, therefore, testatrix's subsequent Will in favour of defendants was valid - Plaintiff filed appeal before the HC - HC allowed the appeal and reversed the Trial Court order - Hence the present appeal by defendants - Whether defendants entitled to inherit the suit property by virtue of a subsequent Will of testatrix - Held, first part of cl. 6 of the earlier Will makes an unequivocal and absolute bequest in favour of daughters of testatrix, however, second part of cl. 6 indicated that devolution upon their female off springs all such property as remained available in the hands of the legatees at the time of their demise - Therefore, there would be no devolution of any such property upon the female off springs in terms of the said clause if the legatee decided to sell or gift the property bequeathed to her as indeed she had every right to do under the terms of the bequest - Hence, there is no real conflict between the absolute bequest which the first part of cl. 6 of the Will makes and the second part of the said clause which deals with devolution of what and if at all anything that remains in the hands of the legatee - Two parts of cl. 6 operate in different spheres, namely, one vesting absolute title upon the legatees with rights to sell, gift, mortgage etc. and the other regulating devolution of what may escape such sale, gift or transfer by them - Latter part is redundant by reason of the fact that the same was repugnant to the clear intention of the testatrix in making an absolute bequest in favour of her daughters and it could be redundant also because the legatee exercised her rights of absolute ownership and sale thereby leaving nothing that could fall to the lot of the next generation females or otherwise - Therefore, stipulation made in the second part of cl. 6 did not in the least affect the legatee being the absolute owner of the suit property bequeathed to her - Corollary would be that upon the demise of legatee/appellant the estate owned by her would devolve by the ordinary law of succession on her heirs and not in terms of the earlier Will executed by the testator - Impugned HC order is set aside and Trial Court order is restored - Appeal allowed.
