State of Punjab and another v Dr. Viney Kumar Khullar and others

Supreme Court of India

22 October 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, H.L. Gokhale

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 889; (2010) 13 SCC 481; 2010 (5) ESC 685; JT 2010 (11) SC 575; 2010 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 3494; 2010(11) SCALE 219; 2011 (3) SLR 1

Case Digest

Subject: Education; Health & Drug

Summary: Education - Health & Drug - Admission - Prospectus notification dated 17.3.2008 (Prospectus) was issued regarding admission to post graduate degree/diploma in Medical/ Dental Courses for the year 2008 - It was provided that 60% seats shall be filled up from amongst the eligible PCMS/PCMS (Dental)/PDES in service doctors and 40% shall be open to all eligible medical/dental graduates - It also required that all PCMS/PCMS (Dental) PDES doctors who were selected for admission to Post Graduate courses under 60% quota shall have to produce a No Objection Certificate' (NOC) from the Director, Health and Family Welfare/Director, Research & Medical Education - Thereafter, Government Circular Memo dated 13.5.1996 was issued that contains the policy regarding issue of NOC - Subsequently, said circular dated 13.5.1996 was amended by Government Circular dated 30.7.2007 increasing the period of minimum service under the bond from 5 years to 10 years (and bond amount from Rs.2 lakhs to Rs.10 lakhs) for PG courses - Respondents applied for admission to the 2008-2011 post graduate courses and obtained and produced provisional NOCs, along with their applications - They were selected and admitted to different Post Graduate Courses in General Category in the 60% quota for in-service candidates - Later, when they sought NOCs which had to be produced, the department found that all of them were due to retire within 5 to 12 years and could not render the required minimum compulsory service for ten years after completing the three year Post Graduate Course and therefore, the NOC were not issued - Writ petition filed thereagainst was disposed of by HC with a direction to the appellants to verify the provisional NOCs issued to them with a further direction to permit them to continue their studies - Hence, present appeal - Whether HC could have disposed of the writ petitions by virtual non-speaking orders, without considering the various issues, only with a direction to verify the provisional NOCs and permitting the respondents to continue with their studies? - Held, Yes - A candidate should be made known about the requirements to be fulfilled by him and cannot be exposed to unknown liabilities or limitations - In the present case, Prospectus did not referred to the amendment circular dated 30.7.2007 - Further, amendment circular dated 30.7.2007 was issued after the 2007 admissions and was sought to be made applicable for the first time in respect of the 2008 admissions - Therefore, the candidates for 2008 admissions would not know about the said amendment circular dated 30.7.2007 unless it was mentioned in the Prospectus - Candidates would have bonafide proceeded on the basis that eligibility for the NOC was in terms of the government circular dated 13.5.1996 - Also the fact that provisional NOCs had been issued to them also would have led them to believe that prima facie they were eligible to get the NOCs - In these peculiar circumstances, admissions of two candidate should not be interfered - However, as regarding third candidate, who was ineligible to obtain the NOC even as per the circular dated 13.5.1996, he would not be entitled to the relief extended to the other two candidates - However, as he also completed more than three-fourth of a valuable post-graduate course, he should be given a chance to complete the course, but subject to conditions - Appeals partly allowed.
