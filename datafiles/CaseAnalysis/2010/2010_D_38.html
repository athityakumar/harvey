Naresh Kumar v Department Of Atomic Energy & Ors

Supreme Court of India

08 July 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Swatanter Kumar, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 539; (2010) 7 SCC 525; AIR 2010 SC (Supp) 62; 2010 (6) AWC 5559; 2010 (4) ESC 543; 2011 (128) FLR 64; JT 2010 (7) SC 77; 2010(6) SCALE 738; [2010] 8 S.C.R. 627; 2010 (5) SLR 571

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces; Service

Summary: Defence & Security Forces - Service - Central Civil Services (Pension) Rules, 1972, rr. 18 and 19 - Appellant served in the Air Force and after putting in 15 years of service, he joined Department of Atomic Energy (DAC) - Thereafter, he served at the Nuclear Power Corporation of India Limited (NPCIL) - Appellant exercised his option to receive benefits from the Air Force and did not opt for combined benefits of Civil and Military Pension - Thereafter, appellant made a representation that pension for combined service put in both under DAE and NPCIL be granted to him by change in option and that he was willing to refund the amount of pro-rata pension paid to him which was rejected - Appellant made a representation to the Additional Secretary of DAE for reconsideration of his grievance which was also rejected - Appellant file writ petition before HC for combined benefits of Civil and Military Pension - HC dismissed said petition on the ground of unexplained delay and laches - Hence, present appeal - Whether HC rightly rejected appellant's petition for combined benefits of Civil and Military Pension? - Held, appellant's representation was rejected on 18-02-1999 but still the appellant chose not to challenge the same and waited for considerable years - Circular dt. 11-04-2001 hardly had any bearing on the case of the appellant as it was applicable to the re-employee pensioners who opted for separate military and civil pension and whose cases were earlier decided were permitted to be reconsidered - Moreover, merely because the case of the appellant was forwarded by the Department vide its letter dt. 27-01-2007 for favourable consideration, would not vest any right in the petitioner and can hardly be of any material consequence - If an employee keeps making representation after representation which are consistently rejected then the appellant cannot claim any relief on that ground - HC rightly dismissed the appellant's writ petition on the ground of unexplained delay and laches - Appeal dismissed.
