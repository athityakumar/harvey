South Bengal State Transport Corporation v Ashok Kumar Ghosh and others

Supreme Court of India

06 May 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Chandramauli Kumar Prasad, R.V. Raveendran, R.M. Lodha

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 378; (2010) 11 SCC 71; 2010 (126) FLR 162; 2010 (3) JhrCR(SC) 9; JT 2010 (5) SC 350; 2010 (3) LLJ 10; 2010(5) SCALE 374; [2010] 6 S.C.R. 176; 2010 (4) SLR 87

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: South Bengal State Transport Corporation Service Regulations

Summary: Service - South Bengal State Transport Corporation Service Regulations, regns. 36, 38(2) - Misconduct - Relegation to the different cadre - Legality - 1st respondent/Conductor of the appellant/Corporation bus was charge-sheeted for misconduct - 1st respondent gave reply to the charge-sheet - Enquiry was conducted by the Disciplinary Authority and charges against the 1st respondents were found to be proved - Disciplinary Authority inflicted punishment and relegated the 1st respondent to the status of Daily Rated Conductor - 1st respondent challenged the order of punishment before HC - HC quashed the order of punishment on the ground of bias in the appointment of Enquiry Officer and non-compliance of the Regulations - Held, mere appointment of Enquiry Officer while framing the charge sheet, even before considering the reply of the delinquent employee, does not reflect any bias and hence proper - Failure to give opportunity of hearing before the imposition of penalty would not be bad due to non-compliance of the regn. 38(2) since regn. 38(2) does not contemplate any such opportunity to the delinquent employee - However, relegation of the 1st respondent to the status of Daily Rated Conductor cannot be said to be a reduction to a lower stage in the time scale of pay or reduction to a lower grade as 1st respondent has been deprived of the post of Conductor and such reduction to a lower stage has to be in the scale of pay of the Conductor itself - Therefore, punishment inflicted does not come within the ambit of reduction to a lower post or grade as contemplated u/regn. 36(5) of the Regulations - Reversion to a lower post or service does not permit reversion to a post outside the cadre that is from regular post to a daily wage post, therefore, punishment inflicted on the delinquent employee not being one of the punishments enumerated in regn. 36, which is not permissible in law - Reasoning of the HC for quashing the order of punishment on the ground of bias is not sustainable, hence set aside, however, punishment of lowering the grade was modified by substituting the punishment of reducing the lowest stage of the time scale applicable to the post of conductor with effect from the date of imposition of punishment - Appeal partly allowed.
