Oswal Fats And Oils Limited v Additional Commissioner (Administration), Bareilly Division, Bareilly And Others

Supreme Court of India

01 April 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 444; (2010) 4 SCC 728; 2010 (5) ADJ 317; 2010 (3) AWC 2394; JT 2010 (3) SC 510; 2010 (4) MLJ(SC) 567; 2010 (109) RD 696; 2010(3) SCALE 476; [2010] 5 S.C.R. 927

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Gujarat Agricultural Land Ceiling Act, 1960, U.P. Imposition of Ceiling on Land Holdings Act, 1960, Uttar Pradesh Cooperative Societies Act, 1965, Uttar Pradesh General Clauses Act, 1904, Gujarat Ceiling Act, 1961, Gujarat General Clauses Act

Summary: (A) Land & Property - U.P. Zamindari Abolition and Land Reforms Act, 1950, Sections 154, 167, 168A and 194 - Restriction on transfer by a bhumidhar - Appellant company submitted application to Joint Director of Industries for grant of permission u/s. 154(2) of the Act for purchase of land in excess of 12.5 acres - However without waiting for response of the concerned authority, the appellant purchased 40.45 acres of land- State of Uttar Pradesh challenged transfer of various parcels of land in favour of the appellant - Appellant virtually conceded that the land was purchased in contravention of the Act and stated that it may be allowed to retain 12.5 acres out of the disputed land - Collector declared that the purchase made by the appellant in excess of 12.50 acres is against the provisions of ss. 154/167 of the Act and that it will be entitled to retain only 12.50 acres and the remaining land measuring 27.95 acres would vest in the State Government - Revision filed thereagainst was dismissed - On appeal, Single Judge held that the purchase made by the appellant in excess of 12.50 acres was illegal per se and its case does not fall within the ambit of the exceptions carved out in sub-section (2) of s. 154 - Challenged - Whether the restriction contained in s. 154(1) is not attracted in a case involving transfer of land by Bhumidhar in favour of a company? - Held, s. 154(1) declares that no Bhumidhar shall be entitled to transfer any land other than tea gardens by sale or gift to any person if holding of the transferee would exceed 12.50 acres - An explanation was subsequently added to clarify that the word 'person' shall include and be deemed to have included a cooperative society - Primary object of s. 154(1) is to put a restriction/ceiling on the transfer of land by a Bhumidhar to any other person by sale or gift - Though sub-sections (2) and (3) of s. 154 empowers the Government to dilute the rigor of the restriction contained in s. 154(1), if that section is read in conjunction with ss. 166 and 167 which provide for consequences of transfer made in contravention of the Act including s. 154(1), the word 'person' appearing in s. 154(1) cannot be construed in a manner which would defeat the object and purpose of legislation - Moreover, if the word 'person' used in Section 154(1) is interpreted keeping in view the object of legislation and by applying the rule of contextual interpretation, it becomes clear that the same would include human being and a body of individuals which may have juridical or non juridical status - Appeal dismissed.

(B) Practice & Procedure - U.P. Zamindari Abolition and Land Reforms Act, 1950, s. 154(1) - Uttar Pradesh General Clauses Act, 1904, s. 3 and 4(33) - Meaning of word 'person' in s. 154(1) - Held, since, the word 'person' has not been defined in the Act, reference can usefully be made to the definition of that word in the Uttar Pradesh General Clauses Act, 1904 as the provisions contained in the U.P. General Clauses Act are applicable to all Uttar Pradesh Acts - In view of the definition contained in s. 4(33) of the U.P. General Clauses Act, the word 'person' appearing in s. 154(1) would include any company or association or body of individuals, whether incorporated or not - Also, language of explanation added to s. 154(1) declared that the expression 'person' shall include a cooperative society - Appeal dismissed.
