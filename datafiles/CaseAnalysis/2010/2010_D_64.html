U. O. I. and others v Dineshan K. K.

Supreme Court of India

30 November 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

D.K. Jain, H.L. Dattu

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 1529

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Keywords: SERVICE, Affidavit

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Directions by SC - Compliance of - Application filed to seek direction to respondent authority to implement final judgment and order of SC dt. 4-1-2008 and order of HC dt. 10-2-2005 - Respondent officer filed affidavit stating that directions in the said judgment were duly implemented - A report in tabular form also annexed with affidavit in support of stand that all actions in terms of said judgment/order were taken - Applicant submitted that directions contained in said judgment were partially complied with and some further actions, which were duly enumerated in rejoinder affidavit, were required to be taken by respondent - Held, in light of affidavit filed by a Senior Officer, there is no reason why stand of respondents should not be believed and accepted - It would be proper and expedient to direct parties/their representatives to sit down together, discuss points at issue and try to iron out differences, if any, in their respective stands - Discussions would be held in congenial atmosphere to understand and appreciate each other's view point and a sincere effort should be made to resolve disputes to satisfaction of all concerned - Order accordingly.
