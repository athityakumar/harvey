Mansoor Ali v Special Land Acquisition Officer

Supreme Court of India

15 January 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, Surinder Singh Nijjar

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 178

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Administrative - Land Acquisition - Legality - Claimant in a land acquisition case was the appellant - Land was acquired in pursuance of preliminary notification dt. 23-4-1981 - Land Acquisition Officer, by his award dt. 11-3-1991, determined the compensation as Rs.7200/- per acre - Reference Court increased it to Rs.20,000/- per acre by its judgment and award dt. 19-3-2004 by adopting the method of capitalising the income from yield - HC, by its impugned judgment dt. 28-8-2008, further increased the compensation to Rs.24,000/- per acre, following the said method - Hence instant appeal - Appellant contended that there was a serious clerical and calculation error in calculating the compensation - Appellant pointed out that the reference Court had assumed Rs.117/- as the price of sugarcane per metric tonne instead of Rs.236 - It was pointed out that the sugar factory had given a certificate dt. 6-12-2002 which showed that the appellant had delivered 117 metric tonnes of sugarcane in the year 1981-1982 and the sugarcane price was Rs.236/- per metric tone - But the Trial Court, instead of taking Rs.236/- per MT as the price for the purpose of calculating the market value of capitalisation method, wrongly assumed Rs.117/- as the price per MT - That was not corrected even by the HC - It was contended that if the certificate issued by the sugar factory was taken note of with reference to the price disclosed therein, the compensation would completely be different -

Held, appellant was right in his submission that a wrong sugarcane price was made the basis for calculation - But HC found that if the correction, as pointed out by the appellant, was to be made then it had to be made also with reference to other entries in the certificate - If the yield was taken as 117 MT from the land measuring 7 acres 15 cents, then the yield would be less than 16 metric tonnes which was far less than 35 metric tonnes assumed by the reference Court - There was no evidence about the extent of land under sugarcane cultivation - Therefore, the entire matter would have to be re-assessed with reference to any further evidence that might be led in that behalf - Appeal allowed.
