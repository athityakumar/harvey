 Union of India v Ramesh Ram and others

Supreme Court of India

07 May 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

K.G. Balakrishnan, S.H. Kapadia, R.V. Raveendran, B. SUDARSHAN REDDY, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 353; (2010) 7 SCC 234; AIR 2010 SC 2691; 2010 (6) AWC 6348; JT 2010 (5) SC 212; 2010 (5) MLJ(SC) 1067; 2010(5) SCALE 193; [2010] 6 S.C.R. 698; 2010 (5) SLR 452

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service

Keywords: Migration, Civil Services Examination Rules, 2005

Summary: (A) Constitution - Service - Civil Service Examination Rules, 2005, r. 16(2) to 16(5) - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 16(4) and 335 - Constitutional validity of sub-rules (2) to (5) of r. 16 of the Rules - Whether candidates belonging to reserved category, who get recommended against general/unreserved vacancies on account of their merit (without the benefit of any relaxation/concession), can opt for a higher choice of service earmarked for Reserved Category and thereby migrate to reservation category? - R. 16(2) of the Rules enabled the meritorious candidate of any of the reservation categories to get a service of higher preference so that he may not be placed at a disadvantaged position vis a vis other candidates of his category - Held, Meritorious Reserved Category (MRC) candidates who avail the benefit of r. 16(2) and are eventually adjusted in the Reserved Category should be counted as part of the reserved pool for the purpose of computing the aggregate reservation quotas - Seats vacated by MRC candidates in the general pool will therefore be offered to General Category candidates - This is the only viable solution since allotting these General Category seats (vacated by MRC candidates) to relatively lower ranked Reserved Category candidates would result in aggregate reservations exceeding 50% of the total number of available seats - Hence, reserved category candidates "belonging to OBC, SC/ ST categories" who are selected on merit and placed in the list of General/Unreserved category candidates can choose to migrate to the respective reserved category at the time of allocation of services - Such migration as envisaged by r. 16(2) is not inconsistent with r. 16(1) or arts. 14, 16(4) and 335 of the Constitution - Appeals allowed.

(B) Constitution - Service - Civil Service Examination Rules 2005, r. 16(1) to 16(5) - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 16(4) and 335 - Vacancies were notified by the participating services for the Civil Service Examination, 2005 - Consolidated Reserve list (wait-list) was prepared - Certain OBC candidates in the Reserve (wait list) filed applications before the Central Administrative Tribunal challenging r. 16(2) - It was contended that adjustment of OBC merit candidates against OBC reservation vacancies was illegal - Tribunal, concluded that meritorious OBC candidates who were selected on merit must be adjusted against the 'General Category' - However, it ordered that r. 16(2) may be applied in terms of decision of SC in Anurag Patel vs. U.P. Public Service Commission & Ors., 2004 INDLAW SC 953 to ensure that allocation of service is in accordance with rank-cum-preference with priority given to meritorious candidates for service allocation - On appeal, HC held r. 16(2) as unconstitutional - HC set aside the select lists and directed the Government of India and UPSC to redo service allocation de hors r. 16(2) - Present appeal - (A) Whether rr. 16(2),(3),(4) and (5) of the Rules ultra vires being inconsistent with r. 16(1) of the Rules, as violative of arts. 14, 16(4) and 335 of Constitution? - Held, art. 16(4) of the Constitution empowers the State to initiate measures in order to protect and promote the interests of backward classes (OBC, SC & ST) - R. 16 of the Rules protects the interests of a Reserved Category candidate selected in the general (unreserved) category by giving him the option either to retain his position in the open merit category or to be considered for a vacancy in the Reserved Category, if it is more advantageous to him/her - R.16 in essence and spirit protects the pledge outlined in the Preamble of the Constitution which conceives of equality of status and opportunity - By the operation of r. 16 (2), the reserved status of an MRC candidate is protected so that his/her better performance does not deny such candidate the chance to be allotted to a more preferred service - Further, proviso to r. 16(1) and r. 16 (2) operate in different dimensions and it was untenable to argue that these provisions are contradictory or inconsistent with each other - Impugned measures in no way offend the equality clause since this particular clause was inserted to safeguard the concerns of certain classes and shield their legitimate claims in the domain of public employment - Validity of r. 16 of Rules upheld - (B) Whether the order of the Central Administrative Tribunal was valid to the extent that it relied on case of Anurag Patel (which in turn had referred to the judgment in Ritesh R. Sah v. Dr. Y.L.Yamul and Others, 1996 INDLAW SC 560 which dealt with reservations for the purpose of admission to post graduate medical courses) - Held, order of the CAT is valid to the extent that it relied on the ratio propounded by SC in Anurag Patel - Even though that decision had in turn relied on the verdict of this Court in Ritesh R. Sah, the latter case is distinguishable from the present case with respect to the facts in issue - However, conclusions arrived at in the Central Administrative Tribunal order cannot approve as it failed to take note of the unique characteristics of the UPSC examinations - Appeals allowed.
