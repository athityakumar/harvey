Commissioner Of Central Excise,Ahmedabad v Solid And Correct Engineering Works And Others

Supreme Court of India

08 April 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 259; (2010) 5 SCC 122; 2010 (3) G.L.R. 2108; JT 2010 (3) SC 575; 2010(3) SCALE 598

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - Indirect Tax - Central Excise Act, 1944, ss. 2(d) and 5(A) - Central Excise Rules, 1944, rr. 9(1) and 173F - Demand - Penalty - Excisable goods - Exemption under notification no.1//93-CE, dt. 28-02-1993 - Respondents are partnership concerns engaged in the manufacture of parts and components for road and civil construction machinery and equipments like Asphalt Drum/Hot Mix Plants and Asphalt Paver Machine etc - Department issued show cause notice to respondents allegedly on the grounds of having wrongly declared and classified parts and components being manufactured by them as complete plants/systems, even when they were merely parts and components and not machines or plants functional by themselves - Show cause notice also alleged that the units manufacturing parts and components of the plants had availed benefit of exemption wrongly and in breach of the provisions of rr 9(1) and 173F and other rules regulating the grant of such benefit - Commissioner confirmed the duty demanded in the show cause notice and levied suitable penalties upon each one of the units (respondents) - Tribunal partly allowed the appeal filed against said order however, deleted the demand of duty as also the penalty in toto in rectification order - Hence, present appeal - (A) Whether setting up of an Asphalt Drum Mix Plant by using duty paid components tantamounts to manufacture of excisable goods within the meaning of s. 2(d) of the Central Excise Act, 1944? - Held, in present case assessee submitted that machine is fixed by nuts and bolts to a foundation not because the intention was to permanently attach it to the earth but because a foundation was necessary to provide a wobble free operation to the machine - An attachment of this kind without the necessary intent of making the same permanent cannot constitute permanent fixing, embedding or attachment in the sense that would make the machine a part and parcel of the earth permanently - Hence, impugned plants were not immovable property so as to be immune from the levy of excise duty - (B) Whether respondents engaged in the manufacture of parts and components used for setting up of Asphalt Drum/Hot Mix Plant were entitled to the benefit of Notification No.1/93-CE, dt. 28-02-1993 issued under sub-section (1) of s. 5A of the Central Excise Act, 1944 as amended from time to time? - Held, since the Tribunal's view on the question of exemption was unsustainable the order passed by the Tribunal has to be set aside and the matter remitted back for a fresh disposal qua the said units by reference to the other contentions urged on behalf of the units which the Tribunal has not examined - Revenue's appeals allowed.
