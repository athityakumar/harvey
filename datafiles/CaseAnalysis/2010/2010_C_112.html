Commissioner of Customs (Gen), Mumbai v Abdulla Koyloth

Supreme Court of India

29 October 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

D.K. Jain, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 916; (2010) 13 SCC 473; 2010 (259) E.L.T. 481; JT 2010 (12) SC 267; 2011 (1) RCR(Civil) 142; 2010(11) SCALE 495

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Customs Valuation (Determination Of Price Of Imported Goods) Rules, 1988

Summary: Customs - Indirect Tax - Customs Act, 1962, ss. 14, 111 and 112(a) - Customs Valuation (Determination of Price of Imported Goods) Rules, 1988, rr. 3(i), 4, 6A and 7(1) - Assessable value of the imported goods - Respondent imported a consignment of assorted consumer goods and filed bill of entry for the same - On an examination of the bill of entry, mis-declaration was found - Commissioner rejected the value declared by the respondent for the purpose of s. 14 of 1962 Act and held that the assessable value of the goods had to be determined u/rr. 6A and 7 of 1988 Rules - Commissioner also ordered the confiscation of the said goods u/s. 111(d) and (m) of 1962 Act with the option of redemption on payment of fine - The Commissioner also imposed a penalty of Rs. 10 lakhs on the respondent u/s. 112(a) of the 1962 Act - Tribunal allowed the appeal filed against said order, however, deleted the penalty levied on the respondent on the ground that since the value enhancement had not been upheld by it, there was no cause for imposition of penalty - Hence, present appeal - Whether order of the tribunal could be upheld? - Held, both s. 14(1) of the 1962 Act and r. 4 of the 1988 Rules provide that the price paid by an importer to the vendor in the ordinary course of commerce shall be taken to be the transaction value in the absence of any of the special circumstances indicated in s. 14(1) of the 1962 Act and particularized in r. 4(2) of the 1988 Rules - Therefore, the Customs authorities are bound by the declaration of the importer unless on the basis of some contemporaneous evidence the Revenue is able to demonstrate that the invoice does not reflect the correct value - In the present case, the Tribunal has failed to apply the procedure envisaged in s. 14(1) of the 1962 Act r/w. 1988 Rules as the information collected by the revenue from the market, veracity whereof was not questioned by the respondent, has also not been examined by the Tribunal - The Tribunal has also overlooked the statement made by the respondent, whereby he admitted that there was difference between the items declared, and the items actually seized by the Customs authorities, and that the value arrived at aftermarket enquiries was acceptable to him - Hence, tribunal needs to re-examine the entire matter afresh, particularly in relation to the manner of valuation, redemption fine and penalty - Matter remanded back to the tribunal for fresh consideration - Appeal allowed.
