P.K. Mohan Ram v B.N. Ananthachary And Others

Supreme Court of India

15 March 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 302; (2010) 4 SCC 161; AIR 2010 SC 1725; 2010 (4) ALD(SC) 40; 2010(3) ALL MR 930; 2010 (80) ALR 279; 2010 (3) AWC 2200; 2010 (1) CLT(SC) 451; 2010 (5) CTC 113; JT 2010 (3) SC 122; 2010 (4) MLJ(SC) 345; 2010 (2) RCR(Civil) 446; 2010 (110) RD 415; 2010(3) SCALE 7; [2010] 3 S.C.R. 401

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, ss. 19 and 21 - Nature of document executed - Determination of - 'K' owner of the suit property executed Settlement Deed in favour of the appellant, respondent nos.1 and 2 and 13 others and declared that from the date of execution he and the beneficiaries shall enjoy the land and house etc. without creating any encumbrance or making any alienation whatsoever - Settlor recorded that he shall have no right whatsoever to cancel the 'Settlement Deed' for any reason whatsoever or alter the terms thereof - After the death of settler, appellant filed a suit for partition of his share in the suit property and for grant of a declaration that in view of the negative covenant contained in the 'Settlement Deed', the settlor had no right to execute Revocation Deed or Will - Trial Court held that document was 'Settlement Deed' and not 'Will' and that the settlor did not have the right to execute 'Revocation Deed' - Appeal filed thereagainst was dismissed by lower Appellate Court - However, second appeal was allowed, it was held that even though it was titled and described as 'Settlement Deed', it was a 'Will' and that appellant has no right in the suit property because the settlor had bequeathed the same in favour of respondent Nos.1 and 2 - Hence, present appeal - Whether document was a Settlement Deed or a Will? - Held, the consistent view of SC and various HCs while interpreting an instrument to find out whether it is of a testamentary character, which will take effect after the life time of the executant or it is an instrument creating a vested interest in praesenti in favour of a person, the Court has to very carefully examine the document as a whole, look into the substance thereof, the treatment of the subject by the settlor/executant, the intention appearing both by the expressed language employed in the instrument and by necessary implication and the prohibition, if any, contained against revocation thereof - In the present case, in the title itself the document was described as Settlement Deed - By executing that document, settlor expressed his intention, in no uncertain terms, to settle the property in favour of 16 persons who were his own relatives - This was an unequivocal creation of right in favour of 16 persons in praesenti - Though, the beneficiaries were to become absolute owners of their respective shares after the death of the settlor, the language of the document clearly shows that all of them were to enjoy the property along with settlor during his lifetime and after his death, each of the beneficiaries was to get a specified share - In the concluding portion, the settlor made it clear that he will have no right to cancel the Settlement Deed for any reason whatsoever or to alter the terms thereof - It was a 'Settlement Deed' - Impugned judgment of HC set aside - Judgment of Trial Court and the lower appellate Court restored - Appeal allowed.
