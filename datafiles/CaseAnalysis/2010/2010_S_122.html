Sameer Kumar Pal & Another v Sheikh Akbar & Others

Supreme Court of India

28 July 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Dalveer Bhandari, K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 808; (2010) 11 SCC 777; 2010 (82) ALR 530; 2011 (1) AWC 197; JT 2010 (9) SC 46; 2011 (1) MLJ(SC) 1037; 2010 (3) RCR(Civil) 914; 2010 (2) RCR(Rent) 213; 2010(7) SCALE 386; [2010] 9 S.C.R. 94

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land & Property - M.P. Accommodation Control Act, 1961, ss. 12(1)(c), 12(1)(f) and 12(1)(g) - Eviction - Appellants filed a suit for eviction against the respondents u/s. 12(1)(c), 12(1)(f) and 12(1)(g) of the M.P. Accommodation Control Act, 1961 - Trial court held that appellants were in bona fide need of the impugned property and decreed the suit in favour of the appellants - On appeal, First Appellate Court upheld the findings of the trial court and concluded that the respondents have failed to prove that the appellants are in possession of any other non-residential accommodation in the entire city - However, HC set aside the concurrent findings of fact without any basis, justification or cogent grounds - Hence, present appeal - Whether order of the HC could be upheld? - Held, HC in the impugned judgment, without any pleadings or basis, held that the impugned property is a joint family property - HC erroneously observed that the impugned property was purchased by the father of the appellants and his brothers, whereas in fact the property was purchased by the appellants vide sale deed dt. 31.12.1991 - The assumption of wrong fact has led to total erroneous finding and conclusion - Neither there was any pleading nor it was the case of the respondents either before the trial court or the first appellate court - HC gravely erred in arriving at the finding without any basis whatsoever - Impugned judgment of HC set aside and order of trial court as affirmed by the first appellate court restored - Appeal allowed.
