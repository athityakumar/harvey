Abu Thakir And Others v State Rep. By Inspector Of Police, Tamil Nadu

Supreme Court of India

19 April 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

B. SUDARSHAN REDDY, Surinder Singh Nijjar

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 289; (2010) 5 SCC 91; AIR 2010 SC 2119; 2010 CRLJ 2840; 2010 (3) JhrCR(SC) 39; JT 2010 (4) SC 86; 2010 (3) RCR(Criminal) 237; 2010(4) SCALE 131; [2010] 4 S.C.R. 794; 2010 (2) UC 766

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120B, 147, 148, 149, 302 and 341 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 161 - Murder - Conviction - Challenged - Appellants were convicted u/ss. 120B, 148, 341, 147, 302 r/w ss. 149 and 109 of IPC - Trial Court held that the prosecution proved its case beyond reasonable doubt against the appellants and held them guilty of having entered into a criminal conspiracy, unlawful assembly and committing murder of the deceased - On appeal, HC confirmed the conviction and sentenced appellants u/s. 302 of IPC while setting aside conviction and sentence for other offences - Present appeal - (A) Evidence of Eye witnesses (PWs 2 to 4) - Whether courts below committed any manifest error in relying on the evidence of eye witnesses, PWs 2, 3 and 4 to convict the appellants for the charge u/s. 302 of IPC? - Held, no reason or justification to disbelieve the evidence of PWs 2, 3 and 4 along with the evidence of PWs 8 and 20 and the medical evidence - HC also critically assessed and analyzed every nuance of the evidence and found a clear case against the appellants - Re-appreciation of the evidence by the appellate court did not result in any manifest injustice - Therefore, no reason to interfere with the concurrent finding of fact arrived at by courts below in order to convict the appellants for the offence punishable u/s. 302, IPC - (B) Delay in submitting the statements recorded u/s. 161, CrPC - Held, it is not possible to lay down that delay in despatch of the vital documents in each and every case defeats the prosecution's case - Question as to what is the effect of delay in sending the vital documents to the Court may have to be assessed and appreciated on the facts and circumstances of each case - In the present case, there was a clear explanation available on record for delay - (C) Motive - Held, in view of direct evidence of PWs 2 to 4, and 8 and 20, the motive part has no significance - Appeal dismissed.
