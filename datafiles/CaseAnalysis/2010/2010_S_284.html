Sharda Kailash Mittal v State Of M.P. And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

12 January 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, K.G. Balakrishnan

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 8; (2010) 2 SCC 319; AIR 2010 SC 3450; 2010 ILR(MP) 1248; JT 2010 (1) SC 591; 2010(2) M.P.H.T. 486; 2010 (3) MPLJ 131; 2010(1) SCALE 260; [2010] 1 S.C.R. 451; 2010 (2) SLR 9

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Madhya Pradesh Municipalities Act, 1961

Summary: Municipalities & Local Governments - Administrative - Madhya Pradesh Municipalities Act, 1961, s. 41-A and 51 - Scope of application of s. 41-A - Removal of President of Nagar Palika - Legality - Appellant was elected as President of Nagar Palika - Show cause notice was issued to appellant u/s. 41-A of Act - Charges levelled against appellant was that (i) she had caused monetary loss to Panchayat by publishing advertisements, (ii) appellant had struck off her signature from minutes, (iii) appellant had shown undue haste in appointing a person as Chief Municipal Officer - Chief Secretary found that appellant violated provisions of s. 51 of Act and appellant was removed from post of President of Nagar Palika - HC upheld order of removal - Hence, present appeal - Held, president under the Act is a democratically elected officer, and the removal of such an officer is an extreme step which must be resorted to only in grave and exceptional circumstances - Actions of appellant, even if proved, only amount to irregularities, and not grave forms of illegalities, which may allow State Government to invoke its extreme power u/s. 41-A of Act - No charge of causing financial loss to Nagar Palika could be established by State Government - Further, State Government failed to appreciate that decisions for publication of advertisements, calling for tenders and payment of salaries were made by entire council and appellant could not be singled out for those decisions taken by the Council - Order of State Government set aside - Appeal allowed.
