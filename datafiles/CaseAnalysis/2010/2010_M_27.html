K. K. Ramachandran Master v M. V. Sreyamakumar and others

Supreme Court of India

06 July 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 474; (2010) 7 SCC 428; JT 2010 (6) SC 480; 2010 (3) KLT 189; 2010 (7) MLJ(SC) 145; 2010 (3) RCR(Civil) 617; 2010(6) SCALE 325

Case Digest

Subject: Election; Parliament & Legislature

Keywords: Electorate, Connivance

Summary: Election - Parliament & Legislature - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 81, 82, 86, 117, 123(4), 123(5) and 123(6) - Conduct of Election Rules, 1961, rr. 86 and 90 - Corrupt practice - Appellant filed election petition before HC assailing the election of the respondent on the ground that the latter had committed corrupt practices within the meaning of ss. 123(4), 123(5) and 123(6) of the Act - Said petition was contested by the respondent on the ground that the election petition did not state either the material facts or give the necessary particulars so as to disclose a complete cause of action justifying a trial - Respondent also alleged that the petition was not properly verified and was, therefore, liable to be dismissed on that additional ground as well - HC dismissed said election petition holding that the averments were insufficient to disclose a complete cause of action or give rise to an issue that needed trial - Hence, present appeal assails the correctness of the order of HC - Whether HC rightly dismissed election petition of the appellant? - Held, averments made in the election petition sufficiently set out the material facts and gave sufficient particulars that would justify the grant of an opportunity to the appellant to prove his allegations - In as much as the HC found otherwise, it committed a mistake - If there was any deficiency in the particulars required to be furnished in terms of s. 83(b) of the Act the HC could and indeed ought to have directed the petitioner to disclose and provide the same with a view to preventing any miscarriage of justice on account of non-disclosure of the same - So long the material facts had been stated, which were stated in the present case, the absence of particulars, if any, could not justify dismissal of the petition by the HC - Moreover, if a pleading on a reasonable construction could sustain the action, the court should accept that construction and be slow in dismissing an election petition lest it frustrates an action only on technical grounds - Impugned order of HC set aside - Matter remanded back to HC for disposal of the election petition in accordance with the law - Appeal allowed.
