State of West Bengal and Another v West Bengal Minimum Wages Inspectors Association And Others

Supreme Court of India

15 March 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, Swatanter Kumar

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 168; (2010) 5 SCC 225; AIR 2010 SC (Supp) 758; 2010(3) ALL MR 432; 2010 (3) CalLT 69; 2010 (2) ESC 212; 2010 (125) FLR 156; 2010 (2) JhrCR(SC) 198; JT 2010 (3) SC 148; 2010(4) M.P.H.T. 200; 2010(3) SCALE 45; [2010] 3 S.C.R. 367; 2010 (2) UPLBEC 929

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: West Bengal Services Revision of Pay and Allowances Rules, 1981, WBS (ROPA) Rules, 1970

Summary: Service - West Bengal Services Revision of Pay and Allowances Rules, 1981 - Parity in pay scale - Respondents 3 to 295 were employed as ad-hoc Inspectors-AMW, in Pay Scale No.9 (300-600) - They were subsequently absorbed into regular service and appointed against permanent vacancies - However, Inspector Agricultural Minimum Wages (Inspectors-AMW) were not included in the West Bengal Subordinate Labour Services - In the Second Pay Commission Recommendations holders of the posts of Inspector (Cooperative Societies), Extension Officers (Panchayat) and KGO-JLRO (reference category posts) who were also in Pay Scale No.9 earlier were granted the higher Pay Scale but respondents were granted the same - Feeling aggrieved, the respondents filed a writ petition - Single Judge held that the HC in its writ jurisdiction cannot take upon itself the responsibility of giving higher scales of pay claimed - Appeal was filed thereagainst - During the pendency of the appeal, Pay Review Committee was appointed, who recommended that all the posts which were in the scale of pay of Rs.300-600 to be awarded the revised scale - State Government did not accepted the recommendations of the Pay Review Committee - Fourth Pay Commission revised the existing pay scales and the new Pay Scale No.9 was introduced - DB allowed the writ appeal, set aside the order of the Single Judge - Hence, present appeal - (A) Whether the respondents, holding the post of Inspector-AMW', were entitled to parity in pay scale, from April 1981, with those holding the reference category posts? - Held, granting parity in pay scale depends upon comparative job evaluation and equation of posts - Benefit of higher pay scale can only be claimed by establishing that holders of the subject post and holders of reference category posts, discharge duties and functions identical with, or similar to, each other and that the continuation of disparity is irrational and unjust - Therefore, the mere fact that at an earlier point of time, two posts were carrying the same pay scale does not mean that after the implementation of revision in pay scales, they should necessarily have the same revised pay scale - Comparison merely based on designation of posts is misconceived - In present case parity claimed was not on the basis of equal pay for equal work, but on the basis of previous equal pay - (B) Whether the respondents entitled to higher pay scale on the basis of the recommendations of the Pay Review Committee? - Held, said Committee did not take note of the fact that different posts having the same pay scale, may have different duties and functions and some may deserve a higher pay scale than the others - State Government rejected the recommendation of the said Committee, for valid and justifiable reasons - Impugned order of the DB of the HC set aside - Appeal allowed.
