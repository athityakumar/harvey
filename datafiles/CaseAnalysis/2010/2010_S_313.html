Dr. Sunil Kumar Sambhudayal Gupta & Ors v State Of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

11 November 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 963; (2010) 13 SCC 657; 2011 ALL MR (Cri) 288; 2011 CRLJ 705; 2010 (4) JCC 3040; 2011 (1) JhrCR(SC) 106; JT 2010 (12) SC 288; 2011 (1) MLJ(Crl) 672; 2011 (1) RCR(Criminal) 57; 2010(11) SCALE 696

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 306 and 498A - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 161 - PW.2, brother of the deceased, filed a complaint against the appellants i.e. the husband and parents-in-law of the deceased, alleging that they had been demanding dowry and had given ill treatment to the deceased - Charge sheet was filed against appellants u/s. 306 r/w s. 34 of IPC and s. 498A r/w s. 34 of IPC - Sessions Court held that the deceased had committed suicide, however, no role could be attributed to any of the appellants for the same, and prosecution failed to prove any of the charges beyond reasonable doubt against the appellants - Trial Court further held that the deceased was suffering from epilepsy, psychosis and depression - On appeal, HC reversed the judgment and order of the Trial Court - Present appeal - (A) Material Contradictions - Effect thereof - Held, while appreciating the evidence, the court has to take into consideration whether the contradictions/omissions had been of such magnitude that they may materially affect the trial - Minor contradictions, inconsistencies, embellishments or improvements on trivial matters without effecting the core of the prosecution case should not be made a ground to reject the evidence in its entirety - Courts have to label the category to which a discrepancy belongs - In the present case, there had been a lot of improvements and contradictions in statements of PW 10 - Such contradictions in his statements cannot be held to be mere explanations or elaborations of his version, but were tantamount to material contradictions or vital omissions - Also, statement of PW.2, the brother of the deceased was also full of contradictions and suffers from major improvements and contradictions of such a nature that they impair the whole of his evidence - Thus, no reliance can be placed on these depositions - (B) Appeal against acquittal - Held, while dealing with a judgment of acquittal, an appellate court must consider the entire evidence on record, so as to arrive at a finding as to whether the views of the Trial Court were perverse or otherwise unsustainable - Findings of fact recorded by a court can be held to be perverse if the findings have been arrived at by ignoring or excluding relevant material or by taking into consideration irrelevant/inadmissible material - In the present case, HC did not dislodge the reasons given by the Trial Court for acquittal - HC did not make any attempt to appreciate the evidence with accuracy and reversed the findings of the Trial Court which were based on the evidence on record and for which detailed reasons had been assigned - Further, conviction of the appellants cannot be sustained on the aforesaid counts based upon the inconsistent, embellished and improved statements of the witnesses, which materially contradict their respective statements recorded earlier - Judgment of HC set aside - Appeal allowed.
