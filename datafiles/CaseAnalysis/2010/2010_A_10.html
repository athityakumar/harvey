K. Naina Mohamed (Dead) Through L.Rs. v A.M. Vasudevan Chettiar (Dead) Through L.Rs. And Others

Supreme Court of India

07 July 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 527; (2010) 7 SCC 603; 2010 (6) ALD(SC) 35; 2010 (5) AWC 5127; JT 2010 (6) SC 546; 2011 (3) MLJ(SC) 379; 2010 (4) MPLJ 348; 2010(6) SCALE 449; [2010] 7 S.C.R. 927

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Land & Property

Keywords: Punjab Pre-Emption Act, 1913, Assignees, Bazaar, Rule Against Perpetuity, Bengal Tenancy Act, 1885, Oudh Laws Act, 1876, Perpetuities and Accumulations Act, 1964, Transfer of Land Act

Summary: Family & Personal - Land & Property - Validity and legality of the Will - Rule against perpetuity - Original owner executed registered Will in respect of her properties and created life interest in favour of her two sisters, with a stipulation that after their death their male heirs will acquire absolute right in 'A' and 'B' properties respectively subject to the rider that they shall not sell the property to strangers - However, 'X' and her son 'Y' executed registered sale deed in favour of the appellant in respect of the suit property - Respondent Nos.1 and 2 challenged the same and pleaded that in view of the restriction embodied in clause 11 of the Will, 'X' and 'Y' could not have sold the property to a stranger - They prayed that the sale deed be declared void - Trial Court negatived said challenge by observing that being a purchaser from one of the legatees, he does not have the locus to question legality of the Will - Trial Court further held that clause 11 is valid and binding on the legatees and it does not violate the rule against perpetuity - On appeal, appellate court agreed with the trial Court that the appellant before it was not entitled to challenge the Will but opined that the restriction contained in clause 11 of the Will was void and not binding on respondents - Single Judge rejected the appellants' plea that right of pre-emption was not available to respondent Nos.1 and 2 against respondents and restored the decree passed by the trial Court - Hence, present appeal - (A) Whether the second appeal filed by respondent Nos.1 and 2 stood abated due to their alleged failure to bring on record the legal representatives of respondent and her son, who died much before the disposal of the second appeal? - Held, no - Neither the factum of death of 'X' and her son 'Y' was brought to the notice of the Single Judge who decided the appeal nor any argument was made before him that the second appeal will be deemed to have abated on account of non impleadment of the legal representatives of the deceased - The reason for this appears to be that 'X' and 'Y', who had also signed the sale deed as one of the vendors did not challenge the judgment and decree of the trial Court and only the appellant had questioned the same by filing an appeal - 'Y' did not even contest the second appeal preferred by respondent Nos.1 and 2 - Before this Court, the issue of abatement has been raised but the memo of appeal is conspicuously silent whether such a plea was raised and argued before the HC - Therefore, appellant cannot be allowed to raise this plea for frustrating the right of respondent Nos.1 and 2 to question alienation of the suit property in violation of the restriction contained in clause 11 of the Will - (B) Whether the restriction enshrined in clause 11 of the Will executed by original owner can be declared as void on the ground that it violates the rule against perpetuity? - Held, no - Object of incorporating the restriction was to ensure that the property does not go out of the families of the two sisters - The male heirs of original owner's sisters did not question the conditional conferment upon them of title of the properties - Therefore, the appellant who purchased 'B' property in violation of the aforesaid condition cannot be heard to say that the restriction contained in clause 11 of the Will should be treated as void because it violates the rule against perpetuity - Appeal dismissed.
