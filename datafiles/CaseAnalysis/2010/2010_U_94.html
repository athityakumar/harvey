Anirudh Singh Katoch v Union of India and others

Supreme Court of India

05 May 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 341; (2010) 12 SCC 368; 2011 (85) ALR 493; 2011 (266) E.L.T. 321; 2010(5) SCALE 7

Case Digest

Subject: Customs

Keywords: Import Licence, Foreign Trade (Development And Regulation) Act, 1992, Baggage Rules, 1998, Baggage Rules, 1994, Arms (Amendment) Act, 1983, Baggage (Conditions of Exemption) Rules, 1975, Transfer of Residence Rules, Arms Rules, 1962, Baggage Rules, 1959, Baggage Rules

Summary: Customs - Exim Policy 1997-2002 - Import of firearms - Detention order - Validity - Appellant brought three fire arms from abroad when he transferred his residence to India - Customs authority detained two fire arms and released one - Appellant made application to Director General of Foreign Trade (DGFT) for importing the detained fire arms - DGFT dismissed appellant's application - Appellant challenged the detention order before HC - Single Judge dismissed appellant's writ petition - Appellant preferred Letters Patent Appeal - DB dismissed appeal - Whether appellant was entitled to bring three firearms into India for which he holds valid license on transfer of his residence - Held, Exim Policy 1997-2002 clearly showed that import or export of firearms is clearly prohibited under para 4.5 of the policy and therefore, the appellant is not entitled to bring firearms to the country on transfer of his residence - DB rightly considered the provisions contained in Customs Act, 1962, Baggage Rules, 1998 and Foreign Trade (Development and Regulation Act), 1992 and the Exim Policy and did not commit any error in holding that a person is not entitled by virtue of Arms Act, 1959 or the Rules framed thereunder to bring into India such licensed firearms, if any provision of law prohibits or restricts the bringing of such articles - Import of firearms is permissible only against an import licence issued by the DGFT to renowned shooters / rifle clubs for their own use on the recommendation of the Department of Youth Affairs and Sports and the appellant has been denied import licence because he is not covered by any of the said categories - No interference on the DB order - Appeal dismissed.
