Mohd. Laiquiddin and another v Kamala Devi Misra (Dead) by L.Rs. and another

Supreme Court of India

05 January 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Tarun Chatterjee, V.S. Sirpurkar

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 59; (2010) 2 SCC 407; 2010(2) ALL MR 490; 2010 (80) ALR 444; 2010 (1) AWC 707; 2010 (1) CLT(SC) 168; JT 2010 (1) SC 440; 2010 (2) MLJ(SC) 820; 2010 (1) RCR(Civil) 687; 2010(1) SCALE 227

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Trusts & Associations

Summary: Trusts & Association - Practice & Procedure - Indian Partnership Act, 1932, s. 4 - Mismanagement - Dissolution of firm - Original plaintiff executed a partnership deed with original defendant to start a cinema theatre on plaintiff's land for a consideration of a share of profit - Original defendant failed to furnish the statement of accounts and stopped to make payment of share of profit to the original plaintiff - Original plaintiff filed a suit for dissolution of the firm - Trial Court decreed for dissolution and for rendition of accounts - Original defendant challenged the Trial Court order before First Appellate Court and in the meantime, original plaintiff and defendant expired - Since the original parties to the suit expired, their legal representatives were substituted as respective plaintiffs and defendants - First Appellate Court dismissed the appeal - Defendants filed second appeal before the HC - HC modified the First Appellate Court order and held that the defendants were entitled to take the possession of all movable properties and value of those structures remained in the land which could not be removed due to its embedding - (A) Whether the HC was justified in permitting the defendants in raising a question of law for the first time in second appeal, which was not in the pleading before the Trial Court or the First Appellate Court - Held, although a question of law is raised on the basis of the pleadings and evidence on record which might not have been raised before the courts below, such question of law can be permitted for the first time before the HC - Further, plaintiffs were not aggrieved by the finding of the HC even assuming that the HC had formulated a new question of law, which was not raised before the courts below - (B) Whether the HC was justified in holding that there had been dissolution of the partnership firm on account of death of a partner - Held, when there are only two partners constituting the partnership firm, on the death of one of them, the firm is deemed to be dissolved despite the existence of a clause in partnership deed which says otherwise - Further, plaintiffs were not interested in continuing the firm and there is no legal obligation upon them to do so - Partnership is not a matter of heritable status but purely one of the contract u/s. 4 of the Act - Therefore, the Trial Court was justified in holding that the firm dissolved by virtue of death of one of the partners - (C) Whether the HC was justified in permitting the defendants to remove the movables from the disputed property, contrary to the deed of partnership - Held, there was no intention from either of the parties to treat the disputed properties as the properties of the firm - Directing the delivery of the entire property to the plaintiffs would cause prejudice to the rights of the defendants and would put them to loss - Since partnership was dissolved on the death of the original plaintiff, it would be reasonable to allow both the parties to take their respective properties - Therefore, plaintiffs are entitled to the exclusive possession of the land and the defendants are entitled to take away the movables from the property and recover the value of the buildings and structure embedded to the land - No infirmity in the reasoning given by the HC - Appeals dismissed.
