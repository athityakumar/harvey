Raju And Another v State Of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

10 February 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Swatanter Kumar

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 299; (2010) 3 SCC 235; AIR 2010 SC (Supp) 507; 2010 CRLJ 1924; JT 2010 (2) SC 376; 2010(2) SCALE 304; [2010] 2 S.C.R. 574

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34 and 302 - Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act, 2000 - Murder - Common intention - Appellants along with other accused person were convicted u/s. 302 r/w s. 34 of IPC - Hence, present appeal filed by appellant nos. 1 and 2 against conviction - Held, ultimate assault on deceased causing his death was the culmination of an incident which had occurred earlier during the marriage ceremony, where the ladies who were participating in the festivities, were teased by the deceased in an inebriated state - Resultant fall-out was the immediate response to the said incident with the intention of preserving the honour and dignity of the said women - It is on account of the said incident that subsequently the accused persons assaulted deceased - Role of appellants in the incident in chasing the victim and, thereafter, holding him, was more likely to teach him a lesson as was sought to be projected as his defense - In the absence of any common intention, the conviction of the appellants u/s. 302 with the aid of s. 34 of IPC cannot be sustained - Role attributed to them would, attract the provisions of s. 304 Part I IPC - Conviction of appellants u/s. 302 r/w s. 34 of IPC set aside and they are convicted instead u/s. 304 Part I r/w s. 34 of - Appeal disposed of.
