State Of Punjab v Lakhwinder Singh And Another

Supreme Court of India

05 April 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Mukundakam Sharma, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 256; (2010) 4 SCC 402; AIR 2010 SC 1557; 2010 CRLJ 2415; 2010 (3) JCC (Narcotics) 142; JT 2010 (3) SC 497; 2010 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1231; 2010 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1447; 2010 (3) MLJ(Crl) 348; 2010 (2) RCR(Criminal) 582; 2010(3) SCALE 554; 2010 (1) UC 680

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985, s. 15 - Possession of contraband - Conviction - Challenged - Trial Court convicted respondents u/s. 15 of the Act - On appeal, HC held that there was delay of about seven days in sending the samples of the case property to the Forensic Science Laboratory was fatal, inasmuch as in the intervening period tampering of the case property could have been easily done - HC acquitted the respondents - Hence, present appeal - (A) Delay in sending the samples - Held, seal was found intact at the time of the examination by the Chemical Examiner and this was recorded in his report - Case property was produced in Court and there was no evidence to show that the same was ever tampered with - Discrepancies referred to by the HC were minor discrepancies which do not in any manner affect the sub-stratum of the case and the offence alleged against the respondents - View taken by the HC was wrong and the findings recorded were perverse - (B) Conscious possession - Whether respondents could be said to have been in conscious possession of the contraband goods? - Held, evidence adduced prove and establish that the respondents were in conscious possession of the contraband goods - Judgment and order passed by the HC set aside and the order of the Trial Court restored - Appeal disposed of.
