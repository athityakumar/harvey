Ajanta Pharma Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax-9, Mumbai

Supreme Court of India

09 September 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 721; (2010) 9 SCC 455; [2010] 327 ITR 305; JT 2010 (9) SC 501; 2010(9) SCALE 236; [2010] 11 S.C.R. 404; [2010] 194 TAXMAN 358

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Finance Act, 2000, Finance Act, 1996, Exports, Book Profits, Finance Act, 1983

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 80 HHC and 115JB - A/y 2001-02 - Deduction u/s. 80HHC - Book Profits - Assessee-MAT company filed its return of income for a/y 2001-02 which was accompanied by statutory audit report claiming deduction u/s. 80HHC of the IT Act, 1961 - While computing the 'book profits' u/s. 115JB of the 1961 Act, assessee claimed reduction, under clause (iv) of Explanation to s. 115JB, of 100% export profits - Assessing Officer allowed only 80% of the export profits in terms of s. 80HHC(1B) of IT Act - On appeal, Commissioner held that 100% export profits earned by the assessee as computed u/s. 80HHC(3) was eligible for reduction - Order of Commissioner was upheld by the Tribunal - HC allowed appeal filed against said order - Hence, present appeal - Whether for determining the 'book profits' in terms of s. 115JB, the net profits have to be reduced by the amount of profits eligible for deduction u/s. 80HHC of IT Act or by the amount of deduction u/s. 80HHC of IT Act? - Section 115JB 'book profits' have to be reduced by deduction 'eligible' u/s. 80HHC of IT Act - Held, section 115JB is a self-contained Code and taxes deemed income - Section 115JB begins with a non-obstante clause and requires vide clause (iv) for the 'eligible' profits derived from exports to be excluded from the 'book profits' and s. 80HHC operates in a different sphere as it is concerned with the 'extent of deduction' - Thus, if an assessee earns Rs.100 crores then for the a/y 2001-02, the extent of deduction is 80% thereof and so on which means that the principle of proportionality is brought in to scale down the tax incentive in a phased manner - In s. 115JB, it has been clearly stated that the relief will be computed u/s. 80HHC(3)/(3A), subject to the conditions under sub-clauses (4) and (4A) of that Section - The conditions are only that the relief should be certified by the Chartered Accountant - Such condition is not a qualifying condition but it is a compliance condition - Therefore, one cannot rely upon the last sentence in clause (iv) of Explanation to s. 115JB (subject to the conditions specified in sub-clauses (4) and (4A) of that Section) to obliterate the difference between 'eligibility' and 'deductibility' of profits as contended on behalf of the Department - Hence, impugned judgment of HC set aside and order of the tribunal restored - Appeal allowed.
