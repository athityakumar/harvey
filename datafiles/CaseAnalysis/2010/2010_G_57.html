T.G. Ashok Kumar v Govindammal And Another

Supreme Court of India

08 December 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 1061; 2011 (4) ALD(SC) 43; 2011(1) ALL MR 462; 2011 (84) ALR 717; 2011 (3) AWC 2739; 2011 (4) CTC 585; JT 2010 (13) SC 390; 2011 (2) MLJ(SC) 317; 2012 (3) RCR(Civil) 282; 2011 (113) RD 345; 2010(13) SCALE 201

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 52 - Sale pendente lite - Legality - 1st respondent filed a suit against 2nd respondent for partitioning the suit property by metes and bounds - 2nd respondent, in the meantime, sold the suit property to appellant - Suit for partition was decreed and 3/4th share of suit property was allotted to 1st respondent - Appellant, subsequently, being a bona fide purchaser, filed a suit for declaration and permanent injunction against respondents - Trial Court dismissed the suit - Appellant's appeal before the First Appellate Court was dismissed - Appellant filed second appeal - HC dismissed second appeal holding that appellant was a pendente lite purchaser, attracting the doctrine of lis pendens u/s. 52 of the Act - Hence the present appeal - Whether the HC ought to have decreed the appellant's suit for declaration and consequential injunction at least in respect of the portion of the suit property which was allotted to the share of 2nd respondent in the earlier partition suit filed by the 1st respondent - Held, sale by the 2nd respondent in favour of the appellant did not in any way affect the right of the 1st respondent (plaintiff in the partition suit) or the decree made in her favour in the said partition suit - Sale by 2nd respondent in favour of the appellant though not void, did not bind the 1st respondent who was the plaintiff in the partition suit - Sale in favour of appellant was subject to the right declared or recognized in favour of the 1st respondent under the decree passed in the pending partition suit - Sale pendente lite would therefore be subject to the decree in the partition suit - In the final decree passed in the partition suit, the major portion of the suit property was allotted to the share of the 1st respondent and to that extent, the sale in favour of the appellant would be ineffective, however, in regard to the remaining portion of the suit property which stood allotted to the share of the 2nd respondent in the final decree in the partition suit, the sale by the 2nd respondent in favour of the appellant was effective, valid and binding on the 2nd respondent and to that extent, the appellant is entitled to a declaration of title and consequential injunction - Therefore, suit ought not to have been dismissed in entirety even if the sale by the 2nd respondent in favour of appellant was hit by the doctrine of lis pendens - 2nd respondent cannot avoid the sale made by her on the ground that she was held to be not the exclusive owner, in the pending partition suit - Impugned judgment of the HC is set aside to the extent of holding that the appellant is not entitled to any relief - Appeal partly allowed.
