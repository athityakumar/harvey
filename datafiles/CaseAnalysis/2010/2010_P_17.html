Paramjeet Singh @ Pamma v State Of Uttarakhand

Supreme Court of India

27 September 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 788; (2010) 10 SCC 439; AIR 2011 SC 200; 2010 ALL MR (Cri) 3624; 2011 CRLJ 663; JT 2010 (10) SC 260; 2010 (4) MLJ(Crl) 481; 2010 (4) RCR(Criminal) 548; 2011 (1) RLW 908; 2010(10) SCALE 258; [2010] 11 S.C.R. 1064; 2010 (3) UC 1691

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 307 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant allegedly shot the deceased persons to death - Trial Court convicted the appellant for murder - Appellant filed appeal before the HC - HC dismissed the appeal by confirming the Trial Court order- Whether conviction of appellant by the courts below is sustainable - Held, PW1 and PW2/injured eye-witnesses turned hostile, however, both of them were medically examined and their version in the FIR stands corroborated by the medical evidence - Statement of PW1 recorded by the Investigating Officer u/s. 161 CrPC was in consonance with his version made in the FIR - PW2/injured eye-witness also testified that appellant had committed the crime - PW1 and PW2 in their respective depositions had admitted their presence at the place of incident and admitted to suffering of injuries on their persons - PW1 and PW2 denied the role of appellant on the ground that Panchayat had pardoned the appellant, however, such unwarranted attitude on the part of the said eye-witnesses disentitles any benefit to the appellant, who had committed a heinous crime - Further, weapon used for the crime was recovered at the behest of appellant - Courts below considered that the appellant could not furnish any explanation for his absence for about six days after the incident - FIR in the case was lodged promptly, naming the appellant as the person who committed the offence - All the eye-witnesses, including the injured witnesses, attributed the commission of the offence only to the appellant in their statements u/s. 161 CrPC - Therefore, no cogent reasons available to interfere with the concurrent findings of fact by the courts below - Appeal dismissed.
