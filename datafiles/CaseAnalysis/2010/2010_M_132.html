Jaipur Development Authority v Mahesh Sharma & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

21 September 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Mukundakam Sharma, A.R. Dave

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 818; (2010) 9 SCC 782; JT 2010 (10) SC 397; 2010 (8) MLJ(SC) 763; 2011 (5) RCR(Civil) 141; 2010(10) SCALE 84; [2010] 11 S.C.R. 1002

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Jagirdar, Rajasthan Land Reforms and Resumption of Jagirs Act, 1952

Summary: Land & Property - Rajasthan Land Acquisition Act, 1953, s. 48 - Rajasthan Land Reforms and Resumption of Jagirs Act, 1952, s. 36 - Deacquisition of land - Land of the respondent (Property belonged to Idol) was acquired by the Government u/s. 4(1) of Rajasthan Land Acquisition Act, 1953 - Respondent submitted a statement of his claim for payment of compensation by treating his Maufi rental income of Rs. 1,000/- before the Jagir Commissioner who sanctioned the payment of a sum of Rs. 2250/- as interim compensation - Said compensation was received by the respondent - Subsequently, despite vesting of the land on the State Government by virtue of the provisions of Jagir Act and despite the payment of interim compensation under Jagir Act, an Award was passed by the Land Acquisition Officer determining a sum of Rs. 2,62,680/- as compensation in lieu of the acquisition of the land and also recommended allotment of developed plot of land measuring 2,500 sq. yds. to be given to the respondent - The Government of Rajasthan issued an order deacquiring the impugned land vide notification issued u/s. 48 of the Act, 1953 stating that the possession of the impugned land stood resumed on the day under the Jagir Act - Later, another Land Acquisition Officer moved an application for withdrawal of reference on the ground that the land already stood deacquired, consequent upon which the Civil Judge dismissed the reference as withdrawn - Respondent aggrieved by the aforesaid order, preferred an application before the Land Acquisition Officer for payment of compensation amount, which was rejected by the Land Acquisition Officer holding inter alia that since the land stood resumed under the Jagir Act there is no question of payment of compensation - Respondent preferred writ petition against said order - HC allowed said writ petition and quashed the notification issued by the State Government u/s. 48 of Act, 1953 with a direction to the State Government to deposit the compensation amount in the trial court and also to allot a plot of land of 2500 sq. Yds - DB dismissed appeal filed against said order - Hence, present appeal - Whether HC rightly quashed the notification issued by the State Government u/s. 48 of Act, 1953 which deacquiring the impugned land? - Held, no - Issuance of notifications u/ss. 4 and 6 of the Act as also the Award passed for acquisition of the land was a nullity and the subsequent action of the Government de-requisitioning land by issuance of notification u/s. 48 was just and proper as that was an action for rectification of the mistake - The subsequent land acquisition officer was justified in refusing to refer to the Reference Court in view of the fact that the land was already a Government land - The Land Acquisition Officer, who passed the award, committed an illegality by not only determining the compensation under the Land Acquisition Act but also directing for retention of the interim compensation paid under Jagir Act and also in directing for allotment of a developed plot of land admeasuring 2500 sq. Yds - Respondent have no claim or right to a fresh allotment of land, as directed by the Land Acquisition Officer - Appeals allowed.
