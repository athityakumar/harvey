Dr. Basavaiah and another v Dr. H. L. Ramesh and others

Supreme Court of India

29 July 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Dalveer Bhandari, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 565; (2010) 8 SCC 372; 2011 (1) ALD(SC) 42; 2010 (127) FLR 888; JT 2010 (7) SC 558; 2011 (2) KarLJ 579; 2010(7) SCALE 529; [2010] 9 S.C.R. 227; 2010 (5) SLR 105; 2010 (3) UPLBEC 2310

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Appointment of Readers by Expert Committee - Validity - Appellants were appointed as Readers in Sericulture in the University - Respondent challenged the appointments on the ground that the appellants were not well-qualified for the post as per the advertisement - Single Judge dismissed respondents' writ petition, however, DB allowed respondent's appeal and set aside appellants' appointment - Held, DB did not properly comprehend the qualifications for the appointment of the Reader given in the advertisement - Advertisement clearly indicated that the qualification for appointment as Reader was a Doctorate degree or equivalent published work and both the appellants had extensive published work in the national and international journals of repute to their credit - Further, experts had evaluated the qualification, experience and published work of the appellants and thereafter recommendations for their appointments were made to the University - DB ought not to have sit as an appellate court on the recommendations made by the country's leading experts in the field of Sericulture - If appellants were selected by the Commission and appointed by the Government, no fault can be found in the same - Therefore, impugned judgment of the DB is set aside and restored the Single Judge order - Direction issued to the University to give regular pay-scale to the appellant - Appeals allowed.
