Chaturbhuja Modi and others v State of Orissa and another

Supreme Court of India

11 August 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Mukundakam Sharma, A.R. Dave

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 611; (2010) 12 SCC 234; 2011 (1) ALD(SC) 13; 2010(5) ALL MR 989; 2010 (82) ALR 516; 2010 (6) AWC 6525; 2011 (1) CLT(SC) 11; 2010 (4) RCR(Civil) 167; 2010 (111) RD 531; 2010(8) SCALE 148; [2010] 9 S.C.R. 849

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4(1) and 18 - Land acquisition - Enhancement of compensation - Appellants land was acquired for construction of additional building, office, garage and staff quarters of Orissa State Financial Corporation - The Land Acquisition Officer assessed the market value of the land at the rate of Rs. 75,000/- per acre - Appellants sought for a reference to the Civil Judge who enhanced the compensation to Rs. 1,50,000/- per acre - Appellants being dissatisfied with the aforesaid determination of compensation, filed an appeal before the HC, claiming a higher compensation at the rate of Rs. 12, 50,000/- per acre - HC enhanced the compensation for the acquired land to Rs. 3,00,000/- per acre and also held that the appellants should be entitled to other statutory benefits as available under the Act - Appellants, still aggrieved filed present appeal for enhancement of compensation submitting that the acquired land is situated in the heart of the Cuttack City and close to the National Highway No. 5 - Whether compensation could be enhanced? - Held, no - Only evidence that could be considered and relied upon is a certified copy of the registered sale deed (Exhibit 1) - Although the land whose sale is evidenced in Exhibit 1 is not an excellent comparison in terms of area, the same indicates a sales transaction completed at around the same time as the acquisition of the said land - Moreover, Exhibit 1 also concerns a plot that is in geographical proximity to the acquired land - HC have taken notice of said registered deed and has given some discount in compensation as the land - After giving the said discount, the HC computed the compensation at the rate of Rs. 3,00,000/- per acre for the acquired land which seems to be just and proper - No merit in appeal - Appeal dismissed
