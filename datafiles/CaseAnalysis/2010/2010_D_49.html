Dharamveer And Others v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

09 March 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Chandramauli Kumar Prasad, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 159; (2010) 4 SCC 469; AIR 2010 SC 1378; 2010 ALL MR (Cri) 1286; 2010 CRLJ 2393; 2010 (3) JCC 1931; JT 2010 (2) SC 592; 2010 (2) RCR(Criminal) 280; 2010(2) SCALE 776; [2010] 3 S.C.R. 162

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 148, 149, 302 and 307 - Murder - Conviction - Challenged - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Trial Court convicted appellants u/ss. 148 and 302/149 and u/ss. 307/149, IPC - Conviction affirmed by HC - Hence, present appeal - (A) Scope of the power u/art. 136 of the Constitution, hearing appeal against the judgment of conviction and sentence - Held, power u/art. 136 of the Constitution is very wide - However, appreciation of evidence is resorted to, in exceptional circumstances when it comes to the conclusion that the finding of guilt recorded by the HC is perverse - Where the HC has analysed the evidence in great detail and found the evidence reliable there is no scope for interference by SC - (B) Delay in receipt of the Special Report by the Magistrate - Held, information in regard to the incident was given immediately after the occurrence and the First Information Report was lodged on the same day - No delay in lodging the First Information Report - Therefore, mere delay in receipt of the Special Report, in no way causes doubt to the case of the prosecution - (C) Evidence of eye witnesses - Appellant submitted that the entire prosecution case was dependent upon the evidence of PW.1 and PW.2 and they being inimical to the appellants, their evidence deserve to be rejected - Held, evidence of an eye witness cannot be rejected only on the ground that enmity exists between the parties - Further, evidence of the two eye-witnesses, with minor contradictions has withstood the test of cross-examination and therefore the case of the prosecution cannot be discarded on these grounds - No merit in the appeal - Appeal dismissed.
