Nirmal Jeet Singh Hoon v Irtiza Hussain and others

Supreme Court of India

26 October 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 923; 2011 (2) AWC 1592; 2010(11) SCALE 302

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Keywords: U.P. Urban Buildings (Regulation of Letting, Rent and Eviction) Act, 1972

Summary: Rent Control - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 106 - Eviction - Arrears of rent - Plaintiffs filed suit for ejectment and arrears of rent on the ground that defendants illegally sub-let the suit property to the petitioner and had also refused to pay the amount due as rent - Petitioner denied the plaintiffs' claims and submitted that no proper notice u/s. 106 of the 1882 Act had been issued to him and so he could not be evicted - Petitioner also questioned the jurisdiction of the court to entertain the suit - Trial Court decreed the suit against the defendants with cost for ejectment, arrears of rent, restoration of the items of furniture, crockery and cutlery and the defendants were further directed to pay pendent lite and future mesne profits till the date of actual delivery of possession of the suit property - It also held that the petitioner was not a sub-tenant in possession of the property and defendants were in exclusive possession of the premises - On appeal, HC upheld the decree of the Trial Court - Hence, present petitions - Whether order of the trial court as affirmed by the HC could be upheld? - Held, no illegality had been committed by the courts in refusing to frame an issue on the alleged fraud involving the transfer of property as it cannot adjudicate upon the issue of title - Moreover, as defendants have been the tenants of the original plaintiffs, the question of title could not be adjudicated at the behest of the petitioner under any circumstance - Impugned order of the trial court as affirmed by the HC upheld - Petitions dismissed.
