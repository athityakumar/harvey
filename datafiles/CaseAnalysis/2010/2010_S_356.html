Pal @ Palla v State Of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

22 September 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 783; (2010) 10 SCC 123; 2010 ALL MR (Cri) 3989; JT 2010 (10) SC 495; 2011 (2) MLJ(Crl) 487; 2011 (1) OLR 232; 2010 (4) RCR(Criminal) 511; 2010(10) SCALE 127; [2010] 11 S.C.R. 716; 2010 (3) UC 1708

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 147, 302 and 323 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 200, 202, 210 and 482 - Clubbing of cases - Appellant lodged a FIR in regard to offences u/ss. 147, 323 and 302 of IPC against five accused persons - Thereafter, appellant and 'G' filed writ petition before HC and prayed for the investigation to be entrusted to an independent agency - During pendency of writ petition, Investigating Officer submitted a charge-sheet against two persons who were not named by the appellant in FIR - Subsequently, another charge-sheet was filed by the Investigating Officer in which 'G' was also named as an accused - HC held that the investigation was improper, but, since charge-sheet had already been filed, the relief sought for by the appellant for investigation by a different agency had become infructuous - Thereafter, appellant filed a protest petition before the Judicial Magistrate and the same was treated as a complaint and statements were recorded by the Magistrate u/ss. 200 and 202 of CrPC - Magistrate issued summons to all the five accused who were named in the complaint and whose names also appeared in the FIR lodged by the appellant - HC upheld the said order but directed that both the cases, one on the basis of the charge-sheet filed by the police and the other on the basis of the complaint filed by the appellant, would run simultaneously - Magistrate clubbed the two proceedings together - Appellant filed petition u/s. 482 of CrPC for a direction that the trial of the two cases be held separately, which was dismissed - Hence, present appeal - Whether a common trial can be held in respect of two cases, one on the basis of the charge-sheet filed by the police and the other on the basis of a protest petition which was treated as a complaint having been committed to the Court of Sessions, although, none of the accused in the said two cases were common? - Held, s. 210 of CrPC contemplates a situation where having taken cognizance of an offence in respect of an accused in a complaint case, in a separate police investigation such a person is again made an accused, then the Magistrate may inquire into or try together the complaint case and the case arising out of the police report as if both the cases were instituted on a police report - However, in the present case, version in the complaint case and the police report were totally different, though, arising out of the same incident - Moreover, accused were different in the two separate proceedings and the situation was, in fact, arisen where prejudice in all possibility likely to be caused in a single trial where a person was both an accused and a witness in view of the two separate proceedings out of which the trial arises - This was a case where the two trials should be held simultaneously but not as a single trial - Facts of the case also warrant that the two trials should be conducted by the same Presiding Officer in order to avoid conflict of decisions - Order of the HC set aside - Trial Court shall proceed to hear the two cases simultaneously, but separately - Appeal disposed of.
