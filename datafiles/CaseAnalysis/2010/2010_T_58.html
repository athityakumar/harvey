M/S Nava Bharat Ferro Alloys Ltd v Transmission Corporation Of A.P. Ltd. And Anr.

Supreme Court of India

18 November 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 975; (2011) 1 SCC 216; 2011(1) ALL MR 420; 2011 (1) JhrCR(SC) 70; JT 2010 (12) SC 554; 2011 (3) RCR(Civil) 599; 2010(12) SCALE 161

Case Digest

Subject: Electricity & Energy

Summary: Electricity & Energy - Delay in payment of tariffs - Charge of interest - Legality - Appellant/Industry was receiving electric supply from respondent/Board at an agreed rate - Respondent/Board, subsequently, revised the rate - Appellant challenged the revision before the HC - HC stayed the revision through an interim order, however, main petition was dismissed ultimately - Appellant filed appeal before the SC - SC dismissed the appeal - Appellant made a representation to respondent for paying the dues in instalment, however, respondent, in the meantime, send a demand notice to appellant claiming the amount in dispute - Respondent, subsequently, invoked the bank guarantee of appellant and send a separate notice demanding additional charges and interest for delayed payments - Appellant challenged the demand of interest before the HC - HC dismissed appellant's writ petition - Hence the present appeal - Appellant contended that respondent cannot charge interest on delayed payment since there was a SC decision in Kerala State Electricity Board v. MRF Limited, 1995 INDLAW SC 1913 - Whether the decision relied on by the appellant was applicable to the instant case - Held, SC, in Kerala State Electricity Board case, upheld the claim for payment of interest primarily because of the stipulation contained in the tariffs/agreement executed between the Board and the consumer providing for payment of interest at that rate in the event of delay in the payment/discharge of the bills raised against the consumer - Further, the said decision of SC did not grant any relief to a defaulting consumer once the demand was upheld nor did it interfere with the principle of restitution which would entitle the successful party to be relegated back to the position it would hold had there been no judgment adverse to it - Kerala State Electricity Board's case does not lend any support to the appellant in its endeavour to avoid payment of the amount which became recoverable from it no sooner the judgment of the HC was reversed in the earlier round of litigation upholding the revision of the tariffs - Question of restitution of the benefit drawn by a party during legal proceedings that eventually fail as also on the general principle that a party who fails in the main proceedings cannot benefit from the interim order issued during the pendency of such proceedings, the SC found against the consumers and upheld the demand for payment of additional charges recoverable on account of the delay in the payment of the outstanding dues - Far from lending any assistance to the appellant the decision relied upon by them squarely goes against it and has been correctly appreciated and applied by the HC - Appeals dismissed.
