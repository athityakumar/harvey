Assistant Commissioner, Commercial Tax Department, Works Contract And Leasing, Kota v M/S Shukla And Brothers

Supreme Court of India

15 April 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Swatanter Kumar, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 276; (2010) 4 SCC 785; 2010 (254) E.L.T. 6; JT 2010 (4) SC 35; 2010 (4) KarLJ 256; 2010(3) SCALE 749; [2010] 4 S.C.R. 627; 2011 (22) S.T.R. 105; 2010 (2) WLN(SC) 45

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Sales Tax

Keywords: Recording Of Reasons

Summary: Sales Tax - Practice & Procedure - Rajasthan Sales Tax Act, 1994, ss. 37 and 86 - Recording of reason - Essential requirement - Respondent, contractor, obtained an impartible contract of constructing shops - Assessing authority opined that shutters and doors were not manufactured from tax paid raw material in impartible contract and as such shutter was excluded from labour charges in the above years, and levied tax, interest, penalty and surcharge upon the respondent - On appeal, Rajasthan Tax Board accepted the plea of the respondent that the shutters and doors were manufactured from tax paid raw material in a work contract, therefore, could not be the goods transferred for the purposes of levy of tax - Revision filed thereagainst before HC was dismissed - Hence, present appeal - It was submitted that the order passed by the HC does not record any reasons for dismissing the revision petition filed by the Department - Held, providing of reasons in orders is of essence in judicial proceedings - By practice adopted in all Courts and by virtue of judge made law, the concept of reasoned judgment has become an indispensable part of basic rule of law and is a mandatory requirement of the procedural law - Judgment of the HC must speak for itself to enable the higher Court to do complete and effective justice between the parties - However, there is hardly any statutory provision under the Income Tax Act or under the Constitution itself requiring recording of reasons in the judgments but it is no more res integra and stands unequivocally settled by different judgments of SC holding that, the courts and tribunals are required to pass reasoned judgments/orders - In present case, question of law, mixed questions of law and facts were not examined by the HC in detail, but, by one line order the revision petition was dismissed - This legal infirmity has, prejudicially affected the case of the appellant - Matter remitted to HC for de novo consideration - Matter remitted to HC.
