Charanjit Lamba v Commanding Officer, Southern Command and others

Supreme Court of India

06 July 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 458; (2010) 11 SCC 314; AIR 2010 SC 2462; 2010 (6) AWC 5898; 2010 (3) ESC 388; 2010 (126) FLR 989; 2010 (3) JCC 2232; JT 2010 (6) SC 595; 2010 (7) MLJ(SC) 367; 2010 (3) RCR(Criminal) 476; 2010(6) SCALE 319; [2010] 7 S.C.R. 820; 2010 (4) SLR 385; 2010 (2) UC 1182

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Defence & Security Forces; Service

Summary: Criminal - Defence & Security Forces - Service - Army Act, 1950, ss. 45 and 52(f) - Dismissal from service - Petitioner-Major in the Indian Army upon a finding recorded against him in a Court of Inquiry brought up for trial before a General Court Martial (GCM) on the charges that he improperly claimed Rs.16,589.30 on account of transfer of his household luggage and car to Chandigarh and also on non-payment of electricity bill - GCM held appellant guilty for improperly claiming Rs.16,589.30 on account of transfer of his household luggage and car to Chandigarh and sentenced him to forfeiture of ten years past service for purposes of pension - In so far as the second charge, viz. non-payment of electricity bill was concerned, the GCM declared the appellant not guilty - On appeal, General Officer Commanding, Maharashtra and Gujarat Area (GOC M & G Area) remanded the matter back to the GCM for re-consideration on the question of sentence to be awarded to the appellant on the first charge and whether the appellant could be held guilty on the second charge - GCM while sticking to the reasons given by it in regard to the first charge found the second charge also to have been proved - GCM on that basis revoked the earlier sentence and sentenced the appellant to dismissal from service - On appeal, HC held that the punishment of dismissal imposed upon the petitioner was in no way disproportionate to the gravity of the offence committed by him - Hence, present appeal - Whether punishment of dismissal imposed upon the petitioner is disproportionate to the gravity of the offence committed by him? - Held, appellant was holding the rank of a Major in the Indian Army at the time he committed the misconduct alleged and proved against him - As an officer of disciplined force like the Army he was expected to maintain the highest standard of honesty and conduct and forebear from doing anything that could be termed as unbecoming of anyone holding that rank and office - Making a false claim for payment of transport charges of household luggage and car to Chandigarh was a serious matter bordering on moral turpitude - Breach of the rule requiring him to clear his electricity dues upon his transfer from the place of his posting was also not credit worthy for an officer - Competent authority was therefore justified in taking the view that the nature of the misconduct proved against the appellant called for a suitable punishment - Inasmuch as the punishment chosen was dismissal from service, the competent authority, did not take an outrageously absurd view of the matter - As an officer holding the rank of Major has to lead by example not only in the matter of his readiness to make the supreme sacrifice required of him in war or internal strife but even in adherence to the principles of honesty, loyalty and commitment. An officer cannot inspire those under his command to maintain the values of rectitude and to remain committed to duty if he himself is found lacking in that quality - Any act on the part of an officer holding a commission in the Indian Army which is subversive of army discipline or high traditions of the Army renders such person unfit to stay in the service of the nation's Army especially when the misconduct has compromised the values of patriotism, honesty and selflessness which values are too precious to be sacrificed on the altar of petty monetary gains, obtained by dubious means - Order of HC upheld - Appeal dismissed.
