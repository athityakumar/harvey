Ramjas Foundation And Another v Union Of India And Others

Supreme Court of India

09 November 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 949; 2011 (1) ALD(SC) 103; 2010 (6) AWC 6511; 2010 (174) DLT 100; JT 2010 (12) SC 134; 2011 (2) MLJ(SC) 162; 2011 (1) RCR(Civil) 176; 2010(11) SCALE 598

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property; Trusts & Associations

Keywords: Muslim Wakfs Act, 1954

Summary: Land & Property - Trusts & Associations - Constitution - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4, 5A - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 15 - Land acquisition - Wakf property - Exclusion - Entitlement - Predecessor-in-title/a non-Muslim purchased the suit property for starting up a School - Predecessor-in-title formed a College Society and got the same registered under the Societies Registration Act, 1860 as a charitable institution - Predecessor-in-interest, subsequently, transferred suit property to the said Society through a release deed and in the meantime, he made a public announcement that he would create a Wakf and dedicate and donate all his movable and immovable properties to the said Wakf - Society was, later, changed to the 1st appellant/Foundation - 1st appellant/Foundation's land was acquired by the respondent/State for planned development of its territory - 1st appellant filed objection u/s. 5A of the Act, however, competent authority rejected objection - Respondent, subsequently, issued declaration u/s. 6 of the Act - 1st appellant challenged acquisition proceedings in various writ petitions - HC dismissed all the petitions - Appellant challenged the HC orders before the SC - SC directed the Lt. Governor of the State to consider appellant's representation for de-notification of the land - Lt. Governor, subsequently, considered appellant's representation and passed an order rejecting the prayer of de-notification - 1st appellant challenged the Lt. Governor's order before the Single Judge - 1st appellant contended that cl.(d) of notification issued u/s. 4 of the Act excluded the lands dedicated to Wakf, therefore, property in dispute also should be excluded from acquisition - Respondent contended that property in dispute was neither a Wakf property nor it can be treated as Wakf property because it had not been created by a Muslim - Single Judge dismissed 1st appellant's writ petition - DB also, subsequently, dismissed 1st appellant's appeal - Hence the present appeal - Whether the Wakf created by non-Muslims was excluded from cl. (d) of the notification issued u/s. 4 of the Act would be a violation of art. 14 and 15 of the Constitution - Held, plea of violation of arts. 14 and 15 of the Constitution for excluding Wakf created by non-Muslim from notification u/s. 4 was not pressed before the Single Judge and consequently, DB deemed that 1st appellant had abandoned that part of challenge to the acquisition proceedings - Predecessor-in-title allegedly made an announcement that he had created a Wakf and donated all his moveable and immoveable properties to the Society for charitable purposes but no evidence was produced before the Single Judge to prove the said fact - Evidence produced before the Single Judge showed that even after the so called dedication of land for charitable purposes, the same continued in the name of predecessor-in-title till when he executed the release deed in favour of the Society of which he himself was the founder trustee - Objects of the Society created by the predecessor-in-title did not make a mention of the Wakf allegedly created by predecessor-in-title - Deed of settlement of the Society was executed by the British Government wherein the institution was described as a public educational charity and not as a Wakf - Therefore, the concurrent finding recorded by the Single Judge and the DB that what was created by predecessor-in-title was a public charitable trust and not a Wakf and the property acquired was not a Wakf property does not call for interference - Appeal dismissed.
