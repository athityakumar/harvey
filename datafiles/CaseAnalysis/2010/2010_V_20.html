Mysore Urban Development Authority By Its Commissioner v Veer Kumar Jain And Others

Supreme Court of India

01 April 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, R.M. Lodha

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 273; (2010) 5 SCC 791; AIR 2010 SC 2153; 2010 (2) CLT(SC) 156; JT 2010 (4) SC 63; 2014 (1) KarLJ 142; 2010 (4) MLJ(SC) 775; 2010 (2) OLR 445; 2010 (2) RCR(Civil) 851; 2010(3) SCALE 655

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Karnataka General Clauses Act, 1899

Summary: Land & Property - Karnataka Urban Development Authorities Act, 1987 - Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - Withdrawal from acquisition u/s. 48(1) of the LA Act - Principles of natural justice - Violation of - Declaration u/s. 19(1) of the KUDA Act was issued by the state government that land proposed to be acquired by Mysore Urban Development Authority (MUDA) for the purposes of the scheme is required for a public purpose - Award was made and possession of the lands was taken - However, acting on the applications of some land owners, the state government issued a notification dated 15.9.2001 dropping the acquisition proceedings, in regard a portion of land - Immediately thereafter, the land owners sold the de-notified lands to first respondent - MUDA challenged the de-notification - It was submitted that the acquired lands could not be de-notified without hearing it - In view of it, the state government issued another notification dated 22.7.2002 withdrawing the notification dated 15.9.2001 - First respondent filed a writ petition challenging the notification dated 22.7.2002 on the ground that the owners of the lands were not heard before withdrawing the notification dated 15.9.2001 - Writ petition was allowed - Writ appeal filed thereagainst was dismissed - Hence, present appeal - Whether the notification dated 22.7.2002 valid; and what would be the appropriate relief on the facts and circumstances? - Held, power u/s. 48(1) of the LA Act to withdraw acquisition in regard to land could only be exercised before the possession of the acquired lands is taken - No order u/s. 48(1) of the LA Act can be passed by the government, without hearing the local authority for whose benefit the acquisition is made - In the present case, land owners were not heard before issuing the cancellation notification dated 22.7.2002 - Therefore, the notification dated 22.7.2002 is illegal being opposed to principles of natural justice - However, notification dated 22.7.2002 is inextricably linked to notification dated 15.9.2001 which was invalid for the same reasons as the notification dated 22.7.2002 - Further, the notification dated 22.7.2002 was passed to set right the violation of principles of natural justice in making the notification dated 15.9.2001 - Interests of justice would be served if both the notifications dated 22.7.2002 and 15.9.2001 quashed and the state government directed to hear the request of the landowners for de-notification afresh - Appeal allowed.
