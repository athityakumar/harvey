Union of India and others v Kamla Verma

Supreme Court of India

13 August 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

A.R. Dave, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 661; (2010) 13 SCC 511; 2011 (85) ALR 601; 2010 (6) AWC 5633; JT 2010 (8) SC 577; 2010(8) SCALE 443; [2010] 9 S.C.R. 1157

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: British Statutes (Application to India) Repeal Act, 1960

Summary: Land & Property - Old Grant - De-hire of property - Respondent filed a writ petition before HC praying that the land in question be de-hired - HC allowed the petition by directing the appellant-authorities (Government authorities) to permit the respondent to undertake repair works of the premises in question and to de-hire the same - Hence, present appeal - Appellant-authorities submitted the land in question was in a cantonment area and in the past, land in cantonment area, belonging to the government, was given for a limited use to civilians and it was open to the government to resume such land at any time - Held, land in question was held by original grantee on "old grant" terms - Being allotted the land on "old grant" terms, the said allottee never became a full-fledged owner and, therefore, he could not have transferred any right better than what he had in respect of the land in question to the respondent - Thus, respondent was holding the land in question only on "old grant" terms and, therefore, she was not a full-fledged owner of the land but she had the right only in respect of the super-structure put up on the land in question, which had been given on lease to the appellant-authorities - Hence, it was always open to the appellant-authorities to resume the land in question and the appellant-authorities cannot be prevented from resuming the land in question - HC was in error while considering the respondent as an owner of the property in question - Order passed by HC set aside - Appeal allowed.
