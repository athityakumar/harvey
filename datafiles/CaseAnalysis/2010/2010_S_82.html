Venture Global Engineering v Satyam Computer Services Limited and another

Supreme Court of India

11 August 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Ganguly, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 609; (2010) 8 SCC 660; AIR 2010 SC 3371; 2010 (6) ALD(SC) 15; 2010(6) ALL MR 983; 2011 (86) ALR 775; 2010 (6) AWC 5643; 2010 (3) CompLJ 673; JT 2010 (8) SC 583; 2010 (7) MLJ(SC) 712; 2010(8) SCALE 159; [2010] 9 S.C.R. 858

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Civil Procedure

Keywords: English Arbitration Act, Andhra Pradesh Arbitration Rules, 2000

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Civil Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 227 - Limitation Act, 1963, s. 5 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 8 r. 9 - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, s. 34 - Appellant entered into a Shareholders Agreement and a Joint Venture Agreement with the first respondent, for establishing a company (second respondent) - As per terms of agreement, appellant and the first respondent each held 50 per cent shareholding in the second respondent - Thereafter, second respondent entered into a sub-contract - Appellant alleged concealment and dereliction of duty as a joint venture partner - Disputes referred to arbitration - Arbitrator passed the award whereby appellant was to transfer its entire shareholding in second respondent to first respondent - First respondent filed a petition for the recognition and enforcement of the award before the U.S. District Court which was allowed and was confirmed in appeal - Appellant filed a suit for setting aside award - Suit was dismissed on the ground that a foreign award could not be challenged u/s. 34 of Act - Appeal filed thereagainst was also dismissed - In appeal SC held that a foreign award could be challenged u/s. 34 of Act and remanded the case to the Trial Court - Appellant filed an interim application before the Trial Court to bring certain facts on record and also filed additional pleadings in respect of the same under O. 8 r. 9 of the CPC - Trial Court allowed the application - Revision petition filed thereagainst was allowed - HC held that appellant cannot be permitted to raise any additional grounds after expiry of stipulated period - Hence, present appeal - (A) Interpretation of Explanation u/s. 34 of Act - Expression "in the making of the award" - Held, concept of public policy in Act as given in the explanation is that if anything is found in excess of jurisdiction and depicts a lack of due process, it will be opposed to public policy of India - When an award is induced or affected by fraud or corruption, the same will fall within the aforesaid grounds of excess of jurisdiction and a lack of due process - Further, expression 'fraud in the making of the award' cannot be narrowly construed because fraud being of 'infinite variety' may take many forms, and secondly, the expression 'the making of the award' will have to be read in conjunction with whether the award 'was induced or affected by fraud' - (B) Whether grounds sought to be incorporated by way of amendment come within the concept of public policy explained in the Explanation to s. 34 of the Act? - Held, concealment of relevant and material facts, which should have been disclosed before the arbitrator, is an act of fraud - Further, facts concealed must have a causative link and if the concealed facts, disclosed after the passing of the award, have a causative link with the facts constituting or inducing the award, such facts are relevant in a setting aside proceeding and award may be set aside if affected or induced by fraud - In the present case, facts which appellant wanted to bring on record by way of amendment would show that the representations made by the first respondent about its financial position were prima facie unreliable - The question in present case, was therefore one of relevance of the materials which the appellant wants to bring on record by way of amendment in its plea for setting aside the award - In the interest of justice and considering the fairness of procedure, the Court should allow the appellant to bring those materials on record as they may have a bearing on the appellant's plea for setting aside the award - Appeal allowed.
