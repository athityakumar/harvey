Madras Cements Limited v Commissioner of Central Excise

Supreme Court of India

06 May 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 349; (2010) 6 SCC 606; 2010 (254) E.L.T. 3; 2010(5) SCALE 387; [2010] 6 S.C.R. 169

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Modvat Rules

Summary: Excise - Indirect Tax - Central Excise Act, 1944 - Central Excise Rules, 1944, rr. 57Q and 57U - Central Excise Tariff Act, 1985 - MODVAT Credit - Capital goods - Assessee' was engaged in the manufacture of cement and clinker coming within the ambit of Chapter 25 of the Central Excise Tariff Act, 1985 - Revenue issued show cause notice to assessee alleging that assessee was not entitled to MODVAT Credit on ineligible capital goods amounting to Rs. 8,42,843/- as it had taken MODVAT Credit on items which did not come within the purview of capital goods u/r. 57Q of Rules, 1944 - Assessee contended that said items comprised components, spares and accessories within the meaning of Explanation 1(d) of r. 57Q(1) of Rules, 1944 relating to capital good - Assessee also contended that parts of the Bucket Elevator (8434.00) and Wagon Loaders (8431.00) were parts of machinery and were eligible for MODVAT Credit as per the Board's Circular No.276/110/96 TRU - Asst. Commissioner disallowed MODVAT Credit amounting to Rs.4,31,749/- with regard to some of the items on the ground that mandatory requirement stipulated in serial no.5 of the Table to r. 57Q had not been complied with - Commissioner dismissed appeal filed against said order - Tribunal upheld the order of Commissioner - Hence, present appeal - Whether order of tribunal could be upheld and MODVAT credit on certain goods for the period comprising November and December, 1999 was rightly denied to assessee? - Held, yes - In order to avail of MODVAT/CENVAT credit, an assessee has to satisfy the Assessing Authorities that the capital goods in the form of component, spares and accessories had been utilized during the process of manufacture of the finished product - In the present case, assessee was not able to identify the machinery for which the goods in question was used - In the absence of such identification, it was not possible for the Assessing Authorities to come to a decision as to whether MODVAT - Order of tribunal upheld - Appeals dismissed.
