S. Khushboo v Kanniammal and another

Supreme Court of India

28 April 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, Deepak Verma

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 357; (2010) 5 SCC 600; AIR 2010 SC 3196; 2010 CRLJ 2828; 2010 (3) CTC 205; 2010 (3) JhrCR(SC) 56; JT 2010 (4) SC 478; 2010 (4) MLJ(Crl) 99; 2010 (2) OLR 156; 2010 (2) RCR(Criminal) 793; 2010(4) SCALE 462; [2010] 5 S.C.R. 322

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Media & Entertainment

Keywords: Morality, Indecent Representation of Women (Prohibition) Act, 1986

Summary: Criminal - Media & Entertainment - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 482 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 499, 500 and 505 - Indecent Representation of Women (Prohibition) Act, 1986, ss. 4 and 6 - Quashing of criminal proceedings - Defamation - Appellants expressed her opinion about the increasing incidence of pre-martial sex, especially in the context of live-in relationships and called for the societal acceptance of the same in an interview to a leading news magazine i.e. India Today and Dhina Thanthi - Publication of these statements in those leading news magazine drew criticism from some quarters and several persons and organisations filed criminal complaints against the appellant - Appellant approached HC to seek quashing of all the criminal proceedings instituted against her through the exercise of its inherent power u/s. 482 of Cr.PC - Said plea was rejected by HC - Aggrieved by the aforesaid judgment, the appellant approached SC by way of a batch of Special Leave Petitions - Whether HC rightly rejected the plea of the appellant for quashing of criminal proceedings instituted against her and whether the appellant's remarks could reasonably amount to offence of defamation as defined u/s. 499 IPC? - Held, no - Statement of the appellant has been made in the context of a survey which has touched on numerous aspects relating to the sexual habits of people in big cities - Even though this survey was not part of a literary or artistic work, it was published in a news magazine thereby serving the purpose of communicating certain ideas and opinions on said subject - There is neither any intent on part of the appellant to cause harm to the reputation of the complainants nor any actual harm done to their reputation as both the elements i.e. mens rea and actus reus are missing - Moreover, appellant's remarks were not directed at any individual or a readily identifiable group of people - Institution of the numerous criminal complaints against the appellant was done in a mala fide manner - Impugned order of HC set aside - Criminal proceedings quashed - Appeals allowed.
