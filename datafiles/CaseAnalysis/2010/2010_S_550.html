Anand Singh and another v State of Uttar Pradesh and others

Supreme Court of India

28 July 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 558; (2010) 11 SCC 242; 2010 (5) AWC 5386; JT 2010 (8) SC 15; 2010 (4) RCR(Civil) 32; 2011 (112) RD 231; 2010(7) SCALE 353; [2010] 9 S.C.R. 133; 2010 (3) UPLBEC 2318

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Land Acquisition (U.P. Amendment) Act

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4(1), 5A, 6, 17(4) - Land acquisition - Dispensation of enquiry u/s. 5A - Justifiability - Appellants' lands were notified u/s. 4(1) of the Act by respondent for construction of a residential colony - Since the lands were required urgently, enquiry u/s. 5A of the Act was dispensed with - Respondent issued declaration u/s. 6 of the Act - Appellants challenged the acquisition before the HC on the ground that there was no material before the respondent for dispensing with the enquiry u/s. 5A of the Act - HC dismissed appellant's writ petitions - Whether the impugned notifications invoking urgency clause and dispensation of enquiry u/s. 5A for the public purpose viz., 'development of residential colony' are legal and valid - Held, although notification u/s. 4(1) has been issued abruptly, nothing has been done more than one year thereafter - If the matter has been considered before the notifications u/s. 4 were issued and for about a year thereafter in issuance of declaration u/s. 6, acquisition proceedings could have been arranged in a manner so as to enable the land owners and/or the interested persons to file their objections u/s. 5A within the prescribed time and complete the enquiry expeditiously - Although some correspondence between the authorities and the respondent was placed before the HC by the acquisition authority, but no material has been placed on record by the respondent either before the HC or before SC indicating the application of mind that the urgency was of such nature which warranted elimination of the enquiry u/s. 5A of the Act - Therefore, the respondent completely failed to justify the dispensation of an enquiry u/s. 5A by invoking s. 17(4) of the Act - Since appellants accepted the acquisition, it is too late in setting aside the acquisition proceedings initiated by the respondents - Therefore, in the peculiar facts and circumstances of the case, the appellants are not entitled to any relief although dispensation of enquiry u/s. 5A was not justified - However, liberty granted to appellants to make appropriate representation to the State Government for de-notifying the acquisition - Appeals dismissed.
