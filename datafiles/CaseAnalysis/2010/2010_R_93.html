Suhrid Singh @ Sardool Singh v Randhir Singh And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

29 March 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, R.M. Lodha

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 227; (2010) 12 SCC 112; AIR 2010 SC 2807; 2010 (5) ALD(SC) 94; 2010 (3) CGLJ 252; JT 2010 (3) SC 472; 2010 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1356; 2010 (3) MLJ(SC) 1344; 2010 (158) PLR 707; 2010 (2) RCR(Civil) 564; 2010 (110) RD 145; 2010(3) SCALE 389

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Court-fees Act, 1870, ss. 7(iv)(c) r/w. 7(v) - Court fee for cancellation of sale deeds - Appellant filed suit for several reliefs like a declaration that two houses and certain agricultural lands purchased by his father were co-parcenary properties as they were purchased from the sale proceeds of ancestral properties, and that he was entitled to joint possession thereof, for a declaration that the will dt. 14-7-1985 with the codicil dt. 17-8-1988 made in favour of the third defendant, and gift deed made in favour of fourth defendant were void and non-est 'qua the co-parcenary' etc. - Appellant claims to have paid a court fee of Rs.19.50 for the relief of declaration, Rs.117/- for the relief of joint possession, and Rs.42/- for the relief of permanent injunction, in all Rs.179/- - Civil Judge held that prayers relating to the sale deeds amounted to seeking cancellation of the sale deeds and therefore ad valorem court fee was payable on the sale consideration in respect of the sale deeds - Appellant filed revision petition contending that he had paid the court fee u/s. 7(iv)(c) of 1870 Act and that the suit was not for cancellation of any sale deed and therefore the court fee paid by him was adequate and proper - HC dismissed said revision petition - Hence, present appeal - What is the court fee payable in regard to the prayer for a declaration that the sale deeds were void and not 'binding on the co-parcenary', and for the consequential relief of joint possession and injunction? - Held, Section 7(iv)(c) of 1870 Act provides that in suits for a declaratory decree with consequential relief, the court fee shall be computed according to the amount at which the relief sought is valued in the plaint - In the present case, there is no prayer for cancellation of the sale deeds - Prayer is for a declaration that the deeds do not bind the 'co-parcenery' and for joint possession - Plaintiff in the suit was not the executant of the sale deeds - Therefore, the court fee was computable u/s. 7(iv)(c) of 1870 Act - Trial court and HC's order set aside - Appeals allowed.
