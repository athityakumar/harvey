Anil Kumar v B. S. Neelkanta and others

Supreme Court of India

07 May 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 940; (2010) 5 SCC 407; AIR 2010 SC 2715; 2010 (5) ALD(SC) 62; 2010 (3) AWC 2811; 2010 (2) CLT(SC) 250; JT 2010 (6) SC 255; 2010 (3) MPLJ 514; 2010 (3) RCR(Civil) 128

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Practice & Procedure - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996 - Appointment of arbitrator - State Tourism Corporation (Lesser) awarded a lease to a private Company (Holding Company) for developing an hotel - Holding Company, subsequently, entered into an agreement with 1st and 2nd respondents whereby parties agreed to execute the said project through 4th respondent/new Company - 1st and 2nd respondents agreed to take over 74% equity whilst Holding Company retained its 24% equity in 4th respondent - Consequently, lesser executed lease deed in favour of 4th respondent (Lessee) wherein 4th respondent agreed that they would not change its constitution without prior consent of the lesser - 1st and 2nd respondents, thereafter, took over the whole equity of 4th respondent from Holding Company through a subsequent agreement - Since the change of constitution of 4th respondent was not with the prior consent of the lesser, lesser issued a notice of termination to 4th respondent - 1st and 2nd respondent, hence, immediately sold 76% of their holdings in 4th respondent to petitioner through impugned agreement, however, petitioner subscribed the shares of 4th respondent in the name of its associate Company - Sale agreement between the petitioner and respondent was submitted to lesser for consent and lesser granted consent accordingly - 1st respondent, being the 24% equity holder of the 4th respondent, send an e-mail to petitioner stating that petitioner's association with the project was cancelled due to non-investment of any amount in the project and hence, any supplemental agreements entered into by the petitioner with any other parties in connection with the project also would be terminated consequentially - Petitioner invoked arbitration clause in the impugned agreement, however, neither the lesser nor did the respondents reply to the notice of petitioner - Petitioner filed a petition u/s. 11(6) of the Act before the HC to appoint an arbitrator - HC appointed an arbitrator - Respondents challenged the HC order before the SC contending that HC had no jurisdiction to appoint an arbitrator in the present case since it was an international commercial arbitration and, therefore, the jurisdiction to appoint an arbitrator was vested with the Chief Justice of India alone - SC granted leave to respondents and as a result, petitioner filed the present petition u/ss. 11(5) and (6) of the Act for appointment of an arbitrator - Whether the dispute regarding termination of relationship between 4th respondent and the petitioner is dead one in the sense that on alleged allotment of equity in favour of an associate of the petitioner, impugned agreement between the petitioner and respondents, has worked itself out and no live issue in terms of the said agreement subsists - Held, materials on record showed that: (i) there are disputes between the parties on the issues/claim raised by the petitioner and countered by the respondents, including whether the claim still subsists or has been extinguished as alleged by the respondents, which cannot be resolved without evidence; (ii) there is an Arbitration Agreement in cl. 41 of the impugned agreement, to which the petitioner is a party along with the respondents; (iii) the issues/claim raised by the petitioner, on a mere assertion cannot be said to be a dead one without evidence to be produced by the parties in support of and rebuttal thereto, on their respective stands, regarding rights and obligations of the parties under various other agreements, on allotment of 76% of equity in favour of the associate Company of petitioner and petitioner's right to nominate or being himself on the Board of Directors of 4th respondent/Company; and (iv) the arbitrator is competent u/s. 16 of the Act to rule on its own jurisdiction, including rule on any objections with respect to existence or validity of the Arbitration Agreement - Therefore, sole arbitrator is appointed in the present case to adjudicate upon the claims/disputes raised by the petitioner, subject to his consent and such terms as he may deem fit and proper - Petition allowed.
