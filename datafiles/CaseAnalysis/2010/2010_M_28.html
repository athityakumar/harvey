M.C. Ali And Another v State Of Kerala

Supreme Court of India

13 April 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Surinder Singh Nijjar, B. SUDARSHAN REDDY

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 265; (2010) 4 SCC 573; AIR 2010 SC 1639; 2010 ALL MR (Cri) 1593; 2010 CRLJ 2791; 2010 (4) JhrCR(SC) 45; JT 2010 (3) SC 626; 2010 (3) MLJ(Crl) 141; 2010(3) SCALE 662; 2010 (2) UC 793

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 143, 147, 148, 149, 307 and 302 - Conviction - Challenged - Six appellants convicted u/ss. 143, 147, 148, 307 and 302 r/w s. 149 of the IPC - On appeal, HC remanded the case back to the Trial Court for fresh disposal - On a reappraisal of the evidence, Trial Court noticed that the names of the accused were mentioned in First Information Statement, but they were not mentioned in the relevant column of the inquest report - Trial Court also noticed that due to the long enmity of P.W.5 and his family with the accused, the evidence had to be scrutinized carefully - Trial Court also noticed delay in recording the statement of P.W.2 - Trial Court noticed that there was absolutely no explanation with regard to the injuries suffered by the accused - It was also observed that all the witnesses being interested witnesses, their evidence could not be believed in the absence of independent corroboration - Trial Court came to the conclusion that the prosecution had failed to prove the offences alleged against the appellants and they were, therefore, acquitted - Appeal was filed before HC - HC held that the prosecution conclusively proved the case against appellants and the findings recorded by the Sessions Judge were perverse and manifestly erroneous - Appellants were convicted u/ss. 302, 307, 149 and 34 of the IPC - Hence, present appeal - Whether findings of the Sessions Court were just and reasonable and the HC ought not to have interfered in the appeal? - Held, it is settled law that if two views are possible, the one which favours the accused has to be accepted, Appellate Court should not disturb the findings of acquittal - In the present case, HC reviewed the entire evidence and reached the conclusions which were contrary to the conclusions recorded by the Trial Court - Two views being reasonably possible the HC ought not to have interfered with the verdict of acquittal recorded by the Trial Court - Further, taking into consideration the entire facts and circumstances of the case, it would not be possible to agree with the HC that the findings recorded by the Trial Court were perverse or that only one conclusion consistent with the guilt of the accused was possible - Judgment of the HC set aside - Appeals allowed.
