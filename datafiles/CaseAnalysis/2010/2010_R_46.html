Madan Mohan Singh and others v Rajni Kant and another

Supreme Court of India

13 August 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 617; (2010) 9 SCC 209; 2010 (9) ADJ 263; AIR 2010 SC 2933; 2010 (6) ALD(SC) 178; 2010 (6) AWC 5637; 2010 (5) CTC 503; JT 2010 (8) SC 515; 2010 (7) MLJ(SC) 706; 2011 (1) RCR(Civil) 152; 2011 (112) RD 63; 2010(8) SCALE 406; [2010] 10 S.C.R. 30

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: U.P. Consolidation of Holdings Act, 1953

Summary: Land & Property - U.P. Consolidation of Holdings Act, 1953, s. 9-A(2) - Indian Evidence Act, 1872 - 'C' was recorded as the khatedar - Respondents claimed themselves to be his sons and filed objections u/s. 9-A(2) of Act and asked for inclusion of their names as his heirs - Another objection was filed by appellants - Consolidation Officer recorded that after the death of 'C''s wife in 1945, 'C' had live-in-relationship with respondent's mother - Respondents and other four daughters were born out of this relationship - Therefore, Consolidation Officer allowed the objections filed by the respondents and directed to record their names - Appeal filed before the Settlement Officer dismissed - Writ petition filed thereagainst was also dismissed - Hence, present appeal - Appellants' contended that respondents were born prior to 1960 i.e. prior to the year 'C' started living with respondents mother - Appellants placed reliance on documents namely, School Leaving Certificates, School Registers, Voter Lists for proving the same - Held, authenticity of the entries would depend on whose information such entries stood recorded and what was his source of information - Further, if a person wants to rely on a particular date of birth and wants to press a document in service, he has to prove its authenticity in terms of s. 32(5) of the Evidence Act - In the present case, kind of material placed by the appellants on record cannot be termed enough to disbelieve the claim of the respondents - Findings of facts recorded by the courts below cannot be disturbed on this material - Thus, appeal does not present special facts and circumstances which may warrant further re-appreciation of the evidence, appeal being based on totally unreliable/contradicting documents and not worth placing any reliance - Appeal dismissed.
