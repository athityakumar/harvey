Sukhendra Singh and others v State of Uttar Pradesh and others

Supreme Court of India

26 August 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 674; (2010) 9 SCC 27; 2011 (85) ALR 814; 2011 (1) AWC 304; 2011 (112) RD 597; 2010(8) SCALE 535

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property

Keywords: U.P. Consolidation of Holdings Act, 1953

Summary: Land & Property - Administrative - U.P. Consolidation of Holdings Act, 1953, ss. 9A(2), 52(2) - Appellants initiated proceedings u/s. 9A(2) of the Act for declaring them as Bhumidar and for removing the name of Gaon Sabha with respect to the disputed land - Consolidation Officer declared appellants as Bhumidar - Appellants filed application u/s. 52(2) of the Act for implementation of the said declaratory order - Consolidation Officer ordered to correct the Revenue records by entering appellants' name as Bhumidar - Since no action was done pursuant to Consolidation Officer's order, appellants filed writ petition before the HC - Single Judge ordered the concerned authorities to make necessary corrections in the revenue record - Respondent/Gram Panchayat, in the mean time, moved an application before the Consolidation Officer for recalling the earlier orders - Consolidation Officer took cognizance of the said application and send notices to appellants - Appellant challenged the said notices before the HC which was still pending - Subsequently, respondent/Gram Panchayat challenged the Single Judge order before DB - Respondent contended that appellants had obtained the HC order fraudulently and by misrepresentation and concealment of facts - DB disposed off the appeal by directing the Consolidation Officer to decide the dispute afresh - Whether impugned DB order remanding the matter back to Consolidation Officer is justifiable - Held, respondent did not challenge the Consolidation Officer declaratory order before the HC and therefore, Consolidation Officer cannot go into the grievances raised by the respondent - Appellants in their writ petition before the HC had raised the grievances with regard to continuous disregard of the Single Judge order for carrying out corrections in Revenue records as well as the legality and justification of the notices issued to them for their appearance before Consolidation Officer and in reopening the consolidation proceedings - Issues raised by the parties were required to be considered judiciously by the HC and not in the slipshod manner in which it had been done - No application of mind from the side of HC, therefore, impugned HC order is set aside and matter remanded back to HC for fresh consideration - Appeals disposed of.
