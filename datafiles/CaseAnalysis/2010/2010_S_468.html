Myladimmal Surendran and another v State of Kerala

Supreme Court of India

01 September 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Surinder Singh Nijjar, B. SUDARSHAN REDDY

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 707; (2010) 11 SCC 129; AIR 2010 SC 3281; 2010 ALL MR (Cri) 3279; JT 2010 (9) SC 282; 2011 (1) MLJ(Crl) 47; 2010 (4) RCR(Criminal) 331; 2010(9) SCALE 10; [2010] 10 S.C.R. 916; 2010 (3) UC 1397

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 143, 147, 148, 149, 302 and 341 - Conviction - Challenged - Appellants convicted u/ss. 143, 147, 148, 341, 302 r/w s. 149 of IPC - On appeal, HC confirmed the conviction of appellants but converted sentence of death to imprisonment for life - Present appeal - (A) Delay in lodging FIR - Held, it was rightly observed by the HC that there was no unexplained delay in lodging the FIR and in sending the copy of the same to the Magistrate - Hence, no prejudice caused to appellants - (B) Evidence of eyewitness - PW1 (wife of deceased), PW2 and PW3 - Held, evidence given by the wife of the deceased was unimpeachable - It could not be discarded, on the basis that she was an interested witness - Also, evidence given by the wife was consistent with the evidence of PW2 and PW3, who witnessed the assault - This was further corroborated by dying declaration made by the injured within minutes of being assaulted - Dying declaration also adds further corroboration to the eye-witness account given by PW1, PW2 and PW3 - (C) No Test identification parade was conducted - Effect thereof - It was submitted that since no test identification parade was held, prior to the witnesses deposing in court, the identity of the accused was not established - Held, not holding the test identification parade would not weaken the eye-witness account of PW1, PW2 and PW3 - All of them have identified the accused in court - Their identification was further strengthened by the fact that all the accused were known to the two witnesses earlier - Therefore, they were identified by name in court - Judgment of the HC do not call for any interference - Appeals dismissed.
