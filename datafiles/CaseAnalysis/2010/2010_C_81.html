Kamal Kumar Agarwal v Commissioner Of Commercial Taxes, West Bengal And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

26 March 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 220; (2010) 5 SCC 142; AIR 2012 SC (Supp) 722; JT 2010 (3) SC 390; 2010(3) SCALE 375; [2010] 31 VST 50

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Sales Tax

Keywords: Additional Commissioner, Transhipment, Transporter, West Bengal Sales Tax Act, 1994, West Bengal Sales Tax Rules, 1995

Summary: Sales Tax - Customs - West Bengal Sales Tax Act, 1994, s. 68(3) - West Bengal Sales Tax Rules, 1995, r. 211A (6) - Scope of s. 68(3) - Penalty - 'X' appointed appellant as it's Customs House Agent (CHA) to clear the consignment of goods imported from Hamburg and sent all the relevant documents required for getting the goods cleared along with copy of the Declaration in Form 44A - Appellant imported said goods and handed the same to the transporter along with all the necessary documents for onward transportation of goods to it's final destination - Show cause notice issued to appellant alleging contravention of s. 68 of 1994 Act as appellant failed to produce endorsed copy of the declaration in terms of r.211A(6) of 1995 Rules - Appellant contended that show cause notice was not maintainable as he was appointed by the importer only to get the documents cleared from the Customs and Port Authorities and not for movement of goods for which it is the importer who appoints a transporter - Asst. Commissioner of Commercial Taxes imposed a penalty on the appellant for failure to produce the endorsed copy of the declaration in terms of r. 211A(6) of the 1995 Rules - Tribunal rejected appeal filed against said order - On appeal, HC upheld the order of Tribunal - Hence, present appeal - Whether Asst. Commissioner rightly imposed penalty on appellant? - Held, yes - Sub-rule (6) of r. 211A, applies to a declarant who could be a transporter, CHA, clearing and forwarding agent or any person taking delivery of the consignment of goods from the Port for despatch of the same outside West Bengal - Clause (4) of the Declaration refers to an obligation being undertaken by the declarant that the delivery is required to be taken by him for transporting such consignment to it's destination outside West Bengal - Hence, with the making of the Declaration, the appellant undertook the obligation to transit the consignment to the destination outside the State for which the proof was the countersigned copy of the Declaration - Hence, Asst. Commissioner of Commercial taxes rightly imposed penalty on appellant on failure of producing endorsed copy of the declaration in terms of r. 211(A)(6) of 1995 Rules - Impugned order upheld - Appeal dismissed
