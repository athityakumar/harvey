Mandal Revenue Officer v Goundla Venkaiah And Another

Supreme Court of India

06 January 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 18; (2010) 2 SCC 461; AIR 2010 SC 744; 2010 (2) ALD(SC) 115; 2010 (5) MLJ(SC) 134; 2010(1) SCALE 206

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Andhra Pradesh Civil Courts Act, 1972, Andhra Pradesh Land Grabbing (Prohibition) (Amendment) Act, 1987, Andhra Pradesh Land Encroachment Act, 1905, Andhra Pradesh Land Grabbing (Prohibition) Amendment Act, 1987, Andhra Pradesh (Telangana Area) Land Revenue Rules

Summary: Land & Property - Administrative - Civil Procedure - Andhra Pradesh Land Encroachment Act, 1905, s.7 - Andhra Pradesh Land Grabbing (Prohibition) Act, 1982 - Land Grabbing - Adverse Possession - Eviction - Challenged - Predecessor of respondents illegally occupied 5 acres land comprised in Survey No. 42,which was classified in revenue records as Kharizkhata-Sarkari - In 1965 and 1986, notices were issued to predecessor of respondents u/s. 7 of the 1905 Act but no order appears to had been passed for his eviction - Appellant/Mandal Revenue Officer filed application before Special Tribunal constituted under Land Grabbing Act for recovery of possession of 5 acres land by alleging that same was illegally occupied by predecessor of respondents which by its Order dt 27-5-1997 declared that schedule land was Govt. land which had been grabbed by Predecessor of respondents and his successors and directed them to hand over possession thereof to Govt. within 2 months - Aggrieved, respondents filed appeal before Special Court which was dismissed by detailed Order dt. 18-8-1998 - Respondents challenged said Orders before DB which did not disturb concurrent finding recorded by Special Tribunal and Special Court that schedule land was Govt. land but set aside orders passed by them on premise that respondents had acquired title by adverse possession and as such they could not be evicted by being treated as land grabbers - Hence, instant appeal - Whether DB's Order and adverse possession claimed by respondents was justifiable - Held, relied on Annakili v. A. Vedanayagam 2007 Indlaw SC 1475 and P.T. Munichikkanna Reddy v. Revamma 2007 Indlaw SC 411 - Therefore, respondents miserably failed to establish that they had acquired title over schedule land by adverse possession and HC was not at all justified in upsetting orders passed by Special Tribunal and Special Court - Hence, set aside impugned order and dismissed writ petition filed before HC by respondents and restored orders passed by Special Tribunal and Special Court and directed respondents should hand over vacant possession of schedule land to officer not below rank of Additional Collector/ appellant, who should be nominated by District Collector/appellant - If respondents fail to hand over vacant possession of schedule land to officer nominated by appellant/ District Collector then he should take possession of land and, if necessary, use appropriate force for that purposes - Appeal allowed
