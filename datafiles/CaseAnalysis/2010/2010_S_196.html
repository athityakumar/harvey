J. Kodanda Rami Reddy v State Of A.P. & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

11 November 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 953; (2011) 1 SCC 197; 2011 (1) ALD(SC) 65; JT 2010 (12) SC 429; 2011 (1) MLJ(SC) 798; 2011 (2) MPLJ 18; 2011 (3) RCR(Civil) 581B; 2010(11) SCALE 665

Case Digest

Subject: Contract & Commercial

Summary: (A) Arbitration & ADR - Contract & Commercial - Arbitration Act, 1940, ss. 8, 30, 33 - Post-revision of arbitration clause - Applicability -1st respondent/State awarded a construction contract to appellant/Contractor, however, 1st respondent, by GOM dated 24-10-1983, revised the arbitration clause therein where it was stipulated that the claims of more than Rs. 50,000/ would be resolved through a competent civil Court - Dispute arose between parties - Appellant filed a petition u/s. 8 of the Act before the Sub Court for appointment of an arbitrator - Sub Court appointed an arbitrator - Arbitrator entered into reference and passed an award - Arbitrator, subsequently, applied to the Sub Court for making the award a rule of the Court - 1st respondent, in the meantime, filed petitions u/ss. 30 and 33 of the Act for setting aside the award - Sub Court dismissed 1st respondent's petitions and made the award a rule of the Court - 1st respondent challenged the Sub Court order before the HC - HC held that disputes relating to the claims of more than Rs.50000/- should not be adjudicated by appointment of an arbitrator u/s. 8 of the Act, but should be resolved by a competent civil court - HC, consequently, allowed 1st respondent's appeal against the Sub Court order dismissing 1st respondent's petitions u/ss. 30, 33 of the Act - Hence the present appeal by the appellant/Contractor - Whether the GOM dated 24-10-1983 provided for arbitration - Held, SC, in Vishakhapatnam Urban Development Authority v. V. Narayana Raju,1999 (9) SCC 572, held that GOM dated 24-10-1983 clearly provided that the arbitration was available only in regard to claims up to Rs.50,000/- and not in regard to claims above Rs.50,000/- which had to be adjudicated by the court of competent jurisdiction - Larger Bench of the SC, in State of Andhra Pradesh vs. Obulu Reddy, 1999 INDLAW SC 34, upheld the view of expressed in Vishakapatnam Urban Development Authority - Therefore, GOM dated 24-10-1983 does not contain any provision for arbitration in regard to claims exceeding Rs.50,000/- Appeal allowed.

(B) Arbitration & ADR - Practice & Procedure - Arbitration Act, 1940, ss. 8, 30, 33 - Appointment of arbitrator - Finality of order - Whether the Sub Court order appointing arbitrator u/s. 8(2) of the Act, could be challenged by the 1st respondent/State, two years later, after participating in the arbitration proceedings without protest and after the arbitral award being made a rule of the court - Held, 1st respondent specifically admitted in the petitions filed u/ss. 30 and 33 of the Act before the Sub Court that the arbitrator was appointed in terms of the agreement between the parties - Having failed to contend that there was no arbitration agreement in the proceedings u/ss. 30 and 33 of the Act, and having specifically conceded that the arbitrator was appointed in terms of the agreement, the 1st respondent was estopped from subsequently contending in the appeal against the arbitration award that there was no arbitration agreement and the remedy of the contractor was only by way of a suit - Such a contention of 1st respondent would be barred by the principle of res judicata as it was directly and substantially in issue in the proceedings u/s. 8(2) of the Act and the decision therein had attained finality - Therefore, 1st respondent could not challenge the order appointing the arbitrator, which had attained finality, by contending that there was no arbitration agreement between the parties - Appeal allowed.

(C) Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration Act, 1940, s. 8 - Appointment of arbitrator - Interference by HC -Whether the HC was justified in setting aside the order appointing the arbitrator u/s. 8(2) of the Act and the common order rejecting the 1st respondent's application for setting aside the award and making the award a rule of the court - Held, order u/s. 8(2) of the Act, by the Sub Court was not challenged and was allowed to attain finality - Said order was challenged only after the award was made and that award was made a rule of the court, therefore, in such circumstance, the rule of finality would come into play in regard to the order u/s. 8(2) of the Act appointing the arbitrator -Though the 1st respondent had initially contended that there was no arbitration agreement, when the Sub Court held that there was an arbitration agreement and appointed the arbitrator, the 1st respondent did not challenge the decision, but accepted the said position and participated in the arbitration proceeding without protest - Further, when the award was made by the arbitrator, the 1st respondent filed an application for setting aside the award wherein it admitted specifically that the appointment of the arbitrator was in terms of the agreement between the parties, but challenged the award on other grounds - Therefore, when there was an adjudication by a court of competent jurisdiction that there was an arbitration agreement and when that was not challenged and the arbitrator was allowed to proceed on the basis that there was an arbitration agreement, and when it was specifically conceded that the appointment of arbitrator is in terms of the agreement between the parties, the award would not be a nullity - An order by a competent court, even if erroneous, is binding, unless it is challenged and set aside by a higher forum - Hence impugned HC order is set aside and restored the Sub Court order u/s. 8(2) of the Act - Appeal allowed.
