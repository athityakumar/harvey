Secretary and Curator, Victoria Memorial Hall v Howrah Ganatantrik Nagrik Samity And Others

Supreme Court of India

09 March 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, Deepak Verma

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 166; (2010) 3 SCC 732; AIR 2010 SC 1285; 2010 (5) AWC 4997; JT 2010 (2) SC 566; 2010(2) SCALE 739; [2010] 3 S.C.R. 190

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Constitution

Keywords: Victoria Memorial Act, 1903

Summary: Administrative - Constitution - Victoria Memorial Act, 1903 - PIL filed alleging mismanagement, misuse and various types of abuses of the Victoria Memorial Hall (VMH) - HC constituted an Expert Committee for improving the environment of VMH, the appellant - After considering the views of the Expert Committee, the Board of Trustees accepted the proposal for construction of an annexe building replacing the existing cluster of annexe buildings which had become dilapidated - HC while disposing of the Writ Petition dis-approved the recommendation for having a centre and exhibition area within the VMH compound - HC also rejected recommendations made by the Expert Committee regarding further construction within the VMH Area - Appellant's application to modify the order to the extent that it may be construction of building after demolition of non-residential staff quarters was also rejected by HC - Hence, present appeal - Held, while deciding an issue, the Court is bound to give reasons for its conclusion - Recording of reasons is principle of natural justice and every judicial order must be supported by reasons recorded in writing - It ensures transparency and fairness in decision making - HC did not assign valid and good reasons for rejecting the recommendation made by the Expert Committee for allowing the construction in question in its judgment and order nor reasons have been recorded in the impugned judgment rejecting the application for modification of the earlier order - HC was not justified in imposing a total prohibition of construction of the Annexe in place of the existing cluster of buildings, which are in a dilapidated condition - Impugned judgment and order of HC set aside - Application filed by the appellant for modification of the order allowed - Appeal allowed.
