M/S. Jai Vijai Metal Udyog Private Limited Industrial Estate, Varanasi v The Commissioner, Trade Tax, Uttar Pradesh Lucknow

Supreme Court of India

16 April 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

D.K. Jain, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 282; (2010) 6 SCC 705; 2010 (253) E.L.T. 161; JT 2010 (6) SC 161; 2010(4) SCALE 87

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax; Trade

Keywords: Regional Sales Tax Act

Summary: Sales Tax - Trade - U.P. Trade Tax Act, 1948 - A/y 1988-89 - Re-assessment - Taxability - 'Properzi' redraw rods - Petitioner-Dealer engaged in the manufacture of aluminium 'properzi' redraw rods from aluminium ingots - For the a/ys 1983-84, 1984-85, 1985-86 and 1987-88, the 'properzi' rods manufactured by the dealer were classified by the Assessing Authorities to be a metal/primary metal and were assessed in terms of Entry 24 of the Schedule to the 1948 Act - Similarly, for the a/y 1988-89, the Assessing Authority taxed the 'properzi' rods at the rate of 2.2% - However, subsequently proceedings u/s. 21 of the Act were initiated against the dealer on the ground that assessment in respect of the said a/y had escaped assessment or had been under-assessed - Reason for re-opening the assessment was that while assessing another dealer in the State, Assessing Officer (AO) had held that 'properzi' redraw rods were not primary metal in terms of Entry 24 - In the re-assessment, the AO held that 'properzi' redraw rods manufactured by the dealer being a 'rolled product' were not primary metal and being commercially different from metal, were liable to be taxed as an unclassified item at the rate of 10% in terms of s. 3A(1)(c) of the U.P. Act - Appeal filed against said order was allowed - On revision, HC held that the 'properzi' rods manufactured by the Dealer are, in fact, wire bars and not wire rods, and have, therefore, to be taxed as unclassified items - Hence, present appeal - (A) Whether the aluminium 'properzi' redraw rods can be classified as metal under Entry 24? - Held, SC in Hindustan Aluminium Corporation Ltd. vs. State of Uttar Pradesh & Anr. 1981 INDLAW SC 243, held that that expression 'Metal' has been generally employed to refer to the metal in its primary sense, i.e. the metal in the form in which it is marketable as a primary commodity - Moreover, AO examined the entire issue afresh in the case of another dealer and then came to the conclusion that 'properzi' redraw rod falls in the category of metal and alloy, covered under Entry 24 - It was submitted that since the item, viz. 'properzi' redraw rod manufactured by the dealer and another dealer are similar, the Revenue cannot treat two dealers differently for the purpose of levying sales tax under the Regional Act as well as the Central Act - (B) When admittedly, the foundation for re-opening Dealer's assessment in respect of a/y 1988-89 was the assessment in the case of another dealer 'X', in which case, after the remand by the HC assessment for that year has attained finality, can the Revenue be permitted to take a different view in the case of the Dealer from that taken in the case of another dealer 'X' in respect of the same a/y? - Held, two different interpretations of a particular entry by the same authority on same set of facts, cannot be immunised from the equality clause u/art. 14 of the Constitution - In the present case, the basis for re-opening the assessment of the dealer in respect of a/y 1988-89, was the view taken by the AO in the case of another dealer , therefore, subsequently when on fresh assessment in another dealer's , the said product was held to be a 'metal' falling under Entry 24, the entire foundation for re-opening of the assessment vanished - Having accepted the view of the AO in the case of another dealer, the Sales Tax Authorities in the State cannot be permitted to take a different stand in the case of the Dealer in respect of a similar item for the same a/y - Impugned order of HC set aside - Appeal allowed.
