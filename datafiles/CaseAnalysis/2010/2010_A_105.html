Ashok Pal Singh and others v Uttar Pradesh Judicial Services Association and others

Supreme Court of India

13 September 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 742; (2010) 12 SCC 635; JT 2010 (10) SC 131; 2010(9) SCALE 339; [2010] 12 S.C.R. 25; 2011 (1) UPLBEC 297

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Service

Keywords: Sources Of Recruitment, UP Higher Judicial Services (Fourth Amendment) Rules, 1996, U.P. Higher Judicial Services Rules, 1975

Summary: Service - Advocates & Judges - U.P. Higher Judicial Services Rules, 1975, rr. 4(4), 8 - UP Higher Judicial Services (Fourth Amendment) Rules, 1996, rr. 22(3)(4), 26 - Inter se seniority - Determination - Promotees in Uttar Pradesh Higher Judicial Services challenged the inter se seniority list with regard to the seniority of direct recruits before the SC in P.K. Dixit v. State of Uttar Pradesh, 1987 INDLAW SC 28768 - SC issued directions to the HC and pursuant to the said direction, HC issued a tentative seniority list and invited objections - Promotees were satisfied that the said list was drawn in conformity with the judgment in P.K.Dixit and unobjectionable - HC constituted a Five-Judge Committee to finalise the list and on the basis of the report of the committee, final seniority list was issued - Aggrieved by the seniority list, promotees and direct recruits challenged the HC's final seniority list before the SC in O.P. Garg v. State of U.P. & Ors., 1991 INDLAW SC 265 - SC quashed the final seniority list with a direction to the HC to prepare, circulate, invite objections and then finalize the seniority list keeping in view the directions given in the judgment - In pursuance of the decision in O.P. Garg, the HC calculated the vacancies under different quotas for recruitment/promotion for different periods - However, method of calculation made by the HC in regard to the ratio between direct recruits and promotees in a given year, was again challenged before the SC in Srikant Tripathi & Ors. v. State of U.P. & Ors., 2001 INDLAW SC 12- SC issued directions in the said case to HC - Chief Justice of the HC placed the matter before the Administrative Committee of the HC, for implementation of the directions in Srikant Tripathi - Administrative Committee in turn constituted a three Member Sub-Committee to examine and submit a report - Sub-Committee examined the matter and submitted a report determining the actual number of vacancies available for the relevant period and the actual recruitments made and with other details - Said report was approved by the Administrative Committee and was approved by the Full Court of the HC - Promotees were aggrieved by the acceptance of the Report by the Full Court and had challenged the resolution of Full Court accepting the Sub-Committee report before the HC - DB allowed the writ petition of promotes by the impugned order whereby HC quashed the resolution of the Full Court and directed a fresh exercise to be carried out in the light of Srikant Tripathi to determine the vacancies and their distribution between the three sources of recruitment as per their quota under the Rules - Hence the present appeal - (A) Whether the vacancies occupied by judicial officers promoted and appointed against temporary posts u/rr. 22(3), 22(4) of 1996 Rules should be excluded when computing the respective quotas for promotees and direct recruits - Held, in O. P. Garg, SC struck down rr. 22(3) and 22(4) which confined the appointment to temporary posts to only promotees wherein SC held that when temporary posts u/r. 4(4) of the 1975 Rules are created as addition to the cadre, the direct recruits could not be denied their share of the quota as provided u/r. 6 of the said Rules - Therefore all the consequences of striking down rr. 22(3) and 22(4) followed, the only consequence that was excluded was the invalidation of appointments already made by applying the said sub-rr. 22(3) and 22(4) - Such protection cannot be widened or extended, to deny the quota of direct recruits in the temporary vacancies and thereby nullify the striking of the said sub-rules - Nor can the protection against invalidation of promotion be used to exclude the number of vacancies equal to the number of officers who were given protection, while computing the vacancies to be filled by different sources - Consequently, in spite of the protection against invalidation and reversion, all the appointments of promotees to temporary vacancies will have to be counted and adjusted against the substantive vacancies under promotee quota under the Rules - Direct recruits should be given quota in the temporary posts also - Therefore, the first direction in the impugned judgment of the HC (that vacancies occupied by promotees in excess of their quota shall be excluded from computation of respective quotas for direct recruitment and promotion) was contrary to the decision in O. P. Garg, and cannot be sustained - (B) Whether the direct recruits are entitled to 15% of the vacancies as a fixed quota or whether the said percentage is a ceiling imposed in regard to direct recruitment meaning that the vacant posts shall not be filled up more than 15% by the direct recruits - Held, r. 8 clarifies that direct recruits are entitled to 15% quota not only in the vacancies to be filled, but also 15% in the strength of the service - Rules also make it clear that when a shortfall in a particular recruitment is made up at the next recruitment, there is no question of the direct recruits appointed to the shortfall vacancies claiming seniority over the promotees who filled the shortfall post of direct recruits at the previous recruitment - Though the shortfall is made good at the next recruitment, the question of seniority will be governed by r. 26 - No direct recruit at a subsequent recruitment can claim that as he is being appointed against a direct recruitment vacancy of previous recruitment, his seniority should be reckoned from any date earlier to the date of his joining the service - Thus though the quota of direct recruits is fixed, there is flexibility in fixing the vacancies to be filled by direct recruitment and vacancies to be filled by promotion - HC can make adjustments in fixing the number of officers to be appointed by promotion and direct recruitment as shown in r. 8(2) and the provisos thereto ensuring that the number of direct recruits does not exceed 15% of the total strength of the service - (C) Whether the words '15% of the total permanent strength of the service' occurring in first proviso to sub-r. (2) of r. 8 of the 1975 Rules (as contrasted from '15% of the strength of the service' after the amendment), shall be given effect in computing the respective quotas of promotees and direct recruits till the amendment of Rules (effective from 15-3-1996) deleting the word 'permanent' in the said first proviso - Held, proviso to r. 8(2) should be read in the context of the quashing of sub-rr. (3) and (4) of r. 22 - If so read, it would be clear that when sub-rr. (3) and (4) of r. 22 were struck down holding that direct recruits were entitled to a quota in temporary posts also, the word 'permanent' in the first proviso to r. 8(2) is deemed to have been impliedly struck down or omitted by the decision in O.P. Garg - As the quota of direct recruits is 15% of the strength of the service, the number of appointments of direct recruits might have never exceeded their quota - (D) Whether the procedure of carrying forward vacancies adopted by the full court of the HC was erroneous having regard to the specific provisions of r. 8(2) - Held, there was no question of unfilled vacancies being carried forward for the purpose of fixing the number of officers to be taken at the next recruitment - Total vacancies to be filled at a recruitment shall have to be filled by applying sub-rr. (1) and (2) of r. 8 and its provisos - In that sense all vacancies, which are not filled by direct recruitment, get filled by promotion and there will be no carry over - Only a limited 'carry over' of unfilled direct recruitment vacancies in the manner stated in the r. 8(2) and the first proviso thereto would be allowed - Appeal allowed.
