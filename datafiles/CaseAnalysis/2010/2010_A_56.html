Amalendu Sahoo v Oriental Insurance Company Limited

Supreme Court of India

25 March 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Ganguly, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 205; (2010) 4 SCC 536; 2010 ACJ 1250; AIR 2010 SC 2090; 2010 (79) ALR 749; 2010 (3) AWC 2503; 2008 (4) CPJ(Sc) 9; JT 2010 (3) SC 218; 2010 (2) RCR(Civil) 635; 2010(3) SCALE 184; 2010 (2) UC 1043

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection; Insurance

Keywords: Comprehensive

Summary: Consumer Protection - Insurance - Complainant had taken a comprehensive insurance policy in respect of his private car and paid insurance premium duly - Impugned vehicle met with an accident during the subsistence of the policy - Complainant lodged a claim before the insurance company submitting that he handed the impugned vehicle by way of a good gesture and did not take any rent from 'X' for handed the impugned vehicle - Insurance company refused to allow the claim inter alia on the ground that the vehicle was given on hire and as per the policy terms such use was not permitted and the insured was not entitled to any compensation for such unauthorised use - Complainant filed complaint before District Forum who dismissed the claim of the complainant after going through the policy - On appeal, State Consumer Forum upheld the order of District Forum - National Consumer Forum also upheld the concurrent finding of the District and State Consumer Forums - Hence, present appeal - Whether insurance company rightly repudiate the claim of the complainant? - Held, no - SC in National Insurance Company Limited vs. Nitin Khandelwal 2008 INDLAW SC 848, held that insurance Company is liable to indemnify the owner of the vehicle when the insurer has obtained comprehensive policy for the loss caused to the insurer - Even assuming that there was a breach of condition of the insurance policy, the insurance company ought to have settled the claim on non-standard basis - Following the said guidelines, insurance company cannot repudiate the claim in toto - Insurance company directed to pay a consolidated sum of Rs.2,50,000/- even though compensation claimed is Rs.5,00,000/- - Appeal allowed
