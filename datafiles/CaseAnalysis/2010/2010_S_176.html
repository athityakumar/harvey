Sagunthala (Dead) through Lrs. v Special Tehsildar (L.A.) and Others

Supreme Court of India

24 February 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Ganguly, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 143; (2010) 3 SCC 661; AIR 2010 SC 984; 2010 (3) ALD(SC) 1; 2010(2) ALL MR 933; 2010 (81) ALR 295; 2010 (1) CLT(SC) 318; JT 2010 (2) SC 405; 2010 (6) MLJ(SC) 440; 2010 (3) MPLJ 313; 2010 (2) RCR(Civil) 378; 2010 (110) RD 651; 2010(2) SCALE 620; [2010] 3 S.C.R. 50

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - Land acquisition - Compensation - Enhancement of - Appellants' lands were acquired by the respondents for the purpose of expanding a State owned company - Land Acquisition Officer fixed the compensation - Reference Court enhanced the compensation, however, HC reduced the compensation on the ground that proximity of residential colonies would not make the acquired lands to be treated as house sites - Appellants contended that the acquired lands were potential house sites and the HC was not justified in ignoring the documentary evidence in that regard - Held, number of buildings were situated in the acquired lands since the said lands are also to be treated as house sites - As a portion of the land is being considered as house site, the adjoining lands have the potential of being put to better use as house sites in the near future - Further, acquired lands are very proximate to the plots of two residential colonies and are adjoining the main road - Reference Court after the close perusal of sale deeds produced by the appellants, held that those adjacent lands were sold as house sites - Therefore, calculation rendered by the HC was wrong and hence, HC order is set aside and order of Reference Court is upheld - Appeals allowed.
