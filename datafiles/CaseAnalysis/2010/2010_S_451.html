N. Rajanna and others v State of Karnataka and others

Supreme Court of India

04 May 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 335; (2010) 6 SCC 596; AIR 2010 SC 2667; JT 2010 (6) SC 282; 2010 (2) OLR 281; 2011 (112) RD 49; 2010(4) SCALE 657

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Keywords: Occupancy Rights, Karnataka Industries (Facilitation) Act, 2002

Summary: Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Karnataka Industries (Facilitation) Act, 2002 - Acquisition of land - Respondent no. 3 filed an application under the Karnataka Industries (Facilitation) Act, 2002 for approval of its proposed project to be set up on land measuring 500 Acres - Said project was approved by State High Level Clearance Committee - After 8 days, the State Government revised its decision and issued order for withdrawal of the approval accorded to the project of respondent No.3 on the ground of pendency of the case of appellants before the competent authority - Said action of the State Government was ratified by the Committee - Respondent no. 3 challenged said order in writ petition by submitting that under the Facilitation Act, the State Government does not have the power to review the approval accorded to its project - Single Judge however, instead of quashing order of State Government directed the State Government to hear respondent No.3 and pass fresh order - Respondent no.3 challenged said order - DB allowed the said appeal, quashed order of State Government and directed the State Government to proceed on the basis of approval granted by it - Appellants contended that impugned judgment is liable to be set aside as DB disposed of the appeal without complying with the basics of the natural justice, inasmuch as no notice or opportunity of hearing was given - Hence, present appeal - Whether DB could modify order passed by the Single Judge without issuing notice and giving opportunity of hearing to the appellants, who claim to have right over the land sought to be acquired by the State Government on behalf of respondent No.3? - Held, it is not in dispute that while considering the project of respondent No.3 and approving the same, the Committee and the State Government were aware of the fact that the issue relating to grant of occupancy rights in respect of the land is pending adjudication before the competent authority - It is also not in dispute that even before consideration of the project of respondent No.3 by the Committee, the appellants had filed objections against proposed acquisition of the land - They had also filed an application u/o. 1 r. 10 CPC for being impleaded as parties - Single Judge also made it clear that land owners must be heard before their land is acquired - Therefore, DB should have afforded an opportunity of hearing to the appellants before deciding the appeal preferred by respondent No.3 and directing the State Government to act in accordance with approval accorded - Impugned order set aside - Appeals allowed.
