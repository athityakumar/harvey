In Re: Mehar Singh Saini, Chairman, Hpsc & Ors. v Na

Supreme Court of India

12 November 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Swatanter Kumar, S.H. Kapadia, K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 967; (2010) 13 SCC 586; JT 2010 (12) SC 373; 2010(12) SCALE 5; 2010 (6) SLR 717

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Constitution

Keywords: Public Servants (Inquiries) Act, 1850

Summary: (A) Constitution - Administrative - Supreme Court Rules, 1966 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 317(1) - Removal and suspension of member of a Public Service Commission - (A) Whether principles of service jurisprudence are applicable to the inquiry proceedings u/art. 317(1) of the Constitution? - Held, principles of service jurisprudence may not be strictly applicable to the inquiry proceedings u/art. 317(1) of the Constitution for the reason that being a constitutional body, the Chairman and Members of the Commission are to maintain much higher standards of performance and behaviour than the civil servants appointed to the state services - Further, under the service jurisprudence, a person may be found guilty even on the charge being proved on the basis of preponderance of probabilities while in the proceedings of the present kind, conduct of a person may amount to misbehaviour requiring his removal u/art. 317(1) of the Constitution on the basis of rule of reasonable preponderance of probabilities - (B) Applicability of principles of criminal jurisprudence to the present proceedings in regard to opportunity of being heard, burden of proof and content of charges - Held, principles of criminal jurisprudence contemplate different standards of proof, language of charge and protections available to a suspect/accused - Proceedings before SC neither akin to proceedings under service law nor criminal law - Rule of 'reasonable preponderance of probabilities' would be the right standard to be applied to such cases - Contention of the respondents that the burden of proof applied to such cases 'beyond reasonable doubt' rejected - Order accordingly.

(B) Constitution - Administrative - Supreme Court Rules, 1966 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 317(1) - Removal and suspension of member of a Public Service Commission - 'Misbehaviour' in contradistinction to 'misconduct' and 'proved misbehaviour' - Held, language of Art. 317(1) indicates that the expression 'on the ground of misbehaviour' is an expression of wide connotation and cannot be given a restricted meaning - Further, framers of the Constitution opted to use different expressions in other Articles relating to misconduct or misbehaviour of other Constitutional institutions - Hence, use of different expressions in different Articles of the Constitution itself indicates the clarion intent of the framers to correlate a misconduct or misbehaviour to the constitutional status held by the person in terms of expected standards of performance, functions, integrity and rectitude - All these expressions fall under different domains of jurisdiction and operate in distinct and different fields - Distinction, primarily, is that of degree, intent and expected standard required to be maintained in relation to the office that the delinquent holds - Onus of proof and severity of misconduct will be relatable to the office one is holding - Order accordingly.

(C) Constitution - Administrative - Supreme Court Rules, 1966 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 317(1) - Whether SC has jurisdiction to go into the merit or otherwise of the additional articles of charge? - It was contended that jurisdiction of SC in terms of Art. 317(1) is a limited jurisdiction and the Court has to conduct its inquiry and record its finding in the report only in relation to the articles of charge referred to by the President in exercise of its powers u/art. 317(1) - Held, SC can examine explanatory, incidental or intrinsically inter-connected charges to the charges mentioned in the Reference and in that respect can take note of additional facts, take evidence and examine the entire record before it; but where the charges, apparently and in substance, are new and do not appear to be justifiable on the basis of the record and there is no direct evidence to substantiate them, it may not be in the ends of justice that SC should examine those articles of charge in the present inquiry - In the present case, approved articles of charge were totally independent and are not incidental or explanatory to the articles of charge specified in the Presidential Reference - Hence, alleged malicious acts of influencing the subordinates to carry out the intended manipulations to favour desired persons and abuse of the public office and defending the illegal acts referred to in approved article 6 of the charges, therefore, cannot be gone into by SC - Order accordingly.

(D) Constitution - Administrative - Supreme Court Rules, 1966 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 317(1) - Haryana Public Service Commission (Commission) was constituted by the Governor of State of Haryana - During its tenure, the Commission had made selections and recommended candidates for appointment to various posts in different cadres of the State - Subsequently, it came to the notice of the Government that various irregularities and illegalities, such as acts of favouritism, discrimination and violation of rules/regulations had been committed by the Commission in the process of selection made by them - After conducting preliminary enquiries, the Government initiated vigilance enquiries as well as First Information Reports were registered for the alleged irregularities, illegalities and acts of commissions and omissions by the Chairman and Members of the Commission - Matter was referred to the President of India for removal of the Chairman and Members of the Commission in terms of art. 317(1) of the Constitution - President, after examining the records, referred the matter to SC u/art. 317(1) of the Constitution for inquiry and report, as to whether the existing Chairman and Members of the Commission ought to be removed from the office on the alleged grounds of misbehaviour - Only three articles of charge were stated in the Presidential Reference - However, during the pendency of the matter before the Court and because of subsequent events the State Government filed additional articles of charge - (A) Whether respondents not guilty of any misbehaviour as contemplated u/art. 317(1) as it was alleged that qualifications, experience and stature were not of the required standard for appointment to their respective constitutional offices? - Held, provisions of Art. 316 of the Constitution do not lay down any qualification, educational or otherwise, for appointment to the Commission as Chairman/Member - Similarly, no specific experience of any number of years is required for appointment as Non- official Member - Hence, Constitutional provisions do not provide any qualification for Official/Non-official Members - Government failed to prove Charge 1 - Respondents not guilty of any misbehaviour as contemplated u/art. 317(1) on this count - (B) Whether conduct of the Chairman/Members of the Commission amounts to misbehaviour to justify their removal in terms of art. 317(1) of the Constitution? - Approved articles of charge 3, 5, 8 and 9 pertained to alleged acts, omissions and commissions amounting to misbehaviour have been framed against all the respondents in the present inquiry - Held, condition precedent to an order being passed by the President of India, removing the Chairman/Member of the Commission from office, is a report of the Supreme Court to the President recording a finding after inquiry that the Chairman/Member ought to be removed from office on the ground of misbehaviour - In the present case, expected standards of performance and functions was clearly been infringed by the private respondents - Further, withholding of record and non cooperative attitude adopted by the then Chairman/Members of the Commission, were not for bona fide reasons and, much less, to protect the constitutional stature of the Commission - Moreover, there was definite non-cooperation on the part of the Chairman/Members of the Commission in furnishing record and documents to the investigating agencies - Further, claim of privilege for non-production of documents lacks bona fide and was, primarily, intended to withhold the records from the Investigating Agencies to cover up the above misdeeds, irregularities and illegalities - Hence approved articles of charge 2 to 5, 8 and 9 stand established and private respondents were guilty of misbehaviour on these counts - Thus, there exist justifiable grounds for removal of the private respondents from their respective offices in terms of art. 317(1) of the Constitution - Presidential Reference answered accordingly - Order accordingly.
