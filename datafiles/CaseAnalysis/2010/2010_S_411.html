Rameshbhai Mohanbhai Koli and others v State of Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

20 October 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, A.R. Dave

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 878; (2011) 11 SCC 111; 2010 ALL MR (Cri) 3968; 2011 (1) G.L.R. 860; JT 2010 (11) SC 605; 2010 (4) MLJ(Crl) 495; 2010 (4) RCR(Criminal) 807; 2010(11) SCALE 120

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Bombay Police Act, 1951, s. 135 - Appellants allegedly murdered the deceased - Trial Court convicted the appellants for murder - Appellants filed appeal - HC confirmed the Trial Court order - Hence the present appeal - (A) Whether HC was justified in confirming the conviction and sentence imposed by the Trial Court when all the eye-witnesses did not support the case of the prosecution as against appellants - Held, all the eye-witnesses examined on the prosecution side had en bloc turned hostile due to influence and pressure of the appellants which included a sitting MLA of the ruling party - However, prosecution was successful in bringing home the guilt of the appellants for the commission of murder and the eye-witnesses turning hostile, did not, in any manner, create a dent in the case of the prosecution - Therefore, conclusion of conviction arrived at by the Trial Court and approved by the HC is justifiable - (B) Whether the Courts below are justified in convicting and awarding life sentence based on circumstantial evidence - Held, panch witnesses confirmed the contents of panchnamas in their oral testimony before the Court - Panch witnesses also asserted that appellants had shown their willingness and on that basis, the preliminary panchnama was drawn and thereafter, the appellants had taken the panchas and the police personnel at the place where they had concealed the weapons used for the crime and recovered the weapons from those places - Material evidence of discovery of weapons through proper panchnamas was sufficient to connect the appellants with the crime - Serological report showed that the recovered material objects were found to be stained with blood of deceased - All the materials and the evidence of panchas and circumstances, unmistakenly lead to the conclusion that appellants were the culprits and the complicity for commission of murder of the deceased was proved and said aspects were fully discussed by the Trial Court and rightly affirmed by the HC - Evidence in the present case convincingly established that the respective places from where the recoveries were effected were exclusively within the knowledge of the appellants and the same could not have been effected by the investigating agency in the absence of the disclosure statements made by the appellants - Circumstances were highly incriminating and conclusively established the case against the appellants - Therefore, Courts below are justified in convicting and awarding life sentence based on circumstantial evidence - Impugned conviction and sentence by the Trial Court which was affirmed by the HC is upheld - Appeal dismissed.
