Dharnidhar and others v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

08 July 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Swatanter Kumar, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 494; (2010) 7 SCC 759; JT 2010 (7) SC 411; 2011 (1) MLJ(Crl) 132; 2010 (4) RCR(Criminal) 291; 2010(7) SCALE 12; [2010] 8 S.C.R. 173; 2010 (2) UC 1196

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: (A) Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 302 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 313 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellants allegedly shot the deceased persons to death - Trial Court convicted appellants u/s. 302 of IPC - Appellants challenged Trial Court order before the HC - HC dismissed appellant's appeal - Whether conviction by the Trial Court is sustainable - Held, eye witnesses were present at the place of occurrence and had actually seen the occurrence - Statements of the eye witnesses were duly supported by the medical evidence, as well as by the statements of the investigating officers - Though the eye witnesses were the interested witnesses, their statements can be safely relied upon - Motive for commission of offence was present and prosecution could prove that motive beyond doubt - Report of the Chemical Examiner and Serologist certified that the weapon used for the offence contained human blood - Recovery of the weapons and the fact that human blood was traced on the recovered weapon completes the chain of events relating to the commission of the crime - Further, statement of the appellant u/s. 131 of the CrPC before the Court, to some extent, falls in line with the case of the prosecution and to that extent, the case of the prosecution can be substantiated and treated as correct by the Court - Statements of the eye-witnesses, read in conjunction with the documents filed on record, expert evidence, recovery of weapons and blood stained earth, clearly established beyond reasonable doubt, the guilt of the appellants - Appellants constituted a common unlawful assembly and were carrying weapons with an intention to commit an offence - Appellants had knowledge and intention in mind that they were going to kill the deceased persons, as was evident from the evidence on record - Appellants committed the murder with the furtherance of criminal intention - Therefore, there is no legal infirmity in the judgments of Trial Court and HC - Appeals dismissed.

(B) Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 313 - Statement of accused before the Court u/s. 313 - Scope and evidentiary value - Held, statement made by the accused u/s. 313 of the CrPC can be used by the Court to the extent that it is in line with the case of the prosecution, however, the same cannot be the sole basis for convicting an accused - S. 313 provides an opportunity to the accused to explain the circumstances appearing against him and provides an opportunity to the Court to examine the accused and to elicit an explanation from him, which may be free from the fear of being trapped for an embarrassing admission or statement - Proper methodology to be adopted by the Court while recording the statement of the accused u/s. 313 of the CrPC is to invite the attention of the accused to the circumstances and substantial evidence in relation to the offence, for which he has been charged and invite his explanation - Accused has to avail of that opportunity and if he fails to do so then it is for the Court to examine the case of the prosecution on its evidence with reference to the statement made by the accused u/s. 313 of the CrPC - Appeals dismissed.
