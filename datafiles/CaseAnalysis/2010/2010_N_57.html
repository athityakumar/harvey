Shakti Devi v New India Insurance Co. Ltd. & Anr

Supreme Court of India

09 November 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 942; 2011 ACJ 15; 2011 (1) AWC 261; 2011 (1) JhrCR(SC) 1; JT 2010 (12) SC 106; 2010 (4) RCR(Civil) 950; 2010(11) SCALE 571; 2010 (4) WLN(SC) 57

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Insurance - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, s. 166 - Quantum of compensation - Appellant filed a claim petition u/s. 166 of 1988 Act claiming compensation for the death of her son (who died in the motor accident) in the sum of Rs. 2 Lakhs from the owners and insurers of the two vehicles - Insurance company contest the claim petition - Tribunal pegged the earning of the deceased at Rs. 1000/- per month and after deducting personal expenses to the extent of 1/3rd, fixed the annual dependency at Rs. 7920/- - Tribunal applied the multiplier of 8 and held that the compensation so computed would come to Rs. 63,360/- - Thereafter, tribunal then made it a round figure of Rs. 60,000/- and after adjusting Rs. 25,000/- which was paid to the claimant towards no- fault liability held that the claimant was entitled to a further sum of Rs. 35,000/- and awarded her simple interest @ 10% p.a. - HC dismissed appeal filed against said order - Appellant filed appeal against said order contending that the multiplier of 18 should have been applied keeping in view the age of the deceased - Whether order of the tribunal as confirmed by the HC could be upheld? - Held, in a case where the age of the claimant is higher than the age of the deceased, the age of claimant and not the age of the deceased has to be taken into account for the capitalization of the lost dependency - It is so because the choice of multiplier is determined by the age of the deceased or that of the claimant, whichever is higher - The age of the claimant on the date of the accident would be about 54-55 years - Hence, as per the table prepared in Sarla Verma and Others v. Delhi Transport Corporation and Another 2009 INDLAW SC 488, the multiplier of 11 would, therefore, be applicable - By multiplying the annual loss of dependency (Rs.12000/-) with the multiplier of 11, the claimant becomes entitled to the compensation in the sum of Rs. 1,32,000/- - The compensation determined by the Tribunal at Rs. 60,000/- and confirmed by the High Court in the appeal is manifestly erroneous and is enhanced to Rs. 1,32,000/- - Appeal allowed.
