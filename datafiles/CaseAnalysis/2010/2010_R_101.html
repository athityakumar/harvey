Rasid Javed & Ors. v State Of U.P. & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

05 July 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 510; (2010) 7 SCC 781; AIR 2010 SC 2275; 2010 (5) ALD(SC) 71; 2010 (5) AWC 4375; JT 2010 (7) SC 285; 2010 (3) RCR(Civil) 696; 2010(6) SCALE 227; [2010] 7 S.C.R. 535

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Keywords: Nationalization

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1939, ss. 68-C and 68-D - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, ss. 102(1) and 102(2) - Inter-State route - Validity of notification - On February 26, 1959, a draft scheme was published u/s. 68-C of the Motor Vehicles Act, 1939 in respect of the inter-State route viz; Saharanpur-Delhi proposing to authorize the State Transport Undertaking (STU) of Uttar Pradesh to operate stage carriages on the said route to the exclusion of all other operators - On September 29, 1959 the State Government approved the said draft scheme and published it under sub-section (3) of s. 68-D of 1939 Act ('the 1959 Scheme') - A group of writ petitions, one by 32 operators and the other by 18 operators was filed questioning the validity of the 1959 Scheme - HC directed the State Government not to enforce the 1959 Scheme against 50 operators who had filed the first batch of writ petitions and it was directed that the State Government should hold a fresh enquiry into the question whether the scheme should be approved or not - Thereafter, writ petitions were filed by Ram Krishna Verma and others challenging the grant of permits for Saharanpur to Ghaziabad via Shahdara route were dismissed - On appeal, SC in Ram Krishna Verma and Ors. v. State of U.P. & Ors., 1992 INDLAW SC 949, held that the nationalization of Saharanpur - Delhi route by the 1959 Scheme is operative to the total exclusion of every operator except UPSRTC and 50 operators whose objections were upheld by the HC - SC quashed the permits granted to the private operators - After decision of SC in Ram Krishna Verma, the competent authority approved the Scheme and directed the same to be published - On May 29, 1993, the approved scheme ('the 1993 Scheme') was published in the Gazette - In the Notification published on April 16, 1999 u/s. 102(1) of the Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, the State Government proposed to modify the 1993 Scheme - Objections were invited - Objections heard - Hearing Authority proposed modification be approved, i.e. private operators be allowed to ply their vehicles - State Government, however, issued another Notification dated 15-04-1999 u/s. 102 of 1988 Act r/w. s. 21 of General Clauses Act, 1897 and thereby rescinded the earlier Notification dated 16-04-1999 - Writ petitions challenging Notification were dismissed and the same is in challenge - (A) Effect of publication of a scheme u/s. 68D - Held, once a scheme is published u/s. 68-D in relation to any area or route or portion thereof, whether to exclusion, complete or partial of other persons or otherwise, no person other than STU may operate on notified area or notified route except as provided in scheme itself - (B) Status of appellants' permits - Permits granted to appellants related to routes which overlapped Delhi-Saharanpur notified route - Held, Saharanpur-Delhi route on its nationalization stood frozen under the 1959 Scheme against everyone except 50 operators - By the 1993 Scheme, Saharanpur-Delhi route stood frozen against 50 operators as well - Effect of two schemes (1959 Scheme and 1993 Scheme) has been that entire Saharanpur-Delhi route became fully nationalized for exclusive operation by STU i.e., UPSRTC and no private operator could operate on said route - Consequent upon decision of SC in case of Ram Krishna Verma, 1992 INDLAW SC 949 and settled legal position that RTA cannot either renew permit of such private operators or give any fresh permit in respect of a route which overlaps notified route, appellants' permits stood cancelled - In this view, whole exercise undertaken by State Government under sub-section (1) of s. 102 of 1988 Act proposing to modify 1993 Scheme relating to Saharanpur-Delhi notified route was misconceived as permits specified in that Notification did not exist in law - (C) Cancellation or modification of scheme - It was submitted that decision u/s. 102(1) of 1988 Act has to be by same Authority who heard objections and there could not be divided responsibility of a quasi judicial act - Whether order passed by Hearing Authority is order of State Government u/s. 102(1) and 102(2) of 1988 Act? - Held, No - A delegatee must confine his activity within four corners of powers invested in him and if he acted beyond that, his action cannot have any legal sanction unless ratified by delegator - A distinction must be maintained where hearing authority is empowered by State Government to hear objections and approve proposed modification or modify approved scheme and a case where hearing authority is authorized to hear objections/representations relating to proposed modification to approved scheme - In latter case, authority delegated to Hearing Authority is limited and he is not authorized to approve proposed modification or modify approved scheme - Notification dated 16-04-1999 does not empower Hearing Authority to approve or modify scheme but only to hear objections - Present case falls in latter category and accordingly order of Hearing Authority is in excess of authority given to him and cannot be construed as a final order of approval u/s. 102(1) of 1988 Act - (D) Invocation of S. 21 of General Clauses Act - Whether valid? - Held, what s. 21 of General Clauses Act requires is that authority empowered to issue notification must exercise its power to rescind such notification in like manner - In view of fact that order of Hearing Authority is in excess of authority given to it and that said order has no legal effect, there can be no impediment for State Government in exercising its power u/s. 102 of 1988 Act r/w s. 21 of General Clauses Act, 1897 to rescind Notification - Since order of Hearing Authority is not an order of approval u/s. 102(1) of 1988 Act and cannot be treated as such, power of State Government to rescind Notification did not get exhausted - Order of Hearing Authority cannot be treated as an order of State Government u/s. 102(1) of 1988 Act - Notification is valid and does not suffer from any legal flaw - Appeals dismissed.
