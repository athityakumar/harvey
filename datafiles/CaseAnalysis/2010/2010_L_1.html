Tamil Nadu Housing Board v L. Chandrasekaran and others

Supreme Court of India

29 January 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 68; (2010) 2 SCC 786; 2010 (2) ALD(SC) 148; 2010 (79) ALR 241; 2010 (1) CLT(SC) 223; JT 2010 (1) SC 647; 2010 (1) RCR(Civil) 950; 2010 (110) RD 310; 2010(1) SCALE 701; 2010 (1) UPLBEC 649

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Tamil Nadu Land Acquisition Rules, Tamil Nadu Land Acquisition (Amendment) Act, 1997

Summary: Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4, 4(1), 48-B, 6, 6(1) - Tamil Nadu Land Acquisition (Amendment) Act, 1997 - Tamil Nadu Land Acquisition Rules, r. 3(b) - Acquisition - Public purpose - Not Complied - Reconveyance - Land of respondents was acquired by Govt. for Ambattur Neighborhood Scheme - Respondents were paid compensation in lieu of their land - Respondent filed writ petition for issue of direction to appellant-Board to certify that acquired land was no longer needed for purpose for which it was acquired - Single Judge allowed writ petition and held that petitioner was entitled to get no objection certificate - DB held that writ petitioners were entitled to make representation for reconveyance of acquired land in accordance with s. 48-B of the Act - As direction given by DB, respondent submitted representation to Secretary, Housing and Urban Development Department, that land may be reconveyed to him in terms of s. 48-B of the Act - After considering report of Board, State Govt. rejected representation of respondent - Respondent filed Writ Petition and prayed that State Govt. may be directed to release his land because same had not been utilized for purpose enumerated in notification issued u/s. 4 of the Act - Single Judge dismissed both writ petitions and held that in terms of s. 48-B of the Act, Govt. was empowered to decide whether acquired land was no longer required for purpose for which it was acquired or for any other public purpose and decision taken in that regard cannot be nullified by Court unless it was shown to be totally arbitrary or malafide - Respondents challenged orders of Single Judge in Writ Appeal - DB did not find any illegality in decision taken by Govt. or error in orders passed by Single Judge, but allowed writ appeals by simply relying upon order dated 18.2.2000 passed in Writ Appeal No.2430/1999 and directed appellant-Board to reconvey land to respondents subject to their depositing amount of compensation together with interest - Hence, instant appeal - Whether DB was justified in allowing writ appeal simply relying upon order passed in Writ Appeal No.2430/1999 and directed appellant-Board to reconvey land to respondents subject to their depositing amount of compensation together with interest - Held, direction given by DB to appellant-Board to reconvey acquired land to respondents was per se against plain language of s. 48-B of the Act in terms of which only Govt. could transfer acquired land if it was satisfied that same was not required for purpose for which it was acquired or for any other public purpose - Appellant-Board was not an authority competent to transfer acquired land to original owner -Therefore, DB could not have issued mandamus to appellant-Board to reconvey acquired land to respondents - If acquired land had already been transferred to other agency, Govt. could not exercise power u/s. 48-B of the Act and reconvey same to original owner - Thus, Govt. could not be compelled to reconvey land to original owner if same can be utilized for any public purpose other than one for which it was acquired - Therefore, HC clearly erred in proceeding as if scheme stood abandoned - Impugned judgment was set aside and orders passed by Single Judge in writ petitions filed by respondents were restored - Appeals allowed.
