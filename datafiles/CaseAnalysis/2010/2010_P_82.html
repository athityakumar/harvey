Municipal Committee, Hoshiarpur v Punjab State Electricity Board and others

Supreme Court of India

19 October 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 935; (2010) 13 SCC 216; JT 2010 (11) SC 615; 2011 (1) MLJ(SC) 730; 2010 (4) RCR(Civil) 859; 2010(11) SCALE 69

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Electricity & Energy

Summary: Electricity & Energy - Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, ss. 100, 103 - Revised electricity bill - Demand - Justifiability - Appellant/Municipal Committee took electricity connection from the respondent/State Electricity Board for running a tube well for public - Appellant was regularly paying the bill, however, respondent raised an extra bill/revised bill to the tune of an exorbitant amount - Appellant filed a suit against respondent challenging the revised bill - Respondent contended that connection had not been made properly at the initial stage, therefore, meter was showing only 1/3rd of the actual consumption but once the connection was made properly reading of the meter jumped to three times - Trial Court decreed the suit in favour of appellant holding that defect was due to the negligence of the respondent, and the appellant could not be burdened for the appellant's negligence - First Appellate Court, in appeal, confirmed the Trial Court order - Respondent filed second appeal before the HC - HC reversed the lower court orders - Hence the present appeal - Whether HC committed a grave error in deciding the second appeal without meeting the mandatory requirement of s. 100 or s. 103 CPC - Held, a second appeal lies only on a substantial question of law and it is necessary to formulate a substantial question of law before the second appeal is decided - HC reached the conclusion that prior to correct the connection, the meter was recording only 1/3rd of the total energy consumed, which is factually incorrect - First Appellate Court concurred with the findings of the fact by the Trial Court - However, the HC, without framing a substantial question of law and without making any reference to s. 103 CPC, decided against the appellant by merely placing reliance on a meter reading chart produced by the respondent - Two courts below had correctly understood and appreciated the contents of the said chart and the HC had interfered with the concurrent findings of fact in a most casual and cavalier manner and such a course was totally unwarranted and uncalled for - HC committed a grave error in considering the findings of fact recorded by the courts below to be perverse - Further, cl. 23 of the Conditions of Supply of respondent/Board, stipulated that respondent/Board must issue a show cause notice to the consumer before the issuance of a revised bill - However, no prior intimation of checking was given to the appellant, nor was any responsible officer present at the time of checking - A copy of the checking report/chart was not given to the appellant for filing of objections nor was any show cause notice given along with the demand notice - Thus, present case is a clear cut case of violation of the principles of natural justice as well as of cl. 23 of the Conditions of Supply - Further, no check meter was ever installed and thus, it could not be held that the meter did not record the quantity of energy actually consumed - Therefore, Trial Court as well as the First Appellate Court had considered all factual and legal issues involved in the present case - While deciding the case, the courts below had appreciated the relevant evidence including the chart prepared, and so heavily relied upon by the respondent/Board, in correct perspective - As the respondent/Board did not install a check meter, the readings shown by the meter after correction of the connection could not be held to be correct - Thus, the revised bill could not be held to be showing the correct quantity of energy actually consumed by the appellant - In such a fact-situation, there was no occasion for the HC to decide the second appeal without framing the substantial question of law - Impugned judgment and decree of HC is set aside - Appeal allowed.
