Raj Kumar Shivhare v Assistant Director, Directorate Of Enforcement And Another

Supreme Court of India

12 April 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Ganguly, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 274; (2010) 4 SCC 772; AIR 2010 SC 2239; 2010 (253) E.L.T. 3; JT 2010 (4) SC 54; 2010 (4) MLJ(SC) 964; 2010 (3) RCR(Civil) 483; 2010(3) SCALE 705; [2010] 4 S.C.R. 608

Case Digest

Subject: Exchange Control & FDI; Fema; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Family Courts (Amendment) Act, 1991, Trustee Extension Act, 1852, Debt Recovery Tribunal Act, Arbitration Act, 1975, Bengal Chamber of Commerce, Rules

Summary: (A) FEMA - Exchange Control & FDI - Foreign Exchange Management Act, 1999, s. 35 -Statutory remedy - Non-avail of - Appellants allegedly received unauthorised payments under the instructions from persons living outside India - 1st respondent/Enforcement Directorate imposed penalty on appellant - Appellant filed an appeal accompanied with an application for dispensation of pre-deposit of penalty before the Appellate Tribunal - Appellate Tribunal dismissed the application and posted the appeal for hearing - Appellant challenged the Appellate Tribunal order before HC under art. 226 of the Constitution - HC dismissed appellant's writ petition on the ground of lack of territorial jurisdiction - Whether Appellate Tribunal order can be appealable to the HC under art. 226 of the Constitution - Held, any 'order' or 'decision' of the Appellate Tribunal would mean all decisions or orders of the Appellate Tribunal and all such decisions or orders are, subject to limitation, appealable to the HC on a question of law u/s. 35 of the Act - When a statutory forum is created by law for redressal of grievance and that too in a fiscal Statute, a writ petition should not be entertained ignoring the statutory dispensation - HC is a statutory forum of appeal on a question of law u/s. 35 of the Act and such statutory jurisdiction of HC should not be abdicated and given a go bye by a litigant for invoking the forum of judicial review of the HC under writ jurisdiction - HC fell into a manifest error by not appreciating the main aspect of the matter, however, it has dismissed the writ petition on the ground of lack of territorial jurisdiction - Even if HC had territorial jurisdiction it should not have entertained a writ petition which impugns an order of the Tribunal when such an order on a question of law, is appealable before the HC u/s. 35 of the Act - Therefore, liberty granted to the appellant to file statutory appeal before the jurisdictional HC u/s. 35 of the Act - Appeal dismissed.

(B) Practice & Procedure - Right of appeal - Interpretation - Scope of - Held, right of appeal, being always a creature of a Statute, its nature, ambit and width has to be determined from the Statute itself - When the language of the Statute regarding the nature of the order from which right of appeal has been conferred is clear, no statutory interpretation is warranted either to widen or restrict the same - Appeal dismissed.
