D.R. Rathna Murthy v Ramappa

Supreme Court of India

08 October 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 850; (2011) 1 SCC 158; 2011 (1) ALT 58; 2011 (2) AWC 1162; JT 2010 (11) SC 398; 2011 (4) MLJ(SC) 548; 2011 (2) MPLJ 507; 2011 (2) RCR(Civil) 414; 2010(10) SCALE 625; [2010] 12 S.C.R. 755

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Keywords: Conditional Sale

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Registration Act, 1908, s. 20 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 100 - Karnataka Registration Rules, 1965, r. 42 - Specific performance of contract - Appellant sold the land in question vide registered sale deed to the respondent and delivered the possession to him - Subsequently, appellant served a legal notice upon the respondent demanding the reconveyance of the suit property on the ground that registered sale deed executed in favour of respondent was a conditional sale deed and appellant had a right to repurchase the sale land for the same consideration within a period of ten years from the date of execution of the sale deed - Thereafter, appellant filed suit seeking specific performance - Respondent contended that there was an absolute sale deed in his favour and it was not a conditional sale deed, the term of reconveyance had been fraudulently inserted by the appellant after the execution of the document - Suit was dismissed - On appeal, First Appellate Court held that it was a conditional sale deed, thus, directed the respondent to execute the sale deed in favour of the appellant - Second appeal filed before HC was allowed - Hence, present appeal - Whether sale executed by the appellant in favour of the respondent was a conditional sale deed or absolute deed? - Held, r. 42 of the Rules mandatorily requires that if there is any interlineation, erasure, alteration etc., it must be mentioned and described at the foot of the document and must be duly signed by the executant before the document is accepted for registration - In the present case, word "Avadhi" was inserted in the margin of the sale deed and it was not attested by the executant - Manner in which interlineations have been made in the document itself reveal that addition was made subsequent to the execution of the document - Therefore, it was clear that such insertion was made to convert the absolute sale deed into a conditional sale deed - Moreover, provisions of r. 42 of the Rules were not complied with - Thus, the additions so made by the appellant cannot be binding on the respondent - Appeal dismissed.
