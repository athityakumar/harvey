Project Officer, IRDP and others v P. D. Chacko

Supreme Court of India

11 May 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 387; (2010) 6 SCC 637; AIR 2010 SC 2626; 2010 (2) KLT 720; 2010(5) SCALE 597; [2010] 6 S.C.R. 846; 2010 (3) SLR 569

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Kerala Public Services Act, 1968

Summary: Service - Kerala Service Rules, 1959, r. 60(b) - Continuity of service up to 60 years of age - Entitlement - Respondent worked as a full time menial in an aided school up to 9-4-1976 and subsequently, resigned from the post and joined as a Peon in a government department - Respondent raised a claim that he was entitled to continue in service up to 60 years of age as per r. 60(b) of the Rules since he had service in an aided school - Appellant rejected respondent's claim - Respondent challenged appellant's rejection before HC - Single Judge allowed respondent's writ petition directing the appellant to confer the benefit of r. 60(b) on respondent - Appellant filed appeal before the DB - DB dismissed the appeal - Whether the respondent who was in service of an aided school as on 7-4-1970, was entitled to continue in service till he attained 60 years of age as per r. 60(b) of the Rules - Held, in order to get benefit of r. 60(b), concerned government servant must have been in last grade service as on 7-4-1970 and continues to be in that last grade service as per the specifications of Special Rules for the Kerala Last Grade Service, 1996 as defined in r. 12 (16A) of the Rules -Respondent failed to produce any documents to show that the post he was holding i.e., full time menial in an aided school was included in the categories of posts in the Special Rules for Last Grade Service nor did he show that he had continued to be in the last grade service as defined in r. 12(16A) of the Rules as on 7-4-1970 - Therefore, respondent is not legally entitled to any benefit u/r. 60(b) of the Rules, however, salary, if any, paid to the respondent beyond his superannuation age of 55 years, cannot be recovered - Impugned HC judgment is set aside - Appeal allowed.
