The District Collector, Srikakulam And Others v Bagathi Krishna Rao And Another

Supreme Court of India

02 June 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, Swatanter Kumar

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 426; (2010) 6 SCC 427; AIR 2010 SC 2617; 2010 (4) ALD(SC) 115; 2010 (81) ALR 247; 2010 (4) AWC 3874; JT 2010 (6) SC 227; 2010 (8) MLJ(SC) 575; 2010 (3) RCR(Civil) 318; 2010 (2) RCR(Rent) 1; 2010(6) SCALE 146B; [2010] 7 S.C.R. 280; 2010 (3) UC 1610

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Keywords: Application For Impleadment, Bihar Land Reforms Act

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 79 - Non-joinder of necessary party - Respondents filed suit for seeking declaration of title and possession of the suit land and for other consequential relief, i.e. permanent injunction - Appellants filed written statement contending that the suit land being forest land had vested in the State of Andhra Pradesh - Trial Court decreed the suit - Appeal filed by appellants was dismissed - Second appeal filed thereagainst was also dismissed - Hence, present appeal - Whether appeal in the form it had been presented before the HC could be entertained without State of Andhra Pradesh being the appellant party? - Held, s. 79 of the CPC specifically deals with suits by and against the Government and provides that in suits by and against the Government, the authority to be impleaded as the plaintiff or defendant, would be the Union of India or Central Government or the State or State Government - In the present case, relief sought by the respondents was declaration of title in respect of the suit land which according to the appellants was in favour of the State of Andhra Pradesh and in physical possession of the Forest Department - In view of the above, State of Andhra Pradesh was necessary party - Thus, the second appeal filed by the officials was not maintainable - Judgment and order of the HC set aside - Matter remanded to the HC to decide afresh after framing the substantial question of law - Appellants permitted to file an application for impleadment of the State of Andhra Pradesh as appellant - Appeal allowed.
