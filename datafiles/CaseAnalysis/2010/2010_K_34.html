Kanwar Natwar Singh and another v Director Of Enforcement & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

05 October 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

B. SUDARSHAN REDDY, Surinder Singh Nijjar

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 838; (2010) 13 SCC 255; AIR 2010 SC (Supp) 9; [2010] 160 Comp Cas 301; 2010 (262) E.L.T. 15; [2011] 330 ITR 374; JT 2010 (11) SC 282; 2010 (8) MLJ(SC) 983; 2010 (4) RCR(Criminal) 813; 2010(10) SCALE 401

Case Digest

Subject: Fema

Keywords: Human Rights Act, 1998, Criminal Justice Act, 2003, Criminal Procedure and Investigations Act, 1996, Foreign Exchange Management Bill, 1998

Summary: FEMA - Foreign Exchange Management (Adjudication Proceedings and Appeal) Rules, 2000, r. 4(1) - Foreign Exchange Management Act, 1999, s. 16(3) - Natural justice - Non-supply of documents - A complaint in writing has been filed by an officer authorized against the appellants u/s. 16(3) alleging that the appellants along with others, jointly and severally, without general or special permission of the Reserve Bank of India dealt in and acquired Foreign Exchange totalling US $ 8,98,027.79 in respect of two oil contracts with 'X' - Adjudicating Authority issued a notice to the appellants requiring them to show cause why an inquiry should not be held against them - Appellants having received the show cause notice, instead of submitting their reply, requested the Adjudicating Authority to furnish 'copies of all the documents in possession in respect of the instant case, including the 83000 documents allegedly procured by one 'Y' from USA in connection with the instant case - Adjudicating Authority, however, declined to furnish copies of other documents and decided to hold an inquiry in accordance with the provisions of FEMA and the Rules - Aggrieved by the said communications sent by the Authority, appellant no. 1 filed writ petition before HC who dismissed said writ petition - Hence, present appeal - Whether a noticee served with show cause notice u/r. 4(1) of the Rules, 2000 is entitled to demand to furnish all the documents in possession of the Adjudicating Authority including those documents upon which no reliance has been placed to issue a notice requiring him to show cause why an inquiry should not be held against him? - Held, no - Even the principles of natural justice do not require supply of documents upon which no reliance has been placed by the Authority to set the law into motion - No Court can compel the Authority to deviate from the statute and exercise the power in altogether a different manner than the prescribed one - As noticed, a reasonable opportunity of being heard is to be provided by the Adjudicating Authority in the manner prescribed for the purpose of imposing any penalty and not at the stage where the Adjudicating Authority is required merely to decide as to whether an inquiry at all be held into the matter - In present case, appellants insisted for supply of all documents in possession of the Authority and such demand is based on vague, indefinite and irrelevant grounds - The appellants are not sure as to whether they are asking for the copies of the documents in possession of the Adjudicating Authority or in possession of authorized officer who lodged the complaint - The only object in making such demand is obviously to obstruct the proceedings and the appellants, to some extent, have been able to achieve their object as is evident from the fact that the inquiry initiated as early as in the year 2006 still did not even commence - Hence, Adjudicating Authority directed to make inquiry into the allegations made in the complaint strictly in accordance with the law - Appeals dismissed.
