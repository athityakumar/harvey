Baij Nath Sah v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

29 April 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

H.S. Bedi, Chandramauli Kumar Prasad

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 362; (2010) 6 SCC 736; 2010 CRLJ 3821; 2010 (3) JCC 2086; 2010 (3) JhrCR(SC) 105; JT 2010 (5) SC 398; 2010 (2) OLR 255; 2010 (3) RCR(Criminal) 434; 2010(5) SCALE 593; [2010] 5 S.C.R. 619

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 164 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 366A - Statement u/s. 164 of CrPC - Evidentiary value of - Appellants were charged for an offence u/s.366-A of IPC for having kidnapped the minor girl 'S'- Trial Court convicted the accused - On appeal, Single Judge altered the conviction from one u/s. 366-A to s. 363 of the IPC and reduced the sentence of the appellants - Present appeal - Whether Courts below correct in convicting appellant only on the basis of statement u/s. 164 of the CrPC made by 'S' before the Magistrate? - It was contended that this statement was inadmissible in evidence but even if taken into account did not involve or implicate the appellant in any manner - Held, relied on SC judgment in Ram Kishan Singh vs. Harmit Kaur and Another 1971 INDLAW SC 547 wherein it was held that a statement of s. 164 CrPC is not substantive evidence and can be utilized only to corroborate or contradict the witness vis-a-vis. statement made in Court - In the present case, 'S' was not produced as a witness - Therefore, her statement u/s. 164 of CrPC cannot be used against the appellant - Further, allegation against appellant was that after she had been kidnapped by the other accused she had been brought to their home, where the appellant was also present - Hence, when she had been brought to the appellant's home the kidnapping had already taken place - Appellant could therefore not be implicated in the offence u/ss. 363 or 366-A of the IPC de hors other evidence to show his involvement in the events preceding the kidnapping - Appellant acquitted - Appeal allowed.
