R. Jayarama And Others v State Of Kerala And Others

Supreme Court of India

29 November 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 1030; 2010 (5) ESC 713; 2011 (128) FLR 338; JT 2010 (13) SC 124; 2010(12) SCALE 563

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service

Keywords: Kerala State and Subordinate Services Rules, 1958, A.P. State and Subordinate Services Rules

Summary: Service - Constitution - Kerala State and Subordinate Services Rules, 1958, r. 17A - Seniority list - Government of Kerala by order dt. 18.11.1974 prescribed that 50% of the posts of Sub Inspectors in the District Armed Reserve will be filled up by direct recruitment - Appellants filed writ petition seeking direction to respondent nos. 1 and 2, to give seniority to direct recruits including themselves based on the advice and appointment by pushing down the promotees including respondent No.3 below the appellants working out the ratio prescribed in the Government Order dt. 18.11.1974 - HC held that the seniority of respondent no. 3 above the appellants is illegal - HC also held that the order retaining persons including the appellant in service cannot operate retrospectively to adversely affect the seniority of persons, who were already promoted before the date of its issue - Review petition filed against said order was dismissed by HC - Hence, present appeal - Whether order of the HC could be upheld? - Held, appellants were appointed as Reserve Sub Inspectors in the District Armed Reserve of the Kerala Police as per the advice of the Kerala State Public Service Commission - Said advice was made on 4.1.1993 by the Government to the PSC on the basis of interim order passed by HC - After dismissal of the main petition, interim order also gets vacated and after that appellants cannot claim any benefit based on said interim order - The seniority of the appellants have to be reckoned on the basis of actual availability of post - In fact, to avert the discharge of the appellants, the Government brought an order safeguarding their interest and the same was upheld by the DB by retaining the services of the appellant w.e.f. 17.06.1999 - Appeals dismissed.
