Nahar Singh Yadav & Anr. v Union Of India & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

19 November 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

D.K. Jain, V.S. Sirpurkar, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 977; (2011) 1 SCC 307; AIR 2011 SC 1549; 2011 CRLJ 997; 2011 (1) JhrCR(SC) 45; JT 2010 (12) SC 641; 2011 (1) RCR(Criminal) 120; 2010(12) SCALE 199

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: (A) Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 406 - Power u/s. 406 of the CrPC - Transfer of trial - Held, some of the broad factors which could be kept in mind while considering an application for transfer of the trial are: (i) when it appears that the State machinery or prosecution is acting hand in glove with the accused, and there is likelihood of miscarriage of justice due to the lackadaisical attitude of the prosecution; (ii) when there is material to show that the accused may influence the prosecution witnesses or cause physical harm to the complainant; (iii) comparative inconvenience and hardships likely to be caused to the accused, the complainant/the prosecution and the witnesses, besides the burden to be borne by the State Exchequer in making payment of travelling and other expenses of the official and non-official witnesses; (iv) a communally surcharged atmosphere, indicating some proof of inability of holding fair and impartial trial because of the accusations made and the nature of the crime committed by the accused; and (v) existence of some material from which it can be inferred that the some persons are so hostile that they are interfering or are likely to interfere either directly or indirectly with the course of justice - Order accordingly.

(B) Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120-B, 409, 420, 467, 468, 471 and 477-A - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 8, 9, 13(2), 13(1)(d) and 14 - Delhi Special Police Establishment Act, 1946, s. 6 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 406 - Ghaziabad P.F. Scam - Transfer of trial - Pursuant to and in furtherance of the findings of the Vigilance Department of the HC and subsequent authorisation given by the HC, case u/ss. 409, 420, 467, 468, 471, 477-A, 120-B of IPC and ss. 8, 9, 13(2) r/w ss. 13(1)(d) and 14 of the Act, 1988 was registered against then Central Nazir, District Court, Ghaziabad and 82 other accused persons which included 13 Class-III employees, 30 Class-IV employees of District Court, Ghaziabad and 39 outsiders - It was alleged that then Central Nazir in collusion with other accused named in the FIR fraudulently withdrew huge sums of money in the name of GPF of Class-IV employees of District Court, Ghaziabad - Subsequently, the present special leave petition was filed with a request to transfer the investigation of case to the CBI - During pendency of the proceedings, notification u/s. 6 of the Act, 1946 was issued for the transfer of case to the CBI - SC transferred the investigation to CBI - CBI filed final report and according to the report, a chargesheet u/s. 120-B r/w ss. 420, 467, 468 and 471 of the IPC and s. 13(2) r/w s. 13(1)(d) of the Act, 1988 against accused persons, as named in the chargesheet, filed in the Court of Special Judge, CBI, Ghaziabad - CBI stated that there was an immediate need to transfer the trial of the case to any other place outside the State of U.P - (A) Whether CBI which was the investigating agency can be said to be an interested party within the meaning of the s. 406(2) of the CrPC and entitled to move such an application for transfer of the trial? - Held, once a notification has been issued by a State Government u/s. 6 of the Act, 1946 the CBI assumes the role of an investigating agency and as the CBI is also invested with the role of the prosecuting agency in relation to that particular case, and therefore, entitled to move an application u/s. 406(2) of the CrPC - (B) Whether trial arising out of the chargesheet filed by the CBI deserves to be transferred from the Court of Special Judge, CBI at Ghaziabad to some other court of competent jurisdiction? - Held, prayer for transfer should be allowed only when there is a well-substantiated apprehension that justice will not be dispensed impartially, objectively and without any bias - This power has to be exercised cautiously and in exceptional situations, where it becomes necessary to do so to provide credibility to the trial - In the present case, apprehension entertained by the CBI that the trial of the case at Ghaziabad may not be fair, resulting in miscarriage of justice, was misplaced and cannot be accepted - Request rejected - Trial Court directed to proceed with the case expeditiously - Order accordingly.
