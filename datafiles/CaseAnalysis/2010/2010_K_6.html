A.P. Housing Board v K. Manohar Reddy & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

30 September 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Mukundakam Sharma, A.R. Dave

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 831; (2010) 12 SCC 707; 2011 (1) ALD(SC) 117; 2011 (2) AWC 1238; JT 2010 (11) SC 165; 2011 (1) RCR(Civil) 835; 2010(10) SCALE 363; [2010] 11 S.C.R. 1107

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Valuation Of Land

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 18, 23(1)(A) and 54 - Acquisition of land - Compensation - Enhancement of - Appellant's land was acquired by Government by issuing a notification u/s. 4(1) of Act, 1894 - Land Acquisition Officer thereafter, taking into consideration the sale transactions of adjoining lands for a period of 3 years prior to the publication of notification in question, passed an award determining the market value of the impugned land at Rs. 36,000/- per acre - Appellants being dissatisfied with the aforesaid award passed by the Land Acquisition Officer, sought for a reference u/s. 18 of the Act to the Civil Court claiming compensation at Rs. 100/- per square yard for the acquired land - District Court fixed the market value of the land acquired at Rs. 50/- per square yard and also awarded 30% solatium on the market value and a further sum of 12% additional market value in terms of the s. 23(1)(A) of the Act - On appeal, HC held that the appellants were entitled to compensation of Rs. 75/- per square yard for the acquired lands and then deducted 1/3rd from the said amount, which is Rs. 25/- per square yard, and consequently, held that the appellants would be entitled for payment of compensation at the rate of Rs. 50/- per square yard with other benefits - Hence, present appeal - Whether HC rightly held that appellants would be entitled for payment of compensation at the rate of Rs. 50/- per square yard with other benefits? - Held, HC interfered with the rate of market value fixed by the reference court and raised it to Rs. 75/- per square yard from Rs. 50/- per square yard as fixed by the reference court and thereafter deducted 1/3rd from the aforesaid rate towards the development charges - However, the HC has not given any reason, let alone cogent reasons, for increasing the aforesaid rate from Rs. 50/- to Rs. 75/- - Such a course was not permissible in view of the clear evidence which was relied upon and exhibited by the appellants themselves - Those were sale deeds which were executed in proximity to the date of acquisition and there is also evidence on record to indicate that the proposal for acquisition of the acquired land was initiated as on 12.10.1982 - Therefore, such an increase without any supporting reasons not valid and legal - Impugned order of HC set aside - Appeals allowed.
