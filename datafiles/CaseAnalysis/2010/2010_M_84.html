Kanpur Elect. Supply Co. Ltd. & Anr v M/S. L.M.L. Limited & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

07 May 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Cyriac Joseph, Chandramauli Kumar Prasad

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 352; (2010) 6 SCC 165; AIR 2010 SC 1946; 2010 (4) AWC 3809; 2010 (3) BC 134; 2010 (2) CCC 51; 2011 (1) DRTC 161; JT 2010 (5) SC 364; 2010 (8) MLJ(SC) 1057; 2010(5) SCALE 404; [2010] 6 S.C.R. 419

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Electricity & Energy

Keywords: U.P. Electricity Supply Code, 2005, Electricity Code, 2003, U.P. Industrial Undertaking (Special Provisions for Prevention of Unemployment) Act, 1966

Summary: Electricity & Energy - Corporate - U.P. Industrial Undertaking (Special Provisions for Prevention of Unemployment) Act, 1966 - U.P. Electricity Supply Code, 2005 - Electricity Act, 2003, s. 50 - Sick Industrial Companies (Special Provisions) Act, 1985, s. 22(3) - Interpretation of Clause 4.41 r/w Clause 4.49 of the Code - Reduction in contracted load - Respondent-Company owing to market fluctuations put a halt to its manufacturing activities and to make a representation to the State Government for declaring it to be a "Relief Undertaking" under the relevant provisions of the Act, 1966 - Responding to the said representation, the State Government issued a notification, suspending all contracts, agreements and other instruments in force for a period of one year leading to strikes and complete disruption of the work of the Respondent-Company, impelling the Respondent-Company to apply to the Petitioners for reduction of the contracted load from 8 MVA to 1.25 MVA from 01.04.2006 - Pursuant to said application, a Meeting was held on 19.04.2006 between the Managing Director of KESCO and the representatives of the Respondent-Company in which a decision was taken for reduction of the load with certain conditions - Respondent-Company was also declared as a "Sick Company" and an order was passed by BIFR u/s. 22(3) of SICA inter alia, directing that KESCO would continue to accept Rs.5 lakhs per month against the arrear dues together with the current dues on the basis of the actual consumption - In the meantime, after the amendment of Clause 4.49 of the Code, a letter was sent to the Respondent- Company asking it to submit a Bank Guarantee/Bond securing the amount of outstanding as arrears - Respondent-Company, accordingly, submitted a Bond - However, since the two affidavits and the Bond did not secure the outstanding dues of the Petitioners and were also not to its satisfaction, the load was not reduced - KESCO informed that the load reduction could not be considered owing to non-submission of the Bank Guarantee by the Respondent-Company for the balance amount of the bill raised for the month of May, 2006 - Respondent-Company filed Writ Petition - HC came to the conclusion that the decision with regard to the reduction of the load of the Respondent-Company stood approved on 19.04.2006 and, accordingly, the effective date of such reduction would have to be reckoned from the first day of the following month, namely, from 1.5.2006, in terms of Clause 4.41(e) of the Code - Hence, present Special Leave Petition - Held, when the decision was taken on 19.04.2006 to reduce the contract load, the unamended version of Clause 4.49 of the Code was in existence and that the same provided for submission of either a Bank Guarantee or a Bond or any other instrument to the satisfaction of the licensee of the equal amount of pending dues - Therefore, decision not to accept the Bond was not in accordance with the decision arrived at on 19.04.2006 to reduce the contract load from 8 MVA to 1.25 MVA - Result of the continued insistence of KESCO that a Bank Guarantee should be provided by the Respondent-Company in respect of its outstanding dues, had the effect of negating the decisions to revive the Company - No interference called for in impugned order of the HC - Special Leave Petition dismissed.
