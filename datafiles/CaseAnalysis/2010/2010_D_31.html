Manisha Tyagi v Deepak Kumar

Supreme Court of India

10 February 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Surinder Singh Nijjar, V.S. Sirpurkar

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 95; (2010) 4 SCC 339; AIR 2010 SC 1042; 2010 (3) ALD(SC) 16; 2010(2) ALL MR 925; 2010 (79) ALR 447; 2010 (2) AWC 1902; 2010 (3) Bom.C.R. 76; 2010 (2) CTC 214; 2010 (2) JhrCR(SC) 54; JT 2010 (2) SC 82; 2010 (3) MLJ(SC) 358; 2010 (2) RCR(Civil) 199; 2010(2) SCALE 294; [2010] 2 S.C.R. 554

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Summary: (A) Family & Personal - Hindu Marriage Act, 1955, s. 13 and 13(1)(i-a) - Concept of mental cruelty in matrimonial causes - Held, in today's era it would be sufficient to show that the conduct of one of the spouses is so abnormal and below the accepted norm that the other spouse could not reasonably be expected to put up with it - Conduct is no longer required to be so atrociously abominable which would cause a reasonable apprehension that it would be harmful or injurious to continue the cohabitation with the other spouse - Therefore to establish cruelty it is not necessary that physical violence should be used - However continued ill-treatment cessation of marital intercourse, studied neglect, indifference of one spouse to the other may lead to an inference of cruelty - Appeal allowed.

(B) Family & Personal - Hindu Marriage Act, 1955, s. 13 and 13(1)(i-a) - Cruelty - Divorce - Husband filed a petition u/s. 13 of the Hindu Marriage Act for dissolution of the marriage - Husband alleged numerous instances of cruelty in the divorce petition - He described the wife as quarrelsome, rude and ill-mannered - Husband also alleged that the wife was making baseless complaints to his superiors - Wife made counter allegations that the husband and his family had started treating her with cruelty when demands for dowry were not met by her parents - Trial Court and Single Judge of HC concluded that the behaviour of the husband as well as the wife falls short of the standard required to establish mental cruelty in terms of s. 13(1)(i-a) of the Act - Single Judge of HC granted a decree for judicial separation u/s. 10 of the Act - Appeal was filed thereagainst by wife - DB of HC held that cruelty alleged by the husband stands proved and granted decree of divorce as prayed by the husband - Hence, present filed by the wife - Whether wife was exercising such cruelty towards the husband so as to entitle the husband to the dissolution of the marriage? - Held, Trial Court and the Appellate Court accepted that the conduct of the wife did not amount to cruelty of such a nature to enable the husband to obtain a decree of divorce - It was not a case where it was necessary for the DB of HC to correct any glaring and serious errors committed by the court below which had resulted in miscarriage of justice - There was no compelling necessity, independently placed before the DB of HC to justify reversal, of the decree of judicial separation - HC erred in granting a decree of divorce to the husband - Judgment passed by DB of HC set aside and order passed by the Single Judge restored - Appeal allowed.
