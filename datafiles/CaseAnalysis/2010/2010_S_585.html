K. Neelaveni v State, Rep. By Insp. of Police and Others

Supreme Court of India

22 March 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Chandramauli Kumar Prasad, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 216; (2010) 11 SCC 607; AIR 2010 SC 3191; 2010 CRLJ 2819; JT 2010 (3) SC 156; 2010 (3) MLJ(Crl) 352; 2010 (1) OLR 791; 2010 (2) RCR(Criminal) 457; 2010(3) SCALE 261; [2010] 3 S.C.R. 548; 2010 (2) UC 882

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 406, 494 and 498A - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 190 - Quashing of proceeding - Cruelty - Bigamy - Appellant gave a written report to the Inspector alleging that her husband, mother-in-law, brother-in-law and sister-in-law assaulted her and had driven her out from the matrimonial home - Appellant further alleged that her husband had married another lady namely 'X' without consent with the help and in the presence of other accused persons and a female child was born to them in the wedlock - Case u/ss. 406, 494 and 498A of IPC was registered against the accused persons - Accused persons filed petition before HC for quashing the charge sheet u/ss. 406 and 494 of IPC contending that in the absence of any material to show that 'the second marriage was duly performed with religious rites and essential ceremonies' charge sheet u/s. 494 of IPC is fit to be quashed - HC quashed the charge sheet as against the accused persons on the ground that charge sheet do not reveal the ingredients constituting the offences u/s. 494 and 406 IPC - Hence, present appeal - Whether HC rightly quashed the charge sheet? - Held, no - While considering the application for quashing of the charge sheet, the allegations made in the FIR and the materials collected during the course of the investigation are required to be considered - Truthfulness or otherwise of the allegation is not fit to be gone into at this stage as it is always a matter of trial - Essential ceremonies of the marriage were gone into or not is a matter of trial - Hence, HC erred in holding that the charge sheet does not reveal the ingredients constituting the offences u/ss. 494 and 406 of the IPC - Moreover, accused persons approached the HC for quashing of the charge sheet even before any order was passed by the Magistrate in terms of s. 190 of the Cr.P.C. - When a report is submitted to the Magistrate he is required to be prima facie satisfied that the facts disclosed therein constitute an offence - Quashing of ss. 406 and 494 of IPC from the charge sheet even before the exercise of discretion by the Magistrate u/s. 190 of the Cr.P.C. is undesirable - Impugned order not sustainable - Appeal allowed.
