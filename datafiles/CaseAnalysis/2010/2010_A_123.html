Managing Director, Hassan Co-operative Milk Producer's Society Union Limited v Assistant Regional Director, Employees State Insurance Corporation

Supreme Court of India

26 April 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 311; (2010) 11 SCC 537; AIR 2010 SC 2109; 2010(4) ALL MR 403; 2010 (3) AWC 3160; 2010 (2) CLT(SC) 220; 2010 (125) FLR 742; JT 2010 (4) SC 240; 2010 (5) KarLJ 375; 2010 (2) LLJ 860; 2010(4) SCALE 337; [2010] 5 S.C.R. 232; 2010 (3) SLR 311; 2010 (2) UPLBEC 1195

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Employees' State Insurance Act, 1948, ss. 2(9), 45A, 75, 76 and 77 - Payment of contribution under the Act, 1948 - Liability of the appellants to pay ESI contribution in respect of the workers employed by the contractors in performance of the contract awarded to them for transportation of milk - ESI Court held that the work of employees engaged by the contractors is incidental to the main work carried out by the appellant and that supervision over the work of such employees by the appellant is also established - Appeal filed thereagainst dismissed by HC - Hence, present appeal - (A) Whether the workers employed by the contractors in performance of the contract awarded to them by the appellants covered by the definition of 'employee' u/s. 2(9) of the Act? - Held, expression "on the premises of the factory or establishment" u/s. 2(9) comprehends presence of the persons on the premises of the factory or establishment for execution of the principal activity of the industrial establishment and not casual or occasional presence - However, said expression does not comprehend every person who enters the factory for whatever purpose - It has to be held that the persons employed by the contractor for loading and unloading of milk cans are not the persons employed on the premises of the appellants' establishment - (B) Whether these workers can be said to be working under the supervision of the appellants? - Held, expression 'supervision of the principal employer' u/s. 2(9) means something more than mere exercise of some remote or indirect control over the activities or the work of the workers - From the agreements entered into by the appellants with the contractors, it does not transpire that the appellants have arrogated to themselves any supervisory control over the workers employed by the contractors - Said workers were under the direct control of the contractor - However, exercise of supervision and issue of some direction by the principal employer over the activities of the contractor and his employees is inevitable in contracts of this nature and that by itself is not sufficient to make the principal employer liable - In the circumstances, even if it be held that the transportation of milk is incidental to the purpose of factory or establishment, for want of any supervision of the appellants on the work of such employees, these employees are not covered by the definition of 'employee' u/s. 2(9) of the Act - Impugned orders set aside - Appeals allowed.
