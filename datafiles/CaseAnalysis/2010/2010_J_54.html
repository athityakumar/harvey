Ravi Kumar v Julmidevi

Supreme Court of India

09 February 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Ganguly, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 298; (2010) 4 SCC 476; AIR 2010 SC (Supp) 544; 2010 (4) ALD(SC) 36; 2010(3) ALL MR 977; 2010 (5) Bom.C.R. 387; 2010 (2) G.L.R. 1161; JT 2010 (2) SC 213; 2010 (7) MLJ(SC) 407; 2010 (2) RCR(Civil) 178; 2010(2) SCALE 289; [2010] 2 S.C.R. 545

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Summary: (A) Family & Personal - Hindu Marriage Act, s. 9, 13(1)(ia), 13(1)(ia) and 28 - Appellant (husband) filed a proceeding u/s. 9 of the Act for restitution of conjugal rights - Petition later withdrawn, having ended in a compromise - Thereafter, appellant filed a petition for a decree of divorce and dissolution of marriage on the grounds of cruelty and desertion alleging that respondent (wife) did not comply with terms of compromise - Decree of divorce was granted - On appeal, HC in exercise of its powers u/s. 28 of the Act reversed the finding of the District Judge - Hence, present appeal - (A) Condonation - HC observed that by filing a petition u/s. 9 of the Act, the appellant had condoned the earlier alleged acts of cruelty of the respondent - Held, condonation is a question of fact and reasoning of the HC on condonation in the facts of present case is correct - (B) Whether HC acting as a First Appellate Court ought not to reverse a finding which was arrived at by the Trial Court? - Held, while exercising its power u/s. 28 of the Act, the HC, as the first Court of Appeal is both a Court of Law and also of facts - Therefore, in exercise of its power, the First Appellate Court can come to a finding different from the one arrived at by the Trial Court especially in a case where appreciation of evidence by the Trial Court is not proper - (B) Cruelty and desertion - HC noted that in absence of specific allegation by the appellant of wife's cruelty Trial Court was in error by granting a divorce on grounds of cruelty - Held, evidence of the child is very vital in the facts and circumstances of this case of matrimonial discord - in the present case the child clearly stated the cruelty of the appellant towards respondent - Therefore, there is sufficient reason for the respondent to stay apart - Therefore, it cannot be said the wife is guilty of either cruelty or desertion - Appeal dismissed.

(B) Family & Personal - Hindu Marriage Act, s. 13(1)(ia) - Cruelty - Matrimonial dispute - Held, in matrimonial relationship, cruelty would mean absence of mutual respect and understanding between the spouses which embitters the relationship and often leads to various behavioral tendencies which can be termed as cruelty - Sometimes cruelty in a matrimonial relationship may take the form of violence, sometimes it may take a different form - At times, it may be just an attitude or an approach - Silence in some situations may amount to cruelty - Therefore, cruelty in matrimonial behavior defies any definition and its category can never be closed - Whether husband is cruel to his wife or the wife is cruel to her husband has to be ascertained and judged by taking into account the entire facts and circumstances of the given case and not by any pre-determined rigid formula - Cruelty in matrimonial cases can be of infinite variety, it may be subtle or even brutal and may be by gestures and words - Appeal dismissed.
