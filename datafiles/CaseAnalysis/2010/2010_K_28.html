Kanchanganga Sea Foods Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax and another

Supreme Court of India

07 July 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Chandramauli Kumar Prasad, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 471; (2010) 11 SCC 144; [2010] 325 ITR 540; JT 2010 (7) SC 51; 2010(6) SCALE 442; [2010] 7 S.C.R. 866; [2010] 192 TAXMAN 187

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 5(2) - Income received in kind - Assessee-fishing company entered into an agreement chartering two fishing vessels from a foreign company - The charter fee was payable from earning from the sale of fish and for that purpose 85% of the gross earnings from the sale of fish was to be paid to the foreign company - As per agreement the charters were to be delivered at Chennai Port for commencement of fishing operation - Actual fishing operations were done outside the territorial waters of India but within the exclusive economic zone - The catch made at high seas were brought to Chennai where surveyor of Fishery Department verified the log books and assessed the value of the catch over which local taxes were levied and paid - Assessee thereafter arranged customs clearance for the export of the fish and the charters, which were used for fishing, carried the fish to destination chosen by foreign company - Assessee did not deduct the tax from the foreign company nor produced any clearance certificate during the a/ys 1991-92 to 1994-95 - Notice u/s. 201(1) of IT Act was issued to assessee alleging assessee as in-default u/s 201 of IT Act - Assessee submitted that foreign company did not carry out activities or operations in India which have the effect of resulting in accrual of income in India and hence it was not obliged to make any deduction - Commissioner (Appeal) rejected the contentions of the assessee -Tribunal and HC also rejected appeal of the assessee filed against said order - Hence, present appeal - Whether assessee is in default u/s. 201 of IT Act? - Held, chartered vessels with the entire catch were brought to the Indian Port, the catch were certified for human consumption, valued, and after customs and port clearance non-resident company received 85% of the catch - So long the catch was not apportioned the entire catch was the property of the assessee and not of foreign company as the latter did not have any control over the catch - It is after the foreign company was given share of its 85% of the catch it did come within its control - Therefore, the receipt of 85% of the catch was in India and this being the first receipt in the eye of law and being in India would be chargeable to tax - Foreign company having received the charter fee in the shape of 85% of fish catch in India, sale of fish and realization of sale consideration of fish by it outside India shall not mean that there was no receipt in India - According to the terms and conditions of the agreement charter fee was to be paid in terms of money i.e. US Dollar 600,000/= per vessel per annum 'payable by way of 85% of gross earnings from the fish-sales' - Hence, income earned by the foreign company was chargeable to tax u/s. 5(2) of the IT Act and assessee was liable to deduct tax u/s 195 and was rightly held to be in default u/s 201 of IT Act - Appeals dismissed.
