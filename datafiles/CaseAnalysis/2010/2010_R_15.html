Radha Mudaliya and others v Spl. Tahsildar (Land ACQ.), T. N. H. Board and another

Supreme Court of India

08 October 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Swatanter Kumar, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 934; (2010) 13 SCC 384; AIR 2011 SC 54; 2011 (2) ALD(SC) 108; 2011 (2) AWC 1168; JT 2010 (12) SC 185; 2011 (1) MLJ(SC) 587; 2010(11) SCALE 195

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4, 5, 6, 23 - Land acquisition - Compensation - Enhancement of - Entitlement - Notification u/s. 4(1) of the Act was issued by the respondent/State proposing to acquire appellant's land for setting up a Export Processing Zone - Appellants filed objections u/s. 5A of the Act, however, Land Acquisition Officer (LAO) rejected objections and issued declaration u/s. 6 of the Act - LAO, subsequently, passed award of compensation at the rate of Rs. 145/- per cent - Appellants raised objections with regard to the quantum of compensation - Matter referred to the Reference Court - Reference Court enhanced compensation at the rate of Rs. 3,600/- per cent - Respondent challenged the Reference Court order before the HC - HC reduced compensation to Rs. 2018/- per cent - Hence the present appeal - Whether appellants are entitled for the enhanced compensation - Held, appellants produced sale deeds of various sale instances from the same Revenue Estate and those were quite close to the date of notification u/s. 4 - None of the parties to the proceedings had questioned the genuineness, legality or otherwise of the said sale deeds and, in fact, there was hardly any objection regarding their admissibility or being read in evidence - Therefore, such sale deeds are admitted in evidence in accordance with law as they are genuine transactions and are the closest sale instances to the date of the notification as available on record and the land, subject matter of the transaction, is quite similar to the acquired land and, in fact, it is from the same village - Both the Reference Court as well as the HC had fallen in error of law in computing the compensation payable to the appellants - HC ignored an important aspect of the case in not awarding enhancement in the value of the land as it had come in evidence that there was increasing trend in the sale price of the land in that area whereas the Reference Court fell in error in giving 40% increase for a short intervening period of ten months from the date of notification till the date of award - Both the HC as well as the Reference Court had applied the deduction at the rate of 40% but still awarded compensation at antipodal rates - Land in question certainly has potential and even the sale instances show that the land from the Revenue Estate of the same village was sold as plots and a number of facilities were available in the vicinity - Further, cumulative effect of the evidence on record in relation to location, potential and similarity of land, it is appropriate that deduction of more than 30% would be prejudicial to the interest of the appellants whose lands have been acquired by the State in exercise of its power of eminent domain - Therefore, applying 30% deduction to the value indicated in sale deeds, the appellants would be entitled to receive compensation at the rate of Rs. 2,800/- per cent for the acquired land with consequential benefit u/s. 23(1)A of the Act - Appeals partly allowed.
