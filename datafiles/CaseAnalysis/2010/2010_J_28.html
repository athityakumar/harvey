State Of U.P. & Ors v Jasvir Singh & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

26 November 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 1111; (2011) 4 SCC 288; 2011 (85) ALR 733; 2011 (2) AWC 1975; 2011 (2) CLT(SC) 35; JT 2011 (1) SC 446; 2011 (3) MLJ(SC) 201; 2011(1) SCALE 373

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Land & Property - Constitution of India, 1950 - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4 and 6 - Personal presence of State Governments officer - Land acquisition proceedings - Quantum of Compensation - Award was made by Land Acquisition officer was increased by the Reference Court, and was further increased by HC - On further appeal by the respondents, SC set aside the judgment of the HC and remanded the matter back to the HC - Respondents thereafter filed a writ petition seeking a direction for issue of a fresh notification u/ss. 4 and 6 of the Act - In the said writ proceedings, the HC suggested to the State Government that it should settle the claim of the respondents but there was resistance from the state government - HC passed an interim order directing Principal Secretary (Finance) and Principal Secretary (Revenue) of the State Government to appear in person - Hence, present appeal - (A) Whether HC while hearing a writ petition challenging a land acquisition can force the State Government to settle the matter outside the Court? - Held, where the State has a definite policy or taken a specific stand and that has been clearly explained by way of affidavit, the court should not attempt to impose a contrary view by way of suggestions or proposals for settlement - Further, Court can express its views and issue directions through its reasoned orders, subject to limitations in regard to interference in matters of policy; but it should not, it cannot attempt to impose its views by asking an unwilling party to settle on the terms suggested by it - (B) Whether the HC was justified in calling the senior officers of the State Government and directing them to settle the matter, when the writ petition filed by respondents was being resisted by State Government? - Held, HC has the power to summon or require the personal presence of any officer, to assist the court to render justice or arrive at a proper decision; but there are well settled norms and procedures for exercise of such power - Courts should avoid directing the senior officers to be present in court to settle the grievances of individual litigants - Requiring the presence of the senior officers of the government in court should be as a last resort, in rare and exceptional cases, where such presence is absolutely necessary - Interim directions of HC, issued requiring the Principal Secretary (PWD), Principal Secretary (Finance) or Principal Secretary (Revenue) to be present on different dates, improper and therefore, set aside - Order accordingly.
