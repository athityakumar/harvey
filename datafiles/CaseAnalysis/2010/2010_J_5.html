The Special Deputy Collector, Land Acquisition C.M.D.A. v J. Sivaprakasam & Ors

Supreme Court of India

18 November 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, H.L. Gokhale

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 971; (2011) 1 SCC 330; AIR 2011 SC 922; 2011 (2) AWC 1981; 2011 (1) CLT(SC) 38; 2011 (1) CTC 608; JT 2010 (12) SC 577; 2011 (1) MLJ(SC) 788; 2012 (1) RCR(Civil) 437; 2010(12) SCALE 110; 2010 (4) WLN(SC) 82

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: (A) Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4(1), 5A and 6 - Publication in newspapers circulating in the locality - (A) Consequences of publication of notification in two newspapers having reasonably wide circulation - Held, if the two newspapers carrying the publication of the notification have reasonably wide circulation in the locality, then the requirements of s. 4(1) are complied with and all persons concerned in the locality shall be deemed to have notice of the notification - In that event, neither the notification u/s. 4(1), nor the consequential acquisition proceedings would be open to challenge, on the ground of violation of s. 4 of the Act - (B) Consequences of bonafide publication of the notification in two newspapers which do not have a wide circulation in the locality - Held, in this case neither the notification u/s. 4(1) nor the consequential acquisition proceedings, will become vitiated automatically - If the person aggrieved, apart from demonstrating that the two newspapers did not have reasonably wide circulation in the locality, also asserts that as a consequence, he did not have notice of the proposed acquisition that was provided for in s. 4(1) of the Act, in the absence of evidence to the contrary, the acquisition to the extent of the land of such person will be vitiated; but if such assertion is rebutted by the acquiring authority by placing evidence to show that the person concerned had in fact notice, the acquisition will not be vitiated on the ground of violation of s. 4A of the Act - Appeal allowed.

(B) Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4(1), 5A and 6 - Publication in newspapers circulating in the locality - Violation of s. 4(1) - Notice to persons interested - Held, if the person challenging the acquisition is able to establish that the notifications were deliberately and with malafides, published in newspapers having negligible circulation, to avoid notice to the persons concerned, then s. 4(1) will be violated - However, acquiring authority need not prove actual notice of the proposal to acquire u/s. 4(1) of the Act, to the person challenging the acquisition - Further, when notice is directly served upon a party in a formal manner or when it is received personally by him, there is actual notice - Also if from the facts it can be inferred that a party knew about the subject matter of the notice, knowledge is imputed by implied notice - Appeal allowed.

(C) Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4(1), 5A and 6 - Validity of acquisition - Land in question was notified for acquisition under preliminary notification issued u/s. 4(1) and final declaration issued u/s. 6 of Act - Respondent Nos. 1 to 11 challenged the said acquisition contending that the acquisition was invalid for the following reasons: (a) opportunity to file objections and participate in the enquiry u/s. 5A of the Act was denied (b) preliminary notification was published in two Tamil Dailies which had no circulation in the locality, thereby violating the provisions of s. 4(1) of the Act - Single Judge of HC allowed the writ petition holding that publication of the notification u/s. 4(1) of the Act in the newspapers which had no circulation in the locality, did not fulfill the mandatory statutory requirement u/s. 4(1) of the Act - Appeal filed thereagainst was dismissed - Hence, present appeal - Whether finding of HC that the two regional language newspapers in which the Notification u/s. 4(1) was published did not have reasonably wide circulation in the locality and therefore there was non-compliance with the provisions of s. 4(1), calls for interference? - Held, by Amendment in 1984, s. 4(1) was amended introducing the additional requirement relating to publication of the notification in two daily newspapers circulating in the locality - Object and purpose of the amended s. 4(1) of the Act was to provide for publication of the preliminary notification in two daily newspapers having reasonably wide circulation in the locality so that people (persons interested) in that locality may become aware of the proposals for acquisition - However, publications in two newspapers having regular and steady circulation, but having a market share of only 2% to 3% of the total newspapers cannot invalidate the acquisition proceedings automatically, on the ground that such publication violates the requirement of s. 4(1) relating to newspaper publication - In the present case, as both the newspaper were sold and circulated in Chennai it may not be possible to hold that the said newspapers were not 'regional daily newspapers circulating in the locality' - It would not be possible to invalidate the entire acquisition on the ground that the publication in the said two newspapers did not fulfill requirement of publication in 'newspapers circulating in that locality' - Further, publication of the notification u/s. 4(1) of the Act, in two newspapers which did not have wide circulation in the locality, did not affect respondent Nos. 5 to 11 in any manner as they had notice of the proposals for acquisition and participated in the enquiry u/s. 5A of the Act - Hence, no prejudice was caused on account of the publication of the notification in two newspapers having limited circulation in the locality - Acquisition upheld - Appeal allowed.
