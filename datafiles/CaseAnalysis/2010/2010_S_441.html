Jai Krishna Mandal and another v State of Jharkhand

Supreme Court of India

03 August 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

H.S. Bedi, Chandramauli Kumar Prasad

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 833; 2010(9) SCALE 54

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 366, 376 - Rape - Conviction- Sustainability - Appellants allegedly raped PW6/Prosecutrix - Trial Court convicted appellants u/ss. 376, 366 IPC - HC affirmed Trial Court order in appeal - Whether conviction of appellants by the Trial Court is sustainable - Held, Doctor who medically examined the PW6 testified that there was no evidence of rape, no injury on her person - Appellants allegedly disappeared from the village and came back after three days but FIR was recorded three days thereafter although they had been interrogated by the Investigating Officer on the day in which they had come back - Further, Investigating Officer was not examined in the Trial Court which also caused prejudice to the appellants - Doctor allegedly handed over the clothing of PW6 to Investigating Officer, however, such material objects were neither sent for examination nor produced before the Trial Court - Appellants were charged only with an offence u/s. 366 r/w s. 34 IPC at the initial stage, however, the same was altered at later stage and added s. 376 IPC as well - Therefore, conviction recorded by the Trial Court is not sustainable and hence orders of Trial Court and HC were set aside - Appeal allowed.
