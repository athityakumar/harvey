Commissioner Of Central Excise v M/S. International Auto Limited

Supreme Court of India

08 January 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 31; (2010) 2 SCC 672; AIR 2010 SC (Supp) 449; 2010 (250) E.L.T. 3; JT 2010 (1) SC 294; 2010(1) SCALE 400; [2010] 1 S.C.R. 211

Case Digest

Subject: Excise

Summary: Excise - Central Excise Act, 1944, s. 11AB - Interest on differential duty - Assessee supplied auto parts to their customers - Price difference arose between the price on the date of removal and the enhanced price at which the goods stood ultimately sold - Department issued a show-cause notice proposing to levy interest on the differential duty, paid by the assessee, u/s. 11AB of Act - Assessee contended that interest was not leviable as prices indicated in the purchase orders were final during the period of supply of goods and not liable to change at the time of removal of goods - Whether interest on differential duty could be leviable? - Held, when the differential duty is paid after the date of clearance, it indicates short- payment/short-levy on the date of removal, hence, interest which is for loss of revenue, becomes leviable u/s. 11AB of the Act - Revenue's appeal allowed.
