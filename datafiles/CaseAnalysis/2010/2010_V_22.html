Vijay @ Chinee v State Of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

27 July 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 551; (2010) 8 SCC 191; 2010 ALL MR (Cri) 3326; JT 2010 (7) SC 576; 2011 (1) MLJ(Crl) 242; 2010 (3) RCR(Criminal) 794; 2010(7) SCALE 502; [2010] 8 S.C.R. 1150; 2010 (3) UC 1440

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 217, and 376 - FIR under Section 376/34 IPC was registered against the appellant and six others - Trial Court convicted all the accused persons including the appellant for committing gang rape - On appeal, HC upheld the conviction of appellant and acquitted the remaining accused - Hence, present appeal - (A) Sole Evidence of Prosecutrix - It was submitted that conviction cannot be based on the sole deposition of the prosecutrix - Held, statement of prosecutrix, if found to be worthy of credence and reliable, requires no corroboration - Court may convict the accused on the sole testimony of the prosecutrix - (B) Test Identification Parade - Held, Test Identification is a part of the investigation and is very useful in a case where the accused are not known before hand to the witnesses - It is used only to corroborate the evidence recorded in the court - Therefore, it is not substantive evidence - Actual evidence is what is given by the witnesses in the court - (C) Discrepancies and inconsistencies in depositions of witnesses - Held, evidence of the witnesses must be read as a whole and the cases are to be considered in totality of the circumstances and while appreciating the evidence of a witness, minor discrepancies on trivial matters, which do not affect the core of the prosecution case, should not be taken into consideration as they cannot form grounds to reject the evidence as a whole - (D) Discrepancies between the statement of the prosecutrix and the other evidence on record - Held, contradictions, inconsistencies and discrepancies pointed out are immaterial for the reason that the Trial Court as well as the HC have considered these aspects and came to the conclusion that none of those contradictions goes to the root of the case - Conviction upheld - Appeal dismissed.
