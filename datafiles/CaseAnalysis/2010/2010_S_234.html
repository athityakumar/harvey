Banarsi Dass v State Of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

05 April 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Swatanter Kumar, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 244; (2010) 4 SCC 450; AIR 2010 SC 1589; 2010 ALL MR (Cri) 1608; 2010 CRLJ 2419; 2010 (3) JCC 1842; JT 2010 (3) SC 552; 2010 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1201; 2010 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1441; 2010 (3) MLJ(Crl) 132; 2010 (2) OLR 28; 2010 (2) RCR(Criminal) 553; 2010(3) SCALE 531; 2010 (2) UC 784

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Illegal Gratification

Summary: Criminal - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947, s. 5(4) - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 161 - Demand and acceptance of illegal gratification - Conviction - Challenged - Appellant was convicted u/s. 5(2) of the Act and s. 161, IPC - Conviction upheld by HC - Hence, present appeal - Alleged, demand and acceptance of illegal gratification by appellant for changing the Khasra name in the name of mother of complainant (PW-2) - (A) Inference of demand and receipt of illegal gratification - Held, offence should be proved against the accused beyond reasonable doubt - In the present case, HC erred in so far as it has drawn the inference of demand and receipt of the illegal gratification from the fact that the money was recovered from the appellant - (B) Recovery of currency notes -Appellant submitted that recovery was not proved in accordance with law - Held, money was recovered from the pocket of the appellant, thus, it cannot be said that the recovery from the pocket of the appellant was unsustainable in law and was of no consequence - (C) Contradiction between the statements of the prosecution witnesses - Held, statements of both PW-2 and PW-4 made before the Court were different from the one made by them before the police during the investigation - In light of the statement of two hostile witnesses PW-2 and PW-4, the demand and the acceptance of illegal gratification alleged to have been received by the appellant cannot be said to have been proved by the prosecution in accordance with law - Therefore, giving the benefit of doubt on technical ground to the appellant, judgment of HC set aside and appellant acquitted of both the charges - Appeal allowed.
