A.S. Mohammed Rafi v State Of Tamil Nadu Rep. By Home Dept. And Others

Supreme Court of India

06 December 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Markandey Katju, Gyan Sudha Misra

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 1053; (2011) 1 SCC 688; AIR 2011 SC 308; 2011 (1) CTC 602; 2015 (319) E.L.T. 10; JT 2011 (3) SC 636; 2011 (1) KLT 39; 2011 (1) OLR 531; 2011 (1) RCR(Civil) 812; 2011 (1) RCR(Criminal) 617; 2010(13) SCALE 119

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Practice & Procedure - Bar Council of India Rules, 1975, Chapter II - Professional ethics - Refusal to appear for a person - Resolution by Bar Association - Legality - Whether passing a resolution by HC Bar Associations or District Court Bar Associations that their members would not defend a particular person or persons in a particular criminal case is legal - Held, passing a resolution by the Bar Association that none of its members will defend for a particular person(s) is wholly illegal, against all traditions of the Bar, and against professional ethics of lawyers - Every person, however, wicked, depraved, vile, degenerate, perverted, loathsome, execrable, vicious or repulsive he may be regarded by society has a right to be defended in a court of law and correspondingly it is the duty of the lawyer to defend him - Professional ethics requires that a lawyer cannot refuse a brief, provided a client is willing to pay his fee, and the lawyer is not otherwise engaged - Hence, the action of any Bar Association in passing such a resolution that none of its members will appear for a particular accused, whether on the ground that he is a policeman or on the ground that he is a suspected terrorist, rapist, mass murderer, etc. is against all norms of the Constitution, the Statute and professional ethics - Such resolutions are against the great traditions of the Bar which has always stood up for defending persons accused for a crime - Further, such a resolution is, in fact, a disgrace to the legal community and it is the duty of a lawyer to defend no matter what the consequences, and a lawyer who refuses to do so is not following the message of the Gita - Therefore, declaration issued to the effect that all such resolutions of Bar Associations in India are null and void and the right minded lawyers should ignore and defy such resolutions if they want democracy and rule of law to be upheld in India - Direction issued to the SC Registry to circulate copies of the present judgment/order to all HC Bar Associations and State Bar Councils in India and the HC Bar Associations, thereafter, should circulate the present judgment/order to all the District Court Bar Associations in their States/Union territories - Appeal disposed of.
