V. Kishan Rao v Nikhil Super Speciality Hospital and another

Supreme Court of India

08 March 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Ganguly, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 364; (2010) 5 SCC 513; 2011 ACJ 500; 2010 (5) ALD(SC) 46; 2010 (3) AWC 3252; 2010 (6) Bom.C.R. 155; JT 2010 (4) SC 630; 2010 (5) MLJ(SC) 121; 2010 (4) MPLJ 9; 2010 (2) RCR(Civil) 929; 2010 (2) RCR(Criminal) 878; 2010(4) SCALE 662; [2010] 5 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection

Keywords: Human Rights Act, 1988

Summary: (A) Consumer Protection - Consumer Protection Act, 1986, s. 2(1)(g) - Medical negligence - Deficiency in service - Appellant-Complainant submitted that he got his wife admitted in the Respondent No. 1 hospital on 20-07-02 since his wife was suffering from fever which was intermittent in nature and was complaining of chill - His wife was subjected to certain tests by the respondent No.1 but the test did not show that she was suffering from malaria - Complainant further submitted that his wife was not responding to the medicine given by the respondent No.1 and thus her condition was deteriorating day by day - Patient was finally shifted to Yashoda Hospital from the respondent No.1. in a very precarious condition and was virtually, clinically dead - Complainant filed complaint against respondent no. 1 alleging his wife was not given proper treatment and the respondent No.1 was negligent in treating the patient - District Forum found the respondent No. 1 negligent in his services, thus directed to refund Rs. 10,000/- to the complainant and to pay Rs. 2 lacs as compensation - On appeal, State Commission overturned the order of the District Forum and held that the complainant failed to establish any negligence on the part of the hospital authorities - National Commission upheld the orders of the State Commission - Hence, present appeal - Whether order of National Commission's could be upheld? - Held, it is a case of wrong treatment in as much as the patient was not treated for malaria when the complaint is of intermittent fever and chill - Instead the respondent No.1 treated the patient for Typhoid and as a result of which the condition of the patient deteriorated - When the condition became very critical the patient was removed to Yashoda Hospital but patient could not be revived - Expert evidence not required as negligence is evident - Impugned orders of the State Commission and of the National Commission set aside and orders of the District Forum restored - Appeal allowed

(B) Consumer Protection - Consumer Protection Act, 1986, s. 2(1)(g) - Medical negligence - Deficiency in service - Expert opinion - In a case where negligence is evident, the principle of res ipsa loquitur operates and the complainant does not have to prove anything as the thing (res) proves itself - In such a case it is for the respondent to prove that he has taken care and done his duty to repel the charge of negligence - Appeal allowed
