Chandigarh Housing Board v Avtar Singh and others

Supreme Court of India

22 September 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 895; (2010) 10 SCC 194; AIR 2011 SC 130; 2011 (2) AWC 1257; JT 2010 (10) SC 360; 2011 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1328; 2011 (2) MLJ(SC) 601; 2010 (4) RCR(Civil) 579; 2010(10) SCALE 106; [2010] 12 S.C.R. 96

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Consumer Protection; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Consumer Protection - Constitution - Chandigarh Allotment of Land to Co-operative House Building Societies Scheme, 1991 - Chandigarh Lease Hold of Sites and Buildings Rules, 1973 - Consumer Protection Act, 1986, s. 2(d) - Jurisdiction - District Forum - Forfeit of earnest money - Administrator, Chandigarh framed a scheme called 'Chandigarh Allotment of Land to Co-operative House Building Societies Scheme, 1991' for allotment of land to Co- operative House Building Societies through Chandigarh Housing Board - Societies filed writ petition challenging said 1991 Scheme praying for direction to be issued to respondent to give individual and developed plots to the members of the petitioner societies in view of the Scheme of 1983 - DB permitted the Societies to deposit 10% of the tentative price and also recorded the undertaking given by them that if the writ petition is dismissed, they shall deposit the balance amount with interest @ 18% per annum, if they were to accept allotment of flats under the new scheme - Societies collected 10% of the tentative price from their members and deposited the same in the bank - Subsequently, Finance Secretary issued memo dt. 9.6.1993 directed that if any member of the Society was to seek refund, then 10% out of 25% of the earnest money should be deducted and balance amount to be refunded without interest - Petitioner applied through their respective Societies for refund of the amount paid by them by indicating that they were no longer interested in the flats - In the meanwhile, Finance Secretary issued an another memo dt. 9.3.2000 directing full refund of earnest money to the societies/and its members without forfeiting 10% of the earnest money - Board forfeited 10% earnest money of the petitioner and refunded the balance without 18% interest as per memo dt. 9.6.1993 - Petitioner filed complaint before District Forum in respect of forfeit of 10% earnest money and non-refund of 18% interest - District Forum held that forfeiture of 10% earnest money and non-refund of 18% interest were contrary to the 1991 Scheme and the 1973 Rules and the same amounted to deficiency in service and unfair trade practice - On appeal, State Commission held that once the Finance Secretary had issued instructions vide memo dt. 9.3.2000 that full refund of earnest money will henceforth be made to the Societies/and its members without forfeiting 10% of the earnest money already deposited, the Board should have refunded the remaining amount to the complainants and its failure to do so amounted to deficiency in service - State Commission also opined that the instructions issued by the Finance Secretary to the Board not to refund 18% interest deposited by the members of the Society did not have any legal sanction - National Commission dismissed the revisions filed by the Board - Petitioner filed appeal against said order submitting that District Forum did not have the jurisdiction to entertain the complaints filed by the members of the Societies because there was no privity of contract between them and the Board - (A) Whether District Forum had the jurisdiction to entertain the complaints filed by petitioner for refund of 10% earnest money forfeited by the Board and 18% interest paid by them? - Held, Consumer Protection Act has a wide reach and the Commission has jurisdiction even in cases of service rendered by statutory and public authorities - Commission/Forum has a statutory obligation to award compensation, where there has been capricious or arbitrary or negligent exercise or non-exercise of power by an officer of the authority - Hence, District Forum had the jurisdiction to entertain the complaints filed by petitioner for refund of 10% earnest money forfeited by the Board and 18% interest paid by them - (B) Whether the members of the Societies, who would have been benefited by allotment of land under the 1991 Scheme were consumer within the meaning of s. 2(d) of the Act? - Held, by making applications for allotment of land, the Societies will be deemed to have hired or availed the services of the Chandigarh Administration and the Board in relation to housing construction - If the scheme had been faithfully implemented and land had been allotted to the Societies, their members would have been the actual and real beneficiaries - Therefore, Societies were certainly covered by the definition of 'consumer' u/s. 2(d)(ii) of Act and members of the Societies had every right to complain against illegal, arbitrary and unjustified forfeiture of 10% earnest money and non-refund of 18% interest - (C) Whether the Chandigarh Administration and the Board were justified in refusing to refund 18% interest paid by the members of the Societies? - Held, no - The 1991 Scheme did not provide for levy of interest on the delayed payment of earnest money - Notwithstanding this, the members of the Societies had to pay 18% interest because while dismissing the writ petition, the HC observed that the Societies who had deposited 10% of the sale consideration and found eligible for allotment or have been allotted sites would be liable to pay the balance 15% with a further interest at the rate of 18% per annum - However, there was nothing either in the interim or the final order of the HC from which it can be inferred that the Chandigarh Administration or for that reason the Board was authorised or empowered to refuse refund of 18% interest to the members who did not seek allotment of flat - The Finance Secretary misinterpreted the orders of the HC and issued wholly arbitrary and unjust directive to the Board not to refund 18% interest to the members of the Societies who had applied for refund before allotment of land by the Board - The Chandigarh Administration and the Board had no right to refuse refund of 18% interest - State Commission did not commit any error by directing refund of the amount of interest by treating it to be a case of deficiency in service and National Commission rightly confirmed the order of the State Commission - Appeals dismissed.
