Chief Executive Officer, Zilla Parishad v State of Maharashtra and others

Supreme Court of India

22 April 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Panchal, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 399; (2010) 6 SCC 592; 2011 (6) Bom.C.R. 370; 2010 (125) FLR 738; JT 2010 (4) SC 338; 2010(4) SCALE 272; [2010] 5 S.C.R. 127; 2010 (7) SLR 453

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Termination - Caste certificate - Invalidation of - Respondent No. 2 was appointed as Assistant Teacher by the appellant on the post reserved for Scheduled Tribe - Certificate indicating that he belongs to Scheduled Tribes was invalidated by the Scrutiny Committee - Therefore, the appellant terminated services of the respondent No. 2 - Writ petition was filed thereagainst - HC set aside the order invalidating caste claim of the respondent No. 2 and directed the Scrutiny Committee to decide the matter afresh after affording necessary opportunity of hearing to him - Thereafter, respondent No.2 applied to the Government to reinstate him in service claiming that he belongs to SBC category and should be granted protection of Government Resolution dated 15.06.1995 - Said Resolution inter alia, specifies as to which Caste should be considered as Special Backward Class - However, respondent No. 2 was not reinstated in service - Writ petition filed thereagainst was allowed - Hence, present appeal - (A) State of Maharashtra submitted that respondent No. 2 was entitled to the protection of Government Resolution - Held, well settled principle of law is that once the certificate indicating that a person belongs to Scheduled Tribe is invalidated by the Caste Scrutiny Committee, his appointment becomes void from the beginning - Void appointment could not have been validated by the Government by addressing a communication to the appellant - (B) Termination - Held, in the present case, no SBC post was available - In view of the fact that no post belonging to the SBC category was available with the appellant, interest of justice would be served if the Government is directed to create supernumerary post in the appellant No. 1 institution to accommodate the respondent No. 2 with liberty to get the Caste Certificate verified through the Caste Scrutiny Committee - Appeal disposed of.
