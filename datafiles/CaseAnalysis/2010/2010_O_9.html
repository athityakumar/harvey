Dolphin Drilling Limited v Oil and Natural Gas Corporation Limited

Supreme Court of India

17 February 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 123; (2010) 3 SCC 267; AIR 2010 SC 1296; 2010(3) ALL MR 428; 2010 (79) ALR 444; 2010 (2) AWC 1911; 2010 (2) RCR(Civil) 265; 2010(2) SCALE 456; 2010 (1) UC 481

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Contract & Commercial - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, s. 11(6) - Appointment of arbitrator - Agreement - Succeed - Applicant and respondent entered into agreement dt. 17-10-2003, in terms of agreement, applicant was to carry out drilling operations for respondent in offshore waters of India as allocated by respondent - According to applicant, though period of agreement came to an end on dt. 13-2-2007 on being called upon by respondent, it continued to provide further services till dt. 10-4-20007 for which it was entitled to paid additionally on comparable rates under agreement - Applicant makes grievance that number of its invoices were not paid or only paid in part by respondent and on demands made by it respondent did not even give any satisfactory reply for non-payment/part-payment of those invoices - Therefore, applicant was left with no option but to invoke arbitration clause under agreement and filed application u/s. 11(6) of the 1996 Act, for appointment of Arbitrator for and on behalf of respondent - Accordingly addressed notice to respondent invoking arbitration on disputes broadly set-out in notice and further stated that respondent did not respond to arbitration notice in manner as provided in arbitration clause in agreement - Hence, instant petition - - Respondent contended that arbitration clause in agreement envisaged one, single arbitration for all disputes between parties and not repeated arbitrations for different disputes arising between parties at different times under same agreement - Whether application filed by applicant for appointment of Arbitrator was succeed - Held, words 'all disputes' in cl. 28 of agreement could only mean 'all disputes' that might be in existence when arbitration clause was invoked and one of parties to agreement gives arbitration notice to other - Hence, present form cl. 28 of agreement could not be said to be one time measure and it could not be held that once arbitration clause was invoked, remedy of arbitration was no longer available in regard to other disputes that might arise in future - Therefore application was allowed and applicant had nominated 'X' as its arbitrator and 'Y' was appointed as arbitrator on behalf of respondent - Petition disposed of.
