State Of Rajasthan v M/S. Nav Bharat Construction Company

Supreme Court of India

08 January 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Tarun Chatterjee, R.M. Lodha

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 20; (2010) 2 SCC 182; AIR 2010 SC 739; 2010 (1 ) AWC 584; 2010 (1) CLT(SC) 235; 2010 (1) JhrCR(SC) 180; JT 2010 (1) SC 523; 2010 (2) MLJ(SC) 959; 2010 (1) RCR(Civil) 834; 2010(1) SCALE 244; [2010] 1 S.C.R. 312; 2010 (1) WLN(SC) 49

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration Act, 1940, ss. 30, 33 - Jurisdiction of SC in making an award a rule of court - Appellant awarded a contract of construction to respondent - Respondent failed to complete the work within the stipulated time - Appellant terminated the contract - Respondent invoked the arbitration clause - Matter referred to two arbitrators - Since there was a difference of opinion between the arbitrators, the matter was referred to an Umpire - Appellant filed application before the HC to remove the Umpire on the ground of bias - HC dismissed the application - Umpire entered into reference and passed the award - Appellant filed objections to the award u/ss. 30 and 33 before SC - SC set aside the award of Umpire and appointed another Umpire - Second Umpire passed the award - Appellant filed application before the SC to make the award a rule of the Court, however, respondent filed objections to the award - (A) Whether SC has the jurisdiction to make the award a rule of the Court - Followed the decision of SC in Mcdermott International Inc. v. Burn Standard Co. Ltd and Others, 2006 INDLAW SC 1268 - Held, since the arbitrator was directed to file his award in SC, the objections as well as the entertainability of the application of the appellant for making the award a rule of the Court must be filed in SC alone and, therefore, the SC has the jurisdiction to entertain the application of the appellant and also the objections filed by the respondent - (B) Whether SC ought to have set aside the award of Umpire on the ground that the jurisdiction of the court was not appellate in nature - Held, jurisdiction of the court to set aside an award u/s. 30 of the Act has now been settled by various decisions of the SC and different HCs taking those principles into consideration, u/s. 30 of the Act it must be said that the court is not empowered to re-appreciate the evidence and examine the correctness of the conclusions arrived at by the Umpire in considering an application for setting aside the award - Award made a rule of the court - Appeals disposed of.
