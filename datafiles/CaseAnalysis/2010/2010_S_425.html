Devender Kumar and another etc. v State of Haryana others etc.

Supreme Court of India

05 May 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 382; (2010) 6 SCC 753; 2010 ALL MR (Cri) 1965; 2010 CRLJ 3849; 2010 (3) JhrCR(SC) 106; JT 2010 (4) SC 595; 2010 (2) OLR 232; 2010 (3) RCR(Criminal) 380; 2010(5) SCALE 325; [2010] 5 S.C.R. 1195; 2010 (3) ShimLC 365; 2010 (2) UC 731

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 167(1) - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 498-A, 406, 506, 323 r/w. s. 34 - Bail - Appellant was produced before Judicial Magistrate on 8-10-2008 in connection with FIR registered u/ss. 498A, 406, 506, 323 r/w. s. 34 of IPC - An application was made for police remand which was rejected on the ground that said application was contrary to the provisions of s. 167(1) Cr.P.C. - Subsequently, an application was made by the S.H.O., praying for grant of police remand of the appellant for a period of three days - Said application was also dismissed and bail was granted to appellant by Judicial Magistrate on 10-10-2008 - Respondent no. 4 filed petition before HC praying for cancellation of the bail granted to the appellant - HC allowed said petition and quashed the orders dt. 8-10-2008 and 10-10-2008 - Hence, present appeal - Whether HC rightly cancelled the bail of the appellant? - Held, reason given by the HC for cancellation of the orders granting bail and directing the arrest of the appellant on the ground that disclosures have been made by the appellant and that his police custody was necessary for recovery of the same, is not sufficient for the purpose of cancellation of bail granted earlier - Moreover, police remand can only be made during the first period of remand after arrest and production before the Magistrate, but not after the expiry of the said period - Impugned order set aside - Appeals allowed.
