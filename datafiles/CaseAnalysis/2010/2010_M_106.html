Oil & Natural Gas Corporation v M/S. Wig Brothers Builders & Engineers Pvt. Ltd.

Supreme Court of India

08 October 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, H.L. Gokhale

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 851; (2010) 13 SCC 377; 2011 (2) AWC 1599; JT 2010 (11) SC 145; 2011 (1) RCR(Civil) 833; 2010(10) SCALE 614; 2010 (4) WLN(SC) 35

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Contract & Commercial - Arbitration Act, 1940, ss. 30, 33 - Delay - Compensation - Admissibility - Appellant/Corporation entrusted a construction work to the respondent under a contract with arbitration clause - Certain disputes arose between the parties in regard to the said contract and they were referred to a sole arbitrator - Arbitrator awarded the claims of respondent and dismissed appellant's counter claims - Appellant challenged the award before the District Court u/ss. 30, 33 of the Act - District Court dismissed appellant's petition and made the award a rule of the court - HC upheld the District Court award - Hence the present appeal by appellant/Corporation - Whether the respondent is entitled to get the compensation for the delay in execution of the contract which was attributed by appellant - Held, cl. 5A of the contract pertains to extension of time for completion of work and specifically bars any claim for damages - Therefore, in the event of the work being delayed for whatsoever reason, that is even delay which is attributable to appellant, the contractor would only be entitled to extension of time for completion of work but would not be entitled to any compensation or damages - Arbitrator exceeded his jurisdiction in ignoring the said express bar contained in the contract and in awarding the compensation for delay in execution of work - Therefore, HC order and that of the District Court making the award the rule of the court is partly set aside in so far as it relates to the award of compensation with regard to the delay in execution and the award of interest thereon - Appeal partly allowed.
