State of Orissa and another v Sangram Keshari Misra and another

Supreme Court of India

19 October 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, H.L. Gokhale

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 867; (2010) 13 SCC 311; 2010 (127) FLR 902; JT 2010 (11) SC 221; 2011 (1) LLJ 731; 2010(11) SCALE 90; 2011 (7) SLR 283

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: Service - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Quashing of charge memorandum - State Government issued a charge memorandum dt. 29.04.2002 on the ground that respondent no. 1 impersonating as Special Secretary to the Government went to the office of the Additional Govt. Counsel and received the copy of the order dt. 17.1.2002 in Misc. Case No.238/2001 - Respondent no. 1 challenged the said charge memorandum before Tribunal who issued notice regarding admission but did not grant interim relief sought for - -On appeal HC issued notice in regard to the said writ petition and stayed the enquiry in pursuance of the said charge memorandum - Subsequently, when the said interim stay was in operation, State issued a corrigendum dt. 22.6.2002 to the charge memorandum dt. 29.4.2002, amending the case number - Respondent no. 1 initiated contempt proceedings against State alleging that issue of the corrigendum dt. 22.6.2002 when there was interim stay of the enquiry in pursuance of the charge memorandum dt. 29.4.2002 amounted to violation of the interim order and interference with the administration of justice - HC quashed the charge memorandum dt. 29.4.2002 and corrigendum dt. 26.2.2002 - HC also issued a warning to the Senior Officers of the State Government that in future they will not venture to do anything which may have the tendency to interfere with the administration of justice - Said orders was challenged by State in present appeals - (A) Whether the HC was justified in quashing the charge memorandum and corrigendum thereto? - Held, the reasoning of the HC was that if the corrigendum dt. 22.6.2002 had to be ignored as it was issued in violation of the interim order of stay dt. 12.6.2002, the charge dt. 29.4.2002 would be false and incorrect as respondent no. 1 did not receive the copy of the order in Misc. case No.238/2001, as there was no proceedings with the number 'Misc. case No. 238/2001' - HC while so quashing the charge memorandum dt. 29.4.2002 and the corrigendum dt. 22.6.2002 reserved liberty to the Government to deal with the matter in accordance with the law - Hence, it cannot be said that the order of the HC quashing the charge memorandum and the corrigendum reserving liberty to Government to take action in accordance with law, calls for interference in exercise of jurisdiction u/art. 136 of the Constitution - (B) Whether the HC justified in making adverse remarks against the officers of the State Government in regard to issuance of corrigendum? - Held, HC has held that issue of a corrigendum without the leave of the court, when there was a stay of the enquiry in pursuance of the charge, was clearly a step taken in the disciplinary enquiry - Even if it was correction of a genuine error, it would still be technically a violation of the order of interim stay - However, State submitted that the corrigendum was issued under a bona fide impression that correction of a typographical error in the charge memorandum could be issued when there was an order of interim stay, and therefore there was no intention to violate the interim stay - Consequently, HC was justified in closing the contempt proceedings - But the HC was not justified in expressing disapproval and issuing a warning to the Senior Officers of the State Government - Said adverse remark to be deleted from the impugned order of HC - Appeal partly allowed.
