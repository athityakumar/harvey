Dinesh Jaiswal v State Of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

12 January 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

H.S. Bedi, J.M. Panchal

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 262; (2010) 3 SCC 232; AIR 2010 SC 1540; 2010 CRLJ 1917; JT 2010 (2) SC 210; 2010 (2) RCR(Criminal) 139; 2010(2) SCALE 311; [2010] 1 S.C.R. 1063

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 376, 323 and 506 - Rape - Conviction - Challenged - Appellant was convicted u/s. 376, IPC - Conviction confirmed on appeal by HC - Hence, present appeal - It was contended that the evidence of the prosecutrix was liable to be believed save in exceptional circumstances - Held, test always is as to whether the given story prima facie inspires confidence - In the present case, the two primary witnesses, both relatives of the prosecutrix, had been declared hostile and had not supported the prosecutrix's case - Also the doctor was also unable to confirm the factum of rape - Therefore, argument that a prosecutrix must be believed irrespective of the improbabilities in her story can never be accepted as story given by the prosecutrix does not inspire confidence - Present matter is an exceptional one - Impugned judgments set aside - Appeal allowed.
