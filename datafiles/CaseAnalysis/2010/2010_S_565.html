Satyavir Singh v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

11 February 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Swatanter Kumar, Altamas Kabir

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 132; (2010) 3 SCC 174; AIR 2010 SC (Supp) 651; 2010 CRLJ 1659; 2010 (2) JhrCR(SC) 1; JT 2010 (2) SC 340; 2010 (3) MLJ(Crl) 120; 2010(2) SCALE 313; [2010] 2 S.C.R. 729; 2010 (1) UC 415

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Conjectures

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 307 - Arms Act, 1959, ss. 25, 25(1)(a) and 27 - Scope of interference by HC - Appellant was convicted for an offence u/s. 307, IPC and s. 27 of the Act by the Assistant Sessions Judge - On appeal, benefit was given to the appellant primarily on certain surmises and conjectures and doubting the presence of the witnesses and appellant was acquitted of both the charges and was convicted u/s 25(1)(a) of the Act - HC set aside the order of acquittal and convicted the appellant u/s. 307, IPC - Hence, present appeal - Whether HC was justified in reversing the judgment of acquittal and convicting the accused of an offence u/s. 307, IPC? - Held, a person proved to be guilty by the prosecution by leading cogent and reliable evidence, normally would not be given the benefit of doubt on the basis of certain assumptions or presumptions of facts - Assumptions raised by the First Appellate Court are not supported on record - Statement of the eye witnesses, medical evidence and the investigation conducted by the Investigating Officer clearly show that the prosecution was able to prove its case beyond reasonable doubt - Some discrepancies per se would not prove fatal to the case of the prosecution particularly when there was no reason before the Court to doubt the statement of the eye witnesses - HC has not exceeded its jurisdiction in law and with reference to the evidence on record while reversing the judgment of acquittal to one that of conviction - Appeal dismissed.
