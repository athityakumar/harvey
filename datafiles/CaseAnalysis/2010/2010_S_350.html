Jitendra Singh @ Babboo Singh & Anr. v State Of U.P.

Supreme Court of India

19 November 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 974; (2010) 13 SCC 523; 2011 CRLJ 1004; 2010 (4) JCC 3093; JT 2010 (12) SC 331; 2011 (2) MLJ(Crl) 399; 2011 (1) OLR 336; 2011 (1) RCR(Criminal) 917; 2010(12) SCALE 171

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 304-B and 498-A - Juvenile (Care and Protection of Children) Act, 2000, s. 2(k) - Claim of juvenility - Appeal filed by appellant challenging conviction for offences punishable u/ss. 304-B and 498-A of IPC - Appellant prayed for permission to raise an additional ground in support of the appeal to the effect that he was a minor within the meaning of s. 2(k) of the Act on the date of the commission of offence and that he ought to have been dealt with under the Act - Whether claim of juvenility can be raised at this stage? - Held, in the ordinary course any one claiming to be a minor on the date of the incident ought to make such a claim at the earliest available opportunity before the Trial Court or at least before the HC, but the very fact that no such claim was for any reason made, may not by itself disentitle him to do so before the Apex Court - In the present case, burden of making out a prima facie case for directing an enquiry was discharged - Appellant allowed to urge the additional ground regarding juvenility of the appellant on the date of the commission of the offence and an enquiry for determining the age of the appellant on the date of the commission of the offence also directed - Order accordingly.
