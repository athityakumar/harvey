Union of India and others v Hari Singh

Supreme Court of India

10 September 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Dalveer Bhandari, Deepak Verma

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 771; (2010) 15 SCC 201; 2010 (5) Law Herald (P&H) 3931; 2011 (1) RCR(Civil) 134; 2010(10) SCALE 205

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Accord And Satisfaction, Solicitor

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Arbitration & ADR - Settled claims - Reference to arbitration - Sustainable - Respondent/Contractor was awarded a contract by the appellant/Railway Department - Contract was executed by the respondent and the entire amount due and payable to the respondent was paid to him - Respondent entered into a Supplementary Agreement for further work - Respondent send a legal notice to appellant seeking claims over the earlier settled contract - Respondent, consequently, filed an arbitration case before the HC - HC referred the claims of respondent to two arbitrators - Hence the present appeal - Appellant contended that after receiving the entire amount, the respondent also signed the supplementary agreement and thereafter the respondent was not justified in invoking arbitration - Whether respondent's claim can be referred to arbitration - Held, when the parties by a supplementary agreement obtained a full and final discharge after paying the entire amount, due and payable to the contractor, the contractor would not be justified in invoking arbitration because there was no arbitral dispute for reference to the arbitration - Relied on the decision of SC in National Insurance Company Limited v. Boghara Polyfab Private Limited, 2008 INDLAW SC 1506 - Impugned judgement is unsustainable and hence set aside - Appeal allowed.
