North Delhi Power Limited and Another v Government of National Capital Territory of Delhi and Others

Supreme Court of India

03 May 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

V.S. Sirpurkar, Surinder Singh Nijjar

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 333; (2010) 6 SCC 278; AIR 2010 SC 2302; 2010 (169) DLT 575; JT 2010 (4) SC 597; 2010(4) SCALE 546; [2010] 5 S.C.R. 1039

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Delhi Electricity Reforms (Transfer Scheme) Rules, 2001

Summary: Service - Delhi Electric Reforms Act, 2000 - Delhi Electricity Reforms (Transfer Scheme) Rules, 2001, rr. 2 (k), 2(n), 2(l), 3, 5(2), 6(8), 6(9), 6(11) and 12 - Liabilities relating to employees - From 1.7.2002, the DVB unbundled into six companies, they being DISCOM 1 (BSES Yamuna Power Ltd.), DISCOM 2 (BSES Rajdhani Power Ltd.) and DISCOM 3 (North Delhi Power Ltd.), Delhi Power Supply Company Ltd. (TRANSCO) and generation company (GENCO) - DISCOM are the appellants in the present appeal - Another company called "DPCL" (holding company) was also constituted with aims and objects to hold shares in the aforementioned DISCOM companies - (A) Whether appellants responsible for meeting the liabilities relating to employees who ceased to be the employees of erstwhile DVB prior to 1.7.2002 on account of their retirement, removal, dismissal or compulsory retirement in accordance with the provisions of Delhi Electric Reforms Act, 2000? - HC held that the appellants alone would be responsible to meet such liabilities - HC concluded that not only the assets and liabilities were transferred to the transferee company but the entire past and future litigation were also transferred to the transferee company and such litigation could have been in respect of the employees, consumers and other parties - It reiterated that the Rules provided that all corresponding employees were transferred by way of forming list in respect to employees who were working in the respective area while all employees who were under suspension or termination and in respect of whom any kind of proceedings were pending at that stage, were also specifically made the responsibility of the transferee company - Lastly, the HC relied on the letter dated 21-22.01.2004 which was issued by the Government for removal of doubt, dispute and difference under its power u/r. 12(1) which clearly fixed the responsibility on the DISCOMS - Appeal filed - Held, a glance at the rules and sub-rules is sufficient to come to the conclusion that the liabilities have undoubtedly been transferred to the DISCOMS which include both NDPL as well as the BSES - (B) It was submitted that DISCOMS were not the only transferees but it was also the holding company, namely, the Delhi Power Company Ltd (DPCL) - Held, argument is incorrect as no employees were ever transferred to the DPCL - All transferees came only to the DISCOMS under the transfer scheme and not the holding company - HC correctly interpreted the Rules and correctly come to the conclusions that the liabilities would rest with the DISCOMS including NDPL and BSES - (C) Interpretation of r. 8(3) of the transfer scheme - Held, purpose of sub-Rule (3) is to cap any liability arising out of litigation, suits, claims etc. either pending on the date of transfer and/ or arising due to events prior to the date of transfer to be borne by the relevant DISCOM 1, DISCOM 2 or DISCOM 3, respectively - It includes all the liabilities including the liabilities on account of the personnel - (D) Whether non obstante clause in r. 8(3) unconstitutional? - Held, language is clear, unambiguous and must be given its natural meaning and if such a meaning is given, clause cannot be rendered unconstitutional in any manner - (E) Transfer of personnel and all the principles - Held, transfer of personnel and all the principles are governed by r. 6 alone - By using the words "existing pensioners" and by providing that the relevant transferee would stand substituted for the Board for all purposes, the legislative intention is very clearly displayed to the effect that the existing pensioners on the day of transfer were also covered and stood transferred to the DISCOMS and not to DPCL and it is only the transferee DISCOM, who would substitute for the Board - There can be no dispute that those who retired and those who were serving with the Board would stand transferred in respect of their liabilities etc. to the successor company, i.e. DISCOM-3 - HC correctly appreciated this position - (F) Whether decision given by the Government on such liability was without any authority or non est in the light of the provisions of the Act and the Rules? - Held, r. 12 provides that the decision of the Government shall be final and sub-Rule (1) of r. 12 stipulates that if any doubt, dispute, difference or issue shall arise in regard to the transfers under these Rules, subject to the provisions of the Act, the decision of the government thereon, shall be final and binding on all parties - Finality of the Government decision is writ large from the provisions of sub-Rule (1) of r. 12, while under the provisions of sub-Rule (2), the Government has the power to make provisions by order published in the Official Gazette - (G) It submitted that once the Government has exercised the powers u/r. 12(1), the power gets exhausted and the decision becomes final and binding on all the parties, including the Government - Held, there can be no finality in the matter of removal of doubts or the removal difficulties and also taking the decisions u/r. 12(1) - Nothing stopped the Government from taking any decision and it has taken a clearest possible decision by letter dated 21.01.2004 which is binding on all the parties - View taken by the HC is the correct view - Appeals dismissed.
