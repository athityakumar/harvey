Main Pal v State of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

07 September 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, H.L. Gokhale

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 718; (2010) 10 SCC 130; AIR 2010 SC 3292; 2010 CRLJ 4450; 2010 (4) JCC 2802; JT 2010 (10) SC 99; 2010 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 3042; 2010 (5) Law Herald (P&H) 3473; 2011 (2) MLJ(Crl) 506; 2011 (1) OLR 276; 2010 (4) RCR(Criminal) 375; 2010(9) SCALE 98; [2010] 11 S.C.R. 336; 2010 (3) UC 1389

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 354 and 452 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 212 - House-trespass - Appellant allegedly trespassed into PW 1's house and tried to outrage the modesty of PW 1 - Magistrate held appellant guilty of offences u/ss. 452 and 354 of IPC and sentenced him for rigorous imprisonment for six months and a fine of Rs.1,000/- - Appellant filed appeal against said order which was dismissed by Addl. Sessions Judge - HC upheld the conviction of the appellant u/ss. 452 and 354 of IPC but reduced the sentence from 6 to 4 months rigorous imprisonment - Appellant filed appeal against said order - Held, there was a material error in the charge as it violated the requirement of sub-section (1) of s. 212 of the Code, that the charge shall contain particulars as to the person against whom the offence was committed - In view of the specific charge, the appellant concentrated on showing that the charge was false - He did not attempt to meet the case made out in the trial that the offence was against PW 2 - The appellant was thus clearly misled by the error in the charge which caused prejudiced to the appellant thereby occasioning failure of justice - Hence, there should be a new trial after charging him with the offence of outraging the modesty of PW 2 - Conviction of the appellant set aside and matter remanded back to the trial court with a direction for a new trial after framing a charge - Appeal allowed.
