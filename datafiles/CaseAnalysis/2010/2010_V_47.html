Punjab State Electricity Board v Vishwa Caliber Builders Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

19 March 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 192; (2010) 4 SCC 539; AIR 2010 SC 2893; JT 2010 (3) SC 197; 2010 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1281; 2010 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1297; 2010 (2) RCR(Civil) 625; 2010(3) SCALE 162

Case Digest

Subject: Electricity & Energy

Summary: Electricity & Energy - Electricity Act, 2003 - Sanctioned connection - Unauthorized use of electrical energy - Installation of new transformer - Not avail balance load - Respondent submitted application dt. 23-5-1995 to competent authority of appellant for sanction of NRS connection with load of 2548 kW and deposited ACD - Engineer-in-Chief sanctioned registration of application of respondent subject to condition that connection would be released only after shifting of 8 MVA load from 66 KV - On 11-5-1999 new transformer was installed at 66 KV substation, thereafter, Memo dt. 11-8-1999 was issued to respondent to give consent for release of balance load, respondent failed to avail balance load - Electricity connection installed in premises of respondent was checked on 27-8-2004 by team of officers of Board which found that as against sanctioned load of 1500 KW, respondent was using total load of 1981.637 KW, upon receipt of report of checking team, demand notice dt. 25-1-2005 was issued to respondent requiring it to deposit Rs.15, 41,492 - Respondent challenged notice by filing petition before DSA, which was dismissed vide order dt. 20-2-2006 - Respondent carried matter before Ombudsman, who ruled that load of 1918.637 KW should be accepted as built up load and same should be deemed as regularized with effect from 1-3-2000 and relevant portions of order dt. 5-9-2007 passed by Ombudsman held, load of 1981.637 KW detected as on 27-08-2004 should be accepted as built up load and deemed to be regularized w.e.f. 01-03-2000 as per commitment of petitioner - It would mean that there was no unauthorized or excess load as on checking date of 27-08-2004, load surcharge of Rs.7,22,455/- levied on account of excess load found during checking were not recoverable from petitioner - Writ petition filed by appellant was dismissed by HC by observing that having failed to fulfill its obligation to release connection with load of 2548 KW, appellant could not put respondent in disadvantageous position and make it liable for load which was not utilized - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order of Ombudsman and HC was justified - Held, Ombudsman committed jurisdictional error by directing regularization of unauthorized use of electricity by respondent and refund of alleged excess amount charged by appellant - Hence, appellant could not release connection with load of 2548 KW on account of non-availability of transformer necessary for transfer of 8 MVA load from 66 KV substation, had no bearing on issue of consumption of electricity by respondent beyond sanctioned load - Undisputedly, in terms of request made by respondent, Chief Engineer had sanctioned connection on existing system with a load of 1500 KW, but respondent used excess load to tune of 481.637 KW and this amounted to unauthorized use of electrical energy, it was also not in dispute that after installation of new transformer - This being position, fault, if any, for non-release of balance load lay at doors of respondent and Ombudsman committed serious error by directing appellant to refund alleged excess amount collected from respondent on account of use of electricity over and above sanctioned load - Therefore, orders of HC and Ombudsman were set aside - Appeal allowed.
