Subhan Rao And Others v Parvathi Bai And Others

Supreme Court of India

14 September 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 857; (2010) 10 SCC 235; 2010(6) ALL MR 1002; 2011 (85) ALR 525; 2011 (2) AWC 1779; 2011 (2) Bom.C.R. 762; 2011 (1) CLT(SC) 46; JT 2010 (9) SC 553; 2011 (1) MPLJ 503; 2010 (4) RCR(Civil) 542; 2011 (113) RD 67; 2010(9) SCALE 547

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Keywords: In Lieu Of Maintenance, Kulkarni Watan Abolition Act

Summary: Family & Personal - Hindu Succession Act, 1956, s. 14(1) - Suit for declaration of title - 'X' executed maintenance deed , whereby possession of impugned land was given to 'Y' (Son of "X) for his maintenance with a stipulation that in case of his death without having natural male issue, his wife 'Z' shall enjoy the lands for her maintenance till her life time and the same shall revert to the executants after her death - Said deed also contained a condition that 'Y', his wife 'Z' and natural heirs shall not create any encumbrance or alienate the impugned land - 'Y' died in 1957 and 'Z' died in 1979 - During her life time, 'Z' executed sale deeds in favour of respondents in respect of some of the lands - Appellant (Adopted son of 'X') filed suit for declaration of title and for setting aside the sale deeds executed by 'Z' - Appellant also filed another suit for grant of injunction to restrain the respondents for alienating the impugned land - Trial Court held that 'Z' had a pre-existing right of maintenance from the family of 'X' and in view of s. 14(1) of the Act, she became full owner of the lands after the death of her husband and, as such, the alienations made by her were legal and valid - On appeal, Lower Appellate Court and HC affirmed the order of the trial court - Hence, present appeal - Whether 'Z', who was given right to enjoy certain lands belonging to 'X' (predecessor-in- interest of the appellants) in lieu of maintenance became full owner thereof in terms of s. 14(1) of the Hindu Succession Act, 1956 and the sale deeds executed by her were valid? - Held, by virtue of s. 14(1) of the Act, 'Z' acquired absolute right over the lands in respect of which she was given right of enjoyment and she became full owner thereof without any restriction on her right to deal with the property in the manner she liked - Therefore, the sale deeds executed by her were legal and the courts below as well as the HC did not commit any error by declining to nullify the same. - Impugned order of the HC upheld - Appeal dismissed.
