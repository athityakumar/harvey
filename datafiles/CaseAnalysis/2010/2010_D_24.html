Daya Shankar Yadav v Union Of India & Ors

Supreme Court of India

24 November 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, H.L. Gokhale

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 1002; 2011(1) ALL MR 468; 2011 (3) AWC 3246; 2011 (128) FLR 168; JT 2010 (13) SC 791; 2011 (1) MLJ(SC) 995; 2010(12) SCALE 477

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Central Reserve Police Force Rules, 1955

Summary: Service - Defence & Security Forces - Central Reserve Police Force Rules, 1955, rr. 5(1) and 14(b) - Consequences of making a false statement or suppressing material information in verification forms - Appellant was selected and appointed as a Constable in the Central Reserve Police Force - As per r. 14(b) of the Rules, appellant was required to furnish factual information about and sign a Verification Roll which he did - Relevant clause in the verification form which required him to disclose whether any criminal case registered against him was pending before any court and whether he had been convicted by any court; he answered the relevant query in the negative - After verification it was found that a criminal case was registered against the appellant and that the court had however discharged him after trial - Show cause notice was issued to appellant alleging that the appellant had given false information in the verification form by concealing facts - Services of appellant was terminated u/r. 5(1) of the Rules - Order upheld by HC - Hence, present appeal - Whether termination of the service of appellant was justified? - Appellant submitted that the English version of the questions were complex, ambiguous, tangled and confusing and he was misled into answering the question wrongly - Held, purpose of seeking the said information is to ascertain character and antecedents of the candidate so as to assess his suitability for the post - Therefore, the candidate will have to answer the questions in these Columns truthfully and fully and any misrepresentation or suppression or false statement therein, by itself would demonstrate a conduct or character unbefitting for a uniformed security service - However, if the consequence of a wrong answer can be rejection of his application for appointment, or termination from service if already appointed, the least that is expected of the employer is to ensure that the query was clear, specific and unambiguous - Employer cannot dismiss/discharge/ terminate an employee, for misunderstanding a vague and complex question, and giving a wrong answer - However, in the present case, even assuming that there was ambiguity in the English version of the questions, a reading of the Hindi version of the questions shows a clear indication of the information that was required to be furnished by the declarant - Hence, appellant had knowingly made a false statement that he was not prosecuted in any criminal case appellant not entitled to any benefit of doubt on the question whether he knew the meaning and purport of questions - Therefore, the employer (CRPF) was justified in terminating his services for not being truthful in giving material information regarding his antecedents which were relevant for employment in a uniformed service, and that itself justified his discharge from service - Appeal dismissed.
