Karam Kapahi And Others v M/S Lal Chand Public Charitable Trust And Another

Supreme Court of India

07 April 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Ganguly, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 445; (2010) 4 SCC 753; AIR 2010 SC 2077; 2010 (82) ALR 320; 2010 (3) CGLJ 290; 2010 (168) DLT 501; 2010 (4) MLJ(SC) 585; 2010 (2) RCR(Civil) 683; 2010 (1) RCR(Rent) 360; 2010(3) SCALE 569; [2010] 4 S.C.R. 422

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 114 - Equitable relief - Respondent no.1, a registered charitable trust ('Trust') was the lessor and the Club was the lessee - Trust filed a suit against the Club in view of termination of club's lease for non-payment of lease rent by the Club - Club also filed a suit contending that Trust was not the lessor of the suit premises and has no right to let out the same to the Club and thus inter alia the title of the Trust over the suit premises was challenged - However, the suit was dismissed for default - In the suit filed by Trust, Club admitted that there was an execution of sub-lease between the parties though the title of the trust over the suit property was disputed and that Club withheld the payment of rent and was ready to deposit the same before the Registrar of the HC - In the said suit the Club filed an application inter alia praying that the Trust be restrained from receiving the lease money - HC held that since the Club admitted that it was inducted as a tenant in the suit premises under the lease deed, it cannot withhold the payment of rent/ damages inter alia on the ground that the suit premises belong to MCD who had never demanded any rent - Appeal filed thereagainst was dismissed - Club filed an application u/s. 114 of the Transfer of Property Act in the suit filed by the Trust seeking the equitable remedy of forfeiture - Trial Judge refused to grant relief u/s. 114 of the Transfer of Property Act and it was held that since there was clear admission by the club about non-payment of rent the plaintiff is entitled to a decree for possession in respect of the entire suit property - Appeal filed thereagainst was dismissed - DB also held that the conduct of the Club disentitles it from the equitable relief u/s. 114 - DB directed the Club to hand over vacant possession in respect of the suit property to the Trust - SLP filed, inter alia, on the ground that they were affected by the judgment and decree of the HC to which they were not made parties and on such representation - Club also filed a petition and got the petition tagged with the petition filed by the Club members - Present special leave petitions - It was submitted that principles of s. 114 of the Transfer of Property Act are independent of the provision of O. 12 r. 6 - Further, s. 114 of the Transfer of Property Act is an equitable remedy for a lessee in a given case and the stand taken in a proceeding u/s. 114 cannot be taken into consideration to reach a finding under O. 12 r. 6 of the CPC - Held, principles behind O. 12 r. 6 are to give the plaintiff a right to speedy judgment - However, the Court always retains its discretion in the matter of pronouncing judgment - In the present case, controversy is between the parties on an admission of non-payment of rent, judgment can be rendered on admission by Court - Therefore, in the instant case even though statement made by the Club in its petition u/s. 114 of the Transfer of Property Act does not come within the definition of the word 'pleading' under O. 6 r. 1 of the CPC, but in O. 12 r. 6 of the CPC, the word 'pleading' has been suffixed by the expression 'or otherwise' - Thus the stand of the Club in its petition u/s. 114 of the Transfer of Property Act can be considered by the Court in pronouncing judgment on admission under O. 12 r. 6 in view of clear words 'pleading or otherwise' used therein especially when that petition was in the suit filed by the Trust - However, the provision under O. 12 r. 6 of the CPC is enabling, discretionary and permissive and is neither mandatory nor it is peremptory since the word "may" has been used - Club is not entitled to any equitable relief u/art. 136 of the Constitution having regard to its conduct - Judgment of the HC affirmed - Appeals dismissed.
