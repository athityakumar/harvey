Union of India and others v Ram Prakash

Supreme Court of India

05 July 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Mukundakam Sharma, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 467; (2010) 11 SCC 220; 2010 (5) AWC 4563; 2010 (3) ESC 405; 2010 (126) FLR 821; JT 2010 (7) SC 93; 2010 (8) MLJ(SC) 358; 2010(6) SCALE 368; [2010] 7 S.C.R. 506; 2011 (1) SLR 98

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces; Service

Keywords: Disability Pension, Air Force Pension Regulations

Summary: Defence & Security Forces - Service - Pension Regulations, regn. 37 and 153 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Entitlement for disability pension - Respondent-Air Force Personnel was unwell and consequently was examined by a Medical Board who found that the respondent suffered from Retinal detachment to the extent of 60% and that the degree of disablement was permanent - Medical Board gave an opinion that the said disability suffered by the respondent during his service was neither attributable to nor aggravated by Air Force Service - Respondent on being discharged from service claimed payment of disability pension which was denied to him - Respondent filed a suit claiming payment of disability pension submitting that at the time of his entry to the Air Force service, no such disease was recorded in his records and therefore, onset of the aforesaid disease during the course of service should be considered as attributable to service - Trial Court dismissed said suit - Appellate court dismissed appeal filed against said order - However, second appeal was allowed by the Single Judge- Hence present appeal - Whether respondent entitled for disability pension? - Held, no - Medical Board consists of an expert body and that its opinion is entitled to be given due weight and value - As the Medial Board has given a categorical opinion that the ailment of the respondent was constitutional and the same is not attributable to or aggravated by Air Force Service, it was unjustified for the Judge to set aside the aforesaid concurrent opinions of the appellate Board and Released Medical Board and also the findings recorded by the trial court and also by the First Appellate Court merely because the Single Judge felt that there could be a presumption that the respondent was undergoing arduous nature of job as he was appointed as an Air Force personnel - Order of Single Judge set aside - Appeal allowed
