National Hydroelectric Power Corpn. Limited. v Commissioner Of Income Tax

Supreme Court of India

05 January 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 63; (2010) 3 SCC 396; AIR 2010 SC (Supp) 498; JT 2010 (1) SC 21; 2010(1) SCALE 5; [2010] 1 S.C.R. 16; [2010] 187 TAXMAN 193

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 115JB, Explanation-I clause (b) - A/y 2001-2002 - Accounting treatment of Advance Against Depreciation (AAD) - Government of India introduced a mechanism to generate additional cash flow by allowing generating companies to collect AAD by way of tariff charge which means that once the loan stood re-paid, the Advance so collected would get reduced from the Normal Depreciation of the later years, and such reduced depreciation would be included in the tariff, in turn lowering the tariff - Authority for Advance Rulings (AAR) ruled that reduction of AAD from the 'sales' was nothing but a reserve which has to be added back on the basis of Clause (b) of Explanation-I to s. 115JB of IT Act, 1961 - Whether AAD from the 'sales' was a reserve which has to be added back? - Held, AAD is a timing difference, it is not a reserve, it is not carried though profit and loss account and that it is "income received in advance" subject to adjustment in future and, therefore, Clause (b) of Explanation-I to s. 115JB is not applicable- Impugned ruling set aside - Appeal allowed.
