Aftab Ahmad Anasari v State Of Uttaranchal

Supreme Court of India

12 January 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Panchal, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 26; (2010) 2 SCC 583; AIR 2010 SC 773; JT 2010 (1) SC 424; 2010 (2) MLJ(Crl) 601; 2010 (1) RCR(Criminal) 832; 2010(1) SCALE 408; 2010 (1) UC 193

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 376 and 201 - Murder and Rape - Circumstantial evidence - Conviction based on circumstantial evidence - Appellant allegedly raped and murdered the deceased, a five year old child - Trial Court convicted appellant for rape and murder and sentenced him to death - Appellant filed appeal before HC - HC confirmed the conviction, however, modified the sentence to one of life imprisonment - Whether conviction of appellant is legally sustainable - Held, circumstance that the appellant was found fleeing away from near the place where the dead body was found lying was satisfactorily proved - Blood stained clothes of the deceased concealed in the house of sister of the appellant, were recovered pursuant to voluntary disclosure statement made by the appellant while in police custody - Further, prosecution could satisfactorily prove that the clothes, recovered pursuant to the voluntary disclosure statement made by the appellant, belonged to the deceased - Circumstance that the blood stained underwear of the appellant was recovered during the course of investigation, cannot be ignored - Extra judicial confession of appellant referred to by the witness is reliable and is rightly accepted by the Trial Court and the HC - Chain of circumstantial evidence is complete and does not leave any reasonable ground for conclusion consistent with the innocence of the appellant - Further, chain of circumstances is such as to show that within all human probability the rape and murder of the deceased were committed by the appellant - Appeal dismissed.
