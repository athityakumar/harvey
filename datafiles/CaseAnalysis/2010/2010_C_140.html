M/S. Nicholas Piramal India Ltd. v Commnr. Of Central Excise, Mumbai

Supreme Court of India

29 November 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Mukundakam Sharma, Anil R. Dave

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 1005; 2010 (260) E.L.T. 338; JT 2010 (13) SC 140; 2011 (5) RCR(Civil) 160; 2010(12) SCALE 530

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - Indirect Tax - Central Excise Act, 1944, ss. 11A and 11AC - Limitation - Dutiability and marketability - Crude Vitamin A - Assessee engaged in the manufacture of Vitamin A in a finished and marketable form and cleared same on payment of applicable duty - Assessee also engaged in the manufacture of animal feed supplements - During the intermediate stage of manufacture of vitamin A, 'Vitamin A Acetate Crude' and 'Vitamin A Palmitate' or Vitamin A in its crude form emerges - 5 show cause notice issued to the assessee demanding excise duty on crude vitamin A - Appellant submitted that the crude vitamin A, is an intermediary and that such demand is untenable and unjustified inasmuch as the said product cannot be termed as 'goods' and is incapable of being marketed, particularly in view of the fact that the life of the item would not be more than two days - Commissioner rejected said submission and confirmed the liability to duty for the impugned goods manufactured and cleared between December, 1989 and February, 1995 and imposed a penalty on the appellant - On appeal, Tribunal confirmed the order of the Commissioner - Tribunal also held that show cause notice dt. 29.12.1994 would be barred by limitation for the period from February, 1993 to October, 1993 but so far other 3 notices relating to the period November, 1994 to April, 1994, May, 1994 to October, 1994 and November, 1994 to February, 1995 were concerned, they were held to be within limitation - Hence, present appeal - (A) Whether 'Vitamin A Acetate Crude' and 'Vitamin A Palmitate' or 'Crude Vitamin A' is excisable to duty? - Held, the orders passed by the Commissioner as also the Tribunal clearly demonstrate that the product in question is commercially known and is capable of being marketed - The facts that the appellants have chosen not to sell the product in question does not mean that the same is not capable of being marketed - There is also no dispute that the said product in question is used in the manufacture of the animal feed supplement sold by the Appellant - Had the Appellant not used the product in question, they would have had to buy the same from the market to manufacture and sell the Animal Feed Supplement - This clearly shows that a marketable product emerges - Hence, Vitamin A Acetate Crude' and 'Vitamin A Palmitate' or 'Crude Vitamin A' is excisable to duty - (B) Whether tribunal order in respect of limitation could be upheld? - Held, it is disclosed from the records that the appellant did not disclose to the revenue that the assessee was manufacturing the impugned product - Assessee also did not maintain an account and paid no duty on this - Therefore, the Commissioner as also the Tribunal were justified in holding that the extended period of limitation u/s. 11A (1) of 1944 Act could be invokable - Hence, demand made for the aforesaid three periods cannot be said to be time-barred - Appeal dismissed.
