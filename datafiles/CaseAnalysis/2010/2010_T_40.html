Sree Kamatchi Amman Constructions v The Divisional Railway Manager (Works), Palghat & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

20 August 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, H.L. Gokhale

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 650; (2010) 8 SCC 767; AIR 2010 SC 3337; 2010 (82) ALR 762; 2011 (2) AWC 1358; JT 2010 (9) SC 57; 2010 (8) MLJ(SC) 817; 2010(8) SCALE 293

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, s. 34 - Arbitral award - Non-award of interest pendente lite and for pre-reference period - First respondent entrusted certain construction work to the appellant under a contract - Alleging breach by the first respondent, the appellant invoked the arbitration clause - Disputes were referred to an arbitral tribunal - Arbitral tribunal made a non-speaking award in favour of the appellant - On appeal, HC set aside the said award and remitted the matter to the arbitral tribunal - Pursuant thereto, arbitral tribunal passed an award and awarded only future interest and refused to award the interest for pre-reference period and interest pendente lite - First respondent filed a petition u/s. 34 of the Act - Aggrieved by the rejection of its claims and failure to award interest for pre-reference period and pendente lite, the appellant also filed a petition u/s. 34 of the Act - HC rejected both the challenges to the award - Appeals filed thereagainst - Appeal filed by first respondent was allowed but appellant's appeal was dismissed - Hence, present appeal - (A) Award of interest - Two claims on which amounts were awarded by arbitral tribunal were with reference to claim No. (4) relating to erroneous billing and claim No. (6) relating to security deposit - Held, clause 16(2) of the agreement in terms specifically bars payment of interest on security deposit - Claim No.4 related to a work executed by the contractor as a part and parcel of the work contemplated under the agreement - Payment directed by the arbitral tribunal for such work was also in accordance with the Agreement - Therefore, the amount awarded in regard to claim No. (4) was an amount payable to the contractor under the contract and no interest could be paid thereon having regard to the bar under clause 16(2) of the General conditions of contract - (B) Jurisdiction and authority of the arbitrator to award interest - Whether the arbitral tribunal was justified in refusing interest for the period between the date of cause of action to date of award? - Held, in view of the specific bar under Clause 16(2), we are of the view that the arbitral tribunal was justified in refusing interest from the date of cause of action to date of awards - Appeals dismissed.
