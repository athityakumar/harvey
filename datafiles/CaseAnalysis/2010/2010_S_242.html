Ashok Kumar v State Of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

08 July 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Swatanter Kumar, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 502; (2010) 12 SCC 350; AIR 2010 SC 2839; 2010 ALL MR (Cri) 2642; 2010 CRLJ 4402; 2010 (4) JCC 2754; JT 2010 (7) SC 460; 2010 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 2561; 2010 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 2849; 2010 (4) MLJ(Crl) 259; 2010 (3) RCR(Criminal) 900; 2010(7) SCALE 30; [2010] 7 S.C.R. 1119; 2010 (2) UC 1081

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: (A) Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34 and 304-B - Dowry Prohibition Act, 1961, ss. 2 and 4 - Dowry death - Conviction - Challenged - Appellant, his brother and mother were charged u/s. 304-B r/w s. 34 of the IPC - Trial Court held all the three accused guilty of the offence punishable u/s. 304-B of the IPC - On appeal, mother and brother of the appellant were acquitted, while the conviction of appellant was upheld - Hence, present appeal - Held, definite evidence to show that nearly 20-22 days prior to her death the deceased had come to her parental home and informed her father about the demand and harassment and torture to which she was subjected to by the appellant and her in-laws - In the present case, ingredients of s. 304-B of IPC have been satisfied - FIR was registered one day after the incident, therefore, there was no abnormal or inordinate delay in lodging the FIR in the facts of this case - Contradictions or variations in substance in the statement of the prosecution witnesses are so insignificant that they would no way be fatal to the case of the prosecution - No infirmity in concurrent judgments of Sessions Judge and HC - Appeal disposed of.

(B) Criminal - Dowry Prohibition Act, 1961, ss. 2 and 4 - Meaning of Dowry - Held, 'dowry' means any property or valuable security given or agreed to be given either directly or indirectly by one party to another, by parents of either party to each other or any other person at, before, or at any time after the marriage and in connection with the marriage of the said parties but does not include dower or mahr under the Muslim Personal Law - Further, expression 'in connection with the marriage' u/s. 2 cannot be given a restricted or a narrower meaning - Object being that everything, which is offending at any time i.e. at, before or after the marriage, would be covered under this definition, but the demand of dowry has to be 'in connection with the marriage' and not so customary that it would not attract, on the face of it, the provisions of this section - Appeal disposed of.

(C) Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 304-B - Expression 'soon before her death' in s. 304-B - Meaning of - Held, expressions 'soon before her death' cannot be given a restricted or a narrower meaning - They must be understood in their plain language and with reference to their meaning in common parlance - However, the expression would normally imply that there has to be reasonable time gap between the cruelty inflicted and the death in question - There must be existence of proximate link between the acts of cruelty along with the demand of dowry and the death of the victim - For want of any specific period, the concept of reasonable period would be applicable - Moreover, expression 'soon before her death' has to be accorded its appropriate meaning in the facts and circumstances of a given case - Cruelty and harassment by the husband or any relative could be directly relatable to or in connection with, any demand for dowry - Appeal disposed of.

(D) Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 304-B - Presumption u/s. 304-B - Concept of deeming fiction - Held, legislature has applied the concept of deeming fiction to the provisions of s. 304-B of IPC - For constituting an offence u/s. 304-B of the IPC, the Court has to attach specific significance to the time of alleged cruelty and harassment to which the victim was subjected to and the time of her death, as well as whether the alleged demand of dowry was in connection with the marriage - Once these ingredients are satisfied, it would be called the 'dowry death' and then, by deemed fiction of law, the husband or the relatives would be deemed to have committed that offence - Such a presumption can be drawn by the Court keeping in view the evidence produced by the prosecution in support of the substantive charge u/s. 304-B of the IPC - Appeal disposed of.

(E) Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, s. 313 - Object of recording statement of accused u/s. 313 of CrPC - Held, object of recording the statement of the accused u/s. 313 of the CrPC is to put all incriminating evidence to the accused so as to provide him an opportunity to explain such incriminating circumstances appearing against him in the evidence of the prosecution - At the same time, also permit him to put forward his own version or reasons, if he so chooses, in relation to his involvement or otherwise in the crime - Use of a statement u/s. 313 of CrPC as an evidence is permissible as per the provisions of the Code but has its own limitations - Conviction of the accused cannot be based merely on the statement made u/s. 313 of the CrPC as it cannot be regarded as a substantive piece of evidence - Appeal disposed of.
