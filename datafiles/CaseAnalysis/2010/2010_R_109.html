Godrej Sara Lee Limited v Reckitt Benckiser Australia Private Limited And Another

Supreme Court of India

29 January 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 94; (2010) 2 SCC 535; AIR 2010 SC 1331; 2010(2) ALL MR 961; 2010 (6) AWC 6047; 2010 (2) CalLT 81; 2010 (1) CLT(SC) 268; JT 2010 (2) SC 148; 2010 (6) MLJ(SC) 325; 2010 (42) PTC 417; 2010 (2) RCR(Civil) 99; 2010(1) SCALE 714; [2010] 2 S.C.R. 147

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Intellectual Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Cancellation Of Registration, Patent Rules, 1972

Summary: Intellectual Property - Constitution - Practice & Procedure - Designs Act, 1911, s. 51A - Designs Act, 2000, s. 19(1) and 22(3) - Jurisdiction - Cancellation of registration - Respondent No. 1 and appellant filed suits against each other alleging infringement of their registered designs - Controller of Patents and Designs, Kolkata cancelled 2 registered designs belonging to the respondent No. 1 - Respondent no. 1 filed appeal against said order before Delhi HC - In respect of jurisdiction Delhi HC held that it had jurisdiction to entertain the appeal against the order passed by the Controller of Patents and Designs, Kolkata - Hence, present appeal - Whether Delhi HC has jurisdiction to entertain appeal filed against order of Controller of Patents and Designs, Kolkata? - Held, cause of action for the suit arose in Kolkata, which, in any event, had jurisdiction to entertain the suit - Having erroneously applied the decision in Girdharilal Gupta vs. K. Gian Chand Jain & Co. (1978) 14 D.L.T. 132, to the facts of the case, HC was led into error in holding that the consequence of the cancellation gave jurisdiction to the Delhi HC to entertain the suit, without considering in its proper perspective the provisions of s. 51A of the 1911 Act in contrast to the provisions of s. 19 of the 2000 Act - Impugned order of the Delhi HC cannot be sustained and set aside - Calcutta HC will have jurisdiction to entertain the appeal u/s. 19 of the 2000 Act- Appeal allowed.Intellectual Property - Constitution - Practice & Procedure - Designs Act, 1911, s. 51A - Designs Act, 2000, s. 19(1) and 22(3) - Jurisdiction - Held, question as to which HC would have jurisdiction to entertain an appeal u/s. 19 of Act, had to be determined on the basis of the statutory provisions and not on the basis of dominus litus or the situs of the Appellate Tribunal or the cause of action - Appeal allowed
