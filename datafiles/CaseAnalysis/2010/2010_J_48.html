M/S Southern Technologies Ltd. v Joint Commnr. Of Income Tax, Coimbatore

Supreme Court of India

11 January 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 21; (2010) 2 SCC 548; 2010 (1) BC 625; [2010] 153 Comp Cas 674; 2010 (1) CompLJ 577; JT 2010 (1) SC 145; 2010(1) SCALE 329; [2010] 1 S.C.R. 380; [2010] 187 TAXMAN 346

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Directions and Companies Act

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 2(24), 36(1)(vii), 37 and 145 - Deduction u/s. 36(1)(vii) of Act - Assessee claimed deduction u/s. 36(1)(vii) of the Act being provision for non- performing assets (NPA) in terms of RBI Directions 1998 on ground that assessee had to debit said amount to P&L Account reducing its profits, contending it to be a write off - In alternative, assessee submitted that consequent upon RBI Directions 1998 there has been diminution in value of its assets for which assessee was entitled to deduction u/s. 37 as a trading loss - Claim of appellant was dismissed on ground that provisions of s. 36(1)(vii) of IT Act could not be by-passed by RBI Directions 1998 - On appeal, Tribunal held that since assessee had debited said sum to P&L account it was entitled to claim deduction as a write off u/s.36(1)(vii) of Act - HC rejected said view - Department contended that IT Act is a separate code by itself; that taxable total income has to be computed strictly in terms of provisions of IT Act; that RBI Act, 1934 operates in the field of monetary and credit system and that said RBI Act never intended to compute taxable income of Non-banking Financial Company (NBFC) for income tax purposes; and, hence, there was no inconsistency between two Acts - Hence, present appeal - Whether Department is entitled to treat 'Provision for NPA', which in terms of RBI Directions 1998 is debited to P&L Account, as 'income' u/s. 2(24) of the IT Act, while computing profits and gains of business u/s. 28 to 43D of IT Act? - Held, basis of 1998 Directions is that anticipated losses must be taken into account but expected income need not be taken note of - These directions ensure cash liquidity for NBFCs which are now required to state true and correct profits, without projecting inflated profits - Therefore, RBI Directions 1998 deal only with presentation of NPA provisions in Balance Sheet of an NBFC - It has nothing to do with computation or taxability of provisions for NPA under IT Act - Though RBI Directions deviate from accounting practice as provided in Companies Act, they do not override provisions of IT Act - RBI Directions 1998 and IT Act operate in different fields - Provision for NPA in terms of RBI Directions 1998 does not constitute expense on basis of which deduction could be claimed by NBFC u/s. 36(1)(vii) of Act - Provision for NPAs is an expense for Presentation under 1998 Directions and in that sense it is notional - RBI Directions 1998 govern business of NBFCs - To protect investors, RBI has prescribed norms for provisioning and disclosure - These norms have nothing to do with computation of taxable income under IT Act - These Directions 1998 do not apply to banks - Ultimately, challenge is to validity of a taxing enactment - In such cases, some latitude has been given to law makers in enacting laws which impose reasonable restrictions u/art. 19(6) - This can be say for two reasons - Firstly, impugned allowance u/s. 36(1)(viia) of Act cannot be extended to NBFCs which are vulnerable to economic and financial uncertainties - Secondly, RBI Directions 1998 are only Disclosure Norms - They require NBFCs to make a Provision for possible loss to be made and disclosed to public - Such debits are only notional for purposes of disclosure, hence, they cannot be made an excuse for claiming deduction under IT Act, hence, 'add back' - Since RBI Direction 1998 is not applicable to Banks, there is no question of extending benefit of deduction to NBFCs u/s. 36(1)(viia) or u/s. 43D of Act - Hence, restriction, if any placed on NBFC by not giving them benefit of deduction, satisfies principle of 'reasonable justification - No merits in appeal - Appeals dismissed.Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 2(24), 36(1)(vii), 37 and 145 - Whether provision on NPA is allowable u/s. 37(1) of Act? - Held, s. 36(1)(vii) after 1-4-1989 draws a distinction between write off and provision for doubtful debt - IT Act deals only with doubtful debt - It is for assessee to establish that provision is made as loan is irrecoverable - However, in view of Explanation which keeps such a provision outside scope of 'written off' bad debt, s. 37 cannot come in - Section 37 applies only to items which do not fall in ss. 30 to 36 - If a provision for doubtful debt is expressly excluded from s.36(1)(vii) then such a provision cannot claim deduction u/s. 37 of IT Act even on basis of 'real income theory' - Appeals dismissed.
