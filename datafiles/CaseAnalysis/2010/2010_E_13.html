Executive Engineer (Electrical), Karantaka Power Transmission Corporation Limited (Earleir Karnataka Electricity Board) v Asstt. Commissioner and Lao, Gadag and others

Supreme Court of India

11 February 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 1109; (2010) 15 SCC 60; 2011(1) SCALE 288

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - Quantum of Compensation - Land acquisition proceedings - Award was made at the rate of Rs. 60,000 per acre - Reference was filed for enhancement of compensation - Claimants relied on four sales deeds for their claim for increase in compensation - Reference Court excluded the two sale deeds dated 19.9.1991 and 21.10.1991 on the ground that the said sales were not proximate in time and related to the transactions which had taken place about three years prior to acquisition - Reference Court relying on sale deed dated 27.7.1994 in regard to a plot virtually adjoining the acquired land increased the compensation to Rs. 3,79,260 per acre - On appeal, HC took into account not only the sale transaction dated 27.7.1994, but also the sale transactions dated 19.1.1991 and 21.10.1991 - Compensation was increased from Rs.3,79,260 to Rs. 6,79,935 per acre - Hence, present appeal - (A) Whether HC was not justified in taking into account sale transactions dated 19.1.1991 and 21.10.1991 in respect of far away plots? - Held, if other satisfactory evidence was not available and if they were in respect of similar land situated nearby, they could have been considered; but when a sale transaction relating to a nearby land, of the year 1994 itself, proximate in time to the date of preliminary notification, is available, there is no need or justification to refer to or rely upon sales which took place three years ago and that too in regard to lands which are far away from the acquired land. - Hence, when the sale transaction dated 27.7.1994 relating to a nearby land was available, there was no justification for the HC to have relied upon sale deeds dated 19.9.1991 and 21.10.1991 relating to faraway lands which were more advantageously situated - (B) Whether deduction for development cost ought to have been only 33% and not 50% adopted by the HC? - Held, lands formed a contiguous block and were all level lands - Having regard to these special features, deduction towards development cost should have been only 25% and not one-third as adopted by the Reference Court - Compensation reduced to Rs. 4,26,670 per acre (instead of Rs. 6,79,935 per acre awarded by the HC) with all statutory benefits - Appeals disposed of.
