L.M.L. Ltd. v Commissioner Of Customs

Supreme Court of India

21 September 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Mukundakam Sharma, Anil R. Dave

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 792; (2010) 10 SCC 503; 2010 (258) E.L.T. 321; JT 2010 (10) SC 286; 2010(10) SCALE 143; [2010] 12 S.C.R. 80

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Hsn Explanatory Notes

Summary: Customs - Indirect Tax - Customs Tariff Act, 1975, headings 49.06, 4901.90 and 8524.90 - Classification - 'CD ROM' containing images of drawing and designs of engineering goods - Appellant filed a bill of entry for clearance of the 'CD ROM' containing images of drawing and designs of engineering goods at Nil rate of duty by claiming classification of the same under the Tariff Heading 4906.00 r/w. heading 4901.90 coupled with the benefit of Nil rate of duty under Notification No. 17/2001 dt. 1.3.2001 - Adjudicating Officer rejected said claim of the appellant and held that the impugned goods are covered under heading 8524.90 - Commissioner dismissed appeal filed against said order - Tribunal also dismissed appeal filed against said order - Hence, present appeal - (A) Whether impugned goods are classifiable under the Tariff Heading 4906.00 r/w. heading 4901.90 of Customs Tariff Act? - Held, no - Products fall under Tariff Heading 49.06 only if consisting of originals either drawn or written by hand or of photographic reproductions on sensitized paper and carbon copies of such originals - The use of the word 'only' in the HSN Explanatory Notes goes to show that the said Heading was meant exclusively for that purpose alone and not otherwise - Hence, CD-ROM containing images of drawings and designs of engineering goods are not classifiable under the Tariff Heading 49.06 or under Heading 49.11. as other printed matter - (B) Whether data in a compact disk falls within the meaning of the term software and are classifiable under heading 8524.39 or 8524.90 of the Tariff? - Held, no - Impugned engineering drawings and designs do not provide instructions for the computer hardware to perform - Said drawings and designs can be said to be are by-products and outputs of the computer software, which generate the designs and drawings - Therefore, such engineering drawings or designs data in a CD cannot be placed in the category of the term 'software' - Hence, engineering drawings and designs contained in a CD ROM will not be covered Heading 85.24 of the Tariff - Appeal dismissed.
