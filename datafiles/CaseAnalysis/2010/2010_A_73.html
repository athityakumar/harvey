G. Vallikumari v Andhra Education Society and others

Supreme Court of India

02 February 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 69; (2010) 2 SCC 497; AIR 2010 SC 1105; 2010 (125) FLR 1048; JT 2010 (3) SC 75; 2010 (3) MLJ(SC) 1375; 2010(2) SCALE 1; 2010 (2) SLR 175

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Kerala University Act, 1957

Summary: Service - Constitution - Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 30(1) - Delhi School Education Act, 1973, ss. 12, 3(3)(a), 4, 8, 8(1), 8(2), 8(3), 8(4), 8(5) - Kerala University Act, 1957, s. 19(j) - Delhi School Education Rules, 1973, rr. 120, 120(1)(iv), 120(2) - Misconduct - Suspension - Reinstatement - Justification - Respondent No.1 was educational society and respondent No.2 was private linguistic minority school established by respondent No.1 - Appellant was appointed as Upper Division Clerk (UDC) by respondent Nos. 1 and 2 and was approved by Director - Appellant was granted permission by management for doing PG Diploma in HRD Programme and she applied for study leave for attending training programme and for preparing for examination - Management refused to accept her joining when she reported after three days of her leave and initiated inquiry against her on charges of neglecting duties as UDC, availing leave without prior permission, absence from duty, misplacing office records, failure to submit important office records/registers to superiors and flouting directions given by management of school - Appellant filed reply and considering same, disciplinary committee recommended appellant's removal from service - Managing Committee accepted same and sent letter to Director seeking his approval in terms of Rule 120(2) of Rules and Director declined to approve proposal - As a follow up, Chairman of Managing Committee passed order removing appellant from service - Appeal filed by appellant was allowed by Tribunal mainly on ground of violation of S. 8(2) of the Act and Rule 120(2) of Rules and held that management of school could not have removed appellant from service without obtaining permission of Director - Writ Petition filed by respondents was allowed by DB of HC and declared S. 12 of 1973 Act ultra vires to provisions of Constitution and in so far applicability of exclusion clause contained therein was restricted to unaided minority institutions, that S. 8(2) of the Act was not applicable to minority institutions and set aside direction given by Tribunal for reinstatement of appellant with all consequential benefits - Hence, instant appeal - (A) Whether s. 12 of the 1973 Act was ultra virus to provisions of Constitution and whether s. 8 (2) of the Act was applicable to minority institution - Held, s. 8(1), (3), (4) and (5) of the Act did not violate right of the minorities to establish and administer their educational institutions - However, s. 8(2) interferes with said right of minorities and was, therefore, inapplicable to private recognized aided/unaided minority educational institutions - Further, s. 12 of the Act, which makes provisions of Chapter IV of the Act inapplicable to unaided private recognized minority educational institutions was discriminatory except to extent of s. 8(2) of the 1973 Act - In other words, Chapter IV of the Act except s. 8(2) was applicable to private recognized aided as well as unaided minority educational institutions and concerned authorities of education department were bound to enforce same against all such institutions - (B) Whether DB was justified in setting aside direction given by Tribunal for reinstatement of appellant with consequential benefits - Held, Chairman of Managing Committee did refer to allegations leveled against appellant and representation submitted by her in light of findings recorded by inquiry officer but without even adverting to contents of her representation and giving semblance of indication of application of mind in context of r. 120(1)(iv) of Rules, he directed her removal from service - Therefore, there was no escape from conclusion that order of punishment was passed by Chairman without complying with mandate of relevant statutory rule and principles of natural justice - Tribunal's order shows that though it did not find any procedural infirmity in inquiry against appellant, order passed by Chairman of Managing Committee was nullified only on the ground of violation of s. 8(2) of the Act r/w r. 120(2) of Rules inasmuch as permission of Director was not obtained before removing appellant from service - HC set aside order of Tribunal and indirectly restored order passed by Chairman of Managing Committee because it was of view that S. 8(2) was not applicable to minority institutions - Neither Tribunal nor DB dealt with and decided appellant's challenge to findings recorded by inquiry officer and her plea that extreme penalty of removal from service imposed on her was not justified because she was not found guilty of any serious misconduct - Impugned order of HC was set aside - Punishment of removal from service imposed on appellant was substituted with penalty of stoppage of three increments without cumulative effect - Appeal allowed.
