India Meters Limited v State of Tamil Nadu

Supreme Court of India

07 September 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Dalveer Bhandari, Deepak Verma

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 745; (2010) 9 SCC 423; JT 2010 (9) SC 373; 2010(9) SCALE 75; [2010] 11 S.C.R. 22; [2010] 34 VST 273

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Keywords: Sale Price, Addition, Rajasthan Sales Tax Act, 1954, Tamil Nadu General Sales Tax Rules, 1959, Insurance Charges, Freight And Insurance Charges, Kerala General Sales Tax Rules, 1963

Summary: Sales Tax - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956 - Tamil Nadu General Sales Tax Act, 1959 - Tamil Nadu General Sales Tax Rules, 1959, r. 6(c) - A/y 1986-87 - Deduction for freight and insurance charges - Appellant-Manufacturer and dealer of electric meters, made original assessment without taking the freight charges into account for the a/y 1986-87 - During inspection, Inspecting Officer found that the assessee had collected freight charges and insurance charges separately under the debit notes for a total sum of Rs.16,96,530/- but the same had not been shown in the monthly returns - Assessing Officer determined 50% of that amount of Rs.16,96,530/- as freight charges, after making allowance for the insurance amount and levied tax on that amount of the freight - Assistant Commissioner allowed appeal filed against said order by appellant - Revenue filed appeal against order of Assistant Commissioner which was allowed by Tribunal - HC affirmed the order of the Tribunal - Appellant filed appeal against said order submitting that since the contract separates the ex-factory price and the insurance and freight charges, and, u/r. 6(c) of Rules, the freight when specified and charged for by the dealer separately, without including the same in the price charged by the dealer, the freight charged here could not have been treated as part of the sale price and subjected to tax - Whether freight and insurance charges incurred by the dealer could be treated as part of the sale price and subjected to tax? - Held, as per English cases, Paprika Ltd. & Another v. Board of Trade [1944] K.B. 327 and Love v. Norman Wright (Builders) Ltd. [1944] K.B. 484 and SC decision in Dyer Meakin Breweries Ltd. v. State of Kerala 1969 INDLAW SC 56, amount of freight and insurance charges incurred by the dealer forms part of the sale price - But in present case, there was specific contract entered into by and between the parties and according to the relevant clause of the contract, the ownership of the goods will remain with the supplier till they are delivered at the destination station - In view of the clear clause of the contract, no other view is possible - HC was totally justified in affirming the judgment of the Tribunal - No interference is called for - Appeals dismissed.
