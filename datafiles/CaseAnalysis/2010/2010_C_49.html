Seshambal (Dead), Through L.Rs. v Chelur Corporation Chelur Building And Others

Supreme Court of India

17 February 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 104; (2010) 3 SCC 470; 2010 (2) ADJ 807; AIR 2010 SC 1521; 2010 (79) ALR 462; JT 2010 (2) SC 260; 2010 (1) KLT 834; 2010 (3) MLJ(SC) 174; 2010 (1) RCR(Rent) 230; 2010 (1) RLW 848; 2010(2) SCALE 414; [2010] 2 S.C.R. 960

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - Kerala Buildings (Lease and Rent Control Act), 1965, s. 11(3) - Eviction petition - Bona fide requirement - Landlord filed a eviction petition on the ground that they required the same for their bona fide personal occupation within the meaning of s. 11(3) of the Act - Rent Controller and the Rent Control Appellate Authority dismissed the eviction petition - Revision petition filed thereagainst before HC was also dismissed - During the pendency of the revision petition landlord passed away - Present appeal - Whether the proceedings instituted by the deceased-owners of the demised property could be continued by the legal heirs left behind by them? - Held, requirement pleaded in the eviction petition by the original landlord was their own personal requirement and not the requirement of the members of their family whether dependant or otherwise - On the death of the original landlord their right to seek eviction on the ground of personal requirement for the demised premises became extinct and no order could on the basis of any such requirement be passed at this point of time - Appeal disposed of.
