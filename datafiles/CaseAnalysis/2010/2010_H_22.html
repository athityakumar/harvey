Haridwar Development Authority v Raghubir Singh

Supreme Court of India

29 January 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 305; (2010) 11 SCC 581; AIR 2010 SC 1754; 2010 (3) ALD(SC) 123; 2010 (2) AWC 1974; 2010 (1) CLT(SC) 420; JT 2010 (2) SC 502; 2010 (4) MLJ(SC) 546; 2010 (2) RCR(Civil) 301; 2010 (110) RD 151; 2010(2) SCALE 503; [2010] 2 S.C.R. 201

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4, 6, 23(1A), 23(2) and 28 - Land acquisition proceedings - Compensation - Grant of - Land Acquisition Collector made an award dividing acquired lands into three belts - Reference Court restricted the division of the acquired land into two belts and adopted the rates fixed by the Collector for the first and second belts - On appeal, HC awarded a uniform rate for all the acquired lands and awarded additional amount u/s. 23(1A), solatium u/s. 23(2) and interest u/s. 28 of the Act - Present appeal - (A) Whether HC ought to have adopted the belt method for award of compensation? - Held, question whether the acquired lands have to be valued uniformly at the same rate, or whether different areas in the acquired lands have to be valued at different rates, depends upon the extent of the land acquired, the location, proximity to an access road/Main Road/Highway or to a City/Town/Village, and other relevant circumstances - In the present case, land acquired relates to a small extent of village - HC was of the view that the size and situation did not warrant any belting and all lands deserved the same rate of compensation - Authority has not placed any material to show that any area was less advantageously situated - Therefore, the view of the HC that compensation should be awarded at an uniform rate does not call for interference - (B) Whether the compensation awarded requires to be increased? - Held, value arrived at by the Collector, and accepted by the Reference Court and the HC requires to be increased by 12% in view of the fact that the preliminary notification was one year after the relied upon sale transaction - (C) Whether the award of interest u/s. 28 of the Act not sustainable? - Held, scheme of the Act is that in regard to the increase in such compensation, which is awarded by the Reference Court or any appellate court, such interest is awarded u/s. 28 of the Act - Award of interest on the enhanced amount u/s. 28 of the Act is the normal rule - Contention of the Authority that the HC ought not to have awarded interest u/s. 28 is therefore untenable - Claimants entitled to all the statutory benefits - Appeals disposed of.
