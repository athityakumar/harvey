Indian Oil Corporation Limited v Commissioner of Central Excise, Vadodara

Supreme Court of India

22 October 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

D.K. Jain, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 880; (2010) 12 SCC 750; 2010 (259) E.L.T. 172; JT 2010 (11) SC 581; 2010(11) SCALE 215

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - Indirect Tax - Central Excise Act, 1944, s. 11A - Central Excise Rules, 1944, r. 173Q - Demand - Penalty - Concessional rate of Excise duty under Notifications No. 5/98-CE and 5/99-CE - Kerosene - Assessee-Manufacturer of petroleum products, claimed the benefit of concessional rate of duty under notification no. 5/98-CE and under notification no. 5/99-CE for their kerosene products - Subsequently, department issued three show cause notices to the assessee, dt. 31.03.1999, 12.07.1999 and 19.11.1999 for the periods September, 1998 to February 1999, March to April 1999 and May to September 1999, proposing to recover the amounts of Rs. 86,089/-, '1,46,731/- and '47,012/- respectively for the said periods - Asst. Commissioner confirmed the said demands u/s. 11A of the Act and held that the benefit of concessional rate of duty under the said Notifications cannot be extended to kerosene products sold and used for industrial purposes - Asst. Commissioner also imposed a penalty of Rs.10,000/- u/r. 173Q of the 1994 Rules - Commissioner (Appeals) dismissed appeal filed against said order, however, he deleted the penalty - On appeal, Tribunal confirmed the order of the Commissioner (Appeals) - Hence, present appeals - Whether benefit of concessional rate of duty under the Notification Nos. 5/98-CE and 5/99-CE cannot be extended to kerosene products sold and used for industrial purposes? - Held, from a perusal of the said two notifications it is plain that the benefit of concessional rate of duty extends only to that variety of kerosene that: (i) has a smoke point of 18mm or more, and (ii) is ordinarily used as an illuminate in oil burning lamps - It is manifest that these two conditions are conjunctive, and therefore, the twin conditions need to be satisfied in order to avail of the concessional rate of duty - In the present case, the fact that the assessee cleared kerosene manufactured by it to industrial consumers would entail that the assessee cannot claim the benefit of Notifications No. 5/98-CE and 5/99-CE. - Impugned order of the tribunal upheld - Appeals dismissed.
