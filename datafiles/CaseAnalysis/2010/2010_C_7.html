C.I.T., Ahmedabad v Reliance Petroproducts Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

17 March 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

V.S. Sirpurkar, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 215; (2010) 11 SCC 762; AIR 2010 SC 1881; [2010] 322 ITR 158; JT 2010 (3) SC 88; 2010(3) SCALE 1; [2010] 3 S.C.R. 510; [2010] 189 TAXMAN 322

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 14A, 36(1) (iii) and 271(1)(c) - A/Y 2001-02 - Penalty u/s. 271(1)(c) - Concealment of income/furnishing of inaccurate particulars of income - Assessee claimed deduction u/s. 36(1)(iii) of Act for interest paid on loan taken for purchase of shares - Assessing Authority disallowed said claim regarding interest u/s. 14A of Act and levied penalty u/s. 271(1)(c) on the ground that said claim was unsustainable - Assessee filed appeal before Commissioner contending that mere disallowance of the claim in the assessment proceedings could not be the sole basis for levying penalty u/s. 271(1)(c) of Act - Commissioner (Appeals), however, deleted the said penalty - Order of the Commissioner (Appeals) was confirmed by Income Tax Appellate Tribunal and HC - Whether the assessee liable to pay the penalty u/s. 271(1)(c) of the Act ordered by the Assessing Authority? - Held, no in order to expose the assessee to the penalty, there has to be concealment of the particulars of the income of the assessee and secondly, the assessee must have furnished inaccurate particulars of his income - It must be shown that the conditions u/s. 271(1)(c) of the Act must exist before the penalty is imposed - Present is not the case of concealment of the income as making an incorrect claim in law cannot tantamount to furnishing inaccurate particulars - In the present case, there is no finding that any details supplied by the assessee in its return were found to be incorrect or erroneous or false - This not being the case, there would be no question of inviting the penalty u/s. 271(1)(c) of the Act - Penalty levied u/s. 271(1)(c) of the Act not sustainable - Revenue's appeal dismissed.
