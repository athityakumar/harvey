Vijendra Kumar Verma v Public Service Commission, Uttarakhand and others

Supreme Court of India

08 October 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Mukundakam Sharma, A.R. Dave

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 852; (2011) 1 SCC 150; 2011 (2) AWC 1503; 2010 (5) ESC 649; 2010 (127) FLR 723; JT 2010 (11) SC 111; 2010(10) SCALE 632; [2010] 12 S.C.R. 944; 2010 (4) UPLBEC 2930

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Service

Keywords: Uttaranchal Judicial Service Rules, 2005

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Service - Uttaranchal Judicial Service Rules, 2005 - Selection to the lower judiciary - Requirement of Computer Knowledge - Respondent/State Service Commission invited applications for the posts of Civil Judge (Junior Division) - Appellant/candidate applied for the post and succeeded in preliminary examination and viva voce examination thereafter - Appellant, who secured the aggregate mark of 576, was not selected, however, persons getting 568 marks were inducted into service - Appellant filed writ petition before the HC - Respondent contended that appellant did not have the basic knowledge of computer operation which was one of the essential qualification for the post - HC dismissed appellant's writ petition - (A) Whether appellant is entitled to the selection for the post of Civil Judge (Junior Division) - Held, in cl. 4(c) of the recruitment notification, it was specifically mentioned that the candidate should have basic knowledge of computer operation - In the interview letter send to the appellant also, it was specifically mentioned that basic knowledge of the computer operation would be essential to the candidate - Basic knowledge of the appellant in computer operation was tested at the time of his interview by an expert who was sitting in the interview board - Said expert after testing the knowledge, the suitability of the appellant and his basic knowledge in computer operation gave his opinion that the appellant did not possess the basic knowledge of computer operation - Since possession of such knowledge of computer operation was one of the eligibility criteria for being selected for the post of Civil Judge and as the appellant was not found suitable and lacking in basic knowledge of computer operation, he was not selected - Therefore, appellant is not entitled to the selection for the post of Civil Judge (Junior Division) - (B) Whether requirement of having computer knowledge to the post of Civil Judge need to be diluted - Held, Indian judiciary is taking steps to apply e-governance for efficient management of courts - In the near future, all the courts in the country will be computerized - Therefore, the new judges who are being appointed are expected to have basic knowledge of the computer operation and it will be unfair to overlook basic knowledge of computer operation to be an essential condition for being a judge in view of the recent development being adopted - Hence, requirement of having basic knowledge of computer operation should not be diluted - Appeal dismissed.
