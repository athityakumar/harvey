Dharampal Singh and another v State Of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

09 September 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Chandramauli Kumar Prasad, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 777; (2010) 9 SCC 608; JT 2010 (10) SC 513; 2010 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 2998; 2010 (4) RCR(Criminal) 504; 2010(10) SCALE 229; [2010] 10 S.C.R. 1160; 2010 (3) UC 1774

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Opium Act, 1878

Summary: Criminal - Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985, ss. 18 and 50 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 313 - Possession of contraband - Conviction - Challenged - Charge-sheet u/s. 18 of the Act was submitted against appellants - It was alleged that appellants were in possession of opium recovered from the vehicle driven and occupied by them - Trial Court on appreciation of evidence held that the prosecution had failed to prove, the compliance of s. 50 of the Act and accordingly acquitted both the appellants - On appeal, HC held that since the recovery was effected from the vehicle and not from the person of the appellants, the provisions of s. 50 of the Act were not applicable and as such the question of violation thereof did not arise - HC further held that appellants were in conscious possession of opium and convicted them - Hence, present appeal - (A) Presumption - Whether fact that opium was found in the vehicle, which the appellants were occupying, sufficient to establish conscious possession? - Held, to bring the offence within the mischief of s. 18 of the Act possession has to be conscious possession - Initial burden of proof of possession lies on prosecution and once it is discharged legal burden would shift on accused - Further, s. 54 of Act creates a legal fiction and presumes the person in possession of illicit articles to have committed the offence in case he fails to account for the possession satisfactorily - Once possession is established the Court can presume that the accused had culpable mental state and committed the offence - Possession, therefore, has to be understood in the context thereof - In the present case, appellants have not been able to account for satisfactorily the possession of opium - (B) Whether conviction of appellants was vitiated on the ground that circumstance that the appellants were in conscious possession of the opium was not put to them while being examined u/s. 313 of the CrPC? - Held, trial would be vitiated on this ground only when on fact it was found that it had occasioned a failure of justice - In the present case, circumstances appearing against appellants were put to them in their statement u/s. 313 of the CrPC - No failure of justice - (C) No independent witness of search and seizure examined - Effect thereof - Whether prosecution case can be rejected on the ground that independent witnesses have not been examined? - Held, No - Case of the prosecution cannot be rejected only on the ground that independent witnesses have not been examined, in case on appraisal of the evidence on record the court finds the case of prosecution to be trustworthy - In the present case, an attempt was made to join person from public at the time of search but none was available - Mere absence of independent witness at the time of search and seizure will not render the case of the prosecution unreliable - Appeals dismissed.
