Oriental Aroma Chemical Industries Limited v Gujarat Industrial Development Corporation and another

Supreme Court of India

26 February 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 149; (2010) 5 SCC 459; AIR 2010 SC (Supp) 697; 2010 (3) ALD(SC) 116; 2010 (79) ALR 647; 2010 (3) AWC 2609; 2010 (1) CLT(SC) 333; 2010 (1) DRTC 617; JT 2010 (2) SC 389; 2010 (4) MLJ(SC) 141; 2010 (3) MPLJ 506; 2010 (2) OLR 212; 2010 (2) RCR(Civil) 284; 2010 (110) RD 27; 2010(2) SCALE 645; [2010] 2 S.C.R. 1172

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Civil Procedure - Contract & Commercial - Limitation Act, 1963, s. 5 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, o. 41 r. (3A) - Appellant was allotted a piece of land for setting up an industrial unit subject to the terms and conditions embodied in License Agreement which, among other things, provided for consumption of specified quantity of water by the appellant - Agreement also provided for payment of 70% of the cost of agreed quantity of water irrespective of consumption - In 1982, respondent no.1 demanded non utilization charges which were deposited by the appellant - Later, respondent no.1 issued bill dt. 13-1-1996 towards water charges - Appellant challenged the same in special civil suit which was decreed on 30-10-2004 and it was declared that the appellant is not liable to pay Rs.22,96,207/- by way of minimum charges for water for the period between 1978 and 16-4-2001 and, thereafter, till the water was supplied by respondent no.1 - Appellant filed another suit for issuance of no objection certificate in its favour - Said suit was also decreed and respondent no. 1 was directed to issue no objection certificate to the appellant - Respondent filed an appeal against said order with an application for condonation of delay on the ground that petitioner company being a Government Corporation is bound to follow the rules and regulations as it is and cannot deviate itself from the provisions of law - HC condoned the delay - Hence, present appeal - Whether HC was justified in condoning more than four years' delay in filing of appeal by the respondents? - Held, no - HC did make a bald reference to the application for condonation of delay filed by the respondents but allowed the same without adverting to the averments contained therein and the reply filed on behalf of the appellant - Not only this, HC erroneously assumed that the delay was of 1067 days, though, as a matter of fact, the appeal was filed after more than four years - Law Department of respondent No.1 was aware of the proceedings of the first as well as the second suit but none of the officers is shown to have personally contacted either of the advocates for the purpose of filing written statement and preparation of the case and none bothered to appear before the trial Court on any of the dates of hearing - Delay not to be condoned - Impugned order of HC set aside - Appeal allowed.
