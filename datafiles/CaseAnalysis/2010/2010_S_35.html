Sajjan Kumar v Central Bureau of Investigation

Supreme Court of India

20 September 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, A.R. Dave

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 763; (2010) 9 SCC 368; JT 2010 (10) SC 413; 2011 (1) MLJ(Crl) 552; 2010 (4) RCR(Criminal) 382; 2010(10) SCALE 22; [2010] 11 S.C.R. 669; 2010 (3) UC 1719

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: (A) Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 227 and 228 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120-B, 147, 148, 149, 153A, 295, 302, 339, 395, 427, 435, 436, and 505 - Anti-Sikh riot cases - Framing of charges - In view of statement by the victims before Justice Nanavati Commission and its recommendation, the Government of India took a decision to entrust further/re-investigation in respect of 1984 anti-Sikh riots through CBI - Thereafter, Closure Report was filed by Delhi Police - After fresh investigation, CBI filed charge-sheet and charges were framed - Additional Sessions Judge ordered the framing of charges against the appellant for offences punishable u/s. 120B r/w ss. 153A, 295, 302, 395, 427, 436, 339 and 505 of the IPC and for the offence u/s. 109 r/w ss. 147, 148, 149, 153A, 295, 302, 395, 427, 435, 339 and 505 IPC, besides framing of a separate charge for offence punishable u/s. 153A of IPC and rejected the application for discharge filed by the appellant - Revision filed thereagainst before HC was also dismissed - Hence, present appeal - Whether framing of charges against the appellant and others was in consonance with ss. 227 and 228 of the CrPC? - Held, presumption of the guilt of the accused which is to be drawn at the initial stage is only for the purpose of deciding prima facie whether the Court should proceed with the trial or not - Further, at the stage of framing of charge u/s. 228 of the CrPC or while considering the discharge petition filed u/s. 227, it is not for the Magistrate or a Judge concerned to analyse all the materials including pros and cons, reliability or acceptability etc - It is at the trial, the Judge concerned has to appreciate their evidentiary value, credibility or otherwise of the statement, veracity of various documents and free to take a decision one way or the other - In the present case, it cannot be presumed that there was no case at all to proceed - It cannot be concluded that framing of charges against the appellant by the Trial Judge was either bad in law or abuse of process of law or without any material - However, de hors to comments, observations and explanations emanating from the judgment of the Single Judge, Trial Judge was free to analyse, appreciate, evaluate and arrive at a proper conclusion based on the materials being placed by prosecution as well as the defence - Appeal disposed of.

(B) Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 227 and 228 - Scope of ss. 227 and 228 of the CrPC - Principles to exercise of jurisdiction u/ss. 227 and 228 of the CrPC - Held, (i) Judge while considering the question of framing the charges u/s. 227 of the CrPC has the power to sift and weigh the evidence for the limited purpose of finding out whether or not a prima facie case against the accused has been made out - (ii) Where the materials placed before the Court disclose grave suspicion against the accused which has not been properly explained, the Court will be fully justified in framing a charge and proceeding with the trial - (iii) Court has to consider the broad probabilities of the case, the total effect of the evidence and the documents produced before the Court, any basic infirmities etc - However, at this stage, there cannot be a roving enquiry into the pros and cons of the matter and weigh the evidence as if he was conducting a trial - (iv) If on the basis of the material on record, the Court could form an opinion that the accused might have committed an offence, it can frame the charge, though for conviction the conclusion is required to be proved beyond reasonable doubt that the accused committed the offence - (v) At the time of framing of the charges, the probative value of the material on record cannot be gone into but before framing a charge the Court must apply its judicial mind on the material placed on record and must be satisfied that the commission of offence by the accused was possible - (vi) If two views are possible and one of them gives rise to suspicion only, as distinguished from grave suspicion, the Trial Judge will be empowered to discharge the accused and at this stage, he is not to see whether the trial will end in conviction or acquittal - Appeal disposed of.
