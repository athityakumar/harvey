Grid Corporation of Orissa Limited and others v Eastern Metals and Ferro Alloys and others

Supreme Court of India

31 August 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, H.L. Gokhale

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 705; (2011) 11 SCC 334; JT 2010 (9) SC 404; 2010(8) SCALE 687; [2010] 10 S.C.R. 779

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Electricity & Energy

Keywords: Orissa Electricity Reform Act, 1995

Summary: Electricity & Energy - Constitution - Orissa Electricity Reform Act, 1995 - State Government u/s. 14(iv) of Act, 1995, issued 3 licences to the appellant authorising it to engage in the business of transmission, bulk supply and retail supply and distribution of electrical energy within the State of Orissa - Appellant took over the transmission, distribution and supply of electricity from the Orissa State Electricity Board (OSEB) and on that time interim tariff which came into effect on 5.11.1995, contained in the notification dt. 28.10.1995 was in force - Appellant by notification dt. 13.5.1996, revised/prescribed the electricity charges for different categories of consumers of electricity in the State - Respondent filed writ petition challenging the notification dt. 13.5.1996 and proviso of licence which enabled the appellant to increase the tariff not exceeding on average 117% of those permitted under the interim tariff issued by the State Government - HC held that while the appellant could increase tariff upto 17% in terms of the licence, there was no power or authority to increase the tariff rates beyond 17% in respect of any particular category of consumers - HC also quashed the tariff notification dt. 13.5.1996 as also the demands under the respective bills raised against the respondents - Hence, present appeal - Whether HC rightly held that appellant has no power or authority to increase the tariff rates beyond 17% in respect of any particular category of consumers and rightly quashed the tariff notification dt. 13.5.1996? - Held, appellant, discharging the functions of the state government under the Act, had to ensure that the burden of increase on the agriculturist-consumers, that is those consuming electricity for "irrigation", should be the minimum - Similarly, increase in the tariff rate for electricity consumed by railway traction had to be kept minimal in national economic interest - Similarly, the increase in tariff for residential user should be comparatively lesser than commercial user - At the same time, the appellant had to ensure an increase in its revenue by 17% - If increase beyond 17% was not permissible in regard to any category of consumers, and if some categories had to be subjected to only small increases far below 17%, due to economic or social justice criteria, the appellant would never be able to achieve any increase beyond the range of 17% - Only by adopting the process of applying a higher than 17% increase in the case of some categories of consumers, it can offset the effect of small or marginal increase in the case of some other categories like 'irrigation' and 'railway traction" - Therefore, if appellant chose to charge a lesser increase in percentage to some categories of consumers and higher increases in regard to other categories of consumers, it cannot be found fault with so long as its total revenue does not exceed 17% over the corresponding revenue with reference to the old interim tariff rates - Hence, HC was not justified in holding that the tariff rate in regard to none of the category of consumers can exceed 17% over the previous rates - Impugned judgment of HC set aside - Appeals allowed.
