Chirra Shivraj v State Of Andhra Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

26 November 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Anil R. Dave, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 1000; 2011 CRLJ 971; 2011 (1) JCC 61; 2011 (1) MLJ(Crl) 812; 2011 (2) RCR(Criminal) 96; 2010(12) SCALE 487

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 304 Part II - Conviction - Challenged - Appellant was convicted for the offence punishable u/s. 304 Part II of the IPC - It was alleged that the deceased had strained relations with her husband's brother, the appellant - Deceased stated in her statement that she was being abused by the appellant and on the day of the incident while she was being abused, she poured kerosene on herself where after the appellant threw a lighted match stick on her thereby severely burning her - On appeal, HC confirmed the order of conviction passed by the Trial Court - Hence, present appeal - (A) Whether Trial Court erred in convicting the appellant only on the basis of the dying declaration? - Held, if dying declaration is trustworthy and if it can be shown that the person making the statement was not influenced by any exterior factor and made the statement which was duly recorded, it can be made the basis for conviction - Trial Court had duly considered the fact that the dying declaration was trustworthy and reliable and it was supported by the complaint and as a result thereof, the order of conviction was also confirmed by the HC in the appeal - (B) Whether case of the prosecution was based on the second FIR which was filed upon the death of the deceased? - It was submitted that there could not have been a second FIR and investigation was made under the first FIR and ultimately the order of conviction was passed in pursuance of the second FIR, which was bad in law - Held, in the present case, by virtue of the second FIR, information was given to the Authorities with regard to the death of the deceased which resulted due to septicemia on account of the burn injuries suffered by the deceased - There was no fresh investigation in pursuance of the second FIR - Further, appellant could not show that the information with regard to the death of the deceased, which was recorded as second FIR caused any prejudice to the accused - Hence, it cannot be said that merely because second FIR was filed, the entire investigation was defective and that should result into acquittal of the accused - Appeal dismissed.
