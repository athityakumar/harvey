Mannu Sao v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

22 July 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Swatanter Kumar, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 582; (2010) 12 SCC 310; JT 2010 (8) SC 449; 2011 (1) MLJ(Crl) 80; 2010 (3) RCR(Criminal) 805; 2010(7) SCALE 138; [2010] 8 S.C.R. 811

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 201 and 302 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 313 - Appellant convicted for offence punishable u/ss. 201 and 302 of IPC - Appeal filed thereagainst was dismissed - Hence, present appeal - (A) Circumstantial evidence - Whether complete chain of events established by the prosecution beyond reasonable doubt? - Held, circumstances proved by the prosecution are of a conclusive nature and they do exclude the possibility of any other view which could be taken rationally and reasonably - Further, appellant had clearly and in unambiguous terms admitted that the deceased was his wife and she died of burn injuries - Medical evidence had shown that the deceased was strangulated or throttled before her body was burnt - (B) Motive - Held, deceased had left her earlier husband and was living with the appellant, who was also staying away from his family - There was definite protest raised by the villagers to their living together - Social embarrassment could be a plausible motive for the appellant to commit the crime - Motive appears to be reasonable and is in consonance with the behaviour of a person placed in a situation like the appellant - No infirmity in judgment - Appeal dismissed.
