Techno Shares and Stocks Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax IV

Supreme Court of India

09 September 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 733; (2010) 9 SCC 410; 2010 (4) CompLJ 60; [2010] 327 ITR 323; JT 2010 (9) SC 488; 2010(9) SCALE 251; [2010] 11 S.C.R. 437; [2010] 193 TAXMAN 248

Case Digest

Subject: Capital Market; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Capital Market - BSE Membership - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 32(1)(ii) - A/ys 1999-2000, 2000-2001, 2001-2002 and 2002-2003 - Depreciation u/s. 32(1)(ii) - Assessee claimed depreciation u/s. 32(1)(ii) of IT Act regarding BSE membership card submitting that BSE membership card is a "licence" or "business or commercial right of similar nature" u/s 32(1)(ii) of IT Act and is, therefore, an intangible asset eligible for depreciation u/s 32(1)(ii) - Said submission was not accepted by the Assessing Officer who held that membership is only a personal permission which is non-transferable and which does not devolve automatically on legal heirs and, therefore, it is not a privately owned asset - Order of the AO upheld by the Commissioner - On appeal, Tribunal held that since the assessee had acquired a right to trade on the floor of BSE through the membership card, it was not entitled to depreciation u/s 32(1)(ii) of the IT Act - HC held that expression "licence" as well as the expression "business and commercial rights of similar nature" in s. 32(1)(ii) of the IT Act are referable to IPRs such as know-how, patent, copyright, trademark and franchise and since the BSE membership card does not fall in any of the above categories, the claim for depreciation was not admissible on the BSE membership card - Hence, present appeals - (A) Is depreciation allowable on the cost of a Stock Exchange Membership Card u/s. 32(1)(ii) of the IT Act? - BSE Card is an "intangible asset" and eligible for depreciation u/s 32(1)(ii) of IT Act - Held, right of BSE membership is a "business or commercial right" which gives a non-defaulting continuing member a right to access the Exchange and to participate therein and in that sense it is a licence or akin to licence in terms of s. 32(1)(ii) of the IT Act - Hence, BSE membership card is eligible for depreciation u/s. 32(1)(ii) of IT Act - (B) Whether the right of nomination in the non-defaulting continuing member comes within the expression "business or commercial right of similar nature" in s. 32(1)(ii) of the IT Act? - Held, right of membership (including right of nomination) gets vested in the Exchange on the demise/ default committed by the member; that, on such forfeiture and vesting in the Exchange the same gets disposed of by inviting offers and the consideration received thereof is used to liquidate the dues owed by the former/ defaulting member to the Exchange, Clearing House, etc. - It is this right of membership which allows the non-defaulting member to participate in the trading session on the floor of the Exchange - Thus, the said membership right is a "business or commercial right" conferred by the Rules of BSE on the non-defaulting continuing member - (C) Whether the membership right could be said to be owned by the assessee and used for the business purpose in terms of s. 32(1)(ii) of IT Act? - Held, membership right is a licence which enables the member to trade on the floor of the Exchange and to participate in the trading session on the floor of the Exchange - It is this licence which enables the member to access the market - Right to participate in the market has an economic and monetary value - It is an expense incurred by the assessee which satisfies the test of being a "licence" or "any other business or commercial right of similar nature" in terms of s. 32(1)(ii) of IT Act - Hence, membership right is a right which is owned by the assessee and used for the business purpose - Tribunal was right in holding that depreciation was allowable on the cost of the membership card u/s. 32(1)(ii) of the IT Act - Impugned judgment of HC set aside - Appeals allowed.
