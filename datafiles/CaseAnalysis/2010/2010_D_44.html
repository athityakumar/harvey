Dhan Singh v State of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

22 July 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Swatanter Kumar, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 583; (2010) 12 SCC 277; JT 2010 (8) SC 272; 2011 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1454; 2011 (1) RCR(Criminal) 342; 2010(7) SCALE 420; [2010] 8 S.C.R. 794

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 148, 149, 452, 323, 325, 302 and 304 Part II - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 32(1) - Nine accused were convicted for offences u/ss. 148, 452, 325 and 323 r/w s. 149 IPC and appellant was convicted for offences u/ss. 148, 452, 323 r/w s. 149 IPC and s. 302 of IPC - On appeal, HC upheld the conviction of appellant - Hence, present appeal - (A) Dying declaration - Appellant contended that statement in question cannot be relied upon as dying declaration of deceased and that Head Constable could not have recorded the dying declaration and as per established practice it has to be recorded by a competent Magistrate - Held, provisions of s. 32 of the Act, by themselves, do not mandatorily require that dying declaration has to be recorded by any designated or particular person - In terms of s. 32(1) of the Act, the statement made by the person as to the cause of his death or to the circumstances, are admissible - In the present case, deceased specifically stated the role that was attributable to different accused persons and his statement, in the form of dying declaration, was clear and unambiguous about the role of appellant - Statement was fully corroborated by medical evidence - Further, statement of deceased was recorded only after he was declared fit to make the statement by the concerned doctor - FIR itself was registered on the statement of deceased, which was recorded by the Head Constable, who was competent to do so at the relevant time - (B) It was submitted that conviction ought to be u/s.304 Part II of IPC and not u/s.302 of IPC - Held, from the collective analysis and examination of the evidence on record, it appears that the appellant had no intention of killing the deceased and did not give him a blow with the intention to kill or with the knowledge that it was likely to cause death - Offence of the appellant altered from s. 302 to s. 304 Part II of the IPC - Appeal disposed of.
