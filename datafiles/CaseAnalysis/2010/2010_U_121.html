The Amravati District Central Co-Operative Bank Limited v United India Fire And General Insurance Co.Limited

Supreme Court of India

15 April 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 270; (2010) 5 SCC 294; 2010 (2) ACC 622; 2010(4) ALL MR 461; 2010 (80) ALR 752; 2010 (3) Bom.C.R. 555; JT 2010 (4) SC 1; 2010(3) SCALE 715; [2010] 4 S.C.R. 661; 2010 (2) WLN(SC) 64

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Insurance

Summary: Insurance - Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration Act, 1940, ss. 14, 17 and 30 - Respondent-Insurer issued a Renewal Insurance Policy to the appellant-Bank - Said policy indemnified and insured the Bank against losses caused by acts or omission of the Bank's employees to a limit of Rs.6 lacs (Basic cover) plus Rs. 9 lacs (cash in safe) - 'X' employee of the Bank committed a series of embezzlements - Bank claimed indemnity from the Insurer in terms of the policy in respect of Rs.3,58,000/- embezzled by the said employee - Insurer informed the Bank that its assessors had assessed the reimbursable loss as Rs.29,000/- and offered the said sum in full settlement of the claim - Bank was not agreeable and sought arbitration - Arbitrator held that the insurer could not apply the Excess clause to each and every loss separately; that having regard to the terms of the policy, the amounts embezzled had to be aggregated; and that out of the total loss, the Bank had to bear 25% and the insurer was liable to pay the balance - On appeal, Civil Judge upheld the award and directed that the award be made a rule of the court - HC followed the decision of Single Judge in Central Bank of India vs. New India Assurance Company Limited 1980 INDLAW MUM 3869 and held that arbitrator ought to have considered each item of embezzlement separately and could not aggregate the amounts embezzled by 'X', for the purpose of arriving at the claim and fixing liability of the insurer - Hence, present appeal - Whether HC's order could be upheld? - Held, words "each and every claim" were used in the Schedule with reference to losses under Contingency 4 by describing the Excess as "25% on each and every claim or Rs.11,500/- whichever is higher on D.A.R." - This clearly shows that the stipulated exemption from indemnity is in regard to each and every loss - It is therefore necessary to identify each act of embezzlement by 'X' in regard to each account, as the loss on account of each embezzlement forms a separate claim - Bank has to bear 25% of the amount embezzled (or 11500/- whichever is higher) in regard to each and every embezzlement, and not by aggregation of the embezzlements - Hence, the award of the arbitrator is liable to be set aside as there is a clear error apparent on the face of the award - HC's order upheld - Appeal dismissed.
