Election Commission Of India v Telangana Rastra Samithi And Another

Supreme Court of India

03 December 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 1047; (2011) 1 SCC 370; 2011 (4) AWC 4079; JT 2010 (13) SC 233; 2011 (1) RCR(Civil) 1; 2010(13) SCALE 129

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Election

Keywords: Election Commission, Elections, Any Other Candidate, State Legislative Assemblies, Casual Vacancies

Summary: Election - Constitution - Representation of the People Act, 1951, ss. 84, 98, 101, 150, 151A - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 190(3)(b) - State Legislature Bye-election - Withholding of - 12 members of State Legislature Assembly submitted their resignation pursuant to a claim of separate Statehood - Speaker of the House notified the vacancies in State Gazette - Appellant/Election Commission, subsequently, issued notification for bye-election to 10 vacancies, however, withheld notification for bye-election to the remaining 2 vacancies due to pendency of election petition - Respondent/Committee challenged the decision of appellant/Commission before the HC - HC held that provisions of s. 151A of the 1951 Act could be interpreted so as to render ss. 84, 98 and 101 of the said Act otiose - HC, consequently, directed the appellant/Commission to conduct bye-election to the remaining 2 vacant seats - Hence the present appeal - Appellant/Commission contended that the approach of the HC in the impugned judgment and the final conclusion reached was completely contrary to the views expressed by the SC in D. Sanjeevayya v. Election Tribunal Andhra Pradesh and others, 1967 INDLAW SC 133 - Whether s. 151A of the Act can be interpreted so as to render ss. 84, 98 and 101 of the said Act otiose - Held, in addition to claiming a declaration that the election of all or any of the returned candidates is void, an election petitioner, by virtue of s. 84 of the Act, can claim a further declaration that he himself or any other candidate has been duly elected - SC, in D.Sanjeevayya's case, held that Election Commission was not bound u/s. 150 of the Act to hold a bye- election forthwith on the occurrence of vacancies, but it was entitled to suspend taking action under the said section till the decision in the election petition u/s. 84 was known - HC was clearly wrong in coming to a finding that the non-obstante clause of s. 151A, which was not available, when the decision in D.Sanjeevayya's case was rendered, altered the whole legal scenario and rendered the decision in D.Sanjeevayya's case to be no longer good law - 1951 Act is a complete Code for the conduct of elections by the Election Commission of India appointed u/art. 324 of the Constitution which provides for superintendence, direction, control and conduct of elections to Parliament and to the legislature of every State - Provisions of art. 190(3)(b) of the Constitution have, therefore, to be read along with the provisions of the 1951 Act - S. 84 of the 1951 Act cannot be rendered otiose by holding that all vacancies on account of the art. 190(3) of the Constitution become immediately available for being filled up by way of a bye-election - Introduction of s. 151A in the 1951 Act did not alter the position as far as the provisions of s. 84 and consequently, ss. 98(c) and 101(b) of the 1951 Act are concerned, since although a casual vacancy may have occurred within the meaning of s. 150 of the 1951 Act, those vacancies in which election petitions had been filed and were pending cannot be held to have become available for the purposes of being filled up within the time prescribed u/s. 151A of the 1951 Act - Art. 190(3)(b) of the Constitution merely indicates that if a Member of a House of a Legislature of State resigns his seat by writing to the Speaker and such resignation is accepted, his seat shall become vacant, however, it does not introduce any element of compulsion on the Election Commission to hold a bye-election ignoring the provisions of s. 84 of the Act - In such cases, it cannot be said that such casual vacancies are not available for being filled up and the Commission will have to wait for holding elections in such Constituencies until a decision is rendered in regard to the latter part of s. 84 of the 1951 Act during the life of the House - Impugned order of the HC is set aside - Appeal allowed.
