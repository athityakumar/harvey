Ritesh Tewari and another v State of Uttar Pradesh and others

Supreme Court of India

21 September 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 766; (2010) 10 SCC 677; AIR 2010 SC 3823; 2011 (84) ALR 292; 2011 (2) AWC 1770; JT 2010 (10) SC 1; 2011 (3) MLJ(SC) 491; 2010(10) SCALE 38; [2010] 11 S.C.R. 589

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Administrative - Urban Land (Ceiling and Regulation) Act, 1976, ss. 5(3), 8(4),10(3) - Declared surplus land - Alienation - Legality - Respondent/Authority passed ex-parte assessment orders u/s. 8(4) against the tenure holders holding that disputed land which was in the possession of them was surplus land - Respondent/Authorities published notification u/s. 10(3) which effectuated the deemed vesting of disputed land in the State and directed the tenure holders to hand over the possession of disputed land to Statutory Authority - Tenure holders, however, did not hand over the possession but sold the properties to a third party who in turn sold the same to appellants - Appellants applied for sanction of plan for construction of buildings in the disputed/surplus land - Statutory Authority accorded the sanction - In the meantime, 1976 Act was repealed and appellants apprehended that they could be dispossessed by the authorities in view of certain inter-departmental communications - Appellants challenged the said inter-departmental communications before the HC - HC dismissed appellants' writ petition - Hence the present appeal - Whether appellants have any right or interest over the disputed land - Held, ex-parte assessment orders u/s. 8(4) were not challenged by the original tenure holders and hence the same attained finality - Transfer of such declared surplus land by the tenure holders in favour of anyone was not only prohibited but null and void in view of ss. 5 and 10 of the Act - HC after considering the statutory provisions of ss. 5 and 10 and taking note of the fact that the appellants did not disclose the date of notification u/s. 10(1) of the Act, nor annexed the copy of the same and further presuming that the said notice must have preceded the notice u/s. 10(3) of the Act 1976, reached the conclusion that the transfer which had been effected by the recorded tenure holders in favour of third parties was deemed to be null and void by operation of law u/ss. 5(3) and 10(4) - No cogent reason available to take any contrary view against the findings of HC - Further, sale deed in favour of third parties was not on record and there was nothing to establish whether the sale deed was a genuine, forged or fabricated document - Merely making a statement that it was a registered sale deed and, therefore, it was genuine, cannot be accepted - Therefore, alleged sale deed in favour of third parties was a void transaction and hence all subsequent transactions had merely to be ignored - Appellants do not have any right over the dispute surplus land - Appeal dismissed.
