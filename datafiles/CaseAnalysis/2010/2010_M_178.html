Md. Nooman & Ors. v Md. Jabed Alam & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

22 September 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Aftab Alam, R.M. Lodha

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 774; (2010) 9 SCC 560; 2011 (2) AWC 1604; JT 2010 (10) SC 354; 2011 (1) MLJ(SC) 339; 2010 (4) RCR(Civil) 744; 2010 (2) RCR(Rent) 432; 2010(10) SCALE 63; [2010] 11 S.C.R. 729

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Keywords: U.P. Urban Buildings (Regulation of Letting, Rent and Eviction) Act, 1972

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Title over the suit property - Plaintiff instituted a suit for eviction against defendant submitting that impugned property originally belonged to her mother-in-law 'Y' who sold the suit property to her through a registered sale deed - Plaintiff further submitted that she let out the suit property to defendant but subsequently asked him to vacate the suit property on the ground that defendant did not pay the rent - Defendant denied that 'Y' executed any sale deed in favour of the plaintiff and that the sale deed relied upon by the plaintiff was a forged and fabricated document - Defendant further submitted that 'Y' had transferred the suit property in his favour in 1950, by an oral gift - Defendant also denied any relationship of landlord and tenant with the plaintiff - Trial court upheld the plaintiff's claim of title to the property but denied any relationship of landlord and tenant between plaintiff and defendant - Additional District Judge dismissed appeal filed against said order - Appellate Court also upheld the order of the trial court - Plaintiff did not take the matter any further but filed another suit seeking declaration of title over the property and recovery of its possession from the defendant - In another suit, trial court held that the plaintiff had succeeded in proving her title whereas the defendant had failed to prove his adverse possession - On appeal, ADJ held that the plaintiff had failed to prove her title to the suit premises on the basis of the sale deed - On appeal, HC held that the earlier suit (for eviction) and the later suit for declaration of title and recovery of possession were between the same parties and were contested on exactly the same claims raised by the two sides, hence, principle of res judicata to be applied - Hence, present appeal - Whether the judgment and decree regarding title passed in the eviction suit shall operate as res judicata between the parties on the question of title? - Held, Patna HC in Pardip Singh vs. Ram Sundar Singh 1949 INDLAW PAT 672, held that decision in a rent suit is not res judicata on the question of title unless the question of title had to be decided, was expressly raised, and was expressly decided between the parties and in each case it is necessary to examine carefully the decision in the rent suit before any opinion can be formed as to whether it operates as res judicata on the question of title or not - In present case, issue of title was expressly raised by the parties in the earlier eviction suit and it was expressly decided by the eviction court - Parties filed another suit on the same issue - Hence, HC was right in holding that the finding recorded in favour of the plaintiff in the earlier suit for eviction would operate as res judicata in the subsequent suit for declaration of title and recovery of possession between the parties - Appeal dismissed.
