Hari Ram and Another v State of Haryana and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 February 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 115; (2010) 3 SCC 621; AIR 2010 SC (Supp) 709; 2010(2) ALL MR 1012; 2010 (2) AWC 1921; 2010 (1) CLT(SC) 353; 2010 (2) CTC 336; JT 2010 (2) SC 235; 2010 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1041; 2010 (3) MLJ(SC) 605; 2010 (3) MPLJ 273; 2010 (2) RCR(Civil) 181; 2010(2) SCALE 339; [2010] 2 S.C.R. 756; 2010 (1) UPLBEC 674

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property

Keywords: Withdrawal From Acquisition

Summary: Land & Property - Constitution - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4, 6, 48 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Release from acquisition - Discrimination - Violation of art. 14 - Respondent issued notification u/s. 4 of the Act proposing to acquire a large tract of land - Land owners filed objections u/s. 5-A before the Land Acquisition Officer (LAO) - LAO released lands of some landowners and issued declaration u/s. 6 of the Act with regard to the remaining lands - Respondents released the lands of various landowners on the basis of their representations, therefore, appellant/landowners also filed representations - Respondents rejected appellants representation - Whether the action of the State Government/respondent in rejecting the appellants' representations for withdrawal from acquisition of their land is an ultra vires act and discriminatory - Held, respondents rejected the representation of appellants by relying upon the Land Acquisition Policy, 2007, however, respondent released the land of several other landowners without applying the same standards - Further, lands of more than 40 landowners out of the same acquisition proceedings have been released by the respondent u/s. 48 of the Act - It is not the case of the respondents that withdrawal from acquisition in favour of such landowners has been in violation of any statutory provision or contrary to law nor that the release of land from acquisition in favour of such landowners was wrong action on their part or it was done due to some mistake or a result of fraud or corrupt motive - State Government exercises its power u/s. 48 of the Act for withdrawal from acquisition in respect of a particular land, the landowners who are similarly situated have right of similar treatment by the State Government - Government has obligation of acting with substantial fairness and consistency in considering the representations of the landowners for withdrawal from acquisition whose lands have been acquired under the same acquisition proceedings - Passing different orders in exercise of its power u/s. 48 of the Act in respect of persons similarly situated relating to same acquisition proceedings and for same public purpose is violation of art. 14 of the Constitution and discriminatory - Therefore, action of respondents in treating the present appellants differently although they are situated similar to the landowners whose lands have been released cannot be allowed and is bad in law, hence, direction issued to the respondent to issue appropriate order/s concerning the appellants' lands on the same terms and in the same manner as has been done in the matters of other landowners - Appeals allowed.
