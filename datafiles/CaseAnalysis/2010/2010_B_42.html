Bengai Mandal @ Begai Mandal v State Of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

11 January 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Mukundakam Sharma, V.S. Sirpurkar

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 5; (2010) 2 SCC 91; AIR 2010 SC 686; 2010 ALL MR (Cri) 637; 2010 CRLJ 1420; 2010 (1) JCC 637; JT 2010 (1) SC 49; 2010 (1) RCR(Criminal) 733; 2010(1) SCALE 182; [2010] 1 S.C.R. 439; 2010 (1) UC 355

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302 r/w. 34, 324, 326 and 452 - Death due to acid attack - Conviction - Trial Court convicted appellant and accused no. 1 u/s. 302/ 34, 326/34, 452 and 324 of IPC - On appeal, HC upheld decision of trial court - Appellant contended that HC as well as trial court had erred in convicting appellant u/s. 302 IPC and if at all a case existed against appellant, it was u/s. 304 part II IPC, for it was accused no. 1 who had carried the container of acid and actually poured acid on deceased causing her death - (A) Whether appellant shared a common intention with accused no.1 so that he may be convicted u/s. 302 IPC by invoking aid of s. 34 of IPC - Held, in absence of any active role played by appellant or overt act being done by appellant, it cannot be said with certainty that appellant had accompanied accused no.1 to house of deceased with a common intention to murder deceased - Hence, conviction of appellant u/s. 302 r/w. s. 34 of IPC cannot be sustained - (B) Whether appellant rightly convicted u/s. 304 Part II and u/s. 326 of IPC by HC? - Held, appellant had accompanied accused no.1 who was carrying the acid in his hand at night and it can be said with certainty that appellant had done so with the intention to inflicting bodily harm on deceased - Since appellant was present at scene of occurrence and simply watched accused no.1 throwing acid on deceased without preventing accused no.1 from doing so clearly establishes that appellant had intended to cause injury to and also disfigure deceased and as such is liable to be punished u/s. 326 of IPC - Also since appellant could be said to be possessing knowledge that throwing of acid is likely to cause death of deceased, a case u/s. 304 part II of IPC is also made out - Considering facts that death ensued after twenty six days of incident as a result of septicemia and not as a consequence of burn injuries, period already undergone by appellant would be sufficient to meet ends of justice - Appeal partly allowed.
