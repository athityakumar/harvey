State Of Haryana v M/S Anil Pesticides Ltd. & Anr

Supreme Court of India

06 May 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

D.K. Jain, P. Sathasivam, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 511; (2010) 12 SCC 606; 2011 (3) RCR(Civil) 875; 2010(7) SCALE 452

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Keywords: Haryana General Sales Tax Rules, 1975, Haryana General Sales Tax (Fifth Amendment) Rules, 1996

Summary: Sales Tax - Haryana General Sales Tax Rules, 1975, r. 28A - Haryana General Sales Tax Act, 1973 - Exemption - Respondent-manufacturer of 'Monocrotophos (Technical)' and 'Dichlorvos (Technical)' claimed sales tax exemption in terms of the industrial policy announced by State as per r. 28 of Rules - Subsequently, in 1996 amendments made in the Rules included more items in negative list of Schedule III - One of the items so included in the list was Item No.43 'Pesticides manufacturing and Formulations' - Thereafter, exemption claimed by the respondent was denied to him on the ground that impugned items were covered under the category of 'pesticides' falling in Entry 43 of the negative list - Commissioner allowed appeal filed against said order and held that the items manufactured by the Dealer did not fall in the category of 'Pesticides and formulations' as referred to in Entry 43 of the negative list - HC dismissed writ petition filed against said order - Hence, present appeal - Whether respondent is entitled for exemption claimed under industrial policy? - Held, SC in Mahabir Vegetable Oils (P) Ltd. & Anr. vs. State of Haryana & Ors. 2006 INDLAW SC 273, held that amendments carried out in 1996 as also the subsequent amendments made prior to 2001, could not take away the rights of the appellant with retrospective effect - Hence, case of the respondent shall be considered afresh by the authorities concerned in terms of the said decision of this Court - Appeal partly allowed.
