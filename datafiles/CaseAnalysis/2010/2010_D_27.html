Neelam Kumar v Dayarani

Supreme Court of India

06 July 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Aftab Alam, B. SUDARSHAN REDDY

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 461; (2010) 13 SCC 298; AIR 2011 SC 193; 2011 (3) ALD(SC) 64; 2010 (81) ALR 771; 2011 (1) AWC 90; 2010 (6) CTC 108; JT 2010 (6) SC 441; 2010 (7) MLJ(SC) 111; 2010 (3) RCR(Civil) 577; 2010(6) SCALE 335; 2010 (3) UC 1603

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Summary: Family & Personal - Hindu Marriage Act, 1955, ss. 13(1)(ia) and 28 - Dissolution of marriage - Cruelty and irretrievable breakdown - Appellant filed the petition for dissolution of marriage u/s. 13(1)(ia) of the Act, 1956 on grounds of cruelty - Respondent denied all the allegations made against her by the appellant but did not get herself examined nor did she produce any witness - On the basis of the ex parte evidence adduced before it, the trial court allowed the appellant's application and granted him the decree of divorce u/s. 13(1)(ia) of the Act, 1956 - Having dealt with all the allegations made by the appellant and having considered the materials on record in some detail, HC found that the appellant had not been able to bring his case u/s. 13(1)(ia) of Act, 1956 and set aside the judgment and decree passed by the trial court - Appellant filed appeal against said order and made a plea for dissolution of marriage on the ground of its irretrievable breakdown - Whether decree of dissolution of marriage could be granted to the appellant? - Held, no - There is nothing to indicate that the respondent has contributed in any way to the alleged breakdown of the marriage - If a party to a marriage, by his own conduct brings the relationship to a point of irretrievable breakdown, he/she cannot be allowed to seek divorce on the ground of breakdown of the marriage - That would simply mean giving someone the benefits of his/her own misdeeds - Moreover, SC in Vishnu Dutt Sharma vs. Manju Sharma 2009 INDLAW SC 330 held that irretrievable breakdown of marriage is not a ground for divorce as it is not contemplated u/s. 13 and granting divorce on this ground alone would amount to adding a clause therein by a judicial verdict which would amount to legislation by Court - Agreed with the view taken by the HC - No merit in appeal - Appeal dismissed.
