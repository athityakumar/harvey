Sau. Laxmi Verma v State Of Maharashtra And Others

Supreme Court of India

19 April 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Deepak Verma, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 290; (2010) 5 SCC 329; AIR 2010 SC 2653; 2010 (4) Bom.C.R. 732; JT 2010 (4) SC 218; 2010 (5) MLJ(SC) 307; 2010(4) SCALE 147; [2010] 4 S.C.R. 782

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Municipalities & Local Governments - Maharashtra Municipal Councils, Nagar Panchayats and Industrial Townships Act, 1965, s. 41(2) - Resignation of Councillor - Non-compliance of mandatory requirements - Respondent No. 6, an elected Councillor of Municipal Council, submitted his resignation to Collector, who held that the resignation was valid and accepted it - On his resignation and acceptance thereof, since the seat fell vacant, Collector proceeded to arrange for elections - Meantime, respondent No. 6 filed a revision application before Additional Commissioner stating therein that he had in fact not resigned in accordance with law and there was complete violation of s. 41(2) of the Act - Therefore, election programme already announced were made subject to the decision of pending proceedings - In the fresh elections, appellant was elected as Councillor - Additional Commissioner held that Collector rightly accepted the resignation of respondent no. 6 - Writ petition was filed wherein HC remanded the matter back to Additional Commissioner - Pursuant thereto, Additional Commissioner set aside the order of Collector, thereby election of appellant, which was subject to the final result of the lis pending before Additional Commissioner stood set aside - Writ petition filed thereagainst was dismissed - Hence, present appeal - Whether resignation tendered by respondent no. 6 was valid? - Held, as per s. 41(2) of the Act a Councillor may resign his office unconditionally at any time by notice in writing in his hand, to be addressed to the Collector - It further requires that such resignation has to be delivered in person and signed before the Collector, then only such resignation shall be effective - Thus, mere putting initials at certain places scored out before the Collector, would not amount to putting the signatures in the resignation letter before the Collector himself - In the present case, letter of resignation was already typed, on which the signature of respondent no. 6 was already appearing - There was no valid, proper and legal resignation tendered by respondent no. 6 in as much as admittedly, the said resignation was not signed by respondent no. 6 in presence of the Collector which was mandatorily required to be done - Appeals dismissed.
