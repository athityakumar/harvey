Northern Mineral Limited v Union of India and another

Supreme Court of India

07 July 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Chandramauli Kumar Prasad, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 479; (2010) 7 SCC 726; AIR 2010 SC 2829; 2010 ALL MR (Cri) 2593; 2010 CRLJ 4414; JT 2010 (7) SC 81; 2010 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 2472; 2011 (2) MLJ(Crl) 532; 2010 (3) RCR(Criminal) 763; 2010(6) SCALE 416; [2010] 8 S.C.R. 1; 2010 (2) UC 1301

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Insecticide Analyst

Summary: Criminal - Insecticides Act, 1968, s. 29 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 245 - Appellant company engaged in the manufacturing of insecticides including Monocrotophos 36 SL - On 10-09-1993, Insecticide Inspector had collected the sample of Monocrotophos 36 SL from the shop of the appellant's dealer (respondent no. 2) and sent it to the Insecticide Analyst for analysis and report who submitted its report on 13-10-1993 that impugned sample was misbranded as it did not conform to the relevant ISI specifications - Show cause notice issued to appellant - Notice of the report was sent to the appellant on 1-11-1993 in reply whereof by letter dt. 17-11-1993 it intimated its intention to adduce evidence in controversion of the report - After the Joint Director gave its consent for prosecution of the appellant company and respondent No. 2 on 23-02-1994 the Insecticide Inspector filed the complaint on 16-03-1994 alleging commission of offence u/s. 29 of the Insecticides Act - Appellant filed application for discharge u/s. 245 of the CrPC on the ground that shelf life of the insecticides had expired even prior to the filing of the complaint - Said application was dismissed by trial court - HC also dismissed appeal filed against said order - Hence, present appeal - Held, every insecticide has its shelf life and with passage of time, it loses its efficacy and therefore after the expiry of the shelf life of the insecticide, it may not conform to the standard specifications - In the present case, shelf life of the insecticides had expired even prior to the filing of the complaint - Had the authority competent to grant consent, given consent and complaint lodged immediately after the receipt of intimation of the accused, sample could have very well been sent for analysis and report, before the expiry of shelf life - Section 24(3) and (4) of the Act obliges the Insecticide Analyst and Central Insecticides Laboratory to make the test and analysis and report within 30 days - When 30 days is good enough for report, there does not seem any justification not to lodge complaint within 30 days from the receipt of the intimation from the accused and getting order for sending the sample for test and analysis to the Central Insecticides Laboratory - All who are entrusted with the implementation of the provisions of the Act, would be well advised to act with promptitude and adhere to the time-schedule, so that innocent persons are not prosecuted and real culprits not left out - Appeal allowed.
