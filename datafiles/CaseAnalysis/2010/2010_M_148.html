Abdul Razak (D) Through L.Rs. And Others v Mangesh Rajaram Wagle And Others

Supreme Court of India

07 January 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 19; (2010) 2 SCC 432; AIR 2010 SC (Supp) 661; 2010 (2) ALD(SC) 136; 2010 (2) AWC 1135; 2010 (2) Bom.C.R. 286; 2010 (1) CLT(SC) 185; 2010 (1) CTC 466; JT 2010 (1) SC 508; 2010 (2) MPLJ 272; 2010 (1) RCR(Civil) 717; 2010 (110) RD 239; 2010 (1) RLW 954; 2010(1) SCALE 267

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: (A) Practice & Procedure - Application for striking off additional written statement - Delay thereof - Respondents/plaintiffs filed a suit against defendants for a declaration that they were lawful tenants - However, 2nd defendant expired during the pendency of the suit and their Legal Representatives/appellants were brought on record - Appellants filed additional written statement - Respondents filed an application for striking off the additional written statement after the completion of their evidence - Trial Court dismissed the application on account of delay - Respondents challenged the Trial Court order before HC - HC set aside the Trial Court order - What is the effect of delay in filing the application by respondents for striking off the additional written statement - Held, respondents filed application for striking out the additional written statement after a long time gap of three years and six months without explaining as to why they did not object to the taking on record of the additional written statement and framing of additional issues - Therefore, appellants cannot be blamed for the delay since the respondents have delayed the proceedings for over two years by filing frivolous application for striking off the additional written statement - Appeal allowed.

(B) Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 6 r. 16 - Striking off additional written statement - Jurisdiction of HC - Whether the HC could pass an order for striking off the additional written statement despite the fact that respondents failed to make out a case for exercise of power by the court u/O. 6 r. 16 CPC - Held, HC did not have the jurisdiction to direct striking off the additional written statement without being satisfied that respondents were able to make out a case for exercise of power by the court under either of three clauses of O. 6 r. 16 CPC - Appeal allowed.

(C) Practice & Procedure - Civil Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 227 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 6 r. 16 - Error of jurisdiction or error of law - Whether the HC was justified in setting aside the order of the Trial Court without being satisfied that the same was vitiated by an error of jurisdiction or an error of law apparent on the face of the record and that such error resulted in substantial failure of justice - Held, if respondents had invoked the HC's jurisdiction u/art. 226, then the HC ought to have considered whether the Trial Court committed a jurisdictional error by refusing to strike off the additional written statement filed by the appellants or it was a case of failure on the part of the Trial Court to exercise the power vested in it u/O. 6 r. 16 CPC or the order under challenge was vitiated by an error of law apparent on the face of the record or there was violation of the rules of natural justice - In either case, the HC was also required to consider whether there has been substantial failure of justice or manifest injustice has been caused to respondents on account of the Trial Court's refusal to strike off the additional written statement, however, HC, in the instant case, decided the same as if it was exercising appellate jurisdiction - Therefore, impugned HC order is set aside - Appeal allowed.
