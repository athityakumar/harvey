Pallawi Resources Ltd. v Protos Engineering Company Pvt. Ltd.

Supreme Court of India

26 March 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Mukundakam Sharma, V.S. Sirpurkar

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 222; (2010) 5 SCC 196; AIR 2010 SC 1969; 2010 (80) ALR 700; JT 2010 (3) SC 482; 2010 (1) RCR(Rent) 318; 2010(3) SCALE 356

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Rent Control

Keywords: West Bengal Premises Tenancy (Amendment) Act, 2002, West Bengal Premises Tenancy Rules, 1999

Summary: Rent Control - Constitution - West Bengal Premises Tenancy Act, 1997, s. 20 r/w. s. 17(4A) - West Bengal Premises Tenancy (Amendment) Act, 2002 - Lease deed was executed between appellant and respondent for grant of lease for office purposes for a period of 20 years and rent mutually settled and agreed upon by the parties was Rs. 2,250/- per month as the basic component of the rent - Upon expiry of the term of 20 years, appellant issued notice u/s. 20 of 1997 Act to respondent intending to increase the rent of the said premises to Rs. 13,500/- per month and recoverable from the month of May, 2007 - Subsequently, appellant filed suit terminating the tenancy and calling upon the respondent to hand over vacant, peaceful and khas possession of the said premises submitting that u/s. 17(4A) of 1997 Act there is a mandate for increase of rent which automatically comes in operation upon a notice in that regard being issued u/s. 20 without the landlord requiring to perfect the demand before any other authority - Respondent contended that s. 17(4A) has to be read in conjunction with the other sub-sections of that section and that application of s. 17(1) which requires the Rent Controller to fix the fair rent cannot be dispensed with - HC rejected appellant's submissions - Hence, present appeal - Whether the fair rent in respect of a tenancy which subsists for 20 years or more in respect of the premises constructed in or before the year 1984 and used for commercial purpose is required to be determined by the Rent Controller or whether the same would stand automatically determined u/s. 17(4A) r/w. s. 20 of the West Bengal Premises Tenancy Act, 1997? - Held, s. 17 lays down different types of causes of action as to when such an increase could be sought for - Sub-section (1) of s. 17 makes it crystal clear that on the happening and fulfilment of the criteria laid down in each of the cause of action, an application would be required to be filed before the Rent Controller who would then determine as to what would be the fair rent - Although, it could only be a case of mathematical calculation yet an order in that regard is to be passed by the Rent Controller on the basis of an application filed before it by determining the quantum of such fair rent - In case there is a case of deemed increase of fair rent or an automatic increase, still somebody would have to determine that it has so increased and that authority is definitely the Rent Controller who could exercise the jurisdiction only when he receives an application - Hence, it cannot be said that sub-section 4A of s. 17 was sought to be brought in by way of an exception to the general rule of s. 17 of 1997 Act - Appeal dismissed.
