Chimanlal Kuberdas Modi (D) By Lrs v Gujarat Industrial Development Corp. & Ors

Supreme Court of India

22 October 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Mukundakam Sharma, A.R. Dave

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 886; (2010) 10 SCC 635; 2011 (1) AWC 764; 2011 (2) Bom.C.R. 639; JT 2010 (11) SC 317; 2011 (4) MLJ(SC) 327; 2011 (112) RD 682; 2010(11) SCALE 210

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4, 6, 18, 23(1A), 23(2) and 34 - Land acquisition proceedings - Payment of interest on solatium - Land belonging to the appellant was acquired - Award was passed - Being aggrieved by the award, appellant filed references u/s. 18 of the Act - Reference Court allowed the prayer for compensation towards the market value of the land as also solatium at the rate of 30% and also granted increase of 12% from the date of publication, as also an increase in compensation in terms of s. 23(1A) of the Act - Consequent thereto, the appellant filed an execution application for recovery of balance amount as awarded by the Reference Court - During pendency of the aforesaid execution application, the respondent paid an amount as part payment of the dues payable by the respondent to the appellant - While the said execution case was pending, the appellant also filed an application for payment of the amount of interest payable u/s. 34 of the Act, at the rate of 9% for the first year and 15% thereafter on the amount awarded by the Reference Court u/s. 23(1A) and 23(2) of the Act - As against the said prayer of the appellant, the respondents filed objection - Executing court rejected the objection filed by the respondent No. 1 and issued a warrant for recovery of balance amount and further interest relying on the ratio of the judgment in Sunder v. Union of India 2001 INDLAW SC 67 - Revision petition was filed thereagainst contending inter alia that the Reference Court did not award interest on the aggregate amount and therefore, the Executing Court was not justified in awarding an amount u/s. 34 of the Act - HC allowed the appeal filed by the respondent and set aside the order passed by Executing Court - Hence, present appeal - Whether appellant would be entitled to payment of interest u/s. 34 of the Act on the solatium on the compensation paid, in terms of the decision of the case in Sunder and further explained in the case of Gurpreet Singh v. Union of India 2006 INDLAW SC 721? - Held, if in the Award passed, the Reference Court makes a specific reference to payment of interest but without any such reference to the payment of interest on solatium and merely payment of interest on compensation is granted, then it would be open to the Executing Court to apply the ratio of Sunder case and declare that the compensation awarded includes solatium, and consequently, interest on the amount could be directed to be deposited in execution - That being the legal position as prevailing, compensation awarded includes solatium and therefore interest on the said amount shall be paid by the respondent in the pending execution - In the present case, since a direction was made for payment of interest at the rate of 9% per annum and thereafter at 15% per annum in the decree, the appellant cannot be denied the benefit of the interest on market value, which also includes solatium - Further, ratio of the aforesaid decision also applicable in view of the fact that such interest on solatium was claimed by the appellant in the pending adjudication and therefore, executing court was justified to permit recovery of interest on solatium from the date of judgment in Sunder and not for any prior period - Appeal allowed.
