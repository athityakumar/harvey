The Commissioner Of Central Excise, Aurangabad v M/S Bajaj Auto Ltd., Waluj, Aurangabad, Through Its Vice President (Materials) And Ors

Supreme Court of India

12 November 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Dattu, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 959; (2010) 13 SCC 117; 2010 (6) Bom.C.R. 803; 2010 (260) E.L.T. 17; JT 2010 (12) SC 218; 2011 (5) RCR(Civil) 115; 2010(12) SCALE 56

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Central Excise (Valuation) Rules, 1975, Finance Act, 2000, Central Excise Rules, 1994

Summary: Excise - Indirect Tax - Central Excise Act, 1944, s. 11A - Central Excise Rules, 1994, rr. 57F(2) and 57(3) - Limitation - Undervaluation - Respondent no. 4 engaged in the manufacture of aluminium castings, inputs of which i.e. aluminium ingots was supplied by respondent no. 1 under the cover of invoices issued u/rr. 57F(2) and 57(3) of the 1994 Rules after reversing the MODVAT credit availed on the said input - Show cause notice issued to the respondent no. 4 alleging that it was receiving inputs from respondent no. 1 at an under-valued landed cost by not including expenses on account of sales tax, octroi, freight, insurance, loading- unloading charges and handling charges, and that respondent no. 1 was charging only the basic price of such inputs equal to the basic price charged by the original manufacturers of the said - Respondents contended that that there was no provision in Central Excise Laws which imposed an obligation on a person to sell his goods at a particular price in such transactions - Respondents also contended that the notice and the demand therein was time-barred - Adjudicating Authority held that the prices charged by respondent no. 4 were depressed prices coloured as negotiated prices and levied duty of Rs. 27,71,594/- penalty of Rs. 27,71,594/- and interest against the respondent no. 4 - Adjudicating authority also imposed penalty on respondent no. 1 - Tribunal allowed the appeal filed against said order and has set aside the order passed by the adjudicating authority - Hence, present appeal - Whether order of tribunal could be upheld? - Held, Tribunal while considering the issue has not stated whether or not there were any such circumstances which would not allow the revenue to invoke extended period of limitation - It only observes in its order since both the respondents are situated under the jurisdiction of the same division and as such it cannot be reasonable to conclude that the revenue was not aware of the transactions - Since this is not what is envisaged under the proviso to s. 11A(1) of 1944 Act, the reasoning and the conclusion reached by the Tribunal cannot be upheld - Impugned order set aside - Matter remanded to the tribunal for fresh consideration of all the issues raised by both the parties - Appeal allowed.
