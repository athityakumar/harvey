C.I.T., Mumbai v M/S. Emptee Poly-Yarn Private. Limited.

Supreme Court of India

20 January 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, H.L. Dattu

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 50; (2010) 2 SCC 720; AIR 2010 SC (Supp) 474; 2010 (250) E.L.T. 321; JT 2010 (1) SC 456; 2010(1) SCALE 571; [2010] 1 S.C.R. 801; [2010] 188 TAXMAN 188

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Finance Act, 2009

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 80IA - A/y 1996-97 - Manufacture - Whether twisting and texturising of partially oriented yarn (POY) amounts to 'manufacture' in terms of s. 80IA of IT Act, 1961? - Held, POY simplicitor is not fit for being used in the manufacture of a fabric - It becomes usable only after it undergoes the operation/process which is called as thermo mechanical process which converts POY into texturised yarn, which, in turn, is used for the manufacture of fabric - Hence, twisting and texturising of partially oriented yarn (POY) amounts to 'manufacture' in terms of s. 80IA of IT Act, 1961 - This judgment is to be confined to the facts of the present case - Texturising or twisting per se in every matter does not amount to manufacture, it is the thermo mechanical process embedded in twisting and texturising when applied to a partially oriented yarn which makes the process a manufacture - Revenue's appeals dismissed.
