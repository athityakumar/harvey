New India Assurance Company Limited v Raghuvir Singh Narang And Another

Supreme Court of India

25 February 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 435; (2010) 5 SCC 335; 2010 (3) AWC 2865; 2010 (125) FLR 672; JT 2010 (3) SC 645; 2010 (2) LLJ 853; 2010 (4) MPLJ 1; 2010(3) SCALE 586; [2010] 4 S.C.R. 299; 2010 (7) SLR 1

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: General Insurance (Rationalization of Pay Scales and Other Conditions of Service of Development Staff) Amendment Scheme, 2003, General Insurance (Rationalization of Pay Scales and Other Conditions of Service of Development Staff) Scheme, 1976

Summary: (A) Service - Voluntary retirement scheme - Principles of Contract Law - Applicability of - Whether the general principles of contract would apply to voluntary retirement scheme? - Held, where the voluntary retirement is governed by a contractual scheme, as contrasted from a statutory scheme, the said principle of Contract will apply and consequently the letter of voluntary retirement will be considered as an offer by the employee and therefore any time before its acceptance, the employee could withdraw the offer - But the said general principle of Contract will be inapplicable where the voluntary retirement is under a statutory scheme which categorically bars the employee, from withdrawing the option once exercised - Terms of the statutory scheme will prevail over the general principles of contract - In present case, the statutory scheme contains a specific provision that a Development Officer shall not be eligible to withdraw the option once made for Special Voluntary Retirement Package - In view of the said statutory provision, the general principle of contract that an offer could be withdrawn any time before its acceptance stands excluded - Appeal allowed.

(B) Service - General Insurance Business (Nationalisation) Act, 1972 - General Insurance (Rationalization of Pay Scales and Other Conditions of Service of Development Staff) Scheme, 1976 - General Insurance (Rationalization of Pay Scales and Other Conditions of Service of Development Staff) Amendment Scheme, 2003 - Special Voluntary Retirement Package - Respondents were working as Development Officers under the appellant-New India Assurance Co. Ltd. - In exercise of the powers u/s. 17A of the Act, the Central Government framed a Scheme to amend the Scheme, 1976 - Respondent nos. 1 and 2 opted for the Special Voluntary Retirement Package - Thereafter, respondents requested the appellant to extend the scheme and give more time for exercising the option under the Scheme, and if that was not possible, then treat the option earlier exercised by them as withdrawn as till that day there was no communication from the appellant regarding acceptance of the voluntary retirement - Appellant relieved the respondents from the services stating that the competent authority has accepted the voluntary retirement of the respondents - Appellant informed the respondents that they cannot withdraw from the option already given - Feeling aggrieved, the respondents filed a writ petition seeking a direction to the appellant to reinstate them in the post of Development Officer - Writ petition was allowed relying on decision of SC in Bank of India vs. Swaranakar & Ors., 2002 INDLAW SC 1562 - It held that the SVRP contained in the Amendment Scheme of 2003 was not a statutory scheme, but was contractual in nature; that the option exercised by the respondents to retire voluntarily in terms of SVRP was merely an offer by the respondents, and that before the acceptance of the request of respondents by the appellant, the respondents could withdraw their offer - Whether reliance placed by the HC upon the decision of SC in Swarnakar case, to assume that every scheme for voluntary retirement is always contractual and not statutory, correct? - Held, no - In said decision it was held that if a contractual scheme provides that the voluntary retirement by exercise of option by the employee, will come into effect only on its acceptance by the employer, it will not create any enforceable right in the employee to claim SV retirement - Any term in such a scheme that the employee shall not withdraw from the option once exercised, will be an agreement without consideration and therefore, invalid - It was further held that where the scheme is statutory in character, its terms will prevail over the general principles of contracts and the provision of the Contract Act - In the present case, special voluntary retirement package is a part of the Scheme, 2003, made by the Central Government in exercise of the power u/s. 17A of the Act - Paragraph 5(4) of the Special Voluntary Retirement Package categorically states that a Development Officer shall not be eligible to withdraw the option once made for the Special Voluntary Retirement Package Scheme as it is statutory in character - Consequently, the provisions of the Scheme will prevail over the provisions of Contract Act or any other law or any principle of contract, and having regard to the binding nature of the scheme, the employee upon exercising the option, cannot withdraw from the same - Judgment of the HC set aside - Appeal allowed.
