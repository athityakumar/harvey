Anjani Molu Dessai v State Of Goa And Another

Supreme Court of India

07 December 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 1102; (2010) 13 SCC 710; 2011 (85) ALR 211; 2011 (3) AWC 2650; 2012 (2) CLT(SC) 18; JT 2010 (13) SC 705; 2011 (3) RCR(Civil) 696; 2011 (112) RD 586; 2010(13) SCALE 524

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Orchard

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - Land acquisition - Compensation - Enhancement of - Entitlement - Appellant's land was acquired for laying railway line - Land Acquisition Collector (LAC) passed an award - Appellant sought enhancement of compensation, however, Reference Court and HC affirmed the valuation done by the LAC - Hence the present appeal for enhancement of compensation - Whether compensation awarded should have been at a higher rate - Held, acquired lands were not undeveloped rural land but could be urbanisable land situated near a developed semi-urban village with access to all infrastructure facilities - Appellant produced three sale deeds of adjacent lands, however, Reference Court and HC concurrently rejected such sale deeds - LAC, on the other hand, relied upon two other sale instances which related to a comparable land and furnished a reasonable basis for assessing the market value of the acquired land, by providing appropriate increase to cover the appreciation in prices during the gap of about two years between the date of sale and the date of publication of the preliminary notification - LAC, however, committed a serious error in deducting 45% from the sale price disclosed by the sale deeds towards the cost of development - Where the lands of relying sale instances and the acquired lands are of similar nature the question of making any deduction towards development cost to arrive at the cost of 'undeveloped land' would not arise - Therefore, LAC was not justified in making 45% deduction from the price disclosed by the sale deed - Further, once it is found that the first sale deed was in regard to a comparable land and the second sale deed was not in regard to a comparable land, the second sale deed ought to have been excluded from consideration -Therefore, LAC was not justified in averaging the sale prices of the two sale deeds - Hence appellant is entitled to an enhanced compensation, however, not entitled to any separate compensation for standing trees in the property - Appeal allowed.
