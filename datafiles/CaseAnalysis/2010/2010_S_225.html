H.S. Vankani And Ors. v State Of Gujarat And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

16 March 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 229; (2010) 4 SCC 301; AIR 2010 SC 1714; 2010 (2) ESC 269; 2010 (126) FLR 558; JT 2010 (3) SC 109; 2010(3) SCALE 136; [2010] 3 S.C.R. 485; 2010 (7) SLR 28; 2010 (2) UPLBEC 937

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Rangers (Subordinate Forest Service Recruitment Examination (Amendment) Rules, 1979, Ranger (Subordinate Forest Service Recruitment Examination) Rules, 1974, Rangers (Subordinate Forest Service) Examination Rules, 1983, Rangers (Subordinate Forest Service) Recruitment Rules, 1969

Summary: Service - Rangers (Subordinate Forest Service) Recruitment Rules, 1969 - Ranger (Subordinate Forest Service Recruitment Examination) Rules, 1974 - Rangers (Subordinate Forest Service Recruitment Examination (Amendment Rules) 1979 - Rangers (Subordinate Forest Service) Examination Rules, 1983 - Inter se seniority - Direct recruits - Dispute regarding inter-se seniority between two batches of direct recruits Range Forest Officers viz., 1979-81 batch (non-graduates)(appellants) and 1980-81 batch (graduates)(respondents) of the Subordinate Forest Services of the State of Gujarat and their further promotion to the post of Assistant Conservator of Forests - Graduates (1980-81 batch) successfully completed the training course earlier, and the non-graduates (1979-81 batch) completed the course later for the post of Forest Rangers - Three gradation lists were published, recognizing the seniority of the respondents over the appellants - Neither the Government order nor the Gradation lists were challenged before any forum which had attained finality - However, an order was issued on 29.09.1993 stating the candidates of 1979-81 batch (appellants) should be placed above the candidates of 1980-81 batch (respondents) - Noticing that the abovementioned order would unsettle the settled seniority the respondents preferred a representation - Respondents approached HC - Single judge dismissed the writ petition - On appeal, DB of HC held that the respondents are entitled to seniority from the date of their appointment after completing the Rangers Course with higher standard certificate and that their inter-se seniority would be governed by R. 22 of the 1974 Rules - Impugned order dated 29.9.1993 issued by the Government was also quashed - Hence, present appeal - (A) Inter se seniority - Held, seniority is a civil right which has an important and vital role to play in one's service career - R. 14 of the Rules determines the inter se seniority of the candidates of a particular batch and does not determine the inter-se seniority between two batches, whose educational qualification, years of training and the date of joining, etc. differ - R. 14 of 1969 Rules and R. 22 of 1974 Rules also further re-emphasis that fact - Order dated 29.09.1993 is, therefore, contrary to R. 14 of 1969 Rules and R. 22 of the 1974 Rules - Government committed a grave error in unsettling the settled seniority vide its proceedings dated 29.9.1993 - (B) Enforcing R. 10 of 1969 Rules and R. 18 of 1974 Rules - Held, courts have to avoid a construction of an enactment that leads to an unworkable, inconsistent or impracticable results, since such a situation is unlikely to have been envisaged by the Rule making authority - Legislature expects the court to observe the maxim ut res magis valeat quam pereat (it is better for a thing to have effect than to be made void) - Rule making authority also expects Rule framed by it to be made workable and never visualises absurd results - Strict interpretation of R. 10 of 1969 Rules and R. 18 of 1974 Rules was unworkable and literal interpretation would have resulted in absurd results - Decision taken by the government in deputing the non-graduates (1979-81 batch) to a two year training course and graduates (1980-81 batch) to a one year training is in due compliance with R. 10 of 1969 Rules and R. 18 of 1974 Rules and the seniority of the both batches rightly settled - No illegality in the judgment of the HC in upholding the seniority of the candidates of 1980-81 batch over the candidates of 1979-81 batch - Appeal dismissed.
