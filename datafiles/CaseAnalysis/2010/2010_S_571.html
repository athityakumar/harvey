Sunder Singh v State of Uttaranchal

Supreme Court of India

16 September 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

V.S. Sirpurkar, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 752; (2010) 10 SCC 611; 2010 (4) JhrCR(SC) 191; JT 2010 (9) SC 632; 2011 (1) MLJ(Crl) 581; 2010 (4) RCR(Criminal) 484; 2010(9) SCALE 571; [2010] 11 S.C.R. 927; 2010 (3) UC 1753

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 307 and 436 - Chargesheet for offences u/ss. 302, 307 and 436 of IPC - Sessions Judge on the basis of the evidence of PW-1 and the dying declaration of deceased and the other substantive evidence held that it was appellant who torched the room resulting in the victims being burnt alive - Sessions Judge convicted the appellant for offences u/ss. 302, 307 and 436 of IPC and awarded death sentence - On appeal, HC also held that this was a rarest of rare case and, therefore, affirmed the death sentence awarded to the appellant - Hence, present appeal - (A) Conviction - Held, PW-1 was a natural witness and there can be no dispute about her presence on the spot - She was also an injured witness as she herself suffered 70% burns - Considering the overall evidence which was accepted by the Trial Court and HC, it was clear that this witness was reliable - Hence, considering overall evidence led by the prosecution it must be said that it was the appellant and appellant alone was guilt and his guilt was proved beyond all reasonable doubts - Judgments of the Trial Court and the HC confirmed - (B) Sentence - Whether present was the rarest of rare case? - Held, in the present case, five persons lost their life while the sixth person, PW-1, has to live her life with 70% burn injuries - Murder was committed in a cruel, grotesque and diabolical manner - There was no immediate provocation to the appellant - Considering the overall circumstances, present case is rarest of rare case and death sentence was rightly awarded by the Trial Court and was rightly confirmed by the HC - Appeal dismissed.
