Dilip D. Chowdhari and another v Maharashtra Executor and Trustee and others

Supreme Court of India

29 April 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Dattu, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 361; (2010) 6 SCC 633; 2010 (4) ALD(SC) 128; 2011 (3) Bom.C.R. 215; JT 2010 (6) SC 157; 2010 (4) MPLJ 280; 2010 (110) RD 523; 2010(4) SCALE 507

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Keywords: High Court (Original Side) Rules

Summary: Family & Personal - Companies Act, 1956 - Beneficiaries - Execution of will - Suit property was residential building having ground and three floors - Deceased testator was owner of suit property having purchased same under registered sale deed out of his personal earnings - Will was duly probated by executors/company formed under the Act - Executors/company sought interpretation of certain covenants of Will and determination of certain questions that had arose in administration of Will - Several questions regarding right of residence as conferred by Will were framed for determination by HC which was denied by HC - Appellant made Appeal before Division Bench of HC and that also resulted into dismissal - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether appellant's right to use and occupy third floor of suit building in terms of Will of testator was restricted to - Held, will specifically provided that ultimate beneficiaries of entire building were four Grandsons - Though, it was in dispute which required executors of Will to file originating summons before HC, seeking interpretation of certain covenants of will and also determination of certain questions that had arose in administration of Will - Therefore, HC both single Judge and Division Bench by adopting highly hyper technical approach, should given very narrow interpretation of contents of Will and had restricted appellant's right to use and occupy third floor of Suit building to only one right hand side last room - Thus, after carefully perusing sketch produced by appellant which was not disputed by other side and also keeping in view bare necessity of middle class family it was held that appellant had right of occupation of third floor of suit building along with wife of testator - Appeal allowed

Legislation Cited

Companies Act, 1956

Hindu Succession Act, 1956
