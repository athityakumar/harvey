 Dr. K. Krishna Murthy and others v Union of India and another

Supreme Court of India

11 May 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

K.G. Balakrishnan, R.V. Raveendran, D.K. Jain, P. Sathasivam, J.M. Panchal

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 377; (2010) 7 SCC 202; 2010 (4) ALD(SC) 70; 2010 (4) AWC 3819; JT 2010 (5) SC 601; 2010 (4) MLJ(SC) 393; 2010 (2) OLR 530; 2010 (3) RCR(Civil) 108; 2010(5) SCALE 448; [2010] 6 S.C.R. 972; 2010 (3) WLN(SC) 17

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Affirmative Action, Local Self-Government, Karnataka Panchayati Raj Act, 1993, Uttar Pradesh Kshetra Panchayat and Zilla Panchayat Act, 1961

Summary: (A) Constitution - Municipalities & Local Governments - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 15(4), 16(4), 243-D(6), 243-T(6), 243-D(4) and 243-T(4) - Constitution (Seventy-third) Amendment Act, 1992 - Constitution (Seventy- fourth) Amendment Act, 1992 - Constitutional validity of some aspects of reservation policy prescribed for the composition of elected local self-government institutions - Reservations in elected local bodies - Whether art. 243-D(6) and art. 243-T(6) are constitutionally valid since they enable reservations in favour of backward classes for the purpose of occupying seats and chairperson positions in Panchayats and Municipalities respectively? - Held, art. 243-D(6) and 243-T(6) do not provide guidance on how to identify the backward classes and neither do they specify any principle for the quantum of such reservations - Art. 243-D(6) and art. 243-T(6) are constitutionally valid since they are in the nature of provisions which merely enable State Legislatures to reserve seats and chairperson posts in favour of backward classes - Concerns about disproportionate reservations should be raised by way of specific challenges against the State Legislations - Writ petition disposed of.

(B) Constitution - Municipalities & Local Governments - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 15(4), 16(4), 243-D(6), 243-T(6), 243-D(4) and 243-T(4) - Validity of reserving Chairperson positions - Main criticism against the reservation of chairperson positions in local self-government is that the same amounts to cent-per-cent reservation since they are akin to solitary posts - Whether art. 243-D(4) and art. 243-T(4) are constitutionally valid? - Held, art. 243-D(4) provides a clear Constitutional basis for reserving the Chairperson positions in favour of SC and STs (in a proportionate manner) while also providing that one-third of all chairperson positions in each tier of the Panchayati Raj Institutions would be reserved in favour of women - Considerations behind the provisions of art. 243-D cannot be readily compared with those of art. 16(4) which is the basis for reservations in public employment - Reservation of chairperson posts in the manner contemplated by arts. 243-D(4) and 243-T(4) is constitutionally valid - These chairperson posts cannot be equated with solitary posts in the context of public employment - Writ petitions disposed of.

(C) Constitution - Municipalities & Local Governments - Reservations in context of Local-Self Government - Overbreadth in the State legislations - Petitioners objected to reservations in favour of OBCs to the tune of 33% in the State of Karnataka and 27% in the State of Uttar Pradesh - It was contended that the reservations in favour of OBCs do not meet the tests of 'reasonable classification' and proportionality - Petitioner's real concern was with overbreadth in the State legislations - Held, no adequate materials or argumentation provided about the same - In the absence of updated empirical data, Court not in a position to examine the claims about overbreadth in the quantum of reservations provided for OBCs under the impugned State Legislations since there is no contemporaneous empirical data - Open to the petitioners or any aggrieved party to challenge any State legislation enacted in pursuance of the constitutional provisions before the HC - Writ petitions disposed of.

(D) Constitution - Municipalities & Local Governments - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 15(4), 16(4), 243-D(6), 243-T(6), 243-D(4) and 243-T(4) - Nature and purpose of reservations in the context of local self-government - Held, nature and purpose of reservations in the context of local self-government is considerably different from that of higher education and public employment - In this sense, art. 243-D and art. 243-T form a distinct and independent constitutional basis for affirmative action and the principles that have been evolved in relation to the reservation policies enabled by arts. 15(4) and 16(4) cannot be readily applied in the context of local self-government - Even when made, they need not be for a period corresponding to the period of reservation for purposes of arts. 15(4) and 16(4), but can be much shorter - Writ petitions disposed of.

(E) Constitution - Municipalities & Local Governments - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 15(4), 16(4), 243-D(6), 243-T(6), 243-D(4) and 243-T(4) - Validity of reserving Chairperson positions - Whether reservation of chairperson posts in local bodies amounts to an unreasonable limitation on the rights of political participation of persons who do not belong to the reserved categories? - Held, Constitution empowers the Election Commission of India to prepare electoral rolls for the purpose of identifying the eligible voters in elections for the Lok Sabha and the Vidhan Sabhas - Right to vote is not an inherent right and it cannot be claimed in an abstract sense - Furthermore, the Representation of People Act, 1951 gives effect to the Constitutional guidance on the eligibility of persons to contest elections - This includes grounds that render persons ineligible from contesting elections such as that of a person not being a citizen of India, a person being of unsound mind, insolvency and the holding of an 'office of profit' under the executive among others - It will suffice to say that there is no inherent right to contest elections since there are explicit legislative controls over the same - Writ petition disposed of.

(F) Constitution - Municipalities & Local Governments - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 15(4), 16(4), 243-D(6), 243-T(6), 243-D(4) and 243-T(4) - Upper ceiling - Vertical reservations - Held, upper ceiling of 50% vertical reservations in favour of SC/ST/OBCs should not be breached in the context of local self-government - Exceptions can only be made in order to safeguard the interests of Scheduled Tribes in the matter of their representation in panchayats located in the Scheduled Areas - Writ petitions disposed of.
