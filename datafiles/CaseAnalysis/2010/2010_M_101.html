M/S. Modern Industries v M/S. Steel Authority Of India Limited And Others

Supreme Court of India

15 April 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 268; (2010) 5 SCC 44; AIR 2010 SC 1625; 2010(3) ALL MR 464; 2010 (5) AWC 4461; 2010 (2) CLT(SC) 165; 2010 (2) DRTC 153; JT 2010 (4) SC 10; 2010 (4) MLJ(SC) 416; 2010(3) SCALE 794; [2010] 190 TAXMAN 436

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Conveyancing and Law of Property Act, 1881, Interest on Delayed Payments to Small Scale and Ancillary Industrial Undertakings (Amendment) Act. 1998, Interest on Delayed Payments to Small Scale and Ancillary Industrial Undertakings Ordinance, 1992

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Constitution - Interest on Delayed Payments to Small Scale and Ancillary Industrial Undertakings Act, 1993, s. 6 as amended - Recovery of due amount - Petitioner-Supplier got an order of Rs. 8.19 lakhs from the respondent-buyer for manufacture of Right Manipulator Side Guard - Initially buyer did not issue raw-materials but subsequently buyer agreed to supply the materials free of cost and also extended the period of supplies - Buyer ordered for release of Rs. 6,07, 493/- as an interim payment but deducted the balance payment of Rs. 2,11,506/- out of Rs. 8.19 lakhs of the original order as the cost of the supply of materials - Supplier, accordingly, raised a dispute in respect of balance payment together with interest on delayed payment before Industry Facilitation Council (IFC) u/s. 6(2) of 1993 Act - Buyer objected to the jurisdiction of IFC in dealing with the matter - IFC passed an ex-parte award against the buyer - IFC also directed that the interest would be payable w.e.f. 24-9-1997 till the date of full payment - On appeal, DB quashed and set aside the awards of IFC - Hence, present appeal - Whether DB's order could be upheld? - Held, whole purpose and object behind 1993 Act as amended in 1998 is to ensure that buyer promptly pays the amount due towards the goods supplied or the services rendered by the supplier - It also provides for payment of interest statutorily on the outstanding money in case of default - Plain reading of s. 6 would show that nature of dispute to be adjudicated by the IFC as an arbitrator or resolution thereof as a conciliator is in respect of the matters referred to in sub-section (1), i.e., the amount due from a buyer together with the amount of interest calculated in accordance with the provisions of ss. 4 and 5 of 1993 Act - Original contract was for a sum of Rs. 8.19 lakhs, out of which Rs. 6.07 lakhs have already been paid in July, 1997 and goods worth balance amount were given to the supplier and yet buyer is saddled with the liability for an amount of Rs. 24,86,998/- with interest at the rate of 18 per cent compounded with monthly interests from 24-09-1997 - Situation in which the buyer has been placed is their own creation as they chose not to contest the claim of the supplier before IFC on merits - No written statement was filed despite opportunity granted by IFC - Even before the HC, no submission seems to have been made on merits of the award at all - In the circumstances, the buyer does not deserve any indulgence from this Court - Pertinently, though 1993 Act provides a statutory remedy of appeal against the award but the buyer did not avail of the statutory remedy and instead challenged the award passed by IFC before HC in extraordinary jurisdiction under Article 226 of the Constitution by passing statutory remedy which was not justified - HC's order set aside - Appeals allowed
