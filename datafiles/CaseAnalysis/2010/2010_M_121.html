Snehadeep Structures Private Limited v Maharashtra Small Scale Industries Development Corporation Limited

Supreme Court of India

05 January 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Tarun Chatterjee, V.S. Sirpurkar

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 53; (2010) 3 SCC 34; AIR 2010 SC 1497; 2010(2) ALL MR 445; 2010 (2) AWC 1981; 2010 (5) Bom.C.R. 371; 2010 (1) CLT 127; 2010 (1) DRTC 305; JT 2010 (1) SC 70; 2010 (3) MPLJ 11; 2010 (1) RCR(Civil) 705; 2010(1) SCALE 108; [2010] 1 S.C.R. 76

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Keywords: Arbitral Award, Maharashtra Cooperative Societies Act, Micro, Small and Medium Enterprises Development Act, 2006, Industrial Tribunals (Appellate Tribunal) Act, 1950, High Court of Bombay Rules

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Interest on Delayed Payments to Small Scale and Ancillary Undertakings Act, 1993, s. 7 - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, ss. 11, 34 - Respondent/Corporation issued a supply order to appellant/Small Scale Unit for the supply of pipes - However, respondent delayed the payment of bills, therefore, appellant demanded interest under the provisions of Interest Act - Respondent denied appellant's demand of interest - Appellant filed an application u/s. 11 of the Arbitration Act before the HC for appointment of an arbitrator - HC appointed an arbitrator - Arbitrator, by his award, directed respondent to pay the interest - Respondent challenged the award before the HC u/s. 34 of the Arbitration Act - Single Judge ordered the respondent to make a pre-deposit of 75 per cent of award amount whilst the pendency of arbitration application - Respondent challenged the Single Judge order before DB - DB set aside the Single Judge order and held that application to challenge the award u/s. 34 of the Arbitration Act could not be an appeal under the provisions of Interest Act - Whether the expression 'appeal' used in s. 7 of the Interest Act includes an application to set aside the arbitral award filed u/s. 34 of the Arbitration Act - Held, word 'award' in s.7 of the Interest Act includes an arbitral award - Only way of challenging an arbitral award in a Court is filing an application u/s. 34 of the Arbitration Act - If such challenge is not construed as an 'appeal', the requirement of pre-deposit of interest u/s. 7 of the Interest Act before the buyer challenging an award passed against him, becomes a total nullity - Legislative intent behind s. 7 of the Interest Act was to target buyers, who, only with the end of pushing off the ultimate event of payment to the small scale industry undertaking, institute challenges against the award /decree/order passed against them - Such buyers cannot be allowed to challenge arbitral awards indiscriminately, especially when the s. 7 of the Interest Act requires pre-deposit of 75 per cent interest even when appeal is preferred against an award, as distinguished from an order or decree - Therefore, the term 'appeal' appearing in s. 7 of the Interest Act should include an application u/s. 34 of the Arbitration Act as well, hence the impugned DB judgment is set aside - Appeal partly allowed.
