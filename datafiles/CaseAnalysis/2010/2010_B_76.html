State of West Bengal v Bireswar Dutta Estate Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

18 November 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 1018; 2010(13) SCALE 103

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Keywords: West Bengal Premises Requisition and Control (Temporary Provisions) Act, 1947

Summary: Rent Control - Suit for eviction - Decree - Mesne profit - Liability - Premises in dispute originally belonged to respondent and entire building was requisitioned by appellant and appellant became liable to vacate and deliver vacant possession but continued in unauthorized possession - By order made in contempt petition filed by respondent, HC granted three months time to appellant but period of three months expired - Respondent filed suit seeking physical possession before Single Judge of HC was decreed - Decree was challenged by appellant by filing appeal aggrieved by quantum of mesne profits before DB where judgment upheld decision of Single Judge - Thereafter, it heard parties on issue of mesne profits and set aside decree of Single Judge in so far as determination of mesne profits and referred matter to Commissioner (retired Judge) for quantifying mesne profits - Commissioner appointed by DB issued notice to parties for determination of mesne profits but State did not participate in proceedings and considering evidence placed by respondent, Commissioner submitted report under which he determined mesne profits - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether appellant was liable to pay mesne profit and compensation to respondent as ordered by HC - Held, appellant was given sufficient number of opportunities by Commissioner to place its evidence in regard to mesne profits but appellant did not do so - It was found from report of Commissioner which was accepted by HC that Commissioner had determined mesne profits with reference to rent that was being paid by ground-floor tenant after making appropriate adjustment for fact that valuation related to upper floors - It was not in dispute that premises was situated in heart of city in commercial area - HC having regard to that facts had accepted Commissioner's report and directed that mesne profits should be paid for to date of delivery of possession - Thus, award of compensation/mesne profits did not suffer from any infirmity nor call for interference - Appellant was not able to demonstrate any prejudice on account of report of Commissioner - Hence, judgment of HC in regard to quantum of mesne profits and period for which it was found payable, was upheld and appellant should pay compensation and mesne profits to respondent - Appeal allowed.
