Indu Bhushan Dwivedi v State Of Jharkhand And Another

Supreme Court of India

05 July 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, Chandramauli Kumar Prasad

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 454; (2010) 11 SCC 278; AIR 2010 SC 2472; 2010 (5) AWC 5251; 2010 (4) ESC 469; 2010 (126) FLR 994; 2010 (3) JhrCR(SC) 188; JT 2010 (6) SC 385; 2011 (1) LLJ 719; 2010 (6) MLJ(SC) 603; 2010(6) SCALE 212; [2010] 7 S.C.R. 465; 2010 (4) SLR 422

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Consideration of un-communicated adverse ACRs - Legality - Appellant, a Sub-Divisional Judicial Magistrate was placed under suspension by HC with a direction that he shall not leave the headquarter without obtaining prior permission from the Registrar General of the HC - A regular departmental inquiry was initiated against appellant on charges amounting to insubordination, indiscipline and unbecoming a Judicial Officer - Inquiry was held against appellant on three charges, (i) after having consumed liquor, he had misbehaved and manhandled an accused and a constable; this charge was not found proved; (ii) that he had left headquarter without seeking permission from Registrar General of HC in violation of direction issued by HC and (iii) that he had used derogatory words qua communication sent by HC, which charges were proved - A show cause notice was issued to appellant - After considering his reply HC recommended his dismissal from service - State Government accepting recommendation of HC passed order whereby appellant was dismissed from service - Appellant challenged aforementioned order by contending that same was vitiated due to violation of rules of natural justice because while recommending his dismissal from service, HC had considered un-communicated adverse remarks recorded in Annual Confidential Report (ACR) without informing him that same were being relied upon for deciding quantum of punishment - It was held that the appellant himself made a request in his reply that his past record may be considered, no prejudice can be said to have been caused to him on account of consideration of the adverse reports - DB concluded that punishment of dismissal imposed on appellant is not sustainable but declined to set aside same on ground that substantial time has lapsed since initiation of inquiry and proceeded to impose punishment of compulsory retirement upon appellant - Present appeal - (A) Whether HC could have while recommending appellant's dismissal from service taken into consideration un-communicated adverse ACRs? - Held, while recommending or imposing punishment on an employee, who is found guilty of misconduct, the disciplinary/competent authority cannot consider his past adverse record or punishment without giving him an opportunity to explain his position and considering his explanation - However, such an opportunity is not required to be given if the final punishment is lesser than the proposed punishment - In present case, since un-communicated adverse remarks contained in ACRs of appellant became foundation of decision taken by HC to recommend his dismissal from service and he was not notified about proposed consideration of those remarks, it must be held that appellant was seriously prejudiced - (B) Whether Division Bench of HC was right in distinguishing judgment of Constitution Bench in State of Mysore v. K. Manche Gowda 1963 INDLAW SC 319 on ground that appellant had himself made a request for consideration of past record? - Held, ratio of Manche Gowda's case is that past adverse record of delinquent employee cannot be considered at stage of imposing punishment unless he is put to notice and given an opportunity to explain his position - A reading of representation made by appellant makes it clear that he had only mentioned that there was no report against his integrity and honesty and he was never found guilty of any act of insubordination or indiscipline in his service career - This assertion, cannot be construed as a request by appellant for consideration of his past record - Thus, DB of HC clearly misread representation made by appellant and distinguished judgment of Constitution Bench in Manche Gowda's case without any tangible reason - Finding recorded by Division Bench of HC that appellant's cause was not prejudiced on account of consideration of past adverse record clearly erroneous and unsustainable - Impugned order of Division Bench of HC set aside - HC to consider issue of quantum of punishment afresh - Appeal allowed.
