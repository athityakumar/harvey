Siddanki Ram Reddy v State of Andhra Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

27 July 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Patnaik, R.M. Lodha

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 560; (2010) 7 SCC 697; AIR 2010 SC 3000; 2010 CRLJ 3910; JT 2010 (8) SC 48; 2010 (4) MLJ(Crl) 694; 2010 (3) RCR(Criminal) 818; 2010(7) SCALE 332; [2010] 8 S.C.R. 1119; 2010 (2) UC 1371

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant allegedly murdered the deceased - Trial Court convicted the appellant for murder on the basis of the evidences of three eye-witnesses - Appellant filed appeal before the HC - HC confirmed the Trial Court order - Whether conviction by the Trial Court is sustainable - Held, evidence of the PW1/eye-witness was not reliable since he had failed to mention any injury inflicted on deceased by appellant in the FIR - PW1/eye-witness could not identify any person as assailant of the deceased in the Test Identification Parade including the appellant - PW5/eye-witness identified the appellant as assailant in the Test Identification Parade, however, PW5 had prior knowledge that the appellant was the accused in the present case - PW6/eye-witness stated in the Test Identification Parade that the suspect had injury mark on his right cheek, however, the Magistrate conducting the Test Identification Parade had stated in his evidence that none of the two suspects including appellant had injury mark on the right cheek, therefore, evidence of the PW6 was also unreliable - Although eight suspects were arrested, only the appellant and another one were produced before the witnesses at the Test Identification Parade, therefore, Test Identification Parade was not fair to the appellant - In the instant case, both the Trial Court and the HC mechanically relied on the evidences of eye-witnesses that it was the appellant who had attacked the deceased with an axe without appreciating that it was unsafe to rest conviction upon the evidence of eye-witnesses with regard to the identification of the assailant - Therefore, impugned judgments of the Trial Court and HC were set aside - Appeal allowed.
