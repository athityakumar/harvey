Singapagu Anjaiah v State Of Andhra Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

06 July 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Chandramauli Kumar Prasad, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 459; (2010) 9 SCC 799; JT 2010 (6) SC 502; 2010 (3) RCR(Criminal) 747; 2010(6) SCALE 374; 2010 (2) UC 1261

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 148, 307/149, 302/149 and 324 - Conviction u/ss. 148, 307, 302 and 324 - Challenged - Trial court relying on the evidence of the prosecution witnesses acquitted all the accused persons for the offence u/s. 307/149 of the IPC but sentenced all of them for offence u/ss. 148, 324/149 and 302/149 and sentenced them to undergo imprisonment for life for the offence u/s. 302/149 and rigorous imprisonment for nine months for the offence u/ss. 148 and 324/149 of the IPC - On appeal, conviction of all the accused except the appellant u/s. 302/149 of the IPC was set aside - Appellant's conviction and sentence u/ss. 148, 324 and 302 of the IPC has been maintained - Appellant filed appeal against said order submitting that even if the case of the prosecution is accepted in its entirety, no offence u/s. 302 of the IPC is made out - Appellant further submitted that the allegation at the most make out the case u/s. 304 Part II of the IPC and that conviction u/s. 302 be altered to that of 304 Part II - Whether appellant was rightly convicted u/ss. 148, 324 and 302 of the IPC? - Held, all the injured witnesses namely P.W. 1 to 5 have consistently stated that the appellant assaulted the deceased with a crow bar on his head - As nobody can enter into the mind of the accused, the intention has to be gathered from the weapon used, the part of the body chosen for the assault and the nature of the injuries caused - Here, the appellant had chosen a crow bar as the weapon of offence - He has further chosen a vital part of the body i.e. head for causing the injury which had caused multiple fractures of skull - Weapon used, the part of the body chosen for assault and the intensity with which the appellant assaulted the deceased clearly go to show that he intended to cause the death of the deceased - Hence, appellant rightly convicted /ss. 148, 324 and 302 of the IPC- Appeal dismissed
