Executive Engineer, Uttaranchal Power Corporation v M/S. Kashi Vishwanath Steel Ltd. & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

12 May 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 799; (2010) 6 SCC 738; 2010 (81) ALR 303; 2010 (4) AWC 3689; JT 2010 (6) SC 166; 2010(5) SCALE 687; [2010] 6 S.C.R. 1086

Case Digest

Subject: Electricity & Energy

Keywords: Supply Of Energy, Uttar Pradesh Electricity Reforms Act, 1999

Summary: Electricity & Energy - Surcharge - Exemption of - Respondent entered into an agreement with Uttar Pradesh Power Corporation Ltd. for the supply of electricity for the production of Furnace and Steel Rolling Manufacturing (Process) unit - Subsequently, Corporation issued a circular dt. 8.09.2000 providing that if consumers connected to independent feeders did not want electricity supply for the guaranteed period of 500 hours, no surcharge of 15% could be levied provided they intimate to the Executive Engineer - Respondent addressed letter to the Executive Engineer of the Corporation to the effect that the former did not require the assured supply of electricity for 500 hours - Executive Engineer issued an office memo dt. 24.10.2000 granting exemption to the respondent from payment of 15% surcharge - Meanwhile, 'X', filed a petition before HC submitting that respondent was liable to pay 15% surcharge under the tariff determined by the U.P. Electricity Regulatory Commission as payment of 15% surcharge by consumers drawing energy from an independent feeder was not subject to the observance of peak hours restrictions because those who consume power between 6 p.m. to 1 p.m. had to pay an additional amount of 1% surcharge on the energy charge - DB held that only benefit that the consumers who drew power from such independent feeders but who do not get supply for the minimum 500 hours in a month were granted a rebate @ 1% for every 10 hours or part thereof if the continuous supply had failed - Relying on this decision, Corporation withdraw the exemption granted to respondent - Respondent filed writ petition against said withdrawal - Said writ petition was allowed by HC who held that since the respondent did not require assured supply of 500 hours electricity in a month it was not liable to pay 15% surcharge and that the exemption granted by the Corporation was valid - Hence, present appeal - Whether Corporation rightly withdrew the earlier exemption from payment of 15% surcharge granted to respondent? - Held, Commission approved a new tariff by notification dt. 10.07.2001 which does not any longer provide for 15% surcharge from consumers getting supply of energy from independent feeders - There is not even a murmur in the writ petition filed by the respondent to the effect that either the Corporation or its successor had at any point of time made any promise to the company that supply of energy would be without any surcharge - There is similarly no averment whatsoever in the writ petition to the effect that the respondent had altered its position acting upon any such promise - Not only that the agreements executed between the parties, did not contain any unequivocal promise for supply of energy, no matter the supply was made from an independent feeder - In the absence of even an averment to the effect that there was a promise made by the Corporation regarding supply of energy without payment of surcharge and in the absence of any material to show that the respondent had indeed acted upon any such promise it is difficult to see how the said company can insist upon any such non-existent promise being made good - Hence, Corporation rightly withdraw the earlier exemption from payment of 15% surcharge granted to respondent - Appeal allowed.
