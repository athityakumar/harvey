Amal Kumar Ghosh and others v Basanta Kumar Almal

Supreme Court of India

28 April 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, R.M. Lodha

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 360; (2010) 11 SCC 78; 2010 (3) AWC 3247; 2010 (4) CalLT 56; JT 2010 (6) SC 355; 2010 (2) RCR(Civil) 875; 2010 (111) RD 308; 2010(4) SCALE 651; [2010] 5 S.C.R. 357

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Keywords: Receivers, Outgoings

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Suit for specific performance - First respondent filed a suit for specific performance against the appellants which was dismissed by Single Judge of HC - First respondent filed an appeal against the dismissal of the suit before the DB - During the pendency of the appeal, first respondent and appellants arrived at a settlement and in terms of the said settlement, appellants agreed to sell 90 Kottahs of land to the first respondent or his nominees at a price of Rs.10,000/- per Kottah - Second respondent was appointed as Receiver - In pursuance of the settlement, after payment of Rs.3,00,000/- to the appellants, the balance sale consideration of Rs.6,00,000/- was deposited with the Receiver - Appellants informed the receiver that they had transferred the suit premises to eleven nominees of the first respondent and gave the instructions to the Receiver in regard to Rs.6,00,000/- deposited by the purchasers - But the Receiver did not pay the sum of Rs.3,00,000/- to 'X' - Appellants made an application to the HC seeking a direction to the Receiver to render accounts and make payments of the amounts due to them - HC dismissed said application - Hence, present appeal - Whether Receiver discharge his obligations on the 'Terms of Settlement'? - Held, appellants had instructed the Receiver to pay Rs.3,00,000/- to 'X' towards statutory liabilities and other outgoings in regard to the property - But the Receiver did not comply with the said instructions as he admitted that the sum of Rs.3,00,000/- was not paid by him to the 'X' - Nor did 'X' make a demand for payment of the said amount by the Receiver - There is also no explanation from the Receiver as to why he directly paid Rs.1,08,341.64p. to the Calcutta Municipal Corporation as that was not part of the instructions by the appellants - Demand for payment was ignored and the Receiver chose to pay the amount to the purchasers without any satisfactory explanation - Impugned order of HC set aside and matter remitted back for fresh decision - Directions issued - Appeal allowed.
