Dasrath v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

29 July 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

V.S. Sirpurkar, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 563; (2010) 12 SCC 198; AIR 2010 SC 2592; 2010 CRLJ 4382; 2010 (4) JCC 2553; JT 2010 (7) SC 553; 2010 (3) RCR(Criminal) 814; 2010(7) SCALE 389; [2010] 9 S.C.R. 266; 2010 (3) ShimLC 400

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 201 and 304B - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 113 - Dowry death - Conviction - Challenged - Appellant was convicted u/ss. 201 and 304B of IPC - Conviction upheld by HC - Hence, present appeal - Held, in the present case, all the three ingredients of s. 304B of IPC (1) that the death of a woman has been caused by burns or bodily injury or occurs otherwise than under normal circumstances; (2) that such death has been caused or has occurred within seven years of her marriage; and (3) that soon before her death the woman was subjected to cruelty or harassment by her husband or any relative of her husband in connection with any demand for dowry as also the presumption u/s. 113B of Evidence Act are fully established - Appeal dismissed.
