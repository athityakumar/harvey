Varun Chaudhary v State of Rajasthan

Supreme Court of India

29 October 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

A.R. Dave, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 938; AIR 2011 SC 72; 2011 CRLJ 675; JT 2010 (11) SC 419; 2011 (4) RCR(Criminal) 475; 2011 (1) RLW 791; 2010(11) SCALE 502; 2010 (3) UC 2026

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 302 and 307 - Murder - Conviction - Challenged - Accused Nos.1, 2 and 3 were arrested and charge-sheeted for offence punishable u/s. 302 of IPC - It was the alleged that the deceased, an Income Tax Officer had raided the premises belonging to some scrap dealers and, therefore, he had received some threats from such scrap dealers - Accused allegedly committed the offence of murder by inflicting serious injuries upon the deceased - Trial Court held that accused no.1 was guilty of having committed an offence u/s. 302 of IPC but acquitted accused Nos. 2 and 3 - On appeals, HC confirmed the order of conviction of accused No.1 and held that accused Nos. 2 and 3 were also guilty and convicted them u/s. 302 r/w s. 34 of IPC - Present appeals - Held, in the instant case, where there was no eye witness or where there was no scientific evidence to connect the accused with the offence, prosecution ought to have established that there was some motive behind commission of the offence of murder of the deceased - However, accused were not scrap dealers or there was nothing to show that the accused had been engaged by scrap dealers to commit the offence - Thus, there was no motive behind the commission of the offence so far as the accused - Prosecution could not establish the purpose for which the deceased was murdered by the accused - Further, there was nothing to show that the accused had committed the offence - Conclusion reached by the courts below was not correct - Impugned orders set aside - Appeals allowed.
