 Baburao Shantaram More v The Bombay Housing Board Andanother.,

Supreme Court of India

18 December 1953

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhi Ranjan Das, M. Patanjali Sastri, Vivian Bose, Ghulam Hasan, B. Jagannadhadas

Where Reported

1953 Indlaw SC 51; AIR 1954 SC 153; 1954 SCJ 210; [1954] 1 S.C.R. 572

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Co-operative Societies Act, Land Acquisition (Bombay Amendment) Act, 1948, Bombay Housing Boards Act, 1948, Bombay Rent, Hotel and lodging House Rates Control Act, 1947

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition (Bombay Amendment) Act, 1948, ss. 3-A and 4 - Bombay Housing Boards Act, 1948 - Bombay Rent, Hotel and lodging House Rates Control Act, 1947 - Co-operative Societies Act - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Petitioner is in occupation of two rooms in Sion Dharavi Camp in Greater Bombay - In or about February, 1950, Board served a notice on petitioner calling upon him to quit and vacate rooms in his occupation at end of March, 1950 - Meanwhile, s. 3-A was added by amending Act which exempted lands or buildings belonging to or vested in Board from operation of Bombay Rent Act - (A) Whether s. 3-A offends against equal protection clause of Constitution as tenants of Co-operative Housing Societies are protected by Bombay Rent Act while tenants of Board are denied same? - Held, though these Co-operative Housing Societies are no doubt incorporated bodies, they nevertheless may earn profits which may be distributed amongst their members - Board, on other hand, is an incorporated body brought into existence for purpose of framing housing schemes to solve problem of acute shortage of accommodation in Bombay - No material has been placed before us which may even remotely be regarded as suggesting, much less proving, that Co-operative Housing Societies or their 'members stand similarly situated vis-a-vis Board and its tenants - (B) Whether s. 4 discriminates against tenants of properties belonging to Govt. in that these tenants are denied benefits of Bombay Rent Act which are available to all other tenants in Bombay? - Further held, it is not to be expected that Govt. or local authority or Board, would be actuated by any profit making motive as to unduly enhance rents or eject tenants from their respective properties - Therefore, tenants of Govt. are not in need of such protection as tenants of private landlords are and this circumstance is a cogent basis for differentiation - Two classes of tenants are not, by force of circumstances, placed on an equal footing and tenants of Govt. cannot, therefore, complain of any denial of equality before law or of equal protection of law - Petitions dismissed.
