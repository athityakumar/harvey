Basir-Ul-Huq and Others Nur-Ul-Huda v State of West Bengal

Supreme Court of India

10 April 1953

Case Analysis

Bench

Mehr Chand Mahajan, Vivian Bose, B. Jagannadhadas

Where Reported

1953 Indlaw SC 29; AIR 1953 SC 293; 1953 CRLJ 1232; 1953 (1) MLJ(SC) 775; 1953 SCJ 405; [1953] 4 S.C.R. 836

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure 1898

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898, ss. 195 and 500 - Scope of s. 195 of CrPC, 1898 - False allegation made against Dhirendra Nath Bera of killing his mother - Bosy dent for re examination and no proof in support of allegation found - Petition was files against appellants that they had made imputations mala fide out of enmity against him with intention of harming his reputation and that to wound his religious feelings they had trespassed on cremation ground and caused dead body to be taken out by making false imputations - (A) Whether s. 195 of CrPC, 1898 bars trial of an accused person for a distinct offence disclosed by same facts? - Held, s. 195 of CrPC, 1898 does not bar trial of an accused person for a distinct offence disclosed by same facts and which is not included within ambit of that section but provisions of that section cannot be evaded by resorting to devices or camouflages - (B) Whether offence u/ss. 500, 182 and 297 of IPC, 1860 can be tried without sanction u/s. 195 of CrPC, 1898? - Ingredients of offence u/s. 182 cannot be said to be ingredients for offence u/s. 500 - Nor can it be said that offence relating to giving false information relates to same group of offences as that of defamation - Further held, offences u/ss. 500, 297 and 182 of IPC, 1860 can be tried without sanction u/s. 195 of CrPC, 1898 - Appeal dismissed.
