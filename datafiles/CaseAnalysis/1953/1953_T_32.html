 Motipur Zamindari Company Limited v The State of Bihar and Another. Raja Jankinath Roy and Narendra Nath Roy and Company Limited

Supreme Court of India

17 April 1953

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhi Ranjan Das, Bijan Kumar Mukherjea, Ghulam Hasan, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, M. Patanjali Sastri

Where Reported

1953 Indlaw SC 95; AIR 1953 SC 320; 1953 (32) ILR(Pat) 529; 1953 SCJ 451; [1953] 4 S.C.R. 720

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Land & Property - Constitution (First Amendment) Act, 1951 - Bihar Land Reforms Act, ss. 3, 2(o), 2(r) and 41 - Zamindari Estates - Taking over of by State of Bihar under provisions of Act - Vide Notification D/- 06-11-1951 tenures/estates belonging to appellants/company as specified in notification had passed to and become vested in State - Whether Act was, on its true construction, intended to apply to Zamindari estates of companies incorporated under Indian Companies Act? - First part of definition of 'proprietor', is a person holding in trust or owning for his own benefit an estate or a part of an estate and second part says that it includes heirs and successors in interest of proprietor - Appellant-company come within first part of definition, but word 'heir' in second part is inappropriate with regard to a company, but there is nothing inappropriate in company having a successor in-interest - Upon a notification being issued u/s. 3, Zamindari estate will vest in State and company will cease to have any interest in it - Its only right will be to receive compensation - In case of winding up liquidator will have to pursue remedy provided by Act - He or company will be in no worse position than official assignee or official receiver of an individual proprietor who may happen to become insolvent in another State - No reason to differentiate between an individual proprietor and a company which owns estates or tenures - Indeed, there is not only nothing repugnant in subject or context of Act which should prevent inclusion of a company owning estate within definition of 'proprietor', such inclusion is necessary in order to give full effect to very object of Act - Held, Act apply to Zamindari estates of companies incorporated under Companies Act - Appeals dismissed.
