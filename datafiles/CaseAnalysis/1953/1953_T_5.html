State of Assam v Keshab Prasad Singh and Another

Supreme Court of India

14 April 1953

Case Analysis

Bench

Vivian Bose, Mehr Chand Mahajan, B. Jagannadhadas

Where Reported

1953 Indlaw SC 56; AIR 1953 SC 309; 1953 SCJ 429; [1953] 4 S.C.R. 865

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Land & Property

Keywords: Notions, Assam Land and Revenue Regulation , 1886

Summary: Land & Property - Indian Evidence Act, 1872 - Constitution Of India, 1950 - Assam Land and Revenue Regulation, 1886, r. 190 A - Whether High Court had jurisdiction to act as an appellate tribunal u/r. 190? - Held, apart from r. 190-A, Deputy Commr. alone could effect a 'settlement' and he was bound to follow a prescribed procedure and also that his 'settlement' was subject to sanction of Commr. - R. 190-A permits a departure but it does not consider it necessary in this case to determine exact extent of departure permitted because Deputy Commr. was directed to put fishery to auction and he did so - Only departure from rules was that instead of sending result of auction to Commr. for settlement it was sent to State Govt. direct - That was a permissible departure but it was for all that a departure within Rules - One of statutory safeguards against arbitrary executive action is appeal to Revenue Tribunal, which in this case is High Court - Now only act which would be in consonance with r. 190-A and which would at same time be in conformity with letter of first February would be for Deputy Commr. to sell by auction and then send matter to Govt. direct for sanction instead of to Commr. - That would be a permissible departure and would make action of Govt. legal and would bring matter u/r. 190 - It follows that settlement was act of Deputy Commr. and fell within four corners of rules that vested first respondent with a good and legal title to lease - Act of cancellation and act of resettlement were his acts however much he may have acted under direction and orders of a third party - That at once vested HC with jurisdiction to entertain appeal against his actions u/r. 190 - Appeals dismissed.
