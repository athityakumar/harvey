Sidhakamal Nayan Ramanuj Das v Bira Naik and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 April 1953

Case Analysis

Bench

Vivian Bose, Mehr Chand Mahajan

Where Reported

1953 Indlaw SC 131; AIR 1954 SC 336; 1954 (20) CutLT 92

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Trusts & Associations

Summary: Land & Property - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 225 - Indian Trusts Act, 1882, s. 90 - First plaintiff was a tenant of suit lands - He mortgaged them to defendant - Rent payable by first plaintiff to landlord fell into arrears - A sale decree was obtained - Defendant paid up arrears and obtained possession of property - Suit out of which this appeal arises was for possession and, in alternative, for redemption of plaint properties - Whether defendant being a mortgagee in possession was bound to pay rent and so cannot take advantage of his own default to deprive his mortgagors of their property - Held, in absence of any special statutory provision to contrary is governed by s. 90, Trusts Act - Defendant is a mortgagee and apart from special statutes, only way in which a mortgage can be terminated as between parties to it is by act of parties themselves by merger or by an order of court - Therefore, when defendant entered upon possession, he was there as a mortgagee, and being a mortgagee, plaintiffs have a right to redeem unless there is either a contract between parties or a merger or a special statute to debar them - Appeal dismissed.
