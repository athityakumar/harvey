 Ebrahim Aboobaker and Another v Tekchand Dolwaniebrahim Aboobaker and Anotherv.Custodian-Gen

Supreme Court of India

10 April 1953

Case Analysis

Bench

Ghulam Hasan, M. Patanjali Sastri, Bijan Kumar Mukherjea, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati

Where Reported

1953 Indlaw SC 31; AIR 1953 SC 298; 1953 SCJ 413; [1953] 4 S.C.R. 691

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Land & Property

Keywords: Administration of Evacuee Property Act, 1950, Influx from Pakistan (Control) Act, 1949

Summary: Land & Property - Civil Procedure Code, 1908 - Influx from Pakistan (Control) Act, 1949 - Administration of Evacuee Property Act, 1950 - Indian Succession Act, 1925 - Presidency Towns Insolvency Act, 1909 - Provincial Insolvency Act, 1920 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Whether a person can be declared an evacuee after his death and whether properties which upon his death vest in his heirs under Mohammedan law can be declared evacuee properties? - Held, use of present tense 'leaves' or 'has left' in definition of evacuee and 'has' in definition of evacuee property is relied upon in support of contention that object of legislature in enacting these provisions was to confine their operation to a living person only - Enquiry u/s. 7 is a condition precedent to making of a declaration u/s. 8 and right of custodian to exercise dominion over property does not arise until declaration is made - There is no doubt that when property does so vest vesting takes effect retrospectively, but where man dies before any such declaration is made, doctrine of relation-back cannot be invoked so as to affect vesting of such property in legal heirs by operation of law - There is no provision in Act that after a man is dead, his property can be declared evacuee property - If such a provision had been made, then vesting contemplated in s. 8 of Act would have by its statutory force displaced vesting of property under Mohammedan law in heirs after death - Appeal allowed.
