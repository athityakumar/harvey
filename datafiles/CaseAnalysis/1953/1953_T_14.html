Hoosein Kasam Dada (India) Limited v State of Madhya Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

25 February 1953

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhi Ranjan Das, Mehr Chand Mahajan

Where Reported

1953 Indlaw SC 2; AIR 1953 SC 221; 1983 (13) E.L.T. 1277; 1953 SCJ 276; [1953] S.C.R. 987; 1953 (4) STC 114

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Keywords: Bengal Tenancy Act, Central Provinces and Berar Sales Tax Act 1947, Judiciary Act, 1903, Letters Patent, 1865

Summary: Sales Tax - Central Provinces & Berar Sales Tax Act, 1947, s. 22(1) - Judiciary Act, 1903 - Letters Patent, 1865 - Code of Civil Procedure , 1908 - Bengal Tenancy Act - Whether imposition of a restriction on appeal by amendment of section can affect assessee's right of appeal from a decision in proceedings which commenced prior to such amendment and which right of appeal was free from such restriction under section as it stood at time of commencement of proceedings? - Proviso to s. 22(1) as it stood prior to amendment that an aggrieved assessee had only to pay such amount of tax as he might admit to be due from him, whereas under proviso to s. 22(1) as amended appeal has to be accompanied by satisfactory proof of payment of tax in respect of which appeal had been preferred - Pre-existing right of appeal is not destroyed by amendment, if amendment is not made retrospective by express words or necessary intention - As old law continues to exist for purpose of supporting pre-existing right of appeal that old law must govern exercise and enforcement of that right of appeal and there can then be no question of amended provision preventing exercise of that right - Held, appellant's appeal should not have been rejected on ground that it was not accompanied by satisfactory proof of payment of assessed tax - As appellant did not admit that any amount was due by it, it was under section as it stood previously entitled to file its appeal without depositing any sum of money - Appeal allowed.
