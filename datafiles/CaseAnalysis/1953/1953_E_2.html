 Election Commission Of India v Saka Venkata Rao

Supreme Court of India

27 February 1953

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Patanjali Sastri, Bijan Kumar Mukherjea, Vivian Bose, Ghulam Hasan, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati

Where Reported

1953 Indlaw SC 38; AIR 1953 SC 210; 1953 (1) MLJ(SC) 702; 1953 SCJ 293; [1953] 4 S.C.R. 1144

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Keywords: Bombay Prohibition Act 1949

Summary: Election - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 226, 190, 191, 192, and 193- Representation of People Act, 1951 - (A) Whether High Court has jurisdiction to issue a writ of mandamus or prohibition u/art. 226 against Election Commission of India? - Held, No - HC is not competent to issue writ u/art. 226 to Election Commission of India - Writs issued by HC cannot run beyond territories subject to its jurisdiction - Person or authority to whom such writ is issued must be within those territories - (B) Whether art. 190 (3) and 192 (1) are applicable to disqualifications only after a member is elected? - Further held, yes - Arts. 190(3) and 192(1) are applicable only to disqualifications to which a member becomes subject after he is elected - Neither governor nor Election Commission of India has jurisdiction to enquire into respondent's disqualification which arose long before his election - Appeal allowed.
