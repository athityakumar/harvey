Sidheshwar Mukherjee v Bhubneshwar Prasad Narainsingh and Others

Supreme Court of India

05 October 1953

Case Analysis

Bench

Bijan Kumar Mukherjea, Mehr Chand Mahajan, B. Jagannadhadas

Where Reported

1953 Indlaw SC 14; AIR 1953 SC 487; 1953 (2) MLJ(SC) 789; 1953 SCJ 700; [1954] 1 S.C.R. 177

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Debt Laws

Summary: Socio-Economic - Family & Personal - Practice & Procedure - Hindu law - (A) Whether sons of judgment-debtor who was a junior member of a coparcenary is liable in law to discharge decretal debt due by their father? - Held, obligation of sons is not a personal obligation existing irrespective of receipt of any assets - It is a liability confined to assets received by him as his share of joint family property or to his interest in same - Obligation exists whether sons are major or minor and whether father is alive or dead - If debts have been contracted by father and they are not immoral or irreligious, interest of sons in coparcenary property can always be made liable for such debts - It is not necessary that father should be manager or karta of joint family or that family must be composed of father and his sons only and no other male member - No such limitation is deducible either from original texts or principles which have been engrafted upon doctrine by judicial decisions - Liability of his sons to pay such debt does not rest on principle of agency according to which junior members of a family are made to pay family debts - It is a special liability created on purely religious grounds - Entirely on relationship between father and son - Not dependent upon constitution of family either at time when debt was contracted or when obligation is sought to be enforced - Sons cannot be said to be necessary parties to money suit which is instituted by creditor against father on basis of a promissory note executed by him - If a decree is passed against father and sons jointly, latter would be personally liable - Decree can be executed against their separate or personal property as well - A money decree passed against father certainly creates a debt payable by him - Sons interest in coparcenary liable if debt not tainted with immorality - (1) Whether purchaser of property in execution sale of undivided share of debtor in joint family property would be entitled to possession and other rights? - Further held, purchaser did not acquire title to any defined share in property and was not entitled to joint possession from date of his purchase - He could work out his rights only by a suit for partition and his right to possession would date from period when a specific allotment was made in his favour - Purchaser's right to profits would date from date of partition - Appeal partly allowed.
