Deputy Commissioner, Hardoi v Rama Krishna Narain Andothers

Supreme Court of India

08 October 1953

Case Analysis

Bench

Mehr Chand Mahajan, Bijan Kumar Mukherjea, B. Jagannadhadas

Where Reported

1953 Indlaw SC 24; AIR 1953 SC 521; 1954 ALJ 106; 1954 BLJR 53; 1954 (1) ILR(All) 168; 1953 SCJ 664; [1954] 1 S.C.R. 506

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Debt Laws

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Uttar Pradesh Encumbered Estates Act, 1934, ss. 11, 11(1), 11(2), 11(4), 13, 14, 14(1), 18, 27, 3, 4, 45, 8, 14(7) - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 1 r. 1, O. 1 r. 3, O. 21 r. 63, s. 110, sch. 1 r. 1, sch. 1 r. 3, [O.1 r.1, O.1 r.3, O.1 r.10(2), O.1 r.10(2), O.21 r.63, O.1 r.10(2) - Indian Limitation Act, 1908, s. 5 - Decree - Execution - Jurisdiction - Appeal was on certificate granted by HC u/s. 110 CPC - Appellants submitted application u/s. 4 of the 1934 Act to sub- divisional officer praying that provisions of Act be applied to them - Application was eventually transferred to Trial court - Landlords submitted written statement u/s. 8 of the 1934 Act and stated that they had pro-prietary interest - Ward of Court of Wards, filed claim petition u/s. 11(2) of the 1934 Act and alleged that he was proprietor of few items of property - Claim was disallowed - Deputy Commissioner filed appeal to HC - Appellant made application for impleading them as respondents in appeal and prayed that he be given benefit of s. 5 of the 1908 Act - Application was rejected - Cross-objection filed by Bank with respect to costs was allowed - Appellant filed petition for leave to appeal to HC - HC without deciding whether appeal raised substantial question of law granted leave to appellant u/s. 110 CPC on ground that judgment of HC being one of variance, and value of subject-matter in dispute in Trial court as well as in appeal to HC being over Rs. 10, 000, case fulfilled requirements of that section - Hence, instant appeal - Whether appeal preferred by appellant to HC was imperfectly constituted as all creditors were not impleaded as parties to that appeal - Held, regarding impleading of parties should not be invoked and that matter should be viewed in more liberal way, regard always being had to fact that there was no collusion between debtor and claimant and that there were persons who were bona fide litigating in respect of title of claimant u/s. 11of the 1934 Act, and if there had been such bona fide fight which resulted in decree in appeal against that decree it was sufficient that those who took active part in proceedings u/s. 11 of the 1934 Act were impleaded - It was not necessary to implead each and every creditor who either did appear or put forward written statement u/s. 10 the 1934 Act or took no active part in proceedings under s. 11(2) of the 1934 Act - Appeal allowed.
