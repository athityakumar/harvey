 State of West Bengal v Subodh Gopal Bose and Others

Supreme Court of India

17 December 1953

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Patanjali Sastri, Mehr Chand Mahajan, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Ghulam Hasan, B. Jagannadhadas

Where Reported

1953 Indlaw SC 50; AIR 1954 SC 92; 1954 (1) MLJ(SC) 314; 1954 SCJ 127; [1954] 1 S.C.R. 587

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Preventive Detention Act, 1950, court of Wards Act, Bengal Land Revenue Sales Act, 1859, Bombay Prohibition Act 1949, Insurance (Amendment) Act, Railway Companies Emergency Powers Act, 1951, Australia Constitution Act, 1900 (ENG), Bengal Revenue Sales Act, 1859, Bengal Land Revenue Sales (West Bengal Amendment) Act, 1950

Summary: Land & Property - Bombay Prohibition Act, 1949 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Court of Wards Act - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Insurance (Amendment) Act - Preventive Detention Act, 1950 - Railway Companies Emergency Powers Act, 1951 - Australia Constitution Act, 1900 (ENG) - Bengal Revenue Sales Act, 1859 - Bengal Land Revenue Sales (West Bengal Amendment) Act, 1950, ss. 4 and 7 - Bengal Land Revenue Sales Act, 1859, s. 37 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 31 (1) and (2) - Court Fees Act, 1870 - Govt. of India Act, 1935 - Industries (Development and Regulation) Act, 1951 - Insurance Act, 1938 - Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - Respondent acquired u/s 37 of Bengal Revenue Sales Act, 1859 right to avoid and annul all under-tenures and forthwith to eject all under tenants - An amending Act substituted by s. 4, new s. 37 in place of original s. 37, and it provided by s.7 that all pending suits, appeals and other proceedings which had not already resulted in delivery of possession shall abate - Whether s. 7 r/w s.4 of amending Act infringes fundamental right of respondent u/art. 31? - Held, matter has to be looked at from point of view of art. 31 - A comparison of scope and effect of old s. 37 which is substituted in its place by s. 4 of amending Act and which s. 7 shows to be clearly retrospective, discloses that, although right of a purchaser to annual under-tenures and evict under-tenants is curtailed by new s. 37 by enlarging scope of exceptions in old section, it entitles purchaser, as a countervailing advantage, to enhance rent payable by tenure holders and tenants newly brought within exception - Purchaser is left free in other respects to continue in enjoyment of property as before - This amendment is in line with traditional tenancy legislation in this country affording relief to tenants whenever tenancy laws were found, due to changing conditions, to operate harshly on tenants - Hence, it is difficult to hold that abridgement sought to be effected retrospectively of rights of a purchaser at a revenue sale is so substantial as to amount to a deprivation of his property within meaning of art. 31(1) and (2) - Appeal allowed.
