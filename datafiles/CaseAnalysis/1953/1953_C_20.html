Commissioner of Income Tax, West Bengal v H. Hirjee

Supreme Court of India

17 April 1953

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Patanjali Sastri, Bijan Kumar Mukherjea, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Ghulam Hasan

Where Reported

1953 Indlaw SC 96; AIR 1953 SC 324; [1953] 23 ITR 427; 1953 (1) MLJ(SC) 849; 1953 SCJ 445; [1953] 4 S.C.R. 714

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Legal Expenses, Hoarding and Profiteering Ordinance, 1943

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Hoarding and Profiteering Ordinance,1943,s.13 - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, ss.10(2)(xv),66-A,66-A(2) - Selling agents - Search & Seizure - Profits - Deduction - - Respondent carries on business as selling agents of company and prosecuted u/s13 of 1943 Ordinance, on a charge of selling goods at prices higher than were reasonable in contravention of provisions of s.6 - Respondent's business premises were searched and a part of his stock was seized - Respondent defended, spending a sum of Rs.10,895 and prosecution ended in an acquittal - In assessment respondent claimed deduction of Rs.10,895 from profits of his business u/s.10(2)(xv) of Act - Income-tax Officer disallowed the claim but Appellate Assistant Commissioner allowed it and his decision confirmed by Income-tax Appellate Tribunal - Hence instant appeal - Whether amount spent by respondent as prosecution expenses could be allowed for deduction in his total assessment - Held, income-tax assessments have to be-made for every year and cannot be assumed up until final result of a legal proceeding, which may pass through several courts, was announced - Deductibility of such expenses u/s.10(2) (xv)of Act, must depend on nature and purpose of legal proceeding in relation to business whose profits are under computation and cannot be affected by final outcome of that proceeding - No question could arise as to the primary or secondary purpose for which the legal expenses could be said to have been incurred as in the case of a criminal prosecution where the defence cannot easily be dissociated from the purpose of saving the accused person from a possible conviction and imposition of the prescribed penalty - Appeal allowed.
