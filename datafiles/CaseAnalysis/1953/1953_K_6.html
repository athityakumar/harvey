Kalyanpur Lime Works Limited v State of Bihar and Another

Supreme Court of India

14 December 1953

Case Analysis

Bench

Ghulam Hasan, Bijan Kumar Mukherjea

Where Reported

1953 Indlaw SC 119; AIR 1954 SC 165; 1954 (1) MLJ(SC) 305; [1954] S.C.R. 958

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Contract and Commercial - Indian Contract Act, 1872, ss. 20 and 21 - Govt. of India Act, s. 30 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. VI, r.8, O. VII, r. 2, O. VIII - Specific Relief Act, 1877, ss. 15 and 18 - Whether contract is void and unenforceable u/s.20 of Act, 1872 because both parties were under a mistake of fact as regards title of Govt. to subject-matter of proposed lease? - Held, mistake, if any, was with regard to effect of law of registration upon validity of assignment deed - At most, such mistake would be a mistake of law and u/s. 21 of Act, 1872 - Contract would not be void on that ground - No misapprehension of facts found as both parties aware of each others status - Appeal allowed.
