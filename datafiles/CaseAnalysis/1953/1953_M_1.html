 Union of India v Madan Gopal Kabra

Supreme Court of India

16 December 1953

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Patanjali Sastri, Mehr Chand Mahajan, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Ghulam Hasan, B. Jagannadhadas

Where Reported

1953 Indlaw SC 49; AIR 1954 SC 158; [1954] 25 ITR 58; 1954 SCJ 110; [1954] S.C.R. 541

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: General Clauses Act, Indian Independence Act, 1947, misa, General Clauses Act, , 1897

Summary: Constitution - Finance Act, 1950 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Finance Act, 1950 - Maintainance of Internal Security Act - General Clauses Act, 1897 - Government of India Act, 1935 - Indian Independence Act, 1947 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 226, 245 and 246(1) - Finance Act, 1950, ss. 2 and 13 - (A) Whether the High Court u/art. 226 of the Constitution of India, 1950 that the UOI, should not levy income-tax on the income the other party in Rajasthan (excluding the area of the former covenanting State of Bundi) prior to 01-04-1950? - Arts. 245 and 246(1) r/w entry No. 82 of List 1, conferred to make laws for the whole or any part of the territory of India and exclusive power to make laws with respect to taxes on income other than agricultural income - (B) Whether no income-tax in Rajasthan was leviable on the income of the year 1949-50 under the Finance Act, 1950? - Held, three stages in connection with the imposition of a tax - The first is the declaration of liability, the second is the assessment and the third is the collection - S. 13, Finance Act, 1950 saves any law relating to income-tax or supertax or tax on profits of business, such law shall cease to have effect except for the purposes of the levy, assessment and collection of income- tax and super-tax - (C) Whether the Parliament is competent to make a law imposing a tax on the income of any year prior to the commencement of the Constitution, 1950? - Held, it is competent for Parliament to make a law imposing a tax on the income of any year prior to the commencement of the Constitution, 1950 - Held, amendment of s. 2, cl.(14-A) of the Indian Independence Act, 1947, by the Finance Act, 1950, to authorise the levy of tax on income accruing in the territory of Rajasthan in the year 1949-50 is within the competence of Parliament and therefore valid - Appeal allowed.
