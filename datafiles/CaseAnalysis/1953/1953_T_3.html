Seksaria Cotton Mills Limited v State of Bombay

Supreme Court of India

30 March 1953

Case Analysis

Bench

Vivian Bose, Mehr Chand Mahajan, B. Jagannadhadas

Where Reported

1953 Indlaw SC 8; AIR 1953 SC 278; 1953 CRLJ 1116; 1953 SCJ 355; [1953] 4 S.C.R. 825

Case Digest

Subject: Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Del Credere

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Essential Supplies (Temporary Powers) Act, 1946, ss. 7 and 9-A - Govt. of India Notification D/-02-02-1946 required every manufacturer to submit 'true and accurate information relating to his undertakings' to Textile Commr. C.S.T. Section at Bombay - In compliance with this Order first appellant submitted a return, signed by third appellant - Two returns filed of same date but gave varied information - (A) Whether entry in Column 3 is to be confined to quotaholders or their agents? - Held, entry in Column 3 is not intended to be confined to quotaholders or their agents but means what it says, namely, person to whom Physical delivery of goods has been made whoever he may be - (B) Whether Dwarkadas Khetan,the del credere agent is person to him goods were delivered? - In a penal statute of this kind it is court's duty to interpret words of ambiguous meaning in a broad and liberal sense so that they will not become traps for honest, unlearned (in law) and unwary men - Further held, in given circumstances Dwarkadas Khetan can be described, as person to whom goods were actually delivered - It follows conviction on this count cannot stand - Appeal allowed.
