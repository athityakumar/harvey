Keshav Mills Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax, Bombay

Supreme Court of India

30 January 1953

Case Analysis

Bench

Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, Mehr Chand Mahajan, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Vivian Bose

Where Reported

1953 Indlaw SC 110; AIR 1953 SC 187; [1953] 23 ITR 230; 1953 SCJ 243; [1953] 4 S.C.R. 950

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act,1922,ss.10,10(2)(xi),13, 16(1)(c),18(4),19(2),42 - Goods - Guaranteed brokers - Commission - Mercantile system - Profits - Appellant/company manufactures textile goods and after goods were manufactured they are sold by company ex-mills - Company employs 'J' as guaranteed brokers - Firm guarantees sale price of goods sold by company ex-mills to purchasers and receives commission as consideration for guarantee and work which it does for company - Company was a non-resident and its accounts maintained according to mercantile system - In the assessment year total sales of goods by company amounted to Rs. 29,68,808 - Income-tax Officer brought to tax the profits derived by company on the basis that sale proceeds received in British India the profits were received in British India - Appellate Assistant Commissioner on appeal assumed that profits were exempt from British Indian tax while item B were rightly taxed - Appellate Tribunal found in regard to item B that merchants in British India were not absolved either in law or in fact from their responsibility to pay to company its dues by virtue of debit entries in the account of 'J' - Hence instant appeal - Whether the profits accrued by assessee inside British India - Held, if whole of transactions occurred outside British India and buyers or their agents went to Petlad and received the goods there and paid 'J' Company outside British India, then it was clear that profits and gains did not accrue or arise in British India - But if 'J' Company or their agents were paid in British India, profits and gains, arose there in the same way as in 4 lakhs case - Agree that Rs. 4,40,878 was part of assessee's income in British India and so also in the other case, provided the payments were made in British India - S.42of Act, attracted because at the very least there would be a 'business connection', provided of course the payments were made in the taxable territories - Order accordingly.
