 State of West Bengal v Mrs. Bela Banerjee and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 December 1953

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Patanjali Sastri, Mehr Chand Mahajan, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Ghulam Hasan, B. Jagannadhadas

Where Reported

1953 Indlaw SC 44; AIR 1954 SC 170; 1954 (1) MLJ(SC) 162; 1954 SCJ 95; [1954] 1 S.C.R. 558

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Impugned Act, Bombay Prohibition Act 1949, West Bengal) Land Development and Planning Act 1948 (West Bengal) Act XXI of 1948)

Summary: Constitution - Municipalities & Local Govovernments - West Bengal Land Development and planning Act, 1948, s. 8 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 31 (5) and (6) - Constitutional validity of West Bengal Land Development and Planning Act, 1948 - (A) Whether provision s. 8 of West Bengal Land Development and Planning Act is unconstitutional? - Held, provision in s. 8 of West Bengal Land Development and Planning Act relating to conclusiveness of declaration of Govt. as to nature of purpose of acquisition held is unconstitutional as it is not saved by art. 31(5) of Constitution - (A1)Whether principle of measure of compensation takes into account all elements which make up true value of property? - Further held, measure of compensation is based on amount which owner has been deprived of after property has been appropriated - Basic requirement is to fully indemnify expropriated owner - (B) Whether fixing of an anterior date for ascertainment of value of land, a violation of Constitutional requirement? - Any principle for determining compensation which denies to owner any increment in value cannot result in ascertainment of true equivalent of land appropriated - Further held, fixing of an anterior date, which might have no relation to value of land when it is acquired, may be, many years later when it is acquired, may be many years later cannot but be regarded as arbitrary - Appeal dismissed.
