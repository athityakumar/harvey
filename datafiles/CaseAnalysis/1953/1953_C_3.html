Central National Bank Limited v United Industrial Bank Limited

Supreme Court of India

26 November 1953

Case Analysis

Bench

Bijan Kumar Mukherjea, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, B. Jagannadhadas

Where Reported

1953 Indlaw SC 47; AIR 1954 SC 181; 1954 (1) MLJ(SC) 87; 1954 SCJ 54; [1954] 1 S.C.R. 391

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Sales Tax; Water; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Contract Act, Fraudulent, Indian Contract Act, misa, English Sale of Goods Act (ENG), Factors Act

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Indian Contract Act, 1872, s. 13 - Indian Sale of Goods Act, 1930, s. 30(2) - A claim for recovery of possession of 800 shares and for, damages suffered by plaintiff by wrongful denial of its title by the defendant bank - Whether it could be held that Mukherjee got possession of the shares with the consent of the defendant's agent? - Held, the defendant's agent never consented to Mukherjee's obtaining possession of the shares as buyer - There was no intention to give delivery at all - Held, taking the evidence as a whole, the decision of the High Court is correct and that on the facts and circumstances of this case it cannot be held that Mukherjee got possession of the shares with the consent of Paul - Appeal dismissed.
