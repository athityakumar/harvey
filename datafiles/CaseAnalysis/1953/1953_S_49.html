 Rao Shiv Bahadur Singh and Another v State of Vindhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

22 May 1953

Case Analysis

Bench

B. Jagannadhadas, M. Patanjali Sastri, Bijan Kumar Mukherjea, Vivian Bose, Ghulam Hasan

Where Reported

1953 Indlaw SC 92; AIR 1953 SC 394; 1953 CRLJ 1480; 1953 SCJ 563; [1953] 4 S.C.R. 1188

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Municipalities & Local Governments; Parliament & Legislature

Keywords: Ex Post Facto Law

Summary: Criminal - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14 and 20 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120-B, 161, 465 and 466 - Vindhya Pradesh Criminal Law Amendment (Special Court) Ordinance No. V of 1949 s. 3 - Appellants charged for criminal conspiracy and for taking of illegal gratification by public servant for doing official act and for commission of forgery in connection therewith - Appellants convicted for said offence - Whether conviction of appellants is sustainable? - Acts committed in Rewa State in 1940 before Vindhya Pradesh Ordinance - Rulers of native States had prior to 1947, authority to pass extra-territorial laws relating to offences committed by their own subjects and vesting in their own courts power to try them - Conviction of appellants in respect of all offences of which they are charged including extra-territorial offence said to have been committed by first appellant at New Delhi is not open to objection u/art. 20 - Conviction under ex post facto law - Held, appeal to Judicial Commr. from acquittal be Special Judge was competent - Trial of appellants under Vindhya Pradesh Criminal Law Amendment (Special Courts) Ordinance No. V of 1949 is not open to objection u/art. 14 of Constitution, 1950 - Criminal law relating to offences charged against appellants at time of their commission was substantially same as that which obtained at time of convictions and sentences by the Appellate Court - Appeal is accordingly directed to be posted for consideration whether it is to be heard on merits.
