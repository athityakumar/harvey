Jagadguru Gurushiddaswami v Dakshina Maharashtra Digambarjain Sabha

Supreme Court of India

14 October 1953

Case Analysis

Bench

Bijan Kumar Mukherjea, Mehr Chand Mahajan, B. Jagannadhadas

Where Reported

1953 Indlaw SC 32; AIR 1953 SC 514; 1954 (56) BomLR 783; 1953 (2) MLJ(SC) 819; 1953 SCJ 730; [1954] 1 S.C.R. 235

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Attorney-General

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Civil Procedure - Indian Limitation Act, 1908, art. 134B, s. 10 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 47 - Whether property of a religious institution alienated by its head for 'adequate consideration' be recovered after limitation u/art. 134B has expired by virtue of exception contained in s. 10 of 1908 Act ? - In order that a suit may have benefit of s. 10 of 1908 Act, it must be a suit against a person in whom property has become vested in trust for any specific purpose not being assigned for valuable consideration, expression 'valuable consideration' has a clear connotation in law and is not synonymous with 'adequate consideration' - Held, a suit of this description is governed by art. 134-B of Limitation Act, 1908 - Cause of action arose in 1920 and suit was brought more than 12 years after that and hence, was time-barred - Appeal dismissed.
