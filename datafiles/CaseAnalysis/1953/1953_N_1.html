 Nain Sukh Das and Another v State of Uttar Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

22 May 1953

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Patanjali Sastri, Bijan Kumar Mukherjea, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Ghulam Hasan, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati

Where Reported

1953 Indlaw SC 19; AIR 1953 SC 384; 1953 (2) MLJ(SC) 257; 1953 SCJ 546; [1953] 4 S.C.R. 1184

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Election - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 32 and 15(1) - Petitioners allege that bye-election to municipal board at which respondents were elected were held on communal lines - Violative of fundamental rights u/art. 15 (1) - Whether petitioners are entitled to relief u/art. 32 ? - Held, No - Any right which petitioners have as rate payers in municipality to insist that board should be legally constituted and those members who are not properly elected or nominated, should not be permitted to take part in proceedings of board is outside purview of art. 32 - Such right, even if it exists, is not a part of fundamental rights conferred by Part III of constitution - Petition dismissed.
