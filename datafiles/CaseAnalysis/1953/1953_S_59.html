 Syed Qasim Razvi v State of Hyderabad and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 January 1953

Case Analysis

Bench

Bijan Kumar Mukherjea, Ghulam Hasan, M. Patanjali Sastri, N. Chandrasekhara Aiyar, Vivian Bose

Where Reported

1953 Indlaw SC 34; AIR 1953 SC 156; 1953 CRLJ 862; 1953 SCJ 151; [1953] 4 S.C.R. 589

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Keywords: Committal Proceedings, Code of Criminal Procedure 1898, Bombay Public Security Measures Act, 1947, India (Provisional Constitution) Order, 1947, Hyderabad Criminal Procedure Code, Hyderabad Penal Code, West Bengal Special Courts Act

Summary: Constitution - Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Essential Supplies (Temporary Powers) Act, 1946 - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 132 and 134 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1898, s. 342 - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Hyderabad Criminal Procedure Code, s.273 - Hyderabad Penal Code, ss. 66, 123, 124, 330 and 177 - West Bengal Special Courts Act, 1950 - Bombay Public Security Measures Act, 1947 - India (Provisional Constitution) Order, 1947 - Whether procedure for trial of criminal offences laid down in Special Tribunal Regulation is discriminatory in its character and offends against provision of art. 14 and 21 of Constitution? - What exactly is legal position where admittedly a considerable portion of trial was gone through prior to coming into force of Constitution and that portion is immune from challenge on ground of discrimination, as rights guaranteed under Constitution are not retrospective in their operation - Procedure laid down in the, Special Tribunal Regulation for trial of offences departs, in material particulars, from that under ordinary law and these differences do abridge rights of accused and deprive them of benefits to which otherwise they would have been entitled under general law - If procedure subsequently followed was also discriminatory - Continuation of such procedure constituted a breach of fundamental right guaranteed by art. 14 and being inconsistent with provisions of that article special procedure became void under art. 13 - Question as to what procedure was actually followed after that date may be relevant for purpose of determining whether trial could be regarded as vitiated on ground of infringement of equal protection rule - Constitution has no retrospective operation to invalidate that part of proceedings that has already been gone through but it does not permit special procedure to stand in way of exercise or enjoyment of post-constitutional rights and must strike down discriminatory procedure if it is sought to be adopted after Constitution came into operation - Held, Regulation is void under art. 13, r/w arts. 14 and 21 of Constitution, and conviction and sentence of petitioner should be set aside - Petitions dismissed.
