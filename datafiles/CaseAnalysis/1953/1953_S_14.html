Nalinakhya Bysack v Shyam Sunder Haldar and Others

Supreme Court of India

29 January 1953

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhi Ranjan Das, Mehr Chand Mahajan, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati

Where Reported

1953 Indlaw SC 134; AIR 1953 SC 148; 1953 SCJ 201; [1953] S.C.R. 533

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - Presidency Small Causes Courts Act, 1882, Chap VII - West Bengal Premises Rent Control (Temporary Provisions) Act,1950, s. 18(1) -West Bengal Premises Rent Control Act, 1948, s. 11 - (A) Whether marginal note can at all be referred to in construing provisions of a section of an Act? - Held, marginal note cannot control meaning of body of section if language employed therein is clear and unambiguous - (B) Whether s. 18 (1) of Act of 1950 applies to an order for possession made u/s. 43 of Presidency Small Cause Court Act, 1882? - Held, s. 18 (1) of Act of 1950, does not refer to 'decree' simpliciter but to 'any decree for recovery of possession of any premises on ground of default in payment of arrears of rent under provisions of Act of 1948 - S. 11 of Act of 1948 refers to 'order or decree for recovery of possession of any premises' - Non-Obstante clause of s. 11 of Act, 1882 clearly indicates that order for recovery of possession refers to orders passed u/s 43 of Act of 1882 - S. 11 speaks of both 'order' for recovery of possession and 'decree' for recovery of possession - 'order' and 'decree' in s. 11 connote two different things - Decree for possession on ground of non-payment of rent under Act of 1948 is treated distinctly from an order for possession on ground of non-payment of rent under said Act - Chapter VII of Presidency Small Cause Courts Act and Small Cause Court can,only pass a decree for possession in a suit which is saved by proviso to s. 12(1) and with regard to which a special jurisdiction is conferred on that Court by virtue of s. 16 of said Act - Order set aside - Appeal allowed.
