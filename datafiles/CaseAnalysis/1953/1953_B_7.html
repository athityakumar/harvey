 Biswambhar Singh v State of Orissa and Anothe

Supreme Court of India

18 December 1953

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhi Ranjan Das, M. Patanjali Sastri, Mehr Chand Mahajan, Vivian Bose, Ghulam Hasan

Where Reported

1953 Indlaw SC 53; AIR 1954 SC 139; 1954 SCJ 219; [1954] 1 S.C.R. 842

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property

Keywords: Intermediary, Constitutionality, Indian Independence Act, 1947, Jagirdar, Zamindar, Government of India Act

Summary: Constitution - Orissa Estates Abolition Act, 1952 - Indian Forest Act, 1927 - Government of India Act - Constitution of India, 1950 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872 - Indian Independence Act, 1947 - States' Merger (Governor's Province) Order, 1949 - S. 2(h) of the Orissa Estates Abolition Act, 1952 - Arts. 14, 31 and 226 of the Constitution of India, 1950 - (A) Whether state Govt. has power to issue a notification in respect of any property unless such property is an estate as defined in s. 2(g)? - Held, u/s. 2(g) the state Govt. had no jurisdiction or authority to issue any notification u/s. 3 with respect to their properties - (B) Whether the appellant are intemediaries within s. 2(h) of the Act? - Held, appellant is an intermediaries as defined in s. 2(h) of the Act and the estate is an estate within the meaning of s. 2(g) and consequently there is no escape from the conclusion that the state Govt. had ample jurisdiction or authority to issue a notification u/s. 3 of the Act - All three appeals allowed - High Court decision set aside - Not to take possession of the several estates of the three petitioners under the Act.
