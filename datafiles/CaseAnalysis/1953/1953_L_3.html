Lala Durga Prasad and Another v Lala Deep Chand and Others

Supreme Court of India

18 November 1953

Case Analysis

Bench

Vivian Bose, Bijan Kumar Mukherjea, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati

Where Reported

1953 Indlaw SC 82; AIR 1954 SC 75; 1954 ALJ 73; 1954 (1) MLJ(SC) 60; 1954 SCJ 23; [1954] 1 S.C.R. 360

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Trusts & Associations

Summary: Land & Property - Indian Trusts Act, 1882 - Specific Relief Act, 1877 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882 - This appeal arises out of a vendee's suit for specific performance of a contract of sale - Vendor is first defendant - Plaintiff is vendee and second and third defendants, who appeal, are subsequent purchasers - Plaintiff's case is that Nawab agreed to sell plaint property to him on 07-02-1942 for Rs. 62,000 and accepted Rs. 10,000 as earnest money same day - Later, on 04-01-942, Nawab sold same property to appellants for a sum of Rs. 72,000 - Plaintiff states that appellants had notice of his prior agreement - Appellant's case that plaintiff's so called agreement of 07-02-1942, was not a concluded one as parties never reached finality - Whether parties reached finality on 07-02-1942? - Held, question here is one of fact - Plaintiff founds on a contract which defendants deny - He must therefore, prove it - Initial burden is on him - He relies on two facts in plaint - First, is that he paid a sum of Rs. 10,000 to Nawab, by two cheques - Nawab accepted this money and cashed cheques and money went into his own account in his bank - Second, is that Nawab gave plaintiff a receipt on that date for this money - Motif of the theme is that there was a concluded contract which fell to ground because certain conditions, presumably conditions precedent, were not fulfilled - In any case, this does not explain receipt, and circumstances in which it came to be given apart from explanation which is contained in plea of fraud - But that, imports important averment that there was a concluded contract which bound both sides until it was set aside at option of defrauded party - There was a completed contract on 07-02-1942, which plaintiff is entitled to have specifically performed - Except for 4 modifications, decree of High Court stands - Appeal dismissed.
