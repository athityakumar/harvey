Atherton West and Company Limited v Suti Mill Mazdoor Union and Others

Supreme Court of India

16 March 1953

Case Analysis

Bench

Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, Mehr Chand Mahajan

Where Reported

1953 Indlaw SC 5; AIR 1953 SC 241; 1953 (2) LLJ 321; 1953 SCJ 333; [1953] 4 S.C.R. 780

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Labour Appellate Tribunal, misa, United Provinces Industrial Disputes Act 1947 (UP 28 of 1947) (ENG)

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Uttar PradeshEncumbered Estates Act (XXV of 1934), ss. 4, 11, 6, 144 - United Provinces Industrial Disputes Act 1947 (UP 28 of 1947) (ENG), ss. 3 (d), 3 (g), 8 - Settlement- Decretal amount- Maintainability - TRS applied u/s. 4 of the 1934 Act for administration of his estate to liquidate his debts - Board of Revenue quashed proceedings under the 1934 Act - No order for stay of execution proceedings was obtained by RS from Chief Court or PC - Civil judge to whom ordered judgment-debtor to execute sale deed and on his making default civil judge executed sale deed on behalf of judgment-debtor in, favour of RD - U.P. Encumbered Estates Amendment Act (XI of 1939) came into operation after this sale which allowed applicants to amend applications, proceedings in respect of which had been quashed previously - RS applied for amendment of his application which was allowed by sub-divisional officer and forwarded amended application to special judge- Special judge passed order to effect that proceedings would start afresh - RS went up in revision to Chief Court against this order contending that proceedings should not be deemed as fresh proceedings - Chief Court dismissed revision- On notification issued u/s. 11 of the 1934 Act, HKD filed objection u/s. 11of the 1934 Act - Objection was contested by debtor - Special judge declared RD to be proprietor of all eight villages included in sale deed - Against decree of special judge appeal was filed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order passed by HC was justified - Held, decree holder in instant case had derived no advantage to which he was not entitled and judgment-debtor had lost nothing - In either event he had to discharge and satisfy decretal debt due from him whether under first decree or under second and that debt could only be discharged by sale of villages selected by decreeholder - As held in DayalSardar v. TariDeshi 1931 Indlaw CAL 29 judgment debtor was not entitled to recover properties except upon showing that sale was in substance and truth consequence of error in reversed decree - Sale being inevitable under amended decree 'judgment-debtor was clearly not entitled to restitution - It was further held in, Gansu Ram v. ParvatiKuer 1940 Indlaw PAT 210, that where judgment-debtor could not have paid even reduced decretal amount and sum realized at sale was less than decretal amount situation could not have been altered in any way had decree been modified before, instead of after sale, and judgment-debtor could not invoke provisions of s.144 of the 1934 Act, except by showing that sale was in substance and truth consequence of error in original decree - Observations made in this case had apposite application to facts and circumstances of instant case - Appeals dismissed.
