 Habeeb Mohamed v State of Hyderabad

Supreme Court of India

30 March 1953

Case Analysis

Bench

Bijan Kumar Mukherjea, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, M. Patanjali Sastri, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Ghulam Hasan

Where Reported

1953 Indlaw SC 7; AIR 1953 SC 287; 1953 CRLJ 1158; 1953 SCJ 361; [1953] 4 S.C.R. 661

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Constitution - Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1898 - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Hyderabad IPC - Hyderabad CrPC - Constitution, 1950, art. 13 (1) - Special Tribunal Regulation - Hyderabad Regulation X of 1359 F - Procedure for trial laid down in Regulation X of 1359 F became void after 26th of January, 1950, by reason of its being in conflict with equal protection clause embodied in art. 14 of Constitution - Held, in determining validity of legislation two principles would have to be borne in mind - Firstly, Constitution has no retrospective effect and even if law is in any sense discriminatory, it must be held to be valid for all past transactions and for enforcement of rights and liabilities accrued before coming into force of Constitution - Secondly, art. 13 (1) of Constitution does not necessarily make whole statute invalid even after advent of Constitution - It invalidates only those provisions which are inconsistent with fundamental rights guaranteed under Part III of Constitution and statute becomes void only to extent of such inconsistency but otherwise remains valid and operative - Further held, question whether this right is a substantive right which vests in petitioner or one relating to a more matter of procedure will have to be considered and decided when appropriate stage arrives - Petition dismissed.
