Gaya Electric Supply Company, Limited v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

03 February 1953

Case Analysis

Bench

Mehr Chand Mahajan, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Ghulam Hasan

Where Reported

1953 Indlaw SC 76; AIR 1953 SC 182; 1953 BLJR 308; 1953 (32) ILR(Pat) 364; 1953 (1) MLJ(SC) 605; 1953 SCJ 217; [1953] 4 S.C.R. 572

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration Act, 1940, s. 34 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 80 - Indian Electricity Act, 1910 - Whether a dispute falls within an arbitration clause in a contract or not? - Whether a dispute falls within an arbitration clause in a contract must depend on (i) what is dispute, and (ii) what disputes arbitration clause covers - If arbitration agreement is broad and comprehensive and embraces any dispute between parties 'in respect of' agreement, or in respect of any provision in agreement, or in respect of anything arising out of it and one of parties seeks to avoid contract, dispute is referable to arbitration if avoidance of contract arises out of terms of contract itself - Held, where, however, party seeks to avoid contract for reasons dehors it, arbitration clause cannot be restored - Appeal allowed.
