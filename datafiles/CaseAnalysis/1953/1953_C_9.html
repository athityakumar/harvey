 Sohan Pathak And Sons v Commissioner Of Income-Tax, U.P.

Supreme Court of India

23 September 1953

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Patanjali Sastri, Bijan Kumar Mukherjea, Vivian Bose, Ghulam Hasan, B. Jagannadhadas

Where Reported

1953 Indlaw SC 26; AIR 1953 SC 456; [1953] 24 ITR 395; 1953 SCJ 630; [1954] 1 S.C.R. 158

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 25(3), 25(4) - Money-lending - Equal shares - Assessment - Partial partition - Partnership - Transaction - Appellants constitute Hindu undivided family - Family carried on business in money-lending and Banaras brocade - In assessment relating to chargeable accounting period ending dt. 8-10-1943 - Appellants alleged that there was partial partition among members of family dt.16-7-1943 and claimed that family as such ceased to carry on business in Banaras brocade, though they continued to remain joint in status and that profits derived by two partnerships could not be assessed as profits of the original joint family business - Income tax Officer treated old business as discontinued by family after partial partition and granted relief u/s. 25(3) of Act, in assessment to income-tax of appellants as Hindu undivided family - Excess Profits Tax Officer rejected that main purpose of partial partition and creation of two partnerships was to avoid or reduce the liability of appellants to excess profits tax, and he made adjustments u/s.10-A of Act, by adding to profits made by appellants as joint Hindu family till the date of partition the profits made by two firms during chargeable accounting periods - Whether transfer was effected for purpose of avoiding reduction of liability to excess profits - Held, partial partition as well as the subsequent formation of partnerships found to be transactions the main purpose of which was the avoidance or reduction of liability to excess profits tax, officer had authority to assess appellants old family business in Banaras brocade on the basis of its continued existence during relevant chargeable accounting periods - Assessee continued to carry on his business in the same place as before such transfer, even if it was found that transfer was effected for the main purpose of avoiding or reducing his liability to excess profits - Old joint family business in Banaras brocade was wound up and was no longer carried on by joint family as such during the relevant chargeable accounting periods, the same business could not legally be treated as having continued unbroken in respect of such periods for purpose of s. 10-A r/w ss.4 and 5 of 1940 Act - Appeals allowed.
