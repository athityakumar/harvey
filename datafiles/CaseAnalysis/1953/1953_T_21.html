Shibban Lal Saksena v State of Uttar Pradeshand Others

Supreme Court of India

03 December 1953

Case Analysis

Bench

Bijan Kumar Mukherjea, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati

Where Reported

1953 Indlaw SC 42; AIR 1954 SC 179; 1954 CRLJ 456; 1954 (1) MLJ(SC) 143; 1954 SCJ 73; [1954] 1 S.C.R. 418

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Socio-Economic

Keywords: Preventive Detention Act, 1950

Summary: Criminal - Preventive Detention Act, 1950, ss. 3, 7 and 8 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 22 and 32 - Habeas Corpus - Petitioner made speeches instigating cane growers not to supply sugarcane and thereby, causing defiance of lawful orders of Govt. - Govt. revoked detention of petitioner but still kept in detention - Held, what Govt. has done in this case is to confirm detention order and at same time to revoke it under one of sub-clause of s. 3 (1) (a) of Act - This is not what section contemplates - Govt. could either confirm order of detention made u/s. 3 or revoke it completely and there is nothing in law which prevents Govt. from making a fresh order of detention if it so chooses - Order made u/s. 3 (1) (a) is bad in law and detention of petitioner is consequently illegal - Petition allowed.
