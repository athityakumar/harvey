Rizwan-Ul-Hasan and Another v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

05 February 1953

Case Analysis

Bench

Mehr Chand Mahajan, Sudhi Ranjan Das

Where Reported

1953 Indlaw SC 39; AIR 1953 SC 185; 1953 CRLJ 911; 1953 SCJ 255; [1953] 4 S.C.R. 581

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Contempt of Courts Act, 1926

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code Of Criminal Procedure, 1898, s. 145 - Contempt of Courts Act, 1926 - Case u/s. 145, CrPC, 1898 was fictitious - Whether superior officers of Sub-Divisional Magistrate guilty of contempt of court? - Held, officers who have to transmit communications to courts of justice should be vigilant and careful to see that nothing is transmitted which can have any effect, even remotely on, merits of a case - Jurisdiction in contempt is not to be invoked unless there is real prejudice which can be regarded as a substantial interference with due course of justice and that purpose of court's action is a practical purpose and it is reasonably clear on authorities that court will not exercise its jurisdiction upon a mere question of propriety - Appeal allowed.
