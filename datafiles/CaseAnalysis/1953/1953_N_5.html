National Sewing Thread Company Limited v James Chadwick and Bros. Limited (J. and P. Coats Limited, Assignee) Registrar of Trade Marks - Intervener

Supreme Court of India

07 May 1953

Case Analysis

Bench

Mehr Chand Mahajan, Vivian Bose, B. Jagannadhadas

Where Reported

1953 Indlaw SC 94; AIR 1953 SC 357; 1954 (56) BomLR 21; 1953 (2) MLJ(SC) 215; 22 PTC 475; 1953 SCJ 509; [1953] 4 S.C.R. 1028

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Court Of Record

Summary: Intellectual Property - Constitution - Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 109 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Indian Companies Act, 1913 - Trade Marks Act, 1940, ss. 15, 76 and 77, r. 30 - Government of India Act, 1915, ss. 106 and 108 - Government of India Act, 1935 - Indian High Courts Act, 1861 - General Clauses Act, 1897 - Charter Act, 1861, s. 9 - Whether judgment of High Court was subject to appeal u/cl. 15 of Letters Patent of Bombay High Court? - Court held, s. 76 of Trade Marks Act confers a right of appeal to HC therefore HC being seized as such of appellate jurisdiction conferred by s. 76 it has to exercise that jurisdiction in the same manner as it exercises its other appellate jurisdiction and when such jurisdiction is exercised by a single Judge, his judgment becomes subject to appeal u/cl. 15 of Letters Patent - Whether Mr. Justice Shah was right in interfering with the discretion exercised by the Registrar in refusing registration of the appellants' mark? - Held, what has been named by plaintiffs as a vulture is really an eagle seated in a different posture, that being so, HC was right that Mr. Justice Shah was in error in interfering with discretion possessed and exercised by Registrar - Appeal dismissed.
