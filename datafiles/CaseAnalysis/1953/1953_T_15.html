 Poppatlal Shah v State of Madras and Others

Supreme Court of India

30 March 1953

Case Analysis

Bench

Bijan Kumar Mukherjea, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, M. Patanjali Sastri, Vivian Bose, Ghulam Hasan

Where Reported

1953 Indlaw SC 9; AIR 1953 SC 274; 1953 CRLJ 1105; 1953 (1) MLJ(SC) 739; 1953 SCJ 369; [1953] 4 S.C.R. 677; 1953 (4) STC 188

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Keywords: Madras General Sales Tax Act, Madras Sales Tax Act (IX of 1939)

Summary: Sales Tax - Sales of Goods Act, 1930, s. 4 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, (A) Validity of Act - (B) Transaction of sale completed within another Province u/s. 3 of Act - (C) Legislative intent behind connotation of 'sale' u/s. 2(h) - Held, a valid piece of legislation - Mere entering into contract in state not sufficient transfer of property also necessary - 'Sales' u/s. 2 (h) emphasises on transfer of property in a sale and no other meaning like popular one - All constituent parts of statute to be construed together in light of purpose and object of Act - Appeal allowed.
