Trojan and Company Limited v Rm. N. N. Nagappa Chettiar

Supreme Court of India

20 March 1953

Case Analysis

Bench

Mehr Chand Mahajan, Sudhi Ranjan Das

Where Reported

1953 Indlaw SC 6; AIR 1953 SC 235; [1953] 23 Comp Cas 307; 1953 (1) MLJ(SC) 729; 1953 SCJ 345; [1953] 4 S.C.R. 789

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure; Tort

Keywords: Ex-Parte

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Indian Contract Act, 1872, s. 73 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, ss. 34, 112, O.6, r.2 and O.20, r.4 - Tort - Claim for damages - Measure of damages - Future interest, where money deposited as per decree and prayed not to be paid out of insolvent's estate to creditors of insolvent plaintiff, pendelite appeal and Head Official Assignee decree-holder having taken money and kept it - Held, circumstances indicated for measure and difficulty no ground for refusing to answer for measurement - Supreme Court held had to arrive at its own finding in spite of concurrent finding - Future interest could not be allowed - Appeal partly allowed.
