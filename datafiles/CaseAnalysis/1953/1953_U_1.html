Kumbha Mawji v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

16 April 1953

Case Analysis

Bench

B. Jagannadhadas, Mehr Chand Mahajan, Vivian Bose

Where Reported

1953 Indlaw SC 12; AIR 1953 SC 313; 1953 (1) MLJ(SC) 841; 1953 MWN 600; 1953 SCJ 436; [1953] 4 S.C.R. 878

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Practice & Procedure - Arbitration Act, 1940, ss. 14(2) and 31(4)- Filing of arbitration award in court -(A)Whether s. 14(2) of Act of 1940 implies a requirement of authorisation of an umpire for filing an award in court? - Held, s. 14(2) of Act of 1940 clearly implies that where award or a signed copy thereof is in fact filed into court by a party he should have authority of umpire for doing so - Where awards are handed over by umpire to party, it cannot be assumed that mere handing over of awards necessarily implies authority of umpire to file same into court on his behalf - That authority has to be specifically alleged and proved- (A1) Whether u/s. 14(2) of Act of 1940 actual filing of award by umpire is essential in court? - U/s. 14(2) actual filing by umpire is not essential- Further held, where in response to a notice issued to him by court, umpire forwards to court award by post, there is sufficient compliance with s. 14(2) of Act of 1940 - (A2)What is reasonable inference of phrase 'in any reference' in context of s. 31(4)? - Further held, word 'reference' having been defined in Act as 'reference to arbitration' phrase 'in a reference' would mean 'in matter of a reference to arbitration - Phrase 'in a reference' if, therefore, comprehensive enough to cover also an application first made after arbitration is completed and a final award is made- Appeal dismissed.
