 Maqbool Hussain v State of Bombay

Supreme Court of India

17 April 1953

Case Analysis

Bench

Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, Ghulam Hasan, M. Patanjali Sastri, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Bijan Kumar Mukherjea

Where Reported

1953 Indlaw SC 13; AIR 1953 SC 325; 1954 (56) BomLR 13; 1953 CRLJ 1432; 1983 ECR 1598; 1983 (13) E.L.T. 1284; 1953 (2) MLJ(SC) 113; 1953 SCJ 466; [1953] 4 S.C.R. 730

Case Digest

Subject: Customs

Keywords: Importation, Preventive Detention Act, 1950, Prison Act, 1894, Sea Customs Act 1878, Bombay Prohibition Act 1949, Punjab Communist Detenus Rules, Punjab Communist Detenus Rules, 1950

Summary: Customs - Constitution - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898 - Prison Act, 1894, ss. 45, 46 and 52 - Bombay Prohibition Act, 1949 - Foreign Exchange Regulation Act, 1947, s. 23 - Sea Customs Act 1878, s. 167(8) - General Clauses Act, 1897, s. 3(37) - Indian Penal Code, 1860, 147, 149, 332 and 353 - Preventive Detention Act, 1950 (a) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 20(2) and 228 - Punjab Communist Detenus Rules, 1950 - Whether prosecution in court of Chief Presidency Magistrate was in violation of fundamental right guaranteed to appellant u/art. 20(2) of Constitution and whether case involved a substantial question of law as to interpretation of Constitution? - A true judicial decision presupposes an existing dispute between two or more parties and then involves four requisites : (1) presentation (not necessarily orally) of their case by parties to dispute; (2) If dispute between them is a question of fact, ascertainment of fact by means of evidence adduced by parties to dispute and often with assistance of argument by or on behalf of parties on evidence; (3) If dispute between them is a question of law, submission of legal argument by parties; and (4) A decision which disposes of whole matter by a finding upon facts in dispute and application of law of land to facts so found, including where required a ruling upon any disputed question of law - Held, neither proceedings taken before Sea Customs Authorities constituted a prosecution nor did order of confiscation constitute a punishment inflicted by a court or judicial tribunal and he could not be said to have been - Prosecuted and punished for same offence - Appeal No 81 /1952 dismissed.
