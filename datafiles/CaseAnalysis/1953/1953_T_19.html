 Brahma Prakash Sharma and Others v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

08 May 1953

Case Analysis

Bench

Bijan Kumar Mukherjea, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, M. Patanjali Sastri, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Ghulam Hasan

Where Reported

1953 Indlaw SC 22; AIR 1954 SC 10; 1954 CRLJ 238; 1953 (2) MLJ(SC) 231; 1953 SCJ 521; [1953] 4 S.C.R. 1169

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Libel, Contempt of Courts Act, 1926

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Contempt of Courts Act, 1926 - Whether defamatory statement directed against a judge amounts to contempt? - No doubt, there was publication as, is required by law of libel, but in contempt proceedings, that is not by any means conclusive - Where question arises as to whether a defamatory statement directed against a judge is calculated to undermine confidence of public in capacity or integrity of judge or is likely to deflect court itself from a strict and unhesitant performance of its duties, all surrounding facts and circumstances under which statement was made and degree of publicity a was given to it would undoubtedly be relevant circumstances - Held, what is material is nature and extent of publication and whether or not it was likely to have an injurious effect on minds of public or of judiciary itself and therefore to interference with administration of justice - Else contempt, if any is of technical nature - Appeal allowed.
