Bachan Singh S/O Saudagar Singh v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

04 May 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

P.S. Kailasam, P.N. Bhagwati, V.R. Krishna Iyer

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 393; (1979) 3 SCC 727; (1979) SCC (Cr) 830; 1979 CRLR 425; [1979] 3 S.C.R. 1193

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 326 - Appellant was found guilty of the charge of three murders u/s. 302, I.P.C. and was sentenced the death on each count and he was also found guilty u/s. 326, I.P.C., for causing grievous hurt with a sharp cutting weapon and sentenced to three years' rigorous imprisonment and a fine - Instant was the appeal against the conviction and sentence of the appellant - Whether, on the facts and the circumstances of the case, appellant was entitled for the relief - Held, after due consideration of the facts and the circumstances of the case, instant court opined that, taking advantage of the situation, when the victims could not defend, the appellant killed three and seriously wounded the fourth - Hence, the courts below rightly characterised the offence as heinous and rightly appropriated sentence of death and rigorous imprisonment as the extreme penalty - However, conclusion arrived in the matter by the instant court was placed before the Chief Justice for constituting a larger bench to decide the case and for getting the conformity for the decision of the instant court - Order accordingly.
