S. K. Gupta and Another v K. P. Jain and Another

Supreme Court of India

30 January 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

D.A. Desai, P.S. Kailasam, A.D. Koshal

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 156; (1979) 3 SCC 54; AIR 1979 SC 734; [1979] 49 Comp Cas 342; 1980 (17) DLT 114; [1979] 2 S.C.R. 1184; 1979 TAXLR 2053

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Corporate - Practice & Procedure - Companies (Court) Rules, 1959, r. 87 - Companies Act, 1956, ss. 2(29) , 391(1) , 391(2) , 392(1) and 483 - - Whether the Divisional Bench rightly refused to make an order for winding up of the Co. on the grounds that the court was not in a position to come to an affirmative finding that the scheme cannot be satisfactorily worked with or without modification and the matter should be left to the Co. Judge as to what future course of action should be taken? - Compromise or arrangement has to be between creditors and/or members of the company and the Co. - It is open to the Co. to offer a compromise to any of the creditors or enter into arrangement with each of the members - The scheme is essentially a compromise between the Co. and its unsecured creditors, when sanctioned does not merely operate as an agreement between the parties but has statutory force and is binding not only on the Co. but even dissenting creditors or members - The effect of the sanctioned scheme is to supply by recourse to the procedure thereby prescribed the absence of that individual agreement by every member of the class to be bound by the scheme which would otherwise be necessary to give it validity - Only the creditor or member of that Co. or a liquidator in the case of a Co. being wound up is entitled to move an application proposing a compromise or arrangement. By necessary implication anyone other than those specified in the s. would not be entitled to move such an application - All directions that the court may consider appropriate to give or make such modifications in the scheme, must be for the proper working of the compromise and/or arrangement - Held, judgment of the Division Bench is set aside and the order of the company judge is restored .
