Jaspal Singh and another v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

20 April 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, A.D. Koshal

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 405; (1979) SCC (Cr) 920; (1980) 1 SCC 487; AIR 1979 SC 1708; 1979 CRLJ 1386; 1979 CRLR 487; 1979 MLJ 222; 1979 UJ 718

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 302 Part-II - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Deceased along with appellants/accused persons had gone to village and consumed lot of liquor and accused persons assaulted deceased and put him in front of wheel of cart - Thereafter appellant drove cart passing wheels over body of deceased and running him over - Police after usual investigation submitted charge-sheet as result of which accused were prosecuted and committed to Court of Session who convicted them u/s. 304, Part II of IPC and sentencing them to three years' rigorous imprisonment - Appellants filed appeal before HC, which was affirming convictions and sentences passed by Sessions Judge - Hence, instant appeal - Whether judgment of HC and Sessions Court was sustainable - Held, deceased was actually put on ground and then run over by cart driven by appellant and at same time conduct of appellant in meeting witnesses in hospital and taking steps to bear expenses of treatment of deceased clearly shows that they had no clear intention to cause murder of deceased- Hence, Sessions Judge was right in convicting appellants not u/s. 302 but u/s. 304, Part II of the IPC, apart from that HC made no mention of evidence of PWs 5, 6 and 11 which proves oral dying declaration and extra judicial confession made by appellant - Therefore, on careful scrutiny of evidence and circumstances of case, SC find complete agreement with view taken by Sessions Judge and HC was right - Appeal dismissed.
