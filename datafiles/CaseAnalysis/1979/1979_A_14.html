Dy. Commissioner of Sales Tax (Law) v Advani Coorlikon Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

12 October 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Pathak, N.L. Untwalia

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 3; (1980) 1 SCC 360; AIR 1980 SC 609; 1981 (8) E.L.T. 801A; [1980] 1 S.C.R. 931; 1980 (45) STC 32; 1979 UJ 845

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Keywords: Sale Price, Trade Discount

Summary: Sales Tax - Indirect Tax - A/y 1971-1972 - Claim for deduction - Entitled to - Assessee (Private limited company) filed its return for A/y 1971-1972, and claimed deduction of sum paid as trade discount to retailers but the same was refused by Sales Tax Officer - Aggrieved assessee appealed, before Appellate, Assistant Commissioner and the same was allowed - Aggrieved Revenue filed second appeal before Tribunal and the same was dismissed - Revenue filed revision application before HC and the same was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether for computing turnover assessed to sales tax under the Act, sale price of goods was determined by including amount paid by way of trade discount - Held, trade discount was deduction from catalogue price of goods allowed by wholesalers to retailers engaged in trade and allowed enables retailer to sell goods at catalogue price and yet made reasonable margin of profit after taking into account his business expense - Outward invoice send by wholesale dealer to retailer shows catalogue price and against that deduction of trade discount was shown - The net amount was sale price, and it was that net amount which was entered in books of respective parties as amount realisable - Hence, sale price was arrived at after deducting trade discount, no question raised of deducting from sale price any sum by way of trade discount nor was there any question of two successive agreements between parties, one providing for sale of goods at catalogue price and other providing for an allowance by way of trade discount - Having regard to nature of trade discount, there was only one sale price between dealer and retailer, and that was price payable by retailer calculated as difference between catalogue price and trade discount and there was only one contract between parties, contract being that goods would be sold by dealer to retailer at aforesaid sale price - Therefore, in SC judgment, sale price which enter into computation of assessee's turnover for purpose of assessment under the Act, was obtained after deducting trade discount from catalogue price and trade discount allowed by assessee could not be included in turnover - Appeal dismissed.
