Sharif-Ud-Din v Abdul Gani Lone

Supreme Court of India

12 November 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

E.S. Venkataramiah, V.D. Tulzapurkar

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 101; (1980) 1 SCC 403; AIR 1980 SC 303; [1980] 1 S.C.R. 1177; 1980 UJ 39

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Election; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Custody Of Court

Summary: Election - Jammu and Kashmir Representation of the People Act, 1957, ss. 89(3) and 94 - Assembly elections - Attestation by petitioner - Legality - Appellant and respondent were candidates for seat to be filled from H Assemble Constitution - Respondent was declared as successful candidate by Returning Officer - Appellant filed election petition before HC challenging validity of respondent's election - HC dismissed the election petition on ground that copy of election petition had not attested by appellant under his own signature to be a true copy of petition as required by s. 89(3) of the Act - Hence, instant appeal - Whether HC was justified in passing the impugned order - Held, s. 89(3) of the Act provided that a copy of petition should be attested by petitioner 'under his own signature' to be a true copy of petition - However, s. 94 of the Act which required HC to dismiss an election petition when petitioner had not complied with provisions of s. 89 of the Act - Object of requiring copy of an election petition to be attested by petitioner under his own signature to be a true copy of petition appears to be that petitioner should take full responsibility for its contents and that respondent or respondents should have in their possession a copy of petition duly attested under signature of petitioner to be true copy of petition at earliest possible opportunity to prevent any unauthorized alteration or tampering of contents of original petition after it was filed into Court - It was probably to obviate any scope for such an allegation being made or to protect interest of respondent, Legislature thought of enacting s. 89(3) of the Act so that respondent might rely on copy served on him when he finds that original document in Court contained allegations different from those in copy in his custody - Importance of provision contained in s. 94 of the Act which made it obligatory on part of HC to dismiss a petition when it was established that s. 89 of the Act had not complied with also could not be over-looked - HC was justified in dismissing the petition filed by appellant - Appeal dismissed.
