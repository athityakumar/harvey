Chettiam Veettil Ammad and Another v Taluk Land Board and Others

Supreme Court of India

02 May 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Shingal, O. Chinnappa Reddy

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 235; (1980) 1 SCC 499; AIR 1979 SC 1573; [1979] 3 S.C.R. 839

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Rubber, Kerala Land Reforms (Amendment) Act, 1969

Summary: Land & Property - General Clauses Act, 1897 - Kerala Land Reforms Act, 1963 - Kerala Land Reforms (Amendment) Act, 1969, ss. 100-A, 85(2) - Conversion of Land - Ceiling of Land - Exemption - Certificate of Purchase - Appellants/landowners were tempted by exemption in favor of plantations and converted their lands into plantations after commencement of Act - Any land converted into a plantation had necessarily to be exempted from operation of ceiling law u/s. 81 of Act - Tribunal hold that dominant legislative intent was imposition of ceiling on lands and consequential obligation to surrender lands owned or hold in excess of ceiling area on notified date - Gifts of excess land made were ignored, which appellants could not be availed - Tribunal was competent to pass orders on application for purchase, including determination of compensation and purchase price u/s. 72F of Act - Hence instant appeal - Whether Tribunal's had erred in holding that imposition of ceiling on lands and determination of compensation for same were justifiable - Held, Tribunal would be a conclusive proof, so as to bind respondent - If a certificate of purchase was issued by Land Tribunal to any such person respondent/Board was required by law to treat it as conclusive proof that right, title and interest of appellant/land owner over land had been assigned to him - It would not be therefore permissible for respondent to disregard evidentiary value of certificate of purchase merely on ground, that it had not been issued on a proper appreciation or consideration of evidence - Tribunal's finding suffered from procedural error - Respondent/Board had to arrive at its own decision u/s. 85(5) of Act, if it was permissible by law and examine, if certificate was inaccurate on its face, or had been obtained by fraud or collusion - Appeal allowed and case was sent back to HC for fresh disposal according to law - Appeal allowed.
