Commissioner of Income Tax (Central) v B. N. Bhattacharjee and Another

Supreme Court of India

04 May 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

V.R. Krishna Iyer, V.D. Tulzapurkar

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 125; (1979) 4 SCC 121; (1979) SCC (Tax) 297; AIR 1979 SC 1725; 1979 (10) CTR 354; [1979] 118 ITR 461; [1979] 3 S.C.R. 1133; 1979 TAXLR 1180; [1979] 1 TAXMAN 348

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income-tax Act, 1961, s. 245 - Cynosure of suspicion - Colossal imposts - Settlement Commission - Respondent was cynosure of suspicion of Income Tax officials which led to search and seizure of around Rs. 30 lakhs in cash from him - Assessments were also re-opened and tax burden thus ran into a crore or so plus awesome prosecutions u/s. 77 of the Act with unpredictable prospects of sentences - Income Tax officer rejected respondent's explanations - Appeals to Appellate Assistant Commissioner by assessee against colossal imposts, resulted in marginal reduction of assessable income by around Rs. 10 lakhs - Both parties were dissatisfied, thus appealed to Income Tax Appellate Tribunal - While appeal was pending respondent sought sanctuary before Settlement Commission through application u/s. 245C of the Act - Respondent abandoned appeals before Tribunal -Tribunal dismissed appeals as they were treated withdrawn - However, Settlement Commission rejected respondent's application but later reversed its conclusion - Appellants stated that Settlement Commission had no power of review, once it had declined to proceed with application for settlement, therefore reopening of settlement proceedings was invalid - It was further urged that no bar of estoppel forbid Commissioner from exercising his statutory power of withholding consent to - Whether Settlement Commission's decision on jurisdictional and other legal grounds was justified - Held, Order of Appellate Tribunal clearly granted permission to withdraw appeals - It was true that Commissioner withdrew appeals of Department, but it was not true that he had made representations to respondents to act in particular manner with promise of doing something to his advantage leading to respondent in turn acting to his own prejudice by withdrawing his appeals - Withdrawal of appeals was independently decided upon by him so that he could move the Settlement Commission - Thereafter he moved Department to withdraw its appeals so as to entitle him to make application to Commission - Plea of estoppel which had found favour with Commission was overruled and objection raised by Tribunal was held as potent interdict on jurisdiction of commission - It was not inappropriate to state that policy of law as disclosed in Chapter XIXA was not to provide rescue shelter for big tax-dodgers who indulged in criminal activities by approaching Settlement Commission - Settlement Commission was to take due note of gravity of economic offences on wealth of nation which Wanchoo Committee had emphasised and was to exercise its 15 power of immunisation against criminal prosecutions by using its power only sparingly and in deserving cases; otherwise such orders would become vulnerable if properly challenged, thus holistic perspective in correct statutory setting made conclusion irresistible that appeal must be allowed, that Settlement Commission was to be inhibited from proceeding with application of respondent and appeals by the respondent before Appellate Tribunal was to be revived and disposed of expeditiously - Departmental appeals, that were admitted by Commissioner himself as weak and frivolous, were not to be revived as it was waste of public time and public money - Appeals allowed.
