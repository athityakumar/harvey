Union of India and Others v Jardine Henderson Limited and Vice Versa

Supreme Court of India

16 March 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

N.L. Untwalia, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 122; (1979) 2 SCC 258; AIR 1979 SC 972; 1979 (10) CTR 94; [1979] 118 ITR 112; [1979] 3 S.C.R. 555; [1979] 1 TAXMAN 367

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Bengal Public Demands Recovery Act, 1913, ss. 10, 7, 8, 9, Sch. II r. 39, Sch. II r. 40 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Income-tax Act, 1961 - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 35(4) and 46(2) - Taxation Laws (Continuation and Validation of Recovery Proceedings) Act, 1964, s. 3(1) - For realization of arrears of income-tax dues the Certificate Officer, forwarded to the Collector a certificate in accordance with s. 46(2) of the 1922 Act, specifying the amount of arrears due from respondent no. 2 - Certificate case was started against him under the 1913 Act - Petition was filed, decided in favour of the respondent - Hence, the present appeal - Whether the HC rightly decreed - Held, that the requirement of sub-cl. (ii) stood fulfilled and nothing further had to be done in the matter by the taxing authority and the judgment and order of the HC were set aside and it was directed that the certificate case should proceed to disposal in accordance with law as expeditiously as possible - Proceedings initiated on the basis of the notice of demand served upon the assessee before the reduction of the amount in appeal could be continued in relation to the amount so reduced from the stage at which such proceedings stood immediately before such disposal as provided for in sub-cl. (iii) Cl. (c) of s. 3(1) of the 1964 Act was also important and it clearly and expressly provided that no proceedings in relation to the Govt. dues should be invalid merely because no fresh notice of demand was served upon the assessee after the dues were enhanced or reduced in any appeal or proceeding - Appeal dismissed.
