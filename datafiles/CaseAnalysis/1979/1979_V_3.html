Commissioner of Income Tax Kerala, Ernakulam v V. Damodaran, Trivandrum

Supreme Court of India

15 October 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Pathak, N.L. Untwalia

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 73; (1980) 1 SCC 173; AIR 1980 SC 478; 1979 (13) CTR 191; [1980] 121 ITR 572; [1980] 1 S.C.R. 944; 1980 TAXLR 178

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income-tax Act, 1961, ss.2(6A)(e), 256(1), 254 - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Total income - Accumulated profits - Assessment - Limitation - Assessee was Managing Director of company maintains accounts showed that sum of Rs. 36,546.17/- was due to him by company - Assessee was originally assessed for assessment year on total income of Rs. 43,407 - Assessee claimed that accumulated profits of company amounted to Rs.1,050 and amount alone could be considered as dividend u/s. 2(6A)(e) of 1961 Act - Income Tax Officer rejected contention of assessee - Appellate Tribunal upheld the claim of assessee that accumulated profits in s. 2(6A)(e) of1961 Act - HC on the ground that provision for payment of tax and dividend could be taken into account when computing accumulated profits - Whether Appellate Tribunal was competent to refer provision for payment of tax to HC - Held, no reference application was made by assessee - S.256(1) of 1961Act, entitles assessee or Commissioner to apply to Appellate Tribunal to refer to HC any question of law arising out of order made by Appellate Tribunal u/s.254 of 1961 Act - Period of limitation for making such application was prescribed - If application was rejected by Appellate Tribunal applicant was entitled to apply to HC again within prescribed period of limitation and HC - Appellate Tribunal not competent to refer provision for payment of tax and dividend could be taken into account when computing accumulated profits and such reference to HC must be considered void - Appeal allowed.
