Premji Bhai Parmar and Others Etc v Delhi Development Authority and Others

Supreme Court of India

21 December 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

D.A. Desai, V.R. Krishna Iyer

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 109; (1980) 2 SCC 129; AIR 1980 SC 738; 1980 (17) DLT 101; [1980] 2 S.C.R. 704

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Land & Property - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 12, 14 and 32 - Delhi Development Act, 1957 - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 5, 5 A and 57 - Whether petitioners have been subjected to discriminatory treatment in contravention of art. 14 of Constitution inasmuch as no surcharge has been collected from allottees of flats in MIG schemes prior to November 1976 and subsequent to January, 1977? - Held, no - Allottees of flats in MIG scheme at any place and executed at any time will not form one class for purpose of pricing policy - Only valid basis for classification would be income-wise, area-wise, time-wise and scheme-wise, meaning all flats constructed at or about same time in same area in one project for particular income-group will form a class and there is no discrimination amongst them - Petitions dismissed.
