Chonampara Chellappan and Others v State of Kerala

Supreme Court of India

30 March 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, A.D. Koshal

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 418; (1979) 4 SCC 312; (1979) SCC (Cr) 1029; AIR 1979 SC 1761; 1979 CRLJ 1335; 1979 CRLR 744

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Tainted Evidence

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Accused was tried by Trial Court who after considering evidence, acquitted accused of charges framed against him on ground that there were certain infirmities in investigation conducted by police officer - State filed appeal before HC - HC reversed order of acquittal and sentenced appellant u/s. 302 IPC and has been sentenced to imprisonment for life - Whether order of HC was sustainable - Held, knocks bottom out of prosecution case regarding circumstances in which FIR was lodged, if injury was fresh, then it could not have been sustained during occurrence - Hence, story put forward by informant becomes extremely suspicious, there could be no doubt that in these tell-tale circumstances Trial Court was fully justified in entertaining serious doubt about truth of prosecution case - HC seems to have overlooked fact that entire fabric of prosecution case would collapse if FIR was held to be fabricated or brought into existence long after occurrence and any number of witnesses could be added without there being anything to check the authenticity of their evidence - Therefore, SC fully satisfied that view taken by Trial Court was reasonably possible and this was surely not fit case in which HC should have interfered with order of acquittal of appellant passed by Trial Court and set aside order of HC - Appeal allowed.
