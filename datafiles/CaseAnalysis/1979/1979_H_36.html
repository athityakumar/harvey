Hussainara Khatoon and Others (V) v Home Secretary, State of Bihar, Patna

Supreme Court of India

19 April 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Bhagwati, A.P. Sen, O. Chinnappa Reddy

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 558; (1980) SCC (Cr) 50; (1980) 1 SCC 108; AIR 1979 SC 1377; 1979 CRLJ 1052; 1980 MLJ 93; [1979] 3 S.C.R. 760

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Constitution - Constitution Of India, art. 21 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973 - Under-trial prisoners in detention for period longer than maximum term of sentence if convicted - (A) Whether detention of under-trial prisoners for periods longer than maximum term for which they could have been sentenced violative of art.21 of Constitution? - Where certain Under-trial prisoners remained in jail without trial for periods longer than maximum term for which they could have been sentenced if convicted, and such persons had been in detention for period longer than maximum without their trial having been commenced - Held, the continued detention was clearly illegal and in violation of their fundamental right u/art. 21 of Constitution - (B) What is duty of magistrate and Govt. towards under trial prisoner ? - Held, Magistrate must, before making an order of further remand to judicial custody, point out to under-trial prisoner of his entitlement to be released on bail - State Govt. to provide at its own cost a lawyer to under-trial prisoner with a view to unable him to apply for bail in exercise of his right under proviso (a) to Sub-s (2) of s. 167(2) proviso (a), CrPC, 1973 - Directions accordingly.
