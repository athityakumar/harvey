Munshi Ram And Ors. v Municipal Committee, Chheharta

Supreme Court of India

06 March 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Sarkaria, P.S. Kailasam, O. Chinnappa Reddy

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 259; (1979) 3 SCC 83; (1979) SCC (Tax) 205; AIR 1979 SC 1250; AIR 1995 SCW 1497; [1979] 118 ITR 488; [1979] 3 S.C.R. 463; 1979 TAXLR 871

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Indirect Tax - Municipalities and Local Governments - Professional Tax - Punjab Municipal Act, 1911, s. 61(b) - Punjab General Clauses Act, s. 2(40) - Appellants filed a suit for permanent injunction restraining defendant - Committee from realizing profession tax demanded by it - Appellants challenged validity of assessment contending that construed in light of definition given in s. 2(40) of Act, term 'person' occurring in s. 61(b) Act includes a 'firm' and since trade carried on by 'firm' is one, tax could be levied only on firm, and not on partners individually - On these premises, it was pleaded that Municipal Committee in levying tax on individual partners had exceeded its statutory powers under Act - Trial court dismissed suit - On appeal by plaintiffs, ADJ reversed judgment of trial court and decreed suit - High Court affirmed judgment and decree of first appellate court, on reasoning that term 'person' in s. 61(b) of Municipal Act, interpreted in light of definition given in s. 2(40) of Punjab General Clauses Act, includes a 'partnership' - Under cl. (b) of s. 61(1) of Municipal Act, basis on which liability to pay tax arises, is trade, profession or business; and if trade and business is one carried on by several persons collectively in partnership, then partnership alone, and not individual partners, are liable to pay tax - Aggrieved, respondent preferred a LPA - Appellate Bench of HC held that to import definition of term 'person' occurring in s. 2(40) of Act into s. 61(1) (b) of Municipal Act, will be repugnant to subject - In opinion of Bench, under scheme of statute in question, tax cannot be levied on a firm or factory as such, but only on individual owners of factory or of firm - Whether persons collectively doing business in partnership, in municipality, fulfill second condition? - Held, whether definition of 'person' given in s. 2(40) of Punjab General Clauses Act, can be imported into statute under consideration, so as to include a contractual firm, also, within purview of expression 'persons' used in clause (b) of s. 61(1) - Indeed, entire effort to import definition of 'person' given in General Clauses Act, into s. 61(1)(b) of Municipal Act, is directed to find a foundation for argument, that construction adopted by HC could lead to double taxation and even unconstitutional results - But in instant case, nothing of this kind has happened - Firm has not been assessed - No question of double taxation or exceeding Constitutional ceiling of Rs. 250/- fixed by art. 276(2) of Constitution, arises on facts of present case - Arguments advanced on behalf of appellants on this aspect of matter are merely hypothetical, and speculative - Appeal dismissed.
