Rameshwar Lal Sanwarmal v Commissioner of Income Tax, Assam

Supreme Court of India

05 December 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Bhagwati, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 171; (1980) 2 SCC 371; AIR 1980 SC 372; 1980 (14) CTR 372; [1980] 122 ITR 1; [1980] 2 S.C.R. 369; 1980 TAXLR 193; 1980 UJ 276

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax and Direct Taxes - Constitution, art. 136 - Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 2(6A)(e) - (A) Reference - Decision of High Court - Appeal against - Error of court - Prejudice, if any - (B) Word 'shareholder' u/s.2 (6A)(e) - Meaning - Assessee (HUF), merely beneficial owner of certain shares of a company - Loans advanced to business concerns of assessee - Whether taxable as deemed dividend - Held, assessee should not be prejudiced on account of error of court - Held, they could not be taxed as 'deemed dividend' within s. 2 (6A)(e) - Appeal allowed.
