Syad Akbar v State of Karnataka

Supreme Court of India

25 July 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Sarkaria, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 216; (1980) 1 SCC 30; (1980) SCC (Cr) 59; 1980 ACJ 38; AIR 1979 SC 1848; 1979 CRLJ 327; 1980 MLJ 265; [1980] 1 S.C.R. 95

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Tort - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 313 - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 114 - Presumption - Culpable Negligence - Eye-witnesses - Rebutting Evidence - Appellant/Driver was driving a bus and when he swerved vehicle, child came across and child died at spot - Lower Courts convicted appellant u/s. 304-A of Code, 1860 and sentenced him to simple imprisonment with a fine, regarding unreliability of eye-witnesses - In Revision, HC also confirmed same on principle of res ipsa loquitur - Hence instant appeal - Whether Courts below were right in discarding entirely evidence of said eye-witnesses merely on ground that they were treated as hostile by prosecution and cross-examined and Whether principle of res ipsa loquitur was applicable in criminal proceeding - Held, Whole of testimony of witnesses were not impugned in cross examination by prosecution - Courts below were not justified in brushing aside their testimony - Not proved reasonable doubt that appellant had caused death of child by negligent or rash driving - Culpable negligence of appellant, resulted in accident and death of child and it was thus evident that accident happened due to an error of judgment, and not negligence or want of driving skill - Circumstance of taking vehicle suddenly to extreme right of road, did not speak negligence or dereliction of duty to exercise due care and control, on part of appellant - Maxim res ipsa loquitur had no manner of application - Appeal allowed.
