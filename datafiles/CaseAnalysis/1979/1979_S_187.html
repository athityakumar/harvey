B. N. Nagarajan and Others v State of Karnataka and Others

Supreme Court of India

03 May 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

A.D. Koshal, V.R. Krishna Iyer, P.S. Kailasam

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 600; (1979) 4 SCC 507; (1980) SCC (L&S) 4; AIR 1979 SC 1676; 1979 LabIC 1206; 1979 (2) LLJ 209; 1980 (1) LLN 9; [1979] 3 S.C.R. 937; 1979 SLJ 468; 1979 (3) SLR 116; 1979 UJ 518

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Constitution - Service - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 162 and 309 - Karnataka Govternment Servants (Seniority), Rules, 1957, r. 2(a) - Issue with respect to seniority - Direct recruits who were graduate supervisors of erstwhile merged States promoted to posts of Asst. Engineers - Promotion regularized by subsequent State Govt. - Held, word 'regularisation' does not have connotation of permanence and therefore promotees will not take precedence over direct recruits in matter of seniority - Appeal allowed.
