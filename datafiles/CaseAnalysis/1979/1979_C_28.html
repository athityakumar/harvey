Cement Marketing Company of India Limited v Commissioner of Commercial Axes, Karnataka

Supreme Court of India

30 October 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Bhagwati, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 621; (1980) Supp SCC 373

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956, s. 2(h) - Sale Price - Whether in respect of transactions of sale of cement effected by the appellant under the provisions of the Cement Control Order, the amount of freight formed part of the "sale price" within the meaning of the definition of that term in s. 2(h) of the Central Sales Tax Act, 1956 and was includible in the taxable turnover of the appellant? - Held, said question was no longer res integra and it stands concluded by SC judgment in Hindustan Sugar Mills v. State of Rajasthan 1978 INDLAW SC 292 wherein it was held that by reason of the provisions of the Cement Control Order which governed the transactions of sale of cement entered into by the assessee with the purchasers, the amount of freight formed part of the "sale price" within the meaning of the first part of the definition of that term in s. 2(h) of the Central Sales Tax Act, 1956 and was includible in the turnover of the assessee - Said decision completely covers the instant case - Hence, HC was right in taking the view that the amount of freight formed part of the sale price and was rightly included in the taxable turnover of the appellant - Appeal dismissed.
