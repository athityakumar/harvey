Ramana Dayaram Shetty v International Airport Authority of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 May 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Bhagwati, V.D. Tulzapurkar, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 16; (1979) 3 SCC 489; AIR 1979 SC 1628; 1979 (2) LLJ 217; 1981 (1) LLN 270; [1979] 3 S.C.R. 1014

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Administrative - International Airport Authority Act, 1971 - Prevention of Food Adulteration Act - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 226, 19(1)(g) and 12 - Whether or not State entitled to deal with its property in any manner it likes or award a contract to any person it chooses without any constitutional limitations upon it? - Held, Govt. shall be at liberty to accept or reject any bid without assigning any reason, therefore, is not violative of art. 14 and that in matters relating to contracts with Govt., latter is not bound to accept tender of person who offers highest amount - Nowhere does it say that such a condition permits Govt. to act arbitrarily in accepting a tender or that under guise or pretext of such a condition - Govt. may enter into a contract with any person it likes, arbitrarily and without reason - Writ Petition was filed by appellant more than five months after acceptance of tender of 4th respondents and during this period - 4th respondents incurred considerable expenditure aggregating to about Rs. 1, 25, 000 in making arrangements for putting up restaurant and snack bars and in fact set up snack bars and started running same - It would now be most iniquitous to set aside contract of 4th respondents at instance of appellant - Position would have been different if appellant had filed WP immediately after acceptance of tender of 4th respondents - But appellant allowed a period of over five months to elapse during which 4th respondents altered their position, hence WP is also rejected - Appeal dismissed.
