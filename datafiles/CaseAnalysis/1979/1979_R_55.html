N. Jayaram Reddy and Another v Revenue Divisional Officer and Land Acquisition Officer, Kurnool

Supreme Court of India

23 March 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Shingal, D.A. Desai

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 425; (1979) 3 SCC 578; AIR 1979 SC 1393; 1979 HLR 393; [1979] 3 S.C.R. 599

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, ss. 86, O. 22, rr. 2, 3, 4(1) and 11, O. 41, r. 22 - Land Acquisition Act, s. 4 - Market value - Appellants accepted compensation under protest - Cross appeals were filed by Govt. and appellants in High Court on 07-12-1963 and before 03- 04-1964 respectively - (A) Whether Govt. appeal abater and stood dismissed because Legal Representatives of respondent 2, in Govt. appeal who died on 03-1964, were not brought on record within period of 90 days provided by law? - Held, as respondent 2 in Govt. appeal, died on 03-1964, and an application was not made to bring his LRs. on record within specified time limit, appeal automatically abated as against deceased respondent, and it is not correct to say that appeal automatically stood dismissed against surviving respondents because of that default - Further, in view of facts and circumstances, there is no doubt that any plea that may have been available to LRs. of deceased in Govt. appeal because of its abatement, was wilfully abandoned by them - (B) Whether finding of fact of HC that present appellants were entitled to compensation at Rs. 4 per square yard suffers from any such error as to require interference by this Court - Further held, it cannot be said that HC ignored or misread any important piece of evidence in arriving at its finding - Appellants bought land for Rs. 2 per square yard, and HC doubled that rate, and raised it to Rs.4 per square yard - Such a finding cannot be said to have been vitiated for any reason whatsoever - Appeal dismissed.
