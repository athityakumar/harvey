State Of M.P & Anr. Etc. v Ram Raghubir Prasad Agarwal & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

07 February 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

V.R. Krishna Iyer, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 609; (1979) 4 SCC 686; AIR 1979 SC 888; [1979] 3 S.C.R. 41

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Constitution - Education - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 136 - Madhya Pradesh Prathamik, Middle School Tatha Madhyamik Shiksha (Pathya Pustakon Sambandhi Vyavastha) Adhiniyam, 1973, ss. 3(2) and 5 - Syllabus - Directions for 'Rapid Reading' - Whether State has facultative power to compile and distribute its own text-books u/s. 5? - Syllabus for 'rapid reading' is not bad as falling short of definitional needs, although it is desirable for Board to be more expressive when laying it down - Willful vagueness in syllabi will invite an adverse verdict - 'Rapid reading', as a rubric, is itself, somewhat slippery as a substantive topic and so syllabus for it also may share that trait - New plea urged specifically for first time at argument stage in Court (and controverted by State) that no syllabus has been laid down, as a fact, for 'Rapid Reading' is too late to be permitted - Held, syllabus for 'Rapid Reading' suffers invalidation u/s. 3 because it has not been published - Publication must precede prescription of text-books u/s. 4 or their preparation u/s. 5 - Syllabus was published only on 30-06-1978, while text-books were prescribed in October, 1977 - So ss. 3 and 4 have been breached and a fresh decision by Govt. prescribing text-books for 'Rapid Reading' must be taken - Private sector has no right but State should not discriminate private sector if books of excellence, inexpensive and well designed available - Appeal partly allowed.
