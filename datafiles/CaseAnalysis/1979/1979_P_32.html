State of Uttar Pradesh v Pt. Chandra Bhushan Misra

Supreme Court of India

06 November 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy, R.S. Sarkaria

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 178; (1980) 1 SCC 198; AIR 1980 SC 591; 1980 ALJ 246; [1980] 1 S.C.R. 1131; 1980 UJ 12

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Court Fees Act 1870 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 41 r. 23 - Refund of Court fees - Whether refund of Court Fee could be ordered u/s. 13 of the Act, was attracted to a case where the appellate court remanded the case to the lower court in the interest of justice as provided by the provisions of O. 41 r. 23 of CPC as amended by HC.

Held, rules made by a HC altering the rules contained in the first schedule as originally enacted by the legislature shall have the same force and effect as if they had been contained in the first schedule and therefore, necessarily became part of the CPC for all purposes. Refund of Court Fee could be ordered u/s. 13 of the Act, even where the remand was made under the amended provisions of O. 41 r. 23 of CPC. Appeal dismissed.
