Bishan Devi and Others v Sirbaksh Singh and Another

Supreme Court of India

20 August 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

P.S. Kailasam, A.C. Gupta

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 326; (1980) 1 SCC 273; 1979 ACJ 496; AIR 1979 SC 1862; [1981] 51 Comp Cas 128; [1980] 1 S.C.R. 300; 1979 UJ 736

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate

Summary: Motor Vehicles Act, 1939, s.110B - Constitution, art. 136 - Appeal arising out of rejection of claim u/s.110-B of 1939 Act - Deceased, a patwari was expected to contribute for many years for the family - Appeal by special leave - Neither claims Tribunal, nor the HC considered relevant evidence - Insurer not establishing that person driving vehicle was not properly licensed - Pleas taken by the owner and the insurer, palpably false - Relief sought - SC, interfered and decreed the compensation with interest from the date of claim - Suggestions for making provisions for immediate and adequate reliefs to dependents, made - Appeal Allowed.
