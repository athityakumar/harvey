Shiv Shanker Dal Mills Etc v State of Haryana and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 November 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

V.R. Krishna Iyer, R.S. Pathak, A.D. Koshal

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 62; (1980) 2 SCC 437; AIR 1980 SC 1037; [1980] 1 S.C.R. 1170; 1980 UJ 55

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Practice & Procedure - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Punjab Agricultural Produce Markets Act, 1961 - Increased market fees - Refund of illegal portion - Legality - Appellants and writ petitioners had paid market fees at increased rate of 3% - Many dealers challenged levies as unconstitutional, ruled that excess of 1% over original rate of 2 % was ultra vires before HC - HC passed order - Agreed appellants filed instant appeals - Whether sums collected by respondents were liable to pay into HC - Held, all sums collected by various market committees/ respondents in these various appeals should be liable to be paid into HC of Punjab and Haryana within one week of intimation by Registrar of amount so liable to be paid into court - Statement of amounts collected in excess (1%) should be put into SC by dealers with copies to various market committees aforesaid within stipulated time, and if there was any difference between parties it should be brought to notice of SC in shape of miscellaneous petitions - Registrar of HC should issue public notice and otherwise give due publicity to fact that dealers who had not passed on liabilities to others and others who had contributed to or paid excess 1% covered by these petitions and appeals might make claims for such sums as were due to them from him within 1 month or such other period as he might fix - Registrar should scrutinize such claims and ascertain sums so proved - He would thereupon demand of all market committees concerned payment into Registry of such sums in regard to which proof of claims have been made - On such intimation, market committees should pay into Registry amounts so demanded by Registrar within one week of such intimation - Amount should be paid together with interest - He would devise mechanics of processing claims as best as he might and, in event of dispute, might refer to HC for its decision of such disputes, if he thinks it necessary - He might dispose of objections finally - If parties eligible for repayment of amounts do not claim within one year from today Registrar would not entertain any further claims - It would be open to such parties to pursue their remedies for recovery for any sums that might be due to them - Each State Marketing Board would deposit within 10 days from today sum of Rs. 5,000/- before Registrar for preliminary expenses of publicity and other incidentals for implementation of directions given above - Any unexpended amount, at end of 1 year, would be repaid to respective State Marketing Board - Unclaimed amounts, should be permitted to be used by respective Marketing Committees for purposes falling within statute as interpreted by SC - Winners being both sides before us, invisible small consumers and above all, justice, equity and good conscience to inarticulate community, which was functional triumph of law in action within hailing distance of each other - Appeals disposed of.
