Deputy Commissioner of Sales Tax (Law) Board of Revenue v G.S. Pai and Company

Supreme Court of India

15 October 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Bhagwati, V.D. Tulzapurkar, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 74; (1980) SCC (Tax) 70; (1980) 1 SCC 142; AIR 1980 SC 611; 1980 E.L.T. 343; 1979 KLT 871; [1980] 1 S.C.R. 938; 1980 (45) STC 58; 1980 TAXLR 1603; 1980 UJ 210

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Kerala General Sales Tax Act, 1963 - Classification - Common parlance meaning - 'Bullion and specie' - (A) Whether certain ornaments and other articles of gold purchased by the assessee with a view to melting them and making new ornaments or other articles out of the melted gold fall within expression 'Bullion and specie' - Held, (A) the ornaments and other articles of gold purchased by the assessee do not fall within Entry 56 and they are, accordingly, liable to be taxed not at the lesser rate of 1 per cent applicable to 'bullion and specie' but at the general rate of 3 per cent u/s. 5A r/w s. 5(1)(ii) - 'Water supply and sanitary fittings' - (B) Whether G.I. Pipes sold by the assessee fall within Entry 26A in the First Schedule to the Act which reads 'Water Supply and Sanitary Fittings' - The G.I. Pipes sold by the assessee would, fall within the description of 'sanitary fittings' only if it can be shown that they were meant for use in lavatories, urinals or bathrooms - This is a question which has not been considered by the revenue authorities and the case would, therefore, have to be remanded to Appellate Asst. Commr. - Appeal allowed.
