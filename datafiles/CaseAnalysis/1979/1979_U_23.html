Hoshnak Singh v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

27 February 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

D.A. Desai, P.N. Shingal

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 263; (1979) 3 SCC 135; AIR 1979 SC 1328; [1979] 3 S.C.R. 399

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property

Keywords: Evacuee Property & Displaced Persons

Summary: Land & Property - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 226 - Administration of Evacuee Property Act, 1950, s. 56 - Aministration of Evacuee Property (Central) Rules, 1950, r. 15(6) - Displaced Persons (Compensation and Rehabilitation Act, 1954, ss. 10 and 12 - Displaced Persons (Compensation and Rehabilitation) Rules, 1955, rr. 49 and 69 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 11 - (A) Res judicata - Dismissal of Writ Petition u/art. 226 in limine without speaking order - Challenged by statutory remedy of appeal or revision - Decided - Whether second writ petition against it is barred by principle of res judicata? - (B) Allottment of agricultural lands to displaced persons on quasi-permanent basis - Nature of rights - Extent of power to cancel allotments after 22-07-1952 - Allotment of land under conditions published in Notification D/-08-1949 - Govt. taking over part of land which allottee was holding on date of Notification u/s. 12 of 1954 Act - Held, such a dismissal would not constitute a bar of res judicata to a subsequent petition on same cause of action - Allottee has right to demand compensation in cash - Appeal allowed.
