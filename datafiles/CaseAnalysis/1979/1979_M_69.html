Mohd. Iqbal Ahmed v State of Andhra Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

18 January 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, A.D. Koshal

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 592; (1979) 4 SCC 172; (1979) SCC (Cr) 926; 1979 (16) ACC 132; AIR 1979 SC 677; 1979 CRLJ 633; 1979 CrLR(SC) 242; [1979] 2 S.C.R. 1007; 1979 UJ 499

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947, s. 6 - Proof of sanction for prosecution u/s. 6 of Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947 and its validity - When does question of sanction arise - (A)Whether present prosecution was launched with valid sanction? - Any case instituted without a proper sanction must fail because this being a manifest defect in prosecution, entire proceedings are rendered void ab initio - What Court had to see is whether or not sanctioning authority at time of giving sanction was aware of facts constituting offence and applied its mind for same - Any subsequent fact which may come into existence after grant of sanction is wholly irrelevant - Held, present prosecution was launched without any valid sanction and, therefore, cognizance taken by Special Judge was completely without jurisdiction - Appeal allowed.
