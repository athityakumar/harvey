Hukam Chand Jute Mills Limited v Second Industrial Tribunal, West Bengal and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 April 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

V.R. Krishna Iyer, V.D. Tulzapurkar, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 246; (1979) 3 SCC 261; (1979) SCC (L&S) 266; AIR 1979 SC 876; 1979 (38) FLR 383; 1979 LabIC 612; 1979 (1) LLJ 461; 1979 (1) LLN 509; [1979] 3 S.C.R. 644; 1979 UJ 467

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Payment of Bonus Act, 1965 - Customary bonus - Legality -Appellant was a jute mill in Bengal employing several thousand workers - Customary bonus had been claimed, conceded and settled between the parties for long years - In 1976, Act was amended restricting workers claim to Bonus - Management denied the legality of Customary bonus claimed by the workmen - Reference was made to the Industrial Tribunal - Award was passed - Management challenged the award of the Industrial Tribunal and urged that the Act as amended by Act 23 of 1976, annihilates all species of bonus including customary and contractual bonus - Whether customary bonus, as claimed in this case, is impaired or eliminated by the 1976 amendment Act.

Held, Act does not deal with customary bonus and is confined to profit-based or productivity-based bonus, the provisions of the Act have no say on customary bonus and cannot, therefore, be inconsistent therewith. Statutory bonus and customary bonus operate in two fields and do not clash with each other. The claim was rooted in custom but quantified by contract. It did not originate in any agreement, but was organised by it. Appeal dismissed.
