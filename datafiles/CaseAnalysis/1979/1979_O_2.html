Pramod Kumari Bhatia v Om Prakash Bhatia And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

15 November 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy, R.S. Sarkaria

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 92; (1980) 1 SCC 412; AIR 1980 SC 446; 1980 ALJ 163; 1980 HLR 380; [1980] 2 S.C.R. 53; 1980 UJ 51

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Family & Personal - Practice & Procedure - Will - Interpretation of - Testator 'S' died leaving behind him a will, a widow 'L' being his second wife, a son 'P' by 'L' and widow and daughters 'M', 'R' and 'K' respectively of pre-deceased son by pre-deceased first wife - 'N' and her daughter 'R' had left family house and moved away while 'K' stayed on with her grand-father and was brought up by him - 'L', widow of 'S' died - SC was concerned with title to sum of Rs. 16,490/- lying in deposit with State Bank of India and District Cooperative Bank - 'P' claimed the amount under will - Said will was registered one - 'P' filed a suit before Trial Court - Trial Court held that 'P' was not entitled to succeed, under will and that amount had to be divided among 'P', 'M', 'R' and 'K' - Aggrieved by said order 'P' appealed before HC - HC held that on true construction of will 'P' alone was entitled to amount - Aggrieved by said order 'K' filed instant appeal - Whether HC was right in passing impugned order - Held, it was clear and unambiguous intention of testator that his son 'P' should succeed to his estate after death of 'L' - Necessary words to that effect could and must be read into will - Trial Court on strict and narrow construction of will, came to conclusion that 'P' was not entitled to succeed, under will, on death 'L' and that amount had to be divided among 'P', 'M', 'R' and 'K' - On appeal HC held that on true construction of will 'P' alone was entitled to amount - SC agreed with conclusion of HC - Before HC 'K' filed an application for reception of additional evidence - Principal additional evidence sought to be adduced was an alleged letter said to have been written by late 'S' to bank nominating 'K' as person entitled to amount in deposit with Bank - Letter itself was not filed along with application but request was made to summon letter from Bank - HC rejected application - Application to HC was made very many years after suit had been filed, and also quite some years after appeal had been filed before HC, and SC did not think that SC would be justified in interfering with discretion exercised by HC in refusing to receive additional evidence at that stage - Appeal dismissed.
