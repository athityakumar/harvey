State of Punjab v Gurdial Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

25 October 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

V.R. Krishna Iyer, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 83; (1980) 2 SCC 471; AIR 1980 SC 319; [1980] 1 S.C.R. 1071

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 19 and 226 - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4, 6 and 17 - Acquisition proceeding quashed by High Court on the ground that action was vitiated by mala fides u/art. 226 of Constitution - Fresh attempt for acquiring land was struck down - Judgment of HC challenged by state - Held, in absence of any denial of allegations made by respondents in Writ Petition by a person having personal and direct knowledge in matter, it is difficult to resist conclusion that averments in Writ Petition alleging mala fides must be accepted - Petition dismissed.
