Shankar Chakravarti v Britannia Biscuit Company and Another

Supreme Court of India

04 May 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

D.A. Desai, V.R. Krishna Iyer, A.D. Koshal

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 17; (1979) SCC (L&S) 279; (1979) 3 SCC 371; AIR 1979 SC 1652; 1979 (39) FLR 70; 1979 LabIC 1192; 1980 (2) LLJ 78; 1979 (2) LLN 72; [1979] 3 S.C.R. 1165; 1979 SLJ 385

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 33(2)(b) and 10 - Uttar Pradesh Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Misconduct - Application for approval of dismissal order - Finding that violation of principles of natural justice in enquiry - No duty cast on Tribunal suo moto to call employer who was not asking for opportunity to lead evidence to prove charges - Grant of opportunity by High Court - Validity - High Court committed error in granting such non-sought opportunity at stage of Letters Patent Appeal - Appeal allowed.
