Babu Ram Jagdish Kumar & Co., Etc., Etc. v State Of Punjab & Ors., Etc., Etc.

Supreme Court of India

04 May 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

E.S. Venkataramiah, N.L. Untwalia, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 231; (1979) SCC (Tax) 265; (1979) 3 SCC 616; AIR 1979 SC 1475; [1979] 3 S.C.R. 952; 1979 (44) STC 159; 1979 TAXLR 1830; 1979 UJ 605

Case Digest

Subject: Electricity & Energy; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure; Sales Tax

Summary: Administrative - Punjab General Sales Tax, 1948, ss. 2(d), (ff), 4, 5 and 6 - Appeal to pronounce on validity of s. 31 of Punjab General Sales Tax Act, 1948 - Whether s. 31 of Act was void on ground that it suffered from vice of excessive delegation of legislative power and whether notification is valid? - Held, authorities are clear that it is not unconstitutional for legislature to leave it to executive to determine details relating to working of taxation laws, such as selection of persons on whom tax is to be laid, rates at which it is to be charged in respect of different classes of goods, and like - Tax in question has been imposed by Governor and not by Legislature who alone had power to impose it - Duties levied under Order-in-Council are really levied by authority of Act under which Order is issued - Inclusion of paddy in Schedule 'C' to Act was against policy underlying Act - Impugned notification cannot be treated as one issued against policy of statute - Hence, s. 31 of Act and Notification issued there under do not suffer from vice of excessive delegation of legislative power - Appeals dismissed.
