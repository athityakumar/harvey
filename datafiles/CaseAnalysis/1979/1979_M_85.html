Municipal Council, Pusad v Gokaldas Dossa and Company Limited

Supreme Court of India

13 November 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Sarkaria, O. Chinnappa Reddy

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 89; (1980) 1 SCC 354; AIR 1980 SC 428; [1980] 2 S.C.R. 7; 1980 UJ 34

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Professions Tax Limitation Act, 1941, Berar Municipal Law, 1886

Summary: Indirect Tax - Municipalities & Local Governments - Government of India Act, 1935 - Professions Tax Limitation Act, 1941 - Berar Municipal Law, 1886 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Boja Tax - Demand Notice - Validity - Appellant/Municipal Council was constituted as Municipal Committee - Appellant with sanction of local govt. imposed tax known as Boja Tax & Bale Tax on ginning and pressing cotton - Respondents M/s. Gokuldas Dossa & Co. Ltd. were doing business of ginning and pressing cotton by mechanical processes within limits of said Municipality - Appellant issued demand notice and bill for Rs. 3,971.75 in respect of Boja and Bale Tax for year 1965-66 requiring respondents to pay that amount of tax - Respondents submitted objections before appellant and same was rejected by appellant - Aggrieved respondents filed petition before HC seeking declaration that Boja & Bale Tax imposed on them was ultra vires and unconstitutional - HC allowed the petition and granted an interim stay of recovery of tax from respondent No. 1 and quashed Demand Notice on ground that tax was in excess of ceiling limit of Rs. 250 per annum - Hence instant appeal - Whether HC was right in passing impugned order - Held, first item No. 4 was an exemption from limitation imposed by s. 2 of 1941 Act, and exemption must be construed strictly - Secondly, effect of s. 3 and item 4 of Schedule was to continue levi-ability of tax and, this item must be construed strictly like a taxing statute - If party would convince SC that item would be otiose if this interpretation was put, there would be something to say in his favor - But item would not be otiose even if SC did not treat item 4 as case of mis-description but give plain meaning that 1922 Act means 1922 Act, and not 1922 Act - Various taxes must have been imposed by Municipalities in Central Provinces by virtue of notifications issued u/s. 66(1)(b) of 1922 Act and they would fall within ambit of item 4, the word "imposed" means that taxes which could continue to be levied should have been imposed in past before 1941 Act came into force - This was in consonance with s. 142A(2) of 1935 Act - Crucial words were those which have been underlined - These words clearly lay down that if tax in question had in fact, been imposed u/s. 66(1)(b) of 1922 Act before coming into force of 1941 Act, it would fall within exemption of item 4 r/w s. 3 of 1941 Act and continuance of such an imposition in excess of constitutional limit, would be in consonance with proviso to s. 142A(2) of 1935 Act and also art. 276(2) of constitution - Tax in question was imposed u/s. 66(1) (b) of 1922 Act, in 1932, long before 1941 Act came into force, and no question of invoking any deeming, fiction was involved, ratio of Municipal Committee Akot's case, in fact, supports contention of appellant, and highlights error in HC decision - In view of all that had been said above, we were of opinion that demand notice in question does not contravene s. 142A(2) of 1935 Act and art. 276(2) of constitution, and was valid - Judgment of HC was set aside - Appeal allowed.
