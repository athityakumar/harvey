Vithal Dattatraya Kulkarni and Others v Shamrao Tukaram Power and Others

Supreme Court of India

21 March 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy, R.S. Sarkaria, P.S. Kailasam

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 251; (1979) 3 SCC 212; AIR 1979 SC 1121; [1979] 3 S.C.R. 572; 1979 UJ 663

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Keywords: Bombay Tenancy Act, 1939

Summary: Rent Control - Bombay Tenancy and Agricultural Lands Act, 1948, ss. 37 and 39 - Heritability of protected tenant - Landlords filed tenancy case, before the Aval Karkun, to recover possession of the land from tenant - Application was dismissed - On appeal, the Collector directed that possession of the land should be given to the landlord - Landlords accordingly recovered possession of the land - After around 12 years, tenant's heirs filed tenancy case against the landlords and their alienees, u/ss. 37 and 39 of the Act alleging that the landlords had ceased to cultivate the lands personally within twelve years from the date of dispossession of the tenant and, therefore, they were entitled to recover possession of the land - Aval Karkun made an order in favour of Tenant's heirs - Order was confirmed by the Special Deputy Collector - Revision applications filed thereagainst before the Maharashtra Revenue Tribunal was allowed - Writ petition was filed, wherein HC quashed the order of the Tribunal and restored the order of the Aval Karkun as affirmed by the Special Deputy Collector - Hence, instant appeal - Whether the right of the tenant to have the possession of the land restored on the failure of the landlord to cultivate the land personally at any time during the twelve years subsequent to his obtaining possession was a heritable right.

Held, under the provisions of the Act, as it stood before it was amended in 1956, the right of a tenant to recover possession of land from a landlord who had obtained possession of such land on the ground that he required it to cultivate it personally was not a heritable right. Appeal allowed.
