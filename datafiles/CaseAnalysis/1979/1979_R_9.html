State of Rajasthan v Raghubir Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

05 February 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy, R.S. Sarkaria

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 608; (1979) 3 SCC 102; AIR 1979 SC 852; [1979] 3 S.C.R. 6

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Interest Act, 1839 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Plaintiff respondent took a contract work from erstwhile Govt. of State of Udaipur - Despite demands and notices issued by plaintiff a considerable amount due to him remained unpaid - Respondent filed suit out of which appeal arises together with interest at rate of 12% - Suit was contested by State of Rajasthan - After full trial a decree for Rs. 1, 67, 619 (including sum of Rupees 66, 517 for which a preliminary decree had already been passed) was passed - Decree also awarded interest at rate of 41/2 % on amount decreed from date of suit till date of realisation - Plaintiff and defendant preferred appeals to High Court - HC reduced decretal amount by a sum of Rs. 9, 991 - HC held that plaintiff was entitled to interest from 01-01-1951, and not merely from date of suit and enhanced rate of interest to 6% - Whether awarding of interest by the HC, is justifiable in law? - Held, appellant contended that contract prohibited award of interest - Appellant relied upon following sentence occurring in para 16 of contract 'Neither earnest money deposit nor withheld amount shall bear any interest' - This sentence far from supporting case of appellant appears to support case of plaintiff - Reference to 'the withheld amounts' is to amounts representing 5% of running bills which are required to be withheld at time of payment of running bills - Provision that contractor is not entitled to interest on these withheld amounts appears to imply that interest is claimable on other amounts due to contractor - While awarding interest pendente lite Trial Court adopted rate of 41/2% but Trial Court gave no reasons for so doing - HC considered matter in some detail and having regard to various continuous defaults committed by defendant and its Officers, HC enhanced rate of interest to 6% - HC was justified in doing so and no reason to interfere with discretion exercised by HC - Appeal dismissed.
