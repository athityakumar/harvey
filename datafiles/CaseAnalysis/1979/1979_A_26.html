Supdt. and Remembrancer of Legal Affairs, West Bengal v Anil Kumar Bhunja and Others

Supreme Court of India

23 August 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Sarkaria, P.N. Shingal, O. Chinnappa Reddy

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 548; (1979) 4 SCC 274; (1979) SCC (Cr) 1038; AIR 1980 SC 52; 1979 CRLJ 1390; 1979 CrLR(SC) 664; [1980] 1 S.C.R. 323

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Arms Act, 1959, s. 29(b) - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Repair fire-arms - No valid license - punishment - Sustainability - On or about dt. 4-17-1971, Police while investigating case, went to premises No. 4, on ground floor of building, they discovered workshop run by 'X', who was then working on revolver and said premises, police found several other guns, revolvers and rifles, all these fire-arms were seized by police - 'X' had no valid license to keep or repair these fire-arms under the Act and respondents 1 to 4, however, were holding license under the Act to run business of repairing and dealing in fire-arms - On dt. 4-17-1970, police charge-sheeted and respondents 1 to 4 to stand their trial in Court of the Presidency Magistrate, in respect of offences u/s. 25(1) (a) and 27 of the Act - Trial Magistrate, held that there were materials to make out a prima facie case u/s. 25(1) (c) of the Act against 'X' and u/s. 29(b) of the Act against 'Y' and charged them accordingly - So far as respondents 1 to 4 were concerned, Magistrate took view that giving of arms to accused 'X' by respondents 1 to 4 for limited purpose of repairs, did not amount to delivery of possession of those arms within meaning of S. 29(b) of the Act and discharged respondents by order, dt. 11- 17-1971 - Aggrieved by this State filed Criminal Revision against Magistrate's order before HC, which was dismissed and held, respondent nos. 1to 4 could not be said to have delivered fire-arms, concerned into possession of 'X' within meaning of S. 29(b) of the Act, because respondents who possessed valid licenses for repairs as well as for sale of fire-arms, had given only temporary custody of those arms to 'X' for limited purpose of carrying repair job - In its view, there was no delivery of possession of fire-arms so long as control over arms and authority to use those arms was not transferred to custodian - Hence instant appeal - Whether order of HC was sustainable - Held, set aside orders of Courts below whereby respondents 1 to 4, herein, were discharged, although offences u/s. 29(b) and 30 of the Act were summons cases, Magistrate has followed warrant procedure, obviously because an offence u/s.25 of the Act, for which 'X' was being jointly tried with respondents 1 to 4, was warrant case - Moreover, trial of summons case as warrant case does not amount to illegality, but was mere irregularity that does not vitiate trial unless there was prejudice - Therefore, send case back to Trial Magistrate with direction that he should frame charges in respect of offences u/ss. 29(b) and 30 of the Act against accused respondents 1 to 4 and proceed further with trial in accordance with law - Appeal allowed.
