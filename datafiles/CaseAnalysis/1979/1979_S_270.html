Ratan Singh v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

03 October 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

V.R. Krishna Iyer, P.N. Shingal

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 184; (1979) 4 SCC 719; (1980) SCC (Cr) 17; 1980 ACJ 91; AIR 1980 SC 84; 1980 CRLJ 11; 1980 CRLR 44; [1980] 1 S.C.R. 846; 1980 (4) ShimLC 292; 1980 UJ 58

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 304-A - Rash and negligent driving - Road accidents - Conviction - Sentence - Challenged - Accused was convicted u/s. 304-A and was sentenced to 2 years imprisonment - Instant petition filed challenging the same.

Held, law u/s. 304-A of IPC and under the rubric of negligence, must have due regard to the fatal frequency of rash driving of heavy duty vehicles and of speeding menaces. Thus viewed, it is fair to apply the rule of resipsaliqueur, of course, with care. Instant case deserves no consideration on the question of conviction. Further, victimization of the family of the convict may well be a reality and is regrettable. It is a weakness of jurisprudence that the victims of the crime, and the distress of the dependents of the prisoner, do not attract the attention of the law. Indeed, victim reparation is still the vanishing point of our criminal law. This is a deficiency in the system which must be rectified by the Legislature. Petition dismissed.
