Concord of India Insurance Company Limited v Smt. Nirmala Devi and Others

Supreme Court of India

16 April 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

V.R. Krishna Iyer, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 126; (1979) 4 SCC 365; (1979) SCC (Cr) 996; 1980 ACJ 55; AIR 1979 SC 1666; [1979] 49 Comp Cas 463; [1979] 118 ITR 507; [1979] 3 S.C.R. 694; 1979 UJ 486

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Limitation Act, 1963, s. 5 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - (A) Appeal against Tribunal's award - Condonation of delay in motor accident case to insurance Company - Availability - Whether mistake of counsel in calculating period of limitation is sufficient cause? - (B) Special leaves - Absence of manifest injustice or gross misappreciation or perversity in factual findings - Held, condonation cannot be refused on ground that manager of company is not an illiterate or so ignorant person who couid not calculate period of limitation and that such appeals are filed by such companies daily - On merits also no ground shown for intervention - Petition dismissed.
