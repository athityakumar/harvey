 D. C. Gouse and Company v State of Kerala and Another

Supreme Court of India

21 September 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Shingal, Y.V. Chandrachud, V.R. Krishna Iyer, N.L. Untwalia, A.D. Koshal

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 70; (1980) 2 SCC 410; AIR 1980 SC 271; [1980] 1 S.C.R. 804

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Municipalities & Local Governments - Practice & Procedure - Kerala Building Tax Act, 1975 - Kerala Municipal Corporation Act, 1961 - Kerala Panchayats Act, 1960 - Corporations Act, 2001, s. 102(2) - the Kerala Panchayats (Taxation and Appeal) Rules, 1963 - Building Tax Assessment - Sustainability - Cases relate to the validity of certain provisions of 1975 Act and were directed against the judgment of the Kerala High Court dated 12 June 1978, by which the validity of those provisions was upheld - Whether the law governing the levy and fixation of annual value of buildings in the areas of the local authorities concerned provide the necessary procedure and the machinery for their assessment and final fixation - Held, it was not disputed before SC that the three Acts in question were 1961 Act, Municipal Act, and Panchayats Act - SC had occasion to refer to section 102(2) of 2001 Act earlier, with specific reference to the annual value of buildings - S.138 of 2001 Act provided, inter alia, that the rules embodied in Schedule II of 2001 Act should be read as part of the chapter on 'Taxation' - RR. 4 to 16 of 2001 Act provided the procedure and the machinery for assessment of the property tax including the procedure for moving the Commissioner by revision petition to reduce the tax - R. 22(2) of 2001 Act provided for the hearing of such applications by the Commissioner and for their determination by him u/r. 22(3) of 2001 Act - R.23 of 2001 Act provided for the filing of appeal to the Standing Committee against the revisional order of the Commissioner - Then there was provision in r.24 of 2001 Act for the filing of appeal to the District Court and there was further provision in r.26 of 2001 Act to the effect that the Court might, if it thinks fit, state a case on any appeal for the decision of the HC and would do so whenever a question of law was involved if either the Commissioner or the appellant applied in writing in that behalf - R.27 of 2001 Act provided for the disposal of the case by the District Court in conformity with the decision of the HC - Moreover r.28 of 2001 Act provided for the correction of the assessment books according to the decision of the Standing Committee, or the District Court - 2001 Act thus provided all the necessary procedure and machinery for determining the annual value of buildings in a fair and reasonable manner - SC had gone through the provisions of the Municipalities Act also, in regard to the procedure and the machinery for determining the annual value of buildings - Chap. VI of Part III of Municipalities Act dealt with 'Taxation and Finance' - S.150 of Municipalities Act stated that the rules and tables embodied in Schedule II should be read as part of that Chap. - R.7 of Municipalities Act provided that the value of the building for purposes of the property tax (including the annual value) should be determined by the Commissioner - R.12 of Municipalities Act provided for the filing of a revision petition and r.13 of Municipalities Act provided for its disposal only after hearing the revision petitioner - R.24 of Municipalities Act provided for the filing of appeal to the Municipal Council against the Commissioner's assessment - R.30 of Municipalities Act provided for the appointment of Special officer to exercise the Councils appellate power - So the Municipalities Act also provided the necessary procedure and the machinery for the proper fixation of the annual value of buildings - In the Panchayat Act also, provision were made in s.68 of Panchayat Act for ascertaining the annual rental value of buildings - S.144 of Panchayat Act provided for appeals and revisions - U/s.144 (1) of Panchayat Act appeal lied to the Panchayat and then u/s.144(2) of Panchayat Act to the Deputy Director - S.144(3) Panchayat Act gave power to the State Govt. also to call for and examine the record and pass an appropriate order - Then there were Rules - Panchayat Act Act also thus provided the necessary procedure and machinery for determining the annual value of buildings in a satisfactory manner - It was therefore futile to contend that there was no adequate procedure or machinery in the three Acts for the satisfactory and proper determination of the annual value, of buildings - That value could therefore very well be made the basis for determining the capital value of a building and thereby fixing the building tax under the charging section - Moreover, ss. 9 to 16 of 1975 Act contained the procedure and the machinery for the assessment of the building tax on the returns filed u/ss. 7 and 8 of 1975 Act - These provisions were adequate in all respects and are not open to challenge - Appeal dismissed.
