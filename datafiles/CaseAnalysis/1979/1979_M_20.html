Mahant Amar Parkash & Ors. v Parkasha Nand & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

19 January 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy, R.S. Sarkaria

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 292; (1979) 3 SCC 221; AIR 1979 SC 845; [1979] 2 S.C.R. 1012; 1979 (2) UJ 219

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Trusts & Associations

Summary: Land & Property - Trusts & Associations - Appointment - Dispute raised on ground of coercion - Sustainability - It was alleged by the plaintiff that Amar Parkash A who held power of attorney from Mahant Krishan Das K, the previous Mahant of the Dera, had mismanaged the properties and had granted leases of lands belonging to the Dera to his mistress and his brother-in-law - K came to know about the mismanagement by A and cancelled the power of attorney which he had previously executed in favour of A - K summoned the general assembly of the Udasi Ghekh and held a meeting, with the help of the Deputy Commissioner and the Police - Meeting was attended by other Mahants - In the general assembly K tendered resignation of office of Mahant on the ground of old age and ill health and appointed the plaintiff as his successor Mahant - It was accepted by all the Mahants - K, treating the plaintiff as his Sadaq Chela, applied Tilak, performed Pagri ceremony with his own hand and duly installed the plaintiff as the Mahant - Visiting Mahants also performed the Pagri ceremony - Despite the installation of the plaintiff as Mahant of the Dera, A continued his activities and started obstructing the plaintiff from discharging his obligations as Mahant - Plaintiff, therefore, filed the suit for a declaration that he was the Mahant of the Dera and for other reliefs - Trial court found that the plaintiff was duly and validly installed as Mahant of the Dera by K in the presence of and with the approval of other Mahants - Trial Court held that the plea of fraud and undue influence raised by the defendant was not established - On those findings the suit was decreed - HC concurred with decision of Trial Court - (A) Whether K was coerced into appointing the plaintiff as his successor Mahant - Held, On the question whether K was subjected to any pressure to appoint the plaintiff as Mahant, both the Courts below had concurrently found that he was subjected to no such pressure - Finding was one of fact and SC was unable to see any ground justifying our interference with a concurrent finding of fact - Appellant submitted that the HC failed to consider the complaint said to have been made by K a few days after the installation of the plaintiff as Mahant in which he stated that he had been coerced into appointing the plaintiff as Mahant - Appellant also submitted that the evidence showed that the services of the Police had been requisitioned to pressurise K - Submission that the HC did not consider the complaint said to have been made by K was without basis since SC found that the HC did refer to the complaint - HC confirmed the finding of the Trial Court that K who was previously under the influence of A had again come under the influence of A when he made the complaint - With regard to the presence of the Police at the installation ceremony SC was of the view that the presence of the police, at the ceremony, far from advancing the appellant's case, destroyed the case that K acted under coercion - (B) Whether the appointment of the plaintiff was invalid on the ground of his not being a Chela of K - Held, in document which was executed by K and attested by all visiting Mahants the plaintiff was described as 'Sadaq Chela' of K - SC was satisfied that late Mahant Krishan Das accepted the plaintiff as his Chela and appointed him as his successor Mahant - Appeal dismissed.
