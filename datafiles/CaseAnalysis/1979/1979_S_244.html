Somappa Vamanappa Madar and Shankarappa Ravanappa Kaddi v State of Mysore

Supreme Court of India

24 April 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

P.S. Kailasam, R.S. Sarkaria

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 403; (1979) SCC (Cr) 910; (1980) 1 SCC 479; AIR 1979 SC 1831; 1979 CRLJ 1358; 1979 CRLR 499

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 302 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Two appellants and accused no. 3 were charged for offence u/s. 302 r/w s. 34 IPC of murder of deceased, had been committed by them - Trial Court found that prosecution had failed to establish guilt of accused beyond reasonable doubt and acquitted all three accused of offence which they were charged - State filed appeal before HC, which was allowed and found them guilty of offence u/s. 302 r/w s. 34 IPC and sentenced each of them to rigorous imprisonment for life - Hence, instant appeal - Whether judgment of HC was sustainable - Held, SC agree with HC that Trial Judge was not justified in coming to conclusion and HC rightly placed considerable reliance on presence of blood-stains on weapons - Evidence of prosecution witnesses was that they surrounded accused and caught hold of them and snatched their weapons - Hence, SC had taken through relevant portion of evidence of eyewitnesses and see no reason for rejecting their evidence and as observed by HC recovery of blood-stained weapons corroborates evidence of eyewitnesses - Therefore, SC had no hesitation in agreeing with reasoning and conclusion arrived at by HC and confirm conviction and sentence imposed on them - Appeal dismissed.
