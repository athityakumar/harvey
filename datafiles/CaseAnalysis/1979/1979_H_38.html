Madras Port Trust v Hymanshu International By Its Proprietor

Supreme Court of India

03 January 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Bhagwati, A.D. Koshal

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 547; (1979) 4 SCC 176; AIR 1979 SC 1144; 1985 ECR 2310; 1979 (4) E.L.T. 396; 1979 (1) SLR 757; 1979 UJ 157

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Madras Port Trust Act, s. 110 - Revocation of appeal by special leave - Respondent claimed refund of wharfage, demurrage and transit charges - Claim supported by recommendation of Asst. Collector of Customs - Challenged as barred by limitation under Act - Supreme Court refused to hear appeal and adjudicate upon plea of appellant (Madras Port Trust) that claim was barred by limitation u/s. 110 of Act - Such a plea should not ordinarily be taken by a Govt. or a public authority - Leave revoked - Appeal dismissed.
