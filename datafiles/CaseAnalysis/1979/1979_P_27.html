S. B. Noronah v Prem Kumari Khanna

Supreme Court of India

16 August 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

V.R. Krishna Iyer, P.N. Shingal

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 198; (1980) 1 SCC 52; AIR 1980 SC 193; 1979 (2) RCJ 375; 1979 (2) RCR(Rent) 455; 1979 (2) RentLR 217; [1980] 1 S.C.R. 281; 1979 UJ 756

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure; Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - Delhi Rent Control Act, 1958, s. 21 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 115 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 6,r. 2 - Tenancy - Sanction for lease for temporary period - Eviction application - Referring to lease but not stating that it was 'in writing' - (A) Effect of ommission - (B) Legality of sanction challenged by tenant - Held, application could not be treated as defective for such omission - Tenant not estopped from challenging, rent controller can also examine whether sanction for lease granted was a make believe, vitiated by fraud or collusion - Appeal dismissed.
