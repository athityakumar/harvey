Khorshed Shapoor Chenai Etc v Assistant Controller of Estate Duty

Supreme Court of India

04 December 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

V.D. Tulzapurkar, P.N. Bhagwati, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 174; (1980) 2 SCC 1; (1980) SCC (Tax) 156; AIR 1980 SC 775; 1980 (14) CTR 356; [1980] 122 ITR 21; [1980] 2 S.C.R. 315; 1980 TAXLR 303

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Bihar Land Reforms Act, 1950 - Estate Duty Act, 1953, ss. 59(a), 61 - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 11, 18 36, 53, 53(3) 59(a), 73-A - Wealth-tax Act, 1957 - Notification - Land Acquisition - Enhanced Compensation - Re-assessment - Parcels of agricultural land was acquired by Notifications by respondent/Govt. under the 1894 Act - Special Duty Collector(SDC) of Land Acquisition awarded compensation and was received by appellant's husband/deceased, when he was alive - Agricultural land acquired after appellant's husband/deceased death and compensation received by his heirs, on whom estate was devolved in equal shares - Estate duty assessment completed by respondent was not accepted by appellants and referred before Civil Court u/s.18 of the 1894 Act which enhanced compensation awarded - Respondent/Govt. challenged enhancement before HC and issued Notices for revised estate duty assessment for extra compensations awarded, which were challenged by appellants - Hence instant appeal - Whether heirs of deceased had right to sue for further compensation- Held, extra compensation together with compensation awarded could not be regarded as proper evaluation of right to receive compensation after death of deceased/appellant's husband and re-assessment made by respondent, was also liable to be quashed - Issuance of notice u/s.59(a) of 1953 Act was unsustainable in law and liable to be quashed - u/s. 61 of 1953 Act, mistake discovered by reference was not mistake apparent on record for land acquisition proceedings, hence, impugned notice was liable to be quashed - Re-assessment had to be done by respondent, within period of 3 years from date of original assessment u/s. 73 A of 1953 Act - Appeals disposed of.
