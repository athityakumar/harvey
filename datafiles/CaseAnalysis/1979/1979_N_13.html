Commissioner of Income Tax, Central, Calcutta v National Taj Traders

Supreme Court of India

27 November 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

V.D. Tulzapurkar, E.S. Venkataramiah

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 152; (1980) SCC (Tax) 124; (1980) 1 SCC 370; AIR 1980 SC 485; 1980 (14) CTR 348; [1980] 121 ITR 535; 1980 (1) SCJ 426; [1980] 2 S.C.R. 268; 1980 TAXLR 185; [1979] 2 TAXMAN 546

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s, 33 - Notice - Erroneous assessments - Territorial jurisdiction - Respondent submitted voluntary returns, inter alia, for said A/Ys along with declaration - Assessment for said years were completed on total incomes of unregistered firm consisting of partners with equal shares - Commissioner of Income-Tax issued show cause notice u/s. 33B of the Act as he felt that completed assessments were erroneous that prejudiced interests of revenue and that Income-Tax Officer had no territorial jurisdiction over case of respondent - Commissioner passed ex parte order u/s. 33B of the Act - Commissioner cancelled the assessments made by Income-Tax Officer on three grounds - In appeals preferred to Appellate Tribunal u/s. 33B(3) of the Act, Respondent challenged order of Commissioner - Tribunal concluded assumption of jurisdiction u/s. 33B of the Act by the Commissioner was justified but held that Commissioner had not conformed to requirements of natural justice by putting to respondent case it had to meet and by giving it due opportunity to explain it - Tribunal vacated Commissioner's order and remanded case with direction to dispose of it afresh after respondent was given due opportunity - Aggrieved, appellant referred questions of law to HC - HC affirmed that provision of s. 342(b) of the Act was absolute and covered revisional order of Commissioner passed in pursuance of direction given by appellate authority - Hence, instant appeal - Whether direction to dispose off case afresh could be given to Commissioner by Appellate Tribunal when period of limitation prescribed under s. 34(2)(b) had expired, and its effect of attenuating or curtailing appellate powers of Tribunal - Held, limitation prescribed was applicable only to initial order to be made by income tax Officer and not to order that was made pursuant to direction from Tribunal - Literal construction of s. 34(2)(b) would lead to manifestly absurd and anomalous results, which were not intended by - Tribunal's order that vacated Commissioner's order and directed Commissioner to make assessment afresh after due opportunity was given to respondent was proper - No order as to costs were passed - Appeal allowed.
