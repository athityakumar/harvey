State Of Tamil Nadu v R. Krishnamurthy

Supreme Court of India

15 November 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy, R.S. Sarkaria

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 90; (1980) 1 SCC 167; (1980) SCC (Cr) 200; AIR 1980 SC 538; 1980 CRLJ 402; 1980 CrLR(SC) 50; [1980] 2 S.C.R. 59

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection; Criminal; Health & Drug

Keywords: External Use

Summary: Health and Drug - Criminal - Prevention of Food Adulteration Act, 1954, s. 2(v), 2(1)(a), 16(1) - Sale punishable - Whether Gingelly oil mixed with ground-nut oil is food ? - Held, yes - It is enough if it is generally or commonly used for human consumption or in the preparation of human food - Sale of Gingelly oil mixed with groundnut oil is punishable - Gingelly oil, mixed or not with groundnut oil or some other oil is 'food' within the meaning of the definition contained in s. 2 (v) of the Act - Appeal dismissed.
