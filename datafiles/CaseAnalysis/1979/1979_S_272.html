State of Rajasthan v Gurcharandas Chadha

Supreme Court of India

06 April 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, A.D. Koshal

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 411; (1980) SCC (Cr) 208; (1980) 1 SCC 250; AIR 1979 SC 1895; 1979 CRLJ 1416; 1979 CRLR 655; 1979 UJ 887

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988 - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Revision - Judgment - Special Leave Appeal - SC - Appeal against Judgment of High Court of Rajasthan by which an application for revision filed by respondent against an order of Special Judge framing charges against respondent under various sections of Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988 and IPC, 1860 was quashed and case was sent back to Special Judge for consideration matter afresh after excluding materials which according to HC were inadmissible in evidence - Appeal - Whether in peculiar facts and circumstances of this case, discretionary powers u/art. 136 should be exercised when HC by virtue of impugned order has merely given effect to a recent decision of SC as to admissibility of certain statements? - Held, order of HC may be wrong or even without jurisdiction, but there can be no doubt that it has passed a correct and just order which is in consonance with decision of SC and is calculated to promote ends of justice - Therefore, this is not a fit case in for interfering particularly when HC has itself sent back case to Special Judge for excluding portions of statements recorded and signed by maker thereof and then decide matter afresh - Appeal disposed of.
