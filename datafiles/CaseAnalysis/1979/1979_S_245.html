Ananta Mohanta v State of Orissa

Supreme Court of India

22 March 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, A.D. Koshal

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 429; (1979) 3 SCC 423; (1979) SCC (Cr) 523; AIR 1979 SC 1433; 1979 CRLJ 1091; 1979 CRLR 352; 1979 (48) CutLT 515; 1979 UJ 436

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 3 and 30 - Murder case - Appeal u/s. 2(a) of Supreme Court (Enlargement of Criminal Appellate Jurisdiction) Act - (A) Appreciation of evidence - Only two relatives present when deceased was being attacked and was shouting names of assailants - Evidence discarded - Legality - (B) Hostile witness - Witness supporting prosecution case against his nephew in committing court - Held, their evidence cannot be discarded simply because evidence of others who came later was not recorded - His statement cannot be disbelieved simply because he is declared hostile - Appeal dismissed.
