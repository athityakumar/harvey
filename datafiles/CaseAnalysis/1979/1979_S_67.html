State of Haryana v Smt. Darshana Devi and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 February 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

V.R. Krishna Iyer, O. Chinnappa Reddy

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 477; (1979) 2 SCC 236; 1979 ACJ 205; AIR 1979 SC 855; [1979] 3 S.C.R. 184; 1979 UJ 389

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 39 A and 41 - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, O. XXXIII, r. 9 A - Motor Vehicles Act, 1939 - Whether access to justice denied by State of Haryana to an indigent person due to failure to pay court-fee is justified? - Held, no - Effective access to justice can be seen as most basic requirement, most basic human right, of a system which purports to guarantee legal right - Jurisprudence of access to justice should be expanded as an integral part of social justice and examine constitutionalism of court-fee levy as a facet of human rights highlighted in the Constitution - If State itself should travesty this basic principle, in teeth of arts. 14 and 39 A, where an indigent widow is involved, a second look at its policy is overdue - Court must give benefit of doubt against levy of a price to enter temple of justice until one day whole issue of validity of profit-making through sale of civil justice, disguised as court-fee, is fully reviewed by this Court - State of Haryana has failed to remember is its duty u/art. 41 of Constitution to render public assistance, without litigation, in cases of disablement and want - Petition dismissed.
