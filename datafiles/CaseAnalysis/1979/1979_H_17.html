K. Balakrishna Rao and Others v Hazi Abdulla Sait And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

10 October 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

E.S. Venkataramiah, A.C. Gupta

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 72; (1980) 1 SCC 321; AIR 1980 SC 214; 1980 (1) MLJ 20; 1980 (1) MLJ(SC) 20; 1980 (1) RCJ 179; 1980 (1) RCR(Rent) 374; 1979 (2) RentLR 412; [1980] 1 S.C.R. 875

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 20 r. 12, O. 9r. 9 - Limitation Act, 1963 - Madras Non-residential Buildings Rent Control order, 1946 - Madras Buildings (Lease and Rent Control) Act. 1949 - Eviction - Sustainability - Father of plaintiff leased it out in favour of defendant under lease deed dt.8-7-1940 for purpose of Running restaurant for period of 3 years w.e.f.15-7-1940 on monthly rent - Agreed period of lease expired in July, 1943 but defendant continued to be in possession of building as tenant holding over - On coming into force of 1946 order, defendant became statutory tenant of said building and fair rent in respect of it was fixed under that order in the year 1946 at Rs.1,680/p.m. - Said order was replaced by 1949 Act which was also applicable to said building - On death of petitioners/father/landlord, under partition amongst his heirs plaintiff became owner of building - Protection which defendant was enjoying under 1949 Act came to end on the passing of principal Act by virtue of s. 35 thereof which repealed 1949 Act and s. 30(iii) thereof which provided that nothing contained in principal Act was applicable to any non-residential building, rental value of which on date of commencement of principal Act as entered in property tax assessment book of municipal council, district board, panchayat or panchayat union council or Corporation of Madras exceeded Rs. 400/per mensem - Defendant, however, continued to be in possession of building by paying rent every month - Plaintiff issued notice to defendant terminating tenancy w.e.f. expiry of 29-2-1964 and as building was not governed by principal Act at that point of time - Plaintiff filed suit for eviction and for damages - Trial Court dismissed said suit - Plaintiff filed 2applications before Trial Court under O. 9 r. 9 of CPC to set aside order of dismissing suit as having abated and another u/s. 5 of 1963 Act for condoning delay in filing application, under O. 9 r. 9 of CPC - Plaintiff also filed appeal against Trial Court - Both applications were allowed by Trial Court - Defendant filed revision petitions against order of Trial Court allowing said applications - Meanwhile, owing to alteration of pecuniary jurisdiction of Trial Court, suit stood transferred to file of HC - HC proceeded to dispose of suit as if it was a fresh suit instituted after death of original defendant No. 1 - Trial Court passed decree for possession and damages for use and occupation - Quantum of damages was directed to be determined under O. 20 r. 12 of CPC - Defendants Nos. 2 to 10 were, however, allowed three years' time to deliver vacant possession of premises - Aggrieved by decree passed by Trial Court, appeals were filed - DB dismissed appeals filed by plaintiff and defendant no. 2 - Hence instant appeal - Whether suit for ejectment filed in respect of any nonresidential building or part thereof pending before any court on date on which 1960 Act was amended by 1964 Act could have been proceeded with after that date - Held, since plaintiff based his claim in the year 1973 when he made application for amendment of plaint, the date of institution of fresh suit could not be earlier than date on which application for amendment was made even if it was permissible to do so - HC passed decree for possession against defendants Nos. 2 to 10 and for damages to be determined under O. 20 r. 12 of CPC without specifying date from which damages would be payable - In absence of specification, plaintiff became entitled to claim damages under O. 20 r. 12 of CPC even from date of suit i.e. 2-3-1964 - DB affirmed that part of decree of Trial Court - Direction for payment, of mesne profits given in decree of Trial Court without specify date from which damages should be computed could not have been passel consistently with its judgment in which it was stated that suit was being treated as fresh suit - This defect, however was of a minor character - Further, there was no proceeding in eye of law rending after 10-6-1964, cause of action on basis of which relief was claimed was totally different and persons against whom relief was sought were also different - Parties could not either by consent or acquiescence confer jurisdiction on court when law had taken it away - In these circumstances, claim of plaintiff to get possession of property from defendants Nos. 2 to 10 on the ground that they were not statutory tenants, decree passed by Trial Court and Appellate Court were unsustainable - Appeal allowed.
