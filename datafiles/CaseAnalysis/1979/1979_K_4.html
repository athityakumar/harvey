Union of India and Another v K. G. Khosla and Company Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

06 March 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.V. Chandrachud, D.A. Desai, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 49; (1979) SCC (Tax) 101; (1979) 2 SCC 242; AIR 1979 SC 1160; 1979 (15) DLT 259; [1979] 3 S.C.R. 453; 1979 (43) STC 457; 1979 TAXLR 1817; 1979 (2) UJ 381

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Sales Tax; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Sales Tax - Bengal Finance (Sales Tax) Act, 1941 - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956, ss. 3 and 9(1) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 133(1)(b) - Whether sales made by respondent were made at Faridabad in course of inter-State trade as contended by State of Haryana or whether they were intra-State sales effected within Union Territory of Delhi as contended by appellant, UOI? - In order that a sale may be regarded as an inter-State sale, it is immaterial whether property in goods passes in one State or another - Question as regards nature of sale, that is, whether it is an inter-State sale or an intra-State sale, does not depend upon circumstances as to in which State property in goods passes - It may pass in either State and yet sale can be an inter-State sale - Held, High Court right in holding that sales in question are inter-State sales and that turnover of sales is assessable to sales tax under Central Sales Tax Act, 1956, at instance of sale, tax authorities, at Faridabad - Appeal dismissed.
