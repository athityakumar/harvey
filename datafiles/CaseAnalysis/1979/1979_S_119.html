Bandi Mallaiah and Others v State of Andhra Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

16 November 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy, R.S. Sarkaria

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 342; (1980) 3 SCC 136; (1980) SCC (Cr) 672; AIR 1980 SC 1160; 1980 CRLJ 914; 1980 CRLR 55; 1980 UJ 105

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Supreme Court (Enlargement of Criminal Appellate Jurisdiction) Act, 1970

Summary: Criminal - Supreme Court (Enlargement of Criminal Appellate Jurisdiction) Act, 1970 - Indian Penal Code, ss. 34 and 302 - Whether the High Court erred in convicting the appellants in view of the various infirmities in the prosecution case and that the first information report Ex. P-1 was not given at the time when it was claimed to be given? - PW 11 in his statement, recorded when he was in the hospital, did not mention A-1 or A-2 who according to the prosecution's case led the attack but mentioned three other names as the persons who led them; also PW 5, PW 8, PW 11 and PW 12 had not mentioned about the attack on the deceased by A-1 to A-3 in their statements to the police though they gave full details of the alleged attack in their evidence in court; these omissions (amounting to contradiction) shows that there was a deliberate attempt to introduce names of A-1 and A-2 into the case at a later stage; the part now attributed to A-2 in the attack on the deceased was not mentioned in the FIR or in the former statement of PW 9; further, if there was already a first information report in existence in which A-1 had been named as the person who led the attack, surely in the requisitions through which the witnesses were sent to the Hospital, the assailants would have been mentioned as 'Bandi Mallaiah and others' and not 'Golla Gopaiah and others', which throws doubt on the FIR being given at 10 p.m. - Held, at the earliest point of time the names of A-1 and A-2 did not figure as assailants and they were introduced at a later stage and it was at that point of time that Ex. P1 was brought into existence; conviction and sentences set aside - Appeal allowed.
