State of Maharashtra v Natwarlal Damodardas Soni

Supreme Court of India

04 December 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Sarkaria, O. Chinnappa Reddy

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 2; (1980) 4 SCC 669; (1981) SCC (Cr) 98; AIR 1980 SC 593; 1980 CRLJ 429; 1980 CRLR 105; 1983 (13) E.L.T. 1620; [1980] 2 S.C.R. 340; 1980 UJ 171

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Customs Act, 1962, ss. 135 (a), 135 (b) and 135 (i) - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 103 and 165 - Defence of India rules, 1962, r. 126-H(2)(d), 126-P(2)(iv) - (A) Whether illegality of search will invalidate seizure and further investigation by Custom Authorities? - Held, illegality of search will not affect validity of seizure and further investigation by Customs Authorities or validity of trial which followed -(B) Can Prosecution invoke provisions of s. 123 of Customs Act, in light of fact that seizure was made by Police? - Further held, it is trite law that even in cases where s. 123(1) of Customs Act is not attracted, prosecution can discharge its burden by establishing circumstantial evidence -(C) Whether High Court has erred in holding that Defence of India Rules, 1962 do not apply because accused/respondent had not acquired possession of these gold biscuits by purchase or otherwise within meaning of those Rules? - Further held, HC erred in adopting too narrow a construction - These provisions have to be specially construed in a manner which will suppress mischief and advance object which Legislature had in view - Appeal allowed.
