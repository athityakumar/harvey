Kanan and Others v State of Kerala

Supreme Court of India

07 March 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, A.D. Koshal

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 440; (1979) 3 SCC 319; (1979) SCC (Cr) 621; AIR 1979 SC 1127; 1979 CRLJ 919; 1979 CRLR 601

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, u/ss. 326, 149 - Dying declaration was lost - Corroborated by other evidence - Conviction - Sustainability - Eleven accused were tried by Trial Court but all of them were acquitted on finding that prosecution case was not proved - State filed appeal before HC, which after reviewing evidence came to conclusion that case against appellants was foolproof and held, circumstances of this case HC would rely on evidence of PW 3 only if it was corroborated by some other evidence - HC relied on Ex. 77 which was recorded by Magistrate It was true that original dying declaration was not produced before Court but from evidence, it was clear that original was lost and was not available - Hence, in these circumstances prosecution was entitled to give secondly evidence which consisted of statement of Magistrate as also of Head Constable who had made copy from original and testified that copy was correct one - Therefore, HC convicting three appellants u/ss. 326/149 of IPC - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order of HC was sustainable - Held, finding of HC regarding veracity of dying declaration was correct and does not call for any interference - SC satisfied that view taken by HC in convicting appellant was fully borne out by evidence on record and does not need to be set aside by SC - Appeal dismissed.
