V. S. Kuttan Pillai v Ramakrishnan and Another

Supreme Court of India

18 September 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

D.A. Desai, O. Chinnappa Reddy

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 572; (1980) SCC (Cr) 226; (1980) 1 SCC 264; 1980 (17) ACC 120; AIR 1980 SC 155; 1980 CRLJ 196; 1979 CrLR(SC) 657; 1980 MLJ 402; [1980] 1 S.C.R. 673

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Criminal Procedure Code, 1898 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, ss. 91, 93 and 94 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 403, 409, 420 and 477 - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 20(3) - Search warrant issued by Magistrate against petitioner - Petitioner contended that order to produce documents would violate art.20 being self-incriminating evidence - High Court held that provisions relating to search contained in s. 93(1) of CrPC are not hit by art. 20(3) of Constitution - Held, immunity against self-crimination extends to any incriminating evidence which accused may be compelled to give - It does not extend to cover such situation as where evidence which may have tendency to incriminate accused is being collected without in any manner compelling him or asking him to be a party to collection of evidence - Search of premises occupied by accused without accused being compelled to be a party to such search would not be violative of constitutional guarantee enshrined in art. 20(3) - A clear application of mind by Magistrate must be discernible in order granting search warrant - No justification for interfering with order of HC - Appeal dismissed.
