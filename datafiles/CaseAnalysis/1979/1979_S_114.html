Swami Saran Saksena v State Of U.P.

Supreme Court of India

11 October 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Pathak, V.R. Krishna Iyer

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 88; (1980) 1 SCC 12; (1980) SCC (L&S) 129; AIR 1980 SC 269; 1980 (40) FLR 79; 1980 LabIC 219; 1980 (1) LLJ 103; [1980] 1 S.C.R. 923; 1980 SLJ 1; 1979 (2) SLR 781; 1980 UJ 193

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Order of compulsory retirement - Sustainability - Appellant was appointed by State Govt. in November, 1954 as temporary judicial officer - State Govt. terminated his services in December, 1962 but, on representation made by him, termination order was withdrawn on 16-1-1963 - Appellant rejoined service and resumed his duties - However, by order dt.18-5-1966, appellant services were terminated again - Aggrieved by that, appellant filed writ petition - HC quashed said termination order on 8-8-1969 - Appellant was reinstated with the benefit of continuity of service - Although appellant had served for about 15 years, several representation made by him to State Govt. for his confirmation met with no response, and he continued to remain a temporary Govt. servant - On 2-8-1974 State Govt. made order compulsorily retiring appellant from service - Order purported to have been made in exercise of powers mentioned in Note I to art. 465-A of Civil Service Regulations, which provided for compulsory retirement of temporary Govt. servant - Appellant filed writ petition - HC allowed writ petition and quashed order he declared that appellant continued to remain in service - Further held that appellant was not covered by terms of art. 465-A - On appeal, DB allowed appeal and dismissed writ petition - Hence instant appeal - Appellant contended that impugned order was arbitrary inasmuch as he was allowed to cross second Efficiency Bar in June, 1973, which could only have been if his work showed distinct ability and his integrity was beyond doubt and, nothing had taken place since to justify order of compulsory retirement passed shortly thereafter - Held, although for the purpose of crossing second Efficiency Bar appellant was considered to have worked with distinct ability and with integrity beyond question yet within few months thereafter he was found so unfit as to deserve compulsory retirement - Entries in between in records pertaining to appellant need to be examined and appraised in that context - There was no evidence to show that suddenly there was such deterioration in the quality of appellant's work or integrity that he deserved to be compulsorily retired - Hence, order of compulsory retirement was set aside - Appellant would be deemed to have continued in service on date of impugned order - Appeal allowed.
