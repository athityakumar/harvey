Lingari Obulamma v L. Venkata Reddy and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 January 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, A.D. Koshal

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 516; (1979) 3 SCC 80; (1979) SCC (Cr) 654; AIR 1979 SC 848; 1979 CRLJ 849; 1979 CRLR 439; [1979] 2 S.C.R. 1019; 1979 UJ 494

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Sacred

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 148, 149, 302, 307 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 342 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Prosecution case against appellants has been fully proved as held by HC, there does not appear to be any error of law or misreading of any evidence - Hence, appellants were also convicted u/ss. 307/149 and sentenced to five years' rigorous imprisonment and u/s. 148 to two years' rigorous imprisonment - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order of HC was sustainable - Held, reliance on finding of HC which appears to have found that even though appellant was alleged to have been armed with gun he may have caused injury with lathi as he has admitted in his statement u/s. 342 CrPC - Evidence of PW proves clearly two facts against this appellant, firstly appellant before occurrence exhorted other assailants of deceased persons and injured to open assault by guns and other weapons, secondly, that this appellant was also armed with gun and there was consistent evidence of eyewitnesses that all three guns were fired though only one fire hit - Hence, mere fact that only one person was hit by gun could not exclude possibility of other guns having been fired because it may be that even though other guns were also fired their bullets did not hit anybody - Therefore, HC was fully justified in upholding his conviction and first contention put forward by counsel for appellant was overruled - Appeal dismissed.
