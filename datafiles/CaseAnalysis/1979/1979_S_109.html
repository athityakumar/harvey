Gopal Lal v State Of Rajasthan

Supreme Court of India

30 January 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, A.D. Koshal

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 283; (1979) 2 SCC 170; (1979) SCC (Cr) 401; 1979 (16) ACC 115; AIR 1979 SC 713; 1979 CRLJ 652; 1979 CrLR(SC) 255; 1979 HLR 636; 1979 MLJ 480; [1979] 2 S.C.R. 1171

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Family & Personal

Keywords: Bigamy

Summary: Criminal - Family & Personal - Hindu Marriage Act, 1955 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 494 - Bigamy - Conviction - Challenged - Appellant contracted second marriage during the subsistence of first marriage with complainant - Complainant, the first wife, filed a complaint, on the basis of which appellant was prosecuted and convicted - Conviction of the appellant u/s. 494 of IPC and sentence was upheld by HC - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, when a person contracts a second marriage after the coming into force of the Act, while the first marriage is subsisting he commits the offence of bigamy. It is essential, for the purpose of s. 17 of the Act that the marriage to which s. 494 of IPC applies on account of the provisions of the Act should have been celebrated with proper ceremonies and in due form. S. 17 of the Act requires that the marriage must be properly solemnized in the sense that the necessary ceremonies required by law or by custom must be duly performed. In the instant case, it was established that the second marriage which was performed by the appellant as a valid marriage according to the custom of the nata marriage prevalent in the Telli community to which the appellant belonged. This being so and the validity of the first marriage not having been disputed, s. 494 of IPC applies in terms and the appellant must be held to have committed the offence of bigamy as contemplated by s. 494 of IPC. Appeal dismissed.
