Krishna Kumar v Divisional Assistant Electrical Engineercentral Railway And

Supreme Court of India

17 July 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.V. Chandrachud, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, E.S. Venkataramiah

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 320; (1979) 4 SCC 289; (1980) SCC (L&S) 1; AIR 1989 SC 1912; 1979 LabIC 1314; 1980 (1) LLJ 209; [1980] 1 S.C.R. 50; 1979 SLJ 532; 1979 (2) SLR 291

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Dismissal from service, Power to pass dismissal orders

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 311(1) - Dismissal from service - Power to pass dismissal orders - Legality - Appellant (employee) was removed from service by respondent no. 1, Divisional Asst. Engineer - Aggrieved appellant filed a petition before HC, which was dismissed by DB - Aggrieved appellant filed instant appeal - Whether an Authority subordinate to appellant could remove the appellant from service - Held, appellant was appointed under an order issued by Chief Electrical Engineer and was removed from service under an order passed by Divisional Asst. Electrical Engineer - Appellant was removed from service by an authority which was subordinate in rank to that by which he was appointed - Delegation of power to make a particular appointment did not enhance or improve the hierarchical status of delegate - An Officer subordinate to another would not become his equal in rank by reason of his coming to possess some of the powers of that another - Divisional Engineer, did not cease to be subordinate in rank to Chief Electrical Engineer merely because the latter's power to make appointments to certain posts was delegated to him - Respondent no. 1 had no power to remove the appellant from service - Impugned order of removal was in patent violation of provisions of art. 311(1) of Constitution - Impugned order was set aside - Appeal allowed.
