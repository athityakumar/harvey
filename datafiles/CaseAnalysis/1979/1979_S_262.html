Chanan Singh v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

24 January 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, A.D. Koshal

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 507; (1979) 4 SCC 399; (1979) SCC (Cr) 989; AIR 1979 SC 1114; 1979 CRLJ 916; 1979 CRLR 171; 1979 UJ 346

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 136 - Appeal by special leave - Acquittal of five out of six accused by Appellate Court - But sixth convicted u/s. 304 Part of Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Finding of High Court that there was no free fight and origin of occurrence not known - It erred in upholding conviction for individual gun shot fired in self defence by reconstructing a new case - Prosecution failed to prove case beyond reasonable doubt - Held, appellant acquitted - Appeal allowed.
