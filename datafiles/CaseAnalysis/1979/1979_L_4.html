Kewal Singh v Lajwanti

Supreme Court of India

04 October 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, P.N. Shingal, A.P. Sen

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 182; (1980) 1 SCC 290; AIR 1980 SC 161; 1980 (1) RCJ 167; [1980] 1 S.C.R. 854

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Practice & Procedure; Rent Control

Keywords: Constructive

Summary: Rent Control - Civil Procedure - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, O. 2, r.2 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Delhi Rent Control Act, 1958, ss. 14, 14 A and 25 B - Suit for eviction on bonafide need - Rent Controller allowed suit - Defendant filed application for permission to defend suit on ground that amendment sought for by plaintiff was uncalled for and illegal and could not be allowed - Rent Controller rejected application and High Court dismissed revision petition - Whether second application for amendment filed by plaintiff was barred by doctrine of constructive res judicata? - Held, matter must be finally decided between parties, so when plaintiff relinquished her first cause of action defendant was no where in picture, and there being no adjudication between parties doctrine of res judicata does not apply - (B) Whether s. 25 B which lays down procedure for disposing of applications filed by landlord under provisions of ss. 14 A and 14(1)(e) are violative of art. 14 of Constitution? - Held, statute puts personal necessity of landlord as a special class requiring special treatment for quick eviction of tenant and cuts out all delays and plugs all loopholes which may cause delay in getting relief by landlord, therefore classification made by legislature is in public interest and is in complete consonance with objectives sought to be achieved - Appeal dismissed.
