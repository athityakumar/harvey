 H. H. Shri Swamiji Of Shri Admar Mutt, Etc. v The Commissioner, Hindu Religious & Charitable Endowmentsdep

Supreme Court of India

27 August 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.V. Chandrachud, V.R. Krishna Iyer, N.L. Untwalia, P.N. Shingal, A.D. Koshal

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 317; (1979) 4 SCC 642; (1980) SCC (Tax) 16; AIR 1980 SC 1; [1980] 1 S.C.R. 368

Case Digest

Subject: Trusts & Associations

Keywords: Arms Act, 1878, Bhopal Act, Hyderabad Endowments Regulations, 1940

Summary: Trust & Association - Madras Hindu Religious & Charitable Endowments Act, 1951, s. 76(1) - States Reorganisation Act, 1956, s. 119 - Civil Procedure Code, 1908 - Hyderabad Endowment Regulations, 1940 - Appellant who is Mathadhipati of Shri Admar Mutt in South Kanara District received a notice from Commissioner, Hindu Religious and Charitable Endowments, Mysore demanding payment of contribution - Appellant disputed his liability to pay contribution on ground that Commr. was not entitled to make any demand for period subsequent to November 1956, that even assuming that he had lawful authority to make demands, amount demanded was excessive bearing no relationship with services rendered by Department and that expenditure which was incurred on maintenance of office and staff of Commr. and Deputy Commr. could not wholly or in part be recovered from appellant by way of contribution u/s. 76(1) of Madras Act of 1951 - Since Commr. did not accept appellants contention, appellants filed WPs asking that demand notices be quashed as illegal - Those WPs were dismissed by HC but it has given to appellants certificates to appeal - Held, for purpose of finding whether there is a correlationship between services rendered to fee payers and the fees charged to them, it is necessary to know cost incurred for organizing and rendering services, but matters involving consideration of such a correlationship are not required to be proved by a mathematical formula What has to be seen is whether there is a fair correspondence between fee charged and cost of services rendered to fee payers as a class - Further held, in absence of any acceptable evidence showing that Department had built up large accumulations or reserves out of fees collected from various institutions and considering that services are required to be rendered to a large class of institutions consisting of major and minor institutions - It cannot come to conclusion that there is no approximation or correspondence between fees levied on appellants and services rendered to class to which they belong - Contention made by counsel for appellants is that application of Madras Act of 1951 to one district only of State of Karnataka offends against guarantee of equality contained in art. 14 of Constitution, 1950 which provides that State shall not deny to any person equality before law or equal protection of laws within territory of India - It is urged that as a result of application of Madras Act of 1951 to Mutts and temples in South Kanara District, they are required to pay fees under Act which similar institutions situated in other areas of Karnataka do not have to pay - Burden thus imposed on appellants is said to be an act of hostile discrimination and therefore unconstitutional - Madras Act of 1951, in its application to South Kanara District of Mysore, now Karnataka, does not infringe art. 14 of Constitution, 1950 - If Karnataka Legislature does not act promptly and remove inequality arising out of application of Madras Act of 1951 to District of South Kanara, Act will have to suffer a serious and successful challenge in distant future - SC expressed its hope that Govt. of Karnataka will act promptly and move an appropriate legislation - Appeal dismissed.
