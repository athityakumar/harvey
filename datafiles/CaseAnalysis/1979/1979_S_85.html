State (Delhi Administration) v Shri Gulzari Lal Tandon

Supreme Court of India

03 April 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, A.D. Koshal

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 416; (1979) 3 SCC 316; (1979) SCC (Cr) 526; AIR 1979 SC 1382; 1979 CRLJ 1057; 1979 CRLR 448

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 342 - Suspicious death - Conviction - Sustainability - Deceased was married to respondent on dt. 6- 8-1969 but was unfortunately found dead at house of respondent on morning of dt. 6-14-1969 - Thereafter parents of deceased were informed but as they suspected some foul play, they lodged FIR and After Police investigation, submitted charge sheet against respondent who was then committed to Court of Sessions and tried by Sessions Judge who convicted respondent u/s. 302, IPC and sentenced him to imprisonment for life - Respondent filed appeal before HC, which was allowed and respondent was acquitted - Aggrieved by this state filed instant appeal before SC - Whether judgment of HC was sustainable - Held, where case of prosecution rests purely on circumstantial evidence, motive undoubtedly plays important part in order to tilt scale against accused, it was also well-settled that accused could be convicted on circumstantial evidence only if every other reasonable hypothesis of guilt was completely excluded and circumstances were wholly inconsistent with innocence of accused - In instant case, prosecution had clearly fallen short of proving this fact as rightly found by HC - Therefore, SC satisfied that HC was fully justified in holding that prosecution case had not been proved beyond reasonable doubt and there could be no doubt that circumstances raise serious suspicion against respondent but suspicion however grave if may be, could not take place of proof - Appeal dismissed.
