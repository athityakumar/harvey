Superintendent and Remembrancer of Legal Affairs To Government of West Bengal v Abani Maity

Supreme Court of India

06 March 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Sarkaria, P.S. Kailasam, O. Chinnappa Reddy

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 583; (1979) 4 SCC 85; (1979) SCC (Cr) 902; AIR 1979 SC 1029; 1979 CRLJ 897; 1979 CrLR(SC) 551; 1979 MLJ 557; [1979] 3 S.C.R. 472; 1979 UJ 823

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Excise - Bengal Excise Act, 1909, ss. 46(a), 63 and 64 - Respondent convicted u/s. 46(a) of Act - Magistrate did not pass any orders for disposal of contraband Ganja, and confiscation of seized car - On application by respondent to Magistrate regarding return of car and other article seized by Excise Staff, Magistrate directed return of seized car and other articles to respondent - Whether justified? - Question regarding interpretation of ss. 63 and 64 of Act - Expressions shall be liable to confiscation and may in aforesaid provisions were intended to have a compulsive force - Court must give effect to will and inbuilt policy of Legislature as discernible from object and scheme of enactment and language employed therein - Held, scheme and language of provisions in question, as soon as on proof of conditions necessary u/s. 63, a conveyance incurs liability to confiscation, word may used in s. 64(1) acquires force of must, and Magistrate is bound to abide by either of two alternatives viz, confiscation of conveyance or imposition of fine in lieu thereof in accordance with that section - Thus, discretion of Magistrate is restricted to choice between these two alternatives - This limited discretion, also, is not to be exercised whimsically, but judicially, in a manner which will not emasculate these provisions or debilitate their potency as an instrument for suppressing mischief which legislature had in view - Further held, therefore, it was imperative for Magistrate, to pass, at conclusion of trial, in addition to conviction of respondent, an order of confiscation of car by means of which offence was committed - Appeal disposed of.
