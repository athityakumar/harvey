 P. V. Sivarajan v Union of India and Another

Supreme Court of India

11 December 1958

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Mohammad Hidayatullah, S.K. Das, Sudhi Ranjan Das

Where Reported

1958 Indlaw SC 136; AIR 1959 SC 556; 1959 CompLJ 214; [1959] 1 S.C.R. 779; [1959] Supp1 S.C.R. 779

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Trade

Keywords: Factories Act, Bombay Prohibition Act 1949, Coir Industry Act, 1953

Summary: Constitution - Labour & Industrial Law - Socio-Economic - Factories Act, 1948 - Coir Industry Act, 1953 - Constitution of India, 1950 arts. 14, 19, 19(1) and 32 - (A)Whether rr. 18, 19, 20(1)(a), 21 and 22(a) under Coir Industry Act, 1953, s. 26 are valid? - Held, Act has deliberately left it to rule-making authority to frame rules which it may regard as appropriate for regulating trade - Even if Committee had expressly recommended adoption of a qualitative, not a quantitative test, Coir Board is not bound to accept said recommendation - Central Govt. is competent to make rules contrary to recommendations of Committee - Validity of rules can be successfully challenged on grounds of inconsistency with provisions of Act or that they have been made in excess of powers conferred on rule-making authority by s. 26 of Act - No such infirmity has been established in respect of impugned rules - (B) Whether rr. 18, 19, 20(1)(a), 21 and 22(a) under Coir Industry Act (45 of 1953), s. 26 are in contravention with art. 19 of Constitution? - Further held, regulation of coir industry is done in public interest - Rule-making authority decides which test would meet requirements of public interest and what method would be most expedient in controlling industry for national good - Disqualification on grounds of non satisfaction of a test laid down cannot be treated as contravening fundamental rights under art. 19 - (1) Whether rr. 18, 19, 20(1)(a), 21 and 22(a) under Coir Industry Act, 1953, s. 26 are in contravention with art. 14 of Constitution? - Further held, classification of traders made by rr. 18 and 19 is clearly rational and is founded on an intelligible differentia in relation to object sought to be achieved by Act - Main object which rules propose to achieve is to improve anomalies and malpractices prevailing in export trade of coir commodities and to put said trade on a firm and enduring basis in interest of national economy - Rules don't contravene art. 14 - Appeal dismissed.
