K. Kamaraja Nadar v Kanju Thevar and Others. (And Connected Appeals)

Supreme Court of India

22 April 1958

Case Analysis

Bench

Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, A.K. Sarkar, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1958 Indlaw SC 187; AIR 1958 SC 687; 1958 (2) MLJ 52; 1958 (2) MLJ(SC) 52; 1958 SCJ 680; [1959] 1 S.C.R. 583

Case Digest

Subject: Election; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Contesting Candidate, Any Other Candidate, Prohibition Act

Summary: Election - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 38, 55 A, 82 and 117 - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 133(1)(c) and 226 - (A) What is exact connotation of expression 'contesting candidate' in s. 82? - Held, candidates who survive date of withdrawl of candidatures - Candidates who were included in list of validly nominated candidates and who have not withdrawn their candidatures within said period - (B) Whether a candidate whose name was included in list of contesting candidates but who retired from contest is included in that expression? - Further held, respondent who did not join as party respondents to petition and two candidates whose names had been included by returning officer in list of contesting candidates but who had subsequently retired from contest before commencement of poll were necessary parties to petition - Appeals allowed.
