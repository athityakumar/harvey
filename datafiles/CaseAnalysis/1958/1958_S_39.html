Siraj-Ul-Haq Khan and Others v Sunni Central Board of Waqf, U.P. and Others

Supreme Court of India

16 September 1958

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, A.K. Sarkar

Where Reported

1958 Indlaw SC 213; AIR 1959 SC 198; 1959 SCJ 367; [1959] 1 S.C.R. 1287

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Land & Property - United Province Muslim Wakf Act, 1936, ss. 5(1), (2)and 53 - Limitation Act, 1908, ss. 13 and 15(1) - Waqf suit against Central Board - (A) Meaning of expression 'any person interested in a waqf' used in s. 5(2) Of 1936 Act - Where a literal construction defeats object of statute and makes a part of it meaningless, it is legitimate to adopt a liberal construction that gives a meaning to entire provision and makes it effective - Held, expression 'any person interested in a waqf' used in s. 5(2) Act 1936 means any person interested in a transaction that is held to be waqf by Commr. of Waqfs appointed under Act and as such appellants fell within that category - (B) Application of s. 15 of 1908 Act - Rules of limitation are arbitrary in nature and in construing them it is not permissible to import equitable considerations, and effect must be given to strict grammatical meaning of the words used - Further held, s. 15 of Limitation Act, 1908 can be attracted only where a suit has been stayed by an injunction or order and test would be whether its institution would or would not be an act in contempt of court's order and since order of court in earlier suit was neither an injunction nor an order of nature contemplated by s. 15 Of Limitation Act, that section was inapplicable and further held that Offerings made from time to time by devotees visiting Dargah Sharif were, by their very nature an income of Dargah, and failure to mention them in notification u/s. 5(1) Of Act, did not render notification defective - Appeal dismissed.
