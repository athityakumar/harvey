 State of Mysore and Another v Mysore Spinning and Manufacturing Company

Supreme Court of India

11 February 1958

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhi Ranjan Das, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, S.K. Das, A.K. Sarkar, Vivian Bose

Where Reported

1958 Indlaw SC 200; AIR 1958 SC 1002; 1958 (9) STC 188

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Sales Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Constitution of India,1950, art. 286(1)(b) - Contract Act, 1872, s. 182 - Appellant sought to impose a sales tax or certain sales made by two respondents - Sales made in course of export - Exemption u/art. 286(1)(b) of Constitution - (A) Whether sales preceding one that occasions export are taxable? - Held, all sales that precede one that occasions export are taxable - Sales made for purpose of export are not protected unless they themselves occasion export - (A1)Whether sale made in course of export are non taxable and exempted u/art. 286(1)(b)? - Further held, sale made in course of export is not exempted from taxation as export control order itself prescribes course of export - Export only through licensed exporters - Directing all goods to specifically marked and packed - Right to sales tax given to States - (A2) Whether sale made by a person through export license would make latter an agent of seller? - Further held, Where sales were sales to export - License holders in India and not to overseas buyers directly as sellers were obliged to follow procedure as under export control order - Clearly not a case of agency because a principal does not sell to his agent and even if this was only way in which an export could be effected, that would not make exporter agent of seller because, by very act of purchase, exporter became a principal and bought as such - Only kind of agency that will serve sellers is an agent as understood in law of principal and agent and not an agency in its wider connotation - Case remitted to High Court.
