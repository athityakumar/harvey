Hanskumar Kishanchand v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

22 August 1958

Case Analysis

Bench

T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, A.K. Sarkar

Where Reported

1958 Indlaw SC 145; AIR 1958 SC 947; [1959] 1 S.C.R. 1177

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Civil Procedure

Keywords: Calcutta Improvement Act, 1911

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Civil Procedure - Arbitration Act, 1940, s.19 (1)(b) - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, ss. 109 and 110 - Defence of India Act, 1939, s. 19(1)(f) - Maintainability of appeal - Whether decision sought to be appealed against was an award and not a judgment of court and was, therefore, not appealable? - A proceeding which is at inception an arbitration proceeding must retain its character as arbitration, even when it is taken up in appeal - Held, appeal u/s. 19(1)(f) is an arbitration proceeding - Further maintained decision of High Court in appeal under that provision is not a judgment, decree or order and therefore, present appeals are incompetent - Appeal dismissed.
