 Moran Mar Basselios Catholicos v Thukalan Paulo Avira and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 September 1958

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhi Ranjan Das, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, K. Subba Rao, Kailas Nath Wanchoo

Where Reported

1958 Indlaw SC 204; AIR 1959 SC 31; 1958 ILR(Ker) 1340; 1958 KerLJ 874; 1958 KLT 721

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Aliens

Summary: Family & Personal - Indian Evidence Act, 1872 - Constitution Of India, 1950 - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 11, O. 1, r. 8, O. 6, r. 2 - (A) Whether in case of a decree passed before commencement of Constitution appeal u/art. 133 is maintainable? Held, objection not seriously pressed and would, at any rate, not affect maintainability of art. 32 petition - Hence, rejected - (B) Whether plaintiffs are entitled to a declaration of their own title as trustees and for possession of trust properties? - Held, in such a suit if plaintiffs are to succeed they must do so on strength of their own title - Plaintiffs base their title to trusteeship on their election at a meeting of churches alleged when original plaintiff is said to have been elected Malankara Metropolitan and plaintiffs 2 and 3 as Kathanar and lay trustees - That meeting was admittedly held without any notice to members of Catholicos party, for they were, quite erroneously regarded as having gone out of church therefore, election of plaintiffs was not valid and their suit, in so far as it is in nature of a suit for ejectment, must fail for want of their title as trustees - (C) Whether question of burden of proof at end of case, when both parties have adduced their evidence is important? - Further held, question of burden of proof at end of case, when both parties have adduced their evidence is not of very great importance and court has to come to a decision on a consideration of all materials - Further although in cause title or in body of plaint plaintiffs do not claim to have instituted suit for themselves and on behalf of all other members of community proceedings were taken under provisions of Travancore CPC corresponding to O. I, r. 8 of CPC, 1908 - Therefore this appeal is decided on basis that plaintiffs were entitled to maintain this suit as members of Malankara Jacobite Syrian Christian community on behalf of all members of said community - Therefore decision in that interpleader suit is binding on all members of Malankara Jacobite Syrian Christian community - Therefore res judicata applicable to matters in issue in this suit, if other conditions for its application are satisfied - (D) Whether defendants had become heretics or aliens to Church or had voluntarily gone out of church only by reason of certain conduct? - Further held, it is now impossible to permit plaintiff-respondent to go outside pleadings and set up a new case that supremacy of Patriarch has been taken away by mere fact of adoption of new Constitution or by any particular clause there of other than those relating to matters specifically referred to in pleadings - Case in present suit and that charges founded on those specific acts and conduct concluded by final judgment of High Court of Travancore in interpleader suit which operates as 'res judicata' - Petition dismissed.
