 C. K. Achuthan v State of Kerala and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 December 1958

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, Sudhi Ranjan Das, S.K. Das, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo

Where Reported

1958 Indlaw SC 85; AIR 1959 SC 490; 1959 KerLJ 103; 1959 KLT 1; 1959 (1) MLJ 164; 1959 (1) MLJ(SC) 164; [1959] 1 S.C.R. 787; [1959] Supp1 S.C.R. 787

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 16(1), 19(1), 31, 32 and 226 - (A) Whether a person can claim protection of art. 14 for a breach of contract by Govt. when a tender is awarded to another person? Held, no - Choice of person to fulfill a particular contract must be left with Govt. - A contract with Govt. is same as contract with a private party - Aggrieved person may sue for damages or specific performance but not for deprivation of fundamental rights - (B) Whether in circumstances protection under art. 16(1) is available? - As art. 16(1) is applicable only in cases of employees of state working under a contract of service and not to independent contractors who are under a contract for service - Petition dismissed.
