Mahant Ramdhan Puri v Bankey Bihari Saran and Others

Supreme Court of India

23 May 1958

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, A.K. Sarkar, Vivian Bose

Where Reported

1958 Indlaw SC 181; AIR 1958 SC 941; 1959 (1) MLJ 53; 1959 (1) MLJ(SC) 53; [1959] 1 S.C.R. 1085

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Constitution Of India, 1950 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882 - Suit for redemption - (A) Whether transaction is a lease as contended by contesting respondents? - Held, only guiding rule that can be extracted from cases on subject is that intention of parties must be looked into and that once you get a debt with security of land for its redemption, then arrangement is a mortgage by whatever name it is called - From recitals in document, it emerges that there was a relationship of creditor and debtor between parties and property was given as security for payment of amount advanced with interest - Whatever ambiguity there might be in recitals is dispelled by unambiguous declaration made by parties that property was given as security for loan and document was executed as a mortgage - List of document was not a letting of premises, with a rent reserved, but a mortgage of premises with a small portion of income of it made payable to plaintiff - There is, therefore, no scope for argument in this case that document is a lease and not a mortgage - (B) Whether s. 77 of Transfer of Property Act applies to this document? - Further held, whether transaction is a usufructuary mortgage or an anomalous mortgage, in circumstances of case, there will not be any difference in matter of rendition of accounts, for in ultimate analysis, true construction of relevant terms of document would afford an answer to question raised - In view of clearly expressed intention of parties, it cannot hold, from mere fact that rate of interest is mentioned, that document does not come under purview of s. 77 - There is a contract between mortgagee and mortgagor within meaning of s. 77, to effect that receipts from mortgaged property should be taken in lieu of interest - Court hold that s.77 applies to document and therefore mortgagee is not liable to render any account to mortgagor - Not only there is such a recital but there is a specific term whereunder mortgagor expressly agreed not to claim any produce received by mortgagee - Whether s. 77 applies or not, under express terms of contract, appellant is not liable to render accounts for excess receipts - Appeal allowed.
