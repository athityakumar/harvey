S. B. Adityan v S. Kandaswami and Others

Supreme Court of India

30 April 1958

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sarkar

Where Reported

1958 Indlaw SC 52; AIR 1958 SC 857; 1958 SCJ 1072; [1959] 1 S.C.R. 868

Case Digest

Subject: Election; Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Allegations Of Corrupt Practice

Summary: Election - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 81, 82, 90(3), 117 and 123 - Allegations of corrupt practice - Bribery - Whether allegations of corrupt practice are made against them in election petition ? - Held, no - Definition makes corrupt practice of bribery is a gift, offer or promise by a candidate or his agent or by any other person, of any gratification made with object mentioned - That s. 123(1) does not contemplate acceptance of a gift to be a corrupt practice - To say that a candidate consented to a corrupt practice being committed by accepting a gift made to him to induce him to withdraw his candidature, is not to say that he himself committed a corrupt practice - Appeal dismissed.
