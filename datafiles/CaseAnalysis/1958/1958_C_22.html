Badridas Daga v Commissioner of Income Tax

Supreme Court of India

25 April 1958

Case Analysis

Bench

T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, A.K. Sarkar

Where Reported

1958 Indlaw SC 111; AIR 1958 SC 783; [1958] 34 ITR 10; 1958 (2) MLJ(SC) 168; 1958 SCJ 968; [1959] 1 S.C.R. 690

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Embezzlement By Employee

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income-tax Act, 1922, ss. 10(1), 10(2)(Xi), 10(2)(XV) -Withdrew moneys - Personal debts - Deduction - Misappropriation by employee - Appellant/agent for purposes of carrying on his business and large powers of management including authority to operate on bank accounts - While acting under such authority agent withdrew moneys from bank and used them for discharge of his personal debts - Appellant was able to recover from agent only a part of amount misappropriated by him, and balance had to be written off at end of accounting year as irrecoverable - Whether amount was misappropriated and irrecoverable was allowable as deduction under Indian Income-tax Act in determining profits of appellant - Held, that amount was not allowable either as a bad debt u/s. 10(2)(Xi) or as business expenditure u/ss.10(2)(XV) of 1922 Act - It can be deducted in computing profits of appellant u/s. 10(1) of Act as loss incidental to carrying on of his business - Where an agent or an employee of businessman in charge of business was given - Operate on bank accounts and withdraws moneys in purported exercise of that authority - Action was referable to his character as such authorized agent or employee - Any loss resulting from misappropriation of money by him would be one incidental to carrying on business - It was not necessary to show that money was withdrawn for conduct of business - Appeal allowed.
