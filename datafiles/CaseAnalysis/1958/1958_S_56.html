 Smt. Shantabai v State of Bombay and Others

Supreme Court of India

24 March 1958

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhi Ranjan Das, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, S.K. Das, A.K. Sarkar, Vivian Bose

Where Reported

1958 Indlaw SC 32; AIR 1958 SC 532; 1958 SCJ 1078; [1959] 1 S.C.R. 265

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Madhya Pradesh Abolition of proprietary Rights (Estates, Mahals, Alienated Lands) Act, 1950, Central Provinces Land Revenue Act

Summary: Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 32 and 19(1)(f), (g) - Transfer Of Property Act, 1882, s. 3 - Madhya Pradesh Abolition of Proprietary Rights (Estates, Mahals, Alienated Lands) Act, 1950, s. 3 - Maintainability of Petition u/art. 32 - Regarding setting aside order made by respondent No. 3 - (A) Grievance of Petitioner is that offending order has infringed her fundamental right u/arts. 19(1)(f) and 19(1)(g), 31(1) and 32? - State is not a party to contract and is not bound by contract and accordingly acknowledges no liability under contract which being purely personal does not run with land - If petitioner maintains that, by some process not quite apparent, State is also bound by that contract, even then she, as owner of that contract, can only seek to enforce contract in ordinary way and sue State if she be so advised, as to which we say nothing, and claim whatever damages or compensation she may be entitled to for alleged breach of it - Held, for reasons stated above, whatever rights, if any, may have accrued to petitioner under that document on any of several interpretations, petitioner cannot complain of infringement by State of any fundamental right for enforcement of which alone a petition u/art. 32 is maintainable - (B) Difference between standing timber and a tree? - Held, 'Standing timber' must be a tree that is in a state fit for these purposes and, further, a tree that is meant to be converted into timber so shortly that it can already be looked upon as timber for all practical purposes even though it is still standing - Petition dismissed.
