Nanni Bai and Others v Gita Bai

Supreme Court of India

14 April 1958

Case Analysis

Bench

Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Syed Jaffer Imam, K. Subba Rao

Where Reported

1958 Indlaw SC 44; AIR 1958 SC 706; 1958 SCJ 925; [1959] 1 S.C.R. 479

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land &

Property - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 22, r.4, O 34, r.1 - Registration Act, 1908, ss. 17 (1) (b) and 49 - Sangli State Agriculturists Protection Act I, 1936 - Limitation Act, arts. 12, 134 and 148 - Suit on granting certain reliefs from indebtedness to agriculturists of that State which was then outside what used to be called 'British India' - Whether deed declaring allotment of property is admissible as partition deed ? - Held, if document does not evidence any partition by metes and bounds, it does not come within purview of s. 17(1)(b) of Registration Act and would, therefore, not come within mischief of s. 49 which prohibits reception into evidence of any document 'affecting immoveable property' and, therefore, those documents have rightly been received in evidence for limited purpose - Appeal allowed.
