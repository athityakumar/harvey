Erin Estate v Commissioner of Income Tax, Madras

Supreme Court of India

22 April 1958

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, A.K. Sarkar, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar

Where Reported

1958 Indlaw SC 109; AIR 1958 SC 779; [1958] 34 ITR 1; 1958 (2) MLJ(SC) 145; 1958 SCJ 912; [1959] 1 S.C.R. 573

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Trusts & Associations - Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 66 A, r.4 A - Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 101, 102, 103 and 104 - Partnership Act, 1932, s.12 - (A) Whether assessee is a resident firm in taxable territories or not ? - Held, normal presumption would be that firm is a resident in taxable territories where partners of a firm are resident of country - Presumption rebuttable - Onus to rebut lies on assessee - (1) Whether status of assessee be determined by power of control and management vested?- Further held, once it is shown that control and management in affairs of firm was exercised by partners residing in India status of assessee will be determined - Exercise of control and management even in part in taxable territories would be enough to fix assessee with character of a resident within - (2) Whether or not there is evidence that control and management has been exercised by majority of resident firm? - Further held, usual presumption is that control and management would be exercised by majority - It is however not correct to say that there is no evidence of majority control and management exercised - Appeal dismissed.
