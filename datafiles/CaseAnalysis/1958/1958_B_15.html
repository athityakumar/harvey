Santosh Kumar v Bhai Mool Singh

Supreme Court of India

05 February 1958

Case Analysis

Bench

Vivian Bose, Sudhi Ranjan Das, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, A.K. Sarkar

Where Reported

1958 Indlaw SC 53; AIR 1958 SC 321; 1958 (1) AnWR 159; 1958 (1) MLJ(SC) 159; 1958 SCJ 434; [1958] 1 S.C.R. 1211

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 37, rr. 2 and 3 - Plaintiff filed suit for dishonour of cheque - Trial judge permitted defence of suit on condition giving security - Review and appeal u/art. 227 failed - Whether leave to defend must be granted on triable issues? - Held, court if satisfied, that facts alleged by defendant are duly proved and will afford a good, or even a plausible, answer to plaintiff's claim then it cannot withhold leave or impose conditions - Appeal is allowed.
