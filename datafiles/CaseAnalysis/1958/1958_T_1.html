 Sewpujanrai Indrasanarai Limited v Collector of Customs and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 May 1958

Case Analysis

Bench

S.K. Das, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, K. Subba Rao, Vivian Bose

Where Reported

1958 Indlaw SC 70; AIR 1958 SC 845; 1958 CRLJ 1355; 1983 ECR 1701; 1983 (13) E.L.T. 1305; 1958 SCJ 1199; [1959] 1 S.C.R. 821

Case Digest

Subject: Customs

Keywords: Person Concerned, Importation, Customs Act, Sea Customs Act 1878, Bombay Prohibition Act 1949

Summary: Customs - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 5, 19 and 226 - Sea Customs Act, 1978, s. 167 - Foreign Exchange Regulation Act, 1947, ss. 8(3) and 23 - Sea Customs Act, ss. 88, 167(8), 182 and 183 - Foreign company - Smuggling of Gold - Order of confiscation or penalty under Sea Customs Act - Invalid conditions imposed for release of gold on payment of fine in lieu of confiscation - Points under consideration - Validity of order - Writs of certiorari, Prohibition - When lies - Fundamental rights of Foreign Co. - FERA, 1947, ss. 8(3) and 23 and Sea Customs Act, s. 167(8) under examine - Separation of conditions from rest of order - Confiscation without prejudice to provisions of sea Customs Act - Prohibition order issued - Distinction drawn between an action in rem and a proceeding in personam u/s. 23 and s. 167(8) - Appeal partly allowed.
