Mizaji and Another v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

18 December 1958

Case Analysis

Bench

J.L. Kapur, Syed Jaffer Imam, S.K. Das

Where Reported

1958 Indlaw SC 129; AIR 1959 SC 572; 1959 ALJ 408; 1959 CRLJ 777; [1959] 1 S.C.R. 940; [1959] Supp1 S.C.R. 940

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure 1898

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Land & Property - Criminal - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1898, ss. 87 and 88 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 147, 148, 149 and 302 r/w 149 - Whether an offence committed in prosecution of common object must be one which is pre-concerted in sense of a meeting of members of unlawful assembly as to common object or not ? - Held, no - A pre- concerted meeting is not necessary - It is enough if it is adopted by all members and is shared by all of them - An offence which members knew was likely to be committed, even if offence committed is not in direct prosecution of common object of assembly, may fall under s. 149 - Extent of preparation for achieving common objective depends upon conduct as a whole - Appeal dismissed.
