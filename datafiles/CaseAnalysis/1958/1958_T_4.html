Maharaj Kumar Kamal Singh v Commissioner of Income Tax, Bihar and Orissa

Supreme Court of India

01 October 1958

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, A.K. Sarkar

Where Reported

1958 Indlaw SC 144; AIR 1959 SC 257; 1959 (38) ILR(Pat) 369; [1959] 35 ITR 1; 1959 (1) MLJ(SC) 92; [1959] 1 S.C.R. 10; [1959] Supp1 S.C.R. 10

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Interest On Arrears

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 34, 19 A, 20 A, 22(3), 28, 33 B and 35 - Whether assessment order u/s. 34 of interest on arrears of rent is legal, subsequent to decision of case? - Two conditions must be satisfied before ITO can act u/s. 34 (1)(b) - Word 'information' in s. 34(1)(b) includes information as to relevant judicial decisions - Held, assessment of interest on arrears of rent u/s. 34 was justfied as subsequent decision on case was considered as 'information' that came to ITO subsequent to making of assessment order and on basis of this, he concluded that income chargeable to tax has escaped assessment - Appeal dismissed.
