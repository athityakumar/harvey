Dr. S. B. Dutt v University of Delhi

Supreme Court of India

03 September 1958

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sarkar, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar

Where Reported

1958 Indlaw SC 202; AIR 1958 SC 1050; [1959] 1 S.C.R. 1236

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Corporate; Education; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Delhi University Act, 1922, s. 45 - Government of India Act, 1935 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Specific Relief Act - Arbitration Act, 1940 - (A) Whether specific enforcement of a contract of personal service which was forbidden by s. 21(b) of Specific Relief Act and therefore disclosed an error on face of arbitration award? - Held, there is no doubt that a contract of personal service cannot be specifically enforced u/s 21, cl. (b) of Specific Relief Act, 1877 - (B) Whether an erroneous arbitration award that contains erroneous legal proposition be set aside on this ground or is it a mere surplusage and is severable? - Further held, appeal must be dismissed for reason that award contains an error on face of it - Appeal dismissed.
