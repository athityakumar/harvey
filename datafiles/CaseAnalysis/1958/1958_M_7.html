Madhavrao Narayanrao Patwardhan v Ramkrishna Govind Bhanu and Others. (With

Supreme Court of India

18 April 1958

Case Analysis

Bench

Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Syed Jaffer Imam, K. Subba Rao

Where Reported

1958 Indlaw SC 188; AIR 1958 SC 767; 1958 (2) MLJ 164; 1958 (2) MLJ(SC) 164; 1958 SCJ 963; [1959] 1 S.C.R. 564

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Limitation Act 1908, s. 14 - Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 101, 102, 103 and 104 - Plaintiff/respondent had instituted a suit on very last day of limitation, in Munsiff's Court at Miraj - This suit was registered as Original Suit in that court - Plaintiff prayed for possession and mesne profits in respect of lands at Malgaon and Takli, on ground that, then the State of Miraj had wrongfully resumed those lands in 1910 -(A)Whether instituting a suit and carrying on proceedings in wrong court plaintiff had acted in due care and attention? - Since limitation act has its own definition question should be examined in light of that definition that is whether plaintiff acted with due care and attention- Held, in view of fact that in both suits plaintiff himself drew up plaint and presented them in Court and inasmuch as he had given value of property in other suit it was for him to adduce reasons as to why he failed to give value of property in suit in question or waited such a long time in absence of such reasons it could not be said that he had acted with care and caution - (B) Whether burden of proof was on plaintiff that he acted with due care and attention? - Further held, burden of bringing his case within meaning of s. 14 of Limitation Act, 1908 lays upon plaintiff to prove that he acted with due care and attention - Petition dismissed.
