Commissioner of Income Tax, Bihar and Orissa v Sri Ramakrishna Deo

Supreme Court of India

14 October 1958

Case Analysis

Bench

T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, A.K. Sarkar

Where Reported

1958 Indlaw SC 79; AIR 1959 SC 239; [1959] 35 ITR 312; 1959 (1) MLJ(SC) 142; [1959] 1 S.C.R. 176; [1959] Supp1 S.C.R. 176

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 4(3)(viii)and 66(1) - (A) Whether income received by sale of trees growing in forests is agricultural income exempt from tax? - Held, crucial fact to be determined is that trees must be planted by proprietors of estate and must not be of spontaneous growth - Certain basic operations must be carried out by assessee - Mere performance of subsequent operations will render income to be regarded as agricultural income - Appeal allowed.
