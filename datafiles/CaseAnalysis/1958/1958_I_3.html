 A. N. Lakshmana Shenoy v Income Tax Officer, Ernakulam and Another

Supreme Court of India

28 April 1958

Case Analysis

Bench

S.K. Das, Sudhi Ranjan Das, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, A.K. Sarkar, Vivian Bose

Where Reported

1958 Indlaw SC 147; AIR 1958 SC 795; [1958] 34 ITR 275; 1958 KerLJ 813; 1958 SCJ 1023; [1959] 1 S.C.R. 751

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Definite Information, Indian Independence Act, 1947

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922 - Finance Act, 1950, s.13 (1) - Jurisdiction to Impose Tax - Assessee carried on its trade and business in the then States of Travancore and Cochin and was assessed to income-tax in both States under income-tax law in force there namely Cochin Income-tax Act and Travancore Income-tax Act - Appellant appealed to HC that respondent did not possess jurisdiction to impose Tax but HC rejected appellant contention - Hence, instant appeals - The instant court along with instant appeal disposed off another set of appeal in which by reason of the circumstances, common questions of law and fact had arisen - Whether, Income-tax authorities had rightly assessed the assessee firm - Held, word 'assessment' was used in more than one sense in Income-tax law and so far as s. 13 (1) of the 1950 Act was concerned there was no doubt that expression that, 'levy assessment and collection of income-tax', had been used in a comprehensive sense so as to include whole procedure for imposing liability upon taxpayer i.e. assessee - Hence, instant set of appeals in which assessee was appellant was dismissed and the set of appeals in which Income-tax authorities were appellants was allowed - Appeals disposed of.
