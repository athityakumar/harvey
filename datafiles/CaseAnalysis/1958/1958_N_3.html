 Nagpur Electric Light and Powerco., Limited and others v K. Shreepathirao

Supreme Court of India

11 April 1958

Case Analysis

Bench

S.K. Das, Sudhi Ranjan Das, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Vivian Bose

Where Reported

1958 Indlaw SC 149; AIR 1958 SC 658; 1958 (2) LLJ 9; 1958 SCJ 1087; [1959] 1 S.C.R. 463

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Attorney-General

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Industrial Employment (Standing Orders) Act, 1946, standing order no 16 - Termination from service without disclosing reasons - Whether Standing Orders of Company apply to respondent? - Held, name and ticket number in departmental muster was an essential characteristic of an employee - Mere fact of employment in Office, Mains Department, Stores, Power House or Receiving Station of Company was not enough to make a, person so employed an 'employee' - Respondent did not fulfil necessary condition of having his name and ticket number included in departmental muster, he was not an 'employee' as defined for Standing Orders - Company chose to terminate service of respondent in accordance with Standing Order and did not think fit to proceed against respondent for any alleged misconduct, and it was open to Company to do so - Appeal allowed.
