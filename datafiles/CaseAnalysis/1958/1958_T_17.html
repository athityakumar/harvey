Ratan Gond v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

19 September 1958

Case Analysis

Bench

S.K. Das, Syed Jaffer Imam, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1958 Indlaw SC 74; AIR 1959 SC 18; 1959 CRLJ 108; [1959] 1 S.C.R. 1336

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Keywords: Evidence Act, Indian Evidence Act, Code of Criminal Procedure 1898, Bihar Panchayat Raj Act, 1948

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Constitution - Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 24, 32 and 33 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - (A) Whether confession made by deceased was admissible u/s. 33 of Evidence Act, 1872 or not? - Held, deceased A made certain statement about B to her mother - No statement was made in a judicial proceeding or before any person authorized by law to take her evidence, hence confession made by A does not attract s. 33 of Act - (1) Whether confession made by deceased A is admissible under s. 32 of Evidence Act 1872 or not? Further held, no - S. 32 cl. 1 which related to statements made by a person as to cause of his death or as to any of circumstances of transaction which resulted in his death - Statements made by A did not relate to cause of her death or to any of circumstances relating to her death, rather it related to death of her sister and not her own death - Statements did not come within s. 32(1) of Act - (B) Whether appeal filed by special leave by appellant was maintainable u/art. 136 of Constitution or not? - Further held, art. 136 of Constitution is not normally open to appellant to raise questions of fact or to ask for interference with concurrent findings of fact, unless findings are vitiated by errors of law or conclusions reached by courts below are so patently opposed to well established principles - Special leave was granted to appellant - (C) Whether statements made by appellant for murder of deceased B amounts to extra judicial confession u/s. 24 of Evidence Act or not? - Further held, as a matter of caution courts require some material corroboration to an extra-judicial confessional statement, corroboration which connects accused person with crime - (1) Whether confession made by appellant u/s. 24 of Evidence Act is a voluntary confession or not? - Further held, extra-judicial confession was voluntary and it did not appear to have been caused by any inducement, threat or promise having reference to charge made against he appellant - Attracts s. 24 of Act - Appeal dismissed.
