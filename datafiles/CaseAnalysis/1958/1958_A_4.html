Commissioner of Income Tax, Bombay v Amritlal Bhogilal and Company

Supreme Court of India

28 April 1958

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, A.K. Sarkar, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar

Where Reported

1958 Indlaw SC 97; AIR 1958 SC 868; [1958] 34 ITR 130; 1958 (2) MLJ(SC) 174; 1958 SCJ 1007; [1959] 1 S.C.R. 713

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Tax - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 23(3), 23(5), 26A, 23(6), 31(3) and 33B - Registered firm - Assessment - Total income - Validity - Respondent/assessee registered as a firm u/s. 26A of Act for year 1946-47 - For assessment years 1947-48, 1948-49 and 1949-50, Income-tax Officer made assessment on respondent and respectively under s. 23(3) of Act - An estimate about profits of respondent under the proviso to s. 13 of the Act and computed total income of respondent at Rs. 95,053, Rs. 93,430 and Rs. 83,752 for the years - Respondent applied for and obtained renewal of registration of firm - Income-tax Officer had also passed an order u/s. 23(6) of Act and allocated shares of various parties - Against assessment orders respondent preferred an appeal to Appellate Assistant Commissioner - Reduced respondent's estimated profit by Rs. 28,250 assessment year 1947-48 and by Rs. 19,000 in assessment year 1948-49 - Respondent's appeal in regard to the assessment year 1949-50 was pending before Appellate Assistant Commissioner - Commissioner took action u/s. 33B(1) of Act and issued notice to respondent to show cause why the assessments made u/s. 23(3) of Act and registration granted u/s. 26A of the Act should not be cancelled - Commissioner passed an order u/s. 33B(1) of the Act on 5-6-1951 by which commissioner cancelled the registration of firm u/s. 26A and directed Income-tax Officer to make fresh assessments against respondent as an unregistered firm for all three years - As a result of this revisional order passed by Commissioner of Income-tax passed fresh orders - Whether validity of procedure adopted by Income-tax Officer in passing fresh orders against respondent - Held, Commissioner had authority u/s. 33B(1) of the Act to set aside the orders of registration made by Income-tax Officer - An order of Income-tax Officer granting registration was not appealable before Appellate Assistant Commissioner - Registration or non-registration of firm does not at all affect computation of taxable income - It merely governs procedure to be adopted in recovering tax found due - Income-tax Officer merely proceeded to adopt a different machinery to recover tax due from respondent in consequence of cancellation of respondent's registration, there was no occasion or need to issue another notice against respondent - Appeal allowed.
