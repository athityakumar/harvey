Kochu Govindan Kaimal and Others v Thayankoot Thekkot Lakshmi Amma and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 October 1958

Case Analysis

Bench

T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, A.K. Sarkar

Where Reported

1958 Indlaw SC 18; AIR 1959 SC 71; 1959 KLT 9; 1959 (1) MLJ 44; 1959 (1) MLJ(SC) 44; [1959] Supp1 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Summary: Family & Personal - Practice & Procedure - Succession Act, 1925, s. 74 - Whether will executed by executants is a mutual will or separate will? - Held, under will all three testators did not become joint owners of all properties on which it operated - There were three executants of will - Each of them possessed properties, which were his or her self-acquisitions, they also owned some properties which they had jointly acquired, but their title to such properties was as tenants-in-common and not as joint tenants - It did not confer any rights inter se on testators, it only vested title to properties disposed of by it in legatees on death of testators - Will must be held to be a testamentary disposition by three testators of their properties operating on death of each testator on his properties, and was in effect, three wills combined in one - Where legatees are distinct from testators, there can be no question of a mutual will - Appeal disposed of.
