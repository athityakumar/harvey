Special Land Acquisition Officer, Bangalore v T. Adinarayan Setty

Supreme Court of India

07 November 1958

Case Analysis

Bench

S.K. Das, Syed Jaffer Imam, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1958 Indlaw SC 125; AIR 1959 SC 429; 1959 CRLJ 526; [1959] 1 S.C.R. 404; [1959] Supp1 S.C.R. 404

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, s. 26(2) - (A) Whether Supreme Court will look into each and every question regarding valuation of compensation under Land Acquisition Act, 1894? - Held, SC will interfere only if there is something to show that judgment cannot be supported by reason of a wrong application of principle or because some important point affecting valuation has been overlooked or misapplied - (B) Whether valuation of land acquired under Act is affected by extraneous considerations? - Further held, extraneous considerations such as purpose for which land was acquired, report of certain authorities as to unsuitability of land for purpose for which it was acquired and delay in putting land to use for which it was acquired have no bearing on valuation of land so acquired - Courts should not consider such factors - (C) Whether court can exercise its discretion in assessing compensation under Act on basis of price paid within reasonable time in transactions of sale of adjacent lands? - Further held, compensation is to be assessed on basis of market value of land acquired at date of notification under s. 4(1) - One of methods of determining compensation is on basis of price paid, within reasonable time - Arbitrary selection of such transactions by court, without giving reasons for same vitiates findings - Appeal partly allowed.
