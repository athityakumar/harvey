 Pandit M. S. M. Sharma v Shri Sri Krishna Sinha and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 December 1958

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhi Ranjan Das, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, K. Subba Rao, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati

Where Reported

1958 Indlaw SC 135; AIR 1959 SC 395; 1959 (2) MLJ(SC) 125; [1959] 1 S.C.R. 806; [1959] Supp1 S.C.R. 806

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Freedom Of Speech And Expression, Private Party, Speech And Expression, Bombay Prohibition Act 1949

Summary: Media & Entertainment - Foreigners - Constitution - Municipalities & Local Governments - Constitution of India, 1950 arts. 19(1)(a), 19(2), 194(1) and 194(3) - (A)Whether a non citizen is entitled to freedom of speech and expression under art. 19(1) (a) of Constitution ? Held, no - Freedom of speech and expression is conferred only on a citizen - (B) Whether freedom of speech referred to in art. 194 be cut down in any way by any law contemplated by art. 19(2)? - Art. 194 is not subject to art. 19 - Provisions of art. 19(1)(a), which are general, must yield to art. 194(1) and latter part of art. 194(3) which are special - (C) On whom does burden of proof lie in case of conflict between privilege claimed by respondents and fundamental rights of citizens? - Further held, where privilege claimed by respondents is in derogation of fundamental right of a citizen, burden lies heavily upon them to establish by clear and unequivocal evidence that House of Commons possessed such a privilege - Petition dismissed.
