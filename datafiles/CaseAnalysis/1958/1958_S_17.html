Saurashtra Salt Manufacturing Company v Bai Valu Raja and Others

Supreme Court of India

28 April 1958

Case Analysis

Bench

Syed Jaffer Imam

Where Reported

1958 Indlaw SC 6; AIR 1958 SC 881; 1958 (2) LLJ 249

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Workmen's Compensation Act, 1923, s. 3(1) - Boat carrying certain workmen who had been employed by appellant in salt works, while crossing a creek capsized due to bad weather and overloading - As result of accident some of workmen were drowned resulting in seven cases for compensation being filed under Workmen's Compensation Act -(A)Whether an employer is liable to compensate a workman who has died outside place of his employment? - As a rule, employment of a workman does not commence until he has reached place of employment and does not continue when he has left place of employment - Held, since worker did not die in place of his employment hence liability as against employer does not arise- Matter remitted back to lower court.
