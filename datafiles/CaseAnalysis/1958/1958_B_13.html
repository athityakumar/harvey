 State of Bihar v Basawan Singh

Supreme Court of India

21 March 1958

Case Analysis

Bench

S.K. Das, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, A.K. Sarkar, Vivian Bose

Where Reported

1958 Indlaw SC 31; AIR 1958 SC 500; 1958 CRLJ 976; 1958 (2) MLJ(SC) 136; 1958 SCJ 856; [1959] 1 S.C.R. 195

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Socio-Economic

Summary: Constitution - Criminal - Evidence Act (1 of 1872), s. 133 - Code of Criminal Procedure (5 of 1898), ss. 367 and 37- Constitution of India, art. 136 - Arrest on charged of black marketing- Demand of bribe by police for release from detention -(A)Whether uncorroborated evidence of an accomplice is admissible under Evidence Act?- Held, that uncorroborated evidence of an accomplice is admissible in law - It is necessary that Judge should give some indication in his judgment that he has had this rule of caution in mind and should proceed to give reasons and show why he considers it safe to convict without corroboration in that particular case - (B) Whether judiciary requires Magistrate should not be relegated to position of partisan witness on disposing of matters? - Held, that independence and impartiality of judiciary requires that Magistrates whose normal function is judicial should not be relegated to position of partisan witnesses and required to depose to matters transacted by them in their official capacity unregulated by any statutory rules of procedure or conduct - (C)Whether it is duty of court to appreciate evidence and be judged on its own merits u/s. 367 of CrPC?- Held, that whatever be difficulties of detecting corruption cases and of securing conviction in such cases admissible evidence given in a case must be judged on its own merits, with due regard to all circumstances of case under CrPC - (D) whether tainted evidence corroborated with other facts has higher value than partisan evidence?- Held, that that degree of corroboration necessary is higher in respect of tainted evidence than for partisan evidence - Assessment of value of evidence and degree of corroboration necessary to inspire confidence, no rigid formula can or should be laid down - (E) Whether evidence of witness of raiding party must be discarded in absence of any independent corroboration? - Held, there isn't any inflexible rule that evidence of witnesses of raiding party must be discarded in absence of any independent corroboration - Correct rule is that if any of witnesses are accomplices who are 'praticeps criminis' in respect of actual crime charged, their evidence must be treated as evidence of accomplices is treated - If they are not accomplices but are partisan or interested witnesses, who are concerned in success of trap, their evidence must be tested in same way as other interested evidence is tested by application of diverse considerations which must vary from case to case- (F) Whether there must be independent corroboration for every detail of what witness of raiding party have said? - Held, that independent corroboration does not mean that every detail of what witnesses of raiding party have said must be corroborated by independent witnesses - Corroboration can be circumstantial evidence of his connection with crime and need not be necessary of being direct evidence - (G) Whether exercise of jurisdiction u/art. 136 of Constitution lies when order is passed by judge? - Held, that where Judge accepted as correct all essential facts constituting offence with which accused was charged, but he passed an order of acquittal on a misconception as to effect of a decision of Supreme Court it is a fit case for exercise of jurisdiction under art. 136 of Constitution - Appeal allowed.
