 State of Rajasthan v Shri G. Chawla and Dr. Pohumal

Supreme Court of India

16 December 1958

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, S.K. Das, Sudhi Ranjan Das

Where Reported

1958 Indlaw SC 134; AIR 1959 SC 544; 1959 CRLJ 660; [1959] 1 S.C.R. 904; [1959] Supp1 S.C.R. 904

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure 1898, Government of Part C States Act, 1951, Bombay Prohibition Act 1949, Ajmer (Sound Amplifiers Control) Act, 1952

Summary: Municipalities & Local Governments - Ajmer (Sound Amplifiers Control) Act, 1952, s. 2 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1898, s. 432 - Govt. of Part C States Act, 1951, ss. 3 and 21 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 132, 246(4) - (A)Whether legislatures in our country possess plenary powers of legislation under arts. 245 and 246 of Constitution? - Held, plenary powers exist despite division of legislative powers - supremacy of legislatures is confined to topics mentioned as Entries in Lists conferring respectively powers on them - In event of a conflict between rival lists, it is necessary to examine impugned legislation in its pith and substance - Only if that pith and substance falls substantially within an Entry or Entries conferring legislative powers, is legislation valid, a slight transgression upon a rival List, notwithstanding - (B) Whether Ajmer (Sound Amplifiers Control) Act, 1953 is intra vires State Legislature? - Further held, substance of legislation is within powers conferred by Entry No. 6 and conceivably Entry No. 1 of State List and it does not purport to encroach upon field of Entry No. 31 - Power to legislate in relation to public health includes power to regulate use of amplifiers as producers of loud noises argument that pith and substance of Act falls within Entry 31 of Union List is not valid because other loud noises, result of some other instruments etc, are not equally controlled and prohibited - (C) Whether Ajmer (Sound Amplifiers Control) Act, 1953 is intra vires State Legislature? - Further held, substance of legislation is within powers conferred by Entry No. 6 and conceivably Entry No. 1 of State List and it does not purport to encroach upon field of Entry No. 31 - Power to legislate in relation to public health includes power to regulate use of amplifiers as producers of loud noises - Argument that pith and substance of Act falls within Entry 31 of Union List is not valid because other loud noises, result of some other instruments etc, are not equally controlled and prohibited - Appeal allowed.
