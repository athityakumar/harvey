 M.C. V. S. Arunachala Nadar Etc v State of Madras and Others

Supreme Court of India

06 October 1958

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Kailas Nath Wanchoo

Where Reported

1958 Indlaw SC 59; AIR 1959 SC 300; 1959 (1) MLJ 133; 1959 (1) MLJ(SC) 133; [1959] 1 S.C.R. 92; [1959] Supp1 S.C.R. 92

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Constitution - Madras Commercial Crops Markets Act, 1933 - Rules made thereunder - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 19(1)(g)and (6) - Constitutional validity of Act, rules and notifications issued thereunder - Reasonableness of restriction under cl. (6) of art. 19 - Held, Act enacted for better regulation of buying and selling of commercial crops in State of Madras and for that purpose to establish markets and make rules for their proper administration - Market Committee (MC) constituted in areas under two of Districts in question - Requirement

of licence for conducting business in cotton and groundnut, imposed by MC - Prosecution sought to be initiated in cases of violation - Traders, producers and weighmen were all brought into effect of notifications issued by MCs - Constitutionality challenged on touchstone of art. 19(1)(g) and (6) - Concept of 'reasonableness' of restriction u/art. 19(6) deliberated upon, precedents quoted and relied - Object of Act, circumstances under which it was passed and its provisions and whether provisions have any reasonable relation to object which legislature seeks to achieve, deliberated upon - Historical background of Act, noted - Agricultural marketing, marketing legislation, protection to producers from middlemen and profiteers - Having regard to entire scheme of Act, impugned provisions of Act constitute reasonable restrictions on a citizen's right to do business, and therefore, they are valid - Appeal dismissed.
