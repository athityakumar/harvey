Mithan Lal and Others v State of Delhi and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 April 1958

Case Analysis

Bench

T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, A.K. Sarkar, S.K. Das, Sudhi Ranjan Das

Where Reported

1958 Indlaw SC 194; AIR 1958 SC 682; 1958 SCJ 899; [1959] 1 S.C.R. 445; 1958 (9) STC 412

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Keywords: Bengal Finance (Sales Tax) Act, 1941, Madras General Sales Tax Act, 1939, Part B States (Laws) Act, 1951

Summary: Sales Tax - Bengal Finance (Sales Tax) Act, 1941 - Sale of Goods Act, 1930 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 32, 248 and 246 - Govt. of India Act, 1935 - Delhi Laws Act, 1912 - Part B States (Laws) Act, 1951, s. 2 - Whether State Legislature acting under Entry 48 had competence to enact laws imposing tax on supply of materials in execution of works contract as there is no sale of those materials by contractor? - When a notification is issued by appropriate Govt. extending law of a Part A State to a Part C State, law so extended derives its force in State to which it is extended from s. 2 of Part C States (Laws) Act enacted by Parliament - Modification made by Central Govt. assuming, that is its true character, does not involve any change of policy underlying Bengal Finance (Sales Tax) Act, 1941 - Held, State had required competence - Petitions dismissed.
