 Atma Ram v State of Punjab and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 December 1958

Case Analysis

Bench

Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, K. Subba Rao, Kailas Nath Wanchoo

Where Reported

1958 Indlaw SC 162; AIR 1959 SC 519; [1959] 1 S.C.R. 748; [1959] Supp1 S.C.R. 748

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Market value - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14, 19(1)(f), 19(5), 31(A)and 46(3) - Punjab Security of Land Tenure Act, 1953 - Petitions u/art. 32 of Constitution impugned constitutionality of Act - (A) Whether Legislature had no legislative competence to enact Act? - Held, Entry 18 in List II of Seventh Schedule to Constitution, r/w art. 46(3) of Constitution, has vested exclusive power in State to make laws with respect to 'rights in or over land, land tenures including relation of landlord and tenant' - Act lays down provisions which are in nature of modifications of rights in estates within meaning of art. 31(A) - That being so, art.19(5) is wholly out of way in this case - There is no legal foundation for contention that impugned Act is beyond legislative competence of State Legislature - (B) Whether provisions of Act contravene petitioners fundamental rights enshrined in art.14, 19(1)(f) and 31 of Constitution? - Further held, if impugned Act comes within purview of any of cls. of art. 31 A, law will be immune from attack on any of grounds based on provisions of art. 14, 19 and 31 - Art. 31(A) was enacted by two successive amendments, with retrospective effect, in order to save legislation effecting agrarian reforms - Hence, we have every reason to hold that expressions in art. 31(A) have been used in their widest amplitude, consistent with purpose behind those amendments - It is enough for purpose of this case to hold that provisions of Act, amount to modification of landowner's rights in lands comprised in his 'estate' or 'holding' - There cannot be least doubt that provisions of Act, very substantially modify land-owner's rights to hold and dispose of his property in any estate or a portion thereof - Provisions of art. 31A save impugned Act from any attack based on provisions of art. 14, 19 and 31 of Constitution - Petitions dismissed.
