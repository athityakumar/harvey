Punjab Distilling Industries Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax, Simla

Supreme Court of India

24 November 1958

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sarkar, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar

Where Reported

1958 Indlaw SC 165; AIR 1959 SC 346; [1959] 35 ITR 519; [1959] 1 S.C.R. 683; [1959] Supp1 S.C.R. 683

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Companies Act, 1956 - Excess Profits Tax Act, 1940 - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s.10 - General trading Account - General Ledger - Security Deposit - Trading recipients - Appellant was carrying on business as a distiller of country liquor and due to scarcity of bottles government devised scheme where appellant can sell back bottles of liquor - Price of bottles received by appellant under buy-back scheme was entered by it in its general trading account while additional sum received for them was entered in general ledger under heading ' Empty Bottles Return Security Deposit Account - Respondent imposed tax which was confirmed by Tribunal and HC - Hence, instant appeal - Whether collections by assesses company/appellant described in its accounts as 'empty bottle return security deposits' were income assessable u/s. 10 of 1922 Act - Held, yes - Payment insisted upon as a condition upon which alone liquor would be supplied with an agreement that they would be repaid oil return of bottles - Further, they were part of transactions of sale of liquor which produced profit and therefore they had a profit making quality - Amounts paid to appellant and described as 'Empty Bottles Return Security Deposit ' were trading receipts and therefore income of appellant assessable to tax - Appeal dismissed.
