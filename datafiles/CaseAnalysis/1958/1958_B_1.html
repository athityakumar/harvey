 Gordhandas Lalji v B. Banerjee and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 April 1958

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Sudhi Ranjan Das, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, S.K. Das, Vivian Bose

Where Reported

1958 Indlaw SC 193; AIR 1958 SC 1006; 1958 (9) STC 581

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Sales Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Constitution Of India, 1950 , art. 286 (1) (b) - Bengal Finance (Sales Tax) Act, 1941, s. 5(2)(a)(v) - As required by Act of 1941, appellant submitted his return for year 1949-50 which showed that his turnover was Rs. 24,21,023 - He claimed that taxable turnover for relevant period was 'nil'- He claimed that goods worth Rs. 1,79,733 were sold to registered dealers and were thus exempt from tax u/s. 5(2)(a)(ii) of Act 1941 - (A)Whether deduction claimable in respect of sale of goods in instant case is within meaning of Bengal Finance (Sales Tax) Act, 1941 u/s. 5(2)(a)(v)? - In this case property in goods passed in favour of Bombay parties, as goods were appropriated by Bombay parties and so despatch of goods outside West Bengal was not on behalf of dealer but was by and on behalf of Bombay parties - Held, in this view of matter s. 5(2)(a)(v) of Bengal Finance (Sales Tax) Act would be wholly inapplicable and deduction not claimable - (A1) Whether dealer in a sale of goods is entitled to claim protection u/art. 286 of Constitution once title has been passed to foreign dealers? - Further held, that dealer would not be entitled to claim protection u/art. 286 of Constitution as before dispatch, title to goods had passed in favour of foreign party - Appeal dismissed.
