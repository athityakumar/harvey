Mazagaon Dock Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax and Excess Profits Tax

Supreme Court of India

12 May 1958

Case Analysis

Bench

T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, A.K. Sarkar

Where Reported

1958 Indlaw SC 93; AIR 1958 SC 861; 1959 (61) BomLR 554; [1958] 34 ITR 368; 1958 (2) MLJ(SC) 210; 1958 SCJ 1261; [1959] 1 S.C.R. 848

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 66(1), 42(2) - Non-Resident Companies - Excess Profits Tax - Vicarious Liability - Appellant was a private limited company carrying on business as marine engineers and ship repairers and its entire share capital is beneficially owned by two British companies - Agreement was entered into two companies by appellant and appellant repairs their ships at cost and charges no profits - Whether appellant was chargeable to tax u/s. 42(2) of the Act - Held, where a person not resident or not ordinarily resident in taxable territories carries on business with a person resident in taxable territories, and it appears to Income-tax Officer that owing to close connection between such persons the course of business was so arranged that business done by resident person with person not resident or not ordinarily resident produces to resident either DO profits or less than ordinary profits which might be expected to arise in that business profits derived there from or which may reasonably be deemed to have been derived there from shall be chargeable to income-tax in name of resident person who shall be deemed to be for all purposes - Non-resident Companies make a saving and that was not profit or gains of a business liable to be taxed under the Act - Non-resident Companies could derive no profits from dealings with appellant would not detract from their character as business with appellant - Profits chargeable u/s. 42(2) of the Act must be separately assessed and not added on to other profits or income of appellant and s. 42(2) of the Act imposed on appellant a vicarious liability charge being in reality on profits of nonresident - Appeal dismissed.
