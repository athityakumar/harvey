Radha Sundar Dutta v Mohd. Jahadur Rahim and Others

Supreme Court of India

18 September 1958

Case Analysis

Bench

T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, A.K. Sarkar

Where Reported

1958 Indlaw SC 72; AIR 1959 SC 24; 1959 SCJ 212; [1959] 1 S.C.R. 1309

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Zamindar, Patnidar, Bengal Patni Taluks Regulation, 1819, Village Chaukidari Act 1870 (Ben VI of 1870), Bengal Patri Toluks Regulation 1819 (Bom Regulation 8 of 1819)

Summary: Land & Property - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Village Chaukidari Act, 1870 - Bengal Patri Toluks Regulation, 1819 - Bengal Patni Taluks Regulation, 1819 - This village was granted by Maharaja of Burdwan in Patni settlement to predecessors in title of defendants I to 7 - Maharaja applied u/s. 8 of Regulation to bring suit lands to sale for realisation of arrears, and at auction held on 15-05-1937, himself became purchaser - On 13-02-1941, he granted lands again on Patni to appellant, who filed suit, out of which present appeal arises, in Court of Subordinate Judge, Burdwan, to recover possession thereof from defendants alleging that they had trespassed thereon - Respondents contested suit on ground that, in fact, there were no arrears of rent due under Ex. B, and that sale was therefore void - Whether grant, Ex. B properly construed must be held to create a new Patni distinct from lot Ahiyapur, and its sale is therefore valid? - Held, when s. 51 of Act recognises and saves rights which had been acquired under contract with Zamindar, its reasonable implication is that rights so recognised are same as under contract, and that, in consequence, settlement of Chaukidari Chakaran lands in Patni must be taken to be a continuance of Patni of village in which they are included - But it is open to parties to agree that Chaukidari Chakaran lands should form a new and distinct Patni - Reading cls. in Ex. B, it seems clear that intention of parties was that while a transfer of Ahiyapur Patni by sale should extinguish title of holders of Chaukidari Chakaran lands a transfer of these lands would have no effect on title to lot Ahiyapur Patni - Thus, Ex. B creates a distinct Patni, sale thereof on 15-05-1937, is valid, and plaintiff has therefore acquired a good title to suit lands under grant d/- 13-02-1941 - Appeal allowed.
