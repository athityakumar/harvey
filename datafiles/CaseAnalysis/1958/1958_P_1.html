P. Krishna Menon v Commissioner of Income Tax, Mysore, Travancore-Cochin and Coorg, Bangalore

Supreme Court of India

07 October 1958

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sarkar, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar

Where Reported

1958 Indlaw SC 140; AIR 1959 SC 75; [1959] 35 ITR 48; 1959 (1) MLJ(SC) 49; [1959] 1 S.C.R. 133; [1959] Supp1 S.C.R. 133

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act,1922, s. 4(3) (vii) -(A) Whether teaching of Vedanta is carrying on of vocation or not? - Held, yes - Teaching is a vocation, if not a profession - Teaching of Vedanta is as much teaching and, therefore, a vocation as any other teaching - (1) Whether there are materials for Tribunal to hold that deposits into assessee's bank account in Bombay by John H Levy from 1941 as aforesaid represented income that accrued to assessee outside Travancore State?- Held, appellant was carrying on a vocation or occupation in that State and income derived therefrom should be considered as having arisen in Travancore, and that therefore appellant was liable to be taxed not on amounts which he brought into Travancore but on amounts which had been paid to credit of his account at Bombay by Levy during relevant periods - (B) Whether voluntary payment exempt from being taxable as income under Income-Tax Act, 1922, s. 4(3)(vii) or not? - Further held, it is impossible to hold in this case that payments to appellant had not been made in consideration of teaching imparted by appellant - Payments were repeated and came with same regularity as Levy's visits to appellant for receiving instructions in Vedanta - Payments being voluntary were arising from vocation of appellant as a teacher of Vedanta cannot be considered as gift not in lieu of services - No question of exemption under s. 4(3)(vii) - Appeal dismissed.
