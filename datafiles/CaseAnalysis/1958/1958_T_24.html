Shri Chintaman Rao and Another v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

18 February 1958

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Syed Jaffer Imam

Where Reported

1958 Indlaw SC 22; AIR 1958 SC 388; 1958 CRLJ 803; 1958 (2) LLJ 252; 1958 SCJ 753; [1958] S.C.R. 759; [1958] 1 S.C.R. 1340

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Independent Contractor, Hours Of Work, Workmen's Compensation Act

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Practice & Procedure - Factories Act, 1948, ss. 62, 63 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 2(1) - Adult Workers - Register - Maintainability - 1st Appellant was Managing-Partner of firm while 2nd appellant was its active Manager of Messrs. Brijlal Manilal and Company which manufactures bidis. The process of manufacture, was carried out in two stages - 1st stage management entered into contract with independent contractors, known as Sattedars, for supply of bidis locally and pays cost of manufacture of bidis after deducting cost of tobacco supplied to them - In 2nd stage process of manufacture begins in factory, it was carried out exclusively by labourers employed in factory - On 9-12-1952, Inspector of Factories, visited factory at 5-30 p. m. at the time of his inspection he found few persons came to factory for delivering bidis manufactured by them to factory - Chief Inspector of Factories filed complaint before Trial Court against appellants for violation of the provisions of ss. 62 and 63 of the 1948 Act Trial Court convicted appellants u/s.92 of the Act and levied fine - Aggrieved appellants filed appeal before Second Additional Sessions Judge who affirmed Trial Court's order - Aggrieved by said order appellants filed revision before HC which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether contract of employment was there between workers and employer - Held, in State v. Shri Krishna Prasad Dar Allahabad 44 1953 Indlaw ALL 31. need not be considered in detail as Judges therein accepted the same interpretation that Court had placed on the provisions of s. 2(1) of the 1947 Act and came to conclusion, on the facts of that case, that the persons therein were workers of the factory - Court, therefore, hold that neither Sattedars nor the coolies found by Inspector to be working in factory were workers, as they were not employed by factory - Therefore, non-inclusion of their names in register of adult workers or absence of any entries in regard to them in the said register would not constitute offence u/s. 92 of the 1948 Act - Subsequently, Court made one observation that under no circumstances Sattedar could be considered to be worker within the meaning of its definition in 1948 Act - Whether particular person, under whatever designation he might be known, was worker or not under the 1948 Act depends upon terms of contract entered into between him and employer - In instant case no attempt had been made by prosecution to establish that Sattedars were employed by management for doing work in factory and un- contradicted evidence was that they were independent contractors who came to factory to deliver bidis or sent their coolies to do the same - Hence, set aside convictions of appellants u/s.92 of the 1948 Act - Appeal allowed.
