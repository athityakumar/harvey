Seth Ganga Dhar v Shankar Lal and Others

Supreme Court of India

15 April 1958

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sarkar

Where Reported

1958 Indlaw SC 46; AIR 1958 SC 770; 1958 (2) MLJ 150; 1958 (2) MLJ(SC) 150; 1958 SCJ 935; [1959] 1 S.C.R. 509

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Whether provision of term of 85 years and also other terms and circumstances amount to a clog on equity of redemption? - whether appellant can redeem mortgage? - Held, any provision inserted to prevent redemption on payment or performance of debt or obligation for which security was given is what is meant by a clog or fetter on equity of redemption and is therefore void - Term providing that right to redeem will arise after eighty-five years does not, of course, take away mortgagor's right to redeem and is not, therefore, in that sense, a clog on equity of redemption - Rule against clogs on equity of redemption no doubt involves that Courts have power to relieve a party from his bargain - Court's jurisdiction to relieve a mortgagor from his bargain depends on whether it was obtained by taking advantage of any difficulty or embarrassment that he might have been in when he borrowed moneys on mortgage - Length of term of mortgage can never by itself show that bargain was oppressive - Suit for redemption was brought over forty-seven years after date of mortgage - It seems to Court impossible that if term was oppressive, that was not realised much earlier and suit brought within a short time of mortgage - Bargain was a reasonable one and eighty-five years term of mortgage should be enforced - Appeal dismissed.
