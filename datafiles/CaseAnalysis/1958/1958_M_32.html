Maktul v Mst. Manbhari and Others

Supreme Court of India

23 May 1958

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, A.K. Sarkar, K. Subba Rao

Where Reported

1958 Indlaw SC 180; AIR 1958 SC 918; 1958 SCJ 1268; [1959] 1 S.C.R. 1099

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Civil Procedure Code, 1908 - Practice & Procedure - Whether a Hindu governed by customary law prevailing in Punjab succeeds to his maternal grandfather's estate, is property in his hands ancestral property qua his own sons? - Rule of stare decisis is not so imperative or inflexible but its application must be determined in each case by discretion of court - Held, as per customary law Punjab Property inherited by Hindu male from his grandfather was not ancestral property qua his sons - Rule of stare decisis is inapplicable where decision is erroneous and when its reversal does not shake any title or contract - Appeal dismissed.
