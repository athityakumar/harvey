 Basheshar Nath v Commissioner of Income Tax, Delhi and Rajasthan

Supreme Court of India

19 November 1958

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhi Ranjan Das, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, S.K. Das, J.L. Kapur, K. Subba Rao

Where Reported

1958 Indlaw SC 89; AIR 1959 SC 149; [1959] 35 ITR 190; [1959] 1 S.C.R. 528; [1959] Supp1 S.C.R. 528

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Taxation on Income (Investigation Commission) Act, 1947 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Validity of a settlement made u/s. 8A of the Act, 1947 - Infringement of fundamental rights - Waived - Whether fundamental right guaranteed by Constitution could be waived - Held, fundamental right guaranteed u/art. 14 could not be waived - It was open to any person whose rights had been infringed to waive it when there was waiver and there was no legal impediment to enforcement of law - Person affected by infringement of fundamental rights could waive constitutional protection guaranteed to him - Appellant waived his fundamental right u/ art. 14 of Constitution as right was only in respect of his liability to tax and he could legitimately waive - In criminal cases doctrine that a constitutional privilege may be waived to a very limited extent - Supra doctrine of waiver extends to rights and privileges of any character since word waiver covers every conceivable right it was general rule that a person may waive any matter which affects his property and any alienable right or privilege of which he was owner or which belongs to him or to which he was legally entitled, whether secured by contract, conferred by statute, or guaranteed by constitution - Everyone had a right to waive and agree to waive and advantage of a law or rule made solely for benefit and protection of individual in his private capacity if it can be dispensed with and relinquished without infringing on any public right and without detriment to community at large - Rights relating to procedure and remedy are subject to waiver if a right was fundamental in its nature as to be regarded by state as vitally integrated in immemorially established processes of administration of justice it cannot be waived by anyone - Doctrine of waiver can be invoked when Constitutional or Statutory guarantee of a right was not conceived in public interest or when it does not affect jurisdiction of authority infringing right and if privilege conferred or right created by statute was solely for benefit of individual he can waive it - Court cannot import any limitations on fundamental rights other than those contained in Part III by any doctrine such as waiver and fundamental rights incorporated in Part III of Constitution cannot be waived - If doctrine of waiver was to be excluded a person can apply to SC u/art.32 of Constitution for relief provided - Appeal allowed.
