Babulal Bhuramal and Another v Nandram Shivram and Others

Supreme Court of India

31 March 1958

Case Analysis

Bench

Syed Jaffer Imam, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, K. Subba Rao

Where Reported

1958 Indlaw SC 38; AIR 1958 SC 677; 1958 SCJ 880; [1959] 1 S.C.R. 367

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Presidency Small Causes Courts Act, 1882, Bombay Rent, Hotel and lodging House Rates Control Act, 1947

Summary: Rent Control - Bombay Rent, Hotel and Lodging House Rates Control Act, 1947, ss.28, 29 A - Presidency Small Causes Courts Act, 1882 - Provincial Small Cause Courts Act, 1887 - Premises - (A) Whether High Court correctly decided that City Civil Court had no jurisdiction to entertain suit filed by appellants, having regard to provisions of s. 28 of Act, 1947? - Held, HC correctly decided that suit filed by plaintiffs, could not be determined by City Civil Court - S. 28 applies to a suit where admittedly relationship of landlord and tenant within the meaning of Act subsists between parties - S. 28 not only contemplates a suit between a landlord and a tenant in which that relationship is admitted but also a suit in which it is claimed that relationship of a landlord and a tenant within meaning of Act subsists between parties - Courts which have jurisdiction to entertain and try such a suit are courts specified in s. 28 and no other - In a suit u/s. 28 court has to determine all questions relating to recovery of rent or relating to possession and all claims or questions arising out of Act or any of its provisions - (B) Does provisions of s. 28 cover a case where in a suit one party alleges that he is landlord and denies that other is his tenant or vice versa and relief asked for in suit is in nature of a claim which arises out of Act or any of its provisions ? - Further held, a suit does not cease to be a suit between a landlord and a tenant merely because defendants denied claim of plaintiffs which could not be established outside Act but which arose under provisions of Act by virtue of a claim made thereunder must be determined by a court specified in s. 28 and a title de hors Act may be determined in any other court of competent jurisdiction - Appeal dismissed.
