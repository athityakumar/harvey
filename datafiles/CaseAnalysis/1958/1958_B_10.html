Bala Subrahmanya Rajaram v B. C. Patil and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 March 1958

Case Analysis

Bench

Vivian Bose, Syed Jaffer Imam, K. Subba Rao

Where Reported

1958 Indlaw SC 142; AIR 1958 SC 518; 1958 (1) LLJ 773; 1958 (2) MLJ(SC) 121; 1958 SCJ 851; [1958] 1 S.C.R. 1504

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Practice & Procedure - Payment of Wages Act, 1936, ss. 2(vi) and 15 - (A) Whether bonus paid, not because of contract but by award of Industrial Court, is included within expression 'wages' as defined under Payment of Wages Act, 1936? - Held, no - 'Wages' means remuneration which would be payable if terms of contract of employment, express or implied, are fulfilled -(B) Whether Authority under Payment of Wages Act, 1936 can entertain petitions made to it under s. 15 in respect of such bonus? - Further held, no - Authority has no jurisdiction to entertain such petitions - Appeal allowed.
