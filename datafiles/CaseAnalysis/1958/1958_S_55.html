 Tata Iron and Steel Company, Limited v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

19 February 1958

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhi Ranjan Das, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, S.K. Das, A.K. Sarkar, Vivian Bose

Where Reported

1958 Indlaw SC 152; AIR 1958 SC 452; 1958 SCJ 818; [1958] 1 S.C.R. 1355; 1958 (9) STC 267

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Keywords: Tax Levied, Indian Companies Act, Bihar Sales Tax Act, 1947

Summary: Sales Tax - Bihar Sales Tax Act, 1947, s. 4(1), 2(g), second Provison - Government of India Act, 1935, ss. 100(3), ss.ch 7, Li, II, entry 48, Li, I, entry 45 - Constitution of India, 1950 arts. 246, 286 and list 1, entry 84, list 2, entry 54 - Sales tax and Excise duty - Validity of ss. 4(1) r/w 2(g), second Proviso of 1947 Act - Legislative competence considering 'Theory of territorial connection or nexus' in light of Constitution - Whether retrospective levy affects its nature? - Whether sufficient nexus? - Held, valid - Within legislative competency considering arts. 246 and 286 - Retrospective levy does not destroys its character as a sales tax - Sufficient nexus - Appeals dismissed.
