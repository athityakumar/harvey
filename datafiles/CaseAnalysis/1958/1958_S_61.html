 Pandit Banarsi Das Bhanot v State of Madhya Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

03 April 1958

Case Analysis

Bench

T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, Sudhi Ranjan Das, S.K. Das, A.K. Sarkar, Vivian Bose

Where Reported

1958 Indlaw SC 25; AIR 1958 SC 909; 1958 MPLJ 467; [1959] 1 S.C.R. 427; 1958 (9) STC 388

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Keywords: Sale Price, Building Contract, Madras General Sales Tax Act, Delhi Laws Act, 1915, Central Provinces and Berar Sales Tax Act 1947, Tariff Act, Sales Tax Rules, 1947

Summary: Sales Tax - Constitution Of India, 1950, sch. 7, list 2, entry 54 - Central Provinces and Berar Sales Tax Act, 1947, ss. 2(b), 2(c), 2(d), 2(g), 2(h), 2(j), 4(2), 6(1) and 6(2), ss. chap. II, item 33 - Taxation Laws - (A) 'Sale of goods' - Sale of material involved in building contract - Legality of Sales Tax - Validity of provisions of ss. 2 and 4 of Act - (B) Constitution of India, 1950 art. 245 - Delegated legislation - Notification D/- 18-09-1950 amending sch. II, item 33 of Act - Validity of Notification - Held, provisions ultra vires as it is beyond powers of Provincial Legislature to impose tax on supply of materials, except when a combination of an agreement to sell and an agreement to work - Impugned notification is intra vires and not open to challenge.
