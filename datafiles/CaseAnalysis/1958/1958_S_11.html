Razia Begum v Sahebzadi Anwar Begum and Others

Supreme Court of India

23 May 1958

Case Analysis

Bench

Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Syed Jaffer Imam, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1958 Indlaw SC 57; AIR 1958 SC 886; 1958 (2) AnWR 193; 1958 (2) MLJ 193; 1958 (2) MLJ(SC) 193; 1958 SCJ 1214; [1959] 1 S.C.R. 1111

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Ground Of Res Judicata, Attorney-General

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 1, r. 10 (2) - Specific Relief Act, 1963, ss. 42 and 43 - Lower court allowed intervention of respondents adding them as defendants in suit instituted by appellant against her alleged husband, who was sole defendant in suit as originally framed - Whether court had jurisdiction to add respondents as defendants in suit within scope of r. 10 (2) of O. 1 of CPC, 1908 ? - Held, in order that a person may be added as a party to a suit he should have a direct interest in subject-matter of litigation whether it raises questions relating to movable or immovable property - Appeal dismissed.
