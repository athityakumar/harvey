Ram Prakash v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

02 September 1958

Case Analysis

Bench

Syed Jaffer Imam, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Kailas Nath Wanchoo

Where Reported

1958 Indlaw SC 19; AIR 1959 SC 1; 1959 CRLJ 90; [1959] 1 S.C.R. 1219

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 30 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898, s. 164 - Constitution of India, 1950 art. 136 - (A) Whether retracted confession of co-accused is admissible against other accused or not? - Held, confession of co-accused though retracted will be voluntary and true if it had been corroborated in material particulars regarding general story told by accused in his confession and which tends to connect co-accused with murder of deceased - (B) Whether in an appeal by special leave, it is permissible to make submissions on question of fact or not? - Held, principal matter in this appeal is confession had been corroborated in material particulars - In an appeal by special leave, it is not ordinarily permissible to make submissions on questions of fact - Appeal dismissed.
