 Konduri Buchirajalingam v State of Hyderabad and Others

Supreme Court of India

03 April 1958

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sarkar, Sudhi Ranjan Das, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, S.K. Das, Vivian Bose

Where Reported

1958 Indlaw SC 196; AIR 1958 SC 756; 1958 ALT 1063; 1958 (9) STC 397

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Sales Tax

Summary: Constitution - Sales Tax - Hyderabad General Sales Tax Act,1950, s. 2(m) - Constitution Of India,1950, art. 286 - Essential Supplies (Temporary Powers) Act,1946 - Members of Association of oil mill owners, in course of their business purchased ground-nuts for purpose of converting them into oil and trading in same - Hyderabad General Sales Tax Act came into force on 01-05-1950 - Soon after Act came into force Sales Tax Officer, Warangal, verbally informed members of Association that they would have to pay tax on purchase of ground-nuts made by them- -(A)Whether a turnover in ambit of Hyderabad General Sales Tax Act,1950 is that of agriculturist? - Held, Act of 1950 shows that 'turnover' only refers to turnover of a dealer and not of an agriculturist - Proviso to s. 2(m) deals with a person who is both an agriculturist and a dealer and it excludes proceeds of sale by him of his own agricultural produce from his turnover as a dealer in goods - (A1) Whether buyer and seller both are to be taxed in respect of same transaction of sale under the rules of 1950? - Held, u/r. 5(2) of Hyderabad General Sales Tax Rules,1950 buyer and seller are not both to be taxed in respect of same transaction of sale, and where a dealer is taxed as buyer of goods he shall not be taxed in respect of a subsequent sale by him of same goods (A2) whether tax will be levied on dealer or agriculturist? - Held, in a transaction of sale tax shall be levied on buyer provided he is dealer Tax payable by dealer on his turnover of purchases of goods, seller of goods whether he is an agriculturist or not, shall not be liable to pay tax - (A3) Whether dealer entitled to collect tax payable from agriculturist? - Held, Under Act a dealer is liable to pay tax and an agriculturist not being a dealer has no such liability, a dealer therefore has no right to collect any tax from an agriculturist - (B) Whether Essential Supplies (Temporary Powers) Act, 1946 is an act within contemplation of art. 286 (3) of Constitution of India? - Held, Any Act of Parliament declaring certain goods to be essential for its own purposes is not an Act within contemplation of Art. 286 (3) - (B 1) Whether a commodity declared essential by act 24 of 1946 is exempted from tax? - Held, tax is not to be levied on sale of commodity declared essential by act 24 of 1946 -Appeal dismissed.
