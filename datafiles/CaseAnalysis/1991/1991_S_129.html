Soni Devrajbhai Babubhai v State Of Gujarat And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

28 August 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

J.S. Verma, L.M. Sharma

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 783; (1991) 4 SCC 298; (1991) SCC (Cr) 959; AIR 1991 SC 2173; 1992 (1) CCR 59; 1991 (3) Crimes 271; 1991 CRLJ 3135; 1991 CrLR(SC) 804; 1991 (45) DLT 605; 1991 (2) DMC 392; 1992 (3) HLR 228; JT 1991 (3) SC 542; 1991(2) SCALE 410; [1991] 3 S.C.R. 812

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Family & Personal

Keywords: New Offence

Summary: Criminal - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 113 - Dowry Prohibition Act, 1961 - Dowry Prohibition (Amendment) Act, 1986, ss. 7, 11 and 12 - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 20 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 498 A, 304 B and 34 - Petitioner's daughter died in her matrimonial home - Petitioner filed application which was transferred to Judicial magistrate u/s. 304 B for committal to court of sessions - Magistrate dismissed application as s. 304 B was newly inserted in IPC and did not apply to petitioner's case, HC also dismissed petitioner's application - Whether s. 304 B applicable to case of petitioner, where death took place prior to insertion of s. 304 B? - Contention that s. 304-B inserted in IPC does not create a new offence and contains merely a rule of evidence is untenable - Held, rule of evidence to prove offence of dowry death is contained in s. 113-B of Indian Evidence Act providing for presumption as to dowry death which was a simultaneous amendment made in Indian Evidence Act for proving offence of dowry death - Contention rejected - Further held, views of court below don't have any infirmity - Respondents cannot be tried for s. 304 B IPC as offence took place prior to its insertion - Appeal dismissed.
