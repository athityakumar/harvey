Abdul Sathar Ibrahim Manik and another v Union Of India And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

08 October 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Jayachandra Reddy, S. Ratnavel Pandian

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 736; (1992) 1 SCC 1; AIR 1991 SC 2261; AIR 1991 SCW 2603; 1992 (1) CCR 12; 1991 (3) Crimes 475; 1991 CRLJ 3291; 1992 CRLR 21; 1992 CrLR(SC) 21; JT 1991 (4) SC 103; 1991(2) SCALE 758; [1991] Supp1 S.C.R. 435

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Solitary Incident

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Conservation of Foreign Exchange & Prevention of Smuggling Activities Act, 1974, s.3(1) - Constitution of India, 1950, art.22(5) - Customs Act, 1962, s.108 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s.437 - Detention without allowing proper representation - Sustainability - Petitioner-detenu was a foreign national being a resident of Republic of Maldives - On 25-10-90 petitioner landed at Airport from Male and was moving towards the exit gate of the Customs Import Baggage Hail - Petitioner was intercepted by the Air Customs Officers and on examination he was found to be carrying 50 gold biscuits of foreign origin which were seized from either side of the handle inside the lock system of the red colour suit-case belonging to the petitioner - Petitioner's passport and other documents were also seized - Petitioner's statement was recorded u/s. 108 of 1962 Act wherein he was alleged to have confessed the guilt - After the arrest petitioner was produced in the Court of Chief Judicial Magistrate and was remanded to judicial custody for a period of 14 days - Thereafter petitioner was shifted to the Court of the Additional Chief Judicial Magistrate (Economic Offences) - While petitioner was in jail he made an application for granting of bail u/s. 437 CrPC but it was rejected by the Additional Chief Judicial Magistrate (Economic Offences), Ernakulam. While the petitioner. was confined in jail an order of detention was passed u/s. 3(1) of 1974 Act by the Secretary to the Govt. and the same was served on the petitioner - Grounds of detention alongwith the list of documents annexed thereto were served in time - Petitioner made a representation and it was rejected - Hence instant petition - It was submitted that since petitioner's bail application was rejected and since he was in jail and his passport was also seized, there was no compelling necessity for such a detention - It was also contended that no antecedents were there showing petitioner's involvement in such incidents and this was the solitary incident, therefore the provisions of 1974 Act were not attracted - Next main and important submission was that the copies of the bail application filed by him and the order refusing bail, which were relevant documents, were suppressed and not placed before the detaining authority nor they were supplied to the detenu and therefore there was non-application of mind and the petitioner also was denied a reasonable opportunity u/art. 22(5) of the Constitution of India - Held, petitioner being in custody in India could no more indulge in smuggling and therefore the detention on the ground that he was likely to indulge in smuggling was non-existent - SC did not see force in that submission - Potentialities of the detenu as gathered from his act of smuggling that form basis for detention - It was difficult to comprehend precisely the manner in which such a detenu with such potentialities might likely to indulge in the activities of smuggling - It was for the detaining authority to derive the necessary satisfaction on the basis of the materials placed before him - Petition dismissed.
