Nethala Pothuraju And Ors. v State Of Andhra Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

11 September 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

Kuldip Singh, M.M. Punchhi

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 64; (1992) SCC (Cr) 20; (1992) 1 SCC 49; AIR 1991 SC 2214; AIR 1991 SCW 2476; 1992 (1) CCR 87; 1991 (3) Crimes 418; 1991 CRLJ 3133; 1992 CrLR(SC) 9; JT 1991 (4) SC 135; 1991(2) SCALE 538; [1991] Supp1 S.C.R. 4; 1992 (1) UJ 204

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 147, 148, 302, 323 and 379 - Murder - Unlawful assembly - Whether s. 149, IPC applicable in facts of case? - As persons convicted by Trial Court were only three in number, question of unlawful assembly does not arise - Held, keeping in view manner of attack as disclosed by eye-witnesses and number and nature of injuries, no hesitation in holding that appellants made murderous attack on deceased and caused his instantaneous death - Appellants acted in furtherance of their common intention of murdering deceased - Non-applicability of s. 149, IPC is no bar in convicting accused u/s. 34, IPC - Conviction of appellants altered to s. 302 r/w 34, IPC but sentence of life imprisonment maintained - Order accordingly.
