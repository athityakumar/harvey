Koyappathodi M. Ayisha Umma v State Of Kerala

Supreme Court of India

13 August 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, N.M. Kasliwal

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 212; (1991) 4 SCC 8; AIR 1991 SC 2027; JT 1991 (6) SC 105; 1991(2) SCALE 332; [1991] 3 S.C.R. 548; 1991 (2) UJ 606

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - Kerala Land Acquisition Act, 1961 - Land Acquisition (Amendment) Act, 1984 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Land Acquisition - Compensation - Enhancement of compensation - A notification was passed by respondents u/s. 3 of 1961 Act acquiring six acres of land to construct staff quarters of P&T of Govt. of India - Land Acquisition Officer awarded compensation of Rs. 230/- per cent and also value of trees by capitalization method in sum of Rs. 2,69,421.55 ps towards land improvement together with 15% solatium and 4 % interest - Appellant filed reference before Trial court - Trial Court enhanced market value by judgment and award and confirmed award passed by Land Acquisition Officer - On appeal before HC - HC set aside award passed by Trial Court and upheld award passed by Land Acquisition Officer - Hence instant appeal - Whether impugned order passed by HC was justified - Held, reading of provisions would reveal legislative intendment that transitional provisions could apply to every proceeding for acquisition of any land under principal Act, Act 1 of 1894 Act, pending on 30-4-1982 introduction of Land Acquisition (Amendment) Bill in House of People, in which no award was made by Collector before that date or award made by Trial Court at date of Amendment Act 24-9-1984, thus it was clear that 'Amendment Act 68 of 1984 including subsection (2) s. 23 per se was inapplicable to acquisition of land under 1961 Act - Award was made by Collector - On reference Trial Court made award - SC by interpretation of transitional provision of s. 30(2) avoided injustice by eschewing literal construction and advanced justice by mending law - Benefit of enhanced solatium was extended by s. 30(2) to case of an award made by Trial Court between 30-4-1982 and 24-9-1984 even though it be upon reference from award made before 30-4-1982 - Thus it was held that pendency of appeals against award made preceding afore stated two dates in HC or SC would not attract application of s. 30(2) and that, therefore, enhanced solatium u/s. 30(2) r/w s. 23(2) was inapplicable - Thereby, appellant was not entitled to enhanced solatium at 30% - As regards interest was concerned it was fairly conceded that claimant was entitled only to 4 % as awarded by Courts below - Judgment passed by HC was set aside and held that appellant was entitled to Rs. 3,10,000 as enhanced compensation with 15 % solatium and interest at 4 % on enhanced market value from date of dispossession - Appeal allowed.
