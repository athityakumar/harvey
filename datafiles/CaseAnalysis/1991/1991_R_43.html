Ram Pyari Smt. and Others v Jagdish Lal

Supreme Court of India

05 December 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mohan, T.K. Thommen

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 289; (1992) 1 SCC 157; AIR 1992 SC 1537; 1992 ALJ 742; 1992 (19) ALR 114; 1992 CRLJ 2315; 1992 CrLR(SC) 242; JT 1991 (4) SC 515; 1992 (1) RCR(Rent) 9; 1991(2) SCALE 1207; [1991] Supp3 S.C.R. 117

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Contempt of Courts Act 1971, s. 12 - Contempt proceedings - Orders that may be passed - Decree for eviction - Supreme Court granting three months' time to judgment-debtor - On condition of filing undertaking assuming payment of arrears of rent as well as future compensation and giving peaceful possession - Undertaking not filed - Held, Court passing decree can be directed to cause delivery of possession of premises to decree holder in view of fact that judgment-debtor derived specific advantages under Supreme Court order -

Order accordingly.
