M.L. Jain v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

22 January 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

M.H. Kania, Ranganath Misra

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 472; (1991) 1 SCC 644; (1991) SCC (L&S) 694; AIR 1991 SC 928; 1991 (16) ATC 544; 1991 (62) FLR 404; JT 1991 (5) SC 133B; 1991 (1) LLJ 499; 1991 (1) LLN 1078; 1991(1) SCALE 64; [1991] 1 S.C.R. 98; 1991 (2) SLR 143; 1991 (1) UJ 369

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Service

Keywords: High Court Judges (Conditions of Service) Act, 1954

Summary: Service - Advocates & Judges - High Court Judges (Conditions of Service) Act, 1954 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Petitioner, retired Judge, challenging ceiling on additional pension appearing in clause (b) of para 2 of Part II of First Schedule to Act - Whether once a ceiling limit was fixed as contained in proviso of Third Part of Act, para 2(b), ceiling on additional pension appearing in Part II of First Schedule to Act is justified? - Ceiling of Rs.8000 is not necessary to be imposed and if that is applied, a situation giving rise to application of art. 14 of Constitution does arise; presence of proviso clearly brings out intention that no attempt is sought to be made between Judges recruited from different sources for matter of ceiling on pension - Held, ceiling in para 2(b) of Part III of First Schedule is unsustainable u/art. 14 of Constitution and would not be operative - Petition disposed of.
