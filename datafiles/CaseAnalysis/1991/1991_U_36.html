Laxmi Shankar Pandey v Union Of India And Ors

Supreme Court of India

19 March 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Jayachandra Reddy, S. Ratnavel Pandian

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 576; (1991) 2 SCC 488; (1991) SCC (L&S) 684; AIR 1991 SC 1070; 1991 (16) ATC 524; 1991 (1) CLR 807; 1991 CRLR 546; 1991 (62) FLR 652; JT 1991 (2) SC 43; 1991 (1) LLN 1043; 1991(1) SCALE 491; [1991] 1 S.C.R. 894; 1992 (2) SLJ 14; 1991 (2) SLR 373; 1991 (2) UJ 86; 1991 (2) UPLBEC 864

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Defence & Security Forces - Service - Central Reserve Police Force Act, 1949, ss. 8 and 11 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 311 - Dismissal from service - Appellant, head constable in Central Reserve Police Force - Accused of abetting escape of two prisoner from custody - Held, proper enquiry held - Opportunity to defend granted despite change of place of enquiry - Punishment was not disproportionate - Considering meritorious service of delinquent question of awarding lesser sentence directed to be considered if representation is made by delinquent - Petition dismissed.
