Revathinnal Balagopala Varma v His Highness Shri Padmanabha Dasa Bala Rama Varma (Since Deceased) and Others

Supreme Court of India

28 November 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Ranganathan, M. Fathima Beevi

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 401; (1993) Supp1 SCC 233; JT 1991 (5) SC 301; 1991(2) SCALE 1142; [1991] Supp3 S.C.R. 30

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Land & Property

Keywords: Tarwad, Sthanamdar, Kerala Joint Hindu Family System Abolition Act, 1975

Summary: Land & Property - Family & Personal - Suit for partition - Personal properties - Legality -Respondent no. 1 was erstwhile Maharaja of Travancore - Appellant and defendant nos. 1 to 34 filed suit for partition under the Act claiming that suit properties were divisible among himself and 34 defendants in equal shares and that respondent no.1 was entitled only to an one-thirty fifth share - Trial Court dismissed suit holding that appellant and defendant nos.1 to 34 did not belong to an undivided marumakkathayam tarwad and that respondent no.1 was not karnavan of any such tarwad nor were properties in suit tarwad properties as claimed by appellant - Aggrieved, appellant preferred appeal before HC which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeals - Whether family of appellant and defendant nos.1 to 34 was an undivided marumakkathayam tarwad governed by marumakkathayam law and suit properties belonged to tarwad and respondent 1 had been managing same as its karnavan as alleged by appellant - Held, properties in suit were not joint family properties as asserted by appellant but were personal properties of respondent no.1 - However, properties in suit were earmarked by respondent no. 1 as his personal properties which he was competent to do as a sovereign and Govt. by accepting or approving list of properties submitted by him as his personal properties in pursuance of covenant did not purport on its own to create any right in favour of respondent no.1 in such properties - Govt. could have disputed list submitted by respondent no.1 but it chose not to do so and assertion of respondent no.1 that properties in suit were his personal properties was accepted - In instant case, appellant asserting that properties in suit belonged to a joint family and respondent no. 1 even though a sovereign ruler held them as head of family to which property belonged appellant had neither specifically pleaded nor produced any convincing evidence in support of such an assertion - Therefore, properties in suit were not joint family properties as asserted by appellant but were personal properties of respondent no. 1 - Appeals dismissed.
