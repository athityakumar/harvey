Jaswant Singh Mathura Singh and Another v Ahmedabad Municipal Corporation and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 October 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, A.M. Ahmadi, K. Jagannatha Shetty

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 972; (1992) Supp1 SCC 5; AIR 1991 SC 2130; JT 1991 (4) SC 46; 1991(2) SCALE 749; [1991] Supp1 S.C.R. 226; 1992 (1) UJ 14

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Rent Control

Keywords: Suit For Permanent Injunction

Summary: Land & Property - Rent Control - Suit for permanent injunction - Decreed - Maintainability - Appellants filed suit for permanent injunction restraining the respondent not to enforce the reconstituted - Suit was decreed by Trial Court - Appeal was filed by responded before Single Judge which was allowed - DB upheld the order passed by Single Judge - Hence instant appeal - Whether the orders passed by Court was maintainable - Held, appellant was in possession as tenant for well over half century and it was injuriously affected by scheme which had effect of terminating appellants possession and adversely affects its business in demised premises - Since it was running business over the years respondent was directed to provide alternative premises by allotting suitable shop within the city to appellant to put it in possession thereof and until then allow its occupation of demised shop - In instant case appellant did not vacate or created any obstruction in any form in matter of possession it would be open to respondent to have appellant ejected - There was no interfere with scheme - Appeal allowed.
