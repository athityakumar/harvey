N. K. Jain and Others v C. K. Shah and Others

Supreme Court of India

26 March 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Jayachandra Reddy, S. Ratnavel Pandian

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 589; (1991) SCC (Cr) 328; (1991) 2 SCC 495; (1991) SCC (L&S) 656; AIR 1991 SC 1289; 1991 (2) Crimes 94; 1991 CRLJ 1347; 1991 CRLR 363; 1991 (62) FLR 657; JT 1991 (2) SC 52; JT 1991 (2) SC 52B; 1991 LabIC 1013; 1991 (2) LLN 443; 1991(1) SCALE 519; [1991] 1 S.C.R. 938

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Central Provinces And Berar Industrial Disputes Settlement Act, 1947

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Practice & Procedure - Employees' Provident Funds and Miscellaneous Provisions Act, 1952, ss. 6, 14, 17, 14, 14(1A), 14(2), 14(2A), 14A(1), 14A(2) - Employees' Provident Fund Scheme 1952 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, chap. 20 - Provident fund - Failure to contribute - Criminal proceedings - Exemption under the Act - Legality - Appellants were connected with management of a company, which was exempted under the Act from the applicability of the Scheme - Criminal complaints were filed against appellants for alleged failure to pay contributions to provident fund trust and thereby committed offences u/ss. 14(1A), 14(2), 14(2A), 14A(1), 14A(2) of the Act and para 76 of the Scheme - Appellants filed an application praying that the proceedings against them should be dropped and they should be acquitted on the ground that s. 6 of the Act was not applicable to establishment exempted u/s. 17 of the Act and thus no proceedings u/s. 14 of the Act could be initiated against them - Magistrate rejected the application - Aggrieved appellants filed 3 criminal revision applications in Lower Appellate Court, which was dismissed by common order holding that s. 6 of the Act covers and attracts all establishments including the exempted establishment - Hence, instant appeals - Whether criminal proceedings can be instituted u/s. 14 of 1952 Act against an establishment exempted u/s. 17 of the Act for the contravention of provisions of s. 6 of the Act - Held, it was difficult to agree that cancellation of exemption granted u/s. 17(4) of the Act amount to a penalty under the Act within meaning of s. 14(12A) of the Act - By a cancellation of exemption only the privilege granted was withdrawn by an executive order - Suffice it to say that such a cancellation did not penalise the management and consequently did not result in any punishment that was normally awarded in respect of an offence - Thus, in instant case, a purposive approach was necessary, the interpretation of word 'penalty' used in s. 14(2A) of the Act did not present any difficulty and cancellation was not a punishment amounting to penalty within the meaning of that section - In view of object underlying the Act the context did definitely require a reasonable interpretation of s. 14(2A) so as to make it applicable also to a case of failure to contribute to the fund as per conditions under which the exemption was granted - Like-wise it should be interpreted to mean that cancellation did not amount to a penalty - Thus, the submission that s. 14(2A) of the Act was not attracted did not merit acceptance - Mere fact that s. 17(1A) of the Act was introduced in year 1988 did not necessarily lead to an inference that ss. 14(IA) and 14(2A) of the Act were not intended to be made applicable to an exempted establishment - Object underlying every amendment was mainly intended to render the penal provisions more stringent in order to check the growth of arrears and to punish the defaulters - Likewise in Amendment Act No. 33 of 1988 also it was intended to make the existing penal provisions more stringent - That Amendment Act was passed on the recommendations of a high-level committee set up to review the working of employees provident fund organisation and to suggest improvements - One of the recommendations was to make the existing penal provisions more stringent and also make the existing legal and penal provisions as applicable to unexempted establishments being made applicable to exempted establishments so as to check the defaults on their part - Thus, under the Amendment Act, Legislature wanted to make as far as possible those existing legal and penal provisions which were applicable to unexempted establishments, applicable also to exempted establishments - That did not mean that there were no penal provisions earlier applicable to exempted establishments - Ss. 14(1A) and 14(2A) of the Act were attracted to present case and thus it could not be said that there was no prima facie case and consequently accused could not claim any acquittal, even before conclusion of trial under chap. 20 of CrPC - Thus, Trial Court might proceed with trial for offences u/ss. 14(1A) and 14(2A) against appellants and dispose of matter - Appeals disposed of.
