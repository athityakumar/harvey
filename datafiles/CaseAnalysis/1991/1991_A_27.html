Narain Das Jain (Since Deceased) By L.Rs v Agra Nagar Mahapalika, Agra

Supreme Court of India

14 February 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

M.M. Punchhi, K.N. Saikia

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 1055; (1991) 4 SCC 212; AIR 1991 SCW 540; 1991 (1) CCC 548; JT 1991 (1) SC 461; 1991(1) SCALE 215; [1991] 1 S.C.R. 389; 1991 (2) UJ 26; 1991 (1) UPLBEC 553

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4, 18 and 23(2) - Uttar Pradesh Town Improvement Act, 1919, s. 36(2) - Uttar Pradesh Town Improvement (Appeals) Act, 1920 - Appellant was owner of 48613 sq. yards of land - Same was acquired by Agra Town Improvement Trust under provisions of Uttar Pradesh Town Improvement Act, 1919, which is analogous to s. 4 of Land Acquisition Act - For land acquisition appellant was paid a partly sum of Rs. 1344 -2 annas and 6 paise as compensation and no solatium was awarded as none was awardable under Uttar Pradesh Town Improvement Act, 1919 - Feeling dissatisfied appellant sought a reference u/s. 18 of Land Acquisition Act, 1894 before Tribunal - Tribunal partly accepted claim raising compensation to rate of Rs. 3 per sq yard and interest on amount due at rate of 4 -1/2% per annum - Tribunal of High Court disposed appeals and appellant was awarded enhanced compensation at rate of Rs. 4 per sq yard - HC also changed rate of interest to 6% p.a. - Claim of appellant to solatium at rate of 15 % on sum awarded uptill - Tribunal's level was rejected as appellant had not claimed same before Tribunal and had not made a grouse thereof in his appeal before HC - Whether appellant liable to receive claim of solatium which has been declined by HC? - Held, claim to solatium arose on basis of Om Prakash &Another v. State of U.P. and Others v. State of U.P. and Others (1974 (2) SCC 731) on 14/12/1973 by which provisions of Uttar Pradesh Town Improvement Act whereunder solatium was withheld were struck down, and on that date appeal of appellant against order of Tribunal dated 5/11/1965 was pending before HC - Claim to solatiun surfaced and compulsory acquistition of land but also on law on subject being declared by SC in Om Prakash's case (supra) - Thus, HC should have measured claim of appellant to solatium on sum awarded by Tribunal with same yardstick as to sum awarded by it and modified in decree accordingly - No hesitation in upsetting judgment and order of HC in this regard and award to appellant solatium at rate of 15% on entire market value of land - Appellant further shall be entitled to interest at rate of 6% per annum - Appeal allowed.
