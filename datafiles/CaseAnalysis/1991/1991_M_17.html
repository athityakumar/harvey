The Collector Of Central Excise, Madras v M/S. M.M. Rubber And Co. Tamil Nadu

Supreme Court of India

04 September 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

Veeraswami Ramaswami, S. Ranganathan

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 600; (1992) Supp1 SCC 471; AIR 1991 SC 2141; 1992 CRLR 35; 1992 CrLR(SC) 35; 1992 (37) ECC 16; 1993 (49) ECR 177; 1991 (55) E.L.T. 289; JT 1991 (3) SC 587; 1991(2) SCALE 473; [1991] 3 S.C.R. 862; [1992] 63 TAXMAN 131; 1991 (2) UJ 658

Case Digest

Subject: Excise

Keywords: Customs and Excise Revenues Appellate Tribunal Act, 1986

Summary: Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Central Excises and Salt Act 1944, s. 35 E(3) - Question was computation of limitation period of one year was to be done from which date - Central Board of Excise and Customs directing Collector to file appeal against order by him - Held, power has to be exercised by Board within one year from date of Collector's order whereas, date of knowledge of order to Board was irrelevant.
