K.M. Mathew v State Of Kerala And Anr.

Supreme Court of India

19 November 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Jagannatha Shetty, Yogeshwar Dayal

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 791; (1992) SCC (Cr) 88; (1992) 1 SCC 217; AIR 1992 SC 2206; AIR 1992 SCW 2666; 1992 (1) CCR 316; 1992 CompLJ 3779; 1991 (3) Crimes 820; 1992 CRLJ 3779; 1992 CrLR(SC) 170; 1992 (57) E.L.T. 370; JT 1991 (4) SC 464; 1992 (1) KLT 1; 1992 (1) MWN 36; 1992 (2) PLR 35; [1991] Supp2 S.C.R. 364

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Customs; Indirect Tax; Media & Entertainment

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Criminal - Media & Entertainment - Code Of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 204 - Press And Registration Of Books Act, 1867, s. 7 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 500 and 34 - Whether court has jurisdiction to drop proceedings against accused? - Held, yes - If read carefully provisions relating to trial of summons-cases, power to drop proceedings against accused cannot be denied to Magistrate u/s. 204 of CrPC - Proceedings before Magistrate commences upon taking cognizance of offence and issue of summons to accused - Need to try 'the accused arises' when there is an allegation in complaint that accused has committed crime - For a Magistrate to take cognizance of offence as against Chief Editor, there must be positive averments in complaint of knowledge of objectionable character of matter - Complaint in instant case does not contain any such allegation - In absence of such allegation, Magistrate was justified in directing that complaint so far as it relates to Chief Editor could not be proceeded with - If there is no allegation in complaint involving accused in commission of crime, it is implied that Magistrate has no jurisdiction to proceed against accused - S. 7 raises presumption in respect of a person who is named as editor and printed as such on every copy of newspaper - Act does not recognise any other legal entity for, rising presumption - Even if name of Chief Editor is printed in newspaper there is no presumption against him u/s. 7 of Act - Appeal allowed.
