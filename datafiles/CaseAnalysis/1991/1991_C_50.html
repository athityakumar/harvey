Vania Silk Mills (P) Limited v Commissioner Of Income-Tax, Ahmedabad

Supreme Court of India

14 August 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

P.B. Sawant, K.N. Singh

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 747; (1991) 4 SCC 22; AIR 1991 SC 2104; 1991 (3) CompLJ 124; 1991 (98) CTR 153; [1991] 191 ITR 647; JT 1991 (3) SC 394; 1991(2) SCALE 327; [1991] 3 S.C.R. 577; [1991] 59 TAXMAN 3; 1991 (2) TLR 935

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Destruction

Summary: Income Tax and Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, ss. 2(47) and 45 - Income tax charged on capital gains on transfer of asset in any of the prescribed modes - But for that the asset must be in existence during process of transfer - Destruction or damage of capital asset - Brings about extinguishment of right in that asset - But it does not involve transfer of asset - Insurance claim received - Whether Insurance claim received on account of destruction or damage of a capital asset is chargeable to capital gain by deeming it to be received on account of transfer of asset - Held, not chargeable to capital gains - Appeal allowed.
