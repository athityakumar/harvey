Collector of Central Excise, Jaipur v Rajasthan State Chemical Works, Deedwana, Rajasthan and Others

Supreme Court of India

17 September 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Fathima Beevi, S. Ranganathan, N.D. Ojha

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 819; (1991) 4 SCC 473; AIR 1991 SC 2222; 1992 CRLR 77; 1991 (36) ECR 465; 1991 (55) E.L.T. 444; JT 1991 (4) SC 6; 1991(2) SCALE 602; [1991] Supp1 S.C.R. 124; 1992 (1) UJ 25

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Continuous

Summary: Excise - Indirect Tax - Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944, s. 2(f), chap. I, Item 68 - Exemption - Notification No. 179/77-CE dt. 18.06.1977 - Respondent, manufacturers of crude sodium sulphate, pumping brine in salt pans with diesel pumps for manufacturing sodium sulphate, claimed benefit of notification no. 179/77-CE - Benefit of said notification was denied to the respondent on the ground that the pumping of brine into the pans was carried on with the aid of power - Collector allowed appeal filed against said order - Said order was affirmed by the tribunal - Department filed appeal against said order contending that pumping the brine into the pan or lifting the raw materials to the kiln head is a process in relation to the manufacture of the final product and since that process with the aid of power is integrally connected with the manufacture, the exemption would not apply - Whether respondent is entitled for exemption under notification no. 179/77-CE? - Held, no - If any operation in the course of manufacture is so integrally connected with the further operation which result in the emergence of manufactured goods and such operation is carried on with the aid of power, the process in or in relation to the manufacture must be deemed to be one carried on with the with the aid of power - In the present case, contention that since the pumping of the brine into the salt pans or the lifting of coke and limestone with the aid of power does not being about any change in the raw material, the case is not taken out of the notification is not accepted - Hence, respondent is not entitled for exemption under notification no. 179/77-CE as both are manufacturing processes carried with aid of power - Revenue's appeal allowed.
