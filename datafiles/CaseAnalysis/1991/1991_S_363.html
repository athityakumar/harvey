State of Punjab and Others v Surinder Kumar and Others

Supreme Court of India

20 December 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

L.M. Sharma, S.C. Agrawal, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 952; (1992) SCC (L&S) 345; (1992) 1 SCC 489; AIR 1992 SC 1593; AIR 1992 SCW 1728; 1992 (19) ATC 500; [1992] 73 Comp Cas 490; 1992 (64) FLR 433; [1992] 194 ITR 434; JT 1991 (6) SC 540; 1992 (1) LLN 537; 1991(2) SCALE 1429; 1992 (1) SLR 335; [1991] Supp3 S.C.R. 553

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 141, 142 and 226 - Precedent - Judgment of Supreme Court - Requirement of assigning reasons for order or judgment is not imperative - Order of Supreme Court granting certain relief in exercise of powers u/art. 142 - Whether similar order can be issued by High Court - Held, cannot be issued - Appeal allowed.
