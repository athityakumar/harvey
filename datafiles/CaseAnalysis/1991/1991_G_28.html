R. Mcdill And Company Pvt. Ltd. v Gouri Shankar Sarda And Others

Supreme Court of India

13 March 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

N.M. Kasliwal, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 1021; (1991) 2 SCC 548; AIR 1991 SCW 826; 1991 (1) ARBLR 290; 1991 (2) CalLT 9; 1992 (2) CalLT(SC) 9; 1991 (1) GLH 650; JT 1991 (1) SC 645; [1991] 1 S.C.R. 809

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Civil Procedure; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Practice & Procedure - Arbitration Act, 1940, ss. 4, 14, 17, 34 and 41 - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, ss. 20, 86 and 87-B, O. 23, O. 21, r. 46 - (A) Whether provisions of O. 23 CPC apply to an application for stay of suit u/s. 34 of 1940 Act ? - S. 41 of 1940 Act itself provides that provisions of CPC shall apply to all proceedings before Court - No provision in Act so as to take away provisions of O. 23 CPC from being applied to applications filed u/s. 34 of Act, in a suit - Held, issue arises from a plaint filed by plaintiff and in such a suit if any application is filed under Act, same ought to be governed by provisions of CPC - (B) Whether High Court was justified in dismissing appeal u/O. 23 on ground that two applications were not maintainable as same were not covered within liberty granted to make fresh application? - Explanation given by appellants for moving two separate applications is that they were given a legal advice to move two separate applications as there were two different agreements between appellants 1 and 2 and respondent 1- No element of malafide - Both applications submitted within 30 days of order - Further held, HC was not correct in dismissing applications on ground that two applications were not maintainable as same were not covered within order passed by HC - Thus,the impugned order of HC is set aside - Appeals allowed.
