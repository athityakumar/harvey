Jai Prakash v State (Delhi Administration)

Supreme Court of India

05 February 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Jayachandra Reddy, M. Fathima Beevi, S. Ratnavel Pandian

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 651; (1991) 2 SCC 32; (1991) SCC (Cr) 299; AIR 1991 SCW 654; 1991 (2) AWC 890; 1991 (1) Crimes 474; 1991 CRLR 167; 1991 CrLR(SC) 167; JT 1991 (1) SC 288; 1991(1) SCALE 114; [1991] 1 S.C.R. 202; 1991 (1) ShimLC 330

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Kirpan

Summary: Criminal - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 300, 302 and 100 - Murder - Prosecution case solely rests on one eye-witness - One injury caused by appellant on deceased - Whether injury was sufficient to cause death and whether appellant can be convicted for murder? - Held, in absence of any explanation that appellant did not intend to cause death, appellant can be convicted for murder - Virsa Singh v. State of Punjab 1958 Indlaw SC 82 followed - 'Knowledge ' and 'Intention' needs to be contrasted - And even if only one injury is caused which was sufficient to cause instantaneous death appellant liable to be convicted for murder - Further held, circumstances would show that the appellant intentionally inflicted that injury though it may not be premeditated one - All the circumstances indicate such a state of mind namely that he aimed and inflicted that injury with a deadly weapon - Appeal dismissed.
