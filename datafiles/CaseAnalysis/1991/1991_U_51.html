 Shankarsan Dash v Union Of India

Supreme Court of India

30 April 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

L.M. Sharma, B.C. Ray, K. Jagannatha Shetty, M.N. Venkatachaliah, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 188; (1991) 3 SCC 47; (1991) SCC (L&S) 800; AIR 1991 SC 1612; AIR 1991 SCW 1583; 1991 (17) ATC 95; 1991 (62) FLR 981; JT 1991 (2) SC 380; 1991 LabIC 1460; 1992 (2) LLJ 18; 1991 (2) LLN 65; 1991(1) SCALE 848; [1991] 2 S.C.R. 567; 1992 (1) SLJ 7; 1991 (2) SLR 779; 1991 (2) UJ 212; 1991 (2) UPLBEC 933

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Challenged

Summary: Service - Vacancies - Challenged - Appellant was selected in combined Civil Services Examination held by Union Public Service Commission for appointment to several services including IPS and Police Services Group 'B'. Position of the appellant in merit list was not high enough to be included in the IPS and he was offered appointment to Delhi Andaman and Nicobar police Service (DANIP)in Police Service Group 'B' which he accepted - On account of several candidates, allotted to Police Services Group 'B' not joining, the position of the appellant improved and ultimately he was on the top of the list. Three vacancies in the reserved category were filled up by candidates who had been earlier appointed in DANIP Service - Appellant made representation and prayed that these vacancies also should be filled up. The request was turned down - Application before HC which was dismissed by impugned order - Hence instant appeal - Whether candidate whose name appeared in the merit list on the basis of competitive examination, acquires indefeasible right of appointment as Government servant if a vacancy exists.

Held, it is fully established that there has not been any arbitrariness what's over on the part of the respondent in filling up the vacancies in question or the other vacancies referred to by appellant. The process of final selection had to be closed at some stage as was actually done. A decision in this regard was accordingly taken and the process for further allotment to any vacancy arising later was closed. Certain appointments actually made subsequent to this stage and urged that by those dates the further vacancies in the IPS had arisen to which the appellant and the other successful candidates should have been adjusted. It is not material if in pursuance of decision already taken before closing the process of final selection, the formal appointments were concluded later. Mere completing the formalities cannot be of any help to the appellant. SC do not consider it necessary to mention all the details in this connection available from the large number of documents which we closely examined during the hearing at considerable length and do not have any hesitation in rejecting in this regard based on the factual aspect. So far the decision to adopt a different policy with respect to filling up of the reserved vacancies is concerned the same is justified on account of the special circumstances mentioned in the respondent's affidavits. The decision to depart from the confirmed policy was taken after consideration by the authorities of the position in regard to unavailability of qualified candidates from year to year adversely affecting the desired strength of the reserved candidates in the services and cannot be condemned on the grounds of arbitrariness and illegal discrimination. Hence, there was no merit in instant appeal. Appeal dismissed.
