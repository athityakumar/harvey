Chairman/Director, Combined Entrance Examination (CEE) 1990 v Osiris Das and Others

Supreme Court of India

26 July 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

K.N. Singh, P.B. Sawant

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 716; (1992) 3 SCC 543; JT 1992 (Supp) SC 108

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Education - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 -Admission - (A) Whether reservation quota of 5 percent towards sons, daughters and spouses of employees of University valid? - Held, G.B. Pant University is aided and financed by State Govt. and University is an instrumentality of State - Any instrumentality of State cannot give preferential treatment to a class of persons without there being justification for same - Reservation of seats for admission to B.Tech. course in favour of sons and wards of employees of University is violative of doctrine of equality enshrined u/art. 14 of Constitution of India, 1950 - No rationale for reservation of seats in favour of sons and wards of employees of University nor such any reservation has any reasonable nexus with object which is sought to be achieved by University - Further held, once State Govt. and University both decided not to have any reserved quota for sons and wards of employees of University, no legal right could be claimed by respondent-students for being given preferential treatment in matter of admission to B.Tech. courses of University - (B) Whether High Court right in issuing interim orders directing provisional admission of respondent-students to B.Tech. course of G. B. Pant University of Agriculture and Technology against reserved quota of 5 percent towards sons, daughters and spouses of employees of University? - Held, since reservation of seats in favour of sons and wards of employees of University was apparently violative of art. 14 of Constitution of India, 1950, no court could issue directions for enforcement of any such reservation - (B) Admission - Right of - Held, once admission to an institution or a course of study is determined on merit on basis of evaluation of marks at Entrance Examination, no student securing lower marks has any legal right for admission much less a right enforceable in a Court of Law - Order accordingly.
