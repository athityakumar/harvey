State Of U.P. v Kapil Deo And Another

Supreme Court of India

21 August 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

M.M. Punchhi, Kuldip Singh

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 223; (1991) Supp2 SCC 170; (1991) SCC (Cr) 1008; 1991 (28) ACC 478; AIR 1991 SC 2257; 1992 (1) CCR 85; 1991 CRLJ 3321; 1991 CrLR(SC) 795; JT 1991 (3) SC 482; 1991(2) SCALE 384; [1991] 3 S.C.R. 692

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 34 and 201 - Eye witness - Whether an accused can be prosecuted for offence u/s. 201, IPC with aid of s. 34? - Duvvur Dasratharammareddy v State of Andhra Pradesh wherein it was held that if evidence relating to offence of murder and disappearance of evidence is same and case of prosecution regarding Offence of murder is not accepted, it follows that accused can not be convicted' for offence u/s. 201, IPC relied on - Held, High Court observed that there was hardly any evidence to connect accused with murder especially when eye witnesses account given by PWs 1 to 3 was not very reliable - Nor could it be inferred that murder was committed in furtherance of common intention of all - State of evidence is insufficient to prove accused to have placed dead body in trunk - No reason to differ from the views expressed by Trial Judge, and which was a possible view, having regard to evidence - Appeals dismissed.
