Texmaco Ltd. v Collector Of Central Excise, Calcutta

Supreme Court of India

31 July 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

M.N. Venkatachaliah, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 10; (1991) Supp2 SCC 305; AIR 1992 SC 1801; AIR 1992 SCW 2020; 1991 (37) ECR 369; 1995 (77) E.L.T. 501; JT 1991 (6) SC 180; 1991(2) SCALE 755; [1991] 3 S.C.R. 323

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944, s. 3 - Central Excise Rules, 1944, r. 8, Condition IV of Notification - Assessable value - Goods manufactured by using material and components supplied free by customer - Whether assessable value of manufactured goods would include costs of free supplies ? - Ownership of goods, if a consideration for levy of excise duty - Benefit of calculating duty on basis of invoice price - Whether condition (IV) of Notification requires that invoice value should be same as assessable value ? - Held, assessable value would include costs of free supplies - Ownership of goods is no consideration - Condition (IV) cannot be read so - Appeal allowed.
