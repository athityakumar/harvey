M. B. Sanghi, Advocate v High Court of Punjab and Haryana and Others

Supreme Court of India

31 July 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 53; (1991) 3 SCC 600; (1991) SCC (Cr) 897; AIR 1991 SC 1834; 1992 (1) CCR 394; 1991 CRLJ 2648; 1991 CrLR(SC) 635; JT 1991 (3) SC 318; 1991(2) SCALE 228; [1991] 3 S.C.R. 312

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Practice & Procedure - Contempt of Courts Act, 1971, s. 19 - Appellant-advocate made disparaging remarks against judicial officer - High Court took notice of incident and initiated contempt proceedings against appellant and sentenced to seven days simple imprisonment - Appellant made unconditional apology before HC but was rejected as appellant was found to be involved in such practice even prior to this incident - Present appeal - With regard to apology in proceedings for contempt of court, it is well settled that an apology is not a weapon of defence to purge guilty of their offence; nor is it intended to operate as a universal panacea, but it is intended to be evidence of real contriteness - Held, if not punished for such practice, it will tarnish image of independent judiciary - Appeal dismissed.
