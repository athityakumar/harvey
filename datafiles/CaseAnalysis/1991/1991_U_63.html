 K. M. Abdulla Kunhi and B. L. Abdul Khader v Union of India and Others State of Karnataka and Others

Supreme Court of India

23 January 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Jagannatha Shetty, L.M. Sharma, M.H. Kania, B.C. Ray, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 473; (1991) 1 SCC 476; (1991) SCC (Cr) 613; AIR 1991 SC 574; AIR 1991 SCW 362; [1992] 73 Comp Cas 470; 1991 (1) Crimes 341; 1991 CRLJ 790; 1991 CrLR(SC) 153; 1991 (52) E.L.T. 321; JT 1991 (2) SC 216; 1991(3) SCALE 58; [1991] 1 S.C.R. 102

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Comments, Preventive Detention Act, 1950

Summary: Criminal - Detention - Conservation of Foreign Exchange and Prevention of Smuggling Activities Act, 1974 - ss. 3, 8, 10 and 11 - Constitution of India, 1950 - arts. 22 - Appellant was detained for dealing in contraband gold - Advisory Board opined that sufficient cause existed for the detention of detenue - Representation of the detenu was not considered before confirming the detention order - Whether the order of detention was vitiated - Held, no - Confirmation of detention cannot be vitiated on ground that representation is considered after confirmation of detention - Purpose of Advisory Board is to examine whether reasons for detention were sufficient or not - Consideration by Advisory Board is additional safeguard and not substitute for consideration by Govt. - Disposed accordingly.
