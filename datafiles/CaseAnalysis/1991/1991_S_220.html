Ashok Alias Somanna Gowda And Anr. v State of Karnataka By Its Chief Secretary and

Supreme Court of India

11 October 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

N.M. Kasliwal, M.M. Punchhi

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 346; (1992) 1 SCC 28; (1992) SCC (L&S) 38; AIR 1992 SC 80; 1992 (19) ATC 68; JT 1991 (4) SC 160; 1991 LabIC 2407; 1992 (1) LLJ 89; 1991 (2) LLN 1185; 1991(2) SCALE 796; 1992 (3) SLR 149; [1991] Supp1 S.C.R. 493; 1992 (1) UJ 441

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Karnataka State Civil Services (Direct recruitment by Selection) Rules 1973

Summary: Service - Constitution - Karnataka State Civil Services Rules, 1973, r. 1- Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 16, 136, 309 and 311 - (A) Recruitment - Examination and interview test prescribed - Allotment of 33.3% of total marks for interview - Whether justified ? - (B) Recruitment - Only two candidates aggrieved by appointments seeking relief by approaching Administrative Tribunal in time - Appointments made some four years back - Whether to grant relief ? - Held, (A) Not justified - (B) Supreme Court did not feel inclined to grant relief to others not approaching for redress within reasonable time - Appeal allowed.
