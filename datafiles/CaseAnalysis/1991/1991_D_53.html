Chakkan Lal and Others v District Judge, Agra and Others

Supreme Court of India

05 December 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

G.N. Ray, M.M. Punchhi

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 286; (1992) Supp2 SCC 166; (1992) SCC (L&S) 727; AIR 1992 SC 561; 1992 (21) ATC 124; 1992 LabIC 308

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Interlocutory applications - Maintainability - Instant interim applications were made in Civil Appeal No. 1104 of 1982 which was decided by Court on 9-1-1980 - In Interim Application No. 2 of 1991, a direction was sought by appellants of Civil Appeal No. 1104 of 1982 requiring HC to decide issue regarding applicability of G.O regarding grant of benefits of reservation in matter of promotion for employees working in District Courts - In Interim Application No. 3 of 1991 some people approached this Court to make them parties to appeal and variation was sought in final orders of disposal - Hence instant applications.

Held, after disposal of Civil Appeal No. 1104 of 1982, an IA No. 1 of 1990 was filed by some persons seeking impleadment and clarification. Court expressed no opinion on claim of then applicants but left HC free to deal with matter indicating that if there is no direction for reservation in accordance with law, it was open to HC to meet situation by making its own order. That order was passed on 9-5-1991. HC it appears taking stock of mandate of both above said orders to made an order on administrative side on 26-7-1991 whereby benefits already granted to and being enjoyed by appellants/revisionists in Civil Appeal No. 1104 of 1982 were ordered not to be disturbed. Consequential orders were passed to effect that pecuniary benefits admissible to revisionists in said order dt.9-1-1991 be made available to them promptly if not done already. In face of this order direction sought by applicants in IA No. 2 of 1991 is now of no substance. Similarly directions sought for impleadment and variation of the order in Civil Appeal No. 1104 of 1982 by means of IA No. 3 of 1991 becomes of no substance. Still applicants in IA No. 2 of 1991 have gone on to assail orders of HC dt.26-7-1991 and have pressed for a direction. HC was found faulted on administrative side to have relied upon that decision and for its administrative advice to District Judge on that basis and as a consequence District Judge to have recalled benefits of reservation. Resultantly it was ordered by this Court that benefits already available to appellants therein shall be restored to them with all consequential benefits and reference made by District Judge shall be deemed to be pending. Now that pending reference of District Judge had to be decided by HC on administrative side, requested as it was by this Court to take decision on the basis of law laid down in a series of cases of this Court relevant to point and thereafter to advise District Judge as to what action need be taken for the purpose. It seems that HC in pursuit of that goad has made a decision on 26-7-1991. The claim of applicants in IA No. 2 of 1991 to effect that Court must correct the order of HC passed on administrative side on anvil of ratio in Mohan Lal Mehrotra case appears to misplaced. As far as this Court is concerned it has disposed of matter in substance in Civil Appeal No. 1104 of 1982. By means of an interim application, such relief is not admissible. Applications shall stand disposed of without any order. Order accordingly.
