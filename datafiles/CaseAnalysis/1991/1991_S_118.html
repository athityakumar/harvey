Bollavaram Pedda Narsi Reddy And Ors. v State Of Andhra Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

07 May 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Fathima Beevi, Kuldip Singh

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 86; (1991) 3 SCC 434; (1991) SCC (Cr) 586; AIR 1991 SC 1468; 1991 CRLJ 1833; 1991 CrLR(SC) 494; 1991(1) SCALE 909; [1991] 2 S.C.R. 723; 1991 (2) UJ 473

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 300 - Appreciation of evidence in murder case - Witnesses were strangers to accused - No cogent evidence that witnesses had clear vision of action of accused persons in order to enable them to recollect and identify accused even after lapse of long time as there was absence of proper light at place of occurrence - Held, accused entitled to acquittal as identification parade was not conducted properly and testimony of witnesses were not acceptable as well as involvement of accused not established beyond reasonable doubt.
