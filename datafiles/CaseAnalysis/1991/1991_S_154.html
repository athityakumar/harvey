Gurmej Singh And Ors. v State Of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

16 July 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, M. Fathima Beevi, Veeraswami Ramaswami

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 50; (1991) Supp2 SCC 75; (1991) SCC (Cr) 992; AIR 1992 SC 214; 1991 (3) Crimes 220; 1992 CRLJ 293; 1991 CrLR(SC) 563; JT 1991 (3) SC 90; 1991(2) SCALE 69; [1991] 2 S.C.R. 966

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 3, 114 and 8 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 300 - Evidence Act - Offence of murder - (A) Criminal case - Adverse inference against prosecution for dropping eye-witness - On ground of being won over - Defence not questioning it - Judgments revealing that no submission was made regarding non-examination of witness - His presence not required, prosecution story unfolded by other witnesses - (B) Appreciation of evidence - Prosecution witnesses clearly stating that blunt side of weapon was used - (C) Motive - Election disputes - Few months before murder, quarrel over passage of sullage water - Obstruction by deceased - Collecting near accused's house - Proceedings under CrPC on that score pending - Held, (A) Non-examination cannot reflect on credibility of other witnesses - (B) That being so, there is no scope for believing that sharp side was used - (C) Old enmity coupled with later incident regarding passage of sullage water constituted sufficient motive - Appeal dismissed.
