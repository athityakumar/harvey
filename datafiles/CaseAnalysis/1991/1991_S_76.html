Chief Security Officer and Others v Singasan Rabi Das

Supreme Court of India

09 January 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

M.H. Kania, Veeraswami Ramaswami, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 902; (1991) SCC (L&S) 415; (1991) 1 SCC 729; AIR 1991 SC 1043; 1991 (16) ATC 453; 1991 (1) CLR 882; 1991 (62) FLR 501; JT 1991 (5) SC 117; 1991 LabIC 1318; 1991 (1) LLJ 308; 1991 (1) LLN 1068; 1991(1) SCALE 47; 1991 (2) SLR 140; 1991 (1) UJ 366; 1991 (1) UPLBEC 201

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Railway Protection Force Rules 1959

Summary: Service - Railway Protection Force Act, 1957, s. 9 - Railway Protection Force Rules, 1959, rr. 44, 45 and 46 - Constitution of India, 1950, art.311(2), proviso, cl. (b) - Removal - Dispensation with departmental enquiry - Reasons given that at confronted enquiry witnesses of security/ railway employees would suffer personal humiliation and insults and their family members might become targets of acts of violence - Whether same are sufficient for dispensation? - Held, ground, personal humiliation to witnesses, totally insufficient - Appeal dismissed.
