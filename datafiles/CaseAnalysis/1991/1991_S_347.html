C.E.S.C. Ltd. Etc. v Subhash Chandra Bose And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

15 November 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranganath Misra, K. Ramaswamy, M.M. Punchhi

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 364; (1992) SCC (L&S) 313; (1992) 1 SCC 441; AIR 1992 SC 573; AIR 1992 SCW 202; 1992 (64) FLR 248; JT 1991 (6) SC 373; 1992 LabIC 332; 1992 (1) LLJ 475; 1992 (1) LLN 353; 1991(2) SCALE 996; 1992 (3) SLR 16; [1991] Supp2 S.C.R. 267; 1992 (1) UJ 111

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Convention, Supervisor, Medical Care, Social Security, Safety, Immediate Employer, Committee On Labour Welfare, 1969

Summary: Labour & Industrial Laws - Employees' State Insurance Act, 1948, s. 2(9) - Whether on facts found, right of principal employer to reject or accept work on completion, on scrutinising compliance with job requirements, as accomplished by a contractor, immediate employer, through his employees, is in itself an effective and meaningful 'supervision' as envisaged u/s. 2(9) of Act ? - Held, when employee is put to work under eye and gaze of principal employer, or his agent, where he can be watched secretly, accidentally, or occasionally, while work is in progress, so as to scrutinise quality thereof and to detect faults therein, as also put - To timely remedial measures by directions given, finally leading to satisfactory completion and acceptance of work, that would be supervision for purposes of s. 2(9) - Appeal dismissed.
