Tribhovandas Haribhai Tamboli v Gujarat Revenue Tribunal And Ors

Supreme Court of India

10 May 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, Kuldip Singh

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 850; (1991) 3 SCC 442; AIR 1991 SC 1538; 1992 (1) Bom.C.R. 290; 1991 (2) GLH 24; JT 1991 (2) SC 604; 1991(1) SCALE 958; [1991] 2 S.C.R. 802; 1991 (2) UJ 337

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Lunacy Act, 1912

Summary: Land & Property - Bombay Tenancy and Agricultural Lands Act, 1948, ss. 32(1) and 88(1) - (A) Interpretation of Statutes - Proviso - Clear and unambiguous language of main enactment - Whether Proviso can have repercussion on interpretation of main enactment? - (B) Tenant becoming 'deemed purchaser' from tillers day - Fixation of price by mamalatdar - Landlord was lunatic - While he was under disability, vendee alleged to have purchased land from son of landlord - Permission to alienate property not obtained under Lunacy Act - Effect on sale - (C) Benefit of proviso - Applicability of second proviso to s. 88(1)(b) and its effect on transfer or acquisition of land - Held, (A) proviso can have no repercussion on interpretation of main enactment so as to exclude from it, by implication what clearly falls within its express terms - (B) Sale is per se illegal - (C) second proviso to s. 88(1)(b) not applicable, right of tenant not affected - Transfer or acquisition must be valid and bona fide one and not colourable, fraudulent, fictitious or nominal - Appeal allowed.
