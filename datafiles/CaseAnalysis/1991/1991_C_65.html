Chandrakant Manilal Shah and Another v Commissioner of Income Tax

Supreme Court of India

24 October 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

N.D. Ojha, Veeraswami Ramaswami, S. Ranganathan

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 946; (1992) 1 SCC 76; AIR 1992 SC 197; 1991 (100) CTR 91; [1992] 193 ITR 1; JT 1991 (4) SC 171; 1991(2) SCALE 827; [1991] Supp1 S.C.R. 546; [1992] 60 TAXMAN 106; 1992 (2) TLR 30

Case Digest

Subject: Education; Family & Personal; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Registration of firm - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 26-A - Assessment year under reference is 1961-62 - 'C' was karta of a Hindu Undivided Family - 'N' one of sons of karta - Business converted into partnership between karta and 'N'- 'N' was admitted as a working partner - Application for registration of firm was dismissed by ITO on ground that there was no valid partnership - Whether, on facts and in circumstances of case, there was a valid partnership between 'C' as karta of Hindu Undivided Family and 'N' member of family? - Appellants contended that mere fact that 'N' had neither separated from Hindu Undivided Family nor brought in any cash asset as his capital contribution to partnership but was contributing only his skill and labour could not, in law, detract from a valid partnership being created - Relied on Lachhman Das v. CIT 1947 Indlaw PC 4 - Several High Courts uphold validity of a partnership between karta of a Hindu Undivided Family and an individual member of family where latter is taken in as working partner - It is illogical to hold that an undivided member of family can qualify for a share of profits in family business by offering moneys either his own or those derived by way of partition from family but not when he offers to be a working partner contributing labour and services of much more valuable expertise, skill and knowledge for making family business more prosperous - Held, it cannot be said that, when a coparcener enters into a partnership with karta of a Hindu Undivided Family and contributes only his skill and labour, no contribution of any separate asset belonging to such partner is made to meet requirement of a valid partnership - Question referred answered in affirmative, in favour of assessee and against revenue - Appeal allowed.
