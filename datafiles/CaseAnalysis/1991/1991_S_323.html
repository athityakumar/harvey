O.P. Garg and Others v State of Uttar Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

23 April 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

Kuldip Singh, M.H. Kania, P.B. Sawant

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 265; (1991) Supp2 SCC 51; (1991) SCC (L&S) 1146; AIR 1991 SC 1202; 1991 (17) ATC 380; 1991 (63) FLR 89; JT 1991 (2) SC 359; 1991 LabIC 1240; 1991 (1) LLN 1080; 1991(1) SCALE 768; [1991] 2 S.C.R. 424; 1991 (3) SLJ 61; 1991 (2) SLR 650; 1991 (2) UJ 571

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Keywords: Eligibility - Seniority - Cadre

Summary: Service - Advocates & Judges - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 309 - Uttar Pradesh Higher Judicial Service Rules, 1975, r. 8(2) - Dispute regarding inter se seniority of members of Uttar Pradesh Higher judicial service - All promotee officers working as Additional and District Judges on date on which Rules came into force against 236 posts - Whether can be deemed to be existing members of higher judicial service as constituted under 1975 Rules? - All 236 promotee officers working as Additional and District Judges on date on which Rules came into force against 236 posts shall be deemed to be existing members of Higher judicial service as constituted under Rules - Appeal allowed.
