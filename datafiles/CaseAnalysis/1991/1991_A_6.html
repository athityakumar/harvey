State of Jammu and Kashmir v A. R. Zakki and Others

Supreme Court of India

06 December 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Agrawal, L.M. Sharma, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 292; (1992) SCC (L&S) 427; (1992) Supp1 SCC 548; AIR 1992 SC 1546; AIR 1992 SCW 1711; 1992 (20) ATC 285; 1992 (64) FLR 724; JT 1992 (1) SC 59; 1992 (1) LLJ 891; 1992 (2) LLN 23; 1991(2) SCALE 1331; 1992 (3) SLR 3; [1991] Supp3 S.C.R. 216

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Constitution of Jammu and Kashmir

Summary: Constitution - Service - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 226 and 234 - Jammu and Kashmir Civil Services (Judicial) Recruitment Rules, 1967, r. 4 - Constitution of Jammu and Kashmir, ss. 110 and 111 - Recruitment - State Civil (Judicial) Services - Recommendations to reserve quota for employees of HC - State Government declined to implement and given effect - WP in High Court directed to implement - Letters Patent Appeal by appellant dismissed by DB - Whether direction by way of mandamus, could be issued u/art. 226 of Constitution in the matter of exercise of the rule making power u/s. 110 of the Jammu and Kashmir Constitution which is legislative in character? - Court held, No, s. 110 of the J and K Constitution, which is on the same lines as art. 234 of the Constitution, vests in the Governor, the power to make rules for appointments of persons other than the DJ to the Judicial Service of the State of J and K and for framing of such rules, the Governor is required to consult the Commission and the HC - This power to frame rules is legislative in nature, therefore writ of mandamus cannot, be issued directing the State Government to make the rules in accordance with the proposal made by the HC - Appeal allowed.
