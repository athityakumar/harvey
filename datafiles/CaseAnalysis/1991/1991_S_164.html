State Of Tamil Nadu And Ors v St. Joseph Teachers Training Instituteand Anr. Etc

Supreme Court of India

08 April 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

K.N. Singh, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 730; (1991) 3 SCC 87; JT 1991 (2) SC 343; 1991(1) SCALE 737; [1991] 2 S.C.R. 231; 1991 (2) SLR 605; 1991 (2) UJ 162; 1991 (2) UPLBEC 941

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Education - Constitution - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 30 - Permission to write at public examination - No recognition accorded to respondent institutions but students admitted for course of study for conferring Diploma in Teachers Training - Denial of such students to appear in public examination by state Govt. - Full Bench of High Court did not give any relief to aggrieved institutions - But directed on humanitarian grounds to State Govt. and Education Department to hold supplementary examination and held declaration of their result will be subject to ultimate settlement of question of recognition - Whether students of unrecognised educational institutions can be permitted to write public examinations held by Govt.? - Held, Courts cannot grant relief to a party on humanitarian grounds contrary to law - Since students of unrecognised institutions were legally not entitled to appear at examination held by Education Department of Govt., HC acted in violation of law in granting permission to such students for appearing at public examination - Directions issued by Full Bench are destructive of rule of law - Since Division Bench issued impugned orders following judgment of Full Bench, impugned orders are not sustainable in law - Court further, even if a minority community has fundamental right to establish and administer educational institution, it has no right to insist upon State to allow students to appear at public examinations without recognition or without complying with conditions prescribed for such recognition - Appeal allowed.
