Ajudh Raj And Ors. v Moti, S/O Mussadi

Supreme Court of India

03 May 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

L.M. Sharma, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 554; (1991) 3 SCC 136; AIR 1991 SC 1600; AIR 1991 SCW 1576; JT 1991 (2) SC 591; 1991(1) SCALE 896; [1991] 2 S.C.R. 690; 1991 (2) UJ 443

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Limitation Act, 1963, art. 65, 100 and 113 - Himachal Pradesh Abolition of Big Landed Estates and Land Reforms Act, 1954, s. 27 - Principle for deciding question of limitation in a suit filed after an adverse order under a Special Act - Held, in case order impugned in suit is such that it has to be set aside before any relief can be granted art. 100 will be attracted and if no particular art is applicable, suit must be governed by residuary art. 113 prescribing period of three years - If order is passed without jurisdiction, it is nullity and such suit will be covered by art. 65 - Appeal allowed.
