Satinder Singh Arora v State Bank of Patiala and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 October 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, K. Ramaswamy, M.M. Punchhi

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 452; (1992) Supp2 SCC 224; (1992) SCC (L&S) 660; 1992 (21) ATC 118

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: State Bank of Patiala (Officers) Service Regulations 1979

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14 and 311 - State Bank of Patiala (Officers) Service Regulations 1979, regns. 67(g) and 68(1) (ii) - (A) Whether Board of Directors could only dismiss petitioner it being appointing authority? - Held, Managing Director was designated as Appointing Authority and General Manager Disciplinary Authority for Junior and Middle Management ranks - Regn. 68(1) provides that disciplinary authority may itself or when so directed by its superior authority institute disciplinary proceedings against an officer - It is clear from charge-sheet that it was issued by General Manager (Operations) - Enquiry Officer had forwarded report to said General Manager - It was thereafter that Managing Director passed impugned order of removal from service - (B) Whether regn. 67(g) r/w regn. 68(1) (ii) permits hostile discrimination as u/art. 311(1) only appointing authority can terminate services? - Further held, petitioner does not belong to category of employees covered by art. 311(1) and as regulations stood at date of passing of impugned order Managing Director was clearly competent to pass impugned order of removal - Appeal dismissed.
