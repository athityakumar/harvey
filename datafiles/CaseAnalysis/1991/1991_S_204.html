Chandrakant Chimanlal Desai and another v State of Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

12 December 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

Veeraswami Ramaswami, A.M. Ahmadi, M. Fathima Beevi

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 418; (1992) SCC (Cr) 157; (1992) 1 SCC 473; AIR 1992 SCW 2028; 1992 (1) Crimes 232; 1992 CRLJ 2757; 1992 CRLR 235; JT 1991 (5) SC 373; 1991(2) SCALE 1352

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Evidence Act, 1872 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 201, 302, 404 and 507 - Murder - Appellants kidnapped deceased child and offered his life to deity to get a child - Appellants acquitted by Trial Court but on appeal convicted by High Court - Circumstantial evidence - Retracted confession - Confession not recorded in satisfactory manner and confessional statement was in hands of police before it was given to judge goes to show that confession was not voluntary - Prosecution case highly artificial and unconvincing - Held, appellants entitled to benefit of doubt - Convictions set-aside - Appeals allowed.
