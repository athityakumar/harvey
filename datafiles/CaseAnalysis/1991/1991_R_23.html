Ujjain Vikas Pradhikaran v Raj Kumar Johri And Ors. Etc.

Supreme Court of India

14 November 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranganath Misra, Kuldip Singh, S. Mohan

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 1003; (1992) 1 SCC 328; AIR 1992 SC 1538; JT 1991 (4) SC 424; 1991(2) SCALE 1097; [1991] Supp2 S.C.R. 247; 1992 (1) UJ 100

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, s. 4 - Madhya Pradesh Nagar Tatha Gram Nivesh Adhiniyam, 1973, s. 50(2) - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 133 - Land Acquisition Act - Notification - Purpose, scheme framed under M P Adhiniyam - Pre-conditions under M P Adhiniyam for completing Scheme not complied with - No mala fide in framing - Fact that if acquisition is not made land owners would enjoy benefit on account of development of neighbouring area made under scheme or if requisition is made they would claim higher compensation - Held, Supreme Court did not quash notification but enhanced date of notification by 3 years for purposes of calculating market value - Appeal allowed.
