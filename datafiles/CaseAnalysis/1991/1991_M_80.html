Shrisht Dhawan Smt v Messrs Shaw Brothers

Supreme Court of India

13 December 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

T.K. Thommen, R.M. Sahai

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 422; (1992) 1 SCC 534; AIR 1992 SC 1555; AIR 1992 SCW 1649; JT 1991 (5) SC 378; 1992 (1) RCJ 339; 1992 (1) RCR(Rent) 442; 1992 (1) RentLR 584; 1991(2) SCALE 1386; [1991] Supp3 S.C.R. 446; 1992 (1) UJ 346

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure; Rent Control

Keywords: Bona Fide Grounds

Summary: Rent Control - Delhi Rent Control Act, 1958, s. 21 - Property - Eviction - Limited tenancies - Summary procedure - Scope s. 21 of Act - Period expired and landlady applied for release of premises - Objection raised by tenant on ground of fraud - Burden of proof lied on tenant - Objection allowed by authorities - Ground, failure to prove her intention to get back premises for her son in absence of averment to that effect in her application - Held, authorities committed manifest error of law and misconstrued s. 21 - Appeal allowed.
