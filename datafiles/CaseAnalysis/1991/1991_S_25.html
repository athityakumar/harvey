Dayachand v Sahib Singh and Another

Supreme Court of India

05 March 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

J.S. Verma, B.C. Ray

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 652; (1991) 2 SCC 379; (1991) SCC (Cr) 438; AIR 1991 SC 930; 1992 (19) ALR 254; 1992 (2) CCR 152; 1991 (2) Crimes 31; 1991 CRLJ 1370; 1991 CRLR 315; JT 1991 (5) SC 444; 1991(1) SCALE 466

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Human Rights; Women & Children

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Criminal Procedure Code, 1974, ss. 437(1) FIR, proviso. and 401 - Age of accused at time of incident - There were conflicting evidence on record from two schools which couldn't be relied upon and medical examination showing age of accused not below 18 years of age - Concurrent finding of lower courts established, age of accused was above 16 years, and he was not juvenile - Held, respondent was not a juvenile, being above age of 16 years on July 26, 1988, date of incident - Appeal allowed.
