Bhikha Ram v Ram Sarup And Ors

Supreme Court of India

31 October 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, P.B. Sawant, Ranganath Misra

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 344; (1992) 1 SCC 319; AIR 1992 SC 207; JT 1991 (4) SC 199; 1991(2) SCALE 909; [1991] Supp2 S.C.R. 119; 1992 (1) UJ 445

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Punjab Pre-Emption Act, 1913

Summary: Land & Property - Punjab Pre-emption Act, 1913, ss. 15(1)(b), cl. 4 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14 and 15 - Expression 'other co-sharers' - Cover all co-sharers including those specified in erstwhile preceding three clauses - Those clause were based on rule of consanguinity and therefore were struck down - Exclusion of co-sharers specified in those clause was never intended - Interpretation - Held, word 'other' rendered redundant after striking down of three preceding clause - Appeal allowed.
