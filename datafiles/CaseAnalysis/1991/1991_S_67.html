East India Corporation Limited v Shree Meenakshi Mills Limited

Supreme Court of India

16 April 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

T.K. Thommen, R.M. Sahai

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 617; (1991) 3 SCC 230; AIR 1991 SC 1094; AIR 1991 SCW 1088; JT 1991 (2) SC 397; 1991 (1) RCJ 514; [1991] 2 S.C.R. 310; 1991 (2) UJ 165

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Suit for recovery of possession

Summary: Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Tamil Nadu Buildings (Lease and Rent Control) Act, 1960, ss. 10, 30(ii) - Suit for recovery of possession - Eviction - Legality - Respondent (plaintiff) filed a suit for recovery of possession of a building on the ground of arrears of rent against appellant (defendant) - Respondent alleged that appellant defendant was the tenant of building in question and that it has not paid agreed rent for a long period, despite persistent demands, and was a willful defaulter - Appellant specifically denied any relationship of landlord and tenant between parties - Munsif Court decreed the suit, which was affirmed in appeal by First Appellate Court and HC - Hence, instant appeal - Appellant contended that as a result of declaration by SC of the Constitutional invalidity of s. 30(ii) of the Act, which excluded from the purview of the Act any building or part thereof let out on a monthly rent of Rs.400 the decree of civil Court, whatever be the merits of findings on respective contentions of parties, had become null and void and of no effect whatever - Whether impugned judgments passed by Courts below were justified - Held, at the time of institution of suit, building in question did not come within purview of 1960 Act for the agreed rent, as alleged by respondent and as found by all Courts, was Rs.900 p.m and outside the limit prescribed u/s. 30(ii) of the Act for a residential building to fall within statutory ambit - Any suit instituted by the landlord for eviction of a tenant from a building falling within the ambit of the Act, otherwise - Than as stipulated by section, was, thus, incompetent for lack of jurisdiction of the Court and any decree of the Court in such a suit was null and void and no effect - In instant case, procedure stipulated in second proviso to s. 10 of the Act was not complied with - Impugned judgments were set aside - Appeal allowed.
