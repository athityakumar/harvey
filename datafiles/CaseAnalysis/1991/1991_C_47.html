A.L.A. Firm v Commissioner Of Income Tax, Madras

Supreme Court of India

21 February 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Ranganathan, N.M. Kasliwal, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 920; (1991) 2 SCC 558; 1991 (93) CTR 133; [1991] 189 ITR 285; JT 1992 (2) SC 7; 1991(1) SCALE 364; [1991] 1 S.C.R. 624; [1991] 55 TAXMAN 497

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961 - Whether reassessment of assessee u/s. 147 was justified and whether assessment of income on revaluation of assets was justified ? - Income Tax O missed to take note of law laid down in G. R. Ramachari and Co. [1961] during original assessment as there is nothing to show that it had been brought to his notice - Initiation of reassessment proceedings was based on definite material not considered at time of original assessment - Held, principle as laid down in referred case was rightly applied for valuation of stock in trade at market price to determine real rights of partners - Reassessment was justified and revaluation of assets resulted into profits of firm which is chargeable to tax - Appeals dismissed.
