District Exhibitors Association, Muzaffarnagar and Others v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

25 April 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

Yogeshwar Dayal, K. Jagannatha Shetty

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 633; (1991) 3 SCC 119; (1991) SCC (L&S) 822; AIR 1991 SC 1381; 1991 (17) ATC 272; 1991 (2) CLR 125; 1991 (62) FLR 812; JT 1991 (2) SC 330; 1991 LabIC 1332; 1991 (2) LLJ 115; 1991 (2) LLN 1; 1991(1) SCALE 788; [1991] 2 S.C.R. 477; 1992 (3) SLR 100; 1991 (2) UJ 143; 1991 (2) UPLBEC 944

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Media & Entertainment; Service

Keywords: employees provident fund

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Employees' Provident Funds and Miscellaneous Provisions Act, 1952, ss. 3(b), 5, 7(1) - Employees' Provident Funds (Amendment) Scheme, 1986 - Payment of employees contribution - Deduction of wages - Liability - Central Govt. issued the notification dt.30-4-1986 in exercise of powers conferred by s. 5 r/w s. 7(1) of the 1952 Act, Central Govt. made the Scheme further to amend the amend the 1952 Scheme, and Scheme might be called Employees' Provident Funds (Amendment) Scheme, 1986 - Appellants filed writ petitions before HC - Appellants contended that notification dt.30-4-1986 was ultra vires of provisions of the 1952 Act inasmuch as Central Govt. could not extend Scheme to an establishment which was neither an industry nor a notified establishment u/s. 3(b) of the 1952 Act - HC dismissed the petitions and uphold the impugned notification - Hence, instant appeal - Whether HC was justified in passing the impugned order - Held, it was clear from reading of s. 5 of the 1952 Act that before Provident Funds Scheme could become applicable, Central Govt. had to frame a Scheme and also specify establishment to which said Scheme should apply - Till impugned Notification was published the Scheme was not applicable to such cinema theatres who were employing less than 20 employees and it became applicable to cinema theatres employing 5 or more workers only when impugned Notification was issued u/s. 5 of the 1952 Act - In instant case for period from 1-10-1984 up to date of impugned Notification employer had paid full wages to employees since during that period, there was no scheme applicable to his establishment - By retrospectively applying Scheme, could be asked to pay employees contribution for period antecedent to impugned notification - However, the 1952 Act and the Scheme neither permit any such payment nor deduction - Appellants could not be saddled with liability to pay the employees' contribution for retrospective period, since he had no right to deduct same from future wages payable to employees - Therefore, Scheme had given retrospective effect w.e.f. 1-10-1984 - Employer could not take benefit for deducting employees contribution in their wages payable in future - Appellants were not liable to pay employees contribution for period from 1-10-1984 to 30-4-1986 - Impugned order passed by HC was set aside - Appeals allowed.
