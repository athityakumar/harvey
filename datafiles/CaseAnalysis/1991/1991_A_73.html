Ashok Kumar Alias Golu v Union Of India And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

10 July 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, P.B. Sawant, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 58; (1991) 3 SCC 498; (1991) SCC (Cr) 845; AIR 1991 SC 1792; 1991 (3) Crimes 185; 1991 CRLJ 2483; JT 1991 (3) SC 46; [1991] 2 S.C.R. 858

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Constitution - Criminal - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 72, 141, 161, 245 and 246 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, ss. 428, 433 and 433A - (A) Legislative fraud or colourable legislation - Enactment of one legislation, s. 433A of CrPC and failure to enact another, Indian Penal Code (Amendment) Bill 1972 - Whether question of legislative fraud or colourable legislation is involved? - (B) Validity of s. 433A of CrPC - Ambiguity in language - (C) Sentence - Remissions and set off - Period of detention as under- trial - When would ensure to benefit of convict? - (D) Grant of remission, pardon etc - Framing of guidelines - Recommendations by Supreme Court in certain predecided case - If mandatory - Held, (A) Question of mala fides on part of legislature not involved - (B) No question of legislative fraud or colourable legislation involved in backdrop of legislative history of s. 433A of CrPC - No extrinsic aid needed and reading down or interpreting in light of proposed changes by IPC (Amendment) Bill, 1972 is impermissible - (C) Only when appropriate Govt. had passed order u/s. 432/433 - (D) Mere recommendation and not ratio decidendi having binding effect - Petition dismissed.
