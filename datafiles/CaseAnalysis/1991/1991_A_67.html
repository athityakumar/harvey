Parvatibai Subhanrao Nalawade Smt v Anwarali Hasanali Makani and Others

Supreme Court of India

20 December 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

L.M. Sharma, S.C. Agrawal, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 439; (1992) 1 SCC 414; AIR 1992 SC 1780; 1992 (19) ALR 151; 1991 (4) Bom.C.R. 752; 1993 (1) Bom.C.R. 403; JT 1992 (1) SC 50; 1992 (1) RCJ 186; 1992 (1) RCR(Rent) 464; 1992 (1) RentLR 1; 1991(2) SCALE 1434; [1991] Supp3 S.C.R. 558; 1992 (1) UJ 513

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution

Summary: Civil Procedure - Constitution - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 23, r. 3 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - (A) Consent decree - Tenant vacating premises on express stipulation of landlord that on construction of new building tenant would get identical area - Consent decree passed accordingly - Could it be defeated on ground of sympathy to third party inducted by landlord in said premises - (B) Writ jurisdiction - Exercise of - Cases relating to immovable property governed by ordinary civil law - Special (Writ) jurisdiction, when to be exercised - Held, could not be defeated on sympathetic grounds - Should not be exercised by High Court unless circumstances are exceptional - Appeal allowed.
