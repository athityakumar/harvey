Mathura Refinery Mazdoor Sangh Through Its Secretary v Indian Oil Corporation Limited, Mathura Refinery Project, Mathura and Another

Supreme Court of India

15 February 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

M.M. Punchhi, K.N. Saikia

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 507; (1991) 2 SCC 176; (1991) SCC (L&S) 533; AIR 1991 SCW 574; 1991 (1) CLR 684; 1991 (62) FLR 425; JT 1991 (1) SC 472; 1991 (1) LLN 963; 1991(1) SCALE 297; [1991] 1 S.C.R. 468; 1992 (3) SLR 126; 1991 (2) UJ 23

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Contract Labour (Regulation & Abolition) Act, 1971 - Termination of contract - Sustainability - Appellant was the Mathura Refinery Mazdoor Sangh - Contesting respondent was Refinery - Union represented about 900 casual labourers working in respondent - These labourers were contract labourers coming under the Act - Nature of their work has grouped them - Some of the labourers had formed themselves into cooperative societies and those societies had entered into labour contracts with the respondent - Other labourers were working under labour contractors who had contracts with the Refinery - Theirs was not a constant relationship with one contractor and these labourers keep shifting from one contractor to another - However it was claimed that these casual labourers, had been working in respondent for so many years in the past ranging between ten to fifteen years but they were denied wages and other benefits as also other beneficial service conditions enjoyed by workmen who were regular employees of the Refinery - However, their services were terminated - Tribunal had directed that Refinery to approach the Advisory Board to make a study with regard to the desirability of continuance of the contract labour system in the Refinery - Tribunal had also directed Refinery to give preference to those workmen in its employment by waiving the requirement of age and other qualifications wherever possible and it might also consider the creation of a benevolent fund for the contract labour - Hence instant appeal - Whether award of Tribunal could be improved - Held, in the instant case before SC, the contract labourers were not, found to be, having a direct connection with respondent, even though it was a State for the purpose of enforcement of fundamental rights - Suggestions/directions given by the Tribunal, appeared to SC to be the only relief which was due to the appellant and its members in the given situation and circumstances - Therefore, the impugned Award of the Tribunal could not be improved upon - Appeal dismissed.
