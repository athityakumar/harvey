Gurcharan Singh Baldev Singh v Yashwant Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

15 November 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Sahai, M.H. Kania

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 724; (1992) 1 SCC 428; 1992 (1) ACC 507; AIR 1992 SC 180; 1992 CRLR 59; 1992 CrLR(SC) 59; JT 1991 (6) SC 256; 1991(2) SCALE 985; [1991] Supp2 S.C.R. 305

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Practice & Procedure - General Clauses Act, 1897, s. 6 - Motor Vehicles Act, 1939, ss. 57(3) and 58(2) - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, ss. 81 and 217- Whether, an application filed by an operator for renewal of his permit u/s. 58 of repealed Act was rendered non-existent in eye of law after coming into force of new Act or it being a right with in meaning of cl. (c) of s. 6 of General Clauses Act, 1897 survived and continued despite repeal of repealed Act? - Objective of s. 6 of General Clause Act, 1897 is to ensure protection of any right or privilege acquired under repealed Act, only exception to it is legislative intention to contrary i.e. repealing Act may expressly provide or it may impliedly provide against continuance of such right, obligation or liability - Although s. 58(2) uses word 'may' but r/w proviso it creates a preference in favour of a permit holder to claim renewal if other conditions were equal, thus a holder of a permit stands on a better footing - Preference created by s. 58(2) for consideration of permit and its grant cannot be said to be a mere incohate right; it is a right which is enforceable in law; further there is no express provision debarring renewal of permits applied for under repealed Act; s. 81 specifically provides for renewal and it is kept alive by s. 217(4) - Held, this right accrued to appellant as he had already applied for renewal and his application had been notified, legal machinery was set in motion by him, he therefore had a right to get his application for renewal processed and considered in accordance with 1939 Act; by virtue of s. 6(c) of General Clauses Act, 1897 appellant's right to get his application considered and decided in accordance with law was saved by s. 217(4) of new Act - Appeal allowed.
