State of Andhra Pradesh v Dr. M.V. Ramana Reddy and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 August 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, M.M. Punchhi

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 1047; (1991) 4 SCC 536; (1991) SCC (Cr) 1018; AIR 1991 SC 1938; 1992 (1) CCR 837; 1991 (3) Crimes 199; 1991 CRLJ 2703; 1991 CrLR(SC) 666; JT 1991 (3) SC 421; 1991(2) SCALE 337; [1991] 3 S.C.R. 600; 1991 (2) UJ 674

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 300 - Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 3 and 9 - Murder - (A) Appreciation of evidence - Presence of eye-witness, daughter of deceased, at scene established - Her statement as to injuries in process of warding off blows aimed at her, supported by medical evidence - And as to identity of accused not doubtful - Accused failed to prove his plea of alibi - Validity of conviction - (B) Identification of accused - Delay in holding identification parade - No valid explanation - Evidence of prosecution not absolutely reliable - Held, was proper - Accused entitled to benefit of doubt - Appeal dismissed.
