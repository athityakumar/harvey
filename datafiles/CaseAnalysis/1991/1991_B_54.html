Dahanu Taluka Environment Protection Group and Another v Bombay Suburban Electricity Supply Company Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 March 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Ranganathan, S.C. Agrawal, N.D. Ojha

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 579; (1991) 2 SCC 539; 1991 (3) Bom.C.R. 39; JT 1991 (2) SC 1; 1991(1) SCALE 472; 1991 (1) UJ 578

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Environment, Wildlife & Animal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Environment, Wildlife & Animal - Practice & Procedure - Environment Protection Rules, 1986 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Petitioners, Environment Protection Groups objected to clearance, by State of Maharashtra and UOI, of a proposal of Bombay Suburban Electricity Supply Co. Limited for construction of a thermal power plant over an area of 800 hectares or thereabouts in Dahanu, Maharashtra - (A) Whether clearance of a proposal of Bombay Suburban Electricity Supply Co. Ltd for construction of a thermal power plant in Dahanu, Maharashtra is without application of mind? - EAC's findings cannot be treated as conclusive or binding on Central Govt.; Central Govt. had before it not only this 'report' but also findings of a State Expert Committee which had gone into matter in detail and recommended Dahanu site - Held, a comparative study of two sites on all aspects such as pollution, contamination of fresh water sources, effect on fisheries, effect on plantation, agriculture and forests and effect on tribal population living in affected areas was looked into, therefore, it cannot be said that Govt. decision should be faulted as it runs counter to views of EAC or Govt. has not applied its mind to all relevant aspects of setting up of a thermal power station at Dahanu - (B) Whether clearance of site is contrary to environmental guidelines for thermal power plants? - Held, no - Guidelines are of general nature applicable to proposals for thermal power stations all over country but in locating a thermal power plant in a particular region, special features of that region have to be taken into account - Distance mentioned in guidelines are only intended as a safeguard against possible pollution effects and cannot be treated as rigid and inflexible irrespective of local conditions - Further restriction in guidelines is only for buildings of thermal station and cannot be read so as to mean that no part of site of thermal station of about 800 hectares should at all fall within distance of 500 meters - (C) Whether clearance is in contravention to notifications D/- 15-12-1990 and 08-02-1991? - Held, one of conditions imposed while granting clearance was that in future State Govt. would not allow any industrial estate or any private industries to come up in Dahanu Taluka which has also been mentioned in memorandum D/- 29-06-1990; it is in implementation of this condition that these notifications have been issued to prevent further proliferation of industries in area and they do not affect validity of clearance granted to company's thermal power station - Special Leave Petitions dismissed.
