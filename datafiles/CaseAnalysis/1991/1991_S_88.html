Shivaji Dayanu Patil And Anr v Smt. Vatschala Uttam More

Supreme Court of India

17 July 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Agrawal, B.C. Ray

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 696; (1991) 3 SCC 530; (1991) SCC (Cr) 865; 1991 (2) ACC 306; 1991 (2) ACJ 777; AIR 1991 SC 1769; AIR 1991 SCW 1867; 1991 (3) CCC 71; [1992] 75 Comp Cas 348; 1991 CrLR(SC) 618; JT 1991 (3) SC 133; 1991 (2) PLR 359; 1991(2) SCALE 92; [1991] 3 S.C.R. 26; 1991 (2) UJ 290

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Engine, Death Or Permanent Disablement, No Fault Liability

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Labour & Industrial Law - Workmen's Compensation Act, 1923 - Motor Vehicles Act, 1939, ss. 2(18) and 92 A - Petrol tanker collided with truck that was lying turtle on its side at some distance from road - Deceased died due to injuries caused by collision - (A) Whether petrol tanker is motor vehicle within meaning of s. 2(18) - (B) Whether use of vehicle ceases on account of vehicle being rendered immobile on account of break-down or mechanical defect or accident? - (C) Whether injuries caused to deceased by explosion and fire, due to accident arising out of use of vehicle i.e., petrol tanker? - Held, (A) petrol tanker was vehicle manufactured for purpose of transporting petrol - At the time when petrol tanker collided with truck, it was being used for purpose of transporting petrol - Therefore, it cannot be disputed that when said collision took place it was a motor vehicle as defined in s. 2(18) of Act of 1939 - (B)use of vehicle does not cease on account of break-down or accident - (C) injuries which led to death of deceased could be said to be due to accident arising out of use of vehicle - Petition dismissed.
