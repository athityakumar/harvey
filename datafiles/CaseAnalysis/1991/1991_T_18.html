Thota Sesharathamma and Another v Thota Manikyamma (Dead) By Lrs. and Others

Supreme Court of India

23 August 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

N.M. Kasliwal, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 773; (1991) 4 SCC 312; 1991 (3) CCC 157; 1992 (103) CTR 308; 1991 (45) DLT 297; 1992 (2) DMC 467; 1991 (2) HLR 256; JT 1991 (3) SC 506; 1991(2) SCALE 434; [1991] 3 S.C.R. 717; 1991 (2) UJ 685

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Keywords: In Lieu Of Maintenance

Summary: Family & Personal - Hindu Married Women's Right to Separate Maintenance and Residence Act, 1946 - Hindu Women's Rights to Property Act, 1937, s. 3(1) - Indian Succession Act, 1925 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Hindu Succession Act, 1956, ss. 14(1) and 14(2) - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 15(1), 15(3), 16(1), 21, 29(2), 38, 39(a), 39(d), 39(e), 42 and 46 - Whether life interest in property acquired by wife under will executed by her husband and continued to be in her possession became her absolute property u/s. 14(1) of Hindu Succession Act,1956? - Held, yes - S. 14(1) of Hindu Succession Act, 1956 enlarges restricted estate into full ownership when Hindu female has pre-existing right to maintenance etc. - S. 14(2) operates when grant was made for first time under document with no pre-existing right, must be read as an exception or a proviso to s. 14(1) - Both sub-sections r/w. explanation to be pragmatically considered as a constituent integral scheme - Hindu females limited estate gets full ownership u/s. 14(1) with a right to bequeath, gift over, alienate or to deal in any manner recognised by law - Restrictions contained in will, though falls both u/s. 14(2) as well s. 14(1) - Right to maintenance being a pre-existing right over property 'res ad rem' s. 14(1) would apply - Appeal dismissed.
