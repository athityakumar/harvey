(1) Orissa Cement Limited; (2) Tata Iron and v (1) State of Orissa and Others; (2) State of

Supreme Court of India

04 April 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Ranganathan, N.M. Kasliwal, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 596; (1991) Supp1 SCC 430; AIR 1991 SC 1676; 1991 (34) ECR 497; JT 1991 (2) SC 439; 1991(1) SCALE 617; [1991] 2 S.C.R. 105

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax

Keywords: Finance Act, 1982, Finance Act, 1984, Finance Act, 1985, Finance Act, 1981, Madhya Pradesh General Clauses Act, 1957, Tea Act, 1953, Government of Ireland Act, 1920, Madras Panchayats Act, 1958, Punjab Minor Mineral Concession Rules, 1964, Punjab Minor Minerals Concession Rules, 1964, Madhya Pradesh Municipalities Act, 1961, Orissa Cess Act, 1962, Orissa Panchayat Samiti and Zilla Parishad Act, 1959, Bengal Cess Act 1880, Orissa Cess Act (Act 17 of 1989), Local Mines Labour Welfare Fund Act, 1947, Orissa Grama Panchayats Act, 1984, Bihar Finance Act, 1982, Bombay Sales Tax Act, 1948, Orissa Cess Rules, 1963, Madhya Pradesh Mineral Areas Development Cess Rules, 1982

Summary: Constitution - Indirect Tax - Mines & Minerals - Mines and Minerals (Development and Regulation) Act, 1957,s.2 - Orissa Cess Act,1962,ss. 4, 10, 5, 7 - Orissa Cess Act (Act 17 of 1989) - Constitution of India,1950,arts. 142, 246, 265, 277, 301, 304(b) - Orissa Mining Areas Development Fund Act, 1952 - Bihar Finance Act,1982 - Madhya Pradesh General Clauses Act, 1957, s. 24A - Madhya Pradesh General Sales Tax Act, 1958,s. 3 - Coal Mines (Nationalisation) Act 1973 - Orissa Cess Rules,1963 - Madhya Pradesh Mineral Areas Development Cess Rules,1982, r. 3 - Levy of tax by State Govt.s - Challenged -- Levy of 'cess' based on royalty derived from mining lands by respondents/States of Bihar and Orissa and Madhya Pradesh - Challenged by appellants by instant appeals and petitions - As involved common dispute heard together and disposed by common judgment - Whether act of imposing levy by respondents/State Govt.s was justified - Held, entries 52 and 54 in List I-(Union List) and entries 18,23,45,49,50 and 66 in List II ( state list) in Seventh Schedule to Constitution deals with issues involved - Language of Entry 52 r/w Entry 54 would suggest that once Parliament had declared by law that control of particular industry by Union was expedient in public interest then State legislatures would completely lose all competence to legislate with respect to such industries in any respect whatsoever - Language of Entry 54 deprives State legislatives competency only to extent to which law of Parliament considers control of Union to be expedient in matters of regulation of mines and mineral development - Taxes on mineral rights must be different from taxes on goods produced regarding excise duty - Comparison of List I and II of the Seventh Schedule shows that instant tax was not put in both Lists - SC in India Cement Limited and Others v State of Tamil Nadu and Others 1989 Indlaw SC 284 held that tax on royalties could not be tax on minerals and made clear distinction between tax on land and tax on income arising from land - State Govt.s were not justified to levy under Entry 45, 49 and 50 of List II of the Seventh Schedule -State legislations encroachment on field covered by the Act 1957 would be ultra vires - Levy of cess squarely fall under Central Act and object of s.9 of the Central Act could not be ignored - S. 18(1) empowers Central Govt. to take all necessary steps for conservation and development of minerals in India and for protection of environment - Union control envisaged by Entry 45 and the 1957 Act were meant to provide proper development of mines and mineral areas and also to bring about uniformity all over country with regard to minerals specified in Schedule I in matter of royalties and prices - Central Govt. submition that prices of exports were fixed and could not be escalated with enhancement of different royalties charged by different States and their working would become impossible had to be considered - SC in India Cement 1989 Indlaw SC 284 held that Central Act bars an enhancement of royalty directly or indirectly except by Union and in manner specified by the 1957 Act and instant tax levied by State Govt.s were contrary to it - Hence validity of impugned Acts could not be upheld by reference to Entry 23 or Entry 50 of List II - Levy of cess was beyond competence of State Legislatures and levy of cess imposed by Orissa Bihar and Madhya Pradesh enactments was held invalid and struck down - Though levy of cess was unconstitutional there would be no direction to refund amount to assessees collected until levy in question had been declared unconstitutional - Appeals disposed of.
