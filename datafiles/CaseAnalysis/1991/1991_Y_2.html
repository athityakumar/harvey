Y. Narasimha Rao and Others v Y. Venkata Lakshmi and Another

Supreme Court of India

09 July 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

P.B. Sawant, Ranganath Misra

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 797; (1991) 3 SCC 451; (1991) SCC (Cr) 626; 1991 (2) Crimes 855; 1991 CrLR(SC) 555; 1991 (2) DMC 366; 1991 (2) HLR 154; JT 1991 (3) SC 33; 1991(2) SCALE 1; [1991] 2 S.C.R. 821

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Summary: Family & Personal - Civil Procedure - Hindu Marriage Act, 1955 - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, ss. 13 and 14 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 41, 63, 65, 74, 76, 77 and 86 - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Criminal complaint again, appellant, for bigamy - Appellants filed an application for their discharge in view of decree for dissolution of marriage passed by Missouri Court - Magistrate discharged appellants - Respondent 1 filed criminal revision at High Court which set aside order of Magistrate holding photostat copy of foreign judgment of Missouri Court was not admissible in evidence - Whether courts in this country should recognise foreign judgment in personal and family matters? - Held, the present decree dissolving the marriage passed by the foreign court is without jurisdiction according to the Act as neither the marriage was celebrated nor the parties last resided together nor the respondent resided within the jurisdiction of that court - The decree is also passed on a ground which is not available under the Act which is applicable to the marriage - What is further, the decree has been obtained by appellant 1 by stating that he was the resident of the Missouri State when the record shows that he was only a bird of passage there and was ordinarily a resident of the State of Louisiana - The respondent had, if at all, only technically satisfied the requirement of resident of 90 days with the only purpose of obtaining the divorce - Appellant 1 was neither domiciled in that State nor had he an intention to make it his home - Appellant 1 had also no substantial connection with the forum - Appellant 1 has further brought no rules on record under which the St. Louis Court could assume jurisdiction over the matter - On the contrary, as pointed out earlier, he has in his petition made a false averment respondent 1 had refuse to continue to stay with him in the State of Missouri where she had never been - In the absence of the rules of jurisdiction of that court, this Court not aware whether the residence of respondent 1 within the State of Missouri was necessary to confer jurisdiction on that court, and if not, of the reasons for making the said averment - To recognise a foreign matrimonial judgment in this country, jurisdiction assumed by foreign court as well as grounds on which relief is granted must be in accordance with matrimonial law under which parties are married - Further held, since with regard to jurisdiction of forum as well as ground on which it is passed foreign decree is not in accordance with Act under which parties were married, cannot be recognised by courts in this country and is, therefore, unenforceable - Appeal dismissed.
