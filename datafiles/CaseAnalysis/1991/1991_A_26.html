Administrator, Municipal Corporation, Bilaspur v Dattatraya Dahankar, Advocate and Another

Supreme Court of India

05 December 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Jagannatha Shetty, R.M. Sahai, Yogeshwar Dayal

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 976; (1992) 1 SCC 361; AIR 1992 SC 1846; AIR 1992 SCW 2081; JT 1991 (4) SC 500; 1992 (1) RCR(Rent) 1; 1991(2) SCALE 1215; [1991] Supp3 S.C.R. 112; 1992 (1) UJ 332

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Municipalities & Local Governments - Madhya Pradesh Municipalities Act, 1961, s. 127-A - Construction of s. 127-A of Madhya Pradesh Municipalities Act, 1961 - High Court pointed out that under Scheme of Act for purpose of imposition of property tax u/s. 127-A(1), each tenement has to be separately assessed and no tax can be levied for a building with annual letting value up to Rs. 1800 - Whether there is provision for taxation of building and land of which annual letting value is up to Rs. 1800? - Held, mechanical approach to constrictions altogether out of step with modern positive approach modern positive approach is to have a purposeful construction that is to effectuate object and purpose of Act s. 127-A must, therefore, receive a purposeful construction - There is no provision for taxation in respect of a building having annual letting value less than Rs. 1800 - S. 127A(2)(b) expressly exempts buildings and lands, annual letting value of which does not exceed Rs. 1800 - Purpose of proviso is to deny exemption to buildings or lands owned by same person and of which total annual letting value exceeds Rs. 1800 - Each building is a unit for purpose of taxation and there is no provision for taxation of building and land of which annual letting value is up to Rs. 1800 - But when aggregation of annual letting value of all buildings or lands is permitted, then, all such buildings or lands have to be taken as one unit for purpose of taxation - Legislature could not have intended that all buildings or lands owned by a single individual should get exemption from taxation even if their total letting value exceeds Rs. 1800 - Appeal allowed.
