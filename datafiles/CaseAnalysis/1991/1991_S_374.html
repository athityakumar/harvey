Nagaraj Shivarao Karjagi v Syndicate Bank Head Office Manipal And Anr.

Supreme Court of India

30 April 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Jagannatha Shetty, Yogeshwar Dayal

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 189; (1991) 3 SCC 219; (1991) SCC (L&S) 965; AIR 1991 SC 1507; 1992 (19) ATC 639; 1992 (2) BC 534; 1991 (63) FLR 133; JT 1991 (2) SC 529; 1991 LabIC 1339; 1992 (2) LLJ 149; 1991 (2) LLN 810; 1991 (2) MLJ 7; 1991 (2) MLJ(SC) 7; 1991(1) SCALE 832; [1991] 2 S.C.R. 576; 1991 (2) SLR 784; 1991 (2) UJ 217; 1991 (2) UPLBEC 1049

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Banking Companies (Acquisition and Transfer of Undertakings) Act, 1970, s. 19 - Syndicate Bank Officer Employees' (Discipline and Appeal) Regulations, 1976, regulations 17 and 20 - Disciplinary proceedings initiated against Officer of nationalised bank - Consultation of Vigilance Commission sought - Compulsory retirement advised and imposed - Power of appellate authority to reconsider punishment completely fettered by directive of Ministry of Finance to adhere to punishment advised by Commission - Whether punishment and directive should be quashed? - Held, punishment and directive quashed - Appeal and petition allowed.
