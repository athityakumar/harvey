Des Raj Bhatnagar And Anr. Etc. v Union Of India

Supreme Court of India

13 February 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

N.M. Kasliwal, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 990; (1991) 2 SCC 266; (1991) SCC (L&S) 495; 1991 (16) ATC 469; 1991 (62) FLR 428; JT 1991 (1) SC 443; 1991 (1) LLN 959; 1991(1) SCALE 208; [1991] 1 S.C.R. 356; 1991 (2) SLR 116; 1991 (2) UJ 65

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Service - Central Civil Services (Pension) Rules, 1972 - Pensionary benefits - Entitled to - Whether an officer on his retirement commutes a portion of his pension, is eligible for relief and ad hoc relief in pension on full amount of original pension as admissible to him? - Relied on 'Common Cause' case - After getting a lump sum in lieu of entire pension, they do not fall in class of Central Govt. pensioners and are not entitled to any benefit granted to Central Govt. pensioners - Case of such Central Govt. pensioners who got their one-third pension commuted also fall in a different class inasmuch as they get two-third pension, and after 15 years of such commutation or having attained age of 70 years whichever was later they become entitled to full pension - Held, petitioners cannot fall in category of Central Govt. pensioners for purposes of getting benefit of Liberalised Pension Rules which can be made applicable only to Central Govt. pensioners - Petitions dismissed.
