VINOD GURUDAS RAIKAR v NATIONAL INSURANCE Company Limited and OTHERS

Supreme Court of India

06 September 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

L.M. Sharma, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 1032; (1991) 4 SCC 333; 1991 (2) ACC 449; 1991 (2) ACJ 1060; AIR 1991 SC 2156; AIR 1991 SCW 2503; 1991 (3) CCC 249; [1992] 75 Comp Cas 611; JT 1991 (3) SC 660; 1991(2) SCALE 493; [1991] 3 S.C.R. 912; 1992 (1) UJ 129

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Keywords: Prohibition Act

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1939, ss. 110 and 110-A(3) - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 20(b) - General Clauses Act, 1897, s. 6 - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, s. 166(3) - Accident took place on January 1989 Motor Vehicles Act, 1939 was repealed by s. 217(1) of Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, came into force on 1-07-1989 - Period of limitation for filing a claim petition both under old Act and new Act being six months expired on July 1989 - Claim petition was filed belatedly on March 1990 with a prayer for condonation of delay - Tribunal held, in view of provisions of new Motor Vehicles Act, delay of more than six months could not be condoned - Application dismissed - HC upheld - Whether right or privilege to claim benefit of a provision for condonation of delay can be governed only by law in force at time of delay? - Held, occasion to take benefit of provision for condonation of delay in filing claim arose only after repeal of old law - Ground for condonation set up as 'sufficient cause' also relates to time after repeal - Benefit of repealed law could not, therefore, be available simply because cause of action for claim arose before repeal -'Sufficient cause' as a ground of condonation of delay in filing claim is distinct from 'cause of action' for claim itself - Condonation of delay must be governed by new law - Appeal dismissed.
