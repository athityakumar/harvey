 Abdul Rehman Antulay & Ors v R.S. Nayak & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

10 December 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, G.N. Ray, K.N. Singh, N.M. Kasliwal, P.B. Sawant

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 313; (1992) SCC (Cr) 93; (1992) 1 SCC 225; AIR 1992 SC 1701; AIR 1992 SCW 1872; 1992 (1) CCR 495; 1992 (1) Crimes 193; 1992 CRLJ 2717; 1992 CRLR 93; 1992 CrLR(SC) 93; JT 1991 (6) SC 431; 1991(2) SCALE 1273; [1991] Supp3 S.C.R. 325

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Keywords: Approver

Summary: Criminal - Constitution - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, s. 482 - Criminal Law Amendment Act 1952, ss. 6 and 7 - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 21 - Speedy trial - Guranteee of speedy trial emanating from art. 21 - Is property reflected in provisions of Code ? - Time limit for criminal proceeding - Drawing -Infringement of right of speedy trial - Quashing of charges and/or conviction - Supreme Court issued guidelines in regard to speedy trial - Corruption case - Constitution of Special Court - Evidence recorded earlier by High Court - Direction for re-adducement of evidence, whether be issued - Held, time limit cannot be drawn - Quashing is not only order open to court - Directions regarding right to speedy trial encompasses all stages - Period of remand and pre-conviction detention should be as short as possible - Frivolous proceedings to be avoided - Mere non-asking for a speedy trial cannot be put against accused, etc. - Treating it as evidence recorded by Special Court, Supreme Court declined to issue direction to that effect - Petition allowed.
