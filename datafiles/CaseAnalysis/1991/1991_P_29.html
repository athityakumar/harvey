Patel Roadways Limited, Bombay v Prasad Trading Company

Supreme Court of India

06 August 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

N.D. Ojha, S. Ranganathan, M. Fathima Beevi

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 666; (1991) 4 SCC 270; 1992 (1) ACC 532; 1991 (2) ACJ 1001; AIR 1992 SC 1514; 1992 (1) Bom.C.R. 456; [1992] 74 Comp Cas 11; 1991 (2) CompLJ 80; JT 1991 (3) SC 337; 1992 (2) RCR(Rent) 312; 1991(2) SCALE 257; [1991] 3 S.C.R. 391; 1991 (2) UJ 432

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Corporate; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - CPC, 1908, s. 20 - Determination of territorial jurisdiction - Suit for damages filed against a corporation having a subordinate office at the place where the cause of action - Held, Court at such place would have jurisdiction - and the parties could not confer jurisdiction on the courts at Bombay by an agreement, where Corporation has its principal office - Appeals dismissed.
