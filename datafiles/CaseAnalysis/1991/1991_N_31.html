National Institute of Mental Health and Neuro Sciences v Dr. K. Kalyana Raman and Others

Supreme Court of India

28 November 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Jagannatha Shetty, R.M. Sahai

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 400; (1992) Supp2 SCC 481; (1992) SCC (L&S) 959; AIR 1992 SC 1806; 1992 (21) ATC 680; 1992 LabIC 1800; 1992 (2) LLJ 616; 1992 (2) LLN 845; 1992 (6) SLR 727

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 16 and 226 - (A) Whether function of Recruitment Selection Committee is judicial? - (B) Whether it is required to give reasons for selecting candidates in absence of statutory requirement? - Observance of procedural fairness - Selection Committee consisting of experts holding high status - Interference by courts - Held, (A) its function is administrative and not judicial or adjudicatory -(B) No need to give reasons - It must, however, observe procedural fairness - Courts would be slow to interfere - Appeal dismissed.
