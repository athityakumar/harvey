PANDEY ORAON v RAM CHANDER SAHU and OTHERS

Supreme Court of India

18 July 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

Kuldip Singh, M.H. Kania, Ranganath Misra

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 1039; (1992) Supp2 SCC 77; AIR 1992 SC 195

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Chotanagpur Tenancy Act, s. 71-A - Appellant's ancestor was admittedly a raiyat of certain lands of which, transferor of respondent 1 was landlord - Respondent 1 has failed to establish resumption of property by his transferor - A proceeding for evicting respondent was taken by authorities u/s. 71-A of Chotanagpur Tenancy Act as amended and on being satisfied that respondent 1 exposed himself to liability of eviction an order was made - Whether a transfer of type involved in this case comes within ambit of s. 71-A of Chotanagpur Tenancy Act? - Held, what exactly is contemplated in provision is where possession has passed from one to another and as a physical fact member of Scheduled Tribe who is entitled to hold possession has lost it and a non-member has come into possession would be covered by transfer and a situation of that type would be amenable to exercise of jurisdiction within ambit of s. 71-A of Act - Provision is beneficial and legislative intention is to extend protection to a class of citizens who are not in a position to keep their property to themselves in absence of protection - Appeal allowed.
