M. C. Mehta v Union India and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 March 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranganath Misra, Kuldip Singh, M.H. Kania

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 571; (1991) 2 SCC 353; 1991 (2) CompLJ 226; JT 1991 (1) SC 620; 1991(1) SCALE 427; [1991] 1 S.C.R. 866; 1991 (2) UJ 586

Case Digest

Subject: Environment, Wildlife & Animal

Keywords: Environment (Protection) Act 1986, Biosphere disturbance, Closing of hazardous industries in populated areas

Summary: Environment, Wildlife & Animal - Carriers & Transportation - Practice & Procedure - Motor Vehicles Act 1939 - Air (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1981 - Environment (Protection) Act, 1986 - Central Motor Vehicles Rules, 1989 - Biosphere disturbance - Closing of hazardous industries in populated areas - Legality - Petitioner was practicing advocate who was Chairman of Environment Protection Cell operating at Delhi - Instant application was filed in the public interest litigation sector to issue directions for closing down of hazardous industries located in densely populated areas of Delhi and for regulation of air pollution caused by automobiles operating in area as also thermal units generating power for Delhi Electric Supply Undertaking/DESU - Whether setting up of high-powered committee was justified - Held, setting up of high-powered committee was mooted - Deliberations in course of hearing was taken concrete shape and Ministry of Environment & Forests had ultimately instructed Attorney General that Committee could be set up by Court to look into problem of vehicular pollution in Delhi and for devising methods of solution of problem - Ministry had agreed that retired Judge of SC could act as Chairman and had suggested that petitioner and N.S. Tiwana, Chairman of Central Pollution Control Board will be made Members of Committee - SC found suggestion of Ministry acceptable subject to certain modification - SC viewed that Sudhakar Girdharlal Shah representing Association of Indian Automobile Manufacturers could be taken as member of Committee and Committee would also had power to co-opt experts not exceeding three for its efficient working from time to time - Justice K.N. Saikia who was recently retired as Judge of SC was appointed as Chairman of Committee with N.S. Tiwana, petitioner and. S.G. Shah as Members - Joint Secretary in Ministry of Environment and Forests should be convener Secretary of Committee - Justice Saikia should be entitled to all benefits to which retired Judge of SC while called back to duty was entitled - Committee would be constituted with effect from 18-3-1991 under an appropriate Notification of Union Govt. in relevant Ministry - Petition should be deemed to be pending for purpose of monitoring - Committee should furnish report to SC once in two months as to steps taken in matter - Govt. and Delhi Administration were directed to effectively cooperate with Committee for its successful operation - Order accordingly.
