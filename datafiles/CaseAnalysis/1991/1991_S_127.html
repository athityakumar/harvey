Yusufbhai Noormohmed Nendoliya v State Of Gujarat And Anr.

Supreme Court of India

17 September 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

M.H. Kania, M. Fathima Beevi, N.M. Kasliwal

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 1030; (1991) 4 SCC 531; AIR 1991 SC 2153; AIR 1991 SCW 2528; 1991 (3) CCC 321; 1991(2) SCALE 608; [1991] Supp1 S.C.R. 158; 1992 (1) UJ 21

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, s. 11 A, Explanation - Acquisition of land - No award passed within two years of declaration - Lapsing of acquisition - Whether explanation includes proceeding for making of award or taking of possession - Explanation is intended to confer benefit on landholder whose land is acquired after declaration u/s. 6, is made in cases covered by explanation - Benefit is that award must be made within a period of two years of declaration, failing which acquisition proceedings would lapse and land would revert to landholder - In order to get benefit of said provision what is required is that landholder who seeks benefit must not have obtained any order from a Court restraining any action or proceeding in pursuance of declaration u/s. 6 - Appeal dismissed.
