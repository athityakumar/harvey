Vatticherukuru Village Panchayat and Others v Nori Venkatarama Deekshithulu and Others

Supreme Court of India

26 April 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, N.M. Kasliwal

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 638; (1991) Supp2 SCC 228; AIR 1991 SCW 1303; JT 1991 (5) SC 140; 1991(1) SCALE 818; [1991] 2 S.C.R. 531; 1991 (2) UJ 309

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Special Relief Act, 1963, Andhra Pradesh Charitable and Hindu Religious Institutions and Endowments Act, 1966, Andhra Pradesh (Andhra Pradesh ) Inams (Abolition and Conversion) into Ryotwari Act 1956 (AP Act 38 of 1956), Andhra Pradesh Gram Panchayat Act 1964

Summary: Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Socio-Economic - Provincial Insolvency Act, 1920 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Andhra Pradesh Inams (Abolition and Conversion) into Ryotwari Act, 1956, ss. 3, 4, 5, 7, 9, 12, 13, 14 and 15 - Andhra Pradesh Charitable, Hindu Religious Institutions Endowments Act, 1966, s.77 - Andhra Pradesh Gram Panchayat Act, 1964, ss. 85 and 64 - Special Relief Act, 1963, s. 6 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Civil Procedure Code, 1908 - Trial Court and High Court held lands endowed to Nori Lakshmipathi Somayajulu for maintenance of tank and descendants ryotwari patta under Inams Act entitled to remain in possession and enjoyment as owners subject to maintaining - (A) Whether the trial court has the jurisdiction? - Held, s. 9 of the CPC, 1908 provides that whenever a question arises before the civil court whether its jurisdiction is excluded expressly or by necessary implication, the court naturally feels inclined to consider whether remedy afforded by an alternative provision prescribed by special statute is sufficient or adequate - In cases where exclusion of the civil court's jurisdiction is expressly provided for, the consideration as to the scheme of the statute in question and the adequacy or sufficiency of the remedy provided for by it may be relevant, but cannot be decisive - Where exclusion is pleaded as a matter of necessary implication such consideration would be very important and in conceivable circumstances might become even decisive - (B) Whether tank and appurtenant land stood vested in Gram Panchayat? - Held, tank is a public tank and not public trust and that u/ss. 85(1) and 64, of Panchayat Act vesting of tanks, appurtenant land and common land is only for purpose of possession, supervision, control and use thereof for villagers for common use subject to overriding title by Govt. and its assumption of management should be in terms of s. 85(3) of Act - Ryotwari patta shall, be in favour of institution - (C) Whether patta granted in individuals' names, would be their individual property or for endowment? - Held, Division Bench was right in holding, pattas were obtained in individuals' name, trustees of an institution cannot derive personal advantage from administration of trust property and grant to patta was for maintenance of trust - Appeal disposed of.
