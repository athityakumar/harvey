Gokak Patel Volkart Limited v Dundayya Gurushiddaiah Hiremath and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 February 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

K.N. Saikia, M.M. Punchhi

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 506; (1991) 2 SCC 141; (1991) SCC (Cr) 315; AIR 1991 SCW 505; [1991] 71 Comp Cas 403; 1991 (1) CompLJ 235; 1991 (1) Crimes 556; 1991 CrLR(SC) 207; JT 1991 (1) SC 376; 1991 (1) LLN 970; 1991(1) SCALE 193; [1991] 1 S.C.R. 396

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Torts, Continuous

Summary: Criminal - Companies Act, 1956, s. 630(1)(b) - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 441 and 448 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, ss. 468 and 473 - Whether offence u/s. 630(1)(b) of Companies Act, 1956 is a continuing offence for purpose of limitation? - Offence u/s. 630 is not such as can be said to have consummated once for all; wrongful withholding, or wrongfully obtaining possession and wrongful application of company's property, that is, for purposes other than those expressed or directed in articles of company and authorised by Companies Act, 1956 cannot be said to be terminated by a single act or fact but would subsist for period until property in offender's possession is delivered up or refunded - Held, it is an offence committed over a span of time and last act of offence will control commencement of period of limitation and need be alleged; offence continues until property wrongfully obtained or wrongfully withheld or knowingly misapplied is delivered up or refunded to company, for failure to do so sub-s. (2) prescribes punishment; offence u/s. 630 of Companies Act, 1956 is not one time but a continuing offence and period of limitation must be computed accordingly, and when so done, instant complaints could not be said to have been barred by limitation - Appeals allowed.
