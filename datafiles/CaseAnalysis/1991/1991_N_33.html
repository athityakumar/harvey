T .P. Srivastava v National Tobacco Co. Of India Ltd

Supreme Court of India

11 October 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

Veeraswami Ramaswami, Kuldip Singh

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 349; (1992) SCC (L&S) 263; (1992) 1 SCC 281; AIR 1991 SC 2294; 1991 (63) FLR 672; JT 1991 (4) SC 121; 1991 LabIC 2371; 1992 (1) LLJ 86; 1992 (1) LLN 13; 1991(2) SCALE 787; 1992 (1) SLJ 83; 1992 (3) SLR 140; [1991] Supp1 S.C.R. 472; 1991 (2) UJ 751

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Termination

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Termination - Justifiability - Appellant was terminated from service of respondent-company on the ground that he was on unauthorized absence - Application before Labour Court by its award held that the appellant was not 'workman' therefore, the reference was incompetent - Hence instant appeal.

Held, Labour Court considered the merits in detail and ultimately held that the termination of the appellant from service was illegal but dismissed the application only on the ground that the Act was not applicable. SC would not have interfered with that finding had differed from the Labour Court on the question whether the appellant is a workman. In the light of holding that the Act is not applicable to him and in view of the fact that long period of over 16 years had passed it would be unjust to leave the appellant without any remedy at this stage. In the circumstances, SC considered that direction to the Management to pay some compensation is necessary to meet the ends of justice. SC accordingly directed the Company to pay amount equivalent to 3 years salary at the rate he was drawing when appellant's services were terminated, in addition to whatever amount they were paying during the pendency of the appeal under orders of SC. But this direction will not be treated as precedent. Thus, no interference is called for with the decisions of the Labour Court. Appeal dismissed.
