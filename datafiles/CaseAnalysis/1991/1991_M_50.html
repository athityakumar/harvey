Chandigarh Administration And Anr. Etc. Etc v Manpreet Singh And Ors. Etc. Etc.

Supreme Court of India

18 November 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, P.B. Sawant

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 368; (1992) 1 SCC 380; AIR 1992 SC 435; AIR 1992 SCW 28; JT 1991 (4) SC 436; 1991(2) SCALE 1030; 1992 (1) SLR 238; [1991] Supp2 S.C.R. 322; 1992 (1) UPLBEC 491

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Defence Service

Summary: Education - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Ambit and scope of art. 226 - College admission - Reservation - For children/spouses of recipients of certain gallantry decorations - (A) Writ petition - Rule found discriminatory - High Court strike down rule and direct to reframe it - Direction to admit son of recipient of gallantry award not notified in Rule - Whether under jurisdiction or not? - (B) Writ Court cannot assume role of or act as appellate authority over, rule-making authority - Rule mentioning various categories of students eligible for reservation in order of priority - HC changing priority of categories - Jurisdiction - (C) Practice and procedure - Direction to admit student in educational institution issued - Stay of operation of HC order - Held, (A) Is without jurisdiction - (B) Order is without jurisdiction, more so as neither reasons nor opportunity of hearing was given by HC - (C) HC should grant stay for 3 to 4 weeks so requested by educational institution or by State - Order accordingly.
