N.Parthasarathy Etc. v Controller Of Capital Issues And Another Etc.

Supreme Court of India

16 April 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

B.C. Ray, N.M. Kasliwal

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 616; (1991) 3 SCC 153; AIR 1991 SC 1420; 1992 (2) Bom.C.R. 327; [1991] 72 Comp Cas 651; 1991 (2) CompLJ 1; JT 1993 (2) SC 218; 1991(1) SCALE 675; [1991] 2 S.C.R. 329

Case Digest

Subject: Disinvestment; MRTP

Keywords: Capital Issue (Control) Act, 1947, Capital Issues (Control) Act,1947

Summary: Corporate - Capital Issue (Control) Act, 1947, ss. 3 and 36(6) - Constitution, art. 14 - Companies Act, 1956, ss. 55, 61, 72 and 108 - MRTP Act,1969, ss. 21 and 22 - Contract Act, 1972, s. 23 - Disinvestment achieved indirectly and with a motive to conceal the real nature of the deal - Alleged secret agreement - Pleaded for a declaration that the divestment by the respondents of the controlling shares in Larsen & Toubro to the house of Ambanis in a secret and circuitous arrangement is arbitrary and a fraud on the statutory powers of the respondents - Whether the consent accorded by Controller of Capital Issues was without application of mind - Enabling the Ambani Group to control the L&T Company which is ultra vires arts. 14 and 39(b) and (c) of Constitutions - The consent given by the Controller of Capital Issues was challenged on the ground that it was given in undue haste - Also that it was not cleared under the MRTP Act - Held, BOB Fiscal Services Ltd. merely acted as a conduit for funnelling shares from the public financial institutions to the Ambani Group - But no decision required on this point - In future public financial institutions must consider whether such a transfer will lead to acquisition of a large proportion of the shares of a public company and thereby create a monopoly in favour of a particular group - proved on facts that consent was given after due consideration under s. 4 of the Capital Issue (Control) Act - Also that there was approval of Central Govt. u/s. 22(3)(d) of the MRTP Act - Challenge to the consent is rejected - Order accordingly.
