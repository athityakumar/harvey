Jagdish Singh v Natthu Singh

Supreme Court of India

25 November 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

M.N. Venkatachaliah, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 1041; (1992) 1 SCC 647; AIR 1992 SC 1604; AIR 1992 SCW 1747; 1992 ALJ 620; 1992 (19) ALR 297; 1992 (1) CCC 154; JT 1991 (5) SC 400; 1991(2) SCALE 1363; 1991 (Supp) RD 294; [1991] Supp2 S.C.R. 567; 1992 (1) UJ 483

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Special Relief Act, 1963

Summary: Contract and Commercial - Contract Act, 1872 - Specific Relief Act, 1877 - General Clauses Act ,1897, s. 27 - Evidence Act, 1872, s.114 - CPC, 1908, ss.100-100A , O. 6, r. 17 - Specific Relief Act, 1963, s. 21(5), Proviso - Agreement to sell land - Acquisition of Land - Suit for specific performance among parties (A) Whether refusal by addressee to accept notice is to be presumed as service of notice in accordance with s.114 of Evidence Act - (B) Reappreciation of evidence in second appeal - CPC, 1908 , ss. 100 to 100A attracted - Whether the findings by Court of facts is vitiated by non-consideration of relevant evidence or by essentially wrong approach - (C ) Whether to permit amendment of plaint and thereby conversion of suit into one for damages for breach of contract sought in the light of Provisio to s. 21(5) - (D) Whether there can be specific performance of agreement to sell - Held, notice must be presumed to have been served - In second appeal - Court not precluded from recording proper findings - Court will allow amendment - And s. 21 enables award of compensation to plaintiff in lieu and substitution of specific performance - There can't be specific performance but cost incurred is to be paid to vendor - Decree modified.
