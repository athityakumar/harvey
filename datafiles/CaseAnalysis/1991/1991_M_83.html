Zamindara Engineering Company v Messrs Sunil Tractor Company

Supreme Court of India

09 December 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

L.M. Sharma, R.C. Patnaik

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 303; (1993) Supp1 SCC 468; AIR 1992 SC 1545; 1992 (2) ARBLR 279

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Keywords: Challenged

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration Act, 1888, ss. 20, 20(2) - Indian Limitation Act, 1908, s. 14 - Jurisdiction - Challenged - Respondent filed application u/s. 20 of the Act before District Judge - Appellant contended in view of arbitration agreement that, only court entitled to entertain the application and District Judge had no jurisdiction to deal with the matter. Application was allowed, which was confirmed by HC - Hence instant appeal.

Held, there was no provision therein on the basis of which the term restricting the jurisdiction of the court could be held to be inapplicable to petitions u/s. 20 of the Act and it is not permissible to say that such an application is not a proceeding before a Court. S. 20(2) of the Act directs that such an application is to be treated as a suit and the disputed issue has to be tried and the decision is to be pronounced by court in accordance with the law. Stand of the respondent, therefore, has to be rejected. Thus, court at Fazilka alone had jurisdiction to entertain the application u/s. 20 of the Act and set aside the impugned judgments of both the courts below and remit the matter to the Court of the District Judge for being dealt with in accordance with law. Respondent suggested that since the application if filed before Fazilka Court it shall be apparently barred by limitation, some observations may be made by SC on the question of condonation of delay. Appellant replied that even ignoring the period during which the present proceeding has remained pending, such application would be fit to be dismissed on account of limitation. SC do not express any opinion on this aspect except observing that it will be open to the respondent to rely on s. 14 of the 1908 Act with respect to the period that has been spent in pursuing the present remedy. Appeal allowed.
