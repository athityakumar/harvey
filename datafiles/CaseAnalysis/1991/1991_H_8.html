Harbans Singh and Another v Guran Ditta Singh and Another

Supreme Court of India

20 February 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, N.M. Kasliwal

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 533; (1991) 2 SCC 523; JT 1991 (2) SC 138; 1991(1) SCALE 345; [1991] 1 S.C.R. 614

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Clog

Summary: Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 60 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908, arts. 14, 105, 134 and 145 - Limitation Act, 1963, s. 61, art. 100 - Redemption of Mortgages (Punjab) Act, 1913, ss. 4, 11, 12 and 13 - Whether suits for redemption of mortgages are barred by limitation? - Order of Collector binds parties or persons claiming right, title or interest from parties - Creation of mortgage is an act inter vivos and not a statutory or common law right; Act accords summary remedy and default of compliance with entails dismissal of application and s. 13 prohibits second application for selfsame relief and remedy of civil suit for redemption available at common law, subject to limitation, is not taken away; further applying principle of justice, equity and good conscience, though s. 60 of Transfer of Property Act, 1882 per se did not apply, principles in s. 60 would apply and though application for redemption was dismissed u/s. 11 and became conclusive u/s. 12 mortgagor's right to redemption is not barred - Held, a suit for redemption u/s. 60 of Transfer of Property Act, 1882 will be maintainable and civil court has jurisdiction to grant decree of redemption and suits are admittedly within limitation either under old Limitation or under new Limitation Act; bar of s. 12 does not oust jurisdiction of Civil Court to entertain and grant decree of redemption - Appeals dismissed.
