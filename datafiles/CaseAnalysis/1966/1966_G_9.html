Municipal Corporation of Delhi v Ghisa Ram

Supreme Court of India

23 November 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

Vishishtha Bhargava

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 134; AIR 1967 SC 970; 1967 CRLJ 939; 1967 (3) DLT 206; [1967] 2 S.C.R. 116

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection; Health & Drug; Socio-Economic

Keywords: Food Adulteration

Summary: Criminal - Health & Drug - Prevention of Food Adulteration Act, 1954, ss. 7, 13 and 16 - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 136 - Respondent, Halwai dealing in milk and milk products, including Dahi - Respondent prosecuted for committing offence u/s 16 of Act for contravening s. 7 of Act - Magistrate acquitted respondent - High Court upheld order of Magistrate - Present appeal - Valuable right is conferred by s.13 (2) of Act on vendor to have sample given to him analysed by Director of Central Food Laboratory - Right is a valuable one, because certificate of Director supersedes report of Public Analyst and is treated as conclusive evidence of its contents - S.13(2) specifically mentions that accused vendor may make application after institution of a prosecution under Act - In a case where there is denial of this right on account of deliberate conduct of prosecution, vendor, in his trial, is so seriously prejudiced that it would not be proper to uphold his conviction on basis of report of Public Analyst, even though that report continues to be evidence in case of facts contained therein - Principle must be applied to cases where conduct of prosecution has resulted in denial to vendor of any opportunity to exercise this right - Different considerations may arise if right gets frustrated for reasons for which prosecution is not responsible - Held, in the present case, prosecution committed inordinate delay in launching prosecution and no explanation is forthcoming why complaint in Court was filed about seven months after report of Public Analyst had been issued by him - Respondent's conviction is vitiated by this circumstance of denial of this valuable right guaranteed by Act, as a result of conduct of prosecution - Acquittal of respondent was justified - Appeal dismissed.
