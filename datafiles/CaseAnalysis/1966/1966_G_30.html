Janak Raj v Gurdial Singh and Another

Supreme Court of India

08 November 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

G.K. Mitter, Kailas Nath Wanchoo

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 120; AIR 1967 SC 608; 1967 ALJ 524; 1967 BLJR 639; [1967] 2 S.C.R. 77

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Sale Of Property, Restitution

Summary: Civil procedure - Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Whether sale of immovable property in execution of a money decree ought to be confirmed when it is found that ex parte decree which was put into execution has been set aside subsequently? - Policy of legislature is that unless a stranger auction-purchaser is not protected against erratic outcomes of suit sales in execution it would not attract customers and would be in detriment to creditors and borrowers - Respondent judgement-debtor did not choose to protect himself by invoking provisions of O.21, r.89 as decree was for a very small amount - Held, on facts of present case, sale in execution of decree has to be confirmed - Appeal allowed.
