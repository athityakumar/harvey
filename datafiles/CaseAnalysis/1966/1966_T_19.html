Travancore Titanium Product Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax, Kerala

Supreme Court of India

17 January 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, S.M. Sikri, K. Subba Rao

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 234; AIR 1966 SC 1250; [1966] 60 ITR 277; [1966] 3 S.C.R. 321

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922 - Wealth Tax Act, 1957 - Wealth Tax Rules,1957, r.3 - Business - Capital Expenditure - Depreciation Allowance - Commercial Expediency - Permissible deduction - In computing the total earned income of the appellant-company for the calendar year 1959, Income Tax Officer, disallowed a claim for deduction of Rs. 80, 255 in respect of liability for payment of tax under 1957 Act, incurred by the company for the calendar years 1957-58 - Order was confirmed by Appellate Assistant Commissioner and by Appellate Tribunal - Whether tax paid on net wealth by assessee under 1957 Act was a permissible deduction u/s.10(2)(xv) of 1922 Act - Held, amount of tax paid on the net wealth of an assessee under Wealth tax Act was not a permissible deduction u/s. 10(2)(xv) of 1922 Act in his assessment to income-tax - Tax was imposed under 1957 Act on the owner of assets and not on any commercial activity - Appeal dismissed.
