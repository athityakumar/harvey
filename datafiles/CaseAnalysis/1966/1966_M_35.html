State of Gujarat v Messrs Kailash Engineering Company

Supreme Court of India

26 September 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

Vishishtha Bhargava, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 184; AIR 1967 SC 547; [1967] 1 S.C.R. 543; 1967 (19) STC 13

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Sales Tax

Keywords: Bombay Sales Act, 1953

Summary: Sales Tax - Bombay Sales Act, 1953 - Respondent engineering concern executed contract with Western Railway Administration for construction of 3 class passenger coaches - Application for determination before Addl. Collector held against respondent - Whether works of building, erecting and furnishing 3 B.G. Coaches over chassis supplied by Railway is a works contract not amounting to sale, or whether it is a transaction of sale? - Held, all terms of contract lead to only inference that respondent was not to be owner of ready coach bodies and that property in those bodies vested in Railway even during process of construction - This was, therefore, clearly a works contract which did not involve any sale - Not liable to any tax - Appeal dismissed.
