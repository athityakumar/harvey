Commissioner of Income Tax, West Bengal v East Coast Commercial Company Limited

Supreme Court of India

11 October 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, Vishishtha Bhargava

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 169; AIR 1967 SC 768; [1967] 63 ITR 449; [1967] 1 S.C.R. 821

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Substantially Interested, Taxation of Income (Investigation Commission) Act, 1947

Summary: Income & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 23A - Actual exercise of control - Whether public was substantially interested in Co.? - Held, to establish that Co. is one in which public are not substantially interested, it is not a condition that actual exercise of control by a group must be established - And it is holding in aggregate of a majority of shares issued by a person or a persons acting in concert in relation to affairs of Co. which established existence of a block.
