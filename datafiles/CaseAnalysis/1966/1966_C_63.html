Swadeshi Cotton Mills Company Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax, U. P.

Supreme Court of India

20 September 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

Vishishtha Bhargava, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 197; [1967] 63 ITR 57

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922,s.10(2) - Capital expenditure - Assessment - Deduction - Compensation - Appellant was a public limited company carrying on the business of manufacturing and selling cloth and other textile goods - During the previous year ending corresponding to Assessment Year - Appellant entered into two contracts with two other parties for purchase of textile machinery in order to expand its factory - On cancellation of these contracts, appellant had to pay a sum of Rs.15,000 as compensation to one of the contracting parties and Rs.20,000 to the other contracting party who demanded compensation for breach of contract - Deduction claimed u/s.10(2)(xv) of 1922 Act, disallowed by Income Tax Officer and that order was upheld by Appellate Assistant Commissioner and Income Tax Appellate Tribunal - Whether payment of compensation amounting to Rs.35,000 had been rightly disallowed as capital expenditure within the meaning of s. 10(2)(xv) of 1922 Act - Held, that sum of Rs. 35,000 claimed as deduction u/s.10(2)(xv) was really paid for breach of contracts in respect of purchase of textile machinery which would have been a capital asset - Payment was therefore made to avoid a larger capital expenditure that would not have served interests of appellant/company - Payment made was clearly in nature of a capital expenditure and not an expenditure incurred wholly or exclusively for the purpose of business - Payment was made with the object of avoiding an unnecessary investment in capital assets - Computation of which deductions were allowable for expenditure incurred wholly and exclusively for earning those profits and gains - Therefore, that amount could not have been claimed as a legitimate deduction u/s.10(2)(xv) of 1922 Act - Appeal dismissed.
