Benjamin v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

13 December 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 23; 1967 (15) FLR 347; 1967 (1) LLJ 718

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Central Civil Services (Temporary Service) Rules, 1949 - Appellant was temporarily employed as a stores officer in Central Tractor Organization - Not a confirmed Govt. servant his services could be terminated u/r. 5 of Central Civil Services (Temporary Service) Rules, 1949 - Services of appellant were terminated - Appellant filed a suit for a declaration that order of discharge was illegal as it was in violation of provisions of art. 311 of Constitution, 1950 - Dismissed - Appellant second appeal was allowed - Whether order of Union Govt.,is an order by which punishment has been inflicted upon appellant and whether it is consequently an order of dismissal or removal within meaning of Constitution? - Appropriate authority possesses two powers to terminate services of a temporary Govt. servant - It can either discharge him purporting to exercise its power under terms of contract or relevant rule, and in that case, provisions of art. 311 will not be applicable Alternatively, authority can also act under its power to dismiss a temporary servant and make an order of dismissal in which case provisions of art. 311 will be applicable - Held, it is open to authority to make an order of discharge simpliciter is terms of contract of service or relevant statutory rule - Appeal dismissed.
