State of Madhya Pradesh v Shobharam and Others

Supreme Court of India

22 April 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sarkar, R.S. Bachawat, Mohammad Hidayatullah

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 92; AIR 1966 SC 1910; 1966 CRLJ 1521; [1966] Supp S.C.R. 239

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal -Constitution of India, 1950, art. 22(1) - Madhya Bharat Panchayat Act, 1949, s. 63 - Arrest - Denial of right to be defended by a lawyer of one's own choice - Validity of act - (A) Whether Madhya Bharat Panchayat Act, 1949 is violative of art. 22(1), Constitution? - Held, act is violative of art. 22(1),constitution and is void to extent it denies any person who is arrested, right to be defended by a legal practitioner of his choice in any trial of crime for which he is arrested - (B) Whether right to be defended by a counsel is limited to a trial in which arrested person is in jeopardy of being sentenced to death or to a term of imprisonment? - Further held,there is no warrant for giving a restricted interpretation to second part of clause by reference to Art. 21 and for saying that right to be defended by counsel is limited to a trial in which arrested person is in jeopardy of being sentenced to death or to a term of imprisonment - (C) Whether right of defence by a counsel does not extent to proceedings before Nyay Panchayat? - Further held, suggestion that right of defence by counsel given by art. 22(1) of Constitution does not extend to a trial of an offence before Nyaya Panchayat because Madhya Bharat Panchayat Act, 1949 does not authorise any arrest and, as a matter of fact, respondents were arrested by police in exercise of its powers u/s. 54 of Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 cannot be accepted - A person arrested on accusation of a crime has constitutional right to be defended by counsel at a subsequent trial of crime for which he is arrested - (D) Whether HC was justified in quashing trial and conviction? - Further held, since accused had not claimed to be defended by counsel during trial they could not be said to have been prejudiced by s. 63 of Act which denied protection guaranteed by art. 22(1) of Constitution and hence High Court should not have quashed trial and conviction - Appeal allowed.
