Union of India v Bungo Steel Furniture Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

14 September 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, Vishishtha Bhargava, Raghubar Dayal

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 402; AIR 1967 SC 1032; 1967 ALJ 419; 1967 (2) SCJ 440; [1967] 1 S.C.R. 324

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration Act, 1940 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 34 - Appellant applied to High Court for setting aside award on ground that there was an error of law apparent on face of award and arbitrator had exceeded his authority in awarding interest - Application was dismissed by Single Judge and a decree was granted to Company on basis of award - Appellant preferred appeals to HC - These appeals were heard by Division Bench who allowed appeal in part and reduced principal amount adjudged to be payable under award by Rs. 30, 970 and modified award accordingly - Whether arbitrator has the authority to award interest on the amount due? - Held, arbitrator had jurisdiction to grant interest on amount of award from date of award till date of decree - Reason is that it is an implied term of reference that arbitrator will decide dispute according to existing law and give such relief with regard to interest as a Court could give if it decided dispute - Though, in terms, s. 34 of CPC, 1908 does not apply to arbitration proceedings, principle of that section will be applied by arbitrator for awarding interest in cases where a Court of law in a suit having jurisdiction of subject-matter covered by s. 34 could grant a decree for interest - Appeal allowed.
