 State of Uttar Pradesh v R. B. Agarwal

Supreme Court of India

04 February 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, S.M. Sikri, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 373; AIR 1966 SC 1135; 1966 CRLJ 815; [1966] 3 S.C.R. 462

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Constitution - Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 134(1)(c) - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, ss. 417 and 423 - Supreme Court Rules, O. 21 r. 2 - (A) Whether State can move High Court for certificate of fitness against a person convicted by trial court but acquitted by HC on appeal? - Held, relevant words in art. 134(1)(c) are wide in their sweep - Application by state for a certificate under art. 134(1)(c) cannot be rejected in limine on ground that it is incompetent - It has to be entertained and considered and decided on merits - O. 21, r. 2 - No application to Supreme Court for special leave to appeal shall be entertained unless HC has first been moved and it has refused to grant a certificate - Case remitted to HC.
