Delhi Cloth and General Mills Company Limited v Workmen and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 October 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

G.K. Mitter, Kailas Nath Wanchoo

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 159; AIR 1967 SC 469; 1967 (14) FLR 4; 1967 (1) LLJ 423; [1967] 1 S.C.R. 882

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Bombay Industrial Disputes Act, 1938 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Payment of Bonus Act, 1965 - Dearness allowance - An order u/ss. 10(1) and 12(5) of the 1938 Act was passed over the signature of Secretary (Industries and Labour) referring to the Special Industrial Tribunal certain matters set forth in the Schedule annexed thereto for adjudication - According to the recitals in the order, it appeared to the Administration from a report submitted by the Conciliation Officer u/s. 12(4) of the Act that an industrial dispute existed between the managements of Delhi Cloth Mills and Swatantra Bharat Mills and their workmen represented by four different Unions and the Chief Commissioner, Delhi, was satisfied on a consideration of the said report that the said dispute should be referred to an Industrial Tribunal - Report of the Conciliation Officer showed that trouble had arisen over the claim of bonus in the Delhi Cloth and General Mills and Swatantra Bharat Mills, two units of the same company - Report also showed that at a meeting convened, the Works Committee recommended that the payment of bonus should be suspended pending examination of the entire issue in conciliation or otherwise - But before this could be announced, workers started demonstration outside the mill premises of the first named unit and became violent - Whether the parties were bound by any agreement with regard to issue No. 1 and the Tribunal would have to take evidence to come to a finding on that issue - Held, there was a binding agreement between the parties which had not been terminated and consequently, the Tribunal had to go into the question and if it came to the conclusion that there was such a binding agreement, it was precluded from examining the matter any further - It was impossible to spell out any agreement between the parties with respect to the bonus for the year 1964-65 or the allocation of capital and reserves of the company to the two units in calculating the bonus statement - Therefore, the parties were not bound by any agreement with regard to issue No. 1 and the Tribunal will have to take evidence to come to a finding on that issue - Appeals dismissed.
