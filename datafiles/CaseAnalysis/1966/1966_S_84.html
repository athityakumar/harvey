State of Madhya Pradesh and Others v Vishnu Prasad Sharma and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 February 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sarkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 364; AIR 1966 SC 1593; 1966 (2) SCJ 231; [1966] 3 S.C.R. 557

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4, 5A, 6, 17 and 48(1) - (A) Whether any number of declarations u/s. 6 can be issued successively in respect of different pieces of lands included within locality specified in a notification? - Held, supposed difficulty would provide any justification for accepting an interpretation of a statute against ordinary meaning of language used in it - Difficulty of Govt. does not lead to conclusion that Act contemplates making of a number of declarations u/s. 6 - Only one declaration can be made u/s. 6 that also would exhaust notification u/s. 4 and that notification would no longer remain in force to justify successive declarations u/s. 6 in respect of different areas included in it - Ss. 4, 5A and 6 of Act have to be read together and so read, conclusion is clear that Act contemplates only a single declaration u/s. 6 in respect of a notification u/s. 4 - (B) Whether u/s. 48 which gives Govt. power of withdrawal from acquisition before taking possession implies that a notification u/s. 4 remains in force for all purpose till such withdrawal? - Held, it can be cancelled at any time by Govt. u/s. 21 of General Clauses Act and what s. 48(1) shows is that once Govt. has taken possession it cannot withdraw from acquisition - Before that it may cancel notifications u/ss. 4 and 6 or it may withdraw from acquisition u/s. 48(1) - Appeal dismissed.
