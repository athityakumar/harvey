Har Swarup & Another v Brij Bhushan Saran & Others

Supreme Court of India

14 September 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, G.K. Mitter, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 301; AIR 1967 SC 836; [1967] 1 S.C.R. 342

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Keywords: Any Other Candidate, Purity

Summary: Election - Representation of the People Act, s. 79(b), 82 (b) and 90(3) - Interpretation of s. 82 (b) - Held, a person who is a candidate as defined in s. 82 (b) of the Act, will remain a candidate even after he withdraws till the election is over, and if he commits a corrupt practice whether before, or after his withdrawal he would be a necessary party u/s. 82(b) of the Act - Date of withdrawal cannot be a dividing line as to the time upto which he can be treated as a candidate and the time after which he cannot be treated as a candidate - Even though Raturi Vaid might have withdrawn his candidature u/s. 37 of the Act, he would certainly be covered by the words 'any other candidate' in s. 82(b) - Appeal dismissed.
