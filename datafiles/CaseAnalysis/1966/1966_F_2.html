Faqir Chand v Harnam Kaur and Another

Supreme Court of India

05 August 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Bachawat, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 326; AIR 1967 SC 727; [1967] 1 S.C.R. 68

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Joint Family Property - Mortgage - Sale of Property - Challenged - Appellant was member of joint family his father borrowed loan from respondents and securing repayments of loan executed registered mortgage deed which was joint family property - Respondent filed suit for claiming preliminary decree for sale of property before Trial Court which was obtained - Meanwhile appellant filed amended plaint claimed that decree was not binding on him before Trial Court which dismissed suit - Respondent took steps to execute decree - Aggrieved appellant filed appeal before HC which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Where father mortgages property of joint family consisting of himself and his sons for payment of his debt, but mortgage was neither for legal necessity nor for payment of his antecedent debt and mortgagee had obtained decree against father for sale of property but sale had not yet taken place, had sons any right to restrain execution of decree or sale of property in execution proceedings without showing either there was no debt which father was personally liable to repay or that debt had been incurred for illegal or immoral purpose - Held, Court answered in negative and stated that appellant obtained an interim order for stay of sale of property - Therefore, appellant had no right to obtain either interim or final order for stay of sale having regard to fact that admittedly debt was owing by father and was not incurred for immoral or illegal purposes - Nor did he acquire such right because he instituted suit before decree for sale was passed in mortgage suit - Hence, mortgage in its entirety bound property including interest of appellant therein - Appeal dismissed.
