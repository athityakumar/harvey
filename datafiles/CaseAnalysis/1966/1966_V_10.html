N. S. Shethna v Vinubhai Harilal Panchal

Supreme Court of India

25 August 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Shelat, K. Subba Rao

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 325; AIR 1967 SC 1036; 1967 (8) G.L.R. 260; [1967] 1 S.C.R. 174

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Summary: Media & Entertainment - Bombay Cinema Rules, 1954, rr. 101-107 and 116 - Respondent was carrying on business of exhibiting cinematographic picture and had obtained for that purpose a licence for sale of tickets which was valid upto 31-12-1960 - Suspension of licence for a period of two months - Writ petition - High Court took view that renewed licence was a separate licence and not in continuation of licence for year 1960 and a fresh show cause notice ought to have been served for suspending renewed licence and that not having been done inquiry was not in consonance with rules and quashed order - Appeal - Whether on licence issued for year 1960 having been renewed for next year renewed licence was in continuation of licence previously issued and whether show cause notice issued in relation to original licence would be sufficient and relate to renewed licence also? - No separate rule or provision conferring power to renew - No provision in these Rules laying down procedure for renewal or for fees for such renewal or conditions on which it may be granted except insertion in cl. 4 of word 'renewed' which only suggests that a renewal is competent - That being so, a renewal cannot, unless context requires otherwise, be regarded as a continuation of licence previously issued - There is also nothing in Chap. VIII or in Form F indicating that renewal is such a continuation - Held, fact that renewal is not a matter of course, fact that licensing authority can in proper circumstances refuse an application for renewal and is not precluded from imposing different conditions and can grant it for a different period coupled with absence of any Rules for renewal are all indications leading to result that renewal is a fresh grant and is not merely continuation of licence previously issued - HC was therefore correct in allowing writ petition on a conclusion that show cause notice relating to licence for year 1960 could not be regarded as a show cause notice in respect of renewal for next year and if renewed licence was sought to be affected in inquiry a fresh show cause notice relating to renewed licence was necessary - Appeal dismissed.
