Joint Family of Udayan Chinubhai v Commissioner of Income Tax, Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

14 October 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Vishishtha Bhargava, Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 212; AIR 1967 SC 762; 1967 (1) ITJ 175; [1967] 63 ITR 416; [1967] 1 S.C.R. 913

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961 - Partition - Separate possession - Income - Assessment - S, his wife T and his three sons U,K and A were originally assessed to Income Tax in status of a Hindu undivided family by Income Tax Officer - Joint family dissolved and that all members of family had become separate and that each member of family was entitled to a fifth share in properties - Properties which were allotted to S were set out and properties which were collectively allotted to share of U,K,A and T were set out - Other properties remained undivided between U,K,A and T - Income Tax Officer, initiated proceedings for assessing family of four members U, K, A and T/Assessees on plea that income of family had escaped assessment - Appellate Assistant Commissioner assumed that decree passed by HC brought about a complete disruption and severance of joint status of original family, and merely because assessees after severance had lived and traded together, they could not be assessed as a Hindu undivided family - Income Tax Appellate Tribunal restored order passed by Income Tax Officer - Hence instant appeal - Whether assessments made on assesses as Hindu undivided family was justifiable - Held, income from property of a Hindu undivided family assessed as undivided may be assessed separately if an order u/s.25A(1)of Act, had been passed - When such an order was made, family ceases to be assessed as a Hindu undivided family - If no order has been made, notwithstanding severance of joint family status, family continue to be liable to be assessed in status of a Hindu undivided family, but once an order has been passed, recognition of severance was granted by Income Tax Department and s.25A(3) of Act, have no application - Appeal allowed.
