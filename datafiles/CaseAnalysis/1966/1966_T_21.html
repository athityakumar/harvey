Raghubans Narain Singh v Uttar Pradesh Government Through Collector of Bijnor

Supreme Court of India

23 September 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Shelat, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, G.K. Mitter

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 275; AIR 1967 SC 465; [1967] 1 S.C.R. 489

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4, 23 and 28 - (A) Whether potentiality of land acquired ought to have been considered for determining compensation in present case? - Held, there is no evidence on record of any building activity of substantial nature being carried on in neighbourhood of acquired land at about time when notification was issued in 1945, there is equally no evidence of any trend of development of town in direction of acquired land, therefore, no question of valuation having to be made on basis of potentiality of land as building site can possibly arise - (B) Whether evidence of willing prospective buyer rightly rejected by High Court in determining rate of compensation? - An offer does not come within category of sales and purchases but nonetheless if a person who had made an offer himself gives evidence such evidence is relevant in that it is evidence, that in his opinion land was of a certain value - Further held, there being nothing to show that buyer or appellant knew that a notification for acquisition was about to be issued or that he colluded with appellant to fabricate evidence of an offer to enable appellant to get better compensation, it is impossible to treat evidence of Zaidi, prospective buyer, either as unacceptable or irrelevant - (C) Whether annual crop value an adequate method of valuation? - Further held, such a method is not always adequate as owner may not have so far put his property to its best use or in most lucrative manner and in a case like present grove had not yet started giving maximum yield, such method can and should be resorted to only when no other alternative method is available - (D) Whether u/s. 28, Court has a discretion to grant interest at a rate less than 6%? - Further held, where court exercises its discretion and grants interest u/s. 28, interest has to be at rate of 6% - Appeal allowed.
