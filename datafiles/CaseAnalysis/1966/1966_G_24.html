Gowli Buddanna v Commissioner of Income Tax, Mysore, Bangalore

Supreme Court of India

10 January 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, S.M. Sikri, K. Subba Rao

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 205; AIR 1966 SC 1523; [1966] 60 ITR 293; [1966] 3 S.C.R. 224

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961 - Hindu undivided family - Coparcenary Property - Income - Assessment - Appellant was member of a Hindu undivided family - Appellant was assessed during his life time in status of a manager of Hindu undivided family - Income Tax Officer assessed appellant in respect of income of previous year as a Hindu undivided family - Order of assessment was confirmed in appeal by Appellate Assistant Commissioner, subject to variation - Income Tax Appellate Tribunal confirmed order of Appellate Assistant Commissioner - Hence instant appeal - Whether income received by appellant was taxable as income of Hindu undivided family - Held, property of a joint family does not cease to belong to family merely because the family represented by a single coparcener who possesses rights which an owner of property may possess - Property which yielded income originally belonged to a Hindu undivided family - Family was represented by appellant alone after death of his father but property still continued to belong to that undivided family and income received there from was taxable as income of the Hindu undivided family - Appeal dismissed.
