Travancore Sugars and Chemicals Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax, Kerala

Supreme Court of India

20 September 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, Vishishtha Bhargava, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 199; AIR 1967 SC 477; 1966 (2) ITJ 841; [1966] 62 ITR 566; [1967] 1 S.C.R. 423

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 10(2)(xv) - Nature of expenditure whether revenue or capital - Whether the payment by the assessee to the concerned Govt. under the terms of agreement was allowable u/s. 10(2)(xv) of the Act? - Held, High Court merely regarded the expenditure as capital expenditure and hence was not allowable u/s. 10(2)(xv) and not examined as it being a division of profits - HC should consider all these aspects of the case before furnishing an answer to the question of law referred to it - HC order set aside and the case remanded to HC for rehearing - Appeal allowed.
