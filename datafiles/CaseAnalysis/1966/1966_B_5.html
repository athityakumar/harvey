Babu Manmohan Das Shah and Others v Bishun Das

Supreme Court of India

12 October 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Shelat, K. Subba Rao, R.S. Bachawat

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 171; AIR 1967 SC 643; [1967] 1 S.C.R. 836

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Uttar Pradesh (Temporary) Control of Rent and Eviction Act, 1947

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Rent Control - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Uttar Pradesh (Temporary) Control of Rent and Eviction Act, 1947, s. 3 (1)(c) - (A) S. 3(1)(c) of 1947 of U.P. Tenancy Act - Intrepretation - Ordinary rule of construction is that a provision of a statute must be construed in accordance with language used therein unless there are compelling reasons, such as, where a literal construction would reduce provision to absurdity or prevent manifest intention of legislature from being carried out - Held, word 'or' should be construed in its ordinary meaning - (B) Whether alterations carried out by respondent-tenant were alterations which materially altered accommodation within meaning of cl.(c) of s. 3(1) of Act? - Expression 'material alterations' in its ordinary meaning would mean important alterations, such as those which materially or substantially change front or structure of premises and it may be that such alterations in a given case might not cause damage to premises or its value or might not amount to an unreasonable use of leased premises or constitute a change in purpose of lease - Further held, in this view construction carried out by respondent must fall within mischief of cl.(c) and entitles appellants to maintain their suit for eviction without permission of District Magistrate and to a decree for eviction - Appeal allowed.
