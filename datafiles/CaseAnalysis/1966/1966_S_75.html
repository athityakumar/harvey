State of Gujarat v Vinaya Chandra Chhota Lal Patni

Supreme Court of India

05 September 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

Raghubar Dayal, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, Vishishtha Bhargava

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 64; AIR 1967 SC 778; 1967 CRLJ 668; [1967] 1 S.C.R. 249

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 45 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 408 - Trial court accepting testimony of complainant, convicted respondent of offence u/s. 408 for committing breach of trust with respect to amounts withdrawn in respect of three cheques - On appeal, High Court acquitted respondent - HC considered it unsafe to rely on evidence of complainant alone and held various documents, to be inadmissible in evidence - Whether documents provided by complainant provide strong corroboration to statement of complainant? - Statement of complainant about circumstances in which document was written and what it purported to indicate, is admissible - Held, documents handed over by respondent to complainant and statement of respondent provide strong corroboration to statement of complainant - Appeal allowed.
