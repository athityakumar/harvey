Rm. Ar., Ar. Rm. Ar. Ramanathan Chettiar v Commissioner of Income Tax, Madras

Supreme Court of India

27 October 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, Vishishtha Bhargava, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 166; AIR 1967 SC 657; [1967] 63 ITR 458; 1967 (1) MLJ(SC) 149; [1967] 1 S.C.R. 965

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Keywords: Estate Duty Act, 1953

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code Procedure Code, 1908, s. 34 - Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 4(3) - Assessee's assessement included money refunded by Estate Duty Authorities, Ceylon, and assessee claimed expemtion u/s. 4(3) of Indian Income Tax Act, 1922 on ground that receipt was of a casual and non-recurring nature - Whether receipt of interest, even if it constituted income, was exempt u/s. 4(3)(vii) of Act as receipt of a casual and non-recurring nature? - Held, interest was granted under decree of court from date of institution of proceedings in district court and was calculated upon footing that it accrued de die in diem, and hence it has essential quality of recurrence which is sufficient to bring it within scope of Act - Appeal dismissed.
