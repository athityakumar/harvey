C. P. Sarathy Mudaliar v Commissioner of Income Tax, A. P

Supreme Court of India

21 September 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Vishishtha Bhargava, Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 200; [1966] 62 ITR 576

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s.2(6A)(e), 23A, 66 - Hindu undivided family - Shares - Dividend - Supplementary statement - Assessee was a Hindu undivided family of which manager was S - Shares were acquired with funds of Hindu undivided family and therefore were taken to be property of Hindu undivided family - Dividend earned on these shares was also regarded as income of Hindu undivided family and was assessed accordingly - Hindu undivided family was sought to be charged to tax in respect of sums of Rs.5,790 and Rs.39,085 advanced as loans that they represented dividend income falling within s.2(6A)(e) of 1922 Act - Tribunal assumed that undivided family was not itself and could not be registered shareholder of company and individual members were registered shareholders - Whether HC had power to set aside the order of Tribunal - Held, it was duty of Tribunal to pass such orders as were necessary to give effect to judgment of HC - HC finds that material facts were not stated in statement or Tribunal had not stated its conclusions on material facts - HC may call upon the Tribunal to submit a supplementary statement of case u/s.66(4) of 1922 - But HC had no power to set aside the order of the Tribunal - Procedure followed by HC was erroneous - HC in a reference u/s.66 of 1922 Act, was exercising advisory jurisdiction it was not sitting in appeal over judgment of Tribunal - Appeal disposed of.
