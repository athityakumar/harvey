Kuppuswamy Chettiar v A.S. P. A. Arumugam Chettiar and Another

Supreme Court of India

06 September 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Bachawat, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 60; AIR 1967 SC 1395; [1967] 1 S.C.R. 275

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 101, 102, 103 and 104 - Contract Act, 1872, s. 18 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 6, r. 4 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, ss. 5, 123 and 9 - Misrepresentation in deed - Conveyance - (A) On whom does onus to prove misrepresentation lie? - Held, onus is upon person who alleges deed executed by him was vitiated by misrepresentation, to establish plea of misrepresentation - (B) Whether deed of release can operated as a conveyance of suit properties in favor of respondents? - A deed called a deed of release can, by using words of sufficient amplitude, transfer title to one having no title before transfer - Further held, deed was in favor of a person having no interest in property, and it could not take effect as an enlargement of an existing estate - It was intended to be and was a transfer of ownership - Appeal dismissed.
