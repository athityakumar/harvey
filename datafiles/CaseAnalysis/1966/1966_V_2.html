V. D. Jhangan v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

03 March 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, K. Subba Rao, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 347; AIR 1966 SC 1762; 1966 CRLJ 1357; [1966] 3 S.C.R. 736

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Illegal Gratification

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947, s. 4(1) - Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 101, 106 and 114 - Civil Procedure Code, 1898, s. 367 - (A) What are conditions to be satisfied u/s. 4(1) of Prevention of Corruption Act in order to raise presumption that accused has received gratification? - Held, accused should have received gratification other than legal remuneration - A receipt of money would be enough to raise presumption - (B) What is degree of burden of proof to be satisfied by accused to rebut presumption? - Held, accused has to prove on principle of preponderance of probability as is need in civil cases to dispel presumption - (C) Whether charges under s. 161, IPC and s. 5(2) r/w s. 5(1)(d) of Prevention of Corruption Act have not been proved against appellant? - Held, appellant has not proved his case by test of preponderance of probability and lower Courts rightly reached conclusion that amount was taken by appellant not as a loan but as illegal gratification - There is sufficient evidence to support conviction of appellant on charges under s. 161, IPC and s. 5(2) r/w s. 5(1)(d) of Prevention of Corruption Act - Appeal dismissed.
