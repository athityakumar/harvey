P. Arulswami v State of Madras

Supreme Court of India

29 August 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, Vishishtha Bhargava, Raghubar Dayal

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 65; AIR 1967 SC 776; 1967 CRLJ 665; [1967] 1 S.C.R. 201

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Defence & Security Forces

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure 1898

Summary: Criminal - Government of India Act, 1935 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 409 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1898, s. 197 - Appellant was elected President of Nerinjipet Panchayat Board on 17-05-1958 - At that time, he was a duly elected member of Board it appears that a sum of Rs.4,000 of Board had been invested in four National Plan Savings Certificates in Bhavani Post Office - It was alleged that appellant cashed them on 11-02-1959 and did not bring amount in account books of Panchayat Board - Defence of appellant was that he signed certificates and handed them over to P.W. 4, Deputy Panchayat Officer of block within which village was located - Sub-Divisional Magistrate, Erode was not satisfied that prosecution had proved charge and therefore acquitted appellant - But on appeal, High Court accepted prosecution evidence that it was appellant who, cashed certificates at Post Office and not P.W. 4 and accordingly found appellant guilty of offence - Whether sanction of Govt. u/s. 106 of Madras Act is necessary for prosecution of appellant for offence u/s. 409, IPC, 1860? - Held, it was held that sanction of State Govt. was not necessary for prosecution of appellant u/s. 409 of IPC, 1860 because act of criminal misappropriation was not committed by appellant while he was acting or purporting to act in discharge of his official duties and that offence had no direct connection with duties of appellant as a public servant, and official status of appellant only furnished appellant with an occasion or an opportunity of committing offence - S. 106 of Madras Act is similar in language to s. 197 of CrPC, 1898 - Sanction of State Govt. was not necessary for prosecution of appellant u/s. 409, IPC, 1860 - Appeal dismissed.
