Commissioner Of Income-Tax, U.P. v Nainital Bank Ltd.

Supreme Court of India

15 September 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, Vishishtha Bhargava

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 264; AIR 1967 SC 453; [1966] 36 Comp Cas 876; 1966 (2) ITJ 848; [1966] 62 ITR 638; [1967] 1 S.C.R. 348

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Land & Property

Summary: Income Tax Act, 1922, s.10(2)(xv) and 37(1) - Income Tax - Assesse Bank - Jewellery pledged by constituents with bank stolen in dacoity - Settlement - Bank agreeing to adjust market value of jewellery against amount payable by constituents - Whether the settlement and consequent accounting entries could be regarded as forbearance to enforce claim of Bank to recover loans - Whether expenditure having been laid out for protecting and furthering business of bank was properly admissible u/s. 10(2)(xv) of 1922 Act - Held, settlement could not be regarded as forbearance - Properly admissible u/s. 10(2)(xv) - Appeal Dismissed.
