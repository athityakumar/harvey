 Krishna Coconut Company and Another v East Godavari Coconut and Tobacco Marketcommittee

Supreme Court of India

27 October 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Shelat, K. Subba Rao, Mohammad Hidayatullah, S.M. Sikri, R.S. Bachawat

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 289; AIR 1967 SC 973; 1968 (1) MLJ(SC) 60; [1967] 1 S.C.R. 974

Case Digest

Subject: Trade

Summary: Trade - Madras Commercial Crops Market Act, XX of 1933, s. 11(1) - State Govt. of Andhra Pradesh by a Notification declared coconuts and copra to be commercial crops - The said Market Committee levied fees - Contesting the levy of fees under items 2 to 5 as being illegal on the ground that they sold coconuts and copra to customers outside the notified area and in some cases outside the State, appellants filed various suits in the court of the District for refund of the said fees collected by the said Committee at different times - Market Committee resisted the said suits - Said suits were tried together and the District Munsif held that the Committee had no power to levy and collect the said fees, and therefore, appellants were entitled to refund of the said fees and accordingly passed decrees in all the suits - In appeals by the Committee, the Subordinate Judge, dismissed the appeals and confirmed the judgment and decree of the Trial Court - Market Committee thereupon filed Second Appeals in the HC - Hence, the present appeal - Whether construction placed by HC was justified - Held, the object in passing the Act was to prevent the mischief of exploitation of producers of commercial crops such as coconuts and copra and to see that such producers got a fair price for their goods - The Act thus aimed at transactions which such a producer would enter into with those who bought from him - The words 'bought and sold' used in s. 11(1) of the Act aimed at those transactions where under a dealer bought from a producer who brought to the market his goods for sale - Such a construction was commendable because it was not only in consonance with the words used in s. 11(1) but was consistent with the object of the Act as expressed through its various provisions - The construction on the other hand canvassed by appellants was defeative of the purpose of the Act and should, be avoided - The construction which inclined to accept acquired some support from the fact that s. II made the purchaser and not the seller primarily responsible for payment of the fee and it was only when purchaser could not be identified that seller was made liable - Construction placed by HC on s. 11(1) of the Act was a correct construction and therefore respondent-committee had rightly charged appellants with said fee - Appeals dismissed.
