 Bhagat Ram and Others v State of Punjab and Others

Supreme Court of India

02 December 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri, K. Subba Rao, Mohammad Hidayatullah, R.S. Bachawat, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 268; AIR 1967 SC 927; [1967] 2 S.C.R. 165

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Bombay Sales Act, 1953 - East Punjab Holdings (Consolidation and Prevention of Fragmentation) Act, 1948 - Constitution Of India, 1950 - This is a petition u/art. 32 of Constitution challenging scheme made in respect of consolidation of village Dolike Sunderpur - (A) Whether scheme in so far as it makes reservations of land for income of Panchayat is hit by second proviso to art.31 A? - Held, scheme reserves lands for phirni, paths, manure pits, cremation grounds, etc., and also reserves an area of 100 kanals 2 marlas for income of Panchayat - Beneficiary is Panchayat which falls within definition of word 'State' u/art. 12 of Constitution - Consolidation Officer could easily defeat object of second proviso to art.31A by reserving for income of Panchayat a major portion of land belonging to a person holding land within ceiling limit - Therefore, reservation of 100 kanals 2 marlas for income of Panchayat in scheme is contrary to second proviso and scheme must be modified by competent authority accordingly - (B) Whether acquisition had already taken place before Seventeenth Amendment came into force, and, therefore, scheme is not hit by second proviso to art.31 A? - Further held, from provisions of Act, it is clear that till possession has changed u/s. 24, management and control does not vest in Panchayat u/s.23A - It was urged that by virtue of repartition u/s. 21, rights to possession of new holdings were finalised and could be enforced - This may be so but this cannot be equivalent to 'acquisition' within second proviso to art. 31A - Petition allowed.
