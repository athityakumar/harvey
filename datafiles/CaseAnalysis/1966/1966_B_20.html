 Bihar Mines Limited v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

03 October 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

Raghubar Dayal, K. Subba Rao, Mohammad Hidayatullah, S.M. Sikri, R.S. Bachawat

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 145; AIR 1967 SC 887; 1967 (2) SCJ 797; [1967] 1 S.C.R. 707

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property

Summary: Mines& Minerals - Bihar Land Reforms Act, 1950, ss. 3(1), 3(A), 10(1), 10(2) - Mining Leases (Modification of Terms) Rules, 1956, rr. 2 and 6 - Mines and Minerals (Regulation and Development) Act, 1957 - Bihar Head Reforms Act, 1950 - Mines and Minerals (Regulation and Development) Act, 1948, ss. 4(1), 4(2), 5 and 7 - Mineral Concession Rules, 1949 - (A) Whether mining leases that were considered, after date of vesting, to be leases by State Govt. could be defined as existing mining leases u/r. 2(c) of Rules, 1956 - Held, statutory lease now held by head lessee from State Govt. is a new lease granted after October 25, 1949 - When head lease could not be modified, it being not an existing mining lease, sub-leases could also not be modified - They too would be deemed to be new leases granted by new lessee from State Govt, as rights of lessor under original head lease had ceased on vesting of estate and he is deemed to have got a new lease from State - (B) Whether power of controller to modify terms and conditions of leases under Act, 1957 r/w. 1956 rules was applicable to such leases? - Further held, Controller could not have modified lease in suit under 1957 Act and 1956 rules - It follows that in pursuance of proviso to s. 10, terms of statutory lease could not be modified when lease be held to be a new lease from date of vesting - Appeal dismissed.
