 Gulabhai Vallabhbhai Desai Etc v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

27 September 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, K. Subba Rao, S.M. Sikri, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 152; AIR 1967 SC 1110; 1968 (1) SCJ 1; [1967] 1 S.C.R. 602

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Competence Of The Legislature

Summary: Land & Property - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 240, 14, 19, 31 and 31 A - Contribuicao Predial Regulation, 1896 - Daman (Abolition of Proprietorship of Villages) Regulation, 1962, ss. 2(g) - Validity of Daman (Abolition of Proprietorship of Villages) Regulation, 1962 - General scheme of Regulation follows that of other Reform Acts abolishing intermediaries in India - The Regulation makes a special provision in view of laws in force in former district of Daman - The Regulation purports to abolish proprietorship of villages in Daman District - (A) Whether proprietary interest can be regarded as an estate or its equivalent in relation to land tenures in force in Daman? - Held, 'estate' or its equivalent land which pays land revenue and is held in accordance with a law relating to land tenures - Lands in these petitions cannot be said to be 'estate' nor they pay land revenue - (A1) Whether land such as hilly land, salt, pans, salt land, quarries comes within description of 'estate' u/art. 31 A of Constitution ? - Definition in u/s 2 (g) of Daman (Abolition of Proprietorship of Villages) Regulation being at variance with Definition of 'estate' u/art. 31 A of Constitution cannot stand with it, but it being severable would not affect operation of Regulation - Held, land such as hilly land, salt, pans, salt land, quarries does not answer description of estate in art. 31 A - Petition dismissed.
