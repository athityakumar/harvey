Samarendra Nath Sinha and Another v Krishna Kumar Nag

Supreme Court of India

01 November 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Shelat, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, G.K. Mitter

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 113; AIR 1967 SC 1440; 1968 (1) SCJ 68; [1967] 2 S.C.R. 18

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Final Decree For Sale, Conditional Sale, Final Decree For Foreclosure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Land & Property - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, ss. 151, 152, O. 20, r. 3 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, ss. 52, 59 A and 60 - Appellant mortgaged property by conditional sale - Mortgagor assigned their right, title and interest to another person - Repayment time expired - Suit filed by respondent for enforcement of his rights - Appeal against said final decree was misconceived - (A)Whether once a judgment is signed by Judge it can be altered or added? - As per O. 20 r. 3 of CPC,1908 once a judgment is signed by Judge it cannot be altered or added to, but the rule expressly provides that a correction can be made under 152 of Code - Rule does not also affect Court's inherent power u/s 151 - Held, mistakes in judgments, decrees or orders or errors arising therein from any accidental slip or omission may at any time be corrected by court either on its own motion or on an application by any of parties - (A) Whether High Court was in error in allowing in passing said order of remand directing Trial Court to reopen question of redemption? - Final foreclosure decree was competently passed by Trial Court, respondent's right to equity of redemption was extinguished by that decree - He no longer had any right to redeem said mortgage, respondent's appeal against said final decree was misconceived and HC was in error in allowing it and in passing said order of remand directing Trial Court to reopen question of redemption - Appeal allowed.
