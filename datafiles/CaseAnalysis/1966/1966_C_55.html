S. Srinivasan v Commissioner of Income Tax, Madras

Supreme Court of India

04 October 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

Vishishtha Bhargava, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 147; AIR 1967 SC 517; 1967 (1) ITJ 138; [1967] 63 ITR 273; 1967 (1) MLJ 45; 1967 (1) MLJ(SC) 45; [1967] 1 S.C.R. 727

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Constitution of India, 1950 - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 16 - Whether the provisions of s. 16(3)(a)(i)(ii) were ultra vires as being beyond the legislative powers of the Parliament and whether interest credited by the aforesaid firm to the assessee's wife and minor children attributable to past profit accumulations only is includable in the assessment of the assessee under s. 16(3)(a)(i) and (ii)? - Held, the provisions of s. 16(3)(a)(i) and (ii) did not impose any unreasonable restriction on the fundamental rights of the assessee under Article 19(1)(f) and (g) of the Constitution, and were, consequently, valid - The cases when interest is earned on a deposit or a loan differ from a case of the type before us where interest was earned on amounts of which the minors permitted the use by the firm, because they were their accumulated profits arising from the firm itself and because of their interest in the firm as persons admitted to the benefits of the partnership - In the circumstances, the answer returned by the High Court to the second question was also correct - Appeal dismissed.
