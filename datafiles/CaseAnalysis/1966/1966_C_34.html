Miss Dhun Dadabhoy Kapadia v Commissioner of Income Tax, Bombay

Supreme Court of India

31 October 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

Vishishtha Bhargava, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 128; AIR 1967 SC 614; [1967] 63 ITR 651; [1967] 2 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act,1922 - Special resolution - Renouncement - Depreciation - Principles of accounting - Appellant for assessment year 1957-1958, holding 710 ordinary shares inherited as investment as she was not dealer in shares - Under special resolution passed at meeting of company appellant as holder of 710 ordinary shares became entitled to purchase new ordinary shares issued in ratio of one new ordinary share for one existing ordinary share - Appellant chose to renounce her right to all 710 ordinary in open market and as result of she actually realized sum of Rs. 45,262/- - Appellant raised plea that on issue of new ordinary shares value of her old ordinary shares depreciated because assets of Company remained stationary while number of shares increased - Plea of appellant was rejected by Income Tax Authorities as well as by Income Tax Appellate Tribunal - On reference, HC answered against appellant - Hence instant appeal - Whether assessee entitled to claim deduction from full value of consideration received for capital asset - Held, appellant claim should be deducted in computing her capital gain and claim was justified because net capital gain by her in transaction consisted of issue of new shares together with her renouncement of right to receive new shares and make some money - As per referred case, principles of Accountancy applicable to valuation of right to receive new shares issued by company are not applicable when computation made for purposes of taxation - Principles of accounting indicated that principle applied in order to find out net capital gain or loss arising out of transaction of nature which are concerned - Net capital gain could only be properly computed after deducting sum approximately represents loss incurred by appellant in her original asset of 710 old shares as result of depreciation in their value - Answer returned by HC set aside - Appeal allowed.
