Commissioner of Income Tax, Mysore v Canara Bank Limited

Supreme Court of India

13 October 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, J.C. Shah, Hans Raj Khanna

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 157; AIR 1967 SC 417; 1967 (1) ITJ 110; [1967] 63 ITR 328; [1967] 1 S.C.R. 859

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Devaluation

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922 - Respondent bank based in Mangalore, with a branch in Karachi - After partition, exchange rate between Indian and Pakistani rupees was determined - As a result of exchange rate, bank made a profit due to remittance from Karachi branch, which it deposited in its Contingencies Reserve Account - Claimed that this amount was a capital gain, and not taxable - Whether profit of Bank on account of fluctuation of exchange arose in course of trading operation of Bank or whether it was incidental to any such trading operation? - Held, appreciation of money did not arise in course of any trading operation - Sum of the money was at no material time employed, expended or used for any banking operation or for any foreign exchange business - Money changed its character of 'stock-in-trade' when it was 'blocked' and 'sterilised' and increment in its value owing to exchange fluctuation must be treated as a capital receipt, so not taxable - Appeal dismissed.
