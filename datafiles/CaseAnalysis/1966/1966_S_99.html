S. S. Rajalinga Raja v State of Madras

Supreme Court of India

26 October 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, Vishishtha Bhargava

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 164; AIR 1967 SC 814; 1967 (1) AnWR 54; 1967 (1) ITJ 311; [1967] 63 ITR 617; 1967 (1) MLJ(SC) 54; [1967] 1 S.C.R. 950

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Socio-Economic; Agriculture & Agri. Products

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Agriculture & Agri Products - Madras Plantations Agricultural Income Tax Act, 1955, ss. 3, 4 and 20(1) - Bengal Agricultural Income Tax Act, 1944, s. 2(1) - Coffee Act, 1942, s. 25 - Balance tax, and a penalty levied u/s. 20(1)(c) of Act, 1955 - Tribunal modified assessment and penalty set aside - High Court in revision allowed petition and restored assessment made by Department - Whether agricultural produce itself is income and becomes charged to tax under Act 1955, when it is received, and not when it is sold, used or consumed? - A tax on income whether agricultural or non-agricultural is, unless Act provides otherwise, a tax on monetary return-actual, or notional, not necessary for income to accrue that there must be a sale of a commodity, consumption or use of a commodity in business of assessee from which assessee obtains benefit of commodity may be deemed to give rise to income - Appeals dismissed.
