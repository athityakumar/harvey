George Da Costa v Controller of Estate Duty, Mysore, Bangalore

Supreme Court of India

28 October 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, Vishishtha Bhargava, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 222; AIR 1967 SC 849; [1967] 63 ITR 497; [1967] 1 S.C.R. 1004

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: (A) Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Estate Duty Act, 1953, s. 10 - Assessment - Estate duty - Property was purchased by appellant's father (deceased) in joint names of himself and his wife and gifted to sons - Donees accepted gift but parents continued to be in possession of house though municipal tax was paid in names of sons - Appellant filed return showing value of estate left by his father excluding value of house - Assistant Controller of Estate Duty included sum as value and determined aggregate value of estate and assessed estate duty - Appellant filed appeal to Central Board of Revenue (Board) which dismissed appeal and affirmed view of Assistant Controller - Board referred question for determination of HC who held that appellant was liable to pay estate duty - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether property was correctly included in estate of deceased as property passing or deemed to pass on his death u/s. 10 of the Act - Held, Board found that though deceased had gifted house to his children four years before date of death, he still continued to stay in house till his death as head of family and was looking after affairs of house - As matter of construction words 'by contract or otherwise' would not control words 'to entire exclusion of donor' - In order to attract s. 10 of the Act, it was not necessary that possession of donor of gift should be referable to some contractual or other arrangement enforcement in law or in equity - Even if donor was content to rely upon mere filial affection of his sons with view to enable him to continue to reside in house, could not be said that he was 'entirely excluded from possession and enjoyment' within meaning of first limb of section, and therefore property would be deemed to be passed on death of donor and would be subject to levy of estate duty - Appeal dismissed.

(B) Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Practice & Procedure - Estate Duty Act, 1953, s. 10 - Finance Act, 1965 - Interpretation of provision - Right of residence - Amendment - Whether proviso should be taken as legislative interpretation s. 10 of the 1953 Act as it stood previous to amendment and since no right of residence was reserved to donor under deed of gift imposition of estate duty was not justified - Held, amendment brought about by the 1965 Act was effective only from April 1 and was not retrospective - Insertion of second proviso to s. 10 of the 1953 Act should be taken to have been made deliberately by Parliament to be effective from date of amendment - There was no reason for holding that earlier provision in s. 10 of the 1953 Act should be interpreted with reference to language of amendment brought about by the 1953 Act - Appeal dismissed.

(C) Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Assessment - Half share of property - Whether as property belonged joint names only half share of property should be taken for estate duty - Held, Board found that property was purchased entirely out of funds of deceased that for purpose of income tax deceased declared entire property as his own and income was exclusively assessed in his hands - Board held that though property stood in joint names of deceased and his wife, she was merely name-lender and entire property belonged to deceased and was rightly included in his estate for purpose of estate duty - Thus, it was not possible to accept that only half share of property should be taken for purpose of estate duty assessment - Appeal dismissed.
