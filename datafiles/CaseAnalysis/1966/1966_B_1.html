Gujarat Pottery Works v B. P. Sood, Controller of Mining Leases For India

Supreme Court of India

03 October 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

Raghubar Dayal

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 144; AIR 1967 SC 964; 1968 (1) SCJ 30; [1967] 1 S.C.R. 695

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property

Keywords: Evacuee Property & Displaced Persons

Summary: Mines & Minerals - Mines and Minerals (Regulation and Development) Act, 1957, s. 13 - Mining Lease (Modification of Terms) Rules 1956, r. 2 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 31 A(1) - Whether period of lease was from commencement of Mines and Minerals (Regulation and Development) Act, 1957 or earlier, when agreement to lease with possession was delivered to lessee in 1939 and was executed in 1951? - Held, controller was competent to modify lease in favour of appellant to bring it into conformity with provisions of Act - Further, to bring lease in conformity with Act, its period could be cut down to 20 years from 1939 - Lease was really granted in December 1939 and that execution of lease in November 1951 was only to give a formal shape of lease granted much earlier lease in suit therefore is a lease which comes within expression 'existing mining lease' within r. 2(c) of 1956 rules - It further questioned validity of modification under Act - However, controller cut down period to 25 years from 1939 - The case focused on meaning of existing mining lease under Mining Leases (Modification of Terms) Rules, 1956 and also questioned continuance and validity of said Rules Court ruled that Rules were made in connection with regulation of mines and for development of minerals and Central Legislature was competent to provide for making of such rules by 1948 Act - Case focused on meaning of term winning given under Constitution of India Court ruled that said term in art. 31 A(1)(e) of Constitution should be construed to mean getting or extracting minerals from mines and other incidental purposes - Appeal dismissed.
