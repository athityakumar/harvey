Commissioner of Income Tax, Gujarat v Girdhardas and Company Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

07 October 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Vishishtha Bhargava, Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 190; AIR 1967 SC 795; 1967 (1) CompLJ 1; 1967 (1) ITJ 81; [1967] 63 ITR 300; [1967] 1 S.C.R. 777

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income-tax Act, 1961- Indian Income Tax Act, 1922,s.2(6A)(c) - Dividend - Liquidator - Accumulated profits - Assessment - Income Tax Officer brought in assessment year to tax Rs. 52,400 as dividend under 1922 Act. distributed Rs.30/- per share among shareholders - Income Tax Officer in the course of assessment sought to bring the entire amount of Rs.75,000/ distributed to tax as dividend within meaning of s.2(6A)(c) of 1961 Act as amended by 1956 Act - Objections raised by liquidator were rejected and amount was brought to tax - In appeal to Tribunal on behalf of assessee, it was urged that entire accumulated profit was exhausted when Rs.17,25,000/ were distributed and thereafter there were no accumulated profits in hands of the liquidator which could be distributed - Whether sum of Rs.75,000/ could be treated as dividend u/s.2(6A)(c) of 1922 Act - Held, amounts distributed to shareholders by a liquidator were therefore distributed as capital of company, since liquidator had no power to distribute dividend and sums received by shareholders cannot be disintegrated into capital and profits by examining the accounts of Company - S.2(6A)(c)of 1922 Act, no warrant that in course of liquidation accumulated profits exist as a separate fund even in a notional sense - Each distribution was of a consolidated amount which represents both capital and accumulated profits - Tribunal had not determined part of Rs.75,000/- represents accumulated profits - Appeal partly allowed.
