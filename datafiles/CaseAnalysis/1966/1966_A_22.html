State of Maharashtra v Atma Ram and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 March 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, K. Subba Rao, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 407; AIR 1966 SC 1786; 1966 CRLJ 1498

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Defence & Security Forces

Keywords: Wrongful Confinement

Summary: Criminal - Bombay Police Act, 1951, ss. 64 and 161(1) - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 330, 342, 343 and 348 - Wrongful confinement - Respondents/police constables ill-treated complainants and tried to implicate them in false cases - Respondents were convicted by Trial Court but on appeal High Court set aside convictions as prosecution of respondents was barred u/s. 161 of Bombay Police Act - Hence, present appeal - Held, there must be reasonable nexus between alleged act and duty imposed upon officer - If act has no nexus with duty of officer, he can be prosecuted - In present case, there is no connection between acts complained of and office of respondents and duties and obligations imposed on them by law - Respondents are not entitled to mantle of protection conferred by s. 161(1) of Bombay Police Act - HC order set aside - Appeal allowed.
