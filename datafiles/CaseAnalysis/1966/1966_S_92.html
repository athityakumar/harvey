 State of Madras v A. R. Srinivasan

Supreme Court of India

02 March 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, S.M. Sikri, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 408; AIR 1966 SC 1827; 1967 (5) FLR 104; 1967 (2) MLJ 21

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Constitution - Service - Whether the appellant ultimately decided to compulsorily retire the respondent merely on suspicion? - High Court allows petition - Appeal against - Held, the Tribunal had made definite findings against the respondent in respect of three charges, and the appellant accepted those findings before it imposed the penalty of compulsory retirement on the respondent - Unreasonable to suggest that the State Govt. must record its reasons why it accepts the findings of the Tribunal - It is conceivable that if the State Govt. does not accept the findings of the Tribunal which may be in favour of the delinquent officer and proposes to impose a penalty on the delinquent officer, it should give reasons why it differs from the conclusions of the Tribunal, though even in such a case, it is not necessary that the reasons should be detailed or elaborate - Where the State Govt. agrees with the findings of the Tribunal which are against the delinquent officer - Cannot think as a matter of law, it could be said that the State Govt. cannot impose the penalty against the delinquent officer in accordance with the findings of the Tribunal unless it gives reasons to show why the said findings were accepted by it - The proceedings are, no. doubt, quasi-judicial - Having regard to the manner in which these enquiries are conducted - No obligation can be imposed on the State Govt. to record reasons in every case - It may be that in disciplinary proceedings taken against public servants, the technicalities of criminal law cannot be invoked, and the strict mode of proof prescribed by the Evidence Act may not be applied with equal rigour, but even in disciplinary proceedings - Charge framed against the public servant must be held to be proved before any punishment can be imposed on him - Appeal allowed.
