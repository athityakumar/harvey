Smt. Shyam Kishori Devi v Patna Municipal Corporation and Another

Supreme Court of India

04 February 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, Mohammad Hidayatullah, R.S. Bachawat

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 372; AIR 1966 SC 1678; [1966] 3 S.C.R. 466

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Civil Procedure Code, 1908 - Bihar, Orissa Municipal Act, 1922, s. 117(1) - Whether appointing officer can act as a Commr. and as committee both? - High Court held that after supersession of Municipality no committee could be constituted u/s. 117 of Act - Special Officer, in absence of any specific machinery to deal with such an application, had perfect jurisdiction to lay out his own machinery to dispose - Asst. Special Officer enhanced valuation of holding of appellant and assessment thereof u/s. 107(1)(c) of 1922 Act - Held, conduct of officer was without any jurisdiction and burden of proof to establish assessment was incorrect and ground of assessment laid upon Commr. u/s. 107(1)(c) - Appeal allowed.
