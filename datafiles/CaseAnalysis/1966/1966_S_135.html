 Rajaanand Brahma Shah v State of Uttar Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

16 September 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, K. Subba Rao, Mohammad Hidayatullah, S.M. Sikri, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 54; AIR 1967 SC 1081; 1967 ALJ 144; [1967] 1 S.C.R. 373

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 2, 6 and 17 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, ss. 3 and 8 - Mirzapur Stone Mahal Act, 1886, s. 5 - Finality as to declaration of Govt. - (A) Whether declaration of Govt. under Land Acquisition Act, 1894 is final without exception? - Held, declaration as to public purpose by Govt. is final except where it is colorable exercise of power - (B) Whether High Court has jurisdiction to decide that finding of fact is correct under land acquisition statute? - Determination of character of land being preliminary finding of fact - Further held, HC is entitled in proceeding of writ of certiorari to determine upon its independent judgment, whether or not that finding of fact is correct (C) Whether opinion of Govt. can be challenged in a court of law? - Held, opinion of Govt. can be challenged as ultra vires in a court of law if it could be shown that state Govt. never applied its mind to matter or that action of state Govt. is mala fide - Order accordingly.
