Sree Meenakshi Mills Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax, Madras

Supreme Court of India

19 September 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Vishishtha Bhargava

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 195; AIR 1967 SC 444; [1967] 63 ITR 207; 1967 (1) MLJ(SC) 36; [1967] 1 S.C.R. 392

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s.10(2) - Specific Relief Act, 1877 - Cotton Cloth and Yarn (Control) Order, 1945 - Yarn manufactured - Assessment - Expenses - Deduction of amounts - Company/appellant incorporated under Indian Companies Act with its registered office carries on business of cotton spinning and weaving - Premises of factory of Company were installed 80 handlooms - Handlooms were found inadequate to weave yarn produced by factory and a part of yarn produced was distributed to weavers outside factory who were engaged by Company to weave yarn into clot -Textile Commissioner issued an order directing Company not to sell or deliver any yarn manufactured by Company except to such person or persons as Textile Commissioner may specify - Agreeing with HC that expression deliver in cl.18-B1(b) of 1945 Order - Company claimed deduction of amounts of Rs. 20,035/- and Rs.5,9121 for assessment years respectively as being expenditure wholly and exclusively laid out for the purpose of its business - Claims were rejected by departmental authorities and by Income Tax Appellate Tribunal - Whether appellant/Company could claim deduction u/s.10(2) (xv) of 1922 Act - Held, u/s.10(2)(xv) of 1922 Act as amended by Act 7 of 1939 expenditure even though not directly related to earning of income may still be admissible as a deduction in proceedings - HC that expenditure to fall within terms of s.10(2)(xv) of 1922 Act for purpose of earning income - Expenditure incurred to resist in a civil proceeding enforcement of a measure-legislative or executive which imposes restrictions on carrying on of a business, or to obtain a declaration that the measure was invalid - Conditions were satisfied u/s.10(2)(xv) of 1922 Act, as a permissible deduction in computation of taxable income - Appeal disposed of.
