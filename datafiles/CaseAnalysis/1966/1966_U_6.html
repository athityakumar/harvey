 P.L. Lakhanpal v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

21 September 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Shelat, K. Subba Rao, Mohammad Hidayatullah, S.M. Sikri, G.K. Mitter

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 176; AIR 1967 SC 908; 1967 (69) PLR 48; [1967] 1 S.C.R. 433

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Defence & Security Forces; Land & Property; Service

Keywords: Defence Regulations

Summary: Defence & Security Forces - Defence of IndiaAct, 1962 rr.30(i)(b), 3(2)(15)(i), 30A(9) - Detention - Challenged - Petitioner was detained by order dt. 10-12-1965 under r. 30(i)(b) of the Act preventing him from acting in any manner prejudicial to defence of india, civil defence, public safety and maintenance of public order - On 11-06- 1966 Central Govt. passed order under r.30A(9) for continuation of said detention - Aggrieved petitioner filed instant Writ alleging r. 30(i)(b) was ultra vires r. 3(2)(15)(i) of the Act - Whether order of detention passed by Minister of State under r. 30A of the Act was sustainable.

Held, affidavit filed by Deputy Secretary discloses detention order under r. 30A of the Act was arrived at by the Minister after an examination of all the materials before him. The affidavit discloses the activities of the petitioner and the conclusion arrived at by the Minister that the petitioner had acted and was likely to act in a manner prejudicial to the defence of India and civil defence. Thus, instant court does not sit in appeal against such a decision it would not ordinarily examine the adequacy or the truth of those materials and would not interfere with that decision on the ground that if the Court had examined them it would have come to a different conclusion. Therefore, contention of petitioner of mala fide exercise of power or non-application of mind by the authority concerned was disagreed. Petition dismissed.
