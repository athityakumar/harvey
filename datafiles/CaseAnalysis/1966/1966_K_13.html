Messrs Ram Chand and Sons Sugar Mills Private Limited v Kanhaya Lal Bhargava and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 March 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 123; AIR 1966 SC 1899; 1967 ALJ 102; [1967] 37 Comp Cas 42; 1966 (2) CompLJ 224; 1966 CurLJ 736; 1967 (3) DLT 21; [1966] 3 S.C.R. 856

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Corporate; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 11, r. 21, O. 29, r. 1, r. 3, s. 151 - Appellant's defence was struck off due to non-presence of director of appellant's company - Whether court can, strike off defence of appellant for default made by its director to appear in court? - Held, unless there is a finding of collusion between appellant and director where the former prevented latter from appearing in court, it is difficult to make company constructively liable for default of one of its directors, and it is not possible to hold that director in refusing to respond to notice given by court was acting within scope of powers conferred on him - If it was established that company was guilty of abuse of process of court by preventing director from attending court, court would have been justified in striking off defence - But no such finding was given by courts below - Appeal allowed.
