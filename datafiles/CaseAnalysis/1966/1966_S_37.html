Shyam Sunder v Satya Ketu and Others

Supreme Court of India

05 October 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, G.K. Mitter, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 399; AIR 1967 SC 923; [1967] 1 S.C.R. 752

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Election; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Election - Representation of the People Act, 1951, ss. 73(2), 98 and 116-A - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 96 - Allahabad High Court Rules (1952), Chap. XIV A - Conduct of Election Rules, 1961, s. 73(2) - Main question for decision before Tribunal was whether votes which should have been declared invalid in view of provision of r. 73 (2) of Rules had been counted as valid in favour of declared candidate - Tribunal held, that certain ballot papers bore Roman numeral I instead of Arabic numeral 1 - It is therefore held, that ballot papers marked with Roman numeral 1 were invalid u/r. 73 (2) of Rules as they did not bear Arabic figure 1 - Declared candidate then went in appeal to High Court, and his contention was that Tribunal was wrong in holding that ballot papers which had been marked by Roman numeral I were invalid - In addition, appellant raised a preliminary objection, namely, that appeal should be dismissed as it was not accompanied by a copy of decree - HC overruled preliminary objection and held that no copy of decree was necessary in view of provisions of ss. 98 and 116-A of Act - Whether it is legal? - Held, Supreme Court has opinion that in view of provisions of Act it is unnecessary to prepare a decree after conclusion of trial of an election petition - S. 90(1) would not make those provisions of CPC which require preparation of a decree applicable to trial of an election petition - Preparation of a decree is not necessary after conclusion of trial of an election petition - It is true that sub-s. (2) of s. 116-A lays down that HC shall follow same procedure with respect to such an appeal as if appeal were an appeal from an original decree passed by a Civil Court - But that does not mean that a copy of decree is necessary before an appeal u/s. 116-A for simple reason that Act shows that no decree is necessary to be prepared by tribunal at all and appeal u/s. 116-A (l) is also from an order and not from a decree - Rule does not say that figure 1 which has to be marked must be marked in what are called Arabic numerals or International form of Indian numerals - If that was intention it would have specifically mentioned in rule - It is true that in r. 73 (2) (a), figure 1 is shown in form of Arabic numeral, but that does not mean that rule intended that figure 1 on ballot paper can only be marked in Arabic form and in no other - It would not be right to read cl. (a) as laying down that figure 1 has to be marked in Arabic notation and if that is not so, ballot paper would be invalid - What rule provides is that ballot paper has to be marked with figure 1 to show first preference - If there is figure 1, first preference would be shown irrespective of whether figure was put down in form of Arabic numerals or in any other form - Appeal dismissed.
