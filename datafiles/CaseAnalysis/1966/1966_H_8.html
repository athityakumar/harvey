Hasan Nurani Malak v Assistant Charity Commissioner, Nagpur and Others

Supreme Court of India

22 August 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Shelat, K. Subba Rao

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 308; AIR 1967 SC 1742; 1967 MLJ 135; [1967] 1 S.C.R. 110

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Trusts & Associations

Summary: Trusts & Associations - Practice & Procedure - Societies Registration Act, 1860, s.18 - Bombay Public Trusts Act, 1950, ss.19 and 86(3) - Madhya Pradesh Public Trusts Act, 1951, ss.2(4), 5, 5(2), 6, 7, 7(1) and 8 - General Clauses Act, 1897, s.6 - (A) Whether Assistant Charity Commissioner appointed under Bombay Public Trusts Act, 1950, as extended to area of Vidharbha, has jurisdiction to hold an inquiry under s.19 in spite of a previous finding by Registrar under Madhya Pradesh Public Trusts Act, 1951, that trust in question was not a public trust within meaning of latter Act?- Held, Registrar is enjoined upon to make entry in register of public trusts irrespective of whether trust is found to be public trust or not - To say that he is required to make entry only if finding is in affirmative but not when it is in negative would be contrary to express language of ss.6 and 7 - (B) Whether fresh enquiry to ascertain whether said trust was a public trust or not is governed by M.P. Act or Bombay Act? - Further held, failure of Registrar to make entry as required by s.7 - Inquiry is incomplete and pending -Proceeding is governed by M.P. Act in view of s.86(3)(a) of Bombay Act - Fresh inquiry under Bombay Act precluded - Appeal allowed.
