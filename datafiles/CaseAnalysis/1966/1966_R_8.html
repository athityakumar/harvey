 State of Uttar Pradesh v Raja Anand Brahma Shah

Supreme Court of India

16 September 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri, K. Subba Rao, Mohammad Hidayatullah, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 53; AIR 1967 SC 661; 1967 (1) ILR(All) 538; [1967] 1 S.C.R. 362

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Constitution (Seventeenth Amendment) Act, 1964 - Uttar Pradesh Zamindari Abolition and Land Reforms Act, 1950 - U P Z A and L R (Amendment) Act, 1963 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 31A(2) - Whether amendment of definition of word 'estate' in s. 3(8) of Reforms Act made by s. 2 of Amendment Act of 1963 (the impugned Act) is within definition of word 'estate' in art. 31A(2) of Constitution? - Held, if State desires to invoke art. 31A and rely on definition contained in first part of cl. (a) it must show that area sought to be acquired is an 'estate' within definition contained in a law relating to land tenures passed before commencement of Constitution - Area in dispute is a grant in nature of Jagir or inam and consequently an estate within art. 31A(2), and hence impugned Act can claim protection of art. 31A - Area in dispute is a grant in nature of Jagir or inam, its acquisition like acquisition of all Jagirs, inams, or similar grants, was a necessary step in implementation of agrarian reforms and was clearly contemplated in art. 31A - Appeal allowed.
