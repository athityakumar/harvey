Khub Chand and Others v State of Rajasthan and Others

Supreme Court of India

22 August 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 71; AIR 1967 SC 1074; [1967] 1 S.C.R. 120

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - Rajasthan Land Acquisition Act, 1953 - Whether entire acquisition proceedings were void inasmuch as mandatory provision of s. 4 of Act was not complied with? - Held, provisions of a statute conferring power on Govt. to compulsorily acquire lands shall be strictly construed - Statutory intention is, therefore clear, namely, that giving of public notice is mandatory - U/s. 4(2), after such a notice was given, officer authorised by Govt. in that behalf could enter land and interfere with possession of owner in manner prescribed there under - If so, notification issued u/s. 4 without complying with said mandatory direction would be void and land acquisition proceedings taken pursuant thereto would be equally void - Appeal allowed.
