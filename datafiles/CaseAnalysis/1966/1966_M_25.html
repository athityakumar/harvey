Maturi Pullaiah and Another v Maturi Narasimham and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 March 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 409; AIR 1966 SC 1836; 1967 (2) MLJ 14; 1976 (1) SCJ 848

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Family & Personal; Land & Property

Summary: Family & Personal - Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Registration Act, 1908, s. 17 - Family arrangement - Sustainability -PV had no ancestral property all his properties were self-acquisitions - After death of father N was in charge of management -During course of his management large extent of properties were purchased in his name - After death of V, disputes arose between N and V's son - Suit came up before Subordinate Judge - Subordinate Judge, considered evidence and held that properties standing in name of respondent no. 1 were also joint family properties and that ex. B-1, dated 4-11-1939, embodied family arrangement effected between V and N whereunder defendant no. 1 branch would be entitled to 3 shares and branch of V would be entitled to 2 shares in all joint family properties and that said family arrangement was valid and binding on plaintiff - In result he gave decree to plaintiff for two-fifths of joint family properties - On appeal, DB confirmed view Trial Court both on factum and validity of family arrangement - Hence , instant appeal - Whether order passed by HC was justified - Held, N contributed to prosperity of family and their father who acquired properties before his death, gave direction that N should be given larger share in property - N was demanding partition and was asking his brother to give him larger extent of property as directed by their father - V accepted directions given by their father and agreed to give N three shares in properties and to take 2 shares for himself - Court held that lower Courts were correct in finding that this was family arrangement binding on members of family - With regard to validity of family arrangement i.e. Ex. B-1 there was neither division in status nor division by metes and bounds in 1939 - Its terms relating to shares would come into effect only in future if and when division took place - Document did not create any interest in immovable properties in instant case in favor of parties mentioned - Hence, it follows that document was not hit by s. 17 of Indian Registration Act - Appeal dismissed.
