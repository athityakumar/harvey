Sheodan Singh v Smt. Daryao Kunwar

Supreme Court of India

14 January 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, P. Satyanarayana Raju

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 389; AIR 1966 SC 1332; 1966 ALJ 578; [1966] 3 S.C.R. 300

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Res-judicata - Whether trial court was right in dismissing petition on ground which was preliminary in nature as being time barred or default in printing? - Held, where trial court has decided two suits having common issues on merit and there are two appeals therefrom and one of them is dismissed on some preliminary ground, like limitation or default in printing, with result that trial court's decision stands confirmed, decision of appeal court will be res judicata and appeal court must be deemed to have heard and finally decided matter - In such a case result of decision of appeal court is to confirm decision of trial court given on merits, and if that is so, decision of appeal court will be res judicata whatever may be reason for dismissal - It would be a different matter, however, where decision of appeal court does not result in confirmation of decision of trial court given on merit, as for example, where appeal court holds that trial court had no jurisdiction and dismisses appeal even though trial court might have dismissed suit on merits - Appeal dismissed.
