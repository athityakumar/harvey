 State of Punjab v Amar Singh Harika

Supreme Court of India

06 January 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, P. Satyanarayana Raju, Mohammad Hidayatullah

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 14; AIR 1966 SC 1313; 1966 (2) LLJ 188

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Constitution - Pepsu General Provisions (Administration) Ordinance, ss. 13 and 14(2) - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 311(2) - (A) Whether dismissal of plaintiff from service of defendant illegal, void and ultra-vires of art. 311(2)? Govt. officer - Dismissal of - Suit filed by officer to challenge validity of order of dismissal - Dismissal without giving a fair hearing and without being told exact reasons for such dismissal is bad in law - Held, an order of dismissal passed by an appropriate authority and kept on its file without communicating it to officer concerned or otherwise publishing it does not take effect as from date on which order is actually written out by said authority; such an order can only be effective after it is communicated to officer concerned or is otherwise published, and not otherwise - (B) Whether the suit is maintainable? (B) Farmani-Shahi which was law in Patiala had provided that no suit shall be instituted by any private individual against State or any State Officer in respect of his dismissal from State service - After fusion new law (Ordinance) will have superiority over Farman-Shahi - Specific provisions of s. 14(2) of Ordinance override bar created by Farman-Shahi against competence of suit - S. 14, in substance, corresponds to art. 311 of Constitution - Having regard to significance and importance of guarantee contained in s. 14(2) of Ordinance, it would be reasonable to hold that suits filed by public servants for purpose of challenging validity of orders of dismissal passed against them in contravention of s. 14(2) are competent - Respondent had not received a reasonable opportunity to make his defense and that the proceedings of enquiry and report made by committee, as well as final order of dismissal passed against respondent, have contravened safeguards guaranteed by s. 14(2) of the Ordinance - Appeal dismissed.
