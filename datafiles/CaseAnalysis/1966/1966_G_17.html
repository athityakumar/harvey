Gomathinayagam Pillai And Ors. v Pallaniswami Nadar

Supreme Court of India

02 September 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, R.S. Bachawat

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 303; AIR 1967 SC 868; [1967] 1 S.C.R. 227

Case Digest

Subject: Contract & Commercial

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Indian Contract Act, s. 55 - Whether under the agreement of sale, time was of the essence and whether as alleged by appellants, 1, 2 and 3, the respondent was not ready and willing to perform his part of the contract, and was on that account disentitled to a decree for specific performance? - Held, in the present case there is no express stipulation, and the circumstances are not such as to indicate that it was the intention of the parties that time was intended to be of the essence of the contract - The Trial Court recorded a clear finding against the respondent that he was at no time ready and willing to perform his part of the contract-decree passed by the HC is set aside, and the decree passed by the Trial Court restored - Appeal allowed.
