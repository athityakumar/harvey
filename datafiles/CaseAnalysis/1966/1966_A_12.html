 Ajit Singh v State of Punjab and Another

Supreme Court of India

02 December 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri, K. Subba Rao, Mohammad Hidayatullah, R.S. Bachawat, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 287; (1986) 1 SCC 58; AIR 1967 SC 856; [1967] 2 S.C.R. 143

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Constitution Of India, 1950, art.31 A(1), Proviso 2, 31(2 A) and 12, sch.7, list 3, entry 42 - EP Holdings (Consolidation and Prevention of Fragmentation Act, 1948 - Interpretation of Statutes - Expression 'acquisition by State of an estate - Whether appellant, a small landholder holding land within ceiling and has lost some of it, is entitled to compensation at market rate as required by second proviso to art.31 A of Constitution? Held, when State acquires almost entire bundle of rights, it is acquisition- Total land of holder for compensation must be assessed at market value and value of dimunition of area determined proportionately - The appellant is entitled to compensation and he cannot be deprived of land within his ceiling without payment of compensation - Admittedly his land has been reduced to something less than ceiling applicable to him - Appeal allowed.
