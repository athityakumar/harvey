Ramchandra Aggarwal and Anr v State of Uttar Pradesh and Anr

Supreme Court of India

05 May 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 281; 1966 (3) ACC 19; AIR 1966 SC 1888; 1966 ALJ 1122; 1966 AllCriR 397; 1966 CRLJ 1514; [1966] Supp S.C.R. 393

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure 1898, Agra Tenancy Act, 1926, Agra Tenancy Act

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Persona Designata - Jurisdiction - Maintainability - Agra Tenancy Act, s. 271 - Arbitration Act, 1940, s. 41 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, r. 3, ss. 141, 24, 24(1)(b) - Code of Criminal Procedure 1898, ss. 145, 145(6), 146, 146 1-D, 146(1) -Constitution of India, 1950, art. 133(1)(c) - Proceedings u/ s. 145, CrPC were initiated by Magistrate - Magistrate attached property to which dispute related and called upon parties to adduce evidence and was unable to make up his mind as to which of parties was in possession - Magistrate referred case u/s. 146(1) CrPC to Civil Court - Reference went to court of Munson but thereafter respondent No. 2 made application to District Judge u/s. 24, CPC for transfer of case on ground that in execution case out of which proceedings u/s.145,CPC had arisen, same Munsiff had made order against him depriving him of costs - Munsifftransferredcase to District Judge of another Munsiff - Appellants acquiesced in order of transfer and did not raise any question as to jurisdiction of transferee court - After receiving finding I Magistrate heard parties and held that it was respondent no. 2 who was in possession at relevant date and passed order u/s. 145(6), CrPC - Revision application was preferred by appellants before court of Sessions - Learned Additional Sessions Judge rejected contentions on ground that they were not raised earlier -Appellants then took matter to HC in revision and claimed that s. 24, CPC was not available in respect ofreference u/s. 146(1) CrPC and that, therefore, proceedings subsequent to transfer of reference from court of one Massif to that of another were a nullity -HC permitted point to be urgedthat reference u/s. 146(1), CrPC was to persona designate and that provisions of s. 24, CPCwere not available with respect to it -Secondly proceeding before civil court was not civil proceeding within meaning of s. 141, CPC -HC rejected both grounds on which contention was based - Hence, instant appeal - Whether District Judge had jurisdiction u/s. 24 CPC to transfer reference made by Magistrate to particular civil court u/s. 146 CrPC to another civil court - Held, in Kochadai Naidu v. Nagavasami Naidu 1960 Indlaw MAD 153 was called upon to consider very question which arised in instant case - Court held that proceeding before civil court arising out of reference to it u/s. 146(1),CrPC could be transferred by HC or District Court u/s. 24, CPC because it was in any case proceedings - Nature of proceeding was considered and expressed view that proceeding was civil proceeding to which procedure for suits could, with aid of s. 141, CPC be applied - If indeed term 'proceeding' in s. 24 was not confined to civil proceeding there was no need whatsoever of taking aid of S. 141, CPC - Appeal dismissed.
