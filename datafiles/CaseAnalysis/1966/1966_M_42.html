 Jalan Trading Company (Private Limited ) v Mill Mazdoor Union (With Connected Petitions

Supreme Court of India

05 August 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Mohammad Hidayatullah, S.M. Sikri, Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 99; AIR 1967 SC 691; [1966] 36 Comp Cas 901; 1966 (13) FLR 298; 1966 (2) LLJ 546; 1967 (1) SCJ 189; [1967] 1 S.C.R. 15

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Set On, Minimum Bonus, Rehabilitation, Available Surplus, Maximum Bonus, Full Bench Formula, Allocable Surplus, Super Profit Tax Act, 1963, Business Profits Act, 1947

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Constitution - Companies (Profits) Surtax Act, 1964 - Companies Act, 1956 - Govt. of India Act, 1935 - Coal Mines Provident Fund and Bonus Schemes Act, 1948 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Payment of Bonus Act, 1965, ss. 32, 33, 34 and 37 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Whether ss. 33, 34 and 37 of Payment of Bonus Act are ultravires? - Held, s. 37 which authorises Central Govt. to provide by order for removal of doubts or difficulties in giving effect to provisions of Act, in judgment of court, delegates legislative power which is not permissible - By providing that order made must not be inconsistent with purposes of Act, s. 37 is not saved from vice of delegation of legislative authority - If in giving effect to provisions of Act any doubt or difficulty arises, it is for Legislature to remove that doubt or difficulty and not an Executive body - Ss. 33 and 34 are patently discriminatory as there is no nexus between classification and object of Act - Ss. 33 and 34 wrongly discriminate between two similarly placed industries of one of them had a dispute pending on 29-05-1965 - Ss. 33, 34 and 37 of Act are invalid - Appeal allowed.
