Srichand K. Khetwani v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

27 September 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

Raghubar Dayal, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, Vishishtha Bhargava

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 276; AIR 1967 SC 450; 1967 (69) BomLR 313; 1967 CRLJ 414; [1967] 1 S.C.R. 595

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Trade

Keywords: Postal Receipt, Code of Criminal Procedure 1898, Bombay Sales Act, 1953

Summary: Criminal - Trade - Bombay Sales Act, 1953 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120B and 409 - Appellant convicted for offence punishable u/s. 120B r/w s. 409 IPC, 1860 and s. 5(2) r/w s. 5(1)(d) of Prevention of Corruption Act - Whether conviction of appellant is sustainable? - Appellants contended that charge of conspiracy as laid is not established - Courts below relied on statement of accomplice - Material provided by writing on acknowledgment receipt was very scanty - Investigating officer had not stated his reasons for not obtaining opinion of handwriting expert - Adverse inference against prosecution can be drawn only if it withholds certain evidence and not merely on account of its failure to obtain certain evidence - No question of presuming that evidence would have been against prosecution u/s. II 4, illustration (g) of Evidence Act can arise - Accomplice evidence received indirect corroboration from subsequent conduct of appellant - Held, conviction of appellant correct - Appeal dismissed.
