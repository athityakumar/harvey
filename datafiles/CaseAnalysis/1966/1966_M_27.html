Gopalakrishna Pillai and Others v Meenakshi Ayal and Others

Supreme Court of India

31 March 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Bachawat, A.K. Sarkar, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 82; AIR 1967 SC 155; 1967 ALJ 239; 1967 BLJR 222; 1967 CRLJ 691; 1967 (1) MLJ(SC) 89; [1966] Supp S.C.R. 128

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Court Fees Act, 1870 - Immovable property - Mesne profits - Decree-holder - Examination in chief - Person died issueless - He had bequeathed certain items by his will of the suit properties to his wife and the rest of the items to his mother - He appointed his mother as the trustee of certain items for the benefit of a temple - Wife died and after her death, his mom inherited her properties as a limited heir - Defendants 6 and 7 claimed that their father purchased certain item from an agent under sale deed - Deceased mother executed a deed of gift in giving certain items - Deceased mother was said to have executed a will bequeathing to a certain person the remaining properties belonging to her and inherited by her as a limited heir of the deceased wife and also certain items from her trusteeship - Deceased mother died and the plaintiff were the heirs - Plaintiffs and the defendants preferred separate appeals - Will was genuine and was duly executed and attested but it was inoperative with regard to items and the trusteeship rights in those items - Whether the provisions of 0. 20, r. 12 apply to the present suit - Held, the plaintiffs distinctly pleaded in paragraph 9 of the plaint that they were entitled to call upon the defendants to account for mesne profits since the death of mother in respect of the suit properties - For purposes of jurisdiction and court-fees, they valued their claim for possession and mesne profits for three years prior to the date of the suit and paid court-fee thereon - Plaintiffs claimed recovery of possession, an account of mesne profits for three years prior to the date of the suit, costs and such other relief as may seem fit and proper to the Court in the circumstances of the case - Suit was for recovery of possession of immovable property and for mesne profits - Provisions of 0. 20, r. 12 were, therefore, attracted to the suit and the Court had power to pass a, decree in the suit for both past and future mesne profits - O. 20, r. 12 enabled the Court to pass a decree for both past and future mesne profits but there are important distinctions in the procedure for the enforcement of the two claims - Plaint claimed only declaration of title and recovery of possession of immovable properties and made no demand or claim for either past or future mesne profits or rent - Suit was not one 'for the recovery of possession of immovable property and for rent or mesne profits', and the Court could not pass a decree for future mesne profits under 0. 20, r. 12 of the CPC - Suit was for the recovery of possession of immovable property and for past mesne profits, the Court had ample power to pass a decree' directing an enquiry as to future mesne profits, though there was no specific prayer for the same in the plaint - Appeal dismissed.
