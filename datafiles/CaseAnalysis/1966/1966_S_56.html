 Naresh Shridhar Mirajkar And Ors v State Of Maharashtra And Anr

Supreme Court of India

03 March 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, A.K. Sarkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, S.M. Sikri, R.S. Bachawat, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.C. Shah, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 346; AIR 1967 SC 1; [1966] 3 S.C.R. 744

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure; Trade

Keywords: Furnishing Of Security, Court Of Limited Jurisdiction, Attachment Of Property, Attorney-General

Summary: Civil Procedure - Municipalities & Local Governments - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908 Order 39 R.1 - Constitution, Art. 32 - 19(1)(a) and (g), - Whether whether a judicial order passed by the High Court prohibiting the publication in newspapers of evidence given by a witness pending the hearing of the suit, is amenable to be corrected by a writ of certiorari issued by this Court under Art. 32(2). ? - The High Court has jurisdiction to decide if it has jurisdiction to restrain the publication of any document or information relating to the trial of a pending suit or concerning which the suit is brought.-In a jurisdiction not vested in it by law, its decision may be set aside in appropriate proceedings, but the decision is not open to attack on the ground that it infringes the fundamental right. - there should be no restraint on the publication of the report of the Court proceedings. The publicity generates public confidence in the administration of justice Held, In rare and exceptional cases only, the Court may hold the trial behind closed doors, or may forbid the publication of the report of its proceedings during the pendency of the litigation. - Held, petitions are not maintainable, and is dismissed.
