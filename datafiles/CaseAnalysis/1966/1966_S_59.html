 State of Assam and Others v Shri Kanak Chandra Dutta

Supreme Court of India

03 October 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Bachawat, K. Subba Rao, Mohammad Hidayatullah, S.M. Sikri, Raghubar Dayal

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 296; AIR 1967 SC 884; 1967 (14) FLR 299; 1968 (1) LLJ 288; [1967] 1 S.C.R. 679

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Assam Land and Revenue Regulation , 1886

Summary: Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 311(2) - Protection u/art. 311(2) - Whether a Mauzadar in the Assam Valley holds a civil post under the State of Assam, and was entitled to the protection of art. 311(2) of the Constitution.

Held, a Mauzadar in the Assam Valley was the holder of a civil post under the State. The State had the power and the right to select and appoint a Mauzadar and the power to suspend and dismiss him. Mauzadar was a subordinate public servant working under the supervision and control of the Deputy Commissioner. Mauzadar receives by way of remuneration a commission on his collections and sometimes a salary. There was a relationship of master and servant between the State and him. Mauzadar holds an office on the revenue side of the administration to which specific and onerous duties in connection with the affairs of the State were attached, an office which falls vacant on the death or removal of the incumbent and which was filled up by successive appointments. HC rightly held that the respondent held a civil post under the State of Assam, and was entitled to the protection of art. 311(2) of the Constitution. Appeal dismissed.
