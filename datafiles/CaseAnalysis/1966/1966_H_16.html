Firm Madanlal Roshanlal Mahajan v Hukumchand Mills Limited, Indore

Supreme Court of India

19 August 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Bachawat, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 109; AIR 1967 SC 1030; 1967 ALJ 360; 1967 BLJR 353; 1967 JabLJ 1; 1967 MahLJ 181; 1967 MLJ 181; 1967 MPLJ 35; [1967] 1 S.C.R. 105

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure; Socio-Economic

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration Act, 1940 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 34 - Dispute between parties was referred to arbitration and award was filed in court, appellant filed application to set aside award which was dismissed - Whether arbitrator can award interest during pendency of arbitration proceedings if claim regarding interest is referred to arbitration? - Held, though, in terms, s. 34 of CPC does not apply to arbitrations, it was an implied term of reference in the suit that arbitrator would decide dispute according to law and would give such relief with regard to pendente lite interest as the Court would give if it decided the dispute - This power of arbitrator was not fettered either by arbitration agreement or by Arbitration Act, 1940 - Appeal dismissed.
