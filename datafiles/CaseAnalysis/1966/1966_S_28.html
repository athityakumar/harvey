Shivnarayan Kabra v The State Of Madras

Supreme Court of India

23 August 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Ramaswami, Vishishtha Bhargava, Raghubar Dayal

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 307; AIR 1967 SC 986; 1967 CRLJ 946; 1967 (1) MLJ(SC) 159; [1967] 1 S.C.R. 138

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Wagering Contract

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 420 - Forward Contracts (Regulation) Act, 1952, ss. 21(d) and (e) - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Appellant was charged and convicted for having committed offences u/s. 420, of the IPC and s. 21(d) and (e), of the 1952 Act - Hence, the present appeal - Whether the appellant was rightly convicted - Held, that even though it was accepted that the appellant was merely acting as an agent and had placed an order through a member of the recognised association, a breach of the provision of the Act had occurred, as the appellant was doing forward contract business as a Pucca adatia, and it was well-established that the pucca adatia had no authority to pledge the credit of the upcountry constituent to the Bombay merchant and there was no privity of contract as between the upcountry constituent and the Bombay merchant - Appeal dismissed.
