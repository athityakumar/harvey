Harinagar Sugar Mills Limited v M. W. Pradhan

Supreme Court of India

21 March 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, J.M. Shelat, V. Ramaswami

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 310; AIR 1966 SC 1707; 1966 (68) BomLR 632; [1966] 36 Comp Cas 426; 1966 (2) CompLJ 17; [1966] 60 ITR 508; 1966 MahLJ 1089; [1966] 3 S.C.R. 948

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Corporate; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1922, s.46 - Income Tax Act, 1961, s.222 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O.40, r.1(d) - Companies Act, 1956, ss.434 and 439 - Income Tax - Company - Realisation of debt due to Hindu Undivided Family from company - Court receiver appointed pending suit for partition - Clothed with all powers u/r. 1(d), O.40 of Code - (A) Whether receiver can maintain winding up petition - (B) Whether interim receiver appointed u/r.1(d), O. 40 of Code is creditor within meaning of s.439 (1)(b) of Companies Act - (C) Consideration of notice by creditor calling upon company to pay entire amount of debt to Collector towards income tax dues in light of s.434 of companies Act - Company also served with notice u/s.46 of 1922 Act - (D) Company not complying with Collector's requisition u/s. 46 of Income-tax Act and with direction of creditor to pay entire amount of debt to Collector towards income tax dues - Held, receiver can maintain winding up petition for realisation of debt, though it is not normal alternative to ordinary procedure for realisation of debts - Propriety does not affect power but only its exercise - Further held, notice by creditor strictly complied with requirements of section and payment by company would have constituted full discharge of its liabilities to creditor - Company must be considered to have neglected to pay amount - Appeal dismissed.
