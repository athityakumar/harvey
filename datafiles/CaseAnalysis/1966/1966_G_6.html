 General Assurance Society Limited v Chandmull Jain and Another

Supreme Court of India

07 February 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, P. Satyanarayana Raju, Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 254; 1966 ACJ 267; AIR 1966 SC 1644; [1966] 36 Comp Cas 468; [1966] 3 S.C.R. 500

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate

Summary: Insurance Act, 1938, Preamble - Deed - Contract of insurance - Ambiguity - (A) Construction and interpretation of contract - (B) Whether a condition in policy giving mutual rights to parties to cancel insurance at any time is reasonable - Held, in case of ambiguity or doubt contract is to be construed contra proferentem that it is against insurance company - Duty of court is to interpret words in which contract is expressed by parties, because it is not for court to make a new contract - Condition is not unreasonable and cannot be read as given right to terminate only for reasonable cause - Appeal allowed.
