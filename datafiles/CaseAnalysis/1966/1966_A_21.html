Associated Clothiers Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax, Calcutta

Supreme Court of India

23 September 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Vishishtha Bhargava, Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 202; AIR 1967 SC 788; [1967] 63 ITR 224; [1967] 1 S.C.R. 512

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 10(2)(vii) - Indian Companies Act, 1913, s. 11(4) - Whether difference between the original cost and the written down value of the property transferred in the circumstances of the case could be deemed to be profits of assessee company u/s. 10(2)(vii)? - New company incorporated with the earlier name of the assessee company took over all the assets and liabilities of the assessee company - Assessee company contended that the transfer was a slump sale and was 'in substance to self' - Agreement for transfer expressly mentioned the consideration of each item of property sold which was not shown to be notional - Held, appellant-company sold the property in the second schedule for a stated consideration which was not shown to be notional, and since the consideration was in excess of the original cost of the building, the difference was profit within the meaning of s. 10(2)(vii), second proviso - Excess of the consideration over original cost was profit u/s. 10(2)(vii), second proviso - Appeal dismissed.
