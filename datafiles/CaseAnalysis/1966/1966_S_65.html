State of Bihar v Roy (S. K.)

Supreme Court of India

25 April 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 12; AIR 1966 SC 1995; 1966 CRLJ 1538; 1966 (13) FLR 111; 1966 (2) LLJ 759; [1966] Supp S.C.R. 259

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Criminal - Labour & Industrial Law - Practice & Procedure - Coal-mines Provident Funds and Bonus Schemes Act, 1948, ss. 2(b)and 2(e) - Mines Act, 1952 - Indian Mines Act, 1923 - Respondent was prosecuted under para. 70 of Coal-Mines Provident Fund Scheme - High Court set aside conviction and sentence imposed on respondent holding that coke-plant owned by respondent was not a coal-mine within meaning of scheme and that coke-plant was not subject to provisions of scheme and respondent was not owner of mine within meaning of Act and scheme - Whether respondent is not owner of a coal-mine within meaning of s. 2(b) and 2(e) of Act? - Indian Mines Act, 1923, has been repealed and substituted by Mines Act, 1952 - Definition should be construed with reference to definition of 'owner' in cl. (1) of s. 2 of Mines Act, 1952 which repealed and re-enacted 1923 Act - Word 'or' occurring before expression 'belonging to a coal-mine' in main definition has to be read to mean 'and' - Expression 'belonging to a coal-mine' is controlling expression governing all aspects of activities of coal-mine within definition of s. 2(b) and all subsidiary things such as works, machinery, tramways and sidings are brought within definition of 'coal mine' only if they appertain to coal-mine, that is to say, if they are under same ownership - Held, change in language of s. 2(b) of earlier Act was not meant to bring about a change of law in this respect but was meant to fix a proper interpretation upon earlier Act - It is a well-recognized principle in dealing with matters of construction that subsequent legislation may be looked at in order to see what is proper interpretation to be put upon earlier Act where earlier Act is obscure or ambiguous or readily capable or more than one interpretation - Further held, respondent is not owner of a coal-mine within meaning of s. 2(b) of Act - HC rightly acquitted respondent of offences alleged against him under scheme - Appeal dismissed.
