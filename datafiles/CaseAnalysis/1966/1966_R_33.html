Chief Conservator of Forests and Others v Rattan Singh

Supreme Court of India

07 April 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, S.M. Sikri

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 103; AIR 1967 SC 166; 1966 MahLJ 905; [1966] Supp S.C.R. 158

Case Digest

Subject: Environment, Wildlife & Animal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Forest Contract Rules

Summary: Environment, Wildlife & Animal - Indian Forest Act, 1927, s. 20 - Forest Contract Rules, rr. 15(1)and 30(1) - Under a contract, respondent was granted right to forest produce in Saiura Borgain Reserved Forest - Divisional Forest Officer held an enquiry in respect of certain breaches committed by respondent of terms of contract and directed respondent in exercise of authority u/r. 15(1) to pay compensation for damage done in reserved forest and also imposed penalty u/r. 30(1) - Petition filed by respondent was granted - Present appeal - Held, where a dispute arises whether there has been a breach of any of terms of contract, it is for reasons already stated, to be determined by Chief Conservator of Forests - That has admittedly not been done - Divisional Forest Officer is not invested with authority to determine whether damage was done by Contractor - That is a matter which must be determined in a reference u/cl. 9 of contract - Further held, order passed by Divisional Forest Officer imposing liability for compensation for damage done by illegal fellings cannot be sustained - Second part of order imposing penalty u/r. 30(1) also suffers from same infirmity - Appeal dismissed.
