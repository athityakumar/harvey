Bakhshish Singh Dhaliwal v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

31 August 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

Vishishtha Bhargava, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, Raghubar Dayal

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 67; AIR 1967 SC 752; 1967 CRLJ 656; [1967] 1 S.C.R. 211

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: War Diaries, Code of Criminal Procedure 1898, Government of India Act 1935

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 420 - Contract - Claim - Forged documents - Conviction - Justifiability - In 1942, Govt. of Burma and Allied Forces were forced to leave Burma because of Japanese invasion - Govt. Of Burma had to execute certain construction works, some of them were executed by themselves and rest were given to contractors - As no exact records were available for works done by various contractors, it notified inviting claims from contractors who had executed works or supplied materials - Appellant submitted claims for various works out of which amount had been paid for few claims - Because of suspicion enquiry had been held and found that claims were false and bogus - Appellant was prosecuted for 10 different charges - Special Tribunal convicted appellant of all charges - Aggrieved appellant appealed before HC which upheld conviction in respect of 3 charges only - Hence, instant appeals by appellant against conviction and appeal by respondent against acquittal - Whether acquittal and conviction u/s. 420 of IPC by HC was sustainable - Held, sentence of substantive imprisonment awarded by Tribunal had already been very substantially reduced by HC and unable to find any justification for interference with it - Further, HC proceeded on amount paid to appellant in respect of bogus claim and overlooked fact that payment had actually made only to extent of 50 per cent of claim verified - Hence, compulsory fine imposed by HC was reduced to extent which had already paid and prosecution had failed to prove beyond doubt for appeals filed by State and does not warrant any interference - Appeals dismissed.
