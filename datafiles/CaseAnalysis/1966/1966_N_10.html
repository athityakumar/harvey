 Nathia Agarwalla and Another v Musst. Jahanara Begum and Others

Supreme Court of India

15 March 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, S.M. Sikri, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 342; AIR 1967 SC 92; [1966] 3 S.C.R. 926

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Assam Non-Agricultural Urban Areas Tenancy Act, 1955 ss. 5 (1) (a) (b), 6 - Decree - Execution petition - Appeal - Maintainability - Respondents predecessor-in -interest in suit land and was leased to appellants against whom decree was passed for ejectment - Respondents filed for execution of decree against appellants before Subordinate Judge - During pendency of execution petition Assam Non-Agricultural Urban Areas Tenancy Act, 1955 came into force and appellants claimed benefit u/s. 5 of the Act, which was decreed in favour of respondents - Aggrieved appellants filed appeal before H C which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether provisions of s. 5(1)(a) of the Act apply to pending execution proceedings - Held, sub-section of the Act did not speak of ejectment decree, but right of landlord to eject his tenant - It begins by stating 'notwithstanding anything in any contract or in any law for time being in force' but it did not include decrees for ejectment already obtained, in non-obstante clause operative parts of sub-section protect tenants under two circumstances if tenant effects improvements on land which he was not entitled to effect, landlord should not eject him unless respondent/tenant pays reasonable compensation - Therefore s. 5 (1) (b) of the Act was intended to operate on rights of landlord which were being enforced by suit but not on rights already enforced and determined - Further, respondent/tenant might not be ejected except for non-payment of rent - Thus, there was nothing to distinguish cl.(a) from cl.(b) in so far as execution of decrees already granted - Therefore, enactments, which were quite explicit, show that where Assam Legislature wished it included execution proceedings within protection and if execution proceedings were to be included they need to be mentioned - Hence, Language chosen places right under an embargo but did not say that decrees already won would become un executable thus stating clearly that they were not to be affected - Appeal dismissed.
