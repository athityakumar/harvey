Maharaja Pratap Singh Bahadur v Thakur Manmohan Deo and Others

Supreme Court of India

28 February 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 353; AIR 1966 SC 1931; [1966] 3 S.C.R. 663

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property

Summary: (A) Land & Property - Administrative - Bengal Ghatwali Lands Act, 1859, s. 9 - Court of Wards Act, 1870 - Deputy Commissioner on behalf of Court of Wards leased some land to defendant No. 2 - Defendant No. 2 enjoyed possession of leased land as well as adjacent suit land - Plaintiff filed suit for recovery of suit land contending that lease was void as it was not sanctioned by Board of Revenue (BoR) - Trial Court declared that lease in question was void and plaintiff was entitled to suit land - HC confirmed Trial Court decree - Hence, instant appeal - Whether condition of sanction of BoR imposed by 1859 Act would prevail over conditions stipulated by 1870 Act - Held, 1859 Act was a special statute and 1870 Act was a general statute - Special statute did not make BoR sanction a pre-condition for validity of lease executed by a Court of Wards so as to bind future possessors of said land - However, s. 9 of 1859 Act imposed such a condition - General Act in regard to leases of Ghatwali lands should yield to special Act - Condition for validity of lease in question was that it should have been executed by Court of Wards for the purpose of erection of dwelling houses - Lease in question expressly stated that lease was granted for erecting dwelling houses - Hence, no reason why it was not special provisions made under 1859 Act in regard to the conditions imposed for validity of such a lease should prevail over those imposed under 1870 Act - Appeal Partly allowed.

(B) Land & Property - Administrative - Whether lease in question was valid - Held, land covered by lease was in possession and enjoyment of lessee for about 80 years - Validity of said grant was not questioned all these long years - Even in plaint its validity was not challenged on the ground that sanction of BoR was not given - When lease was granted all statutory requirements were complied with, that is to say BoR gave its sanction - Hence, lease in question was valid and binding on plaintiff - Appeal partly allowed.

(C) Land & Property - Administrative - Whether plaintiff was entitle the recovery of suit land - Held, courts below concurrently held that suit property was not the subject-matter of lease - Title to the property, therefore, clearly vested in plaintiff - Further, lower courts found that the said property was a waste land in regard to which there could be no effective enjoyment - HC, therefore, rightly drew presumption that possession followed title - Decree of HC was modified - Appeal partly allowed.
