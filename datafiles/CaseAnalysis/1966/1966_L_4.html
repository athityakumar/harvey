Lakhan Mahto and Others v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

24 February 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, K. Subba Rao

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 356; AIR 1966 SC 1742; 1966 CRLJ 1349; [1966] 3 S.C.R. 643

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Criminal Procedure Code, ss. 423, 423(1)(b) and 439 - Scope of HC's powers in disposing of an appeal provided u/ss. 423 and 439 - Held, if an order of conviction is challenged by convicted person but order of acquittal is not challenged by State then it is only order of conviction that falls to be considered by Appellate Court and not order of acquittal - In exercising powers conferred by s. 423(1)(b), HC cannot convert order of acquittal into one of conviction and that result can be achieved by adopting procedure prescribed u/s. 439 - Conviction and sentence modified - Appeal dismissed.
