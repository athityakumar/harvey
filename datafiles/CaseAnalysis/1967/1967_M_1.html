M. A. Jabbar v Commissioner of Income Tax, Andhra Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

23 November 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

Vishishtha Bhargava, J.C. Shah, Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 359; AIR 1968 SC 745; [1968] 68 ITR 493; 1968 (2) MLJ(SC) 1; [1968] 2 S.C.R. 413

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Assessment Year

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961 - Whether expenditure for lease money paid to the Govt., in accordance with the lease deed executed to acquire right to quarry sand from the river bed, is of a capital nature or a revenue expenditure? - Held, there is no such reservation of an exclusive right in respect of any land - Expenditure incurred by the assessee was not related to the acquisition of an asset or a right of a permanent character - It was for the specific object of enabling the assessee to remove sand lying loose on the surface which was the stock-in-trade of the business of the assessee, so that the expenditure has to be regarded as revenue expenditure - Appeals allowed.
