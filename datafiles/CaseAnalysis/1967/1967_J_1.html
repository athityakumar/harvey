Custodian of Evacuee Property Punjab and Others v Jafran Begum

Supreme Court of India

20 April 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Vishishtha Bhargava, G.K. Mitter

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 442; AIR 1968 SC 169; 1968 (70) PLR 1; [1967] 3 S.C.R. 736

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Administration of Evacuee Property Act, 1950, ss. 7, 8, 24, 27, 28 and 46 - Suit for dispute relating to evacuee property - Jurisdiction of High Court - (A) Whether civil courts has jurisdiction to entertain suit for evacuee property? - Held, Custodian has to decide whether certain property is or is not evacuee property - Relied on Ram Gopal Reddy v. Addl. Custodian, (1966 Indlaw SC 393) - U/s. 7 Custodian has power to determine what properties are evacuee properties - (B) Whether ss. 28 or 46 bar jurisdiction of HC u/art. 226? - Further held, no - Power u/art. 226 conferred on HC under Constitution - Suit not maintainable in Civil Court and matter will go back to HC - In case where matter had been adjudicated upon in accordance with provisions of Act not be possible for courts to interfere by reason of provisions of s. 46 of Act - Appeal allowed.
