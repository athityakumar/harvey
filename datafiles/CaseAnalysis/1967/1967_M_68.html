Mulraj v Murti Raghonathji Maharaj

Supreme Court of India

02 March 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, R.S. Bachawat, Vishishtha Bhargava

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 506; AIR 1967 SC 1386; 1967 ALJ 593; 1967 BLJR 665; 1967 (2) ILR(All) 412; [1967] 3 S.C.R. 84

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Prohibition Act

Summary: Rent Control - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Prohibition Act - Uttar Pradesh (Temporary) Control of Rent and Eviction Act, 1947 - Whether Magistrate had jurisdiction to grant permission to sue at time there was a stay order? - Held, an order of stay is as much a prohibitory order as an injunction order and unless court to which it is addressed has knowledge of it, it cannot deprive that court of jurisdiction to proceed with execution before it, there is one difference between an order of injunction and an order of stay arising out of fact that an injunction order is usually passed against a party while a stay order is addressed to court as stay order is addressed to court, as soon as court has knowledge of it, it must stay its hand; if it does not do so, it acts illegally but so long as court has no knowledge of stay order it does not lose jurisdiction to deal with execution which it has under CPC - Appeal dismissed.
