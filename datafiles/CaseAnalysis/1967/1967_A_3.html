Commissioner of Income Tax, Gujara v A. Raman and Company

Supreme Court of India

18 July 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, S.M. Sikri, Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 338; AIR 1968 SC 49; 1972 CTR 245; [1968] 67 ITR 11; [1968] 1 S.C.R. 10; 1977 TAXATION 49

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 147(b) - A/ys 1959-60, 1960-61 and 1961-62 - Conditions for re-opening the assessment - Assessee firm consisted of two partners who were managers of their respective Hindu Undivided Families (HUF) - Assessee sold its goods to the aforesaid families and families again sold on their own account - For a/ys 1959-60, 1960-61 and 1961-62 the assessee and the HUF were separately assessed in respect of their incomes - Subsequently, Income Tax Authorities took view that the sale of the goods by the assessee to the families was only a device to divert the profits of the assessee, issued show cause notices as to why the assessments for the years 1959-60, 1960-61 and 1961-62 should not be reopened - Assessee filed writ petition against said order before HC contending that the Income Tax Officer had no jurisdiction to reopen the assessments since the HUF of the two partners and the assessee had submitted 'correct and complete returns of income' - HC quashed those notices and restrained the Income Tax Officer from taking proceedings in pursuance thereof - Hence, present appeal - Whether said show cause notices issued u/s. 147 of 1961 Act was served with proper grounds? - Held. u/s. 147(b) of 1961 Act reason to believe that income chargeable to tax has escaped assessment in consequence of information in the possession of the Income Tax Officer is a condition precedent to the exercise of his jurisdiction to assess or reassess the income of the assessee - If that condition does not exist, steps taken by the Income Tax Officer to assess or reassess the income will be without jurisdiction - In the present case, Income Tax Officer had no reason to believe that any income had escaped assessment as income that could be earned and not earned by the assessee is not any reason - Income which accrues to a assessee is taxable in his hands, income which he could have, but has not earned, is not made taxable as income accrued to him - Order of HC upheld - Appeal dismissed.
