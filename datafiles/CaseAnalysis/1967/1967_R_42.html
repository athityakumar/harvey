Tribhuvandas Purshottamdas Thakur v Ratilal Motilal Patel

Supreme Court of India

05 September 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, S.M. Sikri, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 424; AIR 1968 SC 372; 1968 (70) BomLR 73; 1968 (9) G.L.R. 212; 1968 MLJ 287; [1968] 1 S.C.R. 455

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Trusts & Association - Constitution - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 21, r. 89 - Bombay Public Trusts Act, s. 36 - Respondents trustees of a public trust and trustees mortgaged a house belonging to trust to secure repayment of a certain sum - An action instituted by mortgagee against trustees to enforce mortgage was compromised, and it was decreed that trustees do pay a certain sum - Trustees did not pay installments and in an application for execution by mortgagee mortgaged property was put up for sale and bid of appellant was accepted by executing Court - Trustees applied under O. 21, r. 89 of CPC for setting aside sale - Subordinate Judge passed an order disposing of execution application and directed that a certain sum out of amount deposit, by trustees be paid over to appellant - In appeal District Court reversed order - High Court in exercise of powers u/s. 115 of CPC set aside order of District Court - Whether HC was justified in setting aside order of District Court? - Held, transactions of mortgage, exchange or gift or lease of any immovable property in cls. (a) and (b) contemplated to be made by trustees are voluntary transactions, and in absence of any clear provision in Act, expression 'sale' in cl. (a) would only mean transfer of property by trustees for a price - There is nothing to indicate, either in words of section, or in context in which it occurs, that sale prohibited without sanction of Charity Commr. includes a court sale in execution of a decree - Court have no doubt that Legislature did not intend to put any restriction upon power of Civil Court executing a decree for recovery of money due from trust, by sale of property of trust - Appeal allowed.
