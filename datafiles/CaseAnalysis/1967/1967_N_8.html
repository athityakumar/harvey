 State of Andhra Pradesh and Another v Nalla Raja Reddy and Others

Supreme Court of India

28 February 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, Vishishtha Bhargava, G.K. Mitter, J.C. Shah, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 460; AIR 1967 SC 1458; 1967 (2) ITJ 777; [1967] 3 S.C.R. 28

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Andhra Pradesh Land Revenue (Additional Assessment and Cess Revision Act, 1962

Summary: Land & Property - Andhra Pradesh Land Revenue (Additional Assessment) and Cess Revision Act, 1962 - Andhra Pradesh Land Revenue (Additional Assessment) and Cess Revision (Amendment) Act, 1962, ss. 3 and 4 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Petition filed u/art. 226 of the Constitution - Constitutional validity of the relevant provision of the Act of 1962 - (A) Whether the Act of 1962 id ultra vires art. 14 of the Constitution? - Not only the scheme of classification, has no reasonable relation to the objects sought to be achieved, viz., fixation and rationalisation of rates but the arbitrary power of assessment conferred under the Act enables the appropriate officers to make unreasonable discrimination between different persons and lands - The whole imposition of assessment was left to the arbitrary discretion of the officers not named in the Act without giving any remedy to the assessees for questioning the correctness of any of the important stages in the matter of assessment - Held, the Act of 1962 clearly offends art. 14 of the Constitution - (B) Whether the classification based on ayacut is reasonable in relation to the duration of water supply? - The classification based on ayacut has no reasonable relation to the duration of water supply - It is, therefore, clear that the ayacuts do not correspond to the number of months of water supply, indeed, many tanks which supply water for a longer duration have smaller ayacuts - Held, attempt has been made to classify different systems on the basis of the ayacuts but the said test is unreasonable and has no relation to either the duration of water supply or to the quality or the productivity of the soil - Appeals dismissed.
