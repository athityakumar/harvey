Raghubans Dubey v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

19 January 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri, Mohammad Hidayatullah, C.A. Vaidyialingam

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 472; AIR 1967 SC 1167; 1967 CRLJ 1081; [1967] 2 S.C.R. 423

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Code Of Criminal Procedure, s. 173 - Appellants name was mentioned in First Information Report but during investigation, he set up an alibi which was accepted by police and his name was not mentioned in final report submitted to Magistrate but was mentioned in charge sheet under heading not sent up - During evidence, prosecution witness implicated him as accused - Whether Magistrate has power to summon appellant when his name was not mentioned in report u/s. 173 - Held, yes - Magistrate is entitled to summon additional accused against whom he considers that there was good evidence, after perusal of statements recorded by police u/s. 161 and other documents referred to in s. 173 even without examination of witnesses in court - Appeal dismissed.
