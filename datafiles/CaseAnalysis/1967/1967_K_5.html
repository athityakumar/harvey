Commissioner of Income Tax, Andhrapradesh, Hyderabad v K.Adinarayana Murty

Supreme Court of India

03 April 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 107; AIR 1967 SC 1545; [1967] 65 ITR 607; [1967] 3 S.C.R. 388

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 34 - Notice u/s. 34 of Act - Whether it was competent for ITO to issue second notice dated 12-02-1958 and continue proceedings thereon ignoring return already filed by assessee in pursuance of first notice u/s. 34? - ITO could not have validly acted on return filed by assessee in status of Hindu Undivided Family and assessment made by ITO on such a return would have been invalid in law because notice u/s. 34 had been issued in status of 'individual' and sanction of Commr. for issue of a notice u/s. 34 was also obtained on that basis - Held, ITO was legally justified in ignoring first notice issued u/s. 34 of Act and return filed by assessee in response to that notice and consequently assessment made by ITO in pursuance of second notice issued on 12-02-1958 was a valid assessment - ITO was entitled to ignore return filed by assessee as non est in law - Appeal allowed.
