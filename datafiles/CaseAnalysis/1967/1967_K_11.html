Kamani Metals and Alloys Limited v Their Workmen

Supreme Court of India

24 January 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, S.M. Sikri, C.A. Vaidyialingam

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 132; AIR 1967 SC 1175; 1967 (15) FLR 1; 1967 (2) LLJ 55; [1967] 2 S.C.R. 463

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Fair Wage

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Bombay Industrial Disputes Act, 1938 - Wages Revision - Whether Tribunal in revising wages, pays and dearness allowance has followed wrong principle? - Held, It can hardly be maintained that wages fixed so far back do not need revision, when, commodity prices have soared high, general level of wages has gone up and in some industries there have been two or three revisions already and in some others Wage Boards have been appointed to revise or fix wages - In questions of this type it is first desirable to consider what amount is necessary to maintain and even improve workers standard of living, how wages of workers concerned compare with those paid to workers of similar grade and skill by other employers in similar or other industries in region and what wages establishment or industry can afford to pay - Burden of increased wage bill will not be more than 1/10th of its net profits, to say nothing of some other savings by way of reduction of income-tax - Where number of industries of same kind in a particular region is small, it is region part of industry-cum-region formula which assumes importance - Tribunal took into consideration in deciding this question of adjustment fact that it had been extremely cautious as regards increasing old wage scales - It was fair to give some relief to existing employees by means of such increase by way of adjustment while at same time not burdening employer with higher rates of wages for new incumbents - Tribunal was justified in making such order in view of facts of case - Appeal dismissed.
