 Shri A. C. Aggarwal Sub-Divisional Magistrate, Delhi and Another v Mst. Ram Kali, Etc

Supreme Court of India

16 August 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, R.S. Bachawat, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, G.K. Mitter

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 247; AIR 1968 SC 1; 1968 CRLJ 82; 1968 (70) PLR 261; [1968] 1 S.C.R. 205

Case Digest

Subject: Socio-Economic; Women & Children

Keywords: Brothel, Prostitution

Summary: Criminal - Women & Children - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art.14 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898, s. 432 - Suppression of Immoral Traffic in Women and Girls Act, 1956, s. 18 - Immoral trafficking - Cognizance - Appellant no.1/Sub-Divisional Magistrate issued notices to respondents to show cause why premises occupied by them should not be attached under the Act - Those notices were issued on basis of police reports that those premises were being used as brothels - Respondents challenged validity of s.18 of the Act - They moved magistrate to refer question as to validity of s. 18 to HC u/s. 432 of CrPC - Magistrate rejected said prayer - Respondents filed writ petitions before HC challenging vires of s.18 of the Act - HC allowed said petitions by common order and quashed the notices issued to respondents - Hence instant appeals - Whether s.18 of the Act was ultra vires art. 14 of the Constitution - Held, it was clear that they disclose offences u/s.3 against respondents - Therefore, question was whether magistrate could choose to ignore cognizable offence complained of and merely have recourse to s.18 and thus deprive the parties proceeded against of benefit of a regular trial as well as right of appeal in the event of their conviction - In cases like these, the magistrate who was also a court as provided in s.22 should at the first instance proceed against persons complained against under penal provisions in ss.3 or 7 as the case might be, and only after disposal of those cases take action u/s.18 if there was occasion for it - U/s.190(1) (b) of CrPC, Magistrate was bound to take cognizance of any cognizable offence brought to his notice - Magistrate had no discretion in the matter, otherwise that section would be violative of art. 14 of Constitution - There was no difficulty for the magistrate to take cognizance of those cases - Otherwise it was open to him to direct fresh investigations by competent police officers before deciding whether facts placed before him disclose any cognizable offence - Hence, proceedings taken by Magistrate against respondents were not in accordance with law as he had proceeded against them u/s. 18 without first taking action u/s. 3 - SC uphold conclusions reached by HC but on grounds other than those relied on by them - However, these conclusion did not debar Magistrate from taking fresh proceedings against respondents in accordance with law - Appeals dismissed.
