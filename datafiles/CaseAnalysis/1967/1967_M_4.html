M. Gopala Krishna Naidu v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

24 August 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Shelat, S.M. Sikri, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 388; AIR 1968 SC 240; 1968 (16) FLR 432; 1968 LabIC 216; 1968 (2) LLJ 125; 1968 MahLJ 48; 1968 MPLJ 49; [1968] 1 S.C.R. 355; 1967 SLR 800

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Service - Fundamental Rules, rr. 54(1) and (2) - Suspension - Validity - Whether there was a duty on competent authority to afford an opportunity to appellant to show cause before that whether he was fully exonerated and whether his suspension was wholly unjustified? - U/r. 54 where a Govt. servant is reinstated, authority has to consider and make a specific order (i) regarding pay and allowances payable to him for period of his absence from duty and (ii) whether such period of absence should be treated as one spent on duty - Authority has power in suitable cases to direct that such period of absence shall be treated as period spent on duty where Govt. servant would be entitled to full pay and allowances - Held, r. 54 contemplates a duty to act in accordance with basic concept of justice, fair play - Authority therefore had to afford a reasonable opportunity to appellant to show cause why cls. 3 and 5 should not be applied and that having not been done order must be held to be invalid - Appeal allowed.
