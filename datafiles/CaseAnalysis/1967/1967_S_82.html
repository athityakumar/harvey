 Krishna Kumar Narula Etc v State of Jammu and Kashmir and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 March 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, S.M. Sikri, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, C.A. Vaidyialingam, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 507; AIR 1967 SC 1368; [1967] 3 S.C.R. 50

Case Digest

Subject: Excise

Keywords: Excise Regulation, Excess Profits Tax Act, 1940, Prohibition Act

Summary: Excise - Constitution of India, 1950 art. 19 - Excess Profits Tax Act, 1940 - Excise Act, 1958 - Whether appellants had a fundamental right to do business in liquor? - A combined reading of cl.(1) and (6) of art. 19 makes it clear that a citizen has a fundamental right to carry on any trade or business, and State can make a law imposing reasonable restrictions on said right in interests of general public - Held, dealing in liquor is business and a citizen has a right to do business in that commodity, but State can make a law imposing reasonable restrictions on said right, in public interests - Appeal dismissed.
