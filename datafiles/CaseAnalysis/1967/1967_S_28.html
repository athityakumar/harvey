 Shah and Company, Bombay v State of Maharashtra and Another

Supreme Court of India

06 April 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

C.A. Vaidyialingam, K. Subba Rao, Mohammad Hidayatullah, R.S. Bachawat, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 125; AIR 1967 SC 1877; 1968 (70) BomLR 119; [1967] 3 S.C.R. 466

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Bombay Land Requisition Act, 1948, s. 6(4)(a) - Bombay Rents, Hotel and Lodging House Rates Control Act, 1947, s. 15(1) - Constitution Of India, 1953, art. 19 - (A) Whether Rent Act and Requisition Act can be considered to be in pari materia? - Rent Act deals substantially with relationship of landlord and tenant, in matter of eviction, payment of rent, increase of rent under certain circumstances and circumstances under which landlord can get possession of property whereas the Requistion Act relates to circumstances under which requisition of land can be made for a public purpose and procedure to be adopted for same, as well as payment of compensationl - Requistion Act deals with a matter so totally different from that dealt with by Rent Act and there is absolutely no similarity between two enactments - Held, Requisition Act does not relate to same person or thing, or to same class of persons or things, as Rent Act, hence two Acts cannot be considered to be in pari materia - (B) Whether assignment of 18-08-1964 in petitioners' favour is protected by cls. (2) of Notification D/- 18-08-1964 and there is no vacancy of premises, so as to give jurisdiction to authorises to pass requisition order? - S. 6 of Requisition Act gives power to State Govt. to requisition vacant premises and under explanation to s. 6, there will be deemed to be a vacancy if a tenant assigns or transfers, in any manner, his interest in premises - S. 15 of Rent Act cannot be read into any part of Requisition Act, much less with s. 6, u/s. 6, notwithstanding fact that an assignment of tenancy rights may have been made which is permissible under Rent Act, such an assignment can be deemed to create a vacancy of premises, so as to give jurisdiction to State Govt. to requisition same - Further held, having regard to object underlying cl. 2 of Notification, assignment claimed by petitioners must be regarded only as a colourable device for really obtaining a transfer of tenancy rights which is otherwise prohibited by s. 15(1) of Rent Act; transaction in question is not saved by cl. 2 of Notification and petitioners cannot claim any rights on basis of assignment deed, either in respect of tenancy rights, or to carry on any business there - (C) Whether provisions of Requisition Act infringe petitioners' fundamental right u/arts. 19(1), (f) and (g) of Constitution? - Further held, Requisition Act deals only with property and does not deal with trade, or business as such, and hence, constitutionality of that Act, having regard to art. 19(1)(g) does not arise for consideration - Further since petitioners cannot claim any rights on basis of assignment deed, hence, they cannot complain that any fundamental rights u/art. 19(1), (f) or (g) have been infringed - Petition dismissed.
