 State of Uttar Pradesh v Madan Mohan Nagar

Supreme Court of India

05 January 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, K. Subba Rao, J.C. Shah, C.A. Vaidyialingam

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 156; AIR 1967 SC 1260; 1967 (14) FLR 262; 1967 (1) ILR(All) 423; 1967 (2) LLJ 63; [1967] 2 S.C.R. 333; 1967 SLR 147

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Service - Civil Service Regulations, art. 465 A - Respondent filed Writ Petition in High Court for quashing order of compulsory retirement against him - Reason for compulsory retirement was that respondent had outlived his utility - Contended that this does not lead to conclusion that order amounts to dismissal or removal because in every case of compulsory retirement it is implied that person who is compulsorily retired had outlived his usefulness - Held, order clearly attaches a stigma to respondent and any person who reads order would consider that there is something wrong with him or his capacity to work - Attaching of stigma expressly makes position worse than mere implication that public servant has outlived his utility - Appeal dismissed.
