Lakshmi Rattan Engineering Works Limited v Assistant Commissioner Sales Tax, Kanpur and Another

Supreme Court of India

12 September 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, Vishishtha Bhargava, C.A. Vaidyialingam

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 422; AIR 1968 SC 488; 1968 (2) SCJ 1; [1968] 1 S.C.R. 505; 1968 (21) STC 154

Case Digest

Subject: Parliament & Legislature; Sales Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Parliament & Legislature - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Uttar Pradesh Sales Tax Act, 1948 - U.P. Sales tax Rules, 1948 - Turnover - Sales tax - Condonation of delay - Appeal - Appellant appealed to the Assistant Commissioner (Judicial) 1, Sales tax, Kanpur Range, Kanpur for assessing different turnover by Sales tax authorities as compare to actual turnover declared by him - Appeal was filed within time - Appellant was required u/s. 9 of the 1948 Act to give satisfactory proof, at time of entertainment of appeal, that tax was duly paid - He do not present proof of deposit at time of filing appeal that the balance of tax had been paid - Afterwards, appellant addressed a letter to Sales tax Officer and asked for a certificate of payment of tax and certificate had been furnished - He filed it before Assistant Commissioner and also filed an application for condonation of delay u/s. 9(6) of the 1948 Act r/w s. 5 of 1908 Act - Appeal of the assesses was rejected as according to Assistant Commissioner s. 9 of the 1948 Act r/w r. 66(2) of the 1948 Rules had not been complied with since no proof had been given along with memorandum of appeal that tax had been paid - Application for condonation of delay was also dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether grant of special leave appeal was justified - Held, matter was more easily disposed of by giving special leave in HC and ends of justice would be better served by avoiding a circuity of action and by dealing directly - Hence, appeal should be allowed and should be remitted to Assistant Commissioner (Judicial) 1, Sales Tax, Kanpur Range, Kanpur, for disposal in accordance with law - Appeal allowed.
