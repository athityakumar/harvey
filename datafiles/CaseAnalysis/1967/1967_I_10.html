 T.M. Kanniyan v Income Tax Officer, Pondicherry and Another

Supreme Court of India

30 October 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Bachawat, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, G.K. Mitter, K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 186; AIR 1968 SC 637; 1968 (1) ITJ 466; [1968] 68 ITR 244; [1968] 2 S.C.R. 103

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Pondicherry (Administration) Act, 1962, Constitution (Fourteenth Amendment) Act, 1962

Summary: Constitution - Taxation Laws (Extension to Union Territories) Regulation No. 3, 1963 - Challenge vires of Taxation Laws (Extension to Union Territories) Regulation No. 3, 1963 - Contention is that President had no power to promulgate Regulation u/art. 240 of Constitution - Regulation No. 3 of 1963 was made by President in exercise of power conferred on him to make regulations for peace, progress and good government of Union territories - Art. 240 of Constitution confers on President a general power of making regulations for peace, progress and good government of specified Union territories - In exercise of this power, President may make a regulation repealing or amending any Act made by Parliament or any existing law which is for time being applicable to Union territory - Regulation when promulgated by President has stone force and effect as an Act of Parliament which applies to that territory - President can thus make regulations on all subjects on which Parliament can make laws for territory - Parliament has plenary power to legislate for Union territories with regard to any subject - By express words of art. 240, President can make regulations for peace, progress and good govt. of specified Union territories - Proviso to art. 240(1) on its true construction does not fetter power of President to make regulations for any of Union territories specified in art. 240(1) including Pondicherry as long as no Legislature is created for territory - Held, no force in contention that President cannot make a law with respect to income-tax in absence of an express grant of such a power - For Union territories, Parliament has plenary powers to make laws and President has general powers to make regulations - In exercise of his powers u/art. 240, President could make Regulation No. 3 of 1963 extending Income-tax Act, 1961 and other laws to Union territories - Petitions dismissed.
