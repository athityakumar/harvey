State of Assam and Another v Bharat Kala Bhandar Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 April 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Vishishtha Bhargava, G.K. Mitter

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 446; AIR 1967 SC 1766; 1968 (16) FLR 96; 1968 (1) LLJ 25; [1967] 3 S.C.R. 490

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Defence & Security Forces

Keywords: Defence of India Rules, 1962, Defence of India Act, 1962

Summary: Defence & Security Forces - Defence of India Rules, 1962 - Notification - Ultra vires - Governor of Assam issued a notification u/r. 126-AA of the Rules - He applied r. 126-AA to a large number of employments - Another notification was issued u/r. 126-AA(4) - Validity of these three notifications was challenged by W.P. before the HC by the respondents - HC held that the conditions precedent to the exercise of the power conferred by r. 126-AA had not been complied with and therefore the notification under sub-rule (1) was bad - HC also held that the exercise of power under sub r. (1) was colourable as it was not shown that the employments mentioned in the notification under sub-r. (1) were essential for securing public safety and thus one of the purposes mentioned in the notification was non existent - HC held that the notification under sub-r. (1) was mala fide in law - Notifications under sub-r. (4) the HC held that they were invalid - HC held that the notifications under sub-r. (4) were also mala fide - HC struck down the notification u/sub-r. (1) and the two notifications u/sub-r. (4) of r. 126-AA - Hence these appeals - Whether the power under sub-r. (4) was a power which can be exercised merely on the subjective opinion of Govt. or sub-r. (4) requires anything more - Held, power conferred by sub-r. (4) is consequential to the issue of a notification u/sub-r. (1) - Except notification u/sub-r. (1) the other two notifications must be struck down - Appeals allowed.
