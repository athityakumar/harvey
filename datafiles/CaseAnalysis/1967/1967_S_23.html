Andhra Prabha Limited and Others v Secretary, Madras Union of Journalists and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 May 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

G.K. Mitter, Kailas Nath Wanchoo

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 229; AIR 1967 SC 1869; 1967 (15) FLR 435; 1968 (1) LLJ 15; 1968 (1) MLJ(SC) 100; [1967] 3 S.C.R. 901

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Closure of company, Payment of wages

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 10(3) and 25F - Closure of company - Settlement - Payment of wages - Entitled to - Respondent company gave notice to appellants (workmen and working journalists) that it was going, to close down its business at M w.e.f 1-12-1958 - However, settlement was arrived at between respondent and appellants in the presence of Labour Commissioner - In view of settlement Management would withdraw notice of closure and announce same on notice board - Further, settlement was to be operative for 2 1/2 years - Appellants had notified that Management had decided to close with immediate effect undertaking and publication of all 7 newspapers at M and to dispense with services of appellants - Notice was also given that they should be paid their wages for period during which they had worked, besides one month's salary in lieu of notice prescribed u/s. 25F of the Act - Govt. issued a notice u/s. 10(3) of the Act prohibiting the continuance of strike, and lockout - Adjudication was taken up by Special Industrial Tribunal which some witnesses including 'R' were examined and a large number of documents tendered in evidence - Tribunal scrutinised evidence both oral and documentary and observed that it was not satisfied that 'R' had given any verbal assurance imputed to him - Tribunal further held that workers should not be entitled to any relief because of transfer of 2 publications of 3 appeals - Hence, instant appeal - Whether Tribunal was justified in passing the impugned order - Held, Tribunal held that 'it was only M company and V company that relied upon support of parent company after May 1959 for printing and publishing papers acquired by them' - Tribunal inferred that suspension of business was a lockout at inception and became a genuine closure only in October-November, 1959 - It had very valuable property on its hands after April 1959 and some persons had to be retained in service to look after property - Fact that one of them was a reporter could not lead to inference that company did not close down its business but could take it up whenever it wanted to - Further, failure to inform Provident Fund authorities was an omission but that could not mean that workers continued to be in service of company or were meant to be taken back into its service as soon as they became submissive to R - Evidence on record shows that R plan was not to give up business of newspaper publications altogether but he wanted to distribute his business to different places - Whatever might be motive behind such plan, he had only carried out that plan into effect after publication of award and it could not lead us to conclusion that closure was an assumed one - Thus, strike was not justified and Management was entitled to close undertaking on 29-4-1959 - Impugned order passed by Tribunal was set aside - Appeal allowed.
