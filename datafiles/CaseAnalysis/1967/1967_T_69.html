Management of Bangalore Woollen, Cotton and Silk Mills Company v Workmen and Another

Supreme Court of India

18 September 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

C.A. Vaidyialingam, Mohammad Hidayatullah, Vishishtha Bhargava

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 212; AIR 1968 SC 585; 1968 (16) FLR 242; 1968 LabIC 558; 1968 (1) LLJ 555; [1968] 1 S.C.R. 581

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Sick, Casual Leave, Privilege Leave, Industrial Disputes (Amendment And Miscellaneous Provisions) Act, 1956, Industrial Employment (Standing Order ) Act, 1946

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes (Amendment and Miscellaneous Provisions) Act, 1956 - Uttar Pradesh Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Industrial Employment (Standing Orders) Act, 1946 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 19 - (A) Whether notice, contemplated u/s. 19(6) of Act, should be in writing or can it be oral, and have expressed concurrent view that such notice can be oral also? - High Court had proceeded on basis that decision of this Court, in Workmen of Western India Match Co Ltd v Western India Match Co. Ltd. (1962 Indlaw SC 91), supports proposition that an inference of an intention to terminate an award or a settlement, can be gathered from various correspondence that passed, between Management and Union - That decision does not lend any support to such a view unless settlement is terminated - Union had no right to make any demands regarding leave facilities, as it has purported to do in June - This letter cannot be considered to be a notice, inasmuch as it has been given, even before settlement was terminated - (B) Whether Tribunal has jurisdiction under Industrial Disputes Act, to adjudicate upon a dispute, which may result in modification of standing orders framed by management under Standing Orders Act? - There is no warrant for holding that merely because Standing Orders Act is a self-contained statute, with regards to matters mentioned therein, jurisdiction of Industrial Tribunal, under Act, to adjudicate upon matters, covered by standing orders, has been, in any manner, abridged or taken away - Held, lack of proper notice does away with jurisdiction of Tribunal - Appeal partly allowed.
