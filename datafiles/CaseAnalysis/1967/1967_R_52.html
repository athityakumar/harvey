Municipal Corporation of Greater Bombay v Royal Western India Turf Club

Supreme Court of India

13 September 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Shelat, J.C. Shah, S.M. Sikri

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 496; AIR 1968 SC 425; 1968 (70) BomLR 810; [1968] 1 S.C.R. 525

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Property Tax Act, 1893 - Lease - Expenses disallowed - Net rateable value - Property tax - corporation had given land on lease to appellant-club for thirty years - Accounts of the appellant showed gross receipts of Rs. 124 lacs of expenses and loss of Rs. 7 lacs - Municipal Commissioner disallowed expenses of Rs. 22 lacs determined Rs. 13,22,430 as the gross annual rent and deducting therefrom the 10 percent deduction allowable u/s. 154 of 1893 Act of the Act assessed the net rateable value at Rs, 11,90,187 - Appellant was not satisfied with the deductions allowed by HC hence, filed an appeal against the order of HC - Whether, the decision of HC was justified - Held, the net rateable value under s. 154(1) of 1893 Act was what a hypothetical tenant i.e appellant would pay as rent and that would depend upon the amount of profits earned from race-meetings held on the race-course - To arrive at the correct amount of such profit all expenses reasonably and properly incurred which go to the making of the receipts had to be deducted from the gross receipts - There was no challenge at any stage that expenses were not properly incurred for the upkeep and maintenance of the race course hence, HC had rightly allowed the deduction of those expenses - Net rateable value as calculated by the HC amounted to Rs. 1,94,175 but the rateable value need not be equal to the actual rent, appellant was expected to pay for a lease from year to year on the property as it existed with all its privileges, advantages and burdens - Decision of HC was correct - Appeal dismissed.
