Employers of Firestone Tyre and Rubber Company Limited v Their Workmen

Supreme Court of India

22 August 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, C.A. Vaidyialingam

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 199; AIR 1968 SC 236; 1967 (15) FLR 462; 1968 LabIC 212; 1967 (2) LLJ 715; 1968 (2) MLJ(SC) 29; [1968] 1 S.C.R. 307

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Enquiry - Validity of - Whether enquiry was opposed to principles of natural justice and conclusive? - In certain cases it may even be fair to delinquent to take his version first so that enquiry may cover point of difference and witnesses may be questioned properly on aspect of case suggested by him - In the present case Subramaniam had complained earlier that his version ought to have been elicited first before enquiry against him was ordered - This is exactly what was done by enquiring officer - Officer asked respondent again and again whether he was defending himself properly or not and he always expressed his satisfaction - Held, enquiry did not offend any principles of natural justice and was proper law - Appeal allowed.
