State of Andhra Pradesh v K. Satyanarayana and Others

Supreme Court of India

22 November 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, C.A. Vaidyialingam

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 192; AIR 1968 SC 825; 1968 (2) AnWR 50; 1968 CRLJ 1009; 1968 (2) MLJ(SC) 50; [1968] 2 S.C.R. 387

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Media & Entertainment; Socio-Economic

Keywords: Game, Hyderabad Gambling Act (2 of 1305 F)

Summary: Socio-Economic - Public Gambling Act, 1867, ss. 4, 5 and 7 - Hyderabad Gambling Act (2 of 1305 F) - (A) What constitutes definition of 'common gambling place'? - Definition of 'common gambling place' is dependent not merely on structure within which gambling is alleged to have taken place but is decided on evidences which prove that there was gambling going on and that club benefited from same - A club cannot be brought under definition merely because it provides certain facilities and that card games are also played by members and non-members or because they make payments for services rendered - Further there should be credible evidence in account books of club that they have profited from same - (B) Whether game 'rummy' is a game of skill or pure chance? - Held, in fact in all games in which cards are shuffled and dealt out, there is an element of chance, because distribution of cards is not according to any set pattern but is dependent upon how cards find their place in shuffled pack - From this alone it cannot be said that Rummy is a game of chance and there is, no skill involved in it - Appeal dismissed.
