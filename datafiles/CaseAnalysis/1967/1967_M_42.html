State of Punjab and Others v Messrs Tara Chand Lajpat Rai

Supreme Court of India

28 February 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Shelat, G.K. Mitter

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 143; AIR 1967 SC 1408; [1967] 3 S.C.R. 10; 1967 (19) STC 493

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Sales Tax

Summary: Indirect Tax - Sales Tax - Punjab General Sales Tax Act, 1948, ss. 10, 10(3), 11, 11(1), 11(2), 11(3), 11(4) and 11(5) - Punjab General Sales Tax Rules, 1949, rr. 17, 18, 19, 20, 32(3) and 33 -(A) Whether an assessment order is time barred and without jurisdiction where sales tax authority is not satisfied with returns filed by a registered dealer and issues a notice under s. 11(2) of Punjab General Sales Tax Act, 1948 before expiry of three years from termination of period for furnishing returns but finalizes assessment order after three years from aforesaid date? - Held, in the case of a registered dealer the proceeding before Commr. start factually when a return is made or a notice is issued and no question of limitation would arise where such proceedings are taken before expiry of prescribed period though an assessment order is finalised after expiry of such period - A clear authority for proposition that assessment proceedings commence in case of a registered dealer either taken when he furnishes a return or when a notice is issued to him under s. 11(2) of the present Act and that if such proceeding are taken within prescribed time though assessment is finalised subsequently even after expiry of prescribed period, no question of limitation would arise - Dealer filed returns - Assessment proceedings under Punjab General Sales Tax Act commence in case of a registered dealer either when he furnishes a return or when a notice is issued to him under s. 11(2) of Act - If such proceedings are taken within prescribed time of three years though assessment is finalised subsequently even after expiry of prescribed period, no question of limitation would arise - (B) Whether claim by assessing officer that he made order on basis of best judgment assessment should be treated as conclusive? - Further held, Assessing Authority stating that be made order on best judgment basis - Such statement not conclusive, because merely by calling order as best judgment assessment, it does not become one - Appeal allowed.
