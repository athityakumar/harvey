National Engineering Industries Limited v Hanuman

Supreme Court of India

25 July 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, G.K. Mitter

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 241; AIR 1968 SC 33; 1967 (15) FLR 259; 1968 LabIC 3; 1967 (2) LLJ 883; [1968] 1 S.C.R. 54

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Practice & Procedure - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 33-A - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Industrial Disputes (Appellate Tribunal) Act, 1950, s. 23 - Employees State Insurance Act, 1948, s. 73 - Whether termination of services of respondent was valid and justified? - Standing Order in inartistically worded, but when standing order provides that a workman will lose his lien on his appointment in case he does not join his duty within eight days of expiry of his leave, it obviously means that his services are automatically terminated on happening of contingency - Held, in present case respondent had been totally unable by evidence produced by him to establish that his absence beyond period of leave originally granted was due to continued illness and therefore finding of Labour Court in his favour in this respect was perverse - Further held, where a workman's service terminates automatically under standing order s. 33 would not apply and so an application u/s. 33A would not be maintainable, as there is no question in such a case of contravention of s. 33 of Act - Appeal allowed.
