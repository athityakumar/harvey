The Vishnu Pratap Sugar Works (P) Ltd. v The Chief Inspector Of Stamps, U.P.

Supreme Court of India

04 May 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Shelat, R.S. Bachawat, Vishishtha Bhargava

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 230; AIR 1968 SC 102; [1967] 3 S.C.R. 920

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Court Fees Act, ss.7(iv)(a), 7(iv-A) and 7(iv-B)(b) - Uttar Pradesh Sugarcane (Regulation of Supply, Purchase) Act, 1953 - Sugarcane Cess Act, 1956 - Uttar Pradesh Sugar Cane Cess (Validation) Act, 1961 - Uttar Pradesh Sugar Cane Purchase Tax Act, 1961 - Whether an Act passed by Central or State Legislature can be called an 'instrument' and, if so, an 'instrument securing money or other property having such value'? - Held, ordinarily a statute is not an instrument unless definition includes it or statute creates a settlement and as such statute treated as an instrument - It would be incorrect to say that impugned Acts are instruments within meaning of s.7(iv-A) - It is not necessary to decide whether Acts are instruments securing money or other property having such value - Further held, for purposes of Act, it is substance and not form which has to be considered while deciding applicable provision - But if merely because validity of impugned act is called into question, a prayer for injunction is treated as one for declaratory decree of which consequential relief is injunction, all suits where injunction is prayed for would have to be treated as falling u/s. 7(iv)(a) - Appeal allowed.
