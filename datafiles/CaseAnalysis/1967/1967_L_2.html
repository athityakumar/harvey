L. M. Chhabda and Sons v Commissioner of Income Tax, Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

21 March 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, S.M. Sikri, Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 295; [1967] 65 ITR 638

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Vocation

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s.10(1) - Expiry of lease -Ejectment - Payment of mesne profits - Assessment - Different ventures - Appellants were lessees of cinema theatre and carried on business of exhibiting cinematograph films - After expiry of lease landlord filed an action in civil court in ejectment against appellants and obtained decree for possession of theatre - An order for payment of mesne profits - Civil Court determined liability of appellants to pay mesne profits at Rs.2,57,963 - In appeal claim of landlord for mesne profits was settled by consent and the amount was reduced to Rs.1,90,220 - In proceedings for assessment of income for assessment year 1955-56 appellants claimed Rs.92,240 out of amount of mesne profits as permissible allowance in computation of their business income - Income-tax Officer disallowed claim holding that business of P was not carried on by appellants during previous year relevant to year of assessment - Assessee carrying on business ventures of same character at different places it must be held as a matter of law that ventures are parts of single business - Whether different ventures carried on by an individual or company form same business or different businesses - Held, cinema theatres acquired by appellants from time to time on lease or otherwise were run independently of one another with separate identifiable books and that opening of a new theatre or closure of another did not affect working of remaining theatres - Appellants were conducting cinema theatres and result of accounts of different ventures was entered in accounts maintained at head office - But from that circumstance no inference necessarily arises that exhibition of films in different theatres constituted the same business - It was for the appellants to establish that different ventures constitute parts of the same business - No evidence about unity of control and management, or inter-relation of the business, or employment of same staff to run business, or possibility of one theatre being closed without affecting rest of business - Appeal dismissed.
