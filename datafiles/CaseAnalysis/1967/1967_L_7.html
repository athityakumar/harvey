Lallu Yeshwant Singh v Rao Jagdish Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

29 November 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 486; AIR 1968 SC 620; 1968 MLJ 496; [1968] 2 S.C.R. 203

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Land & Property - Board of Revenue Act, 1913 - Qanoon Ryotwari, ss. 82(3),137, 163 and 326 - Specific Relief Acts, s. 9 - Whether appellant is entitled to recover possession of property if he is dispossessed from prior juridical possession, on ground tenancy is alleged to have been extinguished? - Tenant who has ceased to be a tenant may sue for possession against his landlord if landlord deprives him of possession otherwise than in due course of law, but a trespasser who has been thrown out of possession cannot go to Court - Held, if reasoning of HC is accepted to be correct, proviso would become a dead-letter and in every case where there are arrears of land revenue, landlord would take possession forcibly without trying to recover land revenue - Possession of a tenant who has ceased to be a tenant is protected by law, though he may not have a right to continue in possession after termination of tenancy his possession is juridical and that possession is protected by statute - Further held, HC erred in quashing order of Board of Revenue - Appeal allowed.
