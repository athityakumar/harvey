Additional Tahsildar, Raipur, and Others v Gendalal

Supreme Court of India

12 April 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, J.C. Shah, S.M. Sikri

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 98; 1968 MahLJ 250; 1968 MPLJ 165; 1968 (21) STC 263

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Sales Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Civil Procedure - Punjab General Sales Tax Act, 1948 - East Punjab General Sales Tax Act, 1948 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Central Provinces and Berar Sales Tax Act, 1947 - (A) Whether since there was dissolution of partnership on 13-09-1952, and when firm had ceased to exist, it could no longer be assessed and impugned orders of assessment were therefore void? - Held, on dissolution of firm it ceases to be a legal entity, and on principle, thereafter, unless there is a statutory provision permitting assessment of a dissolved firm, there is no longer any scope for assessing firm, which ceases to have legal existence - No assessment can be made on a firm which has lost its character as an assessable entity - (B) Question relating to res judicata - Principle of doctrine of res judicata is that judgments and decrees bind only parties and privies - To make a person a privy he must have acquired an interest in subject-matter of action by inheritance, succession or purchase subsequent to action or he must hold property subordinately, i.e., as a sub-lessee - Further held, partnership was dissolved in present case on 13-09-1952, and after dissolution of partnership there is no question of agency as between partners and decision in previous writ petition against 'L' cannot be binding as regards other partner - Appeal dismissed.
