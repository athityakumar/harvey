G.J. Fernandez v State of Mysore and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 April 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Vishishtha Bhargava, G.K. Mitter

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 252; AIR 1967 SC 1753; [1967] 3 S.C.R. 636

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Constitution - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 14 and 162 - (A) Whether rules in Mysore Public Works Department Code (hereinafter referred to as Code) were not followed in award of Tender? - Regard must be as to whether these instructions have any statutory force - If they have no statutory force, they confer no right on any body and a tenderer cannot claim any rights on basis of these administrative instructions - Art. 162 does not confer any power on State Govt. to frame rules and it only indicates scope of executive power of State - Of course, under such executive power, State can give administrative instructions to its servants on how to act in certain circumstances, but that will not make such instructions statutory rules - Held, no claim for any relief before a court of law can be founded by a member of public, like appellant, on breach of mere administrative instructions - (B) Whether there was unequal treatment between various tenderer which was in violation of art. 14 of Constitution? - Further held, there is no given period of limitation and it cannot be said that it was not open to Chief Engineer to take into account a letter which came a few days later - Appeal dismissed.
