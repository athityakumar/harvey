 New Manek Chowk Spinning and Weavingmills Company Limited and Others v Municipal Corporation of The City Ofahmedabad and Others

Supreme Court of India

21 February 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

G.K. Mitter, Vishishtha Bhargava, K. Subba Rao, J.C. Shah, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 465; AIR 1967 SC 1801; [1967] 2 S.C.R. 679

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Bombay Provincial Municipal Corporation Act, 1949, Madras District Municipalities Act, 1920

Summary: Municipalities & Local Government - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 32 - Bombay Provincial Municipal Corporations Act, 1949, ss. 127 (3) and (4), 128, 129, 2 (5), 2 (30), 2 (49), 2(53), 2(54) and 453, Sch., Chap.VIII, rr. 7(1), 9(b), 15 and 18 - Writ petitions - Property Tax on textile mills, factories buildings of universities, etc., imposed by Municipal Corporation of Ahmedabad - Flat rate method according to floor area adopted for determining rent for fixing rateable value -(A) Whether assessment book relating to special Property section prepared and published by the Municipal Corporation of the City of Ahmedabad by which the Municipality seeks to impose or has imposed property tax on properties described as Special Properties like textile mills, factories, buildings of the universities, etc. on the basis of a flat rate per 100 sq. ft. of the floor area of the property situate within the municipal limits of the city is valid? - There was nothing to show that conditions prerequisite for determination of annual value of textile factories in Ahmedabad on the basis of rental value per foot super of floor area existed at the relevant time nor had it been shown that the so-called contractor's basis was adopted by the municipal authorities of Ahmedabad - Held, applied indiscriminately it was sure to give rise to inequalities - The Municipality did not observe the law and failed in its duty to determine the rateable value of each building and land comprised in each of the textile factories in terms of R. 9 (b) of the Rules under the Bombay Provincial Municipal Corporations Act, 1949 - (B) Whether assessee object or file appeals against the decisions on rateable value to the authorities prescribed by the Act? - It would be useless for the assessee to take objections or file appeals against the decisions on rateable value to the authorities prescribed by the Act if he was challenging the determination of the ratable value as being violative of art. 14 of the Constitution, 1950 - Held, the Act and the appeal rules do not make any provision for relief to an assessee who complains that the assessment book has been prepared in violation of the law - (C) Whether r. 7(2) of the rules framed under the Bombay Act 1949 was beyond the legislative competence of the State? - If the State Legislature had power to levy a tax only on land and buildings the same could not be levied on machinery contained in or situate on the building even though the machinery was there for the use of the building for a particular purpose - Held, r. 7 (2) of the rules framed under the Bombay Act of 1949 is beyond the legislative competence of the State - Petition allowed.
