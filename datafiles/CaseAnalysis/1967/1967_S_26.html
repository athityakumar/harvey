Sevantilal Maneklal Sheth v Commissioner of Income Tax (Central), Bombay

Supreme Court of India

22 November 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 191; AIR 1968 SC 697; [1968] 68 ITR 503; [1968] 2 S.C.R. 360

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income-tax Act, 1961 - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922 - Profits or gains - Sale of assets - Avoidance of tax - Income from sale - Preference shares - Ordinary shares - Capital gain -Total value of transferred shares was Rs. 68,730/- Subsequent to transfer company converted the preference shares into ordinary shares giving the shareholders 8 ordinary shares for each preference share with result B held in all 2,424 ordinary shares of the mills - B sold 2,400 shares resulting in capital gain - Income Tax Officer included the amount which was profit made by B on sale of shares in all three assessment - One appeal dismissed and other two partly allowed - Whether there was any distinction between income from asset transferred to wife and income from sale of assets so transferred - Held, no - There is no logical distinction between income arising from asset transferred to wife and income from sale of assets so transferred - Profits or gains which arise from sale of asset would arise or spring from asset, although operation by which profits or gains is made to arise out of the asset is operation of sale - No reason why restricted interpretation be given to provisions of s.16(3)(a)(iii) as contended for appellant - Object of enactment of section is to prevent avoidance of tax or reducing the incidence of tax on the part of assessee by transfer of his assets to his wife or minor child - Appeal dismissed.
