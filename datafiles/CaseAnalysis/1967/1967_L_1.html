Balwant Singh and Another v L. C. Bharupal, Income Tax, Officer New Delhi, and Another

Supreme Court of India

27 November 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Shelat, S.M. Sikri, K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 362; [1968] 70 ITR 89

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 26 A and 37 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 193, 196, 193, 196, 199, 200, 205, 211, 228, 463, 471, 475 and 476 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898, ss. 561 A, 476 and 479 A - Appellant firm was registered u/s. 26 A Income Tax Act for 1959-60 - Respondent noticed that firm had not applied for renewal of registration in 1960-61 - Firm was, therefore, liable to be assessed as an unregistered firm - Appellant represented to respondent that firm had filed an application for renewal for assessment year 1961-62 within prescribed period, and, therefore, its registration should be renewed - Appellant produced a certificate of posting purporting to have been issued by post office in proof of application having been posted and also a duplicate application - Respondent lodged complaint before Magistrate alleging that said certificate of posting and said duplicate application were forged documents and had been used by appellants in proceedings before him - (A) Whether ITO while acting u/s 26 A was a court and that being so, it was incumbent on him to follow procedure laid down in ss. 476 and 479 A of CrPC, before he could file a complaint? - ITO however, cannot be treated as a Revenue Court - Though, therefore, proceedings before ITO are judicial proceedings in a Court and s. 195(1)(b) applies, neither s. 476 nor s. 479 A CrPC would be applicable - Held, it was, therefore, not incumbent upon respondent to follow procedure laid down in either of these two sections - (B) Whether since respondent had ceased to hold charge of post of ITO on date of filing of said complaint complaint was without jurisdiction and Magistrate could not take cognizance of such an illegal complaint? - Though respondent might have been posted to another income-tax district when complaint was filed, it is possible that Commr. in exercise of powers reserved to him under Income-Tax Act may have allotted or transferred or directed him to continue to deal with certain cases including cases pending before him at time of his transfer - If that were to be so, contention that he was no longer court for purposes of s. 195(1)(b), CrPC with reference to present case would not prevail - Question being one of evidence, can be raised before Magistrate trying complaint - Appeal dismissed.
