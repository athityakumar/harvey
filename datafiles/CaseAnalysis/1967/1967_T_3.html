Niranjan Shankar Golikari v Century Spinning and Manufacturing Company Limited

Supreme Court of India

17 January 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Shelat, R.S. Bachawat

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 383; AIR 1967 SC 1098; 1967 (14) FLR 337; 1967 (1) LLJ 740; [1967] 2 S.C.R. 378

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Municipalities & Local Governments; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Supervisor, Engagement, Restraint Of Trade

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Indian Contract Act, 1872, s. 27 - (1) Whether agreement constituted a restraint on trade, was opposed to public policy? - Held, no - Negative covenants do not fall within purview of agreements in restraint of trade and are necessary to protect interests of respondent company - (2) Whether injunction was reasonable in space and time and to extent necessary to protect employer's right of property - Further held, yes - Injunction issued against appellant restricted in terms of time, nature of employment and area and cannot be said to be unreasonable - Courts have discretion to grant a limited injunction necessary to protect employer's interests - (3) Whether injunction to enforce a negative stipulation can only be granted for purpose of safeguarding trade secrets of employer - Appellant restrained from divulging information, instruments, documents, reports, which came to his knowledge while he was serving respondent company - Appeal dismissed.
