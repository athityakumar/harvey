 Sant Ram Sharma v State Of Rajasthan & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

07 August 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, R.S. Bachawat, G.K. Mitter, K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 234; AIR 1967 SC 1910; 1991 (2) CompLJ 255; 1968 (2) LLJ 830; 1968 (1) SCJ 672; [1968] 1 S.C.R. 111; 1967 SLR 906

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Service - Constitution Of India, 1950 - arts. 14, 16, 162 and 309 - Indian Police Service (Pay) Rules (1954), rr. 3, 8, Sch III Part B - Indian Police Service (Regulation of Seniority ) Rules (1954), r. 6 - Respondent No. 3 was promoted as Inspector General of Police, Rajasthan superseding petitioner, and respondent No. 4 as Addl. Inspector General of Police superseding petitioner - Petitioner has also prayed for a writ in nature of mandamus commanding respondents 1 and 2 to consider petitioner's claim as senior-most officer in Rajasthan to be promoted to post of Inspector General of Police - Whether promotion to selection grade posts are carried as per administrative instructions it would amount to violation of art.14 and 16 of Constitution ? - It cannot be said that till statutory rules governing promotion to selection grade posts are framed Govt. cannot issue administrative instructions regarding principles to be followed - Held, If cases of all eligible candidates are considered before appointment to such posts, no violation of art. 14 and 16 occurs - Petition dismissed.
