Ram Laxman Sugar Mills v Commissioner of Income Tax, U.P., and Another

Supreme Court of India

17 March 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, S.M. Sikri, Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 293; [1967] 66 ITR 613

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Axt, 1922, ss. 26A, 33B, 66(1) - A/ys. 1949-50, 1950-51 Partnership Deed - Hindu Undivided Family - Registration of Firm - Maintainability - Appellant/asseesee had partnership deed among members of family, had partial partition among members of family and joint family status was served - Appellant filed application for registration of firm u/s. 26A of the Act for a/ys 1949-50, 1950-51 was granted by respondent/IO and brought to tax - Respondent/Commissioner of Income-tax cancelled said order u/s.33B of the Act - Appellant filed appeal before Tribunal which affirmed said order - Aggrieved appellant referred question u/s.66(1) of the Act before HC which answered affirmative against appellant - Hence, instant appeal - Whether, on facts and in circumstances of case, order cancelling renewal of registration was proper and justified - Held, deed had not been carefully drawn up, and somewhat inconsistent recitals had been made - But taking overall view of diverse covenants - Therefore, Court hold that partnership agreement was between karta of joint family one hand, and other persons of that joint family, which later ceased to exist by reason of partition would not affect validity of partnership or its continuance - Thus, Karta being contracting party, application for registration could not be rejected because other members of joint family, did not sign application for renewal of registration of firm for year 1950-51 - Hence, set aside order of HC - Appeal allowed..
