Deputy Commercial Tax Officer, Saidapet and v Enfield India Limited, Co-Operative Canteen

Supreme Court of India

23 November 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, Vishishtha Bhargava

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 164; AIR 1968 SC 838; 1968 (2) MLJ(SC) 68; [1968] 2 S.C.R. 421; 1968 (21) STC 317

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Keywords: Madras General Sales Tax Act 1908 (IX of 1908)

Summary: Constitution, sch. 7, list 2, entry 54 - T.N. General Sales Tax Act, 1959, ss. 2(d) and 2(g), expln. and s. 2(n), 2(p) and 3 - Sale of Goods Act, 1930, s. 4 - (A) Interpretation of Statutes - Meaning of expression 'sale of goods - Transaction lacking element of transfer of property - Legislative competancy - (B) Sales Tax - Co-operative society supplying refreshment to members for price - (1) Activity not profit motivated - Society is dealer within s. 2 (g) - (2) Property in refreshments with society - Transaction amounts to sale - Taxability - Held, legislature incompetent to make it sale by deeming clause in taxing statute - Expln. to s. 2 (g) not ultra vires the State Legislature -Turnover is taxable -
