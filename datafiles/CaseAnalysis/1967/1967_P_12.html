 Prem Dulari v Raj Kumari

Supreme Court of India

23 March 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Shelat, K. Subba Rao, Mohammad Hidayatullah, R.S. Bachawat, C.A. Vaidyialingam

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 117; AIR 1967 SC 1578; [1967] 3 S.C.R. 273

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Right of Prior Purchase Act (J K Act 11 of 1993)

Summary: Land & Property - Jammu and Kashmir Right of Prior Purchase Act, 1993, s. 15 - Respondent 1 filed suit for possession of house in dispute, owned by second respondent and sold by her to appellant - Right of prior purchase u/s. 15 (fourthly) of Right of Prior Purchase Act, II of 1993 - Common entrance to both houses - (A) Whether proper construction of s. 15 (fourthly) of act of 1993, such a common outer entrance would not give rise to a right of prior purchase? - Where sale is of property having a common outer entrance with other properties, right of prior purchase shall vest in owners of such properties - There is nothing in section to warrant construction that such a right would vest only if common outer entrance is jointly owned by owners of such houses - What section requires is existence of a common outer entrance which need not be owned by person claiming right of pre emption - Held, such a common outer entrance would not give rise to a right of prior purchase unless owner claiming such a right and owner of house in question jointly own common outer passage - In instant case from evidence led by parties that there does exist a common outer entrance for both houses -(B) Whether s. 15(fourthly) providing for right of prior purchase amounts to an unreasonable restriction? - Law under first ground providing for pre-emption by co-sharers imposed reasonable restriction in interest of general public on right under art. 19(1)(f) - Further held, there can be no doubt that such a provision amounts to a restriction in sense that a person purchasing such a property has to give way to person claiming such a right - Appeal dismissed.
