Bhanji Bagawandas v Commissioner of Income Tax, Madras

Supreme Court of India

18 July 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, S.M. Sikri, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 237; AIR 1968 SC 139; [1968] 67 ITR 18; 1968 (1) MLJ(SC) 53; [1968] 1 S.C.R. 17

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Practice & Procedure - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 34(1)(a), 34(1), 34(3) and 66(1) - Assessment year involved in this appeal is 1948-49, corresponding previous year being financial year 1947-48 - Whether on facts and in circumstances of case, assessment made is saved from bar of limitation under second proviso to s. 34(3)? - High Court answered questions in favour of respondent and against appellant - HC followed an earlier decision in A.S. Khader Ismail v. Income-tax officer in which it had held that word 'finding' in proviso to s. 34(3) of Act must be given a wide significance so as to include not only findings necessary for disposal of appeal but it would apply to cases where it is held that income in question was in respect of an earlier year which was not subject - Held, Supreme Court in Income-tax Officer, A-Ward, Sitapur v. Murlidhar Bhagwan Das 1964 Indlaw SC 296 it was held that expressions 'finding' and 'direction', in second proviso to s. 34(3), meant respectively, a finding necessary for giving relief in respect of assessment for year in question, and a direction which appellate or revisional authority, as case may be, was empowered to give under sections mentioned in that proviso - Second proviso to s. 34(3) did not save time-limit prescribed u/s. 34(1) in respect of an escaped assessment of a year other than that which was subject-matter of appeal or revision, as case may be - SC overruled judgment of A. S. Khader Ismail v- Income-tax Officer - Further held, view taken by HC in present case is not correct in law and must be overruled - Where question itself was under issue, there is no further limitation imposed by section that reference should be limited to those aspects of question which had been argued before Tribunal, and it will be an over-refinement of position to hold that each aspect of a question is itself a distinct question for purpose of s. 66(1) of Act - Judgment of HC set aside - Case remanded to HC - Appeal allowed.
