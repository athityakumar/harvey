 I. C. Golaknath and Others v State of Punjab and Anrs.(With Connected

Supreme Court of India

27 February 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, S.M. Sikri, R.S. Bachawat, Vishishtha Bhargava, G.K. Mitter, C.A. Vaidyialingam, J.C. Shah, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 1493; AIR 1967 SC 1643; 1967 ALJ 813; 1967 (2) SCJ 486; [1967] 2 S.C.R. 762; 1967 SLR 301

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Intermediary, Universal Declaration of Human Rights, Doctrine Of Pith And Substance, Constitutionality, Legislative Procedure, Indian Independence Act, 1947, Constitution (Seventeenth Amendment) Act, 1964, State Laws, Titles, Constitution (Fifth Amendment) Act, 1955, Abolition of Privy Council Jurisdiction Act 1949, Constitution (First Amendment) Act, 1951Constitution (Second Amendment) Act, 1952

Summary: Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 13(2), 226, 368, 248, 246, 33, 34, 16, 304, 2(A), 19(f), (g), 14, 32, 31, (A), (1), (2), and 245 - Constitution (Seventeenth Amendment) Act, 1956, ss. 3 and 9 - (A) Whether amendment of Constitution u/art. 368 is 'law' within meaning of art. 13(2)? - Held, art. 3 enables Parliament by law to form new States and alter areas, boundaries or names of existing States - Imposed two, further conditions, of President, and in therein, views expressed by Legislatures - Notwithstanding said conditions, it cannot be suggested that expression 'law' under said Article is not one made by Legislative process - U/art. 4, such a law can contain provisions for amendment of Schedules I and IV indicating thereby that amendments are only made by Legislative process - Cl. (2)introduces a fiction to affect that such a law shall not be deemed to be an amendment to Constitution - This shows that amendment is law and but for fiction it would be an amendment within meaning of art. 368 - (B) Whether the present decision should be given retrospective operation? - Constitution (First Amendment) Act, 1951, amended arts. 15 and 19, and arts 31- A and 31-B were inserted with retrospective effect - Object of amendment was said to validate acquisition of zamindaries or abolition of permanent settlement without interference from courts - (C) Whether Constitution (Seventeenth Amendment) Act, 1964 was valid? - Held, from history of amendments, two things appear, namely, unconstitutional laws were made and they were protected by amendment of Constitution or amendments were made in order to protect future laws which would be void but for amendments - But fact remains that Supreme Court held as early as in 1951 that Parliament had power to amend fundamental rights - Further Seventeenth Amendment Act was also approved by Supreme Court - Petitions dismissed.
