 Collector of Varanasi v Gauri Shankar Misra and Others

Supreme Court of India

29 August 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, R.S. Bachawat, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, G.K. Mitter

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 203; AIR 1968 SC 384; 1968 ALJ 139; 1968 BLJR 114; [1968] 1 S.C.R. 372

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Defence & Security Forces; Land & Property

Keywords: Defence Regulations

Summary: Constitution - Defence & Security Forces - Land & Property - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 136 - Defence of India Act, 1939, s. 19 - Supreme Court Rules, O. 13, r.2 - (A) Whether no special leave could have been granted u/art. 136 as judgment appealed from was neither that of a court nor of a tribunal or whether HC while acting u/s. 19(1)(f) is functioning as a court? - While acting u/s. 19(1)(f), HC functions as a 'Court' and not as a designated person - Decision rendered by HC u/s. 19(1)(f) is a 'determination' and not a decree, judgment or final order - Provisions of art.133 are not attracted - Case is outside scope of O. 13, r.2 - Court was competent to grant special leave u/art. 136 - HC cannot be thought of as anything other than a 'Court' - (B) Whether sale deeds produced for purpose of calculating compensation in respect of lands acquired by Govt. were reliable and that they evidenced genuine transactions? - Sale in question cannot be called a contemporaneous transaction - Held, none of sale deeds produced can afford any assistance in determining compensation payable - They do not evidence sale of lands similar to acquired lands, at about time of acquisition - Appeal allowed.
