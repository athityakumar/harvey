 Northern India Caterers Private Limited, and Another v State of Punjab and Another

Supreme Court of India

04 April 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Shelat, K. Subba Rao, Mohammad Hidayatullah, R.S. Bachawat, C.A. Vaidyialingam

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 448; AIR 1967 SC 1581; [1967] 3 S.C.R. 399

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Rent Control

Keywords: Occupiers, Punjab Requisitioning and Acquisition of Immovable Property Act, 1953, Uttar Pradesh Sugar Factories Rules, 1938

Summary: Land & property - Punjab Public Premises and Land (Eviction and Rent Recovery) Act, 1959, s. 5 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - State of Punjab leased the "Mount View Hotel" at Chandigarh to the appellants for a period of six years' offered to sell the hotel to the appellants which was declined -Respondents demanded for vacation of the premises - (A) Whether an earlier enactment is repealed by latter one the dame subject automatically? - Held, where the later Act does not contain such express words, if the co-existence of the two sets of provisions is destructive of the object with which the later Act was passed, the Court would treat the earlier provision as impliedly repealed - The well settled rule of construction is that when the later enactment is worded in affirmative terms without any negative it does not impliedly repeal the earlier - (B) Whether s. 5 of Punjab Public Premises and Land (Eviction and Rent Recovery) Act, 1959 violates art. 14 of the Constitution? - The unauthorised occupant has full opportunity of being heard and of producing his evidence before the Collector - He is not denied the equal protection of the laws because the Govt. has the option of proceeding against him either by a suit or under the Act - Held, the impugned Act makes no unjust discrimination - It promotes public welfare and is a beneficent measure of legislation - If the Act is struck down, it will give a free charter to unauthorized occupants and to officers squatting on public premises after they have vacated their offices to continue in occupation for an indefinite time until they are evicted by dilatory procedure of a title suit - Appeal allowed.
