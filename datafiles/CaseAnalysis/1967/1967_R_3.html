 R. S. Seth Gopikrishan Agarwal v R. N. Sen, Assistant Collector

Supreme Court of India

05 January 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, S.M. Sikri, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, J.C. Shah, C.A. Vaidyialingam

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 155; AIR 1967 SC 1298; 1967 (69) BomLR 459; 1967 CRLJ 1194; 1985 ECR 2210; 1983 (13) E.L.T. 1434; 1967 MahLJ 492; [1967] 2 S.C.R. 340

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Customs - Constitution - Customs Act, 1962, ss. 2(34), 105, and 110(3) - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898, s. 105 - Constitution Of India, 1953, art. 14 - Defence of India (Amendment) Rules, 1963 (Gold Control Rules), r. 126 (L) 4 (2) - The appellant a mining proprietor, doing business in many articles - On information assistant collector issued an authorization - Appellant premises searched - Gold and other articles were found - Appellant filed a petition challenging validity of search and seizure of document u/art. 226 of Constitution -(A) Whether Assistant Collector is required to specify goods of search? - Held, Assistant Collector need neither state reasons nor give particulars of nature of goods and of documents, in his authorization for search - (B) Whether s. 105 is ultra vires, art. 14 of Constitution? - Further held, s. 105 does not confer arbitrary power on Asistant Collector of Customs - Provisions of Act indicate a policy as well as effective controls on power of Assistant Collector - S. 105 of Customs Act is not ultra vires art. 14 of Constitution - Appeal dismissed.
