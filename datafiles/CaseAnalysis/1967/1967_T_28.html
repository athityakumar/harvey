Balvantray Ratilal Patel v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

12 December 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

Vishishtha Bhargava, J.C. Shah, V. Ramaswami

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 402; AIR 1968 SC 800; 1968 (17) FLR 445; 1968 LabIC 984; 1968 (2) LLJ 700; 1968 MLJ 523; [1968] 2 S.C.R. 577; 1968 SLR 593

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Pending Enquiry, Civil Services Rules, Bombay Civil Service Rules

Summary: Service - Bombay Civil Service Rules, rr. 53, 54, 151, 152, 153 and 156 - Civil Services Rules - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 80 - Orissa Service Code, r. 93A - Conviction and sentence passed against appellant set aside by High Court - Govt. directed departmental enquiry and order of dismissal made against appellant - Appellant filed suit for declaration that order of suspension, illegal and inoperative in law - Declaration granted - In appeal Division Bench of HC held order of suspension made by respondent, legally valid - (A) Whether Govt. had power to suspend appellant pending enquiry into his alleged misconduct? - Held, order of interim suspension can be passed against employee while an inquiry is pending into his conduct even though there is no such term in contract of appointment or in rules, but in such a case employee would be entitled to his remuneration for period of suspension if there is no statute or rule under which it could be withheld - (B) Whether order of suspension came to an end, when appellant was acquitted by HC in revision and appellant is entitled to full pay for period, he ultimately dismissed? - Held, order of suspension recites that appellant should be suspended with immediate effect 'pending further orders', therefore order of suspension could not be automatically terminated but it could have only been terminated by another order of Govt. - Until further order of State Govt. was made terminating suspension, appellant had no right to be reinstated to service - Appeal dismissed.
