Khan Bahadur Ahmed Alladin and Sons v Commissioner of Income Tax, Andhra Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

24 November 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, J.C. Shah, Vishishtha Bhargava

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 361; AIR 1968 SC 788; [1968] 68 ITR 573; [1968] 2 S.C.R. 439

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Vocation

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Contract of sale - Purchase - Resale of stores - Adventure in nature of trade - Govt. of India decided to sell 'B' factory - Contract of sale made subject to condition that Govt. was no longer interested in factory and also subject to confirmation by Board of Directors - Assessee firm purchased 'B' factory and properties attached to it in addition to stores from Govt. of India - Price of 'B' factory and properties together with furniture fixed at Rs.27 lakhs while price of stores fixed at Rs.8 lakhs - Assessee firm paid off dues to Govt. and also discharging its debts by selling fractions of property - During relevant accounting years, assessee firm sold part of stores and factory buildings, furniture, railway siding - - Appellate Tribunal decided that transactions in question constitute an adventure in nature of trade and any surplus got by sale of portions of properties were liable to tax - HC answered question against assessee firm and expressed same view taken by Appellate Tribunal - Hence instant appeal - Whether HC was right in holding that transaction was an adventure in nature of trade - Held, that HC was right in holding that transaction was an adventure in nature of trade - Assessee firm was purchasing, selling and liquidating loans would all show commercial nature of transaction - HC rightly concluded that purchase of the site and buildings of 'B' factory was an adventure in nature of trade and was in course of a profit making scheme and question was rightly answered by HC against assessee firm - Appellate Tribunal should state clearly its conclusions and findings of fact and should not leave it to HC or that Court to deduce findings or to collect facts from a large number of documents which were part of record of case - Appeal disposed of.
