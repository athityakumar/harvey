 Punjab State, Chandigarh v Sansari Mal Puran Chand

Supreme Court of India

22 August 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Bachawat, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, G.K. Mitter, K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 201; AIR 1968 SC 331; 1968 CurLJ 182; [1968] 1 S.C.R. 336; 1968 (21) STC 91

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure; Sales Tax

Keywords: Sales Tax Laws Validation Act, 1956, Essential Goods (Declaration And Regulation Of Tax On Sale Or Purchase) Act

Summary: Constitution - Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Practice & Procedure - Sales Tax - Socio-Economic - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 286- Constitution (Sixth Amendment) Act, 1956 - Essential Goods (Declaration And Regulation Of Tax On Sale Or Purchase) Act - East Punjab General Sales Tax Act, 1948 - s. 5 - Issues Raised - (A)Whether s. 5 of the East Punjab Act as originally passed in 1948, invalid? - (B) Whether the invalidity of s. 5 invalidate the other provisions of the Act? - (C) Whether s. 5 of the East Punjab Act as amended by East Punjab Act of 1962 is invalid? - The first three Issues are concluded by the decision of this Court in M/s. Devi Das Gopal Krishan and others v. State of Punjab and others(1967 Indlaw SC 90.) where it was held that s. 5 of the Act as originally passed in 1948, was void on the ground of excessive delegation of legislative power to the State Govt. &that the striking down of s. 5 did not render void s. 4 and the other sections of the Act though till an appropriate s. 5 was inserted s. 4 remained unenforceable - S. 5 as amended by the East Punjab Act was not invalid on the ground of excessive delegation of legislative authority nor was it invalid on the ground that Act purported to amend a stillborn section - (D) Whether the amended s. 5 is a law imposing or authorising the imposition of a tax within the meaning of art. 286(3) of the Constitution as it stood before the Constitution (Sixth Amendment) Act? - The amended s. 5 inserted in East Punjab Act by East Punjab Act authorising the fixation of the rate of tax leviable on the taxable turnover was a law authorising the imposition of a tax within the purview of the unamended Art. 286(3) of the Constitution. As the East Punjab Act did not receive the assent of the President, the amended s. 5 could not take effect at all either prospectively or retrospectively in respect of sales and purchases of essential goods while the ban of Art. 286(3) continued. But it could take effect in respect of sales and purchases of other goods.- (E) Whether the notification dated August 5, 1954 issued under s. 6(2) is valid? - As from 11-09-1956 the amended s. 5 inserted by East Punjab Act No. took effect on sales or purchases of edible oil also - (F) Whether the notifications issued under s. 5 before the passing of the East Punjab Act No. 19 of 1952 are valid? - The notification issued by the State Government was not a "law made by the legislature of a State" within the meaning of art. 286(3) - Though issued after the passing of Central Act No. 52 of 1952, it did not require the assent Of the President for affecting essential goods. The impugned notification took effect in respect of edible oil as from 05-08-1954 and thereafter sales of edible oil produced in ghanis run by mechanical power were taxable - (G) Whether tax was effectively imposed on sales of the edible oil in question during the relevant assessment years? the respondents are not liable to pay tax on sales of edible oil produced in ghanis run by mechanical power effected by them before September 11, 1956. But they are liable to pay tax on such sales made after 11-09-1956 - Held, Appeals partly allowed.
