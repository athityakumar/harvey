 Bhawani Cotton Mills Limited v State of Punjab and Another

Supreme Court of India

10 April 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

C.A. Vaidyialingam, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, S.M. Sikri, K. Subba Rao, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 269; AIR 1967 SC 1616; [1967] 3 S.C.R. 577; 1967 (20) STC 290

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Keywords: Declared Goods

Summary: Sales Tax - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956 - Punjab General Sales Tax Act, 1948, ss. 2, 4, 5, 7, 10, 12, 15, 23 and 27 - (A) Whether second proviso to s. 5(1) and cl. (vi) of s. 5 (2)(a) of Punjab General Sales Tax Act, are opposed to any of relevant provisions of Central Act? - Provisions contained in a statute, with respect to exemptions of tax or refund or rebate, on one hand, must be distinguished from total non-liability or non-imposition of tax, on other - Held, decision of High Court upholding orders of assessment passed by Officer, in question, cannot be sustained - (B) Whether Notification issued by State Govt. u/s. 5 on 26-09-1961, is valid in law? - It is open to State to tax all purchases which conic within definition of s. 2(ff) as it now stands, but State, it is pointed out, must be considered to have chosen to levy tax only if purchases have been made for use in manufacture of goods for sale - Further held, said notification is not valid - Appeals allowed.
