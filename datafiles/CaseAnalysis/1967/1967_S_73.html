Rameshwar Lal Patwari v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

01 December 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, C.A. Vaidyialingam

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 406; AIR 1968 SC 1303; 1968 CRLJ 1490; [1968] 2 S.C.R. 505

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Preventive Detention Act, 1950

Summary: Criminal - Constitution of India, art. 226 - Code of Criminal Procedure, s. 491 - Preventive Detention Act, 1950, s. 3 - Preventive Detention - Purpose of detention was to prevent appellant from acting in any manner prejudicial to maintenance of supplies and essential services - Appellant contended that the grounds of his detention were vague and irrelevant and even false - Same contentions urged before Supreme Court - Whether the detention of the appellant is justified? - Held, no - Two grounds are vague, one ground is found to be false and of the remaining in one there is no explanation - Where grounds are found to be precise, the detention cannot be justified - Grounds of detention are defective and detention without a trial would cause great prejudice to accused - Appeal allowed.
