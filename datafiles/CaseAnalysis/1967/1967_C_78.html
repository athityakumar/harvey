T. N. K. Govindarajulu Chetty v Commissioner of Income Tax, Madras

Supreme Court of India

17 April 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, S.M. Sikri, Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 254; AIR 1968 SC 129; 1967 (2) ITJ 770; [1967] 66 ITR 465; 1968 (1) MLJ(SC) 1; [1967] 3 S.C.R. 653

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Defence Regulations

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Defence of India Act, 1939 - Income-tax Act, 1961 - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922 - Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - Requisition of Land (Continuance of Powers) Act, 1947 - Requisitioned Land (Continuance of Powers) Act, 1947 - Compensation - Interest - Capital receipts - By order Collector exercising power requisitioned a property belonging to assesses - Property continued to remain under requisition till it vested in the Govt - Assessees declined the offer made by Collector to pay compensation for acquisition of property - Dispute arose relating to compensation payable to assessee - HC ordered that assessee be paid Rs. 5,00,0001- as compensation for property with interest 6% - Income-tax Officer apportioned the amount of interest and assessed the amount to income-tax - Appellate Commissioner found that apportioned amounts were of the nature of revenue and not capital receipts - Commissioner and assessees appealed to appellate Tribunal against the order of Appellate Assistant Commissioner - Appellate Tribunal accepted the contention of assesses that receipts were not assessable to tax because they were of the nature of capital receipts - Whether the interest received by assessee was subject to taxation - Held, yes - Interest received by the assessee was properly held taxable - Right to interest arose by virtue of provisions of ss. 28 and 34 of the Land Acquisition Act, 1894, and arbitrator and HC merely gave effect to that right in awarding interest on the amount of compensation - Provisions relating to payment of interest are not, part of law relating to arbitration and there is nothing in cl 1. (g) which excludes application of substantive law relating to payment of interest when arbitration is determining amount of compensation - Appeal dismissed
