 S.K. Dutta, Income Tax Officer and Others v Lawrence Singh Ingty

Supreme Court of India

07 November 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, R.S. Bachawat, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, G.K. Mitter

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 174; AIR 1968 SC 658; [1968] 68 ITR 272; [1968] 2 S.C.R. 165

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Extra Territoriality

Summary: Constitution - Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 4(3)(xxi) - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 10(26) - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 14 - Whether exclusion of Govt. servants from exemption u/s. 4(3)(xxi) and later u/s. 10(26) violates art.14 of Constitution? - Administrative convenience which can afford just basis for classification must be a real and substantial one - Validity of impugned provisions cannot be tested from what our legislatures or Govt. did or omitted to do before Constitution came into force - Classification based on past legislative practice and history does not mean that because in past legislature was enacting arbitrary laws it could do so now - To over-paint picture of a Govt. servant as embodiment of all power and prestige would sound ironical - For purpose of valid classification what is required is not some imaginary difference but a reasonable and substantial distinction, having regard to purpose of law - Govt. servants have been discriminated against and discrimination in question is written large on face of provisions in question - Held, ss. 4(3)(xxi) and 10(26) of Acts must be stricken down as being violative of art. 14 - Appeal dismissed.
