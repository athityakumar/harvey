C. T. Senthilnathan Chettiar v State of Madras

Supreme Court of India

20 July 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri, J.C. Shah, Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 341; [1968] 67 ITR 102

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Vocation

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1922 - Madras Agricultural Income Tax Act, ss. 54(1), 9(1) -Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s.126 - Agricultural Income - Gift - Settlement Deed - Revocation - Validity - Assessee executed settlement deed in favour of settlee with a life interest without any powers of alienation - Settlor reserved his right to cancel or revoke or alter settlement - Agricultural Income-tax Officer had included income from lands settled under the deed in hands of assessee - Revision was filed before commissioner of agricultural Income-tax - It was held that document evidenced a revocable transfer and settled lands were an asset remaining in property of settlor - Commissioner refused to exclude income from lands settled in deed as assessable income - Assessee filed a revision before HC, which agreed with Commissioner, that settlement deed amounted to a revocable transfer of assets - Assessee obtained leave from HC - Hence, instant appeal - Whether settlement deed reserved power to settlor to cancel or revoke or alter settlement in any circumstances - Held, in computing total agricultural income, all agricultural income arising to any person by virtue of a settlement or disposition, whether revocable or not, and whether effected before or after commencement of Act, from assets remaining property of settler or disposer, shall be deemed to be agricultural income of settlor - All agricultural income arising to any person by virtue of a revocable transfer of assets shall be deemed to be agricultural income of transfer or a settlement, disposition or transfer shall be deemed to be revocable if it contains any provision for retransfer directly or indirectly of agricultural income or assets to settlors or transferor or in any way gives settlor, disposer or transferor a right to reassume power directly or indirectly over the agricultural income or assets - ' settlement or disposition' shall include any disposition, trust, covenant, agreement or arrangement and 'settlor or disposer ' in relation to a settlement or disposition shall include any person by whom settlement or disposition was made shall not apply to any agricultural income arising to any person by virtue of a settlement or disposition which is not revocable for a period exceeding 6 years or during lifetime of person and from which agricultural income settlor derives no direct or indirect benefit - Settlor shall be liable to be assessed on agricultural income as and when power to revoke arises - Therefore, settlement deed reserved power to settlor to cancel or revoke or alter settlement in any circumstances which required cancellation or alteration of deed - assesse could at any time reassume power over assets or agricultural income by cancelling or altering terms of deed - Appeal dismissed.
