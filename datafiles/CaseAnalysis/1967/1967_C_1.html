 C. A. Rajendran v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

29 September 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, R.S. Bachawat, G.K. Mitter, K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 385; AIR 1968 SC 507; 1968 (17) FLR 350; 1968 (2) LLJ 407; [1968] 1 S.C.R. 721; 1968 SLR 65

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 15, 16(4), 29, 32 and 226 - (A) Whether there is constitutional duty or obligation imposed upon Union Govt. to make reservations for Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes either at initial stage of recruitment and at stage of promotion in Railway Board Secretariat Service Scheme? - Court held, art. 16(4) does not confer any right on petitioner and there is no constitutional duty imposed on Govt. to make a reservation for Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes, either at initial stage of recruitment or at stage of promotion - Further held, art .16(4) is an enabling provision and confers a discretionary power on State to make a reservation of appointments in favour of backward class of citizens which, in its opinion, is not adequately represented in Services of State - (B)Classification as between direct recruits and promotees - Whether reasonable? - It is well-established that there can be a reasonable classification of employees for purpose of appointment by promotion and classification as between direct recruits and promotees is reasonable - Held, petitioner being an employee of Railway Board' is governed by rules applicable to officers in service to which he belongs - Employees of Central Secretariat Service belong to a different class and it is not possible to accept argument that there is any discrimination against petitioner and violation of guarantee u/art. 14 of Constitution - Appeal dismissed.
