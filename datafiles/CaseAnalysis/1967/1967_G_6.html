Sukhram and Another v Gauri Shankar and Another

Supreme Court of India

11 September 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, S.M. Sikri, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 497; AIR 1968 SC 365; [1968] 1 S.C.R. 476

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Hindu Women's Right to Property Act, 1937 - Hindu Succession Act, 1956, s. 14 - Appellants, who constitute a joint family and are governed by Mitakshara law of Benares school, are brother and son of one Hukam Singh - Hukam Singh died in 1952 leaving him surviving his wife Kishan Devi - Kishan Devi sold a half share in a house and a shop belonging to joint family, to Gauri Shankar - Whether sale deed executed by Kishan Devi was binding upon coparceners of her husband? - Held, under Benares School of Mitakshara, a coparcener may not, without consent of other coparceners, sell his undivided share in family estate for his own benefit - But words of s. 14 of Hindu Succession Act are express and explicit - thereby a female Hindu possessed of property whether acquired before or after commencement of Act holds it as full owner and not as a limited owner - Thus, u/s. 14(1) of Hindu Succession Act, Kishan Devi acquired rights of full ownership in interests which Hukam Singh had in property of family during his life time, and she was competent without consent of male members of family to sell property for her own purposes - Appeal dismissed.
