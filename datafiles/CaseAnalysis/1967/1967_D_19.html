Commissioner of Income Tax, Madhya Pradesh v Dewas Cine Corporation

Supreme Court of India

08 November 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, Vishishtha Bhargava

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 384; AIR 1968 SC 676; [1968] 68 ITR 240; [1968] 2 S.C.R. 173

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Debt Laws

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 10 - Indian Partnership Act, 1932 - Dissolution of partnership - Depreciation - Sale of property - Payment of debts - 'S' and 'H' entered into an agreement to carry on business in partnership as exhibitors of cinematograph films - Each partner was an owner of a cinematograph theatre and each partner brought his theatre into books of partnership as an asset of partnership - For assessment years IO allowed depreciation aggregating to Rs.44,380/- in respect of two theatres - Later, partnership dissolved and on dissolution it was agreed between partners that theatres should returned to their original owners - In proceedings for assessment, respondent was treated as a registered firm - Appellate Tribunal observed that by restoring two theatres to two original owners was a transfer by firm and entries adjusting depreciation and writing off assets at original value amounted to total recoupment of entire depreciation by partnership u/s.10(2)(vii) of 1922 Act - Commissioner of Income tax appealed to Court with certificate granted by HC - HC disagreed with the view of Income- tax Appellate Tribunal that since the theatres were returned to the partners in settling the accounts of the partners on dissolution, the theatres were in law sold to the partners - Hence, instant appeal - Whether partnership property should be converted into money for payment of debts on absence of special agreement - Held, on dissolution of partnership each theatre must deemed to be returned to original owner in satisfaction partially or wholly of his claim to a share in residue of assets after discharging debts and other obligations - Theaters were not in law sold by partnership to individual 176 partners in consideration of their respective share in residue - Transaction must deemed in law to take form of a notional sale of property to partner in consideration of money value of his share - In absence of a special agreement to contrary right of each partner on a dissolution was to have partnership property converted into money by a sale even although a sale might not necessary for payment of debts - HC was right in deciding question referred in favour of assessee - Appeal dismissed.
