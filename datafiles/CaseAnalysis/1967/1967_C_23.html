Tirumala Venkateswara Timber Andbamboo Firm v Commercial Tax Officer, Rajahmundry

Supreme Court of India

28 November 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, J.C. Shah, Vishishtha Bhargava

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 167; AIR 1968 SC 784; 1968 (1) MLJ(SC) 64; [1968] 2 S.C.R. 476; 1968 (21) STC 312

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Keywords: Collected

Summary: A.P. General sales Tax Act, 1957, s. 2(1)(n), expln. III - Constitution, arts. 14, 226 and 245, sch. 7, list 2, entry 54 - Sale of Goods Act, 1930, s. 4 - Interpretation - Sales Tax - Constitutionality of the Expln.III to s. 2(1)(n) of 1957 Act challenged in the light of art.14 and on the ground of vires - Distinction between contract of sale and contract of agency -

Held, not ultra vires of powers of State Legislature - Not unconstitutional - Distinction between pointed out - Appeal Dismissed.
