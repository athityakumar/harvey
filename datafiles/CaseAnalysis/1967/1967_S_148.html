State of Mysore v Syed Ibrahim

Supreme Court of India

21 February 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Shelat, G.K. Mitter

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 466; AIR 1967 SC 1424; 1967 CRLJ 1215; [1967] 2 S.C.R. 361

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Summary: Carriers & Transporatation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1939, ss. 42(1), 123, 2(18), 2(16), 2(25), and 2(33) - 'Public Service vehicle' - Respondent, the owner of a motor car - Carried 8 passengers in his car for a charge of Rs. 5 per person - charge sheeted u/s. 42(1) read with s. 23 of the Act of 1939 -(A) Whether the purpose of using the motor vehicle is to be given any consideration while determining the applicability of provisions of Motor Vehicles Act, 1939? - Held, Applicability of s. 42(1) is determined by the purpose for which the motor vehicle is used - Carrying passengers or goods for hire or reward renders the motor vehicle a "Public Service vehicle" - (B) Whether the order of acquittal passed by the Trial Magistrate and confirmed by the High Court is justified? - It is the use of the motor vehicle for carrying passengers for hire or reward which determines the application of s. 42(1) - S. 42(1) of the Act of 1939 has been enacted for the purpose of controlling vehicles carrying passengers, the object of such control being obviously to ensure safety of passengers - Held, the order of acquittal passed by the Trial Magistrate and confirmed by the High Court is set aside - Appeal allowed.
