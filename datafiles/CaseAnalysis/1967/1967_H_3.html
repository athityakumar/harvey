Controller of Estate Duty, Gujarat v H. H. Iqbal Mohomed Khan, Nawab of Palanpur

Supreme Court of India

19 April 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 315; [1967] 66 ITR 484

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Estate, Estate Duty

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Transfer of Property Act, 1882 - Estate Duty Act, 1953 - Gift - Respondent's father sold property to Govt. for a sum of money for Rs.15 lacs out of which Rs.5 lacs were disbursed - Respondent contended that the aforesaid sum of Rs. 10 lacs was due from the Govt. and a sum of Rs. 9 lacs was gifted to him by his father and the amount should not be included in the estate of his deceased father - Deputy Controller accordingly held that the amount of Rs.9 lacs would be deemed to be passed to the respondent on the death of the deceased u/s. 9 of the 1953 Act - On an appeal to HC decision was made in favour of respondent hence the present appeal was made by respondent Controller of Estate Duty - Whether, the gift of Rs.9 lacs should be regarded as been made within two years of the death of the deceased and liable to estate duty - Held, there was merely a proposal or expression of the intention of the deceased to make the gift of the sum of Rs.9 lacs to the respondent and there was no transfer of that amount to the respondent carried out and effected by means of that letter within the meaning of s. 130 of 1882 Act therefore transfer of actionable claim was not completed by request made by deceased by letter to Govt. to pay Rs. 9 lacs directly to respondent - The gift of Rs.9 lacs should be regarded as made within two years of the death of the deceased and was liable to estate duty hence, decision of HC was to be set aside - Appeal allowed.
