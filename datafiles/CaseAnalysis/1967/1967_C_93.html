Rampyari Devi Saraogi v Commissioner of Income Tax, West Bengal, and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 May 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri, J.C. Shah, Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 275; [1968] 67 ITR 84

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922 - Assessment Year - Illegal and Void - Evidence - Overruled - Commissioner of Income-tax sent a notice under s.33B to assessee - proposed to pass orders thereon as circumstances of cases justify after giving an opportunity of being heard under powers vested and to produce necessary evidence in support of your contentions, objections in writing accompanied by the necessary evidence - Tax consultant/C, on behalf of assessee wrote a letter to Commissioner stating that show cause notice under s.33B was bad in law, illegal and void and without jurisdiction - Assessee was not in a position to produce any evidence before Commissioner - Assessee appeared and Commissioner passed an order under s.33B of Act - Assessee filed a writ petition, that order be quashed - HC overruled contention of the assessee - Hence instant appeal - Whether Income Tax Officer had jurisdiction and whether income assessed in the assessment orders which were passed was correct - Held that all assessments made are without jurisdiction ab initio null and void and agreed with HC that all this material was supporting material and did not constitute basic grounds on which orders under s.33B were passed by Commissioner - Appeal dismissed.
