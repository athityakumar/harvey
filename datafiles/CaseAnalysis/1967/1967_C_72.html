East India Industries (Madras) Private Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax, Madras

Supreme Court of India

03 April 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, S.M. Sikri, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 283; AIR 1967 SC 1554; [1967] 65 ITR 611; [1967] 3 S.C.R. 356

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Assessment Year, Vocation, Charitable or Religious entities

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 4(3)(i) and 15B - Whether assessee is entitled to claim deduction u/s. 15B in respect of the donation paid to a Trust? - As per the deed of trust one of the objects of the trust, was not for charitable or religious purposes - It follows that the Trust does not fulfil the conditions imposed by s. 4(3)(i) and the donation made by the assessee to the trust cannot be exempted u/s. 15B - Held, High Court rightly answered the question of law against the assessee and in favour of the CIT - Appeal dismissed.
