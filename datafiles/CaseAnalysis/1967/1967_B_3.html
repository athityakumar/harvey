State of Maharashtra and Another v B. K. Takkamore and Others

Supreme Court of India

02 February 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Bachawat, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 470; AIR 1967 SC 1353; 1967 (2) SCJ 705; [1967] 2 S.C.R. 583

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: City of Nagpur Corporation Act, 1948

Summary: Municipalities & Local Governments - City of Nagpur Corporation Act, 1948, ss. 57(1)(k), 408(1) and 409(1) - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 226 - Govt. of Maharashtra had issued a notice to Mayor of Nagpur Municipal Corporation - Appellant filed a writ challenging notice and order superseding Municipal corporation of Nagpur - Asking him to show cause why corporation should not be superseded - (A)Whether one of grounds being irrelevant affects order which renders corporation incompetent ? - Where court is satisfied that authority would have passed order on basis of other relevant and existing grounds and exclusion of irrelevant or non-existing ground could not have affected ultimate opinion or decision of authority, order has to be sustained - Held,the fact that one of those grounds was irrelevant, did not affect order inasmuch as second ground showed that in opinion of State Govt. ground was serious enough to warrant action u/s 408 (1) and was sufficient to establish that Corporation was not competent to perform its duties under Act - (B) Whether appeal to court from order u/s 408? - Held, there is no appeal to court from order u/s 408 of City of Nagpur Corporation Act - In a writ application court will not review facts as an Appellate Body - (C) Whether Corporation is to be given an opportunity to show cause why order u/s 408 of Act of 1948 should not be made? - Further held, except in cases of emergence, State Govt. must give to Corporation an opportunity to show cause why order u/s 408 of Act of 1948 should not be made - If on a consideration of explanation submitted by Corporation State Govt. considers that there is no ground for making order- Govt. may drop proceeding - Appeal allowed.
