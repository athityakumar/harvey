M. M. Ipoh and Others v Commissioner of Income Tax, Madras

Supreme Court of India

26 July 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, S.M. Sikri, Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 343; AIR 1968 SC 317; 1967 (2) ITJ 885; [1968] 67 ITR 106; 1968 (1) MLJ(SC) 32; 1968 (1) SCJ 106; [1968] 1 S.C.R. 65

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 3, 2(9), 23(2) and 63(2) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Whether conclusion of tribunal that in fact an association of persons existed in year 1952-53 and subsequent years was based on any evidence and whether order passed by High Court in exercise of its advisory jurisdiction under Income Tax Act, is correct? - Held, a firm is a 'person' within meaning of Income Tax Act, and a firm and an individual or group of individuals may form an association of persons within meaning of s. 3 of Indian Income Tax Act - There is nothing in Act which indicates that a minor cannot become a member of an association of persons for purposes of Act - Facts proved clearly show that there was such an association in years 1952-53 and subsequent years - Pursuant to three partitions no division by metes and bounds of shares of owners was made, only shares in income of owner were entered in books of account - There was common management of properties, and there was even a common selling agency - Association which has earned income in years 1952-53 and thereafter is an association different from association in 1951-52 - Finding recorded by HC that in year 1951-52 there was no association of persons will not in present case have any effect on finding of tribunal that in year 1952-53 and subsequent years such an association existed - Finding recorded by tribunal is one of fact, and was not liable to be questioned before HC - Appeal dismissed.
