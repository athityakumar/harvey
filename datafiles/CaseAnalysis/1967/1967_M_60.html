Municipal Council, Raichur v Amar Chand Prasanna Etc

Supreme Court of India

01 August 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, S.M. Sikri

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 224; AIR 1968 SC 255; [1968] 1 S.C.R. 87

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Unauthorized allotment of land, Permission for opening a school

Summary: Land & Property - Administrative - Delhi Development Act, 1957, s. 8 - Unauthorized allotment of land - Permission for opening a school - Legality - Respondent No. 1/Delhi Development Authority(DDA) permitted a nursery school to be opened in park by respondent No. 2 - Appellants/residents of colony filed writ petition alleging that permitting a nursery school in park to respondent No. 2 was in complete violation of provisions of the Act - HC dismissed the writ petition - Hence, instant appeal - Whether HC was justified in passing the impugned order - Held, zonal development plan visualised by s. 8 of the Act, land used for nursery school might not be indicated, as a distinction was permissible to be made between a high school and a primary school on one hand and nursery school on other - Land which was allotted to respondent No.2 was part of a park - It was not open to DDA to carve out any space meant for park for a nursery school - However, allotment in favour of respondent No.2 was misuse of power, for reasons which need not be adverted - Allotment in favour of respondent No.2 should be cancelled - Appeal allowed.
