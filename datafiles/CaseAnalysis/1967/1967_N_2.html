N. Kt. Sivalingam Chettiar v Commissioner of Income Tax, Madras

Supreme Court of India

27 March 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, S.M. Sikri, Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 301; [1967] 66 ITR 586

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s.34(1), 34(3), 34(3)prov.2 - Assessment - Undisclosed sources - Bar of limitation - Reassessment - Assessment of income-tax against appellant/assessee claimed that he had deposited Rs.10,000 with R out of Rs.16,000 - Income-tax Officer proceedings against assessee for assessment of tax u/s. 34 of 1922 Act for assessment year 1947-48 and brought to tax Rs.16,000 as income from undisclosed sources - Appellate Assistant Commissioner modified order and Rs.10,000 deposited with R alone were taxable in year of assessment 1947-48 - In appeal, Appellate Tribunal cancelled order of assessment - Tribunal accepted version of assessee that he had brought Rs.16,000 with him and amount was not taxable in assessment year 1947-48 - Income-tax Officer upheld u/s. 34(1)(a) of 1922 Act for assessment of income of assessee for year 1946-47, consequent upon finding recorded by Appellate Assistant Commissioner in proceedings for assessment of assessee's income relating to year 1947-48 - Whether reassessment u/s. 34 of 1922 Act was valid in law - Held, HC was in error in holding that a finding or direction by an appellate authority in an order relating to assessment of one year may warrant the avoidance of the bar of limitation u/s. 34(1) of the Act against initiation of proceedings for assessment for another assessment year against same assessee or against another assessee - Court finding within second proviso to s. 34(3) must be necessary for giving relief in respect of assessment of year - Answer recorded by HC cannot be sustained - Appeal allowed.
