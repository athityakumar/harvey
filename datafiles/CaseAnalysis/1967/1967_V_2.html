State of Madras v V. P. S. A. Narayana Nadar and Company

Supreme Court of India

04 May 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, J.C. Shah, S.M. Sikri

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 67; 1968 (21) STC 25

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Keywords: Madras General Sales Tax Act, 1939, Madras Generals Sales Tax Act, 1959, Madras General Sales Tax Rules, 1959

Summary: Sales Tax - French coffee - Liability to pay tax - Tax paid - Respondent assessee was dealer in various commodities including coffee powder - Commercial Tax Officer assessed him on taxable turnover of Rs. 17,40,960.52 - Respondent's case was that local sales were Rs. 41,154.32 and outside sales were Rs. 9,693.93, he contended that local sales were made from his local purchases of coffee seeds that amounted to Rs. 57,952.22 - Commercial Tax Officer, assessed respondent on turnover of Rs. 28,817.46 at 5 per cent, and held that it represented first sales of coffee powder within State - Respondent preferred appeal to Appellate Assistant Commissioner of Commercial Taxes and contended that he was not last purchaser in State and that he was not liable to pay tax on French coffee turnover of Rs. 28,817.46 - Appellate Assistant Commissioner found that AO had assessed on some proportion his purchases made from outside State, thus remanded matter to Commercial Tax Officer for fresh assessment - Further appeal before Sales Tax Appellate Tribunal had held that item of turnover should be deleted from taxable turnover - Appellants revision petition was dismissed by HC in limine - Hence, instant appeal - Whether decision of HC was maintainable and decision of Appellate Tribunal so far as it concerned turnover of assessee relating to French coffee was o be set aside - Held, there was no clear finding by Appellate Tribunal quantity of coffee apportioned as French coffee has already suffered tax in State under its order as regarded non-taxability of French coffee under item 33 was obscure - Order of Madras HC was set aside as also order of Sales Tax Appellate Tribunal case was remanded to Sales Tax Appellate Tribunal for hearing appeal afresh and determining it in accordance with law - Appellant was to pay costs of instant appeal - Appeal disposed of.
