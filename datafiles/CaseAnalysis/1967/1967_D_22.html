Abhinandan Jha and Others v Dinesh Mishra

Supreme Court of India

17 April 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

C.A. Vaidyialingam, Mohammad Hidayatullah

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 445; 1967 ACC 306; AIR 1968 SC 117; 1968 ALJ 373; 1968 CRLJ 97; [1967] 3 S.C.R. 668

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Criminal Procedure Code, ss. 156 and 173 - Magistrate directed police to submit charge sheet even when final report after s. 173 has been filed - Whether the Magistrate has power to direct police to file a charge sheet after report has been filed u/s. 173? - Held, no - Formation of an opinion as to whether or not there is a case to place the accused on trial, has been left to the investigating officer - Magistrate can at most he can ask police to carry out further investigation u/s. 156 (3) if not satisfied by final report - There is no power, expressly or impliedly conferred, under the Code, on a Magistrate to call upon the police to submit a charge-sheet, when they have sent a report u/s. 169, that there is no case made out for sending tip an accused for trial - Appeal allowed.
