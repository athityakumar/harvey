Calcutta Insurance, Limited v Their Workmen

Supreme Court of India

06 February 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

G.K. Mitter, Vishishtha Bhargava

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 33; AIR 1967 SC 1286; 1967 (14) FLR 345; 1967 (2) LLJ 1; [1967] 2 S.C.R. 596

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Delhi Shops and Establishments Act, 1954, ss. 11(b), 22 - Uttar Pradesh Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Factories Act, 1948, s.79 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 10(1)(d) - Award passed against appellant by tribunal with respect to scales of pay, dearness allowance, adjustments in scale, privilege and sick leave and gratuity - Whether tribunal can direct employer to give leaves in excess of prescribed limits ? - Tribunal could direct that employees should have leave in excess of limits specified in West Bengal Shops and Establishments Act, 1963 - Employees were enjoying leave at a rate which exceeded limits prescribed - Held, leave rules should be modified to extent that privilege leave would be allowable at rate of 30 days for each completed year of service with a right to accumulate same up to 60 days; and sick leave at rate of 15 days per year with full pay with right to accumulate same up to three months - Award shall stand modified in view of divided success - Appeal partly allowed.
