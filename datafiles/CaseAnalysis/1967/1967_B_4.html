B. M. Lall (Dead) By LRs v Dunlop Rubber and Company Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

18 July 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Bachawat, Vishishtha Bhargava, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 236; AIR 1968 SC 175; [1968] 1 S.C.R. 23

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - West Bengal Premises Tenancy Act, 1956, ss. 13(1)(f) and 105 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 52 - Indian Easements Act, 1882 - Whether officer to whom a flat is allotted by company would occupy same as a licensee or as a tenant? - Held, a service occupation is a particular kind of license whereby a servant is required to live in premises for better performance of his duties - Formerly, occupation of servant was regarded as a tenancy unless it was a service occupation of officer ceases not only on termination of his employment but also on his transfer from particular place and on his death, company is at liberty to allot any other flat to officer - Agreement on its true construction read in light of surrounding circumstances operates as a license and not as a tenancy - It creates no interest in land - It gives only a personal privilege or license of servant to occupy premises for greater convenience of his work - Appeal dismissed.
