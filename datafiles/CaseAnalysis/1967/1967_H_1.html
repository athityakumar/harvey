Swastik Oil Mills Limited v H. B. Munshi, Deputy Commissioner of Sales Tax, Bombay

Supreme Court of India

29 November 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

Vishishtha Bhargava, J.C. Shah, Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 95; AIR 1968 SC 843; [1968] 2 S.C.R. 492; 1968 (21) STC 383

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Sales Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Bombay Sales Tax Act, 1946, s. 22 - Bombay Sales Tax Act, 1953, s. 31 - Business Profits Tax Act, 1947, s. 11 - Madras General Sales Tax Act, 1959, ss. 76, 77 and 6(3) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Madras General Sales Tax Act, 1939, s. 12(2) - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 34(1)(b) - Bombay Sales Tax Act, 1959 - Revisional Power of Deputy Commr. of Sales Tax - Extent of - (A) Whether Deputy Commr. of Sales Tax entitled to act on conjecture or to institute any enquiry so as to include additional material in order to judge correctness of order sought to be revised? - Held, whenever a power is conferred on an authority to revise an order, authority is entitled to examine correctness, legality and propriety of order and to pass such suitable orders as authority may think fit in circumstances of particular case before it - There is no reason why authority should not be entitled to hold an enquiry or direct an enquiry to be held and, for that purpose, admit additional material - Proceedings for revision, if started suo motu, must not, of course, be based on a mere conjecture and there should be some ground for invoking revisional powers - (B) Whether Deputy Commr. of Sales Tax is entitled to exercise his revisional power suo motu? - Further held, in present case, notice issued by Deputy Commr. of Sales Tax, on face of it, discloses reasons which led him to take proceedings for exercising his revisional power suo motu, and it cannot be said on those facts that he was acting merely on conjecture - (C) Whether proceedings instituted are barred by time in exercise power of revision? - Further held, s. 22 of Act of 1946, s. 31 of Act of 1953 do not lay down any limitation for exercise of power of revision by a Deputy Commr. suo motu - No limitation must be read in two Acts - (D) Whether in instant case Deputy Commr. is encroaching upon jurisdiction of any other authority? - Held, this is not a case where powers are being exercised for purpose of assessing or reassessing an escaped turnover - It is one where revisional powers are sought to be exercised to correct what appears to be an incorrect order passed in appeal by Asst. Collector - Not a proceeding u/s. 11 A of Act of 1946 - In exercising his revisional powers Deputy Commr. is not encroaching upon jurisdiction of any other authority specially entrusted with taking such proceedings - Appeal dismissed.
