 Maneklal Chhotalal and Others v M. G. Makwana and Others

Supreme Court of India

02 March 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

C.A. Vaidyialingam, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, S.M. Sikri, K. Subba Rao, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 138; AIR 1967 SC 1373; [1967] 3 S.C.R. 65

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces; Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Municipalities & Local Governments - Practice & Procedure - Constitution - Govt. of India Act, 1935 - Town Planning Scheme, No 19 (Memnagar), Ahmedabad - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 14, 19(1) (f) and (g), and 31 - Bombay Town Planning Rules, 1955, rr. 3, 4,12, 21, 23, 33, 34 - Bombay Town Planning Act, 1954 - Validity of - Whether Bombay Town Planning Act, 1954 as originally framed, and also after its amendment, by Bombay Town Planning (Gujarat Amendment and Validating Provisions) Act, 1963 is valid? - A perusal of rules clearly shows that elaborate provisions have been made for giving as wide a publicity, as possible, at all stages, to public and to owners of land, who may be affected by scheme - They provide for objections being filed, and their being heard by authorities concerned - Rules also deal, elaborately with various other matters relating to scheme, dealt with by Act - Competence of State Legislature to enact same can be rested either on Entry No. 18 of List II and Entry No. 20 of List III - Relied on Sri Ram Narain Medhi v State of Bombay (1959 Sup 1 SCR 4891) - It is well-settled that these heads of legislation should not be construed in a narrow and pedantic sense but should be given a large and liberal interpretation - Held, contention of learned counsel for appellant that State Legislature was not competent to enact statute is unacceptable - Appeal dismissed.
