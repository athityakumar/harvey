Commissioner of Income Tax, West Bengal, and Another v George Henderson and Company Limited

Supreme Court of India

26 April 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, J.C. Shah, S.M. Sikri

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 319; [1967] 66 ITR 622

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income-tax Act, 1961 - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, ss.12B, 66 - Market value of shares - Avoidance of tax - Consideration for sale - Capital gains -Respondent/company purchased 1,500 shares of Jute Company Limited - During accounting year ending respondent was managing agent - 'G' had described as beneficial owner of respondent as he had purchased all but five shares of respondent - Market value of shares on that date was Rs.620 per share and difference of Rs.484 per share on 1,500 shares was capital gain arising from sale of shares u/s.12B of 1922 Act - Appellate Assistant Commissioner affirmed order of Income-tax Officer but varied quantum of capital gain at Rs.6,93,000 - Appellate Assistant Commissioner considered that respondent and transferee were directly connected and sale was effected with object of avoidance of tax and conditions of first proviso to s.12B(2) of Act, were satisfied and market value of shares on date of transfer should be taken as full value of consideration for sale - Respondent appealed to Income-tax Appellate Tribunal which dismissed appeal by its order - Appellate Tribunal assumed that sale was not effected with object of avoidance or reduction of liability of assessee u/s.12B of Act, and therefore first proviso to s.12B(2) of Act, did not apply to the case - Whether sum of Rs.6,93,000 had rightly assumed to be capital gains of assessee assessable u/s.12B of 1922 Act - Held, s.12B(2) of Act, cannot be construed to market value of asset transferred but expression only means full value of thing received by transferor in exchange for capital asset transferred by him - It was evident that legislature itself had made a distinction between two expressions full value of consideration and fair market value of capital asset transferred and it was provided that if certain conditions were satisfied in first proviso to s.12B(2) of Act, market value of asset transferred though not equivalent to full value of consideration for transfer deemed to be full value of consideration - Full value of consideration cannot be construed as market value but as price bargained for by parties to sale - Appellate Assistant Commissioner assessed tax on footing that first proviso to s.12B(2) of Act, applied and therefore market value of shares must be taken to be full value of consideration for transfer - Appeal allowed.
