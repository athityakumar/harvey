State of Maharashtra v Dr. M. N. Kaul (Deceased By His Legal Representatives) and Another

Supreme Court of India

28 March 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, C.A. Vaidyialingam

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 371; AIR 1967 SC 1634; [1968] 38 Comp Cas 1; 1967 MahLJ 998; 1968 (1) MLJ(SC) 74; 1967 MPLJ 937

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Sales Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Bombay Sales Tax Act, 1959- Court granted conditional stay of operation of an assessment order passed by Sales Tax Office and of notice of demand issued by him against 'D' - Stay was granted on condition that 'D' must furnish bank guarantee in favour of Registrar of Supreme Court - 'D' furnished guarantee and it was accepted by Court and Sales Tax Officer - State filed petition stating that order of stay had come to an end and amount became payable - State, therefore, sought to recover amount from guarantor - As there was a deficit of some sum, State asked to be paid that amount from security for costs deposited by 'D' in Court - Whether guarantee is enforceable - That depends upon terms under which guarantor bound himself - Under law he cannot be made liable for more than he has undertaken - Guarantee was accepted by party concerned as well as court and it is impossible to ignore time limit which is an integral part of guarantee - Contention of bank that guarantee is no longer enforceable is therefore right - Notices of motion must fail and they are dismissed.
