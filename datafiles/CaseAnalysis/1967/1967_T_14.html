Workmen of Brahmputra Tea Estate, Represented By Assam Chah v Incoming Management of Braumputra Teaestate and Others

Supreme Court of India

25 September 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

C.A. Vaidyialingam, Mohammad Hidayatullah, Vishishtha Bhargava

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 215; AIR 1968 SC 514; 1967 (15) FLR 390; 1968 LabIC 521; 1969 (2) LLJ 685; [1968] 1 S.C.R. 626

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Industrial Disputes (Amendment) Act, 1957, Industrial Disputes (Amendment) Act, 1956

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes (Amendment) Act, 1956 - Industrial Disputes (Amendment) Act, 1957 - Uttar Pradesh Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - (A) Whether view of Labour Court that first respondent is not liable for claims of workmen is erroneous? - First respondent cannot be considered to be a successor-in-interest of Tea Company nor can he be considered to claim through Receiver, or Liquidator - Even on basis that first respondent is considered to be a person, to whom ownership of undertaking has been transferred, it will be seen that claims of workmen will have to be considered as against Tea Company, in accordance with s. 25 FF of Act - Held, workmen have no claim towards First Respondent - (B) - Whether even if it is held that first respondent is not liable, Labour Court, which had ample jurisdiction, in this regard, should have issued notice, either to Receiver, appointed in suit, or Official Liquidator, to both of them, and proceeded to investigate and adjudicate upon claims of workmen? - Even after negativing claims, of workmen, as against first respondent, Labour Court should not have merely closed proceedings, by holding that reference has lapsed - Further held, Labour Court should have issued notices to Receiver, or Liquidator or to both, and, in their presence, should have considered question as to whether workmen were entitled to claim any relief - Appeal partly allowed.
