S. Sankappa and Others v Income Tax Officer, Central Circle Ii, Bangalore

Supreme Court of India

14 December 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Vishishtha Bhargava, Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 366; AIR 1968 SC 816; [1968] 68 ITR 760; [1968] 2 S.C.R. 674

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 155 and 297(2)(a) - Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 26A and 35(5) - Applicability of s. 35(5) of 1922 Act or s. 155 of 1961 Act - Return was filed as registered firm and simultaneously application for registration made - Application for registration of firm was allowed in appeal and that fresh assessment of the firm was invoked - S. 297(2)(a) deal with pending assessments at the enactment of Income Tax Act, 1961 - Held, s. 35(5) applicable to set aside the assessment as unregistered firm and impose tax on the partners in their individual assessment - Appeal dismissed.
