 State of Mysore v H. Sanjeeviah

Supreme Court of India

16 January 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, K. Subba Rao, S.M. Sikri, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, C.A. Vaidyialingam

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 474; AIR 1967 SC 1189; [1967] 2 S.C.R. 673

Case Digest

Subject: Environment, Wildlife & Animal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Environment, Wildlife & Animal - Constitution - Mysore Forest Act, 1900, s. 37- Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 301 and 305 - Whether rules framed under Mysore Forest Act is ultra virus constitution? - Power conferred upon State Govt. is merely 'to regulate transit' of forest produce and not to restrict it - art. 301 in terms prohibits imposition of any restriction on trade, commerce and intercourse throughout territory of India, and by enactment of two provisos clearly a restriction is imposed upon freedom of trade - Held, art. 304(b) exempts from operation of art. 301 reasonable restrictions on freedom of trade, commerce and intercourse with or within State as may be required in public interest - There is no evidence of an enquiry made by State before provisos were framed, and no case is made out that they are reasonable restrictions on freedom of trade, commerce and intercourse imposed in public interest - Proviso to rule enacted by State Govt. deemed to be invalid as infringing guarantee u/art. 301 on freedom of trade, commerce and intercourse - Appeal dismissed.
