Raja Bahadur Giriwar Prasad Narain Singh v Dukhu Lal Das and Others

Supreme Court of India

20 April 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

Vishishtha Bhargava, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, G.K. Mitter

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 440; AIR 1968 SC 90; [1967] 3 S.C.R. 759

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Land Registration Act, 1876

Summary: Land & Property - Bihar Land Reforms Act, 1950 -Constitution of India, 1950 - Land Registration Act, 1876 - Decree - Lease - Ownership - Appeal raised a question of interpretation regarding the provisions of the 1950 Act - Act was declared void by HC - Objections were filed o behalf of the defendant and the plaintiff - HC decided an appeal holding that defendant no. 1 had no rights under which he could grant the lease to the plaintiff and therefore liable to refund not only the sum furnished as security but also the sum which he had realised from the plaintiff as lease money - Decree of the trial court was set aside and defendant no. 2 was held entitled to realise the lease money - Thus, defendant no. 1 had come up to this Court in this appeal on certificate granted by the HC - Whether, for the year 1952, the lease money was payable to defendant No. 1 or to defendant no. 2 by the plaintiff, and this question obviously depends on whether defendant No. 1 was still the owner of the estate when he gave the lease to the plaintiff on 12-04-1952 and continued to be so until 13-06-1952, or whether be had ceased to be, the owner of the property with effect from 14-11-1951, and the property from that date vested in defendant no. 2 - Held, the Court had given an opportunity to defendant no. 2 to produce the issues of the newspapers - Inspite of opportunity given, defendant no. 2 could not produce them - Failure to produce issues of the newspapers in which the notification were published lead to a conclusion that there must not have any such publication - Omission brought about non-compliance with the mandatory provision of s. 3(2) requiring publication in at least two issues of two newspapers, with the result that s. 4(a) of the Act did not become applicable at that time and, consequently, it must be held that defendant no. 1 continued to be the proprietor and was not divested of his rights in the estate, by this notification at that stage - No material was forthcoming to show that that notification was ever published in any newspapers even subsequently in the lower courts, the case proceeded on the basis of the admission by defendant no. 1 himself that he was dispossessed on 13-06-1952 and it was with effect from that date that he was divested of his proprietary rights - Consequently, he had the full right to grant the lease to the plaintiff on 12-04-1952 and the rights under that lease were exercised by the plaintiff during the period when defendant no. 1 was still the proprietor - Lease-money was in these circumstances, rightly realised by defendant no. 1 from the plaintiff - Defendant no. 2, in which the rights did not vest until 13-06-1952, had no right to realise the lease-money for the year 1952, because, by the time the rights vested in defendant no. 2, the collection of Bidi leaves for that year had been completed by the plaintiff - On this ground, defendant no. 1 was entitled to succeed in respect of the decree for the sum of Rs. 22,500/- which he was not liable to pay, so that the decree against him has to be vacated - Instead, the decree for this sum had to be passed against defendant no. 2, as the sum of Rs. 22, 500/- realised by defendant no. 2 from the plaintiff was not justified tinder law, because the rights of the lessor had not vested in defendant no. 2 for the year 1952 - Appeal allowed.
