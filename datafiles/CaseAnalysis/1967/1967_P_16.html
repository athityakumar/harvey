 Pritam Singh Chahil v State of Punjab and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 February 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, Vishishtha Bhargava, G.K. Mitter, J.C. Shah, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 130; AIR 1967 SC 930; 1967 (2) SCJ 325; [1967] 2 S.C.R. 536

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Rent Control - Bihar Land Reforms Act, 1950 - PEPSU Tenancy and Agricultural Lands Act, 1955, ss. 32 A, 32 D, 32 E, 32 FF, 32 G and 32 P - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 13(2), 14, 19, 31 A and 32 - PEPSU Tenancy and Agricultural Lands Rules, 1958, rr. 23 A and 28 - Whether provisions of r. 28 of Rules are inconsistent with provisions of s. 32 G of Act and therefore art. 31 A is not a bar against enforcement of petitioner's fundamental right u/arts. 19, 13 (2) and 14 of Constitution? - Held, petitioner's land is being acquired under a law of acquisition and that petitioner is precluded, by reason of art. 31 A of Constitution, from questioning validity of Act or Rules made thereunder on ground that his fundamental right u/arts. 19, 14 or 31 of Constitution is infringed - Rules are not ultra vires Act - S. 32 FF is valid and as land acquired is admittedly above ceiling, second proviso to art. 31 A has no application - Petition dismissed.
