Modi and Company v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

07 December 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, J.C. Shah, Vishishtha Bhargava

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 160; AIR 1969 SC 9; [1968] 2 S.C.R. 565

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Keywords: Forward Contracts (Regulation ) Act, 1952

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration Act, 1940 - Calcutta Municipal Act, 1951 - Forward Contracts (Regulation) Act, 1952 - Arbitration - Contract - Delivery - Refund - Appellant made an offer for sale to the respondent for certain number of bags on the terms and conditions mentioned in a letter - Offer was accepted by the Director- General, Supplies & Disposals on behalf of the respondent - Appellant deposited with the Reserve Bank of India a certain sum as required by the acceptance letter - Appellant intimated to the respondent that the contract was void and illegal and requested that the security deposit should be refunded - Case of the appellant was that the contract was in violation of the provisions of the 1952 Act - Respondent incurred extra expenditure and after giving credit to the appellant for the amount of security deposit a certain sum still remained due to be paid by the appellant to the respondent - Appellant failed to pay, the respondent took recourse to the arbitration cl. 21 of the contract and appointed an Arbitrator to determine the dispute between the parties regarding the agreement - Before the Arbitrator could give his award, the appellant filed an application before the HC under s. 33 of the Arbitration Act praying for a declaration that the arbitration clause was illegal and void and for an injunction restraining the respondent from prosecuting the arbitration proceedings - HC held that the contract was a 'non-transferable specific delivery contract' and was not hit by the provisions of the 1952 Act and accordingly dismissed the application of the appellant - Whether the contract in question was a transferable or non- transferable specific delivery contract within the meaning of the 1952 Act - Held, Contractor shall not subject, transfer or assign the contract or any part thereof without the written permission of the Purchaser - It was not contemplated by the parties that the rights under the contract should be transferred either by the buyers or by the sellers - On a proper construction of the terms of the contract and having regard to the surrounding circumstances there was an implied agreement between the parties that the rights and liabilities under the contract were not to be transferred and the Bill of Lading relating to the contract was also not to be transferred - Hence, contract in question was a non-transferable specific delivery contract within the meaning of s. 2(f) of the 1952 Act and the contract was not hit by the notification issued by the Central Govt. u/s. 15(1) of the 1952 Act - Appeal dismissed.
