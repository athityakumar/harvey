Esthuri Aswanthiah v Commissioner of Income Tax, Mysore

Supreme Court of India

18 April 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, S.M. Sikri, Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 255; AIR 1968 SC 36; [1967] 66 ITR 478; [1967] 3 S.C.R. 681

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922 - Groundnuts trade - Notice of reassessment - Books of account - Jurisdiction - Lost or Stolen - Appellant/trader in groundnuts was taxed - Assessee pleaded that his books of account upto June 30, were lost and that the amount of Rs. 1,87,000 represented cash brought from an iron safe kept in his house - Income-tax Officer found that in every previous year assessee pleaded that his books of account were either lost or stolen in succeeding year - Order passed by Officer was set aside by Appellate Commissioner - Income-tax Officer issued a notice of reassessment - Assessee submitted petition to HC that the notice was without jurisdiction and prayed for quashing - HC dismissed the petition - Hence instant appeal - Whether s.66(5) of 1922 act requires Tribunal to dispose the case based of judgment of HC - Held, yes - S. 66(5) of the Indian Income-tax Act, 1922, requires the Tribunal on receiving a copy of the judgment of the High Court to pass such orders as are necessary to dispose of the case conformably to such judgment - HC has held, and SC agree with HC , that the judgment of Tribunal based on no reasoning and is on that account speculative - It is not to be implied that order of Appellate Assistant Commissioner is confirmed - But it was necessary to give, certain effective directions, lest a bald order of dismissal of appeal may result in injustice, especially when the assessee had not a fair trial of his case before Tribunal - Appeal dismissed
