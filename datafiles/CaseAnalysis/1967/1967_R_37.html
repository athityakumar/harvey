Ram Adhar Singh v Ramroop Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

26 October 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

C.A. Vaidyialingam, Mohammad Hidayatullah, Vishishtha Bhargava

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 185; AIR 1968 SC 714; 1968 ALJ 46; 1968 (2) SCJ 480; [1968] 2 S.C.R. 95

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Uttar Pradesh Consolidation of Holding Act, 1954

Summary: Land & Property - Uttar Pradesh Consolidation of Holding Act, 1954 - Uttar Pradesh Zamindari Abolition and Land Reforms Act, 1950, s. 209 - Uttar Pradesh Consolidation of Holdings Act, 1953, ss. 3(11), 4, 5, 5(2)(a)(b), 6(1), 8, 8 A, 9(2), 11, 11 A, 24, 28, 40, 48, 49 and 52 - Respondent, claiming to be a bhumindar of land, in question, instituted suit, out of which second appeal arose, against appellant, for recovery of possession of property, on ground that appellant was a trespasser and that he was not entitled to remain, in possession of property - Trial Court, as well as Appellate Court, have held that respondent was bhumidar, and appellant has not established his tenancy right in property - HC has agreed with conclusions - (A) Whether amended s. 5 applies to proceedings and proceedings are all matters now falling for adjudication, within exclusive jurisdiction of authorities constituted under UPCH Act? - Held, under amended s. 5, there is no direct reference to 'suits for possession of land' - 'Suits for possession', as such, has not been expressly referred to, in new s. 5 but expression 'every suit and proceeding in respect of declaration of rights or interest in any land are comprehensive enough to take in suits for possession of land, because, before a claim for possession is accepted, Court will have, necessarily, to adjudicate upon right or interest of plaintiff - Therefore, suit, instituted by Respondent is covered by amended s. 5 of UPCH Act - Various provisions, contained in UPCH Act, also clearly indicate that disputes, of nature which exists between parties in present litigation, are all now within jurisdiction of authorities, constituted under UPCH Act, to adjudicate upon - Held, subject matter of dispute, between parties in this litigation, are all matters falling for adjudication, within purview of authorities, constituted under UPCH Act - (B) Whether provisions of amended s. 5 are ultra vires? - Held, State Legislature has not passed any legislation affecting jurisdiction of this Court - On other hand, what State Legislature has done is only to make provision in respect of matters, within its jurisdiction and to declare that a suit, instituted in a Court, within its area, has abated - Therefore, there is no question of Legislature of State having passed any legislation affecting jurisdiction of this Court - Petition allowed.
