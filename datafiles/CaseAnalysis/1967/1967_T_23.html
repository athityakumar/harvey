State of Gujarat v Jaswantlal Nathalal

Supreme Court of India

23 November 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, S.M. Sikri, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 163; AIR 1968 SC 700; 1968 CRLJ 803; 1968 (2) SCJ 334; [1968] 2 S.C.R. 408

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Breach of Trust - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 405, 406 and 409 - Respondent committed breach in respect of 40 bags of cement - Whether respondent is liable to be convicted for offence of breach of trust? - Where the Govt. sells cement to its contractor solely for the purpose of being used in connection with the construction work the circumstance does not make the transaction anything other than sale - After delivery of the cement the Govt. has neither any right nor dominion over it - Held, No after sale of cement, Govt. had no proprietary right over same - No fiduciary relationship subsisted between Govt. and respondent - Transaction of sale was effected - No case of entrustment made out as no right of Govt. remained after delivery of cement - Prosecution failed to prove the entrustment pleaded - No breach of trust - Appeal dismissed.
