 State of Mysore and Another v P. Narasing Rao

Supreme Court of India

31 August 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, R.S. Bachawat, G.K. Mitter, K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 208; AIR 1968 SC 349; 1968 (17) FLR 179; 1968 LabIC 360; 1968 (2) LLJ 120; [1968] 1 S.C.R. 407; 1967 SLR 885

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service

Summary: Service - States Reorganisation Act, 1956, s. 115 - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 14 and 16 - Whether respondent was entitled to higher pay scale? - Basis of promotion to higher grade was inter-State seniority list prepared under provisions of States Reorganisation Act - It was stated that seniority of respondent was not affected and he had not been deprived of any accrued benefits - Held, basis of promotion to higher grades was selection based on merit-cum-seniority - In other words, both matriculate and non-matriculate tracers were eligible for promotion on basis of inter-State seniority list prepared for this Department - Counsel on behalf of respondent is unable to make good his submission on this aspect of case - Appeal allowed.
