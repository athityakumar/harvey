Banarsi Das Ahluwalia v Chief Controlling Revenue Authority, Delhi

Supreme Court of India

28 September 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Shelat, S.M. Sikri, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 219; AIR 1968 SC 497; [1968] 1 S.C.R. 685

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Nature of document, Reference to HC

Summary: Administrative - Practice & Procedure - Indian Stamp Act, 1898, s. 38(2), 57, 57(1) - Nature of document - Imposition of penalty - Reference to HC - Legality - Appellant executed a trust deed and presented, which was impounded, for its registration forwarded to Collector for action u/s. 38(2) of the Act - Show cause notice was issued that document should be charged with deficient stamp duty and penalty - Aggrieved appellant filed a revision before respondent (Chief Controlling Revenue Authority), which was partly allowed reducing amount of deficit duty and penalty - Aggrieved appellant applied to respondent to state the case to HC u/s. 57(1) of the Act, which was rejected - Aggrieved appellant filed a petition before HC, which was dismissed in limine - Hence, instant appeal - Whether impugned order passed by HC was justified - Held, s. 57(1) of the Act imposed a duty on respondent to state a case when it raised a substantial question of law - S. 57 of the Act affords a remedy to citizen to have his case referred to HC against an order of a Revenue Authority imposing stamp duty and/or penalty provided the application involved a substantial question of law and imposed a corresponding obligation on respondent to refer it to HC for its opinion - Such a right and obligation could not be construed to depend upon any subsidiary circumstance such as the pendency of case before the Authority - When a, reference was made to Authority or the case has otherwise come to his notice, if an application was made u/s. 57(1) of the Act and it involved a substantial question of law, whether the case was pending or not, Authority was bound to state the case in compliance with its obligation - Authority erred refusing to state the case and HC was equally erred in summarily dismissing writ petition as the question whether document was a declaration of trust or was a deed of settlement was a substantial question of law - Thus, impugned orders of HC was set aside - Respondent was directed to state the case to HC u/s. 57(1) of the Act - Appeal allowed.
