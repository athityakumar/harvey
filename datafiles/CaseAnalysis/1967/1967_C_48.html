Commissioner of Income Tax, Calcutta v Rai Bahadur Hardutroy Motilal Chamaria

Supreme Court of India

07 April 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, J.C. Shah, S.M. Sikri

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 128; AIR 1968 SC 153; [1967] 66 ITR 443; [1967] 3 S.C.R. 508

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 31, 33 B,and 34 - Whether Appellate Assistant Commr. was within his authority in enhancing tax assessment of assessee? - Power of enhancement u/s. 31 (3) is restricted to subject matter of assessment or sources of income which have been considered by ITO from view point of taxability - It is not open to Appellate Assistant Commr. to travel outside record, i. e., return made by assessee or assessment order of ITO to find out new sources of income - Assessment order must manifest that ITO applied his mind to particular subject matter or particular source of income as to its taxability or otherwise and not to any incidental connection - Held, as ITO did not consider entry of Rs. 5,85,000 from view point of taxability therefore Appellate Assistant Commr. had no jurisdiction to enhance assessment - Appeal dismissed.
