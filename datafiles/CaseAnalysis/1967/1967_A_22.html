Tata Engineering and Locomotive Company Limited v Assistant Commissioner of Commercial Taxes and Another

Supreme Court of India

24 February 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, G.K. Mitter, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 105; AIR 1967 SC 1401; 1967 (46) ILR(Pat) 848; [1967] 2 S.C.R. 751; 1967 (19) STC 520

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Sales Tax; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Writ Under Constitution

Summary: Sales Tax - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 26 - Bihar Sales Tax Act, 1959 - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956 - Indian Contract Act, 1872 - Whether High Court ought in this case to have exercised jurisdiction and if it took jurisdiction whether any settled principle governing art. 226 would have been departed from? - Held, there are certain exceptions to exercise of jurisdiction u/art. 226 - One such exception is where action is being taken under an invalid law or arbitrarily without sanction of law - In such a case, HC may interfere to avoid hardship to a party which will be unavoidable if quick and more efficacious remedy envisaged by art. 226 were not allowed to be invoked - Present is an example of exceptional situation - Question was whether in law such sales could be regarded as in course of inter-State trade or commerce or outside sales, subject of course to claim of company being found on record to be good - It would certainly have avoided circuity of action and proved altogether more satisfactory if HC had considered whether sample transaction as illustrated by documents, disclosed a transaction of sale outside State of Bihar and not in course of inter-State trade or commerce - HC ought to have taken jurisdiction in this case at least to issue a rule nisi to see what Assistant Commr. had to say - Appeal allowed.
