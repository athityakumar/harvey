Mahendra Pratap Singh v Sarju Singh and Another

Supreme Court of India

20 November 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, C.A. Vaidyialingam

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 387; AIR 1968 SC 707; 1968 CRLJ 665; 1968 (2) SCJ 193; [1968] 2 S.C.R. 287

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Murder - Acquittal - Retrial - Sustainability - Appellant/accused was charged for murder, Trial Court acquitted accused on benefit of doubt - Revision application was filed by 3rd party before HC, upon which HC set aside order of Trial Court and ordered fresh Trial - Hence, instant appeal - Whether impugned order of HC was justified - Held, Judge reweighed evidence from his own point of view and reached inferences contrary to those of Sessions Judge on almost every point, which SC did not conceive to be his duty in dealing in revision with acquittal when Govt. had not chosen to file appeal against it - HC had not attended to rules laid down by SC and had acted in breach of them, D. Stephens v. Nosibolla, 951 Indlaw SC 4, relied on - SC was satisfied that Sessions Judge acted within his rights in deciding case which appeared also to be somewhat doubtful in many respects and HC was therefore in error in taking upon itself duty of hearing revision application as if it was appeal and setting aside acquittal not by convicting accused but reaching same result indirectly by ordering retrial - Therefore, judgment of HC could not be allowed to stand, order of retrial was revoked and acquittal was restored - Appeal allowed.
