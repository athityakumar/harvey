Udhoji Shrikishandas v Commissioner of Income Tax, Bombay City I

Supreme Court of India

14 April 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, S.M. Sikri, Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 310; [1967] 66 ITR 462

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, ss.33, 34 - Migration - Money-lending business - Value of assets - Assessment of income-tax - Assessee migrated from Pakistan to India and set up a money-lending business in Bombay - Assessee opened a set of books in name of his wife and made credit entries totalling Rs.7,85,712 - In proceedings for assessment of income-tax, Income-tax Officer accepted claim of assessee that out of amount credited, Rs.4,51,843 represented value of assets brought by assessee when he migrated to India and rejected his claim for balance - Income Tax Officer in proceedings for reassessment commenced u/s.34 of 1922 Act, brought to tax Rs.79,989 in Assessment Year Rs.1,89,800 - Tribunal assumed that assessee had failed to prove claim that Rs.55,293 out of amount of Rs.64,880 assessed to tax represented value of ornaments, which the assessee claimed were sold by him after the death of his wife - Tribunal set aside order of Appellate Assistant Commissioner and directed that Rs.55,293 be brought to tax in A/y - Whether credit of Rs. 55, 293 in account of wife of assessee represent the sale proceeds of her gold ornaments and whether sum of Rs. 55, 293 could be assumed as undisclosed income of assessee - Held, Tribunal had not adjudicated upon truth of case of assessee adduced by assessee - U/s. 66(1) of Act, Tribunal observed that there was no conclusive proof of assessee's version that gold ornaments were melted and sold - Documentary evidence tendered by assessee, Appellate Assistant Commissioner had accepted claim of assessee relating to sale of 'G' ornaments - All the evidence in favour of and against assessee - Income Tax authorities to prove that a particular receipt was taxable - Item of receipt was taxable as income, evidence in light of statements made by assessee, his conduct and probabilities, but in arriving at its conclusion there must be a fair and reasonably full review of evidence - Order accordingly.
