 Rajasthan State Electricity Board, Jaipur v Mohan Lal and Others

Supreme Court of India

03 April 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

Vishishtha Bhargava, K. Subba Rao, J.C. Shah, J.M. Shelat, G.K. Mitter

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 23; AIR 1967 SC 1857; 1970 (21) FLR 59; 1968 (1) LLJ 257; 1968 (1) SCJ 461; [1967] 3 S.C.R. 377; 1967 SLR 373

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Electricity & Energy; Service

Summary: Service - Electricity & Energy - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 12, 16, 19(1)(g), 298 - Electricity Supply Act 54, of 1948, s. 78A - Promotion - Ambit of Board - Challenged - Supply of electricity in State was controlled by State Govt. named Electrical and Mechanical Department which was later constituted as Appellant/Electricity Board of Rajasthan on 1-7-1957 under the Act - Respondent No.1 and other respondents Nos. 4 to 14 was permanent employees of Electrical and Mechanical Department - After constitution of appellant/Board most of employees, including all respondents, were provisionally placed at disposal of board by notification issued by Govt. to exercise its powers u/s. 78A of the Act- Respondent was deputed in Public works Dept. by State Govt. - Later respondent No.1 reverted to appellant/Board from Public Works Department - When respondent No.1 was working in Public Works Department respondent No. 4 to 14 had been promoted by appellant/board as Assistant Engineers while respondent No.1 was promoted as Assistant Engineer in Public Works Department - Respondent 1 claimed that he was also entitled to be promoted as Assistant Engineer under appellant/board, because some respondents promoted were junior to him and was entitled for promotion and made request before appellant/Board as well to State Govt. which was turned down - Aggrieved respondent No.1 filed writ petition before HC which was allowed and quashed order of promotions of respondents 4 to 14 and issued direction to board to consider promotions afresh taking claims of respondent 1 - Hence, instant appeal - Whether appellant/Board fall under ambit of art.12 of the Constitution and order of HC was just and proper - Held, that expression 'other authorities' in art. 12 of the Constitution would include all constitutional or statutory authorities on whom powers were conferred by law - Therefore, circumstance that board under the Act was required to carry on some activities of nature of trade or commerce did not give any indication that board must be excluded from scope of word 'State' as used in art. 12 of the Constitution - On other hand, there were provisions in the Act which clearly show that powers conferred on board include power to give directions, disobedience of which was punishable as criminal offence - In said circumstances, Court did not consider it at all necessary to examine that board could not be held to be agent or instrument of Govt. - Appellant/board was clearly authority to which provisions of Part III of the Constitution were applicable - Court already held that, in dealing with case of respondent No. 1, board did not treat him on terms of equality with respondent Nos. 4 to 14 and did not afford to him opportunity for being considered for promotion to which he was entitled on that basis - Hence, HC was right in allowing petition of respondent No.1 - Appeal dismissed.
