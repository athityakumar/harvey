Union of India and Others v Messrs Indo-Afghan Agencies Limited

Supreme Court of India

22 November 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, S.M. Sikri, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 411; AIR 1968 SC 718; 1968 (2) SCJ 889; [1968] 2 S.C.R. 366

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax

Keywords: Defence of India Rules, 1939

Summary: Customs - Indirect Tax - Imports and Exports (Control) Act, 1947, s. 3 - Export Promotion Scheme - Licence - On 10.10.1962, the Textile Commissioner published a scheme called the Export Promotion Scheme providing incentives to exporters of woollen goods - Said scheme was extended by a Trade Notice dt. 1.1.1963, to exports of woollen goods to Afghanistan - Respondents, a firm dealing in woollen goods exported woollen goods of the F.O.B. value of Rs. 5,03,47, 173/- to Afghanistan - The Deputy Director issued an Import Entitlement Certificate only for Rs. 1,99,459/- to the respondents - Respondents made representations to the Deputy Director and to the Union Government that they be granted Import Entitlement Certificate for the full F.O.B. value of the goods exported - Said representations failed to produce any response - Respondents filed writ petition u/art. 226 of the Constitution against said order - HC held that the Export Promotion Scheme specifically provided for granting certificates to import materials of the 'value equal to 100% of the F.O.B. value of the goods exported' , and the respondents were entitled to obtain import licences for an amount equal to 100% of the F.O.B. value, unless it was found on enquiry duly made under clause 10 of the Scheme against respondents - The Union of India, the Textile Commissioner and the Joint Chief Controller of Imports and Exports filed appeal against said order - Whether HC rightly held that the respondents were entitled to obtain import licences for an amount equal to 100% of the F.O.B value? - Held, the Government was not exempt from liability to carry out the representation made by it as to its future conduct and it cannot on some undefined and undisclosed ground of necessity or expediency fail to carry out the promise solemnly made by it, nor claim to be the judge of its own obligation to the citizen on an ex parte appraisement of the circumstances in which the obligation had arisen - In the instant case, enquiry under clause 10 of scheme was not made against respondents - There was no charge for 'over-invoicing' proved against respondents - Moreover, Textile Commissioner was not the sole judge of quantum of import license to be granted to exporter - Respondents were not seeking to enforce any contractual right, they were seeking to enforce compliance with the obligation which was laid upon the Textile Commissioner by the terms of the Scheme - The claim of the respondents was rounded upon the equity which arose in their favour as a result of the representation made on behalf of the Government in the Export Promotion Scheme, and the action taken by the respondents acting upon the representation - Hence, Textile Commissioner and Joint Chief Controller of Imports and Exports directed to issue to respondents import certificates for total amount equal to 100% F.O.B. value of goods exported by them - Appeals dismissed.
