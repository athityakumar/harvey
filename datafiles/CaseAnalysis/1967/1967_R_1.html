R. B. Bansilal Abirchand Firm v Commissioner of Income Tax, M. P

Supreme Court of India

22 November 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

Vishishtha Bhargava, J.C. Shah, Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 356; [1968] 70 ITR 74

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s.34(1)(b) - Income-tax Amendment Act, 1948 - Reopening assessment - Payment of interest - Taxable profits - Assessee firm was financing another firm, 'firm no. 2' - Firm no. 2 used to pay interest on advances made to it by assessee firm and assessee firm was assessed in relevant A/Y - Amount of interest received from firm no. 2 was treated as income accrued to assessee firm in capacity of a partner in firm no. 2 - ITO (Income tax Officer) disallowed interest paid to assessee firm as an expenditure on ground that it was interest paid to a partner - Tribunal allowed claim for interest as an expenditure to extent of Rs. 5,10,788/- as if that payment of interest was a payment to a banker - ITO decided to take action u/s. 34(1)(b) of 1922 Act in order to include amount of interest in taxable profits and losses of assessee firm - Assessee objected the grounds on which ITO had reopened the assessment u/s. 34(1)(b) of 1922 Act - Tribunal and HC found that ITO had jurisdiction to proceed u/s. 34(1)(b) of 1922 Act - Assessee filed instant appeal to challenge order of HC - Whether proceedings u/s. 34(1)(b) of 1922 Act was applicable - Held, s. 34(1)(b) of 1922 Act was not applicable because in that case it was not possible to hold that as a result of information received in assessment proceedings of another company, ITO discovered that income of assessee concerned had escaped assessment - Information that came into possession of ITO as a result of assessment proceedings of another company led to discovery that income of assessee had escaped assessment was not result of that information - HC was correct to state that ITO came into possession of information on basis of which he could initiate proceedings u/s. 34(1)(b) of 1922 Act - No fresh facts were brought to notice of ITO to justify his proceeding u/s. 34(1)(b) of 1922 Act - HC rightly pointed out that there were enough materials on which ITO could initiate proceedings u/s. 34 of 1922 Act for three A/Y - Information came into possession of ITO was hold to justify resort to s. 34 of 1922 Act - Appeal dismissed.
