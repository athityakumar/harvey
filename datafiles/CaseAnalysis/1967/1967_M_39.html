 Messrs Haji Esmail Noor Mohammad and Company and Others v Competent Officer, Lucknow and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 March 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, Vishishtha Bhargava, G.K. Mitter, J.C. Shah, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 146; AIR 1967 SC 1244; [1967] 3 S.C.R. 134

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property

Keywords: Evacuee Property & Displaced Persons

Summary: Constitution - Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Constitution Of India, 1950, Art. 19(1)(f), 32, 226 [before Constitution (Fifteenth Amendment) Act of 1963], and 227 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, ss. 11, 115 and 148 - Evacuee Interest (Separation) Act, 1951, ss. 2(a), 8, 13,and 14 - Administration of Evacuee Property Act 1950, ss. 7, 8(2), 8(2-A), 24,and 27 - Uttar Pradesh Administration of Evacuee Property Ordinance, 1949, ss. 5, 7 and 2(d) - Administration of Evacuee Property (Chief Commissioners' Provinces) Ordinance, 1949, s. 5 - Administration of Evacuee Property Ordinance, 1949, s. 8(2) - Administration of Evacuee Property (Amendment) Act, 1960, s. 2 (E) - Appellate Officer under Evacuee Interest (Separation) Act, 1951 - Appointed by State Govt. of U.P. - Had his main office at Mussorie in U.P. - Heard an appeal at Allahabad against decision of a Competent Officer and dismissed it there - Later his main office was shifted to Delhi, a place outside territorial jurisdiction of High Court of U.P. - (A) Whether HC of Allahabad has jurisdiction to issue a writ under art. 226 of Constitution against Appellate Officer? - Held, to hold that such an authority which was appointed by State Govt. and held office, entertained and disposed of appeals within State was outside jurisdiction of HC was to carry technicality beyond reasonable limits - Such an appellate authority was located within territorial limits of HC for purpose of disposing of appeals under Act - Legally and factually appellate authority was located in State though for convenience it also held office in New Delhi - HC therefore had jurisdiction to issue a writ in appropriate cases against such an authority under art. 226 - (B) Whether any fundamental right of petitioners were violated with regard to interest of evacuee in order to entitle them to maintain writ petition? - Further held, petitioners had no interest in evacuee property which was vested in Custodian - Hence they had no fundamental rights in regard to interest of evacuee to entitle them to maintain writ petition before Supreme Court - Appeal remanded.
