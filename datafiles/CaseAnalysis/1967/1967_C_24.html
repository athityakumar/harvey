Commissioner Of Income Tax, Hyderabad v M/S. Motor And General Stores (P.) Ltd

Supreme Court of India

02 May 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, S.M. Sikri, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 279; AIR 1968 SC 200; [1967] 66 ITR 692; 1968 (1) MLJ(SC) 26; [1967] 3 S.C.R. 876

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 10(2)(vii) - Sale of Goods Act, ss. 2(10) and 4(1) - Indian Contract Act, s. 78 - Transfer of Property Act, ss. 118 and 119 - Whether the nature of the transaction is sale or exchange? - Held, operative part of the document executed for the transaction confirms that there was an exchange of the properties - It is not open to the income tax authorities to deduce the nature of the document from the purported intention or to consider the substance of the matter - There was no price paid or promised to be paid for the transfer of the properties - Answered in favour of the assessee - Appeal dismissed.
