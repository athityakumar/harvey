Sindhu Resettlement Corporation Limited v Industrial Tribunal of Gujarat and Others

Supreme Court of India

13 September 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

Vishishtha Bhargava, Mohammad Hidayatullah, C.A. Vaidyialingam

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 209; AIR 1968 SC 529; 1968 (16) FLR 307; 1968 LabIC 526; 1968 (1) LLJ 834; [1968] 1 S.C.R. 515

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: (A) Labour & Industrial Law - Constitution of India, 1950 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Uttar Pradesh Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Retrenchment - Reinstatement - Industrial tribunal - Respondent no. 3 was employed by a Resettlement Corporation which was the appellant as an Accounts Clerk on a salary and site allowance - Site allowance was discontinued - Govt. of India decided to develop a place as a port and a subsidiary company was formed by the appellant in which one of the principal shareholders was the appellant - Subsidiary Company wanted some trained employees and amongst others services of respondent no. 3 were placed at its disposal by the appellant - Respondent no. 3 was appointed by order as Accounts Clerk - Father of Respondent no. 3 died and he was granted leave by the appellant - Respondent no. 3 worked for a certain period after which his services were terminated after payment of retrenchment compensation and dues payable to him - Respondent No. 3 went to the office of the appellant, reported himself for duty and requested that he might be given posting orders in the appellant corporation - Respondent no. 3 was informed by the appellant about its inability to re-employ him on that post as it was permanently filed up - Respondent No. 3 demanded retrenchment compensation from the appellant also - It was refused - Case was taken up by respondent no. 2 - Conciliation proceedings took place and the dispute was referred to Industrial Tribunal - Tribunal gave its award directing reinstatement of respondent No. 3 and payment of back wages - Petition was dismissed and appeal was filed by special leave - Whether respondent No. 3, having been given permanent appointment in sister concern of the appellant company and having obtained retrenchment compensation from that Company, could claim that he was still holding a post in the appellant corporation and could he therefore, claim reinstatement - Held, at the time of termination of respondent no. 3 services, he was given retrenchment compensation which he had accepted - If he had continued to be in the service of the appellant company he would not have been entitled to retrenchment compensation - Claim was halfhearted - Position taken up by respondent No. 3 himself thus shows that he was aware that his services under the appellant corporation had already come to an end - Services of respondent No. 3 under the appellant corporation had come to an end and, when he was retrenched by sister concern of appellant corporation, he could not claim reinstatement in the appellant corporation and hence could not claim reinstatement - Appeal dismissed.

(B) Labour & Industrial Law - Constitution of India, 1950 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Uttar Pradesh Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Retrenchment - Reinstatement - Industrial tribunal - Whether the dispute that was raised by respondent No. 3 as well as respondent No. 2 with the management of the appellant was confined to compensation for retrenchment and did not relate to the validity of the retrenchment or reinstatement, so that the Govt. of Gujarat had no jurisdiction to refer the dispute to the Industrial Tribunal which it did - Held, this ground was being taken for the first time in this Court and had not been raised at any earlier stage, so that it won't be allowed to be taken in this Court - Question of jurisdiction of the State Govt. to refer the demand for reinstatement for adjudication to the Tribunal was specifically urged in the HC and the HC actually dealt with it in its judgment, dismissed the petition filed on behalf of the appellant - HC clearly mentioned that the counsel for the appellant contended that the Industrial Tribunal had no jurisdiction as the question referred to it and which it was called upon to adjudicate relating to reinstatement of respondent No. 3 in the service of the Corporation would not fall within the scope of item 3 in the Second Schedule to the Act - Appeal dismissed.
