Konchada Ramamurthy Subudhi and Another v Gopinath Naik

Supreme Court of India

07 December 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri, K.S. Hegde, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 159; AIR 1968 SC 919; 1968 (34) CutLT 733; [1968] 2 S.C.R. 559

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Rent Control Act - Whether compromise decree created a lease or a licence and whether an agreement between the parties creates the relationship of landlord and tenant or merely that of licensor and licensee, decisive consideration being intention of parties? - Fact that decree holder had brought a suit for ejectment of judgment debtor and that a compromise was entered into in that suit is important - It is difficult to impute to him an intention to create a fresh tenancy while fact that he brought suit shows that his intention was to eject judgment debtor after having purported to terminate tenancy - Word 'rent' is not conclusive - Very significant is cl. (d) which enables decree holder to execute decree if judgment debtor fails to pay rent for any three consecutive months - This, it seems to us, shows that intention of parties was not to enter into relationship, of a landlord and tenant - Length of period, in circumstances, does not militate against construction that compromise only created a licence - Held, compromise deed did not create a lease - Therefore, judgment debtor is not a tenant within s. 2(5) of Act - Appeal allowed.
