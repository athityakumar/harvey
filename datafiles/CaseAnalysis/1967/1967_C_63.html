Commissioner of Income Tax, Kerala v R. R. Ramakrishna Pillai

Supreme Court of India

05 May 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, S.M. Sikri, Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 327; [1967] 66 ITR 725

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, ss.10(2) (Vii), 10(2) (Vii) Prov. (Ii) - Sale value of assets - An association of five persons carried on a bus business 'the service' - The business was transferred to 'the company' - In a proceeding for assessment to tax, the Income-tax Officer held that out of the consideration of Rs. 74, 000, Rs. 4,000 should be allocated to the workshop assets and the balance of Rs. 70,000 be taken into account u/s. 10(2)(vii), proviso two, of the 1922 Act, as profit of the service - Appellate Assistant Commissioner agreed with the view of the Income-tax Officer - Tribunal, reversed the order of the Appellate Assistant Commissioner - Tribunal referred the question to HC - HC answered the question in the negative - Hence, the present appeal - Whether order of the HC was correct - Held, the Appellate Assistant Commissioner observed that the value of the motor buses transferred was Rs. 70,000 - In his view whatever may be the reasons for transfer of the business, the purchase price entered by the company had to be taken into account in applying the second proviso to s. 10(2)(vii) of the 1922 Act - Tribunal did not attempt to ascertain the true nature of the transaction, but assumed that there was no sale of the assets of the society to the company, because 'in identical circumstances' HC had held that there was no sale - Therefore, the transaction may be deemed to be one of sale, and the application of s. 10(2)(vii), proviso 2, of the 1922 Act would be invited - If, the transaction was one in which in consideration of the transfer of seven buses, the company allotted shares of the face value of Rs. 70,000, the transaction would be one of exchange - Court unable to record any answer on the question referred to HC - Order accordingly.
