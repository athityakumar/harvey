 V. D. Dhanwatey v Commissioner of Income Tax, M.P. Nagpur

Supreme Court of India

26 October 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, R.S. Bachawat, G.K. Mitter, K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 184; AIR 1968 SC 683; 1968 (1) ITJ 604; [1968] 68 ITR 365; [1968] 2 S.C.R. 62

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Keywords: Hindu Gains of Learning Act, 1930

Summary: Family & Personal - Hindu Gains of Learning Act, 1930 - Whether Shri M D Dhanwatey was one of partners in partnership representing Hindu undivided family? - Held, the business carried on by partnership was of lithography and art printing and was carried on through a Press under name and style of Shivraj Fine Art Litho Works - The capital of partnership under partnership deed was Rs. 10,50,000 - Cl. (4) of partnership deed enumerated share capital contributed by partners - Cl. (5) states interest at rate of 5% per annum shall be payable to each partner - HC answered reference against assessee, holding that entire capital contribution made by Hindu Joint family, that remuneration paid to Shri V D Dhanwatey was paid under a clause of deed of partnership, and said amount of remuneration was taxable in hands of assessee - Material facts of present case are identical with Shri V D Dhanwatey v Commissioner of Income Tax A P Nagpur - Shri. M D Dhanwatey was one of partners in partnership representing Hindu undivided family consisting of himself and his two minor sons - Appeal dismissed.
