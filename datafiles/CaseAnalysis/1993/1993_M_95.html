Mohammed Anis v Union Of India

Supreme Court of India

16 July 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, N. Venkatachala

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 1226; (1994) Supp1 SCC 145; (1994) SCC (Cr) 251; [1993] Supp1 S.C.R. 263; 1993 (2) UJ 305

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14, 21, 32, 136, 142 and 142(1) - Delhi Special Police Establishment Act - Whether Court can order Central Bureau of Investigation to investigate an alleged offence without consent and orders of concerned State Govt? - Apex Court has been conferred extraordinary powers by art. 142(1) of Constitution and such powers cannot be limited or restricted by provisions contained in statutory law - No enactment made by Central or State Legislature can limit or restrict Court's power u/art. 142(1) - Power of Apex Court u/art. 142(1) of constitution cannot be diluted merely because statute stipulates that State Govt's permission will be necessary if Central Bureau of Investigation is to investigate any offence committed within territorial jurisdiction of a State Govt. - Held, statute does not prohibit investigation by CBI but only requires certain formalities to be completed which have no relevance when Apex Court makes an order in exercise of its power under art. 142(1) - Appeal dismissed.
