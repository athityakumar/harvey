P. Murugesan and Others v State of Tamil Nadu and Others

Supreme Court of India

03 February 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, A.S. Anand, L.M. Sharma

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 937; (1993) 2 SCC 340; (1993) SCC (L&S) 445; AIR 1993 SCW 1021; 1993 (24) ATC 149; 1993 (1) CLR 708; 1993 (66) FLR 769; JT 1993 (2) SC 115; 1993 (1) LLJ 944; 1993 (1) LLN 667; 1993 (2) MLJ(SC) 5; 1993(1) SCALE 423; [1993] 1 S.C.R. 405; 1993 (1) SLR 830

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Municipalities & Local Governments; Service

Keywords: Eligibility - Qualifications

Summary: Service - Madras City Municipal (Corporation) Act, 1919, s. 87 - Whether it is not permissible to rule-making authority, if it thinks it necessary in interests of administration to limit promotional chances of non-graduates to one out of four vacancies, on basis of academic qualifications? - Held, s. 87 does indicate manifest concern of legislature that vacancies occurring in Corporation service should not be kept unfilled for a period of more than three months - sub-s. (3) which provides for consequence of default on part of council to abide by subs.(1) emphasises concern of Legislature - So does sub-s. (2) - s. (4) says that if there is going to be any delay or if a suitable or qualified person is not available, council may appoint a person on temporary basis - Appeal allowed.
