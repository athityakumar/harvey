Commissioner of Income Tax, Calcutta v Braithwaite and Company Limited

Supreme Court of India

03 March 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

Kuldip Singh, N.M. Kasliwal

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 383; (1993) 2 SCC 262; 1993 (110) CTR 290; [1993] 201 ITR 34; [1993] 201 ITR 343; JT 1993 (3) SC 159; 1993(1) SCALE 761; [1993] 2 S.C.R. 187; [1993] 67 TAXMAN 155; 1993 (2) TLR 478

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Banking & Finance - Corporate - Companies (Profits) Surtax Act, 1964, r. 1(v) of the Second Schedule - Whether Tribunal was right in holding that only Rs. 16,00,000 out of the loan of Rs. 50,00,000 taken from the Bank qualified for inclusion in the capital base u/r. 1(v) of the Second Schedule to the Act? - Held, repayment of borrowed amount during the period of seven years does not mean repayment 'during a period of not less than seven years', hence the entire term loan of Rs. 50,00,000 does not qualify for inclusion in the capital base u/r. 1(v) of the Second Schedule to the Act - However, since the order of the Tribunal granting relief to the respondent-company to the extent of Rs. 16 lacs not challenged by the department, Revenue entitled to relief of Rs. 34 lacs only as not qualified for inclusion in the capital base - Appeal allowed.
