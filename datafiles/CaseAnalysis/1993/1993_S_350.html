K.B. Handicrafts Emporium and Others v State of Haryana and Others

Supreme Court of India

28 April 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, N. Venkatachala

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 949; (1993) Supp4 SCC 589; AIR 1994 SC 1697; JT 1993 (4) SC 545; 1993(2) SCALE 675; [1993] 3 S.C.R. 454; 1993 (90) STC 477

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Keywords: Penultimate Sale

Summary: Sales Tax - Indirect Tax - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Haryana General Sales Tax Act, 1973 - Petitioners were engaged in manufacture and sale of handicrafts items, which was sold to dealers in the capital, who in turn, exported them out - Authorities levied purchase tax on the purchase of raw materials made by the petitioner, u/s. 9, of the 1973 Act - Hence, the present petition - Whether the sales were inter-State sales - Held, that the question whether a particular sale was an intra-state sale, an interstate sale ,an export sale within the meaning of s. 5(1), of the 1956 Act or a penultimate sale within the meaning of s. 5(3), of the 1956 Act or otherwise, was always a question of fact to be decided by the appropriate authority in the light of the principles enunciated by Courts - Petitions disposed of. in
