Harbans Lal v Collector of Central Excise and Customs, Chandigarh

Supreme Court of India

14 July 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

M.M. Punchhi, A.M. Ahmadi

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 1213; (1993) 3 SCC 656; (1993) SCC (Cr) 983; AIR 1993 SC 2487; 1993 (4) CCR 388; 1993 (48) ECR 219; 1993 (67) E.L.T. 20; JT 1993 (4) SC 135; 1993(3) SCALE 64; [1993] Supp1 S.C.R. 131

Case Digest

Subject: Customs

Summary: Customs - Customs Act, 1962, ss. 110 and 124 - Gold Control Act, 1968, ss. 78 and 79 - Whether ss. 110 and 124 of Customs Act, 1962 are inter se independent, distinct and exclusive or are they interwoven, interconnected and inter playing and what is fallout of order extending time u/s. 110(2) of Act being vitiated? - It is a settled position of law that while extending time u/s. 110(2), owner of seized goods is entitled to notice, because seized goods on expiry of period of six months are required to be returned to him, and if that period was to be extended for another period of six months he had right to be heard - Held, High Court rightly observed that it was not disputed before it that ex parte order extending time by another 6 months as postulated in ss. 110(2) and 124 was vitiated; ratio of Charan Das Malhotra case [ 1971 Indlaw SC 523] settles question; ss. 110 and 124 are independent, distinct and exclusive of each other, resulting in survival of proceedings u/s. 124, even though seized goods might have to be returned, or stand returned, in terms of s. 110 after expiry of permissible period of seizure - Appeal dismissed.
