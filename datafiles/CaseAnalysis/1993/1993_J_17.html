Jagtar Singh v Director, Central Bureau of Investigation and Others

Supreme Court of India

13 April 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

Kuldip Singh, Yogeshwar Dayal

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 675; (1993) Supp3 SCC 49; (1993) SCC (L&S) 922; 1993 (25) ATC 81; 1993 (67) FLR 218; JT 1993 (2) SC 703; 1993 LabIC 1133; 1994 (1) LLJ 610; 1993 (2) LLN 55; 1993(2) SCALE 553; [1993] 3 S.C.R. 77; 1993 (2) SLR 539; 1993 (2) UPLBEC 1217

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Service

Summary: Service - Advocates & Judges - Appointment - Denied - Challenged - Appellant was selected for appointment to the post of Senior Public Prosecutor, but no appointment order was issued - Appellant filed an application before the Tribunal, where the respondents said that it was found that the appellant was not a suitable person for appointment to the said post - Hence, the present appeal - Whether the appellant was not suitable for the said post - Held, there had been total lack of application of mind on the part of the respondents - Only on the basis of surmises and conjectures arising out of a single incident which happened in the year 1983 it had been concluded that the appellant was not a desirable person to be appointed to the Govt. service - Appellant had been unjustifiably denied his right to be appointed to the post to which he was selected and recommended by the Union Public Service Commission - Respondents were not justified in refusing to appoint the appellant, and the respondents should be directed to appoint the appellant, but keeping in view the time lapse and further that the appellant had already entered 50th year of his age and had put in about 23 years of practice as in advocate, it would not be in the interest of justice to issue a direction to that effect, so the respondents should pay the costs of the litigation to the appellant, quantified as Rs. 10, 000 - Appeal dismissed.
