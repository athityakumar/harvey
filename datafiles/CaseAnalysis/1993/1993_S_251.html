Joginder Singh Wasu v State Of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

29 October 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mohan, M.N. Venkatachaliah

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 747; (1994) 1 SCC 184; JT 1993 (6) SC 259; 1993(4) SCALE 289; 1994 (1) SLR 3; 1994 (1) UJ 35

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Defence & Security Forces; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Advocates & Judges - Constitution of India, 195, art. 165 - Government of India Act, 1935 - Appellant appointed as Advocate-General of State of Punjab by President - Fee of Advocate-General was fixed at Rs. 100 for each writ petition - Later on altercation was in fee structure by notification - Appellant protested altercation and requested that enforcement of notification may be kept in abeyance - Held, relationship between Advocate-General and State Govt. is essentially that of an Advocate and a client in relation to his appearance in court and arguing case before court - Notification clearly stipulates 'as amended from time to time' therefore, fees fixed in cl. 6(f) under notification cannot remain unaltered - Appeal dismissed.
