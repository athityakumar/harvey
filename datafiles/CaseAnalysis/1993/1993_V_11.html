Vanka Radhamanohari Smt v Vanka Venkata Reddy and Others

Supreme Court of India

20 April 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

N.P. Singh, Kuldip Singh

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 1088; (1993) 3 SCC 4; (1993) SCC (Cr) 571; AIR 1993 SCW 3595; 1993 (2) CCR 186; 1993 (2) Crimes 275; 1993 CRLR 382; JT 1993 (4) SC 17; 1993(2) SCALE 570; [1993] 3 S.C.R. 287

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure; Tort

Summary: Criminal - Practice and Procedure - Quashing of proceedings - Indian Limitation Act, 1908, ss.3, 4, 5 - CrPC, 1973, ss.482, 473, 468 - IPC,1860, ss.498-A, 494 - Appellant filed a complaint against her husband, respondent that she was being maltreated and abused - HC on an application filed on behalf of respondents u/s. 482 CrPC, quashed said criminal proceeding saying that after expiry of period of three years, no cognizance for an offence u/s.498-A IPC could have been taken - Whether proceedings u/s.498-A are liable of being quashed in view of s. 468 CrPC? - In view of allegation regarding second marriage by respondent during continuance of first marriage, prima facie an offence u/s. 494 IPC was disclosed in complaint and re was no question of s. 468 CrPC being applicable to an offence u/s.494 - In respect of criminal offences, provisions regarding limitation cannot be prescribed on a par with provisions in respect of civil disputes - In view of s. 473 CrPC a court can take cognizance of an offence not only when it is satisfied on facts and in circumstances of case that delay has been properly explained but even in absence of proper explanation if court is satisfied that it is necessary so to do in interests of justice - S. 473 has a non-obstante clause which means that it has an overriding effect on s. 468 - Object of introducing s. 468 was to put a bar of limitation on prosecutions and to prevent parties from filing cases after a long time - But that consideration cannot be extended to matrimonial offences where allegations are of cruelty torture and assault by husband or members of family to complainant - As such, courts while considering question of limitation for an offence u/s. 498-A should judge that question, in light of s. 473, which requires Court not only to examine as to whether delay has been properly explained, but as to whether 'it is necessary to do so in interests of justice' - Held, that in view of allegation that complainant was being subjected to cruelty by respondent, it was in interests of justice to take cognizance even of offence u/s. 498-A ignoring bar of s. 468 - Appeal allowed.
