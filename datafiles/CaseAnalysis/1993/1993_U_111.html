Shriram Chits and Investment Private Limited v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

13 July 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

Yogeshwar Dayal, N. Venkatachala, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 1724; (1993) Supp4 SCC 226; AIR 1993 SC 2063; AIR 1993 SCW 2443; [1994] 79 Comp Cas 298; 1994 (2) CompLJ 430; JT 1993 (4) SC 399; 1993(3) SCALE 125; [1993] Supp1 S.C.R. 54

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance

Keywords: Kerala Chitties Act, 1975, Prohibition Act

Summary: Banking and Finance - Chit Funds Act ,1982, Pre., ss. 4, 6, 6(3), 9(1), 12, 13, 16, 17, 21and 48 - Constitution, arts. 19(1)(g) and 245, sch.VII, list 2, entry 30 and list III entry 7 - (A) Chit fund agreement is special contract and not deals with money lending - Dominant purpose of the Chit Funds Act is to regulate chit - Legislative competence - Whether Parliament is competent to enact it under entry 7, list III sch. VII of Constitution - (B) Whether the provision u/s. 4 ,6(3), 9(1), 12, 13 ,16, 17and 21 are violative of art. 19(1)(g) - (C) Whethher provision u/s. 25 s reasonable and is against the subscriber's interest - (D) Whether s. 48 of winding up, of chits is intra vires the arts. 19(1)(g) and 245 - Held, the Act , in pith and substance, deals with special contract and consequently falls within entry 7 of List III of the Third Schedule. Therefore, the Chit Funds Act is within the legislative competence of the Parliament - The s. 4 is not voilative of art. 19(1)(g) - Ceiling on the higer side is neither arbitrary nor un-reasonable - Provision u/s. 9(1) is regulatory and in interest of subscribers - And s. 12 is not hit by art. 19(1)(g) - Aggregate limit u/s. 13 is not voilative - Provisionsof ss.16 and 17 are regulatory and are intended to avoid fraud on subscribers by delaying their payments - Provision u/s. 25 is not unreasonable - Further s. 48 is intravires - Petition dismissed.
