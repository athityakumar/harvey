Vipin Kumar v Roshan Lal Anand And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

24 March 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, R.M. Sahai

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 1000; (1993) 2 SCC 614; JT 1993 (3) SC 171; 1993 (1) RCJ 468; 1993 (1) RCR(Rent) 675; 1993 (1) RentLR 754; 1993(2) SCALE 456B; [1993] 2 S.C.R. 640; 1993 (2) UJ 379

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - East Punjab Urban Rent Restriction Act, 1949, s. 13(1) - Whether finding of courts below which concurrently found that appellant-tenant had constructed a wall in verandah which materially affected value or utility of shop is vitiated by law? - First limb of cl. (iii) of sub-section (2) of s. 13 is impairment of building due to acts committed by tenant and second limb is of utility or value of building has been materially impaired - Acts of tenant must be such that by erecting wall he had materially impaired value or utility of demised premises - If tenant had committed such acts as are likely to impair materially value or utility of building, he is liable to ejectment - Held, by constructing wall, whether value or utility of building has materially been impaired is an inferential fact to be deduced from proved facts - Proved facts are that appellant without consent of landlord had constructed wall and put up a door therein as found by Rent Controller, flow of air and light has been stopped - He removed fixtures - From these facts it was inferred that value or utility of building has been materially affected - No illegality in granting decree of ejectment - Appeal dismissed.
