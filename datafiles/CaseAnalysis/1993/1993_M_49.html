Rattan Lal Sharma v Managing Committee, Dr. Hari Ram (Co-Education)Higher Secon

Supreme Court of India

14 May 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

G.N. Ray, M.N. Venkatachaliah

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 1167; (1993) 4 SCC 10; (1993) SCC (L&S) 1106; AIR 1993 SC 2155; 1993 (25) ATC 449; 1993 (2) CLR 1; 1993 (67) FLR 364; JT 1993 (3) SC 487; 1993 LabIC 1808; 1993 (2) LLJ 549; 1993 (2) LLN 253; 1993(2) SCALE 924; [1993] 3 S.C.R. 863; 1993 (4) SLR 109; 1993 (2) UPLBEC 1460

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Administrative - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Limitation Act, 1908 - Punjab Aided (Schools Security of Service) Act, 1969, s. 3(2) - Natural justice - (A) Rules of - (1) Applicability - (2) Applicability to administrative bodies - (3) Defining parameter not possible and practicable - (4) Cardinal principle - (B) Bias - Member of Departmental Enquiry Committee deposing as witness against delinquent - Objection of delinquent over-ruled by Committee - (1) Validity considering fact that delinquent did not raise specific plea before authority granting approval to proposed punishment or before appellate authority - (2) Bias - Test is whether there is real likelihood of bias, its taking place is immaterial? - (3) To prove one of several charges - His bias percolates throughout enquiry proceedings - Findings given by Committee on remaining charges - (C) New plea - Cannot be allowed, unless it goes to root of question - Objection raised before Committee but over-ruled - Plea not raised before Tribunal or administrative authorities - Raised for first time in writ proceeding - Held, :(A) They are foundational and fundamental concepts in Administrative law - Principles are applicable to almost whole range of administrative powers - Not possible and practicable to precisely define parameter of natural justice - No one can be judge of his own cause, maxims, Nemo debet esse judex in propria causa - (B) Inquiry vitiated, fact immaterial - Bias takes place - Findings on remaining charges also get vitiated - (C) Can be allowed - Appeal allowed.
