Tekchand Etc. v Competent Authority

Supreme Court of India

31 March 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, N. Venkatachala

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 1049; (1993) 3 SCC 84; (1993) SCC (Cr) 606; 1993 (2) CCR 177; [1993] 77 Comp Cas 336; 1993 (112) CTR 458; 1997 (68) ECR 773; 1993 (65) E.L.T. 355; [1993] 201 ITR 658; JT 1993 (4) SC 197; 1993(2) SCALE 325; [1993] 2 S.C.R. 864; 1993 (2) UJ 79

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Customs

Keywords: Excess Profits Tax Act, 1940, Super Profit Tax Act, 1963, Voluntary Disclosure Of Income And Wealth Act, 1976, Business Profits-tax Act, 1947

Summary: Exchange Control and FDI - Smugglers and Foreign Exchange Manipulators (Forfeiture of Property) Act, 1976, ss.2(2)(b), 3(c), 4, 6, 8, 9, 24, 26 - Conservation of Foreign Exchange and Prevention of Smuggling Activities Act, 1947 - Voluntary Disclosure of Income and Wealth Act, 1976, ss. 3(1), 8(1), 11, 16 - (A) Whether SAFEMA applies only in case of persons who have been detained under COFEPOSA prior to commencement of Act and that it does not apply to persons who have been detained under COFEPOSA after commencement of SAFEMA? - Held, there is nothing in Act to indicate either directly or by necessary intendment that Act is confined only to those persons who have been detained under COFEPOSA or who have been convicted under Customs Act or FERA prior to commencement of SAFEMA; use of words 'has been made' in s.2(2)(b) provides that every person in respect of whom an order of detention has been made and which detention order has not been revoked or withdrawn by competent authority nor has been set aside by a competent court, can be proceeded against under SAFEMA; a mere detention under COFEPOSA is not enough; not only there must have been an order of detention under said Act, other conditions prescribed in proviso to cls (b) should not also have taken place; Explanation 4 appended to s.2(2) is relevant which provides that question whether any person is a person to whom provision of this Act apply may be determined with reference to any facts, circumstances or events (including any conviction or detention) which occurred or took place before commencement of this Act - (B) Whether authorities erred in not giving effect to immunity conferred by Voluntary Disclosure Act and calling upon appellants to explain source of income declared under said Act? - Held, immunity conferred u/ss. 11 and 16 of Voluntary Disclosure Act is of a limited character and cannot be extended beyond confines specified by said provisions; there is no reason to presume that Parliament intended to extend any immunity to smugglers and manipulators of foreign exchange who are proceeded against under enactments other than those mentioned in ss.11, 16 of said Act - Appeals dismissed.
