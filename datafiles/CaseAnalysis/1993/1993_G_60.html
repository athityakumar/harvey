Anil Kumar Sawhney v Gulshan Rai

Supreme Court of India

11 October 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

Kuldip Singh, Yogeshwar Dayal

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 1637; (1993) 4 SCC 424; (1993) SCC (Cr) 1243; 1993 (22) ALR 534; [1994] 79 Comp Cas 150; JT 1993 (6) SC 280; 1994 (1) KLT 111; 1994 (1) RCR(Rent) 150

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Corporate; Criminal

Keywords: Bill Of Exchange

Summary: Banking & Finance - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, ss. 5, 6, 19, 138, 139 and 140 - What is date from which period of six months as contemplated u/s. 138 (a) is to be reckoned? - 'Post-dated cheque' is bill of exchange when written or drawn, becomes 'cheque' when payable on demand - So, six-month period to be reckoned from date written on it - Section to be construed with reference to context - Held, post-dated cheque be deemed to be drawn on date it bears - Appeals allowed.
