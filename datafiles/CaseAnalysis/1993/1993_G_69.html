Guru Amarjit Singh v Rattan Chand and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 August 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, S. Mohan

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 809; (1993) 4 SCC 349; AIR 1994 SC 227; 1993 (3) CCC 364; JT 1993 (4) SC 536; 1994 (1) MLJ(SC) 50; 1993 (2) RCJ 437; 1994 (1) RCR(Rent) 12; 1993(3) SCALE 363; [1993] Supp1 S.C.R. 523

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Land & Property - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 114 - (A) Entries in Jamabandi - Evidentiary value of - If proof of title - (B) S. 111(g), cl. (1) - Lease forfeiture of - Same alleged on ground of sale of land - Lease not disclosing any covenant prohibiting alienation of land providing right to re-entry for breach thereof - If cl. (1) attracted - (C) Lease - Cl. (2) Whether disclaimer by denial of landlord's title or setting up of title in himself or third party results in forfeiture - Held, (A) They are not proof of title - (B) Cl. (1) is not attracted - (C) It results in forfeiture - Appeal dismissed.
