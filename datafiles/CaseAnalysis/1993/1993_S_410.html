Hindustan Paper Corporation Ltd. v State of Kerala and Others

Supreme Court of India

26 March 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, N. Venkatachala

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 416; (1993) Supp3 SCC 350; JT 1993 (2) SC 458; 1993 (1) KLT 689; 1993 (2) MLJ 100; 1993 (2) MLJ(SC) 100; 1993(2) SCALE 238; [1993] 2 S.C.R. 655; 1993 (89) STC 473

Case Digest

Subject: Electricity & Energy; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure; Sales Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Intrastate sale or purchase - Exemption - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956, ss. 3(A), 6 and 8(2A) - Kerala Sales Tax Act, 1963, s. 10 - Notification RS0415 of 1971 - Whether exemption granted under notification exempting produce of a factory manufacturing newsprint from state sales tax for a period of 2 years from date of commencement of production in factory can be called an exemption from Central Sales Tax by virtue of s. 8(2A) of Central Sales Tax Act? - Held, following binding decision in Pine Chemicals [1992 Indlaw SC 830] which deals with an almost similar exemption notification, wherein, it was held that s. 8(2-A) provides that if an intra-state sale or purchase of a commodity by dealer is exempt from tax generally or subject to tax generally at a rate which is lower than 4% than his liability to tax under Central Sales Tax Act when such commodity is sold on inter-state trade would be either nil or as case may be shall be calculated at a lower rate - Explanation states as to when an intra-state sale or purchase of a commodity shall not be deemed as exempt from state tax generally if exemption is given only (1) in specified circumstances or under specified conditions or (2) tax is leviable on sale or purchase of such goods at specified stages or (3) otherwise than with reference to turnover of goods - These conditions are with reference to transaction of sale or purchase, main clause deals with turnover of 'a dealer' which term would include 'any dealer' or 'any class of dealers' and existence or otherwise of three Limitations will therefore have to be tested with reference to transaction of sale or purchase as case may be of dealer who claims exemption in respect of his intrastate sale or purchase of same goods, thus specified circumstances and specified conditions referred to in explanation should be with reference to local turnover of same dealer who claims exemption u/s. 8(2-A) of Central Sales Tax Act - Appeals allowed.
