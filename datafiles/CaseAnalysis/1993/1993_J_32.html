Smt. Chand Dhawan v Jawaharlal Dhawan

Supreme Court of India

11 June 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

M.M. Punchhi, Yogeshwar Dayal

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 198; (1993) 3 SCC 406; (1993) SCC (Cr) 915; AIR 1993 SCW 2548; 1993 (22) ALR 240; 1993 CRLJ 2930; 1993 (2) DMC 110; 1993 (2) HLR 203; JT 1993 (4) SC 22; 1993(3) SCALE 1; [1993] 3 S.C.R. 954; 1993 (2) UJ 356

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Family & Personal - Code of Criminal Procedure, s. 125 - Hindu Marriage Act, ss. 13B, 24 and 25 - Indian Evidence Act, s. 41 - Whether payment of alimony is admissible without relationship between spouses being terminated? - Held, when by court intervention under Hindu Marriage Act, affection or disruption to marital status has come by, court, while passing decree, has power to grant permanent alimony or maintenance, if that power is invoked at that time and it also retains power subsequently to be invoked on application by a party entitled to relief - Such order, in all events remains within jurisdiction of that court, to be altered or modified as future situations may warrant - In contrast, without affectation or disruption of marital status, a Hindu wife sustaining that status can live in separation from her husband, and whether she is living in that state or not, her claim to maintenance stands preserved in codification u/s. 18(1) of Hindu Adoption and Maintenance Act - Court is not at liberty to grant relief of maintenance simpliciter obtainable under one Act in Act in proceedings under other - Relief to wife also due u/s. 125 of CrPC - Appeal dismissed.
