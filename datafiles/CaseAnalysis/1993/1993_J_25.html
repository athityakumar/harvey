Union Of India v Jalyan Udyog

Supreme Court of India

14 September 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, S.P. Bharucha

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 615; (1994) 1 SCC 318; AIR 1994 SC 88; 1994 (46) ECC 18; 1993 (49) ECR 7; 1993 (68) E.L.T. 9; [2001] ITR 611; JT 1993 (5) SC 266; 1993(3) SCALE 758; [1993] Supp2 S.C.R. 293

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Relevant Date, Warehouse, Tariff Valuation, Stores

Summary: Customs Act, 1962, ss. 15 and 25 - Merchant Shipping Act, 1958, s. 42(1) - Exemption from duty - (A) Is there any limitation as to power u/s. 25 of 1962 Act - Can power of exemption be used for altering relevant date prescribed by s. 15 - Ship imported as ocean going vessel but subsequently broken up - Exemption notification shifted date of import from date of actual import to date of breaking up by creating legal fiction - If illegal - Held, (A) No limitation - Can be used - (B) Not illegal - However date of breaking up should be deemed to be date of grant of permission for breaking up by Director General of Shipping - Appeal dismissed.
