Sarva Shramik Sangh, Bombay v Indian Hume Pipe Company, Limited, and Another

Supreme Court of India

12 February 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, Kuldip Singh

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 1305; (1993) 2 SCC 386; (1993) SCC (L&S) 627; AIR 1993 SCW 982; 1993 (2) CLR 355; 1993 (67) FLR 1; JT 1993 (4) SC 40; 1993 LabIC 870; 1993 (1) LLJ 965; 1993 (2) LLN 583; 1993(1) SCALE 596; [1993] 1 S.C.R. 1050; 1993 (4) SLR 376; 1993 (1) UJ 587

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 2(k) - Whether or not Industrial Tribunal has power to award relief with effect from a date anterior to date of raising dispute by Labour Union? - Definition of expression 'industrial dispute' in cl. (k) of s. 2 does not contain any such limitation - There is no justification for holding that Industrial Tribunal or a Labour Court has no jurisdiction to grant relief from a date anterior to date on which dispute is raised - Distinction between existence of power and its exercise - It is one thing to say that tribunal has no power to grant such relief and it is an altogether different thing to say that in a given case it ought not to grant such relief - Held, there is no reason why tribunal could not have awarded relief from date earlier then 1973 if it found that such a demand was justified and warranted in facts of case - Consent order passed in Writ Petition says that fresh dispute to be raised was to be 'in respect of same demands' which mean very same demand which was being raised by workmen from November 1965 onwards, hence order doesn't imply any restriction upon workmen to limit benefit claimed by them only from date of raising of fresh demand - Appeal allowed, Writ Petition filed by management in Bombay High Court dismissed - Order accordingly.
