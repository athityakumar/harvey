Nandyal Co-Op. Spinning Mills Ltd. v K.V. Mohan Rao

Supreme Court of India

05 March 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, R.M. Sahai

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 1718; (1993) 2 SCC 654; AIR 1993 SCW 2260; 1993 (2) ARBLR 359; 1993 (1) CCC 667; JT 1993 (Supp) SC 89; 1993(2) SCALE 8; [1993] 2 S.C.R. 280

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Civil Procedure

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration Act, 1940, s. 8(1) (a) - (A) Whether s. 8(1)(a) of Arbitration Act does not apply to facts of this case as contract abstracted herein before makes respondent to abide by appointment of an arbitrator by Administrative Head of appellant? - Held, if no arbitrator had been appointed in terms of contract within 15 days from date of receipt of notice, Administrative Head of appellant had abdicated himself of power to appoint arbitrator under contract - Court gets jurisdiction to appoint an arbitrator in place of contract by operation of s. 8(1)(a) - (B) Whether a contract can be nullified on plea of bias of arbitrator, considering that it provides power to nominate an arbitrator to a sole party ? - There is universal agreement amongst jurists of all countries that it is of first importance that judicial tribunals should be honest, impartial and disinterested - Further held, respondent rightly objected to nomination of arbitrator - Such nomination, therefore, does not bind him - Appeal dismissed.
