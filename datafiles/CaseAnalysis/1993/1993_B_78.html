Panchu Gopal Bose v Board Of Trustees For Port Of Calcutta

Supreme Court of India

23 April 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, S. Mohan

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 1801; (1993) 4 SCC 338; 1993 (2) CCC 129; 1993(2) SCALE 696; 1993 (2) UJ 213

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Keywords: Arbitration Act, Gaming Act

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Practice & procedure - Arbitration Act, 1940, ss. 5, 12(1)(b) and 37 - Other party to arbitration agreement sleeping over rights sends notice after 10 years - (A) Whether court has power to permit revocation of arbitration agreement ? - Held, court has power and jurisdiction under ss. 5 and 12 to grant leave to applicant in exceptional circumstances to revoke contract of arbitration - Power to be used sparingly, cautiously and with circumspection to permit a party to contract of arbitration voluntarily entered into to relieve party from dispute or difference and to order that dispute or difference shall cease to have effect in respect of dispute or difference - (B) Period of Limitation - Further held, as in case of actions claim is not to be brought after expiration of a specified number of years from date on which cause of action accrued, so in case of arbitrations, claim is not to be put forward after expiration of specified number of years from date when claim accrued - Petition dismissed.
