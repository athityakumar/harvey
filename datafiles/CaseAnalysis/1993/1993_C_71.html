Navin Chemicals Manufacturing and Trading Company Limited v Collector of Customs

Supreme Court of India

15 September 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Bharucha, B.P. Jeevan Reddy

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 626; (1993) 4 SCC 320; 1993 (52) DLT 276; 1993 (49) ECR 1; 1993 (68) E.L.T. 3; JT 1993 (5) SC 362; 1993(3) SCALE 776; [1993] Supp2 S.C.R. 326

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Customs Act, 1962, ss. 111(d), 129-C, 130(1) and 130-E - Import and Export (Control) Act, 1947, s. 3 - Whether a member of CEGAT sitting singly could in law have heard appeal before it? - Yes - Appeals against orders which involve 'determination of any question having a relation to rate of duty of customs or to value of goods for purposes of assessment' are specially treated and are required to be heard by a Special Bench - Held, in present case did not have any direct or proximate relation, for purpose of assessment, either to rate of duty applicable to said goods or to value thereof - Appeal dismissed.
