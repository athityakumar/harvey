Madan Mohan v Krishan Kumar Sood

Supreme Court of India

12 January 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

Yogeshwar Dayal, N. Venkatachala, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 1397; (1994) Supp1 SCC 437; AIR 1993 SCW 743; JT 1993 (1) SC 162; 1993 (1) RCJ 170; 1993 (1) RCR(Rent) 290; 1993 (1) RentLR 198; 1993(1) SCALE 71; [1993] 1 S.C.R. 107

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure; Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - Practice & Procedure - Himachal Pradesh Urban Rent Control Act, 1987,s. 14(2)(i) - Whether executing court had jurisdiction to extend time to pay ordered arrears of rent when period of 30 days had been fixed in Act itself and what is meaning of words 'amount due' occurring in third proviso to cl. (i) of s. 14(2) of Act? - 'Amount due' occurring in third proviso in context will mean amount due on and up to date of order of eviction and it will take into account not merely arrears of rent but also include rent which accumulated during pendency of eviction petition as well - If tenant has been paying rent during pendency of eviction petition to landlord, 'amount due' will be only arrears which have not been paid; landlord, as per scheme of section, cannot be worse off vis-a-vis a tenant who was good enough to deposit in court arrears of rent together with interest and costs on first date of hearing - Held, order which Controller passed was a composite order of eviction; but at stage when execution application was filed, Rent Controller could not go behind its own order D/- 29-07-1986 and likewise, High Court could not go behind order in revision against Controller's order refusing execution, therefore, respondent's warrants of possession for ejectment from premises in dispute ought to be issued and landlords to be placed in possession - Order accordingly.
