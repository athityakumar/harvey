D. K. Yadav v J.M.A. Industries Limited

Supreme Court of India

07 May 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, V. Ramaswami, Kuldip Singh

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 279; (1993) 3 SCC 259; (1993) SCC (L&S) 723; AIR 1993 SCW 1995; 1993 (24) ATC 363; 1993 (2) CLR 116; 1993 (67) FLR 111; JT 1993 (3) SC 617; 1993 (2) LLJ 696; 1993 (2) LLN 575; 1993(3) SCALE 39; [1993] 3 S.C.R. 930; 1993 (3) SLJ 73; 1993 (4) SLR 126; 1993 (2) UJ 348

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Certified Standing Orders, cl. 13(2)(iv) - Termination - On the ground of unauthorized absence - Validity of - Held, if a workman remains absent without sanctioned leave or beyond period of leave, he shall lose his appointment unless: (a) he returns within eight calendar days, or (b) explain to satisfaction of manager/management - Action of management in terminating appellant's service is violative of principles of natural justice u/cl. 13(2)(iv) of Certified Standing Orders - Otherwise, it would become arbitrary, unjust and unfair violating art. 14 - Management did not conduct any domestic enquiry nor gave appellant any opportunity to put forth his case - Despite his reporting to duty and readiness to join duty he was prevented to report to duty - Award of Labour Court is set aside - Direction to respondent to reinstate appellant forthwith and pay him 50 per cent of back wages within a period of three months - Appeal allowed.
