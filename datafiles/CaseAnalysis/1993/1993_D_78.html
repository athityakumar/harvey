Dr. M. S. Mudhol and Another v S. D. Halegkar and Others

Supreme Court of India

13 July 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

P.B. Sawant, Yogeshwar Dayal

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 1206; (1993) 3 SCC 591; (1993) SCC (L&S) 986; 1993 (25) ATC 91; 1993 (67) FLR 379; JT 1993 (4) SC 143; 1993 (2) LLJ 1159; 1993(3) SCALE 61; 1993 (4) SLR 364; [1993] Supp1 S.C.R. 115; 1993 (2) UJ 398; 1993 (3) UPLBEC 1782

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Tort

Summary: Service - Constitution - Writ of quo warranto - Eligibility - Question related to eligibility of 1st respondent to occupy post of Principal of school - Appointment was challenged after 9 years of appointment - Rule requires both academic Masters degree and teaching degree, later being not substitute for former - First respondent did not possess requisite educational qualifications to be selected for post of Principal - Default on part of Director of Education in approving appointment of first respondent although he did not have requisite academic qualifications - No fault of first respondent when his selection was made - No interference with appointment of first respondent - Petition dismissed.
