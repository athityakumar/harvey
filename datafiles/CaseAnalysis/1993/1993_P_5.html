Forest Range Officer and Others v P. Mohammed Ali and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 May 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, R.M. Sahai

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 1557; (1993) Supp3 SCC 627; (1993) SCC (Cr) 1070; AIR 1994 SC 120; 1993 (2) ALT 152; 1993 (2) CCR 200; 1993 (2) Crimes 415; 1993 CRLR 399; 1993 CrLR(SC) 399; JT 1993 (3) SC 222; 1993 (1) KLT 886; 1993(2) SCALE 792; [1993] 3 S.C.R. 497

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Khandsari, Kerala Forest Act, 1961

Summary: Criminal - Practice and Procedure - Karnataka Forest Act, 1963 - Kerala Forest Act, 1961,ss.2(f), 2(k), 51(1) - CrPC, 1973,s. 482 - Forest Conservation Act, 1980 - When proceedings were laid u/s. 51(1) of Act against respondents questioned jurisdiction of court - HC in exercise of its power u/s. 482 of CrPC, 1973 quashed complaint holding that sandalwood oil is not 'wood oil' as defined in s.2(f)(i) of Act and so it is not a forest produce - Appeals by special leave - Whether 'sandalwood oil' is forest produce within meaning of s.2(f)(i) of Kerala Forest Act? - Forest produce defined u/s. 2(f) is an inclusive definition; an interpretation clause of inclusive definition is not meant to prevent word receiving its ordinary, popular and natural sense whenever that word would be properly applicable, but to enable word as used in Act, when there is nothing in context or subject-matter to contrary, to be applied to something to which it would not ordinarily be applicable; process by which oil is extracted is not decisive as oil may be extracted by natural process of exudation or it may be extracted by subjecting to chemical or mechanical process and sandalwood (Santalum album) is cut into pieces, its heartwood and roots of sandalwood trees removed from forest are used as a raw material at a factory level that too by mechanised process to extract sandalwood oil; purpose for which oil is used is not decisive, therefore, word wood oil used in Act will require purposive interpretation drawing upon context in which words are used and its meaning will have to be discovered having regard to intention and object which legislature seeks to subserve - Held, that sandalwood oil is wood oil within meaning of s.2(f)(i), therefore it is a forest produce and Trial Court has jurisdiction to proceed with trial - Order accordingly.
