Rajasthan Roller Flour Mills Assn. v State Of Rajasthan

Supreme Court of India

01 September 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, S.P. Bharucha

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 1407; (1994) Supp1 SCC 413; AIR 1994 SC 64; AIR 1993 SCW 3118; JT 1993 (5) SC 138; 1993(3) SCALE 600; 1993 (91) STC 408; [1993] Supp2 S.C.R. 72

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Keywords: Declared Goods, Tax Levied, Central Sales Tax (Amendment) Act, 1972, Bombay Prohibition Act 1949, Essential Goods (Declaration And Regulation Of Tax On Sale Or Purchase) Act

Summary: Sales Tax - Indirect Tax - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956, s. 14(i)(iii) - Wheat - Whether the expression 'wheat' in s. 14(i)(iii) of Act includes flour, maida and 'suji'.

Held, flour, maida and suji derived from wheat were not 'wheat' within the meaning of s. 14(i)(iii) of the Act. Flour, maida and suji were different and distinct goods from wheat. Order accordingly.
