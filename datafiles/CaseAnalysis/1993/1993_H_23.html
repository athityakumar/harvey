His Highness Maharaja Pratap Singh and Others v Her Highness Maharani Sarojini Devi and Others

Supreme Court of India

17 August 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mohan, Kuldip Singh

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 568; (1994) Supp1 SCC 734; 1993 (2) HLR 272; JT 1993 (Supp) SC 288; 1993(3) SCALE 394; [1993] Supp1 S.C.R. 607

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Family & Personal; Land & Property

Keywords: Indian Independence Act, 1947

Summary: Civil Procedure - Family & Personal - Land & Property - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, ss. 86, 87B - Government of India Act, 1935, art. 372 - Hindu Succession Act, 1956, s. 5 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Indian Independence Act, 1947 -

Hindu Women's Rights to Property Act,1937 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Royal family - Disputes regarding property - Counter suits - Validity - Disputes between family members of King 'X' regarding housing properties situated at Simla and Delhi - Eldest son 'A' claimed properties as his private properties and vested in him as chief of State - Challenged by his mother and younger brothers by sending legal notice and their attempt to sue 'A' was denied by Central Govt. - 'A' sold house property at Simla to defendent no. 1 and 2 - Mother and younger brothers filed suit and during pendency defendants 1 and 2 sold this property to defendants 4 to 8 by sale deed - Original suit filed in Simla was transferred to Himachal Pradesh HC on its formation - Single Judge granted preliminary decree for partition and recovery of mesne profits in favour of plaintiffs holding that it was joint family property - Aggrieved defendants filed appeal and was allowed by DB - Hence instant appeal CA no. 1208/1990 by plaintiffs/appellants/ mother and younger brothers of 'A' - Similarly 'A' also filed suit in Delhi HC for possession of house situated in Delhi and was allowed by Single Judge - Aggrieved defendants/mother and younger brothers of 'A' filed appeal and was allowed by DB holding that Covenant did not create any new rights on 'A' but only recognises and declares pre existing rights and that property belonged to 'X' and after his death became joint family property - Hence instant appeal CA. no. 5857/1983 by plaintiff/appellant/'A' - Whether Orders of DB were justified - Held, failed to prove that wife had purchased Simla house from out of funds of 'X' - Except oral testimony no single document was filed to prove that property was purchased benami by 'X' in 'Y' name - Property belonged to State as could be seen from municipal registry and rightly declarared by 'A' as his personal property - Onus of proof would be on plaintiffs/appellants to prove that property was purchased by State out of its funds - Well settled law was that where benami was pleaded five principles to be considered as held in Mayne's Hindu Law 13th Edn at page 1201 - U/art. 372 of the 1935 Act law of succession relating to primogeniture continues till repealed - - In 1923 itself permission declined to 'X' to retain this property as his personal property and hence appellants claim that it was personal property of 'X' was disbelieved - Exs. P-73 to P-108 clearly establish that funds for repair of building came from Govt. treasury - By 'X' conduct in not evincing any interest in this property could be held that it was not purchased by 'X' from his personal funds - 'Y' stated property was purchased for State as benamidar and accordingly executed release deed in favour of State was not protested by 'X' eventhough he was alive for 5 years after release deed - Ex. DW 9/1 relied by respondents could not be considered as there was no reference of this suit property and hence could not be claimed as private property of 'X' - Present plea that suit property was joint family property was contradictory to earlier contention that this property was given by 'X' in consideration of amount paid by her for purchase of Rolls Royce Car for 'X' - Recognition by sovereign parties to covenant u/art.XII that suit property was private property of 'A' would amount to act of State - Orders of DB were set aside - CA 1208/90 was dismissed and CA no. 5857/83 was allowed - Appeals disposed of.
