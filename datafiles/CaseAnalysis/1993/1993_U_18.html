Ram Chand v Union Of India (N.P. Singh, J)

Supreme Court of India

30 September 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

N.P. Singh, S.P. Bharucha, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 1507; (1994) 1 SCC 44; AIR 1993 SCW 3479; 1993 (52) DLT 103; JT 1993 (5) SC 465; 1993 (2) RentLR 593; 1993(3) SCALE 906; [1993] Supp2 S.C.R. 558; 1994 (1) UJ 21

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4, 6, 11(1), 16 and 23(1) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 31A - Constitution (Forty-Fourth Amendment) Act, 1978 - Land Acquisition (Amendment) Act, 1984 - Land Acquisition (Amendment And Validation) Act, 1967, s. 4(3) - Whether issuance of declarations in respect of lands in question and taking no further steps for making of award or payment of compensation for more than 14 years from date of declarations u/s. 6 of Land Acquisition Act, was only to peg market value of lands between years 1959 and 1965 and whether this has inflicted great injury to petitioners, in as much as compensation has been worked out with reference to dates of notifications u/s. 4? - It is settled that in a statute where for exercise of power no time-limit is fixed, it has to be exercised within a time which can be held to be reasonable, 1984 Amendment Act has introduced a time-limit before which a declaration u/s. 6 has to be made, by same Act, s. 11 A has been introduced, which fixes a time-limit for making of award u/s. 11 of Act, failing which proceeding for acquisition of land is to lapse, s. 11-A neither purports to validate any pending acquisition, nor it condones any delay which had already occurred; proviso, says that in cases where declaration u/s. 6 had been published before commencement of 1984 Amendment Act, award shall be made within a period of 2 years from such commencement; proviso to s. 11 A, does not, in any manner, validates or justifies inaction on part of authorities in delaying and keeping land acquisition proceedings pending for a decade and a half, after declarations had been made u/s. 6, also s. 11-A will not be applicable to land acquisition proceedings, where awards had been made, as in present cases, prior to 24-09-1984 when 1984 Amending Act came in force, by not questioning validity of acquisition proceedings for a long time since declarations were made u/s. 6, relief of quashing acquisition proceedings has become inappropriate, because in meantime, lands notified have been developed and put to public, grant of a modified relief, considered appropriate in circumstances, would be proper course to adopt, High Court or Supreme Court, can grant a modified relief taking into consideration injury caused to claimants by inaction on part of respondents and direct payment of any additional amount, in exercise of power u/arts. 226 or 32 of Constitution - Held, there was no justification on respondents' part for delay in completion of proceedings after judgment of Supreme Court in Aflatoon case after which, reasonable time for making awards was about 2 years from that date, beyond 2 years, time taken for making of awards will be deemed to be unreasonable and as such, after expiry of period of 2 years, some additional compensation has to be awarded to cultivators - Appeal partly allowed.
