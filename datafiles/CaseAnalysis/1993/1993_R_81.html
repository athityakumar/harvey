Dhondiram Tatoba Kadam v Ramchandra Balwantrao Dubal

Supreme Court of India

27 July 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Sahai, T.K. Thommen, Veeraswami Ramaswami

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 1448; (1994) 3 SCC 366; 1994 MahLJ 1284; 1994 (1) RentLR 210; 1994(1) SCALE 329; [1993] Supp1 S.C.R. 419; 1994 (1) UJ 371

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Rent Control - Bombay Tenancy and Agricultural Lands Act, 1948,ss.29, 32, 32(1-A), 32(1-B) - (A) Whether a tenant under Bombay Act, who surrendered tenancy can be said to have been dispossessed so as to claim benefit u/s. 32(1-B) of Act? - S. 32 entitles tenant to restoration of possession provided he was in possession on appointed date, ie, 15-06-1955 and was dispossessed before 1-04-1957 and his landlord was in possession on 31-07-1969; legislature intended not only to grant rights to those tenants who were in possession but also to restore land from which tenant had been evicted prior to 1957 on satisfying conditions mentioned in ss.32(1-A), 32(1-B) and make such a person on restoration of possession, purchaser of land - Held, s. 32(1-B) should be construed in a manner which must effectuate legislative objective of making every tenant purchaser of land if he satisfies conditions laid down in s.32(1-B) whether he was in possession or not - (B) Whether appellant entitled to declaration that he became a purchaser of land by operation of law u/s. 32(1-B)? - Voluntary giving up of possession does not amount to dispossession unless law provides for it; dispossession should have been either by legal process or by physical act of exclusion; tenant surrendering land either in accordance with provisions of law or leaving possession voluntarily would not be covered in expression 'dispossessed' - Held, appellant did not satisfy second requirement and consequently he did not become purchaser of land u/s. 32(1-B) - Appeal allowed.
