Government Of Andhra Pradesh Etc. Etc. v P. Dilip Kumar And Anotheretc. Etc

Supreme Court of India

03 February 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, M.M. Punchhi

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 935; (1993) 2 SCC 310; (1993) SCC (L&S) 464; 1993 (24) ATC 123; 1993 (1) CLR 647; JT 1993 (2) SC 138; 1993 (1) LLN 657; 1993 (2) MLJ 28; 1993 (2) MLJ(SC) 28; 1993(1) SCALE 245; [1993] 1 S.C.R. 435; 1993 (1) SLR 731

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: University Grants Commission

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14 and 16 - Validity of concept of preference in appointment - Held, zone of consideration was narrowed by eliminating candidates who did not succeed in qualifying test and out of those who succeeded in qualifying test and secured minimum marks after interview were considered and thereafter in process of selection preference rule was applied by first choosing post-graduates and thereafter graduates - That classification on basis of higher educational qualification with a view to achieving improvement in administrative performance is not abhorrent to arts. 14 and 16 of Constitution - Impugned orders of Tribunal are set aside - Civil Appeal Nos. 720 and 721 of 1988, 1260 and 1710 of 1990 and 1011 to 1024 of 1992 are allowed.
