Venkateshwara Theatre v State Of Andhra Pradesh And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

10 May 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Agrawal, M.M. Punchhi

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 312; (1993) 3 SCC 677; AIR 1993 SC 1947; AIR 1993 SCW 2098; JT 1993 (3) SC 270; 1993(2) SCALE 825; [1993] 3 S.C.R. 616; 1995 (96) STC 130

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law; Sales Tax; Service

Summary: Indirect Tax - Constitution - Andhra Pradesh Entertainments Tax Act, 1939, ss. 4 and 5 - Constitution of India, 1950, sch. 7, List 2, entry 62 - (A) Validity - Imposition of tax - Consolidated levy on basis of gross collection capacity per show - Whether ss. 4 and 5 ultra vires powers of State legislature under entry 62 List 2, sch. 7 - (B) Imposition of tax - Classification of theatres - Ss. 4 and 5, if violative of art. 14 - (C) Imposition of tax - Provision for enhancement u/s. 5(6) of 1939 Act, in case of increase in amount of gross collection capacity - Whether scheme suffers from inequality on account of absence of a corresponding provision for reduction of amount of tax? - Held, (A) not ultra vires powers of State legislature - (B) Not violative of art. 14 - Relates to cases where proprietor of theatre opts for payment of weekly consolidated amount -(C) Proprietor cannot complain that scheme suffers from inequality - Appeals and special leave petition dismissed.
