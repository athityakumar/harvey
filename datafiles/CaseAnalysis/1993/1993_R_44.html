State of Rajasthan and Another v Rajendra Kumar Godika and Others

Supreme Court of India

18 February 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

J.S. Verma, N. Venkatachala, Yogeshwar Dayal

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 901; (1993) Supp3 SCC 150; (1993) SCC (L&S) 934; 1993 (25) ATC 218; 1993 (2) CLR 378; 1993 (67) FLR 900; JT 1993 (4) SC 52; 1993 LabIC 964; 1993 (2) LLJ 1110; 1993(1) SCALE 637; [1993] 1 S.C.R. 1087; 1993 (2) SLJ 199; 1993 (2) SLR 8; 1993 (1) UJ 538

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Clubbing, Qualification And Experience, Rajasthan Educational Subordinate Service Rules, 1971

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 and 16 - Rajasthan Educational Subordinate Service Rules, 1971 - Whether policy adopted by State Govt. of first considering Group 'E' for promotion to Group 'D', Section II and promoting all found suitable, and then only considering qualified persons in Group 'F' for appointment to remaining vacancies for want of suitable persons in Group 'E' for promotion, when posts in Group 'D', Section II are required to be filled 100 per cent by promotion, is invalid for any reason? - Held, consideration first of all persons in Group 'E' for promotion to Group 'D', Section II and moving to Group 'F' if necessary, for filling only remaining vacancies by selecting outstanding amongst them ensures fairness to all while also maintaining efficiency in administration - Those found unsuitable in Group 'E' cannot complain of discrimination if persons duly qualified and more suitable performing similar functions are selected, since unsuitable left out in Group 'E' are excluded from competition on ground of unsuitability and cease to remain contenders for remaining posts - Appeal allowed.
