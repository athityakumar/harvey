D. Dasegowda v State of Karnataka and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 February 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, R.M. Sahai

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 366; (1993) Supp4 SCC 53; (1994) SCC (L&S) 173; 1994 (26) ATC 220; JT 1993 (Supp) SC 18; 1994 LabIC 28; 1994 (2) LLJ 873; 1993(3) SCALE 307; [1993] 2 S.C.R. 54; 1993 (3) SLJ 86; 1993 (4) SLR 649

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Karnataka Municipal Corporations Rules, 1977 - Recruitment - Promotion - Validity of appointment - Appellant was working as Assistant Engineer in Public Works Department and was further promoted as Executive Engineer and Superintending Engineer - Appellants appointment was challenged in the HC in contempt jurisdiction for non-implementation of the order passed in, Rules - Govt. repatriated the appellant to his parent department, just few months before his retirement - Appeal made to HC was dismissed and stated that appointment of appellant was illegal - Whether, appointment of appellant was invalid - Held, the basis on which the 1977 Rules were declared void was removed and a appointments made or continued before the commencement of the Amendment Act were declared to be valid and shall always be deemed to have been validly made for all purpose as if the said appointments had been made in the principal Act as amended by the Act - It was deemed and shall always been deemed that the appointment of the appellant as Addl. Chief Engineer is legal and valid - Appeal allowed.
