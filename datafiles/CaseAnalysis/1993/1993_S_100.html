Narpatchand A. Bhandari v Shantilal Moolshankar Jani and Another

Supreme Court of India

18 March 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Venkatachala, N.M. Kasliwal

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 973; (1993) 3 SCC 351; AIR 1993 SC 1712; 1994 (1) Bom.C.R. 608; JT 1993 (4) SC 510; 1993 (1) RCJ 417; 1993 (2) RCR(Rent) 133; 1993 (1) RentLR 519; 1993(2) SCALE 103B; [1993] 2 S.C.R. 471

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Rent Control - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 58 - Bombay Rents, Hotel and Lodging House Rates Control Act, 1947, s. 13(1)(c) - Eviction - Usufructuary mortgage - Quit notice - (A) Whether an usufructuary mortgagee was not entitled to recover possession of a premises from a tenant u/s. 13(1)(c)? - (B) Whether findings of appellate court recorded respecting acts of nuisance and annoyance constituting ground for recovery of possession of premises by plaintiffs from defendants was correct? - Held, (A) Tenanted premises, if is mortgaged by landlord by way of usufructuary mortgage, mortgagee thereunder would become entitled to receive rents and profits accruing from such property in his own right and on his own account - Legislative intent - Expression 'landlord' in s. 13(1) includes a usufructuary mortgagee where tenanted premises are subject of usufructuary mortgage - (B) No statutory definitions of 'nuisance' or 'annoyance' which u/s. 13(1)(c) constitute a ground for recovery of possession by landlord of a premises in occupation of a tenant - Appeal dismissed.
