J.S. Jadhav v Mustafa Haji Mohamed Yusuf and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 April 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mohan, Kuldip Singh

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 1350; (1993) 2 SCC 562; (1993) SCC (Cr) 645; AIR 1993 SC 1535; AIR 1993 SCW 390; 1993 (22) ALR 87; 1993 (2) BLJR 858; JT 1993 (2) SC 652; 1993 (2) LLJ 1225; 1993 (2) LLN 830; 1994 (1) MLJ(SC) 34; 1993(2) SCALE 429; [1993] 2 S.C.R. 1006; 1993 (1) UJ 728; 1993 (2) UPLBEC 1229

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Advocates Act, 1961 - Counsel - Compromise decree - Amount unpaid - Appellant was appointed as counsel by respondent - Appellant was given letter of authorisation to receive amount on behalf of respondent - Appellant received money from court receiver but not paid full amount to respondent - Respondent complained to Bar Council of India - Bar Council viewed burden of proof was upon appellant and receipts produced as evidence were not accepted - Appellant was suspended for two years - Hence instant appeal - Whether Bar Council was correct in suspending the appellant for two years and directing to pay Rs. 500 to respondent - Held, receipt was got up on a blank signed paper - Hence, due execution of receipt had not been proved by appellant - Statement of appellant that account books lost in transit had been rightly disbelieved - Misappropriation by appellant was fully established - SC decision in M. Veerabhadra Rao v. Tek Chand, 1984 Indlaw SC 222, followed - Appeals dismissed.
