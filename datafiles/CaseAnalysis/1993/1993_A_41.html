C.I.T. v Ahmedeabad Cotton Mfg.Co. Ltd.

Supreme Court of India

15 October 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Venkatachala, B.P. Jeevan Reddy

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 1526; (1994) 1 SCC 632; 1994 (1) BC 95; 1993 (115) CTR 401; [1994] 205 ITR 163; JT 1993 (6) SC 155; 1993(4) SCALE 250; [1993] 71 TAXMAN 56

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure; Socio-Economic

Summary: Income Tax and Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922,s.10(2)(xv) - Coffee Market Expansion Act, 1942 - Income Tax Act, 1961,ss. 37, 256(1) - Cotton Textiles (Control) Order, 1948, cls. 21-A, 21-C(1)(b) - Whether amount paid by Mills concerned to Govt. under one or other scheme becomes business expenditure of Mills u/s. 37 of Income Tax Act, 1961 as would entitle such Mills to claim deduction as its business expenditure for accounting year? - A payment made by an assessee doing business in accounting year is not entitled to claim deduction, u/s. 37 of 1961 Act, of an amount paid by such assessee during year as an amount of penalty or an amount akin to penalty for any breach or infraction of law or any public policy which is sought to be achieved by such law, but if such payment is made by assessee during relevant accounting year without any breach or infraction of any law or any public policy sought to be achieved by it and in fact in obedience to provisions of such law as a measure of business expediency, there could be no valid reason not to allow such payment as deductible expenditure of assessee u/s. 37 of Act; what needs to be done by an assessing authority under Act, in examining claim of an assessee that payment made by such assessee was a deductible expenditure u/s. 37 although called penalty, is to see whether law or scheme under which amount was paid required such payment to be made, as penalty or as something akin to penalty; if amount so paid is found to be not a penalty or something akin to penalty due to fact that amount paid by assessee was in exercise of option conferred upon him under very law or scheme concerned, assessing authority has to regard such payment as business expenditure of assessee, allowable u/s. 37 as an incident of business laid out and expended wholly and exclusively for purposes of business, however, if such payment of assessee is that which is made in exercise of option given to such assessee by law or statutory scheme there arises no need for assessing authority to go into question whether payment could be regarded as that made as a measure of business expediency, for it cannot ignore fact in that law or statutory scheme enables incurring of such expenditure in course of assessee's business - Held, HC's judgment under present appeal being rendered following its earlier judgments in Rustam Mills case and Tarun Mills case which are found to have been rendered in consonance with law, same is not liable to be interfered with - Appeals dismissed.
