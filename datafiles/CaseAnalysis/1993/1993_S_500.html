Sarbir Singh v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

11 February 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

N.P. Singh, K. Jayachandra Reddy

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 1372; (1993) Supp3 SCC 41; (1993) SCC (Cr) 860; 1993 (1) CCR 75; 1993 (1) Crimes 616; 1993 CRLJ 1395; 1993 CrLR(SC) 226; JT 1993 (2) SC 159; [1993] 1 S.C.R. 1027

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Aerated Water

Summary: Criminal - Murder - Indianz Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Appellant and deceased were close friends but appellant haboured a suspicion that deceased was misbehaving with his wife - Appellant murdered deceased - Only circumstantial evidence available against appellant - Whether conviction of appellant is sustainable? - Held, yes - Based on circumstantial evidence, chain of events proves that appellant committed murder of deceased - Circumstances which point to guilt of appellant are that deceased and appellant were last seen together by prosecution witnesses, blood stained shirt of appellant seized from laundry, injuries on hand of appellant - Most important aspect of crime was grudge of appellant towards deceased suspecting that deceased was misbehaving with his wife - Further held, evidence adduced on behalf of prosecution is clear, unambiguous and in unmistakable terms establish that the appellant is the perpetrator of the crime - No gap in circumstances to establish the guilt of appellant - Conviction sustainable - Appeal dismissed.
