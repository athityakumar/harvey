Panni Lal v Rajinder Singh and Another

Supreme Court of India

05 May 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Bharucha, M.M. Punchhi

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 1205; (1993) 4 SCC 38; 1993 (2) AWC 1117; 1993 (2) CCC 176; 1993 (1) DMC 603; 1993 (1) HLR 663; JT 1993 (3) SC 340; 1993(2) SCALE 806; [1993] 3 S.C.R. 589; 1993 (2) UJ 69

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Hindu Minority and Guardianship Act, 1956 - Natural guardian - Hindu minor - Permission of court - Sale deed executed by mother when respondents were minor - Deed was also attested by father - Upon attending majority respondents contended that sale made without permission of court was void - Trial court said sale voidable - HC dismissed appeal - Whether sale was effected by father of respondents because he had attested the deed executed by mother of respondents and thus satisfied requirements of s. 8 of the Act - Held, sale was neither for legal necessity nor prior permission of court was obtained - Notwithstanding the fact that father attested it, could not be held to be a sale by father and natural guardian satisfying requirements of s. 8 of the Act - Sale deed was void and not voidable - Appeal dismissed.
