Radhey Shyam Khemka and Another v State of Bihar and Another

Supreme Court of India

26 March 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

N.P. Singh, A.S. Anand

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 1622; (1993) 3 SCC 54; (1993) SCC (Cr) 591; AIR 1993 SCW 2427; 1993 (2) CCR 144; [1993] 77 Comp Cas 356; 1993 (1) Crimes 1132; 1993 CRLJ 2888; 1993 CRLR 358; 1993 CrLR(SC) 358; JT 1993 (2) SC 523; 1993(2) SCALE 266; [1993] 2 S.C.R. 699; 1993 (2) UJ 5

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Criminal

Summary: Corporate - Criminal - Companies Act, 1956, ss. 69 and 73 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, s. 482 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 405 and 409 - Whether the criminal proceeding against the appellants under the Indian Penal Code, 1860 for the offence of cheating, etc. amount to an abuse of the process of court since the different provisions of the Companies Act, 1956 are sufficient to protect the interest of shareholders? - The promoters or those in charge of managing the affairs of the company who are found to have committed offences like cheating, criminal breach of trust, criminal misappropriation or the like, cannot use the juristic entity and corporate personality of the company as a shield to evade themselves from prosecution for offences under IPC, 1860 if it is established that the primary object of the incorporation and existence of the company is to defraud the public, however, the court must be satisfied that prima facie an offence under IPC, 1860 has been disclosed on the materials produced, the ingredients of the different offences under IPC, 1860 need not be proved only by direct evidence and they can be shown from the circumstances of a particular case that the intention of the promoters or the directors was dishonest from the very inception or that they developed such an intention at some stage for their wrongful gain and causing wrongful loss to the investors - Held, the complaint mentions that in spite of the rejection of the application by the stock exchange, Calcutta, the appellants retained the share moneys of the applicants with dishonest intention, thus the prosecution pending against the appellants cannot be quashed only on the ground that it was open to the applicants to have recourse to the Companies Act, 1956, the power u/s. 482, Cr.P.C., 1973 cannot be exercised by the High Court to hold a parallel trial only on the basis of the statements and documents collected during investigation or enquiry, for purpose of expressing an opinion whether the accused concerned is likely to be punished if the trial is allowed to proceed - Appeals dismissed.
