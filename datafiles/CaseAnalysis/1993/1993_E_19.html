English Medium Students Parents Assn v State Of Karnataka

Supreme Court of India

08 December 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mohan, M.N. Venkatachaliah

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 1456; (1994) 1 SCC 550; AIR 1994 SC 1702; JT 1993 (6) SC 634; 1993(4) SCALE 627; 1994 (1) UJ 291

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Tort

Summary: Constitution - Education - Constitution, 1950, arts. 14, 29(1), 30(1), 32, 39(f) and 350-A - Committee Report regarding Kannad as a subject in primary school syllabus - Govt. Order - Whether order throws an undue burden on the students since the children are obliged to study three languages from the primary school stage? - Court held, careful reading of GO would clearly indicate that the element of compulsion at the primary stage is no longer there because the GO is unequivocal when it says from 1st to IVth standards mother tongue will be the medium of instruction, only one language from Appendix I will be compulsory subject of study and from IIIrd standard onwards Kannada will be an optional subject for non-Kannada speaking students, therefore this part of the GO is clearly in consonance with art. 350-A of Constitution - Further held, there is no violation of art. 29 or 30 of Constitution infringing the right of the minorities - Appeal dismissed.
