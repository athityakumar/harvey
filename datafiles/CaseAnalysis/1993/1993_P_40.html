Pavitri Devi and Another v Darbari Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 September 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, Kuldip Singh

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 501; (1993) 4 SCC 392; JT 1993 (5) SC 197; [1993] Supp2 S.C.R. 162

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Family & Personal; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Coparcener, Testamentary Succession, Succession & Inheritance, Coparcenary Interest, Evacuee Property & Displaced Persons

Summary: Family & Personal - Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 22, rr. 3 and 10 - Hindu Succession Act, 1956, ss. 6 and 30 - Indian Succession Act, 1925,s. 55 - One 'B' filed Partition Title Suit against his brothers and there heirs claiming share in coparcenary properties - Trial Court dismissed suit - 'B' died during pendency of suit - Appellant filed an application for substitution of her and her son as LRs on two grounds, namely as daughter of 'B' as well as registered gift deed - High Court held that appellant is not his heir and gift deed executed by 'B' was doubtful and dismissed appeal - Order of HC challenged - Held, evidence establishes that appellant is daughter of 'B', being Class I heir succeeded to estate of deceased by intestate succession u/s. 6 of Succession Act and by operation of s. 6 of Act r/w O. 22, r. 3 CPC, 1908 entitled to represent estate in partition action - Appeal allowed.
