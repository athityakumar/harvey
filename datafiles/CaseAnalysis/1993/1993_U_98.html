Jain Exports Private Limited and Another v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 July 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, M.N. Venkatachaliah, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 1217; (1993) 4 SCC 51; AIR 1993 SC 2383; 1993 (48) ECR 225; 1993 (66) E.L.T. 537; JT 1993 (4) SC 126; 1993(3) SCALE 54; [1993] Supp1 S.C.R. 185

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Trade

Summary: Trade - Imports and Exports (Control) Act, 1947, s. 3(1) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 133 - Customs Act, 1962, s. 111(d) - (A) Confiscation - Redemption of fine - Experienced importers assumed in good faith that non-edible variety of coconut oil was not a canalised item and could be imported under Open General Licence - Plea by importers that redemption of fine should be wholly waived or substantially reduced as their action was bona fide - (B) Whether rehearing of appeal is allowed on failure of party to produce documents, non-adjournment and thereby conclusion of hearing - Subsequent application for rehearing on very same grounds on which an adjournment was sought - Held, not tenable - Application rejected and demand of party strongly deprecated - Petition and appeal dismissed.
