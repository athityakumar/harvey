Mrs. Rekha Chaturvedi v University of Rajasthan and Others

Supreme Court of India

13 January 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

P.B. Sawant, N.P. Singh

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 327; (1993) Supp3 SCC 168; (1993) SCC (L&S) 951; 1993 (25) ATC 234; 1993 (66) FLR 464; JT 1993 (1) SC 220; 1993 LabIC 1250; 1993 (1) LLJ 818; 1993 (1) LLN 617; 1993(1) SCALE 93; [1993] 1 S.C.R. 186; 1993 (1) SLR 544; 1993 (1) UJ 333; 1993 (1) UPLBEC 745

Case Digest

Subject: Education

Keywords: Research

Summary: Education - Selection Procedure - University invited applications by its advertisement for appointment to 10 posts of Assistant Professors (Lecturers) - Out of 112 applications received, the Scrutiny Committee of the University selected six candidates who were respondents nos. 5 to 10 - Scrutiny Committee also placed five other candidates including petitioner on the waiting list - Petitioner had challenged the appointment of six respondents from General Category as Assistant Professors (Lecturers) in the University - Hence, the present appeal - Whether selection of the candidates acted illegally - Held, selected candidates had been working since eight years - There was no record to show as to how the Selection Committee had proceeded to weigh the respective merits of the candidates and to relax the minimum qualifications in favour of some in exercise of the discretionary powers vested in it under the University Ordinance - If the considerations, which weighed with the Committee in relaxing the requisite qualifications were valid, it would result in injustice to those who had been selected - When the Selection Committee, took into consideration the requisite qualifications as on the date of selection rather than on the last date of preferring applications, it acted with patent illegality, and on this ground itself the selections in question were liable to be quashed - Petition dismissed.
