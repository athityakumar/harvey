Binay Kant Mani Tripathi v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 May 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

Kuldip Singh, Yogeshwar Dayal

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 1754; (1993) 4 SCC 49; (1993) SCC (L&S) 1121; AIR 1994 SC 502; 1993 (25) ATC 462; 1994 (2) LLJ 30

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Administrative - Administrative Tribunals Act, 1985, s. 6 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 7(3)(c) - Constitution of India, art. 217(1) - Whether, appointment of Agarwal to the office of Vice-Chairman, 1950 Central Administrative Tribunal is in violation of the Administrative Tribunals Act, 1985 as he has crossed the age of 62 years? - The prescription as to age for the retirement of a Judge of the High Court under art. 217(1) of the Constitution of India, 1950 is a condition attached to the tenure of the office and not a qualification for appointment to the said office - Held, the contention of the petitioner is rejected - The age of superannuation for the office of Vice-Chairman is 65 years and Agarwal being qualified to be a Judge of the HC, his appointment cannot be challenged on the ground that he has crossed the age of 65 years - The writ petition is dismissed.
