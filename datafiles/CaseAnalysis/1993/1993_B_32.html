Hiralal Moolchand Doshi v Barot Raman Lal Ranchhoddas (Dead) By Lrs

Supreme Court of India

18 February 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

Yogeshwar Dayal, N.M. Kasliwal

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 363; (1993) 2 SCC 458; AIR 1993 SC 1449; 1994 (1) Bom.C.R. 632; 1993 (2) GLH 193; JT 1993 (4) SC 97; 1993 (1) RCJ 360; 1993 (1) RCR(Rent) 524; 1993 (1) RentLR 491; 1993(1) SCALE 629; [1993] 1 S.C.R. 1113

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Rent Control - Contract Act, 1872 - CPC, 1908, O. 23, r. 3 - Bombay Rents, Hotel and Lodging House Rates Control Act, 1947, ss. 12(3)(a) and 13(1)(g) - Suit for eviction filed u/ss.12(3)(a) and 13(1)(g) of 1947 Act - Decree passed on basis of compromise petition of parties -Terms of compromise containing admission of tenant as to existence of grounds on which eviction was sought - (A) Whether the decree is valid - (B) Whether landlord is required to plead his own comparative hardship in plaint itself - Held, decree is executable - The question of comparative hardship is not necessary to plead in the plaint - Appeal allowed.
