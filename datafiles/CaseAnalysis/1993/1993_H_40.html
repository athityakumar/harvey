Hoshiar Singh v State of Haryana and Others

Supreme Court of India

25 August 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Agrawal, R.M. Sahai

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 584; (1993) Supp4 SCC 377; (1994) SCC (L&S) 249; AIR 1993 SC 2606; 1994 (26) ATC 325; JT 1993 (5) SC 63

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Defence & Security Forces; Indirect Tax; Service

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 16 and 311 - (A) Selection by Board - Requisition by Govt. for certain number of posts - Selection and recommendation of larger number of persons by Board - Whether appointment on additional posts than number for which requisition was sent on basis of such selection valid? - (B) Recruitment - Selection by Board - Standards of physical fitness laid down in advertisement, within competence of authority - Advertisement silent about relaxation of standards by Board - Whether selection by Board by relaxing standards liable to be quashed? - (C) Recruitment - Selection on basis of examination and interview - Rejection of candidate by Board on ground of misbehaviour at time of interview - Valid - (1) Adopting course of giving zero mark in interview and making assessment on basis of marks in written examination - Whether justified? - (2) Minimum marks in interview not prescribed in Rules - (3) If material - Held, (A) Appointment, not valid - (B) Liable to be quashed - (C) Rejection, valid - (1) Not justified - (2) Minimum marks, not being prescribed - (3) Immaterial - Appeal allowed.
