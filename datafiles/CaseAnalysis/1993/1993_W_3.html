Warlu v Gangotribai

Supreme Court of India

23 February 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

J.S. Verma, B.P. Jeevan Reddy, N. Venkatachala

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 369; (1995) Supp1 SCC 37; AIR 1994 SC 466; AIR 1993 SCW 3882

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Appellant filed three Writ Petition Nos. 677, 679 and 760 of 1974 against the common order made by the Land Revenue Tribunal rejecting the three revisions filed by him - High Court by a common order dated 20-08-1980 has rejected all the writ petitions - Appellant has filed Civil Appeal No. 244 of 1982 by special leave against the HC's order only insofar as it relates to dismissal of Writ Petition No 677 of 1974 - Against the common order of the HC dismissing Writ Petition Nos. 679 and 760 of 1974, appellant has filed special leave petitions which are time barred - Issues arise - (A) Whether there is any ground to condone the delay in filing the special leave petitions? - Held, it does not find any cogent ground given in the application for condemnation of delay which in law can constitute sufficient cause to explain the inordinate delay in filing of the special leave petitions - (B) What is the effect of the dismissal of the special leave petitions on Civil Appeal No. 244 of 1982? - Further Held, after dismissal of the special leave petitions resulting in finality of the common order dated 20-8-1980 relating to dismissal of Writ Petition Nos. 679 and 760 of 1974, correctness of that order relating to dismissal of the Writ Petition No. 677 of 1974 cannot be examined for the obvious reason that interference in this appeal is bound to result in the making of conflicting orders regarding tenancy rights in the same lands - This alone is sufficient to require dismissal of Civil Appeal No. 244 of 1982 - Appeals dismissed.
