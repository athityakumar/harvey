V. Bhagat v D. Bhagat

Supreme Court of India

19 November 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, Kuldip Singh

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 780; (1994) 1 SCC 337; AIR 1994 SC 710; AIR 1994 SCW 45; 1994 (23) ALR 77; 1994 (1) ALT(SC) 14; 1994 (1) BLJR 1; 1994 (1) GLH 186; JT 1993 (6) SC 428; 1994 (2) MLJ(SC) 1; 1994 (1) PLR 603; 1993(4) SCALE 488; 1994 (1) UJ 70

Case Digest

Subject: Tort

Keywords: Lunatic Or Idiot

Summary: Women & Children - Hindu Marriage Act, 1955, s. 13 - Divorce - (A) Mental cruelty - A practicing advocate filed petition alleging adultery by his wife, a Vice-President in Corporation - While wife responded that he and all members of his family are lunatics - If constitutes mental cruelty to husband - (B) Legality of divorce granted on basis of pleadings, admitted material and without full trial - Held, constitutes mental cruelty - Permissible only to clear insoluble mess and in interest of both parties- Petition allowed.
