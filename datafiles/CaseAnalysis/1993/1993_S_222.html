Palghat Jilla Thandan Samudhaya Samrakshna Samithi v State Of Kerala

Supreme Court of India

03 December 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Bharucha, R.M. Sahai, N. Venkatachala

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 391; (1994) 1 SCC 359; JT 1993 (6) SC 622; 1994 (1) KLT 118; 1994 (2) MLJ 10; 1994 (2) MLJ(SC) 10; 1993(4) SCALE 584; 1994 (1) SLR 239; 1994 (1) UJ 476

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Human Rights; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Constitution - Reservation - Scheduled Castes - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 341 and 366(24) - Constitution (Scheduled Castes) Order, 1950 - Constitution Scheduled Castes (Modification) Order, 1956 - Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes Orders (Amendment) Act, 1976 - Whether decision of State of Kerala not to treat members of Thandan community belonging to erstwhile Malabar District, including present Palghat District, of State of Kerala as members of Scheduled Castes, is valid? - By reason of art. 341, a part or group or section of a caste, race or tribe, which, as a whole, is not specified as a Scheduled Caste, may be specified as a Scheduled Caste - Assuming, therefore, that there is a section of Ezhavas/Thiyyas community (which is not specified as a Scheduled Caste) which is called Thandan in some parts of Malabar area, that section is also entitled to be treated as a Scheduled Caste, for Thandans throughout State are deemed to be a Scheduled Caste by reason of provisions of Scheduled Castes Order as it now stands - Therefore it is not open to State Govt. to say otherwise, as it has purported to do in 1987 order - Court could not assume jurisdiction and order an inquiry to determine whether terms of Presidential Order included a particular community - No action to modify plain effect of Scheduled Castes Order, except as contemplated by art. 341, is valid - Held, Thandan community in instant case having been listed in Scheduled Castes Order as it now stands, it is not open to State Govt. or, indeed, to SC to embark upon an inquiry to determine whether a section of Ezhavas/Thiyyas which was called Thandan in Malabar area of State was excluded from benefits of Scheduled Castes Order - State Govt. is directed to grant to all members of Thandan community, including those belonging to erstwhile Malabar District and present Palghat District, benefits due to a Scheduled Caste included in Schedule to Constitution Scheduled Castes Order as amended up to date and to issue to them community certificates accordingly - Order accordingly.
