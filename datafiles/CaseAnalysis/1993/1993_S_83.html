V. K. Sood v Secretary, Civil Aviation and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 May 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, N.P. Singh

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 272; (1993) Supp3 SCC 9; (1993) SCC (L&S) 907; AIR 1993 SC 2285; 1993 (25) ATC 68; 1993 (2) CLR 735; 1993 (67) FLR 214; JT 1993 (3) SC 520; 1993 (2) LLJ 54; [1993] 3 S.C.R. 772; 1993 (4) SLR 91; 1993 (2) UPLBEC 1173

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 16 and 309 - (A) Constitutionality of recruitment Rules under proviso to art. 309 on grounds that they prescribe tailor made qualifications to suit particular individual or are discriminatory - (B) Appointment - Prescription of qualification, which is province of rule making authority or legislature - Interference sought from Supreme Court - Held, Rules cannot be impeached - Supreme Court cannot regulate prescription of qualification - Appeal dismissed.
