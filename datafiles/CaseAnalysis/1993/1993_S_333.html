Kathi Ramku Aligbhai v State of Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

24 March 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Jayachandra Reddy, G.N. Ray

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 1781; (1994) SCC (Cr) 171; AIR 1993 SC 2472; AIR 1993 SCW 2569; JT 1993 (Supp) SC 474

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Supreme Court (Enlargement of Criminal Appellate Jurisdiction) Act - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Evidence Act, 1872 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 379 - Murder - Appellant acquitted by Trial Court but on appeal convicted by High Court - Three eye-witnesses who were relatives of deceased - Enemity between appellant and deceased's family - Whether evidence of eye-witnesses reliable to justify conviction ? - Evidence of eye-witnesses corroborated by medical evidence - Held, testimony of eye-witnesses consistent and no indication of false implication - Appeal dismissed.
