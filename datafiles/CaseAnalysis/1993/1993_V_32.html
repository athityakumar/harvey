Central Board of Secondary Education v Vineeta Mahajan (Ms) and Another

Supreme Court of India

15 October 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

Kuldip Singh, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 729; (1994) 1 SCC 6; AIR 1994 SC 733; AIR 1994 SCW 77; 1993 (22) ALR 532; 1993 (52) DLT 429; 1994 (1) GLH 71; JT 1993 (6) SC 165; 1994 (2) MLJ 18; 1994 (2) MLJ(SC) 18; 1993(4) SCALE 153; 1993 (5) SLR 759

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Education - Use of unfair means at examination - Proprietary of - Whether High Court decision to quash decision of respondents in cancelling examination of petitioner for year 1993 is quashed and respondents are directed to declare result of petitioner within two weeks from today is correct? - Held, r. 36 1(iv) clearly defines 'the use of unfair means at examination' and lays down in simple language that a candidate having in possession papers, relevant to examination, in paper concerned, shall be deemed to have used unfair means at examination - Sine qua non, for misconduct under rule, is recovery of incriminating material from possession of candidate - Once candidate is found to be in possession of papers relevant to examination, requirement of rule is satisfied and there is no escape from conclusion that candidate has used unfair means at examination - Rule does not make any distinction between bona fide or mala fide possession of incriminating material - Set aside judgment of HC and dismiss writ petition filed by respondent - Appeal allowed.
