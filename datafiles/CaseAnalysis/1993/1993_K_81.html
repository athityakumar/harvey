Collector of Customs, Bombay v Krishna Sales Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

09 September 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, S.P. Bharucha

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 22; (1994) Supp3 SCC 73; AIR 1994 SC 1239; 1994 (54) ECR 37; 1994 (73) E.L.T. 519; 1993(4) SCALE 22

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Custom - Customs Act, 1962, ss. 130 E, 111, 157 and 130 E - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Confiscation - (A) Appeal to Supreme Court involving question of law or fact - Controversy about name and working of machinery imported and confiscated for misdeclaration - Tribunal sought expert opinion - In favour of Company - Appeal against order allowing release of goods (A) Interference sought - (B) Refusal to issue certificate to enable clearing of machinery - When appeal does not operate as stay of order appealed against - Held, issue in controversy is question of fact, that too a technical one, hence no interference - Not proper - Order accordingly.
