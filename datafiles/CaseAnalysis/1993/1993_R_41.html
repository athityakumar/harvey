Rajasthan S.R.T.C. v Krishna Kant

Supreme Court of India

23 September 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 1496; (1994) Supp1 SCC 268; (1994) SCC (L&S) 462; 1994 (26) ATC 841; 1994 (68) FLR 105; JT 1993 (5) SC 454; 1994 (1) LLJ 136; 1993(3) SCALE 884; 1993 (5) SLR 577; 1993 (2) UJ 733

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour and Industrial Law - Practice and Procedure - Road Transport Corporation Act, 1950 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Industrial Employment (Standing Orders) Act - Constitution of India, 1950, art.311(2) - Whether dispute between Corporation and employees whose services have been terminated was an industrial dispute under Industrial Disputes Act and in view of decision of SC in Premier Automobiles Ltd v Kamlekar Shantaram Wadke of Bombay, jurisdiction of civil courts is barred? - Held, respondents' case is that said action has been taken in contravention of standing orders framed by Corporation under Industrial Employment (Standing Orders) Act, therefore, instant cases are governed by decision in Jitendra Nath Biswas case and it must be held that jurisdiction of civil courts is excluded; from point of view of workmen also remedy of adjudication available under Industrial Dispute Act would be more beneficial to them than that of a civil suit in as much as civil court cannot grant relief of reinstatement which relief can be granted by Labour Court/Industrial Tribunal - In view of constraint placed by Order dt 18-10-1989 in SLP No 9386 of 1988, it is appropriate that matter is heard by a Bench of three Hon'ble Judges, therefore, matters are directed to be placed before Hon'ble Chief Justice for suitable directions - Matter referred to Larger Bench.
