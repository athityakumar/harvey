Parsinni (Dead) By Lrs. and Others v Sukhi and Others

Supreme Court of India

15 September 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, Kuldip Singh, M.M. Punchhi

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 621; (1993) 4 SCC 375; AIR 1993 SCW 3606; JT 1993 (5) SC 435; 1994 RD 49; 1993 (2) RentLR 438; 1993(3) SCALE 784; [1993] Supp2 S.C.R. 315; 1993 (2) UJ 737

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Land & Property - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Limitation Act, 1963, art. 65 - Immovable property - Appellants in possession and enjoyment of property for over 30 years without any let or hindrance and appellants shown as owners in entries in revenue records - Possession of appellants adverse to right of respondent - Suit was filed in 1963 asserting their rights as owners for first time by which date appellant have perfected their titles by prescription - Whether suit barred by limitation? - Held, burden to prove that appellant remained in possession in their right adverse to respondent lies on appellant - Plea that appellants were never in possession and enjoyment is belied by entries in revenue records - Appellants have perfected their title to 53 kanals 12 marlas by prescription and suit is barred by limitation u/art. 65 - Appeal allowed.
