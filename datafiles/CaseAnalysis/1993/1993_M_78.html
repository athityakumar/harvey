Sterling Computers Limited v Messrs M. and N. Publications Limited, and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 January 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

N.P. Singh, N.M. Kasliwal

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 1295; (1993) 1 SCC 445; AIR 1996 SC 51; AIR 1993 SCW 683; 1993 (1) CCC 86; JT 1993 (3) SC 187; 1993(1) SCALE 36; 1993 (2) SCJ 455; [1993] 1 S.C.R. 81; 1993 (1) UJ 456

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Municipal Council

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Constitution of India, 1950,arts. 12 and 14 - Agreement between United Database (India) Pvt. Ltd.(UIP), United Database (India) Pvt. Ltd.(UDP) and Mahanagar Telephone Nigam Limited(MTNL), a Government of India undertaking to publish telephone directories in stipulated time - UIP and UDI defaulted and committed breach of terms of agreement - Supplemental agreement was entered between UIP, UDI, MTNL and Sterling Computers Ltd. to carry out unexecuted portion of agreement - Writ before High Court questioning validity and legality of supplemental agreement on ground of mala fide and under garb of a supplemental agreement - Supplemental agreement is not extension of original agreement - Whether supplemental agreement was tainted with malice-the object being to provide unjust enrichment to UIP/UDI/ Sterling? - MTNL has applied 'irrelevant considerations' doctrine while granting a fresh contract for a period of five year through supplemental agreement has failed to take into account considerations which were necessarily relevant - Following rule of inviting tenders while granting contract for a further period of five years on fresh terms and conditions - Once process through which supplemental agreement was executed is held to be against mandate of art. 14 of Constitution supplemental agreement shall be deemed to be void - Appeal dismissed.
