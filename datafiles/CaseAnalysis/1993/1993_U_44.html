Noor Salman Makani v Union Of India

Supreme Court of India

27 October 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Jayachandra Reddy, G.N. Ray

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 121; (1994) 1 SCC 381; (1994) SCC (Cr) 521; AIR 1994 SC 575; 1994 (23) ALR 71; 1993 (4) CCR 428; 1994 CRLJ 602; 1994 (46) ECC 33; JT 1993 (6) SC 491; 1993(4) SCALE 262; 1993 (2) UJ 788

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Conservation of Foreign Exchange and Prevention of Smuggling Activities Act, 1975, ss. 3 and 9 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 22(5) - Detention - Representation to Ministry's office - Postal delay - (A) Delay of five days by sponsoring authority in forwarding parawise commented and well informed remarks to Ministry's office after excluding holidays - If amounts to undue delay - (B) Declaration - Detenu already in jail - (1) Release on bail - Order of bail not vital document - Whether declaration is liable to be set aside on ground that such order was not placed before authority before making declaration? - (2) Subsequent retraction of confession by detenu - Retraction found to be afterthought - Non-placing of such retraction before declaring authority - Held, (A) Not undue - (B) It liable to be set aside - Subjective satisfaction of authority not vitiated - Appeal and writ petition dismissed.
