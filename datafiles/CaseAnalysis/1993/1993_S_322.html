Kishun Singh and Others v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

11 January 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, N.P. Singh

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 197; (1993) 2 SCC 16; (1993) SCC (Cr) 470; AIR 1993 SCW 771; 1993 (1) BLJR 309; 1993 (1) Crimes 494; 1993 CRLJ 1700; JT 1993 (1) SC 173; [1993] 1 S.C.R. 31

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, ss. 193 and 319 - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Power to summon additional accused - Whether, Court of Sessions to which a case is committed for trial by a Magistrate can, without itself recording evidence, summon a person not named in police report presented u/s. 173, CrPC, 1973 to stand trial along with those already named therein, in exercise of power conferred by s. 319, CrPC, 1973? - Held, s. 319 covers post-cognizance stage where in course of an inquiry or trial involvement or complicity of a person or persons not named by investigating agency has surfaced which necessitates exercise of discretionary power conferred by said provision - S. 319 can be invoked both by court having original jurisdiction as well as court to which case has been committed or transferred for trial - Sweep of s. 319 is limited, in that, it is an enabling provision which can be invoked only if evidence surfaces in course of an inquiry or a trial disclosing complicity of a person or persons other than person or persons already arraigned before it - Scope of its operation or area of its play would also be limited to cases where after cognizance involvement of any person or persons in commission of crime comes to light in course of evidence recorded at inquiry or trial - On Magistrate committing case u/s. 209 to Court of Session bar of s. 193 is lifted thereby investing Court of Session complete and unfettered jurisdiction of Court of original jurisdiction to take cognizance of offence which would include summoning of person or persons whose complicity in commission of crime can prima facie be gathered from material available on record - Further held, once it is found that power exists exercise of power under a wrong provision will not render order illegal or invalid - Appeal dismissed.
