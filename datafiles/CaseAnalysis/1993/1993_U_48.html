Rama Kant Pandey v Union Of India

Supreme Court of India

05 February 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

L.M. Sharma, S. Mohan, S.P. Bharucha

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 1081; (1993) 2 SCC 438; AIR 1993 SC 1766; JT 1993 (1) SC 440; 1993(1) SCALE 434; [1993] 1 S.C.R. 786; 1994 (Supp) ShimLC 193; 1993 (1) UJ 578

Case Digest

Subject: Election; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Freedom Of Speech And Expression

Summary: Election - Constitution - Representation of People Act, 1951, s. 30(d) - Constitution, art. 14 - Reduction of period of twenty days to fourteen in s. 30(d) providing that date of poll shall not be earlier than the twentieth day after last date for withdrawal of candidatures - Whether violative of art. 14 of Constitution - Reduced period, if inadequate - Held, Not violative - Cannot be said to be inadequate in the changed circumstances prevailing in the country - Petition dismissed.
