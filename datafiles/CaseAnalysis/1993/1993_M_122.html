Shiv Kumar Chadha and Others v Municipal Corporation Of Delhi And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

04 May 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

N.P. Singh, M.N. Venkatachaliah, P.B. Sawant

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 1063; (1993) 3 SCC 161; JT 1993 (3) SC 238; 1993 (1) RentLR 792; 1993(2) SCALE 772; [1993] 3 S.C.R. 522; 1993 (2) UJ 381

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Central Provinces and Berar Municipalities Act, 1922, Delhi Municipal Corporation (Amendment) Act, 1984

Summary: Municipalities and Local Governments - Civil Procedure - Practice and Procedure - Delhi Municipal Corporation Act, 1957,ss.332, 343, 345-A, 347-B, 347-C, 347-D, 347-E, -Delhi Municipal Corporation (Amendment) Act, 1984,s. 7 - CPC, 1908, ss.9, O. 39, r. 3 - Order passed by Delhi High Court directing Municipal Corporation of Delhi to issue appropriate notices where illegal constructions have been made without sanctioned plans or by making deviations from sanctioned plans and also ordered that 'no civil suit will be entertained by any court in Delhi in respect of any action taken or proposed to be taken by corporation with regard to sealing and/or demolition of any building or any part thereof any person aggrieved by an order of sealing or demolition have right of filing an appeal to only appellate tribunal under Municipal act - Court also directed Corporation to approach those courts which have already issued injunction 'for variation and vacation of injunction orders in light of said order - Order of HC challenged - (A) Jurisdiction of Courts - Unauthorised construction - Held, regulations and bye-laws in respect of buildings, are meant to serve public interest but at same time it cannot be held that in all circumstances, authorities entrusted with demolition of unauthorised constructions, have exclusive power, to absolute exclusion of power of court - Court should not ordinarily entertain a suit in connection with proceedings initiated for demolition, by Commissioner, in terms of s. 343 (1) of Act - Court should direct persons aggrieved to pursue remedy before appellate tribunal and before administrator in accordance with provisions of Act - Further held, court should entertain a suit questioning validity of an order passed u/s. 343 of Act, only if court is of prima facie opinion that order is nullity in eyes of law because of any 'jurisdictional error' in exercise of power by Commissioner or that order is outside Act - (B) Temporary Injunction - Grant of - Held, grant of injunction is within discretion of court and such discretion is to be exercised in favour of plaintiff only if it is proved to satisfaction of court that unless defendant is restrained by an order of injunction, an irreparable loss or damage will be caused to plaintiff during pendency of suit - Purpose of temporary injunction is, thus, to maintain status quo - Before grant of an injunction, direct notice of application to be given to opposite party, except where it appears that object of granting injunction itself would be defeated by delay - But any such ex parte order should be in force up to a particular date before which plaintiff should be required to serve notice on defendant concerned - Order accordingly.
