Chandrakala Trivedi Smt v Dr S. P. Trivedi

Supreme Court of India

24 August 1993

Case Analysis

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 1736; (1993) 4 SCC 232; (1993) SCC (Cr) 1154; [1993] Supp1 S.C.R. 796

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Summary: Family & Personal - Hindu Marriage Act, s. 13(1) (i-a) - Cruelty - Petition for divorce - Allegations and counter allegations - Wife alleged intimacy of husband with another lady doctor-husband alleged wife's undesirable association with boys - Whether in matrimonial proceedings wife has to prove beyond all reasonable doubt that husband was leading an adulterous life? - Held, contention that Division Bench committed error in observing that matrimonial proceedings are quasi criminal in nature therefore, it was for wife to prove beyond all reasonable doubt that husband was leading an adulterous life appears to have some merit in view of a decision of this Court in Narayan Ganesh Dastane v Sucheta Narayan Dastane (1975 Indlaw SC 159) - But since marriage is dead and findings of fact cannot be set aside by this Court except that appeal can be sent back to Division Bench to decide it again which would mean another exercise in futility leading to tortuous litigation and continued agony of parties therefore it is futile to examine same - Findings recorded by three courts on unbecoming behaviour of appellant wife appear to be shaky therefore, such findings in judgement of all courts directed to be deleted - The husband directed to pay Rupee five lakhs or purchase one-room flat in Thane and Rupee two lakhs - Appeal dismissed.
