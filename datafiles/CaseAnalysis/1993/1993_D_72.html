Dr. H. Mukherjee v Union Of India

Supreme Court of India

28 September 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, M.M. Punchhi, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 1502; (1994) Supp1 SCC 250; (1994) SCC (L&S) 454; AIR 1994 SC 495; 1994 (26) ATC 833; 1993 (67) FLR 1223; JT 1993 (5) SC 439; 1993(3) SCALE 887; 1994 (1) SLJ 107; 1993 (5) SLR 590; [1993] Supp2 S.C.R. 529; 1993 (2) UJ 780; 1994 (1) UPLBEC 101

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Constitution, arts. 311 and 323 - Union Public Service Commission - Selection through UPSC - Not acceptance of recommendations of Commission by the Govt. - President / Governor has to forward memorandum containing reasons for non-acceptance for being placed before legislature - Whether aforesaid authority is obliged to communicate reasons either to commission or concerned candidate - Development subsequent to recommendation by Commission - Consideration by govt. - Held, not obliged - Govt. can take development into consideration - Appeal dismissed.
