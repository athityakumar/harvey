Anil Phukan v State of Assam

Supreme Court of India

17 March 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

A.S. Anand, N.P. Singh

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 403; (1993) 3 SCC 282; (1993) SCC (Cr) 810; AIR 1993 SC 1462; AIR 1993 SCW 1336; 1993 (2) ALT 54; 1993 (2) CCR 152; 1993 (1) Crimes 1180; 1993 CRLJ 1796; JT 1993 (2) SC 290; 1993(2) SCALE 88; [1993] 2 S.C.R. 389

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Murder - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Appellant charged for murder of deceased - Challenged - Held, no - Conviction cannot be based upon testimony of sole eye-witness - Testimony of sole eye-witness not reliable and his conduct was also doubtful - Single eye-witness not found to be wholly reliable witness as there were some circumstances which may show that he could have an interest in prosecution - Courts must insist upon independent corroboration of his testimony, in material particulars - Appellant entitled to be acquitted - Appeal allowed.
