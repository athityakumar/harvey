Bakshish Singh v Darshan Engineering Works

Supreme Court of India

11 October 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

P.B. Sawant, Yogeshwar Dayal

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 1536; (1994) 1 SCC 9; (1994) SCC (L&S) 302; AIR 1994 SC 251; 1994 (26) ATC 483; 1993 (67) FLR 1070; JT 1993 (6) SC 85; 1994 (1) LLJ 197; 1993 (2) LLN 650; 1993(4) SCALE 99; 1993 (5) SLR 742

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Qualifying Period Of Service, Working Journalists (Conditions of Service) and Miscellaneous Provisions Act, 1955

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Payment of Gratuity Act, 1972, s. 4(1)(b) - Claim of gratuity - Denied - Challenged - Appellant (Employee) gave his resignation which was accepted by the respondent (Employer) - Appellant claimed have not been accepted - Appellant approached the Controlling Authority - Said claim was resisted by the employer on the ground firstly that the appellant was entitled to gratuity only till the date he reached his superannuation age which was 58 years and since he had not completed 5 years of service by the time he attained 58 years of age, he was not entitled to gratuity u/s. 4(1) of the Act - Controlling Authority negatived the contention of the respondent - Appellate Authority confirmed the finding of the Controlling Authority and dismissed the appeal - HC affirmed the order of the Appellate Authority - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, the provisions for payment of gratuity contained in s. 4(1)(b) of the Act are one of the minimal service conditions which must be made available to the employees notwithstanding the financial capacity of the employer to bear its burden and that the said provisions are a reasonable restriction on the right of the employer to carry on his business within the meaning of art. 19(6) of the Constitution, the said provisions are both sustainable and valid. Hence the decision of the HC has to be set aside. Appeals allowed.
