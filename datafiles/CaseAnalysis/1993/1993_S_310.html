Shyam Nandan Prasad and Others v State of Bihar and Others

Supreme Court of India

13 July 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

M.M. Punchhi, A.M. Ahmadi

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 1789; (1993) 4 SCC 225; 1993 (51) DLT 541

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: English Companies Act, 1862, Bihar Panchayat Raj Act, 1947, Companies Act, 1982

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - Land Acquisition (Amendment) Act, 1984 - Bihar Panchayat Raj Act, 1947 - Companies Act, 1982 - English Companies Act, 1862 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Companies Act, 1956 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Land Acquisition (Companies) Rules, 1963 - Appellant Society put a proposal to State Govt. to acquire 59.95 acres of land - State Govt. issued a notification u/s. 4 of Act on 21-04-1981 inviting interested persons to file their objections u/s. 5A of said Act - Declaration u/s. 6 of Act was made to acquire land to extent aforementioned except 5 acres which were set apart for allotment to persons likely to be disturbed by acquisition - (A) Whether mandatory provisions of s. 5-A had been violated? - Held, provision embodies a just and wholesome principle that a person whose property is being, or is intended to be acquired should have occasion to persuade authorities concerned that his property be not touched for acquisition - This right is not absolute, however, if appropriate Govt, in its discretion, chooses to dispense with its applicability by invoking urgency provisions of s. 17 of Act - But once s. 5-A is kept applicable, there is no cause to treat its provisions lightly or casually - (B) Whether there was a supposed violation of s. 40 of Act and r. 4 of Land Acquisition (Companies) Rules, 1963? - Further held, s. 40 provides that consent of appropriate Govt. shall not be available unless it is satisfied either on report of Collector u/s. 5-A - Society as a public company would require to satisfy in an enquiry u/s. 40 of Act that it requires land for any of purposes mentioned in cls. (a), (aa) and (b) before it can obtain consent of appropriate Govt. on basis thereof and enter into an agreement as envisaged u/s. 41 - In order to approach Govt. with a request for acquisition company has to satisfy provisions of rules also - R. 4 is mandatory and essential to be complied with has been ruled by this court in various decisions - Matters remitted to High Court - Appeals allowed.
