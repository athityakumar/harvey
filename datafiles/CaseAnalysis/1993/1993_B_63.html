Management of Reserve Bank of India, New Delhi v Bhopal Singh Panchal

Supreme Court of India

03 November 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

P.B. Sawant, S. Mohan, S.P. Bharucha

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 460; (1994) 1 SCC 541; (1994) SCC (L&S) 594; AIR 1994 SC 552; 1994 (26) ATC 619; 1994 (68) FLR 22; JT 1993 (6) SC 266; 1994 LabIC 80; 1994 (1) LLJ 642; 1994 (1) LLN 552; 1993(4) SCALE 360

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Absence From Duty, Reserve Bank Of India (Staff) Regulations, 1948

Summary: Service - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 3-C(2) - Reserve Bank Of India (Staff) Regulations, 1948, regns. 39, 46(1), 46(3), 46(4) and 47 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Whether order of suspension is automatically set aside on reinstatement and whether management cannot deal with period of suspension according to regulations governing service conditions? - Employee who is absent from duty on account of his arrest for debt or on a criminal charge or on account of his detention, is not to be considered to be absent on account of circumstances beyond his control, his absence throughout such period is to be treated as period spent on extraordinary leave when absence is not followed by discharge, termination of service or dismissal, as case may be - In view of regn. 46(5) r/w regn. 39(2) even if employee was reinstated in service, it was competent for bank, in circumstances of case, to treat employee as having absented himself without leave and to treat said period as being spent on extraordinary leave - Further, when he is treated as being under suspension during said period, he is entitled to subsistence allowance, however, subsistence allowance paid to him is liable to be adjusted against his pay and allowances if at all he is held to be entitled to them by competent authority, it is only if such employee is acquitted of all blame and is treated by competent authority as being on duty during period of suspension that such employee is entitled to full pay and allowances for said period - Held, during period, employee renders no work, he is absent for reasons for his own involvement in misconduct and bank is in no way responsible for keeping him away from his duties, therefore, bank, cannot be saddled with liability to pay him his salary and allowances for period, as it is, even during such period, employee earns subsistence allowance by virtue of Regulations, in circumstances, bank's power in that behalf is unassailable - Labour Court while acting u/s. 33-C(2) had no jurisdiction to decide said question - Appeal allowed.
