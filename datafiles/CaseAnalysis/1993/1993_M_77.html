Messrs Kishan Lal Lakhmi Chand and Others v State of Haryana and Others

Supreme Court of India

29 July 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, R.M. Sahai, S.P. Bharucha

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 1255; (1993) Supp4 SCC 461; JT 1993 (4) SC 426; 1993(3) SCALE 296; [1993] Supp1 S.C.R. 433; 1993 (2) UJ 534

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Municipalities & Local Governments; Service; Agriculture & Agri. Products

Summary: Indirect Tax - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950,arts. 246(3) and 286 (2) - Haryana Rural Development Act, 1986,ss. 3, 5 and 6 - Haryana Rural Development Act ,1983,s. 3 - Haryana Municipal Act, 1973 - Whether fee imposed under Haryana Rural Development Act, 1986 is fee not tax? - Object of Act is to improve agricultural production and marketing and sale of agricultural produce, bought or sold or bought for processing - Levy is on agricultural produce and burden is passed on to second purchaser - Dealer bears no burden u/s. 5(3) - From scheme of Act it would be clear that there is broad reasonable and general correlationship between levy and resultant benefit to producer of agricultural produce, dealer and purchasers as a class though no single payer of fee receives direct or personal benefit from those services - Court held, ss. 5(1) and 11 are valid and fee levied therein is not a tax but a fee towards fund to expend for purpose enumerated u/s. 6(5) of Act - Appeal dismissed.
