Union of India and Others v R. Reddappa and Another

Supreme Court of India

05 August 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Sahai, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 1681; (1993) 4 SCC 269; (1994) SCC (L&S) 142; 1993 (25) ATC 932; JT 1993 (4) SC 470; 1993 (2) LLJ 123; [1993] Supp1 S.C.R. 503

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Keywords: Ex Gratia Payment

Summary: Service - Railway Servants (Discipline and Appeal) Rules, r. 14 (ii) - Dismissal - For paraticipating strike - Validity - Can writ jurisdiction of High Court supercede appellant and revisional jurisdiction of Disciplinary Authority ? - Jurisdiction exercised by HC u/art. 226 or tribunal is not as wide as it is in appeal or revision but once court is satisfied of injustice or arbitrariness then restriction, self-imposed or statutory, stands removed and no rule or technically on exercise of power, can stand in way of rendering justice - Held, jurisdiction to exercise power u/r. 14 (ii) was dependent on existence of primary fact - If there was no material on which any reasonable person could have come to conclusion as is envisaged in rule then action was vitiated due to erroneous assumption of jurisdictional fact - Therefore Tribunal was well within its jurisdiction to set aside orders - An illegal order passed by disciplinary authority does not assume character of legality only because it has been affirmed in appeal or revision unless higher authority is found to have applied its mind to basic infirmities in order - Employees who were dismissed u/r. 14 (ii) for having participated in Loco Staff strike of 1981 shall be restored to their respective post within a period of three months from today - Appeals disposed of.
