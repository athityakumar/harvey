State of West Bengal v Orilal Jaiswal and Another

Supreme Court of India

23 September 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

G.N. Ray, K. Jayachandra Reddy

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 192; (1994) 1 SCC 73; (1994) SCC (Cr) 107; AIR 1994 SC 1418; AIR 1994 SCW 844; 1994 (1) BLJR 267; 1994 CRLJ 2104; 1994 (1) GLH 537; JT 1993 (6) SC 69; [1993] Supp2 S.C.R. 461

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Marriage - Suicide - Conviction - Acquisition - In suicide after marriage case, FIR was lodged against accused and case was filed - After considering evidences adduced in case and other materials on record Sessions Judge held that case was not properly investigated by police officer in charge and there were lapses on part of IO - Sessions Judge held that accused persons were guilty of offences u/s 306 read with s. 34 of IPC and u/s. 498-A read with s. 34 of IPC and passed order of conviction and sentences - In appeal, HC found that prosecution had failed to establish charges against appellants and cruelty as enumerated in s. 498-A of IPC had not been established and hence presumption tinder s. 113A of Indian Evidence Act could not be pressed into service - Accordingly, s. 306 of IPC also could not be invoked - Lack of independent evidence of inducement to commit suicide by accused conviction of accused persons was unwarranted - HC set aside conviction and sentences and passed order of acquittal in favour of accused persons - Hence instant appeal - Whether order of acquittal made by HC in favour of accused persons was justified - Held, offence u/s. 498-A of IPC was established against both accused and Order of acquittal was set aside - Both accused were convicted u/s. 498-A of IPC but considering age of accused 2, imposed sentence to suffer rigorous imprisonment for 2 years and fine of Rs. 2000 in default to further imprisonment for four months - Accused 1 was sentenced to suffer rigorous imprisonment for 3 years and fine of Rs. 2000 in default to further rigorous imprisonment of four months u/s. 498-A of IPC - There were materials on record to indicate that both accused were also guilty u/s. 306 of IPC but benefit of doubt was inclined and acquitted - Hence impugned judgment of HC was altered - Appeal partly allowed.
