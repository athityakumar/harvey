 Commissioner Of Wealth Tax v Dr. Karan Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 February 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

L.M. Sharma, B.P. Jeevan Reddy, S. Mohan, S.P. Bharucha, S. Ratnavel Pandian

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 1055; (1993) Supp4 SCC 500; AIR 1993 SCW 790; 1993 (110) CTR 221; [1993] 200 ITR 614; JT 1993 (2) SC 321; 1993 (2) MLJ 57; 1993 (2) MLJ(SC) 57; 1993(1) SCALE 270; [1993] 1 S.C.R. 569; [1993] 67 TAXMAN 3

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Finance Act, 1969, Constitution (Application to Jammu and Kashmir) Order, 1954

Summary: Constitution - Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Constitution of India, 1950 - Wealth Tax Act, 1957 - Whether Act is not applicable to the State of Jammu and Kashmir inasmuch as s. 1(2) of the Act in so far as it extends the Act to Jammu and Kashmir, is ultra vires the power of Parliament.

Held, the wealth-tax, as originally enacted was covered by Entry 86 of List I of the Constitution, and its extension to the State of Jammu & Kashmir was perfectly constitutional and consequently the impugned judgment of the HC is not correct. The impugned judgment is set aside and the Writ Petitions filed before the Jammu & Kashmir HC are dismissed. Petitions dismissed.
