Bhuwaneshwar Singh v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 September 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

A.S. Anand, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 429; (1993) 4 SCC 327; (1994) SCC (Cr) 1; AIR 1993 SCW 3072; 1993 (2) CalLT(SC) 31; 1993 CRLJ 3454; JT 1993 (5) SC 154; [1993] Supp2 S.C.R. 56

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Defence & Security Forces - Constitution of India, 1950 - Army Act, 1950, ss. 101, 102, 162(2), 166 and 169(2) - Army Rule, 1954, rr. 22, 27 and 71 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 428 - Court-martial - Pre-trial detention beyond three months - Without obtaining approval of Central Govt. - Legality of - Held, Act and r. 27 of Rules, illegal detention of person charged under Army Act, for a period beyond prescribed one, before commencement of his trial by court -Martial, would neither vitiate constitution of District Court-Martial nor affect trial held by District Court-Martial under provisions of Army Act - Failure to prevent unnecessary and prolonged custody prior to trial by Court-Martial would not, in any way affect Court-Martial proceedings or render nugatory findings of District Court-Martial or General Court-Martial, as case may be - Since, proceedings of District Court-Martial were held strictly in accordance with provisions of Army Act and Rules framed there under and do not suffer from any infirmity whatsoever, pre-trial illegal detention of appellant for a period of about one month or so would not vitiate finding of guilt and sentence recorded by District Court-Martial, which was confirmed by confirming authority and against which departmental appeal was dismissed by appellate authority and challenge in HC failed - Court, therefore, hold that for failure of authorities to obtain approval of Central Govt. for detaining appellant in custody, prior to his trial by District Court-Martial, for a period beyond 3 months, would not vitiate his trial by District Court-Martial or otherwise affect his conviction and sentence - Pre-trial illegal detention does not affect jurisdiction of District Court-Martial, validly convened, and such an illegal detention would not amount to a jurisdictional defect vitiating trial or findings - It would be appropriate to award him adequate compensation for violation of his fundamental right of personal liberty as guaranteed by art. 21 of Constitution - Supreme Court does not find any infirmity in any other finding recorded by High Court - Appeal dismissed.
