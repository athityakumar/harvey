State of Haryana and Others v Sant Lal and Another

Supreme Court of India

09 September 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Bharucha, B.P. Jeevan Reddy

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 319; (1993) 4 SCC 380; AIR 1993 SCW 3615; JT 1993 (5) SC 425; 1993 (91) STC 321; [1993] Supp2 S.C.R. 238

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Summary: Constitution - Indirect Tax - Sales Tax - Constitution of India, 1950 - Haryana General Sales Tax Act, 1973, ss. 38, 38(1) - Haryana General Sales Tax Rules, 1975, rr. 48, 53 - Legislative Powers - Dalal - Clearing and forwarding agents - Tax - Ultra virus - Respondents were clearing and forwarding agents and rendered to their clients the service of booking and taking delivery of consignments at the railway stations at Sirsa and Hissar - On the instructions of their clients, respondents had arranged for consignments to be transported by railway from the aforesaid railway stations - Respondents were asked to produce records for these consignments and the Income tax department held they were debarred from carrying such business - Appellants alleged that respondents were dalals, middle-men, and indulged in sale transaction thus they were liable to tax - Respondents were also made liable to the imposition of a penalty for an amount equivalent to 20 per cent of the value of the goods in respect of which particulars and information had not been furnished - Respondents appealed before the HC and the DB of the HC held in favor of respondents - Hence, the instant appeal by the appellant-State - Whether the State legislature had power to make rule u/s. 38 of the 1973 Act and whether s. 38 of the 1973 Act was ultra-vires and unconstitutional - Held, that the State Legislature was entitled to impose sales tax upon a person who carried on the business of selling goods and who had in the customary course of business authority to sell goods belonging to the principal - A clearing or forwarding agent, 'dalal' or person transporting goods did not carry on the business of selling goods and also he had no authority to sell goods belonging to the dealer - Moreover, there had to be a reasonable and proximate connection between the transaction of sale and the clearing or forwarding agent, 'dalal' or person transporting goods before the State Legislature could levy sales tax, by a legislation concerning the clearing and forwarding agent - Hence, s. 38 of the 1973 Act was beyond the purview of the State Legislature and was held to be ultra vires and bad in law. - Appeal dismissed.
