Union of India and Others v Rajendra Kumar Sharma

Supreme Court of India

23 February 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, Kuldip Singh

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 915; (1993) Supp2 SCC 366; (1993) SCC (L&S) 538; AIR 1993 SC 1317; 1993 (24) ATC 366; 1993 (1) CCC 559; 1993 (67) FLR 321; JT 1993 (4) SC 153; 1993 LabIC 1018; 1993 (2) LLJ 1041; 1993 (2) LLN 848; 1993(1) SCALE 729; 1993 (2) SLR 25; 1993 (1) UJ 454

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Reinstatement - Payment for off-days - Respondent was appointed as casual labour peon on daily wages and later his services were terminated - Respondent approached Tribunal for reliefs of payment for off days, reinstatement with back wages and regularization of service - Tribunal granted all three reliefs and hence, instant appeal - Whether impugned order of tribunal is sustainable.

Held, neither decision of SC nor proceedings of P and T Department issued on 10-2-1988 provide that casual laborers are entitled to be paid even on the days they do not work. Further, Tribunal has not examined if there is any other basis upon which respondent is entitled to such payment and also Tribunal observed that P and T Department is an 'industry' without full and proper discussion. Hence, order of Tribunal is set aside and matter is remitted to Tribunal for decision on questions arising in OA afresh. Order accordingly.
