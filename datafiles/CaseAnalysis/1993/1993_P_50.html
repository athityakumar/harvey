State of Maharashtra and Others v Prabhu

Supreme Court of India

03 November 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Sahai, N.P. Singh, S.P. Bharucha

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 461; (1994) 2 SCC 481; (1994) SCC (L&S) 676; 1994 (27) ATC 116; 1994 (2) CLR 12; 1995 (1) LLJ 622; 1993 (7) SLR 671

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950 - Initially, circular debarring respondent from being assigned any examination duty was issued which was subsequently withdrawn - Respondent was appointed as a member of the Divisional Education Board of respondent - On being apprised of the facts regarding circular issued against the respondent the Govt. issued a show-cause notice to respondent, answer to which was not accepted by appellant and cancelled the membership of the respondent - So, respondent filed petition against the order of the cancellation of his membership which was allowed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether, the HC was right in holding its decision while it allowed the petition of respondent - Held, even if the order of the Govt. regarding cancellation of respondent's membership was vitiated either because it omitted to issue a proper show cause notice or it could not have proceeded against the respondent for his past activities, the HC should have refused to interfere in exercise of its equity jurisdiction as the facts of the case did not warrant interference - Because, what could be more harmful to society than appointing the respondent as member of the Board, a position of importance and responsibility, who was found responsible for mass copying at the examination centre of which he was a supervisor - It was the responsibility of the HC as custodian of the Constitution to maintain the social balance by interfering where necessary for sake of justice and refusing to interfere where it was against the social interest and public good - So, it was concluded that, HC erred while allowing the petition of the respondent - Appeal allowed.
