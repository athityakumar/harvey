Chiranjilal Shrilal Goenka (Deceased) Through Lrs. v Jasjit Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

18 March 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, R.M. Sahai

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 972; (1993) 2 SCC 507; AIR 1993 SCW 1439; 1993 (1) ARBLR 443; 1993 (1) CCC 749; 1993 (1) HLR 502; JT 1993 (2) SC 341; 1993(2) SCALE 146; [1993] 2 S.C.R. 454

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Family & Personal

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Family & Personal - Indian Succession Act 1925,ss. 213, 276, 280, 295, 217, 223, 224, 227, 248, 273, 30, 8 and 20(4) - Criminal Law Amendment Act, 1952 - Hindu Succession Act, 1956, ss. 3(f) and 8 - Code Of Civil Procedure, O. 22, r. 22, O. 22, r. 102(11) - Petitioner died leaving behind last will in which he appointed his younger daughter as sole executrix of his will - Radhey Shyam claims to be adopted son of Petitioner - By order retired Chief Justice of Bombay High Court entered upon arbitration - Simultaneously proceedings in probate suit is being pursued in Bombay HC wherein Judge, on application, expressed doubt whether arbitrator has jurisdiction to decide probate suit - Whether arbitration is valid? - By operation of s. 211(1) executor of a deceased person is his LR for all purposes, and all property of deceased person vests in him as such - Grant of a probate by court of competent jurisdiction is in nature of a proceeding in rem - So long as order remains in force it is conclusive as to due execution and validity of will unless it is duly revoked as per law - Probate granted by competent court is conclusive of validity of will until it is revoked and no evidence can be admitted to impeach it except in a proceeding taken for revoking probate - Probate court has been conferred with exclusive jurisdiction to grant probate of will of deceased and on grant or refusal thereof, it has to preserve original will produced before it - Award deprives parties of statutory right of appeal provided under s. 299 - Probate court alone has exclusive jurisdiction and civil court on original side or arbitrator does not get jurisdiction, even if consented to by parties - Arbitrator cannot proceed with probate suit to decide dispute - Application allowed.
