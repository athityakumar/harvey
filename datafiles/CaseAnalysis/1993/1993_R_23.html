State of Himachal Pradesh v Raghubir Singh

Supreme Court of India

18 February 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

Yogeshwar Dayal, N. Venkatachala, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 365; (1993) 2 SCC 622; (1993) SCC (Cr) 674; 1993 (2) ALT 286; 1993 (3) AWC 1715; 1993 (1) CCR 87; 1993 (2) Crimes 887; 1993 CRLR 235; 1993 CrLR(SC) 235; JT 1993 (2) SC 152; 1993(1) SCALE 708; [1993] 2 S.C.R. 17

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal -

Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 376 - Rape - Conviction - On appeal, acquitted by High Court - (A) Whether conviction can be recorded on sole testimony of prosecutrix? - No legal compulsion to look for corroboration of evidence of prosecutrix before recording an order of conviction - Evidence has to be weighed and not counted - Conviction can be recorded on sole testimony of prosecutrix, if her evidence inspires confidence and there is absence of circumstances which militate against her veracity - Held, in present case evidence of prosecutrix is found to be reliable and trustworthy - Her statement receives ample corroboration from her father - Medical evidence has supported prosecutrix in all material particulars - Further held, Courts must be wary, circumspect and slow to interfere with reasonable and proper findings based on appreciation of evidence as recorded by lower courts, before upsetting same and acquitting an accused involved in commission of heinous offence of rape of hapless girl child - (B) Whether absence of injuries on male organs is fatal to case of prosecution? - No inflexible axiom of law which lays down that absence of injuries on male organ of accused would always be fatal to prosecution case and would discredit evidence of prosecutrix, otherwise found to be reliable - Held, that presence of injuries on male organ may lend support to prosecution case, but their absence is not always fatal - Respondent held guilty of an offence u/s. 376 IPC, 1860 and sentenced to suffer rigorous imprisonment for a period of five years - Appeal allowed.
