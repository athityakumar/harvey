S. Ganapathy and Others v Air India and Another

Supreme Court of India

16 June 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

M.M. Punchhi, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 1185; (1993) 3 SCC 429; (1993) SCC (L&S) 796; AIR 1993 SC 2430; 1993 (2) CLR 350; 1993 (67) FLR 246; JT 1993 (4) SC 10; 1993 LabIC 1966; 1993 (2) LLJ 731; 1993(3) SCALE 19; [1993] 3 S.C.R. 1006; 1993 (4) SLR 338; 1993 (2) UJ 317

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 2 (rr), 33 (2)(b) - Professional tax - Deduction - Allowable - Whether employer is justified in reducing amount by statutory tax deductions while computing amount of month's wages, to be paid u/s. 33(2)(b) of Industrial Disputes Act, 1947? - Held, when an order of discharge or dismissal of a workman is incomplete and inchoate until it's approval is obtained from Tribunal, there is no effective termination of relationship of employer and employee - Not only (sic even) in a limited way that relationship is snapped factually and one month's wage is given to employee to soften rigour of his factual unemployment, but content and character of wage would extendedly tend to remain same so far as subjection to statutory tax deduction is concerned, being remuneration paid as understood in s. 2(rr) of Act, on supposition that terms of employment, expressed or implied, were fulfilled and same was due as wages payable to workman in respect of his employment, or of work done in such employment, even though he was not put to work - Tribunal was in error in refusing approval on ground of suggested short payment and hence breach of s. 33(2)(b) of Act - Appeal dismissed.
