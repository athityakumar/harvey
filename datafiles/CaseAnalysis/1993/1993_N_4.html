C.I.T. v N.C.Budharaja And Co.

Supreme Court of India

07 September 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, N. Venkatachala

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 1439; (1994) Supp1 SCC 280; AIR 1993 SC 2529; AIR 1993 SCW 3317; 1993 (114) CTR 420; [1993] 204 ITR 412; JT 1993 (5) SC 346; 1993(3) SCALE 726; 1993 (91) STC 450; [1993] Supp2 S.C.R. 185; 1993 TAXLR 1117; [1993] 70 TAXMAN 312; 1993 (2) TLR 1117

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Finance Act, 1990, Finance Act, 1977

Summary: (A) Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 80-HH and 84 - A/ys 1974-75 and 1975-76 - Deduction u/s. 80HH - Manufacturing - Assessee was a firm of contractors constituted for the purpose of construction of a dam in Orissa - Assessee was awarded a contract of constructing a dam which was a notified backward district for the purpose of s. 80-HH of the Act - Assessee claimed the relief u/s. 80-HH of the Act - Income Tax Officer (ITO) allowed said claim - On appeal, Commissioner reversed the order of the ITO and held that the assessee engaged in the construction of a dam cannot be said to be engaged in manufacture or production of an article inasmuch as 'a dam is constructed and not manufactured - Commissioner also held that assessee cannot be held as an industrial undertaking merely because it has to undertake certain manufacturing process in the course of construction of the irrigation project - Tribunal allowed appeal filed against said order and held that the activity of constructing a dam can be characterized as an industrial activity - Tribunal also held that the activity of construction of a dam can be characterised as processing as well as manufacturing - On appeal. HC affirmed the order of the Tribunal - Hence, the instant appeal - Whether the construction of a dam to store water (reservoir) can be characterised as amounting to manufacturing or producing an article or articles within the meaning of s. 80 HH (2)(i) of the Act? - Held, since s. 80-HH of the Act was intended to encourage establishment of industrial undertakings in backward areas for the reason that such establishment leads to development of that area besides providing employment, a liberal interpretation must adopt which advances the purpose and object underlying the provision - The said principle, however, cannot be carried to the extent of doing violence to the plain and simple language used in the enactment - In the instant case, a dam was constructed; it was not manufactured or produced - The expressions 'manufacture' and 'produce' were normally associated with movables-articles and goods, big and small-but they were never employed to denote the construction activity of the nature involved in the construction of a dam or for that matter a bridge, a road or a building - Hence, it was not possible to accede to the contention that the activity of construction of a dam can be characterised as manufacture or producing of article or articles, as the case may be, within the meaning of s. 80 HH (2)(i) of the Act - Revenue's appeal allowed.

(B) Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 80-HH and 84 - A/ys 1963-64 and 1964-65 - Deduction u/s. 84 - Assessee engaged in the business of laying foundations for buildings and other structures by a specialised patented method known as pressure piling - Assessee claimed the benefit u/s. 84 of the Act on the ground that it was a newly established industrial undertaking within the meaning of the said provision - ITO denied the said relief on the ground that the assessee was not engaged in manufacture or production of articles - Asst. Commissioner dismissed appeal filed against said order - Tribunal allowed appeal filed against said order - HC held that it can be characterised as manufacture or production of an article - Hence, the instant appeal - Whether assessee was engaged in the 'manufacture' or 'production of articles' within the meaning of s. 84(1) of the Act for the a/y 1963-64, and relief u/s. 84(1) and s. 101 of the Act for the a/y 1964-65? - Held, if the construction of a dam, a bridge or a building as a whole does not amount to manufacture or production of an article, it was difficult to see how the laying of foundation or foundations for such dam, bridge or building can be characterised as manufacturing or production of articles - The piles, which the assessee lays by his particular method become a fixture in the earth - The process of laying foundation was an integral part of the construction of a dam, bridge or building - Each such structure requires a foundation - It was not as if the assessee supplies pre-fabricated piles, which were bore into the earth by the contractor or owner, as the case may be - The work was done on the spot and it was a works - contract - It was no different from any other works-contract which was done on the spot and becomes a part and parcel of a larger construction - Hence, assessee was not engaged in the 'manufacture' or 'production of articles' within the meaning of s. 84(1) of the Act - Revenue's appeal allowed.

(C) Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 32-A, 80-HH and 84 - A/ys 1974-75 and 1975-76 - Investment allowance - Assessee claimed deduction on account of investment allowance in terms of s. 32-A of the Act - The claim for the said allowance was disallowed by the Assessing Officer - Commissioner dismissed appeal filed against said order - Tribunal allowed the revenue's appeal filed against order of the Commissioner - Assessee thereupon applied for and obtained a reference u/s. 256 of the Act - HC referred the matter to the SC - Whether tribunal was justified in law in upholding the disallowance of investment allowance? - Held, it was not possible or permissible to read the word 'construction' as referring to construction of dams, bridges, buildings, roads or canals - Sub-cl. (iii) of cl. (b) of sub-s. (2) of s. 32A does not comprehend within its ambit construction of a dam, a bridge, a building, a road, a canal and other similar constructions - Hence, construction of dams and canals does not fall within the ambit of s. 32- of the Act and machinery or plant installed for that purpose not qualify for deduction - Revenue's appeal allowed.
