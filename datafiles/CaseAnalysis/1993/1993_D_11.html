Commissioner of Income Tax v Dalmia Cement (Bharat) Limited

Supreme Court of India

21 September 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, S.P. Bharucha

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 644; (1993) Supp4 SCC 737; 1993 (115) CTR 121; JT 1993 (5) SC 419; [1993] Supp2 S.C.R. 369; [1993] 70 TAXMAN 535

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 24(3) - Whether decision of High Court holding that Tribunal has justification to direct ITO to quantify losses for assessment yrs. 1952-53 to 1954-55 and allow set off against share income from partnership firm for 1960-61 and 1961-62, is correct? - Held, judgment requires reconsideration by a larger Bench as loss having been determined for relevant year in assessment proceedings in that behalf, it is difficult to see how assessee could be permitted to raise in course of proceedings for assessments for subsequent years question as to correctness of determination of that loss - There cannot be, at one and same time, two different assessments of income or loss in respect of assessment year - It is also a little difficult to see why a formal order notified by ITO to assessee u/s. 24(3) of 1922 Act is imperative before an appeal is maintainable - Matter to placed before larger Bench.
