State Of Bihar v Rameshwar Prasad Order

Supreme Court of India

07 May 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, S.P. Bharucha

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 1353; (1994) Supp1 SCC 574; AIR 1994 SC 501; 1994 (1) ARBLR 116

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Limitation Act, 1963 - Service of notice - Arbitral award - Arbitrator had forwarded the arbitral award in a seal to the court - Court, on request by the parties, had read the award in presence of the parties and their council - Objection-petition was filed on the 31st day after the award was made known to the parties - Respondent alleged that the period for limitation ran from 'the date of service of the notice of the filing of the award' and thus the objection petition was barred by limitation - HC had ruled that objection petition was barred by limitation - Hence, the instant appeal - Whether the period of limitation ran 'from the date of service of the notice of the filing of the award' and whether the objection petition was barred by limitation - Held, that the fact that the parties had notice of the filing of the award was not enough, the notice was to be served by the court - Hence, the period of limitation began upon the date of service of such notice u/cl. (b) of art. 1 19 in the Schedule of the 1963 Act and the limitation period expired on the expiry of the 30 days of the service of that notice for an application for setting aside of the award - Court had not served any such notice, and therefore, the objection petition was not barred by limitation - Objection petition was filed on the 31 day after the award was made known to the parties - In calculating limitation the day the order was read was to be excluded - Hence, the objection petition was within time prescribed by art. 1 19, cl. (b) of the 1963 Act - Appeal was allowed and the order passed by the HC was set aside - Matter was sent to trial court for disposal of the objection petition in accordance with law - Appeal allowed.
