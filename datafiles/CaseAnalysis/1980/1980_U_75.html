Shyam Ambalal Siroya v Union of India, and Others

Supreme Court of India

20 February 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

P.S. Kailasam, A.D. Koshal, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 248; (1980) 2 SCC 346; (1980) SCC (Cr) 447; AIR 1980 SC 789; [1980] 50 Comp Cas 737; 1980 CRLJ 555; [1980] 2 S.C.R. 1078; 1980 UJ 372

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Conservation of Foreign Exchange and Prevention of Smuggling Activities Act, 1974, ss. 11 and 3(1) - Central Govt. ignoring representation made by detenu for revocation of order of detention - Appellant detained under COFEPOSA,s.3(1) - Made a representation but wasn't considered for long time - Whether detention of appellant can be justified as being according to procedure? - It may be permissible for Central Govt. to take reasonable time for disposing any revocation petition, but it would not be justified in ignoring representation for revocation of detention as a statutory duty is cast upon Central Govt. - Appellant representation for revocation of order of detention was properly addressed but not forwarded to Central Govt. for four months and no action taken thereon even after filing of petition u/art. 32 -Held, detention of appellant cannot be justified as being according to procedure - Appeal allowed.
