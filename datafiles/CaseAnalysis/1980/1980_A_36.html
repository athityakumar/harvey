 P. S. R. Sadhanantham v Arunachalam and Another

Supreme Court of India

01 February 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

V.R. Krishna Iyer, R.S. Pathak, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, D.A. Desai, A.D. Koshal

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 329; (1980) 3 SCC 141; (1980) SCC (Cr) 649; AIR 1980 SC 856; [1980] 2 S.C.R. 873; 1980 UJ 403

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Constitution of India, 1950; Code Of Criminal Procedure, 1973- Right of a private party - not complainant, first informant - loose nexus with the complainant - special power u/art.136 of the Constitution, for leave to appeal against acquittal of the accused - Whether in a murder case where an appeal against acquittal was not filed by State, a private person could invoke the special power u/art.136 for leave to appeal against acquittal? - The Supreme Court has jurisdiction to entertain appeals against judgments of acquittal by the High Court at the instance of private parties - Held, Thus where in a murder case an appeal against acquittal was not filed by State, a brother of the deceased, a private citizen who is neither a complainant, nor a first informant could invoke the special power u/art.136 for leave to appeal against acquittal - (B) Whether appeal against acquittal in a murder case y a private person is violative of art.21 of the Constitution? - While considering the petition u/art.136 for leave to appeal against acquittal, the Court will pay attention to the question of liberty the person who seeks such leave from the Court, his motive and his locus standi and the weighty factors which persuade the Court to grant special leave- Held, when this conspectus of processual circumstances and criteria play upon the jurisdiction of the Court u/art.136, it is reasonable to conclude that the desideratum of fair procedure implied in Art. 21 is adequately answered- Petition dismissed.
