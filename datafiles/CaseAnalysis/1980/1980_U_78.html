N. C. Shinghal v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

19 March 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

D.A. Desai, E.S. Venkataramiah

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 321; (1980) 3 SCC 29; (1980) SCC (L&S) 269; AIR 1980 SC 1255; 1980 LabIC 710; [1980] 3 S.C.R. 44; 1980 SLJ 408; 1980 (2) SLR 118

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Eligibility - Qualifications

Summary: Service - Central Health Service Rules, 1966 - Central Health Service Rules 1963 - Promotion - Contravention - Vacancy - Qualification - Govt. had set up various institutions for medical relief and medical education under health scheme - Appellant came to be promoted to super time grade II - Respondents were promoted on different dates to super time grade II - Respondents promotions were in contravention of r. 8 of 1966 Rules - Appellant alleged to consider him for post of Director of Health Services - Appellant filed W.P.'s was not allowed by HC - Hence instant appeal - Whether appellant entitled to post of director of health services - Held, appellant was least qualified to challenge promotions of respondents - Even if respondents promotions were struck down appellant would not get post vacated by them - No material on record that appellant was qualified for post and it would be for Govt. to consider to deal with post - It would be giving him unfair advantage by giving technical benefit of situation - There was no justification for giving direction that appellant should be considered for post which was deemed to had fallen vacant - Appellant would be Head of Department and would continue to be in super time grade II and appellant could not appeal - Appeal dismissed.
