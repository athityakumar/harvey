Madan Lal v Mst. Gopi & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

29 August 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.V. Chandrachud, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, A.D. Koshal

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 182; (1980) 4 SCC 255; AIR 1980 SC 1754; 1980 (6) ALR 545; [1981] 1 S.C.R. 594

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: CPC, 1908, ss.100 and 101 - (A) Second appeal - Principles for interfering with concurrent findings of fact - (B) Hindu Law - Validity of adoption - Held, where both courts below ignored the weight of preponderating circumstances and allowed their judgments to be influenced by inconsequential matters, High Court justified in reappreciating evidence and arriving at its own conclusions - Held, the deed of adoption was invalid as the adopter could not be said to be in a fit state of mind to execute the adoption - Appeal Dismissed.
