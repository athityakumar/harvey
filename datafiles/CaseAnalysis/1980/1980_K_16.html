Commissioner of Income Tax, West Bengal-II, Calcutta v Kalyanji Mavji and Company

Supreme Court of India

14 January 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Pathak, N.L. Untwalia

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 332; (1980) 2 SCC 78; AIR 1980 SC 640; 1980 (15) CTR 154; [1980] 122 ITR 49; [1980] 2 S.C.R. 758; 1980 TAXLR 374; [1980] 3 TAXMAN 35

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act,1961 - Expenditure - Assessment - Deduction - Jurisdiction - Respondent assessee was a registered firm and owns several collieries - South Samla colliery was under military occupation - After colliery was released by military, assessee incurred a further expenditure amounting to Rs.1,61,742 on colliery with a view to resuming mining operations - In assessment proceedings for assessment assessee claimed a deduction of Rs. 1,61,742 u/s.10(2) (xv) of 1961 Act, but deduction was disallowed by Income-tax Officer on ground that expenditure was capital in nature - On appeal, Appellate Assistant Commissioner affirmed that expenditure was in nature of capital expenditure - HC assumed that expenditure was incurred by assessee for purpose of carrying on an existing concern and not for acquiring any concern not in existence - Expenditure was in nature of revenue expenditure and answered in favour of assessee - Appellant contended that HC had no jurisdiction to reappraise facts and finding on nature of expenditure was vitiated - Hence, instant appeal - Whether expenditure incurred by assessee in colliery was allowed as a permissible deduction and whether expenditure was a capital nature - Held, colliery was regarded as an asset of a continuing business all along, even during the period of military occupation was evidenced by fact that expenditure incurred by assessee during that period in respect of colliery was allowed as a permissible deduction in its income tax assessments - Nature of expenditure was clearly revenue in character - HC was right in holding that expenditure was not of a capital nature - Expenditure of Rs. 1,61,742 under consideration was also expenditure laid out as part of process of profit earning - Appeal dismissed.
