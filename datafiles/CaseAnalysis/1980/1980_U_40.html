Chowgule and Company Private Limited and Another v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

25 November 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Bhagwati, V.D. Tulzapurkar, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 276; (1981) SCC (Tax) 51; (1981) 1 SCC 653; AIR 1981 SC 1014; 1985 ECR 263; 1993 (67) E.L.T. 34; [1981] 2 S.C.R. 271; 1981 (47) STC 124; 1981 TAXLR 2929

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Keywords: Bombay Sales Act, 1953

Summary: Sales Tax - Indirect Tax - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956, s. 8(3)(b) - Central Sales Tax Rules, 1957, r. 13 - Process of mining iron ore and selling it in the export market after dressing, washing, screening and blending - Blending in the course of loading through mechanical ore handling plant - (A) Whether the blending of ore whilst loading it in the ship by means of the Mechanical Ore Handling Plant constituted manufacture or processing of ore for sale within the meaning of s. 8(3)(b) of 1956 Act and r. 13 of Rules 1957? - Held, when the chemical and physical composition of each kind of ore which goes into the blending is changed, there can be no doubt that the operation of blending would amount to 'processing' of ore within the meaning of s. 8(3) (b) of 1956 and r. 13 of 1957 Rules - (B) Whether items of goods purchased by the assessee for use in carrying the ore from mining site to the river side and from the river side to the one harbour could be said to be goods purchased for use in mining or in processing of ore for sale? - Held, the mining of ore was done by the assessee with a view to processing the mined ore through the Mechanical Ore Handling Plant at the harbour and the entire operation of mining ore and processing the mined ore was one integrated process of which transportation of the mined ore from the mining site to the harbour was an essential part and, in the circumstances, the machinery, vehicles, barges and other items of goods used for transporting the mined ore from the mining site to the harbour are goods used in processing of ore for sale - Appeal allowed.
