Life Insurance Corporation of India v D. J. Bahadur and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 November 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

V.R. Krishna Iyer, R.S. Pathak, A.D. Koshal

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 280; (1981) SCC (L&S) 111; (1981) 1 SCC 315; AIR 1980 SC 2181; 1980 LabIC 1218; 1981 (1) LLJ 1; 1980 (2) LLN 575; [1981] 1 S.C.R. 1083

Case Digest

Subject: Insurance; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Research, Closure, Public Sector, Appointed Day, Period Of Operation, Industrial Disputes (Appellate Tribunal) Act, 1950, Personality, Terms And Conditions Of Service, Instructions, Life Insurance Corporation (Modification of Settlements) Act, 1976, Assets And Liabilities, Existing Employees, Transfer Of Service, Employees Of The Corporation, Sastry Award, Circumstance, Profit Sharing Bonus, Controlled Business, Standardisation, Industrial Disputes (Banking Companies) Decision Act, 1955, Industrial Employment (Standing Order ) Act, 1946, Transferred Employees, Bombay Industrial Relation Act, 1946, Payment Of Bonus (Amendment) Act, 1976

Summary: Insurance - Life Insurance Corporation Act, 1956, s. 11 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 9 and 19 - Dispute regarding bonus between Life Insurance Corporation and classes III and IV employees - Remuneration, terms and conditions and other rights and privileges of transferred employees - Purpose of the section was smooth take-over and to promoting some common conditions of service - Employees absorbed irrespective of previous contracts of employment or industrial settlements or awards - Corporation reached a settlement with its employees on providing for payment of cash bonus under Industrial Disputes Act - Notices issued by Corporation u/ss. 9 and 19 of the Life Insurance Corporation Act depriving them of benefits of bonus - High court struck down the notices - Whether the previous settlements are applicable even after the notice u/ss. 9A and 19(2) of the Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 was served - Held, yes - As long as the award terminated u/s. 19(6) has not been substituted by another award, the industry has to proceed on the basis that the terms and conditions of the award would continue to govern the terms of employment - Specific period contractually or statutorily fixed as the period of operation of award/settlement - Thereafter award or settlement does not become non est but continues to be binding - Disposed accordingly.
