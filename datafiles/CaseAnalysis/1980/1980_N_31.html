Nishi Maghu Etc. Etc. v State Of Jammu And Kashmir And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

09 May 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

A.C. Gupta, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 303; (1980) 4 SCC 95; AIR 1980 SC 1975; [1980] 3 S.C.R. 1253

Case Digest

Subject: Education; Health & Drug; Indirect Tax; Service

Keywords: Eligibility - Experience

Summary: Education - Indian Medical Council Act, 1956 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 15, 32 - The selection of candidates admitted to the Government Medical College, Jammu, for the academic year 1979-80 is challenged in these petitions under art. 32 of the Constitution by some of the candidates who were not selected - 50 per cent of the seats earmarked for being filled upon the basis of open merit - Of the remaining seats, 25 per cent is reserved for candidates falling under the categories, to be filled on the basis of merit in each category - The selection of one candidate has been questioned on the ground that he does not fall under any of the categories mentioned in the orders - It is not necessary to examine the validity of the classification for the simple reason that this is not a category mentioned in any of the orders - Earlier it has been stated that following a request made by the Govt. of India 10 per cent of the seats in the College was reserved for students from other States on reciprocal basis a policy decision - The candidates, applying for admission to the M.B.B.S. course, should specifically mention in their application forms that they have not been selected/nominated by the Govt. of J &K for any training course within or outside the J &K State - The candidates, who have already been selected for any other training course within or outside the State, are not eligible - A candidate who tries to cheat will be disqualified and even if selected will not be given admission, or if secured admission, it will stand null and void - Kulbhushan Gupta was not eligible for selection though no objection was raised when he appeared for interview - Held, the admission of Harish Kumar has been set aside - To complete the quota of nominated candidates another candidate in his place may be nominated if the Govt. so desires - Appeal dismissed.
