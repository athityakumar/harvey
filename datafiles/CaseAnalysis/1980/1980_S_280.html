Rafiq v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

14 August 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

V.R. Krishna Iyer, O. Chinnappa Reddy

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 170; (1980) 4 SCC 262; (1980) SCC (Cr) 947; AIR 1981 SC 96; 1981 ALJ 139; 1980 (7) ALR 120; 1980 CRLJ 1344; 1981 CrLR(SC) 78; [1981] 1 S.C.R. 402

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Conviction and sentence for an offence of rape - Investigation followed resulted in a charge-sheet, a trial and eventually, in a conviction based substantially on the testimony of the victim - 7 year sentence of rigorous imprisonment was awarded - Appeal before High Court proved unsuccessful - Appeal against conviction and sentence - Strategy for a crime-free society is not draconian severity in sentence but institutional sensitivity, processual celerity and prompt publicity among the concerned community - 'Lawlessness is abetted by a laggard, long-lived, lacunose and legalistic litigative syndrome rather than by less harsh provisions in the Indian Penal Code, 1860' - Focus must be on the evil, not its neighbourhood - 7 year sentence was not too severe - Because, rape for a woman is deathless shame and must be dealt with as the gravest crime against human dignity - No interference on the score of culpability or quantum of punishment is called for in the circumstances - Special Leave Petition dismissed.
