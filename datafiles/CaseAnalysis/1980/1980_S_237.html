 Gurbaksh Singh Sibbia v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

09 April 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.V. Chandrachud, P.N. Bhagwati, N.L. Untwalia, R.S. Pathak, O. Chinnappa Reddy

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 316; (1980) SCC (Cr) 465; (1980) 2 SCC 565; AIR 1980 SC 1632; 1980 CRLJ 1125; 1980 CrLR(SC) 475; [1980] 3 S.C.R. 383

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Defence & Security Forces

Keywords: Defence Regulations

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 438(1) - Grant of Anticipatory bail - Conditions to be satisfied - Held, s. 438(1) of the CrPC lays down condition which had to be satisfied before anticipatory bail could be granted - Firstly, applicant must show that he had 'reason to believe' that he may be arrested for a non-bailable offence - The use of the expression 'reason to believe' showed that the belief that the applicant may be so arrested must be founded on reasonable grounds - The grounds on which the belief of the applicant was based that he may be arrested for a non-bailable offence, must be capable of being examined by the court objectively, because it was then alone that the court could determine whether the applicant had reason to believe that he may be so arrested - Secondly, if an application for anticipatory bail was made to the High Court or the Court of Session it must apply its own mind to the question and decide whether a case was made out for granting such relief - It could not leave the question for the decision of the Magistrate concerned u/s. 437 of the CrPC, as and when an occasion arises - Thirdly, the filing of a FIR was not a condition precedent to the exercise of the power u/s. 438 of CrPC - The imminence of a likely arrest founded on a reasonable belief could be shown to exist even if an FIR was not yet filed - Fourthly, anticipatory bail could be granted even after an FIR was filed, so long as the applicant was not arrested - Fifthly, the provisions of s. 438 could not be invoked after the arrest of the accused - Grant of 'anticipatory bail' to an accused who was under arrest involved a contradiction in terms, in so far as the offence or offences for which he 'was arrested', were concerned - After arrest, the accused must seek his remedy u/s. 437 or s. 439 of the CrPC, if he wants to be released on bail in respect of the offence or offences for which he was arrested - Appeal partly allowed.
