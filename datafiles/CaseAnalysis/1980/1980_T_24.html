Thammanna v K. Veera Reddy and Others

Supreme Court of India

23 July 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

P.S. Kailasam, R.S. Sarkaria

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 606; (1980) 4 SCC 62; AIR 1981 SC 116; [1981] 1 S.C.R. 73

Case Digest

Subject: Election; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Election - Practice &; Procedure - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 9A, 86(4), 109 and 110 (3)(c) - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, ss. 87(1), 98, 116A and 116C (1), O.23, r.1(1) - Whether appellant was entitled to challenge elections of respondent no. 1? - Appellant or any other elector did not make any application or complaint at trial of Election Petition in High Court, that election-petitioner has abandoned prosecution of petition or withdrawn from it and that applicant be substituted for election-petitioner to continue proceedings u/s. 110(3)(c) of Act - It will bear repetition that appellant took no interest, whatever, in controversy in election petition which was confined only to election-petitioner and respondent no. 1 - Conditions 1 and 3, satisfaction of which is necessary to give locus standi to a person to file an appeal u/s. 116-C, have not been filled in instant case - Appellant cannot, by any reckoning, be said to be a 'person aggrieved' by decision of HC, dismissing election petition - Appeal dismissed.
