Malak Singh Etc. v State Of Punjab & Haryana & Ors

Supreme Court of India

05 December 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 221; (1981) SCC (Cr) 169; (1981) 1 SCC 420; AIR 1981 SC 760; 1981 CRLJ 320; [1981] 2 S.C.R. 311; 1981 UJ 35

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Keywords: Surveillance Register, Punjab Police Rules 1933

Summary: Defence and Security Forces - Constitution, arts. 19(1)(d) and 21 - Punjab Police Rules, 1934, r. 23(4) - Appellants seeking removal of their names from the surveillance register - Contended that they had constitutional right to be given opportunity to show cause against such inclusion - Held, after going through relevant records, it was found that police had enough reasons to put their name in surveillance register and it would be contrary to the public interest to reveal the information in the history sheet particularly the source of information - The nature and character of the function involved in the making of an entry in the surveillance register is so utterly administrative and non- judicial, that it is difficult to conceive of the application of the rule of audi alteram partem - Appeal dismissed.
