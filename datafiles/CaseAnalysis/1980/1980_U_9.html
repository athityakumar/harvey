Jodh Singh v Union Of India & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

09 October 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

D.A. Desai, E.S. Venkataramiah

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 193; (1980) 4 SCC 306; (1980) SCC (L&S) 549; AIR 1980 SC 2081; 1980 (41) FLR 347; 1980 LabIC 1275; 1981 (1) LLN 75; [1981] 1 S.C.R. 929; 1981 (1) SLJ 129; 1981 UJ 210

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Service - Defence & Security Forces - Pension Regulations for the Air Force Rules, r. 74 - Will - Widow of deceased - Special family pension - Entitlement - Deceased was serving in Air force and bequeathed all his property to his father/petitioner and appointed him as executor under his Will - Deceased appointed petitioner and his mother as nominees in respect of his provident fund and nominated his parents, brothers and sisters to claim pensionary benefits in event of his death - On 10-3-1967 by order of President, Special family pension and gratuity was awarded to respondent no.2 being widow of deceased officer - Petition was filed by petitioner/father admitted to probate Will of deceased, which was granted but excluded special family pension and gratuity awarded to respondent no.2 - Petitioner filed suit against respondent nos. 1 and 2 for declaration that order awarding special family pension to respondent no.2 was illegal, unjust and improper, which was decreed by trial court - On appeal be respondent no.2, appellate court set aside decree of trial court and decision of appellate court was affirmed in second appeal before HC - Hence, instant petition - Whether special family pension awarded by President to the widow of deceased officer could be the subject matter of a testamentary disposition by the deceased in his life time.

Held, Special family pension is not admissible to the employee but to the specified members of the employee's family and that too in the event of his death while in service or after his retirement as provided in the Regulations. Thus, whether a widow has qualified for a special family pension, gratuity or ordinary family pension is a matter to be determined by the President. If the President is satisfied that the widow is eligible for pension, she cannot be denied the benefit by some other dependents of the deceased claiming that instead of the widow he or she should have been held eligible for special family pension. Therefore, it is irrelevant whether the deceased had shown his wife as his dependent or not if the President is satisfied that she as the widow of the deceased officer was eligible for special family pension. Therefore, special family pension is payable to the widow on the death of the officer. It is not payable in his life time. What is not payable during life time of the deceased over which he has no power of disposition cannot form part of his estate. It is the event of his death that provides the eligibility qualification for claiming special family pension. Such qualifying event which can only occur on the death of the deceased and which event confers some monetary benefit on someone other than the deceased albeit related to the deceased, cannot form part of the estate of the deceased which he can dispose of by testamentary disposition. Thus, it is unquestionably established that special family pension sanctioned to the widow of an officer of the Indian Air Force by the President of India u/r.74 of the Rules could not be subject-matter of testamentary disposition. Petition dismissed.
