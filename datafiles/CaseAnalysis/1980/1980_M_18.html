Advocate General, State of Bihar v Madhya Pradesh Khair Industries Limited

Supreme Court of India

05 March 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy, R.S. Sarkaria

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 151; (1980) 3 SCC 311; (1980) SCC (Cr) 688; AIR 1980 SC 946; 1980 BLJR 233; 1980 CRLJ 684; 1980 CRLR 260; 1980 MLJ 511; [1980] 2 S.C.R. 1172; 1980 UJ 474

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Practice & Procedure - Contempt of Courts Act, 1971, ss. 2 and 19 - Appeal u/s. 19 for contempt of court - Respondents involved in series of continuous litigation preventing decision of almost every case from being implemented - Whether it amounts to contempt? - Held, filing of repeated cases without obeying any one of previous proved malafide - Abuse of judicial process calculated to obstruct due course of judicial proceedings confirmed - Respondent expressed unconditional apology - Conduct of respondents is so reprehensible as to warrant condemnation by imposition of a sentence - Appeal allowed.
