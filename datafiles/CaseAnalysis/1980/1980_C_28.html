Commissioner Of Income-Tax, Andhra Pradesh Hyderabad v Toshoku Ltd., Guntur Etc.

Supreme Court of India

29 August 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

E.S. Venkataramiah, P.N. Bhagwati

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 181; (1980) Supp SCC 614; AIR 1981 SC 148; 1980 (19) CTR 192; [1980] 125 ITR 525; [1981] 1 S.C.R. 587; [1980] 4 TAXMAN 1

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income-tax Act, 1961 ss. 161, 143(3), 163 - Remittance of commission amount - Taxable - Challenged - 'X', statutory agent was dealer in tobacco and exported his goods to Japan and France where they were sold through assesses - Entire sale price was received in India by statutory agent who made credit entries in his books of account regarding commission amounts payable to assessees and remitted commission amounts to them subsequently - During assessment ITO held amounts sent to assesses were taxable and assessed them u/s. 143(3) r/w s. 163 of the Act - Statutory agent preferred appeals before Appellate Assistant Commissioner and Tribunal were unsuccessful - Aggrieved by said order of dismissal appeal was filed before HC u/s. 256(1) of the Act which answered against department - Hence, instant appeals - Whether commission amounts sent to assesses were assessable u/s. 161 of the Act.

Held, non-resident assessees did not carry on any business operations in the taxable territories. They acted as selling agents outside India. The receipt in India of the sale proceeds of tobacco remitted or caused to be remitted by the purchasers from abroad does not amount to an operation carried out by the assessees in India as contemplated u/s. 9(1)(i) (a) of the Act. Therefore, commission amounts which were earned by the non-resident assessees for services rendered outside India cannot, be deemed to be incomes which have either accrued or arisen in India. Thus, HC was, right in answering the question against the Department. Appeals dismissed.
