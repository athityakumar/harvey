Akhil Bharatiya Soshit Karamchari Sangh (Railway)Represented v Union Of India And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

14 November 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

V.R. Krishna Iyer, R.S. Pathak, O. Chinnappa Reddy

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 277; (1981) SCC (L&S) 50; (1981) 1 SCC 246; AIR 1981 SC 298; 1980 LabIC 1325; 1981 (1) LLJ 209; 1981 (1) LLN 27; [1981] 2 S.C.R. 185; 1980 SLJ 734; 1980 (3) SLR 645

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Unconstitutionality, Kerala State and Subordinate Service Rules, 1958, Railway Act 1890, Railway Board Act, 1905, Indian Railway Establishment Manual

Summary: Constitution -Constitution Of India,1950, arts. 14, 16 and 46 - Locus standi - Railway Board issued ten circulars favouring SC and ST - Appellants, a non-recognised association - Validity of circulars -Whether appellants being a non-recognised association can file a writ petition? - Held, that though appellants were non-recognised they are eligible to file petition as they are aggrieved parties and circulars are not violative of art. 14 to 16 as fundamental right of equality of opportunity has to be read as justifying categorisation of SC and STs separately for purpose of 'adequate representation' in services under State - Petition dismissed.
