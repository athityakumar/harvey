Mrs. Raj Kanta v Financial Commissioner, Punjab and Another

Supreme Court of India

07 May 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, P.S. Kailasam

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 612; (1980) 3 SCC 589; AIR 1980 SC 1464; [1980] 3 S.C.R. 1006

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Rent Control; Agriculture & Agri. Products

Summary: Land & Property - Rent Control - Punjab Security of Land Tenures Act, 1953, s. 9 - Whether or not High Court was right in interpreting s. 9 of Act by holding that term 'regularly' used in s.9(1)(ii) would not include a single default? - Legislature clearly intended to use word 'regularly' to mean payment of rent in a uniform and consistent manner without any breach or default and never contemplated that a single default could be condoned, and this inference is fortified by words 'without sufficient cause' i.e, if tenant had committed a default, whether one, two or more, same could only be condoned if sufficient cause is shown and not otherwise; words 'failure to pay rent regularly without sufficient cause postulate following conditions:(i) there must be a failure on part of tenant to pay rent; (2) such failure must be to pay rent regularly, i.e, rent should be paid punctually, consistently, without any break or breach; (3) if there is any default ranging from one to several, tenant has got to show sufficient cause if his case is to be taken out of mischief of s. 9(1)(ii) - Held, HC took an erroneous view of law in interpreting cls.(ii) of s. 9(1) and as tenants have been proved to have committed default in payment of rent for Kharif 1961, they must be held to have failed to pay rent regularly without sufficient cause as envisaged by cls.(ii) and therefore, legally entitled to ejectment - Appeals allowed.
