 Ramesh Chandra Etc. v State Of U.P. Etc.

Supreme Court of India

25 March 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

N.L. Untwalia, Y.V. Chandrachud, V.R. Krishna Iyer, P.N. Shingal, A.D. Koshal

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 131; (1980) Supp SCC 27; AIR 1980 SC 1124; 1980 ALJ 490; [1980] 3 S.C.R. 104

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Trade

Keywords: Tobacco

Summary: Trade - Administrative - Punjab Agricultural Produce Markets Act, 1961, ss. 9(2), 11 - Agricultural produce - Levy of market fee - Validity - Appellant/traders were carrying on business in State within jurisdiction of several Market Committees, filed writ petitions challenging levy of fee in HC, which were dismissed - Hence, instant appeals - Whether there was any perversity or illegality in judgment and decree of HC - Held, traders were required to take out licenses u/s. 9(2) r/w s. 11 of the Act for such place which was either a Principal Market Yard or a Sub-Market Yard or at any specified place in Market Area - Nobody could be permitted to carry on his business anywhere in Market Area as Market Committee would not be able to control and levy fee throughout Market Area - Further, it was not correct to say that agricultural produce should have been produced in market area in which first levy was made - It might have been produced in another market area or even outside State however, if a transaction of sale and purchase takes place of an agricultural produce as defined in the Act and covered by notification within a particular market area then fee should be charged in relation to said transaction - On the other hand, no provision in the Act to limit operation of law in a particular market area only in respect of agricultural produce produced in that area - If goods were brought in any market area and were dispatched outside it without any transaction of sale taking place therein, then no market fee should be charged - Hence, market fee should be regularised and be charged - Appeals disposed of.
