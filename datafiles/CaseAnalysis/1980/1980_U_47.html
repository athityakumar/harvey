Ichhu Devi Choraria v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 September 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Bhagwati, E.S. Venkataramiah

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 191; (1980) 4 SCC 531; (1981) SCC (Cr) 25; AIR 1980 SC 1983; 1980 CRLJ 1487; 1980 CRLR 616; 1980 CrLR(SC) 616; [1981] 1 S.C.R. 640

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Fema; Socio-Economic

Summary: Criminal - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 21, 22(5) and 32 - Conservation of Foreign Exchange and Prevention of Smuggling Activities Act, 1974, s. 3 - Non-compliance with requirements of art. 22(5) of Constitution and s. 3(3) of Act - Detention - Legality - Detenu was detained u/s. 3 of the Act to prevent him from smuggling goods and abetting smuggling of goods - Detenu was not supplied with two tapes and transcripts which formed basis of his detention - It was contended that in view of fact that the detaining authority did not serve on the detenu along with the grounds of detention, copies of those statements, documents and tapes it could not therefore be said that the grounds of detention were duly served on the detenu as required by art. 22(5) of the Constitution and s. 3(3) of the Act - Whether there was any breach of the requirements of art. 22(5) of the Constitution and s. 3(3) of the Act, for that is the breach which is claimed by the petitioner as invalidating the continued detention of the detenue? - Held, one of the basic requirements of art. 22(5) of the Constitution is that the authority making the order of detention must, as soon as may be, communicate to the detenu the grounds on which the order of detention has been made - If the grounds of detention are not furnished to the detenu within five or fifteen days, as the case may be, the continued detention of the detenu would be rendered illegal both on the ground of violation of art. 22(5) of the Constitution as also on the ground of breach of requirement of s. 3(3) - Further, if there are any documents, statements or other materials relied upon in the grounds of detention, they must also be communicated to the detenu, because being incorporated in the grounds of detention, they form part of the grounds and the grounds furnished to the detenu cannot be said to be complete without them - If this requirement of art. 22(5) of the Constitution r/w s. 3(3) is not satisfied the continued detention of the detenu would be illegal and void - Hence, u/art. 22(5) of the Constitution r/w s. 3(3), the detaining authority was bound to supply copies of the documents, statements and other materials relied upon in the grounds of detention to the detenu within five days from the date of detention - In the present case, grounds of detention were detention were served upon the detenu did not include the documents, statements and other materials relied upon in the grounds and forming part of them - There was a delay of more than one month in supply of copies of the documents, statements and other materials to the detenu - Also, burden of satisfactorily explaining this delay and showing that there was sufficient cause for it was not discharged by the detaining authority - Continued detention of the detenu held to be illegal on the ground of non-compliance with the requirements of art. 22(5) of the Constitution and s. 3(3) - Order accordingly.
