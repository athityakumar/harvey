Karbalai Begum v Mohd. Sayeed and Another

Supreme Court of India

07 October 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, P.N. Bhagwati

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 160; (1980) 4 SCC 396; AIR 1981 SC 77; 1980 ALJ 902; [1981] 1 S.C.R. 863; 1980 UJ 979

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Suit For Possession, Rights of co-sharer

Summary: Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Uttar Pradesh Zamindari Abolition and Land Reforms Act, 1950 - Uttar Pradesh Consolidation of Holdings Act, 1953, s. 49 - Suit for possession - Rights of co-sharer - Legality - Appellant (plaintiff) and respondents (defendants) were co-bhumidars and were in joint possession of plots in dispute - During consolidation proceedings, appellant's name was deleted from khewat and entire property was mutated in the consolidation of holding proceedings in the name of respondents - Aggrieved appellant filed a suit for joint possession over the share - Trial Court dismissed suit and on appeal Lower Appellate Court decreed the suit partly - Aggrieved appellant filed an appeal before HC, which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Respondents contended that lands in dispute were joint but during consolidation proceedings the plots in possession of appellant were occupied by Adhivasi who having acquired the rights of a Sirdar under the Act, appellant lost her title by operation of law - Whether impugned order passed by HC was justified - Held, it was well settled that mere non-participation in rent and profits of land of a co-sharer did not amount to an ouster so as to give title by adverse possession to the other co-sharer in possession - In instant case, Lower Appellate Court, however, found that on the admitted facts even after the abolition of zamindari, position was that in 1357 Fasli the appellant's name was clearly recorded as a co-sharer with respondents and continued to be so until 1359 Fasli - Lower Appellate Court further found that name of appellant was suddenly deleted after 1359 Fasli and there was no order of any authority or Court to show the circumstances under which the appellant's name was suddenly deleted nor were there any judicial proceedings under which the name of appellant as a co-bhumidar was deleted - Lower Appellate Court after a careful consideration of documentary evidence, conclude that some sort of fraud should have been committed by respondent when in 1362 Fasli the plots were entered exclusively in the name of respondents - Even if no share was given to appellants by the respondents, as the respondents were co-shares, unless a clear ouster was pleaded or proved the possession of the respondents as co-sharers would be deemed in law to be the possession of appellant - Admitted position was that if respondents were co-bhumidars with appellant in the khewat and had also sirdari tenants under them, how could the sirdari tenants occupy the land of one of the co-sharers leaving the respondent's alone so that the plots were re-allotted to them - Respondents being co-sharers of appellant would become constructive trustees on behalf of appellant and the right of appellant would be deemed to be protected by trustees - Thus, possession of respondents, apart from being in the nature of constructive trustees, would be in law the possession of appellant - Findings of Lower Appellate Court was a clear finding of fact and even if it was wrong (though it was absolutely correct) it was not open to HC to interfere with that finding of fact in second appeal - HC erred in law in holding that suit of appellant was barred by s. 49 of 1953 Act - Impugned judgment of HC was set aside - Appeal allowed.
