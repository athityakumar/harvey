Rachapudi Subba Rao v Advocate-General, Andhra Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

10 December 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Sarkaria, O. Chinnappa Reddy

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 223; (1981) SCC (Cr) 566; (1981) 2 SCC 577; AIR 1981 SC 755; 1981 CRLJ 315; 1981 CrLR(SC) 87; 1981 MLJ 340; [1981] 2 S.C.R. 320

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Judicial Officers Protection Act, 1850

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Practice & Procedure - Contempt of Courts Act, 1971, s. 2(c) - Judicial Officers Protection Act, 1850 - Dispute between appellant with defendant over title of property - Session Judge decreed suit in favour of defendants - Appellant raised some allegations against Session Judge - Sessions Judge sends a notice to High Court - HC convicted appellant for contempt of court on ground that allegations against Sessions Judge was criminal contempt u/s. 2(c) - Whether HC justified in convicting appellant u/s. 2(c) of 1971 act? - Held, appellant contended that Sessions Judge has deliberately delivered dishonest judgment so was guilty of serious misbehavior in performance of his duties - Facts revealed that tone, temper and contents of notice constituted deliberate attempt to scandalize judge to terribly embarrass him and lower authority of his office - Act of appellant falls u/s. 2(c) of criminal contempt - Though contempt committed was not in connection with any pending proceeding yet case falls u/s. 2(c), so conviction of appellant is justified - Appeal dismissed.
