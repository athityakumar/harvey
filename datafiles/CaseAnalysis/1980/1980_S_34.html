Shankarlal Gyarasilal Dixit v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

17 December 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.V. Chandrachud, A.P. Sen, Baharul Islam

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 47; (1981) 2 SCC 35; (1981) SCC (Cr) 315; AIR 1981 SC 765; 1981 CRLJ 325; 1981 MLJ 320; [1981] 2 S.C.R. 384

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Rape - Murder - Circumstantial evidence - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302 and 376 - Appellant convicted for offences u/ss. 376 and 302 on charge that he raped a five-year old girl and thereafter committed her murder - Whether appellant can be held guilty for either or both of these offences? - Judgment must show that finding of guilt, if any, has been reached after a proper and careful evaluation of circumstances in order to determine whether they are compatible with any other reasonable hypothesis - Held, circumstances relied upon by Courts are not full of suspicious - Appellant was not present in house at time when dead body was discovered - That makes it impossible to hold that appellant's plea is false - Besides, falsity of defence cannot take place of proof of facts which prosecution has to establish in order to succeed - A false plea can at best be considered as an additional circumstance, if other circumstances point unfailingly to guilt of accused - Sentence of death as also sentence of seven years' imprisonment imposed upon appellant is set aside - Appeal allowed.
