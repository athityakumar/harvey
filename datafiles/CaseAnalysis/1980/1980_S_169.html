International Tourist Corporation and Others v State of Haryana and Others

Supreme Court of India

15 December 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy, R.S. Sarkaria

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 264; (1981) SCC (Tax) 103; (1981) 2 SCC 318; AIR 1981 SC 774; [1981] 2 S.C.R. 364; 1981 TAXLR 289

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Indirect Tax - Haryana Passengers and Goods Taxation Act, 1952, s. 3 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 301 and 14 - Haryana Passengers and Goods Taxation Act, 1952, s. 3(3) - U P Motor Vehicles Taxation Act, 1935, ss 4 and 5A - Uttar Pradesh Motor Gadi Mal-Kar Rule, 1964 - Bihar Taxation on Passengers and Goods (Carried by Public Service Motor Vehicles) Act, 1961, s. 3(6) - (A) Whether it was incompetent for State Legislature to levy passengers and goods tax on passengers and goods carried on National Highways? - Held, before exclusive legislative competence can be claimed for Parliament by resort to residuary power, legislative incompetence of State legislative must be clearly established - Entry 97 itself is specific that a matter can be brought under that entry only if it is not enumerated in List II or List III and in case of a tax if it is not mentioned in either of those lists - Where competing entries are an entry in List II and entry 97 of List I, entry in State list must be given a broad and plentiful interpretation - Taxes on passengers and goods carried on National Highways also fall directly and squarely within and are included in entry 56 of List II - (B) Whether taxing power of state legislature could only be exercised with respect to goods and passengers carried on roads, maintained by state Govt. and not on National Highways which were maintained by Union Govt.? - Held, Haryana Passengers and Goods Taxation Act is a law made pursuant to power given to State Legislature by entry 56 of List II - Maintenance of highways other than National Highways is exclusively responsibility of State Govt. - State Govt. is not altogether devoid of responsibility in matter of development and maintenance of a national highway, though primary responsibility is that of Union Govt. - It cannot therefore, be said that State Govt. confers no benefits and renders no service in connection with traffic moving along national highways and is, therefore, not entitled to levy a compensatory and regulatory tax on passengers and goods carried on national highways - Held, sufficient nexus between tax and passengers and goods carried on national highways to justify imposition - Petitions dismissed.
