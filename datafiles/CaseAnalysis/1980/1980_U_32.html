Smt. Shalini Soni Etc v Union of India and Others Etc

Supreme Court of India

24 October 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy, R.S. Sarkaria

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 283; (1980) 4 SCC 544; (1981) SCC (Cr) 38; AIR 1981 SC 431; 1980 CRLJ 1487; 1980 CrLR(SC) 660; [1981] 1 S.C.R. 962

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Conservation Of Foreign Exchange And Prevention Of Smuggling Activities Act, 1974, ss. 3 and 8 - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 22(5) - Petitioners detained without being furnished reasons and other documents - Detenu is to be informed not merely of inferences of fact but of all factual material which have led to inferences of fact - It is clear that 'grounds' in art. 22(5) do not mean mere factual inferences but mean factual inferences plus factual material which led to such factual inferences - The 'grounds' must be self-sufficient and self- explanatory - In our view, copies of documents to which reference is made in 'grounds' must be supplied to detenu as part of 'grounds' - Held, delay in supplying copy stating reasons of detention illegal - Non-supply of documents violation of art. 22(5) - Detentions set aside - Petitions allowed.
