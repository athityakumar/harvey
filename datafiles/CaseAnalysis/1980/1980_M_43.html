Bai Dosabai v Mathurdas Govinddas and Others

Supreme Court of India

21 April 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy, R.S. Sarkaria

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 312; (1980) 3 SCC 545; AIR 1980 SC 1334; 1980 (21) GLR 776; [1980] 3 S.C.R. 762; 1980 UJ 655

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Land & Property; Service; Trusts & Associations

Keywords: Equitable Doctrine

Summary: Land & Property - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 136 - Transfer of property Act, 1882, ss. 40 and 54 - Specific Relief Act, 1877, s. 12(a) - Trusts Act, 1882, ss.3 and 94 - Appeal by Special leave as to suit for specific performance of specifically enforceable contract - (A) Obligation attached to ownership of property, if amounts to interest in property - (B) Effect of events and change in law in pendency of appeal - Held, not amounting to an interest, rather is in nature of a trust which can be specifically enforced - Can be taken into consideration and decree can be moulded accordingly by Supreme Court - Appeal dismissed.
