Baldev Raj Chadha v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

18 August 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

V.R. Krishna Iyer, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 173; (1980) 4 SCC 321; (1981) SCC (L&S) 1; AIR 1981 SC 70; 1980 (41) FLR 276; 1980 LabIC 1184; 1980 (2) LLJ 459; 1981 (1) LLN 1; [1981] 1 S.C.R. 430; 1981 (1) SLJ 188; 1980 (3) SLR 1

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Compulsory retirement - Legality - Appellant was promoted as Accounts Officer, however, was compulsorily retired in public interest u/r. 56(j)(i) of Fundamental Rules - Appellant challenged the retirement order before the HC - HC dismissed petition in limine - Hence, instant appeal - Whether HC was justified in passing the impugned order - Held, order of compulsory retirement fails because vital material, relevant to decision, had ignored and obsolete material, less relevant to decision, had influenced decision - Any order which materially suffers from blemish of overlooking or ignoring, willfully or otherwise, vital facts bearing on decision was bad in law - However, any action which irrationally digs up obsolete circumstances and obsessively reaches a decision based thereon, could not be sustained - It was directed to Accountant General, to take a fresh decision based on legal material and guided by legal principles - Appellant had reached age of superannuation in normal course and result was that consequence of any fresh order might only be financial - HC was not justified in passing the impugned order - Impugned order of compulsory retirement was quashed - Appeal allowed.
