Anantharam Veerasingaiah & Co. v Commissioner Of Income Tax, A.P.

Supreme Court of India

15 April 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Pathak, N.L. Untwalia, E.S. Venkataramiah

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 144; (1980) Supp SCC 13; AIR 1980 SC 1146; 1980 (16) CTR 189; [1980] 123 ITR 457; [1980] 3 S.C.R. 618; 1980 TAXLR 722; [1980] 3 TAXMAN 56

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Practice & Procedure - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 260 and 271(1)(c) - Whether Tribunal is justified in holding that no penalty is leviable? - Held, finding of Tribunal that no penalty leviable rests on an erroneous legal basis - High Court erred in entering into the facts of the case as it should not have rendered findings of fact - Appellate Tribunal directed to take up the appeal and to redetermine it - Appeal disposed of.
