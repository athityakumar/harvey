Hazari Lal v Delhi Administration

Supreme Court of India

15 February 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy, R.S. Sarkaria

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 120; (1980) 2 SCC 390; (1980) SCC (Cr) 458; AIR 1980 SC 873; 1980 CRLJ 564; 1980 CrLR(SC) 242; 1980 MLJ 756; [1980] 2 S.C.R. 1053

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Socio-Economic

Keywords: Illegal Gratification

Summary: Criminal - Corruption - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947,ss. 4, 5 - IPC, 1860, s.161 - Accused demanded and took bribe from prosecution witness - Trap was laid and accused was arrested - Whether conviction of accused was justified? - Held, Yes - Presumption u/s.4 (1) was attracted - Prosecution has proved guilt of accused beyond reasonable doubt - Chemical test on clothes of appellant confirmed that he had taken bribe - Orders of Trial Court and HC were sustainable - Appeal dismissed.
