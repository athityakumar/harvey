Surendra Kumar Verma and Others v Central Government Industrial Tribunal, New Delhi

Supreme Court of India

23 September 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy, V.R. Krishna Iyer, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 72; (1980) 4 SCC 443; (1981) SCC (L&S) 16; AIR 1981 SC 422; 1980 LabIC 1292; 1981 (1) LLJ 386; 1980 (2) LLN 456; [1981] 1 S.C.R. 789; 1981 (1) SLJ 107

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Termination - Validity of - Held, termination of appellants-services was a consequence of their failure to pass test prescribed for permanent absorption - Labour Court refused to give workmen relief of reinstatement in service with full back wages, even after passing test - But, instead, directed payment of compensation of six months' salary to each of workmen, in addition to retrenchment compensation - The appellants claim that they should be awarded relief of reinstatement with full back wages as was done in case of Santosh Gupta v. State Bank of Patiala (1980 Indlaw SC 334) - S. 25F, had full discretion not to direct reinstatement with full back wages, but, instead, to direct payment of suitable compensation - Court found 'that discharge of workman on ground that she did not pass test, which would have enabled her to be confirmed, was retrenchment within meaning of s. 2(oo) and, therefore, requirements of s. 25F had to be complied with' - Relief awarded - Appeal allowed.
