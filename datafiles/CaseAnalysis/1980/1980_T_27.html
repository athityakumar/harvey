Kishinchand Chellaram v The Commr. Of Income-Tax Bombay City Ii, Bombay

Supreme Court of India

16 September 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Bhagwati, E.S. Venkataramiah

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 194; (1980) Supp SCC 660; AIR 1980 SC 2117; 1980 (19) CTR 360; [1980] 125 ITR 713; [1981] 1 S.C.R. 720; [1980] 4 TAXMAN 29; 1980 UJ 952

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 34 - Concealed income - Re-assessment - Burden to proof lies on revenue - Tribunal found that amount remitted by an employee of assessee at Madras to other employee of Bombay was undisclosed income of assessee - Finding based on letter of Manager of Bank - But letter not disclosed to assessee - Nor proof of personal knowlege of statement - Tribunal held ought not to have accepted unsupported statement of Manager, based on hearsay, that the amount was remitted by assessee and ought not to have come to the conclusion that the said amount represented concealed income of assessee - Appeal Allowed.
