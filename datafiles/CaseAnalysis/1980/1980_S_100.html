 B. S. Yadav And Others Etc. v State Of Haryana And Others Etc.

Supreme Court of India

05 November 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.V. Chandrachud, P.N. Bhagwati, V.R. Krishna Iyer, V.D. Tulzapurkar, A.P. Sen

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 220; (1980) Supp SCC 524; (1981) SCC (L&S) 343; AIR 1981 SC 561; 1981 LabIC 104; 1981 (1) LLJ 280; 1981 (1) LLN 235; [1981] 1 S.C.R. 1024; 1981 (2) SLJ 67; 1980 (3) SLR 591

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Power To Make Rules, Determination Of Seniority, Bombay Prohibition Act 1949, Punjab/Haryana Superior Judicial service Rules 1963

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 235, 309 -Punjab/Haryana Superior Judicial service Rules, 1963, rr. 8, 12, 22 - Judicial Officers - Promotion - Direct recruitment - Legality - Petitioners were promoted in an officiating capacity to Superior Judicial Service - However, respondents were appointed directly from Bar to Superior Judicial Service - Petitioners filed instant writ petitions seeking for consideration that, first, as to the Rules governing seniority between direct recruits and promotees appointed to Superior Judicial Services of Punjab and Haryana and second, between control over District Courts and Subordinate Courts vested in HC u/art. 235 of Constitution and power conferred on Governor by proviso to art. 309 of Constitution to make rules regulating recruitment and conditions of service of persons appointed, inter alia, to Judicial Service of State - Whether method of confirmation adopted by HC by rotation of promotees and direct recruits in ratio of 2:1 was justified on a proper interpretation of relevant rules if so, was operation of r. 8 of the Rules confined to stage of initial recruitment to service by promotion and by direct appointment - Held, on a proper interpretation of the Rules governing Punjab and Haryana Superior Judicial Service, rule of rotation could not be read into the Rules of quota - Thus, ratio of 2:1 should have to be applied at stage of recruitment but could not on language of relevant rules be applied at stage of confirmation - Therefore, HC was not justified in applying the rule of rotation at time of confirmation of members of Superior Judicial Service who were appointed to that Service by promotion and by direct recruitment - in N. S. Rao v. State of Haryana, HC held that rr. 8 and 22 of the Rules were independent of each other, that rotational system could not impliedly be read into quota rule prescribed by r. 8 of the Rules and that members of Superior Judicial Service were entitled to claim seniority, strictly in accordance with provisions of r. 12 of the Rules - SC was unable to understand how, in discharge of its administrative functions HC could have failed to follow a judgment of its own special Bench consisting of five learned Judges - However, in absence of exceptional circumstances justifying reduction of their normal probationary period of 2 years, SC unable to uphold order of HC by which respondents were confirmed before they were normally due for confirmation - Further, power conferred by proviso on Governor was ex facie bad because such a power directly impinges on control vested in HC by art. 235 of Constitution - If at all any authority could exercise such a power, it was HC and not Governor - Thus, HC and not Governor had power of confirmation that normal period of probation of direct recruits was 2 years and that unless there were exceptional circumstances attaching to each individual case, a direct recruit could not be confirmed from a date earlier than date on which he had satisfactorily completed his probation of 2 years - Therefore, HC was not free to fix any period of probation as it likes or to reduce period of 2 years at its will and pleasure - Hence, impugned order of HC was in clear violation of guarantee of equal opportunity by which petitioners were prejudiced and same should be set aside - Petitions partly allowed.
