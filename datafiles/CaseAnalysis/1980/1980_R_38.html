Palace Administration Board v Rama Verma Bharathan Thampuran and Others

Supreme Court of India

27 March 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

V.R. Krishna Iyer, D.A. Desai, A.D. Koshal

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 133; (1980) Supp SCC 234; AIR 1980 SC 1187; [1980] 3 S.C.R. 187

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Keywords: Per Capita, Joint Hindu Family System, Kerala Joint Hindu Family System Abolition Act, 1975, Valiamma Thampuran Kovilakam Estate and the Palace Fund (Partition) Act 1961

Summary: Family & Personal - Kerala Joint Hindu Family System Abolition Act, 1975 - Valiamma Thampuran Kovilakam Estate and the Palace Fund (Partition) Act, 1961 - The subject-matter is the partition of the assets of the erstwhile royal family of the Maharajah of Cochin - Whether s. 4 of the Kerala Joint Hindu Family System (Abolition) Act, 1975 r/w amended s. 3 of the 1978 Act is applicable to the Cochin royal family? - After the promulgation of the 1978 Act, the Proclamation has to govern the Cochin royal family subject to s. 3 of the 1961 Act as amended by the 1978 Act which would fully apply to that family "notwithstanding anything contained" in the 1978 Act or any other law for the time being in force - Finality has thus been given to the provisions of that section which states that the partition is to be made among all the members entitled to a share of the estate and the Palace Fund u/s. 4 of the Kerala Joint Hindu Family System (Abolition) Act, 1975 (30 of 1976) - S. 4 of the 1976 Act is thus made specifically applicable to the Cochin royal family by reason of the amendment of s. 3 of the 1961 Act by the 1978 Act; and if this be so, the crucial date for determining the number and identity of the members of the family entitled to a share of the estate and the Palace Fund would be the 01-12-1976, i.e., the date on which the 1976 Act came into force - Held, petition partly allowed
