Smt. Rukmanibai Gupta v Collector Jabalpur and Others

Supreme Court of India

22 October 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

D.A. Desai, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 267; (1980) 4 SCC 556; AIR 1981 SC 479; 1980 UJ 988

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Constitution

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 133(1), 226 and 299 - Madhya Pradesh Minor Mineral Rules, 1961, rr. 24 and 50 - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1940, ss. 2, 32 and 33 - Whether cl. 15 of lease spell out an arbitration agreement? - Held, cl. 15 read as a whole provides for referring future disputes to arbitration of Governor - Arbitration agreement is not required to be in any particular form - What is required to be ascertained is whether parties have agreed that if disputes arise between them in respect of subject-matter of contract such dispute shall be referred to arbitration, then such an arrangement would spell out an arbitration agreement - U/cl. 15, there is a provision for referring disputes to lessor and decision of lessor is made final - On its true construction it spells out an arbitration agreement - Appeal dismissed.
