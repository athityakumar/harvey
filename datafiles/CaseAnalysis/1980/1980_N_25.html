New Satgram Engineering Works and Another v Union of India and Others and Vice Versa

Supreme Court of India

14 August 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

A.P. Sen, V.R. Krishna Iyer, O. Chinnappa Reddy

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 171; (1980) 4 SCC 570; AIR 1981 SC 124; [1981] 1 S.C.R. 406

Case Digest

Subject: Mines & Minerals

Keywords: Colliery

Summary: Mines & Minerals - Coal Mines (Nationalisation) Act, 1973, ss. 2(h), 2(h)(xii), 3(1), 19(3) and 26 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Issues arise - (A) Whether High Court rightly held, there was no special machinery for determining whether a particular asset fell within definition of 'mine' contained in s. 2(h) of Act? - Held, yes - Proper remedy is by way of suit - (B) Whether Central Govt. is empowered u/s. 19(3) of Act to receive up to specified date, any money due to a coal mine even though realization pertains to a period prior to that date and such amounts may not be current assets by way of explanation to s. 2(h)(xii), and apply realisations to discharge liabilities of such coal? - Held, owner of a coal mine is entitled to payment by Commr. of Payments u/s. 26, of balance out of total amount of money credited to account of a coal mine - No question of owner of coal mine, divested of his right, title and interest u/s. 3(1) to realise from Central Govt. any amount due to a coal mine - Petitioners not entitled to receive any payments - Appeal disposed of.
