Pabitra N. Rana v Union Of India And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

30 January 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, P.S. Kailasam, A.D. Koshal

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 113; (1980) 2 SCC 338; (1980) SCC (Cr) 450; AIR 1980 SC 798; [1981] 51 Comp Cas 450; 1980 CRLJ 562; 1980 CRLR 237; [1980] 2 S.C.R. 869

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Conservation of Foreign Exchange and Prevention of Smuggling Activities Act, 1974 s. 3(1) - Constitution of India, 1950 art. 22(5) - Detention - Inordinate delay - Petitioner/detenu was served with detention order u/s. 3(1) of the Act and made representation to Respondent /Govt. on 28-9-1979 and it was rejected on 3-11-1979 - Aggrieved detenue filed writ before SC contending inordinate delay caused by respondent amounts to violation of art.22(5) of Constitution.

Held, relied on case of Sk. Abdul Karim & Ors. v. State of West Bengal, 1969 Indlaw SC 262, that art. 22(5) of Constitution enjoins upon the detaining authority to afford to the detenu the earliest opportunity to make a representation must implicitly mean that such representation must, when made, considered and disposed of as expeditiously as possible, otherwise, it is obvious that the obligation to furnish the earliest opportunity to make a representation loses both its purpose and meaning. Further, obligation of the appropriate detaining authority to take a decision on the representation filed by the detenu is quite apart and distinct from its obligation to constitute a Board and to send the representation to it. The detaining authority is not entitled to wait for the opinion of the Board but has to take its decision without the least possible delay. Thus, satisfied that there has been unreasonable delay in deciding the representation filed by the detenu and that by itself is sufficient to render the detention void. Hence, petition is allowed and order of detention is set aside and direct that the detenu be released forthwith. Petition allowed.

Ratio - Representation made by the detenu must be considered by the Government as expeditiously as possible.
