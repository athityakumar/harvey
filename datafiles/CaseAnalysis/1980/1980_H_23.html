Himalayan Tiles and Marble Private Limited v Francis Victor Coutinho (Dead) By Lrs

Supreme Court of India

28 March 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, A.D. Koshal

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 500; (1980) 3 SCC 223; AIR 1980 SC 1118; [1980] 3 S.C.R. 235; 1980 UJ 457

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 18, 40, 40(1) and 41 - Land Acquisition (Amendment) Act, s. 7 - (A) Whether appellant-company had locus standi to file an appeal before Letters Patent Bench? - Held, definition of 'a person interested' given in s. 18 is an inclusive and must be liberally construed so as to embrace all persons who may be directly or indirectly interested either in title to land or in quantum of compensation - In instant case, it is not disputed that lands were actually acquired for purpose of company and once land vested in Govt., after acquisition, it stood transferred to company under agreement entered into between company and Govt. - Since under agreement company had to pay compensation, it was most certainly interested in seeing that a proper quantum of compensation was fixed so that company may not have to pay a very heavy amount of money - For this purpose, company could undoubtedly appear and adduce evidence on question of quantum of compensation - Further held, appellant was undoubtedly a person interested as contemplated by s. 18(1) of Act - (B) Whether land acquisition proceedings are cured by s. 7 of Amending Act? - Held, even u/s. 7 of Amending Act, and acquisition made for a company prior to 20-7-1962 must fulfill following conditions: (a) that land has been acquired and is vested in Govt; (b) that acquisition has been made u/cl. (a) and (b) of s. 40(1); (c) that acquisition and any proceeding, order etc., shall be deemed always as valid as if provisions of ss. 40 and 41 of Act, as amended by amending Act, were in force at all material times; and (d) that by virtue of s. 7 validity to acquisition is given to all actions taken in connection therewith in spite of any judgment, decree or order of any Court to contrary - Further held, pleas of both parties taken together clearly show that entire possession of property did not pass to Govt. and thus no title vested in Govt. despite notification acquiring land - In these circumstances, therefore, it is unmistakably clear that properties not having vested in Govt. acquisition was not complete and its invalidity could not be cured by s. 7 of Amendment Act - Appeal dismissed.
