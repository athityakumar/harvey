Messrs Sri Raja Lakshmi Dyeing Works and Others v Rangaswamy Chettiar

Supreme Court of India

26 March 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 506; (1980) 4 SCC 259; AIR 1980 SC 1253; 1980 (2) RCJ 165; 1980 UJ 610

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Rent Control - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 115 - Tamil Nadu Buildings (Lease and Rent Control) Act, 1960, ss. 10, 23 and 25 - Respondent filed eviction petition u/s. 10 of Tamil Nadu Buildings (Lease and Rent Control) Act - Rent Controller and appellate authority dismissed - Revision u/s. 25 High Court allowed application for eviction - Whether HC exceeded its jurisdiction in interfering, with concurrent finding of fact arrived at by subordinate Tribunal? - Held, to warrant exercise of revisional power it must, be shown that there was a taint of such unreasonableness resulting in a miscarriage of justice, therefore concurrent finding, based on evidence, that landlord did not bona fide require premises for his own use and occupation is not a finding which can be touched by HC exercising jurisdiction u/s. 25 of Act - Appeal allowed.
