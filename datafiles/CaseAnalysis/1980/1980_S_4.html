S. Gurdip Singh v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

13 November 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, A.D. Koshal

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 37; (1981) SCC (Cr) 168; (1981) 1 SCC 419; AIR 1981 SC 362; 1981 CRLJ 2; 1980 CRLR 678; 1981 UJ 179

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Detention Order, Non supply of relevant documents

Summary: Administrative - Practice & Procedure - Conservation of Foreign Exchange and Prevention of Smuggling Activities Act, 1974, s. 3(1) - Detention order - Non supply of relevant documents - Legality - Petitioner (detenu) filed instant petition against the order of detention passed u/s. 3(1) of the Act against him - Petitioner contended that he was arrested on 5-5-1980 and according to State the date of his arrest was 13-5-1980.

Held, if documents which form the basis of the order of detention are not served on the detenu along with the grounds of detention, in the eye of law there will be no service of the grounds of detention and that circumstance would vitiate his detention and make it void ab initio. In instant case, it is not disputed that when the grounds of detention were served on the petitioner, on 13-5-1980, they were not accompanied by the documents forming the basis thereof and his detention must be held to be without jurisdiction. Petition allowed.
