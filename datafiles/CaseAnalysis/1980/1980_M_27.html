State of Gujarat v Maheshkumar Dhirajlal Thakkar

Supreme Court of India

13 February 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Sarkaria, O. Chinnappa Reddy

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 537; (1980) 2 SCC 322; (1980) SCC (Cr) 442; AIR 1980 SC 1167; 1980 CRLJ 919; 1980 (21) GLR 752; 1980 MLJ 761; 1980 UJ 579

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Training - Stipend - Railways - Accused-respondent was serving as a Tracer in the Office of the Sub-Divisional Soil Conservation Officer - While still in service as a Tracer, he applied to the Railway Authorities, for receiving training as an Apprentice Electrical Signal Maintainer - His application was accepted by the Divisional Assistant Signal and Telecommunication Engineer - Accused obtained earned leave from the Sub-Divisional Soil Conservation Officer for a certain period and joined as apprentice for receiving training at Ajmer - Whether the engagement of the respondent as the Railway Service apprentice, amounted to engaging in 'trade' within the contemplation of s. 168, IPC - Held, the word 'trade' in its narrow popular sense means 'exchange of goods for goods or for money with the object of making profits' - Respondent entered into an agreement with the Railway Administration not for the purpose of engaging in trade, business or profession, but for the sole purpose of receiving training, so that on completion of the training, he could be employed by the Railway Administration - Mere fact that he was paid a stipend during the training period as apprentice did not make him an employee of the Railway Administration did not bind itself to employ him on the completion of the training - Hence, the act of the accused-respondent did not amount to engaging in 'trade' in s. 168, IPC - Engagement of the respondent as an apprentice-trainee would not bring him within the purview of the expression 'trade' - Appeal dismissed.
