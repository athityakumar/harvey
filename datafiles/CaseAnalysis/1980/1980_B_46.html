Raghunandan Singh & Ors. v Brij Mohan Singh & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

15 February 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, A.D. Koshal

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 121; (1980) 3 SCC 107; AIR 1980 SC 869; 1980 ALJ 335; 1980 (6) ALR 318; [1980] 2 S.C.R. 1063; 1980 UJ 389

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Uttar Pradesh Zamindar Abolition and Land reforms Act 1950 (Uttar Pradesh 1 of 1951)

Summary: Land & Property - U.P. Zamindari Abolition and Land Reforms Act, 1950 ss. 12,13 - Lease(Theka) - Status of hereditary tenants - Appellants were landlords(Bhoomidars) of lands in dispute and they granted lease(Theka) to respondents on 10-7-1933 and on 24-5-1943 - Disputes arise between appellants and respondents with regard to interpretation of terms of 'Theka' u/s. 12 of the Act. Respondents claimed that they were in cultivating possession of lands and acquired status of hereditary tenants conferred on them u/s. 12 of the Act - Appellants succeeded before Settlement Officer(Consolidation) - In revision Deputy Director of Consolidation held appellants were Bhoomidars and the respondents could not get any status u/s.12 of the Act. Appellants filed writ before HC which was dismissed in limine - Hence, instant appeal - Whether instant case is governed u/ss. 12 or 13 of the Act.

Held, followed case of Babu Noorul Hasan Khan (Dead) by Lrs. v. Ram Prasad Singh and Others, 1979 Indlaw SC 76, and held that Theka executed on 10-7-1933 and 24-5-1943 was almost in the same terms and in favour of the Thekedars, clearly shows that the lease holders were to remain in possession of the entire agricultural land either through themselves or by arranging with temporary tenants and by recovering government revenue. Thus, the terms of the Theka, do not spell out the fact that the respondents had taken the lease for the purpose of personal cultivation only because other purposes also are indicated as part of the Theka i.e., to sublet the land or to appoint temporary tenants and the like. Therefore, condition required u/s. 12 of the Act are clearly not fulfilled in the case of the respondents and the view taken by HC is in direct conflict with the decision of this Court and held to be incorrect. Hence, order of HC and Deputy Director of Consolidation is set aside and restore the order of the Settlement Officer (Consolidation). Appeal allowed.

Ratio - The dominant intention of the statute, as of other land reforms legislation, is to secure land for the tiller of the soil who alone would be clothed with the special rights of a hereditary tenant.
