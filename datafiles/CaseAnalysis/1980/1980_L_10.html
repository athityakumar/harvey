Ram Swaroop Rai v Lilavathi

Supreme Court of India

07 May 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

V.R. Krishna Iyer, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 157; (1980) 3 SCC 452; AIR 1982 SC 945; 1980 ALJ 651; 1980 (6) ALR 359; [1980] 3 S.C.R. 1034

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Keywords: Uttar Pradesh Urban Building (Regulation of Letting, Rent and Eviction) Act, 1972

Summary: Rent Control - Uttar Pradesh Urban Building (Regulation of Letting, Rent and Eviction) Act, 1972 - Eviction - Sustainability - Respondent purchased shop no. 66 in 1969 from one 'B'/DW2, occupied first floor and allowed appellant, as tenant, to occupy ground floor in 1970 on a lease deed which recited that building was erected in 1965 - In 1975 eviction action was instituted on basis that building was new, that the Act did not debar eviction of new constructions put up within 10 years of suit and so a decree was inevitable - Trial Court decreed eviction - On appeal, HC upheld decreed of Trial Court - Hence instant appeal - Whether respondent had made out her case for exemption from the operation of the Act based on vital fact that building was completed only within 10 years of the suit - Held, it was common case that Shop Nos. 65 and 66 were owned by common owner, DW2 - DW 2 sold only Shop No. 66 to respondent - There was no doubt, that there was an existing building, Shop No. 66, long prior to 10 year period mentioned in statute - According to testimony of DW2, old construction continued, but certain additions and remodelling were done - It shown existence of a prior building, proposal being for addition or partial reconstruction and not for total demolition - It was not possible to conclude automatically that there was a new construction - If SC go by DW2's evidence, owner of building at relevant time, SC could not necessarily hold that existing building was substantially demolished and reconstructed - Indeed, his evidence was to the effect that construction such as was made was beyond 10 year period - Best testimony was municipal records about completion of building and verification by municipal authorities as to whether a new construction had come into being or an old construction was remodeled and, if so, when exactly completion took effect - Municipal assessment record produced in the court merely state 'increased assessment' - Failure of Trial Court specifically to record when building was completed and what was extent of re-building, whether it was a case of total demolition and reconstruction or such extensive additions as to push existing building into a minor part, becomes fatal - These basic issues had failed to receive any attention from courts below - A finding recorded on speculative basis was no finding and that was the fate of holding in instant case - Statute made it clear that reliance upon municipal records, rather than on the lips of witnesses, was indicated to determine date of completion and nature of construction - This statutory guideline was wholly overlooked and burden lying on landlord was not appreciated - Result was that eviction order had to be demolished - It might still be open to landlady-respondent to make out his case by producing better municipal evidence - SC did not wish to deny landlady this opportunity because Trial Court had not approached problem from correct legal angle - Order of Courts below were set side and matter was remitted back to Trial Court - Appeal allowed.
