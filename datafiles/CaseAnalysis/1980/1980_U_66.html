Sabir Ahmed v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

18 April 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Sarkaria, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 313; (1980) 3 SCC 295; (1980) SCC (Cr) 675; [1982] 52 Comp Cas 55; 1982 CRLJ 146; 1980 CRLR 735; 1980 CrLR(SC) 735; [1980] 3 S.C.R. 738

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Conservation Of Foriegn Exchange And Prevention Of Smuggling Activities Act, 1974, ss. 3(1) and 11 - Whether power conferred by s. 11 on Central Govt, carries with it a duty to consider any representation made by detenu, expeditiously? - Held, once a representation is made to Central Govt, it is duty bound to consider same in order to exercise its discretion either in rejecting or accepting it - If there is inordinate delay in considering representation that would clearly amount to violation of art. 22(5) - Although, four months have gone by since despatch of representation by detenu, yet Central Govt. has not applied its mind to it - It has just ignored it - Appeal allowed.
