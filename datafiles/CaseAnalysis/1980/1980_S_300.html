Bar Council of Delhi and Another v Surjeet Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 May 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

N.L. Untwalia, V.D. Tulzapurkar, A.P. Sen

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 308; (1980) 4 SCC 211; AIR 1980 SC 1612; 1980 (18) DLT 208; [1980] 3 S.C.R. 946

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Election - Advocates Act, 1961 - Bar Council of Delhi Election Rules, 1968 - Bar Council of India Rules, 1975 - Delhi Bar Council Election Rules, 1968, r. 3 - High Court allowed petition for setting aside election of Bar Council of Delhi - (A) Whether HC right is setting aside election of Bar Council of Delhi? - (B) Whether proviso to r. 3(j) is ultra vires and invalid? - Held, no provision in Advocates Act or any rule enabling Delhi Bar Council to remove name of a person from state roll - Electoral roll prepared by Delhi Bar council on basis of proviso to r. 3(j), resulting in exclusion of names of advocates from state roll of advocates was not valid - Rules are itself invalid so preparation of electoral roll was completely vitiated matter which cannot be put within narrow limits of such rules - Appeals dismissed.
