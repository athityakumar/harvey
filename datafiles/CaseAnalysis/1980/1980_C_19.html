Consolidated Coffee Limited and Another v Coffee Board, Bangalore

Supreme Court of India

15 April 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

V.D. Tulzapurkar, D.A. Desai

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 336; (1980) SCC (Tax) 279; (1980) 3 SCC 358; AIR 1980 SC 1468; [1980] 3 S.C.R. 625; 1980 (46) STC 164; 1980 TAXLR 1723; 1980 TLR 1723

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Keywords: Sale Price, Sales Tax Authorities, Statement Of Objects And Reasons, Coffee Act, 1942, Sale In The Course Of Export, Penultimate Sale, Transfer Of Documents Of Title

Summary: Sales Tax - Coffee Act, 1942, ss. 4, 5, 10, 20, 21, 25, 26 and 34 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 19, 31 and 286(2) - Constitution (Sixth Amendment) Act, 1956 - Indian Sale of Goods Act, 1930, s. 64(2) - Central Sales Tax Act 1956, ss. 5 and 6 - (A) Whether amendment introduced by insertion of s. 5(3) of the Central Act is ultra vires art. 286(2) of Constitution - S. 5(3) formulates a principle of general applicability in regard to all penultimate sales provided they satisfy specified conditions mentioned therein and there is no question of the said provision creating a legal fiction - Held, s. 5(3) is intra vires s. 286(2) of the Constitution - (B) Whether agreement referred to in s. 5(3) of Central Act means only the agreement with a foreign buyer or would include any binding or enforceable agreement to export even with a local party to implement which penultimate sale should have taken place? - Where two or more words, which are susceptible of analogous meaning, are coupled together they are understood to be used in their cognate sense - Parliament has not said 'an agreement' or 'any agreement' for or in relation to such export and in the context the expression 'the agreement' would refer to that agreement which is implicit in the sale occasioning the export - Held, the expression 'the agreement' occurring in the relevant phrase means or refers to the agreement with a foreign buyer and not an agreement or any agreement with a local party containing the covenant to export - (C) At what point of time the property in the coffee sold at export auctions conducted by the Coffee Board passes to the Registered Exporters? - It is by virtue of goods being specific and in a deliverable state that u/s. 20 the property in such goods passes to the buyer at the completion of the contract at the fall of hammer at such sale - Question at what point of time the property in the coffee sold there at passes to the auction purchaser (Registered Exporter) must depend upon the intention of the parties - Held, cl. 31 properly read amounts to a defiance clause and nothing more, especially when it is clear that property in the coffee sold at auction passes to the buyer u/cls. 19 and 20 immediately upon payment of price, vehement and setting apart of the coffee for delivery to buyer - Appeal partly allowed.
