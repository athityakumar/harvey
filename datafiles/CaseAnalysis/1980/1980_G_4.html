Gapadibai v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

08 February 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, P.S. Kailasam, A.D. Koshal

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 542; (1980) 2 SCC 327; AIR 1980 SC 1040; 1980 UJ 498

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Attachment - Legality - Recovery proceedings were initiated against defendant No. 5, husband of the plaintiff, for recovery of sums due to the govt. and in consequence the house in suit was attached - Plaintiff claimed release of property on the ground that the house was her personal property and could not be attached for sums owing by her husband - Defence of the State was that the property belonged to the husband and that, therefore, attachment was valid - Trial Court accepted the plaintiff's case and decreed the suit - HC disagreed with the Trial Court and dismissed the suit - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, in the instant case as the plaintiff relied on a registered document which was proved to have been duly executed, a finding which has not been reversed by the HC, the onus lay on the State to prove that what was apparent was not the real. No evidence on behalf of the State was adduced to displace the presumption arising from the registered sale deed in favour of the plaintiff. Trial Court, after a careful consideration of the evidence of the plaintiff and the documents produced by her found that she had established that the property in question belonged to her and not to defendant No.5. The judgment and decree of the HC are set aside and the plaintiff's suit stands decreed. Appeal allowed.
