Jolly George Verghese and Another v Bank of Cochin

Supreme Court of India

04 February 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

V.R. Krishna Iyer, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 140; (1980) 2 SCC 360; AIR 1980 SC 470; [1982] 52 Comp Cas 70; 1983 (11) E.L.T. 1619; 1980 KLT 375; [1980] 2 S.C.R. 913; 1980 UJ 379

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Human Rights

Keywords: Universal Declaration of Human Rights

Summary: Human Rights - Arrest - Civil Procedure Code, 1908 ,s. 51, O. 21, r.37 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 19 and 21 - Appeal against court warrant for arrest and detention in civil prison for non-payment of an amount due to a respondent bank - Court appointed a receiver and attached property of appellant and also issued a warrant for arrest - Revision dismissed by High Court - Whether order of court was justified? - Held, no - Element of bad faith not present - International Covenant on Civil and Political Rights prohibits imprisonment for a mere non-performance of contract - State is obligated not to violate rights of human dignity and person enshrined in art. 21, r/w arts. 14 and 19 except under law which is fair, just and reasonable in its procedural essence - Matter remitted for reconsideration - Court will take note of other honest and urgent pressures on assets of petitioner - Executing court to decide means of judgment-debtors to discharge decree in light of interpretation given - Appeal disposed of.
