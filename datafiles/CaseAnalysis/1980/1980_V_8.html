Ved Prakash Wadhwa v Vishwa Mohan

Supreme Court of India

26 March 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

V.R. Krishna Iyer

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 504; (1981) 3 SCC 667; AIR 1982 SC 816; 1981 (2) RCJ 665; 1980 UJ 725

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Framing Of Issues

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, O. 10, r. 1, O. 14, r. 1(5), O. 15, r. 1 - Uttar Pradesh Rent Act, 1972, s. 20(4) - Arrears of rent - (A) When first hearing of suit arrives in course of a case? - Held, first hearing of suit is when, after framing of issues, suit is posted for trial, that is, production of evidence - First hearing of suit can never be earlier than date fixed for preliminary examination of parties and settlement of issues - (B) Whether date of deposit is date on which challan was passed by Court or date on which money was put into treasury? - Further held, expressions but must be guided by practice prevailing in Courts in States - Relied on Daya Ram v. Virendra Kumar Goel, (1977 (UP) R C C 227) - When money is tendered before Court, challan is passed by Ministerial Officer then money deposited in treasury with challan, such deposit relates back to date on challan presented - Appeal disposed of.
