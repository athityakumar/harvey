Deputy Commissioner, Sales Tax (Law) Board Of Revenue(Taxes) v Pio Food Packers

Supreme Court of India

09 May 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Pathak, P.N. Bhagwati, V.D. Tulzapurkar

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 189; (1980) Supp SCC 174; (1980) SCC (Tax) 319; AIR 1980 SC 1227; 1981 ECR 47; 1980 (6) E.L.T. 343; [1980] 3 S.C.R. 1271; 1980 (46) STC 63; 1980 TAXLR 1706

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Keywords: Other Goods, Processing

Summary: Sales Tax - Kerala General Sales Tax Act, 1963, s. 5A(1)(a) - Purchase tax - Respondent engaged in business of manufacturing and selling canned fruit - Conversion of pineapple to pineapple slices - Whether it can be said that the pineapple fruit is consumed in the process and thus whether the transaction fall within s. 5-A(1)(a) attracting purchase tax? - S. 5-A(1)(a) envisages consumption of a commodity in manufacture of another commodity - The clause, truly read, speaks of goods consumed in the manufacture of other goods for sale or goods consumed in the manufacture of other goods for purposes other than sale - Held, pineapple slices must be held to possess the same identity as the original pineapple fruit - When pineapple fruit is processed into pineapple slices for the purpose of being sold in sealed cans there is no consumption of the original pineapple fruit for the purpose of manufacture - Case does not fall within s. 5-A(1)(a) - Appeal dismissed.
