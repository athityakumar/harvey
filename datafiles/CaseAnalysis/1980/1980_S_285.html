Sunil Kumar Banerjee v State of West Bengal and Others

Supreme Court of India

26 March 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy, V.R. Krishna Iyer, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 132; (1980) 3 SCC 304; (1980) SCC (L&S) 369; AIR 1980 SC 1170; 1980 (40) FLR 434; 1980 LabIC 654; [1980] 3 S.C.R. 179; 1980 (2) SLR 147; 1980 UJ 479

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Service - All India Civil Services s 1969, r. 8(19) - All India Services (Discipline and Appeal) Rules, 1955 - (A) Whether enquiry under All India Services (Discipline and Appeal) Rules, 1955, which had been repealed is valid? - Held, in charges framed against appellant and in first show cause notice reference was clearly to 1969 rules - Appellant himself mentioned in one of his letters that charges have been framed under 1969 rules - Enquiry report mentions that Shri Mukherji was appointed as an Enquiry Officer under 1969 rules - Failure to comply with requirements of r. 8 (19) of 1969 rules does not vitiate enquiry unless delinquent officer is able to establish prejudice - Held, there is no substance in contention of appellant that 1955 rules and not 1969 rules were followed - (B) Whether Vigilance Commr. had statutory status and he should have been consulted by Govt.? - Held, conclusion of disciplinary authority was not based on advice tendered by Vigilance Commr. but was arrived at independently, on basis of charges, relevant material placed before Enquiry Officer in support of charges, and defence of delinquent officer - In fact final conclusion of disciplinary authority on several charges are so much at variance with opinion of Vigilance Commr. that it is impossible to say that disciplinary authority's mind was in any manner influenced by advice tendered by Vigilance Commr. - Further held, disciplinary authority committed any serious or material irregularity in consulting Vigilance Commr., even assuming that it was so done - Other circumstances were that the Vigilance Commr. did not permit appellant to engage a lawyer and that he allowed Presenting Officer to introduce extraneous matters - Rules give a discretion to Enquiry Officer to permit or not to permit a delinquent officer to be represented by a lawyer - In present case appellant cross examined prosecution witnesses and also examined defence witnesses - Thereafter when matter was posted for arguments and was adjourned atleast once at instance of appellant, appellant came forward with an application seeking permission to engage a lawyer - Enquiry Officer rejected application noticing that it was made at a very belated stage -(C) Whether he was denied a reasonable opportunity of defending himself as important witnesses were not called so as to enable him to cross examine them though notings made by them in files were relied upon against him ? - Held, when matter was posted for arguments and was adjourned atleast once at instance of appellant, appellant came forward with an application seeking permission to engage a lawyer - Enquiry Officer rejected application noticing that it was made at a very belated stage - Enquiry Officer was right in doing so - Appeal dismissed.
