Gurnam Kaur v Bakshish Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

03 October 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy, R.S. Sarkaria

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 8; (1980) Supp SCC 567; (1981) SCC (Cr) 496; AIR 1981 SC 631; 1981 CRLJ 34; 1980 CRLR 686

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Keywords: Ballistics

Summary: CrPC, 1973, s. 154 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 3 - Appreciation of evidence - (A) Effect of ommission to mention incidental facts in FIR filed by a rustic lay woman - (B) Reliability evidence of eye-witnesses - In the light of the fact that police had asked them to affix their finger impressions on statement - (C) Credibility of evidence of eye-witness who had lodged FIR when corroborated by medical and expert evidence - Held, cannot be attached any importance - Cannot cast any doubt on their reliability - Held implicit reliance could have been placed on the evidence - Appeal Allowed.
