S S. Sharma and Others v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 November 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Pathak, V.R. Krishna Iyer, O. Chinnappa Reddy

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 279; (1981) SCC (L&S) 184; (1981) 1 SCC 397; AIR 1981 SC 588; 1981 LabIC 131; 1981 (1) LLJ 381; 1981 (1) LLN 597; [1981] 1 S.C.R. 1184; 1981 (1) SLJ 443; 1980 (3) SLR 511

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Service - Central Secretariat Service Rules, r. 12(2a) - Dreservation of vaccancies - The newly enacted r. 12 (2) in Central Secretariat Service Rules, and related Regulations, providing for a limited departmental competitive examination for members of Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes - Because the Central Govt. should have dereserved twenty-seven vacancies when it was found that suitable Scheduled Caste and Scheduled Tribe candidates were not available for inclusion within field of selection - Whether it is valid? - Held, there is no right in candidates seeking to fill vacancies belonging to general category to insist on de reservation of reserved vacancies so long as it is possible in law to fill reserved vacancies - If at all, a claim in that behalf can arise only if no valid arrangement can be made for filling reserved vacancies, and de reservation is called for by reason of prohibition, in cl. (v) of para 2 of Office Memorandum D/- 20-07-1974, against carry forward of reservations from year to year in event of an adequate number of Scheduled Caste and Scheduled Tribe candidates not being available in any particular year - Two avenues of promotion pointed out by learned counsel for petitioners consist in, one, preparation of a list of officers falling within field of selection, both of general category as well as members of Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes and their selection on basis of principles laid down and, two, selection of candidates of Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes consequent upon limited departmental competitive examination - Sub-rule (2a) of r. 12, newly enacted in Central Secretariat Service Rules is constitutionally valid - Writ petitions dismissed.
