Katyani Dayal and Others v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

26 March 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy, R.S. Sarkaria, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 319; (1980) 3 SCC 245; (1980) SCC (L&S) 380; [1980] 3 S.C.R. 139; 1980 (2) SLR 79

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Keywords: Eligibility - Age Limit

Summary: Service - Indian Railway Establishment Code, 1951, r 106 - Constitution, 1950, art.14, 16 - R 106 - Validity of - Which provided that temporary Assistant Officers would be neither Class I nor Class II - Alleged discrimination between Assistant Officers recruited through competitive exam and those recruited on recommendation of UPSC - (A) Whether all Assistant Officers (temporary and otherwise) comprise a single class.- Held, it was distinctly understood by the appointing authority as well as the persons appointed that those who were appointed as Temporary Assistant Engineers on the basis of the selection made by the Union Public Service Commission did not belong either to Class I or to Class II - (B) Whether a service not contemplated by the Indian Railway Establishment Code could be created, and whether appointments of Gazetted Railway servants not falling in Class I or Class II and therefore falling outside the provisions of the Code could be made? - Further Held, the previous existence of the Indian Railway Service of Engineers and the rules made for recruitment to that service do not bar the constitution of another service or the creation of posts outside the cadres of the Indian Railway Service of Engineers - Temporary Assistant Officers were to be a source of recruitment to the Indian Railway Service of Engineers Class I, and were not directly appointed to Class I - Subsequent continuance of the posts indefinitely would not make persons appointed to the posts members of the regular service - (C) Whether 'note' to r 106 could amend the Rules? - Further Held, it merely states an existing fact known to all concerned, and classification outside Classes I and II not dependant on the note, but on the Presidential action in regard to creation of posts - Amendments made in 1975 redefined 'Assisstant Officer' so as not to include Temporary Assisstant Officers who were not classified either as Class I or II - Further Held, the word 'Temporary' in the expression 'Temporary Assistant Officer' was not used to qualify the words Assistant Officer - Whole of the expression was intended to describe the particular post, which was neither in Class I nor Class II - (D) Whether classification of Temporary Assistant Officers separately from the Indian Railway Service of Engineers Class I was discriminatory and violative of art.14 and 16? - Petitioners argued that minimum academic qualifications, scale of pay, functions and duties were all similar to Class I - Class I officers recruited much later were placed much higher than petitioners in order of seniority - Further Held, there are certain fundamental differences between two classes which cannot be ignored - Object of recruitment to the Indian Railway Service of Engineers is to provide Officers of highest quality to meet requirements of all posts in the service, while object of recruiting Temporary Assistant Officers was to meet specific requirements of various projects with a prospect of promotion in a temporary capacity to a senior scale post and absorption into the Indian Railway Service - They were not to be members of the Indian Railway Service of Engineers but were to be a source of recruitment to the Indian Railway Service of Engineers - While brilliance was the beacon light which beckoned those aspiring to become members of the Indian Railway Service of Engineers Class I, it was replaced by experience in the case of those wanting to be Temporary Assistant Officers - For the Indian Railway Service of Engineers the training is an intensive and comprehensive one designed to equip them for higher posts in the Department too, while the training for Temporary Assistant Engineers was a brief six months' training intended merely to equip them for carrying out the specific jobs - Doctrine of equal pay for equal work does not apply here, although it is a goal worthy of attainment - Petition dismissed.
