Ganga Ramchand Bharvani v Under Secretary To The Government of Maharashtra and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 August 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Sarkaria, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 104; (1980) 4 SCC 624; (1981) SCC (Cr) 86; AIR 1980 SC 1744; 1980 CRLJ 1263; 1980 CRLR 610; 1980 CrLR(SC) 610; [1981] 1 S.C.R. 343

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Detention - Conservation of Foreign Exchange and Prevention of Smuggling Activities Act, 1974,ss.3, 11 - Constitution of India, 1950, art.22(5) - Gems, articles and other valuables recovered from business and residential premises of detenu - Detenu was detained u/s.3 of COFEPOSA - Wife of detenue applied for materials on which order of detention was based in order to make an effective representation - Detaining Authority refused to supply copies - Whether order of detention was justified - Held, No - Refusal and belated supply of these copies had violated that right of detenu - Detention is vitiated on account of delay - Constitutional imperatives in art.22(5) of Constitution provide (i) detaining authority must, as soon as may be, after detention, communicate to detenu grounds on which order has been made; and (ii) detaining authority must afford detenu earliest opportunity of making a representation against detention order - Above mandate not followed - Fundamental right of detenu violated - Petition allowed.
