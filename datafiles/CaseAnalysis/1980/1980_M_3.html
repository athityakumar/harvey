N. K. V. Bros (P) Ltd. v M. Karumai Ammal And Ors. Etc.

Supreme Court of India

21 March 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

V.R. Krishna Iyer, D.A. Desai

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 129; (1980) 3 SCC 457; (1980) SCC (Cr) 774; 1980 ACJ 435; AIR 1980 SC 1354; 1980 CRLR 711; 1980 (41) FLR 152; 1980 (2) MLJ(SC) 33; [1980] 3 S.C.R. 101; 1980 UJ 414

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Labour & Industrial Law; Tort

Keywords: Act Of God

Summary: Motor Vehicles Act, 1939, s. 110B - Duty of Accidents Tribunals as to when inference as to culpability be drawn - Held, save in plain cases, culpability must be inferred from the circumstances where it is fairly reasonable - Court should not succumb to niceties, technicalities and mystic maybes - Petition Dismissed.
