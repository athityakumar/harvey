Kewal Krishan S/O Lachman Das v Suraj Bhan and Another

Supreme Court of India

01 August 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Sarkaria, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 440; (1980) Supp SCC 499; (1981) SCC (Cr) 438; AIR 1980 SC 1780; 1980 CRLJ 1271; 1980 CRLR 534

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: CrPC, 1973, ss. 200, 202, 203, 204, 209, 218 and 227 - Constitution, art. 136 - (A) Counter cases exclusively triable by Sessions Court - How to be tries - (B) Preliminary inquiry u/ss. 203/204 - Magistrate has to see whether there is prima facie evidence in support of charges - He meticulously appreciating evidence - Jurisdiction and Legality / Constitutionality - (A) Held, they should be tried separately but by same Court - Magistrate in weighing evidence meticulously exceeds his jurisdiction u/s.203 - (B) It is only a case of irregularity and not illegality - No gross miscarriage of justice - Supreme Court will not interfere - Petition Dismissed
