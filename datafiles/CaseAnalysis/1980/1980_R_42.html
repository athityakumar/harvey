 State of Gujarat and Others v Raman Lal Keshav Lal and Others

Supreme Court of India

30 July 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

E.S. Venkataramiah, Y.V. Chandrachud, R.S. Sarkaria, N.L. Untwalia, P.S. Kailasam

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 447; (1980) 4 SCC 653; (1981) SCC (L&S) 34; AIR 1981 SC 53; 1980 LabIC 1167; [1981] 1 S.C.R. 144; 1980 (3) SLR 407; 1980 UJ 817

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Gujarat Panchayats Act, 1962, ss. 203 (1) and 206 - Whether Panchayat service constituted under Panchayats Act is civil service of State of Gujarat? - Held, true test for determination of question whether a person is holding a civil post or is a member of civil service is existence of a relationship of master and servant between State and person holding a post under it and that existence of such relationship was dependent upon right of State to select and appoint holder of post - Its right to suspend and dismiss him, its right to control manner and method of his doing work and payment by it of his wages and remuneration - Relationship of master and servant may be established by presence of all or some of factors referred to above in conjunction with other circumstances - S. 203 (1) of Panchayats Act provides that there shall be constituted a Panchayat Service in connection with affairs of Panchayats i.e. gram and nagar panchayat, taluka panchayat and district panchayat for purpose of bringing about uniform scales of pay and uniform conditions of service of persons employed in discharge of functions and duties of Panchayats - Persons who were working as extra departmental agents of posts and telegraphs Department were persons holding civil post - Panchayat Service contemplated u/s. 203 is a single service for whole State and it is not a collection of distinct and separate services of each individual Panchayat - S. 206 authorises State Govt. to pool together four classes of persons mentioned therein who originally belonged to four different sources and to allocate them to Panchayat Service and one class of such persons are those who belong to State Service - Panchayat Service is constituted by Panchayats Act and State Govt. is empowered to make orders and rules regarding its organisation and management - Panchayats constituted under Panchayats Act derive their authority from statute and are under control of State Govt - Panchayat Service constituted u/s. 203 of Panchayats Act has all characteristics of a Civil Service of State - Panchayat Service constituted under Panchayats Act is a civil service of State of Gujarat - Appeal allowed.
