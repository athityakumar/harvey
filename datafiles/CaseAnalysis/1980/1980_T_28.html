C. C. Padmanabhan & Others v The Director Of Public Instructions And Others

Supreme Court of India

30 July 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

A.D. Koshal, V.R. Krishna Iyer, O. Chinnappa Reddy

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 166; (1980) Supp SCC 668; (1981) SCC (L&S) 439; AIR 1981 SC 64; 1980 LabIC 1178; [1981] 1 S.C.R. 128; 1981 (1) SLJ 165; 1980 (2) SLR 599; 1980 UJ 833

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Education

Keywords: Kerala Education Subordinate Service (Special) Rules 1972

Summary: Constitution- Constitution,arts.14 and 16 - Reversion of post - Appellants degraded from posts of Assistant Education Officers to that of High School Assistants - Whether the impugned reversions were constitutionally valid? Held, that the direction was wholly arbitrary and not based on any principle? -Held, the post of A.E.O. lies in a category and carries a grade higher than those of the post of H. S. A. and is, therefore, a post of promotion vis-a-vis the other so that the two cannot be considered as interchangeable especially because there is no rule, direction or instruction laying down expressly or by necessary implication that they are equivalent to each other. Further even if the post of an A. E. O. is not regarded as higher to that of an H. S. A. either category-wise or grade-wise, the impugned reversions were still hit by Article 14 of the Constitution of India - It was, therefore, violative of art. of the constitution - Appeal Allowed.
