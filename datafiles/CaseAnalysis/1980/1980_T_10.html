T. V. Mahalinga Iyer v State of Madras and Others

Supreme Court of India

06 August 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

V.R. Krishna Iyer, R.S. Pathak, O. Chinnappa Reddy

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 430; (1981) 1 SCC 445; AIR 1980 SC 2036; 1981 (1) MLJ(SC) 1; 1980 UJ 865

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Trusts & Associations

Keywords: Determination, Private or public Temple

Summary: Trusts & Associations - Land & Property - Madras Hindu Religious & Charitable Endowments Act, 1959, 6(20) - Private or public Temple - Determination - Legality - Appellant (plaintiff) filed a suit claiming that temple in question is a private temple and respondent had no jurisdiction to manage the temple - Trial Court decreed the suit, which was reversed in appeal by HC - Hence, instant appeal - Whether temple in question is a private temple or a public temple, that is, a temple within the meaning of s. 6(20) of the Act.

Held, founder of temple is no more and he died issueless with the result that his family is extinct. A will had been executed by him and the trustees under the will are now claiming the institution as a private temple. Temple itself is situate on Govt. property, that procession with the deity are taken out and that offerings are made, that the structure especially of Gopuram and Mandapam also indicates the public nature of the temple. Many other facts have been accumulated by the HC and the evidence (Ex.B1) in the case also supports the conclusion that there had been contributions made by the public and the temple was not a private one. Court thoroughly satisfied that no serious error of law or perspective or misappreciation of evidence has been pointed out in impugned judgment of HC. The case of appellant that institution is private or that the deity is a family idol is rejected. Appeal dismissed.
