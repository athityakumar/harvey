Census Commissioner and others v R. Krishnamurthy

Supreme Court of India

07 November 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, R.F. Nariman, Uday Umesh Lalit

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 763; (2015) 2 SCC 796; 2015 (1) AWC 757; 2015 (1) CLT(SC) 30; JT 2014 (12) SC 563; 2014 (8) MLJ 241; 2014 (4) RCR(Civil) 991; 2014(12) SCALE 549; [2014] 11 S.C.R. 463

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Madhya Pradesh Municipal Corporation Act, 1956, Census Act,1948

Summary: Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Powers of judicial review - Interference with policy decision - Writ petition was filed before HC for quashment of the order passed by the Central Administrative Tribunal on the foundation that when there was no Scheduled Tribe population in the Union Territory of Pondicherry there cannot be any reservation for Scheduled Tribe in the said Union Territory - HC directed the Census Department of the Govt. of India to take measures towards conducting the caste-wise census in the country in a time bound manner - Hence, instant appeal - Whether the HC could have issued such a mandamus commanding the appellant to carry out a census in a particular manner.

Held, it is not within the domain of the courts to embark upon an enquiry as to whether a particular public policy is wise and acceptable or whether a better policy could be evolved. The court can only interfere if the policy framed is absolutely capricious or not informed by reasons or totally arbitrary and founded ipse dixit offending the basic requirement of art. 14 of the Constitution. The HC, to say the least, had no justification to pave a path and said path is a colossal transgression of power of judicial review, and that makes the order sensitively susceptible. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Courts are not to plunge into policy making by adding something to the policy by way of issuing a writ of mandamus.
