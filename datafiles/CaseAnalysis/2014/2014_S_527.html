Sudhir Vasudeva, Chairman and MD. ONGC and others v M. George Ravishekaran and others

Supreme Court of India

04 February 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjan Gogoi, P. Sathasivam, Shiva Kirti Singh

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 66; (2014) 3 SCC 373; AIR 2014 SC 950; 2014 (2) AWC 1991; 2014 (1) CLT(SC) 320; 2014 (141) FLR 7; JT 2014 (2) SC 481; 2014 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1253; 2014 (2) MLJ 756; 2014(2) SCALE 50; [2014] 4 S.C.R. 27; 2014 (2) UPLBEC 935

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Contempt, Modified Recruitment and Promotion Regulations, 1980, Absorption as Marine Asst. Radio Operators, Non-compliance of directions

Summary: (1) Contempt-Civil contempt-Wilful disobedience of order-Appellants complied with alternative direction passed in writ petition by High Court-Contention that appellants in purported compliance of Court's order would still make them liable for contempt which can be purged only by creation of supernumerary posts as directed by High Court-Not tenable-No order or direction supplemental to what has been already expressed should be issued by Court while exercising jurisdiction in domain of contempt law-Courts must act with utmost restraint before compelling executive to create additional posts-Impugned direction of High Court for creation of supernumerary post virtually amounts to supplementing directions contained in order of High Court-Direction to create supernumerary posts at stage of exercise of contempt jurisdiction has to be understood to be addition to initial order passed in writ petition-Appellants as complied with alternative direction issued by High Court, they cannot be understood to have acted in wilful disobedience of court's order.(2) Contempt-Contempt plea-Duty of court while considering contempt plea-Adjudication of contempt plea involves process of self determination of sweep, meaning and effect of order in respect of which disobedience is alleged-Courts must not, therefore, travel beyond four corners of order which is alleged to have been flouted or enter into questions that have not been dealt with or decided in judgment or order violation of which is alleged-Only such directions which are explicit in judgment or order or are plainly self evident ought to be taken into account for purpose of consideration as to whether there has been any disobedience or wilful violation of same-Courts must ensure that while considering contempt plea power available to court in other corrective jurisdictions like review or appeal is not trenched upon.
