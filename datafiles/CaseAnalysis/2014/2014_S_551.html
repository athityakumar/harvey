State of Rajasthan and another v Surendra Mohnot and others

Supreme Court of India

30 June 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 531; (2014) 14 SCC 77; AIR 2014 SC 2925; 2014 (6) ALD(SC) 17; JT 2014 (9) SC 28; 2014(8) SCALE 41; [2014] 6 S.C.R. 191; 2014 (5) SLR 257

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Rajasthan Civil Services (Absorption of Surplus Personnel) Rules, 1969

Summary: Service - Increment - Denied - Sustainability - Respondent Nos. 1 to 6 were appointed on ad hoc basis as Lower Division Clerks either directly or from amongst the class IV employees for a fixed tenure for smooth functioning of administrative work - The nature of appointments are clear from the appointment orders dt. 26-6-1986, 5-7-1986 and 25-10-1986 - Respondent No. 7 was appointed on similar conditions in January, 1998 - On 28-4-1993, the respondents appeared in the requisite test and, accordingly, were regularized on the posts of Lower Division Clerk by order dt. 28-4-1993 - However, appellant/state decided not to include respondent Nos.1 to 6 in the circular which pertained to prescription of Selection Grades for employees in Class IV service - Whether non inclusion of respondents in the circular pertaining to increment was sustainable -

Held, analysis would clearly show that the dictum in one case of SC covered the controversy - State of Rajasthan and Others v Jagdish Narain Chaturvedi 2009 Indlaw SC 2091, relied on - Respondents prior to regularization were not members of service or a part of the cadre and hence, the benefit of the circular pertaining to selection grade was not applicable to them - Therefore, the irresistible conclusion was that respondents were only entitled to the benefit of selection grade from the date of regularization - Period of nine years, eighteen years and twenty seven years had to be computed from that date - True it was, respondents might have been given the first benefit on an erroneous understanding of the circular and also prior to the decision in one case - State of Rajasthan and Others v Jagdish Narain Chaturvedi 2009 Indlaw SC 2091, relied on - But that would not entitle them to assert their claim on that basis, for that would be contrary to the law of the land as stated in one case of SC - State of Rajasthan and Others v Jagdish Narain Chaturvedi 2009 Indlaw SC 2091, relied on - Be it noted, the State, as the latter circular would indicate, had decided not to take any steps for recovery of the benefit - Therefore, SC concluded and held that the writ petition preferred by the respondents before the HC deserved dismissal and, accordingly, the order passed by the writ court and the decision in intra-court appeal are set aside and the writ petition stood dismissed - Petitions dismissed.
