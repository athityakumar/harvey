S. P. Nayyar v Union of India and others

Supreme Court of India

30 June 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya, Kurian Joseph

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 420; (2014) 14 SCC 370; 2014 (4) AWC 4018; 2014 (142) FLR 634; 2014(8) SCALE 174; [2014] 6 S.C.R. 116; 2015 (3) SLR 1

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces; Service

Keywords: Character Roll

Summary: Service - Defence & Security Forces - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Promotion - Instant appeal filed by appellants against the order passed by the DB of HC - By the impugned judgment, the DB of the HC after going through the relevant record including ACRs of respondent, opined that due to personal bias of his superior officer, the respondent was targeted and was wrongly superseded in the matter of departmental promotion and hence allowed the writ petition filed by the respondent directing the appellants to promote the respondent as Additional DIG with back wages - Whether HC was not right in directing the appellants to grant promotion to the respondent rather than directing the appellants to reconsider the case under DPC -

Held, selection to the post of Additional DIG was based on merit-cum-suitability which was to be adjudged on the basis of ACRs of different candidates - Merit position could be adjudged by the Selection Committee on appreciation of their Character Roll - In absence of the Character roll of other candidates, who were also in the zone of promotion, it was not open to the HC to assess the merit of one individual who moves before the HC, to give a finding whether he comes within the zone of promotion or fit for promotion - Further, bias and malafide acts could be adjudged only on the basis of evidence - Assessment of Character Roll by one or the other officer, giving a general grade such as 'Good' could not be the sole ground to hold that the officer was biased against the person whose Character Roll was assessed - In the instant case, there was nothing on record to suggest that the superior was biased against the respondent - Merely because he assessed the ACR of the respondent as 'Good' as against assessment of 'Very Good' made by IO it could not be said that he was biased against the respondent - HC was wrong in holding that the respondent was targeted due to the personal bias of his superior officer - HC was also not justified in directing the authorities to promote the respondent to the post of Additional DIG - Findings of the judgment passed by HC set aside - Appeal allowed.
