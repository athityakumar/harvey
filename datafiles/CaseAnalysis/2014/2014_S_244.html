State of Haryana v Satender

Supreme Court of India

23 May 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 382; (2014) 7 SCC 291; 2014 CRLJ 4052; JT 2014 (7) SC 616; 2014 (3) RCR(Criminal) 332; 2014(7) SCALE 456

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: List Of Witnesses

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 109, 120B, 302 and 34 - Constitution of India, 1950, s. 136 - Murder - Conviction - Challenged - Prosecution alleged that appellant nos. 1 to 6 conspired with each other and committed murder of X with common intention - Trial Court held that the prosecution had successfully proved the allegations contained in the charge sheet that appellant nos. 1 to 3, in furtherance of their common intention, committed the murder of X and thereby committed an offence punishable u/s. 302 r/w s. 34 of the IPC - It was also held that appellant nos. 4 to 6 had abetted the appellant nos. 1 to 3 in the said offence and thereby committed an offence punishable u/s. 109 r/w s. 302 of the IPC - On appeal, HC allowed the appeal of appellant No. 4 thereby acquitting him of the charges framed against him - However, appeal of appellant no. 2 was dismissed maintaining the conviction and sentence as ordered by the trial court - Appeal was dismissed qua appellant no. 1, upholding his conviction and sentence, and allowed qua appellant no. 5 and 6, acquitting them of the charge of conspiracy and abetment framed u/s. 109 and 120-B of IPC respectively - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order of the HC could be upheld -

Held, evidence of the eye witnesses was corroborated by the medical evidence available on the record - In addition, there was also recovery of country made pistol of appellant no. 1 along with the motorcycle, which was used for the crime - Hence, appellant no. 1 was rightly convicted for the offence punishable u/s. 302 of IPC - Conduct, behaviour and active role of appellant no. 3, along with appellant no. 1, clearly depicts his knowledge as well as common intention of two of them to commit the crime - Hence, appellant no. 3 was also rightly convicted for the offence punishable u/s. 302 of IPC - Sufficient reasons were given by the HC in order to come to a conclusion that case against appellant no. 2 had not been proved beyond reasonable doubt - Once such a benefit of doubt was given after sifting and analysing the evidence and the findings were not perverse, it would not be appropriate to reverse that finding in these proceedings u/art. 136 of the Constitution - Appeal of State as well as the one filed by the complainant against the acquittal of appellant no. 2 were, accordingly, dismissed - Appeal dismissed.
