Municipal Corporation of Greater Mumbai and another v Anil Shantaram Khoje and others

Supreme Court of India

28 February 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Vikramajit Sen, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 133; 2014(3) ALL MR 423; JT 2014 (7) SC 266; 2014(3) SCALE 169; [2014] 3 S.C.R. 511; 2014 (4) SLR 687

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Bombay General Clauses Act, 1904, Jaipur Opium Act

Summary: Service - Bombay General Clauses Act, 1904, s.23 - Advocates Act, 1961 - Promotion granted - Legality - Petitioners before HC who were the contesting respondents were holding the post of Assistant Municipal Commissioners - Petitioner No.1 was holding the post of HOD, and had filed the appeal along with the Mumbai Municipal Corporation for the reason that the impugned judgment had allowed the writ petitions, directing the Mumbai Municipal Corporation to effect promotions to the post of Deputy Municipal Commissioner strictly in accordance with the resolution, sanctioned by the State Govt. in terms of its letter and the Roster point determined therein - Whether HC committed error in allowing promotions to the post of Deputy Municipal Commissioner strictly in accordance with the resolution -

Held, it was relevant for SC to mention s. 23 of 1904 Act, which provided thus: 'Where, in any Bombay Act (or Maharashtra Act), or in any Rule passed under such Act, it was directed that any order, notification or other matter shall be notified or published, then such notification or publication shall, unless the establishment or Rule otherwise provides, be deemed to be tailor made if it is published in Official Gazette' - SC was immediately reminded of the observations made in one case, when SC was called upon to consider a case 1961 Act - Babu Verghese vs Bar Council of Kerala, 1999 Indlaw SC 998, relied on - While doing so, SC applied the principles earlier enunciated in one case - Nazir Ahmad vs King Emperor 1936 Indlaw PC 67, relied on - Court observed as follows: 'It is the basic principles of law long settled that if the manner of doing a particular act is prescribed under any statute, the act must be done in that manner or not at all' - In that conspectus SC was unable to accept the position favoured by the HC in the impugned Judgment - Extant Rules would become operative only from the date of its promulgation by publication in the Official Gazette, i.e. on 28-4-2011 - Promotions made prior to 28-4-2011 under the extant Rules promoting respondent No. 1, respondent No. 5 and another to the post of Deputy Municipal Commissioner could not have been effected in the absence of publication of the extant Rules in the Official Gazette - SC noted that respondent No. 1 and respondent No. 5 had already retired from the post of Deputy Municipal Commissioner while another who was promoted on 5-7-2010 to the post of Deputy Municipal Commissioner, was still holding the post - Being mindful of the fact that their promotion and retiral and other consequential benefits would be adversely impacted by our Judgment, SC directed that the promotion effected prior to 28-4-2011 and consequential retiral and other benefits should not be altered to their detriment - SC, however, upheld the view of HC that, keeping the nature of the reliefs in the writ petition in perspective, the Roster had to be determined by the Mumbai Municipal Corporation in accordance with the extant Rules and all concerned officers would then be entitled to challenge the fixation, if they were aggrieved and if so advised - Appeals allowed.
