CBI, ACB, Mumbai v Narendra Lal Jain and another

Supreme Court of India

28 February 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjan Gogoi, N.V. Ramana, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 134; (2014) 5 SCC 364; 2014 ALL MR (Cri) 1481; JT 2014 (6) SC 638; 2014 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1418; 2014 (2) MLJ(Crl) 124; 2014 (2) RCR(Criminal) 15; 2014(3) SCALE 137; [2014] 3 S.C.R. 444

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, ss.120B and 420 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s.482 - Quashing of criminal proceedings - Sustainability - Appellant/Central Bureau of Investigation (CBI) ACB sought to challenge order passed by HC quashing the criminal proceedings against the respondent Nos.1 and 2 - Respondents had moved the HC u/s. 482 CrPC challenging the orders passed by Trial Court refusing to discharge them and also questioning the continuance of the criminal proceedings registered against them which was allowed by HC - Whether quashing of criminal proceedings by HC was maintainable -

Held, offence with which the accused-respondents were charged were u/ss.120-B and 420 of IPC - Civil liability of the respondents to pay the amount to the bank had already been settled amicably - Terms of such settlement had been extracted - No subsisting grievance of the bank in that regard was brought to the notice of the Court - While the offence u/s. 420 IPC was compoundable the offence u/s.120- B of IPC was not - To the latter offence the ratio laid down by SC would apply if the facts of the given case would so justify - B.S. Joshi and others v State of Haryana and another 2003 Indlaw SC 230 and Nikhil Merchant v Central Bureau of Investigation and Another 2008 Indlaw SC 1321, relied on - In instant case, having regard to the fact that the liability to make good the monetary loss suffered by the bank had been mutually settled between the parties and the accused had accepted the liability in that regard, the HC had thought it fit to invoke its power u/s. 482 CrPC - SC did not see how such exercise of power could be faulted or held to be erroneous - S. 482 CrPC inhered in HC the power to make such order as may be considered necessary to, inter alia, prevent the abuse of the process of law or to serve the ends of justice - While it would be wholly unnecessary to revert or refer to the settled position in law with regard to the contours of the power available u/s. 482 CrPC it should be remembered that continuance of a criminal proceeding which was likely to become oppressive or might partake the character of a lame prosecution would be good ground to invoke the extraordinary power u/s. 482 CrPC - SC, therefore, declined to interfere with the impugned order passed by HC - Appeal dismissed.
