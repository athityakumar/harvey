Somabhai Gopalbhai Patel v State of Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

24 September 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

C. Nagappan, Madan B. Lokur

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 662; 2014 ALL MR (Cri) 4487; 2015 CRLJ 255; JT 2014 (11) SC 397; 2014 (4) RCR(Criminal) 441; 2014(11) SCALE 471; [2014] 10 S.C.R. 668

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Conviction

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 7, 13(1)(d) r/w s. 13(2) - Bribe - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant accused was charged for offence punishable u/ss. 7, 13(1)(d) r/w s. 13(2) of the Act - Trial Court convicted and sentenced accused - Appeal before HC - HC confirmed the order of Trial Court - Hence instant appeal - Whether order of HC was sustainable.

Held, accused stated that sum of Rs.100 was due towards land revenue tax from complainant and he had only taken said amount from him towards the tax. Accused has not substantiated the said plea by producing any document relating to tax due and it appears to be only an afterthought. The Courts below have rightly not accepted the said explanation offered by him. Accused miserably failed to dislodge presumption u/s. 20 of the Act. Thus analyzed and understood, there remains no shadow of doubt that the appellant-accused had demanded the bribe and accepted the same to provide the documents sought for by the complainant. Therefore, the conviction recorded by Trial Court which has been affirmed by HC does not warrant any interference. Thus, imposition of minimum sentence prescribed for offences for which conviction is made would meet the ends of justice. Appeal allowed.
