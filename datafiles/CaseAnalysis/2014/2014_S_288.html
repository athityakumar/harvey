Alber Oraon v State of Jharkhand

Supreme Court of India

23 April 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Vikramajit Sen, K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 898; (2014) 12 SCC 306; AIR 2014 SC 3202; 2014 CRLJ 4238; [2014] 9 S.C.R. 330

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: (A) Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 201, 34 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant convict allegedly murdered deceased and two minors - Trial Court convicted and sentenced appellant to death - HC upheld conviction and sentence - Whether conviction and sentence recorded by the lower courts are sustainable.

Held, appellant living with deceased did not report their prolonged absence to the police. Appellant was a mason and also a civil-works contractor, and was, therefore, accomplished and competent to dig a soak-pit and dispose of the three bodies in that soak-pit and then make a brick covering thereon. Appellant having executed documents indicative of his marriage to the deceased as also her 'Declaration' to transfer her property to him, in the event of her death provide compelling motive for his having committed the crime of murdering her and her two minor children. Hence, appellant was logically found, beyond reasonable doubt, to have committed the murder of deceased and her children. Impugned conviction recorded by the lower courts is uphled. Appeal disposed of.

(B) Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Murder - Death sentence - Propriety - Whether the death sentence awarded by the lower courts in the instant case is appropriate.

Held, in Anil alias Antony vs. State of Maharashtra, SC had countenanced the gruesome murder by strangulation of a minor boy aged 10 years after subjecting him to carnal intercourse. Even though the action of the Convict to be extremely brutal, grotesque diabolical and revolting, it was that incarceration for a further period of 30 years, without remission, in addition to the sentence already undergone, would be appropriate punishment in the facts and circumstances obtaining in that case. The crime which encountered in the present appeals is also of similar reprehensible nature justifying the use of the same adjectives as SC employed in Anil alias Antony. Therefore, the same sentence should be imposed in the present case also. Hence, modifying the death sentence concurrently imposed by the courts below, appellant was sentenced to incarceration for a further period of 30 years without any remission. Appeal disposed of.
