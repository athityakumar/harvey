K. K. Ploycolor India Limited and others v Global Trade Finance Limited and another

Supreme Court of India

04 September 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, C. Nagappan, V. Gopala Gowda

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 585; (2014) 9 SCC 225; 2015 ALL MR (Cri) 4095; 2014 (4) BC 206; 2015 (1) MahLJ 570; 2015 (1) MPLJ 258; [2014] 9 S.C.R. 518

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, s. 138 - Criminal Proceedings - Quashing of - Respondent Company filed complaint before Trial Court u/s. 138 of the Act - Trial Court issued process against appellant/accused persons - Appellant filed revision before Appellate Court - Appellate Court set aside order passed by Trial Court - Aggrieved respondent filed applications before HC - HC set aside orders passed by Appellate Court and ordered Trial Court to proceed with trial - Hence instant appeals - Whether impugned order passed by HC was justified -

Held, Court has on an elaborate consideration of provision of s. 138 and law on subject, held that presentation of a cheque for collection on the drawee bank or issue of a notice from a place of the choice of complainant would not by themselves confer jurisdiction upon Courts where cheque was presented for collection or default notice issued demanding payment from drawer of the cheque. SC have no hesitation in holding that HC was wrong in interfering with order passed by Trial Court - Impugned order passed by HC was set aside and restore those passed by Appellate Court. Appeals allowed.
