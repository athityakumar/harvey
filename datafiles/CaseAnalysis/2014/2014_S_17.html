S.E.B.I. v Sahara India Real Estate Corporation Limited and others

Supreme Court of India

22 July 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, A.K. Sikri

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 478; (2014) 8 SCC 741; 2014 (3) CLT(SC) 112; 2014 (3) CompLJ 353; 2015 CRLJ 2470; JT 2014 (8) SC 383; 2014 (4) RCR(Criminal) 277; 2014(8) SCALE 645

Case Digest

Subject: Securities Exchange Board of India

Keywords: Bonds

Summary: (A)Securities Exchange Board of India - Sale of properties - Permissibility - Applicant/Saharas had made the applications seeking certain directions - In I.As No. 8-9 of 2014, applicant had prayed for temporary/conditional release from judicial custody for a period of 15 days or so to meet his nonagenarian and ailing mother as also for taking steps for compliance with the order of Sc dt. 26-3-2014 - Applicant had, inter alia, stated that his mother who was aged over 93 years suffered from several ailments which complicated matters in view of her being in a fragile emotional state - Applicant was also, according to the averments, not keeping good health requiring medical attention - Application, however, stoppeds short of elaborating the medical condition of the applicant - More importantly, the application sought release of applicant on parole with a view to negotiating deals directly with the purchasers who had shown interest in the purchase of the property being offered for sale by the applicant - In the accompanying I.As Nos.10, 11 & 12 of 2014 applicant had prayed for permission to obtain a bank guarantee of Rs.5,000/- crores by leveraging the three overseas hotel properties by way of sale, mortgage in the light of the Bank of China's consent to such sale or transfer, and certification that the valuation reports were prepared at the instance of the Bank and accepted by it - Applicant also sought permission for sale, hypothecation, mortgage/leverage the land owned by them and situate in Versova - Whether applicant could be allowed the permission to sell overseas properties for submitting the funds with SEBI for securing his release -

Held, contemnors stood committed to jail by the Order of SC dt. 4-3-2014 on account of their failure to comply with the directions of this Court's Orders dt. 31-8- 2012 and 5-12-2012 and those issued on 25-2-2013 in I.A. No.67 of 2013 in Civil Appeal No.9813 of 2011 and I.A No.5 in Civil Appeal No.9833 of 2011 - Interim bail order passed by SC on 26-3-2014 required them to deposit Rs.10,000/- crores, out of which Rs.5,000/- crores had to be in cash while the balance had to be secured by bank guarantee of a nationalised bank furnished in favour of SEBI - It was with a view to enabling the contemnors to comply with the said direction that SC had by Order dt. 4-6-2014 lifted the embargo placed upon operation of the bank accounts and sale/transfer of immovable assets held by the applicant qua nine properties referred to in the said order - Applicant had since then deposited an amount of more than Rs.3,000/- crores with SEBI by encashment of FDs, Bonds and securities - Applicant had also out of the nine properties referred to above sold the property situate in Ahmedabad for a sum of Rs.4,11,82,55,138 - Remaining eight properties, however, remained to be sold or encumbered - SC had in the light of the above asked applicant whether the proposed sale/transfer of the offshore hotel properties was essential when no less than eight other properties apart from Aamby Valley (Pune) remained to be sold or encumbered for raising funds necessary for compliance with the order of SC - Applicant argued that it might be easier for the contemnors-Saharas to leverage the overseas hotel properties for deposit of the deficit of around Rs.2000/- crores and arranging a bank guarantee of Rs.5,000/- crores in comparison to sale or transfer of property situate within the country which might take a relatively longer period leading to continued incarceration of the contemnors in jail -

It was submitted that so long as it was ensured that the offshore properties were sold for the market value they command, the applicant should have the liberty to do so - There was,merit in the contention urged by applicant - What was important was that the properties held by the applicant were sold at their market value and the sale proceeds, subject to any other directions issued by HC, utilised for compliance with the terms of the conditional bail order issued by SC - It was evident that if sale of properties situate within the country was likely to take time, the contemnors might be exposed to a longer period of incarceration on account of their failure to comply with the directions of this Court - On the other hand, quicker the compliance with the directions of the Court's Order for deposit of cash and bank guarantee, the easier would be the way out of jail for them - Anxiety on the part of the applicant generally and the contemnors in particular to sell the offshore properties was, therefore, understandable especially when such sale and transfer was not only going to help applicant in liquidating the outstanding loan amount payable to the Bank of China but leave sufficient surplus with the applicant to not only deposit the balance of Rs.2,000/- crores approximately that needs to be immediately paid by them but also furnish a bank guarantee for a sum of Rs.5,000/- crores, as directed - SC, therefore, saw no legal impediment in permitting the sale of the offshore properties owned by applicant - It was particularly so when not only do we have the valuation reports of the said properties on record prepared as they were by internationally reputed valuers but also the concurrence of SEBI for the sale of such properties at that value subject to the condition that the sale consideration shall as far as possible be at the estimated value of such properties, less, at the most by 5% of such value - SC was, mindful of the fact that applicant had sold the property at Ahmedabad at more than three times the circle rates of such property - No such rates were, however, available or prescribed for offshore properties - SC should, therefore, have to go only by the valuation reports of the valuers as the basis for such proposed sale/transfer subject to a margin of 5% which SC had indicated - In case the offer received was lesser by more than 5%, they would seek prior approval of the Court - SC might incidentally mention at instant stage that applicant had sought a clarification of our Order dt. 4-6-2014 inasmuch as in the para 23 (iii) (b) of the said order, SC had stated that the sale of the properties referred to in the order should not be for an amount lesser than the circle rate for such properties or the estimated value indicated by the applicant whereas in the operative portion of the said order SC had permitted sale at a price that was not lower than the circle rate prescribed for such properties - Having regard, however, to the experience that applicant had had with the sale of properties in Ahmedabad which fetched more than three times the circle rates prescribed for the same, SC was of the view that the actual market value of the property held by applicant was many times more than the circle rates for such property - That was evident not only from the sale transaction relating to Ahmadabad property but also the fact that applicant had themselves estimated the value of the properties much higher than the circle rates for the same - In the circumstances, SC saw no difficulty in clarifying that the sale of the remainder of the properties which SC had permitted to be sold by our order dt. 4-6-2014 should not be lesser than the estimated value of the properties given by applicant less by no more than 5% of such estimated value - In case the offer(s) received was/were less by more than 5%, prior approval of the Court would have to be sought - Petitions dismissed.

(B) Securities Exchange Board of India - Parole - Entitlement of - Whether the contemnors could be granted parole as prayed for in the applications - Held, SC did not see any justification to take a view different from the one taken in its order dt. 4-6-2014 - There was nothing before SC to show that applicant suffered from any serious medical condition - At any rate, SC expect the jail doctors to keep a check on his medical condition and provide necessary medical aid as and when required - Alternative ground urged for the grant of parole also did not stand closer scrutiny - There was no concrete proposal with applicant for sale of the properties situate in India or abroad that might call for any negotiation by applicant - While it might be true that such negotiations could not be said to be advisable when properties of such magnitude as in the instant case were sought to be sold, yet it was pre- mature for SC to make any arrangement to facilitate any such negotiations either by directing release of applicant on parole or otherwise - SC might make it clear that if a situation arises in which negotiations become essential, SC might consider passing orders to facilitate such negotiations - Beyond that SC did not consider it necessary or proper to say anything at instant stage - Petitions dismissed.
