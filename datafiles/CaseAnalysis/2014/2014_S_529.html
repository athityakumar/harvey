Sujoy Kumar Chanda and another v Damayanti Majhi and anothers

Supreme Court of India

20 February 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjana Prakash Desai, Madan B. Lokur

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 113; (2014) 5 SCC 181; 2014 (2) RCR(Criminal) 135; 2014(3) SCALE 117

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Murder, Clubbing of complaint case and police case

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. , 148, 149, 353, 307, 302, 201, 20B, 34 and 326 - Indian Arms Act, 1959, ss. 25, 27 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 319 - Murder - Clubbing of complaint case and police case - Maintainability - Complaint was registered against appellant accused for offence punishable u/ss. 149, 353, 307, 302, 201, 20B, 34 and 326 IPC and u/ss. 25, 27 of the Act - Magistrate refused to issue process against appellants and passed order of clubbing complaint case with police case - Order was challenged before HC - HC set aside the clubbing of both cases - Whether the order passed by HC was maintainable -

Held, HC in its order did not issue any direction to Magistrate to proceed against appellants - HC only set aside order of clubbing of complaint case with police case and observed that after commitment of case, Sessions Judge could, if situation so demanded in exercise of his powers u/s. 319 CrPC, summon other accused persons who have been left out by Magistrate - Thus, Magistrate was to commit case to Sessions Court and Sessions Court in its discretion could have summoned other accused u/s. 319 of CrPC - Magistrate appeared to have misconstrued HC,s order and taken it as direction to issue process against all accused - Complainant being aggrieved by order passed by Sessions Court filed revisional application before HC against order of Sessions Judge - By impugned order, HC set aside order of Sessions Court and restored order of Magistrate - While setting aside order of learned Sessions Judge, HC passed caustic comments on him, which was unwarranted - Sessions Judge was of view that HC's order was not followed by Magistrate and in that anxiety, he modified said order - Hence, Court opined to remit matter to Magistrate for committal of case to Sessions Judge so that the case should proceed after evidence was led - If it appeared to Sessions Judge that involvement of any person was evident, he can summon appellants or any other persons u/s. 319 CrPC - Hence, impugned order of HC was set aside - Appeals disposed of.
