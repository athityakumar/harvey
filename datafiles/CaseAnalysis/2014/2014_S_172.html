State of Assam and others v Susrita Holdings Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

23 April 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, Gyan Sudha Misra

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 288; (2014) 11 SCC 192; AIR 2014 SC 2307; JT 2014 (5) SC 376; 2014 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 2562; 2014(5) SCALE 542

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Auction of land - Set aside - Challenged - Govt. of Assam issued instructions in respect of alienation of tea garden land from time to time - Liquidator, issued a notice inviting tenders for the sale of Chincoorie Tea Estate owned by X (Society) - As on 5-1-2007, no tender had been cast in response to the tender notice issued - Liquidator issued a fresh tender - Respondent had submitted his tender document - Liquidator subsequently, cancelled the tender process by observing that the price quoted by the parties for the 9000 bighas of land was not at all justifiable - Respondent filed a Writ Petition challenging the cancellation Order by the liquidator - Registrar of the Co- operative Societies, permitted the Liquidator to dispose of the property involved in favour of the respondent - However, vide Communication dt. 20-1-2010 from the Secretary, Co- operation Dept. to the Deputy Commissioner, Liquidator was directed to refrain from registering the sale deed in respect of the property in question without the clearance from the Co-operation Dept. - Respondent filed another Writ Petition seeking a direction to the appellant for execution of the sale deed in its favour and also to quash the communication of the Secretary of the Co-operation Dept. which gave direction to the Deputy Commissioner, to refrain him from registering the sale deed without the clearance of the Co- operation Dept. - HC held that since the amount had already been paid by the respondent to the Dept. , there was no question of taking further approval from the govt. - Review Petition filed by the appellant against said order was dismissed by the HC - Hence, instant appeal - Whether the appellants lawfully cancelled the tender process in relation to the property in question in view of the discrepancies crept in the process of transfer of the land in favour of the respondent -

Held, liquidator does not act fairly and reasonably in the best interest of the public of the State whose interest he was required to uphold - As per the material evidence put on record, the liquidator and the concerned authority does not take any step to improve the condition of the land and sell it at reasonable and standard price prevalent at the time of sale of the property in question - Hence, tender process initiated by the appellants was not legal and was liable to be set aside - Court directed the concerned authority to issue fresh notice of tender for selling the land - Notice should be made available in government websites and other local and national newspapers so as to encourage and invite more bidders - Since, the respondent had paid up the entire bid amount, it was entitled to refund of the entire amount - Further, since it was also proved that the amount paid by the respondent had been used to pay the arrears, the respondent was entitled to interest for the amount paid @7% p.a. from the date of payment till the date of refund - Impugned order of the HC was set aside - Appeal allowed.
