Kisan Shankar Kathore v Arun Dattatray Sawant and others

Supreme Court of India

09 May 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, Surinder Singh Nijjar

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 353; (2014) 14 SCC 162; AIR 2014 SC 2069; 2014 (5) ALD(SC) 61; 2014(4) ALL MR 917; 2014 (5) AWC 4619; 2014 (4) Bom.C.R. 596; 2014 (2) CLT(SC) 319; JT 2014 (6) SC 357; 2014 (5) MLJ 109; 2014 (3) RCR(Civil) 131; 2014(6) SCALE 675; [2014] 7 S.C.R. 258

Case Digest

Subject: Election; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Suppression of facts in nomination form, Validity of election

Summary: Election - Practice & Procedure - Representation of the People Act, 1951, ss. 100(1)(d)(i) and (iv), 125A - Suppression of facts in nomination form - Validity of election - Election petition was filed u/s. 100(1)(d)(i) and (iv) of the Act by respondent against appellant on ground that appellant suppressed his dues payable to Government, suppressed assets of his spouse and suppressed information and assets of partnership firm of which he was partner in nomination form - Petition was contested by appellant - HC allowed election petition and set aside appellant election - Hence, instant appeal - Whether the order of HC was valid -

Held, when information was given by candidate in affidavit filed along with nomination paper and objections were raised thereto questioning correctness of information or alleging that there was non-disclosure of certain important information, it might not be possible for returning officer at that time to conduct detailed examination - Summary enquiry might not suffice - At same time, it would not be possible for Returning Officer to reject nomination for want of verification about allegations made by objector - When it was proved that it was case of non-disclosure and either affidavit was false or it did not contain complete information leading to suppression, it should be held at that stage that nomination was improperly accepted - Grounds stated in s. 36(2)of the Act were those which should be examined there and then and on that basis Returning Officer would be in position to reject nomination - Where blanks were left in affidavit, nomination should be rejected there and then - In other cases where detailed enquiry was needed, it would depend upon outcome thereof, in election petition as to whether nomination was properly accepted or it was case of improper acceptance - Once it was found that it was case of improper acceptance, as there was misinformation or suppression of material information, one should state that question of rejection in such case was only deferred to later date - When Court gave such finding, which would have resulted in rejection, effect would be same, such candidate was not entitled to contest and election was void - Otherwise, it would be anomalous situation that even when criminal proceedings u/s. 125A of the Act should be initiated and selected candidate was criminally prosecuted and convicted, however result of his election should not be questioned, which should not be countenanced - Appeal dismissed.
