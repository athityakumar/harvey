Prafulla C. Dave and others v Municipal Commissioner and others

Supreme Court of India

03 December 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjan Gogoi, R.K. Agrawal

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 823; AIR 2015 SC 426; 2015(2) ALL MR 400; 2015 (1) Bom.C.R. 88; JT 2014 (13) SC 420; 2014(13) SCALE 457

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Land & Property - Municipalities & Local Governments - Maharashtra Regional and Town Planning Act, 1966, s. 127 - Land reserved for development plan - De-reservation - Appellants' land was included in development plan - However, land was not acquired - Appellants sent notice u/s. 127 of the Act seeking de-reservation of land after finalizing revised plan - Respondent authority rejected appellants request - Appellants filed appeal u/s. 47 before Respondent - Respondent rejected appellants appeal on the ground that notice u/s. 127 of the Act was premature as it was issued before the completion of the period of ten years from the date of revised development plan - Appellants challenged respondent's order before HC - HC rejected appellants' writ petition - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, notice u/s. 127 can be issued by the owner or any person interested in the land only if the land is not acquired or provisions for acquisition is not initiated within ten years from the date on which the final development plan had come into force. Delayed action on the part of the land owner, that is, after the revised plan has been finalized and published will not invalidate the reservation, allotment or designation that may have been made or continued in the revised plan. In the instant case, notice u/s. 127 by the appellants was issued only two years after the final revised plan u/s. 38 had come into operation. The rejection of the appellants' plea before the appellate authority u/s. 47 of the Act as well as the rejection of the writ petition filed by the appellants before the HC was, therefore, fully justified. No reason was found to interfere with the impugned order of HC. Appeal dismissed.

Ratio - Action on the part of the land owner or the person interested as required u/s. 127 should be anterior in point of time to the preparation of the revised plan. Delayed action on the part of the land owner will not invalidate the reservation, allotment or designation that may have been made or continued in the revised plan.
