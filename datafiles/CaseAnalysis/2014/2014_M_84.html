Mineral Exploration Corporation Limited v Arvind Kumar Dixit and another

Supreme Court of India

03 December 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Prafulla C. Pant, Vikramajit Sen

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 931; (2015) 2 SCC 535; JT 2014 (14) SC 404; 2014(13) SCALE 514; [2014] 11 S.C.R. 255

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Revision Of Pay, Restructuring

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Wage revision - Cut-off date - In pursuance of instructions from Govt. of India, appellant-Corporation issued order dt.25-8-2006, to effect that even though wage revision was due from 1-1-1997, yet, it would be effective only from 1-4-2003 and would be implemented from 1-4-2006 - However, respondent-employees, who opted for voluntary retirement before 1-4-2003 but subsequent to 1997, claimed wage revision and filed writ petitions which were transferred to Tribunal as Transferred Applications - Tribunal vide its common judgment dt.4-8-2010, held that employees who retired on or after 1-4-2003 would be entitled to actual benefits of wage revision, and employees who retired on or before 1-4-2003 would be given similar treatment by revision in notional pay - Aggrieved, appellant-Corporation filed writ petitions before HC but the same were dismissed and hence, instant appeals - Whether fixing 1-4-2003 as cut-off date by Corporation is arbitrary and irrational.

Held, by conjoint reading of letters issued by Govt. of India, wage revision was to be implemented with effect from 1-4-2006 allowing employees who were superannuated or voluntarily retired with effect from 1-4-2003. Hence, only those employees are entitled to wage revision who were on rolls as on 1-4-2003 as mentioned in office order dt.25-8-2006. Further, in view of law laid down by SC in various decisions, cut-off date i.e., 1-4-2003 for granting wage revision, is neither arbitrary nor violative of art.14 of Constitution. Hence, employees who superannuated or voluntarily retired prior to 1-4-2003 from Corporation are not entitled to notional wage revision as directed by Tribunal and HC. Hence, impugned judgments of HC and Tribunal were set aside. Appeals disposed of.
