State of Uttar Pradesh v Arvind Kumar Srivastava and others

Supreme Court of India

17 October 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, J. Chelameswar

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 721; (2015) 1 SCC 347; JT 2014 (12) SC 94; 2014 (7) MLJ 885; 2014(12) SCALE 234; 2014 (6) SLR 456; 2014 (4) UPLBEC 2927

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Cancellation, Claim of parity, Judgment in rem

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Claim of parity - Judgment in rem - Legality - Respondents (employees) filed a suit against the cancellation of their appointments - Said suit was dismissed for non-prosecution - However, other candidates who were also affected by same order of cancellations, approached the Tribunal on the ground that no show-cause notice was given along with other grounds - Tribunal decided in favour of said other candidates, against which State filed a petition before HC - Single Judge dismissed the petition confirming the order passed by Tribunal - SLP filed by State was also dismissed on 12-8-1994 - Order of Tribunal had attained finality and said other candidates got the appointments - Thereafter, respondents claiming parity filed petition before Tribunal for giving appointments to them, which was allowed and HC by impugned order affirmed the order of Tribunal - Whether in the given case, approach of Tribunal and HC was correct in extending the benefit of earlier judgment of Tribunal, which had attained finality as it was affirmed till the SC.

Held, selection process in instant case took place in the year 1986. Appointment orders were issued in the year 1987, but were also cancelled vide orders dt.22-6-1987. The respondents did not challenge these cancellation orders till the year 1996, i.e. for a period of 9 years. It means that they had accepted the cancellation of their appointments. They woke up in the year 1996 only after finding that some other persons whose appointment orders were also cancelled got the relief. By that time, 9 years had passed. The earlier judgment had granted the relief to the parties before the Court. It would also be pertinent to highlight that these respondents have not joined the service nor working like the employees who succeeded in earlier case before the Tribunal. As of today, 27 years have passed after the issuance of cancellation orders. Therefore, not only there was unexplained delay and laches in filing the claim petition after period of 9 years, it would be totally unjust to direct the appointment to give them the appointment as of today, i.e. after a period of 27 years when most of these respondents would be almost 50 years of age or above. Impugned orders of HC and Tribunal were set aside. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - When there is a judgment in rem with intention to give benefit to all similarly situated persons, whether they approached the Court or not, an obligation was cast upon the authorities to itself extend the benefit thereof to all similarly situated person. Those who want to get benefit of said judgment extended to them should have to satisfy that their petition does not suffer from either laches and delays or acquiescence.
