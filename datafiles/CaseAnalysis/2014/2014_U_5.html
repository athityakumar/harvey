Joginder Singh v Union Territory of Chandigarh and others

Supreme Court of India

11 November 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 893; (2015) 2 SCC 377; 2015(4) ALL MR 952; 2015 (144) FLR 183; JT 2014 (13) SC 160; 2014(12) SCALE 653; [2014] 11 S.C.R. 155; 2015 (4) SLR 143

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Punjab Police Rules, 1934

Summary: Service - Appointment - Denied - Challenged - Appellant was called for an interview as he was found fit for selection to the post of Constable - However, after verification of appellant antecedents and character from his native village, it was found that he was involved in a case with FIR No. 200 dt. 14-4-1998, under the provisions of ss. 148, 149, 323, 325 and 307 of IPC - After the trial, the appellant was acquitted from the charges levelled against him - Appellant filed Original Application before the Tribunal, for issuing a direction to the respondent for issuance of an appointment order in view of his selection to the post in the selection process - Tribunal allowed said application and directed the respondents to appoint the appellant to the post of Constable within a period of 30 days from the date of receipt of the certified copy of the order - HC allowed appeal filed against said order - Hence, instant appeals - Whether the denial of the benefit of appointment to the appellant by the HC is legal and valid in the light of the fact that the appellant was acquitted from the criminal case pending against him.

Held, alleged past conduct of the appellant in relation to the criminal case will not debar or disqualify him for the post of the Constable for which he was successfully selected after qualifying the written test, medical test and the interview conducted by the selection authority. Further, there has been no concealment of any relevant fact from the respondents by the appellant. The respondents were thus not justified in denying the said post to the appellant. Appeals allowed.
