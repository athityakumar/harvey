Karedla Parthasaradhi v Gangula Ramanamma (D) Through Lrs. and others

Supreme Court of India

04 December 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Sapre, M.Y. Eqbal

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 848; AIR 2015 SC 891; 2015 (2) ALD(SC) 67; 2015(3) ALL MR 398; 2015 (2) ALT(SC) 18; 2015 (1) CLT(SC) 1; 2015 (1) CTC 114; JT 2014 (14) SC 65; 2015 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 257; 2015 (1) RCR(Civil) 223; 2015 (1) RCR(Criminal) 225; 2014(13) SCALE 588; 2015 (2) WLN(SC) 161

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Keywords: Adopted Son

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 22 rr. 4 and 5 - Hindu Succession Act, 1956 - Legal representative - Plaintiff filed a suit for eviction against defendant no. 1 in Trial Court submitting that on the death of K, the suit house devolved upon the plaintiff being his brother - Further submitted that the defendant no.1 was employed by K to cook his food and defendant no. 1 had neither any ownership nor any tenancy rights over the suit house - Defendant No. 1 contended that she was legally married wife of K and was living with him since decades in the suit house - It was further submitted that after K death, she became the sole owner of the suit house by virtue of law of inheritance being a class-I heir i.e., wife - Trial Court decreed suit in favour of plaintiff and held that defendant no. 1 was working as cook for K during his life time and being his servant, neither acquired nor inherited any right, title and interest and nor did acquire any possessory rights in the suit house after the death of K - Later, defendant No. 1 died - Respondent No. 1 filed an application under O. 22 r. 4 r/w s. 151 of the CPC and prayed that his name be substituted in place of deceased appellant as he was the adopted son of the deceased defendant no. 1 and defendant no. 1 had also executed one Will on 2-1-1984 in his favour bequeathing the suit house to him - HC allowed said application and permitted respondent No. 1 to become the appellant and prosecute the appeal on merits - Respondent No. 1 filed appeal against order of the Trial Court - HC allowed said appeal - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, HC simply allowed the application without recording a finding as to whether any right in the suit property was devolved in favour of respondent no.1 after the death of defendant no. 1 and if so, in what capacity. This finding alone would have enabled respondent no. 1 to become the appellant and prosecute the appeal on merits and this was a case where inquiry into the question was necessary and it could be done only by the Trial Court. Trial Court will decide the question keeping in view the provisions of O. 22 rr. 4 and 5 of CPC and record a finding on the questions (1) whether respondent No. 1 is the adopted son of defendant no.1 and if so, how and on what basis, (2) whether defendant no.1 executed Will dt. 1-2-1984 in favour of respondent No. 1 and if so, whether it is a genuine Will as per law and (3) if Will dt. 1-2-1984 is held as genuine, whether bequeath of the suit property is made by such Will in favour of respondent No. 1. Let the inquiry be held by the Trial Court after affording an opportunity to all parties concerned to file reply to application and adduce evidence in addition to evidence. Matter remanded to the Trial Court. Order accordingly.
