Rajasthan R.S.S. and Ginning Mills Federation Limited v Dy. Commissioner of Income Tax, Jaipur

Supreme Court of India

29 April 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Shiva Kirti Singh

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 321; (2014) 11 SCC 672; [2014] 363 ITR 564; JT 2014 (5) SC 606; 2014(6) SCALE 78; [2014] 7 S.C.R. 336

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Rajasthan Co-Operative Societies Act, 1965

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, s.72 - Rajasthan Co-operative Societies Act, 1965, s.16(8) - Tax assessment - Liability - There were four co-operative societies in the State of Rajasthan wherein the Govt. of Rajasthan had substantial share holding - Upon amalgamation of the said societies into the appellant society, the registration of the said four co-operative societies was cancelled and all the assets and liabilities of the said four societies was taken over by the appellant society by virtue of the amalgamation - Four societies were not sound financially and they had substantial accumulative losses - After the amalgamation of the four co-operative societies into the appellant society, when Income-Tax returns for the assessment years 1994-95 and 1995-96 were filed by the appellant, the appellant wanted to get the accumulated losses of the societies, of about Rs.2,68,39,504/-, carried forward, so that the same could be set off against the profits of the appellant society under the provisions of s.72 of 1961 Act - AO negatived the appellant's claim for the reason that the said societies were not in existence after their amalgamation into the appellant society - As the said four societies were not in existence, according to the AO, their accumulated losses could not have been carried forward or adjusted against the profits of the appellant society - Assessment orders were passed accordingly - Being aggrieved by the assessment orders, appeals were filed before the CIT (Appeals) and the CIT (Appeals) dismissed the said appeals - Further appeals were filed before the Income Tribunal but the Tribunal also dismissed the appeals - Being aggrieved by the common order passed by the Tribunal, the appellant filed Income Tax Appeal before HC and the said Income Tax Appeal was also dismissed - Hence instant appeal - Whether prayer for carry forward of losses so that the same could be set off against the profits of the appellant under the provisions of s.72 of 1961 Act was maintainable -

Held, main submission of appellant society was that the appellant society, being an amalgamated society, should get benefit of setting off losses of the co-operative societies which had been amalgamated into the appellant society - According to appellant by virtue of the provisions of s. 16(8) of 1965 Act, r/w. ss. 72 and 72(A) of 1961 Act, the accumulated losses of the amalgamating societies should have been permitted to be adjusted or set off against the profits of the appellant society. His main submission was that by virtue of S. 16(8) of 1965 Act all legal proceedings initiated against or by the amalgamating co-operative societies would continue and therefore, right of the amalgamating societies with regard to getting their losses carried forward and set off against the profits of the amalgamated society would continue - SC was not in agreement with the submissions made by appellant for the reason that for the purpose of getting carried forward losses adjusted or set off against the profits of subsequent years, there should be some provision in 1961 Act - If there was no provision, the societies which were not in existence cannot get any benefit - Losses were suffered by the societies which were in existence at the relevant time and their existence or legal personality had come to an end upon being amalgamated into another society - Normal principle was that a non-existent person could not file an income tax return and therefore, could not carry forward its losses after its existence comes to an end - All those four societies, upon their amalgamation into the appellant, had ceased to exist and registration of those societies was cancelled - In the circumstances, those societies had no right under the provisions of 1961 Act to file a return to get their earlier losses adjusted against the income of a different legal personality i.e. the appellant - Appeal dismissed.
