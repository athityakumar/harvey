Kumari Kiran S/o Harinarayan v Sajjan Singh and others

Supreme Court of India

11 September 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, Adarsh Kumar Goel

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 613; (2015) 1 SCC 539; 2014 (4) ACC 197; 2014 ACJ 2550; 2015 (1) ALD(SC) 105; 2014 (107) ALR 669; 2014 (6) AWC 5675; JT 2014 (11) SC 279; 2015 (3) MahLJ 626; 2015 (2) MPLJ 662; 2014 (4) RCR(Civil) 362; 2014(10) SCALE 462

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance

Summary: (A) Carriers & Transportation - Insurance - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 - Quantum of compensation - Victims of motor-vehicle accident - Appellant-minor daughter and the appellant-father sustained 30% permanent disability and the appellant-minor-son 20% permanent disability in a motor vehicular accident - Claim petitions were filed before the Motor Accident Claims Tribunal (Tribunal) - Tribunal apportioned contributory negligence at 50% on the part of the appellant-father who was riding the motorcycle on which the appellant-minors were the pillion riders and 50% on the driver of the offending tractor - Tribunal awarded an interest at the rate of 6% p.a. on the total compensation - On appeal, HC held that the appellant-minors who were the pillion riders could not be held for contributory negligence as apportioned by the Tribunal even if their appellant-father who was the motorcyclist was at fault - HC enhanced the compensation of the appellant-minor daughter and son - Hence, instant appeal -

Held, the compensation should be reasonably sufficient so that it equips the victim to return to their normal life to the maximum possible extent. The Tribunal and the HC have failed to show compassion to the appellant-minors and appellant-father by not examining the above relevant aspect of the case on hand and not following the guidelines as laid down by SC to determine just and reasonable compensation. Appeals allowed.

(B) Carriers & Transportation - Insurance - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 - Quantum of compensation - Future loss of income - Permanent disability -

Held, a child's notional income cannot be ascertained as per the figure given for a non-earning individuals in the second schedule of the Act. As the Tribunal and the HC have not followed the principles laid down by SC by awarding loss of future income due to permanent disability, therefore, same set aside. Appeals allowed.

(C) Carriers & Transportation - Insurance - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 - Quantum of compensation - Contributory negligence -

Held, the contributory negligence apportioned by the HC at 25% on the appellant-father and 75% on the driver of the offending tractor is erroneous. The negligence is wholly on the part of the driver of the offending tractor since he was driving the heavier vehicle. Therefore, 25% contributory negligence on the part of the appellant-father as apportioned by the HC set aside. The compensation awarded was enhanced. Appeals allowed.

Ratio - The compensation should be reasonably sufficient so that it equips the victim to return to their normal life to the maximum possible extent.
