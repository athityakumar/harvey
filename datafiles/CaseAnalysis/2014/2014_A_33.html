Securities and Exchange Board of India v Akshya Infrastructure Private Limted

Supreme Court of India

25 April 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Surinder Singh Nijjar, A.K. Sikri

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 308; (2014) 11 SCC 112; AIR 2014 SC 1963; 2014(5) ALL MR 438; 2014 (2) BC 536; 2014 (4) Bom.C.R. 228; [2014] 184 Comp Cas 248; 2014 (3) CompLJ 1; JT 2014 (6) SC 538; 2015 (2) RCR(Civil) 22; 2014(6) SCALE 85

Case Digest

Subject: Securities Exchange Board of India

Keywords: Shares, Open offer

Summary: Securities Exchange Board of India - Securities and Exchange Board of India Act, 1992, s. 15Z - Securities and Exchange Board of India (Substantial Acquisition of Shares and Takeovers) (Second Amendment) Regulations, 2002 - Securities and Exchange Board of India (Substantial Acquisition of Shares and Takeovers) Regulations, 1997, regns. 11, 11(1), 11(2), 12, 13, 14, 18(1), 27 - Open offer - Shares - Legality - Respondent/M/s Akshya Infrastructure Pvt. Ltd., was part of Promoter Group of MARG Ltd., ('the Target Company') - Respondent made voluntary open offer through Public Announcement wherein public shareholders of Target Company were given an opportunity to exit at an offer price of Rs. 91/- per equity share - Respondent by letter addressed SEBI and sought to contend that open offer in question had become outdated, same ought to be permitted to be withdrawn - SEBI offered comment - Comments of SEBI were challenged by respondent before Securities Appellate Tribunal - Tribunal set aside impugned direction and did not make any observation on merits of issue regarding three alleged triggers and contentions of parties in regard were kept open - Aggrieved SEBI filed instant appeal - Whether an open offer voluntarily made through a Public Announcement for purchase of shares of target company could be permitted to be withdrawn at time when voluntary open offer had become uneconomical to be performed -

Held, respondent had failed to place on record either before Tribunal or before SC prejudice that was caused by not observing Rules of Natural Justice - Such breach of Rules of Natural Justice must also entail avoidable prejudice to respondent - Reasoning of ours was supported by number of cases - All information sought by SEBI related to three earlier acquisitions when creeping limit for acquisition was breached for triggering mandatory 2002 Regulations - Tribunal left question with regard to earlier three acquisitions open and to be decided in accordance with law - Clearly no prejudice was caused to respondent - Deletion of regn. 27(1)(a) does not, in any manner, advance case of respondent - It rather reinforces conclusion that an open offer once made could only be withdrawn in circumstances stipulated under regn. 27(1)(b)(c) and (d) - Delay in offering comments by Board on letter containing voluntary open offer, though undesirable was not fatal to decision ultimately taken by Board - Impossibility envisioned under aforesaid regulation would not include contingency where voluntary open offer once made could be permitted to be withdrawn on ground that it had now become economically unviable - SC were not able to approve approach adopted by Tribunal in adopting 2009 Regulations for interpreting provisions contained in regn. 27 of 2002 Regulations - Regulations in Takeover Code had to be interpreted by correlating these regulations to provisions of 1992 Act - Impugned order passed by Tribunal was set aside and directions issued by appellant were restored - Appeal allowed.
