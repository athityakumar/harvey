G. Manikyamma and another v Roudri Co-operative Housing Society Limited and others

Supreme Court of India

25 November 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

J. Chelameswar, Sharad Arvind Bobde

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 938; AIR 2015 SC 720; 2015 (3) ALD(SC) 126; JT 2014 (13) SC 384

Case Digest

Subject: Human Rights; Land & Property

Keywords: Protection Of Human Rights Act, 1993, Andhra Pradesh Land Encroachment Act, 1905

Summary: (A) Land & Property - Human Rights - Eviction of encroachers by State from private land - Justification - Respondent Society no.1 entered into sale agreement for purchase of land in question from six persons - Further, authority permitted respondent no.1 registered Co-operative Housing Societies to purchase land from persons whose lands are declared as surplus lands - However, land in question was encroached by hut dwellers - Further, interim order was passed by HC directing revenue authorities to evict encroachers by removing huts erected in land belonging to Society - Encroachers were evicted from land in question by State authority by means of police force - Complaint was registered before State Human Rights Commission which passed order in favor of hut dwellers and directed to take steps for immediate restoration of water and electricity supply to hut-dwellers whose huts have been demolished, and they be not evicted from said place till consideration as to sanction of pucca houses to said people - Whether eviction of encroacher from land in question by State is justified.

Held, authority of State to evict encroachers for benefit of members of respondent no.1 Society by use of police force is wholly inconsistent with rule of law. Mode of eviction of unauthorised occupants depends on ownership of property. SC held that State could have resorted to summary procedure of eviction of encroachers only if land in dispute vests in State and possession of squatters is of recent origin. There is nothing on record to establish that land in question vests in State. Further, there is nothing on record before us to establish that land in question was duly taken possession by Govt. under provisions of Act. Until possession is duly taken, property still remains private property notwithstanding determination that such property is land in excess of ceiling limit under Act. Persons in possession of such property, whatever be nature of their possession-whether they are encroachers or persons such as respondent no.1 Society cannot be evicted by force. Appeals disposed of.

(B) Land & Property - Human Rights - Protection of Human Rights Act, 1993, s. 12 - Eviction of encroachers - Rehabilitation order passed by Human Rights Commission - Jurisdiction - Whether State Human Rights Commission has any jurisdiction to pass impugned order in favor of hut dwellers.

Held, Human Rights Commission, would not be competent forum for examination of issues mentioned in present case. Both respondent no.1 Society and encroachers wrongly invoked jurisdiction of Human Rights Commission instead of pursuing appropriate remedies available to them in law, and Human Rights Commission was too willing to exercise authority without any jurisdiction. Hence, SC held that Human Rights Commission does not have any jurisdiction to deal with disputed questions of title and possession of property. Further, there is nothing in s. 12 of Act which authorizes Human Rights Commission to adjudicate upon disputes of title and possession of property. Further, there is neither any examination nor any determination by any competent body of rights of respondent no.1 Society and its members on one hand, and encroachers on other hand and even rights and authority of State over property in dispute. Hence, order passed by Human Right Commission and lower Court is set aside leaving it open to parties to seek their remedies before appropriate forum. Appeals disposed of.

Ratio - Human Rights Commission has no authority to adjudicate upon disputes of title and possession of property.
