Manager (Factory) Maharashtra State Cooperative Marketing Federation Limited and another v Suresh S/o Dadarao Gadge

Supreme Court of India

17 December 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Anil R. Dave, R. Banumathi

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 936; (2015) 4 SCC 542; 2015(4) ALL MR 922; 2015 (145) FLR 725; 2015(1) SCALE 104; 2014 (8) SLR 495

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Termination from service - Reinstatement - Irregular appointment - Respondent was appointed as peon on daily wage basis on 1-7-1994 and was discontinued from service from 4-3-1996, without making any payment of retrenchment compensation - Respondent challenged his termination by approaching Labour Court - Labour Court by its Award directed that respondent should be reinstated in service with continuity of service from 4-3-1996, but without back wages - Appellant against said Award filed petition before HC which confirmed order of Labour Court and dismissed petition - Hence instant appeal.

Held, it is admitted fact that respondent had not been engaged to work by following normal practice and therefore he was engaged by way of 'back door entry'. In view of Court that respondent ought not to have been reinstated in service as he was not in regular service. In fact, no other person junior to respondent had been continued at 'P' unit of appellant, which had been closed down. In fact, there was no work at 'P' unit, as said unit had been closed down, respondent, who was working on daily wage basis, was not continued on daily wage basis, but it is admitted fact that he was not given retrenchment compensation. In view of said circumstances, Court opines that, it would be just and appropriate not to reinstate respondent, especially, in view of fact that respondent had hardly worked for period of about year and a half on daily wage basis, his appointment was irregular and 'P' unit of appellant, where respondent was employed, has now been closed down. In view of facts that it would be just and proper to award sum of Rs. 2 lakhs by way of compensation to respondent, it is pertinent to note that he did not lead any evidence or file any affidavit before Labour Court stating that he was unemployed during period of litigation. Order passed by HC is set aside and appeal is allowed to above extent. Appeal allowed.
