V. Mekala v M. Malathi and another

Supreme Court of India

25 April 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, Gyan Sudha Misra

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 318; (2014) 11 SCC 178; 2014 (3) ACC 549; 2014 ACJ 1441; 2014 (5) ALD(SC) 42; 2014 (3) AWC 3242; JT 2014 (6) SC 212; 2015 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 268; 2014 (2) RCR(Civil) 880; 2014(5) SCALE 772; [2014] 5 S.C.R. 774

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance

Keywords: Entitlement of

Summary: Motor Vehicles Act, 1988-Sections 166 and 168-Compensation-Injury case-Enhancement-Appellant (injured) at time of accident was 16 years old brilliant student, studying in XIth standard-High Court enhanced compensation from Rs. 6,46,000 to Rs.18,22,000 with interest @ 7.5% p.a.-Because of accident caused by rash and negligent act of driver of offending vehicle of owner-respondent appellant deprived of her potential income to eke out comfortable livelihood as she became permanently disabled-This legal and factual aspect not taken into consideration both by Tribunal and High Court-Appellant held entitled to total amount of Rs. 30,93,000 as compensation with interest @ 9% p.a. based on principle laid down in Municipal Corporation of Delhi v. Uphaar Tragedy Victims Association, (2011) 14 SCC 481 : 2012 (1) ACCD 409 : 2012 (2) AWC 1731 (SC)
