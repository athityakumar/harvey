Sree Balaji Nagar Residential Association v State of Tamil Nadu and others

Supreme Court of India

10 September 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Shiva Kirti Singh, Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 607; (2015) 3 SCC 353; 2014(6) ALL MR 414; 2014 (106) ALR 685; 2014 (4) CLT(SC) 42; 2014 (5) CTC 857; JT 2014 (14) SC 375; 2015 (5) MahLJ 20; 2014 (8) MLJ 621; 2015 (3) MPLJ 497; 2015 (1) RCR(Civil) 263; 2014 (125) RD 723; 2014(10) SCALE 388; [2014] 7 S.C.R. 799

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Scheme for development, Proposed canal

Summary: Land & Property - Right to Fair Compensation and Transparency in Land Acquisition, Rehabilitation and Resettlement Act, 2013, ss. 24, 24(2) - Scheme for development - Proposed canal - Legality - Respondent/Govt. of Tamil Nadu passed order containing scheme for development of proposed canal - Appellants/Association of Residents of particular locality filed writ petition - Writ petitions were dismissed - Hence instant appeals -

Held, There is nothing in language of proviso to restrict meaning of the words used u/s. 24(2) of the mandating that proceedings shall be deemed to have lapsed if award is five years or more than five years' old but physical possession of land has not been taken over or compensation has not been paid. It is amply clear that though there is lack of clarity on issue whether compensation has been paid for majority of land holdings under acquisition or not, there is no dispute that physical possession of lands belonging to appellants under consideration in these appeals has not been taken by State or any other authority on its behalf and more than five years have elapsed since making of award dtd. 30-11-2006 and 1-1-2014 when the Act came into force Conditions mentioned u/s. 24(2) of the Act are satisfied for allowing plea of appellants that land acquisition proceedings must be deemed to have lapsed in terms of s. 24(2) of the Act. Appeals are disposed of accordingly. It goes without saying that Govt. of Tamil Nadu shall be free, if it so chooses to initiate proceedings of such land acquisition afresh in accordance with the provisions of the Act. Appeals disposed of.

Ratio: S. 24(2) of the Act does not exclude any period during which the land acquisition proceeding might have remained stayed on account of stay or injunction granted by any court.
