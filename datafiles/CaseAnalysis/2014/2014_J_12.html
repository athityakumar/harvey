Jagdamba Prasad (Dead) Through Lrs. and others v Kripa Shankar (Dead) Through Lrs. and others

Supreme Court of India

04 April 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, Gyan Sudha Misra

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 221; (2014) 5 SCC 707; 2014 (6) ADJ 366; 2014 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1445; 2014 (124) RD 1; 2014(4) SCALE 545

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Keywords: Ownership and title dispute, Secondary evidence at revision stage

Summary: Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Uttar Pradesh Consolidation of Holdings Act, 1953, s. 48 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 115 - Ownership and title dispute - Secondary evidence at revision stage - Legality - Appellants filed objections before the Consolidation Officer (CO) for deletion of name of one 'B' (father of respondents), which was alleged to have fictitiously mentioned in revenue records pertaining to land in question - Objections were filed by appellants whose father was 1/3rd share holder of the land which was recorded in the name of 'B' and other shareholders - However, CO accepted the objections of appellants and deleted the name of 'B' from the revenue records by declaring that entry of his name in the records was forged since respondent nos. 1 and 2 failed to produce the alleged 'Will' executed by 'B' in their favour - Aggrieved respondent nos. 1 and 2 filed an appeal before Asst. Settlement Officer (ASO), which was dismissed - Respondents filed revision before Revisional Authority, which was allowed reversing the findings of CO, and upheld the claim of respondents that 'B' had purchased the share of appellants' father in an auction sale - Aggrieved appellants filed a petition before HC and by impugned order Single Judge dismissed petition holding that appellants failed to prove ownership and title over the land on expunction of name of 'B' from revenue records - Aggrieved appellants filed instant appeal - Whether Revisional Authority exceeded its jurisdiction u/s. 48 of the Act in entertaining additional document at revision stage and whether HC was justified in concurring with findings of Revisional Authority -

Held, power of Revisional Authority u/s. 48 of the Act only extend to ascertaining whether the Subordinate Courts have exceeded their jurisdiction in coming to the conclusion - Thus, if Original and Appellate Authorities were within their jurisdiction, Revisional Authority could not exceed its jurisdiction to come to a contrary conclusion by admitting new facts either in the form of documents or otherwise, to come to the conclusion - Thus, Revisional Authority exceeded its jurisdiction u/s. 48 of the Act by admitting documents at revision stage and altering the decision of Subordinate Courts - HC erred in concurring with the findings of Revisional Authority by failing to observe that the Revisional Authority had exceeded its jurisdiction conferred upon it under the Act - HC further erred by recording its reason by interpreting the facts of the case - Further HC erred in concurring with decision of the Revisional Authority on factual grounds which was beyond the jurisdiction of it - Based on factual and legal material evidence produced on record, decision of Appellate Authority rendered by ASO was upheld and orders of both Revisional Authority and HC were set aside - Appeal allowed.
