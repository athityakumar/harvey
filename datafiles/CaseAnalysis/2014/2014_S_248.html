Dhan Raj and another v State of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

09 May 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Pinaki Chandra Ghose, Chandramauli Kumar Prasad

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 356; (2014) 6 SCC 745; 2014 ALL MR (Cri) 2602; 2014 CRLJ 2778; JT 2014 (13) SC 347; 2014 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 2540; 2014 (3) RCR(Criminal) 888; 2014(6) SCALE 620; [2014] 7 S.C.R. 476

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Murder

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 34, 341, 392, 395, 397, 411 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - A case was registered against accused persons/appellants u/ss. 302, 341, 392, 395, 397, 411 r/w s. 34 of IPC - Trial Court passed conviction order - Aggrieved by order passed by Trial Court appellant filed appeal before HC - HC upheld conviction of appellant - Hence instant appeals - Whether impugned order passed by HC was justified -

Held, after analyzing facts, it appeared that there was gap between circumstances tried to be relied upon to hold appellants as guilty - SC found many loopholes in case of prosecution and grounds on which HC had convicted appellants - Court had to examine entire evidence in its entirety especially in case of circumstantial evidence and ensure that only inference drawn from evidence was guilty of appellant - If more than one inference could be drawn then accused must had benefit of doubt as it was not Court's job to assume and only when guilt beyond reasonable doubt was proved then it was fair to record conviction - Each circumstance must be proved beyond reasonable doubt by independent evidence, and circumstances so proved must form complete chain without giving any chance of surmise or conjecture and must also be consistent with guilt of appellant - None of circumstances relied upon by prosecution and accepted by HC could be said to be probability of appellants' guilt or involvement in commission of crime - Order passed by HC was set aside - Appeals allowed.
