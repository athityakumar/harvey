Chairman cum Managing Director Indian Oil Corporation Limited and another v Sunita Kumari and another

Supreme Court of India

18 September 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Madan B. Lokur, C. Nagappan

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 631; AIR 2015 SC 254; 2015 (108) ALR 216; 2015 (1) AWC 248; JT 2014 (12) SC 175; 2014(10) SCALE 649; [2014] 9 S.C.R. 559

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Selection process, Mass cancellation of dealership

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Practice & Procedure - Mass cancellation of dealership - Selection process - Legality - Appellant (Corporation) appointed dealers for superior kerosene oil (SKO) and light diesel oil (LDO) - However, SC held that allotment made to 'N', (who stood first in select panel of candidates) was not on merits but for extraneous considerations and allotment made in her favour stood cancelled - Respondent no. 1 (next in panel of selected candidates) filed a writ petition for SKO-LDO dealership - Single Judge held respondent no. 1 was entitled to be treated as first empanelled candidate upon the cancellation of dealership in favour of 'N' and appeal against which was dismissed by DB - Hence, instant appeal - Whether on cancellation of allotment of a dealership in favour of first ranked or first empanelled candidate, there was an automatic allotment in favour of second ranked or second empanelled candidate, subject to fulfillment of the conditions of allotment -

Held, if the allotment is tainted due to political connections or patronage or other extraneous considerations, the entire selection process is vitiated and, therefore the second ranked or second empanelled candidate is not entitled to an automatic allotment of a dealership or distributorship in his or her favour. Impugned order of HC was set aside. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - When the allotment of dealership or distributorship in favour of first empanelled candidate is cancelled as a result of the Report of the Committee appointed in Onkar Lal Bajaj etc. etc. v. Union of India and another etc. 2002 Indlaw SC 1577, which Report has been accepted by SC, the selection process itself is vitiated.
