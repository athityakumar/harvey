Assam Sanmilita Mahasangha and others v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

17 December 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

R.F. Nariman, Ranjan Gogoi

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 867; (2015) 3 SCC 1; AIR 2015 SC 783; JT 2014 (14) SC 11; 2015 (1) MLJ 219; 2014(14) SCALE 101

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Foreigners

Keywords: Citizenship, Foreigner, Migration, Government of India Act, 1935, Citizen, Foreigners Act, 1946, Tribunals, Citizenship Act, 1955, Bombay Sales Tax Act, 1959, Illegal Migrants (Determination By Tribunals) Act, 1983, Foreigners (Tribunals) Order, 1964, Constitution (Forty-Fourth Amendment) Act, 1978, National, People, Foreigners (Tribunals for Assam) Order, 2006, Immigrants (Expulsion From Assam) Act, 1950

Summary: (A) Constitution - Foreigner - Citizenship Act, 1955, ss. 6A (3) to (5) - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 21, 29 - Illegal migration - Writ petition - Delay - Petitioner filed instant writ petition challenging s. 6A of the Act as discriminatory, arbitrary and illegal and consequently ultra-vires Constitution - Whether the petition is barred by delay and laches.

Held, petitioners raised a plea that the sovereignty and integrity of India is itself at stake as a massive influx of illegal migrants from Bangladesh has affected this core Constitutional value. As a result of this huge influx, periodic clashes have been taking place between the citizens of India and these migrants resulting into loss of life and property, sounding in a violation of arts. 21 and 29 of the Constitution of the Assamese people as a whole. Not only is there an assault on the life of the citizenry of the State of Assam but there is an assault on their way of life as well. The culture of an entire people is being eroded in such a way that they will ultimately be swamped by persons who have no right to continue to live in this country. Further, petitioners have also raised a fervent plea that art. 14 also continues to be violated as ss. 6A (3) to (5) are not time bound but are ongoing. Considering the plea raised specifically with regard to pleas u/arts. 21 and 29, of a whole class of people, namely, the tribal and non-tribal citizens of Assam and given the fact that agitations on this core are ongoing, petitions of this kind cannot be dismissed at the threshold on the ground of delay/laches. Order accordingly.

(B) Constitution - Foreigner - Citizenship Act, 1955, s. 6A - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 145 (3) - S. 6A of the Act - Constitutionality - Whether s. 6A of the Act is ultra vires of the Constitution.

Held, most of the questions raised in the petitions are substantial questions as to the interpretation of the Constitution which have to be decided by a minimum of 5 Judges u/art. 145(3) of the Constitution. Hence, matters be placed before the Chief Justice for constitution of an appropriate bench to answer the questions on merit. However, s. 6A of the Act is deemed to be valid until the larger Bench decides these matters. Order accordingly.

(C) Constitution - Foreigner - Citizenship Act, 1955, s. 6A - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 145 (3) - Illegal migration - Direction by SC - Whether the instant case warrants issuance of any direction.

Held, Assam portion of the border with Bangladesh is 267 Kms. Out of which 44 Kms. are riverine. The entire border between India and Bangladesh is roughly 4000 Kms. The White Paper issued by the State Govt. shows that large portions of the border with Assam are yet to be fenced with double coil wire fencing, making the border an easy place to cross. Further, most parts of the border with West Bengal and other North-Eastern States are also porous and very easy to cross. Hence, by overall consideration of the immediate dimensions of the issues and the potential that the same have for the future directions issued u/art. 142 of the Constitution. The Union will take all effective steps to complete the fencing (double coiled wire fencing) in such parts/portions of the Indo-Bangla border (including the State of Assam) where presently the fencing is yet to be completed. The Gauhati HC is requested to expedite and to finalise the process of selection of the Chairperson and Members of the Foreigners Tribunals, if required in phases, depending on the availability of officers opting to serve in the Tribunals. Direction issued to the UOI to enter into necessary discussions with the Government of Bangladesh to streamline the procedure of deportation. Order accordingly.

Ratio - When it comes to violations of the fundamental right to life and personal liberty, delay or laches by itself without more would not be sufficient to shut the doors of the court on any petitioner.
