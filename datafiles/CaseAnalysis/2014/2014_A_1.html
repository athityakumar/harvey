State Through Inspector of Police v A. Arun Kumar and another

Supreme Court of India

17 December 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Uday Umesh Lalit, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 865; (2015) 2 SCC 417; 2015 CRLJ 914; 2015 (320) E.L.T. 184; 2015 (1) MLJ(Crl) 42; 2015 (1) RCR(Criminal) 295; 2014(14) SCALE 164; [2014] 11 S.C.R. 319

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 420, 467, 468, 471, 477A - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, s. 13 (2) r/w s. 13(1)(d) - Customs Act, 1962, s. 32 - Corruption - Quashing of charge-sheet - Respondent No. 1 Appraiser of Customs and respondent No. 2 Inspector of Customs facilitated other co-accused to create forged shipping bills and thereby claimed inflated duty draw back - Respondents were charge-sheeted u/ss. 420, 467, 468, 471, 477-A IPC and s. 13 (2) r/w s. 13(1)(d) of 1988 Act and s. 32 of 1962 Act - Respondents filed discharge application - Special Court dismissed discharge application - However, on revision, HC set aside charge-sheet against respondents - Hence, instant appeal by State.

Held, digit '2' stands added to the quantity and digit '5' stands added to the value mentioned in the original shipping bill. In the process, the value was inflated which would in turn increase the amount of duty drawback multifold. The documents placed on record which are part of the charge-sheet, raise grave suspicion against the respondents. The material on record discloses grave suspicion against the respondents and the Special Court was right in framing charges against the respondents. Further, the HC was not justified in stating that s. 15 of the 1988 Act could not be invoked in the present case. Since the duty draw back was not actually availed, the prosecution had rightly alleged that there was an attempt to commit offence under the relevant clauses of s. 13(1) of the 1988 Act. The assessment of the HC in that behalf is not correct. Hence, judgment and order passed by the HC was set aside and the order of the Special Court was restored. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - It is not required that in order to charge an accused u/s. 15 of the 1988 Act he must also be charged either u/s. 13(1)(c) of 13(1)(d) of the 1988 Act.
