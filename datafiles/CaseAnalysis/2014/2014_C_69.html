Union of India v Concrete Products and Construction Company Etc.

Supreme Court of India

03 March 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Surinder Singh Nijjar, Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 144; (2014) 4 SCC 416; AIR 2014 SC 1914; 2014 (3) ALD(SC) 174; 2014 (104) ALR 280; 2014 (3) AWC 2527; 2014 (2) CLT(SC) 94; 2014 (4) CompLJ 244; JT 2014 (5) SC 164; 2014 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1619; 2014 (5) MahLJ 87; 2014 (3) MPLJ 481; 2014 (4) RCR(Civil) 582; [2014] 3 S.C.R. 535

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Keywords: Excess payment

Summary: Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996-Section 31 (7)-Award of interest to contractors-Sole Arbitrator in awarding interest to contractors failed to take into account provisions contained in Clauses 2401 and 2403 of Indian Railways Standard Conditions of Contract-Arbitrator could not have awarded any interest from date when recovery was made till award was made-However, interest payable from date when award was made till money was deposited in High Court and thereafter converted to fixed deposit receipts-Upon amount being deposited in High Court, no further interest could be paid to respondents-Award of interest from date when amount deposited in Court-Wholly unwarranted-High Court and Arbitrator committed error of jurisdiction in this respect-In view of aforesaid, respondents (Contractors not entitled to any interest on amount which was recovered by appellant (railway administration) till date of award and thereafter till date when amount awarded was deposited in High Court.
