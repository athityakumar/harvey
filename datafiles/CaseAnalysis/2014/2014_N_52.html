Narinder Singh v New India Assurance Company Limited and others

Supreme Court of India

04 September 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

M.Y. Eqbal, Pinaki Chandra Ghose

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 582; (2014) 9 SCC 324; 2014 (3) ACC 890; 2014 ACJ 2421; AIR 2014 SC 3761; 2014 (6) ALD(SC) 149; 2014 (107) ALR 726; 2014 (5) AWC 5122; 2014 (5) Bom.C.R. 740; 2014 (4) CGLJ 190; 2014 (4) CompLJ 356; 2014 (4) CPJ(Sc) 11; 2014 (3) KLT 1074; 2014 (176) PLR 824; 2014 (4) RCR(Civil) 272; 2015 (1) RLW 517; 2014(10) SCALE 158; [2014] 9 S.C.R. 551; 2014 (4) WLN(SC) 80

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection; Insurance

Summary: Consumer Protection - Insurance -Consumer Protection Act, 1986 - Motor Vehicle Act, 1988, s. 1988 - Non-registration - Entitlement to damages - Petitioner-complainant had purchased a vehicle and got it insured with respondent no.1 for the period 12-12-2005 to 11-12-2006 - Vehicle was temporarily registered for one month period, which expired on 11-1-2006 - However, on 2-2-2006, the vehicle met with an accident and got damaged - Insurance claim was repudiated on the ground that the person, who was driving the vehicle at the time of the accident, did not possess a valid and effective driving licence and also the vehicle had not been registered after the expiry of the temporary registration - Consumer complaint was allowed - Appeals filed thereagainst was also allowed - Revision filed before National Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission (National Commission)was dismissed - Hence, instant appeals - Whether the National Commission was correct in law in holding that the appellant was not entitled to claim compensation for damages in respect of the vehicle when admittedly the vehicle was being driven on the date of accident without any valid registration -

Held, no person shall drive the motor vehicle in any public place without any valid registration granted by the registering authority in accordance with the provisions of the 1986 Act. Therefore, using a vehicle on the public road without any registration is not only an offence punishable u/s. 192 of the 1988 Act but also a fundamental breach of the terms and conditions of policy contract. There was no infirmity in the order passed by the State Commission and the National Commission. Appeal dismissed.

Ratio - Using a vehicle on the public road without any registration is not only an offence punishable u/s. 192 of the Motor Vehicle Act, 1988 but also a fundamental breach of the terms and conditions of policy contract.
