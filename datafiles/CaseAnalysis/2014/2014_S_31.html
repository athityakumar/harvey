Sanjay Kumar Shukla v Bharat Petroleum Corporation Limited and others

Supreme Court of India

07 February 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjan Gogoi, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 74; (2014) 3 SCC 493; AIR 2014 SC 3778; 2014 (2) AWC 2097; JT 2014 (3) SC 230; 2014(2) SCALE 127; [2014] 1 S.C.R. 959; 2014 (3) WLN(SC) 78

Case Digest

Subject: Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Dealership, Denied

Summary: Dealership-Of Petrol/Diesel Retail Outlet-Grant of-Selection-On basis of applications received for grant of dealership in question, selection was held wherein appellant was placed at Sl. No. 1 whereas respondent No. 7 was placed at Sl. No. 2 of merit list-Dealership was to be offered to most meritorious candidate after necessary field verification-Technical Evaluation Committee in its report had found that land offered by appellant suitable for development of retail outlet-Aggrieved by rejection of his claim respondent No. 7 moved High Court-Single Judge of High Court directed that selection process to be redone on the ground that requisite N.O.C. from D.M. in respect of land offered by appellant not having been granted, respondent-Corporation cannot be expected to wait indefinitely-Division Bench of High Court substantially agreed with findings recorded by single Judge and held that once appellant was found to be disentitled, dealership should have been awarded to respondent No. 7, he being, at Sl. No. 2 of merit list-Litigation initiated by respondent No. 7 does not constitute very bona fide exercise-Entire litigation appears to have been driven by desire to deny fruits of selection in which appellant found to be most eligible candidate-In totality of facts of present case, it would be just and proper to direct-respondent-Corporation, if it is of view that operation of retail outlet is still justified by exigencies, to award same to appellant by completing requisite formalities in accordance with procedure laid down by respondent-Corporation itself-Impugned orders of single Judge and Division Bench of High Court-Set aside.
