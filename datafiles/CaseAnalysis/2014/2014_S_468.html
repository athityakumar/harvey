Hema Mishra v State of Uttar Pradesh and others

Supreme Court of India

16 January 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan, A.K. Sikri

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 25; (2014) 4 SCC 453; AIR 2014 SC 1066; 2014 ALL MR (Cri) 1503; 2014 CRLJ 1107; JT 2014 (2) SC 26; 2014 (1) MLJ(Crl) 450; 2014 (1) RCR(Criminal) 666; 2014(1) SCALE 342; [2014] 1 S.C.R. 465

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure (Uttar Pradesh Amendment) Act, 1976, Code of Criminal Procedure (Uttar Pradesh Amendment) Bill, 2010

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, ss. 438, 41A, 41(1)(b) - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 419, 420 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Uttar Pradesh state - Anticipatory bail - Quashing of FIR - Sustainability - Complaint was registered against appellant/petitioner u/ss. 419 and 420 IPC for fraud and forgery - Appellant had filed an application u/art. 226 of Constitution and sought direction for quashing the FIR lodged u/ss. 419 and 420 IPC - Further sought for directing OP No. 2 and 3 to defer the arrest of appellant - Also sought direction to OP No. 2 for compliance of the provision of ss. 41(1)(b) and 41A CrPC in the investigation of impugned FIR - HC dismissed the petition - Appellant, contended that she was falsely implicated in the case and HC had failed to exercise its certiorari jurisdiction u/art. 226 of the Constitution in not quashing the FIR and in refusing to grant anticipatory bail to appellant - Held, FIR was lodged for offences, u/ss. 419 and 420 IPC which carry a sentence of maximum of 3 years and 7 years respectively with or without fine - Benefit of s. 41(a) CrPC should be available in a given case, which provides that an investigating officer shall not arrest the accused of such offences in a routine manner and the arrest be made, only after following the restrictions imposed u/s. 41(b) CrPC - Amended provisions made it compulsory for the police to record the reasons for making arrest as well as for not making an arrest in respect of a cognizable offence for which the maximum sentence upto 7 years - S. 41A CrPC provision made it compulsory for the police to issue a notice in all such cases where arrest was not required to be made s. 41(1)(b) CrPC - But, all the same, unwillingness of a person who was not arrested to identify himself and to whom a notice was issued u/s. 41A, could be a ground for his arrest - Legislation has laid down various parameters, warranting arrest of a person, which itself was a check on arbitrary or unwarranted arrest and the right to personal liberty guaranteed u/art. 21 of Constitution - There was unanimity in the view that in spite of the fact that s. 438 CrPC was specifically omitted and made inapplicable in the State of UP, still a party aggrieved could invoke the jurisdiction of the HC u/art. 226 of the Constitution, being extraordinary jurisdiction and the vastness of the powers naturally impose considerable responsibility in its application - All the same, the High Court has got the power and sometimes duty in appropriate cases to grant reliefs, though it was not possible to pin-point what were the appropriate cases, which have to be left to the wisdom of the Court exercising powers u/art. 226 of Constitution - Language of art. 226 of Constitution does not permit such an action and once the Court finds no merits in the challenge, writ petition would have to be dismissed and the question of granting further relief after dismissal of the writ, does not arise - Consequently, once a writ was dismissed, all the interim reliefs granted would also go - Appeal dismissed.
