I. P. Holding Asia Singapore Private Limited and another v Securities and Exchange Board of India

Supreme Court of India

20 August 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Madan B. Lokur, Kurian Joseph

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 654; (2015) 2 SCC 68; AIR 2015 SC 274; JT 2014 (10) SC 253; 2014(9) SCALE 399; [2014] 8 S.C.R. 399

Case Digest

Subject: Securities Exchange Board of India

Keywords: Shares, Non-compete fee

Summary: Securities Exchange Board of India - Companies Act, 1956 - Securities and Exchange Board of India (Substantial Acquisition of Shares and Takeovers) Regulations, 2011 - Non-compete fee - Shares - Legality - SEBI received information and accordingly issued letter to appellants - Feeling aggrieved by communication sent by SEBI appellants filed appeal before Tribunal the same was dismissed - Hence instant appeal - Whether appellants are liable to pay a non-compete fee to public shareholders of target company as is being to be paid to outgoing promoters of target company which is being taken over by appellants.

Held, absurdity resulting in splitting-up non-compete agreement can be better appreciated from hypothetical example. What if Tribunal had partially agreed with appellants and held that non-compete agreement was valid in respect of say ten or twelve of promoter entities instead of five. This could happen if genuineness of non-compete agreement is examined in relation to each promoter entity, as has been done by SEBI. Does it not, therefore, mean that non-compete agreement has to be split in twenty ways to decide whether it is genuine or sham in respect of five or ten or twelve of promoter entities. Can this be said to be a reasonable construction of non-compete agreement. SC is afraid that this surely cannot be correct way of reading non-compete agreement and that is why we are of the view that Tribunal committed a fundamental flaw in holding only part of non-compete agreement as a sham. Tribunal should have either held entire non-compete agreement as sham or it ought to have held entire non-compete agreement as genuine agreement. The question of half-way house simply does not arise. Non-individual entities did not have business objectives of manufacturing, sale and trading of pulp and paper in their main object clause. This is only stated to be rejected since memorandum of association of corporate entity can always be altered in accordance with procedure under 1956 Act. Two events that have occurred since non-compete agreement was entered. Firstly, non-compete period of three years has expired, in sense rendering this exercise academic. Secondly, the Takeover Code has been repealed and substituted by 2011 Regulations. The new Takeover Code does away with concept of separate non-compete fee, amount being included in offer price in terms of Regulation 8 thereof $10. Directions and orders passed by SEBI and Tribunal are set aside. Appeal allowed.
