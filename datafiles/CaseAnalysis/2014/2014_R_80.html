Sri Sidhharth Viyas and others v Ravi Nath Misra and others

Supreme Court of India

25 November 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Adarsh Kumar Goel, R. Banumathi, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 795; (2015) 2 SCC 701; 2015 (7) ADJ 410; AIR 2015 SC 434; 2015 (112) ALR 728; 2015 (1) AWC 67; JT 2014 (13) SC 241; 2015 (1) RCR(Rent) 100; 2015 (1) WLN(SC) 120

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - Uttar Pradesh Urban Buildings (Regulation of Letting, Rent and Eviction) Act, 1972, s. 12 (3) - Acquisition of alternative accommodation - Right of tenancy - Accommodation in question was let out on 1981 and was first assessed on 1-10-1983 - Tenant purchased another residential building - Rent Controller declared suit property to be vacant u/s. 12 (3) of the Act and ordered to release in favour of landlord u/s. 16 of the Act - However, on appeal, HC held that no vacancy can be declared if the tenant or his family member purchased the house before the Act became applicable - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, mere use of present tense in s. 12 (3) is not intended to limit the applicability of the provision to acquisition of accommodation by the tenant after the Act becomes applicable. In the context, the provision also covers the situation where the tenant has acquired alternative accommodation before the applicability of the Act. This view is further supported by the language of the proviso. The proviso clearly shows that the provision in question is not intended to be limited to a situation where alternative accommodation is acquired after the Act commences or becomes operative. The provision also covers a situation where the alternative accommodation is acquired prior to that. The scope of proviso is narrower than the main provision. The view taken by HC that acquisition of alternative accommodation by the tenant, prior to enforcement of the Act, is not covered by s. 12(3) of the Act is not correct in law. The impugned order passed by HC was set aside and the order passed by the Rent Controller was restored. Mangi Lal vs. Additional District Judge & others, partly overruled. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - A tenant who has already acquired alternative accommodation is not intended to be protected by the Act
