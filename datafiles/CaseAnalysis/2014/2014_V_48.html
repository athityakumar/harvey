State of Haryana and others v Vinod Oil and General Mills and another

Supreme Court of India

23 September 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

R. Banumathi, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 906; 2015(6) ALL MR 940; 2014 (6) AWC 6466; 2014 (4) CalLT(SC) 89; JT 2014 (11) SC 341; 2014(11) SCALE 507

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: (1) Land Acquisition Act, 1894-Section 4-Land acquisition-Validity of-Land in present case released from acquisition initiated earlier-Subsequently another notification issued for acquisition of the land in question-State Government had permitted without any conditions the change of land use for developing the area as an industry-Lapse of 26 years since then-Both acquisitions intended for development of residential and commercial purposes-Held, that permission for change of land use for industry can have no relevance while considering the validity of acquisition-Public interest overrides the individual interest-Requirement of land for residential and commercial purposes and development of the Sector involves in it an element of general interest of the community and whatever furthers the general interest must be regarded as a public purpose as opposed to the particular interest of individuals-Therefore, challenge to acquisition on this ground is rejected.

(2) Land Acquisition Act, 1894-Section 4-Notification issued for re-acquisition of land released in earlier acquisition proceedings-One small strip land contiguous to factory of land owner appearing in corner of development plan and adjustable in Planning Scheme of the Sector-Held, acquisition in respect of this strip of land is rightly quashed-However, another strip of land situated in the midst of the sector and causing hindrance for the wholesome development of the sector-This strip cannot be adjusted in the development of the sector-Therefore, acquisition of this strip of land is upheld.

(3) Right to Fair Compensation and Transparency in Land Acquisition, Rehabilitation, Re-settlement Act, 2013-Section 24 (2)-Land Acquisition Act, 1894-Section 4-Non-payment of compensation and failure to take possession of land acquired under the Act, 1894, alleged-These aspects required to be considered-Therefore, matter is remitted back to High Court after setting aside the order of High Court.
