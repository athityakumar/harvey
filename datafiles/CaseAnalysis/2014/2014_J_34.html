Mohammad Hafizullah and others v Javed Akhtar and others

Supreme Court of India

02 July 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

R.K. Agrawal

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 414; (2015) 5 SCC 650; 2014 (105) ALR 659; 2014 (4) AWC 4221; 2015 (3) CalLT(SC) 82; 2014 (3) CLT(SC) 60; JT 2014 (7) SC 535; 2014 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 2933; 2014 (125) RD 97; 2014(8) SCALE 292; [2014] 8 S.C.R. 144

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Suit for specific performance - Validity - Respondent filed suit for specific performance and held that fraud was committed by deceased X and in pursuance of said fraud share of Y had been transferred to X - Though permission was granted to Y to sell her share to respondents or their nominee, share of Y was not sold to them or their nominee and they had never appointed X to act as their nominee and they were not bound by order whereby Y was directed to sell her share to X as they were not given any notice when orders were passed by HC - Appellants heirs of X claimed that they were the rightful owners of one-fourth share belonged to Y as X had purchased her share in her individual capacity and not as nominee of respondent - Trial Court as well as Appellate Court concluded that fraud had been committed and commission of fraud being matter of fact - Appeal before HC which was dismissed - Hence instant appeal - Whether the order recorded by HC was just and proper -

Held, it was not possible to divide property by metes and bounds - Entire problem arose because property was not divisible by metes and bounds and therefore, receiver had to be appointed - There was no dispute with regard to fact that three-fourth share of property in question had been purchased by respondents in pursuance of permission granted by HC - If property was not divisible, one should very well believe that owner of three-fourth share of indivisible property would be ready and willing to purchase remaining one-fourth share of said property and normally no outsider would ever think of purchasing one-fourth share of indivisible part of residential house - These factors clearly denote that there should be some understanding among Y and respondents in relation to purchase of share of Y - There was nothing on record to show that respondents had appointed X as their nominee - There was nothing to show that any notice was issued to respondents before order was passed - By virtue of said order Y had been directed to execute sale deed in favour of X - Further, no notice was issued to respondents when order was modified - It was important to note that if order had been passed in favour of respondents, there was no reason for HC not to hear these two persons while passing fresh order, whereby buyers had been changed from respondents to X - Thus, findings with regard to fraud were findings of fact - After appreciation of entire evidence, Trial Court as well as Appellate Court concluded that fraud was committed, whereby one-fourth share of Y had been sold in favour of X - Hence, there was no substance in instant appeal and orders passed by Courts below were just and proper - Appeal dismissed.
