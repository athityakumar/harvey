State Through CBI, New Delhi v Jitender Kumar Singh

Supreme Court of India

05 February 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan, A.K. Sikri

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 69; (2014) 11 SCC 724; AIR 2014 SC 1169; 2014 CRLJ 1394; 2014 (207) DLT 323; 2014 (2) JCC 1172; JT 2014 (3) SC 181; 2014 (1) KLT 666; 2014 (1) MLJ(Crl) 566; 2014 (1) RCR(Criminal) 908; 2015 (1) RLW 157; 2014(2) SCALE 65; [2014] 2 S.C.R. 621

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Warrant Case

Summary: (A) Criminal - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 3, 3(1), 4, 4(3), 5, 13(1)(d), 13(2) - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120B, 467, 471, 420 - Death of sole public servant - Jurisdiction of Special Judge to continue with trial - PC and non PC offences - Sustainability - Charge-sheet was filed against A-1, who was a public servant, as well as against non-public servants - Special Judge framed the charges against accused persons u/s. 120B r/w ss. 467, 471 and 420 IPC and also u/ss. 13(1)(d) and 13(2) of the Act and substantive offences u/ss. 420, 467 and 471 IPC and also substantive offences u/ss. 13(1)(d) and 13(2) of the Act against the public servants - A-1 died, A-2 filed an application before the Special Judge for dropping the charges in view of the death of A-1, the sole public servant - A-2 filed revision, calling of the case pending before the Special Judge, so as to consider the propriety of not passing any order on the application for dropping the charges, despite the directions issued by the HC - A-2 prayed for setting aside the charges in view of the death of the sole public servant - HC, held that on the death of a public servant, the offences under the Act could not be proceeded with and directed to modify and alter and/or amend the charges in view of the death of A-1 - Hence instant appeal - Whether, on the death of the sole public servant, the Special Judge would cease to have jurisdiction to continue with the trial against the private persons for non-PC offences -

Held, charges framed against the public servants as well as non-public servants after hearing the prosecution and defence, by the special Judge in respect of PC offences as well as non-PC offences - As indicated, u/s. 4(3) of the Act, when trying any case, a Special Judge may also try any offence other than the offence specified in s. 3 of the Act and be charged in the same trial - Special Judge, in instant case, framed charges against the public servant as well as against the non-public servant for offences punishable u/s. 3(1) of Act as well as for the offences punishable u/s. 120B r/w ss. 467, 471 and 420 IPC and, therefore, the existence of jurisdictional fact that was 'trying a case' under the Act was satisfied - Special Judge after framing the charge for PC and non-PC offences posted the case for examination of prosecution witnesses, thereafter the sole public servant died on 2-6-2003 - Before that, the Special Judge, in the instant case, also exercised his powers u/s. 4(3) of the Act and hence could not be divested with the jurisdiction to proceed against the non-public servant, even if the sole public servant dies after framing of the charges - On death, the charge against the public servant alone abates and since the special Judge has already exercised his jurisdiction u/s. 4(3) of the Act, that jurisdiction could not be divested due to the death of the sole public servant - Where a public servant dies at the fag end of the trial, by that time, several witnesses might have been examined and to hold that the entire trial would be vitiated due to death of a sole public servant would defeat the entire object and purpose of the Act, which was enacted for effective combating of corruption and to expedite cases related to corruption and bribery - Purpose of the Act was to make anti-corruption laws more effective in order to expedite the proceedings, provisions for day-to-day trial of cases, transparency with regard to grant of stay and exercise of powers of revision on interlocutory orders was also provided under the Act - Once the power was exercised by the Special Judge u/s. 4(3) of the Act to proceed against non-PC offences along with PC offences, the mere fact that the sole public servant dies after the exercise of powers u/s. 4(3) of the Act would not divest the jurisdiction of the Special Judge or vitiate the proceedings pending before him - Impugned order of HC was set aside - Special Judge was directed to complete the trial of the cases - Appeals allowed.

(B) Criminal - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 3, 3(1), 4, 4(3), 13(1)(d), 13(2) - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120B, 420 - Non-PC offences - Against private persons - Death before framing of charges - Jurisdiction to try non PC offences - Sustainability - Charge-sheet was filed before the Special Judge for the offences u/ss. 120B, 420, IPC r/w ss. 13(2) and 13(1) of the Act - Accused 9 and 10 died even before the charge-sheet was sent to the Special Judge - Charge against the sole public servant under the Act could also not be framed since he died on 18-2-2005 - Special Judge also could not frame any charge against non-public servants - HC took the view that the jurisdiction conferred on the Special Judge was not divested on the death of an accused - HC held that, upon death, the case against that public servant alone abates and the rest of them could be proceeded against by the Special Judge, since the Court, once vested with the jurisdiction, could not be divested of it on the death of a public servant - Order passed by the Special Judge was set aside and was directed to continue with the trial of the case - Hence instant appeal - Whether the Special Judge had jurisdiction u/s. 4(3) of the Act to try non-PC offences against private persons when no charges have been framed against public servants for trying a case for offences u/s. 3(1) of the Act, since they died before framing of charges under the Act or Act -

Held, u/s. 4(3) of the Act, special Judge could try non-PC offences only when 'trying any case' relating to PC offences - In instant case, no PC offence was committed by any of the non-public servants so as to fall u/s. 3(1) of the Act - There was no occasion for the special Judge to try any case relating to offences under the Act against appellant - Trying of any case under the against a public servant or a non-public servant, was a sine-qua-non for exercising powers u/s. 4(3) of the Act - In instant case, since no PC offence was committed by any of the non-public servants and no charges framed against the public servant, while he was alive, the Special Judge had no occasion to try any case against any of them under the Act, since no charge was framed prior to the death of the public servant - Jurisdictional fact, does not exist so far as instant appeal was concerned, so as to exercise jurisdiction by the Special Judge to deal with non-PC offences - SC found no error in the view taken by the Special Judge, CBI, in forwarding the case papers of Special Case in the Court of Chief Metropolitan Magistrate for trying the case in accordance with law - Impugned order passed by the HC was set aside - Appeals allowed.
