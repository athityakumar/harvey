Mohinder Singh and others v State of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

05 August 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

C. Nagappan, T.S. Thakur, Adarsh Kumar Goel

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 502; (2014) 8 SCC 897; 2014 (107) ALR 712; 2014 (5) AWC 5327; 2014 (5) Bom.C.R. 578; 2014 (3) CLT(SC) 240; 2014 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 3051; 2015 (1) MahLJ 39; 2015 (1) MPLJ 50; 2014 (4) RCR(Civil) 360; 2015 (126) RD 450; 2014(9) SCALE 216; [2014] 8 S.C.R. 369; 2014 (3) WLN(SC) 131

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property

Keywords: Entitled to, Compensation, Acquisition of land

Summary: Land & Property - Administrative - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, s. 18 - Acquisition of land - Compensation - Entitled to - State respondents issued Notification intending to acquire acres of land for a public purpose - Land-owners claimants objected over the notification - Collector by his award awarded compensation at different rates per acre to claimants - Claimants filed applications for reference u/s. 18 of the Act before Reference Court - Reference Court awarded uniform compensation at Rs.2,66,400/- per acre - Dissatisfied with award appeals were filed by State and claimants for reduction of compensation and enhancement of compensation - Single Judge partly allowed the appeal filed by State and dismissed appeals of claimants - Claimants filed appeals before DB - DB partly allowed claimants appeals and modified award to extent that claimants were entitled to get compensation at rate of Rs.2,19,696 per acre along with other benefits as awarded by Reference Court - Hence instant appeals by State and claimants - Whether the order recorded by DB was just and proper -

Held, with regard to location and potential of land, Reference Court held that acquired land adjoins abadi of township and it was in its municipal limits and it was in evidence that around this land there exist College, Girls High school, cinema hall, cold storage, rice mills, grain market and private nursing homes and all establishments have sprung up before acquisition and acquired land had great potential value for development of residential commercial and industrial units - Single Judge recorded as agricultural land and held that State had produced no evidence to establish the same and on contrary testimony of PW1 the land lies within municipal limits remained unrebutted - HC held on facts of case and was justified in taking into consideration size of plots which were exhibited for purpose of comparison with size of plot acquired, but SC was unable to uphold cut of 40% which has been imposed by HC since acquired lands were already within developed municipal limits and deduction of 1/4th market value made by Reference Court was appropriate and liable to be restored - Hence, impugned judgment of DB was set aside and award passed by Reference Court was restored - Appeals disposed of.
