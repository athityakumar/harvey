Dr. Malathi Ravi (M.D.) v Dr. B. V. Ravi (M.D.)

Supreme Court of India

30 June 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 528; (2014) 7 SCC 640; AIR 2014 SC 2881; 2014 (5) ALD(SC) 154; 2014(6) ALL MR 400; 2014 (105) ALR 664; 2014 (4) AWC 3992; 2015 (4) CGLJ 487; 2014 (210) DLT 733; JT 2014 (9) SC 63; 2014 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 3096; 2015 (1) MahLJ 107; 2015 (1) MPLJ 53; 2014 (3) RCR(Civil) 621; 2014(8) SCALE 54; [2014] 6 S.C.R. 218

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Keywords: Judicial Separation

Summary: Family & Personal - Dissolution of marriage - Grant of divorce - Legality - Respondent/husband filed petition for dissolution of marriage against appellant/wife - Family Court declined to grant divorce as factum of desertion as requisite in law was not proved - On appeal filed by respondent HC allowed the appeal and granted divorce - Hence instant appeal - Whether under these circumstances the decree for divorce granted by the HC should be interfered with -

Held, despite obtaining decree for restitution of conjugal rights the wife waited till the last day of the expiration of the period as per the decree to join the husband - There might be no legal fallacy, but the attitude gets reflected - Reply also stated that there was expectation of reasonable amount of care and cordiality - That reflect both, a sense of doubt and a hidden threat - That apart, the communications, after the decree for restitution of conjugal rights, indicate the attitude of the wife as if she was playing a game of Chess - Launching of criminal prosecution could be perceived from the spectrum of conduct - Magistrate recorded the judgment of acquittal - Wife had preferred an appeal before the High Court after obtaining leave - After the State Govt. preferred an appeal in the Court of Session, she chose to withdraw the appeal - But she intends, as the pleadings would show, that the case should reach the logical conclusion - Conduct manifestly showed the widening of the rift between the parties - It had only increased the bitterness - In such a situation, the husband was likely to lament in every breath and the vibrancy of life melts to give way to sad story of life - From that kind of attitude and treatment it could be inferred that the husband was treated with mental cruelty and definitely he had faced ignominy being an Associate Professor in a Government Medical College - Sanguine concept of marriage presumably had become illusory and it would not be inapposite to say that the wife has shown anaemic emotional disposition to the husband - Therefore, the decree of divorce granted by the HC deserved to be affirmed singularly on the ground of mental cruelty - SC affirmed the decree for divorce passed by the HC - Appeal disposed of.
