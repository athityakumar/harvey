Maya Devi v Lalta Prasad

Supreme Court of India

19 February 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan, Vikramajit Sen

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 100; (2015) 5 SCC 588; AIR 2014 SC 1356; 2014 (3) ALD(SC) 66; 2014 (3) ALT(SC) 41; 2014 (2) AWC 1865; 2014 (1) CLT(SC) 326; JT 2014 (3) SC 395; 2014 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1085; 2014 (2) RCR(Civil) 193; 2014 (3) RLW 2546; 2014(2) SCALE 536; [2014] 2 S.C.R. 1129; 2014 (3) WLN(SC) 146

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Keywords: Decreed, Suit For Recovery, Execution

Summary: Code of Civil Procedure, 1908-Order XXI, Rule 58-Objection petition under Order XXI, Rule 58-When decree in civil suit sought to be executed-Appellant filed objection petition under Order XXI, Rule 58-Appellant claimed that she became absolute owner of suit property by virtue of registered general power of attorney and that she has been in actual physical possession of suit property-Objection petition contested by decree holder/ respondent stating that appellant/objector had no legal right, title or interest and that execution of general power of attorney and its registration would not confer any ownership right in favour of appellant/objector-Appellant, third party brought into lis by side wind in that her property is sought to be attached with intention of satisfying decree in which she was not directly or intrinsically concerned-Appellant/objector who approached court under Order XXI, Rule 58 is more advantageously or favourably placed inasmuch as she is third party so far as decree is concerned, and her property is not subject-matter of decree-Courts below rejected objections merely because of some sympathy towards decree holder-Objections of appellant deserved to be allowed with disturbing decree, leaving all other remedies open to decree holder/respondent, including proceedings against Estate of Judgment debtor-All question raised by appellant/objector to be comprehensively considered on their merits-Objection under Order XXI, Rule 58 should be meaningfully heard so as to avoid possibility of miscarriage of justice-Impugned order set aside.
