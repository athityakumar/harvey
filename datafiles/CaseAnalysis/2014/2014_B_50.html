Kala Devi and others v Bhagwan Das Chauhan and another

Supreme Court of India

31 October 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, Adarsh Kumar Goel

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 749; (2015) 2 SCC 771; 2014 (4) ACC 645; 2014 ACJ 2875; 2015 (108) ALR 221; 2015 (1) AWC 431; 2014 (4) RCR(Civil) 936; 2014(12) SCALE 513

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance

Keywords: Enhancement of

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Insurance - Minimum Wages Act, 1948 - Motor accident - Compensation - Enhancement of compensation - Appellants (claimants) filed a claiming petition seeking compensation of Rs.12,96,000/- before Tribunal, on account of death of deceased in a motor accident - Tribunal awarded compensation of Rs.4,40,000/- with interest at the rate of 7.5% p.a - Aggrieved appellants for enhancement of compensation filed an appeal, which was partly allowed granting Rs.6,99,800/- with 9% interest p.a. and appeal filed by respondent (Insurance) was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Appellants contended that assessment of loss of dependency based on Rs.3,000/- p.m. as the income of deceased was wrong.

Held, deceased was 25 years of age at the time of death and was a matriculate, working as a driver with a valid license for driving heavy motor vehicles. A driver in the State on an average earns Rs.9,000/- p.m. as per the Act. Therefore, the Courts below have failed to take judicial notice of the same and the fact that the post of a driver is a skilled job. Considering the facts and circumstances of case, gross monthly income of the deceased at Rs.9,000/- p.m., i.e. Rs.1,08,000/- p.a. On deduction of 20% towards income tax, the net income comes to Rs.86,400/- p.a. Further, deducting 1/3rd towards personal expenses and applying the appropriate multiplier of 18, the loss of dependency is calculated at Rs.10,36,800/-. The total compensation payable to the appellants by respondent will be Rs.14,61,800/- with interest at the rate of 9% p.a. from the date of filing of the application till the date of payment. Appeal allowed.
