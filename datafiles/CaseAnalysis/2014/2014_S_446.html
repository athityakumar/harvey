K. Saravanan Karuppasamy and another v State of Tamil Nadu and others

Supreme Court of India

16 September 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

R. Banumathi, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 617; (2014) 10 SCC 406; AIR 2015 SC 214; 2014 (4) CLT(SC) 20; JT 2014 (10) SC 381; 2014(10) SCALE 573; [2014] 7 S.C.R. 1149

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Human Rights; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Directions, Tamilnadu Property (Prevention of Damage and Loss) Act, 1992, Tamil Nadu Police Rules, Violation of human rights, Probe by CBI or SIT

Summary: Human Rights - Administrative - Practice & Procedure - Protection of Human Rights Act, 1993, s. 21(2)(a) - Violation of human rights - Probe by CBI or SIT- Directions - Law college students attacked each other - Criminal cases were registered and few police personnel were suspended - Commission of Enquiry headed by a retired HC Judge filed its report and the same was accepted by State Govt. - Petitioners filed instant petition seeking an independent investigation preferably by CBI or SIT - Petitioner alleged that there was violation of human rights and dereliction of duty on the part of police personnel in preventing the incident and that they were facing considerable harassment at the hands of Police and frivolous cases were registered against the petitioners -

Held, the matter was not proceeded with seriousness with which it ought to have been proceeded with. The matter should have been addressed by the police and the State with great concern and promptitude. Though the matter was not proceeded in the way in which it should have been proceeded with, Court feel that at this distant point of time, it is not necessary to hand over the investigation to CBI or to SIT. The reason being criminal cases have been registered and charge-sheets are also filed and departmental action was also initiated against the police personnel and punishment though may be nominal was imposed on those police personnel. Since charge-sheets in all three cases have already been filed before the VIIth Metropolitan Magistrate Court, one of which is already taken on file. Having regard to the benevolent objects of the Act and the effective mechanism for redressal of grievances of the citizens against human rights violations, the office of Chairperson of State Human Rights Commission (SHRC) cannot be allowed to remain vacant for a long time. It will be in order if the State takes steps to fill up the vacancy of the post of Chairperson, SHRC, in terms of s. 21(2)(a) of the Act by constituting a Search Committee at an early date. Metropolitan Magistrate Court, was directed to immediately take the cases on file relating to the Law College incident and expedite the trial and dispose of the cases expeditiously in accordance with law within a period of 1 year. Petition disposed of.

Ratio - Order to conduct investigation by CBI is not to be passed as a matter of routine merely because the party has leveled allegations against the local police. The extra-ordinary power in handing over investigation to CBI must be exercised cautiously and in exceptional circumstances.
