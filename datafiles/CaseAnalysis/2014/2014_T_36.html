Tulip Star Hotels Limited and another v Special Director of Enforcement

Supreme Court of India

16 January 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla, Surinder Singh Nijjar

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 30; (2014) 5 SCC 162; AIR 2014 SC 1028; 2014 (2) Bom.C.R. 511; 2014 (302) E.L.T. 19; JT 2014 (2) SC 17; 2014(1) SCALE 391; [2014] 1 S.C.R. 434

Case Digest

Subject: Fema

Keywords: Penalty, Violation, Unauthorised transaction of foreign currency

Summary: FEMA - Foreign Exchange Regulation Act, 1973, ss. 6(4), 6(5), 7, 50 r/w s. 49 (3) & (4) - Unauthorised transaction of foreign currency - Violation - Penalty - Challenged - Appellants company allegedly sold foreign currency through unauthorized persons deputed by purchaser - Appellants were called upon to show-cause why penalty should not be imposed against them u/s. 50 r/w s. 49 (3) & (4) of the Act - Respondent imposed penalty of Rs.50,000/- - Appellants filed appeals before Tribunal which were dismissed - Appeals before DB - DB confirmed orders of Tribunal - Hence instant appeals - Whether the order recorded by DB was maintainable - Held, transaction that took place between appellants and purchaser, it should have been carried on only through their respective authorized representatives - Statement of appellant disclosed that on each occasion transaction was negotiated by Branch Manager of appellant with purchaser - It was not case of respondent that neither of these two persons who indulged in transaction of money changing business were not authorized officials of their respective establishments - If said factum related to business transactions, which had taken place as between appellants and purchaser was not in controversy, no violation could be alleged against appellants - For any violation or contravention of provisions of FERA or FEMA at instance of purchaser after money changing transaction as between appellants and said concern had come to end, appellants should not in any way be held responsible or proceeded against - Hence, Courts below and DB failed to appreciate issue in proper perspective while holding appellant guilty of violation alleged - Therefore, in impugned orders of Courts below and DB, sale effected by appellants on rate higher than rate prevailing in market was not the basis for alleged violation of paragraph 3 of the FLM r/w s. 6(4), 6(5) and 7 of the Act - In confiscation order passed by Customs Authorities, where again appellants were also one of noticees, no fault was found as against appellants on that ground - Hence, impugned orders by which appellants were found guilty of violation of paragraph 3 of FLM r/w ss. 6(4), 6(5) and 7 of FERA and consequential imposition of penalty of Rs.50,000/- was wholly unjustified - Impugned orders were set aside - Appeals allowed.
