Life Insurance Corporation of India and others v S. Vasanthi

Supreme Court of India

14 August 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, J. Chelameswar

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 526; (2014) 9 SCC 315; 2014 (5) AWC 4939; JT 2014 (9) SC 294; 2014(9) SCALE 379; 2014 (5) SLR 405

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: L.I.C. of India (Staff) Regulations, 1960

Summary: Service - Disciplinary order - Department enquiry - Punishment - Legality - Appeal was preferred against the final judgment and order passed by HC whereby DB modified the punishment imposed by the disciplinary authority of appellant No.1 on respondent employee in a departmental enquiry - Hence instant appeal - Whether impugned order passed was justified -

Held, HC transgressed its limits of judicial review by itself assuming the role of sitting as departmental appellate authority, which was not permissible in law - Lucknow Kshetriya Gramin Bank (Now Allahabad, Uttar Pradesh Gramin Bank) & Anr. v. Rajendra Singh, 2013 Indlaw SC 470 - Respondent had no answer to the said position in law and could not justify the stance of the HC in modifying the punishment in the manner indicated - Therefore, sidetracking the central issue, he made a vain attempt to argue that the charges against respondent could not be held to be proved as per the records - Obviously, that was not even the issue before SC - There were consistent findings, not only of the departmental authorities, but even the Single Judge as also the DB to the effect that charges against respondent stood established in the departmental enquiry - Impugned judgment which modified the penalty was set aside and the penalty imposed by the disciplinary authority was restored - Appeal allowed.
