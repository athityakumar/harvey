Raman v Uttar Haryana Bijli Vitran Nigam Limited and others

Supreme Court of India

17 December 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, C. Nagappan

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 878; 2015 (1) ACC 88; 2015 ACJ 484; 2015 (2) ALD(SC) 135; 2015(1) ALL MR 896; 2015 (109) ALR 230; 2015 (1) AWC 656; JT 2014 (14) SC 286; 2015 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 705; 2015 (1) OLR 461; 2015 (1) RCR(Civil) 353; 2014(14) SCALE 354; 2015 (1) WLN(SC) 100

Case Digest

Subject: Electricity & Energy

Keywords: Indian Succession Act, 1925, Human Rights, Indian Electricity Rules, 1956

Summary: Electricity & Energy - Indian Electricity Act, 2003 - Electrocution - Compensation - Reduced -Challenged - Appellant, a four year old boy was electrocuted by coming in direct contact with the naked electric wire lying open on the roof of his house - On account of said electrocuted appellant lost all the amenities and become a deadwood throughout his life - Appellant approached the HC by filing a petition seeking for an award of compensation from the respondents on account of the negligence on the part of the respondents which resulted in the tragic electric shock leading to triple amputation of the appellant - Single Judge awarded compensation of Rs. 60 lakhs to the appellant - On appeal, DB reduced the compensation amount awarded by the Single Judge from Rs. 60 lakhs to Rs.30 lakhs and reducing the monthly payment from Rs.20,000/- to Rs.10,000/- - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, compensation awarded at Rs. 60 lakhs in the judgment of the Single Judge of the HC, out of which 30 lakhs were to be deposited jointly in the name of the appellant represented by his parents as natural guardian and the Chief Engineer or his nominee representing the respondent-Nigam in a nationalised Bank in a fixed deposit till appellant attains the age of majority, is just and proper but SC set aside that portion of the judgment of the Single Judge directing that if appellant survives, he is permitted to withdraw the amount, otherwise the deposit amount shall be reverted back to the respondents as the same is not legal and valid for the reason that once compensation amount is awarded by the court, it should go to the claimant/appellant. SC directed that after attaining the age of majority, the appellant is at liberty to withdraw the said amount also. If for any reason the appellant does not stay alive, his heirs/legal representatives can withdraw the said amount. Appeal allowed.
