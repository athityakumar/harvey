J. Rajiv Subramaniyam and another v Pandiyas and others

Supreme Court of India

14 March 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Surinder Singh Nijjar, A.K. Sikri

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 168; (2014) 5 SCC 651; AIR 2014 SC 1710; 2014 (104) ALR 177; 2014 (2) CLT(SC) 83; [2014] 186 Comp Cas 73; 2014 (2) CompLJ 331; 2014 (2) CTC 323; 2014 (2) DRTC 166; JT 2014 (5) SC 144; 2014 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 2312; 2014 (6) MahLJ 22; 2014 (3) MLJ 121; 2014 (4) MPLJ 270; 2014 (176) PLR 809; 2014 (3) RCR(Civil) 167; 2014 (123) RD 669; 2014(3) SCALE 646; [2014] 3 S.C.R. 1140

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance

Summary: Banking & Finance - Securitisation and Reconstruction of Financial Assets and Enforcement of Security Interest Act, 2002, s. 13(2), 13(4) - Auction Sale - Confirmation - Legality - Respondent No.1 and respondent No. 2 took various loans from respondent No.3-Bank - Assets of respondent No.1 and respondent No. 2 which was mortgaged with respondent No.3 were classified as non-performing assets (NPA) - Respondent No.3 issued notice u/s. 13(2) of the Act followed by possession notice u/s. 13(4) of the Act - respondent No.1 and respondent No. 2 challenged two notices and filed petition - Respondent No.1 and respondent No. 2 cancellation of auction notice and sought permission of respondent No.3 to sell secured assets by private Treaty - Respondent No.1 and respondent No. 2 were informed by respondent No.3 that secured assets were sold to appellant who was bona fide purchaser and paid full consideration - Respondent No.1 and respondent No. 2 filed petitions before Single Judge - Petitions were allowed by Single Judge - Appeal was filed by appellant before HC - Appeal was dismissed - Hence instant appeals -Whether the order recorded by HC was legal -

Held, though order of Single Judge about validity of sale was affirmed, DB interfered with other direction of Single Judge which should not have been done as bank had not challenged order of Single Judge - Therefore, once payment was made to appellant by respondent No.1 and respondent No. 2 in the manner stated, possession of property should be delivered to respondent No.1 and respondent No. 2 with no further liability towards bank - Hence, sale in favour of appellants and subsequent delivery of possession to appellants was null and void - Sale was accordingly set aside - Appellants were directed to deliver possession of property purchased by them under Sale Deed to respondent No.1 and respondent No. 2 immediately upon receiving entire amount as directed to respondent No.3 to refund entire proceeds of FDR in which sale consideration was deposited together with accrued interest forthwith by respondent No.1 and respondent No. 2 would ensure that entire amount due to appellants was paid - Upon receipt of entire amount, possession should be delivered to respondent No.1 and respondent No. 2 - Appeals disposed of.
