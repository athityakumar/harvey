Anand Brothers Private Limited Through M.D. v Union of India and others

Supreme Court of India

04 September 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, Adarsh Kumar Goel, C. Nagappan

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 588; (2014) 9 SCC 212; AIR 2015 SC 125; 2014 (107) ALR 903; 2014 (4) CompLJ 269; JT 2014 (10) SC 523; 2015 (2) MahLJ 1; 2015 (1) MPLJ 495; 2014(10) SCALE 313; [2014] 10 S.C.R. 382

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration Act, 1940 - Award - Legality - A non-speaking arbitral award was passed in favor of appellant company - On appeal before Single Judge same was set aside on ground that Arbitrator had not recorded his 'findings' as required u/cl. 70 of General Conditions of Contract and matter was remitted back to Arbitrator - Appellant filed appeal before DB, DB affirmed order passed by Single Judge - Hence instant appeals - Whether impugned order passed by DB was justified -

Held, expression 'finding' appearing in cl. 70, needs to be so construed as to promote that object and include within it not only ultimate conclusion which the arbitrator arrives at but also process of reasoning by which he does so. cl. 70 could not, have meant to be only a wooden or lifeless formality of indicating whether claim was accepted or rejected. Any such statement would have made no qualitative addition to adjudication of claim for arbitrator would award sum of money but withhold reasons for the same. Arbitrator's award was admittedly unsupported by any reason, no matter arbitrator had in column captioned 'findings' made comments like 'sustained', 'partly sustained', 'not sustained'. Single Judge was justified in setting aside award made by arbitrator and remitting matter to him for making of a fresh award. Arbitration proceedings had been conducted under the old Act any remission to arbitrator could only be under provisions of the said Act. SC attention was, drawn to a letter dtd. 2/3/2009 whereby respondents have appointed Engineer Headquarter as Sole Arbitrator to adjudicate upon dispute between parties as 'B', earlier appointed had expressed his inability to continue nor was 'K', initially appointed, ready to go on with proceedings. It was urged that if for any reason 'B', newly appointed arbitrator is also unable to take up assignment, respondents could be directed to appoint another arbitrator within time frame with a direction to arbitrator so appointed to conclude proceedings as early as possible. SC see no reason to decline limited prayer made by appellant especially when respondent submitted that in case 'B' was also unable to enter upon reference for any reason respondents shall, within such time, as may be fixed by SC nominate another arbitrator. SC make it clear that consequent upon orders passed by HC the arbitrator shall conclude proceedings in terms of provisions of the Act expeditiously. In case Arbitrator already nominated is for any reason unable to take up assignment respondents within time framed appoint a substitute arbitrator who shall then enter upon reference and conclude proceedings as early as possible. Appeals dismissed.
