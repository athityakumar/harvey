Balbir and another v Vazir and others

Supreme Court of India

01 July 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjana Prakash Desai, Madan B. Lokur

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 434; (2014) 12 SCC 670; AIR 2014 SC 2778; 2014 CRLJ 3697; JT 2014 (8) SC 291; 2014 (3) RCR(Criminal) 560; 2014 (4) RLW 2990; 2014(8) SCALE 337

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Murder - Acquittal - Sustainability - Respondents allegedly committed offence of murder - However, HC acquitted respondents - Hence instant appeal - Whether acquittal of respondents by HC was sustainable -

Held, PW1who claimed to be at the scene of offence lodged FIR - PW1 turned hostile - It was, however, pertinent to note that in the FIR, PW1 involved four persons - PW1 did not name them but he stated that he could identify them - According to the FIR, both deceased were sent in a Ceilo Car to Pilani - That version differed from the version of PW3 because PW3 stated that they were taken in two different cars - FIR further stated that on the way one deceased had died and another deceased was taken to hospital at Hissar - Thus, when the FIR was written at 12.30 P.M on 23-7-2000, information about one deceased's death and departure of another deceased to Hissar was conveyed at Rampura village - No names of persons who accompanied the deceased to hospital were stated in the FIR - Names of people were mentioned as persons who had seen the incident - Out of them first person was examined as PW9, second person was examined as PW10 and third person was examined as PW12 - Rest of the persons were not examined - Persons named in the FIR did not know the assailants - PWs9, 10 and 12 stated that they could identify the assailants if they were brought before them - However, no identification parade was held - PW9, PW1 and PW12 turned hostile and hence their evidence was of no use to the prosecution - HC had rightly noted that when the FIR was lodged by PW1 at about 12.30 P.M, the fact that one deceased had died on the way to Pilani and another deceased was taken to Hissar was known at the Ashram at Rampura village - By the time, alleged dying declaration was also made by deceased - Therefore, the names of the assailants also should have reached Rampura village along with information that deceased was being taken to Hissar - PW3 should have communicated the names of the assailants - However, names of the assailants were not disclosed by anyone to the police - PW4 stated that one deceased was taken to Pilani - When PW4 along with others reached Pilani, X told him that deceased was taken to Hissar - Prosecution should have examined X who could have said whether any information about assailants was communicated to him - Prosecution failed to examine X - All that casts a shadow of doubt on PW3's evidence that deceased made a dying declaration to him in which he disclosed the names of the assailants - It might also be mentioned that PW12 who turned hostile made a statement that Priest who was present disclosed the names of the assailants to him, but he did not remember the names - Pertinently Priest's name was mentioned in the FIR hence he was present at the scene of offence - However, Priest's statement was recorded by the police as late as on 30-8-2000 and he was not examined by the prosecution - It bears repetition to state that in Priests statement recorded on 30-8-2000, Priest did not disclose names of the assailants - PW3 stated that he disclosed the names of the assailants to PW31 at Hissar where PW31 conducted the inquest proceedings - However, the names of the assailants found no mention in inquest report - In fact, PW31stated that he had recorded the statement of PW3, but PW3 had not disclosed to him the dying declaration or the names of the assailants - PW29, the Investigating Officer stated that he went to Hissar on 23-7-2000 and met PW31 - PW29 got a lot of information about the incident from PW31 except the names of the assailants - According to PW29, he went to Balakdera on 25-7-2000, but nobody disclosed the names of the assailants to him - Names of the alleged assailants surfaced three days after the incident when the statements of PW3, PW4 and PW5 were recorded - No acceptable explanation was given for delay in recording the statements - Reluctance of the prosecution witnesses to come out with the truth and name the assailants, the delay in recording the statements of eye-witnesses and statement of PW3, the unnatural dying declaration giving minute diverse particulars about the assailants reflect on the credibility of the prosecution case - Another significant aspect of instant case was absence of identification parade - Persons who were named in the FIR and others, who had witnessed the incident at different stages did not know all the assailants but they claimed that they could identify the assailants - But the prosecution failed to hold test identification parade - Thus, prosecution had failed to prove its case beyond reasonable doubt - SC was mindful of the fact that the case involved two murders and use of firearms - Crime was grave - But HC had scrutinized the evidence correctly in light of settled legal principles - Appeals dismissed.
