Metro Exporters Private Limited and another v State Bank of India and others

Supreme Court of India

23 April 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan, Vikramajit Sen

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 544; (2014) 11 SCC 161; AIR 2014 SC 3206; 2014 (2) DRTC 609; JT 2014 (8) SC 336; [2014] 9 S.C.R. 64

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Banking & Finance - Practice & Procedure - Writ petition - Dismissal - Appropriate remedy - Sustainability - Writ Petition was filed by appellant challenging the action of the Bank which was dismissed by the HC on the ground that it was a dispute which arose out of a contractual relationship between the parties and hence the appropriate remedy for the appellant was by way of a civil suit and not a writ petition u/art. 226 of the Constitution - Hence instant appeal - Whether the State Bank of India, was right in debiting appellant's EEFC A/c EURO No.10937619705 an amount of €136,027.03, after a long lapse of time, on the ground that it was wrongly deposited in appellant's account and driving appellant to recover the amount by way of civil proceedings -

Held, so far as importer was concerned, he had already paid the amount and the SBI, had received the amount in the Nostro account of Bank of America with remittance advice to Bank of India, instead of SBI - Appellant was also informed on 2-12-2006 of the export collection advice by the SBI for US$ 199,959.74 and appellant received the amount - Bank might committed a mistake, but now it would be impossible for appellant to recover the amount from the importer since, so far as the importer was concerned, it had paid the amount - If the SBI, had not given credit of the amount, then, appellant could have proceeded against the importer at the earliest opportunity, but now the question was whether appellant should suffer for the mistake committed by the Bank, to which our answer is in the negative - Appeal allowed.
