Kulai Ibrahim @ Ibrahim v State Represented by the Inspector of Police, Coimbatore

Supreme Court of India

03 July 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjana Prakash Desai, Madan B. Lokur

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 429; (2014) 12 SCC 332; AIR 2014 SC 2726; 2014 CRLJ 3775; 2014 (5) CTC 535; JT 2014 (8) SC 14; 2014 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 2910; 2014 (3) RCR(Criminal) 550; 2014 (4) RLW 3024; 2014(8) SCALE 178

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Birth and Death Registration Act, 1969

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act, 2000, ss. 7-A and 12 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 147, 148, 149 and 302 - Plea of juvenility - Appellant along with others was tried for offences punishable u/ss. 147, 148, 149 and 302 of the IPC - Sessions Court convicted the appellant and 2 others for offence punishable u/ss. 302 and 148 of the IPC - Appeal filed thereagainst was dismissed - HC noted in the impugned judgment that the plea of juvenility was neither raised before the Trial Court, nor raised in the memo of appeal before the HC - HC noted that no application was filed seeking permission to adduce evidence to establish that the appellant was a juvenile and therefore, rejected the plea - Hence, instant appeal - Whether the appellant was 'a juvenile' within the meaning of the term 'juvenile' as defined under the Act when the offence was committed and whether the plea of juvenility could be raised by him at this stage

Held, all persons who were below the age of 18 years on the date of commission of the offence even prior to 1-4-2001, which was the date of commencement of the Act could be treated as juveniles even if the claim of juvenility was raised after they have attained the age of 18 years on or before date of the commencement of the Act and were undergoing sentences upon being convicted - Therefore, the claim of juvenility could be raised by the appellant - Further, in cases where documents mentioned in s. 12(a)(i) to (iii) of the Act were unavailable or where they were found to be fabricated or manipulated, it was necessary to obtain medical report for age determination of the accused - In the instant case, documents were available but they were, according to the police, fabricated or manipulated and therefore if the fabrication was confirmed, it was necessary to go for medical report for age determination of the appellant - Delay could not act as an impediment in seeking medical report as s. 7-A of the Act gives right to an accused to raise the question of juvenility at any point of time even after disposal of the case - Order accordingly.
