Union of India and others v Mahindra and Mahindra Limited

Supreme Court of India

09 May 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya, J. Chelameswar

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 488; (2014) 12 SCC 154; 2014 (303) E.L.T. 481; JT 2014 (8) SC 81; 2014(6) SCALE 696; [2014] 7 S.C.R. 504

Case Digest

Subject: Excise

Keywords: Duty Of Excise, Central Excises And Salt Act, 1944, Normal Price, Central Excise (Valuation) Rules, 1975, Place Of Removal, Show-cause Notice, Central Excises and Salt (Second Amendment) Act, 1973, Self-removal of excisable goods, Short payment of excise duty

Summary: Excise - Central Excise and Salt Act, 1944, ss. 4, 4(b)(iii), 11A - Central Excise (Valuation) Rules, 1975, r. 6(a) - Self-removal of excisable goods - Short payment of excise duty - Show-cause notice - Legality - Respondent (assesse) was paying the excise duty on goods removed at factory gate regularly - Appellant (competent authority) in exercise of power conferred under Act issued 2 show-cause notices that assessee sold the duty paid stocks from their sales depots at the higher price; that sales depots were related persons and that therefore the price at which the goods were sold from the depots should be considered as the assessable value; that though retail price included excise duty, freight and dealer's margin, excise duty had been paid at the factory gate without including the said amounts and that that was incorrect in view of r. 6(a) of the Rules; that the assessee had not included in the assessable value (1) after sales services charges (2) dealer's margin (3) marketing and selling expenses and (4) excess freight; that assessee concealed the invoice value - Aggrieved respondent filed petitions before HC and DB by judgment quashed the impugned show-cause notices for short payment of excise duty u/s. 11A of the Act - Hence, instant appeal - Appellant contended that HC was not justified in interfering at the stage of issuance of show-cause notice when notices were pertaining to under assessment due to incorrect application of s. 4 of the Act and that HC was not justified in holding that the ex-factory gate price was ascertainable in instant case, even when it was not disputed that there was over charging at the depots in excess over the alleged ex-factory price and that HC failed to take into account the fact that in case of sale through depots expenses on account of cost of transportation including insurance freight only was allowed deduction -

Held, assessee failed to bring the ascertainable price of tractor, cost of transportation to depot, etc. to the notice of HC - Assessee simply challenged the show-cause notices on the ground that the amended s. 4 of the Act was not applicable - HC without looking into the relevant fact, only on the ground that s. 4(b)(iii) of the Act was subsequently added by amendment including 'depot', 'premises of consignment agent' or 'any other place' or 'premises' from where the excisable goods were to be sold after their clearance from the factory, declared the notices illegal and set aside the same - Even the matter was not remanded back to competent authority allowing the assessee to bring to its notice 'normal price', in course of wholesale trade, place of removal of tractors, transportation charges, etc - Thus, instant case was remitted to competent authority granting liberty to the assessee to forward a copy of each of show-cause replies already filed within 4 weeks - Assessee was also given liberty to produce relevant evidence in support of its claim - Competent Authority would pass appropriate order(s) in accordance with law - Impugned judgment was set aside - Appeal allowed.
