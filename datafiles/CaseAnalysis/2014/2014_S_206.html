Bharatkumar Shantilal Thakkar v State of Gujarat and another

Supreme Court of India

01 April 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, Shiva Kirti Singh

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 234; 2014 (6) ALT(SC) 29; 2014(4) SCALE 700; [2014] 4 S.C.R. 1147; 2015 (1) SLR 17

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges

Keywords: Higher qualification, Advance increments

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Higher qualifications - Advance increments - Pay Commission - Petitioner joined judicial service - Prior to his joining judicial service, petitioner had done post-graduation in law - Petitioner filed instant petition u/art. 32 of the Constitution for direction to respondents to implement the Ist National Judicial Pay Commission which was approved by SC -

Held, Registrar General of HC by communication dtd. 2-4-2008 sent to Secretary, Legal Department advised him to move Government for insertion of r. 7-A in 2005 Rules, proposed by HC - Candidate selected for post of Civil Judge who possesses higher qualification in law, such as LL.M., M.Phil in Law, Ph.D. in Law should be entitled to get three additional increments, however such increments should be released upon successful completion of probation period - Pertinently, in proposed r. 7-A of the 2005 Rules, there was no cut- off date with regard to acquisition of higher qualification in law such as LL.M. in law, M.Phil in Law, Ph.D. in Law - By subsequent communication dt. 27-7-2009, Registrar General advised Secretary to the Government, Legal Department that insertion of r. 7-A in 2005 Rules might not be necessary if recommendation of granting three advance increments to candidates have had higher qualification in law from 1-11-1999 was incorporated as addendum to Government resolution dt. 16-3-2007 and given effect from 1-11-1999 - It appeared that sentence 'if present recommendation of granting three advance increments to candidates having had higher qualification in law from 1-11-1999' in letter dt. 27-7-2009 had really created confusion which led to cut-off date (1.11.1999) being provided in resolution dt. 14-6-2012 - Date 1-11-1999 was referable to implementation date for three advance increments and not as cut-off date for acquiring higher qualification in law, this was clear from sentence preceding controversial sentence which read '..the Government in Legal Department have issued resolution No. Pay/102003/1233/D dt. 16-03-2007 and given effect to the same from 1-11-1999 - It was not in dispute that while recommending insertion of r. 7-A in 2005 Rules, no cut-off date had been given - HC submitted that by letter dt. 27-7-2009, it was neither intended nor meant that three advance increments should be available only to those judicial officers who had acquired higher qualification in law from 1-11-1999 - As it was there was no rational in providing that those candidates who possessed higher qualification in law on or after 1-11-1999 would be given advance increments, criteria provided in para 2 was irrational - Accordingly, it was held that expression 'on or after 1-11-1999' in para 2 of resolution dt. 14-6-2012 should be read as 'on or before 1-11-1999' - Thus, all financial benefits as per order should be paid to petitioner as early as possible and in no case later than two months from date of receipt of copy of order - Order should be applicable to all Judicial Officers who had been denied benefit of three advance increments on basis that they acquired higher educational qualification in law before 1-11-1999 - Petition allowed.
