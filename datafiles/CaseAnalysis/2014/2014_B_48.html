Bastiram v State of Rajasthan

Supreme Court of India

13 February 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Madan B. Lokur, Ranjana Prakash Desai

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 172; (2014) 5 SCC 398; 2014 CRLJ 1761; JT 2014 (3) SC 136; [2014] 2 S.C.R. 567

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s.302 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant allegedly committed offence of murder - Appellant was convicted u/s.302 IPC by Trial Court and affirmed by HC - Hence instant appeal - Whether conviction of appellant was sustainable -

Held, on the basis of the statements made by the defence witnesses it was not possible to accurately state when appellant left Bikaner, but he was certainly there till about 5.00 or 5.15 p.m. if not a little later - Information about the incident at Nokha was received by Tara Chand in Police Station Nokha at about 7.15 p.m. meaning thereby that the incident had taken place a short while before that - As per the parcha bayan given by one person the incident took place between 6.30 and 6.45 p.m. - There was therefore a window of about one hour and thirty or forty-five minutes between the time of appellant appellant's departure from Bikaner and his arrival at Nokha - It was, therefore, quite possible, given the flexibility of time and a lack of exactitude that appellant was present when the incident took place and as testified by the eye witnesses - Trial Court rejected the evidence of the defence witnesses with regard to the absence of appellant on three grounds: Firstly, there was no occasion for him to remain in Bikaner after office hours, that was, after 5.00 p.m. - That might not be a good enough reason per se for rejecting the testimony of the defence witnesses - But a reasonable conclusion could be drawn on the basis of the material on record that appellant was in Bikaner till about 5.00 or 5.15 p.m. but it was of no consequence - Secondly, Trial Court also noted some discrepancies in the evidence of the defence witnesses with regard to the timings given by the various defence witnesses - These were minor discrepancies and, as rightly noted by the Trial Court, no person keeps an eye on the clock or notes the time of meeting - It was for that reason that some flexibility in the timings should be given - Thirdly, since appellant was the President of the Patwar Sangh at Nokha and the defence witnesses were either Patwaris or related to the Revenue Dept., appellant could have influenced them on account of being a leader - It might be a real possibility considering the fact that though appellant was named in the parcha bayan as one of those armed with a pistol and who caused the death of deceased, he was in fact arrested about two and a half years later on 21-1-1998 - Cumulatively considered, the reasons given by the Trial Court for rejecting the testimony of the defence witnesses were adequate - In any event, what was perhaps more important was the cogent and consistent eye witness testimony relating to the presence of appellant - That could not be simply discarded on the basis of possible guesswork by the defence witnesses about the timings of the meetings that appellant had in Bikaner - In that regard, the conduct of appellant was also significant - Appellant produced a copy of his travelling allowance bill and daily diary which showed that he left Bikaner at about 7.30 p.m. - Trial Court noted that both these documents were prepared after the incident and in the daily diary it was recorded that appellant met DW9 in connection with a party and left Bikaner at about 7.30 p.m. and arrived at Nokha at 9.30 p.m. - None of the defence witnesses support the case of appellant that he left Bikaner at 7.30 p.m. - It was quite clear that appellant manufactured that evidence with a view to cover his tracks when there was no need for him to do so, assuming his witnesses were speaking the truth - Under the circumstances, on a consideration of the evidence on record there was no doubt that appellant was present when the incident occurred and, as stated by the eye witnesses, participated in it - SC found no reason to upset the concurrent finding of fact in that regard by the Trial Court and the HC - Appeals dismissed.
