Badal Murmu and others v State of West Bengal

Supreme Court of India

05 February 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjana Prakash Desai, Madan B. Lokur

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 68; (2014) 3 SCC 366; (2014) 4 SCC 780; AIR 2014 SC 1150; 2014 (1) JCC 599; JT 2014 (3) SC 307; 2014 (2) MLJ(Crl) 188; 2014 (1) MLJ(Crl) 580; 2014 (1) RCR(Criminal) 1017; 2014 (3) RLW 1948; 2014(2) SCALE 43; [2014] 2 S.C.R. 323

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Murder, Culpable homicide not amounting to murder

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 304 Part II, 148, 302 r/w s. 149 - Murder - Culpable Homicide not amounting to murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellants (accused) allegedly assaulted the deceased and beaten to death and thereby committed offence u/s. 148 and s. 302 r/w s. 149 of IPC - Trial Court convicted appellants and sentenced them to undergo imprisonment for life for causing death of deceased - Aggrieved appellants filed an appeal before HC, which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Appellants contended that admittedly they have used lathis and, thus, s. 304 Part II of IPC was clearly attracted to instant case - Whether impugned conviction of appellants was justified and which offence was committed by appellants -

Held, evidence of PW1 (first wife of deceased), PW3 (sister of deceased), PW6 (second wife of deceased) and PW7 (friend of deceased) was truthful and had rightly been relied upon - They were rustic witnesses and have candidly stated all that they had seen - Pertinently, PW7 did not hesitate to name his brother as one of the assailants - No doubt, those witnesses were related to deceased, but the tenor of their evidence was such that it was not possible to say that they have falsely involved the appellants - Their evidence has a ring of truth - Prosecution had proved that appellants assaulted deceased with lathis which resulted in his death - In instant case, appellants were stated to have assaulted the deceased simultaneously - No individual role was ascribed to anyone - Doctor had not stated which injury was fatal - It was difficult therefore to say that all the appellants were guilty of murder - None of the eye witnesses have given specific role to any of appellants - They have not stated which appellants gave which blow and on which part of the deceased's body - They have not stated which injury was caused by which accused - Doctor has not stated which injury was fatal - In peculiar facts of instant case, it was not possible to hold that appellants shared common object to murder the deceased and in prosecution of that common object they caused his death - It would not be possible to sustain their conviction for offence punishable u/s. 302 r/w s. 149 of IPC - It would be just and proper to resort to s. 304 Part II of IPC and treat the sentence already undergone by them as sentence for said offence - Impugned conviction of appellants for offences punishable u/s. 302 r/w s. 149 of IPC was quashed - Appeal disposed of.
