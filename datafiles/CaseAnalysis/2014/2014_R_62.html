Coffee Board v Ramesh Exports Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

09 May 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Pinaki Chandra Ghose, Chandramauli Kumar Prasad

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 354; (2014) 6 SCC 424; AIR 2014 SC 2301; 2014 (5) ALD(SC) 33; 2014 (105) ALR 700; 2014 (2) CLT(SC) 354; 2014 (3) CTC 728; JT 2014 (8) SC 90; 2015 (2) KarLJ 248; 2014 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 3079; 2014 (6) MahLJ 531; 2014 (4) MLJ 479; 2015(1) M.P.H.T. 363; 2014 (4) MPLJ 500; 2014 (3) RCR(Civil) 104; 2014 (125) RD 115; 2014 (4) RLW 3584; 2014(6) SCALE 651; [2014] 7 S.C.R. 461

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Keywords: Entitled to, Suit for reliefs

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 2 r. 2 - Coffee Act, 1942 - Suit for reliefs - Entitled to - Appellant-Board was statutory body under the 1942 Act - Appellant was member of International Coffee Organization(ICO) - Respondent filed suits against appellant for decree of Rs.5,32,012.31 p. and Rs.11,70,446.39 p. with interest at rate of 19% per annum - Suit was resisted by respondents - Suits were decreed by Trial Court - Appellant filed appeals before HC - Appeals were partly allowed by HC - Hence instant appeal - Whether the order recorded by HC was sustainable -

Held, facts required to be proved in suits by respondent was to succeed in its claims that on account of failure of appellant to provide required ICO stamps as assured by it, respondent had to suffer losses - Two separate reliefs claimed by respondent were dependent on same fact being omission of duty by appellant - Grounds of disparity in suits were amount of coffee and dates when same was purchased, however it should be noted that period between 11-8-1982 and 18-11-1982 was common to both suits and there were no specific pleadings differentiating the same - Further, suits were filed within span of 9 days of each other - Court opined that suits should have been merged with claims against coffee purchased between 25-7-1982 and 8-9-1982 clubbed together in same suit from which two reliefs, first being losses due to delayed shipment and second being costs and losses arose due to recall of shipment, could have been claimed - Both reliefs were being claimed separately in two concerned suits - SC opined that Trial Court in its judgment correctly held that suit was barred under O. 2 r. 2 CPC - Hence, HC misappreciated facts and reasoning of HC should not be sustained in the eye of law and judgment and order of HC was set aside and order of Trial Court was upheld - Appeal allowed.
