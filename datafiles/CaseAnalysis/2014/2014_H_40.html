Hindustan Coca Cola Beverage (Private) Limited v Union of India and others

Supreme Court of India

04 September 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, A.M. Sapre

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 609; 2014 (308) E.L.T. 433; JT 2014 (12) SC 278; 2014(10) SCALE 495; [2014] 7 S.C.R. 632

Case Digest

Subject: Excise

Keywords: Demand, Amendment of Finance Act

Summary: Excise - Finance Act, 2003, s. 153 - Central Excise Act, 1944, s. 35 - Amendment of Finance Act - Demand - Govt issued various notification, however 2003 Act came into force and u/s. 153 of the Act certain notification were amended with retrospective effect - Respondent department demanded some amount in question - Aggrieved by order appellant filed petition before HC - Validity of Notification dt. 6-8- 2003 and certain other notifications including original notification were called in question - Constitutional validity of the amendment of the Act was also called in question - HC accepted the stand of the revenue - Hence instant appeal - Whether HC was justified addressing the lis on merits when series of factual aspects are involved -

Held, documents mean the transactions, quantum of CENVAT availed of, the amount that was taken as refund by paying from the P.L.A. and further not availing refund of CENVAT credit at any point of time. There is no cavil over the fact that an appeal lies u/s. 35 of the 1944 Act to the Commissioner who can address both the issues relating to facts and law keeping in view the applicability of the relevant notifications. It is borne out from the record that the appellant assessee had furnished a bank guarantee amounting to Rs.2,20,18,124/- for obtaining an order of stay. It would not be appropriate to give an opportunity to the appellant to prefer statutory appeals and allow it to enjoy the benefit of stay of recovery on the basis of a bank guarantee. Therefore, it was directed to assessee to deposit Rs.2.5 crores before the adjudicating authority within 6 weeks and after the said deposit is made and the receipt obtained, the appeal would be entertained within the said period. On an appeal being filed, Commissioner shall deal with the matter on merits. Attorney General very fairly stated that the Revenue would not raise the issue of limitation as the period spent before HC and SC and the time granted for depositing of the amount would stand excluded for the purpose of preferring the appeal. Thus, judgment and orders of HC are set aside and appellants are directed to prefer appeals with the conditions precedent as imposed . Appeal partly allowed.
