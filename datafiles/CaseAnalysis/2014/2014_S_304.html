Muralidhar @ Gidda and another v State of Karnataka

Supreme Court of India

09 April 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, Shiva Kirti Singh

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 235; (2014) 5 SCC 730; AIR 2014 SC 2200; 2014 CRLJ 2365; 2014 (3) JCC 1573; 2014 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 2113; 2014 (2) MLJ(Crl) 341; 2014 (2) RCR(Criminal) 507; 2014(4) SCALE 693; [2014] 4 S.C.R. 817

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Murder

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 144, 148, 149, 302, 307 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - A case was registered against accused persons/appellants u/ss. 144, 148, 149, 302, 307 of IPC - Trial Court passed acquittal order - State/respondent filed appeal before HC - HC vide its judgment convicted appellants - Hence instant appeals - Whether HC was justified in upsetting view of Trial Court on re-appreciation of evidence of PW-25, PW-30 and PW- 36 and statement/Exhibit P-22 -

Held, sanctity was attached to dying declaration because it comes from mouth of dying person - If dying declaration was recorded not directly from actual words of maker but as dictated by somebody else, in our opinion, this by itself creates lot of suspicion about credibility of such statement and prosecution had to clear same to satisfaction of Court - Trial Court on over-all consideration of evidence of PW-25, PW-30 and PW-36 coupled with fact that there was over-writing about time at which statement was recorded and also insertion of two names by different ink did not consider it safe to rely upon dying declaration and acquitted accused for want of any other evidence - It could not be said that view taken by trial court on basis of evidence on record was not possible view - Appellants were entitled to benefit of doubt which was rightly given to them by Trial Court - HC on consideration of same evidence took different view and interfered with judgment of acquittal without properly keeping in mind that presumption of innocence in favor of accused had been strengthened by their acquittal from Trial Court and view taken by Trial Court as to credibility of statement and evidence of PW25, PW30 and PW36 was possible view - HC while upsetting judgment of acquittal had not kept in view well established principles in hearing appeal from judgment of acquittal - Impugned judgment passed by HC was set aside - Judgment of Trial Court was restored - Appeals allowed.
