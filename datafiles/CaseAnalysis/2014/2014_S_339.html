Sri Ram Builders v State of Madhya Pradesh and others

Supreme Court of India

25 April 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Surinder Singh Nijjar, A.K. Sikri

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 310; (2014) 14 SCC 102; 2014 (6) AWC 5987; 2014 (3) BC 186; JT 2014 (6) SC 134; 2014(5) SCALE 725; [2014] 5 S.C.R. 686

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Lease, Privity of contract

Summary: Administrative - Contract & Commercial - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Lease - Privity of contract - Respondent No.2 Madhya Pradesh Road Transport Corporation (MPRTC) proposed to construct bus stand - Agreement for Lease was entered into between Transport Corporation and respondent No.5 Indore Development Authority(IDA) - In pursuance of Lease Agreement possession of proposed site was handed over to MPRTC and authorised Transport Corporation to construct commercial complex on land owned by it or allotted to it on lease, under Build, Own-Operate and Transfer (BOT) Scheme through open tenders - Notice for inviting bids for selection of developer under BOT Scheme was issued and published - Appellant was placed as candidate satisfying eligibility criteria - MPRTC, approved its bid with appellant vide acceptance letter and directed appellant to deposit 25 per cent amount of total premium amount - Accordingly, appellant deposited first installment of Rs.1,16,37,750/- MPRTC issued work order to appellant for demolishing existing structure on land, to be replaced by commercial complex - Appellant requested MPRTC to hand over possession of proposed site, so that structure existing thereon could be demolished and new bus stand-cum-commercial complex could be constructed, in accordance with the terms and conditions of tender/agreement - Petition was filed by another person before HC and assailed Notification dt. 11-5-2004, by this notification, reservation of land use of 3.59 acres was changed by State Government - Appellant filed petition before HC to seek direction to MPRTC to immediately hand over possession of land in question to appellant and grant permission to demolish existing structure - Petition was disposed of by HC - Hence instant appeal - Whether the order recorded by HC was just and proper -

Held, HC rightly observed that appellant could seek appropriate relief by way of civil suit - HC in exercise of its jurisdiction u/art. 226 of the Constitution would not normally grant relief of specific performance of contract - At no stage, appellant had any privity of contract with IDA - MPRTC entered into BOT contract with appellant contrary to terms and conditions of lease which provided specifically that land should be used for constructing bus stand-cum commercial complex - MPRTC had no legal right to create any further right in favour of appellant with regard to receiving of premium on constructed units sold to third part - Possession of proposed site was delivered to MPRTC and maximum lease period was for 30 years - By efflux of time aforesaid lease period expired - Court didn't accept that as entire rent had been paid, MPRTC would be entitled to automatic renewal of lease for 90 years - Renewal clause in lease subsequently provided that renewal should be with consent of IDA - Consent by IDA was not mere formality - Even proposed site where bus stand - cum - commercial complex was to be constructed was under attachment - Claim made by appellant was in nature of damages for breach of contract and/or relief of specific performance of contract - So far as breach of contract was concerned, appellant would have no cause of action against IDA as there was no privity of contract between parties - So far as specific performance was concerned, it appeared that entire purpose of contract had been frustrated by subsequent events - Hence, there was no merit in instant appeals - Appeals dismissed.
