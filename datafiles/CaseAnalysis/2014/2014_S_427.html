State of Rajasthan v Roshan Khan and others

Supreme Court of India

15 January 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Patnaik, Gyan Sudha Misra

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 22; (2014) 2 SCC 476; 2014 CRLJ 1092; 2014 (2) JCC 1106; JT 2014 (1) SC 581; 2014 (1) MLJ(Crl) 308; 2014 (1) RCR(Criminal) 676; 2014 (2) RLW 953; 2014(1) SCALE 329; [2014] 1 S.C.R. 418

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Acquittal, Gang Rape

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 366 and 376(2)(g) - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 114A, 157 - Gang rape - Presumption - Acquittal - Sustainability - Respondents (accused) allegedly gang raped the prosecutrix (PW2) (daughter of PW1) and thereby committed offences u/ss. 366 and 376(2)(g) of IPC - Trial Court convicted respondents u/ss. 366 and 376(2)(g) of IPC and sentenced them 10 years RI - Aggrieved respondents filed an appeal before HC - HC by impugned judgment set aside the conviction of respondents - Aggrieved appellant (State) filed instant appeals - Appellant contended that HC could not have held that there were circumstances to suggest that prosecutrix could have gone on her own with accused persons and should have taken presumption u/s. 114A of the Act - Whether HC was justified in setting side conviction judgment passed by Trial Court - Held, evidence of prosecutrix was clear that all respondents, committed rape on her without her consent and forcibly - That evidence of prosecutrix was also corroborated by evidence of informant (PW1), who had himself witnessed one of the accused 'A' committing rape on prosecutrix - PW2 had also informed PW1 soon after the rape by accused persons that not only 'A' but the other 5 respondents also had forcibly committed rape on her - Evidence of PW1 and PW2 that all 6 respondents had committed rape on prosecutrix was also corroborated by complaint (Ext.P1) made by PW1 to police within a few hours of the incident as provided in s. 157 of the Act - Medical evidence also corroborated the evidence of PW1 and PW2 that there was sexual intercourse between the prosecutrix and accused persons - Since prosecutrix had categorically said that sexual intercourse was committed by accused without her consent and forcibly, Court had to draw the presumption that she did not give consent to the sexual intercourse committed on her by accused persons - Defence had not led any evidence to rebut that presumption - Judgment of HC was thus contrary to evidence on record and was liable to be set aside - Judgment passed by Trial Court was restored - Appeals allowed.
