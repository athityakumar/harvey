Polamrasetti Manikyam and another v Teegala Venkata Ramayya and another

Supreme Court of India

19 February 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan, Vikramajit Sen

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 101; (2014) 5 SCC 603; AIR 2014 SC 1286; 2014 (3) ALD(SC) 79; 2014(2) ALL MR 928; 2014 (103) ALR 687; 2014 (2) ALT(SC) 56; 2014 (2) CTC 458; JT 2014 (3) SC 106; 2014 (3) RCR(Civil) 951; 2014 (123) RD 490; 2014(2) SCALE 523; [2014] 1 S.C.R. 1127; 2014 (3) WLN(SC) 126

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Keywords: Cancellation, Kerala Court-Fees and Suits Valuation Act, 1959, Andhra Pradesh Court Fees and Suits Valuation Act, 1956

Summary: Civil Procedure - Andhra Pradesh Court Fees and Suits Valuation Act, 1956, ss. 37, 7, 7(iv)(a) - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 7 r. 10, O. 9 r. 1, O. 9 r. 2 - Kerala Court-Fees and Suits Valuation Act, 1959, s. 40 - Sale deed - Cancellation - Legality - Appellants/plaintiffs filed suit before Trial Court for cancellation of sale deed and to direct defendants/respondents to pay cost of suit - Trial Court held that the Court had no pecuniary jurisdiction to entertain suit and plaint was returned for presentation before proper Court - Aggrieved appellants filed appeal before Appellate Court and the same was dismissed - Aggrieved appellant filed revision before Single Judge - Single Judge viewed that for cancellation of sale deed suit should be valued on basis of market value of property governed by sale deed on date of presentation of plaint for purposes of Court Fee and jurisdiction and not on basis of sale consideration mentioned in sale deed - Appellants filed review petition and the same was dismissed - Hence instant appeals - Whether impugned order passed by HC was liable to be quashed -

Held, S. 37 of 1956 Act, which deals with suits for cancellation of decrees etc. was not governed by other Sections of 1956 Act, such as s. 7 and other related provisions - If s. 37 of 1956 Act was interpreted in light of expression 'save as otherwise provided' used in s. 7 of 1956 Act, it became clear that rule enshrined therein was clear departure from one contained in s. 7 r/w ss. 24, 26, 28, 29, 34, 35, 42, 45 of 1956 Act which provide for payment of Court Fee on market value of property - S. 37 of 1956 Act was stand-alone provision, wherein legislature designedly not used expression 'market value of the property' - S. 37 of 1956 Act contained special rule for valuing property for purpose of Court Fee and jurisdiction and Court could not see any reason why expression 'value of the property' used in s. 37 be substituted with expression 'market value of the property' - Hence, impugned order passed by HC was set aside - Orders passed by Appellate Court as well as HC were quashed - Trial Court was directed to proceed with suit in accordance with law and declaration made by Court - Appeals allowed.
