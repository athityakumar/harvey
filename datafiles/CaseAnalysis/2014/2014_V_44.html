Satendra Singh v Vinod Kumar Bhalotia

Supreme Court of India

18 December 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Jagdish Singh Khehar, Arun Mishra

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 870; 2015 (109) ALR 362; 2015 (2) AWC 1341; 2015 (1) RCR(Rent) 44; 2014(14) SCALE 188; 2015 (1) WLN(SC) 148

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Keywords: Uttar Pradesh Urban Buildings (Regulation of Letting, Rent and Eviction) Act, 1972, Bonafide pleas

Summary: Rent Control - Uttar Pradesh Urban Buildings (Regulation of Letting, Rent and Eviction) Act, 1972, s. 2(2) - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 106 - Suit for eviction - Bonafide pleas - Legality - Respondent (landlord) field a suit for eviction of petitioner (tenant) from shop in question - However, HC by impugned judgment ordered the petitioner to vacate the premises - Hence, instant SLP - Petitioner contended that suit filed by respondent was not sustainable, in view of provisions of the 1972 Act and that the shop in question, was constructed by the respondent, in the year 1970 and that eviction suit was not sustainable because notice issued by respondent u/s. 106 of 1882 Act, was invalid.

Held, in the rent agreement executed between the parties, it was expressly mentioned, that the shop in question was constructed in the year 1978. Interestingly the petitioner himself relied on a judgment rendered between the respondent and 5 other co-owners of the land, on which the rented shop, had been constructed. The said judgment recording the compromise, expressed the respective shares of the 6 co-owners, in the land. The suit was filed in 1970. It is therefore clear, that the petitioner was also aware, that the nature of property in 1970 was only land. If any building had been raised thereon, partition would have been sought in respect of land and buildings. Besides, the respondent while adducing evidence, had produced the first Municipal Assessment Order, depicting that the first assessment was made in consonance with the submissions advanced by the respondent. Mandate emerges from s. 2(2) of the 1972 Act, more particularly Explanation 1(a), thereof, which provides that the construction of a building would be deemed to have been completed, on the date of which the completion thereof is recorded by the Local Authority, having jurisdiction. The respondent has established, that the construction of the premises was completed in the year 1978, to the hilt. It was not justified for the petitioner to have raised such a plea, when in the rent agreement itself, it was acknowledged that the building leased out by the respondent to the petitioner, had been constructed in the year 1978. It is therefore apparent, that contention raised by petitioner, lacked bona fides. Rent agreement was for a period from August, 1981 to June, 1982, and the petitioner should have voluntarily vacated the suit premises, and should have handed over the possession of the suit premises voluntarily to the respondent in July, 1982, he has successfully repulsed all attempts of the respondent-landlord, to recover the possession of the suit premises. The petitioner is still in occupation of the premises even though more than 32 years have been elapsed, since the expiry of the rent agreement. It was in the instant background, Court satisfied, that petitioner should be imposed with user charges, for remaining in occupation of the rented premises, after the expiry of the rent agreement. Petitioner is directed to pay user charges at the rate of Rs.1,000/- per month, to respondent. Petition dismissed
