P. Ramakrishnam Raju v Union of India and others

Supreme Court of India

31 March 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, N.V. Ramana, Ranjan Gogoi

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 195; (2014) 12 SCC 1; AIR 2014 SC 1619; 2014 (4) ALD(SC) 113; 2014 (3) ALT(SC) 25; 2014 (3) AWC 2738; 2014 (142) FLR 218; JT 2014 (5) SC 102; 2014 (6) KarLJ 367; 2014 (2) KLT 282; 2014 (3) MLJ 377; 2015 (177) PLR 26; 2014(4) SCALE 329B; [2014] 4 S.C.R. 562; 2014 (3) SLR 599

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Constitution

Keywords: More pensionary benefits of retired Judges in HC

Summary: (1) Constitution of India-Article 217 (2) (b)-Employment-Pension-Fixation of pension-High Court Judges, who appointed from Bar under Article 217 (2) (b), on retirement, are entitled for addition of 10 years to their service for purpose of their pension-Benefit of 10 years addition to their service for purpose of pension is being denied to Judges of High Court appointed from Bar-Is arbitrary and violative of Article 14 of the Constitution-When persons who occupied constitutional office of Judge, High Court retire, there should not be any discrimination with regard to fixation of their pension-Irrespective of source from where Judges are drawn, they must be paid same pension just as they have been paid same salaries, allowance and perks as serving Judges.

(2) Constitution of India-Articles 217 (2) (b) and 14-Employment-Pension-Fixation of pension-Appointment of High Court Judges from Bar under Article 217 (2) (b)-Meagre pension for Judge drawn from Bar and served for less than 12 years on Bench adversely affects image of judiciary-Fixation of higher pension to Judges drawn from subordinate judiciary who have served for shorter period in contradistinction to Judges drawn from Bar who have served for longer period with less pension-Is highly discriminatory and breach of Article 14-Classification itself is unreasonable without any legally acceptable nexus with object sought to be achieved.
