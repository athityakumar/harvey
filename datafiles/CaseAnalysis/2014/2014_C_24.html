Chaman Lal v State of Punjab and others

Supreme Court of India

16 May 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, A.K. Sikri

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 389; AIR 2014 SC 3640; 2014 (4) AWC 4118; 2014 (143) FLR 244; 2014(7) SCALE 80; [2014] 6 S.C.R. 311; 2015 (4) SLR 137

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Retrial benefits

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14, 300 - Societies Registration Act, 1860 - Retrial benefits - Legality - Appellant had worked in Army as Truck driver - Appellant instituted suit before Trial Court claiming retrial benefits and the same was dismissed - Appellant filed appeal before HC and the same was dismissed - Aggrieved appellant filed second appeal and the same was dismissed - Hence instant appeal - Respondent contended that Fish Farmers Development Agency was a society registered under 1860 Act, if a Trial Court had granted relief by mistake or plaintiff therein succeeded in getting collusive decree, which was contrary to law, its benefit could not be extended to other similarly situated persons for reason that art. 14 of the Constitution was not meant to perpetuate an illegality -

Held, suit was wrongly decreed - It was also intriguing to note from said judgment that defendant appeared and filed written statement and admitted claim of plaintiff - Suit was which was decreed on admission of defendant against whom no relief was prayed for, and without impleading necessary party - SC was equally amazed to find that State of Punjab implemented judgment - It leaves much to be desired - It was also settled legal proposition that art. 14 of Constitution does not envisage for negative equality - In case wrong benefit was conferred upon someone inadvertently or otherwise it might not be a ground to grant similar relief to others - SC did not find any of arguments advanced by appellant worth acceptance - Appeal dismissed.
