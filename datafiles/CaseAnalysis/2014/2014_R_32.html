Bhushan Power and Steel Limited v Rajesh Verma and others

Supreme Court of India

22 April 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, Surinder Singh Nijjar

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 279; (2014) 5 SCC 551; 2014(4) ALL MR 407; 2014 (3) AWC 2968; 2014 (2) CLT(SC) 236; JT 2014 (5) SC 557; 2014(5) SCALE 458; [2014] 5 S.C.R. 493

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: (1)Contempt-Civil contempt-Contempt petition seeking enforcement of directions contained in Supreme Court's judgment dated 14.3.2012 passed in Civil Appeal No. 2790 of 2012-Respondents/ contemners do not dispute that directions contained in Apex Court's judgment have become final-State Government/contemners pleaded their helplessness by narrating certain circumstances-Held, respondents/ contemners are in contempt of orders dated 14.3.2012 passed by Supreme Court in not complying with directions-However, respondents/ contemners given one final opportunity to purge contempt by transmitting requisite recommendations to Central Government-Central Government to consider recommendations on its own merits and in accordance with law-In case recommendation is duly sent to Central Government, no further action to be taken and respondents/ contemners shall stand discharged from contempt petition-However, in case respondents/contemners do not purge in manner mentioned-Open to petitioners to point out same to Supreme Court by moving appropriate application and in that event respondents/contemners shall be proceeded against.

(2)Constitution of India-Article 32-Writ petition-Maintainability-Grant of mining lease-No fundamental right violated by non-granting of mining lease]-Petitioners cannot approach Supreme Court directly under Article 32 by filing writ petitions.
