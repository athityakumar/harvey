Anand Agro Chem. India Limited v Suresh Chandra and others

Supreme Court of India

24 January 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

C. Nagappan, T. S. Thakur

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 48; (2014) 3 SCC 631; 2014 (103) ALR 496; 2014 (123) RD 436; 2014(1) SCALE 614

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Trade

Keywords: Nonpayment of dues

Summary: Trade - Practice & Procedure - Nonpayment of dues - Legality - Respondents supplied sugarcane to appellant company for sugar mill for which appellant did not pay the price, in spite of several representations made by respondents - Respondent filed petition before DB - DB directed District Magistrate to take immediate action against the Directors and occupiers of the appellant-sugar mill - Magistrate ordered arrest of Directors and occupiers of sugar mill to recover dues and in event of such arrest, they would not be released until they have paid entire amount due against them - Aggrieved appellant prayed for stay of arrest of directors and occupiers of appellant - DB rejected appellants prayer - Hence instant appeal - Whether order recorded by DB was sustainable - Held, DB directed District Magistrate to take immediate action against Directors of sugar mill had already been affirmed by Court in appeal - Company and its Directors did not make their promises by paying even amounts which they had offered to pay - Appellant and its Directors had assured Commissioner that they would pay Rs.160 lacs towards price of sugarcane within two weeks besides amount of Rs.700 lacs to be paid in installments - No payment was made by company and its Directors - It was evident that company and its Directors have been despite promises made on their behalf committing breach of such assurances on one pretext or the other - There was nothing to suggest that company and its Directors were incapable of raising funds for liquidating outstanding liability towards dues payable to farmers - Simply because sugar factory was attached, there was no reason to assume that company or its Directors were in any financial distress thereby disabling them from making payments recoverable from them - Hence there was no reason to interfere with order passed by HC - Appeal dismissed.
