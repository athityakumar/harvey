State of Punjab v Labh Singh

Supreme Court of India

17 December 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Uday Umesh Lalit, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 864; 2015 (1) CGLJ 259; 2015 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 77; 2015 (1) MLJ(Crl) 112; 2015 (1) RCR(Criminal) 287; 2015 (1) RLW 236; 2014(14) SCALE 160; [2014] 11 S.C.R. 312

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, s. 19 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 197 - Retired govt. servant - Sanction for prosecution - Requirement - Respondent public servant allegedly caused loss to the exchequer - Appellant State sought sanction for prosecution - Department refused sanction - However, Special Court framed charge against respondent under the provisions of IPC and the Act on the premises that no sanction was required after retirement - Respondent challenged framing of charge - HC allowed petition and set aside charge - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, public servants having retired from service there was no occasion to consider grant of sanction u/s. 19 of the Act. The HC was not therefore justified in setting aside the order passed by the Special Judge insofar as charge under the Act was concerned. However as regards charges for the offences punishable under the IPC concerned the HC was absolutely right in setting aside the order of the Special Judge. Unlike s. 19 of the Act, the protection u/s. 197 of CrPC is available to the concerned public servant even after retirement. Therefore, if the matter was considered by the sanctioning authority and the sanction to prosecute was rejected first on 13-9-2000 and secondly on 24-9-2003, the court could not have taken cognizance insofar as the offences punishable under the IPC are concerned. Hence, the order under appeal passed by the HC is correct insofar as charges under IPC are concerned but must be set aside as regards charge under the Act is concerned. Appeal partly allowed.

Ratio - Sanction to prosecute the public servant for the offences under the Act is not required if the public servant has already retired on the date of cognizance by the court.
