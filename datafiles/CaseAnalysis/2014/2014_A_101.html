Sasi Enterprises v Assistant Commissioner of Income Tax

Supreme Court of India

30 January 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Radhakrishnan, A.K. Sikri

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 65; (2014) 5 SCC 139; [2014] 361 ITR 163; JT 2014 (2) SC 355; 2014 (1) MLJ(Crl) 473; 2014(1) SCALE 694; [2014] 2 S.C.R. 175; [2014] 222 TAXMAN 78

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Failure to file returns, Assessment, Legality

Summary: (A) Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Practice & Procedure - Income-tax Act, 1961, ss. 139, 139(1), 139(4), 142, 142(1)(i), 144, 148, 234A, 276, 276CC, 278E, 279(1) - Assessment - Failure to file returns - Legality - Respondent/Commissioner filed complaint before Trial Court for committing offences punishable u/s. 276 CC of 1961 Act - Appellants filed discharge petitions before Trial Court and same was dismissed - Appellants preferred revision before HC and same were dismissed - Hence instant appeals - Whether an assessee has liability/duty to file a return u/s. 139(1) of the Act within due date prescribed therein -

Held, s. 139 of 1961 Act indicated that it was mandatory on part of assessee to file return before due date - Explanation (a) to said section defines term 'due date', which was 30th November of assessment year - Consequence of non-filing of return on time had also been stipulated in the Act - Further reference to ss. 142 and 148 of the Act was also necessary to properly understand scope of s. 276CC of the Act - Appeals dismissed.

(B)Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Practice & Procedure - Income-tax Act, 1961, ss. 139, 139(1), 139(4), 142, 142(1)(i), 144, 148, 234A, 276, 276CC, 278E, 279(1) - Assessment - Returns - Liability - What was the effect of best judgment assessment u/s. 144 of the Act and would it nullify liability of assessee to file its return u/s. 139(1) of the Act -

Held, s. 139 of the Act prior to 1989-90 and after, placed statutory mandate on every person to file an income tax return in prescribed form and in prescribed manner - The 1987 Act made various amendments to 1961 Act, by which assessing officer had no power to extend time for filing return of income u/s. 139(1) of the Act and to extend time for filing u/s. 139(3) of the Act, a return of loss intended to be carried forward - Time prescribed for filing belated return u/s. 139(4) of the Act or revised return u/s. 139(5) of the Act was reduced to one year from end of relevant assessment year - Provision of s. 139(2) of the Act stood incorporated in s. 142(1)(i) of the Act - Appeals dismissed.

(C) Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Practice & Procedure - Income-tax Act, 1961, ss. 139, 139(1), 139(4), 142, 142(1)(i), 144, 148, 234A, 276, 276CC, 278E, 279(1) - Non-filing of return - Invocation of Provisions - Whether non-filing of return u/s. 139(1) of the Act, as well as non-compliance of the time prescribed u/ss. 142 and 148 of the Act are grounds for invocation of the provisions of s. 276CC of the Act -

Held, assessee was bound to file return u/s. 139(1) of the Act on or before due date - Outer limit was fixed for filing of return as 31st August of the assessment year, over and above, in present case, not only return was not filed within due date prescribed u/s. 139(1) of the Act, but also time prescribed u/s. 142 and 148 of the Act and further opportunity given to file return in prescribed time was also not availed of - S. 276CC of the Act applied to situations where an assessee had failed to file return of income as required u/s. 139 of the Act or in response to notices issued to assessee u/s. 142 or s. 148 of the Act - Proviso to s. 276CC of the Act gives some relief to genuine assesses - Proviso takes in only s.139(1) of the Act and provisions of s. 142(1)(i) or 148 of the Act were conspicuously absent - Consequently, benefit of proviso was available only to voluntary filing of return as required u/s. 139(1) of the Act - Proviso would not apply after detection of failure to file return and after a notice u/s. 142(1)(i) or 148 of the Act was issued calling for filing of return of income - Envisages filing of even belated return before detection or discovery of failure and issuance of notices u/s. 142 or 148 of the Act - Appeals dismissed.

(D) Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Practice & Procedure - Income-tax Act, 1961, ss. 139, 139(1), 139(4), 142, 142(1)(i), 144, 148, 234A, 276, 276CC, 278E, 279(1) - Assessment - Non-filing of returns - Proceedings - Legality - Whether pendency of appellate proceedings relating to assessment or non-attaining finality of assessment proceedings was a bar in initiating prosecution proceedings u/s. 276CC due to non-filing of returns -

Held, on failure to file returns by appellants, income tax department made best judgment assessment u/s. 144 of the Act and later show cause notices were issued for initiating prosecution u/s. 276CC of the Act - Proviso to s. 276CC nowhere states that offence u/ss. 276CC of the Act had not been committed by categories of assesses who fell within scope of that proviso, but it was stated that such person should not be proceeded against - It only provides that under specific circumstances subject to proviso, prosecution might not be initiated - An assessee who comes within cl. 2(b) to the proviso, no doubt has also committed offence u/s. 276CC of the Act but was exempted from prosecution since tax falls below Rs. 3,000/- - Such an assessee might file belated return before detection and avail benefit of proviso - Proviso could not control main section, it only confers some benefit to certain categories of assesses - Offence u/s. 276CC of the Act was attracted on failure to comply with provisions of s. 139(1) of the Act or failure to respond to notice issued u/s. 142 or s. 148 of the Act within time limit specified - Appeals dismissed.

(E) Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Practice & Procedure - Income-tax Act, 1961, ss. 139, 139(1), 139(4), 142, 142(1)(i), 144, 148, 234A, 276, 276CC, 278E, 279(1) - Presumption of Court - Stage - What was the scope of s. 278E of 1961 Act, and at what stage presumption could be drawn by Court -

Held, S. 278E of 1961 Act deals with presumption as to culpable mental state, which was inserted by 1986 Act - Question was on whom burden lies, either on prosecution or assessee, u/s. 278E of 1961 Act to prove whether assessee had or had not committed willful default in filing returns - Court in prosecution of offence, like s. 276CC of 1961 Act had to presume existence of mens rea and it was for appellant to prove contrary and that too beyond reasonable doubt - Resultantly, appellants had to prove circumstances which prevented them from filing returns as per s. 139(1) of 1961 Act or in response to notices u/ss. 142 and 148 of the 1961 Act - No reason to interfere with order passed by HC - Appeals dismissed.
