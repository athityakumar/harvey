Gorkha Security Services v Government of (NCT of Delhi)

Supreme Court of India

04 August 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, J. Chelameswar

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 501; (2014) 9 SCC 105; AIR 2014 SC 3371; 2014 (5) AWC 4545; 2014 (3) CLT(SC) 170; 2014 (143) FLR 591; JT 2014 (9) SC 165; 2014 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 3040; 2014 (6) MLJ 756; 2014(9) SCALE 148

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Blacklisting of firm, Failure to mention in show cause notice

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Administrative - Blacklisting of firm - Failure to mention in show cause notice - Show cause notice was given for alleged failure on part of appellant firm to commence work that was awarded to it - Respondents passed impugned order against appellant firm who violated terms and conditions of Contract Labour Laws and imposed various penalties including blacklisting upon appellant - Appellant filed petition before Single Judge challenging penalties - petition dismissed - Appellant filed appeal before DB - DB dismissed appeal and affirmed the view taken by Single Judge - Hence instant appeal - Whether action of blacklisting could be taken without specifically proposing/ contemplating such action in show cause notice -

Held, no doubt show cause notice was served upon appellant - Show cause notice was conspicuously silent about blacklisting action - On contrary, after stating in detail nature of alleged defaults and breaches of agreement committed by appellant notice specifically mentioned that because of said defaults appellant was 'as such liable to be levied the cost accordingly' - It further said 'why the action as mentioned above may not be taken against the firm, besides other action as deemed fit by the competent authority' - Main action which respondents wanted to take was to levy cost - No doubt, notice further mentioned that competent authority could take other actions as deemed fit - However, that might not fulfill requirement of putting defaulter to notice that action of blacklisting was also in mind of competent authority - Without any specific stipulation , respondent could not have imposed penalty of black listing - It was well established proposition of law that unless statutory provision either specifically or by necessary implication excluded application of any rules of natural justice, in exercise of power pre-judicially affecting another must be in conformity with the rules of natural justice - SC pointed out that no such case was set up by respondents that by omitting to state the proposed action of blacklisting, appellant in show cause notice had not caused any prejudice to appellant - Further, had action of black listing being specifically proposed in show cause notice, appellant could have mentioned as to why such extreme penalty as not justified - It could have come out with extenuating circumstances defending such an action even if defaults were there and the Department was not satisfied with the explanation qua the defaults - It could had even pleaded with Department not to blacklist appellant or do it for lesser period in case Department still wanted to black list the appellant - Therefore, it was not at all acceptable that non mentioning of proposed blacklisting in show cause notice had not caused any prejudice to appellant - Further, extreme nature of such harsh penalty like blacklisting with severe consequences, would itself amount to causing prejudice to appellant - Thus, impugned judgment of HC did not decide issue in correct prospective - Impugned order passed by respondents blacklisting appellant without giving appellant notice thereto, was contrary to principles of natural justice as it was not specifically proposed and, therefore, there was no show cause notice given to this effect before taking action of blacklisting against appellant - Therefore, impugned action of blacklisting appellant was set aside an d quashed - Appeals allowed.
