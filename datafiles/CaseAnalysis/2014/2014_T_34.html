North Eastern Railway and others v Tripple Engineering Works

Supreme Court of India

13 August 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjan Gogoi, M.Y. Eqbal

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 520; (2014) 9 SCC 288; AIR 2014 SC 3506; 2014 (6) ALD(SC) 133; 2015 (108) ALR 231; 2014 (5) AWC 5329; 2014 (3) CLT(SC) 207; JT 2014 (9) SC 220; 2014 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 3492; 2015 (1) MahLJ 548; 2015 (1) MPLJ 248; 2014 (4) RCR(Civil) 176; 2014(9) SCALE 351

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Summary: Arbitrator & ADR - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, s. 11 - Appointment of arbitrator - Contracts awarded to the respondent-contractor were terminated on 7-11-1994 - General Conditions of Contract of the Railways, which included an arbitration clause, governed the parties - After the termination of the two contracts the respondent-contractor approached the HC challenging the terminations - Though a panel of arbitrators as per Cls. 64(3)(a)(ii) and (iii) of the General Conditions of Contract was appointed as far back as in the year 1996, till date the award(s) in respect of the disputes arising out of either of the two contracts was yet to be passed - HC appointed a retired Chief Justice as the arbitrator to resolve the disputes and differences between the parties - Hence, instant appeal - Whether HC was justified in appointing retired Chief Justice as the arbitrator to resolve the disputes and differences between the parties -

Held, power of the Court under the Act had to be exercised to effectuate the remedy provided thereunder and to facilitate the mechanism contemplated therein - Even if the arbitration agreement was to specifically provide for any particular qualification(s) of an arbitrator the same would not denude the power of the Court acting u/s. 11(6) of the Act, in an appropriate case to depart therefrom - In the instant case, cls. 64(3)(a)(ii) and (iii) of the General Conditions of Contract did not prescribe any specific qualification of the arbitrators that were to be appointed under the agreement except that they should be railway officers - Further, as period of nearly two decades elapsed since the contractor had raised his claims for alleged wrongful termination of the two contracts - No infirmity or failure of justice could be said to be occasioned by the order passed by the HC so as to warrant any interference - Appeal dismissed.
