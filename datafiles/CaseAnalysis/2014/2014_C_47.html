Collector of Land Acquisition and others v Andaman Timber Industries

Supreme Court of India

11 December 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, C. Nagappan

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 853; JT 2014 (13) SC 598; 2014(14) SCALE 42

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Displaced Persons (Compensation and Rehabilitation) Rules, 1955, Andaman and Nicobar Islands, Land Revenue and Land Reforms Regulation, 1966, Modification of judgment

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, s. 4(1) - Andaman and Nicobar Islands, Land Revenue and Land Reforms Regulation, 1966, s. 146(ii) - Supreme Court Rules, 1966, O. 40 - Mistake of fact - Modification of judgment - Legality - Land in question was allotted in faovur of respondent (licence) u/cl. (ii) of S. 146 of the Regulations for a period of 30 years under Form AG-3 - Land acquisition notification u/s. 4(1) of the Act was issued - Against the notification a petition was filed before HC, which was allowed - Appeal preferred by appellants, was dismissed on merits and cross-objections filed by respondent was allowed and said judgment and order of DB was affirmed by SC vide order dt.28-11-2013 by recording its reasons - Appellant filed instant application to modify the said order in appeal - Appellant contended that the mistake committed by Land Acquisition Collector in passing the award which was contrary to the acquisition notification, was neither brought to the notice of Single Judge and DB nor SC, which was a mistake on the part of the appellants.

Held, s. 143(a) and (b) of the Regulation, clearly state that a person granted licence u/cl. (ii) of s. 146 of the Regulation, with respect to any agricultural land is a licensee or a non-occupancy tenant. Therefore, the Condition No. 6 clearly states that the licence granted on the land by the Govt. can be cancelled and resumed by it. On careful perusal of the acquisition notifications, it is made very clear that acquisition is only in respect of buildings and structure existing on the land in respect of which licence right has been granted in favour of the respondent for a specified period. These facts were not noticed by the Land Acquisition Collector at the time of passing of the award. The award was passed in respect of the land, the buildings and structures which is not permissible in law and compensation of Rs.3,03,03,567/- awarded in favour of the respondent, for which he is not entitled to in law, is the legal ground urged on behalf of the appellants by highlighting various provisions of the Regulation, 1966, along with the licence granted in favour of the respondent. However, the said part of the award has been complied with by paying the compensation amount to the workmen working in the factory of the respondent in pursuance of the award passed by the Land Acquisition Collector though he is not entitled to the same as per law. The said fact was not brought to the notice of the DB of HC and SC at the time of hearing. Therefore, the appellant has rightly contended that it is a mistake of fact. A factual mistake has been committed by SC in affirming the order of the HC in issuing a writ of mandamus to the appellants for its compliance by holding that the extent of land notified in the acquisition notifications are not passed because neither the acquisition proceedings of the land have lapsed nor the possession of the land was taken by the Govt. from the respondent. Therefore, the order passed by the HC for issuing a writ of mandamus for payment of the compensation to the respondent in respect of the land has also been affirmed by the DB of the HC and SC in the civil appeal by passing the judgment and the same is sought to be modified by the appellants by filing the application. The procedure prescribed under the Rules, for the purpose of review of the judgment and order of SC on either facts or error in law, which is apparent on the face of the record, has to be followed. Therefore, Cine Exhibition Private Limited and others v. Collector, District Gwalior and others, 2013 Indlaw SCO 48, shall be followed. Appellants are given liberty to file review petition within 6 weeks along with condonation of delay application within 6 weeks. Order accordingly.
