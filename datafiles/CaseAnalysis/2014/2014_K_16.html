E. Bapanaiah v K. S. Raju

Supreme Court of India

07 November 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Prafulla C. Pant, Vikramajit Sen

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 759; (2015) 1 SCC 451; 2014 (4) BC 644; [2015] 191 Comp Cas 246; 2015 (4) CompLJ 67; 2015 CRLJ 567; 2015 (1) DRTC 153; 2015 (2) JCC 898; JT 2014 (12) SC 552; 2014 (8) MLJ 502; 2014 (4) RCR(Criminal) 914B; 2014(12) SCALE 569

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Conviction, Disobedience, Special Court (Trial Of Offences Relating To Transactions In Securities) Act, 1992, Companies (Second Amendment) Act, 2002

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Contempt of Courts Act, 1971, ss. 10, 12 - Companies Act, 1956, s. 634A - Contempt of court - Conviction - Legality - Appellant (petitioner/depositor) filed contempt petition u/s. 12 r/w s. 10 of the Act against respondents (contemnors) before HC, for alleged willful disobedience of order passed by Company Law Board and for breach of undertakings/affidavits - Single Judge found respondent guilty of contempt and convicted them to undergo imprisonment for 6 months - Aggrieved respondent filed an appeal, which was allowed by impugned judgment - Hence, instant appeal - Whether DB rightly allowed the contempt appeal or not.

Held, it is clear that respondent was not only the Promoter Director of Finance Company, but the Managing Director of said Company, working for a decade, was his nominee, and practically all the powers to run the compay vested with respondent, the Promoter Director, and his nominees, whom he appointed u/arts. 104 and 140 of Articles of Association. The DB erred in law in holding that respondent was not guilty of willful disobedience of the order of the Company Law Board. After giving undertaking, respondent submitted his resignation, which clearly reflects that the same was done in order to save himself and his company, from making the repayment directed to be made by the Company Law Board, and thereby dishonestly made attempt in not making repayment to the depositor (appellant). Case relates to a civil contempt wherein an undertaking given to Company Law Board is breached. Normally, the general provisions made under the Act are not invoked by the HCs for forcing a party to obey orders passed by its subordinate courts for the simple reason that there are provisions contained in CPC to get executed its orders and decrees. It is settled principle of law that where there are special law and general law, the provisions of special law would prevail over general law. As such, in normal circumstances a decree holder cannot take recourse of the Act else it is sure to throw open a floodgate of litigation under contempt jurisdiction. It is not the object of the Act to make decree holders rush to the HCs simply for the reason that the decree passed by the subordinate court is not obeyed. However, there is no such procedure prescribed to execute order of Company Law Board particularly after proviso is added to s. 634A of 1956 Act, vide Companies (Second Amendment) Act, 2002. Respondent willfully disobeyed the order of Company Law Board and breached the undertaking given to Company Law Board, and thereby committed Contempt of Court subordinate to HC as such the DB has erred in law in allowing the appeal filed by respondent and setting aside his conviction and sentence, recorded against him by the Single Judge. The extent of conviction and sentence recorded against respondent stands restored. Impugned judgment was set aside. Appeal allowed.
