Executive Engineer, Road Development Division No. III, Panvel and another v Atlanta Limited

Supreme Court of India

16 January 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Jagdish Singh Khehar, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 29; (2014) 11 SCC 619; AIR 2014 SC 1093; 2014 (2) ALD(SC) 118; 2014(2) ALL MR 479; 2014 (2) AWC 1295; 2014 (1) CLT(SC) 209; 2014 (4) CompLJ 205; JT 2014 (2) SC 1; 2014(2) M.P.H.T. 114; 2014(1) SCALE 277; [2014] 1 S.C.R. 507; 2014 (1) WLN(SC) 109

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Civil Procedure

Summary: (1) Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996-Sections 2 (1) (e), 34 and 42-Arbitration award-Setting aside-Jurisdiction to adjudicate arbitral dispute arising between rival parties-Determination of jurisdiction-Challenge to arbitration award wherein jurisdiction lies with more than one court-Both High Court on original side and district court had jurisdiction in respect of subject-matter in issue-Not case of appellants that because of pecuniary dimensions, and/or any other considerations, jurisdiction in two alternatives mentioned above, would lie in district court-Submissions advanced at behest of appellants on issue of jurisdiction are submissions without reference to any principles known to law-Not open to appellants to canvass that High Court in exercise of its "ordinary original civil jurisdiction" could not adjudicate upon present controversy on account of lack of jurisdiction-For determination of present controversy reliance cannot be placed on Section 42-Under scheme of provisions of Arbitration Act, if choice is between High Court on one hand and district court on the other, Section 2 (1) (e) has made choice in favour of High Court-In case option is to be exercised between High Court on one hand and district court on the other-Choice under Arbitration Act has to be exercised in favour of High Court-Legislative choice clearly in favour of High Court-Therefore, matters have to be adjudicated upon by High Court alone-Impugned order of High Court requiring matters to be adjudicated on "ordinary original civilside" by High Court-Upheld-District court directed to transfer miscellaneous applications of appellants filed for seeking quashment and setting aside of arbitral award to High Court, for disposal in accordance with law. (2) Jurisdiction-Question of jurisdiction-Is pure question of law-It needs to be adjudicated only on basis of statutory provisions.
