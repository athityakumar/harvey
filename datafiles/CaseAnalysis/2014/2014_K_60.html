Rajni Rani and another v Khairati Lal and others

Supreme Court of India

14 October 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, V. Gopala Gowda

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 701; (2015) 2 SCC 682; 2015 (1) ALD(SC) 13; 2014(6) ALL MR 895; 2014 (107) ALR 729; 2015 (1) ALT(SC) 11; 2015 (1) AWC 329; 2014 (4) CGLJ 624; 2014 (4) CLT(SC) 461; 2014 (6) CTC 122; JT 2014 (12) SC 405; 2014 (4) KLT 261; 2014 (5) Law Herald (P&H) 3836; 2015 (5) MahLJ 526; 2015 (4) MPLJ 12; 2015 (1) OLR 47; 2014 (4) RCR(Civil) 850; 2014 (2) RCR(Rent) 378; 2014(12) SCALE 29; [2014] 10 S.C.R. 971; 2014 (4) WLN(SC) 194

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Keywords: Legality, Dismissal of counter claim

Summary: Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 2 r.2 - Constitution of India, 1950, art, 227 - Dismissal of counter claim - Legality - Judgment and decree passed in suit was assailed in appeal and Appellate Court modified judgment and decree - One of sisters filed declaratory suit to effect that she was owner in possession of land in respect of 1/9th share in the suit land and in said suit - Counter-claim was filed by defendant Nos. 12 to 14 stated that they had become owners in possession of suit property on basis of properly registered Will - Trial Court adverted to lis in first suit, factum of not raising the plea with regard to Will in the earlier suit and came to hold that counter-claim could not be advanced solely on the ground that the existence of the Will had come to the knowledge of the defendants only in the year 2003. Trial Court allowed the application filed by defendant Nos. 1 and 2 and resultantly dismissed the counter-claim filed by the defendant Nos. 12 to 14 - Revision before HC - Revision was dismissed by HC - Hence instant appeal - Whether order of dismissal of counter-claim being barred by principles of O. 2 r. 2 CPC.

Held, when opinion is expressed holding that the counter-claim is barred by principles of O. 2 r. 2 CPC it indubitably adjudicates the controversy as regards the substantive right of the defendants who had lodged the counter-claim. It cannot be regarded as an ancillary or incidental finding recorded in the suit. Court may draw up formal decree or may not, but if by virtue of the order of the Court, the rights have finally been adjudicated, irrefutably it would assume the status of a decree. As is evincible, in instant case, counter-claim which is in the nature of a cross-suit has been dismissed. Nothing else survives for the defendants who had filed the counter-claim. Therefore, there was no hesitation in holding that the order passed by Trial Court has the status of decree and challenge to the same has to be made before the appropriate forum where appeal could lay by paying the requisite fee. It could not have been unsettled by HC in exercise of the power u/art. 227 of the Constitution of India and order passed by the High Court is indefensible. Thus, order passed by HC is set aside. Appeal allowed.
