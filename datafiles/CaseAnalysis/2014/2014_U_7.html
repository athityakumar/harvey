Union of India and another v Jai Kishun Singh (Dead) Through Lrs. and others

Supreme Court of India

10 September 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Arun Mishra, Vikramajit Sen

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 611; (2014) 10 SCC 352; 2014 (6) ALD(SC) 193; 2014(6) ALL MR 438; JT 2014 (10) SC 249; 2014(10) SCALE 422; [2014] 8 S.C.R. 1149; 2014 (6) SLR 343

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative

Keywords: Swatantrata Sainik Samman Pension Scheme, 1980

Summary: Socio-Economic - Freedom fighter pension - Entitlement of - Respondent No. 1 was declined for granting freedom fighters pension by appellant - Respondent No.1 unsuccessfully impugned the said order in writ petition filed before Single Judge - However, DB of the HC in the appeal has quashed the order - Hence instant appeal - Whether Freedom Fighter pension had been undeservingly extended to respondent No.1 inspite of the fact that he did not participate in freedom struggle as he was a child of 7 to 8 years in the year 1942 -

Held, freedom fighter pension is a form of gratitude extended by an indebted nation in recognition of the sacrifice made by the freedom fighters to achieving independence. Liberal approach has to be adopted in such matters so that rightful persons are not deprived of deserving benefit for lack of evidence, after a lapse of long time. SC, unable to rely upon determination of age by the Medical Board as it is based upon physical appearance only and not based upon any scientific medical test like ossification test and radiological examination. When it is based on such scientific tests as laid down in Om Prakash v. State of Rajasthan & Anr. 2012 Indlaw SC 126, it is of strong corroborative value. Medical Board held on 11-4-2002 has opined on physical appearance basis age about 73 years. It is not based on scientific tests hence it cannot be accepted, more so in view of other documentary evidence on record. Judgment and order of the DB is set aside. As the respondent has been wrongly allowed pension after rejecting claim at the first instance by appellant, the HC has directed suo motu inquiry and on this basis pension had been withdrawn, and also considering the fact that the recipient of pension respondent No. 1 has died, we direct that the amount paid to him shall not be recovered from his legal representatives. Appeal allowed.
