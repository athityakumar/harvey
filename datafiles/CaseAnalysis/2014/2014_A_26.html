Ahmedabad Municipal Corporation and others v Ahmedabad Green Belt Khedut Mandal and others

Supreme Court of India

09 May 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, J. Chelameswar, M.Y. Eqbal

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 358; (2014) 7 SCC 357; AIR 2014 SC 2377; 2014(4) ALL MR 936; 2014 (3) G.L.R. 2516; JT 2014 (6) SC 401; 2015 (1) RLW 887; 2014(6) SCALE 630; [2014] 11 S.C.R. 855

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property

Keywords: Improvements, Bombay Town Planning Act, 1915

Summary: Constitution - Land & Property - Gujarat Town Planning and Urban Development Act, 1976, s. 40(3)(jj) - Bombay Town Planning Act, 1964 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 19 and 300-A - Validity and issues of interpretation of s. 40(3)(jj) of 1964 Act - In 1963, Ahmedabad Municipal Corporation (AMC) prepared and submitted a development plan under 1964 Act whereby the lands of the respondents known as the 'green belt' were kept for open space and recreation - AMC prepared its revised development plan whereby lands of the respondents were reserved for 'public housing' - 1964 Act was replaced by 1976 Act under which AUDA was alone competent to draft development plan - AUDA prepared draft revised development plan - Land reserved for 'public housing for different government organizations' was de-reserved and put under the category as 'restricted residential utility services and other uses zones' - AUDA in exercise of the powers u/s. 21 of 1976 Act came out with a draft revised development plan in the year 1998 - Respondents filed a Writ Petition challenging the draft revised development plan and for direction to the appellants to acquire their lands as per the plan of 1987 within a period of 6 months failing which the plan would lapse - 1976 Act was amended and s. 40(3)(jj) of 1976 Act was inserted - Said writ petition was partly allowed by the HC - Hence, these appeals - Whether the provisions contained in s. 40(3)(jj) of 1976 Act were ultra- vires of arts 14, 19 and 300-A of the Constitution -

Held, s. 40(3)(jj) of 1976 Act only regulates discretion of the Area Development Authority (ADA) while making the draft development plan - Land acquired u/s. 20 r/w s. 12 of 1976 Act would need infrastructural facility and the original plot which was acquired would require to be re-constituted as a final plot and to make a building site - The settled legal proposition in respect of interpretation of statute was that the provisions of the 1976 Act have to be read as a whole and therefore the provision of s. 40(3)(jj)(a)(iv) of 1976 Act for sale had to be read inconsonance/conjointly with the other statutory provisions and not in isolation - Sale upto the extent of 15% was from the total area covered under the scheme and not in respect of every plot of land - In order to generate financial resources for the development of infrastructure, the saleable plot for residential, commercial and industrial use were allotted by the appropriate authority - Similarly, while re-constituting the plots, final plot was offered to the original owner for its beneficial use - HC had committed an error interpreting the provisions under challenge as it failed to appreciate that the provisions of the Town Planning Scheme in Chapter-V, nowhere indicate that the lands u/s. 20 of 1976 Act could not be subject matter of the Town Planning Scheme - Interpretation given by the HC runs contrary to the intention under the scheme and may frustrate the scheme itself as in the pockets left out in the scheme the basic amenities may not be available - The result would be that a portion of the land would be left without infrastructural facility while the adjacent area belonging to neighbours would be provided infrastructural facility - In the instant Transferred Case, as the authority was only dealing with the issues at a draft stage and the applicants have ample opportunity to file their objections and were entitled to personal hearing, the matter could be adjudicated before the statutory authority - Apprehensions raised by the applicants at this stage were pre-mature - Appeals disposed of.
