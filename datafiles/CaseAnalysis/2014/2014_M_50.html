Manjeet Singh v State of Himachal Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

25 April 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 301; (2014) 5 SCC 697; 2014 CRLJ 3970; JT 2014 (7) SC 365; 2014 (4) RCR(Criminal) 260; 2014(5) SCALE 648; [2014] 5 S.C.R. 796

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 313 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 300, 302, 304 and 324 - Murder - Conviction - Challenged - Prosecution alleged appellant murdered deceased - Trial Court convicted the appellant and sentenced him to imprisonment for life and also to pay fine of Rs.5,000/- for the offence u/s. 302 of IPC - Appellant was also sentenced by the Trial Court for the offence u/s. 324 of IPC to undergo imprisonment for 6 months and to pay fine of Rs.500/- - On appeal, HC affirmed the order of the Trial Court - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order of the Trial Court as affirmed by the HC could be upheld -

Held, evidence produced against the appellant does not show that the appellant had any motive to cause death of the deceased or have intended to cause such bodily injuries which were sufficient in the ordinary course of nature to cause the death of the deceased - Evidence on record also does not establish that the injuries caused on the body of the deceased must in all probability cause his death or likely to cause his death - On the spur of the moment, during the heat of exchange of words appellant caused injuries on the body of the deceased which caused his death - Therefore, the ingredients of the murder as defined in s. 300 of IPC, have not been established against the appellant - Appellant was guilty of culpable homicide not amounting to murder u/s. 304 of IPC, and considering the fact that the appellant had no intention to either cause the death of the deceased or cause such bodily injury as was likely to cause death of the deceased, it would be sufficient to impose on appellant a sentence of 7 years rigorous imprisonment and to impose on him a fine of Rs.5,000/- - Conviction of the appellant u/s. 302 of IPC was set aside but hold him guilty of the offence u/s. 304 of IPC and sentence him to 7 years rigorous imprisonment and fine of Rs.5,000/- - Appeal disposed of.
