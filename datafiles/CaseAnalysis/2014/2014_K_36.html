Leela Rajagopal and others v Kamala Menon Cocharan and others

Supreme Court of India

08 September 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjan Gogoi, R.K. Agrawal

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 597; AIR 2015 SC 107; 2015 (2) ALD(SC) 78; 2014(6) ALL MR 419; 2014 (107) ALR 328; 2014 (6) ALT(SC) 58; 2014 (6) AWC 5450; 2014 (3) CLT(SC) 182; 2014 (6) CTC 80; JT 2014 (10) SC 111; 2014 (5) Law Herald (P&H) 4043; 2015 (126) RD 218; 2014(10) SCALE 307; [2014] 7 S.C.R. 697; 2015 (3) WLN(SC) 13

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Keywords: Probation of Will

Summary: Family & Personal - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Indian Succession Act, 1925, ss. 281, 282 - Probation of Will - Legality - Plaintiff/respondent no. 1 filed suit before Trial Court for probation of Will - Trial Court concluded that Will ought not to be accepted as valid instrument executed on free will and volition of the testator - On appeal before HC - HC reversed findings of Trial Court - Hence instant appeal -

Held, a close reading of Will indicates its clear language, and its unambiguous purport and effect. The mind of testator is clearly discernible and reasons for exclusion of sons are apparent from the Will itself. The participation of respondent no. 1 in execution and registration of Will cannot be said to be circumstance that would warrant an adverse conclusion. Conduct of respondent no. 1 in summoning her friend PW3 to be an attesting witness and in taking the testator to the office of Sub Registrar should, again, not warrant any adverse conclusion. It also cannot escape notice that Will dtd. 11/1/1982 is identical with contents of earlier Will dtd. 28/12/1981. Non-production of original Will and reliance on certified copy thereof is circumstance which has been reasonably explained by respondent no. 1/plaintiff. Original Will, after its execution on 11/1/1982, was in custody of testator and it is only on day or her death that respondent no. 1 could find that Will was missing from envelope marked 'KPP Will'. Stand of plaintiff that original Will was lost while in custody of her mother and her knowledge of such loss on day of her mother's death cannot be disbelieved merely because no report in this regard was lodged before the police.SC cannot find any fault with conclusions reached by HC while reversing judgment of Trial Court - An appeal to HC can be lodged only upon grant of special leave to appeal would indicate highly circumscribed nature of jurisdiction of HC. In contrast to statutory appeal, an appeal lodged upon grant of special leave pursuant to a provision of the Constitution would call for highly economic exercise of power which though wide to strike at injustice wherever it occurs must display highly judicious application thereof. Determination of facts made by HC sitting as Appellate Court or even while concurring as second Appellate Court would not be reopened unless same give rise to questions of law that require a serious debate or discloses wholly unacceptable conclusions of fact which plainly demonstrate a travesty of justice. Appreciation or re-appreciation of evidence must come to a halt at some stage of the judicial proceedings and cannot percolate to Constitutional Court exercising jurisdiction u/art. 136 of the Constitution. Order passed by DB was affirmed. Appeals dismissed.
