Bangalore Turf Club Limited and others v Regional Director, Employees State Insurance Corporation and others

Supreme Court of India

31 July 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Dattu, R.K. Agrawal, Arun Mishra

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 548; (2014) 9 SCC 657; AIR 2015 SC 221; 2014 (104) ALR 170; 2014 (142) FLR 946; JT 2014 (9) SC 266; 2015 (1) MahLJ 646; 2015 (1) MPLJ 318; 2014(9) SCALE 177; [2014] 8 S.C.R. 1021

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Race-club

Summary: Administrative - Practice & Procedure - Employees State Insurance Act, 1948, ss. 1(4), 1(5) - Race-club - Establishment - Legality - Instant matter was referred to three-Judge Bench of SC as two-Judge Bench of SC was of view that decision of two-Judge Bench might require reconsideration where in it was observed that 'race-club' was an 'establishment' within meaning of said expression as used u/s. 1(5) of the Act - Whether a race-club would fall under scope of definition of the word 'shop', for purposes of notification issued u/s. 1 (5) of 1948 Act -

Held, it could be said that 'shop' was a business establishment where systematic or organized commercial activity takes place with regard to sale or purchase of goods or services, and includes an establishment that facilitates above transaction as well - Word 'shop' was not defined either in the Act or in notification - Definition of shop which meant a house or building where goods were sold or purchased has now undergone great change - Word 'shop' occurring in notification was used in larger sense than its ordinary meaning - What was required was systematic economic or commercial activity and that was sufficient to bring that place within sphere of a 'shop' - In view of fact that an 'establishment' has been found to be a place of business and further that a 'shop' was a business establishment, it could be said that a 'shop' was indeed covered under, and might be called a sub-set of, the term 'establishment' - Scheme and context of the Act must be given due consideration by SC - A narrow meaning should not be attached to words used in the Act - SC should bear in mind that the Act seeks to insure employees of covered establishments against various risks to their life, health and well-being and places said charge upon employer - Activities of appellant/Turf Clubs was in nature of organized and systematic transactions, and further that said Turf Clubs provide services to members as well as public in lieu of consideration - Appellant/Turf Clubs were 'shop' for purpose of extending benefits under the Act - A 'race-club' was an 'establishment' - Appellant/Turf Clubs were duly covered under term 'shop' for purposes of the Act and notifications issued - Aforementioned were only two issues that arise in matter pertaining to Bangalore Turf Club Ltd., and as a consequence were only issues dealt with in present reference - Appeal disposed of.
