Amrit Banaspati Company Limited v Commissioner of Wealth-Tax, Ghaziabad

Supreme Court of India

30 June 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya, Kurian Joseph

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 539; AIR 2014 SC 3425; [2014] 365 ITR 515; JT 2014 (9) SC 196; 2014(8) SCALE 31; [2014] 8 S.C.R. 46; [2014] 226 TAXMAN 147

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Assessment

Summary: Income Tax & direct Taxes - Wealth-tax Act, 1957, ss. 16A, 2(m), 35, 7, 7(1) - Wealth Tax (Second Amendment) Rules, 1989 - Wealth Tax Rules, 1957, r. 1BB - Wealth Tax - Returns - Assessment - Legality - Instant appeal was filed against order passed by HC wherein HC upheld order passed by Tribunal - Whether impugned order4 passed by HC was justified -

Held, word 'practicable' was to be construed widely - In present context if in opinion of AO, if value determined by tax payer on basis of r. 3 to 7 of the Rules was absurd or has no correlation to fair market value or otherwise not practicable, it was open to AO to invoke r. 8 of Schedule III and determine value of asset either u/r. 20 of the Rule or refer u/s. 16A of the Act for determination of valuation of asset - It was true that invocation of r. 8(a) of the Rules could not based on ipsi dipsi of the AO - AO refused to accept self-assessment as there was wide variation between market value and valuation done by assessee as per municipal taxes - Property was used as guest house - Value for levy of municipal tax was very low, as total ratable value of assessee was done by municipal authorities @ Rs.6,573/- per annum - Assessee was tenant of property @ Rs.500/- per month - After purchase of property a lot of expenditure was incurred from time to time on improvement of property which was very difficult to ascertain - Value of building was grossly understated as assessee himself entered into an agreement to sell same in year 1995 for sum of Rs. 10,26,00,000/- - Considering above factors, AO assessed value of property at Rs. 2,60,73,000/- as valued by Departmental Valuation Officer - CWT held that reference made by AO to Departmental Valuation Officer was justified - Tribunal also justified Action of AO and on appeal, same was affirmed by HC vide impugned judgment - AO was justified in holding that it was not practicable to apply r. 3 of the Rules in instant case and rightly referred matter to Valuation Officer u/s. 16A of the Act for determination of value of asset - AO rightly assessed wealth tax on basis of such value determined by Valuation Officer - Appeal dismissed.
