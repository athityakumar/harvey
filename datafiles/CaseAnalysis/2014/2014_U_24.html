Union of India and others v Hindustan Zinc Limited

Supreme Court of India

06 May 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 331; 2014 (303) E.L.T. 321; JT 2014 (7) SC 565; 2014(6) SCALE 750; [2014] 6 S.C.R. 287

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Modvat Credit Rules, Central Excise Rules, 2000

Summary: Excise - Indirect Tax - Central Excise Rules, 1944, r. 57CC - Central Excise Rules, 2000, 57AD - Cenvat Credit Rules, 2004, r. 6 - Modvat Cenvat Credit - Liability - Appellant/Revenue filed instant appeals in regard to entitlement of respondents /assessees to Modvat/ Cenvat Credit for use of inputs in manufacture of final products which were exempt or subject to nil rate of duty and requirement of assessee to maintain separate accounts with respect to inputs used in dutiable goods as well as exempted goods and liability arose on failure of assessee to maintain such separate accounts - Whether assessee were liable to pay 8% excise duty as amount u/r. 57CC of the 1944 Rules or 57AD of the 2000 Rules or r. 6 of the 2004 Rules - on the value of by-product namely sulphuric acid which was cleared to fertilizer plants under exemption in terms of bonds executed by fertilizer plants -

Held, question of intention of assessee to manufacture the exempted product was not relevant - It may be intended or unintended but if what results in the course of a manufacturing process was a 'final product' falling within the meaning of the said provisions - Provisions would apply in full with the attendant consequences - Assessee also argued that r. 57D use the words 'waste and refuse' alongwith 'by-products' - Word 'by-product' would necessarily have to take its colour and meaning from the accompanying words 'waste and refuse' - 'By-products' could not, in any event, mean 'final products' - R. 57D means that Modvat Credit could not be denied on the ground that in the course of manufacture, non-excisable goods also arise - Provisions of r. 57CC could not be read in isolation - In order to understand the scheme of Modvat Credit contained in r. 57CC of Rules, a combined reading of rr. 57A, 57B and 57D alongwith r. 57CC of Rules becomes inevitable - It could be easily discerned from a combined reading of the aforesaid provisions that the terms used are 'inputs', 'final products', 'by-product', 'waste products' etc. - SC was of the opinion that these terms were used taking into account commercial reality in trade - In that context when we scan through r. 57 CC of Rules, reference to final product being manufactured with the same common inputs becomes understandable - Said r. 57CC of Rules did not talk about emergence of final product and a by-product and still said that Rule 57 CC would apply - Appellant sought to apply r. 57CC of Rules when r. 57D of Rules does not talk about application of r. 57CC of Rules to final product and by-product when the by-product emerged as a technological necessity - Accepting the argument of appellant would amount to equating by-product and final product thereby obliterating the difference though recognised by the legislation itself - In the writ petition even the vires of Rule 57 CC were challenged - That was a reason because of which writ petitions were entertained, and rightly so, it was a different matter that while interpreting the rule, the HC chose to read down the said rule and to give an interpretation which would save it from the vice of unconstitutionality - Moreover, other statutory appeal filed by the Department was against the order of CESTAT, which involved same question - Appeals dismissed.
