G. Sundarrajan v Union of India and others

Supreme Court of India

08 May 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan, Vikramajit Sen

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 339; (2014) 6 SCC 776; 2014(6) SCALE 536

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Administrative - Practice & Procedure - Direction - Non Compliance - Sustainability - SC, while disposing of one case gave 15 directions for due compliance by AERB, NPCIL, DAE, MoEF, TNPCB, State of Tamil Nadu, etc. - Complaining that those directions were not complied with, petitioner filed Writ Petition before HC praying for a declaration that the clearance granted by AERB for 'First Approach to Criticality' (FAC) of Unit 1 of Kudankulam Nuclear Power Project should be declared as null and void - Writ Petition was heard along with few other writ petitions and the same were disposed of by a common judgment dt. 29-7-2013, against which the petitioner in Writ Petition came up with this Special Leave Petition - Petitioner had also moved appeal for a direction to the respondents not to commission the Kudankulam Nuclear Plant till each of the 15 directions given by SC was complied with and till they were properly verified by an independent expert committee appointed by SC - Whether non compliance of directions issued by SC was maintainable -

Held, after perusing the various affidavits filed by the respondents, SC noticed that the directions given by it were being properly addressed by respondents and there was no laxity on the part of the respondents in not carrying out various directions of SC - For full implementation of directions, evidently, it might take some more time and SC was sure that the respondents would make earnest efforts to give effect to all the directions of SC in letter and spirit - Petitioner, submitted that a team headed by a former Chairman of the AERB should be constituted to examine as to whether these directions were being properly implemented or not - SC found it unnecessary to appoint any Committee at instant stage since the status report and the affidavits indicate that the respondents were taking necessary steps so as to give effect to various directions, even though some of the directions were yet to be fulfilled, which naturally would take some more time - At the moment, SC found no reason to give any further directions - Petition disposed of.
