Brihanmumbai Electric Supply and Transport Undertaking v Mahrashtra Electricity Regulatory Commission (MERC) and others

Supreme Court of India

08 May 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, Surinder Singh Nijjar

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 433; (2015) 2 SCC 438; AIR 2015 SC 1224; 2014(4) ALL MR 957; 2014 (3) Bom.C.R. 481; 2014 (3) CLT(SC) 64; JT 2014 (6) SC 421; 2014(6) SCALE 556; [2014] 6 S.C.R. 50

Case Digest

Subject: Electricity & Energy

Keywords: Supply of electricity

Summary: Electricity & Energy - Electricity Act, 2003, ss. 12, 125, 14, 15, 16, 172(b), 2(15), 3, 42, 42(3), 42(5), 43, 43(1), 49, 51 - Supply of electricity - Rejection - Legality - Respondent no. 3/consumer of electricity whose premises was situated within area of supply of appellant/Brihanmumbai Electricity Supply and Transport Undertaking (BEST) - Respondent no. 3 approached respondent no. 2/Tata Power Company Ltd (TPC) and requested for supply of electricity - Respondent no. 2 vide letter asked to approach appellant for its permission - Respondent no .3 requested appellant to give permission and the same was rejected - Respondent no. 3 filed petition before Mumbai Electricity Regulatory Commission and the same was allowed - Appellant challenged order passed by Regulatory Commission by filing appeal before Tribunal and the same was dismissed affirming direction of Regulatory Commission - Hence instant appeal - Whether impugned order passed by Tribunal was justified -

Held, respondent no. 2 has successfully refuted submission by pointing out that these words in s. 43(1) of the Act were required to be read in context in which they appear - Said words were inserted in section by way of an amendment to the Act in 2007 - An 'Explanation' to s. 43(1) of the Act was added by same amendment providing that 'application' by a consumer in s. 43(1) of the Act means an application complete in all respects along with documents showing payment of necessary charges and other compliances, meaning that obligation of distribution licensee to supply within specified time period would begin only after it has received such completed application by applicant - SS. 45, 46 of the Act provide for distribution licensee's powers to recover charges for electricity supplied and expenditure incurred in providing electric line or plant for giving supply - S. 47 of the Act provides that distribution licensee might require any person demanding electricity supply from him to give reasonable security, failing which distribution licensee might refuse to give supply of electricity to such consumer - SC opined that it was in this context that expression 'save as otherwise provided in this Act' in s. 43 (1) of the Act was to be construed - There was dispute between respondent no. 2 and R-infra/respondent no. 9 which was subject matter of Civil appeal - Respondent no. 9 was distribution licensee in suburban Bombay where respondent no. 2 was also licensee - Both supply electricity to different consumers - Dispute was between them with regard to cross subsidiary surcharge payable by consumer taking supply from respondent no. 2 or respondent no. 9 - Civil appeal should be decided on its own merits - SC did not find merit in any of contentions of appellant - Appeal dismissed.
