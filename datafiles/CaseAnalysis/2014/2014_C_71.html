Shree Ram Urban Infrastructure Limited (Formerly Known as Shree Ram Mills Limited) v Court Receiver, High Court of Bombay

Supreme Court of India

09 May 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Pinaki Chandra Ghose, Chandramauli Kumar Prasad

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 357; (2015) 5 SCC 539; AIR 2014 SC 2286; 2014 (4) ALD(SC) 186; 2014(4) ALL MR 911; 2014 (105) ALR 743; 2014 (6) AWC 5630; 2014 (5) Bom.C.R. 41; 2014 (3) CGLJ 53; 2014 (2) CLT(SC) 313; JT 2014 (14) SC 514; 2014 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 2569; 2014 (3) RCR(Civil) 315; 2014 (125) RD 224; 2014 (4) RLW 3476; 2014(6) SCALE 613; [2014] 7 S.C.R. 446

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Keywords: Decreed, Legality

Summary: Rent Control - Suit for eviction - Decreed - Legality - An immovable property known as 'Dev Ashish' was tenanted property - Respondent was appointed by HC to take charge of suit property - Respondent filed suit against appellant/defendant/tenant before Trial Court - Trial Court decreed suit in favor of respondent - Aggrieved filed appeal before Appellate Court and the same was dismissed - Appellant filed revision before HC and the same was dismissed - Hence instant appeal - Whether impugned order passed by HC was justified -

Held, SC noticed that HC duly considered question of notice and correctly came to conclusion that Legislature wanted to plug loopholes and to redress mischief by making change in law - If notice was short of period but suit or proceeding was filed after expiry of period, notice should not be deemed to be invalid - Notice was issued and suit was actually filed on 6-2-2002 after six months and, therefore, notice could not be declared or deemed to be invalid - Question which was tried to be urged had no substance since SC receiver was holding properties as custodia legis and had acted in matter as reasonable prudent trustees used to do in this matter and such action on part of Court Receiver was nothing but for preservation of property in question, therefore contention of appellant does not have any substance - Although point tried to be taken by appellant was that appellant was a sick company but SC did not find that such point was ever urged before HC and furthermore it appeared that admittedly tenancy was about residential premises - Reasoning given by HC does not warrant any interference - Appeal dismissed.
