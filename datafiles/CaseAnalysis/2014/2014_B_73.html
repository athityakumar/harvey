Haryana State Agricultural Marketing Board v Bishamber Dayal Goyal and others

Supreme Court of India

26 March 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Pinaki Chandra Ghose, Gyan Sudha Misra

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 184; AIR 2014 SC 1766; 2014 (104) ALR 518; 2014 (2) CPJ(Sc) 11; JT 2014 (4) SC 354; 2014 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 1907; 2014 (2) RCR(Civil) 586; 2014(4) SCALE 134; [2014] 4 S.C.R. 141

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection; Land & Property

Keywords: Allotment Rules, 1997

Summary: Consumer Protection - Land & Property - Punjab Agricultural Produce Markets Act, 1961 - Deficiency in service - By a notification dt. 16-11-1971, State Govt. notified the area of New Grain Mandi as Market Area - Subsequently, in the year 1974, the areas/limits were further extended by 5 kms - In 1980, the State Govt. notified a sub-market yard of New Grain Mandi - Appellant allotted plots to the respondents upon depositing the 25% of the price of the said plots vide allotment letter dt. 25-7-1991 - Respondents does not pay the instalments in terms of the allotment letters - Appellant issued demand letters to the respondents who filed a complaint before the District Forum submitting that appellant was failed to provide basic amenities such as sewerage, electricity, roads etc. at the said Mandi Area - District Forum held that due to the omission of the appellant, respondents were deprived of doing the grain business for which the plots were purchased and in the absence of the notification of the area as a sub-yard, there was a grave deficiency of service - District Forum awarded the respondents interest at 12% per annum on the entire deposited amount after 2 years from the date of issuance of allotment letters to the respondents till the development and notification of the area in question was not done - On appeal, State Commission upheld the order of the District Forum - Appellant filed a revision petition before the National Commission resulting in dismissal - Hence, the instant appeal - Whether order of the District Forum as affirmed by the State Commission and National Commission could be upheld -

Held, inaction on the part of the appellant in providing the requisite facilities for more than a decade clearly establishes deficiency of services as the respondents were prevented from carrying out the grain business - However, the respondents were also incorrect in refusing to pay the instalments and violating the terms of the instalment letter - Thus, considering the surrounding circumstances wherein the appellant had been unable to develop the area for more than two decades and the resultant loss suffered by the respondents, Court were of the opinion that in the present situation, there was a need for proportionate relief as the levy of penal interest and other charges on the respondents would be grossly unfair - No grounds have been made out by the appellant to interfere with the order passed by the National Commission - Appeal dismissed.
