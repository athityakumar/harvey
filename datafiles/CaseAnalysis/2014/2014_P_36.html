Periyasami S/o Duraisami Novanagar and another v State Represented Through Inspector of Police, 'Q' Branch CID, Tiruchirappalli, Tamil Nadu

Supreme Court of India

11 April 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjana Prakash Desai, P. Sathasivam, Ranjan Gogoi

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 240; (2014) 6 SCC 59; 2014 CRLJ 2350; JT 2014 (5) SC 7; 2014 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 2506; 2014 (2) MLJ(Crl) 484; 2014 (2) RCR(Criminal) 512; 2014(4) SCALE 601; [2014] 11 S.C.R. 483

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Slogans, Prevention of Damage To Public Property Act, 1984, Bomb blasts causing derailment of trains

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Terrorist And Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Act, 1987, ss. 3(3), 4(1), 19 - Indian Railways Act, 1989, s. 150 - Explosives Act, 1884, ss. 3, 5 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120-B, 124 - Prevention of Damage to Public Property Act, 1984, s. 4(1) - Bomb blasts causing derailment of trains - Criminal conspiracy - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellants ( A1 and 2) along with other co-accused planted bombs to cause derailment of trains and to cause damage to Central and State Govt. properties and to secede the State from UOI - Trial Court framed charges against appellants u/s. 120-B of IPC r/w ss. 3(3), 4(1) of 1987 Act, u/ss. 3(2)(ii), 4(1), 5 of 1987 Act, u/ss. 3, 5 of 1884 Act, u/ss. 150(2)(b) of 1989 Act r/w s. 3 r/w s. 4(1) of 1984 Act - Trial Court convicted appellant (A1) to undergo imprisonment for life and appellant (A2) to undergo RI for 5 years - Aggrieved appellants filed instant appeals - Appellants contended that prosecution case entirely rests on the confessional statement of A1 and that prosecution had not proved that A2 was a member of any banned organization -

Held, submission that prosecution had not examined any independent witnesses - It was common knowledge that when terrorists unleash a way of terror, no independent witnesses were ready to come forward and depose against them - Prosecution case could not be rejected on that ground - In any case, evidence on record was cogent and reliable and, thus, non-examination of independent witnesses did not have any adverse impact on the prosecution case - Evidence of defence witnesses did not inspire confidence and has rightly not taken into consideration by Trial Court - Retraction did not always dilute or reduce or wipe out the evidentiary value of a confessional statement - Quite often retraction was an afterthought - It could be the result of legal advice or pressure exerted by those whose involvement may be likely to be disclosed or confirmed by the confessional statement of the accused - Thus, in each case, Court would have to examine whether the confession was voluntary and true and whether the retraction was an afterthought - It was clear from evidence of PW15 that recording of his evidence was unduly prolonged, and in that period, an effort was made to win him over - Those facts would have to be taken into consideration while considering the evidentiary value of his evidence - It would be safe to rely on that part of the evidence of that witness, which was corroborated by other evidence on record - PW15 stated how A1 came to his house along with 'L' and how 2 other persons joined him -PW15 stated how they prepared the dough with gelatin sticks and broken glass pieces and that they left the house telling him that he should not ask them anything about their activities and he should read the next day's newspaper to know what they were doing - PW15 stated that after the blast, he met A1 and he told him that he, 'K', 'L' and 'R, destroyed the railway bridge - A1 left the place telling him that if he disclosed it to anyone, all members of his family would be killed - That portion of his evidence found sufficient corroboration from other evidence on record and, thus, reliance can be placed on it - Thus, appellant's (A1) involvement in the crime was proved to the hilt by his confessional statement recorded by PW37; by evidence of PW13 who stated that posters were seized from the place where blast occurred; by the evidence of PW32 which indicated that those posters were in his handwriting and the statement of PW15 which indicates his role - Thus, Trial Court, rightly convicted A1 - There was no reference to A2 in confessional statement of A1 - PW15 has stated that on 22/12/1993 A2 came to him and stated that 'L' and others would visit him and they would stay till night and food should be provided to them - It appeared from the confessional statement of A1 and evidence of PW15 that A2 did not participate in manufacturing of bombs, carrying them to the scene of offence, planting them under the railway bridge and causing the blast - There was a passing reference in PW15's evidence that after the blast when he asked A2 about the blast, he told him that 'L', 'K' and 'R' were responsible for the blast and if he discloses that to anyone, all members of his family would be killed - That part of statement of PW15 was not corroborated by any evidence on record - Prosecution was not been able to establish its case against A2 beyond reasonable doubt - A2 should get benefit of doubt - In the circumstances, impugned judgment and order so far as it convicts A1 was confirmed and conviction of A2 was set aside - Appeals disposed of.
