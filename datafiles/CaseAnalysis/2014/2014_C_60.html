Dr. Balwant Singh v Commissioner of Police and others

Supreme Court of India

07 November 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Sapre, Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 762; (2015) 4 SCC 801; 2015 (5) CTC 562; JT 2014 (12) SC 477; 2014 (8) MLJ 485; 2014 (4) RCR(Civil) 1006; 2014(12) SCALE 580; [2014] 11 S.C.R. 123

Case Digest

Subject: Tort

Keywords: Human Rights Commission Act, 2005

Summary: Tort - Constitution of India, 1950 - Nuisance - Appellant approached authorities to protect the interest of the appellant, his property and his peaceful living - Appellant alleged that the thousand/hundreds of people belonging to political/non-political parties would gather on the road approaching to Vidhan Sabha, which was in front of his house - Protestors use indiscriminately loudspeakers, the demonstrators would indiscriminately make use of the compound walls of nearby houses including that of the appellant's house to ease themselves frequently at any time - Complaint was filed before Rajasthan State Human Rights Commission (RSHRC) for taking appropriate action in accordance with law - RSHRC directed the Additional Home Secretary to order the concerned officials to effectively stop interference with the right of the appellant to lead an independent and peaceful life and ensure - Despite issuance of the aforementioned directions, the State did not ensure its compliance - Writ petition was filed before HC - HC held that since the State already taken all necessary steps in the light of the directions given by the RSHRC and hence no more orders were called for in the writ petition - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, the Constitution, inter alia, casts a duty on the State and their authorities to ensure that every citizen's cherished rights guaranteed to him under the Constitution are respected and preserved, and he/she is allowed to enjoy them in letter and spirit subject to reasonable restrictions put on them, as dreamt by the framers of the Constitution. Intervention of the Court is called for at the instance of citizen when these rights are violated by fellow citizens or by any State agency. The respondents directed to ensure strict compliance of the conditions/steps. Appeal partly allowed.
