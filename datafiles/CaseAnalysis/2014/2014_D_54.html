Renu and others v District and Sessions Judge, Tis Hazari and anothers

Supreme Court of India

12 February 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, M.Y. Eqbal, J. Chelameswar

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 86; (2014) 14 SCC 50; AIR 2014 SC 2175; 2014(2) ALL MR 957; 2014 (2) ALT(SC) 24; 2014 (2) AWC 1698; 2014 (207) DLT 486; 2014 (142) FLR 415; JT 2014 (3) SC 1; 2014 (3) LLJ 257; 2014(2) SCALE 262; [2014] 2 S.C.R. 537; 2014 (3) SLR 1; 2014 (2) UPLBEC 942; 2014 (3) WLN(SC) 113

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Karnataka State Civil Services (Recruitment to Ministerial Posts) Rules, 1966

Summary: (1) Constitution of India-Article 229-Employment-Appointment of Class IV employees in subordinate courts-Irregularities and illegalities in recruitments of staff in subordinate courts-Employment whether of Class IV, Class III, Class II or any other class in High Court or courts subordinate to it fall within definition of "public employment"-Such employment, therefore, has to be made under rules and under orders of competent authority-Powers under Article 229 (2) cannot be exercised by Chief Justice in unfettered and arbitrary manner-Appointment in judicial institutions should be made giving adherence to provisions of Articles 14 and 16 of the Constitution and/or such Rules as made by Legislature-Under no circumstance any appointment which is illegal should be saved-Certain directions issued.(2) Constitution of India-Articles 14 and 16-Employment-Appointment-Any appointment made in violation of Articles 14 and 16-Is not only irregular but also illegal and cannot be sustained. (3) Constitution of India-Article 14-Right to equality-Each and every State action is to be tested on touchstone of equality. (4) Constitution of India-Article 13-Objective of Article 13-Main objective of Article 13 is to secure paramountcy of Constitution especially with regard to fundamental rights. (5) Writ of quo warranto-Issuance of-For issuance of writ of quo warranto, Court has to satisfy that appointment is contrary to statutory rules and person holding post has no right to hold it.
