Commissioner of Wealth Tax, Rajkot v Estate of Late HMM Vikramsinhji of Gondal

Supreme Court of India

16 April 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, Shiva Kirti Singh

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 315; (2015) 5 SCC 666; [2014] 363 ITR 679; 2014(6) SCALE 529; [2014] 9 S.C.R. 1; [2014] 225 TAXMAN 166

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Trusts & Associations

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Trusts & Associations - Income-tax Act, 1961 - Wealth-tax Act, 1957 - Discretionary trust - Ex-Ruler of Gondal Shri Vikramsinhji executed 3 deeds of settlements (trust deeds) in the US and 2 deeds in the UK - 3 settlements executed in US were in identical terms - Similarly, the 2 settlements executed in UK were similar - Trusts were created for the benefit of (a) the Settlor, (b) the children and remoter issue for the time being in existence of the Settlor and (c) any person for the time being in existence who was the wife or widow of the Settlor or the wife or widow or husband or widower of any of them, the children and remoter issue of the Settlor - Settlor, was including the whole of the income arising from these trusts in his returns of income - Said income was also included in the 2 returns filed by his son X for the assessment year 1970-71 - Thereafter, it appears that the X took the stand that the income from these trusts was not includible in his income - X also took the stand that inclusion of the said income in the returns submitted by his father for the a/ys 1964-65 to 1969-70 and by himself for the a/y 1970-71 was under a mistake - X approached the Settlement Commission with an application for settlement relating to income from UK trusts just as he made application for settlement relating to US trusts - Settlement Commission, computed the taxable income of the Settlor under both the sets of trusts US and UK for the a/ys 1964-65 to 1970-71 (up to the date of the death of the Settlor) as also the income of X for the a/ys 1970-71 to 1982-83 - On appeal, Tribunal held that due to failure on the part of the Maharaja to appoint discretion exercisers as per cl. 3(2), cl. 4 had become operative and the UK trusts have to be held to be specific trusts - HC held that on interpretation of the relevant clauses of the deeds of settlement executed in UK, character of the trusts appears to be discretionary and not specific - Hence, instant appeals - Whether order of the HC could be upheld -

Held, a discretionary trust was one which gives a beneficiary no right to any part of the income of the trust property, but vests in the trustees a discretionary power to pay him, or apply for his benefit, such part of the income as they think fit - Trustees must exercise their discretion as and when the income becomes available, but if they fail to distribute in due time, the power was not extinguished so that they could distribute later - They have no power to bind themselves for the future - Beneficiary thus had no more than a hope that the discretion would be exercised in his favour - In the instant case, income had been retained and not disbursed to the beneficiaries, the view taken by the HC could not be said to be legally flawed - Merely because the Settlor and after his death, his son does not exercise their power to appoint the discretion exercisers, the character of the subject trusts does not get altered - 2 UK trusts continued to be 'discretionary trust' for the subject a/ys - HC had taken a correct view that the value of the assets could not be assessed on the estate of the deceased Settlor - Appeals dismissed.
