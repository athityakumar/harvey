Indus Airways Private Limited and others v Magnum Aviation Private Limited and another

Supreme Court of India

07 April 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, Shiva Kirti Singh

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 232; (2014) 12 SCC 539; 2014 ALL MR (Cri) 1948; 2014 (2) BC 698; 2014 (2) CGLJ 545; 2014 (3) CTC 721; 2014 (3) G.L.R. 2482; 2014 (2) JCC (NI) 115; JT 2014 (5) SC 51; 2014 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 2109; 2014 (2) MLJ(Crl) 353; 2014(3) M.P.H.T. 28; 2014 (2) OLR 99; 2014 (175) PLR 809; 2014 (2) RCR(Civil) 611; 2014 (2) RCR(Criminal) 494; 2014(4) SCALE 645; [2014] 5 S.C.R. 56

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, s. 138 - Issuance of cheque - Stop payment of cheque - Legally enforceable debt - Criminal Liability - Issuance of summons - Sustainability - Purchasers/appellants issued two post-dated cheques to supplier/respondent No. 1 - Said cheques got dishonoured when they were presented on the ground that the purchasers had stopped payment - Supplier filed a complaint against purchasers u/s. 138 of the Act - Trial Court took cognizance of the alleged offence and issued summons to purchasers - Said order of issuing summons was challenged in revision where revisional court quashed the process issued - On appeal HC allowed the petition, set aside the order Revisional court - Hence instant appeal - Whether the post-dated cheques issued by purchasers as an advance payment in respect of purchase orders could be considered in discharge of legally enforceable debt or other liability, and, if so, whether the dishonour of such cheques amounts to an offence u/s. 138 of the Act -

Held, in reasoning HC was clearly flawed, it failed to keep in mind the fine distinction between civil liability and criminal liability u/s. 138 of the Act - If at the time of entering into a contract, it was one of the conditions of the contract that purchaser to pay the amount in advance and there was breach of such condition then purchaser may have to make good the loss that might have occasioned to the seller but that does not create a criminal liability u/s. 138 of the Act - For a criminal liability to be made out u/s. 138 of the Act, there should be legally enforceable debt or other liability subsisting on the date of drawal of the cheque - View of HC that the issuance of cheque towards advance payment at the time of signing such contract to be considered as subsisting liability and dishonour of such cheque amounts to an offence u/s. 138 of the Act - HC traveled beyond the scope of s. 138 of the Act by holding that the purpose of enacting s. 138 of the Act would stand defeated if after placing orders and giving advance payments, the instructions for stop payments were issued and orders were cancelled - If a cheque issued as an advance payment for purchase of the goods and for any reason purchase order, not carried to its logical conclusion either because of its cancellation or otherwise and material or goods for which purchase order was placed not supplied by the supplier, in SC's considered view, the cheque could not be said to have been drawn for an existing debt or liability - Impugned judgment was set aside and the order of Trial Court was restored - Appeal allowed.
