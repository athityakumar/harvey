S. V. A. Steel Re-rolling Mills Limited Etc. v State of Kerala and others

Supreme Court of India

06 February 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 73; (2014) 4 SCC 186; AIR 2014 SC 1086; 2014 (3) ALD(SC) 130; 2014 (103) ALR 678; 2014 (2) AWC 1103; 2014 (2) CLT(SC) 32; JT 2014 (13) SC 370; 2014 (2) MLJ 249; 2014(2) SCALE 154; [2014] 2 S.C.R. 336

Case Digest

Subject: Electricity & Energy

Keywords: Enhanced power tariff, Benefits

Summary: (1) Electricity Act, 1910-Section 22B-Control on distribution and consumption of energy-Assurance by State Government for uninterrupted electricity supply-Appellants set up their industries in respondent-State because of promise given by respondent-State for uninterrupted 100% electricity supply for 5 years-Respondent-State not adhered to promise given to appellants with regard to uninterrupted 100% electricity supply-No case made out by respondent-State that appellants had committed any breach or were not entitled to any of benefits or concessions offered to them by respondents-State-Provisions of Section 22B are not much relevant for the reason that respondents-State given assurance with regard to uninterrupted supply of electricity and, therefore, respondents ought to have made provision for uninterrupted electricity supply to appellants and other similarly situated persons by regulating electricity supply in proper manner-Respondents cannot riggle out of their liability by making policy to the effect that benefit by way of incentive would be extended only if electricity supply was reduced to less than 50% on particular day-Respondents not wholly fair when it extended benefit to appellants only for period during which electricity supply was reduced to less than 50% on certain days-Respondents to compensate appellants by supplying assured electricity for some extended period at specified tariff-Respondents ought to have extended period even for days when supply was more than 50% but not 100% as assured under Government orders-Respondents directed to calculate period during which 100% electricity supply was not given to appellants and extend period of incentive accordingly-Impugned order of High Court-Set aside.

(2) State Policy-Framing of State policy-Before laying down any State policy which would give benefits to its subjects-State must think about pros and cons of policy and its capacity to give benefits-Without proper appreciation of all relevant factors ; State should not give any assurance, not only because that would be in violation of principles of promissory estopppel but it would be unfair and immoral on part of State not to act as per its promise.
