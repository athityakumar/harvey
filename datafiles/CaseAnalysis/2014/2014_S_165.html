Bairam Muralidhar v State of Andhra Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

31 July 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, Pinaki Chandra Ghose

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 493; (2014) 10 SCC 380; AIR 2014 SC 3437; 2014 CRLJ 4242; JT 2014 (8) SC 519; 2014 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 3053; 2014 (4) RCR(Criminal) 1; 2015 (1) RLW 431; 2014(9) SCALE 62; [2014] 8 S.C.R. 328

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s.321 - Withdrawal of pending case - Permissibility - In the appeal, by special leave, the assail was to the defensibility of the order passed by HC whereby Single Judge had concurred with the view expressed by the Trial Court, where under Trial Court had declined to grant permission to withdraw the case pending against the accused-appellant in exercise of the power u/s. 321 of CrPC - Whether the public prosecutor had really applied his mind to all the relevant materials on record and satisfied himself that the withdrawal from the prosecution would subserve the cause of public interest or not -

Held, in the case at hand, as the application filed by the public prosecutor would show that he had mechanically stated about the conditions-precedent - It could not be construed that public prosecutor had really perused the materials and applied his independent mind solely because he had so stated - Application must indicate perusal of the materials by stating what are the materials he had perused, may be in brief, and whether such withdrawal of the prosecution would serve public interest and how he had formed his independent opinion - As SC perceived, public prosecutor was totally guided by the order of the Govt. and really not applied his mind to the facts of the case - Trial Court as well as HC had observed that it was a case under the Act - Trial Court as well as HC had taken note of the fact that the State Govt. had already granted sanction - It was also noticeable that the Anti Corruption Bureau had found there was no justification of withdrawal of the prosecution - A case under the Act had its own gravity - Regard being had to the gravity of the offence and the impact on public life apart from the nature of application filed by the public prosecutor, SC was of the considered opinion that view expressed by Trial Court as well as HC could not be found fault with - SC was inclined to think that there was no ground to show that such withdrawal would advance the cause of justice and serve the public interest - Appeal dismissed.
