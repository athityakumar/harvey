Nand Kumar v State of Chhattisgarh

Supreme Court of India

31 October 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Sapre, Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 750; (2015) 1 SCC 776; 2014 (4) CGLJ 499; 2015 CRLJ 381; JT 2014 (12) SC 376; 2014(5) M.P.H.T. 365; 2014 (4) RCR(Criminal) 895; 2014(12) SCALE 497

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Murder, Conviction

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 r/w ss. 149, 148 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellants (accused) were convicted ss. 302 r/w s. 149, 148 of IPC and were directed to undergo life imprisonment by Trial Court - Aggrieved appellants filed appeals before HC, which were dismissed by impugned judgment - Hence, instant appeals - Whether Courts below were justified in holding the appellants guilty for committing murder of 3 persons.

Held, names of accused were mentioned in complaint (Ex.P1). Their names are also mentioned in the statements of PW1 and 3, which were recorded u/s. 161 of CrPC. PW1 and 3 also categorically stated in their evidence in Court about the overt act played by accused persons in committing the murders of deceased. A conjoint reading of these two statements clearly establishes the overt acts played by the accused persons while killing three persons one after another on the same day. The ocular evidence of two eye witnesses (PWs 1 and 3) conclusively prove not only the involvement of the accused persons but their actual active role played in killing these three persons. It is amply established from the medical evidence of three doctors PW12 and 13 and 14 and their respective post-mortem reports (Exs-P-56, 59 and 61) as also ocular evidence of 2 eye-witnesses, PW1 and PW3. In instant case, the mother and daughter saw from their naked eyes that their father/husband and two sons/brothers were being killed in their presence with the use of Lathis, battle axe, sword and rods by the accused persons mercilessly and both the helpless ladies standing in front of the mob (accused persons) with folded hands praying 'please do not kill them and leave them'. The accused persons did not listen to their prayer and with a pre-determined motive killed the deceased persons by beating them due to which two of them died on the spot and one succumbed in the hospital after some time. PWs-1 and 3 elaborately narrated the entire incident by taking the names of every accused whom they knew to be the residents of the same area. Appeals dismissed.
