State of Bihar and others v Rajmangal Ram

Supreme Court of India

31 March 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjan Gogoi, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 196; (2014) 11 SCC 388; AIR 2014 SC 1674; 2014 ALL MR (Cri) 1882; 2014 CRLJ 2300; 2014 (2) MLJ(Crl) 348; 2014 (2) RCR(Criminal) 400; 2014(4) SCALE 338; [2014] 4 S.C.R. 602

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 465 - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 19, 19(1) and 19(3) - Sanction to prosecute - Whether a criminal prosecution ought to be interfered with by the HCs at the instance of an accused who seeks mid-course relief from the criminal charges levelled against him on grounds of defects/omissions or errors in the order granting sanction to prosecute including errors of jurisdiction to grant such sanction -

Held, HC had interdicted the criminal proceedings on the ground that the Law Department was not the competent authority to accord sanction for the prosecution of the respondents - Even assuming that the Law Department was not competent, it was still necessary for the HC to reach the conclusion that a failure of justice had been occasioned - Such a finding was conspicuously absent rendering it difficult to sustain the impugned orders of the HC - HC in both the cases had also come to the conclusion that the sanction orders in question were passed mechanically and without consideration of the relevant facts and records - This was treated as an additional ground for interference with the criminal proceedings registered against the respondents - HC was not justified in coming to the said findings at the stage when the same were recorded - Impugned orders passed by the HC could not be sustained in law, hence set aside - Appeals allowed.
