Yogendra Pratap Singh v Savitri Pandey and another

Supreme Court of India

19 September 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, Kurian Joseph, R.F. Nariman

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 636; (2014) 10 SCC 713; AIR 2015 SC 157; 2014 ALL MR (Cri) 4137; 2014 (6) ALT(SC) 1; 2015 (2) BC 1; 2014 (6) Bom.C.R. 47; 2015 (1) CalLT(SC) 1; 2014 (4) CompLJ 337; 2015 CRLJ 51; 2015 (1) DRTC 474; JT 2014 (10) SC 444; 2014 (4) KLT 154; 2014 (5) Law Herald (P&H) 4021; 2015 (2) MahLJ 575; 2014(5) M.P.H.T. 208; 2015 (2) MPLJ 9; 2015 (1) RCR(Civil) 550; 2014 (4) RCR(Criminal) 321; 2015 (2) RLW 1040; 2014(10) SCALE 723; 2014 (4) WLN 119

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance

Summary: (A) Banking & Finance - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881 (Act), ss. 138(c) and 142(b) - Dishonour of cheque - Maintainability of - complaint filed prior to expiry of 15 days notice period as required vide Section 138(c) of the Act - Whether cognizance on such complaint be taken the same being premature?

Held, Section 138(c) of the Act provides that no complaint can be filed for an offence under section 138 of the Act unless the period of 15 days notice period commencing from the date on which notice was served upon the drawer has elapsed and thus any complaint filed prior to expiry of said period would be no complaint in the eye of law and not just being a premature complaint. Accordingly, no cognizance of an offence can be taken on such complaint. Further, merely because at the time of taking cognizance by the Court, the 15 days notice period stood expired the Court does not get clothed with the jurisdiction to take cognizance of an offence u/s. 138 on a complaint filed in the above circumstances. In other words no complaint can be maintained against the drawer of the cheque before the expiry of 15 days from the date of receipt of notice because the drawer/accused cannot be said to have committed any offence until then. Further, criminal proceedings in the above circumstances also become liable to be quashed. Ordered accordingly.

(B) Banking & Finance - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881 (Act), ss. 138(c) and 142(b) - Dishonour of cheque - Complaint alleging offence of cheque dishonour filed prior to expiry of 15 days notice period due to be served upon drawer of the cheque - Whether Complainant, if complaint filed earlier held to be premature in the above circumstance can be permitted to present the same again notwithstanding the fact that the period of 1 month stipulated u/s. 142 (b) of the Act for the filing of complaint has expired?

Held, a complaint alleging dishonour of cheque if filed prior to expiry of 15 days notice period commencing from the date of receipt of notice issued u/cl. (c) of the proviso to s. 138 of Act is no complaint in the eyes of law and thus not maintainable. The complainant thus cannot be permitted to present the very same complaint at any later stage and the only remedy that remains with the Section 142 of the NI Act prescribes the mode and so also the time within which a complaint for an offence under Section 138 of the Act can be filed. A complaint made under Section 138 by the payee or the holder in due course of the cheque has to be in writing and needs to be made within one month from the date on which the cause of action has arisen under clause (c) of the proviso to Section 138. The period of one month under Section 142(b) begins from the date on which the cause of action has arisen under clause (c) of the proviso to Section 138. However, if the complainant satisfies the Court that he had sufficient cause for not making a complaint within the prescribed period of one month, a complaint may be taken by the Court after the prescribed period and this direction as held would deemed to be applicable to pending cases. In the instant case, as has already been observed and held that a complaint filed before the expiry of 15 days from the date of receipt of notice issued under clause (c) of the proviso to Section 138 will not be maintainable, the complainant cannot be permitted to present the very same complaint at any later stage.

Ratio: Complaint alleging offence of dishonour of cheque prior to expiry of 15 days notice period is no complaint in the eyes of law.
