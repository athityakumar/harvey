Goa Foundation v Union of India and others

Supreme Court of India

21 April 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Patnaik, Surinder Singh Nijjar, Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 280; (2014) 6 SCC 590; JT 2014 (5) SC 486; 2014(5) SCALE 364; [2014] 5 S.C.R. 302

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Mines & Minerals

Keywords: Commissions Of Inquiry Act, 1952, Interest Of Mineral Development, Mineral Conservation and Development Rules, 1988, Mining Leases (Modification of Terms) Rules, 1956, Goa, Daman and Diu Mining Concessions (Abolition and Declaration As Mining Leases) Act, 1987, Goa (Prevention of Illegal Mining, Storage and Transportation of Minerals) Rules, 2013

Summary: Mines & Minerals - Administrative - Mines and Minerals (Development & regulation) Act, 1957, s. 25(c) - Minerals Concession Rules, 1960 - Illegal mining - Termination of Leases - Expiration - Regulating mining operations - Legality - Directions - On the basis of findings in the report of the Justice Shah Commission on illegal mining in the State of Goa (State Govt.) the petitioner has filed writ petition as Public Interest Litigation praying for directions to the UoI and the State Govt. to take steps for termination of the mining leases of lessees involved in mining in violation - Goa Foundation prayed that a direction be issued to respondents to prosecute all those who have committed offences under the different laws and were involved in the pilferage of State revenue through illegal mining activities in the State Govt. including the public servants who have aided and abetted the offences - Goa Foundation also sought for appointment of an independent authority with full powers to take control, supervise and regulate mining operations in the State Govt. and to ensure the implementation of the laws - Petitioner has also prayed for some incidental and consequential reliefs - (A) Whether in future the mining leases were to be auctioned or have to be granted in accordance with the policy of the State and the provisions of the 1957 Act and the 1960 Rules and Whether suspension of mining operations in the State order dt. 10-9-2012 of the State Govt. and the suspension of the Environmental Clearances granted to the mines in the State order dt. 14-9-2012 were legal and valid -

Held, SC was of the considered opinion that it was for the State Govt. to decide as a matter of policy in what manner the leases of these mineral resources would be granted, but the decision had to be taken in accordance with the provisions of the 1957 Act and the Rules made thereunder and in consonance with the Constitutional provisions and the decision taken by the State to grant a mining lease in a particular manner or to a particular party could be examined by way of judicial review by the Court - Renewal of all the deemed mining leases in the State had expired on 22-11-2007, the mining lessees would not be entitled to the sale value of the ores sold in e-auction but they would be entitled to the approximate cost (not actual cost) of the extraction of the ores - On account of suspension of mining operations in the State Govt., the workers who were employed by the lessees claim that they have not been paid their wages - U/s. 25C of the 1947 Act, when a workman whose name was borne on the muster rolls of an industrial establishment and who has completed not less than one year of continuous service under an employer was laid-off, he was entitled to be paid by the employer for all the days which he was so laid-off, except for such weekly holidays as may intervene, compensation which shall be equal to 50% of the total of the basic wages and dearness allowance that would have been payable to him had he not been so laid-off - Following the said principle of lay-off compensation, SC held that workers who could not be paid wages by the lessees would have to be paid compensation at the rate of 50% of their basic wages and dearness allowance during the period of non-employment on account of suspension of mining operations - Moreover, Port Trust would have to be paid 50% of their charges for storage of the mineral ores after 5-10-2012 - SC directed that the deemed mining leases of the lessees in Goa expired on 22-11-1987 and the maximum of 20 years renewal period of the deemed mining leases in Goa expired on 22-11-2007 and consequently mining by the lessees after 22-11-2007 was illegal and hence the impugned order dt. 10-9-2012 of State Govt. and the impugned order dt. 14-9-2012 of the MoEF, GoI were not liable to be quashed - Dumping of minerals outside the leased area of the mining lessees was not permissible under the 1957 Act and the 1960 Rules made thereunder - Until the order dt. 4-8-2006 of SC was modified by in T.N. Godavarman Thirumulpad v. Union of India & Ors. 2006 Indlaw SC 1617, there could be no mining activities within one kilometer from the boundaries of National Parks and Sanctuaries in State - By the order dt. 4-12-2006 SC has not prohibited mining activities within 10 kilometers distance from the boundaries of the National Parks or Wildlife Sanctuaries - It was for the State Govt. to decide as a matter of policy in what manner mining leases were to be granted in future but the Constitutionality or legality of the decision of the State Govt. could be examined by the Court in exercise of its power of judicial review - Petition allowed.
