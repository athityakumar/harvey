Subrata Roy Sahara v Union of India and others

Supreme Court of India

06 May 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

J. S. KHEHAR, K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 334; (2014) 8 SCC 470; AIR 2014 SC 3241; 2014 (2) CompLJ 449; 2014 CRLJ 3437; JT 2014 (6) SC 1; 2014 (2) MLJ(Crl) 527; 2014(6) SCALE 257

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Interim Maintenance, Indian Bar Councils Act, 1926, Litigant, Obedience

Summary: (A) Criminal - Banking & Finance - Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 21 r. 37, O. 21 r. 37(1), O. 21 r. 37(2), O. 21 r. 40, ss. 51, 55, 58, 58(1), 58(2) - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 125, 128, 145 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 129, 136, 137, 14, 141, 142, 19, 21, 32 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 109, 120B, 161, 165, 384, 420 - Securities and Exchange Board of India Act, 1992, ss. 11(3), 11C, 15U, 15Y, 15Z - Cheating - Incarceration of petitioner - Legality - Instant writ petition was filed by petitioner to declare order dtd. 4-3-2014 as void, nullity and non-est in eyes of law and further to declare that incarceration and custody of petitioner was illegal - Whether judicial orders be obeyed at all costs -

Held, no merit in contention advanced on behalf of petitioner, Calculated psychological offensives and mind games adopted to seek recusal of Judges, need to be strongly repulsed - SC deprecated such tactics and commend similar approach to other Courts, when they experience such behavior - Disobedience of orders of Court striked at very root of rule of law, on which judicial system rests - Judicial orders were bound to be obeyed at all costs - Howsoever grave effect might be, was no answer for non-compliance of judicial order - Judicial orders could not be permitted to be circumvented - In exercise of contempt jurisdiction, Courts had power to enforce compliance of judicial orders, and also, power to punish for contempt - Petition dismissed.

(B) Criminal - Banking & Finance - Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 21 r. 37, O. 21 r. 37(1), O. 21 r. 37(2), O. 21 r. 40, ss. 51, 55, 58, 58(1), 58(2) - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 125, 128, 145 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 129, 136, 137, 14, 141, 142, 19, 21, 32 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 109, 120B, 161, 165, 384, 420 - Securities and Exchange Board of India Act, 1992, ss. 11(3), 11C, 15U, 15Y, 15Z - Cheating - Demeanor of company - Legality - Whether facts reflecting demeanor of two companies, petitioner, and other directors of SIRECL and SHICL, in process of litigation, leading up to passing of order dtd. 31-8-2012 -

Held, two companies of which petitioner was a promoter, flouted orders passed by SEBI, SAT, HC and of SC, with impunity - Facts and information solicited were never disclosed - Position adopted by two companies was always projected on basis of unverifiable material - SC recorded in its order that factual assertions made on behalf of two companies seemed to be totally unrealistic and could well be fictitious, concocted and made up, and also remarked, that affairs of two companies seemed to be doubtful, dubious and questionable - Position had remained unaltered, inasmuch as, no authentic and verifiable material sought had ever been furnished by two companies - Two companies remained adamant while frittering away repeated opportunities granted by SC to comply with orders - Companies adopted demeanor of defiance constituting a rebellious behavior, not amenable to rule of law - Petition dismissed.

(C) Criminal - Banking & Finance - Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 21 r. 37, O. 21 r. 37(1), O. 21 r. 37(2), O. 21 r. 40, ss. 51, 55, 58, 58(1), 58(2) - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 125, 128, 145 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 129, 136, 137, 14, 141, 142, 19, 21, 32 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 109, 120B, 161, 165, 384, 420 - Securities and Exchange Board of India Act, 1992, ss. 11(3), 11C, 15U, 15Y, 15Z - Cheating - Demeanor of company - Legality - Whether efforts made by this Court, to cajole the contemnors, including petitioner, for compliance of orders of this Court, dated 31.8.2012 and 5.12.2012 -

Held, efforts made to cajole two companies and petitioner were always stonewalled and brushed off - All intermediary means to secure compliance of SC's orders, were evaded and skirted - Even proposals to secure payments to be made to investors, in terms of SC's orders, were systematically frustrated - Similar proposals made unilaterally by counsel representing two companies and petitioner himself, turned out to be ploys to sidetrack and derail process of law - Such unilateral proposals, were unilaterally withdrawn - Since all efforts to cajole two companies and petitioner were methodically circumvented, SC started adopting sequentially harsher means to persuade compliance of SC's orders, leading finally to passing of the impugned order - Petition dismissed.

(D) Criminal - Banking & Finance - Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 21 r. 37, O. 21 r. 37(1), O. 21 r. 37(2), O. 21 r. 40, ss. 51, 55, 58, 58(1), 58(2) - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 125, 128, 145 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 129, 136, 137, 14, 141, 142, 19, 21, 32 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 109, 120B, 161, 165, 384, 420 - Securities and Exchange Board of India Act, 1992, ss. 11(3), 11C, 15U, 15Y, 15Z - Execution of money-decree - Detention order - Legality - Whether there is no provision, where under an order of arrest and detention could be passed for execution of a money-decree -

Held, CrPC which regulates civil proceedings in India, expressly contemplates arrest and detention for enforcement of money decree and CrPC, which regulates criminal proceedings in India, envisages arrest and detention as mean for enforcing financial liability - Submission made by petitioner to effect, that execution of money decree or enforcement of a financial liability by way of arrest and detention was procedure unknown to law, was therefore, wholly misconceived - Petition dismissed.

(E) Criminal - Banking & Finance - Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 21 r. 37, O. 21 r. 37(1), O. 21 r. 37(2), O. 21 r. 40, ss. 51, 55, 58, 58(1), 58(2) - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 125, 128, 145 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 129, 136, 137, 14, 141, 142, 19, 21, 32 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 109, 120B, 161, 165, 384, 420 - Securities and Exchange Board of India Act, 1992, ss. 11(3), 11C, 15U, 15Y, 15Z -Procedures - Sustainability - Whether it was imperative for SC to adopt procedure prescribed u/s. 51 of CPC, Whether if above procedure was not followed, impugned order passed by SC on 4-3-2014 was rendered void, and as such, unsustainable in law -

Held, submission made by petitioner, that SC was obliged to comply with procedure contemplated u/s. 51, and O. XXI, r. 37 and 40 of CPC, before ordering arrest and detention of petitioner was devoid of any merit, because s. 51 of CPC and other allied provisions referred, were not applicable to actions emanating out of SEBI Act - SC before passing impugned order had immaculately followed procedure contemplated under provisions of CPC as were relied upon by counsel for petitioner, before ordering petitioner's arrest and detention - Petition dismissed.

(F) Criminal - Banking & Finance - Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 21 r. 37, O. 21 r. 37(1), O. 21 r. 37(2), O. 21 r. 40, ss. 51, 55, 58, 58(1), 58(2) - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 125, 128, 145 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 129, 136, 137, 14, 141, 142, 19, 21, 32 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 109, 120B, 161, 165, 384, 420 - Securities and Exchange Board of India Act, 1992, ss. 11(3), 11C, 15U, 15Y, 15Z -Violation of rules - Natural Justice - Legality - Whether impugned order dtd 4-3-2014, was passed in violation of rules of natural justice -

Held, in response to prayer made by SEBI inter alia, seeking arrest and detention of petitioner and two other contemnors, petitioner filed personal reply by way of an affidavit - Petitioner in his written reply raised all possible legal and factual defenses - Different orders were passed from time to time in furtherance of prayers made in aforementioned interlocutory applications, including order preventing petitioner from leaving country, as also, order restraining two companies from parting with any movable or immovable property - A number of opportunities of hearing were given to counsel representing two companies and contemnors - Finding attitude of contemnors defiant and non-cooperative, their personal presence was ordered - Petitioner, who was directed to be present on 26-2-2014, did not enter personal appearance, his personal presence was enforced through non-bailable warrants on 4-3-2014 - During course of their personal presence in Court, petitioner and other contemnors were afforded an opportunity of oral hearing - Petitioner repeatedly addressed SC on 4-3-2014 - Only thereafter, impugned order was passed - It was not possible for SC to accept, that impugned order was passed without following rules of natural justice or without affording petitioner an opportunity of hearing - Petition dismissed.

(G) Criminal - Banking & Finance - Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 21 r. 37, O. 21 r. 37(1), O. 21 r. 37(2), O. 21 r. 40, ss. 51, 55, 58, 58(1), 58(2) - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 125, 128, 145 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 129, 136, 137, 14, 141, 142, 19, 21, 32 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 109, 120B, 161, 165, 384, 420 - Securities and Exchange Board of India Act, 1992, ss. 11(3), 11C, 15U, 15Y, 15Z -Cheating - Order - Vitiated - Legality - Whether impugned order dtd. 4-3-2014 was vitiated on account of bias -

Held, law laid down by SC in Jaswant Singh v. Virender Singh & Ors., was found to be fully applicable to facts of this case, particularly mannerism and demeanor exhibited by petitioner and some of counsel - Our recusal from case sought on ground of bias, was found to be devoid of any merit - Each and every insinuation levelled by petitioner during course of hearing, was considered and rejected on merits - Instant petition was not maintainable as no fresh petition was shown to be maintainable, under provisions arts. 129 and 142 of the Constitution relied upon by Counsel - SC deliberations on merits of controversy further revealed that there was neither any jurisdictional error, nor any error in law was shown to be made out, from impugned order dtd. 4-3-2014 - Instant petition was not maintainable - Petition dismissed.
