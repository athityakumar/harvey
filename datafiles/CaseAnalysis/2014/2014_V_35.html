Vijayander Kumar and others v State of Rajasthan and another

Supreme Court of India

11 February 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Shiva Kirti Singh, P. Sathasivam, Ranjan Gogoi

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 79; (2014) 3 SCC 389; 2014 (1) MLJ(Crl) 734; 2014 (1) RCR(Criminal) 983; 2014(2) SCALE 217; [2014] 1 S.C.R. 1012

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Sustainability, Quashing of proceedings

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 420 r/w s. 120B - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, s. 138 - Cheating - Criminal conspiracy - Quashing of proceedings - Sustainability - Case was registered against appellant accused u/s. 420 r/w s. 120-B IPC and u/s. 138 of the Act by respondents - HC quashed complaint against appellant under the Act - Appellant filed petition before HC for quashing of FIR which was dismissed and held that case was worthy of investigation - Police concluded investigation and submitted final report to Magistrate - Magistrate took cognizance of offence u/s. 420 r/w s. 120-B IPC against all appellants - Petition before HC which was dismissed - Hence instant appeal - Whether order passed by HC was sustainable -

Held, when informant and witnesses supported allegations made in FIR, it would not be proper for Court to evaluate merit of allegations on basis of documents annexed with memo of appeal - Such materials should be produced by appellants in their defence in accordance with law for due consideration at appropriate stage - Respondents was correct in contending that given set of facts might make out civil wrong as also criminal offence and only because civil remedy might also be available to informant/complainant that itself should not be ground to quash criminal proceeding - It was found that facts were properly noticed by HC on earlier occasion while examining petition preferred by appellants for quashing of FIR - Same view was reiterated by HC in order under appeal for not interfering with order of cognizance by Magistrate - Hence, there was no good ground to interfere with criminal proceedings against appellants - Appeal dismissed.
