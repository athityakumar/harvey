Ram Kishan v Tarun Bajaj and others

Supreme Court of India

17 January 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, J. Chelameswar

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 33; 2014 (3) ALD(SC) 28; 2014 (103) ALR 281; 2014 (3) AWC 2457; 2014 (2) CLT(SC) 294; 2014 (6) CTC 236; 2014 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 721; 2014 (175) PLR 765; 2014 (1) RCR(Criminal) 890; 2014 (123) RD 126; 2014(1) SCALE 401; [2014] 1 S.C.R. 538; 2014 (2) UPLBEC 1202

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Consequential benefits, Compulsorily retirement

Summary: (1) Contempt-Civil contempt-Wilful disobedience of order of court-No direction of any court to give benefit of revised post to applicant, nor his candidature ever considered for that post-State authority cannot be forced to pay salary to two persons for one post, particularly in view of fact that re-designated/promoted candidate never made party to lis, nor re-designation/promotion of that candidate ever challenged by applicant or someone else-Applicant could not point out service rules applicable to applicant to assess his eligibility etc.-No wilful disobedience of any order of court-In such fact-situation, leaving issue of entitlement of applicant, no case made out to initiate contempt proceedings against respondents-Contempt petition totally misconceived and devoid of merit-Contempt petition dismissed.

(2) Contempt - Contempt proceedings are quasi-criminal in nature, and, therefore, standard of proof required in these proceedings is beyond all reasonable doubt-It would rather be hazardous to impose sentence for contempt on authorities in exercise of contempt jurisdiction on mere probabilities.

(3) Contempt-Wilful disobedience of order-Even if there is disobedience of order, but such disobedience is result of some compelling circumstances under which it was not possible for contemnor to comply with order-Contemnor can be punished-In order to punish contemnor, it has to be established that disobedience of order is 'wilful'-Element of willingness is indispensable requirement to bring home charge within meaning of Contempt of Courts Act, 1971-Effect and purport of order is to be taken into consideration and same must be read in its entirety.
