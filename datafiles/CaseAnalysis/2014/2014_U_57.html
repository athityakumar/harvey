National Legal Services Authority v Union of India and others

Supreme Court of India

15 April 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan, A.K. Sikri

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 250; (2014) 5 SCC 438; AIR 2014 SC 1863; 2014 (4) ALD(SC) 125; 2014 (3) AWC 3116; 2014 (2) CLT(SC) 248; 2014 (3) CTC 46; 2014 (209) DLT 38; JT 2014 (5) SC 182; 2014 (2) KLT 378; 2014 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 2119; 2014 (3) MLJ 595; 2014 (3) RLW 2676; 2014(5) SCALE 1; [2014] 5 S.C.R. 119

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Human Rights

Keywords: Affirmative Action, Prisoners, Universal Declaration of Human Rights, Criminal Tribes Act, 1871, Sex Discrimination Act, 1984, U.S. Constitution, Sex Discrimination Amendment (Sexual Orientation, Gender Identity and Intersex Status) Act, 2013, Equal Treatment and the Promotion of Equal Opportunities Act, 2003, Alteration of Sex Description and Sex Status Act, 2003, Births, Deaths and Marriages Act, 1995, Legal Services Authority Act, 1997, Hate Crimes Prevention Act, 2009, General Recommendation Act, 2004, Gender Reassignment Act, 2000, Sex Discrimination Act, 1986, German Civil Statutes Act, Family Law Act, 1986, Equality Act, 2010

Summary: Constitution of India-Article 14, 15, 16, 19 and 14-Gender identity-Recognition of gender identity-Transgender community-Issue pertaining to constitutional and other legal rights of transgender community and their gender identity and sexual orientation-Transgender community as "third gender" for purpose of safeguarding and enforcing appropriating their rights guaranteed under Constitution-Non-recognition of identity of Trans-genders/Hijras in various legislations denies them equal protection of law and they face wide-spread discrimination-By recognition Transgenders as "third gender" they would be able to enjoy their human rights to which they are largely deprived for want of recognition-Directions issued to safeguard constitutional rights of members of Transgender community.
