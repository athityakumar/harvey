Sushil Kumar Singhal v Pramukh Sachiv Irrigation Department and others

Supreme Court of India

17 April 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Vikramajit Sen

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 267; 2014(4) ALL MR 909; 2014 (3) AWC 3162; 2014 (142) FLR 225; JT 2014 (7) SC 606; 2014(5) SCALE 431; 2014 (6) SLR 535

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Recovery of excess salary, Reducing pension

Summary: Employment-Recovery of excess salary paid and reduction in pension-By virtue of G.O. dated 16th January, 2007 mistake committed in pay fixation beyond period of 34 months prior to retirement of appellant could not be taken into account by respondent- employer-Admittedly, if any mistake committed in pay fixation, mistake committed in 1986, i.e., much prior to retirement of appellant-Therefore, by virtue of G.O. dated 16th January, 2007, neither any salary paid by mistake to appellant could be recovered nor pension of appellant could be reduced-As far as G.O. dated 16th January, 2007 is in force, respondent-employer could not have passed any order for recovery of excess salary paid to appellants or for reducing pension of appellant-If Government feels that mistakes are committed very often, it would be open for Government to change its policy-Impugned action of respondent-employer with regard to recovery of excess salary paid and reduction in pension-Not justified-Impugned judgment of High Court quashed-Respondents directed not to recover any amount of salary which already paid to appellant in pursuance of some mistake committed in pay fixation in 1986-Amount of pension also not to be reduced-Appellant to be paid pension as fixed earlier at time of his retirement.
