Sameer Singh and another v Abdul Rab and others

Supreme Court of India

14 October 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, V. Gopala Gowda

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 708; (2015) 1 SCC 379; AIR 2015 SC 591; 2014 (6) ALD(SC) 100; 2015(1) ALL MR 450; 2015 (110) ALR 516; 2015 (1) ALT(SC) 27; 2015 (1) AWC 321; 2014 (4) CLT(SC) 446; 2014 (6) CTC 98; JT 2014 (12) SC 149; 2014 (5) Law Herald (P&H) 3842; 2015 (3) MahLJ 489; 2015 (2) MPLJ 580; 2014 (4) RCR(Civil) 914; 2015 (128) RD 412; 2014(12) SCALE 111; [2014] 10 S.C.R. 1004

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Keywords: Jurisdiction of executing Court

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 21 rr. 97, 99, 101, 100, 103, s. 115 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 227 - Application for execution - Jurisdiction of executing court - Appellants applicants filed application under O. 21 rr. 97, 99 and 101 CPC and claimed that they should be put in possession of scheduled property and respondents be restrained from changing the nature and character of the property till adjudication of application - Respondents raised objection - Single Judge dismissed application holding that dispute between parties regarding jurisdiction of executing court could be determined under O. 21 r. 100 CPC and that when decision had been rendered on that score it would be deemed decree under O. 21 r. 103 CPC - Hence instant appeal - Whether executing court has jurisdiction to adjudicate application by appellants.

Held, if subordinate court exercises its jurisdiction not vested in it by law or fails to exercise the jurisdiction so vested, the said order u/s. 115 CPC is revisable as has been held in Joy Chand Lal Babu v Kamalaksha Chaudhury and Others 1949 Indlaw PC 28. The same principle has been reiterated in Keshardeo Chamria v Radha Kissen Chamria And Other sradha Kissen Chamria And Othe 1952 Indlaw SC 66 and Chaube Jagdish Prasad And Another v Ganga Prasad Chaturvedi 1958 Indlaw SC 55. After the amendment of s. 115 CPC with effect from 1-7-2002, the said power is exercised u/art. 227 of the Constitution as per the principle laid down in Surya Dev Rai v Ram Chander Rai and Others 2003 Indlaw SC 598. Had the executing court apart from expressing the view that it had become functus officio had adjudicated the issues on merits, question would have been different for in that event there would have been an adjudication. The HC has fallen into error by opining that the decision rendered by executing court is decree and therefore appeal should have been filed and resultantly allow the appeal and set aside the impugned order. The HC shall decide the matter as necessary u/art. 227 of the Constitution. Appeal allowed.
