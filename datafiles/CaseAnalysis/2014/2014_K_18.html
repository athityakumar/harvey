Renikuntla Rajamma (Dead) by Lrs. v K. Sarwanamma

Supreme Court of India

17 July 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, C. Nagappan, V. Gopala Gowda

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 458; (2014) 9 SCC 445; AIR 2014 SC 2906; 2014 (5) ALD(SC) 173; 2014(6) ALL MR 976; 2015 (108) ALR 140; 2014 (6) ALT(SC) 21; 2014 (4) AWC 4226; 2014 (5) Bom.C.R. 305; 2014 (3) CLT(SC) 138; 2014 (4) CTC 572; JT 2014 (8) SC 283; 2014 (3) KLT 469; 2014 (6) MLJ 102; 2015(1) M.P.H.T. 396; 2015 (1) MPLJ 15; 2014 (2) OLR 727; 2014 (175) PLR 792; 2014 (3) RCR(Civil) 925; 2015 (126) RD 616; 2014(8) SCALE 579; [2014] 8 S.C.R. 890; 2014 (3) WLN(SC) 100

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Suit for cancellation of revocation deed, Revocation of Gift deed

Summary: Land & Property - Family & Personal - Practice & Procedure - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, ss. 12, 123 - Suit for cancellation of revocation deed - Delivery of possession - Revocation of Gift deed - Legality - Respondent (plaintiff) filed a suit for a declaration to the effect that revocation deed dt.5-3-1986 executed by Appellant (defendant) purporting to revoke a gift deed earlier executed by her was null and void - Plaintiff's case was that gift deed executed by defendant was valid in the eyes of law and had been accepted by plaintiff when the donee (defendant) had reserved to herself during for life, the right to enjoy the benefits arising from suit property - Purported revocation of gift in favour of plaintiff in terms of revocation deed was, on that basis, assailed and a declaration about its being invalid and void ab initio prayed for - Trial Court found that defendant failed to prove that gift deed set up by plaintiff was vitiated by fraud or undue influence or that it was a sham or nominal document - Gift, according to trial Court, had been validly made and accepted by plaintiff, hence, irrevocable in nature - In first appeal preferred against said judgment and decree, First Appellate Court affirmed the view taken by Trial Court and held that plaintiff had satisfactorily proved the execution of a valid gift in his favour and that the revocation of a validly made gift deed was legally impermissible - First Appellate Court also affirmed the finding of Trial Court that the donee had accepted the gift made in his favour - Appeal filed by defendant was on those findings dismissed - Aggrieved defendant filed second appeal before HC, which was dismissed by impugned judgments and orders holding that case set up by defendant that the gift was vitiated by undue influence or fraud had been thoroughly disproved at trial - Hence, instant appeal - Conflict between 2 earlier decisions of SC in Naramadaben Maganlal Thakker v. Pranjivandas Maganlal Thakker & Ors, 1996 Indlaw SC 2529 and K. Balakrishnan v. K. Kamalam & Ors, 2003 Indlaw SC 1263 has led to instant reference for an authoritative pronouncement as to the true and correct interpretation of ss. 122 and 123 of 1882 Act - Defendant contended that since the donor had retained to herself the right to use the property and to receive rents during her life time, such a reservation or retention rendered the gift invalid and that a conditional gift was not envisaged by the provisions of 1882 Act and that gift deed failed to transfer, title, possession and the right to deal with the property in absolute terms in favour of the donee the same was no gift in the eyes of law -

Held, there was indeed no provision in law that ownership in property could not be gifted without transfer of possession of such property - S. 123 of 1882 Act did not make the delivery of possession of the gifted property essential for validity of a gift - It was true that the attention of Court did not appear to have been drawn to earlier decision in 1996 Indlaw SC 2529 where SC had on a reading of the recital of the gift deed and the cancellation deed held that the gift was not complete - SC had in that case found that donee had not accepted the gift thereby making the gift incomplete - Court, further, held that donor cancelled the gift within a month of gift and subsequently executed a Will in favour of the appellant on a proper construction of the deed and the deed cancelling the same Court held that the gift in favour of the donee was conditional and that there was no acceptance of the same by the done - Gift deed conferred limited right upon the donee and was to become operative after the death of the done - Said decision clearly rests on facts of that case - If the gift was conditional and there was no acceptance of the donee it could not operate as a gift - Absolute transfer of ownership in the gifted property in favour of the donee was absent in that case which led SC to hold that the gift was conditional and had to become operative only after the death of the done - Judgment was in that view clearly distinguishable and could not be read to be an authority for the proposition that delivery of possession was an essential requirement for making a valid gift - In instant case, execution of registered gift deed and its attestation by 2 witnesses was not in dispute - It had also been concurrently held by all the three Courts below that the donee had accepted the gift - Recitals in the gift deed also prove transfer of absolute title in the gifted property from the donor to the done - What was retained was only the right to use the property during the lifetime of the donor which did not in any way affect the transfer of ownership in favour of the donee by the donor - HC was in that view perfectly justified in refusing to interfere with the decree passed in favour of done - K. Balakrishnan v. K. Kamalam & Ors, 2003 Indlaw SC 1263, relied on - Appeal dismissed.
