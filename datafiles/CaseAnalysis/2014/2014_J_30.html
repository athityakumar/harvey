Perumal v Janaki

Supreme Court of India

20 January 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

J. Chelameswar, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 32; (2014) 5 SCC 377; 2014 CRLJ 1454; 2014 (1) CTC 664; JT 2014 (2) SC 180; 2014 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 726; 2014 (1) MLJ(Crl) 505; 2014 (1) RCR(Criminal) 851; 2014(1) SCALE 406; [2014] 1 S.C.R. 591

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Superintendence

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 417, 506 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 190, 193, 195 - Private complaint - Dismissal - Sustainability - Complaint was filed against appellant by N - Respondent registered the complaint u/ss. 417 and 506 IPC - Trial Court acquitted appellant against both charges - Appellant filed complaint before Judicial Magistrate u/s. 190 CrPC against respondent for an offence u/s. 193 IPC - Complaint was dismissed as not maintainable - Aggrieved on appeal HC the dismissed the appeal - Hence instant appeal - Whether Judicial Magistrate was justified in dismissing the complaint - Held, in the light of the language of s. 195 CrPC SC did not find fault with the conclusion of the Magistrate in dismissing the complaint of appellant for the reason that the complaint was not filed by the person contemplated u/s. 195 CrPC - Court before which the instant complaint was lodged was not the same Court before which appellant was prosecuted by respondent - In instant case when appellant alleged that he had been prosecuted on the basis of a palpably false statement coupled with the further allegation in his complaint that respondent did so for extraneous considerations, SC was of the opinion that it was an appropriate case where the HC ought to have exercised the jurisdiction u/s. 195 CrPC - Allegation such as the one made by complainant against respondent was not uncommon - As was pointed earlier by this Court in a different context 'there was no rule of law that common sense should be put in cold storage' in Prakash Chandra Mehta v. Commissioner and Secretary, Government of Kerala & Ors., 1985 Indlaw SC 76 - Matter was remitted to the HC for further appropriate course of action to initiate proceedings against respondent on the basis of the complaint of appellant in accordance with law - Appeal allowed.
