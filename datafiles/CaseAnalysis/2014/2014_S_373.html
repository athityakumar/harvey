Anil @ Anthony Arikswamy Joseph v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

20 February 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Radhakrishnan, Vikramajit Sen

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 103; (2014) 4 SCC 69; 2014 ALL MR (Cri) 1080; 2014 CRLJ 1608; JT 2014 (3) SC 217; 2014 (2) MLJ(Crl) 160; 2014 (1) RCR(Criminal) 1004; 2014(2) SCALE 554; [2014] 3 S.C.R. 34

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Cries

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 377, 201 - Unnatural sex - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant/accused committed unnatural carnal intercourse on a minor boy aged 10 years and after that strangulated him to death - Charge sheet against appellant was filed u/ss. 302, 377 and 201 IPC - Trial Court convicted appellant - On appeal filed by appellant HC dismissed the appeal - Hence instant appeals - Whether the society abhors such crimes and whether such crimes shock the conscience of the society and attract intense and extreme indignation of the community -

Held, SC had no doubt that such type of crimes preceded by Pederasty were extremely brutal, grotesque diabolical and revolting, which shock the moral fiber of the society, especially when the passive agent was a minor - SC found that there was no mitigating circumstance favouring appellant, age was not a factor favouring him - In instant case offence u/s. 377 IPC had fully proved so also the offence u/s. 302 IPC - Indian society and also the International society abhor pederasty, an unnatural sex, i.e. carnal intercourse between a man and a minor boy or a girl - When the victim was a minor, consent was not a defence, irrespective of the views expressed at certain quarters on consensual sex between adults - Murder was committed in an extremely brutal, grotesque, diabolical and dastardly manner and appellant was in a dominating position and the victim was an innocent boy, the only son of his mother - Appellant was aged 35 years when the crime was committed he was a fully matured person - Life of a boy, the only son of PW7, the mother, was taken away in a gruesome and barbaric manner which pricks not only the judicial conscience but also the conscience of the society - From the evidence of PW2, 3, 8 and 9 in its entirety and, in SC's view, they were trustworthy and reliable - Prosecution succeeded in establishing its case beyond reasonable doubt that the deceased was last seen in the company of accused and that the findings recorded by Trial Court and affirmed by HC call for no interference - SC was of the view that incarceration of a further period of thirty years, without remission, in addition to the sentence already undergone - would be an adequate punishment, rather than death sentence - Appeals disposed of.
