Shiv Chander and others v Lieutenant Governor and others

Supreme Court of India

07 March 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, C. Nagappan

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 753; (2014) 11 SCC 744; AIR 2014 SC 2669; [2014] 4 S.C.R. 417

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Res Judicata, Refusal, Andaman and Nicobar Islands Land Revenue and Land Reforms Regulation, 1966, Andaman and Nicobar Islands (Land Tenure) Regulation, 1926

Summary: Land & Property - Administrative - Andaman and Nicobar Islands (Land Tenure) Regulation, 1926 - Andaman and Nicobar Islands Land Revenue and Land Reforms Regulation, 1966 - Grant of land - Predecessor of appellants was allotted a plot of land in terms of regn. 4 of 1926 Regulations for a period of 30 years but could be renewed for another term of 30 years - Grant was renewed till 1994 - With the expiry of a total period of 60 years, appellant was called upon to release the land property in favour of the Administration as the same was required for developmental purposes - Writ petition was filed, wherein it was held that the petitioner had no right in the land - Second renewal of the grant was held to be impermissible - Representations filed, were also rejected - Writ petition filed thereagainst was allowed - Appeal was filed thereagainst before SC - SC set aside the order passed by the HC holding that the order passed by the Lieutenant Governor was legal and proper - Deputy Commissioner relying upon the decision of SC directed the petitioners to handover the possession of the subject land - Writ petition was filed thereagainst - HC held that the petitioners were not entitled to raise any question relating to the refusal of renewal or a fresh grant in their favour in the light of the judgment of SC and the orders passed in the earlier stages of the proceedings - Appeal filed thereagainst was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal.

Whether the appellants are debarred from resisting eviction from the land in question on the ground that they have acquired the right to continue in possession even without renewal and a fresh grant in their favour under the 1966 Regulation.

Held, doctrine of res judicata is founded on a two-fold logic, namely, (1) that there must be finality to adjudication by competent Court and (2) no man should be vexed twice for the same cause. Principles of constructive res judicata which are also a part of the very same doctrine have been held to be applicable to writ proceedings. Hence, it is no longer open to the appellants to contend that the principles of constructive res judicata would not debar them from raising the question which, as observed earlier, could and indeed ought to have been raised by them in the previous round of litigation. Further, essence of the Regulation in so far as right of a grantee to continue in possession is concerned, is the same under the 1926 Regulations and the subsequent Regulations of the year 1966. In either of the cases, the grantee cannot stay in possession for more than 60 years. The appellants, in the instant case, no doubt may have protection under the 1966 Regulations because the grant in their favour was deemed to have been renewed upto 1994 was in existence in 1966 but such protection would cease with the expiry of the 60 years period in 1994. Appeal dismissed.
