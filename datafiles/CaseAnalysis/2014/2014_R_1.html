R. N. Agarwal v R. C. Bansal and others

Supreme Court of India

14 October 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

M.Y. Eqbal, Pinaki Chandra Ghose

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 714; (2015) 1 SCC 48; JT 2014 (12) SC 79; 2014 (5) Law Herald (P&H) 3790; 2014 (4) MLJ(Crl) 414; 2014 (4) RCR(Criminal) 644; 2014(12) SCALE 89

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Fraudulently

Summary: Criminal - Trusts & Associations - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120(B), 420, 468, 471, 217 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988 s.13(1)(d) - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 ss. 190, 193 - Forged documents - Investigation - Cognizance - Summons - Challenged - Some persons who were not members of society, forged records of society to show that original members of society had resigned and new members were enrolled - Forged records were submitted in office of Registrar of Co-operative societies and Delhi Development Authority (DDA) was approached for allotment of land by certifying new list of members and meetings were duly held - On completion of investigation CBI filed charge sheet against six persons, Trial court took cognizance of offences punishable u/ss. 120(B), 420, 468,471 of IPC and u/s.13(1)(d) of 1988 Act, ordered for summoning accused - Meanwhile appellant moved application u/s.190 r/w s. 193 CrPC before Special Judge for summoning three more persons who were prosecution witness as accused which was allowed and directed CBI to register case against IO u/s. 217 of IPC for letting these three persons - Aggrieved by order prosecution witness/respondents filed separate petitions before HC u/s. 482 CrPC and CBI filed revision petition, which quashed orders passed by Special Judge and allowed petitions filed by respondents and revision filed by CBI - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, HC instead of relying on decisions of instant Court reversed the order passed by Special Judge by following the decision of Delhi HC in Anirudh Sen vs. State, (2006) 3 JCC 2081, (Delhi), -Therefore, impugned order passed by HC quashing issuance of summons by Special Judge against respondents is erroneous in law and cannot be sustained. However, it was not necessary for the Special Judge to issue directions to CBI to get a case registered against the guilty officers who have investigated the case. Hence, appeals were allowed and quashed orders passed by HC and restore order passed by Special Judge except direction issued to CBI. - Appeals allowed.

Ratio : special judge may take cognizance of the offence without the accused being committed to him for trial and the court of special judge shall be deemed to be a court of session.
