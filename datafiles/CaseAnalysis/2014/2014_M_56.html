Manohar Lal v State of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

01 July 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 411; (2014) 9 SCC 645; AIR 2014 SC 2555; JT 2014 (8) SC 265; 2014 (3) RCR(Criminal) 787; 2015 (1) RLW 488; 2014(8) SCALE 89; [2014] 6 S.C.R. 263

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 304B and 498A - Dowry death - Conviction - Challenged - Accused persons were arrested and dowry articles were recovered and were taken into possession - After completion of the investigation, all the accused persons were charge-sheeted for offence u/s. 498A r/w s. 34 of IPC and s. 304B r/w s. 34 of IPC - Trial Court acquitted all the accused persons except appellant who was convicted for the offence punishable u/s. 304B of IPC - On appeal, HC upheld the conviction and sentence of the appellant - Hence, instant appeal - Whether conviction of appellant u/s. 304B of IPC was justified -

Held, as per the statement of PW1 death took place within seven years of marriage and death of the deceased was due to burning i.e. not in normal circumstances - However, statement of the complainant PW1 was general and not specific - No specific incidence had been indicated suggesting the cruelty or harassment made by the appellant - Though the allegation of demand of dowry was made none of the witnesses including PW1 stated that the deceased was harassed 'soon before her death' for or in connection with demand of dowry - Also, Trial Court had not convicted the appellant u/s. 498A of IPC - Thus, prosecution failed to prove that the appellant harassed the deceased soon before her death for or in connection with a demand of dowry - Judgment passed by the Trial Court as upheld by HC by impugned judgment could not be upheld - Appellant acquitted - Appeal allowed.
