Paramsivam and others v State Through Inspector of Police

Supreme Court of India

01 July 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 401; AIR 2014 SC 2936; 2014 CRLJ 4085; JT 2014 (8) SC 585; 2014 (4) KarLJ 197; 2014 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 2889; 2014 (3) MLJ(Crl) 682; 2015 (1) RCR(Criminal) 305; 2014(8) SCALE 105; [2014] 8 S.C.R. 813

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss.302 and 364 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s.27 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellants allegedly committed offence of kidnapping and murder - Appellants were convicted by Trial Court u/ss.364 and 302 IPC - Hence instant appeal - Whether conviction of appellants was sustainable -

Held, in the instant case, the prosecution brought on record the evidences that accused no.1 to 3 had abducted the deceased - Therefore, it was accused nos.1 to 3 alone knew what happened to him as the deceased was found murdered within a short time after abduction - Accused nos.1 to 3 had failed to give any explanation and the Court rightly draw presumption that the accused have murdered the deceased - Prosecution relied upon the statement of accused persons leading to discovery of facts as envisaged u/s 27 of Act - Accused Nos. 1, 2 and 5 were arrested on 28-1-2004 at different times - Confession statement of accused no.1 led to recovery of Voter ID Card of Mani (MO9) and other arts. i.e. Mos.4 and 5 - Confession statement recorded from accused no.2 led to recovery of Pocket Diary (MO2), Money purse (MO3) with photo of deceased and other articles-Mos.10 to 12 - Confession statement of accused no.5 Driver led to seizure of Taxi MO1-Maruthi Omni Van - Accused no.3 and accused no.4 were surrendered before the Court and on application, PW41/IO took them for police custody - Confession statement recorded from accused no.3 led to recovery of Torch light(MO6) which was handed over to the deceased by PW2 at the time when he left the house - PW2 also identified MO6 as the torch light handed over by her at the time when the deceased left the house - Recovery of various articles of the deceased from accused nos.1 to 3 was a strong incriminating circumstance connecting the appellants with the crime - SC had noticed the confessional statement of the appellants, on the basis of which the discovery of material evidence took place - From the evidence on record, SC found that the prosecution was successful in bringing on record the circumstantial evidences i.e. existence of motive; the circumstances in which the deceased was last seen alive in the company of appellants-accused nos.1 to 3; death was homicidal and body was found on the railway track mutilated; the body of the deceased was identified through DNA test; on arrest of accused incriminating articles were recovered - Upon analysis of evidence, SC was of the view that prosecution had succeeded in proving the facts that the accused nos.1 to 3 took away deceased - What happened thereafter to deceased was especially within the knowledge of the appellants-accused nos.1 to 3 - It was for the appellants-accused nos. 1 to 3 to explain what happened to deceased after they took him away but they failed to explain the same - Deceased was found dead immediately thereafter - Therefore, it was clear that the accused nos.1 to 3 who abducted deceased intentionally withhold the information from the Court and, therefore, there was every justification for drawing inference that appellants-accused nos.1 to 3 murdered deceased - Stand of the appellants was a bare denial of prosecution case - In the absence of any explanation, the inevitable inference was that appellants were responsible for the death of deceased - Thus, the guilt of the appellants-accused nos. 1 to 3 was proved beyond all reasonable doubt - Appeal dismissed.
