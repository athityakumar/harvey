State of Punjab v Gurmit Singh

Supreme Court of India

02 July 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Chandramauli Kumar Prasad, Pinaki Chandra Ghose

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 436; (2014) 9 SCC 632; 2014 (6) ADJ 98; AIR 2014 SC 2561; 2014 CRLJ 3586; 2014 (3) JCC 2023; JT 2014 (8) SC 195; 2014 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 2896; 2014 (2) OLR 570; 2014 (3) RCR(Criminal) 584; 2014 (3) RLW 2131; 2014(8) SCALE 259; [2014] 9 S.C.R. 182

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s.319 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s.304B - Summons - Set aside - Sustainability - State of Punjab aggrieved by the order passed by HC in Criminal Revision whereby it had set aside the order of the trial court summoning the respondent to face trial u/s. 319 of CrPC - Whether setting aside of summoning order passed by the respondent to face trial u/s. 319 of CrPC was valid -

Held, it was well known rule of construction that when the Legislature used same words in different part of the statute, the presumption was that those words were used in the same sense, unless displaced by the context - SC did not find anything in context to deviate from the general rule of interpretation - Hence, SC had no manner of doubt that the word 'relative of the husband' in s.304 B of the IPC would mean such persons, who were related by blood, marriage or adoption - When SC could apply the principle the respondent was not related to the husband of the deceased either by blood or marriage or adoption - Hence, HC did not err in passing the impugned order - SC hastened to add that a person, not a relative of the husband, might not be prosecuted for offence u/s.304B IPC but that did not mean that such a person could not be prosecuted for any other offence viz. s.306 IPC, in case the allegations constitute offence other than s.304B IPC - Appeal dismissed.
