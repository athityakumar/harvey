Puducherry S. C. People Welfare Association v Chief Secretary to Government, Union Territory of Pondicherry and others

Supreme Court of India

07 August 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, Kurian Joseph, Madan B. Lokur

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 525; (2014) 9 SCC 236; AIR 2015 SC 880; 2014 (5) AWC 5126; 2014(9) SCALE 385; 2015 (1) SLR 680

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Constitution (Pondicherry) Scheduled Castes Order, 1964, Constitution (Scheduled Castes) Order (Second Amendment) Act, 2002, Presidential Order, 1964

Summary: Service - Reservation benefits - Entitlement of - Appellant was an association representing the Scheduled Caste residents who had permanent residence/place of abode in Puducherry - The association filed two Writ Petitions before the HC challenging the two Government Orders which was related to reservation benefits in promotion and employment to Group C and D posts and the other G.O.M. 12/2005 related to reservation benefits in professional courses - HC was not persuaded by the arguments of the writ petitioner and dismissed the two Writ Petitions by a common order dt. 21-7-2008 - Hence instant appeal - Whether Scheduled Caste candidates were eligible for reservation -

Held, it was important to bear in mind that it was by virtue of the notification of President u/art. 341(1) of Constitution that the Scheduled Castes came into being - The members of the Scheduled Castes were drawn from castes, races or tribes, they attain a new status by virtue of Presidential Order - Cl. (2) of Art. 341 empowered Parliament alone by law to include or exclude from the list of Scheduled Castes specified in a notification issued u/cl. (1) by the President - By no executive power, the amendment, modification, alteration or variance in the Presidential Order was permissible - It was not open to the executive to do anything directly or indirectly which might lead to any change in the Presidential Order - Once Presidential Order was issued u/art. 341(1) or Art. 342(1) of Constitution, any amendment in the Presidential Order could only be made by the Parliament by law as provided in arts. 341(2) or 342(2) of Constitution, as the case may be, and in no other manner - Interpretation of 'resident' in the Presidential Order as 'of origin' amounts to altering the Presidential Order - Thus, SC found that the impugned Government Orders - G.O.M. 11/2005 and G.O.M. 12/2005 - not being in conformity and consonance with the Presidential Order, 1964 could not be sustained in law and have to be set aside - Appeals allowed.
