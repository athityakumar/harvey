Gulf Goans Hotels Company Limited and another v Union of India and others

Supreme Court of India

22 September 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjan Gogoi, M.Y. Eqbal

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 641; (2014) 10 SCC 673; AIR 2015 SC 2032; JT 2014 (10) SC 557; 2014 (4) KLT 862; 2015 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 844; 2014(11) SCALE 83; [2014] 10 S.C.R. 536

Case Digest

Subject: Environment, Wildlife & Animal; Land & Property

Keywords: Environment (Protection) Act, 1986, Air Corporations Act, 1953, Air Act, 1981, Jaipur Opium Act, 1923, Authentication (Orders and other Instruments) Rules, 1958

Summary: Environment, Wildlife & Animal - Land & Property - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 21 and 136 - Environment Protection Act, 1986, ss. 3 and 6 - Violation of Coastal Regulation Zone (CRZ) - Demolition order - Legality - Appellant hotel owners allegedly raised construction violating CRZ - Respondent authorities ordered for demolition - HC upheld demolition order - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, constructions raised by the appellants are not per se illegal in the conventional sense. They are not without permission and sanction of the competent authority. The guidelines, are not 'law' so as to constitute activities contrary thereto as acts of infringement of the law and hence illegal. Such guidelines do not confer the power of enforcement and lack the authority to bring about any penal consequences. In the absence of due authentication and promulgation of the guidelines, the contents thereof cannot be treated as an order of the Govt. and would really represent an expression of opinion. Further, in the event the statute does not contain any prescription and even under the subordinate legislation there is silence in the matter, the legislation will take effect only when it is published through the customarily recognized official channel, namely, the official gazette. The 'guidelines' were not gazetted. Hence, violation of art. 21 on account of alleged environmental violation cannot be subjectively and individually determined when parameters of permissible/impermissible conduct are required to be legislatively or statutorily determined u/ss. 3 and 6 of the Act which has been so done by bringing into force CRZ Notification w.e.f. 19-2-1991. The orders impugned in the writ petitions filed by the appellants cannot be sustained. Appeals allowed.
