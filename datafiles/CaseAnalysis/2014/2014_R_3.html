Registrar General, High Court of Madras v R. Gandhi and another

Supreme Court of India

05 March 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, M.Y. Eqbal, J. Chelameswar

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 149; (2014) 11 SCC 547; 2014 (3) AWC 2571; 2014 (2) CLT(SC) 64; JT 2014 (4) SC 261; 2014 (2) MLJ 621; 2014 (175) PLR 360; 2014 (2) RCR(Civil) 275; [2014] 4 S.C.R. 77; 2014 (3) SLR 85; 2014 (2) WLN(SC) 22

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Constitution

Summary: Constitution of India-Articles 217 (2) and 124 (3)-Appointment of High Court Judge or Supreme Court Judge-Judicial review of appointments as High Court Judge or Supreme Court Judge-Is permissible only on assessment of eligibility and not on suitability-Suitability cannot be subject-matter of judicial review-Even when President, has appointed person to constitutional office, qualification of that person to hold that office can be examined in quo warranto proceedings and appointment can be quashed-In instant case to adjudge suitability, writ petitioners or members of Bar could approach C.J.I. or Law Minister-But instead of resorting to such procedure, writ petitioners adopted unwarranted short cut knowing it fully well that on ground of suitability, writ petitions not maintainable.
