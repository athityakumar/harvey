Binod Kumar and others v State of Bihar and another

Supreme Court of India

30 October 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

R. Banumathi, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 741; (2014) 10 SCC 663; JT 2014 (12) SC 286; 2014 (4) RCR(Criminal) 881; 2014(12) SCALE 465; [2014] 11 S.C.R. 85; 2015 (2) WLN(SC) 13

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Quashing of criminal case

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 406, 120B - Criminal conspiracy - Quashing of criminal case - Entitled to - Respondent No.2 filed criminal complaint for criminal breach of trust, alleging that the amount of Rs.34,505/- was not paid to him and that amount was utilized by appellants in some other work before Trial Court - Appellants accused filed application for discharge from criminal case - Petition was dismissed and framed charges u/ss. 406, 120B IPC - Appellants filed petition before HC for quashing of impugned order - Petition was dismissed by HC - Hence instant appeal - Whether criminal complaint was liable to be quashed.

Held, no allegations are made attracting the ingredients of s. 405 IPC. Likewise, there are no allegations as to cheating or dishonest intention of appellants in retaining the money in order to have wrongful gain to themselves or causing wrongful loss to complainant. Excepting the bald allegations that appellants did not make payment to respondent no.2 and that appellants utilized amounts either by themselves or for some other work, there is no iota of allegation as to the dishonest intention in misappropriating the property. To make out case of criminal breach of trust, it is not sufficient to show that money has been retained by appellants. It must also be shown that the appellants dishonestly disposed of the same in some way or dishonestly retained the same. The mere fact that appellants did not pay money to the complainant does not amount to criminal breach of trust. Even if all allegations in the complaint taken at face value are true, basic essential ingredients of dishonest misappropriation and cheating are missing. Criminal proceedings are not a short cut for other remedies. Since no case of criminal breach of trust or dishonest intention of inducement is made out and the essential ingredients of ss. 405, 420 IPC are missing, prosecution of appellants u/ss. 406, 120B IPC is liable to be quashed. Hence, impugned order of HC is set aside. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - At the stage of quashing complaint/FIR, HC is not to embark upon enquiry as to probability, reliability or genuineness of the allegations made.
