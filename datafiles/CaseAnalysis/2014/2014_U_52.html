Ishwar Chandra Jayaswal v Union of India and others

Supreme Court of India

03 January 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Vikramajit Sen, T. S. Thakur

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 1; (2014) 2 SCC 748; 2014(2) ALL MR 952; 2014 (1) AWC 578; 2014 (140) FLR 914; 2014(1) SCALE 155; [2014] 1 S.C.R. 59; 2014 (5) SLR 667; 2014 (1) UPLBEC 625; 2014 (1) WLN(SC) 79

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Railway Servants (Discipline & Appeal) Rules, 1968, Illegal demand of money, Removal from service, Proportionality

Summary: Railway Servants (Discipline and Appeal) Rules, 1968 (Rules 1968)-Part III Penalty 6 (vii)-Employment-Removal-Appellant taken very small sums of money for issuance of Fit Certificate to other Railway employees-Not established that appellant had, as matter of habit or an wide scale, made illegal demands from Railway servants desirous of obtaining Fit Certificate-Appellant presently 75 years of age-At time when Articles of charge served upon him, he had already give best part of his life to service of respondent-Indian Railways-Appellant served respondents for period of twenty three years and removal from service for two charges levelled against him shocks judicial conscience of court-Part III of Rules 1968 contains penalties that can be imposed against Railway servant, both minor penalties as well as major penalties-Since two of three charges proved, imposition of compulsory retirement under Part III penalty 6 (vii) would better and more appropriately met ends of justice-While this would instill sufficient degree of fear is mind of employees, it would also set at naught several years of service which appellant already given to respondent-Indian Railways-Deprivation of retiral benefits in addition to loss of service entirely incommensurate with charge of appellant-Appellant deemed to have compulsorily retired under Part III Penalty 6 (vii)-Impugned judgment and order of Division Bench of High Court-Set aside.
