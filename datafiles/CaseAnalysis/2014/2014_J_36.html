State of (NCT of Delhi) and others v Jaysukh Bavanji Shingalia and another

Supreme Court of India

04 September 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

M.Y. Eqbal, Pinaki Chandra Ghose

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 580; (2014) 9 SCC 772; AIR 2015 SC 75; 2014 CRLJ 4854; 2015 (1) G.L.R. 778; JT 2014 (10) SC 9; 2014 (3) KLT 1033; 2014 (4) RCR(Criminal) 211; 2015 (2) RLW 1456; 2014(10) SCALE 166; [2014] 9 S.C.R. 1063

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Mines & Minerals; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Sea Customs Act, Gold (Control) Act, 1968, Environment, Wild Life (Protection) Act, 1972, Same Offence, Two Offences, Distinct, Prohibitory Order, Extraction, Rajasthan Sati (Prevention) Ordinance, 1987, Tamil Nadu Mines and Minerals Concession Rules, 1959, Gujarat Mineral (Prevention of Illegal Mining, Transportation and Storage) Rules, 2005, Madhya Pradesh Trade Tax Act, Illegal mining

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Mines & MInerals - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 378, 379, 114 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 2(c), 2(d), 2(h), 482 - Mines and Mineral (Development and Regulation) Act, 1957, ss. 21, 22 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 20 - Illegal mining - Cognizance - Legality - Appellants (accused) were allegedly involved in illegal mining of sand, illegal mining in those areas where miming lease was already revoked and transporting loads of black trap stone - Criminal complaints were lodged against appellants for alleged offence u/ss. 379, 114 of IPC and 21(4) of the Act - Aggrieved appellants filed applications u/s. 482 of CrPC before HC for quashing of criminal proceedings on the ground that cognizance should have been taken under the Act that too on the basis of complaint to be filed u/s. 22 of the Act by an authorized officer - HC by impugned order held that offence under the Act being cognizable offence, Police could have registered an FIR, however, cognizance under the Act, it could be taken by Magistrate only on the basis of a complaint filed by an authorized officer, and question of offence u/s. 21 of the Act being an offence u/s. 379 of IPC did not arise because the Act makes illegal mining as an offence only when there was no permit/licence for such extraction and a complaint in that regard was filed by an authorized officer - Hence, instant appeals - Whether provisions contained in ss. 21, 22 of 1957 Act and other sections operate as bar against prosecution of a person who was charged with allegation which constitutes offences u/ss. 379, 114 of IPC and other provisions -

Held, considering the principles of interpretation and the wordings used in s. 22 of the Act, the provision is not a complete and absolute bar for taking action by the police for illegal and dishonestly committing theft of minerals including sand from the river bed. The prohibition contained in s. 22 of the Act against prosecution of a person except on a complaint made by the officer is attracted only when such person sought to be prosecuted for contravention of s. 4 of the Act and not for any act or omission which constitute an offence under IPC. In a case where there is a theft of sand and gravels from the Govt. land, the police can register a case, investigate the same and submit a final report u/s. 173 of CrPC before a Magistrate having jurisdiction for the purpose of taking cognizance as provided in s. 190(1)(d) of CrPC. Ingredients constituting the offence under the Act and ingredients of dishonestly removing sand and gravel from the river beds without consent, which is the property of the State, is a distinct offence under the IPC. Hence, for the commission of offence u/s. 378 of IPC, on receipt of the police report, the Magistrate having jurisdiction can take cognizance of the said offence without awaiting the receipt of complaint that may be filed by the authorized officer for taking cognizance in respect of violation of various provisions of the Act. Appeals disposed of.

Ratio - S. 22 of the Act was not a complete and absolute bar for taking action by police, except prosecution for contravention of s. 4 of the Act, for illegal and dishonestly committing theft of minerals including sand from river bed.
