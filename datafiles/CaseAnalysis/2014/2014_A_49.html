National Aluminium Company Limited and others v Ananta Kishore Rout and others

Supreme Court of India

08 May 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, Surinder Singh Nijjar

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 343; (2014) 6 SCC 756; 2014 (4) AWC 3457; 2014 (142) FLR 643; JT 2014 (6) SC 153; 2014 (6) MLJ 115; 2014 (2) OLR 83; 2014(6) SCALE 540; [2014] 7 S.C.R. 406; 2014 (4) SLR 483

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Pay Scales

Summary: Service - Orissa Education Act - Societies Registration Act, 1860 - Pay scales - Legality - Appellant/National Aluminium Company Limited (NALCO) had established two schools for the benefit of wards of its-employees - Two Writ Petitions were filed before HC by employees of each school for declaration that they were employees of NALCO and be treated as such, with consequential prayer that these employees be also accorded suitable pay scales as admissible to employees of NALCO - HC allowed the petitions - Hence instant appeals - Whether impugned order passed by HC was sustainable -

Held, only because Saraswati Vidya Mandir (SVS) agreed to take over employees, would not mean that appellant becomes employer - This clause suggests that but for intervention of appellant, school staff that was engaged by Central Chinmoy Mission Trust (CCMT) would have been dealt with by CCMT - It was matter of record that CCMT runs other schools as well - Appellant was directed to stand committed by assurance and would adhere to same for all times to come - Position which emerged, in view of aforesaid assurance, was that service tenure of these employees was protected - Employees directly employed by appellant were discharging altogether different kinds of duties - Main activity of appellant was manufacture and production of alumina and aluminum for which it had its manufacturing units - Process and method of recruitment of those employees, their eligibility conditions for appointment, nature of job done by those employees etc. was entirely different from employees of these schools - Cost of repetition that there was no parity in nature of work, mode of appointment, experience, educational qualifications between appellant employees and employees of two schools - Such a comparison could be made with their counter parts in Govt. schools or aided or unaided schools - There could not be any grievance of staff which was getting better emoluments and enjoying far superior service conditions - Impugned order passed by HC was un-sustainable and was set aside - Appeals allowed.
