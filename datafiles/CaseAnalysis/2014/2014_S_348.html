P. L. Tatwal v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

19 February 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Kurian Joseph, Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 119; (2014) 11 SCC 431; 2014 ALL MR (Cri) 1470; 2014 CRLJ 1880; 2014 (2) JabLJ 122; JT 2014 (3) SC 409; 2014 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 2372; 2014 (1) MLJ(Crl) 723; 2014 (2) RCR(Criminal) 36; 2014 (4) RLW 3634; 2014(2) SCALE 423; [2014] 2 S.C.R. 1116

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Madhya Pradesh Municipal Corporation Act, 1956

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 13(1)(d), 13(2), 19 and 19(1) - Corruption - Sanction for prosecution - Appellant, working as the Asst. Engineer in the Corporation was sought to be prosecuted u/ss. 13(1)(d) and 13(2) of Act - Sanction was granted by the Standing Committee of the Corporation while he was in service - Though the said sanction was subsequently withdrawn, that order was set aside by the HC holding that the order on withdrawal was passed without proper application of mind - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order of the HC could be upheld -

Held, grant of sanction was only an administrative function - It was intended to protect public servants against frivolous and vexatious litigation - It also ensures that a dishonest officer was brought before law and was tried in accordance with law - Thus, it was a serious exercise of power by the competent authority - It had to be apprised of all the relevant materials, and on such materials, the authority had to take a conscious decision as to whether the facts would reveal the commission of an offence under the relevant provisions - No doubt, an elaborate discussion in that regard in the order was not necessary - But decision making on relevant materials should be reflected in the order and if not, it should be capable of proof before the court - Though the appellant made a specific objection in this regard before the Special Judge, unfortunately in the order dt. 27-12-2004, it was seen that there was no inquiry by the court in this regard - There was no reference at all to the recommendation made by the Municipal Commissioner - In such circumstances, trial court should conduct a proper inquiry as to whether all the relevant materials were placed before the competent authority and whether the competent authority had referred to the same so as to form an opinion as to whether the same constituted an offence requiring sanction for prosecution - Hence, impugned order of the HC was set aside and remitted the matter to the Special Judge - Appeal partly allowed.
