Sadashiv Prasad Singh v Harendar Singh and others

Supreme Court of India

08 January 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

J. S. KHEHAR, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 15; (2015) 5 SCC 574; AIR 2014 SC 1078; 2014 (3) ALD(SC) 120; 2014 (103) ALR 239; 2014 (1) BC 654; 2014 (1) CompLJ 417; 2014 (1) CTC 732; 2014 (1) DRTC 609; JT 2014 (1) SC 481; 2014 (1) OLR 564; 2014 (123) RD 22; 2014(1) SCALE 230; [2014] 1 S.C.R. 249

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Loan, Auction of property

Summary: Banking & Finance - Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Practice & Procedure - Income-tax Act, 1961, s. 29 - Recovery of Debts Due to Banks and Financial Institutions Act, 1993 - Income Tax (Certificate Proceedings) Rules, 1962, rr. 11, 11(2) - Loan - Recovery - Auction of property - Legality - Borrower company acquired loan form bank - Since loan amount was not repaid, Bank filed application before Tribunal for recovery of dues and the same was allowed - Recovery Officer attached plot of 'J' who was one of the partners - Respondent/brother of 'J', filed objection petition before Recovery Officer - Recovery officer passed order for sale of property by way of public auction - Auction was held and appellant was highest bidder - Appellant took physical possession of the property - Respondent filed writ petition before Single Judge and the same was dismissed - Aggrieved respondent filed appeal before DB - DB allowed the appeal and set aside proceedings conducted by Recovery Officer, including sale of property by public auction - Hence instant appeal - Whether impugned order passed by HC was justified - Held, HC ought not to have interfered with in matter agitated by respondent in exercise of its writ jurisdiction - Single Judge rightfully dismissed petition filed by respondent - All facts cumulatively lead to conclusion that after 26-10-2005, respondent lost all interest in property in question and had therefore, remained silent spectator to various orders which came to be passed from time to time - Respondent had, therefore, no equitable right in his favor to assail auction-purchase made by appellant on 28-8-2008 - Finally, public auction under reference was held on 28-8-2008 and thereafter the same was confirmed on 22-09-2008 - Possession of property was handed over to auction-purchaser appellant on 11-3-2009 - Auction-purchaser initiated mutation proceedings in respect of property in question - Appellant did not raise any objections in said mutation proceedings - Said mutation proceedings were also finalized in favor of appellant - Appellant approached HC only on 27-11-2009 - challenge raised by respondent ought to have been rejected on grounds of delay and latches, especially because third party rights had emerged in meantime - Further, because auction purchaser was bona fide purchaser for consideration, having purchased property in furtherance of duly publicized public auction, interference by HC even on ground of equity was clearly uncalled for - Impugned order passed by HC was set aside - Right of appellant was confirmed - Appeal allowed.
