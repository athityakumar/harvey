A. T. Prakashan v Excise Inspector and another

Supreme Court of India

04 April 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Radhakrishnan, Vikramajit Sen

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 223; 2014 ALL MR (Cri) 1945; JT 2014 (4) SC 408; 2014 (2) RCR(Criminal) 446; 2014(4) SCALE 553

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Abkari Act, 1077, Possession of contraband

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Kerala Abkari Act, ss. 8, 55(a) - Possession of contraband - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant (accused) allegedly found in possession of contraband and thereby committed offence u/s. 55(a) of the Act - Trial Court convicted appellant and sentenced appellant to undergo RI for 2 years and 6 months with fine - Aggrieved appellant filed an appeal before HC, which was partly allowed by impugned judgment modifying sentenced to RI for 1 years and fine - Hence, instant appeal - Appellant contended that he should have charge sheeted u/s. 8 of the Act and not u/s. 55(a) of the Act -

Held, it was true that proper section, which was attracted in instant case was s. 8(1) of the Act, as amended by Act 10 of 1996, not s. 55(a) of the Act - But, misquoting of the section or misapplying the provisions had caused no prejudice to appellant, since the offence was clearly made out - Offence u/s. 55(a) of the Act could always be altered to s. 8(1) of Act 10 of 1996, thus, Court found no error in the conviction recorded by Courts below - However, considering fact that appellant had no previous history of committing such offence, sentence was modified to that of 6 months' simple imprisonment and fine of Rs.50,000/-, and in default, to undergo further simple imprisonment for 3 three months - Appeal partly allowed.
