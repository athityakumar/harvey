Licil Antony v State of Kerala and another

Supreme Court of India

15 April 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Chandramauli Kumar Prasad, Pinaki Chandra Ghose

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 253; (2014) 11 SCC 326; (2014) 11 SCC 335; 2014 CRLJ 2414; 2014 (2) MLJ(Crl) 448; 2014 (2) RCR(Criminal) 631; 2014(5) SCALE 100

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Smuggling of red sanders, Detention

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Conservation of Foreign Exchange and Prevention of Smuggling Activities Act, 1974, s. 3 - Biological Diversity Act, 2002, s. 55(2) - Wildlife Protection Act, 1972, s. 29 - Andhra Pradesh Forest Act, 1937, ss. 29, 32 - Andhra Pradesh Sandalwood and Red Sanders Wood Transit Rules, 1969, r.3 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 379 - Code of Criminal Procedure, ss. 82, 83, 84 and 85 - Smuggling of red sanders - Detention - Sustainability - Appellant's husband detenu was arrested by Directorate of Revenue Intelligence and case was registered against him u/s. 3 of the COFEPOSA - It was alleged that detenu was part of well-organised gang operated in smuggling of red sanders in India and abroad - Detaining authority issued order and confirmed order of detention for period of one year with effect from 11-6-2013 - Detenu was earlier arrested in connection u/ss. 29 and 32 of the 1937, u/s. 29 of the 1972 Act, u/s. 55(2) of the 2002 Act and u/r. 3 of the 1969 and u/s. 379 IPC - Magistrate released him on bail and while doing so directed him to appear before concerned police station on specified days - Appellant challenged her husband's detention before HC in petition - HC by impugned order dismissed appellants appeal - Hence instant appeal - Whether the order recorded by HC was sustainable -

Held, order of detention on ground of its was delayed execution - Order of detention was passed on 6-5-2013, whereas it was served on detenu on 11-6-2013 - Detenu was absconding, appropriate Government ought to have taken recourse to s. 7 of COFEPOSA which confers power on detaining authority to make report to competent Magistrate in relation to absconding person so as to apply provisions of ss. 82, 83, 84 and 85 CrPC which provided for publication of order in Official Gazette, directing detenu to appear - It was admitted position that no such report or publication was made - Accordingly, order of detention was vitiated on ground of delay in its execution also - Undue and unexplained delay in execution of order of detention vitiates it, but in facts of instant case, it should not be said that such delay had occurred - As stated earlier, order of detention dated 6-5-2013 was served on detenu on 11-6-2013 - It was expected of detaining authority to take recourse to ordinary process at first instance for service of order of detention on detenu and it was only after order of detention was not served through said process that recourse to modes provided u/s. 7 of COFEPOSA were to be resorted - That occasion did not arose as order of detention was served on detenu on 11-6-2013 - Therefore, order of detention should not be said to have been vitiated on this ground - It was submitted that order of granting bail to detenu and conditions put had not been considered by detaining authority, while passing order of detention - Order of preventive detention deprived citizen of his precious fundamental right of liberty and as such, detaining authority erred in passing order of detention without considering the same - Court should not expect detaining authority to know each and every detail concerning detenu in different parts of country, not only this, conditions imposed while granting bail to detenu which had reproduced in no way restrained him from continuing with his prejudicial activity or consequences, if he continued to indulge - Thus, order of HC, that bail order passed by Trial Court in Andhra Pradesh was not crucial and vital document and omission by detaining authority to consider the same had, in no way affected its subjective satisfaction - Hence, there was no error in order of detention and order passed by HC, refusing to quash same and there was no merit in instant appeal - Appeal dismissed.
