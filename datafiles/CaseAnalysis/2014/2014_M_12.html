Satti Paradesi Samadhi and Philliar Temple v M. Sankuntala (Dead) Through Lrs.

Supreme Court of India

03 July 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, V. Gopala Gowda

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 456; (2015) 5 SCC 674; 2014(5) ALL MR 903; 2014 (106) ALR 261; 2014 (4) AWC 4239; 2014 (3) CLT(SC) 107; 2015 (1) CTC 791; JT 2014 (8) SC 455; 2014 (175) PLR 775; 2014 (3) RCR(Civil) 938; 2014 (125) RD 517; 2014(8) SCALE 469; [2014] 9 S.C.R. 221

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 14 r. 2 - Limitation Act, 1963, s. 10 - Suit for declaration - Preliminary issue - Limitation - Dismissal - Legality -.Plaintiff/appellant instituted a suit for declaration seeking that the three settlement deeds executed by former trustee in favour of his two daughters and a granddaughter as null and void, and for the relief of recovery of possession of the land to the trust - Defendant made a submission before the Single Judge that the suit was barred by limitation - Accepting the submission of defendant/respondent, Single Judge thought it appropriate to take up the issue No. 1 as a preliminary issue - Single Judge dismissed the suit on the ground of limitation - On appeal filed by appellant DB dismissed the appeal - Hence instant appeal - Whether an issue of limitation could at all have been taken up as a preliminary issue -

Held, unless there was determination of the fact which would not protect the plaintiff u/s. 10 of the 1963 Act the suit could not be dismissed on the ground of limitation - It was not a case which would come within the ambit and sweep of O. 14 r. 2 CPC which would enable the court to frame a preliminary issue to adjudicate thereof - Single Judge, as it appeared, remained totally oblivious of the said facet and adjudicated the issue as if it falls under O. 14 r. 2 CPC - SC repeated that on the scheme of s. 10 of the 1963 Act - Certain facts were to be established to throw the lis from the sphere of the said provision so that it would come within the concept of limitation - DB has fallen into some error without appreciating the facts in proper perspective - That apart, DB, by taking recourse of arts. 92 to 96 without appreciating the factum that it uses the words 'transferred by the trustee for a valuable consideration' in that event the limitation would be twelve years but in the instant case the asseveration of plaintiff was that the trustee had created three settlement deeds in favour of his two daughters and a granddaughter - Issue of consideration not yet emerged - Settlement made by the father was whether for consideration or not to be gone into and similarly whether the property belongs to the trust as trust was understood within the meaning of s. 10 of the 1963 Act has also to be gone into - Ergo, there could be no shadow of doubt that the issue No. 1 that was framed by Single Judge was an issue that pertained to fact and law and hence, could not have been adjudicated as a preliminary issue - Therefore, the impugned order was wholly unsustainable - SC had not expressed any opinion with regard to the issue of limitation except saying that the said issue could not have been taken up as a preliminary issue - Impugned judgments were set aside - Appeal allowed.
