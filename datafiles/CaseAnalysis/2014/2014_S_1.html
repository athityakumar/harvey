S. Bhaskar Reddy and another v Superintendent of Police and another

Supreme Court of India

28 November 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, C. Nagappan

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 812; (2015) 2 SCC 365; 2015 (1) ALT(SC) 52; JT 2014 (13) SC 284; 2015 (1) SLR 776; 2015 (3) WLN(SC) 209

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Andhra Pradesh Civil Services (Classification Control and Appeal) Rules, 1991

Summary: (A) Service - Andhra Pradesh Civil Services (Classification, Control & Appeal) Rules, 1991, rr. 16, 30 - Deputation - Dismissal by transferred authority - Legality - Appellant State Armed Reserve Constables were transferred on deputation to respondent No. 1 Railway Police department - Appellants were implicated in a murder case - Respondent No. 1 initiated enquiry and passed dismissal orders - In the meantime, appellants were repatriated to their parent department - Appellants challenged dismissal order before Tribunal - Tribunal set aside dismissal order - However, on respondent No1's writ petition, HC set aside Tribunal order - Whether the respondent No. 1 is the competent Disciplinary Authority to pass an order of dismissal against the appellants.

Held, appointment orders of the appellants specifically stated that they were required to give an undertaking to the respondent No. 2 to serve in the Railway Police for a period of 5 years and were required to undergo necessary training. Therefore, it is not open for them to contend that the Railway Police is not a part of the Police department of the State but the department of Central Govt. The HC with reference to the first proviso to r.16 of the Rules has rightly set aside the findings recorded by the Tribunal in its judgment by correctly interpreting rr. 16 and 30 of the Rules. Hence, the impugned HC order does not call for interference. Appeal partly allowed.

(B) Service - Andhra Pradesh Civil Services (Classification, Control & Appeal) Rules, 1991, r. 9 (vii) - Involvement in criminal case - Effect on disciplinary proceedings - Whether dismissal orders passed against appellants are liable to be set aside in view of the order of Criminal Court after the trial in which proceeding the appellants were honourably acquitted.

Held, from the charge sheet issued in the disciplinary proceedings and the enquiry report submitted by the Enquiry Officer and the judgment in the criminal case, it is clear that they are almost similar and one and the same. In the criminal trial, the appellants have been acquitted honourably for want of evidence on record. The trial judge has categorically recorded the finding of fact on proper appreciation and evaluation of evidence on record and held that the charges framed in the criminal case are not proved against the appellants and therefore they have been honourably acquitted for the offences charged on. The HC has not considered and examined this legal aspect of the matter while setting aside the impugned judgment and order of the Tribunal. The Tribunal has also not considered the same. Both the HC and Tribunal have erred in not considering this important undisputed fact regarding honourable acquittal of the appellants on the charges in the criminal case which are similar in the disciplinary proceedings. Therefore, the orders of dismissal passed against the appellants were set aside. However, it would be appropriate to pass an order of compulsory retirement for appellants from their service as provided u/r. 9 (vii) of the Rules taking their service from the date of their appointment. Appeal partly allowed.
