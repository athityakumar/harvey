HDFC Bank Limited v Kumari Reshma and others

Supreme Court of India

01 December 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, R.F. Nariman, Uday Umesh Lalit

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 816; (2015) 3 SCC 679; 2014 (4) ACC 929; 2015 ACJ 1; AIR 2015 SC 290; 2015 (2) ALD(SC) 1; 2015 (108) ALR 461; 2015 (1) AWC 599; 2015 (1) Bom.C.R. 1; 2015 (1) JabLJ 318; JT 2014 (13) SC 438; 2015 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 277; 2015 (6) MahLJ 51; 2015(1) M.P.H.T. 193; 2015 (4) MPLJ 251; 2015 (1) RCR(Civil) 1; 2015 (1) RLW 125; 2014(13) SCALE 418

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance

Keywords: Financier, Registered Owner, Assam Requisition and Control of Vehicles Act, 1968, Assam Act, Financer's liability

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Insurance - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, ss. 2(30), 146 - Motor accident - Compensation - Financer's liability - Legality - Motor Cycle belonged to respondent no. 2 and driven by respondent no. 3, in a rash and negligent manner dashed against the scooter of respondent no. 1 (claimant) as a consequence of which she sustained a fracture and other injuries - Claimant filed a claim petition before Tribunal, seeking compensation for injuries, which was allowed awarding Rs.1,75,000/- with interest - On appeal, filed by predecessor-in-interest the appellant (bank) - HC dismissed the appeal and enhanced the compensation in appeal of claimants - HC also dismissed the review petition filed by appellant - Hence instant appeals - Appellant contended that definition of 'owner' u/s. 2(30) of the Act would not cover a financer who has entered into a hypothecation agreement with the borrower who was in possession and control of vehicle andn that it had nothing to do with financer's liability.

Held, appellant bank had financed the owner for purchase of the vehicle and the owner had entered into a hypothecation agreement with the bank. The borrower had the initial obligation to insure the vehicle, but without insurance he plied the vehicle on the road and the accident took place. Had the vehicle been insured, the insurance company would have been liable and not the owner. In instant case, vehicle was subject of an agreement of hypothecation and was in possession and control under the respondent no. 2. The HC has proceeded both in impugned judgment as well as in the review that the financier steps into the shoes of the owner. It is found that there is a common thread that the person in possession of the vehicle under the hypothecation agreement has been treated as the owner. If the vehicle is insured, the insurer is bound to indemnify unless there is violation of the terms of the policy under which the insurer can seek exoneration. The predecessor-in- interest of appellant, was the registered owner along with respondent no. 2. The respondent no. 2 was in control and possession of the vehicle. He had taken the vehicle from the dealer without paying the full premium to the insurance company and thereby getting the vehicle insured. The HC has erroneously opined that the financier had the responsibility to get the vehicle insured, if the borrower failed to insure it. The said term in the hypothecation agreement does not convey that the appellant financier had become the owner and was in control and possession of the vehicle. It was the absolute fault of the respondent no. 2 to take the vehicle from the dealer without full payment of the insurance. Nothing has been brought on record that this fact was known to the appellant financier or it was done in collusion with the financier. When the intention of the legislature is quite clear to the effect, a registered owner of the vehicle should not be held liable if the vehicle is not in his possession and control and there is evidence on record that the respondent no.2, without the insurance plied the vehicle in violation of the statutory provision contained in s. 146 of the Act, the HC could not have mulcted the liability on the financier. The appreciation by Single Judge in appeal, both in fact and law, is wholly unsustainable. It is held that the liability to satisfy the award is that of the owner, the respondent no. 2 and not that of the financier and accordingly that part of the direction in the award is set aside. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Person in control and possession of a vehicle under an agreement of hypothecation should be construed as owner and not alone the registered owner and registered owner of the vehicle should not be held liable if the vehicle is not in his possession and control.
