Ashok Shankarrao Chavan and others v Dr. Madhavrao Kinhalkar and another

Supreme Court of India

05 May 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla, Surinder Singh Nijjar

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 346; (2014) 7 SCC 99; AIR 2014 SC 3102; 2014 (6) ALD(SC) 33; 2015 (1) AWC 502; 2015 (3) CLT(SC) 99; JT 2014 (7) SC 77; 2014 (2) RCR(Civil) 1009; 2014(6) SCALE 200

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Election

Keywords: Election Expenses, Contesting Candidate, Electorate, Purity, Power Of Election Commission, Return Of Election Expenses, Himachal Pradesh Panchayat Raj Act, 1994, Shikshan Sevak Scheme, Non-disclosure of election expenses

Summary: Election - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 329(b) - Representation of the People Act, 1951, ss. 10A, 7(c), 8(b), 77, 77(1), 77(3), 78, 123(6) - Conduct of Election Rules, 1961, ss. 86(3), 89, 90 - Non-disclosure of election expenses - Disqualification - Justifiability - Appellant contested in Assembly elections and results of election to Assembly were declared - Respondent no. 1 was one of the candidates who contested the said election as against appellant - Appellant was declared elected and respondent no. 1 was an unsuccessful candidate - As per the provisions of the Act and the Rules, within 1 month from the date of publication of results, a statement of election expenses had to be filed by the candidate with the District Election Officer (DEO) - Appellant stated to have filed his statement of election expenses - DEO forwarded his report to Election Commission of India (EC) (respondent no. 2) and that according to appellant, nothing adverse was stated in said report - However, respondent no. 1 filed a complaint with EC alleging violation of Election Code based on newspaper reports - Besides said complaint of also filed an Election Petition before Election Tribunal (HC) - Election Petition was dismissed by HC on the ground of want of material particulars - Aggrieved respondent no. 1 preferred instant statutory appeal, which was dismissed - However, EC on being satisfied about the failure to lodge a correct account of all election expenditure in the manner required by or under the Act, disqualified the appellant for the period of 3 years - Aggrieved appellant filed an appeal before DB, which was dismissed by impugned judgment - Hence, instant appeals - Appellants contended s. 10A of the Act did not empower the EC to take oath, nor assume the powers of a quasi-judicial authority and, thus EC could not exercise a jurisdiction to the extent of passing the order of disqualification and that Act and the make a clear distinction as between lodgment and maintenance of accounts and that s. 78 of the Act along with rr. 86(3) and 89 of the Rules, only talk of lodgment of account and not correctness of account - Whether EC u/s. 10A of the Act, could conduct an enquiry to determine the falsity of the return of election expenses by an elected candidate, especially after a decision was rendered by HC in Election Petition preferred by respondent no. 1 -

Held, reading s. 77(3) of the Act along with r. 90 of the Rules and s. 77(1) of the Act, what ultimately emerges was that every candidate contesting in an election should maintain a separate account relating to the election, that such account should contain all the expenditures incurred by him in connection with the election and most importantly such details of the account and expenses incurred should reflect the correct particulars apart from ensuring that such expenditure did not exceed the maximum limit prescribed u/r. 90 as stipulated u/s. 77(3) of the Act - S. 10A of the Act made it clear that EC had to find out whether a person has failed to lodge an account of election expenses within the time and in the manner required by or under the Act - Specific expression 'by or under' used in s. 10A(a) of the Act emphasizes that wherever the Act stipulates as regards the maintenance and the lodgment of account, such stipulations in its substance would be the requirement for verification to be made while holding an enquiry u/s. 10A of the Act - S. 10A of the Act was comprehensively enacted replacing earlier ss. 7(c) and 8(b) of the Act in order to ensure that the contesting candidate in an election could not deal with the expenses in regard to the election in any manner he likes but such expenses could be incurred only in the manner required under the law - Exercising power u/s. 10A of the Act, EC could not set aside the election of a successful candidate - S. 10A of the Act talks of only an order of disqualification that could be passed by an EC - Further, such disqualification order could be passed for failure to lodge an account of election expenses and such failure was within the time and in the manner required by or under the Act - Thus, scope of an Election Petition to be tried by an Election Tribunal (HC) and the scope of an order of disqualification to be passed u/s. 10A of the Act were entirely different and it should be stated that one did not conflict with the other - Very same allegation of a corrupt practice may form part of the failure to lodge the account in the manner required by or under the Act as has been specified in ss. 77 and 78 of the Act in an Election Petition -Thus, simply because such an issue might form part of a corrupt practice as provided u/s. 123(6) of the Act and the failure might be in contravention of s. 78 of the Act, it might also be one of the grounds in challenging the successful election of a candidate concerned in an Election Petition - On that score, it could not be held that area of disqualification to be considered by EC, u/s. 10A of the Act, was fully covered in an Election Petition and thereby, the power and jurisdiction of EC would stand excluded - It could not thus, be contended that once the Election Petition having been rejected for want of particulars, which order had become final, a complaint u/s. 10A of the Act could not be pursued - Thus, reject the said contention raised on behalf of appellant - Order of EC, which was upheld by HC in stating that it had got every jurisdiction to enquire into the complaint made before it as regards the incorrect and untrue statement of accounts of election expenses lodged by appellant could be enquired into for the purpose of passing an order of disqualification u/s. 10Aof the Act was perfectly justified - Decision of EC as upheld by HC to the effect that s. 10A of the Act clothes the EC with the requisite power and authority to enquire into the allegations relating to failure to submit the accounts of election expenses in the manner prescribed and as required by or under the Act, was perfectly justified - In the absence of glaring illegality or irregularity pointed out, there was no reason to interfere with those finding of facts arrived at by EC Commission, which was confirmed by DB after a thorough examination - Thus, no good ground to interfere with impugned order of EC disqualifying the Appellant for a period of 3 years - Appeals dismissed.
