International Amusement Limited v India Trade Promotion Organisation and another

Supreme Court of India

17 December 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, C. Nagappan

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 879; AIR 2015 SC 749; 2015 (2) ALD(SC) 159; 2015 (2) ALT(SC) 31; 2015 (1) AWC 1041; 2015 (1) CLT(SC) 22; JT 2014 (14) SC 277; 2015 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 985; 2015 (1) RCR(Civil) 413; 2014(14) SCALE 196

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Summary: Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996-Section 11 (6)-Public Premises (Eviction of Unauthorised Occupants) Act, 1971-Section 4A-Appointment of Arbitrator-Appellant called upon to vacate premises-Land allotted to appellant by India Trade Promotion Organisation (I.T.P.O.)-Appellant raised various disputes, claims and counter claims against I.T.P.O.-Appellant made representation to I.T.P.O. with request to renew lease of land for further period-Being unsuccessful, appellant commenced proceeding under Arbitration Act invoking its right under Clause 28 of licence agreement-Arbitration notice issued to I.T.P.O. invoking provisions of Clause 28 of licence agreement and filed application under Section 8 of Arbitration Act before Estate Officer for referring matter for arbitration-Application for arbitration filed by appellant rejected by Estate Officer-Clause 28 in licence agreement not arbitration clause-Appointment of Arbitrator by nominee of Chief Justice rightly set aside in impugned judgment of Division Bench of High Court-Proceedings pending under provision of Public Premises Act before Estate Officer to be continued by him-No reason to interfere with impugned judgment and order.
