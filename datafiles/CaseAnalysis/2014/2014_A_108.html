D. R. Somayajulu, Secretary D.L.S. and S. E. Railway House Building Co-Operative Society Limited, Visakhapatnam and another v Attili Appala Swamy and others

Supreme Court of India

19 November 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

R. Banumathi, Adarsh Kumar Goel, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 780; (2015) 2 SCC 390; AIR 2015 SC 569; 2015 (1) ALD(SC) 151; 2015(1) ALL MR 470; 2015 (108) ALR 719; 2014 (4) CLT(SC) 401; JT 2014 (13) SC 79; 2015 (6) MahLJ 582; 2016 (1) MPLJ 11; 2014(13) SCALE 1

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Administrative - Urban Land (Ceiling and Regulation) Act, 1976 - Urban Land (Ceiling and Regulation) Repeal Act 1999 - Sale of surplus land - Legality - Appellant Society purchased suit land from respondent No. 1's grandmother - Competent authority declared suit land as surplus land of respondent No.1 and his grandmother - In the meantime, respondent No.1's grandmother died - Later, appellate authority affirmed competent authority order - Respondents filed writ petitions challenging authorities orders - HC dismissed writ petitions - HC later dismissed concerned writ appeals also - However, on review, HC set aside its own orders and orders of competent authorities holding that legal representatives of respondent No.1 's grandmother were not brought on record - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, in the review petition while setting aside its own order and then orders of the authorities under 1976 Act, HC observed that there was no proper representation of the estate of respondent No.1's grandmother and any proceedings taken against a dead person are totally void ab initio. However, HC has completely ignored the participation of sons, daugthers and grandchildren of respondent No.1's grandmother in the proceedings before the competent authority. Hence, the impugned order passed by HC in the review petition is erroneous and not sustainable. Further, terms of various agreements and also the tenor of the GO.Ms.No.1900 dt. 20-12-2006 clearly indicate that possession was already handed over to the appellant and the respective allottees were in occupation of the plots. By virtue of earlier agreements and GO.Ms.No.1900 dt. 20-12-2006, on the date when the 1999 Act was adopted in the State i.e. on 27-3-2008, the appellant was already in possession of suit land and 1999 Act is not applicable to that land. However, correct details of the consideration paid to the vendors, the balance amount payable to the vendors and whether amount has been paid to the government in lieu of allotment are not clear. Hence, appellant should pay the entire sale consideration to the vendors apart from the amount, if any, paid to the Govt. Therefore, impugned HC orders were set aside and matter remitted back to HC for determining the balance sale consideration payable by the appellant to the vendor legal heirs. Appeals allowed.
