V. Sreeramachandra Avadhani (Dead) by Lrs. v Shaik Abdul Rahim and another

Supreme Court of India

21 August 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

J. S. KHEHAR, R.F. Nariman

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 542; (2014) 9 SCC 350; AIR 2014 SC 3464; 2014 (6) ALD(SC) 123; 2014(5) ALL MR 929; 2014 (107) ALR 203; 2014 (5) AWC 5308; 2014 (3) CLT(SC) 197; JT 2014 (9) SC 314; 2014 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 3483; 2015 (1) MahLJ 553; 2015 (1) MPLJ 262; 2014 (2) OLR 637; 2015 (126) RD 81; 2014(9) SCALE 483

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Family & Personal

Keywords: English

Summary: Family & Personal - Civil Procedure - Gift - The trial court expressed, that the gift deed 26-4-1952, was not in the nature of a usufruct - Dissatisfied with the order passed by the Trial Court, the respondents preferred an appeal before the Second Additional District Judge - First Appellate Court accepted the appeal preferred by the respondents on 5-1-2004 - On the issue whether Banu Bibi had an absolute right over the 'tiled house' with open space, gifted to her, the First Appellate Court recorded its finding on the basis of the text of the gift deed, dt. 26-4-1952 - Perusal of the judgment rendered by the First Appellate Court revealed, that the appeal was adjudicated, as if the controversy was in the nature of a disputed question of fact, without appreciating the legal implications pertaining to gift, under Muhammedan Law - While determining the controversy, the First Appellate Court did not examine whether the gift dt. 26-4-1952, constituted transfer of the corpus of the property, or merely its usufruct - The First Appellate Court, without any reference to the judgment of the Privy Council relied upon by the Trial Court, while interpreting the text of the gift deed dt. 26-4-1952, arrived at the conclusion, that Banu Bibi had merely been transferred a life interest in the 'tiled house' with open space, gifted to her on 26-4-1952 - Dissatisfied with the judgment rendered by the First Appellate Court, the vendee V.Sreeramachandra Avadhani preferred an appeal before the HC - HC while disposing of the Second Appeal on 2-8-2004 affirmed the determination recorded by the First Appellate Court - Hence instant appeal - Whether First Appellate Court committed error in not examining whether the gift dt. 26-4-1952, constituted transfer of the corpus of the property, or merely its usufruct -

Held, having given thoughtful consideration to the text of the gift deed dt. 26-4-1952, SC was of the view that the same contemplates the transfer of the corpus and not the usufruct - SCs reasons for the conclusion, were as under - Firstly, the donor records, having purchased the gifted property from his own earning on 16-7-1944, through a registered purchase deed, whereby he was vested with the absolute right of possession and enjoyment of the property - It was then asserted, that there was no dispute about the title of the donor, over the gifted property - Secondly, the use of the words 'We shall have no right to cancel this conveyance with silly reasons' also revealed, the intention of the donor to transfer the corpus of the property, to the done - Thirdly, the use of the words 'Neither myself nor my successors shall raise any objection in respect of this conveyed property either against you or against your successors', recognises the rights of the donee as well as her successors - These words extinguish, not only the donor's rights in the property, but also that of his successors - There was recognition of the rights of the donee and her successors to the extent, that in the event of transfer of the gifted property to the successors of the donee, the same would not be assailable by the donor or his successors - That also depicts, the intention of the donor to transfer the corpus of the gifted property - Fourthly, the gift deed records that 'after your life time this property shall devolve upon your off spring'- Use of the words 'your off spring', expresses an intention which was separate and distinct from 'our off spring' - In other words, the gift deed contemplated the transfer of the gifted property by the donee, to her children, even if, such children were not the children of the donor - That too showed that the intention of the donor, contemplated the transfer of the corpus - Fifthly, the gift deed records 'I am herewith filing transfer memos, along with this deed for registration, to get your name mutated in revenue records - Therefore you shall pay the Municipal Taxes and shall enjoy the same freely and happily' - That expression in the gift deed, brings out the intention of the donor, that the transfer of the gifted property should not remain a matter of understanding within the family, but should be an open declaration to the public - The assertion in the gift deed, that Municipal Taxes would be borne by the donee, shows that the donee was to henceforth bear all liabilities of the gifted property, as its owner -

Lastly, the handing over of the earlier title deeds of the gifted property to the donee, by recording in the gift deed that 'I have handed over the link sale deed and the voucher to you" also indicates, that the donor clearly expressed in the gift deed, that he had not retained any documents of title pertaining to the gifted property with himself, but had handed over the same to the done - That also showed the intention of the donor to relinquish all his existing rights, in the gifted property - That also showed the intent of the donor, to transfer the corpus of the property to the done - For the reasons recorded hereinabove, there could be no doubt whatsoever, that the intention of the donor in the gift deed dt. 26-4-1952, was to transfer the corpus of the immovable property to the donee, and not merely a usufruct therein - Having concluded that the donor Sheikh Hussein through the gift deed dt. 26-4-1952, had transferred the corpus of the immovable property to his wife Banu Bibi, it was natural to conclude that the gift deed executed in favour of Banu Bibi, was valid - Likewise, while applying the principles of Muhammedan Law expressed in recognized texts, and the decision of the Privy Council in Sardar Nawazish Ali Khan v Sardar Ali Raza Khan 1948 Indlaw PC 46 it was inevitable to hold, that all conditions depicted in the gift deed dt. 26-4-1952, which curtail use or disposal of the property gifted are to be treated as void - In that view of the matter, the conditions depicted in the gift deed, that the donee would not have any right to gift or sell the gifted property, or that the donee would be precluded from alienating the gifted immovable property during her life time, were void - Similarly, the depiction in the gift deed, that the gifted immovable property after the demise of the donee, would devolve upon her off spring and in the event of her not bearing any children, the same would return back to the donor or to his successors, would likewise be void - Having held that the gift deed dated 26.04.1952 irrevocably vested all rights in the immovable property in Banu Bibi, it was natural for us to conclude, that the sale of the gifted immovable property by Banu Bibi to V.Sreeramachandra Avadhani on 2-5-1978, was legal and valid - Consequently, the claim of the respondents to the gifted property, on the demise of Banu Bibi on 17-2-1989, was not sustainable in law - Appeal allowed.
