Jaipur Shahar Hindu Vikas Samiti v State of Rajasthan and others

Supreme Court of India

17 April 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

N.V. Ramana, P. Sathasivam, Ranjan Gogoi

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 265; (2014) 5 SCC 530; JT 2014 (5) SC 577; 2014(5) SCALE 200; [2014] 11 S.C.R. 677

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Trusts & Associations

Keywords: Rajasthan Public Trust Act, 1959

Summary: Trusts & Associations - Land & Property - Rajasthan Public Trust Act, 1959, s. 24 - There was long standing dispute with regard to properties of the Galta Peeth/Thikana which was established in the 15th century by Mahant - Later on 6-7-1943, X, the father of respondent No.4 was appointed as Mahanth by the ruler - Act had come into force w.e.f. 1-7-1962 - Mahant submitted a list of properties to the Jagir Commissioner showing some of the properties of the Trust as his personal properties - Then the Mahanth had executed gift deeds in favour of his wife and sons - On 31-12-1962, Mahant made an application for registration of the Mandir Thikana Shri Galtaji as a Public Trust under the Act - On 26-4-1963, the Asst. Commissioner passed order registering the Trust - Later on, a series of litigation went on between the parties with regard to the properties of the Trust/Math - When the Govt. appointed a 5 -Member Committee for proper management of the Trust, challenging the same writ petition was filed - When the Asst. Commissioner re-opened the issue of succession, respondent no. 4 filed writ petition - 2 PILs were filed seeking to declare (a) the properties were trust properties, (b) the mode of succession, (c) direct the Govt. to take over the management of the trust and (d) to appoint a Board to manage the properties in line with Vaishno Devi Shrine or Tirupati Balaji Temple - HC felt that those issues could be effectively decided by the Asst. Commissioner, and accordingly, permitted the appellant to implead himself in the pending applications before the authority - (A) Whether HC was right in relegating the parties to the Asst. Commissioner without going into the merits and legal issues involved in the case and whether the Asst. Commissioner had got the authority and jurisdiction under the Act to deal with complicated issues involved in the matter -

Held, extensive powers were given to the Asst. Commissioner and Commissioner, in some cases the power of the civil Court to effectively decide the issues of the Public Trust, by providing effective mechanism - Court were unable to agree with the contentions that the Asst. Commissioner had no jurisdiction to adjudicate the disputes involved, because the Act clearly demonstrates the power and jurisdiction of the Asst. Commissioner in deciding the issues pertaining to public trust - Hence, HC by the impugned order, was justified in relegating the parties to the Asst. Commissioner, before whom the applications were pending adjudication - Appellant having got impleaded himself in the applications before the Asst. Commissioner and having invited an order from the HC, now could not be permitted to question the said order of the HC - (B) Whether the appellant was aggrieved by the order passed in D.B. (Civil) W.P. No. 6607 of 2004, wherein the writ petition was dismissed as infructuous -

Held, case of the appellant was that the HC should not have dismissed the writ petition as withdrawn basing on the submission that the term of the Committee had expired - It ought to have decided the issue on merits - By this order, the HC had created a vacuum not contemplated by the Act, which was against Public Interest, in view of the status quo orders passed by the Court, the Committee could not function its full period - Hence, the Committee had to be allowed to function till a permanent Committee was appointed by the Govt. - Court was also not able to appreciate the argument advanced by the appellant for reason that D.B. (Civil) W.P. No. 6607 of 2004 was filed by the father of Respondent No.4 questioning the constitution of the Committee - When the Court directed the parties to appear before the Asst. Commissioner for proper adjudication of the issues as the 5-year term of the Committee expired, the respondent No. 4 sought permission of the Court and withdrew the writ petition, with a liberty to raise all the issues before the authority - Appeals dismissed.
