State of Madhya Pradesh v Deepak and others

Supreme Court of India

10 September 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, J. Chelameswar

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 602; (2014) 10 SCC 285; AIR 2014 SC 3747; 2014 CRLJ 4509; 2015 (1) JabLJ 166; JT 2014 (10) SC 240; 2014 (4) RCR(Criminal) 202; 2015 (1) RLW 697; 2014(10) SCALE 427

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Compromise

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 307, 34 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 482 - Criminal Proceedings - Compromise - Quashing of - Respondents (accused) allegedly committed offence u/s. 307 r/w s. 34 of IPC and an FIR was registered for said offence - Aggrieved respondent filed a petition before HC u/s. 482 of CrPC for compounding/quashing of criminal proceedings on the basis of compromise - HC by impugned judgment allowed petition - Aggrieved appellant (State) filed instant appeal -

Held, it was not a case where HC should have quashed the proceedings in exercise of its discretion u/s. 482 of CrPC. Injuries inflicted on the complainant were very serious in nature. The accused was armed with sword and had inflicted blows on the forehead, ear, back side of the head as well as on the left arm of the complainant. The complainant was attacked 5 times with the sword by the accused person out of which two blows were struck on his head. But for the timely arrival of brother of the complainant and another lady named 'P', who rescued the complainant, the attacks could have continued. In a case like this, the HC should not have accepted the petition of accused u/s. 482 of CrPC, Narinder Singh & Ors. v. State of Punjab & Anr., 2014 Indlaw SC 189, followed. Order of the High Court is set aside. Appeal allowed

Ratio - Offence u/s. 307 of IPC is not treated as a private dispute between the parties inter se but is held to be a crime against the society and guidelines are laid down for the Courts to deal with such matters when application for quashing of proceedings is filed, after the parties have settled the issues between themselves.
