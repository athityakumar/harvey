Navodaya Mass Entertainment Limited v J. M. Combines

Supreme Court of India

26 August 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Pinaki Chandra Ghose, M.Y. Eqbal

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 563; (2015) 5 SCC 698; 2014(5) ALL MR 938; 2014 (6) AWC 5740; 2014 (4) CLT(SC) 78; 2015 (1) CTC 797; JT 2014 (9) SC 401; 2014(9) SCALE 687; [2014] 7 S.C.R. 1098; 2014 (4) WLN(SC) 60

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitral award - Challenged - Appellant and respondent entered into agreement - Dispute arose between the parties - arbitrator allowed respondents claim of Rs.13,94,240/- with interest but disallowed the Minimum Guaranteed amount of Rs.69,416/- per month - Respondent and appellant challenged award before HC - HC dismissed both applications - Appeals before DB - DB allowed respondents application and dismissed appellants application - Hence instant appeals - Whether the order recorded by DB was sustainable -

Held, scope of interference of the Court is very limited. Court would not be justified in reappraising the material on record and substituting its own view in place of the Arbitrator's view. Once the Arbitrator has applied his mind to the matter before him, the Court cannot reappraise the matter as if it were an appeal and even if two views are possible, the view taken by the Arbitrator would prevail. Thus, reasoning given by the DB cannot be said to be perverse. Furthermore, the appellant never terminated the Agreement or requested the first respondent to take back the machinery. further it was not proper to opine in the matter when the matter/dispute has already been concluded by the Arbitrator and the award has been affirmed by the High Court. There was no merit in instant appeals. Appeals dismissed.

Ratio - Where there is an error apparent on the face of the record or the Arbitrator has not followed the statutory legal position, then and then only it would be justified in interfering with the award published by the Arbitrator.
