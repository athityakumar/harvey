National Insurance Company Limited and others v Kirpal Singh and others

Supreme Court of India

10 January 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, Vikramajit Sen

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 18; (2014) 5 SCC 189; AIR 2014 SC 1440; 2014 (2) AWC 1943; 2014 (140) FLR 798; JT 2014 (1) SC 538; 2014 (1) LLJ 513; 2014 (3) RLW 2183; 2014(1) SCALE 320; [2014] 1 S.C.R. 380; 2014 (2) SLR 158; 2014 (2) UPLBEC 955

Case Digest

Subject: Insurance; Service

Keywords: Entitiled to

Summary: Employment-Pension-Whether respondents who opted for voluntary retirement from service of appellant-Companies under 2004 scheme entitled to claim pension under General Insurance (Employees) Pension Scheme, 1995?-Held, "yes"-Nothing in context of 1995 Scheme which would exclude its beneficial provisions from application to employees who have opted for voluntary retirement under Special Scheme, 2004 or vice versa-Any provision for payment of pension is benefited in nature which ought to receive liberal interpretation so as to serve object underlying not only of Pension Scheme, 1995 but also any special scheme under which employees given option to seek voluntary retirement upon completion of prescribed number of years of service and age.
