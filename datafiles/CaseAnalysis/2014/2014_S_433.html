Mithilesh Kumar Singh v State of Rajasthan

Supreme Court of India

11 December 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, Adarsh Kumar Goel, R. Banumathi

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 919; (2015) 9 SCC 795; 2015 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 994; 2015 (1) RCR(Criminal) 437; 2014(14) SCALE 25

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Medical Report, Delhi Special Police Establishment Act, 1946

Summary: R. BANUMATHI, J. (Dissenting)

Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 32 - Investigation of case by CBI - Instant writ petition filed by the petitioner seeking writ of mandamus to initiate an independent investigation by the Central Bureau of Investigation (CBI) into the death of his daughter X, a student of B. Tech 1st Year of Rajasthan College of Engineering for Women, Jaipur, who succumbed to injuries which she sustained by falling from the fourth floor railing of her hostel room - Petitioner contended that despite the fact that X remained alive for three days and was in her full senses, under the influence of college authorities police have not recorded her - Petitioner alleged that the medical report of the victim was prepared on the instruction and connivance of the college authorities making a false allegation and chastising the character of the deceased.

Held, based upon the statement of witnesses, medical report and other materials, the investigating officer found that it is a case of suicide and filed the final report. Further, having regard to the materials on record, instant case is not such an exceptional situation warranting special investigation by the CBI. Petition dismissed.

T. S. Thakur, J.

Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 32 - Investigation of case by CBI - Whether prayer for transfer to CBI ought to be accepted.

Held, the decision whether transfer should or should not be ordered rests on the Court's satisfaction whether the facts and circumstances of a given case demand such an order. Transfer can be ordered once the Court is satisfied on the available material that such a course will promote the cause of justice, in a given case. In the instant case, circumstances leading to the death of young college student girl have become the subject matter of investigation. The issue is sensitive not only because of loss of an invaluable human life but also because of the reasons which are sought to be attributed for the sordid affair. Investigation transferred to CBI. Petition allowed.

ADARSH KUMAR GOEL, J.

Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 32 - Investigation of case by CBI - Whether apprehension of the petitioner that investigation by local police may not be fair has some basis and whether his prayer for transfer to CBI ought to be accepted.

Held, the prayer for transfer of investigation from State police to CBI can be allowed only in rare and exceptional circumstances when fair investigation by State police does not inspire confidence on account of any external influence or otherwise. In the instant case, the death of a young girl student has taken place in mysterious circumstances. According to the petitioner, the statement of the girl was not recorded even though it could have been done and thus, truth has not come out. In these circumstances, it will be appropriate that the matter is investigated by the CBI. Petition allowed.
