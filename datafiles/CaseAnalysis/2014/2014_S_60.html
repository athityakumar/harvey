Saurabh Kumar v Jailor, Koneila Jail and another

Supreme Court of India

22 July 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

N.V. Ramana, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 474; (2014) 13 SCC 436; 2014 ALL MR (Cri) 2744; JT 2014 (14) SC 459; 2014 (3) RCR(Criminal) 856; 2014(8) SCALE 656; [2014] 8 S.C.R. 909

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Legality

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Detention order - Legality - Petitioner stated that he was unnecessarily and illegally detained by police - Further, petitioner stated that Magistrate also caused prejudice as he was out of vengeance against his parents - When they approached local MLA, MLA contacted SHO and police informed the MLA that there was no complaint against petitioner and they were going to release him but in spite of repeated requests they had not released him - Hence instant petition - Whether petitioner could be said to be in unlawful custody -

Held, record which showed that petitioner was accused facing prosecution for offences, cognizance whereof had already been taken by Competent Court - Petitioner was presently in custody pursuant to order of remand made by said Court - Writ of Habeas Corpus was, in circumstances, totally mis-placed - Had said that, SC viewed that petitioner could and indeed ought to have filed application for grant of bail which prayer could be allowed by Court below, had regard to nature of offences allegedly committed by petitioner and attendant circumstances - Petitioner had for whatever reasons chosen not to do so, instead, had been advised to file instant petition in SC which was no substitute for his enlargement from custody - Magistrate too acted rather mechanically in remanding petitioner to judicial custody without so much as making sure that remaining accused persons were quickly served with process of Court and/or produced before Court for early disposal of matter - Magistrate appeared to have taken process in cavalier fashion that betrayed his insensitivity towards denial of personal liberty of citizen who was languishing in jail because police had taken no action for apprehension and production of other accused persons - It was difficult to accept contention that other accused persons who all belonged to one family absconded - Nature of offences alleged to have been committed was also not so serious as to probablise the version of respondent that accused have indeed absconded - Suffice it to say that petitioner was free to make application for grant of bail to Court concerned who should consider the same no sooner same was filed and pass appropriate orders thereon expeditiously - Order accordingly.
