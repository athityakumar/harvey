Municipal Corporation, Gwalior v Puran Singh alias Puran Chand and others

Supreme Court of India

02 July 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya, A.K. Sikri

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 419; (2015) 5 SCC 725; AIR 2014 SC 2665; 2014 (6) ALD(SC) 28; 2014(5) ALL MR 403; 2014 (3) CGLJ 379; 2014 (3) CLT(SC) 93; JT 2014 (8) SC 561; 2014 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 3088; 2014(8) SCALE 325; [2014] 8 S.C.R. 115; 2014 (3) WLN(SC) 161

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Suit for declaration and permanent injunction, Letters Patent Appeal Rules

Summary: Land & Property - Madhya Pradesh Land Revenue Code, 1959, s. 117 - Suit for declaration and permanent injunction - Dismissal - Legality - Respondents/plaintiffs filed suit before Trial Court against appellant/defendant/Municipal Corporation seeking declaration that disputed land was owned and possessed by them and sought for permanent injunction - Trial Court dismissed the suit - Respondents filed appeal before HC and the same was allowed and order passed by Trial Court was set aside - Aggrieved appellant filed appeal before DB and the same was dismissed - Hence instant appeal - Whether impugned order passed by HC was justified -

Held, Gita Ram Verma(PW3) in her statement stated record of samvat 1977 to samvat 1992 in 'Abhilekha Gar' (record room) of Director Land Record - Respondents could not correspond how they could get Exhibit P-10 and P-11 which were available in record room and could not prove correctness of those exhibits - Errors and omissions have been also found in Khasara entries produced by plaintiffs - Trial Court doubted correctness of those khasaras - Fact has not been dealt with by HC in proper perspective - Merely on basis of Khasara of year Samvat 1992 Ex.P/10, Khasara of year Samvat 1996 Ex.P/11, Khasara of year Samvat 2003 Ex.P/2 declaration was given in favor of plaintiffs - HC noticed Khasara of year Samvat 2004 Ex.P/3, Samvat 2005 Ex.P/4 and Samvat 2006 Ex.P/5, and then Khasara of years Samvat 2010 to 2014 Ex.P/6, Khasara of years Samvat 2013 to 2017 Ex.P/7 and Khasara of years Samvat 2035 to 2038 Ex.P/8 - In Khasara of years Samvat 2035 to 2038 Ex.P/8 the nature of land was mentioned as Nazul Abadi - In such exhibit Municipality has been mentioned in column no. 3 - On basis of aforesaid Khasaras, Single Judge decided title in favor of appellant - HC committed grave and manifest error of law in reversing well-reasoned judgment and decree passed by Trial Court by simply placing reliance upon Khasaras entries even without properly appreciating settled law that Khasara entries do not convey title of suit property as same was only relevant for purposes of paying land revenue and it has nothing to do with ownership - Impugned order and decree passed by Single Judge was set aside and judgment and decree passed by Trial Court was confirmed - Appeal allowed.
