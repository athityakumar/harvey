Suresh and another v State of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

28 November 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Adarsh Kumar Goel, V. Gopala Gowda

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 815; (2015) 2 SCC 227; AIR 2015 SC 518; 2015 CRLJ 661; JT 2014 (13) SC 266; 2015 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 460; 2015 (1) OLR 322; 2015 (1) RCR(Criminal) 148; 2014(13) SCALE 390

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Interim Compensation, Interim Maintenance, Developments

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 364A, 201, 120B - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 27 - Kidnapping and Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant allegedly kidnapped and murdered the deceased - Trial Court convicted appellants u/ss. 34, 364A, 201, 120B IPC - HC affirmed Trial Court order - Whether the conviction recorded against the appellants is sustainable.

Held, recovery of dead bodies from covered gutters and personal belongings of the deceased from other places disclosed by the accused stood fully established. It casts a duty on the accused as to how they alone had the information leading to recoveries which was admissible u/s. 27 of the Act. Failure of the accused to give an explanation or giving of false explanation is an additional circumstance against the accused. Hence, no ground was found to interfere with the conviction and sentence of the appellants. Appeal disposed of.

Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 357A - Victims of crime - Compensation by State - Applicability of s. 357A - Whether the family members of the deceased are entitled to compensation.

Held, gravity of offence and need of victim are some of the guiding factors to be kept in mind, apart from such other factors as may be found relevant in the facts and circumstances of an individual case. Impugned judgment shows that the de facto complainant PW2 filed Criminal Revision for compensation to the family members of deceased. The same has been dismissed by the HC without any reason. In fact even without such petition, the HC ought to have awarded compensation. There is no reason as to why the victim family should not be awarded compensation u/s. 357A by the State. Thus, State is liable to pay compensation to the family of the deceased. Rs. 10 lacs was awarded as interim compensation to the family members of victims. Appeal disposed of.

Ratio - S. 106 of the Indian Evidence Act requires the accused to explain the facts in their exclusive knowledge, though the burden of proof is on the prosecution and s. 106 is not meant to relieve it of that duty but the said provision is attracted when it is impossible or it is proportionately difficult for the prosecution to establish facts which are strictly within the knowledge of the accused.
