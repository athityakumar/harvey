Nirmal Singh and Etc. v State of Haryana Through Collector

Supreme Court of India

26 September 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, Adarsh Kumar Goel

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 672; (2015) 2 SCC 160; AIR 2015 SC 453; 2014(6) ALL MR 964; 2015 (2) AWC 1166; 2014 (4) CLT(SC) 295; JT 2014 (10) SC 594; 2015 (4) MahLJ 484; 2015 (3) MPLJ 308; 2014 (4) RCR(Civil) 782; 2014(11) SCALE 593

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property

Keywords: Municipal Limits, Entitlement of

Summary: Land & Property - Administrative - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4, 18, 23(1A), 23(2), 28 - Land acquisition - Enhancement of compensation - Entitlement of - Respondent (State) issued notification u/s. 4 of the Act for acquisition of land in question - An award was passed assessing the market value of acquired land at the rate of Rs.6,00,000/- per Acre - Aggrieved appellants (land owners) filed objections and Reference Court u/s. 18 of the Act determined the marked value at the rate of Rs.6,60,000/- per Acre besides other statutory benefits u/ss. 23(1A), 23(2) and 28 of the Act - For enhancement of compensation appeals were filed by appellants - HC by impugned Judgment and award, by applying a thumb rule, determined and enhanced the amount of compensation at Rs.9,00,000/- per Acre - Hence, instant appeals - What should be appropriate rate of compensation and extent of deductions towards developmental charges for the acquired land in question and whether sale-consideration mentioned in sale deeds of small pieces of land, which are situated close to acquired land could be considered for determination of compensation in favour of appellants.

Held, the acquired land is situated within the developed area of the municipal limits of 'P', there is no doubt that it has acquired potential value to be utilised for both residential and commercial purposes in the future. Perusal of record shows that there is significant variation in the consideration paid in the array of sale instances submitted by the parties. Moreover the consideration paid for the sale instances produced by the appellants are in relation to small pieces of land which are near the acquired land. To determine just and reasonable compensation for the acquired land on the basis of the sale instances as submitted by the appellants by taking the average of the sale considerations mentioned therein that are relevant to the date of issue of Notification u/s. 4 of the Act. However, the same is to be determined keeping in mind that developmental costs are higher for larger areas of land as compared to small portions of land. The rate of compensation must be subject to deductions towards developmental purpose that will have to be incurred by the State. Therefore, based on facts and circumstances of the instant cases, to allow 60% deduction on the market value of the acquired land towards developmental expenses. However, having regard to the location, potentiality of the acquired land and other relevant factors and circumstances of the cases, appellant are entitled for enhancement of compensation at Rs.12,00,000/- per Acre. The appellant are also entitled to get interest on the compensation at the rate of 15% p.a. under the proviso to s. 28 of the Act. They will also be entitled to the statutory benefits in accordance with ss. 23(1A) and 23(2) of the Act on the compensation awarded. Appeals allowed.

Ratio - Sale instances in relation to small pieces of land situated near the acquired land can be considered, subject to reasonable deductions for developmental costs that will be incurred in the future, and the evidence that these lands can be compared to the acquired land in terms of its vicinity and the comparable benefits and advantages.
