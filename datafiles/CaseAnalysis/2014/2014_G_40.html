A.P.N.G.O.'s Association v Government of Andhra Pradesh and others

Supreme Court of India

03 April 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

J. Chelameswar, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 215; (2014) 6 SCC 419; AIR 2014 SC 2374; 2014 (4) ALD(SC) 173; 2014(3) ALL MR 972; 2014 (124) RD 584; 2014(4) SCALE 529; [2014] 4 S.C.R. 637

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property; Trusts & Associations

Keywords: A.P. (Andhra Area) Tenancy and Agricultural Lands Act, 1956

Summary: Trusts & Associations - Administrative - Land & Property - Andhra Pradesh Charitable and Hindu Religious Institutions and Endowments Act, 1987, s. 80 - Govt. order - Gazette publication - Quashed - State Govt. issued G.O.Ms. No.911 purporting the sale of land in question in favour of appellants as proposed by the Commissioner - Said G.O. was challenged, later on those petitions were withdrawn - One impleaded petitioner in the said petition contested the matter, consequently Single Judge dismissed the petition - On appeal filed was disposed of by DB by setting aside the judgment of Single Judge and also G.O.Ms. No.911 was quashed - Hence instant appeal - Whether DB was justified in passing the impugned judgment -

Held, approach of DB was not in tune with the language of s. 80 of the Act - Purpose of making an endowment in favour of a deity was to generate income for the various services required to be rendered to the deity - Therefore, the prospect of getting a higher income was certainly relevant consideration than the possibility of an appreciation in the value of the asset endowed - Entire higher annual income accruing as interest on the sale proceeds of the asset need not be utilised every year only for the services but part of it could always be reinvested in proper asset to beat the inflation - Single Judge recorded a finding that all the original writ petitioners withdrew the writ petitions and rightly observed that there were no bona fides on the part of petitioners who pursued the litigation subsequent to the withdrawal of the writ petition by the original petitioners - DB erred in interfering with the judgment of Single Judge - Impugned judgment was set aside and judgment of Single Judge was restored - Validity of G.O.Ms. No.911 dt. 14-12-2000 was upheld - Appeal allowed.

Legislation Cited

A.P. (Andhra Area) Tenancy and Agricultural Lands Act, 1956

Andhra Pradesh Charitable and Hindu Religious Institutions and Endowments Act, 1987
