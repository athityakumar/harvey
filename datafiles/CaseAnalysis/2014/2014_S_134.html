Anil Gupta v Star India Private Limited and others

Supreme Court of India

07 July 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya, V. Gopala Gowda

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 440; (2014) 10 SCC 373; AIR 2014 SC 3078; 2014 ALL MR (Cri) 2726; 2014 (3) BC 599; [2014] 185 Comp Cas 251; 2014 (4) CompLJ 27; 2014 CRLJ 3884; JT 2014 (8) SC 190; 2014 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 2916; 2015 (2) MPLJ 208; 2014 (176) PLR 138; 2014 (175) PLR 787; 2014 (3) RCR(Civil) 642; 2014 (3) RCR(Criminal) 587; 2014(8) SCALE 480; [2014] 8 S.C.R. 183

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Cheque bounce, Quashing of criminal complaint

Summary: Criminal - Banking & Finance - Practice & Procedure - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, s. 138 r/w s. 141 - Cheque bounce - Quashing of criminal complaint - Maintainability - Subscription agreement was entered into between respondent no.1 and no. 2 whereby respondent no.2-Company was appointed as distributor of Star Channels and collecting subscription fee - Respondent no.2 issued three cheques - Cheques were dishonoured - Respondent no.1 filed complaint u/s. 138 r/w s. 141 of the Act - Trial Court issued summons against respondent no.2 - Thereafter, respondent no.2 and appellant jointly filed petition before HC quashing criminal complaint filed by respondent no.1 - HC, held that complaint was barred by limitation and quashed summon order against respondent no.2 - Hence instant appeal - Whether order passed by HC was maintainable -

Held, HC by impugned judgment held that complaint against respondent no.2 was not maintainable and quashed summon issued by Trial Court against respondent no.2 - Thereby, Company being not party to proceedings u/s. 138 r/w s. 141 of the Act and in view of fact that part of judgment referred to by HC in Anil Hada v Indian Acrylic Limited 1999 Indlaw SC 853 had been overruled by SC and in Aneeta Hada v M/S Godfather Travels & Tours Pvt.Ltd 2008 Indlaw SC 1015, there was no other option but to set aside rest part of impugned judgment whereby HC held that proceedings against appellant could be continued even in absence of Company - Thus, impugned judgment passed by HC so far it related to appellant was set aside and quash summon and proceeding pursuant to complaint qua appellant - Appeal allowed.
