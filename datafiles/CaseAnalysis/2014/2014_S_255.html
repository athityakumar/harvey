Krishan v State of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

16 May 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

N.V. Ramana, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 365; (2014) 13 SCC 574; 2014 ALL MR (Cri) 2611; 2014 (3) MLJ(Crl) 360; 2014 (3) RCR(Criminal) 22; 2014(7) SCALE 76; [2014] 7 S.C.R. 552

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss.376 and 506 - Rape -Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant allegedly committed offence of rape and criminal intimidation - Appellant was convicted by Trial Court u/ss.376 and 506 IPC - HC concurred with view of Trial Court - Hence instant appeal - Whether conviction of appellant was sustainable -

Held, two grounds on which appellant laid stress to show that there was no offence committed by the accused are (i) the medical evidence, and (ii) false implication by the prosecutrix - To appreciate appellant's contention, SC had perused the evidence of PW8, who relying upon the report of the Chemical Examiner stated that human semen was detected on the underwear of the victim - In addition to that, as per the evidence of PW2 , there were bone injuries on the right forearm, swelling and contusion both on the left hand and right shoulder and abraded contusion of the right leg of the accused - PW2 opined that these injuries were caused within a duration of 24 to 72 hours - All these injuries indubitably support the version of the prosecutrix-victim who stated that in the scuffle, she had bitten the accused - In addition to that, PW2 also stated that he found nothing which suggested that the accused could not perform sexual intercourse - Further contention of the counsel to rule out rape by the accused, that the prosecutrix was habitual of sexual intercourse and there were no signs of recent forcible sexual intercourse or injuries on her body, also could not help - It was not expected that every rape victim should have injuries on her body to prove her case - Findings of the medical experts clearly established that there was a rape committed against the victim - Other ground taken by appellant was that the prosecutrix had falsely implicated the appellant as his father/DW 1 had complained to her father that she was roaming around with the company of some boys and hence she had threatened that she would implicate the appellant falsely to take revenge for complaining against her, but that plea had also no basis - To prove that fact, appellant had relied upon the only circumstance that after the arrest of the appellant, DW 1 made a complaint to the Superintendent of Police about the false implication of his son which was signed by two villagers - However, there was no corroborative evidence on record to establish the fact and even the said two persons were not examined - Further submission made by appellant was that after the alleged occurrence, the girl straightaway went to her house and only thereafter she went to the police station to lodge the complaint and hence the story of rape cannot be believed - SC found nothing unusual in that, one could not expect every rape victim to straightaway go to police station and lodge complaint - Taking into consideration the totality of circumstances and on appreciation of entire evidence, supported by the PW2' opinion and chemical examination report, there was nothing to disbelieve the prosecution story that the appellant had committed the offences of rape and criminal intimidation - SC, therefore, found no error in the judgments and orders passed by the Courts below and hence SC found no reason to interfere - Appeal dismissed.
