Ramaiah @ Rama v State of Karnataka

Supreme Court of India

07 August 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, J. Chelameswar

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 512; (2014) 9 SCC 365; AIR 2014 SC 3388; 2014 CRLJ 4159; 2014 (4) JCC 2530; JT 2014 (9) SC 334; 2014 (6) KarLJ 1; 2014 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 3306; 2014 (4) RCR(Criminal) 490; 2014(9) SCALE 253; [2014] 7 S.C.R. 995

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 176, 201, 304-B and 498-A - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 113-B - Dowry Prohibition Act, 1961, ss. 3 and 4 - Conviction - Challenged - Deceased, was 14 years of age when she was married to the appellant - Within six months of her marriage, she died an unfortunate unnatural death and her body was recovered from a well - It was cremated on that day - PW1, maternal uncle of the deceased, lodged the complaint - Case was registered against the husband (appellant), father-in-law, mother-in-law and brother-in-law of the deceased - Chargesheet was filed only for offences punishable u/ss. 498-A, 304-B, 201 and 176 of the IPC r/w ss. 3, 4 and 6(2) of the 1961, Act - Trial Court acquitted appellant of the charges with the findings that prosecution was not able to prove the guilt of the appellant beyond reasonable doubt - On appeal, HC held that appellant was in fact guilty of offence punishable u/ss. 3 and 4 of the Act and ss. 498-A, 304-B, 201 and 176 of IPC - Hence, instant appeal - Whether the prosecutor proved that the appellant was guilty of offence punishable u/ss. 3 and 4 of the 1961, Act and ss. 498-A, 304-B, 201 and 176 of IPC -

Held, Trial Court correctly appreciated and analysed the evidence of PWs1 to PW3 and PWs4 to PW6 - None of the witnesses had made any specific allegation for the demand of the dowry in so far as the appellant was concerned - Prosecution also could not establish that any dowry articles were given at the time of marriage - In the absence of any particular allegation against the appellant in this behalf, would be improper to convict the appellant u/s. 498-A of IPC - Demand of dowry and harassment was not proved beyond reasonable doubt, question of invocation of s. 113 of the 1872, Act would not arise - HC could not have convicted the appellant by finding him guilty of offence u/s. 304-B of IPC as well by primarily relying upon the provisions of s. 113 of the 1872, Act - Finding of the Trial Court was correct - No reasons to reverse the verdict of acquittal and to convict the appellant - Judgment of the HC, holding that the appellant was guilty of the charges foisted against him set aside - Appeal allowed.
