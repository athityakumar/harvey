Abdul Basit @ Raju and others v Md. Abdul Kadir Chaudhary and another

Supreme Court of India

15 September 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Dattu, Sharad Arvind Bobde

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 696; (2014) 10 SCC 754; 2014 (4) JCC 2810; JT 2014 (12) SC 269; 2014 (4) RCR(Criminal) 716; 2014(11) SCALE 96; [2014] 10 S.C.R. 571

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Police Case

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 362 and 439 - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Cancellation of bail - Validity - Criminal case was registered against petitioners - After police investigation, Trial Court directed further investigation by the CID, stayed the trial proceedings and rejected the bail application of petitioners - Writ petition was filed - HC being of the view that since direction of further investigation had been issued by the Sessions Judge, the charge sheet submitted earlier by the police had become infructuous, granted bail to the petitioners - Respondent filed petition seeking cancellation of the bail - HC allowed the prayer of the respondent, thus cancelling the bail granted to all the petitioners - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, in the instant case, the order for bail in the bail application preferred by the petitioners finally disposes of the issue in consideration and grants relief of bail to the petitioners. Since, no express provision for review of order granting bail exists under the CrPC, the HC becomes functus officio and s. 362 of the CrPC applies barring the review of judgment and order of the Court granting bail to the petitioners. Even though the cancellation of bail rides on the satisfaction and discretion of the Court u/s. 439(2) of the CrPC, it does not vest the power of review in the Court which granted bail. Even in the light of fact of misrepresentation by the petitioners during the grant of bail, the HC could not have entertained the respondent/informant's prayer by sitting in review of its judgment by entertaining miscellaneous petition. HC has assigned an erroneous interpretation to the well settled position of law, assumed expanded jurisdiction onto itself and passed an order in contravention of s. 362 of the CrPC cancelling the bail granted to the petitioners. Therefore, HC is not justified in reviewing its earlier order of grant of bail and thus, the impugned judgment and order set aside. Order accordingly.
