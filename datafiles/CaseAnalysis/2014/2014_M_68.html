Mcleod Russel India Limited v Reg. Provident Fund Commissioner, Jalpaiguri and others

Supreme Court of India

02 July 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Vikramajit Sen, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 418; AIR 2014 SC 2573; 2014 (6) ALD(SC) 115; 2014(5) ALL MR 415; 2014 (5) AWC 4912; 2014 (142) FLR 406; JT 2014 (7) SC 474; 2014 (4) LLJ 309; 2014(8) SCALE 272; [2014] 9 S.C.R. 162

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Payment, Remitting Contributions and accumulations, Defaulted

Summary: Service - Employees Provident Funds and Miscellaneous Provisions Act, 1952, ss. 14A, 14AA, 14AB, 14AC, 14B, 17B, 2(e), 7Q - Remitting Contributions and accumulations - Payment - Defaulted - Legality - Instant appeal was filed by appellant/Mcleod Russel India Ltd. against order passed by DB - DB has allowed appeal preferred against order of Single Judge who in turn had applied and implemented opinion of DB - In impugned order DB had advantage of perusing view taken by Special Bench of three learned Judges of HC - Special Bench was constituted in view of reference submitted by Single Judge who had entertained an opinion which differed with three earlier decisions rendered by Single Judges in three separate matters - Along with petition, an appeal was pending before DB against one of those Single Judge decisions was also taken up by Special Bench - Whether impugned order passed by DB was justified -

Held, s. 14B of 1952 Act was complete in itself so far as computation of damages was concerned - It was conceivable that money due from an employer would have to be calculated u/s. 7A of the Act and in event default or neglect of employer was contumacious and contains requisite mens rea and actus reus yet another exercise of computation has to be undertaken u/s. 14B of the Act - Where Authority was of opinion that damages need to be imposed, computations would come within purview of s. 14B of the Act and it would be recoverable jointly and severally from erstwhile as well as current managements - A perusal of appeals s. 7I was illustrative of fact that these exercises were distinct from each other as per enumerations found in first sub-Section of s. 7I of the Act - It also appeared logical to us, in wake of numerous and different dates of amendments, that s. 7A(2) would also be available to proceedings u/s. 14B of the Act - Applicability of CPC to proceedings u/s. 14B of the Act has not specifically been barred by statute - It was necessary to clarify that Eveready Industries (India) Ltd. had in interregnum of litigation changed its name to Mcleod Russel India Ltd - Impugned Judgment deserved to be upheld - It contained detailed and logical exposition of facts as well as law pertaining to present dispute - SC approved pithy observations of RPF Commissioner, in subject Order that failure on part of employers to make remittances of accumulations and contributions, undermines objectives and purposes of statute - SC underscored that liability of Fund to pay interest to subscribers regardless of whether employers has paid their dues, runs relentlessly - Commissioner has specifically recorded that he has taken lenient view in matter and has eschewed imposition of damages to extent of 100 per cent of arrears even though this was envisaged by the ACt - Appellant has been also rightly burdened with payment of interest u/s. 7Q of Act - Interim orders were recalled - Appeal dismissed.
