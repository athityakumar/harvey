Gian Chand and others v York Exports Limited and another

Supreme Court of India

25 April 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, Gyan Sudha Misra

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 319; (2015) 5 SCC 609; AIR 2014 SC 3584; 2014 (6) ALD(SC) 144; 2015(2) ALL MR 441; 2014 (107) ALR 692; 2014 (5) AWC 4849; 2015 (1) CLT(SC) 55; JT 2014 (6) SC 616; 2014 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 3118; 2014 (3) RCR(Civil) 497; 2014 (124) RD 595; 2014(6) SCALE 35

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Payment Of Interest, Himachal Pradesh Tenancy & Land Reforms Act

Summary: Civil Procedure - Contract & Commercial - Indian Contract Act, 1872, ss.56, 73, 74 and 75 - Himachal Pradesh Tenancy & Land Reforms Act, 1974, s.118 - Interst on decreetal amount - Sustainability - Plaintiffs/respondents and the defendants/appellants executed an agreement to sell 164 bighas, 7 biswas of land in question on 2-8-1995 - As pre-condition for sale, permission from the competent authority u/s. 118 of 1974 Act was necessary - Onus to obtain the relevant permission was cast on the plaintiffs in the agreement to sell - Respondents managed to obtain permission only for 145 bighas of land - As the stipulated time for obtaining permission for the entire area expired, respondents sought extension of time from appellants - That extension was denied which eventually led to filing of the suit in question - In the suit, respondents pleaded frustration of contract and sought refund of the money already paid with interest - Single Judge decreed the suit, finding that there was frustration of contract - Aggrieved by the same, appellants filed an appeal before DB of HC - DB of HC held that (i) the contract in question was not 'frustrated' as understood in Indian law in terms of s.56 of 1872 Act (ii) Plaintiffs were at fault for their failure to obtain the necessary permission for the entire area as the obligation to obtain the permission rested with them - Respondents had committed breach of the agreement, however, the appellants had not committed any breach and (iii) Permission having been obtained for at least 145 bighas of land, sale could have been completed with regard to the substantial portion of the suit property- Hence instant appeal - Whether award of interest on decreetal amount was maintainable -

Held, DB of HC did not accept the finding of Single Judge who had conducted the trial of the suit, who had erroneously held that the contract stands frustrated u/s. 56 of 1872 Act - DB of HC did also examine the said aspect of the matter by adverting to the provisions of ss.73, 74 and 75 of 1872 Act and also taken into consideration the decisions of various HCs and HC had held that the appellants did not prove that they sustained losses on account of the non performance of the contract by respondents - DB of HC with reference to the allegation made by the defendants in their written statement that they suffered loss in the liquor business was not relatable to the contract and the same was not supported by material evidence on record - It was further submitted by appellants that due to the breach of contract on the part of the plaintiffs, the sum of Rs. 4,00,000/- in the agreement which was the earnest money and advance of Rs. 4,00,000/- should be forfeited towards loss of compensation - DB of HC after proper evaluation of the pleadings and evidence on record had rightly rejected the said contention and decreed the suit for sum of Rs. 39,20,000/- with 6% interest per annum from the date of institution of the suit till the date of payment of money and it had rightly recorded the concurrent finding for grant of the decree in favour of the respondent directing the appellants for repayment of the consideration amount with interest after holding that there was no frustration of contract entered between the parties though the sale of the land could not take place for non grant of permission to the entire extent of 165 bighas 7 biswas of land, the said finding of fact was accepted by HC at the time of issuing notice and therefore, the correctness of the same had attained finality for the reason that HC at the time of issuing notice to respondents had clarified that this appeal was confined on the issue of payment of interest awarded on decreetal amount from the date of institution of the civil suit till the payment to be made by the defendants to respondents - Money being paid as part consideration to the appellants was utilised by them in its liquor business - Therefore, award of interest in the judgment against the principal amount upto the date of the institution of the suit at 9% and 6% thereafter from the date of institution of case till the date of payment was legal and valid as the said amount was utilized by the defendants in the liquor business but they had failed to prove not obtaining the sale deed in respect of the land agreed upon to be sold in favour of respondents to the extent of area for which permission was granted by the State Govt. - Award of interest on the principal amount and decreetal amount in the impugned judgment was perfectly justifiable on the basis of the facts and circumstances of the case - Appeal dismissed.
