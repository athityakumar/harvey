Government of Andhra Pradesh and another v B. Jagjeevan Rao

Supreme Court of India

12 May 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, N.V. Ramana

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 395; (2014) 13 SCC 239; 2014 (143) FLR 39; 2014(7) SCALE 434; [2014] 7 S.C.R. 523

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Reinstatement, Dismissal from services, Convicted

Summary: Service - Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 389(1) - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 7 and 13(1)(d) r/w s. 13(2) - Bribe - Convicted - Dismissal from services - Reinstatement - Challenged - Respondent was charge sheeted for offences punishable u/ss. 7 and 13(1)(d) r/w s. 13(2) of the Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988 and after trial respondent was convicted and sentenced - Respondent filed appeal before HC - HC directed suspension of sentence and enlargement of bail on certain conditions - After conviction respondent was dismissed from service - Application was file by respondent before Tribunal - Tribunal dismissed respondents application - Petition was filed by respondent before HC - HC quashed the order of Tribunal and lancinated order of dismissal - Hence instant appeal - Whether the order of HC was sustainable -

Held, HC had only directed suspension of sentence - There was no order of stay of conviction - In instant case respondent was convicted u/ss. 7 and 13(1)(d) r/w s. 13(2) of the Act and sentenced to one year rigorous imprisonment and view was taken from The Deputy Director Of Collegiate Education v S. Nagoor Meera 1995 Indlaw SC 1581; Shanker Dass v Union Of India And Anr 1985 Indlaw SC 297 - Thus, keeping in view expected standard of administration, conviction on charge of corruption had to be viewed seriously and unless conviction was annulled, employer should not be compelled to take employee back in service - Therefore, HC had clearly erred in its interpretation of r. 25(1) and further committed illegality in not keeping in mind distinction between stay of conviction and suspension of sentence as envisaged u/s. 389(1) CrPC - Hence, impugned judgment and order of HC being sensitively susceptible was set aside - It was well settled in law that there was distinction between suspension of sentence and stay of conviction - Rama Narang v Ramesh Narang & Ors. 1995 Indlaw SC 2249; K. C. Sareen v C. B. I., Chandigarh 2001 Indlaw SC 20745 followed on - Appeal allowed.
