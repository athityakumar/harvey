Subrata Chattoraj and another v Union of India and others

Supreme Court of India

09 May 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, C. Nagappan

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 359; (2014) 8 SCC 768; JT 2014 (7) SC 435; 2014 (3) RCR(Criminal) 419; 2014(6) SCALE 590; [2014] 6 S.C.R. 783

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Chit fund scam - Transfer of investigations from State Agencies to CBI - Instant case was of a major financial scam nicknamed 'Chit Fund Scam' affecting lakhs of depositors across several States in the Eastern parts of country - Petitioner had floated several schemes to allure the depositors to collect from the market a sizeable amount on the promise of the depositors getting attractive rewards and returns - Report suggests that the investors were promised very high returns by way of interest rate ranging from 10% to 18% - Said returns promised to the depositors were, according to the Report, too good to be true - Failure of the group companies to refund the deposits made with them was bound to as it indeed has led to a public outcry against the scam on account of the huge amount that was collected by these companies by defrauding a very large section of the public majority of whom appear to be from middle class, lower middle class or poorer sections of the society - The Govt. of West Bengal acted in response to the protests and the public anguish over a fraud of such colossal magnitude and set up a Commission of Inquiry headed by Mr. Justice Shyamal Kumar Sen, retired Chief Justice, Allahabad HC with 4 others to be nominated by the Govt. to inquire into the matters - X, Member of Parliament, Rajya Sabha, was arrested in connection with the case registered in Bidhannagar South Police Station after being interrogated on several occasions - X was the media CEO of Saradha Group of Companies - Y, Member of Parliament was also interrogated by serious Fraud Investigation Office in relation to the petitioner and that the Special Investigating Team and the police authorities were extending full support and cooperation to the Central Agencies like Enforcement Directorate, Serious Fraud Investigation Office etc. for effective investigation of the scam - Writ petitions filed seeking transfer of investigation from the State Agencies to the CBI - The State had in that view opposed the prayer of the petitioner for transfer of the investigation from the State Police to the CBI - Whether investigations on petitioner could be transferred from the State Agencies to the CBI -

Held, no basis of the apprehension expressed by the State Govts. - A lot could be said about the independence of CBI as a premier Investigating Agency but so long as there was nothing substantial affecting its credibility it remains a premier Investigating Agency - Those not satisfied with the performance of the State Police more often than not demand investigation by the CBI for it inspires their confidence - SC could not, therefore, decline transfer of the cases only because of certain stray observations or misplaced apprehensions expressed by those connected with the scam or those likely to be affected by the investigation - Investigation could be transferred from the State Police Agency to the CBI - State Police Agencies currently investigating the cases should provide the fullest cooperation to the CBI including assistance in terms of men and material to enable the latter to conduct and complete the investigation expeditiously - Petitions disposed of.
