K. Subramani v K. Damodara Naidu

Supreme Court of India

13 November 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

C. Nagappan, V. Gopala Gowda

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 769; (2015) 1 SCC 99; 2015 ALL MR (Cri) 789; 2015 (1) BC 244; 2015 CRLJ 912; JT 2014 (12) SC 620; 2015 (4) KarLJ 118; 2015 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 34; 2015 (3) MahLJ 21; 2014 (4) MLJ(Crl) 608; 2015(3) M.P.H.T. 202; 2015 (2) MPLJ 251; 2015 (1) OLR 260; 2014 (4) RCR(Civil) 1028; 2014 (4) RCR(Criminal) 985; 2014(12) SCALE 677; [2014] 11 S.C.R. 169; 2015 (1) WLN(SC) 157

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Retrial, Sustainability

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, s. 138 - Dishonour of cheque - Retrial - Sustainability - Appellant accused borrowed loan from respondent complainant and issued cheque for the liability - Respondent presented the check , it was dishonored with endorsement 'fund insufficient' - Respondent filed a complaint u/s. 138 of the Act against appellant - Trial Court acquitted accused - Respondent filed appeal before HC - HC set aside the judgments of acquittal and remanded the cases to courts concerned directing retrial - Hence instant appeal - Whether the order recorded by HC was sustainable.

Held, respondent and appellant were working as Lecturers in Govt. college at relevant time and alleged loan of Rs.14 lakhs is claimed to have been paid by cash and it is disputed. Both of them were governed by Govt. Servants' Conduct Rules which prescribes mode of lending and borrowing. There is nothing on record to how that the prescribed mode was followed. The source claimed by complainant is savings from his salary and amount of Rs.5 lakhs derived by him from sale of site belonging to him. Neither in complaint nor in chief-examination of complainant, there is any averment with regard to sale price of site. The concerned sale deed was also not produced. Though the complainant was income-tax assessee he had admitted in his evidence that he had not shown the sale of site in his income-tax return. On the contrary complainant has admitted in his evidence that in the year 1997 he had obtained loan of Rs.1,49,205/- from LIC. It is pertinent to note that the alleged loan of Rs.14 lakhs is claimed to have been disbursed in the year 1997 to the accused. Further, complainant did not produce bank statement to substantiate his claim. Trial Court took into account testimony of the wife of complaint in another criminal case arising u/s. 138 of the Act in which she stated that appellant had not taken any loan from her husband. On consideration of entire oral and documentary evidence Trial Court came to the conclusion that complainant had no source of income to lend sum of Rs.14 lakhs to accused and he failed to prove that there is legally recoverable debt payable by the accused to him. In view of said conclusion of the Trial Court has been arrived at on proper appreciation of material evidence on record. Thus, impugned judgment of remand made HC is unsustainable and liable to be set aside. Appeal allowed.
