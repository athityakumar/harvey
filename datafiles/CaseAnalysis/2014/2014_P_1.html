P. C. Mishra v State (C.B.I.) and another

Supreme Court of India

27 March 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan, Vikramajit Sen

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 187; (2014) 14 SCC 629; AIR 2014 SC 1921; 2014 CRLJ 2482; JT 2014 (8) SC 227; 2014 (2) MLJ(Crl) 238; 2014 (2) RCR(Criminal) 356; 2014(4) SCALE 183; [2014] 4 S.C.R. 183

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Curable, Demand of bribe, Power to grant pardon

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 234, 306 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, s. 7, 13(1)(d), 13(2) - Demand of bribe - Power to grant pardon - Legality - Respondent (CBI) registered a case u/s. 7 of the Act against appellant (accused), the then Asst. Commissioner of sales Tax and his Reader (respondent no. 2), for demanding Rs.4,000/- as bribe for settling the appeal filed against the order of Sales Tax officer - CBI on investigation, noticed that respondent no. 2 was not a leading accused in the case, had filed an application u/s. 306 of CrPC before Special Judge, for grant of pardon to respondent no. 2, which was allowed and was not challenged and had attained finality - Thereafter, appellant filed an application u/s. 234 of CrPC questioning the pardon granted to respondent no. 2, which was dismissed holding that Metropolitan Magistrate had power to grant pardon during investigation u/s. 306 of IPC - Aggrieved appellant filed a revision before HC, which was dismissed by HC - Hence, instant appeal - Whether impugned order granting pardon to respondent no. 2 was within jurisdiction and justified -

Held, both Magistrate as well as the Special Judge had concurrent jurisdiction in granting pardon u/s. 306 of CrPC while the investigation was going on - But, in a case, where Magistrate has exercised his jurisdiction u/s. 306 of CrPC even after the appointment of a Special Judge under the Act and has passed an order granting pardon, same was only a curable irregularity, which would not vitiate the proceedings, provided the order was passed in good faith - In fact, in instant case, Special Judge himself has referred the application to Chief Metropolitan Magistrate/Metropolitan Magistrate to deal with the same since the case was under investigation - In such circumstances, Court did not find error in Special Judge directing the Chief Metropolitan Magistrate or Metropolitan Magistrate to pass appropriate orders on the application of CBI in granting pardon to respondent no. 2 so as to facilitate the investigation - Appeal dismissed.
