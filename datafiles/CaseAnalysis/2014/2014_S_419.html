Manmohan Sharma v State of Rajasthan and others

Supreme Court of India

01 April 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, C. Nagappan

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 214; (2014) 5 SCC 782; JT 2014 (4) SC 536; 2014(4) SCALE 516; 2014 (3) SLR 667

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Fraudulent appointment

Summary: Service - Fraudulent appointment - Maintainability - HC dismissed petitions filed by candidates who were said to have obtained fraudulent appointment orders in their favour - HC recorded a finding that those appointed and removed in terms of directions issued by State Government did not qualify for such appointment on true and proper interpretation of order passed by SC in Kailash Chand Sharma v State of Rajasthan and Others 2002 Indlaw SC 1748 - Hence instant appeals - Whether order recorded by HC was just and proper -

Held, appellants were appointed and served for nearly a decade, however there were allegations that such appointments were obtained by mis- representation of facts and fraudulently - Court did not consider, appellants to be entitled to relief of regularization of their services as prayed for by them - Had, said that Court should not ignore fact that appellants would be left without any alternate avenues of employment at this stage of their lives - Subject to any finding that might be recorded by competent Court, as regards alleged fraudulent nature of appointments secured by appellants, it was directed that appellants as were appointed as teachers and as had now been terminated might be given one-time concession of relaxation of upper age limit and considered in next selection process in relaxation of rules regarding such age limit prescribed for appointment as teachers - It was made clear that one-time relaxation for appellants to try their luck in next selection process - Appellants or such of them as wish to avail of concession should file undertaking before appointing authority concerned to effect that fresh appointment if any given to them pursuant to age relaxation should stand terminated in case they were found guilty and sentenced to imprisonment in criminal case registered against them for obtaining fraudulent appointment - Beyond that it was not considered appellants to be entitled to any relief from Court - Appeals disposed of.
