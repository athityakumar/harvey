State of Uttarakhand and others v Kanhaya Lal

Supreme Court of India

29 April 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Vikramajit Sen, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 322; (2014) 14 SCC 388; 2014(6) SCALE 75; 2014 (6) SLR 259

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Advertised post - Submission of documents - Appointment - Directions of HC - SLP - Maintainability - Petitioner filed instant petition to dislodge and reverse the findings of the Single Judge, which order was affirmed by DB - Single Judge had directed by order that the case of respondent be considered within three months for appointment to the post of Assistant Teacher (Language) L.T. Grade, if there was no other impediment in his selection - Hence instant petition - Whether petitioner was justified in not appointing respondent though there were directions from HC -

Held, only infirmity in the application of respondent was that he had failed to include his Marksheet along with his application form, which was submitted by him on 4-11-1997, he had made full compliance by personally filing his Marksheet on 12-11-1997 - Keeping in perspective the fact that a corrigendum was issued preponing the last date of submission of Forms from 21-11-1997 to 10-11-1997, it would have been advisable and prudent to infuse some elasticity or laxity in the observance of the last date for submission of forms - Order/Report of the Additional Director of Education, was wholly contrary to the directions given by Single Judge, inasmuch as it failed to unravel any 'other impediment' in granting appointment to writ petitioner after treating his application to be in conformity with the subject advertisement as per the judgment of Single Judge - Respondent already stands selected and appointed as Assistant Teacher (Language) L.T. Grade on 4-10-2005 - SC directed the State to appoint respondent to the post of Assistant Teacher (Language) L.T. Grade, i.e. the advertised post, treating writ petitioner to have been appointed along with the other candidates who were selected in response to the subject advertisement for appointment to the post of Assistant Teacher (Language) L.T. Grade - Respondents seniority should, therefore, be fixed such that it was not detrimental to the services already rendered by him - Petition dismissed.
