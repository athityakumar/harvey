Bihar State Government Secondary School Teachers Association v Ashok Kumar Sinha and others

Supreme Court of India

07 May 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, Surinder Singh Nijjar

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 348; (2014) 7 SCC 416; AIR 2014 SC 2824; JT 2014 (7) SC 454; 2014(6) SCALE 730; [2014] 7 S.C.R. 371; 2014 (4) SLR 718

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Bihar Education Service Department of Examination Rules, 1973, Bihar Education Rules, 2014

Summary: Service - Bihar Education Rules, 2014 - Merging of cadres - Sanction by Court - Government's non performance of direction issued by Court - Sustainability - Petitioner was an Association representing the teachers of the Bihar Subordinate Education Service (BSES) - Petitioner had filed a writ petition in the HC claiming merger of their cadre with the Bihar Education Service (BES) - Writ petition was allowed and the Letter Patent Appeal(LPA) and the Special Leave Petition(SLP) filed against the same were dismissed - Since the benefits of merger of cadre were still not being granted, another writ petition was filed, which too was allowed and affirmed in LPA - Although leave was granted in the SLP filed by the State of Bihar, ultimately the Civil Appeal was dismissed by the judgment dt. 19-4-2006 resulting in the outcome in favour of the petitioner - In compliance of the said judgment of SC, a Resolution merging the cadre of BSES with BES was issued on 7-7-2006 and the BSES teachers were granted benefits of the merger, like enhancement of payscale, promotion etc. - However, the benefits of merger were not granted - Hence instant petition - Whether denial of benefits of merger was sustainable -

Held, by placing the erstwhile BSES teachers in teaching sub cadre, were allowed to go upto the position of Principal which was the highest promotional post in their sub cadre - On the other hand BES Officers who were put in administrative sub cadre would continue to control the schools - Moreover, each sub cadre was to have its separate seniority list - It means the combined gradation list was given a go bye and even by bringing BSES in BES, segregation between the two cadres was achieved with these provisions - To SC's mind the provisions of Rules negated the very effect of merger which was envisaged way back in the year 1977 - In spite of succeeding in three rounds of litigation, the petitioners were not only treated as a distinct and separate class with the creation of sub cadre, the benefit which could accrue to them in a combined seniority list, as a result of merger, had been snatched away from them - What was given to these petitioners by the respondents in compliance of the judgment earlier, was taken away with the promulgation of Rules - Lest SC might be misunderstood, SC made it clear that it was the prerogative of the Govt. to frame service rules in one or the other manner - In case provisions contained in those Rules offend the rights of any of the employees, they had an independent right to challenge the same which could be judicially scrutinized by the Courts, applying the settled principles of judicial review - However, if such an exercise was undertaken on the premise that it was done to comply with the directions contained in the judgment and the Court found that, ex facie, it was not so and on the contrary offended the directions in the judgment, such a move could not be countenanced - It was also crystal clear and borne from the record that the whole exercise was done to go out of way to help BES Officers - In fact, respondent even argued on those lines by pointing out that the promotions in BES cadres were made in two stages i.e. upto 31-12-1995 in one stage and from 1-1-1996 till the second stage - From 1-1-1996 no promotion was given to BES because it was need based and since the posts were to be identified, only the additional charge was given to them - What was lost sight of, in the entire arguments, was that, the merger was to take effect from 1977 and even Resolution to that effect was passed by the Cabinet - Further, once that was done and the combined gradation list issued in the year 2007 was to be necessarily revived, further steps were to be taken from that stage - SC was not suggesting that those of the petitioners who become senior to their counterparts in BES, should be given automatic promotion to second or third stages which was the apprehension expressed - Such officers, as a result of merger and combined gradation list, would take their rightful place and thereafter their career progression would be permissible as per the Rules - For that purpose it was open to the Government to frame the Rules and make provisions laying down eligibility conditions - However, by well-crafted technique of creating sub cadres and treating teaching category as dying sub cadre, almost the same result, which was the position before the merger, was achieved - It was obvious that such provisions in Rules were made with the sole intention to frustrate the effect of the judgment - SC had no hesitation to say that would amount to contempt of the Court - Petitions disposed of.
