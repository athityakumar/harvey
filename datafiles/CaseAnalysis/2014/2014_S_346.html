Manoharlal v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

21 November 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Uday Umesh Lalit, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 787; 2015 (1) JabLJ 124; JT 2014 (13) SC 125; 2015 (1) RCR(Criminal) 94; 2014(13) SCALE 45

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 376 - Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes (Prevention of Atrocities) Act, 1989, ss. 3 - Rape - Conviction - Challenged - Appellant was charged for having committed offence u/s. 376 of IPC and u/s. 3(2)(v) of the Act - PW3 did not support the case of the prosecution and was declared hostile - Trial Court relying on the testimony of the victim convicted the appellant u/s. 376 of IPC - Trial Court however acquitted the appellant of the offence u/s. 3(2)(v) of the Act - On appeal HC relied on the testimony of the victim and confirming the order of conviction and sentence it dismissed the appeal - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, though sole testimony of the prosecutrix can sufficiently be relied upon to bring home the case against the accused, in the instant case victim version was improbable and difficult to accept on its face value and there is complete lack of corroboration on material particulars. The medical examination of the victim did not result in any definite opinion that she was subjected to rape. PW3 who was like a brother to the victim has not supported the case of the prosecution. The sequence of events as narrated would show that she had allegedly accompanied the appellant to various places. In the circumstances, it is difficulty in relying upon the version of the victim alone to bring home the charge against the appellant. Appellant to be given benefit of doubt. Order of conviction and sentence passed against the appellant set aside. Appeal allowed.
