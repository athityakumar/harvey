H. C. Kulwant Singh and others v H. C. Daya Ram and others

Supreme Court of India

30 June 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 530; (2015) 3 SCC 177; AIR 2014 SC 3083; JT 2014 (8) SC 305; 2014(8) SCALE 150; [2014] 11 S.C.R. 1053

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Promotions

Summary: (A)Service - Punjab Police Rules, 1934 , r. 13.7 - Punjab Police (Chandigarh Amendment) Rules, 1988 - Promotions - Sustainability - Appellants and respondent Nos. 1 to 34 were recruited as Constables in Chandigarh Police - Departmental Promotion Committee on basis of test scheme in (i) Parade (ii) written test in general law and (iii) examination of service record - After amendment of said Rule batch of confirmed Constables were sent for Lower School Course at Police Training College - Said course was for six months and it was held twice a year one commencing in April and the other in October - Batch of fifteen Constables duly selected on basis of amended Rules was sent for Lower School Course in April, 1988 - Thereafter vide notification dtd. 17-6-1988 r. 13.7 of the 1934 Rules was amended by the 1988 Rules which came into force on date of publication in Chandigarh Administration Gazette - After Rule was amended, Senior Superintendent of Police, Chandigarh Administration issued letter dated 27-6-1988 to effect that test would be held some time in September, 1988 as laid down in the amended Rules - Being aggrieved by appellant and 24 others filed original application before Tribunal challenging validity of the said order - Tribunal opined that all confirmed Constables, including applicants before it, serving under Union Territory Chandigarh became eligible for consideration for promotion to posts of Head Constables on basis of unamended r. 13.7 of the 1934 Rules and question of Head Constables being appointed in accordance with the amended r. 13.7 of the 1934 Rules could arose only thereafter - Further issued directions to recast seniority list of Head Constables on foundation of seniority rules and not to revert any Head Constable or Assistant Sub-Inspector with rider that they should avail further promotion solely on basis of their revised seniority warranting no interference by SC or acted beyond ambit of jurisdiction in its appreciation and application of well settled principles that would make the order pregnable inviting its extinction - Further, application was filed by appellants before Tribunal for quashing of order dtd 28-12-1989 seeking direction to respondents to place private respondents in List 'C' in the context of their seniority - Respondents filed petition before HC - HC quashed orders dtd. 23-9-1998 and 8-1-1990 and HC passed protective order to effect that Administration should finalise seniority of Head Constables on basis of Seniority Rule in respect of 71 Constables, but as result of finalization of seniority in accordance with Rules, respondents should not revert any Head Constable or Assistant Sub Inspector - Such Head Constable or Assistant Sub Inspector should continue to discharge his/her duties but should avail further promotion only on basis of his/her turn as per revised seniority -

Hence instant appeal - Whether the order recorded was just and proper -

Held, Tribunal, on first occasion, while quashing order dtd. 27-6-1988 which was resultant order after amendment dtd. 17-6-1988, clearly laid down that authorities were required to prepare fresh list for sending Constables to Lower School Course in accordance with pre-amended r. 13.7 as it existed prior to 17-6-1988 so far as vacancies of Head Constables which had come into existence prior to date of notification and criterion to be adopted by them could be seniority-cum-merit as prescribed therein - It was also unequivocally ruled that it would be open to respondents to act in accordance with amended Rule in respect of vacancies/posts of Head Constables which might have occurred subsequent to coming into force of amended Rule or which might fall vacant thereafter - Reliance was placed on Y.V. Rangaiah and Others; (2) State of Andhra Pradesh and Others v J. Sreenivasa Rao and Others 1983 Indlaw SC 19 wherein, in factual matrix, it was ruled by SC that vacancies had occurred prior to amended rules would be governed by old rules and not by amended rules and Court further reiterated that it did not have slightest doubt that posts which fell vacant prior to amended rules would be governed by old rules and not by new rules - Thus, decision of tribunal, on first round, related to vacancies of Head Constables that had come into existence prior to date of notification, 17-6-1988 - Union Territory of Chandigarh in his written note of submissions stated that 50 vacancies were found to have occurred before amendment and, accordingly, list was prepared in order of seniority as per provisions of un-amended rules - It was necessary to clear maze that as far as this exercise was concerned there was no cavil - Dispute arose when authorities on 28-10-1988 prepared list of eligible Constables/ad hoc Head Constables who fulfilled prescribed conditions to sit in competitive examination to be held in January, 1989 - Said action of authorities compelled applicants to file three Original Applications challenging validity of Rule and quashment of order dtd. 28-10-1988 whereby list was drawn up of eligible candidates- Tribunal on 31-3-1989 modified its original interim order and directed that selection of Head Constables might be made and given effect to subject to final decision of those Original Applications - Tribunal observed that validity of selection would be subject to final decision of the case - After tribunal passed said interim order, authorities conducted test as per amended rule which had come into force with effect from 17-6-1988 and made selections and appointed successful candidates as Head Constables on the basis of merit - Tribunal noted rival submissions which was adverted to earlier, and opined that applicants in Original Applications vested right under pre-amended Rule as confirmed Constables and they had been deprived of the said vested right - Thus, decision which was sanctuary of errors could not have been allowed to gain benefit of sanctuary of protection and acceptance, that would be travesty of justice - Appeal allowed.

(B)Service - Promotions - Legality - Whether order dtd. 8-1-1990 was legally justified -

Held, there was no shadow of doubt that it was based entirely on earlier order dtd. 28-9-1988 which was rendered by Tribunal being approached by real aggrieved parties and Tribunal relied on binding precedents, had held that applicants were entitled to be considered under amended Rule that came into force on 4-3-1982 in respect of vacancies accrued during period that's 4-3-1982 to 17-6-1988 - Latter decision dtd. 8-1-1990 completely misread said decision and erroneously observed that every confirmed employee had vested right - It did not properly appreciate that the right was restricted to accrued vacancies and assumedly remained oblivious to categorical findings of earlier decision that it was open to respondents therein to take steps in accordance with amended Rule in respect of vacancies to post of Head Constables which might have accrued subsequent to coming into force of amended Rules which might fall vacant thereafter - Thus, order was absolutely unsustainable and cornerstone of the impugned order dtd. 28-9-1998 was order dtd. 8-1-1990 - Thus, decision which was sanctuary of errors could not have been allowed to gain benefit of sanctuary of protection and acceptance, that would be travesty of justice - Appeal allowed.
