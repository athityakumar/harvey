B. S. Sandhu v Government of India and others

Supreme Court of India

21 May 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Patnaik, Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 368; (2014) 12 SCC 172; AIR 2014 SC 3409; JT 2014 (7) SC 315; 2014 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 3070; 2014 (5) MLJ 503; 2014 (3) RCR(Civil) 109; 2014(7) SCALE 390; [2014] 6 S.C.R. 329

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Forest (Conservation) Rules, 1981, Punjab Land Preservation Act, 1900

Summary: (A) Land & Property - Punjab Land Preservation Act, 1900, ss. 3, 4 and 5 - Forest (Conservation) Act, 1980 - Change of land use - PIL was filed stating that Forest Hill Golf and Country Club in Village Karoran, District Ropar, near Chandigarh was being developed in blatant violation of the environmental and forest laws as well as the orders passed by HC in December 1996 - Further stated that Punjab Govt. permitted change of land use as a quid pro quo because a large number of top IAS and IPS officers and other decision makers have been given honorary membership of the club or have been allowed to use the premises and facilities of the Club for private functions - HC allowed the writ petitions directing A and the companies and/or the societies floated by him to immediately close down its entire enterprise known as 'Forest Hill Country Club Resort and Golf Course' and to demolish all the illegally erected buildings within a period of 3 months and to handover the 'management' and 'control' of the land in question to the State Forest Department - Hence, instant appeals - Whether the land on which the Forest Hill Golf and Country Club of A was situated was forest land as on 25-10-1980 irrespective of its classification or ownership -

Held, this was a factual question and the HC should have decided this factual question on the basis of Govt. records as on 25-10-1980 and other materials filed before the HC, but the HC had instead decided this question by reference to the provisions of the 1900 Act and the records of the Forest Department in which the land was shown to be under the Forest Department because of the fact that the land was closed under the 1900 Act several decades before the enactment of the 1980 Act - Matter remanded to the HC for fresh hearing and fresh order in accordance with law - Consequently, all directions in the impugned order which flow out of the aforesaid finding of the HC that the land was 'forest land' for the purpose of s. 2 of the 1980 Act were set aside - Order accordingly.

(B) Land & Property - Punjab Land Preservation Act, 1900, ss. 3, 4 and 5 - Forest (Conservation) Act, 1980 - Change of land use - PIL was filed stating that village X was located in Kharar Tehsil of District Ropar and was about 8 kms. to the North-west of Chandigarh and the entire area of the village measuring about 3700 acres was covered under 1900 Act - Further submitted that 1980 Act was applicable to any land in the X village and, therefore, the land could not have been diverted for non-forest activities without the prior permission of the Central Govt. - HC held as the land in village Karoran was recorded in the records of the Forest Department of the Govt. of Punjab to be 'forest land', the same was 'forest land' within the meaning of s. 2 of the 1980 Act - Hence, instant appeals - Whether the conclusion of the HC that the land which was notified u/s. 3 of the 1900 Act and was regulated by the prohibitory directions notified u/ss. 4 and 5 of the 1900 Act was 'forest land' was correct in law -

Held, by recording a blanket finding that all land in village Karoran, District Ropar, was 'forest land' for the purpose of s. 2 of the 1980 Act, the HC had affected the legal rights of several villagers, agriculturists, farmers, shop owners, inhabitants of village Karoran, District Ropar, who were carrying on their respective occupations on their land even before the enactment of the said Act on 25-10-1980 - HC should have been very careful before recording findings which affect the property rights of persons protected by art. 300A of the Constitution - HC had not enquired into the basis of inclusion of the areas in forest by the State Forest Department nor had HC considered as to whether a land becomes 'forest land' by mere inclusion of the same under the notification under s. 3 of the 1900 Act - Findings of the HC that the entire land in village Karoran, District Ropar, was 'forest land' for the purpose of s. 2 of the 1980 Act - Matter remand the matter to the HC for fresh hearing - Order accordingly.
