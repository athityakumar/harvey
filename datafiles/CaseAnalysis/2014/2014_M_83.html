Purshottam Das Tandon Dead by Lrs. v Military Estate Officer and others

Supreme Court of India

13 August 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjan Gogoi, M.Y. Eqbal

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 519; (2014) 9 SCC 344; AIR 2014 SC 3555; 2015 (1) ALD(SC) 41; 2014 (106) ALR 901; 2014 (6) AWC 5620; 2014 (3) CLT(SC) 211; JT 2014 (9) SC 225; 2014 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 3479; 2015 (1) MahLJ 574; 2014 (6) MLJ 371; 2015 (1) MPLJ 253; 2015 (1) RLW 9; 2014(9) SCALE 357; [2014] 7 S.C.R. 1085; 2014 (4) WLN(SC) 63

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Family & Personal; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Family & Personal - Civil Procedure - Entitlement - Maintainability - Appellant filed petitions before HC and contended that property was purchased by his predecessors-in-interest and had fallen to his share in a family settlement - Petitions were dismissed by HC and further held that the entitlement of appellant to reliefs claimed therein would have to be adjudicated in suit for declaration of title - Hence instant appeal - Whether order recorded by HC was maintainable -

Held, it was abundantly clear that issue of title was kept open in proceedings - Subject matter of inter-pleader suit and proceedings arose there from clearly pertained to entitlement of present contesting parties to receive rent in respect of property in question - On other hand, in writ petitions appellant claimed ownership, sought mutation, as owner in cantonment records and also permission to raise construction, right flowing from incidence of ownership of land - Subject matter of two proceedings that's inter-pleader suit and appeals arose there from and petitions filed by appellant were, therefore, not directly and substantially the same so as to attract principle of res judicata enshrined in s. 11 CPC - HC dismissed petitions and left it open for appellant to avail remedy of civil suit to get title to property adjudicated by competent Civil Court, no fault, much less any infirmity could be found so as to warrant our interference - Appeal dismissed.
