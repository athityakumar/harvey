Kerala State Toddy Shop Contractors Association v T. N. Prathapan, MLA and others

Supreme Court of India

01 September 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, Vikramajit Sen

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 679; 2014 (4) KLT 705; 2014(10) SCALE 686; [2014] 7 S.C.R. 589

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Excise

Keywords: Sale, Indian Made Foreign Liquor, Ban

Summary: Excise - Constitution - Foreign Liquor (Compounding, Blending and Bottling), Rules 1975, r. 28A - Indian Made Foreign Liquor - Sale - Ban - State of Kerala in violation of r. 28A of 1975 Rules, had issued orders to allow sale of Indian Made Foreign Liquor (IMFL) from licensed premises - PIL was filed before HC that the licenced premises for sale of liquor are required to remain closed on the days specified in 1975 Rules - HC allowed the petition - Hence instant appeals.

Held, as evident, in writ petition, there was no assail to 2002 Rules. There was no pleading in that regard and no relief was sought on that score. From aforesaid authorities, it is clear as day that in absence of any assertion how particular provision offends any of Articles of the Constitution, the same cannot be adverted to. It is a settled principle of law that a person who assails a provision to be ultra vires must plead the same in proper perspective. HC was required to interpret r. 28A of the Rules. Under such circumstances, HC has fallen into grave error by declaring another Rule as discriminatory and unreasonable. Suo motu assumption of jurisdiction in this regard is totally uncalled for and, therefore, that makes the judgment and order declaring the 2003 Rules as discriminatory sensitively susceptible. Judgment and order of HC, as far as it declares the 2002 Rules as ultra vires in respect of toddy shops being kept open on first day of all English Calendar month is set aside. Appeals allowed.
