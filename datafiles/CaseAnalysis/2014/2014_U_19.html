Union of India and other v S. P. Verma

Supreme Court of India

11 February 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

C. Nagappan, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 80; (2014) 3 SCC 491; 2014 (2) AWC 1711; 2014(2) SCALE 221; 2014 (5) SLR 351; 2014 (3) WLN(SC) 48

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Dismissal from service, Illegal occupation of land

Summary: Employment-Dismissal-Quash-ability-Dismissal on account of respondent-employee having occupied parcel of land owned by Indian Railways with whom respondent-employee was employed at relevant point of time-Admittedly, appeal preferred by respondent-employee under Section 9 of Public Premises (Eviction of Unauthorised Occupants) Act, 1971 was pending when punishment was inflicted on respondent-employee and on that ground Central Administrative Tribunal quashed dismissal order but gave liberty to disciplinary authority to restart proceedings after final outcome of appeal or other event mentioned therein-High Court upheld order of Central Administrative Tribunal and held that since appeal filed by respondent-employee against order of eviction passed by Estate Officer had not been disposed of, appellant would be free to take further steps in matter once appeal disposed of-High Court rightly held so-No interference called with impugned judgment and order of High Court.

Legislation Cited

Public Premises (Eviction of Unauthorised Occupants) Act, 1971

Public Premises (Eviction of Unauthorised Occupants) Act, 1971 s. 9
