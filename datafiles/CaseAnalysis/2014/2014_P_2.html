Baluram v P. Chellathangam and others

Supreme Court of India

10 December 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Adarsh Kumar Goel, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 845; AIR 2015 SC 1264; 2015(1) ALL MR 949; 2015 (109) ALR 680; 2015 (2) ALT(SC) 44; 2015 (1) AWC 3; 2014 (4) CLT(SC) 424; 2014 (6) CTC 803; JT 2014 (13) SC 571; 2015 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 71; 2015 (1) RCR(Civil) 333; 2015 (127) RD 552; 2014(13) SCALE 733; 2015 (1) WLN(SC) 84

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 1 r. 10 - Adding parties - Respondent Nos.2 and 3 acting as trustees of the trust entered into the agreement to sell the suit property in favour of the respondent No. 1 (plaintiff) - Respondent No. 1 was ready and willing to perform his part of the contract but the respondent Nos.2 and 3 failed to execute the sale deed even in extended time - Suit was filed wherein appellant filed application for being impleaded as defendant, pleading that he would suffer prejudice being beneficiary of the Trust if the sale was effected at a throw away price - Trial Court held that the appellant was not a stranger to the subject matter of dispute and was entitled to be impleaded as a party - Revision filed thereagainst was allowed by HC - Hence, instant appeal - Whether the HC was justified in reversing the order of the Trial Court allowing the prayer of the appellant to be added as a party in a suit for specific performance filed by Respondent No.1-plaintiff.

Held, the court can add anyone as a plaintiff or as a defendant if it finds that he is a necessary party or proper party. Such deletion or addition can be without any conditions or subject to such terms as the court deems fit to impose. In the instant case, the appellant could not be held to be a stranger being beneficiary of the Trust property. The Trial Court was justified in impleading him as a party. The HC erred in interfering with the order of the Trial Court. Appeal allowed.
