Krishna Kumar Rastogi v Sumitra Devi

Supreme Court of India

20 August 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Prafulla C. Pant, Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 537; (2014) 9 SCC 309; AIR 2014 SC 3635; 2014(5) ALL MR 917; 2014 (107) ALR 323; 2014 (5) AWC 5318; JT 2014 (9) SC 300; 2014 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 3436; 2014 (2) RCR(Rent) 245; 2014(9) SCALE 498; [2014] 9 S.C.R. 423

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - Civil Procedure - Uttar Pradesh Urban Buildings (Regulation of Letting, Rent and Eviction) Act, 1972, s. 21(1)(a) - Eviction - Denial - Challenged - Appellant landlord filed application u/s. 21(1)(a) of the Act against respondent tenant for release of shop in question - Appellant asked respondent to vacate premises occupied by her as same was required by appellant - Respondent contested application for release of accommodation - Prescribed Authority allowed the application of appellant - Respondent filed appeal before Additional District Judge - Appeal was allowed and order passed by Prescribed Authority was set aside - Aggrieved by said order appellant filed petition before HC - Petition was dismissed by HC - Hence instant appeals - Whether the order recorded by Trial Court was sustainable -

Held, on going through papers on record SC found that HC gave too much emphasis to affidavit filed by witness, that appellant attempted to sell disputed shop to him - It was relevant to mention that said fact was denied by appellant - SC opined, merely for reason that some witness stated that appellant attempted to sell property, his statement should not be said to be reliable, as had been believed by HC or Appellate Court, unless such fact was supported with documentary proof - There appeared no document on record to support bald statement of witness to dislodge case of bonafide requirement of shop claimed by appellant for his son who was unemployed - Another fact relied by HC pertained to fact that elder son of appellant was running business as tenant in shop opposite to disputed shop - Had it been found that son for whom landlord needed shop had already got his own shop, it could have been said that need for landlord was not genuine, but in instant case if one of sons was running his business that too in rented accommodation, it should not be said that need of landlord was not bonafide - Sons of appellant were not supposed to starve on street till shop was actually vacated for them - Thus, HC erred in law in petition of the appellant - Appeals allowed.
