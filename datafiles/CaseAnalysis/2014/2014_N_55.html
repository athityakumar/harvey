Union of India Through C.B.I. v Nirala Yadav @ Raja Ram Yadav @ Deepak Yadav

Supreme Court of India

30 June 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, N.V. Ramana

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 527; (2014) 9 SCC 457; AIR 2014 SC 3036; 2014 CRLJ 3952; JT 2014 (8) SC 610; 2014 (4) MLJ(Crl) 323; 2014 (3) RCR(Criminal) 534; [2014] 6 S.C.R. 148

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 167(2) - Enlargement on bail - Benefit under the proviso s. 167(2) CrPC - Sustainability - Instant appeal was directed against the order passed by Single Judge enlarging respondent on bail solely on the ground that he was entitled to the benefit under the proviso appended to s. 167(2) CrPC - Hence instant appeal - Whether Single Judge was right in enlarging respondent on bail solely on the ground that he was entitled to the benefit under the proviso appended to s. 167(2) CrPC -

Held, prior to the date of expiry of 90 days which was the initial period for filing the charge-sheet, the prosecution neither had filed the charge-sheet nor had it filed an application for extension - Had an application for extension filed, then the matter would have been totally different - After the accused respondent filed the application, prosecution submitted an application seeking extension of time for filing of the charge-sheet - In instant case, the day accused filed the application for benefit of the default provision as engrafted under proviso to s. 167(2) CrPC the Court required the accused to file a rejoinder affidavit by the time the initial period provided under the statute had expired - There was no question of any contest as if the application for extension filed prior to the expiry of time - Adjournment by the Magistrate was misconceived - He was obliged on that day to deal with the application filed by the accused as required u/s. 167(2) CrPC - Such procrastination frustrate the legislative mandate - A Court could not act to extinguish the right of an accused if the law so confers on him - Law had to prevail - Prosecution could not avail such subterfuges to frustrate or destroy the legal right of the accused - Such an act was not permissible - Prosecution exhibited sheer negligence in not filing the application within the time which it was entitled to do so in law but made all adroit attempts to redeem the cause by its conduct - Appeal dismissed.
