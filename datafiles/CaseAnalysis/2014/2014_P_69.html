Shaha Ratansi Khimji and Sons v Proposed Kumbhar Sons Hotel Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

10 July 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya, Dipak Misra, R.M. Lodha

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 467; (2014) 14 SCC 1; AIR 2014 SC 2895; 2014 (5) ALD(SC) 181; 2014(6) ALL MR 465; 2014 (106) ALR 249; 2014 (5) AWC 4394; 2014 (3) CLT(SC) 126; 2015 (3) CTC 339; JT 2014 (8) SC 406; 2014 (3) KLT 1014; 2014 (2) RCR(Rent) 193; 2014 (125) RD 506; 2014(8) SCALE 455; [2014] 6 S.C.R. 1101

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Keywords: Restrain from digging of suit property

Summary: Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Restrain from digging of suit property - Justifiability - Appellants case was that respondent purchased 11/16th share of the plot - Respondent demolished RCC building existed over property and started digging for basement for construction of hotel - Respondent(s) without obtaining any requisite permission from municipality started digging ditch towards northern side wall of suit property, thereby exposing northern base of godown to vagaries of nature - Appellant filed suit and prayed that respondent be restrained from closing access of appellant to suit property from municipal road - Appellant further prayed that respondent be restrained from digging in manner which would caused damage to godown - Suit was dismissed by Trial Court - Appeal before Appellate Court - Appellate Court dismissed appellants appeal - Appeal before HC - HC dismissed appellants appeal on ground that tenancy right of appellant lapsed and no substantial question of law was involved in the appeal - Hence instant appeal - Whether the order recorded by HC was maintainable -

Held, it was not in dispute that respondent purchased lessor's interest - Lease continued even thereafter and did not extinguish - Lease was subsisting when shares of the land were purchased by respondent - However, interest of lessee was not purchased by respondent - What had been purchased by respondent was right and interest of ownership of property - Interest of appellant as lessee had not been vested with respondent - Therefore, SC was in view that tenancy of appellant should not be said to have been determined consequent upon demolition and destruction of tenanted premises - In view of fact and circumstances of the case, Court had no other option but to set aside impugned judgment and decree passed by HC - However, taking into consideration the fact that appellant was not in possession of suit property since long and Court was not inclined to direct restoration of possession of suit property to appellant - Instead respondent was directed to pay sum of Rs. 20,00,000/- in favour of appellant towards compensation for depriving appellant from enjoying suit property - Appeal allowed.
