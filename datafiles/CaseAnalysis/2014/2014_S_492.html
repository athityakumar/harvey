Ganesh Datt v State of Uttarakhand

Supreme Court of India

11 June 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

C. Nagappan, Jagdish Singh Khehar

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 393; (2014) 12 SCC 389; AIR 2014 SC 2521; 2014 CRLJ 3128; JT 2014 (7) SC 608; 2014 (3) MLJ(Crl) 370; 2014 (3) RCR(Criminal) 498; 2015 (1) RLW 501; 2014(7) SCALE 670; [2014] 6 S.C.R. 99

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 149, 307, 149, 324, 149, 147, 148 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellants/accused were charged for the offences punishable u/ss. 147, 148, 302 r/w ss. 149 and s. 307 r/w s. 149 and s. 324 r/w s. 149 IPC - Trial Court convicted appellants 302, 149, 307, 149, 324, 149, 147, 148 IPC - On appeal filed by appellants, HC dismissed the appeal - Hence instant appeals - Whether HC was justified in convicting appellants for the offences charged against them -

Held, prosecution examined PW1, 2 and 3 as having witnessed the occurrence - Said witnesses testified that accused persons armed with weapons attacked on deceased - Eye-witnesses who deny the presence of injuries on the person of accused were lying on most material point, and therefore, their evidence was unreliable - It assumed much greater importance where the evidence consists of interested or inimical witnesses - In instant case admittedly there was enmity between the accused family and the deceased family and PWs 1 to 3 were interested as well as inimical witnesses and their denial of injuries on the person of accused, makes their evidence unreliable - Sites of attack was also alleged to be not established by prosecution - Though blood- stained earth was claimed to seized from the occurrence place by IO PW7 it was not sent for chemical examination which could have fixed the sites of the assault - In almost all criminal cases the blood- stained earth found from the place of occurrence was invariably sent to the chemical examination and the report along with the earth was produced in the Court and yet that was one exceptional case where procedure was departed from for reasons best known to the prosecution - Prosecution failed to prove the guilt of appellants beyond reasonable doubt, and therefore, they were entitled to be acquitted - Appeals allowed.
