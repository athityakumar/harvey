K. Gunavathi v V. Sangeeth Kumar and others

Supreme Court of India

07 March 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjan Gogoi, N.V. Ramana, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 155; (2014) 11 SCC 491; AIR 2014 SC 3562; 2014 (141) FLR 574; JT 2014 (4) SC 216; 2014 (3) MLJ 247; [2014] 4 S.C.R. 395; 2014 (3) SLR 56

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Cancellation

Summary: Service - Appointment of under qualified instructors - Test - Directions - Validity - Tussle between under-qualified Computer Instructors appointed on ad-hoc basis (many of them have acquired the requisite qualification i.e. B.Ed. Degree in the meantime) and the B.Ed. qualified candidates who were yet to be appointed but claim to have been waiting for such appointment for long have surfaced once again, albeit, in a different manner - Aggrieved by the directions, both the B.Ed. degree holders and the terminated teachers had filed appeals - Petitions that were filed by the terminated Computer Instructors were heard alongwith the writ appeals - All such cases were disposed of by the impugned common order - It was the validity of the said common order, with directions that, 'Govt. shall follow the present policy of recruitment of teachers, while appointing computer instructors viz. recruitment through Teachers Recruitment Board and petitioners are eligible to apply along with others pursuant to the notification issued by the Teacher Recruitment Board - Writ petitioners are not entitled for any kind of preference. However, they are at liberty to apply for age relaxation to apply for the recruitment and the request for age relaxation, if any, would be considered on merit' thereof, was assailed in instant appeals - Whether directions issued by HC was valid -

Held, order dt. 19-11-2009 directing filling up of 175 existing vacancies and future vacancies of Computer Instructors on the basis of the employment exchange seniority was a conscious decision taken in departure from the virtually settled position in law that recruitment to public service, normally, ought to be by open advertisement and requisitions through the employment exchange could at best be supplemental - Such departure was felt necessary due to the compulsive needs dictated by peculiar facts of the case - At that point of time, out of the 1880 available posts 1683 posts had already been filled up by adhoc and underqualified Computer Instructors already working leaving only 175 vacancies and an unknown number of further vacancies which was contingent on the result of the second recruitment test ordered by SC as a one time measure - Both the recruitment tests, ordered by HC as well as SC, were exclusive to the adhoc and unqualified persons leaving a large number of qualified candidates like petitioners out of the arena of consideration - What would be the extent of the 'adverse' effect on the failed teachers if the remaining appointments were to be made on the basis of employment exchange seniority could not be determined with any degree of accuracy, inasmuch as a large number of such persons had qualified in the meantime and by virtue of cl. (v) of Para 53 of the impugned order, the names of the failed computer instructors who were earlier registered in the employment exchanges have been directed to be re- entered and their earlier seniority restored - While it was also correct that by ordering recruitment on the basis of employment exchange seniority other eligible candidates who could have taken part in the competitive examination would loose out, no such person was present before SC to persuade us to take the view that for the purpose of recruitment to the 652 posts of Computer Instructors the earlier order of SC dt. 19-11-2009 should not prevail - Said directions in the impugned order HC set aside and directed that recruitment to the 652 vacant posts should be made on the basis of employment exchange seniority - Appeals allowed.
