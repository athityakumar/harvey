Umakant and another v State of Chhatisgarh

Supreme Court of India

01 July 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

N.V. Ramana, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 430; (2014) 7 SCC 405; AIR 2014 SC 2943; 2014 (3) CGLJ 432; 2014 CRLJ 4078; JT 2014 (7) SC 543; 2014 (3) MLJ(Crl) 753; 2014 (2) OLR 675; 2014 (3) RCR(Criminal) 674; 2014 (3) RLW 2100; 2014(8) SCALE 141; [2014] 8 S.C.R. 69

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 302, 304-B and 498-A - Dowry Prohibition Act, 1961,ss. 3 and 4 - Murder - Dying declaration - Conviction - Challenged - It was alleged that deceased was married to appellant No.1 and after six months of the marriage, her husband and in-laws started harassing her to bring money from her father whenever she visits her parental home and also made a demand dowry - After the death of the deceased, investigation continued - Trial Court framed charges against the accused persons u/ss. 3 and 4 of Act and u/s. 304-B r/w s. 34, u/s. 302 r/w ss. 34 and 498-A of IPC - Trial Court basing on the evidence available on record convicted and sentenced the appellants u/ss. 498-A, 304-B, 302 r/w s. 34 of IPC and ss. 3 and 4 of the Act - On appeal, HC acquitted the appellants of the charges u/ss. 498-A, 304-B of IPC and ss. 3 and 4 of the Act and convicted them for the offence u/s. 302 of IPC, on the basis of dying declaration of the deceased - Hence, instant appeal - Whether the HC was right in convicting and sentencing the appellants u/s. 302 of IPC basing on the dying declaration of the deceased -

Held, burden of proof in criminal law was beyond all reasonable doubt - Prosecution had to prove the guilt of the accused beyond all reasonable doubt and it was also rule of justice in criminal law that if two views were possible on the evidence adduced in the case, one pointing to the guilt of the accused and the other towards his innocence, the view which was favourable to the accused should be adopted - In the instant case, evidence available on record and the dying declaration did not inspire confidence to make it the basis for the conviction of the appellants - HC basing on the same dying declaration, ought not to have convicted the appellants u/s. 302 of IPC, when they were acquitted u/ss. 304-B and 498-B of IPC and ss. 3 and 4 of the Act by the HC - Conviction and sentence imposed by the HC set aside - Appeal allowed.
