Rajendra Kumar Agrawal and another v State of Uttar Pradesh and others

Supreme Court of India

23 September 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Shiva Kirti Singh, Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 698; (2015) 1 SCC 642; AIR 2015 SC 1322; 2014 (107) ALR 24; 2015 (3) AWC 2233; 2015 (145) FLR 55; JT 2014 (11) SC 555; 2014(11) SCALE 136; 2015 (1) SLR 722

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Length Of Service, U.P. Avas Evam Vikas Parishad (Appointment and Conditions of Service of Chief Engineer) Regulations, 1990, Uttar Pradesh Government Servants Relaxation in Qualifying Service for Promotion Rules, 2006, U.P. Eligibility List Rules 1986

Summary: (A) Service - U.P. Public Services (Reservation for Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribes and Other Backward Classes) Act, 1994 - U.P. Government Servants Seniority Rules, 1991 - U.P. Government Servants Relaxation in Qualifying Service for Promotion Rules, 2006 - Filling of vacancies - Appellants and private respondents, Superintending Engineers in the service of U.P. Avas Evam Vikas Parishad (Parishad), were aspirants for the single post of Chief Engineer in the Parishad - Meanwhile appeal (U.P. Power Corporation Ltd. v. Rajesh Kumar & Ors 2012 Indlaw SC 461) with other connected appeals, came to be finally decided - By that judgment, SC declared the relevant provisions in the Act and the Rules providing for reservation in matters of promotion to be ultra vires - Subsequently, Parishad constituted a Selection Committee for selecting the most eligible candidate to hold the additional charge of Chief Engineer - Later, Parishad considered the recommendations of the Selection Committee and decided to relax the eligibility criteria - Decision of relaxation was communicated to the State Govt. - Decision of the Parishad were challenged - HC allowed the writ petition - Hence, instant appeals - Whether the Parishad should be allowed, as pleaded by it, to proceed with making regular promotion to the vacant post of Chief Engineer in the light of subsequent judgment of SC in the case of U.P. Power Corporation v. Rajesh Kumar & Ors. 2012 Indlaw SC 461.

Held, after the judgment of SC in the case of U.P. Power Corporation v. Rajesh Kumar & Ors. 2012 Indlaw SC 461 there was no impediment in the way of the Parishad in proceeding with regular selection for filling up the vacant post of Chief Engineer in a regular manner. SC had directed for making officiating arrangement by selecting a suitable Superintending Engineer to hold additional charge of the post of Chief Engineer only on account of order of status quo qua the process of regular promotion in pending civil appeals that were finally disposed of. Appeals disposed of.

(B) Service - Relaxation of eligibility - Validity - Whether the decision of the Parishad in compliance with request of the State Govt., to relax the minimum qualifying experience of five years was bad in law.

Held, Parishad acted as per letter of the State Govt. and granted relaxation to the extent permissible although this exercise could also result in enlarging the zone of consideration only to six candidates. There is material to show that the State Govt. or the Parishad resorted to exercise of said power for some unauthorized or oblique purpose. The exercise of power of relaxation appears to be in the interest of Parishad because the post of Chief Engineer, is a single post of considerable importance. The enlargement of zone of consideration with addition of relatively senior persons would only benefit the public cause by enabling selection of most meritorious person from a larger group of eligible persons. The power of relaxation under the Regulations was always available and has been exercised in a manner which does not call for any interference. Appeal disposed of.
