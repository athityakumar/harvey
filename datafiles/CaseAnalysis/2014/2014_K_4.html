K. K. Dixit and others Etc. v Rajasthan Housing Board and another Etc.

Supreme Court of India

05 September 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Shiva Kirti Singh, Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 594; (2015) 1 SCC 474; JT 2014 (10) SC 118; 2014(10) SCALE 255; [2014] 10 S.C.R. 397; 2014 (5) SLR 738; 2015 (1) UPLBEC 386

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Rajasthan Housing Board Act, 1970, Rajasthan Housing Board Employees Conditions of Recruitment and Promotion Regulations, 1976

Summary: Service - Promotion - Eligibility - Instant appeals were for resolving disputes between 'Diploma Holder' and 'Degree Holder' Engineers in the matter of eligibility for further promotion - Hence instant appeal - Whether the diploma holders who acquired the qualification of AMIE during service should be given the benefit of experience of service rendered by them as diploma holders for promotion to the post of Project Engineer (Senior) against the 20% quota for the degree holders or they need to acquire further three years' experience of service after acquiring the qualification of AMIE for availing such benefit -

Held, Project Engineers (Junior) recruited on the basis of diploma, upon their acquiring the qualification of 'AMIE', are not entitled to count their experience of service prior to acquisition of such qualification for the purpose of eligibility for promotion to the post of Project Engineer (Senior) against the 20% quota fixed for promotion of degree holder Project Engineers (Junior). In order to claim promotion against such 20% quota the three years' experience of service must be acquired after obtaining the qualification or degree of AMIE. SC directed the Board and its authorities to treat the writ petitions filed in the High Court as disposed of in the light of views and to determine the controversies raised in the writ petitions in that light by granting relief to the eligible persons expeditiously and preferably within 4 months, without upsetting the transactions which had taken place earlier and were not under challenge in the writ petitions. Regular promotions made in the past prior to 1992, which were not subject matter of writ petitions filed in 1992 will not be re-opened on account of views expressed in this judgment. Appeals disposed of.
