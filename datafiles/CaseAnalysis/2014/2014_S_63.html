Secr., Govt. of (NCT of Delhi) and others v Grade-I DASS Officers' Association and others

Supreme Court of India

30 July 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Shiva Kirti Singh, Vikramajit Sen

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 524; (2014) 13 SCC 296; JT 2014 (9) SC 361; 2014(9) SCALE 25; [2014] 8 S.C.R. 976; 2015 (1) SLR 647

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, arts.14 and 16 - Par scale - Grant of - Respondents in these appeals were applicants before Tribunal - Respondents preferred application against the appellants seeking quashing of the order dt. 10-8-2001 and also sought a direction to grant the scale of Rs.10000-325-15200/- to them and all other members of Grade-I (DASS) Officers' Association from the date they had completed 24 years of regular service or 9-8-1999, whichever was later - HC allowed application of respondents - To assail the judgment of the High Court whereby the appellants were directed to consider the placement of the Respondents in the next to next scale in the hierarchy, i.e., Rs.10000-325-15200/- by way of second upgradation in the ACPS - Appellant highlighted various stipulations in the ACPS particularly Condition Nos.1, 5.1, 7, 8, 9, 10 and 12 - Appellant had also distinguished the letter dt. 10-2-2000 discussed in Paragraph 17 of the HC judgment by showing that Doubt (1) was based upon entirely different fact situation and hence the clarification against that doubt was not attracted in the case of respondents - Appellant also assailed the adverse findings dt. 18-7-2001 containing Clarification No.52 discussed in Paragraph 18 of the HC judgment - According to appellant, the plea of the appellants that under ACPS the respondents could not be placed in a scale higher than what was provided for under the hierarchy to normal promotees had also been wrongly rejected in Paragraph 19 of the judgment - It was further contention on behalf of appellants that ACPS was a policy decision under which the burden of financial upgradation was continued every month and hence the appellants have good reasons to prefer these appeals also on the ground that HC should not have granted a relief which tantamounts to changing the policy of State -

Held, in view of stipulations and conditions in the ACPS, it could be safely concluded that the financial upgradation under the ACPS was not only in lieu of but also in anticipation of regular promotion - In such a situation, the contention advanced on behalf of appellants that financial upgradation claimed by the respondents could not be granted because the same would be much in excess of what the officer would gain on actual promotion in the hierarchy, was found to have substance - As a corollary, such claim of the respondents must be rejected on the ground that persons having better claims on actual promotion could be fitted only in the promotional post of Grade II (Group B) of DANICS, i.e. Rs.6500-200-10500/- whereas the respondents, on their claims being accepted, would get much higher pay scale of Rs.10000-325-15200/- available only to Grade I (Group A) in the DANICS - Such a situation would be violative of rules of fairness and Arts. 14 and 16 of the Constitution of India - The claim of the respondents had to be rejected as was done by the Tribunal in view of Cl. 7 of the ACPS read with other relevant clauses as well as on the basis of aforenoticed ground - Fairness on part of State was a constitutional obligation and hence a pay scale, which regularly promoted employee earlier belonging to Grade I (DASS) could not get due to established hierarchy for promotion, could not be granted to those like the Respondents on the plea that the financial upgradation to which they are found entitled as per existing hierarchy is too meager - In case Respondents' claim was to be allowed on the ground accepted by the HC that financial upgradation must be real and substantial, in case of regular promotion in future, employees like the Respondents would have to be reduced in their pay scale because actual or functional promotion as per established hierarchy could be only on a post in Grade II (Group B) in DANICS - Besides being grossly unfair to the regular promotees, the view taken by the HC would also violate the Government policy reflected by the stipulations in ACPS and the conditions attached to the same - In the facts of the case, it would not be proper to exercise power of judicial review so as to reverse or modify the policy decision of the concerned Government - Judgment in the case of The Government Of Tamil Nadu & Anr. v S.Arumugham & Ors. 1997 Indlaw SC 3004 had rightly been relied upon by Appellants on the aforesaid issue - So far as the judgment relied upon by respondents was concerned, it was found that the facts and issues decided in the case of Prakash Chand (supra) were quite different - The issue of fairness and constitutional obligation u/arts. 14 and 16 of Constitution did not arise in that case - So far as the judgment in the case of Council of Scientific and Industrial Research and Another v K. G. S. Bhatt and Another 1989 Indlaw SC 211 was concerned, the Court had found in that case that the Respondent-Civil Engineer had suffered injustice and had stagnated in one post for 20 years and, therefore, SC refused to exercise its powers u/art. 136 of the Constitution of India although promotion granted to the respondent in that case by Tribunal appeared to be erroneous - In the instant case, however, the benefit claimed by the respondents would not be restricted to them alone, rather, the policy of Government reflected by the ACPS should suffer a mis-interpretation for all times to come and the results would be violative of rules of fairness guaranteed by arts. 14 and 16 of the Constitution of India - Petitions dismissed.
