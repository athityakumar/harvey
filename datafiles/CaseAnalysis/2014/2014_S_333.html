M. P. Singh Bargoti v State of Madhya Pradesh and another

Supreme Court of India

27 November 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Shiva Kirti Singh, Vikramajit Sen

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 811; AIR 2015 SC 556; 2015 (2) AWC 2125; 2015 (1) JabLJ 42; JT 2014 (14) SC 544; [2014] 11 S.C.R. 211; 2015 (4) SLR 431

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Gradation List, Madhya Pradesh Police (Gazetted Officers) Recruitment Rules, 1987

Summary: Service - Promotion - Denial - Tribunal, on a case filed by appellant along with 29 others bearing O.A. No.893 of 1997 allowed on 11-3-1998 for preparation of a Combined Gradation List for promotion to the post of Deputy Superintendent of Police - Appellant was deprived of benefits of timely consideration and promotion to the said post although there was a Tribunal direction - Appellant filed application challenging inaction of respondent department - Tribunal dismissed application - Appellant's writ petition before HC was also dismissed thereafter - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, Tribunal and the HC erred in law as well as on facts in denying relief to the appellant. The position would have been different if appellant's junior had been promoted from a date subsequent to his superannuation. Then appellant would have suffered only on account of passage of time or innocuous delay but in the present facts he has suffered hostile and arbitrary discrimination vis-à-vis a junior. The order under appeal is therefore set aside. Since the appellant was in service only till 31-3-1998, he is held entitled to notional promotion to the post of Deputy Superintendent of Police w.e.f. 29-5-1997 till 31-3-1998. He will be deemed to have superannuated on that post and shall be given all the post-retirement benefits by re- calculating the same on the premise that he held the post of Deputy Superintendent of Police from 29-5-1997 till his superannuation on 31-3-1998. Appeal allowed.

Legislation Cited

Madhya Pradesh Police (Gazetted Officers) Recruitment Rules, 1987
