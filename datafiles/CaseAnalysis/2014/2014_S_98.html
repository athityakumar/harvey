Ghanshyam Sarda and another v Shiv Shankar Trading Company and another

Supreme Court of India

13 November 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Uday Umesh Lalit, A.R. Dave

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 770; (2015) 1 SCC 298; AIR 2015 SC 403; 2015(1) ALL MR 421; 2014 (4) BC 711; [2015] 188 Comp Cas 268; 2015 (1) CompLJ 565; 2014 (6) CTC 544; 2015 (1) DRTC 1; JT 2014 (13) SC 17; 2015 (4) MahLJ 157; 2015 (1) MLJ 497; 2015 (3) MPLJ 34; 2014(12) SCALE 732

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Sick

Summary: Banking & Finance - Practice & Procedure - Sick Industrial Companies (Special Provisions) Act, 1985, ss. 22, 26, 32 - Jurisdiction of Civil Court - Company filed reference before Board for Industrial and Financial Reconstruction (BIFR) under the Act and while proceedings were pending, applications were filed by unsecured creditors (SSTC) seeking permission from BIFR to institute Civil Suit for recovery of money from Company - While so, Company submitted before BIFR that application by appellant for restoration of shareholding pattern in Company could not be considered as BIFR no longer retained jurisdiction over the Company since as per audited balance sheet for period of nine months, the net worth of the Company turned positive and Company ought to be discharged - While so, SSTC filed suit before Civil Court against Company seeking inter alia, declaration that Company was no longer sick company and that BIFR ceased to have jurisdiction and all proceedings after filing of positive balance-sheet be declared without jurisdiction - Civil Court vide order dt.13-5-2013 held that BIFR ceased to have jurisdiction over Company and restrained defendants including BIFR from proceeding with reference - Hence, appellant filed First Appeal before HC challenging order dt.13-5-2013 of Civil Court - While so, in proceedings before it, BIFR observed that in absence of permission u/s. 22(1) of the Act, the suit filed by SSTC before Civil Court was not competent and that Company had not yet been deregistered from BIFR and filing of Civil Suit without taking permission is violative of the Act - On further proceedings on 1-7-2013, BIFR observed that order passed by Civil Court without jurisdiction was nullity and not binding upon it and also directed State Bank of India (SBI) to appoint independent auditor for Special Investigative Audit and file report about net worth position of Company as on 31-12-2012 - SSTC filed writ petition before HC challenging orders of BIFR- While so, SBI filed report before BIFR pointing out manipulations in balance sheet submitted by Company and that net worth of Company as on 31-12-2012 was in fact on negative side - On 5-9-2013, said report was taken on record by BIFR and so, Company filed writ petition before HC, questioning said order of BIFR - HC clubbed First Appeal by appellant, writ petitions by SSTC and Company and passed common order holding that First Appeal by appellant is not maintainable and disposed writ petitions on ground that since all proceedings before BIFR stood stayed, further proceeding in BIFR would of no legal consequence - Aggrieved, six SLPs were filed and leave to appeal granted in all SLPs - Hence, instant appeals - Whether suit filed by SSTC before Civil Court was maintainable.

Held, BIFR having correctly assumed jurisdiction and when all financial affairs of such company were directly under the supervisory control of the BIFR, the power to decide whether it has since then lost the jurisdiction or not, is also in exclusive domain of BIFR. The BIFR alone is empowered to determine whether net worth has become positive as a result of which it would cease to have such jurisdiction. Any inquiry into such issue regarding net worth by anyone outside the Act, including Civil Court, would be against the express intent of the Act. Further, the suit as framed seeking declaration that the Company was no longer a sick company within the meaning of the Act, was therefore not competent and maintainable and Civil Court was not right and justified in issuing injunction. Further, BIFR was considering Draft Rehabilitation Scheme which is a stage u/s. 18(3) and is completely covered by period u/s. 22 of the Act. The suit in instant case as framed for recovery of money filed without the consent of the BIFR was not competent and maintainable and could lie or proceed only after express consent of BIFR. Hence, appeals are allowed and order dt.6-1-2014 passed by HC in First Appeal and writ petitions is set aside. Further, suit pending before Civil Court in so far as it seeks declaration that company was no longer sick company within the meaning of the Act and that BIFR ceases to have jurisdiction and proceedings before it after filing positive balance-sheet were without jurisdiction is not maintainable and consequentially, order of injunction passed is set aside. However, suit as it pertains to claim for recovery of money from Company, could lie and proceeded with only after receipt of express consent of BIFR. Further, since SSTC in suit had not disclosed fact that he sought consent of BIFR u/s. 22(1) of the Act or that BIFR was considering whether net worth of company had turned positive, costs of Rs.5 lakh is imposed on SSTC. Appeals disposed of.

Ratio - Recovery of money is covered by s. 22(1) of the Act which refers to entire period beginning from inquiry u/s.16 till implementation of sanctioned scheme for revival. During said period, the Act grants protection to company and leave it to the discretion of BIFR whether to permit filing and maintaining of suit or other proceedings.
