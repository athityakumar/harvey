Siemens Aktiengeselischaft and S. Limited v DMRC Limited and others

Supreme Court of India

14 February 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, C. Nagappan

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 89; (2014) 11 SCC 288; AIR 2014 SC 1483; 2014 (2) AWC 2049; 2014 (1) CLT(SC) 275B; JT 2014 (3) SC 290; 2014(2) SCALE 315; [2014] 2 S.C.R. 824

Case Digest

Subject: Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Letter Of Acceptance, Award of contract, Parameters of evaluation

Summary: Contract-Award of contract-Respondent-Delhi Metro Rail Corporation (D.M.R.C.) planned to implement Phase-III of Mass Rapid Transit System (M.R.T.S.) for Delhi to keep pace with ever increasing traffic demands in Delhi-Award of contract for supply of 486 Standard Gauge Cars Electrical Multiple Units meant for use in Phase-III of M.R.T.S. for Delhi and its extension corridors-High Court taken view that process of evaluation of bids received from eligible bidders culminating in award of contract in favour of respondent No. 2 was transparent and did not suffer from any illegality, irregularity or perversity of any kind to warrant interference by it-High Court held that bidders were well aware of and had accepted tender conditions which were free from any vagueness or uncertainty-Parameters of evaluation conditions were held to have been applied uniformly to all bidders under a procedure that was open, transparent and fair as required by law-No good reason to scuttle entire process at this stage when respondent No. 2, successful bidder, has already taken substantial steps in direction of executing works allotted to it-Division Bench of High Court justified in declining to interfere with award of contract-Appeal dismissed with costs of R 5 lakhs.
