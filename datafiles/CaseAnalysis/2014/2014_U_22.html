Union of India and others v Col. G. S. Grewal

Supreme Court of India

28 May 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 390; (2014) 7 SCC 303; AIR 2014 SC 3494; JT 2014 (8) SC 70; 2014(7) SCALE 498

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces; Service

Keywords: Armed Forces Tribunal Act, 2007

Summary: Service - Defence & Security Forces - Original application - Tribunal - Jurisdiction - Maintainability - Appeal was filed against the judgment and order passed by Tribunal, whereby Tribunal partly allowed the Original Application (OA) which was preferred by respondent - Appellants questioned the jurisdiction of Tribunal to deal with subject matter - Hence instant appeal - Whether Tribunal had jurisdiction to entertain the OA -

Held, Tribunal partly allowed the OA of respondent primarily on the ground that the decision contained in the Govt. order dt. 23-4-2010 amended the promotion policy retrospectively thereby taking away the rights already accrued to respondent in terms of the earlier policy - It was also mentioned that the revised policy fundamentally changes the applicant's prospects of promotion - What was ignored was that the promotions already granted to respondent have not been taken away - Insofar as future chances of promotions were concerned, no vested right accrues as chance of promotion is not a condition of service - Therefore, in the first instance, Tribunal would have to spell out as to what was the vested right which had already accrued to the respondent and that was taken away by the Policy decision dt. 23-4-2010 - Once respondent was permanently seconded in DGQA and he was allowed to remain there, could there be a change in his service conditions vis-a-vis others who were his counterparts in DGQA, but whose permanent Secondment was not in cloud - To put it otherwise, the sole reason for issuing Govt. Policy dt. 23-4-2010 was to take care of those cases where permanent Secondment to DGQA was wrongly given - As per the appellants, since respondent suffered Final Supersession, he was not entitled to be Seconded permanently to DGQA - That was disputed by respondent -That aspect would have to be decided first - Appellants had not recall the permanent Secondment order - They have allowed respondent to stay in DGQA maintaining his promotion as Colonel as well, which was given pursuant to this Secondment - Question, in such circumstances, that would arise, whether respondent could be treated differently even if he was allowed to remain in DGQA, viz. whether not allowing him to take further promotions, which benefit was still available to others whose permanent Secondment was not in dispute, would amount to discrimination or arbitrariness thereby offending arts. 14 and 16 of Constitution - Impugned order passed by Tribunal was set aside - Matter was remitted to the Tribunal for deciding the OA by a larger Bench - Appeal allowed.
