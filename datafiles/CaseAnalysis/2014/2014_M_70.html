Md. Jamiluddin Nasir and another v State of West Bengal

Supreme Court of India

21 May 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 367; (2014) 7 SCC 443; AIR 2014 SC 2587; 2014 CRLJ 3589; 2014 (3) MLJ(Crl) 176; 2014 (4) RLW 3329; 2014(7) SCALE 571

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Murder, Material Evidence, Offences, Confession of accused, Attack on American centre, Criminal Consipracy, In accordance of proviso, Used by prosecution, Extend to, Corroborated, Proved

Summary: (A) Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 121, 121A, 122, 120B, 302, 333, 427 and 21 - Indian Arms Act, 1959, ss. 25(1B)(a), 27(3) - Attack on American centre - Criminal Consipracy - Murder - Death sentence - Challenged - Appellants and other co-accused allegedly attacked American Centre and killed several police officials and civilians - Trial Court convicted appellants (A1 and A8) and other accused (A2, A3, A6, A7, A8 and A9) u/ss. 121, 121A, 122, 120B, 302, 333, 427 and 21 and u/ss. ss. 25(1B)(a), 27(3) of the Act - Trial Court imposed death sentence for offence u/s. 27(3) of the Act and u/s. 121 IPC to appellants, however, acquitted A4 and A5 of all charges framed against them - On appeal, HC acquitted A2 and A3 of all the charges and given lesser sentence to A6, A7 and A9 - HC confirmed the death reference of appellants - Hence instant appeals by A1 and A8 and appeals by State against the part of judgment of HC which modified conviction and sentence of A6, A7 and A9 - Whether charge u/s. 27 (3) of the Act should be maintained in light of decision of Court in State Of Punjab v Dalbir Singh 2012 Indlaw SC 80 -

Held, SC held that s. 27(3) of the Act had been struck down on ground that it was ultra vires of the Constitution and declared as void, convictions and sentence imposed on accused u/s. 27(3) of the Act should not survive and said question was therefore, answered in affirmative - Appeals disposed of.

(B) Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Confession of accused - Material evidence - Whether apart from confession of A1 what were material legal evidence oral and documentary as well as in form of material objects to support case of prosecution -

Held, recovery of large quantities of arms and ammunition from premises, revolver recovered from A1, contents of e-mail messages and letter of A8 to the wife of deceased after the killing of deceased - Various e-mail messages transacted between A1 with A8 and other conspirators which took place revealed loaded mind set with which each one of them were operating for executing plans which were originally developed by deceased and A8 which were ultimately implemented with much more vehemence and vengeance by resorting to shooting spree towards police force who were posted at American Centre - In fact, messages between 14-1-2002 and 21-1-2002 exchanged between A1, A8 and other accused disclosed mind set with which gang members hatched conspiracy to operate and to carry out their mission of attacking police force posted at American Centre - Thus, overwhelming documentary evidence which was placed before Courts below it would have to be held that conclusion reached by Courts below based on materials coupled with oral evidence sufficiently established that accused along with other accused conspired for waging war against State - Appeals disposed of.

(C ) Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure , 1973, ss. 164, 313 - Confession of accused - In accordance of proviso - Whether confession of A1 was recorded in accordance with s. 164 CrPC -

Held, it was sought to be demonstrated that in s. 313 CrPC questioning, A1 denied to have made any confession in presence of PW97 and, therefore, that would amount to retraction - In fact, while referring to those questions and answers made by A1, Court have recorded finding and that same did not in any way affect genuineness of confession made by A1 - Further, manner in which confessional statement was made starting from days when A1 developed his friendship with deceased in school days, their subsequent reunion in the year 1999 and ultimate execution of horrendous attack on police force at American Centre in which as many as 5 police personnel died and 13 number of police personnel suffered gun shot injuries along with civilians, which narration could not have been prepared by prosecution agency with so many details pertaining to men and material and sequence with which it was disclosed - Narration made in confession by A1 was natural and cogent and, therefore, that also established that confession of A1 was not only recorded in accordance with s. 164 CrPC, however deserved to be given due consideration while appreciating evidence relied upon by prosecution - Hence, Court held that confession of A1 was recorded in accordance with s. 164 CrPC - Appeals disposed of.

(D) Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Contents of confession - Used by prosecution - Extend to - Whether contents of confession could be used by prosecution and up to what extend and Whether there was any contradiction in evidence both oral as well as documentary vis-a-vis confession made by A1 -

Held, confession of A1 was recorded in accordance with law and since it contained facts and figures disclosing reflection of what really transpired in relation to transaction indulged by accused namely, crimes in which they were involved as gang by conspiring together with evil mindset guided by foreign nationals, who were all Pakistanis along with some terrorist organisation and Kashmiri militant and other person - Court found that there was no contradiction to any of statement of A1 in his confession however had fully corroborated those facts and events in form of oral and documentary evidence - Therefore, prosecution was fully justified in relying upon confession while resting its case on other evidences - Appeals disposed of.

(E) Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Confession of accused - relied on - Whether confession of A1 should be relied upon as against A8 -

Held, e-mail message sent by A8 about the death of deceased in encounter confirmed their joint operation during lifetime of deceased as well as subsequently - Subsequent to A8's letter to deceased wife, serious development took place - Even before that flat arranged by co-accused was proved by evidence of its owner - Rental receipts signed by co-accused on some occasions and by deceased on some other occasions proved tenancy secured in respect of said premises - Parking of Maruti 800 and Maruti Zen in which other accused who were lodged in the flat was also spoken to by landlord of said building - Meeting of other accused and A1 in premises was supported by e-mail messages exchanged between A1 and other accused on one side and A8 on the other - Contents of those messages disclosed exact manner in which accused planned to execute their mission of taking revenge on police - Specific message of A8 directing that other accused should launch attack on Bhavani Bhawan further reinforced case of prosecution about full involvement of A8 in the transaction - Reply message sent to A8 wherein objection raised by co accused as to why attack should not be made on Bhavani Bhawan and further reply from A8 through e-mail that in that event they should look for American Base and pursuant to which co-accused visited both American Embassy as well as American Centre and thereafter, sent further message to A8 that American centre would be more fitting place to carry out their mission and green signal given by A8 again by way of e-mail were all factors which independently established full fledged role played by A* in whole transaction which ultimately resulted in hazardous attack on police force posted at American Centre - When such overwhelming evidence independent of confession of A1 was on record Court was convinced that confession of A1 should be fully applied and thereby, involvement of A8 in criminal conspiracy and following insurrection on police force at American Centre stood fully established and accordingly confession of A1 could be relied on as against A8 - Appeals disposed of.

(F) Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Confession of accused - Corroborated - Whether there was sufficient corroboration of contents of confession of A1 with other evidence both oral and documentary relied upon by prosecution -

Held, PW62 was concerned and that there was no description made while identifying photograph of deceased accused - It was true, in said decision, while rejecting TIP in respect of one of accused Court expressed serious doubts about identification of accused by Witnesses in TIP which was held three weeks after arrest of accused and that there was no explanation - It should be stated that each case depended upon peculiar facts of that case - In case on hand, Court noted that to magnitude of offence committed, namely, attack on police force at American Centre, where indiscriminate shooting was carried out by assailants in which 5 policemen lost their life and several others got seriously injured - Further fact remained that before executing attack rehearsal was held by A1 along with assailants on previous days - Therefore, witness who identified accused namely, PW62 had advantage of looking at accused with some amount of anxiety as A1 created ruckus while parking car on two occasions prior to date of occurrence - In such circumstances, it was difficult to apply said decision to facts of instant case 0 That apart, PW39 who identified A1 and A8 had known both of them earlier and, therefore, identification of accused should not be controverter at all - Thus various witnesses facts which were all crucial to case of prosecution as found in confession of A1 noted were all corroborated fully by other evidence, with oral and documentary as well as material objects and, therefore, question was answered in affirmative - Appeals disposed of.

(G) Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Offences - Proved - Whether offence charged against accused for which accused were proceeded against were conclusively proved -

Held, there was no hesitation to held that accused were guilty of charges levelled against them as found proved except charge u/s. 27(3) of the Act which Court had held that should not be maintained in view taken from State Of Punjab v Dalbir Singh 2012 Indlaw SC 80 - Therefore, Court held that accused were guilty of all other charges as found proved against them by Courts below - Appeals disposed of.

(H) Criminal - Practice & Procedure - DEath sentence - Justifiability - Whether sentence of death should be held to be appropriate punishment, if not, what was punishment to be imposed -

Court found that from year 1999 after release of deceased from Tihar Jail where he developed close contact with A8 he was definitely aware of nature of activities indulged in by deceased along with A8 and others - Even on his own self, it was revealed that he was aware that in 1991 deceased went to Kashmir and joined Jehadi movement - Therefore, while involvement of appellants in respect of offences for which they were charged and found proved were devilish, diabolic, horrendous, vicious, wicked and its magnitude should not be lessoned on any account , Court opined, role of A1 was always shade lesser than what should be attributed to A8 - Therefore, even while holding that charges found proved against appellant were of grave in nature, when it came to question of sentence, imposition of death penalty was not warranted in facts and circumstances of case though it called for other deterrent punishment - Hence, punishment of death for offence u/ss. 121, 121A, 122 r/w s. 120B IPC was modified as one of life and Court held that A1 should undergo RI for minimum period of 30 years without any remission - In regard to A8 punishment of death should stand modified and he should undergo imprisonment of life till end of his life - Appeals disposed of.
