Ramesh v State Through Inspector of Police

Supreme Court of India

01 August 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 495; (2014) 9 SCC 392; AIR 2014 SC 2852; JT 2014 (8) SC 492; 2014 (3) MLJ(Crl) 671; 2014 (4) RCR(Criminal) 122; 2014 (4) RLW 3449; 2014(9) SCALE 85; [2014] 7 S.C.R. 956

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Murder, Conviction

Summary: A)Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 376, 302, 201 - Murder - Rape - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant accused allegedly committed rape on deceased(minor) and murdered her - Accused was charged for offence punishable u/ss. 376. 302, 201 IPC - Trial Court convicted and sentenced accused - HC upheld the judgment and order of conviction and sentence of Trial Court - Hence instant appeal - Whether absence of name of accused in FIR points towards innocence of accused and entitles him for acquittal -

Held, it was mentioned by HC in impugned judgment that FIR initially did not mention name of accused and on other hand, PW1 father of deceased child suspected one of his relatives for offence - It was however, revealed after investigation that it was accused who committed the act and police in fact was proceeding in right path - Involvement of accused had been further corroborated by recovery of shawl of deceased on basis of confession of accused which was made in presence of witnesses - SC intend to concur with decision of HC that non mentioning of name in initial FIR was not fatal to case of prosecution as held in case of (1) Jitender Kumar (2) Sunil Kumar And Another v State Of Haryana 2012 Indlaw SC 579 - Therefore, contention of accused that since his name did not appear in FIR, he was entitled to acquittal was not maintainable - Appeal dismissed.

(B)Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 376, 302, 201 - Murder - Rape - Conviction - Sustainability - Whether instant case was fit case to apply the last seen theory to establish guilt of accused -

Held, it was case of prosecution that PW3, grandmother of accused had sent child to see whether floor was grinded - However, when child did not return for some time, PW3 went home - At this juncture, there was evidence through PW5 and PW12 who were employees under accused that accused took child to backyard while he unusually permitted PW12 to go for lunch - Further, accused could not explain need of taking an 8 year old child to backyard - In this aspect of last seen theory, it had been held by SC in Kusuma Ankama Rao v State of Andhra Pradesh 2008 Indlaw SC 1126 - In instant case , deceased child was taken to backyard of mill by accused and same was seen by PW5 and PW12 - Deceased child went missing since then and was found dead next morning - Accused did not explain why did he took the child to backyard - On other hand, he confessed to his crime which was corroborated by recovery of shawl at instance of accused himself in presence of witnesses - Therefore, in light of principle laid down by SC, HC was justified in holding accused guilty of rape and murder of deceased child - Appeal dismissed.

(C)Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 376, 302, 201 - Murder - Rape - Conviction - Sustainability - Whether circumstantial evidence indicated towards guilt of accused and whether these evidences were sufficient to establish guilt of accused -

Held, accused opened the mill in odd hours witnessed by PW6 and PW7 - When PW8 came out for attending call of nature and heard noise from well which was situated behind the mill and on seeing accused proceeding towards mill, he stopped accused and asked as to what he was doing - Dead body was found next day from well, circumstantial evidence pointed the involvement of accused in throwing dead body of child in the well, previous night - HC was justified in construing that accused had kept dead body in the mill and threw dead body in the well - There was no direct evidence which proved that rape and murder of deceased child was committed by accused - There were no witnesses available on record who testified having witnessed accused committing the crime - However, all circumstances point towards accused as being author of the crime as it was held in Govinda Reddy & Anr v State Of Mysore 1958 Indlaw SC 214 - In instant case recovery of body of deceased child from same well where PW8 had seen accused previous night throwing something in well provided for strong circumstantial evidence - Unusual behaviour of accused in taking deceased child to backyard of mill, sending of his employee for lunch at same time and also opening mill in odd hours of night very same evening points towards guilt of accused - Hence, HC was correct in upholding decision of Sessions Judge in convicting accused of rape and murder of deceased child - Appeal dismissed.
