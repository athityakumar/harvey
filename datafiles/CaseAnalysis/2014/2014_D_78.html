Duryodhan Rout v State of Orissa

Supreme Court of India

07 January 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 943; 2014 (4) JCC 2347

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Life Imprisonment, Rape, Indian Evidence Act, 1872, Transportation, CONSTITUTION, General Clauses Act, 1897, Marks, Dead Body, Imprisonment For Life, Appropriate Government, Proviso, Prisoner, Remission, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 201, 302, 376, 376(f), 45, 53, 55, 57, 60 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 31, 31(2), 401, 432, 433, 433A, 55 - Rape and murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant/accused allegedly raped victim/deceased and committed murder of deceased - Charge sheet was filed against accused u/ss. 376/302/201 IPC - Trial Court found accused guilty for offence u/ss. 376(f)/302/201 IPC and sentenced him to death for the offence punishable u/s. 302 IPC and further sentenced accused to undergo RI for 10 years - On appeal, HC upheld the conviction of appellant for the offence u/ss. 376, 302 and 201 IPC however, converted capital sentenced to life imprisonment - Hence, instant appeals - Whether the judgment passed by Trial Court as affirmed by HC was contrary to the proviso to s. 31(2) CrPC.

Held, a sentence of imprisonment for life means a sentence for entire life of the prisoner unless appropriate Govt. chooses to exercise its discretion to remit either the whole or a part of sentence under the provisions of CrPC. S. 31 CrPC relates to sentence in cases of conviction of several offences at one trial. S. 31(2) CrPC lays down the embargo whether aggregate punishment of prisoner was for a period of longer than 14 years. In view of the fact that life imprisonment means imprisonment for full and complete span of life, the question of consecutive sentences in case of conviction for several offences at one trial does not arise. Therefore, in case a person was sentenced of conviction of several offences, including one that of life imprisonment, the proviso to s. 31(2) CrPC should come into play and no consecutive sentence could be imposed. Trial Court was not justified in imposing the sentence u/ss. 376(f)/302/201 IPC to run consecutively and HC failed to address the said issue. SC was not inclined to interfere with the order of conviction and sentence considering the fact that accused was awarded life imprisonment for the offence u/s. 302 IPC, SC direct that all the sentences imposed under IPC were to run concurrently. Judgment passed by Trial Court as affirmed by HC stands modified to the extent above. Appeals partly allowed.
