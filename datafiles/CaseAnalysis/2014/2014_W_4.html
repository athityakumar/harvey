World Sport Group (Mauritius) Limited v MSM Satellite (Singapore) Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

24 January 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Patnaik, Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 46; (2014) 11 SCC 639; AIR 2014 SC 968; 2014 (5) Bom.C.R. 693; 2014 (1) CLT(SC) 257; JT 2014 (2) SC 444; 2014 (3) RCR(Civil) 485; 2014(1) SCALE 596; [2014] 1 S.C.R. 796

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; International

Keywords: Interference by HC, Matter referred to arbitration

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - International - Indian Contract Act, 1872, ss. 23 and 28 - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996. s. 45 - Matter referred to arbitration - Interference by HC - Sustainability - Respondent MSM Satellite (Singapore) Private Limited filed suit for declaration that Facilitation Deed was void and for recovery of Rs.125 crores already paid to appellant World Sport Group (Mauritius) Limited - Appellant acting under Cl. 9 of Facilitation Deed requested for arbitration to ICC Singapore and ICC issued notice to respondent to file its answer to request for arbitration - In the meantime, respondent filed second suit for declaration that as Facilitation Deed stood rescinded - Respondent filed application for temporary injunction against appellant from continuing with arbitration proceedings commenced by appellant under aegis of ICC - Single Judge dismissed application for temporary injunction of respondent - Respondent challenged the order of Single Judge before DB in appeal - Appeal was allowed by DB - Hence instant appeal - Whether the order passed by DB was sustainable - Held, DB held that Facilitation Deed was part of several agreements entered into amongst different parties, therefore, could not be considered as stand apart agreement between appellant and respondent and considered Facilitation Deed as contrary to public policy of India because it was linked with finances, funds and rights of BCCI, which was public body - Approach of DB was not in consonance with provisions of s. 45 of the Act, which mandated that in case of arbitration agreements covered by New York Convention, Court which was seized of matter would refer parties to arbitration unless arbitration agreement was null and void, inoperative or incapable of being performed - In view of the provisions of S. 45 of the Act, DB was required to only consider in this case whether Cl. 9 of Facilitation Deed which contained arbitration agreement was null and void, inoperative or incapable of being performed - Finding of DB was that Cl. 9 of the Facilitation Deed was opposed to public policy and was void u/ss. 23 and 28 of the 1872 Act was clearly erroneous - Reasoning adopted by DB in impugned judgment was alien to provisions of s. 45 of the Act which did not empower Court to decline reference to arbitration on ground that another suit on same issue was pending in Indian court - SC did not express any opinion on dispute between appellant and respondent as to whether Facilitation Deed was voidable or not on account of fraud and misrepresentation - Cl. 9 of Facilitation Deed stated inter alia that all actions or proceedings arising in connection with, touching upon or relating to Facilitation Deed, breach thereof and/or scope of provisions of section should be submitted to ICC for final and binding arbitration under its Rules of arbitration - Arbitration agreement in Cl. 9 was wide enough to bring this dispute within scope of arbitration - Hence, it had been rightly held by Single Judge that it was for arbitrator to decide instant dispute in accordance with arbitration agreement - Impugned judgment of DB was set aside and Singled Judges order was restored - Appeal allowed.
