Deepak Bhandari v Himachal Pradesh State Industrial Development Corporation Limited

Supreme Court of India

29 January 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 54; (2015) 5 SCC 518; AIR 2014 SC 961; 2014 (4) ALD(SC) 18; 2014 (103) ALR 217; 2014 (3) AWC 2442; 2014 (1) CLT(SC) 145; 2014 (5) CTC 791; 2014 (2) DRTC 305; 2014 (1) DRTC 761; JT 2014 (2) SC 435; 2014 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1109; 2014 (2) MLJ 241; 2014 (123) RD 8; 2014(1) SCALE 673; [2014] 2 S.C.R. 138

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance

Keywords: State Development Corporation Act

Summary: Limitation Act, 1963-Article 55-State Financial Corporation Act, 1951-Section 29-Suit for recovery by State Financial Corporation-Period of limitation-Appellant Director of Company, was guarantor for payment of loans taken by company-Company defaulted on repayment of loan amount disbursed to it by State Financial Corporation-State Financial Corporation issued recall notice dated 21.5.1990-Company failed to make repayment and accordingly State Financial Corporation proceeded under Section 29 of State Financial Corporation Act to take over mortgaged/ hypothecated assets of company-Mortgaged/hypothecated assets of company sold by State Financial Corporation on 31.3.1994-Question involved-Whether limitation for filing suit would start on 21.5.1990, when notice of recall issued or starting point would be 31.3.1994, when assets of company were sold?-Held, period of limitation is to be counted from date when assets of company were sold and not when recall notice was given-Impugned judgment of High Court-Upheld.
