State of Madhya Pradesh and others v Parvez Khan

Supreme Court of India

01 December 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Adarsh Kumar Goel, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 814; (2015) 2 SCC 591; AIR 2015 SC 602; 2015 (1) AWC 735; 2015 (1) CGLJ 216; 2015 (1) JabLJ 79; JT 2014 (14) SC 107; 2015 (5) MahLJ 26; 2015(1) M.P.H.T. 1; 2015 (3) MPLJ 485; 2014(13) SCALE 409; 2015 (1) SLR 489; 2015 (3) WLN(SC) 137

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Involvement in criminal case - Compassionate appointment - Entitlement - Respondent applied for compassionate appointment in State Police service - Appellant department rejected his candidature on the ground of his involvement in two criminal cases - Respondent challenged appellant's order before HC contending that he was acquitted from cases - Single Judge dismissed respondent's writ petition - However, DB, on appeal, directed appellant to consider respondent's candidature - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, as per Guidelines dt. 5-6-2003, acquittal is not conclusive. Even after acquittal, basis of order of the Court has to be gone into by the competent authority. Even after order based on compromise or lack of evidence may render a candidate ineligible. Superintendent of Police has gone into the matter. The Superintendent of Police is the appointing authority. There is no allegation of mala fides against the person taking the said decision nor the decision is shown to be perverse or irrational. There is no material to show that the respondent was falsely implicated. Basis of impugned judgment is acquittal for want of evidence or discharge based on compounding. Hence, DB was not justified in interfering with the order rejecting the claim of the respondent for recruitment to the police service by way of giving him compassionate appointment. Impugned DB order was set aside. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - A person having criminal antecedents will not fit to be recruited to the police service. Though he is acquitted or discharged, it cannot be presumed that he was completely exonerated.
