Zorawar Singh and another v Gurbax Singh Bains and others

Supreme Court of India

04 December 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Uday Umesh Lalit, A.R. Dave

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 828; (2015) 2 SCC 572; 2015 ALL MR (Cri) 2830; JT 2014 (13) SC 614; 2015 (1) RCR(Criminal) 259; 2014(13) SCALE 560

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 173 and 482 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Investigation - Challenged - A complaint given by the father of the deceased that his son had not died in that accident but was murdered in a pre-planned manner and prayed for the investigation by the independent agency - State Govt. entrusted the matter to DIG (Crime) to conduct independent investigation and submit his report - DIG (Crime) had concluded his enquiry and had recommended addition of offence u/s. 302 of IPC - A Special Investigation Team (SIT) was constituted by the DGP who did not agree with the findings in the aforesaid report - SIT submitted its report that whatever happened was road accident and that the allegations levelled by respondent were absolutely baseless - Immediately after the aforesaid report, another SIT was constituted - When matter came up before the HC for final hearing and it was of the view that the SIT was constituted to nullify the enquiry and report of DIG (Crime) - It observed that it was mandatory duty of the State to implement the report submitted by DIG (Crime) and it therefore directed the State to act in terms of said report within 3 months - Appellants filed petition against said order submitting that the order of the HC was based on the report of the DIG, which report itself had recommended further investigation and thus the SIT was rightly constituted and that once final report u/s. 173 of CrPC was filed, the court could only have directed further investigation in terms of s. 173 (8) of CrPC and could not have passed the instant directions.

Held, HC was completely justified in leaving out the reports of the SIT from its consideration and directing the State Govt. to act in terms of the report of DIG. Constituting the SIT on the premise that the DGP did not agree with the report of the DIG or that the DIG himself had recommended further investigation, was completely incorrect. Once the matter was before HC, nothing could and ought to have been undertaken without its express leave. The State therefore had rightly disbanded such SIT. It is inexplicable how the SIT could go ahead and submit the report, which then led to constitution of fresh SIT. Impugned order of the HC was affirmed . Appeal dismissed.
