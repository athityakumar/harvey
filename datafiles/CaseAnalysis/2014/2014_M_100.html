Motilal Yadav v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

25 November 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Prafulla C. Pant, Vikramajit Sen

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 798; (2015) 2 SCC 647; JT 2014 (14) SC 466; 2014 (4) MLJ(Crl) 741; 2015 (1) RCR(Criminal) 115

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Conviction, Kidnapping for ransom

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120B, 364A r/w s. 34 - India Evidence Act, 1872, s. 9 - Kidnapping for ransom - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant (accused) allegedly kidnapped PW5, grandson of PW6 (informant) and thereby committed offence u/ss. 120B, 364A r/w s. 34 of IPC - Trial Court convicted appellant u/s. 364A r/w s. 34 of IPC and u/s. 120B r/w s. 364A of IPC and sentenced him to undergo imprisonment for life - On appeal HC by its impugned judgment upheld the conviction of appellant - Hence, instant appeal - Appellant contended that HC decided the appeals without scrutinizing the entire evidence on record.

Held, statement of each of the witnesses, PW1 (Rickshaw Puller), PW2 (mother of the victim), PW3 (father of the victim), PW4 (sister of the victim), PW5 (victim) and PW6 (informant and grandfather of the victim), has been discussed at length by the HC, apart from discussing the evidence of formal witnesses, namely, PW7 (constable), PW9 and the IO PW8 and PW10. The HC has also taken note of the statement of DW1. The HC, after discussing the evidence of each of the witnesses, has further discussed the evidence on record as to how from the corroboration of the statements of the witnesses, the entire prosecution story and the charge stood proved. PW3 has given all the details as to how money was taken to the place where his son was released and handed over to him from H-B Road. HC, in the impugned order, has discussed at length the prosecution evidence which was believed by it. Court find no force in the argument that the HC's order is cryptic or brief. In instant case the testimony of PW3 is natural as he explained in what manner he reached 'H', and he had enough time to identify the accused who accompanied him to the persons who took money from him whereafter the victim was released. Not holding of test identification parade in the instant case is not fatal for the prosecution. Appeal dismissed.
