State Bank of Patiala and another v Ram Niwas Bansal (Dead) Through LRs.

Supreme Court of India

03 March 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, H.L. Gokhale

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 138; (2014) 12 SCC 106; AIR 2014 SC 1264; 2014(3) ALL MR 480; 2014 (141) FLR 271; JT 2014 (3) SC 545; 2014 (3) LLJ 191; [2014] 3 S.C.R. 984; 2014 (3) SLR 285

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: State Bank of India Officers Service Rules, 1992, Madhya Pradesh Municipal Service (Executive) Rules, 1973, UCO Bank Officer Employees' Service Regulations, 1979, State Bank of Patiala (Officers') Service Regulations, 1979

Summary: Service - State Bank of Patiala (Officers') Service Regulations, 1979, Regn. 68(1)(ii) - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s.17B - Madhya Pradesh State Municipal Service (Executive) Rules, 1973, r.29 - Payment of back wages - Validity - Respondent was charged with certain financial irregularities - Disciplinary authority concurred with the findings recorded by the Enquiry Officer and recommended for removal of the delinquent officer from the Bank's service to the appointing authority in accord with the terms of Regn. 68(1)(ii) of Regulations and the appointing authority, i.e., Managing Director, agreeing with the findings recorded by the Enquiry Officer and the recommendations of the disciplinary authority, imposed the penalty of removal - Being grieved by the orders, respondent preferred petition before HC for quashment of all the orders and for issue of appropriate direction to reinstate him in service with full service benefits - HC ruled that non-supply of comments of the General Manager had caused serious prejudice to the delinquent officer and there was denial of fair and reasonable opportunity and on that basis set aside the order of punishment - However, it directed the disciplinary authority to grant an opportunity to the petitioner therein to reply to the enquiry report and pass appropriate orders after granting personal hearing to the petitioner therein in accordance with law - Dissatisfied with the judgment and order, the appellant-Bank preferred Special Leave Petition - HC directed stay of reinstatement of the respondent therein with the direction that the Bank would comply with the provisions of s.17B of Act - Bank then filed Interlocutory Application for modification of the order on the ground that Section 17B of the Act was not applicable - It was dismissed by HC - Hence instant appeal - Held, disciplinary proceeding was initiated against the delinquent officer while he was in service - First order of dismissal was passed on 23-4-1985 - Said order of punishment was set aside by the HC and the officer concerned was directed to be reinstated for the limited purpose, i.e., supply of enquiry report and to proceed in the disciplinary proceeding from that stage - Said order was not interfered with by HC - Appellant continued the proceeding - Needless to emphasise, the said continuance was in pursuance of the order of the Court - Under these circumstances, it had to be accepted that the concept of deemed continuance in service of the officer would have full play and, therefore, an order of removal could have been passed after finalization of the departmental proceeding on 22-11-2001 - HC had already held that the said order would not have been made retrospectively operative, but that would not invalidate the order of dismissal but it would only have prospective effect - R. Jeevaratnam v State of Madras 1965 Indlaw SC 438, relied on - Having said that, it became necessary to determine the date of retirement and thereafter delve into how the period from the date of first removal and date of retirement would be treated - HC might hasten to add that for the purpose of deemed continuance the delinquent officer would not be entitled to get any benefit for the simple reason, i.e., the continuance was only for finalisation of the disciplinary proceedings, as directed by HC - Hence, the effect and impact of Regn. 19(1) of Regulations would come into full play - On a seemly construction of the first proviso HC was of the considered view that it required an affirmative act by the competent authority, for it was an exercise of power of discretion and further the said discretion had to be exercised where the grant of extension was deemed desirable in the interest of appellant - As the facts would reveal, in the year 1992 the concerned officer stood removed from service and at that juncture to expect the Bank in law to intimate him about his date of superannuation or to pass an order would be an incorrect assumption - Conclusion which appeared logical and acceptable was that unless an extension was granted by a positive or an affirmative act by the competent authority, an officer of appellant/Bank retired on attaining age of 58 years or upon the completion of 30 years of service, whichever occurred first - C.L. Verma v. State of Madhya Pradesh and another, 1989 Indlaw SC 274, relied on - In the said case the effect of r.29 of Rules fell for interpretation - In the said Rule it was provided that a member of the service should attain the age of superannuation on the date he completes his 58 years of age - Proviso to the said Rule stipulated that the State Govt. might allow a member of the service to continue in employment in the interest of Municipal Council or in public interest and, however, no member of service should continue in service after he attains the age of 60 years - Appellant therein had attained the age of 58 years two days prior to the order of dismissal - Court opined that the tenor of the proviso clearly indicated that it was intended to cover specific cases and individual employees - Another issue pertained to how the period from the date of order of first removal, i.e., 23-4-1985 till 25-2-1992 would be treated and to what benefits the officer concerned would be entitled to - Order of removal from service, would come into effect from the date of passing of the order, i.e., 22-11-2001 as it had to be prospectively operative and, therefore, as a natural corollary respondent remained in service from 23-4-1985 till he attained the age of superannuation, i.e., 25-2-1992 or till the end of February, 1992, being the last day of the month. - Thus, HC had to restrict the period for grant of benefit till the date of retirement - It was worthy to note that during the continuance of the disciplinary proceeding the delinquent officer was not put under suspension - After the order of punishment passed by the disciplinary authority and affirmed by the appellate authority was quashed by the HC on 22-5-1998, the concerned officer had to be treated to be in service from his date of first removal till his date of retirement - Had the appellant/Bank brought to the notice of HC about the legal position under the Regulations, in all probability, the matter would have been dealt with differently - Grant of salary in entirety for the period as determined by HC to be the period of continuance in service would not be apposite and similarly, the submission advanced on behalf of appellant/Bank that payment of rupees five lacs would meet the ends of justice would not deserve acceptance - Ordinarily, HC would have directed respondent/Bank to pay fifty per cent of the back wages for the period commencing 23-4-1985 till the end of February, 1992, with some interest but HC did not want that the legal heirs of the delinquent officer should further go through any kind of tribulation in computation and face further legal hassle as regards the quantum - HC was of the opinion that the controversy should be given a quietus and, therefore, instead of fixing fifty per cent of the back wages HC directed that appellant/Bank should deposit a further sum of rupees five lacs with the Registrar General of the HC within two months hence and the respondents would be entitled to withdraw the same - HC further clarified that if the amount earlier deposited was not withdrawn by the original respondent, the same should also be withdrawn by the legal heirs - Order accordingly.
