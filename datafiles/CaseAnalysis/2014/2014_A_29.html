Ashish Kumar Mazumdar v Aishi Ram Batra Charitable Hospital Trust and others

Supreme Court of India

22 April 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjan Gogoi, P. Sathasivam, N.V. Ramana

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 276; (2014) 9 SCC 256; 2014 ACJ 1393; AIR 2014 SC 2061; 2014 (4) ALD(SC) 86; 2014 (104) ALR 717; 2014 (3) AWC 2978; 2014 (3) Bom.C.R. 621; 2014 (2) CPJ(Sc) 5; 2014 (4) CTC 566; 2014 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 3115; 2015 (1) MahLJ 544; 2015 (1) MPLJ 280; 2014 (175) PLR 532; 2014 (2) RCR(Civil) 862; 2014 (124) RD 160; 2014 (4) RLW 3186; 2014(5) SCALE 340; [2014] 5 S.C.R. 519; 2014 (4) WLN(SC) 15

Case Digest

Subject: Health & Drug; Tort

Summary: (1) Suit-On account of damages for injuries suffered by plaintiff while undergoing treatment in hospital-Plaintiff suffered multiple fracture of lumbar vertebrae with complete dislocation of spinal cord and despite treatment he became paraplegic, i.e., 100% disabled below waist-Allegation of negligence and absence of due care on part of hospital authorities-Defendants liable for negligence and failure to take due care of plaintiff, who was indoor patient in hospital-Duty and responsibility of hospital authorities to take care of plaintiff who was suffering from high fever and was in delirious state-Evidence on record attracted principle of res ipsa loquitur-Single Judge of High Court awarded sum of Rs. 7 lakhs with interest @ 12% p.a.-Division Bench of High Court enhanced amount of damages from Rs. 7 lakhs to Rs. 11 lakhs with interest @ 12% p.a.-Amount of damages awarded neither inadequate nor inappropriate so as to justify interference-Impugned judgment and order upheld.

(2) Res ipsa loquitur-Applicability-Maxim, res ipsa loquitur applies to case in which certain facts proved by plaintiff, by itself, would call for explanation from defendant without plaintiff having to allege and prove any specific act or omission of defendant.

(3) Compensation-Determination-Precise quantum of compensation that should be awarded in any given case cannot and, in fact, need not be determined with mathematical exactitude or arithmetical precision-So long compensation awarded broadly represents what could be entitlement of claimant in any given case discretion vested in trial court and regular first appellate court ought not to be lightly interfered.
