State of Jharkhand and others v Kamal Prasad and others

Supreme Court of India

23 April 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, Gyan Sudha Misra

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 287; (2014) 7 SCC 223; JT 2014 (6) SC 243; 2015 (1) MahLJ 16; 2015(1) M.P.H.T. 419; 2015 (1) MPLJ 34; 2014(5) SCALE 558; 2014 (4) SLR 609

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Termination, Reinstatement

Summary: Service - Termination - Reinstatement - Maintainability - Respondent-employees were appointed in posts of Junior Engineers with appellant employer - Respondents were subsequently appointed on ad-hoc temporary basis as Assistant Engineers - Further respondents were dismissed from their services - Respondents filed petitions before Single Judge - Petitions were allowed after quashing show cause notices issued and orders of termination of services of respondent - Appeals before HC - DB held that respondents should be entitled to all consequential benefits - Hence instant appeal - Whether the order of HC was maintainable -

Held, evidence on record produced by respondent would clearly showed that they have been rendering services in posts as ad-hoc Engineers since 1987 and have been discharging their services as permanent employees with appellants - However, respondents continued in their services as ad hoc employees without any disciplinary proceedings against them which prove that they have been discharging services to their employers to their satisfaction - Appellants failed to prove that respondents have failed to render continuous services to appellants at least for 10 years without intervention of orders of Court - Findings of facts recorded by DB was based on record, hence same should not be termed as erroneous in law - DB rightly held that respondent were entitled for relief, same should not be interfered with by SC - It should not be said that findings and reasons recorded by HC should be termed either as erroneous or error in law - DB in regard to respondent service, had continuously worked in their posts for more than 29 years discharging permanent nature of duties and they have been paid their salaries and other service benefits out of budget allocation, no objection was raised by CAG - Action of appellants in terminating services of respondent who have rendered continuous service in their posts during pendency of appeals was quashed by HC after it had felt that action was not only arbitrary but shocked its conscious and therefore it had rightly exercised its discretionary power and granted reliefs to respondent which did not call for our interference - Therefore, Court would not interfere with opinion of HC and on contrary, decision of HC was upheld both on factual and legal aspects as same was legally correct and it had done justice to respondent - Hence, HC was correct in reinstating respondent into their services under appellants and appellants were directed to implement orders of DB of HC thereby continuing respondents in their services and extend all benefits as have been granted by it in impugned judgment - Secretary, State of Karnataka and Others v Umadevi and Others 2006 Indlaw SC 125 - Appeals dismissed.
