Vinay Kumar Shailendra v Delhi High Court Legal Services Committee and another

Supreme Court of India

04 September 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, C. Nagappan, V. Gopala Gowda

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 591; (2014) 10 SCC 708; 2015 (1) ALD(SC) 9; 2014 ALL MR (Cri) 4477; 2014 (4) BC 225; 2014 (5) Bom.C.R. 714; 2015 CRLJ 166; 2014 (4) JCC (NI) 197; JT 2014 (10) SC 519; 2015 (2) MahLJ 528; 2014 (8) MLJ 103; 2015 (2) MPLJ 116; 2014 (2) OLR 987; 2014 (4) RCR(Civil) 731; 2014 (4) RCR(Criminal) 641; 2014(10) SCALE 292; [2014] 9 S.C.R. 510

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s.482 - Negotiable Instrument Act, 1881, s.138 - Constitution of India, 1950, art.226 - Return of complaints - Legality - Appeals arose out of a judgment passed by DB of HC whereby the HC had invoked its jurisdiction u/art. 226 of the Constitution of India r/w. s. 482 of CrPC and directed return of all complaints filed u/s. 138 of the Act in which the Metropolitan Magistrates in Delhi have taken cognizance only because the statutory notices in terms of proviso to s. 138 of the Act have been issued to the drawers of the cheque from Delhi - Whether complaints u/s. 138 of Act could be maintained at a place other than the place where the drawee bank was situate -

Held, appeals arise out of a judgment dt. 23-9-2009 passed by a DB of HC whereby the HC has invoked its jurisdiction u/art. 226 of the Constitution of India r/w. s. 482 of CrPC and directed return of all complaints filed u/s. 138 of the Act in which the Metropolitan Magistrates in Delhi have taken cognizance only because the statutory notices in terms of proviso to s. 138 of the Act have been issued to the drawers of the cheque from Delhi. Notice from Delhi or deposit of the cheque in a Delhi bank by the payee or receipt of the notice by the accused demanding payment in Delhi would not confer jurisdiction upon the Courts in Delhi. What is important is whether the drawee bank who dishonoured the cheque is situate within the jurisdiction of the Court taking cognizance. Appeals dismissed.
