Soma Isolux NH One Tollway Private Limited v Harish Kumar Puri and others

Supreme Court of India

17 April 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Gyan Sudha Misra, Pinaki Chandra Ghose

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 262; (2014) 6 SCC 75; AIR 2014 SC 2495; 2014 (2) CLT(SC) 171; JT 2014 (5) SC 451; 2014(5) SCALE 252

Case Digest

Subject: Contract & Commercial

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Termination of contract - Imposition of fine - Legality - Appeal by special leave was filed assailing the order passed by HC whereby certain adverse directions were issued having grave implication on the contractual rights of the appellant/ Concessionaire Company as it was saddled with a fine of Rs.60 crores and Rs.7 crores to be paid by the appellant and its Director respectively - Respondent No.6/National Highways Authority of India/NHAI was further directed to proceed in the matter forthwith and take possession of the Highway project and ensure that collection of toll was deposited in a separate account and the work of repairs of the highway commenced within a week and the work of construction of highway commenced and completed within a month thereafter - HC further issued direction that the entire matter relating to the contract, the completion of the work of the highway, collection from tolls without existence of six- lanes be enquired into and a report in that regard be placed before the HC within three months - It was also made clear that the enquiry should not be construed to be an excuse to delay the construction of the highway - It was further observed that the Chairman, NHAI should be personally responsible for ensuring that the work of six-laning of the highway between Panipat and Jullunder was completed within six months failing which the Chairman, NHAI would be held personally liable to pay fine similar to the terms imposed on the Concessionaire Company - Hence instant appeal - Whether the directions issued by the HC which have far reaching consequences against the petitioner/appellant and which directions by a judicial fiat, had the effect of nullifying the terms of the Concession Agreement defeating the rights and obligations arising therefrom in a Public Interest Litigation while exercising jurisdiction u/art. 226 of the Constitution of India was an act of judicial overreach under the garb of public interest -

Held, analysis of the background of the matter and the sequence of events arising out of a Public Interest Litigation which led to the execution of a Concession Agreement creating contractual relationship between the appellant and the respondent No. 6, it was manifest that the HC had issued over zealous directions which had resulted into termination of the concession agreement itself when it directed the respondent No.6 to take over the project from the appellant and ensure the balance construction of the highway project by itself - Although, the HC had observed that it was not entering into the arena of the contractual terms and conditions of the agreement, it had clearly nullified and terminated the contract itself when it directed respondent No. 6 to take over the project from the appellant-company overlooking the bonafide of the appellant-company which had already completed major portion of the construction which was 71 per cent of the total area of 291 KMs and only 29 per cent was to be constructed further, when a dispute arose between the appellant-company and the respondent No.6 in regard to shifting of the Toll Plaza from KM 146 to KM 110 and KM 212 to KM 211 and the NHAI for the first time in 2013 reneged from its consent to permit shifting contrary to the terms of the agreement - However, in course of oral argument before SC, respondent No. 6 had yielded and did not seriously dispute the shifting from KM 212 to KM 211 and rightly so as the shifting was hardly at a distance of 1KM from KM 212 to KM 211 at Shambhu Toll Plaza which was negligible and the shifting had already taken place as discussed hereinbefore. But, the respondent No.5 NHAI has raised serious objections in regard to shifting of the Toll Plaza from KMs 146 to KM 110 which appears to have been done for the first time in the year 2013 when the new incumbent took over as Chairman of respondent No. 6 ignoring the fact that the Independent Engineer in terms of the agreement had granted approval to the same way back on 30-6-2010 - In addition, the dispute regarding shifting of Toll Plaza had already been settled by the HC when it permitted the appellant to shift the Toll Plaza to KMs 110 against which NHAI did not file any appeal challenging the order of the HC - Thus, in spite of the fact that the dispute regarding shifting of toll plaza had attained finality by virtue of a series of judicial verdict, the newly appointed Chairman, respondent No. 6 for the first time in 2013 restrained the appellant-company from shifting the Toll Plaza to KMs 110 and it clearly resulted into putting the entire project to a standstill since the project was to be financed by way of Build, Operate, Trade mode (BOT mode) and affected financing of the project and the matter got enmeshed into a series of litigations related hereinbefore - But HC refused to go into the dispute stating that it did not want to go into the terms and conditions of the contract and directed for undertaking from the appellant attributing the entire delay to the appellant-company for non performance and finally terminated the contract when it directed the respondent No.6 to take back the project from the appellant - Assuming the HC did it in public interest to expedite the construction of the highway, it was more than obvious that this direction of the HC could not possibly and practically expedite the construction as admittedly respondent No. 6 itself did not undertake any construction work of the highway which clearly implied that it will have to issue a fresh tender for construction of the balance area of the project which was bound to result into greater delay of the project apart from the fact that the dispute between the appellant-company and respondent No. 6 would still survive and finally a 3rd party being a new player was bound to get entangled giving rise to further legal complications in the whole process - In addition to this it was difficult to overlook that the HC was merely seized with the limited issue in the pending PIL as to whether the Show Cause Notice issued by respondent No. 6 to the appellant- company which was ordered to be kept in abeyance by order of the HC itself, was fit to be vacated on an application filed by respondent No. 6, but while doing so the HC took a leap much way ahead by cancelling the agreement itself for non performance ignoring the main issue as to whether the Show Cause Notice issued by respondent No. 6 was fit to be sustained or the order keeping it in abeyance was fit to be vacated and straightway concluded that the appellant-company had delayed the project completely overlooking that the contractual violation had been done by respondent No. 6 when it declined to permit shifting of the Toll Plaza from KMs 146 to KM 110 in spite of the reasoned orders of the HC to that effect against which no appeal was filed either by the NHAI or the PIL petitioner or any other concerned party - It was no doubt true that in a public interest litigation the court at times might forego/overlook the technicalities coming in the way of issuance of any direction which may conflict or jeopardise the public interest - But the same could not be allowed to reach to the extent or affect the contractual agreement itself which reduced a valid and a legal document into a worthless piece of paper or a waste paper which clearly meant that the relationship between the parties although were to be governed and supported by a valid legal document, the same would finally turn out to be a document having no legal significance in spite of its validity in the eye of law. If this were to be permitted, it was bound to lead to a chaotic situation affecting the very fabric of the rule of law which could not be allowed to prevail over a valid and legally supported document conferring certain rights on the person or entity possessing it - As a consequence and fall out of the above position, SC deemed it just and appropriate to set aside the impugned directions of the HC, and permitted the appellant to restore the construction of the balance stretch/area of the highway project by further directing respondent No. 6 to permit the appellant to shift the Toll Plaza from KM 146 to any point between KMs 110 and KMs 117 with concurrence of respondent No. 6 - Appeal allowed.
