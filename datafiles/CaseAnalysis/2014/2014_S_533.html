Sumer Singh v Surajbhan Singh and others

Supreme Court of India

05 May 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 335; (2014) 7 SCC 323; AIR 2014 SC 2840; 2014 CRLJ 3246; 2014 (3) JCC 2282; JT 2014 (8) SC 251; 2014 (2) RCR(Criminal) 741; 2014 (2) RLW 1494; 2014(6) SCALE 187; [2014] 5 S.C.R. 882

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Attempt to murder, Reduction of sentence

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 147, 148, 149, 307, 323, 326 and 447 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 377(3) - Attempt to murder - Conviction - Reduction of sentence - Sustainability - Respondent (accuse) allegedly committed offence u/ss. 147, 148, 149, 307, 323, 326 and 447 of IPC - Trial Court by its judgment convicted accused u/ss. 307, 447, 326, 323 r/w s. 149 of IPC for 5 years of RI - Aggrieved accused filed an appeal before HC - Single Judge by impugned judgment converted the conviction of accused from one u/s. 307 of IPC to one u/s. 308 of IPC and sustaining the conviction u/ss. 148, 147, 326 and 323 of IPC r/w s. 149 of IPC and restricted the period of sentence to 7 days which the accused had already undergone and to impose a fine of Rs.50,000/-, in default of payment of fine, to suffer additional RI of 2 years - Aggrieved appellant (injured) filed instant appeal - Whether Impugned judgment passed by Single Judge was justified -

Held, it was manifest from evidence on record that victims were not armed with weapons and peacefully carrying on their agricultural activities when accused persons came armed with weapon and attacked them - Injury reports of appellant (PW4) and others contained in Ext. P17 to 19 clearly show that they had received injuries and injuries inflicted on appellant were grievous in nature - On a scrutiny of injury report, it appeared that the injuries were absolutely simple in nature - It could not be said that defence had been able to establish the plea of right of exercise of private defense, the question of exceeding the said right doesn't arise - Thus, irresistible conclusion was that accused persons had assaulted the injured persons and HC had correctly recorded the conviction against respondent u/s. 326 of IPC - Manner in which the crime was committed speaks eloquently about its brutality - Gravity of the offence speaks for itself - A young man's hand was cut off from the wrist - How the fear psychosis would have reigned in the society at the relevant time does not require Solomon's wisdom to visualize - It was difficult to fathom what possible reason the HC could have envisioned or thought of while reducing the sentence to the period already undergone, i.e., 7 days for such an offence - Possibly, HC felt that increase of fine amount would serve the cause of justice and ameliorate the grievance of the victim and pacify the collective cry - Paramount principle that should be the guiding laser beam was that the punishment should be proportionate - It was the answer of law to the social conscience - In a way, it was an obligation to the society which had reposed faith in the Court of law to curtail the evil - While imposing the sentence it was the Court's accountability to remind itself about its role and the reverence for rule of law - It should evince the rationalized judicial discretion and not an individual perception or a moral propensity - But, if in the ultimate eventuate the proper sentence was not awarded, the fundamental grammar of sentencing was guillotined - Law could not tolerate it; society did not withstand it; and sanctity of conscience abhors it - In instant case, victim still cries for justice - Increase in fine amount or grant of compensation under CrPC would be a justified answer in law - Money could not be the oasis - It could not assume the centre stage for all redemption - Interference in manifestly inadequate and unduly lenient sentence was the justifiable warrant, for the Court could not close its eyes to the agony and anguish of the victim and, eventually, to the cry of society - Respondent was sentenced to undergo RI of 2 years apart from the fine that was imposed by Trial Court - Appeal partly allowed.
