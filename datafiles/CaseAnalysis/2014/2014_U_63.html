Rajiv Chowdhrie HUF v Union of India and others

Supreme Court of India

10 December 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, C. Nagappan

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 841; (2015) 3 SCC 541; AIR 2015 SC 614; 2015 (109) ALR 224; 2015 (1) AWC 668; 2014 (4) CLT(SC) 430; 2014 (6) CTC 808; JT 2014 (13) SC 563; 2015 (6) MahLJ 113; 2015 (4) MPLJ 360; 2015 (1) RCR(Civil) 368; 2015 (127) RD 289; 2014(13) SCALE 710

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Administrative - Right to Fair Compensation and Transparency in Land Acquisition, Rehabilitation and Resettlement Act, 2013, s. 24 (2) - Land acquisition - Failure to take physical possession - Respondent authority issued notification u/s. 4 and declaration u/s. 6 of the Act for acquiring appellant's land - Appellant challenged acquisition before HC - HC dismissed appellant's writ petition - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, the physical possession of the land belonging to the appellant has neither been taken by the respondents nor compensation paid to him in respect of the land acquired even though more than five years have lapsed since the award was passed on 6-8-2007, when the Act came into force. Therefore, the conditions mentioned in s. 24(2) of the Act are satisfied in this case for allowing the plea of the appellant that the land acquisition proceedings are deemed to have lapsed in terms of s. 24(2) of the Act. Therefore, the acquisition proceedings in respect of the appellant's land have lapsed. Hence, the acquisition proceeding notification in so far as the land of the appellant is concerned, is quashed. Appeal allowed.
