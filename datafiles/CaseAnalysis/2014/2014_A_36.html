State of Uttar Pradesh Through Exe. Engineer and another v Amar Nath Yadav

Supreme Court of India

10 January 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 21; (2014) 2 SCC 422; 2014 (3) ALD(SC) 152; 2014(2) ALL MR 942; 2014 (302) E.L.T. 26; 2014 (140) FLR 730; JT 2014 (1) SC 494; 2014 (3) MPLJ 476; 2014 (1) RCR(Civil) 861; 2014 (1) RCR(Criminal) 716; 2014(1) SCALE 304; 2014 (5) SLR 2; 2017 (48) S.T.R. 120

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Delay of 481 days - Time barred - Maintainability - There was a delay of 481 days in filing Special Leave Petition - Hence instant application for condonation - Petitioner contended that the delay to the moving of file from one Department/ Officer to the other - Whether delay of 481 days could be condoned - Held, in Postmaster General and Ors. v. Living Media India Ltd., 2012 Indlaw SC 55, relied on - There was no reason to take a different view - SC thus, dismissed instant SLP on the ground of delay - Petition dismissed.
