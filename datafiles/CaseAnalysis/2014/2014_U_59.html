Occupational Health and Safety Association v Union of India and others

Supreme Court of India

31 January 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan, A.K. Sikri

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 63; (2014) 3 SCC 547; AIR 2014 SC 1469; 2014 (2) CLT(SC) 123; JT 2014 (2) SC 427; 2014(1) SCALE 734; [2014] 4 S.C.R. 10

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Trusts & Associations

Keywords: Indian Boilers Act, 1923

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 32 - Thermal power plants - Safety and health regulations - Occupational health disorders - Writ or directions - Maintainability - Petitioner filed instant application u/art. 32 of Constitution - Petitioner represented 130 Coal Fired Thermal Power Plants (CFTPPs) spread over different States in the country, but no proper occupational health services with adequate facilities for health delivery system or guidelines with respect to occupational safety were in place - Many Acts were in place, but the lack of proper health delivery system, evaluation of occupational health status of workers, their safety and protection cause serious occupational health hazards - Petitioner filed an application for reducing the occupational hazards of employees working in various thermal power stations in the country and 9 suggestions were put forward - Suggestions 1 to 7 were accepted by the Central Govt. and for suggestions 8 and 9 a committee of experts was constituted by (National Institute of Occupational Health) NIOH - Report was submitted - Petitioner contended that the Union of India as also the Committee misunderstood the scope of the suggestion nos. 8 and 9 and further contended that not much importance was given to the serious health problems being faced by the workers who were working in the thermal power plants and the treatment they require as well as the payment of wages and compensation to those workers who were suffering from serious illness - Held, recommendations made were to be welcomed, but how far they were put into practice and what preventive actions were taken to protect the workers from the serious health-hazards associated with the work in CFTPPs calls for serious attention - Many workers employed in various CFTPPs were reported to be suffering from serious diseases referred to earlier - What were the steps taken by CFTPPs and the Union of India and the statutory authorities to protect them from serious health hazards and also the medical treatment extended to them, including compensation etc. calls for detailed examination - CFTPPs were spread over various States in the country and it would not be practicable for SC to examine whether CFTPPs were complying with safety standards and the rules and regulations relating to the health of the employees working in various CFTPPs throughout the country - SC felt that these aspects could be better examined by the respective HC's in whose jurisdiction these power plants were situated - HC should examine whether there was adequate and effective health delivery system in place and whether there was any evaluation of occupational health status of the workers - HC should also examine whether any effective medical treatment was meted out to them - SC therefore, felt that it was appropriate to relegate it to the various HC's to examine the issues with the assistance of the State Govt's. after calling for necessary Reports from the CFTPPs situated in their respective States - SC made it clear that Report was not at all comprehensive in certain aspects and the respective HC's could examine the issues projected in instant Judgment independently after calling for the reports about the CFTPPs functioning in their respective States - Petition disposed of.
