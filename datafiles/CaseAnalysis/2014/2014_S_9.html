R. Rajanna v S. R. Venkataswamy and others

Supreme Court of India

20 November 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, R. Banumathi

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 784; AIR 2015 SC 706; 2015 (1) ALT(SC) 47; 2015 (1) AWC 766; 2014 (4) CLT(SC) 412; JT 2014 (13) SC 71; 2014 (4) KLT 780; 2015 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 56; 2015 (1) OLR 305; 2015 (1) RCR(Civil) 96; 2015 (2) RLW 1438; 2014(13) SCALE 19; 2014 (4) WLN(SC) 167

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 23 rr. 3, 3A - Challenge to compromise decree - Separate suit - Maintainability - Appellant filed suit for declaration and permanent injunction - Trial Court decreed suit - Respondent filed appeal before HC - Respondent filed compromise petition under O. 23 r. 3 CPC - HC set aside Trial Court order in terms of compromise - Appellant disputed that no such compromise was entered into by parties - HC directed appellants to file separate suit - Appellant filed separated suit before City Civil Court - City Civil Court rejected plaint - Appellant filed application before HC to set aside compromise decree - HC rejected application - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, HC fell in a palpable error in directing the plaintiff to take recourse to the remedy by way of separate suit. The HC in the process remained oblivious of the provisions of O. 23 rr. 3 and 3A CPC as also orders passed by the City Civil Court rejecting the plaint in which the Trial Court had not only placed reliance upon O. 23 r. 3A but also the decision of the Court in Pushpa Devi Bhagat (D) The. Lr. Sadhna Rai v Rajinder Singh and Others, 2006 Indlaw SC 315, holding that a separate suit was not maintainable and that the only remedy available to the aggrieved party was to approach Court which had passed the compromise decree. Hence, impugned order of HC was set aside and matter was remitted back to HC. Appeals allowed.

Ratio - The Court cannot direct the parties to file a separate suit challenging the decree passed in terms of compromise for no such suit will lie in view of the provisions of O. 23 r. 3A of CPC.
