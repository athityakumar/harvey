Savita v Bindar Singh and others

Supreme Court of India

25 March 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Pinaki Chandra Ghose, Gyan Sudha Misra

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 180; (2014) 4 SCC 505; 2014 (2) ACC 244; 2014 ACJ 1261; 2014 (104) ALR 730; 2014 (2) CLT(SC) 131; 2014 (3) CTC 427; JT 2014 (8) SC 605; 2014 (5) MahLJ 95; 2014 (3) MPLJ 534; 2014 (174) PLR 724; 2014 (2) RCR(Civil) 473; 2014 (2) RLW 1221; 2014(4) SCALE 128; [2014] 3 S.C.R. 810

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Keywords: Entitlement of

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, s. 166 - Motor accident - Compensation - Enhancement of compensation - Entitlement of - Appellants (claimants) filed a claim petition, seeking compensation of Rs.20,20,000/- along with interest at the rate of 12% p.a from respondents (opposite parties) on account of death of deceased in a motor accident - Tribunal by an award granted compensation of Rs.4,28,000/- to appellants - Aggrieved appellants filed an appeal before HC, which was dismissed confirming the award passed by Tribunal - Hence, instant appeal - Appellants contended that salary certificate produced before Tribunal was not accepted by Tribunal without any reason and that compensation granted by Tribunal and affirmed by HC did not include the future prospects which should have been added to claim and that deduction of personal expenses could not be made more than 1/10th of the total salary received by deceased -

Held, at the time of fixing compensation, Court should not succumb to niceties or technicalities to grant just compensation in favour of claimant - It was the duty of Court to equate, as far as possible, the misery on account of the accident with the compensation so that the injured or dependants should not face the vagaries of life on account of discontinuance of the income earned by the victim - Thus, it would be the bounden duty of the Tribunal to award just, equitable, fair and reasonable compensation judging the situation prevailing at that point of time with reference to the settled principles on assessment of damages - In doing so, Tribunal could also ignore the claim made by the claimant in the application for compensation with the prime object to assess the award based on principle that the award should be just, equitable, fair and reasonable compensation - In instant case, compensation under the head 'loss of consortium' to the spouse, loss of love, care and guidance to children and funeral expenses amounts should have been awarded under such heads, that was, for Rs.1,00,000/- and Rs.25,000/- respectively and Cout award such compensation under the said heads - So far as head of 'salary' was concerned, Court did not express any opinion since Court have found that appellant could not prove the salary certificate and for such reason, Court did not intend to interfere with the opinion expressed by Tribunal on the established principle of notional income and accordingly, Court did not want to disturb the said notional income while calculating the total compensation in favour of appellant - Court failed to understand why Tribunal and HC lost its sight to hold that the victim could have had future prospects with regard to the amounts the victim used to earn during his life-time - Thus, notional income was increased by at least 30% and total amount of compensation to be paid would be Rs.2,600x17x12, thus amounting to Rs.6,55,400/- plus interest at the rate of 8% p.a - Impugned award of Tribunal and order of HC was modified - Appeal partly allowed.
