Vadodara Municipal Corporation v Purshottam V. Murjani and others

Supreme Court of India

10 September 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Adarsh Kumar Goel, V. Gopala Gowda

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 603; 2014 (3) ACC 912; 2014 ACJ 2566; AIR 2015 SC 321; 2015 (1) ALD(SC) 4; 2014(6) ALL MR 928; 2014 (107) ALR 389; 2014 (5) AWC 4934; 2014 (5) Law Herald (P&H) 4033; 2014 (7) MLJ 629; 2014 (4) RCR(Civil) 370; 2015 (1) RLW 776; 2014(10) SCALE 381; [2014] 7 S.C.R. 810

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection

Keywords: Comprehensive, Inland Vessels Act, 1917, Negligence in plying boat, Tortuous liability

Summary: Consumer Protection - Consumer Protection Act, 1986 - Bombay Provincial Municipal Corporation Act, 1949, ss. 62, 63, 66 - Indian Vessels Act, 1917 - Public Liability Insurance Act, 1991 - Negligence in plying boat - Tortuous liability - Legality - Appellant (Corporation) for plying boats for joy rides and boating club granted licence agreement under a contract to a Contractor - Contractor took insurance policy and against the capacity of 20 persons, 38 passengers were allowed to ride in the boat which capsized resulting in the death of 22 passengers - Victims approached State Commission under 1986 Act claiming compensation and that under 1949 Act, appellant had the tortuous liability - State Commission held that appellant was vicariously responsible for the negligence of Contractor - Aggrieved appellant and Insurer filed an appeals before National Commissioner, which was dismissed by impugned judgment - Appellant contended that it was not a service provider and had no privy contract with victims -

Held, it is not in dispute that the boat was carrying 38 passengers as against the capacity of 22 passengers. Neither any life guards were deployed nor any lifesaving jackets were provided to the passengers. The finding of negligence concurrently recorded by the State Commission and National Commission does not call for any interference. Primary liability of the contractor stands established. The victims were consumers and the contractor was service provider. Deficiency of service stood established. The stand of the Insurance based on second policy dt.1-12-1992 limiting its liability is untenable. Having issued policy dt.1-11-1992 covering loss to the extent of Rs.20 lakhs per accident with Rs.80 lakhs as maximum in 1 year, the Insurance Company could not avoid its responsibility, as rightly held concurrently by State Commission and National Commission. Risk was required to be statutorily covered under 1991 Act. Activity in question was covered by the statutory duty of the Corporation u/ss. 62, 63, 66 of 1949 Act. Mere appointment of a contractor or employee did not absolve the Corporation of its liability to supervise the boating activities particularly when there are express stipulations in the contract entered into with the contractor. The Corporation was not only discharging its statutory duties but also was acting as service provider to the passengers through its agent. The Corporation had a duty of care, when activity of plying boat is inherently dangerous and there is clear foreseeability of such occurrence unless precautions are taken like providing lifesaving jackets. Appeals dismissed.

Ratio - Not only Constitutional Courts have to, in suitable cases, uphold claims arising out of loss of life or liberty on account of violation of statutory duties of public authorities, in private law remedies, just and fair claims of citizens against public bodies have to be upheld and compensation awarded in Tort
