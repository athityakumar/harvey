B. Prabhakar Rao And Ors. Etc. v State Of Andhra Pradesh And Ors. Etc.Etc.

Supreme Court of India

19 August 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy, V. Balakrishna Eradi, V. Khalid

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 249; (1985) SCC (L&S) 49; (1985) Supp SCC 432; (1986) SCC (L&S) 49; AIR 1986 SC 210; 1985 (2) CCC 555; 1985 (51) FLR 501; 1985 LabIC 1555; 1985(2) SCALE 256; 1985 (3) SLR 138; [1985] Supp2 S.C.R. 573

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Liberalised Pension Scheme, Andhra Pradesh Public Employment (Regulation of Age of Superannuation) Act, 1984, Andhra Pradesh Public Employment Act 1984

Summary: Administrative - Constitution - Practice & Procedure - Service - Andhra Pradesh Public Employment (Regulation of Age of Superannuation) Act, 1984 - Andhra Pradesh Public Employment Act, 1984 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Whether the classification of employees, who attained the age of 55 years between 28-02-1983 and 23-08-1984 as a separate group for the purpose of excluding them from the benefit of the redressal of the wrong done and the relief given to them by the amending Ordinance and the Act, was bad as an unreasonable classification having no nexus with the object of the legislation? - Art. 14 forbids class legislation, but permits reasonable classification - However two conditions must be fulfilled, (i) classification must he founded on an intelligible differentia which distinguishes persons or things that are grouped together from those that are left out of the group (ii) that differentia must have a rational relation to the object of the statute in question - Art. 14 eschews arbitrariness in any form - Held, if all employees hit by the reduction of the age of superannuation formed a class and no sooner than the age of superannuation was reduced, it was realised that injustice had been done and it was decided that steps should be taken to undo what had been done, there was no reason to pick out a class of persons who deserved same treatment and exclude from the benefits of the beneficent treatment by classifying them as a separate group merely because of the delay in taking the remedial action already decided upon - It is not open to the State to make an arbitrary classification by making the date dependent on an uncertain event - Whenever a law is made or whenever an action is taken, it has to take effect from a certain date but choice of the date is open to scrutiny to find out if it has resulted in discrimination - Legislations to remedy wrongs ought not to exclude from their purview, wronged persons unless circumstances make the redressal of the wrong either impossible or so detrimental to the public interest that the mischief of the remedy outweighs the mischief sought to be remedied - No such impossibility or detriment to the public interest is involved in reinducting into service those who had retired as a consequence of the legislation which was inequitable and sought to be remedied - Writ Petitions Nos. 3420-26 of 1985 allowed - Writ Petitions Nos. 5447-5546 of 1985 dismissed.
