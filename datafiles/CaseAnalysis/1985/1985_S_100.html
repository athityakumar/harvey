Man Singh And Ors. v State Of Punjab And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

23 August 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Pathak, V.D. Tulzapurkar, S. Mukharji

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 252; (1985) 4 SCC 146; AIR 1985 SC 1737; 1986 (1) CCC 309; 1985(2) SCALE 367; [1985] Supp2 S.C.R. 662; 1986 (1) UJ 335

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Punjab Cycle Rickshaw (Regulation Of Licence) Act, 1976

Summary: Constitution - Constitution, arts.14, 16, 19(1)(g) - Punjab Cycle Rickshaws (Regulation of Licence) Act, 1976, Pre., ss. 3 and 5 - Constitutionality of the Act - Scheme propounded in previous case under consideration - Act held, is not unconstitutional - Scheme not unworkable - Further held that given appropriate compliance the scheme will provide adequate relief to the rickshaw pullers and constitute an effective supplementary code fulfilling the object of the Punjab Act - Petition dismissed.
