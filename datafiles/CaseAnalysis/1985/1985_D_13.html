Dhanjibhai Ramjibhai v State of Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

22 January 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 2; (1985) 2 SCC 5; (1985) SCC (L&S) 379; AIR 1985 SC 603; 1985 (22) E.L.T. 640; 1985 (51) FLR 77; 1985 LabIC 744; 1985 (2) LLJ 521; 1985 (1) LLN 770; 1985(1) SCALE 65; [1985] 2 S.C.R. 632; 1985 (1) SLR 595

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 136, 309 and 311 - (A) Scope for interference in special leave appeal with concurrent finding of fact - (B) When no rules have been framed indicating manner for extending period of probation, if probation can be extende - (C) Whether completion of probation period, prescribed training and passing requisite tests confirm any right - Held, Supreme Court could be reluctant to interfere unless there is very strong reason - In absence of such rules, it is sufficient that power is exercised fairly and reasonably - No right to be confirmed - Appeal dismissed.
