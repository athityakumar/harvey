H. Anraj and another v Government of Tamilnadu and another

Supreme Court of India

04 October 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

V.D. Tulzapurkar, S. Mukharji

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 380; (1986) SCC (Tax) 190; (1986) 1 SCC 414; AIR 1986 SC 63; 1985(2) SCALE 641; 1986 (61) STC 165; [1985] supp3 S.C.R. 342; 1986 TAXLR 2217

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Keywords: Sale Price, Madras City Tenants Protection Act, 1921

Summary: Constitution - Tamil Nadu General Sales Tax Act, 1959 - Bengal Finance (Sales Tax) Act, 1941 - Levy of sales tax on sale of lottery tickets - (A) Goods - Lottery tickets are goods and not actionable claims - Levy of tax - Legislative Competency - (B) Levy by State Govt. only on sale of lottery tickets of other State - Exemption granted to its own lottery tickets - Constitutionality - Held, is within legislative competence of concerned State Legislature under entry 54 of List II in Seventh sch - Discriminatory and violative of art. 301 r/w art. 304 (a) of Constitution - Appeal dismissed.
