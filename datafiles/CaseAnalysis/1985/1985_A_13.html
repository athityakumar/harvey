Union of India and Others v Ahmedabad Manufacturing and Calico Printing Co. Limited

Supreme Court of India

12 August 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

E.S. Venkataramiah, R.B. Misra

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 394; (1985) 3 SCC 693; AIR 1986 SC 121; 1985 (2) CCC 734; 1985 CRLR 434; 1985 CrLR(SC) 434; 1985 ECR 1529; 1985 (21) E.L.T. 633; 1985(2) SCALE 233; [1985] Supp2 S.C.R. 537

Case Digest

Subject: Excise

Summary: Excise - Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944 - Constitution of India,1950 - Show cause notice - Consideration - Liability - Contrary - Respondent was manufacturer of processed fabrics was levied of excise duty - Superintendent of Central Excise proposed to treat respondent goods as falling under Item No. 19 - Respondent repudiated claim made by Superintendent - Superintendent issued Show Cause Notice to respondent - Respondent replied that product consisted of less than 40 per cent, of cotton and of more than 60 per cent of artificial Silk, and liable to be taxed only under Item No. 22 - Appellant/Central Excise declared that goods were liable to payment of excise duty under Item No. 19 and not under Item No. 22 - Aggrieved respondent filed W.P u/art. 226 Constitution was allowed - Hence instant appeal - Whether respondent goods liable to be taxed under item No.19 of 1944 Act - Held, process involved by respondent could be taken into consideration for determining liability under 1944 Act - Manufactured product for purpose of excise duty should depend upon its nature and character as its final stage of production unless contrary intention appears from statute - Product had under Item No. 19 in First Schedule to Act and at final stage duty became eligible and became taxable under Item No.22 only - Hence, goods fell under Item No.22 and not under Item No. 19 in First Schedule 1944 Act for purposes of payment of excise duty - Appeal dismissed.
