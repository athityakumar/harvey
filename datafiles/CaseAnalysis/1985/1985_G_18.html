Sri Chand Etc v Government Of U.P. Lucknow And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

23 August 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

E.S. Venkataramiah, R.B. Misra

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 254; (1985) 4 SCC 169; 1985 (2) ACC 571; AIR 1986 SC 242; 1985 (11) ALR 638; 1985(2) SCALE 351; [1985] Supp2 S.C.R. 688

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Civil Procedure; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Special Relief Act, 1963

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Constitution - Trade - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14 and 19(1)(g) - Motor Vehicles Act 1939, s. 68-C - Validity of the proceedings pending before the State Govt. pursuant to a draft scheme published u/s. 68-C of the Act, proposing to authorise the State Transport Undertaking of Uttar Pradesh to operate stage carriages on the inter state route between Saharanpur and Delhi to the total exclusion of all other operators is questioned - HC directed the Govt. not to enforce the scheme against the persons who had filed the writ petitions - Petitioners who had filed the writ petitions were able to operate their stage carriages on the route those who had not filed the writ petitions could not operate resulting in discrimination - Held, unreasonable delay in the approval of the scheme has resulted in the violation of arts. 14 and 19(1)(g) - Petitions allowed.
