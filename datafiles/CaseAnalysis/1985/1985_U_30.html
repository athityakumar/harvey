Abdul Sattar v Union Territory, Chandigarh

Supreme Court of India

24 September 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranganath Misra, Amarendra Nath Sen

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 67; (1985) SCC (Cr) 505; (1985) Supp SCC 599; AIR 1986 SC 1438; 1986 CRLJ 1072; 1985 CRLR 485; 1985(2) SCALE 1409

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 27 and 133 - (A) Credibility of uncorroborated testimony of co-accused in absence of conspiracy - (B) Admissibility of statement of accused leading to discovery - Recovery of wearing apparel of deceased after 3 weeks from date of incident from public place - Held, association of co-accused with murder is not believable - And statement of accused leading to discovery of articles was not admissible - Appeal allowed.
