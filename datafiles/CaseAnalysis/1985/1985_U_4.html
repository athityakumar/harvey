 Lohia Machines Ltd. & Anr. v Union Of India & Ors

Supreme Court of India

25 January 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Bhagwati, Amarendra Nath Sen, M.P. Thakkar, Y.V. Chandrachud, D.P. Madon

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 307; (1985) 2 SCC 197; (1985) SCC (Tax) 245; AIR 1985 SC 421; 1985 (1) CompLJ 249; 1985 (44) CTR 328; [1985] 152 ITR 308; 1985(1) SCALE 115; [1985] 2 S.C.R. 686; 1985 TAXLR 353; [1985] 20 TAXMAN 9

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Constitution; Corporate; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax and Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss.80J(1), 80J(1A) and 296 - Income Tax Rules, 1962, r.19A - Validity - Retrospective effect and vires - Question of law relating to interpretation of s. 80J of 1961 Act, and on the basis of certain interpretation, petitioners challenge the validity of r.19A of the 1962 Rules, and also call in question the constitutionality of the retrospective amendment made in s. 80J, by the Finance Act, 1980 - As per provision, while computation of relief to new undertaking, borrowed capital excluded and capital take as on first day of previous year - Both held to be valid; r.19A is neither ultra vires rule making power nor suffers from vice of excessive delegation and s.80J(1A) not invalid on ground that it is retrospective - Petitions dismissed.
