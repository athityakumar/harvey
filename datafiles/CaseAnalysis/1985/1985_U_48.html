H.V. Pardasani, Etc v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 March 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranganath Misra, P.N. Bhagwati, Amarendra Nath Sen

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 433; (1985) 2 SCC 468; (1985) SCC (L&S) 482; AIR 1985 SC 781; 1985 (51) FLR 101; 1985 LabIC 654; 1985 (1) LLN 964; 1985(1) SCALE 556; [1985] 3 S.C.R. 286; 1985 (1) SLJ 395; 1985 (2) SLR 43; 1985 UJ 402

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950 - Direct recruits - Seniority - Section officers - Statutory rules - Petitioners in these applications u/art. 32 of the Constitution belonged to the cadre of Section Officers in the Central Secretariat Service - They challenged the combined seniority list of all the Section Officers which belonged to the Service and had asked for a direction that the select list in Grade I of the Service be recast - They also asked for further direction that certain direct recruits included in the eligibility list of Section Officers should be omitted from it and a direction should issue from the Court to appoint promotees to Grade I with effect from the date on which junior directly recruited Section Officers have been appointed to Grade I - According to the petitioners, the seniority between the direct recruits and promotees in the grade of Section Officers had to be fixed on the basis of length of service in the grade and not by the process envisaged under the Rules and the relevant Regulations - Respondents on the other hand had taken the stand that the provision for fixing seniority had been made by the statutory Rules and the combined seniority list as also the eligibility list are in accordance with the statutory scheme - Whether the bunch of direct recruits were placed group of promotees by operation of the quota rule and that the fixation of seniority was arbitrary - Held, in the list of eligible officers, names of many who had already retired or had been promoted to other grades had not been shown - As per the working chart, the quota rule had been implemented while drawing up the eligibility list in accordance with Regulation 5(2) (c) (i) and (ii) - It was relevant to point out that of the 11 petitioners as many as 9 had got into the cadre of Assistants as direct recruits and they had themselves got advantage over promotees who had put in a longer period of service in such cadre - Petitions dismissed.
