Dr. (Mrs.) Sushma Sharma Etc. Etc v State Of Rajasthan And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

12 March 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, E.S. Venkataramiah

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 492; (1985) Supp SCC 45; (1985) SCC (L&S) 565; AIR 1985 SC 1367; 1985 LabIC 243; 1985(1) SCALE 523; [1985] 3 S.C.R. 243; 1985 (2) SLJ 276; 1985 (2) SLR 382; 1985 UJ 744

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Education - Rajasthan University Teachers and Officers (Special Conditions of Service) Act, 1974, s.3 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14 and 16 - (A) Eligibility for absorption - Interpretation of cls. 3 of the Ordinance of 1978 - Interpretation in Yogendra Kumar Tiwari v. University of Rajasthan case [Civil Writ Petition No. 446 of 1978] could not be treated an authoritative pronouncement - Hindi version of cls.3 as well as the English version noted - (B) Relevant date for benefit u/s. 3 - Whether the choice of 25-06-1975 as the date prior to which temporary teachers must have been in employment to be eligible for screening is bad - Held, 'were continuing as such...' in the Act must mean that to be eligible for absorption these temporary lectures should have been in continuous employment from a date prior to June 25, 1975 to the date of the commencement of the Ordinance of 1978 i.e. June 12, 1978 - Held, not arbitrary - Appeal Dismissed.
