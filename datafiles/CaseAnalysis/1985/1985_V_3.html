Supreme Court of India

24 January 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, A. Varadarajan, S. Mukharji

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 443; (1985) 1 SCC 591; AIR 1985 SC 582; 1985 (1) CCC 904; 1985 (1) RCJ 267; 1985 (1) RCR(Rent) 422; 1985 (1) RentLR 414; 1985(1) SCALE 74; [1985] 2 S.C.R. 643

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Keywords: Finance (No.2) Act, 1980, Board Of Revenue Act, 1913

Summary: Rent Control - Willful Default - Tamil Nadu Buildings (Lease and Rent Control) Act, 1960, s. 10(2) - (1) What is the real purport of Willful Default? - Held, deliberate and intentional default knowing full well the legal consequences - 'Willful default' means default must be intentional, deliberate, calculated and conscious, with full knowledge of legal consequences - What is the scope of a proviso and what is the ambit of an explanation either to a proviso or to any other statutory provision? - Held, a proviso is an exception to provision within the main enactment or to qualify something enacted which would be within the purview of the enactment if not for the proviso - Proviso cannot be removed from the main enactment - Proviso cannot be used to nullify the real object of the main enactment - Effect of proviso is confined to that part of enactment - Functions of a proviso may be - (1) Qualifying or excepting certain provisions from the main enactment - (2) Entirely change the purpose of the enactment by insisting on certain mandatory conditions to be fulfilled in order to make the enactment workable - (3) Become an integral part of the substantive enactment by way of being embedded in legislation itself - (4) It may be used merely to act as an optional addenda to the enactment with the sole object of explaining the real intendment of the statutory provision - Individual appeals disposed according to abovesaid principles - Disposed accordingly.
