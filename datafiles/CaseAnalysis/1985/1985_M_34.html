Sardar Tota Singh v Messrs Gold Field Leather Works, Bombay

Supreme Court of India

15 January 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Pathak, E.S. Venkataramiah, V. Balakrishna Eradi

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 172; (1985) 1 SCC 414; AIR 1985 SC 507; 1985 (1) Bom.C.R. 488; 1985 (1) CCC 707; 1985 (1) RCJ 261; 1985 (1) RCR(Rent) 250; 1985 (1) RentLR 745; 1985(1) SCALE 22; [1985] 2 S.C.R. 563; 1985 UJ 459

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Criminal; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Bombay Rent, Hotel and lodging House Rates Control Act, 1947

Summary: Rent Control - Bombay Rent, Hotel and Lodging House Rates Control Act, 1947 - Whether appellant could rightly claim tenancy rights in premises? - Sub-tenant had created a further sub-tenancy in favor of appellant - Question is whether further sub-tenancy can fall within scope of s. 15 (2) - Held, it is apparent that in respect of subsequent sub-tenancy, sub-tenant could be described as a tenant and appellant as his sub-tenant - So there is no reason why appellant's sub-tenancy should not be regarded as a valid sub-tenancy in as much as it was created before May 21, 1959 and he had entered into possession of premises before that date and was continuing in possession on that date - Appeal allowed.
