Forward Construction Co. And Ors. Etc. Etc. v Prabhat Mandal (Regd.) Andheri And Ors. Etc. Etc.

Supreme Court of India

26 November 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

R.B. Misra, R.S. Pathak, O. Chinnappa Reddy

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 291; (1986) 1 SCC 100; AIR 1986 SC 391; 1986 (1) CCC 379; 1985(2) SCALE 1123; [1985] 3 S.C.R. 766; [1985] supp3 S.C.R. 766; 1986 (1) UJ 167

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Maharashtra Regional Town Planning Act, 1966, Bombay Municipal Act, 1888

Summary: Civil Procedure - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, s. 11 Explanation VI - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Maharashtra Regional and Town Planning Act, 1966, ss. 23 and 59 - Bombay Municipal Corporation Act, 1888, ss. 61(t), 63(k) and 87 - (A) CPC, 1908 - Writ Petition - Res judicata - (1) Petition challenging commercial use of a plot reserved for bus depot dismissed - Subsequent writ petition challenging commercial user on a ground absent in earlier petition - (2) Applicability to public interest litigation - (B) Maharashtra Regional and Town Planning Act, 1966, s. 59 - Town Planning Scheme, Andheri No 1 - (1) (Final) Public property - Proof of secretive disposal at under-value - Plot was allotted to BEST, which resolved to develop it through contractors - Advertisement inviting offers published in newspapers - Notices to well known builders - Contract entered with highest bidder - (2) (1 st variation) - Building Regulations framed under scheme, regn. 3 Proviso - Change in user of plot, after modification of development plan - Connotion of word 'change' - (C) Plot allotted to Corporation for bus depot - Resolved to construct bus depot and commercial accommodation - Vires as regards to Bombay Municipal Corporation Act - (A) Held, judgment in earlier petition would nonetheless operate as res judicata - It applies to public interest litigation - (B) Further held, disposal of BEST property was neither secretive nor for inadequate consideration - Modification of plan necessary only when user is completely changed - (C) Transaction not outside purview of Act - Petition Dismissed.
