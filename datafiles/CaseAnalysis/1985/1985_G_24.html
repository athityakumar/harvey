S. Guin and Others v Grindlays Bank Limited

Supreme Court of India

11 December 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

E.S. Venkataramiah, R.B. Misra

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 19; (1986) SCC (Cr) 64; (1986) 1 SCC 654; AIR 1986 SC 289; 1986 (1) Crimes 289; 1986 CRLJ 255; 1986 CrLR(SC) 82; 1986 (1) LLN 471; 1985(2) SCALE 1264; [1985] 3 S.C.R. 818; [1985] supp3 S.C.R. 818; 1986 (1) UJ 449

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Criminal; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Criminal - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, s. 378 - Appellants had without reasonable cause obstructed officers of Bank from lawfully entering premises of Bank - Had obstructed transaction of normal business at said branch- Allegation that they had thereby committed offence punishable u/s. 147, Indian Penal Code,1860 - Whether High Court was justified to have remanded case for fresh trial, when order of acquittal had been passed nearly six years before judgment of HC? - A fresh trial nearly seven years after alleged incident is bound to result in harassment and abuse of judicial process - Held, whatever might have been error committed by Magistrate- In circumstances of case, it was not just and proper for HC to have remanded case for fresh trial, when order of acquittal had been passed nearly six years before judgment of HC - Appeal Allowed.
