Gopal v The Administrative Officer, Madhya Pradesh Khadi And Village

Supreme Court of India

19 August 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Khalid, V. Balakrishna Eradi

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 250; (1985) 4 SCC 138; (1985) SCC (L&S) 962; AIR 1986 SC 504; 1985 (51) FLR 486; 1985 LabIC 1551; 1986 (1) LLJ 58; 1985 (2) LLN 823; 1985(2) SCALE 324; [1985] Supp2 S.C.R. 641; 1986 (1) UJ 257

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Madhya Pradesh Khadi And Village Industries Act 1959

Summary: Labour and Industrial law - M.P. Industrial Relations Act, 1960, s. 2(19) - M.P. Khadi and Village Industries Board - Whether "Industry" ?- Function is 'to support, encourage, assist and carry on Khadi and Village Industries and in the matters incidental to such trade or business' - Supplies raw wool to Co-operative Societies - Societies convert raw wool into blankets - Supply them to the Board - Blankets are not properties of the societies as they could not sell it - blankets supplied back are sold in open market - Held, Board was an "industry" as the above act of sale would clearly come within the definition of the word trade or business as contemplated in s. 2(19) of the Act - Appeal allowed.
