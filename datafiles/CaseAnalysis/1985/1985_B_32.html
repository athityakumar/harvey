Surya Narain Yadav and Others v Bihar State Electricity Board and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 May 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranganath Misra, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, A. Varadarajan

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 408; (1985) 3 SCC 38; (1985) SCC (L&S) 539; AIR 1985 SC 941; 1986 (52) FLR 179; 1985 LabIC 961; 1985 (2) LLN 291; 1985(1) SCALE 1106; 1985 (2) SLJ 315; 1985 (2) SLR 479; [1985] Supp1 S.C.R. 605

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Whether High Court decision to not to give a direction to Board to encadre them is correct? - Held, Board had waived requirement of examination and had, while taking advantage of services of appellants when it was in need, delayed implementation of its representations - But it appears that several engineers have also been recruited either on permanent or temporary basis against regular vacancies and they are not parties to the present appeals - Appellants cannot have seniority above those people and it would not be justified in making any direction which would prejudice their seniority behind their back - It appears that there have been requirements even during pendency of the appeals - While granting leave and while disposing of miscellaneous petitions for directions, the Court has already made it clear that appointments pendente lite would be subject to result of appeals - The recruits of 1983 are bound to be subject to court's directions - Further held, judgment of HC is reversed and Board is directed to give effect to directions indicated above within specified time - Appeals allowed.
