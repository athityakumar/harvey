Govind Saran Ganga Saran v Commissioner of Sales Tax and Others

Supreme Court of India

26 April 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Pathak, E.S. Venkataramiah

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 418; (1985) Supp SCC 205; AIR 1985 SC 1041; [1985] 155 ITR 144; 1985(1) SCALE 986; [1985] 3 S.C.R. 985; 1985 (60) STC 1; 1985 TAXLR 2917; 1985 UJ 945

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Indirect Tax - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956 ss. 14, 15 - Bengal Finance (Sales Tax) Act, 1941 (the State Act) s. 20(3) - Bengal Finance (Sales Tax) (Delhi Amendment) Act 1959 s. 5A - A/Y 1968-69 - Sales tax - Assessment - Validity - Appellant was dealer in re-sale of cotton yarn, he was registered under the 1941 Act, as applied to Union Territory of Delhi - For a/y appellant submitted his return of turnover under the State Act and claimed exemption in respect of turnover of sales of cotton thread - Sales Tax Officer concluded that sales were effected in respect of cotton yarn and therefore they were liable to tax - On appeal, Assistant commissioner struck down assessment - Deputy Commissioner of Sales Tax acting suo motu in exercise of revisional jurisdiction, revers order of Assistant Commissioner - Appellant applied in revision to Financial commissioner, revision was allowed and assessment was quashed - Commissioner of Sales Tax filed Writ Petition, HC by its judgment and order dt. 10-9-1974 quashed order of Financial Commissioner - Hence instant appeal - Whether impugned judgment and order of HC dt. 10-9-1974 was justified - Held, in order that tax should not be levied at more than one stage it was imperative that sales tax law of State should specify either expressly or be, necessary implication single point at which tax might be levied - Alternatively, it might empower statutory authority to prescribe such single point for purpose, where such point was not prescribed, either by statute or by statutory delegate, no compliance was possible with s. 15 of the 1956 - Single point at which tax might be imposed should be definite ascertainable point so that both dealer and sales tax authorities might know clearly point at which tax was to be levied - It might be noted that the State Act as applied to Union Territory of Delhi was amended by Parliament in 1959, and s. 5A, of the 1959 Act was inserted, that provision clearly empowers Chief Commissioner to specify single point in series of sales at which single point taxation might be levied - Thus, there was ample power u/s. 5A of the 1959 Act enabling Chief Commissioner to specify single point at which tax might be levied in series of sales, this could, however, be done by him only by notification in Official Gazette - No such notification was placed before SC which could relate to assessment year under consideration - SC hold therefore that vital prerequisite of s. 15 of the 1956 Act, namely, that tax should not be levied at more than one stage, had not been satisfied in respect of turnover of cotton yarn and accordingly assessment complained of was liable to be quashed - Judgment and order of HC of Delhi was set aside and assessment of turnover of cotton yarn was quashed - Appeal allowed.
