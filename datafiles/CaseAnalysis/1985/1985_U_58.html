Shiv Ratan Makim S/O Nandlal Makim v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

16 December 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Bhagwati, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 301; (1986) SCC (Cr) 74; (1986) 1 SCC 404; AIR 1986 SC 610; 1986 (1) Crimes 465; 1986 CRLJ 813; 1986 CRLR 89; 1986 CrLR(SC) 89; 1985(2) SCALE 1504; [1985] 3 S.C.R. 843; [1985] supp3 S.C.R. 843

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Conservation of Foreign Exchange and Prevention of Smuggling Activities Act, 1974, s. 3 - Preventive detention - Validity of - Whether order of detention is vitiated by solitary incident, considerable time elapsed between date of recovery and date of order and by malafides? - Held, Order of detention was made with a view to circumventing or bypassing criminal prosecution instituted against petitioner and detaining authority had not applied its mind to vital aspect that power of detention cannot be used to subvert, supplant or substitute punitive law - Having regard to nature of activity of smuggling and facts set out by him in written statement, an inference could legitimately be drawn even from a single incident of smuggling that petitioner was indulging in smuggling of gold - Where an unreasonably long period has elapsed between date of incident and date of order of detention, an inference may legitimately be drawn that there is no nexus between incident and order of detention and order of detention may be liable to be struck down as invalid - But there can be no hard and fast rule as to what is length of time which should be regarded sufficient to snap nexus between incident and order of detention - Purpose of preventive detention being different from conviction and punishment and subjective satisfaction being necessary in former while proof beyond reasonable doubt being necessary in latter - The order of detention would not be bad merely because criminal prosecution has failed - Order of detention was plainly and indubitably with a view to preventing petitioner from continuing activity of smuggling - Order of detention is perfectly valid - Petition dismissed.
