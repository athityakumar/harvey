G.S. Lamba and Others v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

22 March 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

D.A. Desai, V. Khalid

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 199; (1985) SCC (L&S) 491; (1985) 2 SCC 604; AIR 1985 SC 1019; 1985 (28) DLT 281; 1985 (51) FLR 192; 1985 LabIC 822; 1985 (2) LLJ 282; 1985 (2) LLN 76; 1985(1) SCALE 563; [1985] 3 S.C.R. 431; 1985 (1) SLJ 676; 1985 (1) SLR 687

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Indian Foreign Service Branch 'B' Rules 1964

Summary: Service - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14 and 16 - Indian Foreign Service Branch 'B' Rules, 1964 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Seniority - Determination - Whether seniority list have been drawn up on principle of quota-rule and that in facts of relevant rules it is violative of arts. 14 and 16 of Constitution? - Held, enormous departure or by power to relax, quota rule was not adhered to - Quota rule for inter-se seniority as prescribed in s. 25(1)(ii) cannot be given effect - Therefore in absence of any other valid principle of seniority it is well established that continuous officiation in cadre, grade of service will provide a valid principle of seniority but seniority lists having not been prepared on this principle are in violation of provisions of arts.14 and 16 of Constitution, therefore quashed - Petition allowed.
