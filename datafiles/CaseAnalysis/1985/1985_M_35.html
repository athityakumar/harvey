Deputy Commissioner of Sales Tax (Law), Board of v Messrs K. Kelukutty

Supreme Court of India

03 May 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Pathak, E.S. Venkataramiah

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 138; (1985) 4 SCC 35; AIR 1985 SC 1143; 1986 (24) E.L.T. 186; [1985] 155 ITR 158; 1985(1) SCALE 1264; 1985 (60) STC 7; [1985] Supp1 S.C.R. 135; 1985 TAXLR 2921; [1985] 22 TAXMAN 25; 1986 (1) UJ 34

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Sales Tax

Summary: Contract and Commercial - Partnership Act, 1932, s. 4 - Kerala General Sales Tax Act, 1963, ss.2(viii), 2(xvi-a) - Partnership firm - SLP against the judgment of High Court dismissing two tax revision petitions arising out of assessments - Partners of a firm constituting another firm - And carrying on separate business - Determination of identities - Whether two distinct partnership firms - Held, intention of partners is a decisive factor - On the material on record it is not possible to say, whether there is one firm or two - Hence matter remanded to the Appellate Assistant Commissioner, Sales Tax, for taking up the appeals - Appeal disposed of.
