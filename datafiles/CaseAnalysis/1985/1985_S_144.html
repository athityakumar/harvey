Ramesh Balkrishna Kulkarni v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

31 July 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, A. Varadarajan

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 397; (1985) SCC (Cr) 407; (1985) 3 SCC 606; AIR 1985 SC 1655; 1986 (1) Bom.C.R. 67; 1985 (2) Crimes 378; 1986 CRLJ 14; 1985 CrLR(SC) 407; 1985(2) SCALE 254; [1985] Supp2 S.C.R. 345

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 21 and 161 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, s. 5 - Municipal Councillor - Whether public servant - Conviction - Challenged - Appellant, who was a Municipal Councillor, was prosecuted u/s. 161 of the IPC and s.5(1)(d) r/w s. 5(2) of the Act - HC, after holding that sanction by the competent authority to prosecute the appellant was valid, confirmed his conviction and sentence - Hence, instant appeal - Whether Municipal Councillor who was not assisting any public servant is a 'public servant' within the meaning of s.21 of the IPC.

Held, a 'public servant' is an authority who must be appointed by Govt. or a semi-governmental body and should be in the pay or salary of the same. Secondly, a 'public servant' is to discharge his duties in accordance with the rules and regulations made by the Government. In the instant case, a Municipal Councillor does not owe his appointment to any governmental authority. Such a person is elected by the people and functions undeterred by the commands or edicts of a governmental authority. The mere fact that a MLA gets allowance by way of honorarium does not convert his status into that of a 'public servant'. The appellant, not being a public servant, could not be prosecuted under the provisions of the Act whether or not sanction to prosecuted him is obtained which is wholly irrelevant to the issue. Appeal allowed.
