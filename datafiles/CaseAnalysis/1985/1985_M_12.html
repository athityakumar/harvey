American Home Products Corporation v Mac Laboratories Private Limited And Anr.

Supreme Court of India

30 September 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Madon, Amarendra Nath Sen

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 464; (1986) 1 SCC 465; AIR 1986 SC 137; 1986 (1) CCC 665; 1986 (6) PTC 71; 1985(2) SCALE 933; [1985] 3 S.C.R. 264; [1985] supp3 S.C.R. 264

Case Digest

Subject: Intellectual Property

Keywords: Registered User, Notice Of Opposition, Trade Marks Rules, 1959, Trade and Merchandise Marks Rules, 1959, Trade Marks Act, 1938, Defence of India Rules, 1939, Trade Marks Registration Act, 1875, Constitution (Thirtieth Amendment) Act, 1972, Patents, Designs and Trade Marks Act, 1883, Trade Marks Act, 1905, Trade Marks Rules, 1942

Summary: Intellectual Property - Trade and Merchandise Marks Act, 1958, ss. 18, 46(1)(a)(b) and 48 - Constitution (Thirtieth Amendment) Act, 1972art. 133(1) - Trade Mark - 'Dristan' - Removal from register u/s. 46(1)(a) of the Act - Proof - Procedure - (A) Whether a proprietor of a trade mark who intends to use it solely by a registered user is entitled to registration of his trade mark, u/s.18 of the Trade Marks Act - Burden of proof - Intention of the appellant to use the trade mark 'Dristan' through the Indian Company which was subsequently to get itself registered as the registered user of the said trade mark - no question of any trafficking in a trade mark - Held, intention cannot but be characterized as bona fide; appellant cannot be said to have practised any deception in stating in its application that it intended to use the trade mark itself; Court observed that it would be better if the Central Govt. were to amend Form No. TM-1 to require the applicant to state whether he proposes to use the trade mark himself or through a registered proprietor and if so, to state the particulars of the proposed registered user - (B) Interpretation of Statutes - Question of construction of s.48(2) and its effect on s. 18(1) of the 1958 Act - Construction of Statute, leading to manifest absurdity, futility, palpable injustice or absurd inconvenience or anomaly - It should be avoided - Appeal allowed.
