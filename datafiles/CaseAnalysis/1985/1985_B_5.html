Babubhai And Co. And Ors. v State Of Gujarat And Ors

Supreme Court of India

09 April 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

V.D. Tulzapurkar, V. Khalid

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 256; (1985) 2 SCC 732; AIR 1985 SC 613; 1985 (2) CCC 161; 1985(1) SCALE 658; [1985] 3 S.C.R. 614; 1985 UJ 935

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Constitution, art.14 - Bombay Town Planning Act, 1955, s. 54 - Bombay Town Planning Rules, 1956, r. 27 - Constitutionality validity of Town Planning Statute - Lands reserved for construction of roads and public purposes - Final scheme - Occupants to be evicted there under - Summary eviction is an exclusive remedy - So local authority could not adopt normal remedy of Civil Suit - Held, s. 54 and r. 27 are not ultra vires art. 14 of Constitution - Mere absence of corrective machinery by way of appeal would not render the provision invalid - Appeal dismissed.
