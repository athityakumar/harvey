The Commissioner Of Income Tax, Bangalore v Sri J.H. Gotla, Yadagiri

Supreme Court of India

29 August 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, Ranganath Misra, V.D. Tulzapurkar

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 329; (1985) 4 SCC 343; (1985) SCC (Tax) 670; AIR 1985 SC 1698; 1985 (48) CTR 363; [1985] 156 ITR 323; 1985(2) SCALE 723; [1985] Supp2 S.C.R. 711; [1985] 23 TAXMAN 14; 1986 (1) UJ 628

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 16(3) and 24(2) (ii) - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 64 - Interpretation of Taxing Statute - Set off of loss - Assessee, an individual, was carrying on business in the relevant a/y in purchase and sale of groundnut oil and was also running a mill - On 1.6.1957, assessee had gifted away a part of the oil mill to his wife and three minor children - A firm was constituted by the assessee's wife and another person to the profits of which the three minor sons of the assessee were also admitted - The mill premises as well as the remaining machinery of the assessee were leased out to this firm which carried on the business of the manufacture and sale of groundnut oil - Assessee also entered into an agreement with the firm under which certain services were rendered to the firm by way of management - Assessee was entitled to get commission at the stipulated rates on the purchase of oil cake and sale of de-coiled cake made by the firm - Assessee had incurred huge losses in his individual business in the earlier years which were being carried forward from year to year upto the a/y 1958-59 and the loss carried forward from the a/y 1958-59 was over Rs. 7,88,734/- - Assessee claimed set off of the loss carried forward from the a/y 1958-59 against the profits of his own business as also the share income of his wife and minor children - The Income Tax Officer (ITO) rejected said claim for set off in so far as it related to the share income of his wife and minor children - Similar claims for set off were made in the a/ys 1960-61 and 1961-62 but were rejected - On appeals, Appellate Asst. Commissioner allowed the set off claimed on the ground that the assessee himself was deemed to be carrying on the business from which the share income was derived by his wife and minor children - Tribunal held that the assessee was not entitled u/s. 24(2) of the Act to claim set off of his losses against the income of his wife and minor children - HC held that the share income of the assessee's wife and minor children included in the assessee's total income u/s. 16(3) of the said Act should be regarded as business income derived from business carried on by the assessee and in that view of the matter the assessee was entitled to set off his loss carried forward from the previous year - Hence, the instant appeal - Whether assessee would be entitled to carry forward and set off the losses against the share income of the assessee's wife and minor children in respect of the a/y 1959-60 under Section 24(2) of the Act, 1922? - Held, to set off the carried forward loss of the assessee, two conditions were required to be fulfilled u/s. 24(2) of 1922 Act, firstly, business, profession or vocation must be carried on by him in that year and secondly, that the business profession or vocation in which loss was originally sustained must continue to be carried on by the assessee in the year in question - The principle underlying s. 24(2)(11) of the Act was to restrict the set off only to the business income of the year to which it was carried forward so that the loss sustained by the assessee in any other business, profession or vocation could be set off against the income from any business, profession or vocation carried on by him in that year - The object of s. 16 (3) of the Act has to be read in conjunction with s. 24(2) of the Act - Income of wife and minor children included in assessee's total income u/s. 16 (3) of the Act should be regarded as business income derived from business carried on by assessee - Hence, assessee entitled to set-off his loss against income of his wife and minor children - Appeals dismissed.
