Hirachand Kothari (Dead) Through LRs v State of Rajasthan and Another

Supreme Court of India

09 May 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

A.P. Sen, V. Khalid

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 258; (1985) Supp SCC 17; AIR 1985 SC 998; 1985 (2) CCC 632; 1985(1) SCALE 1131; [1985] Supp1 S.C.R. 644

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Evidence Act, 1872, s.20 - Interest Act, 1839, s.1 - Disputed land - Suit for damages against Govt. - Entitlement - (1) Whether the parties by mutual consent had agreed to appoint Superintending Engineer and Town Planning Officer, Jaipur to ascertain the value of the disputed land as an appraiser or valuer and therefore the appraisement or valuation thereof by him in his Report at Rs. 35,826.50p. should be treated as an admission u/s.20 of 1872 Act, on the basis of which the plaintiff's claim for damages had to be decreed - Held, while SC felt that the HC was not right in excluding from its consideration the Chief Minister's letter on the ground of want of proof, the document by itself did not substantiate the plaintiff's claim that the parties had by mutual consent agreed to appoint Superintending Engineer to ascertain the value of the disputed plot as an appraiser or valuer and therefore the valuation thereof put by him in his report at Rs. 35,826.50p. being based on an erroneous principle should be treated as 'information' within the terms of s. 20 of 1872 Act and therefore an admission which should operate as estoppel against respondent - HC was therefore justified in upholding the judgment of Subordinate Judge that the report of Superintending Engineer making an appraisement or valuation at Rs. 35,826.50p. could not be treated as an admission u/s. 20 of 1872 Act on the basis of which the plaintiff's claim for damages had to be decreed - (2) Whether the plaintiff being deprived of property was, on general principles, entitled to payment of interest on the amount payable to him as the value of the property taken by respondent - Held, it was further held by SC in one case that the Court had ample power under proviso to s.1 of 1839 Act to award interest on equitable grounds - In all the facts and circumstances of the case, the reasonable rate of interest would be 6% per annum on the compensation amount of Rs. 25,000 from date of dispossession till date of judgment of Subordinate Judge, and thereafter at 9% per annum thereon till realization - It more or less works out to Rs. 95,000 which was multiple of 20 times the actual investment of the appellant in purchasing in Scheme in the city of Jaipur - Satinder Singh v. Amrao Singh, 1961 Indlaw SC 503, relied on - Appeal partly allowed.
