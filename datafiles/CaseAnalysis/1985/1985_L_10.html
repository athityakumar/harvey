State Delhi (Administration) v Laxman Kumar and Others

Supreme Court of India

23 September 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranganath Misra, Amarendra Nath Sen

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 384; (1985) 4 SCC 476; (1986) SCC (Cr) 2; AIR 1986 SC 250; 1985 (2) Crimes 758; 1986 CRLJ 155; 1985 CRLR 501; 1985 (28) DLT 500; 1985(2) SCALE 701; [1985] Supp2 S.C.R. 898

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Bride Burning

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872 - Murder - Bride burning - Appreciation of evidence - Witnesses -Animosity Witnesses in a bride burning case, who had not only - Dying declaration - Credibility - Declaration recorded by investigating officer - Time and signature of deponent absent - Capability of declarant to mark declaration, doubtful -Held, could not be said witnesses to have animosity towards accused -Since capability of declarant to mark declaration was doubtful, declaration cannot be accepted.
