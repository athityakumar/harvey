Ram Singh and Others v Col. Ram Singh

Supreme Court of India

07 August 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, A. Varadarajan, S. Mukharji

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 395; (1985) Supp SCC 611; AIR 1986 SC 3; 1985(2) SCALE 1142; [1985] Supp2 S.C.R. 399

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Election; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Furnishing Of Security

Summary: Election - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 3, 100, 123, 86, 87 and 123(2) - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, O 8, r. 5 - Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 3, 67 - Election Petition - Allegations of fraud or corrupt practice - Evidence - (A) Declaring election to be void - Two views, one in favour of elected candidate and other against him, possible - (B) Setting aside of election - Allegation that elected candidate changing parties frequently to suit his needs - (C) Specific allegations with facts and figures regarding practices not alleged in main part of petition but only in statement of particulars - (D) Proof of corrupt practice - It must be committed by candidate or his polling agent or by other with implicit or explicit consent of candidate or polling agent - Supporters indulging in corrupt practice on their own - (E) Admissibility of Tape recorded statement - (F) Admissibility of evidence - Voter deposing to his having witnessed altercation between supporters of a candidate and a voter near polling booth and his having rescued voter - Witness, though chowkidar of village, not reporting incident either to DC or SDO or ASI who had visited spot subsequently - (G) Credibility of witness - Polling agent of rival party candidate deposing to his having witnessed a candidate coming with 50, 60 persons to polling booth and telling his polling agent that he was leaving certain persons behind and agents should see that nobody voted for rival party candidate - Witnesses making only verbal complaint - (H) Documentary evidence - Admissibility - Witnesses, SDO relying in support of his testimony on complaint handed over to him by certain witnesses - Witness stated that not his signature - And there were two other persons of same name as his - Admission shows that witness only handed over complaint and contents of complaint were neither proved nor maker of complaint examined - (I) Allegations of corrupt practice - Standard of proof - (J) Election petition - Procedure - Certain persons alleged to be involved in specified incident near a polling booth were relations of elected candidate - No specific denial of allegation by candidate - (K) Diary of Presiding Officer - In column relating to interruption and obstruction to poll, officer mentioning about putting of pressure on polling party by respondent and getting bogus votes polled in his favour but not making any mention of incident in column relating to intimidation and crossing same - (L) Undue influence - Forcible polling of bogus votes after forcible marking of ballot papers in favour of a candidate after removing them from polling officers - Held, election should not be declared void - No ground for setting aside of his election - It is a factor relevant in judging truth of particulars mentioned in statement - Election cannot be voided - Conditions precedent - He is not credible witness - He being most interested witness, his evidence does not carry any weight - Complaint is clearly inadmissible - They have to be proved beyond reasonable doubt almost just like criminal case - CPC, 1908 being applicable to trial of election petition, in view of O 8, rr. 5 of CPC, 1908, fact alleged being material, candidate just be deemed to have admitted it - Document is self contradictory and not inspiring confidence - It constitutes interference with electoral right - Appeal dismissed.
