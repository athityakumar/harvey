Commissioner Of Income Tax, A.P. v Messrs T.Veerabhadra Rao, K. Koteswara Rao and

Supreme Court of India

08 July 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Pathak, E.S. Venkataramiah

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 239; (1985) Supp SCC 403; AIR 1985 SC 1600; 1985 (48) CTR 123; [1985] 155 ITR 152; 1985(2) SCALE 1; [1985] Supp2 S.C.R. 20; 1985 TAXLR 930; [1985] 22 TAXMAN 45; 1986 (1) UJ 354

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Bad Debts, Debt Laws

Summary: Income Tax and Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 37 - Income Tax - Take over of firm by assessee with its rights and liabilities - (A) Whether bad debt and the legal expense were allowable deductions in the assessment of the assessee firm for the assessment year 1965-66 - Settlement by partial payment of debt - (B) Maintainability of claim for deduction of Legal expenditure - Legal expenses in connection with an appeal filed in this Court to recover an amount due from the Central Govt. - Transaction related to the predecessor firm and the suit instituted by it was continued by the assessee on taking over the assets and liabilities of the predecessor firm - Held, entitled to the deduction as a bad debt written off by it in its accounts of the previous year as irrecoverable - Claim tenable - Appeal dismissed.
