Ashok Kumar Yadav and Others Etc v State of Haryana and Others Etc

Supreme Court of India

10 May 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Bhagwati, Y.V. Chandrachud, Amarendra Nath Sen, V. Balakrishna Eradi

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 407; (1985) 4 SCC 417; (1986) SCC (L&S) 88; AIR 1987 SC 454; 1986 LabIC 1417; 1986 (2) LLN 804; 1985(1) SCALE 1290; 1985 (2) SLJ 482; 1985 (3) SLR 200; [1985] Supp1 S.C.R. 657

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Constitution - Punjab Civil Service (Executive Branch) Rules, 1930 - Principles of natural justice - Writ petition challenging selections made by Chairman and members of Public Service Commission - (A) Chairman and some members not impleaded - High Court observed, their 'appointment on political and caste considerations' and they did not satisfy test of 'men of high integrity, caliber and qualification' - But was legal - Validity of appointments not challenged nor relief to set aside their appointments claimed - Validity - And effect on selections made by them - (B) Natural justice - Bias - One should not be judge of his own cause - Applicability of, to administrative bodies - (C) Close relative of a member of Commission appearing for selection - Member if required to withdraw from selection process - (D) Selection on Composite written test and viva voce - (1) Candidate obtaining 45% marks in written test - If entitled to appear for interviews - (2) Selection - Written test and viva voice - No. of candidates to be called for interview should not exceed 2-3 times number of vacancies - Calling for interview all candidates securing minimum eligibility marks in written test - Validity - (3) Allocation of 12.2% marks for viva for general and 25% for ex-service officers recommended for all State PSC - Higher percentage - Constitutionality -

(A) Held, violated principles of natural justice as they were not parties to the proceedings, condemnatory observations were unjustified, unwarranted and not supported by averments Selections made by them could not be set aside on ground that they lacked integrity, caliber and qualification - Moreover, their integrity etc. could not be questioned collaterally by HC while considering validity of selection made by them - (B) Principle applicable to administrative bodies - (C) Need not completely withdraw - Sufficient, if he does not participate in interview of that candidate - (D) Held, is eligible but not entitled and there is no obligation on Commission to call for interview all candidates securing 45% in written test - Not proper - Higher percentage would render selections arbitrary - Appeal allowed.
