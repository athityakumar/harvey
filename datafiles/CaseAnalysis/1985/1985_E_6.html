 Life Insurance Corporation of India v Escorts Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 December 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy, E.S. Venkataramiah, V. Balakrishna Eradi, R.B. Misra, V. Khalid

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 368; (1986) 1 SCC 264; AIR 1986 SC 1370; [1986] 59 Comp Cas 548; 1986 (1) CompLJ 91; 1986 (8) ECC 189; 1985(2) SCALE 1289; [1985] 3 S.C.R. 909; [1985] supp3 S.C.R. 909; 1986 TAXLR 1826

Case Digest

Subject: Exchange Control & FDI; RBI

Keywords: Companies Act 1913

Summary: RBI - Exchange Control and FDI - Corporate - Companies Act, 1956, ss. 111- FERA, 1973, ss. 29(1) and 76 - Transfer of Shares - 'political' processes of 'corporate democracy' are sought to be subject to investigation by court by invoking the principle of the Rule of Law, with emphasis on the rule against arbitrary State action - Purchase of shares of Indian Companies by non-resident companies - Permission of RBI required u/ss. 29(1) and 76 of FERA, 1973 - RBI consulting Govt. as enormous foreign exchange - Granting permission by changing its earlier negative attitude - Complaints about irregularities in purchase of its shares from Indian Company - Enquiries from Authorised dealer - Petition by a person in management - Litigation complicated by non-appearance of non-resident person having control over management of foreign companies and broker employed for purchase of shares - Scrutiny on touchstone of reasonableness - Questions arise (A) Whether previous permission of RBI is needed - (B) Whether transferee becomes owner even before transfer is registered in the books of the company u/s. 111 of the Companies Act, 1956 - (C) Whether the company can refuse to register transfer on due to doubt on validity of permission u/s. 29(1) of FERA - (D) - Whether bank can be said to have acted under pressure - (E) Whether bank is guilty of non-application of mind as failure of bank to reply to communications of company u/art. 226 of - (F) Whether corporate veil can be lifted u/s 34 of the Companies Act or art.19 of the Constitution - (G) Whether Shareholders are required to give reasons in support of resolutions they propose to move in the General meeting of the company on the requisition of the Shareholders u/s. 173(2) of the Companies Act - (H) Whether State or instrumentality of State purchasing shares of company can be expected to state reasons for seeking to change management of company u/arts.14, 12, 32, and 226 (I) On whom cost should be imposed - Held, ex post facto permission can also be granted - Transferee becomes owner of beneficial interest though legal title continues with transferor until transfer is registered in books of company - Neither company nor other authority nor any individual can question validity of grant of permission - Pressure or malafides cannot be attributed to Bank - Veil can be lifted -No failure on part of company - Shareholders not required to give reason - Court will not direct it to state reasons for seeking to change management - Litigants, person having control of foreign companies and broker and the nationalised bank, authorised dealer saddled with costs - Appeal allowed.
