Pratibha Rani v Suraj Kumar and Another

Supreme Court of India

12 March 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, A. Varadarajan, S. Mukharji

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 318; (1985) SCC (Cr) 180; (1985) 2 SCC 370; AIR 1985 SC 628; 1985 (1) Crimes 614; 1985 CRLJ 817; 1985 CrLR(SC) 149; 1985 (49) CTR 318; 1985 (1) DMC 308; 1985 (1) HLR 605; [1985] 155 ITR 190; 1985 (1) RCR(Rent) 539; 1985(1) SCALE 458; [1985] 3 S.C.R. 191

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Family & Personal

Keywords: Common Use, Stridhana

Summary: Constitution - Criminal - Family & Personal -Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 405 and 406 - Hindu Succession Act, 1956, s. 14 - Hindu Marriage Act, 1955, s. 27 - Possession of stridhan - Whether criminal proceedings for its misappropriation is maintainable? - Held, husband and in-laws are trustees of stridhan and are bound to return same if and when demanded by married woman and criminal proceedings for its misappropriation against husband or in-laws is maintainable - Appeal allowed.
