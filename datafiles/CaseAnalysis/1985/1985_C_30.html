Maya Rani Punj v Commissioner of Income Tax, Delhi

Supreme Court of India

11 December 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, V.D. Tulzapurkar, Ranganath Misra

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 134; (1986) SCC (Tax) 220; (1986) 1 SCC 445; AIR 1986 SC 293; 1986 (1) CCC 908; 1986 (50) CTR 191; [1986] 157 ITR 330; 1985(2) SCALE 1267; [1985] 3 S.C.R. 827; 1987 (65) STC 416; [1985] supp3 S.C.R. 827; 1986 TAXLR 241; [1986] 24 TAXMAN 1; 1986 (2) TLR 241; 1986 (1) UJ 192

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Constitution - Income Tax Act, 1961 - Income Tax Act, 1922 - (A) Return - Filing of, for Assessment Year 1961-62, due on 28-09-1962 - Delay in filing - Continuing offence - Validity imposition of Penalty u/s. 28 of 1922 Act - (B) Constitutionality of s. 271 (1) (a) of 1961 Act, prescribing minimum rate of penalty - (C) Precedents - Stare decisis - Supreme Court - Decision of Constitution Bench of SC holding field for a quarter of century without challenge - Binding effect, in light of fact that some American decision was not considered by Constitution Bench - Held, penalty is leviable u/s. 271(1)(a) and not u/s. 28 of 1922 Act - And it is not violative of art. 20 of Constitution - It is binding on Supreme Court and fact does not justify reconsideration of decision - Appeal dismissed.
