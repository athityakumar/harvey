Satyavir Singh and Others v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 September 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Madon, V.D. Tulzapurkar, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 125; (1985) 4 SCC 252; (1986) SCC (L&S) 1; AIR 1986 SC 555; 1986 (1) CCC 145; 1986 (52) FLR 62; 1986 LabIC 1; 1986 (1) LLJ 36; 1986 (1) LLN 496; 1985(2) SCALE 488; 1986 (1) SLJ 1; 1986 (1) SLR 255; [1985] Supp2 S.C.R. 791

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 311 (2) Second Proviso cl. (b) - Central Civil Services (Classification, Control and Appeal) Rules, 1965, r. 19 - Uttar Pradesh Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Service Rules - Dispensation of enquiry u/cl. (b) of second proviso to art. 311(2) - Satisfaction of authority that it is not reasonably practicable to hold such inquiry - Disturbance by employees of Research and Analysis Wing of Govt. - Disciplinary Authority was of opinion that material witnesses would not be available due to intimidation - Validity of dismissals after dispensing with inquiry - Held, dispensation was valid - Appeal dismissed.
