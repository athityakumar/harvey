State of Uttar Pradesh v Ballabh Das and Others

Supreme Court of India

02 August 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, A. Varadarajan

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 36; (1985) SCC (Cr) 452; (1985) 3 SCC 703; AIR 1985 SC 1384; 1985 CRLJ 2009; 1985 CRLR 393; 1985(2) SCALE 1361; 1985 UJ 906

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 3 and 5 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1974, s. 154 - Acquittal - Whether evidence of interested witnesses should not be accepted in absence of independent witness? - Held, there is no Law which says that in absence of any independent witness, evidence of interested witnesses should be thrown out, what law requires is that, where witnesses are interested, court should approach their evidence with care and caution in order to exclude possibility of false implication - Evidence of interested witnesses is not like that of an approver which is presumed to be tainted and requires corroboration but said evidence is as good as any other evidence - Therefore conviction of accused is proper -Appeal allowed.
