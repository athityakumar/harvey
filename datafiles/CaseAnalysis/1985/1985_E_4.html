Messrs Gasket Radiators Private Limited v Employees State Insurance Corporation and Another

Supreme Court of India

28 February 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy, E.S. Venkataramiah, R.B. Misra

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 132; (1985) 2 SCC 68; (1985) SCC (L&S) 400; AIR 1985 SC 790; 1985 (50) FLR 426; 1985 LabIC 682; 1985 (1) LLJ 506; 1985 (1) LLN 773; 1985(1) SCALE 337; [1985] 2 S.C.R. 1085; 1985 UJ 588

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial law - Employee's State Insurance Act, 1948, Chap. V-A - Constitutionality - Whether Chap. V-A of Employee's State Insurance Act, 1948 is ultra vires? - Held, it may be noted that Chap. V-A was inserted by an amending Act only in 1951 - Object of amendment was to make an equitable distribution of contributions by all employers - It was not considered fair that only employers of those regions to which benefit provisions were extended should alone make contributions and thereby help to set up a Corporation - Benefit provisions will sooner or later be extended to all areas - Therefore, amendment provides that employers of regions to which benefit clauses are not extended must also make their contributions though at a lesser rate - Object of Act is that benefits which it provides should become available to employees in all factories throughout India as soon as circumstances make it practicable - There are various steps that Govt. have to take before such benefits can be given to employers - Statutory bodies have to be set up, various officers has to be appointed and arrangements have to be made for providing medical help - All these required time and money and in some areas time required may be more than in other areas - Chap. V-A is for meeting needs of transitory period - When whole Act is brought into force in whole of India, it would not be necessary to retain this Chapter - Whether special contribution is to be viewed as a tax, fee or neither, Chap. V-A has sufficient constitutional protection - Appeal dismissed.
