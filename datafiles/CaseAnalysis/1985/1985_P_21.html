Anil Kumar v Presiding Officer and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 May 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

D.A. Desai, V. Balakrishna Eradi, V. Khalid

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 475; (1985) 3 SCC 378; (1985) SCC (L&S) 815; AIR 1985 SC 1121; 1986 (52) FLR 487; 1985 LabIC 1219; 1986 (1) LLJ 101; 1985 (2) LLN 579; 1985(2) SCALE 1365; 1985 (3) SLR 26; 1985 UJ 639

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Labour and Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, sch. II, items 3 and 6 - (A) What is the need for reasonability of report of enquiry officer in a disciplinary enquiry into is an industrial dispute - Held, must be reasoned one - Where a disciplinary enquiry affects the livelihood and is likely to cast a stigma, it has to be hold in accordance with the principles of natural justice - The minimum expectation is that the report must be a reasoned one - (B) Question raised that even though s.11A was introduced in the statue after the date of the termination of the appellant, yet when the matter was before the Labour Court, it was obligatory upon the Labour Court to consider whether the punishment was disproportionate to the gravity of the misconduct charged - Held, once satisfied that the order of termination of service is unsustainable, Supreme Court decline to go into the larger question - Appeal allowed.
