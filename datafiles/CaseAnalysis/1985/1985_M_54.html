Dr. Dinesh Kumar And Ors. v Motilal Nehru Medical College Allahabad And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

01 May 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Bhagwati, Ranganath Misra, Amarendra Nath Sen

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 169; (1985) 3 SCC 22; AIR 1985 SC 1059; 1985(1) SCALE 1013; [1985] Supp1 S.C.R. 41

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Education - Constitution - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts.14, 15 and 32 - MBBS and PG course in medical faculty - (A) Admission to open seats is to made only on the basis of marks in entrance examination held by Central Govt. or IMC - Whether admission made on the basis of marks in qualifying examination of Universities/States is violative of art. 14 - (B) Educational institutions having system of one year house job followed by admission to 2 years PG course - Applicability of certain predecided case to admission for academic year 1985-86 - Held, is violative of art. 14 - Admission not to be governed by new principle laid down in the judgment in Dr. Pradeep Jain's case (1984 INDLAW SC 72) but by the old rules, which prevailed prior to the delivery of that judgment - Petition partly allowed.
