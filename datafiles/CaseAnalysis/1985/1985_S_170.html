 Adarsh Travels Bus Service and Another v State of Uttar Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

17 October 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy, E.S. Venkataramiah, V. Balakrishna Eradi, R.B. Misra, V. Khalid

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 375; (1985) 4 SCC 557; AIR 1986 SC 319; 1986 ALJ 215; 1985(2) SCALE 880; [1985] 3 S.C.R. 661; [1985] supp3 S.C.R. 661

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1939, ss. 2 (28 A), 68 C, 68 D(3), 68 FF - (A) Scope of scheme of nationalisation of route prohibiting private operators from plying stage carriages on route - (B) Nationalisation of route A B to C and D falling between A B - It includes every part of particular highway from A to B - Only terminals cannot be considered - Nationalisation of route C D does affect person plying on common sector - But private operator cannot ply on part of route even with corridor restrictions - Held, plying on common sector is to ply stage carriage permit on two points, even if he does not pick up or set down passengers between those two points - Appeals and petitions dismissed.
