Saroj Aggarwal v Commissioner of Income Tax, Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

30 September 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, V.D. Tulzapurkar

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 336; (1985) 4 SCC 539; AIR 1986 SC 376; 1985 (49) CTR 183; [1985] 156 ITR 497; 1985(2) SCALE 803; [1985] 3 S.C.R. 209; [1985] supp3 S.C.R. 209; 1985 TAXLR 1432; [1985] 23 TAXMAN 76; 1986 (1) UJ 572

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Legal Heirs, Assessable Entity, Question Of Fact And Law, Change In The Constitution Of Firm, Set Off, Speculation, Death, Unabsorbed loss

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 78 (2) - Partnership firm - Carry forward and set off of losses - Unabsorbed loss from speculation - Death of partner - Legal representatives succeded to business by inheritance - Whether profits can be set off against losses - Held, profits can be set off against losses - Appeal allowed.
