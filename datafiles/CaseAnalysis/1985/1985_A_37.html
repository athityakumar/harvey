Dr. Kohli And Ors v Atul Products Ltd

Supreme Court of India

12 February 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

E.S. Venkataramiah, S. Mukharji, O. Chinnappa Reddy

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 294; (1985) 2 SCC 77; AIR 1985 SC 537; 1985 (2) CompLJ 311; 1985 CRLR 236; 1985 CrLR(SC) 236; 1985 ECR 937; 1985 (20) E.L.T. 212; 1985(1) SCALE 266; [1985] 2 S.C.R. 832; 1985 TAXLR 2489

Case Digest

Subject: Excise

Keywords: Synthetic, Finance Act, 1961, Provisional Collection Taxes Act, 1931

Summary: Excise - Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944, s. 3, sch.1, item 14D - Central Excise Rules, 1944, rr. 8(1),10 and 10A - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 115 - Excise Duty - (A) Notification D/- 23-11-1961 - Exemption under - Availability - On processed dyes - (B) Applicability of rr. 10, 10A of the Central Excise Rules - Error - Short levy of duty ot at the time of assessment or when assessment ought to have been made - Discussion and correspondence between Officers and manufacturer leading to short levy - Without reference to actual goods - Before manufacture and clearence - Reply was an advice and not assessment u/r. 52 - Held, can be availed only if duty on basic dyes used is paid at the time of their manufacture (Principle of estoppel) - Held, r. 10-A not prescribing limitation and not attracted - Appeal allowed.
