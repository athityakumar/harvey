Shanker Dass v Union Of India And Anr

Supreme Court of India

12 March 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.V. Chandrachud, D.A. Desai, Amarendra Nath Sen

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 297; (1985) 2 SCC 358; (1985) SCC (L&S) 444; (1986) SCC (Cr) 242; AIR 1985 SC 772; 1985 (1) ALT(SC) 490; 1985 (1) CCC 1085; 1985 CRLR 228; 1985 CrLR(SC) 228; 1985 (28) DLT 74; 1985 (51) FLR 68; 1985 LabIC 590; 1985 (2) LLJ 184; 1985(1) SCALE 391; [1985] 3 S.C.R. 163; 1985 (2) SLJ 454; 1985 (2) SLR 109

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Probation of Offenders Act 1958, Denied, Dismissal from service, Suit for reinstatement

Summary: Service - Probation of Offenders Act, 1958, s. 4, 12 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 311(2)(a) - Dismissal from service - Suit for reinstatement - Denied - Legality - Appellant (employee) filed a suit for setting aside his dismissal from service, mainly on the ground that since he was released under 1958 Act, it was not permissible to authorities to visit him with penalty of dismissal from service - Trial Court dismissed suit on the ground that since appellant was convicted of a criminal charge, he was liable to be dismissed u/art. 311(2)(a) of Constitution - Aggrieved appellant filed an appeal before Lower Appellate Court, which was dismissed - Aggrieved appellant filed a second appeal before HC, which was allowed - Aggrieved GOI filed an letter patent appeal, which was allowed by impugned judgment - Hence, instant appeal - Whether appellant was entitled to be reinstated - Held, order of dismissal from service consequent upon a conviction was not a disqualification within meaning of s. 12 of 1958 Act - There were statutes which provide that persons who were convicted for certain offences should incur certain disqualifications - Art. 311(2)(a) of Constitution conferred on Govt. the power to dismiss a person from service 'on the ground of conduct which has led to his conviction on a criminal charge' - But, that power, like every other power, had to be exercised fairly, justly and reasonably - Surely Constitution did not contemplate that a Govt. servant who was convicted for parking his scooter in a non- parking area should be dismissed from service - He might, perhaps, not be entitled to be heard on the question of penalty since art. 311(2)(a) of Constitution make the provisions of that article inapplicable when a penalty was to be imposed on a Govt. servant or the ground of conduct which has led to his conviction on a criminal charge - But right to impose a penalty carries with it the duty to act justly - Considering facts of case, there could be no two opinions that the penalty of dismissal from service imposed upon the appellant was whimsical - Appellant should be reinstated in service along with full back wages - Impugned judgment of HC was set aside - Appeal allowed.
