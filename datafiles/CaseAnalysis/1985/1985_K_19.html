 Kirpal Singh, M.L.A v Uttam Singh and Another

Supreme Court of India

09 October 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy, E.S. Venkataramiah, V. Balakrishna Eradi, R.B. Misra, V. Khalid

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 261; (1985) 4 SCC 621; AIR 1986 SC 300; 1985(2) SCALE 749; [1985] 3 S.C.R. 622; [1985] supp3 S.C.R. 622; 1986 (1) UJ 25

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Election

Keywords: Election, Nomination papers, candidate

Summary: Election - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 191(1) - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 116 A, 10 and 119 - (A) Appeal u/s. 116 A of Act by returned candidate - Scope of Supreme Court's power to pass interim order - Election set aside by High Court for improper rejection of nomination paper and for no fault of returned candidate - Appeal to Supreme Court - (B) Extent of disqualification of employees of Public Corporations from contesting election - (C) Election petition - Costs - Election set aside by HC - Ground, improper rejection of one candidate's nomination paper and for no fault or disqualification of returned candidate - Validity of awarding costs against candidates - (A) Held, not proper as election is set aside not for his fault - Proper order would be to grant absolute stay so that Constituency may not go unrepresented - Interim order by Supreme Court enabling returned candidate to attend Assembly but prohibiting him from participating in proceedings, or voting and from drawing remuneration - (B) Need for appropriate legislation by Parliament expressed - (C) Improper - Order accordingly.
