Raghavendra Singh v Superintendent, District Jail, Kanpur and Others

Supreme Court of India

28 November 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy, V. Khalid

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 22; (1986) SCC (Cr) 60; (1986) 1 SCC 650; AIR 1986 SC 356; 1986 ALJ 397; 1986 (12) AWC 668; 1986 (2) Crimes 465; 1986 CRLJ 493; 1986 CRLR 333; 1985(2) SCALE 1095; 1986 (1) UJ 224

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal -National Security Act, 1980, ss. 3 and 14 - Appellant contended that there was an enormous delay (75 days) in disposal of his representation by Central Govt. and for that reason alone his further detention was illegal and he was entitled to be set at liberty - State contended that representation when received by home ministry, concerned department to look into matter, disposed matter in 6 days - President or Prime minister's office is not appropriate department - The appellant challenged his detention after his representation for its revocation/cancellation u/s. 4 of Act of 1980 made to Central Govt. - Unduly long delay of 75 days in disposal of representation by Central Govt. - Further detention of detenu becomes illegal - Representation to Central Govt.- Whether sentence in instant case is valid in view of delay in disposing representation? - Held, in view of wholly unexplained and unduly long delay in disposal of representations by Central Govt, further detention of appellant must be held illegal and he must be set at liberty - Appeal Allowed.
