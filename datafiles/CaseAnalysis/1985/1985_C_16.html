U.P. State Electricity Board. Lucknow And Ors. v City Board, Mussoorie and Others Etc. (And Vice

Supreme Court of India

08 February 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

E.S. Venkataramiah, S. Mukharji, O. Chinnappa Reddy

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 228; (1985) 2 SCC 16; AIR 1985 SC 883; 1985 ALJ 243; 1985 CRLR 413; 1985 CrLR(SC) 413; 1985(1) SCALE 196; [1985] 2 S.C.R. 815; 1985 UJ 633

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Electricity & Energy; Land & Property

Summary: Electricity and Energy - Electricity (Supply) Act, 1948, ss. 46(1), 4979(h) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 -Electricity - Fixation of Grid Tariff - (A) Whether framing of regulations u/s. 79(h) of Act is a condition precedent - Held, not a condition precedent - (B) Validity of the levy of additional charges - Held, issue could not be raised by the City Board u/art.226 of the Constitution in respect of the period prior to the filing of the writ petition - (C) Permissibility as to fixation of common grid tariff for an area - DB of the High Court observed that there is ample guidance available in the various provisions of the Act and that the rates fixed are subject to the control of the State Govt. - Held, it is permissible for the electricity Board to fix a common Grid Tariff for an area so that there may be a reasonably uniform development of the area by the supply of electric energy to all licensees or consumers in the area at a uniform rate with such reasonable variations as may be permissible in law subject to the condition that no undue preference is shown to any of them - Appeal disposed of.
