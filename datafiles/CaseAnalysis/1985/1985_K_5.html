Chairman-Cum-Managing Director, United India Fire and General Insurance Company Limited and Others v K. S. Vishwanathan and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 August 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy, V. Balakrishna Eradi, V. Khalid

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 80; (1985) 3 SCC 686; (1985) SCC (L&S) 912; AIR 1985 SC 1405; 1985 (51) FLR 354; 1985 LabIC 1637; 1985 (2) LLJ 580; 1985(2) SCALE 123; 1985 UJ 825

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Insurance

Summary: (A) Insurance - Administrative - General Insurance (Emergency Provisions) Act, 1971, s. 16(1)(a) - Insurance business - Nationalization - Effect - Appellant insurance companies were nationalized by 1971 Act - Central Government framed a scheme u/s. 16(1)(a) of the Act by which existing insurance companies were so grouped together that only four companies remained to carry on business of general insurance - Whether categorization made by Govt. of India was sustainable - Held, Govt. of India did not applied its mind to question of categorization and that no opportunity was afforded to petitioners before categorization was affected - On that ground categorization was quashed and respondents were directed to reconsider question of categorization in accordance with law - Appeal allowed.

(B) Insurance - Administrative - General Insurance (Emergency Provisions) Act, 1971- Nationalization of insurance business - Categorization by Lower Management Committee - Propriety - As contemplated by Scheme, A Lower Management Committee was appointed by Board of General Insurance Corporation - whether categorization made by Committee was proper - Held, Govt. observed that there was no reason to depart from categorization made by Lower Management Committee, it was not see how it follows that GOI did not applied its mind to question - Even an improperly constituted Committee could come to a right conclusion and that conclusion could properly be accepted as correct by authority to whom matter was referred by consent - It was not see any justification for straight away drawing conclusion, without anything more, that authority to whom matter was referred did not apply its mind - Appeal allowed.

(C) Insurance - Administrative - General Insurance (Emergency Provisions) Act, 1971 - Nationalization of insurance business - Categorization by Lower Management Committee - Propriety - Whether integration of service of employees of former Indian insurance companies and other existing insurers should be made in accordance with Scheme - Held, there could be no question of applying s.7 of the Act to erstwhile officers and employees of Indian insurance companies - However, question whether erstwhile officers or employees of Indian insurance company could be categorized as an officer of any four unites of General Insurance Corporation had to be decided in accordance with provisions of Schemes made u/s. 16 of the Act - Decision of HC could not be sustained - Impugned order passed by HC was set aside - Appeal allowed.
