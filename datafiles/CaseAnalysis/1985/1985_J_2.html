J.M. Bhatia, Appellate Assistant Commissioner Of Wealth Tax& v J.M. Shah

Supreme Court of India

19 September 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

V.D. Tulzapurkar, S. Mukharji

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 332; (1985) 4 SCC 655; AIR 1986 SC 268; 1985 (49) CTR 382; [1985] 156 ITR 474; 1985(2) SCALE 845; [1985] Supp2 S.C.R. 872; [1985] 23 TAXMAN 58; 1986 (2) TLR 127; 1986 (1) UJ 134

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Finance (No.2) Act, 1971

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Wealth Tax Act, 1957 - Rectification of mistakes - Exemption - Computation of total wealth, value of jewellery for personal use was excluded in 1970 - No appeal - Finance Act, 1971 retrospectively ammended s. 5 (1) (viii) from 01-04-1963 - Thereby, jewellery was not exempted - Challenge to proceedings for rectification taken by Appellate Assistant Commissioner in 1972 - Held, cannot be challenged on ground that there was no error apparent on face of record - Appeal allowed.
