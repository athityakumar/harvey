Smt. Vijay Laxmi Gangal v Mahendra Pratap Grag

Supreme Court of India

08 May 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

A. Varadarajan, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, Ranganath Misra

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 235; (1985) 3 SCC 364; AIR 1986 SC 753; 1985 (11) ALR 569; 1986 (12) AWC 517; [1985] Supp1 S.C.R. 583

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Uttar Pradesh Urban Building (Regulation of Letting, Rent and Eviction) Act, 1972

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Rent Control - Uttar Pradesh Urban Building (Regulation of Letting, Rent and Eviction) Act, 1972 - Interpretation of s. 20 (4) of the Act - Respondent had deposited the full amount of rent as claimed together with damages for use and occupation, interest and costs as required by s. 20 (4) of the Act - Held, deposit of the amount on the first hearing date, as claimed could not be said to be not unconditional merely because the respondent had contended in the written statement that the rent was only Rs. 125 per mensem - Submission, that for the deposit to be unconditional must be on acknowledgment of the liability for rent as claimed by the landlord, if accepted would render the provisions in s. 20(6) of the Act nugatory - Appeal dismissed.
