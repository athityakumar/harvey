 Olga Tellis and Others v Bombay Municipal Corporation and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 July 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.V. Chandrachud, A. Varadarajan, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, V.D. Tulzapurkar, O. Chinnappa Reddy

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 525; (1985) 3 SCC 545; AIR 1986 SC 180; 1985 (2) Bom.C.R. 434; 1986 CrLR(SC) 23; 1985(2) SCALE 5; [1985] Supp2 S.C.R. 51

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments; Socio-Economic; Tort

Keywords: Bombay Municipal Act, 1888

Summary: Municipalities & Local Govts - Constitution of India, 1950, Part III (General), art. 14, 16, 19, 21, 226, 32, 39(a) and 41 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 115 - Bombay Municipal Corporation Act, 1888, ss. 314, 312(1) and 313(1)(a) - (A) - Estoppel against or waiver of Fundamental rights, if lies - (B) Maintainability of Writ Petition filed in Supreme Court - Petition challenging removal of huts from pavements - Plea, procedure prescribed u/s. 314 of 1988 Act is arbitrary, unfair and is not 'procedure established by law' within art. 21- (C) Right to life covers right to livelihood - Deprivation of, except in according to just and fair procedure established by law - Whether it can be challenged - (D) Procedure established by law - (1) Must be reasonable and conform to norms of justice and fair play - Statute prescribing procedure impermissible under Constitution - (2) Reasonableness of - Test - (E) Validity of procedure prescribed under Act for removal of encroachments from pavements and public streets - (F) Eviction of pavement dwellers - Normal rule is to provide with opportunity of being heard - Municipal Commissioner can dispense with previous notice in special circumstances - It is not a ground that no useful purpose will be served by giving it - Held, no estoppel against or waiver of - Further held, petition is maintainable - Can be challenged as violative of art. 21- Is liable to be struck down - Depends on facts of each case - Further held, is reasonable - Encroachment on pavements is not criminal trespass and law of torts also requires notice to trespasser before eviction - Order accordingly.
