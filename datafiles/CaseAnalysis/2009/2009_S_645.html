Uma Nath Pandey And Ors v State Of U.P. And Anr

Supreme Court of India

16 March 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 293; (2009) 12 SCC 40; AIR 2009 SC 2375; 2009 (75) ALR 329; 2009 (2) CTC 185; 2009 (2) CTC 663; JT 2009 (4) SC 121; 2009(4) SCALE 347; [2009] 4 S.C.R. 374; 2010 (20) S.T.R. 268

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Revision petition was allowed even without issuing notice to the present appellants and to the other parties - Appeal against - Whether principles of natural justice have been violated? - Held, in the absence of a notice of the kind and such reasonable opportunity, the order passed becomes wholly vitiated - It is but essential that a party should be put on notice of the case before any adverse order is passed against him - Remit the matter to the High Court - Appeal allowed.
