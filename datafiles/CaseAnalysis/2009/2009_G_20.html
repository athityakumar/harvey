State of Karnataka and Others v G. V. Chandrashekar

Supreme Court of India

25 February 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 244; (2009) 4 SCC 342; 2009 (1) CLR 874; 2009 (121) FLR 521; JT 2009 (4) SC 367; 2009 (3) LLJ 45; 2009 (2) LLN 405; 2009 (5) MLJ(SC) 612; 2009(3) SCALE 653; [2009] 3 S.C.R. 624; 2009 (4) SLR 43

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service

Keywords: Irregular Appointment

Summary: Service - Whether the respondents having been appointed on an ad-hoc basis could be treated to have been regularized in their services? - Held, initial recruitment of respondents being wholly illegal and contrary to the constitutional scheme of the country, the impugned judgment of the High Court cannot be upheld - Appeals allowed.
