(1) Pantangi Balarama Venkata Ganesh; (2) State v (1) State of Andhra Pradesh; (2) Vistaria Prakash

Supreme Court of India

23 July 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1091; (2009) 14 SCC 607; AIR 2009 SC 3129; 2009 CRLJ 4144; 2009 (3) JCC 2470; 2010 (5) RCR(Criminal) 217; 2009(10) SCALE 69; [2009] 11 S.C.R. 636

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Fingerprints, Blood Stains, Specimen Handwriting, Fire Arms, Gun Shot Injury

Summary: (A) Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s.302 - Arms Act, 1959, s.25(1-A) - Murder - Conviction - Deceased shot to death by appellant (A1), alleged - Trial Court convicted appellant for murder and offences under Arms Act - HC dismissed A1's appeal - Legality - Held, PW1 was grievously injured at time of recording FIR, hence he was not in a position to reveal minute details - PW1's statement also recorded immediately after FIR, hence he might not have told color of shirt worn by A1 - PW2 was also got injured so he would not be expected to make a statement regarding color of A1's shirt - However, PW2 made such a statement before Investigating Officer (IO) - PW13 categorically stated that MO10 pink color shirt was worn by A1 - A1 was a member of nexalite group and his presence at spot was also proved - PW58 IO has proved collection of blood samples from floor of guest room as well as floor of bath room of deceased - PW58 proved blood samples of PW1 and PW2 - MO10 subjected to DNA finger print examination - PW55 First Class Magistrate proved statement of A1 at hospital - PW46 DNA expert proved blood stains on MO10 and other MOs which were tallied with that of appellant - PW49 ballistic expert proved that bullets recovered from person of PW2 were released from appellant's pistol - Appellant's pistol was not licensed one - Fact of A2's car was used for crime, proved by PW10 and PW11 - Prosecution evidence sufficient to prove appellant's participation in the crime.P /&gt;(B) Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s.302 - Arms Act, s.25(1-A) - Murder - Conviction - Deceased shot to death by respondent (A2), alleged - Trial Court convicted respondent for murder and offences under Arms Act - HC allowed A2's appeal and thereby acquitted him - Legality - Held, A2 was arrested 15 days after occurrence from railway platform - A2 was alleged to be the driver of car used for crime - A2's participation sought to be proved by PW10 and PW11 - PW10 stated that A2 was arrested on same day of incident, however record shown that A2 was arrested after 15 days - PW11 could not identify A2 - No identification parade was held - No recovery was made from A2 - PW9 landlord of A1 stated that she had not seen A2 used to visit A1 in Ambassador car - Therefore, acquittal of A2 by HC was valid - Appeals dismissed
