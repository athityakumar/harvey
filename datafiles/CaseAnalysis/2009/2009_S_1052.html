State of Rajasthan v Yusuf

Supreme Court of India

27 April 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 623; (2009) 12 SCC 139; AIR 2009 SC 2674; 2009 CRLJ 3470; JT 2009 (7) SC 159; 2010 (5) RCR(Criminal) 95; 2009(6) SCALE 449; [2009] 6 S.C.R. 1138

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Demeanour Of Witness, Power Of The Court, Finding Of Facts, Hearing Of Appeal, Match-Box

Summary: Criminal - Evidence Act, 1872 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302 - Dying declaration - Conviction by Trial Court - Acquittal by High Court - Whether proper? - Deceased was wife of accused - Burn injury by pouring kerosene inflicted allegedly by accused - Death due to burn injury - On the basis of dying declaration(DD), Trial Court convicted the accused - HC found several variations in DD and found it to be unreliable and untruthful - HC found consistent improvement in deceased's version in her four statements described as DD and in the statement made u/s. 161 of CrPC - Principles of law on DD deliberated upon - Powers of appellate Court while dealing with an appeal against an order of acquittal, as summed up in 'Chandrappa v. State of Karnataka (2007) (4) SCC 415', noted - Interference uncalled for - Held, acquittal by HC to be proper - Appeal dismissed.
