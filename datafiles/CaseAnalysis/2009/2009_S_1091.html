Ram Pal Singh and Others v State of Uttar Pradesh and Another

Supreme Court of India

13 February 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1908; (2009) 2 SCC (Cr) 326; (2009) 4 SCC 423; AIR 2009 SC 1723; 2009 (2) CGLJ 226; 2009 CRLJ 1553; JT 2009 (2) SC 402; 2009 (2) RCR(Criminal) 131; 2009(2) SCALE 578; [2009] 2 S.C.R. 291

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 319 - Summoning of accused - De facto complainant in the murder case filed application u/s. 319 before Trial Court for summoning the appellants to face trial - Application dismissed - De facto complainant filed revision before HC - HC allowed the revision and directed Trial Court to re-hear the application - Trial Court dismissed the application again - De facto complainant filed second revision before HC - HC sought explanation from Trial Court for the defiance of its order and directed Trial Court to issue summons to appellants - Whether HC order is proper - Held, although the appellants were named in the FIR, they were not named as accused in the charge-sheet during the trial - Despite eye witness naming the appellants as persons who were involved in the murder case, Trial Court rejected de facto complainant's application on two separate occasions - Therefore, HC did not commit any error in directing that the appellants be summoned to stand trial along with the co-accused - Appeal dismissed.
