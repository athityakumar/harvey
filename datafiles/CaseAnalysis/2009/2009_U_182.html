Union of India v Rattan Mallik @ Habul

Supreme Court of India

23 January 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

D.K. Jain, R.M. Lodha

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 2146; (2009) 2 SCC 624; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 1567; 2009 CRLJ 3042; 2009 (1) JCC (Narcotics) 31; JT 2009 (2) SC 121; 2009 (4) MLJ(Crl) 379; 2009 (1) RCR(Criminal) 938; 2009(1) SCALE 51; 2009(2) SCALE 51; [2009] 1 S.C.R. 533; 2009 (1) UJ 346

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 439 - Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985, ss. 8, 27A, 29, 37 and 37(1)(b) - Grant of bail in non-bailable offence - Legality - Respondent was convicted u/ss. 27A and 29 of the NDPS Act - Appeal along with an application for suspension of sentence and grant of bail during pendency of appeal was filed before HC - HC allowed the bail application and suspended the sentence awarded by the Trial Court - Hence, present appeal - Whether HC while accepting the prayer for grant of bail, had kept in view the parameters of s. 37 of the NDPS Act? - Held, when a prosecution/conviction is for offence(s) under a special statute and that statute contains specific provisions for dealing with matters arising thereunder, including an application for grant of bail, these provisions cannot be ignored while dealing with such an application - In the present case, the respondent was convicted and sentenced for offences under the NDPS Act and therefore, while dealing with his application for grant of bail, in addition to the principles to be applied in prosecution for offences under IPC the relevant provision in the said special statute in this regard had to be kept in view - Further, as per non-obstante clause in the s. 37 and s. 37(2) of the NDPS Act power to grant bail to a person accused of having committed offence under the NDPS Act is not only subject to the limitations imposed u/s. 439 of the CrPC, it is also subject to the restrictions placed by s. 37(1)(b) of the NDPS Act - The conditions are (i) the satisfaction of the Court that there are reasonable grounds for believing that the accused is not guilty of the alleged offence; and (ii) that he is not likely to commit any offence while on bail, have to be satisfied - Satisfaction contemplated regarding the accused being not guilty, has to be based on "reasonable grounds" - However, while considering an application for bail with reference to s. 37 of the NDPS Act, the Court is not called upon to record a finding of 'not guilty' - In the present case, impugned order was passed ignoring the mandatory requirements of s. 37 of the NDPS Act, it cannot be sustained - Matter remitted back to the HC for fresh consideration - Appeal allowed.
