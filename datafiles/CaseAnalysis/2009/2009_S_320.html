E.I.C.M. Exports Ltd. v South Indian Corporation (Agencies) Limited and

Supreme Court of India

21 July 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Markandey Katju, V.S. Sirpurkar

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 922; (2009) 14 SCC 412; AIR 2009 SC 3127; 2010 (1) AWC 496; 2009 (1) CCC 415; 2009 (3) CPJ(Sc) 73; 2009 (4) CTC 498; JT 2009 (14) SC 425; 2009(10) SCALE 22; [2009] 11 S.C.R. 275

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection

Summary: Consumer Protection - Consumer Protection Act, 1986, s. 24-A - Negligence - Delay in delivery of consignment - Foreign buyer refused to accept consignment - Negligence against carriage, alleged - Compensation sought - National Commission dismissed the complaint on account of limitation u/art. III cl. 6 of Indian Carriage of Goods by Sea Act, 1925 - Legality - Held, provisions of Sea Carriage Act applicable only in suit - Appellant has not filed any suit but a complaint before Consumer forum - Complaint before Consumer Forum is not a suit, hence, Sea Carriage Act was not applicable - Consumer Protection Act only would applicable - National Commission can entertain a complaint after the limitation period of 2 years u/s. 24-A of Act if it had sufficient cause for delay in filing the same - Therefore, matter remitted back to National Commission to decide the complaint in accordance with s. 24-A of the Act - Appeal allowed.
