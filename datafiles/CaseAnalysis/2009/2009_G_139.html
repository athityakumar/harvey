Jai Singh and Others v Gurmej Singh

Supreme Court of India

19 January 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 49; (2009) 15 SCC 747; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 1570; 2009(2) ALL MR 407; JT 2009 (1) SC 550; 2009 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 557; 2009 (1) RCR(Civil) 874; 2009 (108) RD 156; 2009(1) SCALE 679; [2009] 1 S.C.R. 413

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Keywords: Punjab Pre-Emption Act, 1913

Summary: Civil Procedure - Punjab Pre-emption Act, 1913 - Held, when a co-sharer is in exclusive possession of some portion of the joint holding he is in possession thereof as a co-sharer and is entitled to continue in its possession if it is not more than his share till the joint holding is partitioned - Vendor cannot sell any property with better rights than himself - Sale of subsequent portion of the land out of the joint holding by one of the co-owners is nothing but a sale of a share out of the joint holding and is pre-emptible under s. 15(1)(b) - Appeal dismissed.
