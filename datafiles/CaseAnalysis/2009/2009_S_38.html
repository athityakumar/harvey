Edukanti Kistamma (Dead) Thr. Lrs. and Others v S. Venkatareddy (Dead) Thr. Lrs.And Others

Supreme Court of India

03 December 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1830; (2010) 1 SCC 756; AIR 2010 SC 313; 2010 (2) ALD(SC) 1; JT 2009 (15) SC 48; 2010 (2) MLJ(SC) 713; 2009(14) SCALE 337; [2009] 16 S.C.R. 47

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Ex-Parte, Andhra Pradesh Land Ceiling Act, 1973, Tenancy and Agricultural Lands (Amendment) Act, 1955

Summary: Land & Property - Andhra Pradesh (Telangana Area) Tenancy and Agricultural Lands Act, 1950, s. 38-E - Protected tenants - Respondents purchased suit property from original land owner - Appellants claimed that their predecessor-in-interest was a protected tenant on the said suit property - Respondents filed objection to the issuance of ownership certificate to appellants by Revenue Divisional Officer (RDO) - RDO dismissed the objection - Respondents filed appeal before Appellate Authority on the ground that they had been in possession for the last 50 years - Appellate Authority remanded the matter to RDO - RDO again found that predecessor-in-interest of appellants was a protected tenant - Respondents filed appeal before Appellate Authority - Appeal dismissed - Respondents filed revision before HC - HC allowed the revision by holding that the ownership certificates in favour of the appellants had been issued without any proper appreciation of the material available on record - However, appellants got an order from HC for restoration of possession through another writ proceedings in the meantime - Whether HC's intervention by way of revision in the findings of fact by statutory authority, is proper - Once a protected tenant gets a certificate of ownership u/s. 38-E(2) of the Act, he has a right to apply for restoration of possession to him if he has been dispossessed and has a right to ask for summary eviction of trespasser - Held, HC was not permissible to reopen the issue of grant or issuance of tenancy certificate u/s. 38-E (2) or deal with the issue of restoration of possession either - Order of the writ petition filed by appellants with regard to the issuance of ownership certificate and restoration of possession attained finality - Respondent did not challenge the said HC order in any further proceedings - Further, it was not permissible for the HC to re-open the issue in respect of all the appellants as to whether they were entitled for making the applications for restoration of possession - Impugned HC order set aside - Appeal allowed.
