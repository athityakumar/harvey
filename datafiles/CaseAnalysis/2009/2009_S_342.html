Sripathi and Others v Stae of Karnataka

Supreme Court of India

04 March 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, H.S. Bedi, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1846; (2009) 11 SCC 660; AIR 2010 SC 249; 2009 CRLJ 4646; JT 2009 (7) SC 146; 2009 (6) KarLJ 413; 2009 (2) RCR(Criminal) 273; 2009 (2) RCR(Criminal) 597; 2009(5) SCALE 5; [2009] 5 S.C.R. 309

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 302 and 304 Part II - Applicability of s. 34, IPC - Culpable homicide not amounting to murder - Conviction - Challenged - Trial Court held that prosecution has not been able to establish guilt and acquitted the accused persons - In appeal, HC analyzed the medical evidence and the ocular evidence to conclude that the evidence of the eye witnesses was cogent and trustworthy - HC held that acquittal as recorded was unsustainable and convicted accused persons in terms of s. 304 Part II r/w s. 34, IPC - Hence, present appeal - Held, s. 34, IPC is only a rule of evidence and does not create a substantive offence - In order to bring home the charge of common intention, the prosecution has to establish by evidence, whether direct or circumstantial - In present case, evidence not very specific as regards the role played by accused nos. 1, 2 and 3 - As per prosecution accused no. 4 stabbed the deceased - Conviction of the appellant Nos. 1,2 and 3 by application of s. 34, IPC not proper - Conviction accused no. 4 is well founded and no interference is called for - Order accordingly.
