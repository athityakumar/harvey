State Of Chhatisgarh & Ors v Dhirjo Kumar Sengar

Supreme Court of India

05 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1487; (2009) 13 SCC 600; AIR 2009 SC 2568; 2009(4) ALL MR 987; 2009 (4) AWC 3253; 2010 (2) ESC 236; 2009 (122) FLR 764; 2009(8) SCALE 713; [2009] 7 S.C.R. 1016; 2009 (7) SLR 140

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Family & Personal; Service

Keywords: Conditions For a Valid Adoption, Compassionate Ground, Datta Homa

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872 - Hindu Adoptions and Maintenance Act, 1956 - Compassionate appointment on the basis of adoption - Deceased died in harness - Respondent and his father applied for grant of a succession certificate, which was granted - Thereafter, respondent filed an application for appointment on compassionate ground, which was rejected - Appointment, however, was offered to the respondent which was cancelled thereafter - Respondent filed an application questioning the validity of the said order, which was dismissed - Writ petition filed thereagainst was allowed - HC held that deed of adoption and certificate of succession proved that the petitioner was the adopted son of the deceased - (A) Whether said order justified? - Held, purported deed of adoption was not a registered one - It, therefore, did not carry with it a presumption as envisaged u/s. 16 of the Hindu Adoptions and Maintenance Act, 1956 - Also in terms of s. 106 of the Indian Evidence Act, 1872, the respondent having special knowledge in regard thereto, the burden of proving the fact that he was adopted by deceased was on him and he did not furnish any evidence in that behalf - Further, succession certificate merely enabled him to collect the dues of the deceased - No status was conferred on him thereby - HC committed a serious error in passing the impugned judgment - (B) Principle of natural justice - Held, respondent by suppressing the fact that application for grant of appointment on compassionate ground was rejected by the Joint Director of Education obtained the offer of appointment from an authority which was lower in rank than the Joint Director - Respondent not only committed a fraud on the Department but also committed a fraud on the Constitution - As commission of fraud by him was proved, the principles of natural justice were not required to be complied with - Impugned judgment set aside - Appeal allowed.
