State Of Madhya Pradesh v Sheikh Shahid

Supreme Court of India

15 April 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Lokeshwar Singh Panta, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 489; (2009) 12 SCC 715; AIR 2009 SC 2951; 2010 (1) RCR(Criminal) 220; 2009(5) SCALE 679; [2009] 5 S.C.R. 1038

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Mitigating Factor

Summary: Criminal - IPC, 1860, s. 376 - Whether Single Judge was right in reducing the sentence as imposed on the respondent? - Held, imposition of sentence without considering its effect on the social order in many cases may be in reality a futile exercise - Court must not only keep in view the rights of the criminal but also the rights of the victim of the crime and the society at large while considering the imposition of appropriate punishment - Court has the discretion to impose a sentence of imprisonment less than the prescribed minimum for 'adequate and special reasons' - If the Court does not mention such reasons in the judgment there is no scope for awarding a sentence lesser than the prescribed minimum - Accused belonged to rural areas can by no stretch of imagination be considered either adequate or special - Appeal partly allowed.
