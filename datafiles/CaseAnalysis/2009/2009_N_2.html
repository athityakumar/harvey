(1) Nawab Shaqafath Ali Khan and Others; (2) v (1) Nawab Imdad Jah Bahadur and Others; (2) Nawab

Supreme Court of India

05 March 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 240; (2009) 5 SCC 162; 2009(3) SCALE 934; [2009] 4 S.C.R. 589

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Trusts & Associations

Keywords: Appeals Disposed Of, Principle Of Res Judicata, Nizam's Trust Deeds (Validation) Act, 1950

Summary: Trusts & Associations - Trust Deed executed by Nizam of Hyderabad - Dispute is with regard to the terms 'Remaining sons and Remaining Daughters Fund' expressed in the Will - Held, this trust deed is not an ordinary one - It is a part of a statute - In the case of a wrong interpretation of a statute relating to jurisdiction of a court enabling it to issue a direction, it would amount to a jurisdiction error - In that sense, the courts were required to exercise their jurisdiction with more care and caution - Matters are directed to be considered afresh by the High Court - Appeals disposed of.
