Prithu @ Prithi Chand And Anr. v State Of H.P.

Supreme Court of India

18 February 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 2021; (2009) 11 SCC 588; AIR 2009 SC 2070; JT 2009 (3) SC 440; 2010 (5) RCR(Criminal) 590; 2009(3) SCALE 192; [2009] 2 S.C.R. 765

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 r/w. 34 - Murder - Conviction - Eye-witness - Trial Court did not accept evidence to be credible and directed acquittal - HC noted that evidence clearly established that accused beat deceased with stones due to enmity as a result of Panchayat elections and set aside order of acquittal - Accused pleaded that evidence of eye witnesses could not be accepted as there were omissions, contradictions and discrepancies in evidence of most of prosecution witnesses - Whether HC's order could be upheld? - Held, SC in Bharwada Bhoginbhai Hirjibhai vs. State of Gujarat 1983 INDLAW SC 103, held that undue importance should not be attached to omissions, contradictions and discrepancies which do not go to root of matter and shake basic version of prosecution witnesses - Witness cannot be accepted to possess a photographic memory and to recall deals of an incident verbatim - Minor discrepancies were bound to occur in statement of witnesses - HC analysed the evidence in aforesaid background and has rightly come to conclusion that guilt of accused persons has been established - Appeal dismissed
