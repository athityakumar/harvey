State Of Orissa And Others v Chitrasen Bhoi

Supreme Court of India

16 September 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Dalveer Bhandari, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1170; (2009) 17 SCC 74; AIR 2010 SC (Supp) 397; 2010 (2) ALD(SC) 74; 2010 (2) AWC 1273; JT 2009 (13) SC 388; 2010 (1) RCR(Civil) 145; 2010 (109) RD 297; 2009(13) SCALE 36; [2009] 14 S.C.R. 558

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, s. 28A - Land acquisition - Award made by Land Acquisition Collector (LAC) - 1st respondent landowner not agitated award - Other landowners referred award to Reference Court - Market value increased by Reference Court - Application u/s. 28A, claiming the increased market value, filed by 1st respondent - Rejection of application by LAC - Writ Petition filed by 1st respondent - Writ Petition allowed by HC, directing LAC to decide 1st respondent's application u/s. 28A - Whether application u/s.28A barred by limitation - Held, application filed by 1st respondent within 3 months from date of reference award - Factual position of limitation not laid down by appellant - Contention by appellant that LAC bound to enhance compensation without considering any other facts, is preposterous - Matter remitted back to LAC - Directed LAC to consider issue of maintainability of 1st respondent's application u/s. 28A without going to the issue of limitation - Appeal dismissed.
