(1) Pawan; (2) Arjun; (3) Aamir; (4) Babu v State of Uttaranchal

Supreme Court of India

26 February 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, S.B. Sinha, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 222; (2009) 15 SCC 259; 2009 CRLJ 2257; JT 2009 (3) SC 87; 2009 (3) MLJ(Crl) 212; 2009 (2) RCR(Criminal) 451; 2009(3) SCALE 195; [2009] 3 S.C.R. 468; 2009 (1) UC 684

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - IPC, 1860, ss. 302/34, 376 and 201/34 - Appeal against conviction and sentence - Held, while evaluating circumstantial evidence, which of course has to be done carefully, the circumstances must be of such a nature as to be capable of supporting the exclusive hypothesis that the accused is guilty of the crime of which he is charged and the circumstances so shown by the prosecution are compatible with no other reasonable hypothesis - If the plea of juvenility was not raised before the trial court or the High Court and is raised for the first time before Supreme Court, the judicial conscience of the court must be satisfied by placing adequate and satisfactory material that the accused had not attained age of eighteen years on the date of commission of offence; sans such material any further enquiry into juvenility would be unnecessary - Appeals dismissed.
