Shakuntala Devi and Others v Chamru Mahto and Another

Supreme Court of India

10 February 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 123; (2009) 2 SCC (Cr) 8; (2009) 3 SCC 310; AIR 2009 SC 2075; 2009 ALL MR (Cri) 1535; 2009 CRLJ 1770; 2009 (2) CTC 242; JT 2009 (3) SC 502; 2009 (2) RCR(Criminal) 125; 2009(2) SCALE 613; [2009] 3 S.C.R. 85

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Civil Action

Summary: Criminal - CrPC, 1973, s. 145 - Appeal against order directing restoration of possession of the land in dispute to the respondent by High Court - Whether the High Court was right in quashing the order passed by the Magistrate, which was confirmed by the Sessions Judge, on the ground that the application made by the appellants under s. 145(6) of the Code was barred firstly by limitation under art. 137 of the Limitation Act and also by virtue of s. 6 of the Specific Relief Act, 1963? - Held, power of the High Court to entertain a petition under Section 482, was not subject to the prohibition under s. 397(3) of the Code, and was capable of being invoked in appropriate cases - Provisions of the Specific Relief Act had been misapplied by the High Court in holding that the appellants should have come for an order under s. 145(6) of the Code within six months from the date of dispossession, as provided in s. 6 of the said Act, as the Specific Relief Act has no application to a proceeding under s. 145 Cr.P.C - Bar under art. 137 of the Limitation Act cannot be avoided and the application made by the appellants for being restored to possession in terms of a declaration made more than three years before the making of the application has to be rejected - Appeal dismissed.
