Girijan Co-Op. Corporation Limited, Andhra Pradesh v K. Satyanarayana Rao

Supreme Court of India

24 July 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Deepak Verma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1729; (2010) 15 SCC 322

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O.41 r. 27 - Departmental Enquiry - Superannuation - Resolution - Justifiability - Departmental proceedings were initiated against respondents, who were employees of appellant-corporation and had reached age of superannuation - Appellant/corporation passed resolution - Respondents filed petition before HC, which held that no disciplinary proceeding could be maintained subsequent to retirement of respondents/employees - Appellants filed appeal before DB, which affirmed order of HC - Hence, instant appeals - Appellant contended that having regard to bye-laws of appellant-corporation, powers were vested in Board of Directors and Managing Director was merely to follow rules which were adopted by resolution of Board of Directors and he did not had any independent power to adopt or accept same - Whether order of DB was justifiable - Held, in instant case documents where upon reliance was now sought to be placed, along with affidavit were additional documents which could be admitted by SC in terms of O.41 r. 27 CPC - There was absolutely no reason as to why authorities of appellant did not brought same to notice of HC, they being within power and possession of e Corporation - Instant case does not satisfied parameters laid down, required for exercising jurisdiction under O.21 r.27 CPC - Further, word 'adopt' had been used in circular, does not lead to conclusion that same had been used in sense of following rules as word 'adopt' had been preceded by the words 'shall have power to' - There could not be any doubt or dispute that an employer could initiate a departmental proceedings and/or continue same only in terms of rules framed by it - It well settled law that disciplinary proceedings were initiated only when a charge- sheet was issued - Therefore, in absence of any rules, a disciplinary proceeding against a retired employee could not had been continued - Appeals dismissed.
