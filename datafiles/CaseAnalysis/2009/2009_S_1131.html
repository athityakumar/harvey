State of Uttar Pradesh v Santosh Kumar

Supreme Court of India

03 September 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Dalveer Bhandari, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1102; (2009) 9 SCC 626; 2009 ALL MR (Cri) 3116; 2009 (4) JCC 2661; 2010 (1) MLJ(Crl) 679; 2009(12) SCALE 269; [2009] 14 S.C.R. 106; 2010 (1) UC 435

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss.34, 302, 304-B and 498-A - Dowry Prohibition Act, 1961, ss.3 and 4 - Murder - Appeal against acquittal - Respondent and other accused were charged u/ss. 34, 302, 304-B and 498-A, IPC and ss.3 and 4 of Dowry Act - Trial Court convicted respondent u/ss.302 and 498-A, IPC and ss.3 and 4 of Dowry Act but acquitted them u/s.304-B, IPC - On appeal, HC set aside conviction imposed by Trial Court - Hence, present appeal - (A) Whether finding of HC that once charge u/s.304-B, IPC could not be proved, then conviction u/s.498-A, IPC and ss.3 and 4 of Dowry Act also cannot be recorded, correct? - Held, finding of HC palpably wrong and unsustainable - Ingredients of s.498-A, IPC and ss.3 and 4 of Dowry Act are different from ingredients of s.304-B IPC - Ambit and scope of ss.3 and 4 of Dowry Act is different from ambit and scope of s.498-A, IPC - Demand of dowry is an essential ingredient to Dowry Act, s.304-B IPC, whereas u/s.498-A IPC demand of dowry is not basic ingredient of offence - A person charged and acquitted u/s.304-B, IPC can be convicted u/s.498-A IPC - (B) Dying declaration - Reliability - Entire prosecution case hinges on three dying declarations made by deceased - Held, all three dying declarations made by deceased are totally consistent and lead to only one conclusion that respondent had poured kerosene oil on deceased and lit fire - Act is clearly corroborated from testimonies of PW1, Investigating Officer and Tehsildar/Magistrate PW13 - Respondent clearly guilty of offence u/s.302 IPC - Impugned judgment of HC cannot be sustained and that of Trial Court restored as far as sentence of respondent u/s.302 IPC is concerned - Appeal disposed of.
