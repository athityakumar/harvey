Sumesh Singh v Phoolan Devi & Ors

Supreme Court of India

15 April 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 458; (2009) 12 SCC 689; AIR 2009 SC 2831; 2009 (75) ALR 789; 2009 (3) AWC 2755; 2009(6) SCALE 342; [2009] 5 S.C.R. 996

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Keywords: Code of Civil Procedure (Amendment) Act, 2002

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. VI r. 17 - Amendment of pleadings - Written statement - Land in question was sold by original defendant 2(D2) in pursuance of power of attorney(POA) alleged to have been executed by her father, the original plaintiff(OP) - OP filed suit for declaration on ground that POA was illegal being obtained fraudulently - In the meanwhile appellant herein filed an application for partition on basis of such sale deed which was executed in his favour - Written statement filed by D2 raising plea that suit filed by OP be dismissed - Death of OP pending suit - LRs brought in - Undisputedly D2 was also one of heir and LR of OP - Amendment of plaint was sought and allowed - Amendment of WS also sought by D2, which was also allowed - Allowing of WS, challenged - Ground of challenge, D2 in her original WS prayed for dismissal of suit and through amendment sought to take plea that suit filed by her father be decreed, so such turn round should not be permitted - Allowing of amendment of WS, whether proper? - Ordinarily, an amendment of pleadings should not be allowed by reason whereof a party to the suit would resile from the admission made by him in the same proceedings at an earlier stage - Gautam Sarup v. Leela Jetly, 2008 - In instant case averments in plaint were merely denied and there was no categorical or unequivocal admission as such, thus present is not the case where a party to the suit is resiling from his statement made in the earlier part of the proceedings - Held, no interference called for in order allowing amendment of WS - Appeal dismissal.
