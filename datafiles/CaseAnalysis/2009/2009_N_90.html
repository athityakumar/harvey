National Insurance Company Limited v Meena Aggarwal

Supreme Court of India

23 January 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 58; (2009) 2 SCC 523; 2009 (4) ACC 459; 2009 (2) AWC 1634; 2009 (4) CPJ(Sc) 25; JT 2009 (1) SC 612; 2009 (4) MahLJ 20; 2009 (3) MPLJ 309; 2009 (153) PLR 512; 2009 (1) RCR(Civil) 877; 2009(2) SCALE 8; [2009] 1 S.C.R. 492

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance

Keywords: Breach Of Condition, Definition Clause, Subject Matter Of Insurance, Cause Of Accident, Intervening Cause

Summary: Insurance - National Commission held that even though the vehicle was being used as a commercial vehicle and the driver did not have a valid driving licence, there was no fundamental breach of the terms of the policy - Appeal against - Held, owner of a vehicle cannot contend that he has no liability to verify the fact as to whether the driver of the vehicle possessed a valid licence or not - State Commission and the National Commission have not practically indicated any reason for coming to the conclusion that there was no fundamental breach of the terms of the policy - Appeal allowed.
