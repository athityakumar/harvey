Subhash Soni and Another v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

06 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 629; (2009) 3 SCC (Cr) 291; (2009) 6 SCC 647; JT 2009 (7) SC 482; 2010 (5) RCR(Criminal) 610; 2009(7) SCALE 222; [2009] 8 S.C.R. 136

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Police Record

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 r/w 34 - Murder - Dying declaration - Conviction by Trial Court - Confirmation by High Court - Whether proper? - Two eye witnesses and dying declaration - Enmity due to civil litigation from side of deceased - While deceased was taken to the hospital Doctor witness (PW4) overheard deceased saying to PW 1 about the assailants - Evidence of PW4 doctor relied upon, as an independent witness by the Trial Court and HC; injuries were sustained on thigh and leg and thus it cannot be said that deceased could not have spoken to PW 1 or that deceased was in an unconscious condition - Findings by Trial Court and HC, upheld - Held, impugned judgement to be proper - Appeal dismissed.
