Kaliaperumal v Rajagopal and Another

Supreme Court of India

20 February 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Panchal, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 812; (2009) 4 SCC 193; AIR 2009 SC 2122; 2009(3) ALL MR 984; 2009 (75) ALR 301; 2009 (3) AWC 2414; 2009 (6) CTC 741; JT 2009 (7) SC 124; 2009 (4) MahLJ 706; 2009 (6) MLJ(SC) 601; 2009 (3) MPLJ 595; 2009 (2) RCR(Civil) 471; 2009 (107) RD 63; 2009(4) SCALE 60; [2009] 2 S.C.R. 814

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Land & Property

Keywords: Mode Of Transfer

Summary: Land & Property - Transfer of Property Act, 1882 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 100 - Transfer of title - Intention of parties - Sale deed was executed between appellant and 1st and 2nd respondent - Appellant presented document for registration - 1st respondent stated that they were not paid the remaining consideration as stipulated in the deed - Sub-Registrar registered the document but recorded that no amount was tendered or paid by the purchaser to the vendors in his presence - Appellant filed suit for declaration of title to the suit properties and possession thereof - Subordinate Judge passed a decree in favour of appellant and same was affirmed District Judge - Respondents filed second appeal before HC - HC was of the view that title to the suit properties never passed to the appellant as he had not paid full consideration to the respondents and set aside the decree - Hence, present appeal - Whether title to the disputed properties passed to the appellant when the sale deed was registered, though admittedly no amount was paid towards consideration to the respondents? - Held, true test of passing of property is the intention of parties - Though registration is prima facie proof of an intention to transfer the property, it is not proof of operative transfer if payment of consideration (price) is a condition precedent for passing of the property - In instant case, parties intended that ownership of the property would be transferred to the appellant only after receipt of the entire consideration by the vendors, as a condition precedent - Parties did not intend that there should be transfer of ownership merely on execution and registration of the deed - Therefore title in fact did not pass either on execution or registration of the sale deed - HC rightly set aside the decree passed in favour of the appellant and dismissed the suit - Appeal dismissed.
