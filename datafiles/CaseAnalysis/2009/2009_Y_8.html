Yerneni Raja Ramchander @ Rajababu v State of Andhra Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 August 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Deepak Verma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1086; (2009) 15 SCC 604; 2009 ALL MR (Cri) 3761; 2009 (4) JCC 3036; JT 2009 (12) SC 198; 2009 (3) RCR(Criminal) 956; 2009(10) SCALE 607; [2009] 12 S.C.R. 494

Case Digest

Subject: Parliament & Legislature

Summary: Parliament & Legislature - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s.321 - Withdrawal from prosecution - Doctrine of 'malice in law' - Appellant, an MLA claimed bogus medical re-imbursements - Criminal case was pending before Trial Court - Respondent nos. 3 and 4 levelled charges of misappropriation against appellant on premise that he had obtained a huge amount on basis of false and fabricated medical bills - Matter referred to Ethics Committee - Apology was tendered before Ethics Committee, which was accepted; amount in question was refunded to Govt. - Ethics Committee recommended for withdrawal of criminal case pending against appellant - Govt. issued respective GO directing District Collector to direct Public Prosecutor (PP) to withdraw case - PP filed application u/s. 321 of CrPC before Trial Court - Application was dismissed - In the meanwhile writ petition was preferred by respondent no. 3 and 4 against respondent/State, attacking undue protection sought to be given to appellant by issuing GOs - Writ was allowed - Respective GO was issued in terms of recommendations of Ethics Committee - Whether Ethics Committee had power to make recommendations which it had made? - Held, no - Whether GOs issued by Govt., legal? - Held, no - If State had acted on basis of recommendations made by a body (Ethics Committee) who had no role to play, its action would be vitiated in law; recommendations of Ethics Committee being unauthorised, action of State would attract doctrine of malice in law - State is protector of law; when it deals with public fund, it must act in terms of procedure established by law; doctrine of public trust would also be applicable; Govt. could not except for very strong and cogent reasons have issued GO in question - Impugned judgment of HC allowing writ petition against GO, held to be unassailable - Direction issued to Trial Court to proceed with matter independently - Appeals dismissed.
