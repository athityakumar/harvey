Anil Kumar Jain v Maya Jain

Supreme Court of India

01 September 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1494; (2009) 10 SCC 415; AIR 2010 SC 229; 2009 (4) AWC 3635; 2010 (2) Bom.C.R. 774; 2009 (5) CTC 467; 2010 (2) JhrCR(SC) 183; JT 2009 (14) SC 139; 2009 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 3316; 2009 (6) Law Herald (P&H) 3892; 2009 (8) MLJ(SC) 405; 2010 (157) PLR 248; 2009 (4) RCR(Civil) 310; 2009(12) SCALE 115; [2009] 14 S.C.R. 90; 2010 (1) WLN(SC) 26

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Summary: Family & Personal - Hindu Marriage Act, 1955, s. 13-B - Constitution of India, art. 142 - Marriage Laws (Amendment) Act, 1976 - Mutual divorce - Whether a decree can be passed on a petition for mutual divorce u/s 13-B of Hindu Marriage Act, 1955, when one of petitioners withdraws consent to such decree prior to passing of such decree? - Under existing laws, consent given by parties at time of filing of joint petition for divorce by mutual consent has to subsist till second stage when petition comes up for orders and a decree for divorce is finally passed and it is only SC, which, in exercise of its extraordinary powers u/art. 142 of Constitution can pass orders to do complete justice to parties - Held, present case is a fit case where powers vested u/art. 142 of Constitution should be exercised - Stand of respondent-wife that she wants to live separately from her husband but is not agreeable to a mutual divorce not acceptable - Living separately is one of grounds for grant of a mutual divorce and admittedly parties are living separately for more than seven years hence, petition for grant of mutual divorce u/s. 13-B of Hindu Marriage Act, 1955 accepted - Appeal allowed.
