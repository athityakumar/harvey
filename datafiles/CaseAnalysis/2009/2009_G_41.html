Gangai Vinayagar Temple and Others v Meenakashi Ammal and Others

Supreme Court of India

03 September 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Markandey Katju, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1081; (2009) 9 SCC 757; JT 2009 (12) SC 167; 2009 (4) RCR(Civil) 696; 2010 (109) RD 420A; 2009(12) SCALE 215; [2009] 13 S.C.R. 1209

Case Digest

Subject: Trusts & Associations

Keywords: Principle Of Res Judicata

Summary: Trusts & Associations - Plea of res judicata - Sustainability - Suit property of temple trust leased to respondents - Respondents defaulted in paying rent - Suit for recovery of arrears of rent filed by appellant trustees - Meanwhile, appellant sold suit property to third parties - Another suit filed by respondent for non-interference of possession - Trial Court in its common judgment held that respondent was entitled to retain possession till the expiry of lease by relying on the statement of appellants in their written statement - However, suit of appellant was dismissed by Trial Court - No appeal filed by appellant - Respondents filed appeal before HC - Plea of res judicata raised by appellant - Single Judge held that temple was a public temple, therefore, trustees could not have alienated suit property without requisite permission - Appeal of appellant before DB was also dismissed - Legality - Held, appellants had conceded that they were not going to forcibly evict respondents - Question of title was unnecessarily determined by Trial Court - Trial Court should have straightaway dismissed suit - No merit - Appeal dismissed - Asok Kumar Ganguly, J. (contra) - Held, public character of temple was raised in plaint and controverted in written statement at Trial Court itself - Issue relating to nature of property and its title had to be framed by Trial Court - Unless title of trustees was determined, dismissal of suit could not be reached - Assurance of non-interference of possession, did not mean that title of trustees would not become a substantial issue - Therefore, whether appellant had title to validly transfer suit property was inextricably connected with ultimate decision of Trial Court - Finding of Trial Court can be modified even in absence of proper appeal - Neither Single Judge nor DB has taken correct view - Appeal allowed.
