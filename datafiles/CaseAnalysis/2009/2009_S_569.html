Harbhajan Singh & Anr. v State Of Punjab & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

29 July 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 933; (2009) 13 SCC 608; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 1977; 2009 CRLJ 4429; 2009 (4) JCC 2580; JT 2009 (12) SC 428; 2009 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 2518; 2010 (1) MLJ(Crl) 1260; 2009 (3) RCR(Criminal) 916; 2009(10) SCALE 349; [2009] 11 S.C.R. 1015

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Obscene

Summary: (A) Criminal - CrPC, 1973, s. 319 - Implication of person as accused at pre-trial stage - Jurisdiction - Only because of no charge-sheet has been submitted against certain persons, the court would not decline to exercise its jurisdiction to add other persons as accused for trying an offence which appears to have committed by them.P /&gt;(B) Criminal - CrPC, 1973, s. 319 - Application for adding additional accused persons - Allowed by the Sessions Court - Revision against - HC dismissed revision - Legality - Deceased in her dying declaration named appellant also as accused - Correctness of dying declaration cannot be questioned at the pre-trial stage - Statements made by prosecution witness showed that appellants also partake the commission of an offence - Evidences disclosed some offence from the side of appellant - Court cannot wait till the cross-examination of PWs are over for implicating a person - Court exercised the jurisdiction u/s. 319 on the basis of available materials - No interference - Appeal dismissed.
