Dr. Ram Deen Maurya v State Of U.P. And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

17 April 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Dattu, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 2020; (2009) 6 SCC 735; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 1719; 2009(6) SCALE 189; [2009] 6 S.C.R. 703; 2009 (2) UPLBEC 1315

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: U.P. Higher Education Services Commission Act, 1980, State Universities Act, 1973, Uttar Pradesh Aided College Transfer of Teachers Rules, 2005

Summary: Service - U.P. Higher Education Services Commission Act, 1980 - Uttar Pradesh Aided College Transfer of Teachers Rules, 2005 - Transfer from one college to another - Permission - Appellant and Respondent No. 5 both wanted to be transferred to D.A.V. Post Graduate College, Lucknow - State Government accorded permission for transfer of appellant - Later respondent no. 5 made an application transfer to D.A.V. Post Graduate College which was granted and Director of Education, forwarded his recommendation to Secretary, Allahabad Education Department - Secretary, Government of Uttar Pradesh, accorded permission for transfer of petitioner from A.P.N College to D.A.V. Post Graduate College - Respondent No. 5 filed writ petition against said order - HC while allowing writ petition, quashed order passed by State Government, wherein and whereunder State Government had granted permission for transfer of petitioner - Hence, present appeals - Whether HC's order could be allowed? - Held, D.A.V. Post Graduate College, Lucknow is affiliated to Lucknow University, Lucknow - Provisions of State Universities Act, 1973, and provisions of U.P. Higher Education Services Commission Act, 1980 are made applicable to teachers of said College - By U.P. Ordinance No.14 of 2004, s. 2 of Act, 1980 is amended and a provision for transfer of teachers/lecturers from one aided degree College to another aided degree College, on fulfillment of certain conditions is provided therein - Rules are framed to give effect to amended provisions and they are known as Uttar Pradesh Aided College Transfer of Teachers Rules, 2005 - Findings of HC, cannot be criticized as perverse findings - HC's order upheld - Appeals dismissed.
