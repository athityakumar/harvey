Oriental Insurance Company Limited v Angad Kol and Others

Supreme Court of India

18 February 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, V.S. Sirpurkar

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 197; (2009) 11 SCC 356; 2009 (2) ACC 313; AIR 2009 SC 2151; 2009 (3) AWC 2924; 2010 (2) JhrCR(SC) 174; JT 2009 (2) SC 587; 2009 (154) PLR 25; 2009 (2) RCR(Civil) 419; 2009(3) SCALE 749; [2009] 2 S.C.R. 695

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Cross Objection, Breach Of Condition, Licence Holder, Application Form, Driver Had No Licence

Summary: Insurance - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 - Held, finding of fact arrived at that the vehicle in question was not proved to be a goods vehicle is not correct - It is proved that respondent No.6 did not hold a valid and effective driving licence for driving a goods vehicle - Breach of conditions of the insurance is, therefore, apparent on the face of the records - Directed the insurance company to deposit the balance amount before the Tribunal with liberty to the claimants to withdraw the same give right to the appellant to recover the said amount from the owner and the driver of the vehicle being Respondent Nos.6 and 7 - Appeal allowed.
