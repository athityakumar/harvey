Bihari Lal Rada v Anil Jain (Tinu) and others

Supreme Court of India

13 February 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

B. SUDARSHAN REDDY, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 2170; (2009) 4 SCC 1; JT 2009 (2) SC 455; 2009 (1) RCR(Civil) 944B; 2009(2) SCALE 559; [2009] 3 S.C.R. 251

Case Digest

Subject: Election; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Scheduled Caste, Gujarat Municipal Act, Haryana Municipal Elections Rules, 1978

Summary: Municipalities & Local Governments - Election - Constitution of Indian, 1950, art. 243T - Haryana Municipal Act, 1973, s. 10(5) - Haryana Municipal Elections Rules, 1978 - Election to post of President - Reservation of seats - Whether the provisions of the Act and the Rules framed thereunder put any embargo on the right of the Municipal Councilors elected from the reserved wards to contest election for the office of President Municipality if by virtue of roaster such office is notified to be filled in by the members belonging to the general category? - It was submitted that all members of the Municipality, irrespective of the fact that they have been elected from reserved wards, are eligible to contest to the office of the President/Chairperson when it falls in the general category - Held, neither art. 243T of the Constitution nor s. 10(5) of the Act provide for any reservation to the office of the President in favour of any candidate who does not belong to Scheduled Caste or Backward Class - There is nothing in the provisions of the Act suggesting that in case the office of the President of a Municipality is required to be filled in from the members belonging to the general category then only a member who was elected from an unreserved ward alone can stand for election - Furthermore, there is nothing in law that a person belonging to Backward Class and got himself elected from a ward reserved for that class is debarred from contesting the election to the office of President/Chairperson when that office is not reserved and meant to be filled in from the members belonging to the general category - Therefore, wherever the office of the President of a Municipality is required to be filled in by a member belonging to Scheduled Caste, Scheduled Tribe or Backward Class as the case may be it would be enough if one belongs to one of those categories irrespective of the fact whether they have been elected from a general ward or a reserved ward - Likewise, the office of the President of a Municipality if not reserved or meant for general category, all the candidates irrespective of their caste, class or community and irrespective of the fact whether they have been elected from a reserved ward or a general ward are entitled to seek election and contest to the office of the President of the Municipality - Appeal allowed.
