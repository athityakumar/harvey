Seth Ramdayal Jat v Laxmi Prasad

Supreme Court of India

15 April 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 472; (2009) 11 SCC 545; AIR 2009 SC 2463; 2009 (75) ALR 658; 2009 (4) AWC 3673; 2009 (4) CTC 295; 2009 (108) RD 145; 2009 (4) RLW 3579; 2009(5) SCALE 527; [2009] 7 S.C.R. 17

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Keywords: Madhya Pradesh Money Lenders Act, 1934

Summary: Civil Procedure - Limitation Act, 1963, art. 70 - Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 43 and 58 - For institution of suit for recovery of 'pledged ornaments', what would be the period of limitation? - Respondent brother of appellant, took certain loan by pledging ornaments - Transaction undocumented - Loan repaid but ornaments not returned - Civil suit filed for ornaments- Criminal prosecution of appellant under MP Money Lenders Act for contravention of its provision in respect of loan given to respondent; admission of guilt by appellant - Fact of case, suit was filed within 3 years from date of demand of ornaments and refusal by appellant - (A) Whether admission of guilt in criminal case in respect of some transaction made by appellant is admissible in the instant civil case to the extent of fact that there was transaction between the parties? - (B) Whether the present suit barred by limitation? - (A) Decided cases on the subject referred to - Submissions heard - Provisions perused, esp. s. 43 and 58 of Evidence Act - Relevant facts noted - Position well settled, judgement of criminal court shall not be admissible in a civil suit, save and except for s. 43 of Evidence Act - Per s. 43, judgement in a criminal case shall be admissible provided it is a relevant fact in issue - Thus the thing which is admissible is admission made by a party in a previous proceeding - In instant case while appellant was deposing in the suit, he was confronted with the question as to whether he had admitted his guilt and pleaded guilty of the charges framed, for which he had answered in the affirmative - Such admission in civil suit, admissible in evidence - Held, admission in civil proceeding as to admission of guilt in a criminal proceeding, admissible in evidence - Per s. 58 'things admitted need not be proved' - Finding of HC on the point, upheld - (B) Art. 70 of Limitation Act, perused - Period of limitation began to run from the date of refusal after demand, and it is 3 years - Relevant facts on point noted - Held, suit filed within limitation period - Appeal dismissed.
