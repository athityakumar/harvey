Union of India and Others v Dalbir Singh and Another

Supreme Court of India

08 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Dattu, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1292; (2009) 7 SCC 251; AIR 2009 SC 2438; 2009 (4) AWC 3421; 2009 (122) FLR 454; 2009(7) SCALE 69; 2009 (7) SLR 233

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Service - Administrative - Reserved category - Appointment - Validity - Appellants advertised for selection to 20 vacant posts of Mazdoor both in general and reserved category - Respondent No. 1 applied for selection in reserved (OBC) category - Separate Board Proceedings was prepared for candidates who had claimed concession on basis of OBC certificate and name of respondent No. 1 was also in said proceedings - Authenticity of OBC certificate was sought from issuing authority - It was intimated by SDO(C) vide their letter that certificate was not issued by their office - Respondent No.1 produced another certificate issued by SDO(C) in which respondent No.1 was shown to be of 'Saini' caste and later on it was found that 'Saini' caste was not in OBC category - Candidature of respondent No.1 was rejected by appellants - Aggrieved respondent filed O.A before Tribunal- Tribunal accepting assertion made by appellants regarding non-suitability of certificates furnished by respondent directed appellants to issue letter of appointment to respondent by considering his candidature in general category as he had scored more marks then one of candidate in general category - Against this decision appellants filed writ petition before HC and HC vide its order had dismissed writ petition - Hence instant appeal - Whether Tribunal was justified in directing appellants to appoint respondent No. 1 as selected candidate in general category even though he had not applied under that category - Held, respondent at no point of time had claimed before authorities that if for any reason, his case could not be considered under OBC category, at least appellants should consider his case under general merit list - Respondent at no point of time had taken exception to procedure adopted by appellants in preparing select list - Court further held that having opted to consider respondents case only under OBC category, he could not thereafter claim that his case requires to be considered in general merit, only because, he had scored better percentage of marks than last selected candidate in general merit - Hence, Tribunal having found that appellants were justified in not considering respondent's case for appointment under OBC category, ought not to have directed appellants to consider his claim under general category - Appeal allowed.
