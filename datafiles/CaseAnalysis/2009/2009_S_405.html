State Of Bihar v Upendra Narayan Singh & Others

Supreme Court of India

20 March 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 420; (2009) 5 SCC 65; 2009 (2) ESC 287; JT 2009 (4) SC 577; 2009 (2) LLN 754; 2009 (5) MLJ(SC) 39; 2009(4) SCALE 282; [2009] 4 S.C.R. 866

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service

Keywords: Affirmative Action, Reduction In Rank, Ad Hoc Appointee, Public Undertakings, Irregular Appointment, Services Under The Union And The States, Irregular Appointees, Continuance In Service, Civil Service Act, 1883

Summary: Service - Constitution - Ad hoc appointments - Legality of respondents' initial appointment - Whether the High Court was justified in directing reinstatement of the respondents with consequential benefits ignoring that their initial appointments were ex facie illegal inasmuch as the concerned authority did not follow any procedure consistent with the doctrine of equality enshrined in arts. 14 and 16? - Held, initial appointments of the respondents were made in gross violation of the doctrine of equality enshrined in arts. 14 and 16 - If the initial appointments of the respondents are found to be illegal per se, the direction given by the High Court for their reinstatement with consequential benefits cannot be approved by relying upon the regularization of their services - Appeal allowed.
