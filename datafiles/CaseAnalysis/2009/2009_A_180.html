M/S. Becil v Arraycom India Ltd. & Others

Supreme Court of India

20 October 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Markandey Katju, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1448; (2010) 1 SCC 139; 2010 (78) ALR 238; 2010 (3) AWC 2328; 2009 (163) DLT 764; JT 2009 (13) SC 331; 2010 (157) PLR 398; 2010 (1) RCR(Civil) 50; 2009(13) SCALE 322; [2009] 15 S.C.R. 277; [2010] 29 VST 555

Case Digest

Subject: Contract & Commercial

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Interpretation of bid document - Respondent No.2 issued invited tender for supply of two transmitters - Respondent No.1 and appellant were both technically qualified - Appellant's bid was lowest bid and contract was given to him - Respondent No.1 filed writ petition thereagainst, which was allowed - Hence, present appeal - Held, paragraph 2 of respondent No.1 bid has two interpretations (i) it is an inclusive bid; and (ii) that sales tax can be added to that bid - Respondent No.2, who has to make payment, has taken second interpretation which, is a reasonable and possible interpretation - If respondent No.2 has taken up one possible interpretation, HC should not have intervened, as scope of judicial review in administrative matters is limited - Referred Tata Cellular vs. Union of India 1994 INDLAW SC 17 - Appeal allowed.
