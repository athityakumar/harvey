Sonu Babu Bhambid & Ors. v Dream Developers & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

21 July 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Deepak Verma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 923; (2009) 17 SCC 124; 2009 (76) ALR 636; JT 2009 (13) SC 397; 2009(9) SCALE 754; [2009] 10 S.C.R. 1176

Case Digest

Subject: Socio-Economic

Keywords: Grant Of Injunction, Development Control Regulations, 1991

Summary: Socio-Economic - Rehabilitation of slum dwellers - Agreement entered into between appellants-slum dwellers and respondents-developers - Failure of getting eligibility certificate as a condition of agreement - Trial Court held that appellants failed to obtain the essentiality certificate and area in dispute was not declared as slum area - HC affirmed the Trial Court's judgment - Validity - Held, appellants were trespassers and acquired title by possession for more than 30 years - Rehabilitation scheme applicable only to declared slum areas - Appellants failed to get the eligibility certificate from local administration to declare the area as slum area - Cl.3 of agreement categorically provides that failure to obtain the certificate would cause to end the agreement - Therefore, agreement in question would ipso facto came to end, hence, rehabilitation of appellants would not arise - No merit - Appeal dismissed.
