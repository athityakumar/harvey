S. P. Devaraju v State of Karnataka

Supreme Court of India

12 February 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 156; (2009) 12 SCC 676; AIR 2009 SC 1725; JT 2009 (2) SC 408; 2009 (3) KarLJ 177; 2010 (5) RCR(Criminal) 467; 2009(2) SCALE 572; [2009] 2 S.C.R. 308

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - IPC, 1860, s. 304 part II - Appeal against judgment of a Division Bench of High Court allowing the State's appeal and setting aside the judgment of acquittal - Held, case where the basis of conviction of the accused is the dying declaration - Situation in which a person is on the deathbed is so solemn and serene when he is dying that the grave position in which he is placed, is the reason in law to accept the veracity of his statement - Once the court is satisfied that the declaration was true and voluntary, undoubtedly, it can base its conviction on the same without any further corroboration - It cannot be laid down as an absolute rule of law that the dying declaration cannot form the sole basis of conviction unless it is corroborated - There is no material to show that the dying declaration was the result or product of imagination, tutoring or prompting - It is trustworthy and has credibility - Appeal dismissed.
