Bhagyalakshmi And Others v United Insurance Co. Ltd. And Another Etc

Supreme Court of India

06 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 742; (2009) 7 SCC 148; (2009) 3 SCC (Cr) 321; 2009 (76) ALR 822; 2009 (3) AWC 3000; JT 2009 (9) SC 329; 2009 (156) PLR 760; 2010 (2) RCR(Civil) 384; 2009(7) SCALE 550; [2009] 7 S.C.R. 1031

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance

Keywords: Insurer's Liability, Amount Of Premium, Comprehensive Policy, Course Of Employment, In The Course Of Employment, Damage To Property, Third Party Risk, Owner Of Vehicle, Registered Owner, Gratuitous Passenger, Extra Premium, Premium Paid, Carried For Hire Or Reward, Matter referred to Larger Bench, Death Or Injury, Refund Of Premium, Cross-Objections, Wear And Tear, Liability To Third Party, Commercial Vehicle Comprehensive

Summary: Insurance - Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, s. 166 - Insurance Act, 1938, s. 64-VB - Act policies vis-a-vis 'package policies' - 'Comprehensive Policy' and 'Comprehensive Insurance' - Liability of an insurance company for death of a person traveling in a private car - Policy in instant case was in the nature of 'package policy' and the issue arose in the case in the opinion of the Court, required deeper scrutiny because none of the decision of the Court dealt with 'package policy', thus matter was opined to be referred to larger bench - U/s. 166 of MV Act compensation claimed to the tune of Rs. 1,50,00,000/- - MACT awarded compensation of Rs. 98,64,428/- - Appeal thereagainst by respondent/insurer before High Court - HC decided in favour of R1/insurer - Hence present appeal - Instant is a private car policy; premium was paid Rs. 605 out of which Rs. 15 was towards 'increased third party property limit' - Contention was raised that only a sum of Rs. 15/- having been paid towards third party limit, the policy cannot be said to have covered the life of passenger traveling in the car - For acceptability of contention Court perused s. 64-VB of Insurance Act and referred to the decisions of the Court - Court observed that policy in question is a package policy; and contract of insurance if given its face value covers the risk not only of a third party but also of person traveling in the car including the owner thereof - In instant case the schedule of the policy does not indicate the limits of liability; it does not indicate exclusion of any person; it takes any person including 'insured' - There being no limitation with regard to coverage, in terms of the provisions of the Act, no upper limit is fixed unlike the old MV Act, thus contention was rejected - Position of law is that passenger would not be a third party within the meaning of the provisions of MV Act - But Court noted that before the Court nature of polices which came up for consideration were Act policies and it never had dealt with a package policy; Court further observed that if the Tariff Advisory Committee under Insurance Act seeks to enforce its decision in regard to coverage of third party risk which would include all persons including occupants of the vehicle and the insurer having entered into a contract of insurance in relation thereto, matter than requires deeper scrutiny - Matter to be referred to larger bench.
