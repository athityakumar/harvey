(1)R.K. Anand; (2)I.U. Khan v Registrar, Delhi High Court

Supreme Court of India

29 July 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Aftab Alam, B.N. Agrawal, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 920; (2009) 8 SCC 106; 2009 (6) CompLJ 67; 2009 (161) DLT 130; JT 2009 (10) SC 1; 2009 (3) MLJ(Crl) 609; 2009 (5) MLJ(SC) 1377; 2009 (4) RCR(Civil) 17; 2009(10) SCALE 164; [2009] 11 S.C.R. 1026; 2009 (3) UPLBEC 2555

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Wrongful Confinement, Bride Burning, Court Of Record, Fair Criticism, Appeals Disposed Of, Police Record, Tape-Record, Comments, Police Protection, Court's Function, Contumacious Conduct, List Of Witnesses, Civil Case, Electronic Media, Blood Stains, Due Course Of Justice, Civil Action, Non- Examination, English Decision, Abuse Judge, Innocent Publication

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Contempt of Courts Act, 1971, ss. 2(c)(ii)(iii), 3(3) exp. B proviso (ii), 12 and 19(1) - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 145, 215, 227 and 235 - Advocates Act, 1961, s. 34 - BMW hit and run case - Trial went on for eight years; weak prosecution; mighty and wealthy defense; flip flop of prosecution witnesses; public attention and reaction; active media - Telecast of programme by English language news channel, viz. NDTV in which one Sunil Kulkarni (earlier prosecution witness and later court witness) was shown meeting with IU Khan, the Special Public Prosecutor and RK Anand, the Senior Defence Counsel, along with two others, negotiating for his sell out in favour of the defence for a price - Shocked by the programme the Delhi High Court suo moto initiated contempt proceeding; materials on which the telecast was based sought from the NDTV - HC held RK Anand and IU Khan guilty of contempt of Court u/cls. (ii) and (iii) of s. 2(c) of Contempt Act, and prohibited them, by way of punishment, from appearing in the Delhi HC and the Courts subordinate to it for a period of four months from date of judgment - Present appeal thereagainst - P /&gt;(A) Whether the conviction of the two appellant for committing criminal contempt of court is justified and sustainable? - Held, yes for RK Anand, and no for IU Khan - Facts and circumstances of the case noted, developments in the Court's proceedings, both in the Trial Court and the High Court, outside development i.e. the whole spy operation, and the two telecasts by NDTV and live shows, examined, original unedited record, perused - Submissions considered - (i) RK Anand's appeal - Authenticity and integrity of the sting recordings was never disputed or doubted by RK Anand; he kept on changing his stand in regard to the sting recordings - Finding, in facts and circumstances of the case, there was no requirement of any formal proof of the sting recordings - Finding, there was no violation of the principles of natural justice in as much as he was given copies of all the sting recordings along with their transcripts; he was fully made aware of the charge against him; he was given fullest opportunity to defend himself and to explain his conduct - Held further, sting recordings were rightly made the basis of conviction and the irresistible conclusion is that the conviction of RK Anand for contempt of Court is proper, legal and valid, calling for no interference - (ii) IU Khan - Sting involving him and Sunil Kulkarni, perused - Reference was to some 'Bade Sahab' in their conversation; and in the conversation with one of the member of sting operation who was with Sunil Kulkarni all along - Question as to whom they were referring to by saying 'Bade Sahab', found to be very significant towards holding IU Khan guilty of contempt - Records and sting meticulously perused - Finding as to that 'Bade Sahab' was meant to be RK Anand, by the HC, found to be unjustified - Conduct of IU Khan in the sting, examined - Finding, conduct to be improper, which crossed the limits of proper professional conduct of a prosecutor(esp. engaged to conduct a sensational trial), and a designated Senior Advocate of long standing - Held, charge of criminal contempt not satisfactorily established against IU Khan - Benefit of doubt granted - P /&gt;(B) Whether the procedure adopted by the HC in the contempt proceedings was fair and reasonable, causing no prejudice to the two appellant? - (i) Direction was given by HC to NDTV to keep microchips containing the original sting recordings and the magnetic tapes with it - Such directions was objected to by the appellants - Direction found to be justified, on ground that once the HC obtained their copies there was no possibility of any tampering with such records from that stage; Court observed that HC might have felt that the NDTV with its well equipped studio/laboratory would be a much better place for the handling and conservation of such articles than the HC Registry - (ii) Non arraying as contemnors, of NDTV, found to be faulty on part of HC - Court observed that by all reckoning, at the time of initiation of the proceeding, the place of NDTV was along with the appellants facing charge of contempt; such a course would have put the proceeding on a more even keel and given it a more balanced appearance; there would than have been no scope for the grievance that the HC put the NDTV on the complainant's seat, Court observed - P /&gt;(C) Whether it was open to the HC to prohibit the appellants from appearing before the HC and the Courts sub-ordinate to it for a specified period as one of the punishments for criminal contempt of Court? - Held, Yes - Position of law vide decided cases, quoted - Contention was raised that the direction issued was beyond the jurisdiction of HC, on ground that bar is akin to revocation/suspension of the lawyer's licence which is a punishment for professional misconduct that can only be inflicted by the Bar Council after following the procedure prescribed under the Advocates Act; Contention finds support from the Constitution Bench decision in 'Supreme Court Bar Association vs. UOI, (1998) 4 SCC 409' - In SC Bar Association's case, Court had observed that in a given case it might be possible for this Court or the HC to prevent the contemnor advocate to appear before it till he purge himself of the contempt; in Ex. Capt. Harish Uppal vs. UOI, (2003) 2 SCC 45', it was held not as punishment for professional misconduct but as a measure necessary to regulate the Court's proceedings and to maintain the dignity and orderly functioning of the Courts - Court added, that in a given case a direction disallowing an advocate who is convicted of criminal contempt from appearing in Court may not only be a measure to maintain dignity and orderly functioning of the Courts but may become necessary for the self protection of the Court and for preservation of the purity of Court proceedings - Court submitted that the decision in Ex-Capt. Harish's case places the issue in correct perspective and must be followed to answer the question at issue in instant case - P /&gt;(D) Whether in the facts and circumstances of the case the punishments awarded to the appellants can be said to be adequate and commensurate to their misdeeds? - Held, No - Misdeeds of RK Anand noted, diversionary and intimidatory tactics adopted by him throughout the proceedings before HC further noted - Finding, leniency shown by the HC in meting out the punishment, misplaced - Action of appellant in trying to suborn the Court witness in a criminal trial was reprehensible enough but his conduct before the HC aggravates the matter manifold; no remorse for his gross misdemeanour; tried to take on the HC by defying its authority - Notice thus issued to appellant/RK Anand for enhancement of punishment - Held further, by his actions and conduct the appellant has established himself as a person who needs to be kept away from the portals of the Court for a longer time - Show cause notice thus issued - Appeal by IU Khan, allowed; his conviction set aside - Appeal by RK Anand, dismissed, subject to the notice of enhancement of punishment issued to him - Further directions issued - Appeals disposed of.
