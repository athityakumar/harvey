Sanjay Kumar Kedia @ Sanjay Kedia v Intelligence Officer, Narcotic Control Bureau And Another

Supreme Court of India

20 August 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

H.S. Bedi, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 2070; (2009) 17 SCC 631; AIR 2010 SC (Supp) 744; 2010 ALL MR (Cri) 1310; 2010 CRLJ 2054; 2010 (3) JCC (Narcotics) 135; JT 2009 (15) SC 463; 2010 (1) RCR(Criminal) 942; 2010(2) SCALE 145; [2010] 1 S.C.R. 555

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 167(1) - Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985, ss. 24, 29, 30, 36A(4) and 38 - Appellant was arrested for offences punishable u/ss. 24, 29, 30 and 38 of the Act and was produced before the Special Judge who remanded him to judicial custody for fifteen days, the period being extended from time to time - Appellant filed an application for bail, which was rejected - Thereafter, before the expiry of period of 180 days fixed u/s. 36A(4) of the Act r/w s. 167(2), CrPC, application seeking a further period of six months for the completion of the investigation and the filing of the complaint was filed - Special Judge allowed said application and period was extended - Another application filed u/s. 36A(4) of the Act for extension of time was also allowed - Appellant filed another application for bail on the plea that the investigation had not been completed within the stipulated period of time fixed by the Special Judge - Application was rejected - Hence, present appeal - Whether the two applications for extension that had been filed by the public prosecutor seeking an extension beyond 180 days met the necessary conditions? - Held, maximum period of 90 days fixed u/s. 167 (2), CrPC has been increased to 180 days for several categories of offences under the Act but the proviso authorizes a yet further period of detention upto one year, provided the stringent conditions provided therein are satisfied and are complied with - Conditions provided are (1) a report of the public prosecutor,(2) which Indicates the progress of the investigation, and (3) specifies the compelling reasons for seeking the detention of the accused beyond the period of 180 days, and (4) after notice to the accused - In the present case, application does not indicate the progress of the investigation, nor the compelling reasons which required an extension of custody beyond 180 days - Further, no notice was issued to the appellant - Hence, extensions granted to the investigating department under the proviso to s. 36A(4) of the Act did not satisfy the conditions laid down and both the extensions, therefore, being contrary to law, must be struck down accordingly - Appeal allowed.
