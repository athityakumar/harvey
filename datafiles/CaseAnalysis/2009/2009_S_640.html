Sharda Singh v State Of U.P. & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

13 April 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Tarun Chatterjee, H.L. Dattu

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1322; (2009) 11 SCC 683; 2009(5) ALL MR 953; 2009 (3) AWC 2553; 2010 (124) FLR 1004; 2010 (1) LLJ 253; 2009(6) SCALE 712; 2010 (3) SLR 712

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Constitution of India-Article 226 - Employment - Suspension-Exoneration of charge-Reinstatement - Adverse entry-Denial of back salary-Whether when High Court sets aside order of appellate authority, it is justified in declining to set aside order of disciplinary authority?-Held, “no”-Order appealed against merges with appellate order-Order of High Court set aside-When Government servant exonerated of charges framed against him-He cannot be deprived of any portion of his salary for period of suspension-Subject to any rules/ regulations to contrary-High Court not justified in declining to decide issue-Order of disciplinary authority culminating in appellate order-Set aside-Matter remitted to disciplinary authority to consider claim of appellant for back salary in accordance with law and also rules/ regulations, if any.
