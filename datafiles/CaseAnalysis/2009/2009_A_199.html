Food Corporation of India and Others v Ashis Kumar Ganguly and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 821; (2009) 7 SCC 734; AIR 2009 SC 2582; 2009 (4) ESC 629; 2009 (122) FLR 1; JT 2009 (14) SC 370; 2009(8) SCALE 218; [2009] 8 S.C.R. 806; 2010 (3) SLR 787

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Food Corporations Act, 1964, Executive Instructions, Office Manual, Food Corporation of India (Staff) Regulations, 1971

Summary: Service - Food Corporations Act, 1964, ss. 12 and 12A(inserted in 1968) - Food Corporation of India(Staff) Regulations, 1971, reg. 81 proviso - Circular letters of FCI - Grant of advance increments to 57 deputationist employees from the State of West Bengal - By High Court - Whether proper or whether different treatments meted out to the respondents vis-a-vis the Central Govt. employees although drawn from separate cadre, for the purpose of grant of benefit to one class only amounted to discrimination? - Held, Yes - Employees were deputed from the States and from the Central Govt. to the appellant/corporation - Initial deputationists were from Central Govt. and those were absorbed later on - On the basis of one circular letter one extra increment was given to such C.Govt. deputationists - Respondents/deputationists were also given opportunity of absorption to which they had opted - Writ petition was filed in 1997 by respondents contending that in terms of proviso to reg. 81 of Regulations, 1971 they were entitled to grant of one additional increment, which was allowed by single Judge and further confirmed by Division Bench - Hence the instant appeal - Contentions heard, provisions perused and relevant circular letters looked into - Court in its earlier judgement involving respondents on another issue took notice of the fact that the respondents had served the Corporation for a period of 18 years on deputation in the post of Assistant Grade-II - On noting s. 12A(3), wherein employees of Central Govt. who were transferred, were deemed to be the employees of the corporation upon cessation of the relationship of employer and employee between the C. Govt. and themselves, and that they would be subject to the provisions of the same regulations which were applicable to the regular employees; Court took surprise on this as to why the benefit of the said regulations shall be denied to the employees who were from the State Govt. - On their absorption respondents were to be treated as direct recruits and were to be subjected to the terms and conditions as prescribed in Regulations, as per relevant circular; Court observed that if that be so than they were also required to be treated alike as having entered the services of the corporation for the first time - Further contentions by appellant/corporation rejected - Being without merit - Appeal dismissed.
