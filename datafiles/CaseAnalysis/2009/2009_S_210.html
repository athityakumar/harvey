Vishwanath Bapurao Sabale v Shalinibai Nagappa Sabale & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

23 March 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 383; (2009) 12 SCC 101; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 1525; 2009 (76) ALR 403; 2009 (2) AWC 1726; 2009 (3) Bom.C.R. 741; 2009 (3) JhrCR(SC) 5; 2009 (2) RCR(Civil) 748; 2009(5) SCALE 74; [2009] 4 S.C.R. 796

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Land & Property

Summary: Family & Personal - Land & Property - Whether all the transactions evidenced by four instruments executed on 2-07-1955 were sham transactions in support whereof reliance was placed? - Held, there does not seem to have any apparent reason to hold that the deed of sale was sham or nominal in character - For claiming title by adverse possession, it was necessary for the plaintiff to plead and prove animus possidendi - Long possession by itself would not be sufficient to prove adverse possession - Appeals dismissed.
