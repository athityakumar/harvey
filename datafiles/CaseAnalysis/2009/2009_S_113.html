Santosh Kumar and another v State of Maharashtra and others

Supreme Court of India

13 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1921; (2009) 6 SCC 498; (2009) 2 SCC (Cr) 1149; AIR 2010 SC (Supp) 612; 2009 (2) MLJ(Crl) 1092; 2010 (2) RCR(Criminal) 221; 2010 (5) RCR(Criminal) 618; 2009(7) SCALE 341; [2009] 9 S.C.R. 90

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Mitigating Circumstance, Life Convict, Mitigating Factor, Approver, Judicial Record, Co- Accused, Non- Examination, Committal Stage, Indian Penal Code (Amendment) Bill, 1972

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 364A - Death reference - Appellants allegedly kidnapped and murdered the deceased - Trial Court awarded death sentence to 1st appellant - HC confirmed the death sentence - Hence the present appeal - (A) Whether the Trial Court acted illegally in granting pardon to PW1, an accomplice - Held, Trial Court not only applied its mind on the application for grant of pardon but also examined the appellant by putting relevant questions to him - Trial Court did not pass the order purely on the basis of purported confessional statement made by PW1 - Evidence of PW1 was recorded only after grant of pardon - Therefore, Trial Court order granting pardon to the approver, is legal and valid - (B) Whether the present case can be said to be a 'rarest of rare cases' so as to enable the courts below to award the death penalty - Held, reasons assigned by the Trial Court to award death penalty do not satisfy the test laid down by SC in Bachan Singh v. State of Punjab, 1980 INDLAW SC 586 - Appellants were not criminals or professional killers - 1st appellant was a young man in age - Reformation is possible in the case of present appellants - Therefore, no special reasons assigned by the Trial Court to record death penalty and the mitigating factors in the present case are sufficient to place it out of the 'rarest of rare' category - Death sentence awarded to 1st appellant modified to rigorous imprisonment for life - Appeal dismissed.
