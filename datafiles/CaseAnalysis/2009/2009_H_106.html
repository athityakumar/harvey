Nahar Industrial Enterprises Limited and others v Hong Kong and Shanghai Banking Corporation and others

Supreme Court of India

29 July 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 931; (2009) 8 SCC 646; 2009(5) ALL MR 453; 2009 (3) BC 539; 2009 (6) CompLJ 400; 2009 (4) CTC 74; JT 2009 (10) SC 199; 2009 (8) MLJ(SC) 701; 2009(10) SCALE 360; [2009] 12 S.C.R. 54

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance

Keywords: Cross Suits, New India Assurance Co, Appointed Day, Entertain And Decide, Recovery Officer, Legally Recoverable, Multiplicity Of Proceedings, Performance Of Contract, Public Sector Banks, Shall Not Be Bound, Employees' State Insurance Act, 1948, Reserve Bank of India Act, 1934 (As Amended up to 2000), APCS Act, 1964, Maharashtra Cooperative Societies Act, 1961

Summary: Banking & Finance - Recovery of Debt Due to Banks and Financial Institutions Act, 1993, ss. 17, 18, 25 - CPC, 1908, ss. 96, 100, 151 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 142 - Breach of International Swaps and Derivatives Agreement - Appellant filed civil suit in Ludhiana - Respondent-banks filed application before Debt Recovery Tribunal in Mumbai - Respondents sought transfer of civil suit to Tribunal - HC allowed the Transfer Application and thereby transferred civil suit of appellant to Tribunal - Legality - Held, any of the provisions of DRT Act conferred upon HC the power to transfer case from court to Tribunal - Tribunal specified u/s. 25 of Act is not a civil court - Debtor or a third party cannot approach directly to Tribunal as it is possible in civil court - Continuance of counter-claim of debtor depends upon the continuance of application of bank in the Tribunal - Tribunal cannot pass a decree but only issue recovery certificate - Entire proceedings in the Tribunal revolves around legally recoverable dues of bank, therefore, tribunal is not a civil court - Ss. 17 and 18 of the Act ousted the jurisdiction of the civil court only in respect of applications from banks and financial institutions for recovery of its debt - Civil court has the jurisdiction to try all types of suits except those which are expressly ousted - Transferring the suit from civil court to Tribunal would curtail the right of appeal of the plaintiff - Only civil suits are the subject matter of inter State transfer from one civil court to another - Transfer a suit from one state to another under extra ordinary jurisdiction u/s. 151 of CPC, can be exercised only in exceptional situations - Right to sue and right to appeal u/ss. 96 and 100 of CPC would loose if the civil suit transferred from court to Tribunal - SC cannot exercise its jurisdiction to transfer the present case by invoking power u/art. 142 of the Constitution - HC could not transfer the case to Tribunal from civil court - Appeal allowed.
