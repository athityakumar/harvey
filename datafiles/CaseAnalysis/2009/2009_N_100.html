Jai Prakash v National Insurance Company and others

Supreme Court of India

17 December 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, Mukundakam Sharma, K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 2096; (2010) 2 SCC 607; 2010 (1) ACC 1; 2010 ACJ 455; 2010(2) ALL MR 436; 2010 (1) AWC 636; 2010 (4) Bom.C.R. 844; JT 2009 (15) SC 443; 2010 (1) KLT 774; 2010(3) M.P.H.T. 326; 2010 (1) RCR(Civil) 635; 2010(1) SCALE 8; [2009] 16 S.C.R. 710

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance

Summary: (A) Insurance - Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 - Suggestions For Legislative/Executive Intervention - Held, to ensure that all accident victims get compensation, it is necessary to formulate a more comprehensive scheme for payment of compensation to victims of road accidents, in place of the present system of third party insurance - Order accordingly.

(B) Insurance - Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 - Rationalisation of II schedule to Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 - Held, Central Government may consider amendment of the Second Schedule to the Act to rectify the several mistakes therein and rationalize the compensation payable thereunder, repeatedly pointed out by SC - Order accordingly.

(C) Insurance - Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 - Delhi Motor Accident Claims Tribunal Rules, 2008, r. 6 - Securing the compensation to the victims of accidents involving uninsured vehicles - Held, where there is no insurance cover for a vehicle, the owner should be directed to offer security or deposit an amount, adequate to satisfy the award that may be ultimately passed, as a condition precedent for release of the seized vehicle involved in the accident - If such security or cash deposit is not made, within a period of three months, appropriate steps may be taken for disposal of the vehicle and hold the same proceeds in deposit until the claim case is disposed of - Appropriate Governments may consider incorporation of a rule on the lines of r. 6 of the Delhi Motor Accident Claims Tribunal Rules, 2008 in this behalf - Order accordingly.
