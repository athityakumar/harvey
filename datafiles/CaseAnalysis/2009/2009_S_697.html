Tirupati Jute Industries Private Limited and v State Of West Bengal & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

13 April 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1323; (2009) 14 SCC 406; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 1079; 2009 (2) AWC 1867; 2009 (3) CalLT(SC) 20; 2009 (122) FLR 166; 2009 (3) JhrCR(SC) 68; 2009 (3) LLJ 247; 2009 (3) LLN 14; 2009(5) SCALE 715; 2009 (4) SLR 505

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Age Of Superannuation, Backwages

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Enquiry - Standing Order - Sustainability - Respondents were workmen of appellant - It was alleged that respondents along with some other workmen kept General Manager of company under wrongful confinement, misbehaved with him and used filthy language and threatened to assault him physically if charge-sheet cum suspension earlier issued to some workmen was not withdrawn - Enquiry was held and Enquiry Officer dismissed them from service for proved misconduct - Disputes were raised and appropriate govt. referred disputes to Tribunal - Tribunal held that workmen were not given due opportunity to contest enquiry and directed reinstatement of workmen with full back wages - Awards were challenged by management in writ petitions (WP No.1941-44/1998) contending that enquiry was fair and proper and adequate opportunity was given to employees - Workmen raised fresh contention that their orders of dismissal were illegal as they were not approved by Manager of establishment or employer as required by Standing Order 14(e) - HC disposed of writ petitions and held that Tribunal had erred - HC further quashed orders of dismissal for want of approval under Standing Order 14(e) and directed that workmen be reinstated with all consequential benefits as per award of Tribunal - Appellant challenged common order of HC - DB disposed of four appeals - Appellate court confirmed finding of HC -- DB upheld order of HC - Hence, instant appeals - Whether HC could have permitted workmen to raise contention based on disputed question of fact for first time in writ proceedings and then decide same against management without giving it opportunity to let in evidence thereon - Held, HC felt that matter need not be remitted and that it should decide issue on merits, it ought to have given due opportunity to appellant employer to produce before it, relevant material to establish that it had complied with Standing Order 14(e) that was also not done - Therefore, finding of HC affirmed by DB holding that there was no approval as required by Standing Order 14(e) was set aside, as same was based on no evidence - Court further held that in present case events have necessitated exercise of our jurisdiction u/art. 142 to do complete justice - Orders of termination in regard to employees were passed in years 1990 and 1991 - All four employees have reached age of superannuation long ago - Therefore no question of any of them being reinstated, even if matter was referred to Tribunal and they succeed before Tribunal - HC had found that charges were proved - Only technical contention about approval remained - On facts and circumstances Court held that appellant was to pay one-third of back-wages as final settlement, to four workmen - Hence, awards of Tribunal and orders of DB were set aside - Appeals allowed.
