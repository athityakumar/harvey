Regional Manager, Bank of Baroda v Anita Nandrajog

Supreme Court of India

01 September 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Markandey Katju, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1065; (2009) 9 SCC 462; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 1798; 2010 (3) AWC 2330; 2009 (3) CLR 312; 2010 (1) ESC 9; 2009 (123) FLR 154; 2009 (4) LLJ 601; 2009(12) SCALE 94; [2009] 13 S.C.R. 1073; 2009 (5) SLR 379; 2010 (1) UPLBEC 335

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s.10 - Fifth Bipartite Settlement, cl.17(b) - Misconduct - Unauthorized absence - Voluntary cessation of employment - Respondent no.2 was employed in office of petitioner- Bank as accounts clerk - She remained absent from duty for more than 266 days - Petitioner-Bank condoned acts of absence - Respondent no.2 again remained absent without permission and without any sanction of leave for more than 150 consecutive days - Petitioner-Bank invoked provisions of Cl.17(b) of Fifth Bipartite Settlement dated 10.4.1989 and issued notice to respondent no.2 to report for duty within 30 days, failing which it would be presumed that she has voluntarily retired from service of Bank - Respondent no.2 failed to report for duty - Petitioner-Bank treated respondent No. 2 as having voluntarily terminated her employment - Dispute referred to Industrial Tribunal - Industrial Tribunal held that order passed by petitioner-bank as illegal and unjustified - Hence, present appeal - (A) Termination - Legality - U/cl.17(b) of Bipartite Settlement, if an employee is absent without leave for more than 150 days and has no more leave to his/her credit then Bank can validly order voluntary cessation of employment and bank can call upon employee to report for duty within 30 days failing which action could be taken u/cl.17(b) - Held, in present case such a notice was given by Bank but respondent No. 2 failed to join and wanted leave for another eight months - It is thus clear that she had no intention of resuming duty within 30 days - Action of petitioner-bank in terminating her service on ground of voluntary cessation of employment valid - (B) Reliance on Clarification of January 1990 of Fifth Bipartite Settlement - Termination order was passed on 25.8.1989 whereas above clarification was made in 1990 when service of respondent has already come to an end - Held, clarification is in fact an amendment to earlier cl.17(b) and hence will have no retrospective effect in absence of any express intention to that effect - It has no retrospective effect - Appeal allowed.
