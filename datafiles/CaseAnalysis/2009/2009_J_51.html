Jaishree Anant Khandekar v State Of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

23 March 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Ganguly, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 344; (2009) 11 SCC 647; JT 2009 (4) SC 198; 2009 (3) RCR(Criminal) 765; 2009(4) SCALE 172; [2009] 4 S.C.R. 992

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - IPC, 1860, s. 302 - Appeal against conviction and sentence - Issue regarding admissibility of dying declaration - Held, core concept is that the expectation of death must be absolute and not susceptible to doubts and there should be no chance of operation of worldly motives - Court has to appreciate the dying declaration as a whole to see whether a ring of truth emerges from the same - Appeal dismissed.
