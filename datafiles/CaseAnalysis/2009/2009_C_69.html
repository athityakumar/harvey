Champaben Govindbhai v Popatbhai Manilal And Others

Supreme Court of India

31 July 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Ganguly, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 932; (2009) 13 SCC 662; JT 2009 (10) SC 180; 2010 (5) RCR(Criminal) 421; 2009(10) SCALE 307; 2009(10) SCALE 519; [2009] 11 S.C.R. 776

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Power Of The Court, Blood Stained Clothes, Sufficient In Ordinary Course Of Nature, Cross- Complaint

Summary: Criminal - IPC, 1860, ss. 302 r/w 34 r/w 114 - CrPC, 1973, s. 378 - Murder - Acquittal - Accused persons were acquitted by Trial Court - HC also affirmed the Trial Court's order on the ground of insufficient evidence - Legality - Held, HC has duty to consider material on record and to appreciate the evidence carefully before confirmation of acquittal - Medical report clearly opined that death was due to stab injury on chest - HC's finding of injury as those were not sufficient for death, is contrary to the record - Characterization of injuries mentioned in the post-mortem report by HC as not sufficient in the ordinary course of death, is grave error and perversity - Discarding of 3 eye-witness by HC on the ground of interested witness, is a glaring infirmity - Delay of 2 hours for lodging the FIR, not a ground for discrediting prosecution case - Acquittal on the basis of such delay is not reasonable - Weapons recovered had blood stains of deceased - HC has not properly appreciated the material on record by exercising its jurisdiction u/s. 378 of CrPC, hence, order of acquittal set aside - Matter remitted back to HC for fresh disposal.
