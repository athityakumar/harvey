M. D., Ramakrishna Poultry Private Limited v R. Chellappan and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 703; (2009) 16 SCC 743; JT 2009 (9) SC 168; 2009(7) SCALE 157; [2009] 8 S.C.R. 1055

Case Digest

Subject: Electricity & Energy

Keywords: Sub- Station, Telephonic Line

Summary: Electricity & Energy - Indian Telegraph Act, 1885, s. 10(d), 16 and 17 - Opening up of poultry farms - Laying of transmission towers by respondent no. 3/corporation through land belonged to appellant where its poultry farm was to be situated - On opinion being taken by appellant from Department of Animal Husbandry regarding effect on the layer birds on account of emission of electro magnetic fields from High Voltage Transmission Lines passing over the poultry sheds, writ petition was filed before HC - Adverse effect was opined by the Department - Direction was issued to District Magistrate by HC for appropriate action - By its order u/s. 17(3) of Act DM directed the Corporation to realign the transmission power line in such a way that it did not pass above poultry sheds of the appellant - Further writ petition was filed by respondent no. 1 in view of proposed realigned lines being passing over his lands situated adjacent to that of appellant - Division Bench allowed the writ appeal of respondent no. 1 - Present appeal - Court observed that balance have to struck between conflicting interests; both the technical as well as techno ecological feasibility of altering the route of the transmission lines in keeping with the directions given by the DM has to be considered - Court noted one fact which was against the appellant that purchases of land for starting poultry business was done at a time when the process of identifying the route of the transmission lines was already in progress and survey work was being undertaken by corporation so it is difficult to accept that appellant had no knowledge about the said facts - Next best option considered by Court, i.e. to increase the clearance between the lowest point of sag of the transmission cable and the top most portion of the appellant's poultry sheds; and in that regard appropriate direction issued - Impugned judgement set aside - Appeal partly allowed.
