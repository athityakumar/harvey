Tameezuddin @ Tammu v State of (NCT) of Delhi

Supreme Court of India

26 August 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

H.S. Bedi, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 2156; (2009) 15 SCC 566; 2009 (4) JCC 2809; 2010 (1) MLJ(Crl) 74; 2009(12) SCALE 303; [2009] 14 S.C.R. 80

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 376 and 506(ii) - Rape - Conviction - Challenged - Appellant was convicted u/ss. 376 and 506(ii) of the IPC by Trial Court solely on the basis of evidence of prosecutrix and her husband - Conviction affirmed by HC - Hence, present appeal - Held, in a case of rape the evidence of the prosecutrix must be given predominant consideration, but it cannot be held that this evidence has to be accepted even if the story is improbable and belies logic - In the present case, prosecution story was improbable and the medical evidence also do not support the commission of rape - Hence, judgment of Trial Court and HC set aside - Appellant acquitted - Appeal allowed.
