Reeta Nag v State of West Bengal and Others

Supreme Court of India

13 August 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1909; (2009) 9 SCC 129; (2009) 3 SCC (Cr) 1051; AIR 2010 SC (Supp) 401; 2010 ALL MR (Cri) 1337; 2010 CRLJ 2245; 2009 (4) JCC 2520; 2009 (4) MLJ(Crl) 1004; 2009(11) SCALE 395; [2009] 13 S.C.R. 276

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 173 - Reinvestigation - Whether Magistrate can suo moto direct the investigating authorities to conduct a re-investigation or even further investigation u/s. 173(8) of CrPC after filing charge-sheet u/s. 173(2) - Magistrate cannot suo moto direct a further investigation u/s. 173(8) or direct a re-investigation into a case on account of the bar of s. 167(2) of the CrPC - Held, investigating authorities did not apply for further investigation and it was only upon the application filed by the de facto complainant u/s. 173(8), Magistrate had given direction to re-investigate the matter - Not only was the Magistrate wrong in directing a re-investigation on the application made by the de facto complainant, but he also exceeded his jurisdiction in entertaining the said application - Petition dismissed.
