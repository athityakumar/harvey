Smt. Rumi Dhar v State Of West Bengal And Another

Supreme Court of India

08 April 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 417; (2009) 6 SCC 364; (2009) 2 SCC (Cr) 1074; 2009 (3) ADJ 701; AIR 2009 SC 2195; 2009 (2) RCR(Criminal) 704; 2009 (4) RLW 3434; 2009(5) SCALE 471; [2009] 5 S.C.R. 553; 2009 (1) WLN(SC) 170

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Post Dated Cheque

Summary: Criminal - Procedure - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, ss. 320 and 239 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 43 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120B, 420, 467, 468 and 471 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 13(2) r/w 13(1)(d) - Compounding of offences - Charge - Framing thereof - Discharge - Bank fraud - Availing of overdraft facilities without any security - Collusion of bank officers - Charges under IPC and Corruption Act - Bank moved Debt Recovery Tribunal for recovery - Settlement arrived at between parties and repayment made - Serious allegations of conspiracy, falsification of accounts and forgery of records - Initiation of criminal proceedings - Charge sheet filed - Special Judge to frame charge - Application moved u/s. 239 of CrPC for discharge - Dismissed - Revision application moved before HC - Dismissed - Contention raised that as settlement had been arrived, and loan had been repaid, continuation of criminal proceeding would amount to an abuse of process of Court - Contention whether acceptable? - Position of law well settled, that in a given case, a civil proceeding and a criminal proceeding can proceed simultaneously; when a settlement is arrived at by and between the creditor and the debtor, the offence committed as such does not come to an end; judgment of a tribunal in a civil proceeding more so when it is rendered on the basis of settlement entered into by and between the parties, would not be of much relevance in a criminal proceeding having regard to the provisions contained in s. 43 of the Evidence Act - Finding of Special Judge and High Court as to existence of prima facie case against the accused/appellant for purpose of framing of charges - Contention unacceptable - Appeal dismissed.
