Rachpal Singh and Others v Gurmit Kaur and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Dattu, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1290; (2009) 15 SCC 88; AIR 2009 SC 2448; 2009 (76) ALR 672; 2009 (3) RCR(Civil) 786; 2009 (2) RCR(Rent) 112; 2009(7) SCALE 197

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Landlord And Tenant, East Punjab Urban Rent Restriction Act, 1949

Summary: Land & Property - Rent Control - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - East Punjab Urban Rent Restriction Act, 1949, s. 13-B - Eviction- Validity - Respondent /landlady was owner of disputed property - Respondent claiming to be NRI and wanting of having possession of tenanted premises sought eviction of tenants by filing separate petitions u/s. 13-B of Act against all four tenants - Tenants appeared in all four eviction petitions, out of which three were pending before Rent Controller - Tenants filed applications for leave to contest u/s. 18-A of act - Rent Controller vide its order allowed petition - In respect of other three petitions, Rent Controller vide its order had granted to tenants leave to defend petition filed by respondent u/s. 13-B of Rent Act - Aggrieved by order respondent had filed revision petitions before HC - HC while allowing petitions set aside impugned orders and had remitted back to Rent Controller to reconsider application filed by tenants u/s. 18-A of Act in accordance with law - Aggrieved by order of remand so passed, appellants filed appeal - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order passed by HC was justified - Held, that in instant Revision Petitions HC had erred in giving finding that Rent Controller had considered respondent as NRI - If some triable issues were raised then controversy could be properly adjudicated after ascertainment of truth through cross-examination of witnesses who had filed their affidavits and other material documents - Burden was on landlord to prove requirements and assertion was required to be tested more so when status of respondent had been specifically challenged and also when landlord-tenant relationship was in question - Therefore, there was no infirmity in order passed by HC and connected matters - Accordingly, impugned order passed by HC was sustained- Rent Controller was directed to independently examine applications filed by appellants u/s. 18-A of Act in accordance with law - Appeal disposed of.
