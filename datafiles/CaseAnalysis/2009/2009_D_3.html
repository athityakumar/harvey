Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Keywords: Cross Objections, Appeals Disposed Of, Course Of Employment, In The Course Of Employment, Span Of Life

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, ss. 166 - Vehicular accident - Claim of compensation - Application of lower multiplier to heavy amount of multiplicand - Age, 22 years - Compensation claimed, Rs. 75,00,000/- on premise that while pursuing his studies in Business Administration in UK deceased was also doing a part time job with World Bank on monthly salary of Rs. 80,000(UK 1008.31 pounds) and was offered an employment in India at an annual remuneration of Rs. 18,00,000 p.a. - Undisputedly the offer was not accepted as he wanted to pursue his higher studies - MACT took monthly salary at Rs. 18,000/mn and 1/3rd was taken as loss of dependency and multiplier of 13 was applied in view of age of the claimants; amount awarded was Rs. 9,36,000/- plus Rs. 2,000/- for funeral expense - In appeal HC took loss of dependency to 2/3rd and applied multiplier of 13, calculating loss of dependency at Rs. 18,72,000/-, and towards medical expenses, awarded Rs. 1,25,000/-, further Rs. 25,000 was granted towards funeral expenses - (A) Monthly salary of Rs. 18,000/mn taken by MACT and HC whether proper? - Held, No - Admitted facts noted as to that deceased was a brilliant student, who had graduated from UK and was drawing salary from part time job, had big offer from a company based in USA etc. - At the relevant time he was not working as he had rejected the offer for pursuing his MBA - Cost of living in the western countries would be higher; standard of living in the western countries cannot be followed; even in a case where the victim of an accident was earning salary in US Dollars, this Court opined that a lower multiplier should be applied; 'United India Insurance Co. Ltd. vs. Patricia Jean Mahajan, (2002) 6 SCC 281' - Amount of compensation at Rs. 25,000/mn thus calculated, being 1/3rd of the amount which he was receiving in the UK - (B) What should be loss of dependency, i.e. whether 1/3rd from Rs. 25,000/- should be deducted or 2/3rd - Held, 2/3rd - Precedents referred and relied - Deduction of 1/3rd towards personal expenses is the ordinary rule in India and in facts and circumstances of the case same should be applied, Court held - Concept of joint family should be taken into account; deceased's father was a Doctor working in Govt. hospital; was aged about 51 years at the time of accident; he would have retired in few years; Court opined that he might not be completely dependent upon his son considering such facts - Held further, considering all the circumstances as to age of father, his wife, multiplier of 10 should be applied - Court observed that in a case initiated u/s. 166, multiplier u/sch. II may not automatically be applied, so multiplier indicated in the case is indicated keeping in view such fact - Court further observed that choice of multiplicand and multiplier would depend upon a large number of factors and the aspect has been considered in various judgments of the Court; Court on it said that in an appropriate case it may require consideration by a larger Bench - Appeal by insurance company dismissed and of claimants allowed - Directions to MACT issued - Appeals disposed of.
