Ravindra Kumar Gupta and Company v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

03 December 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Surinder Singh Nijjar, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1698; (2010) 1 SCC 409; AIR 2010 SC 972; 2010 (78) ALR 491; 2010 (1) AWC 7; JT 2009 (14) SC 591; 2010 (1) MLJ(SC) 841; 2010 (2) MPLJ 24; 2010 (1) RCR(Civil) 88; 2009(13) SCALE 303; [2009] 16 S.C.R. 142

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Keywords: Reasoned Award

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Indian Arbitration Act, 1940 - Jurisdiction of Courts to interfere with arbitration award - Appellant was allotted certain civil works - Disputes arose between the parties after completion of the work regarding the work and payment for the same - Arbitration clause invoked - Arbitral award passed - Award challenged before Civil Court - Award made rule of the court - On appeal, HC held that Arbitrator acted unreasonably and irrationally in ignoring the limits and the provisions of the contract - Hence, present appeal - Whether HC erred in re-appreciating the evidence led by the parties before the arbitrator? - Held, yes - Evidence was duly scrutinized and evaluated by the arbitrator - Therefore, finding recorded by the arbitrator cannot said to be either perverse or based on no evidence - Conclusion was erroneously substituted by the HC with its own opinion on appreciation of the evidence - Such a course was not permissible to the HC while examining objections to the award u/s. 30 of the Act - Further, impugned judgment of the HC does not fall within the limited jurisdiction available to the Court for interference in the award of an arbitrator and therefore set aside - Appeal allowed.
