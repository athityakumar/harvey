C.J. Paul & Ors. v District Collector & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

31 July 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Deepak Verma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 938; (2009) 14 SCC 564; JT 2009 (13) SC 528; 2009 (6) MLJ(SC) 592; 2009 (3) RCR(Civil) 952; 2009 (108) RD 566; 2009(10) SCALE 446; [2009] 12 S.C.R. 233

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Gudalur Janmam Estates (Abolition and Conversion into Ryotwari) Act, 1969

Summary: Land & Property - Indian Stamp Act, 1899, ss. 47A(1) and 19B(As amended by the State) - Limitation period - Purchase of properties situate in the State of Tamil Nadu, by a registered deed of sale D/-01-02-1990; some lands were situated in the State of Kerala also - Respective Sub-Registrar of TN on coming to know on or about 30-03-1996 about such execution of deeds of sale, initiated proceeding purportedly u/s. 47A(1) and u/s. 19B of Act, for collection of deficit stamp duty, on or about 05-05-1998; letter was issued to the Collector, and notice was also issued after about one month - Writ petition preferred against such notice, which was dismissed; writ appeal was also dismissed - HC found transactions in question to be done with ulterior purposes - Judgment of HC, whether sustainable? - Held, No - Provisions perused, contentions heard, amendments brought in by the State further noted - Contention was raised by the appellants that proceeding u/s. 47A of Act could be initiated only within a period of two years from the date of registration and as in instant case it has been initiated after about 8 years, the same was barred by limitation; further contention was raised that HC committed a serious error insofar as it failed to take into consideration that the amendments to the Act subsequent to the execution of the deeds of sale are not attracted to the facts of the present case - Contentions upheld - Ss. 47A and 19B provides for penalty; and a statute of limitation conferring jurisdiction upon the statutory authorities to impose penalty must, therefore, be construed strictly; unless otherwise specifically provided, such statute should be given effect prospectively - Relevant amendment was made in the year 2000, wherein it was provided that period of limitation is to begin from the date of knowledge; whereas unamended provision provided four years limitation period from the date of registration - As Deed of sale was registered in 1990, and proceeding was initiated in 1998, proceeding held to be barred by limitation, applying unamended provisions which was applicable at the relevant time - Court observed that only at a later stage when wisdom dawned, the Legislature amended the provision and inserted to the effect that limitation to be commenced from the date of knowledge - Settled position of law, Court cannot supply casus omissus - Impugned judgment being unsustainable, set aside - Appeal allowed.
