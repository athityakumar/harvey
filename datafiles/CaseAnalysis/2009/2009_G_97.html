Rani Aloka Dudhoria And Others v Goutam Dudhoria And Others

Supreme Court of India

05 March 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 295; (2009) 13 SCC 569; AIR 2010 SC 53; 2009 (4) ALD(SC) 79; JT 2009 (3) SC 626; 2009(3) SCALE 865; [2009] 4 S.C.R. 204

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Limitation Period

Summary: Family & Personal - Suit for partition - Partition Act, 1893 - Held, s. 3 envisages sale of the property within the shareholders - It unlike the provisions of the Code of Civil Procedure, does not debar a shareholder from taking part in auction inter alia on the premise that the shareholder may be interested in keeping the property to himself - Court has no power to direct sale de' hors the provisions of the Partition Act - If any auction had taken place by fraud or collusion the same is non est in the eyes of law. Not suggesting that mere suspicion of fraud would amount to proof thereof but the High Court should atleast have gone into such a question - Appeals allowed.
