Bimla Devi & Ors v Himachal Road Transport Corpn. & Ors

Supreme Court of India

15 April 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 459; (2009) 13 SCC 530; 2009 (2) ACC 444; AIR 2009 SC 2819; 2012 (4) ALD(SC) 147; 2009 (75) ALR 625; 2009 (3) AWC 3042; 2009 (6) CTC 604; 2009 (155) PLR 67; 2009 (3) RCR(Civil) 805; 2009(5) SCALE 706; [2009] 6 S.C.R. 362; 2009 (2) ShimLC 79

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Keywords: Rashness And Negligence

Summary: Carriers and Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, s. 166 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 106 - Vehicular accident - Grant of Compensation - Factum of accident denied and disputed by the respondents - MACT accepted the claim and passed judgement - High Court reversed the judgement of MACT - Deceased was police constable - Fact of accident disputed on ground that deceased was already dead the previous evening from the morning of accident in question and thus driver is being falsely implicated - Reversal of judgement of MACT by HC, whether justified? - Held, No - Head injury clearly came out from post mortem injury - Relevant facts of case noted - Core issue was whether the bus in question was involved in the accident and for determining it, principle u/s. 106 of Evidence Act as to burden of proof has to be applied by the Courts - Burden of proof lied on respondents 2 and 3 - Standard of proof for claimant is preponderance of probabilities - No material on record in support of findings given by HC - Impugned judgement set aside - Appeal allowed.
