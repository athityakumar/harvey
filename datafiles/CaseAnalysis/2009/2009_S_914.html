State of Madhya Pradesh v Laakhan @ Lakhan

Supreme Court of India

21 April 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 2153; (2009) 14 SCC 433; 2009(8) SCALE 89; [2009] 8 S.C.R. 47

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34 and 302 - Acquittal - Challenged - Respondent and one more person was charged u/s. 302 r/w s. 34 of IPC - It was alleged that they murdered PW 11's child - Trial Court held version of PW 1, the informant, to be cogent and credible and convicted respondent u/s. 302 r/w s. 34 of IPC - On appeal, HC held that the evidence of PW 11 was not fully reliable and there was a great difference as regards the time of occurrence and accordingly acquittal was directed - Hence, present appeal - Held, even the evidence of a solitary witness can be sufficient to record conviction if the same is wholly reliable - It is the quality and not the quantity of the evidence that matters - In the present case, evidence of PW 11 has no deficiency except the time factor - Hence, there was no other discrepancy in her evidence to warrant rejection of her evidence - Also there was no particular reason as to why she would falsely implicate the respondent - That being so, the HC was not justified in directing acquittal - Judgment of the HC set aside and that of the Trial Court restored - Appeals allowed.
