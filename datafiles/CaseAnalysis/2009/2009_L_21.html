Lalita Prasad & Ors. v Gyan Singh & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

10 September 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

H.S. Bedi, J.M. Panchal

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 2248

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 7 r. 11 - Cause of action - Rejection of plaint - Legality - Plaint filed by appellant was rejected under O. 7 r. 11 CPC on the ground that no cause of action had been made out - Order of Trial Court was affirmed by HC and thereafter by the DB - Hence instant appeals.

Held, dispute would be covered by the Quanoon Mal of Gwalior State was not disputed by the appellants. This appears to be so because in the earlier litigation referred to above, a finding that the Quanoon Mal of Gwalior State would be applicable to the property in dispute had been affirmed right upto this Court. In this view of the matter, SC is of the opinion that no interference is called for. Appeals disposed of.

Civil Appeal No. 6199 of 2001

Civil Procedure - Substitution of legal representatives - Application seeking substitution of the legal representatives of the deceased respondent No. 3 is allowed. In the light of the fact that connected Appeal viz. Civil Appeal No. 346 of 2004 is dismissed, SC allow the appeal, set aside the order of the HC dt. 12-5-2009 and remit the matter to the HC to decide the matter afresh. Appeals disposed of.
