Wahid Khan v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

01 December 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Deepak Verma, J.M. Panchal

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1583; (2010) 2 SCC 9; AIR 2010 SC 1; 2010 ALL MR (Cri) 303; 2010 CRLJ 517; 2010 (1) JhrCR(SC) 78; JT 2009 (14) SC 510; 2010 (1) MLJ(Crl) 1021; 2010 (1) RCR(Criminal) 151; 2010 (1) RLW 999; 2009(14) SCALE 220; [2009] 15 S.C.R. 1207; 2009 (3) UC 1821

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Wrongful Confinement

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 342, 366 and 376 - Rape of minor - Conviction - Trial Court convicted appellant u/s. 376 of IPC - On appeal, Single Judge confirmed order of Trial Court - Appellant contended that medical report of prosecutrix reflected that her hymen was still in-tact, hence no rape was committed on her - Whether conviction of appellant correct? - Held, corroboration is not sine qua non for conviction in a rape case - Doctor who examined prosecutrix did not appear on behalf of the prosecution to depose - Even without examination of doctor, evidence of prosecutrix was fully corroborated by evidence of P.W.3-B.B, sub-inspector of police station who had virtually caught the appellant red-handed - Evidence of prosecution witnesses is fully trustworthy and there is no reason to doubt genuineness thereof - Appeal dismissed.
