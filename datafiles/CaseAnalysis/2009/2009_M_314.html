Oriental Insurance Co. Ltd v Mohd. Nasir & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

12 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 784; (2009) 6 SCC 280; (2009) 2 SCC (Cr) 987; 2009 (4) ACC 891; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 1619; 2010 (1) ALD(SC) 74; 2009 (3) AWC 2759; 2009 (3) LLJ 559; 2009 (3) LLN 508; 2009 (156) PLR 778; 2009 (3) RCR(Civil) 849; 2009(8) SCALE 161; [2009] 8 S.C.R. 829

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance

Keywords: Employer's Liability, Legislative Intent, Appeals Disposed Of, Higher Compensation, Compensation For Injury, Death Or Permanent Disablement, Use Of Motor Vehicle, Liability To Pay Interest, Amending Act, Non Obstante Clause, Third Party Risks, Future Loss Of Income, Physical Disability, Grant Of Interest, Permanent Total Disablement, Permanent Partial Disablement

Summary: Insurance - Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, ss. 163A, 166, 168 and Sch. II Para 5 note appended thereto - Workmen's Compensation Act, 1973, ss. 4, 4A(3) and sch. I Part I and II - Vehicular accidents - Claims under Act, 1923 and 1988 - 'Third party' and 'workmen' - Applicability of respective provisions of both the Acts in respect of claimants who had suffered disability is in question in the instant appeals - Commissioner under Act, 1923 determined percentage of disability and percentage loss of earning capacity and calculated compensation in the respective cases and MACT awarded compensation in one the case before it, determining permanent disability suffered - Statutory provisions of both the Acts noted - (A)Whether the percentage of loss of earning capacity and the physical disability shall be the same? - Held, functional disability has a direct relationship with the loss of limb - Both the Acts are beneficent legislation insofar as they provide for payment of compensation to the workmen employed by the employers and/or by use of motor vehicles by the owner thereof and/or the insurer to the claimants suffering permanent disability - Expeditious relief to the victims of accident is the aim of both the Acts - Statutes deserve liberal construction - Both the Acts provides for the mode and manner in which the percentage of loss of earning capacity is required to be calculated; they provide that the amount of compensation in cases of this nature would be directly relatable to the percentage of physical disability suffered by the injured vis-a-vis the injuries specified in the sch. I of 1923 Act; undisputedly where injuries are specified in the sch. I, the mode and manner provided for the purpose of calculating the amount of compensation would be applicable; provisions were made for determination by a qualified medical practitioner, of extent of physical disability suffered to enable him to assess the loss of earning capacity - Tribunal or HC must record reasons for arriving at their conclusion as to loss of earning capacity - (B) Payment of interest - Interest would be from the date of default and not from the date of award of compensation - Provision of s. 4A(3) of 1923 Act perused - Provision penal in nature; it does not take into consideration the chargeability of interest on various other grounds including the amount which the claimant would have earned if the amount of compensation would have been determined as on the date of filing of the claim petition; 1923 Act does not prohibit grant of interest at a reasonable rate from the date of filing of the claim petition till an order is passed; only when sub/s. (3) of s. 4A would be attracted, a higher rate of interest would be payable wherefor a finding of fact as envisaged therein has to be arrived; thus only because in a given case, penalty may not be held to be leviable, by itself may not be a ground not to award reasonable interest - In cases under MV Act interest stipulated therein shall become payable - (C) Whether any amount could be directed to be paid in excess of the amount claimed? - Held, Yes - Function of Commissioner is to determine the amount of compensation as laid down under the Act; even if no amount is claimed, the Commissioner must determine the amount which is found payable to the workman; even in the cases arising out of the MV Act, it is the duty of the Tribunal to arrive at a just compensation having regard to the provisions contained in s. 168 thereof - Direction issued as to that Commissioner/Tribunal should determine the amount of compensation in the respective cases in the light of the observations thus made - Appeals disposed of.
