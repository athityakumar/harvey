Santosh Devidas Behade And Ors. v State Of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

06 March 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 307; (2009) 13 SCC 680; JT 2009 (7) SC 417; 2009(3) SCALE 727; [2009] 4 S.C.R. 83

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - IPC, 1860, ss. 147, 148, 302 r/w 149 - Appeal against conviction and sentence - Held, Common object is different from a 'common intention' as it does not require a prior concert and a common meeting of minds before the attack - For determination of the common object of the unlawful assembly, the conduct of each of the members of the unlawful assembly, before and at the time of attack and thereafter, the motive for the crime, are some of the relevant considerations - Merely because the eye-witnesses are family members their evidence cannot per se be discarded - When there is allegation of interestedness, the same has to be established - Mere statement that being relatives of the deceased they are likely to falsely implicate the accused cannot be a ground to discard the evidence which is otherwise cogent and credible - Appeal dismissed.
