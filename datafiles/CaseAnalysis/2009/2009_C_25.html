H. B. Basavaraj (dead) by Lrs and another v Canara Bank and Others

Supreme Court of India

28 October 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Tarun Chatterjee, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 2090; (2010) 12 SCC 458; 2010 (1) KarLJ 588; 2010 (1) MLJ(SC) 383; 2009(13) SCALE 474; [2009] 15 S.C.R. 504

Case Digest

Subject: Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Continuing, Lokashikshana Trust (Taking over of Management) Act

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Indian Contract Act, 1872, ss. 62, 72 and 129 - Appellant nos. 1 and 2 formed for the purpose of the management of the running of the business of 'X' Trust - Respondent-Bank granted loan for proper functioning of said trust - Respondent filed suit against appellants for recovery of said loan amount - Trial Court directed appellant to pay Rs. 19,97,839.51 with future interest at 17% p.a. - HC allowed appeal filed by the appellants against said order - Hence, present appeal - (A) Whether the deceased surety and his legal representatives are liable to repay the disputed loan amount? - Held, yes as agreement executed for the purpose of a continuing liability despite the variation of terms of the contract and in the absence of a specific written document by 'Y' (since deceased) revoking the guarantee, the guarantee stands and the legal representatives of the deceased are liable to repay the loan - (B) Whether the conduct of parties amounts to novation of the contract between the parties? - Held, no - No record to show that there were withdrawals from the loan account of 'X' Trust from which it could be inferred that the Bank had permitted the receivers to withdraw money - HC rightly held that only a withdrawal from the said loan account would constitute acquiescence on behalf of the Bank to a change in the liability by novation - Mere deposit of amounts by a supposed third party towards liquidation of an outstanding amount cannot ipso facto lead to the novation of a contract - (C) Whether the 'X' Trust alone is responsible to reply the disputed loan amount being the beneficiary of the same? - Held, yes - Board of Trustees was competent to take any loan, which would be considered to be loan taken by the Trust - In such a case, any loan taken by administrator appointed by the State shall also be deemed as loan taken by the trust and, hence, the trust would be liable to repay the loan - (D) Whether the 'X' trust having benefited from the loan transaction disputed herein can be estopped from denying its liability? - Held, trust being the sole beneficiary is not only liable for the repayment but is also estopped from denying its liability under the contract - HC's order set aside - Judgement of the trial court restored - Appeal allowed.
