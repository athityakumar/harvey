Lunaram v Bhupat Singh And Ors

Supreme Court of India

27 February 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 255; (2009) 2 SCC (Cr) 200; (2009) 3 SCC 749; 2009 CRLJ 1899; 2009 (3) JCC 1696; JT 2009 (3) SC 155; 2010 (5) RCR(Criminal) 530; 2009(3) SCALE 363; [2009] 3 S.C.R. 706

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Power Of The Court

Summary: Criminal - IPC, 1860, ss. 302, 307, 352, 323 r/ 34; Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes (Prevention of Atrocities) Act, s. 3(2)(5) - Trial Court convicted the accused bur High Court reversed the conviction - Appeal against acquittal - Held, order of acquittal shall not be interfered with because the presumption of innocence of the accused is further strengthened by acquittal - A miscarriage of justice which may arise from acquittal of the guilty is no less than from the conviction of an innocent - In a case where admissible evidence is ignored, a duty is cast upon the appellate court to re-appreciate the evidence where the accused has been acquitted, for the purpose of ascertaining as to whether any of the accused really committed any offence or not - If the impugned judgment is clearly unreasonable and irrelevant and convincing materials have been unjustifiably eliminated in the process, it is a substantial reason for interference - View of the High Court cannot be termed to be perverse and is a possible view on the evidence - Appeal dismissed.
