Buddu Khan v State Of Uttarakhand

Supreme Court of India

12 January 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 38; (2009) 12 SCC 260; AIR 2009 SC 1387; 2009 ALL MR (Cri) 502; JT 2009 (3) SC 495; 2009 (2) MLJ(Crl) 253; 2009(1) SCALE 442; [2009] 1 S.C.R. 178; 2009 (2) UC 763

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Subsequent Conduct

Summary: IPC, 1860, s. 302 - Appeal against conviction and sentence - Held, for bringing in operation of Exception 4 to s. 300 it has to be established that the act was committed without premeditation, in a sudden fight in the heat of passion upon a sudden quarrel without the offender having taken undue advantage and not having acted in a cruel or unusual manner - For the application of Exception 4, it is not sufficient to show that there was a sudden quarrel and there was no premeditation - Appropriate conviction would be under s. 304 Part I - Appeal partly allowed.
