R.B. Ramlingam v R.B. Bhvaneswari

Supreme Court of India

13 January 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, H.L. Dattu

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 790; (2009) 2 SCC 689; (2009) 1 SCC (Cr) 840; 2009 (75) ALR 147; 2009 (235) E.L.T. 3; 2009 (122) FLR 731; JT 2009 (2) SC 167; 2009 (3) LLJ 7; 2009 (1) RCR(Civil) 892; 2009 (1) RCR(Criminal) 951; 2009 (1) RCR(Rent) 136; 2009 (106) RD 813

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Petition admitted

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Limitation Act, 1963, s. 5 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Delay of 568 days due to prosecution of Review Application before HC - Condonation of - Appellant filed Review Application before the HC - HC dismissed the Review Application - Appellant filed Special Leave Petition (SLP) before SC with a petition for condoning the delay of 568 days - Appellant contended that delay occurred due to the pendency of Review Application before the HC - Whether the prosecution of a Review Application would be a sufficient cause for not filing the SLP in time for the purposes of s. 5 of the Act - Held, filing of the Review Petition is no impediment to the filing of the SLP - Court has to examine whether delay in filing the SLP stands properly explained in each and every case and whether the petitioner has acted with reasonable diligence in the prosecution of his appeal/petition - In exercise of discretion u/art. 136 to decide whether delay should be condoned or not, SC is not bound by the considerations applicable to an Appellate Court but nonetheless general principles which would weigh with the Appellate Court in determining sufficient cause can be the guiding factor/guideline - Therefore, it cannot be stated as a proposition per se that the prosecution of Review Proceedings would not be a sufficient cause at all for purposes of s. 5 of the Act - Direction issued to the Registry of SC to list the main matter for admission hearing - Order accordingly.
