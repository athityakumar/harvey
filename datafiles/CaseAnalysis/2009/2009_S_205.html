Ludhiana Improvement Trust, Ludhiana And Another v Shakti Co-Operative House Building Society Limited

Supreme Court of India

13 April 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

D.K. Jain, R.M. Lodha

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 441; (2009) 12 SCC 369; 2009 (2) AWC 1981; 2009 (2) CompLJ 321; 2009 (2) CPJ(Sc) 40; 2009 (2) CPR 341; 2009 (3) RCR(Civil) 447; 2009(5) SCALE 373; [2009] 6 S.C.R. 12

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection

Summary: Consumer Protection - Consumer Protection Act, 1986, s. 2(1)(r) - Unfair trade practice - Housing scheme - Acquisition of lands for plotting - Lands to be acquired also belonged to certain societies - Societies moved representations for exemption from such acquisitions - Exemption granted to the respondent society with certain conditions - Awards for acquisition made - Position was that respondent/society's land was neither taken into possession nor any compensation was paid - Govt. issued a notification u/s. 56 of Punjab Town Improvement Act, 1922, wherein proposal for acquisition of lands belonging to societies, was abandoned, respondent/society was one of them - In view of certain exigencies faced, respondent society requested the appellant/trust to allot plots to its member as large part of its land was encroached - Request denied - Later in 1990 request accepted when chairperson of the appellant/trust was changed - Carving out of plots by appellant/trust on land belonging to respondent/society - Dispute arose in respect of one plot which was not given by the appellant/trust to the respondent/society on ground of pending civil suit by one private party, which was decreed subsequently - After decree respondent/society requested issuance of alternative plot - Requested not acceded - Complaint to District Forum - Court noted the fact of registration of criminal complaints against inter alia former Chairperson of appellant/trust in view of irregularities committed in connivance with the management of societies - DF allowed the complaint - Order of DF affirmed by State Commission - National Commission dismissed revision petition filed thereagainst - Whether non-delivery of plot in question in facts of case, amounted to 'unfair trade practice' u/s 2(1)(r) of Act? - Definition of 'unfair trade practice' deliberated upon, precedent settled in M/s Lakhanpal's case looked into - Held, no 'unfair trade practice' made out - Cogent material in support thereof required - The averments in the complaint by the consumer cannot be taken as a Gospel truth - No reason(s) found in decisions of fora below in support of the conclusion that the appellant/trust was obliged to deliver to the respondent/society possession of plot in question or an alternative plot in lieu thereof - Facts showing no such obligation on part of appellant/trust - Absence of obligation further fortifies from the fact of existence of decree against that plot - Relevant factors ignored by fora below - Held, no material on record to return a finding that appellant/trust had indulged in 'unfair trade practice' - Appeal allowed.
