Nirbhai Kumar v Maya Devi & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

24 March 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Lokeshwar Singh Panta, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 376; (2009) 5 SCC 399; 2009 (75) ALR 606; 2009 (3) AWC 2233; JT 2009 (7) SC 32; 2009 (3) RCR(Civil) 921; 2009 (2) RCR(Rent) 184; 2009(5) SCALE 328; [2009] 4 S.C.R. 1052

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Rent Control

Keywords: Regulation Of Letting, U.P. Urban Buildings (Regulation of Letting, Rent and Eviction) Act, 1972

Summary: Rent Control - Land & Property - U.P. Urban Buildings (Regulation of Letting, rent and Eviction) Act, 1972 - Scope and ambit of proviso to s. 21(1)(a) - Conflicting decisions in Martin & Harris Ltd. v. VIth Additional District Judge and Ors. [1998 (1) SCC 732] and Anwar Hasan Khan v. Mohd. Shafi & Ors. [2001 (8) SCC 540] - Held, notice can be given either before or after the three years period - After expiry of the three years period the protection given to the tenant from being evicted has no further relevance - Thereafter it is only the question of notice - Decision in Martin & Harris Ltd.'s case expressed the correct view - Appeal allowed.
