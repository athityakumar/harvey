Villianur Iyarkkai Padukappu Maiyam v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Panchal, K.G. Balakrishnan, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1970; (2009) 7 SCC 561; 2009(8) SCALE 310; [2009] 9 S.C.R. 225

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Environment, Wildlife & Animal; Practice & Procedure; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Pondicherry (Administration) Act, 1962, Pondicherry (Laws) Regulation, 1963, Supreme Court Rules, 1960, Pondicherry Administration Regulations Act, 1963, Pondicherry (Alteration of name) Act, 2006

Summary: (A) Contract & Commercial - Legality of the awarding of contract - Respondent-State invited interested parties for the development of port in question though private investment - Several companies evinced interest to the project including 11th respondent-Company - Respondent shortlisted 4 companies including 11th respondent - Port Privatisation Committee (PPC) invited the shortlisted companies for presentation - PPC recommended the name of 11th respondent for the issuance of Letter of Intent (LoI) - Respondent issued LoI to 11th respondent - Lt. Governor granted approval to the Detailed Project Report submitted by 11th respondent on Build, Operate and Transfer basis (BOT) and to the Concession Agreement entered into between the respondent-State and the 11th respondent - Director of Ports issued direction to the officers concerned to prepare a list of all the existing moveable and immoveable assets of the port for handing over the same to 11th respondent - Appellants challenged the award of contract in a Public Interest Litigation (PIL) before HC - HC dismissed appellants writ petitions -Whether SC can consider the issue of selection of Developer in such circumstance wherein the appellant has fairly conceded before the HC that the selection of the Developer was correctly done by the respondents - Held, appellants fairly conceded before the HC that the selection of 11th respondent as Developer of the port was never canvassed nor the relative merits of the parties were pointed out to the HC and, therefore, the HC has not recorded any finding as to whether selection of 11th respondent as Developer was correct or not - In view of the concession made by the appellants before the HC in respect of the issue of selection of Developer, the appellants are not entitled to canvass before the SC that the selection of 11th respondent as Developer of the port was not correctly made.P /&gt;(B) Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Locus standi - Whether the appellant has locus standi to file the writ petition before HC challenging the award of contract for the development of the port to 11th respondent, which is purely commercial in nature - Held, neither the parties, which had participated in the process of selection of the consultant/Developer nor one of those, who had expressed desire to develop the port but was not selected, has come forward to challenge the selection procedure adopted by the respondent-State or the selection of 11th respondent as Developer of the port in question - Only ground on which the appellants could have maintained a PIL before the HC was to seek protection of the interest of the people of that area by safeguarding the environment - Appellants raised such issue before the HC and HC issued directions regarding the same, therefore, the element of public interest of the appellants' case no longer survives - Appellants cannot, therefore, proceed to challenge the award of contract in favour of 11th respondent on other grounds as that would amount to challenging the policy decision of the State through a PIL, which is not permissible.P /&gt;(C) Practice & Procedure - Supreme Court Rules, 1960, O. 16 r. 14(1)(B) - Whether the present appeal is liable to be dismissed on the reason that the list of events and dates submitted by the appellants is misleading - To enable the SC to know the factual background, in the absence of records, O. 16 r. 4(1)(b) of the Rules requires a list of dates in chronological order with relevant material facts or events pertaining to each of the dates to be furnished along with the special leave petition - Held, synopsis/list of dates filed by the appellant suffers from the defect of filing of a list of dates without relevant material facts/events or synopsis and from the defect of filing of inaccurate and incomplete synopsis/list of dates - List of events filed on behalf of the appellants would result in unintended miscarriage of justice to the respondents - List of events submitted on behalf of the appellants cannot be termed as accurate and such a practice of filing of incomplete/inaccurate list of events is not approved by SC.P /&gt;(D) Environment, Wildlife & Animal - Precautionary and Trusteeship principles - Whether impugned judgment is liable to be set aside on the ground that the environmental aspects were not examined by the State before sanctioning the project - Held, appropriate directions addressing all the issues raised on behalf of the appellants relating to the environment have been issued by the HC in its impugned order - Ministry of Environment and Forest which has to be given clearance for the project has to examine the proposals of the developer and follow due procedure before granting approval - Therefore, the judgment impugned is not liable to be set aside on the ground that environmental aspects were not examined by the State in total violation of the Precautionary and Trusteeship principles or that the project in question is completely prohibited under the Coastal Regulation Zone notification - Appeals dismissed.
