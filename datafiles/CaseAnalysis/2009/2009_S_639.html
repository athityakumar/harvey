Satish & Ors. v State Of U.P. & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

16 July 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 909; (2009) 14 SCC 758; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 1776; 2009 (6) ALD(SC) 91; 2009 (77) ALR 315; 2010 (1) AWC 438; JT 2009 (9) SC 479; 2009 (108) RD 511; 2009(9) SCALE 632; [2009] 11 S.C.R. 65; 2009 (3) UPLBEC 2327

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Appeals Disposed Of, Oil Mills, Valuation Of Land, Years Purchase

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 51A, 23, 4, 6, 9, and 17(1)(4) - Compensation - Valuation thereof - Sale deeds or comparable exemplars - Land acquisition for Thermal Power Station - Due to urgent and imminent need, provisions of s. 17(1)(4) were invoked - With due regard to extent of fertility of lands in question, they were categorized into two categories, 'Dakar Doyat Khaki' and 'Dakar Doyat Aabi' - Possession taken, award made - Out of submitted sale deeds, only one considered - Compensation determined was, Rs. 60, 189.72 p. per bigha for 'Dakar Doyat Khaki' and Rs. 82, 454.20 p. per bigha for 'Dakar Doyat Aabi', alongwith other sums on different considerations like interest, solatium, tree standing on the lands etc. - Reference u/s. 18 made - Reference was answered in two bunches, for first bunch compensation @ 115/-sq. yd was awarded and for another @ Rs. 155/-sq. yd. - In second reference, compensation was awarded @155/-sq. yd. - In appeal, High Court upheld award made by Collector, upholding thereby reliance placed by Collector on the sole sale deed which it had considered, keeping all other sale deeds out of consideration on the ground that they were measured in terms of square yards only - Judgment whether proper? - Held, No - Provisions perused, contentions heard - (A) Refusal to take into consideration large number of deeds of sale, whether proper? - Held, No - Position of law, after decision of Constitution Bench in 'Cement Corporation of India vs. Purya, (2004) 8 SCC 270', is that for praying a certified copy of a registered deed of sale, the vendor and vendee thereof need not be examined; further it was held that a presumption as to the genuineness of the contents of the document has to be raised - Thus all the deeds of sale which have been brought on record subject to the applicability thereof, ought to have been considered - Provisions of s. 23 of Act, further noted - Without assigning appropriate reasons, deed of sale submitted cannot be discarded; it must be done on some legal principle, rationality and cogent reasons - Court noted peculiar features of the case; acquired lands were located on both sides of the road, one side of which belonged to 'Gaon Sabha' and the other side was under a Municipality; and evidence was placed with respect to 'Gaon Sabha' land to show that the entire area is commonly known as 'Murad Pura area', i.e. the Municipality; this factor, Court noted, has to be considered - When comparable exemplars are brought on record, the one carrying the highest market value amongst them may be followed; Court upheld rate of Rs. 115/-per sq. yd. - Appeals disposed of.
