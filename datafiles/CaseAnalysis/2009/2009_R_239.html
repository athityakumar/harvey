Ratan Singh v State of Madhya Pradesh and Another

Supreme Court of India

24 April 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 559; (2009) 12 SCC 585; AIR 2010 SC 597; 2010 (5) RCR(Criminal) 561; 2010 (1) RCR(Criminal) 927; 2009(6) SCALE 499; [2009] 6 S.C.R. 976

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - IPC, 1860, 307, 148, 323 r/w 149, 307 r/w 149, 341, 294 - Cr.P.C, 1973, s. 482 - Petition questioning framing of charges under s. 307 or 307 r/w 149 allowed - Appeal against - Held, Court has to see whether the act, irrespective of its result, was done with the intention or knowledge and under circumstances mentioned in the Section - An accused charged under s. 307 cannot be acquitted merely because the injuries inflicted on the victim were in the nature of a simple hurt - Scope of interference under s. 482 at the present juncture as was done by the High Court is clearly unsustainable - Appeal allowed.
