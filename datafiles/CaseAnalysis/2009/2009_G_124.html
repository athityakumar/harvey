Guljar Singh And Ors v Deputy Director Consolidation And Ors

Supreme Court of India

15 April 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Tarun Chatterjee, Arijit Pasayat

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1990; (2009) 12 SCC 590; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 1560; 2009 (2) AWC 1856; 2009 (107) RD 78; 2009(5) SCALE 600; [2009] 5 S.C.R. 1080

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Constitution; Land & Property

Keywords: Uttar Pradesh Imposition of Ceiling on Land Holdings Act, 1961, UP Land Revenue Act, 1901

Summary: (A) Land & Property - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Appellants purchased the property in dispute and got their names entered into the revenue records - Appellants filed an application before the Sub-Divisional Officer, stating that 17 tenure holders including appellants divided the property in dispute and they were in possession but their names had not been recorded in the revenue records - Sub-Divisional Officer allowed application and correction proceedings started before Assistant Recording Officer - Assistant Recording Officer passed an order directing that entries were to be made in accordance with the order of the Sub-Divisional Officer - However, Consolidation Officer, in an application by a co-sharer, directed that entries in the revenue records were to be maintained - Appellants filed appeal before the Settlement Officer (Consolidation) - Settlement Officer (Consolidation) dismissed the appeals - Appellants filed revision before Deputy Director of Consolidation (DDC) - DDC dismissed the revision - Appellants challenged the order of DDC before HC - HC remanded the matter back to DDC - DDC allowed the revision of appellants after appreciating the evidence on record - Being dissatisfied with the order of DDC, appellants filed another writ petition before HC - HC affirmed the order of DDC - Whether SC can interfere with the findings of HC and DDC - SC is entitled to interfere with the judgment of the HC u/art. 136 of the Constitution only when there is gross irregularity in the judgment of the HC or any substantial grounds of law which are of public importance have been raised in such petition - Held, concurrent findings of fact arrived at by the authorities and affirmed by the HC would clearly show that such findings were arrived at on consideration of all materials placed before the court and after giving proper hearing to the parties - Such findings of fact, cannot be interfered with in the exercise of power u/art. 136 of the Constitution.P /&gt;(B) Administrative - Jurisdiction of DDC - Uttar Pradesh Reorganization Act, 2000, ss. 86, 88 - Uttar Pradesh Imposition of Ceiling on Land Holdings Act, 1961, s. 48 - (A) Whether the Additional District Magistrate of the newly created State has the jurisdiction to exercise power of the Deputy Director of Consolidation (DDC) of the erstwhile State - Held, all the notifications issued by the erstwhile state are applicable to the newly created State u/s. 86 r/w s. 88 of the Act - Therefore, Additional District Magistrate of the newly created State has the jurisdiction to exercise power of the DDC of the erstwhile State - (B) Jurisdiction of DDC - Whether DDC has the jurisdiction to interfere with the concurrent findings of the Consolidation Officer and the Settlement Officer (Consolidation) - Held, the entries in the Revenue record are fictitious or forged or they were recorded in contravention of the statutory provisions contained in the Uttar Pradesh Land Records Manual or other allied statutory provisions, the DDC would have full power u/s. 48 to re-appraise or re-evaluate the evidence on record so as to finally determine the rights of the parties by excluding forged or fictitious revenue entries or entries not made in accordance with law - Appeals dismissed.
