Suresh Kumar Bansal v Krishna Bansal and Another

Supreme Court of India

14 December 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Tarun Chatterjee, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1923; (2010) 2 SCC 162; AIR 2010 SC 344; 2010 (1) ALD(SC) 146; 2010(1) ALL MR 961; 2010 (78) ALR 452; 2010 (1) AWC 816; 2010 (2) JhrCR(SC) 32; JT 2009 (15) SC 247; 2010 (5) MLJ(SC) 1120; 2010 (2) MPLJ 304; 2010 (1) RCR(Civil) 839; 2010 (1) RCR(Rent) 137; 2010 (109) RD 256; 2009(14) SCALE 556; [2009] 16 S.C.R. 419; 2010 (1) UC 590

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Keywords: Application For Impleadment

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Impleadment of heir - Plaintiff filed suit for eviction against defendant - Plaintiff died during pendency of proceedings - Respondent No.1 (wife of plaintiff) filed application for substitution as an heir and legal representative in suit - Appellant (brother of plaintiff) also sought substitution/impleadment as heir on basis of Will executed by plaintiff in his favour - Application filed by respondent no. 1 was allowed but application of appellant was rejected on ground that execution of Will was suspicious - Writ petition filed thereagainst also rejected - Hence, present petition - Whether appellant entitled to be impleaded/substituted in suit for eviction along with natural heirs and legal representatives of plaintiff? - Held, yes - Since question of genuineness of will cannot be conclusively gone into by court in a proceeding for substitution in a pending eviction suit and in view of the fact that an application was made at the instance of appellant for impleadment as a legal representative of deceased on basis of the Will which is yet to be probated, impleadment of appellant in eviction proceeding should be allowed - However, in case decree is to be passed for eviction of tenant from suit premises such eviction decree shall be subject to grant of probate of Will alleged to have been executed by plaintiff - In case probate is not granted, court shall proceed to grant eviction decree in favour of respondent no.1 and not in favour of appellant - Impugned order of HC set aside - Application for impleadment filed by appellant allowed - Appeal allowed to extent indicated above - Order accordingly.
