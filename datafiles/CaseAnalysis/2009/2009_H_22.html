National Insurance Company Limited v Hamida Khatoon and Others

Supreme Court of India

06 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 628; (2009) 13 SCC 361; 2009 (2) ACC 462; AIR 2009 SC 2599; 2009 (6) ALD(SC) 86; 2009 (76) ALR 665; 2009 (4) AWC 3326; 2010 (125) FLR 331; 2010 (1) LLJ 323; 2009 (156) PLR 755; 2009 (4) RLW 3706; 2009(7) SCALE 133; [2009] 8 S.C.R. 248

Case Digest

Subject: Insurance

Keywords: Employees' State Insurance Act, 1948, Insured Person, Employees State Insurance (Central) Rules, 1950, Permanent Total Disablement, Permanent Partial Disablement

Summary: Insurance - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, s. 173 - Employees' State Insurance Act, 1948, s. 53 - Compensation under MV Act vis-a-vis benefits under ESI Act - Bar created u/s. 53 of ESI Act - Claim petition u/s. 173 of MV Act for compensation - Bar against receiving or recovery of compensation or damages under any other law as provided u/s. 53 of ESI Act - Maintainability of claim petition challenged in view of s. 53 of ESI Act - HC rejected the challenge on ground that such plea was not taken specifically in the written statement - Whether s. 53 of ESI Act is to be considered by Motor Accident Claim Tribunal(MACT) before deciding upon the entitlement under MV Act - Held, Yes - Decision in 'A Trehan v. Associated Electrical Agencies (1996 (4) SCC 255' referred and relied, wherein elaborate discussion on the subject was made - 'Bar is against receiving or recovering any compensation or damages under the Workmen's Compensation Act or any other law for the time being in force or otherwise in respect of an employment injury; the bar is absolute as can be seen from the use of the words...' - Position of law as settled looked into - Direction issued as to that entitlement shall be worked out by MACT by taking note of s. 53 of ESI Act - Appeal allowed.
