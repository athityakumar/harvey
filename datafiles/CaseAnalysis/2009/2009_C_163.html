Kushal Fertilisers Private Limited v Commissioner of Customs and Central Excise, Meerut

Supreme Court of India

06 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 642; (2009) 13 SCC 515; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 1102; 2009 (238) E.L.T. 21; JT 2009 (13) SC 293; 2009(7) SCALE 476; [2009] 8 S.C.R. 238

Case Digest

Subject: Excise

Keywords: Levy Of Duty

Summary: Excise - Central Excise Act, 1944, ss. 11A, 11 A(1) 35G - Central Excise Rules, 1944, rr. 174, 209 A - Limitation - Demand - Penalty - Exemption - Notification No. 202/88 - CE., dt. 20-05-1988 - M.S. conduit pipes - Appellant engaged in manufacturing of M.S. conduit pipes w.e.f. 29-03-1990 - On 22-01-1991 appellant informed Section Officer of Central Excise that it had been manufacturing M.S. conduit pipes and its production is exempt from payment of Excise Duty in terms of Notification No.202/98-CE - Show cause notice issued on or about 28-03-1994, proposing demand of duty u/s. 11A of Act, 1944 and also asking as to why penalty shall not be levied in terms of r. 209A of Rules contending that appellant was not entitled for exemption as gate passes covering inputs described product as 'bars' and it suppressed said fact with an intention to evade payment of duty - On appeal, Commissioner held that as appellant suppressed material facts with an intention to evade payment of duty as a result extended period of limitation could be invoked - On appeal, Tribunal held that dept. had acquired knowledge, on receipt of letter dt. 22-01-1991 and extended period is applicable only prior to 22.01.91 - (A) Whether non furnishing of information was willful and would amount to suppression of material fact in terms whereof extended period of limitation as provided for in s. 11A of Central Excise Act, 1944 could be invoked or not? - Held, if on materials produced by parties, Tribunal had arrived at a finding of fact that there had been no suppression on part of appellant after 22.01.91, question of invoking extended period of jurisdiction did not arise - Show cause notice dt. 28-03-1994 issued after expiry of period prescribed u/s. 11A of Act, was clearly barred by limitation - (B) Whether Reference filed by Commissioner of Central Excise before HC was maintainable u/s. 35G of Act? - Held, HC although initially treated reference to be an appeal in terms of s. 35G of Act, a Division Bench thereof was of opinion that same was a reference in terms of s. 35-G of Act - Reference could be made on a question of law - Such a question of law is required to be formulated by HC itself - Even otherwise question of law purported to have been referred to by Commissioner of Central Excise would have been maintainable provided a substantial question of law arose for consideration of High Court and not otherwise - In present case, whether a party is guilty of suppression of fact or not is essentially a question of fact and it does not per se give rise to substantial question of law per se, hence reference not maintainable - Appeal allowed.
