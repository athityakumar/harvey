Zindar Ali Sk v State of West Bengal and Another

Supreme Court of India

06 February 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

V.S. Sirpurkar, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 114; (2009) 2 SCC (Cr) 737; (2009) 3 SCC 761; AIR 2009 SC 1467; 2009 ALL MR (Cri) 896; 2009 (2) CalLT(SC) 48; 2009 CRLJ 1324; JT 2009 (2) SC 253; 2009 (2) MLJ(Crl) 596; 2009 (2) RCR(Criminal) 26; 2009(2) SCALE 248; [2009] 1 S.C.R. 968

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - IPC, 1860, ss. 376, 417 - Appeal against conviction and sentence - Held, truthful version of the prosecutrix cannot be ignored - Defence cannot take advantage of bad investigation where there is clinching evidence available to the prosecution - Confirmed the finding of the High Court that the accused is guilty of the offence under s. 376 - If the promise of marriage was given and the girl had succumbed on that account, by itself, may not amount to cheating - Acquitted the accused of the offence under s. 417 - Sentence already suffered would be sufficient - Appeal disposed of.
