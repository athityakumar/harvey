M. P. Palanisamy And Others v A. Krishnan and Others

Supreme Court of India

15 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

V.S. Sirpurkar, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 678; (2009) 6 SCC 428; AIR 2009 SC 2809; 2009 (3) ESC 523; 2009 (6) MLJ(SC) 65; 2009(8) SCALE 437; [2009] 9 S.C.R. 490; 2010 (1) SLR 485

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Ad Hoc Appointee, Temporary Appointees, Tamil Nadu Public Service Commission Regulations, 1954

Summary: Service - Tamil Nadu State and Subordinate Services Rules, rr. 10(a)(i)(1) and 23(a)(i) - GOMs 1813, D/-12-12-1988 - Inter se seniority - Of higher secondary school teachers called Post Graduate Assistants(PGAs) who were appointed from the year 1978 - Sudden decision by State of TN introducing 10+2 system; consequent upgradation of 800 odd matriculation schools; unprecedented rise in school students and the resultant demand of the teachers - To the post of PGAs initially appointments were made from those teachers who were earlier called BT Assistants who possessed Master degrees; and rest of the demand was met through employment exchanges - After 28-04-1981 adhoc appointment were also made u/r. 10(a)(i)(1) of Rules - TN Public Service Commission than in 1984 began appointments to the post of PGAs through competitive examination - Teachers were thus in three categories, first, those PGAs who were appointed in between 01-07-1978 and 28-04-1981, second, who were duly selected by TNPSC and appointed in 1986, third, who were appointed temporarily u/r. 10(a)(i)(1) after 28-04-1981 and regularized ultimately by order D/- 21/22-12-1988 placing them below the TNPSC candidates of second category - Grievance thus raised by third category who claimed that unbroken and continuous service of them from the date of their appointment, though in the ad hoc capacity, should be recognized as such, over and above those who come in the service for the first time in 1986 by way of selection by TNPSC; third category PGAs were regularized in the year 1988 and their claim was based on such regularization - Claim of the appellants whether sustainable? - Held, No - Court noted the exigencies of the situation faced by the State and the consequent actions taken thereupon - Admitted facts noted by Court as to that TNPSC had given opportunity to the adhoc PGAs for sitting in the examination but almost no one sit in for such examination and the ones who gave were failed; and after constant representations by such adhoc PGAs there demand of regularization was met in the year 1988; significant fact was that that the regularization was conditional one; conditions noted; such conditions were accepted by the appellants; appellants preferred OA in 1991 and had not challenged the second condition of regularization - Second condition was that adhoc PGAs would have to take their seniority below the last candidate selected by TNPSC in the examination - Accepting conditions of regularization and selectively challenging the second condition not at the first opportunity but later on, one of reason for rejecting claim of appellants - Non-giving of examination, appointment on adhoc basis and regularization on constant pressurization; they were thus never the members of the service but for such regularization - Proviso to r. 23(a)(i) of rules noted, which showed that in case of an ad-hoc employee, he cannot claim any seniority to the 'junior most person already in service', words used found to be very important; all the TNPSC PGAs were already in service when the question of regularization of adhoc PGAs came for consideration; stance of Govt. in providing for said conditions found to be absolutely correct and by mere subsequent regularization, that too without taking any examination under TNPSC or undergoing any recruitment process and facing general competition from the other candidates, the ad-hoc PGAs could not be held seniors to those, who were already in service; Court remarked upon the fact of failure of some of the adhoc PGAs in the examination, that it would be preposterous if those failed candidates who were later on regularized, are placed over and above the successful candidates in TNPSC examination - Impugned judgement upheld - Appeals dismissed.
