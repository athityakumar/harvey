Union Bank Of India And Another v Panchanan Subudhi

Supreme Court of India

02 March 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Agrawal, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1228; (2010) 15 SCC 552

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance

Summary: Banking & Finance - Securitisation and Reconstruction of Financial Assets and Enforcement of Security Interest Act, 2002, ss. 13(2), 13(4), 17 - Non-performing asset - One time settlement - Jurisdiction - Respondent had obtained financial facility from appellant bank, subsequently his account became non-performing asset - Appellant filed suit for recovery before Tribunal, which decreed suit in favour of appellant - On appeal, HC stayed proceeding subject to respondent depositing sum of Rs.10 lakhs - Mean time respondent approached appellants and agreed to one time settlement, but failed to abide by terms of one time settlement - Appellant issued notice for possession of secured assets - Aggrieved respondent filed writ petition restraining appellants, which was allowed by HC - Hence instant appeal.

Held, perusal of record shows that appellant extended financial facility to respondent to extent of Rs.10 lakhs. Respondent deposited title deeds of land and building as security. On 31-12-2000, account of respondent was declared as non-performing asset and petition was filed before Debts Recovery Tribunal. During pendency of petition appellant issued notice u/s. 13(2) of Act. This was followed by notice issued u/s. 13(4) of Act. By order Tribunal passed decree in favour of appellant for sum of Rs.16, 10,957/- along with pendent elite and future interest at 12%. Respondent challenged proceedings initiated under Act in Writ Petition. HC stayed proceeding subject to respondent depositing sum of Rs.10 lakhs, which latter never deposited. In interregnum, respondent approached bank for one time settlement. Bank agreed for settlement, but respondent failed to abide by conditions of settlement. Consequently, bank issued notice for possession of secured assets. Respondent challenged said action writ petition, which has been disposed of by HC in manner indicated hereinabove. In Court’s view, approach adopted by HC was clearly erroneous, when respondent failed to abide by terms of one time settlement, there was no justification for HC to entertain writ petition and that too by ignoring fact that statutory alternative remedy was available to respondent u/s. 17 of Act. Order is set aside and writ petition filed by respondent before HC is dismissed. Appeal allowed.
