Amarjeet Singh and Others v Devi Ratan and Others

Supreme Court of India

18 November 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

H.S. Bedi, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 2066; (2010) 1 SCC 417; AIR 2010 SC 3676; 2010 (2) AWC 2107; 2010 (124) FLR 286; JT 2009 (15) SC 110; 2010 (1) MLJ(SC) 833; 2009(14) SCALE 149; [2009] 15 S.C.R. 1010; 2010 (6) SLR 380

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: U.P. Government Servants Seniority Rules, 1991, U.P. Assistant Excise Commissioners Service Rules, 1992, U.P. Excise Service (Class-II) Rules, 1970

Summary: Service - U.P. Excise Service (Class-II) Rules, 1970 - U.P. Assistant Excise Commissioners Service Rules, 1992 - Inter se seniority - Determination of - Appellants and respondents were appointed as Excise Inspectors - Parties became eligible for consideration for promotion under the Rules, 1970 - However, Rules were amended in the year 1994, changing the criterion for promotion from "merit" to "seniority subject to rejection of unfit" - 42 posts of AEC were to be filled up - State Government wanted to fill up the said vacancies by applying the amended rules - On being challenged, the HC held that the vacancies which occurred prior to the amendment of 1992 Rules had to be filled up according to the unamended rules - Operation of the judgment and order of the HC was stayed by SC making it clear that promotions so made under the amended rules would be subject to the decision in special leave petition - Accordingly, 61 Officers/respondents were promoted under the amended rules - Subsequently, SC dismissed the SLP - Respondents so promoted were not reverted - DPC was held on 19.12.1998 to fill up said 42 vacancies, but only 30 candidates (appellants) were found eligible to be promoted and respondents were found unsuitable - State Government carried forward the remaining 12 vacancies and another DPC was convened on 22.1.1999 and respondents were promoted on the basis of different criterion - Promotions were made with retrospective effect determining the year wise vacancies - Subsequently, State Government issued the order giving notional promotions with retrospective effect to appellants as well as all the reverted officers/respondents - As a consequence, a seniority list was issued, wherein the appellants were placed over and above the respondents - Being aggrieved, the respondents approached the HC challenging the said seniority list - HC held that as the postings to both set of officers had been given notional promotion from one and the same date, their inter se seniority was to be fixed as it existed in the feeding cadre of Excise Inspectors and thus quashed the seniority list and further directed the State to prepare a fresh seniority list placing the appellants below the respondents - Hence, present appeals - Held, HC committed an error while recording the finding of fact that both set of officers had been promoted notionally from one and the same date - Admittedly, promotions were not made with effect from one and the same date - Appellants and respondents were promoted against the vacancies which had occurred in different recruitment years under different Rules and on different criterion - Thus, the respondents would rank below the appellants in seniority - Therefore, there could be no justification to hold that their inter se seniority in the feeding cadre would be relevant for determining the seniority of AECs - Appellants had been promoted with an earlier date, thus, are bound to be senior than respondents who had been promoted with respect from a later date - Appeals allowed.
