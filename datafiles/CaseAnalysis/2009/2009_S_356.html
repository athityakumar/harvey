U. Suvetha v State By Inspector of Police and Another

Supreme Court of India

06 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, R.M. Lodha

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 652; (2009) 3 SCC (Cr) 36; (2009) 6 SCC 757; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 1451; 2009 CRLJ 2974; 2009 (3) JCC 1677; 2009 (2) KLT 686; 2009 (2) RCR(Criminal) 923; 2009(7) SCALE 149; [2009] 7 S.C.R. 902

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Family & Personal

Keywords: Grand-Daughter, Concubine

Summary: Criminal - Family & Personal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 498A - Whether the term 'relative of husband of a woman' within the meaning of s. 498A IPC should be given an extended meaning so as to include 'girlfriend' of husband within it? - Held, No - Allegations made in FIR, roping in 'girlfriend' of husband among others, noted by Court - Application for discharge was made by appellant/'girlfriend', which was dismissed and further revision application filed before High Court was also dismissed, thus the present appeal - Provision of s. 498A, perused; meaning of 'relative' as per lexicons, statutes and decisions of Court, looked into - Genesis of s. 498A i.e. its insertion in IPC vide Criminal Law (Second Amendment) Act, 1983 further noted - Fact of appellant not being charged for abetment of a crime, and non existence of any sort of conspiracy entered into between accused persons, noted; it was not a case of first informant/wife that appellant had any role to play with regard to demand of dowry - Further important facts as to that relationship of the appellant with the husband of first informant was existent before the marriage and they lived separately, and purported torture by husband was either inflicted at her in-laws' place or at her parents' place, noted; no allegation that appellant had any role to play in that regard - Word 'cruelty' per s. 498A, defined in terms of explanation cannot be given any other meaning; thus living with another woman may be an act of cruelty on the part of the husband for the purpose of judicial separation or dissolution of marriage but the same, would not attract the wrath of s. 498A, opined the Court - If appellant had been instigating the husband of the first informant/wife to torture her, husband would be committing some offence punishable under other provisions of IPC and appellant may be held guilty for abetment of commission of such an offence but not an offence u/s. 498A, Court reasoned - Word 'relative'; meaning to be given, in absence of definition, as understood in common parlance; and principally it includes a person related by blood, marriage or adoption - Provision of s. 498A being penal it deserves strict construction, save and except where a contextual meaning is required to be given to a statute - Finding, 'girl friend' or even a 'concubine' cannot be in an etymological sense a 'relative'; word 'relative' brings within its purview a status and such a status must be conferred either by blood or marriage or adoption and if no marriage has taken place, the question of one being relative of another would not arise - Appellant not a relative of husband of the first informant - Impugned judgment unsustainable - Appeal allowed.
