Ram Pat and Others v State of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

12 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 970; (2009) 3 SCC (Cr) 488; (2009) 7 SCC 614; AIR 2009 SC 2847; JT 2009 (7) SC 579; 2010 (2) MLJ(Crl) 89; 2010 (5) RCR(Criminal) 145; 2009(8) SCALE 145; [2009] 8 S.C.R. 1115; 2010 (1) ShimLC 438

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Revenue Record, Self-Defence, Specific Plea, Non Explanation, Right Of Self Defence, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006.)

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 302 r/w 149, 447, 148 and 506 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 110 - Right of self/private defence with respect to property; who was the aggressor? - Injuries on the person of accused; non-explanation thereof; effect thereof - Presumption as to possession of land - Acquittal by Trial Court - Reversed by High Court - Whether proper? - Held, yes - First informant(PW8) was father of deceased; at the time of occurrence PW8 was ploughing his field with the help of tractor and his father i.e. the deceased was standing at a distance - Accused persons entered the field armed with lathies and jellies, and launched an attack on the deceased; incidence lasted for only 2 to 21/2 minutes - Defence case was that they had purchased the land in question alongwith other lands belonging to the deceased, under respective two of the deeds of sale; their further case was that, that they had sown the crop of Bajra 16 days prior to the occurrence - Admitted factual position of the case was that the lands in question were comprised in the properties which were undivided - Sole issue of the case was, whether the purported overt acts, as to sowing of Bajra crops, committed by the accused on 28-06-1993 would amount to 'settled possession' so as to enable them to exercise their right of private defence in respect of the property? - Held, no - Contentions heard, litigation between the parties at various stages, noted, revenue records looked into, materials on record perused -P /&gt;(A) Question of possession of land - Admitted position, accused had not purchased any specific portion of the property; they purchased undivided share - In law, they had not acquire any right to obtain possession of the lands; deceased and his family being the co-sharers did not give any consent for handing over their possession in their favour - Further, in law, the accused persons being purchasers of an undivided share merely acquired a right to sue for partition; even their vendor could not have put them in possession - Settled decisions quoted - Nothing brought on record to establish that in fact physical possession had been handed over by all the co-sharers - Despite of the fact that accused had committed some overt acts, Court noted the fact that questioning the validity or otherwise of the deeds of sale in question, father of the deceased filed a suit and an ad interim order of injunction was passed in their favour; complaint u/ss. 107 and 151 of CrPC was also filed and was strongly relied upon; Court perused the complaint - Entries in the revenue records stood in the name of the deceased and his family - There was no denial or dispute, that the deceased and his family were in possession prior to the date of overt act by the accused - Presumption u/s. 110, Evidence Act, raised by the Court, that the deceased and the members of his family continued to be in possession - (i) Inference drawn by the Court - Solitary overt act on the part of the accused in sowing the crop of Bajra, which had not been repeated on days subsequent to 14-06-1993 (date of order of injunction against accused) in respect whereof even some litigations started and, thus, the same cannot give rise to an inference that the accused were in settled possession of the land and other attributes in regard thereto have been satisfied so as to enable them to claim a right of private defence in respect of the property - 'Trespassers not only must be put in actual physical possession of the property but also must continue to be in possession', Puran Singh's case - Acquiescence to act of purported possession by the accused on the part of the complainant would arise only if an attempt is made to take possession in their presence - (ii) Whether non-explanation of injuries on the persons of two of the accused, fatal to the prosecution case? - Held, No - Facts noted - First informant/PW8 was unarmed, so also the deceased; it was only one LS/PW12 who had in his hand a small twig(Kamari), which he used in his sole defence as a result whereof the two accused sustained injuries; twig was kept by PW12 not as any preparation, but it has a general use to drive camels - Injuries in question, perused; found to be simple in nature - Position is well settled, that whereas grievous injuries suffered by the accused are required to be explained by the prosecution, simple injuries need not necessarily be -P /&gt;(B) Question of right of private defence - Facts noted - Right of private defence can be exercised provided any occasion arises therefor - Accused persons were not in settled possession; if they were not, they had no right of private defence to defend the possession of the property - Accused persons, were, thus, the aggressors being fully armed -P /&gt;(C) Position of law, if two views are possible, the appellate Court should not interfere with a judgment of acquittal, but with exceptions - Whether interference by HC in judgment of acquittal by Trial Court, proper or whether in the present case, two views were not possible? - Held, yes, present is the case where two views were not possible - Appeal dismissed.
