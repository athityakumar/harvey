Bhupinder Kumar v Angrej Singh

Supreme Court of India

28 August 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, Deepak Verma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 2016; (2009) 8 SCC 766; 2009(6) ALL MR 464; 2010 (2) AWC 1378; 2009 (8) MLJ(SC) 417; 2010 (1) MPLJ 474; 2009 (4) RCR(Civil) 249; 2009(12) SCALE 4; [2009] 13 S.C.R. 978

Case Digest

Subject: Contract & Commercial

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Specific Relief Act, 1963, s. 28 - Power to extend time when decree holder failed to deposit balance amount of money - Appellant filed suit alleging that he entered into an agreement to purchase land and paid earnest amount to respondent but respondent failed to execute sale deed - Addl. Civil Judge decreed suit and directed respondent to get sale deed executed and registered in favour of appellant qua suit property - Appellant moved an application for extension of time to deposit balance of sale price as respondent failed to get sale deed executed - Addl. Civil Judge dismissed application for extension of time to deposit balance sale price and held that sale agreement stood rescinded as contemplated u/s. 28 of Specific Relief Act - On appeal, district judge dismissed appeal as not maintainable - HC also dismissed revision petition upholding order passed by executing court - Hence, present appeal - (A) Whether Court has power to extend time in favour of a decree holder to pay balance amount/perform conditions as mentioned in decree for specific performance? - Held, Yes - Court has power to extend time in favour of decree holder to pay amount as directed or perform conditions mentioned in decree - In absence of plausible reasons, executing court has dismissed his application for extension of time and also allowed application filed by judgment debtor for rescission of contract u/s. 28 of Act - (B) Whether appellant had shown sufficient and reasonable ground for extension of time? - Held, no - Neither any material showed that appellant was having required money nor had he tendered or deposited same as per terms of decree - Both executing court and HC found that there was no just and reasonable cause to extend time for depositing balance consideration - Appeal dismissed.
