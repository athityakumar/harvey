Ashok Kumar Mittal v Ram Kumar Gupta & Anr

Supreme Court of India

09 January 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, J.M. Panchal

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 781; (2009) 2 SCC 656; (2009) 1 SCC (Cr) 836; 2009 (1) AWC 651; 2009 (1) CTC 648; 2009 (234) E.L.T. 193; JT 2009 (1) SC 382; 2009 (1) KLT 398; 2009 (1) RCR(Civil) 713; 2009 (1) RCR(Rent) 114; 2009(1) SCALE 321; [2009] 1 S.C.R. 125; 2009 (1) UC 498

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Award Of Costs, Compensatory Costs, Limitations

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Legal Services Authorities Act, 1987 - 'State' - Meaning of - Petitioner filed a suit for specific performance against the defendant - Trial Court dismissed the suit - Plaintiff filed appeal before HC - HC imposed the cost of Rs. 1,00,000/- each on plaintiff and on defendant which were directed to pay to High Court Legal Services Committee (HCLSC) - Whether the direction to pay the cost to HCLSC, is proper - Held, HCLSC, a statutory authority, is not the 'state' - Once the Court held that costs had to be paid to the state, it should have directed payment of the costs to the state and not to the HCLSC - No litigant should be made to feel that heavy costs are being levied in some cases by Judges to create a corpus or expense fund for the HCLSC - Therefore, direction issued to HCLSC to make over the amount received from the parties to the State - Petition dismissed.
