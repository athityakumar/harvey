T. Gnanavel v T.S. Kanagaraj And Anr.

Supreme Court of India

25 February 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Tarun Chatterjee, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 347; (2009) 14 SCC 294; AIR 2009 SC 2367; 2009 (75) ALR 515; 2009 (3) AWC 2341; 2010 (2) JhrCR(SC) 164; JT 2009 (3) SC 197; 2009 (4) MLJ(SC) 131; 2009 (2) RCR(Civil) 707; 2009 (1) RCR(Rent) 470; 2009 (107) RD 305; 2009(4) SCALE 482; [2009] 3 S.C.R. 451; 2009 (3) UC 1994

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Death Of Defendant, Application For Impleadment, Several Defendants, One Of Several Defendants, Death Of One Of Several Plaintiffs

Summary: Civil Procedure - Question relates to the interpretation of O. 21 r. 4(4) - Whether the decree passed by the civil court in ignorance of the death of the defendant, without granting any exemption to the appellant before the judgment was pronounced, as required under O. 21 r. 4 (4), is sustainable in law? - Held, where within the time limited by law, no application is made under sub-rule 1, the suit shall abate as against the deceased defendant - Exemption to bring on record the heirs and legal representatives of the deceased could be taken or granted by the court only before the judgment is pronounced and not after it - High Court had rightly interpreted the provision of O. 22 r. 4 (4) and accordingly held that the decree passed by the trial court was a nullity - Appeal dismissed.
