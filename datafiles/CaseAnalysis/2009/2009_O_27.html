Venkateswara Syndicate v Oriental Insurance Company Limited and Another

Supreme Court of India

24 August 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Dattu, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1046; (2009) 8 SCC 507; 2010 (2) AWC 1809; 2009 (3) CPJ(Sc) 81; JT 2009 (12) SC 63; 2009 (4) RCR(Civil) 872; 2009(11) SCALE 597; [2009] 14 S.C.R. 57; 2010 (1) UC 162

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection

Keywords: Fire Insurance Policy, Insurance (Amendment) Act, 1968, Insurance Surveyors and Loss Assessors (Licensing, Professional Requirements and Code of Conduct) Regulations, 2000

Summary: Consumer Protection - Consumer Protection Act, 1986 - Fire Insurance - Settlement of claim - Delay - Appellant-firm's cotton stock has been met with a fire accident - Respondent-insurer appointed several surveyors including joint surveyor - Being dissatisfied with report of joint surveyors, respondent appointed a chartered accountant (CA) - Appellant filed complaint before National Commission for delay in disbursal of insurance money with interest - National Commission ordered for settlement of claim as per chartered accountant report with 6% interest - Legality - Held, respondent constrained to appoint CA due to discrepancies in joint surveyors report - Joint surveyors failed to go through records of appellant-firm - CA noticed all omissions and books of accounts of appellant-firm before giving final report - Respondent in its affidavit stated valid reasons for appointment of 2nd surveyor and non-acceptance of their report - Respondent accepted CA report after rejecting reports of 1st surveyor and joint surveyors, hence, no unnecessary delay in settling claim - However, appellant-firm entitled interest at rate of 9% from date of assessment done by CA till its realization - Appeal partly allowed.
