Revenue Divisional Officer-Cum-L.A.O v Shaik Azam Saheb Etc

Supreme Court of India

13 January 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Lokeshwar Singh Panta, B. SUDARSHAN REDDY

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1006; (2009) 4 SCC 395; AIR 2009 SC 1914; 2009(2) ALL MR 972; 2009 (2) AWC 1617; JT 2009 (3) SC 456; 2009 (5) MahLJ 160; 2009 (3) RCR(Civil) 114; 2009 (107) RD 640; 2009(1) SCALE 545; [2009] 1 S.C.R. 289; 2009 (2) UPLBEC 1188

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Valuation Of Land

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 18, 4(1) - Acquisition of land - Award - Market value - Sustainability - State issued notification u/s. 4(1) of Act expressing intention to acquire land for establishing Post Graduation Centre - Out of the said 87 Acres 96 cents of land, Court was concerned only with 25 acres of land of which respondents were owners - Land Acquisition Officer made award fixing market value of lands at rate of Rs.15,000/- per acre for lands situated in 'T' village and Rs.16,000/- per acre for the lands situated in 'P' - Dissatisfied with quantum of compensation awarded respondents sought for reference from Collector before Civil Court in terms of s. 18 of the said Act - Trial court by a judgment and award enhanced market value thereof from Rs.15,000/- to Rs.90,000/- per acre for the lands situated in 'T' village and from Rs.16,000/- to Rs.1,00,000/- per acre for lands situated in 'P' - Appeal was preferred by parties - DB enhanced market value of land to Rs.1,90,000/- per acre uniformly for lands situated in both villages - Hence, instant appeals -Whether DB was justified in enhancing market value of land to Rs.1,90,000/- per acre uniformly for lands situated in both villages - Held, determination of market value of land acquired in terms of provisions of Act depends upon large number of factors, nature and quality of land, irrigated or non-irrigated, extent of facilities available for irrigation, location of land, closeness thereof from any road or highway, evenness of land, its position in different seasons particularly in rainy season, existence of any building or structure as also development in and around area - One other important factor which should also be borne in mind was that it might could not be safe to rely only on award involving a neighboring area irrespective of nature and quality of land - Therefore, to determine market value again, positive and negative factors germane should be taken into consideration - Court had adopted same method which had been adopted by Reference Judge inasmuch as Reference Judge had fixed market value for lands situated in 'P' village at Rs. 1,00,000/- per acre and Rs.90, 000/- per acre in respect of 'T' village - Appeal allowed.
