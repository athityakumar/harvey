Haryana State Electricity Board & Anr v Gulshan Lal & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

06 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 763; (2009) 12 SCC 231; AIR 2009 SC 3139; JT 2009 (6) SC 95; 2009(8) SCALE 525; [2009] 8 S.C.R. 950; 2010 (8) SLR 244

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service

Keywords: Doctrine Of Equal Pay For Equal Work

Summary: Service - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14 and 39(d) - Principle of 'equality' - Doctrine of 'equal pay for equal work' - Appellant/Board had two Thermal plants both having different cadre strengths - Suits were filed by respondents and others belonging to one of the plant, claiming higher pay scale as was getting paid to their counterparts in the other plant, pointing out anomaly in the scale of pay of two thermal power stations - There was one another suit which was filed by Anil Kapoor and others and which was pending disposal before High Court - Suits filed and writ petitions preferred were decreed/allowed on premise that the plaintiffs/petitioners were similarly situated to Anil Kapoor and others - Whether such decrees and judgements proper and justified? - Held, No - AK has joined before 01-01-1986 and respondents had joined after 01-01-1986, thus they could not have claimed parity with AK moreover their scales of pay were also different - Averments made in plaints, contentions perused - No fundamental fact as regards basis of their entitlement was laid; entire claim proceeded on the basis that they were senior to AK - Respondents and others were found to be woefully short in qualifications - Court noted that appellant is a 'State' within the meaning of art. 12 of Constitution; for holding a public office, an employee must possess the requisite prescribed qualification, in absence whereof the additional reliefs could not have been granted to them relying on or on the basis of the judgment passed in the earlier cases - Unequals cannot be treated as equals; and in instant case equality doctrine has been invoked only on the basis of relief granted in the case of AK; decree granted by a competent court of law is no doubt binding on the employer, but when in a subsequent litigation the absurd result emanating from the cascading effect thereof becomes apparent before another Court and it is found that the said judgment is illegal, it is well settled, that by application of art. 14 alone, similar relief should not be granted; equality clause carries with it a positive effect - Doctrine of equal pay for equal work cannot be applied automatically, as it involves several factors like qualification, experience and other factors; same or similar nature of work, by itself, does not entitled an employee to invoke the doctrine - If impugned judgments are sustained, anomalous situation would be created as to that juniors of the respondents may be getting a higher pay; it has a cascading effect, viz., those employees who are junior to the respondents and/or even AK would be entitled to claim parity in the scale of pay as also in designation - Position is well settled that 'nobody should suffer owing to the mistake on the part of the Court in view of the maxim 'actus curiae neminem gravabit' - Impugned judgments unsustainable, set aside - Appeals allowed.
