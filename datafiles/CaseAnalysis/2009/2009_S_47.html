Steel Authority of India Limited v SUTNI Sangam and Others

Supreme Court of India

29 July 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 972; (2009) 16 SCC 1; AIR 2010 SC 112; 2009(5) ALL MR 492; JT 2009 (10) SC 509; 2009 (6) MLJ(SC) 510; 2009(10) SCALE 416; [2009] 12 S.C.R. 929

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Apportionment Of Compensation, Land Acquisition Court, Entitled To Act, Oil Mills

Summary: (A) Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4(1), 6, 18(2) - Land Acquisition proceedings - Finality of award - Scheme of Act - Finality of awards is subject to review by Reference Court u/s. 18 r/w s. 31(2) or s. 30 of Act - An appeal lies u/s. 54 to High Court from an award of Reference Judge and a second appeal lies to Supreme Court from decision of High Court - Held, Act can be said to be a complete code, only for special purpose of adjudicating any dispute with respect to three factual matters of area, value of land and apportionment of compensation among interested persons - There exists neither any express provision that no order or proceeding taken under Act shall be called in question in any court, nor any implied intendment barring jurisdiction of civil court - No finality attached to any of proceedings taken under Act or as to validity of award, which cannot be called in question in a court of law on any judicially recognized grounds - No machinery for determining all questions of law, which may conceivably arise under Act - Order accordingly.

(B) Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4(1), 6, 9, 18(2) and 28A - Land acquisition proceedings - (A) Reference application - Whether mandatory? - Right to receive compensation, having been circumscribed by conditions attached, ordinarily, they should be held to be imperative in character - Even those who had not made any application for reference in terms of S. 18 of Act have, however, a right to obtain a similar amount of compensation in terms of S. 28A thereof - Thus, only because at one stage, a holder of a land does not file any application for reference, same would not mean that they do not have any further remedy at all - S. 28A of Act seeks to deal with a situation where a person because of ignorance of his right was not in a position to file any application for enhancement of compensation - It provides that even in such a case, he should receive a just amount of compensation - (B) Land owners received amount of compensation without any protest - Whether estopped and precluded from filing a writ application through their Association or otherwise in view of second proviso appended to S. 31 of Act? - When an application for reference is filed, protest to the award is implicit - Those who received compensation without any protest keeping in view second proviso appended to S. 31 must be held to have expressed no reservation in regard thereto whatsoever - Objections, however, appeared to have been filed in printed forms contending that all awards should be subject to objections and payments would be received on protest - Raising of such an objection in response to a notice u/s. 9 of Act, cannot have same effect as if an application has been filed for reference u/s. 18 of Act - (C) 'Person interested' - Expression 'person interested' as contained in S. 3(b) of Act is an inclusive definition although not an exhaustive one - Expression 'person interested' for purpose of S. 18 of Act may be given a restricted meaning - Company or a local authority for whose benefit lands are acquired, having regard to provisions of Sub-s. (2) of S. 50 of Act, is not entitled to file any application for reference - Statute provides for filing of claim applications as also filing of objections by land holders and not by and/or on behalf of Association and that too an independent body corporate - Order accordingly.

(C) Land & Property - Practice and Procedure - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 18(2)(b) and 18(2) - Limitation Act, 1963, s. 5 - Land Acquisition proceedings - Challenge to award - Limitation period - (A) Constructive notice of award in year 1981 - Limitation period - Constructive knowledge would also subserve purpose of Act - Whether a person had actual or constructive knowledge of contents of a document is essentially a question of fact - Onus would be on landholder to show that he did not have any knowledge of contents of award - Notification issued by State directing Collector to exercise its jurisdiction u/s. 18 of Act would amount to a constructive knowledge - State, however, clarified said notification on or about 25.01.1983 stating that its earlier notification of year 1981 would not mean that statutory period of limitation provided for under Act should be given a complete go-by - It is only on or about 2.12.1983 that writ petition was filed - Writ petition, therefore, was also filed after inordinate delay - (B) Whether s. 5 of Limitation Act, 1963 being not applicable, HC could not have issued any writ after expiry of period of limitation or as specified in sub-s. (2) of S. 18 of Act? - Held, when statute provides for a law of limitation, compliance thereof is mandatory - For purpose of applying statute of limitation, courts should, however, be liberal in their approach - S. 18(2)(b) of Act provides for maximum period of six months from date of Collector's award - It was, therefore, impermissible to direct references to be made after a long period particularly when provisions of S. 5 of Limitation Act, 1963 cannot be said to have any application - Order accordingly.
