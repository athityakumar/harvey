State of Madhya Pradesh and Others v Ramesh Chandra Bajpai

Supreme Court of India

28 July 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, G.S. Singhvi, Deepak Verma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1992; (2009) 13 SCC 635; 2010 (3) ESC 327; 2009 (123) FLR 525; 2009(11) SCALE 619; [2009] 12 S.C.R. 23; 2009 (7) SLR 341

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: A.P. Agricultural University Act, 1963, Madhya Pradesh (Indian Systems of Medicine and Homeopathy) Class III Ministerial Services Recruitment Rules, 1987, Madhya Pradesh Educational Service (Collegiate Branch) Recruitment Rules, 1990

Summary: Service - Madhya Pradesh (Indian Systems of Medicine and Homeopathy) Class III Ministerial Services Recruitment Rules, 1987 - Madhya Pradesh Educational Service (Collegiate Branch) Recruitment Rules, 1990 - Whether the respondent, physical training instructor entitled to claim parity of pay with the teachers who have been granted UGC scale of pay or not? - Tribunal dismissed the application of the respondent observing that the applicant having not been holding the post of Sports Officer and having not been working in the Government College administered by the Department of Higher Education, he was not entitled to any relief - Respondent filed an application for review of the order - During the pendency of that application, the Tribunal was abolished and matter was transferred to HC - High Court allowed the writ petition relying upon the judgment of SC in P.S. Ramamohana Rao vs A.P. Agricultural University and another (1997 INDLAW SC 2029) and directed that the pay of the writ petitioner be fixed in the scale prescribed for the post of teacher - DB dismissed the writ appeal preferred by the petitioners primarily on the ground that a large number of Physical Training Instructors had already been granted U.G.C. scale and an order declining relief to the respondent would result in discrimination - Held, the decision in Ramamohana Rao has been misapplied and misconstrued by the High court - In Ramamohana Rao, SC, having regard to the nature of duties and functions of Physical Director, held that post came within the definition of teacher - Proposition laid down in that case should not have been automatically extended to other case like the present one, where employees are governed by different sets of rules - Appeal Allowed.
