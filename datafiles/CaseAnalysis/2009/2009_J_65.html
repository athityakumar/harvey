Jaswant Kaur And Another v Subhash Paliwal And Others

Supreme Court of India

15 December 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 2038; (2010) 2 SCC 124; 2010 (1) AWC 926; 2010 (1) CLT(SC) 209; JT 2009 (15) SC 379; 2010 (3) MLJ(SC) 345; 2010 (1) RCR(Civil) 617; 2010 (1) RCR(Rent) 79; 2010 (109) RD 554; 2009(14) SCALE 695; [2009] 16 S.C.R. 481

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Keywords: Rajasthan Premises (Control of Rent & Eviction) Rent Act, 1950

Summary: Civil Procedure - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, o.VI, r. 17 and o. XLI r. 27 - Application for amendment in written statement - Respondents filed a suit against petitioners for their eviction from shop room in question on ground of personal necessity and bona fide necessity for his son painting business - Petitioners contended that respondent no. 1 was not owner of suit shop room and suit for eviction for bona fide need of his son was not maintainable - Civil Judge dismissed said application of petitioner and decreed suit for eviction - Petitioner filed appeal against said order - Subsequently petitioner filed an application in pending appeal u/o. VI r. 17 of CPC for amendment of written statement to incorporate submissions that in view of Will purportedly executed by X, respondent was not owner of suit property and could not, therefore, ask for eviction of petitioners there from for personal and bona fide requirement of his son - Said application allowed by First Appellate Court - On appeal, HC rejected applications for amendment in written statement by petitioners - Hence, present appeal - Whether HC's order could be allowed? - Held, by virtue of two applications for amendment, new case attempted to be made out by petitioners - No amendment of pleadings in view of petitioners admission in their written statement that respondents were their landlords in respect of suit shop room - Petition dismissed.
