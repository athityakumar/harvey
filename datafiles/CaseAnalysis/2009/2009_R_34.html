K. Subramanian v R.Rajathi Rep. By P.O.A. P. Kaliappan

Supreme Court of India

05 November 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Panchal, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1563; (2010) 15 SCC 352; 2010 (1) RCR(Criminal) 184; 2009(14) SCALE 186

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Banking & Finance - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 320 - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, ss. 138, 147 - Review Petition - Condoning of Delay - Recall of Order - Sustainability - Petitioner had to pay sum of Rs. 4,52,289/- to respondent - In discharge of debt, petitioner issued three cheques, which were returned unpaid by Bank - Trial Court convicted petitioner u/s. 138 of the Act - Aggrieved, petitioner filed appeal before Sessions Court, which was dismissed - HC dismissed revision petition filed by petitioner - Thereafter, compromise was entered into and petitioner claimed that petitioner had paid Rs.4,52,289/- to respondent - Petitioner was arrested on 30-7-2008 and had undergone sentence imposed on him by Trial Court and confirmed by Sessions Court, HC as well as by SC - Thereafter, petitioner filed review petition to review and recall order of SC, moreover, petitioner filed another petition to condone delay in filing review petition - Hence, instant review petition - Whether review petition filed by petitioner, after delay of 39 days, to review and recall order of SC to compound offence, was sustainable - Held, in view of salutary provisions of s. 147 of the Act r/w. s. 320 CrPC, petitioner should be permitted to compound offence committed by him u/s. 138 of the Act - Prayer to condone delay of 39 days caused in filing review application was allowed and delay was condoned - Hence, Order of conviction and sentence recorded by all Courts were set aside and petitioner was acquitted of charge leveled against him - Petition disposed of.
