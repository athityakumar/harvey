Punjab State Electricity Board and others v Jagjiwan Ram and others

Supreme Court of India

12 February 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, B.N. Agrawal

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 2158; (2009) 3 SCC 661; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 246; JT 2009 (3) SC 400; 2009 (3) LLJ 216; 2009(2) SCALE 511; [2009] 3 S.C.R. 209; 2009 (5) SLR 499

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Haryana Service of Engineers, Class-II, Public Works Department (Irrigation Branch) Rules, 1970, Rajasthan Service (Concessions on Project) Rules, 1962, PSEB (Revised Pay) Regulations, 1988

Summary: (A) Service - Work charged employees - Past service - Reckoning of - Appellant/State Electricity Board introduced a Scheme for giving time bound promotional scales/increments to the employees who were stagnating in particular positions for a long period of time on completion of their 9/16/23 years of regular service - Respondents, who were engaged as work charged employees and were appointed on regular basis on a later date, filed writ petitions for issue of a direction to the appellant/Board and its officers to count their work charged service for the purpose of grant of time bound promotional scales/promotional increments from the date of completion of 9/16/23 years service - Appellants contended that the benefit of time bound promotional scales could be given only from the date of completion of 9/16 years regular service and promotional increments could be given on completion of 23 years regular service and that work charged service could not be equated with regular service for the said purpose - HC allowed the writ petitions by relying on the decision of SC in State of Haryana and Ors. v. Ravinder Kumar and Ors. (Order dated 31-10-2000 passed in Civil Appeal Nos. 5740-5741/1997) - Hence the present appeal by appellant/Board - Whether the work charged employees are entitled to count their past service for claim any service benefit - Held, work charged employees are engaged for execution of a specified work or project and their engagement comes to an end on completion of the work or project - Source and mode of engagement/recruitment of work charged employees, their pay and conditions of employment are altogether different from the persons appointed in the regular establishment against sanctioned posts after following the procedure prescribed under the relevant Act or rules and their duties and responsibilities are also substantially different than those of regular employees - Work charged employees can claim protection under the Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 or the rights flowing from any particular statute but they cannot be treated at par with the employees of regular establishment - Work charged employees can neither claim regularization of service as of right nor they can claim pay scales and other financial benefits at par with regular employees - If the service of a work charged employee is regularized under any statute or a scheme framed by the employer, then he becomes member of regular establishment from the date of regularization, however, his service in the work charged establishment cannot be clubbed with service in a regular establishment unless a specific provision to that effect is made either in the relevant statute or the scheme of regularization - Further, if the statute or scheme under which service of work charged employee is regularized does not provide for counting of past service, the work charged employee cannot claim benefit of such service for the purpose of fixation of seniority in the regular cadre, promotion to the higher posts, fixation of pay in the higher scales, grant of increments etc. - Appeals allowed.

(B) Service - Scheme for time bound promotional scale - Work charged employees - Entitlement - Whether the respondents are entitled to the benefit of time bound promotional scales/promotional increments as per the Scheme introduced by the appellant/Board - Held, benefit of time bound promotional scales was to be given to the employees only on their completing 9/16 years regular service as per the terms of Scheme - Further, benefit of promotional increments could be given only on completion of 23 years regular service - Use of the term 'regular service' in various paragraphs of the Scheme showed that service rendered by an employee after regular appointment could only be counted for computation of 9/16/23 years service and the service of a temporary, ad hoc or work charged employee cannot be counted for extending the benefit of time bound promotional scales or promotional increments - If the appellant/Board intended that total service rendered by the employees irrespective of their mode of recruitment and status should be counted for the purpose of grant of time bound promotional scales or promotional increments, then instead of using the expression '9/16 years regular service' or '23 years regular service', the concerned authority would have used the expression '9/16 years service' or '23 years service' - Scheme in its plainest term embodies the requirement of 9/16 years regular service or 23 years regular service as a condition for grant of time bound promotional scales or promotional increments as the case may be - Therefore, respondents are not entitled to the benefit of time bound promotional scales / promotional increments on a date prior to completion of 9/16/23 years regular service and the HC committed serious error by directing the appellants to give them benefit of the Scheme by counting their work charged service - Further, Ravinder Kumar case is clearly distinguishable with the present case - In Ravinder case, State conceded that period during which an employee had worked on work charged basis was counted for the purpose of grant of increment as well as for computation of qualifying service for pension - In view of State's stand in the said case, the Court held that there was no reason why such service should not be counted for the purpose of giving additional increment on completion of 8/12 years service and higher scale on completion of 10/20 years service - However, judgment of the case did not contain any discussion on the issue whether the work charged service could be equated or clubbed with regular service for grant of service benefits admissible to regular employees - Therefore, the ratio of Ravinder Kumar case cannot be treated as laying down any proposition of law which can be treated as precedent for other cases including the present case - Impugned HC order is set aside - Appeals allowed.
