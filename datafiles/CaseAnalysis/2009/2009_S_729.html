Bala Baine Linga Raju v State of Andhra Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

05 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 602; (2009) 3 SCC (Cr) 13; (2009) 6 SCC 706; 2009 CRLJ 3426; 2009 (3) JCC 1847; JT 2009 (13) SC 657; 2009 (3) RCR(Criminal) 7; 2009(7) SCALE 73; [2009] 7 S.C.R. 595; 2009 (2) UC 886

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Probation of Offenders Act, 1958, ss. 4 and 5 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 299, 300 exception I Proviso 1 and 2, explanation thereto and 304 Part I - Applicability of PO Act - Conviction u/s. 304 part I by Trial Court and imposition of sentence of 7 years - Conviction upheld by High Court but accused was directed to be released by applying PO Act - Whether invocation of PO Act by High Court, justified? - Held, No - PW2, wife of deceased while feeding her child scolded him by describing him as mischievous, but accused 2 thought that the said remarks of PW 2 were directed against her; A2 and her husband started quarreling with PW 2; appellant who was inside the house came out with a scissor and stabbed the deceased - Injuries sustained to lung and heart, and death caused due to 'lot of bleeding' - Applicability of s. 300 deliberated upon by inter alia referring to Virsa Singh's case and to facts of the case, for the purpose of examining applicability of PO Act - Ss. 4 and 5 of PO Act, perused - Finding made, appellant was charged with commission of an offence u/s. 302, IPC and found guilty u/s. 304 Part I which provides for 'imprisonment for life or ...'; provisions of Act held not to be applicable - Held, PO Act not applicable - Trial Court's judgement restored - Impugned judgment set aside - Appeal disposed of.
