Ankush Maruti Shinde and Others v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

30 April 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 825; (2009) 3 SCC (Cr) 308; (2009) 6 SCC 667; AIR 2009 SC 2609; 2009 ALL MR (Cri) 1872; 2010 (1) MLJ(Crl) 329; 2009 (2) RCR(Criminal) 868; 2009(6) SCALE 388; [2009] 7 S.C.R. 182

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Bride Burning, Mitigating Circumstance, Mitigating Factor, Tape Record

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 376, 376(2)(g), 395, 396 and 397 - Murder - Rape - Conviction - Alteration of death sentence to life sentence - Accused persons were convicted for offence punishable u/s. 307 r/w s. 34 and were also convicted u/s. 397 r/w ss. 395 and 396, IPC - Accused Nos.1, 2 and 4 were also convicted for offence punishable u/s. 376(2)(g), IPC - Reference was made by Additional Sessions Judge u/s. 366 of CrPC for confirmation of death sentence - HC while upholding conviction and death sentence of accused Nos.1, 2 and 4, altered sentence in respect of accused Nos.3, 5 and 6 to life sentence with fine and order of conviction and sentence u/s. 376(2)(g) in respect of accused nos. 3, 5 and 6 was set aside - Present appeal challenging alteration of life sentence to death and acquittal of three of accused persons for offence punishable u/s. 376, IPC - Whether death sentence justified in facts and circumstances of present case? - HC held that those who were guilty of rape and murder deserve death sentence, while those who were convicted for murder only were to be awarded life sentence - HC itself noticed that five members of a family were brutally murdered, they were not known to the accused and there was no animosity towards them - Four of the witnesses were of a young age, they were defenseless and attack was without any provocation - A minor girl was gang raped and done to death - Held, there can be no doubt that case at hand falls under rarest of rare category - There was no reason to adopt a different yardstick for A2, A3 and A5 - Appeal filed by accused persons deserves dismissal, State's appeal deserves to be allowed - A2, A3 and A5 also awarded death sentence - All six accused persons deserve death sentence - Order accordingly.
