M/S Century Textiles Industries Ltd. v Deepak Jain & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

20 March 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

D.K. Jain, R.M. Lodha

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 341; (2009) 5 SCC 634; 2009 (3) ALD(SC) 43; 2009 (3) AWC 2841; JT 2009 (4) SC 342; 2009 (4) MPLJ 90; 2009 (2) RCR(Civil) 614; 2009 (108) RD 120; 2009(4) SCALE 501; [2009] 4 S.C.R. 750; 2009 (3) UC 2003

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Appeal allowed, Judgment Debtor, Decree Holder, Ex- Parte

Summary: Civil Procedure - CPC, 1908, s. 152 - When a specific issue regarding the identity of the judgment-debtor had been raised and entertained by the High Court, and the Court having remitted the matter to the Executing Court, the enquiry conducted by the Executing Court in furtherance of the said direction, could its order be said to be without jurisdiction? - Held, Executing Court had no option but to determine the question of identity of the judgment-debtor because of the direction of the High Court and the issues raised before it - Appeal allowed.
