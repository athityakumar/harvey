Airports Authority of India v Rajeev Ratan Pandey and Others

Supreme Court of India

17 August 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 966; (2009) 8 SCC 337; AIR 2010 SC (Supp) 322; 2009 (3) CLR 136; 2009 (123) FLR 159; JT 2009 (10) SC 472; 2009(11) SCALE 549; [2009] 13 S.C.R. 343; 2009 (8) SLR 555; 2009 (4) UPLBEC 3169

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Transfer order - Ad-interim stay given by HC - Legality - Respondent No. 1, Senior Manager, Engineering (Civil) transferred from Lucknow to Calicut by appellant - Order of transfer challenged before HC - In writ petition, transfer order assailed on sole ground that it was violative of transfer policy framed by appellant - Thereafter, respondent filed a supplementary affidavit before HC alleging therein for first time that transfer order was actuated with mala-fides - DB passed an ad-interim order staying operation of transfer order - Hence, present appeal - Whether DB of HC justified in passing ad-interim Order staying operation of transfer order? - No allegations of mala-fides pleaded in writ petition - Allegations of mala-fides not supported by any material whatsoever - Held, where plea of mala-fides not made in writ petition and assertion of mala-fides made for first time in a supplementary affidavit which too not supported by any convincing and cogent material, plea of mala-fides hardly deserved acceptance, prima facie, justifying stay of operation of a transfer order - Further held, in a matter of transfer of a govt. employee, scope of judicial review being limited HC would not have interfere with an order of transfer lightly, be it at interim stage or final hearing - Courts do not substitute their own decision in matter of transfer - In present case, HC fell into a grave error in staying transfer order which, if allowed to stand, may cause prejudice to administrative functioning of appellant - Impugned order set aside - Appeal allowed.
