Suraj Lamp And Industries (P) Limited. Thru. Dir v State Of Haryana And Another.

Supreme Court of India

15 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, J.M. Panchal

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 2047; (2009) 7 SCC 363; AIR 2009 SC 3077; 2009 (76) ALR 792; 2010 (3) Bom.C.R. 808; 2011 (6) KarLJ 62; 2010 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 321; 2010 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 355; 2009 (4) MPLJ 315; 2010 (1) RCR(Civil) 46; 2009 (108) RD 660; 2010 (1) RLW 251; 2009(9) SCALE 36; [2009] 9 S.C.R. 1048

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Registration Act, 1908, ss. 17 and 49 - Registration of deeds of conveyance - After receiving full payment, avoidance of execution and registration of deeds of conveyance as mode of transfer of freehold immovable property by increasing tendency to adopt 'Power of Attorney Sales' - Held, Power of Attorney Sales', was adopted to overcome restrictions/prohibitions in terms of allotment and rules of allotment of DDA governing allotment of flats - Such transactions were obviously irregular and illegal being contrary to rules and terms of allotment - Further, in absence of a registered deed of conveyance, no right, title or interest in an immovable property could be transferred to purchaser - However, Delhi HC in a few cases accepted such 'Power of Attorney Sales' as creating an 'interest' in DDA flat which was so 'transferred' and consequently, protected such interest of purchaser by issuing injunctions or decrees preventing vendor from further dealing with property - This led to a general impression 'Power of Attorney Sales' were valid recognized modes of transfer and very purpose DDA prohibiting transfers and requiring permission on payment of certain difference in price was defeated by this process - Notice to issue to States of Punjab, Haryana, Delhi, Uttar Pradesh and Maharashtra as to whether transactions by means of SAGPA Will instead of regular sales are prevalent in their States and their views in respect of such transactions as also what steps have been taken and/or and proposed to be taken by respective States to deal with such transactions and their effects - Order accordingly
