National Insurance Company Limited v Parvathneni and Another

Supreme Court of India

31 August 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Markandey Katju, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1074; (2009) 8 SCC 785; (2009) 3 SCC (Cr) 943; 2009 (77) ALR 166; 2009 (2) CCC 406; 2009 (4) CTC 798; JT 2009 (12) SC 275; 2009 (3) KLT 995; 2010 (157) PLR 228; 2009 (4) RCR(Civil) 270; 2009(12) SCALE 82; [2009] 13 S.C.R. 1034

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Insurance

Keywords: Matter referred to Larger Bench

Summary: (A) Insurance - Motor Vehicle Act, 1988 - Constitution of India, 1950, art.142 - Compensation - Liability of Insurance Company - No valid insurance coverage - Whether in event an Insurance Company can prove that it does not have any liability to pay any amount in law to claimants under Motor Vehicles Act or any other enactment, can Court yet compel it to pay amount in question giving it liberty to later on recover same from owner of vehicle? - National Insurance Co. Ltd. vs. Yellamma & Another (2008) 7 SCC 526, Samundra Devi vs. Narendra Kaur (2008) 9 SCC 100, Oriental Insurance Co. vs. Brij Mohan 2007 INDLAW SC 555, New India Insurance Co. vs. Darshan Devi state that even if insurance company has no liability, yet it must pay and later on recover it from owner of the vehicle - Held, reservations about correctness of aforesaid decisions of this Court - If insurance company has no liability to pay at all, then it cannot be compelled by order of Court in exercise of its jurisdiction u/art.142 of Constitution to pay compensation amount and later on recover it from the owner of the vehicle - Matter referred to a larger BenchP /&gt;(B) Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art.142 - Scope of art.142 - Question whether direction can be issued to Insurance Company u/art.142 to pay any amount in law to claimants in case it is proved that it does not have any liability - Does Art.142 permit Court to create a liability where there is none - Matter referred to Larger Bench.
