Rasiklal Dalpatram Thakkar v State Of Gujarat & Ors

Supreme Court of India

06 November 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1561; (2010) 1 SCC 1; AIR 2010 SC 715; 2010 ALL MR (Cri) 342; 2010 CRLJ 884; 2010 (1) MLJ(Crl) 88; 2010 (2) RCR(Criminal) 765; 2009(13) SCALE 628; [2009] 15 S.C.R. 722; 2010 (1) UC 87

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Corporate Body, Criminal Misappropriation, Court's Jurisdiction

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 156, 156(1), 156(2), 156(3) and 181 - Multi State Cooperative Societies Act, 2002 - Power of Investigating Agency - Territorial jurisdiction - Determination of - Complaints were filed against the appellant before the Chief Metropolitan Magistrate, Ahmedabad who directed the Economic Offences Wing, Ahmedabad, u/s. 156(3) CrPC to carry out investigation - Investigating Agency submitted report stating that the allegations complained of had been committed within the territorial limits of the city of Mumbai, and that the investigation should, therefore, be transferred to the Investigation Agency in Mumbai - Said report was rejected by the Magistrate on the ground that it was not for the Investigating Agency to decide not to investigate a complaint forwarded to it u/s. 156(3) CrPC on the ground that the offence complained of was allegedly committed outside the territorial jurisdiction of the Investigating Agency - Magistrate directed the Investigating Agency to carry out a further investigation - Revision filed against said order dismissed - HC accepted the views expressed by the Magistrate and dismissed the writ petition - Hence, present appeal - Whether in regard to an order passed u/s.156(3) CrPC the police authorities empowered under Sub-Section (1) of s. 156 can unilaterally decide not to conduct an investigation on the ground that they had no territorial jurisdiction to do so? - Held, powers vested in the Investigating Authorities, u/s. 156(1) CrPC, did not restrict the jurisdiction of the Investigating Agency to investigate into a complaint even if it did not have territorial jurisdiction to do so - It was for the Court to decide whether it had jurisdiction to entertain the complaint as and when the entire facts were placed before it - Appeal dismissed.
