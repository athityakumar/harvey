Anand Kumar v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

20 February 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

H.S. Bedi, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 190; (2009) 2 SCC (Cr) 28; (2009) 3 SCC 799; AIR 2009 SC 2155; JT 2009 (2) SC 662; 2009 (3) RCR(Criminal) 30; 2009(3) SCALE 1; [2009] 2 S.C.R. 775

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Mother- In-Law

Summary: Criminal - IPC, 1860, s. 306; Dowry Prohibition Act 1961, s. 4 - Appeal against conviction and sentence - Held, courts have relied heavily on the letter Exhibit P-20 to support the finding of conviction against the appellant - This excerpt from his evidence cannot be said to be proof of the document as no statement was made that he recognized the handwriting or the signature of the appellant - Under s. 113-A of Evidence Act the Court 'may presume', having regard to all the other circumstances of the case, an abetment of suicide as visualized by s. 306 but in s. 113-B which is relatable to s. 304-B the word 'may' has been substituted by 'shall' and there is no reference to the circumstances of the case - Conviction of the appellant has been recorded under s. 306 which is relatable to s. 113-A and though the presumption against an accused has to be raised therein as well, the onus is not as heavy as in the case of a dowry death - Appeal allowed.
