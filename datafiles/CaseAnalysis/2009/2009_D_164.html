(1) Malay Kumar Ganguly; (2) Dr. Kunal Saha v Dr. Sukumar Mukherjee and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 August 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Deepak Verma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 994; (2009) 9 SCC 221; AIR 2010 SC 1162; 2009 (4) CalLT(SC) 40; 2009 (3) CPJ(Sc) 17; JT 2009 (10) SC 256; 2009 (4) MLJ(Crl) 551; 2009 (4) RCR(Civil) 14; 2009 (4) RLW 3625; 2009(10) SCALE 675; [2009] 13 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection; Criminal

Keywords: Medical Service, Hospitals, Medical Profession, Professional Negligence, Hospital And Doctors, Methyl, Old Age, Civil Case, Warrant Case, Withdrawal Of Case, Specific Plea, Postgraduate, Compassionate Ground, Civil Action, Individual Liability, Blood Test, Criminal Appeal dismissed; Civil Appeal disposed of

Summary: (A) Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s.304-A - Medical Negligence - Wife of appellant died due to Toxic Epidermal Necrolysis (TEN) during the treatment by respondent-doctors - Medical negligence alleged - Trial Court sentenced 1st and 2nd respondents and acquitted 3rd respondent - However, HC allowed the appeal of respondents and dismissed Revision Petition of appellant against acquittal of 3rd respondent - Legality - Appellant contended that respondents administered excess dosage of steroids on the deceased and thereby committed breach of medical protocol - Held, Expert opinions could not be admissible as the experts failed to prove the opinion in the court - However, chemical composition of steroid medicine was detrimental to the TEN patients - Instructions in the medicinal packet with regard to the dosage had not been followed by respondents - No symptomatic therapy administered nor emergency care provided to patient - Nursing care of respondent hospital was abysmal - No pathological examination was carried out nor routine check-up had been done - No dressing had been done on the body of deceased at respondents hospital - 1st respondent prescribed steroid at the preliminary stage itself - 2nd respondent prescribed a high dose - Respondents did not consult medical literatures - No prudent physician would suggest two steroid injections daily - Used steroid medicine was not a quick acting steroid but persist in the human body for long time - Administered drug was a real risk for the patient suffering from TEN - Respondent did not follow the universally accepted medicated treatment protocol - 1st respondent's sheer ignorance of basic hazards of use of steroid lead to the death of deceased - Interference of appellant in the treatment of the deceased did not diminish the primary responsibility and default on part of respondents - Patient was not informed prior to the adverse effect of medicine and admitted risk involved on it - Negligent action attributed by more than one respondents - Cumulative incidence led to the death of patient, however, cumulative effect is not available in criminal law - Therefore, respondent could not be held liable for the offence u/s. 304-A of IPC - Criminal Appeal dismissed.P /&gt;(B) Consumer Protection - The Consumer Protection Act, 1986 - Medical Negligence - Appellant's wife died due to Toxic Epidermal Necrolysis (TEN) during the treatment by respondent-doctors - Appellant contended that respondents were guilty of negligence and deficiency of service - Respondent contended that they were not received any professional fees from appellant - National Commission dismissed the complaint of appellant - Legality - Held, respondents administered higher dosage of steroids to the patient - Respondent's sheer ignorance of hazardous nature of medicine lead to the death of deceased - Therefore, respondents guilty of negligence in their duty, hence matter remitted back to National Commission for determining the quantum of compensation - Civil Appeal disposed of.
