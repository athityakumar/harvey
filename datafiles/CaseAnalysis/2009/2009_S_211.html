Jugesh Sehgal v Shamsher Singh Gogi

Supreme Court of India

10 July 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

D.K. Jain, R.M. Lodha

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 865; (2009) 14 SCC 683; 2009 (3) BC 690; 2010 (1) Bom.C.R. 903; 2009 (3) JCC (NI) 210; JT 2009 (9) SC 292; 2009 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 2485; 2009 (3) RCR(Criminal) 712; 2009 (4) RLW 3506; 2009(9) SCALE 455; [2009] 10 S.C.R. 857

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Criminal

Keywords: Legal Notice

Summary: Criminal - Banking & Finance - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, s. 138 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 482 - Complaint u/s. 138 NI Act, was filed against four persons - Petition by the four, for quashing the complaint, u/s. 482 of CrPC before High Court - Petition allowed for three of them - Whether prima case u/s. 138 of NI Act made out vide the complaint so as to justify issuance of notice against appellant? - Held, no - Provisions of s. 138 perused, content of complaint taken note of - On receipt of return memo from the bank, the complainant had realized that the dishonoured cheque was issued from an account which was not maintained by accused/appellant but by someone else - Undisputed was the position that cheque, subject matter of the complaint u/s. 138 NI Act, had not been drawn by the accused/appellant on an account maintained by him in the Bank as is referred to in the complaint - Finding, very first ingredient of s. 138 of NI Act, not satisfied - (A) Whether or not in the light of factual position of case as coming out of complaint, it was a fit case where the HC should have exercised its jurisdiction u/s. 482 of CrPC? - Held, Yes - Pending criminal complaint quashed - Appeal allowed.
