Uttar Pradesh State Sugar Corporation Limited Now v Niraj Kumar and Others

Supreme Court of India

31 July 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1892; (2009) 14 SCC 712; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 1943; 2010 (124) FLR 1009; 2010 (1) LLJ 247; 2009(10) SCALE 621; [2009] 11 S.C.R. 898

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Cooperative, Daily Rate, Seasonal, Reemployment

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Appellant corporation, a seasonal industry, engaged 1st respondent as Weighment Clerk on temporary basis - Service of 1st respondent terminated at the end of the season - However, appellant did not engage 1st respondent on the next season - 1st respondent raised an industrial dispute - Labour Court directed appellant to engage 1st respondent in the next season - Appellant challenged the award before HC - HC affirmed the award of Labour Court, holding that 1st respondent was a seasonal workman - (A) Whether 1st respondent was a temporary workman or seasonal workman - If an employee is engaged for work of a temporary or casual nature like additional workload during a season, his engagement would be that of a temporary workman and not that of a seasonal workman - Held, finding recorded by the Labour Court that the 1st respondent was engaged as a seasonal workman, is based on no legal evidence and HC was not justified in affirming the same - (B) Whether 1st respondent is entitled to be re-employed in the succeeding year - To be entitled for reemployment in the succeeding season, a seasonal workman has to show that he worked in the previous full season or in whole of the second half of the last preceding season - Merely because workman has worked during the part of the previous season, he does not become entitled for re-employment in the succeeding season - Held, 1st respondent had neither worked in the previous full season nor he remained in employment during the whole of the second half of the last preceding season - Therefore, 1st respondent has no right to be re-employed in the succeeding season - Impugned award and HC order set aside - Appeals allowed.
