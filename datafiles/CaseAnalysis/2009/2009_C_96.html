Lakshmi & Anr v Chinnammal @ Rayyammal & Ors

Supreme Court of India

08 April 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 449; (2009) 13 SCC 25; 2009 (3) ADJ 612; AIR 2009 SC 2352; 2009 (75) ALR 464; 2009 (1) ARC 805; 2009 (3) AWC 2215; 2009 (3) JhrCR(SC) 90; JT 2009 (7) SC 18; 2009 (4) MLJ(SC) 1040; 2009 (2) RCR(Civil) 906; 2009 (1) RCR(Rent) 497; 2009 (107) RD 188; 2009 (4) RLW 3287; 2009(5) SCALE 150; [2009] 5 S.C.R. 661

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - CPC, 1908 - 'Procedural Mechanics' involving interpretation of O. 13 r. 10 - Held, if bringing on record a document is essential for proving the case by a party, ordinarily the same should not be refused; the Court's duty being to find out the truth - The procedural mechanics necessary to arrive at a just decision must be encouraged - Where a Civil proceeding as also a Criminal proceeding is pending, the latter shall get primacy - Appeal allowed.
