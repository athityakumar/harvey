Union of India v Dharam Pal Etc

Supreme Court of India

12 February 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 170; (2009) 4 SCC 170; 2009 (122) FLR 207; JT 2009 (2) SC 679; 2009 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 1746; 2009 (2) LLN 415; 2009(3) SCALE 408; [2009] 2 S.C.R. 193; 2009 (8) SLR 274

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Determination Of Seniority, Temporary Promotion

Summary: Service - Indian Railway Establishment Manual - Regularisation - Tribunal opined that despite the fact that ad hoc appointments were de hors the recruitment rules but in view of the purported policy decision taken by the appellant, respondents' services were required to be regularized - Appeal against - Held, a policy decision taken by the authorities of the Railway to regularize the services of those MCCs who had been appointed on ad hoc basis was a one time measure - It was not to be followed as a precedent - Even power of relaxation even specifically provided in the appointing authority himself being created a statute cannot be exercised in an arbitrary and cavalier fashion - Appeal allowed.
