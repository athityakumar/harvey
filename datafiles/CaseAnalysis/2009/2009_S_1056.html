Motilal and Another v State of Rajasthan

Supreme Court of India

05 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1951; (2009) 3 SCC (Cr) 444; (2009) 7 SCC 454; AIR 2009 SC 2790; 2009 CRLJ 3971; 2009 (3) RCR(Criminal) 884; 2010 (1) RLW 27; 2009(9) SCALE 1; [2009] 8 S.C.R. 303

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Blood Stains

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 157 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34 and 302 - Delay in sending report to Elaqa Magistrate - Effect thereof - Murder - Conviction - Appellants convicted for offence punishable u/s. 302/34, IPC - HC held that delayed receipt of report by Elaqa Magistrate would not be fatal to prosecution and convicted appellants - Whether conviction correct? - Held, HC rightly observed that if the FIR is timely lodged and investigation is undertaken immediately, in a given case, the delayed receipt of the report by the Elaqa Magistrate would not be fatal to the prosecution - However, it would depend upon the facts of each case and there cannot be any generalisation - Statutory requirement that the report has to be sent forthwith that itself shows that the urgency attached to the sending of the report - It is open to the prosecution to indicate reasons for the delayed despatch or delayed receipt and same has to be established by evidence - Further, it is true that a faulty investigation cannot be a determinative factor and would not be sufficient to throw out a credible prosecution version - In instant case, there is no explanation offered even to explain the discrepancies cumulative effect of the factors would show that the prosecution failed to establish the accusations - Delay in sending report to Elaqa Magistrate also not been explained - Appeal allowed.
