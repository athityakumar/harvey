Ramesh Chandra Agrawal v Regency Hospital Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 September 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Dattu, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1120; (2009) 9 SCC 709; AIR 2010 SC 806; 2009 (6) ALD(SC) 119; 2009 (77) ALR 479; 2009 (4) AWC 4218; 2010 (3) Bom.C.R. 367; 2009 (4) CompLJ 283; 2009 (4) CPJ(Sc) 27; 2010 (1) JhrCR(SC) 30; JT 2009 (12) SC 377; 2010 (1) RCR(Civil) 15; 2009(12) SCALE 474; [2009] 14 S.C.R. 424; 2010 (1) UC 174

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection

Summary: Consumer Protection - Consumer protection Act, 1986 - Medical negligence - Appellant was diagnosed with T.B. infection by 1st respondent doctor - 2nd and 3rd respondent doctors performed 2 operations on appellant - 3rd operation on appellant performed in another hospital - Appellant cured on 3rd operation but became handicapped - Medical negligence, alleged - Appellant sought compensation - National Consumer Commission (NC) dismissed appellant's complaint - Legality - Held, appellant filed application seeking expert opinion before NC - NC appointed expert - However, expert sought X-Rays and MRI reports to form a detailed opinion - Registry of NC failed to forward sought reports to expert - NC rejected appellants 2nd application for reconsideration of expert opinion - NC failed to appreciate that whether expert opinion desirable in present case or not - NC should have directed for expert opinion on 2nd application of appellant - Appellant should not have been suffered for the negligence of Registrar or NC - Appellant brought notice to NC that Registrar failed to forward records to expert - NC rejected 2nd application of appellant on ground that expert opinion was not necessary at that stage - Natural Justice required that a fair opportunity should be given to appellant to prove his claim on expert opinion - Therefore, NC's order cannot be sustained, hence matter remitted back to NC for fresh consideration - Further, directed Registrar of NC to forward X-Rays and MRI reports to expert for an opinion - Appeal allowed.
