V.N. Devadoss v Chief Revenue Control Officer-Cum-Ins.And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

08 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, D.K. Jain, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 741; (2009) 7 SCC 438; 2009 (76) ALR 730; 2009 (4) AWC 3423; 2009 (5) CompLJ 1; JT 2009 (13) SC 545; 2009 (3) RCR(Civil) 496; 2009 (107) RD 679; 2009(8) SCALE 23; [2009] 8 S.C.R. 1043

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Keywords: Registering Officer, Procedure On Receipt, Tamil Nadu Stamp (Prevention of Undervaluation of Instruments) Rules, 1968

Summary: Civil Procedure - Indian Stamp Act, 1899, ss. 47-A - Tamil Nadu Stamp (Prevention of Undervaluation of Instruments) Rules, 1968, rr. 3 and 4 - Purchase of land belonging to a sick company for which proceeding under Sick Industrial Companies (Special Provisions) Act, 1985 was undergoing - Sale of such land by statutory authority under the SICA by constituting Assets Sales Committee(ASC) - Appellant was the highest bidder and his bid was accepted by the authorities and sale deed was executed in his favour - On reference by Sub-registrar, proceedings u/s. 47-A was initiated - Proceedings so initiated whether called for or whether s. 47-A has application in instant case? - Held, No - Rr. 3 and 4 of Rules and s. 47-A perused, contentions heard - For s. 47-A there must be a reason to believe that the market value of the property which is the subject matter of the conveyance has not been truly set out in the instrument; it is not a routine procedure to be followed in respect of each and every document of conveyance presented for registration without any evidence to show lack of bona fides of the parties to the document by attempting fraudulently to under value the subject of conveyance with a view to evade payment of proper stamp duty and thereby cause loss to the revenue - Basis for s. 47-A is thus 'willful under valuation' of subject matter of transfer with 'fraudulent intention' to evade payment of proper stamp duty - (i) Can it be said that such bases were there in instant case? - No - Market value is a changing concept and as per explanation to sub-r.(5), value would be such as would have fetched or would fetch if sold in the open market on the date of execution; in instant case property was offered for sale in the open market and bids were invited - Properties were disposed of by BIFR and AIFR (authorities under SICA) on basis of value fixed by ASC; and the view was expressed by the ASC which consisted members such as representatives of IDBI, Debenture Holders, Govt. of West Bengal, Special Director of BIFR, thus there is no possibility of any undervaluation so as to warrant application of s. 47-A - Appeal allowed.
