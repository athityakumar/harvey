Bhuwan Singh v M/S Oriental Insurance Company Ltd. & Anr

Supreme Court of India

05 March 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 270; (2009) 5 SCC 136; (2009) 2 SCC (Cr) 619; 2009 (2) ACC 296; AIR 2009 SC 2177; 2009 (3) ALD(SC) 35; 2009(2) ALL MR 913; 2009 (3) AWC 2989; 2009 (1) CCC 141; 2009 (2) CTC 452; 2009 (2) JhrCR(SC) 47; JT 2009 (3) SC 333; 2009 (4) MLJ(SC) 116; 2009 (4) MPLJ 335; 2009 (156) PLR 739; 2009 (2) RCR(Civil) 563; 2009(3) SCALE 798; [2009] 4 S.C.R. 195

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance

Keywords: Breach Of Condition, Non Joinder, Renewal Of Licence, Claim Tribunal, Central Motor Vehicle Rule, 1988

Summary: Insurance - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 - Held, only because the appellant held a learner's licence which had expired and was not valid on the date of accident, he cannot be said to be duly licensed - Burden of proof ordinarily would be on insurance company to establish that there has been a breach of conditions of the contract of insurance - Burden in terms of s. 106 of the Evidence Act was on the appellant - He failed to discharge the said burden - Appeal dismissed.
