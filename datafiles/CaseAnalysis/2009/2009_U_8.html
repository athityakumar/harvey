Jayabalan v U. T. of Pondicherry

Supreme Court of India

06 November 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Mukundakam Sharma, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1572; (2010) 1 SCC 199; AIR 2010 SC (Supp) 352; 2009 ALL MR (Cri) 3786; 2010 CRLJ 2234; JT 2009 (14) SC 401; 2010 (1) MLJ(Crl) 1269; 2009(13) SCALE 614; [2009] 15 S.C.R. 736

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Power Of The Court

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Murder - Conviction - Challenged - Trial Court acquitted appellant for offence punishable u/s. 302, IPC - On appeal, HC reversed order of acquittal and convicted appellant u/s. 302, IPC - Present appeal against conviction - Marital life of deceased with appellant was not smooth and relationship between couple was strained - (A) Oral dying declaration - Whether HC justified in relying upon oral dying declaration made by deceased? - A dying declaration can be made sole basis of conviction of an accused provided dying declaration is found to be true and voluntary and is not a result of tutoring or prompting or a product of imagination - Held, in present case, PW-1 stated in his deposition before Trial Court that deceased told him that appellant had beaten and burnt her after pouring kerosene oil on her - Statement of PW-1 as to cause of death due to burns caused by appellant by pouring kerosene oil on her also finds corroboration in statements of PW-3 and PW-4 - (B) Evidence of PWs 1 to 4 (brother, father and sisters of deceased) - Reliability of - Held, evidence of a witness cannot be ignored or thrown out solely because they are closely related to victim - In present case, evidence of PWs 1 to 4 is clear and categorical in reference to frequent quarrels between deceased and appellant - They have clearly and consistently supported prosecution version - PWs 1 to 4 unequivocally stated that deceased feared threat to her life from appellant - (C) Whether it was a case of suicide or accidental death? - Held, none of prosecution witnesses in their testimonies stated about possibility of deceased committing suicide - Also after considering oral and material evidence cumulatively, including written statement of appellant, suicide theory is found to be unsustainable - Further, from facts and circumstances of case, it is clear that it is appellant poured kerosene on deceased, thereby causing her death - Appeal dismissed.
