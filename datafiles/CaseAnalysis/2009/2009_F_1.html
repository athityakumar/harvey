FGP Limited v Saleh Hooseini Dr. and Another

Supreme Court of India

15 September 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Ganguly, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1137; (2009) 10 SCC 223; 2009(5) ALL MR 990; 2010 (3) Bom.C.R. 348; JT 2009 (12) SC 210; 2009 (4) RCR(Civil) 449; 2009 (2) RCR(Rent) 324; 2010 (109) RD 438; 2010 (1) RLW 434; 2009(12) SCALE 516; [2009] 14 S.C.R. 995

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Rent Control

Keywords: Equitable Doctrine, Executors, Revisional Order, Doctrine Of Part Performance, Transfer of Property Amendment Act, 1929

Summary: Rent Control - Land & Property - Maharashtra Rent Control Act, 1999 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s.53-A - Indian Succession Act, 1925, ss.211, 213, 222 and 234 - Maintainability of suit - Appeal filed by appellant-tenant against order passed by HC in its revisional jurisdiction - Appellant entered into a tenancy agreement with mother (original owner of suit premises) of respondents - Suit for eviction filed by respondents against appellant - Said suit allowed - Appeal thereagainst dismissed - Revision application filed before HC also dismissed - Hence, present appeal - (A) Appellant contended original owner entered into sale agreement with it to sell suit premised for a sum of Rs.5 lacs and entire sale consideration had been received by original owner - As sale agreement was not completed within time, appellant filed a suit for specific performance which is still pending - Whether suit for eviction maintainable? - As per tenancy agreement said amount of Rs.5 lacs was given as a security deposit - Held, claim of appellant that said deposit of Rs.5 lacs was sale consideration money for suit premises contradicted by its averments in suit and also from material documents on record - Appellant has not succeeded in making out a strong case on specific performance so as to restrain respondents from proceeding with their suit for eviction - (B) Whether submission by appellant on part performance of contract u/s.53-A of Transfer of Property Act can be accepted? - There can be no part performance where there is no completed agreement in existence - Appellant is required to show that it either performed or is willing to perform its part of contract - Admitted facts of case are to contrary - Held, no case for part performance of contract has been made out - In facts and circumstances of case, doctrine of part performance u/s.53-A cannot be invoked - (C) Competence of respondents to file suit - Original owner, appointed her husband to be executor of Will and failing him appointed her sons and daughter to be executors/executrix of Will jointly as well as severally for all purposes - Recitals in Will in consonance with ss.222 and 234 of Indian Succession Act - Also respondent no.2 along with others is residuary legatee and is one of owners of suit premises - Held, co-owner can always maintain a suit for eviction - (D) Challenge to maintainability of suit on ground that it was filed by respondents as co- owners - Held, a co-owner of a property is an owner of property, till property is partitioned - Since in instant case, no partition has taken place, respondents status as co- owners cannot be disputed by tenant - Suit maintainable - (E) Challenge to maintainability of suit on ground of absence of any probate having been obtained by respondents - Held, grant of probate does not give title to executor - It just makes his title certain - U/s.213, grant of probate is not a condition precedent to filing of a suit in order to claim a right as an executor under will - This vesting of right is enough for executor or administrator to represent estate in a legal proceeding - No merit in appeal - Appeal dismissed.
