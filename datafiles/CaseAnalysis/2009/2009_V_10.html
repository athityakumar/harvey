K. K. Ahuja v V. K. Vora and Another

Supreme Court of India

06 July 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 850; (2009) 10 SCC 48; 2009 (3) BC 676; 2009 (4) Bom.C.R. 558; [2009] 152 Comp Cas 520; 2009 (4) CompLJ 295; 2009 (5) CTC 81; 2009 (3) JCC (NI) 194; 2009 (4) MLJ(Crl) 1330; 2009 (3) RCR(Civil) 788; 2009 (3) RCR(Criminal) 571; 2009(9) SCALE 87; [2009] 9 S.C.R. 1144

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Corporate; Criminal

Keywords: Specific Part, Dishonoured Cheque, Cheques, Employees' State Insurance Act, 1948, Offences By Companies, Employees' Provident Funds and Miscellaneous Provisions Act, 1952, Whole-Time Director, Ex-Director, Accustomed To Act

Summary: Criminal - Banking & Finance - Corporate - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, ss. 141(1)(2) and 138 - Companies Act, 1956, ss. 2(24)(26)(30)(31)(45), 5 and 291 - Offences by company - Expression, 'in-charge of, and was responsible to the company for the business of the company' - Meaning thereof - Criminal complaint u/s. 138 against the company and eight other persons - One of the person was Deputy General Manager (respondent no. 1) of the company who filed petition u/s. 482 Code of Criminal Procedure for quashing the proceedings against him on the ground that as 'DGM' he was not 'in-charge of and was responsible to the company for the conduct of the business of the company' - Whether DGM comes within the expression? - Held, No - Provision of s. 141(1)(2) perused and provisions of Companies Act read conjointly - (i) In the complaint the complainant averred that 'at the time of the commission of offence, accused 2 to 9 were in-charge of and responsible for the conduct of day to day business of accused no.l/company' and that therefore they were deemed to be guilty of offence u/s. 138 r/w s. 141 of NI Act - Averment whether sufficient? - Held, No - Apart from the company two kinds of persons are deemed to be guilty of the offence and shall be liable to be proceeded against and punished, they are, one, who was in charge of and was responsible to the company for the conduct of the business of the company, two, any director, manager, secretary or other officer of the company with whose consent and connivance, the offence u/s. 138 has been committed, or whose negligence resulted in such offence being committed by the company - First category person is vicariously liable by reason only of his fulfilling the requirements of sub/s. (1) of s. 141 - Court observed that if the person responsible to the company for the conduct of business of the company, was not in charge of the conduct of the business of the company, then he can be made liable only if the offence was committed with his consent or connivance or as a result of his negligence - Second category persons as mentioned in sub/s. (2) of s. 141, are liable not on account of any legal fiction but on account of the specific part played i.e. consent and connivance or negligence - Court pointed that if a person is to be made liable u/sub-s. (2) of s. 141, then it is necessary to aver consent and connivance, or negligence on his part - (ii) Who are the persons who are responsible to the company for the conduct of the business of the company, and who could be said to be in charge and was responsible to the company for the conduct of the business of the company? - Relevant provisions of Companies Act, 1956 perused - Settled position, a MD is prima facie in charge of and responsible for the company's business and affairs and can be prosecuted for offences by the company; but insofar as other directors are concerned, they can be prosecuted only if they were in charge of and responsible for the conduct of the company's business - Court combined read provisions of s. 5 and 291 of Companies Act, 1956 with the definitions in cls. (24), (26), (30), (31), (45) of s. 2 of such Act and found seven persons who are considered to be the persons who are responsible to the company for the conduct of the business of the company, they are inter alia, MD(s), whole-time director/s, the manager, the secretary - Court observed, that a person who can be made vicariously liable u/sub-s.(1) of s. 141 is a person who is responsible to the company for the conduct of the business of the company and in addition is also in charge of the business of the company - Court summarized the position u/s. 141 of NI Act - Court observed that DGM is not a person who is responsible to the company for the conduct of the business of the company; he does not fall under any of the categories (a) to (g) listed in s. 5 of Companies Act - DGM cannot thus be made vicariously liable u/s. 141(1) of the NI Act - Court further observed that if he has to be made liable u/s. 142(2) the necessary averments relating to consent/connivance/negligence should have been made - DGM thus could not be prosecuted u/sub-ss. (1) and (2) of s. 141 of NI Act - Impugned order upheld - Appeals dismissed.
