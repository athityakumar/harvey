Rasiklal v Kishore

Supreme Court of India

20 February 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Panchal, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1910; (2009) 2 SCC (Cr) 338; (2009) 4 SCC 446; AIR 2009 SC 1341; 2009 CRLJ 1887; 2009 (3) G.L.R. 2099; JT 2009 (3) SC 26; 2009 (4) MPLJ 412; 2009 (2) RCR(Criminal) 161; 2009 (1) RLW 580; 2009(3) SCALE 9; [2009] 2 S.C.R. 795

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006.)

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 499, 500 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 436 - Cancelation of bail - Appellant was charge-sheeted for the offence of defamation - Appellant filed bail application before the Trial Court - Trial Court allowed bail application - However, complainant sought the cancellation of bail on the ground that he was not heard, thus, principles of natural justice violated - Single Judge cancelled the bail and directed the Trial Court to re-hear the matter - Whether cancellation of bail by HC is proper - Held, bail granted to the appellant for alleged commission of bailable offence, could not have been cancelled by the HC on the ground that the complainant was not heard and, thus, principles of natural justice were violated - Therefore, impugned HC order set aside and Trail Court order restored - Appeal disposed of.
