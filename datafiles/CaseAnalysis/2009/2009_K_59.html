Jharu Ram Roy v Kamjit Roy and Others

Supreme Court of India

13 January 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Mukundakam Sharma, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 672; (2009) 4 SCC 60; 2009(3) ALL MR 441; 2009 (76) ALR 896; 2010 (2) AWC 2003; 2009 (2) CTC 337; 2010 (2) JhrCR(SC) 179; 2009 (4) MahLJ 676; 2009 (108) RD 559; 2009(2) SCALE 35; [2009] 1 S.C.R. 305

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Equitable Doctrine, Unauthorised Person, Fraudulently Or Erroneously Represents, Interest In Land, Confirmation Of Possession

Summary: Land & Property - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 43 - Doctrine of feeding the estoppel - Appellant purchased the suit property from the predecessor-in-interest of the property - However, 1st respondent was put in possession of the suit property as a result of a criminal proceeding - 1st respondent filed a suit for confirming his title and possession - Trial Court dismissed the suit as barred by limitation - 1st respondent filed appeal before the First Appellate Court - First Appellate Court held that the predecessor-in-interest of the suit property had no valid title, therefore, sale deeds executed by him in favour of the appellant were void ab initio - Appellant filed appeal before HC - HC dismissed the appeal - Whether doctrine of feeding of estoppel u/s. 43 of the Act, is applicable to the present case - Held, predecessor-in-interest of the suit property made a stipulation in the sale deeds that he had inherited the property from his deceased father, however, his father had not expired at the time of execution of the sale deeds - Appellant was also aware of the fact that the father of the predecessor-in-interest had not expired at the time of execution of sale deeds - Sale deeds were vitiated by the element of fraud, hence the doctrine of feeding of estoppel u/s. 43 of the Act, not applicable in the present case - Appeal dismissed.
