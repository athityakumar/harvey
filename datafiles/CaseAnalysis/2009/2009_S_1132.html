State of Uttar Pradesh v Sattan @ Satyendra and Others

Supreme Court of India

27 February 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 238; (2009) 2 SCC (Cr) 572; (2009) 4 SCC 736; JT 2009 (4) SC 347; 2010 (1) MLJ(Crl) 859; 2009 (2) RCR(Criminal) 319; 2009(3) SCALE 394; [2009] 3 S.C.R. 643

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Bride Burning, Appeals Disposed Of, Mitigating Circumstance, Mitigating Factor, Pellet Mark

Summary: Criminal - IPC, 1860, ss. 302 r/w 149, 307 r/w 149 - High Court altered death sentence to life sentence - Appeals against - Held, undue sympathy to impose inadequate sentence would do more harm to the justice system to undermine the public confidence in the efficacy of law and society could not long endure under such serious threats - It is the duty of every court to award proper sentence having regard to the nature of the offence and the manner in which it was executed or committed etc - Murder of six members of a family including helpless women and children having been committed in a brutal, diabolic and bristly manner and the crime being one which is enormous in proportion which shocks the conscious of law, the death sentence as awarded in respect of accused Sattan and Guddu was the appropriate sentence and the High Court ought not to have altered it - Appeals disposed of.
