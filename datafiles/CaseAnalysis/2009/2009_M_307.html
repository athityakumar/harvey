Mohd. Asif v State Of Uttaranchal

Supreme Court of India

06 March 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 313; (2009) 11 SCC 497; 2009 ALL MR (Cri) 1202; 2009 CRLJ 2789; JT 2009 (4) SC 1B; 2009 (2) RCR(Criminal) 440; 2009(3) SCALE 695; [2009] 4 S.C.R. 12; 2009 (2) UC 868

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Sufficient In Ordinary Course Of Nature

Summary: Criminal - IPC, 1860, s. 302 - Appeal against conviction and sentence - Whether a case for converting the sentence from s. 300 to 304 has been made out? - Held, if dying declaration is trustworthy, a judgment of conviction can be based thereupon - Question with regard to finding out the intention on the part of the accused to cause death depends upon the facts and circumstances of each case - It is not a case which attracts the provisions of s. 304 Part II or s. 326 thereof - It is not a case where the intervening ailment was wholly unconnected with the injury - Appellant has rightly been found guilty of commission of an offence under s. 302 - Appeal dismissed.
