State of Madhya Pradesh v Devendra

Supreme Court of India

05 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, D.K. Jain, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1906; (2009) 14 SCC 80; AIR 2009 SC 3009; 2009 ALL MR (Cri) 3801; 2009 CRLJ 4130; 2009 (5) G.L.R. 4286; 2009 (3) JCC 2159; JT 2009 (13) SC 679; 2010 (1) RCR(Criminal) 223; 2010 (1) RLW 164; 2009(8) SCALE 816; [2009] 8 S.C.R. 98

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Dangerous Drugs Act, 1930, Bombay Public Security Measures Act, 1947, Bombay Beggars Act, 1945, Karnataka Habitual Offenders Act, 1961, Karnataka Prohibition of Beggary Act, 1975

Summary: Criminal - Identification of Prisoners Act, 1920 - Respondent filed application for bail before HC, however, respondent did not press the application as it was infructuous - HC dismissed the application and issued general directions to the State to give photographs of the witnesses as well as the accused persons to the prosecution at the time of filing charge sheet to curb delay due to non-service of summons and warrants in criminal trials - Whether directions issued by the HC is called for - Held, HC directions are subject to the provisions of the Act, Madhya Pradesh Police Regulation and Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 and in case of conflict statute itself prevails - In case of complainant as well as witnesses, where prosecution wants to protect the identity, the reasons must be recorded - In case of rape victims, photographs should not be taken - HC directions modified - Appeal disposed of.
