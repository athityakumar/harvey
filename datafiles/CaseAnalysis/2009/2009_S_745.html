Precious Oil Corporation and Others v State of Assam

Supreme Court of India

05 February 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, P. Sathasivam, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 817; (2009) 15 SCC 752; AIR 2009 SC 1566; 2009 ALL MR (Cri) 1763; 2009 CRLJ 1330; 2009 (1) FAC 400; 2009 (3) G.L.R. 2049; JT 2009 (3) SC 515; 2009 (1) RCR(Criminal) 964; 2009(2) SCALE 234; [2009] 1 S.C.R. 762

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection; Criminal; Socio-Economic

Keywords: Control Order, Lubricating Oil and Greases (Processing, Supply & Distribution Regulation) Order, 1987

Summary: Criminal - Health & Drug - Essential Commodities Act, 1955 - Lubricating Oil and Greases (Processing, Supply & Distribution Regulation) Order, 1987, cl. 3 - Probation of Offenders Act, 1958 - Appellants convicted for offence punishable under Section 7(1)(a)(i) of Essential Commodities Act, 1955 - Conviction upheld by HC - Hence, present appeal - (A) Whether conviction correct? - Held, appellant violated Cl. 3 of Control Order - At time of inspection, large quantity of stocks and/or products was stored - No licence was obtained, proper books of accounts were not maintained and adulterated lubricating oil was stored - Trial Court and HC rightly decided that there has been contravention of Cl. 3 of Control Order - (B) Whether Probation Act can be applied? - Held, application of probation principles is negatived by imperatives of social defence and improbabilities of moral proselytisation - Neither casual provocation nor motive against particular persons but planned profit-making from numbers of consumers furnishes incentive - No merit in appeal - Appeal dismissed.
