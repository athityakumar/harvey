Malik Mazhar Sultan and another v Uttar Pradesh Public Service Commission and others

Supreme Court of India

14 July 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

K.G. Balakrishnan, P. Sathasivam, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 2141; 2010(3) SCALE 342

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Civil Judge - Promotion - Oral interview - Petitioners were Civil Judges (junior division) in cadre of State Subordinate Judicial Service - Petitioners were denied promotion to cadre of Civil Judge (Senior Division) - Petitioners contented that they were in zone of consideration for promotion to cadre of Civil Judge (Senior Division) were subjected to oral interview and based on marks secured in interview, promotions were given and 33 candidates, including present petitioners, were denied promotion - Hence instant petitions.

Held, that Court does not think that these officers should be subjected to oral interview for purpose of promotion. Normally promotions are given based on evaluation of ACRs and seniority and Court does not think that they shall be subjected to oral interview for purpose of their promotion to Civil Judge (Senior Division). Direction of Court in said judgment that for purpose of promotion, their performance based on oral interview shall also is considered as deleted. If any HC/State Govt. has framed any rules pursuant to direction of Court, that rule also is treated as deleted. These 33 candidates who have been denied promotion to Civil Judge (Senior Division) would be at liberty to approach HC to seek appropriate relief. Application files main grievance is that 25% of direct recruitment is not being complied with, according to these petitioners, cadre strength of District and Sessions Judges is 166 including Fast Track Court Judges. Whereas stand of HC that many of posts are temporary in nature and at any time Tribunals or Special Judges posts likely to be abolished, they are not taken as part of permanent cadre and according to HC total cadre strength of District Judges is 96 and already there are 24 District Judges. As there is dispute regarding cadre strength, without prejudice to rights of petitioners they may agitate question before HC. Petitions disposed of.
