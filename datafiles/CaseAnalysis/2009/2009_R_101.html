Rajendra Singh Etc.Etc. v State Of U.P. & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

31 July 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 935; (2009) 15 SCC 178; 2009(5) ALL MR 514; 2010 (1) AWC 539; 2010 (2) CTC 575; JT 2009 (10) SC 187; 2009(10) SCALE 551; [2009] 12 S.C.R. 243; 2010 (1) SLR 632

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Non-application Of Mind, Executive Instructions

Summary: Service - Transfer order - Validity of - Scope of interference of Court - Writ Petitioner, working as Sub-Registrar, Ghaziabad transferred to Hapur-II while Respondent No. 5, working as Sub-Registrar, Hapur-II transferred to Ghaziabad - Transfer order challenged before HC on ground that order of transfer was arbitrary, stigmatic and suffers from non-application of mind - HC upheld order of transfer of Writ Petitioner but quashed order of transfer of respondent No. 5 - Challenged - Whether order passed by HC justified? - No vested to Govt. Servant to remain posted at a place of his choice nor can he insist that he must be posted at one place or other - He is liable to be transferred in administrative exigencies from one place to other - Courts are always reluctant in interfering with transfer of an employee unless such transfer is vitiated by violation of some statutory provisions or suffers from mala fides - As to at what place Respondent No. 5 should be posted is an exclusive prerogative of State Govt. and in exercise of that prerogative, Respondent No. 5 was transferred from Hapur-II to Ghaziabad-IV keeping in view administrative exigencies - HC went into comparative conduct and integrity of writ petitioner and Respondent No. 5 while dealing with a transfer matter - Only question required to be seen was whether transfer of Respondent No. 5 was actuated with malafides or otherwise in violation of statutory rules - Held, HC seriously erred in deciding as to whether Respondent No. 5 was a competent person to be posted at Ghaziabad-IV as Sub-Registrar - Exercise undertaken by HC did not fall within its domain and was rather uncalled for - Impugned order of HC casts stigma in service of Respondent No. 5 which may also act prejudicial to his interest in pending appeal against adverse remarks - Transfer of Writ Petitioner from Ghaziabad-IV to Hapur-II cannot be said to be stigmatic - Order passed by HC quashing transfer of Respondent No. 5 from Hapur-II to Ghaziabad-IV set aside - Order accordingly.
