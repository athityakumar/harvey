State Of Punjab v Gurnam Kaur & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

06 March 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Mukundakam Sharma, H.L. Dattu

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 311; (2009) 11 SCC 225; (2009) 3 SCC (Cr) 1311; JT 2009 (3) SC 373; 2009 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 944; 2009 (2) RCR(Criminal) 309; 2009(4) SCALE 343; [2009] 3 S.C.R. 1195

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Health & Drug; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act - Appeal against acquittal - Held, there are several infirmities in the prosecution case - If by a reason of statements made by an accused some facts have been discovered, the same would be admissible against the person who had made the statement in terms of s. 27 of the Indian Evidence Act - Prosecution has not examined any independent witness - Why independent witnesses could not be found has not been explained - Appeal dismissed.
