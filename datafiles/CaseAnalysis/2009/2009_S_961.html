Mohd. Farooq Abdul Gafur and Another v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

06 August 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1977; AIR 2010 SC (Supp) 884; 2009(11) SCALE 327; [2009] 12 S.C.R. 1093

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Mitigating Circumstance, Ill Treatment, New Accused, False Entry, TADA Act, Co- Accused, Post- Mortem Report, Arms (Amendment) Act, 1983, Terrorist and Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Rules, 1987, Maharashtra Control of Organized Crime Rules, 1999

Summary: Criminal - Maharashtra Control of Organised Crime Act, 1999, s. 18 (MCOCA) - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 120B - Arms Act, 1959, ss. 25(1A), 25(1B), 27(3), 39 - Appellants allegedly fired indiscriminately at the office of a political party and thereby, caused the death of 3 persons and also attempted to murder the party leader - Trial Court convicted appellants for the murder of 3 persons, promoting insurgency, causing injuries and endangering the life of 7 persons and sentenced appellants to death - HC also confirmed the conviction, however, substituted the death sentence with rigorous imprisonment for life - (A) Whether conviction of the 1st appellant on the basis of his confession is valid in law - Held, preliminary warning was given to 1st appellant prior to the recording of his confessional statement under the provisions of MCOCA, hence such confessions could be the basis of conviction under the MCOCA - Therefore, 1st appellant guilty of all the charges which were already found to be proved and established by the Trial Court and affirmed by the HC - Sentence passed by the HC confirmed and punishment awarded by the Trial Court u/s. 212 r/w s. 52A r/w 120B of IPC is restored - (B) Whether conviction of 2nd appellant on the basis of his retracted confession is valid in law - Held, confession of 2nd appellant was trustworthy and reliable - Confessional statement was found to be in substantial compliance with the requirements of s. 18 of MCOCA - HC rightly held that 2nd appellant had played an active and important role in the conspiracy even though he did not participate in the actual shoot out and that he had the knowledge of the conspiracy - Therefore, conviction of 2nd appellant is sustainable on the basis of his retracted confession - (C) Whether confessional statements of co-accused admissible as evidence against other accused persons - Held, no non-obstante clause present in s. 18(3) of MCOCA which precludes the application of s. 313 of CrPC and, therefore, the evidence of a co-accused is admissible as a piece of substantive evidence - (D) Whether conviction of appellants under the provisions of Arms Act is sustainable - Held, report of ballistic expert and medical evidence showed that the death of the 3 deceased persons was caused by the injuries sustained due to fire arms during the shootout and, therefore, it can be inferred that the weapon was used in the shootout - Finding of the HC that the sanction order u/s. 39 of the Arms Act suffered from non-application of mind is not sustainable in view of the material available on record shows that the sanction order in the present case is a detailed one and displays proper application of mind - Therefore, conviction of appellants under the Arms Act is sustainable - Appeals dismissed.
