State Of Madhya Pradesh And Others v Yogendra Shrivastava

Supreme Court of India

07 October 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, B. SUDARSHAN REDDY

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 2221; (2010) 12 SCC 538; JT 2009 (13) SC 448; 2009(13) SCALE 329; [2009] 14 S.C.R. 1137; 2010 (3) SLR 368

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Madhya Pradesh Employees State Insurance Service (Gazetted) Recruitment Rules, 1981

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 309 - Salary - Appointment - Sustainability - Whenever there was increases in pay on account of annual increments, NPA was not correspondingly increased so as to maintain it at 25% of basic pay, but continued to be paid at fixed lump sum rate - State Govt. was revising fixed lump sum NPA, whenever there was revisions in pay scales, by issuing executive orders - Respondents in instant appeals and several other medical officers brought this anomaly to notice of department and requested that NPA be paid to them at 25% of pay in accordance with Rules - Request was not acceded to and respondents approached Administrative Tribunal - Tribunal allowed applications and directed appellant to calculate and pay NPA at rate of 25% of pay from dates of joining service - State Govt. filed writ petitions challenging orders of Tribunal - HC dismissed petitions, upholding orders of Tribunal - Orders of HC were challenged in appeals by special leave - Hence, instant appeals - Whether rates of NPA specified in letters of appointment and orders of department, could prevail over provisions relating to NPA Rules retrospective amendment of Rules by notification could deny benefit which had accrued under unamended Rules and whether financial benefit could be extended beyond three years prior to filing of original applications before Tribunal - Held, Executive orders could not be made or given effect in violation of what was mandated by Rules - If appointment letters provides for payment of NPA which was not in consonance with Rules, they could be corrected or set right by Tribunals/Courts - While amendment, even if it was to be considered as otherwise valid, could not affect rights and benefits which had accrued to employees under unamended rules - Right to NPA @ 25% of pay, having accrued to respondents under unamended Rules, it follows that respondents-employees would be entitled to Non-Practicing Allowance @ 25% of their pay upto 20-5-2003 - It was further held that lesser payment might be consequence of error that was committed at time of appointment, claim for higher allowance in accordance with Rules could not be rejected merely because it arises from wrong fixation made several years prior to claim for correct payment - In respect of grant of consequential relief of recovery of arrears for past period, principle relating to recurring and successive wrongs would apply - Therefore, consequential relief of payment of arrears would have to be restricted to period of 3 years prior to date of original application - Hence, Tribunal decision was upheld and respondents were entitled to NPA @ 25% of pay - With regard to arrears, respondents were entitled to recover difference in NPA only in regard to salary which accrued due during 3 years prior to date of filing of original applications by respondents before Tribunal and not from date of appointments - Appellants, in pursuance of orders of Tribunal/HC had paid difference in NPA, for any period beyond 3 years before date of respective original applications, they were at liberty to recover same from respondents - Appeals partly allowed.
