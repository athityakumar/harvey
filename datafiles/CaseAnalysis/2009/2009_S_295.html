Abdul Rahim & Ors v Sk. Abdul Zabar & Ors

Supreme Court of India

06 March 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, A.K. Ganguly, R.M. Lodha

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 297; (2009) 6 SCC 160; AIR 2010 SC 211; 2009 (75) ALR 707; 2009 (2) AWC 2040; 2009 (5) G.L.R. 4347; 2009 (4) JhrCR(SC) 106; JT 2009 (3) SC 569; 2009 (4) MLJ(SC) 1075; 2009 (4) MPLJ 81; 2009 (2) RCR(Civil) 934; 2009(5) SCALE 43; [2009] 4 S.C.R. 32

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Testamentary Disposition

Summary: Family & Personal - Interpretation and/or application of the Islamic Law on Gift vis-'-vis handing over of possession of the property gifted - Held, if by reason of a valid gift the thing gifted has gone out of the donee's ownership, the same cannot be revoked - Constructive possession of the suit premises must be held to have been handed over by the donor as he had himself prayed for mutation of Razak's name in the revenue record - Having failed to take into consideration the import and purport of the donor's application before the Tahasildar committed a manifest error in holding that the order of mutation on that basis was not decisive - Appeal allowed.
