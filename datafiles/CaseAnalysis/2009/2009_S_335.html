Sheetala Prasad And Others v Sri Kant And Another

Supreme Court of India

17 December 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Panchal, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 2042; (2010) 2 SCC 190; AIR 2010 SC 1140; 2010 ALL MR (Cri) 2326; 2010 CRLJ 1404; JT 2009 (15) SC 227; 2010 (1) MLJ(Crl) 1265; 2010 (1) RCR(Criminal) 382; 2009(14) SCALE 604; [2009] 16 S.C.R. 686; 2010 (1) UC 259

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Private Complainant

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 308 and 324/149 - Attempt to commit culpable homicide - Appellants with common object, assaulted sons of respondent no. 1 - Trial court held that no case for commission of offence punishable u/s. 308 IPC was made out against appellants, but it was proved by prosecution that appellants had committed offences pun1ishable u/ss.148, 324 r/w. s. 149 IPC and s. 429 r/w. s. 149 IPC - On appeal, HC set aside acquittal of appellants u/s. 308 IPC as well as their conviction u/s. 324 and also direction to release them on probation - HC further remitted matter to Trial Court, to pass fresh order of conviction and sentence on appellants - Hence, present appeal - Whether HC's order could be allowed? - Held, when HC decides to interfere with judgment of Trial Court in exercise of revisional jurisdiction, retrial of case is ordered based on certain well settled principles - However, after recording guilt of an accused under particular provision of IPC, matter could not have been remitted to Trial Court for passing appropriate order of conviction and punishment - HC's order set aside - Trial Court's order restored - Appeal allowed.
