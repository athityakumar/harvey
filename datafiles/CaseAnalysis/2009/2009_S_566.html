State Of Punjab & Anr v Surjit Singh & Ors

Supreme Court of India

04 August 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Deepak Verma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1054; (2009) 9 SCC 514; JT 2009 (10) SC 424; 2009(11) SCALE 149; [2009] 12 S.C.R. 394; 2010 (1) SLR 678

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Appeals Disposed Of, Mode Of Selection, Absence From Duty, Temporary Or Ad Hoc, Irregular Appointment, Temporary Appointees

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, arts.14, 16 - Doctrine of equal pay for equal work - Applicability of - Respondents appointed in different capacities in State health service - Respondents were put in number of years of service - Parity in pay claimed by respondents - Writ Petition filed by respondents before HC - HC allowed petitions - Legality - Held, recruitment rules had not been followed while making appointments of respondents - Manner in which names of respondents called from employment exchange has not been disclosed - Appointments were made by a Committee which was not competent thereof - Appointment of clerk and above post were required to be made by Public Service Commission - Burden of proof of parity on all aspects to be established by respondents, not State - Similarity in qualification, interchangeability of positions of regular and casual employees and other relevant factors have to be considered - However, directed the State to examine cases of respondents in the light of decision of SC in State of Haryana v. Charanjit Singh ((2006) 9 SCC 321) in order to meet the interest of justice - Appeals disposed of.
