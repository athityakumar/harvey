Karnataka Urban Water Supply and Drainage Board, Etc v K. S. Gangadharappa and Another

Supreme Court of India

15 April 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Lokeshwar Singh Panta, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 498; (2009) 11 SCC 164; 2009 (75) ALR 628; 2009 (3) AWC 2592; JT 2009 (7) SC 7; 2009 (5) KarLJ 369; 2009 (2) RCR(Civil) 895; 2009 (107) RD 317; 2009(6) SCALE 1; [2009] 6 S.C.R. 250

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Appeals Disposed Of, Hypothetical, Madhya Pradesh Town Improvement Trust Act, 1960

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, s. 18 - Compensation - Enhancement - Market value - Comparable sales - Land Acquisition Officer determining compensation at Rs. 12,500 per acre - On reference u/s. 18 Civil Court raised compensation to Rs. 54,500 p/acre - HC remanded the matter again to Civil Court - Civil Court under remand raised compensation to Rs. 1,35,000 p/acre in respect of land measuring 5 acres 5 guntas - In Appeal HC raised compensation to Rs. 4,00,000 p/acre - For determination of market value HC had taken sale price of certain land measuring 30' x 50' as foundation - HC's conclusion whether proper? - Trite proposition; prices fetched for small plots cannot form safe bases for valuation of large tracts of land as the two are not comparable properties - However, if large tracts of land are to be used for building purposes or plotting, than valuation indicated by sale of comparable small sites in the area at or about the time of notification would be relevant - Necessary deductions in that case has to be made for the extent of land required for the formation of roads and other civic amenities etc. - Inevitable element of speculation and guess work in the exercise of fixation of market value with reference to comparable sales can be reduced to the minimum, if sale is within a reasonable time of the date of notification u/s 4(1); transaction is bonafide; it should be of the land acquired or the land adjacent to the land acquired; and it should possess similar advantages - HC conclusion found to be without any basis - Market value has a definite concept and it cannot be evaluated without any foundation or basis - Matter remitted for consideration afresh - Appeals disposed of.
