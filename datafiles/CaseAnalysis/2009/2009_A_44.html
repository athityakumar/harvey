Kailashi Bai v Aarti Arya and Another

Supreme Court of India

27 April 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1914; (2009) 13 SCC 548; 2009 (4) JhrCR(SC) 51; 2009 (3) RCR(Criminal) 1; 2009(7) SCALE 304; [2009] 7 S.C.R. 312

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Private Complainant

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 306 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 482 - Criminal Proceedings - Quashing of - 1st respondent allegedly abetted the deceased in committing suicide - Trial Court summoned appellant to face trial - Appellant sought quashing of proceedings - Single Judge quashed the criminal proceedings by holding that ingredients of s. 306 were not established - Whether HC order is legally sustainable - Exercise of jurisdiction u/s. 482 of the CrPC has to be done only in exceptional cases and where there is prima facie material trial was to be held - Held, in the peculiar facts and circumstances of the case, HC was right in exercising its jurisdiction u/s. 482 of the CrPC - No interference on HC order - Appeal dismissed.
