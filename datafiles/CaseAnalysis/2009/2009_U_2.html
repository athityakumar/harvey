(1) Southern Railway Officers Assn. and Another; ( v (1) Union of India and Others; (2) G. Lakshmanan;

Supreme Court of India

04 August 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1072; (2009) 9 SCC 24; AIR 2010 SC 1241; 2009 (3) CLR 171; 2009 (5) ESC 733; 2009 (123) FLR 417; JT 2009 (15) SC 148; 2009 (4) LLJ 1; 2009(11) SCALE 263; [2009] 12 S.C.R. 429; 2009 (8) SLR 532

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Reduction In Rank, Absence From Duty, Dispensing With Enquiry, Railway Servants (Discipline and Appeal) Rules, 1968, Railway Servants (Conduct) Rules, 1966

Summary: Service - General indiscipline and insubordination - Dismissal - Respondent-employees created ugly scenes at platform and assaulted an officer during his send off at railway station - Disciplinary authority dismissed respondents from service - Appellate authority dismissed appeals of respondents - Revisional Authority dismissed respondents' revision except one who had been ordered to reinstate in lower grade - Tribunal set aside dismissal of 2 respondents, however it declined to interfere punishment on other respondents - Writ sought against - HC ordered to reinstate all respondents with back wages - Legality - Held, appellate order satisfied dicta laid down in Ram Chander v. Union of India and others, (1986 Indlaw SC 69 ) - Disciplinary Authority could not conduct departmental proceedings due to prevailing situations - Appellate Authority was entitled to consider situation prevailing from confidential reports submitted by other employees - Appellate Authority has recorded reasons in its order - Order of disciplinary authority themselves disclosed existence of sufficient materials - Incident was not denied before statutory authority nor denied the fact of lodging FIR - Fact of one respondent was arrested on the day of incident itself and others were arrested after a period of 2 weeks, admitted - Display of handwritten poster both at workshop and at railway station had also not been denied - No material brought before HC to show that dismissal were wholly non-existent - No mala fide on part of disciplinary authority - Disciplinary authority had not taken any irrelevant fact not germane or failed to take into consideration any relevant fact - Acquittal in a criminal case by itself cannot be a ground for interfering with an order of punishment imposed by disciplinary authority - Entire record satisfied itself that same was sufficient for dispense with enquiry - Appellants can file SLP before SC even though they have given undertaking with respect to waiving of their right to appeal - Therefore, impugned judgment and Tribunal's order set aside - Appeal allowed.
