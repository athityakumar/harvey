B. H. E. L. v Globe Hi-fabs Limited

Supreme Court of India

12 December 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Markandey Katju, A. K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 2297; (2015) 5 SCC 718

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Keywords: Limitation Act, 1963, Arbitration Act, 1940, Bombay Land Requisition Act, 1948, Sunday Observance Act, 1780

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Application of principle of ejusdem generis - Justification - Appellant contended that in case of arbitration proceedings, interest can only be awarded from date of award and not before date of award - However, respondent contended that clause of agreement has to be read ejusdem generis and hence literal interpretation cannot be given to words 'or on any money due to contractor by employer' - Whether interest can be awarded before date of award.

Held, principle of ejusdem generis does not apply in every situation and if preceding words do not constitute mere specifications of genus but constitute description of complete genus, rule has no application. Further, SC held that it is not inviolable rule of law, but it is only permissible inference in absence of indication to contrary, and where context and object and mischief of enactment do not require restricted meaning to be attached to words of general import, it becomes duty of courts to give those words their plain and ordinary meaning. SC held that in present case that clause barring interest is very widely worded. It uses words 'any amount due to contractor by employer' and these words cannot be read as ejusdem generis alongwith earlier words 'earnest money' or 'security deposit'. Hence, interest is only payable from date of award. However, lesser interest cannot be awarded when statute clearly states that rate of interest will be 18% p.a. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Principle of ejusdem generis cannot be applied when there is statutory indication to the contrary.
