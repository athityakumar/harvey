Union of India and Others v M. K. Sarkar

Supreme Court of India

08 December 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1854; (2010) 2 SCC 59; 2010(1) ALL MR 982; 2010 (1) AWC 872; 2010 (124) FLR 582; JT 2009 (15) SC 70; 2010 (2) LLJ 321; 2010 (1) MLJ(SC) 847; 2009(14) SCALE 425; [2009] 16 S.C.R. 249; 2009 (6) SLR 756

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Liberalised Pension Scheme, SRPF (contributory) Rules

Summary: Service - Pension - Exercise of option - Appellant introduced the pension scheme to its employees - Appellant provided option to those employees who were the subscribers of the Contributory Provident Fund Scheme (CPFS) to switch over to the pensions scheme - Respondent-employee, a CPFS subscriber, did not opt for the pension scheme on relevant time - However, respondent sought exercise of option after 22 years of his retirement - Appellant rejected respondent's request - Respondent filed application before Tribunal - Tribunal directed appellant to permit the respondent to exercise option - Appellant challenged Tribunal order in HC - HC dismissed appellant's writ petition - Whether the respondent was entitled to exercise an option to switch over to the pension scheme after 22 years of his retirement and receipt of the retirement dues under CPFS - Held, a person, who is aware of the availability of option, cannot contend that he was not served a written notice of the availability of the option after 22 years - Therefore, the respondent who did not exercise the option available was not entitled to seek an opportunity to exercise option to shift to the pension scheme, after the expiry of the validity period for the option scheme - Impugned orders of Tribunal and HC set aside - Appeal allowed.
