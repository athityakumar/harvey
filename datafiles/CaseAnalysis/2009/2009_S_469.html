State Of Jharkhand & Ors v Shiv Karampal Sahu

Supreme Court of India

15 April 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 457; (2009) 11 SCC 453; 2009 (4) AWC 3714; 2009 (3) ESC 393; 2009 (121) FLR 730; 2009(6) SCALE 261; [2009] 6 S.C.R. 63; 2009 (2) UPLBEC 1339

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Policy Decisions

Summary: Service - Circulars D/-21-09-1987 and 09-08-2000 - Interpretation and application - Compassionate appointment - Policy decision by State Govt. to grant monetary compensation to victims of incidents of terrorism and/or naxalism - Further policy decision of giving compassion appointment to the dependents vide Circular of 2000 - Respondent's father who was not a Govt. servant, was killed by extremists on 19-05-2000 - Compassionate appointment claimed - Reorganisation of State of Bihar - Policy decision of State of Jharkhand D/-07-05-2003, for giving such appointments from date of its formation i.e. 15-10-2000 - Claim of respondent accepted by High Court by giving retrospective effect to the Circular 2000 - Whether proper? - Held, No - Ordinarily, a subordinate legislation should not be construed to be retrospective in operation - Circular D/- 07-05-2003 was given a prospective effect - Father of the respondent died on 19-05-2000 - Nothing to show that circular of 2000 was given retrospective effect - Held, HC committed serious error in giving retrospective effect to circular in question indirectly which it could not do directly - Impugned judgement set aside - Appeal allowed.
