Sarabjit Singh & Anr. v State Of Punjab & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

12 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 773; (2009) 16 SCC 46; AIR 2009 SC 2792; 2009 ALL MR (Cri) 3148; 2009 CRLJ 3978; 2009 (3) RCR(Criminal) 388; 2009(8) SCALE 175; [2009] 8 S.C.R. 762; 2009 (2) UC 1105

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Power Of The Court

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 319 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 148, 302 r/w 149 - Power to proceed against other persons appearing to be guilty of offence - PW1, the first informant witnessed the main incident wherein accused persons were attacking the deceased with weapons and till they reached the satisfaction that the deceased had died they kept on attacking and afterwards left; later on appellants who were standing near the village suggested accused persons that the deceased had not died yet, so accused persons than again attacked the deceased - On the statement of PW1 application u/s. 319 CrPC was filed and allowed - Revision petition filed before High Court thereagainst - Dismissed - Whether exercise of jurisdiction u/s. 319 CrPC, in circumstance of the case proper? - Held, No - For interpretation and/or application of s. 319 CrPC, decisions of the Court referred and deliberation entered into on the subject - Provision of s. 319 perused - '..an extraordinary power which is conferred on the Court which should be used very sparingly and only if compelling reasons exist for taking cognizance against the other person against whom action has not been taken', 'Municipal Corporation of Delhi v. Ram Kishan Rastogi (1983) 1 SCC 1' - Nature of the evidence and materials brought before the Court must be such that would make out grounds for exercise of extraordinary power; order should not be passed only because the first informant or one of the witnesses seeks to implicate other person(s); sufficient and cogent reasons are required to be assigned by the Court so as to satisfy the ingredients of the provisions - One of the test is 'whether evidence on record is such which would reasonably lead to conviction of the person sought to be summoned?' - 'Mere existence of a prima facie case may not serve the purpose'; different standards are required to be applied at different stages, whereas the test of prima facie case may be sufficient for taking cognizance of an offence at the stage of framing of charge, the Court must be satisfied that there exists a strong suspicion - While framing charge in terms of s. 227 of CrPC, the Court must consider the entire materials on record to form an opinion that the evidence if unrebutted would lead to a judgment of conviction - Standard is high, for invoking jurisdiction u/s. 319 CrPC - Impugned judgement unsustainable - For consideration afresh - Matter remitted to Sessions Judge - Directions issued - Appeals allowed.
