Ramchandra Dagdu Sonavane (Dead) By L.Rs. and v Vithu Hira Mahar (Dead) By Lrs. And Others

Supreme Court of India

09 October 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Dattu, B.N. Agrawal, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1214; (2009) 10 SCC 273; AIR 2010 SC 818; 2010 (1) ALD(SC) 6; 2010 (1) Bom.C.R. 366; 2009 (5) G.L.R. 3569; JT 2009 (13) SC 338; 2009 (8) MLJ(SC) 871; 2009(13) SCALE 226; [2009] 15 S.C.R. 222

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Bombay Revenue Jurisdiction Act, 1876, Bombay Hereditary Office Act, 1874, Bombay Inferior Village Watan Abolition Act, 1958, Bombay Hereditary Offices Act, 1958

Summary: (A) Land & Property - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s.9 - Bombay Hereditary Offices Act, 1874, s.5(B) - Watandari rights - Suit for permanent injunction - 1st respondent allegedly having illegal possession of Watanland - Appellants claimed to be the nearest family members of deceased Watandar - Appellant filed petition before District Deputy Collector - District Deputy Collector ordered Watanland to appellants - Non-transfer of possession by respondent - Suit for permanent injunction filed by appellant - Plea of res judicata raised by respondent - Trial Court decreed suit in favor of appellants - Second Appeal of respondent dismissed by HC - Respondent filed an application before State Government - State Government directed an enquiry - Sub Division Officer (SDO) held an enquiry and re-granted Watandari rights to respondent - Order of SDO challenged by appellants in a civil suit - Civil Court decreed suit in favor of appellants on the ground that proceedings before SDO barred by res judicata - Respondent filed First Appeal before District Court - First Appeal dismissed - Respondent filed Second Appeal before HC - Second Appeal allowed - Whether provisions of Bombay Hereditary Offices Act, 1874 exclude the jurisdiction of Civil Court u/s. 9 of CPC to decide the Watandari rights under the Act? - Held, s. 4 of the Act, specifically puts an embargo on civil courts in exercising its jurisdiction in matters where claims against the government relating to any property appertaining to the office of any hereditary officer appointed or recognized under Hereditary Office Act, 1874, however, s.5 of the Act, an exception to s.4 authorizes civil courts from entertaining suits between private parties for the purpose of establishing any private right, although it may be affected by any entry in any record of revenue survey or settlement or in any village papers - However, the said suit can only be entertained in the districts mentioned in Second Schedule of 1874 Act - Therefore, civil court can adjudicate the issue of Watandari rights of private parties.P /&gt;(B) Practice & Procedure - Judgment and Decree of Civil Court - Binding effect - Whether the Judgment and Decree of Civil Court, holding that respondent was not the adopted son of deceased Watandar would be binding on parties in subsequent suit and other proceedings? - When a person claims on the basis of adoption, such adoption cannot be decided by Collector as the same involves legal status/character of a person which can only be decided by civil court - Held, whether respondent is an adopted son or not is concluded and decided in civil suit - A specific issue had been framed and a finding was recorded though it was a suit for injunction and the findings on this issue confirmed by Appellate Court and by HC in Regular Second Appeal, therefore, such findings of civil courts bound by parties in subsequent suit and proceedings.P /&gt;(C) Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, , s.11 - Res judicata - Whether Judgment and Decree passed in earlier proceedings would operate as res judicata in subsequent proceedings before SDO? - Held, Trial Court decided the issue of whether respondent was an adopted son of deceased Watandar or not - Since the issue of adoption already decided between same parties by a competent civil court, SDO cannot decide that issue again - Therefore, Principles of Res- judicata would apply to the proceedings before SDO.P /&gt;(D) Land & Property - Watandari rights - Whether the order passed in earlier civil suit, holding that plaintiffs are the representatives of Watandars, accepted by HC in Regular Second Appeal, would debar the right of respondent in approaching State Government in re-agitating the matter? - Held, once proceedings of Deputy District Collector is accepted by civil court and a decree was passed, the said decree becomes final and binding on parties - Respondent disentitled to approach State Government nearly after 14 years for re-agitating the matter again.

(E) Land & Property - Watan Abolition Act, 1858, s.5(1) - Watandari rights - Whether SDO was justified in passing the order conferring Watandar rights upon respondent, despite the Judgment and Decree of Civil Court which denied such right to respondent and the order of re-grant passed in favor of the appellants u/s. 5(1) of Act? - Held, Collector can only decide whether a person has got a hereditary interest in an inferior village of Watan under the existing Watan law - Appellants had approached the Collector with a request to re-grant the Watanlands, since they are holders of Watan and pursuant to the request made, Collector passed an order in exercise of his powers u/s. 5(1) of the Act, and if any person was aggrieved by that order, could have filed an appeal within the time limit prescribed - Since respondent not filed any appeal, order of Collector has become final and, therefore, SDO could not have passed any order u/s. 5(1) of the Act re-granting Watandari rights to respondents - Judgments of HC set aside - Appeals allowed.
