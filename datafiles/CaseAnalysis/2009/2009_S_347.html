R.P. Tyagi v State (Govt. Of Nct Of Delhi)..

Supreme Court of India

12 November 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

H.S. Bedi, J.M. Panchal

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 2035; (2009) 17 SCC 445; AIR 2010 SC (Supp) 201; 2010 CRLJ 609; 2010 (1) JCC 367; JT 2009 (15) SC 341; 2010 (1) RCR(Criminal) 610; 2009(14) SCALE 616; [2009] 15 S.C.R. 1147

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302 and 304 Part II - Custodial death - Conviction - Deceased's father filed a criminal complaint in Magistrate's Court alleging that deceased died due to severe beating by appellant - Sessions court convicted appellant u/s. 302 of IPC - On appeal, HC noted that almost all prosecution witnesses including mother, brothers and other close relatives of deceased and co-accused had not supported prosecution and had been declared hostile and complainant had also died before trial concluded - HC modified conviction u/s. 304 Part II of IPC and a sentence of 8 years rigorous imprisonment along with a fine of Rs.2,00,000/- Hence, present appeal - Whether HC's order could be upheld? - Held, appellant presently in jail as per orders of trial court and HC and has undergone about 3 years of sentence and has paid fine of Rs. 2,00,000/- - Appellant was also denied his retrial benefits on account of his conviction in present matter and is now 67 years of age, and is in extremely indigent circumstances - Conviction as recorded by HC confirmed though sentence reduced from 8 years to 5 years and fine increased from Rs. 2,00,000 to Rs, 5,00,000 - Appeals disposed of
