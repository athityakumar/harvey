Kolawana Gram Vikas Kendra v State Of Gujarat & Others

Supreme Court of India

20 October 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

V.S. Sirpurkar, Deepak Verma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1446; (2010) 1 SCC 133; 2010 (3) AWC 2543; JT 2009 (13) SC 581; 2010 (2) MLJ(SC) 133; 2009(13) SCALE 361; [2009] 15 S.C.R. 272

Case Digest

Subject: Education

Keywords: Gujarat Secondary Education Act, 1972

Summary: Education - Gujarat Secondary Education Act, 1972 - Appointment in minority educational institution - Failure to obtain prior approval from Government - Refusal by Education Officer to provide no-objection certificate for the purpose of grant - Appellant filed application before Single Judge - Application dismissed - Appeal before DB - DB dismissed appeal, holding that Government has the power to insist on no-objection certificate for the purpose of grant - Whether condition of obtaining prior approval from the Government to appoint teachers would amount to interference in the internal administration of minority institution - Held, requirement of prior approval is necessitated because it is for the Government to see as to whether posts actually available in the said institution as per the strength of students and whether the candidates, who were sought to be appointed, having the requisite qualifications in terms of the rules and regulations of the Education Department - Therefore, any circular of Government insisting no-objection certificate from competent authority not invalid nor an interference on the internal administration of minority institution - Appeal dismissed.
