Dakshin Haryana Bijli Vitran Nigam & Others v Bachan Singh

Supreme Court of India

30 July 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Dalveer Bhandari, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 926; (2009) 14 SCC 793; AIR 2009 SC 2745; 2009 (3) CLR 110; 2009 (123) FLR 435; JT 2009 (10) SC 352; 2009 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 2153; 2009 (5) Law Herald (P&H) 2897; 2009 (4) LLJ 392; 2009 (4) LLN 132; 2009(10) SCALE 293; [2009] 11 S.C.R. 710; 2009 (5) SLR 476

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Ex Gratia Payment

Summary: Service - Pensionary benefits - Respondents joined the appellant on work-charge capacity on 16-05-1963 till 14-10-1981, when they were regularized - Respondent was a member of the Employees' PF Scheme - Respondent on attaining the age of superannuation, retired from the service on 28-02-2001 - Appellants computed respondent's pensionary benefits by taking into account only the services rendered by him on regular basis and he was denied benefits of the services rendered by him w.e.f. 16-05-1963 to 13-10-1981 on work-charge basis - Appellants had issued instructions on two occasions for the grant of benefit of work-charge service towards pensionary benefits, on 06-08-1993 and 09-08-1994, under which work charge employees were to submit an option within a particular period - Benefit under such instructions was denied to the respondents on ground of non-exercise of such option within the period - Writ petition was preferred before the High Court, which was allowed - Hence present appeal - HC came to the definite conclusion that the appellants had failed to produce any record showing that the instructions were actually got noted in writing from the respondent; further it had observed that in the absence of any material, it can be inferred that the respondent had no knowledge about the options; further observation made that it would be unreasonable to deny pensionary benefits to the respondents despite the said circulars issued by the appellants - Whether findings and judgment of HC, proper and justified? - Held, Yes - 'Pension is reward for long service rendered by the employee and not a bounty' - Impugned judgement upheld - Being without merit - Appeals dismissed.
