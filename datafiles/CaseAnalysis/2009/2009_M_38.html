(1) D. Venkatasubramaniam and Others; (2) Abinesh v M. K. Mohan Krishnamachari and Another

Supreme Court of India

14 September 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

B. SUDARSHAN REDDY, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1121; (2009) 10 SCC 488; 2009 (4) MLJ(Crl) 347; 2009(12) SCALE 483; [2009] 14 S.C.R. 441

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Power Of The Court, Court's Function

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s.482 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss.406 and 420 - Inherent powers of HC u/s.482 - Scope of - Respondent lodged FIR against appellants alleging commission of offences u/ss.406 and 420 of IPC - Even while investigation was in progress respondent filed petition u/s.482 of CrPC - HC directed police to expedite and complete investigation - Hence, present appeal - Whether it is open to HC in exercise of its jurisdiction u/s.482 of CrPC to interfere with statutory power of investigation by police and if such a power is available with Court, what are parameters for its interference? - Power u/s.482 of CrPC can be exercised by HC either suo motu or on an application (i) to secure ends of justice; (ii) to give effect to any order under Code; (iii) to prevent abuse of process of any Court - HC cannot direct investigating agency to investigate case in accord with its views as that would amount to unwarranted interference - In present case, HC, without recording any reason whatsoever, directed police that it is obligatory on their part to record statements from witnesses, arrest, seizure of property and filing of charge sheet - HC interfered with investigation of crime which is within exclusive domain of police - Held, HC in present case, without realizing consequences, issued directions in a casual and mechanical manner without hearing appellants - Impugned order must be held to be an order passed overstepping limits of judicial interference - Impugned order is a nullity and liable to be set aside - Impugned judgment of HC set aside - Appeals allowed.
