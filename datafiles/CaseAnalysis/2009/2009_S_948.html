Vijay Narayan Thatte and Others v State of Maharashtra and Others

Supreme Court of India

18 August 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Markandey Katju, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 985; (2009) 9 SCC 92; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 1952; 2009 (6) ALD(SC) 59; 2010 (1) AWC 121; 2009 (5) Bom.C.R. 549; 2010 (1) CLT(SC) 93; JT 2009 (15) SC 92; 2009 (4) RCR(Civil) 283; 2009(11) SCALE 525; [2009] 14 S.C.R. 891

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, s. 6 - Land acquisition - Notification issued thereunder - Interpretation of statute - Mimansa Principles of Interpretation - Respondent issued a Notification u/s. 6 for land acquisition on 18-6-2003 - HC quashed the said Notification on the ground of limitation specified under the proviso to s. 6 - Respondent issued another Notification u/s. 6 on 30-10-2006 - Appellant filed second writ petition before HC which was rejected - Validity - Held, as per Kalanja maxim of the Mimansa system, proviso to s. 6 of the Act implies a general prohibition against the whole world and not against a particular person - Therefore, proviso to s. 6 is belonging to the class of Nishedha Vidhis to be interpreted comprehensively and as mandatory without exceptions - Since the language of proviso to s. 6 is clear, literal rule of interpretation must be applied to it - Assurance of the appellant counsel in the earlier writ petition of the HC to the extent that the appellant would not raise the objection with regard to the limitation u/s. 6, has no effect - Period of limitation provided in cl. (ii) of proviso to s. 6 of the Act to be followed - Proviso is mandatory in nature and must operate with full rigor - No statement or concession of counsel can override the mandatory statutory provision - Further, earlier judgment of HC in the writ petition was a judgment per incuriam - Therefore, impugned judgment set aside, however respondent was at the liberty to issue further Notifications under the Act - Appeal allowed.
