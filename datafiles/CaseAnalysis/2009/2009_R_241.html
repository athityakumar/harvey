State of West Bengal and Others v Ratnagiri Engineering Private Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

24 February 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Markandey Katju, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 218; (2009) 4 SCC 453; JT 2009 (3) SC 37; 2009(3) SCALE 215; [2009] 2 S.C.R. 982

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Appeals Disposed Of, West Bengal Town and Country (Planning and Development) Act, 1979, West Bengal Estates Acquisition Act, 1953, West Bengal Non-Agricultural Tenancy Act, 1949, West Bengal Land Reforms (Amendment) Act, 1981

Summary: Land & Property - West Bengal Estates Acquisition Act, 1953 - Held, once an order is passed by the State Government under s. 6(3) thereafter the power under the proviso to s. 6(3) the Act can be exercised only if (i) some fraud or misrepresentation was made to the State Government for obtaining the order under s. 6(3) of the 1953 Act, or (ii) there was a genuine and important mistake made by the State Government in passing the order under s. 6(3) of the 1953 Act - Power under the proviso to s. 6(3) cannot be exercised on the ground that after the order of the State Government was passed under the main part of s. 6(3) of the 1953 Act, some subsequent developments have taken place - Once an order under the main part of s. 6(3) of 1953 Act is passed by the State Government, the power under the proviso to s. 6(3) of the 1953 Act cannot be exercised by the State Government by taking into consideration events which occurred after the said order was passed - Appeals disposed of.
