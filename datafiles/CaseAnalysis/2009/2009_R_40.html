Omprakash and Others v Radhacharan and Others

Supreme Court of India

05 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Yogeshwar Dayal

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 597; (2009) 15 SCC 66; 2009 (5) ALD(SC) 1; 2010(1) ALL MR 453; 2009 (76) ALR 818; 2009 (4) AWC 3413; 2009 (3) CalLT(SC) 49; 2009 (2) CTC 875; 2009 (4) JhrCR(SC) 17; 2009 (5) MLJ(SC) 761; 2009 (3) RCR(Civil) 797; 2009 (108) RD 166; 2009(7) SCALE 51; [2009] 7 S.C.R. 1007; 2009 (2) UC 890

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Keywords: General Rules Of Succession, Heirs Of The Father, Heirs Of The Mother, Self- Acquired Property

Summary: Family & Personal - Hindu Succession Act, 1956 - Self-acquired property of a female - Whether s. 15(1) or s. 15(2) thereof would be applicable? - Held, self-acquired property of a female would be her absolute property and not the property which she had inherited from her parents - S. 15 (1) would apply and not the sub-Section (2) - Sentiment or sympathy alone would not be a guiding factor in determining the rights of the parties which are otherwise clear and unambiguous - Appeal dismissed.
