Mallappa Siddappa Alakanur and Others v State of Karnataka

Supreme Court of India

07 July 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

V.S. Sirpurkar, R.M. Lodha

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 852; (2009) 14 SCC 748; AIR 2009 SC 2959; 2009 ALL MR (Cri) 3045; JT 2009 (9) SC 33; 2009 (6) KarLJ 177; 2009(9) SCALE 159; [2009] 10 S.C.R. 196

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Blood Stained Clothes, Graphic Description

Summary: Criminal - IPC, 1860, ss. 302 r/w 149 - Murder - Conviction for - Trial Court acquitted, however, HC reversed the acquittal and convicted the appellants - Legality - HC has found fault with rejection of testimonies of witnesses by Trial Court on immaterial contradictions - PW7 who graphically described the identity of the accused and his dress - Presence of food in the stomach of deceased and late recording of statement of PW7 were found to be insignificant circumstances by the HC - HC discussed the injuries and found that medical evidence proved number of injuries which were not proved by the witness - Further, HC observed that late recording of statement of PW7 justifiable in the circumstance that he had seen the ghastly murder of his friend and usual apathy of police to record statements in the late hours - Presence of PW7 at the spot well explained - Fact of PW7 went along with the deceased and his narration of how attack occurred, remained unshaken in his cross-examination - No evidence nor any suggestion that PW7 was influenced by his father or relatives of the deceased - Even though PW5 turned hostile, his evidence need not be washed out - HC found that any contradictions in the statement might have occurred in the mistake of dictation - No contradictions found by the HC in the evidence of IO - Therefore, HC has rightly reversed that acquittal of Trial Court - No merit - Appeal dismissed.
