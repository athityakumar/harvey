D.D.A. v Rajendra Singh & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

30 July 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, K.G. Balakrishnan, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 924; (2009) 8 SCC 582; AIR 2010 SC 2516; 2009 (161) DLT 330; JT 2009 (10) SC 137; 2009 (6) MLJ(SC) 375; 2009(10) SCALE 273; [2009] 12 S.C.R. 163

Case Digest

Subject: Environment, Wildlife & Animal

Keywords: Appeals Disposed Of, Poverty, Delhi Development Act, 1956

Summary: (A) Environment, Wildlife & Animal - Commonwealth Games Village (CGV) - CGV site complex being constructed on the Yamuna 'riverbed' or 'floodplain', alleged - HC directed to setting up a Committee to enquire the matter - Validity - Held, Pocket III, Phase I area declared as 'Development Area' - Yamuna Committee constituted for development of Pocket III area for public and semi-public facilities - Suggestion/objections from public duly considered - Clearance from Central Water Commission obtained and the plan approved by DDA - Dr. Pachauri appointed for monitoring the CGV constructions - Ecological integrity of river, concept of 'riverbed', 'floodplain' and 'riverzone' were duly considered - Expert bodies like NEERI and CWPRS duly consulted - Only Pocket III reclassified and Master Plan Delhi 2001 suitably amended - SC approved the site plan of Akshardham Temple earlier - CGV is adjacent to Akshardham site - NEERI suggested that area beyond bund could be reclaimed for development and construction in 1999 report - Water recharge pond has not been disturbed by constructions - Construction is not at 'floodplain' - HC's comment on NEERI report, 2008 wherein NEERI approved the constructions work, unjustifiable - Site in question is neither a 'floodplain' nor a 'riverbed' - Therefore, HC's recommendation for appointment of another Committee not sustainable.P /&gt;(B) Environment, Wildlife & Animal - Commonwealth Games Village (CGV) - CGV site complex being constructed on the Yamuna 'riverbed' or 'floodplain', alleged - SC cleared the site of Akshardham Temple earlier in a writ petition - HC observed that the said decision of SC, not applicable to CGV - Validity - Akshardham construction approved after obtaining opinion from Central Water Commission and NEERI - Said sanctioned plan approved by DDA also - Area of Akshardham site form the part of CGV area and both the sites are adjacent to each other - Therefore, HC's comment that the decision of SC in Akshardham site was not applicable to CGV, not justifiable.P /&gt;(C) Environment, Wildlife & Animal - Commonwealth Games Village (CGV) - CGV site complex being constructed on the Yamuna 'riverbed' or 'floodplain', alleged - Delay and laches - Site in question reclassified and declared as 'Development area at way back in 1999 itself - Decision regarding construction of CGV was taken in 2003 - Process of tender of work etc. completed in 2006-2007 - Environmental clearance obtained on 14.12.2006 - Environmental clearance studies were completed on April, 2007 - However, respondents approached HC only in the middle of 2007, hence, the writ petitions before HC to be dismissed on the ground of delay and laches.
