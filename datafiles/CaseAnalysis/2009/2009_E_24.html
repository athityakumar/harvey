Citation Infowares Limited v Equinox Corporation

Supreme Court of India

20 April 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

V.S. Sirpurkar

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1932; (2009) 7 SCC 220; 2009 (3) AWC 2683; 2009(6) SCALE 430; [2009] 6 S.C.R. 737

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Keywords: Foreign Award, Curial Law, Appointment Of Sole Arbitrator, International Commercial Arbitrations

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, s. 11(5) - Applicability of Part I of Act to international commercial arbitration - Appointment of arbitrator - Applicant and respondent entered into an outsourcing agreement signed in India - All three agreements were signed in India and services were being provided and rendered under said agreement by applicant in India - Under last agreement governing law was the law of California, USA - The clause also provided for arbitration in case of disputes - Respondent terminated agreements - Applicant invoked arbitration clause - Parties failed to agree on appointment of sole Arbitrator within time limit prescribed therefore present application for appointment of an Arbitrator was filed - Whether SC would have jurisdiction, in present factual scenario and on backdrop of fact that parties vide clause 10.1 agreed that governing law would be that of California, USA? - Held, appointment can be made by SC only and only if Part I of the Act is applicable to present arbitration proceedings - In Bhatia International Vs. Bulk Trading S.A. 2002 INDLAW SC 265 it was specifically held that unless language of provisions of Part I are excluded by agreement between parties either expressly or by implication, Part I of the Act including s. 11 of the Act would be applicable even where international commercial agreements are governed by clause of another country - In present case, substantive law of contract governing the agreement is specifically agreed upon as Californian law - However, there is no agreement in respect of law governing procedure of arbitration - One of contracting parties is Indian party - Obligations under contract were to be completed in India - It cannot be said that there was any implied exclusion of provisions of Part I - Followed Bhatia International, Venture Global Engineering Vs. Satyam Computer Services Ltd. 2008 INDLAW SC 61, and Indtel Technical Services Private Ltd. Vs. W.S. Atkins Rail Limited 2008 INDLAW SC 1363 - Arbitrator appointed to arbitrate upon disputes - However, law governing contract would be Californian Law - Application allowed.
