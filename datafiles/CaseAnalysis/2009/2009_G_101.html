Thahira Haris Etc v Government Of Karnataka & Others

Supreme Court of India

15 April 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Dalveer Bhandari, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 437; (2009) 11 SCC 438; AIR 2009 SC 2184; 2009 ALL MR (Cri) 3451; 2009 CRLJ 2451; 2009 (2) RCR(Criminal) 723; 2009(5) SCALE 388; [2009] 5 S.C.R. 941

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Appeals Disposed Of, Non-Supply, Past Activities

Summary: Criminal - Preventive detention - Conservation of Foreign Exchange and Prevention of Smuggling Activities Act, 1975, ss. 3(3) and 22(5) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 22(5) - Order of detention - Validity thereof - Impact of non-supply of relied upon and relevant documents on the detention order - Detenu detained for abetting in smuggling of red sanders - Master mind of such smuggling activities was one Anil Kumar who was also detained - (1) Whether it was imperative for the detaining authority to supply detention order of AK? - Admitted position, that detention order of AK was not supplied to the detenu, instead confirmation order u/s. 8 of COFEPOSA was supplied - As per detaining authority, AK was the master mind in the entire operation and allegation against the detenu is of abetting him in smuggling - Finding, order of detention passed against AK is of great significance and same would weigh heavily with the detaining authority while passing the detention order - Thus detention order of AK was relied upon document - Precedents quoted and deliberated upon - Art. 22(5) r/w 3(3) of COFEPOSA construed - Held, detenu prevented from making effective representation, thus constitutional right u/art. 22(5), violated - Order of detention, invalid - Directions issued - Appeals disposed of.
