Secretary To The Government of Haryana and Others v Vidya Sagar

Supreme Court of India

16 July 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Deepak Verma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 888; (2009) 14 SCC 652; AIR 2010 SC 196; JT 2009 (9) SC 402; 2009 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 1998; 2009(9) SCALE 588; [2009] 10 S.C.R. 584; 2009 (5) SLR 435

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Punjab Services (Medical Attendance) Rules, 1940

Summary: Service - Punjab Services (Medical Attendance) Rules 1940 - Pensioners - Medical reimbursement - Respondent underwent a bye-pass surgery - Submitted bill towards medical expenses borne by him for reimbursement - State of Haryana relying on circular dated 30.11.1993 sanctioned payment for a sum of 75% only - Writ petition filed - HC was of opinion that heart disease having been treated to be one of chronic ailments in terms of circular letter dated 11.8.1992, respondent was entitled to reimbursement of medical bill in its entirety - Whether HC was right in holding respondent was entitled to reimbursement of entire expenses incurred by him in his treatment? - By Circular letter dated 11.8.1992 reimbursement of all expenses incurred on treatment of chronic diseases outdoor as well indoor was - State can change its policy decision from time to time - Once State in its magnanimity adopted a decision that medical bills in their entirety in connection with heart diseases shall be reimbursed, authorities of State being bound thereby were obligated to comply therewith - Held, no reason to differ with views of HC - Although circular dated 28.5.2003 does not expressly supersede circular letter dated 30.11.1993, but latter having been issued subsequent to former and in relation to a particular category of disease, namely, chronic diseases, shall prevail over earlier general circular letter - Impugned judgment must be sustained - Appeal dismissed.
