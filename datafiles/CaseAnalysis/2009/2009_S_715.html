Raj Kumar Khurana v State of (Nct of Delhi) and Another

Supreme Court of India

05 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 587; (2009) 6 SCC 72; (2009) 2 SCC (Cr) 936; 2009 ALL MR (Cri) 1881; 2009 (3) BC 664; 2009 (5) Bom.C.R. 315; 2009 (3) CompLJ 11; 2009 CRLJ 3454; 2009 (3) CTC 217; 2010 (1) G.L.R. 798; 2009 (3) JCC (NI) 181; JT 2009 (7) SC 351; 2009 (2) KLT 632; 2009 (4) MPLJ 151; 2009 (3) RCR(Criminal) 15; 2009(7) SCALE 55; [2009] 7 S.C.R. 434; 2009 (2) UC 894

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, s. 138 - Whether return of a cheque by the bank on the ground that it was reported lost by the drawer would attract the penal provisions contained in s. 138? - Held, No - Provision of s. 138 perused - Legal fiction created therein, has to be given full effect to but has its own limitations, thus it cannot be taken recourse to for any purpose other than the one mentioned in the statute itself - A penal provision created by reason of a legal fiction must receive strict construction - Legal fiction attracted when cheque is returned by the bank unpaid, firstly because of the amount of money standing to the credit of that account is insufficient to honour the cheque, secondly, it exceeds the amount arranged to be paid from that account by an agreement made with that bank - Appeal allowed.
