Smt. Anjali Kapoor v Rajiv Baijal

Supreme Court of India

17 April 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Dattu, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 479; (2009) 7 SCC 322; AIR 2009 SC 2821; 2009 (3) AWC 2679; 2009 (5) CTC 283; 2009 (2) JhrCR(SC) 124; 2009 (5) MLJ(SC) 987; 2010 (1) MPLJ 60; 2009 (3) RCR(Civil) 903; 2009 (108) RD 138; 2009(6) SCALE 597; [2009] 6 S.C.R. 560

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Keywords: Grandmother, Welfare Of Minor, Step Mother

Summary: Family & Personal - Guardian and Wards Act, 1890 - Custody of child - Natural guardian vis-a-vis foster guardian - Wife for her delivery was in her mother's home - Death of wife due to pre-mature delivery - Baby in incubator for 45 days - Appellant, mother of wife - After discharge from hospital infant brought to appellant's residence - Respondent filed an application under the Act claiming himself to be the natural guardian, so the custody of minor child - Family Court decided in favour of respondent/father - High Court upheld decision of Family Court - Under existent circumstances of the case, whether it would be proper to direct the appellant to hand over the custody of minor child to the respondent/father, or who is fit and proper to be the guardian of minor child? - Under the Act father is the guardian of the minor child until he is found unfit to be the guardian of the minor female child - In deciding such questions, the welfare of the minor child is the paramount consideration and such a question cannot be decided merely based upon the rights of the parties under the law - Decisions on the point referred and deliberated upon - Facts of case noted - Madras HC's decision in 'Muthuswami Moopanar, AIR 1935 Madras 195'; 'if a minor has for many years from a tender age lived with grand parents or near relatives and has been well cared for and during that time the minor's father has shown a lack of interest in the minor, these are circumstances of very great importance, having bearing upon the question of the interest and welfare of the minor and on the bonafide of the petition by the father for their custody' - Since the birth, child was with appellant, she was the one who was taking care of child - Photographs produced which were undisputed, clearly showing amount of care, affection and the love esp. in circumstance that she lost her daughter in tragic circumstances - Studies of child in reputed school - Proper care and attention in upbringing of the child founded by Court - Appellant further found to be financially sound as she runs her own independent business - Indebtedness of respondent, his meager income, his contracting second marriage and having a child from such marriage and his non-appearance before the Court personally or through his counsel on notice being issued, noted by Court - Rights of natural guardians to have custody of the child is not absolute and Courts are expected to give paramount consideration to the welfare of the minor child - Held, custody of child to be remained with appellant till she attains majority - Impugned judgment set aside - Appeal allowed.
