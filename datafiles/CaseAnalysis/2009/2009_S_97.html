Smruti Pahariya v Sanjay Pahariya

Supreme Court of India

11 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Ganguly, K.G. Balakrishnan, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1022; (2009) 13 SCC 338; AIR 2009 SC 2840; 2009 (76) ALR 145; 2009 (4) AWC 3467; 2010 (1) Bom.C.R. 888; 2009 (3) G.L.R. 2583; 2009 (3) JhrCR(SC) 49; 2009 (5) MLJ(SC) 1203; 2009 (155) PLR 473; 2009 (2) RCR(Civil) 943; 2009 (108) RD 586; 2009(7) SCALE 331; [2009] 8 S.C.R. 631; 2010 (1) ShimLC 224; 2009 (2) UC 898

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Keywords: Divorce and Matrimonial Causes Amendment Act, 1920

Summary: Family & Personal - Hindu Marriage Act, 1955, s. 13B(2) - Family Courts Act, 1984 - Divorce by mutual consent - Joint petition was filed before the Family Court(FC) and was registered on 19-05-2007 - Statutory period of six months expired on or about 19-11-2007; parties were counseled there between on two occasions but both the days parties were absent and no counseling took place - Both the parties had same advocate; husband was duly informed about the date of hearing i.e. 19-11-2007, as per the affidavit filed by wife, but husband had sent an SMS expressing his inability to come to the said date; next date of hearing was fixed on 01-12-2007; wife on 19-11-2007 had applied for sending summon to the husband directing him to be present in the Family Court; which was send by courier and the courier returned with the remark 'not accepting'; on this FC passed an order on 01-12-2007 on perusal of the service report; in that order it gave a finding that service was not proper one and allowed the wife to serve the notice by pasting it on the address available in record; petition was returnable on 04-12-2007; husband was still absent; FC adjourned the matter to 10-12-2007, but on 05-12-2007 the wife filed a petition before FC with a prayer that the hearing of the matter may be pre-poned and be taken up on the very same day i.e. 05-12-2007; and on such day though matter was not on the board, it was taken on the board by the FC and the decree was passed ex-parte on that date itself - Thus FC pre-poned the hearing on wife's application and in the absence of the husband; pre-ponement was admittedly done ex-parte - Four issues - (A) Whether impugned decree of divorce passed by the FC on 05-12-2007 is vitiated by procedural irregularity? - Held, Yes, even if it is held that it was correct in presuming the continuing consent of the husband - Facts noted and record of FC perused - Service return was before the FC on 01-12-2007 and looking to it FC found that service was not a proper one and it was also not satisfied with the endorsement of the courier but still it directed for substituted service u/O 5 R 20 of Civil Procedure Code; such direction held not to be proper one; FC should have directed another service in the normal manner as it was not satisfied or it has no satisfaction of its own as to that husband was evading the service - Court strongly disapproved the manner in which proceeding was conducted by FC; Court's proceeding must have a sanctity and fairness; it cannot be conducted for the convenience of one party alone; it could not have pre-poned the matter on an exparte prayer and grant the decree of divorce on that day itself by treating the matter on the board in the absence of the husband - Held further, a flagrant abuse of the judicial process and on this ground alone, decree D/-05-12-2007 has to be set aside, Court stressed - (B) Whether by conducting the proceeding, in the manner it did, the FC acted contrary to the avowed object of the Family Courts Act, 1984? - Held, Yes - FC has not acted in a manner which is required of it having regard to the jurisdiction vested on it under the FC Act - Genesis of FC Act noted and avowed object and the scheme examined - FC Act was enacted for adopting a human approach to the settlement of family disputes and achieving socially desirable results - 59th report on HM Act of Law Commission noted - '..the court ought to adopt a human approach.....an approach radically different from that adopted in ordinary civil proceedings, ..' - Statutory obligation u/s. 13B(2) of HM Act of hearing the parties, not fulfilled by FC; FC has not at all shown a human and a radically different approach which it is expected to have while dealing with s. 13B case, Court noted - Court deliberated upon 'fault' based divorce laws in the past and the present laws wherein s. 13B is in place, from the international perspective also - (C) Whether from the absence of the husband before the FC on 19-11-2007, 01-12-2007 and 04-12-2007 it can be inferred that his consent for grant of divorce on a petition on mutual consent subsists, even though he has not withdrawn the petition for divorce on mutual consent? - Held, No - Judgement of 'Smt. Sureshta Devi Vs. Om Prakash - (1991) 2 SCC 25' relied - Wording of s. 13B(2) recognizes the possibility of withdrawing the petition filed on consent during the time when such petition has to be kept pending - '..if there is no mutual consent at the time of the enquiry, the court gets no jurisdiction to make a decree for divorce. If the view is otherwise, the court could make an enquiry and pass a divorce decree even at the instance of one of the parties and against the consent of the other. Such a decree cannot be regarded as decree by mutual consent.' - Mutual consent should continue till the divorce decree is passed - 'The consent must continue to decree nisi and must be valid subsisting consent when the case is heard' - (D) Whether on a proper construction of s. 13B(2) of HM Act, which speaks of 'the motion of both the parties', this Court can hold that the FC can dissolve a marriage and grant a decree in the absence of one of the parties and without actually ascertaining the consent of that party who filed the petition for divorce on mutual consent jointly with the other party? - Held, No - It is only on the continued mutual consent of the parties that decree for divorce u/s. 13B of HM Act can be passed by the Court; if petition for divorce is not formally withdrawn and is kept pending then on the date when the Court grants the decree, the Court has a statutory obligation to hear the parties to ascertain their consent - Court viewed that it is only the mutual consent of the parties which gives the Court the jurisdiction to pass the decree for divorce u/s. 13B; thus cases u/s. 13B, mutual consent of the parties is a jurisdictional fact - Impugned judgment of HC upheld - Directions issued to FC - Appeal disposed of.
