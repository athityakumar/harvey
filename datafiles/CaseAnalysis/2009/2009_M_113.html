N. Radhakrishnan v M/S. Maestro Engineers and Ors.

Supreme Court of India

22 October 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Tarun Chatterjee, V.S. Sirpurkar

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1452; (2010) 1 SCC 72; AIR 2010 SC (Supp) 307; 2009 (6) ALD(SC) 130; 2009 (77) ALR 490; 2010 (2) AWC 1581; 2010 (1) CompLJ 154; 2010 (2) CTC 327; JT 2009 (13) SC 491; 2010 (1) MLJ(SC) 401; 2010 (3) RCR(Civil) 445; 2009(13) SCALE 403; [2009] 15 S.C.R. 371

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, s. 8 and 8(2) - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 7, r. 1 - Appointment of Arbitrator - Appellant entered into a partnership with respondents - Differences arose - Respondents filed a suit under O. 7, r. 1 of CPC - Appellant's filed application u/s. 8 of Act to refer matter to arbitrator, which was rejected - Appeal filed thereagainst dismissed - Hence, present appeal - (A) Whether Arbitrator was competent to deal with dispute raised by parties? - Held, HC in its impugned judgment rightly held that since case relates to allegations of fraud and serious malpractices in account books and manipulate finances of partnership firm, on part of respondents, such a situation can only be settled in court through furtherance of detailed evidence by either parties and such a situation cannot be properly gone into by Arbitrator - HC justified in dismissing petition of appellant to refer matter to an Arbitrator - (B) Non-compliance of s. 8(2) of Act - Held, since original deed was not filed within requirement of s. 8(2) of Act, it must be held that mandatory requirement under Act had not been complied with - Appeal dismissed.
