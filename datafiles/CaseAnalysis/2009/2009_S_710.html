Abuthagir And Ors. v State Rep. By Inspector Of Police, Madurai

Supreme Court of India

08 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 765; (2009) 17 SCC 208; AIR 2009 SC 2797; 2009 CRLJ 3987; JT 2009 (13) SC 25; 2009 (3) MLJ(Crl) 1016; 2010 (5) RCR(Criminal) 137; 2009(7) SCALE 585; [2009] 8 S.C.R. 432

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Co- Accused

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302 r/w 34, 120B and 148 - Conviction by Trial Court - Confirmation by High Court - Accused persons five in numbers - Certain connected incidences - Main connected incidence occurred in the Madurai Central Jail when a TADA accused was to be body searched and to show identification marks to which he denied on religious grounds - Deceased was Assistant Jailor who had tried to remove the shirts - Complaints were filed; agitation resorted to by members of association which belonged to such accused and a threatening was given vide certain letter which was received by PW5/Superintendent - Main incidence thereafter occurred on 29-08-1997 at around 3 pm near the central jail when deceased was attacked by assailants with 'aruval', a sharp edged weapon - No immediate clue - Appellant was arrested in another crime case and during his interrogation disclosures were made revealing names of other accused persons, who were than arrested and from further interrogation recoveries were made - Photographs of accused persons were published; on such publication two prime eye witnesses PW3 and PW4 gave their statements to police - Precedents referred on acts of terrorism - Defence sought to weaken prosecution case on ground that first investigation suspected four accused persons whereas second investigation came up with five different accused persons with no material against them except the admissions made before the eye witnesses - Court observed that prosecution case has to be judged as a whole having regard to the totality of the evidence; integrated approach - Contention was raised as to delayed examination of PWs by defence; on it Court noted that delay of examination of PWs by the police during the course of investigation ipso facto may not be a ground to create a doubt regarding the veracity of the prosecution's case; it cannot be laid down as a rule of universal application that if there is any delay in examination of a particular witness the prosecution version becomes suspect - Arrest was made after 8 months; and till then the statements of PWs 3 and 4 were not recorded u/s. 161 of CrPC; and after arrest because photos were published, PWs 3 and 4 came to PS on their own accord and gave statements - Testimonies of PWs 3 and 4 found to be disinterested and independent - Court appreciated the courage of PWs 3 and 4 after observing that they were from lowest status of the society, one was mason and other was a petty seller of sarees, who had taken courage to stand up, picked and identified the accused persons - As murder was committed in broad day light, no infirmity found in the identification - Court further deliberated upon the concept of 'conspiracy' - Direct evidence to prove conspiracy is rarely available; thus circumstances proved before, during and after the occurrence have to be considered to decide about the complicity of the accused - Confession u/s. 27 Evidence Act; discoveries and recoveries in instant case noted, one was Lodge record, other was handwritings of first and third appellant in the registers and last was impersonation as Kumer and David by the said appellants; evidences of PWs 10, 19 and 22 clearly proved said recoveries and discoveries; investigator was able to locate the STD booth from where the accused talked with others - Impugned judgement upheld - Appeal dismissed.
