M. R. Engineers and Contractors Private Limited v Som Datt Builders Limited

Supreme Court of India

07 July 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, J.M. Panchal

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 870; (2009) 7 SCC 696; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 1786; 2009 (3) AWC 2810; JT 2009 (9) SC 374; 2009(9) SCALE 298; [2009] 10 S.C.R. 373

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Keywords: Insurance Contract

Summary: Arbitration & Adr - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, s. 7(5) - Arbitration clause - Reference to main contract by sub-contract - Whether provision for arbitration contained in contract between principal employer and contractor, was incorporated by reference in the sub-contract between contractor and sub-contractor? - Held, intention of parties was not to incorporate main contract in entirety in sub-contract - Intention would only to adopt parts relating to execution of work - In the absence of specific indication of applicability of arbitration clause, no intention could be inferred - Further, entire arbitration agreement in the main contract was tailor-made to meet the requirements of contract between employer and principal contractor, not sub-contractor - Such agreement was wholly inapt and inapplicable in the context of dispute between the contractor and sub-contractor - Thus, arbitration clause in the main contract would not apply to the disputes arising with reference to sub-contract - Appeal dismissed.
