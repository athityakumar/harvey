State of Punjab v Hardial Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 713; (2009) 15 SCC 106; AIR 2010 SC (Supp) 838; 2009 (3) JCC 1839; JT 2009 (14) SC 90; 2010 (5) RCR(Criminal) 12; 2009(8) SCALE 18; [2009] 8 S.C.R. 559

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 482 - Inherent power of High Court - Order thereunder - Propriety thereof - Offences u/ss. 364, 324, 323, 149 and 148 of IPC and 3(2)(v) of Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes (Prevention of Atrocities) Act, 1989 - Stand was taken that as per r. 7 of Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes (Prevention of Atrocities) Rules, 1995, investigation had to be undertaken by an officer not below the rank of DSP specially appointed by State Govt. - As to investigating authority HC gave negative finding and held that charges under IPC and SCST Act were to be dropped after giving further observation, HC went on to give the finding that it cannot be said that there was no prima facie case against the petitioners to frame charges - Contention was raised that order was confusing - Contention upheld - The only question was 'whether investigation done by the police officer specifically authorised to do so in terms of r. 7 of Rules is illegal qua offences not relatable to any provision under SCST Act - Controversy settled in 'State of M.P. v. Chunnilal @ Chunni Singh, Criminal Appeal no. 943 of 2003' - Held, only investigation qua the offence under SCST Act is vulnerable and not those relatable to IPC - Court said that it is open to the State to authorise any person as per Rules to investigate into offence under SCST Act - Appeal partly allowed.
