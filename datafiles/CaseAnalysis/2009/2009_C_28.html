Capt. S.D. Kathuria (Retd.) v (1) N. Ravi and Others; (2) Vikram Philips and

Supreme Court of India

06 March 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

D.K. Jain, R.M. Lodha

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1616; (2010) 13 SCC 771; 2010 (4) RCR(Criminal) 619

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 482 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 107, 120B, 499, 500 - Defamatory Publication - Dropping of Charges - Quashing of Proceedings - Sustainability - Appellant and his son filed criminal complaint against respondents u/ss. 499 and 500 IPC for publishing defamatory statement against appellant and his son - During pendency of said complaint, son of appellant committed suicide - Subsequently, at request of appellant, proceedings against respondent-accused no. 6 were dropped - Trial Court took cognizance of offence and directed issue of summons to other 5 respondents - However, respondents- accused nos. 1 to 4 filed two petitions u/s. 482 CrPC for quashing of complaint against them - HC quashed order of Trial Court, consequently, complaint against said respondents had been quashed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether dropping charges against respondents and quashing of proceedings against respondents was sustainable - Held, as per order sheet of Trial Court, proceedings against respondent-accused no.5 were not dropped - It was factual error of HC that since proceedings against principal tort teasers viz. accused nos. 5 and 6 had been dropped, whole genesis of complaint vanished, hence, complaint against accused nos. 1 to 9 for offence u/ss. 499/500 IPC could not continue - Dropping of proceedings against accused no. 5, who was alleged to have got news item published, had influenced Court to great extent, resulting in miscarriage of justice was erroneous - Hence, impugned orders were set aside and both matters were remitted back to HC for fresh consideration in accordance with law - Appeal disposed of.
