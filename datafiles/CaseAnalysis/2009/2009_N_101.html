Redaul Hussain Khan v National Investigation Agency

Supreme Court of India

19 November 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 2062; (2010) 1 SCC 521; AIR 2010 SC (Supp) 480; 2010 ALL MR (Cri) 1018; 2010 CRLJ 1413; 2010 (1) JCC 152; JT 2009 (15) SC 224; 2009(14) SCALE 105; [2009] 15 S.C.R. 1038

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: National Investigation Agency Act, 2008

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 167 - Unlawful Activities (Prevention) Act, 1967, s. 43D(2)(b) - Jurisdiction - Petitioner was arrested for sending Rs.1 crore to an extremist organization, to enable it to purchase arms and ammunitions - Magistrate rejected the bail application filed by petitioner - Thereafter, petitioner filed a bail application before the HC and while the same was pending, the Government of India issued a notification declaring said extremist organisation along with its factions, wings and front organizations to be an unlawful association - Single Judge dismissed petitioner's bail application on ground of jurisdiction - Sessions Judge (Special Court) extended the period for completion of investigation by a further period of 60 days in terms of s. 43D(2)(b) of the 1967 Act - Appeal against said order was dismissed - Hence, present special leave petition - Held, as grant of statutory bail rejected since, since, Sessions Judge (Special Court) had the jurisdiction to extend the time for completion of the investigation - Appeal dismissed.
