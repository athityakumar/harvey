Haryana Vidyut, Parsaran Nigam Limited and Another v Gulshan Lal and Others

Supreme Court of India

06 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 660; (2009) 13 SCC 354; 2009 (5) ALD(SC) 52; 2009 (76) ALR 776; 2009 (4) AWC 3416; JT 2009 (13) SC 567; 2009 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 3215; 2009 (4) RCR(Civil) 880; 2009(7) SCALE 543; [2009] 8 S.C.R. 937; 2009 (7) SLR 185

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Service

Summary: Civil Procedure - Service - Civil Procedure Code, 1903, s. 47 - Execution of decree - Objection thereto - Interpretation of judgement of Trial Court - Respondents were employees of a Thermal Power Plant at Faridabad under appellant/ Board - Suit filed - Relief was for declaring plaintiffs' entitlement to receive higher pay scale alongwith interest as is given to their co-employees in another Thermal plant on principle of equal pay for equal work and further decree of mandatory injunction was prayed for - Issues were framed by Trial Court and thereafter suit was decreed in favour of plaintiff/respondents; suit was decreed granting relief in terms of benefit as ordered by the High Court in another case which was said to be similarly situated, and decree order was made subject to the decision of that case which was pending disposal in second appeal - Execution petition filed - Objection thereto, contending that a mere declaratory relief was passed in favour of decree holder thus plaintiffs were not entitled to the arrears of pay - Objection dismissed - Revision thereagainst before High Court - Revision again dismissed - Present appeal - Notable fact, decree attained finality as appeal and revision preferred thereagainst was dismissed - Only question remained to be answered is whether having regard to the nature of the decree passed, is it executable? - Held, Yes - Decree should ordinarily be confined to the prayer made in the plaint; and as noted prayer was not only for declaration but also for mandatory injunction and interest - In clear terms Trial Court held that no interest shall be payable thereupon; and on this Court drew inference that the denial of interest leads to the conclusion that the Court was conscious of the fact that not only plaintiffs were entitled to a declaration but also to a mandatory injunction - Judgment must be read as a whole; and for it issues framed holds great significance; Court found that both the issues framed by Trial Court had correlation with the reliefs claimed for - Scope of power of Executing Court in dealing with application u/s. 47 of CPC, looked into; it is incumbent upon objector to show that the decree is ex-facie nullity; and for that purpose executing Court cannot go behind the decree - Court further observed that 'we are not oblivious of the fact that the respondents legally would not have been entitled to the reliefs prayed for..., but as a decree has been passed, we do not intend to go behind the same' - Appeal dismissed.
