Commissioner Of Central Excise, Indore v Grasim Industries Limited

Supreme Court of India

30 July 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

D.K. Jain, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1218; 2009 (241) E.L.T. 321; JT 2009 (13) SC 186; 2009(11) SCALE 401; [2009] 12 S.C.R. 204

Case Digest

Subject: Excise

Keywords: Levy Of Duty, Normal Price, Place Of Removal, Valuation Of Goods

Summary: Excise - Central Excise Act, 1944, ss. 4, 4(1)(a), 3 - Finance Act, 2000, s. 94 - Assessable value (Valuation) - Assessees collected certain amounts from their customers under different heads and failed to show said amounts in their sale invoices - Revenue alleged that said amounts to be added to price of goods for determining assessable value of impugned goods u/s. 4 of Act - On appeal, Tribunal held that said charges recovered by assessees are not to be added to price of goods etc., for purpose of determination of assessable value u/s. 4 of Act - Whether such charges recovered by assessees from buyers are to be taken into consideration for determination of value of excisable goods in terms of s. 4 of Act? - Held, new s. 4, substituted w.e.f. 1-7-2000, came up for consideration before a three-Judge Bench of SC in Commissioner of Central Excise v. Acer Ltd. 2004 INDLAW SC 811 in which they held that s. 4 is a machinery provision and that said machinery provision as well as definition of 'Transaction Value' contained therein would be subject to charging provision of s.3 - Present Bench is of the opinion that interpretation of ss. 3 and 4 of Act after substitution of s. 4 is not in conformity with scheme of Act - Hence, matter referred to a Larger Bench.
