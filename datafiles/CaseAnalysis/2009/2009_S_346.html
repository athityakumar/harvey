Chitresh Kumar Chopra v State (Government of Nct of Delhi)

Supreme Court of India

10 August 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

D.K. Jain, H.L. Dattu

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1078; (2009) 16 SCC 605; AIR 2010 SC 1446; 2010 ALL MR (Cri) 1331; 2009 (162) DLT 257; 2009 (4) JCC 2507; JT 2009 (10) SC 698; 2010 (1) MLJ(Crl) 143; 2009(11) SCALE 24; [2009] 13 S.C.R. 230

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Probable Consequence, Last Opportunity

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s.401 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 306 r/w s. 34, s. 107 - Criminal proceedings - Quashing of - Sessions Court charge sheeted appellant for the offence of abetment to commit suicide on deceased - Appellant sought quashing of criminal proceedings - Appellants' revision dismissed by HC - Legality - Held, deceased has made implicating statements against appellants in his suicide note - Statements recorded by police showed that deceased was put under tremendous pressure to do something which he was not willing to do, hence s.107 of IPC attracted - Trial Court was correct in drawing inference that appellant had 'instigated' deceased to commit suicide - Sufficient ground for presuming that appellant has committed an offence punishable u/s.306 r/w s.34 - Since power of HC u/s. 401 of CrPC being limited, HC was justified in dismissing Revision Petition of appellant - No merit - Appeal dismissed.
