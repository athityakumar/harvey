Parminder Kaur v State Of U.P. & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

26 October 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

V.S. Sirpurkar, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1455; (2010) 1 SCC 322; AIR 2010 SC 840; 2010 ALL MR (Cri) 332; 2010 CRLJ 895; JT 2009 (13) SC 507; 2010 (1) MLJ(Crl) 840; 2009(13) SCALE 424; [2009] 15 S.C.R. 430

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Revenue Record

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 482 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 420, 467, 468 and 471 - Quashing of prosecution - HC dismissed application filed by appellant u/s. 482 CrPC for quashing proceedings arising out of charge sheet u/ss. 420/467/468/471 of IPC - Present appeal - Appellant is 74 years old lady - Civil litigations are pending between parties - Held, there is total absence of bona fides on part of respondent no.2 to file FIR - No basis for allegation against appellant - This is a case for a malicious and vengeanceful prosecution which has no base - It is, therefore, well covered under Guidelines 1 and 7 laid down by SC in judgment of State of Haryana vs. Bhajan Lal (1992) Supp. 1 SCC 335 - Impugned judgment set aside and prosecution Case quashed - Appeal allowed.
