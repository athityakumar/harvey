Jaswant and Another v State of Rajasthan

Supreme Court of India

13 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 820; 2009(8) SCALE 275; [2009] 8 S.C.R. 701; 2009 (2) UC 913

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Police Record, Power Of The Court, Specially Empowered

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 190(1)(a)(b)(c), 193, 209, 319 and 173(2)(8) - Whether a trial of a sessions case could have commenced and completed although no cognizance of it could have been taken against the appellants? - Held, No - Appellants were two among five accused persons and were named in FIR for commission of offences inter alia u/ss. 302, 326, 323 Indian Penal Code; subsequent submission of charge sheet wherein appellants were shown as absconding - Admitted position investigation against them was not completed; cognizance of offence was however taken; case was also committed to the Court of Sessions in terms of s. 209 of CrPC vide its respective order - No cognizance was taken as against the appellants pursuant to or in furtherance of the charge sheet submitted by the investigation officer but relying on or on the basis of the order of committal, charges were framed against all the five accused persons including appellants - Position was, neither any order taking cognizance was passed against the appellants nor their names figured in the order committing the case to the Court of Sessions - Provisions perused, contentions heard and purported special circumstances given by Sessions Judge to arrive at the conclusion that the defect, if any, was merely a technical one, noted - (A) Whether in the peculiar facts and circumstances of the case, the trial of the appellant is wholly illegal or merely irregular? - Held, illegal - 'Cognizance is taken of an offence and not against the offender' - S. 193 of CrPC bars a Court of Sessions to take cognizance as a Court of original jurisdiction although a Court of Magistrate has that power; cl. (b) of s. 190 attracted in instant case; when a police report is filed, it is only the Magistrate concerned who is empowered to take cognizance of an offence; a police report cannot be said to have been filed before a competent Court when investigation in respect of some of the accused although named in FIR remain pending; Sub/s. (2) of s. 173 of CrPC subject to the provisions of sub/s. (8) thereof envisages that a final report can be filed only when an investigation is completed and not prior thereto - Only recourse available to Sessions Judge is s. 319 and for the purpose of attracting the said provision, wherein extraordinary power is conferred upon a Sessions Court, orders summoning additional accused could have been passed only on the basis of some evidences brought before the Court during the trial; thus such a power can be exercised only when such a case is made out; further proper application of mind on the part of Sessions Judge is imperative in character - Sessions Judge had apparently issued a direction in instant case that the charge sheet be filed before the competent Court; direction held to be illegal - Court noted that a Court of Sessions, apart from the legal hurdle that it cannot take cognizance of an offence in exercise of its original jurisdiction, even otherwise was not empowered to direct the investigating officer to submit a charge sheet - Power of an IO to complete the investigation is a statutory power; Magistrate may have a duty that a fair investigation is conducted, but even then, the Magistrate would not have any jurisdiction to direct the IO to file a charge sheet, 'Emperor v. Nazir Ahmed, AIR 1945 PC 18' - So long as investigation is not completed or evidences are not brought on record, Sessions Judge could not have exercised his jurisdiction either u/s. 193 or s. 319 CrPC - 'Kishun Singh v. State of Bihar (1993) 2 SCC 16' - Impugned order set aside - Directions issued to IO - Observations made - Appeal allowed.
