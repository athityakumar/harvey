Indian Hume Pipe Company Limited v State Of Rajasthan

Supreme Court of India

19 October 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Deepak Verma, V.S. Sirpurkar

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1219; (2009) 10 SCC 187; 2010(1) ALL MR 422; 2009 (4) AWC 3998; 2010 (1) CompLJ 88; JT 2009 (13) SC 210; 2009 (8) MLJ(SC) 948; 2010 (1) RCR(Civil) 620; 2009(13) SCALE 316; [2009] 15 S.C.R. 254

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration Act, 1940 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, ss. 34, 29 - Interest - Interest for pre-reference, pendente lite interest and future interest awarded by arbitrator - Objections filed before District Court by respondent - Pendente lite and future interest disallowed by District Court - Appeal before HC filed by appellant - HC dismissed appeal by holding that though arbitrators have got the power to grant interest at all the stages of proceedings but it is not mandatory for the arbitrators to have awarded the same - Whether it is mandatory on the part of arbitrator to award interest - Held, if the amount has been withheld wrongly and without any justification then the aggrieved party would be fully justified in claiming interest u/ss. 34 and 29 of CPC - Power to award interest at all stages vests with arbitrators - Arbitrators bound to make award in accordance with law and if there is no embargo or legal hurdle in awarding interest for pre-reference, pendente lite and for future, therefore, denying interest to appellant unjustifiable - Appeal allowed.
