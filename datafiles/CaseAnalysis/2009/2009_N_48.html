Revajeetu Builders And Developers v Narayanaswamy And Sons And Others

Supreme Court of India

09 October 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Dalveer Bhandari, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1213; (2009) 10 SCC 84; 2009 (77) ALR 654; 2009 (4) AWC 4002; JT 2009 (13) SC 366; 2010 (1) KarLJ 1; 2009 (8) MLJ(SC) 907; 2010 (1) RCR(Civil) 27; 2009 (2) RCR(Rent) 568; 2010 (109) RD 12; 2010 (1) RLW 322; 2009(13) SCALE 241; [2009] 15 S.C.R. 103

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Keywords: Filing Of Applications

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O.6 r.17 - Suit for recovery of money - Amendment of plaint - Appellant plaintiffs sought amendment of plaint - Amendment allowed by Trial Court - Respondent defendants filed writ petition before HC - Writ petition allowed by HC on grounds that appellant trying to introduce a new case by amendment - Whether application for amendment deserves to be allowed - Guidelines for deciding applications for amendment laid down - Held, amendment application allowed by Trial Court without following the essential conditions for amendment of plaint, hence the same deserves to be dismissed - Cost imposed on appellants on the reason that respondents were compelled to oppose amendment application before different Courts - No merit - Appeal dismissed.
