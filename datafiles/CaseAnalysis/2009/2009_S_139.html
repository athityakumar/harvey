Satish @ Dhanna v State of Madhya Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

17 April 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 512; (2009) 14 SCC 187; AIR 2009 SC 2643; 2009 (3) RCR(Criminal) 891; 2009(5) SCALE 702; [2009] 6 S.C.R. 486; 2009 (2) UC 878

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Women & Children

Keywords: Offence Punishable, Date Of Occurrence, Madhya Pradesh Children Act, 1928

Summary: Criminal - Women & Children - IPC, 1860, ss. 147, 148, 302 r/w 149 - Juvenile Justice Act, 1986 - Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act, 2000 - Held, on the date in question, i.e. on the date of occurrence and the date of production before the Court the appellant had not completed 18 years of age stands fully established on record - S. 16 of the 2000 Act provides that no juvenile shall be sentenced to death or imprisonment for life or committed to prison in default of payment of fine or in default of furnishing security - An accused who was juvenile cannot be denied the benefit of provisions of 2000 Act - Sentence awarded is restricted to the period already undergone - Appeal allowed.
