Maharashtra State Co-Operative Bank Limited v (1) Assistant Provident Fund Commissioner and

Supreme Court of India

08 October 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, B.N. Agrawal, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1208; (2009) 10 SCC 123; AIR 2010 SC 868; 2010 (3) AWC 2233; 2010 (1) Bom.C.R. 255; 2009 (123) FLR 653; JT 2009 (13) SC 106; 2010 (1) LLJ 644; 2009 (8) MLJ(SC) 758; 2009(13) SCALE 280; [2009] 15 S.C.R. 1; 2009 (6) SLR 117

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Sick, Social Security, Living Wage, Standard Of Life, Rajasthan Sales Tax Act, 1954, M.P. General Sales Tax Act, 1958, Tamil Nadu Pawnbrokers Act, 1943, Presidency-Towns Insolvency Act, 1909, Karnataka Pawnbrokers Act, 1961, Recovery of Debts Due to Banks and Financial Institutions Act, 1963

Summary: Banking & Finance - Provident Fund and Miscellaneous Provisions Act, 1952, s.11(2) - Realization of Provident Fund (PF) dues - Loan advanced to Sugar Industry by securing its inventory by appellant bank - Failure of Sugar Industry to remit PF dues - 1st respondent PF authorities issued warrant of attachment of inventory - Appellant bank challenged warrant of attachment in a writ proceedings before HC - HC ordered for a joint auction - HC allowed PF authorities to withdraw a portion auction amount from Court - (A) Whether appellant bank has prior charge over the inventory of Sugar Industry than respondent authorities - Held, no - Deeds of pledge executed by Sugar Industry as security for repayment of loan to appellant bank, have no effect of transferring of ownership of inventory to appellant - No illegality committed by respondent authorities with respect to the issuance of warrant of attachment - HC fully justified in directing payment of a portion of sale price to respondent authorities for being appropriated towards the provident fund dues of the workers - (B) Whether the inventory pledged with the appellant-bank constitute assets of the Sugar Industry within the meaning of s. 11(2) of the Act - Held, appellant bank has only symbolic custody of inventory of Sugar Industry as security for payment of loan - Sugar Industry continued to be the owner of inventory which could be attached and sold by respondent authorities - Therefore, inventory pledged with appellant bank constituted the assets of Sugar Industry - Appeal dismissed.
