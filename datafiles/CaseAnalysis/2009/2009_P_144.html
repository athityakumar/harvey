Nizam Institute of Medical Sciences v Prasanth S. Dhananka and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

H.S. Bedi, B.N. Agrawal, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1047; (2009) 6 SCC 1; 2010 ACJ 38; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 1503; 2009 (4) ALD(SC) 42; 2009 (3) AWC 2154; 2009 (1) CCC 240; 2009 (2) CPJ(Sc) 61; 2009 (3) CPR 81; 2009 CRLJ 3012; 2009 (155) PLR 1; 2009 (3) RCR(Civil) 174; 2009 (3) RCR(Criminal) 124; 2009 (4) RLW 3448; 2009(7) SCALE 407; [2009] 9 S.C.R. 313

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection

Keywords: Medical Services, Medical Profession, Professional Negligence, Treatment By Doctors

Summary: Consumer Protection - Consumer Protection Act, 1986, ss. 2(1)(o), and 3 - Medical negligence - Compensation - Complainant filed complaint alleging negligence by petitioner before, during and after operation resulting in acute paraplegia with complete loss of control over lower limbs and claimed compensation of Rs. 4.61 crores - Consumer Commission held that there was negligence and deficiency of service and direct compensation of Rs. 14 Lakhs - Complainant claim that compensation to be enhanced to Rs, 7.50 crores and direction to keep aside Rs. 2 crores for use in future if some developments beneficial to complainant in medical field take place - Whether compensation could be enhanced and Rs. 2 crores could be kept aside for use in future? - Held, doctors attended to complainant were remiss in their conduct - Once initial burden has been discharged by complainant by making out a case of negligence, onus shifts onto hospital or attending doctors to prove lack of negligence - Compensation of Rs. 1 crore would be appropriate - Claim of Rs. 2 crores is hypothetical hence cannot be allowed - Appeal partly allowed.
