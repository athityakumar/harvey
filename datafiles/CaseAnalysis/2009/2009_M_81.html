Union Of India & Ors. v M/S Martin Lottery Agencies Ltd.

Supreme Court of India

05 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 596; (2009) 12 SCC 209; 2009 (3) CompLJ 26; 2009(7) SCALE 34; [2009] 7 S.C.R. 946; [2009] 24 VST 1

Case Digest

Subject: Service Tax

Keywords: Charge Of Service Tax

Summary: Service Tax - Finance Act, 1994, s. s. 65(105) - Whether sale, promotion and marketing of lottery tickets would be exigible to 'Service Tax' within the meaning of the provisions of s. 65(105) - Held, when a new type of tax is introduced or a new concept of tax is introduced so as to widen the net, it, should not be construed to have a retrospective operation on the premise that it is clarificatory or declaratory in nature - Service tax, if any, would be payable only with effect from May, 2008 and not with retrospective effect - Explanation is not clarificatory or declaratory in nature - Appeal dismissed.
