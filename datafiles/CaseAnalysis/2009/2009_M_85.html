Bihar State Electricity Board v M/S Pulak Enterprises And Ors

Supreme Court of India

15 April 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 2119; (2009) 5 SCC 641; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 1462; 2009 (2) JhrCR(SC) 182; 2009(5) SCALE 611; [2009] 6 S.C.R. 292

Case Digest

Subject: Electricity & Energy

Summary: Electricity & Energy - Electricity (Supply) Act, 1948 - Rate of fuel surcharge in respect of the years 1996-97 and 1997-98 - Method of calculation - Bihar State Electricity Board (Board) has its own power generation units namely, however, power generating from these units was not sufficient and, therefore the Board purchased power from other sources in order to meet requirements - Tariff were framed by the Board in exercise of its powers conferred upon it under the provisions of s. 49 of the Act vide Tariff Notification dated 21.6.1993 which came into effect from 1.7.1993 prescribing rates for supply of power to the consumers of the Board - 1993 Tariff provides for a formula for levy and collection of fuel surcharge from the consumers of the Board from time to time - Validity of the formula for levy of fuel surcharge was upheld by SC in Bihar State Electricity Board and Anr. v. Bihar 440 Volt Vidyut Upbhokta Sangh and Ors. 1996 INDLAW SC 3325 - On 4.4.1994 the Board issued circular stating that on final calculation the fuel surcharge for the period 1992-1993 had been determined as 26.14 paise per Kwh - On 5.1.1995 the Board issued another circular calculating the fuel surcharge for the period July 1993 ( i.e. after coming into force of the new tariff) to March 1994 to be 25.98 paise per Kwh - Consumers were billed accordingly - During the year 1996-97 the Board started purchasing electricity from generating unit of Tenughat Vidyut Nigam Limited (TVNL) established in 1996 - Board by its Circular dated 31.5.1999 fixed the rate of fuel surcharge by applying formula for the years 1993-94 to 1996-97 - By treating TVNL as a source falling under the category of other source, the Board calculated weighted average cost of power as Rs.60.57 paise/Kwh - Writ Petitions were filed challenging the rates - During the pendency of the said writ petitions the Board proposed certain amendments in clauses 16.10.3, 16.10.3.1 and 17, but implementation of the circular dated 5.1.1995 was kept pending vide circular dated 8.2.1995 in the meantime - Validity of the rates of fuel surcharge was challenged mainly on two grounds:- (a) the cost of generation at the Board's own generating stations was worked out treating 1991-92 as the base year but the cost of the unit purchased from DVC, NTPC etc. had been calculated on the basis of 1992-93 rates - It was submitted that in working out the rate the values to be taken into account must correspond to the same year lest determination could become irrational and arbitrary: (b) the merger of fuel surcharge as on 1.7.93 in terms of clause 17 of the tariff was not correct - Petitioners submitted that since TVNL came into existence in the year 1996-97 there cannot be any incremental cost in respect of electricity purchased from TVNL inasmuch increase in the average unit rate of purchase of electricity has to be calculated treating 1991-92 as the base year - It was further submitted that the Board has treated the supply of electricity by DVC to TISCO as deemed supply by the Board to TISCO at a different rate which was not in accordance with the formula - HC noted that the objections of the petitioners were in consonance with the Board's own decision by which the Board had suggested certain amendments in the relevant clauses of the tariff to the State Government - HC noted that the purchase of electricity from TVNL was treated as a component of H3 i.e. "any other source", but as the TVNL came into existence only in the year 1996-97 it could not be treated as a relevant factor having regard to the fact that in computing the fuel surcharge, increase in the average unit rate of purchase of electricity has to be calculated treating 1991-92 as the base year - Fact that TVNL did not exist in the year 1991-92 and came into existence only in the year 1996-97 was sufficient to justify its deletion as component of H3 - Stand of the writ petitioners was accepted by the HC and so far as TVNL and deemed supply to TISCO were concerned, HC held that there was no infirmity in the fixing of rates of fuel surcharge except on the aforesaid two grounds - Present appeals - (A) Whether HC was right in directing to re-work out the rates of fuel surcharge for the years 1996-97 onwards after deleting the purchase of electricity from TVNL as a component of H3 in the formula? - Held, yes - Fuel surcharge has to be calculated strictly within the framework of the formula - If any extraneous element has crept in the computation to that extent must be held to be not in accordance with law and accordingly modified - Computation of the rates of fuel surcharge by the impugned circular for the years 1996-97 and onwards so far as it relates to the purchase of electricity from TVNL and "deemed supply" by the Board to TISCO does not appear to be in accordance with the formula - No infirmity in conclusions of HC - (B) Whether direction of the HC to re-work out the rates of fuel for the year 1996-97 onwards after treating the deemed supply i.e. supply of electricity by DVC to TISCO as supplied by DVS to the Board as an element of D3 in the formula was correct? - Held, Yes - No infirmity in conclusions of HC - Appeals disposed of.
