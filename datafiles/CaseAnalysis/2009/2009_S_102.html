Shyam Lal @ Kuldeep v Sanjeev Kumar and Others

Supreme Court of India

15 April 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Dalveer Bhandari, H.L. Dattu

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 518; AIR 2009 SC 3115; 2009 (4) ALD(SC) 106; 2009 (3) AWC 2672; 2009 (3) JhrCR(SC) 30; 2009 (5) MLJ(SC) 1195; 2010 (1) RLW 779; 2009(5) SCALE 507; [2009] 5 S.C.R. 1049; 2010 (1) ShimLC 258

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Land & Property

Keywords: Coparcenary Interest, Legality Of Marriage

Summary: Family & Personal - Land & Property - Hindu Succession Act, 1956, ss. 4 and 30 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 112 - Execution of Will and consequent mutation of property challenged by instituting a suit - Will executed by one Balak Ram in favour of his grandsons who were defendants no. 1 and 2(D1/D2) - Plaintiff challenged mutation of property pursuant to the Will - Plaintiff and defendants 3 & 4 were son of BR and defendants 5 & 6 were daughters - Plaintiff placed challenge on ground that property was inherited by BR from his father, and thus is an ancestral property and they were Hindu Undivided Family which was governed by the Hindu Law and Customs in matter of alienation and succession whereby BR could not bequeath the ancestral property - Defendants made submission that plaintiff alongwith D4 were fathered by another man and not BR, plaintiff countered it with submitting a school leaving certificate having name of BR as his father - Trial Court decided against plaintiff/appellant - In Appeal before District Judge, DJ partly allowed the appeal - Second appeal before High Court by defendants - Appeal allowed by HC - Judgement of HC whether proper? - Substantial questions of law framed by HC - (A) Aspect of parentage - (B) Will, its authenticity, nature of property - (A) Presumption u/s. 112 of Evidence Act as to parentage - Point answered in favour of appellant/plaintiff - Continuance of a valid marriage will prevent an inference being drawn to the effect that the children born to a woman during the continuance of the valid marriage were born to another person as a result of adulterous intercourse is only a rule of evidence; presumption is conclusive presumption of law which can only be displaced by proof of particular fact mentioned in s. 112, namely, non-access between the parties to the marriage at a time when according to the ordinary course of nature, the husband could have been the father of the child - Deliberation on the subject entered upon by the HC - Admitted fact that plaintiff and D4 were born to the mother during the continuance of her marriage with the deceased BR - On ground of public policy, it is undesirable to enquire into the paternity of a child whose parents 'have access' to each other - The presumption of legitimacy arises from birth in wedlock and not from conception - Findings of HC on the point, upheld - (B) Authenticity of Will - Concurrent finding of Courts below HC, upholding validity of Will cannot be interfered, as it is question of fact - On nature of property and Will, HC observed that question whether under the custom governing the parties, a Will could be executed in respect of ancestral property, answer to it is not res integra - In view of s. 30 r/w 4 of Act, 1956, a male Hindu governed by Mitakshara system is not debarred from making a Will in respect of coparcenery/ancestral property; 'Tek Chand v. Mool Raj, 1997(2) Hindu L.R. 306' - Findings given by HC after close examination, found to be based on correct evaluation of evidence and record of the case - Impugned judgement upheld - Appeal dismissed.
