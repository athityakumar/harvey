Dr. V. Ravi Chandran v Union Of India & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

17 November 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, Tarun Chatterjee, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1548; (2010) 1 SCC 174; AIR 2010 SC (Supp) 257; 2009 (77) ALR 793; JT 2009 (14) SC 233; 2009 (4) RCR(Civil) 961; 2009(14) SCALE 27; [2009] 15 S.C.R. 960

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Foreigners

Keywords: Foreign Court, Foreign Judgment, By Birth, National, Guardianship of Infants Act, 1925

Summary: Foreigners - Family & Personal - Custody of minor son, an US citizen - Custody order from a US court - Petitioner, father of the minor, is an American citizen - He and respondent no. 6, mother of the minor, married according to Hindu rites, filed for divorce - Parties obtained series of consent orders concerning the minor's custody/parenting rights, maintenance etc. from the competent courts of jurisdiction in the US and the courts ordered joint legal and physical custody of the minor son and, in this regard, a comprehensive arrangement in respect of the custody of the minor was made - However, the minor was removed by the mother contrary to the custody orders of the US court passed - Subsequently, father filed a petition in Supreme Court of India praying for a writ of habeas corpus and for handing over the minor son's custody and passport to him - Whether, the facts and circumstances of the case warrant an elaborate enquiry into the question of custody of minor and should the parties be relegated to the said procedure before appropriate forum in this country in this regard? - Held, this is not required - A consent order governing the issues of custody and guardianship of minor was passed by the appropriate courts in the US - The fact that all orders concerning the custody of the minor son have been passed by the US courts by consent of the parties shows that the objections raised by mother in counter affidavit about deprivation of basic rights of the child by the father are without any substance - Further, the objection raised by the mother regarding jurisdiction of the US courts prima facie does not seem to have any merit since despite being in India for over two years, she has not pursued any legal proceeding for the sole custody of her son - Finally, just because the minor has been brought to India by the mother, the custody issue concerning the minor does not deserve to be gone into by the courts in India and it would be in accordance with principles of comity as well as on facts to return the child back to the US from where he has been removed and enable the parties to establish the case before the US courts - Moreover, there is nothing on record which may even remotely suggest that it would be harmful for the child to be returned to the US - Therefore, in the present case, return of minor child to United States of America, would be in the best interest of the child - Order accordingly.
