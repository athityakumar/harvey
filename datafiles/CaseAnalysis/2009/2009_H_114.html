Himachal Road Transport Corporation and Another v Hukam Chand

Supreme Court of India

03 February 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 107; (2009) 11 SCC 222; 2009 (1) CLR 683; 2009 (121) FLR 171; 2009 (2) LLJ 324; 2009 (3) LLN 40; 2009 (2) ShimLC 77

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Appeal against holding that the respondent's date of birth could not have been changed to his disadvantage, without enquiry, and therefore, he ought to have been continued in service till 31.1.2006 with reference to the date of birth earlier entered - Held, absence of enquiry before altering the date of birth as 02.5.1945 did not affect the validity of the retirement of respondent - Nor did the acquittal in the criminal appeal subsequent to his retirement, entitle the respondent to claim that his date of birth should have been treated as 11.1.1948 or that he should have been reinstated and continued in service till 31.1.2006 - Holding an employee guilty of a misconduct on admission, or altering the conditions of service with consent, without enquiry or opportunity to show cause, does not violate principles of natural justice - Appeal allowed.
