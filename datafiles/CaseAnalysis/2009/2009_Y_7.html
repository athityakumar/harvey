Nand Kishore v Yashpal Singh

Supreme Court of India

21 July 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Tarun Chatterjee, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 885; (2009) 16 SCC 634; AIR 2009 SC 2406; JT 2009 (9) SC 421; 2009 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 1937; 2009 (6) MLJ(SC) 606; 2009 (156) PLR 746; 2009 (3) RCR(Civil) 829; 2009 (2) RCR(Rent) 144; 2009(9) SCALE 707; [2009] 10 S.C.R. 1201

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Keywords: Residential Purposes, Non- Residential Building, Capital of Punjab (Development and Regulation) Act, 1951

Summary: Rent Control - East Punjab Urban Rent Restriction Act, 1949, ss. 13(2)(ii)(b) and 11 - Capital of Punjab (Development and Regulation) Act, 1951 - Eviction petition - Ground, usage of rented premises for commercial purposes without the permission of landlord - Eviction petition dismissed by Rent Controller; appeal thereagainst allowed by Appellate Authority - High Court in revision restored order of RC and dismissed order of Appellate Authority -P /&gt;(A) Demised premises which is situated in a residential area and in a residential building whether can be used for commercial purposes even by consent of the appellant in view of s. 11 of Act, 1949 and the provisions of Act, 1951 - Provision of Act, 1949 perused and contentions heard - Held, No - (i) Whether the tenant was inducted in the demised premises for residential use or for commercial use or was he inducted for residential use but he converted such tenancy to be used for commercial use at a later date? - Provisions of s. 11 and materials on record noted - Demised premises is situated in a residential area and the building in which the demised premises is situated is also a residential building and he had also not taken any permission from the RC for carrying on commercial activities and that the demised premises can be resumed at any time because of carrying on commercial activity - S. 11 clearly prohibits a landlord or a tenant to convert the purpose of tenancy without the permission of the RC -P /&gt;(B) If the residential premise is let out for commercial purposes, by a mutual agreement between the landlord and the tenant, can the landlord still seek eviction of the tenant on the ground that using of such residential premises for commercial purposes entails the tenant to be evicted from the demised premises? - Held, Yes - 'Vinod Kumar Arora v. Surjit Kaur, 1987 (3) SCC, 711' and 'Kamal Arora v. Amar Singh, 1986 Suppl. SCC 481' - Vide these decisions position is that, landlord cannot permit a tenant to use the premises which is situated in a residential area for commercial purposes as it would be violative of s. 11 of Act, 1949 which is mandatory in nature - Impugned judgement set aside - Application for eviction allowed - Directions issued - Appeal allowed.
