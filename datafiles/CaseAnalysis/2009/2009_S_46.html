Commissioner of Central Excise, Pune v SKF India Limited

Supreme Court of India

06 July 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Aftab Alam, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 844; (2009) 13 SCC 461; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 1106; 2009 (239) E.L.T. 385; JT 2009 (9) SC 438; 2009(9) SCALE 241; [2009] 10 S.C.R. 714

Case Digest

Subject: Excise

Keywords: Recovery Of Duties, Levy Of Duty, Refund Of Duty, Mis-Statement, Adjustment Of Duty

Summary: Excise - Central Excise Act, 1944, ss. 11A, 11AB, 11AC, 37(3) - Penalty - Interest on differential duty - Supplementary invoices - Respondent-assessee sold goods manufactured by it on certain prices on payment of excise duty leviable on price on which goods were sold - Revision of prices with retrospective effect - Following revision assessee demanded from its customers balance of higher prices and issued to them supplementary invoices and also paid differential duty on goods sold earlier - Revenue alleged that assessee was liable to pay interest on differential duty - Assessee contended that payment of differential duty was made by it at time of issuing supplementary invoices to customers and, therefore, there was no question of charging interest much less any penalty - Asst. Commissioner confirmed demand of interest - Commissioner set aside order of Asst. Commissioner - On appeal, Tribunal confirmed order of Commissioner - Hence, present appeal - Whether interest is leviable on delayed or deferred payment of duty? - Interest is leviable on delayed or deferred payment of duty for whatever reasons - Held, at time of sale goods carried a higher value and those were cleared on short payment of duty - Differential duty was paid only later when assessee issued supplementary invoices to its customers demanding balance amounts - Thus it was clearly a case of short payment of duty - Payment of differential duty thus clearly came under sub-section (2B) of s. 11A and attracted levy of interest u/s. 11AB of Act - Appeals allowed
