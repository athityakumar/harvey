Joginder Singh v State Of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

27 February 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 256; (2009) 11 SCC 467; AIR 2009 SC 2263; 2009 CRLJ 2805; 2009 (2) RCR(Criminal) 589; 2009(4) SCALE 329; [2009] 3 S.C.R. 714

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Close Range, Fire-Arms

Summary: Criminal - IPC, 1860, s. 302 - Appeal against conviction and sentence - Held, merely because the eye-witnesses are family members, their evidence cannot 'per se' be discarded - When there is allegation of interestedness, the same has to be established - Mere statement that being relatives of the deceased they are likely to falsely implicate the accused cannot be a ground to discard the evidence which is otherwise cogent and credible - Appeal dismissed.
