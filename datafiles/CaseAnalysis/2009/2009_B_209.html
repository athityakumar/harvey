Branch Manager, Magma Leasing and Finance Limited v Potluri Madhavilata And Another

Supreme Court of India

18 September 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1152; (2009) 10 SCC 103; AIR 2010 SC 488; 2010 (2) AWC 1315; 2010 (1) CompLJ 684; 2010 (1) JhrCR(SC) 37; JT 2009 (12) SC 385; 2009 (8) MLJ(SC) 441; 2009 (4) RCR(Civil) 900; 2009(12) SCALE 668; [2009] 14 S.C.R. 815

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Repudiation By One Party, Doctrine Of Separability

Summary: (A) Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, s.8 - Alternative Dispute Resolution - Reference of dispute to arbitrator - Once pre-requisite conditions of s.8 satisfied the court must refer parties to arbitration.

(B) Contract & Commercial - Hire purchase agreement - Arbitration clause - Invoking of - A hire purchase agreement entered into by 1st respondent hire purchaser and appellant finance company for buying a vehicle - Default in payment of installments by 1st respondent - Vehicle seized by appellant - A suit for recovery of possession filed by 1st respondent before Civil Court - Mean while, an application for referring the dispute to arbitrator filed by appellant - Application of appellant dismissed by Civil Court - Having held that arbitration clause does not survive upon the termination of hire purchase agreement, appellant's revision petition dismissed by HC - Legality - Held, arbitration clause in hire purchase agreement applicable to all disputes, differences, claims and questions between parties arising out of such agreement - Arbitration clause survives for the purpose of resolution between parties although contract has come to an end on account of termination - Further, pre-requisite conditions of s.8 of Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, having been satisfied by the agreement in dispute - Therefore, Civil Court ought to have referred the dispute to arbitrator, hence impugned HC order set aside - Original Suit restored on the file of Civil Court with the direction to pass appropriate order - Appeal allowed.
