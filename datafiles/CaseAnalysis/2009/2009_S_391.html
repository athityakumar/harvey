Abbas Ahmad Choudhary v State Of Assam

Supreme Court of India

25 November 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

H.S. Bedi, J.M. Panchal

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 2084; (2010) 12 SCC 115; AIR 2010 SC (Supp) 742; 2010 ALL MR (Cri) 1625; 2010 CRLJ 2060; 2010 (2) RCR(Criminal) 120; 2010(2) SCALE 308; [2010] 1 S.C.R. 869

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 336 and 376 - Rape - Conviction - Challenged - Appellants convicted u/ss. 376 and 336 r/w s. 34 of the IPC - Appeal filed before HC against conviction was also dismissed - Hence, present appeal - Held, in a matter of rape, the statement of the prosecutrix must be given primary consideration, but, at the same time, the broad principle that the prosecution has to prove its case beyond reasonable doubt applies equally to a case of rape and there can be no presumption that a prosecutrix would always tell the entire story truthfully - In the present case, statements of the prosecutrix, the constable and the Investigating Officer are categoric insofar in regard to appellant 'M' - Prosecutrix in her statement recorded u/s. 164 of the CrPC, also clearly stated that she was kidnapped and raped by appellant 'M' - However, involvement of appellant 'A' was doubtful - Therefore, 'A' acquitted but the conviction of 'M' confirmed - Appeals disposed of.
