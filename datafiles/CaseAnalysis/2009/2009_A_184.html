G. Jayalakshmi and Others v Arulmighu Pazhikanchiya Vinayagar and Its Temple

Supreme Court of India

28 July 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, G.S. Singhvi, Deepak Verma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1699; (2009) 16 SCC 409; 2009 (5) CTC 181; JT 2009 (12) SC 576; 2009 (8) MLJ(SC) 1495; 2009(11) SCALE 1; [2009] 12 S.C.R. 14

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Trusts & Associations

Keywords: Village Site

Summary: Trusts & Associations - Land & Property - Tamil Nadu Hindu Religious & Charitable Endowments Act, 1959, ss. 6(11), 6(20), 70 - Petitioner filed an application before the Deputy Commissioner, Hindu Religious and Charitable Endowment (Administration) Department, for declaration that the respondent was not a religious and charitable endowment within the meaning of the Act and that petitioner was the hereditary trustee of the temple - Deputy Commissioner held that the temple was not a religious institution falling within scope of Act - On appeal, Commissioner upheld order of Deputy Commissioner - Respondent filed suit against said order for grant of a declaration that temple was a public temple - Trial Court decreed said suit and declared that temple in question was a public temple - HC reversed said judgment and decree of Trial Court - Hence, present appeal - Whether HC's order could be upheld? - Held, HC should have gone into other aspects of matter in the backdrop of documents produced by parties and should not have disposed of the appeal simply by relying upon some observations made with regard to temple properties in earlier round of litigation by Courts - Large number of documents produced by respondents to show that impugned properties belong to temple - Impugned judgment set aside - Matter remanded to HC for consideration matter afresh - Appeal disposed of

Legislation Cited

Madras Hindu Religious and Charitable Endowments Act, 1959

Madras Hindu Religious and Charitable Endowments Act, 1959 s. 63(b)

Madras Hindu Religious and Charitable Endowments Act, 1959 s. 70
