Suman v State Of Rajasthan And Another

Supreme Court of India

13 November 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1536; (2010) 1 SCC 250; AIR 2010 SC 518; 2010 ALL MR (Cri) 316; 2010 CRLJ 851; JT 2009 (14) SC 169; 2009(13) SCALE 716

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Police Record

Summary: Criminal - Family & Personal - Code of Criminal Procedure,1973,ss. 161,164 and 482 - Indian Penal Code,1860, ss. 323, 354, 377, 406 and 498A - Dowry - Harassment - Justifiability - Respondent no.2 named appellant as one of accused in complaint filed u/s.498A, IPC , alleging harassment and torture by her husband and in laws - After investigation IO found that appellant was innocent and filed negative report - Trial court after adverting contents of complaint took cognizance of offence and passed order u/s. 319 CrPC to summon appellant - Aggrieved, appellant filed revision which was allowed- On appeal, revision was set aside by HC and matter was remitted back to sessions court which upheld order passed by Trial Court - Hence, instant appeal - Whether, Trial Court was vitiated by legal infirmity in taking cognizance for summoning appellant u/s. 319 CrPC - Held, Trial Court had objectively considered entire matter and judiciously exercised discretion u/s.319 of CrPC for taking cognizance against appellant - Sessions Court in post remanded order independently, examined entire record and prima facie case was made out for initiating proceedings against appellant u/s.498A IPC - Hence, appeal was dismissed and Trial court was directed to conduct Trial and decide matter expeditiously - Appeal dismissed.
