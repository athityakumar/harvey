Vishnu Dutt Sharma v Daya Sapra

Supreme Court of India

05 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1976; (2009) 13 SCC 729; 2009(6) ALL MR 436; 2009 (4) AWC 3405; 2010 (2) CompLJ 79; JT 2009 (13) SC 44; 2010 (3) MLJ(Crl) 489; 2009 (3) RCR(Civil) 483; 2009(8) SCALE 698; [2009] 7 S.C.R. 977

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Keywords: Rejection Of Plaint, Special Rules

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - O. 7, r. 11(d) - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, s. 138 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 420 - Rejection of plaint - Dishonour of cheque - Appellant filed criminal case as well as civil suit against respondent alleging dishonour of cheque - Criminal court acquitted respondent on ground that he had successfully proved that cheque in question was not issued to appellant by way of repayment of any loan - Respondent filed an application u/o. 7 r. 11 (d) r/w. s. 151 of CPC for rejection of plaint on ground that criminal complaint had already been dismissed - Civil Judge dismissed said application on ground that finding of criminal court would not operate as res judicata in civil suit - On appeal, HC set aside impugned order and held that principle of res judicata was applicable and civil suit by plaintiff was abuse of process of law - Hence, present appeal - Whether HC's order could be upheld? - Held, No - Principles of res judicata are not applicable in facts and circumstances of the present case - Judgment of a criminal court in a civil proceeding will only have limited application - It would not be binding in civil proceeding - Civil suit must be determined on its own, in view evidence on record and not in terms of evidence brought in criminal proceedings - HC's order set aside - Appeal allowed.
