Union Of India And Ors v Dipak Kumar Santra

Supreme Court of India

06 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1582; (2009) 7 SCC 370; AIR 2010 SC 462; 2009 (122) FLR 474; 2009(8) SCALE 822; [2009] 8 S.C.R. 281; 2011 (7) SLR 553

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Army Rules, 1954, r. 13(3) - Discharge from service - Respondent was discharged from service on ground that he had failed twice in clerks' proficiency and aptitude test - Single Judge held that as per r. 13 competent authority is entitled to discharge present respondent - On appeal, Division Bench of HC held that there was a vacancy available and writ petitioner can be appointed against vacancy if such vacancy is released for his appointment - Hence, present appeal - Whether Division Bench's order could be upheld? - Held, reference has been made by appellants to the letter issued by the Army Headquarter laying down procedure required to be followed in respect to individuals who fail in clerks proficiency and aptitude test while undergoing basic military training - Division Bench's order set aside - Appeal allowed.
