Dipali Dey (Baxi) v Mira Das

Supreme Court of India

14 July 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Tarun Chatterjee, R.M. Lodha

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1343; (2009) 11 SCC 495; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 1386; 2009(9) SCALE 461

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950 art. 227 - Tenancy - Disconnection of electricity - Appeal - Appellant-tenant had regularly paid rent but no rent receipts were granted to her - Further, her electricity supply was disconnected by respondent - Appellant filed applications to restore electricity supply - Both the lower Courts allowed the application and directed to restore electricity - Revision was allowed by HC in favour of respondent - Aggrieved by this order appellant filed the present appeal on special leave - Whether, the decision of HC was justified - Held, the impugned order of HC was set aside and the orders passed by the lower courts below were restored - HC was not justified to interfere in the exercise of its power u/art. 227 of the Constitution, with the orders passed by the lower Courts which directed the landlord to restore the electric supply in the suit - Appellant was a tenant or not shall be gone into at the time of disposal of the suit when the evidence would be produced by the parties in support of their respective cases - Since there was no dispute about possession of the suit premises and appellant claimed to be a tenant, the electric supply to the premises in question was to be restored - Appeal allowed.
