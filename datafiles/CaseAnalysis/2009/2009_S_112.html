Santosh Kumar Tripathi and Others v Uttar Pradesh Power Corporation and Others

Supreme Court of India

15 June 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Aftab Alam, B. SUDARSHAN REDDY

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1662; (2009) 14 SCC 210; 2009 (3) AWC 2833; 2009 (122) FLR 650; JT 2009 (14) SC 223; 2009 (4) LLJ 333; 2009(8) SCALE 782

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Service

Keywords: Impleadment Application, Uttar Pradesh State Electricity Board (Limitation of Functions) Regulations, 1978

Summary: Service - Electricity & Energy - Apprentices Act, 1961 - Uttar Pradesh State Electricity Board (Limitation of Functions) Regulations, 1978 - Apprenticeship - Regularization - Exemption from Exam - Validity - Appellants had done term of apprenticeship in different trades with respondent- State Electricity Board - On completion of apprenticeship, appellants claimed absorption as Junior Engineers and on different posts of Operating Staff on basis of their apprentice training in respective trades suitable for posts - However, respondent issued advertisement inviting applications for filling-up various posts - Appellants filed petition before HC seeking direction in their favour to consider them for appointment on respective posts - HC disposed petition without any relief - Appellants filed appeal before SC - SC held that HC had not examined relevant Rules concerning recruitments to find out whether or not appearing in written test, even for an apprentice, was essential to selection process, accordingly, remitted matter to HC - Meanwhile, another advertisement was issued in 2001 and vacancies were filled up on that basis - HC held that recruitment Regulations provided for written examination and hence, it was condition precedent for all candidates, apprentices not excluded, to appear in examination and to qualify for appointment - Hence, instant appeal - Whether or not appellants were required to appear in written examination would depend upon statutory Rules and Regulations governing recruitments in question - Held, HC took into consideration vast amount of other materials for coming to its conclusion that recruitment Regulations provided for written examination and hence, it was condition precedent for all candidates, apprentices not excluded, to appear in examination and to qualify for appointment - Judgment of HC under appeal was not contrary to spirit of decision of SC in U. P. State Road Transport Corporation vs. U. P. Parivahan Nigam Shikshuk Berojgar Sangh, 1995 Indlaw SC 1775 - Hence, HC had taken correct view of matter that did not call for any interference - Appeal dismissed.
