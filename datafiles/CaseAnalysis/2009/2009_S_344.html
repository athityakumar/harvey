Syed Askari Hadi Ali Augustine Imam & Anr. v State (Delhi Admn.) & Anr

Supreme Court of India

03 March 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Lokeshwar Singh Panta, B. Sudarshan Reddy

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 278; (2009) 5 SCC 528; AIR 2009 SC 3232; 2009 (3) JCC 1705; JT 2009 (4) SC 522; 2009 (5) MahLJ 127; 2009 (4) MLJ(Crl) 1045; 2009 (2) RCR(Criminal) 520; 2009(3) SCALE 604; [2009] 3 S.C.R. 1017; 2010 (1) ShimLC 286

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Family & Personal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Delay In Prosecution, Civil Case, Previous Judgment, Sheriff, Succession Act, 1865

Summary: Criminal - Effect of pendency of a probate proceeding vis-'-vis a criminal case involving allegations of forgery of a Will - Held, ordinarily a criminal proceeding will have primacy over the civil proceeding - Precedence to a criminal proceeding is given having regard to the fact that disposal of a civil proceeding ordinarily takes a long time and in the interest of justice the former should be disposed of as expeditiously as possible - Criminal case was instituted much prior to the initiation of the probate proceeding - Should not interfere with the impugned judgment because of the conduct of the appellant and the stage in which the probate proceedings are pending - Appeal dismissed.
