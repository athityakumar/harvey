Mahendra Pratap Singh v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

24 February 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Lokeshwar Singh Panta, B. SUDARSHAN REDDY

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 223; (2009) 11 SCC 334; JT 2009 (3) SC 120; 2010 (5) RCR(Criminal) 513; 2009(3) SCALE 122; [2009] 2 S.C.R. 1033

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Blood-Stained Earth, Fire Arms, Gunshot, Supreme Court (Enlargement of Criminal Appellate Jurisdiction) Act, 1970

Summary: Criminal - IPC, 1860, ss. 304 Part-II, 307, 324; Arms Act, s. 25 - Appeal against conviction and sentence - Held, discrepancies coming on record in the evidence of PWs 4, 8, 9, 10 and 13 in no circumstances can be termed to be minors in nature which are vital for disbelieving and discrediting the evidence of the eye-witnesses - High Court discarded the important pieces of evidence on the basis of surmises and conjectures - Unless the conclusion of the trial court drawn on the evidence on record are found to be unreasonable and perverse or unsustainable, the High Court should not interfere with the order of acquittal - Prosecution has failed to prove its case beyond all reasonable doubt and the High Court committed error in interfering with the trial court's order of acquittal - Appeal allowed.
