Vadiraj Naggappa Vernekar v Sharad Chand Prabhakar Gogate

Supreme Court of India

24 February 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1069; (2009) 4 SCC 410; AIR 2009 SC 1604; 2009(4) ALL MR 459; 2009 (76) ALR 229; 2009 (3) AWC 2364; 2009 (5) Bom.C.R. 460; 2009 (4) JhrCR(SC) 55; JT 2009 (7) SC 202; 2009 (5) MahLJ 142; 2009 (4) MLJ(SC) 288; 2009 (2) RCR(Civil) 508; 2009 (1) RCR(Rent) 502; 2009 (107) RD 770; 2009(4) SCALE 90; [2009] 2 S.C.R. 1071; 2009 (3) UC 1969

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Re-Examination

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 18 r. 4, O. 18 r. 7 - Recalling of witnesses - Whether a witness having been examined by way of affidavit evidence can be recalled for giving further evidence with regard to facts not mentioned in affidavit? - Both Single Judge and Division Bench of HC rejected appellants' application under O. 18 Rule 17, CPC - Held, power under provisions of O. 18 r. 17, CPC is to be sparingly exercised and in appropriate cases and not as a general rule merely an ground that his recall and re-examination would not cause any prejudice to parties - Such power is to be invoked not to fill up lacunae in evidence of witness which has already been recorded but to clear any ambiguity that may have arisen during course of his examination - In present appeal, no such case made out - Appeal dismissed.
