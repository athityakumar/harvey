Jagriti Devi v State Of H.P.

Supreme Court of India

06 July 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Mukundakam Sharma, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 859; (2009) 14 SCC 771; AIR 2009 SC 2869; 2009 (4) CalLT(SC) 15; 2010 (1) MLJ(Crl) 671; 2010 (1) RCR(Criminal) 101; 2009(9) SCALE 48; [2009] 10 S.C.R. 167; 2010 (2) ShimLC 46

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302 and 304 Part I and II - Culpable homicide amounting to murder, and culpable homicide not amounting to murder - Injury on the person of accused - Conviction u/s. 302 IPC by Trial Court - Affirmed by High Court - Whether proper? - Held, No - Accused/appellant was first wife of 'MS' and deceased was the second wife - 'Khukri' blow on the person of deceased; time of occurrence 6.00 am - Evidence of prosecution witnesses examined, medical evidence and statement of accused/appellant u/s. 313 Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 and written statement u/s. 233 CrPC perused - S. 299 and 300 deliberated upon - There was an altercation preceding the incident of murder in which the accused/appellant was insulted by the deceased and by doing so the deceased provoked the accused/appellant - Court noted important fact of deceased taking out the 'khukri' which was kept under her pillow where she was sleeping; she took out the 'khukri' with the intention of assaulting the accused/appellant and the accused/appellant in order to save herself grappled with the deceased and during that process she also received injuries - Court noted prosecution's failure in giving any explanation with regard to such injuries which was sustained by the accused/appellant - Finding, there was no intention on the part of the accused/appellant to kill the deceased - Held further, conviction to be altered to s. 304 Part II - Court observed that it is trite law that s. 304 Part II comes into play when the death is caused by doing an act with knowledge that it is likely to cause death but there is no intention on the part of the accused either to cause death or to cause such bodily injury as is likely to cause death - Conviction awarded by TC and HC u/s. 302 set aside - Sentence altered to that of period already undergone and punishment of fine set aside - Appeal disposed of.
