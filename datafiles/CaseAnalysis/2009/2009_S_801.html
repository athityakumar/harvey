Dr. Gulshan Prakash and others v State of Haryana and others

Supreme Court of India

02 December 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, K.G. Balakrishnan, M.N. Venkatachaliah

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 2304; 2009 (8) MLJ(SC) 1569

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Education

Keywords: Mandamus, Backward Classes, Special Provision, Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribes, Medical Council Of India, Kerala State and Subordinate Services Rules, 1958, Constitution of India, 1950

Summary: Education - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 16(4), 16(4A), 32 - Reservation for SC/ST/BC categories at PG level - Maharshi Dayananad University (MDU) issued prospectus for holding entrance examination for MD/MS/PG Diploma and MDS courses in Govt. Medical and Dental Colleges in the State for year 2008-09 - While so, appellants made representation to State Govt. for implementation of SC/ST reservation in PG courses in accordance with guidelines issued by State Govt. on 19-3-1999 - However, as there was no response, appellants preferred writ petition before HC for quashing of prospectus issued by MDU but the same was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Further, another writ petition was also filed in relation to prospectus issued by MDU for same courses for academic session 2009-10 - Whether mandamus could be issued for implementation of reservation for SC/ST/BC categories at PG level to State Govt.

Held, art.15(4) is an enabling provision and it is for the State Govt. to grant reservation for SC/ST/BC categories at PG level in admission or not. As such, decision of State Govt. not to make any provision for reservation at PG level suffers no infirmity. Further, just because State Govt. decided to grant reservation for SC/ST/BC categories in admission at under graduate level, it does not follow that it is bound to grant reservation at PG level also. Furthermore, State Govt. conveyed its decision that it is not in favour of reservation for SC/ST/BC categories at PG level. Under such circumstances, Court could not issue mandamus against their decision and their prospectus could not be faulted with for not providing reservation in PG courses. Hence, appeal and WP are dismissed. Order accordingly.

Ratio - Art.15(4) is only an enabling provision and it is for respective States either to enact a legislation or issue an executive instruction for providing reservation in PG courses.
