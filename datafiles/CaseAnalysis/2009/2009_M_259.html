Medha Ashok Panchabhai v Ashok Atmaram Panchabha

Supreme Court of India

13 April 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Agrawal, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1647; (2010) 15 SCC 558

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Keywords: Grounds For Divorce

Summary: Family & Personal - Hindu Marriage Act, 1955, ss. 13(1)(ia), 25 - Cruelty and desertion - Grant of divorce - Quantum of permanent alimony - Respondent/Husband married appellant/wife, due to their wedlock child was born - Appellant filed divorce petition u/s. 13(1)(ia) of 1955 Act against respondent on grounds of desertion and cruelty - Trial Court allowed petition but decided issues of desertion and cruelty against appellant and directed respondent to pay lump sum of Rs. 20,000/- towards permanent alimony u/s. 25 of Act - Appellant preferred appeal and challenged issues of desertion and cruelty, First Appellant Court partly allowed appeal - Aggrieved appellant filed appeal before HC, which allowed appeal and directed respondent to pay permanent alimony of Rs. 2000/- per month - Hence instant appeal.

Held, appellant argued that permanent alimony granted by HC is very meagre and same is wholly insufficient for sustaining appellant and her daughter. She fairly admitted that she is receiving rupees one thousand five hundred per month by doing temporary job but submitted that keeping in view income of respondent, HC should have awarded higher amount by way of permanent alimony. Respondent, who has appeared in person, stated that his present salary is rupees twelve thousand per month. He also gave out that after divorce, he has contracted second marriage and has daughter from that marriage. Court has considered respective submissions. Taking into consideration quantum of monthly earnings of appellant and respondent as also need of their respective families, Court is of view that it would be just and expedient to fix quantum of permanent alimony payable to appellant and her daughter at rate of rupees four thousand per month. Accordingly, appeal is allowed in-part and order is modified by directing that respondent shall pay maintenance at rate of rupees four thousand per month to appellant and daughter from date of filing petition for divorce. Appeal disposed of.

Ratio - When wife had sufficient earnings then she was liable to pay permanent alimony but if she doesn’t had then husband was liable to pay permanent alimony to his wife due to divorce.
