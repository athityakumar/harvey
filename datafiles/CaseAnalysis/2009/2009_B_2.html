Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Service

Keywords: Communication Of Acceptance, Appeals Disposed Of, Public Sector Banks, Retirement Scheme, Nationalized, Nationalised Banks Employees' Pension Regulations, 1995

Summary: Service - Banking & Finance - Nationalised Banks Employees' Pension Regulations, 1995 - Nationalized banks - Scheme VRS 2000 - Years of service - Govt. of India, Ministry of Finance (Banking Division), advised nationalized banks to carry out detailed manpower planning - Human Resource Management Committee was constituted - Committee placed draft Voluntary Retirement Scheme - With approval of Central Govt., Indian Bank Association circulated salient features of draft scheme to nationalized banks for consideration and adoption - Board of Directors, keeping in view objectives, considered draft scheme and adopted it separately - Made amendment with retrospective effect which allowed minimum age for retirement as 15 years after service - Those opted had been given retrial benefits by respective banks under VRS 2000 save and except benefit of pension u/regn. 29(5) of the 1995 Regulations - Representation did not yield any result and approached various HC - Views of HCs differed - Whether employees having completed 20 years of service who had opted for voluntary retirement under VRS 2000 were entitled to addition of five years of notional service in calculating length of service for purpose Scheme as per regn. 29(5) of 1995 Regulations - Held, respective banks should recalculate, within one month from date of judgment, pension payable to concerned employees by giving them benefit of regn. 29(5) - Contrary view expressed by some of HCs did not laid down correct legal position - Employees should not be entitled to interest on unpaid pension - Appeals disposed of.
