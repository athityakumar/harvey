State of Uttar Pradesh v Paras Nath Singh

Supreme Court of India

05 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, D.K. Jain, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1905; (2009) 6 SCC 372; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 1615; 2009 CRLJ 3069; JT 2009 (13) SC 625; 2009 (4) MLJ(Crl) 317; 2009(8) SCALE 553; [2009] 8 S.C.R. 85

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Power Of The Court, Discharge Of Duty

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 409, 468 - Trial Court convicted respondent, a public servant, for forgery and criminal breach of trust - Respondent filed appeal before First Appellate Court - First Appellate Court allowed respondent's appeal on the ground that the person who accorded sanction for prosecution was not authorised to do so - Appellant State sought leave to appeal against the Trial Court order - HC refused to grant leave on the ground that appellant had failed to show that failure of justice had been occasioned - Whether HC's refusal is proper - No finding, sanction or order by a court of competent jurisdiction becomes invalid unless a failure of justice has in fact been occasioned because of any error, omission or irregularity in the charge including in mis-joinder of charge - Held, burden is on the respondent to show that failure of justice has been occasioned and not on the appellant - Impugned HC order set aside - Direction issued to HC to grant leave to appeal - Appeal allowed.
