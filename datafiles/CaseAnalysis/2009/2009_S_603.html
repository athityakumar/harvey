State Of Rajasthan & Ors. v Heritage Crafts

Supreme Court of India

10 November 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1547; 2010(2) SCALE 695

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax

Keywords: Rajasthan Motor Vehicles Taxation Act, 1951, Rajasthan Finance Act, 1997, Assam Taxation (on Goods carried by Roads or Inland Waterways) Act, 1954, Rajasthan Finance Act, 2000

Summary: Indirect Tax - Constitution of India, 1950, arts.301, 265 - Rajasthan Motor Vehicles Taxation Act, 1951, s. 4(1)(b) - Transfer of vehicles - Levy of addition one time tax - Struck down of proviso - According to Statement of Objects and Reasons, insertion of proviso of s. 4 of the Act by said Finance Act, 2000 was to check frequent transfers of vehicles - Said proviso was added to s. 4(1)(b) of the Act which made provision for payment of additional one time tax - It was levy of additional one time tax which stood challenged before HC who struck down levy of additional one time tax - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether impugned law puts restraint in form of taxation on movement of trade and, such law fell u/art. 301 of the Constitution - Held, on reading reasoning of HC for striking down impugned proviso, it showed that according to HC, second proviso to s. 4(1)(b) of the Act violated art. 265 of the Constitution and, therefore, was liable to be struck down - Further, it was doctrine of 'direct and immediate effect' which constituted working test propounded - Therefore, however law was impugned as violative of art. 301 of the Constitution, Courts had to examine effect of operation of impugned law on inter-State and intra-State movement of goods which had not been done - Thus, impugned judgment of HC was set aside and cases were remitted back for de novo consideration in accordance with law - However, liberty was granted to each of assessees to amend petition - Appeals disposed of.
