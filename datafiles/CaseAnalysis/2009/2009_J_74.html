Ram Babu Agarwal v Jay Kishan Das

Supreme Court of India

07 October 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Markandey Katju, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1207; (2010) 1 SCC 164; 2009 (9) ADJ 256; AIR 2010 SC 721; 2010 (78) ALR 458; 2010 (1) JhrCR(SC) 13; JT 2009 (13) SC 97; 2009 (8) MLJ(SC) 431; 2009 (4) RCR(Civil) 748; 2009 (2) RCR(Rent) 455; 2009(13) SCALE 175; [2009] 14 S.C.R. 1148; 2010 (1) WLN(SC) 42

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - Madhya Pradesh Accommodation Control Act, 1961 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Suit for eviction filed on grounds of (i) default in payment of rent; (ii) bonafide need - HC recorded a finding of fact that entire rent deposited by tenant in compliance with order of HC - (A) Whether provisions of s. 13(6) of Act would apply if tenant committed default in payment of rent after judgment of HC and during pendency of special leave petition u/art. 136 of Constitution? - Held, provisions of s. 13(6) of Act will apply only to statutory appeals under Act and not to constitutional remedy u/art. 136 of Constitution - (B) Bona fide need - Appellant required premises for his son for starting footwear business - Held, HC should not have rejected contention based on bona fide need merely on ground that the son has no experience in foot wear business, a person can start a new business even if he has no experience in new business - Matter remanded to Trial Court to decided matter on bona fide need - Order accordingly.
