Oriental Insurance Co. Ltd. v Ram Prasad Varma & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

13 January 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 27; (2009) 2 SCC 712; (2009) 1 SCC (Cr) 853; 2009 (1) ACC 357; AIR 2009 SC 1831; 2009 (2) ALD(SC) 8; 2009 (74) ALR 830; 2009 (1) AWC 940; 2009 (2) CTC 87; JT 2009 (1) SC 461; 2009 (1) KLT 462; 2009 (4) MahLJ 765; 2009 (4) MLJ(SC) 1193; 2009 (154) PLR 502; 2010 (1) RCR(Civil) 151; 2009(1) SCALE 598; [2009] 1 S.C.R. 209; 2009 (1) WLN(SC) 103

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Computation Of Compensation, Course Of Employment, In The Course Of Employment, Assessment Of Damages, Loss Of Future Earning, Life Expectancy

Summary: Motor Vehicles Act, s. 166 - Tribunal awarded a sum of Rs.19, 63, 000/- - Appeal against - Held, a claimant who had suffered injuries in a motor vehicle accident resulting in amputation of both legs is entitled to 100% compensation in terms of the First Schedule appended to the Workmen's Compensation Act, 1923 - Amount of compensation which represents the loss of income can be calculated either in terms of the structured formula as contained in the Second Schedule appended to the Motor Vehicles Act or on the basis of the other materials brought on record - A person, although alive, but when he is not in a position to move and even for every small thing he has to depend upon the services of another, a direction to deduct 1/3rd of the amount from his total income need not always be insisted upon - Appeal is dismissed subject to the modification that from the gross income of the respondent, the amount of income tax as was applicable at the relevant time should be deducted - Order accordingly.
