Commissioner of Customs, New Delhi v Euroasia Global

Supreme Court of India

23 March 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 803; (2009) 6 SCC 58; 2009 (236) E.L.T. 627; 2009(14) SCALE 132

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Stay Granted

Summary: Customs - Customs Act, 1962, ss. 2(39), 110 and 121 - Confiscation - Misdeclaration - Revenue alleged that importer was indulge in fraudulent import of PVC cloth under Tariff Heading 5903 by way of misdeclaration of its value and description - Importer contended that amount seized in importer house represented the sale proceeds of the imported goods which goods were cleared on payment of duty after assessment and therefore s. 121 of the Customs Act, 1962 was not applicable - Whether misdeclaration of description and value in the facts and circumstances of this case constituted 'smuggling' as defined in s. 2(39) of the Customs Act, 1962? - Held, main ground of the Department was that there was some hawala transaction going on and that the said amount appeared to have been acquired in such a transaction - U/s. 110, the competent officer is authorized to seize such goods in respect of which he has reason to believe that they are liable to confiscation - Hence, before adjudication, in exercise of writ jurisdiction HC ought not to have granted unconditional release of the cash - Impugned judgement of HC stayed - Petition disposed of.
