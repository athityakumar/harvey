L. N. Aswathama & Anr v P. Prakash

Supreme Court of India

21 April 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1588; (2009) 13 SCC 229; 2010 (1) AWC 543; JT 2009 (9) SC 527; 2009 (108) RD 764; 2009(9) SCALE 658; [2009] 10 S.C.R. 615

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Suit for declaration of title, possession, permanent injunction and mense profits - Appellants came to know about the impugned property at the time of clearing a bank loan taken by their father whereby the impugned property was mortgaged - Appellants filed suit claiming ownership of impugned property - Defendant opposed the suit and contended that as he was in continuous undisturbed possession of suit property for more than 30 years, he had perfected his title by adverse possession - Trial Court decreed suit - On appeal, HC reversed judgment of Trial Court - Hence, present appeal - Whether HC's order could be upheld? - Held, respondent has not produced any evidence to show that he was in possession of suit property for a period of 12 years prior to filing of suit by plaintiff - When defendant claimed title and that was proved to be false or fabricated, then burden is heavy upon him to prove actual, exclusive, open, uninterrupted possession for 12 years in which Defendant failed - Hence, impugned order of HC set aside - Appeal allowed
