Shesh Mani Shukla v D.I.O.S. Deoria & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

31 July 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 937; (2009) 15 SCC 436; AIR 2010 SC 1433; JT 2009 (10) SC 309; 2009(10) SCALE 457; [2009] 11 S.C.R. 841; 2010 (7) SLR 539; 2009 (4) UPLBEC 3197

Case Digest

Subject: Education; Service

Keywords: Ad Hoc Appointment, Board of High School and Intermediate Education Act, 1921, U.P. High School and Intermediate Colleges (Payment of Salaries & Teachers and Other Employees) Act, 1971, UP Secondary Education Services Commission and Selection Boards Act, 1982, First Removal of Difficulties Order, 1981

Summary: Service - Education - UP Secondary Education Services Commission and Selection Boards Act, 1982, s. 18(2) - First Removal of Difficulties Order, 1981, para 5 - Board of High School and Intermediate Education Act, 1921 - U.P. High School and Intermediate Colleges (Payment of Salaries & Teachers and Other Employees) Act, 1971 - Inter College in question was recognized under Act, 1921 and Act, 1971 - Vacancy had arisen in the post of teacher in CT grade - Intimation given by the Management of the college of said vacancy, to the respondent/authority with a request to forward the name of an eligible teacher - Respondent no. 3's name was recommended - Recommendation was ignored; advertisement issued; appellant was selected - School authorities refused to grant approval to appellant's appointment, and insisted on the Mgt. to accept joining of respondent no. 3; still no offer of appointment given and the ground was that respondent no. 3 was not suitable for holding the post of assistant teacher - Despite disapproval of recommendation of the name of appellant, he continued to work in the school as an ad hoc employee - Such disapproval was challenged in writ - Writ petition and appeal was dismissed - Whether the Committee of Management can make appointment in the post of a teacher ignoring the recommendations of the DIOS? - Held, No - Provisions perused, decided cases on the vires of provision further noted - Recommendation by DIOS was made within the limitation period; on what premise, the candidature of respondent no. 3 was not found suitable or eligible, not disclosed - Letter by DIOS wherein directions, reminders, further directions were given, noted - College found not to have complied with the provisions of the 1982 Act as amended, as also para 5 of 1981 Order by the HC - Contention was raised that appellant had worked for a long time - Court observed that it will not make a difference as his appointment was void-ab-initio - Being without merit - Appeal dismissed.
