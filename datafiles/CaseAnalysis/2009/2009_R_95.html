Rajendra Kumar Srivastava & Ors v Samyut Kshetriya Gramin Bank & Ors

Supreme Court of India

17 November 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1549; (2010) 1 SCC 335; AIR 2010 SC 699; 2010 (1) AWC 988; 2009 (5) ESC 825; 2010 (1) JhrCR(SC) 153; JT 2009 (14) SC 252; 2010 (1) MLJ(SC) 135; 2009(14) SCALE 67; [2009] 15 S.C.R. 936; 2009 (6) SLR 639; 2010 (1) UPLBEC 507

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Criteria For Promotion, Feeder Post, Mode Of Selection, Regional Rural Banks (Appointment and Promotion of Officers and other Employees) Rules, 1988, Provincial Rural Banks Act, 1976

Summary: Service - Regional Rural Banks (Appointment and Promotion of Officers and Other Employees) Rules, 1988, r. 5 - Promotion - Appellants sought quashing of resolution of Board of Directors prescribing promotion procedure, contending that restricting promotion to only those who secured minimum qualifying marks, was violative of seniority-cum-merit principle - HC upheld process of selection - Said decision is challenged in present appeal by special leave - (A) Whether minimum qualifying marks could be prescribed for assessment of past performance and interview, where promotions are to be made on principle of seniority-cum-merit? - Held, prescribing minimum qualifying marks to ascertain minimum merit necessary for discharging functions of higher post, is not violative of concept of promotion by seniority-cum-merit - (B) Whether respondent bank was justified in fixing a high percentage as minimum qualifying marks for promotion? - Held, what should be minimum necessary merit for promotion, is a matter that is decided by management, having in mind requirements of post to which promotions are to be made - Employer has discretion to fix different minimum merit, for different categories of posts, subject to relevant Rules - Officers promoted to Scale II were required to head larger branches or departments in Head Office, with higher responsibilities, it cannot be said that fixing minimum qualifying marks at 78% is excessive, unreasonable or arbitrary - Appeal dismissed.
