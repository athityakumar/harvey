Commissioner of Central Excise, Bhubaneswar-I v Champdany Industries Limited

Supreme Court of India

08 September 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Ganguly, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1101; (2009) 9 SCC 466; 2009 (241) E.L.T. 481; JT 2009 (12) SC 156; 2009(12) SCALE 317; [2009] 14 S.C.R. 211

Case Digest

Subject: Excise

Keywords: Tariff Item, Revenue's appeals dismissed, Test Of Predominance

Summary: Excise - Central Excise Tariff Act, 1985, Chapter 57 - Classification - Carpets - Respondent are manufacturers of carpets by interlacing yarns of three different types, namely, jute, cotton and polypropylene - Respondents submitted that in carpets which are manufactured jute always predominates by weight over each of the other single textile material - Report of Indian Jute Industries Research Association shows that jute predominates by weight over each other single textile material in said carpets and said carpets do not have any base fabric - Despite said report, Revenue's case is that surface of carpet being entirely of polypropylene, same cannot be classified as jute carpet - Revenue even after accepting those reports issued a show-cause notice on ground that surface of jute carpets was entirely of polypropylene and therefore, as per Note 1 of Chapter 57 of First Schedule to Central Excise Tariff Act, 1985 those carpets cannot be classified as jute carpets but are classifiable as polypropylene carpets - Asst. Commissioner held that the carpets cannot be classified as jute carpets but are polypropylene carpets - On appeal, Commissioner held that as per Section Notes any product which contains two or more textile materials are to be classified as consisting of that textile material which predominates by weight over similar textile materials - Tribunal dismissed appeal filed by revenue against said impugned order - Hence, present appeal - Whether goods in question are carpets and other textile floor coverings for purposes of Chapter 57 or not? - Since goods admittedly fall under Chapter 57 and consist of more than two or more textile materials, it has to be classified on basis of that textile material which predominates by weight over any other single textile material - Held, it is not disputed by Revenue that by predominance test content of jute in carpet is highest and more than 50% - As jute admittedly predominates by weight over each other single textile material, said Carpet could only be classified as jute carpets and nothing else - Revenue's appeal dismissed.
