Director, Central Bureau of Investigation and v D. P. Singh

Supreme Court of India

16 December 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1948; (2010) 1 SCC 647; 2010 (2) AWC 1734; 2010 (1) ESC 50; 2010 (125) FLR 393; JT 2009 (15) SC 232; 2009(14) SCALE 593; [2009] 16 S.C.R. 571; 2010 (1) SLR 112

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: By Transfer On Deputation, Tenure Post, Deputationist

Summary: Service - Special Police Establishment (Executive Staff) Recruitment Rules, 1963 - Seniority - Respondent, Sub-Inspector in the State Police Service, transferred to Central Bureau of Investigation (CBI) by way of deputation - Respondent was promoted to the post of Inspector and further, was appointed to the post of Deputy Superintendent of Police (Dy. SP) on ad hoc basis - Respondent exercised his option for absorption to the post of Dy. SP in CBI - Respondent sought the seniority from the date of his initial appointment as Dy. SP in CBI - Appellant did not consider the request of respondent - Respondent filed application before the Tribunal - Tribunal dismissed application, however, HC allowed respondent's writ petition, directing appellant to consider the seniority of respondent from the date of his initial appointment in the cadre - Held, respondent was not holding the post of Dy. SP or equivalent post in his parent department any time prior to his absorption in CBI - Initial appointment of respondent was purely on ad hoc basis and his selection was not according to the Rules - Respondent can be absorbed only after receipt the recommendation from Union Public Service Commission - Therefore, the period during which the respondent worked as Dy.SP in CBI on officiating basis prior to his absorption, cannot be counted - No infirmity in the Tribunal order, however, impugned HC order set aside - Appeal allowed.
