Masroor v State of Uttar Pradesh and Another

Supreme Court of India

27 April 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

D.K. Jain, R.M. Lodha

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 550; (2009) 14 SCC 286; 2009 (3) CTC 90; 2009 (3) JCC 1926; 2009 (2) RCR(Criminal) 847; 2009(6) SCALE 358; [2009] 6 S.C.R. 1030; 2009 (2) UC 1072

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Eye- Witness, Gunshot

Summary: Criminal - IPC, 1860, ss. 147, 148, 149, 307, 302 - Cr.P.C, 1973 -Order granting bail - Whether having regard to the nature of the offences the second respondent has been charged with; the background in which these were committed and the stage of the trial, the High Court was justified in granting bail to the said respondent and set him free? - Held, High Court has completely ignored the basic principles which are to be kept in view while dealing with an application filed under s. 439 of the Code for grant of bail and has thus, committed a manifest error in the matter of grant of bail to the second respondent, warranting interference by the Court - There is a need to indicate in such order reasons for prima facie concluding why bail was being granted particularly where the accused is charged of having committed a serious offence - Order granting bail to the second respondent is set aside - Appeal allowed.
