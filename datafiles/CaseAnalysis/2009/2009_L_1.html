(1) General Manager, Uttaranchal Jal Sansthan; (2) v (1) Laxmi Devi and Others; (2) Kishore Chandra

Supreme Court of India

15 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 676; (2009) 7 SCC 205; (2009) 2 SCC (L&S) 304; AIR 2009 SC 3121; 2009 (3) ESC 515; JT 2009 (13) SC 573; 2009 (5) MLJ(SC) 1213; 2009(8) SCALE 503; [2009] 9 S.C.R. 791; 2010 (2) SLR 630

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Ad Hoc Appointee, Temporary Or Ad Hoc, Executive Instructions, U.P. Water Supply and Sewerage Act, 1975

Summary: Service - U.P. Recruitment of Dependants of Government Servant Dying in Harness Rules, 1974, r. 2(a)(iii) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 309 Proviso - Compassionate appointment - 'Govt. Servant' - Whether dependent of a deceased who was not a permanent or temporary employee would be entitled to appointment on compassionate ground? - Held, No - Respondents were daily wage labourers who worked for around 11 to 12 years before their death; at the time of their death writ petition filed by them were pending wherein relief of regularization was claimed - Art. 309 proviso and rules perused, esp. r. 2(a) - Undisputed position that deceased were neither in permanent employment nor were appointed on temporary basis; at the relevant time a complete ban was imposed on appointment of daily wagers - Contention raised by respondents that their cases would come u/r. 2(a)(iii); question thereupon arose, (A) whether the appointment had been made in regular vacancy in such appointment? - No - Cl. (iii) provides for that, though not regularly appointed if a person had put in three years' continuous service in regular vacancy he would be a 'Govt. servant' - Court observed that having regard to the equality clause contained in arts. 14 and 16 such question is question of fact; as existence of a regular vacancy would mean a vacancy which occurred in a post sanctioned by the competent authority; for the said purpose the cadre strength of the category to which the post belongs is required to be taken into consideration; as a regular vacancy is which arises within the cadre strength; Court observed that it is a trite law that a regular vacancy cannot be filled up except in terms of the recruitment rules as also upon compliance of the constitutional scheme of equality; on perusing r. 2 esp. sub cl. (iii) with explanation, Court raised assumption that regular appointment was not necessarily to be taken recourse to and if that be so sub cl. (iii) of cl. (a) plus its explanation would be rendered unconstitutional; and provision of law which ex facie violates the equality clause and permits appointment through the side door being unconstitutional must be held to be impermissible and in any event requires strict interpretation; it was, therefore, for the respondents to establish that at the point of time the deceased employees were appointed, there existed regular vacancies; offers of appointment made in favour of the deceased have not been produced - Regular need vis-a-vis regular vacancy; contention in that regard made - Court observed that regular need in itself would not mean that there was a regular vacancy; a distinction must be made between a need of regular employees and existence of regular vacancies; HC's finding thereupon rejected - Landmark decision in 'Secy., State of Karnataka v. Umadevi (3), [ (2006) 4 SCC 1' referred and relied - Rendition of service for a long time does not confer permanency; it furthermore not a mode of appointment - Deceased held to be not covered in the definition of 'Govt. Servant' u/r. 2 of Rules - 'Equality clause cannot apply in a case where it arises out of illegality' - Impugned judgement being unsustainable, set aside - Appeals allowed.
