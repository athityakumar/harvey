Hari v State Of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

23 March 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Ganguly, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 343; (2009) 11 SCC 96; (2009) 3 SCC (Cr) 1254; JT 2009 (4) SC 234; 2009(4) SCALE 103; [2009] 4 S.C.R. 1012

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Pelting Stones, Non- Explanation, Self- Defence, Right Of Self- Defence, Partisan Evidence

Summary: Criminal - IPC, 1860, s. 302 - Appeal against conviction and sentence - Whether evidence of eye witnesses should be rejected just because they are related to the deceased? - Held, there was some enmity in view of the land dispute but that by itself is not a ground to discard the evidence of the witnesses, who are relatives when their evidence is cogent and credible - Does not find that any error was committed by the High Court in affirming the judgment of conviction passed by the Trial Court - Appeal dismissed.
