Md. Ibrahim and Others v State of Bihar and Another

Supreme Court of India

04 September 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, R.M. Lodha

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1095; (2009) 8 SCC 751; (2009) 3 SCC (Cr) 929; AIR 2010 SC (Supp) 347; 2010 CRLJ 2223; 2009 (4) JCC 2753; 2009(12) SCALE 250; [2009] 13 S.C.R. 1254

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss.420, 467, 471, 504 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s.482 - Forgery and Cheating - Criminal proceedings - Quashing of - Execution of two forged sale deeds by 1st appellant in favor of 2nd appellant, alleged - Trial Court framed charges against appellants - Appellants sought discharge - Trial Court dismissed appellant's application for discharge - Appellants filed application for quashing of criminal proceedings before HC - HC dismissed application - Legality - Held, execution of sale deed by appellant who was not the owner, would not be a false document - If executed document was not a false document, then there was no forgery - Respondent was not by purchaser, however 2nd appellant who was the purchaser, has no complaint - No any appellants tried to deceive respondent - Therefore, 1st appellant by act of executing sale deeds in favor of 2nd appellant or being purchaser 2nd appellant or being witness 3rd appellant or being scribe 4th appellant or being stamp vendor 5th appellant has not deceived respondent in any manner - Ingredients of cheating has not been proved - Appellant could defraud purchaser but respondent who was not the purchaser, cannot complain fraud against him - No intentional insult caused by appellant - However, ingredients of wrongful restraint and hurt has been shown in respondent's complaint before Trial Court - Therefore, charges against appellant with respect to offences u/ss.420, 467, 471 and 504 have been quashed - HC's impugned order set aside - Appeal partly allowed.
