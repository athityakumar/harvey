Y. Satyanarayan Reddy v Mandal Revenue Officer, Andhra Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

28 August 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Aftab Alam, B.N. Agrawal, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1059; (2009) 9 SCC 447; AIR 2010 SC 1440; 2009 (6) ALD(SC) 102; JT 2009 (12) SC 181; 2010 (2) MLJ(Crl) 495; 2009(12) SCALE 18; [2009] 13 S.C.R. 872

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Andhra Pradesh Land Grabbing (Prohibition) Act, 1982, ss.8 and 8(7) - Scope of power of Land Grabbing Tribunal and Special Court - Respondent filed application before Land Grabbing Tribunal-cum-District Judge stating that appellant had unauthorisedly encroached upon Govt. land and also stated that land in question was meant for public purpose - Respondent prayed to declare appellant as 'land grabber' and direct his eviction - Tribunal held appellant was in its unauthorised occupation and directed appellant to hand over possession of land to respondent - In appeal, Special Court upheld finding that disputed land was Govt. property - It however, did not agree with Tribunal that land was required for any public purpose and held that on depositing sum of Rs.15, 50, 000, appellant would be entitled to continue in possession of same - On appeal, HC set aside judgment passed by Special Court - Hence, present appeal - (A) Whether Tribunal or Special Court constituted under Act empowered to determine market value of grabbed land and direct complainant before it to receive such market value from land grabber in lieu of grabbed land? - Held, No - From plain and unambiguous language of S.8(7) it is impossible to deduce that it empowers and authorises Tribunal or Special Court to allow 'land grabber' to continue in his/her illegal possession of land on payment of its market value to land owner - Compensation envisaged by provision is not for continued illegal possession in future, but for wrongful possession of grabbed land by 'land grabber' in past - (B) Whether Tribunal or Special Court has power to examine question as to whether a particular grabbed land is required for public purpose or not? - HC held that neither scheme of Act nor provisions contemplated thereunder, empower Tribunal or Special Court, to go into question as to whether a particular land is required for public purpose or not, which domain is exclusively vested with competent Govt. - Held, no infirmity in HC judgment - (C) Whether appellant reliance on judgment in C.P. Roy vs. Special Court, under A.P. Land Grabbing Act and Anr. correct? - Held, in said judgment HC substituted words 'for wrongful possession', as appearing in s.8(7) for 'in lieu of handing over possession' of lands grabbed and thereby gave a meaning that is exactly opposite to what is envisaged u/s.8 - Appeal dismissed.
