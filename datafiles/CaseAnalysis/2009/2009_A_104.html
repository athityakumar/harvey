M. N. Ojha and Others v Alok Kumar Srivastav and Another

Supreme Court of India

21 August 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

B. Sudarshan Reddy, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 987; (2009) 9 SCC 682; AIR 2010 SC 201; 2009 (4) BC 424; 2009 CRLJ 4642; 2010 (1) DRTC 625; 2009 (4) JCC 2575; JT 2009 (10) SC 590; 2009 (4) MLJ(Crl) 999; 2010 (1) RCR(Criminal) 108; 2009(11) SCALE 573; [2009] 13 S.C.R. 444

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, s. 482 - Criminal proceedings - Quashing of - Respondent took a loan from bank with guarantee of Fixed Deposit Receipts (FDR) of guarantors of loan - Respondent made default in repayment - Bank lodged a FIR against respondents for cheating and mis-appropriation - Bank initiated recovery proceedings by appropriating FDR of one guarantor - Respondent-guarantor filed a criminal complaint against appellant-bank official alleging mis-conduct - Trial Court issued non-bailable warrant against appellant - Appellant filed application before HC for quashing of criminal proceedings - HC dismissed the application - Legality - Held, respondent filed complaint against appellant after 10 months of FIR filed by latter against former, hence it is a clear case of abuse of judicial process to harass the appellants - Appellant alleged to have colluded with the brother of respondent, however brother was not arrayed as parties - Complaint of respondent did not reveal commission of any offence by appellant - Appellant only took the steps to realize the amount due to bank as official of bank - Trial Court did not scrutinize the contents of complaint and material documents, illustrated non-application of mind - HC committed manifest error in disposing the petition of appellant for quashing the proceedings without even adverting to the basic facts of the case - Criminal proceedings instituted by the respondent maliciously for wrecking vengeance against appellant - Therefore, impugned HC order set aside and pending criminal case against appellant being quashed - Appeal allowed.
