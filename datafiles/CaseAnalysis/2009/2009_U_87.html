Attar Singh and Another v Union of India and Another

Supreme Court of India

04 August 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1075; (2009) 9 SCC 289; 2009(5) ALL MR 966; 2009 (77) ALR 173; 2010 (2) AWC 1495; 2010 (1) CLT(SC) 219; 2009 (4) RCR(Civil) 225; 2009 (108) RD 492; 2009(11) SCALE 57; [2009] 12 S.C.R. 315

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Value Of Agricultural Land, Mines And Quarries

Summary: Land & Property - Land acquisition - Compensation - Enhancement of - Land Acquisition Collector fixed market value of appellant's land by dividing it into 3 categories - Appellants filed Reference - Reference Court enhanced value - Appellants filed appeal before HC for further enhancement - DB enhanced value of appellant's property - Appellant contended that higher value has been fixed in a Lok Adalat Settlement at the instance of UOI in another case - Held, basis of settlement of said Lok Adalat was not known - Details of land in said Lok Adalat settlement were absent - Particulars regarding similarity of land in question with settled land in Lok Adalat was also absent - Therefore, said settlement had rightly not been made the basis for determining the value of land in question - HC determined fair market value based on its earlier decision arising out of same notification - No appeal has been preferred against the said judgment - Further, no contention has been raised that HC was failed to consider the well settled legal principles - Therefore, appellants should not be treated differently from parties of said judgment in respect of fixation of value - No merit - Appeal dismissed.
