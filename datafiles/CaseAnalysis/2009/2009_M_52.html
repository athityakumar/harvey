State Of A.P. v M. Radha Krishna Murthy

Supreme Court of India

06 March 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 303; (2009) 5 SCC 117; (2009) 2 SCC (Cr) 612; 2009 ALL MR (Cri) 1567; 2009 CRLJ 1896; 2009 (5) G.L.R. 3983; JT 2009 (3) SC 410; 2009 (2) RCR(Criminal) 334; 2009(3) SCALE 649; [2009] 4 S.C.R. 67

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Service; Socio-Economic

Keywords: Chloral Hydrate

Summary: Criminal - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 7, 13(2) r/w 13(1)(d) - Appeal against acquittal - Held, no rule of universal application was laid down that whenever a part of the case relating to demand and acceptance is not acceptable, the whole case would fail even if the case relating to trap, recovery of money and chemical test by the prosecution is established - Courts should not place reliance on decisions without discussing as to how the factual situation fits in with the fact situation of the decision on which reliance is placed - Appeal allowed.
