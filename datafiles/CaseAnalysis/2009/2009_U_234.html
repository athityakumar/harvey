Uttar Pradesh State Road Transport Corporation v Assistant Commissioner of Police (Traffic), Delhi

Supreme Court of India

12 February 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 171; (2009) 2 SCC (Cr) 164; (2009) 3 SCC 634; 2009 (2) ALD(SC) 130; 2009 (2) AWC 1492; JT 2009 (2) SC 553; 2009 (3) RCR(Civil) 68; 2009(2) SCALE 526; [2009] 2 S.C.R. 234

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Departmental Action, Permit Holder, Payment Of Tax, Exercise Of Jurisdiction, Transport Department, Road Transport Corporation Act, Speed Limit, Inter-State Permit, Reciprocal Agreement, Compounding Fee, Suspension Of Permit, Primary Permit, Countersigning Authority, Road Transport Corporation Act, 1951, Delhi Motor Vehicles Rules, 1993

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 - Interpretation of a judgment issuing directions of the norms of safety on the roads vis-'-vis the action taken by the first respondent pursuant to or in furtherance thereof - Whether even in terms of the said delegated power, the respondent could direct suspension of permit of a bus belonging to appellant and in respect whereof inter-State permit had been granted by the U.P. State Transport Authority? - Held, violation of the directions of the Court would merely entail the consequences which would mean that the respondent could proceed to take action only in accordance with law including the provisions contained in s. 88 - In case of any violation by any driver of any vehicle for which such carriage permit had been granted, it is the authorities of the State of U.P. who could take action - Countersigning State would not be entitled to suspend or cancel the permit of a vehicle wherefor a stage carriage permit has been granted by the State of U.P - Appeals allowed.
