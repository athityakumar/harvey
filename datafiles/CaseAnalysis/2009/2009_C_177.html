Commissioner of Income Tax, Delhi v Atul Mohan Bindal

Supreme Court of India

24 August 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1034; (2009) 9 SCC 589; AIR 2010 SC (Supp) 170; [2009] 317 ITR 1; 2009(11) SCALE 590; [2009] 13 S.C.R. 464

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Appeal By Special Leave

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, s.271(1)(c) - Penalty u/s. 271(1)(c) - Mandatory Penalty - Assessee filed return of his income - AO added an amount in respect of salary income earned in Singapore, additionally income from erstwhile employer and amount of interest to income declared by assessee in return and also imposed penalty u/s. 271(1)(c) of Act - Assessee accepted order of assessment but challenged order of penalty - Commissioner allowed appeal and held that assessee has neither concealed particulars of his income nor had he furnished any inaccurate particulars thereof - On appeal, Tribunal upheld order of Commissioner - HC relied upon its earlier decision in case of CIT vs. Ram Commercial Enterprises Ltd 1998 INDLAW DEL 447 and accepted view of Tribunal and Commissioner - Hence, present appeal - Whether HC decision could be upheld and penalty u/s. 271(1)(c) could be imposed on assessee? - Penalty u/s. 271(1)(c) is a civil liability - Willful concealment is not an essential ingredient for attracting civil liability - Held, HC relied upon its earlier decision in Ram Commercial Enterprises which has been approved by SC in Dillip N. Shroff case - However, Dillip N. Shroff has been held to be not laying down good law in UOI vs. Dharamendra Textiles Processors 2008 INDLAWSC 1837 - Dharamendra Textiles is explained by SC in Rajasthan Spining and Weaving Mills 2009 INDLAW SC 635 - Having thoughtfully considered matter, matter needs to be reconsidered by HC in light of decisions of SC in Dharamendra Textiles and Rajasthan Spinning and Weaving Mills - Revenue's appeal allowed - Matter remanded to HC
