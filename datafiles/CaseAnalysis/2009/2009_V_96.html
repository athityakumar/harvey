Uttaranchal Transport Corporation Limited v Vimla Devi and Others

Supreme Court of India

16 February 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 748; (2009) 2 SCC (Cr) 309; (2009) 4 SCC 377; 2009 (2) ACC 1; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 270; 2009 (2) AWC 1507; 2009 (1) CCC 79; JT 2009 (4) SC 480; 2009(2) SCALE 666; [2009] 2 S.C.R. 512

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Keywords: Appeal allowed partly, Assessment Of Damages, Fatal Accident Act, 1976

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, s. 173 - Fatal Accident Act, 1976 - Compensation - Enhancement of compensation - Appropriate multiplier - Deceased was aged 43 years at time of accident and was a hawker - Tribunal fixed Rs. 15,000/- p.a. as notional income of deceased and awarded compensation of Rs. 1,84,500/- with 9% p.a. from date of claim - On appeal, HC took notional income as Rs. 36,000/- and adopting multiplier of 15 enhanced compensation to Rs. 3,60,000/- Hence, present appeal - Whether enhancement of compensation made by HC could be upheld? - Held, considering age of deceased, multiplier as adopted appears to be on higher side - In view of parameters, appropriate to fix multiplier at 10 and rate of interest @ 6% p.a. - Appeal partly allowed
