Azhar Sultana v B. Rajamani and Others

Supreme Court of India

17 February 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 186; (2009) 17 SCC 27; AIR 2009 SC 2157; 2009 (4) ALD(SC) 21; 2009 (2) AWC 1546; 2009 (4) JhrCR(SC) 118; JT 2009 (2) SC 622; 2009 (2) RCR(Civil) 123; 2009 (107) RD 39; 2009(3) SCALE 159; [2009] 2 S.C.R. 537; 2009 (3) UC 1941

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Urban Land Ceiling and (Regulation) Act, 1970

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Specific Relief Act, 1963 - Suit for Specific Performance of Contract - Held, not only the original vendor but also a subsequent purchaser would be entitled to raise a contention that the plaintiff was not ready and willing to perform his part of contract - If the plaintiff has failed to establish that she had all along been ready and willing to perform her part of contract, it would not be necessary to enter into the question as to whether the defendant Nos.5 and 6 were bona fide subsequent purchasers for value without notice or not - Appeal disposed of
