U.O.I. v Bal Mukund & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

31 March 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Mukundakam Sharma, H.L. Dattu

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 754; (2009) 12 SCC 161; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 1811; 2009 ALL MR (Cri) 1570; 2009 CRLJ 2407; 2009 (2) RCR(Criminal) 574; 2009(4) SCALE 606; [2009] 5 S.C.R. 205; 2010 (1) UC 181

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Customs

Keywords: Samples

Summary: Criminal - Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985, ss. 42, 55 and 67 - Search leading to seizure of contraband goods - Trial court relying on or on basis of purported confessions made by respondents recorded a judgment of conviction and sentence - On appeal, HC set aside conviction - Hence, present appeal - Whether HC's order could be upheld? - Held, non-compliance of mandatory requirement in respect of search, seizure and taking of samples - There was doubt as to whether purported confessions by accused were voluntary - Moreover, said confessions were retracted subsequently - HC's order upheld - Appeal dismissed.
