Laxmi Kant Bajpai v Hazi Yaqoob and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 December 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Dattu, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1962; (2010) 4 SCC 81; AIR 2010 SC (Supp) 102; 2010 (2) ALD(SC) 16; 2010(1) ALL MR 469; 2010 (1) ESC 85; JT 2009 (15) SC 315; 2010 (1) RCR(Civil) 213; 2009(14) SCALE 447; [2009] 16 S.C.R. 526

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Keywords: Election Petition, General Elections, Electoral Roll, Right To Vote, District Election Officer, Grounds For Declaring Election To Be Void, Electoral Registration Officers, Dismissed, Statement Of Fact, False Statement, Ballot Box, Ballot Papers, House Of The People, Non-Disclosure, Ordinarily Resident, Scheduled Caste, Allegations Of Corrupt Practice, Corrupt Practice, Registration Of Electors, Registration of Electors Rules, 1960, Union Territories, Improper Reception Of Votes, Polling Station, Delimitation Act, 1972, Delimitation Act, 2002, U.P Municipalities Act, 1994

Summary: Election - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 83, 100(1)(d)(iii) and 100(1)(d) (iv) - Delimitation Act, 1972, s. 10(1) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 325 - Registration of Electors Rules, 1960 - Rejection of election petition - Non-disclosure of cause of action - Delimitation Order - Respondent was elected candidate in elections held from 381 Meerut Assembly Constituency to U.P. Legislative Assembly - Appellant filed election challenging respondent's elections - HC dismissed election petition - Hence, present appeal - (A) Change of electoral roll for constituency - Appellant submitted that delimitation can be carried out only by Delimitation Commission and yet in contravention of published order of delimitation, 21 colonies have been included in Meerut constituency - Held, in present case, there has been no re-delimitation of Meerut assembly constituency carried out by Delimitation Commission under auspices of Delimitation Act - Electoral roll published becomes electoral roll of constituency, and therefore electoral roll containing names of people residing in 21 localities, is final and valid electoral roll for Meerut assembly constituency - Alteration to electoral roll can only be brought about by following procedure prescribed in relevant rules - Courts cannot decide any issue relating to issuance or revision of an electoral roll - Power of delimitation of constituencies vests in Delimitation Commission and preparation of electoral rolls vests in Election Commission - In absence of a fresh order of delimitation issued by Delimitation Commission, Election Commission had no power to change electoral roll for constituency - Hence contention of appellant that delimitation is to be carried out only by Delimitation Commission fails as notifications issued by Delimitation Commission in 1973 and 1976 specify that extent of 381 Meerut constituency shall be same as Meerut municipality - (B) Material facts and cause of action - Held, election petition has to disclose all material facts on which election petitioner relies to establish existence of a cause of action - In absence of material facts and insufficient cause of action, election petition is liable to be dismissed - In present case, averments of appellants in election petition do not disclose any definite cause of action - Averments also do not disclose any material facts and, therefore, HC was right in summarily dismissing election petition - Appeal dismissed.
