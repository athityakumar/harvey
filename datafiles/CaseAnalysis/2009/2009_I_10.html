Shabana Bano v Imran Khan

Supreme Court of India

04 December 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Deepak Verma, B. Sudarshan Reddy

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1702; (2010) 1 SCC 666; AIR 2010 SC 305; 2010 ALL MR (Cri) 278; 2010 (1) Bom.C.R. 752; 2010 CRLJ 521; 2010 (1) JCC 146; 2010 (1) JhrCR(SC) 59; JT 2009 (14) SC 554; 2010 (1) MLJ(Crl) 701; 2010 (3) MPLJ 438; 2010 (1) RCR(Criminal) 158; 2010 (1) RLW 566; 2009(14) SCALE 331; [2009] 16 S.C.R. 190; 2010 (1) UC 114

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Women & Children

Keywords: Iddat Period, Woman, Divorced Woman, Wakf Act, 1954, Order For Payment Of Maintenance

Summary: Women & Children - Family & Personal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 125 - Muslim Women (Protection of Rights on Divorce) Act, 1986 - Family Courts Act, 1984 - Maintenance - Divorced Muslim women - Appellant was married to the respondent according to Muslim rites - Appellant filed petition u/s. 125, CrPC for maintenance - Respondent contended that the appellant and he had already divorced - Family Court allowed claim of the appellant towards maintenance from the date of institution of the petition till the date of divorce and further from the said date till the expiry of iddat period but amount of maintenance thereafter was denied - Whether a Muslim divorced wife would be entitled to receive the amount of maintenance from her divorced husband u/s. 125, CrPC? - Held, provisions of Family Act shall have overriding effect on all other enactments in force dealing with matters relating to maintenance - Even if a Muslim woman has been divorced, she would be entitled to claim maintenance from her husband u/s. 125, CrPC after the expiry of period of iddat also, as long as she does not remarry - Appellant's petition u/s. 125, CrPC would be maintainable before the Family Court as long as appellant does not remarry - Relied on Danial Latifi & Anr. v. Union of India 2001 INDLAW SC 20886 - Appeal allowed.
