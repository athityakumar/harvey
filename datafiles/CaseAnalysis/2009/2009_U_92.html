People's Union For Civil Liberties and Another v Union of India and Another

Supreme Court of India

23 February 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, B.N. Agrawal

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 204; (2009) 3 SCC 200; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 285; JT 2009 (3) SC 136B; 2009 (5) MLJ(SC) 587; 2009(3) SCALE 22; [2009] 2 S.C.R. 921

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Election; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Election Dispute, Freedom Of Speech And Expression, Council Of States, Speech And Expression, Ballot Papers, Right To Be Informed, Contesting Candidate, House Of The People, Election Matter, Casting Of Votes, Maintenance Of Secrecy Of Voting, Representation of the People (Second Amendment) Act, 2003

Summary: Election - (A) Whether the Election Commission is empowered to issue directions as ordered by the High Court? - (B) Whether a voter -- a citizen of this country -- has right to get relevant information, such as assets, qualification and involvement in offence for being educated and informed for judging the suitability of a candidate contesting election as MP or MLA? - Issue needs a clear exposition of law by a larger Bench - Order accordingly.
