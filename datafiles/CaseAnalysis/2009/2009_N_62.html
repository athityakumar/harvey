Anil Vasudev Salgaonkar v Naresh Kushali Shigaonkar

Supreme Court of India

20 August 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Dalveer Bhandari, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1037; (2009) 9 SCC 310; 2009 (5) Bom.C.R. 535; JT 2009 (10) SC 684; 2009 (4) RCR(Civil) 141; 2009(11) SCALE 556; [2009] 14 S.C.R. 10

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Keywords: Election Expenses, Grounds For Declaring Election To Be Void, Statement Of Fact, Allegations Of Corrupt Practice, Contents Of Petition, Union Territories, Procedure Before The High Court

Summary: Election - Representation of People Act, 1950, s.100 (1) (B) r/w s.123 (1), s.83 (1) (a) - Corrupt Practices - Allegation of - Appellant, returned candidate, dug bore wells in the constituency and provided ambulance services to villagers to induce them to vote for him, alleged - Appellant contended that the material facts relating to the alleged corrupt practices were not disclosed in the election petition before HC - Facts which are essential to clothe the election petitioner with complete cause of action are 'material facts' - Such material facts must be pleaded and failure to place even a single material fact amounts to disobedience of the mandate of s.83 (1) (a) of Act - Held, election petition of respondent did not contain the averment whether bore wells were dug with the consent or active knowledge of appellant - Failure to mention such imperative averment was fatal for respondent - Election petition of respondent lacked material facts and non-disclosure of cause of action - Therefore, election petition pending before HC stands dismissed - Appeal allowed.
