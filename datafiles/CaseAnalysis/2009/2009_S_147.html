Prithipal Singh v Satpal Singh (Dead) Through Its Lrs

Supreme Court of India

18 December 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Tarun Chatterjee, R.M. Lodha

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 2048; (2010) 2 SCC 15; AIR 2010 SC (Supp) 249; 2010 (78) ALR 730; 2010 (1) CLT 114; JT 2009 (15) SC 423; 2010 (1) RCR(Civil) 608B; 2010 (1) RCR(Rent) 53; 2009(14) SCALE 672; [2009] 16 S.C.R. 736

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Rent Control

Keywords: Delhi Rent Control Rules, 1959

Summary: Rent Control - Civil Procedure - Delhi Rent Control Act, 1958, ss. 14(1)(e), 25B, 25B(1) and 25(B)(4) - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 9 r. 13, O. 37 r. 4 and s. 151 - Jurisdiction of Additional Rent Controller - Ex parte order of eviction - Landlord-appellant filed an eviction petition u/s. 14(1)(e) of the Act before the Rent Controller - Summons was issued in compliance with s. 25(3)(a) of the Act - Application for leave to defend) was rejected by the Additional Rent Controller as it was filed 8 days late - Since the prayer for leave to defend the proceeding was rejected as a follow up action, an eviction order was passed by the Additional Rent Controller in favour of the landlord-appellant - Tenant-respondent's application for setting aside the ex parte order of eviction was allowed and the eviction proceeding was restored to its original file by the Additional Rent Controller - Additional Rent Controller also granted leave to contest the eviction proceeding in compliance with s. 25(B)(4) of the Act - On appeal, HC affirmed the order of the Additional Rent Controller - Hence, present appeal - Whether the Additional Rent Controller, exercising powers and jurisdiction under the Rent Act, which is a special Act, was justified in setting aside the order of eviction? - Held, s. 25B of the Act is a complete code by which the entire procedure to be adopted for eviction of a tenant on the ground of bona fide requirement filed by the landlord in respect of a premises, shall be followed - As sub-section (4) of s. 25B of the Act, tenant shall not be permitted to contest the prayer for eviction unless he files an affidavit before the Controller stating the ground on which he seeks to contest the application for eviction and obtains leave from the Controller - Power to entertain an application filed by the tenant-respondent for setting aside the order of eviction and restored the application for leave to contest the eviction proceeding was not conferred on the Additional Rent Controller - HC acted illegally and with material irregularity in the exercise of its jurisdiction in setting aside the order of eviction and in allowing the affidavit filed by the tenant for the purpose of defending the proceedings for eviction - Impugned order of the HC and Additional Rent Controller set aside - Order of eviction passed by the Additional Rent Controller stands restored - Appeal allowed.
