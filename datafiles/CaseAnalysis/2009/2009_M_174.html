Mahesh Choudhary v State Of Rajasthan & Anr

Supreme Court of India

03 March 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 279; (2009) 2 SCC (Cr) 332; (2009) 4 SCC 439; 2009 ALL MR (Cri) 1563; 2009 (3) JCC 2253; JT 2009 (3) SC 314; 2009 (3) RCR(Criminal) 717; 2009(4) SCALE 66; [2009] 3 S.C.R. 1005; 2009 (2) UC 841

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: False Entry

Summary: Criminal - Alleged criminal breach of trust and/or of cheating and forgery - Petition to quash criminal proceeding dismissed - Held, court shall ordinarily exercise the said jurisdiction, inter alia, in the event the allegations contained in the FIR or the Complaint Petition even if on face value are taken to be correct in their entirety, does not disclose commission of an offence - Although allegations contained in the complaint petition may disclose a civil dispute, the same by itself may not be a ground to hold that the criminal proceedings should not be allowed to continue - Appeal dismissed.
