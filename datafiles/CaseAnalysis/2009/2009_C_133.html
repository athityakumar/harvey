Commissioner Of Income Tax, Udaipur Rajasthan v Mcdowell & Co. Ltd

Supreme Court of India

08 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 668; (2009) 10 SCC 755; [2009] 314 ITR 167; 2009(8) SCALE 82; 2009(8) SCALE 266; 2009(8) SCALE 268; 2009(8) SCALE 269; 2009(8) SCALE 272; [2009] 8 S.C.R. 983

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Superannuation Fund, Rajasthan Excise Rules, 1962

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 35(1)(iv), 43B, 139(1) - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 73, 162, 366(28) - Deductions - Bottling fees - (A) Whether unpaid amount of bottling fee collected by State Government under Excise Rules has, on furnishing of bank guarantee, to be treated as actual payment and accordingly allowing deduction in respect of same u/s. 43B of Act? - Held, bottling fees for acquiring a right of bottling of IMFL which is determined under Excise Act and r. 69 of Rules is payable by assessee as consideration for acquiring exclusive privilege - It is neither fee nor tax but consideration for grant of approval by Government as terms of contract in exercise of its rights to enter a contract in respect of exclusive right to deal in bottling liquor in all its manifestations - Hence, such fees not covered u/s. 43B of Act and such deductions not allowed u/s. 43B of Act - (B) Whether furnishing of bank guarantee amounts to actual payment and fulfils conditions stipulated in s. 43B of Act? - Held, requirement of s. 43B of Act is actual payment and not deemed payment as condition precedent for making claim for deduction in respect of any of expenditure incurred by assessee during relevant previous year specified in s. 43B - Furnishing of bank guarantee cannot be equated with actual payment which requires that money must flow from assessee to public exchequer as required u/s. 43B - Bank guarantee is nothing but a guarantee for payment on some happening and that cannot be actual payment as required under s. 43B of Act for allowance as deduction in computation of profits - (C) Whether ITAT was justified in allowing depreciation on research and development assets which related to closest business of fast food division/unit of assessee company as such not used during previous year? - Held, whether research and development related to both units or only to fast food unit which is admittedly closed has not been examined in detail - Therefore, matter remanded to Assessing Officer to examine this aspect - Assessee permitted to place material in support of its claim that machinery was used for both units - No infirmity in Tribunal and HC order - Appeal disposed of.
