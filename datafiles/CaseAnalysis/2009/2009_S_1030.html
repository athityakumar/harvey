Sushil Kumar v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

01 September 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Deepak Verma, V.S. Sirpurkar

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1080; (2009) 10 SCC 434; AIR 2010 SC 832; 2010 ALL MR (Cri) 2292; 2009 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 2439; 2010 (1) MLJ(Crl) 691; 2009(12) SCALE 105; [2009] 13 S.C.R. 1082

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Murder Reference, Mitigating Circumstance, Mitigating Factor, Blood Stains, Previous Judgment

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s.302 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s.366 - Murder - Conviction - Death Reference - Rarest of rare case - Appellant murdered his wife and two children, alleged - Appellant was convicted and sentenced to death by Trial Court - Death sentence confirmed by HC - Appellant raised plea of alibi - Sustainability - Held, no serious material discrepancies in the evidences of PW2, PW3, PW4 and PW5 - Nothing has come on record that witnesses were having strained relations with appellant - Appellant has not offered any explanation as to where was he on fateful day - PW4 deposed that he had seen appellant was coming out of his own house after the incident - Plea of alibi has not found to be truthful - Appellant was guilty of commission of murder of his wife and 2 children, hence his conviction upheld - However, extreme poverty driven appellant to commit gruesome murder - Appellant was not a habitual offender - No witness has complained about appellant's bad or intolerable behavior in past - Appellant was about 35 years of age having fairly good chances of getting reformed - Therefore, case is not 'rarest of rare case', hence death sentence modified into life imprisonment - Appeal disposed of.
