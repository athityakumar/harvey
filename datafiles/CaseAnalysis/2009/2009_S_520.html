Shri Rajendra Ramchandra Kavalekar v State Of Maharashtra & Anr

Supreme Court of India

23 January 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Tarun Chatterjee, H.L. Dattu

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 36; (2009) 11 SCC 286; (2009) 3 SCC (Cr) 1330; AIR 2009 SC 1792; 2009 ALL MR (Cri) 954; 2009 CRLJ 1592; 2009 (1) JCC 617; JT 2009 (2) SC 125; 2009 (4) MLJ(Crl) 383; 2009 (1) RCR(Criminal) 885; 2009(1) SCALE 751; [2009] 1 S.C.R. 480

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice and Procedure - IPC, 1860, ss. 120(b), 420, 467, 468 and 471 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 13(1)(d) and 13(2) - CrPC, 1973, s. 177 - Complaint against appellant that he entered into criminal conspiracy with officials of the Ranchi University and had obtained fake degree certificates - Whether High Court of Judicature at Bombay was right in passing impugned order rejecting criminal writ petition filed by appellant on ground, that, the Special Judge (CBI), Ranchi has heard case and all the documents pertaining to the case are in custody and possession of Special Judge (CBI), Ranchi - The territorial jurisdiction of court with regard to criminal offence would be decided on the basis of place of occurrence of the incident and not on basis of where the complaint was filed and mere fact that FIR was registered in a particular State is not the sole criterion to decide that no cause of action has arisen even partly within the territorial limits of jurisdiction of another court - The venue of enquiry or trial is primarily to be determined by the averments contained in the complaint or charge sheet - As per s. 177 of CrPC every offence shall ordinarily be inquired into and tried by a court within whose local jurisdiction it was committed - Held, a bare perusal of the complaint filed by CBI, Ranchi would clearly go to show that the cause of action arose within the jurisdiction of Special Judge (CBI), Ranchi, the investigation is completed in Ranchi, all the records and the documents pertaining to complaint and the charge sheet are before Special Judge (CBI), Ranchi, and therefore, the HC of Bombay was perfectly justified in declining to entertain the writ petition filed by the appellant - Appeal dismissed.
