Shanti Sports Club and Another v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

25 August 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, B.N. Agrawal

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1048; (2009) 15 SCC 705; AIR 2010 SC 433; 2010(1) ALL MR 911; 2010 (1) AWC 253; 2010 (2) RCR(Civil) 900; 2009(11) SCALE 731; [2009] 13 S.C.R. 710

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Withdrawal From Acquisition

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1984, s.48(1) - Illegal construction - Notice for demolition - Illegal structures raised by appellant in govt acquired land - Delhi Development Authority (DDA) issued notification for demolition - Writ petition filed by appellant - Writ petition dismissed by Full Bench of HC with grant of liberty to make representation to authorities for de-notifying land in question - Non-demolition ordered by Minister for Urban Development - However, demolition ordered by successor-in-office - Legality - Held, noting of non-demolition in the file of erstwhile Minister was never translated into an order nor published in official gazette - Noting not even communicated to appellants or DDA - Matter left by Minister for his successor's consideration - Request for de-notification rejected by successor on the ground that land in question was acquired already by govt for a public purpose - Therefore, noting of Minister could not be relied upon by appellants for de-notification u/s. 48(1) - Non-withdrawal of acquisition did not suffer any vice of discrimination or arbitrary exercise of power or non-application of mind - Possession of land came to appellant more than 10 years after finalization of acquisition proceedings, therefore, protection of structures could not be raised on basis of plea of equity - Sale transaction of owners of acquired land to appellant was void - Illegal constructions could not be protected by court by nullifying acquisition proceedings of govt - Appeals dismissed.
