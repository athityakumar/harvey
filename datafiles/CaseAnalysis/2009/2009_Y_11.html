Yumnam Ongbi Tampha Ibemma Devi v Yumnam Joykumar Singh & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

06 March 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, V.S. Sirpurkar, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 299; (2009) 4 SCC 780; 2009 (75) ALR 170B; 2009 (2) AWC 1442; 2009 (3) JhrCR(SC) 10; JT 2009 (4) SC 307; 2009 (4) MPLJ 1; 2009 (2) RCR(Civil) 474; 2009 (107) RD 48; 2009(3) SCALE 571; [2009] 4 S.C.R. 157; 2009 (3) UC 1959

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Family & Personal; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Disposal Of Property, Mental Capacity

Summary: Family & Personal - Indian Succession Act, 1925 - Application for granting letters of administration with the Will - Held, since a Will is required by law to be attested, execution has to be proved in the manner laid down in section and the Evidence Act which requires that at least one attesting witness has to be examined for the purpose of proving the execution of such a document - Attesting witness should speak not only about the testator's signature or affixing his mark to the will but also that each of the witnesses had signed the will in the presence of the testator - No issue was framed regarding the validity of the will - Appeal dismissed.
