Krishna Kumar Sharma v Rajesh Kumar Sharma

Supreme Court of India

27 March 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 366; (2009) 11 SCC 537; AIR 2009 SC 3247; 2009 (3) AWC 2237; 2009 (3) CTC 891; JT 2009 (4) SC 533; 2009 (2) KLT 149; 2009 (4) MLJ(SC) 309; 2009 (2) RCR(Civil) 807; 2009 (108) RD 616; 2009(5) SCALE 286; [2009] 4 S.C.R. 1223; 2009 (3) UC 2000

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Family & Personal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Succession Act, 1925 - Indian Limitation Act, 1963, art. 137 - Whether art. 137 of the Limitation Act is applicable? - Held, art. 137 is clearly applicable to the petition for grant of Letters of Administration - Rightly observed by the High Court in such proceedings application merely seeks recognition from the Court to perform a duty because of the nature of the proceedings - Though the nature of the petition has been rightly described by the High Court, it was not correct in observing that the application for grant of probate or letters of Administration is not covered by art. 137 in view of what has been stated in Kerala State Electricity Board, Trivandrum v. T.P. Kunhaliumma - Remitted the matter to High Court - Appeal partly allowed.
