State of Uttar Pradesh and Others v Committee of Management, Mata Tapeshwari

Supreme Court of India

02 December 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1787; (2010) 1 SCC 639; AIR 2010 SC 402; 2010 (2) AWC 2079; 2010 (1) ESC 44; JT 2009 (14) SC 605; [2009] 15 S.C.R. 1276; 2010 (1) SLR 32

Case Digest

Subject: Education

Keywords: Part-Time Instructors, Part-Time Teachers, U.P. Basic Education Act, 1972, U.P. Recognized Junior High Schools (Payment of Salaries of Teachers and Other Employees) Act, 1978, U.P. High Schools and Intermediate College (Payment of Salaries of Teachers and Other Employees) Act, 1971

Summary: Education - U.P. Recognized Junior High Schools (Payment of Salaries of Teachers & Other Employees), 1978 - Grant-in-aid Scheme - Respondent institutions denied benefit of grant in aid Scheme for their Junior High School section on ground that they had not been granted recognition as Junior High Schools prior to cut off date - State Government inserted s. 13A in Act, 1978 to protect interests to those institutions which had been upgraded, but were already receiving grant-in-aid for Junior High School section, by creating class within a class - On appeal, Single Judge held that creation of a class within a class was not only unfair and unreasonable but also offended provisions of Article 14 of Constitution of India - Appeal against impugned judgement dismissed by Division Bench - Hence, present appeal - Whether Junior High Schools, which had previously not been brought within ambit of grant-in-aid Scheme, but had been allowed to upgrade their institutions to impart education at High School and Intermediate College level, would stand disentitled to benefit of said scheme in view of clause 2(13) of Government Order dt. 7-09-2006? - Held, application of 1978 Act only to educational institutions which received grant-in-aid prior to 30-06-1984, has been rightly held to be arbitrary by HC - Such provision is in violation of equality clause enshrined in Article 14 of Constitution. - If it was the intention of State Government to extend benefit of grant-in-aid Scheme to 1000 unaided permanently recognized (A Class) Junior High Schools, then it would not be fair, to exclude such unaided institutions which besides imparting education at Junior High School level were also imparting education, either at Primary or Higher Secondary level, inasmuch as, they too continued to have Junior High Schools imparting education - Object sought to be achieved by notification of 9th September, 2006, has no intelligible nexus with object it wishes to achieve - Orders of Single Judge and Division Bench upheld - Petitions dismissed.
