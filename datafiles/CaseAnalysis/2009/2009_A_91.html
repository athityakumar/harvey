Akhtar & Ors v State Of Uttaranchal

Supreme Court of India

09 April 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Mukundakam Sharma, S.B. Sinha, B. SUDARSHAN REDDY

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 429; (2009) 13 SCC 722; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 1676; 2010 (4) RCR(Criminal) 621; 2009(5) SCALE 447; [2009] 5 S.C.R. 771

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Semi-Digested Food, Eye- Witness

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 148, 302 r/w149, 307 r/w149 and 324 r/w149 - Culpable homicide amounting to murder - Rioting with deadly weapon - Unlawfully assembly - Common object - Testimony of injured eye-witnesses - Interestedness of eye witnesses on ground of they being relatives of deceased - Land feud - Accused eight in numbers - Armed with deadly weapons and firearms attacked deceased persons alongwith two others - Acquittal by Trial Court on ground that prosecution was unable to prove the case beyond reasonable doubt - High Court in appeal convicted all the accused persons except two of them as they were dead pending appeal - Conviction by HC reversing acquittal by Trial Court whether proper? - Genuineness of injury reports, post mortem reports and of Ballistic Expert's report, undisputed - Two eye witnesses were relatives of the deceased persons and were also injured in the attack - Evidence of injured witnesses has greater evidentiary value and unless compelling reasons exist, their statements are not to be discarded lightly, State of M.P. v. Mansingh, 2003 (10) SCC 414 - Fact that the witness was injured at the time and in the same occurrence lends support to the testimony that the witness was present during occurrence and he saw the happening with his own eyes - Merely because the witnesses in question were related to the deceased cannot be a ground for non-acceptance of their evidence, which otherwise was found to be trustworthy, on the ground that they themselves were got injured - It is extremely difficult to believe that the injured witnesses who themselves got injured and whose close relatives lost their lives would shield the real culprits and name somebody else only due to some enmity - Evidences adduced in instant case, despite minor discrepancies, clearly establish the involvement of the accused appellant on the date of occurrence - Impugned judgement of conviction and sentence, upheld - Appeal dismissed.
