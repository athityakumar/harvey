(1) Commissioner of Income Tax, Shimla; (2) Green v (1) Green World Corporation, Parwanoo; (2) Income

Supreme Court of India

06 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 644; (2009) 7 SCC 69; [2009] 314 ITR 81; 2009(7) SCALE 721; [2009] 8 S.C.R. 175

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Appeals Disposed Of

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 33(2), 133A, 148, 263, 234B/C, 271(1) (c ) - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 136, 142 - Jurisdiction - Deduction u/s 80IA/IB - Appellant set up two units for manufacturing exercise books, writing pads, etc. at Parwanoo in State of Himachal Pradesh - Started filing income tax returns showing huge profits of about 49% to 66% - Assessing officer conducted survey u/s. 133A and after verification submitted return submitted by assessee - It appears that Assessing Officer passed order on direction of Commissioner - Commissioner on whose dictates order of assessment purported to have been passed was transferred - His successor issued notice to assessee u/s. 263 of Act that said order of assessment dated 19.12.2002 was prejudicial to interests of Revenue - Appellant filed an application for transfer of its cases from CIT (Shimla) to CIT (Delhi) - CIT (Shimla) passed an order that Assessing Officer while finalizing Assessment had not examined case properly and held that as assessee does not fulfill many of conditions for being entitled to deduction u/s 80IA/IB, no part of total income, not even one estimated @ 5% of turnover at Parwanoo, would be entitled for deduction u/s 80IA/IB - Assessee filed appeal which was allowed by ITAT setting aside order of CIT (Shimla) on jurisdictional issue alone - Pursuant thereto, notices u/s. 148 of Act were issued to Assessee for Assessment Years 1996-97 to 1999-2000, 2001-2002 and 2002-2003 - Assessee questioned legality of notice u/s. 148 of Act by filing a Writ Petition before Himachal Pradesh HC on ground that case was transferred from jurisdiction of CIT (Shimla) to that of CIT (Delhi) with effect from 5.9.2005 - CIT (Shimla) preferred an appeal before HC u/s. 260A of Act - HC while allowing Appeal filed by CIT (Shimla) dismissed writ petition filed by assessee - Assessee filed appeal in which it contended that having regard to order of transfer passed by CBDT transferring case from CIT (Shimla) to CIT (Delhi), CIT (Shimla) had no locus standi to maintain appeal preferred before HC u/s. 260A of Act - (A) Whether order of assessment was passed at instance of Higher Authority? - Held, CIT (Shimla), has specified a number of reasons in support of its order that Assessing Officer passed an order at instance of higher authority which is illegal - They might have proceeded bona fide but order of assessment passed by Assessing Officer on dictates of higher authorities being wholly without jurisdiction, it was a nullity - (B) Whether CIT (Shimla) had no locus standi to maintain appeal preferred before HC u/s. 260 A of Act? - Held, CIT (Shimla) could have preferred an appeal only by time when administrative decision could be taken by him to prefer an appeal - Right to prefer an appeal arose on date on which Tribunal passed an order - In view to do complete justice between parties, assessment be reopened by Commissioner of Income-tax, Delhi -VII - Appeals disposed of.
