Central Bank of India v State of Kerala and Others

Supreme Court of India

27 February 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, B.N. Agrawal, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 224; (2009) 4 SCC 94; [2010] 153 Comp Cas 497; 2009 (2) CompLJ 36; 2009 (6) CTC 656; 2009 (1) DRTC 697; JT 2009 (3) SC 216; 2009(3) SCALE 451; [2009] 3 S.C.R. 735; [2009] 21 VST 505

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Practice & Procedure; Sales Tax

Keywords: Legal Notice, Suit For Injunction, Cash Credit, Demand Notice, Withdrawal, Sale Price, Commercial Transaction, Sales Tax Authorities, Entertain And Decide, Industrial Reconstruction Bank Of India, Recovery Officer, Save As Otherwise Provided, Industrial Reconstruction Bank Of India Act, 1984, Legally Recoverable, Public Sector Banks, Cash Credit Facility, Shall Not Be Bound, Letters Of Credit, Employees' Provident Funds and Miscellaneous Provisions Act, 1952, Service Of Demand Notice, Attachment And Sale, Grammatical Construction, Legislative Entry, Rajasthan Sales Tax Act, 1954, Recording Of Reasons, Small Industries Development Bank Of India Act, 1989, Bihar Finance Act, 1981, Private Transfer, Presidency-Towns Insolvency Act, 1909, Procedure For Recovery, Business Activity Undertaken By Bank, Communication Of Reasons, Maharashtra Cooperative Society Act, 1960

Summary: Banking & Finance - Sales Tax - Whether provisions of State legislations by which first charge has been created on the property of the dealer or such other person, who is liable to pay sales tax etc., are inconsistent with the provisions contained in the Recovery of Debts Due to Banks and Financial Institutions Act, 1993 for recovery of 'debt' and the Securitisation and Reconstruction of Financial Assets and Enforcement of Security Interest Act, 2002 for enforcement of 'security interest'? - Held, DRT Act and Securitisation Act do not create first charge in favour of banks, financial institutions and other secured creditors and the provisions contained in s. 38C of the Bombay Act and s. 26B of the Kerala Act are not inconsistent with the provisions of the DRT Act and Securitisation Act so as to attract non obstante clauses contained in s. 34(1) of the DRT Act or s. 35 of the Securitisation Act - Since in terms of s. 26B of the Kerala Act, the State has got prior charge over the property of the dealer and the facts of the case show that the revenue authorities had already attached the property, there is no valid ground to interfere with the order passed by the Division Bench - Appeals dismissed.
