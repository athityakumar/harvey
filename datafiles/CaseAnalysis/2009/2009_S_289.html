Shub Karan Bubna @ Shub Karan Prasad Bubna v Sita Saran Bubna and Others

Supreme Court of India

21 August 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, B. SUDARSHAN REDDY

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1108; (2009) 9 SCC 689; 2010 (2) AWC 1325; 2010 (1) JhrCR(SC) 172; 2009 (8) MLJ(SC) 921; 2009 (4) RCR(Civil) 304; 2010 (109) RD 541; 2009(12) SCALE 259; [2009] 14 S.C.R. 40

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Keywords: Final Decree For Foreclosure, Partition Of Property, Mortgage Suits, Partition Of Immovable Property, Separate Possession Of a Share, Special Leave Petition dismissed

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O.20, r.18 - Limitation Act, 1963, art.137 - Final decree - Limitation period - Suit for partition - Preliminary decree passed - First respondent filed application for drawing up a final decree - Petitioner filed an application to drop final decree proceedings as it was barred by limitation - Said application was dismissed - Whether there is no limitation for an application for affecting actual partition/division in accordance with preliminary decree? - Where an application does not invoke jurisdiction of court to grant any fresh relief based on a new cause of action, but merely reminds or requests court to do its duty by completing remaining part of pending suit, there is no question of any limitation - Such an application in a suit which is already pending, which contains no fresh or new prayer for relief is not one to which Limitation Act, 1963 would apply - It is only a reminder to court to do its duty to appoint a Commissioner, get a report, and draw a final decree in pending suit so that suit is taken to its logical conclusion - Initiation of final decree proceedings does not depend upon an application for final decree for initiation (unless local amendments require same) - Held, CPC does not contemplate filing an application for final decree - Therefore, when a preliminary decree is passed in a partition suit, proceedings should be continued by fixing dates for further proceedings till a final decree is passed - It is duty and function of court - Performance of such function does not require a reminder or nudge from litigant - Application filed in this case for drawing up of a final decree, was rightly held to be not subject to any period of limitation - Special leave petition dismissed
