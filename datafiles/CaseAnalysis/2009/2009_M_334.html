Mrs. Shilpa Aggarwal v Mr. Aviral Mittal & Anr

Supreme Court of India

09 December 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 2187; (2010) 1 SCC 591; 2010 CRLJ 844; 2010 (1) CTC 359; 2010 (1) JCC 474; JT 2009 (15) SC 188; 2010 (2) MLJ(Crl) 313; 2010 (1) RCR(Civil) 231; 2009(14) SCALE 511; [2009] 16 S.C.R. 287

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Family & Personal - Practice & Procedure - Custody of the minor - Appellant was married to the Respondent and settled her matrimonial home in U.K. - A girl child was born from the said marriage in U.K. - Being born in the U.K., the child acquired British citizenship and was the holder of a British passport, although, her parents continued to hold Indian passports - Soon after the birth of the child, some disagreements developed between the couple - Appellant alongwith the minor child came to India - Respondent filed writ petition before HC seeking direction that the child be returned to the jurisdiction of the Courts of England and Wales on the ground that the parties had set up their matrimonial home in U.K. - HC directed the appellant to return the minor child to the jurisdiction of the U.K. - Hence, instant appeal - Whether a minor child, who was born in U.K. of Indian parents and was a British citizen by birth, can be kept in the custody of the mother who was now residing in India? - Held, minor child been declared a ward of the English Court till she attains majority - Hence, HC had not committed error in relying on the doctrine of Comity of Courts since the question of what is in the interest of the minor still has to be considered by the U.K. Court - No interference to the impugned order of the HC - Appeal dismissed.
