Rajinder Singh Bhatti and Others v State of Haryana and Others

Supreme Court of India

03 March 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 232; (2009) 11 SCC 480; AIR 2009 SC 2232; 2009 (3) AWC 3062; JT 2009 (3) SC 206; 2009 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 839; 2009 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 861; 2010 (1) MLJ(SC) 158; 2009 (2) RCR(Civil) 693; 2009 (107) RD 649; 2009(3) SCALE 525; [2009] 3 S.C.R. 964

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Withdrawal From Acquisition

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - (A) Whether in view of the decision of the government in not approving the award proposed by the Collector, the award could not be made within the period of two years from the date of publication of declaration and the acquisition of land lapsed, would such lapse of acquisition proceedings amount to withdrawal from the acquisition by the State Government under s. 48(1) - Held, lapse of acquisition proceedings in the circumstances under s. 11-A cannot and would not amount to withdrawal from acquisition by the government under s. 48(1) - (B) Whether the decision of the State Government for withdrawal from the acquisition under s. 48 (1) is mandatorily required to be published in the official gazette ? - Held, Act provides for the publication of notification and declaration under ss. 4, 6 in official gazette - Obviously the withdrawal from land acquisition proceedings by taking resort to s. 48(1) also must be in the like manner - Decision of the government for withdrawal from acquisition has to be published in the official gazette - Appeals dismissed.
