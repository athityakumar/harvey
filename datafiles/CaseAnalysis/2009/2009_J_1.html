Himachal Pradesh Housing and Urban Development v (1) Janak Gupta; (2) R.P. Kapur; (3) S.K. Monga

Supreme Court of India

26 March 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

D.K. Jain, R.M. Lodha

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1743

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection

Keywords: Uniform Rate

Summary: Consumer Protection - Interest - Uniform rate - Legality - National Consumers Disputes Redressal Commission awarded to complainants, interest @ 18 % p.a on the amount deposited for one year, on account of delay in delivery of possession of flats allotted to applicants - Hence instant appeal.

Held, Ghaziabad Development Authority v. Balbir Singh-I, Indlaw SC 941 relied on. The order of the Commission, awarding interest at the rate of 18% per annum cannot be sustained. With regard to facts and the circumstances of the instant case, award of interest @ 12% p.a would meet the ends of justice. Appeals partly allowed.

Ratio: Award of interest is to be considered on the facts and circumstances of each case and interest cannot be awarded in all cases at a uniform rate
