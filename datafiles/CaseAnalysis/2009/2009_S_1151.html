Satbir Singh and Others v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

25 February 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 221; (2009) 13 SCC 790; AIR 2009 SC 2163; JT 2009 (3) SC 293; 2009 (2) MLJ(Crl) 1063; 2009 (2) RCR(Criminal) 232; 2009 (1) RLW 601; 2009(3) SCALE 439; [2009] 3 S.C.R. 406

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Self-Inflicted, Semi Digested Food

Summary: Criminal - IPC, 1860, ss. 302 r/w 149, 148, 323/149 - Appeal against conviction and sentence - Held, only because the witnesses are not independent ones may not by itself be a ground to discard the prosecution case - If the prosecution case has been supported by the witnesses and no cogent reason has been shown to discredit their statements, a judgment of conviction can certainly be based thereupon - If all the witnesses could not attribute specific roles to each of the accused, the same is natural - Prosecution has been able to establish the existence of common object amongst all the accused persons - Appeal dismissed.
