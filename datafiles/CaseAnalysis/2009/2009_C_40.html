C. M. Girish Babu v Central Bureau of Investigation, Cochin, High Court of Kerala

Supreme Court of India

24 February 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

B. SUDARSHAN REDDY, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 210; (2009) 2 SCC (Cr) 1; (2009) 3 SCC 779; AIR 2009 SC 2022; JT 2009 (3) SC 32; 2009 (3) MLJ(Crl) 341; 2009 (2) RCR(Criminal) 134; 2009(3) SCALE 107; [2009] 2 S.C.R. 1021

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Socio-Economic

Keywords: Illegal Gratification

Summary: Criminal - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, s. 7, 13 (2) r/w 13(1) (d) - Appeal against conviction and sentence - Whether the appellant had demanded any amount as gratification to show any official favour and whether the said amount was paid by PW-10 and received by the appellant as consideration for showing such official favour? - Held, burden of proof placed upon the accused person against whom the presumption is made under s. 20 of the Act is not akin to that of burden placed on the prosecution to prove the case beyond a reasonable doubt - Appellant has proved his case by the test of preponderance of probability and amount was not taken by the appellant as gratification - Prosecution failed in establishing the guilt of the accused beyond reasonable doubt that the appellant received any gratification - Appeal allowed.
