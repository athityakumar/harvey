Ram Sukh v Dinesh Aggarwal

Supreme Court of India

18 September 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

D.K. Jain, H.L. Dattu

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1163; (2009) 10 SCC 541; AIR 2010 SC 1227; JT 2009 (12) SC 352; 2009 (4) RCR(Civil) 585; 2009(12) SCALE 638; [2009] 14 S.C.R. 836

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Keywords: Election Petition, Election Tribunal, Grounds For Declaring Election To Be Void, Dismissed, Returning Officer, Non-Disclosure, Allegations Of Corrupt Practice, Contents Of Petition, Corrupt Practice, Security For Costs, Trial Of Election Petitions, Election Matter, Presentation Of Petitions, Polling Station, Procedure Before The High Court

Summary: Election - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 80, 83(1)(a), 86, 87, 100(1)(b) and 100(1)(d) 100(1)(d)(iv) and 116A - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. VI, rr. 16 and 17 and O. VII, r. 11 - Election petition - Maintainability - Non-disclosure of material facts - Election to State Legislative Assembly of Uttaranchal - Election petitioner lost election challenged election of first respondent by filing election petition u/s. 80 r/w s. 100(1)(b) and (d) of Act - First respondent filed application under O. VI, rr. 16 and 17 and O. VII, r. 11 of CPC r/w s. 86 of Act raising preliminary objection to maintainability of petition - HC upheld preliminary objection raised by first respondent and dismissed election petition mainly on ground that it did not comply with mandatory requirement of furnishing material facts alleged/relied upon and was not supported by an affidavit in prescribed form - Hence, present appeal - (A) Whether election petition suffered from vice of non-disclosure of material facts as stipulated in s. 83(1)(a) of Act? - Case of election petitioner is confined to alleged violation of s. 100(1)(d)(iv) - Held, for purpose of s. 100(1)(d)(iv), it was necessary for election petitioner to aver specifically in what manner election result of first respondent was materially affected due to facts alleged - Such averment missing in election petition - HC was justified in coming to conclusion that statement of material facts in election petition was completely lacking and petition was liable to be rejected at threshold on that ground - (B) Whether submission of appellant that HC should not have exercised its power either under O. VI, r. 16 or O. VII, r. 11 of CPC to reject election petition at threshold can be accepted? - Held, by virtue of s. 87 of Act, provisions of CPC apply to trial of an election petition and, therefore, in absence of anything to contrary in Act, court trying an election petition can act in exercise of its power under CPC, including O. VI, r. 16 or O. VII, r. 11 of CPC - Appeal dismissed.
