Vijay Dhanji Chaudhary v Suhas Jayant Natawadkar

Supreme Court of India

30 October 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1498; (2010) 1 SCC 166; 2009 (77) ALR 653; 2010 (3) AWC 2540; 2010 (3) Bom.C.R. 689; 2010 (3) CTC 782; JT 2009 (15) SC 472; 2010 (1) MLJ(SC) 156; 2009(13) SCALE 652; [2009] 16 S.C.R. 518

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Supreme Court Rules, 1966 - Special Leave Petition - Restoration of - Absence of the role of Advocate-on-Record - Whether an Advocate-on-Record can file appearances as mere name-lender for facilitating filing of petitions by others, without performing any of the functions associated with an Advocate-on-Record - In order to enforce discipline in the working of advocates-on-record and to avoid the misuse of the system, and to ensure that the court has the benefit of effective assistance of the Advocates-on-record, a solution has to be found - Notice issued to the Advocates-on-record Association and the Supreme Court Bar Association to assist SC to find appropriate solutions and provides necessary checks and balances - Order accordingly.
