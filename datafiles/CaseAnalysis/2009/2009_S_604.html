State Of Rajasthan And Another. v M/S. Ferro Concrete Construction Private. Limited.

Supreme Court of India

22 April 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 2052; (2009) 12 SCC 1; 2009(8) SCALE 753; [2009] 10 S.C.R. 31

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Summary: (A) Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration Act, 1940 - Award - Grounds for challenge - Held, an award is not open to challenge on the ground that the arbitrator has reached a wrong conclusion or failed to appreciate the facts - Further, while considering the challenge to an award, the court would not sit in appeal over the award nor re-appreciate the evidence for the purpose of finding whether on the facts and circumstances, the award in question could have been made - Appeal partly allowed.

(B) Arbitration & ADR - Mobilization advance - Delay in release of - Loss of profit and overheads - Appellant entered into a contract with respondent-contractor for supply of pipelines - Appellant allegedly delayed releasing the mobilization advance to respondent - Respondent contended that the delay in releasing the mobilization advance caused loss of production - Held, mobilization advance had to be released only against a bank guarantee to be furnished by the respondent - However, respondent failed to furnish the bank guarantee within the stipulated time, therefore, respondent was only responsible for the delay, hence question of compensating the respondent under the head of loss of profit and overhead does not arise - Therefore, compensation awarded by the arbitrator under the head of loss of profit and overhead is set aside - Appeal partly allowed.

(C) Arbitration & ADR - Mobilization advance - Delay in releasing the mortgage - Appellant entered into a contract with respondent-contractor for supply of pipelines - Appellant released the mobilization advance to respondent and respondent mortgaged its pipe manufacturing unit in favour of the appellant - Respondent contended that the appellant had delayed in releasing the machinery installed in the factory which caused the machinery to be kept idle, therefore, he was entitled to reimbursement for loss of production - Held, respondent deposited the title deeds relating to the plant by way of mortgage in terms of the contract and specifically agreed that the original deeds would remain in deposit with the employer till the entire mobilization advance was repaid with interest - Though a mortgage security was created on the plant, it continued to be in the possession, enjoyment and control of the respondent, as the appellant did not take over physical possession of the plant at any point of time - Therefore, arbitrator, at best, can direct return of the documents of title to the respondent and cannot direct payment of damages, hence award of compensation for delay in releasing the mortgaged plant and machinery is set aside - Appeal partly allowed.

(D) Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration Act, 1940 - Interest Act, 1978, s. 3(1) - Damages - Award of interest - Held, award of interest at the rate of 18 per cent in an award governed by the 1940 Act is an error apparent on the face of record - Award of interest is governed by provisions of the Interest Act - Interest cannot be exceeded the current rate of interest which means the highest of the maximum rates at which interest may be paid on different classes of deposits by different classes of scheduled banks in accordance with the directions given or issued to banking companies generally by the Reserve Bank of India under the Banking Regulation Act, 1949 - Therefore, pre-reference interest should be only at the rate of 9 per cent per annum and it is appropriate to award the same rate of interest even by way of pendente lite interest and future interest up to date of payment - Appeal partly allowed.
