New Bus Stand Shop Owners Association v Corporation Of Kozhikode & Another

Supreme Court of India

18 September 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Ganguly, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1458; (2009) 10 SCC 455; JT 2009 (13) SC 164; 2009 (4) KLT 476; 2009 (8) MLJ(SC) 1175; 2010 (1) RCR(Civil) 404; 2009(13) SCALE 510; [2009] 14 S.C.R. 793

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Kerala Municipalities Act, 1994

Summary: Municipalities & Local Governments - Kerala Municipalities Act, 1994, s. 215 - Kerala Stamp Act, 1959, s. 2(1)(iii) - Licence or lease - Determination of - Whether agreement under which the appellant-Association has been granted shops and is carrying on business is an agreement for lease or it is a licence? - Corporation contended that the said licences should be treated as lease and stamp duty payable on lease - Licences were issued to the appellant-association u/s. 215 of Municipalities Act - Corporation retained exclusive possession of shops - Held, intention of the parties in present case is to create a licence and not a lease and right of exclusive possession was retained by Corporation - Stamp duty on licence agreement should be governed by Entry 5(c) of Stamp Act, which is a residuary Clause in the Schedule and not by Entry 33 - Appeal allowed.
