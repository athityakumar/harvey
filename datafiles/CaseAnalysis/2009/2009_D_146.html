Divisonal Manager, Rajasthan S. R. T. C. v Kamruddin

Supreme Court of India

12 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 2301; 2009 (4) SLR 177

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Punishment, Wages, Award, Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, Leave, Standing, Appeal, Dismissal, Road Transport Corporations Act, 1950, Misconduct, Employee

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s.11A - Misconduct - Termination - Quantum of punishment - Respondent was appointed as conductor by appellant/ corporation as daily wager for specific period on probation for period of two years by offer - On probation period, inspections were carried out in buses, he was found guilty of having not issued tickets to two passengers and carrying 180 kilograms luggage without ticket - Disciplinary proceeding was initiated against respondent on basis of agreement with them and he was terminated from his services - Respondent approached Tribunal against said dismissal was allowed which was challenged by petitioner before HC was dismissed - Hence instant appeal.

Held, it is not case where misconduct against respondent had not been proved. It is also not case where domestic enquiry was found to have been conducted in unfair manner or contrary to the principles of natural justice. Services of respondent had been terminated while period of probation was not over. As conductor, his performance during period of probation was found to be unsatisfactory. It is not in dispute that disciplinary proceeding was initiated against him while he was found to have committed similar misconduct for fifth time. It is also beyond any doubt or dispute that he had also been served with letter of warning. Power of Tribunal and Industrial Tribunal in terms of s.11A of 1947 Act to interfere with quantum of punishment although cannot be denied, but it is also well settled principle of law that said power should be exercised judiciously. It is misplaced sympathy by Tribunal in such cases when on checking it is found that Bus Conductors have either not issued tickets to large number of passengers, though they should have, or have issued tickets of lower denomination knowingfully well correct fare to be charged. It is responsibility of Bus Conductors to collect correct fare from passengers and deposit same with Company. They act in fiduciary capacity and it would be case of gross misconduct if knowingly they do not collect any fare or correct amount of fare. It may be true that in execution of Award passed by Labour Court, for short time respondent was put back in service. Court, indicated that, stayed operation of judgment by reason whereof Award as also judgment of HC became non- operational. Court is of opinion that by itself that may not be ground to refrain itselves from following authoritative binding precedents. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - When main duty or function of conductor is to issue tickets and collect fare and then deposit same with Road Transport Corporation and when conductor fails to do so, then it will be lost sympathy to order his reinstatement instead of dismissal.
