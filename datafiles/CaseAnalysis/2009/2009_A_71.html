Grasim Industries Ltd. & Anr v Agarwal Steel

Supreme Court of India

20 October 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Markandey Katju, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1462; (2010) 1 SCC 83; AIR 2010 SC (Supp) 291; 2009 (77) ALR 638; 2010 (2) AWC 1578; JT 2009 (13) SC 413; 2010 (2) MLJ(SC) 514; 2010 (1) RCR(Civil) 932; 2009(13) SCALE 374; [2009] 15 S.C.R. 283

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, ss. 34 and 37 - Appellant entered into an agreement with respondent - Disputes arose - Matter referred to an arbitrator - Arbitrator rejected the plea of the claimant-respondent that the signature on joint statement of account were only in lieu of a receipt - Arbitrator held that the signature on behalf of the claimant-respondent was made erroneously and hence the same was not binding and appellant was liable to pay the respondent - Whether contention of respondent that document was signed erroneously acceptable? - Held, when a person signs a document, there is a presumption, unless there is proof of force or fraud, that he has read the document properly and understood it and only then has affixed his signatures thereon, otherwise no signature on a document can ever be accepted - Impugned judgment of HC set aside - Matter remitted back to HC - Appeal Partly allowed.
