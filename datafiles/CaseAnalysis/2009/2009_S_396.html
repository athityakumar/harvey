M/S. Shakti Tubes Ltd. v State Of Bihar & Ors

Supreme Court of India

07 July 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Mukundakam Sharma, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 913; (2009) 7 SCC 673; 2009 (3) AWC 2820; 2010 (1) JhrCR(SC) 161; JT 2009 (9) SC 386; 2009(9) SCALE 270; [2009] 10 S.C.R. 739

Case Digest

Subject: Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Alteration Of Contract, Interest on Delayed Payment To Small Scale and Ancillary Industrial Under-Taking Act, 1993, Customs Act, 1873

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Interest on Delayed Payment to Small Scale Industries Act, 1993, ss. - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 34 - Money suit, with pendente lite and future interest compounding at monthly rest @24% p.a. - Supply contract with State of Bihar for supplying pipes - Stipulation in the agreement that payment would be made at the escalated rate applicable for the quantity of pipes supplied after such escalation come into force - Suit filed for payment of such escalated rate, as payment was not made at such rates - Suit was decreed by Trial Court - High Court reduced the rate of interest to 9% p.a. - Aggrieved thereagainst, present appeal filed - Whether the appellant/plaintiff is entitled to a direction for payment of interest as claimed in the suit? - Held, No - Supply order was placed on 16-07-1992; appellant/plaintiff was a registered small scale industry under the Act, 1993 - Contention was raised that the interest as claimed has been claimed in terms of the provisions of the Act - Relevant facts noted, contentions heard, provisions of Act perused - Decision in 'Assam Small Scale Industries Development Corporation Ltd. v. J.D. Pharmaceuticals, 2005, (13) SCC 19', relied upon - In Assam's case, applicability of Act, 1993 was considered - Supply order was placed at a date when the Act had not came into force - (A) contention was raised that in Assam's case expression 'transactions', was used; and the transaction would be complete only when the supply is made, and in instant case supply was made after Act came into force - Contention whether acceptable? - Held, No - Issue set at rest in Assam's case, that what is to be considered relevant is the date of supply order placed and when the expression 'transaction' is used by the Court, it only meant a supply order - Further held, that the supply order having been placed herein prior to the coming into of the Act, any supply made pursuant to the said supply orders would be governed not by the provisions of the Act, 1993 but by the provisions of s. 34 of Code of Civil Procedure r/w provisions of the Act, 1993 - Being without merit - Appeal dismissed.
