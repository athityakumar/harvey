Kedar Singh Kushwaha v Dhaniram and Another

Supreme Court of India

04 August 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 992; (2009) 9 SCC 396; AIR 2010 SC 193; 2010 (2) AWC 1503; 2010 (1) RCR(Criminal) 112B; 2009(11) SCALE 421; [2009] 12 S.C.R. 364

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Madhya Pradesh Panchayats (Election Petition, Corrupt Practices and Disqualification for Membership) Rules, 1995

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Contempt of Courts Act, 1971, s.19 - Contempt of HC - HC directed appellant, Specified Officer for determining election disputes, to dispose the election petition of respondent within 2 months - Appellant failed to take action in pursuance of HC order - Respondent filed contempt against appellant-officer - HC issued Rule Nisi and directed the appellant to remain present in the court - Appellant failed to appear before HC - HC found appellant guilty of willful disobedience of its order and thereby convicted him for detention till rising of the court with a fine of Rs. 1000/- - Legality - Held, appellant did not take any steps to dispose the election petition despite the HC order received very earlier - Appellant in his order proceeded on the basis that HC need not be approached for obtaining further direction from it - HC clearly held that appellant had no power to order for recounting the votes - Appellant had not determined the preliminary objection, however, he ordered for recounting the ballot papers again - Plea of not understanding the effect and purport of HC order, not available to appellant - Appellant's appearance called for by HC was in accordance with HC rules relating to Contempt of Courts Act - No record brought on record showed that Collector refused the permission to appellant to appear before HC - Therefore, HC rightly convicted the appellant - No interference - Appeal dismissed.
