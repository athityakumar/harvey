Fiza Developers & Inter-Trade P. Ltd v Amci(I) Pvt. Ltd. & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

27 July 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, B. SUDARSHAN REDDY

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1512; (2009) 17 SCC 796; 2010 (1) AWC 176; 2010 (3) CompLJ 617; 2009 (5) CTC 65; 2009 (6) KarLJ 576; 2009 (8) MLJ(SC) 1155; 2009 (4) RCR(Civil) 288; 2009(11) SCALE 371; [2009] 12 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Civil Procedure

Keywords: Framing Of Issues, Ex Parte Decree, Summary Suits, Arbitration and Conciliation Act, High Court of Karnataka Arbitration (Proceedings Before the Courts) Rules, 2001

Summary: Civil Procedure - Arbitration & ADR - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, o. 14, r.1 - Arbitration & Conciliation Act, 1996, s. 34 (2) - High Court of Karnataka Arbitration (Proceedings before the Courts) Rules, 2001, r. 4(b) r/w r.12 - Framing of issues - Disputes between respondent and appellant were referred to arbitration - Arbitrator made an award - Appellant made an application u/o. 14, rr. 1 and 3 of CPC r/w. r. 4(b) of the High Court of Karnataka Arbitration (Proceedings before the Courts) Rules, 2001 requesting the Court to frame issues in the matter - Civil Court rejected said application - Petitioner's filed writ petition challenging the said order - Said writ petition was dismissed on the ground that applications u/s. 34 were not necessarily in the nature of a adversarial proceeding where a dispute between two parties requires adjudication by the Court - Hence, present appeal - Whether HC's order could be upheld? - Held, yes - Framing of issues as contemplated u/r. 1 of o. 14 of CPC is not an integral part of the process of a proceedings u/s. 34 of the Act - An application u/s 34 has to be considered, bearing three factors in mind i.e. that the Act is a special enactment and s. 34 provides for a special remedy, an arbitration award can be set aside only if one of the grounds mentioned in sub-s. (2) of s.34 exists and that proceedings u/s 34 require to be dealt with expeditiously - Subsection (2) of s. 34 also clearly places the burden of proof on the person who makes the application - Therefore, the question arising for adjudication as also the person on whom the burden of proof is placed is statutorily specified - Framing of issues is necessary only where different types of material propositions of fact or law are affirmed by one party and are denied by the other and it is therefore necessary for the Court to identify the issues and specify the party on whom the burden to prove the same lies - When this exercise has already been done by the statute, there is no need for framing the issues - No reason to interfere with the impugned order of HC - Appeal dismissed.
