C. G. Praveen v Mohd. Tajuddin and Another

Supreme Court of India

22 April 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Agrawal, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1867; (2009) 12 SCC 706; 2009 (3) BC 688; 2009 (2) KLT 991; 2010 (4) RCR(Criminal) 418; 2012 (5) RCR(Criminal) 651A

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal procedure, 1973 s. 482 - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, ss. 138, 142 - Dishonour of Cheque - Quashing of Proceedings Challenged - Cheque issued by respondent no.1 to 'X' was dishonoured - Case was registered - HC quashed proceedings against respondent no.1 in petition on ground that complaint was filed by power of attorney holder of payee and not payee himself - Hence, instant appeals - Whether signing of complaint by payee himself was sine qua non for taking cognizance of offence u/s.138 of the Act.

Held, a complaint u/s.138 of the Act can be filed by the payee through his power of attorney holder. In instant case, the complaint was filed by the payee through his power of attorney holder. HC was not justified in quashing the prosecution of respondent no.1. Hence, impugned order is set aside and petition filed u/s. 482 of CrPC is dismissed. Further, direct the Trial Court to conclude case in accordance with the law. Appeals allowed.
