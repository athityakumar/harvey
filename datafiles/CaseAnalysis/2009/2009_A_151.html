Anna Reddy Sambasiva Reddy and Others v State of Andhra Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

21 April 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 522; (2009) 12 SCC 546; AIR 2009 SC 2661; 2009 ALL MR (Cri) 3474; 2010 (1) MLJ(Crl) 340; 2010 (5) RCR(Criminal) 83; 2009(6) SCALE 223; [2009] 6 S.C.R. 755

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Chance Witness, Distinct Offence, Joinder Of Charges, Individual Liability, Specific Injury

Summary: Criminal - IPC, 1860, ss. 302, 307, 307 r/w 149, 148, 341 - Appeal against conviction and sentence - Held, if the ingredients of the section are obvious or implicit, conviction in regard thereto can be sustained irrespective of the fact that the said section has not been mentioned - There is mere omission to mention s. 149 in charge no. 4 and 5 which at the highest may be considered as an irregularity and since the appellants have failed to show any prejudice, their conviction and sentence is not at all affected - Appeal dismissed.
