Prakash Ratan Sinha v State Of Bihar & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

14 July 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Dattu, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1344; (2009) 14 SCC 690; 2009(9) SCALE 529

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Qualification And Experience

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 21 - Natural Justice - Qualification and Experience - Sustainability - Appellant was appointed on daily wages and he was made permanent - Appellant though was appointed as daily wager, was asked to discharge work of Accounts Clerk in view of his qualification and experience - Appellant filed application before Committee for promotion to post of Accounts Clerk and that request was considered by Committee and recommended was made - Electrical Superintending Engineer issued order changing his nomenclature - In charge Chief Electrical Inspector vide his order approved proposal of Committee and had issued orders - Having found that order passed by in charge Chief Electrical Engineer was contrary to Rules and Govt. Orders issued from time to time had cancelled earlier order - Appellant filed writ petition and was allowed by HC mainly on ground that impugned order had been passed without affording opportunity of hearing to appellant - Appeal filed by respondents was allowed by DB on ground, that even if opportunity of hearing was granted result would have been same - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order passed by Chief Electrical Engineer was amenable to judicial review on touchstone of principles of natural justice - Held, there was no dispute between parties that personal or oral hearing was not granted to appellant before passing impugned order and therefore impugned order was in violation of principles of natural justice - Even assuming it was case of promotion and same had been done without following prescribed procedure and also by ignoring claim of several other employees and therefore respondents were justified in cancelling order passed by in charge Chief Electrical Engineer - Court further held that these were all disputed facts and respondents without affording opportunity of hearing could not have taken any administrative decision unilaterally - Therefore, DB was not justified in concluding "unless formality theory" need not have been followed by respondents - Hence, Court ordered to set aside order passed by Chief Electrical Engineer - Appeal allowed.
