All India Council For Technical Education v Surinder Kumar Dhawan & Ors

Supreme Court of India

18 February 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 2026; (2009) 11 SCC 726; AIR 2009 SC 2322; 2009 (4) MLJ(SC) 148; 2009(4) SCALE 596; [2009] 3 S.C.R. 859; 2009 (4) SLR 520

Case Digest

Subject: Education

Keywords: AICTE Rules

Summary: Education - All India Council for Technical Education Act, 1987, s. 10 - Bridge course - Respondent No. 7 was conducting Post Diploma Courses of four years duration in various engineering disciplines for several years, with entry level qualification of 10+1, sought permission from appellant to convert aforesaid four year Post Diploma Programme into an 'Advance Diploma Programme' also of four years duration - Appellant granted approval for such conversion subject to condition that entry level to course should be raised from 11th (10+1) to 12th (10+2) standard and duration of course shall be 4 years after 10+2 - Consequently, four year Post Diploma Course of respondent-Institute was converted to a four year Advance Diploma Course from 1995 - In order to enable its students who had successfully completed Four Year Post/Advance Diploma Course, to acquire degrees in engineering, respondent wanted to commence a one year bridge course which was allowed by Director of Technical Education - Respondent filed writ petition for quashing of said order contending that approving any bridge course would involve a major policy shift on part of appellant - HC allowed said writ petition and directed appellant to accord approval to Institute to have a bridge course for its students who had studied and who were studying in Advance Diploma Course - Hence, present appeal - Whether HC's order could be upheld? - Held, cumulative effect of HC's orders lead to an unintended dilution of educational standards adversely affecting standards and quality of Engineering Degree Courses - Courts should guard against such forays in field of education - HC's order set aside - Appeals allowed
