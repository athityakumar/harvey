Peethani Suryanarayana and Another v Repaka Venkata Ramana Kishore and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 February 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 168; (2009) 11 SCC 308; AIR 2009 SC 2141; 2009(3) ALL MR 989; 2009 (75) ALR 510; 2009 (4) AWC 3547; 2010 (2) JhrCR(SC) 161; JT 2009 (2) SC 549; 2009 (1) KLT 725; 2009 (4) MLJ(SC) 589; 2009 (2) RCR(Civil) 521; 2009 (1) RCR(Rent) 377; 2009 (107) RD 277; 2009(2) SCALE 461; [2009] 2 S.C.R. 48; 2009 (3) UC 1987

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - CPC, 1908 - Jurisdiction of a civil court to allow an application for amendment of plaint after a final decree is passed - Suit for partition as also for a decree for setting aside some deeds of sale executed in favour of some of the defendants - Held, a wide power on the part of the court is circumscribed by two factors, viz., (i) the application must be bonafide; (ii) the same should not cause injustice to the other side and (iii) it should not affect the right already accrued to the defendants - Appeal dismissed.
