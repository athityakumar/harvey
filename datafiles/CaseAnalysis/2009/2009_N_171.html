North West Karnataka Rd. Transport Corporation v Gourabai and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1897; (2009) 15 SCC 165; 2009 (4) AWC 3372; JT 2010 (6) SC 384; 2010 (160) PLR 162; 2009(8) SCALE 787; [2009] 9 S.C.R. 942

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, s. 166 - Compensation - Tribunal awarded compensation to deceased for injuries sustained - HC confirmed Tribunal's order - Appellant contended before Tribunal as well as before HC that deceased did not sustain any injury in any accident involving bus of corporation - Held, reference was made to evidence of doctor, who had admitted deceased to hospital, that deceased had suffered head injury due to fall from height of 8 to 10 feet of his own house - Though this was specifically stated in written statement Tribunal and HC brushed aside same stating that there was indirect admission about deceased having sustained injury in vehicular accident - Injuries sustained were not on account of any vehicular accident - Award by Tribunal and HC not sustainable, hence set aside - Appeal allowed.
