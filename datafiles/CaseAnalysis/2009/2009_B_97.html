Bawa Ram and Another v State of U. T. Chandigarh

Supreme Court of India

05 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1850; (2009) 13 SCC 270; JT 2009 (7) SC 675; 2009(6) SCALE 791; [2009] 7 S.C.R. 591

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Unsound Mind, Eye Witness

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302 r/w. 34 - Murder - Conviction - HC convicted Appellants on basis of dying declaration of deceased - Appellants contended that dying declarations have to be tested on background of what parents and relatives of deceased deposed as they categorically stated that deceased was mentally unsound and was having suicidal tendency and that he himself tried to commit suicide - Whether conviction of Appellants correct? - Held, in peculiar facts of case it would not be safe to sustain conviction on basis of dying declarations - Appellants are therefore, acquitted of charges - Appeals disposed of.
