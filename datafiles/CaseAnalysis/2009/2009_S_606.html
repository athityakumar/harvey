Amin Khan v State Of Rajasthan And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

25 February 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 335; (2009) 2 SCC (Cr) 749; (2009) 3 SCC 776; 2009 ALL MR (Cri) 1596; 2009 CRLJ 2266; JT 2009 (3) SC 48; 2009 (2) RCR(Criminal) 262; 2009(3) SCALE 391; [2009] 3 S.C.R. 348

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Finger Prints, Fire Arms, Foot Prints, Non Bailable Warrants

Summary: Criminal - IPC, 1860, s. 396; Arms Act, 1959, ss. 3, 35; Cr.P.C, 1973, ss. 390 r/w 482 - Acquittal by trial Court - Application to commit the accused persons to prison after summoning them through non bailable warrants allowed - Appeal against - Under s. 390 High Court has the power to re-arrest the accused pending the disposal of an appeal against his acquittal - High Court has found that prima facie the evidence regarding identification made in court and DNA test has not been considered in the proper perspective by the trial Court - No infirmity in the impugned judgment to warrant interference - Appeal dismissed.
