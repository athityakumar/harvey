Avinash Singh Bagri and Others v Registrar IIT Delhi and Another

Supreme Court of India

12 August 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, K.G. Balakrishnan, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1031; (2009) 8 SCC 220; JT 2009 (15) SC 139; 2009 (8) MLJ(SC) 432; 2009(11) SCALE 535; [2009] 13 S.C.R. 258; 2009 (7) SLR 372

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Education

Keywords: Writ petitions disposed of, Expulsion, Institutes of Technology Act, 1961

Summary: Education - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts.32 and 46 - Writ Petition filed u/art.32 of Constitution - Petitioners belong to reserved categories (SC/ST/OBC) - They were students of IIT-Delhi and pursuing B.Tech. Course - Petitioners got admission in IIT Delhi through All India Joint Entrance Test in year 2004-2005 - Petitioners expelled from B.Tech Course as they could not make required average credits in their second year - Grievance of petitioners that in spite of providing reservation in higher education like Medical and Engineering streams, no coaching or any other facilities are provided to SC/ST/OBC candidates to enable them to compete with other general category students, as a result of which, more than 90% SC/ST/OBC students are dropped out from these higher courses in first year or second year - Held, SC and ST are separate class by themselves and creamy layer principle is not applicable to them - Art.46 of Constitution enjoins upon State to promote with special care educational and economic interests of weaker sections of people and protect them from social injustice and all forms of exploitation - These socially and economically backward categories are to be taken care of at every stage even in specialized institutions like IITs - They must take all endeavour by providing additional coaching and bring them up at par with general category students - Further held, additional/extra efforts were not fully afforded to these students in order to compete with general category students and also secure minimum required credits in first and second year - Ends of justice would be fully met by giving one more opportunity to petitioners - First respondent directed to consider petitioners case afresh and pass appropriate orders by considering all aspects including policy of Govt. of India in providing reservation to bring them in mainstream along with others - Writ petitions disposed of.
