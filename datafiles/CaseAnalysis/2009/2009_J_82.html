Jeewan Kumar Raut & Anr v Central Bureau Of Investigation

Supreme Court of India

07 July 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 860; (2009) 3 SCC (Cr) 475; (2009) 7 SCC 526; AIR 2009 SC 2763; 2009 (4) CalLT(SC) 26; 2009 CRLJ 4109; JT 2009 (9) SC 188; 2009 (4) MLJ(Crl) 1339; 2009 (3) RCR(Criminal) 586; 2009(9) SCALE 381; [2009] 10 S.C.R. 272

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Specially Empowered

Summary: (A) Criminal - Transplantation of Human Organs Act, 1994, s. 22 (TOHO) - CrPC, 1973, s. 167 - Offences under TOHO - Applicability of provisions of CrPC - Whether it is mandatory to file police report within 90 days u/s. 167 of the Code in respect of offences investigated under the provisions of TOHO? - Respondent filed a complaint petition instead of police report - Held, respondent has been statutorily prohibited from filing police report, by virtue of s.22 of TOHO - S. 167 (2) of the Code would not apply to cases under TOHO - Moreover, the said complaint petition filed by the respondents within 90 days of the date of cognizance of offence - Therefore, respondent is not obliged to file police report u/s. 167 of the Code, instead of it they filed complaint petition as per the provisions of TOHO - No merit- Appeal dismissed.P /&gt;(B) Criminal - CrPC, 1973, ss. 167 (2), 309 (2) - Remand of accused - Pre-cognizance stage and Post-cognizance stage - Distinction between - Order of remand at pre-cognizance stage is to be made in terms of s. 167 (2) whereas order at post-cognizance stage can be affected only in terms of s. 309 (2) of Code.
