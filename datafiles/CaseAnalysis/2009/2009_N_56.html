State of Rajasthan v Naresh @ Ram Naresh

Supreme Court of India

26 August 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Mukundakam Sharma, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1050; (2009) 9 SCC 368; (2009) 3 SCC (Cr) 1069; 2010 CRLJ 1928; 2009 (4) JCC 2552; 2010 (1) MLJ(Crl) 117; 2010 (1) RCR(Criminal) 128; 2009(11) SCALE 699; [2009] 13 S.C.R. 589

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Power Of The Court, Bloodstains

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s.302 and 394 - Voluntarily causing hurt in committing robbery - Murder - Acquittal - HC acquitted respondent-accused from charges u/ss.302 and 394 of IPC by setting aside judgment and order of conviction passed against respondent-accused by Trial Court - Appeal against acquittal - Entire case of prosecution rests on circumstantial evidence - (A) Recovery of ornaments belonging to deceased at instance of respondent-accused from custody and possession of PW-20 - Held, recovery of jewellery shrouded in a total mystery as it was not recovered from place and person to whom allegedly respondent-accused sold - (B) Recovery of weapon 'Khurpi' alleged to have been used in commission of offence - Held, 'Khurpi' did not contain any bloodstains on it and it was recovered from an open place - Since there was no bloodstain on it, police also did not send it for chemical examination - Therefore, it cannot be said that said weapon was used for committing murder of deceased - (C) Recovery of strap of wrist watch near dead body of deceased, which allegedly belong to accused - Held, no direct evidence that said strap of wristwatch belongs to watch of respondent-accused - None of witnesses stated that such strap of wristwatch belongs to respondent-accused nor any wristwatch recovered from accused - Further held, none of circumstances relied upon by prosecution stands proved against accused leading to a definite conclusion that it was respondent-accused, who had committed offence - View taken by HC found to be a plausible view, and therefore, benefit must be given to respondent-accused and not to prosecution - Order of acquittal upheld - Appeal dismissed
