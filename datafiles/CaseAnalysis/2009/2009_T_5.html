Basanti Prasad v Chairman, Bihar School Examination Board and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Tarun Chatterjee, H.L. Dattu

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 770; (2009) 6 SCC 791; AIR 2009 SC 3162; 2009 (4) AWC 3449; 2009 (4) JhrCR(SC) 45; 2010 (1) MPLJ 328; 2009(8) SCALE 401; [2009] 9 S.C.R. 367; 2010 (1) SLR 503

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Termination - On account of conviction by Trial Court - Appellant's husband was an assistant in the respondent/Board - Put under suspension in view of initiation of criminal proceedings - Appeal was filed against conviction and therein acquittal was awarded - During the pendency of appeal appellant's husband had died - Writ petition filed before High Court praying for quashing of termination order, and back wages with retiral benefits - Petition was dismissed on ground of delay and latches - Whether judgment proper and justified? - Held, No - Contentions heard - 'If the delay is properly explained, and if the third party rights is not going to be affected, the HC may entertain the petition and consider the case of the aggrieved person on merits' - Services of the appellant's husband was terminated only on the ground, that he was convicted by Trial Court for certain offences under IPC, and not a case where he was dismissed on ground that he was charge sheeted by the police for certain offences under IPC after holding a departmental enquiry; thus till that order is set aside by a superior forum, the appellant's husband or the appellant could not have questioned the same till he was acquitted by the Appellate Court; order of termination was passed on 04-08-1992 and petition thereagainst was filed in year 2005 - No negligence or laches or acquiescence on part of appellant which may have disentitle her for grant of a writ - Held further, order of termination set aside - (B) Whether back wages and retiral benefits are to be granted in the circumstances of the case? - Held, no, for back wages and yes, for retiral benefits, reliance being placed by Court on 'G.M. Tank vs. State of Gujarat' case, as facts and circumstances of such case resembled, Court opined - No back wages only pension - Impugned judgment set aside - Appeal allowed.
