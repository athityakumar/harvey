National Thermal Power Corporation Ltd. v Mahesh Dutta And Others

Supreme Court of India

16 July 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 905; (2009) 8 SCC 339; 2010 (5) ADJ 467; 2010 (1) AWC 447; JT 2009 (9) SC 537; 2010 (2) RCR(Civil) 109; 2009 (108) RD 775; 2009(9) SCALE 591; [2009] 10 S.C.R. 1084; 2009 (3) UPLBEC 2666

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Standing Crops

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 48, 17(1)(4), 4, 6 and 9 - Power of the State to withdraw the Notification of acquisition as envisaged u/s. 48 of Act - Acquisition of land in a village, for the purpose of setting up Thermal Power Station - Land to be acquired was around 65.7125 acres - Emergency provisions u/s. 17(1) and 17(4), invoked; on the premise that generation of electricity was extremely urgent and National Capital Region faced acute shortage of electricity - Notification in terms of s. 4 was issued; declaration in terms of s. 6 was further issued - Notices u/s. 9 were issued for payment of compensation, for the reason of invocation of s. 17(4) - Admitted position, prior to taking over possession of land under the emergency powers, the Collector disbursed 80% of the amount of compensation determined in terms of s. 17(3A) of Act - Possession certificate was issued - Despite such certificate, appellant raised contention that it had obtained the requisitioned physical possession of land admeasuring 10.215 acres only and rest of the land continued to remain in possession of land owners - Ministry of Environment had made recommendation for shifting such proposed Power Station in view of its proximity to NCR - In pursuance thereof site of the plant was shifted - LAO despite the same shift, proceeded to determine the amount of compensation - Award was made; reference was further made thereagainst, which was answered - First appeal preferred by appellant, raising contention as to possession of only a portion of land in question; and the step taken for issuance of a notification denotifying the acquisition for the balance area - State of UP had issued a Notification in terms of s. 48 of Act; consequential effect of which was stayed in litigation pursued by parties - Respondent had filed writ petition against notification issued u/s. 48, which was allowed on giving a finding that physical possession was taken - Whether such finding of fact, correct and proper? - Held, Yes - Power of State to withdraw the Notification of acquisition as envisaged u/s. 48 of Act, considered - Settled proposition of law that in the event possession of the land, in respect whereof a Notification had been issued, had been taken over, the State would be denuded of its power to withdraw from the acquisition in terms of s. 48 of the Act - Background facts noted, contentions heard, material on record, award by LAO and provisions, perused - Possession was shown to have been taken over not only in terms of s. 17(1) but also by grant of certificate and other documents, and further by judicial records; which clearly goes to show that the possession had in fact been taken - Judgement and finding of HC, upheld - Being devoid of merit - Appeals dismissed.
