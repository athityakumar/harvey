Sarla Goel and Others v Kishan Chand

Supreme Court of India

08 July 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Tarun Chatterjee, H.L. Dattu

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 839; (2009) 7 SCC 658; 2009 (76) ALR 831; 2009 (160) DLT 687; JT 2009 (9) SC 21; 2009 (2) RCR(Rent) 115; 2009(9) SCALE 392; [2009] 10 S.C.R. 481

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Keywords: Default In Payment Of Rent, First Hearing, Appeal allowed, Demand Notice, Rent Control Tribunal, Notice Of Demand, Standard Rent, Order Of Eviction, Rent Receipt, Tamil Nadu Buildings (Lease and Rent Control) Act, 1960, Punjab Relief of Indebtedness Act, 1934

Summary: Rent Control - Delhi Rent Control Act, 1958, ss. 14(1)(a) r/w proviso to s. 14(2), 15 and 27 - Deposit of rent by the tenant - Eviction suit - Ground, second default in payment of rent - Respondent/tenant had earlier availed the benefit of s. 14(2) r/w 15 in 2001 - Second default in respect of rent of three consecutive months - On demand notice by appellants/landlord in respect of rent in arrears, respondent/tenant had sent the same through money order; undisputedly appellants/landlords refused to accept such money order - Submission of appellants/landlord before Rent Controller, and the High Court was that that on refusal to receive the money order by them, respondent ought to have taken the recourse of s. 27 of Act by depositing the rent in arrear with RC, as he had not done so he would be liable to be evicted from the suit premises on ground of second default u/s. 14(1)(a) r/w proviso to s. 14(2) of Act - Contentions heard, provisions perused and conjoint reading done of relevant provisions, material on record examined - Whether the respondent/tenant had defaulted in payment of rent inasmuch as he had not deposited the rent with the RC for default period after the refusal by the appellants/landlord, in the manner required by law? - Held, Yes - (i) Whether provision of s. 27 of Act, mandatory? - Held, Yes - Overall object of the Act and intention of the Legislature, deliberated upon - Court relied upon 'Atmaram vs. Shakuntala Rani 2005 (7) SCC 211' and 'E. Palanisamy vs. Palanisamy (2003) 1 SCC, 123' - Conclusion, tenant/respondent has failed to deposit rent in compliance with s. 27, provision of which is mandatory - Held further, as step u/s. 27 was not taken respondent/tenant had committed a second default in payment of rent and therefore is liable to be evicted from the suit premises - Impugned judgement of HC set aside and that of RC restored - Six months time granted for peaceful vacation of suit premises - Appeal allowed.
