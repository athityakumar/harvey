(1) Bombay Anand Bhavan Restaurant; (2) Cow and v (1) Deputy Director, Esi Corporation and Another;

Supreme Court of India

02 September 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Dattu, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1105; (2009) 9 SCC 61; 2010(1) ALL MR 968; 2009 (4) AWC 3587; 2010 (3) Bom.C.R. 423; 2009 (3) CLR 316; 2010 (1) KarLJ 545; 2009 (4) KLT 237; 2009 (4) LLJ 413; 2009(12) SCALE 138; [2009] 13 S.C.R. 1139

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Social Security, Employees State Insurance, Esi Corporation

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Employees' State Insurance Act, 1948, s.45A - Tea restaurant - Applicability of Act - Appellant restaurant employed more than 10 employees and being used power for manufacturing, alleged - ESI Corporation directed appellant to pay contribution - Appellant objected on ground that use of LPG in restaurant cannot be equated with use of power - Corporation issued demand notice - Demand was not complied with by appellant - Dy Director of Corporation passed an order u/s.45A - Appellant filed appeal before ESI Court - ESI Court dismissed appeal on ground that use of LPG amounted to use of power - Appellant filed Misc Appeal before HC - HC dismissed appeal - Legality - Held, appellants prepared sweets, savories and other beverages in restaurant - Cooking and preparing food items qualified manufacturing process under Act - LPG cylinder would be qualified as an appliance which provides power - Transmission of gas by tube qualified as transmission machinery or device by which motion of a primary mover was transmitted - Transmission considered to be end when energy was transformed for distribution to consumer whereas LPG stored in cylinder transmitted to be end at gas cooking stove - LPG was a source of energy which was mechanically transmitted by way of tube attached to machinery - Therefore, LPG satisfied definition of power, hence appellant could be termed as factory - ESI Act would apply to appellant establishments - Appeals dismissed.
