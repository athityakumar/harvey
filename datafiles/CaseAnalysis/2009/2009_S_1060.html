Ravindra Saxena v State of Rajasthan

Supreme Court of India

15 December 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Surinder Singh Nijjar, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1945; (2010) 1 SCC 684; AIR 2010 SC 1225; 2010 ALL MR (Cri) 619; 2010 (1) JCC 371; JT 2009 (15) SC 384; 2010 (2) MLJ(Crl) 855; 2010 (1) RCR(Criminal) 540; 2009(14) SCALE 609; [2009] 16 S.C.R. 433

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 420, 467, 468 and 120-B - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 437 and 438 - Anticipatory bail - Cheating - Appellant alleged committed offences u/ss. 420, 467, 468, 120-B of IPC - Application filed by appellant seeking anticipatory bail was rejected for the third time by HC on ground that challan has now been presented - Hence, present appeal - Whether HC rightly denied anticipatory bail to appellant? - Held, no reason to deny anticipatory bail merely because allegation pertains to cheating or forgery of a valuable security - Merits of these issues shall have to be assessed at time of trial of accused persons and denial of anticipatory bail only on ground that challan has been presented would not satisfy requirements of ss. 437 and 438 Cr.P.C - Moreover, appellant's contention that dispute herein is purely of a civil nature cannot be brushed aside at this stage - HC committed a serious error of law in not applying its mind to facts and circumstances of this case - Impugned order set aside - Anticipatory bail granted to appellant - Appeal allowed.
