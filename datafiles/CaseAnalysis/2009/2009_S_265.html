Ravi Development v Shree Krishna Prathisthan and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, K.G. Balakrishnan

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 637; (2009) 7 SCC 462; AIR 2009 SC 2519; 2009 (4) Bom.C.R. 50; 2009(8) SCALE 96; [2009] 8 S.C.R. 654

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Maharashtra Housing and Area Development Authority Act, 1976

Summary: Land & Property - Maharashtra Housing and Area Development Authority Act, 1976 (MHADA) - Land development for construction of houses - By 'Swiss Challenge Method'(SCM) - Awarding of contract by MHADA to appellant - Appellant was the initiator of proposal as to SCM - As per terms, original proposer was to be given an opportunity to take up the project on the highest eligible bid offer - Vide respective resolution appellant's bid was accepted and contract was awarded to it - Writ petition preferred before High Court; allowed; holding SCM to be arbitrary and unreasonable, and thus action taken thereto was struck down - Whether Swiss Challenge Method (SCM) adopted by MHADA on a pilot basis with respect to a proposal received from a private entrepreneur i.e. appellant, for development of undeveloped Govt. land, valid? - Held, Yes - Facts noted, submissions heard and relevant materials perused including project details - Repeated observations of HC regarding innovativeness of proposal found to be unsustainable, wherein HC gave finding that proposal ought to have been innovative which was not; requirement of innovativeness no where to be found under SCM - SCM adopted in various nations and also in various States in the country; clear mention of SCM and the scheme to be followed under it in the public notice issued for tender, further in such notice rule of 'first right of refusal' to the 'originator of the proposal' was mentioned; statement in the notice that MHADA has received suo motu proposal from a developer though without mentioning the name of appellant, which showed that existence of 'originator of proposal' was very much in the knowledge of other builders at the time of applying for the bidding; appellant vide its proposal showed how to balance with highly developed high-rise with the low-rise building of lower specifications built up for the EWS, LIG and MIG groups and in that way this project plan was in accordance with the objective MHADA was looking for as well as profit sharing mode in a public-private partnership, thus it cannot be said that there was dearth of innovativeness and originality in the proposal; question of acceptability of SCM was raised when there was failure in the bidding and not at the time when public notice was issued; allegation of favoritism also rejected - Court noted need of public private participation for fulfilling housing requirements of various income groups of citizens i.e. EWS, LIG, MIG; Central Govt.'s policy on housing and State Govt.'s policy - Due deliberations found to be entered into seeking pros and cons by authorities, noted by Court - SCM was being applied by State Govt. only on pilot basis - Need of the time further noted - Suggestions made - Impugned judgment set aside - Appeals allowed.
