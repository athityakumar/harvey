Girdhar Kumar Dadhich and another v State of Rajasthan and others

Supreme Court of India

23 January 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, J.M. Panchal

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 2120; (2009) 1 SCC (L&S) 543; (2009) 2 SCC 706; AIR 2009 SC 1899; 2009 (2) AWC 1636; 2009 (1) ESC 161; JT 2009 (4) SC 303; 2009(2) SCALE 98; [2009] 1 S.C.R. 585; 2009 (4) SLR 75

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Appointment - Entitlement - Advertisement was issued for filling up 10 posts of Physical Education Teacher Grade-III wherein 10 bonus marks were to be granted for candidates who were resident of a particular district, while 5 bonus marks to the candidates belonging to rural areas - Validity of the said provision for grant of bonus marks was questioned before the HC - Full Bench of HC held that said provision granting bonus marks on the basis of residence to be unconstitutional - Appellants filed writ petition seeking the benefit of Full Bench decision - SC, in the meantime, in Kailash Chand Sharma v. State of Rajasthan and others, 2002 INDLAW SC 1748, upheld the Full Bench decision, however, directed not to re-open the appointments made up to 17-11-1999 - HC directed the appellants to file representations before the authorities - Authorities rejected appellants representation - Appellants challenged the rejection order before the HC - Single Judge dismissed appellants' petitions and DB also, subsequently, dismissed respective appeals thereof - Hence the present appeal - Whether appellants entitled to get the appointment - Held, select list was prepared in the year 1998 and which was prepared keeping in view of the rules as they existed - Further, in the select list , out of ten posts, eight posts were appointed on or before 18-11- 999 which was the cut-off date - However, select list was modified in view of Kailash Chand Sharma's case in order to protect the service of such appointees who were working in the post for few years - Further, question as to whether the fresh appointees who are, having regard to the said modification, required to be appointed on the premise that they are placed higher in the select list than the appellants or not, cannot be gone into for the first time since such a contention had never been raised before the HC - No merit in the appeal - Appeal dismissed.
