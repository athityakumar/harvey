Undavali Narayana Rao v State Of A.P.

Supreme Court of India

24 July 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 917; (2009) 14 SCC 588; AIR 2010 SC 3708; 2010 (3) G.L.R. 2022; JT 2009 (9) SC 573; 2009 (4) MLJ(Crl) 329; 2009(10) SCALE 125; [2009] 11 S.C.R. 660

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - IPC, 1860, s. 498A - Unlawful demand - Cruelty and harassment - Death of deceased - Appellant (husband of deceased) convicted for offence u/s. 498A IPC by Trial Court - HC upheld conviction and sentence - Hence, present appeal - Whether conviction of appellant proper? - Basic ingredients of s. 498A IPC are cruelty and harassment - Evidence on record, revealed that deceased was being harassed by appellant as well as by his mother - Relations between appellant and deceased were not cordial, and that she had been harassed to meet unlawful demand of appellant - Death of deceased was in house of appellant; neither appellant nor his mother made any attempt to inform family members of deceased about death; her dead body had been cremated hurriedly without any autopsy having been conducted; there were independent witnesses - Trial Court came to conclusion that deceased had been harassed/coerced to extent that she committed suicide - Held, charge u/s. 498-A fully proved against appellant - No cogent reason to take a view contrary to one taken by courts below - Appeal dismissed.
