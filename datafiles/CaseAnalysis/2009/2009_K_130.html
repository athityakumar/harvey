K. K. Kannan (D) By Lrs. v Koolivathukkal Karikkan Mandi and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 December 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 2069; (2010) 2 SCC 239; AIR 2010 SC (Supp) 194; 2010(2) ALL MR 501; 2010 (78) ALR 714; 2010 (1) AWC 813; 2010 (2) JhrCR(SC) 36; JT 2009 (15) SC 441; 2010 (3) MPLJ 185; 2010 (109) RD 386; 2009(14) SCALE 588; [2009] 16 S.C.R. 362; 2010 (1) UC 597

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 100 - HC reversed the concurrent decisions of both the Courts below in second appeal - Present appeal - Appellant contended that HC committed an error in not adhering to the mandates prescribed in s. 100, CPC - Whether HC wrong in interfering with the factual decisions arrived at by both the Courts? - Held, in order to fulfil the conditions mentioned in s. 100, CPC if the HC is satisfied that the substantial question of law is involved, it is to formulate that question, then hear the second appeal on the question so formulated - In the event of formulating such question, it is also the duty of the Court to permit the respondent to argue that the case does not involve any such question - In the present case, HC has merely mentioned grounds as stated in the memorandum of second appeal but has not formulated the question after satisfying that a substantial question of law involved in that appeal - Mere reference to the grounds as stated in the memorandum of second appeal would not satisfy the mandates prescribed in s. 100, CPC more particularly when the HC allowed the second appeal setting aside concurrent decisions of the Courts below - Procedure adopted by the HC is not in accordance with s. 100, CPC - Impugned judgment set aside - Matter remitted back to HC - Appeals allowed.
