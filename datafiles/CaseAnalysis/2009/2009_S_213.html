State Of Rajasthan v Shankar Lal

Supreme Court of India

15 April 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Tarun Chatterjee, H.L. Dattu

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1350; (2009) 16 SCC 116; 2009 (2) JhrCR(SC) 134; 2009 (3) LLJ 573; 2009(5) SCALE 554; 2010 (3) SLR 703

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Practice & Procedure - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Removal of defects - Dismissal of appeal - Respondent raised a dispute regarding his termination of service by petitioner/State and dispute was referred to Labour Court which directed reinstatement of respondent no.1 - Feeling aggrieved by order of Labour Court, petitioner filed a writ petition before HC which directed petitioner to remove defects in filing appeal within one week from that date, failing which, appeal shall stand dismissed - Admittedly, defects were not removed by petitioner and accordingly, special appeal stood dismissed - For restoration of writ appeal, application was filed by petitioner with an application for condonation of delay and HC, by impugned order, rejected same - Hence instant petition.

Held, Court examined impugned order as well as application for restoration and cause shown by petitioner for not complying with directions of HC, which directed him to remove defects indicated in order dt.18-10-2004 and also application for condonation of delay in filing same. Considering fact that poor workman, for not fault of his, could not get fruit of contested award passed in his favour and reasons given for restoration of appeal were not satisfactory and considering findings made in impugned order that State/appellant had unnecessary dragged matter. Court is of view that impugned order, holding that no sufficient cause was shown for restoration of appeal, cannot be disturbed and therefore, Court do not find any ground to interfere with same. Petition dismissed.

Ratio - Object of 1947 Act is for the benefit of workmen and any award arising out of said Act in favour of workmen cannot be dragged by employers on frivolous grounds.
