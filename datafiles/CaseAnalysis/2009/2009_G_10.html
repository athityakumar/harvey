Gangamma Etc v G. Nagarathnamma and Others Etc

Supreme Court of India

06 July 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Ganguly, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 832; (2009) 15 SCC 756; AIR 2009 SC 2561; 2009 (6) ALD(SC) 1; 2009 (76) ALR 477; 2009 (3) AWC 2794; 2010 (3) Bom.C.R. 888; 2009 (3) G.L.R. 2522; 2010 (1) JhrCR(SC) 21; JT 2009 (9) SC 159; 2009 (6) MLJ(SC) 77; 2009(9) SCALE 45; [2009] 9 S.C.R. 1167

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Keywords: Mother- In-Law

Summary: Family & Personal - Hindu Succession Act, 1956, s. 14(1) - Property of a female Hindu to be her absolute property - Partition suit by daughter in law of defendant no. 1 on behalf of her minor daughter claiming 1/3rd of the suit property and possession by metes and bounds plus mesne profits - Husband of plaintiff no. 1 was dead - Other defendants were sons, daughters and tenants - Plaint case was that suit properties are ancestral properties, though some of the properties stand in the name of defendant no. 1 because of the reason that such properties were bought benami in her name by late Ganganna(father-in-law of plaintiff no. 1) out of the income from agricultural lands and income of plaintiff no. 1's husband who was working as an accountant in a private firm - Contention was raised by plaintiff that defendant no. 1 had no income, being a housewife, thus she could not have purchased the properties - Trial Court decreed the suit in part - High Court in appeal dismissed the appeal preferred by defendants/petitioners - Dismissal whether proper? - Held, No - Case clearly attracts s. 14 of HS Act, Court observed - As the properties at item nos. 1 and 2 are recorded in the name of the appellant, in the absence of any evidence to the contrary, the appellant by operation of s. 14(1) of HS Act is the full owner of those properties - Court noted that in facts of the case it has to be accepted that those properties are not joint properties but the appellant is the sole owner of those properties - 'Eramma vs. Veerupana, AIR 1966 SC 1879' - Impugned judgement set aside - Appeal partly allowed.
