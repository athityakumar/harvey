Chunduru Siva Ram Krishna & Anr. v Peddi Ravindra Babu & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

25 March 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Mukundakam Sharma, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 362; (2009) 11 SCC 203; (2009) 3 SCC (Cr) 1297; AIR 2009 SC 3250; 2009 (3) JCC 1688; JT 2009 (4) SC 402; 2009 (4) MLJ(Crl) 371; 2009 (3) RCR(Criminal) 506; 2009(4) SCALE 685; [2009] 4 S.C.R. 1129

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Subsequent Conduct

Summary: Criminal - Cr.P.C, 1973, s. 482 - Charge sheet under ss. 406, 420, 424 r/w 37 - Whether allegations made in complaint make out a case for quashing of the complaint? - Held, when at an initial stage a prosecution is asked to be quashed, the test to be applied by the court is as to whether the uncontroverted allegations as made in the complaint filed prima facie establish the offence - It is also for the court to take into consideration any special feature that may appear in a particular case while considering whether it is expedient and in the interest of justice to permit a prosecution to continue - There is no concrete and direct allegation against all the persons ascribing any definite role to each one of them in the offence alleged - Quashed the proceedings - Appeal partly allowed.
