Ahmed Hussein Vali Mohammed Saiyed and Another v State of Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

12 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, K.G. Balakrishnan, J.M. Panchal

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 634; (2009) 7 SCC 254; (2009) 3 SCC (Cr) 368; AIR 2010 SC (Supp) 846; 2010 (3) MLJ(Crl) 646; 2009 (3) RCR(Criminal) 722; 2009(7) SCALE 282; [2009] 8 S.C.R. 719; 2009 (3) UC 1826

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Game, Joint Trial, Charge- Sheet, TADA Act, Terrorist and Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Rules, 1987

Summary: Criminal - Terrorist and Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Act, 1987, ss. 5, 15, 20(3) and 19 - Terrorist and Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Rules, 1987. rr. 15(3)(b) and 15(5) - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302 r/w 120B - Arms Act, ss. 25(1)(c) and 27 - Murder - Confession under TADA - Dying declaration - Gang rivalry - Liquor sellers - Conviction and sentence by Designated Court - Whether proper and justified? - Held, Yes - Confession of six accused persons - Conditions under provisions of TADA Act for confessional statement and method of recording confessional statements under Rules, deliberated upon; original records perused - Finding, all requirements satisfied as to that all of them were informed about the fact that there was no need and compulsions to make a statement and the same would be used against them in future in the very same case; all of them understood the entire procedure and made voluntary statement to the competent authority that was authorised to record their statement; narration of gang rivalry between the deceased and the main accused; further they also highlighted the number of persons involved in the conspiracy in the murder - Slight discrepancies found but further it was found that they were mere irregularities and were curable and were cured - All the confessional statements brought about similar depiction of facts - Confession by A27 was found to be perfectly as per requirement of law - No striking difference or discrepancy or ambiguity regarding the depiction of fact in each of the confessional statements that has been considered by the Court and all are corroborative to the confessional statement made by A27 - Other materials as to dying declaration, purchase of weapons and use thereof, from complaint to the stage of recording of confession, prosecution fully established its case - Conviction and sentence confirmed - Appeals dismissed.
