Liberty India v Commissioner of Income Tax

Supreme Court of India

31 August 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1073; (2009) 9 SCC 328; 2009 (241) E.L.T. 326; [2009] 317 ITR 218; 2009(12) SCALE 61; [2009] 13 S.C.R. 1037

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Deduction From Profits And Gains, Export Promotion

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss.80I, 80-IA, 80-IB - Customs Act, 1962, s.75 - Deduction u/s. 80-IB - Profit from Duty Entitlement Passbook Scheme (DEPB) and Duty Drawback Scheme - Appellant claimed deduction u/s. 80-IB on increased profits as profit of industrial undertaking on account of DEPB and Duty Drawback credited to Profit & Loss account - AO denied deduction u/s. 80-IB on ground that said two benefits constituted export incentives, and that they did not represent profits derived from industrial undertaking - On appeal, Commissioner held that duty drawback received by appellant was inextricably linked to production cost of goods manufactured by appellant, therefore, AO was not justified in denying deduction u/s. 80-IB - On appeal, Tribunal following decision of Delhi HC in case of CIT v. Ritesh Industries Ltd. 2004 INDLAW DEL 456, held that amount received by appellant on account of duty drawback was not an income derived from business of industrial undertaking so as to entitle appellant to deduction u/s. 80-IB - HC following decision of CIT v. Sterling Foods 1999 INDLAW SC 1201 held that appellant failed to prove nexus between receipt by way of duty drawback/DEPB benefit and industrial undertaking, hence, appellant was not entitled to deduction u/s. 80-IB(3) - Hence, present appeal - Whether profit from DEPB and Duty Drawback Scheme could be said to be profit derived from business of Industrial Undertaking eligible for deduction u/s. 80-IB of Income-tax Act, 1961? - Duty drawback receipt/DEPB benefits do not form part of net profits of eligible industrial undertaking for purposes of ss. 80I/80-IA/80-IB of Income Tax Act, 1961 - Held, DEPB credit under Scheme has to be calculated by taking into account deemed import content of export product as per basic customs duty and special additional duty payable on such deemed imports - Therefore, DEPB/Duty Drawback are incentives which flow from Schemes framed by Central Government or from s. 75 of Customs Act, 1962, hence, incentives profits are not profits derived from eligible business u/s. 80-IB - They belong to category of ancillary profits of such Undertakings - Hence, appellant not entitled to deduction u/s. 80-IB in respect of profit from DEPB and Duty Drawback Scheme - Appeals dismissed.
