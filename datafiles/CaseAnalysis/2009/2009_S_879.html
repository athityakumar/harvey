Shakson Belthissor v State of Kerala and Another

Supreme Court of India

06 July 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Mukundakam Sharma, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 851; (2009) 14 SCC 466; AIR 2010 SC (Supp) 864; 2009 ALL MR (Cri) 2752; 2009 (3) JCC 2233; 2010 (3) MLJ(Crl) 463; 2009(9) SCALE 70; [2009] 9 S.C.R. 1187

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Subsequent Conduct

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 482 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 498A, exp. (a) and (b) - FIR and charge sheet thereunder - Application for quashing - Rejected by the High Court - Whether in the FIR and in the charge sheet a case of s. 498A IPC is made out? - Held, No - Content of FIR and charge sheet noted, contentions heard and provisions of s. 498A IPC perused - Decisions on scope and power u/s. 498A of CrPC quoted - Marriage took place on 23-10-1997 and it was alleged that Rs. 5 lakhs was given by the parents of respondent no. 2 to the family of appellant as dowry; FIR was filed in the month of April, 2002 and in the said FIR there was no allegation that subsequent thereto any harassment was made by the appellant with a view to coercing her or any person related to respondent no. 2 to meet any unlawful demand or any property - Thus neither exp. (a) nor exp. (b) of s. 498A IPC attracted in the instant case - No ingredient of provisions of s. 498A IPC coming out from the FIR or charge sheet, which could prima facie constitute a case of cruelty as defined in that section - Held further, FIR and charge sheet to be liable to be quashed - High Court found to have committed an error on the face of the record - Proceedings commenced u/s. 498A quashed - Appeal allowed.
