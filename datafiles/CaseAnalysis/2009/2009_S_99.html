Haryana State Co-Operative Supply Marketing v Sanjay

Supreme Court of India

22 July 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 903; (2009) 14 SCC 43; AIR 2009 SC 3155; 2009(5) ALL MR 970; 2009 (2) CLR 1048; 2009 (4) ESC 682; 2009 (122) FLR 850; JT 2009 (9) SC 475; 2009 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 1949; 2009 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 2596; 2009 (4) LLJ 53; 2009 (4) LLN 20; 2009 (6) MLJ(SC) 577; 2010 (157) PLR 238; 2009(9) SCALE 731; [2009] 11 S.C.R. 626; 2009 (6) SLR 184

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Failure Report

Summary: (A) Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Dispute Act, 1947, ss. 25-F, 25B (2) - Continuity of service - Applicability of s. 25-F - Workman to show that he has been in continuous service for not less than one year under an employer - A workman deemed to be in continuous service for a period of one year if he worked 12 calendar months - Workman has to work for not less than 240 days by virtue of 25B (2) of the Act for entitling the continuity of service.P /&gt;(B) Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Dispute Act, ss. 25-F r/w 25B (2) - Termination - Validity - Respondent was employed as chowkidar in different periods in different offices - Termination after the expiry of term of contract- Failure to follow procedure in s. 25-F, alleged - Continuity of service claimed - Held, respondent was employed in two distinct and separate establishment - Respondent was employed casually by separate authorities under different contracts - Employment in different establishments of an institution cannot be considered as continuous service under one employer - Employer-officers are under the same superior does not cease the concept of separate establishment - No proof was produced before the Labour Court for sustaining the contention that the respondent was transferred from one office to another - Therefore, services rendered by respondent at two establishments cannot be reckoned for continuity of service u/s. 25-F r/w 25B of the Act - Respondent not entitled the continuity of service - Appeal allowed.
