Amlan Jyoti Borooah v State Of Assam & Ors

Supreme Court of India

23 January 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, V.S. Sirpurkar

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 2110; (2009) 3 SCC 227; (2009) 1 SCC (L&S) 627; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 1903; 2009 (1) ESC 149; JT 2009 (3) SC 361; 2009 (3) MLJ(SC) 513; 2009(2) SCALE 56; [2009] 1 S.C.R. 593; 2009 (4) SLR 588

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 and 16 - Selection process - Respondents applied for vacancies advertisement - Respondents asked by a call letter to appear at the written test and physical ability test - However, despite laying down the procedures in the advertisement, a decision was taken by the Selection Committee to call such candidates for interview only who had acquired 40% in the written test - Call letters were issued accordingly - Appellants as also the private respondents amongst others appeared in the interview - Selection Committee short listed 1803 candidates - Appellant's position in the select list was 750 - Thereafter, a sanction was obtained by Inspector General of Police for appointment of 174 Sub-Inspector of Police - Candidates who were found eligible to be called for physical ability test were asked to appear therein and 169 candidates who had cleared the physical test/medical test were appointed on the post of Sub-Inspector of Police - During the currency of the life of the select list, a sanction was sought to fill up additional vacancies - Pursuant thereto, the Home Commissioner was requested to sanction 80 posts in the existing vacancies in place of 77 vacancies - 75 candidates were found suitable for appointment - Appellant and some other candidates filed a writ petition before HC impugning the said selection - Single Judge opined that fundamental rights of the appellants u/art. 14 and 16 of the Constitution have been infringed by the procedure adopted and yardstick applied did not result in a correct determination of the inter se merit of all the candidates for the purpose of securing appointment - Single Judge set aside the appointment - On appeal, DB while upholding the appointment of the private respondents, set aside the judgment of the Single Judge - Present appeal - (A) Change in selection process - Held, a Selection Committee in a given situation, may lay down a procedure for the purpose of short listing the candidates but that does not mean that for the said purpose the order of holding a requisite test would be changed - In the present case, physical ability test keeping in view the nature of the job required to be performed by the candidates was an extremely important one - Passing in the physical ability test is a sine qua non for selection of the candidates in the post of Sub-Inspector of Police - Merit list, thus, should have been prepared not only on the basis of the written test and interview but also the physical ability test - Selection Committee committed a serious error in changing the order of holding the tests - Single Judge, therefore, was correct in arriving at a conclusion that physical ability test should have been held prior to holding of the interview - (B) Validity of selection process - Held, appellant was aware of his position in the select list - He was also aware of the change in the procedure adopted by the Selection Committee - He appeared at the interview without any demur - Appellant, having accepted the change in the selection procedure sub silentio, by not questioning the appointment of 169 candidates, cannot now be permitted to turn round and contend that the procedure adopted was illegal - Appellant estopped and precluded from doing so - Appeal dismissed.
