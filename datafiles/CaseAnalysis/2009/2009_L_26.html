Brihan Mumbai Electric Supply Transport v Laqshya Media Private Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 December 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, K.G. Balakrishnan, J.M. Panchal

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1587; (2010) 1 SCC 620; AIR 2010 SC 5; 2010 (1) Bom.C.R. 87; JT 2009 (14) SC 495; 2009 (8) MLJ(SC) 1554; 2009(14) SCALE 247; [2009] 15 S.C.R. 1218

Case Digest

Subject: Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Disposal Of Property

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Tenders - Quashing of - Mumbai Municipal Corporation Act, 1888, s. 460M - Appellant invited tenders for the erection of Bus Queue Shelters (BQS) - 1st and 2nd respondents participated in the tender process along with 4th and 5th respondents - Appellant awarded contracts to 4th and 5th respondents who had valid existing licences for the display of advertisements on all BQS - 1st and 2nd respondent challenged the awarding of contracts before HC - HC quashed the award of contract, holding that appellant had awarded the contract on the basis of negotiation on the terms and conditions offered by 4th and 5th respondents bypassing the statutory provisions - (A) Whether the appellants were justified in awarding work order/contract in favour of 4th and 5th respondents without resorting to public tender? - Held, disposal of public properties owned by the State or its instrumentalities should be as per a public auction by inviting tenders - However, respondent Nos. 4 and 5 were having existing agreement with the appellant administration for the display of advertisements in the bus shelters/poles - Due to enormous financial implication, appellant was not in a position to undertake demolition and construction of new BQS - Appellant without spending their money through the existing contracts with respondent Nos. 4 and 5 could complete the modernization work and also earn sizeable income by way of advertisement - Therefore, respondent Nos. 4 and 5 were permitted to build bus shelters as per the norms, display advertisements and pay charges to the appellant in terms of contract - (B) Whether Proviso to s. 460M(2) of the Act enables the appellant to award contract without inviting tenders? - Held, Proviso to s. 460M(2) enables General Manager to deviate the process of inviting tenders subject to reasons duly recorded by the Empowered Committee of appellant authority - Empowered Committee of appellant consisting of the Chief Secretary and other senior officers of various departments after deliberation and taking various relevant aspects authorized the General Manager to finalise the work with Respondent Nos. 4 and 5 since they are having existing contract in advertisement in bus shelters/poles - (C) Whether HC is justified in setting aside the work order/contract to respondent Nos. 4 and 5 and directing appellant to invite fresh tenders as required s. 460M(1) of the Act? - Held, respondent No. 4 has constructed about 1/3rd of the total BQS allotted to it and respondent No. 5 has constructed about 40% of the BQS allotted to it - HC while passing the impugned orders at the request of respondent Nos. 4 and 5, suspended its order for a period of eight weeks and SC while ordering notice on the present proceedings, extended the order of stay of HC order - Respondent Nos. 4 and 5 were continuing the contract work throughout during the pendency of the proceeding before the HC and in the SC - Therefore, in order to render substantial and complete justice to the parties, contract by appellant with the respondent Nos. 4 and 5 ordered to be continued - Impugned HC order modified and direction issued to respondent Nos. 4 and 5 to adhere to the terms of contract strictly - Appeals disposed of.
