Surendra Kumar Bhatia v Kanhaiya Lal and Others

Supreme Court of India

30 January 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, J.M. Panchal

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 80; (2009) 12 SCC 184; AIR 2009 SC 1961; 2009 ALL MR (Cri) 1821; JT 2009 (2) SC 1; 2009 (1) RCR(Civil) 809; 2009 (1) RCR(Criminal) 816; 2009(2) SCALE 80; [2009] 1 S.C.R. 774

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Body Of Persons, Revenue Record, Judicial Capacity

Summary: Criminal - Cr.P.C, s. 482 - Appeal against order quashing FIR - (A) Whether the immunity under s. 77 IPC is available to a Collector/Land Acquisition Officer/Special Officer who makes an award, by way of settlement or otherwise, under the provisions of Land Acquisition Act, 1894? - Held, Collector is neither a Judge as defined under s. 19 nor does he act judicially, when discharging any of the functions under the Act - Therefore he is not entitled to the protection under s. 77 - (B) Whether the allegations in the FIR did not constitute an offence under ss. 420, 467, 468 and 471 of IPC - Held, High Court was justified in holding that the allegations even if taken at their face value did not prima facie constitute an offence under ss. 420, 467, 468 and 471 IPC, by respondents 1 and 2 - Quashing of the FIR with reference to the then Special Officer is set aside - Appeal partly allowed.
