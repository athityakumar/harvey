Chaman Lal & Ors. v State Of Punjab & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

31 March 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Lokeshwar Singh Panta, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 393; (2009) 11 SCC 721; AIR 2009 SC 2972; JT 2009 (4) SC 662; 2009 (3) RCR(Criminal) 19; 2009(5) SCALE 12; [2009] 5 S.C.R. 351

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - IPC, 1860, ss. 409 r/w 120B, 420 r/w 120 B - Whether High Court erred in holding that there are sufficient grounds to presume that the unrebutted evidences of the complainant constitute triable offences? - Held, essence of criminal conspiracy is an agreement to do an illegal act and such an agreement can be proved either by direct evidence or by circumstantial evidence or by both, and it is a matter of common experience that direct evidence to prove conspiracy is rarely available - Circumstances proved before, during and after the occurrence have to be considered to decide about the complicity of the accused - High Court has rightly observed that the charges have to be established beyond reasonable doubt before the prosecution can succeed, but at that stage the challenge can be made - Appeal dismissed.
