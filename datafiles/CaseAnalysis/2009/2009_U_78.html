Union Of India v Shah Alam & Anr

Supreme Court of India

11 June 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Aftab Alam, B. Sudarshan Reddy

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 758; (2009) 16 SCC 644; AIR 2010 SC 1785; 2009 (3) JCC (Narcotics) 108; 2009 (2) KLT 1023; 2009 (3) MLJ(Crl) 109; 2009 (3) RCR(Criminal) 1158; 2009(8) SCALE 735; [2009] 9 S.C.R. 1118; 2009 (2) UC 1100

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Personal Consumption, Non- Examination, Religious Belief

Summary: Criminal - Narcotics and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985, ss. 8 r/w 21 and 50 - Act as stood amended w.e.f. 02-10-2001 vide Act 9 of 2001, s. 21(b) - Conditions under which search of persons shall be conducted - Conviction by Trial Court - Reversed by High Court - Whether proper? - Held, Yes - Court noted the difference between the law as it was at the time of commission of offence and as it stands now after the amendment; difference is vast in terms of substance and in terms of punishment, Court noted - Before amendment ten years RI and fine of Rs. 1 lakh was the minimum punishment but now it is the maximum - Court said that it could have declined to interfere in the matter on the ground that the respondents have already served 4/5th of the (now) maximum permissible punishment for the offence, but still it examined the case on its merits and perused the judgement of HC - HC had set aside the judgment of TC on two grounds, firstly, recovery of heroin was made from the respondents without observing the conditions laid down in s. 50 of the Act in regard to search and secondly, the non-examination of the two independent witnesses in whose presence the recovery and seizures were made - Records of the case perused i.e. evidence of complainant, PW1 and seizure memo - Two respondents were subjected to a body search in course of which packets of heroin were found in the shoulder bags carried by them and were recovered from there - Recovery found to be in complete violation of s. 50 of Act; another grave omission was non-examination of the two independent witnesses of the search and recovery - Impugned judgement upheld - Being without merit - Appeals dismissed.
