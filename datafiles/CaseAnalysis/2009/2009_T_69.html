The State Of Andhra Pradesh v Vangaveeti Nagaiah

Supreme Court of India

15 April 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Lokeshwar Singh Panta, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 496; (2009) 12 SCC 466; AIR 2009 SC 2646; 2009 ALL MR (Cri) 2466; JT 2009 (13) SC 647; 2010 (5) RCR(Criminal) 548B; 2009(5) SCALE 694; [2009] 6 S.C.R. 160

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Private Complainant, A.P. Prohibition Act, 1995

Summary: Criminal - Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 482 - A.P. Prohibition Act, 1995, ss. 7(A) r/w 8(e) - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 109 - Quashing of FIR - Whether justified? - Nature of offences coming out of FIR - Deliberation upon nature and scope of HC's power u/s. 482, decision in R.P.Kapoor's case and Bhajan Lal's case, looked into - Principle 'quando lex a liquid aliquot concedit, conceditur et id sine quo res ipsa esse non potest (when the law gives a person anything it gives him that without which it cannot exist) - Exercise of inherent powers to quash criminal proceedings is called for only in a case where the complaint does not disclose any offence or is frivolous, vexatious or oppressive - No meticulous analysis of the case is required to look out that whether the case would end in conviction or acquittal - Complaint/FIR has to be read as a whole - FIR, Framing of Charge, Trial; Quashing of FIR, Discharge, acquittal/conviction, respectively - Material already in existence or to be collected during investigation, whether would be sufficient for holding the concerned accused guilty has to be considered at time of trial - Even at stage of framing of charge, which comes after FIR, investigation and submission of police report, evidence cannot be gone into meticulously; if there are materials showing possibility about the commission of crime as against certainty, charges can be framed - Court emphasized from this, that the interference at the threshold with the FIR is to be done in very exceptional circumstances; R.P.Kapoor's case - Court observed that instant is not the case where it can be said that FIR did not disclose commission of an offence - Quashing of FIR held not to be justified - Appeal allowed.
