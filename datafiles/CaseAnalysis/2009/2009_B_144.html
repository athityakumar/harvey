Director, Fisheries Terminal Division v Bhikubhai Meghajibhai Chavda

Supreme Court of India

09 November 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Dattu, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1520; (2010) 1 SCC 47; AIR 2010 SC 1236; 2010 (2) AWC 1749; 2010 (1) G.L.R. 731; 2010 (1) JhrCR(SC) 185; 2010 (1) LLJ 3; 2010 (1) MLJ(SC) 130; 2010 (2) MPLJ 30; 2010 (157) PLR 243; 2009(13) SCALE 636; [2009] 15 S.C.R. 761; 2009 (6) SLR 632

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Seasonal

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Dispute Act, 1947, s. 25F - Respondent watchman was terminated from service - Respondent filed claim before the Labour Court - Labour Court directed appellant, a fisheries industry, to reinstate the respondent into service with 20% back wages - Appellant challenged the award before HC - HC dismissed the writ petition of appellant - Appellant contended that the appellant industry is seasonal in nature and respondent was employed on temporary basis - Held, no documents have been produced by the appellants to state that the fisheries industry is seasonal - No mention of any decision on the part of the appropriate Government with regard to declaring fisheries as a seasonal industry - Appellants have inexplicably failed to produce the complete records and muster rolls during the relevant period in order to prove that the respondent did not work for 240 days - Procedure laid down in s.25G has also not been followed by appellant while discharging the respondent - Findings on facts by the labour cannot be termed as perverse and need no interference - Appeal dismissed.
