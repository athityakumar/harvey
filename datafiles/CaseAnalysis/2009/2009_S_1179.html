Haru Ghosh v State of West Bengal

Supreme Court of India

27 August 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

V.S. Sirpurkar, Deepak Verma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1051; (2009) 15 SCC 551; 2009 ALL MR (Cri) 3097; 2009 (4) MLJ(Crl) 48; 2009(11) SCALE 782; [2009] 13 S.C.R. 847

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Bride Burning, Custodial Death, Police Witness, Mitigating Circumstance, Blood Stained Clothes

Summary: (A) Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss.302, 326 - Murder and attempt to murder - Conviction - Two persons were hacked to death and on another an attempt to murder was made by appellant-accused, alleged - Appellant convicted for murder and attempt to murder - Appellant sentenced to death by Trial Court - Conviction confirmed by HC - Legality - Held, PW1 to PW6 who were neighbors, graphically described about the murderous attack of appellant - Nothing suspicious came from testimony of witnesses who were neighbors - Medical evidence showed that appellant had intention to commit murder - No enmity between witnesses and appellant was brought out in cross-examination - PW12 who was subjected to attempt to murder by appellant, had given direct evidence - No further evidence required - Conviction for murder and attempt to murder by Trial Court and HC were correct and therefore upheld - However, additional conviction for attempt to murder separately by Trial Court was not necessary, hence the same set asideP /&gt;(B) Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss.302, 326 - Rarest of rare case - Death sentence - Award of - Appellant convicted for murder and attempt to murder - Appellant sentenced to death by Trial Court - Death sentence confirmed by HC - Legality - Held, outcome of appeal pending before HC with regard to one of appellant's earlier conviction would be taken into consideration - Appellant had not come pre-armed at spot - No aspect of pre-meditation in act of appellant which was at spur of moment - Case would not be a pre-meditated cold blooded murder - Moreover, appellant has two minor children - Case was not a rarest of rare case, hence death sentence was not confirmed - However, life imprisonment with span of not less than 35 years substituted death sentence, meaning thereby appellant/accused would have to remain in for a minimum of 35 years - Appeal disposed of.
