M. D. Thomas v P. S. Jaleel and Another

Supreme Court of India

13 April 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Agrawal, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1649; (2009) 14 SCC 398; 2009 (3) BC 659; 2009 (2) KLT 990; 2010 (3) MLJ(Crl) 556; 2010 (4) RCR(Criminal) 417

Case Digest

Subject: Contract & Commercial

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, ss. 138, 138(b) - Dishonour of cheque - Conviction – Absence of proper notice - Respondent/ complainant filed complaint against appellant for dishonour of cheque - Appellant/accused was charged u/s. 138 of the Act and on conclusion of his trial, he was acquitted of said charge by Trial Court - Respondent filed appeal against order of Trial Court before HC which set aside order of acquittal and convicted appellant - Hence instant appeal.

Held, it was urged by appellant that respondent did not serve upon him notice as per requirement of s. 138(b) of the Act. Appellant submitted that service of notice on appellant's wife cannot be treated as compliance of mandate of law. Dishonour of cheque for insufficiency of funds is dealt by s.138 of the Act. Proviso to s. 138 of the Act specifies conditions which are required to be satisfied before person can be convicted for an offence enumerated in substantive part of section. Proviso to s. 138 of the Act cast on payee or holder in due course of cheque, as case may be, duty to make demand for payment of said amount of money by giving notice in writing, to drawer of cheque, within thirty days of receipt of information by him from bank regarding return of the cheque as unpaid. Notice of demand was served upon wife of appellant and not appellant. Therefore, there is no escape from conclusion that respondent had not complied with requirement of giving notice in terms of s. 138(b) of the Act. Unfortunately, HC overlooked important lacuna in respondent’s case. Therefore, conviction of appellant cannot be sustained. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Issuance of notice to accused u/s. 138(b) of the Act must be complied with before accused can be convicted of an offence.
