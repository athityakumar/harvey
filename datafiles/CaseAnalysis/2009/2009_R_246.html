State of Karnataka and Another v Ravi Kumar

Supreme Court of India

10 February 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 185; (2009) 13 SCC 746; 2009 (2) CLR 11; 2009 (122) FLR 737; 2009 (3) LLJ 206; 2009 (3) LLN 604; 2009 (7) SLR 485

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Respondent was appointed as a cleaner on daily wage basis - Termination - Held, respondent did not choose to challenge the termination for 14 years - Merely because some other daily wagers had got some relief, he belatedly approached the High Court in 1998 - Petition was dismissed with an observation that the respondent was at liberty to make an application seeking reference - As the reference was stale, it ought to have been rejected on that ground alone - Appeal allowed.
