Laxmidas Morarji (Dead ) By Lrs. v Miss Behrose Darab Madan

Supreme Court of India

18 September 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Dattu, B.N. Agrawal, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1154; (2009) 10 SCC 425; 2009 (77) ALR 699; 2010 (1) Bom.C.R. 452; 2010 (1) G.L.R. 825; JT 2009 (12) SC 291; 2009 (2) RCR(Rent) 347; 2009(12) SCALE 678; [2009] 14 S.C.R. 777

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Rent Control

Keywords: Civil Appeals dismissed, Bombay Rents, Hotel and Lodging House Rates Control (Amendment) Act, 1978

Summary: (A) Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, art.142 - Enforcement of decrees and orders - Power of SC - SC cannot pass an order or grant relief u/art.142, which is totally inconsistent or goes against substantive or statutory enactments - However, power is to be used sparingly in cases which cannot be effectively and appropriately tackled by existing provisions of law or when existing provisions of law cannot bring about complete justice between parties.P /&gt;(B) Rent Control - Bombay Rents, Hotel and Lodging Houses Rates Control Act, 1947, s.28 - Suit for eviction - Small Causes Court - Jurisdiction of - Suit for eviction filed by appellant landlord against respondent tenant for arrears of rent and recovery of possession - Suit dismissed by Small Causes Court - Appellant's appeal before First Appellate Court dismissed - Issuance of writ sought - Having held that respondent would be entitled to inherit tenancy of suit premises, writ petition of appellant dismissed by HC - Review Petition of appellants also dismissed by HC - Legality - Contention by appellant that Small Causes Court had no jurisdiction to entertain suit - Held, appellants did not consider respondent as a tenant - Appellants having gone on to adduce evidence to prove that respondent does not qualify the conditions to be deemed as tenant u/s.28 Act - Therefore, Small Causes Court had no jurisdiction to entertain suit filed by appellant - Further, direction issued to the effect that a period from the date of institution of original suit till the date of present judgment should be excluded in computing period of limitation in filling fresh suit - Referred decision of SC in Om Prakash Gupta v. Rattan Singh and Ors. (1962 INDLAW SC 315) - Civil Appeals dismissed.
