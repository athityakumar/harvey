Food Corporation Of India v Sukh Deo Prasad

Supreme Court of India

24 March 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 348; (2009) 5 SCC 665; AIR 2009 SC 2330; 2009 (4) ALD(SC) 68; 2009 (3) AWC 2220; 2009 (2) BC 301; 2009 (2) RCR(Civil) 834; 2009(4) SCALE 318; [2009] 4 S.C.R. 1107; 2009 (3) UC 1700

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Keywords: Garnishee, Non- Compliance, Supplemental Proceedings, Debt Secured

Summary: Civil Procedure - CPC, 1908, O. 39 r. 2A - (A) What is the purport and effect of the order described by the trial court and the High Court as the 'injunction order'? - Held, if there was no prayer in the suit against FCI, obviously no interim relief could have been sought against FCI as a defendant - Order was not an injunction order, but an interim prohibitory (garnishee) order by way of attachment before judgment, in regard to the rents payable for one godown taken by it on lease - (B) Whether the respondent, who was the first defendant in the mortgage suit filed by the bank, could maintain an application under O. 39 r. 2A of the Code for the alleged disobedience by FCI (a co-defendant), of the order dated 27.5.1996 made in an application filed by the plaintiff bank? - Held, if a garnishee, or a defendant, who is directed to pay any sum of money, does not pay the amount, the remedy is to levy execution and not in an action for contempt or disobedience/breach under O. 39 r. 2A - (C) Whether the trial court was justified in allowing such application under O. 39 r. 2A, holding that FCI was liable to pay the rents for three godowns? - Any claim for rent against the defendant in regard to any period beyond three years would be barred by limitation, in the absence of any acknowledgement or payment on account - High Court chose to summarily dispose of the appeal thereby allowing perpetration of a patent abuse of process of court by the respondent - Appeal allowed.
