Mahender Pal and Others v State of Haryana and Others

Supreme Court of India

15 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 663; (2009) 14 SCC 281; AIR 2009 SC 3220; 2009 (76) ALR 170; 2009 (4) AWC 3464; JT 2009 (13) SC 670; 2009 (5) MLJ(SC) 778; 2009 (107) RD 784; 2009(8) SCALE 485; [2009] 9 S.C.R. 418

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 17(4)(2) and 5A - Emergency power u/s. 17 - Whether in the facts and circumstances of the case the emergency powers in terms of s. 17 could have been resorted to by the State? - Notification u/s. 4 r/w 17(4) and a declaration u/s. 6 for acquiring land for public purpose, i.e. for development and utilization of land for outer ring road - Land to be acquired was being used for residential purposes and which had Samadhis of the ancestors of the appellants and a Shiva temple; revenue records confirming the existence of these pucca constructions - Objections filed - Writ petition preferred - Dismissed in limine - Object and purpose of emergency powers deliberated upon - S. 17(4) is an exception to s. 5A - Opinion of Govt. to be formed while exercising s. 17(4) power; existence of foundational fact for invoking such provision is thus a sine qua non for formation of opinion; such an subjective satisfaction must be based on an objective criteria - Finding, appellants made out their case for detailed examination of their cases - Matter remitted for consideration afresh - Impugned judgement set aside - Appeal allowed.
