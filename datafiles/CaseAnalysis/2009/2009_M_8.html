Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Photostat Copy

Summary: Family & Personal - Practice & Procedure - Hindu Marriage Act, 1955, s. 13B(2) - Civil and criminal proceedings - Quashing of - Divorce - Petitioner (husband) and respondent (wife) filed several civil and criminal proceedings against each other before the Lower Courts, at Meerut - Petitioner filed a divorce petition in the Court of District Judge, at Delhi - Petitioner and respondent entered into a compromise/settlement - Hence, instant transfer petitions be disposed of in terms of compromise/settlement - Held, it was an agreed position that in terms of deed of settlement/compromise deed, petitioner (husband) has paid a sum of Rs.13,50,000/- to respondent (wife) towards full and final settlement by way of permanent alimony/maintenance - Fact that they have amicably settled all their disputes and are keen to put a quietus to all litigations between them - A decree of divorce by mutual consent was passed in terms of s. 13B of the Act by waiving the requirement of 6 months period specified in s. 13B(2) of the Act - Petitions disposed of.
