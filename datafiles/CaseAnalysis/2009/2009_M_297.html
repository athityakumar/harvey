Krishna Bhagya Jala Nigam Limited v Mohammed Rafi

Supreme Court of India

28 April 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1901; (2009) 11 SCC 522; 2009 (3) AWC 2734; 2009 (4) LLJ 48; 2009 (3) LLN 629; 2009 (5) MLJ(SC) 973; 2009(6) SCALE 655; [2009] 7 S.C.R. 109; 2011 (6) SLR 9

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Cooperative

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Dispute Act, 1947, s. 25-F - Termination of service - Service of respondent-workman was terminated by appellant-company - Respondent raised industrial dispute - Labour Court set aside the termination order and directed appellant to reinstate the respondent - Appellant challenged the award before HC - Single Judge allowed appellant's writ petition, however, order of Single Judge set aside by DB on the ground that appellant failed to show that the respondent had not completed 240 days of service in relevant period - Held, initial burden of proof was on the workman to show that he had completed 240 days of service and not on appellant - Impugned order of DB set aside - Appeal allowed.
