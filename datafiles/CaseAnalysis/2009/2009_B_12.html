G. Suryakumari And Another v B. Chandramouli And Others

Supreme Court of India

18 December 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Deepak Verma, B. SUDARSHAN REDDY

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 2044; (2010) 2 SCC 254; 2010 (2) ALD(SC) 82; 2010 (78) ALR 699; 2010 (1) AWC 759; 2010 (1) CLT(SC) 214; JT 2009 (15) SC 419; 2010 (3) MPLJ 147; 2010 (109) RD 373; 2009(14) SCALE 711; [2009] 16 S.C.R. 758

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, s. 100, o. 9 Rule IX - Perpetual injunction - Respondents filed original suit against appellants for relief of perpetual injunction restraining them from interfering into their peaceful possession and enjoyment over impugned property which appellants claimed to have been purchased from a society - Impugned property found to be in exclusive possession of respondents - Trial Court decreed suit in favour of respondents and granted temporary injunction - On appeal, HC upheld order of trial court - Hence, present appeal - Whether HC's order could be upheld? - Held, appellants tried to advance some questions of law with regard to o. 9 Rule IX of CPC - At no point of time earlier it was neither raised nor argued or hammered at stage of Trial Court, first appellate Court and HC, thus it cannot be permitted to be taken up for consideration for first time - This Court refrained from taking cognizance of those grounds which were sought to be taken for first time - HC's order upheld - Appeal dismissed.
