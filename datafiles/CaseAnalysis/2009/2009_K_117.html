R. K. Malik and Another v Kiran Pal and Others

Supreme Court of India

15 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Mukundakam Sharma, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 656; (2009) 14 SCC 1; 2009 (2) ACC 705; AIR 2009 SC 2506; 2009 (4) AWC 3347; 2009 (3) JhrCR(SC) 72; 2009 (5) MLJ(SC) 765; 2009 (155) PLR 35; 2009 (3) RCR(Civil) 403; 2009(8) SCALE 451; [2009] 10 S.C.R. 87

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Keywords: Appeals Disposed Of, Higher Compensation, Non-Pecuniary Damages, Expectation Of Life, Loss Of Expectation Of Life, Measure Of Damages, Loss Of Future Earning, Purchasing Power Of Money

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, ss. 163A, Sch. II, 166 r/w 168 - Vehicular accident - Claim of compensation - Prospective pecuniary loss or loss of dependency; Notional income - Non pecuniary loss; 'Standard compensation' or 'Conventional amount/sum' - Bus carrying school children met with an accident and fell into river Yamuna from the Yamuna Bridge - 29 children dead - Motor Accident Claims Tribunal(MACT) held respondents jointly and severally liable and awarded compensation inter alia applying concept of notional income and multiplier as per Sch. II of Act - In appeal High Court enhanced compensation by Rs. 75, 000/- on account of non-pecuniary damages plus awarded interest @ 7.5% - Present appeal thereagainst - Whether compensation awarded by Tribunal on account of pecuniary damage and enhancement made by HC on account of non-pecuniary damage, proper and justified? - Held, Yes - Discussion entered into on damages on account of prospective pecuniary loss or loss of dependency and on account of non-pecuniary losses on account of loss of happiness, pain, suffering and expectancy of life - Court noted, extreme difficulty to quantify non-pecuniary compensation as it is to a great extent based upon the sentiments and emotions, but still compensation under this head is to be determined Court said; Court used expressions like 'standard compensation' and 'conventional amount/sum' to get over such difficulty which ensures consistency and uniformity in awarding compensations; pain and suffering under this head does not depend upon the financial position of the victim or the claimant but rather on the capacity and the ability of the deceased to provide happiness to the claimant; this compensation is paid for loss of prospective happiness which the claimant/victim would have enjoyed had the child not been died at the tender age; Court in 'Lata Wadhwa v. State of Bihar, (2001) 8 SCC 197' case referred to Rs. 50, 000/- as conventional figure; Court further cautioned that unless there is a specific case departing from the conventional formula, non- pecuniary compensation should not be fixed on basis of economic wealth and background - HC's awarding of compensation under non-pecuniary damage found to be justified and upheld - Submission raised by claimants that both Tribunal and HC failed to consider claims with regard to the future prospects of the children - Submission accepted by the Court and finding made that evidence in that regard had been ignored by the Courts below; well settled legal principle, that in addition to awarding compensation for pecuniary losses, compensation must also be granted with regard to the future prospects of the children; while considering such claims, child's performance in school, the reputation of the school etc. might be taken into consideration; and in the present case record showed that children were good in studies and studying in reasonably good school, thus their future prospect would be presumed to be good and bright; thus in facts of case and following decision in Lata Wadhwa and M.S. Grewal's case compensation in this behalf awarded to the tune of Rs. 75, 000 over and above what has been awarded by the HC; appropriate direction in that behalf issued - Appeals disposed of.
