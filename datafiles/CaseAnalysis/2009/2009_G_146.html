National Insurance Company Limited v Gurumallamma and Another

Supreme Court of India

23 July 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1109; (2009) 16 SCC 43; 2009 (3) ACC 619; 2009(5) ALL MR 436; 2009 (4) AWC 3210; 2010 (1) KarLJ 177; 2009(9) SCALE 764; [2009] 11 S.C.R. 298

Case Digest

Subject: Insurance

Keywords: Non-obstante Clause, General Damages

Summary: Insurance - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, s.163A - Motor Vehicle Accident - Compensation - Determination of - Application of Second Schedule - Respondent's son died in an accident - Tribunal awarded compensation to claimant respondent by applying multiplier system of 17 - Appellant Insurance Company filed appeal before HC - HC dismissed appeal - Legality - Appellant contended that multiplier of 13 should have been applied instead of 17 - Held, claimant filed application u/s.163A of Act - Multiplier system would not be applicable in fatal accidental cases - Tribunal required to be determined amount as per Second Schedule - Deceased was running a hotel - Deceased was having some income - No document relating to income of deceased produced by respondent - Basis of making claim of deceased's income has not been disclosed - Deceased was not an income tax payee - Income of deceased should be determined at Rs. 24, 000/- p.a. - Claimant would have been entitled to a compensation of Rs. 4, 22, 000/- - However, one third of such calculated compensation should be deducted - Appeal partly allowed.
