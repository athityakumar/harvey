State of Andhra Pradesh v S. Swarnalatha and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 August 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, R.M. Lodha

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 988; (2009) 8 SCC 383; (2009) 3 SCC (Cr) 873; 2009 (4) JCC 2800; JT 2009 (13) SC 383; 2010 (5) RCR(Criminal) 453; 2009(11) SCALE 7; [2009] 12 S.C.R. 289

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Extra Judicial Confession

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss.120B, 201, 302 and 392 - Murder - Conspiracy to murder - Circumstantial evidence - Trial Court acquitted all accused persons from charges u/ss. 120B, 392 and 201 but convicted them for offence of murder - On appeal, HC acquitted all the accused persons - Appeal against acquittal - Deceased were in-laws of accused No.1 - Entire prosecution case based on circumstantial evidence - Prosecution mainly relied upon evidence of PW3 and PW6 - No eye-witness to occurrence - Extra judicial confession purported to have been made by accused Nos. 1 to PW6 doubtful - Unexplained delay in recording statements of PW3 and PW6 - Evidence of PW3 and PW6 not trustworthy - No motive assign for commission of offence - Held, judgment of HC a well reasoned one and view taken by HC is a plausible one - No interference required - Appeals dismissed.
