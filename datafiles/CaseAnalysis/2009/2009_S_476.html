Bhaskar Ramappa Madar And Ors. v State Of Karnataka

Supreme Court of India

31 March 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 391; (2009) 11 SCC 690; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 1826; 2009 CRLJ 2422; 2009 (3) JCC 1622; JT 2009 (4) SC 640; 2009(5) SCALE 100; [2009] 5 S.C.R. 256

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Demeanour Of Witness, Power Of The Court, Finding Of Facts, Hearing Of Appeal

Summary: Criminal - IPC, 1860, ss. 304B, 306, 498A r/ 34 - Whether High Court is right in reversing the judgment of acquittal? - Held, merely because the complainant conducted the investigation, that would not be sufficient to cast doubt on the prosecution version to hold that the same makes the prosecution version vulnerable - An appellate court has full power to review, reappreciate and reconsider the evidence upon which the order of acquittal is founded - A person has, no doubt, a profound right not to be convicted of an offence which is not established by the evidential standard of proof beyond reasonable doubt - There is no analysis of the conclusions of the trial Court by the High Court - Interference by the High Court with the order of acquittal is not correct - Appeal allowed.
