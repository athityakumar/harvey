Residents Welfare Association, Noida v State of Utta Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

15 April 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Tarun Chatterjee, Arijit Pasayat

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 520; (2009) 14 SCC 716; 2009 (4) ADJ 204; 2009 (75) ALR 47; 2009 (3) AWC 2580; 2009 (3) RCR(Civil) 87; 2009 (3) RD 744; 2009(6) SCALE 94; [2009] 6 S.C.R. 112

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Fixtures, Assignees

Summary: Land & Property - UP Stamp Act, s. 47A, Sch. 1-B, arts. 23 and 63 - Indian Stamp Act, 1899, ss. 2(10)(16)- Transfer of Property Act, 1882, ss. 3, 54 and 105 - Stamp Act of Tamil Nadu and Pondicherry - Lease deed executed by NOIDA(New Okhla Industrial Development Area), para(b) of Section III - Applicability of which article for purpose of stamp duty - Nature of transaction - Nature of document under transaction - 'Sale' - 'Lease' - 'Conveyance' - 'Transfer of lease by way of assignment'; 'conveyance by way of sale' - Allotment of lands by NOIDA to several Co-operative Housing Societies by execution of lease deeds - Conditions of lease, one of it was that transfer of leasehold rights was not to be made without prior consent of NOIDA authorities by transfer memorandum - NOIDA the lessor, societies the lessee, its members sub-lessee, appellant the assignee - Members of appellant/association entering into agreements, for transfer of leasehold rights with societies and its members - Agreements registered with Sub-Registrar, NOIDA - Facts, Noida authorities were injuncted by Civil Court from 1984 to 1997 from issuing transfer memorandums granting permission for transfer of leasehold rights; agreements were denoted as agreements of sale by parties - On 01-07-1997 transfer memorandums were issued on payment of transfer premium - One of the condition of issuing memorandums was that lessees are to execute a regular deeds of transfer with the assignees and to get the same registered with the Sub-Registrar, Noida - On question of stamp duty, Sub-registrar decided that it is payable as applicable to conveyance u/art. 23 of Sch. 1-B of Stamp Act, on the basis of current market value of plot alongwith the constructed portion thereon - Decision of Sub-registrar challenged before High Court in writ - Rejected - Present appeal - (A) Nature of document/deed - Facts noted, lease deed specifically provided for 99 years of land alongwith its appurtenances thereto, with the right of reversion - Nomenclature given to a document cannot be a determining factor for deciding the nature of document - Provisions perused - Finding, document in question a lease and the transfer to the members of appellant/association an assignment of leasehold right - Held, deed presented for registration was a deed of assignment - (B) Which of the article applicable for purpose of Stamp Duty? - Arts. 63 & 23 looked into - Held, art. 63 - Art. 63 in clear terms mentions that in case of an assignment, the duty that would be payable is the same duty as conveyance for a consideration equal to the amount of the consideration for the transfer - Thus duty in such case is not to be calculated on market value but on the amount of consideration mentioned in the deed itself - In instant case Sub-Registrar sought to apply market value u/s. 47A - S. 47A, perused and deliberated upon - Held further, S. 47A would only be applicable when art. 23 is applicable and when art. 63 applies, the registration officer does not have any jurisdiction to enquire into the market value - (C) What is the date of determination of consideration mentioned in the document? - Held, consideration to be mentioned in the document would be the market value of the property on the date when the agreement was entered into and not when it was presented for registration - Impugned judgment set aside - Appeal disposed of.
