High Court of Judicature For Rajasthan v Veena Verma and Another

Supreme Court of India

07 July 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Markandey Katju, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 873; (2009) 14 SCC 734; AIR 2009 SC 2938; 2009 (4) ESC 550; 2009 (122) FLR 590; JT 2009 (9) SC 678; 2009 (3) LLN 614; 2009(9) SCALE 310; [2009] 10 S.C.R. 795; 2010 (1) SLR 711; 2009 (3) UPLBEC 2547

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Service

Keywords: Rajasthan Higher Judicial Service Rules, 1969

Summary: Service - Advocates & Judges - Rajasthan Higher Judicial Service Rules, 1969, rr. 6(2) and 9(2) - Service Cadre strength - Increasing the strength - Appointment to Rajasthan Higher Judicial Service through direct recruitment - Advertisement therefor - Seven vacancies - Advertisement included a stipulation that number of posts could be increased - Respondent no. 1/petitioner was an advocate who stood 8th in the merit list for selection; referring to the rules she claimed to be entitled to be declared selected and appointed; she gave a suggestion that on a correct calculation, the vacancies for direct recruitment in the RHJS came to 10 and not 7 - Single Judge of High Court dismissed the petition, but DB allowed the writ appeal - Judgement in writ appeal by the HC, whether proper? - Held, No - Questions arose were, (A) What would be cadre strength on correct interpretation of the Rules? - (B) Whether the HC correctly calculated the vacancies for direct recruitment at the relevant time? - (C) Whether respondent no. 1/writ petitioner could compel the HC to increase the vacancies to the maximum permissible limit under the restrictions provided by the rule and to appoint or consider appointment of the petitioner to a post in the RHJS? - Provisions of rules perused, esp. rr. 6(2) and 9(2) - P /&gt;(A) What would be cadre strength on correct interpretation of the Rules? - Strength of service as is specified in sch. I to the Rules, r. 6(1) - Further as per r. 6(2) the service may be varied by the Governor from time to time in consultation with the Court - (i) Whether the strength of the service can be taken to be varied by the Governor creating courts of Addl. District Judges or naming the places where such courts are to be established, in consultation with the Court without specifically varying the strength of the service u/r. 6(2)? - No - No order, as required for the purpose, u/r. 6(2) was passed; without such an order it cannot be said that the strength of the service has been increased; posts can be created de hors the cadre of a service which are known as ex cadre posts; thus posts created without a specific order u/r. 6(2) are ex cadre posts; such posts can only be filled in by promotees, and not by direct recruitment - Court opined that the temporary or permanent posts created outside the cadre cannot be taken into consideration for determining the strength of the cadre - P /&gt;(B) Whether the HC correctly calculated the vacancies for direct recruitment at the relevant time? - Held, Yes - Proviso to r. 9(1) provides that the number of persons appointed to the service by direct recruitment shall at no time exceed one third of the total strength of the service - Sub/r.(2) of r. 9 inter alia provides that after every three persons appointed by promotion, the fourth person shall, as far as possible, be appointed by direct recruitment - Practice followed by appointing authorities noted - At the relevant time there were 28 vacancies and as per practice seven posts were advertised for direct recruitment in RHJS - Advertisement of seven vacancies upheld - P /&gt;(C) Whether respondent no. 1/writ petitioner could compel the HC to increase the vacancies to the maximum permissible limit under the restrictions provided by the rule and to appoint or consider appointment of the petitioner to a post in the RHJS? - Held, No - Provisions of rules perused, esp. rr. 6(2) and 9(2) - Resolution of full Court of HC recommending seven candidates for appointment, examined - R. 9 prescribes maximum quota for direct recruits and not for minimum quota; thus it was entirely in the discretion of the authorities to decide how much percent of the total vacancies in RHJS will to allotted to direct recruits, provided the maximum prescribed is not exceeded - Impugned judgment set aside - Appropriate directions issued - Petitions disposed of.
