Urmila Roy & Ors. v Bengal Peerless Housing Development Company

Supreme Court of India

23 March 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

H.S. Bedi, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 345; (2009) 5 SCC 242; AIR 2009 SC 2086; 2009 (2) CalLT(SC) 76; JT 2009 (4) SC 484; 2009 (5) MLJ(SC) 129; 2009(4) SCALE 250; [2009] 4 S.C.R. 834

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Tamil Nadu Housing Board Act, 1961, West Bengal Housing Board Act, 1972

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - (A) Whether Part II or Part VII of the Act is applicable to the present acquisition proceedings? - Held, if the compensation awarded for the property is paid substantially out of the funds of a Corporation owned or controlled by the State, such compensation will be deemed to be paid out of public funds and as such would satisfy the test of acquisition for a public purpose - Procedure envisaged in Part II and not in Part VII of the Act would be applicable - (B) Whether the Housing Scheme was one that satisfied the requirement of s. 27A of West Bengal Housing Board Act, 1972? - Held, s. 17 of the 1972 Act itself gives the power to the Housing Board to make a scheme and that s. 27A further authorizes the Board in public interest to entrust a housing scheme to a Joint Sector Company for execution - (C) Whether the acquisition and the Scheme were a colourable exercise of power so as to give undue benefit to Bengal Peerless - Held, housing scheme fully satisfies the tests laid down by the Supreme Court - Land owners had, in fact, acquiesced to the acquisition and cannot now turn around to say that the acquisition was bad in law - Appeals dismissed.
