Cidco v Vasudha Gorakhnath Mandevlekar

Supreme Court of India

15 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 677; (2009) 7 SCC 283; 2009 (4) AWC 3776; 2009 (5) Bom.C.R. 253; 2009 (121) FLR 991; 2009 (4) JhrCR(SC) 36; 2010 (2) LLJ 674; 2010 (1) MPLJ 55; 2009(8) SCALE 480; [2009] 9 S.C.R. 427; 2009 (8) SLR 1

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Qualification And Experience, Births, Deaths and Marriages Registration Act, I886, Birth and Death Act, 1886, Maharashtra Birth Death Registration Rules, 1976

Summary: Service - Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 79 and 35 - Birth and Death Act, 1886 - Maharashtra Birth Death Registration Rules, 1976 - Date of Birth(DoB) - Respondent claiming her DoB to be 02-10-1950, whereas appellant denying the same - Respondent submitted a copy of the birth certificate issued by Chief Officer, Panvel Nagar Parishad(Municipal Council) which showed DoB to be 02-10-1950 as recorded in the register of births maintained by Parishad - Appellant's case was that in 1975 in response to a memo, respondent submitted a form giving the details of her DoB which was typed as 02-10-1948, but the year was corrected in handwriting as 1950 by way of interpolation and further fact was averred as to that respondent for some time had worked in personnel department; thus Manager (Personnel) of appellant held DoB to be 02-10-1948 - High Court allowed the writ petition filed by respondent - Appeal - Charge of interpolation noted, office records perused and contentions heard - Office records categorically showing DoB to be 02-10-1950; large number of documents produced by respondent like gradation list of Field Officer, retirement list of CIDCO employees all of which were showing the same DoB - Appellant prima facie is bound by its own records; if any fraud is alleged, it must be proved; the Deaths and Births register maintained by the statutory authorities raises a presumption of correctness; such entries made in the statutory registers are admissible in evidence in terms of s. 35 of the Evidence Act - Being without merit - Appeal dismissed.
