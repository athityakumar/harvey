(1) Hem Singh @ Hemu; (2) Vinod @ Raju & Anr. v State Of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

06 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1012; (2009) 3 SCC (Cr) 28; (2009) 6 SCC 748; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 1653; JT 2009 (7) SC 433; 2009(6) SCALE 784; [2009] 7 S.C.R. 888

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Gun Shot Injury

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 307, 353 and 34 - Accused five in number - As per prosecution case on secret information police in civil dress reached the location where accused persons were expected - Gun battle ensued; one accused and one police man dead - Registration of FIR - Conviction by Trial Court - Confirmation by High Court - Whether proper? - Held, No - Court noted admitted facts, perused impugned judgments - A day after registration of FIR, a site plan was prepared wherein name of one of accused, Hemu was not mentioned; several arrests were made, one from a village in UP another from Delhi and pursuant thereto vide interrogation and confessional statements recovery of pistol and a car allegedly used in the crime was recovered - Undisputed fact of arrest of Hemu from whom pistol was recovered and a case was registered under Arms Act wherein he was acquitted on ground that gun was not in a working condition; allegation was leveled against police that it extracted admission from Hemu when he was in police custody as to that he and one other Lilu had fired at the deceased police man; undisputed fact that none of the prosecution witnesses knew any of the accused/appellants, but in FIR, not only their names but also their parentage and complete addresses were stated; admitted position, on the date of commission of offence Hemu was not involved in any other criminal case; accused Vinod has been acquitted in the case of possession of fire arm; Court pointed that three bullets were recovered by IO, two of them had hit deceased accused and deceased constable, which was fired from .38 bore revolver, .38 revolvers indisputably were used by the police officers; PW2, injured policeman, was injured from gunshot injury of .315 weapon, no such weapon was recovered from any of the accused - No holding of test identification parade(TIP) after arrests, despite the claim of PW1 and PW2 that they had not known any of the assailants - Statement by PW2 noted wherein names of accused persons were mentioned, Court took surprise on the same as to how their names could be taken; he admitted that he had not seen any accused persons except one of them, despite the same why not identification parade was conducted - Appellants/accused were identified only in Court and Court ordinarily do not give much credence to identification made in the Court for the first time and that too after a long time - Decisions on the point of TIP referred and discussed - Incident had taken place in an isolated area and in a dark night, thus by mere naming of other accused persons by one of the accused, for which Court entertained doubt, police could not have identified them esp. in the attending circumstances - Court laid down that Sattey one of the accused person could not have been instrumental in murdering one of the police man as he had died of a firearm injury shot from a service revolver, i.e., at the hands of the police party only - Impugned judgement of HC set aside - Appeals allowed.
