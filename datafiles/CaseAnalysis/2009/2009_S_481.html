Kamalavva & Anr. v State Of Karnataka

Supreme Court of India

29 July 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Mukundakam Sharma, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 929; (2009) 13 SCC 614; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 1960; 2009 ALL MR (Cri) 3740; 2009 CRLJ 4408; JT 2009 (10) SC 130; 2009 (4) MLJ(Crl) 340; 2009(10) SCALE 468; [2009] 11 S.C.R. 498

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - IPC, 1860, ss. 34, 302 and 498-A - Murder - Dying declaration - Admissibility of - Deceased was ill-treated by her mother-in-law (A-1) and sister-in-law of her mother-in-law (A-2) - Deceased sustained burn injuries and was admitted to hospital - Taluk Executive Magistrate, recorded dying declaration of deceased - Subsequently, deceased died - Case registered against both A-1 and 2 for offences punishable u/s. 498-A, 302 r/w s. 34 - Sessions Judge acquitted both accused persons - On appeal, HC convicted A-1 and 2 u/s. 302 r/w s. 34 but maintained order of acquittal u/s. 498-A - Hence, present appeal - P /&gt;(A) Delay in lodging FIR - Effect on prosecution case - Held, formal FIR was on 12.04.1995 although incident was reported on 09.04.1995 on basis of which police started investigation by sending a requisition to Taluka Executive Magistrate which was followed up by recording of statement of deceased by police - Delay, therefore, in recording formal FIR stands explained - P /&gt;(B) Admissibility of dying declaration - Dying declaration was recorded in question answer form - Dying declaration was recorded in presence of doctor - Technical objection raised regarding unavailability of doctor's certification and endorsement as to mental fitness of deceased, liable to be rejected same being mere rule of prudence and not ultimate test as to whether or not said dying declaration was truthful or voluntary - Held, dying declaration could be relied upon as same was truthfully recorded and said statement gave a vivid account of manner in which incident had taken place - Same also corroborates in all respect with statement given by deceased to police - Conviction and sentence u/s. 302 r/w s. 34 upheld - Appeal dismissed.
