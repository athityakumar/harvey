Javed Alam v State of Chhattisgarh and Another

Supreme Court of India

08 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 714; (2009) 6 SCC 450; (2009) 2 SCC (Cr) 1085; 2009 ALL MR (Cri) 2769; 2010 (5) RCR(Criminal) 16; 2009(8) SCALE 68; [2009] 8 S.C.R. 398

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Appeals Disposed Of, Extra Judicial Confession

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302 and 302 r/w 34 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 6 - Common intention - Witness protection - Accused four in numbers - Conviction by Trial Court - Confirmed by High Court - Conviction of main accused u/s. 302 and co-accused persons u/ss. 302 r/w 34 - Convictions whether proper? - Held, Yes for the main accused and, no for co-accused persons - Deceased a college student was sitting outside her college alongwith PW7, PW8 and PW17 class mates, in their free period - Deceased's bag was lying alongside her - Accused persons appeared in Jeep and passed by, crushing the bag which contained one tiffin box; on which deceased infuriated and decided to stop the Jeep on its return; she stood in the mid of the road to stop the Jeep and demanded recovery for the loss, to which accused persons laughed and asked the driver/accused to crush her if she would not get out of the way; deceased remained firm and accused drove and deceased was bruised and had fallen down on which accused returned and again struck the deceased crushing her head - Four eye witnesses; one extra judicial confession by one of the co-accused - Eye-witnesses resiled from their statements made during investigation - Court observed the case to be a classic case of deficiency in the criminal justice system to protect the witnesses from being threatened by accused; Court noted plight of the witnesses when questions in cross examination were asked and the manner in which they had been answered and the demeanor of witnesses - Though under tremendous pressure evidences of eye witnesses clearly indicating the guilt of main accused - Deliberation entered into on the aspect of res-gestae facts u/s. 6 of Evidence Act - Applicability of s. 34 looked into; principle of law on 'common intention' discussed by referring to decisions of the Court - No evidence on record which suggests that there was any meeting of minds because things had happened suddenly - S.34 has no application to the facts of case - Appeal by driver/accused dismissed and that of other co-accused persons allowed - Appeals disposed of.
