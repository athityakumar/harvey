(1) Karnataka Power Transmission Corporation and v (1) Ashok Iron Works Private Limited; (2)

Supreme Court of India

09 February 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 116; (2009) 3 SCC 240; AIR 2009 SC 1905; 2009 (3) ALD(SC) 49; 2009 (2) AWC 1154; 2009 (3) CPJ(Sc) 5; JT 2009 (2) SC 447; 2009 (3) KarLJ 353; 2009 (3) MLJ(SC) 504; 2010 (2) MPLJ 519; 2009 (3) RCR(Civil) 412; 2009(2) SCALE 337; [2009] 1 S.C.R. 1109; 2009 (1) WLN(SC) 19

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection; Electricity & Energy; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Appeals Disposed Of, Default, Sub- Station

Summary: Consumer Protection - Consumer Protection Act, 1986 - Petition claiming damages in the sum of Rs. 99, 900/- for delay in supply of electricity - (A) Is a private limited company a 'person' as contemplated under s. 2(1)(d)? - Held, while defining 'person' in s. 2(1)(m), the Legislature never intended to exclude a juristic person like company - Company is a person within the meaning of s. 2(1)(d) r/w s. 2(1)(m) - (B) Whether the supply of electricity by KPTC to a consumer is sale and purchase of goods within the meaning of s. 2(1)(d) (i)? - Held, supply of electricity by the Board or for that matter KPTC to a consumer would be covered under s. 2(1)(o) being 'service' and if the supply of electrical energy to a consumer is not provided in time as is agreed upon, then under s. (2)(1)(g), there may be a case for deficiency in service - Appeals disposed of.
