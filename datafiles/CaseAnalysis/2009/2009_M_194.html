State of Uttar Pradesh and Another v Man Mohan Nath Sinha and Another

Supreme Court of India

17 August 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 965; (2009) 8 SCC 310; AIR 2010 SC 137; 2010(1) ALL MR 426; 2009 (3) CLR 117; 2009 (123) FLR 162; 2009(11) SCALE 377; [2009] 13 S.C.R. 348; 2009 (8) SLR 522; 2009 (4) UPLBEC 3203

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Matter remitted to HC

Summary: Service - Scope of judicial review - Departmental enquiries - Dismissal - Respondent No.1 misappropriated govt. money while he was attached as Private Secretary to then State Minister - Departmental enquiry - Inquiry Officer found respondent No. 1 guilty of charges - Respondent No.1 dismissed from service - Respondent No.1 challenged order of dismissal before HC - DB set aside order of dismissal - Hence, present appeal - Whether HC justified in quashing order whereby respondent No. 1 was dismissed from service? - Power of judicial review is not directed against decision but is confined to decision making process - Court does not sit in judgment on merits of decision - It is not open to HC to re-appreciate and re-appraise evidence led before Inquiry Officer and examine findings recorded by Inquiry Officer as a court of appeal and reach its own conclusions - Held, HC fell into grave error in scanning evidence as if it was a court of appeal - Approach of HC in consideration of matter suffers from manifest error - Matter requires fresh consideration by HC in accordance with law - Order passed by HC set aside - Matter remitted to HC.
