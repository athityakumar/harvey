Choudhury Parveen Sultana v State Of West Bengal And Another

Supreme Court of India

07 January 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 13; (2009) 2 SCC (Cr) 122; (2009) 3 SCC 398; AIR 2009 SC 1404; 2009 ALL MR (Cri) 504; 2009 (2) CalLT(SC) 68; 2009 CRLJ 1318; 2009 (234) E.L.T. 196; 2009 (3) G.L.R. 2082; 2009 (1) JCC 688; JT 2009 (1) SC 347; 2009 (2) MLJ(Crl) 361; 2009 (1) RCR(Criminal) 765; 2009 (4) RLW 3082; 2009(1) SCALE 374; [2009] 1 S.C.R. 99

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Discharge Of Duty, Co- Accused

Summary: Criminal - Whether in view of s. 197 of CrPC, previous sanction of the State Government was necessary for prosecuting the respondent No.2, under s. 384/506 of IPC? - Held, all acts done by a public servant in the purported discharge of his official duties cannot as a matter of course be brought under the protective umbrella of s. 197 - If the authority vested in a public servant is misused for doing things which are not otherwise permitted under the law, such acts cannot claim the protection of s. 197 Cr.P.C. and have to be considered de hors the duties which a public servant is required to discharge or perform - In respect of prosecution for such excesses or misuse of authority, no protection can be demanded by the public servant concerned - Offences complained of cannot be said to part of the duties of the Investigating Officer while investigating an offence alleged to have been committed - Appeal allowed.
