Nandan Biomatrix Limited v D1 Oils Limited

Supreme Court of India

11 February 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1302; (2009) 4 SCC 495; 2009 (4) AWC 3497; JT 2009 (2) SC 478; 2009 (5) MahLJ 153; 2009 (2) RCR(Civil) 431; 2009(2) SCALE 380; [2009] 3 S.C.R. 115; 2009 (1) UC 503

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Subject Matter Of Contract

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, s. 11(5) and 11(9) - Appointment of arbitrator - Application u/s. 11(5) and 11(9) of Act for appointment of arbitrator in terms of Agreement entered into by and between applicant and non-applicant - There existed three Agreements at the relevant time first was Supply Agreement, second was Joint Venture Agreement, third was R&D and Termination Agreement - (A) Whether there exists a valid arbitration agreement between parties - Held, Act does not prescribe any form for an arbitration agreement - Arbitration agreement is not required to be in any particular form - In present case, parties unequivocally agreed for resolution of disputes through Institutional Arbitration and not through an ad hoc arbitration - Therefore, there exists a valid arbitration agreement between parties in Supply Agreement - (B) Whether there exists live claim between parties? - Held, having regard to claim made by applicant and response of non-applicant, number of issues arises for determination - All disputes and differences between parties should be referred to Singapore International Arbitration Centre, which would nominate an Arbitrator from its panel to decide all disputes and differences - Arbitration Application made absolute.
