Pooja Batra v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

27 March 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 808; (2009) 5 SCC 296; (2009) 2 SCC (Cr) 659; AIR 2009 SC 2256; 2009 CRLJ 2797; 2009 (2) CTC 341; 2009 (237) E.L.T. 17; 2009 (2) MLJ(Crl) 455; 2009 (2) RCR(Criminal) 914; 2009(4) SCALE 653; [2009] 5 S.C.R. 67

Case Digest

Subject: Excise

Summary: Customs - Customs Act, 1962, ss. 2(39), 108, 111 - Conservation of Foreign Exchange and Prevention of Smuggling Activities Act, 1974 (COFEPOSA), s. 3 - Smuggling - Detention order passed against husband of appellant under COFEPOSA Act based on seizure of goods covered under eight bills of entry - Appellant appeal against detention order was dismissed by HC - Hence, present appeal - Whether detention order sustainable? - Held, on date of passing of detention order authorities issued notice calling for certain details in respect of import of those eight consignments - Hence, on date of passing of detention order investigation in respect of certain other alleged violations was still pending to ascertain involvement of persons concerned - While determining subjective satisfaction Detaining Authority stated that enquiries were pending on all those documents placed on record and relied upon by them - Use of incomplete material which is either pending or inconclusive cannot be a basis for detention order - Therefore, eight consignments/Bill of Entries relied upon by Detaining Authority become irrelevant and conclusion on extraneous material cannot be sustained - Detention's order quashed - Appeal allowed.
