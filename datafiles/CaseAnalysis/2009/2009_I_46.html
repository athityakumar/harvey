International Airport Authority Of India v International Air Cargo Workers' Union And Another

Supreme Court of India

13 April 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 2074; (2009) 13 SCC 374; AIR 2009 SC 3063; 2010 (1) RLW 222; 2009(6) SCALE 169; [2009] 8 S.C.R. 1; 2010 (2) SLR 682

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Unloading, International Airports Authority Act, 1971, Tamil Nadu Conferment of Permanent Status to Workmen Act, 1984

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Contract Labour (Regulation and Abolition) Act, 1970 - International Airports Authority Act, 1971 - International Airport Authority of India (IAAI) granted a licence to Airfreight to be its ground handling agent in respect of export, import and transshipment cargo consignments - IAAI had no privity of contract, obligation or responsibility towards the workers employed by the Airfreight - When Airfreight ceased to be the ground handling agent, apprehending retrenchment by Airfreight, the workers appealed to the IAAI to provide them some employment - They also approached the HC in a writ petition - IAAI stated that it cannot absorb them - Purely as a humanitarian measure and to mitigate their hardship, the IAAI offered to entrust the work of handling of cargo to a society formed by these workers and the workers through their union, readily agreed to form a society and the cargo handling work was given to the society - Thereafter, Airfreight Workers Union and the society filed Writ Petition seeking a direction to IAAI to hand over the ground handling work to the society, or in the alternative absorb the ex-employees of Airfreight on its permanent rolls - Society entered into a agreement agreeing to provide loaders-cum-packers at the Madras airport complex, for a period of two years and lump sum payment by IAAI was revised for providing 70 loaders and three supervisors - At the end of said term, International Air Cargo Workers Union (first respondent) and the society filed Writ Petition seeking service security to the workers and treat them on par with regular employees of IAAI, which was dismissed - Workers raised demands in the proceedings under the Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Tribunal directed IAAI to absorb the members of the society - Said award was challenged by IAAI - Single Judge of HC set aside the award of the Tribunal - On appeal, DB of the HC set aside the order of the Single Judge and restored the award of the Tribunal - Hence, present appeal - (A) Whether the agreement between the society and the IAAI in regard to cargo handling work was sham and nominal and consequently, the workers engaged as contract labour in regard to cargo handling work, were the direct employees of IAAI? - Held, contract labour agreement was entered by the IAAI with the workers' society not to deny the workers of their right to continue as casual direct labour but, on the other hand, to provide them succour by awarding the contract to their society - Thus, the contracts with the society were genuine, beneficial voluntary bilateral contracts and there was nothing sham or nominal about it - (B) Violation of s. 9A of the Act - Whether the status of loaders-cum-packers engaged in cargo handling work was illegally changed from that of direct casual labour to contract labour in violation of s. 9A of the Act? - Held, notice of change u/s. 9A of ID Act is required only if the employer wants to change the condition of service of its workmen in regard to matters enumerated in the Fourth Schedule to the Act - In present case, the action of IAAI in entering into a contract with the society was something that was proposed when the workers were the employees of Airfreight - Further, the effect of the contract with the society was not to change the conditions of service, but to put an end to the direct casual daily wage employment of the said workers - Further, the workers were specifically put on notice that their casual employment was purely ad hoc and as a humanitarian measure, to be continued only till a contract labour contract was negotiated and finalized with the society - Therefore, the question of violation of s. 9A of ID Act does not arise - (C) Whether in the absence of a notification u/s. 10 of CLRA Act prohibiting the employment of contract labour in the process/operation of cargo handling work, whether the workmen employed as contract labour are entitled to claim absorption? - Held, in the absence of a notification u/s. 10 of CLRA Act prohibiting the employment of contract labour in the operation of cargo handling work, the workmen employed as contract labour are not entitled to claim absorption - Order of the DB set aside and the order of the Single Judge restore - Appeal partly allowed.
