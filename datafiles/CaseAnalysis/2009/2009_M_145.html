Deputy Collector, Land Acquisition, Gujarat and v Madhubai Gobarbhai and Another

Supreme Court of India

29 July 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 971; (2009) 15 SCC 125; 2010(2) ALL MR 975; 2009 (5) G.L.R. 3683; JT 2009 (10) SC 474; 2009(10) SCALE 476; [2009] 11 S.C.R. 523; 2009 (4) UPLBEC 3209

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Appeals Disposed Of, Value Of Agricultural Land, Oil Mills, River, Special Adaptability, Mines And Quarries

Summary: (A) Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - Principles should be applied for determining market value of Land acquired - Grant of compensation - Determination of market value of land acquired, depend upon a large number of factors, nature and quality of lands - Norms for agricultural land and homestead land may be different - Price variation between agricultural land and non-agricultural land almost during same period also is of some significance - Location of land and in particular, closeness thereof from any road or high-way would play an important role for determination of market value wherefor belting system may in appropriate cases have to be resorted to - Position of land, particularly in rainy season, existence of any building etc. also plays an important role - Development in and around acquired land and/or potentiality of development will have a bearing on determination of value of land - Building potentiality of land acquired would also be a relevant consideration - Purpose for which land is sought to be acquired also important factor - Appeal disposed of.

P /&gt;(B) Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - Land Acquisition - Quantum of compensation - State of Gujarat issued diverse notifications for acquisition of a huge tract of lands - Purpose of acquisition was construction of a dam over river Thebi - Lands sought to be acquired were spread over in three villages, namely, Amreli, Baxipur and Giriya - Nature of lands acquired was both agricultural and non-agricultural - Some are situated in urban area which were well developed and some were not completely developed and mainly agricultural lands - (A) Lands at Baxipur were agricultural lands - Purpose for their acquisition was that said lands would be submerged in water - Lands are purely agricultural lands - There were no buildings; there was no residential use; there was no factory - No development had taken place nor was any development expected in immediate future - Lands were acquired only for purpose of submergence and thus even no building potentiality - Held, in view of materials brought on record, valuation of land should be determined at Rs.50 per square meter - (B) Quantum of compensation - Reference Court as also HC determined amount of compensation at rate of Rs.240/- per square meter for non-agricultural lands and deducted one-third therefrom, i.e., Rs.160/- towards compensation for agricultural lands - Reference Court proceeded on basis that market value of land situated in municipal area was Rs.485/- per square meter at time of acquisition, and, thus, lands in question being outside municipal area, market value thereof should be deducted by 50%, i.e., Rs.240/- per square meter - Held, HC adopted correct approach particularly in view of fact that lands in question are not only situated within a developed area but being situated near Aerodrome, Schools, Hospitals, etc. market value thereof could not have been determined at a lesser rate - (C) Amount payable for acquisition of 21 godowns - Respondents claimed compensation under various Heads, namely, price of land, loss suffered due to recovery of rent for 2 years of godowns, loss for construction for deep-well, watchman quarter, etc. - HC granted compensation inter alia applying multiplier of 10 over annual income - Approach of HC correct - Held, for extent of land on which godowns stand, separate compensation need not be paid when compensation with respect to rental income is being paid for godowns - Market value of land would be determined at Rs.240/- per square feet - Lands on which godowns have been constructed, amount of compensation shall be calculated at rental value thereof by following method adopted by HC - Appeals disposed of.
