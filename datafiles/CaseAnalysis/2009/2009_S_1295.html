Swaran Singh Chand v Punjab State Electricity Board and Others

Supreme Court of India

06 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 664; (2009) 13 SCC 758; AIR 2010 SC 151; 2009 (3) ESC 436; 2010 (124) FLR 1101; 2009(7) SCALE 622; [2009] 7 S.C.R. 1084; 2009 (8) SLR 488

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Absence From Duty, Character Roll

Summary: Service - Circular Letter D/- 14-08-1981, cls. (ii), (iii) and (iv) - Compulsory retirement(CR) - Guidelines therefor - Whether an order of compulsory retirement being a stigmatic one would be valid in law? - Held, No - CR w.e.f. 29-09-2003 - Actual date of retirement was 31-10-2004 - Cls. (ii) and (iii) of Circular noted; no retirement to be ordinarily ordered within a period of one year preceding the date of superannuation, cl. (ii); adverse entry within 10 years preceding the review, or if after such recording there has been no change in the class, post, that single entry will be sufficient for ordering premature retirement, cl. (iii); if adverse integrity report relates to distant time i.e. beyond 10 years than if further record is good or if there is promotion thereafter, than no premature retirement, cl. (iv) - Impugned order of CR though not within one year preceding actual date of retirement but still Court observed that spirit of cl. (ii) should have been taken into consideration - Petitioner was promoted to the higher post in the year 2001; thus there was a change of post despite of there being an adverse remark against him, such fact should also have been considered by the respondent in view of cl. (iii); further, Court observed that if adverse report on integrity relates to a distant past or more than ten years old, yet again it should not have been considered - Thus non compliance of guidelines amounted to malice in law, for which neither any averment as such is required to be made nor strict proof thereof is insisted upon - Contention raised by respondent that principles of natural justice was not required to be followed in such cases particularly when no malafide is alleged, noted by Court, further fact noted as to that allegation was not only that petitioner lacked integrity but also that he is unfit to be retained in service - Held, comments of such nature, are stigmatic in nature - Impugned order set aside - Appeal allowed.
