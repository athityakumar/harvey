State Of Kerala v Anilachandran @ Madhu and Others

Supreme Court of India

15 April 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Lokeshwar Singh Panta, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 499; (2009) 13 SCC 565; AIR 2009 SC 1866; 2009 ALL MR (Cri) 3042; 2009 (2) RCR(Criminal) 755; 2009(5) SCALE 675; [2009] 6 S.C.R. 152

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 143, 147, 148, 323, 324 and 302 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 157 - Murder - Hurt - Unlawful assembly - Conviction by Trial Court - High Court reversed conviction - Ground of reversal, original of First Information Statement showed last page which contained the signature of PW1 mother of deceased, to be torn out; thus authenticity of FI Statement was doubted by HC - Second ground, unexplained delay in sending FI Statement to Ilaka Magistrate - Ground of reversal whether proper? - Mandate of s. 157 of CrPC, noted; 'report should be sent to the Magistrate forthwith', which indicates urgency - Unexplained delay by itself may not be fatal, but is a certainly relevant aspect which can be taken note of while considering the role of the accused persons for the offence - HC in greater detail analysed evidence of PW1 and PW2, important eye witnesses, and gave finding that genesis of incidence was not established - Findings of HC, upheld - Appeals dismissed.
