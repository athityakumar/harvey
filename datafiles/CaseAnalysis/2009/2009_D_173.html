Rajni Tandon v Dulal Ranjan Ghosh Dastidar and Another

Supreme Court of India

29 July 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Mukundakam Sharma, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1113; (2009) 14 SCC 782; 2010 (1) AWC 509; 2009 (4) CTC 788; 2009 (3) KLT 607; 2009 (8) MLJ(SC) 361; 2009 (3) RCR(Civil) 878; 2010 (109) RD 34; 2009(10) SCALE 402; [2009] 11 S.C.R. 686

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Registration Act, 1908, ss.32, 33 - Suit for eviction - Appellant's predecessor-in-interest was the erstwhile owner of flat in dispute - Erstwhile owner allowed respondent to stay in said flat on free of charge - Housing Society of building maintained all records and bills in the name of erstwhile owner as real owner of said flat - Respondent served a legal notice to Society to include her name as owner in records - Society asked respondent to prove her ownership - Erstwhile owner filed a suit for eviction against respondent - Meanwhile, erstwhile owner sold the said flat to appellant through his constituted attorney/power of attorney holder - Appellant filed impleadment application to join as a party to the suit of erstwhile owner - Appellant's application rejected by court and eventually, suit of erstwhile owner also dismissed - Appellant filed fresh suit for eviction against respondent as a new owner of flat - Trial Court dismissed suit of appellant - First Appellate Court allowed appeal of appellant and ordered for eviction of respondent - However, Second Appeal of respondent allowed by HC in favor of her - Legality - Appellant contended that erstwhile owner properly executed the sale deed in favor of appellant through his power of attorney holder - Held, constituted attorney executed sale deed in favor of appellant on behalf of erstwhile owner - Said constituted attorney was 'person executing' document u/s. 32 (a) of Act and he acted in manner as such - Constituted attorney executed deed under terms of power of attorney - S.33 of Act would not apply in the case of executant himself presenting the deed for registration and to get it registered - Constituted attorney presented the document for registration by himself before the Registrar - Therefore, constituted attorney validly executed the document in favor of appellant u/s.32(a), hence impugned order of HC set aside and order of eviction restored - Appeal allowed.
