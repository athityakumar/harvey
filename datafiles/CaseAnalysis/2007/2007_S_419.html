Mahmood & Anr v State Of U.P.

Supreme Court of India

15 November 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

B. SUDARSHAN REDDY, Altamas Kabir

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1157; (2007) 14 SCC 16; AIR 2008 SC 515; 2008 ALL MR (Cri) 1121; 2008 (2) CGLJ 489; 2008 CRLJ 696; JT 2007 (13) SC 68; 2008 (1) RCR(Criminal) 130; 2007(13) SCALE 155; [2007] 12 S.C.R. 51

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Ante-Dated, Chance Witness, Fire-Arms, U.P. Police Act

Summary: Indian Penal Code, ss. 149, 148, 302 r/w 149, 147, 379 - Appeal against conviction and sentence - Held, delay contemplated under Section 157 of the Code for doubting the authenticity of the FIR is not every delay but only extra-ordinary and unexplained delay - Where the medical evidence totally improbabilises the ocular version the same can be taken to be a factor to affect credibility of the prosecution version - Not inclined to place any reliance upon the opinion of the Medical Officer that the injury no.1 could have been caused only with bullet since he is not a ballistic expert - Common object of the unlawful assembly of the accused in the present case is evident from the fact that some of them were armed with deadly weapons - Appeal dismissed.
