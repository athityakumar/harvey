In Re : Sayalee Sanjeev Joshi, Member, Maharashtra Public Service Commission

Supreme Court of India

17 May 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

P.K. Balasubramanyan, B.P. Singh, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 561; (2007) 11 SCC 547; AIR 2007 SC 2809; JT 2007 (7) SC 292; 2007(7) SCALE 631; [2007] 7 S.C.R. 116; 2007 (4) SLR 514

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure; Socio-Economic

Keywords: Law Officer, The Attorney General For India

Summary: Reference is made by the Hon'ble President of India under art. 317 of the Constitution of India in relation to the conduct of the respondent, a member of the Maharashtra Public Service Commission - Whether respondent No. 3 is liable to be removed from office on the ground of misbehaviour? - Held, though some suspicions are raised regarding the conduct of the respondent in this court in the light of her own evidence, it is not possible to say that the charge, as such, has been made out so as to enable us to hold that this charge is proved against the respondent - Respondent has not behaved in a manner befitting a member of a constitutional body like the Public Service Commission and under the circumstances answer the reference made by Hon'ble the President of India to us in the affirmative only as regards charge No.3 - Order accordingly.
