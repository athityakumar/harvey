Raghu Lakshminarayanan v Messrs Fine Tubes

Supreme Court of India

05 April 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 301; (2007) 5 SCC 103; (2007) 2 SCC (Cr) 455; AIR 2007 SC 1634; 2007 ALL MR (Cri) 1738; 2007 (2) ALT 148; 2007 (2) BC 490; 2007 (3) Bom.C.R. 259; 2007 (2) CompLJ 227; 2007 CRLJ 2436; 2007 (215) E.L.T. 19; 2007 (3) G.L.R. 2058; 2007 (2) JCC (NI) 137; JT 2007 (5) SC 552; 2007 (5) MahLJ 399; 2007 (146) PLR 457; 2007 (2) RCR(Civil) 728; 2007 (2) RCR(Criminal) 571; 2007(5) SCALE 353; 2007 (3) SCJ 855; [2007] 4 S.C.R. 885; 2007 (4) UC 223

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, s. 138 - Appellant was described as in charge, manager, director of the accused No. 1- Held, a proprietary concern would not answer the description of either a Company incorporated under the Indian Companies Act, 1956 or a firm within the meaning of the provisions of the Section 4 of the Indian Partnership Act, 1932 - No case had been made out for exercise of its jurisdiction under s. 482 - Appeal allowed.
