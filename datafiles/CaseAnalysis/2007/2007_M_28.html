M. Venkataramana Hebbar (D) By L.Rs v M. Rajagopal Hebbar and Others

Supreme Court of India

05 April 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1401; (2007) 6 SCC 401; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 43; 2007 (67) ALR 842; 2007 (6) AWC 5468; JT 2007 (6) SC 164; 2007 (4) KarLJ 183; 2007 (5) MLJ(SC) 318; 2007 (147) PLR 332; 2007 (2) RCR(Civil) 404; 2007 (103) RD 233; 2007(5) SCALE 598; 2007 (7) SCJ 88; [2007] 4 S.C.R. 941; 2007 (3) UC 1516

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Family & Personal; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Family & Personal - Civil Procedure - Registration Act, 1908, s. 17 - Indian Evidence Act 1872, s. 58 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O.8 r. 3 & O.6 r. 5 - Ancestral Property - Partition - Family Settlement - Justifiability - Respondent/plaintiff filed suit for partition claiming 1/4th share in suit property - Family settlement was arrived and one of defendants were not signatory thereto - Family settlement had not been paid to respondents/plaintiff by appellant/defendant - Trial Court decreed suit - Respondent/plaintiff filed appeal before HC - HC reversed order of Trial Court, holding that deed of family settlement was inadmissible in law - Further all parties were not signatories there to - Hence, instant appeal - Appellant contended that deed of family settlement was not required to be compulsorily registered u/s. 17 CPC - Thus, HC committed manifest error - Whether judgment of HC was justifiable - Held, relinquishment of property was to take effect in future - There could not be any doubt whatsoever that before Court rejects claim of partition of joint family property, at instance of all co-owners, it should be established that there had been a partition by metes and bounds - By family settlement complete partition of suit property purported to had taken place - One of co-sharer did not join in said purported family settlement - Further, if plea which was relevant for purpose of maintaining suit had not been specifically traversed, Court was entitled to draw inference that same had been admitted - Thus, fact admitted in terms of s. 58 of the 1872 Act need not be proved - Thus, HC had not committed any error whatsoever in arriving that co-owners had not partitioned in said property and respondents/plaintiff's yet to relinquish their rights in joint family property by receiving said amount - Appeal dismissed.
