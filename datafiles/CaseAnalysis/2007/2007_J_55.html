Apolined'Souza v John D'Souza

Supreme Court of India

16 May 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1011; (2007) 7 SCC 225; AIR 2007 SC 2219; 2007(4) ALL MR 817; 2007 (68) ALR 626; 2007 (5) ALT 46; 2007 (3) AWC 3092; 2007 (3) CHN 54; 2007 (4) G.L.R. 3230; JT 2007 (7) SC 492; 2007 (5) MLJ(SC) 1263; 2007 (3) RCR(Civil) 260; 2007(7) SCALE 766; [2007] 6 S.C.R. 1103

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Family & Personal

Keywords: Old Age

Summary: Family & Personal - Indian Succession Act, 1925, s. 276 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 68 - Application for grant of Letters of Administration by appellant-respondent - Challenging on the ground testatrix aged woman and did not have a proper frame of mind at the time of purported execution - Whether execution of the will has been proved in accordance with law? - Held, the mode of proof of execution of a will has been laid down in s. 63 of the Act - Execution of unprivileged wills - Every testator, not being a solider employed in an expedition or engaged in actual warfare, or an airman so employed or engaged, or a mariner at sea, shall execute his will according to the following rules :(a) The testator shall sign or shall affix his mark to the will, or it shall be signed by some other person in his presence and by his direction (b) The signature or mark of the testator, or the signature of the person signing for him, shall be so placed that it shall appear that it was intended thereby to give effect to the writing as a will (c) The will shall be attested by two or more witnesses, each of whom has been the testator sign or affix his mark to the will or has been some other person sing the will, the presence and by the direction of the testator, or has received from the testator a personal acknowledgement of his signature or mark, or of the signature of such other person, and each of the witnesses shall sign the will in the presence of the testator, but it shall not be necessary that more than one witness be present at the same time, and no particular form of attestation shall be necessary - S. 68 of the Indian Evidence Act, 1872 provides for the mode and manner in which execution of the will is to be proved - Proof of attestation of the will a mandatory requirement - Attestation is sought to be proved by PW-2 only - The will was full of suspicious circumstances - PW-2 categorically stated that the will was drafted before her coming to the residence of the testatrix and she had only proved her signature as a witness to the execution of the will but the document was a handwritten one - The original will is typed in Kannada, although the blanks were filled up with English letters - There is no evidence to show that the contents of the will were read over and explained to the testatrix - PW-2 was not known to her - Why was she called and who called her to attest the will is shrouded in mystery? - Her evidence is not at all satisfactory in regard to the proper frame of mind of the testatrix - There were several cuttings and over writings also in the will - It is for the propounder of the will to remove the suspicious circumstances, which has not been done in this case
