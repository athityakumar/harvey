Amar Singh Munnasingh Suryawanshi v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

27 September 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1478; (2007) 15 SCC 455; AIR 2008 SC 479; 2008 ALL MR (Cri) 324; 2008 CRLJ 705; 2007 (4) RCR(Criminal) 967; [2007] 11 S.C.R. 1; 2008 (Supp) Bom.C.R. 115

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Re-Examination

Summary: Criminal - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, s. 313 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Murder - Conviction - Dying declaration - Appellant convicted u/s. 302 for murder of his wife - Deceased suffered superficial to deep burns being to extent of 43-45% - She died of septicemia with shock due to extensive bums with cardio-respiratory failure - Head Constable recorded dying declaration of deceased - Another dying declaration was recorded by Special Judicial Magistrate on same day - Apart from two dying declarations, no substantive evidence has been brought on record to prove prosecution case - Whether conviction can be based on dying declaration? - It is now a well settled principle of law that a conviction can be based on dying declaration alone, subject of course, to satisfaction of Court that same is trustworthy - Held, in present case, when dying declaration was recorded by P.W. 6, Head Constable, he consulted doctor and recorded dying declaration only after he satisfied himself on basis of advice given to him that patient was in a fit mental condition to give her statement - Second dying declaration was recorded after taking all precautions - Nothing has been brought on record to show that death was a result of any accident - Fact remains that kerosene was put on her body and fire was lit - Plea of appellant that there was no intention on part of accused to cause her death cannot be accepted - He must be held to be aware that such an act was likely to cause death in ordinary course of nature - Appeal dismissed.
