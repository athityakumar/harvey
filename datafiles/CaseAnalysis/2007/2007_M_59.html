Commissioner Of Income-Tax Thiruvananthapuram v M/S Baby Marine Exports, Kollam

Supreme Court of India

30 March 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Dalveer Bhandari, Ashok Bhan

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 492; (2007) 4 SCC 555; AIR 2007 SC 1627; 2007 (211) E.L.T. 12; [2007] 290 ITR 323; JT 2007 (5) SC 56; 2007(5) SCALE 317; [2007] 4 S.C.R. 628

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Appeals Disposed Of

Summary: Income Tax - Sections 80HHC, 80HHC(1A), Income Tax Act, 1961 - Export House Premium received by the assessee - Whether includible in the 'profits of the business' of the assessee while computing the deduction under s. 80HHC - Assessee engaged in the business of selling marine products both in domestic market and also exporting directly to the buyers and also through export houses - Assessee had been filing its income tax returns showing the Export House Premium as part of its total turnover and seeking deductions available to an exporter and/or a supporting manufacturer under s. 80HHC(1A) - Held, since the source of money is within India, the turnover of the assessee/supporting manufacturer is merely a domestic turnover and not the export turnover - Assessee entitled to claim deduction of the premium amount received from the export house in computing the total income, which can be included in the business profit as it is an integral part of business operation of the assessee which consists of sale of goods by the assessee to the export house - Impugned order upheld.
