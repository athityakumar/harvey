Ajay Mohan and Others v H.N. Rai and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 December 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1296; (2008) 2 SCC 507; AIR 2008 SC 804; 2008 (70) ALR 162; 2008 (1) Bom.C.R. 535; 2008 (3) CGLJ 184; 2008 (104) RD 252; 2007(14) SCALE 287; [2007] 13 S.C.R. 298; 2008 (3) WLN(SC) 83

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Revenue Courts

Summary: Transfer of Property Act - Suit for a decree for permanent injunction restraining respondents from creating any right in or over the suit land on the basis of revenue entries as also for a decree for permanent injunction restraining them from interfering with their possession and occupation - Held, only because a further prayer had been made in the suit upon amending the plaint, the same by itself did not bring about a situational change warranting application of mind afresh by City Civil Court - Maintainability of the suit itself does not give rise to a triable issue - Appeal dismissed.
