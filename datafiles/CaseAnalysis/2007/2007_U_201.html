Sarabjit Rick Singh v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

12 December 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1299; (2008) 2 SCC 417; AIR 2008 SC (Supp) 368; 2008 (146) DLT 197; 2008 (1) JCC 440; 2007(14) SCALE 263; [2007] 13 S.C.R. 321

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Fugitive Offenders Act, 1881

Summary: Extradition Act, 1962 - Scope and ambit of the provisions of International Treaty vis-a-vis the Extradition Act, 1962 - Additional Chief Metropolitan Magistrate recommended extradition of the appellant to United States of America - Writ petition against dismissed - Appeal against - Held, any exhibit or deposition which may be received in evidence need not be taken in the presence of the person against whom they are used or otherwise - No fact needs to be proved by evidence - What is necessary is to arrive at a prima facie case finding that a case has been made out for extradition from the depositions, statements, copies and other informations which are to be gathered from the official certification of facts and judicial documents that would include the indictment by the Grand Jury - Appeal dismissed.
