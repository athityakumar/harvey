Bidhannagar (Salt Lake) Welfare Asson v Central Valuation Board and Others

Supreme Court of India

18 May 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 601; (2007) 6 SCC 668; AIR 2007 SC 2276; 2007 (3) CHN 95; JT 2008 (8) SC 118; 2007(7) SCALE 546; 2007 (5) SCJ 435; [2007] 7 S.C.R. 430

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Human Rights Act 1998, Valuation Of Land, Letting Value, Occupiers, West Bengal Central Valuation Board (Amendment) Act, 1994, West Bengal Municipal Act, 1993, West Bengal Central Valuation Board Act, 1978, Nationality, Immigration and Asylum Act 2002, West Bengal Central Valuation Board (Valuation of Lands and Building) Rules, 1984

Summary: West Bengal Central Valuation Board (Amendment) Act, 1994; West Bengal Municipal Act, 1993 - Validity or otherwise of certain provisions of West Bengal Central Valuation Board (Amendment) Act, 1994 - By reason of the impugned amendment provisions relating to publication of the draft valuation list of lands and buildings and finalization thereof, upon hearing objections thereto were deleted - Held, under the Amended provisions, however, the power of the State which was an independent authority, has been taken away - Power to constitute Review Committee has been conferred upon every Corporation or Municipality, as the case may be - Provisions are per se unreasonable and arbitrary - The Review Committee is not independent of the Municipality or the Board - Provisions for review conferred in terms of the statute for all intent and purport are illusory ones and do not satisfy the test of Article 14 of the Constitution of India - No statute which takes away sombody's right and/ or imposes duties, can be upheld where for all intent and purport, there does not exist any provision for effective hearing - Impugned Act is declared unconstitutional being violative of art. 14 of the Constitution - Appeals allowed.
