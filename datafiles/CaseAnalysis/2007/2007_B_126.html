Oriental Insurance Company Limited v Brij Mohan and Others

Supreme Court of India

15 May 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 555; (2007) 7 SCC 56; (2007) 3 SCC (Cr) 304; AIR 2007 SC 1971; 2007 (5) ALD(SC) 70; 2007 (68) ALR 794; 2007 (3) AWC 2471; 2007 (1) CCC 346; JT 2007 (7) SC 472; 2007 (3) KLT 123; 2007 (5) MLJ(SC) 312; 2007 (148) PLR 88; 2007 (3) RCR(Civil) 271; 2007(7) SCALE 753; 2007 (4) SCJ 459; [2007] 6 S.C.R. 843

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Constitution; Insurance; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Public Service Vehicle, Gratuitous Passenger, Amending Act, Use Of The Vehicle In a Public Place

Summary: Motor accident claim - Tribunal awarded a sum of Rs. 1, 96, 100/- by way of compensation in favour of the respondent in respect of the injuries suffered by him as a result of the accident - Appeal against - Held, inclined to exercise extraordinary jurisdiction under art. 142 of the Constitution of India so as to direct that the award may be satisfied by the appellant but it would be entitled to realize the same from the owner of the tractor and the trolley wherefore it would not be necessary for it to initiate any separate proceedings for recovery of the amount as provided for under the Motor Vehicles Act - Appeal allowed.
