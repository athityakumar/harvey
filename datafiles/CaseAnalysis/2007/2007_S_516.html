Sitaram Sao @ Mungeri v State of Jharkhand

Supreme Court of India

12 November 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1125; (2007) 12 SCC 630; (2008) 3 SCC (Cr) 319; AIR 2008 SC 391; 2007 ALL MR (Cri) 3556; JT 2007 (13) SC 157; 2008 (1) RCR(Criminal) 72; 2007(13) SCALE 51; [2007] 11 S.C.R. 997

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Approver, Criminal Appeal Act, 1907

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 364, 396, 120B - Appeal against conviction and sentence - Held, it is not necessary that there should be independent confirmation of every material circumstance in the sense that the independent evidence in the case, apart from the testimony of the complainant or the accomplice, should in itself be sufficient to sustain conviction - Independent evidence must not only make it safe to believe that the crime was committed but must in some way reasonably connect or tend to connect the accused with it by confirming in some material particular the testimony of the accomplice or complainant that the accused committed the crime - Appeals dismissed.
