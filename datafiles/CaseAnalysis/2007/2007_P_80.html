I.G. (Karmik) And Others v Prahalad Mani Tripathi

Supreme Court of India

27 April 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 429; (2007) 6 SCC 162; (2007) 2 SCC (L&S) 417; 2007 (4) ADJ 661; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 1264; 2007 (4) ALJ 308; 2007(3) ALL MR 866; 2007 (6) AWC 6075; 2007 (2) CLR 818; 2008 (1) ESC 107; 2007 (114) FLR 9; JT 2007 (7) SC 556; 2007 (3) LLN 96; 2007(6) SCALE 370; 2007 (3) SCJ 805; [2007] 5 S.C.R. 978; 2007 (4) SLR 595

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service

Summary: Appeal against the decision of High Court declining to interfere in the order passed by Tribunal wherein it directed the appellants to be appoint the respondent in Class III posts - Respondent, dependent of an employee who died in harness, was appointed as peon though considered for the post of constable but was not found medically - Respondent accepted the appointment but filed an application to be appointed as constable with consequential benefits - Held, when an appointment is made on compassionate ground, it should be kept confined only to the purpose it seeks to achieve, the idea being not to provide for endless compassion, once the right is consummated, any further or second consideration for higher post on the ground of compassion would not arise, a person cannot be appointed unless he fulfils the eligibility criteria - Appeal allowed.
