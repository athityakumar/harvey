Oriental Insurance Company Limited v Raj Kumari and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 November 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1438; (2007) 12 SCC 768; (2008) 3 SCC (Cr) 385; 2007 (4) ACC 761; AIR 2008 SC 403; 2008 (1) ALD(SC) 109; 2008 (1) AWC 257; 2007 (1) CCC 630; 2008 (1) MLJ(SC) 501; 2008 (149) PLR 227; 2008 (1) RCR(Civil) 69; 2007(13) SCALE 113; [2007] 11 S.C.R. 1141

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection

Summary: Consumer Protection Act, 1986 - Insurance Policy - Quantum - Determination of - Whether appellant's liability can be limited to quantum specified in insurance policy? - Held, insurer was a private limited company doing transport business - There was no material placed before High Court to show that claimants would have any difficulty in recovering awarded amount from it - That being so, HCs order is modified to extent that insurer shall pay an amount of Rs. 50,000/- With interest awarded to claimants - Balance has to be paid by insured - Appeal allowed.
