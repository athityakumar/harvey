Numaligarh Refinery Limited v Daelim Industrial Company Limited

Supreme Court of India

06 September 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Mathur, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 875; (2007) 8 SCC 466; 2007 (4) AWC 3998; 2008 (1) CompLJ 136; 2007 (4) JhrCR(SC) 151; JT 2007 (11) SC 73; 2007(10) SCALE 577; [2007] 9 S.C.R. 724

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Customs Tariff (Amendment) Act, 1995, International Chamber of Commerce's Arbitration Rules, 1988

Summary: International Chamber of Commerce's Arbitration Rules, 1988; Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996 - Held, when NRL through the communication dated 4.11.1996 have accepted the total value to the extent of Rs.14.19 crores, then there is no reason why this should not have been accepted as they have examined all the items in their letter - A perusal of the contract makes it clear that DIC is under obligation to pay the taxes, duties and levies - Once the price is fixed there is no provision for giving any benefit for fluctuation in terms of the contract then in that case, the claimant DIC cannot raise this claim of excess payment made towards customs duty on account of fluctuation on exchange rate - claimant DIC is entitled to Rs.2 crores for substituted material, Rs.8.9 crores for liquidity damages, Rs.0.2 crore as interest paid on the delayed funds i.e. Rs.11.1 crore ( Rs.2 crore + Rs.8.9 crore + Rs.02 crore) and finally interest at the rate of 12 per cent pendente lite from the date of the claim petition till realization - Appeals disposed of.
