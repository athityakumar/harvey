Dnyaneshwar v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

20 March 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1052; (2007) 10 SCC 445; (2007) 3 SCC (Cr) 728; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 686; 2007 ALL MR (Cri) 2653; 2007 (2) ALT 141; 2007 (2) RCR(Criminal) 764; 2007(5) SCALE 350; [2007] 4 S.C.R. 248

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Ill Treatment

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302 and 498-A - Appellant is husband of deceased - Deceased died within a year of her marriage - Whether appellant can take benefit of failure to notice some injuries? - Deceased was murdered in her matrimonial home, it is for husband to explain ground for unnatural death of his wife - Ante-mortem injuries clearly go to show that she had been done to death - Held, appellant cannot take benefit of failure to notice some injuries so as to arrive at a definite conclusion as to nature of death - Appeal dismissed.
