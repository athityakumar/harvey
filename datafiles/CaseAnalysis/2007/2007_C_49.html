Divisional Manager, Aravali Golf Club and Another v Chander Hass and Another

Supreme Court of India

06 December 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Mathur, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1275; (2008) 1 SCC 683; AIR 2008 SC (Supp) 360; 2008 (146) DLT 1; 2007 (4) ESC 616; JT 2008 (3) SC 221; 2008 (1) MLJ(SC) 155; 2008 (149) PLR 485; 2007(14) SCALE 1; [2007] 12 S.C.R. 1084; 2008 (1) SLR 728; 2008 (2) UPLBEC 1816

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Other Criteria

Summary: Respondents were appointed as Mali (gardener) in the service of the defendant-appellant, which is a golf club run by the Haryana Tourism Corporation on daily wages - They were told to perform the duties of Tractor Drivers, though there was no post of tractor driver - Appellate Court directed defendants to get the post of tractor driver sanctioned, and to regularize the plaintiffs on that post - Appeal against - Held, Court cannot direct the creation of posts - Creation and sanction of posts is a prerogative of the executive or legislative authorities and the Court cannot arrogate to itself this purely executive or legislative function, and direct creation of posts in any organization - High Court and First Appellate Court acted beyond their jurisdiction in directing creation of posts of tractor driver to accommodate the respondents - Appeal allowed.
