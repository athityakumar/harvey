(1) Union of India; (2) Madras Bar Association v (1) R. Gandhi; (2) Union of India

Supreme Court of India

18 May 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

K.G. Balakrishnan, D.K. Jain, V.S. Sirpurkar

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 584; (2007) 2 SCC (L&S) 39; (2007) 2 SCC (Cr) 298; (2007) 4 SCC 341; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 1024; 2007 (6) AWC 5882; [2007] 137 Comp Cas 689; 2007 (3) CompLJ 1; JT 2007 (8) SC 653; 2007 (6) MLJ(SC) 1805; 2007(7) SCALE 589

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Corporate; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Solicitor General, Companies (Second Amendment) Act, 2002

Summary: Validity of the provisions of Companies Act, 1956 as amended by the Companies (Second Amendment) Act, 2002, which provides for setting up of National Company Law Tribunal and National Company Law Appellate Tribunal - Held, question to be determined is whether such 'wholesale transfer of powers' as contemplated by the Companies (Second Amendment) Act, 2002 would offend the constitutional scheme of separation of powers and independence of judiciary, so as to aggrandize one branch over the other - Since the issues raised in the appeals are of seminal importance and are likely to have serious impact on the very structure and independence of the judicial system issue with regard to the constitution of the Tribunals and the areas of their jurisdiction needs to be given a fresh look and therefore, the matter deserves to be heard by a Constitution Bench - Order accordingly.
