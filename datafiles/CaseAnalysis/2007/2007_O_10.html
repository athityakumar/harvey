Onkar Nath Mishra and Others v State (Nct of Delhi) and Another

Supreme Court of India

14 December 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

D.K. Jain, Ashok Bhan

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1336; (2008) 2 SCC 561; AIR 2008 SC (Supp) 204; 2008 ALL MR (Cri) 1360; 2008 CRLJ 1391; 2008 (1) JCC 65; JT 2008 (1) SC 20; 2008 (1) RCR(Criminal) 335; 2007(14) SCALE 403; [2007] 13 S.C.R. 716

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Probable Consequence, Warrant Case

Summary: Indian Penal Code, ss. 498A, 406, 34 - Additional Sessions Judge directed the trial court to proceed with the case against all the appellants and frame the charges - Appeal against - Held, very pre- requisite of entrustment of the property and its misappropriation by the appellants are lacking in the instant case - Additional Sessions Judge and the High Court erred in law in coming to the conclusion that a case for framing of charge under s. 406 was made out - there is not even a whisper of a wilful conduct of appellants No. 1 and 2 of harassment of the complainant at their hands with a view to coercing her to meet any unlawful demand by them so as to attract the provisions of s. 498A - Appeal partly allowed.
