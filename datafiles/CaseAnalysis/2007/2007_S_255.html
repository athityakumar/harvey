S. Nazeer Ahmed v State Bank of Mysore and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 January 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

P.K. Balasubramanyan, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 775; (2007) 11 SCC 75; AIR 2007 SC 989; 2007 (2) AWC 1675; 2007 (2) CHN 88; [2007] 135 Comp Cas 664; 2007 (1) DRTC 421; 2007 (3) JhrCR(SC) 218; JT 2007 (2) SC 500; 2007 (2) KarLJ 260; 2007 (2) KLT 369; 2007 (146) PLR 191; 2007 (1) RCR(Civil) 705; 2007(2) SCALE 349; 2007 (4) SCJ 716; [2007] 1 S.C.R. 843; 2007 (2) UC 733; 2007 (2) UPLBEC 1439

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Mortgage By Deposit Of Title Deeds

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, O. 2, r. 2, O. 34, r. 14 and O. 41, r. 33 - Equitable mortgage - Cross-objection - Necessity of - Defendant No. 1, appellant, borrowed money from plaintiff-bank for purchase of a bus - Bank secured repayment by hypothecating bus and by equitably mortgaging certain immovable properties - Two suits were filed - One for recovery of money due, which was decreed - In execution, in absence of bus, bank tried to proceed against mortgaged properties - Appellant objected, which was upheld - Bank thereupon instituted second suit from which present appeal comes for enforcement of equitable mortgage - Appellant resisted suit by pleading that suit was barred by O. 2, r. 2 - Whether it is legally valid? - Held, respondent in an appeal is entitled to support decree of Trial Court even by challenging any of findings that might have been rendered by Trial Court against him - It is not necessary for a respondent in an appeal, to file a memorandum of cross-objections under O. 41, r. 33, challenging a particular finding that is rendered by Trial Court against him when ultimate decree itself is in his favor - A memorandum of cross-objections is needed only if respondent claims any relief which had been negatived to him by Trial Court and in addition to what he has already been given by decree under challenge - It is not possible to say that present claim of plaintiff-bank has arisen out of same cause of action that was put forward in previous recovery suit - O. 2, r. 2 is directed to secure an exhaustion of relief in respect of a cause of action and not of inclusion in one and same action of different causes of action, even though they may arise from same transactions - In a suit for enforcement of a mortgage, bar u/O. 2, r. 2 has been kept out by O. 34, r. 14 - If appellant wanted to show that causes of action were identical in two suits, he should have marked in evidence earlier plaint and make out that there was a relinquishment of a relief by plaintiff, without leave of court - Appeal dismissed.
