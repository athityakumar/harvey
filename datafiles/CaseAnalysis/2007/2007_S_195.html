Central Bank Of India v Siriguppa Sugars And Chemicals Limited And Others

Supreme Court of India

07 August 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

P.K. Balasubramanyan, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 801; (2007) 8 SCC 353; (2007) 2 SCC (L&S) 919; AIR 2007 SC 2804; 2007(6) ALL MR 431; 2007 (4) AWC 3505; [2007] 139 Comp Cas 149; 2007 (3) CompLJ 393; 2007 (4) CTC 757; 2007 (4) LLN 6; 2007 (6) MLJ(SC) 284; 2008 (1) MPLJ 468; 2007(9) SCALE 641; 2007 (6) SCJ 157; [2007] 8 S.C.R. 898

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Madhya Pradesh Sugar Cane (Regulation and Supply) Act

Summary: Industrial Disputes Act - Challenge to interim order passed by the Division Bench of the High Court in a pending writ appeal, directing disbursement of certain amounts realised on sale of stocks of sugar, owned by the first respondent - company held under pledge by the appellant-bank - Held, goods were validly pawned to the appellant bank and rights of the appellant-bank as pawnee cannot be affected by orders of Cane Commissioner or demands made by him or demands made on behalf of the workmen - Both the Cane Commissioner and the workmen in the absence of a liquidation, stand only as unsecured creditors and their rights cannot prevail over the rights of the pawnee of the goods - Appeals allowed.
