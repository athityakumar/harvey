Lalsai Khunte v Nirmal Sinha & Ors

Supreme Court of India

27 February 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Mathur, V.S. Sirpurkar

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 172; (2007) 3 SCC (Cr) 149; (2007) 9 SCC 330; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 495; 2007 (3) AWC 3078; 2007 (2) RCR(Civil) 577; 2007(3) SCALE 688; 2007 (7) SCJ 900; [2007] 3 S.C.R. 328

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Criminal; Election; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Election Petition, Security Deposit, Application For Stay, Moral Turpitude, Returning Officer, Qualification For Membership, Representation Of People Act, 1951, Disqualification On Conviction For Certain Offences, Remission Of Sentence, Withdrawal Of Candidature

Summary: Representation of People Act, 1951 - High Court set aside the election of the appellant for Malkharaud Assembly Constituency No. 38 to the Chhattisgarh State Legislative Assembly - Appeal against - Whether the order passed by the appellate Court in a Criminal Case on 9.5.2002 whereby the conviction and sentence of the appellant was suspended, whether this amounts to staying the conviction or not? - Held, appellate Court has power to stay the execution of the conviction and if appellate Court has stayed the conviction then in that case, this will not operate as a disqualification - Order of suspension of the sentence will not operate as staying the conviction - Stay of order of the conviction will mean it is temporarily non-operative - Appeal dismissed.
