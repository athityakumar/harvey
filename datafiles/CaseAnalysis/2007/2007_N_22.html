Gurunath Manohar Pavaskar and Others v Nagesh Siddappa Navalgund and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 December 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1285; (2007) 13 SCC 565; AIR 2008 SC 901; 2008 (70) ALR 176; 2008 (1) AWC 4; 2008 (2) JhrCR(SC) 31; 2008 (2) KarLJ 464; 2008 (4) MLJ(SC) 1286; 2008 (1) RCR(Civil) 563B; 2008 (104) RD 243; 2007(14) SCALE 283; [2007] 13 S.C.R. 77

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Criminal; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Prayer that encroached portion of the suit property by erection of structure measuring 369 1/9 sq. yards be directed to be demolished at the cost and risk of Defendant No. 1 to 5 be directed to give the respective vacant possession of the suit - Suit decreed - Held, High Court committed an error in also throwing the burden of proof upon the defendants-appellants without taking into consideration the provisions of s. 101 of the Indian Evidence Act - A revenue record is not a document of title - It merely raises a presumption in regard to possession - Matter is remitted to the Trial Judge for consideration of the matter afresh - Appeal allowed.
