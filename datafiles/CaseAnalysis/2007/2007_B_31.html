State Of Madhya Pradesh v Bacchudas @ Balram & Ors

Supreme Court of India

10 January 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 21; (2007) 3 SCC (Cr) 87; (2007) 9 SCC 135; AIR 2007 SC 1236; 2007 (3) ALT 131; 2007 CRLJ 1661; 2007 (1) JCC 734; JT 2007 (2) SC 607; 2007(2) SCALE 315; [2007] 1 S.C.R. 671

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 304 r/w 34 - Trial Court convicted accused persons but High Court reversed conviction - Appeal against acquittal - Held, principle to be followed by the appellate court considering the appeal against the judgment of acquittal is to interfere only when there are compelling and substantial reasons for doing so - High Court's judgment does not suffer from any infirmity to warrant interference - Appeal dismissed.
