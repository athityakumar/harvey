Lal Mohammad and Others v Indian Railway Construction Company Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 January 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Mathur, Altamas Kabir

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 660; (2007) 2 SCC 513; (2007) 1 SCC (L&S) 725; AIR 2007 SC 2230; 2007 (4) ALJ 592; 2007 (1) CLR 688; 2007 (1) ESC 141; 2007 (2) JhrCR(SC) 343; JT 2007 (2) SC 458; 2007 (2) MLJ(SC) 657; 2007(2) SCALE 138; 2007 (4) SCJ 415; [2007] 1 S.C.R. 784; 2007 (2) SLR 483; 2007 (1) UPLBEC 611

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Keywords: Cooperative, Closure, Daily Rate, Industrial Disputes, Public Sector, General Public, Project Employee

Summary: Constitution - Labour & Industrial Law - Service - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 12, 15, 16 and 21 - Factories Act, 1948, s. 2(m) -Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 25 - Whether the appellants are employees of Company and whether they entitled get their services in the Company regularised in the Respondent Company? - In the appointment orders it was mentioned that appellants appointment was adhoc and they were directed to join the Project - Employment to the Company is regulated by the service rules - Opportunity was given to the incumbents for being regularly recruited in the Company but they failed to avail the same - Simply because the Company had said that these persons will not be permitted to take any other employment or business does not mean that they will be deemed to be employees of the Company - Regular recruitment Rules have been framed with the approval of the Govt. - Held, appellants were appointed for project and as soon as the project was over they cannot claim as a matter of right to be permanent employees or to be regularised in the Company - The services of project employees come to an end as soon as the project is over and they cannot be given permanent status - Held, the appellants are not entitled to regularise their services in the Company and they are not employees of respondent Company - Appellants are only entitled to compensation - Appeal dismissed.
