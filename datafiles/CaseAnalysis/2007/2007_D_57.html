State of Punjab and Others v Des Bandhu

Supreme Court of India

15 March 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 231; (2007) 9 SCC 39; (2007) 2 SCC (L&S) 660; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 17; 2007 (6) AWC 5944; 2007 (2) CLR 238; 2007 (113) FLR 355; 2007 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1678; 2007 (2) LLJ 529; 2007 (2) LLN 605; 2007(4) SCALE 421; [2007] 4 S.C.R. 26; 2007 (4) SLR 233

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 10(1) (c ) - Termination - Labour Court held that the respondent was entitled to be reinstated with continuity of service with full back wages from the date of termination till actual reinstatement - Appeal against - Plea of belated approach for adjudication in the Industrial Forums - Held, alleged order of termination was passed in February, 1989 - Termination and a demand notice was issued in December, 1997 - Instead of reinstatement and/or payment of back wages for a certain period let the appellants pay a sum of Rs.60, 000/- to the respondent - Order modified.
