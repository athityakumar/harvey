Inspector Prem Chand v Government of N.C.T. of Delhi and Others

Supreme Court of India

05 April 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 299; (2007) 2 SCC (L&S) 58; (2007) 4 SCC 566; 2007(6) ALL MR 478; 2007 (2) ESC 287; 2007 (114) FLR 982; JT 2007 (5) SC 294; 2007 (3) LLN 106; 2007(5) SCALE 421; 2007 (8) SCJ 286; [2007] 4 S.C.R. 968; 2007 (4) SLR 240; 2007 (3) UPLBEC 2239

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: Delhi Police (Punishment and Appeal) Rules, 1980 - Service - Charge of illegal gratification - Punishment of forfeiture of one year's approved service was imposed upon the appellant - Held, it was necessary for the disciplinary authority to arrive at a finding of fact that the appellant was guilty of an unlawful behaviour in relation to discharge of his duties in service, which was willful in character - No such finding was arrived at. An error of judgment, as noticed hereinbefore, per se is not a misconduct - A finding of fact was arrived at that the accused did not make demand of any amount from the complainant and thus no case has been made out against him - Appeal allowed.
