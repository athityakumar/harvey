C. C. Alavi Haji v Palapetty Muhammed and Anothers

Supreme Court of India

14 February 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

D.K. Jain, R.V. Raveendran, K.G. Balakrishnan

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1017; (2007) 14 SCC 750; 2007 ALL MR (Cri) 1736; 2007 (3) Bom.C.R. 116

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Corporate; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Legal Notice

Summary: Criminal - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, s. 138 - Service of Notice - Indian Evidence Act - S. 114 - Complainant filed against appellant - Complainant-respondent accepted fact that notice could not be served upon appellant and it was returned unserved with an endorsement of postal peon out of station - Whether court could take cognizance of offence u/s. 138 of Negotiable Instruments Act on basis of averments made in complaint petition? - Question as to whether service of notice has been fraudulently refused by unscrupulous means is a question of fact to be decided on basis of evidence - Court shall have to proceed to try accused for commission of an offence u/s. 138 of Negotiable Instruments Act - Order accordingly.
