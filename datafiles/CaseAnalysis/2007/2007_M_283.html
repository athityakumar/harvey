Aurohill Global Commodities Limited v Messrs M.S.T.C. Limited

Supreme Court of India

31 July 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1380; (2007) 7 SCC 120; AIR 2007 SC 2706; 2007 (3) CompLJ 403; JT 2007 (9) SC 579; 2008 (1) MPLJ 10; 2007 (3) RCR(Civil) 706; 2007(9) SCALE 467; 2007 (7) SCJ 600; [2007] 8 S.C.R. 689

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996 - Indian Contract Act, 1872 - (A) Whether Draft Purchase Order constituted a concluded contract and/or whether such contract was non est could be decided by me in this petition in which petitioner has sought appointment of an arbitrator? - Held, question as to whether Draft Purchase Order acquired character of a concluded contract or not and question as to whether contract was non est can only be decided by arbitrator - Therefore, aforestated question have got to be decided by arbitration proceedings - (B) Whether there is no merit in contention advanced on behalf of M/s M.S.T.C. Ltd. that arbitration petition was misconceived and not maintainable in law? - Further held, there is no merit in contention advanced on behalf of M/s M.S.T.C. Ltd. that arbitration petition was misconceived and not maintainable in law - (C) Whether by virtue of reply to legal notice given by petitioner, M/s M.S.T.C. Ltd. could be said to have waived its right to claim holding of arbitration proceedings in accordance with British Rules of Arbitration as mentioned in cl. 19? - Further held, there is no merit in contention advanced on behalf of petitioner - Letter dated by Advocate for concurred only in principle to offer made by petitioner to be guided by said 1996 Act so far as procedural law is concerned - It cannot constitute a waiver because it is a without prejudice - Petition disposed of.
