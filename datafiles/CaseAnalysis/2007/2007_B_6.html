Special Deputy Collector (Land Acquisition) v B. Chandra Reddy and Others

Supreme Court of India

16 April 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

C.K. Thakker, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 389; (2007) 11 SCC 151; AIR 2007 SC 1579; 2007 (3) AWC 2332; 2007 (3) JhrCR(SC) 1; JT 2007 (6) SC 156; 2007 (3) RCR(Civil) 296; 2007(5) SCALE 700; 2007 (4) SCJ 315; [2007] 5 S.C.R. 163; 2007 (2) UPLBEC 1745

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Insurance; Land & Property

Keywords: LAND &PROPERTY, Requisitioning and Acquisition of Immovable Property Act of 1952, East Punjab Requisition of Immovable Property (Temporary Powers) Act, 1948

Summary: Requisitioning and Acquisition of Immovable Property Act, of 1952 ss.8, 11 ; Constitution of India, 1950, art.136 - Writ Petition - Appellant to comply with the award passed by an Arbitrator - Respondents owners of the disputed land - Land initially requisitioned for defence purpose - Competent Authority initiated proceedings for acquisition of land - Offered compensation not accepted - Case referred to an Arbitrator under the Act - Arbitrator also awarded solatium @ 30% per annum- Whether the order of Arbitrator granting solatium and interest under the Act is legal - Held, The Arbitrator had committed an error of law and of jurisdiction in granting solatium and interest - Provisions of Land Acquisition Act, 1894 cannot be invoked while deciding the cases under the present Act and Arbitrator has no power, authority or jurisdiction to grant solatium and interest - Hence, deserves to be set aside - Not a fit case to exercise discretionary jurisdiction under art. 136 of the Constitution - Appeal dismissed.
