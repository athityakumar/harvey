Uttar Pradesh Power Corporation Limited and Another v Bijli Mazdoor Sangh and Others

Supreme Court of India

17 April 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 380; (2007) 2 SCC (L&S) 258; (2007) 5 SCC 755; 2007 (4) ADJ 566; 2007 (4) ALJ 75; 2007 (6) AWC 6133; 2007 (113) FLR 821; JT 2007 (5) SC 611; 2007 (2) LLJ 832; 2007(5) SCALE 732; 2007 (8) SCJ 111; [2007] 5 S.C.R. 256; 2007 (3) SLR 589

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate; Criminal; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: LABOUR & INDUSTRIAL LAW.

Summary: Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 ; U.P. Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Challenge in the writ petition was to the Award made by the Industrial Tribunal - Respondents appointed on daily wage basis -Services terminated -termination disputed - retrenchment compensation not paid - Referred to the Tribunal - Held that the termination was improper - Their retrenchment was violative of s. 25(F) of the I.D. Act and s.6N of the U.P. Act - Both reinstated in compliance of the Award - The order of the High Court is clearly untenable being cryptic - Orders of the High Court do not warrant any interference -Appeal is accordingly allowed.
