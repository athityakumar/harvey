State of Haryana v Suresh

Supreme Court of India

05 June 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 615; (2007) 15 SCC 186; 2007 ALL MR (Cri) 2020; 2007 (3) JCC (Narcotics) 105; JT 2007 (8) SC 629; 2007(8) SCALE 441; [2007] 7 S.C.R. 961

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Health & Drug; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Alternative Construction, Mandatory Or Directory

Summary: Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985, s. 18 - Additional Sessions Judge convicted the accused but High Court acquitted on the ground that there was non-compliance with the mandatory requirements of s. 50 - Appeal against acquittal - Held, a bag, briefcase or any such article or container, etc. can, under no circumstances, be treated as body of a human being - They are given a separate name and are identifiable as such - They cannot even remotely be treated to be part of the body of a human being - It is not possible to include these articles within the ambit of the word "person" occurring in s. 50 - High Court allowed the appeal filed by the accused only on the ground of non-compliance of s. 50 - It did not examine the other grounds of challenge - Remitted the matter to the High Court - Appeal partly allowed.
