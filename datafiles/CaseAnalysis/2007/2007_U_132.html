Vishwanath Chaturvedi v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 March 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

AR. Lakshmanan, Altamas Kabir

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 326; (2007) 2 SCC (Cr) 302; (2007) 4 SCC 380; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 163; 2007 (2) CalLT(SC) 37; JT 2007 (4) SC 144; 2007 (2) MLJ(SC) 672; 2007 (2) RCR(Criminal) 363; 2007(3) SCALE 714; [2007] 3 S.C.R. 448

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure; Socio-Economic

Keywords: Wealth Tax Act, 1958

Summary: Constitution of India, art. 14, 21 - Petition for seeking enforcement of fundamental rights guaranteed under art. 14 and 21 - Petition to highlight the root of corruption in U.P. Administration - Held, directed the CBI to enquire into alleged acquisition of wealth by respondent Nos. 2-5 and find out as to whether the allegations made by the petitioner in regard to disproportionate assets to the known source of income of respondent Nos. 2-5 is correct or not and submit a report to the Union of India and on receipt of such report, the Union of India may take further steps depending upon the outcome of the preliminary enquiry into the assets of respondent Nos. 2- 5 - Order accordingly.
