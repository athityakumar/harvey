Rugmini Ammal (Dead) By Lrs v V. Narayana Reddiar and Others

Supreme Court of India

13 December 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1369; (2007) 12 SCC 611; (2008) 3 SCC (Cr) 309; AIR 2008 SC 895; 2008 CRLJ 1405; 2008 (1) RCR(Criminal) 387; 2007(14) SCALE 413; [2007] 13 S.C.R. 587

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Counter-Affidavit, Delay In Prosecution, Judicial Proceeding, Earlier Decision, Old Code, Power Of The Court, Private Party, Kerala High Court Act

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860 - High Court was of the view that though s. 341 Cr.P.C. does not provide for an appeal from an order passed under s. 340 Cr.P.C. it does not mean that there was no other provision by which appeal cannot be filed - Held, High Court was, therefore, right in placing reliance on Schida Nand Singh and Anr. v. State of Bihar and Anr. [AIR 1998 SC 1121] - It was held that initiation of the proceeding under s. 340 Cr.P.C. was without jurisdiction - Appeal dismissed.
