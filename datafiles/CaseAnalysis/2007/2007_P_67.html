Uttar Pradesh State Electricity Board v Pooran Chandra Pandey

Supreme Court of India

09 October 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Mathur, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1319; (2007) 11 SCC 92; 2008 (1) AWC 331; 2007 (4) ESC 570; JT 2007 (12) SC 179; 2007 (4) KLT 513; 2008 (1) LLJ 1021; 2007(12) SCALE 304; [2007] 10 S.C.R. 920; 2007 (6) SLR 647; 2008 (1) UPLBEC 466

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: (1) Constitution of India-Article 14-Employment - Regularisation-Daily wagers in Co-operative Electric Supply Society becoming daily wagers in appellant Electricity Board on take over of society by Board-Decision by Board on 28.11.1996 to regularise services of its employees on daily wages working from before 4.5.1990-Whether daily wagers of society taken over working since 1985 can be denied benefit of regularisation?-Held, “no”-Their services were taken over by Board “in same manner and position”-Hence, their services in society cannot be ignored for considering them for benefit of order of regularisation-Writ petitioners/respondents to be deemed to have been appointed in service of Board from date of their original appointment in society-Since they were all appointed before 4.5.1990 in society-They cannot be denied benefit of decision of Board for regularisation-Contrary view would violate Article 14.

(2) Constitution of India-Article 14-Employment-Regularisation-Uma Devi, (2006) 4 SCC 1 : 2006 (2) SCCD 1096 : 2006 (5) AWC 5325 )SC)-Cannot be applied where regularisation sought for in pursuance of Article 14-Uma Devi has to be read in conformity with Article 14-It cannot be read in manner making it in conflict with Article 14-Constitution is supreme law of land-No judgment, even of Supreme Court can violate Constitution.

(3) Precedent-Little difference in facts or even one additional fact-May make a lot of difference in precedential value of decision-Hence, Uma Devi (supra) cannot be applied mechanically without seeing facts of particular case-Little difference in facts-Can make Uma Devi inapplicable to that case.
