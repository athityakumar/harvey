Veneet Agrawal v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

31 October 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhan, V.S. Sirpurkar

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1537; (2007) 13 SCC 116; AIR 2008 SC 351; 2008 (1) BC 220; [2008] 146 Comp Cas 344; 2007 (6) CompLJ 27; [2007] 11 S.C.R. 740

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Securities Exchange Board of India

Keywords: Conduct Of Business, Bye Laws, Securities and Exchange Board of India (Stock- Brokers and Sub-Brokers) Regulations, 1992, Water Act

Summary: Securities Exchange Board of India - Practice & Procedure - Securities and Exchange Board of India Act, 1992, ss. 29, 31 - Securities and Exchange Board of India (Stock-Brokers and Sub-Brokers) Regulations, 1992, regn. 10 - Registration fee - Validity of Regulation - Maintainability - Registration fee was levied on annual turnover of stock broker and sub brokers u/regn. 10 of the 1992 Act - Levy of turnover fee as vires of regn. 10 of the 1992 Act was challenged in HC by filing writ petition which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether there was any vires of rules and regulations of the 1992 Act - Held, rules were laid before both houses as required u/s. 31 of the 1992 Act, but even if it was assumed that rules/regulations in question did not complete requisite period of 30 days, provisions of s. 31 of the 1992 Act not being mandatory and merely directory - Further, rules/regulations could not be held to be ultra vires on ground of non-completion of 30 days period after laying of rules before both Houses of Parliament - Furthermore, once regulations were declared to have been validly made, then, it was not open to argue that it was not examined from a particular angle and Court should examine it again - Therefore, it was not appropriate to ask Court to presume that Court while examining matter was not aware about provisions contained in law and when DB had examined matter from same angle - Thus, regulations were declared to have been validly made then it was not open to argue that same was not examined from a particular angle and Court should re-examine it again - Appeals dismissed.
