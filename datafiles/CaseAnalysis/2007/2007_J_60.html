Julieta Antonieta Tarcato v Suleiman Ismail

Supreme Court of India

20 February 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Singh, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 129; (2008) 1 SCC 173; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 536; 2007 (3) ALD(SC) 69; 2007 (67) ALR 470; 2007 (2) AWC 1624; 2007 (2) Bom.C.R. 578; JT 2007 (4) SC 32; 2007 (4) MLJ(SC) 388; 2007(3) SCALE 492; 2007 (6) SCJ 361; [2007] 2 S.C.R. 811

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Summary: Special Leave Petition - Eviction petition on basis of bonafide personal need - Plaintiff left suit premises on suffering serious burn injuries and then stayed with brother - Appellate Bench reversed Trial court's dismissal order of eviction petition - High Court rejecting eviction petition allowed Writ petition impressed by the subsequent events i.e., appellant's mother's death, settlement of other members elsewhere, her becoming the co-owner of property of her brother's property after his death - Appeal - Held, one cannot compel owner of the premises which exclusively belongs to her to share accommodation with a co-owner of hers in another premises, appellant being owner of suit premises, her need being bonafide and reasonable, it would be unfair to compel her to share accommodation in another premises with its co-owner - Appeal allowed.
