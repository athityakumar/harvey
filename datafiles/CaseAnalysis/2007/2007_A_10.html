Annakili v A. Vedanayagam and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 October 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1475; (2007) 14 SCC 308; AIR 2008 SC 346; 2008 (1) AWC 111; 2008 (2) CGLJ 418; 2008 (1) CTC 329; JT 2007 (12) SC 383; 2007 (6) MLJ(SC) 1494; 2007 (4) RCR(Civil) 780; 2007(12) SCALE 523; [2007] 11 S.C.R. 517

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Limitation Act, 1963, art. 65 - Suit for possession - (A) Title of respondents in suit property had already been adjudicated upon, in a suit for recovery of possession on basis of said title, whether art. 65 of Schedule appended to Limitation Act, 1963, is attracted? - Held, it was for appellant to show that she and her predecessor had been in possession of suit property on basis of hostile title and as a result whereof title of plaintiff-respondent extinguished - (B) Adverse possession - Possessor must have animus possidendi and hold land averse to title of true owner - Further held, for said purpose, not only animus possidendi must be shown to exist, but same must be shown to exist at commencement of possession - Appeal dismissed.
