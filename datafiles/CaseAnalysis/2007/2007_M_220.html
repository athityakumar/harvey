Oriental Insurance Company Limited v Meena Variyal and Others

Supreme Court of India

02 April 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

P.K. Balasubramanyan, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 290; (2007) 5 SCC 428; (2007) 2 SCC (Cr) 527; 2007 (4) ACC 335; AIR 2007 SC 1609; 2007 (3) ALD(SC) 99; 2007 (3) ALJ 596; [2007] 137 Comp Cas 116; 2007 (3) G.L.R. 2356; 2007 (2) JCC 1280; JT 2007 (5) SC 65; 2007 (2) MLJ(SC) 1230; 2007 (147) PLR 501; 2007 (2) RCR(Civil) 698; 2007(5) SCALE 269; [2007] 4 S.C.R. 641

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Contractual Liability, Breach Of Condition, Public Service Vehicle, Gratuitous Passenger, Compensation Claim, Owner Of Goods, Death Or Permanent Disablement, Use Of Motor Vehicle, Use Of Vehicle, f Ob, Third Party Risks, Use Of The Vehicle In a Public Place, Insurance Against Third Party Risk, Death Or Injury, Road Traffic Act, 1972

Summary: Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, s. 166 - Claim of compensation of Rs. 15 lakhs - Liability of insurance company - Held, victim was the Regional Manger of the Company that owned the car - He was using the car given to him by the Company for use - Whether he is treated as the owner of the vehicle or as an employee, he is not covered by the insurance policy taken in terms of the Act --- without any special contract --- since there is no award under the Workmen's Compensation Act that is required to be satisfied by the insurer - Appellant Insurance Company is not liable to indemnify the insured and is also not obliged to satisfy the award of the Tribunal/Court - Appeal allowed.
