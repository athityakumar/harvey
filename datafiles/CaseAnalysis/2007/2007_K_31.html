Rajasthan Public Service Commission v Kaila Kumar Paliwal And Another

Supreme Court of India

02 May 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 449; (2007) 10 SCC 260; AIR 2007 SC 1746; 2007 (113) FLR 920; JT 2007 (6) SC 471; 2007 (3) LLN 58; 2007(6) SCALE 531; 2007 (4) SCJ 113; [2007] 5 S.C.R. 1131; 2007 (3) SLR 636; 2007 (3) UPLBEC 2535

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service

Summary: Rajasthan Educational Service Rules, 1970 - . Respondents were Laboratory Assistants in the Government High Schools and worked in the post of Teacher Grade-III but on the premise that the respondents did not fulfil the requisite eligibility criteria contained their cases for recruitment to the posts of Headmaster of the secondary schools were not considered by the Commission - Appeals against the judgment of Division Bench of High Court allowing the appeals filed against the judgment of Single Judge of High Court dismissing the petition filed for issuance of direction to the Commission to call them for interview for consideration of their appointments to the said posts mentioned in advertisement dated 7.3.2002 - Whether the High Court was correct in opining that the experience gained by the respondents while working as Laboratory Assistants or Teacher Grade-III satisfies the requirements laid down in the said advertisement? - Held, not only the posts of Head Master is governed by a separate set of rules, the posts of Teacher Grade-III provides for a promotional avenue to the posts of Teacher Grade-II which in turn provides for promotion to the other grades of teacher; it is, thus, inconceivable that experience gained by a person holding the post of Teacher Grade-III governed by the subordinate services rules would be entitled to be considered for promotion to the post of Head Master although experience of teaching in particular classes is relevant - Appeals allowed.
