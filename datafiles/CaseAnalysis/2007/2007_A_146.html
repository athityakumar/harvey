C. Natrajan v Ashim Bai and another

Supreme Court of India

11 October 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1593; (2007) 14 SCC 183; AIR 2008 SC 363; 2008 (1) AWC 339; JT 2007 (12) SC 295; 2008 (1) MLJ(SC) 1278; 2007 (4) RCR(Civil) 736; 2007(12) SCALE 163; [2007] 11 S.C.R. 33

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 7 r. 11 - Limitation Act, 1963, art. 58 - Rejection of plaint - Legality - Appellant/plaintiff filed a suit for declaration of his title with consequential reliefs of recovery of possession and permanent injunction with regard to the suit property - Respondent/defendant filed an application under O. 7 r. 11(d) CPC for rejection of plaint on the ground that suit was barred by limitation - Trial Court dismissed application - Respondent filed revision before the HC - HC, by impugned order, reversed the Trial Court order and held that the appellant was already in possession of the suit property in pursuance of a decree in an earlier suit and therefore, the present suit was for permanent injunction and declaration of title, hence limitation specified in the art. 58 was applicable to the case - Whether appellant's suit was barred by limitation - Held, present suit was filed for possession as a consequence of declaration of appellant's title, therefore, art. 58 would not have any application - Appellant claimed his title and possession through a sale deed, however, respondent did not accept appellant's claim of possession, therefore, an issue in that regard required to be framed and the said question required to be gone into - Limitation would not commence unless there was a clear and unequivocal threat to the right claimed by the appellant - In such circumstances, application under O. 7 r. 11(d) was not maintainable - Impugned HC order cannot be sustained and hence set aside - Appeal allowed.
