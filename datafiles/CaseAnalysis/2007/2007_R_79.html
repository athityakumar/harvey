Food Corporation Of India & Another v Ram Kesh Yadav & Another

Supreme Court of India

27 February 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 163; (2007) 9 SCC 531; (2007) 2 SCC (L&S) 559; AIR 2007 SC 1421; 2007 (3) ALJ 260; 2007 (3) AWC 2857; 2007 (113) FLR 251; JT 2007 (4) SC 1; 2007 (2) LLN 67; 2007(4) SCALE 24; 2007 (5) SCJ 922; [2007] 3 S.C.R. 336; 2007 (3) SLR 773; 2007 (2) UPLBEC 1157

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: Service - Compassionate appointment - Respondent seeking compassionate appointment to his son (the first respondent) on his voluntary retirement on medical grounds - Single Judge held that respondent was not entitled to compassionate appointment, as the second respondent had already completed the age of 55 years when he made the application - Division Bench was of the view that once FCI accepted the request of an employee for retirement on medical grounds under the compassionate appointment scheme, it was obliged to give appointment to the dependant of such employee - Appeal against - Whether FCI, having accepted the offer and accepted performance of the offer by the second Respondent, can refuse to perform or comply with the condition subject to which such offer was made? - Held, having accepted the offer, FCI cannot avoid performance of the condition subject to which the offer was made - Appeal dismissed.
