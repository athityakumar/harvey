Sangam Spinners v Regional Provident Fund Commissioner-I

Supreme Court of India

04 December 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1253; (2008) 1 SCC 391; AIR 2008 SC 739; 2008 (1) AWC 133; JT 2007 (13) SC 346; 2008 (1) LLJ 661; 2007(13) SCALE 653; 2007 (8) SCJ 908; [2007] 12 S.C.R. 883

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Employees Provident Funds And Miscellaneous Provisions (Amendment) Act, 1988, Employees Provident Funds (Amendment) Act, 1960, Employees Provident Funds (Amendment) Act, 1958

Summary: Employees Provident Funds Act, 1952, s. 16(1)(d) - High Court held that the infancy protection shall not be available to the appellant factory after 22.9.1997 - Held, unless there are words in the statute sufficient to show the intention of the Legislature to affect existing rights, it is deemed to be prospective only 'nova constitutio futuris formam imponere debet non praeteritis' - Appellant shall be entitled to the protection for the period of three years starting from the date the establishment was set up irrespective of the repeal of the provision for such infancy protection - Appeal allowed.
