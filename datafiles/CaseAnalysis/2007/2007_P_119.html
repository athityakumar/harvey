Ramesh Chandra Sharma v Punjab National Bank and Another

Supreme Court of India

18 May 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 595; (2007) 9 SCC 15; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 284; 2007 (7) AWC 7433; 2007 (114) FLR 477; JT 2007 (8) SC 588; 2007 (3) LLJ 245; 2007(8) SCALE 240; 2007 (4) SCJ 818; [2007] 7 S.C.R. 585; 2007 (4) SLR 672

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Corporate; Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Double Jeopardy, Contract Of Service, Disciplinary Authority, Appeals Disposed Of, State Bank Of India, Judicial Proceeding, Legal Fiction, Loss Of Confidence, Delinquent, Pension Rules, Recovery From Pension, Punjab National Bank Officer Employees (Discipline & Appeals) Regulations, 1977, Punjab National Bank Employees (Pension) Regulations, 1995, Orissa State Financial Corporation Employees' Provident Fund Regulations, 1959, Punjab National Bank (Officers') Service Regulations, 1979

Summary: Service - Whether, in terms of the rules governing the terms and conditions of services of the employees of the Bank, it was permissible for it to continue the disciplinary proceedings despite the fact that the respondent attained the age of superannuation; whether the High Court could have, in the facts and circumstances of the case, substituted the punishment imposed by the Appointing Authority and the Appellate Authority by its own? - Held, it was permissible for the Bank to continue with the disciplinary proceedings relying on or on the basis of reg. 20(3)(iii) of the Punjab National Bank (Officers') Service Regulations, 1979 - Where the employees are pension optees, reg. 48(1) shall apply - If an officer is removed or dismissed from service under reg. 4 of the (Discipline & Appeal) Regulations, Bank need not take recourse to reg. 48 of the Pension Regulations as reg. 22 thereof would be attracted - Appeals disposed of.
