Kamla Prasad & Ors v Sri Krishna Kant Pathak & Ors

Supreme Court of India

09 February 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

C.K. Thakker, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 89; (2007) 4 SCC 213; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 618; 2007 (2) AWC 1764; JT 2007 (3) SC 282; 2007 (102) RD 378; 2007(2) SCALE 607; 2007 (5) SCJ 558; [2007] 2 S.C.R. 395

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: U.P. Zamindari Abolition and Land Reforms Act, 1950

Summary: Land & Property - U.P. Zamindari Abolition and Land Reforms Act, 1950, s. 229B - Plaintiff executed a sale deed in favour of defendant Nos. 1 to 6 with regard to some agricultural land - Plaintiff filed a suit against defendants seeking cancellation of said sale deed - Plaintiff contended that defendant Nos. 10 to 12 were co-bhoomidars along with plaintiff and therefore, he had no sole authority to execute the said sale deed in favour of defendant Nos. 1 to 6 - Plaintiff contended, further, that he was under intoxication and the documents got executed by the defendant Nos. 1 to 6 by taking undue advantage of the said situation - Defendants raised the preliminary issue that the suit was not entertainable by a civil court as only court which had jurisdiction was Revenue Court under the provisions of the Act - Trial Court dismissed the suit in upholding that it had no jurisdiction to decide the question as to validity of sale deeds in respect of agricultural land - First Appellate Court also upheld the Trial Court findings - Plaintiff filed appeal before the HC - HC held that since the prayer of the plaintiff in the plaint was for cancellation of sale deed and declaration that they were void, only Civil Court had jurisdiction which could decide such question - Whether HC order is valid in law - Held, courts below were right in holding that question of cancellation of sale deed can be decided by a Revenue Court in a suit instituted u/s. 229B of the Act - Legality or otherwise of insertion of names of purchasers in Record of Rights and deletion of name of the plaintiff from such record can only be decided by Revenue Court since the names of the purchasers had already been entered into the revenue records - HC, by reversing lower court orders, had committed an error of law and of jurisdiction which deserves interference by SC and as such HC order is set aside - Appeal allowed.
