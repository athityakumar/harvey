Arun Kumar and Others v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

03 April 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, Arijit Pasayat

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 307; (2007) 2 SCC (L&S) 232; (2007) 5 SCC 580; 2007 (2) ESC 296; JT 2007 (5) SC 181; 2007 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1669; 2007(5) SCALE 290; 2007 (6) SCJ 664; [2007] 4 S.C.R. 707; 2007 (3) SLR 198; 2007 (2) UPLBEC 1490

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Defence & Security Forces; Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Feeder Post, Sources Of Recruitment, Deputationist, Inspector-General

Summary: Punjab Police Service Rules, 1959 - Whether State Government could have invoked r. 14 (dealing with the power of relaxation) when there was no provision in the said Rules for absorption of Deputationists on permanent basis from Central Reserve Police Force in Punjab Police Service? - Held, transfer is not the third source of recruitment - Deputation is not the source of recruitment - When her services are regularized by the State not from 16/17.8.1993, when she stood appointed as a deputationist, but from 9.6.1989, when she was appointed as Assistant Commandant in CRPF, then infirmity in the action of the State Government crept in - Appeal partly allowed.
