Uttar Pradesh Cooperative Federation Limited and Others v L.P. Rai

Supreme Court of India

24 July 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

G.P. Mathur, P.K. Balasubramanyan

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 751; (2007) 7 SCC 81; (2007) 2 SCC (L&S) 598; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 756; 2007 (7) AWC 7422; 2007 (3) ESC 445; JT 2007 (9) SC 404; 2007 (3) LLJ 232; 2007 (4) LLN 132; 2007(9) SCALE 348; 2007 (4) SLR 868

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Appeals Disposed Of, Character Roll

Summary: Service - Order of punishment set aside by the high court on ground that no opportunity of hearing was given to the respondent during the course of enquiry inasmuch as he was not given any opportunity to cross-examine the witnesses who were examined on behalf of the employer nor was he given any opportunity to lead evidence in his defence - Appeal against - Held, charges levelled against the employee are not of a minor or trivial nature and, therefore, it will not be proper to foreclose the right of the employer to hold a fresh enquiry only on the ground that the employee has since retired from service - It will be open to the appellant-employer to hold a fresh enquiry - Appeals disposed of.
