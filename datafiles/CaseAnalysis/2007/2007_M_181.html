V.B. Prasad v Manager, P.M.D.U.P. School and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 April 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 349; (2007) 10 SCC 269; AIR 2007 SC 2053; 2007 (2) ESC 291; 2007 (113) FLR 835; JT 2007 (5) SC 378; 2007 (2) KLT 583; 2007(5) SCALE 543; 2007 (4) SCJ 711; [2007] 4 S.C.R. 1077; 2007 (3) SLR 320; 2007 (3) UPLBEC 2883

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Kerala Education Act

Summary: Kerala Education Rules - Service - Post of Head master - Held, Eligibility condition must be satisfied before a person is considered for promotion/appointment in respect of a particular post - Appellant was on study leave for the period 01.06.1991 to 28.02.1993 - During the said period, he was not teaching - He did not gain any teaching experience during the said period - State Government as also different benches of the High Court in various litigations considered only that aspect of the matter, namely, Respondent No. 6 had fulfilled the eligibility criteria and had, therefore, been appointed - Appeal dismissed.
