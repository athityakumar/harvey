(1) Messrs Bajaj Hindustan Sugar and Industries v (1) Balrampur Chini Mills Limited and Others; (2)

Supreme Court of India

19 March 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, AR. Lakshmanan

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 215; (2007) 9 SCC 43; AIR 2007 SC 1906; 2007 (4) ALJ 311; 2007(4) ALL MR 318; 2007 (3) AWC 2289; 2007 (2) CHN 210; JT 2007 (6) SC 609; 2007 (2) RCR(Civil) 647; 2007(4) SCALE 465; 2007 (6) SCJ 255; [2007] 4 S.C.R. 132; 2007 (2) UC 1185

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Public Officer

Summary: Code of Civil Procedure, s. 80(2) - Plea that project of the respondent No. 5 to set up a sugar mill at Itai Maida was in contravention of the rules relating to maintenance of a minimum distance of 15 Kms. between two units - Suit against Union of India - Aplication under s. 80(2) - Interim order vacated by High Court - Directing the trial Judge to reconsider the matter - Held, a suit may be filed against the Government or a public officer without serving notice as required by sub-section (1) with the leave of the Court - When such leave is refused, the question of institution of the suit does not arise and accordingly, no interim relief could also be granted at that stage - Trial Judge does not also appear to have committed any illegality in taking up the petitioners' application for grant of leave once again - Trial court was bound to reconsider the question of grant of leave - Appeal dismissed.
