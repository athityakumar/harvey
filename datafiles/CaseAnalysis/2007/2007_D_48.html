Delhi Electricity Regulatory Commission v BSES Yamuna Power Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

15 February 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, Arijit Pasayat

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 217; (2007) 3 SCC 33; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 601; JT 2007 (8) SC 409; 2007(3) SCALE 289; 2007 (3) SCJ 888; [2007] 2 S.C.R. 747

Case Digest

Subject: Accounts & Audits; Electricity & Energy; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Accumulated Loss, Appeal By Special Leave, Clear Profit, Principles Of Accounting

Summary: Delhi Electricity Reforms Act, 2000 - Tariff fixation by Delhi Electricity Reforms Commission - Whether on the facts and circumstances of the case DERC was right in reducing the rate of depreciation from 6.69% to 3.75%? - Held, Depreciation enables the Utility to work out the charges to be recovered from consumers for supply of electricity, one has to follow the provisions of the schedule to the said Electricity Act and that one has not to follow the provisions of Income-tax Act while calculating depreciation as one of the items of expense under the Electricity Accounting - In the present case, DERC has not considered the difference between the physical life of an asset and the useful life of the asset - Order passed by ATE upheld
