Supreme Court of India

Case Analysis

Bench

Where Reported

Case Digest

Subject: Securities Exchange Board of India

Keywords: Capitalization, Net Asset Value, Sick Industrial Companies (Special Provision) Act, 1956

Summary: Securities and Exchange Board of India Act, 1992; Securities and Exchange Board of India (Substantial Acquisition of Shares and Takeovers) Regulations, 1997 - Plea that Securities and Exchange Board as well as the Merchant Banker had not properly valued the shares of the target company in accordance with the parameters laid down in reg. 20(5) - Held, if there is material on record to show that the Board applied its mind to the offer made and considered it in the light of the relevant provisions of the Regulations and all factors enumerated therein, its decision to approve the offer price to be incorporated in the letter of offer cannot be faulted on the ground that it has not passed a reasoned order - Valuer have considered all the factors relevant under reg. 20(5)) of the Takeover Code and have adopted a reasonable approach which does not call for interference - Appeals dismissed.
