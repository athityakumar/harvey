Aryavrat Gramin Bank v Vijay Shankar Shukla

Supreme Court of India

20 September 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1582; (2007) 12 SCC 413; (2008) 2 SCC (L&S) 489; 2008 (5) AWC 4504; JT 2007 (12) SC 70; 2008 (2) MLJ(SC) 484; 2007(12) SCALE 482; [2007] 10 S.C.R. 593; 2007 (6) SLR 668; 2008 (1) UPLBEC 729

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Regional Rural Banks Act, 1976

Summary: Service - Regional Rural Bank Act, 1976 - Appointment - Respondent was a candidate for being appointed in the post of Officer in the appellant Bank - Appellant was obligated to follow the instructions issued by National Bank for Agriculture and Rural Development (NABARD) as also by the Central Government - NABARD also issued a Circular stating that 50% of the vacancies of the officers are to be filled by direct recruitment and the balance 50% by promotion - Meanwhile, the promotees filed a writ petition questioning the said recruitment process undertaken by the direct recruits wherein an interim order was passed directing the respondent not to fill up the posts of Officers contrary to the quota reserved for promotees - However, appellant was given the liberty for making appointment in proportion to the quota of direct recruits or also the promotees - A select list was published by the appellant-Bank - Respondent who was a general category candidate occupied the 47th position in the category of the general candidates - Appellant in the meantime appointed few candidates in the said 50% quota after one year - Respondent filed Writ Petition inter alia praying for issuance of a writ of or in the nature of mandamus commanding the appellant to appoint him to the Officers' Cadre on the basis of his selection made through an open competitive written examination and interview - A writ of mandamus was issued directing the appellant to appoint the respondent to the post of Officer cadre on the basis of the said select list - Hence, present appeal - (A) It was submitted that the life of the select list having expired and NABARD having categorically refused to extend the life of the panel, no writ of mandamus could be issued only on the ground that a few candidates had been appointed after the said date - Held, merely because the appellant appointed a few persons beyond the said period the same by itself did not confer any legal right on the respondent to seek for issuance of a writ of mandamus nor did it confer any jurisdiction on the HC to issue the said writ - (B) Whether writ petition was barred under the principles of delay and laches? - Held, HC erred in so far as it failed to take into consideration that whereas the life of the panel came to an end in June, 1986, the Writ was filed in September, 1993 - (C) HC held that the employer had stopped the recruitment process mid-way and had arbitrarily picked up persons of its preference - Held, for implementing the policy decision of the Central Government the appellant proceeded on the basis that only 50% of the vacant posts be filled up by direct recruitment - If the policy of the Central Government which was to be implemented in terms of the provisions of the Act had been given effect to by the appellant, no exception thereto, could have been taken - HC misdirected itself in arriving at the aforementioned decision - Respondent was not entitled to get any relief in the writ petition - Impugned judgment cannot be sustained - Appeal allowed.
