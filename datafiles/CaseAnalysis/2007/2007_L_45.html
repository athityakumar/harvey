Sarav Investment and Financial Consultants Private Limited and Another v Llyods Register of Shipping Indian Office Staff Provident Fund and Another

Supreme Court of India

11 October 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1382; (2007) 14 SCC 753; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 1688; 2008 ALL MR (Cri) 300; 2007 (6) Bom.C.R. 470; 2008 (3) CGLJ 421; [2007] 140 Comp Cas 381; 2008 CRLJ 377; 2007 (4) JCC (NI) 350; JT 2007 (11) SC 580; 2007 (4) KLT 590; 2008 (2) MahLJ 787; 2008 (2) MPLJ 92; 2007 (4) RCR(Criminal) 575; [2007] 10 S.C.R. 1110

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Corporate; Criminal

Keywords: Legal Notice, Advocates, Dishonoured Cheque, Cheques

Summary: Banking & Finance - Criminal - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, s. 138 - Service of notice - Requirement - Service of notice is a part of cause of action for lodging complaint u/s. 138 - Service of notice no longer res integra in view of decision in C C Alavi Haji v. Palapetty Muhammed - Notice only required to be dispatched, its contents communicated - Court not to adopt interpretation which helps dishonest - Context in s. 138 invites liberal interpretation of statutory obligation to give notice - U/s. 138 proviso (b) only mode for making demand by payee been prescribed - Therefore, once notice dispatched, payee's obligation done - Held, notice served by hand through employees of complainant's agent - Affidavit filed by such employees in this regard uninformed - Complaint filed was pre-mature - Complaints not subserving requirements of law, so not maintainable - Appeal allowed.
