Alchemist Limited and Another v State Bank of Sikkim and Others

Supreme Court of India

16 March 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

C.K. Thakker, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 212; (2007) 11 SCC 335; AIR 2007 SC 1812; 2007 (4) ALD(SC) 61; [2007] 136 Comp Cas 665; JT 2007 (4) SC 474; 2007 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1228; 2007 (4) MahLJ 586; 2008 (3) MLJ(SC) 812; 2007 (3) MPLJ 284; 2007 (147) PLR 475; 2007(4) SCALE 412; 2007 (4) SCJ 874; [2007] 4 S.C.R. 46

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 226 - Whether a part of the cause of action had arisen within the territorial jurisdiction of the High Court of Punjab & Haryana so as to entertain a writ petition under art. 226 of the Constitution filed by the appellant-Company against the respondents? - Held, after 1963, cause of action is relevant and germane and a writ petition can be instituted in a High Court within the territorial jurisdiction of which cause of action in whole or in part arises - For the purpose of deciding whether facts averred by the petitioner-appellant, would or would not constitute a part of cause of action, one has to consider whether such fact constitutes a material, essential, or integral part of the cause of action - Even if a small fraction of the cause of action arises within the jurisdiction of the Court, the Court would have territorial jurisdiction to entertain the suit/petition - Facts which have been pleaded by the Appellant Company cannot be said to be essential, integral or material facts so as to constitute a part of 'cause of action' - Appeal dismissed.
