Messrs Canon Steels Private Limited v Commissioner of Customs

Supreme Court of India

12 November 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1130; (2007) 14 SCC 464; 2008 (1) CTC 92; 2007 (218) E.L.T. 161; JT 2007 (13) SC 188; 2008 (2) MLJ(SC) 572; 2007(13) SCALE 77; 2007 (8) SCJ 442; [2007] 11 S.C.R. 1053; 2009 (15) S.T.R. 97; 2008 (1) WLN(SC) 157

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Customs; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Specified Area

Summary: Customs Act, 1962 - Appeal under s. 130 dismissed by High Court holding that it had no jurisdiction to deal with the matter as the original order was passed by Adjudicating authority at Mumbai and the appellate order was passed at Delhi - Appeal against - Held, appellate order in this case was issued from CESTAT office at New Delhi - Delhi High Court has jurisdiction to deal with the matter - Punjab & Haryana High Court was justified in its view as original adjudication order and appellate order were not issued by any authority within its territorial jurisdiction - Directed restoration of the said as undisputably, the Delhi High Court has jurisdiction to deal with the matter - Appeal disposed of.
