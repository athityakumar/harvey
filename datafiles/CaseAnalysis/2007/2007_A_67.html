Akhilesh Kumar Singh v State of Jharkhand and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 December 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1331; (2008) 2 SCC 74; 2008 (2) JhrCR(SC) 79; JT 2007 (13) SC 654; 2007(14) SCALE 585; [2007] 13 S.C.R. 868

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: Service - Misconduct - Dismissal from service - Appellant has been found guilty of tampering with records and committing forgery - Plea that in a departmental proceeding involving one Kaushal Kumar who was also found guilty of identical charges, a lenient view was taken - held, charges against the appellant and Kaushal Kumar Singh being not identical in nature, the impugned judgment does not suffer from any legal infirmity - Appeal dismissed.
