Jodhraj Singh v State Of Rajasthan

Supreme Court of India

27 April 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 432; (2007) 15 SCC 294; 2007 CRLJ 2942; JT 2007 (7) SC 118; 2007 (3) RCR(Criminal) 172; 2007(6) SCALE 242; [2007] 5 S.C.R. 850

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Second Trial

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860 - s. 302 - Appeal against the judgment of High Court upholding the conviction and sentence passed by trial court - Held, keeping in view the materials available on record, it is permissible for a court of law to rely upon a part of the testimony of the witness who has been declared hostile, having regard to the materials brought on record by the prosecution, the court is satisfied that the appellant shared common intention with the other accused in committing the crime - Appeal dismissed.
