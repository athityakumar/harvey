Ganga Kisan Sahkari Chini Mills Limited v Jaivir Singh

Supreme Court of India

24 September 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 971; (2007) 7 SCC 748; (2007) 2 SCC (L&S) 781; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 1007; 2008 (5) AWC 4658; 2007 (115) FLR 619; JT 2007 (11) SC 340; 2007 (3) LLJ 1057; 2007(11) SCALE 409; 2007 (7) SCJ 324; [2007] 10 S.C.R. 277; 2007 (6) SLR 219; 2007 (3) UPLBEC 2469

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Seasonal

Summary: Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Labour Court directed re-instatement of the respondents-workmen and payment of back wages and retaining allowance - Writ petition dismissed - Appeal against - Held, High Court noted that the appointment of the workmen was not permanent as the permanent workmen have to complete their probationary period - There was no averment that the workmen had completed their probation period - Orders of the Labour Court and the High Court are set aside - Appeals allowed.
