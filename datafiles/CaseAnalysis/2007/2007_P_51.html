State of Rajasthan v Parthu

Supreme Court of India

13 September 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 977; (2007) 12 SCC 754; AIR 2008 SC 10; 2007 ALL MR (Cri) 2980; 2007 CRLJ 4722; JT 2007 (11) SC 513; 2007 (4) RCR(Criminal) 466; 2007(11) SCALE 460; [2007] 9 S.C.R. 932

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Indian Penal Code, ss. 307, 302 - Appeal against acquittal - Whether two dying declarations could be relied upon or not? - Held, a judgment of conviction can be recorded on the basis of the dying declaration alone subject of course to the satisfaction of the Court that the same was true and voluntary - In the absence of sufficient or cogent explanations in that behalf the Court would be entitled to consider the same as the circumstances against the accused - Appeal allowed.
