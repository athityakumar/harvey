P. Mohanan Pillai v State Of Kerala & Ors

Supreme Court of India

23 February 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 155; (2007) 9 SCC 497; (2007) 2 SCC (L&S) 542; AIR 2007 SC 2840; 2007 (113) FLR 442; 2007 (2) JhrCR(SC) 187; 2007 (2) KLT 551; 2007(3) SCALE 548; [2007] 3 S.C.R. 53; 2007 (3) SLR 876

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: Service - Selection - Held, allocation of marks for interview was in fact misused - The power in this case has been used by the Appointing Authority for unauthorized purpose - When a power is exercised for an unauthorized purpose, the same would amount to malice in law - Why the Company intended to enlarge the zone of consideration from 1 : 3 to 1 : 4 has also not been disclosed - Why the cut-off mark was also lowered remained a mystery - In a given situation, a decision of the State may be changed, but therefor good and sufficient reasons must be assigned - The Company failed to do so - Decision taken in this behalf smacks of arbitrariness - It prejudiced the candidates like the appellant - Appeal allowed.
