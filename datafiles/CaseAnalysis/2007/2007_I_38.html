Indu Nissan Oxo Chemicals India Limited v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 December 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1300; (2007) 13 SCC 487; AIR 2008 SC (Supp) 228; 2008 (221) E.L.T. 7; 2007(14) SCALE 150; [2007] 13 S.C.R. 173; 2008 (3) WLN(SC) 90

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Customs Excise Act, 1944

Summary: Customs Act, 1962, s. 112 (a) - Dispute relates to classification of the product imported by the appellant and consequential benefits claimed by it under various Notifications issued by the Director General of Foreign Trade - Order directing deposit of rupees two crores as a condition precedent for entertaining the appeal - Appeal against - Held, merely establishing a prima facie case, interim order of protection should not be passed - But if on a cursory glance it appears that the demand raised has no leg to stand, it would be undesirable to require the assessee to pay full or substantive part of the demand - Mere assertion about undue hardship would not be sufficient - Not find any infirmity in the order directing deposit of Rupees two crores - Appeal disposed of.
