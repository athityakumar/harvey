Mohan Lal And Ors v State Of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

21 February 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 133; (2007) 3 SCC (Cr) 94; (2007) 9 SCC 151; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 1139; 2007 ALL MR (Cri) 847; 2007 (1) JCC 749; 2007 (3) JhrCR(SC) 170; JT 2007 (3) SC 602; 2007 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 940; 2007 (2) RCR(Criminal) 88; 2007(3) SCALE 282; [2007] 2 S.C.R. 1030

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Family & Personal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Son In Law

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302 r/w 34 - Appeal against conviction and sentence - Allegation that deceased was set on fire by the appellants who were torturing and harassing her for dowry demand - Held, basis of conviction of the accused is the dying declaration - Held, though much was made of the dowry demand by the courts below there is only a vague reference to it in the dying declaration - Prior to the death, neither the deceased nor her parents had complained to the police or told anyone else about any alleged dowry demand - Dying declaration itself was clearly the result of tutoring and was not a free and voluntary one - Appeal allowed.
