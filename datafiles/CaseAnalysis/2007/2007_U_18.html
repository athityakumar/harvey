Ujjagar Singh v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

13 December 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

H.S. Bedi, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1367; (2007) 13 SCC 90; AIR 2008 SC (Supp) 190; 2008 CRLJ 808; JT 2008 (1) SC 1; 2008 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 116; 2008 (1) RCR(Criminal) 305; 2007(14) SCALE 428; [2007] 13 S.C.R. 653

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Finger Prints, Self-Inflicted, Close Range, Gun Shot Injury, Counter Version, Post- Mortem Report

Summary: Indian Penal Code, ss. 376, 309 - Appeal against conviction and sentence - Trial court and the High Court relying on the circumstances available on the evidence convicted and sentenced the appellant - Held, there is however absolutely no evidence to suggest (even assuming that the intercourse had been committed by the appellant) that he had done so without victim's consent or against her will - A case of rape has not been made out. In this background the death sentence is not called for - Acquitted the appellant of the charge under s. 376 of the IPC but maintain his conviction for the other offences but commute his death sentence to life - With this modification, appeal dismissed.
