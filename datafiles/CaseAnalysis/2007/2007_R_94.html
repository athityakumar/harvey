Sunil Kumar v Ram Singh Gaud and Others

Supreme Court of India

02 November 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhan, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1088; (2007) 14 SCC 61; 2007 (4) ACC 716; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 1184; 2008 (1) ALD(SC) 114; 2008(1) ALL MR 413; 2007 (69) ALR 920; 2008 (1) AWC 106; JT 2007 (12) SC 648; 2008 (2) MLJ(SC) 865; 2008 (149) PLR 313; 2007 (4) RCR(Civil) 794; 2007 (8) SCJ 217; [2007] 11 S.C.R. 876

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Motor Vehicles Act, 1998

Summary: Motor Vehicles Act, 1998, s. 166 - Appellant claimed Rs.8, 20, 000/- by way of compensation - Total sum of Rs.72, 000/- was awarded as compensation along with interest @ 6% per annum from the date of the claim petition - Appeal against - Held, Tribunal as well as the High Court have not awarded any compensation towards loss of future income - Disability suffered by the appellant would surely reduce his earning capacity - Loss of income is assessed at Rs.2, 59, 200/- - Appellant would be entitled to the aforesaid amount in addition to the sum already awarded by the Tribunal and interest at 6% per annum - Appeal allowed.
