Diploma Engineers Sangh v State of Uttar Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

20 March 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

H.K. Sema, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 222; (2007) 13 SCC 300; (2008) 2 SCC (L&S) 619; 2007 (5) ADJ 63; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 37; 2007 (5) AWC 5346; JT 2007 (4) SC 532; 2007 (3) LLN 587; 2007(4) SCALE 582; 2007 (6) SCJ 836; [2007] 4 S.C.R. 207; 2007 (5) SLR 665; 2007 (2) UPLBEC 1320

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Feeder Post

Summary: U.P.Service of Engineers (Building and Roads Branch) Class-II Rules, 1936, r. 5(iv), 9(ii) - Whether the writ petitioner had locus standi to challenge the Office Memorandum issued by State Government for purpose of promotion of Diploma Holder's Junior Engineers from the post of Junior Engineers to the post of Assistant Engineers according to U.P. Service of Engineers (Building and Roads Branch) Class-II Rules, 1936?; Whether the office order dated 11.2.2003 runs into the teeth of the 1936 Rules? - Held, no reason to interfere with the order of the High Court quashing the office order dated 11.2.2003 and requiring the diploma-holder Junior Engineers to undergo a qualifying examination - In the absence of any specific provision prescribing interview as the means of ascertainment of fitness for promotion, 'interview' cannot generally be considered to be a qualifying examination nor can it take the place of a qualifying examination - Appeal dismissed.
