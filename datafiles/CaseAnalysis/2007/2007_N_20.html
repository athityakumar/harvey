Mahboob Deepak v Nagar Panchayat Gajraula and Another

Supreme Court of India

13 December 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1344; (2008) 1 SCC 575; AIR 2008 SC (Supp) 342; 2008 (1) ESC 84; JT 2008 (1) SC 150; 2008 (1) LLJ 855; 2007(14) SCALE 504; [2007] 13 S.C.R. 672; 2008 (2) SLR 12; 2008 (2) UPLBEC 1105

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: U.P. Industrial Disputes Act

Summary: U.P. Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Labour law - Appellant was appointed as a daily wager on a remuneration of Rs.20/- per day in the services of respondent - Termination - Held, merely because an employee has completed 240 days of work in a year preceding the date of retrenchment, the same would not mean that his services were liable to be regularized - High Court did not consider the effect of non- compliance of the provisions of s. 6N - As the appellant had worked only for a short period, the interest of justice will be subserved if the High Court judgment is modified by directing payment of a sum of Rs.50, 000/- - Appeal partly allowed.
