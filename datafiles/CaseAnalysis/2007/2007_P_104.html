Commissioner of Municipal Corporation, Shimla v Prem Lata Sood and Others

Supreme Court of India

15 May 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 552; (2007) 11 SCC 40; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 272; JT 2007 (7) SC 336; 2007 (3) RCR(Civil) 249; 2007(7) SCALE 737; 2007 (5) SCJ 4; [2007] 6 S.C.R. 898

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Lay Out Plans, Himachal Pradesh Town and Country Planning Act, 1977, Restriction On Use Of Land, Himachal Pradesh Municipal Corporation Act, 1994, Himachal Pradesh Municipal Corporation Act, 1979, Howrah Municipal Corporation Act, 1980

Summary: Himachal Pradesh Town and Country Planning Act, 1977 - Division Bench held that purported declaration in relation to the core area and heritage zone would not apply in the case of the writ-petitioners in view of the fact that the building plan submitted by them before the Director in terms of the provisions of the 1977 Act had already been allowed, and, thus, the same had conferred a vested right in them - Appeal against - Held, where a statute provides for a right, but enforcement thereof is in several stages, unless and until the conditions precedent laid down therein are satisfied, no right can be said to have been vested in the person concerned - Area in question has been declared to be a heritage zone, and hence no permission to raise any construction can be issued, which would violate the ecology - Public interest, as is well-known, must override the private interest - Appeal allowed.
