Commissioner of Income Tax, Chennai v Messrs Alagendran Finance Limited

Supreme Court of India

27 July 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 778; (2007) 7 SCC 215; AIR 2007 SC 3005; [2007] 293 ITR 1; JT 2007 (9) SC 424; 2007(9) SCALE 387; [2007] 8 S.C.R. 558; [2007] 162 TAXMAN 465

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Corporate; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Wealth-Tax

Summary: Income Tax - Section 263(2), Income Tax Act, 1961 - Limitation - Computation - Revision - Respondent is a company incorporated under the Indian Companies Act, 1956 - Filed its returns for assessment - Assessment for the year 1994-95 was completed on 27.02.1997 and those of the Assessment Years 1995-96 and 1996-97 were completed on 12.05.1997 and 30.03.1998 respectively - Assessee's return under the Head 'Lease Equalization Fund' was accepted - Proceedings for reassessment were initiated by the assessing officer on 05.03.2004 - Orders of reassessment were passed on 28.03.2002 - Commissioner exercising its revisional jurisdiction reopened the order of assessment only in relation to lease equalization fund which being not the subject of the reassessment proceedings - Whether for the purpose of computing the period of limitation envisaged under s. 263(2), the date of order of assessment or that of the reassessment, is to be taken into consideration - Held, only that part of order of assessment which related to lease equalization fund was found to be prejudicial to the interest of the Revenue - As proceedings for reassessment have nothing to do with the head of income, Doctrine of merger would not apply - Period of limitation provided for under of s. 263(2) would begin to run from the date of the order of assessment and not from the order of reassessment - Revisional jurisdiction beyond the period of limitation.
