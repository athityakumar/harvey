Commissioner of Income Tax, Salem v K. Chinnathamban

Supreme Court of India

24 July 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, B. SUDARSHAN REDDY

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 780; (2007) 7 SCC 390; [2007] 292 ITR 682; JT 2007 (9) SC 549; 2007(9) SCALE 350; 2007 (7) SCJ 555; [2007] 8 S.C.R. 496; [2007] 162 TAXMAN 459

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax - Section 132, Income Tax Act, 1961 - Substantial question of law - Seizure - Undisclosed income - Respondent-assessee, was connected with the firm, floated for carrying on the business of prize tickets and for collecting deposits from the public, where a search was conducted when Rs. 1.18 crores (approx.) was seized - The firm was managed by one K. Palanisamy who had filed his Return who was not in a position to explain the source of the deposit amount - Assessing Officer treated the amount as undisclosed income of persons in whose names the deposit appeared - Held, Department was right in coming to the conclusion that there was no evidence in support of the claim of the assessee that the amount was collected from the members of the public - Where a deposit stands in the name of a third person and where that person is related to the assessee then in such a case the proper course would be to call upon the person in whose books the deposit appears or the person in whose name the deposit stands should be called upon to explain such deposit - Onus of proving the source of deposit primarily rested on the persons in whose names the deposit appeared in various banks - Department was right in making individual assessments in the hands of respondent-assessee.
