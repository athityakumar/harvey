Uco Bank and Another v Rajinder Lal Capoor

Supreme Court of India

18 May 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 580; (2007) 2 SCC (L&S) 550; (2007) 6 SCC 694; AIR 2007 SC 2129; 2007 (3) CLR 761; 2008 (1) DRTC 1; 2007 (114) FLR 413; JT 2007 (8) SC 581; 2007 (3) LLJ 352; 2007 (3) LLN 607; 2007(8) SCALE 296; 2007 (5) SCJ 222; [2007] 7 S.C.R. 543

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: UCO Bank Officer Employees' Services Regulations, 1979, UCO Bank Officer Employees (Conduct) Regulations, 1976

Summary: Banking Companies (Acquisition and Transfer of Undertakings) Act, 1970; UCO Bank Officer Employees' Services Regulations, 1979 - Service - Punishment of removal from the service of the respondent - High Court converted punishment into compulsory retirement - Appeal against - Held, an order of dismissal or removal from service can be passed only when an employee is in service - If a person is not in employment, the question of terminating his services ordinarily would not arise unless there exists a specific rule in that behalf - Reg. 20 is not applicable in the case of the respondent entire proceeding initiated against the respondent became vitiated in law - Appeal dismissed.
