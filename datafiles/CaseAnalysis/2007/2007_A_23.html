Iddar and Others v Aabida and Another

Supreme Court of India

25 July 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 776; (2007) 1 SCC (Cr) 22; (2007) 11 SCC 211; AIR 2007 SC 3029; 2007 (3) ALT 401; 2007 CRLJ 4313; 2007 (3) JCC 2050; JT 2007 (9) SC 552; 2007 (4) KLT 652; 2007 (3) RCR(Criminal) 909; 2007(9) SCALE 358; 2007 (8) SCJ 507; [2007] 8 S.C.R. 518

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860. ss. 498A, 406; Criminal Procedure Code, s. 311 - Held, object of s. 311 is to bring on record evidence not only from the point of view of the accused and the prosecution but also from the point of view of the orderly society - If a witness called by Court gives evidence against the complainant he should be allowed an opportunity to cross- examine - Since a witness summoned by the Court could not be termed a witness of any particular party, the Court should give the right of cross- examination to the complainant - Appeal allowed.
