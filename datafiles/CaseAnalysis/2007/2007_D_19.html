Hindustan Aeronautics Ltd v Dan Bahadur Singh & Ors

Supreme Court of India

27 April 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

G.P. Mathur, A.K. Mathur

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 465; (2007) 6 SCC 207; (2007) 2 SCC (L&S) 441; AIR 2007 SC 2733; 2007(4) ALL MR 756; 2007 (6) AWC 6310; 2007 (3) CLR 513; 2008 (1) CompLJ 381; 2007 (3) ESC 369; 2007 (114) FLR 204; JT 2007 (6) SC 320; 2007 (3) LLJ 234; 2007 (4) LLN 93; 2007(6) SCALE 415; 2007 (7) SCJ 933; [2007] 5 S.C.R. 923; 2007 (5) SLR 370; 2007 (3) UPLBEC 2103

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Horticulture, Age Of Superannuation, Daily Rate, Lay Off, Trade Union Act, 1926

Summary: Trade Unions Act, 1926 - Regularize the services of the members of the petitioner union who had put in more than 240 days of continuous service in each calendar year and place them in the pay scale of the post of Mali - Respondents contend the writ petitioners cannot be given appointment on regular post - No continuous and full time work of that nature - Already have surplus labour - Basic object of the company was to produce state of art avionics equipment for aircrafts which was being manufactured for use by the Indian Air Force - Horticulture activities are in no way connected with the production activities of the company - Held, well settled that there is no right vested in any daily wager to seek regularization - Regularization can not be de hors the rules - Impugned judgment set aside.
