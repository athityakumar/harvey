Bal Ram Bali And Another v Union Of India

Supreme Court of India

24 August 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

G.P. Mathur, P.K. Balasubramanyan

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 840; (2007) 6 SCC 805; AIR 2007 SC 3074; 2007 (69) ALR 155; 2007 (4) AWC 3576; 2007 (4) JhrCR(SC) 75; JT 2007 (10) SC 509; 2007(10) SCALE 277; 2007 (6) SCJ 800

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Environment, Wildlife & Animal; Parliament & Legislature; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Prayer to issue a writ of mandamus under art. 32 and direct the respondents to ensure and guarantee with immediate effect for total ban on slaughter of cows, who are regarded by Hindus as their divine mother, to respect the Hindu religion and its followers of all sects - Held, it is not within the domain of the Court to issue a direction for ban on slaughter of cows, buffaloes and horses as it is a matter of policy on which decision has to be taken by the Government - Courts cannot issue any direction to the Parliament or to the State legislature to enact a particular kind of law - Petition dismissed.
