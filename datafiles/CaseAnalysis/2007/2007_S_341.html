Girja Prasad (Dead) By Lrs v State Of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

27 August 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

C.K. Thakker, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 849; (2007) 3 SCC (Cr) 475; (2007) 7 SCC 625; AIR 2007 SC 3106; JT 2007 (10) SC 421; 2007 (4) KLT 99; 2007 (4) RCR(Criminal) 84; 2007(10) SCALE 358; [2007] 9 S.C.R. 483

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure; Socio-Economic

Keywords: Illegal Gratification, Power Of The Court, Police Witness

Summary: Indian Penal Code, ss. 161; Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947, ss. 5(1)(d) r/w 5(2) - Appeal against conviction and sentence - Held, once it is proved that the amount has been received by the accused, presumption under s. 4 of the Act would get attracted - S. 4 provided for presumption where public servant accepted gratification other than legal remuneration - There was no rebuttal by the accused by leading any evidence whatsoever - Trial Court was, therefore, wrong in not invoking s. 4 and raising presumption - High Court was justified in setting aside the order of acquittal and in convicting the accused for the offences with which he was charged - Appeal dismissed.
