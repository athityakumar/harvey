Marripati Nagaraja & Ors v The Government Of Andhra Pradesh & Ors

Supreme Court of India

12 October 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1594; (2007) 11 SCC 522; 2008 (1) ALD(SC) 37; 2008 (3) JhrCR(SC) 43; JT 2007 (12) SC 407; 2007(12) SCALE 397; [2007] 11 S.C.R. 506; 2008 (1) SLR 665

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Andhra Pradesh State and Sunbordinate Services Rules

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 309 - Reservation for women - Andhra Pradesh Public Service Commission issued a notification inviting applications for recruitment to various posts including posts in category of Assistant Director of Agriculture in Andhra Pradesh Agricultural Service - Although in said notification stipulations were made in respect of grant of reservation for women to extent of 30%, no such stipulation was made in respect of vacancies in category of Assistant Director of Agriculture - Appellants, working as Agricultural Officer in Andhra Pradesh Agricultural Service, applied for said posts - . A screening test was to be held therefor - State of Andhra Pradesh issued Government order providing for reservations for women candidates to extent of 30% in matter of direct recruitment with retrospective effect from 2.1.1984 and also percentage of reservation for women was increased to 331/3% - Thereafter, application filed by Andhra Pradesh Public Service Commission to short-list candidates was allowed - Pursuant thereto fresh screening test was conducted - Appellant, although appeared, did not pass said test - Appellant filed application before Tribunal challenging the same - Tribunal held that selections made by Commission did not call for any interference except to the extent that selections revised restricting reservation in favour of women to extent of 30% - Writ petition filed by appellants questioning same was dismissed - A writ application filed by Andhra Pradesh Public Service Commission challenging decision of Tribunal restricting reservation for women to 30% instead of 331/3% was also dismissed - Hence, present appeals - Whether Tribunal and HC committed an error in opining that reservation for women should have been kept confined to 30% only? - Held, State, in exercise of its power conferred upon it under proviso appended to art. 309 of Constitution, is entitled to make rules with retrospective effect and retro-active operation - If some rule has been given a retrospective effect which is within domain of State, unless same is set aside as being unconstitutional, consequences flowing therefrom shall ensure - In such an event, applicable rule would not be rule which was existing but one which had been validly brought on statute book from an anterior date - Hence, Tribunal and HC erred in opining otherwise, particularly when constitutionality of said rule was not in question - Appeals disposed of.
