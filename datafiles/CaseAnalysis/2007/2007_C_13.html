C.S. Azad Krishi Evem Prodyogiki Vishwa v United Trades Congress and Another

Supreme Court of India

13 December 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1346; AIR 2008 SC (Supp) 216; 2008 (1) ESC 23; JT 2007 (13) SC 644; 2008 (1) LLJ 875; 2007(14) SCALE 590; [2007] 13 S.C.R. 704

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Cooperative, Daily Rate, Law Officer, U.P. Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, Uttar Pradesh (Krishi Evam Prodyogik Vishwavidyalaya Adhiniyam) Act, 1958

Summary: Uttar Pradesh (Krishi Evam Prodyogik Vishwavidyalaya Adhiniyam) Act, 1958 - Respondent No. 2 was appointed on daily-wages - Labour Court held that in spite of the fact that respondent workman is working from 1980 and is being treated as daily wager, this clearly amounts to unfair labour practice - Writ petition against dismissed - Appeal against - Held, an employee appointed on a regular basis would not be given an offer of appointment or shall not be placed on a scale of pay - Respondent No. 2 was appointed on daily- wages - Respondent No. 2 had been working for more than 240 days continuously from the date of his engagement itself does not confer any right upon a workman to be regularized in service - Appeal allowed.
