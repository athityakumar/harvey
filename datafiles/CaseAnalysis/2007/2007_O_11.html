Commissioner of Income Tax, New Delhi v Oriental Fire and General Insurance Company Limited

Supreme Court of India

18 May 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 581; (2007) 6 SCC 496; AIR 2007 SC 2248; [2007] 291 ITR 370; JT 2007 (8) SC 349; 2007(8) SCALE 170; 2007 (8) SCJ 323; [2007] 7 S.C.R. 462; [2007] 161 TAXMAN 181

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Insurance; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Entertainment Allowance, General Reserve, Controller Of Insurance, Non Obstante Clause, Revaluation, Assessee Carrying On Business, Calendar Year, Mutual Insurance Company, Revenue Account, Valuation Of Liabilities, Value Of Assets

Summary: Insurance Act, 1938; Income Tax Act, 1961 - Tribunal held that the Assessing Officer was not correct in refusing to accede to the deductions in respect of the provisions of taxation by way of "reserve for bad and doubtful debts" and "entertainment allowance" - High Court answered the reference in favour of the respondent and against the Revenue - Appeal against - Held, ordinarily the annual accounts furnished before the Controller of Insurance would be taken to be the balance of the profits disclosed thereby - The same, however, is subject to the adjustments mentioned therein, namely, any expenditure or allowance which is not admissible under the provisions of ss. 30 to 43A in computing the profits and gains of the business - If the said provision is found to be applicable, the amount may be added back - Appeals dismissed.
