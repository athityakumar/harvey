State of Manipur and Another v Ksh. Moirangningthou Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

26 February 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Markandey Katju, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1415; (2007) 10 SCC 544; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 1711; 2007 (113) FLR 199; 2007 (5) SCJ 929; [2007] 3 S.C.R. 193; 2007 (4) SLR 353

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Tripura Home Guards Act, 1947, Home Guards Act, Manipur Home Guards Act, 1966, Manipur Home Guards Rules, 1981

Summary: Service - Tripura Home Guards Act, 1947 - Manipur Home Guards Act, 1966, ss. 4(4) and 8 - Manipur Home Guards Rules, 1981, r. 3 - Regularisation - Entitled to - Whether respondents are entitled to regularisation under Act? - Home Guards Act has been constituted as a voluntary organization for service in emergencies and hence it cannot be treated at par with other organizations like army, para military organizations or civil police - Initial term of appointment of a member of Home Guards can only be three years, and he can be reappointed from time to time, but he cannot continue after age of 55 years - Initial appointment is for 3 years after which it is at discretion of Commandant (subject to approval of Commandant General) to reappoint a member of Home Guards, or not - Held, a perusal of provisions of Home Guards Act and Rules show that Home Guards was meant to be a reserve force which was to be utilized in emergencies, but it was not a service like police, para military force, or army, and there is no right in a member to continue till age of 55 years - Respondents not entitled to regularisation and regular pay scales - Appeal allowed.
