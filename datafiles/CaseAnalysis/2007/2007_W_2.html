M/S Associated Indem Mechanical Pvt. Ltd. v West Bengal Small Scale Industrial Development Corporation Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

05 January 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

G.P. Mathur, A.K. Mathur

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 15; (2007) 3 SCC 607; AIR 2007 SC 788; 2007 (2) CHN 183; JT 2007 (1) SC 402; 2007 (5) MLJ(SC) 775; 2007(1) SCALE 117; [2007] 1 S.C.R. 174

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Oil Mills, Fixtures, West Bengal Government Premises (Tenancy Regulation) Act, 1976, West Bengal Public Land (Eviction of unauthorized occupants) Act, 1962, West Bengal Government Premises (Regulation of Occupancy) Act, 1984

Summary: West Bengal Government Premises (Tenancy Regulation) Act, 1976 - Appellant's challenging cancellation of lease deed which had been executed in its favour - Held, since it has been found as a fact that the appellant has contravened the provisions of clauses 2(f)1 and B of the lease deed, it cannot claim any right under clause VI(b) - Appellant is not entitled to exercise the option to acquire ownership of the demised industrial sheds and his claim in that regard is wholly baseless - Appeal dismissed.
