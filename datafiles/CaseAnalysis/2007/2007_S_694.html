Rajendra Singh v State of Uttar Pradesh and Another

Supreme Court of India

06 August 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

G.P. Mathur, P.K. Balasubramanyan

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 789; (2007) 3 SCC (Cr) 375; (2007) 7 SCC 378; AIR 2007 SC 2786; 2007 ALJ 485; 2007 ALL MR (Cri) 2655; 2007 (3) ALT 314; 2007 CRLJ 4281; 2007 (3) JCC 2371; JT 2007 (9) SC 613; 2007 (3) KLT 892; 2007 (3) RCR(Criminal) 1021; 2007(9) SCALE 593; 2007 (8) SCJ 307; [2007] 8 S.C.R. 834

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Power Of The Court, Gun Shot Injury

Summary: Indian Penal Code, s. 302; Criminal Procedure Code, ss. 482, 319 - Allahabad High Court set aside the order of Sessions Judge, Allahabad summoning respondent to face trial under s. 302 - Appeal against - Held statements of the witnesses under s. 161 Cr.P.C. being wholly inadmissible in evidence could not at all be taken into consideration - High Court relied upon wholly inadmissible evidence to set aside the order passed by the learned Sessions Judge - That apart, no finding on a plea of alibi can be recorded by the High Court for the first time in a petition under s. 482 Cr.P.C - Whole procedure adopted by the High Court is clearly illegal and cannot be sustained - Appeal allowed.
