Suraj Bhan and Others v Financial Commissioner and Others

Supreme Court of India

16 April 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

C.K. Thakker, P.K. Balasubramanyan

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 379; (2007) 6 SCC 186; 2007 (67) ALR 789; 2007 (3) AWC 2329; 2007 (3) CHN 9; JT 2007 (6) SC 152; 2007 (103) RD 116; 2007(5) SCALE 712; 2007 (7) SCJ 33; [2007] 5 S.C.R. 155

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property

Keywords: Appeal disposed., CONSTITUTION.

Summary: Delhi Land Revenue Act, 1954; Constitution of India, 1950 - Whether the High Court's confirmation of the order passed by Revenue Authorities by which the name of respondent No. 5 was mutated in the Revenue Records proper - Whether the will is genuine - Respondent No. 5 forged a Will and his name was entered in the Revenue Record -Neither notice, nor an opportunity of hearing was afforded to the appellants- Writ petition filed by the appellants was also dismissed - Held, the High Court had also committed an error of law in dismissing the writ petition - The validity and genuineness of the Will can only be decided by a competent Civil Court - By entering the name of respondent No. 5 in Revenue Records, no illegality had been committed by Tehsildar as the High Court confirmed all the orders passed by Revenue Authorities under the Act - Settled law that entries in the Revenue Records have only 'fiscal purpose' i.e. payment of land-revenue, and no ownership is conferred on the basis of such entries - Appeal dismissed
