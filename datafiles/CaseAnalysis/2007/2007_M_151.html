Maharashtra University of Health Sciences Represented By Deputy Registrar v Paryani Mukesh Jawaharlal and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 June 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1216; (2007) 10 SCC 201; AIR 2007 SC 2264; 2007 (6) AWC 5891; 2007 (3) ESC 467; JT 2007 (9) SC 127; 2007(8) SCALE 529; 2007 (5) SCJ 280; [2007] 7 S.C.R. 891; 2007 (5) SLR 45; 2007 (6) SLR 635

Case Digest

Subject: Health & Drug

Keywords: Applications Dismissed, MCI Regulations

Summary: Education - Maharashtra University of Health Sciences Act, 1998 - Indian Medical Council Act, 1956, s. 33 - Regulations on Graduate Medical Education 1997, regns. 4, 12(2) and 12(4) - Contentions of Students are that standards or criteria for passing adopted by University were contrary to MCI Regulations, that on marks obtained by them, they ought to have been declared as having passed in subjects, and that University had shown them as 'failed' by applying cls. 56(2) and 57 of Amended University Ordinance 1/ 2002 which wrongly interpreted cls. (2) and (4) of regn. 12 of MCI Regulations - (A) Declaration sought that cls. 56(2) and 57 of University Ordinance 1 of 2002 and consequential University Notification No. 5 of 2006 D/- 20-2-2006 were illegal and ultra vires - Held, interpretation put forth by University was accepted by Court in respect of MCI regn. 12 as correct and it was held by Court that cls. 56(2) and 57 of amended University Ordinance 1/2002 are in consonance with cls. (2) and (4) of MCI Regn. 12 - (B)Qualification in examination - Internal and external exams - Whether internal assessment marks can be clubbed with University examination (external) marks to ascertain result of a student? - Cls. 56(2) and 57 of amended University Ordinance 1/2002 are in consonance with cls. (2) and (4) of MCI regn. 12 - Further held, internal assessment marks cannot be clubbed with University examination (external) marks to ascertain whether a candidate has passed in theory with orals and practicals - Appeal allowed.
