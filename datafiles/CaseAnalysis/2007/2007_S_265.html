General Insurance Council & Ors v State Of Andhra Pradesh & Ors

Supreme Court of India

09 July 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, P.P. Naolekar

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 726; (2007) 12 SCC 354; (2008) 1 SCC (Cr) 384; 2007 (4) ACC 385; AIR 2007 SC 2696; 2007 (5) ALD(SC) 76; 2007 (4) AWC 3371; 2007 (5) MLJ(SC) 1290; 2008 (149) PLR 588; 2007 (4) RCR(Civil) 54; 2007(9) SCALE 165; 2007 (5) SCJ 397; [2007] 8 S.C.R. 192

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 - Petition is for direction to the various States Governments and the Union Territories to ensure that the mandate of s. 158 (6) is complied with without exception - S. 158(6) mandates the police officer to forward a copy of the report to the Claims Tribunal having jurisdiction and to the concerned insurance company "as soon as any information regarding any accident involving death or bodily injury is recorded or a report under Section 158 is completed by the police officer - Held, since there is a mandatory requirement to act in the manner provided in s. 158(6) there is no justifiable reason as to why the requirement is not being followed - Directed that all the State Governments and the Union Territories shall instruct, if not already done, all concerned police officers about the need to comply with the requirement of s. 158(6) - Petition disposed of.
