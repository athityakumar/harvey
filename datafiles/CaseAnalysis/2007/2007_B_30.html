State of Madhya Pradesh v Babulal

Supreme Court of India

03 December 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

C.K. Thakker, Altamas Kabir

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1262; (2008) 1 SCC 234; AIR 2008 SC 582; 2008 ALL MR (Cri) 329; 2008 (3) CGLJ 217; 2008 CRLJ 714; 2008 (1) JCC 130; JT 2007 (13) SC 272; 2008 (1) RCR(Criminal) 213; 2007(13) SCALE 621; [2007] 12 S.C.R. 795

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Raped Women

Summary: Indian Penal Code, s. 376 - Trial Court imposed minimum sentence of seven years' rigorous imprisonment and to pay fine of Rs.2, 500/- - High Court held that imprisonment for two months and three days which had already undergone by accused could be said to be 'just and proper' - Appeal against reduction of sentence - Held, once a person is convicted for an offence of rape, he should be treated with a heavy hand - An undeserved indulgence or liberal attitude in not awarding adequate sentence in such cases would amount to allowing or even to encouraging 'potential criminals' - There were no reasons much less 'adequate' and 'special' reasons to reduce the sentence less than the minimum required to be imposed under s. 376(1) - Appeal allowed.
