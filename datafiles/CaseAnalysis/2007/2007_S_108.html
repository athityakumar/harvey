Secretary, Uttar Pradesh High School and Intermediate Education, Allahabad and Another v H. K. LaL

Supreme Court of India

11 January 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1551; (2007) 2 SCC 216; AIR 2007 SC 1167; 2007 (6) AWC 5883; 2007(2) SCALE 312; [2007] 1 S.C.R. 822

Case Digest

Subject: Education

Summary: Education - Practice & Procedure - Date of Birth - Alteration - Validity - Respondent was Principal in College - Matriculation certificate granted by appellants respondents date of birth was recorded as 25-6-1938 - In year 1994 writ petition was filed praying for issuance of writ of Mandamus directing appellants to alter date of birth to 17.8.1940 - Writ petition was disposed of by judgment and order directing Board to pass appropriate order - Pursuant to direction, application filed by respondent for alteration of date of birth was rejected - Another writ petition was filed by respondent which was also dismissed on premise that remedy lay in filing suit before Civil Court - Thereafter respondent filed suit in year 1997 before Civil Judge - Suit was decreed - Appeal was preferred by Board against said judgment and order of stay was granted - However, said order of stay was later on vacated and writ petition was filed - HC set aside order and directed District Judge to recall matter and dispose or transfer it to any Court - Respondent filed writ petition before HC and was allowed where under Secretary Education was directed to comply with the decree passed by Civil Court - Against said judgment and decree, Special appeal filed by appellant had been dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether respondent had legal right to alter date of birth recorded in certificate granted by Board - Held, writ petition filed by respondent should therefore, not have been entertained because appeal was pending - Writ jurisdiction was discretionary jurisdiction and should not ordinarily be exercised if there was alternative remedy - In instant case, Court opined that writ petition itself was not maintainable and Single Judge, was wrong in allowing same and consequently impugned judgment passed by DB also suffers from error - Impugned judgment of HC was set aside - Appeal allowed.
