Ashutosh v Indian Airlines Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

18 April 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, B. SUDARSHAN REDDY

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1562; (2007) 12 SCC 406; 2007 (6) AWC 6093; 2007(5) SCALE 803; [2007] 5 S.C.R. 294

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Indian Airlines Recruitment and Promotion Rules

Summary: Service - Air law - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Indian Airlines Recruitment and Promotion Rules - Seniority - Appointment - Promotion - Eligibility - Settlement was reached under which then existing designations were revised Association requested Management to grant conditional promotion in various grades to enable employees like respondents 3 to 17 to complete their eligibility before cut-off date - Management and Union entered into Discussions with regard to conditional promotion - Vide Order respondents 3 to 17 were given conditional promotions to post of Deputy Chief Aircraft Engineers - They were required to obtain nine approvals by 31-3-1995 - Jet Shop set up by Indian Airlines was approved only in February, 1995, therefore, respondents 3 to 17 could not fulfill requisite qualification by cut-off date - Appellant had completed two years service in post of Sr. Aircraft Engineer on 31-3-1993 - Respondent acquired requisite qualification on 15-12-1993 - According to appellant he was qualified for promotion on 15-12-1993 whereas respondents 3 to 17 till date have not acquired requisite qualification - Appellant, therefore, prayed that respondents 3 to 17 were not entitled to promotions to post of Deputy Chief Aircraft Engineers - Appellant had not been given promotion as Deputy Chief Aircraft Engineer from 15-12-1993 when he acquired qualification and, therefore, Indian Airlines had erred in showing respondents 3 to 17 as senior to appellant in post of Deputy Chief Aircraft Engineer with effect from 1-4-1992/1-4-1993 - It was urged that appellant acquired approval qualification on 15-12-1993 but vide impugned order Management gave conditional promotions to respondent no's 3 to 17 with effect from 1-4-1992/1-4-1993 which created discrimination which violated appellant's fundamental right to equality u/art. 14 of Constitution - Hence, instant appeal - Whether appellant was entitled to seniority in matter of promotion to post of Deputy Chief Aircraft Engineer vis-vis respondent nos. 3 to 17 - Held, in instant case, recitals in Industrial Settlement quoted showed that during pendency of proceedings before Tribunal with regard to parity of wage structure, bilateral negotiations took place and Settlement came to be filed before Tribunal - After entering into Settlement, while implementing, some difficulties were detected, therefore, decision was taken by Management in consultation with Association on 9-6-1993 as part of Settlement to have 20% out of 49 posts to be earmarked for Sr. Engineers - This was decision taken as Sr. Engineers were facing stagnation - Court further held that while granting approval DGCA did not go by seniority and consequently approvals were given earlier in point of time to engineers who were junior to respondents which created anomaly - Hence, there was no violation of Recruitment and Promotion Rules - Approval qualifications formed part of Industrial Settlement filed before Tribunal which was subsequently modified by policy decision dated 9-6-1993 read with Note of Discussions dated 23-7-1993 between Management and Association - Appeal dismissed.
