Jagvir Singh and Others v State (Delhi Admn.)

Supreme Court of India

05 June 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 617; (2007) 5 SCC 359; (2007) 2 SCC (Cr) 506; AIR 2007 SC 2244; 2007 ALL MR (Cri) 2019; JT 2007 (8) SC 488; 2007 (3) KLT 278; 2007 (3) RCR(Civil) 375; 2007(8) SCALE 352; [2007] 7 S.C.R. 946

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 342, 365, 330 r/w 34 - Appeal against conviction and sentence - Single Judge observed that conviction was not questioned and held that in view of the concessions made relating to the conviction, the sentence cannot be held to be disproportionate keeping in view the nature of the offence - Appeal against - Held, if a party thinks that happenings in Court have been wrongly recorded in a judgment, it is incumbent upon the party, while the matter is still fresh in the minds of the Judges, to call the attention of the very Judge who has made the record - It is not open to the appellants to contend before this Court to the contrary - Appeal dismissed.
