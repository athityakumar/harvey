Messrs Mahim Patram Private Limited v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

23 February 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 266; (2007) 3 SCC 668; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 323; 2007 (3) ALJ 508; JT 2007 (4) SC 50; 2007(3) SCALE 584; 2007 (7) SCJ 509; [2007] 3 S.C.R. 73; [2007] 6 VST 248

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Keywords: Sale Price, Collected, Determination Of Turnover, Uttar Pradesh framed Central Sales Tax (U.P.) Rules, 1957, Uttar Pradesh Trade Tax Rules, 1948

Summary: Central Sales Tax Act, 1956 - Works contract - Whether there exist sufficient guidelines for determination of the turnover in the hands of the Assessing Authority for the purpose of levy of tax? - Held, If in absence of any rules, the determination of turnover becomes payable, an assessee or a dealer cannot derive any benefit by reason of non-framing of any rule which is contemplated under the Act - Appeals dismissed.
