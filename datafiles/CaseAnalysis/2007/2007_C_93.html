Himadri Chemicals Industries Limited v Coal Tar Refining Company

Supreme Court of India

07 August 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Tarun Chatterjee, P.K. Balasubramanyan

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1307; (2007) 8 SCC 110; AIR 2007 SC 2798; 2007 (4) AWC 3438; 2007 (3) CalLT(SC) 86; [2007] 139 Comp Cas 706; 2007 (5) CompLJ 192; JT 2007 (9) SC 631; 2007 (3) RCR(Civil) 768; 2007(9) SCALE 631; [2007] 8 S.C.R. 869

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Practice & Procedure; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Arbitration & ADR - Practice & Procedure - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, s. 9 - Contract - Letter of Credit - Justifiability - Appellant entered into contract with respondent, who agreed to supply goods as per schedule set out in contract - Irrevocable letter of credit was opened in favour of respondent - Appellant taken various steps to resolve dispute regarding quality of goods - Appellant filed application u/s. 9 of the Act for injunction to stop release of payment under LOC and interim order of status quo was granted for short period and later vacated - Appellant filed appeal before DB of HC, which affirmed order of Single Bench of HC - Hence, instant appeal - whether DB of HC was justified in affirming order of learned Single Judge vacating interim order of status quo in matter of stopping payment in terms of Letter of Credit - Held, While dealing with application for injunction in course of commercial dealings, and when unconditional bank guarantee or letter of credit was given or accepted, the Beneficiary was entitled to realize such a Bank Guarantee or a letter of credit in terms there of irrespective of any pending disputes relating to terms of contract - There were two exceptions when courts could grant order of injunction in favour of aggrieved party in matter of encashment of a bank guarantee or a letter of credit - It had been rightly held by HC that particulars of fraud pleaded in application could not constitute fraud as fraud should be in respect of whole consignment and not in respect of a part of same - Case where allowing encashment of unconditional bank guarantee or LOC would result in irretrievable harm or injustice to on one of parties concerned - In instant case, exceptional circumstances had not been made out by appellant - Therefore, question of irretrievable injury even prima facie which would lead to injustice or harm appellant could not at all be conceived as appellant had been protected by furnishing of bank guarantee - Thus, HC was justified in refusing to grant order of injunction in favour of appellant - Appeal dismissed.
