National Insurance Co. Ltd. v M/S Ishar Das Madan Lal

Supreme Court of India

20 February 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 177; (2007) 4 SCC 105; 2007 (1) ACC 717; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 508; 2007 (3) ALD(SC) 74; 2007 (67) ALR 834; 2007 (2) CalLT(SC) 17; [2007] 136 Comp Cas 320; 2007 (2) CPJ(Sc) 5; 2007 (2) JCC 1082; 2007 (3) JhrCR(SC) 62; JT 2007 (4) SC 21; 2007 (3) MLJ(SC) 726; 2007 (146) PLR 255; 2007(3) SCALE 336; 2007 (6) SCJ 118; [2007] 2 S.C.R. 1014

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection; Criminal; Insurance; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Fraudulent Conduct, Insurance Agreement, Reinsurance, J & K Consumers Protection Act, 1987

Summary: J & K Consumers Protection Act, 1987 - Insurance - Respondent obtained a policy known as 'Jeweller Block Policy' - Theft of 140 gms of jewellery worth of Rs.63, 000/- occurred - Whether exclusion clause 8 of the policy is applicable to the facts and circumstances? - Held, Clause 8 of the contract of insurance would be attracted only where the offences specified therein are committed by any of the persons mentioned therein - For defeating the claim of the respondent, it was, thus, obligatory on the part of the appellant to establish that the conditions prescribed therein were satisfied - A customer contemplated thereunder must have to be one who would be a man of trust - If a customer is not a man of trust or the property had not been entrusted to him, the exclusion clause would not apply - No occasion for the respondent to entrust the jewellery to him - Appeal dismissed.
