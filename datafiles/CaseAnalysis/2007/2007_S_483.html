State of Gujarat v Turabali Gulamhussain Hirani and Another

Supreme Court of India

04 October 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Markandey Katju, A.K. Mathur

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 980; (2007) 14 SCC 94; AIR 2008 SC 86; 2007 ALL MR (Cri) 3299; 2008 (2) G.L.R. 1061; JT 2007 (12) SC 308; 2007 (4) KLT 656; 2007 (4) RCR(Civil) 531; 2007 (4) RCR(Criminal) 560; 2007(11) SCALE 556; [2007] 10 S.C.R. 531

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Case related to an incentive scheme of the State government for attracting new entrepreneurs - Criminal Appeal was filed with a delay of 25 days - Order directing the Chief Secretary and Law Secretary of the Gujarat government to be personally present - Appeal against - Held, High Court has power to summon these officials, but that should be done in very rare and exceptional cases when there are compelling circumstances to do so - Such summoning orders should not be passed lightly or as a routine or at the drop of a hat - Appeal allowed.
