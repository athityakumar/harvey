R.J. Shah & Co v H.P. State Electricity Board

Supreme Court of India

19 July 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 737; (2007) 10 SCC 620; AIR 2007 SC 2682; JT 2007 (13) SC 520; 2007(9) SCALE 297; 2007 (7) SCJ 890; [2007] 8 S.C.R. 416

Case Digest

Subject: Socio-Economic

Keywords: Indian Independence Act, 1947, New States Act, 1954, State of Himachal Pradesh Act, 1970

Summary: Sustainability of High Court's view that the Letters Patent in Lahore Court was not maintainable as no appeal either under Clause 9 or Clause 10 of the Letters Patent was maintainable in the High Court and the impugned order was not covered by O. 43 r. 1 of the Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Held, remanded the matter to it to decide the controversy afresh in the light of Jugal Kishore's case (1984 (1) SCC 358) and also to take note of the view expressed by this Court in P.S. Sathappan (dead) by Lrs. Vs. Andhra Bank Ltd. and Ors. (2004 (11) SCC 672) - Appeals disposed of.
