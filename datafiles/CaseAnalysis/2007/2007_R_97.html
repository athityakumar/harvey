State Of Uttar Pradesh v Ram Veer Singh And Others

Supreme Court of India

05 September 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 878; (2007) 13 SCC 102; AIR 2007 SC 3075; 2007(10) SCALE 545; [2007] 9 S.C.R. 689

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Blood Stains

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302 r/w 34 - Appeal against acquittal - Held, High Court has examined all the relevant aspects in detail and has recorded the judgment of acquittal - Order of acquittal shall not be interfered with because the presumption of innocence of the accused is further strengthened by acquittal - Reasons indicated by the High Court for recording the order of acquittal do not suffer from any infirmity to warrant interference - Appeal dismissed.
