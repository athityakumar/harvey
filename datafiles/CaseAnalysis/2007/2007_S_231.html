Nelson Fernandes and others v Special Land Acquisition Officer, South Goa and others

Supreme Court of India

02 March 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

AR. Lakshmanan, Altamas Kabir

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1584; (2007) 9 SCC 447; AIR 2007 SC 1414; 2007 (2) AWC 1473; 2007 (2) Bom.C.R. 737; 2007 (3) JhrCR(SC) 161; JT 2007 (8) SC 285; 2007 (3) MLJ(SC) 751; 2007 (2) RCR(Civil) 508; 2007(4) SCALE 52; [2007] 3 S.C.R. 563

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4, 22, 23 and 24 - Compensation - Petitioner's land was acquired by government for construction of railway line - Special Land Acquisition Officer granting separate compensation for land and trees - Appellant made an application before Land Acquisition Officer to refer the matter for determination of compensation u/s. 18 of Act and claimed a sum of Rs.89,06,250/- for the acquired land and Rs.71,000/- for the trees standing thereon - Reference Court enhanced compensation for the land but rejected the compensation for the trees - Both the appellants and the respondents preferred an appeal before HC against said judgment - DB rejected the report of the valuer and the findings of the District Judge and reduced the rate of compensation from Rs. 192/- per sq. metre as awarded by the District Judge to Rs. 38/- per sq. Metre - Appellants contended that the HC has not considered the provisions of s. 23 of 1894 Act while fixing compensation for the land and no cogent reasons were given by the HC for reduction of the compensation - Appellants also contended that impugned lands are of high commercial value and is well connected to other cities and that the trees existed on the land were of high value hence, separate compensation should be given for the trees - Hence, present appeal - Whether appellant's contentions could be accepted and order of HC could be set aside? - Held, HC has adopted a rough and ready method for making deductions which is impermissible in law - No reason whatsoever was given by the Reference Court or by the HC as to why the report of the valuer and her evidence cannot be relied on - Compensation awarded by the HC had no basis whatsoever and was not supported by cogent reasons and that it did not consider the future prospect of the development of the land in question - HC also did not assess the injury that the appellant is likely to sustain due to loss of his future earnings from the said land and also did not assess the damage already suffered due to diminution of the profits of the land between the time of publication of the notice and time of the collector taking possession - Hence, DB has miserably erred in passing the order impugned thereby reducing the rate of compensation from Rs. 192/- to Rs. 38/- and in utter mis-reading of the evidence on record and acted in a flagrant error of law and facts - Orders passed by DB set aside - Appeals allowed.
