J. Yashoda v K. Shobha Rani

Supreme Court of India

19 April 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 384; (2007) 3 SCC (Cr) 9; (2007) 1 SCC (L&S) 651; (2007) 5 SCC 730; AIR 2007 SC 1721; 2007(3) ALL MR 823; 2007 (68) ALR 146; 2007 (3) AWC 2353; 2007 (6) Bom.C.R. 783; 2007 (3) CHN 139; 2007 (1) CLR 670; 2007 (3) CTC 781; 2007 (212) E.L.T. 458; JT 2007 (6) SC 64; 2007 (2) KLT 804; 2007 (4) MLJ(SC) 958; 2007 (147) PLR 310; 2007 (2) RCR(Civil) 840; 2007 (103) RD 191; 2007(6) SCALE 6; 2007 (3) SCJ 825; [2007] 5 S.C.R. 367; 2007 (2) SLR 42

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Photostat Copy

Summary: Indian Evidence Act, ss. 63, 64, 65 - Whether photo copies ought to have been received as secondary evidence - Admittedly photo copies, there was no possibility of the documents being compared with the originals - Held, secondary evidence is only taken in the absence of better evidence - universal rule that the best evidence the nature of the case will admit shall be produced - To enable a party to produce secondary evidence it is necessary for the party to prove existence and execution of the original document - s. 65 permits secondary evidence to be given of the existence, condition or contents of documents under the circumstances mentioned.
