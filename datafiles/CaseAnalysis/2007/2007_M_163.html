Maruti Udyog Limited v Mahinder C. Mehta and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 October 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1384; (2007) 13 SCC 220; AIR 2008 SC 309; 2008 (1) AWC 989; 2008 (1) BC 151; [2007] 140 Comp Cas 449; 2008 (1) CompLJ 90; JT 2007 (12) SC 27; 2007 (4) RCR(Civil) 817; 2007 (4) RCR(Criminal) 959; 2007(11) SCALE 750; [2007] 10 S.C.R. 933; 2008 (1) UPLBEC 560

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Constitution; Practice & Procedure; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Arbitration & ADR - Constitution - Practice & Procedure - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, ss. 8,9,13 & 17 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 129 - Contempt of Courts Act, 1971 - Reference of Dispute to Arbitration - Directions - Contempt of Court Order - Justifiability - Respondents filed application u/s. 18 of the 1996 Act for settlement of difference with regard to furnishing of bank guarantee, which was dismissed - Appeal preferred by respondent was dismissed by DB - SLP was filed before SC, which passed interim order directing respondents to furnish bank guarantee within 4 week from date of order - Hence, instant contempt petition - Petitioner contended that alleged contemnors were bound to comply with orders passed by Arbitrator in terms of SC order dt. 6-9-2002 as they had not furnished bank guarantee pursuant thereto or in furtherance thereof and in fact alleged contemnors had committed a contempt of SC - Whether Contempt of Court order on part of respondents was justifiable - Held, in instant case, if contemnors were not in a position to furnish bank guarantee or otherwise, they could had taken such unequivocal stand before Courts - They had not only suppressed material facts, but also made proposal before Court for sale of property only on basis of title which they could only derive on happening of regularization by State - Proposal for sale of property could be made only if respondents had any subsisting title thereto and not otherwise - Thus, contemnors had mislead SC and had committed gross contempt of SC and hence petition allowed - Rule discharged.
