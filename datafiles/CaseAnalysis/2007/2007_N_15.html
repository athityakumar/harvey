Bcpp Mazdoor Sangh & Anr v N.T.P.C. & Ors

Supreme Court of India

11 October 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1601; (2007) 14 SCC 234; AIR 2008 SC 336; 2008 (1) ALD(SC) 56; 2008 (5) AWC 4625; JT 2007 (12) SC 156; 2007(12) SCALE 204; [2007] 10 S.C.R. 1084; 2007 (6) SLR 678

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Public Sector, Madhya Pradesh Industrial Relations Act

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Transfer of employees - Legality - On 26.07.1987, NTPC conducted a test for the post of Artisan Trainee (Fitter/Electrician) and the appellants were selected and received appointment orders in the name of NTPC (a Government of India Enterprise) BCPP - Later, Bharat Aluminium Company Limited (BALCO) entered into an agreement with NTPC on 22.05.1990 - In view of clause 8.2 of the said agreement, recruitment of non-executive staff shall be undertaken by NTPC specifically for BCPP as per NTPC recruitment norms and policies - Clause 16.3 of the said agreement makes it clear that in the event of transfer of management, BALCO shall ensure transfer to the successor organization of all non-executive staff recruited for BCPP as per provision contained in clause 8.0 and such NTPC executives posted at BCPP who were declared by NTPC as surplus to its requirements as a result of the transfer of management - Subsequently, when they were working with NTPC, which was managing BCPP, the Government of India decided to disinvest shares of BALCO, which resulted into conversion of BALCO a public sector enterprise to private sector organization and the existing management decided to manage BCPP by themselves - Subsequently, steps were being taken for transferring them to BALCO - Aggrieved by the decision of their transfer from a Public Sector Undertaking to private management, employees filed a writ petition - NTPC contended that it was merely an agent of BALCO on a specific Power of Attorney given to them and ownership of BCPP belongs to BALCO - Recruitment was made pursuant to an agreement exclusively for BCPP with specific undertaking from the non-executive employees - It was further contended that BCPP was being managed by NTPC on behalf of its owner i.e. BALCO which was being operated under the specific Power of Attorney - Further, that as per clauses 8.0 and 16.3 of the agreement dated 22.05.1990 entered into between NTPC and BALCO, since these employees were recruited and appointed for BCPP they can be transferred to BCPP which was made clear to them by mentioning in clause 14 of majority of appointment letters and once BCPP owned by BALCO was taken over by BALCO, the entire non-executive staff of BCPP will continue to remain in BCPP under the management of BALCO in terms of clauses 8.0 and 16.3 of the Agreement - Writ petition was dismissed - Hence, present appeals - (A) Clause 14 stipulated that appointment was made against the post sanctioned for BALCO and in case at a later date it was decided by BALCO to directly manage the Plant/Station or transfer its management to some other existing or new organization then services would stand transferred to BALCO or such successor organization as the case may be and no benefit/compensation for the past services from NTPC would be available - Whether terms sustainable? - Held, at the time of insertion of clause 14 in the appointment letter and obtaining undertakings from the employees there was no agreement between NTPC and BALCO for making recruitment and appointment on behalf of BALCO for its BALCO Captive Power Plant - Further, by virtue of the clause 14, the status of public servants have been sought to be changed which was violative of art. 14 - During the course of employment process, NTPC did not disclosed to the appellants that they were employing them for and on behalf of BALCO as their agent - NTPC was not justified in inserting clause 14 in the appointment letters and obtaining undertakings from the selectees - Clause 14 was against public policy and contrary to s. 23 of the Indian Contract Act as well as violative of art. 14 of the Constitution for the reason that undue influence was exercised by NTPC management and the selected candidates to accept the terms and conditions stipulated therein - (B) Whether action of management in transferring employees to a private management justifiable? - Held, Government or its instrumentality cannot alter the conditions of service of its employees and any such alteration causing prejudice cannot be effected without affording opportunity of pre-decisional hearing and the same would be arbitrary and violative of art. 14 of the Constitution - In the present case, NTPC being an undertaking of the Government of India and an instrumentality of State was under constitutional obligation to act fairly with its employees - (C) Whether transfer of employer was not permissible without tripartite agreement? - Held, in order to bind the employees, there must be a tripartite agreement - Also no employee could be transferred without his consent from one employer to another - In the present case, employees were neither party to tripartite agreement nor they have been heard before changing their service condition - Since there was no tripartite agreement the transfer from one employer to another cannot be effected - Therefore, the action of the management was violative of art. 14 of the Constitution - Appeals allowed.
