Poonam Verma and Others v Delhi Development Authority

Supreme Court of India

13 December 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1345; (2007) 13 SCC 154; AIR 2008 SC 870; 2008 (1) CCC 291; JT 2007 (13) SC 604; 2007(14) SCALE 485; [2007] 13 S.C.R. 552

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Consumer Protection Act, 1985, Court Fees and Suit Evaluation Act

Summary: Delhi Development Act, 1957 - Fifth Self Financing Housing Registration Scheme, 1982 - Allotment of flats - District Forum held that action of the respondent in not considering the cases of the appellants for allotment through the process of draw of lots amounted to unfair trade practice, apart from being unilateral and unjustified - State commission set aside the order of the District Forum - National Commission held that DDA reserves the right to withdraw the Scheme at any time - Scheme in question was closed as far back as in the year 1994 - Central Government in terms of the provisions of the Act or otherwise had no jurisdiction to revive the same - All the authorities under the Act including the Central Government being the creature of statute were bound to act within the four corners - Having professed to abide by the Brochure which contained the policy of reservation the Central Government could not in absence of any statutory provision directed creation of any quota and that too after closure of the Scheme - Appeal dismissed.
