Kolkata Municipal Corporation and others v Board of Trustees For Port of Kolkata and others

Supreme Court of India

29 November 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, B. SUDARSHAN REDDY

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1614; (2008) 1 SCC 615

Case Digest

Subject: Accounts & Audits; Land & Property

Summary: Accounts & Audits - Land & Property - Calcutta Port Act, 1890, s. 66K(3)(4) - Method of valuation - Alteration of - Appellant/Corporation entered into a lease agreement with respondent/Trust - Appellant, subsequently, issued special notices for altering the method of valuation from rental method to cost method and for enhancement of annual valuation, which was to be given effect to from the first quarters of 1996-1997 - Respondent objected to the effect being given to the said alteration from 1996-1997 - Hearing Officer passed an order making alteration in the valuation with effect from the first quarter of 1996-1997 - Respondent challenged the Hearing Officer order before the HC - Single Judge allowed the writ petition setting aside the Hearing Officer order - DB, in appeal, affirmed the Single Judge order - Whether the impugned alteration would be operated with effect from 1996-1997 - Held, in cases where the basis of valuation was altered, from rental to cost method, which led to increase in taxes, the alteration in the valuation finally confirmed by the Hearing Officer after hearing the parties, should operate from the quarter next succeeding that quarter in which the alteration was made and till such time the previous valuation should continue u/ss. 66K(3)(4) of the Act - Hence the impugned alteration would not operate with effect from 1996-1997 - No merit in appeal - Appeal dismissed.
