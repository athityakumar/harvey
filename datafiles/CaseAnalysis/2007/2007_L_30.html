Tamil Nadu Wakf Board v Larabsha Darga Panruti

Supreme Court of India

23 November 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1648; (2007) 13 SCC 416; AIR 2008 SC (Supp) 1237; 2008 (70) ALR 492; 2007 (7) AWC 6975; 2008 (2) JhrCR(SC) 18; JT 2008 (1) SC 123; 2008 (1) MLJ(SC) 982; 2008 (1) RCR(Civil) 807; 2007(13) SCALE 397; [2007] 12 S.C.R. 518

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Land & Property

Keywords: Muslim Wakfs Act, 1954, Dedication

Summary: Family & Personal - Land & Property - Wakf property or private trust property - Determination of - Suit property was wakf property being a part of a wakf property belonging to one 'N' - The 5th Janishan of 'N' conveyed the suit property to his disciple 'S' and he was in possession and enjoyment of the same through his disciple 'L' - 'L' conveyed the suit property to his wife 'K' by way of a Hibba with the intention of doing certain pious, religious and charitable purposes - 'K' conveyed the suit property to her grandson - After his death, his wife 'S' was managing the suit property and was performing the said pious, religious and charitable purposes - In 1978, 'S' filed suit for declaration that the suit property was not wakf property and it was their private property - Said suit was dismissed and it was held that the suit property was wakf property belonging to 'L' Darga - Appeal filed thereagainst was dismissed - During the pendency of second appeal, 'S' died - Thereafter, Wakf Board appointed the respondents as joint Muthavallis recognizing their right to be hereditary trustees and legal representatives of 'L' - HC dismissed the second appeal holding that the suit property was wakf property and not a private trust property - SLP filed thereagainst was dismissed - Respondents filed suit for a declaration that the suit Darga and its property belonged to a wakf i.e., wakf-alal-aulad, and the wakf Board had no jurisdiction to appoint Muthavallis for the said Darga and for injunction restraining the wakf Board from interfering with the suit Darga and its property except claiming contribution from the net income of the wakf - Trial Court decreed the suit holding that the suit Darga and its property belonged to a private wakf - On appeal, District Court held that the suit Darga and its property did not belong to a private wakf - Second appeal filed thereagainst was allowed reversing the judgment of the First Appellate Court and restoring the judgment of the Trial Court - Hence, the instant appeal filed by the Tamil Nadu Wakf Board - Whether the suit property was wakf property or a private trust property? - Appellant-Tamil Nadu wakf Board contended that in view of the decision in the earlier proceedings filed by 'S' and subsequent proceedings in respect of the same issue/property could not be proceeded with and was hit by the principle of res judicata - Held, it was rightly observed by the HC, inasmuch as a portion of the income was to be spent for the family apart from pious, religious and charitable purposes, it satisfied the character of a private wakf i.e. wakf-alal-aulad - The proforma maintained by the wakf Board also supported the claim of the respondents that they were the hereditary Muthavallis of the private wakf - Further, inasmuch as in appreciation of acceptable material, the Trial Court as well as the HC arrived at a conclusion that the suit property was a private wakf and not a private property, said decision had no bearing to the issue in the latter proceeding - HC rightly concluded from proforma that there was no indication that the wakf was a public wakf and Hibba only indicated that certain things have got to be carried out in respect of pious, religious and charitable purpose - Appeal dismissed.
