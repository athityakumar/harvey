Hamida v Rashid @ Rasheed And Others

Supreme Court of India

27 April 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

G.P. Mathur, A.K. Mathur

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 467; (2008) 1 SCC (Cr) 234; (2008) 1 SCC 474; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 361; 2007 (3) ALT 395; 2007 CRLJ 3422; JT 2007 (6) SC 392; 2007 (2) RCR(Criminal) 917; 2007(6) SCALE 517; 2007 (8) SCJ 101; [2007] 5 S.C.R. 937; 2007 (4) UC 479

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Indian Penal Code, ss.304, 324, 352 and 506; Criminal Procedure Code, s. 439 and 482 - Grant of bail by the High Court in exercise of power under s. 482 - Contended that bail could not have been granted under s. 482 - Specific provisions in s. 439 to approach the appropriate court for bail - Also, offence subsequently converted from one under 324, 352 and 506 to one under s. 304, IPC -Held, s. 482 Cr.P.C. saves the inherent powers of the High Court and its language is quite explicit - Well established that inherent power has to be exercised only to correct patent illegalities or some miscarriage of justice - In the case in hand, the accused respondents could have applied for bail afresh after the offence had been converted into one under Section 304 IPC - Since the procedure laid down for grant of bail under s. 439 has not been availed of by the accused, the exercise of power by the High Court under Section 482 Cr.P.C. is clearly illegal.
