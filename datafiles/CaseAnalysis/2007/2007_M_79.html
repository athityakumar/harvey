Commissioner Of Income Tax, Kerala v M/S Tara Agencies

Supreme Court of India

09 July 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Dalveer Bhandari, Ashok Bhan

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 711; (2007) 6 SCC 429; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 725; 2007 (214) E.L.T. 491; [2007] 292 ITR 444; JT 2007 (9) SC 65; 2007 (3) KLT 624; 2007(9) SCALE 100; 2007 (8) SCJ 887; [2007] 8 S.C.R. 136

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Finance Act, 1978, Marine Products Export Development Authority Act, 1972

Summary: Income Tax - Section 35B(1A), (2), Income Tax Act, 1961 - Weighted deduction - 'Manufacture, production and process' - Construction and meaning of - Respondent-assessee engaged in purchase of different qualities of tea and blending the same for the purpose of export - Claimed entitlement to weighted deduction being a small scale exporter - Disallowed by the Income Tax Officer - Appeal filed before the Commissioner was allowed on the ground that the respondent assessee was a small scale industrial unit in the light of certificate of registration granted to it by the Directorate of Industries, Kerala State - On appeal, the Income Tax Appellate Tribunal endorsed the view of the Commissioner - On further appeal, the High Court upheld the judgment of the Tribunal - Appellant submitted that the benefit under s. 35B(1A) can be extended to the assessee if the goods exported were either manufactured or produced and not when the goods were merely processed - Respondent contended that after the process of mixing, blending and approval by surveyor, the finished product is totally different from the various blended tea in respect of colour, texture, liquor, appearance, characteristic and even pricing, hence can be considered to have undergone the manufacturing process - Whether the business activity of the respondent-assessee falls within the ambit of production, manufacturing or processing and therefore entitled to weighted deduction under s. 35B(1A) in respect of expenditure incurred for its export - Held, in order to derive benefit under s. 35B(1A), the goods have to be either manufactured or produced by the small scale industrial undertaking - Intention of the legislature has to be gathered from the language used in the statue which means that attention should be paid to what has been said as also to what has not been said - Section 35(1)(b) governing the instant case incorporated the terms 'manufacture and production' and omitted the term 'processing' - Processing of tea of the respondent-assessee falls short of either manufacturing or production, therefore, because of the language of s. 35(1)(B), the respondent-assessee can not be extended the benefit - It is the bounden duty and obligation of the court to interpret the statute as it is - Respondent not entitled for weighted deduction under s. 35B(1A) - Impugned judgment set aside.
