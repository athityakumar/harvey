M/S. Transport Corporation Of India Ltd v M/S. Veljan Hydrair Ltd

Supreme Court of India

22 February 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 140; (2007) 3 SCC 142; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 543; 2007 (3) ALD(SC) 63; [2007] 136 Comp Cas 377; 2007 (2) CPJ(Sc) 35; JT 2007 (4) SC 8; 2007 (3) MLJ(SC) 762; 2007 (2) RCR(Civil) 587; 2007(3) SCALE 423; 2007 (6) SCJ 195; [2007] 2 S.C.R. 1082; 2008 (Supp) Bom.C.R. 29

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Consumer Protection; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Carrier, Common Carrier

Summary: Carriers Act, 1865 - Non-delivery of consignment - State Commission directed the appellant to pay the value of the consignment, (Rs.5, 83, 440/-) less the freight charges, with interest at the rate of 9% per annum from the date of booking, and costs of Rs.2000/- - Whether the complaint was barred by the provisions of s. 10 of the Carriers Act, 1865?; Whether the complaint was barred by limitation under s. 24A of the Consumer Protection Act, 1986?; Whether there was no contract for service, as the respondent had refused to pay the freight charges? - Held, appellant did not say that the consignment was lost - It is not possible to attribute knowledge of 'loss' to the person instituting the action for non-delivery - There was no need to issue a notice under s. 10 - Complaint is not barred under s. 24 - When the carrier informs that the consignment is not traced and is under the process of being traced, obviously the owner/consignor/consignee cannot be expected to pay the freight charges - Appeal dismissed.
