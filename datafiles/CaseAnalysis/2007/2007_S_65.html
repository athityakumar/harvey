Messrs Nopany Investments Private Limited v Santokh Singh (Huf)

Supreme Court of India

10 December 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Tarun Chatterjee, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1269; (2008) 2 SCC 728; AIR 2008 SC 673; 2008 (1) AWC 708; 2008 (146) DLT 217; JT 2007 (13) SC 448; 2008 (3) MLJ(SC) 543; 2008 (1) RCR(Civil) 270; 2008 (1) RCR(Rent) 6; 2007(14) SCALE 90; [2007] 13 S.C.R. 28

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure; Rent Control

Keywords: Foreign Country, Non- Compliance, Transfer of Property Act, 1982, Entitled To Act

Summary: Delhi Rent Control Act, 1958 - Whether Jasraj Singh could file the suit for eviction, in the capacity of the Karta of HUF, when, admittedly, an elder member of the HUF was alive?; Whether the High Court was right in concluding that the first appellate court had duly dealt with all the issues involved and re-appreciated evidence as provided under O.41 R.31 CPC?; Whether the contractual tenancy between the landlord and tenant came to an end merely by filing an Eviction Petition and whether the landlord could seek enhancement of rent simultaneously or post termination of tenancy?; Whether the landlord could issue a notice under s. 6A of the Delhi Rent Control Act, 1958 for increase of rent without seeking leave of the rent controller during the pendency of an order under s. 15 directing the tenant to deposit rent on a month to month basis? - Held, it is usually the Father of the family, if he is alive, and in his absence the senior member of the family, who is entitled to manage the joint family property - First appellate court had affirmed the findings of the trial court, which were based on total consideration of the material evidence documentary and oral on record - If the landlord wishes to increase the rent of any premises at any time, only a notice expressing his intention to make such increase is required to be given to the tenant and s. 6A clearly permits the landlord to increase the rent by 10% every three years - Appeal dismissed.
