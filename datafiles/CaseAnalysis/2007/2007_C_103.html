Commercial Taxation Officer, Udaipur v M/S Rajasthan Taxchem Ltd.

Supreme Court of India

12 January 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

AR. Lakshmanan, Altamas Kabir

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 24; (2007) 3 SCC 124; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 550; 2007 (209) E.L.T. 165; 2007(2) SCALE 120; [2007] 1 S.C.R. 855; 2008 (12) S.T.R. 660

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Keywords: Concessional Rate Of Tax, Inter-State Trade, Mistake Apparent, Processing, Change Of Opinion, Purchase Of Raw Material For Manufacture, Sales Tax Act, 1954

Summary: Rajasthan Sales Tax Act, 1994, s. 10(1) - Respondent claimed a benefit under claiming that diesel purchased is a raw material - Whether diesel can be called raw material in the manufacture of polyester yarn? - Held, definition of raw material under s. 2(34) specifically includes fuel required for the purpose of manufacture as raw material - Respondent purchased diesel as raw material pursuant to the specific entry in its registration certificate by making the payment of tax at concessional rate of 3% in accordance with the provisions of s. 10(1) - Respondent-assessee used diesel as raw material for the manufacture of the end product, namely, yarn and fabric - Diesel used by the assessee is a fuel and lubricant as defined under s. 2(34) - Appeal dismissed.
