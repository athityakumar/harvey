Vijay Singh Charak v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

26 February 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Markandey Katju, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1569; (2007) 9 SCC 743; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 548; 2007 (2) AWC 1920; 2007(3) SCALE 503; [2007] 3 S.C.R. 187; 2007 (4) SLR 227

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Clubbing, Indian Forest Service (Recruitment) Rules, 1965

Summary: Service - Administrative - Indian Forest Service (Recruitment) Rules, 1965 - Select list - Maintainability - Select list was prepared for induction of State Forest Service Officials into Indian Forest Service - Appellants name appeared in list - Select list was challenged by writ petition before DB which was disposed of - Appellant's name was removed after list was revised aggrieved by same writ petition was filed before Single judge which was dismissed - Aggrieved, by same appellant filed Letters patent appeal before DB which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order of lower Court was maintainable - Held, it was not an absolutely mandatory requirement of regulation that a Select List should be prepared every year and if list was not prepared every year, that by itself would not invalidate Select List for that year - Further, that there could not be clubbing of vacancies of several years and there could not be a common Select list for these years - Furthermore, there could not be clubbing of vacancies of several years and there could not be a common Select list for these years - Thus, that clubbing was illegal, - Since clubbing had been done for vacancies arising between 1991-95 in IFS was clearly illegal in view of decision in UOI - Appeal allowed.
