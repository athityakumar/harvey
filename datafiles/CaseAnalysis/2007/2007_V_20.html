K.S.R.T.C v Varghese

Supreme Court of India

09 July 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

P.K. Balasubramanyan, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1407; (2007) 8 SCC 231; (2007) 2 SCC (L&S) 862; JT 2007 (9) SC 82; 2007 (3) KLT 292; 2007 (4) LLN 145; 2007(9) SCALE 115; [2007] 8 S.C.R. 164; 2007 (4) SLR 721

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Service

Keywords: Dearness Relief, Revision Of Pension

Summary: Service - Administrative - Road Transport Corporations Act, 1950, ss. 4, 14(2), 34 & 34(1) - Pensionary benefits - Non- implementation - Justifiability - Employees of appellant-corporation filed writ petition challenging non-implementation of recommendations regarding revision of pensionary benefits - HC allowed petition and directed appellant-corporation to pay arrears - Appellant filed appeal before DB, which was dismissed - Appellant challenged order before SC - SC set aside judgment of HC and directed to re-examine question - DB held that appellant was bound to implement recommendations of V pay commission and to grant revised pensionary benefits to all its employees - Thus, appeal filed by employees were allowed and petitions filed by writ petitioners were dismissed - Hence, instant appeals - Appellant contended that Part III of KSR had only been adopted for KSRTC or by KSRTC and it was neither a case of legislation by incorporation nor a case of legislation by reference - Thus HC was wrong in holding that the adoption of Part III of KSR in KSRTC was a legislation by reference - Whether order of HC was justifiable - Held, appellant was autonomous corporation, which could regulate service of its employees by making appropriate regulations in that behalf - Until such regulations were framed it was entitled to take note of its financial health in considering whether particular recommendation should be adopted by it and if it was to adopted by it, from what point of time, which could be subject to any direction that might be issued by State Govt. in terms of s. 34 of the Act - In instant case, it was found from relevant aspects brought out that financial position of appellant-corporation was not sound and decision taken by State Govt. not to implement here and now were fully justified - Thus, order of HC was set aside - Appeals allowed.
