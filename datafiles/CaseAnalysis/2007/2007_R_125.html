Pratibha v Rameshwari Devi And Ors

Supreme Court of India

17 September 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Tarun Chatterjee, A.K. Mathur

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 911; (2007) 12 SCC 369; (2008) 1 SCC (Cr) 399; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 899; 2007 (3) ALT 323; 2008 CRLJ 329; 2007 (4) JCC 2769; JT 2007 (11) SC 122; 2007 (4) RCR(Criminal) 367; 2007(11) SCALE 174; [2007] 9 S.C.R. 1038

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Family & Personal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Indian Penal Code, ss. 498-A, 406; Criminal Procedure Code, s. 482 - High Court had quashed an FIR - Appeal against - Whether High Court while quashing the FIR in the exercise of its inherent powers under s. 482 of the Code was entitled to go beyond the complaint filed by the complainant; whether the High Court was entitled to look into and consider the investigation report submitted by four officers of the rank of Dy. Superintendent of Police for quashing the FIR even before the same could be filed before the concerned Magistrate? - Held, filing of a divorce petition in a Civil Court cannot be a ground to quash criminal proceedings under s. 482 - Criminal and civil proceedings are separate and independent and the pendency of a civil proceeding cannot bring to an end a criminal proceeding even if they arise out of the same set of facts - High Court while exercising its powers under s. 482 Code has gone beyond the allegations made in the FIR and has acted in excess of its jurisdiction and, therefore, the High Court was not justified in quashing the FIR - High Court has erred in quashing the FIR on consideration of the investigation report submitted before it even before the same could be submitted before the Magistrate - Appeal partly allowed.
