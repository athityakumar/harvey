Centre For Environment Law WWF-I v Union of India and others

Supreme Court of India

30 November 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, K.G. Balakrishnan

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1768; (2013) 8 SCC 266

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Summary: Environment, Wildlife & Animal - Forest Conservation Act, 1980 - Violation of forest conservation - Rehabilitation - Proposal for translocation - Whether Interlocutory Applications for violation of 1980 Act, rehabilitation of 1390 families, Hydro Electric Power Projects and proposal for translocation are allowable.

Held, regard to violation of 1980 Act, State Govt. to initiate action against concerned officials, for violation of 1980 Act, by raising height of existing dam in ‘S’ WLS, without approval of Central Govt. State was directed to file its response. So far, no response has been filed. State is granted four weeks' time to file response. Issue relating to rehabilitation of 1390 families, occupying land within ‘R’ Part, was referred to National Board for Wild Life. CWW was requested to file detailed report before Standing Committee, NBWL and Committee was thereafter required to give its recommendations. No Report has been so far received from NBWL Standing Committee. NBWL is asked to file its Report by February, 2007. NBWL Standing Committee shall also look in to this matter and file Report in that behalf. With regard to two Hydro Electric Power Projects of National Hydro Electric Power Corporation are coming up in State, National Board for Wild Life has already approved project subject to fulfillment of certain conditions. One of conditions was additional land for extending Sanctuary area which is lost by implementation of project work. State has not yet granted additional land nor filed its response. State to file Report within three weeks. There was proposal for translocation of some of Asiatic Lions found in ‘G’ National Park to forest State of ‘G’ and ‘m’. State of ‘G’ has raised certain objections. State of ‘M’ wants to file its response. It is permitted to do so within three weeks. Proposal is directed to be submitted to NBWL which may consider objections of State of ‘G’ and response of ‘M’ and submit its recommendation in this Court in four months. Order accordingly.
