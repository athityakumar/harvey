Shiv Dass v Union Of India And Ors

Supreme Court of India

18 January 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 39; (2007) 9 SCC 274; (2007) 2 SCC (L&S) 395; AIR 2007 SC 1330; 2007(3) ALL MR 820; 2007 (5) AWC 4467; 2007 (1) ESC 57; 2007 (112) FLR 877; 2007 (2) JhrCR(SC) 123; 2007 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 556; 2007 (2) LLJ 212; 2007(2) SCALE 325; [2007] 1 S.C.R. 1127; 2007 (3) SLR 444

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Defence & Security Forces; Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Disability Pension

Summary: Service - Prayer for grant of disability pension dismissed on the ground that it was highly belated - Appeal against - Held, in the case of pension the cause of action actually continues from month to month - That, however, cannot be a ground to overlook delay in filing the petition - It would depend upon the fact of each case - Remitted the matter to the High Court to hear the writ petition on merits - Appeal disposed of.
