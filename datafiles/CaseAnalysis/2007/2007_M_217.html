Mayakaur Baldev Singh Sardar and Another v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

08 October 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1431; (2007) 12 SCC 654; (2008) 3 SCC (Cr) 343; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 1437; 2007 (4) RCR(Criminal) 539; 2007(11) SCALE 714; 2007 (8) SCJ 734; [2007] 10 S.C.R. 752

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Defence & Security Forces; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Murder Reference, Mitigating Circumstance, Anti-Social, Sufficient In Ordinary Course Of Nature, Third Judge

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 120-B, 302 and 307 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872 - Arms Act, 1959, ss. 25(1), (3) and 27(3) - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 366 - Murder - Appellants charged with ss. 34, 120-B, 302 and 307 - Arms Act, 1959, 25(1), (3) and 27(3) - Out of eight accused, four accused was awarded death penalty and two accused with life imprisonment and two more accused to lesser offences - High Court on reference converted death penalty into life imprisonment - Whether HC right in converting death penalty into life imprisonment? - Extreme penalty could be inflicted only in cases of gravest and extreme culpability - Mitigating circumstances in favour of criminal so as to avoid death penalty had also to be taken into account and balance-sheet of aggravating and mitigating circumstances was to be prepared as prelude to award of sentence - Held, having regard to diabolical nature of crime and murder of helpless individuals committed with traditional weapons with extreme cruelty and pre-meditation, reverse life sentences awarded by HC and to re-impose death penalty on accused - Appeals dismissed.
