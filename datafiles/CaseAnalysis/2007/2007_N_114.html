K. Srikanth Singh v North East Securities Limited and Another

Supreme Court of India

20 July 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1383; (2007) 12 SCC 788; (2008) 3 SCC (Cr) 391; 2008 (1) BC 244; [2007] 140 Comp Cas 444; 2007 (3) CompLJ 422; 2007 (4) CTC 765; 2007 (3) JCC (NI) 268; JT 2007 (9) SC 449; 2007 (3) RCR(Criminal) 934; 2007(9) SCALE 371; [2007] 8 S.C.R. 452

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Corporate; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Dishonor of Cheque - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, ss. 138 and 141 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, s. 482 - Complainant alleged that appellant was part of negotiations on behalf of company for obtaining financial assistance - Appellant has sought quashing of proceedings on ground that at relevant point of time he was not Director of Company - Whether proceedings against appellant are liable to be quashed - Held, yes - Complainant has failed to prove that appellant was responsible for conduct of business of Company - No inference can be drawn that Appellant was responsible for day-to-day affairs of Company - Vicarious liability on part of a person must be pleaded and proved and not inferred - Proceedings against appellant quashed - Appeal allowed.
