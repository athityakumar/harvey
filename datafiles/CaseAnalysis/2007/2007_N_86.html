Subhash Mahadevasa Habib v Nemasa Ambasa Dharmadas (D) By Lrs. and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 March 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

P.K. Balasubramanyan, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 218; (2007) 13 SCC 650; AIR 2007 SC 1828; 2007(4) ALL MR 324; 2007 (7) AWC 6889; 2007 (4) CTC 232; JT 2007 (5) SC 12; 2007 (6) MLJ(SC) 992; 2007 (2) RCR(Civil) 662; 2007(5) SCALE 34; 2007 (4) SCJ 766; [2007] 4 S.C.R. 150

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Family & Personal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Benefit Of The Estate

Summary: Suits filed for redemption of property - Alienation effected by Karta of the joint family challenged in one suit and in the other validity of sale deed challenged by the Karta - Question of res judicata - Decree passed in favour of alienation - Held, the decree therein could operate as res-judicata as between them if the conditions therefore are satisfied- there must be a conflict of interest between the defendants concerned, it must be necessary to decide the conflict in order to give the plaintiff the relief he claims, ) the co-defendants must be necessary or proper parties to the suit and the question between the defendants must have been finally decided inter se between them - Composite final decree for redemption - Appeal allowed
