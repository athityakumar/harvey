M/S Mil India Limited v Commissioner Of Central Excise, Noida

Supreme Court of India

01 March 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, B. SUDARSHAN REDDY

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 487; (2007) 3 SCC 533; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 464; 2007 (210) E.L.T. 188; 2007 (2) RCR(Civil) 771; 2007(4) SCALE 31; 2007 (5) SCJ 807; [2007] 3 S.C.R. 476

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax; Trade

Summary: Excise - Section 35B, Income Tax Act, 1961 - Appeal - Right to dispute - Quantum Dispute - Appellants had entered into a composite contract with various companies for the supply of various items which formed part of the fatty acid plant - Under the contract, in addition to the supply of the equipments manufactured by the appellants, various duty paid bought out items were directly supplied by the appellants to the site of the company and these items were never received in the factory premises of the appellants, which were to be erected and commissioned not by the appellant - Show cause notice issued by the department to the appellants demanding duty on the various bought out items - Adjudicating authority confirmed the demand on the ground that the same was necessary for the manufacture of the fatty acid plant at the site of the company - On appeal, the Commissioner held that the value of the bought out items was includible in the assessable value of the equipments manufactured and remanded the matter for quantification of the duty liability on the ground that the demand stood restricted for 6 months - In the quantum dispute, the adjudicating authority confirmed once again the duty demand - On further appeal, the Commissioner confirmed the duty demanded on the ground that the appellant had failed to produce evidence to disprove the quantification made by the department - On appeal, the Tribunal held that no duty was payable on the bought out items, and further held that the appellants were entitled to raise the issue of dutiability of bought out items even though in the earlier round of litigation the appellants did not challenge the order of the Commissioner - On appeal by the Deratment, the High Court held that the order of the Commissioner had become final on merits since that order was never challenged by the assessee - Whether the CESTAT was right in entertaining the assessee's appeal on dutiability against the order passed by Commissioner in the Quantum Dispute, particularly when in the earlier round of litigation, the Commissioner had concluded that the bought out items were dutiable and which order had become final - Held, Tribunal being the highest authority in hierarchy to decide on facts, was not bound by the decision of the Commissioner - Section 35B indicates that the decision of order passed by the Commissioner shall be treated as an order of an adjudicating authority - Demand reduced - Impugned order set aside.
