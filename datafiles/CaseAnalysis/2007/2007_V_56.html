Vinitec Electronics Private Limited v HCL Infosystems Limited

Supreme Court of India

02 November 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

B. Sudershan Reddy, Altamas Kabir

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1093; (2008) 1 SCC 544; 2008 (1) AWC 476B; 2008 (1) BC 170; 2007 (6) CompLJ 51; JT 2007 (12) SC 480; 2008 (149) PLR 335; 2007 (8) SCJ 331; [2007] 11 S.C.R. 897

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Letters Of Credit

Summary: Dispute relates to invocation of the bank guarantee furnished by the appellant to the respondent - Whether bank guarantee furnished is an unconditional and irrevocable one or a conditional one? - Held, bank guarantees which provided that they are payable by the guarantor on demand is considered to be an un- conditional bank guarantee - When in the course of commercial dealings, unconditional guarantees have been given or accepted the beneficiary is entitled to realize such a bank guarantee in terms thereof irrespective of any pending disputes - After careful analysis of the terms of the guarantee find the guarantee to be an unconditional one - Appeal dismissed.
