Promoters and Builders Association of Pune v Pune Municipal Corporation and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 May 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

G.P. Mathur, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 598; (2007) 6 SCC 143; AIR 2007 SC 1956; JT 2007 (7) SC 564; 2007(7) SCALE 218; 2007 (7) SCJ 328; [2007] 6 S.C.R. 459

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure; Trusts & Associations

Keywords: Oil Mills, Maharashtra Regional Town Planning Act, 1966, Bombay Provincial and Municipal Corporation Act, 1949

Summary: Maharashtra Regional Town Planning Act, 1966 - Dispute pertains to Development Control Rules for Pune which has been constituted as a corporation under the Bombay Provincial and Municipal Corporation Act, 1949 - Municipal Corporation passed a Resolution not to allow use of additional 0.4 FSI in the area other then the plot from which the land for road widening has been acquired which was in tune with clause (b) of D.C.R.-2.4.11 - State Government added the words "from the very said plot" towards the end of clause (b) of Development Control Rule N.2.4.11 - Held, review petitions are dismissed, recording the submission on behalf of the Pune Municipal Corporation that the constructions mentioned in categories 1 to 4 above will not be treated to be in violation of clause (b) of D.C.R.-2.4.11 - Petitions dismissed.
