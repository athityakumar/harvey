Mehar Chand Das v Lal Babu Siddique and Others

Supreme Court of India

17 January 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1227; (2007) 14 SCC 253; AIR 2007 SC 1499; 2007 (2) AWC 1774; 2007 (3) JhrCR(SC) 21; 2007 (3) MLJ(SC) 951; 2007 (2) RCR(Civil) 628; 2007(2) SCALE 539; 2007 (6) SCJ 79; [2007] 1 S.C.R. 1108

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Trusts & Associations

Keywords: Consent, Express Or Implied, Tenure-Holder, Bihar Privileged Persons Homestead Tenancy Act, 1947, Bihar Buildings (Lease, Rent & Eviction) Control Act, 1947, Santhal Parganas Tenancy (Supplementary Provisions) Act, 1949, Chotanagpur Tenancy Act, 1908

Summary: Land & Property - Bihar Privileged Persons Homestead Tenancy Act, 1947 - Bihar Buildings (Lease, Rent and Eviction) Control Act, 1947, s. 6 - Specific Relief Act, 1963, s. 34 - Santhal Parganas Tenancy (Supplementary Provisions) Act, 1949 - Chotanagpur Tenancy Act, 1908 - Appellate herein was a tenant under respondents - A Parcha was purported to have been granted on or about 29-09-1969 by Collector in terms of provisions of s. 6 of Bihar Privileged Persons Homestead Tenancy Act, 1947 - Father of respondents filed a suit for eviction on premise that said Parcha u/s. 6(2) of Act was issued without jurisdiction and same had been fraudulently obtained - On a finding that said Parcha was obtained upon committing fraud, suit was decreed - (A) Whether suit was barred u/s. 34 of Specific Relief Act, 1963, in view of decision of Supreme Court in Vinay Krishna's case? - Held, appellant was treated to be a tenant by respondents - Defendant-appellant therefore had been in possession of suit property - In that view of matter plaintiffs-respondents could seek for further relief other than for a decree of mere declaration of title - High Court committed a manifest error in not relying upon decision of this Court in Vinay Krishna - Said decision categorically lays down law that if plaintiff had been in possession, then a suit for mere declaration would be maintainable, logical corollary whereof would be that if plaintiff is not in possession, a suit for mere declaration would not be maintainable - In terms of s. 17A thereof, privileged tenant not having permanent tenancy in homestead is to hold same under State Govt. and amount of rent payable to landlord by privileged tenant in respect thereof would become payable to State Govt. - Once defendant-appellant claimed title as a privileged tenant in terms of provisions of said Act, relationship of landlord and tenant came to an end - (B) Whether instant suit was barred for non-joinder of Collector, who was necessary part to suit? - Further held, apart from fact that in such a suit plaintiff was bound to establish fraud or want of jurisdiction on part of Collector, to grant a Parcha in favour of tenant, Collector was a necessary party, in absence of Collector, therefore suit could not have been decreed - Appeal allowed.
