Raj Kumar And Others v Haryana State And Others

Supreme Court of India

27 August 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

P.K. Balasubramanyan, G.P. Mathur

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 852; (2007) 7 SCC 609; AIR 2007 SC 3124; 2007 (6) ALD(SC) 5; 2007 (69) ALR 508; 2007 (4) AWC 3562; 2007 (4) JhrCR(SC) 72; JT 2007 (10) SC 454; 2007 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 2652; 2007 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 2696; 2007 (4) RCR(Civil) 172; 2008 (104) RD 111; 2007(10) SCALE 375; 2007 (8) SCJ 272; [2007] 9 S.C.R. 455; 2008 (1) UPLBEC 165

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - Appeal by the claimants against compensation - Held, all the relevant aspects have been taken into consideration and not find any error in principle committed by the High Court justifying our interference in appeal - It cannot be said that the High Court has committed any error in discarding those auction sales while determining the compensation payable - Argument that when a compact piece of land is acquired there cannot be adoption of separate rates cannot be accepted - Sufficient grounds not made out for interference - Appeals dismissed.
