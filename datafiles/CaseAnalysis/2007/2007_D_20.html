Sarvesh Narain Shukla v Daroga Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 October 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

H.S. Bedi, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1476; (2007) 13 SCC 360; AIR 2008 SC 320; 2008 ALL MR (Cri) 267; 2007(12) SCALE 290; [2007] 11 S.C.R. 300

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Chance Witness, Fire Arms, Close Range

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 147, 148, 302 and 149 - Culpable homicide amounting to murder - Conviction and punishment - Whether justified? - Accused nine in numbers - Trial Court convicted all accused persons u/s. 302 and sentenced them to life imprisonment - With modern sophisticated weapons like carbines, pistols and shotguns murders committed, shots being shot at car from behind wherein deceased persons were traveling - Delay in sending special report to Magistrate without explanation - (A) Whether made FIR doubtful? - Facts analyzed - Held, suspicion that a line or two might have squeezed in here or there in some of documents prepared during initial investigation would not dislodge huge volume of documentary and ocular evidence on spontaneity of FIR - (1) Credibility of chance witnesses - Held, if court comes to conclusion that testimony of a chance witness is credible, evidence cannot be thrown out merely on ground that witness happened to be present by chance - (2) Hostile witness - Whether testimony thereof to be outrightly rejected? - Held, no, both parties are entitled to rely on such part of his evidence which assists their case - Conviction and sentence justified - Impugned judgment not liable to be interfered - Appeals dismissed.
