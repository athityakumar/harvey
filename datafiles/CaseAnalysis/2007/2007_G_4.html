Government of Andhra Pradesh & Another v G. Jaya Prasad Rao & Others

Supreme Court of India

21 March 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Mathur, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 280; (2007) 11 SCC 528; AIR 2007 SC 1503; 2007 (3) ALD(SC) 42; JT 2007 (4) SC 636; 2007 (4) MLJ(SC) 1039; 2007(4) SCALE 630; 2007 (6) SCJ 37; [2007] 4 S.C.R. 256

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Defence & Security Forces; Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Eligibility Conditions, Eligibility - Qualifications, Armed Forces, Deputy Inspector General, Inspector General, Superior Officer, Deputy Commissioner Of Police, Procedure - Examination, Appointment - Discrimination, Reservation - Promotion, Service Rules - Amendment, Seniority - Classification, Ex-servicemen, Promotion - Accelerated promotion, Police - Police Force, Additional Director General, Punishments, Police Act, Andhra Pradesh Police (Civil) Service Rules, 1998

Summary: Andhra Pradesh Police (Civil) Service Rules, 1998 - Insertion of Note-2 of r. 3 and proviso to r. 6 - Accelerated promotion for the outstanding work in the field of anti extremist operation - Declared Note 2 to r. 3 of the Rules and Proviso to r. 6 being violative of art. 14 & 16 of the Constitution and struck it down - Appeal against - Whether amendment of the rules and insertion of Note 2 in Rule 3 as well as proviso to Rule 6, could be declared ultra vires of Articles 14 & 16 of the Constitution of India, 1950? - Held, guidelines have been made in order to check that there should not be any arbitrary promotion and there should not be any picking and choosing among the persons belonging to the same category - There is least possibility of picking and choosing of the persons under accelerated promotion scheme - Simply because a particular provision is capable of being abused is no ground to strike down the whole system - There is no question of setting aside the promotions which have already been made - Appeals allowed.
