Babulal Jain v State of Madhya Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

24 April 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 408; (2007) 6 SCC 180; (2007) 2 SCC (L&S) 422; JT 2007 (6) SC 59; 2007(6) SCALE 70; [2007] 5 S.C.R. 514; 2007 (3) SLJ 820; 2007 (4) SLR 123

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Constitution; Service

Summary: Appeal against the judgment and Order passed by the Madhya Pradesh High Court with regard to interpretation of FR 22(D) of the M.P. Civil Services (CCA) Rule - Refixation of salary - Recovery of extra amount paid in lieu of salary to appellant - Held, the controlling authority may direct deputation of an employee from one post to the other, it is beyond any doubt that for the purpose of grant of promotion, it was obligatory on the part of the Collector to follow the statutory rules operating in the field, FR 22(D) is not applicable to a case of deputation, it certainly would not apply where a purported order of promotion has been effected from one cadre to the other and that too without following the statutory rules, no error in the judgment of the High Court in this behalf - Direction for recovery not to be made - Appeal partly allowed.
