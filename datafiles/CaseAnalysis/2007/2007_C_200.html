Messrs Ambica Industries v Commissioner of Central Excise

Supreme Court of India

18 May 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 597; (2007) 6 SCC 769; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 1618; 2007 (213) E.L.T. 323; JT 2007 (8) SC 467; 2007(8) SCALE 488; [2007] 7 S.C.R. 685; [2009] 20 VST 1

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Central Excise Act - Issue relates to determination of situs of the High Court in which appeals would lie under s. 35G(1) - Held, keeping in view the expression "cause of action" used in clause (2) of art. 226 of the Constitution of India, indisputably even if a small fraction thereof accrues within the jurisdiction of the Court, the Court will have jurisdiction in the matter though the doctrine of forum convenience may also have to be considered - High Court was correct in its view - Appeals dismissed.
