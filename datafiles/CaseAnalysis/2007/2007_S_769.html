Sukhram v State Of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

17 August 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

D.K. Jain, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 818; (2007) 3 SCC (Cr) 426; (2007) 7 SCC 502; AIR 2007 SC 3050; 2007 CRLJ 4327; JT 2007 (10) SC 177; 2007 (4) RCR(Criminal) 45; 2007(10) SCALE 60; [2007] 9 S.C.R. 44

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Ill Treatment, Supreme Court (Enlargement of Criminal Appellate Jurisdiction) Act, 1970

Summary: Indian Penal Code, ss. 302, 201 r/w 34; Supreme Court (Enlargement of Criminal Appellate Jurisdiction) Act, 1970 - Appeal against conviction and sentence - Held, though charge for offence punishable under s. 302 had been framed against appellant A-1, no such charge was framed against appellant A-2, even with the aid of s. 34 - There is no eye witness and the prosecution had sought to establish the case against the appellants from circumstantial evidence - Prosecution has failed to establish that the conduct of appellant A-2, both at the time of the occurrence and immediately thereafter, is consistent with the hypothesis of his guilt - Appeal partly allowed.
