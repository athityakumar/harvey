Kerala State Electricity Board v Livisha

Supreme Court of India

18 May 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 589; (2007) 6 SCC 792; 2007 (1) CCC 357; 2007 (3) RCR(Civil) 376; 2007(8) SCALE 235; 2007 (5) SCJ 392; [2007] 7 S.C.R. 356

Case Digest

Subject: Electricity & Energy; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure; Telecom

Keywords: Public Officer, Transmission Of Energy, Diminution

Summary: Indian Telegraph Act, 1885, s. 10 - What would be the amount of compensation for the trees cut and removed by the Kerala State Electricity Board, a body corporate, constituted and incorporated under Electricity (Supply) Act, 1948? - Held, situs of the land, distance between the high voltage electricity line laid thereover, the extent of the line thereon as also the fact as to whether the high voltage line passes over a small track of land or through the middle of the land and other similar relevant factors would be determinative - Value of the land would also be a relevant factor - High Court should consider the matter afresh on the merit of each matter having regard to the fact situation obtaining therein - Matters are remitted to the High Court - Appeals allowed.
