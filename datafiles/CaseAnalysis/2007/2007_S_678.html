Devi Lal v State of Rajasthan

Supreme Court of India

12 October 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1049; (2007) 14 SCC 176; AIR 2008 SC 332; 2007 ALL MR (Cri) 3570; 2007 (3) ALT 366; JT 2007 (12) SC 114; 2007 (4) RCR(Criminal) 750; 2007(12) SCALE 265; 2007 (8) SCJ 348; [2007] 11 S.C.R. 219

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Indian Penal Code, ss. 304B, 306 r/w 498A - Appeal against conviction and sentence - Whether a case had been made out for application of s. 113B of the Indian Evidence Act - Held, two courts have concurrently accepted the evidence of the prosecution witnesses - Testimonies of the prosecution witnesses have been relied on for arriving at the finding of guilt of the appellant - Do not see any reason to take a different view - Before an accused is found guilty for commission of an offence, the Court must arrive at a finding that the ingredients thereof have been established - Evidence brought on record by the prosecution clearly suggest that accused had all along been subjected to harassment or cruelty only on the ground that her father had not given enough dowry at the time of marriage - It was not necessary that demand of any particular item should have been made - Appeal dismissed.
