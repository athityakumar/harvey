Jagdish Chander v Ramesh Chander and Others

Supreme Court of India

26 April 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1570; (2007) 5 SCC 719; 2007 (3) AWC 2402; 2007 (3) CompLJ 191; JT 2007 (6) SC 375; 2007 (6) MLJ(SC) 317; 2007 (147) PLR 18; 2007 (2) RCR(Civil) 860; 2007(6) SCALE 325; [2007] 5 S.C.R. 720

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Practice & Procedure - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, ss.7, 7(1)11, 11(5), 11(6) - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 89 - Partnership deed - Appointment of Arbitrator - Maintainability - Appellant and respondent no.1 entered into partnership to carry business - Respondent no.1 filed application for appointment of Arbitrator before Chief Justice of HC Delhi to decide dispute which was allowed and a retired Judge was appointed as Arbitrator - Appellant challenged said order of appointment - Hence, instant appeal - Whether Partnership deed could be considered as Arbitration agreement by lower Court was maintainable - Held, existence of an arbitration agreement as defined u/s. 7 of the Act was condition precedent for exercise of power to appoint an Arbitrator/Arbitral Tribunal, u/s. 11 of the Act by Chief Justice or his Designate - Therefore, it was not permissible to appoint an Arbitrator to adjudicate disputes between parties when there was no arbitration agreement or mutual consent - Thus, designate of CJ of Delhi could not have appointed Arbitrator in absence of an arbitration agreement - Appeal allowed.
