Nahar Enterprises v Hyderabad Allwyn Limited and Another

Supreme Court of India

09 February 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 654; (2007) 9 SCC 466; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 1021; 2008 (1) ALD(SC) 47; 2007 (67) ALR 462; 2007 (2) AWC 1901; 2007 (3) JhrCR(SC) 16; JT 2007 (4) SC 218; 2007 (3) MLJ(SC) 253; 2007 (2) RCR(Civil) 413; 2007 (102) RD 784; 2007(3) SCALE 461; [2007] 2 S.C.R. 413

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Ex-Parte, Copy Of Plaint

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 5, r. 2, O. 9, rr. 2, 6(1)(c) and 13 - Limitation Act, 1963, art. 123 - Application for setting aside the ex parte decree - Denial of - Validity - Whether denying setting aside ex parte decree in the instant case is invalid? - Held, non-service of copy of plaint along with summons in disregard of requirement u/O. 5 r. 2 - When summons is sent calling upon a defendant to appear in Court and file written statement, it is obligatory for Court to send a copy of plaint and other documents appended - Summons having been served upon appellant after date fixed for his appearance, it was obligatory to fix another date for his appearance and filing written statement and direct plaintiff to take steps for service of fresh summons - Court committed an illegality in dismissing application for setting aside ex parte - Summons not having been duly served upon appellant, second part of art. 123 whereby, an applicant would be deemed to have knowledge of passing of said ex parte decree would be date from which limitation will begin to run, would be attracted - Ex parte decree set aside - While exercising its discretionary jurisdiction u/O. 9, r. 13, Court can put applicant to terms which should not be unreasonable - Once unreasonable or harsh conditions are imposed, Appellate Court would have power to interfere therewith - Appeal allowed.
