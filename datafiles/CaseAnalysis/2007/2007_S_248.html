Saroj Kumar Poddar v State (Nct Of Delhi) & Anr

Supreme Court of India

16 January 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 33; (2007) 2 SCC (Cr) 135; (2007) 3 SCC 693; AIR 2007 SC 912; 2007 (3) ALT 150; 2007 (2) BC 218; 2007 (2) Bom.C.R. 764; [2007] 137 Comp Cas 837; 2007 (1) CompLJ 302; 2007 CRLJ 1419; 2007 (1) CTC 529; 2007 (1) JCC (NI) 60; JT 2007 (2) SC 233; 2007 (4) MahLJ 421; 2007 (3) MPLJ 9; 2007 (1) RCR(Criminal) 741; 2007(2) SCALE 36; [2007] 1 S.C.R. 907

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Corporate; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Cheques, Offences By Companies, Post Dated Cheque

Summary: Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, s. 138 - Appellant was a Director of a public limited company - Company issued three cheques which were dishonoured - Complaint against appellant - Plea that appellant having resigned from the Directorship of the said Company, the complaint petitions as against him were not maintainable - Held, there is no averment in the complaint petitions as to how and in what manner the appellant was responsible for the conduct of the business of the Company or otherwise responsible to it in regard to its functioning - Allegations made do not satisfy the requirements of s. 141 - Complaint do not disclose any offence as against the appellant - Appeal allowed.
