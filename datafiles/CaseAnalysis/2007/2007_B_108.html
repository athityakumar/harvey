Jagraj Singh v Birpal Kaur

Supreme Court of India

13 February 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

C.K. Thakker, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 105; (2007) 2 SCC 564; AIR 2007 SC 2083; 2007 (67) ALR 475; 2007 (3) ALT 86; 2007 (2) AWC 1608; 2007 (1) JCC 820; 2007 (3) JhrCR(SC) 134; JT 2007 (3) SC 389; 2007 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 801; 2007 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 865; 2007 (3) MLJ(SC) 370; 2007 (2) RCR(Civil) 108; 2007(3) SCALE 150; 2007 (4) SCJ 102; [2007] 2 S.C.R. 496; 2008 (Supp) Bom.C.R. 61; 2007 (2) UC 1103

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Family & Personal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Personal Presence, Appearance Of Parties, Non-Appearance Of Parties, Personal Appearance

Summary: Hindu Marriage Act, 1955 - Respondent - wife filed a petition for divorce on the ground of desertion and cruelty - In order to bring out reconciliation between the parties, the High Court directed both the parties to remain present in person - Non bailable warrants issued to the respondent-husband - Appeal against - Held, it cannot successfully be contended that the Court has no such power and in case a party to a proceeding does not remain present, at the most, the Court can proceed to decide the case ex parte against him/her - Once the Court holds that it has no jurisdiction in the matter, it should not consider the merits of the matter - Appeal dismissed.
