Chanda (Dead) Through Lrs v Rattni and Another

Supreme Court of India

23 March 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1004; (2007) 14 SCC 26; AIR 2007 SC 1514; 2007 (67) ALR 798; 2007 (2) AWC 1903; 2007 (3) CTC 773; JT 2007 (5) SC 28; 2007 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1081; 2007 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1151; 2007 (3) MLJ(SC) 799; 2007 (147) PLR 307; 2007 (2) RCR(Civil) 534; 2007(5) SCALE 53; 2007 (4) SCJ 142; [2007] 4 S.C.R. 402; 2007 (2) UC 1229

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure; Trusts & Associations

Keywords: Restoration Of Possession

Summary: Land & Property - Specific Relief Act, 1963, s. 28 - Rescission of contracts - For sale or lease of immovable property - Specific performance has been decreed for non-payment of purchase money - High Court dismissed appellant's revision petition - Whether such dismissal is valid? - Held, decree for specific performance has been described as a preliminary decree - Power u/s. 28 is discretionary and court cannot ordinarily annul decree once passed by it - Although power to annul decree exists yet s. 28 provides for complete relief to both parties in terms of decree - Court does not cease to have power to extend time even though Trial Court had earlier directed in decree that payment of balance price to be made by certain date and on failure suit to stand dismissed - It was implicit in courts direction that appellant-plaintiff was required to deposit balance consideration within a period in first instance and it was only then defendants were required to execute sale deed - Since plaintiff did not deposit balance amount, order of Court under which contract was rescinded was perfectly in order - Revision petition was also rightly dismissed - Appeal dismissed.
