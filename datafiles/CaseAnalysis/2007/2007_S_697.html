State of Uttar Pradesh and Others v R.C. Misra

Supreme Court of India

22 March 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

G.P. Mathur, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1488; (2007) 9 SCC 698; (2007) 2 SCC (L&S) 1005; 2007 (5) AWC 5321; JT 2007 (4) SC 558; 2007 (2) LLJ 716; 2007(4) SCALE 595; 2007 (7) SCJ 44; [2007] 4 S.C.R. 360; 2007 (5) SLR 767; 2007 (2) UPLBEC 1329

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Uttar Pradesh Disciplinary Proceedings (Administrative Tribunal) Rules, 1947

Summary: Service - Civil Service Regulations Uttar Pradesh Disciplinary Proceedings (Administrative Tribunal) Rules, 1947, regns. 351A and 470 - Respondent a Block Development Officer was placed under suspension - Charge sheet containing 12 charges was served upon him - Soon after he attained age of superannuation and retired from service - Charges levelled were established and an order was passed by State Govt. directing recovery of Rs. 9,69,141.60 from pension/gratuity of respondent - Whether specific order for its continuance should had been taken from competent authority? - Held, respondent had been placed under suspension and charges were also served upon him while he was in service - In such circumstances, proviso (a) to regn. 351A did not come into play at all and there was no requirement of obtaining sanction of Governor - Enquiry which had been instituted prior to retirement of respondent and was completed after his retirement could not, therefore, be held to be illegal on ground of want of sanction of Governor - Proviso appended to regn. 351A merely controls main proceedings and same would apply in exigencies of situation envisaged therein, namely, when proceedings were initiated after retirement and not prior thereto - Appeal allowed.
