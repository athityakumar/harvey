Messrs Gupta Modern Breweries v State of Jammu and Kashmir and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 April 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

H.K. Sema, V.S. Sirpurkar

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 392; (2007) 6 SCC 317; JT 2007 (5) SC 619; 2007(5) SCALE 842; 2007 (6) SCJ 137; 2007 (7) SCJ 338; [2007] 5 S.C.R. 343

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Parliament & Legislature

Keywords: A.P. Excise Act, 1968

Summary: Jammu and Kashmir Excise Act, 1901 - Jammu and Kashmir Distillery Rules 1946 - Whether Rule 17 of the Rules ultra vires the Act? - Held, Rule 17 has no statutory backing and it is in excess of the Act, is manifestly unjust and arbitrary, Provision of Rule 17 is clearly a tax and not a fee and imposition of tax or fee on the citizens for the services that the State renders to itself and not the tax payers is clearly impermissible, arbitrary and unjustifiable - Appeals allowed.
