Dilawar Singh v State Of Delhi

Supreme Court of India

05 September 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 877; (2007) 12 SCC 641; (2008) 3 SCC (Cr) 330; AIR 2007 SC 3234; 2007 (3) ALT 385; 2008 (2) CGLJ 272; 2007 CRLJ 4709; JT 2007 (10) SC 585; 2007 (4) RCR(Criminal) 115; 2007(10) SCALE 556; 2007 (8) SCJ 37; [2007] 9 S.C.R. 695

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 452, 392 and 397 - Appeal before the High Court was dismissed by the impugned judgment on the ground that PW1's evidence was clear and cogent - Appeal against conviction and sentence - Held, no injury has been inflicted - Use of weapon by offender for creating terror in mind of victim is sufficient - It need not be further shown to have been actually used for cutting, stabbing or shooting, as the case may be - Offence under s. 397 has clearly not been established - Ingredients necessary for offence punishable under ss. 392, 452 have not been established in view of the highly inconsistent version of the complainant PW 1 - Appeal allowed.
