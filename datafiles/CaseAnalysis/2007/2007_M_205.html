Rishi Kumar Govil v Maqsoodan and Others

Supreme Court of India

28 March 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1485; (2007) 4 SCC 465; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 74; 2007 (67) ALR 774; 2007 (2) AWC 1914; JT 2007 (10) SC 99; 2007(5) SCALE 135; 2007 (6) SCJ 902; [2007] 4 S.C.R. 483

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Rent Control

Keywords: Release Application, Uttar Pradesh Urban Building (Regulation of Letting, Rent and Eviction) Rules, 1972

Summary: Rent Control - Practice & Procedure - Uttar Pradesh Urban Buildings (Regulation of Letting, Rent and Eviction) Act, 1972, s. 21(1)(a) - Uttar Pradesh Urban Building (Regulation of Letting, Rent and Eviction) Rules, 1972, r. 16 - Disputed Property - Eviction of Tenant - Justifiability - Respondent no.1 filed application u/s. 21(1)(a) of the Act for eviction of tenant from disputed shop - Rent Control Authority allowed application and directed eviction of appellant/tenant - Appellant filed appeal before Appellate Court and order was upset - Respondent-land lady filed writ petition before HC, which was disposed of with matter remanded back to Appellate authority - Appellate Court affirmed judgment of Rent Control Authority declaring eviction and dismissed appeal - Appellant filed writ petition before HC, which upheld order of lower courts - Hence, instant appeal - Appellant contended that Courts below had failed to consider fact that even partial eviction would suffice - Further in terms of r. 16 of the Rules, parameters for commercial premise had to be different - Whether order of eviction of appellant from disputed shop was sustainable - Held, licence for repairing fire arms could only be obtained when there was vacant shop available and in absence of any vacant shop, licence could not be obtained by him - Therefore, HC came to conclusion concurring with that of lower courts that need of respondent/landlady was bona fide and genuine - In view of considering factual findings recorded, there was no scope for any interference with orders and impugned judgment of Courts below - Appeal dismissed.
