Visitor And Others v K.S. Misra

Supreme Court of India

06 September 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

G.P. Mathur, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 876; (2007) 8 SCC 593; (2007) 2 SCC (L&S) 959; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 821; 2007 (6) ALJ 150; 2007 (4) ESC 593; 2007 (115) FLR 353; JT 2007 (11) SC 549; 2007(11) SCALE 5; 2007 (7) SCJ 37; [2007] 9 S.C.R. 763; 2007 (6) SLR 479; 2008 (1) UPLBEC 531

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: Service - Sanction for payment of pension and gratuity - High Court directed that past service rendered by respondent in Benaras Hindu University shall be counted for the purpose of payment of pension and other retiral benefits - Held, respondent had exercised his option for Liberalized Pension Scheme including the benefit of the Family Pension Scheme for Central Government Employees and it had nothing to do with the option regarding counting of past service - Respondent did not exercise his option at any point of time for counting his past service - He had not applied in the University through proper channel or with the consent of the previous employer - Sub-clause (c) of Statute 61(6)(iv) provides for the consequences which will ensue in the event of non-exercise of option within the prescribed period of one year - Provisions of Statute 61(6)(iv) (b) and (c) should be treated as mandatory as it is a private party who has to do a particular act within a specified time - There is a long gap between time he left Benaras Hindu University and when he joined Aligarh Muslim University - It is not at all a case of transfer of an employee - Respondent is not entitled for counting of service rendered by him in Benaras Hindu University for the purpose of grant of pensionary benefits in Aligarh Muslim University - Appeal allowed.
