Hardev Singh v Gurmail Singh (Dead) By Lrs

Supreme Court of India

02 February 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 70; (2007) 2 SCC 404; AIR 2007 SC 1058; 2007 (66) ALR 814; 2007 (4) ALT 29; 2007 (1) AWC 907; 2007 (3) CalLT(SC) 37; 2007 (2) JhrCR(SC) 310; JT 2007 (3) SC 56; 2007 (3) MLJ(SC) 44; 2007 (146) PLR 813; 2007 (1) RCR(Civil) 876; 2007(2) SCALE 466; 2007 (4) SCJ 187; [2007] 2 S.C.R. 141

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Transfer Of Property Act, 1882 - Interpretation of s. 43 - Suit for declaration pending - Respondent purchased the property - Doctrine of lis pendens - High Court held that s. 43 would applicable - Held, respondent was aware of the litigation, but it is another thing to say that he did not purchase the property on representation of Harcharan Singh - It does not appear that any finding has been arrived at to the effect that the respondent herein was aware that the said Harcharan Singh had no title over the property - Appeal dismissed.
