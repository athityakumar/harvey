New India Assurance Co. Ltd v Prabhu Lal

Supreme Court of India

30 November 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

C.K. Thakker, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1254; (2008) 1 SCC 696; 2008 (1) ACC 54; AIR 2008 SC 614; 2008 (1) AWC 260; 2007 (1) CCC 608; 2008 (2) CGLJ 474; 2008 (2) CompLJ 480; 2008 (1) CPJ(Sc) 1; JT 2007 (13) SC 246; 2008 (1) MLJ(SC) 869; 2008 (149) PLR 434; 2008 (1) RCR(Civil) 198; 2007(13) SCALE 588; [2007] 12 S.C.R. 724; 2008 (1) UC 259; 2008 (1) WLN(SC) 68

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Consumer Protection; Criminal; Insurance

Keywords: Breach Of Condition, Public Service Vehicle, Use Of Motor Vehicle, Person In Charge, Third Party Risks, Prescribed Form, Automobile Accident, Driver Had No Licence, Owner Of Motor Vehicle, Pleading And Proof

Summary: Consumer Protection Act, 1986, s. 12; Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 - Complainant purchased a vehicle'Tata 709 - Insurance claim - Alleged deficiency in service - Claim of the claimant has been upheld finally by the National Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission which has been challenged by the Insurance Company - Held, s. 2 make it clear that if a vehicle is 'Light Motor Vehicle', but falls under the category of Transport Vehicle, the driving licence has to be duly endorsed under s. 3 - If it is not done, a person holding driving licence to ply Light Motor Vehicle cannot ply transport vehicle - District Forum was right in holding that on the basis of the evidence adduced by the Insurance Company, the complainant was not entitled to claim any compensation from the Insurance Company and Insurance Company cannot be held liable - Appeal allowed.
