National Institute Of Technology & Ors. v Niraj Kumar Singh

Supreme Court of India

02 February 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 86; (2007) 2 SCC 481; (2007) 1 SCC (L&S) 668; AIR 2007 SC 1155; 2007 (1) AWC 897; 2007 (2) CHN 98; 2007 (2) JhrCR(SC) 424; JT 2007 (3) SC 163; 2007 (2) LLJ 23; 2007 (2) LLN 20; 2007 (3) MLJ(SC) 100; 2007(2) SCALE 525; 2007 (5) SCJ 905; [2007] 2 S.C.R. 184; 2008 (8) SLR 707; 2007 (1) UPLBEC 939

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Constitution; Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Policy Decisions, Statutory Bodies

Summary: Contempt of Courts Act, 1971, s. 12 - Illegal appointment - Held, appointment on compassionate ground could have been offered only to a person who was the widow of the deceased or a dependent child - Respondent was not the natural grandson of deceased but was a grandson of his cousin brother - He was not entitled for appointment in terms of the scheme of the Institute - Institute, therefore, committed an illegality in granting him such an appointment - Appeal allowed.
