Kamala S v Vidyadharan M.J. and Another

Supreme Court of India

20 February 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 271; (2007) 5 SCC 264; (2007) 2 SCC (Cr) 498; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 1142; 2007 (2) BC 463; 2007 (2) Bom.C.R. 570; [2007] 136 Comp Cas 281; 2007 (2) CTC 648; 2007 (1) JCC (NI) 82; JT 2007 (3) SC 565; 2007 (3) KLT 861; 2007 (3) RCR(Criminal) 865; 2007(3) SCALE 235; [2007] 2 S.C.R. 852

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Corporate; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Legal Notice, Non- Mention

Summary: Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, s. 138 - Appeal against conviction and sentence - Appellant was sentenced to imprisonment till rising of the Court and pay compensation of a sum of Rs. 1 lakh to the complainant in terms of s. 357(3) of the Code of Criminal Procedure; and in default thereof to undergo simple imprisonment for 6 months - Held, defence case cannot said to be wholly improbable one - If it was probable, the findings of the learned Trial Judge could not have been thrown out without meeting the reasonings therefore - High Court was not correct in interfering with the said Judgment - Appeal allowed.
