Managing Director, Tnstc v Sripriya and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 March 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1012; (2007) 13 SCC 641; 2007 (2) ACC 50; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 19; 2007 (67) ALR 813; 2007 (5) AWC 5390; 2007 (4) CTC 251; 2007 (2) JCC 1105; 2007 (4) MLJ(SC) 604; 2007 (146) PLR 259; 2007 (2) RCR(Civil) 422; 2007(4) SCALE 222; [2007] 3 S.C.R. 762

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Keywords: Computation Of Compensation, Act Of God, Measure Of Damages, Assessment Of Damages, Fatal Accident Act, 1976

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 - Fatal Accident Act, 1976 - Appellant-Corporation took stand that there was no negligence which can be attributed to driver of bus - Tribunal allowed Claim Petition by present respondents and awarded a sum of Rs. 7,72,000 with 9% interest from date of petition till payment - Tribunal had adopted a multiplier of 16 on annual income of Rs. 6,000/- And after deduction of 1/3 for personal expenses worked out entitlement of claimants - Whether multiplier as adopted is high and same is case with rate of interest applied? - Held, multiplier method involves ascertainment of loss of dependency or multiplicand having regard to circumstances of case and capitalizing multiplicand by an appropriate multiplier - Choice of multiplier is determined by age of deceased (or that of claimants whichever is higher) and by calculation as to what capital sum, if invested at a rate of interest appropriate to a stable economy, would yield multiplicand by way of annual interest - Considering age of deceased appropriate multiplier would be 12 - Income fixed by tribunal and deduction for personal expenses do not warrant any interference - It would be appropriate to fix rate of interest at 7.5% instead of 9% as done by tribunal and maintained by High Court - Appeal partly allowed.
