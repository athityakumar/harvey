Ramesh Chander Singh v High Court of Allahabad and Another

Supreme Court of India

26 February 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

K.G. Balakrishnan, Lokeshwar Singh Panta, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 658; (2007) 4 SCC 247; 2007 (4) ADJ 653; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 88; 2007 (3) ALJ 502; 2007(3) ALL MR 848; 2007 (6) Bom.C.R. 865; 2007 (113) FLR 841; JT 2007 (4) SC 135; 2007 (4) MLJ(SC) 1055; 2007(3) SCALE 559; 2007 (6) SCJ 371; [2007] 3 S.C.R. 198; 2007 (4) SLR 672; 2007 (2) SLR 880; 2007 (2) UPLBEC 1148

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Keywords: Illegal Gratification, Under-Trial, Fire-Arms, TADA Act

Summary: Service - Advocates & Judges - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Uttar Pradesh Govt. Servants Conduct Rules, 1956, r. 3 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 302 and 307 - Appellant is a judicial officer - Bail application of accused was allowed by appellant - Complaint to High Court alleging that appellant accepted illegal gratification for granting bail to accused - Departmental enquiry - Appellant reduced to rank next below that is, Civil Judge (Senior Division) - Order imposing penalty of withholding two increments to appellant - Present appeal - Held, when a co-accused had been granted bail by HC, appellant cannot be said to have passed an unjustified order granting bail, that too, to an accused who was a student and had been in jail for more than one year - If at all, Inspecting Judge had found anything wrong with Order, he should have sent for officer and advised him to be careful in future - Punishment in facts and circumstances of this case could only be termed as draconian and unjust - Punishment was clearly disproportionate to lapse alleged to have been committed by him - Appellant shall be immediately posted to cadre of District Judge and paid all monetary benefits due to him as a consequence thereof - Appeal disposed of.
