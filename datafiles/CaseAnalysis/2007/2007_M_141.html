Chacko And Another v Mahadevan

Supreme Court of India

29 August 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Markandey Katju, A.K. Mathur

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 860; (2007) 7 SCC 363; AIR 2007 SC 2967; 2007(6) ALL MR 410; 2007 (69) ALR 506; 2007 (4) AWC 3577; 2007 (4) JhrCR(SC) 90; JT 2007 (10) SC 461; 2007 (4) KLT 753; 2007 (6) MLJ(SC) 657; 2008 (1) MPLJ 8; 2007 (4) RCR(Civil) 178; 2008 (104) RD 117; 2007(10) SCALE 444

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Sale deed - Suit seeking to set aside the sale deed on the ground that it was vitiated by fraud and was hence null and void and for a prohibitory injunction - First Appellate Court held that the fact that one cent land was sold for Rs.18000 and three cent land was sold for a sum of Rs.1000, showed that this was an unconscionable transaction - Second appeal allowed - Held, a perusal of the judgment of the First Appellate Court shows that it has been recorded therein that Chacko was not having sound mind when he executed Ext. A3, which is established from Ext.A4 which is the medical certificate - This is a finding of fact which could not have been interfered with by the High Court in Second Appeal - Matter speaks for itself - Chacko sold the land by sale deed dated 11.7.1983 when he was not of sound mind and some fraud was played on him at that time - Appeals allowed.
