R.K. Mobisana Singh v Kh. Temba Singh & Ors

Supreme Court of India

12 December 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1612; (2008) 1 SCC 747; AIR 2008 SC (Supp) 1296; 2007(14) SCALE 239; [2007] 13 S.C.R. 434; 2008 (4) SLR 736

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Andhra Pradesh State and Subordinate Service Rules, Manipur Assistant Engineer (Civil/Mech) Surveyor of Works Recruitment Rules, 1969, Jammu and Kashmir Engineering Rules, J & K (CCA) Rules, 1956

Summary: Service - Manipur Assistant Engineer (Civil/Mech) Surveyor of Works Recruitment Rules of 1969 - Direct recruits and promotes - Inter se seniority - Determination - State Government informed the State Public Service Commission in regard to existence of 39 vacancies in the posts of Assistant Engineer (AE) - Respondent/Promotees were promoted to the post of AE on ad hoc basis and their posts were officiated on the date of joining of the post on the recommendation of the Departmental Promotion Committee (DPC) - Subsequently, seniority list in question was issued and respondent/promotees challenged the said list before the HC on the ground that although they were promoted to the post of Junior Engineer on ad hoc basis in or about 1981, they had been shown as junior to direct recruits who were appointed in 1986 - Single Judge directed regularisation of respondent/promotees with retrospective effect and preparation of a fresh seniority list - Exercise of preparation of fresh and final seniority list took a long time - Appellant/Direct recruits challenged the Single Judge order before the DB - DB dismissed appeals holding that the Single Judge order granting retrospective regularization to the promotees having not been challenged either by the direct recruits or by the State Government had attained finality - Whether regularisation can be retrospectively given effect in the case of determination of seniority - Held, although regularisation was given to promotees in terms of the Office Memorandum dated 22-12-1959 (which lays down general principles for determination of seniority), no retrospective effect could be given to the order of regularisation passed in favour of the promotees, as in absence of any seniority rules operating in the field - In such circumstances, State was required to evolve a policy, however, it, for its own reason, did not do so - In some of the cases, promotion might have been given without following the rules - When promotion is given only in the exigency of situation without following the Rules, the period cannot be counted towards seniority - If promotees had been given regularisation with retrospective effect, the same by itself may not be a ground to apply the said order ipso facto for determining the inter se seniority - Seniority although is not a fundamental right but a civil right and such a right of the direct recruits could not have been taken away without affording an opportunity of hearing to them - It was obligatory on the part of the official respondents to take into consideration that the retrospective regularization could be granted only when there exists such a rule - If rules were not followed at the time of grant of promotion, question of grant of regularization with retrospective effect would not arise - Retrospective regularization whether in terms of the directions of the HC or otherwise, thus, although could confer other service benefits to the officer concerned, but the same cannot be held to be of any assistance for reckoning seniority with retrospective effect - Therefore, question of promotees acquiring seniority over the direct recruits does not arise - Direction issued to the competent authority of the State to determine the inter se seniority of the parties in view of the principles enunciated in the instant judgement - Appeals disposed of.
