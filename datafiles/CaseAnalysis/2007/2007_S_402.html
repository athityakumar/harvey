Suman Sood @ Kamal Jeet Kaur and Others v State Of Rajasthan

Supreme Court of India

14 May 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

C.K. Thakker, P.K. Balasubramanyan

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 516; (2007) 5 SCC 634; (2007) 2 SCC (Cr) 637; AIR 2007 SC 2774; 2007 CRLJ 4080; JT 2007 (7) SC 101; 2007 (3) RCR(Criminal) 119; 2007(7) SCALE 312; [2007] 6 S.C.R. 499

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Extradition (Amendment) Act, 1993

Summary: Indian Penal Code, ss. 364A, 365, 343 r/w 120B and 346 r/w 120B - Appeal against conviction and sentence - Held, neither the trial Court nor the High Court has committed either an error of fact or an error of law in convicting accused Daya Singh for the offences punishable under Sections 365 as also 364A, IPC - There is no evidence at all 'direct or indirect ' to connect Suman Sood with kidnapping for ransom - Appeals disposed of.
