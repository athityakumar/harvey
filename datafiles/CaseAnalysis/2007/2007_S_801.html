State of Punjab and others v Surinder Singh and another

Supreme Court of India

25 October 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Mathur, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1632; (2007) 13 SCC 231; (2008) 2 SCC (L&S) 597; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 1222; 2008 (5) AWC 4853; JT 2007 (13) SC 576; 2007(12) SCALE 602; [2007] 11 S.C.R. 707

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Regularizing of appointment - Equal pay for equal work - Respondents were working as tractor drivers on daily wage basis under petitioner Department - Respondent filed petition before Trial Court for regularizing their appointment - Trial Court directed that respondent who were working was daily wage basis should be granted same pay scale as those who had regularly apponted, on principle of equal pay for work and other relief regarding regularization of their services was declined - On appeal by State, HC affirmed order of Trail Court and dismissed appeal - Hence instant appeal.

Held, principle of equal pay for equal work has gone a sea change. Earlier view of Court was that if two persons are discharging same functions, they will be entitled to same wages. This view has been changed and now view of Court is that there should be complete and total identity between two persons similarly situated so as to grant equal pay for equal work. Recently Court has held that identity between two persons has to be complete and total. In case of a regular appointee, he has undergone a selection process and his services are regular. Principle of equal pay for equal work has to be granted only if there is a total and complete identity between two persons. Granting pay scale is an executive or legislative function, and not a judicial function. There is separation of powers between three organs of State, and judiciary should not encroach into domain of other organs. Montesquieus theory of separation of powers broadly applies in India too. In view of legal position, that view taken by HC granting pay scale of regular employees to daily wage employees does not appear to be well founded. Court allow these appeals and set aside order of HC as also of Trial Court and dismiss suits filed by respondents. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Even if a daily wage employee is discharging same functions as a regular employee, authorities are not bound to grant equal pay to such a person who is appointed on daily wage basis that is appointed for a short term and has not faced selection process.
