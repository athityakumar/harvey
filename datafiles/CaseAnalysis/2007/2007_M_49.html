M.P. State Agro Industries Development Corporation Limited and Another v Jahan Khan

Supreme Court of India

05 September 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

D.K. Jain, Arijit Pasayat

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 880; (2007) 10 SCC 88; AIR 2007 SC 3153; JT 2007 (10) SC 571; 2007(10) SCALE 602; 2007 (6) SCJ 676; [2007] 9 S.C.R. 715; 2008 (1) SLR 5

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: M.P. State Agro Industries Development Corporation Limited Service (Recruitment and Selection) Regulations of 1976 - Service - whether in the context of the Regulations governing the service conditions of the respondent, the recovery of the amount and stoppage of three increments with cumulative effect is a major penalty and if so, the order of punishment is vitiated on any of the grounds noted above, warranting interference by the Court? - Held, withholding of increments with cumulative effect is treated as a major penalty because it has a perpetual effect on the entire tenure of service of the employee - Imposition of penalty directing recovery of loss of Rs.16903.41 and stoppage of three increments with cumulative effect, is a major penalty, clearly envisaging a regular enquiry before punishing the respondent - High Court was justified in coming to the conclusion that imposition of the impugned penalty without holding enquiry was illegal and without jurisdiction - Appeals dismissed.
