The Empire Jute Company Limited And Others v The Jute Corporation Of India Limited And Another

Supreme Court of India

12 October 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1645; (2007) 14 SCC 680; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 1733; 2008 (1) ALD(SC) 70; 2008 (2) ALT 34; 2008 (1) AWC 119; JT 2007 (12) SC 391; 2008 (1) RCR(Civil) 151; 2007(12) SCALE 514; [2007] 11 S.C.R. 388

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996 - Extent of judicial intervention - Existence of arbitration agreement - Jute Commissioner issued Production Control Orders (PCO) to various jute mill owners directing them to manufacture 'B' Twill Gunny Bags of specified quality upon compulsory purchase of raw jute from the Corporation - Thereafter, necessary sale contract were issued in order to enable the Jute manufacturers to take delivery of the requisite quantities of raw jute specified in the PCO which the jute manufacturers were required to compulsorily purchase from the Corporation - However, as the quality of raw jute supplied by the respondent under the contract of sale was said to be of much inferior quality, appellants (owners of a jute mill) did not purchase raw jute from the respondent No. 1 - Apprehending that no further raw material would be allotted to it, and/or punitive action would be taken against them, a writ petition was filed by the appellants - Pursuant to interim order, the appellants deposited the amount, in question - On appeal, DB of HC held that respondent was entitled to get the carrying costs and other charges mentioned in Clause 5.0 of the agreement for breach of the terms of the agreement at the instance of the appellants - DB further directed the parties to settle the amount through arbitration in terms of agreement in regard to the quantum of the carrying charges payable by the appellant to the respondents - Instant appeal - Whether appellant was not liable to pay the carrying cost? - Held, power of judicial review vested in the superior courts has wide amplitude but the same should not be exercised when there exists an arbitration clause - DB took recourse to the arbitration agreement in regard to one part of the dispute but proceeded to determine the other part itself - Hence, if DB was of the opinion that the dispute between the parties being covered by the arbitration clause should be referred to arbitration, it should not have proceeded to determine a part of the dispute itself - Further, the question of payment of carrying cost by the appellant in favour of the respondent would arise provided the same was payable and the payability of such carrying cost would, thus, depend upon construction of Clause 2.0 read with Clause 5.0 of the sale contract - Hence, as the disputed facts as also the law were required to be determined by the Arbitrator, all disputes between the parties should be directed to be resolved upon taking recourse to the arbitration agreement - Appeals allowed.
