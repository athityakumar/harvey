Union of India v Arviva Industries (I) Limited

Supreme Court of India

10 January 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhan, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 343; (2014) 3 SCC 159; 2007 (209) E.L.T. 5; 2008 (10) S.T.R. 534

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Civil Appeals dismissed

Summary: Excise - Binding effect - Circulars issued by the Central Board of Excise & Customs on the Department - Board's Circular No. 39/99-Cus, dtd 25 June 1999 extended the benefit of Brand Rate of Drawback to compensate exporters for the re-rolled steel products and processed fabrics - Whether the circulars issued by the Board are binding on the department - Held, decision of the High Court that the Circulars issued by the Central Board of Excise & Customs are binding on the department and the department can not be permitted to urge that the Circulars issued by the Board are not binding on it, upheld.
