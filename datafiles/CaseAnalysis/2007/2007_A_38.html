Andhra Bank v Abn Amro Bank N.V. And Ors

Supreme Court of India

10 July 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Tarun Chatterjee, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 722; (2007) 6 SCC 167; AIR 2007 SC 2511; 2007 (5) ALD(SC) 37; 2008 (1) Bom.C.R. 836; [2007] 139 Comp Cas 111; 2007 (3) CompLJ 185; 2007 (4) JhrCR(SC) 111; JT 2007 (9) SC 244; 2007 (6) MLJ(SC) 313; 2007 (3) RCR(Civil) 585; 2007 (2) RCR(Rent) 136; 2007(9) SCALE 96; 2007 (3) UC 1649

Case Digest

Subject: Contract & Commercial

Summary: Suit for recovery of Rs.15, 66, 66, 591/- - Application for amendment of the written statement dismissed - Appeal against - Held, appellant in their prayer for amendment has only taken an additional defence that in view of s. 230 of the Indian Contract Act, the suit itself is not maintainable - At the time of considering the prayer for amendment of the written statement it would not be open to the Court to go into the fact whether in fact the suit in view of s. 230 of the Indian Contract Act was or is not maintainable - It is permissible in law to amend a written statement of the defendant by which only an additional ground of defence has been taken - Prayer to tender in evidence of certain paragraph and affidavit in examination in chief of the appellant containing statements are relevant and germane to the issues involved in the suit and, therefore, cannot be rejected only on the ground that the same did not contain any admission - Application for amendment of the written statement is allowed - Appeal allowed.
