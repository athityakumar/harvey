Tax Recovery Officer, Central Range-1 v Custodian Special Court ( T.O.R.T.S) Act, 1992 and Others

Supreme Court of India

17 August 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

G.P. Mathur, P.K. Balasubramanyan

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1030; (2007) 7 SCC 461; AIR 2007 SC 2935; [2007] 139 Comp Cas 1; 2007 (5) CompLJ 11; [2007] 293 ITR 369; JT 2007 (10) SC 188; 2007 (4) RCR(Civil) 113; 2007(10) SCALE 81; [2007] 8 S.C.R. 1051

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Tax Recovery Officer, Special Court (Trial Of Offences Relating To Transactions In Securities) Amendment Act, 1994

Summary: Income Tax - Section 226(4), Income Tax Act, 1961 - Liability - Preferential claim - Section 9A, Special Courts (Trial of Offences Relating To Transactions In Securities) Act, 1992 - X had been notified as a party under s. 3(2), Special Courts Act - Y had not been notified as a party - X owed money from Y and its 9 subsidiary companies of which the former stood as guarantor - In execution of the decrees passed in favour of X that the property of Y was put to auction - Held, tax can be recovered from an assessee only when it becomes a debt due from him and that it becomes a debt due when a notice of demand calling for payment of the tax has been served on the assessee - If an assessee objects to the recovery proceedings taken under s. 226(4) on the ground that there has been no valid service of a notice of demand and that, therefore, no debt is due, the court must decide the objection, and if it upholds the objection, it cannot permit recovery of the tax claimed - What is attached under s. 3(3), Special Courts Act is the property, moveable or immoveable, or both belonging to any person notified under s. 3(2), Special Courts Act - Special Court could not have entertained the application moved by the Income Tax Department under s. 226(4), Income Tax Act for realization of its income tax dues from Y - Intervention Applications were not maintainable before the Special Court.
