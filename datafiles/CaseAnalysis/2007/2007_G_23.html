Directorate of Film Festivals and Others v Gaurav Ashwin Jain and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 April 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1552; (2007) 4 SCC 737; AIR 2007 SC 1640; 2007 (6) AWC 6170; 2007 (3) Bom.C.R. 577; JT 2007 (5) SC 394; 2007 (3) MLJ(SC) 955; 2007(5) SCALE 565; [2007] 5 S.C.R. 7

Case Digest

Subject: Media & Entertainment

Keywords: Certification, Certification Of Films, 53rd National Film Award Regulations

Summary: Media & Entertainment - Constitution - Cinematograph Act, 1952, ss. 4, 5A, 5B, 9 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 19(1)(a) - 53rd National Film Award Regulations , rr. 10(d), 10(e) - Certification Of Films - Validity - Directorate of Film Festivals organizes NFA every year - Awards were given to feature films as also non-feature films - Respondents who were documentary film makers, intended to enter their Non-Feature Films Awards for year 2005 - Respondents were aggrieved by two eligibility requirements prescribed by Directorate, for non-feature films, in cl. (e) of r. 10 of the 53rd NFA Regulations - Respondents filed writ petition seeking declaration that rr. 10(d) and (e) of 53rd National Film Award Regulations were violative of arts. 14 and 19(1)(a) of Constitution - Respondents also sought consequential declaration that films made and released on either 'film format' or 'digital format' were eligible for entry, without any certification by Board - HC directed appellants to permit film-makers to enter their films in digital format without any certification from Board as HC found that requirement relating to certification by Board was violative of art. 14 and therefore, invalid, it did not examine issue with reference to art. 19(1)(a) -- Hence, instant appeal - Whether order passed by HC was justified - Held, film-maker could challenge illegal exemption in favour of Film Institutes and Doordarshan u/cls. (f) and (g) of regn.10, but could not claim similar exemption by placing reliance on such illegality - Therefore, challenge to words 'and certified by Central Board of Film Certification' in regns. 10(d) and (e) had no merit - Respondents had not challenged validity of regn.10(f) and (g) granting exemption to films made by Film Institutes or films entered by Doordarshan and no relief was granted to respondents in that behalf - Hence, judgment of HC was set aside except direction to permit entry of non- feature films in digital format - Appeal partly allowed.
