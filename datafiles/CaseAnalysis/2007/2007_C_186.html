Commissioner of Central Excise, Nagpur v Messrs Ballarpur Industries Limited

Supreme Court of India

30 August 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, B. Sudershan Reddy

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 985; (2007) 8 SCC 89; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 815; 2007 (215) E.L.T. 489; JT 2007 (10) SC 577; 2007(10) SCALE 500; [2007] 9 S.C.R. 650

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Appeals disposed of., Sale Price, Mis-Statement, Normal Price, Central Excise (Valuation) Rules, 1975, Modvat Scheme, Place Of Removal

Summary: Excise - Rule 57CC, Central Excise Rules, 1944 - Section 4(1), (2), Central Excise Act, 1944 - Valuation - Sale - Stock transfer - Assessee is engaged in manufacture of paper and pulp - Pulp is captively consumed for the manufacture of paper - Small portion of the pulp is sent to its sister unit - Assessee submitted that in case of "stock transfer" there is no "sale" and, therefore, r. 57CC was not applicable Whether in the absence of any "sale", r. 57CC would have any application - Held, where the manufacturer does not comply with r. 57CC(9), he shall debit the presumptive sum equal to eight per cent of the value of the exempted goods at the time of clearance from the factory gate - Rule 57CC(9) would apply to stock transfers also - Impugned order set aside.
