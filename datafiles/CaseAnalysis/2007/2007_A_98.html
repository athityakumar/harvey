Union of India v Ananto (Dead) and Another

Supreme Court of India

09 March 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 998; (2007) 10 SCC 748; AIR 2007 SC 1561; 2007(3) ALL MR 856; 2007 (2) AWC 1575; 2007 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1155; 2007 (3) MLJ(SC) 610; 2007 (147) PLR 69; 2007 (2) RCR(Civil) 753B; 2007(4) SCALE 296; [2007] 3 S.C.R. 877

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Requisition and Acquisition of Immovable Properties Act, 1952, National Legal Services Authorities Act, 1987

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Requisition and Acquisition of Immovable Properties Act, 1952 - National Legal Services Authorities Act, 1987, ss. 20(3) and (5) - Lok Adalat - Jurisdiction - Division Bench noted that order of Lok Adalat had merged into order of Single Judge and had therefore, become operative - Lok Adalat can dispose of a matter by way of a compromise or settlement between parties - If no compromise or settlement is or could by arrived at, no order can be passed by Lok Adalat - Held, present case did not involve compromise or settlement and could not have been disposed of by Lok Adalat - Therefore, question of merger of Lok Adalats order does not arise - Remit matter to High Court - Appeal allowed.
