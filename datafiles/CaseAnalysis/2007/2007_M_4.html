(1) Messrs DIT (International Taxation), Mumbai; ( v (1) Messrs Morgan Stanley and Company Inc; (2)

Supreme Court of India

09 July 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, Arijit Pasayat

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 739; (2007) 7 SCC 1; 2007 (5) CompLJ 21; [2007] 292 ITR 416; JT 2007 (9) SC 1; 2007(9) SCALE 1; [2007] 8 S.C.R. 52; [2007] 162 TAXMAN 165

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Issue regarding articles in Double Tax Avoidance Agreement between India and United States which have implication on transfer pricing legislation - Nature of activities performed by stewards and deputationists deployed by MSCo to work in India as employees of MSAS - Question relating to the transaction between the applicant and MSAS on which advance ruling was sought was two fold namely, whether the applicant was having a PE in India under art. 5(1) of the DTAA on account of the services rendered by MSAS under the Services Agreement dated April 14, 2005 entered into by MSAS with the applicant and if so, the amount of income attributable to such PE?; whether the AAR is right in its ruling when it says that once the transfer pricing analysis is under taken there is no further need to attribute profits to a PE - Held, MSCo is merely protecting its own interests in the competitive world by ensuring the quality and confidentiality of MSAS services - Under art. 7(2) not all profits of MSCo would be taxable in India but only those which have economic nexus with PE in India - A foreign enterprise is liable to be taxed in India on so much of its business profit as is attributable to the PE in India - AAR was right in ruling that MSAS would be a Service PE in India under art. 5(2)(l), though only on account of the services to be performed by the deputationists deployed by MSCo and not on account of stewardship activities - Impugned ruling by AAR stands modified - Appeals partly allowed.
