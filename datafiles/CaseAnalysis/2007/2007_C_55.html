Japani Sahoo v Chandra Sekhar Mohanty

Supreme Court of India

27 July 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

C.K. Thakker, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 767; (2007) 3 SCC (Cr) 388; (2007) 7 SCC 394; AIR 2007 SC 2762; 2007 CRLJ 4068; 2007 (4) CTC 740; 2007 (3) JCC 2130; JT 2007 (9) SC 471; 2007 (3) KLT 760; 2007 (3) RCR(Criminal) 912; 2007(9) SCALE 400; 2007 (8) SCJ 826; 2007 (8) SCJ 926; [2007] 8 S.C.R. 582; 2007 (3) Supreme 760

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Indian Penal, 1860, ss. 294, 323 - Limitation - High Court quashed criminal proceedings initiated against the respondent- accused on the ground of limitation - Appeal against - Held, complainant cannot be penalized for delay on the part of the Court nor he can be non suited because of failure or omission by the Magistrate in taking appropriate action under the Code - For the purpose of computing the period of limitation, the relevant date must be considered as the date of filing of complaint or initiating criminal proceedings and not the date of taking cognizance by a Magistrate or issuance of process by a Court - Complaint was filed within a period of three days from the date of alleged offence - Complaint must be held to be filed within the period of limitation even though cognizance was taken by Magistrate after a period of one year - Directed the Magistrate to proceed with the case - Appeal allowed.
