J. K. Industries Ltd. & Anr v Union Of India & Ors

Supreme Court of India

19 November 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, B. SUDARSHAN REDDY

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1497; (2007) 13 SCC 673; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 1047; [2008] 143 Comp Cas 325; 2007 (5) CompLJ 369; [2008] 297 ITR 176; JT 2007 (12) SC 529; 2007(13) SCALE 204; [2007] 12 S.C.R. 136; [2007] 165 TAXMAN 323

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Companies (Amendment) Act, 1988, Cost And Works Accountants Act, 1959, Preliminary Expenses, Taxes On Income, Accrual System, Wealth-tax Rules, Company Secretaries Act, 1980, Companies (Amendment) Act, 1999, Central Provinces and Berar Sales Act, 1947, Super Tax Act, 1963, Finance Act, 2009, Banking Companies (Legal Practitioners Accounts) Act, 1949

Summary: Income Tax - Income Tax Act, 1961 - Companies Act, 1956 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Substantial question of law - Accounting Standard 22 (AS 22) - Deferred taxation - Appellant seeks to challenge AS 22 issued by Institute of Chartered Accountants of India which has been made mandatory for all companies listed in Stock Exchanges in India in preparation of their accounts for the financial year 2001-02 onwards - Whether Accounting Standard 22 (AS 22) entitled "accounting for taxes on income" insofar as it relates to deferred taxation is inconsistent with and ultra vires the provisions of the Companies Act, 1956, the Income Tax Act, 1961 and the Constitution of India, 1950 - Held, deferred tax is nothing but accrual of tax due to divergence between accounting profit and tax profit - Difference arises on two counts, namely, different treatment of items of revenue/expense as per profit and loss account and as per the tax law and the difference between the amount of revenue/expense as per profit and loss account and the corresponding amount considered for tax purposes, e.g., depreciation - AS 22 is neither ultra vires nor inconsistent with the provisions of the Companies Act, including Schedule VI - Impugned judgment upheld.
