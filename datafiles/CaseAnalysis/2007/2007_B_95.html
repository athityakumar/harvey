Bihar State Council of Ayurvedic and Unani Medicine and others v State Of Bihar & Ors

Supreme Court of India

01 November 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

P.P. Naolekar, B.N. Agrawal

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1595; (2007) 12 SCC 728; AIR 2008 SC 595; 2008 (5) AWC 4482; JT 2007 (13) SC 32; 2007(12) SCALE 644; [2007] 11 S.C.R. 824; 2007 (8) SLR 807

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Education

Keywords: Bihar Development of Ayurvedic and Unani Systems of Medicine Act, 1951, Bihar Indigenous Medical Educational Institution (Regulation and Control) Act, 1981, Bihar Indigenous Medical Educational Institution (Regulation and Control) Act, 1982, Bihar Indigenous Medical Educational Institution (Regulation and Control) Ordinance, 1981, Indian Medicine Central Council Bill, Bihar Development of Ayurvedic and Unani Systems of Medicines Regulations, 1959

Summary: Education - Constitution - Indian Medicine Central Council Act, 1970, ss. 13A, 13B and 13C - Bihar Development of Ayurvedic and Unani Systems of Medicine Act, 1951 - Indian Medicine Central Council (Amendment) Act, 2003 - Bihar Indigenous Medical Educational Institution (Regulation and Control) Act, 1982 - Recognition of medical qualifications - (A) Whether with the introduction of the Act, 1982, the students who have studied in the colleges which were not recognized under the said Act could be conferred with Graduate of Ayurvedic Medicine and Surgery (GAMS) degree by the Faculty, and if such degrees are conferred what shall be the fate of the degrees conferred on such students? - Held, Act, 1982 is supplementary to the Act, 1951 - Further, there is no provision in the Act, 1982 which takes away the degree already granted to the students conferred by the Faculty, recognized under the Act, 1951, and is being accepted to be a recognized degree under the Act, 1970 - Therefore, by virtue of introduction of the Act, 1982, it cannot be said that the degrees conferred on the students who have studied in the colleges which have not been granted permission by the State Government as required under the Act, 1982, will be ipso facto illegal and could not be given effect to - GAMS degree conferred on the students shall be treated as a recognized degree for the purposes of taking admission to the higher courses of study and also for the purposes of employment - (B) Non-recognition of medical qualifications in certain cases - Effect of the Amending Act, 2003, particularly ss. 13A, 13B and 13C which have been substituted by way of amendment in the Act, 1970 and on the degrees conferred on the students who have studied in the colleges which have not sought or have not been given permission as required u/s. 13C of the Act, 1970 to open the college or continue the college, by the Central Government - Held, in construing an Act, a construction ought not be put that would work injustice, or even hardship or inconvenience, unless it is clear that such was the intention of the legislature - In the present case, on a reasonable construction of these sections, it is held that the provisions of s. 13B whereby the qualification granted to any student of a medical college would not be deemed to be a recognized medical qualification would not apply and when a degree has been legally conferred on the students prior to the commencement of the Amending Act, 2003, it shall be treated as a recognized degree although the medical college has not sought permission of the Central Government within a period of three years from the commencement of the Amending Act, 2003 - Appeals allowed.
