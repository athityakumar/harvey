Surat Valsad Jilla K.M.G. Parishad v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 May 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1210; (2007) 5 SCC 360; AIR 2007 SC 2056; 2007 (3) G.L.R. 2234; 2007(7) SCALE 21; 2007 (4) SCJ 395; [2007] 6 S.C.R. 257

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Special Provisions Relating To Certain Classes, Constitution (Scheduled Caste) Order, 1950

Summary: Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 341, 341(2) and 342 - Constitution (Scheduled Caste) Order, 1950 -Validity of Constitution (Scheduled Caste) Orders 2nd Amendment 2002, whereby and whereunder 'Mochis' outside Dang District and Umargaon Taluka of Valsad Distirct in State of Gujarat were excluded from Schedule I to Constitution (Scheduled Caste) Order 1950 - Constitution provides for declaration of certain castes and tribes as Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes in terms of arts. 341 and 432 of Constitution - Object of said provisions is to provide for grant of protection to backward class of citizens who are specified in Scheduled Castes Order and Scheduled Tribes Order having regard to economic and educationally backwardness wherefrom they suffer - President of India alone in terms of art. 341(1) of Constitution is authorized to issue an appropriate notification therefore - Held, Constitution (Schedule Castes) Order, 1950 made in terms of art. 341(1) is exhaustive - It is, therefore, not for court to render its opinion as to whether President was correct in confining inclusion of caste Mochi within a particular area - No case has been made out for declaring impugned legislation as unconstitutional - Appeals dismissed.
