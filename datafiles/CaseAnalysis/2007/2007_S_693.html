Raj Kumar Soni and Another v State of Uttar Pradesh and Another

Supreme Court of India

03 April 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

B. Sudershan Reddy, P.K. Balasubramanyan

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 291; (2007) 10 SCC 635; 2007 (4) ADJ 539; 2007 (3) ALJ 607; 2007 (3) AWC 2657; JT 2007 (5) SC 114; 2007 (2) LCD 1345; 2007 (102) RD 724; 2007(5) SCALE 334; 2007 (6) SCJ 503; [2007] 4 S.C.R. 733

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Constitution; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: U.P. Zamindari Abolition and Land Revenue Act

Summary: Irregular allotment - Show-cause as to why the grant of the land made in appellant's favour by the Sub-Divisional Officer, Kotdwar should not be rescinded - Order of transfer made in favour of the appellants by the Sub- Divisional Officer has been quashed - Held, appellants failed to establish that they have lawfully secured allotment of land - It is the duty casts upon the appellants to plead and establish that the order of allotment/grant by the Sub-Divisional Officer in favour of their predecessor-in-title created any legal right and also further establish the transfer of land in their favour has been validly made by the Sub-Divisional Officer - Appeals dismissed.
