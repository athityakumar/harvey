State Of Gujarat v Anand Acharya @ Bharat Kumar Sadhu

Supreme Court of India

22 February 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Lokeshwar Singh Panta, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 142; (2007) 9 SCC 310; (2007) 2 SCC (L&S) 514; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 533; 2007 (2) CLR 421; 2007 (113) FLR 447; JT 2007 (4) SC 124; 2007 (2) LLJ 709; 2007 (3) LLN 29; 2007(3) SCALE 415; 2007 (2) SLR 867

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: Gujarat Civil Services (Conduct) Rules, 1971 - Allegation that respondent has not disclosed the fact about the criminal proceedings - Decision of removal of the respondent - High Court modified punishment and a penalty of stoppage of two increments with future effect was ordered - Appeal against - Held, a court sitting in judicial review against the quantum of punishment imposed in the disciplinary proceedings will not normally substitute its own conclusion on penalty is not in dispute - Inquiry Officer had not found the respondent guilty of having married Smitaben during the subsistence of first wife - Only allegation having been proved against the respondent by the Inquiry Officer was non-disclosure of the criminal proceedings pending against him in which the respondent was ultimately acquitted - Not inclined to interfere with the findings recorded by the learned Single Judge and affirmed by the Division Bench of the High Court modifying the order of punishment - Appeal dismissed.
