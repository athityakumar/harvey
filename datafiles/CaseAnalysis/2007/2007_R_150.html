Usha P. Kuvelkar & Others v Ravindra Subrai Dalvi

Supreme Court of India

20 November 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

V.S. Sirpurkar, G.P. Mathur

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1146; (2008) 1 SCC 330; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 1410; 2008(2) ALL MR 345; 2008 (1) Bom.C.R. 65; 2008 (1) KLT 398; 2008 (1) RCR(Civil) 108; 2007 (2) RCR(Rent) 650; 2007(13) SCALE 316; [2007] 12 S.C.R. 324

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Rent Control

Keywords: Goa Daman & Diu Building (Lease, Rent & Eviction) Control Act, 1968

Summary: Goa Daman & Diu Building (Lease, Rent & Eviction) Control Act, 1968 - Eviction case on the ground of bonafide occupation - Concurrent finding that landlord was justified in making the application for eviction - Matter has been ordered to be remanded back to Additional Rent Controller by High Court - Appeal against - Held, beyond saying that the Additional Rent Controller has not applied his mind, the High Court has not considered anything - Findings of Additional Rent Controller as also the Administrative Tribunal on the bonafide need of the landlord are correct and the High Court has gravely erred in setting aside the concurrent findings of fact that too without giving any reasons worth the name - Appeal allowed.
