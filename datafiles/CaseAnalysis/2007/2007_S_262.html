D. Sailu v State Of A.P.

Supreme Court of India

20 November 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1147; (2007) 14 SCC 397; AIR 2008 SC 505; 2008 ALL MR (Cri) 864; 2008 CRLJ 686; 2008 (1) JCC 151; JT 2007 (13) SC 57; 2007(13) SCALE 356; [2007] 12 S.C.R. 397

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Blood Stained Clothes, Subsequent Conduct, Medical Witness

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 300, 302, 304, 34 - Appeal against conviction and sentence - Held, it would be erroneous to accord undue primacy to hypothetical answers of medical witnesses to exclude eyewitnesses account which had to be tested independently and not treated as 'variable' keeping medical evidence as 'constant' - Where eyewitnesses account is found credible and trustworthy, medical opinion pointing to alternative possibilities is not accepted as conclusive - Considering background facts, appropriate conviction would be u/s. 304 Part I IPC and not s.302 IPC - Appeal partly allowed.
