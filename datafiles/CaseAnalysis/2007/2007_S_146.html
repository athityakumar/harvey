B. Suresh Yadav v Sharifa Bee and Another

Supreme Court of India

12 October 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1044; (2007) 13 SCC 107; AIR 2008 SC 210; 2007 ALL MR (Cri) 3285; 2007 (3) ALT 281; 2008 CRLJ 431; 2008 (1) CTC 338; JT 2007 (12) SC 341; 2007 (4) RCR(Criminal) 870; 2007(12) SCALE 364; 2007 (7) SCJ 858; [2007] 11 S.C.R. 238

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Corporate; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Indian Penal Code - Application for quashing the complaint dismissed - Appeal against - Whether a case of cheating within the meaning of s. 415 of IPC has been made out or not? - Held, for the purpose of establishing the offence of cheating, the complainant is required to show that the accused had fraudulent or dishonest intention at the time of making promise or representation - While executing the sale deed, the appellant herein did not make any false or misleading representation - There had also not been any dishonest act of inducement on his part to do or omit to do anything which he could not have done or omitted to have done if he were not so deceived - No case has been made out for proceeding with the criminal case - Appeal allowed
