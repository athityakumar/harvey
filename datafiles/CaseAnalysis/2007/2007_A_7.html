N. Suriyakala v A. Mohandoss and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 February 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Markandey Katju, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 96; (2007) SCC (Cr) 115; (2007) 9 SCC 196; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 387; 2007 (66) ALR 784; 2007 (3) ALT 156; 2007 CRLJ 1461; 2007 (2) JCC 1074; JT 2007 (3) SC 266; 2007 (2) RCR(Civil) 59; 2007 (2) RCR(Criminal) 1; 2007 (102) RD 490B; 2007(3) SCALE 15; [2007] 2 S.C.R. 419

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Indian Penal Code, ss. 498A, 406; Dowry Prohibition Act, 1961, s. 4 - Madras High Court quashed criminal case instituted by appellant against her husband - Appeal against - Held, ordinarily the last court in the country in ordinary cases was meant to be the High Court - Supreme Court as the Apex Court in the country was meant to deal with important issues like constitutional questions, questions of law of general importance or where grave injustice had been done - This is not fit case to be entertained in exercise of our discretion under art. 136 of Constitution - Appellant has also filed a maintenance petition against her husband - Appeal dismissed.
