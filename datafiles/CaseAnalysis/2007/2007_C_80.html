National Insurance Company Limited v Cholleti Bharatamma and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 October 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1473; (2008) 1 SCC 423; 2008 (1) ACC 225; AIR 2008 SC 484; 2008(1) ALL MR 436; 2007 (4) AWC 3957; 2008 (2) G.L.R. 1403; JT 2007 (12) SC 319; 2008 (1) MLJ(SC) 66; 2008 (149) PLR 315; 2007 (4) RCR(Civil) 755; 2007(12) SCALE 560; [2007] 11 S.C.R. 531

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Legislative Intent, Insurer's Liability, Public Service Vehicle, Passengers, Gratuitous Passenger, Owner Of Goods, Carried For Hire Or Reward, Motor Vehicles (Amendment) Act, 1994, Additional Premium, Gratuitous, Fare Paying Passengers, Andhra Pradesh Motor Vehicles Rules, 1964, R.T.A.

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 - Workmen's Compensation Act, 1923 - Motor Vehicles (Amendment) Act, 1994 - Motor Vehicles Act, 1939, - Andhra Pradesh Motor Vehicle Rules, 1964 - What is liability of insurance company to indemnity owner of vehicle in respect of death of passengers travelling in goods carriage? - Held, keeping in view provisions of 1988 Act, as provisions thereof do not enjoin any statutory liability on owner of a vehicle to get his vehicle insured for any passenger travelling in a goods vehicle, insurers would not be liable thereof - Furthermore, sub-cl. (i) of cl. (b) of s. 147 (1) speaks of liability which may be incurred by owner of a vehicle in respect of death of or bodily injury to any person or damage to any property of a third party caused by or arising out of use of vehicle in a public place, whereas sub-cl. (ii) thereof deals with liability which may be incurred by owner of a vehicle against death of or bodily injury to any passenger of a public service vehicle caused by or arising out of use of vehicle in a public place - Inevitable conclusion, therefore, is that provisions of Act do not enjoin any statutory liability on owner of a vehicle to get his vehicle insured for any passenger travelling in a goods carriage and insurer would have no liability therefore - Appeals allowed.
