Sarva Shramik Sanghatana (K.V) Mumbai v State Of Maharashtra And Others

Supreme Court of India

28 November 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Markandey Katju, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1166; (2008) 1 SCC 494; AIR 2008 SC 946; 2008(2) ALL MR 314; 2008 (3) Bom.C.R. 758; 2008 (2) LLJ 501; 2008 (1) MLJ(SC) 137; 2007(13) SCALE 525; 2007 (8) SCJ 819; [2007] 12 S.C.R. 645; 2008 (3) SLR 167

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Closure

Summary: Industrial Disputes Act - Voluntary Retirement Scheme - 275 employees did not accept the VRS and 230 of these were the petitioners - Writ petition praying that the Deputy Commissioner of Labour should be directed not to take any further proceedings in relation to the closure dismissed - Appeal against - Held, O. 23 r. 1(4) CPC states that where the plaintiff withdraws a suit without permission of the court, he is precluded from instituting any fresh suit in respect of the same subject matter - This provision will apply only to suits - An application under s. 25-O(1) is not a suit, and hence, the said provision will not apply to such an application - Appeal dismissed.
