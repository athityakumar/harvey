Shaikah Bakshu and Others v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

21 June 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 684; (2007) 11 SCC 269; JT 2007 (9) SC 342; 2007(8) SCALE 601; [2007] 7 S.C.R. 1130; 2007 (4) UC 429

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 r/w 34 - Appeal against conviction and sentence - Held, it has not been explained as to what was the necessity of a second dying declaration, if there was already a dying declaration in existence recorded by PW-3, who stated that he had accompanied PW-1 - Conclusion is clearly based on surmises and conjectures - Infirmities - Accusations of prosecution have not been established - Appeal allowed.
