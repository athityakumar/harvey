Indian Airlines Officers' Association and others v Indian Airlines Limited and another

Supreme Court of India

30 July 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

V.S. Sirpurkar, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1590; (2007) 10 SCC 684; AIR 2007 SC 2747; JT 2007 (9) SC 519; 2007 (3) LLJ 791; 2007(9) SCALE 479; [2007] 8 S.C.R. 655; 2007 (5) SLR 23

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Indian Airlines Recruitment and Promotion Rules, Indian Airlines Rules

Summary: Service - Air Corporations Act, 1953 - Vayudoot Pvt. Ltd. was converted into a Public Ltd. Company in 1983 and its shares were held by Indian Airlines and Air India jointly - Government of India took a decision on 25.05.1993 to merge Vayudoot with Indian Airlines - Pursuant thereto a circular dated 25.05.1994 was issued whereby a separate department was created in Indian Airlines called Short Haul Operations Department (SHOD) for absorption of Vayudoot employees - Out of total 1334 employees of Vayudoot, 311 employees were absorbed in Air India while remaining 1023 were absorbed in Indian Airlines - SHOD employees initially were not to be transferred from SHOD to Indian Airlines and no Indian Airlines employee was liable to be transferred to SHOD - Vayudoot employees who were placed in SHOD were to keep their independent identity - However, SHOD employees were not satisfied with this and started demanding some better chances by getting into mainstream of Indian Airlines - It is only because of this that a completely new and independent process was commenced holding several meetings, talks and ultimately a scheme was evolved for absorbing SHOD employees into mainstream of Indian Airlines - After deliberations in various meetings it was decided by a policy decision that they would have an option to join Indian Airlines subject to conditions and one of conditions was that cut off date was to be 10.3.1998 - It was decided that entry of SHOD employees would be in horizontal level and not at entry point of cadre - Writ petitions filed by Officers' Association of Indian Airlines and employees of Vayudoot Limited - Allowing the writ petitions Single Judge held that in ordering horizontal entry of then Vayudoot employees (now SHOD employees) into Indian Airlines, equal treatment would be given to unequals and therefore directed reconsideration of whole process - Hence, present appeals - (A) Different attitude taken in case of Air India and Indian Airlines - It was submitted that merely because a particular policy was taken in case of Air India would not by itself create any obligation that same kind of policy should be taken in case of Indian Airlines also - Held, it was rightly urged that entire process of merger of ex-Vayudoot employees and their absorption in Indian Airlines was a completely independent process - Hence, there was no discrimination nor a contradiction in stand taken by Government of India in case of Air India on one hand and Indian Airlines on other - Further, nothing wrong done in adopting two different methodologies in case of Air India and Indian Airlines - Merely because some of employees of Indian Airlines would suffer in terms of seniority and ultimately in terms of their further chances of promotion, whole scheme cannot be rejected as discriminatory or arbitrary - (B) Vayudoot Karamchari Sangh suggested that this is a merger of their seniority, therefore, cut off date of seniority should have been from 1994 and not from 1998 - Held, fixing cut-off dated on 10.03.1998 when broadly principles of merger were arrived at for first time after thorough discussions, would not be an arbitrary exercise - There was nothing wrong in fixing 10.03.1998 as cut-off date - It balanced equities between erstwhile Vayudoot employees and present Indian Airlines employees, inasmuch as though merger was five years old by then, Indian Airlines employees got five years advantage whereas Vayudoot employees had to sacrifice those five years in lieu of better deal of service they got because of merger - DB of HC was right in upsetting judgment of Single Judge - Appeals dismissed.
