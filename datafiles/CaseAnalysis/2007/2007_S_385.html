Kulwinder Singh v State Of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

06 August 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Markandey Katju, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 802; (2007) 2 SCC (Cr) 51; (2007) 10 SCC 455; AIR 2007 SC 2868; 2007 ALL MR (Cri) 3250; 2007 CRLJ 4290; 2007 (3) JCC 2346; JT 2007 (10) SC 35; 2007 (2) KarLJ 304; 2007 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 2365; 2007 (3) RCR(Criminal) 1029; 2007(9) SCALE 621; [2007] 8 S.C.R. 891

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Appeals Disposed Of, Finger Prints

Summary: Indian Penal Code, s. 302 - Conviction - High Court maintained conviction of appellant under s. 302, but set aside the death sentence and remitted the matter to the Sessions Judge to reconsider the matter of quantum of sentence - Appeal against - Held, no reason to disbelieve the evidence of Sarabjit Singh where he stated that he saw the appellant attacking Joginder Kaur inside the cattle shed and Hardip Kaur lying there with injuries - Fingerprints, locket, weapon and clothes recovered at the instance of the appellant also point to his guilt - Reduced the sentence to life imprisonment since it appears that crime was committed in a fit of passion and does not come within the category of 'rarest of rare' cases - Appeal disposed of.
