Mohd. Masood Ahmad v State Of U.P. And Ors

Supreme Court of India

18 September 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Markandey Katju, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 924; (2007) 8 SCC 150; (2007) 2 SCC (L&S) 806; 2008 (5) AWC 4656; 2007 (115) FLR 363; JT 2007 (11) SC 540; 2007 (4) KLT 457; 2008 (1) MLJ(SC) 1044; 2007(11) SCALE 271; 2007 (7) SCJ 216; [2007] 10 S.C.R. 72; 2007 (6) SLR 469; 2008 (1) UPLBEC 523

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: Petitioner challenging his transfer by the State Government by order dated 21.6.2005 as Executive Officer, Nagar Palika Parishad Mawana, District Meerut - Held, even if the allegation of the appellant is correct that he was transferred on the recommendation of an MLA, that by itself would not vitiate the transfer order - It is the duty of the representatives of the people in the legislature to express the grievances of the people and if there is any complaint against an official the State government is certainly within its jurisdiction to transfer such an employee - High Court has rightly dismissed the writ petition since transfer is an exigency of service and is an administrative decision - Appeal dismissed.
