Tanna and Modi v Commissioner of Income Tax and Others

Supreme Court of India

17 May 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, P.K. Balasubramanyan

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 906; (2007) 7 SCC 434; AIR 2007 SC 2301; [2007] 292 ITR 209; 2007(8) SCALE 511; 2007 (8) SCJ 644; [2007] 7 S.C.R. 233; [2007] 161 TAXMAN 329

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Voluntary Disclosure of Income Scheme, 1997, Voluntary Disclosure of Income Rules, 1997

Summary: Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Voluntary Disclosure of Income Scheme, 1997, ss. 63 and 64(2) - Income Tax - Whether certificate issued by Income Tax Commr. u/s. 68(2) of Voluntary disclosure scheme is null and void u/s. 64(2) and without complying with principle of natural justice on the ground of full immunity to do so? - Held, for construing immunity the objects of statute should not be frustrated and purposive construction should be applied - Further held, not fit for jurisdiction u/arts. 136 and 142 for doing complete justice to the parties - Appeal dismissed.
