Asokan v Lakshmikutty and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 December 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1340; (2007) 13 SCC 210; 2008 (70) ALR 311; 2008 (1) AWC 877; JT 2007 (13) SC 598; 2008 (1) KLT 54; 2008 (1) MLJ(SC) 193; 2008 (1) RCR(Civil) 507; 2007(14) SCALE 627; [2007] 13 S.C.R. 901

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Appellant filed a suit for a declaration that he was the absolute owner of the suit properties - Whether an averment made in the deed of gift in regard to handing over of possession is sufficient proof of acceptance thereof by the donee? - held, once a gift is complete, the same cannot be rescinded - For any reason whatsoever, the subsequent conduct of a donee cannot be a ground for rescission of a valid gift - Appeal allowed.
