Prabhakaran v State of Kerala

Supreme Court of India

21 June 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 685; (2007) 14 SCC 269; AIR 2007 SC 2376; 2008 ALL MR (Cri) 288; 2007 (3) JCC 1997; JT 2007 (9) SC 346; 2007 (3) RCR(Criminal) 605; 2007(8) SCALE 605; [2007] 7 S.C.R. 1141; 2007 (4) UC 496

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 304 part II - Appeal against conviction and sentence of five years custodial sentence - Held, s. 304-A applies to cases where there is no intention to cause death and no knowledge that the act done in all probability will cause death - Provision is directed at offences outside the range of ss. 299 and 300 IPC - The provision applies only to such acts which are rash and negligent and are directly cause of death of another person - Appropriate conviction would be under s. 304 A IPC and not s. 304 Part II - Appeal partly allowed.
