Holicow Pictures Private Limited v Prem Chandra Mishra and Others

Supreme Court of India

06 December 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1501; (2007) 14 SCC 281; AIR 2008 SC 913; 2008 (70) ALR 828; 2008 (1) CTC 711; 2008 (1) MLJ(SC) 1075; 2007(14) SCALE 10; [2007] 12 S.C.R. 1068

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Election - Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Electoral Politics - Allotment of Land -Irregularity/ illegality- Justifiability - Appellant/respondent No.5 was private limited company which represented through person and also takes part in electoral politics who addressed public meetings with present CM - Therefore, it was stated that Bihar Industrial Area Development Authority (Authority) had given large areas of land at various places to respondent no.5 at throwaway prices - Aggrieved by said act petitioner filed writ petition before HC alleged that land plots were given to respondent no.5 at different places in State without issuing any notice, inviting bids and at value far below market price of those land plots, (ii) allotment -was made without following any norms or procedure and (iii) action of State Govt. caused heavy loss to State and action was based on political considerations and was, therefore, malafide - Which quashed all allotments of lands made in favour of appellant/respondent No.5 and directed authorities to resume possession of said lands - Hence, instant appeal - Whether HC interference in said allotment and entertaining writ petition was justifiable - Held, relied on Ashok Kumar Pandey v. State of West Bengal,2003 Indlaw SC 1010, Dr. B. Singh v. Union of India & Ors. 2004 Indlaw SC 196 and Dattaraj Nathuji Thaware v. State of Maharashtra and Ors. 2004 Indlaw SC 1085 stated that it was true that in certain cases even though Court comes to conclusion that writ petition was not in public interest, yet if Court found that there was scope for dealing with matter further in greater public interest, it could be done by keeping writ petitioner out of picture and appointing amicus curiae and could only be done in exceptional cases and not in routine manner - Further, it was true as contented by appellant that HC conclusions were drawn after going through files - It was apparent from records that HC did not ask parties to clarify any doubt it entertained as regards certain crucial aspects - These aspects assume considerable importance because they had formed foundation of HC conclusions about irregularity/illegality in allotment - Hence, set aside HC 's Order and remit matter back to HC for fresh consideration - Appeal allowed.
