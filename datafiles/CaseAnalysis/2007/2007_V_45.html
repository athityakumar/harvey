Vijaykumar Baldev Mishra @ Sharma v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

18 May 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 592; (2007) 12 SCC 687; (2008) 3 SCC (Cr) 362; AIR 2008 SC 961; 2007 ALL MR (Cri) 1767; 2008 CRLJ 1409; 2007 (3) JCC 1941; JT 2007 (7) SC 206; 2007 (3) RCR(Criminal) 269; 2007(7) SCALE 486; [2007] 7 S.C.R. 601

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: TADA Act

Summary: Terrorist and Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Act; Indian Penal Code, ss. 302, 307 - Application for withdrawal from prosecution dismissed by TADA Court - Appeal against the order of TADA Court dismissed as not maintainable - Appeal against - Held, Designated Court was not correct in expressing its opinion in the merit of the matter and the effect of confessions made in terms of the provisions of TADA - It was also not necessary to consider as to whether, the action of the public prosecutor as also the State was bonafide or not - Application filed by the State for withdrawal of the charges under TADA against the appellant shall stand allowed - Appeal allowed.
