Gopal Singh v State Cadre Forest Officers' Association and Others

Supreme Court of India

15 May 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

V.S. Sirpurkar, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 675; (2007) 9 SCC 369; (2007) 2 SCC 819; AIR 2007 SC 1878; JT 2007 (7) SC 167; 2007(7) SCALE 202; 2007 (5) SCJ 252; [2007] 6 S.C.R. 586; 2007 (4) SLR 42

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Environment, Wildlife & Animal; Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Feeder Post, IFS (Appointment by Promotion) Regulations, 1966, Forest Department of Andaman & Nicobar Islands governed by Andaman Forest Department (Class I & Class II Gazetted Posts) Recruitment Rules, 1963, Indian Forest Service (Cadre) Rules, 1966, Andaman & Nicobar Forest Service Rules, 1991, IFS (Fixation of Cadre Strength) Regulations, 1966

Summary: Indian Forest Service (Appointment by Promotion) Regulations, 1966; the Indian Forest Service (Recruitment) Rules, 1966 - Service - Tribunal directed the respondents to take necessary steps forthwith for the consideration of the applicant's case for promotion to the post of DCF - High Court of Calcutta set aside the order of the Tribunal - Appeals against - Held, there is a clear cut finding by the High Court that there was no approval atleast none which was proved before the High Court - Rule clearly suggests that there would have to be approval for any service being included in the State Forest Service - Could not find a single reason in the whole judgment as to how the review was justified and for what reasons - Appeals dismissed.
