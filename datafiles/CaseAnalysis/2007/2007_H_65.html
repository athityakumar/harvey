Anuj Garg and Others v Hotel Association of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

06 December 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1359; (2008) 3 SCC 1; AIR 2008 SC 663; 2008 (146) DLT 347; JT 2007 (13) SC 320; 2008 (1) RCR(Civil) 240; 2007(13) SCALE 762; [2007] 12 S.C.R. 991; 2008 (2) SLR 472

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Maternity Benefit Act, 1961, Chotanagpur Tenancy Act, 1908, Universal Declaration of Human Rights Act, 1948, Admiralty Court Act, 1861, Black Administration Act, 1927, General Child Care Benefits Act

Summary: Punjab Excise Act, 1914 - Constitutional validity of s. 30 prohibiting employment of "any man under the age of 25 years" or "any woman" in any part of such premises in which liquor or intoxicating drug is consumed by the public - Held, only on a pre-supposition that there is a possibility of some incident happening, it cannot be declared a law intra vires which is ex facie ultra vires - Occupation/service in the management of hotel industry is a specialized job - It requires specialized skill - To deprive a large section of successful young men and women from obtaining any job for which they have duly been trained, would be wholly unjust - State cannot invoke the doctrine of 'res extra commercium' in the matter of appointment of eligible persons - Appeals disposed of.
