Union of India and Another v Narendra Singh

Supreme Court of India

13 December 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

C.K. Thakker, J.M. Panchal

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1294; (2008) 1 SCC (L&S) 547; (2008) 2 SCC 750; AIR 2008 SC (Supp) 240; 2008 (1) ESC 52; 2008 (116) FLR 448; 2007(14) SCALE 364; [2007] 13 S.C.R. 504; 2008 (4) SLR 117; 2008 (3) WLN(SC) 64

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: By Transfer On Deputation, Fundamental Rules, 1922, The Indian Audit and Accounts Department (Senior Accountant) Recruitment Rules, 1988, U.P. Forest Service Rules, 1952

Summary: Fundamental Rules, 1922 - Service - Respondent was working as Accountant in the Office of the Accountant General and mistakenly promoted as Senior Accountant - Promotion cancelled - Tribunal allowed the petition - Appeal against - Held, it is not the case of the respondent that he had passed the Departmental Examination for Accountants - It is, thus clear that respondent was not qualified for promotion to the post of Senior Accountant under the Rules - Since the respondent did not fulfill the condition of Recruitment Rules and a large number of Accountants who had passed Departmental Examination were available and awaiting promotion, it would be against their interests to deprive them of promotion and to continue the respondent who had not passed the examination - Directed the appellants to continue the respondent as Senior Accountant (Functional) till he reaches the age of superannuation - He would be deemed to have retired as Accountant and not as Senior Accountant (Functional) and his pensionary and retiral benefits would be fixed accordingly by treating him as Accountant all throughout - Appeal partly allowed.
