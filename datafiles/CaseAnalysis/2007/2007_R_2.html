(1)Rajesh Kumar Daria;(2)Hari Om Awasthi & Ors. v Rajasthan Public Service Commission & Ors

Supreme Court of India

18 July 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

K.G. Balakrishnan, R.V. Raveendran, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1519; (2007) 8 SCC 785; AIR 2007 SC 3127; JT 2007 (10) SC 154; 2007(10) SCALE 50; 2007 (5) SLR 576

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Service

Keywords: Rajasthan Judicial Service Rules, 1955

Summary: Constitution -Rajasthan Judicial Service Rules, 1955, r. 9(3) - Judicial Services - Special Reservation and Social Reservation - Appointment of OBC candidates for the post of munsifs - Contented that Rajasthan Public Service Commission selected women in excess of their reservation quota - And challenged the application of vertical resevationinstead of horizontal reservation of 20% of women categorywise as porovided by the Rules - Appeal against dismissal of writ petition by High Court - Social reservations in favour of SC, ST and OBC were vertical reservations whereas special reservations in favour of handicapped, women etc. were horizontal reservations - Fact that merit list contained number of women equal to quota for 'General Category-Women' - But apart from general category , the next eleven posts were filled under special reservation for women - Held, selection amounted to treating 20% reservation for women as a vertical reservation - And contrary to r. 9 (3) of Rules - Reservation of women would be done on basis of horizontal reservation as it was special reservation different from social reservation - Appeal allowed.
