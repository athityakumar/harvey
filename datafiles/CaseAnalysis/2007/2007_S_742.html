Bishan Singh and Another v State

Supreme Court of India

09 October 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1020; (2007) 13 SCC 65; AIR 2008 SC 131; 2008 ALL MR (Cri) 577; JT 2007 (12) SC 15; 2007 (4) RCR(Criminal) 664; 2007(11) SCALE 726; [2007] 10 S.C.R. 798

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Eye- Witness

Summary: Indian Penal Code, ss. 147, 308/149 - Appeal against conviction and sentence - Held, before an accused can be held to be guilty under s. 308 IPC, it was necessary to arrive at a finding that the ingredients thereof, namely, requisite intention or knowledge was existing - There cannot be any doubt whatsoever that such an intention or knowledge on the part of the accused to cause culpable homicide is required to be proved - Present cases would fall under ss. 323 and 325 - Directed to pay a fine of Rs. 15, 000/- - Appeal partly allowed.
