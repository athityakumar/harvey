Food Corporation Of India And Others v Vikas Majdoor Kamdar Sahkari Mandli Limited

Supreme Court of India

12 November 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1587; (2007) 13 SCC 544; 2008 (1) AWC 495; JT 2007 (12) SC 517; 2008 (2) MLJ(SC) 857; 2008 (1) RCR(Civil) 284; 2007(13) SCALE 126; [2007] 11 S.C.R. 1038

Case Digest

Subject: Contract & Commercial

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Indian Contract Act, 1872, s. 70 - Principle of quantum meruit - Applicability of - Respondent filed a civil suit for injunction restraining the appellant No.1-Corporation and its functionaries from recovering and/or withholding any amount from the bills of the respondent and also for a declaration that action of appellant No.1-Corporation in recovering various amounts without deciding the rates for extra work was bad in law and for directing the appellant No.1-Corporation to make payment for the extra work at the rates demanded by the respondent - It was averred that as per the tender notice the respondent was required to handle 750 MT per day as per the charter party and for handling for which rate was fixed at Rs.108 per MT - Respondent claimed that its entitlement for enhanced transportation charges was Rs.45 per MT in place of Rs.15 per MT which was stipulated in the contract - Trial Judge recorded that respondent had not established by evidence any additional cost incurred by him for such additional discharge - Hence, the suit was dismissed - On appeal, HC reversed the judgment of the Trial Court and decreed the suit partly - Hence, present appeal - It was submitted that principles of quantum meruit u/s. 70 of the Act has no application in view of the specific contractual provisions - Held, principle of quantum meruit is often applied where for some technical reason a contract is held to be invalid - Principle has no application where there is a specific agreement in operation - A person who does work or who supplies goods under a contract, if no price is fixed, is entitled to be paid a reasonable sum for his labour and the goods supplied - If the work is outside the contract, the terms of the contract can have no application; and the contractor is entitled to be paid a reasonable price for such work as was done by him - If a party to a contract has done additional construction for another not intending to do it gratuitously and such other has obtained benefit, the former is entitled to compensation for the additional work not covered by the contract - If an oral agreement is pleaded, which is not proved, he will be entitled to compensation u/s. 70 - Payment under this section can also be claimed for work done beyond the terms of the contract, when the benefit of the work has been availed of by the defendant - In the present case, no material on record to show that extra expenditure had to be incurred for doing the extra work - Quoted rates in terms of the contract was Rs.108 per M.T. - Evidence has not been placed on record to justify the claim at the rate of Rs.215 - Respondent shall be paid at the rate of Rs.108 per M.T. in terms of the contract up to 750 M.T. and at the rate of Rs.215 per MT for quantum beyond that - Interest rate shall be 6% as fixed by the HC - Respondent-Society not entitled to any amount beyond the agreed amount of Rs.15 per M.T. for transportation - Appeals disposed of.
