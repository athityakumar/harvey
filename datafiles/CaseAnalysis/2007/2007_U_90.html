Union of India and Another v Central Electrical and Mechanical Engineering Service (Ce and Mes) Group 'A' (Direct Recruits) Association, Cpwd and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 November 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1082; (2008) 1 SCC 354; AIR 2008 SC 3; 2008 (5) AWC 4502; JT 2007 (13) SC 151; 2007(13) SCALE 23; 2007 (8) SCJ 269; [2007] 11 S.C.R. 863; 2008 (1) WLN(SC) 147

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Executive Instructions, Ministry of Urban Affairs and Employment (Department of Urban Development) Central Engineering (Civil) Group 'A' Service Rules, 1996

Summary: Ministry of Urban Affairs and Employment (Department of Urban Development) Central Engineering (Civil) Group 'A' Service Rules, 1996 - Service - Challenging an office order - Held, office orders are not statutory in character - They even ex-facie do not satisfy the requirements of Article 162 of the Constitution of India - Indisputably, the disciplines of Civil, Electrical and Mechanical in the Central Public Works Department are different and distinct - An executive order must be passed in conformity with the Rules - Power of the State Government to issue executive instructions is confined to filling up of the gaps or covering the area which otherwise has not been covered by the existing Rules - Appeal dismissed.
