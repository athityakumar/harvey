United India Insurance Company Limited v Serjerao And Ors

Supreme Court of India

14 November 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1453; (2008) 3 SCC (Cr) 107; (2008) 7 SCC 425; 2009 (1) ACC 434; AIR 2008 SC 460; 2008 (70) ALR 146; 2008 (1) AWC 104; 2008 (1) Bom.C.R. 420; 2007 (1) CCC 627; 2008 (3) CGLJ 22; JT 2008 (1) SC 82; 2008 (2) MLJ(SC) 855; 2008 (1) RCR(Civil) 73; 2007(13) SCALE 80; 2007 (8) SCJ 612; [2007] 11 S.C.R. 1150

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Keywords: No Fault Liability

Summary: Insurance - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, ss. 148, 160 and 174 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Whether Tribunal is justified in holding Insurance Company as liable for paying compensation without any fault from side of Insurance Company and whether application u/s. 173 is maintainable in Court? - Held, no statutory liability of insurance Company established and application u/s. 173 is maintainable before Tribunal - Further held, liability regarding labourers traveling in trollies Insurance Company has no liability - Matter remitted to High Court for fresh consideration - Appeal disposed of.
