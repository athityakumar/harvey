U.P. Forest Corporation And Anr. v Dy. Commissioner Of Income Tax, Lucknow

Supreme Court of India

27 November 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhan, Altamas Kabir

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1498; (2007) 14 SCC 610; [2008] 297 ITR 1; 2007(13) SCALE 659; [2007] 12 S.C.R. 601; [2007] 165 TAXMAN 533

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Exemption From Payment, U.P. Forest Corporation Act, 1974

Summary: Income Tax - Section 10(20), 11(1)(a), Income Tax Act, 1961 - Exemption - Charitable institution - Non-registration - Corporation's income was assessed by making some additions of income and deleting some deductions claimed in the return of income - On appeal, the High Court declared that the Corporation was a 'local authority' and was entitled to exemption under s. 10(20) and also under s. 11(1)(a) being a charitable institution - Held, for claiming benefit under s. 11(1)(a), registration under s. 12A is a condition precedent - Keeping in view the fact that the appellant-Corporation has not been granted registration under s. 12A, the appellant is not entitled to claim exemption from payment of tax under s. 11(1)(a) and 12 - Directions issued.
