Inder Jeet Khurana v State of Haryana and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 February 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, G.P. Mathur

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1546; (2007) 3 SCC 102; AIR 2007 SC 1190; JT 2007 (3) SC 46; 2007 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 655; 2007 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 671; 2007(2) SCALE 430

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Haryana Revenue (Group/B) Service Rules, 1988, Punjab Tehsildari Rules, 1932

Summary: Service - Administrative - Practice & Procedure - Punjab Land Revenue Act, 1887, s. 9 - Haryana Revenue (Group/B) Service Rules, 1988 , r. 21 - Punjab Tehsildari Rules, 1932 , r. 6 - Transfer - Promotion - Validity - Sustainability - Appellants were working as Ziledars filed writ petitions for quashing recruitment by 'transfer' of Ziledars B-Class Tehsildars and recruitment of candidates 'by transfer' - Engineer-in-Chief recommended names of 39 Ziledars and three appellants who were not selected - State Govt. determined ratio for sharing vacancies as 50% for Ziledars from Irrigation Department and 50% for other four categories - Financial Commissioner accepted merit list and appointed AKG, SLP and BD who received highest marks among Ziledars, as Tehsildars - Appointments were challenged by appellants in three writ petitions - HC found no infirmity in selection of candidates by assessing merit of candidates - HC dismissed all three writ petitions - Hence, instant appeals - Whether order passed by HC was justified - Held, selection by transfer should be by 'seniority-cum-merit' or 'seniority subject to fitness' was rejected - Contention was urged that confidential reports were not properly scrutinized and, marks were wrongly awarded for ACRs - Court held that HC had secured original records and examined them and ascertained marks obtained by candidates - After such examination, HC had recorded finding that selected candidates had secured more marks and that there was no error or infirmity in assigning marks - Original record was produced and no infirmity was shown in marks assigned - During pendency of appeal, appellant had retired and, therefore, considering case for appointment as Tahsildar by transfer, was not considered and contention raised by appellant that he was not given proper marks in interview was, thus, without merit - Court held that 50% of posts of Tahsildars would be filled by transfer from category of 'Ziledars' and remaining 50% from other categories - Appellants, who were Ziledars, were not affected by selection of those candidates falling under other categories, could have no legitimate grievance on that behalf - Appeals dismissed.
