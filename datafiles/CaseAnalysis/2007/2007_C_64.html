Commissioner of Central Excise, Chennai-I v Chennai Petroleum Corporation Limited

Supreme Court of India

19 April 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, B. SUDARSHAN REDDY

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 403; (2007) 13 SCC 393; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 1342; 2007 (211) E.L.T. 193; JT 2007 (5) SC 614; 2007(5) SCALE 851; [2007] 5 S.C.R. 322

Case Digest

Subject: Excise

Summary: Assessee, a refinery and deemed warehouse, produced naptha, sulphur and electricity products from Refinery Fuel Oil (RFO) - Department contented that none of the three products, were petroleum products and, therefore, the refinery was liable to pay duty - Demands were made on the assessee to pay arrears of duty for the period December 1993 to July 1998 and for the period August 1998 to January 1999 - December 1993 to July 1998 (five years) came under show-cause notice dated 22nd December, 1998 whereas the period August 1998 to January 1999 came under show cause notice dated 17th February, 1999 - Whether the Department was entitled to invoke the extended period of limitation under Section 11A with show cause notice dated 22nd December, 1998 - Tribunal rightly held naptha to be a petroleum product and sulphur to be bye-product and thus exempted them from duty - Electricity sold to a Tamil Nadu Electricity Board not to be exempted from duty - Held, Department was wrong in invoking the extended period of limitation under the show-cause notice dated 22nd December, 1998 for the period December 1993 to July 1998, demand to that extent is beyond limitation - Appeals disposed of.
