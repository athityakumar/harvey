Kusheshwar Prasad Singh v State of Bihar and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 March 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

C.K. Thakker, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 248; (2007) 11 SCC 447; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 122; JT 2007 (5) SC 237; 2007(4) SCALE 534; 2007 (6) SCJ 765; [2007] 4 S.C.R. 95

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Bihar Land Reforms (Fixation of Ceiling Area and Acquisition of Surplus Land) Act, 1961, Matter remitted to the High Court

Summary: Bihar Land Reforms (Fixation of Ceiling Area and Acquisition of Surplus Land) Act, 1961 - Allegation that the landholder possessed excess land - Proceedings started - Held, proceedings had been initiated in 1973- 74 and final order was passed on January 7, 1976 - Final statement as required by s. 11(1) ought to have been issued and effect ought to have been given to the final order - Appellant is justified in urging that such default in discharge of statutory duty by the respondents under the Act cannot prejudice him - Matter remitted to High Court.
