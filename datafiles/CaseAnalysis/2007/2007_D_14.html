Heinz Italia and Another v Dabur India Limited

Supreme Court of India

18 May 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

H.S. Bedi, B.P. Singh

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1005; (2007) 6 SCC 1; 2007 (68) ALR 658; 2007 (3) AWC 3155; 2007 (6) MahLJ 7; 2007 (5) MLJ(SC) 594; 2007 (35) PTC 1; 2007 (3) RCR(Civil) 365; 2007 (3) RCR(Criminal) 290; 2007(7) SCALE 608

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Intellectual Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Discretionary Relief, Generic Word, Suit For Permanent Injunction, Similar Mark, Actual Damage, Phonetic Similarity

Summary: Appeal to challenge order rejecting application under O. 39, rr. 1 and 2 of CPC for grant of interim injunction restraining respondent from using trademark "Glucose-D" as it was deceptively similar to petitioner's registered trademark "Glucon-D"- Whether "Glucose-D" is deceptively similar to "Glucon-D"? - Held, both Glucon-D and Glucose-D are items containing glucose and it appears that there is remarkable phonetic similarity in these two words - Colour scheme of Glucose-D and Glucon-D is almost identical with happy family superimposed on both; packaging of Glucose-D is Glucon-D is so similar that it can easily confuse a purchaser - Appeal allowed.
