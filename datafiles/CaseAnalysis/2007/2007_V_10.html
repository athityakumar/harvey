Purnendu Mukhopadhyay And Others v V.K. Kapoor And Another

Supreme Court of India

12 October 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1616; (2008) 14 SCC 403; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 1226; 2008 (5) AWC 4614; 2008 (1) CalLT(SC) 22; JT 2007 (12) SC 439; 2007(12) SCALE 549; [2007] 11 S.C.R. 462; 2007 (6) SLR 740

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Seniority - Promotion - The appellants, who appeared in the examination for appointment to the post of Charge-man Grade II, were appointed as Supervisor Grade-A on account of the marginal difference in the marks obtained by them - The Director General vide order dt. 4.05.1967 directed that the appellants were to be given another chance to appear in the examination to enable them to be graded as Charge-man Grade-II, but the said order was not implemented and a policy of pick and choose was adopted to grant the benefit - Later, both posts merged as Charge-man Grade-II - The issue of the seniority led to the litigation - The Tribunal allowed the claim of appellants and directed to refix their notional seniority as Charge-man Grade II as if all the appellants came out successful in the selection test for promotion to the post of Charge-man Grade-II, from their respective dates of examination - Benefit of order of the Tribunal given to some employees and not to others on the plea of interpretation as regards concept of notional seniority in the judgment of the Tribunal - Appellants filed appeal against said order - Whether department was justified in giving benefits of order of the tribunal to some employees and not to others on the plea of interpretation? - Held, the action on the part of the department giving benefit of the order of the Tribunal to some employees and denying the same to the appellants was wholly unjustifiable - The judgment of a court, as was well known, should not be read as a statute - It has to be read in its entirety - Hence, appellants were entitled to the grant of benefits of order of the tribunal - There was no reason why the same should not be entitled to them - Appeal allowed.
