State of Uttar Pradesh v Atar Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 November 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1129; (2007) 14 SCC 193; AIR 2008 SC 411; 2007 ALL MR (Cri) 3593; JT 2007 (12) SC 445; 2007 (4) RCR(Criminal) 1049; 2007(13) SCALE 30; [2007] 11 S.C.R. 1018

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Inimical Witness, Non Explanation, Injuries On Accused, U.P. Police Regulation

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Appeal against acquittal - Held, order of acquittal shall not be interfered with because the presumption of innocence of the accused is further strengthened by acquittal - Circumstances highlighted by the High Court may not be sufficient to direct acquittal - Non-explanation of injuries by the prosecution will not affect prosecution case where injuries sustained by the accused are minor and superficial or where the evidence is so clear and cogent, so independent and disinterested, so probable, consistent and creditworthy, that it outweighs the effect of the omission on the part of prosecution to explain the injuries - Appeal dismissed.
