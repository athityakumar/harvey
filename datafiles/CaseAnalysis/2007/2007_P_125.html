Sohan Singh Sodhi v Punjab State Electricity Board, Patiala

Supreme Court of India

09 May 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 503; (2007) 2 SCC 198; (2007) 5 SCC 528; AIR 2007 SC 2713; 2007(6) ALL MR 436; 2007(7) SCALE 63; 2007 (4) SCJ 736; [2007] 6 S.C.R. 250; 2007 (6) SLR 91

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Electricity & Energy; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: Electricity (Supply) Act, 1948, s. 15 - Suit questioning the purported discrimination in promotion of Lineman to Line Superintendent between diploma holder linemen vis.-'-vis. non diploma holder linemen - Held, in absence of any rules or regulations governing the service conditions of its employees, issuance of administrative order is permissible in law - Jurisdiction of the Board to lay down different scales of pay for the employees on the basis of educational qualification per se is not discriminated - Parity in the pay cannot be claimed when the educational qualification is different - Appeal dismissed.
