Janatha Cashew Exporting Company v Commissioner of Income Tax, Trivandrum

Supreme Court of India

13 November 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, B. Sudershan Reddy

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1479; [2008] 166 TAXMAN 88

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Appeal allowed, Income Tax Act, 1961, Export House, Exports

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961 - Assessment - Exports - Sales - Deduction - Assessee was a cashew exporter - It had made direct and indirect exports for assessment year and had claimed total deduction of an amount of Rs.97,54,515 u/ss. 80HHC(1) and 80HHC(1A) Act of 1961 - Assessing Officer granted deduction u/ss. 80HHC(1) and 80HHC(1A) in respect of direct and indirect exports in all amounting to Rs. 91,10,306 as against claim of Rs. 96,54,515 - While granting deduction u/s. 80HHC(3) Assessing Officer excluded sales to export houses from export turnover and reworked relief at Rs. 12,63,532 - Order of Assessing Officer was upheld that export turnover included only direct exports since s.80 HHC(3) dealt with quantification of deduction in case of direct exports and quantum of deduction had to be computed only on basis of direct export turnover - Tribunal assumed Assessing Officer should have recomputed income of assessee and allow benefits admissible to export house if such export house had issued a disclaimer certificate - Decision of Tribunal was however set aside by HC - Hence instant appeal - Whether assessee was entitled to benefit of proviso to s.80HHC(3) Act of 1961 - Held that there was no factual finding recorded by HC as to sales made through export houses by assessee is supported by a disclaimer certificate from such export houses - u/s. 80HHC(3) if assessee is a supporting manufacturer on his producing such disclaimer certificate assessee would be entitled to claim the benefit of deduction u/s. 80HHC(3) - Appeal allowed.
