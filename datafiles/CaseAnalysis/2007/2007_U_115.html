Kenailal Bera v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

24 September 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1420; (2007) 11 SCC 517; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 1000; 2008 (5) AWC 4827; 2007 (3) CLR 830; 2007 (115) FLR 505; JT 2007 (11) SC 441; 2007(11) SCALE 656; 2007 (7) SCJ 674; [2007] 10 S.C.R. 612; 2008 (1) UPLBEC 460

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Defence & Security Forces; Service

Keywords: Central Reserve Police Force Rules, 1955

Summary: Defence & Security Forces - Central Reserve Police Force Rules, 1955, r. 27 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 142 - Central Reserve Police Force Act, 1949 - Army Act, 1950 - (A)Whether appellant should be reinstated in service and be given all other consequential benefits? - No interfere with quantum of punishment but keeping in view fact that disciplinary authority must be held to have misdirected itself by not complying with r. 27 of Central Reserve Police Force Rules stricto sensu and having directed a further inquiry after ordering for dismissal of services of appellant, in peculiar facts and circumstances of this case which may not be treated to be a precedent, an appropriate order in exercise of discretionary jurisdiction u/art. 142 of Constitution can be passed - Held, appellant should be directed to be reinstated in service - (B)Whether appellant should be granted back wages? - Where illegality committed by disciplinary authority is apparent on its face, appellant should not be denied back wages otherwise back wages cannot be directed to be granted automatically - Several factors are required to be taken into consideration therefor - Further held, appellant is denied back wages for said period - Appeal allowed.
