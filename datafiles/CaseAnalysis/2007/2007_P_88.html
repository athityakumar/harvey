Manager, ICICI Bank Limited v Prakash Kaur and Others

Supreme Court of India

26 February 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

AR. Lakshmanan, Altamas Kabir

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 641; (2007) 2 SCC 711; AIR 2007 SC 1349; 2007 (3) ALD(SC) 79; 2007 (67) ALR 179; 2007 (2) BC 226; [2007] 136 Comp Cas 327; 2007 (1) CompLJ 561; 2007 (2) CTC 334; 2007 (138) DLT 248; 2007 (1) DRTC 685; 2007 (1) JCC 681; 2007 (3) JhrCR(SC) 175; JT 2007 (4) SC 39; 2007 (1) KarLJ 846; 2007 (2) MLJ(SC) 854; 2007 (2) RCR(Criminal) 76; 2007(3) SCALE 507; [2007] 3 S.C.R. 253; 2007 (2) UC 850

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance

Keywords: Default, Commercial Banks, Debt Recovery

Summary: Banking & Finance - Criminal - Recovery of loan - Held, practice of hiring recovery agents, who are muscleman, is deprecated and needs to be discouraged - Bank should resort to procedure recognized by law to take possession of vehicles in cases where borrower may have committed default in payment of installments instead of taking resort to strong arm tactics - Very many banks and more importantly banks like ICICI have extended liberal credit facilities for purchase of vehicles whether two wheelers or four wheelers, more number targets are achieved - This results in a certain amount of default cases - Default can be two-fold (1) genuine and (2) fraudulent - Both, in case of genuine and fraudulent method usually adopted by these institutions is to engage thug/hooligan/gangster for recovery or two wheelers or four wheelers - Many times even notice is not given to them - They seize vehicles even in public places deliberately to cause embarrassment - Detailed suggestions by Supreme Court - Governed by a rule of law in country - Recovery of loans or seizure of vehicles could be done only through legal means - Banks cannot employ goondas to take possession by force - In present case, matter compromised between Bank and customer - Appeal allowed.
