Jitendra Kumar and others v State of Haryana and another

Supreme Court of India

11 December 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1605; (2008) 2 SCC 161; AIR 2008 SC (Supp) 1256; 2008 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 126; 2008 (3) LLN 605; 2007(14) SCALE 125; [2007] 13 S.C.R. 98; 2008 (3) SLR 139

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service

Keywords: Punjab Civil Service (Executive Branch) Rules, 1930, Haryana Civil Services (Executive Branch) and Allied Services and Other Services Common/ Combined Examination Act, 2002

Summary: Service - Constitution - Haryana Civil Services (Executive Branch) and Allied Services and Other Services Common/Combined Examination Act, 2002, s. 4 - Punjab Civil Service (Executive Branch) Rules, 1930 - Haryana civil services - Issuance of letters of appointment - State of Haryana sent a requisition to the Public Service Commission (PSC) in or about December, 2003 for filling up 58 posts in HCS (Executive Branch) and 44 posts in Allied Services - Pursuant to or in furtherance of the said requisition received by the PSC, an advertisement was issued and the written examination was conducted - After completion of all the stages of competitive examination for the same, the selected candidates (Petitioners) were recommended by the PSC - Meanwhile due to assembly election, Model Code of Conduct became effective and ban was imposed on issuance of appointment letters to the candidates selected by the PSC without its permission till the completion of the election process - Subsequently, writ petitions were filed levelling serious allegation against the then Chief Minister and the Chairman of the PSC in respect of selection - Vigilance inquiry was also directed to be conducted - After election, the new Government took over - As appointment letters to the petitioners were not issued, petitioners filed writ petitions complaining undue delay on the part of the State in issuing the offers of appointment - New Government by a notification dt. 13.05.2005, reduced the cadre strength - Petitioners amended the writ petitions challenging the said notification - HC dismissed said writ petitions on the ground that the efficacy of the earlier selection was doubtful and vigilance enquiry was pending - Hence, the present appeals - Whether HC rightly dismissed the writ petition filed by the petitioners? - Held, HC for good and sufficient reasons, was of the opinion that the State had acted bona fide in issuing the said notification dt. 13.05.2005 - There cannot be any doubt whatsoever that the State in absence of any other factor was obligated to make appointments keeping in view the reduced cadre strength - The PSC holds a constitutional duty to see that the entire selection process is carried out strictly in accordance with law fairly, impartially and independently - The selectors appointed by the PSC or its Chairman and members are forbidden to take recourse to favouritism - In the present case, unsuccessful candidates have leveled serious allegations against the members of the Commission - They may or may not be correct - The Vigilance Bureau has initiated an enquiry into the whole matter - Such an enquiry should be allowed to be continued unless the State in terms of the report made by the Vigilance Bureau and upon making an enquiry of its own satisfies itself that the selection process was not tainted - Its disinclination to make an appointment till then cannot be found fault with - Therefore, it is difficult to hold that the decision of the State was either mala fide or unreasonable or unfair or arbitrary - No case has been made out for interference with the impugned judgment of the HC - Appeals dismissed.
