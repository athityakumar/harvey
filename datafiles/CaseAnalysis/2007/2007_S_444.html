Jesu Asir Singh And Others v State Through Inspector Of Police

Supreme Court of India

20 August 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 824; (2007) 12 SCC 19; AIR 2007 SC 3015; 2007 ALL MR (Cri) 2595; 2007 (3) ALT 267; 2007 CRLJ 4310; 2007 (4) JCC 2629; JT 2007 (10) SC 262; 2007 (4) RCR(Criminal) 38; 2007(10) SCALE 110; 2007 (7) SCJ 868; [2007] 9 S.C.R. 92

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Non- Explanation

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 341 - Appeal against conviction and sentence - Held, mere non-explanation of the injuries by the prosecution may not affect the prosecution case in all cases - To claim a right of private defence extending to voluntary causing of death, the accused must show that there were circumstances giving rise to reasonable grounds for apprehending that either death or grievous hurt would be caused to him - No evidence much less cogent and credible was adduced in this regard - Appeal dismissed.
