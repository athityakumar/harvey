M/S. Hamdard (Wakf) Laboratories v Deputy Labour Commr. And Others

Supreme Court of India

27 April 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 460; (2007) 2 SCC (L&S) 166; (2007) 5 SCC 281; AIR 2008 SC 968; 2007 (6) AWC 6141; 2007 (114) FLR 58; JT 2007 (6) SC 329; 2007 (3) LLJ 1; 2007 (4) LLN 73; 2007(6) SCALE 423; 2007 (5) SCJ 539; [2007] 5 S.C.R. 873; 2007 (5) SLR 352; 2007 (2) UPLBEC 1928

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Keywords: Deferred Wages, Back-Wages, Publication Of Award, Cost Of Living, Employed Person, Available Surplus, U.P. Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, Over-Time, Payment of Wages (Amendment) Act, 1957

Summary: Uttar Pradesh Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - s. 2 cl. (y) - Payment of Bonus Act - s. 2 sub-s.21 - Interpretation of 'bonus' - Contention of the appellant that the Labour Commissioner committed a manifest error in directing payment on the basis that it was deferred wages when wages contained in s. 2(y) of the Act and s. 2(21) of the Payment of Bonus Act, in terms whereof bonus is neither wages nor allowance - Held, bonus may be a deferred wage but the same must be construed in a different context, when used in the context of 'backwages' and that too 50% of it, the same would not include backwages, bonus either in its ordinary meaning or statutory ones would not include wages, definition of 'wages' within the meaning of the Act does not include "bonus", it however, includes allowance, Payment of Bonus Act also excludes bonus for the purpose of calculating the amount of bonus to be determined in terms of Section 10 thereof - Appeal allowed
