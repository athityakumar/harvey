National Insurance Co. Ltd. v Mubasir Ahmed & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

01 February 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 79; (2007) 2 SCC 349; (2007) 1 SCC (L&S) 643; 2007 (2) ACC 374; AIR 2007 SC 1208; 2007 (1) CLR 683; JT 2007 (3) SC 176; 2007 (3) KLT 26; 2007 (1) LLJ 1035; 2007 (3) MLJ(SC) 49; 2007 (146) PLR 188; 2007(2) SCALE 455; [2007] 2 S.C.R. 117; 2007 (2) UPLBEC 1138

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Temporary Disablement

Summary: Workmen's Compensation Act, 1923 - Respondent claiming compensation for alleged personal injuries sustained in course of employment - High Court held that there was 100% loss of earning capacity and, therefore, awarded compensation and also directed grant of interest @ 12% p.a. from date of accident till actual realization - Appeal against - Held, doctor had taken note of the relevant factors relating to loss of earning capacity - Without indicating any reason or basis the High Court held that there was 100% loss of earning capacity - Since no basis was indicated in support of the conclusion, same cannot be maintained - Rate of 12% as fixed by the High Court cannot be faulted - But the period as fixed by it is wrong - Starting point is on completion of one month from the date on which it fell due - Appeals partly allowed.
