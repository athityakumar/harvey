Food Corporation of India and Others v Parashotam Das Bansal and Others

Supreme Court of India

05 February 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1725; (2008) 5 SCC 100; AIR 2008 SC (Supp) 1469; 2008(2) ALL MR 821; 2008 (2) CalLT(SC) 23; 2008 (5) ESC 748; 2008 (117) FLR 136; 2008 (2) MLJ 1009; 2008(2) SCALE 205; [2008] 2 S.C.R. 412; 2008 (3) SLJ 13; 2008 (2) SLR 294; 2008 (1) UPLBEC 625

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Food Corporations Act, 1964, FCI (Staff) Regulations

Summary: Service - Food Corporation of India Act, 1964 - Assured Career Progression (ACP) - Entitlement - No promotional avenues for respondent/Engineers in appellant/Corporation - Respondent sought the benefit of ACP scheme - Appellant requested the Union of India for formulating a scheme of ACP for respondents, however, Union of India rejected appellant's request - Respondents filed writ petition - Single Judge directed the appellant to recommend the case of respondents to Union of India for ACP - DB, in appeal, affirmed the Single Judge order - Hence the present appeal - (A) Whether HC has jurisdiction to direct the appellant to formulate a scheme of ACP for respondents - Held, when employees are denied an opportunity of promotion for long years on the ground that they fell within a category of employees excluded from promotional prospect, the Superior Court will have the jurisdiction to issue necessary direction - Further, if there is no channel of promotion in respect of a particular group of officers resulting in stagnation over the years, the Court although may not issue any direction as to in which manner a scheme should be formulated or by reason thereof interfere with the operation of existing channel of promotion to the officers working in different departments and officers of the Government but the jurisdiction to issue direction to make a scheme cannot be denied to a Superior Court of the country - Thus, the HC has the jurisdiction to issue the impugned direction - (B) Whether the respondents are entitled to the benefit of ACP - Held, respondent/Engineers constitute about one per cent of the total work force of appellant/Corporation - A huge financial benefit was given to unionized employees in other posts constituting 85 per cent strength of the total work force such as ACP scheme, selection grade etc. - Although appellant alleged that the promotional avenues alleged to be existed for the cadre of Engineers, such stand was not taken before the Single Judge - A ground of availability of promotional avenues to respondents was taken in the Memorandum of the Letters Patent Appeal before the DB, however, the same was not pressed - Contention of the appellant that the respondents did not contribute anything towards the main functions of the appellant/Corporation, is untenable - An employee is an employee and how the employees would be structured is undoubtedly within the realm of the statutory authority but by reason thereof, it cannot tinker with their essential fundamental right of being considered for the promotion - No merit in the appeal - Appeal dismissed.
