A.P. Housing Board v Mohammad Sadatullah and Others

Supreme Court of India

13 April 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

C.K. Thakker, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 375; (2007) 6 SCC 566; JT 2007 (8) SC 220; 2007(5) SCALE 681; 2007 (6) SCJ 393; [2007] 5 S.C.R. 107

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Land & Property

Keywords: Jagirdar, Andhra Pradesh Housing Board Act, 1962

Summary: Andhra Pradesh Land Grabbing (Prohibition) Act, 1982 ; Constitution of India, 1950 - Writ petition - Notices were issued - Award passed directing that an amount of Rs.50, 094/- should be paid to the land-owners to evict the hut dwellers and deliver possession of the said land to the Housing Board - Land-owners had not received compensation of Rs.50, 094/- in respect of two acres of land - Whether the petitioners be treated as land grabbers- Special court exceeded it's jurisdiction - Held that, the High Court observed that no land could be acquired without payment of compensation - No order as to payment of compensation could be made subject to condition of handing over possession by the owner - The petitioners had no title over the petition schedule land - Could not be treated as land grabbers - Not entitled to any relief - the Special Court exceeded its jurisdiction in entering into the question of adverse possession which could be decided only by a Civil Court.
