Uttranchal Forest Development Corporation v M.C. Joshi

Supreme Court of India

23 February 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 149; (2007) 9 SCC 353; (2007) 2 SCC (L&S) 813; 2007 (5) AWC 5352; 2007 (3) CLR 84; 2007 (113) FLR 191; 2007 (2) LLJ 390; 2007 (3) LLN 51; 2007(3) SCALE 545; [2007] 3 S.C.R. 114; 2007 (3) SLR 864

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: U.P. Forest Development Corporation Act

Summary: U.P. Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 6N - Service - Respondent was employed as a daily wager - Termination - Labour Court directed the reinstatement of the respondent with 50% of back wages with effect from 9.9.1997 - High Court reduced the back wages to 25% - Appeal against - Held, keeping in view the nature and period of services rendered by the respondent as also the period during which he had worked and the fact that he had raised an industrial dispute after six years, interest of justice would be met if the impugned judgments are substituted by an award of compensation for a sum of Rs. 75, 000/- in favour of the respondent - Appeal partly allowed.
