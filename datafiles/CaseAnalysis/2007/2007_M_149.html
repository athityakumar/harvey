Reliance Energy Limited and Another v Maharashtra State Road Development Corporation

Supreme Court of India

11 September 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, Arijit Pasayat

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1544; (2007) 8 SCC 1; 2007 (6) Bom.C.R. 677; JT 2007 (11) SC 1; 2007(11) SCALE 49; [2007] 9 S.C.R. 853

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Disposal Of Property, Reserve Fund, Cash Flow Statement

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 19(1)(g), 21 and 226 - Tender - Disqualification - Legality - Maharashtra State Road Development Corporation Ltd. (MSRDC) floated Global Tender for completing Mumbai Trans Harbour Link - Bidders were required to submit Request for Qualification Document and under the Pre-Qualification Document, the bidders were required to submit financial statements of three financial years - REL/HDEC formed a consortium - As a consortium they were required to comply with clause 7.2.2 which stipulated net cash profit at Rs. 200 crores - REL/HDEC did not submit audited accounts concerning HDEC for the financial year ending 31.12.2004 by 10.1.2005, REL/HDEC stood excluded from the second stage of the bidding process - HC held that the decision of MSRDC on the future cash impact of 'the provision for bad debts' made by HDEC in its accounts for 2001 cannot be said to be arbitrary or unreasonable - Whether provision for bad debts is or is not a 'non-cash expense' liable to be added back to arrive at net cash profit? - Only reason for excluding REL/HDEC is the future cash impact of the provision made in the accounts of HDEC for the FY 2001 - Held, 'operating cash profit' can be derived by either 'Direct Method' or by 'Indirect Method' also known as 'Reconciliation Method' - No reason given by the Consultants of MSRDC for rejecting the indirect method invoked by KPMG, Chartered Accountants of REL/HDEC - Further, in the evaluation process, the Consultants were entitled to take into account future cash impact but in order to do so they had to say why the indirect method of 'cash flow reporting' should not be accepted and if at all the impact of the provisioning was to be seen then there was no reason for not examining the audited accounts of 2004 - Very purpose of 'cash flow reporting' is to find out the ability of HDEC to generate cash flow in future and if an important method of cash flow reporting is kept out, without any reason, then the decision to exclude REL/HDEC, is arbitrary, whimsical and unreasonable - For non-consideration of the Reconciliation Method, under cash flow reporting system, the impugned decision-making process stood vitiated - REL/HDEC (Consortium) was erroneously excluded from the second stage of bidding process - Appeal allowed.Banking & Finance - Operating cash profit - Direct and Indirect method - Difference - Held, in 'Direct Method' cash items of cash inflow are listed like cash received from customers, payment of interest etc. as against cash outflows like payment to supplier, payment for taxes etc. - 'Indirect Method' also known as 'Reconciliation Method' in which the 'operating cash profit' is derived by adding to the net profit non-cash items like provision for taxes, provision for doubtful debts, loss on sale of fixed assets and investments, depreciation, amortization of intangibles etc. because these items do not affect cash - However, both the methods give the same results in respect of the final total - Appeal allowed.
