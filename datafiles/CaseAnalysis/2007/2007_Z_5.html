Zuari Industries Limited v Commissioner of Central Excise and Customs

Supreme Court of India

29 March 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, B. Sudershan Reddy

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 631; (2007) 14 SCC 614; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 1424; 2007 (210) E.L.T. 648; JT 2007 (5) SC 418; 2007(5) SCALE 153; 2007 (4) SCJ 131; [2007] 4 S.C.R. 553

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Customs; Indirect Tax; Trade

Keywords: Collector Of Customs, Appeal allowed., Project Import, Project Import Regulations, 1986

Summary: Customs - Section, Customs Act, 1962 - Exemption - Notification No. 11/1997-Cus - Denial - Imports made for expansion of a Fertiliser Project - Captive Power Plant - Dispute related to rate of duty applicable - Essentiality Certificate issued by the Sponsoring Ministry - Department submitted that the fertilizer project and the Captive Power Plant are two distinct and separate projects as far as the rate of duty was concerned - Contention of the Department has been accepted by all the authorities below - Assessee submitted that it was not open to the Revenue to say that the Captive Power Plant was not an essential requirement for the expansion of the fertilizer project, once an essentiality certificate stands issued by the Sponsoring Ministry - Held, - Essentiality certificate is a proof that an item like Captive Power Plant in a given case could be treated as a capital goods for the fertilizer project depending upon the facts of each case - Essentiality certificate must be treated as a proof of fulfilment of the eligibility conditions by the importer for obtaining the benefit of the exemption notification - Although, an item like a power plant is a stand-alone equipment, but, when it becomes a part of the entire Project/System, the same power plant can also become one of the items of capital goods - Impugned judgment set aside.
