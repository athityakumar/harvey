Maksud Saiyed v State Of Gujarat And Ors

Supreme Court of India

18 September 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 936; (2008) 5 SCC 668; (2008) 2 SCC (Cr) 692; [2007] 140 Comp Cas 590; 2008 (1) CTC 259; 2008 (5) G.L.R. 3951; JT 2007 (11) SC 276; 2007 (4) RCR(Criminal) 406; 2007(11) SCALE 318; [2007] 9 S.C.R. 1113

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Criminal; Practice & Procedure; Tort

Keywords: Press Note, Suppression Of Facts

Summary: Application for recovery of a sum of Rs. 120.13 lakhs - Offence punishable under ss. 120B, 425, 191, 192, 177, 181 as also 500 of IPC - Application for quashing the complaint and the investigation allowed - Appeal against - Held, an inadvertent mistake committed by the bank in referring to the case being pending before DRT instead of City Civil Court cannot, give rise to a cause of action for filing a complaint petition far less under s. 500 of the Indian Penal Code particularly when the other particulars contained therein were not found to be incorrect - Vicarious liability of the Managing Director and Director would arise provided any provision exists in that behalf in the statute - Indian Penal Code does not contain any provision for attaching vicarious liability on the part of the Managing Director or the Directors of the Company when the accused is the Company - Appeal dismissed.
