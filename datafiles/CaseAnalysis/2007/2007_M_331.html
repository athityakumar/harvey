Directorate Of Revenue & Anr v Mohammed Nisar Holia

Supreme Court of India

05 December 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1251; (2008) 2 SCC 370; 2008 (3) CGLJ 55; 2008 (1) JCC (Narcotics) 1; JT 2007 (13) SC 408; 2008 (1) RCR(Criminal) 241; 2007(13) SCALE 744; [2007] 12 S.C.R. 906

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Health & Drug; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985 - Interpretation of the provisions of ss. 42 and 43 - Judgment of conviction and sentence passed by a Special Judge was reversed by High Court opining that as the statutory requirements of s. 42 had not been complied with, the judgment of the Trial Court could not be sustained - Appeal against - Held, statutory requirements had not been complied with as the person who had received the first information did not reduce the same in writing - An officer who received such information was bound to reduce the same in writing and not for the person who hears thereabout - Apart from proving the fax and the copy of a challan nothing else has been proved - Impugned judgment does not suffer from any legal infirmity - Appeal dismissed.
