Mohd. Shafi v Mohd. Rafiq and Another

Supreme Court of India

09 April 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1313; (2007) 14 SCC 544; AIR 2007 SC 1899; 2007 (3) ALT 107; 2007 CRLJ 3198; JT 2007 (5) SC 562; 2007 (2) RCR(Criminal) 762; 2007(5) SCALE 611; [2007] 4 S.C.R. 1023

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 307 and 324 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 161, 319 and 482 - Discretionary jurisdiction u/s. 319 of CrPC, 1973 - FIR lodged against appellant alleging commission of an offence u/ss. 307/324, IPC, 1860 - Death of injured - Case converted to one u/s. 302 - Application filed for summoning appellant u/s. 319, CrPC, 1973 - Application dismissed - High Court allowed appeal against same - Before Trial Court seeks to take recourse to s. 319 requisite ingredients therefore must be fulfilled - Commission of an offence by a person not facing trial, must, therefore, appears to court concerned - Discretion in this behalf must be judicially exercised, it is incumbent that court must arrive at its satisfaction in this behalf - Held, trial judge, in terms of s. 319, CrPC, 1973 was required to arrive at his satisfaction - If he thought that matter should receive his due consideration only after cross-examination of witnesses is over, no exception thereto could be taken far less at instance of a witness and when state was not aggrieved by same - Appeal allowed.
