Deddappa and Others v Branch Manager, National Insurance Company Limited

Supreme Court of India

12 December 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1297; (2008) 2 SCC 595; 2008 (1) ACC 1; 2008 (2) ACC 267; AIR 2008 SC 767; 2008(1) ALL MR 968; 2008 (70) ALR 148; 2008 (1) AWC 582; 2008 (2) G.L.R. 1774; 2008 (1) KLT 296; 2008 (2) MLJ(SC) 575; 2008 (149) PLR 775; 2008 (1) RCR(Civil) 402; 2007(14) SCALE 257; [2007] 13 S.C.R. 287

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Summary: Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, s. 166; Insurance Act, 1938 - Motor Accidents Claims - Held, if the contract of insurance has been cancelled and all concerned have been intimated thereabout, the insurance company would not be liable to satisfy the claim - Directed the Respondent No.1 to pay the amount of claim to the appellants and recover the same from the owner of the vehicle - Appeal allowed.
