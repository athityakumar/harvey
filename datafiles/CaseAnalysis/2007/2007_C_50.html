Prem Lala Nahata & Anr v Chandi Prasad Sikaria

Supreme Court of India

02 February 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

P.K. Balasubramanyan, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 181; (2007) 2 SCC 551; AIR 2007 SC 1247; 2007 (67) ALR 166; 2007 (1) AWC 772; 2007 (5) Bom.C.R. 220; 2007 (1) CHN 105; 2007 (3) CTC 101; 2007 (2) JhrCR(SC) 335; JT 2007 (3) SC 69; 2007 (1) KLT 910; 2007 (2) MLJ(SC) 1177; 2007 (1) RCR(Civil) 870; 2007 (102) RD 711; 2007(2) SCALE 496; [2007] 2 S.C.R. 261

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Frame Of Suit

Summary: Civil Procedure Code, O. 7 r. 11(d) - Suit for recovery of a sum - Division Bench held that suit was bad for misjoinder of causes of action and gave the appellants an opportunity to elect to proceed with the present suit at the instance of one of them and thus confine the plaint claim to one of them and the transaction relied on by that plaintiff - Appeal against - Held, objections regarding the frame of suit or joinder of parties only as procedural, is further clear from s. 99 of the Code which specifically provides that no decree shall be reversed in appeal on account of any misjoinder of parties or causes of action or non-joinder of parties unless a Court finds that the non-joinder is of a necessary party - Three suits have to be jointly tried and since the evidence, according to us, would be common in any event, the Division Bench was in error in directing the appellants to elect to proceed with one of the plaintiffs and one of the claims - Appeal allowed.
