Deepak Rajak v State of West Bengal

Supreme Court of India

14 June 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, B.P. Singh

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1018; (2007) 15 SCC 305; 2007 ALL MR (Cri) 2015; 2007 (3) CalLT(SC) 59; 2007 (3) JCC 1822; JT 2007 (9) SC 31; 2007 (3) RCR(Criminal) 375; 2007(8) SCALE 544B; [2007] 7 S.C.R. 1022

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, ss. 34, 120-B, 201 and 302 - Appellant faced trial along with several others for offences punishable u/ss. 302/34, 201/34 and s. 120(B) - Co-accused acquitted by Supreme Court in Mousam Singha Roy and Others v State of W B 2003 Indlaw SC 641 -Effect of such acquittal vis-a-vis similarly situated co-accused has been considered by this court in several cases - Held, a departure may be made in cases where accused had not surrendered after conviction in addition to not filing an appeal against conviction - But as in present case, after surrender, benefit of acquittal in case of co-accused on similar accusations can be extended after surrender, benefit of acquittal in case of co-accused on similar accusations can be extended - Conviction and sentence as recorded by Trial Court and upheld by High Court set aside - Appeal allowed.
