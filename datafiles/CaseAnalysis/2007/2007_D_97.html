Ishikawajma Harima Heavy Industries Limited v Director of Income Tax, Mumbai

Supreme Court of India

04 January 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 99; (2007) 3 SCC 481; AIR 2007 SC 929; [2007] 288 ITR 408; JT 2007 (1) SC 508; 2007(1) SCALE 140; 2007 (4) SCJ 151; [2007] 1 S.C.R. 112; 2007 (6) S.T.R. 3; [2007] 158 TAXMAN 259

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Advance Payment, Taxing Provisions, Accrual Or Arising, Non- Resident

Summary: Income Tax Act, 1961 - Offshore supply and offshore services - Issues are (a) the taxation of the price of goods supplied, by way of offshore supply price of which is specified in Ex. D, Clause 2.1; and (b) the taxation of consideration paid for rendition of services described in the contract as offshore services at Ex. D. - Held, distinction between the existence of a business connection and the income accruing or arising out of such business connection is clear and explicit - Permanent establishment's non- involvement in this transaction excludes it from being a part of the cause of the income itself, and thus there is no business connection - Only such part of the income, as is attributable to the operations carried out in India can be taxed in India - Transfer of property in goods as well as the payment, were carried on outside the Indian soil, the transaction could not have been taxed in India - Appeal partly allowed.
