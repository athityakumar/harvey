Government of Andhra Pradesh and Others v Mohd. Taher Ali

Supreme Court of India

09 October 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Mathur, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1492; (2007) 8 SCC 656; (2007) 2 SCC (L&S) 990; AIR 2008 SC 375; 2008 (1) ALD(SC) 100; 2008 (1) AWC 1085; 2007 (115) FLR 794; 2007(12) SCALE 320; 2007 (8) SCJ 482; [2007] 10 S.C.R. 929

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Compulsory retirement - Legality - Whether respondent is liable to penalty of compulsory retirement for absenting from election duty without permission? - Yes - There can be no hard and fast rule that merely because earlier misconduct has not been mentioned in chargesheet it cannot be taken into consideration by punishing authority - Consideration of earlier misconduct is often only to reinforce opinion of concerned authority - Respondent did not consider election duty to be an important business which is very important for whole nation - Respondent was appointed on election duty and was deputed to take security arrangement but absented himself from duty - This is a very serious lapse on part of respondent - Held, respondent deliberately absented himself from duty and did not offer any explanation for his absence from election duty - It is not respondent's first absence - He also absented himself from duty on earlier occasions also - Police force is a disciplined force and if respondent is a habitual absentee then there is no reason to ignore this fact at time of imposing penalty - Order of compulsory retirement confirmed - Appeal allowed.
