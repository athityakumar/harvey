Madhya Pradesh Administration v Tribhuban

Supreme Court of India

05 April 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 298; (2007) 9 SCC 748; 2007(3) ALL MR 876; 2007 (6) AWC 5464; 2007 (2) CLR 694; 2007 (3) JhrCR(SC) 24; 2007 (2) LLJ 577; 2007 (3) LLN 597; 2007(5) SCALE 397; 2007 (8) SCJ 389; [2007] 4 S.C.R. 918; 2007 (4) SLR 457

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Termination, Industrial Disputes, Grant Of Relief, U.P. Industrial Disputes Act

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Termination - Legality - Dispute arose between respondent and appellant which was referred to Industrial Tribunal - Tribunal passed award - Respondent however filed petition before HC and the same was allowed - Hence instant appeal - Whether activities of appellant satisfy the tests laid down in statutory definition of 'Industry' as contained in s. 2(j) of the Act or not.

Held, Industrial Court opined that it was an Industry. Legality of Award of Industrial Court was not questioned. So far as appellant is concerned, it, thus, attained finality. It, therefore, in our opinion cannot now be permitted to turn round and contend that its Delhi establishment does not come within purview of definition of 'Industry'. Industrial Court exercised its discretionary jurisdiction u/s. 11A of the Act. It merely directed amount of compensation to which respondent was entitled to, had provisions of s. 25 F been complied with should be sufficient to meet ends of justice. SC are not suggesting that HC could not interfere with said order, but discretionary jurisdiction exercised by Industrial Court, in SC's opinion, should have been taken into consideration for determination of question as to what relief should be granted in peculiar facts and circumstances of this case. Each case is required to be dealt with in fact situation obtaining therein. SC opined that keeping in view peculiar facts and circumstances of case and particularly in view of fact that HC had directed re-instatement with full back wages, SC are of opinion that interest of justice would be sub-served if appellant be directed to pay a sum of Rs. 75,000/- by way of compensation to respondent. Appeal allowed.
