Manohar and Others v Jaipalsing and Others

Supreme Court of India

20 November 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1151; (2008) 1 SCC 520; AIR 2008 SC 429; 2008 (70) ALR 153; 2007 (7) AWC 7096; 2008 (1) Bom.C.R. 53; 2008 (2) CTC 60; JT 2007 (13) SC 171; 2008 (1) KLT 832; 2007 (6) MLJ(SC) 1796; 2008 (1) RCR(Civil) 220; 2008 (104) RD 166; 2007(13) SCALE 340; 2007 (8) SCJ 542; [2007] 12 S.C.R. 364; 2008 (1) UC 1; 2008 (1) WLN(SC) 141

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Review Application

Summary: Applicability of the provisions of art. 136 as contained in the Sch. appended to the Limitation Act, 1963 - Decree of possession passed in favour of the respondent - Held, decree of possession passed in favour of the respondent became enforceable immediately on its passing - An execution petition therefore was required to be filed within a period of 12 years - Executing Court as well as the High Court committed a manifest error in opining that the Execution Petition was not barred by limitation - Appeal allowed.
