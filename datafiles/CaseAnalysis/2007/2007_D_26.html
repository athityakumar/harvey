United India Insurance Company Limited v Davinder Singh

Supreme Court of India

12 October 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1443; (2007) 3 SCC (Cr) 664; (2007) 8 SCC 698; 2007 (4) ACC 705; AIR 2008 SC 329; 2008 (1) ALD(SC) 42; 2007 (69) ALR 897; 2007 (4) AWC 3964; 2007 (6) CompLJ 311; 2007 (4) CPJ(Sc) 1; 2007 (4) KLT 954; 2008 (1) MLJ(SC) 497; 2008 (2) MPLJ 30; 2008 (149) PLR 309; 2007 (4) RCR(Civil) 790; 2007(12) SCALE 359; 2007 (8) SCJ 674; [2007] 11 S.C.R. 337

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Consumer Protection

Keywords: Use Of Vehicle, Licensed Driver

Summary: Consumer Protection - Insurance - Carriers & Transportation - Consumer Protection Act, 1986, s. 12 - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, ss. 2, 3, 10, 15 and 149 - Whether renewal of a licence granted to drive a motor vehicle which was originally found to be forged would lead to any liability on part of insurance company? - Any counterfeit document showing that it contains a purported order of a statutory authority would ever remain counterfeit albeit fact that other persons including some statutory authorities would have acted on document unwittingly on assumption that it is genuine - Distinction as regard statutory liability of insurer vis-a-vis purport and object sought to be achieved by a beneficent legislation before a forum constituted under Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 and enforcement of contract qua contract before a Consumer Forum - A right on part of Insurance Company not to pay amount of insurance would depend upon facts and circumstances of each case - It, in certain situation may be bound to pay claim made by third party, if same is filed before a forum created under Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 - But defence may be held to be justified before a different forum where question raised is required to be considered in a different manner - Held, Court below committed an error in holding appellant liable to indemnify owner of vehicle in regard to losses sustained by him - Appeal allowed.
