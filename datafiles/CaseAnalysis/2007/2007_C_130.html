M/S. Matsushita Television & Audio (I) Ltd v Commissioner Of Customs

Supreme Court of India

12 April 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, B. Sudershan Reddy

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 399; (2007) 11 SCC 198; 2007 (211) E.L.T. 200; JT 2007 (5) SC 458; 2007(5) SCALE 662; 2007 (4) SCJ 338; [2007] 5 S.C.R. 46

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Appeal dismissed.

Summary: Central Excise Act, 1944, s.35l(B); Customs Valuation (Determination of Price of Imported Goods) Rules, 1988, r.4(2), r.9(1)(c) - Whether the royalty payment was connected with the imported components of Colour TV and if so whether such royalty payment could be included in the assessable value of such components - Assessable value of the components were required to be loaded with the cost of royalty payment - Agreement was clear that the royalty payment was related to components -Amount of royalty to be included in the price paid for components- Royalty payment constituted consideration for technical assistance rendered by MEI - Department was right in including the cost of royalty payment in the assessable value of the components, duly imported - Held, only such royalty which is relatable to the imported goods and which is a condition of sale of such goods alone could be added to the declared price - Royalty payment was to be computed in both the domestic element of the net sale price and on the cost of imported components of the colour T.V.- Both the conditions of r.9(1)(c) of the Valuation Rules are satisfied - Appeal stands dismissed.
