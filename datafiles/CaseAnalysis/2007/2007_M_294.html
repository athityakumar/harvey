State Of Uttar Pradesh And Others v Messrs P.N.C. Construction Company Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 August 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, B. Sudershan Reddy

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 804; (2007) 7 SCC 320; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 770; 2007 (5) ALJ 572; JT 2007 (10) SC 42; 2007(9) SCALE 655; [2007] 8 S.C.R. 927; [2007] 9 VST 115

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate; Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Keywords: Tax Payable, Appeals Dismissed, Additional Tax, Liability To Tax, Rate Of Tax, Deferred Payment, Building Contract, Concessional Rate, Concessional Rate Of Tax, Declared Goods, Rate, Purchase Price, Tax On Sale Or Purchase, Valuable Consideration, Setting Aside, Building Construction, Divisible Contract, Indivisible Contract, Deferred, Uttar Pradesh Trade Tax Act, 1948, Inter- State Trade

Summary: Sales Tax - Trade Tax - Section 4-B, U.P. Trade Tax Act, 1948 - Hot Mix - Assessee was awarded work for widening the roads, maintenance and repairs - In execution of the contract assessee was required to buy raw materials which were used in the manufacture of Hot Mix - Applied for grant of 'recognition certificate' under s. 4-B for purchase of raw materials for manufacture of Hot Mix - Trade Tax Officer rejected the request of the assessee - Appeal before Dy. Commissioner was also dismissed - On appeal, the Trade Tax Tribunal on the ground that as the A.O. had in fact granted recognition certificate to main company for manufacturing of Hot Mix, directed the A.O. to grant recognition certificate against Form No.3-B at a concessional rate of tax - A.O. issued recognition certificate in favour of the assessee - Commissioner, Trade Tax issued a circular stating that purchase of raw-materials used in the construction of roads, cannot be made against Form No.3B and that recognition certificate under Section 4B should be reviewed by the A.O. - Show cause notice was issued to the assessee as to why the recognition certificate earlier granted to the assessee should not be revoked - Impugned circulars were challenged by the assessee before the High Court - High Court set aside the impugned notices - Whether the Department was right in denying the benefit of s. 4-B of the Act to the assessee - Held, impugned show cause notice was based on complete misreading of the provisions of the Act - Withdrawal of recognition certificate was erroneous as it was contrary to the definition of the word "sale" in s. 2(h) which had to be read with s. 4B(2) - The circulars issued by the Commissioner, cannot constitute the basis for issuing the impugned show cause notice.
