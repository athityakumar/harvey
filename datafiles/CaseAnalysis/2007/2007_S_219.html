State Bank Of India & Anr v Somvir Singh

Supreme Court of India

13 February 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

B. SUDARSHAN REDDY, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 106; (2007) 2 SCC (L&S) 92; (2007) 4 SCC 778; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 505; 2007 (3) ALD(SC) 60; 2007 (2) AWC 1552; 2007 (113) FLR 225; JT 2007 (3) SC 398; 2007 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 2960; 2007 (2) LLJ 230; 2007 (2) LLN 73; 2007(3) SCALE 42; 2007 (3) SCJ 715; [2007] 2 S.C.R. 509; 2007 (3) SLR 497

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Executive Instructions

Summary: Service - Appointment on compassionate ground - Bank found that the family of the deceased employee had not been left in penury or without any means of livelihood and rejected the request - High Court directed the appellant-Bank to reconsider the claim of the petitioner - Appeal against - Held, Authority also did not commit any error in taking into consideration the income of the family from other sources viz. the agricultural land - Hardship of the dependant does not entitle one to compassionate appointment de hors the scheme or the statutory provisions as the case may be - It is not a case where the dependants of the deceased employee are left 'without any means of livelihood' and unable to make both ends meet - High Court ought not to have disturbed the finding and the conclusion arrived at by the appellant- Bank that the respondent was not living hand to mouth - Appeal allowed.
