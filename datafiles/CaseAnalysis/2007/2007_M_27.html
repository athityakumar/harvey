Dir. S.C.T.I. For Medical Science and Technical Another v M. Pushkaran

Supreme Court of India

23 November 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1158; (2008) 1 SCC 448; AIR 2008 SC 559; 2008 (5) AWC 4229; JT 2007 (13) SC 315; 2007(13) SCALE 424; 2007 (8) SCJ 614; [2007] 12 S.C.R. 465; 2008 (1) SLR 809

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: Service - Whether respondent had any legal right for being appointed against the post of three security guards advertised by the appellant institute? - Held, Only because the name of a person appears in the select list, the same by itself may not be a ground for offering him an appointment - Respondent was to be offered with the appointment at a point of time when no policy decision was taken - There was, thus, no reason not to offer any appointment in his favour - Why the select panel was ignored has not been explained - Decision to abolish posts was not taken at a point of time when he had filed the writ petition - Policy decision to abolish the posts as also contracting out the security services was taken by the appellant much thereafter, viz., on or about 29.12.2005 - Appeal dismissed.
