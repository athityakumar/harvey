Lachhman Dass v Jagat Ram & Ors

Supreme Court of India

20 February 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 176; (2007) 10 SCC 448; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 1169; 2007 (7) AWC 6878; JT 2007 (3) SC 595; 2007 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1236; 2007 (4) MLJ(SC) 1122; 2007 (146) PLR 297; 2007 (2) RCR(Civil) 300; 2007(3) SCALE 349; 2007 (5) SCJ 246; [2007] 2 S.C.R. 980

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Punjab Pre-Emption Act, 1913

Summary: Punjab Pre-emption Act, 1913 - Interpretation of the provisions - Claiming a right of pre-emption by Respondent No.8 - Consent decree of pre-emption - Mutation also granted - Appeal against - Held, appellant was not impleaded as a party - His right, therefore, to own and possess the suit land could not have been taken away without giving him an opportunity of hearing in a matter of this nature - Right of pre-emption is a very weak right, although it is a statutory right - Plaintiff was not a co-sharer of her father - She could not have claimed a right of pre-emption on the basis of consanguinity - Had, therefore, an opportunity of hearing been given, the plaintiff-appellant could have shown that she did not have any such right - Appeal allowed.
