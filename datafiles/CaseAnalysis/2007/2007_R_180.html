Vinay Devanna Nayak v Ryot Seva Sahakari Bank Limited

Supreme Court of India

07 December 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

C.K. Thakker, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1280; (2008) 2 SCC 305; AIR 2008 SC 716; 2008 (2) BC 71; 2008 (1) Bom.C.R. 523; 2008 CRLJ 805; 2008 (1) JCC (NI) 1; 2008 (1) JCC (NI) 94; JT 2007 (13) SC 382; 2008 (1) KLT 1; 2008 (150) PLR 194; 2008 (1) RCR(Civil) 249; 2008 (1) RCR(Criminal) 249; 2007(13) SCALE 705; [2007] 12 S.C.R. 1134; 2008 (1) WLN(SC) 54

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Corporate; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Legal Notice, Banking Operations, Banking, Public Financial Institutions and Negotiable Instrument Law (Amendment) Act, 1988

Summary: Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, s. 138 - Conviction - Held, since the matter has been compromised between the parties and the amount of Rs.45, 000/- has been paid by the appellant towards full and final settlement to the respondent-bank towards its dues, the appellant is entitled to acquittal - Appeal allowed.
