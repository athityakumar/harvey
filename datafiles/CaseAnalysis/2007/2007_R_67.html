Jaswant Singh and Another v Rajkot Municipal Corporation And Another

Supreme Court of India

11 October 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1577; (2007) 10 SCC 71; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 1668; 2008 (5) AWC 4661; JT 2007 (12) SC 240; 2007(12) SCALE 115; 2007(12) SCALE 123; [2007] 10 S.C.R. 1124

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Absence From Duty

Summary: Service - Bombay Provincial Municipal Corporation Act, 1949, s. 56 - Misconduct for absence from duty without prior leave - Appellant, a major in the Army, joined the Respondent-Municipal Corporation (Corporation) as a Vigilance Officer and was put on probation for a period of six months - Although there does not exist any statutory provision in this behalf, the probation period was extended from time to time - Appellant applied for leave, however, did not join even after the expiry of leave period - Show cause notice was issued to him asking him to show cause as to why his services should not be terminated for alleged misconduct of remaining absent from duty without prior leave - Explanation given by him was rejected and he was discharged from service - Writ petition filed thereagainst was dismissed - Hence, present appeal - (A) Whether order of termination was stigmatic? - Held, when a disciplinary enquiry is initiated on the premise that there are serious allegations of misconduct on the part of the delinquent officer and delinquent officer's explanation is rejected and thereby the formal enquiry initiated culminates in a finding of guilt, the order terminating the service would be held to be stigmatic - In the instant case, the language used in the impugned order was ex facie stigmatic - Disciplinary proceeding was initiated therefor and his explanation to the show cause notice was rejected - Therefore, termination of services of the appellant purporting to discharge him simplicitor cannot be accepted, being stigmatic in nature - (B) Termination - Probation period - Held, taking into consideration the factor as to whether the employee had satisfactorily performed his duties during the period of probation is a relevant factor and the same can form foundation for passing an order of discharge - In the present case, period of probation as provided for under the statute had expired; therefore order impugned was punitive and therefore set aside - Appeal allowed.
