Hindustan Paper Corporation Limited v Kagajkal Thikadar Sramik Union and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 December 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, Arijit Pasayat

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1324; (2008) 2 SCC 545; 2008 (1) ESC 65; JT 2008 (1) SC 156; 2008 (1) LLJ 917; 2007(14) SCALE 457; [2007] 13 S.C.R. 774; 2008 (2) SLR 18; 2008 (2) UPLBEC 1329

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure; Service; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Contract Labour (Regulation and Abolition) Assam Rules, 1971

Summary: Contract Labour (Regulation and Abolition) Assam Rules, 1971 - Order of High Court directing appellant to pay equal and similar wages and other benefits to the contract labourers who work in the finishing job under r. 25 (2) (v) - Appeal against - Whether the order of the Division Bench is justifiable when the Labour Commissioner passed an order as an interim arrangement for continuing the existing conditions of wages and other facilities till final settlement is arrived at? - Held, before sorting out the controversy, authority is free to take interim arrangement pending final decision and in such matters it is not desirable for the courts to interfere and take a decision as if there is no competent authority for the same - Directed Labour Commissioner to decide the issue raised by the Union by way of representation de hors to the observation made by the Division Bench and pass an order - Appeal allowed.
