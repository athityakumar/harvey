Commnr. Sales Tax, U.P v M/S. Bharat Bone Mill

Supreme Court of India

20 February 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 491; (2007) 4 SCC 136; 2007 (3) ALJ 780; 2007 (210) E.L.T. 6; 2007(3) SCALE 383; 2007 (6) SCJ 538; [2007] 2 S.C.R. 886; 2007 (6) S.T.R. 167; [2007] 6 VST 275

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Indirect Tax; Practice & Procedure; Sales Tax

Keywords: Definition Clause, Uttar Pradesh Sales Tax Act, 1948

Summary: Sales Tax - Exigibility - Common law parlance test - 'Crushed bone' - Whether can be treated to be 'fertilizer' - Held, whether a commodity would be exigible to sales tax or not must be considered having regard to its identity in common law parlance - High Court or the Tribunal did not have the advantage of opinion of the expert to the effect as to whether crushed bones can be used only for the purpose of fertilizer or whether crushed bones are sold to the farmers for use thereof only as fertilizer - Impugned order set aside - Question left open for subsequent cases; (2) Rectification of mistake - Section 22, Central Excise Act, 1944 - Assessing Authority proceeded to determine the taxability in view of the prevailing decisions - Whether the Assessing Authority could pass such an order having regard to the power of rectification in terms of s. 22 - Held, provisions for rectification of 'mistakes apparent on the record' can not be equated with a power of a civil court to review its own order as envisaged under O. XLVII, r. 1, CPC, 1908.
