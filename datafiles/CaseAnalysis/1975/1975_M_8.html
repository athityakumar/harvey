 Bar Council of Maharashtra v M. V. Dabholkar and Others

Supreme Court of India

13 August 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Ray, Hans Raj Khanna, Kuttyil Kurien Mathew, M. Hameedullah Beg, V.R. Krishna Iyer, A.C. Gupta, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 525; (1975) 2 SCC 702; AIR 1975 SC 2092; [1976] 1 S.C.R. 306

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Ethics, Attorney-General

Summary: Advocates Act, 1961, ss. 37and 38 - CPC, 1908 - (A) Determination of 'Person aggrieved' - (B) Interpretation of words 'Person aggrieved' - Permissibility to adopt restricted meaning - Held, the State Bar Council is a person aggrieved to maintain an appeal u/s. 38 of the Act - Order Accordingly.
