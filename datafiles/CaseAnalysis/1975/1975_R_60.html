Jasbhai Motibhai Desai v Roshan Kumar, Haji Bashir Ahmed and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 December 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Sarkaria, A.N. Ray, M. Hameedullah Beg, P.N. Shingal

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 372; (1976) 1 SCC 671; AIR 1976 SC 578; [1976] 3 S.C.R. 58

Case Digest

Subject: Media & Entertainment

Keywords: Rice-Milling Industry (Regulation) Act, 1958, Cinematograph Act, 1918, Bombay Cinema Rules, 1954, Board Of Revenue Act, 1913

Summary: Constitution, art. 226 - Cinematograph Act, 1918 - Bombay Cinema Rules, 1954 - Writ of Certiorari - Location of Cinema Theatre - Grant of 'No Objection Certificate' in violation of 1954 Rules - Right of rival in a trade to invoke certiorari jurisdiction - Held, proprietor of a theatre holding a licence of exhibiting cinematograph films is not entitled to invoke the certiorari jurisdiction ex debito justitiae to get a NOC, granted u/r. 6 1954 Rules by the DM in favour of a rival in the trade, brought up and quashed on the ground that it suffers from a defect of jurisdiction - Appeal dismissed.
