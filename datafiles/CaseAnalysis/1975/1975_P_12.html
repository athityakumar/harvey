Paradise Industrial Corporation Bombay v Kiln Plastics Products

Supreme Court of India

29 September 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

A. Alagiriswami, P.K. Goswami, N.L. Untwalia

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 257; (1976) 1 SCC 91; AIR 1976 SC 309; 1976 RCJ 7; 1976 RCR(Rent) 194; [1976] 2 S.C.R. 32; 1975 UJ 832

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Ex-Parte Order, Recovery of possession

Summary: Rent Control - Bombay Rents, Hotel and Lodging House Rates Control Act, 1947, s. 11 (4) - Recovery of possession - Ex-parte decree - Legality - Appellants-plaintiffs filed a suit against respondents defendants for recovery of possession of property leased to them as also rent and mesne profits - Trial Court ordered requiring defendants to deposit Rs. 13,090/- as rent due up to end of December 1968 and interim standard rent of Rs. 308/per month to be paid - Respondents did not deposit the amount ordered by Trial Court - Appellant applied to Trial Court praying for a notice to be issued to respondents to show cause why they should not deposit aggregate amount of rent - Respondents were absent and Trial Court passed the ex-parte decree for possession and recovery of arrears of rent - Respondent filed appeal before Appellate Court, which was dismissed - Respondents filed application before HC, which was set aside the ex-parte decree passed by Trial Court - Hence, instant appeal - Whether HC was justified in setting aside the ex-parte decree passed by the Trial Court - Held, on basis of evidence on behalf of appellant a decree cannot be passed against him, whereas if it was ordered in accordance with s. 11 (4) of the Act that appellant should not be entitled to appear in or defend suit except with leave of Court appellant was placed at a greater disadvantage - Use of the words 'defence struck off' did not in any way affect substance of order and HC was wholly in error in holding that because of form of order passed on 2-6-1960 the order was illegal and without jurisdiction - Further, it was noted that that order itself did not order defenes be struck off, it only fixed 15-7-1969 as date for striking out defences and to fix suit for ex-part hearing - So, till expiry of a month given by that order for deposit of money question of striking out defence did not arose nor was it in fact struck out - On date fixed for striking out defences and fixing the date, for ex-parte hearing respondents did not appear nor did they appear on 5th and 6th of August when suit was fixed for hearing - 'Though they were permitted to deposit Rs. 7,000/- on their application dt. 4-8-1969, respondents were not took any further steps and so notice was dismissed - Respondents did not even apply for setting aside ex-parte decree giving proper reasons for their non-appearance - HC was not justified in setting aside the ex-parte decree - Impugned order passed by HC was set aside and orders of Trial Court and Appellate Court were restored - Appeal allowed.
