Bhagwan Singh v State of Rajasthan

Supreme Court of India

14 August 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Bhagwati, R.S. Sarkaria, Y.V. Chandrachud

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 650; (1975) SCC (Cr) 737; (1976) 1 SCC 15; AIR 1976 SC 985; 1976 CRLJ 713; 1975 CRLR 502; 1975 UJ 680

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Constitution, art. 136 - CrPC, 1898, ss.156 and 173 - Offence u/s. 165A of IPC, 1860 - (A) Appreciation of evidence - Concurrent findings of fact - Interference by Supreme Court when justified - (B) Practice and Procedure - (1) Investigation by complainant police officer himself - (2) Duty of police officer - Where an important document which bears on the offence, alleged to have been committed by an accused, is produced before police officer some days later - (C) Evidence and proof - Benefit of doubt - Availability - When entire prosecution case resting solely on testimony of police witnesses - (A) Held, in those rare and exceptional cases where it finds that several important circumstances have not been taken into account by the trial Court and the High Court and that has resulted in serious miscarriage of justice - (B) This was an infirmity which was bound to reflect on the credibility of the prosecution case - Held, the natural course for a police officer to follow was to take charge of the document under a panchnama or memo - (C) Held that as the evidence led on behalf of the prosecution threw considerable doubt on the prosecution case and did not exclude reasonable doubt in regard to guilt of the accused appellant, he was entitled to acquittal - Appeal allowed.
