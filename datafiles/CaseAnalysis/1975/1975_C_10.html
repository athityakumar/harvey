Ajantha Industries and Others v Central Board of Direct Taxes and Others

Supreme Court of India

05 December 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

P.K. Goswami, Kuttyil Kurien Mathew, N.L. Untwalia

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 324; (1976) 1 SCC 1001; AIR 1976 SC 437; 1976 CTR 79; [1976] 102 ITR 281; [1976] 2 S.C.R. 884; 1976 TAXLR 219; 1976 UJ 157

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income-tax Act, 1961, s. 127 (1) - Transfer of cases - Reasoned order - Central Board transferred income tax cases of appellants from Nellore officer to Hyderabad officer and hence, appellants challenged the same before HC on ground of violation of principles of natural justice - However, HC dismissed petition holding that mere failure to communicate reasons to appellants was not fatal to order - On further appeal, DB of HC also dismissed appeal - Hence, instant appeal - Whether failure to record the reasons in order which was communicated to the appellants is violative of the principles of natural justice for which the order should be held to be invalid.

Held, requirement of recording reasons u/s. 127(1) of the Act is a mandatory direction under the law and non-communication thereof is not saved by showing that the reasons exist in the file although not communicated to the assessee. Hence, non-communication of the reasons in order passed u/s. 127(1) of the Act is a serious infirmity in the order and so, the same is invalid. Hence, impugned orders of transfer are quashed. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - S. 127(1) of the Act requires reasons to be recorded prior to the passing of an order of transfer.
