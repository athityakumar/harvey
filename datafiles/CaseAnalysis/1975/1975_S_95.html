Trilok Chand Jain v State Of Delhi

Supreme Court of India

19 August 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Sarkaria, Y.V. Chandrachud, P.N. Bhagwati

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 220; (1975) SCC (Cr) 725; (1975) 4 SCC 761; AIR 1977 SC 666; 1977 CRLJ 254; 1975 CrLR(SC) 511; [1976] 1 S.C.R. 348

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Illegal Gratification

Summary: Criminal - Corruption - Benefit of Doubt - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947,ss. 4, 5 and 161 - IPC, 1860,ss.161, 165 - Complainant made application for installation of electricity connection in his factory - Despite delay, no installation was made - Bribe was demanded by one inspector - Appellant charged for offence of taking bribe for installation of electric connection of complainant - Whether conviction of appellant was just and proper? - One of the essential ingredients of offence is that the gratification must have been received by the accused as 'a motive or reward' for committing an act or omission in connection with his official functions - Held, No - Essential ingredient of the offence u/s. 5(1)(d) was lacking in this case - Appellant was a labourer who was not concerned with the installation work at the site or with the giving of the power connection to the complainant - No evidence that appellant had demanded any bribe from complainant - Charges against appellant not proved - Appellant acquitted - Appeal allowed.
