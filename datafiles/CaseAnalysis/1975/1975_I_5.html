Gemini Leather Store v Income Tax Officer, B-Ward, Agra, and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 May 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

A.C. Gupta, R.S. Sarkaria

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 313; (1975) 4 SCC 375; AIR 1975 SC 1268; 1975 (4) CTR 127; [1975] 100 ITR 1; 1975 TAXLR 498; 1975 UJ 482

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Failure To Disclose Material Facts

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income-tax Act, 1961, ss. 147(a), 148 - Best Judgment assessment - AO rejected returns filed by assessee and books of account produced for A/Y 1956-57 and made best judgment assessment - However, Appellate Authority and Tribunal reduced turnover so assessed and later notice u/s. 148 of the Act was issued to assessee - Whether AO had reason to believe that income chargeable to tax had escaped assessment for A/Y in question by reason of the omission or failure on the part of the assessee to disclose, fully and truly all material facts.

Held, it was plainly a case of oversight, and it could not be said that the income chargeable to tax for the relevant assessment year had escaped assessment by reason of the omission or failure on the part of the assessee to disclose fully and truly all material facts. On contrary, AO had all material facts before him when he made the original assessment and hence, could not take recourse to s. 147(a) of the Act to remedy the error resulting from his own oversight. Hence, impugned notice and consequential proceedings are quashed. Appeal allowed.
