Dwarka Prasad v Dwarka Das Saraf

Supreme Court of India

11 August 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

V.R. Krishna Iyer, A.N. Ray, Kuttyil Kurien Mathew, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 269; (1976) 1 SCC 128; AIR 1975 SC 1758; 1975 (1) ALR 516; 1975 RCJ 593; 1975 RCR(Rent) 712; [1976] 1 S.C.R. 277

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure; Tort

Summary: U.P. (Temporary) Control of Rent and Eviction Act, 1947, s. 2(1)(a) - Lease - (A) Meaning of accommodation - Lease of Cinema theatre - Eviction - (B) Interpretation of statutes - Scope of Proviso - Held, lease sued on did not fall within the scope of the definition and a long-standing running cinema with outstanding contracts with film distributors cannot be uprooted overnight - If, on a fair construction, principal provision is clear, a proviso cannot expand or limit it and proviso must be limited to subject matter of the enacting clause, prima facie be read and considered in relation to the principal matter to which it is a proviso as it is not a separate or independent enactment - Appeal Allowed.
