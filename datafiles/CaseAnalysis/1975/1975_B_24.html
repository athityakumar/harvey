Bar Council Of Maharashtra v M. V. Dabholkar Etc. Etc.

Supreme Court of India

03 October 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

V.R. Krishna Iyer, R.S. Sarkaria, A.C. Gupta, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 152; (1976) 2 SCC 291; AIR 1976 SC 242; [1976] 2 S.C.R. 48

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Constitution of India, 1950 - Advocates Act, 1961 ss. 35, 36 and 38 - Professional misconduct - Practitioner advocates charged of professional misconduct u/s. 35(1) - Appellate disciplinary committee heard appeal and found them to have committed professional misconduct - Whether such finding is valid? - Held, on appeal appellate disciplinary Tribunal wrong in applying r. 36 - Exercise in discovering three elements of r. 36 was as unserviceable as it was supererogatory - Appeal to be disposed on trust that standards and sanctions befitting national bar will be maintained in such dignified and deterrent manner that public confidence is never shaken and shock - Appeal disposed of.
