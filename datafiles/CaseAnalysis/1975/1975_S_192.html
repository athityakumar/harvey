State of Mysore and Others v V. K. Kangan and Others

Supreme Court of India

21 August 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

Kuttyil Kurien Mathew, A.N. Ray, Y.V. Chandrachud

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 223; (1976) 2 SCC 895; AIR 1975 SC 2190; [1976] 1 S.C.R. 369; 1975 UJ 719

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Tamil Nadu Land Acquisition Rules, r. 3(b) - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 5A and 55 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Rules under 1894 Act issued by Mysore Govt., r. 5(2) - (A) Whether provisions of r. 3(b) were mandatory and therefore failure to issue notice to department concerned as enjoined by rule was fatal to validity of notifications u/ss. 4 and 6 of Act - (B) Determining question whether a provision is mandatory or directory - (C) Effect of non-compliance with r. 5(2) of Rules issue by Mysore Govt. - R. 5(2) being similar to r. 3(b) of Madras Rules, is also mandatory and not ultra vires of s. 5A - (D) Validity of notification u/s. 4 of 1894 Act, for want of public notice should be challenged within reasonable time of its publication - Unreasonable delay in filing petition u/art. 226 of Constitution - Held, failure to give notice is fatal - Determination of question whether a provision is mandatory or directory would, in ultimate analysis, depend upon intent of law makers - Effect is to vitiate proceedings u/s. 5A(2) - Disentitles petitioner to relief - Appeal dismissed.
