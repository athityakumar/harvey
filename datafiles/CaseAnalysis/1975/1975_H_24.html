Government of Andhra Pradesh and Another v Hindustan Machine Tools Limited

Supreme Court of India

01 May 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.V. Chandrachud, Hans Raj Khanna, M. Hameedullah Beg

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 658; (1975) 2 SCC 274; AIR 1975 SC 2037; 1975 (4) CTR 164; [1975] Supp S.C.R. 394; 1975 TAXLR 2013

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Municipalities & Local Governments - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 133(1)(a) - Andhra Pradesh Gram Panchayats Act, 2 of 1964, ss. 2(15), 69(1) (a) - Levy of house tax - Permission fee - Challenged - Appellant no. 2 (Kuthbullapur Gram Panchayat) was established in 1959, and in 1964 Andhra Pradesh Gram Panchayats Act, 2 of 1964, was passed which with exception of Chapter VII of the Act came into force - Respondent had established factory for manufacture of special apparatus machines, presses etc; within geographical limits of Kuthbullapur Gram Panchayat without permission of him but considered skeleton staff which means Panchayat and its skeleton activities, then respondent was asked by Panchayat to obtain requisite permission and respondent asked for ex-postfacto permission - In its meeting Panchayat passed resolution for collecting Permission Fee from respondent at 1/2% on capital value of factory buildings and at 1% on capital value of other buildings and Panchayat called upon respondent to pay house-tax for A/ys 1966-1967, 1967-1968 and 1968-1969 amounting to Rs. 1,83,750 at rate of Rs. 61,250 per annum - Further Panchayat demanded from respondent sum of Rs. 1,65,000 by way of Permission Fee, Rs. 80,000 being for factory buildings and Rs. 85,000 in respect of other buildings - Aggrieved respondent filed petition in HC Challenging levy of house tax and Permission Fee which was allowed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether levied of house tax and permission fee was legal - Held, SC were unable to accept that those services were rendered individually to respondent and laying of roads and drainage or supply of street-lights were statutory function of public authorities and it was difficult to hold, in absence of any material, that any of such services as had been mentioned to this Court had in fact been rendered to respondent - Very circumstance that Permission Fee was levied at certain percentage of capital value of buildings shows that Gram Panchayat itself never intended to correlate fee with services rendered or intended to be rendered by it - There was therefore no warrant for levy of Permission Fee, not even on factory buildings, assuming for sake of argument that permission of Gram Panchayat was necessary for construction of factory buildings - It was alternatively contended appellants that Permission Fee though called fee was really in nature of tax on buildings and may be upheld as such - It was impossible to accept contention that Permission Fee was not tax on buildings was clear from fact that fee may be required to be paid even if building did not eventually come into existence - Therefore, scheme under which Permission Fee was attempted to be levied was that it becomes payable at time when permission to construct building was applied for but levy did not depend upon whether building had been in fact constructed with result that whether building was constructed or not, fee had to be paid - In other words, Permission Fee was in nature of levy on proposed activity and was not tax on buildings - Thus, levy of house-tax was lawful but levy of Permission Fee had to be struck down as being illegal - Accordingly appeal was allowed partly - Appeal partly allowed.
