Ram Narain Singh v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

15 July 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, N.L. Untwalia

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 197; (1975) 4 SCC 497; (1975) SCC (Cr) 571; 1975 (12) ACC 273; AIR 1975 SC 1727; 1975 CRLJ 1500; 1975 CrLR(SC) 447; [1976] 1 S.C.R. 27

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 3 - Murder - Appreciation of evidence - Direct evidence not supported by expert evidence - Whether High Court was justified in convicting accused? - If evidence of witnesses for prosecution is totally inconsistent with medical evidence or evidence of ballistic expert, this is a most fundamental defect in prosecution case and unless reasonably explained it is sufficient to discredit entire case - HC readily accepted prosecution case without noticing that evidence of eye-witnesses in Court was a belated attempt to improve their testimony and bring same in line with Doctor's evidence with a view to support an incorrect case - Held, accused are entitled to acquittal as there can't be conviction on that basis - Appeal allowed.
