Phool Kumar v Delhi Administration

Supreme Court of India

13 March 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

N.L. Untwalia, A. Alagiriswami

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 411; (1975) SCC (Cr) 336; (1975) 1 SCC 797; AIR 1975 SC 905; 1975 CRLJ 778; [1975] 3 S.C.R. 917

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 45 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 392, 397 and 398 - Robbery - Possession of deadly weapon - Eye-witness failed to identify - Conviction on the basis of fingerprint - Convicted by lower court and High Court - (A) Whether opinion of expert being accepted without his examination in court is justified? - Court was bound to summon expert if accused would have filed any such application for his examination - Held, accused not having filed any application grievance of appellant apropos report of expert being used without his examination in court has no substance - (B) Whether mere carrying of a knife in hand while robbery would render convict to be charged u/s. 397, IPC, 1860? - Held, where an accused at time of committing robbery, carries in his hand a knife open to view of victims it is sufficient to frighten or terrorise them and he can be convicted u/s. 397, IPC, 1860 - Any other overt act such as brandishing of knife or causing of grievous hurt with it is not necessary to bring offender within ambit of u/s. 397, IPC, 1860 - (C) Whether carrying of deadly weapon by one person during robbery would render other person also liable u/s. 397, IPC, 1860? - Use of a deadly weapon by one offender at time of committing robbery cannot attract liability u/s. 397, IPC, 1860, for imposition of minimum punishment on another offender who had not used any deadly weapon - Appeal dismissed.
