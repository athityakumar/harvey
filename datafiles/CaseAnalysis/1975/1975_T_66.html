Trustees of The Port of Madras v Messrs Aminchand Pyarelal and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 September 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.V. Chandrachud, A.N. Ray, Kuttyil Kurien Mathew

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 236; (1976) 3 SCC 167; AIR 1975 SC 1935; [1976] 1 S.C.R. 721

Case Digest

Subject: Customs

Keywords: Public Warehouse, Bombay Prohibition Act 1949

Summary: Customs - Tamil Nadu Port Trust Act, 1905, ss. 42, 43 and 43A - Port Trust - Vires of 'Scale of Rates' framed under chap. IV, r. 13(a)(b) challenged along with reasonableness - Questions arised for consideration that whether scale of fees under which appellants charge demurrage is void as being unreasonable and as being beyond their powers and, if answer to first question is in negative, whether 1st respondent is liable to pay demurrage? - Held, scale of rates not ultra vires on ground of unreasonableness - It is not in excess of power - Claim against 1st respondent fail - Appeal dismissed.
