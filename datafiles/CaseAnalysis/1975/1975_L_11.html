Bathutmal Raichand Oswal v Laxmibai R. Tarta and Another

Supreme Court of India

11 February 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Bhagwati, Kuttyil Kurien Mathew, N.L. Untwalia

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 594; (1975) 1 SCC 858; AIR 1975 SC 1297; 1975 UJ 220

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Rent Control - Bombay rents, Hotel and Lodging House Rates Control Act, 1947, ss. 13(1)(a), 13(1)(k) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 227 - Jurisdiction of HC - Findings of facts - Appellant approached HC u/art. 227 of Constitution aggrieved by eviction order passed by Trial Court as confirmed by District Court in respect of shop he occupied as tenant - On remand from HC, Trial Court perused evidence and held that dominant use of shop by appellant was for residence and not business and respondents were hence, entitled to evict appellant from shop u/s. 13(1)(a) and (k) of the Act - On appeal, District Court made findings that dominant purpose for which shop was used was business and hence, neither s. 13(1)(a) nor s. 13(1)(a) (k) were attracted - HC, thereupon re-appreciated evidence and set aside findings of District Court and held that dominant use of shop by appellant was for residence not business and confirmed decree of eviction passed against appellant by Trial court- Hence, instant appeal by appellant - Whether HC has jurisdiction in application u/art. 227 of Constitution to disturb findings of fact reached by District Court.

Held, HC has clearly misconceived scope and extent of its power u/art. 227 of Constitution and overstepped limits of its jurisdiction under said article. HC proceeded to re-appreciate evidence for purpose of correcting errors of fact supposed to have been committed by District Court but the same was impermissible to HC in exercise of its jurisdiction u/art. 227 of Constitution. District Court was final court of fact and there being no appeal provided against findings of fact reached by District Court, it was not open to HC to question propriety or reasonableness of conclusions drawn from evidence by District Courrt. Hence, HC acted outside limits of its jurisdiction u/art. 227 of Constitution in disturbing findings of fact reached by District Court. Hence, findings of fact arrived at by District Court that dominant use of shop by appellant was for business and not residence and there was accordingly no change of use of shop, would stand and so, respondents were not entitled to recover possession of shop from appellant either u/s. 13(1)(a) or (k) of the Act. Hence, judgment of HC is set aside and decree for eviction passed against appellant is reversed and consequently, suit filed by respondents dismissed. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - The power of superintendence u/art. 227 could not be invoked to correct an error of fact which only a superior court could do in exercise of its statutory power as a court of appeal. The HC could not in guise of exercising its jurisdiction u/art. 227 convert itself into court of appeal when Legislature has not conferred right of appeal and made decision of subordinate court or tribunal final on facts.
