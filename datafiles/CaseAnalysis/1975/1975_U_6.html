Ous Kutilingal Achudan Nair And Ors v Union Of India & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

20 November 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Sarkaria, A.N. Ray, M. Hameedullah Beg, P.N. Shingal

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 281; (1976) 2 SCC 780; AIR 1976 SC 1179; 1976 (32) FLR 22; 1976 LabIC 780; [1976] 2 S.C.R. 769; 1976 UJ 43

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Defence Of India Act, 1971

Summary: Constitution, arts. 19(1)(c) and 33 - Bombay Industrial Disputes Act, 1938 - Right to form association or Union - Whether members of the armed forces can also form unions - Held, employees of the Defence Establishment such as cooks, chowkidars, laskars, barbers, carpenters, mechanics, boot-makers, tailors etc. in training establishments answer the description of members of the Armed forces and they cannot form trade unions as their fundamental right has been competently taken away by the Central Govt. - And by virtue of s. 21 of the Army Act, the Central Govt. was competent by notification to make rules restricting or curtailing their fundamental rights u/art. 19(1)(c) - Appeal Dismissed.
