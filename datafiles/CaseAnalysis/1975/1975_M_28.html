Vulcan Insurance Company Limited v Maharaj Singh and Another

Supreme Court of India

03 October 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

N.L. Untwalia, A. Alagiriswami, P.K. Goswami

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 343; (1976) 1 SCC 943; AIR 1976 SC 287; [1976] 46 Comp Cas 110; [1976] 2 S.C.R. 62

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Corporate; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Arbitration Act, 1940, s. 20 - Contract Act, 1872 , s. 28 - Arbitration - Insurance Policy - Deed - Clauses in insurance policy - Providing that Company will not be liable for loss after expiration of 12 months after loss - Application u/s. 20 of 1940 Act - Questions arise as to - (A) Construcion of the deed - (B) Validity of the clause in insurance policy due to limitation - (C) Maintainability of application - Held, dispute raised by the Company was not covered by the arbitration clause - No limitation prescribed for the filing of such an application under the Limitation Act, 1908 - The matter was to be left for the decision of the arbitrator - Appeal allowed.
