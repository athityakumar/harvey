R. K. Lakshmanan v A. K. Srinivasan and Another

Supreme Court of India

01 August 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Sarkaria, Y.V. Chandrachud, P.N. Bhagwati

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 135; (1975) 2 SCC 466; (1975) SCC (Cr) 654; AIR 1975 SC 1741; 1975 CRLJ 1545; 1975 CrLR(SC) 484; [1976] 1 S.C.R. 204

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure 1898, Code of Criminal Procedure 1884

Summary: Criminal - Code Of Criminal Procedure, 1898, ss. 195, 476, 342, 514 and 561A - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 193, 196, 197, 199 and 205 - Appellant, District Magistrate, issued notice against respondent advocate to appear before it although advocate was not a witness in case - Respondent approached High Court against said notice - HC declared order issuing notice illegal and mala-fide - Held, issuing notice to advocate amounted to gross abuse of process of Court, mala-fide and with ulterior motive in circumstances - Interference with HC order not warranted - Appeal dismissed.
