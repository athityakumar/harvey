Ram Autar Singh Bhadauria v Ram Gopal Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

31 July 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Sarkaria, V.R. Krishna Iyer, A.C. Gupta

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 208; (1976) 1 SCC 43; AIR 1975 SC 2182; [1976] 1 S.C.R. 191

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Election; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Election - Representation Of People Act, 1951, ss. 97 and 100(1)(d)(iii) - Representation of People Conduct of Elections Rules, 1961, rr. 56(2) and 38 - (A) Enquiry into allegations in a case falling u/s. 100 (1)(d)(iii) - Limited to see whether election of returned candidate has been materially affected - Validity of general scrutiny and recount of ballot papers - (B) Procedure under election Rules to be strictly followed - Effect of supply of wrong instrument for stamping of ballot papers - Held unjustified - Returning officer would be bound to reject ballot paper on ground of such defect - Appeal dismissed.
