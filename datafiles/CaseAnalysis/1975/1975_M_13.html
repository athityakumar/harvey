Madan Lal v State Of U.P. And Others

Supreme Court of India

28 August 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

A.C. Gupta, Hans Raj Khanna, V.R. Krishna Iyer

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 229; (1975) 2 SCC 779; AIR 1975 SC 2085; [1976] 1 S.C.R. 492; 1975 UJ 759

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Environment, Wildlife & Animal; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Indian Forest Act, 1927 ss. 4, 6, 11(1) - Land - Notification - Challenged - Appellant had preferred claim u/s.6 of the Act in respect of certain plots which were included with other lands in notification u/s.4 of the Act which declared to constitute said land as reserved forest - Forest Settlement Officer(FSO) had passed order u/s.11(1) of the Act in favour of appellant and admitted his claim - Respondent no.1 filed appeal through respondent no.2 before Deputy Commissioner u/s.17 of the Act, which was allowed and order of FSO was set aside - Aggrieved appellant had filed appeal before Tribunal which set aside order passed by FSO - Appellant had preferred writ before HC which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether appeal preferred u/s.17 of the Act covered order passed by FSO.

Held, S. 17 provides a right of appeal from an order passed by the FSO u/s.11 and lays down a time limit of three months from the date of the order for presenting the appeal and order u/s. 11 was recorded by the FSO on 9-5-1955, and the appeal u/s.17 filed on 20-7-1956 was obviously long out of time if the impugned order could be said to have been made on 9-5-1955 when it was recorded. The knowledge of the party affected by the award, either actual or constructive, being an essential requirement of fair play and natural justice the expression 'the date of the award' used in the proviso must mean the date when the award is either communicated to the party or is known by him either actually or constructively. Therefore, it would be unreasonable to construe the words 'from the date of the Collector's award' used in the proviso u/s. 18 of the Act in a literal or mechanical way. Hence, HC was therefore right in holding that the impugned order should be deemed to have been passed on 24-4-1956 when the Forest Department came to know of the order and the right of appeal granted to the Department should be determined on that very basis. Appeal dismissed.

Ratio - Where the rights of a person are affected by any order and limitation is prescribed for the enforcement of the remedy by the person aggrieved against the said order by reference to the making of the order must mean either actual or constructive communication of the said order to the party concerned.
