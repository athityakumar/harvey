M.Naina Mohammed v K. A. Natarajan and Others

Supreme Court of India

23 July 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

V.R. Krishna Iyer, A.N. Ray, Kuttyil Kurien Mathew, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 201; (1975) 2 SCC 352; AIR 1975 SC 1867; [1976] 1 S.C.R. 102; 1975 UJ 595

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Constitution

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 136 and 226 - Motor Vehicles Act, 1939, s. 47 - Whether decision of High Court regarding permit to ply a bus on route between Madurai and Paramakkudi, Tamil Nadu is justified? - Held, no - The appellate judgment is a partial admission that extraneous considerations were inputs of the order of the STAT and a brief disposal of the whole matter in a single sentence, as it were 'Even so, there is nothing in the order of the Tribunal to support it' - Division Bench was observing that while sitting on the writ side, judicial review should have been more restricted that while sitting on the appellate side, its own judgment was vulnerable because of the plain finding that what was not pertinent was taken into consideration by the STAT - Judgement is to be set aside and whole case to be sent back to STAT to hear case afresh, consider relevant factors bearing upon 'public interest' as highlighted in s. 47 of Motor Vehicles Act and dispose of appeal before it in accordance with law, guided by decisions of this Court and untrammeled by any observations made either by Single Judge or by Division Bench - Directions issued.
