Harbajan Singh v State of Jammu and Kashmir

Supreme Court of India

22 July 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.V. Chandrachud, P.N. Bhagwati, R.S. Sarkaria

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 544; (1975) 4 SCC 480; (1975) SCC (Cr) 545; AIR 1975 SC 1814; 1975 CRLJ 1553; 1975 CRLR 465; 1975 UJ 585

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Evidence Act, 1872, s. 8, expln.1 - Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Murder case - (A) Failure of IO to hold identification parade - If fatal to the case - (B) Faitlure of only eye witness to mention names of accused to neighbours soon after ocurrence - Effect - Absence of test identification is not necessarily fatal - Held, that will only show that the accused is not known to the witness, but will not render the prosecution story untrue as also corroborated by circumstances - Appeal Dismissed.
