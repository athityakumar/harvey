Gvindlal Chhaggan Lal Patel v Agricultural Produce Market Committee, Godhra and Others

Supreme Court of India

27 August 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.V. Chandrachud, P.N. Bhagwati, R.S. Sarkaria

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 228; (1975) 2 SCC 482; AIR 1976 SC 263; 1975 CRLJ 1993; 1975 CrLR(SC) 526; [1976] 1 S.C.R. 451

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Agriculture & Agri. Products

Keywords: Agricultural Produce Markets

Summary: Agriculture & Agri. Products - Practice & Procedure - Gujarat Agricultural Produce Markets Act, 1964, ss. 8, 36 - Bombay Agricultural Produce Markets Act, 1939 - Gujarat Agricultural Produce Markets Rules, 1965, r. 3 - Inspector of Agricultural Produce Market Committee filed a complaint against appellant charging him with having purchased a certain quantity of ginger in January and February, 1969 without obtaining a licence as required by the Act - Magistrate accepted the factum of purchase but acquitted appellant on the ground that relevant notification in regard to the inclusion of ginger was not shown to have been promulgated and published as required by the Act - On appeal, HC convicted appellant for offence punishable u/s. 8 r/w s. 36 of 1964 Act - Hence instant appeal - Whether notification issued u/s. 6(5) of 1964 Act, covering additional varieties of agricultural produce like ginger and onion, should not only be published in official gazette but should also be published in Gujarati in a newspaper - Held, scheme of the Act was like this, u/s. 5(1) the Director of Marketing and Rural Finance might by a notification in Official Gazette declare his intention of regulating purchase and sale of agricultural produce in specified area - Such notification was also required to be published in Gujarati in a newspaper having circulation in the particular area - This requirement was mandatory and should be fulfilled - Admittedly notification issued u/s. 6(5) on 16-2-1968 was not published in a newspaper at all, much less in Gujarati, accordingly, inclusion of new varieties of agricultural produce in that notification lacks legal validity and no prosecution could be founded upon its breach - In instant case, prosecution was conducted before Magistrate in an indifferent manner -That was not surprising because beneficent purpose of summary trials was almost always defeated by a summary approach - Inspector in Agricultural Produce Market Committee, who was a material witness for proving the offence, said in his evidence that he did not know whether or not the notifications were published in any newspaper or on the notice board of Godhra Municipality - Magistrate acquitted appellant holding that prosecution had failed to prove beyond reasonable doubt that notifications were published and promulgated as required by law - Appeal allowed.
