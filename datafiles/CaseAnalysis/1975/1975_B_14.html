Balaka Singh and Others v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

16 April 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, N.L. Untwalia

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 168; (1975) 4 SCC 511; (1975) SCC (Cr) 601; AIR 1975 SC 1962; 1975 CRLJ 1734; [1975] Supp S.C.R. 129; 1975 UJ 529

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 143, 149 and 302 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 3 - Code Of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 367 - Riot with murder - Appreciation of evidence - Total nine accused - Four acquitted earlier - Prosecution case against convicted accused and acquitted accused was inextricably mixed up - No possibility to sever one from other - Whether High Court was justified in convicting appellants? - Evidence proves that prosecution case against five appellants had been proved beyond reasonable doubt - Manner in which F.I.R. and inquest report have been made throw considerable doubt on complicity of five appellants in crime - Held, order of conviction and sentence passed on all appellants is set aside - Appeal allowed.
