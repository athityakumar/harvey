Dulal Roy v District Magistrate and Others

Supreme Court of India

15 January 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Sarkaria, V.R. Krishna Iyer

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 425; (1975) SCC (Cr) 329; (1975) 1 SCC 837; AIR 1975 SC 1508; 1975 CRLJ 1322; 1975 CrLR(SC) 147; [1975] 3 S.C.R. 186

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Defence & Security Forces

Keywords: Defence Regulations

Summary: Defense & Internal Security - Maintenance Of Internal Security Act, 1971, s. 3(1)(a) - Theft - Accused in jail as undertrial - Later released - Was again put behind bars under MISA, 1971 - Petition for Habeas Corpus - Whether in absence of apparent reasons for detention on part of State, detention is sustainable? - Held, in absence of clear and complete counter-affidavit by best informed person on behalf of State giving any explanation or apparent reasons as to why his prosecution for substantive offences resulted in his discharge and as to why making of preventive order was deemed necessary even while he was in jail custody and had no freedom of action - Impugned order had been passed mechanically and as a colourable exercise of jurisdiction - Held, order should be quashed and detenu directed to be released - Petition allowed.
