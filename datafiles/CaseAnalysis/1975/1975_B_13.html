State of Gujarat v Bai Fatima and Another

Supreme Court of India

19 March 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

N.L. Untwalia, A. Alagiriswami

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 627; (1975) 2 SCC 7; (1975) SCC (L&S) 184; (1975) SCC (Tax) 200; (1975) SCC (Cr) 305; (1975) SCC (Cr) 384; 1975 (12) ACC 243; AIR 1975 SC 1478; 1975 CRLJ 1079; [1975] 3 S.C.R. 993; 1975 (1) SLR 773; 1975 UJ 324

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 304 Part I, 323 and 114 - Code of Criminal Procedure, s. 342 - Culpable Homicide eye-witnesses and dying declaration - Whether guilt of respondents proved sufficiently to lead to conviction? - Held, no evidence lead to prove private defence - Witnesses well corroborated by dying declaration - Guilt of both respondents have been proved beyond any reasonable doubt - Appeal allowed.
