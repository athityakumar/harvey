Sita Ram v State of Rajasthan

Supreme Court of India

29 April 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

N.L. Untwalia, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 560; (1975) 2 SCC 227; (1975) SCC (Cr) 491; AIR 1975 SC 1432; 1975 CRLJ 1224; 1975 CRLR 417; 1975 UJ 563

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Illegal Gratification

Summary: Penal Code, 1860, s. 161 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947, ss.4(1), 5(1)(d) and 6 - (A) Presumption update - Offence u/s. 5(1)(d) - (B) Inspector and Asst. Inspectors of Rajasthan Co-operative Societies - Sanction of competent authority for there prosecution - (C) Prosecution u/s. 161 of the Act - Demand of bribe by accused from Complainant not proved - Story of payment of money by, Complainant also not established beyond reasonable doubt - Presumption u/s.4(1) - Held, presumption u/s. 4(1) cannot be drawn, only clauses incorporated in s. 4(1) are cls. (a) and (b) of s. 5(1) and not cl.(d) - Appointing authority of such persons being the Registrar and not the State Government sanction given by Registrar for prosecution of the accused was valid - Held, presumption cannot be drawn far convicting the accused - Appeal Allowed.
