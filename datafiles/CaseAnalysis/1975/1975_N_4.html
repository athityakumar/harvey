 State Of Kerala & Anr. v N. M. Thomas & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

19 September 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Ray, M. Hameedullah Beg, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, Kuttyil Kurien Mathew, V.R. Krishna Iyer, Hans Raj Khanna

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 52; (1976) SCC (L&S) 227; (1976) 2 SCC 310; AIR 1976 SC 490; 1976 LabIC 395; 1976 (1) LLJ 376; [1976] 1 S.C.R. 906; 1976 (1) SLR 805

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Equal opportunity

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 16 - Kerala State and Subordinate Services Rules, 1958, r. 13AA - Promotions - Exemptions to Scheduled Casts and Tribes - Respondent filed writ petition in HC seeking declaration that r. 13AA of the Rules which provided exemption to members of Scheduled Castes and Tribes from passing tests to cover period during which two tests would be held by Public Service Commission and results thereof published as unconstitutional - HC upheld contentions of respondent and held that impugned rules and all orders of promotion made there under were violative of arts. 16(1) and 16(2) of the Constitution - Hence, instant appeal by State - Whether impugned rules and orders are unconstitutional.

Held, equal protection of laws necessarily involves classification and the validity of classification should be adjudged with reference to the purpose of law. The classification in instant case is justified because the purpose of classification is to enable members of Scheduled Castes and Tribes to find representation by promotion to a limited extent. Hence, classification of employees belonging to Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes for allowing them an extended period of 2 years for passing the special tests for promotion is a just and reasonable classification having rational nexus to the object of providing equal opportunity for all citizens in matters relating to employment or appointment to public office. Hence, r. 13AA of the Rules is valid piece of statutory provision justified u/art. 16(1) of the Constitution and does not fall within the purview of art. 16(4). Hence, validity of r. 13AA of the Rules and two others orders is upheld and judgment of HC is set aside. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Arts. 14 and 16(1) of the Constitution permit reasonable classification having a nexus to the objects to be achieved. U/art. 16, there could be reasonable classification of the employees in matters relating to employment or appointment.
