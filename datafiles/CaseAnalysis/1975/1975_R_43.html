Rameshwar And Ors v Jot Ram & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

18 September 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

V.R. Krishna Iyer, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 247; (1976) 1 SCC 194; AIR 1976 SC 49; [1976] 1 S.C.R. 847

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Punjab security of Land Tenures Act, 1953, s.18 - CPC, 1908, O. 7, r. 7 - Constitution, art. 136 - (A) Purchase of proprietary right by tenant from big landowner - Right vested in tenant by virtue of s. 18(4) - Availability of right his heirs - (B) Practice and Procedure - Power of Court to take note of subsequent events - Right to relief is to be determined as on date of institution of proceedings - Court's procedural delays cannot affect rights crystallised in initial cause of action - (C) Appeal by special leave - New Point - Point of law demanding inquiry into facts not raised in Courts below - Permissibility - Held, not divested by subsequent death of Landowner and devolution of his rights on his heirs - Courts can take note of subsequent events and mould the relief accordingly, but in exceptional circumstances - Held, not allowed - Appeal Allowed.
