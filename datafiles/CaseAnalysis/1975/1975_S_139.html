Anil Kumar Chowdhury v State of Assam and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 March 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

V.R. Krishna Iyer, A.N. Ray, Kuttyil Kurien Mathew

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 194; (1975) 4 SCC 7; (1975) SCC (L&S) 192; AIR 1975 SC 1061; 1975 LabIC 658; [1975] 3 S.C.R. 878; 1975 SLJ 358; 1975 (1) SLR 853; 1975 UJ 316

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Service - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 32 - Indian Administrative Service (Regulations of Seniority ) Rules, 1954, r. 3(3)(b) - Writ Petition u/art. 32 - Preliminary objection - Duty of Court - Service Rules - Requirement under year of allotment formula is continuity in service - Disruption - Effect - Held, to bar processual doors of justice like harsh judicial janitors, one has a right to relief, is reluctant refuge of a Court and that at instance of State at final stage - Continuity once disrupted, claim breaks down - Appeal dismissed.
