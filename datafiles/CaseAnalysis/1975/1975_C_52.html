Indian Chamber of Commerce v Commissioner of Income Tax, West Bengal II, Calcutta

Supreme Court of India

17 September 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

V.R. Krishna Iyer, A.C. Gupta, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 385; (1976) SCC (Tax) 41; (1976) 1 SCC 324; AIR 1976 SC 348; 1975 CTR 271; [1975] 101 ITR 796; [1976] 1 S.C.R. 830; 1976 TAXLR 210

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Trade Association, Chit Fund, Exclusion From Total Income

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 2(15) and 11(1) - Charitable purpose - Assesses are Indian Chambers of Commerce and Cochin Chambers of Commerce, institution which carries out charitable purpose - Criteria for determining 'charitable purpose' as defined in s. 2(15) - Held, burden of proof is on assessee - Emphasis is shifted from 'advancement' to 'object' used in s. 2(15) - Relevant s. 2(15) excludes from exemption carrying on of activities for profit even they are linked with objectives of general public utility, because statute interdicts, for purposes of tax relief, advancement of such objects by involvement in carrying on of activities for profit - When legislative draftsmanship declines to be simple, interpretative complexity becomes a judicial necessity - Incomes of assessees sought to be taxed are taxable - Appeal partly allowed.
