Bhagwan Singh v State of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

09 December 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

P.K. Goswami, P.N. Bhagwati, N.L. Untwalia

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 242; (1976) SCC (Cr) 7; (1976) 1 SCC 389; AIR 1976 SC 202; 1976 CRLJ 203; 1976 CrLR(SC) 48; [1976] 2 S.C.R. 921; 1976 UJ 78

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 154 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 165A and 411 - Stolen property - Court permitted prosecutor to cross-examine his own witness - Witness turned hostile - Whether evidence by a hostile witness is barred for basing conviction? - Held, fact that witness was declared hostile did not completely efface his evidence - It remained admissible in trial, since his testimony was corroborated by other evidence, there was no legal bar to base his conviction upon it - Appeal dismissed.
