Navneet Lal Alias Rangi v Gokul and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 December 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

P.K. Goswami, Kuttyil Kurien Mathew, N.L. Untwalia

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 443; (1976) 1 SCC 630; AIR 1976 SC 794; 1976 (2) ALR 70; [1976] 2 S.C.R. 924

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Summary: Family & Personal - Practice & Procedure - Will executed by 'B' - 'B' had given an absolute estate to his wife under will - Whether deed of gift and will executed by 'B's' wife in favour of appellant was valid? - Will - Construction of - Principles - Held, (1) in construing a document, fundamental rule is to ascertain intention from words used; surrounding circumstances are to be considered for purpose of finding out intended meaning of words which have actually been employed, (2) in construing language of 'will' court is entitled to put itself into testator's armchair and is bound to bear in mind also other matters than rely words used, (3) true intention of testator has to be gathered not by attaching importance to isolated expression but by reading 'will' as a whole all its provisions and ignoring none of them as redundant or contradictory, (4) court must accept, if possible, such construction as would give to every expression some effect rather than that which would render any of expressions inoperative, where apparently conflicting dispositions can be reconciled by giving full effect to every word used in a document, such a construction should be accepted instead of a construction which would have effect of cutting down clear meaning of words used by testator, where one of two reasonable constructions would lead to intestacy, that should be discarded in favour of a construction which does not create any such hiatus, (5) to extent that it is legally possible effect should be given to every disposition contained in 'will' unless law prevents effects being given to it, if there are two repugnant provisions conferring successive interests, if first interest created is valid subsequent interest cannot take effect but a court of construction will proceed to farthest extent to avoid repugnancy, so that effect could be given as far as possible to every testamentary intention contained in 'will' - Further held, testator intended to bequeath in favour of his widow only a life estate and after her death an absolute estate to respondent - That being position 'will' by wife in favour of appellant fails and her gift in favour of appellant also similarly fails on her death - Respondent suit rightly decreed by Trial Court - Appeal dismissed.
