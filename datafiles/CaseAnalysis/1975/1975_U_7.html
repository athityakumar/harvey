P. C. Sethi & Ors. v Union Of India & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

17 January 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

P.K. Goswami, M. Hameedullah Beg, V.R. Krishna Iyer

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 89; (1975) 4 SCC 67; (1975) SCC (L&S) 203; AIR 1975 SC 2164; 1975 LabIC 1590; 1975 (1) LLJ 520; [1975] 3 S.C.R. 201; 1975 SLJ 202; 1975 (1) SLR 783

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Constitution, arts. 14, 16 and 32 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 115 - Central Secretariat Service Rules, 1962, r. 18(1) - Office Memorandum D/- 07-09-1971 - Service matter - (A) Constitutionality of r. 18(1) of 1962 Rules - (B) Delay - Assistants Grade IV of Central Secretariat Service which were appointed in 1944 - 1954 - Promotion to Grade of Section Officer on basis of Civil List of 1962 challenged - Hopes held out by Govt. from time to time - (C) Validity of M.O. D/- 07-09-1971 - (D) Estoppel against Govt. - Admission in affidavits in earlier similar proceedings - And in Parliament on behalf of Govt. - Mere expression of opinion limited to the context - Vague hopes but not specific assurances - Rule held not violative of arts. 14 or 16 - Writ Petition filed In 1972, held not liable to be dismissed on ground of delay - There was no infirmity in the M.O.of 1971, Simply because it was not in conformity with the M.O.of 22-06-1949 - Admissions, held not binding on Govt. so as to create an estoppel - Appeal Partly Allowed.
