Raja Jagdambika Pratap Narain Singh v Central Board of Direct Taxes and Others

Supreme Court of India

17 July 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

V.R. Krishna Iyer, R.S. Sarkaria, A.C. Gupta

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 199; (1975) SCC (Tax) 356; (1975) 4 SCC 578; AIR 1975 SC 1816; 1975 CTR 206; [1975] 100 ITR 698; [1976] 1 S.C.R. 49; 1975 TAXLR 818; 1975 UJ 558

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Constitution - Practice & Procedure - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 226 and 133(1)(a) - Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 30 and 30(2) - Income from mango grove - Agricultural income - Taxed earlier - Petition for refund - Condonation of delay - High Court held it as agricultural income but dismissed petition on ground of laches - Held, art. 226 was not a blanket power, regardless of temporal and discretionary restraints - If a party is inexplicably insouciant and unduly belated due to laches, court may ordinarily deny redress - If HC has exercised its discretion to refuse, Supreme Court declines to disturb such exercise unless ground is too untenable - Appeal dismissed.
