 Kerala State Electricity Board v Indian Aluminium Company Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 September 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

A. Alagiriswami, P.N. Bhagwati, P.K. Goswami, R.S. Sarkaria, A.C. Gupta

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 389; (1976) 1 SCC 466; AIR 1976 SC 1031; [1976] 1 S.C.R. 552

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Electricity & Energy

Keywords: Mines Act, 1923, Kerala Essential Articles Control (Temporary Powers) Act, 1961, Madras Essential Articles Control And Requisitioning (Temporary Powers) Act

Summary: Miscellaneous - Essential Commodities Act, 1955, ss. 2 (a) and 3 - Essential Supplies (Temporary Powers) Act, 1946 - Kerala Essential Articles Control (Temporary Powers) Act, 1961 - Whether when a declaration is made u/s. 2(a) of Act declaring an article as an essential article or an order is made u/s. 3, such a declaration or order becomes part of Act? - Held, s. 2(a) of Act defines 'essential articles' meaning any article which may be declared by Govt. by notified order to be an essential article - It is true that notification issued u/s. 2(a) for electricity as an essential article, enable orders to be made u/s. 3 of Act - Validity of surcharge order - When industrial areas are selected, necessity of acquisition of land in those areas is apparent - The State Legislature has power to make laws only with regard to matters specified in List II and List III in seventh schedule of Constitution - Provided that nothing in this clause shall prevent Parliament from enacting at any time any law with respect to same matter - The Kerala Essential Articles Control Act, 1961 is invalid on ground of excessive delegation - Appeal allowed.
