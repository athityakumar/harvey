Jamilabai Abdul Kadar v Shankerlal Gulabchand and Others

Supreme Court of India

30 April 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

V.R. Krishna Iyer, R.S. Sarkaria, A.C. Gupta

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 403; (1975) 2 SCC 609; AIR 1975 SC 2202; [1975] Supp S.C.R. 336

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: CPC, 1908, O. 3, rr. 1 and 4 - Constitution, art. 133 - Advocates Act, 1961, ss. 29 and 55 -Bombay Pleaders Act. 1920 - (A) Practice and Procedure - (1) Pleader - Scope and ambit of 'acting' - Competence to enter into compromise without client's consent - Duty of junior when appearing with senior - (2) Role of lawyers vis-a-vis the client - National integration of Indian bar - Rights of pleader - Who elects not to be enrolled as advocate - (B) Constitution - Conflict of judicial opinion in the High Courts on the question - Held, counsel should not rush in with a razi where due care will make them fear to tread, junior should rarely consent on his own when there is a senior in brief and a party may validly impugn an act of compromise by his pleader if he is available for consultation but is by-passed and the lawyer must be above board, especially if he is to agree to an adverse verdict - Held, there rights are what they had enjoyed under 1920 Act, notwithstanding its repeal by the Advocates Act - Costs in the Supreme Court - Appeals dismissed.
