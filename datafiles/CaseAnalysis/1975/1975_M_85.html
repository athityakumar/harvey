Mulchandani Electrical and Radio Industries Limited v Their Workmen

Supreme Court of India

24 January 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

A.C. Gupta, M. Hameedullah Beg, Y.V. Chandrachud

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 7; (1975) SCC (L&S) 429; (1975) 4 SCC 731; AIR 1975 SC 2125; 1975 (30) FLR 169; 1975 LabIC 1508; 1975 (1) LLJ 391; 1975 SLJ 626; 1976 (1) SLR 48; 1975 UJ 161

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Constitution, arts. 32 and 226 - Industrial Employment (Standing Orders) Act, 1946, s. 13A and sch., item 9 - (A) Domestic enquiry - Cross-examination of witnesses - Validity -(B) Misconduct of worker - Disciplinary action against worker - Interpretation of Standing orders to determine what amounts to misconduct - Held, enquiry proceeding cannot be impeached as unfair - An act, wherever committed, if it has the effect of subverting discipline or good behaviour within the premises or precincts of the establishment, will amount to misconduct under Order 24 (1) - Appeal Allowed.
