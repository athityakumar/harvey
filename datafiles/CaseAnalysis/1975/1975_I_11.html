 Indira Nehru Gandhi v Shri Raj Narain &Amp; Anr.

Supreme Court of India

07 November 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Ray, Hans Raj Khanna, Kuttyil Kurien Mathew, M. Hameedullah Beg, Y.V. Chandrachud

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 473; (1975) Supp SCC 1; AIR 1975 SC 2299; [1976] 2 S.C.R. 347

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Election; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Election Expenses, Election Agents, Election Dispute, Sovereignty And Integrity Of India, Betting, Competence Of The Legislature, Right To Work, District Election Officer, Free And Fair Elections, Delimitation Of Constituencies

Summary: Election Laws (Amendment) Act, 1975, Pre., ss. 7 and 10 - Constitution, Pre., arts. 14, 31B, 85, 105(3), 122, 245, 246, 327, 329A(4), 329A(5), 329(b), 368 - Representation of the People Act, 1951, ss. 77, 79(b), 83(1)(b), 100(1)(b), 116A, 123, 123(3), 123(7) proviso and 123(6) - Representation of the People (Amendment) Act, 1974, s. 2 - Evidence Act, 1872, s.114, Illus. (g) - Constitution (Amendment) Act, 1974 - Evidence Act, 1872 - Election - (A) Constitution of India - (1) Constitutional validity of the Constitution Amendment Act and the 1975 Act amending the Representation of the People Act - (2) Validity of s.7 inserting a proviso at the end of s.123 (7) of Representation of the People Act - Amendment relating to certain facilities to Prime Minister by virtue of her Office by Government Officials - Amending Act placed in protected 9th sch. of the Constitution - (3) Validation of election - Constitutional Validity - (4) 'Constituent power' of Parliament - Supremacy - (5) Fundamental Rights - Whether part of basic structure of Constitution (B) Evidence Act, 1872 - Non-production of available evidence - Presumption as to adverse inference - It is not obligatory - (C) Interpretation of Statutes - Provision widely worded - Rule of construction - (D) Representation of the People Act, 1951- Election Petition - Corrupt practice - Determination of - (In the light of Amendment by 1975 Act ) - Considering these ancillary issues - (1) Obtaining or procuring assistance from Government servant - (2) Use of religious symbol, if - Cow and Calf - (3) Official duty u/s. S.123(7), proviso - Meaning of - (4) Validity of provisions of amending Acts - Representation of the People (Amendment) Act, 1974, s.2 and ss.7, 8 and 10 of Election Laws (Amendment) Act, 1975 - (5) Election expenses - Expenditure incurred or authorised - Test of - Allegations that the election expenses exceeded the limit of authorised expenditure - Evidence and proof - (6) Expenditure incurred or authorised by political party - Candidate - Duty - (7) Corrupt practice obtaining or procuring assistance from Government servant - Evidence and proof - Standard - (8) 'Obtaining or procuring or abetting or attempting to obtain or procure' - What amounts to - (9) Amendment definition of 'Candidate' u/s.79(b) retrospectively - Validity - (10) Arrangements made by State Government for rostrums and loudspeakers in connection with election tour of the Prime Minister, the returned candidate (11) Validity of proviso to s.123(7) - Amendment not changing the law but merely clarifying the state of law as it really was even before the amendment - Validity of s.123(7) as it existed before 1975 Amendment, not challenged - (12) Insufficient particulars of corrupt practice in election petition - Pleadings and evidence - Rule as to - (13) Use of vehicles - For the furtherance of the prospects of candidates in more than one constituency - Held, (A) Cannot be challenged on ground that a number of Members of Parliament were in detention - Amendment cannot be challenged on ground of violation of art.14 of the Constitution - Per majority, cl.(4) of art.329-A as introduced by the Constitution 39th Amendment Act of 1975 is unconstitutional - It is the constitution and not the constituent power which is supreme - The limitation inherent in the word 'amendment' according to which it is not permissible by amendment of the Constitution to change the basic structure of the Constitution was to operate equally on articles pertaining to fundamental rights as on other articles not pertaining to those rights - (B) Such presumption is always optional and one of fact depending upon the whole set of facts - (C) If a clause of a Constitution or statutory provision is widely worded, the width of its ambit cannot be circumscribed by taking into account the facts of an individual case to which it applies - (D) Held, corrupt practice contemplated by s.123 (7) cannot be committed by any person before there is a 'candidate' for an election - Cow and Calf is not a religious symbol - Official duty will be a duty in law, it include duty carried out in pursuance of administrative, for security, law and order, and matters in aid of public purpose, in connection with election and to illustrate, s.197 of the Cr.P.C. speaks of official duty - Provisions of amending Acts held valid - No case or evidence that the congress Party was the agent, express or implied, of the returned candidate, or acting as the channel through which any money whatsoever was spent by the respondent/ retuned candidate - Candidate is not required to disavow or denounce expenditure - Proof of mens rea is necessary - It is the act of solicitation for the aid of the officials mentioned in s.123(7), whether successful or not, and not the mere fact that certain advantages flow quite naturally and conventionally from the occupation of an office, without any solicitation, or the mere fact that some assistance is voluntarily given by someone to an election campaign, which is penalized by the provision - Amendment is within powers of Parliament to legislate - Arrangements held was not a corrupt practice within s. 123(7) - There could be no challenge to the validity of the amendment - No amount of evidence can be looked into on a case not really set up - Held, should not be allowed and to prevent such circumvention, it is essential that evidence should be led to show as to what was the extent of the user of the vehicle in the constituency concerned - Appeal No.C. A. No. 909 of 1975 Dismissed; Appeal No C. A. No. 887 of 1975 Allowed
