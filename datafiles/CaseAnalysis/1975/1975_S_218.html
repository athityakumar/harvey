Puran Singh and Others v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

25 April 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, N.L. Untwalia

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 620; (1975) 4 SCC 518; (1975) SCC (Cr) 608; 1975 (12) ACC 355; AIR 1975 SC 1674; 1975 CRLJ 1479; 1975 CrLR(SC) 394; [1975] Supp S.C.R. 299

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 97 - Trespass - Right of private defense - Whether causing death comes under exception to criminal charge? - Held, not exceeded right of private defense of person and property - Accused justified in causing death - Where there is real apprehension that aggressor might cause death or grievous hurt to victim which could extend to causing of death also, and it is not necessary that death or grievous hurt should actually be caused before right could be exercised - Reasonable apprehension enough to take action for private defense - Appeal allowed.
