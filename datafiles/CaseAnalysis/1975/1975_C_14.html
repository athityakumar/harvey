Ch. Razik Ram v Ch. Jaswant Singh Chouhan and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 February 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Sarkaria, A. Alagiriswami

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 592; (1975) 4 SCC 769; AIR 1975 SC 667

Case Digest

Subject: Election; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: List Of Witnesses, High Court Rules, Punjab and Haryana High Court Rules and Orders

Summary: Election - Practice & Procedure - Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Representation of the People Act, 1951 - Election petition - Corrupt practices - Evidence - Petitioner and three others contested the election to the Haryana Vidhan Sabha - Petitioner's nearest rival was declared elected - Petitioner filed an election petition under the 1951 Act challenging the election of the appellant on various grounds - HC held that the appellant was guilty of committing the corrupt practices u/s. 123(5) and 123(6) of the 1951 Act and set aside appellant's election - Hence, instant appeal u/s. 116-A of the 1951 Act - Whether charges of corrupt practice were established against appellant - Held, since a charge of corrupt practice was substantially akin to a criminal charge, standard of proof was the same as in a criminal trial - Appellant against whom the charge of corrupt practice was levelled, is presumed to be innocent unless establish each and every ingredient of the charge by clear, unequivocal and unimpeachable evidence beyond reasonable doubt - Direct evidence produced by the petitioner to establish his various alleged grounds consisted of the oral statement of a solitary witness which did not inspire confidence - His evidence suffered from grave defects and indelible blemishes which made wholly unworthy of credit - Evidence of the PWs was also contradictory during examination-in-chief and during cross-examination and not genuine and worthy of credence - Court was under a duty to scrutinise the evidence with more than ordinary care - Hence, charges of corrupt practice were not established against appellant and findings of the HC was reversed in favour of appellant - Appeal allowed.
