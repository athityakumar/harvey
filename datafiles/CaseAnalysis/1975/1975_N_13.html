Nasiruddin v State Transport Appellate Tribunal

Supreme Court of India

29 August 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Ray, Kuttyil Kurien Mathew, V.R. Krishna Iyer, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 231; (1975) 2 SCC 671; AIR 1976 SC 331; [1976] 1 S.C.R. 505

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Carriers & Transportation

Keywords: United Provinces High Court (Amalgamation) Order 1948 (ENG)

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Administrative - Renewal of permit - Rejection - Sustainability - Regional Transport Authority fixed strength of route in question at 5 stage carriage permits - Appellant and respondent No. 3 applied for grant of permits to said route - Regional Transport Authority by order dt.2-10-1961 instead of granting 5permits, increased strength of route to 15 permits and granted one permit each to appellant, respondent No. 3 and 13 others - Permit granted to appellant was valid from 9-6-1961 to 8-6-1964 - On appeals, Appellate Tribunal allowed all 9appeals and remanded matter to Regional Transport Authority for reconsideration - Regional Transport Authority granted 5permits, one of which was granted to respondent No. 1, appellant was not granted a permit - Regional Transport Authority had, in meanwhile increased strength of routes from 5 to 15 and invited applications - Respondents No. 4, 5 and 7 applied for grant of permits - Appellant filed writ petition no. 750 of 1964 challenging order of Appellate Tribunal as well as order of Regional Transport Authority - HC allowed writ petition and order of Appellate Tribunal and Regional Transport Authority were quashed and Appellate Tribunal was directed to re-hear appeals on merits - Regional Transport Authority rejected application of appellant for renewal of his permit on the ground that permit granted to him originally was set aside by Appellate Tribunal by an order dt.28-3-1963 - On appeal, Appellate Tribunal in pursuance of orders of HC in writ petition No. 750 of 1964 issued notice to 15 persons who were granted permits originally and 9 persons, who had preferred appeals, regarding rehearing of appeals - Appellate Tribunal directed Regional Transport Authority to grant one permanent stage carriage permit to each of respondents Nos. 10 to 12 - Respondent Nos. 1 and 3 filed writ petition No. 3294 of 1970 on the ground that Allahabad HC had no jurisdiction to entertain and decide writ petition because dispute arose at Bareilly in Rohilkhand Division, which was within exclusive jurisdiction of Allahabad HC, and it had nothing to do with Oudh territory - Matter was referred to Full Bench - Full Bench held that case falling within jurisdiction of Judges at Lucknow should be presented at Lucknow and not at Allahabad - Lucknow Bench had no jurisdiction to hear writ petition No. 750 of 1964 which gave rise to writ petition No. 3294 of 1970 - Hence instant appeals - Held, first there was no permanent seat of HC at Allahabad - Seats at Allahabad and at Lucknow might be changed in accordance with provisions of order - Second, Chief Justice of HC had no power to increase or decrease areas in oudh from time to time - Areas in oudh were determined once by Chief Justice and, therefore, there was no scope for changing areas - Third, Chief Justice had power under second proviso to paragraph 14 of order to direct in his discretion that any case or class of cases arising in oudh areas should be heard at Allahabad - Any case or class of cases were those which were instituted at Lucknow - Interpretation given by HC that the word 'heard' confers powers on Chief Justice to order that any case or class of cases arising in oudh areas should be instituted or filed at Allahabad, instead of Lucknow was wrong - The word 'heard' means that cases which were already instituted or filed at Lucknow might in the discretion of Chief Justice under second proviso to paragraph 14 of order he directed to be heard at Allahabad - Fourth, expression 'cause of action' with regard to a civil matter means that it should be left to litigant to institute cases at Lucknow Bench or at Allahabad Bench according to cause of action arising wholly or in part within either of areas - If cause of action arises wholly within oudh areas then Lucknow Bench would have jurisdiction - Similarly, if cause of action arises wholly outside specified areas in oudh then Allahabad would have jurisdiction - If cause of action in part arises in specified oudh areas and part of cause of action arises outside specified areas, it would be open to litigant to frame the case appropriately to attract jurisdiction either at Lucknow or at Allahabad - Fifth, a criminal case arises where offence was committed or otherwise as provided in CrPC - That would attract jurisdiction of Court at Allahabad or Lucknow - In some cases depending on facts and provision regarding jurisdiction, it might arise in either place - Applications u/art. 226 would similarly lie either at Lucknow or at Allahabad as applicant would allege that whole of cause of action or part of cause of action arose at Lucknow within specified areas of oudh or part of cause of action arose at a place outside specified oudh areas - Hence, matters were sent back to HC for disposal in accordance with law - Order accordingly.
