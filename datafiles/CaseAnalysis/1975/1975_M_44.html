Commissioner Of Sales-Tax, U.P. v Mangal Sen Shyam Lal

Supreme Court of India

02 April 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Sarkaria

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 137; (1975) 4 SCC 35; AIR 1975 SC 1106; 1975 (1) ALR 288; 1975 (35) STC 621; [1975] Supp S.C.R. 58; 1975 TAXLR 1535; 1975 UJ 368

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure; Sales Tax

Summary: U.P. Sales Tax Act, 1948, s. 10(3-B), r. 70(1) - (A) Starting point of limitation for revision against original assessment order by Department - Interpreting the word 'Service' - (B) Interpretation of Statutes - Duty of Court - Importance of maxim 'ut res magis valeat quam pereat' - Intent of legislature ambiguous - Held, date of service of the order is the terminus a quo for limitation and not the date of the order - Aids to interpretation not helpful It is a cage for Legislature to amend statute to make intent clear - Appeal Allowed.
