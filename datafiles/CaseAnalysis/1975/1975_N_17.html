Mrs. Shakila Khader and Others v Nausheer Cama and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 March 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

A. Alagiriswami, N.L. Untwalia

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 585; (1975) 4 SCC 122; (1975) SCC (Cr) 379; 1975 ACJ 363; AIR 1975 SC 1324; 1975 CRLJ 1105; 1975 CRLR 255; 1975 UJ 393

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, s. 342 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 304-A, 337, 338 and 427 - Rash and negligent driving - Whether accused was guilty of driving rashly and negligently? - Main criterion for deciding whether driving which led to accident was rash and negligent is not only speed but width of road, density of traffic, and attempt to overtake other vehicles resulting in going to wrong side of road and being responsible for accident - Even if accident took place in twinkling of an eye it is not difficult for eyewitness to notice a car overtaking other vehicles and going to wrong side of road - Held, there is only one conclusion possible on facts of this case, that accused came over to wrong side of road and was responsible for accident and that is clearly a rash and negligent act in condition of road and condition of traffic - Appeals allowed.
