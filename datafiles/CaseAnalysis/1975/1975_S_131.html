A. Panduranga Rao v State of Andhra Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

02 September 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

N.L. Untwalia, A. Alagiriswami, P.K. Goswami

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 144; (1975) SCC (L&S) 407; (1975) 4 SCC 709; AIR 1975 SC 1922; 1975 (1) ALR 584; 1975 LabIC 1452; [1976] 1 S.C.R. 620; 1976 SLJ 3; 1975 (2) SLR 582; 1975 UJ 785

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Constitution - Advocates & Judges - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 233(2) - Meaning of 'Recommendation' - Held, literal meaning of word 'recommended' in Concise Oxford Dictionary is quite simple - It means 'suggest as fit for employment' - In case of appointment from Bar, it is not open to State Govt. to choose a candidate for appointment until and unless his name is recommended by High Court Appointment of District Judges - Recruitment from Bar - Candidates not recommended by HC - No appointment without recommendation of HC under cls. (2) of art. 233 - Appeal allowed.
