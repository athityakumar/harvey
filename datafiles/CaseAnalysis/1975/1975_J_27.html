John Martin v State of West Bengal

Supreme Court of India

21 January 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Bhagwati, Kuttyil Kurien Mathew, N.L. Untwalia

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 603; (1975) SCC (Cr) 255; (1975) 3 SCC 836; AIR 1975 SC 775; 1975 CRLJ 637; [1975] 3 S.C.R. 211

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 141 and 22 - Maintenance of Internal Security Act, 1971, ss. 8(1) and 3(4) - Defence of India Rules, 1962, r. 30(1)(b) - Preventive detention - Validity of - Whether order for detention made by District Magistrate, Burdwan u/sub-s. (1), r/w. sub-s. (2) of s. 3 of Maintenance of Internal Security Act, 1971, is malafide? - Held, merely from fact that authorities decided to drop case under Official Secrets Act and thereafter to order detention of petitioners under rules that order of detention was mala fide - Order for detention of petitioner has not been shown to be mala fide - Same reasons hold that order of detention made by District Magistrate in present case does not suffer from vice of mala fide or colourable exercise of power - Rules discharged.
