Ratnam Chettiar and Others v S. M. Kuppuswami Chettiar and Others

Supreme Court of India

18 September 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, V.R. Krishna Iyer

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 505; (1976) 1 SCC 214; AIR 1976 SC 1; [1976] 1 S.C.R. 863

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Constitution, art. 133 - Hindu law - (A) Concurrent findings of fact - Interference by Supreme Court - (B) Partition of Joint Hindu Family - Valdidity of partition of immovable properties - (C) Partition - Re-opening of - Minors - Held, it is a well-settled practice of the SC not to interfere with a concurrent finding of fact given by the two Courts below in the absence of any extraordinary or special reasons - Held partition could not be held to be unfair or unjust - Held, it is open to the Court to maintain the transaction which is just and fair and to reopen the partition that is unjust and unfair - Appeal Allowed.
