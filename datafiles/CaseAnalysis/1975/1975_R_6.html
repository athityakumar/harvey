Commissioner of Income Tax, West Bengal II v Radha Krishan Nandlal

Supreme Court of India

06 March 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.V. Chandrachud, R.S. Sarkaria, A.C. Gupta

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 319; (1975) 1 SCC 693; AIR 1975 SC 893; 1975 (4) CTR 136; [1975] 99 ITR 143; 1975 TAXLR 393; 1975 UJ 307

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 66(2) - Findings of fact by Tribunal - Reference - Revenue filed application in HC u/s. 66(2) of the Act praying that Tribunal be asked to refer two questions to HC - HC even though granted rule on said application yet discharged it without speaking order - On appeal, SC granted special leave to appeal from said order of HC - Whether order of HC could be upheld.

Held, finding of the Tribunal that Jhunjhunwalla had an 8 annas share in the branch firm seems to be based on no evidence while its attempted estimate of the income of the branch firm on the basis of advances made to it by assessee-firm seems prima facie speculative and opposed to the evidence on record. Hence, judgment of Tribunal raises the questions whether it misdirected itself in law in basing its conclusion on some evidence ignoring the other essential matters on record and whether the conclusion to which it came is such as no reasonable person could have come to. Further, HC was not right in discharging the rule without giving any reasons. Hence, HC was directed to call for a reference from Tribunal on two questions and to deal with the same in accordance with law. Order accordingly.

Ratio - Even though the finding of fact recorded by Tribunal is binding on HC, yet it should appear that the Tribunal had considered evidence covering all the essential matters before arriving at its conclusion and there has to be some evidence to support the finding of the Tribunal.
