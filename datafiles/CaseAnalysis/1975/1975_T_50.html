Ram Ranjan Chatterjee v The State Of West Bengal

Supreme Court of India

22 January 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Sarkaria, V.R. Krishna Iyer, P.K. Goswami

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 91; (1975) 4 SCC 143; (1975) SCC (Cr) 400; AIR 1975 SC 609; 1975 CRLJ 588; 1975 CrLR(SC) 241; [1975] 3 S.C.R. 301; 1975 UJ 398

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Defence & Security Forces; Tort

Keywords: Defence Regulations

Summary: Maintenance of Internal Security Act, 1971, s. 3 - (A) 'Public order' and Law and order' - Distinction between is one of degree - Exploding of bombs and attempt at extortion on pain of death - (B) Detention order - Whether passed mechanically - Exploding of bombs and attempt at extortion on pain of death enough to cause disturbance of public order - Held, the second contention, although attractive, does not stand a close examination, order was passed after due consideration on relevant grounds - Petition Dismissed.
