Management of Indian Oil Corporation Limited v Its Workmen

Supreme Court of India

24 July 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, A.N. Ray, Kuttyil Kurien Mathew, V.R. Krishna Iyer

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 202; (1975) SCC (L&S) 453; (1976) 1 SCC 63; AIR 1975 SC 1856; 1975 (31) FLR 178; 1975 LabIC 1429; 1975 (2) LLJ 319; [1976] 1 S.C.R. 110

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Cost Of Living, Housing

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 9A - Insustrial Dispute - Oil Refineries - Voluntary grant of Assam compensatory allowance - Not binding on management - (A) Whether grant of allowance part of service conditions? - (B) Whether unilateral decision pay house rent by withrawing allowance, attracts s. 9A of Act? - Held, an implied condition - Employer sought to effect a substantial change which adversely and materially affected service conditions of workmen for all times to come, therefore s. 9A was clearly applicable and as s. 9A had not been complied with withdrawal was clearly illegal - Appeal dismissed.
