Commissioner of Income Tax (Central), Delhi v Harparsad and Company Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

25 February 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Sarkaria, Y.V. Chandrachud, A.C. Gupta

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 315; (1975) 3 SCC 868; AIR 1975 SC 1282; 1975 (4) CTR 65; 1975 (1) ITJ 277; [1975] 99 ITR 118; [1975] 3 S.C.R. 696; 1975 TAXLR 404

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 2(6C), 12B, 2292A), 24 - Capital loss - Carry forward - During proceedings before Tribunal, new plea that amount held to be 'capital loss' by authorities should be allowed to be carried forward and set off against profits and gains, if any, under the head 'capital gains' was taken by assessee - Tribunal allowed said new ground and later made reference to HC on whether capital loss could be carried forward in accordance with s. 24 of the Act, when provisions of s. 12B of the Act itself were not applicable to A/Y - HC answered reference in affirmative and hence, instant appeal by revenue - Whether capital losses could be carried forward.

Held, during period 1948-1957, 'capital gains' whether on positive or the negative side, could not be computed and charged u/s. 12B or any other provisions of the Act. Hence, in the relevant previous year and the A/Y, or even in the subsequent year, capital gains or 'capital losses' did not form part of the 'total income' of the assessee which could be brought to charge, and were, hence, not required to be computed under the Act. Hence, HC was in error in answering the question referred to it, in favour of assessee and so, the same is reversed accepting appeal of revenue with costs. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Capital gains would be covered by definition of 'income' in s. 2(6C) of the Act only if they were chargeable u/s. 12B of the Act. Further, after insertion of s. 22(2A) in the Act, it is condition precedent to the carry-forward and set off of, the loss, that the assessee should file a return either in response to a general notice u/s. 22(1) of the Act or voluntarily, without any individual notice u/s. 22(2) of the Act.
