D. L. F. Housing Construction Private Limited v Delhi Municipal Corporation and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 December 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Sarkaria, A.N. Ray, M. Hameedullah Beg, P.N. Shingal

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 446; (1976) 3 SCC 160; AIR 1976 SC 386

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Municipalities & Local Governments - Delhi Municipal Corporation Act, 1957 - Question of fact and law - Writ Jurisdiction - Municipal Corporation issued notices to appellant directing it to transfer plots mentioned there under to it free of cost on ground that all such plots in all the colonies developed by appellant-colonizer which had been set apart for public utility services, vest in Municipal Corporation in accordance with Regulations - Hence, appellant-colonizer filed writ petition before HC but the same was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether appeal could be allowed.

Held, Court has been called upon to decide disputed questions of fact and law relating to the precise nature and extent of right, title and interest of the parties in the plots in question. However, basic documentary evidence, such as the orders granting the sanctions, the conditions of the sanctions, and the agreements in which they are said to have been incorporated, were not produced before the pronouncement of judgment in HC. In the absence of firm and adequate factual foundation, it was hazardous to embark upon determination of the points involved and hence, right course for HC to follow is to dismiss writ petition on preliminary ground itself, without entering upon merits of the case. Hence, findings of HC are set aside and writ petition and instant appeal are dismissed with costs. However, appellant could seek their remedy by regular suit. Appeal dismissed.

Ratio - In a case where the basic facts are disputed and complicated questions of law and fact depending on evidence are involved, then the Writ Court is not the proper forum for seeking relief.
