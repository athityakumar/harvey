Messrs Balabhagas Hulaschand v State of Orissa

Supreme Court of India

09 December 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, Kuttyil Kurien Mathew

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 440; (1976) 2 SCC 44; (1976) SCC (Tax) 164; AIR 1976 SC 1016; 1976 (5) CTR 175; [1976] 2 S.C.R. 939; 1976 (37) STC 207; 1976 TAXLR 1538

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Central Sales Tax Act, 1956, ss. 2(g), 3(a) and 9 - Meaning of word 'Sale' in ss.2(g) and 3(a) - Sale taking place in course of inter-State trade - Conditions to be satisfied - Held, following conditions must be satisfied - (i) that there is an agreement to sell which contains a stipulation express or implied regarding the movement of the goods from one State of another; (ii) that in pursuance of the said contract the goods in fact move from one State to another; and (iii) that ultimately a concluded sale takes place in the State where the goods are sent which must be different from the State from which the goods - Appeal Dismissed.
