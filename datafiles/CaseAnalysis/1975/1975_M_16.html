Udhav Singh v Madhav Rao Scindia

Supreme Court of India

10 October 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Sarkaria, P.N. Bhagwati

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 262; (1977) 1 SCC 511; AIR 1976 SC 744; [1976] 2 S.C.R. 246

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Election; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Lok Sabha election, Corruption practices

Summary: Election - Representation of the People Act, 1951, ss. 82(b) and 123(1) - Lok sabha election - Corruption practices - Legality - Six candidates filed nomination papers for contesting the election to Lok Sabha - Respondent declared elected by a margin of 1,41,090 votes over his nearest rival, D, sponsored by Indian National Congress - Elector of Constituency filed an election petition before HC challenging the election of respondent - HC dismissed the election petition preferred by petitioner - Hence instant appeal - Whether HC was justified in passing the impugned order - Held, corrupt practice charged against respondent should be a full and complete statement of material facts to clothe petitioner with a complete cause of action and to give an equal and full opportunity to respondent to meet case and to defend charges - Merely, alleging that respondent obtained or procured or attempted to obtain or procure assistance were extracting words from statute which would have no meaning unless and until facts were stated to show what that assistance was and how prospect of election was furthered by such assistance - In instant case, it was not even alleged that assistance obtained or procured was other than giving of vote - It was said by respondent that because statute did not render giving of vote a corrupt practice words 'any assistance' were full statement of material fact - However, gist of corrupt practice of 'undue influence' as defined in s. 123(1) of the Act was 'direct or indirect interference or attempt to interfere on part of candidate or his agent, or of any other person with consent of candidate or his election agent with free exercise of any electoral right' - It was clear the S and others of had administered a threat to Congress Worker and an elector, that threat was not to vote for Congress candidate - Allegations of a complete charge of corrupt practice against a candidate, S and in view of s. 82(b) of the Act it was obligatory for petitioner to implead him, as also, as a respondent failure to do so - Appeal dismissed.
