Municipal Corporation Of Delhi v Kacheroo Mal

Supreme Court of India

29 September 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Sarkaria, A.C. Gupta

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 256; (1976) SCC (Cr) 30; (1976) 1 SCC 412; AIR 1976 SC 394; 1976 CRLJ 336; 1975 CrLR(SC) 589; [1976] 2 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection; Criminal; Health & Drug; Socio-Economic

Keywords: Food Adulteration

Summary: Prevention of Food Adulteration Act, 1954, ss. 2(i)(f) and 13 - Evidence Act, 1872 - (A) Scope of expression 'insect infested' - (B) Whether insect infestation is sufficient to hold the article adultrated - (C) Applicability of 2(i)(f) to an article in respect of which no minimum standard of purity is prescribed - Held, includes infestation even by dead insects - Mere insect infestation is not sufficient to hold the article as 'adulterated', further proof that the article was unfit for human consumption is a must - Appeal Dismissed.
