Legal Remembrancer of Government of West Bengal v Haridas Mundra

Supreme Court of India

09 December 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Bhagwati, P.K. Goswami, N.L. Untwalia

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 243; (1976) SCC (Cr) 67; (1976) 1 SCC 555; AIR 1976 SC 2225; 1976 CRLJ 1732; 1976 CrLR(SC) 67; [1976] 2 S.C.R. 933; 1976 UJ 133

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Special Officer, Code of Criminal Procedure 1898

Summary: Criminal - Criminal Procedure Code, 1898, s. 195(1) - Indian Companies Act, 1956, ss. 397 and 398 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 134(1)(c) - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120 B, 409, 418, 468, 471, 475, 476 and 477 A - Applicability of s. 195(1)(c) of CrPC, 1898 - Held, scope and ambit of s. 195(1)(c) is restricted to cases where offence was alleged to have been committed by a party to a proceeding after he became such party and not before - Since offence charged against respondent was one alleged to have been committed by him before he became a party to proceeding in Matter No. 357 of 1957, s. 195(1)(c) had no application - Neither of two forged bills of Indian Machine Tools Co. was produced or given in evidence in proceeding, requirement of s. 195(1)(c) is that document in question should be produced or given in evidence in proceeding - Therefore, s. 195(1) was clearly not satisfied and on this ground also, s. 195(1)(c) was not attracted in present case - Criminal Appeal 256 of 1971 allowed - Criminal Appeals 115 and 257 of 1971 dismissed.
