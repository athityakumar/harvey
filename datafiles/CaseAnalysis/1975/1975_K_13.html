Gurucharan Singh v Kamla Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 September 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

V.R. Krishna Iyer, A.C. Gupta, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 512; (1976) 2 SCC 152; AIR 1977 SC 5; [1976] 1 S.C.R. 739

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Constitution - Municipalities & Local Governments - Constitution of India, 1950 - Estates Partition Act, 1897 - Whether the High Court rightly held that the defendants, urged with success that the plaintiff had lost title in the property as per the provisions of s. 6 of Estates Partition Act, 1897 - The possession of the contesting defendants in the present case was in their own right and adverse to the plaintiffs, even on the case with which the appellants themselves came into court - The plea made by the plaintiffs relevant to the character of the possession of the contesting defendants assumes crucial importance, for if they were admittedly trespassers then they could not be said to hold the property on behalf of the mortgagors - Held, it is not possible for the appellants to contend that the tenants were in possession of the property on behalf of the mortgagor and in the character of their rights being derived from the mortgagor - Held, the appellant had no khas possession of the suit lands and cannot use s. 6 as a rescue raft - Appellants title was lost when s. 4 was notified as applicable to the suit lands by s. 3 in 1956 - Without title one could not maintain the action for recovery of possession - Held, appellants entitled only to mesne profits from the defendants, until the date of vesting - Held, the appeal is dismissed.
