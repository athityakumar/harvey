Tara Singh and Others v State of Rajasthan and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 March 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Ray, Kuttyil Kurien Mathew, V.R. Krishna Iyer

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 407; (1975) 4 SCC 86; (1975) SCC (L&S) 222; AIR 1975 SC 1487; 1975 LabIC 1046; [1975] 3 S.C.R. 1002; 1975 SLJ 619; 1975 (1) SLR 777

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Rajasthan Civil Service Rules, 1951, r. 244(2) - Constitution, art. 309 Proviso, 14, 19, 31 and 311 - Case u/r. 244(2) - (A) Interpretation of Statutes - Notes u/r. 244(2), are promulgated with the rules in exercise of legislative power - Pourpose and scope - (B) Amendment - Effect - Original rule provided that compulsory retirement before superannuation could be ordered in 'public interest' - After amendment the words as to 'public interest' have been omitted - (C) Compulsory Retirement - (1) When may be ordered - (2) Public servant - Order of compulsory retirement made in accordance with the Service Rules made u/art. 309 - Validity - (3) Applicability of art.311(2) - Held : (A) The Notes are made contemporaneously with the Rules - The function of the Notes is to provide procedure and control discretion - The real purpose of the Notes is that when rules are silent, the Notes will fill the gaps - (B) But the 'Notes' appended, to the Rule supply the omission - (C) When persons complete 25 years of service and the efficiency of such persons is impaired and yet, it is desirable not to bring any charge of inefficiency or incompetency, the Government passes orders of such compulsory retirement - Order held, not violative of Arts. 14, 19, 31 and 311 - Held that the order did not contain any stigma against the petitioner so as to attract the applicability of art 311(2) - Petitions dismissed.
