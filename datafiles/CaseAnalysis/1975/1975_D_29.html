Dilip Kumar Sharma and Others v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

10 October 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.V. Chandrachud, P.N. Bhagwati, R.S. Sarkaria

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 484; (1976) SCC (Cr) 85; (1976) 1 SCC 560; AIR 1976 SC 133; 1976 CRLJ 184; [1976] 2 S.C.R. 289

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code,1860 - Constitution of India,1950 - Previous Conviction - Appellants were tried by the Sessions Judge for committing the murder of the respondent - Appellant no. 1 was convicted u/s. 302, appellant no. 2 u/s. 302 r/w s. 34, appellant no. 3 u/s. 303, of the IPC and sentenced to death - HC confirmed the conviction and sentence of appellant nos. 1 and 3 - Conviction of appellant no. 2 was also confirmed but the HC reduced his sentence to imprisonment for life - Appellant no. 3 was earlier convicted on a charge of murder for which he was sentenced to life imprisonment - On appeal, appellant no. 3 was subsequently acquitted by the Court - Sessions Court had no option in the matter of sentence because when it found appellant no. 3 guilty of the murder of respondent in the instant case, he was 'under sentence of imprisonment for life' imposed upon him in the earlier case which was in operation on the date when the Sessions Court delivered its judgment - Therefore, he was sentenced to death with no alternative sentence - Appellant no.3 contended that he could not be sentenced to death since court was required to take into account the subsequent event of acquittal into consideration and by reason of that event s. 303 of the IPC would cease to have application - Hence, instant appeal - Whether the conviction and sentence of appellant no. 3 u/s. 303, IPC was proper - Held, s. 303, IPC was attracted to a case where a person having subsisting sentence of imprisonment for life committed a murder, that murder being committed when the sentence was in force, notwithstanding the fact that in a pending appeal the sentence was set aside subsequent to the commission of the crime - When a person who was sentenced to imprisonment for life committed a murder, the previous conviction assumed a graver proportion and became an aggravating circumstance but the aggravation was on the assumption that the previous conviction was lawful and valid - If a court, whether of the first instance or otherwise, found on the date on which it recorded its decision that the accused before it was no longer under a sentence of life imprisonment, it could not u/s. 303 of the IPC sentence him to death - Death sentence awarded to appellant no. 3 with the aid of s. 303, IPC was set aside - His conviction u/s. 302/34, IPC for the murder of respondent was upheld and he was sentenced to undergo imprisonment for life - Appeal dismissed.
