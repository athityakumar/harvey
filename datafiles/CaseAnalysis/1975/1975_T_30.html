Baldev Singh v Teja Singh Swatantar (Dead) & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

24 January 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

V.R. Krishna Iyer, A. Alagiriswami, R.S. Sarkaria

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 93; (1975) 4 SCC 406; AIR 1975 SC 693; [1975] 3 S.C.R. 381

Case Digest

Subject: Election; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Representation of the People Conduct of Elections Rules, 1961, r. 63 - Representation of People Act, 1951 - Election - (A) When recounting to be ordered - (B) Ballot paper not having signature of presiding officer - Effect - (C) Election personnel - To be fair to all candidates - Held, Court frowns upon frivolous and unreasonable refusals of recount by Returning Officers who forget the mandate of r. 63 that allowance of recount is not the exception and refusal is restricted to cases where the demand itself is 'frivolous' and held that no good grounds for a Court order for inspection and recount existed - The ballot paper should not be rejected merely - The Court must underscore the utmost importance of the independence fairness and activism of the Election personnel from the Commission to the counting staff and frivolous suggestions linking persons in high office should not be allowed to be flung in court, without sound basis previously laid - Appeal Dismissed.
