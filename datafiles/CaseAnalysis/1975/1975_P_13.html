The Commissioner Of Sales Tax, Uttar Pradesh, Lucknow v Parson Tools And Plants, Kanpur

Supreme Court of India

27 February 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Sarkaria, A.C. Gupta, Y.V. Chandrachud

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 371; (1975) 4 SCC 22; (1975) SCC (Tax) 185; AIR 1975 SC 1039; 1975 CTR 88; [1975] 3 S.C.R. 743; 1975 (35) STC 413; 1975 TAXLR 1529; 1975 UJ 267

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Criminal; Practice & Procedure; Sales Tax

Summary: U.P. Sales Tax Act, 1948, ss. 10 - U.P. Sales Tax Rules, 1948, rr.68(5) and 68(6) - Limitation Act, 1963, ss. 5 and 14(2) - Interpretation of Statutes - Special and general statute - Provision prescribing period of limitation in special statutes - Limitation Revision filed beyond time prescribed by s. 10 - Condonation of, delay - Whether principles of ss. 5 and 14(2) of 1963 Act attracted to proceedings under the1948 Act - Held, principles of general statute cannot be imported into special statute - Held, HC was in error in importing whole hog the principle of s.14(2) of 1963 Act into s.10(3-B) 1948 Act - Appeal Allowed.
