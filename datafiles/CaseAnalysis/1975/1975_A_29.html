Arya Vyasa Sabha and Others v Commissioner of Hindu Charitable and Others

Supreme Court of India

24 November 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Sarkaria, A.N. Ray, M. Hameedullah Beg, P.N. Shingal

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 458; (1976) 1 SCC 292; AIR 1976 SC 475

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Trusts & Associations - Religious institution - Competent court - Instant batch of appeals were filed against judgment of HC on ground that HC made some observations in writ petitions indicating that the members of appellants does not belong to religious denomination and that said finding was incorrect as it would debar appellants from agitating questions involved in any other forum - Whether HC left said question undecided.

Held, questions involved were left open by HC since they were disputed questions of fact and could not be appropriately determined in proceedings u/art. 226 and they were to be agitated in proper forum. Further, writ petitioners were left at liberty to establish their claim in regard to their institutions being religious denominations in a competent civil court. Appeals disposed of.
