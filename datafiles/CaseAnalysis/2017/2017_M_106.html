Manuara Khatun and another v Rajesh Kr. Singh and others

Supreme Court of India

21 February 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Sapre, J. Chelameswar

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 129; (2017) 4 SCC 796; 2017 (2) ACC 476; 2017 ACJ 1031; AIR 2017 SC 1204; 2017 (2) ALD(SC) 65; 2017 (123) ALR 215; 2017 (3) Bom.C.R. 18; JT 2017 (2) SC 490; 2017 (2) RCR(Civil) 108; 2017(3) SCALE 93; 2017 (1) WLN(SC) 170

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance

Keywords: Insurance Company, Insurer, National Insurance, Motor Vehicles Act, 1988

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Insurance - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, ss. 147, 173 - Motor accident claim - Enhancement of compensation - Entitled to - Deceased succumbed to death in a motor accident - Appellants filed claim petition before Tribunal - Tribunal allowed petitions and awarded a sum of Rs.24,89,500/- to appellant no. 1 and Rs.24,09,500/- to appellant no. 2 with interest @ 7.5% p.a. - On appeals, HC dismissed appeals holding that insurer was not liable because passengers or occupants were being carried in a private vehicle as "gratuitous passengers" -

Hence, instant appeals - Whether appellants were entitled for enhancement of compensation awarded.

Held, deceased were found travelling as "gratuitous passengers" in offending vehicle and it was for that reason, insurance companies were exonerated. The direction to respondent no. 3 being the insurer of offending vehicle which was found involved in causing accident due to negligence of its driver needs to be issued directing them to first pay the awarded sum to appellants and then to recover the paid awarded sum from the owner of offending vehicle/respondent no.1 in execution proceedings arising in instant case as per the law laid down in Manager, National Insurance Co. Ltd. v. Saju P. Paul And Another, 2013 Indlaw SC 325. Impugned order was modified to the extent that respondent no. 3 was directed to pay the awarded sum to appellants. Respondent no. 3 would be entitled to recover the entire paid awarded sum from the owner/insured of offending Vehicle in those very proceedings by filing execution application against insured. Appeals allowed.
