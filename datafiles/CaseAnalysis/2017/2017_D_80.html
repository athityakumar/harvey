Dinubhai Boghabhai Solanki v State of Gujarat and others

Supreme Court of India

30 October 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, Ashok Bhushan

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 849

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Cancellation Of Bail, Fair Trial, Criminal Justice System, CBI, Jurisdiction, Indian Evidence Act, 1872, Criminal Trial, Presiding Officer, Contents, Jail, Cognizance, Innocent, Criminal Justice, Prosecutor, Dealing, Arms Act, 1959, Interfere, Deposition, Remarks, Judiciary, Threats, Hostility, Registered, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Constitution of India, 1950, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, Complainant, Plea

Summary: (A) Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 114, 120B, 302 - Murder - Acquittal - Sustainability - Appellant/accused allegedly committed murder of complainant's son/deceased - Complainant approached HC for transfer of investigation which was allowed and transferred to CBI - Charge sheet was filed against accused u/s. 302 r/w s. 120B IPC and accused was arrested - On appeal, HC dismissed appeal however, granted conditional bail to accused - Aggrieved, complainant filed criminal miscellaneous petition before HC seeking cancellation of bail on ground that accused was threatening and influencing witnesses which was allowed - Hence, instant appeals - Whether it was a case where entire de novo trial was necessitated.

Held, CBI had stated before HC that de novo trial might not be necessary and the purpose would be served by recalling 46 witnesses, out of which 8 witnesses are cited as eye-witnesses. Examination of all the witnesses once again in de novo trial might not be appropriate in the circumstances of the case. Directing Trial Court to spend such kind of time once again was a tall order and the same purpose which was sought to be achieved by HC could be served by re-examining only those witnesses which were absolutely necessary. However, out of 195 witnesses, if 105 witnesses were declared hostile, 90 other witnesses were examined and cross-examined and their deposition was not required to be recorded again. Further, though some of the witnesses though turned hostile, their testimony might not have much bearing. Therefore, 26 witnesses list was furnished in SC should be re-examined. Appeals disposed of.

(B) Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 114, 120B, 302 - Strictures against Presiding Officer - Justifiability - Whether HC was justified in passing strictures against Presiding Officer of Trial Court.

Held, no fault could be formed about the general observations of HC about the role of Trial Court who was not supposed to be a mute spectator when he finds that witnesses after witnesses were turning hostile. SC require to adopt a balancing approach which takes care of right of liberty of accused as an undertrial and at the same time the interest of society in general, i.e. fair trial was fulfilled. Thus, retrial was ordered by HC was maintained in principle with only modification that instead of all witnesses, 26 witnesses would be re-examined and in order to ensure that there was fair trial in literal sense of the term, at least till the time 8 eye-witnesses were re-examined. Bail granted to accused stands cancelled for the time being. Accused should be taken into custody and remain in custody during the period 8 eye-witnesses are re-examined. Trial Court should endeavour to record the remaining evidence as expeditiously as possible by conducting the trial on day to day basis. Appeals disposed of.
