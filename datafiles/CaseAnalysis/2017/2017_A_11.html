Director of Income Tax (IT) - I v A. P. Moller Maersk A. S.

Supreme Court of India

17 February 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, A.M. Sapre

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 135; (2017) 5 SCC 651; AIR 2017 SC 1756; [2017] 392 ITR 186; 2017(3) SCALE 123; [2017] 246 TAXMAN 309

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Securities, Technical Services, Shipping Business, Income-tax Act, 1961

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income-tax Act, 1961, ss. 115A, 19 - Technical services fees - Tax levied - Applicability - Respondent/assessee a foreign company engaged in shipping business - AO assessed income of assessee and held that amounts paid by agents to assessee was consideration/fees for technical services and taxable u/art. 13(4) of Double Taxation Avoidance Agreement (DTAA) - On appeal, Commissioner dismissed appeal - On further appeal, Tribunal allowed appeal holding that agents were acting as clearing agents and were entitled to utilisation of Maersk Net facility - Aggrieved, appellants filed appeal before HC - HC dismissed appeal holding that Tribunal has correctly observed that utilisation of Maersk Net Communication System was an automated software based communication system which did not require assessee to render any technical services - Hence, instant appeals - Whether HC was correct in holding that the income from use of 'Maersk Net' could be classified as income arising out of shipping business and not as fees for technical services.

Held, no technical services were provided by assessee to agents. Once those were accepted payments made by agents could be treated as fee for technical service. It was reemphasised that neither AO nor Commissioner stated that there was any profit element embedded in the payments received by assessee from its agents. Record shows that assessee had given the calculations of total costs and pro-rata division thereof among the agents for reimbursement. Once the character of payment was found to be in the nature of reimbursement of expenses, it cannot be income chargeable to tax. It was found that Maersk Net System was an integral part of shipping business and business cannot be conducted without the same, which was allowed to be used by agents of assessee as well in order to enable them to discharge their role more effectively as agents, it was only a facility that was allowed to be shared by agents. In instant case, a common facility of using Maersk Net System was provided to all the agents across Countries to carry out their work using the said system. However, it was the principle of law enunciated in Director of Income Tax (International Taxation)-I v. Safmarine Container Lines NV, 2014 Indlaw MUM 841, which was followed. Art.17 of the Agency Agreement between assessee and Indian agents which provides that assessee might from time to time, temporarily place its employees in agents office "for training or other purposes". However, it could nowhere be pointed out that payment in question was made by agents to assessee for aforesaid purposes. Further, it was clearly established that payment made by assessee was not for reimbursement of any technical services. Appeals dismissed.
