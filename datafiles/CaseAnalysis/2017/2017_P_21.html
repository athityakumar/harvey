State of Kerala and others v Palakkad Heritage Hotels

Supreme Court of India

13 April 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Khanwilkar, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 309; AIR 2017 SC 2369; 2017(3) ALL MR 913; JT 2017 (7) SC 1; 2017 (4) MLJ 495; 2017 (3) RCR(Civil) 273; 2017(5) SCALE 7

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Excise

Keywords: Foreign Liquor Rules

Summary: Excise - Administrative - Foreign Liquor Rules - Liquor license - Denial - Challenged - Respondent submitted an application for grant of a Beer/Wine Bar FL-11 Licence under the Rules - However, the Rules came to be amended on 18-4-2012 which prescribes minimum distance of 200 metres from an objectionable institution - Appellants/State called upon Excise Commissioner to examine the proposal of respondent - Excise Commissioner came to the conclusion that application submitted by respondent deserved to be rejected and directed it to be returned to Dpty Commissioner - Aggrieved, respondent filed writ petition before HC - Single Judge allowed petition holding that Excise Commissioner had issued an order in favour of respondent sanctioning FL-11 licence - On appeal, DB dismissed appeal - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, processing of application for grant of licence commences from the date of application. The final decision on the proposal was required to be taken by State Govt. The recommendation made by subordinate authority, even if significant for taking a formal decision by competent authority would be of no avail. In instant case Single Judge has assumed the date on which recommendation was made by Excise Commissioner as the relevant date which assumption was untenable. Whereas, before a final decision could be taken by competent authority on application submitted by respondent, the Rules were amended on 18-4-2012. The application submitted by respondent for grant of licence unquestionably should be treated as pending and under consideration on that date. Thus, application for grant of FL-11 licence submitted by respondent was required to be considered by competent authority keeping in mind the amended provisions which came into force w.e.f. 18-4-2012. That was precisely what was done by Excise Commissioner as could be discerned from his speaking order for invoking the restriction of distance of 200 metres from objectionable site. Single Judge of HC proceeded to decide writ petition filed by respondent merely by referring to pronouncement of DB coupled with the fact that respondent had asked for a wider relief to declare the amendment of 18-4-2012 as void to the extent it has introduced the restriction of distance of 200 meters from objectionable institutions for getting FL-11 licence. SC deem it appropriate to relegate the parties before Single Judge to decide the writ petition afresh. Impugned judgments of HC were set aside. Appeal partly allowed.
