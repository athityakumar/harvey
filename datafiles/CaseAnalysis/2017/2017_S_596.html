Neeraj Kumar Sainy and others v State of Uttar Pradesh and others

Supreme Court of India

21 March 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, Amitava Roy

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 207; AIR 2017 SC 1524; 2017 (2) AWC 1976; 2017(3) SCALE 583; 2017 (2) UPLBEC 1186

Case Digest

Subject: Education; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: SERVICE, Limitation, Contempt of Courts Act, 1971, Relief, Leave, Procedure, Date, Merit, Admission, Delay, Policy, Weightage, Cut-Off Date, Haryana Urban (Control of Rent and Eviction) Act, 1973, Reservation, Writ Petition, Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, Constitution of India, 1950, Post, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, Medical Council of India Postgraduate Medical Education Regulations, 2000

Summary: Education - Practice & Procedure - Denial of admission - Validity - Appellants/students appeared and were successful in written for admission into medical post graduate course - Appellants were granted seats, however denied admission into college of their choice by respondent no.2 on basis of notification issued by respondent no.1/state govt. which stated eligibility criteria, i.e., to serve three years of service in remote areas - HC dismissed writ petition filed by students - Hence, instant appeal and contempt petition.

Held, it is well settled in law that no one should suffer any prejudice because of the act of the court. The legal maxim 'actus curiae neminem gravabit' that has been taken recourse to cannot operate in a vacuum. It has to get the sustenance from the facts. In other words, there cannot be an iota of doubt that no prejudice shall be caused to anyone due to the fault of the court, but it is to be seen in what situations the court can invoke the maxim 'actus curiae neminem gravabit'. In instant case, as is manifest, after the admissions were over as per the direction of this Court in Dinesh Singh case, the appellants, who seemed to have resigned to their fate, woke up to have control over the events forgetting that the law does not assist the non-vigilant. One cannot indulge in luxury of lethargy, possibly nurturing the feeling that forgetting is a virtue, and thereafter, when the time has slipped through, for it waits for none, wake up and take shelter under the maxim 'actus curiae neminem gravabit'. It is completely unacceptable. Once the said principle is not applicable, the rest of the submissions pertaining to seats going waste or the State losing its investment or the suffering of the students or claim of parity with other students have no legs to stand upon. Hence, appeal being sans substance, stands dismissed. Appeal dismissed.
