Neera Yadav v Central Bureau of Investigaton

Supreme Court of India

02 August 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

R. Banumathi, Kurian Joseph

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 547; (2017) 8 SCC 757; JT 2017 (7) SC 593

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: FIR, CBI, Value, Gold, Enhancement, Incomplete, CONSTITUTION, Corrupt, Site Plan, Abuse, Cheque, Inference, Undue Advantage, Copies, Change, Hindu Succession Act, 1956, Two Offences, Pecuniary Advantage, Joint Account, Valuable Thing, Concurrent, Single Transaction, Uttar Pradesh Industrial Area Development Act, 1976, Shops Act, Rigorous Imprisonment, Registered, Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Husband, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, Inquiry, Corruption, Association

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 120B - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 13, 13(1)(d), 13(1)(d)(i), 13(1)(d)(ii), 13(2), 19(1) - Conspiracy in allotments - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant/accused allegedly in conspiracy committed grave irregularities in allotments and conversions of land - FIR was registered against accused - Trial Court convicted appellant for the offences punishable u/s. 120B IPC and s. 13(2) r/w s. 13(1)(d) of the Act and sentenced accused for 3 years - On appeal, HC dismissed appeal and upheld conviction of accused passed by Trial Court - Hence, instant appeal - Whether there was any perversity or illegality in impugned judgment of HC.

Held, prosecution was successful in proving that daughters of appellant were dependant on appellant and that they had purchased the shops and residential plots only out of the money contributed by appellant and her husband. That amounts to grave violation of Rules and being a CCEO of company appellant was guilty of obtaining valuable thing for herself and her daughters by abusing her position as a public servant. Appellant acted in breach of rules and regulations causing financial losses and valuable things were obtained by appellant and her daughters. The fact that the appellant acted in flagrant violation of the rules, by giving a complete go-by to public interest to promote her individual interest, shows that she abused her position to gain undue advantage to herself and to cause loss to the company. An attitude to abuse the official position to gain advantage to herself and such misuse of position erodes collective faith of people in the system. Appellant gaining pecuniary advantage to themselves in altering the plots to their own advantage establishes prior concert of appellant in manipulating the maps/records to suit her convenience. The concurrent findings recorded by Courts below are well balanced and SC do not find any reason warranting interference. Impugned sentence of imprisonment of 3 years imposed on appellant was reduced to 2 years with the only modification in sentence. Appeal partly allowed.
