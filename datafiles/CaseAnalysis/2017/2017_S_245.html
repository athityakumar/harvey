Fazar Ali and others v State of Assam

Supreme Court of India

21 April 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhushan, A.K. Sikri

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 291; AIR 2017 SC 2475; JT 2017 (4) SC 400; 2017 (2) RCR(Criminal) 980; 2017(5) SCALE 138

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: FIR, Blunt, Informant, Statement Of Witnesses, Contradiction, Islam, Registered, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 147, 148, 149, 302, 325, 326, 49 - Criminal assault - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellants/accused armed with dao, lathi, jathi, dagger etc. allegedly attacked complainant, his father, brother and mother-in-law - Charge sheet was filed against accused u/ss. 147, 148, 149 and 326 IPC in which s. 302 IPC was added subsequent to death of deceased - Trial Court convicted accused u/ss. 148, 302/149 IPC and sentenced them to undergo RI for life - On appeal, HC dismissed appeal - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, before Police role of accused was mentioned by eye-witnesses. In their statements and before Court eye-witnesses proved the role of accused and presence of accused. Therefore, eye-witness account of witnesses proves presence of accused. They have been rightly convicted u/s. 302 r/w s. 49 IPC. There was clear evidence of eye-witnesses that accused did not allow the injured to come out from their house for about three hours. In spite of the request being made by neighbours and other persons present on the spot, accused have almost seized the house and did not permit injured to come out or to go for treatment. Finding to that effect was recorded both by Trial Court and HC. Each person being a member of unlawful assembly was guilty of offence being committed in prosecution of common object was held both by Trial Court and HC. SC in Chandrappa and Others v. State of Karnataka, 2008 Indlaw SC 850, has laid down that it was unreasonable to expect from a witness to give a picture perfect report of incident and minor discrepancies in their statement have to be ignored. Finding of guilt recorded by Trial Court was based on correct appreciation of evidence. Minor contradictions and inconsistencies were rightly ignored by Courts below. Appeal dismissed.
