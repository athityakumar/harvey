Pawan Kumar Gupta v B. R. Gupta

Supreme Court of India

09 May 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Abdul Nazeer, J. Chelameswar

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 413; AIR 2017 SC 2834; 2017 (240) DLT 219; JT 2017 (5) SC 509; 2017 (1) RCR(Rent) 550; 2017(6) SCALE 304

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Keywords: Transfer Of Property Act, 1882, Demand Notice, Condonation Of Delay, Delhi Rent Control Act, 1958

Summary: Rent Control - Delhi Rent Control Act, 1958, ss. 14(1)(a), 14(2), 15(1) - Eviction suit - Default in payments - Validity - Respondent/landlord filed suit seeking eviction of appellant/tenant from the premises u/s. 14(1)(a),(b), (d) and (h) of the Act - Rent Controller allowed suit holding that tenant has failed to provide any explanation regarding delay in depositing of rent month by month and passed eviction order against tenant u/s. 14(1)(a) of the Act - On appeal, Tribunal allowed appeal - On further appeal, HC allowed appeal and set aside order of Tribunal and restored order of Rent Controller - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, in instant case tenant was a willful defaulter of rents. Rent Controller passed an order u/s. 15(1) of the Act directing him to pay or deposit the rent at the rate of Rs. 500/- per month w.e.f. 1-10-2004 and continue to pay the same at the aforesaid rate month by month. However, tenant failed to pay the rent in terms of the said order. Rent Controller directed to submit a report for the purpose of consideration of entitlement of tenant to the benefit u/s. 14(2) of the Act. The report showed that tenant had not paid the rent regularly in compliance with the order passed u/s. 15(1) of the Act. There was a long delay in deposit of rents. Condonation of delay could take place only when the defaulting tenants so pleads with justifiable reasons which would show that he was prevented from compliance by circumstances beyond his control. The tenant has not offered any explanation for the delay in deposit of rents. Therefore, SC do not find any justification to interfere with the order of HC. Appellant was granted 3 months time to vacate and deliver vacant possession of premises in question to respondent. Appeal dismissed.
