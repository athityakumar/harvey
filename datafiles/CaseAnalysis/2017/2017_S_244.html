Anjan Kumar Sarma and others v State of Assam

Supreme Court of India

23 May 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

L. Nageswara Rao, Navin Sinha

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 419; AIR 2017 SC 2617; JT 2017 (6) SC 498; 2017 (3) MLJ(Crl) 57; 2017(6) SCALE 556

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Presumption, Weapon, Circumstantial Evidence, Proof, Blood, Chain, Dead Body, Last Seen, Disclosure Statement, Inference, Skull, Bruises, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, Investigating Officer

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 201, 302, 34, 366A, 376(2)(g) - Rape and murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellants/accused allegedly committed rape and murder of deceased - Charges were framed against accused for committing offences u/ss. 302, 376 (2) (g), 201 r/w s. 34 IPC - Trial Court held that accused were not guilty of the offences u/ss. 302, 201 r/w s. 34 IPC and acquitted them - On appeal, HC reversed the acquittal and convicted appellants for offences u/ss. 302, 201 r/w s. 34 IPC and sentenced them to life imprisonment - Hence, instant appeal - Whether the conviction of appellants by HC u/ss. 302, 201 r/w s. 34 IPC was justified.

Held, prosecution relied upon nine circumstances to prove the charges against accused. SC was in agreement with Trial Court that recovery of khukri was not supported by any independent witnesses. Prosecution failed to prove that there were blood stains on the said khukri. The blood stains found in the bathroom of bungalow were sent for examination which resulted in a negative report. The above circumstances not being proved would leave only two circumstances against accused which were that accused were last seen together with deceased and the absence of any explanation forthcoming by accused. HC committed an error in holding that in the absence of any satisfactory explanation by accused the presumption of guilt of accused stood un-rebutted and appellants were liable to be convicted. In a case where the other links were satisfactorily made out and the circumstances point to the guilt of accused, the circumstance of last seen together and absence of explanation would provide an additional link which completes the chain. In the absence of proof of other circumstances, the only circumstance of last seen together and absence of satisfactory explanation cannot be made the basis of conviction. As SC held that the other circumstances relied upon by prosecution were not proved and that the circumstances of last seen together along with the absence of satisfactory explanation were not sufficient for convicting accused. Therefore, due to the lack of chain of circumstances which lead to the only hypothesis of guilt against accused, SC set aside the judgment of HC and acquit appellants of the charges. Appeal allowed.
