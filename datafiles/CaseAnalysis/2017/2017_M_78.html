Government of (NCT of Delhi) v Manav Dharam Trust and another

Supreme Court of India

04 May 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Kurian Joseph, R. Banumathi

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 363; (2017) 6 SCC 751; AIR 2017 SC 2450; 2017 (4) ALD(SC) 147; 2017 (123) ALR 774; 2017 (4) Bom.C.R. 723; 2017 (2) CLT(SC) 247; 2017 (3) CTC 740; 2017 (239) DLT 153; JT 2017 (6) SC 21; 2017 (2) KLT 846; 2017 (5) MLJ 429; 2017 (2) RCR(Civil) 1062; 2017(5) SCALE 777

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property

Keywords: Encumbrance, Acquisition Of Land, Delhi Development Act, 1957, Delhi Lands (Restrictions on Transfer) Act, 1972, Investment, Subsequent Purchaser, Haryana Ceiling on Land Holdings Act, 1972, Land Acquisition Act, 1894, Right to Fair Compensation and Transparency in Land Acquisition, Rehabilitation and Resettlement Act, 2013

Summary: Land & Property - Administrative - Right to Fair Compensation and Transparency in Land Acquisition, Rehabilitation and Resettlement Act, 2013, ss. 105, 11, 11A, 24(2) - Acquisition proceedings - Lapse - Validity - Land acquisition proceedings was lapsed by the operation of s. 24(2) of the Act, either compensation was not paid or possession was not taken within 5 years - Respondents/writ petitioners challenged the same before HC whereby HC allowed petitions - Hence, instant appeals - Whether subsequent purchasers/assignees/power of attorney holders, etc., have locus standi to file a petition for a declaration of lapse of acquisition proceedings u/s. 24(2) of the Act.

Held, on account of lapse, the encumbrance created in favour of State comes to an end, and resultantly, impediment to encumber the land also comes to an end. Once the acquisition proceedings lapse, all impediments cease to exist. There was a clear indication that the Act proposes to protect the interest of those persons, among others who were affected by the acquisition. The subsequent purchasers/successors, etc., in instant case were all people affected by the acquisition and also they were entitled to seek a declaration on lapse under the Act. Further, subsequent purchaser, assignee, successor in interest, power of attorney, etc., were all persons who are interested in compensation/land owners/affected persons in terms of the Act and such persons were entitled to file a case for a declaration that the land acquisition proceedings have lapsed by virtue of operation of s. 24(2) of the Act. It was a declaration qua the land wherein indisputably they have an interest and they were affected by such acquisition. For such a declaration, it cannot be said that respondents do not have any locus standi. Appellants were given a period of 6 months to exercise its liberty granted u/s. 24(2) of the Act for initiation of acquisition proceedings afresh. Appeals dismissed.
