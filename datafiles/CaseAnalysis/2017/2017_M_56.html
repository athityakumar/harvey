Bapusaheb Chimasaheb Naik-nimbalkar (Dead through Lrs.) and another v Mahesh Vijaysinha Rajebhosale and others

Supreme Court of India

25 April 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Arun Mishra, S. Abdul Nazeer

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 362; (2017) 7 SCC 769; AIR 2017 SC 2491; 2017 (4) ALD(SC) 163; 2017 (123) ALR 791; 2017 (4) Bom.C.R. 31; 2017 (2) CLT(SC) 461; 2017 (3) RCR(Civil) 183; 2017(5) SCALE 363

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Immovable Property, Adverse Possession, Limitation Act, 1963, Limitation, Reversioner, Hindu Succession (Amendment) Act, 2005, Code of Civil Procedure, 1908

Summary: (A) Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 2 r. 2 - Limitation Act, 1963, arts. 141, 65(b) - Suit for partition - Barred - Validity - Plaintiffs/respondents filed suit for partition and separate possession of land restraining appellants/defendants who were trying to sell the land in question - Trial Court decreed suit holding plaintiffs and defendants to be co-owners and in joint possession of suit land - On appeal, Appellate Court and HC dismissed appeals affirming the view of Trial Court - Hence, instant appeal - Whether suit was barred by limitation as per the provisions contained in art. 65(b) of the Act.

Held, in instant case possession never became adverse to plaintiffs. There was concurrent finding recorded that plaintiffs were in joint possession of disputed land on the date of filing of suit. Defendants have taken the plea of ouster and suit was filed beyond 12 years of death of successor but they have not been able to prove their adverse possession. On the contrary the finding was that appellant admitted the title of respondent's mother. The finding was that till 1976, appellant never denied the title of respondent's mother. Thus, as adverse possession has not been concurrently found by Courts and in instant case the starting point of limitation would not be the date of death of successor in the year 1962 as she was full owner, as such suit could not be said to be barred by limitation. Appeal dismissed.

(B) Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 2 r. 2 - Separate possession - Entitled to -Whether suit being barred by O. 2 r. 2 CPC.

Held, in instant case cause of action of suit for partition was different and dispute as to mutation was subsequently decided. Thus, suit for partition as filed, could not be said to be barred by O. II r. 2 CPC. Defendants were trying to sell the property in the year 1979 as such plaintiffs in suit prayed for partition and separate possession. Plaintiffs could not have claimed interest in the land in the life-time of successor and cause of action in the previous suit for declaration of title filed by successor was materially different. It cannot be said that second suit for partition was in respect of the same cause of action as that on which the previous suit was based. In respect of the cause of action of previous suit plaintiff was not entitled to more than one relief. Thus, it could not be said that plaintiff has omitted to sue for relief for which second suit was filed. Suit for partition with respect to joint property was based on continuing cause of action, as such suit for partition could not be said to be barred by O. 2 r. 2 CPC. Appeal dismissed.
