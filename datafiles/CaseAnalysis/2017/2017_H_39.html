Heera Lal and another v State of Rajasthan

Supreme Court of India

24 April 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Rohinton Fali Nariman, Mohan M. Shantanagoudar

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 326; AIR 2017 SC 2425; JT 2017 (4) SC 505; 2017 (2) RCR(Criminal) 839; 2017(6) SCALE 152

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Presumption, Suicide, Harassment, Indian Evidence Act, 1872, Abetment, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Husband

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 306, 498A - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 113A - Abetment of suicide - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellants filed instant appeal challenging impugned order of HC whereby HC relying upon dying declaration upheld conviction of appellants u/s. 306 IPC and sentenced them to imprisonment for 3 years.

Held, having absolved appellants of the charge of cruelty, which was the most basic ingredient for the offence made out u/s. 498A IPC the third ingredient for application of s. 113A of the Act was missing, namely, that the relatives i.e., mother-in-law and father-in-law who were charged u/s. 306 IPC had subjected the victim to cruelty. No doubt, it was concurrently found that in-laws did harass victim, but harassment was something of a lesser degree than cruelty. However, assuming the presumption u/s. 113A of the Act would apply, it was fully rebutted, for the reason that there was no link or intention on part of in-laws to assist victim to commit suicide. In the absence of such vital link, fact that there was a finding of harassment would not lead to conclusion that there was "abetment of suicide". Therefore, appellants were acquitted for the crime u/s. 498A IPC, that abetment of suicide u/s. 306 was not made out. Impugned judgment of HC was set aside. Appeal allowed.
