Roopendra Singh v State of Tripura and another

Supreme Court of India

11 April 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Uday Umesh Lalit, Adarsh Kumar Goel

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 276; AIR 2017 SC 1801; 2017 CRLJ 2826; 2017 (3) CTC 330; 2017 (2) JCC 1303; JT 2017 (4) SC 1; 2017 (2) RCR(Criminal) 632; 2017(4) SCALE 468B

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Proviso, Leave To Appeal, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973

Summary: Criminal Appeal @ out of SLP (Crl.) No.7014 of 2012)

(A) Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 2(wa), 372, 373, 374, 377, 378, 378(3), 378(4), 379, 380, 482 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 201, 302, 34, 342, 376(2)(b), 506 - Crime - Acquittal - Sustainability - Appellant/accused was acquitted of charges u/ss. 342, 376(2)(b) and 506 IPC vide judgment of Trial Court - Respondent no. 2/victim filed appeal u/s. 372 CrPC - HC by its judgment and order held that there was an unfettered right conferred upon victim by s. 372 CrPC and there was no necessity for converting appeal to one u/s. 372 r/w s. 378 CrPC - Hence, instant appeals.

Held, right of questioning the correctness of judgment and order of acquittal by preferring an appeal to HC was conferred upon victim including legal heir and others u/s. 2(wa) CrPC under the proviso to s. 372 CrPC, but only after obtaining the leave of HC as required u/s. 378(3) CrPC. HC failed to deal with such important legal aspect of the matter while passing the judgment. Further, in instant case victim had preferred an application to treat appeal initially filed u/s. 372 CrPC to be one u/s. 372 r/w s. 378 CrPC. Though HC observed that no such leave was necessary, the matter now assumes different complexion in the light of the decision in Satya Pal Singh v. State of Madhya Pradesh and others, 2015 Indlaw SCO 253. However, since there was already an application on behalf of the victim to treat the appeal u/s. 372 r/w s. 378 CrPC in SC considered view the leave ought to be granted. The pending appeal should be considered on merits by HC. Appeals disposed of.

Criminal Appeals arising out of SLP(Crl) Nos.8316-17 of 2012

(B) Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 2(wa), 372, 373, 374, 377, 378, 378(3), 378(4), 379, 380, 482 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 201, 302, 34, 342, 376(2)(b), 506 - Appeal filed - Maintainability - Appellant/informant filed instant appeals challenging impugned order of HC whereby HC rejected appeal of victim u/s. 372 CrPC.

Held, s. 372 CrPC has conferred upon a victim a substantive and independent right to maintain an appeal against acquittal. The widow of deceased in instant matter comes within the definition of "victim" as incorporated in s. 2(wa) CrPC. Merely because leave to appeal was not granted to State to prefer an appeal against acquittal, appeal preferred by victim Informant ought not to have been rejected by HC summarily. Impugned judgment of HC was set aside and remit the matter to HC for fresh consideration. Appeals disposed of.
