Secretary to Government Commercial Taxes and Registration Department, Secretariat and another v A. Singamuthu

Supreme Court of India

07 March 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

R. Banumathi, Kurian Joseph

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 180; (2017) 4 SCC 113; AIR 2017 SC 1304; JT 2017 (3) SC 202; 2017 (2) LLJ 514; 2017(3) SCALE 365

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Wages, Daily Wages, Regularisation, Vacancies, Pay, Salary, Employment, Rule, Date, Recruitment, Appeal, EDUCATION, Regular, Time Scale, Scheme, Temporary, Relaxation, Part-Time, Employee, Post, Right to, Appointment

Summary: Service - Regularisation of service - Part time worker - Entitled to - Respondent was appointed as a part-time Masalchi in appellant/dept. and completed 10 years of service on 31-03-1999 - State Govt. passed G.O. Ms. No.22, dt. 28-02-2006 directing that services of fulltime daily wages employees working in Govt. Depts. who rendered 10 years of service as on 01-01-2006 be regularized - Respondent filed writ petition before HC seeking for regularization of his service - Single Judge directed appellant to extend the benefits of G.O. Ms. No. 22 dt. 28-02-2006 and grant regularization to respondent - On appeal, DB dismissed appeal affirming the order of Single Judge - Hence, instant appeal - Whether HC erred in directing regularization of service of respondent from the date of completion of 10 years of his service with salary and other benefits.

Held, Single Judge failed to take note of the fact that as per G.O. Ms.No. 22, dt. 28-02-2006 was applicable only to full-time daily wagers and not to part-time daily wagers like respondent herein. Respondent was temporarily appointed part-time worker as per Tamil Nadu Finance Code Volume (2) Appendix (5) and his appointment was completely temporary. However, respondent being appointed as part-time Masalchi, cannot compare himself to full-time daily wagers and seek benefit of G.O.Ms.No.22 dt. 28-02-2006. Single Judge also failed to consider that Govt. did not grant regularisation of services of any part-time employee on completion of 10 years of his service as envisaged under G.O.Ms. No.22 dt. 28-02-2006. Single Judge erred in extending the benefit of G.O.Ms.No.22 dt. 28-0-.2006 to respondent that too retrospectively from the date of completion of 10 years of service of respondent. Thus, impugned order directing regularization of respondent from the date of completion of their 10 years would adversely affect State exchequer in a huge manner, impugned order cannot be sustained on that score. DB also failed to take note that G.O.Ms.No. 22, dt. 28-02-2006 was applicable only to full-time daily wage employees and who had completed 10 years of continuous service as on 01-01-2006 and not to part-time employees. Impugned order of DB affirming the order of Single Judge granting benefits to respondent from the date of completion of 10 years of service was erroneous and the same was set aside. Appeal allowed.
