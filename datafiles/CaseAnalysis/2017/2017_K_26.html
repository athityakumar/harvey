JSW Infrastructure Limited and another v Kakinada Seaports Limited and others

Supreme Court of India

01 March 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Gupta, Madan B. Lokur

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 175; (2017) 4 SCC 170; AIR 2017 SC 1175; 2017 (121) ALR 867; 2017 (2) CGLJ 163; JT 2017 (3) SC 77; 2017 (4) MahLJ 855; 2017 (3) MLJ 731; 2017(3) SCALE 216

Case Digest

Subject: Contract & Commercial

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Acceptance of bid - Legality - Challenged - Appellants filed instant appeals challenging impugned order of HC whereby HC allowed writ petition of respondents/second consortium holding that first consortium of appellants was not entitled to take part in the bid and acceptance of its bid was illegal.

Held, HC erred in interpreting the clause only in the context of the word "next" and ignored the opening part of Clause which clearly indicates that the Clause was only applicable when there was only one private berth operator. It appears to SC that the intention was that when a port was started, if the first berth for a specific cargo was awarded in favour of one private operator then he cannot be permitted to bid for the next berth for the same type of cargo. However, once there were more than one private operators operating in port then any one of them could be permitted to bid even for successive berths. In instant case there were already 5 private operators other than first consortium. HC erred in interpreting the Clause in the manner which it was done. Once there were more than one private operators then the Clause would not apply. The decision taken by Port Trust could not be termed to be arbitrary, perverse or mala fide and HC was not justified in setting aside the same. Impugned judgment of HC was set aside. Appeals allowed.
