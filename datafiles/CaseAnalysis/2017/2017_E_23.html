Environment and Consumer Protection Foundation v Union of India and others

Supreme Court of India

11 August 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Madan B. Lokur, Deepak Gupta

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 612; 2017 (5) AWC 4825

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Women & Children

Keywords: Welfare, Public Interest Litigation, Health, Begging, Constitution of India, 1950

Summary: Women & Children - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 21, 32 - Widows of Vrindavan - Rehabilitate - Petitioner/society filed instant writ petition requiring UOI and State to take all steps to rehabilitate the widows of Vrindavan so as to bring them to a stage where they can live with dignity - Further, those widows were so unfortunately dealt with, as if they have ceased to be entitled to live a life of dignity and as if they are not entitled to the protection of art. 21 of Constitution.

Held, National Commission for Women was directed to prepare a comprehensive report on the problems faced by the widows. It was also directed that report should contain the age groups of the widows, their family background and all other information. SC directed that in order to mitigate the miseries of widows, a Special Committee should be constituted to undertake an exercise of identification and enumeration of destitute in Vrindavan both those having shelter and those wandering in the streets without any shelter. The Committee was required to collect complete data of the widows including the reason for their shifting to Vrindavan and particulars about their family and their present source of income. Further, the effort put in by all concerned in reports should not go waste it should be gainfully utilized, being in a sense a gold mine of pragmatic and workable suggestions. SC constitute a Committee to study all the reports filed in SC and provide SC with a common working plan (based on the suggestions in the reports) within a period of 2 months. It is to give voice these hapless widows that it became necessary for SC to intervene as a part of its constitutional duty and for reasons of social justice to issue appropriate directions. Order accordingly.
