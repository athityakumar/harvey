Srei Infrastructure Finance Limited v Tuff Drilling Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

20 September 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhushan, A.K. Sikri

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 1048; 2017 (12) SCALE 105

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Keywords: absence, adjudicate, arbitral award, arbitral tribunal, arbitration agreement, Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, Code of Civil Procedure, Conciliation, extension of time, functus officio, jurisdiction, natural justice, obligatory, settlement, termination of proceedings

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, ss. 11, 25(a) - Termination of proceedings - Recall - Jurisdiction - Respondent/claimant filed an application u/s. 11 of the Act for referring the dispute to arbitrator - Tribunal directed respondent to file statement of claim which was not filed thereby rejected the claim - Claimant filed an application seeking for recall of the order - Tribunal dismissed the same holding that it cannot pass an order recommencing arbitration proceedings - Aggrieved, claimant filed revision before HC - HC allowed petition holding that Tribunal has power to recall its own order and remitted matter back to Tribunal - Hence, instant appeal - Whether Tribunal u/s. 25(a) of the Act has jurisdiction to consider the application for recall of the order terminating the proceedings on sufficient cause being shown by claimant.

Held, there cannot be a dispute that the power exercised by Tribunal was quasi-judicial. In view of the provisions of the Act which confers various statutory powers and obligations on the Tribunal, SC do not find any such distinction between the statutory tribunal constituted under the statutory provisions or Constitution in so far as the power of procedural review was concerned. Tribunal after termination of proceedings u/s. 25(a) of the Act on sufficient cause being shown could recall the order and re-commence the proceedings. In instant case, Tribunal has rejected the application of claimant taking the view that after an order was passed by him terminating the proceedings, he cannot pass the order recommencing the arbitration proceedings. Thus, even after passing the order of terminating the proceedings, if sufficient cause was shown, claims of statement could be accepted by Tribunal by accepting the show-cause and there was no lack of jurisdiction in Tribunal to recall the earlier order on sufficient cause being shown. Tribunal committed an error in holding that it has no jurisdiction to recall an order terminating the proceedings u/s. 25(a) of the Act. Tribunal having not considered the cause shown by claimant in its application, it was in the ends of justice that Tribunal be asked to consider the application filed by claimant praying for recall of the order and to grant extension for filing the statement of claim. Appeal dismissed.
