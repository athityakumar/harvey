Jayakantham and others v Abay Kumar

Supreme Court of India

21 February 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

D.Y. Chandrachud, Arun Mishra

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 130; (2017) 5 SCC 178; 2017 (121) ALR 895; 2017 (2) AWC 1729; 2017 (2) CLT(SC) 147; 2017 (2) CTC 647; 2017 (1) ILR(Cut) 363; JT 2017 (2) SC 471; 2017 (2) MLJ 460; 2017 (1) OLR 818; 2017 (2) RCR(Civil) 104; 2017 (135) RD 727; 2017(3) SCALE 61; 2017 (1) WLN(SC) 175

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Keywords: Jurisdiction, Suit For Specific Performance, Specific Relief Act, 1963, Agreement To Sell, Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, Land & Property

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 100 - Specific Relief Act, 1963, s. 20(1) - Specific performance - Decreed - Validity - Respondent filed suit for specific performance of property before Trial Court - Trial Court decreed suit - On appeal, Appellate Court confirmed judgment of Trial Court - Aggrieved, appellants filed appeal before HC which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, the terms of contract, conduct of parties at the time of entering into the agreement and circumstances under which the contract was entered into gave plaintiff an unfair advantage over defendants. Those circumstances make it inequitable to enforce specific performance. A decree for payment of compensation in lieu of specific performance would meet the ends of justice. Father of respondent paid an amount of Rs. 60,000 to appellants in June 1999 of the total agreed consideration of Rs. 1.60 lakhs. Appellants have voluntarily offered to pay an amount of Rs. 10 lakhs, as just compensation in lieu of specific performance. In SC view, the ends of justice would be met by directing appellants to pay to respondent an amount of Rs. 15 lakhs in lieu of specific performance. Decree for specific performance should be set aside and stand substituted with a direction to appellants to pay a sum of Rs. 15 lakhs to respondent in lieu of specific performance within 2 months. Appeal allowed.
