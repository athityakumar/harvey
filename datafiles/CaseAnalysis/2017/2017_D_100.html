Dnyandeo Sabaji Naik and another v Pradnya Prakash Khadekar and others

Supreme Court of India

01 March 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

D.Y. Chandrachud, Jagdish Singh Khehar, S. Ratnavel Pandian

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 162; (2017) 5 SCC 496; 2017(3) ALL MR 431; 2017 (121) ALR 861; 2017 (2) Bom.C.R. 713; 2017 (2) CLT(SC) 275; 2017 (2) CTC 776; JT 2017 (3) SC 222; 2017 (2) RCR(Civil) 310; 2017 (1) RCR(Rent) 268; 2017(3) SCALE 315

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Review, Payment Of Wages Act, 1936, Applicant, Access, Constitution of India, 1950, Presidency Small Causes Courts' Act

Summary: Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Suit for eviction - Frivolous filings - Validity - Respondents filed suit for eviction for recovery of possession of premises against petitioners - Trial Court decreed suit - On appeal, HC directed petitioners to vacate the premises within a year - Petitioners filed an application for extension of time to vacate the premises for a period of 5 years before HC which was dismissed - Hence, instant special leave petitions.

Held, having furnished an unconditional undertaking to vacate the premises, it would be manifestly an abuse of process for petitioners to seek recourse to their remedies on the merits of the issues which arose in appeal. Instant case indicates a blatant abuse of process of Court. Petitioners not only took the benefit of an order of HC granting them 1 year's time to vacate the premises but obtained a further extension of 5 years to vacate the premises. The time of HC and SC as well had to be devoted to a thoroughly frivolous proceeding. Further, specific finding of Trial Court based on the admissions of predecessor-in-interest of petitioners that the premises were granted to them on the basis of a conducting agreement. In the earlier proceeding that was instituted in Trial Court it was found that the premises have been granted under a conducting agreement and there was no relationship of licensor and licensee. That being the position, petitioners would not acquire status as tenants w.e.f. 1-2-1973, there being no licence in their favour. Therefore, it was not merely a matter of discretion but a duty and obligation cast upon all Courts to ensure that the legal system was not exploited by those who use the forms of the law to defeat or delay justice. SC commends all Courts to deal with frivolous filings in the same manner. Petitioners were directed to vacate the premises on or before 7-3-2017 and should pay all arrears for use of the premises computed and costs quantified at Rs 5 lakhs to respondents within 2 months. Petitions dismissed.
