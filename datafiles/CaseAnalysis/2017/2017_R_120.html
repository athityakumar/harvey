Reena Suresh Alhat and another v State of Maharashtra and others

Supreme Court of India

13 February 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

J. Chelameswar, A.M. Sapre

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 112; (2017) 3 SCC 119; AIR 2017 SC 967; 2017 (2) Bom.C.R. 863; JT 2017 (2) SC 413; 2017(3) SCALE 83

Case Digest

Subject: Election; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Bombay Provincial Municipal Corporations Act, 1949, Constitution of India, 1950

Summary: Election - Municipalities & Local Governments - Bombay Provincial Municipal Corporations Act, 1949 - Constitution of India, 1950, art.136 - Exercise of discretionary power by SC - Instant matters arise out of the Act and petitioners were candidates in Elections to Municipal Corporation of Pune - Petitioner-T nomination was rejected and she challenged the same before HC - However, HC dismissed petition on grounds of Constitutional bar and existence of alternative remedy - Petitioner-R was sponsored by BJP and symbol was allotted but contesting candidates questioned allotment of election symbol of BJP by filing petition before HC wherein HC by interim order, stayed orders of Election Commission allotting symbol in favour of petitioner-R - Hence, instant two SLP's - Whether impugned orders of HC are sustainable.

Held, appellate jurisdiction conferred by Constitution u/art.136 of Constitution is purely discretionary and there are no reasons to entertain instant SLPs as ongoing 2017 elections pertain to local body under local law of State Legislature and result of such elections are unlikely to have any effect on affairs of the nation. Further, HC is also a Constitutional court, and petitioners would still have forum for adjudication of their respective rights and grant appropriate relief if they could successfully establish the infringement of their legal rights. Hence, issues involved in instant cases could not be dealt by SC and as such, petitions dismissed. Petitions disposed of.
