Ramesh Chand and others v Tanmay Developers Private Limited and another

Supreme Court of India

26 April 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Arun Mishra, Mohan M. Shantanagoudar

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 343; AIR 2017 SC 2671; JT 2017 (5) SC 20; 2017 (3) RCR(Civil) 125; 2017(5) SCALE 328

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Specific Relief Act, 1963, Apportionment, Failure, Land Acquisition Act, 1894

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 18, 3(b), 30, 4, 9 - Specific performance suit - Validity - Respondents filed suits for specific performance of agreement to sell and recovery of earnest money against appellants/land owners - Reference Court held that dispute u/s. 30 of the Act arising out of apportionment of compensation or any part could not be adjudicated by substituting judicial forum into civil Court - On appeal, HC allowed appeal and directed refund of earnest money to respondents to be paid by appellants - Hence, instant appeals.

Held, respondent no. 1 could not have resorted to the remedy of reference for refund of earnest money as for such purpose he had filed civil suit earlier in point of time. HC had ignored and overlooked that case arose out of civil suit in which specific performance of agreement to sale was sought. SC found that parties were aware of notification u/s. 4(1) as such right of forfeiture could have been exercised. HC has relied upon the decision of SC in Thiriveedhi Channiah v. Gudipudi Venkata Subba Rao (Dead) by Lrs. & Ors., 2007 Indlaw SC 273, facts in the said case were different and said decision could not have been utilized by HC for setting aside the well reasoned award passed by Reference Court declining to entertain the prayer made by respondents in view of the availing remedy of civil suits. HC should have in fairness reflected that the said decision was rendered by SC in the context of civil suit. HC has referred it in the manner as if it was a case which was decided u/s. 30 of the Act with respect to the apportionment of compensation. Impugned judgment and order passed by HC was set aside. The land owners were entitled for disbursement of compensation. Appeals allowed.
