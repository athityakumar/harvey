Umarmiya Ismailmiya Saiyed @ Mamumiya Panju Miya v State of Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

01 February 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

L. Nageswara Rao, Sharad Arvind Bobde

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 68; (2017) 2 SCC 752; AIR 2017 SC 707; JT 2017 (2) SC 54; 2017 (1) RCR(Criminal) 1011; 2017(2) SCALE 119

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: FIR, Arms Act, 1959, Explosive Substances Act, 1908, Terrorist And Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Act, 1987, Registered, Indian Penal Code, 1860

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Terrorist and Disruptive Activities (Prevention)Act, 1987, ss. 20A(1), 3, 4, 5 - Explosive Substances Act, 1908, ss. 3, 4, 5, 6, 9, 9B - Arms Act, 1959, 25(1A), 25(1B) - Smuggling of arms & Explosives - Non-compliance of Provisions - Appellant/ accused along with co-accused was charged for commission of offence of smuggling of contraband goods, arms and explosive - TADA Court denied bail to appellant on ground of involvement in serious offences and enlargement on bail would be detrimental to interest of society - Co-accused was granted bail - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, s. 20A of 1987 Act is mandatory and any violation of the procedure prescribed therein would vitiate the entire proceedings with respect to the TADA offences. In instant case, appellant is entitled to be released on bail only on the ground that the FIR was registered on 16-7-1993 in violation of the procedure prescribed u/s. 20A (1) of 1987 Act. Hence, grant relief of bail subject to the condition of furnish of bail bond by appellant, who shall report daily to police and shall not leave territory. Appeal allowed.
