Manipal University and another v Union of India and another

Supreme Court of India

03 July 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

L. Nageswara Rao, S.A. Bobde

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 479; 2017(7) SCALE 307

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Education

Keywords: Jurisdiction, Indian Medical Council Act, 1956, Medical Council Of India, University Grants Commission Act, 1956, Medical Council of India regulations

Summary: Education - Administrative - Indian Medical Council Act, 1956, s.10A - University Grants Commission Act, 1956 - Medical Council of India Regulations, regn. 5 - Determination of NRI quota in medical colleges - Appellant challenged directions issued by respondent no.2 vide letter dt.8-2-2005 before HC through writ petition and HC held that s.10-A of the Act conferred power on respondent no.2 to determine intake capacity only but had no power to regulate admissions to sub categories and further held that directions issued by SC bind all parties and as appellant admitted NRI students in excess of 15% of the intake capacity in violation of orders of SC, appellant was not entitled to the relief prayed for - Hence, instant appeal - Whether respondent no.2 has jurisdiction to restrict admissions to NRI quota.

Held, there is no express power conferred on respondent no.2 in the MCI Act to interfere in allocation of quotas for sub categories. The determination of a quota for NRI seats is beyond domain of respondent no.2 and direction given by respondent no.2 by its letter dt.8-2-2005 amounts to interfering with the quota and so, is illegal. Further, though Regulations obligate respondent no.2 to ensure merit based selection to admissions in medical colleges, yet, respondent no.2 could not issue directions interfering with quota in the guise of exercising power u/regn.5 of Regulations. Further, direction issued by MCI was not implemented either for years 2005 to 2008 or thereafter. Hence, direction issued by respondent no.2 to appellant not to make admissions to the extent of 103 NRI seats for years 2005 to 2008 is ultra vires and without jurisdiction. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Appellant being a Deemed University is governed by provisions of UGC Act and the competent authority to take any action for violation of the provisions of UGC Act regarding maintenance of standards is the Commission.
