N. Paraeswaran Unni v G. Kannan and another

Supreme Court of India

01 March 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

N.V. Ramana, Prafulla C. Pant

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 262; (2017) 5 SCC 737; AIR 2017 SC 1681; 2017 (3) BC 721; 2017 CRLJ 2838; 2017 (2) JCC (NI) 144; JT 2017 (6) SC 389; 2017 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1292; 2017 (1) OLR 988; 2017 (2) RCR(Criminal) 352; 2017(4) SCALE 90

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, Indian Evidence Act, 1872, Endorsement, General Clauses Act, 1897, Cheque, Proviso

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, s. 138 - Dishonour of cheque - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant/complainant filed instant appeal challenging impugned order of HC whereby HC allowed revision of respondent no. 1 by setting aside concurrent judgments of Courts below, that respondent no. 1 cannot be convicted u/s. 138 of the Act - Whether HC was right in rejecting the case of appellant on ground that second notice was issued beyond the period of limitation i.e. 15 days from the date of receiving dishonour intimation from bank under Cl. (b) of s. 138 of the Act.

Held, once notice was sent by registered post by correctly addressing to drawer of cheque, the service of notice was deemed to have been effected. Then requirements under proviso (b) of s. 138 of the Act stands complied, if notice was sent in the prescribed manner. However, drawer was at liberty to rebut such presumption. There was no bar under the Act to send a reminder notice to drawer of cheque and usually such notice cannot be construed as an admission of non-service of first notice by appellant as has happened in instant case. However, the first notice sent by appellant on 12-04-1991 was effective and notice was deemed to have been served on respondent no. 1. Further, second notice has no relevance at all in instant case. Second notice could be construed as a reminder of respondent's obligation to discharge his liability. As the complaint, was filed within the stipulated time contemplated under Cl. (b) of s. 142 of the Act, s. 138 r/w s. 142 of the Act was attracted. Impugned judgment of HC was set aside. SC direct respondent no. 1 to deposit the said amount of Rs. 2,00,000/- before Trial Court on or before 30-04-2017. Out of the said amount of Rs. 2,00,000/- so deposited, Rs.1,30,000/- should be paid to appellant as compensation. Appeal disposed of.
