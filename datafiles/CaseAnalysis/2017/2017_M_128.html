Orissa Industrial Infrastructure Development Corporation v Mesco Kalinga Steel Limited and others

Supreme Court of India

14 February 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Arun Mishra, Amitava Roy

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 113; (2017) 5 SCC 86; 2017 (122) ALR 1; JT 2017 (2) SC 428; 2017 (3) MLJ 33; 2017(2) SCALE 694

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Indian Contract Act, 1872, Government of India Act, 1935, Lessor And Lessee, Failure, Resumption, Orissa Industrial Infrastructure Development Corporation Act, 1980, Constitution of India, 1950, Administrative, Airports Authority of India Act, 1944

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Land & Property - Execution of lease deed condition precedent for contract - In pursuance of request made by Mesco for allotment of 2500 acres of land, IDCO wrote letter dt.21-3-1995 to Mesco to deposit land cost in 20 half-yearly equal installments and further to deposit Rs.1.25 crore towards first installment and Rs.13.08 Lakh towards ground rent and cess - In pursuance of the same, Mesco deposited Rs.1.25 Crore with IDCO on 3-4-1995 and on 13-6-1996 IDCO requested Mesco to take over possession of 1756.29 acres of land in first phase and submit draft lease deed for execution - Mesco took over possession on 18-6-1996 but lease deed was not executed - While so, on 27-10-1997, IDCO requested Mesco to submit draft lease deed for execution and again on 26-11-1997 requested Mesco to submit draft deed but the same was not done - However, after 5 ½ years, IDCO gave final notice to Mesco on 4-7-2003 to deposit balance defaulted amount of Rs.22.44 crore along with statutory dues and to execute lease deed by 19-7-2003 - Ultimately on 25-7-2003, on failure to get lease deed executed, IDCO resumed land and possession letter of 1756.29 acres of land was cancelled and amount deposited by Mesco was forfeited and adjusted towards compensation for use and occupation of land and damages - Later, IDCO allotted land out of land in question to Visa Industries and Jindal and possession was handed over to them and lease deed was executed - While so, Mesco filed writ petition before HC assailing cancellation and resumption of land but the same was disposed - Mesco again filed writ petition and HC by impugned judgment and order directed IDCO to lease out 825.68 acres of land and enter into lease agreement with Mesco - Hence, instant appeals by IDCO and Jindal.

Held, condition no.18 of General Terms and Conditions to be inserted in lease deed provides that the lessee has to remedy the breach within three months of notice. However, said condition never came into force and remained inoperative as lease deed itself had not been executed. Further, more than three months time was given to Mesco to remedy the breach inasmuch as notice for resumption was initially given in 1997 and for more than 5 1/2 years till resumption in July 2003, breach was not remedied. Furthermore, inspite of receiving the advance possession, there was failure on the part of Mesco to execute the lease deed though draft lease deed was sent to it in January, 1996 for execution. The relationship of lessor and lessee never came into being, in the absence of execution of lease deed. The execution of lease deed was necessary as the State Govt. had only accorded in principle approval and advised IDCO to allot the land that could only be done by execution of lease deed. Furthermore, as Corporation could dispose of the land subject to directions given by State Govt. and as condition precedent was that of execution of lease deed, and as it was so directed by State Govt. also, no concluded contract came into being and the transaction became void due to failure on the part of Mesco to execute a formal lease deed. Further, since due to neglect of Mesco the transaction became void, it was not necessary to serve three months' notice to remdy the breach. Further, HC has totally misdirected itself in directing to lease out the balance land and HC also ignored that certain intervening events have taken place and there was total failure on the part of Mesco to carry out its obligations. Hence, HC could not have issued the direction more so in the changed situation and in view of the defaults committed by Mesco. Hence, appeals are allowed and impugned order of HC is set aside and writ petition is dismissed. Appeals disposed of.
