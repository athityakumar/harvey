Union of India and others v Manomoy Ganguly

Supreme Court of India

10 November 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, Ashok Bhushan

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 887

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces

Keywords: Constitution of India, 1950

Summary: Defence & Security Forces - Promotional rank - Overall profile - Entitled to - Respondent/Major General was not empanelled to the rank of Lt. Gen. by Special Promotion Board (SPB) - Respondent after exhausting dept. remedies filed statutory complaint before Tribunal - Tribunal quashed the proceedings of Review SPB on ground that it had allotted wrong board marks to respondent and directed to convene fresh Review SPB to consider respondent for promotion after allotting entitled board marks and to also restore his seniority - Hence, instant appeal - Whether there was any perversity or illegality in impugned judgment of Tribunal.

Held, when Board Members were entitled to give marks to the candidates on the basis of overall profile that was not done. On the contrary, the marks given to those officers who were considered in that SPB, were strictly on the basis of marks obtained by them out of 93 marks. Tribunal was right in observing that in the meeting held Board Marks to all officers who are considered commensurate with the quantified marks of candidates. Board Members adopted the criteria of looking into the quantified marks as the yardstick for assessing overall profile. Having not undertaken the independent exercise of looking into the "overall profile" in SPB and instead assigning the marks to all the officers out of 2 marks, on the basis of quantified marks of candidates which they had received out of 93 marks by treating the same as "overall profile", when it comes to Review SPB appellant was supposed to stick to the same criteria. Only that would show fairness in approach, which would also be in conformity with the principles of equality. It was because of the reason that Review SPB was nothing but extension of original SPB, wherein respondent was supposed to be considered on the same parameters as if he was participating in promotion process undertaken in original SPB. For such error, there was a possibility of different outcome even on value judgment of respondent by Board Members. SC was not interfering with the judgment of Tribunal. Appellant was directed to take further steps, without loss of time, as stated by Tribunal in impugned judgment. Appeal dismissed.
