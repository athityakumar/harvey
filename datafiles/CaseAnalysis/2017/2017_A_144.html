Anita and others v Arun Yadav and others

Supreme Court of India

07 September 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Kurian Joseph, R. Banumathi

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 696

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Computation of income - Appellant submitted that the deceased was a driver and yet the income that is taken by the Tribunal as well as the HC is Rs. 4,200/- - Appellant submitted that there is a the notification issued by the Government of Haryana, Labour Department, where a skilled person in Category 'B' has been granted minimum wages to the tune of Rs. 4,360/- - Appellant further submitted that a driver is not a semi-skilled person but a skilled person.

Held, SC held that the income should have been assessed at Rs. 4,360/- per month. Adding 50 per cent for the future prospects, it comes to Rs. 6,540/-. After deducting 1/4th towards the personal expenses, what would have been saved to the estate would be Rs. 4,905/- per month. Taking the undisputed multiplier of 18, the amount comes to Rs. 10,59,480/-. The HC has rightly awarded Rs 1,00,000/- towards loss of consortium to the widow. The two minor daughters will be entitled to Rs 1,00,000/- each, i.e., Rs. 2,00,000/- and the mother and father get Rs. 50,000/- each, i.e., Rs 1,00,000/-, on account of loss of love and affection. The transportation expenses awarded by the HC to the tune of Rs 10,000/- is maintained. Towards funeral expenses, the appellant shall be entitled to Rs 25,000/-. The appellant shall also be entitled to litigation costs to the tune of Rs 50,000/-. Thus, the appellant will get a total compensation of Rs 15,44,480/- with interest at the rate of 9 per cent per annum from the date of filing of the Claim Petition.

Appeal allowed
