Shivashakti Sugars Limited v Shree Renuka Sugar Limited and others

Supreme Court of India

09 May 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, Abhay Manohar Sapre

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 994; 2017 (7) SCC 729

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative

Keywords: laches, lis, locus standi, mala fide, natural justice

Summary: (A) Labour & Industrial Law – Administrative – Sugarcane (Control) Amendment Order, 2006 – Establishment of sugar cane factory – Writ Petitions were filed before HC against appellant for staling its project for setting up of the sugar factory – HC heard petitions together and vide impugned judgment, held that distance between the factory of the appellant and M/s.Raibagh Sahakari is less than 15 kms and so, the setting up of the factory is in violation of cl.6A of the 2006 Order and as a consequence, the Industrial Entrepreneurs Memoranda (IEM) of the appellant is held to be derecognized – Further, HC also held that extensions dt.18-8-2010 and 1-12-2010 were without jurisdiction as ‘effective steps’ in terms of 2006 Order were not taken and so, no extension could be given – Hence, instant appeal by appellant – Whether M/s.Raibagh Sahakari would be treated as ‘existing sugar factory’ within the meaning of cl.6A of the 2006 Order. Held, in order to qualify as an existing sugar mill, it needs to crush for five consecutive years but HC wrongly recorded that requirement is of crushing for any of the one season out of five and same led to error on the part of HC in holding that M/s.Raibagh Sahakari was an existing sugar factory. Further, case of the appellant for setting up of the factory was proceeded keeping in view the fact that M/s.Raibagh Sahakari was not in operation and much before IEM of appellant was got acknowledged on 8-6-2006, as on that date, there was no ‘existing’ sugar factory within the meaning of cl.6A of 2006 Order. Hence, requirement of distance as prescribed in cl.6A would be inapplicable. Further, the purpose of distance requirement is that there is sufficient availability of sugarcane in the area so that it could easily cater to all the sugar factories. However, appellant’s factory has not adversely affected the utilization of crushing capacity of either M/s.Doodhganga Sahakari factory or M/s.Raibagh Sahakari factory. Further, for last three years, M/s.Doodhganga Sahakari factory had crushed more sugarcane than their target. Hence, under such circumstances and facts, the necessity of distance requirement between M/s.Raibagh Sahakari factory and appellant’s factory as contained in cl.6A of 2006 Order is not attracted. Hence, directions of HC are set aside and appellant’s factory is allowed to continue its operation subject to the condition that 14 villages which were originally assigned to respondent no.1 would be re-allotted to it after taking said villages from appellant. Appeals disposed of. (B) Labour & Industrial Law – Administrative – Economic considerations – Whether in view of the subsequent events, i.e., the establishment of the sugar mill by appellant and it continuing to crush sugarcane since the year 2011, appellant’s factory could be permitted to continue. Held, apart from equitable considerations there are certain economic factors that tilt the balance totally in favour of appellant such as – appellant had spent around Rs.300 Crore in establishing the factory; loans raised to the tune of Rs.237 Crore; operation cost of Rs.150 crore; generation of employment of 377 persons on regular basis and indirect employment of more than 7000 persons; and setting up of co-generation plant for production of electricity that is giving supply of 37mw of electricity. Further, factors particularly, bank loans, employment, generation and production at the factory serve useful public purpose and such economic considerations could not be overlooked, in the context where there is hardly any statutory violation. Hence, directions of HC are set aside and appellant’s factory is allowed to continue its operation subject to the condition that 14 villages which were originally assigned to respondent no.1 would be re-allotted to it after taking said villages from appellant. Appeals disposed of.
