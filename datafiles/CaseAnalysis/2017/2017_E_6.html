Eera, through Dr. Manjula Krippendorf v State Government of (NCT of Delhi) and another

Supreme Court of India

21 July 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, R.F. Nariman

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 881; 2017 (3) KLT 560; 2017 (3) MLJ(Crl) 452

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Women & Children

Keywords: Guardian, Welfare, Establishment, Health, Hindu Minority And Guardianship Act, 1956, Advocates Act, 1961, Medical Termination Of Pregnancy Act, 1971, Supervision, General Clauses Act, 1897, Appropriate Government, Intermediary, Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, Dowry, Local Authorities, Persons With Disabilities (Equal Opportunities, Protection Of Rights And Full Participation) Act, 1995, Pregnancy, Dowry Prohibition Act, 1961, Juvenile Justice Act, 1986, Children, Woman, Special Courts, Juvenile Justice (Care And Protection Of Children) Act, 2000, Exploitation, Guardians and Wards Act, 1890, Sexual Harassment, Representation Of The People Act, 1951, Maharashtra Recognition of Trade Unions and Prevention of Unfair Labour Practices Act, 1971, Sexual Abuse, Calcutta Thika Tenancy Act, 1949, Limitation Act, 1939, Prize Competitions Act, 1955, National Trust For Welfare Of Persons With Autism, Cerebral Palsy, Mental Retardation And Multiple Disabilities Act, 1999, Employees Provident Funds and Miscellaneous Provisions Act, 1952, U.S. Constitution, Criminal Procedure Act, 1977, Protection of Children from Sexual Offences Act, 2012, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Constitution of India, 1950, Income-tax Act, 1961, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, Protection of Children from Sexual Offences Rules, 2012, Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Rules, 2000, Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act, 2015, Rights of Persons with Disabilities Act, 2016, Mental Healthcare Act, 2017, Canada Evidence Act, South African Criminal Law (Sexual Offences and Related Matters) Amendment Act, Ship Registry Act , 1841, Indian Income Tax Act, 1960

Summary: (A) Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 164, 164A, 357A, 482 - Protection of Children from Sexual Offences Act, 2012, ss. 2(1)(d), 2(d), 24, 25, 26, 27, 28, 32, 35, 36, 42A, 45, 5(k) - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 228A, 304B, 305, 361, 376(2)(l), 377, 491, 89, 90, 98 - Rape - Mentally retarded -Compensation envisaged - Appellant/victim’s mother lodged FIR against respondent no. 2/accused alleging that he had committed rape on victim/mentally retarded - Charge sheet was filed against accused for offence punishable u/s. 376(2)(l) IPC - Trial Court at the time of examination of appellant had various aspects of issues - Appellant’s mother filed petition before HC seeking that the matter should be transferred to Special Court under the Act as the functional age of prosecutrix was hardly around 6 to 8 years and trial to be conducted in a most congenial, friendly and comfortable atmosphere and proceeding should be videographed - HC directed that case should be assigned to Trial Court presided over by a lady Judge - Hence, instant appeals - Whether content and context of the Act would allow space for interpretation that was canvassed by appellant.

Held, the Act comprehensively deals with various facets that are likely to offend physical identity and mental condition of a child. The protection of dignity of child was the spine of legislation. In instant case, stretching of the words "age" and "year" would be encroaching upon legislative function and definition in s. 2(d) of the Act defining the term "age" cannot include mental age. However, when accused has breathed his last and there was medical certificate as regards mental disability of victim, there should be no further enquiry as envisaged u/s. 357A CrPC. Further, when Court makes recommendation for compensation, District Legal Services Authority or State Legal Services Authority was required to decide the quantum of compensation to be awarded under the Scheme prepared by State Govt. in coordination with Central Govt. State/District Legal Services Authority has to conduct an inquiry and award adequate compensation by completing the inquiry. Had the accused been alive, the trial would have taken place in a Court of Session as provided under CrPC. As the accused has died and victim was certified to be a mentally disabled person and was fighting the lis for some time to come within the purview of the Act wherein trial was held in a different manner and the provisions relating to the compensation were different, SC direct that State Legal Services Authority should award compensation keeping in view the Scheme framed by State Govt. Thus, it was a fit case where victim should be granted the maximum compensation as envisaged under the Scheme. Appeals disposed of.

R.F.Nariman, J.

(B) Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Protection of Children from Sexual Offences Act, 2012, ss. 2(1)(d), 2(d), 24, 25, 26, 27, 28, 32, 35, 36, 42A, 45, 5(k) - Disabled person - Physical age - Distinction drawn - Whether SC could extend the definition of "child" in s. 2(1)(d) of the Act to include persons below the mental age of 18 years.

Held, when "the age of 18 years" occurs in a statute, it has reference only to physical age. The distinction between a woman who is a minor and an adult woman who was mentally ill is again brought into sharp focus by statute itself. It should be held that Parliament, when it made the Act was fully aware of such distinction, and yet chose to protect only children whose physical age was below 18 years. Further, whatever was the physical age of the person affected, such person would be a "person with disability" who would be governed by the provisions of the Act. Conspicuous by its absence was the reference to any age when it comes to protecting persons with disabilities under the Act. Thus, it was clear that viewed with the lens of the legislator, SC would be doing violence both to the intent and language of Parliament if SC was to read the word "mental" into s. 2(1)(d) of the Act. Given the fact that it was a beneficial/penal legislation, SC could extend it only as far as Parliament intended. Appeals disposed of.
