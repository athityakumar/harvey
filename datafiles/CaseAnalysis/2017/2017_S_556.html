Pranay Kumar Podder v State of Tripura and others

Supreme Court of India

23 March 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, A.M. Khanwilkar

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 218; 2017(4) SCALE 52

Case Digest

Subject: Education

Keywords: Health & Drug, Indian Medical Council Act, 1956, Medical Council Of India, Persons With Disabilities (Equal Opportunities, Protection Of Rights And Full Participation) Act, 1995, Constitution of India, 1950, Rights of Persons with Disabilities Act, 2016

Summary: Education - Indian Medical Council Act, 1956 - Persons with Disabilities (Equal Opportunities, Protection of Rights and Full Participation) Act, 1995 - Rights of Persons with Disabilities Act, 2016 - Persons with colour blindness - Right to pursue medical profession - Appellants, who appeared for Medical entrance examination, were declared to be ineligible to take admission to MBBS course at the stage of counseling on ground that they suffered partial colour blindness - Aggrieved, appellants filed writ petition seeking relief that declaration of their ineligibility by concerned was legally not permissible as there are no regulations framed by MCI under 1956 Act with regard to said ineligibility - However, DB of HC opined that guidelines issued by MCI are to be given weightage and Courts could not interfere solely on the ground that Regulations are silent with regard to denial of admission to an individual suffering from colour blindness and accordingly dismissed writ petition - Hence, instant appeal - Whether appellants could be granted relief sought.

Held, the nature and severity of colour blindness and the disciplines which persons with colour blindness could practise has to be given a re-look as colour blindness is not considered as a disability under 1995 Act and also not a disability under 2016 Act. Total exclusion for admission to medical courses without any stipulation in which said persons could practise and render assistance would tantamount to regressive thinking. Hence, directions are issued to MCI to constitute a Committee of experts that includes representatives of MCI, experts from genetics, ophthalmology, psychiatry and medical education to review the situation and take note of prevalent conditions of the study and practice and suggest changes for adoption in medical course keeping in view the international practices. Further, expert Committee to also concentrate on diagnostic test for progress and review of the disorder and available prosthetics aids to assist CVD medical practitioners and areas of practice they could undertake without difficulty with these aids. Committee to submit report within three months to SC. Matter listed. Order accordingly.
