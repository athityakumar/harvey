Arihant Udhyog v State of Rajasthan and others

Supreme Court of India

09 June 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, Ashok Bhushan

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 880; (2017) 8 SCC 220; AIR 2017 SC 3074; 2017 (5) MLJ 108

Case Digest

Subject: Agriculture & Agri. Products

Keywords: Seeds, Sale of Goods Act, 1930, Agricultural Produce, Rajasthan Agricultural Produce Markets Act, 1961, Rajasthan Agricultural Produce Market Rules, 1963

Summary: Agriculture & Agri. Products - Rajasthan Agricultural Produce Markets Act, 1961, ss. 14, 17 - Rajasthan Agricultural Produce Market Rules, 1963, rr. 58, 58(4) - Agricultural produce - Market fee levied - Challenged - Appellants filed writ petition before HC challenging the action of respondent no.3 in demanding market fee payable under the Rules - HC dismissed petition holding that ownership in the goods stood transferred to appellant only on delivery of goods which took place within the market area and market fee was payable - Hence, instant appeals - Whether appellants purchasing agricultural produce and bringing the same to market area under the Act and the Rules were liable to pay market fee on the said produce.

Held, title in goods was transferred from the seller to buyer only on the sale of goods. As to when such a sale fructifies and property passes was to be ascertained from the intention of the parties having regard to the terms of contract. If no such intention could be gathered from the terms of contract, property in goods passes where goods were in a deliverable state and there was unconditional contract for sale of specific goods. Therefore, intention was to retain the title in the goods till its delivery inasmuch as till that time it was seller who was responsible for the goods. Such condition would clearly spell out that if the goods are destroyed or lost in transit, i.e. before their delivery, responsibility would be that of seller. Such a responsibility could be only if the ownership remains of seller. HC has rightly held that once the goods bought were agricultural produce on which market fee was leviable in terms of Schedule attached to the Act, then the market fee was payable. If it was used as raw material for manufacturing purpose thereafter would be of no consequence. However, it was to be first ascertained whether agricultural produce was bought and sold in the market area or not was the question which needs to be determined in each case after applying the principles of law as enumerated above. HC would be required to ascertain such on the basis of terms and conditions of sale in each case and that would determine the fate of each of the writ petitions filed by appellants. Appeals disposed of.
