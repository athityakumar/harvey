Maharashtra State Co-operative Housing Finance Corporation Limited v Prabhakar Sitaram Bhadange

Supreme Court of India

30 March 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, R.K. Agrawal

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 226; (2017) 5 SCC 623; AIR 2017 SC 1647; 2017(3) ALL MR 422; 2017 (3) Bom.C.R. 70; 2017 (3) MLJ 365; 2017(4) SCALE 158

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Jurisdiction, Specific Relief Act, 1963, Limitation Act, 1963, Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, Relief, Reinstatement, Employment, Appeal, Dismissal, Civil Court, Employer, Administrative Tribunals Act, 1985, Maharashtra Co-operative Societies Act, 1960, Madhya Pradesh Co-operative Societies Act, 1960, Gujarat Co-operative Societies Act, 1961, Bombay Industrial Relations Act, 1946, Punjab Co-operative Societies Act, 1961, State Co-operative Societies Act, Writ Petition, Notice, Employee, Constitution of India, 1950

Summary: Service - Practice & Procedure - Maharashtra Co-operative Societies Act, 1960, ss. 91, 91(1) - Service dispute - Jurisdiction - Respondent was working in appellant/Corporation as Branch Manager - However, for certain acts of misconduct departmental enquiry was conducted and respondent was dismissed from service - Respondent approached Cooperative Court challenging the orders of dismissal - Simultaneously, Corporation filed an application on ground that Cooperative Court did not have jurisdiction to entertain the application - Cooperative Court dismissed application holding that it had requisite jurisdiction to decide the dispute - On appeal, Appellate Court dismissed appeal confirming orders of Cooperative Court - Aggrieved, appellant filed writ petition before HC which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether Cooperative Court established under the Act has requisite jurisdiction to decide 'service dispute' between a cooperative society established under the Act and its employees.

Held, service dispute between employees of such cooperative society and management of society were not covered by the aforesaid provision. The context in which the word 'officers' was used is altogether different, namely, election of committee or its officers. Thus, dispute of such nature does not come within the scope of 'business of the society'. Dispute raised by respondent was not covered within the meaning of s. 91 of the Act. Therefore, Cooperative Court does not have the jurisdiction to entertain the claim filed by respondent. HC proceeded on the basis that if dispute relates to reinstatement, Cooperative Court would not have any jurisdiction. The main reason for conferring jurisdiction upon Cooperative Court in instant case was that Cooperative Court has replaced Civil Court and powers of Civil Court were given to Cooperative Court. However, HC erred in not further analysing the provisions of s. 91 of the Act which spells out the specific powers that were given to Cooperative Court and those powers were of limited nature. Thus, disputes between cooperative society and its employees were not covered by the said provision. Impugned order of HC was set aside and petition filed by respondent before Cooperative Court was not maintainable. It would be open to respondent to file a civil suit. Appeal allowed.
