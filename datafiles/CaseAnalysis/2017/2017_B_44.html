Ajitsinh Arjunsinh Gohil v Bar Council of Gujarat and another

Supreme Court of India

06 April 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, A.M. Khanwilkar

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 266; (2017) 5 SCC 465; AIR 2017 SC 1927; 2017 (4) ALD(SC) 55; 2017 (122) ALR 447; 2017 (2) ALT(SC) 49; 2017 (3) Bom.C.R. 372; 2017 (2) CLT(SC) 22; 2017 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 929; 2017 (4) MLJ 49; 2017(4) SCALE 308

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges

Keywords: Disciplinary Committee, Advocates Act, 1961, Bar Council Of India, Bar Councils, Disciplinary Proceedings, State Bar Councils, Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Advocates Act, 1961, ss. 35, 35(3), 36(2), 36B(1), 37, 42 - Complaint - Transferred - Validity - Appellant was elected as President of Bar Association - Complainant filed complaint against appellant alleging that he was denied access to certain records and there was misappropriation of huge amount of Bar Association - Bar Council took cognizance against appellant and referred matter to Disciplinary Committee - Disciplinary Committee remanded matter to Bar Council with a direction to dispose of the case within a period of 1 year - Hence, instant appeal - Whether after transfer of a disciplinary proceeding u/s. 36B(1) of the Act to Bar Council of India (BCI) could BCI send it back to State Bar Council with direction to decide the controversy within a stipulated time.

Held, once a complaint was made by a litigant it has to follow a definite procedure and was required to be dealt with as per the command of the Act to conclude the disciplinary proceeding within a period of 1 year from the date of receipt of complaint or the date of initiation of proceedings at the instance of State Bar Council. Disciplinary authority of State Bar Council was not disposing of complaint within stipulated period, as a consequence of which the proceeding stands transferred to BCI. The responsibility to deal with the disciplinary proceedings was cast on State Bar Council which constitutes its disciplinary committee. Every member of Disciplinary Committee was aware that the proceeding has to be concluded within 1 year. The complainant and delinquent advocate were required to cooperate. One might not be always right in the decision but that does not mean to be shirking away from taking a decision and allow the matter to be transferred by operation of law to BCI. A statutory authority was obliged to constantly remind itself that the mandate of statute was expediency and stipulation of time was mandatory. It would not be erroneous to say that Disciplinary Committee was expected to perform its duty within a time frame and not to create a blameworthy situation. It was better to remember offering an explanation to one's own conscience was like blaming everything on "accident". It would be advisable that State Bar Councils take a periodical stock of cases in each meeting with regard to the progress of Disciplinary Committee, find out the cause of delay and guide themselves to act with expediency so that Council, as a statutory body, does its duty as commanded under the Act. Impugned order of Disciplinary Committee of BCI was set aside and remand the matter to Disciplinary Committee of BCI to decide the same in accordance with law within a period of 3 months. Appeal allowed.
