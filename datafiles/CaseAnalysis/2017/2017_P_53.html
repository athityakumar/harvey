Board of Trustees for Port of Calcutta v Port Tenants Welfare Association and others

Supreme Court of India

27 July 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Kurian Joseph, R. Banumathi

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 557

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Rent Control

Keywords: Major Port Trusts Act, 1963, Constitution of India, 1950

Summary: Rent Control - Civil Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 142 - Major Port Trusts Act, 1963 - Fixation of rate of interest - Instant appeal filed by appellant being aggrieved by the judgment of the HC whereby the HC granted the facility of 36 installments for payment of arrears of rent and fixed interest @ 6% per annum - It was submitted that the rate of interest should have been 15 to 18% in terms of the rate schedule fixed by the Board under the Act.

Held, the rate was fixed only in the peculiar facts of these cases. This judgment cannot be used as a precedent in other cases for the purpose of fixation of rent since there is no adjudication by the HC on that aspect. Hence, in view of the apprehension expressed, the judgment of the HC regarding fixation of interest @ 6% per annum for the arrears of rent is set aside. However, having regard to the fact since the party respondents have already paid interest at the rate fixed by the HC there shall be no further levy. This order was passed in exercise of jurisdiction u/art. 142 of the Constitution only in the particular facts and circumstances of these cases and this shall not be treated as a precedent. Appeals disposed of.

Constitution of India, 1950 art. 142
