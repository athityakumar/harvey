Essar Steel India Limited and another v State of Gujarat and another

Supreme Court of India

02 May 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhushan, A.K. Sikri

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 375; (2017) 8 SCC 357; AIR 2017 SC 2699; 2017(4) ALL MR 454; 2017 (4) Bom.C.R. 259; 2017(5) SCALE 744

Case Digest

Subject: Electricity & Energy

Keywords: Corporate, Companies Act, 1956, Industrial Undertaking, Indian Electricity Act, 1910, Electricity (Supply) Act, 1948, Central Excise Tariff Act, 1985, Incorporated, Subsidiary, Andhra Pradesh Electricity Reform Act, 1998, Bombay Electricity Duty Act, 1958, Bombay Electricity Duty Rules, 1968

Summary: Electricity & Energy - Administrative - Bombay Electricity Duty Act, 1958, ss. 125, 3, 3(2), 3(2)(vii), 3(2)(vii)(a)(i), 3(3) - Notification - Electricity duty - Exemption sought - Appellant no.1/company filed an application seeking exemption from payment of electricity duty under notification dt. 27-02-1992 u/ss. 3(3), 3(2)(vii)(a)(i) of the Act - State Govt. passed an order rejecting claim of appellant no.1 for exemption - Aggrieved, appellants challenged impugned order before HC which was dismissed - On appeal, DB dismissed appeal affirming judgment of Single Judge - Hence, instant appeal - Appellant no. 1 contended that State Govt. had extended the benefit of exemption from payment of electricity duty to sister concern and appellant no. 1 who was similarly situated cannot be deprived of benefits of exemption.

Held, in instant case, there was no dispute to the fact that appellant no. 2 was created as a Special Purpose Vehicle by appellant no. 1 itself. Had appellant no. 2 would have been supplying energy to appellant no.1 only, the claim deserved consideration. However, it was a case where appellant no.2 was supplying energy to industrial undertakings with whom it was not jointly generating the energy. Further, where State Electricity Board who was allocated 300 MW was not a participating industry nor appellant no.2 was jointly generating energy with State Electricity Board, even if it was held that appellant no.1 to the extent it holds 42% equity shares of appellant no.2 was jointly generating energy. State Electricity Board which was allocated 58% of electricity generated cannot be said as industrial undertaking jointly generating the energy. HC although has noted the fact that in instant case there was no such MOU between EPL and ECL and HC found that conditions stipulating u/s. 3(2)(vii)(a)(i) of the Act were not satisfied and appellant no.1 was not entitled for exemption. HC rightly came to the conclusion that conditions as enumerated in s. 3(2)(vii)(a) of the Act were not fulfilled. SC do not find any error in the aforesaid finding of HC. Thus, SC do not find any error in rejection of claim of appellant under the notification. HC rightly negatived the claim of appellant u/s. 3(2) as well as under notification issued u/s. 3(3) of the Act. Appeal dismissed.
