Venture Global Engineering LLC v Tech Mahindra Limited and Another etc

Supreme Court of India

01 November 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

J. Chelameswar, Abhay Manohar Sapre

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 852

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Keywords: Estoppel, Arbitral Award, Arbitral Tribunal, Jurisdiction, Companies Act, 1956, Indian Contract Act, 1872, Arbitration And Conciliation Act, 1996, Foreign Exchange Regulation Act, 1973, Foreign Awards (Recognition And Enforcement) Act, 1961, Conciliation, Foreign Exchange Management Act, 1999, Foreign Exchange Management (Transfer or Issue of Security By a Person Resident Outside India) Regulations, 2000, Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Constitution of India, 1950, Companies Act, 2013

Summary: Civil Appeal Nos. 17753-17755 and 17756 of 2017

J. Chelameswar

(A) Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, ss. 34, 34(2), 34(2)(b)(ii), 36, 48 - Arbitral award - Enforcement - Challenged - Appellants/defaulting shareholder and respondents/non-defaulting shareholder entered into an agreement - Disputes arose between parties and matter was referred to arbitration - Arbitrator opined that an Event of Default on part of appellants occurred and was liable to transfer 50 per cent of the shares to respondents - Respondents filed petition in foreign District Court seeking enforcement of the award against appellants which was allowed - Appellants filed an application before Trial Court seeking declaration that the Award was illegal and without jurisdiction restraining respondents from enforcing the Award - Trial Court held that Award was affected and induced by fraud and cannot be enforced being opposed to public policy of Country - On appeal, HC allowed appeal and restored order of Trial Court - Hence, instant appeals - Whether fudging of the accounts of respondents would in any way provide a ground for appellants to seek setting aside the Award.

Held, s. 34(2) of the Act renders an Award liable to be set aside was that the making of Award should be induced by fraud or Award was affected by fraud. Neither does Trial Court judgment identify the legal parameters for recording a conclusion that making of Award was induced by or fraud or that Award was affected by fraud, nor does it explain how the non-disclosure of facts relating to the true financial status of respondents actually was an inducement for making of the Award. No reference was made to the pleadings of appellants as to how appellants believed that the "CONCEALED FACTS" are material for the adjudication of the dispute by arbitrator. Equally absent was the discussion by Trial Court as to how CONCEALED FACTS would become material facts in the context of arbitration. SC only held that CONCEALED FACTS of Chairman were relevant and appellants should be permitted to plead those facts. However, SC did not make any declaration that such facts would constitute material facts rendering Award liable to be set aside on the ground that the non-disclosure of those facts before arbitrator would amount to fraud, inducing the making of Award or that Award was affected by fraud. At the same time, SC categorically declared that nothing said in the judgment would be construed as even remotely expressing any opinion on the legality of award. HC rightly disagreed with the conclusions of Trial Court and reversed judgment of Trial Court. HC ought to have given more cogent reasons for the disagreement. Appeals disposed of.

CIVIL APPEAL No. OF 2017 (ARISING OUT OF SLP (C) No. 8298/2014)

Abhay Manohar Sapre

(B) Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, ss. 34, 34(2), 34(2)(b)(ii), 36, 48 - Companies Act, 2013, ss. 8.03, 8.04, 11.05(c), 209 - Suppression of facts - Arbitral proceedings - Violation - Whether the acts of chairman in the affairs of respondents amounts to misrepresentation/ suppression of material facts and, if so, they constitute a ground to enable SC to set aside the Award u/s. 34 of the 1996 Act.

Held, a fortiori, the acts of chairman in the affairs of respondents had direct bearing over the claim filed by respondents against appellants in arbitration proceedings. Had chairman brought his acts of respondents to the notice of shareholders/Board of Directors of in any Board meeting appellants too would have been able to get first right to terminate Agreement u/s. 8.01(b) r/ w s. 11.05(c) of the 2013 Act and claim appropriate reliefs against respondents in point of time. Appellants deprived of their legal and contractual rights to exercise against respondents but for no fault of theirs. Appellants also lost their right to defend respondent's claim before Arbitrator on those grounds which were deliberately suppressed by respondents from appellants.

Breach on part of respondents who was 50% shareholder was clearly made out under Agreement thereby entitling appellants to take recourse to the remedies provided in ss. 8.03 and 8.04 of the 2013 Act against respondents on happening of such events. Application filed by appellant u/s. 34 of the Act could not be dismissed by applying the principle of "issue- estoppel", which had no application to the civil proceedings. Thus, arbitral proceedings including the Award in question was passed in violation of public policy u/s. 34(2)(b)(ii) of the 1996 Act and not legally sustainable. Impugned judgment of HC was set aside set aside and that of Trial Court was restored. Appeals disposed of.
