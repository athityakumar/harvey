Kattukulangara Madhavan (Dead), through Lrs. and others v Majeed and others

Supreme Court of India

30 March 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

L. Nageswara Rao, S.A. Bobde

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 227; (2017) 5 SCC 568; AIR 2017 SC 2004; 2017 (2) MLJ(Crl) 521; 2017 (2) RCR(Criminal) 447; 2017(4) SCALE 123

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Common Object, FIR, Assembly, Corroboration, List Of Witnesses, Unlawful Assembly, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, Complainant

Summary: L. Nageswara Rao, J.

(A) Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 143, 147, 148, 149, 302, 307, 323, 324, 326, 341, 342 - Unlawful assembly - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellants/accused allegedly attacked deceased and others - Charge sheet was filed against accused for offences u/ss. 143, 147, 148, 341, 323 and 302/149 IPC - Trial Court convicted A1 to A4, A14, A15, and A18 u/s. 302 r/w s. 149 IPC and sentenced them to imprisonment for life and were also convicted for offences u/ss. 143, 147, 148, 341, 342 and 324/149 IPC - Further, Trial Court acquitted A5 to A13, A16 and A17 - On appeals, HC affirmed judgment of Trial Court acquitting A5 to A13, A16 and A17 however, A1 was convicted u/s. 302 IPC and sentenced to imprisonment for life - Further, HC convicted A2 and A4 u/ss. 324/149 IPC and were sentenced to imprisonment of 1 year - Hence, instant appeals.

Held, HC committed a serious error in not taking into consideration the evidence of PW4. The finding recorded by HC that evidence of PW4 could be considered only for limited purpose of corroboration of evidence of PW3 was unreasonable and perverse. After recording a finding that evidence of PW4 cannot be rejected only on ground that he was not questioned by police, HC proceeded to hold that evidence of PW4 could be used only for corroboration of PW3's evidence. There was a consolidated list of witnesses given by prosecution. HC has not given any reason as to why the evidence of PW4 could be used only for corroboration. Trial Court was right in relying upon his testimony and HC was not correct in holding that it could be used only for corroboration of PW3's evidence. The finding of HC that A3, A14, A15 and A18 were entitled for acquittal on the basis that PW3 did not speak about their presence was liable to be set aside as PW4 had categorically mentioned about their involvement. HC held that deceased was attacked due to political rivalry but there was no evidence to show that the members of unlawful assembly had a common object to commit his murder. SC was not in agreement with HC regarding the absence of common object of A3, A4, A14, A15 and A18. The evidence on record shows that deceased and accused belong to two political parties opposed to each other. Thus, A3, A4, A14 and A18 were sentenced to 7 years imprisonment u/ss. 326/149 IPC and accused should surrender within 4 weeks. Appeals disposed of.

S.A. Bobde

(B) Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 143, 147, 148, 149, 302, 307, 323, 324, 326, 341, 342 - Conduct of accused - Inculpatory - Justifiability - Whether mere presence of A4 in unlawful assembly could not be inculpatory since none of the witnesses attributed an overt act to accused.

Held, presence of an accused as part of an unlawful assembly, when not as a curious onlooker or a bystander, suggests his participation in the object of assembly. When prosecution establishes such presence, then it was the conduct of accused that would determine whether he continued to participate in unlawful assembly with the intention to fulfill the object of assembly or not. It could well be that an accused had no intention to participate in the object of assembly. But having participated and gone along with the others, an inference whether inculpatory or exculpatory could be drawn from the conduct of such an accused. In case of A4, there was no such evidence on record that having participated in unlawful assembly which resulted in the death of deceased, he made any attempt to either stop the incident from taking place, or having found out that he could not prevent it, dissociated himself from assembly. Therefore, A4 should be held liable u/ss. 326/149 IPC. Appeals disposed of.
