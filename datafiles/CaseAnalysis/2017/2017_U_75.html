Union of India and others v K. P. Singh and another

Supreme Court of India

12 January 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Khanwilkar, D.Y. Chandrachud

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 33; (2017) 3 SCC 289; AIR 2017 SC 549; JT 2017 (1) SC 264; 2017(1) SCALE 523; 2017 (2) UPLBEC 1169

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces

Keywords: Armed Forces, Corps, Indian Navy, Army Act, 1950, Air Force Act, 1950, Navy Act, 1957, Constitution of India, 1950

Summary: Defence & Security Forces - Denial of Benefit of Scheme - Directions Challenged - Respondents/employees rendered service as officers of army medical corps for more than 20 years of commissioner/ group A gazette service - Respondents were denied carrier progression as per scheme approved by appellant/central govt. - Tribunal allowed applications of respondents on basis of decision rendered in similar matter and directed appellant to issue instructions for implement of scheme in army cadre - Hence, instant appeals and writ petition.

Held, as per para 10 of Army Instructions 74/1976, officers granted permanent commission in the Army Medical Corps receive pay and allowances at the rates laid down in Pay and Allowances Regulations for officers of the Army, as amended from time to time by the Ministry of Defence, govt. of India in consultation with Department of Expenditure, Ministry of Finance. The authorities were directed to issue instructions for the implementation of DACP scheme in the light of Annexure A-1, A-2 and A-3 attached with the Original Application within three months from the date of receipt of copy of the order. That direction will have to be construed to mean that the authorities must act in accordance with law and extend DACP scheme even to the Commissioned Officers of AMC Cadre, if permissible in law. No more and no less. Therefore, SC whilst dismissing the Civil Appeal on 23-9-2011 of similar matter observed that no substantial question of law of general/public importance arises for consideration. That decision cannot be given an expansive meaning so as to be read that de-hors the legal position, DACP scheme be extended even to doctors working as Commissioned Officers in AMC Cadre. However, the issues raised in the present appeals by the govt. and in particular by the Ministry of Defence are not concluded nor have they attained finality. Hence, to do substantial and complete justice to the parties, orders passed by the Tribunal in the appeals are set aside and remand the original application to the Tribunal for reconsideration of the entire matter de novo. Further, writ petition is disposed of with liberty to the writ petitioner to either intervene in the remanded proceedings before the Tribunal or to file a fresh Original Application for the relief claim. Appeals disposed of.
