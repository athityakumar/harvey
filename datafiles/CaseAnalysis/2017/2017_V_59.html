Virupakshappa Gouda and another v State of Karnataka and another

Supreme Court of India

28 March 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, A.M. Khanwilkar

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 225; (2017) 5 SCC 406; AIR 2017 SC 1685; 2017 CRLJ 2769; 2017 (2) JCC 1369; JT 2017 (6) SC 480; 2017 (2) RCR(Criminal) 491; 2017(4) SCALE 133

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Innocent, Dealing, Change, Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 109, 114, 143, 147, 148, 149, 302, 323, 420B, 468, 471, 504 - Criminal assault - Acquittal - Sustainability - Appellants/accused armed with weapons allegedly assaulted deceased who succumbed to injuries - Charge sheet was filed against accused for the offences punishable u/ss. 143, 147, 148, 323, 302, 504, 114 r/w s. 149 IPC - Trial Court released appellants on bail on certain conditions - On appeal, HC allowed the application for cancellation of bail and set aside the order passed by Trial Court - Hence, instant appeal - Whether there was any perversity or illegality in impugned judgment of Trial Court.

Held, in instant case Trial Court has not been guided by the established parameters for grant of bail. It has not kept himself alive to the fact that twice the bail applications were rejected and the matter had travelled to SC. Once, SC declined to enlarge appellants on bail, endevours to project same factual score should not have been allowed. It was absolute impropriety and that impropriety call for axing of order. The gravity of crime should have been taken note by Trial Judge. Deceased and his wife (daughter of accused no.1) were staying in peace away from acrimonious community, but due to some kind of "misconceived class honour", the vengeance reigned and awe for law went on a holiday. The choice of daughter was allowed no space. Her identity was crushed and her thinking was crucified by parental dominance which has roots in an unfathomable sense of community honour. Though the lovers became fugitive, anger founded on anachronistic values prompted accused persons to annihilate the life of a young man. In such a situation, factors that have been highlighted by SC from time to time were required to be adverted to and accused should not have been granted liberty on grounds that have been thought appropriate by Trial Court. The perversity of approach by Trial Court who has enlarged appellants on bail was totally unacceptable. Thus, HC has performed its legal duty by lancinating the order passed by Trial Court. Appellants should surrender to custody and it would be the duty of Trial Judge to see that they were taken into custody. Appeal dismissed.
