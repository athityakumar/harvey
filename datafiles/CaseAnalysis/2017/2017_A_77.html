Amardeep Singh v Harveen Kaur

Supreme Court of India

12 September 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Adarsh Kumar Goel, Uday Umesh Lalit

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 703; (2017) 8 SCC 746; 2017 (5) ALT(SC) 23; 2017 (242) DLT 264; JT 2017 (9) SC 106

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Keywords: divorce, Hindu Marriage Act, 1955, Family Courts Act, 1984, Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, Constitution of India, 1950

Summary: (A) Family & Personal - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 142 - Hindu Marriage Act, 1955, s.13B - Exercise of power by SC - Waiver of statutory period - Disputes arose between the parties which resulted in civil and criminal proceedings but later parties arrived at settlement and sought divorce by mutual consent - Instant petition for waiver of six months period on ground that they have been living separately for last eight years and there is no possibility of their re-union and that any delay would affect chances of their resettlement - Further, contending that only SC could relax said period of six months - Whether exercise of power u/art.142 to waive statutory period u/s.13B of the Act is appropriate.

Held, since the law laid down in Manish Goel case holds the field, in absence of contrary decisions by a larger Bench, power u/art.142 of the Constitution could not be exercised contrary to statutory provisions, especially when no proceedings are pending before SC and where SC is approached only for the purpose of waiver of the statute. Appeal disposed of.

(B) Family & Personal - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O.32 r.3 - Family Courts Act, 1984, s.9 - Hindu Marriage Act, 1955 - Waiver of statutory period - Conditions to be satisfied - Whether s.13(2) of 1955 Act is to be read as mandatory or discretionary.

Held, where the Court dealing with a matter is satisfied that a case is made out to waive the statutory period u/s.13B(2) of 1955 Act, then it could do so after considering - the statutory period of six months specified in s.13B(2), in addition to the statutory period of one year u/s.13B(1) of separation of parties is already over before the first motion itself; all efforts for mediation/conciliation including efforts in terms of O.32A r.3 of CPC read with s.23(2) of 1955 Act and s.9 of 1984 Act to reunite parties have failed; the parties have genuinely settled their differences including alimony, custody of child or any other pending issues between them; that the waiting period would only prolong their agony. In case, said conditions are satisfied, then the waiver of waiting period for second motion would be in the discretion of the concerned Court. Hence, as the period mentioned in s.13B(2) of 1955 Act is not mandatory but directory, it is open to the Court to exercise its discretion in the facts and circumstances of each case where there is no possibility of parties resuming cohabitation. In view of instant order, the parties in instant case are at liberty to move concerned Court for fresh consideration. Appeal disposed of.

Ratio - Jurisdiction of SC u/art.142 could not be used to waive the statutory period of six months for filing the second motion u/s.13B.
