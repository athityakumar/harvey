State of Uttarakhand and others v Kumaon Stone Crusher

Supreme Court of India

15 September 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhushan, A.K. Sikri

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 749

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Mines & Minerals; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Maintenance, Pith And Substance, Jurisdiction, Transfer Of Property Act, 1882, Minerals, Code Of Civil Procedure (Amendment) Act, 1976, Marginal Note, Mineral Concession Rules, 1960, Industries (Development And Regulation) Act, 1951, Forest (Conservation) Act, 1980, Constitution (Forty-Second Amendment) Act, 1976, Chit Funds Act, 1982, Uttar Pradesh Trade Tax Act, 1948, Indian Forest Act, 1927, Government of India Act, 1915, Karnataka Forest Act, 1963, Bombay Public Trusts Act, 1950, Orissa Mining Areas Development Fund Act, 1952, Punjab Courts Act, 1918, Coal Bearing Areas (Acquisition And Development) Act, 1957, Minor Minerals, Mines and Minerals (Development and Regulation) Act, 1957, Kerala Chitties Act, 1975, Calcutta Municipal Corporation (Taxation) Regulations, 1989, Tonnage, Part B States (Laws) Act, 1951, Uttar Pradesh Transit of Timber and other Forest Produce Rules, 1978, Uttar Pradesh Minor Minerals (Concession) Rules, 1963, Mysore House Rent and Accommodation Control Order, 1948, Travancore-Cochin High Court Act, Karnataka Court-fees and Suits Valuation Act, 1958, Uttar Pradesh Establishment and Regulation of Saw-mills Rules, 1978, Constitution of India, 1950, Income-tax Act, 1961, A.P. Sandalwood and Red Sanderswood Transit Rules, 1969, Madhya Pradesh Transit (Forest Produce) Rules, 2000, Uttar Pradesh Establishment and Regulation of Saw Mills (Fourth Amendment) Rules, 2010, Mines and Minerals(Development and Regulation) Act, 1948, Uttar Pradesh Amendment Act, 1956, Fourth and Fifth Amendment Rules, 1978, Uttar Pradesh Transit of Timber and other Forest Produce (Third Amendment) Rules, 2004, Tripura Transit Rules, Uttaranchal Minor Minerals (Concession) Rules, 2001, Transit Fee Rules, 1978, Mineral Transit Pass Regulations, 1996

Summary: Mines & Minerals - Administrative - Indian Forest Act, 1927, ss. 2(4), 2(4)(b), 2(4)(b)(iv), 20, 29(3), 38A(c), 4, 41, 80A - Coal Bearing Areas (Acquisition and Development) Act, 1957 - Madhya Pradesh Transit (Forest Produce) Rules, 2000, r. 5 - Uttar Pradesh Transit of Timber and Other Forest Produce Rules, 1978, rr. 3, 4(1)(b), 5 - Stone crushing - Transit fee levied - Quashing off - Respondents/Stone Crusher company filed writ petition for quashing orders issued by Conservator of Forest and Divisional Forest Officer directing for recovery and levy of Transit Fee upon finished item of stone i.e. stone grits, stone chips etc. contending that after it becomes a commercial commodity, it ceases to be as Forest Produce and no Transit Fee could be charged and recovered - DB allowed petition and quashed impugned orders of appellants - Hence, instant appeals - Whether the stone gittis, sand chips and dust continue to be stone grits, chips and dust or after crushing them get converted into a new commercial products so as to attract the tax on their sale.

Held, crushing of stones, stone boulders into stone grits, stone chips and stone dust does not result into a new commodity different from forest produce. The crushed materials continue to be stone and retain their nature of forest produce. The 1927 Act and the Rules framed u/s. 41 of the 1927 Act were neither overridden nor impliedly repealed, altered or amended by the 1957 Act. Both the above legislations operate in different spheres and fields. The words 'brought from forest' cannot be read as "brought through forest". SC clarify that for an item to be treated as forest produce, its origin might be in any forest within the State or in a forest outside State. The forest has to be understood according to its dictionary meaning which covers the statutory recognised forest and should include any area regarded as forest in Govt. record irrespective of ownership. The meaning of forest cannot be restricted only to reserve forests, protected forests and village forests. Transit fee was payable on all kinds of transit passes and cannot be confined only to transit passes as referred to in r. 4(1)(b) of the 1978 Rules. The fact that no notification u/s. 20 of the Act was issued does not mean that restriction put by State Govt. by Rules are not applicable. DB has rightly struck down Fourth and Fifth Amendment Rules to the 1978 Rules as being excessive and confiscatory in nature. Therefore, notification issued by State in exercise of power u/r. 5 of the 2000 Rules cannot be said to be beyond the scope and s. 41 of the 1927 Act. Thus, State was fully justified in fixing rate of transit fee at the rate of Rs.7/- and Rs.4/- per tonne which was well within the power of State u/r. 5 of the 2000 Rules framed under the 1927 Act. Appeals disposed of.
