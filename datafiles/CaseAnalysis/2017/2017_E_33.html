Union of India and others v Ex Lac Nallam Shiva

Supreme Court of India

10 August 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Khanwilkar, Dipak Misra, Amitava Roy

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 600

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces

Keywords: Armed Forces, Air Force Act, 1950, Defence Service Regulations

Summary: Defence & Security Forces - Air Force Act, 1950, s. 161(2) - Defence Service Regulations, regn. 754(C) - Dismissal - Reinstatement - Entitled to - Respondent/Corporal was dismissed from service who had overstayed casual leave allegedly due to his ill-health and family problems - Respondent filed petition u/s. 161(2) of the Act before authorities seeking for his reinstatement which was rejected - Aggrieved, respondent filed an application before Tribunal challenging impugned order - Tribunal set aside punishment of dismissal and directed appellants to reinstate respondent in service - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, Tribunal ought not to interfere with the order of punishment except in appropriate cases only after recording a finding that punishment imposed was grossly or shockingly disproportionate, after examining all the relevant factors including the nature of charges proved against delinquent officer. Tribunal misdirected itself in invoking regn. 754(C) of the Regulations and to reckon the mitigating circumstance such as respondent has already undergone punishment of sentence for the stated offence. Thus, Tribunal exceeded its jurisdiction in overturning the order of punishment imposed by disciplinary authority and instead directing reinstatement of respondent in service and treating the period between the date of dismissal of respondent and the date of his rejoining service as non-qualifying service, so as to give him a chance of rehabilitation in service. SC modify the order of dismissal from service to one of discharge from service simplicitor. Appeal partly allowed.
