Shivakumar M. v Managing Director, BMTC

Supreme Court of India

14 February 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Kurian Joseph, A.M. Khanwilkar

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 123; (2017) 5 SCC 79; 2017 (122) ALR 18; JT 2017 (3) SC 49; 2017 (3) RCR(Civil) 195; 2017(3) SCALE 265; 2017 (2) WLN(SC) 34

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Motor accident claim - Enhancement of compensation - Entitled to - Appellant filed instant appeal challenging impugned order of HC wherein HC has not given adequate compensation for the injuries suffered by appellant in a motor accident.

Held, there was no evidence available with regard to the income of appellant however there was no dispute on the fact that he was a painter by profession. For a casual worker, who goes from house to house and place to place doing his painting work it was difficult to get any evidence, since there was no employer. Appellant does his daily work, sometimes piece rated work as well. That was why appellant made a moderate self-estimation of his income to Rs.15,000/- to Rs.16,500/-. Tribunal and HC should have accepted the evidence of appellant. Therefore, income was substituted as Rs.10,000/- in the place of Rs.6,500/-, as assessed by HC. The compensation would carry interest at the rate of 9% p.a. from the date of claim petition before Tribunal. Respondent was directed to work out the compensation and deposit the amount before Tribunal within a period of 3 months. Appeal allowed.
