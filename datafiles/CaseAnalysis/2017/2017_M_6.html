Canara Bank v M. Amarender Reddy and another

Supreme Court of India

02 March 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Khanwilkar, Dipak Misra, Mohan M. Shantanagoudar

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 428; (2017) 4 SCC 735; AIR 2017 SC 1441; 2017 (3) ALD(SC) 117; 2017 (3) BC 11; 2017 (2) CLT(SC) 440; 2017 (2) CTC 790; JT 2017 (3) SC 212; 2017 (3) MLJ 317; 2017(3) SCALE 304

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Immovable Property, Tender, Securitisation And Reconstruction Of Financial Assets And Enforcement Of Security Interest Act, 2002, Security Interest (Enforcement) Rules, 2002, Land & Property

Summary: Banking & Finance - Contract & Commercial - Securitisation and Reconstruction of Financial Assets and Enforcement of Security Interest Act, 2002, ss. 13 - Security Interest (Enforcement) Rules, 2002 - Notice of intention of sale to borrower - Public notice - Appellant-bank provided financial assistance of Rs. 1 Crore to Borrower and respondent no.1 was one of the two guarantors for said loan transaction and offered his immovable property as security - However, as principal borrower committed default, appellant-bank issued demand notices u/ss.13(2), 13(4) of the Act and also notice in terms of r.8(6) of the Rules was also given to principal borrower and both guarantors - Later, auction was held on 21-11-2015 and property of respondent no.1 held as security was sold to highest bidder - However, Respondent no.1 preferred writ petition before HC for declaration that e-auction notice dt.15-10-2015 was illegal and in contravention of provisions of the Act and Rules framed there under - HC rendered findings that separate notice of 30 days duration ought to have been given by appellant-Bank to writ petitioner before public notice fixing the date of auction sale was issued and that thirty days notice to borrower about intention to sell the secured asset ought to precede the actual publication of sale notification in newspaper and that both notices could not be issued simultaneously - Hence, instant appeal by appellant-bank - Whether notice of intention of sale to be given to borrower and public notice for sale could be issued simultaneously.

Held, secured creditor, after it decides to proceed with the sale of secured asset consequent to taking over possession, is though required to give a notice of 30 days for sale of the immovable asset as per r.8(6), yet, there is nothing in the Rules, either express or implied, to mean that public notice u/r.8(6) of the Rules has to be issued only after expiry of 30 days from issuance of individual notice by the authorized officer to the borrower about the intention to sell the immovable secured asset. The only restriction is to give 30 days' time gap between such notice and the date of sale of the immovable secured asset. As such, HC committed manifest error in assuming that notice of intention of sale to be given to the borrower and public notice for sale could not be simultaneously issued. In instant matter as public auction sale held on 21-11-2015 has not materialized, appellant could resort to fresh public notice for sale of secured asset of respondent no.1 if outstanding liability is still unpaid and sale is to be effected either by inviting tenders from the public or by holding public auction. Appeal allowed.
