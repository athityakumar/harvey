Sama Aruna v State of Telangana and another

Supreme Court of India

03 May 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

S.A. Bobde, L. Nageswara Rao

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 408; AIR 2017 SC 2662; JT 2017 (6) SC 345; 2017(6) SCALE 327

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Maintenance, Detenu, Public Order, Detaining Authority, Subjective Satisfaction, Arms Act, 1959, Andhra Pradesh Land Grabbing (Prohibition) Act, 1982, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Constitution of India, 1950, Andhra Pradesh Prevention of Dangerous Activities of Boot Leggers, Dacoits, Drug-Offenders, Goondas, Immoral Traffic Offenders and Land Grabbers (Amendment) Act, 1986

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Andhra Pradesh Prevention of Dangerous Activities of Boot Leggers, Dacoits, Drug-Offenders, Goondas, Immoral Traffic Offenders and Land Grabbers (Amendment) Act, 1986, ss. 3(2), 3(3) - Detention - Stale incident - Challenged - Appellant wife of detenue filed instant appeal challenging impugned order of HC whereby HC dismissed writ petition of detenue challenging the order of detention issued against him under the Act by respondent no. 2/Commissioner of Police.

Held, detention order in instant case was vitiated by taking into account incidents so far back in the past as would have no bearing on immediate need to detain him without a trial. The satisfaction of the authority was not in respect of the thing in regard to which it was required to be satisfied. Incidents which were stale, cease to have relevance to the subject matter of enquiry and should be treated as extraneous to the scope and purpose of statute. There was another reason why detention order was unjustified. It was passed when accused was in jail his custody in jail for the said offence was converted into custody under impugned detention order. The incident involved in that offence was sometime in the year 2002-2003. The detenu could not have been detained preventively by taking such stale incident into account, more so when he was in jail. Thus, authority has come to a conclusion so unreasonable that no reasonable authority could ever reach. A detaining authority should be taken to know both, the purpose and procedure of law. Impugned detention order passed by respondent no. 2 and judgment and order passed by HC was set aside. Appeal allowed.
