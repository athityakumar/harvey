Amrutbhai Shambhubhai Patel v Sumanbhai Kantibhai Patel and others

Supreme Court of India

02 February 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Amitava Roy, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 70; AIR 2017 SC 774; 2017 CRLJ 1344; JT 2017 (2) SC 108; 2017 (1) KLT 707; 2017 (1) RCR(Criminal) 1030; 2017 (1) RLW 346; 2017(2) SCALE 198

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: FIR, CBI, First Information Report, Appearance, Final Report, Investigating Agency, Bond, Permission, Sufficient Ground, Protest, Prayer, Cognizance, Police Report, Copies, Further Investigation, Recommendation, Cognizable, Informant, Arms Act, 1959, Explosive Substances Act, 1908, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898, Notary, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, Investigating Officer, Inquiry

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 173(8) - Investigation by police - Verdict rendered by HC setting at naught order passed by Judicial Magistrate where Trial Court had allowed application filed by Appellant, original informant u/s. 173(8) of CrPC for further investigation by police - Hence, instant appeal - Whether, HC erred in setting at naught order passed by Judicial Magistrate where Trial Court allowed application filed by Appellant is proper. Held, in Hasanbhai Valibhai Qureshi v. State of Gujarat and others ruling that police had power to conduct further investigation de hors any direction from Court even after it had taken cognizance was relied upon to reinforce its conclusion. HC amongst others marked that evidence of appellant/informant had been recorded in year 2012 when he did have sufficient opportunity to scrutinise document in question but for inexplicable reasons did wait for more than two years to register prayer for further investigation. HC thus interfered with order of Magistrate permitting further investigation by police in case and ordered for expeditious disposal of trial. That though investigating agency concerned has been invested with power to undertake further investigation desirably after informing Court. Delay on part of informant in making request for further investigation, it was not entertainable as has been rightly held by HC. Appeal dismissed.
