State of M. P. and another v Kedia Great Galeon Limited and another

Supreme Court of India

28 February 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhushan, Ranjan Gogoi

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 471; 2017 (3) MLJ 550; 2017(3) SCALE 221

Case Digest

Subject: Excise

Keywords: Ultra Vires, Jurisdiction, Central Excise Act, 1944, Retrospective Effect, SERVICE, Relief, Pay, Salary, Representation, Rule, Terms And Conditions, Appeal, Amendment, Charges, Madhya Pradesh Excise Act, 1915, Madhya Pradesh Distillery Rules, 1995, Madhya Pradesh Brewery Rules, 1970, Writ Petition, Notice, Right to, Central Provinces Act, 1915, M. P. Excise Act, 1995

Summary: Excise - Madhya Pradesh Excise Act, 1915, ss. 27, 28 - Madhya Pradesh Excise Act, 1995, s.28A - Madhya Pradesh Distillery Rules, 1995, r. 4(41) - Validity of demand notice - Aggrieved by notice dt. 23-3-1999 issued by Excise Officer demanding amount of Rs. 13,24,189.50 as excess expenditure on the establishment of officers and employees pertaining to years 1995-96,1996-97 and 1997-98, respondent no.1 filed writ petition before HC - Single Judge opined that r. 4(41) of the Rules appears to be ultra vires to 1915 Act being beyond the rule making power, yet, as no such prayer is made no order could be passed in the Rules by Bench - Further, it was held that since demand towards establishment charges is more than 150 per cent of the total income of the distilleries, demand is arbitrary and unreasonable - Further, it was also held that decision of SC in Lilasons case renders demand notice void - Aggrieved, State filed appeal before DB of HC but the same was dismissed as not maintainable - Hence, instant appeals assailing said orders of Single Judge and DB of HC - Whether impugned order is sustainable.

Held, the basis of judgment of SC in Lilasons was the judgment in Banerjee case. However, as the scheme of 1915 Act was amended by 1995 Act, the very basis of Banerjee case is knocked down and could not be relied on in view of changed statutory scheme. Further, the judgment in Lilason's case was delivered on 21-4-1992 that is before the said amendment in s.28 by 1995 Act. Further, after amendment in s.28 by Amendment 1995 Act, the contents of it have entirely been changed and s.28 as noticed in Lilasons case could not be relied on for finding out as to whether demand u/r.(41) of the Rules is beyond the scope of s.28 or not. Further, no sufficient foundation was laid in writ petition to enter into the issue as to whether the demand is arbitrary and unreasonable. From the details of demand it is clear that in the demand for the year 1996-97 expenditure on salary was shown as Rs.4,36,897 but no figure pertaining to Revenue of the said year is mentioned and whether the distillery could function during the relevant period or not and without there being any revenue how the expenditure on salary is fastened on respondent, is not explained. Hence, considering overall circumstances, liberty is granted to respondent to represent against the demand notice dt.23-3-1989 before the State. Further, State Govt. to consider such representation taking into consideration all relevant facts relating to concerned years and other factors. As such, impugned judgment of HC is set aside. Appeals disposed of.
