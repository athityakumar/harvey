State Bank of India and others v Kingfisher Airlines Limited and others

Supreme Court of India

09 May 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Uday Umesh Lalit, Adarsh Kumar Goel

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 412; (2017) 6 SCC 654; 2017 (3) BC 325; JT 2017 (5) SC 515; 2017(6) SCALE 228

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance

Keywords: Interlocutory, Jurisdiction, Stock Exchange, Attorney, Contempt, Constitution of India, 1950

Summary: Banking & Finance - Loan recovery - Contempt proceedings - Jurisdiction - Petitioners/banks individually advanced to respondent no.1 loans of thousands of crores of rupees by Master Debts Recast Agreement dt. 21-10-2010 - However, existing loans were restructured and treated as a single facility and respondent nos. 2 and 3 guaranteed repayment of entire amount due - Petitioners filed application against respondent nos. 1 to 9 before DRT seeking recovery of loan from respondent nos. 1 to 4 - DRT heard arguments only with respect to Garnishee Application and posted the matters for orders - Aggrieved, petitioners filed writ petitions before HC seeking appropriate directions to DRT to hear and dispose of applications moved by petitioners - HC refused to pass any ad interim order against respondent nos. 3, 10 and 11 - Hence, instant special leave petitions.

Held, respondent no. 3 in not disclosing the account in question through which the transfers were affected also fall with respect to contempt on first count, SC proceed to exercise contempt jurisdiction even with regard to second count. Respondent no. 3 was adequately put to notice and no prejudice was caused as a result of such assumption of jurisdiction by SC. Violation by respondent no. 3 regarding non-disclosure becomes more pronounced because it was that very account held in Bank that was utilized to transmit funds to the tune of US$ 40 Million. Respondent no. 3 was guilty of having committed contempt of Court on both the counts. At that stage it should be stated that in terms of r. 6(1) of Rules to Regulate Proceeding for Contempt of Supreme Court 1975, respondent no. 3 was obliged and duty bound to appear in person in response to the notice issued by SC in Contempt Petition. Instead, he chose to file application seeking recall of the orders issuing notice. Respondent no. 3 was duty bound to appear in person in contempt proceedings. Since, respondent no. 3 has not filed any reply to Contempt Petition nor did he appear in person, though SC found him guilty of having committed contempt of Court, SC deem it necessary to give him one more opportunity and also hear him on the proposed punishment. Therefore, matter adjourned to 10-07-2017 for hearing respondent no. 3 in person on matters in issue including one regarding the proposed punishment to be awarded to him for contempt of Court. Order accordingly.
