Allahabad Bank and others v Krishna Narayan Tewari

Supreme Court of India

02 January 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, A.M. Khanwilkar

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 1; (2017) 2 SCC 308; AIR 2017 SC 330; 2017 (1) AWC 437; 2017 (1) CGLJ 292; 2017 (152) FLR 600; JT 2017 (1) SC 51; 2017(1) SCALE 89; 2017 (1) UPLBEC 3

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Evidence, Appellate Authority, Enquiry Officer, Salary, Date, Appeal, Remand, Writ Petition

Summary: Service - Removal from Service - Order Challenged - Respondent/employee rendered service with appellant/bank and was charged for misconduct - Disciplinary Authority on basis of enquiry report imposed punishment of removal from service - Appellate Authority dismissed appeal and HC in appeal set aside orders passé by Lower Authorities and directed bank to provide all service/retiral benefits - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, in a case where the Disciplinary Authority records a finding that is unsupported by any evidence whatsoever or a finding which no reasonable person could have arrived at, the writ court would be justified if not duty bound to examine the matter and grant relief in appropriate cases. In such situation the proper course always is to remand the matter back to the concerned authority to redo the same afresh. However, there may be situations where because of a long time lag or such other supervening circumstances the writ court considers it unfair, harsh or otherwise unnecessary to direct a fresh enquiry or fresh order by the competent authority. That is precisely what the HC has done in instant case. Further, in absences of clarity with regard to release of service, benefit, HC direction for release of service benefits would include the release of his salaries also for the period between the date of removal and the date of superannuation. Hence, orders passed by lower authorities are set aside and order of HC is modified to extent that respondent entitled to 50% of the salary for period between the date of his removal from service till the date of superannuation. Appeal partly allowed.
