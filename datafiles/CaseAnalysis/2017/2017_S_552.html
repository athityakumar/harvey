State of Telangana v Habib Abdullah Jeelani and others

Supreme Court of India

06 January 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, Amitava Roy

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 29; (2017) 2 SCC 779; AIR 2017 SC 373; 2017 CRLJ 1702; 2017 (1) ILR(Cut) 174; JT 2017 (1) SC 156; 2017 (1) MLJ(Crl) 375; 2017 (1) RCR(Criminal) 537; 2017(1) SCALE 412

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Anticipatory Bail, FIR, First Information Report, Jurisdiction, Inherent Power, Inherent Jurisdiction, CONSTITUTION, Quash, Diary, Cognizable, Informant, Dealing, Interfere, Registered, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Constitution of India, 1950, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, Inquiry

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 154, 154(1), 438, 482, 491 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 147, 148, 149, 307 - FIR and investigation - Quash - Justifiability - FIR was lodged against accused/respondents for the offences punishable u/ss. 147, 148, 149 and 307 IPC - Respondents filed writ petitions before HC seeking for quashing of FIR and consequential investigation - Single Judge allowed petition and directed direct police not to arrest accused during the pendency of investigation - Hence, instant appeal - Whether HC while refusing to exercise inherent powers u/s. 482 CrPC to interfere in an application for quashment of investigation could restrain the investigating agency not to arrest accused persons during the course of investigation.

Held, in instant case, HC has not referred to allegations made in FIR or what has come out in the investigation. It has noted and correctly that the investigation was in progress and it was not appropriate to stay the investigation of the case. It has disposed of the application u/s. 482 CrPC and while doing that it has directed that investigating agency should not arrest accused persons. Such direction "amounts" to an order u/s. 438 CrPC, albeit without satisfaction of the conditions of the said provision which was legally unacceptable. Court if it thinks fit, regard being had to the parameters of quashing and self-restraint imposed by law, has the jurisdiction to quash the investigation and might pass appropriate interim orders as thought apposite in law, but it was absolutely inconceivable and unthinkable to pass an order of instant nature while declining to interfere or expressing opinion that it was not appropriate to stay the investigation. Such kind of order was inappropriate and unseemly and has no sanction in law. Courts should oust and obstruct unscrupulous litigants from invoking the inherent jurisdiction of Court on the drop of a hat to file an application for quashing of launching an FIR or investigation and then seek relief by an interim order. It was the obligation of Court to keep such unprincipled and unethical litigants at bay. Further, the types of orders like the instant one were totally unsustainable, for it was contrary to the aforesaid settled principles and judicial precedents. It was intellectual truancy to avoid the precedents and issue directions which were not in consonance with law. It was the duty of a Judge to sustain the judicial balance and not to think of an order which could cause trauma to the process of adjudication. Impugned order of HC was set aside. Appeal allowed.
