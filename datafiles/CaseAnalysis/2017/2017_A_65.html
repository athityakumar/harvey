Alka Chandewar v Shamshul Ishrar Khan

Supreme Court of India

06 July 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Rohinton Fali Nariman, Sanjay Kishan Kaul

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 480

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Keywords: Arbitral Tribunal, Arbitration And Conciliation Act, 1996, Contempt of Courts Act, 1971, Code of Civil Procedure, 1908

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, ss. 17,27(5) - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O.39 r.2A - Contempt of interim orders of Arbitral Tribunal - Sole Arbitrator passed interim order dt.7-10-2010 u/s.17 of the Act with directions that no further flats were to be disposed of without the leave of Arbitral Tribunal - However, alleging that respondent transferred five such flats, Arbitration passed order on 22-3-2012 inter alia that order of 7-10-2010 had been breached by respondent and later, by order dt. 5-5-2014, Arbitrator referred contempt of order to HC to pass necessary orders thereon u/s.27(5) of the Act - However, HC held that s.27(5) of the Act does not empower Arbitral Tribunal to make representation to the Court for contempt except in respect of taking evidence and also that petition is beyond period of limitation and so, not maintainable in law and also since final award of Rs.8 Crore is granted in favour of petitioner by Arbitrator, declined petition - Hence, instant appeal - Whether impugned orders of HC are sustainable.

Held, s. 27(5) is not confined to a person being guilty of contempt only when failing to attend in accordance with such process and the said section specifically states that persons guilty of any contempt to the Arbitral Tribunal during the conduct of the Arbitral proceedings is within its ken. Further, in consonance with the modern rule of interpretation of statutes, the entire object of providing that a party could approach the Arbitral Tribunal instead of Court for interim reliefs would be stultified if interim orders passed by such Tribunal are toothless and it to give teeth to such orders that an express provision is made in s.27(5) of the Act. Hence, judgment of HC is set aside and matter is remanded to HC to decide the alleged contempt on facts. Matter remanded to HC. Order accordingly.
