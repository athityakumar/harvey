Special Land Acquisition Officer, KIADB, Mysore and another v Anasuya Bai and others

Supreme Court of India

25 January 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, R.K. Agrawal

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 65; (2017) 3 SCC 313; AIR 2017 SC 904; 2017(4) ALL MR 915; 2017 (1) CLT(SC) 255; JT 2017 (2) SC 76; 2017 (2) MLJ 587; 2017 (1) RCR(Civil) 972; 2017(2) SCALE 41

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property

Keywords: Acquisition Of Land, Land Acquired, Karnataka Industrial Areas Development Act, 1966, Karnataka Land Acquisition Act, Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, Land Acquisition Act, 1894, Right to Fair Compensation and Transparency in Land Acquisition, Rehabilitation and Resettlement Act, 2013, Karnataka Industrial Areas Development Act, 1996, Land Acquisition Rules, 1965

Summary: Land & Property - Right to Fair Compensation and Transparency in Land Acquisition, Rehabilitation and Resettlement Act, 2013, s. 24(1) - Respondents were owner of land in dispute - Appellants issued notification dt. 15-9-2000 to acquire lands in dispute - Advisory committee fixed Compensation after agreement with land owners of which Respondent was not party - Before compensation could be disbursed to Respondents, disputes among family members of Respondents about proportion of compensation - Appellants deposited entire compensation payable to Respondents before Court - Petition filed by Respondents for quashing of notification dt. 15-9-2000 - Single Judge allowed petition and permitted Appellants to fix market value as on date of final notification - On appeal, Division Bench held that since there was no consent on fixation of compensation given by Respondents, case would be governed by s. 24(1) of Act and that since no award was passed after final declaration on 15-6-2005 acquisition proceedings were lapsed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether, fixation of compensation at rate of Rs. 6,50,000 per acre by Advisory Committee is with consent of Respondents. Held, respondents chose to file petition for quashing of acquisition proceedings coming out with plea that they were not consenting parties and had not participated in meeting as even notice was not received by them. Entire move on part of Appellants was bonafide though there was accidental slip on their part that insofar as Respondents are concerned, no consent to amount of compensation fixed was given by them. Appellants-authorities did not proceed further to determine compensation in respect of respondents' land as they nurtured bonafide belief that with fixation of compensation as per Minutes all land owners, including Respondents, had agreed with same. Had Appellants been more careful, they would have noticed that insofar as Respondents are concerned, they are not consenting parties. Single Judge vide his judgment permitted Appellants to proceed on basis of Gazette notification dt. 15-6-2005 acquiring land and determine compensation by making award in this behalf. Single Judge, by adopting this course of action, specifically rejected contention of Respondents to quash proceedings. Division Bench of by impugned judgment, however, has quashed acquisition proceedings itself holding that they have lapsed. View taken by Single Judge was correct in law which should not have been interfered with by Division Bench in impugned judgment. Appeal allowed.
