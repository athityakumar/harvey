National Insurance Company Limited v Hindustan Safety Glass Works Limited and another

Supreme Court of India

07 April 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Madan B. Lokur, Prafulla C. Pant

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 264; (2017) 5 SCC 776; AIR 2017 SC 1900; 2017 (4) ALD(SC) 44; 2017(3) ALL MR 451; 2017 (122) ALR 515; 2017 (3) ALT(SC) 8; 2017 (2) AWC 2115; 2017 (2) CPJ(Sc) 1; 2017 (3) CTC 826; 2017 (3) MLJ 507; 2017 (2) RCR(Civil) 750; 2017 (2) RLW 1668; 2017(4) SCALE 505

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection

Keywords: Consumer, Consumer Protection Act, 1986, National Commission, Surveyor

Summary: Civil Appeal No. 3883 of 2007

(A) Consumer Protection - Consumer Protection Act, 1986, s. 24A - Goods insured - Claimed - Applicability - Respondents/insured filed a complaint with National Commission under the Act claiming an amount of Rs. 52.32 lakhs along with an amount of about Rs.1.81 lakhs being the expenses incurred for the purpose of loss minimization - National Commission by judgment and order awarded insured an amount of Rs. 21,05,803.89 with interest at 9% p.a. and costs of Rs. 20,000/- were also awarded to insured - Hence, instant appeals - Whether claim of respondent for goods insured was rightly accepted by National Commission.

Held, in a dispute concerning a consumer, it was necessary for Courts to take a pragmatic view of the rights of consumer principally since it was consumer who is placed at a disadvantage vis-a-vis the supplier of services or goods. The provision of limitation in the Act cannot be strictly construed to disadvantage a consumer in a case where a supplier of goods or services itself was instrumental in causing a delay in settlement of consumer's claim. Therefore, National Commission was right in rejecting the contention of appellants on that regard. Appellants failed to provide any reason before National Commission or even before SC to remotely suggest that the second report was also tainted either because officer consulted was not authorised to give a report or for any other justifiable reason. National Commission accepted second survey report as well as the addendum to it and SC do not see any reason to disagree with the findings arrived at in the absence of any material to discredit surveyor or report of surveyor. No case was made out by National Insurance to interfere with the order passed by National Commission. Appeals dismissed.

CIVIL APPEAL NO. 1156 OF 2008

(B) Consumer Protection - Consumer Protection Act, 1986, s. 24A - Insured machine - Short circuit - Damaged - Whether the loss or damage to insured machine was caused by an explosion or by a short circuit.

Held, National Commission held that on a consideration of evidence that an explosion had occurred in the machine and that resulted in a short circuit and consequent loss or damage to the machine. Thus, view taken by National Commission was based on the evidence on record and was a possible view. In the absence of any material error in appreciation of evidence, SC do not think it proper to substitute the view taken by National Commission. Appeals dismissed.
