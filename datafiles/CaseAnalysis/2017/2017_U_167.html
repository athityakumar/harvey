Mangalam Organics Limited v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

24 April 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, Ashok Bhushan

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 298; (2017) 7 SCC 221; AIR 2017 SC 2406; 2017 (349) E.L.T. 369; JT 2017 (5) SC 583; 2017 (3) MLJ 882; 2017(5) SCALE 200

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Excise Duty, Duty Of Excise, Central Excise Act, 1944, Central Excises And Salt Act, 1944, Manufacturing Process, Central Excise Rules, 1944, Securitisation And Reconstruction Of Financial Assets And Enforcement Of Security Interest Act, 2002, Constitution of India, 1950

Summary: (A) Excise - Indirect Tax - Central Excise Act, 1944, s. 11C - Assessment - Excise duty levied - Validity - Appellant was in the business of manufacturing Rosin and Turpentine - Show cause notice was issued to appellant by Excise Dept. demanding duty of Rs.10,91,99,456/- - Appellant challenged order of assessment before Tribunal - Tribunal dismissed appeal however, restricted Dept. to recover the dues falling within the period of limitation - Appellant filed writ petition before HC seeking HC to direct Central Govt. to issue a notification u/s. 11C of the Act to the effect that duty payable by appellant on goods manufactured by it should not be paid which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether those conditions were satisfied in instant case.

Held, there was no clinching evidence to suggest the existence of a general practice not to levy excise duty. Under the impression that it was to be demanded from registered units and five such registered units were in fact paying the duty, show cause notices were issued to the remaining two units. That itself negates the contention of existence of general practice of not levying duty of excise. It was stated at the cost of repetition that merely because some unregistered firms which were initially getting SSI exemption, but omitted to be covered under the Act on their crossing SSI limits would not establish any such practice. As far as Dept. was concerned, it had taken a categorical stand that even those units which were using Bhatti method for manufacture of Turpentine and Rosin were covered by the Act and that was the reason for issuing of show cause notices to two units. Such view, which Dept. had nurtured while issuing notices was vindicated in view of the judgment of SC in Commissioner of Central Excise, Nagpur v. Gurukripa Resins Private Limited, 2004 Indlaw CESTAT 2240. However, after the said judgment, even appellant paid the duty of excise. The entire effort was to recover back the said duty by seeking issuance of a notification u/s. 11C of the Act which cannot be countenanced. Appeal dismissed.

(B) Excise - Indirect Tax - Central Excise Act, 1944, s. 11C - Notification - Discretionary power - Effect of - Whether Court could issue a mandamus to Central Govt. to pass such a notification exercising its power u/s. 11C of the Act.

Held, when 'power' was given to Central Govt. to issue a notification to the effect not to recover duty of excise or recover lesser duty than what was normally payable under the Act for deciding whether to issue such a Notification or not, there might be various considerations in the mind of Govt. Merely because conditions laid in the said provisions were satisfied would not be a reason to necessarily issue such a notification which was purely a policy matter. Where the statute vests a discretionary power in an administrative authority, Court would not interfere with the exercise of such discretion unless it was made with oblique end or extraneous purposes or upon extraneous considerations, or arbitrarily, without applying its mind to the relevant considerations, or where it was not guided by any norms which were relevant to the object to be achieved. When the reasons furnished by Govt. in not exercising its power to issue notification u/s. 11C of the Act were seen in that perspective, namely, such a notification, if issued was going to benefit only two units, SC found them to be valid and justified. In instant case, appellant has already paid the duty and s. 11C of the Act contemplates those situations where duty was not paid. It does not cover the situation where duty was paid and that is to be refunded. Appeal dismissed.
