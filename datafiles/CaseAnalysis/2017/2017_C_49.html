Chandrasekar and others v State of Tamil Nadu

Supreme Court of India

22 May 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Navin Sinha, L. Nageswara Rao

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 417; AIR 2017 SC 2600; 2017 (3) MLJ(Crl) 420; 2017(6) SCALE 533

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Vehicle, Bone, Doctor, Credibility, Skull, Indian Penal Code, 1860

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 304, 307, 324, 506(ii) - Criminal assault - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellants/accused allegedly assaulted deceased on the head with a hammer, sickle and iron rod causing instant death - FIR was registered against accused u/ss. 302, 506 (ii) and 307 IPC - Trial Court convicted accused u/s. 302, 324 IPC and sentenced them to life imprisonment - On appeal, HC affirmed impugned judgment of Trial Court - Hence, instant appeals.

Held, fact that the witness might be related to deceased by marriage, cannot be sufficient reason to classify him as a related and interested witness to reject his testimony. It might call for greater scrutiny and caution in consideration of the same. The animosity of appellants was primarily with deceased on account of his acquittal the previous day, in criminal prosecution. The transfer of lands by deceased in favour of witness, being a completed transaction, was considered too remote a circumstance for enmity between appellants and witness as a ground for false implication. Trial Court has rightly believed eye-witnesses. PW4/astrologer, an independent witness, referred to by PW3 as also having been present deposed of appellants attacking deceased. The fact that PW3 in his cross-examination might have stated that he was not aware how appellant and PW1 sustained injuries cannot classify him either as a hostile or completely unreliable witness. The number of injuries caused on the head of deceased speaks for itself regarding the intention of appellants. In view of the clear ocular evidence available, issues with regard to the confession statement and recovery of weapons of assault need not be considered for corroboration. No reason to interfere with the conviction of appellants. Appeals dismissed.
