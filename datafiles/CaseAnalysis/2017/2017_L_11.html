State of Jharkhand, through S.P., CBI v Lalu Prasad @ Lalu Prasad Yadav and others

Supreme Court of India

08 May 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Arun Mishra, Amitava Roy

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 409; (2017) 8 SCC 1; AIR 2017 SC 3389; JT 2017 (5) SC 396; 2017 (3) MLJ(Crl) 18; 2017 (2) RCR(Criminal) 901; 2017(6) SCALE 21

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Breach Of Trust, Estoppel, Misjoinder, Common Object, Cheating, Dishonest, Conspiracy, Double Jeopardy, Criminal Breach Of Trust, Autrefois, CBI, Jurisdiction, Identical, Limitation Act, 1963, Misappropriation, Gold, Omission, Limitation, Insurance Act, 1938, Proof, Sufficient Cause, Abetment, Gold (Control) Act, 1968, General Clauses Act, 1897, Criminal Proceedings, Sea Customs Act, 1878, Special Judge, Reliance, Prayer, Contents, Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, Dealing, Same Offence, Same Transaction, Change, Joint Trial, Two Offences, Contradiction, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898, Distinct Offence, Doctrine, Distinct, Autrefois Acquit, Autrefois Convict, Bihar Reorganisation Act, 2000, Second Prosecution, Explosives Act, 1884, Rajasthan Sati (Prevention) Act, 1987, Scheduled Castes and the Scheduled Tribes (Prevention of Atrocities) Act, 1989, Registered, Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Constitution of India, 1950, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, Plea, Corruption, Highways Act, l959, Customs Act, 1969

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 20, 20(2) - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 11, 120B, 177, 409, 420, 465, 467, 468, 471, 477, 477A - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 120B, 13(1)(c), 13(2), 409, 5(1)(d), 5(2) - Fodder scam - Acquittal - Sustainability - Respondents/accused were prosecuted in the period 1-4-1994 to 31-1-1995 relating to Chaibasa treasury for facilitating dishonest and fraudulent withdrawal of Govt. funds - Charge sheet was filed against accused for offence punishable u/s. 120B r/w ss. 409, 420, 467, 468, 471, 477, 477A IPC and s. 13(1)(c) r/w s. 13(2) of the Act - HC acquitted accused from the offences charged against them - Hence, instant appeals - Whether in view of art. 20(2) of Constitution and s. 300 CrPC was it a case of prosecution and punishment for the "same offence" more than once.

Held, separate trials which are being made in accordance with provisions of law otherwise it would have prejudiced accused persons considering the different defalcations from different treasuries at different times with different documents. Each defalcation would constitute an independent offence. It could be held to be in violation of art. 20(2) of Constitution or s. 300 CrPC. Separate trials in such cases is the very intendment of law. Though evidence of general conspiracy was adduced in cases which were concluded, it might be common to all the cases but at the same time offences are different at different places, by different accused persons. Thus, it cannot be said to be a case of double jeopardy that for the same offence accused persons were being tried again. In instant case, SC was surprised at the conduct of CBI in such important matters how such delay could take place. CBI ought to have been careful in filing Special Leave Petitions within limitation considering the factual matrix of case. The criticism made by counsel for respondent was not wholly unjustified. CBI ought to be guided by its Manual. It was expected of it to be more vigilant. It has failed to live up to its reputation. If CBI fails to act timely, peoples' faith would be shaken in its effectiveness. Let the Director of CBI look into the matter and saddle the responsibility on a concerned person. In important cases Director, CBI should devise methodology which should not be cumbersome as reflected in those cases, otherwise in future, Director, CBI cannot escape the responsibility for delay in such cases to be termed as deliberate one, which was intolerable. Being the head of the institution it was the responsibility of Director, CBI to ensure that appeals were filed within limitation. There should not have been delay in filing special leave petitions. Impugned judgments and orders passed by HC was set aside and direct Trial Court, concerned to expedite the trial and to conclude the same within 9 months. Appeals allowed.
