State of Maharashtra v Nisar Ramzan Sayyed

Supreme Court of India

07 April 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Pinaki Chandra Ghose, Rohinton Fali Nariman

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 267; (2017) 5 SCC 673; AIR 2017 SC 2363; 2017 ALL MR (Cri) 2212; 2017 (2) CGLJ 355; 2017 (2) JCC 1364; 2017 (2) RCR(Criminal) 564; 2017(4) SCALE 331

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Dying Declaration, Kerosene, Spot, Death Sentence, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973

Summary: (A) Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 174, 313 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 498A - Murder - Acquittal - Sustainability - Victim/deceased was married to respondent/accused - Accused allegedly poured kerosene oil on deceased and threw their son on burning body of deceased - Trial Court convicted respondent for offence punishable u/ss. 302 and 498A IPC and imposed him to suffer death sentence - On appeal, HC set aside conviction of respondent and acquitted him - Hence, instant appeals - Whether there was any perversity or illegality in impugned judgment of HC.

Held, SC found the dying declarations of deceased with consistent allegations about demand of dowry and modus operandi of offence which resulted into the death of declarant and her minor child. It was evident that each of the circumstances was established the cumulative effect whereof would show that all the links in the chain were complete and conclusion of guilt was fully established. Therefore, respondent was guilty of the offence causing death of his pregnant wife and minor child. HC erred in acquitting respondent as the spot Panchnama, was duly proved by PW11/IO of the case whereby recovery of kerosene mixed soil, burnt pieces of Saree and Blouse etc. was proved. Appeals allowed.

(B) Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 174, 313 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 498A - Death penalty - Justifiability - Whether in a case of such nature death sentence should be awarded.

Held, a life was at stake subject to human error and discrepancies and doctrine of 'rarest of rare cases', which was not res-integra in awarding death penalty should be applied while considering quantum of sentence in instant case. SC noticed that Law Commission of India has recommended abolition of death penalty for all the crimes other than terrorism related offences and waging war (offences affecting National Security). Today when capital punishment has become a distinctive feature of death penalty apparatus in India which somehow breaches the reformative theory of punishment under criminal law, SC was not inclined to award the same in the peculiar facts and circumstances of instant case. Therefore, confinement till natural life of accused should fulfill the requisite criteria of punishment. Thus, judgment and order passed by HC was set aside and that of Trial Court was restored with regard to conviction of accused respondent. However, death sentence awarded by Trial Court was modified to 'life imprisonment'. Appeals allowed.
