Ravada Sasikala v State of Andhra Pradesh and another

Supreme Court of India

27 February 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, R. Banumathi

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 144; AIR 2017 SC 1166; JT 2017 (3) SC 8; 2017(3) SCALE 179

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Keywords: Hindu Marriage Act, 1955, Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 235(2), 357A, 377(1) -

Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 304, 307, 326, 34, 448 - Acid attack - Conviction - Sustainability - FIR u/ss. 448 and 307 IPC was registered against accused/respondent no. 2 - Trial Court did not find accused guilty u/s. 307 IPC but held him guilty u/ss. 326 and 448 IPC and sentenced him to suffer RI for 1 year - On appeal, Single Judge modified the sentence of imprisonment imposed by Trial Court for the offence u/s. 326 IPC to the period which accused has already undergone while maintaining sentence of fine for both the offences - Hence, instant appeals - Whether imposition of sentence by Single Judge was proportionate to the crime in question.

Held, instant case was an example of uncivilized and heartless crime committed by respondent no. 2 which was completely unacceptable that concept of leniency could be conceived of in such a crime. Respondent no. 2 might not have suffered emotional distress by the denial, yet the said feeling could not to be converted into vengeance to have the licence to act in a manner like he had done. Further, when there was medical evidence that there was an acid attack on the young girl and circumstances having brought home by cogent evidence and conviction was given the stamp of approval, there was no justification to reduce the sentence to the period already undergone. SC was at a loss to understand whether Single Judge was guided by some unknown notion of mercy or remaining oblivious of the precedents relating to sentence or for that matter, not careful about the expectation of collective from Court, for the society at large eagerly waits for justice to be done in accordance with law, has reduced the sentence. When a substantive sentence of 30 days was imposed, in the crime of instant nature, i.e. acid attack on a young girl, the sense of justice, if allowed was not only ostracized, but also was unceremoniously sent to "VAnaprastha" which was impermissible. Impugned sentence imposed by HC was set aside and restore that of Trial Court. SC direct accused to pay a compensation of Rs.50,000/- and State to pay a compensation of Rs.3 lakhs. Appeals allowed.
