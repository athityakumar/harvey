In Re : Inhuman Conditions In 1382 Prisons

Supreme Court of India

15 September 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Madan B. Lokur, Deepak Gupta

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 750; JT 2017 (9) SC 114

Case Digest

Subject: Human Rights; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Fundamental Right, Suicide, Custodial Death, Rape, Jurisdiction, Forum, Communication, Treatment, Cause Of Death, Police Custody, Attorney, Jail, Legal Aid, Violence, Explain, Prisoner, Autopsy, Disappearance, Visitors, Juvenile Justice (Care And Protection Of Children) Act, 2000, Communicate, Stress, Jammu and Kashmir Public Safety Act, 1978, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Constitution of India, 1950, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, Inquiry, Criminal, National Legal Services Authority (Free and Competent Legal Services) Regulations, 2010, Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act, 2015, National Legal Services Authority (Legal Aid Clinics) Regulations, 2011, Standard Minimum Rules, Nelson Mandela Rules

Summary: Human Rights - Practice & Procedure - Custodial deaths - Direction issued - Petitioners filed instant writ petition seeking that National Crime Records Bureau (NCRB) should be directed to explain the difference not only between a natural death and an unnatural death but also to clarify the sub-categorization of 'others' unnatural deaths.

Held, SC requested Chief Justice of HC to register a suo motu PIL with a view to identify the next of kin of prisoners who have admittedly died an unnatural death as revealed by NCRB during the period between 2012 and 2015 and award suitable compensation, unless adequate compensation has already been awarded. UOI through Ministry of Home Affairs would ensure circulation within 1 month. All efforts should be made, as suggested by National Human Rights Commission (NHRC) and others to reduce and possibly eliminate unnatural deaths in prisons and to document each and every death in prisons both natural and unnatural. UOI would direct NCRB to explain and clarify the distinction between unnatural and natural deaths in prisons as indicated on the website of NCRB and in its Annual Reports and also explain the sub-categorization 'others' within the category of unnatural deaths. NCRB should also be required to sub- categorize natural deaths. The necessity of having counselors and support persons in prisons cannot be over-emphasized. Their services could be utilized to counsel and advice prisoners who might be facing some crisis situation or might have some violent or suicidal tendencies. State Govts. were directed to appoint counselors and support persons for counselling prisoners, particularly first-time offenders. While visits to prison by the family of a prisoner should be encouraged, it would be worthwhile to consider extending the time or frequency of meetings and also explore the possibility of using phones and video conferencing for communications not only between a prisoner and family members of that prisoner, but also between a prisoner and the lawyer, whether appointed through the State Legal Services Authority or otherwise. Some proactive steps are required to be taken by eminent members of society who should be included in the Board of Visitors. SC expect the above directions to be faithfully implemented by UOI and State Govts. Order accordingly.
