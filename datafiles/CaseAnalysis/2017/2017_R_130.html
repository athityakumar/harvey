Reliance Telecom Limited and another v Union of India and another

Supreme Court of India

12 January 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, Prafulla C. Pant

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 35; AIR 2017 SC 337; 2017 (1) CLT(SC) 87; JT 2017 (1) SC 603; 2017(1) SCALE 453

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Telecom

Keywords: Dot, Contract, Indian Telegraph Act, 1885, Review, Cellular, Service Provider, Telecommunication, Telecom Regulatory Authority of India Act, 1997, Telecom Regulatory Authority Of India, Licensor, Licence To a Service Provider, Constitution of India, 1950

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Telecom - Telecom Regulatory Authority of India Act, 1997, ss. 11, 11(1)(a), 11(1)(b), 11(1)(c) , 11(4), 18 - Auction of Spectrum - Capping for Allocation Terms Challenged - Respondent/union govt. issued notice invited application(NIA) with certain terms and conditions for allocation of spectrum in various areas with intent to discourage hoarding of spectrum, to encourage level playing fields and advance health competition - Appellants/company dissatisfied with terms filed writ petition for extension of permission to participants in NIA for bid for minimum MHz band - HC granted interim prayer and transferred cases to apex court - SC in SLP by respondent modify HC order, thereby granted liberty to finalize auction - Hence, instant transfer petition.

Held, it is well settled that award of a contract is essentially a commercial transaction which must be determined on the basis of considerations that are relevant to such commercial decision and, hence, the terms subject to which tenders are invited are not open to judicial scrutiny unless it is found that the same have been tailor-made to benefit any particular tenderer or class of tenderers. Further, if the State acts within the bounds of reasonableness, it would be legitimate to take into consideration the national priorities and adopt trade policies and the ultimate test is whether, on the touchstone of reasonableness, the policy decision comes out unscathed. The reasonableness of restriction is to be determined in an objective manner and from the standpoint of the interests of the general public and not from the standpoint of the interests of the persons upon whom the restrictions have been imposed or upon abstract consideration. However, judicial review is intended to prevent arbitrariness or favouritism and it is exercised in the larger public interest or if it is brought to the notice of the court that in the matter of award of a contract power has been exercised for any collateral purpose. In instant case, when auction is held in respect of spectrum after taking into consideration certain range of facts and circumstances which are founded on economic and social policy factors, it is difficult to unsettle the NIA and the consequential effect thereof by applying the principle of judicial review. The procedure adopted in this kind of auction is neither to be equated nor compared with the process meant for grant of ordinary largesse. It is because of its complexity, technical expertise, enormous financial impact and the larger public interest. Hence, transfer cases do not perceive any merit and the same are dismissed. Order accordingly.
