State of Himachal Pradesh v Nirmala Devi

Supreme Court of India

10 April 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, Ashok Bhushan

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 473; (2017) 7 SCC 262; 2017 CRLJ 2886; JT 2017 (4) SC 454; 2017 (3) MLJ(Crl) 70; 2017 (2) RCR(Criminal) 613

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Life Imprisonment, Drug, Description, Drugs and Cosmetics Act, 1940, Settlement, Justification, Mercy, Alternative, Robbery, Special Reasons, Rigorous Imprisonment, Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Constitution of India, 1950, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, Complainant

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 328, 307, 392, 34 - Modification of sentence - Substitution of imprisonment with fine - Respondent was convicted by Trial Court for offences punishable u/ss. 328, 307, 392 read with s.34 of IPC and imposed sentence of imprisonment and fine - However, on appeal, HC though affirmed conviction, had modified sentence by removing imprisonment and substituting the same with fine of Rs. 30,000 - Hence, instant appeal by State assailing impugned order of HC whereby imprisonment was substituted with fine of Rs. 30,000 - Whether HC is justified in modifying sentence awarded to respondent by Trial Court.

Held, there has to be punishment of imprisonment in respect of offences respondent was convicted of and in addition, is also liable to pay fine. Further, awarding punishment of imprisonment is a must and there could not be a situation where no imprisonment is imposed at all. Hence, HC was wrong in not inflicting a sentence of imprisonment, more so, by modifying the sentence awarded by Trial Court and obliterating the sentence of imprisonment altogether. Further, offences for which respondent is convicted prescribe maximum imprisonment and there is no provision for minimum imprisonment. Thus, there is wide discretion given to Court to impose any imprisonment which could vary from one day to ten years/life. Further, two mitigating circumstances sought by respondent viz., that she is a woman and with three minor children has to be balanced with the nature of crime which the respondent has committed. Trial Court has considered said circumstances and on that basis, took lenient view by awarding imprisonment for two years in respect of each of the offences u/ss. 307,328 and 392 of IPC, which were to be run concurrently. As such, there was no reason to show any further mercy by HC and removing the element of imprisonment altogether was in any case, erroneous in law. Hence, sentence part of judgment of HC is set aside and that of Trial Court is restored. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - In cases where after specifying particular term of imprisonment, Sections use the words 'and shall also be liable to fine', then it is imperative to impose both the sentences i.e., imprisonment as well as fine.
