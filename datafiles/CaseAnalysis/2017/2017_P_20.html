Padmini Mahendrabhai Gadda v State of Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

17 July 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

N.V. Ramana, Prafulla C. Pant

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 478

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Circumstantial Evidence, Conspiracy, Jurisdiction, Description, Proof, Enhancement, Blood, Dead Body, Fear, Disappearance, Screening, Bombay Police Act, 1951, Silence, Rigorous Imprisonment, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Husband, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, Complainant, Suspicion

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302,120B,201 - Proving of case beyond reasonable doubt - Upon complaint made by complainant about death of deceased, case was registered u/s. 302 read with ss. 120B and 201 of IPC and charge sheet was filed against accused persons - Trial Court found accused guilty and as such convicted and imposed sentence on accused nos. 1 and 2 - Aggrieved, accused filed appeals before HC but upon considering facts and circumstances of the case, HC initiated suo motu proceedings for enhancement of sentence in respect of accused no.2-appellant - Further, HC rejected appeals of accused and also passed order enhancing sentence imposed by Trial Court in respect of appellant - Hence, instant appeal by appellant - Whether impugned orders of HC are sustainable.

Held, as per opinion of Judge N.V.Ramana, both Trial Court and HC failed to appreciate case in its proper perspective, relied more on assumptions and based on presumptions convicted appellant, contrary to settled law and that prosecution was unable to prove the guilt of appellant for offence punishable u/s.201 of IPC beyond reasonable doubt. As such, impugned orders of HC are liable to be set aside. However, Judge Prafulla C. Pant was of opinion that there was no error of law in orders passed by Trial Court and HC in concluding that appellant is guilty of charge of offence punishable u/s. 201 of IPC. Hence, in view of disagreement between Judges, directions are issued to Registry to place matter before CJI to constitute appropriate bench for disposal of the matter. Reference to larger bench. Order accordingly.
