Punjab State Civil Supplies Corporation Limited and another v Atwal Rice and General Mills, represented by its Partners

Supreme Court of India

11 July 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Abhay Manohar Sapre, R. Banumathi

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 481; (2017) 8 SCC 116; 2017 (5) ALD(SC) 134; 2017 (6) MLJ 326; 2017(7) SCALE 691

Case Digest

Subject: Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Arbitration And Conciliation Act, 1996, Code of Civil Procedure, 1908

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Practice & Procedure - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, ss. 34,35,36 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O.21 rr.1,2 - Execution of Award - Objections to Award - Appellant and respondent no.1 entered into agreement and later, alleging that respondents committed breach of agreement, appellant raised claim against respondents - Matter was referred to Sole Arbitrator and upon hearing parties and perusing evidence adduced, arbitrator passed reasoned award allowing claim of appellant in part - Aggrieved, respondent filed petition u/s. 34 of the Act before Trial Court but the same was dismissed - Later, appellants filed execution petition for enforcement of the award but by order dt.3-11-2012, Executing Court upheld objections raised by respondents and dismissed execution petition by holding that respondents having paid Rs.3,37,885 towards decreetal amount to appellant, award in question stood satisfied fully - Aggrieved, appellant filed revision before HC but the same was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether impugned orders are sustainable.

Held, objections that were raised before Executing Court were not capable of being tried in executing proceedings to challenge the award as they were on facts and pertained to the merits of the controversy, which stood decided by Arbitrator resulting in passing of an award. Further, none of the objections were in relation to the jurisdiction of the Court affecting the root of the very passing of the decree. Furthermore, sum of Rs.3,37,885 which respondents claimed to have paid to appellant towards decreetal sum and which found acceptance to the Executing Court and HC could never have been regarded as the payment made by respondents to the appellant in conformity with the requirements of either O.21 r.1 or O.21 r.2. Further, such payment was not certified by the Court as contemplated under O.21 r.2 at the instance of respondents or at the instance of the appellant. Hence, execution application filed by appellant for execution of the award dt.1-6-2001 is maintainable. Further, no amount is paid by respondents to appellant pursuant to the award so as to enable the Executing Court to record its full satisfaction in accordance with the provisions of O.21 rr.1 ,2 and all objections raised by respondents u/s.47 of CPC against the award are rejected as being wholly devoid of any merits. Appeal allowed with costs and impugned order of Executing Court is set aside and Executing Court it to issue warrant for recovery of entire awarded decreetal amount against respondents. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Executing Court has to execute the decree as it is and it could not go behind the decree and could not hold any kind of factual inquiry which would have the effect of nullifying the decree itself but it could undertake limited inquiry regarding jurisdictional issues which goes to the root of the decree and has the effect of rendering the decree nullity.
