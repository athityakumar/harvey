B. S. Mahabala v Gopala Krishna and another

Supreme Court of India

20 April 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Khanwilkar, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 460; AIR 2017 SC 3046; 2017(4) ALL MR 977; 2017(5) SCALE 145

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Jurisdiction, Karnataka Land Reforms Act, 1961, Occupancy Rights

Summary: Land & Property - Karnataka Land Reforms Act, 1961, s. 21 - Bequeath of occupancy rights - Channappa Poojary-applicant filed application in Form No.7 before Tribunal for grant of occupancy rights in respect of petition lands - However, pending application, applicant died and appellant appeared in said proceedings claiming that applicant during his life time, executed a Will in his favour bequeathing rights in said lands to him - Tribunal accepted claim of appellant and vide order dt.4-10-1980 granted occupancy rights to appellant in respect of said lands and during that time, respondent no.1, who was son of applicant was minor - While so, after attaining majority, respondent no.1 filed writ petition before HC asserting that he had no knowledge about passing of order by Tribunal in favour of appellant - Single Judge of HC observed that the fact as to whether Will executed in favour of appellant was hit by s. 21 or otherwise was a matter to be considered by Tribunal and also that Tribunal could not have finally decided the proceedings without bringing legal representatives of applicant on record and accordingly, set aside order of Tribunal and remanded matter to Tribunal for fresh disposal - Aggrieved, appellant filed appeal but DB of HC upheld the orders of Single Judge and declined to interfere with remand order - Hence, instant appeal by appellant - Whether impugned remand orders of HC are sustainable.

Held, Single Judge has only remanded the matter to Tribunal for fresh consideration of all aspects after giving opportunity to legal representatives of the applicant. Appellant would get an opportunity to raise all contentions that would be available to him in law for grant of occupancy rights in respect of the said lands in his favour on the basis of the Will executed by applicant. Whether that Will is admissible in evidence or is a genuine Will is a matter required to be looked into by Tribunal. Further, Tribunal has to address itself as to whether any transfer by way of a Will in favour of person(s) other than spouse, minor sons, unmarried daughters, is permissible in law keeping in mind the rigors of s.21 read with s.2(12) and other applicable provisions of the Act. Hence, as order of Tribunal is vitiated due to failure to bring on record the legal representatives of applicant, and also as the same was passed without considering the mandate of s.21 of the Act, impugned orders of HC setting aside of orders of Tribunal and remanding matter again to Tribunal could not be interfered with. Appeal dismissed.
