Himanshu Mohan Rai v State of U. P. and another

Supreme Court of India

07 March 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

S.A. Bobde, L. Nageswara Rao

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 216; (2017) 4 SCC 161; AIR 2017 SC 1425; 2017 ALL MR (Cri) 1753; 2017 (2) CGLJ 122; 2017 CRLJ 2250; JT 2017 (3) SC 137; 2017 (2) MLJ(Crl) 220; 2017 (2) RCR(Criminal) 972; 2017(3) SCALE 400

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Weapon, Juvenile, Ballistics, FIR, Expert, Blood, Ocular Evidence, Innocent, Ballistic Expert, Handwriting, Quarrel, Contradiction, Juvenile Justice Act, 1986, Cartridges, Registered, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Investigating Officer

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 34 - Juvenile Justice Act, 1986 - Delay in recording of statement - Corroboration of testimony - FIR was lodged by complainant in relation to murder of deceased allegedly caused by accused and his accomplices - Upon conclusion of trial, Trial Court convicted three accused persons for offence u/s.302 of IPC and as other two persons were found to be juvenile, they were prosecuted under the Act - However, on appeal, HC set aside conviction recorded by Trial Court on grounds inter alia, that PW1 in not a reliable witness, FIR was ante-timed - Hence, instant appeals by PW1 and State against acquittal of accused-respondent no.2 - Whether acquittal of accused-respondent no.2 by HC could be upheld.

Held, the testimony of PW1 has not been shaken in any material, particularly in the cross examination and there was nothing incredible about the testimony of PW1 and there is no reason to discard it. Further, the testimony of PW2 completely corroborates the version of PW1 in all material details of the incident and so, testimony of PW2 could not be rejected on ground that his statement was recorded after 30 days more so when there was change of investigating officers. The testimony of PW2 is credible and it proves that the accused shot the deceased as alleged by prosecution. Furthermore, it could not be inferred from any of the circumstances that FIR was ante-timed. Nor is it possible to disbelieve the timing of FIR because the police constable went to the scene of crime and seized a shirt before the registration of FIR and in fact, PW6-police inspector stated in his evidence that he seized the shirt 'around 10 in the night' and that he does not remember the exact time. Further, in cases where the evidence of eye witness has been found to be truthful and as in instant case corroborated by fact that the bullets were recovered from the body of the deceased, it could not have the effect of an acquittal. Hence, accused-respondent no.2 is convicted u/s.302 of IPC and appeals are allowed and judgment of HC is set aside. Appeals disposed of.
