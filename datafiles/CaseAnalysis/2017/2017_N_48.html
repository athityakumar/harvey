National Insurance Company Limited v Rekhaben and another

Supreme Court of India

07 March 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

S.A. Bobde, L. Nageswara Rao

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 446; 2017 ACJ 2051; AIR 2017 SC 2580; 2017 (5) ALD(SC) 7; JT 2017 (5) SC 601; 2017 (3) RCR(Civil) 289; 2017(6) SCALE 337

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance

Keywords: Claims Tribunal, Insurance Company, Motor Accident Claims Tribunal, Assistance, Motor Vehicles Act, 1988

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Insurance - Accidental Claim - Appellant/insurance company filed instant appeals against impugned orders of HC, whereby HC enhanced sum awarded by Tribunal to claimant-A and affirmed award passed by Tribunal in favour of claimant- 'B' who were compassionate appointed - Whether income of claimants from compassionate employment was liable to be deducted from compensation amount awarded by Tribunal under Statute.

Held, in respect of an accident in which the tort feasor is found to be liable, the owner or the driver of the vehicle or the insurer, as the case may be, may alone be held responsible for the payment of such compensation since the accident has resulted in the injury or death which gives rise to the claim of the claimants. No other party is involved in it. And certainly not the employer who may offer compassionate appointment to the dependants of the injured/ deceased. In instant case, compensation is claimed from the owner of the offending vehicle who is different from the employer who has offered employment on compassionate grounds to the dependants of the deceased/injured. The source from which compensation on account of the accident is claimed and the source from which the compassionate employment is offered, are completely separate and there is no co-relation between these two sources. Since the tort feasor has not offered the compassionate appointment, an amount which a claimant earns by his labour or by offering his services, whether by reason of compassionate appointment or otherwise is not liable to be deducted from the compensation which the claimant is entitled to receive from a tort-feasor under the Act. Hence, the financial benefit of the compassionate employment is not liable to be deducted at all from the compensation amount which is liable to be paid either by the owner/the driver of the offending vehicle or the insurer. Appeal dismissed.
