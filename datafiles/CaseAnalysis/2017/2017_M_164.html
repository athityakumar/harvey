Mukhtiar Singh (Since Deceased), Through his Lrs. v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

14 July 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Amitava Roy, Arun Mishra

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 484; (2017) 8 SCC 136; AIR 2017 SC 3382; JT 2017 (7) SC 257; 2017(7) SCALE 702

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Currency Notes, Proof, Bribe, Trap, Raiding Party, Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, Complainant, gratification

Summary: Criminal - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 7, 13(2) - Illegal gratification - Original accused was convicted by Trial Court for offence u/ss. 7 and 13(2) of the Act and was imposed sentence of RI and fine - Aggrieved, original accused filed appeal before HC but during pendency of appeal, original accused died and appellant got herself substituted with a bid to purge him of the stigma - Upon hearing parties, HC affirmed conviction and sentence awarded by Trial Court and dismissed appeal - Hence, instant appeal - Whether conviction of original accused is sustainable.

Held, evidence with regard to the demand of illegal gratification either of Rs.3,000 which had been paid or of Rs.2,000 as made on the day of trap operation is wholly inadequate to comply with the pre-requisites to constitute the ingredients of the offence with which the original accused had been charged. Further, allegation of complainant with regard to demand and payment of Rs.3,000 and Rs.2,000 remained uncorroborated. As such, prosecution failed to prove the charge levelled against original accused beyond all reasonable doubt. Further, Trial Court and HC failed to analyze the factual and legal aspects as involved in their true perspectives and so, impugned judgments are not sustainable and so, same are set aside. Appeal allowed.
