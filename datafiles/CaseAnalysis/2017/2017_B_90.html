Binoy Kumar Mishra v State of Jharkhand and another

Supreme Court of India

31 March 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, R.K. Agrawal

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 260; 2017(4) SCALE 200B

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Burden Of Proof, Presumption, Omission, Agent, General Manager, Proof, Place Of Occurrence, Innocence, Mines Act, 1952, Rigorous Imprisonment, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, Inquiry, Central Mines Rules, 1957, Systematic Support Rules

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Mines Act, 1952, ss. 17, 18, 18(4), 18(5), 19, 2(c), 20, 21, 22, 23, 23(2), 24, 25, 26, 27, 57, 58, 60, 61, 63, 64, 65, 66, 67, 68, 69, 70, 71, 72, 72A, 72C(1)(a), 72C(1)(b), 73, 74, 75, 76, 77, 78, 79, 80, 81 - Central Mines Rules, 1957, rr. 108, 108(5) - Willful neglect - Conviction - Sustainability - Respondent no. 2/complainant filed complaint against appellants/accused for offences punishable u/ss. 72A, 72C(1)(a) and 72C(1)(b) of the Act - Trial Court convicted accused and sentenced them to undergo RI for 6 months for offences u/s. 72A of the Act, RI for 2 years for offence u/s. 72(C)(1)(a) of the Act and RI for 1 year for offence u/s. 72C(1)(b) of the Act with the direction that sentences would run concurrently - On appeals, Appellate Court dismissed appeals - Aggrieved, accused filed revision petitions before HC which as dismissed - Hence, instant appeals - Whether said incident had taken place and caused serious injury and bodily pain to workers of coal mine happened on willful neglect and omission of any provision of law, rules, regulation and orders of the Act for which accused were liable.

Held, specific responsibility was laid upon appellant no. 1/manager to ensure that no mine or part of a mine should be worked in contravention of Systematic Support Rules (SSR) and provisions of SSR were to be effectively complied with. It was for appellant no. 1 to show as to how he had discharged his responsibility under the aforesaid provisions and had contravened the provisions of r. 108(5) of the Rules. That material placed by prosecution was sufficient to discharge the initial burden that exists upon prosecution. Legal burden, thereby, stood shifted upon appellant no. 1 to prove his innocence. However, in his defence not even an iota of evidence was produced by him. Therefore, no fault can be found in the approach of Courts below in convicting appellant no. 1. In case of appellant no. 2/agent, initial burden stood discharged by prosecution by placing adequate material against him. However, appellant no. 2 failed to discharge his onus as per s. 18(4) of the Act. It was for him to show that he did not take part in the management, control, supervision or direction of the mine, which he failed to do. Further, with regard to other two accused report accepts that they were neither appointed as agent nor manager and were not managers of mines either and were not covered by s. 18(4) of the Act. Thus, it was not appropriate to convict those two appellants and their conviction was set aside. The only role attributed to appellant no. 1 was that he acted in violation of SSR. The fault on his part was more in the nature of negligence in performance of his duties and that he could have exercised little more diligence. Appellant nos. 1 & 2 sentence was modified to that of fine. Appeals disposed of.
