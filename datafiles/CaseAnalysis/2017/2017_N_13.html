Nagaland Public Service Commission v State of Nagaland and others

Supreme Court of India

09 March 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Kurian Joseph, R. Banumathi

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 232; 2017 (153) FLR 498; 2017(4) SCALE 275

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Qualification, Post

Summary: Service - Lecturer post - Qualification prescribed - Permissibility - Appellants/Commission filed instant appeals before SC questioning the qualification for the post of Lecturer, Chemistry in Higher Education Dept. in State.

Held, for the post of Lecturer in Chemistry in Higher Education, the prescribed qualification, being M.Sc. in subject concerned, namely, Chemistry itself appellant was not right in completing selection based on the opinion sought from expert that Biochemistry was Chemistry for all purposes. Qualification was something to be prescribed by State Govt., the appointing authority. Commission was only to go by the qualification and it cannot improve upon that. Thus, State does not have a case that appellant possesses the required qualification and rightly held. In case appellant was eligible in terms of fresh instructions issued by State Govt. and in view of mandatory reservation, it would be open to State Govt. to relax the age of appellant for the purpose of application. Appeals dismissed.
