State of Gujarat and another v I. R. C. G. and others

Supreme Court of India

29 August 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, Prafulla C. Pant

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 680; JT 2017 (8) SC 260

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Constitution

Keywords: Maintenance, Jurisdiction, Central Excise Act, 1944, Limitation, Prevention of Damage To Public Property Act, 1984, Protection Of Human Rights Act, 1993, Central Provinces and Berar Sales of Motor Spirit and Lubricants Taxation Act, 1938, Haj Committee Act, 1959, State Sales Tax Acts, Constitution of India, 1950, Income-tax Act, 1961, Practice & Procedure

Summary: Administrative - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 27 - Communal Riots - Directions - Challenged - Communal riots taken place in state during which religious places and institutions desecrated, damaged or destroyed in year 2002 - HC in writ petition by respondent/institutions issued certain directions whereby appellant/state was directed to restore the same and suitably adequate compensate trust and associations owning such religious places - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, State is obliged under the Constitution to treat persons belonging to all faiths and religions with equality. The individual has his freedom to practice the religion as he desires and it is totally immaterial from the perspective of the State. The protection of property and places of worship is an essential part of secularism. The freedom of individual in this regard has to be respected and there has to be tolerance for each other. This principle has been accepted in the constitutional scheme keeping in view the concrete sustenance of national unity and integrity. However, art. 27 of Constitution deals with freedom as to payment of taxes for promotion of any particular religion and states that 'No person shall be compelled to pay any taxes, the proceeds of which are specifically appropriated in payment of expenses for the promotion or maintenance of any particular religion or religion's denomination.' Being a secular State and there being freedom of religion guaranteed by the Constitution both to individuals and to groups, it is against the policy of the Constitution to pay out of public funds any money for the promotion or maintenance of any particular religion or religious denomination. It is only when a substantial part of the tax is utilised for any particular religion that art. 27 of Constitution would be violated. The power of the court of judicial review to grant compensation in public law is limited. In instant case, by virtue of direction, state govt. has framed scheme, and govt. has fixed maximum amount under caption of ex-gratia assistance and conferred powers on district collector to determine about the ownership or administration rights of religious places concerned. Hence, the impugned order of HC is set aside and the claimants who fulfil the conditions of the scheme shall approach the authorities therein within eight weeks and the said authorities shall determine the same within three months from the receipt of the claims. If any party is aggrieved by the denial of the benefit, he can take appropriate steps in accordance with law. Appeal disposed of.
