Chhanga @ Manoj v State of M.P.

Supreme Court of India

28 February 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Rohinton Fali Nariman, Mohan M. Shantanagoudar

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 150; AIR 2017 SC 1415; JT 2017 (3) SC 82; 2017(3) SCALE 557B

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Additional Sessions Judge, Concurrent, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Constitution of India, 1950

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 307, 34 - Attempt to murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant/accused no.4 filed instant appeal challenging impugned order of HC whereby HC upheld conviction of accused u/s. 34 r/w s. 307 IPC and sentenced to imprisonment for a period of 3 years.

Held, nature of weapon used pre-dominates. Two bombs were hurled, which were lethal weapons, from which it could safely be inferred that the intention was to cause death. Further, the words uttered by accused no. 4 that complainant ought to be killed also lend further credence to that view. The motive of accused was made out that the intention was to kill the person in the shop as he was an informer. Therefore, it was clear that accused no. 4 in coming together with the other four accused and going together with them, and in shouting the words "kill him" certainly attracted the charge u/s. 307 r/w s. 34 IPC. The concurrent Judgments of Courts below do not need to be disturbed. The crime committed was heinous in nature and SC agree with counsel for respondent/State that appellant appears to have got away lightly. SC advert to judgment in Sevaka Perumal and Anr. v. State of Tamil Nadu, 1991 Indlaw SC 683, that undue sympathy leading to imposition of inadequate sentence would do more harm to justice system and would undermine public confidence in the efficacy of law. No reason to interfere with the judgments of Courts below. Appeal dismissed.
