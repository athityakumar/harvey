Dental Council of India v Dr. Hedgewar Smruti Rugna Seva Mandal, Hingoli and others

Supreme Court of India

11 April 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, Mohan M. Shantanagoudar

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 278; AIR 2017 SC 1826; 2017(4) ALL MR 961; 2017 (3) CTC 214; JT 2017 (5) SC 450; 2017 (3) MLJ 625; 2017(4) SCALE 364

Case Digest

Subject: Education

Keywords: Medical Council Of India, Constitution of India, 1950

Summary: Education - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 136, 226 - PG course introduction - Permissibility - Respondent/college submitted its scheme on 29-07-2015 for grant of permission to start post-graduate course - Appellant/Council conducted a pre-PG assessment of college and submitted its report to Council - Executive Committee considered assessment report and observed that there were deficiencies and college was required to submit compliance - UOI after considering the recommendation of Council disapproved the scheme of college for starting MDS course for academic session 2016-2017 - Aggrieved, respondent filed writ petition before HC challenging impugned order - HC by impugned judgment stayed communication until 06-06-2016 and held that admission process undertaken by respondent was at the risk of petitioner - Hence, instant appeal - Whether there was any perversity or illegality in impugned judgment of HC.

Held, Court should not pass such interim orders in the matters of admission when the institution had not been accorded approval. There was no justification or requirement. HC might feel that while exercising power u/art. 226 of Constitution, it could pass such orders with certain qualifiers as done by impugned order, but it really does not save the situation. By virtue of interim order, Court grants approval in a way which was subject matter of final adjudication before it. The anxiety of students to get admission reigns supreme as they feel that the institution was granting admission on the basis of an order passed by HC. The institution might be directed to inform students that the matter was sub judice, but the career oriented students get into the college with the hope and aspiration that in the ultimate eventuate everything should be correct for them and they would be saved. It was the institution that had approached HC and sought a relief of instant nature. By saying that institution might give admission at its own risk invites further chaotic and unfortunate situations. Impugned order passed by HC was absolutely unsustainable. The students who have been admitted should be allowed to continue their courses, but their seats should be adjusted from the academic session 2017-2018. Respondent cannot be allowed to get a premium. Therefore, apart from the adjustment of seats for the next academic session, SC direct respondent to deposit a sum of Rs. 30 Lakhs before Registry of SC within 8 weeks and to ensure such compliance. Appeal disposed of.
