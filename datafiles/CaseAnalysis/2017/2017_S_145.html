Prateek Gupta v Shilpi Gupta and others

Supreme Court of India

06 December 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Amitava Roy, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SCO 329; 2017(14) SCALE 121

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: abet, abuse, access, adjourn, affair, age, agent, alien, alternative, anger, appearance, appreciation, association, background, birth, bond, challenge, change, character, citizenship, compelling, Constitution, correspondence, Court's jurisdiction, dealing, deem, developments, disobedience, disturb, divorce, doctrine, dress, Emergency, employed, encounter, endorsement, environment, expenses, explain, facilitate, fax, fir, forensic, forum, girl, given up, greed, habeas corpus, harm, husband, identical, imputation, Infants, inquiry, interfere, interlocutory, interlocutory order, intervention, jurisdiction, Juvenile, kidnapping, local law, Marriage, matrimonial, minor son, modification, non-compliance, noting, obedience, omitted, opportunity, Passport, past conduct, pendency, permission, plea, prayer, precedent, presume, production, recalling, registered, reliance, repatriation, resorting, restoration, restore, restraint, restraint order, review, semen, special reasons, spirit, stake, stress, suspect, territorial jurisdiction, torture, trap, urgent, variations, version, violence, wealth, welfare

Summary: Women & Children – Guardian and Wards Act, 1890, s.7b – Custody of child – Appellant and respondent got married as per Hindu rites at New Delhi and shifted to USA where they had two sons – Later due to irreconcilable marital issues, got separated and appellant left USA with one child-Aadvik to India – Respondent-wife approached court in USA and court granted sole legal and physical custody of the child-Aadvik to her pending further orders and ordered appellant to return child-Aadvik to the custody and control of respondent-wife – Appellant filed petition for restitution of conjugal rights before Family Court, Delhi and also filed petition u/s.7b of the Act seeking declaration that he was sole and permanent guardian of the child – Later, appellant also instituted suit in HC praying for decree inter alia to adjudge proceedings in court at USA initiated by respondent to be false, malicious etc. and declare order of said court to be null and void and not binding on him –Court at USA again passed orders inter alia granting sole legal and physical custody of child-Aadvik to respondent but appellant failed to appear before Court at both stages and under such circumstances, respondent filed writ petition before HC seeking writ of habeas corpus against appellant for the custody of the child alleging its illegal and unlawful charge by him – Upon hearing parties, HC concluded that most intimate contact with the parties and their children was of the court in US which had the closest concern for their well-being and as such, directed appellant to produce the child in court on the date fixed for consequential handing over of his custody to the respondent – Hence, instant appeal by appellant – However, SC by order dt.3-5-2016, stayed the operation of impugned verdict of HC and as such, the custody of the child as on date remained with appellant – Whether impugned orders of HC are sustainable.

Held, no material has been brought on record, persuasive and convincing enough, to take a view that immediate restoration of the custody of the child to the respondent in the native country is obligatorily called for in its interest and welfare. The HC, had not applied itself to examine the facts and circumstances and other materials on record bearing on the issue of welfare of the child which are unmistakably of paramount significance. Further, appellant being biological father of child-Aadvik, his custody of the child could by no means in law be construed as illegal or unlawful drawing the invocation of a superior Court’s jurisdiction to issue a writ in the nature of habeas corpus. Further, as claimed by appellant, the child is growing in a congenial environment in the loving company of his grand-parents and other relatives. He has been admitted to a reputed school and contrary to the nuclear family environment in US, he is exposed to a natural process of grooming in the association of his elders, friends, peers and playmates, which is irrefutably indispensable for comprehensive and conducive development of child’s mental and physical faculties. Under such circumstances, dislodgment of the child as directed by HC is harmful to the child. Further, considering nature of proceedings before USA and the intervening developments thereafter and prevailing state of affairs, the child till he attains majority, ought to continue in the custody, charge and care of the appellant, subject to any order to the contrary, if passed by a court of competent jurisdiction in an appropriate proceeding deciding the issue of custody in accordance with law. As such, the impugned judgment and order of HC is set aside. However, parties would participate in the pending proceedings relating to custody of the child, if the same is pursued and the courts to decide the same in accordance with law. Appeal allowed.

Ratio – The issue with regard to the repatriation of a child, as the precedential explications would authenticate has to be addressed not on a consideration of legal rights of the parties but on the sole and preponderant criterion of the welfare of the minor.
