Ganesh Shamrao Andekar and another v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

22 March 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Prafulla C. Pant, R.F. Nariman

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 223

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: First Information Report, Review, Blood, Autopsy, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 313, 386(a) - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 143, 147, 148, 149, 201, 302, 34, 426 - Criminal assault - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellants/accused armed with weapons allegedly stabbed deceased causing his death - Charge sheet was filed against accused for offence punishable u/ss. 147, 148, 149, 302 r/w s. 34 and u/s. 201 IPC - Trial Court acquitted accused of all charges - On appeal, HC convicted accused u/s. 302 r/w s. 34 IPC and sentenced them to imprisonment for life - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, deceased was taken in injured condition by PW2 to hospital, the same cannot be doubted only for the reason that name of person was mentioned in the hospital as a person who got admitted injured. The fact relating to taking deceased to hospital by PW2 was corroborated from the evidence on record of PW3. In instant case, FIR was prompt and even the post mortem has been conducted on the same day after investigation started. Keeping those facts in mind as to who got admitted deceased was not of much relevance. SC do not find any reason to disbelieve the statement of two eye witnesses for the reason that a living human being was not supported to remain motionless while being inflicted with blow after blow. If the injury was near thigh or on the iliac crest, instead of groin area, in SC opinion, that was not sufficient to hold that testimony of witnesses was false. Further, ocular evidence of PW2 and PW13 was further corroborated from the report received from Forensic Science Laboratory regarding the blood group 'B+' found on blood stained clothes and earth sample collected. The same blood group was found on the blood stained weapons recovered on disclosure made by appellants. Therefore, there was no error of law committed by HC in re-appreciating the evidence on record and coming to the conclusion that the view taken by Trial Court was perverse with regard to accused. Appeal dismissed.
