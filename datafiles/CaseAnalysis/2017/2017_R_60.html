Ram Chander and others v State of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

02 January 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Sapre, A.K. Sikri

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 8; (2017) 2 SCC 321; AIR 2017 SC 568; JT 2017 (1) SC 493; 2017 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 1; 2017 (1) RCR(Criminal) 717; 2017(1) SCALE 73

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Marriage, Spot, Place Of Occurrence, Blood, Implicate, Imprisonment For Life, Two Courts, Concurrent, Rigorous Imprisonment, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Constitution of India, 1950, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, Investigating Officer

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 299, 313, 82, 83 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 148, 149, 201, 302 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellants/accused allegedly committed murder of complainant's mother and sister(deceased) - Charge sheet was filed against accused u/ss. 148, 302 r/w ss. 149 and 201 IPC - Trial Court convicted accused for offences punishable u/ss. 148, 302/149 and 201/149 IPC and sentenced them to undergo RI for 1 year each u/s. 148 IPC - Trial Court further convicted accused to undergo imprisonment for life u/ss. 302/149 IPC with fine and were sentenced to undergo RI for 2 years each for the offences punishable u/ss. 201/149 IPC - On appeal, HC dismissed appeals and upheld orders of conviction and sentence of Trial Court - Hence, instant appeals.

Held, PW9 was rightly held to be an eye-witness and Courts below were rightly relied upon her sworn testimony for sustaining appellants' conviction. Further, there was no evidence to prove the fact as to whether the canal from where recovery of ashes and bones was made had any water therein or not at the relevant time. SC do not find that any question was put to any witness on that issue and secondly, no independent evidence was brought on record to prove as to whether the canal was full of water or had no water therein Therefore, the disclosure statements made by accused during their interrogation on the basis of which the recoveries of articles were made such as gandasa, bones, ashes, blood stained bricks and earth, tractor with cart, two plastic cans smelling diesel oil, which were duly proved by IO were sufficient to sustain the conviction when it was examined in the context of oral evidence. Merely because no expert opinion was obtained to prove as to whether bones recovered were human or animal bones, in SC view, would not weaken the case of prosecution in the light of overwhelming evidence available on record to prove the complicity of appellants. It was the consistent view of SC that minor discrepancies, even if noticed, would not affect the prosecution case, if there was a sufficient independent evidence to sustain the conviction. Vijay @ Chinee v. State of Madhya Pradesh, 2010 Indlaw SC 551. Thus, in instant case evidence adduced was found sufficient to sustain conviction and no good ground to take a different view from the one taken by Courts below and concur with their findings and views. In case if any of the appellants was on bail, his bail bond stands cancelled and was directed to be taken into custody forthwith to undergo remaining period of sentence awarded to him by Trial Court. Appeals dismissed.
