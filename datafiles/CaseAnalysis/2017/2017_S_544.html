State of Tamil Nadu, represented by Secretary and others v K. Balu and another

Supreme Court of India

31 March 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

D.Y. Chandrachud, Jagdish Singh Khehar, L. Nageswara Rao

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 261; (2017) 6 SCC 715; AIR 2017 SC 1670; 2017 (2) CLT(SC) 431; 2017 (2) KLT 179; 2017 (2) RCR(Civil) 673; 2017(4) SCALE 187; 2017(4) SCALE 199; 2017(4) SCALE 200A

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Excise

Keywords: Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, Constitution of India, 1950

Summary: Administrative - Excise - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 142 - Liquor sale - Prohibition - Fixed distance prescribed - Appellants/State filed instant appeals seeking prescription of distance under State excise rules was interfered by the directions issued by SC which prohibit shops for sale of liquor within a distance of 500 metres from outer edge of national or state highways or of a service lane along highway as topographic and geographical conditions of each State were distinct, it was not appropriate for SC to prescribe a fixed distance of 500 metres.

Held, SC clarify that even in such areas falling under local bodies with a population of less than 20,000, no licence for the sale of liquor should be issued along either a national or state highway or a service lane along highway. Equally, sale of liquor should be from a point which was neither visible from a national or state highway or which was directly accessible from a national or state highway. However, in such a situation, the prohibited distance should be restricted to 220 metres from outer edge of national or state highway or of a service lane along the highway. Further, some modification was warranted due to the prevalence of varying excise years. No licence should either be granted or renewed or should remain in operation in violation of the direction of SC beyond 30-9-2017. In State, liquor vends were operated by TASMAC which is a state owned entity. SC granted time until 1-4-2017 on the request of State. Thus, SC decline to grant any further extension to appellants. Insofar as other two States, it would suffice if they were exempted only from the application of 500 meter distance requirement. In another State exemption which was granted earlier in respect of areas falling under local bodies with a population of 20,000 would sufficiently protect the interests of State. No further relaxation was granted over and above what has already been stated in that regard. Therefore, object of direction was to prevent drunken driving, no such relaxation could be made which would defeat the object which was sought to be achieved. The directions issued by SC cannot be read down, as suggested and should be read as they stand. Appeals disposed of.
