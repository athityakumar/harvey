S. Krishna Sradha v State of Andhra Pradesh and others

Supreme Court of India

19 January 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, R.F. Nariman

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 74; JT 2017 (3) SC 51; 2017(2) SCALE 78

Case Digest

Subject: Education

Keywords: Medical College, Medical Council Of India, Constitution of India, 1950, Goa (Rules for admission to Postgraduate degree and diploma courses of the Goa University at the Goa Medical College) Rules, 2004

Summary: Education - Deprived of admission - Respondent No. 2 rejected her candidature for taking admission to first year MBBS course in Sports and Games quota and granted admission to Respondent Nos. 7, 8 on unacceptable grounds and HC in impugned judgment and order to decline relief to Appellant - Hence, instant appeal - Whether, grant of monetary compensation can be considered as sole and adequate remedy for student who has been deprived of admission to MBBS course. Held, it is clear that in spite of submitting necessary material in support of claim of petitioner for reservation under sports and games category for admission into MBBS course, she was denied due priority and admission into MBBS course. Had participation of Petitioner in World championship been considered as per norms notified by Govt., she was entitled for priority 10, in which event she was also entitled for admission into MBBS course. Where doctrine of restitution can be applied and there is no impossibility it would be anathema to cause of justice to deny same. It is seemly to appreciate that restitution as concept, as is traditionally understood, is restoration of aggrieved party to his condition prior to wrongdoing. It could be limited to monetary quantification only if breach is not capable of being remedied. That being so, compensation cannot be adequate or sole remedy for wrongful deprivation of admission, as it affects academic career of student. Order accordingly.
