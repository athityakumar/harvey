Avishek Raja and others v Sanjay Gupta

Supreme Court of India

19 June 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjan Gogoi, Navin Sinha

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 434; (2017) 8 SCC 435; AIR 2017 SC 2955; 2017(7) SCALE 55

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Civil Contempt, Jurisdiction, Contract, Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, Minimum Wages Act, 1948, Contempt of Courts Act, 1971, Journalist, Working Journalists And Other Newspaper Employees (Conditions Of Service) And Miscellaneous Provisions Act, 1955, Constitution of India, 1950

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 32 - Contempt of Courts Act, 1971, s. 2(b) - Contempt of court - Willful default of implementation of Award of Wage Board - Instant contempt petitions are filed alleging that wages and allowances as per Award of Majithia Wage Board, as duly approved and notified by Central Govt., were not paid - Further, writ petitions are filed alleging arbitrary transfer and termination/retrenchment of journalists and employees, who demanded implementation of Majithia Wage Board Award.

Held, implementation or non implementation of Majithia Wage Board Award by concerned newspaper establishments is on account of what they perceived to be the scope and ambit of the Award as approved and notified by Central Govt. The stand taken for what is alleged to be non-implementation or partial implementation of the Award, having clearly stemmed from the understanding of the Award of the concerned newspaper establishments in a particular manner, said establishments could not be held to have willfully disobeyed the judgment of SC dt.7-2-2014 passed in WP No.246/2011. At the most, alleged default had taken place on account of a wrong understanding of the Award but the same would not amount to willful default so as to attract liability of civil contempt as defined u/s.2(b) of the Act. Further, even though default alleged is evident, yet, in the absence of any willful or deliberate intention to commit it, the same could not make any of newspaper establishments liable for contempt and they are entitled for one more opportunity to implement the Award in its proper spirit and effect. Further, writ petitions seeking transfer/termination are since relatable to service conditions, adjudication of such questions in exercise of jurisdiction u/art.32 of the Constitution is unjustified and so, same are left for determination before appropriate authority. Petitions disposed of.
