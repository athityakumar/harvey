Apollo Institute of Medical Sciences and Research and others v Union of India and another

Supreme Court of India

31 August 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Khanwilkar, Dipak Misra, Dr. D.Y. Chandrachud

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 664

Case Digest

Subject: Education

Keywords: TRUSTS & ASSOCIATIONS, Indian Medical Council Act, 1956, MCI Regulations, Constitution of India, 1950

Summary: Education - Admissions - Denial - Challenged - Petitioners/college filed instant writ petition challenging impugned order of respondents/UOI contending that in view of favourable observations by Hearing Committee it was incomprehensible as to why petitioners' should be deprived from admitting students for the academic session 2017-18 when no significant deficiency was noticed by either Hearing Committee or Competent Authority of respondents.

Held, Hearing Committee, as well as Competent Authority of respondents have shown complete disregard to the fact situation and more so when they have found that infrastructure and academic requirements were fully in place in so far as petitioners' college was concerned. Impugned order acknowledges the fact that petitioners was a compliant college in respect of infrastructure and academic matters. In such a situation, SC were at a loss to discern as to what weighed with Hearing Committee and Competent Authority of Central Govt. to prohibit petitioners' college from admitting students in MBBS course for academic session 2017-18. No tangible reason whatsoever has been assigned by the said authorities in that regard, leave alone any reason. Thus, considering the fact that petitioners' college has fulfilled the infrastructure and academic requirements and has already operated the college for the academic session 2016-17 by admitting the first batch of students in MBBS course and even Competent Authority has noticed that there were no major deficiencies, SC allow application filed by petitioners in the larger public interest. Petition allowed.
