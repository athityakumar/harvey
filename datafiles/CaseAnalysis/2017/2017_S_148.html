State of U. P. and others v Shiv Kumar Pathak and others

Supreme Court of India

25 July 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Adarsh Kumar Goel, Uday Umesh Lalit

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 504; 2017 (4) AWC 3584; JT 2017 (7) SC 352; 2017 (6) MLJ 546

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service

Keywords: Selection, Qualification, Teacher, Rule, Examination, Posts, Qualifications, Recruitment, EDUCATION, National Council for Teacher Education Act, 1993, Criteria, Advertisement, Eligibility, Weightage, Amendment, Right To Information Act, 2005, Uttar Pradesh Basic Education Act, 1972, Right of Children to Free and Compulsory Education Act, 2009, Uttar Pradesh Basic Education Teachers Service Rules, 1981, Constitution of India, 1950, Training, Appointment

Summary: Service - Constitution - Constitution of Indian, 1950, art. 21A - National Council for Teachers Education Act, 1993, ss. 12A, 12(d) - Right of Children to Free and Compulsory Education Act, 2009, s. 23 - Criteria for Recruitment - Order Challenged - NCTE (authority) by virtue of power u/s. 23 of 2009 Act, issued notification as well as guidelines for conduct of TET and provided for weightage to marks in test for recruitment of teachers - Appellant /State govt. amended 1981 Rules in consonance with notification of NCTE - TET was conducted, results were declared and issued advertisement for appointment of trainee teachers in primary schools - However, earlier advertisement was cancelled and fresh advertisement was issued - HC in writ petition by respondent/applicant set aside fresh advertisement on ground of repugnancy of State law with Central law on subject falling in concurrent list - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, the subject of education falls under Entry 25 of List III of 7th Schedule of Constitution after the 42nd Amendment. Thus, by virtue of art. 254 of Constitution, the law made by the Parliament prevails over any law made by the State. The NCTE Act has been enacted by the Parliament to achieve 'planned and coordinated development of the teacher education system'. NCTE acting as an 'academic authority' u/s. 23 of 2009 Act, under the notification issued by the Central govt. as well as u/ss. 12 and 12A of 1993 Act, was competent to issue notifications. The State govt. was under obligation to act as per the said notifications and not to give effect to any contrary rule. However, since NCTE itself has taken the stand that notification dt. 11-2-2011 with regard to the weightage to be given to the marks obtained in TET is not mandatory which is also a possible interpretation the view of the HC in quashing the 15th Amendment to the 1981 Rules has to be interfered with. Hence, upheld the view that qualifications prescribed by the NCTE are binding, requirement of weightage to TET marks is not a mandatory requirement. In normal course the state would have been at liberty to proceed with the selection in terms of earlier advertisement dt.7-12-2012 in accordance with the amended rules by way of 15th amendment, in view of developments which have taken place during pendency of appeals. the said advertisement cannot proceed and while upholding the said advertisement, relief has to be moulded in the light of developments that have taken place in the interregnum. It has been informed that 66,655 teachers have already been appointed in pursuance of the interim orders of SC. Thus, SC is not inclined to interfere with. However, State is at liberty to fill up the remaining vacancies in accordance with law after issuing a fresh advertisement. Order accordingly.
