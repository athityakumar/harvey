Royal Medical Trust and another v Union of India and another

Supreme Court of India

12 September 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, Amitava Roy, A.M. Khanwilkar

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 861

Case Digest

Subject: Education; Trusts & Associations

Keywords: Jurisdiction, Precedent, Medical College, Indian Trusts Act, 1882, Indian Medical Council Act, 1956, Medical Council Of India, Establishment of Medical College Regulations, 1999, Indian Medical Council (Amendment) Act, 2010, Constitution of India, 1950

Summary: Education - Trusts & Associations - Admission - Denial - Permissibility - Petitioners/Trust made an application to respondents/UOI for establishment of a new medical college for academic session 2016-17 - Medical Council of India(MCI) pointed out certain deficiencies and did not grant Letter of Permission (LOP) for academic session 2017-2018 - On appeal, Oversight Committee (OC) approved the scheme for establishment of proposed medical college with annual intake of 150 seats for academic session 2016-17 on certain conditions - An assessment with regard to verification of compliance submitted by college was conducted by MCI and Executive Committee noted certain deficiencies - MCI submitted its recommendation to Central Govt. to revoke the letter of permission - Hence, instant writ petition - Whether the inspection was justified and decision taken by Central Govt. was correct or not.

Held, Hearing Committee held that college was deficient in bed occupancy at the conditional LOP stage other facilities have to be specifically verified and in the absence of satisfaction, the LOP ought not to be granted. Petitioners were not entitled to Letter Of Permission (LOP) for the academic session 2017-2018. SC direct that order passed in instant writ petition should be applicable for academic session 2017-2018 since cutoff date for admissions to MBBS course for academic session 2017-2018 was over and academic session was commenced. No petition should be entertained from any institution/college/society/trust or any party for grant of LOP for 2017-2018. Students who were continuing their studies on the basis of LOP granted for academic year 2016-2017 should be allowed to continue their studies in the college and they should be permitted to continue till completion of the course. The applications submitted for 2017-2018 should be treated as applications for 2018-2019 and petitioners should keep bank guarantee deposited with MCI alive and MCI should not encash the same. MCI should conduct a fresh inspection within 2 months. It should apprise petitioners with regard to the deficiencies and afford an opportunity to comply with the same. The inspection should be carried out for the purpose of grant of LOP for the academic session 2018-2019. After MCI sends its recommendation to Central Govt. it should take the final decision as per law after affording an opportunity of hearing to petitioners. Petition disposed of.
