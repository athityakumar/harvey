Honda Siel Cars India Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax, Ghaziabad

Supreme Court of India

09 June 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, Ashok Bhushan

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 432; (2017) 8 SCC 170; [2017] 395 ITR 713; 2017(6) SCALE 700

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Capital Expenditure, Companies Act, 1956, Revenue Expenditure, Expenditure, Royalty, Income-tax Act, 1961

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income-tax Act, 1961, s. 148 - Nature of expenditure of Technical fee or royalty payments - Assessee filed returns for A/Y 1999-2000, showing technical fee of 30.5 million US Dollar payable in five equal installments on yearly basis as revenue expenditure - As such, notice was issued u/s.148 of the Act and ultimately orders were passed treating technical fee as capital expenditure and for subsequent years also, AO treated royalty paid as capital expenditure - Assessee filed appeals before appellate authority but the same were dismissed - However, on further appeals to Appellate Tribunal, it was held that expenditure is revenue expenditure and allowed appeals - Aggrieved, Department filed appeals before HC wherein orders of Appellate Tribunal were reversed and HC agreed with view taken by AO that payments of royalty are to be treated as capital expenditure - Hence, instant appeals by assessee - Whether royalty payments could be treated as revenue expenditure.

Held, assessee was not in existence and it was result of joint venture of HMCL, Japan and HSCIL, India. The very purpose of Agreement between the two companies was to set up a joint venture company with aim and objective to establish a unit for manufacture of automobiles and parts thereof. As a result of said agreement, assessee company was incorporated which entered into TCA for technical collaboration. The technical collaboration included not only transfer of technical information, but complete assistance, actual, factual and on the spot, for establishment of plant, machinery etc. so as to bring in existence manufacturing unit for the products. Thus, a new business was set up with the technical know-how provided by HMCL, Japan and lump sum royalty, though in five installments, was paid therefore. Further, cls.19 and 21 of the Agreement, establishment of plant, machinery and manufacture of product with the help of technical know-how, is co-extensive, in continuance of Agreement. The Agreement also had a clause of renewal which, in totality of terms and conditions, make the unit continue so long as manufacture of product in plant and machinery, established with aid and assistance of foreign company, will continue. Since, it is found that the Agreement in question was crucial for setting up of the plant project in question for manufacturing of the goods, the expenditure in the form of royalty paid would be in the nature of capital expenditure and not revenue expenditure. Appeal dismissed.
