Asha Ranjan and another v State of Bihar and another

Supreme Court of India

15 December 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, Amitava Roy

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 1020; 2017 (1) MLJ(Crl) 605

Case Digest

Summary: Criminal - Constitution – Practice & Procedure – Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 21, 32, 142 – Indian Penal Code, 1960, ss. 34, 120B, 302 – Transfer of Prisoners Act, 1950, s.3 – Transfer of accused from a jail in one State to a jail in another State – Fair Trial – On 13-5-2016, petitioner’s husband, who was a journalist, was shot dead and FIR was registered with regard to the same under PS Nagar Thana, Siwan District for the offences punishable u/ss.302/120B and 34 of IPC – While so, petitioner informed police that respondent no.3-Shahabuddin and his henchmen were involved in the murder of her husband but that police deliberately not included the name of Shahabuddin in the list of accused persons – Later, investigation of the case was transferred to CBI – While so, during course of investigation, two accused persons were declared as proclaimed offenders and respondent no.3-Shahabuddin was released on bail – Later, feeling insecure, terrorized and helpless as regards her safety and security and of her two minor children, petitioner moved SC – Instant petitions inter alia seeking transfer of respondent no.3-Shahabuddin from Siwan jail, Bihar to a jail in Delhi – Whether SC, in exercise of power u/arts. 32 and 142 of Constitution could direct transfer of an accused from one State to another and direct conducting of pending trials by way of video conferencing.

Held, contention that if the accused is not shifted out of Siwan Jail, the pending trials would result in complete farce, for no witness would be in a position to depose against him and they, in total haplessness, is bound to succumb to the feeling of accentuated fear that is created by his unseen tentacles, is not an artifice and could not be ignored. In such a situation, SC should balance the rights between the accused and the victims and then weigh on the scale of fair trial whether shifting is necessary or not. Further, the discord that arises when there is intra-conflict in the same fundamental right especially, in the context of fair trial, has to be resolved regard being had to the obtaining fact situation. An accused who has been able to, by his sheer presence, erode the idea of safety of witness in court or impairs and rusts the faith of a victim in the ultimate justice and such erosion is due to fear psychosis prevalent in the atmosphere of trial, is not to be countenanced as it is an unconscionable situation. Such a hazard is not to be silently suffered because the ‘Majesty of Justice’ does not allow such kinds of complaints to survive. Hence, submission that shifting of accused outside the Siwan Jail would affect his right u/art.21 of the Constitution could not be accepted. Further, s.3 of the Act does not create an impediment on the part of the Court to pass an order of transfer of an accused or a convict from one jail in a State to another prison in another State since it created bar on the exercise of power on the executive only. As such, State is directed to transfer respondent no.3-Shahabuddin, from Siwan Jail to Tihar jail, Delhi and hand over the prisoner to the competent officer of Tihar jail after giving prior intimation for his transfer in Delhi. Further, authorities escorting respondent no.3 from Siwan Jail to Tihar Jail are to strictly follow the rules applicable to the transit prisoners and no special privilege is to be extended. Further, trial in respect of pending trials is to be conducted by video conferencing by the concerned Trial Court. Petitions disposed of.
