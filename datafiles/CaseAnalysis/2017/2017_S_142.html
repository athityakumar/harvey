Sheikh Juman and another v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

23 February 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Pinaki Chandra Ghose, Ashok Bhushan

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 143; AIR 2017 SC 1121; JT 2017 (2) SC 560; 2017(3) SCALE 161

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Common Object, Assembly, Informant, Arms Act, 1959, Explosive Substances Act, 1908, Overt Act, Unlawful Assembly, Deposition, Explosion, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Constitution of India, 1950, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 172, 172(2) - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 149, 302, 307 - Homicidal death - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellants/accused armed with bomb explosives and guns hurled a bomb at deceased who succumbed to injuries - Charges were framed against accused for the offences punishable u/s. 302 r/w s. 149 IPC, ss. 3, 4 of the Act, and s. 27 of the Act - Trial Court convicted accused nos. 3, 8 and 9 for the offence punishable u/s. 302 IPC and ss. 3, 4 of the Act and sentenced accused nos. 3 and 9 to death and accused no. 8 was sentenced to imprisonment for life - Further, accused no. 7 was convicted for offence punishable u/s. 302 r/w s. 149 IPC and ss. 3 & 4 of the Act and sentenced him to RI for 10 years - On appeals, HC dismissed appeals and confirmed conviction of accused - Hence, instant appeals.

Held, relying on the evidence of PW4, PW5, PW6 and PW16 and other witnesses, it was corroborated that after hurling of bomb by accused they fled away by firing in the air. Prosecution case leaves no room for doubt whatsoever about the commission of offence by appellants. In instant case witnesses have vividly deposed about the genesis of occurrence, participation and involvement of accused persons in the crime. The non-examination of witnesses, who might were there on the way to hospital or hospital itself when deceased narrated the incident, would not make prosecution case unacceptable. Also, evidence of any witness cannot be rejected merely on ground that interested witnesses admittedly had enmity with the persons implicated in the case. The purpose of recoding of evidence should always be to unearth the truth of the case. Conviction could even be based on the testimony of a sole eye-witness, if the same inspires confidence. However, prosecution case was proved by the testimony of eye-witness since corroborated by other witnesses of occurrence. Further, witnesses as HC has found were reliable and have stood embedded in their version and remained unshaken. They have vividly deposed about the genesis of occurrence, the participation and involvement of accused persons in the crime and injuries inflicted on deceased, and on each of them. Impugned judgment passed by HC does not warrant interference. Appeals dismissed.
