Palam Gas Service v Commissioner of Income Tax

Supreme Court of India

03 May 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, Ashok Bhushan

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 369; (2017) 7 SCC 613; AIR 2017 SC 2502; [2017] 394 ITR 300; JT 2017 (5) SC 150; 2017 (4) MLJ 585; 2017(5) SCALE 759; [2017] 247 TAXMAN 379

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Contract, Assessing Officer, Tax Deducted, Accounting, Resident, Tax At Source, Cash System, Labour, Person Responsible For Paying, Income-tax Rules, 1962, Income-tax Act, 1961

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income-tax Act, 1961, ss. 194C, 197C, 200, 200(1), 201, 260A, 40, 40(a)(ia) - Assessment - Tax deduction - Challenged - Appellant/assesse was engaged in the business of purchase and sale of LPG cylinders - AO held that assessee had made a sub-contract and was liable to deduct tax at source from the payment of Rs. 20,97,689/- - On appeal, Commissioner upheld order of AO and thereafter upheld by Tribunal - On further appeal before HC, HC dismissed appeal affirming order of Tribunal - Hence, instant appeal - Whether provisions of s. 40(a)(ia) of the Act should be attracted when the amount was not 'payable' to a contractor or sub-contractor but was actually paid.

Held, s. 40(a)(ia) of the Act covers not only those cases where the amount was payable but also when it was paid. S. 40(a)(ia) of the Act deals with the nature of default and the consequences thereof. When the entire scheme of obligation to deduct the tax at source and paying it over to Central Govt. was read holistically, it cannot be held that the word 'payable' occurring in s. 40(a)(ia) of the Act refers to only those cases where the amount was yet to be paid and does not cover the cases where the amount was actually paid. If the provision is interpreted in the manner suggested by appellant, then even when it was found that a person, like appellant has violated the provisions of Chapter XVIIB (or specifically ss. 194C and 200 of the Act in instant case), he would still go scot free, without suffering the consequences of such monetary default in spite of specific provisions laying down those consequences. Since, the amounts had already been paid, it straightaway concluded that s. 40(a)(ia) of the Act would apply only when the amount was 'payable' and dismissed the appeal of Dept. stating that the question of law framed did not arise for consideration. Thus, view taken by HCs was the correct view and the judgment of Allahabad HC in CIT v. Vector Shipping Services (P) Ltd., (2013) 357 ITR 642 did not decide the question of law correctly. Appeal dismissed.
