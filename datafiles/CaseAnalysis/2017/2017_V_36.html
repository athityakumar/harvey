Union of India v Vijay Krishna Uniyal (Dead) through Lrs.

Supreme Court of India

23 October 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Khanwilkar, Kurian Joseph

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 796

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Keywords: Immovable Property, Res Judicata, Eviction Proceedings, Jurisdiction, Indian Evidence Act, 1872, Review, Second Appeal, Agreement To Sell, Rejection, Occupancy Rights, Trees, Resumption, Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, Constitution of India, 1950

Summary: Land & property - Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Suit for injunction - Jurisdiction of HC - Justifiability - Plaintiff/original respondent filed suit for injunction restraining appellant/defendant from dispossessing suit property - Trial Court dismissed suit holding that defendant had right to resume property and plaintiff was entitled only to get compensation for the structure - On appeal, Appellate Court upheld judgment of Trial Court - Aggrieved, plaintiff filed second appeal before HC - Single Judge dismissed appeal and upheld findings of Courts below - Hence, instant appeals - Whether HC while dismissing second appeal and upholding concurrent finding of Courts below on factum of ownership of land was justified in making an observation which has the potential of reopening already settled issue of title in respect of suit property.

Held, appellant did not produce the Old Grant in relation to suit property, but had produced GLR extract. It was well settled that GLR extract is conclusive of the fact that land is covered by Old Grant and the rights enjoyed by plaintiff were merely possessory or occupancy rights in respect of structures thereon. In absence of any challenge to the judgment and decree passed by HC in second appeal rejecting second appeal on the ground that it lacked merit, respondents (successors in title of plaintiff) could neither succeed nor could be permitted to agitate before SC about the correctness of the finding recorded by Courts below on the issue of ownership of suit property of UOI and that plaintiff was not the absolute owner thereof. The finding of fact so recorded would bind respondents. Appellant has rightly relied upon the decisions of SC which have exposited that determination of appropriate and reasonable compensation could be done even later by referring the matter to Arbitrator as per the regulations. Therefore, liberty given by HC to plaintiff, was plain that liberty was limited to raise the issue of title relating to suit property in the event any eviction proceedings are resorted to by appellant. HC unjustly granted liberty to plaintiff to raise the issue of title in the event eviction proceedings are initiated against him. HC also committed manifest error in clarifying that if such a plea was raised, the same ought to be decided without being influenced by findings given by Trial Court. Impugned judgment of HC was set aside. Appeals allowed.
