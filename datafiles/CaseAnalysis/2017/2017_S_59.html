Prem Prakash v Santosh Kumar Jain and Sons (HUF) and another

Supreme Court of India

30 August 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

R.K. Agrawal, Ashok Bhushan

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 681; JT 2017 (8) SC 606

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Keywords: Suit Premises, Delhi Rent Control Act, 1958

Summary: Rent Control - Delhi Rent Control Act, 1958, ss. 14, 14(1)(a) - Sublet of Premises - Eviction Order - Challenged - Respondent no.1/owner let out suit shop to respondent no.1/ tenant, who in turn sublet suit shop to respondent no.2/sub tenant - Rent Control Court dismissed eviction petition of owner and directed tenant to deposit rent as agreed - Tribunal dismissed appeal of owner and HC set aside order of lower courts, thereby allowed eviction petition of owner - Hence, instant SLP to appeal.

Held, the initial burden to prove that the sub-tenant is in exclusive possession of the property is on the owner, however, the onus to prove the exclusive possession of the sub tenant is that of preponderance of probability only and he has to prove the same prima facie only and if he succeeds then the burden to rebut the same lies on the tenant. Sub-tenancy or sub-letting comes into existence when the tenant gives up possession of the tenanted accommodation, wholly or in part, and puts another person in exclusive possession thereof. This arrangement comes about obviously under a mutual agreement or understanding between the tenant and the person to whom the possession is so delivered. In this process, the landlord is kept out of the scene. In such a situation, it would be difficult for the landlord to prove, by direct evidence, the contract or agreement or understanding between the tenant and the sub-tenant. Payment of rent, undoubtedly, is an essential element of lease or sub-lease. It may be paid in cash or in kind or may have been paid or promised to be paid. It may have been paid in lump sum in advance covering the period for which the premises is let out or sub-let or it may have been paid or promised to be paid periodically. Since payment of rent or monetary consideration may have been made secretly, the law does not require such payment to be proved by affirmative evidence and the court is permitted to draw its own inference upon the facts of the case. The impugned order passed by HC does not warrant any inference. Hence, appeal is devoid of any merit. Appeal dismissed.
