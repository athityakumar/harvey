Dr. Manjeet Kaur Monga (Dead), through her Legal heirs Karan Vir Singh Monga v K. L. Suneja and others

Supreme Court of India

18 July 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Kurian Joseph, R. Banumathi

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 526

Case Digest

Subject: Competition; MRTP

Keywords: Monopolies And Restrictive Trade Practices Act, 1969, Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, Complainant

Summary: Competition - MRTP - Monopolies and Restrictive Trade Practices Act, 1969, s.12B - Payment of interest - Instant appeals by appellant contending that since allotment has been cancelled, appellant is entitled to compound interest @ 15% from original dates of payment from 1989 till the date of payment and that there is no justification in limiting the interest to 30-4-2005.

Held, the amount referred to under the section is the amount @15% compound interest on the amount already deposited, as ordered by Tribunal. Merely, because a liquidated amount is not stipulated or determined by the Tribunal, it could not be said that it is not the compensation. Once the interest, as ordered by the Tribunal is calculated that will be the amount of compensation referred to u/s.12B of the Act. Hence, builder is to pay compensation @15% compound interest upto 30-4-2005. Further, Citibank would stand impleaded as additional respondent in complaint before Tribunal and Tribunal is to examine the issues as to whether there should be any compensation and if so, the amount payable after 30-4-2005 and whether Citibank is liable to pay any interest to account holder. Matters are remitted to Tribunal for the limited extent only. Appeals disposed of.
