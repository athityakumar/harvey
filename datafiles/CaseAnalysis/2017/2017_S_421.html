Nidhi Kaim and another v State of Madhya Pradesh and others

Supreme Court of India

13 February 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Jagdish Singh Khehar, Kurian Joseph, Arun Mishra

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 109; (2017) 4 SCC 1; AIR 2017 SC 986; 2017 (2) AWC 1554; 2017 (2) CLT(SC) 40; JT 2017 (2) SC 337; 2017 (2) MLJ 412; 2017(2) SCALE 626

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Education

Keywords: Unfair Means, Jurisdiction, Criminal Proceedings, Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, Constitution of India, 1950, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973

Summary: Education - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 142 - Exercise of jurisdiction u/art.142 of the Constitution by SC - Consequences of established fraud - Madhya Pradesh Professional Examination Board (Vyapam) passed orders cancelling professional MBBS results of the appellants on ground that they gained admission to the said course, by resorting to unfair means in Pre-Medical Test - Appellants challenged said orders of cancellation before HC vide writ petitions but the same were dismissed - Aggrieved, appellants approached DB of SC (former Division Bench) wherein though HC orders were affirmed vide orders dt.12-5-2016, yet as there was divergent opinion between two judges of DB, matter was referred to larger Bench - Later, in pursuance of orders of instant DB of SC for clarification, former Division Bench passed orders dt.30-8-2016 and in pursuance of the same, instant matter with reference to issue whether jurisdiction of SC u/art.142 of the Constitution could be invoked in favour of appellants to render complete justice in the matter - Whether in exercise of jurisdiction vested in SC u/art.142 of the Constitution, the consequences of established fraud could be ignored to do complete justice in instant matter.

Held, with Vyapam having cancelled the appellant's admission to MBBS course, and with the said orders being upheld by HC and SC, it could not be said that cancellation orders were unjust. If the admission of appellants to MBBS course was improper, the cancellation orders were obviously proper. Under such circumstances, if academic benefits of appellants arising out of their admission cancelled by Vyapam are restored, then cancellation orders would mean nothing and are set at naught and the same would undo, the Vyapam orders as upheld by HC and SC. Such recourse would not serve the 'larger interest of administration of justice' and on contrary such an initiative would cause 'manifest injustice'. Hence, in view of facts of instant matter, art.142 of the Constitution could not be invoked in the larger interest of administration of justice. On contrary, to do justice in the matter, order passed by Vyapam had to be upheld without any further modification or alteration. Further, considering conscious involvement of appellants in gaining admission to MBBS course by means of a fraudulent stratagem of trickery, it could not be ignored or overlooked the declaration of law with reference to fraud. Nothing obtained by fraud, could be sustained and the same is applicable to case of appellants also. Furthermore, conferring rights or benefits on appellants, who had consciously participated in a well thought of, and meticulously orchestrated plan, to circumvent well laid down norms, for gaining admission to MBBS course, would amount to espousing the cause of unfair. Further, appellants, irrespective of their age, were conscious of the regular process of admission. They breached the same by devious means and therefore, have to suffer the consequences of their actions. Appellants had consciously sought the assistance of a syndicate, engaged in manipulating admissions to medical institutions. They were beneficiaries of acts of deceit and deception. Hence, case of the appellants does not deserve any sympathetic consideration and admission of appellants to MBBS course, could not be legalized in the name of justice. Hence, it would not be proper to legitimize the admission of the appellants, to MBBS course, in exercise of the jurisdiction vested in Court under art.142 of the Constitution. Order accordingly.

Ratio - As per observations of SC, the two important parameters for consideration to exercise jurisdiction u/art.142 of the Constitution are 'larger interest of administration of justice' and 'preventing manifest injustice'.
