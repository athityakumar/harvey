Parle Agro Private Limited and others v Commissioner of Commercial Taxes, Trivandrum

Supreme Court of India

09 May 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhushan, A.K. Sikri

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 971; 2017 (6) SCALE 368

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; VAT

Keywords: Assessment, cab, Commercial Tax, dealer, exemption, registration, Revenue, sales tax, Sales Tax Officer, Service Tax, Trader

Summary: (A) VAT - Indirect Tax - Kerala Value Added Tax Act, 2003, ss. 6(1)(a), 6(1)(d) - Product classification - Tax assessment - Applicability - Appellant/dealer classified product fruit juice based drink under Entry 71 of notification issued u/s. 6(1)(d) of the Act and was paying @ 12.5% VAT - Assessment notices were issued to appellant proposing classification of drink u/s. 6(1)(a)of the Act, 2003 as "aerated branded soft drink" and tax liability @ 20% - Appellant filed writ petition before HC seeking direction to respondent/Commissioner to consider and pass order on application for clarification within specified time and proceedings initiated by Commissioner be kept in abeyance - Single Judge directed Commissioner to consider and pass orders on the clarification application within a month - On appeal, DB dismissed appeal and upheld impugned order of Single Judge - Hence, instant appeals - Whether there was any inter-relation between ss. 6(1)(a) and 6(1)(d) of the Act. Held, when fruit juice based drinks were covered under Entry 71, State Govt. knew that fruit juice based drinks were not covered by s. 6(1)(a) of the Act. Applicability of the power of State to issue notification u/s. 6(1)(d) of the Act arises only when goods were not covered by s. 6(1)(a) of the Act. Therefore, fruit juice based drinks were never treated as 'aerated branded soft drinks' which was the understanding of State while issuing notification u/s. 6(1(d) of the Act. Had fruit juice based drinks were also to be covered by aerated branded soft drinks, there was no occasion for subordinate legislative authority, i.e., State to include such products in notification u/s. 6(1)(d) of the Act. The Entry of fruit juice based drinks got subsumed in the residuary entry and the amendment did not change or affect the character and content of the products which were included in Entry 71. Appeals disposed of. (B) VAT - Indirect Tax - Kerala Value Added Tax Act, 2003, ss. 6(1)(a), 6(1)(d) - Common parlance test - Validity - Whether common parlance test was the only test to be applied for understanding the different entries u/ss. 6(1)(a) and 6(1)(d) of the Act. Held, common parlance and commercial parlance test was not the only test which could have been applied for interpreting the entries in items mentioned in s. 6(1)(a) of the Act and the entries which contain scientific and technical word were also to be looked into in technical and scientific meaning. Both HC and Committee of Joint Commissioners discarded the evidence of technical and scientific meaning of word. Appellant has rightly relied on the technical evidence brought on the record which indicates that use of carbon dioxide to the extent of 0.6 per cent was only for the purpose of preservative in packaging the commodities and the product was thermally processed and carbon dioxide was added to as the preservative. Appeals disposed of. (C) VAT - Indirect Tax - Kerala Value Added Tax Act, 2003, ss. 6(1)(a), 6(1)(d) - Principle applicability - Overlooked - Whether principle of Noscitur a Sociis would be applicable to instant case. Held, cl. 5 of Entry 71 has to take colour and meaning from the other items included in Entry 71. Item 5 of Entry 71 uses the words "similar other products not specifically mentioned under any other entry in this list or any other schedule". Thus, the products which are to be covered under Item no.5 are similar other products. When Item no.2 of the Entry 71 that was fruit juice, fruit concentrates, fruit squash, fruit syrup and pulp, and fruit cordial and item no.4 that was health drinks of all varieties were kept in mind the fruit juice based drink should fall in Item no.5. Both HC and Committee of Commissioners overlooked such principle while interpreting item no.5 of Entry 71. Appeals disposed of. (D) VAT - Indirect Tax - Kerala Value Added Tax Act, 2003, ss. 6(1)(a), 6(1)(d), 94 - Clarification application - Sustainability - Whether DB could preclude Committee of Joint Commissioners to examine the materials filed by appellant along with Clarification application u/s. 94 of the Act. Held, order passed by DB was a case of assessment of another assessee which decision was based on the materials brought on the record by the said assessee and could not have precluded appellant from filing the application u/s. 94 of the Act and when DB dismissed the appeal of Revenue, Committee ought to have followed the observation given by DB of another assesse. Thus, judgment of DB did not conclude the issue and Committee was not absolved from its duty of deciding the same in accordance with the materials brought on the record by appellant and although Committee noticed all the pleadings and contentions but mainly relying on the ruling of another assessee dismissed the clarification application which cannot be sustained. Appeals disposed of. (E) VAT - Indirect Tax - Kerala Value Added Tax Act, 2003, ss. 6(1)(a), 6(1)(d) - Classification of product - Significance - Whether Tribunal decision has any relevance with regard to the classification of product in question. Held, though order of Tribunal did not conclude the controversy in favour of appellant but fact that Tribunal did not hold the product to be under the "aerated water" was a factor which necessitated a more deeper consideration by HC to find out as to whether the product was 'aerated branded soft drink' or not. HC in its judgment found that since the product charged with air or carbon dioxide was an aerated drink. From the manufacturing process which was on the record, it was clear that carbon dioxide to the extent of 0.6 percent was added as preservative. Technical note submitted on behalf of appellant clearly mentioned that use of carbon dioxide was only as a preservative of drink. Appeals disposed of. (F) VAT - Indirect Tax - Kerala Value Added Tax Act, 2003, ss. 6(1)(a), 6(1)(d) - Expert opinion - Disregard - Legality - Whether Committee as well as HC has rightly discarded technical and expert opinion relied by appellant. Held, technical opinion clearly mentioned that carbon dioxide was used for preservation purpose only. Before Committee entire process of manufacture of the product was explained along with all relevant orders and certificates of Food Safety Authorities. It was stated that Experts in their opinions and certifications have mentioned that product was commercially and technically distinct from products which have classified as 'aerated branded soft drinks'. The certifications which were relied by appellant indicate that in the case product does not undergo aeration or carbonation product was thermally processed with CO2 which help in preserving the apple juice concentrate which was otherwise perishable in nature. Appellant has successfully proved by relevant scientific and technical materials that product in question was a commodity which is fully covered by Item no.5 of Entry 71. HC discarded scientific and expert opinion with regard to manufacturing process and contents of the product. The orders of Food Safety Authority were also discarded which were relevant for considering the nature and contents of product. The adjudication by Tribunal was relevant at least on the aspect that product is not aerated which was also discarded by HC as well as Committee. Thus, appellant has successfully proved from the materials brought on the record that product was required to be classified under Item no.5 of the Entry 71 as amended with tax liability at 12.5%. Appeals disposed of.
