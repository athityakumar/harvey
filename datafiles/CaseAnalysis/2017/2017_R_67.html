Ram Kumar Patel and others v State of U. P. and others

Supreme Court of India

25 July 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Adarsh Kumar Goel, Uday Umesh Lalit

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 505

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service

Keywords: Qualification, Rule, Appeal, EDUCATION, National Council for Teacher Education Act, 1993, Weightage, Amendment, Uttar Pradesh Basic Education Act, 1972, Constitution (Eighty-Sixth Amendment) Act,2002, Right of Children to Free and Compulsory Education Act, 2009, Uttar Pradesh Basic Education Teachers Service Rules, 1981, Constitution of India, 1950, Training, Appointment, Uttar Pradesh Basic Education (Teachers) Service (Sixteenth Amendment) Rules, 2012

Summary: Service - Constitution - Constitution of Indian, 1950 - Uttar Pradesh Basic Education Act, 1972, s. 19 - Uttar Pradesh Basic Education (Teachers) Service Rules, 1981, r. 8 - National Council of Teachers Education Act, 1993 - Right of Children to Free and Compulsory Education Act, 2009, s. 23 - Criteria for Appointment - Respondent/state govt. framed the Rules by virtue of power u/s. 19 of the Act, which provides for rules to determine qualification for appointment as teachers and conditions of services of teachers of basic school - HC in writ petition by petitioner quashed amended rules on ground that it was in conflict with notification issued by National Council of Teachers Education (NCTE) - Hence, instant appeals.

Held, 86th amendment to Constitution in the year 2002 regulates elementary education and also deals with the qualification for appointment of teacher's u/s. 23 of 2009 Act. NCTE constituted under the 1993 Act has been prescribed as an 'academic authority' by the Central govt. to lay down the minimum qualification prescribed for appointment of a teacher. Accordingly, notification dt. 23-8-2010 has been issued by the NCTE laying down such qualification. Teacher Eligibility Test (TET) is the essential qualification prescribed under the said notification. However, the guidelines/ notification dt. 11-2-2011 provided that in the process of appointment of teachers, weightage has to be given to the marks obtained in TET examination. Further, by virtue of power of the central govt. on a subject in Concurrent List, in case of repugnancy, the State rule was required to comply with the disposition in a central legislation. Since the issue is covered by Entry 25 List III of the VII Schedule to the Constitution, any standard laid down by the central govt. will bind the state and any conflicting decision of the state will be unconstitutional. In instant case there is no conflict in the notification issued by the central govt. and the amendment to the State Rules since the notification dt. 11-2-2011 to the extent of suggesting weightage to TET marks can be held to be merely a guideline. Hence, impugned judgment of HC is set aside and state is at liberty to proceed with matter in accordance with law. Appeals allowed.
