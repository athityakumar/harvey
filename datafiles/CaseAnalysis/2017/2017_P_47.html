Pepsu Road Transport Corporation, Patiala v Amandeep Singh and others

Supreme Court of India

03 January 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhushan, Sharad Arvind Bobde

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 5; (2017) 2 SCC 766; AIR 2017 SC 321; 2017 (2) ALT(SC) 18; 2017 (152) FLR 605; JT 2017 (1) SC 56; 2017 (1) LLJ 696; 2017(1) SCALE 257

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Gratuity, Family Pension, provident fund, Pension, Date, Pensionary Benefits, Appeal, Regular, Interest, Option, Scheme, Pension Scheme, Writ Petition, Notice, Employee, PEPSU Road Transport Corporation Employees/Pension Gratuity and General Provident Fund Regulations, 1992

Summary: Service - PEPSU Road Transport Corporation Employees/Pension Gratuity and General Provident Fund Regulations, 1992 - Retirement - Pensionary benefits - Entitled to - Plaintiff filed suit for declaration seeking for pension and other benefits attached with interest on delayed payment - Trial Court decreed suit holding that although plaintiff was to give option as per the Regulation and he had to deposit the amount of Contributory Provident Fund (CPF) however, pension cannot be declined on technical ground that he had not refunded loan amount - On appeal, Appellate Court dismissed appeal - On further appeal, HC dismissed appeal holding that there was no evidence that pension scheme was circulated within the employees and got noted by them - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, notice inviting option need not to be personally served to the employees unless the Regulation or any instruction so provides. The Regulations which were being considered in the instant case had already been interpreted in PEPSU Road Transport Corporation v. Mangal Singh, 2011 Indlaw SC 377. SC having already held that the Regulations do not contemplate any personal service of notice to employees the finding in judgment of Courts below holding otherwise for decreeing the suit of plaintiff were unsustainable. Though the Regulations were in force from 1992, plaintiff retired on 30-11-2011 and after retirement received CPF benefits without any protest and at no point of time before retirement he has raised any grievance. The benefit which was available to him under CPF scheme was received by plaintiff he cannot be allowed to another benefit flowing from pension scheme which he never opted. Extending benefit of pension scheme to plaintiff should be extending double benefits CPF benefit as well as pension scheme which was never contemplated by the Regulations. Therefore, in instant case plaintiff having not opted for pension scheme, the requirement from refunding the advance taken from CPF within 6 months was not attracted. Impugned judgments of Courts below were unsustainable and the same were set aside. Appeal allowed.
