Charandas Swami v State of Gujarat and another

Supreme Court of India

10 April 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Khanwilkar, Kurian Joseph

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 300; (2017) 7 SCC 177; AIR 2017 SC 1761; 2017 CRLJ 2904; 2017 (2) MLJ(Crl) 444; 2017 (2) RCR(Criminal) 802; 2017(4) SCALE 403

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Kidnapping, Direct Evidence, Conspiracy, FIR, CBI, Gold, Investigating Agency, Legal Evidence, Indian Evidence Act, 1872, Age, Spot, Reliance, Blood, Chain, Doctor, Girl, Dead Body, Time Of Death, Unknown Person, Medical Examination, Last Seen, Disclosure Statement, Inference, Informant, Dealing, Two Courts, Explain, Discovery, Admissible, Knife, Autopsy, Discrepancies, Discrepancy, Identity, Clothes, Disappearance, Panchnama, Concurrent, Inadmissible, Asphyxia, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, Investigating Officer, Seizure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120B, 201, 302, 364 - Kidnap and murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellants/accused allegedly committed kidnap of deceased from Temple complex sedated and then strangled deceased to death - Charge sheet was filed against accused u/ss. 120-B, 364, 302 and 201 IPC - Trial Court convicted accused for offence u/s. 302 r/w s. 120B IPC and sentenced them to death and were also convicted u/s. 364 r/w s. 120B IPC and sentenced to RI for life - On appeal, HC upheld judgment of Trial Court convicting accused for offences u/s. 302 r/w ss. 120B, 364 and 201 IPC and however, commuted the death sentence awarded by Trial Court to a sentence of life imprisonment - Hence, instant appeals - Whether there was any perversity or illegality in impugned judgments of Courts below.

Held, appellants have made fervent effort to persuade Court about the several other discrepancies such as age of deceased or that HC having discarded the circumstance of wrappers of sleeping pills found at Temple. However, it does not discredit the other clinching circumstances established by prosecution which completes the chain of events indicative of involvement of appellants in commission of crime. The circumstances taken into account by both Courts leave no manner of doubt about the complicity of appellants in commission of crime in question. SC was in agreement with the conclusion reached by Courts below that there was sufficient evidence to indicate the complicity of accused no. 5 in commission of crime in question. Suffice it to observe that the circumstances established indicating the complicity of accused no. 5 cannot be compared with the role of accused no. 4, so as to give the same benefit to him. Further, for the presence of deceased at Temple complex on the day of incident, the evidence that he was last seen together with accused no.3 going from Temple complex in a car. The conduct of accused no.3 in misleading investigating agencies, burning of vehicle used in the commission of crime and then filing of a false insurance claim which was rejected by insurance company, strong motive for committing murder of deceased leave no manner of doubt about the involvement of appellants in commission of crime. Therefore, it was not a case of finding of guilt recorded in absence of any legal evidence or contrary to the evidence available on record. SC found that finding of guilt against appellants was inescapable. No tangible reason to interfere with the final conclusion so reached by both the Courts. Impugned judgment of conviction and sentence passed by HC was upheld. Appeals dismissed.
