Chand Devi Daga and others v Manju K. Humatani and others

Supreme Court of India

03 November 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhushan, A.K. Sikri

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 843

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Permission, Summons, Rank, Proviso, Prosecutor, Public Prosecutor, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898, Pendency, Government Advocate, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Husband, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, Complainant

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120B, 201, 256(1), 34, 420, 467, 468, 471, 496, 498, 500 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 256, 249, 482, 495 - Complaint - Procedure for trial - Validity - Respondent nos.1 to 7 filed complaint against appellants/accused alleging offence u/ss. 420, 467, 468, 471, 120B, 201 and 34 IPC - Trial Court dismissed complaint holding that prima facie case u/ss. 420, 467, 468, 120B and 201/34 IPC was not made out against accused - On appeal, Appellate Court dismissed appeal - Aggrieved, respondents filed miscellaneous petition before HC challenging the same which was allowed - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, instant case was a case where offence was alleged u/ss. 420, 467, 468, 471, 120B and 201 r/w s. 34 IPC for which procedure for trial of summons-case was not applicable and there was no provision in Chap XIX "Trial of warrant-cases by Magistrates" containing a provision that in the event of death of complainant complaint was to be rejected. Magistrate u/s. 249 CrPC has power to discharge a case where complainant was absent. The discharge u/s. 249 CrPC, however, was hedged with condition "the offence may be lawfully compounded or is not a cognizable offence". Had the CrPC intended that in case of death of complainant in a warrant case complaint is to be rejected, the provision would have indicated any such intention which was clearly absent. HC did not commit any error in allowing the legal heirs of complainant to prosecute petition before HC. SC do not find any error in the order of HC. Appeal dismissed.
