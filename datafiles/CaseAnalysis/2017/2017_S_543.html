Muttaicose @ Subramani v State of Tamil Nadu, represented by Inspector of Police

Supreme Court of India

03 July 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Prafulla C. Pant, Deepak Gupta

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 822; (2017) 8 SCC 598; AIR 2017 SC 3117; JT 2017 (7) SC 76; 2017 (3) MLJ(Crl) 338; 2017(7) SCALE 212

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: First Information Report, Informant, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Tamil Nadu Properties (Damage and Loss) Prevention Act

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 147, 450, 307, 324 - Murder - Conviction - Challenged - Appellant/accused no.2 along with four co-accused were charged for murder of 'X - Trial Court convicted all accused u/s. 302 of IPC and awarded sentence - HC in appeal upheld/confirmed conviction of A2 u/s. 302 of IPC, however sentenced A1, A4, A5 u/s. 324 of IPC to each one of them for imprisonment already undergone - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, it is trite that mere delay in lodging the first information report is not by itself fatal to the case of the prosecution. Nevertheless, it is a relevant factor of which the court is obliged to take notice and examine whether any explanation for the delay has been offered and if offered, whether it is satisfactory or not. If no satisfactory explanation is forthcoming, an adverse inference may be drawn against the prosecution. However, in the event, the delay is properly and satisfactorily explained; the prosecution case cannot be thrown out merely on the ground of delay in lodging the FIR. The explanation has to be considered in the light of the totality of the facts and circumstances of the case. Further, the evidence of the interested or related witnesses is required to be scrutinized more carefully. They cannot be simply disbelieved for the reason that they are related to informant. In instant case, it is clear from the record that all the four eye witnesses PWs-1-4 are injured eye witnesses and cannot doubt their testimony even after scrutinizing the testimony with caution. There is nothing on the record to show that the deceased gave any provocation to A-2 to make him to assault the deceased. Hence, the impugned order of HC affirming conviction and sentence against A2 does not warrant interference. Appeal dismissed.
