Summary: Dipak Misra, J

(A) Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120, 120A, 120B, 201, 302, 307, 34, 342, 363, 365, 366, 375, 376, 376(1), 376(2), 376(2)(f), 376(2)(g), 377, 395, 396, 397, 412, 420, 468, 471 - Gang rape - Death penalty - Sustainability - Appellants/accused allegedly committed rape on prosecutrix/deceased and brutally inflicted injuries on deceased and her friend - FIR was lodged and charges were framed against accused u/ss. 201/302/365/366/307/376(2)(g)/ 377/395/396/412 r/w s. 120B IPC - Trial Court convicted accused u/ss. 120B/365/366/376(2)(g)/377/201/395/397/412 IPC and sentenced them to death for offence punishable u/s. 302 IPC - On appeal, HC affirmed the conviction and confirmed death penalty imposed on accused - Hence, instant appeals - Whether there was any perversity or ileegality in impugned judgment of HC.

Held, in instant case, the brutal, barbaric and diabolic nature of the crime was evincible from the acts committed by accused persons, viz., the assault on informant/PW1 with iron rod and tearing off his clothes, assaulting informant and deceased with hands, kicks and iron rod and robbing them of their personal belongings like debit cards, ring, informant's shoes, etc. attacking deceased by forcibly disrobing her and committing violent sexual assault by appellants. Their brutish behaviour in having anal sex with deceased and forcing her to perform oral sex, injuries on the body of deceased by way of bite marks and insertion of rod in her private parts that caused perforation of her intestine which caused sepsis and, ultimately, led to her death. The medical history of prosecutrix demonstrates that the entire intestine of prosecutrix was perforated and splayed open due to the repeated insertion of rod and hands and appellants had pulled out the internal organs of prosecutrix in the most savage and inhuman manner that caused grave injuries which ultimately annihilated her life. Those acts itself demonstrate the mental perversion and inconceivable brutality as caused by appellants. Further, accused thrown informant and deceased on the road and tried to run the bus over them so that there would be no evidence against them. They made all possible efforts in destroying the evidence by, inter alia, washing the bus and burning the clothes of the deceased and after performing the gruesome act, they divided the loot among themselves. It was manifest that the wanton lust, servility to absolutely unchained carnal desire and slavery to loathsome beastility of passion ruled the mindset of appellants to commit a crime which could summon with immediacy "tsunami" of shock in the mind of the collective and destroy ivilised marrows of milieu in entirety. Therefore, HC has correctly confirmed the death penalty and SC see no reason to differ with the same. Appeals dimsised.

R. Banumathi J

(B) Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120, 120A, 120B, 201, 302, 307, 34, 342, 363, 365, 366, 375, 376, 376(1), 376(2), 376(2)(f), 376(2)(g), 377, 395, 396, 397, 412, 420, 468, 471 - Death penalty - Rarest of rare - Applicability - Whether instant case could be one of the rarest of rare cases warranting death penalty.

Held, the act was committed in connivance and collusion of 6 who were on a notorious spree running a bus, showcasing as a public transport, with the intent of attracting passengers and committing crime with them. The diabolical manner in which crime was committed leaves one startled as to the pervert mental state of inflictor. Factors like young age of accused and poor background cannot be said to be mitigating circumstances. Similarly, post-crime remorse and post-crime good conduct of accused, statement of accused as to their background and family circumstances, age, absence of criminal antecedents and their good conduct in prison, in SC view, cannot be taken as mitigating circumstances to take the case out of the category of "rarest of rare cases". Further, there was not even a hint of hesitation with respect to the aggravating circumstances outweighing mitigating circumstances and SC do not find any justification to convert death sentence imposed by Courts below to 'life imprisonment for the rest of the life'. The gruesome offences were committed with highest viciousness. Accused might not be hardened criminals but the cruel manner in which gang-rape was committed in the moving bus, iron rods were inserted in private parts of victim and coldness with which both the victims were thrown naked in cold wintery night shocks the collective conscience of society. Therefore, instant case clearly comes within the category of 'rarest of rare case' where the question of any other punishment was 'unquestionably foreclosed'. If the dreadfulness displayed by accused in committing the gang-rape, unnatural sex, insertion of iron rod in private parts of victim does not fall in the 'rarest of rare category', then one might wonder what else would fall in that category. SC concur with the majority in affirming the death sentence awarded to accused persons. Appeals dismissed.
