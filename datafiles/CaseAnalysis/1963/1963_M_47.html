General Manager, B. E. S. T. Undertaking, Bombay v Mrs. Agnes

Supreme Court of India

10 May 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, J.R. Madholkar, Raghubar Dayal

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 296; AIR 1964 SC 193; 1963 (7) FLR 310; 1963 (2) LLJ 615; [1964] 3 S.C.R. 930

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Workmen's Compensation Act, 1923, s. 3(1) - (A) When can an employment be said to end and begin? - Held, employment does not necessarily end when 'down tool' signal is given or when workman leaves actual workshop where he is working - An employment may end or may begin not only when employee begins to work or leaves his tools but also when they use means of access and egress to and from place of employment - A contractual duty or obligation on part of an employee to use only a particular means of transport extends area of field of employment to course of said transport - A theoretical option to take an alternative route may not detract from such a duty if accepted one is of proved necessity or of practical compulsion - Manifest from standing rules of B.E.S.T. - A driver when going home from depot or coming to depot uses bus - Any accident that happens to him is an accident in course of his employment - Wife is entitled for compensation - Appeal allowed.
