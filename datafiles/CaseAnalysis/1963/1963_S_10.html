 Gurugobinda Basu v Sankari Prasad Ghosal and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 August 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

S.K. Das, K. Subba Rao, Raghubar Dayal, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 163; AIR 1964 SC 254; [1963] 33 Comp Cas 1132; 1964 (1) CompLJ 87; 1964 (1) SCJ 259; [1964] 4 S.C.R. 311

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Keywords: Office Of Profit Under, House Of The People

Summary: Election - Corporate - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 102(1)(a) - Companies Act, 1956, ss. 2(18), 617 and 619 - (A) What is decisive test for determining whether a person holds any office of profit Govt. u/art. 102(1)(a) to be chosen/elected to either house of Parliament? - Held, for holding an office of profit under Govt., there need be no relationship of master and servant between them - Constitution makes a distinction between holder of an office of profit under Govt. and holder of a post or service under Govt. - Decisive test for determining whether a person holds any office of profit under Govt. is test of appointment - It is not correct to say that several factors which enter into determination of this question - Appointing authority, is vested with power to terminate appointment, authority determines remuneration, source from which remuneration is paid, and authority is vested with power to control manner in which duties of office are discharged and to give directions in that behalf must all co exist and each must show subordination to Govt. - If one of elements is absent, test of a person holding an office under Govt., Central or State, is not satisfied - (B) Whether auditor of Hindustan Steel, Ltd, and Durgapur Projects Ltd are holding post of profit under Govt. Companies within meaning of s. 2(18) r/w s. 617 of Companies Act, 1956? - Further held, (1) he is a Govt. official- Companies are owned by Govt. - (2) Auditor is appointed and removed on advice of Comptroller and Auditor General of India - Thus he holds an office of profit under Govt. of India within meaning of art. 102(1)(a) of Constitution - Appeal dismissed.
