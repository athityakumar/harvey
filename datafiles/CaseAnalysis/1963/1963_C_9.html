Vriddhachalam Pillai v Chaldean Syrian Bank Limited, and Another

Supreme Court of India

03 December 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, K. Subba Rao, Kailas Nath Wanchoo

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 366; AIR 1964 SC 1425; 1964 KerLJ 478; [1964] 5 S.C.R. 647

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Civil Procedure Code, 1908 - Market Value - Suit filed by respondent for recovery of certain sums due on a mortgage by deposit of title deeds executed by appellant's father who is 2nd respondent - Property belonged to joint family composed of 1st defendant and appellant - Appellant was a minor on date of suit-transaction and even at date of suit - Debt was a personal borrowing by father, not for any purpose binding on joint family - Family had properties both in Cochin and Palghat - A partition deed was entered into which dealt only with properties in Cochin - It contained a recital that father acknowledged that debts incurred by him were for his own personal purposes and were not binding on son - (A) Whether nature of partition could be termed bonafide? - Held, there were a number of creditors who had filed suits against father and he was heavily pressed for discharging them - It was when things were in this state that partition of Cochin properties was brought about - This necessarily showed that partition was not bona fide and is not such as to be binding against Bank - (B) Whether son can be held liable for payment of debts? - Further held, mortgage was fully supported by consideration, debt was not incurred for any necessary or beneficial purpose of family and lastly, purpose for which debt was incurred was neither illegal nor immoral - A son is liable, even after partition for pre-partition debts of his father which are not immoral or illegal and for payment of which no arrangement was made at date of partition - Appeal dismissed.
