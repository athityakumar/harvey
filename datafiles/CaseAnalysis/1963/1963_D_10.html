 Rameshwar Shaw v District Magistrate, Burdwan and Another

Supreme Court of India

11 September 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, K. Subba Rao, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 201; AIR 1964 SC 334; 1964 ALJ 211; 1964 BLJR 128; 1964 CRLJ 257; [1964] 4 S.C.R. 921

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Preventive Detention Act, 1950

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Criminal - Preventive Detention Act, 1950, ss. 3(1)(a) and 3(2) - (A) Whether authority must be satisfied u/s. 3(2) that detention of person is necessary in order to prevent him from acting in any prejudicial manner as indicated in cls. (i) to (iii) of s. 3(1)(a)? - Held, since Act authorizes preventive detention of citizens without a trial, material provisions of Act must be strictly construed and safeguards which Act has deliberately provided for protection of citizens must be liberally interpreted - (B) Whether satisfaction of detaining authority to which s. 3(1)(a) refers is justificiable and detenu can ask court to consider it ?- Further held, it is his subjective satisfaction, and so is not justifiable - Therefore, detenu to can not ask Court - Adequacy of material on which said satisfaction purport to rest also cannot be examined in a Court of law - If any of grounds furnished to detenu are found to be irrelevant while considering application of cls. (i) to (iii) of s. 3(1)(a) and in that sense are foreign to Act, satisfaction of detaining authority on which order of detention is based is open to challenge and detention order liable to be quashed - If some of grounds supplied to detenu are so vague that they would virtually deprive detenu of his statutory right of making a representation, that again may introduce a serious infirmity in order of his detention - It is only in this incidental manner and in support of plea of mala fides that this question can become justifiable; otherwise reasonableness or propriety of said satisfaction contemplated by s. 3(1)(a) cannot be questioned before Courts - (C) On what basis authority can reach conclusion that it is necessary to detain a person?- Further held, in light of evidence about past prejudicial activities of said person - Past conduct or antecedent history of person on which authority purports to act - But it is both inexpedient and undesirable to lay down any inflexible test - Question about validity of satisfaction of authority will have to be considered on facts of each case - (D) Whether detention order can be passed against a person who is already in detention or in jail? - Further held, s.3(1)(a) does not preclude authority from passing an order of detention against a person whilst he is in detention or in jail, but relevant facts in connection with making of order may differ, that may make a difference in application of principle that a detention order can be passed against a person in jail - Antecedent history and past conduct on which order of detention is important for drawing by authority that detention of person after his release is necessary - Satisfaction that it is necessary to detain a person for purpose of preventing him from acting in a prejudicial manner is thus basis of order u/s. 3(1)(a), and this basis is clearly absent in case of a person already in jail custody - So, his detention is not justified by s. 3(1)(a) and is outside its purview - District Magistrate, who orders detention of detenue acts outside his powers conferred on him by s. 3(1)(a) when he holds that it was necessary to detain person in order to prevent him from acting in a prejudicial manner - Petition allowed.
