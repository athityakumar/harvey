Life Insurance Corporation of India v Sunil Kumar Mukherjee and Others

Supreme Court of India

22 November 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, K.C. Das Gupta

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 208; AIR 1964 SC 847; [1964] 34 Comp Cas 299; 1964 (8) FLR 158; 1964 (1) LLJ 442; 1964 (1) SCJ 272; [1964] 5 S.C.R. 528

Case Digest

Subject: Insurance

Keywords: Appointed Day, Employees Of The Corporation, Controlled Business, Life Insurance Corporation (Staff) Regulations, 1960, Life Insurance Corporation Refutations 1958, Life Insurance Corporation (Alteration of Remuneration and Other Terms and Conditions of Service of Employees) Order 1957

Summary: Insurance - Life Insurance Corporation Act, 1956, ss. 11 and 49 - Constitution of India, 1953, art. 226 and 311(2) - LIC field officers (Alteration of Remuneration and other Terms and Conditions of Service) Order, 1957, cls. 5, 10 and 11 - Regulation Order u/s. 11(2) of Act - Fixing remuneration of development officers u/cl. 5 - Services of field Officer, can be terminated only in accordance with cl. 10 - Para 4(h) of Circular u/cl. 4(3) of Regulation Order does not give power independent of cl. 10 of order - But Corporation cannot demote officer under circular - Officer claiming relief u/art. 311(2) challenging validity of order - Held, relief can be given on alternative basis that impugned order terminating service was invalid - Appeal dismissed.
