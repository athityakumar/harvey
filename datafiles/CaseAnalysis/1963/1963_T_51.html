Greaves Cotton and Company, Limited and Others v Their Workmen (Greaves Cotton and Allied

Supreme Court of India

14 November 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, K.C. Das Gupta

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 7; AIR 1964 SC 689; 1964 (8) FLR 100; 1964 (1) LLJ 342; [1964] 5 S.C.R. 362

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Whether (1) Tribunal went wrong in not following the industry-cum-region principle and in leaning on the recommendations of the tripartite conference?

- Industry-cum-region principle puts all concerns on an equal footing in the matter of production costs and competition - Tribunal was not wrong in emphasising on the region aspect of the industry-cum-region formula in so far as clerical and subordinate staff - (2) Tribunal overlooked the wages prevalent for categories which it had considered comparable? - Wage-scales fixed for factory-workmen set aside and the matter remanded to the tribunal to fix wage-scales for factory-workmen - The parties at liberty to lead further evidence - (3) The award will have retrospective effect

- Held, appeals dismissed so far as retrospective effect and adjustments as also fixation of wages and dearness allowance - Appeal allowed with respect to factory-workmen and cases sent back to the tribunal for fixing the wage-structure including basic wage and dearness allowance and for granting adjustments - The appeals with respect to gratuity is dismissed.
