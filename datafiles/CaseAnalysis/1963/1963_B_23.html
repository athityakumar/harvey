Bhagwati Prasad Sah and Others v Bhagwati Prasad Sah and Another

Supreme Court of India

10 October 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 191; [1964] 5 S.C.R. 105

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Vendor

Summary: Family & Personal - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Constitution, 1950, art. 19(1) (f) - Registration Act, s. 61 - Mohammedan law of pre-emption - Applicability to Hindus in Bihar as custom - (A)Whether right of pre- emption infringes fundamental right of a citizen u/art. 19(1) (f) of Constitution? - (B)Whether ceremonies of pre-emption being premature, having been performed before sale was completed, right of pre-emption could be enforced? - (C) Whether right of pre-emption applies to leasehold interest ? - Held, it has been held by this Court in Bhau Ram v Baij Nath (1962 Indlaw SC 540 ) that a right of preemption vis-a-vis co-Sharers was not an unreasonable restriction on fundamental right of a person to acquire, hold and dispose of property - There is no distinction sought between co-sharers who are relatives and co-sharers, who are not relatives - Further held, following decision, Court hold that law of pre-sharers does not infringe fundamental right conferred under art. 19(1)(f) of Constitution - This Court, by a majority, held in Ram Saran v Domini Kuer (1961 Indlaw SC 365 ) that registration under Registration Act is not complete till document to be registered has been copied out in records of Registration Office as provided in s. 61 of that Act - Question as to when a document was copied out in concerned register is certainly a question of fact and was not raised either before Trial Court or before first Appellate Court - No issue was framed on point - It was raised for first time before High Court - HC was certainly right in disallowing appellants from raising question of fact for first time in second appeal - It has not been disputed that Hindus in Province of Bihar came to adopt Mohammedan law of pre-emption as a custom - Being a customary law, it is not permissible for courts to extend custom beyond limits within which upto now it has been recognized - Following propositions are relevant to present enquiry: (1) right of pre-emption is simply a right of sub-situation, but not of re-purchase, i.e.the pre-emptor takes entire bargain and steps into shoes of original vendee, (2) it is a right to acquire whole of property sold and not a share of it and (3) right being a very weak right, it can be defeated by all legitimate methods, such as vendee allowing claimant of a superior or equal right being substituted in his place - A right of pre-emption is annexed to full ownership of property of co-sharers - It is not attached to property held on subordinate tenure, such as leases etc. - It is an incident of co-sharer's property operating both as a right and as a burden in different situations - It is a right of substitution taking in entire bargain - It must take whole or nothing - It does not matter if inability to take whole arises out of a voluntary act or out of a legal limitation inherent in nature of property transferred - It is reciprocal in operation, that is, if situation was reversed and vendor became pre-emptor, he should be in a position to pre-empt co- Sharer's whole bargain - Two doctrines which may, for convenience, be referred to as entire bargain and reciprocity cannot operate unless both co-sharers are full owners of their respective properties - Akar or a house standing on a freehold land is subject to right of pre-emption, but a house on a leasehold land stands on a different footing - As there is no right of pre-emption in respect of a land held on a subordinate tenure, right of pre-emption cannot be enforced against house either, as pre-emptor cannot be substituted for entire bargain - Right must fall also on ground that super-structure disannexed from land would be movable property and it is well settled that right of pre-emption cannot be enforced in respect of movables - First respondent has no right to pre-empt sale executed in favour of appellants - Decrees of Subordinate Judge's Court and HC are set aside and that of Trial Court is restored - Appeal allowed.
