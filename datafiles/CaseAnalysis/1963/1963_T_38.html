 Afzal Ullah v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

20 September 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, K. Subba Rao, Raghubar Dayal, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 182; AIR 1964 SC 264; 1964 CRLJ 156; 1964 (1) ILR(All) 385; [1964] 4 S.C.R. 991

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: United Provinces Municipalities Act 1916 (ENG)

Summary: Municipalities & Local Governments - United Provinces Municipalities Act, 1916, ss. 241, 298A(a), 298A(b), 298A(c) and 298J(d) - Validity of bye law No. 3 and other relevant bye-laws framed by respondent No. 2, Municipal Board of Tanda - Bye-laws purport to have been framed u/s. 298 A(a), (b), (c) and J(d) of Act - If on any plot, more than four stalls or shops are kept and they sell grains, they constitute a market within bye-law 3(a) - On plot belonging to appellant, more than four shops are kept and they sell grains - Thus, there can be no doubt that these shops constitute a market within bye-law 3(a) - Held, when s. 241(1) provides that right of a person to run a market as therein indicated shall be subject to bye-laws, if any, made under heading F of s. 298, Air - Further held, s. 298(2)-F deals, inter alia, with markets, and in absence of any definition of word 'market' prescribed by Act, it would be legitimate to take word 'market' occurring in s. 298(2)-F(d) in its dictionary meaning - Power to regulate establishment of markets which is specified in this clause would sustain relevant bye law framed by respondent No. 2 - Cl. (d) conferred power on respondent No. 2 to make a bye-law in regard to establishment, regulation and inspection of market such as is run on plot belonging to appellant - Impugned bye-laws in regard to markets framed by respondent No. 2 are for furtherance of municipal administration under Act, and so, would attract provisions of s. 298(1) - High Court was right in coming to conclusion that impugned bye-laws are valid - Once it is shown that impugned bye-laws are within competence of respondent No. 2, fact that Pre. to bye-laws mentions clauses which are not relevant, would not affect validity of bye-laws - Validity of bye-laws must be tested by reference to question as to whether board had power to make those bye-laws - If power is otherwise established, fact that source of power has been incorrectly or inaccurately indicated in preamble to bye-laws, would not make bye-laws invalid - Appeal dismissed.
