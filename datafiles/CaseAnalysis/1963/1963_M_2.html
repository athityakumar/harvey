 State of Orissa v M. A. Tulloch and Company

Supreme Court of India

16 August 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, K. Subba Rao, Raghubar Dayal, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 166; AIR 1964 SC 1284; [1964] 4 S.C.R. 461

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Defence & Security Forces; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Attorney-General, Repeal

Summary: Mines & Minerals - Orissa Mining Areas Development Fund Act, 1952, ss. 4z and 5 - Mines and Minerals (Regulation and Development) Act, 1957, s. 18(1)(2) - General Clauses Act, 1897, s. 6 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 246(1) - Whether extent of control and regulation' provided by Central Act takes within its fold area or subject covered by Orissa Act? - Held, since Central Act contains requisite declaration by union parliament under Entry 54 and that Act covers same field as Act of 1948 in regard to mines and mineral development, decision of court in this matter unless there were any material difference between scope and ambit of Central Act 53 of 1948 and that of Act of 1957 - Appeals allowed.
