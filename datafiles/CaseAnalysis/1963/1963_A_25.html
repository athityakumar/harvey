Associated Cement Staff Union and Another v Associated Cement Company and Others

Supreme Court of India

03 December 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

K.C. Das Gupta

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 41; AIR 1964 SC 914; 1964 (1) KerLR 212; 1964 (1) LLJ 12

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Should working hours be fixed for workmen? - Hours of work have to be fixed in consideration of many factors, including question of fatigue on health of workmen, effect on their efficiency, physical discomfort that may result from long and continuous strain, need of leisure in workmen's lives, hours of work prevailing for similar activities in same region and also in similar concerns and other relevant factors - But once a conclusion about normal working hours is reached after considering optimum working hours on a consideration of all relevant factors, industrial adjudication cannot hesitate to give effect to its conclusion merely because workmen would have been entitled to more wages at overtime rates if hours of work had been fixed at less Wage rate to be fixed first and then fix working hours accordingly - Held, figure of 36 working hours in a week tribunal has given proper weight to all relevant considerations - Further held, number of holidays should be fixed at 16 days instead of 21 days - Appeals allowed.
