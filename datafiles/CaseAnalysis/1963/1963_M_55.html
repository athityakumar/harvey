Madamanchi Ramappa and Another v Muthalur Bojjappa

Supreme Court of India

29 March 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, K.C. Das Gupta

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 341; AIR 1963 SC 1633; 1964 (1) ALT(SC) 1; [1964] 2 S.C.R. 673

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Mesne Profits

Summary: Constitution - Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 133(3) and 136 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 77 - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, s. 100 - (A) Whether Supreme Court can allow special leave petition for second appeal against judgment of single judge of High Court u/art. 133(3)? - Held, yes - Only in cases where Supreme Court is satisfied that HC has interfered with question of fact and has thus contravened limits prescribed by s. 100 of CPC - In some cases HC may be, while dealing with second appeal, inclined to take view that what it regards to be justice or equity of case has not been served by findings of fact recorded by Courts of fact - (B) Whether HC is correct in rejecting certified copy of public document which was admissible in evidence without being proved in trial court or in appellate Court ? - Further held, HC has committed error of law in rejecting such document on ground that it had not been proved - Once evidence is admissible and relevant by lower courts, it is not open to a party feeling aggrieved by findings recorded by courts of fact to contend before HC in second appeal that said evidence is not sufficient to justify findings of fact in question - Cannot be agitated in a second appeal - Appeal allowed.
