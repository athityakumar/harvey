 Ranendra Chandra Banerjee v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

18 February 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Mohammad Hidayatullah, K.C. Das Gupta, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 266; AIR 1963 SC 1552; 1964 (1) SCJ 578; [1964] 2 S.C.R. 135

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Service

Summary: Service - Constitution - Civil Services(Classification, Control and appeal) Rules, rr. 49 and 55-B - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 311(2) - Dismisssal of probationer - (A)Whether appellant entitled to the protection of art. 311 (2) of the Constitution? - Held, it is well settled that the protection of art. 311 of the Constitution applies to temporary government servants also where dismissal, removal or reduction in rank is sought to be inflicted by way of punishment - Further held, a Govt. servant who is on probation can be discharged and such discharge would not amount to dismissal or removal within the meaning of art. 311 (2) and would not attract the protection of that article where the services of a probationer are terminated in accordance with the rules and not by way of punishment - (B) Whether appellant entitled to the protection of rr. 49 and 55-B ? - Held, r. 3 (a) applicable to the appellant who was a probationer as it was in force at the relevant time unless it inapplicable in view of the term mentioned in the letter of appointment issued to him - Appeal dismissed.
