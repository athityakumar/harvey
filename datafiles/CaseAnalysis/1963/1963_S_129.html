Sur Enamel and Stampingworks Private Limited v Their Workmen

Supreme Court of India

07 May 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

K.C. Das Gupta, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 150; AIR 1963 SC 1914; 1963 (7) FLR 236; 1963 (2) LLJ 367; [1964] 3 S.C.R. 616

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Labour & Industrial - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 15 and 25 F - Power of Industrial Tribunal - Order of dismissal of workers by management for charge not alleged and enquired into - Validity - (A) Whether industrial tribunal is entitled to consider propriety or correctness of conclusions arrived at by domestic enquiry? - Held, when an industrial employee's services are terminated after a proper domestic enquiry held in accordance with rules of natural justice - Conclusions reached at enquiry are not perverse Industrial tribunal is not entitled to consider propriety or correctness of said conclusions - (B) Whether temporary workmen can claim benefit u/s. 25 F of Industrial Disputes Act? - U/s. 25 F, Industrial Disputes Act 1947, only a workmen who has been in continuous service for not less than one year under an employer is entitled to its benefit - Further held, a workman who has not at all been employed for a period of 12 months would not satisfy requirements of S. 25-B and would not be entitled to benefit u/s. 25 F - Appeal partly allowed.
