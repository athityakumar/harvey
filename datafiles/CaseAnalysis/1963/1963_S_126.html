 Sultan Brothers Private Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax

Supreme Court of India

06 December 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sarkar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Mohammad Hidayatullah, K.C. Das Gupta, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 217; AIR 1964 SC 1389; [1964] 51 ITR 353; 1964 (1) SCJ 232; [1964] 5 S.C.R. 807

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Annual Value

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 10, 12, 12(3),12(4) - Term - Income - Lease - Assessment - Appellant / limited company was owner of a certain building constructed - By a lease appellant let out building fully equipped and furnished to one Voyantzis for a term of six years certain from December 9, 1946 for running a hotel and for certain other ancillary purposes - Lease provided for a monthly rent of Rs. 5,950 for building and a hire of Rs. 5,000 for furniture and fixtures - Question in appeal was how income received as rent and hire was to be assessed, that was, under which section of 1922Act was it assessable - Appellant contended that entire income should be assessed u/s. 10 as income of a business or income should be assessed u/s. 12 as income from a residuary source that was a source not specified in preceding ss. 7 to 11, with allowances respectively specified in ss.12 (3) ,12 (4) - For assessment year 1953-54, appellant was taxed u/s. 9 of 1922 Act in respect of building and u/s. 12 in respect of hire received from furniture and fixtures - Hence instant appeal - Whether rent from building had to be assessed u/s. 12 after allowances mentioned in s. 12(4) had been deducted - Held, rent from building would be computed separately from income from furniture and fixtures and in case of rent from building appellant would be entitled to allowances mentioned in s. 12(4) and in case of income from furniture and fixtures, to those mentioned in s.12(3) and no part of income could be assessed u/s. 9 or u/s. 10 - Appeal allowed.
