 Bengal Bhatdee Coal Company v Ram Prabesh Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

23 January 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 103; AIR 1964 SC 486; 1963 (6) FLR 361; 1963 (1) LLJ 291; [1964] 1 S.C.R. 709

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 10 - Collieries - Misconduct - Disciplinary action - Dismissal - Legality - Appellant/authority dismissed 13 workmen for obstructing work and inciting other workers after holding inquiry for violation of regn. 38(1)(b) of Coal Mine Regulations and Coal Mines Standing Order - Appellant made applications before Tribunal for approval of action taken and same was approved - Central Govt referred the dispute to Tribunal u/s. 10 of the 1947 Act - Tribunal held that it was case of victimisation and set aside order of dismissal ordering reinstatement of workmen - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order of Tribunal could be sustained - Held, no inference of victimisation could be made merely from fact that punishment of dismissal was imposed in this case and not either fine or suspension - It was not in dispute that strike was going on during those days when misconduct was committed - It was case of appellant that strike was unsatisfied and illegal - It appeared that Regional Labour Commissioner agreed with this view of appellant - It was during such strike that misconduct in question took place and misconduct was that these 13 workmen physically obstructed other workmen who were willing to work from doing their work by sitting down between tramlines - This was serious misconduct on part of 13 workmen and if it was found as it had been found-proved punishment of dismissal would be perfectly justified - It should not therefore be said looking at nature of offence that punishment inflicted in this case was grossly out of proportion and the tribunal was not justified in coming to conclusion that this was case of victimization - Because appellant decided to dismiss these workmen and was not prepared to let them off with fine or suspension - Impugned award of Tribunal was set aside and dismissal of 13 workmen upheld - Appeal allowed.
