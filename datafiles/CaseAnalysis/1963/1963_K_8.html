Kale Khan Mohammad Hanif v Commissioner of Income Tax, Madhya Pradesh and Bhopal

Supreme Court of India

08 February 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sarkar, K.C. Das Gupta, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, S.K. Das

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 407; [1963] 50 ITR 1

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax &Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 66 - Whether burden of proving source of cash credits is on assessee? - If assessee disputes liability for tax, it is for him to show either that receipt was not income or is an exempted income - Held, onus of proving source of cash credit on assessee - If he disputes liability for tax, it is for him to show either that receipt was not income or that if it was, it was exempt from taxation under provisions of Act - In absence of such proof, Income tax Officer is entitled to treat it as taxable income - Source from which a particular income is derived is based on facts of case - Taxing authorities are not precluded from treating amounts of credit entries as income from undisclosed sources - Appeal dismissed.
