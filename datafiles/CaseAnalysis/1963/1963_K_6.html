 Syed Yakoob v K.S. Radhakrishnan and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 October 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, K. Subba Rao, Raghubar Dayal, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 153; AIR 1964 SC 477; [1964] 5 S.C.R. 64

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Writ Proceedings

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1939 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Constitution, 1950, art. 226 - What are limits of jurisdiction of High Courts in issuing a writ of certiorari u/art. 226? - Held, jurisdiction to issue a writ of certiorari is a supervisory jurisdiction and court exercising it is not entitled to act as an Appellate Court - This limitation necessarily means that findings of fact reached by inferior Court or Tribunal as a result of appreciation of evidence cannot be reopened or questioned in writ proceedings - What can be corrected by a writ has to be an error of law, but it must be such an error of law as can be regarded as one which is apparent on face of record - Question on which respondent No. 1 sought for intervention of HC u/art. 226 was a simple question of fact, and that question of fact, Appellate Tribunal was justified in coming to conclusion that claim made by respondent No. 1 about existence of a workshop at Chidambaram was not well-founded, but even if said finding did not appear to HC to be satisfactory, that would be no reason for issuing a writ u/art. 226 - There was evidence in support of finding of Appellate Tribunal and it is not a case where finding is based on no evidence at all - Appeal allowed.
