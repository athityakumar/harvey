 New Central Jute Mills Company Limited and Others v State of West Bengal and Others

Supreme Court of India

17 January 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

K.C. Das Gupta, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 400; AIR 1963 SC 1307; 1964 (1) MLJ(SC) 68; 1964 (1) SCJ 195; [1964] 1 S.C.R. 535

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Land & Property; Water

Summary: Land & Property - Merchant Shipping Act, 1958 - Board of Revenue Act, 1913 - Deed of mortgage executed at Lucknow in State of Uttar Pradesh - This deed was duly stamped for a value of Rs. 1,08,751 and registered in Calcutta - On 23-03-1957, by a deed executed between first petitioner and State of Uttar Pradesh a part of mortgage property in West Bengal was released and in its place and a part of some properties of Uttar Pradesh were substituted - Deed of substitution was duly stamped and registered in Uttar Pradesh - Board of Revenue held that Company was liable to pay Rs. 1,74,000/- As stamp duty on mortgage deed before it can avail of concessional rate provided for in substituted security - Whether an instrument executed in Uttar Pradesh and consequently liable to stamp duty under Indian Stamp Act as amended in Uttar Pradesh but relating to property in West Bengal bears stamps overprinted with name of West Bengal comes before a public officer of Uttar Pradesh - Is such officer right in holding that instrument is not duly stamped inasmuch as it does not bear stamps overprinted with name of Uttar Pradesh? - Held, if an instrument after becoming liable to duty in one State on execution there becomes liable to duty also in another State on receipt there, it must first be stamped in accordance with law of first State and it will not require to be further stamped in accordance with law of second State when rate of that second State is same or lower, and where rate of second State is higher, it will required to be stamped only with excess amount and that in accordance with law and rules in force in second State - Mortgage deed which is subject-matter of present petition was executed in Uttar Pradesh, though it related to property situated in West Bengal and was received in that State for registration - First dutiable event was execution, which took place in Uttar Pradesh, second dutiable event was receipt in West Bengal - When it came before officers of Uttar Pradesh for decision whether it was duly stamped or not, officers of Uttar Pradesh were bound to hold that instrument was not duly stamped as it did not bear Uttar Pradesh stamps - Petition dismissed.
