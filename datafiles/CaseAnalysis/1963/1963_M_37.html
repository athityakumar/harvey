 State of Maharashtra v Mishri Lal Tarachand Lodha Andothers

Supreme Court of India

15 November 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

Raghubar Dayal, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, K. Subba Rao, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 308; AIR 1964 SC 457; 1964 (66) BomLR 254; 1964 (1) KerLR 163; 1964 KLT 115; 1964 MahLJ 120; [1964] 5 S.C.R. 230

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Civil Procedure Code, 1908 - Bombay Court Fee Act, 1959 - Plaintiff-respondent instituted suit for recovery on account of principal lent with interest till date of suit - Suit decreed in favour of plaintiff-respondent - Appeal to High Court by defendant-appellant - High Court set aside order of taxing officer - Whether HC rightly held that amount of court-fee paid on memorandum of appeal was proper court-fee? - Provisions of taxing statute have to be construed strictly - Relevant provision governing court-fee to be paid on memorandum of appeal filed in a Civil Court is contained in art. 1 of sch. 1 of 1959 act - Amount of court-fee payable, therefore, depends on amount or value of subject-matter in dispute in appeal - Value of right claimed in suit or appeal which is covered by expression 'amount or value of subject-matter in dispute in art. 1, Schedule 1 of 1959 Act - Held, appellant had no reason to appeal against decree for costs or decree for future interest unless he disputes those amounts wholly or partially for certain reasons - Amount of pendente lite interest decreed is not to be included in 'amount or value of subject-matter in dispute in appeal' for purposes of art. 1 of sch. 1 of 1959 Act - Appeal dismissed.
