Rajabhai Abdul Rehman Munshi v Vasudev Dhanjlbhai Mody

Supreme Court of India

01 May 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, A.K. Sarkar, Mohammad Hidayatullah

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 328; AIR 1964 SC 345; 1964 (66) BomLR 210; 1964 (2) MLJ(SC) 42; 1964 (2) SCJ 196; [1964] 3 S.C.R. 480

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Constitution - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Restrictions on right to appeal for civil litigants u/art. 136 - Held, if there appears on appellants part any attempt to overreach or mislead Court by false or untrue statements or by withholding true information which would have a bearing on question of exercise of discretion, Court would be justified in refusing to exercise discretion or if discretion has been exercised, in revoking leave to appeal granted even at time of hearing of appeal - When defendant misleads Supreme Court and obtains an order for grant of special leave and also has possession of disputed property for three years - It is putting a premium on unfair conduct of defendant - Order accordingly.
