 H.H. The Maharana Sahib Shri Bhagwatsingh Bahadur v The State Of Rajasthan And Others

Supreme Court of India

03 October 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, K. Subba Rao, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Raghubar Dayal

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 313; AIR 1964 SC 444; 1966 (13) FLR 225; 1964 (1) LLJ 33; [1964] 5 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Rajasthan Industrial Tribunal (Constitution and Proceedings) Validating Act, 1959

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 -Constitution (Seventh Amendment) Act, 1956 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Rajasthan Industrial Tribunal (Constitution and Proceedings) Validating Act, 1959 - Privileges - Preliminary objections - By order of the President of India, the Maharana Sahib Shri Bhagwat Singh Bahadur hereinafter called 'the appellant' was recognised as the Ruler of Udaipur in succession to his father the late Maharana Bhupal Singh - Dispute arose between the appellant and his employees in the 'Motor Garage Department' about the conditions of employment and representations were made by the latter to the Govt. of Rajasthan through the Motor Workers Mazdoor Union, Udaipur - Rajasthan Govt. referred u/s. 10 of the 1947 Act - Whether the Maharana Sahib Bahadur of Udaipur was liable to pay to the staff working with him in the Palace Power House and Motor Garage, consequent to their retrenchment, the arrears of claims or the due salary, leave wages, overtime wages and weekly holidays and if so, to what extent. If not, to what relief the staff was entitled to under the provisions of the 1947 Act, as the question of payment of the claims had arisen with the termination of their services due to retrenchment effected by the employers - Held, it was unnecessary to consider the merits of contentions because the Legislature had remedied the defects in the Constitution of the Tribunal, by enacting the 1959 Act which was reserved for the consideration of the President of India and has received his assent - It was clear from the validating provisions that the Tribunal originally constituted u/s. 7 of the 1947 Act, before it was amended by Act 36 of 1956 was to be deemed to have been duly constituted u/s. 7A of the 1947 Act, and the reference made was to be deemed to have been made as if the Tribunal were constituted u/s. 7A of the amended Act - Validating Act was because of Item 22, List III of the Seventh Schedule to the Constitution, within the competence of the State Legislature, and it was reserved for the consideration of the President and had received his assent - It must by virtue of Art. 254(2) prevail in the State of Rajasthan - Appeal dismissed.
