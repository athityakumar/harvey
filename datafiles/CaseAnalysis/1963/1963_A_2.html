 K. Venkataramiah v A. Seetharama Reddy and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 February 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

K.C. Das Gupta, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 419; AIR 1963 SC 1526; 1963 ALJ 903; 1964 (1) MLJ(SC) 13; [1964] 2 S.C.R. 35

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Election - Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Election petition filed by appellant dismissed by election tribunal - High Court took into consideration evidence which was not legally available for such consideration in reaching its conclusion on vital question of age of respondent No. 1 on date of election - Whether it is valid? - Documentary and oral evidence was adduced before Tribunal in considering age - Contended that requirements of O. 41 r.27 was not followed - High Court not in error in taking additional evidence - Held, HC allowed additional evidence to be admitted as it required such evidence either to enable it to pronounce judgment or for any other substantial cause within meaning of r. 27(1)(b) of O. 41- On principle laid down in Jagarnath Pershad v Hanuman Pershad ,36 Ind App 221 (PC) that when additional evidence was taken with assent of both sides or without objection at time it was taken, it is not open to a party to complain of it later on, appellant cannot now be heard to say that additional evidence was taken in this case in breach of provisions of law - No interference - Appeal dismissed.
