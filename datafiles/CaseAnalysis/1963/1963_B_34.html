Bengal Nagpur Cotton Mills v Board of Revenue, Madhya Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

30 July 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, A.K. Sarkar, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 155; AIR 1964 SC 888; 1964 MPLJ 929; [1964] 4 S.C.R. 190

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Indirect Tax - Govt. of India Act, 1935 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Octroi - Whether appellant-company was exempted from operation of bye-laws of Municipality imposing octroi in terms of agreement entered with erstwhile Ruler which must be regarded as law unless set aside by other competent authority? - Municipal Committee had already imposed octroi in State but ruler ordered Municipal Committee not to collect dues from appellant-company because of agreement - Held, after State merged with State of Madhya Pradesh and Municipal Committee was not controlled in any way by Ruler or by his agreement, imposition of octroi upon appellant-company began to take effect from such date as Municipal Committee chose to determine - Further held, agreement of Ruler bound Municipal Committee only indirectly and Ruler's desire that octroi should not be collected ceased to operate from moment he ceased to be Ruler - Hence, resolution of Municipal Committee was thus in order and demand was rightly made - Appeal dismissed.
