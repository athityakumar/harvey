 Addanki Tiruvenkata Thata Desika Charyulu v State of Andhra Pradesh and Another

Supreme Court of India

07 November 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, K. Subba Rao, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 416; AIR 1964 SC 807

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Madras Estates Land (Reduction of Rent) Act, 1947 - Tamil Nadu Estates Land Act (1 of 1908) - Tamil Nadu Estates (Abolition and Conversion into Ryotwari) Act, 1948 - Madras Legislature enacted two Acts having application to particular types of estates - Govt. purporting to act under powers conferred by Rent Reduction Act appointed a Special Officer to conduct an enquiry as to precise reduction in rent payable to appellant by persons in cultivation of lands in Appellant's Shrotriem and after considering his report directed a reduction of rents by notification D/- 2-05-1950 - Whether Shrotriem village is not an 'estate' within Rent reduction Act and it is not an 'inam estate' under Abolition Act? - Held, it has been found in dealing with applicability of Rent Reduction Act, that Shrotriem was an 'inam village' and result would be that preliminary condition would have been satisfied and Settlement Officer was therefore competent to record a finding that 'inam village' of was an 'inam estate' i.e., it was a village of which land revenue without Kudivaram had been granted in inam to a person not owning Kudivaram thereof - Appeal dismissed.
