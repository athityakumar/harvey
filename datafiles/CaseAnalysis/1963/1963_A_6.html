Abdul Aziz Aminudin v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

07 February 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

Raghubar Dayal, J.R. Madholkar, K. Subba Rao, Syed Jaffer Imam

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 1; AIR 1963 SC 1470; 1963 (65) BomLR 669; 1963 CRLJ 403; 1999 (110) E.L.T. 225; 1964 (2) SCJ 128; [1964] 1 S.C.R. 830

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Trade - Import - Export - Administrative - Imports and Exports (Control) Act, 1947, ss. 3(1) and 5 - Imports (Control) order,1955, cl. 5 - (A) Whether courts can consider policy of restricting import or controlling of disposal of goods imported in any part of India u/s. 3(1)? - Held, no - Only appropriate authority can do so - (B) How imports of goods can be controlled? - Further held, import of those goods can be totally prohibited - Total prohibition is not desired, goods could be allowed to come into country in limited quantities - Empowering persons to import under licences certain fixed quantities of goods - Quantity determined on consideration of necessity - Licence amenable to orders of licensing authority - If import allowed for particular purpose but after import diverted to different uses, defeating thereby very purpose of control - So, not possible to restrict scope of provision about control of import to stage of importing of goods at frontiers of country - (C) Whether licensing authority is competent under order to impose condition that imported goods not to be sold to any person? - Held, he can do so - U/cl. 5 of Imports (Control) order,1955 licensing authority can attach a condition to effect that goods covered by licence shall not be disposed of except in manner prescribed by him is a valid provision which comes within powers conferred by s.3 of Act on Central Govt. - (D) Whether for contravening condition of licence possession of imported goods by an agent is enough? - Further held, yes - Actual possession of imported goods is not necessary - It would amount possession as good having possession to principal - Chairman of association, knowing from very beginning that association would not be able to utilise all yarn to be imported under licence - Transfering the same to a company for other work amounted to deliberate case of securing import licence with a view to mix-apply goods imported - Association would be liable to punishment under s. 5 of Act - Appeal dismissed.
