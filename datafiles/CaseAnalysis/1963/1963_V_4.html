 Kanakarathanammal v V. S. Loganatha Mudaliar and Another

Supreme Court of India

18 December 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, K. Subba Rao, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 358; AIR 1965 SC 271; [1964] 6 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Women & Children - Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Civil Procedure Code, 1908 - Mysore Hindu Law Women's Right Act, 1933, ss. 10 (2) (b) and 12 (1) (i) - Suit by appellant for recovery of possession of properties attached to plaint as she was sole heir of her dead mother - Contended by respondent no. 1 that 'will' has been executed by father of respondent under which executor has been appointed and given of possession of properties to him - Trial Judge concluded that will executed by appellant's father was invalid - Suit held bad as non-joinder of necessary parties - High Court with slight modification upheld decree of Trial Court - Whether title to property in question vested in appellant's mother or not? - Actual management of property was done by appellant's father - If property of wife is managed by husband then it cannot be said that title of wife to property is inconsistent - Held, failure to implead her brothers who would inherit property along with her makes suit incompetent - Appeal dismissed.
