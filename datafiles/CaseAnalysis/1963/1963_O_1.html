 State Of Punjab v Okara Grain Buyers Syndicate Ltd.And Ors.(And Connected Appe

Supreme Court of India

15 November 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, K. Subba Rao, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 307; AIR 1964 SC 669; [1964] 5 S.C.R. 387

Case Digest

Subject: Socio-Economic

Keywords: Displaced Persons (Institution of Suits) Act, 1948, Displaced Persons (Claims) Act, 1950

Summary: Banking & Finance - Displaced Persons (Debts Adjustment) Act, 1951, s. 13 - Displaced Persons (Claims) Act, 1950, s. 2(6) - Displaced Persons (Institution of Suits) Act, 1948 - Whether u/s. 13 of Act a displaced creditor could make a claim against Govt. either of State or of Union? - Residence could be attributed to artificial persons like firms or unincorporated associations or to corporations, no reason why notional residence could not be extended to a juristic entity like a State - State is an organised political institution which has several of attributes of a corporation - Govt. of Union and Govt. of a State are enabled to sue and be sued in name of Union of India and of Govt. of State, as case may be - It is improper to speak of Union and State as constitutional entities which have attributes defined by Constitution - Held, such a debt as an asset must be included in schedule of properties and debts owing by State to a displaced debtor ought to be ascertained for determining paying capacity of debtor and relief afforded to displaced debtor on basis that such debts due to him are realisable assets within scope of Act - It would follow that debt due by Govt. or by State is within Act - Therefore debts owing by them are within s. 13 of Act - Displaced creditors can claim from state - Appeal dismissed.
