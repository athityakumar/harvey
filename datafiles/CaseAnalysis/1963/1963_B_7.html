J. K. Cotton Spinning and Weavingmills Company, Limited v Badri Mali and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 May 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, K.C. Das Gupta

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 179; AIR 1964 SC 737; 1963 (7) FLR 289; 1963 (2) LLJ 436; [1964] 3 S.C.R. 724

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Labour Appellate Tribunal, United Provinces Industrial Disputes Act 1947 (UP 28 of 1947) (ENG)

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 2(s) - Dismissal - Claimed reinstatement & other benefits - Validity - Respondents/gardeners raised industrial dispute claiming respondent no.1 reinstatement with back wages and food allowance, weekly holidays and leave with wages etc and was referred to industrial Tribunal - Appellant opposed claim and argued that gardeners would not come under workmen and were not entitled to claim dear food allowance as G.O. dt. 6-12-1948 applied to only workmen and not gardeners - Labour Court by its Award allowed claim of reinstatement with back wages but rejected claim of respondents/ gardeners that they were workmen - Counter appeals were filed and Labour Appellate Tribunal allowed appeal of respondents holding that they were deemed to be workmen and entitled to dearness food allowance , leave with wages but denied weekly holidays and dismissed appellant's appeal challenging reinstatement - Aggrieved, appellant filed writ petition in HC and was dismissed as in fructuous as it had no territorial jurisdiction - Hence, instant appeals - Whether Orders of Lower Courts were justified - Held, as per s. 2(s) of the 1947 Act, gardeners would definitely fall under category of workmen as appellant engaged them , entrusted work, maintaining attendance and salary payment was made by appellant - Persons engaged could be called as workmen whenever they were employed to assist one or other operation incidental to main industrial even though they were not directly concerned with main operation of industry - Sweepers, bus drivers , gardeners etc could be called as workmen as they were engaged in operations which were incidentally connected to main operations of industry - Respondents looking after gardens attached to bungalows occupied by officers of industrial concern were deemed to be workmen of appellant - Respondents as workmen were entitled to benefits of scheme of dear food allowance under G.O. - Appellate Tribunal was justified in allowing claim of respondents and rejecting appellants claim - Appeals dismissed.
