Smt. Srilekha Banerjee And Others v Commissioner Of Income-tax, Bihar And Orissa.

Supreme Court of India

27 March 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, S.K. Das, A.K. Sarkar

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 343; AIR 1964 SC 697; [1963] 49 ITR 112; [1964] 2 S.C.R. 552

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income-tax Act, 1961, s. 34 - Assessment - High denomination notes - Balance cash - Burden of proof - Original assessment was assessed on income of Rs. 1,28,738 - Assessment was then re-opened u/s. 34 of the Act, and was enhanced, but subsequently on appeal, it was reduced to sum little below original assessment - Present assessment was made on second re-opening of case - High denomination notes of value of Rs. 51,000/- was encashed - Assessee claimed it was part of cash balance at head office - Explanation was not accepted as accounts had not mentioned high denomination notes and further such notes were hardly needed to pay wages to labourers - Assessee failed to prove this balance, as books of assessee had not contained entries in respect of banks - Income Tax Officer found discrepancy of nearly Rs. 50,000 in statements filed by assessee - High denomination notes were treated as profits from some undisclosed source and assessed - Appellate Assistant Commissioner and Tribunal authorities upheld order of Income Tax Officer - HC too decided question against assessee - Appellants on behalf of assessee contended that Department had issued notice u/s. 34 of the Act, thus it was incumbent on department to establish that amount in question was income which had escaped assessment - Hence, instant appeal - Whether there was difference between an ordinary assessment and additional assessment u/s. 34 of the Act - Held, HC held on above facts and circumstances that there were materials to show that Rs. 51,000 had not formed part of cash balance, and source of money not having been satisfactorily proved, Department was justified in holding it to be assessable income of the assessee from some undisclosed source - Appellants contention that case had cast special burden on Department to show that this income had escaped earlier was not binding on Court - Proceedings u/s. 34 were commenced under conditions prescribed in said section, but when proceedings were validly commenced, there was no difference between ordinary assessment and additional assessment under s. 34, and same rule as to burden of proof governed additional assessment - Appeal lacked substance; it failed, but with costs - Appeal dismissed.
