 T.Devadasan v Union of India and Another

Supreme Court of India

29 August 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

J.R. Madholkar, K. Subba Rao, Raghubar Dayal, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, Sudhi Ranjan Das

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 172; AIR 1964 SC 179; 1965 (2) LLJ 560; [1964] 4 S.C.R. 680

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Constitution - Service - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 16, 15, 32, 46 and 335 - Provision of reservation of posts made by Govt. of India in favour of SC and ST - Whether carry forward rule as modified in 1955 is unconstitutional as it destroys fundamental right guaranteed by art. 16(l) of Constitution or because it is violative of guarantee contained in art 14 of Constitution? - No provision of the Constitution or of any enactment can be so construed as to destroy another provision contemporaneously enacted therein - Held, rule violates art. 16(l) of Constitution because excessive reservation permitted by it almost destroys guarantee contained in provision - Object of a rule is to make reasonable allowance for backwardness of members of a class by reserving certain proportion of appointments to provide members of backward classes with an opportunity equal to that of members of more advanced classes in matter of appointments - Provision, contemplates reservation of appointments or posts in favour of backward classes who are not adequately represented in services - Rule permits a perpetual carry forward of unfilled reserved vacancies in two years preceding year of recruitment - Result of rule will be that reservation would become more than 50% after some time - Special provision improperly made u/art. 15(4), reservation made u/art. 16(4) beyond permissible and legitimate limits would be liable to be challenged as a fraud on Constitution - Problem of giving adequate representation to members of backward classes enjoined by art. 16(4) of Constitution is, not to be tackled by framing a general rule without bearing in mind its repercussions from year to year - Carry forward rule as modified in 1955 is unconstitutional - Petition allowed.
