Shankarlal Aggarwal and Others v Shankarlal Poddar and Others

Supreme Court of India

24 January 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, Syed Jaffer Imam, K. Subba Rao, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 395; AIR 1965 SC 507; [1965] 35 Comp Cas 1; [1964] 1 S.C.R. 717

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Corporate; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Corporate - Companies Act, 1913, ss. 183(3), 184 and 202 - Companies Act, 1956, ss. 460(4), 46 and 483 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Civil Procedure Code, 1908 - Company - Winding up - Order u/ss. 183(3) and 184, sanction to sale - Order deciding lis, after hearing - Application by creditor for staying handing over possession of property sold in auction pending appeal on order confirming winding up sale - Application disposed of on buyer's undertaking - Appeal allowed but Court ordering resale - (A) Whether administrative or judicial? - (B) Appealability of order passed in matter of winding up in the light of s. 202 of Companies Act, 1913 - (C) Whether court incompetent to order resale? - Held, order is judicial and distinction between administrative and judicial orders stated - Orders passed by District Court or by single Judge of High Court as equally appealable u/s. 202 independently of provisions of ss. 96 and 104 of CPC, 1908 and Letters Patent cl. 15 - Court is competent to order resale as person undertaking to purchase property does not thereby get right to purchase property - Appeal dismissed.
