 K. S. Ramamurthi Reddiar v Chief Commissioner, Pondicherr Y and Another

Supreme Court of India

22 January 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, K.C. Das Gupta, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 102; AIR 1963 SC 1464; [1964] 1 S.C.R. 656

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1939 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 12 and 136 - (A) Whether an appeal could lie against the order of the appellate Authority in Pondicherry? - Held, art. 136 gives power to this court to grant special leave to appeal from any judgment, decree, determination, sentence or order in any cause or matter passed or made by any court or tribunal in the territory of India - Admittedly, Pondicherry was not within the territory of India when the order was passed and therefore art. 136 would not apply to such an order - So far therefore as the appeal is concerned it must be dismissed on the authority of Masthan Sahib's case (1961 Indlaw SC 415) - (B) Whether a judicial or quasi-judicial authority outside the territory of India but within the territory under the administration of the Govt. of India can be said to be under the control of the Govt. of India? - Further Held, the control envisaged u/art. 12 is control of the functions of the authorities and it is only when the Govt. of India can control the function of an authority that it can be said that the authority is under the control of the Govt. of India - Such control is possible in the case of a purely executive or administrative authority, it is impossible in the case of a quasi-judicial or judicial authority, for in the very nature of things, where rule of law prevails, it is not open to the Govt.,to direct a quasi-judicial or judicial authority to decide a particular matter before it in a particular manner - Appellate authority being a quasi-judicial authority would thus not be under the control of the Govt. of India within the meaning of art. 12 - Appeal dismissed.
