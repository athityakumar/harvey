Union of India v Ram Charan and Others

Supreme Court of India

30 April 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

Raghubar Dayal, K. Subba Rao, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 329; AIR 1964 SC 215; 1964 (2) SCJ 324; [1964] 3 S.C.R. 467

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. XXII, r. 4 - Supreme Court Rules, 1950, O. XIII, r. 2 - Appellant in merely said that deceased respondent died on 21-07-1957, and that Shri Bhatia, Divisional Engineer, learnt about it on 3-02-1958 - Shri Bhatia did not say anything more in his affidavit and did not verify it on basis of his personal knowledge If he came to know of death on 3-02-1958 - He was best person to say that this statement was true to his knowledge, rather than true to his belief Further application came up for bearing before Learned Single judge and at that time stand taken by it was that limitation for such an application starts not from date of death of respondent but from date of appellant's knowledge of death of respondent limitation for an application to set aside abatement of a suit does start on death of deceased respondent - Held - HC cannot be said to have taken an erroneous view about appellant's not establishing sufficient ground for not making an application to bring on record representatives of deceased respondent within time or for not making an application to set aside abatement within time - Appeal dismissed.
