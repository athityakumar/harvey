 Sree Mohan Chowdhury. v The Chief Commissioner, Union Territory Of Tripura

Supreme Court of India

29 April 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, K. Subba Rao, J.C. Shah, Raghubar Dayal, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 142; AIR 1964 SC 173; 1964 CRLJ 132; [1964] 3 S.C.R. 442

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Defence & Security Forces; Land & Property; Service

Keywords: Defence Regulations, Repeal

Summary: Constitution - Defence & Security - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 32, 123(1), 352 and 359 - Defence Of India Act, 1962,s. 48 - Defence Of India Rules, 1962, rr. 2(11)and 30 - Whether suspension of right to move court for enforcememnt of fundamental right u/arts. 21 and 22 during emergency by virtue of r. 30 of Defence of India rule is constitutionally valid? - A proper construction of provisions of s. 48 of Act, which has replaced Ordinances,read in light of provisions of s. 8 of General Clauses Act leaves no room for doubt that detention order passed against petitioner was intended to be continued even after repeal of Ordinances which were incorporated in 1962 Act - That being so, Order of President must have effect of suspending petitioner's right to move this Court for a writ of habeas corpus u/art. 32 - After petitioner had been deprived, for time being, of his right to move this Court, it is manifest that he cannot raise any questions as regards vires of Ordinances or of Rules and Orders made thereunder - Therefore, application is held to be not maintainable - Application dismissed.
