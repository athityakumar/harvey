 Valjibhai Muljibhai Soneji and Another v State of Bombay (Now Gujarat) and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 May 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

J.R. Madholkar, S.K. Das, K. Subba Rao, Raghubar Dayal, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 376; AIR 1963 SC 1890; 1964 ALJ 639; [1964] 3 S.C.R. 686

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1939 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Land Acquisitions Act, 1894, ss. 3(31), 4 and 6 - Whether State Govt. for acquiring piece of land belonging to respondent no. 2 for purpose of constructing a bus depot required by State Transport Corp and for constructing office and other buildings by said corp is valid and does not violate any provision of Land Acquisition Act? - Relied on Somawanti's case 1962 Indlaw SC 405 - S. 6(1), proviso clearly precludes Govt. from making a notification u/sub-s. (1) of s. 6 unless compensation to be awarded for such property (a) is to be paid by a company, or (b) is to come wholly or partly our of (i) public revenues or (ii) some fund controlled or managed by a local authority - Held, unless it is shown that State Transport Corporation is an 'authority' and is legally entitled to or entrusted by Govt. with control or management of a local fund it cannot be regarded as a local authority - No material has been placed from which it could be deduced that funds of Corporation can be regarded as local funds - State Transport Corporation having been incorporated by an Indian law is a Company - Acquisition will be bad because provisions of Part VII of Land Acquisition Act have not been complied with - Further held, acquisition impugned in this case having been made for benefit of Corporation, though for public purpose, is bad because no part of compensation is to come out public revenues and provision of part II of Land Acquisition Act have not been complied with - Appeal allowed.
