Sri Athmanathaswami Devasthanam v K. Gopalaswami Aiyangar

Supreme Court of India

09 May 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

Raghubar Dayal, K. Subba Rao, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 271; AIR 1965 SC 338; 1964 (1) MLJ(SC) 42; 1964 (1) SCJ 105; [1964] 3 S.C.R. 763

Case Digest

Subject: Election; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 133 - Tamil Nadu Estates Land Act, 1908, s.3(16) - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, s. 9- Tamil Nadu Hindu Religious Endowment, Act, 1927, s. 76 - Appellant sued respondent for recovery of a sum as damages for use and occupation of lands in suit for Faslis - The respondent was let into possession of land by a previous trustee of Devasthanam when he was being pressed by State authorities for reclaiming land and putting it to cultivation - (A) Whether waste lands covered with shrubs, jungle and like can be regarded as uncultivable merely on that account or on account of their being not cultivated for a long time? - Land which can be brought under cultivation without any undue expenditure of money and labour is cultivable land unless some provision of law provides for holding it otherwise in certain circumstances - Held, waste lands covered with shrubs, jungle and like cannot be held to be uncultivable merely on that account or on account of their being not cultivated for a long time - (B) Whether lease of land in favour of respondent is valid in view of provisions of s. 76 of Endowments Act as Board had not sanctioned lease? - The mere fact that s. 6 of Act confers such a right on a person admitted to a ryoti land, does not make letting of land to such a person equivalent to grant of a lease to him for a term exceeding 5 years, and as such requiring previous sanction of Board - Held,the letting of land to respondent did not amount to leasing of land to him for a term exceeding 5 years and that therefore required no sanction of Board and that letting of land to respondent is valid and good in law -(C) Whether High Court could not have dealt with cross-objection filed by appellant with respect to adjustment of certain amount paid by respondent when Civil Court had no jurisdiction over suit? - Further held, when Court had no jurisdiction over the-subject matter of suit it cannot decide any question on merits - It can simply decide on question of jurisdiction and coming to conclusion that it had no jurisdiction over matter had to return plaint - Appeal dismissed.
