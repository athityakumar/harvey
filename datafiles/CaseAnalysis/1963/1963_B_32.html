 State of Assam and Another v Bimal Kumar Pandit

Supreme Court of India

12 February 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, J.C. Shah, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, K.C. Das Gupta, Mohammad Hidayatullah

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 12; AIR 1963 SC 1612; 1963 (1) LLJ 295; 1964 (2) SCJ 528; [1964] 2 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Constitution - Service - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 311(2) - (A) Whether dismissing authority should indicate in second notice its concurrence with conclusions of enquiry officer before issuing notice u/art. 311(2) of Constitution? - Held, yes - But failure does not necessarily justify conclusion that notice does not afford a reasonable opportunity to delinquent officer and amounts to a contravention of art. 311(2) - If dismissing authority differs from findings of enquiry report, it must be stated in notice - But when it issues notice after accepting enquiry report and a copy of enquiry report was also enclosed along with notice - It indicates,the findings recorded by inquiring officer had been accepted - So it would not be reasonable to accept view that civil servant concerned had no reasonable opportunity as required by art. 311(2) - (B) What does reasonable opportunity contemplated by art. 311(2) indicate? - Further held, a public officer against whom disciplinary proceedings are intended to be taken is entitled to have two opportunities before disciplinary action is finally taken against him - If findings in report are against him and dismissing authority agrees - A stage of issuing second notice is reached - Second opportunity enables public officer to cover whole ground and to plead that no case had been made out against - Petition dismissed - Appeal allowed.
