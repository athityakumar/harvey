Guramma Bhratar Chanbasappa Deshmukh and Another v Malappa

Supreme Court of India

19 August 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, Raghubar Dayal, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 371; AIR 1964 SC 510; 1964 (66) BomLR 284; [1964] 4 S.C.R. 497

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Land & Property

Keywords: Posthumous Son

Summary: Land & Property - Adoption - Validity of - (A) Whether adoption of defendant 3 by plaintiff was void as it was made at a time when defendant 4 had already been conceived? - Held, in case of adoption, it secures mainly spiritual benefit to father and power to adopt is conferred on him to achieve that object - Scope of power must be reasonably construed so as to enable donee of power to discharge his religious duty - Such being significance of adoption, its validity shall not be made to depend upon contingencies that may or may not happen - Existence of a son in embryo does not invalidate an adoption - (B) Whether alienations in favour of defendants 2, 5, 6, 7 and 8 are binding on members of family? - Further held, a managing member of family has power to alienate for value joint family property either for family necessity or for benefit of estate - In instant case impugned alienation were made at a time when 4th defendant was in womb - If alienations were made by father for a purpose not binding on estate, they would be voidable at instance of 3rd or 4th defendant - Father or his representative can make a valid gift, by way of reasonable provision for maintenance of daughter, regard being had to financial and other relevant circumstances of family - It is a moral obligation and it continues to subsist till it is discharged - Court has no doubt that in present case, gift made by father was within his right and certainly reasonable - Gift deed is valid to extent of right conferred on 8th defendant - (C) What is share of an adopted son of sudra in competition with natural born son? - Further held, share of an adopted son in competition with a natural born son among Sudras has always been 1/5th in family property, i.e.,1/4th of natural born son's share - Nothing has been placed before Court to compel to depart from long established rule prevalent in Bombay State - Appeals dismissed.
