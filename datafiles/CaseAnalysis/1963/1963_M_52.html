Municipal Council Palai v T.J. Joseph and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 February 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

J.R. Madholkar, K. Subba Rao, Raghubar Dayal

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 109; AIR 1963 SC 1561; 1964 (2) SCJ 759; [1964] 2 S.C.R. 87

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Travancore Cochin Motor Vehicles Act 1125

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1939 - Travancore Cochin Motor Vehicles Act, 1125, ss. 72, 286 and 287 - Whether provisions of ss. 286 and 287 of Travancore District Municipalities Act stood repealed by implication by virtue of provisions of s. 72 of Travancore-Cochin Motor Vehicles Act, 1125 M E ? - Held, bearing in mind fact that provisions of s. 72 of Travancore Cochin Motor Vehicles Act were intended to apply to a much wider area than those of ss. 286 and 287 of Travancore District Municipalities Act it cannot be said that s. 72 was intended to replace those provisions of Travancore District Municipalities Act - Proper way of construing two sets of provisions would be to regard s. 72 of Travancore-Cochin Motor Vehicles Act as a provision in continuity with ss. 286 and 287 of Travancore District Municipalities Act so that it could be availed of by appropriate authority as and when it chose - Intention of legislature appears to be to allow two sets of provisions to co-exist because, both are enabling ones- Where such is position, we cannot imply repeal - A provision which is added subsequently, that is, which represents latest will of legislature will have an overriding effect on earlier provision in sense that despite fact that some action has been taken by Municipal Council by resorting to earlier provision, appropriate authority may nevertheless take action u/s. 72 of T C Motor Vehicles Act, result of which would be to override action taken by Municipal Council u/s. 287 of District Municipalities Act - No action u/s. 72 has so far been taken by Govt. and, therefore, resolutions of municipal Council still hold good - Appeal allowed.
