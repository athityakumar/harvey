Ram Ran Bijai Singh and Others v Behari Singh Alias Bagandha Singh

Supreme Court of India

25 April 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, K.C. Das Gupta

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 140; AIR 1965 SC 524; 1965 (1) SCJ 83; [1964] 3 S.C.R. 363

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Bihar Land Reforms Act, 1950, ss. 3, 4 and 6 - Bihar Tenancy Act, 1885 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, O. 20 r. 12 - Plaintiffs' ancestors had executed a rehan bond in favour of 3rd and 4th defendant parties in 1897 and 1907 respectively - These lands were in possession of defendants 1st and 2nd parties who claimed title asserting that suit lands were not zeraiti lands in regard to which plaintiff as maliks would be entitled to 'khas possession', but were raiyat-lands from which they, tenants in cultivation could not be evicted - Whether suit which was in substance one for ejectment based on title of plaintiffs should fail and be dismissed because plaintiffs had lost their title to property which vested in State by virtue of notification u/s. 3 of Act with consequence specified in s. 4 and whether it was saved by s. 6? - Held, it was only lands in 'khas possession' of proprietor that were saved from vesting in possession in State u/s. 6 - U/s. 6(1)(c), zeraiti land of a proprietor would be deemed to be in his 'khas possession' if it were subject-matter of a usufructuary mortgage subsisting on 01-01-1955 and mortgagor had a right to recover possession of same - This is not a case of a mortgagee remaining in possession after payment of debt without anything more but of tenants who claimed right to retain possession of property by asserting a title which was as much against mortgagee as against mortgagors - Tenancies in zerait land in suit and whatever tenancies might have been created by them during their possession, ipso facto came to an end when mortgage was redeemed by plaintiffs in 1941 - Possession of defendants 1st party or defendant 2nd party became that of a trespasser as against plaintiffs on redemption of rehan in 1941, and suit having been instituted within 12 years from date of redemption, suit is not barred by limitation and plaintiffs are entitled to recover khas possession - It is not possible for appellants to contend that these tenants were in possession of property on behalf of mortgagor and in character of their rights being derived from mortgagor - S. 6(1)(c) cannot, in terms, therefore apply since mortgagor-mortgagee relationship did not subsist on 1955 - Appeal dismissed.
