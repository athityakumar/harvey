Subodh Gopal Bose v Ajit Kumar Haldar and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 May 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 148; AIR 1967 SC 498; 1964 (1) SCJ 71; [1964] 3 S.C.R. 590

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Bengal Tenancy Act, 1885 - Court Fees Act, 1870 - Bengal Land Revenue Sales (West Bengal Amendment) Act, ss. 4 and 37 - Bengal Land Revenue Sales Act - Whether suit when instituted was a good one in view of provisions of s. 37 of Act, and that s. 4 of Amending Act, which amended s. 37 of main Act would not govern present controversy for two reasons, namely, (1) that delivery of possession had already been given to plaintiff in execution of decree of Trial Court in his favour, and that, therefore, controversy had been finally closed in his favour and (2) because s. 4 was not in terms retrospective? - Held, it is true that s. 4 begins with words For Section 37 of said Act, following section shall be substituted and then follow terms of section, as it is now - Prima facie, therefore, it is prospective in its operation - But when we look to provisions of s. 7, it becomes abundantly clear, as rightly pointed out by High Court, that section was retrospective in so far as it was made applicable to pending litigations - Present suit is one for ejectment in pursuance of s. 37 of Act XI of 1859 - Hence,s. 7(1) (a) comes into operation - Amending Act came into force on 15-03-1950 - S. 4 grants relief to tenure-holders even in respect of revenue sales held before that date, if provisions of s. 7 which give retrospective operation as aforesaid to substantive provisions of Amending Act, which had extensively cut down rigours of old s. 37 are attracted - Present suit, which was pending in HC on date Amending Act came into force, will come within purview of s. 7(1)(a) - Suit pending in HC on appeal had abated on 15-03-1950, u/s. 7(1)(a) as soon as Amending Act came into force - Appeal dismissed.
