Management of Express Newspapers Limited v B. Somayajulu and Others

Supreme Court of India

18 April 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, K.C. Das Gupta

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 134; AIR 1964 SC 279; 1963 (7) FLR 246; 1963 (2) LLJ 385; 1964 (2) MLJ(SC) 48; 1964 (2) SCJ 203; [1964] 3 S.C.R. 100

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Working Journalists (Conditions of Service) and Miscellaneous Provisions Act, 1955, Industrial Employment (Standing Order ) Act, 1946

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Working Journalists Industrial Disputes Act, 1955, s. 2(b) - Termination - Industrial dispute - Validity - Respondent raised industrial dispute as he was terminated from service by appellant demanding reinstatement with full back wages - Labour Court dismissed respondents claim holding that part time worker could not be regarded as working journalist - On appeal, allowed by HC holding that respondent was working journalist u/s. 2(b) of the Act - Hence, instant appeal - Whether respondent was working journalist - Held, as per s. 2(b) of the Act two conditions should be satisfied to hold journalists as working journalists - First condition was that his principal avocation should be journalist and second condition was that he must be employed in relation to newspaper establishment - Respondent should prove that his principal avocation was journalist and gains made from it compared with gains made from other sources - Labour Court erred in holding that respondent as part time employee was not working journalist - HC also erred in holding that respondent was employee as he did not adduce evidence that journalism was his principal avocation - Respondent could be treated as working journalist only if he satisfied two tests prescribed by s. 2(b) of the Act - Respondent should prove that he was in exclusive employment as was disputed by appellant - Order of HC was set aside - Matter remanded to Labour Court for fresh enquiry to decide whether respondent was working journalist when he was terminated - Appeal allowed.
