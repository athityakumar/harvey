Workmen of Dewan Tea Estate and Others v Management

Supreme Court of India

25 November 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, K.C. Das Gupta

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 209; AIR 1964 SC 1458; 1964 (8) FLR 167; 1964 (1) LLJ 358; [1964] 5 S.C.R. 548

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Closure, Industrial Employment (Standing Order ) Act, 1946

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s.25C - Industrial Employment (Standing Order) Act, 1946 - Uttar Pradesh Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Lay-off - Compensation - Standing Order - Inherent right - Industrial dispute between respondent /Management and appellants/ workmen - Appellants raised dispute against respondent in regard to lay-off declared by them in estates - Workmen employed by respondent had been promised continuous work throughout year and declaration of lay off for 45 days exposed them to risk of semi-starvation - Lay-off was justified and alleged that appellants were not entitled to anything more than compensation prescribed by s.25C of 1947 Act - Hence, instant appeal - Whether Tribunal was justified in holding that s.25C to declare lay off for reasons specified in relevant clause of Standing Order - Held, that Tribunal detained that relevant Standing Order justified the lay off - Standing Order was general in terms could be relied upon by respondent in support of its plea that lay off was justified - There was justification for lay off - Tribunal was right in holding that s.25C recognizes inherent right of employer to declare lay off for reasons - Tribunal ordered lay off was justified companies should pay their workmen full wages and not merely compensation prescribed by s.25C of 1947 Act - Workmen were not entitled to full wages for period of the lay off - Tribunal answered in favour of appellants - Tribunal that lay off declared by respondent for 45 days in 1959 was justified - Appeal allowed.
