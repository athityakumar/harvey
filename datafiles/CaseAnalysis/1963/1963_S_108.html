 Babu Lal v State of Uttar Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

18 September 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, K. Subba Rao, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Raghubar Dayal

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 181; AIR 1964 SC 725; 1964 CRLJ 555; 1964 (2) SCJ 424; [1964] 4 S.C.R. 957

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure 1898

Summary: Criminal - Criminal Procedure Code, 1898 ss. 195(1)(b), 195(1)(c), 467 to 479 and 479 A - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 471 - Issue: Question relating to operation of s. 479 A - Whether offence penalised u/s 471 IPC can never be covered by s. 479 A(1)? - Phraseology used in s. 479 A plain and unambiguous, it excludes jurisdiction of Court to proceed u/ss. 476 to 479, in offences specified in s. 195(1)(b) and (c) only where a person appearing before court as witness has intentionally given false evidence in any stage of judicial proceeding or intentionally fabricated false evidence for purpose of being used in any stage of judicial proceeding - Held, offences of forgery and of fabricating false evidence for purpose of using it in a judicial proceeding are distinct and forgery is not included within description of fabricating false evidence for purpose specified in s. 479 A - Offence penalised u/s. 471 IPC can never be covered by s. 479 A(1) - Therefore, for taking proceeding against person who is found to have used a false document dishonestly or fraudulently in any judicial proceeding, resort may only be had to s. 476 - Further held, exclusive operation of s. 479 A was restricted to offence of intentionally giving false evidence in any stage of judicial proceeding - Appeal dismissed.
