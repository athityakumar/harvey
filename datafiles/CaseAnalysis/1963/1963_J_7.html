 State of Punjab v Jagdip Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 September 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, J.R. Madholkar, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 314; AIR 1964 SC 521; 1966 (1) LLJ 749; [1964] 4 S.C.R. 964

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Reduction In Rank

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 311(2) - States Re-organization Act, 1956, s. 116 - Appointment of Tahsildars - Challenging a notification made by the govt. de-confirming the petitioners from permanent posts of Tahsildars and according to them the rank of officiating Tehsildars - Supernumerary posts were created - The States were re organized and the 'deconfirmation' was done by the successor State - The respondents were never validly confirmed in their posts as Tahsildars, no question of the validity of the abolition of substantive posts held by the officers appointed to them could arise - The creation of supernumerary posts appears to be an afterthought and is of no avail as a means of validating the original order of confirmation - Effect of a void order of confirmation - When an order is void on the ground that the authority which made it had in power to make it cannot give rise to any legal rights - Govt. servant has no right to a post or to a particular status, though an authority under the govt. acting beyond its competence had purported to give that person a status which it was not entitled to give he will not in law be deemed to have been validly appointed to the post or given the particular status - Statute empowers the successor govt., which would be the competent authority, to make the kind of notification as in the present case - Held, appeals dismissed.
