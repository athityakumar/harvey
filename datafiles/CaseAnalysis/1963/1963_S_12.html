Sardar Sardul Singh v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

18 March 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 119; AIR 1965 SC 682; 1965 CRLJ 608; 1964 (2) SCJ 771; [1964] 2 S.C.R. 378

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: (A)Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, ss. 120A, 120B, 409 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872 - Empire Conspiracy case - Fraud - Conviction - Sustainability -Deceased group purchased controlling shares of firm X from Y Group and agreed to pay latter Rs. 5,00,000/- as 'black money' and pay balance of Rs. 28,39000 on or before 10-1-1949 - After deceased Group became directors of X, they paid said amount from and out of funds of X - To cover that fraud Deceased group on 5-1-1950 purchased controlling shares of another firm Y (Empire) from Ramsharan Group and consideration thereof was paid from and out of money raised on securities of X - Having purchased controlling shares of Y firm , Shankarlal and his colleagues got their nominees, accused nos. 7, 8 and 9 as directors and accused no. 10 as Secretary of Y firm - Thus, Shankarlal and his co-conspirators conspired together and lifted large amounts of Y and put them into X coffers to cover up loss caused to it by their fraud - Trial Court convicted six of 10 accused and acquitted four - On appeal by State, HC convicted three of the acquitted, accused nos. 7,8,9 - Hence, instant appeals by accused nos. 6 to 10 - Whether conviction of accused no. 6/appellant could be sustained - Held, accused no. 6 was close associate of Shankarlal in political field - There was evidence to show that out of 63,000 shares of X purchased by Shankarlal Group, 4,475 were allotted to accused no. 6 - It was clear that accused no. 6 though ex facie he was neither director nor office bearer in X, had heavy stakes in it - Both courts on aforesaid evidence came to conclusion that accused no. 6 was member of conspiracy and it should not be said that there was no evidence on which courts could have come to conclusion to which they did - It did not appear that he took major part in Empire conspiracy, though he was certainly in it - In the circumstances, sentence of 3 years' rigorous imprisonment would equally suffice in his case - Therefore, SC modified sentence passed on him and sentenced him to undergo rigorous imprisonment for 3 years - Appeal dismissed.

(B)Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120B, 409 - Conspiracy - Conviction - Sentence - Challenge - Whether conviction of accused no. 7/appellant could be sustained - Held, accused no. 7, Managing Director of Y firm, was party to conspiracy and that HC rightly convicted him under s. 120-B IPC - Having regard to evidence in instant case, he should be given comparatively less punishment than his co-conspirators - Though he took part in conspiracy, for one reason or other, he took necessary proceedings to bring to light the fraud - Therefore, it would meet ends of justice if accused was sentenced to rigorous imprisonment for period of two years - Sentence passed by HC accordingly modified - Appeal dismissed.

(C)Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120B, 409 - Conspiracy - Conviction - Sutainability - Whether conviction of accused no. 8 could be sustained - Held, accused became director of Y as member of conspiracy and helped to put through all the transactions necessary to transfer funds from one Company to other - He was rightly convicted by HC - No reason to interfere with sentence passed against him - Appeal dismissed.

(D)Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120B, 409 - Conspiracy - Conviction - Sutainability - Whether conviction of accused no. 9 could be sustained - Held, accused no. 9 was creature of Deceased and was party to diversion of funds of Y to X that when accused no. 7, for his own reasons, was taking steps to stop rot, he, along with accused no. 8, obstructed him from doing so and wholly supported accused no. 10 - Only reasonable hypothesis on evidence was that he was party to conspiracy - Appeal dismissed.

(E)Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120B, 409 - Conspiracy - Misappropriation - Conviction - Sustainability - Whether conviction of accused no. 10 could be sustained - Accused no. 10 carried out resolutions of Board of Director of Y through intricate channels to enable large amounts misappropriated to reach X firm - He was connecting thread passing through web of conspiracy from beginning to end - He was active participant in conspiracy - Appeal dismissed.
