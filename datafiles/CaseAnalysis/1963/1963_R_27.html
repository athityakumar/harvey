 Ramnarayan Mor and Another v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

16 December 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, K. Subba Rao, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, Mohammad Hidayatullah

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 360; AIR 1964 SC 949; 1964 CRLJ 44; [1964] 5 S.C.R. 1064

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Committal Proceedings - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 173 (4) and 207-A - Whether Magistrate, before committing case to Sessions Court, can ask the accused to explain circumstances against him from documents furnished u/s. 173(4)? - Held, yes - (majority view)The object of these provisions is manifestly to give full information to accused about prosecution case - If Magistrate is of opinion that it is necessary in interest of justice to take evidence of other witnesses he may take such evidence, accused having liberty to cross-examine all such witnesses - There is nothing in Code to prevent examination, if in course of hearing arguments, Magistrate entertains opinion that such examination may be necessary in interests of justice for purpose of enabling accused to explain any circumstances appearing against him - Further held, Magistrate has power if necessary to examine accused for purpose of enabling him to explain any circumstances against him - Appeal dismissed.
