 Ram Sarup v Union of India and Another

Supreme Court of India

12 December 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

Raghubar Dayal, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 223; AIR 1965 SC 247; 1965 CRLJ 236; 1964 (2) SCJ 619; [1964] 5 S.C.R. 931

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces

Keywords: Attorney-General

Summary: Defence & Security Forces - Army Act, 1950 ,ss. 69 , 153 - Indian Penal Code,1860, s. 302 - Constitution of India, 1950, art.14,22(1) - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Petitioner/sepoy was charged under 3 charges for killing 2 sepoys and was tried by General Court Martial - General Court Martial found petitioner guilty of all charges and sentenced him to death - On appeal, Central Govt. confirmed findings and sentence awarded by General Court Martial - Hence, instant writ petition - Whether writ petition was maintainable - Held, petitioner's claim that respondents did not allow him to engage civil lawyer of his choice could not be accepted as seen from his affidavit and hence he was not deprived of his fundamental rights - Appeals were not maintainable from orders of highest authority prescribed u/s. 164(2) of the 1950 Act - Petition could not be entertained as central Govt. being highest authority u/s. 164(2) of the 1950 Act had confirmed sentence passed by General Court Martial - Petition dismissed.
