Kunju Kesavan v M. M. Philip I. C. S. and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 May 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, A.K. Sarkar, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 270; AIR 1964 SC 164; 1963 KerLJ 962; 1963 (2) KerLR 238; [1964] 3 S.C.R. 634

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Election; Land & Property

Summary: Constitution - Land &Property - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 133(1) - Travancore Ezhava Act, ss. 2 and 32 - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, O. 6, r. 2, O. 14, r. 1 - (A) How is a property valued in a suit for redemption of otti? - Held, suit for redemption of an otti and for possession- Not required to value his plaint on real or market value of property but on price for redemption - Plaintiff was not concluded by valuation given in plaint - If value of property as found by High Court at two material times is above mark - Certificate by HC is properly granted - (B) Whether impartibility can be imposed with respect of makkathayam property? - Further held, there is nothing in Travancore Ezhava Act to show that impartibility was rule in respect of makkathayam property - Where by a gift deed donor gave some of his properties to his wife and other to his grandson - He divided his properties between his wife and grandson and showed a contrary intention within meaning of s. 32 - Intention manifested that each was to take exclusively - Appeal dismissed.
