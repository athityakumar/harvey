Aluminium Corporation v Their Workmen and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 August 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

K.C. Das Gupta

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 162; 1963 (7) FLR 354; 1963 (2) LLJ 629; [1964] 4 S.C.R. 429

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Rehabilitation, Available Surplus

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Rehabilitation charges and bonus - Entitlement - (A) Whether Tribunal was justified in rejecting claim for rehabilitation? - Unless employer has by proper evidence established its claim to some amount as rehabilitation charge claim must be rejected - Nothing in witness's evidence to substantiate question of multiplier and divisor - Not clear that appellant have special skill in matter of replacement of machinery - (B) Whether tribunal has correctly awarded workmen bonus equivalent to three months' basic wages? - Calculations of reserves used as working capital contains arbitrary figures - Mere statements in balance-sheet as regards current assets and current liabilities cannot be taken as sacrosanct - Correctness of figures in balance-sheet are not established - For determining amount of bonus to be paid wage bill of workmen has to be considered - Held, tribunal committed no error in fixing bonus figures on this basis - Appeals dismissed.
