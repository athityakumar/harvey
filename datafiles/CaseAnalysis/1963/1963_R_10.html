Raizada Topandas and Another v Messrs Gorakhram Gokalchand

Supreme Court of India

22 April 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

S.K. Das, A.K. Sarkar, Mohammad Hidayatullah

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 379; AIR 1964 SC 1348; 1964 (66) BomLR 106; 1964 (2) SCJ 814; [1964] 3 S.C.R. 214

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Rent Control

Summary: Land & Property - Bombay Rents, Hotel and Lodging House Rates Control Act, 1947, s. 28 - Whether in a suit relating to possession of premises as between a landlord and his tenant, Court of Small Causes, Bombay, and not Civil Court, alone had jurisdiction to try suit? - Held, section provides that no court other than a Court of Small Causes shall have jurisdiction to deal with any claim or question arising under Act concerning properties in Greater Bombay - It is important to note here that this part of section does not purport to affect any court's jurisdiction to entertain and try a suit but it only prevents a court from dealing with certain claims or questions - Therefore, a court may try a suit in so far as it does not thereby have to deal with a claim or question arising out of Act - When one has regard to provisions in Part II it seems reasonably clear that exclusive jurisdiction conferred by s. 28 is really dependent on an existing or previous relationship of landlord and tenant and on claims arising under Act as between such parties - A suit which is essentially one between landlord and tenant does not cease to be such a suit merely because defendant denies claim of plaintiff - City Civil Court had jurisdiction to entertain present suit - Appeal dismissed.
