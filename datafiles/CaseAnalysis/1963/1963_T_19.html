New Marine Coal Company (Bengal) Private Limited v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

05 April 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, K.C. Das Gupta

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 144; AIR 1964 SC 152; [1964] 2 S.C.R. 859

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Criminal; Defence & Security Forces; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Constitution - Contract & Commercial - Tort - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 299 - Govt. of India Act, 1935, s. 175(3) - Contract Act, s. 70 - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, O. 6 r. 4, O. 41 - Evidence Act 1872, s. 115 - (A) Whether u/s. 70 of Contract Act, Govt. would be liable to pay compensation for void contract u/s. 175(3) of Govt. of India Act, 1935 if other party in pursuance of said void contract has performed his part and Govt. of India has received benefit of performance of contract? Held, no - S. 70 would not apply in this case - (B) Whether plea of negligence in defence can be raised for first time in appeal? - Should be raised by Defendant in its pleadings - If a plea of negligence was not raised in Trial Court, and allowing in appeal to raise this would be unfair with appellant -(C) When is plea of estoppel on ground of negligence available?- Further held, negligence is not understood in popular language or in common sense - It has a technical denotation - It must be shown that party against whom plea is raised owed a duty to party who raises plea, or towards general public of which he is one - Negligence must be established in this technical sense - Negligence on which it is based should not be indirectly or remotely connected with misleading effect assigned to it, but must be proximate or real cause of that result - It must be in transaction itself and it should be so connected with result to which it led that it is impossible to treat two separately - Appeal allowed.
