Darya Singh and Others v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

25 April 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, K.C. Das Gupta

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 377; AIR 1965 SC 328; 1965 CRLJ 350; 1964 (2) SCJ 319; [1964] 3 S.C.R. 397

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Criminal Procedure Code, 1898, ss. 367 and 540 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 3 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 300 and 302 - Appellants tried for murder - Validity of statement of eye witnesses - Infirmity in examination of witnesses - (A)Whether in a trial for offence of murder where statement of a witness is a close relation of victim and is shown to share victim's hostility would be accepted as evidence? - Held, If criminal Court is satisfied that witness who is related to victim was not a chance witness, then his evidence has to be examined from point of view of probabilities and account given by him as to assault has to be carefully scrutinized - If a witness is shown to be a relative of deceased and that he shared hostility of victim towards assailant, it is not a reason to reject evidence -(B) When does power of Court arise u/s. 540 of CrPC,1898 ? - If at trial it is shown that persons who had witnessed incident have been deliberately kept back, Court may draw an inference against prosecution and may, in a proper case, regard the, failure of prosecutor to examine said witnesses as constituting a serious infirmity in proof of prosecution case - Further held, in cases where examination of witness constitutes serious infirmity may exercise power conferred on it by s. 540 of CrPC, 1898 - Appeal dismissed.
