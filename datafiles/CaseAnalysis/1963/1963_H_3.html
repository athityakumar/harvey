Vora Abbasbhai Alimahomed v Haji Gulamnabi Haji Safibhai

Supreme Court of India

22 October 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, A.K. Sarkar, Raghubar Dayal

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 196; AIR 1964 SC 1341; 1964 (5) G.L.R. 55; [1964] 5 S.C.R. 157

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Ejectment Suit, Court Of First Instance

Summary: Rent Control - Transfer of Property Act, 1882 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, s. 115 - Bombay Rent, Hotel and lodging House Rates Control Act, 1947 - Whether High Court had jurisdiction to set aside order of District Court in exercise of its powers u/s. 115 of CPC? - Held, HC may exercise its powers in revision only if it appears that in a case decided by a Subordinate Court in which no appeal lies thereto Subordinate Court has exercised a jurisdiction not vested in it by law or has failed to exercise a jurisdiction so vested or has acted in exercise of its jurisdiction illegally or with material irregularity - By wrongly deciding that a tenant is or is not entitled to protection, Court does not assume to itself jurisdiction which is not vested in it by law or refuse to exercise a jurisdiction which is vested in it by law - Nor does Court by arriving at an erroneous conclusion on plea of tenant as to his readiness and willingness act illegally or with material irregularity in exercise of its jurisdiction - Appeal allowed.
