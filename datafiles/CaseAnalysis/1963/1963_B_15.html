 Basti Sugar Mills Limited v Ram Ujagar and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 April 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

K.C. Das Gupta, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, J.C. Shah, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 339; AIR 1964 SC 355; 1963 (7) FLR 253; 1963 (2) LLJ 447; 1964 (2) SCJ 811; [1964] 2 S.C.R. 838

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - U.P. Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 2 (i) (iv), 2 (z) and 3 (b) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 19(1)(g) - (A) Whether owner of industry is employer to workmen employed through contractor? - Held, s. 2 (z) does not contain any words to show that contract labour was included therein, words used in section are by themselves sufficiently wide to bring in persons doing work in an industry whether employment was by management or by contractor of management - Definition of 'employer' was extended by sub-cl. (iv) of s. 2 (i) with purpose of making owner of industry, in circumstances mentioned in sub-cl. (iv), employer of workmen engaged in work which is done through contract and words used in sub-cl. are clearly sufficient to achieve this purpose - Therefore, he is owner - Moreover, words 'employed by factory' used in definition of workmen in Standing Orders - Reason why 'by' was used instead of 'in' appears to be to ensure that if a person has been employed to do work of industry, whether work is done inside factory or outside factory, he will get benefit of Standing Orders - So, words 'employed by a factory' are therefore wide enough to include workmen employed by contractors of factory also - (B) Whether s. 2 (i)(iv) amounts to a restriction in interest of general public within art. 19(6) of Constitution? - Further held, for interests of general public require that device of engagement of a contractor for doing work which is ordinarily part of industry should not be allowed to be availed of by owners of industry for evading provisions of Industrial Disputes Act - It cannot, therefore, be doubted that definition is in interest of general public within art. 19(6) of Constitution and is valid - Appeal dismissed.
