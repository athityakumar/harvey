Western India Match Company Limited v Their Workmen

Supreme Court of India

03 May 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

K.C. Das Gupta, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 115; AIR 1964 SC 472; 1963 (7) FLR 256; 1963 (2) LLJ 459; [1964] 3 S.C.R. 560

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Industrial Disputes, Incentive Bonus, United Provinces Industrial Disputes Act 1947 (UP 28 of 1947) (ENG)

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - United Provinces Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Production bonus - Sales office staff - Eligibility - Appellant/Employers were carrying on its business at its four factories one of which was located at Bareilly - Sales office at Bareilly was concerned in instant case - As incentive to larger production, appellant introduced Production Bonus Scheme in 1945 - Appellant withdrew scheme from factory office staff but later on introduced it following adjudication but the scheme was denied to staff of sales office staff - Dispute was referred to Tribunal by Govt - Tribunal held that sales office and factory form one single unit of industry and there being no reason for making any discrimination against sales office staff on question of production bonus held that employer should pay production bonus to workmen employed by their sales office too and directed payment of bonus from date of award - Hence, instant appeal by employers - Staff of sales office also preferred appeal against Tribunal order that payment would be applicable from date of award - Employers contended that Tribunal erred in holding that sales office and factory formed parts of one and the same unit of industrial production - Held, functional integrality was writ large on activities under consideration in instant case - Sales office could not exist without factory while it was equally clear that factory itself would not conveniently function without sales organization - Inter-dependence of two activities, manufacture of matches in factory and their sale by sales office was further emphasized by fact that factory arranged its volume of production in accordance with programmes made from time to time by sales manager - Manner in which financial forecast was prepared by sales manager in respect of functions not only of, sales office but also of factory proper was eloquent testimony that company did consider them as inter-dependent parts of same production - Tribunal was right in its decision that production bonus scheme should be extended to sales office staff - Case remanded to Tribunal for decision after taking note of all relevant facts whether production bonus scheme in force in factory and factory staff should be extended to sales office staff with or without modification - Workmen's appeal challenging correctness of Tribunal's direction that payment of production bonus should start from date award became enforceable in law could not be interfered with as it was discretion of Tribunal - Appeals disposed of.
