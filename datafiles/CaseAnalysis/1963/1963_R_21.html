State of Rajasthan v Ram Saran

Supreme Court of India

10 April 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 185; AIR 1964 SC 1361; 1965 (1) LLJ 103; [1964] 2 S.C.R. 982

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Standing Order

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 133(1)(c), 226 and 311 - States Reorganisation Act, 1956, ss. 114, 115, 116 and 117 - Police Act, 1861, s. 2 - Appeal for restoration of promotion - Respondent, Ram Saran, was appointed a constable in 1947 in Ajmer district police force - Was confirmed in that post after two years - The enactment of Reorganisation Act, 1956 resulted in appointing respondent as an officiating Sub-Inspector in Rajasthan State police force - The Deputy Inspector General of Police, Ajmer Range ordered reversion of respondent to his substantive post of Head Constable - (A)Whether High Court was justified in treating standing order as a condition of service? - Held, HC was in error in treating Standing Order 46 as a condition of service which was violatcd by order of reversion impugned by respondent in his writ petition - (B) Whether it is any condition of service applicable to holder of an officiating post that he shall not be reverted to his substantive post? - Further held, there is no basis for argument that mere reversion to a substantive post is a breach of conditions of service - Proviso to s. 115(7) affords no assistance to respondent - (C)Whether a violation of art. 14 of Constitution, in selection of officers for promotion? - Held, matter should be remitted to HC for consideration of this issue about breach of art. 14 of Constitution and constitutional validity of regionwise seniority lists prepared for promotion, reversion etc. allowing liberty to parties to lead further evidence on matter - Appeal allowed.
