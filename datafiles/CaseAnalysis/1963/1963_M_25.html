Mathuri and Others v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

11 December 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

K.C. Das Gupta, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 363; AIR 1964 SC 986; 1964 CRLJ 57; [1964] 5 S.C.R. 916

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Civil Procedure Code, 1908 - Warrants for delivery of possession in execution of several decrees in favour of several decree-holders was issued - Police and decree-holders went to execute decree and they were attacked by tenants mob - Appellants contended that police joined hands with landlords' people to execute warrants of possession after date of execution had already expired - Whether attempt of landlords to take possession amounted to criminal trespass and appellants were entitled to resist them? - O. 24 r. 24 sub-r. 3 clearly provides that in every process of execution of decree a day shall be specified on or before which it shall be executed - R. 25 provides that officer entrusted with execution of process shall endorse thereon date on and manner in which it was executed - Warrants in present case where date has been specified ceased to be executable in law - Held, court below was right in view that criminal trespass was not committed or apprehended by landlords - Appeal dismissed except as regards modification in sentences.
