State of Uttar Pradesh v Jogendra Singh

Supreme Court of India

04 March 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 125; AIR 1963 SC 1618; 1963 ALJ 617; 1963 (2) LLJ 444; 1964 (1) SCJ 399; [1964] 2 S.C.R. 197

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Land & Property; Service

Summary: Service - Civil Services (Classification, Control and Appeal) Rules, r. 55 - Uttar Pradesh Disciplinary Proceeding (Administrative Tribunal) Rules 1947, rr. 4(1) and 4(2) - Whether gazetted Govt. servants had option to request Governor that their cases should be tried by tribunal and not otherwise? - Held, plain and unambiguous object of enacting r. 4(2) is to provide an option to gazetted Govt. servants to request Governor that their cases should be tried by a tribunal and not otherwise - R. 4(2) imposes an obligation on Governor to grant a request made by gazetted Govt. servant that his case should be referred to tribunal under Rules - Such a request was admittedly made by respondent and has not been granted - Appeal dismissed.
