 Ghanshyam Das v Regional Assistant Commissioner of Salestax Nagpur

Supreme Court of India

16 August 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, S.K. Das, Raghubar Dayal, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 320; AIR 1964 SC 766; [1964] 51 ITR 557; 1964 MahLJ 574; [1964] 4 S.C.R. 436; 1963 (14) STC 976

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Sales tax - Central Provinces and Berar Sales Tax Act, 1947, ss. 8, 10 A, 11, 11 A, 14 and 17 - Whether expression 'escaped assessment' includes a case where no assessment has been made at all or takes in only post-assessment detection of evasion of tax? - Held, expression 'escaped assessment' in s. 11-A of Act includes that of a turnover which has not been assessed at all, because for one reason or other no assessment proceedings were initiated and therefore, no assessment was made in respect thereof - Appeal allowed.
