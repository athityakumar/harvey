 South India Corporation Private Limited v Secretary, Board of Revenue Trivandrum and Another

Supreme Court of India

13 August 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, Raghubar Dayal, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, J.R. Madholkar, Sudhi Ranjan Das

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 357; AIR 1964 SC 207; 1963 (2) KerLR 312; [1964] 4 S.C.R. 280; 1964 (15) STC 74

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property

Summary: Constitution - Administrative - Board of Revenue Act, 1913 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 277, 278 and 372 - (A) What is effect of provisions under Constitution art. 278 ? - Held, effect of provisions in art. 278 is that to extent covered by an agreement power of State Govt. to continue to levy taxes u/art. 277 superseded - (B) Whether pre exiting laws or constitution would prevail in case of a conflict?- Further held, object of art. 372 is to maintain continuity of pre-existing laws after Constitution came into force till they were repealed, altered or amended by a competent authority - Words 'subject to other provisions of Constitution' - Means that if there is an irreconcilable conflict between pre-existing law and a provision or provisions of Constitution, latter shall prevail to extent of that inconsistency - Appeal allowed.
