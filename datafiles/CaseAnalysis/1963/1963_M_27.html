 Lachman Utamchand Kiriplani v Meena alias Mota

Supreme Court of India

14 August 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, K. Subba Rao, Raghubar Dayal, Sudhi Ranjan Das

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 164; AIR 1964 SC 40; 1964 (66) BomLR 297; [1964] 4 S.C.R. 331

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Practice & Procedure; Women & Children

Keywords: Marriage & Divorce

Summary: Family & Personal - Hindu Marriage Act, 1955, ss. 9 and 10(1) - (A) Whether abandoning wife in anger amounts to desertion ? - Held, it is not - As there is no intention to leave spouse permanently - Desertion means intentional permanent forsaking and abandonment of one spouse by other without that other's consent and without reasonable cause - It is a total repudiation of obligations of marriage - (B) What are essential conditions to establish desertion?- Further held, two essential conditions must be there, (1) factum of separation, and (2) intention to bring cohabitation permanently to an end (animus deserendi) - Offence of desertion commences when fact of separation and animus deserendi co-exist - It is not necessary that they should commence at same time - (C) Whether burden of proving desertion lies on petitioner?- Further held, burden of proving desertion - 'factum' as well as 'animus deserendi' is on petitioner, and he or she has to establish beyond reasonable doubt, to satisfaction of Court, desertion throughout entire period of two years before petition as well as that such desertion was without just cause - For once desertion is proved, deserting spouse, so long as she evidences no sincere intention to effect a reconciliation and return to matrimonial home, is presumed to continue in desertion - (D) whether desertion without just cause can succeed?- Further held, no - If he or she so acts as to make it plain to deserting spouse that any offer on part of latter to resume cohabitation would be rejected - Petition cannot succeed, for petitioner would have to establish that desertion was without just cause during entire period referred to in s. 10(1) (a) of Act before he can succeed - (E) When does desertion comes to an end? - Further held, two distinct matters have to co-exist in order that desertion might come to an end - There must be conduct on part of deserted spouse which affords just and reasonable cause for deserting spouse not to seek reconciliation and which absolves her from her continuing obligation to return to matrimonial home - Conduct of deserted spouse should have had such an impact on mind of deserting spouse that in fact it causes her to continue to live apart and thus continue desertion - Where it is clear that conduct of deserted spouse has had no such effect on mind of deserting spouse there is no rule of law that desertion terminates by reason of conduct of deserted spouse - (F) What is burden of proof on a petitioner who seeks relief on ground of desertion?- Further held, petitioner has to prove four essential conditions, namely (1) factum of separation (2) animus deserendi (3) absence of his or her consent and (4) absence of his or her conduct giving reasonable cause to deserting spouse to leave matrimonial home - Offence of desertion must be proved beyond any reasonable doubt and as a rule of prudence evidence of petitioner shall be corroborated - Proof required in a matrimonial case is to be equated to that in a criminal case - (G) Whether desertion can occur even when spouse is staying under same roof ? - Further held, willful conduct must satisfy ingredients of desertion - In our society, it is well known that in many a home husband would be guilty of explosive conduct towards his wife by completely neglecting her to extent of denying her all martial rights, but still wife, because of social and economic conditions, may continue to live under same roof, this amounts to constructive desertion - (H) Whether word reasonable in s. 9(2) has same meaning as that in explanation of s. 10 of Act ?- Further held, s. 9, s. 10 deal with different subjects - One with restitution of conjugal rights and other with judicial separation - S. 10 does not permit separation of spouses on ground of desertion except when desertion is without reasonable cause -'Reasonable cause' must be so construed as to bring about a union rather than separation - S. 9 of Act does not throw any light on construction of expression 'without reasonable cause' and whether there is a reasonable cause or not in a given case shall be decided only on evidence and peculiar circumstances of that case - Appeal allowed.
