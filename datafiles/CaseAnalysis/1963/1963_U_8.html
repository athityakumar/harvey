Kalwa Devadatham and Others v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 April 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, A.K. Sarkar, Mohammad Hidayatullah

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 238; AIR 1964 SC 880; 1963 (2) ALT(SC) 253; 1963 (2) ITJ 123; [1963] 49 ITR 165; [1964] 3 S.C.R. 191; [1983] 14 TAXMAN 455

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 21, r. 58 - Indian Income tax Act, 1922, S. 25(A)(1) - Assessment Order - Attachment of Properties - Sale - Enforcement of Liability - Respondent/Revenue authorities attached immovable properties belonging to Hindu undivided/joint family of 'N' and same was put up for sale - Appellants/Plaintiffs purchased properties at auction, and claimed a decree declaring that assessment orders made by respondent/Income Tax Officer were unenforceable against property of appellants and sale was without jurisdiction, void and illegal and an order restraining respondents/authorities from selling or confirming sale after institution of suit - Appellants resisted suit u/o. r 21, 58, of 1908 CPC which were dismissed by executing Court - On appeal to HC was also dismissed - Hence instant appeal - Whether properties of joint family were liable to be attached and could be sold in enforcement of liability for payment of Income Tax u/s. 25(A)(2) of Act - Held, HC was right in holding, that properties were of joint family and liable to be attached and sold in enforcement of liability for payment of Income Tax - Court hold that liability was not proved, and was illegal or immoral and sale of joint family property, including share of sons for satisfying debts was valid notwithstanding severance of joint family status effected before sale - Court opined that HC was right in holding that partition was a sham transaction and not intended to be operative - If partition was a sham transaction appellant's suit for setting aside attachment failed - Appeals disposed of.
