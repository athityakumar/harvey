 Moti Ram Deka Etc. v General Manager, N.E.F. Railways,Maligaon, Pandu, Etc.(With

Supreme Court of India

05 December 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, K. Subba Rao, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Mohammad Hidayatullah, K.C. Das Gupta, J.C. Shah, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 215; AIR 1964 SC 600; 1964 (2) LLJ 467; [1964] 5 S.C.R. 683

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Reduction In Rank, Power To Terminate, Civil Services Rules, Civil Services (Classification, Control and Appeal) Rules, Railway Fundamental Rules, Indian Railway Establishment Code, 1951, Central Government Services (Temporary Service) Rules, 1949

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 154, 309, 310 and 311 - Government of India Act, 1935, ss. 240 and 241 - Indian Railway Establishment Code, 1951, rr. 148(3) and 149(3) - Central Government Services (Temporary Service) Rules, 1949, r. 5(a) - Whether service of a permanent civil servant is terminated otherwise than by operation of rule of superannuation, or rule of compulsory retirement and does such termination amount to removal u/art. 311(2) or not ? - Held, in exercising power conferred by art. 309, extent of the pleasure recognised by art. 310 cannot be affected or impaired - Parliament or Legislature can make a law regulating conditions of service without affecting powers of President or Governor u/art. 310 r/w art. 311 - It has also been stated at same place that power to dismiss a public servant at pleasure is outside scope of art. 154 and, therefore, cannot be delegated by Governor to a subordinate officer and can be exercised by him only in manner prescribed by Constitution - Appeal allowed.
