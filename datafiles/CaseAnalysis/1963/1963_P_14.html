Chandra Deo Singh v Prokash Chandra Bose and Another

Supreme Court of India

22 January 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

J.R. Madholkar, Syed Jaffer Imam, K. Subba Rao, Raghubar Dayal

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 398; AIR 1963 SC 1430; 1963 CRLJ 397; 1964 (1) SCJ 202; [1964] 1 S.C.R. 639

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 302 - Murder - Prima Facie Case - Evidence - Conviction - Jurisdiction - Respondent No. 1/proprietor, killed deceased by shooting him with a gun - On basis of FIR, police undertook investigation and associates of respondent No. 1, were examined as Court witnesses - Divisional Magistrate found that no prima facie case was made out against respondent No. 1, u/s. 302/34 of 1860 Code - On basis of first report, and dismissed complaint of appellant without assigning any reason - Appellant preferred an application for revision before Sessions judge, who directed Divisional Magistrate to make further enquiry against him - Respondent No. 1 preferred a revision application before HC, which hold that appellant had no locus standee to appear and contest criminal case u/art. 134 of Constitution - Hence instant appeal - Whether respondent and his associates had sufficient ground for conviction u/s. 302/34 of 1860 Code and Whether Magistrate had jurisdiction for making an enquiry - Held, Court hold that enquiry made by Magistrate was vitiated and power to dismiss a complaint rest only with a Magistrate who had taken cognizance and HC was incompetent to do - Therefore order reversing that of Sessions judge could not be sustained - This Court hold that a Magistrate had power to weigh evidence adduced at enquiry and consider same recorded in an enquiry - Evidence adduced by complainant and his own evidence were self-contradictory and nothing intrinsically false in allegations made in complaint - Appellant was entitled to know why his complaint had been dismissed with a view to consider an approach to a revision Court - Court hold that HC had erred in setting aside order of Sessions Court and direct that further enquiry be made into complaint of appellant against respondent No. 1 - There could be no legal impediment to enquiry against respondent - Court, therefore called for a report from Magistrate as there was no material on record regarding enquiry - Appeal allowed.
