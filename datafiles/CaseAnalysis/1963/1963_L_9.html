Laxmidas Dahyabhai Kabarwala v Nanabhai Chunilal Kabarwala and Others

Supreme Court of India

27 March 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, A.K. Sarkar, Sudhi Ranjan Das

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 301; AIR 1964 SC 11; [1964] 2 S.C.R. 567

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Family & Personal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Form Of Pleading

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Trusts & Associations - Constitution - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, O. 8 r. 6, O. 6 r. 17 - Partnership Act, 1932, ss. 37, 55(1) and 69(3) (a) -Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - (A) Whether defendant can claim benefit of that relief in same suit?- Held, relief prayed for by plaintiff is not for rendition of accounts - No question of a defendant claiming benefit of that relief in same suit - Benefit can be claimed only in cases where relief sought is common to parties though ranged on either side - (B) Whether court can treat counter-claim as a plaint in a cross-suit? -Further held, law does not preclude a Court from treating a counter-claim as a plaint in a cross suit - Court not prevented from separating Written Statement proper from what was described as a counter - Claim and treating latter as a cross suit - If counter-claim contains all necessary requisites sufficient to be treated as a plaint making a claim for relief sought - Open to a Court to convert or treat counter - Claim as a plaint in a cross - Suit - (C) When is plaint in a cross suit to be determined?- Further held, date for purpose of determining when plaint in a cross suit should be treated as having been filed is not date on which conversion is ordered but date on which Written Statement containing counter - Claim is filed - Mostly leave to amend under O. 6 r. 17 of Code will ordinarily be refused when effect of amendment would be to take away - Rule applies when fresh allegations added or fresh reliefs sought - (D) Whether legal consequence of an act can be discounted because pecuniary burden borne by party is not heavy?- Further held, court-fee payable for a relief is small - Party has paid it and done all that law requires - Legal consequence of such an act cannot be discounted merely because pecuniary burden borne by party is not heavy - (E) Whether s. 37 can be a bar to relief prayed for by defendants?- Further held, counter - Claim is held to be inadmissible - So long as conversion of it into a plaint is not granted-the question raised by Partnership Act s. 37 would not be within scope of suit - Until such a conversion is effected, no issues could or would be framed - S. 37 is no objection to exercise of discretion by Court to grant prayer for conversion - Appeal allowed.
