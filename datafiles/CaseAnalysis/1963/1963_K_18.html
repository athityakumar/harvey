Kaushalya Rani v Gopal Singh

Supreme Court of India

20 September 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 368; AIR 1964 SC 260; 1964 CRLJ 152; [1964] 4 S.C.R. 982

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 -Criminal Procedure Code,1898, s. 417(3)(4) - Limitation Act, 1908, ss. 5 and 29(2) - (A) Whether s. 5 of Limitation Act (LA) would apply to an application for Special Leave to appeal u/s. 417 (3) of CrPC? -Held, it will not - (1) there is no limitation prescribed by LA for an appeal against an order of acquittal at instance of a private prosecutor - There is a difference between Limitation Act and rule laid down in s. 417(4) of Code in respect of limitation affecting such an application - S. 29(2) is supplemental in its character in so far as it provides for application of s. 3 to such cases as would not come within its purview but for this provision - For purposes of determining any period of limitation prescribed by any special law, it has made provisions of Limitation Act, referred in cl. (a) of sub-s. (2) of s. 29 applicable to such cases to extent to which they are not expressly excluded by such special or local law - Cl. (b) of that sub-section expressly lays it down that remaining provisions of Limitation Act shall not apply to cases governed by any special or local law - Therefore, s. 5 of Limitation Act would not apply to an application for special leave to appeal u/s. 417 (3) of Code - Appeal dismissed.
