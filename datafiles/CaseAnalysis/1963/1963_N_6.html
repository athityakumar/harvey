Nagraj v State of Mysore

Supreme Court of India

08 May 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

Raghubar Dayal, K. Subba Rao, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 159; AIR 1964 SC 269; 1964 CRLJ 161; [1964] 3 S.C.R. 671

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Labour & Industrial Law; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 143, 147, 149, 224. 225, 332, 341 and 395 - General Clauses Act, 1897, s. 3(31) - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898, ss. 132, 127, 197 and 128 - Mysore Police Act, ss. 4, 8, 26 and 72 - Motor Vehicles Act, 1939 - Bombay State Transport Act, s. 29 - (A) Whether sanction from the state Govt. is essential to prosecute the police while he is performing duties under chap. IX of the CrPC, 1898? - Held, courts cannot entertain complains unless there is a sanction by the State Government for prosecution of that police officer - The courts cannot look into the grounds of such sanction - Sanction from the state Govt. is essential in this regard - (B) Whether courts can pass an order in the absence of sanctions of the state Govt. - Held, court need not pass an order of acquittal or discharge of the accused when it finds that sanction from the State Govt. has not been passed to this regard - U/s. 132 of CrPC, 1898the complaint shall not be registered without the sanction of the government and the complaint is deemed to be void - The courts either drop or reject such complaints due to the void nature of the proceedings - Appeal allowed.
