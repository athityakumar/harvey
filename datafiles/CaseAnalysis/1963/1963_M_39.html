State of Uttar Pradesh v Mohammad Naim

Supreme Court of India

15 March 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

S.K. Das, A.K. Sarkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, K.C. Das Gupta

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 349; AIR 1964 SC 703; 1964 CRLJ 549; [1964] 2 S.C.R. 363

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 134(1)(c) - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 452, 34, 195 and 307 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, ss. 439 and 561 - Application u/s. 561(A), CrPC, 1973 for expunging observation given by the Judge - (A) Whether HC has inherent power to expunge remarks made by any subordinate court to secure ends of justice? - Held, HC has no jurisdiction to expunge passages from judgment of an inferior court which has not been brought before it in regular appeal or revision, but an application u/s. 561(A) CrPC, 1973 is maintainable, even though no appeal or revision is preferred to it, to correct judicially observations made -(B) Whether State Govt. has no locus standi to make application u/s. 561(A) CrPC, 1973? - Further held, State Govt. may make an application to HC u/s. 561(A) in same way as it may direct Public Prosecutor to present an appeal on its behalf to HC u/s. 417 or may invoke through one of its officers jurisdiction of HC u/s. 439 of CrPC, 1973 - (C) Whether is present case a case of an exceptional nature in which judge should have exercised his inherent jurisdiction u/s. 561(A) CrPC, 1973? - Held, there is one principle of cardinal importance in administration of justice proper freedom and independence of judges and Magistrates must be maintained - Unless an example is made of such officers by taking most stringent action against them, no improvement in police administration is possible - Appeal allowed.
