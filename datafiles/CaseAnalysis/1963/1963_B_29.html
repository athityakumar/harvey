Manindra Land And Buildingcorporation Ltd. v Bhutnath Banerjee And Others

Supreme Court of India

02 May 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

Raghubar Dayal, K. Subba Rao, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 327; AIR 1964 SC 1336; [1964] 3 S.C.R. 495

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Defence & Security Forces; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Defence & Security Forces - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, O. XXII r. 9(2) - Indian Soldiers (Litigation) Act, 1925, ss. 10 and 11 - Setting aside of abatement of suit - Sufficient cause for not making application within period of limitation -The total period plaintiff served under war conditions was 4 years, 6 months and 3 days - Question was whether this entire period had to be excluded in computing period of limitation for making application for setting aside dismissal of suit - Subordinate Judge excluded it and High Court considered it unjustifiable to exclude period prior to December 14, 1942, date of decree, as that period could not have in any way prevented plaintiffs in making application for setting aside of dismissal order - Held, High Court fell in error in interfering with finding of fact arrived at by Subordinate judge with respect to appellants having sufficient cause for not making an application for bringing respondents on record within time and for not applying for setting aside of abatement within time - Appeal allowed.
