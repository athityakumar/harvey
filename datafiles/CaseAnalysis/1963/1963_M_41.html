 Mohan Singh v Bhanwarlal and Other

Supreme Court of India

03 October 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, K. Subba Rao, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Raghubar Dayal

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 116; AIR 1964 SC 1366; [1964] 5 S.C.R. 12

Case Digest

Subject: Election; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Fair Criticism

Summary: Election - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 100, 101 and 123(4) - Whether election petition not comply with requirements of s. 82? - Held, onus of establishing a corrupt practice is undoubtedly on person who sets it up, and onus is not discharged on proof of mere preponderance of probability, as in trial of a civil suit; corrupt practice must be established beyond reasonable doubt by evidence which is clear and unambiguous - Test in cases u/s. 123(4) is whether imputation beside being false in fact, is it published with object of lowering candidate in estimation of electorate and calculated to prejudice his prospects at election - Imputation was on face of it one reasonably calculated to prejudice prospects of candidate at election, therefore, corrupt practices established against appellant - Appeal dismissed.
