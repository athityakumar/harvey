 Tilkayat Shri Govindlalji Maharaj v State of Rajasthan and Others

Supreme Court of India

21 January 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, K.C. Das Gupta, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 305; AIR 1963 SC 1638; 1964 (2) SCJ 1715; [1964] 1 S.C.R. 561

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Competence Of The Legislature, Attorney-General

Summary: Constitution - Hindu Law - Rajasthan Nathdwara Temple Act,1959, ss. 2(viii), 30(2)(a), 16, 28(2) and 28(3) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14, 19(1)(f), 31(2), 25(1), 32 and 26 - (A) Whether there is distinction between executive commands of absolute ruler or legislative commands by him? - Held, no distinction - Such orders have force of law and governs right of parties affected thereby - Firman issued by Maharana of Udaipur in 1934 is a law by which affairs of Nathdwara Temple and succession to office of Tilkayat were governed after its issue - (B) On what factors is it determined, whether a Hindu temple is public or private where evidence regarding foundation is not available? - Further held, it is determined by answering questions like - Is temple built in such an imposing manner that it may prima facie appear to be a public temple?- Are members of public entitled to an entry in temple? - Are they entitled to take part in offering service and taking Darshan in temple? - Are members of public entitled to take part in festivals and ceremonies arranged in temple? - Are their offerings accepted as a matter of right? - (C)Whether right of Tilkayat is merely a Custodian, Manager and Trustee of temple or his position is similar to that of a Mahant or a Shebait and whether his right is regarded as a right to property u/art. 19 (1)(f)? - Further held, his position is not similar to mahant or a Shabait - No more custodian, manager and trustee - As right over property posses by custodian, manager and trustee, cannot be regarded as a right to property u/art. 19 (1)(f) - So does not constitute property u/art. 31 (2) - Even if may be regarded as mahant he can only manage property under absolute and strict supervision of Darbar of Udaipur - No absolute right, Thats why restrictions can be imposed by Act which would be treated as reasonable and in interests of general public - (D)Whether a law would be constitutional when it is made to regulate single individual u/art.14 of Constitution? - Further held, yes - If, on account of some special circumstances or reasons applicable to him and not applicable to others, that single individual may be treated as a class by himself - Shrine at Nathdwara holds a unique position amongst Hindu shrines and no temple can be regarded as comparable with it - (E)What is test to decide whether a given religious practice is an integral part of religion or not u/arts. 25(1) and 26(b)? - Further held, tests are answer to questions - Whether it is regarded as such by community following religion or not -Whether it is religious in character - Whether it can be regarded as an integral or essential part of religion - Finding of Court would depend upon evidence adduced before it as to conscience of community and tenets of its religion - Appeal allowed and petition dismissed.
