Jang Singh v Brijlal and Others

Supreme Court of India

20 February 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 112; AIR 1966 SC 1631; [1964] 2 S.C.R. 145

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Execution of a decree for pre-emption passed in favour of appellant - High Court held that appellant had not deposited full amount as directed by decree within prescribed time - Result thereof suit for pre-emption ordered to be dismissed - Appeal - Held, appellant approached Court with an application intending to make deposit to be ordered by Court, and Court and its clerk made a mistake by ordering him to deposit an amount which was less by one rupee - Appellant was excused in as much as responsibility was shared by court - Appellant was prevented from depositing full amount by act of Court - Mistake committed by Court must be set right - Case must go back to that stage when mistake was committed by Court and appellant should be ordered to deposit additional rupee - Appeal allowed.
