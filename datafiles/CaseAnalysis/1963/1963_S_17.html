Smt. Kamala Devi v Seth Takhatmal and Another

Supreme Court of India

21 February 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, Raghubar Dayal, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 354; AIR 1964 SC 859; 1967 JabLJ 1020; 1963 MahLJ 730; 1963 MPLJ 738; [1964] 2 S.C.R. 152

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Summary: Socio-Economic - Displaced Persons (Debts Adjustment) Act, 1951, ss. 5 and 15 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 145 - Stay of proceedings - Refusal by Execution Court - Validity - Held, second respondent's application before Tribunal, Dehra Dun, was rejected and said order holds field till it is reversed by appellate Court - As order of Tribunal was not suspended, effect was that there was no application pending in a Tribunal as defined in s. 5 of Act - Order of HC is correct - Executing Court does acted without jurisdiction in refusing to stay execution proceedings against appellant - (B) Construction of terms of a surety bond - Held, If a demand was made or if judgment debtor or surety waived fulfillment of a condition, order sheet must disclose issue of a notice or facts constituting a waiver - There cannot possibly be any facts outside record - Respondent failed to show any entry therein which will support fact of a demand or a waiver respondent would not be prejudiced if appellant was allowed to argue on construction of surety bond words used in this part of undertaking given by surety is clear and unambiguous judgment-debtor shall produce bills or their value and place them at disposal of Court when required to do so - In this case there is no order or entry in order sheet requiring judgment-debtor to produce and place property in Court, nor even execution petition though it describes judgment-debtor in one of columns, asks for any relief against him - A surety bond has to be strictly construed and it would be obvious on express terms of bone - Appeal disposed of.
