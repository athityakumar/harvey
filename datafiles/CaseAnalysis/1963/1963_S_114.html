Maharaja Sir Pateshwari Prasad Singh v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

28 March 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sarkar

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 383; [1963] 50 ITR 731

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Uttar Pradesh Agricultural Income Tax Act, 1949, ss. 2, 3 and 24, r. 17 - Issues arise - (A) Whether condition (b) in the Schedule of Rates is applicable to Part I alone or to both Parts I and II? - Held, it is conceded on behalf of the respondent State that if the words "agricultural income-tax" in condition (b) do not include super-tax, then only persons having an income between Rs. 3,000 and Rs. 3,214 per annum would be benefited - We are unable to conclude from the fact that persons owning estates yielding income between Rs. 3,000 and Rs. 3,214 only would be benefited, that the expression "agricultural income-tax" must be understood to include super-tax so as to enlarge the scope of the benefit - The legislature may have intended to confer the benefit only on persons with a comparatively smaller income - There is nothing to show that it did not do so - The context requires that the definition of "agricultural income-tax" in s. 2(2) should not be imported into condition (b) - Condition (b) is a condition to which the rates mentioned in Part I of the Schedule are made expressly subject - Those rates are only of agricultural income-tax - The obvious intention is that the amount of the agricultural income-tax only, that is to say, excluding the amount of super-tax, that can be imposed, calculated at the rates specified in Part I of the Schedule, is not in any case to exceed the limit mentioned in condition (b) - (B) Whether the Explanation to r. 17 is controlled by the itself or is to be interpreted and applied independently of the rule? - Held, the appellant contends that approval by the Govt. is provided for in the rule only to avoid disputes later as to whether the institution or fund not so approved as is for a charitable purpose within the meaning of the rule - We think that the intention was that there must be approval of the institution or fund - If such approval was not incumbent, then exemptions would have to be granted also in the case of an institution which was on the face of it for a charitable purpose but where the funds were not utilised for charities - It was wholly unnecessary to provide for approval by the Govt. only in cases where it was doubtful whether the purpose of the institution was charitable or not - (C) Whether the hospitals which were subject to the general inspection by the civil surgeon can be treated as hospitals in receipt of a grant from the Govt. within the meaning of the notification issued u/r. 17? - Held, all that we have here is inspection of the hospitals and dispensaries by the Government medical officers - We have nothing to show for what purpose such inspection was held - We find it impossible to infer from the word "inspection" any idea of a service rendered to the patients in the hospitals or for the charitable object for which the hospitals had been maintained - It has not, therefore, been shown that the inspection by the Govt.'s doctors was granted within the notification - Such appointments do not, in our view, amount to rendering services for the charitable purpose for which the hospitals and dispensaries had been established - (D) Whether the scholarships paid to students through an institution recognised by the State Govt. or a local authority were donations within the meaning of r. 17? - Held, it is not disputed that the institutions in contemplation were institutions recognised or aided by Govt. - We feel no doubt that when moneys are paid to such an institution to be distributed as scholarships to its students, this is a donation to the institution for the institution utilises it for its own purpose, namely, helping its students which it may otherwise have done with its own funds - This question must, therefore, be answered in the affirmative as the High Court has done - (E) Whether the scholarships paid to students directly would amount to donations to an institution or to a fund within the scope of r. 17? - Held, for the same reason mentioned above, this question also has to be answered in the affirmative - Appeal disposed of.
