Ramachandra Shenoy and Another v Mrs. Hilda Brite and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 April 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, S.K. Das, A.K. Sarkar

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 97; AIR 1964 SC 1323; [1964] 2 S.C.R. 722

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Summary: Family & Personal - Indian Succession Act, 1925, s. 82 - Life interest or absolute interest - Intention - Testatrix had originally four daughters but by date of will only two of them were alive - Other members of testatrix's family were one grand-daughter and four grandsons - One grandson filed suit for partition and separate possession on grounds that his deceased mother had life interest in particular property and remainder in absolute was conferred upon her male issues - Appellants herein, that contested suit contended that deceased had absolute interest in property - Trial judge as well as District judge on appeal had upheld construction contended by appellants and dismissed suit - Single judge appeal in HC, reversed decree and decreed suit holding that deceased had obtained only life interest in suit property - Correctness of this construction was challenged by appellants - Hence, instant appeal - Whether interest which testarix's eldest daughter' had under bequest was absolute or merely life interest with absolute remainder vesting in her male issues - Held, U/s. 82 of the Act, bequest was presumed to be absolute and point urged was that there was no contrary intention manifested to displace this statutory presumption - Common ground was that testatrix intended to confer absolute and permanent interest on male children of deceased - Conversely, if contentions urged by appellants were accepted legacy in their favour would be void because there could legally be no gift over after absolute interest in favour of their deceased mother - Words in will denoted that interest of deceased was not intended to be absolute but was limited to her life only, thus, Court adopted such construction, for it gave effect to every testamentary disposition contained in will - Accordingly, interpretation adopted by HC was correct - Several authorities were cited about matter of construction of will - However, authorities or precedents were of no help as each will had to be construed in its own terms and in setting in which clauses occurred - Appeal dismissed.
