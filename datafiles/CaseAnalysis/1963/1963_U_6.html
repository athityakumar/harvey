 P. C. Wadhwa v Union of India and Another

Supreme Court of India

27 August 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

S.K. Das, K. Subba Rao, Raghubar Dayal, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 170; AIR 1964 SC 423; 1964 CurLJ 10; 1964 (1) LLJ 395; [1964] 4 S.C.R. 598

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Keywords: Ultra Vires, Charge - Sheet, Right To Promotion

Summary: Service - All India Services Act, 1951, s. 3 - All India Services (Discipline and Appeal) Rules, 1955, r. 3 - Civil Services (Classification, Control and Appeal) Rules, r. 13 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 16 and 311 - Whether order of reversion of appellant was in effect a reduction in rank? - Held, it is appreciable that while a departmental enquiry is being held against a person in respect of a serious offence it would not be in public interest to allow him to hold a responsible position - But in that case more straightforward course would be to suspend him and not to resort to a devious method as employed by Govt. in this case - Had appellant been suspended, he would have been restored to his former post in light of actual action taken against him on basis of findings of Enquiry Officer - But, as a result of what Govt. has done he loses benefit of such a result - Appeal allowed.
