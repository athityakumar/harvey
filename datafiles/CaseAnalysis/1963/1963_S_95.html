State of Punjab v Mst. Qaisar Jehan Begum and Another

Supreme Court of India

11 February 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

S.K. Das, A.K. Sarkar, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 197; AIR 1963 SC 1604; 1964 (1) MLJ(SC) 9; 1964 (1) SCJ 33; [1964] 1 S.C.R. 971

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 18, Proviso, cl. (b) and 12(2) - (A)What do words knowledge of award mean under Act? - Held, it does not mean a mere knowledge of fact that an award has been made - It must relate to essential contents of award - May be known either actually or constructively - If award is communicated to a party u/s. 12(2) of Act, party must be obviously fixed with knowledge of contents of award whether he reads it or not - When a party is present in court either personally or through his representative when award is made by Collector, it must be presumed that he knows contents of award - Such knowledge could not be inferred from date of petition for interim Payment filed by party when party had not known of amount of compensation awarded - Appeal dismissed.
