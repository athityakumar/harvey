Mohd. Ikram Hussain v State of Uttar Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 October 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, K.C. Das Gupta

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 180; AIR 1964 SC 1625; 1964 AllCriR 252; 1989 (1) CLT 76; 1964 CRLJ 590; [1964] 5 S.C.R. 86

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, s. 491 - Proceedings against appellant was started in nature of writ of habeas corpus to release of respondent No. 2 wife Kaniz Fatima from unlawful detention by appellant and for her delivery - High Court considering age of Kaniz Fatima as 19 years gave order to produce her before court within ten days time - HC order not complied with so HC awarded simple imprisonment for three months for contempt - Whether direction given by HC in a proceeding for a writ of habeas corpus for production of body of a person has to be carried out and if disobeyed contemner is punishable by attachment and imprisonment, is valid? - Held, that HC order for contempt was justified as appellant having knowledge of whereabouts of Kaniz Fatima and having her custody was willfully and deliberately disobeying direction of court - Writ of habeas corpus issues not only for release from detention by State but also for release from private detention - Order of HC was not without jurisdiction - Writ nisi for production of Kaniz Fatima should have been preceded by some more enquiry - Further, HC can always order production of body of a person illegally detained and can punish disobedience of its order by attachment and commitment - Appeal dismissed.
