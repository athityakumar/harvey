Ahmed Adam Sait and Others v Inayathullah Mekhri and Others

Supreme Court of India

29 March 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, K.C. Das Gupta

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 342; AIR 1964 SC 107; [1964] 2 S.C.R. 647

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Constructive Res Judicata, Court Of Competent Jurisdiction, Attorney-General

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Civil Procedure Code, 1908 - Suit instituted by respondents and as such they prayed that a scheme should be settled for proper administration of jumma Masjid - Scheme framed earlier sought to be modified by respondents that masjid should be administered by Cutchi Memons - Trial Court rejected Modification - HC held that modification required and suit not barred by res judicata - Held, if word 'worshippers' received a larger denotation, it may show that scheme binds all worshippers interested in Trust, but it would immediately raise question of clarification of scheme because in administration of scheme, word 'worshippers' has consistently received narrow interpretation, and Trustees as well as committee of worshippers has always been constituted out of members of Cutchi Memon Community and no others, so that on this alternative basis, plea of res judicata may be upheld ; but an occasion will clearly arise for either clarifying scheme or medically changing it so as to make other worshippers eligible for appointment as Trustees - Matter remitted back to trial court for modifications - Decree when passed in suit instituted u/s. 92 law assumes that all persons who have same interest as plaintiff had been represented and any subsequent suit by same parties on same issue is barred by res judicata- Further held, HC rightly allowed claim of modified scheme raised by respondents - Appeal dismissed.
