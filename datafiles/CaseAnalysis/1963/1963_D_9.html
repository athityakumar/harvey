S.R. Tewari v District Board Agra and Another

Supreme Court of India

15 April 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 335; AIR 1964 SC 1680; 1963 ALJ 944; 1966 (13) FLR 104; 1964 (1) LLJ 1; 1964 (2) SCJ 300; [1964] 3 S.C.R. 55

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Keywords: Contract Of Service, Contract Of Personal Service

Summary: Constitution - Service - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Specific Relief Act, 1877, s. 21(b) - U. P. District Board Act, 1922, ss. 82 and 84 - (A) Whether powers u/s. 21 (b) of Specific Relief Act are subject to art. 226 of constitution?- Held, powers of a statutory body are always subject to statute which has constituted it and must be exercised consistently with statute, and Court have, in appropriate cases, power to declare an action of body illegal or ultra vires, even if action relates to determination of employment of a servant - Where in a petition u/art. 226 for a writ in nature of Certiorari for quashing resolution of Board terminating petitioner and a writ in nature of mandamus for restoring him in service - High Court had jurisdiction to declare decision of Board as ultra vires - (B) Whether Board had power to determine employment of its servants? - Further held, Board has power and can be purported to exercise that power, in manner and subject to conditions prescribed by r. 3 A - Power to appoint ordinarily carries with it power to terminate appointment - (C) Whether court can ascertain whether termination is by way of punishment u/art. 311? - Further held, form of order under which employment of a servant is determined is not conclusive of true nature of order - Form may be merely to camouflage an order of dismissal for misconduct, and it is always open to court before which order is challenged to go behind form and ascertain true character of order - If Court holds that order though in form merely of determination of employment is in reality a cloak for an order of dismissal as a matter of punishment, Court would not be debarred merely because of form of order in giving effect to rights conferred by statutory rules upon employee - (D) Whether any new plea can be raised u/art. 226? -Further held, it cannot be raised - As question raised was one primarily of fact and was never raised, nor explored in lower court on proper pleadings, it would be taking Board by surprise to allow petitioner to make out this new case at this stage - Appeal allowed.
