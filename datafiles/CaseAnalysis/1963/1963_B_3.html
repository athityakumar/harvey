Bachchoo Lal v State of Uttar Pradesh and Another

Supreme Court of India

25 April 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

Raghubar Dayal

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 298; 1964 CRLJ 76; 1964 (2) SCJ 194; [1964] 3 S.C.R. 358

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Acquittal, Criminal Intimidation, Intentional insult with intent to provoke breach of peace

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 506, 504 - United Province District Board Act, 1922, ss. 107, 182 - Intentional insult with intent to provoke breach of peace - Criminal intimidation - Acquittal - Sustainability - Appellant filed complaint against respondent-accused for obstructing realisation of bayai and bazaar dues on behalf of Raja - Trial Court convicted accused for offences u/ss. 504, 506 IPC and u/s. 107 of the Act - Appeal before Session Judge by accused - Session Judge acquitted accused - Appellant filed appeal before HC - Appeal was dismissed by HC - Whether acquittal order recorded was sustainable - Held, Session Judge ought to have issued notice of hearing of appeal to appellant on whose complaint, respondent was convicted by Trial Court and against which order of conviction he had filed appeal - No such notice was issued to him and therefore order of Session Judge acquitting respondent was not good order - HC was wrong in holding that Raja could not collect bazari dues through his agents - Appellant had requisite sanction u/s. 182 of the Act for prosecuting respondent and therefore finding to contrary was wrong - Requisite authority u/s. 182 of the Act was in favour of not only Raja but in favour of several of his employees including appellant - Hence, no interference was possible with acquittal of respondent on merits - Appeal dismissed.
