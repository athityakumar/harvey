A. S. Krishnappa Chettiar and Others v Nachiappa Chettiar and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 March 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

J.R. Madholkar, K. Subba Rao, Raghubar Dayal

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 352; AIR 1964 SC 227; [1964] 2 S.C.R. 241

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Indian Limitation Act, 1908, ss. 15(1) and 19 - Whether appellant can sought execution of decrees against respondents after four years and execution applications can be said to be within time? - Execution of decree rendered infructuous owing to insolvency of first defendant - S. 15(1) not attracted as it applied only when execution of decrees which was stayed by injunctions or order - Acceptance by insolvency court of composition deed did not operate as stay of execution of deed - S. 19 was also not attracted as second defendant in his letter referred to liability of trustees arising under composition deed - Held, reference of another's liability does not amount to acknowledge one's own liability so execution of decree after four years barred by limitation - Appeal dismissed.
