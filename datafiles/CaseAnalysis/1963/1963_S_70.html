Raja Ram Jaiswal v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

04 April 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

J.R. Madholkar, K. Subba Rao, Raghubar Dayal

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 340; AIR 1964 SC 828; 1964 CRLJ 705; [1964] 2 S.C.R. 752

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Excise

Keywords: Specially Empowered, Bihar and Orissa Excise Act, 1915, Opium Act, 1878

Summary: Excise - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 25 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, ss. 190, 162 and 156 - Indian Telegraph Act, 1885 - Bihar and Orissa Excise Act, 1915, ss. 77(1), (2) and 78(3) - Excise Act, s. 92 - Sea Customs Act, s. 187(A) - Conviction of appellant u/s. 47 (a) of Excise Act - Whether a confession recorded by Excise Inspector who was investigating case is inadmissible u/s. 25 of Indian Evidence Act, 1872? - Held, an Excise Inspector or Sub Inspector in State of Bihar shall be deemed to be an officer in charge of a police station with respect to area to which he is appointed and is in that capacity entitled to investigate any offence under Excise Act within that area without order of a Magistrate - Thus can exercise all powers which an officer in charge of a police station can exercise under Chap. XIV 'of CrPC - As offences under Excise Act are concerned, there is no distinction whatsoever in nature of powers exercises and those which a police officer exercises in relation to offences which it is his duty to prevent and bring to light - It would be logical, therefore, to hold that a confession recorded by him during an investigation into an excise offence cannot reasonably be regarded as anything different from a confession to a police officer - Appeal disposed of.
