Express Newspapers, Limited v Labour Court, Madras, and Another

Supreme Court of India

18 December 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

K.C. Das Gupta

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 14; AIR 1964 SC 806; 1964 (8) FLR 289; 1964 (1) LLJ 9

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Termination - Probationer - Whether High Court was wrong in law in assuming that once period of six months expired employer still continued to be in service of appellant as a probationer? - Employee appointed on probation continues as a probationer even after period of probation if at end of period his services had either not been terminated or he is confirmed - Appointment on probation gives employer no right to terminate service of an employee during probation period except on ground of misconduct - If no action is taken by employer either by way of confirmation or by way of termination, employee continues to be in service as a probationer - Held, HC was right in rejecting management's contention that there was an automatic termination - Appeal dismissed.
