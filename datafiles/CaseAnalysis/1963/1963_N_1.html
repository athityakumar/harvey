State of Andhra Pradesh v N. Venugopal and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 May 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

K.C. Das Gupta, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 176; AIR 1964 SC 33; 1964 CRLJ 16; 1965 (2) MLJ(SC) 87; [1964] 3 S.C.R. 742

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Tort

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 79 - Criminal Procedure Code,1898, ss. 161 and 163 - T. N. District Police Act, 1859, s. 53 - Torture in Police custody - (A) Whether protection of bar of limitation under s. 53 of T. N. District Police Act, 1859, is available to police officer ? - Held, no, protection is not available - Unless act complained of appears to have been done or intended to be done 'under' provisions of Police Act or of other laws conferring powers on police, protection of s. 53 will not be available - Court is to ascertain first what act is complained of and then to examine if there is any provision of Police Act, or other law conferring powers on Police under which it may be said to have been done or intended to be done - An act is not 'under' a provision of law merely because point of time at which it is done coincides with point of time when some act is done in exercise of powers granted by provision or in performance of duty imposed by it - It cannot be said that beating a person suspected of a crime or confining him or sending him away in an injured condition are acts done or intended to be done under provisions of Act or CrPC or any other law - (B) Whether it is mandatory to follow standing order 145? - Further held, no - It is merely directory and not mandatory - Non-compliance with provisions of that order does not make investigation of case illegal - Even if provision that investigation had to be held and completed by a Magistrate had force of law and was mandatory trial would, not be rendered invalid unless it was shown that miscarriage of justice had been caused on account of illegal investigation - Appeal partly allowed.
