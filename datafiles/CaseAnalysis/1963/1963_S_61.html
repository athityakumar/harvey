State of Andhra Pradesh v S. Sree Rama Rao

Supreme Court of India

10 April 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 183; AIR 1963 SC 1723; 1964 (2) LLJ 150; 1964 (1) MLJ(SC) 120; 1964 (1) SCJ 402; [1964] 3 S.C.R. 25

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law; Tort

Keywords: Wrongful

Summary: Constitution - Service - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Respondent dismissed from service - Serving as Sub Inspector of Police - On appeal, order altered into one of removal from service - Whether High Court justified in interfering with departmental proceedings and findings and thereby quashing same? - Held, proceedings before departmental authorities were regular and were not vitiated on account of any breach of rules of natural justice - Conclusions of departmental officers were fully borne out by evidence before them and HC had no jurisdiction to set aside order either on ground that 'approach to evidence was not consistent with approach in a criminal case', nor on ground that HC would have on that evidence come to a different conclusion - Respondent had also ample opportunity of examining his witnesses after he was informed of charge against him - Conclusion recorded by punishing authority was therefore not open to be canvassed, nor was liability of respondent to be punished by removal from service open to question before HC - Appeal allowed.
