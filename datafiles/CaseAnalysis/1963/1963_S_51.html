 Pannalal v State Bombay and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 February 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

K.C. Das Gupta, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 390; AIR 1963 SC 1516; 1964 (66) BomLR 100; 1963 MahLJ 616; 1963 MPLJ 648; [1964] 1 S.C.R. 980

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, O. 41, r. 33 - Contract - Liability of State Govt. - Whether plaintiff was not entitled to any relief as time was essence of contract? - High Court held that Deputy Commr. did not act on behalf of State Govt. in signing contracts - State Govt. not derived benefit from work done by plaintiff - State Govt. not liable in respect of any contracts - Appellate Court can pass order not only between appellant and respondent but also between respondent and respondent - Respondent not filed cross objection within time as in O. 41, r. 33 - Held, HC was wrong in refusing to consider what relief if any, could be granted to plaintiff under O. 41, r. 33 - Matter remitted back to HC.
