 Deputy Director of Consolidation, Azamgarh v Deen Bandhu Rai

Supreme Court of India

23 August 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, S.K. Das, K. Subba Rao, Raghubar Dayal, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 168; AIR 1965 SC 484; [1964] 4 S.C.R. 560

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Buildings - Uttar Pradesh Zamindari Abolition And Land Reforms Act, 1950 - Uttar Pradesh Consolidation of Holdings Act, 1953, ss. 13, 14, 15, 16, 16-A, 18, 19, 20 and 23 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Application for permission to transfer - Rejection by Settlement Officer - If there happened to be conflict between 'a principle' as formulated u/s. 18 or a concrete 'proposal' as confirmed u/s. 23 on one hand and transfer prayed for on other, settlement officer would be entitled to refuse permission to transfer u/s. 16 A(2) of Act - Otherwise application for transfer would be allowed if it satisfied conditions laid down u/ss. 16 A(1) and 16 A(2) of Act and it is for settlement officer to decide whether such conflicts exist or not - Held, where an application for transfer fell within terms of s. 16 A(1) i.e. where it was filed at stage referred to in it, settlement officer is enjoined to allow application unless proposed transfer is likely to defeat scheme of consolidation - Directed to take applications of respondents - Appeal allowed.
