Rejendra Kumar and Others v Rambhai and Others

Supreme Court of India

18 April 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

Brijesh Kumar, D.M. Dharmadhikari, D.P. Mohapatra

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 2090; (2007) 15 SCC 513; AIR 2003 SC 2095; 2002 (48) ALR 331; 2002 (4) AWC 2740; JT 2002 (Supp1) SC 549; 2003 (1) SCW 92

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Accident occurred due to a collision between bus owned by Andhra Pradesh State Road Transport Corporation, respondent No. 3 herein, and lorry owned by respondent No. 1 and insured with respondent No. 2 - In said accident, Smt Susheela Bai, who was a passenger in bus, sustained multiple injuries and expired on same day - Her children are appellants herein - Division Bench reduced compensation amount from Rs. 2,55,000 to Rs. 1,83,000 - Whether Division Bench erred in its decision? - Held, on perusal of order under challenge it is clear that High Court without considering question whether judgment/order sought to be reviewed suffered from any error, entered upon exercise of re-appreciating evidence and on such re-appreciation of evidence re-determined compensation by reducing amount to extent noted earlier - In absence of any such error, finality attached to judgment/order cannot be disturbed - Coming to merits of case, suffice it to say that on perusal of order, which has been reviewed by order under challenge did not suffer from any serious illegality, which called for correction by exercise of review jurisdiction - Appeal allowed.
