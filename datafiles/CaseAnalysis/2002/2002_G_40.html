G.N. Nayak v Goa University and Others

Supreme Court of India

29 January 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

Ruma Pal, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 65; (2002) SCC (L&S) 350; (2002) 2 SCC 712; AIR 2002 SC 790; AIR 2002 SCW 442; 2002 (4) Bom.C.R. 216; 2002 (92) FLR 901; JT 2002 (1) SC 526; 2002(1) SCALE 491; 2002 (1) SCJ 497; [2002] 1 S.C.R. 636; 2002 (2) SLJ 308; 2002 (1) SLT 563; 2002 (1) Supreme 391; 2002 (1) UC 439

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Selection to post

Summary: Service - Goa University Act, 1984, ss. 22, 23, 23(2), 24, 25 - Indian Forest Service (Initial Recruitment) Regulations, 1966 - Selection to post - Seniority - Bias - Appellant and respondent No. 5 applied for post, respondent No. 5 being senior most - Appellant's selection was challenged by respondent No. 5 - Challenge was upheld by HC - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, the respondent No. 2 has, in the note, lavished praise on the performance of the appellant. As the Head of the Department it would be but natural that he formed an opinion as to the abilities of the Readers working under him. It was not the respondent No. 5's case that the respondent No. 2's praise of the appellant was unmerited or that the respondent No. 2 had any extraneous reasons or reasons other than the competence of the appellant for selecting the appellant as Professor. HC was to infer bias merely because at the previous selection in September 1995 the appellant was found unsuitable. If the outcome of the previous selection was conclusive as to the non - suitability of the appellant for all times to come, it was conclusive as far as the respondent No. 5 as well. Yet the respondent No. 5 applied again because he knew that a reappraisal by a new Selection Committee at a later point of time might yield a different result. No Ordinance was drawn to our notice which prescribes a particular mode of rating the respective merits of the candidates. When appointments are being made to posts as high as that of a Professor, it may not be necessary to give marks as the means of assessment. The decision of the HC set aside. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - It is sufficient for litigant to successfully impugn action by establishing reasonable possibility of bias or proving circumstances from which operation of influences affecting fair assessment of merits of the case can be inferred.
