Bharat Sales Limited and Another v Lakshmi Devi Smt. and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 July 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Mohapatra, Brijesh Kumar

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1722; (2002) 5 SCC 629; AIR 2002 SC 2554; 2002 (98) DLT 383; JT 2002 (5) SC 342; 2002 (2) RCR(Rent) 108; 2002(5) SCALE 20; [2002] 3 S.C.R. 1135

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Keywords: Mesne Profits

Summary: Rent Control - Delhi Rent Control Act, 1958, ss. 14(1)(k) and 14(11) - Appellants used suit property in a manner contrary to stipulations in the lease granted by the paramount lessor in favour of landlord - Therefore, paramount passed the order of termination of the lease and of re-entry - The possession of suit property continued with tenants (appellants) - Landlords seek eviction u/cl. (k) of proviso to s. 14(1) of the Act - Controller passed the order of eviction and also order u/s. 14(11) to pay the misuser charges - (A) Whether appellants could be made liable for payment of any amount towards misuser charges as determined u/s. 14(11) of the Act? - Held, appellants are not challenging the order of eviction passed against them and they are ready and willing to deliver vacant possession to the landlord or the paramount lessor - In such circumstances the question of the controller directing the tenant to pay misuser charges does not arise - Order passed by the controller for eviction of appellants maintained - Order passed by the controller u/s. 14(11) determining the misuser charges and apportioning same between the parties set aside - (B) Question as to whom should the tenants be directed to deliver possession of the premises? - Further held, on determination of the lease and the order of re-entry, the right of the landlord to retain possession has been lost and the paramount lessor has acquired right to obtain possession of the leased land ground of eviction contained in cl. (k) of s. 14 r/w s. 14(11) makes the paramount lessor a party to the proceedings and court, therefore, is fully justified in directing delivery of possession to the paramount lessor instead of restoring it to the landlord in the particular facts and circumstances of the case, tenant should deliver possession of the premises to the UOI represented by L&DO - Appeal partly allowed.
