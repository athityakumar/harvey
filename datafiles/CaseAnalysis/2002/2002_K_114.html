State of Rajasthan v Kishanlal

Supreme Court of India

10 May 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Singh, Y.K. Sabharwal

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 338; (2002) 5 SCC 424; (2002) SCC (Cr) 1149; AIR 2002 SC 2250; 2002 (2) CCR 231; 2002 (2) Crimes 401; 2002 (5) CRJ 41; 2002 CRLJ 2963; 2002 (2) JCC 998; JT 2002 (Supp1) SC 240; 2002 (2) RCR(Criminal) 852; 2002(4) SCALE 553; [2002] 3 S.C.R. 1066; 2002 (3) SLT 650; 2002 (4) Supreme 197; 2002 (2) UC 493

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Summary: Indian Penal Code - Section 376 - Rape - Code of Criminal Procedure - Section 377(3) - Held, Section 377 of the Cr. PC is applicable only to an appeal for enhancement of sentence preferred before the High Court and does not apply to an appeal before the Supreme Court under Article 136 of the Constitution of India for enhancement of sentence - Further held, though, the order passed by the High Court under Section 376 of the Indian Penal Code reducing the sentence of the respondent is illegal, but upon consideration of the evidence on record, the truthfulness of the prosecution case that the accused had sexual intercourse with the prosecutrix without her consent is seriously doubted, and therefore, the respondent is entitled to the benefit of doubt.
