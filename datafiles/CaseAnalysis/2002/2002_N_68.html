Joginder Pal v Naval Kishore Behal

Supreme Court of India

10 May 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, B.N. Agrawal

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 337; (2002) 5 SCC 397; AIR 2002 SC 2256; 2002 (4) ALD(SC) 24; 2002 (2) CGLJ 19; JT 2002 (Supp1) SC 219; 2002 (131) PLR 625; 2002 (1) RCR(Rent) 582; 2002(4) SCALE 560; [2002] 3 S.C.R. 1078; 2002 (4) SLT 27; 2002 (4) Supreme 280

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Keywords: Bihar Buildings (Lease, Rent and Eviction) Control Act, 1947, Uttar Pradesh Urban Building (Regulation of Letting, Rent and Eviction) Act, 1972, Mysore Rent Control Act, 1961, Bombay Rent Restriction Act, 1939, West Bengal Premises Rent Control (Temporary Provisions) Act, 1948, East Punjab Urban Rent Restrictions Act, 1949, Bombay Rent, Hotel and lodging House Rates Control Act, 1947, East Punjab Urban Rent Restriction (Amendment) Act, 1956

Summary: East Punjab Urban Rent Restriction Act, 1949 - Section 13(3)(a)(ii) - eviction petition - interpretation of the expression 'for his own use' - Held, the expression 'for his own use' as occurring in Section 13(3)(a)(iii) of the Act must be assigned a wider, liberal and practical meaning, and therefore, the requirement of a member of the family or of a person on whom the landlord is dependent or who is dependent on the landlord can be considered to be the requirement of the landlord for his own use.
