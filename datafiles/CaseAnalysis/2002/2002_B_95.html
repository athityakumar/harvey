Prakash Narain Sharm v Burmah Shell Co-Operative Housing Society Limited

Supreme Court of India

21 August 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, Brijesh Kumar

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1589; (2002) 7 SCC 46; AIR 2002 SC 3062; 2002 (3) ARBLR 1; 2002 (99) DLT 445; 2002 (3) JhrCR(SC) 119; JT 2002 (6) SC 209; 2002 (3) RCR(Civil) 739; 2002(6) SCALE 55; 2002 (5) SLT 1; [2002] Supp1 S.C.R. 643; 2002 (5) Supreme 461

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Constitution; Trusts & Associations

Summary: Trusts & Associations - Co-operative Societies Act, 1912 - Delhi Co-operative Societies Act, 1972, ss. 60 and 93 - Appellant, member of the society illegally and unjustifiably deprived of allotment - Ex-parte award by arbitrator - Restraint order passed by the Civil Court - Whether a Civil Court competent to entertain any civil suit touching a matter which any authority under the Act was competent to adjudicate upon? - Even where exclusion of jurisdiction of the Civil Court is statutorily provided still on availability of requisite grounds the Civil Court can entertain a civil suit - Held, the newly appointed arbitrator to resume proceedings from the date with which the predecessor arbitrator had proceeded ex-parte against Respondent no. 1 after 4-10-1989 subject to the order which may be passed by Civil Court on injunction application filed by the society - Appeal disposed of.
