Jalandhar Improvement Trust v State Of Punjab And Ors

Supreme Court of India

27 November 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

Doraiswamy Raju, P. Venkatarama Reddi

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1457; (2003) 1 SCC 526; AIR 2003 SC 620; 2003 (50) ALR 210; 2003 (1) AWC 439; JT 2002 (10) SC 77; 2003 (1) RCR(Civil) 292; 2004 (1) RD 138; 2002(9) SCALE 194; 2002 (7) SLT 163; [2002] Supp4 S.C.R. 370; 2002 (8) Supreme 559; 2003 (1) UC 428

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land and Property - Land Acquisition Act, ss.28A, 18 - Reference sought u/s.18 for determining enhanced compensation by 4th Respondent - Reference with regard to 4th respondent not maintained for being barred by limitation - Whether claim of children of 4th respondent maintainable? - Held, Yes, claims of children the 4th respondent indisputably is a co-owner alongwith her children who were added as petitioners 2 to 5 to the award dated 5/2/1986, in which case, even on the first principles of law one co-owner is entitled to have the benefit of the enhanced compensation given in respect of the other co-owners in a reference made at his instance in respect of the land acquired, which belonged to all of them, jointly - So far as the fact that in this case the 4th respondent's application for reference u/s.18 was rejected by the Tribunal ultimately on the ground that the reference was made on a belated application, does not make any difference and is no reason to differentiate the claims of such co-owners whose claims came to be really sustained and that of the 4th respondent, for differential treatment - Appeal dismissed.
