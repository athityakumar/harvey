Barium Chemicals Limited v Vishwa Bharati Mining Corporation and another

Supreme Court of India

06 May 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, B.P. Singh

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 2332; (2009) 16 SCC 262

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Stamp Act,1899, ss.35,36 - Admission of document - Payment of duty - Trial Court had directed document to be admitted in evidence for collateral purposes but subject to payment of duty and penalty u/s.35 of the Act - Respondent filed revision before HC which was allowed - Hence, instant Appeal.

Held, order of HC does not conform to the requirements of ss.35,36 of the Act. Document which is not duly stamped and is also not registered though required to be registered can be admitted in evidence for collateral purposes but so far as the stamp duty is concerned, if the document is not duly stamped it has to be dealt with u/s.35 of the Act before it is admitted in evidence failing which, by virtue of s.36 of the Act, admission of document in evidence cannot be questioned at any later stage. Therefore, impugned order of HC is set aside and that of the Trial Court is restored. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - When payment of duty and penalty is justified, then same order is liable to be upheld.
