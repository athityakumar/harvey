Raj Kishor Roy v Kamleshwar Pandey and Another

Supreme Court of India

05 August 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, S.S.M. Quadri

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1637; (2002) 6 SCC 543; (2002) SCC (Cr) 1423; AIR 2002 SC 2861; 2002 (3) CCR 128; 2002 (3) Crimes 67; 2002 (6) CRJ 164; 2002 CRLJ 3780; 2002 (3) JCC 1464; JT 2002 (6) SC 48; 2002 (3) RCR(Criminal) 873; 2002(5) SCALE 471; 2002 (4) SLT 659; 2002 (5) Supreme 190; 2002 (2) UC 563

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 323, 324 and 504 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 197 - Appellant made a complaint that respondent, who is a police officer, had harassed, leveled false charges and involved appellant and his brother in a false case - Judicial Magistrate found that a prima facie case has been made out - High Court held that this would come under purview of s. 197, CrPC, 1973 and respondent could only be said to have over acted in discharge of his duties and thus could only be prosecuted after sanction is obtained from appropriate authority - Whether sanction u/s. 197 CrPC, 1973 required? - S. 197 is mandatory where act has been done by public servant in course of his service or in discharge of his duty - Depending on nature of acts complained of, complaint cannot be quashed at initial stage itself - Question whether these acts were committed and/or whether respondent acted in discharge of his duties could not have been decided in this summary fashion - In this type of case prosecution must be given an opportunity to establish its case by evidence and an opportunity given to defence to establish that he had been acting in official course of his duty - Question whether respondent acted in course of performance of duties and/or whether defence is pretended or fanciful can only be examined during course of trial - Held, question of sanction should be left open to be decided in main judgment which may be delivered upon conclusion of trial - Order accordingly.
