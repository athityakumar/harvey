 T.M.A. Pai Foundation and others v State of Karnataka and Others

Supreme Court of India

31 October 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Kirpal, G.B. Pattanaik, S. Rajendra Babu, V.N. Khare, S.S.M. Quadri

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1375; (2002) 8 SCC 481; AIR 2003 SC 355; AIR 2002 SCW 4957; 2002 (4) AWC 3297; JT 2002 (9) SC 1; 2002(8) SCALE 1; 2002 (6) SLR 627; 2002 (6) SLT 313; [2002] Supp3 S.C.R. 587; 2002 (3) UPLBEC 2817

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure; Trusts & Associations

Summary: Constitution of India, arts. 29(2) and 30(1) - Education - Minority - (A) Is there a fundamental right to set up educational institutions and if so, under which provision? - Held, the right to establish and administer educational institutions is guaranteed under the Constitution to all citizens under arts. 19(1)(g) and 26, and to minorities specifically under art. 30 - (B) Does Unnikrishnan's case require reconsideration? - Held, the scheme framed by the Supreme Court in Unni Krishnan's case and the direction to impose the same, except where it holds that primary education is a fundamental right, is unconstitutional However, the principle that there should not be capitation fee or profiteering is correct -(C) In case of private institutions, can there be Govt. regulations and, if so, to what extent? - Held, unaided professional institutions are entitled to autonomy in their administration while, at the same time, they do not forgo or discard the principle of merit Aided institutions, however, cannot obtain that extent of autonomy in relation to management and administration, but at the same time, it cannot also be treated as an educational institution departmentally run by Govt. or as a wholly owned and controlled Govt. institution and interfere with Constitution of the governing bodies or thrusting the staff without reference to Management - (D) In order to determine the existence of a religious or linguistic minority in relation to article 30, what is to be the unit - State or the country as a whole? - Held, since reorganization of the States in India has been on linguistic lines, therefore for the purpose of determining the minority, the unit will be the State and not the whole of India - (E) To what extent can the rights of aided private minority institutions to administer be regulated? - Held, any law or rule or regulation that would put the educational institutions run by the minorities at a disadvantage when compared to the institutions run by the others will have to be struck down - At the same time, there also cannot be any reverse discrimination - Referred questions answered.
