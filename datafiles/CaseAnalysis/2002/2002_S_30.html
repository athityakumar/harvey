Commissioner of Sales Tax v Sai Publication Fund

Supreme Court of India

22 March 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

Shivaraj V. Patil, B.P. Singh

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 850; (2002) 4 SCC 57; AIR 2002 SC 1582; 2002 (177) CTR 1; [2002] 258 ITR 70; JT 2002 (3) SC 295; 2002(3) SCALE 100; [2002] 2 S.C.R. 743; 2002 (2) SLT 679; 2002 (126) STC 288; 2002 (2) Supreme 529; [2002] 122 TAXMAN 437

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Bombay Sales Tax Act, 1959, ss. 2(5A), 2(11), 2(19), 3 and 52(1)(a) - Whether Trust, 'Sai Publication Fund', which has been set up by some devotees of Saibaba of Shridi for spreading his message, can be held to be a 'dealer' in respect of sale of books, booklets, pamphlets, photos, stickers and other publications containing message of Saibaba and turnover of such publication can be assessed to sales tax under Bombay Sales Tax Act, 1959? - Primary and dominant activity of Trust is to spread message of Saibaba which does not amount to 'business' - It is clear from trust deed and objects contained therein that it was not established with an intention of carrying on business/occupation of selling or supplying goods - Held, it cannot be said that Trust carries on business of selling and supplying goods so as to fall within meaning of 'dealer' u/s. 2(11) of Act - Appeal dismissed.
