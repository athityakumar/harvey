M. Y. Ghorpade v Shivaji Rao M. Poal and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 September 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, Doraiswamy Raju, S.N. Variava

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1790; (2002) 7 SCC 289; AIR 2002 SC 3105; JT 2002 (6) SC 446; 2002 (4) RCR(Civil) 568; 2002(6) SCALE 193; 2002 (5) SLT 107; [2002] Supp2 S.C.R. 99; 2002 (6) Supreme 119

Case Digest

Subject: Election; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Election - Practice & Procedure - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 81, 86 and 117 - Election Petition Rules, r. 8 - Whether there has been non-compliance with s. 117 of Act? - Contention of applicant assailing maintainability of election petition was that there has been non-compliance with s. 117(1) of Act inasmuch as r. 8 of Election Petitions Rules provides that money should be deposited in High Court in cash and that rule must be held to be forming a part of sub-s. (1) of s. 117 - It was thus contended that deposit of money in Reserve Bank of India to credit of Registrar, HC cannot be construed to be a compliance with mandatory requirements of s. 117(1) of Act - Held, there is nothing wrong in procedure adopted in making deposit and when amount so deposited with a pre-receipted challan issued by accounts department to credit of Registrar, HC and Reserve Bank of India made endorsement (received in cash), it must be regarded that payment was made in HC and pre-receipted challan bearing endorsement must be treated as receipt of Registrar - Suffice it to say, that sum of Rs. 2000 must be deposited while filing an election petition and that is undoubtedly mandatory, but through whom amount will be deposited etc cannot be held to be mandatory - Evidence of election petitioner as well as evidence of respondent No. 5 unequivocally pointing out that it is election petitioner who deposited amount of Rs. 2000 - Election petition could not have been dismissed u/s. 86 on ground of non-compliance with s. 117 of Act - Appeal dismissed.
