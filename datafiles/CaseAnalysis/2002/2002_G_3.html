G.V.N. Kameswara Rao v G. Jabilli

Supreme Court of India

10 January 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

K.G. Balakrishnan, D.P. Mohapatra

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 19; (2002) 2 SCC 296; AIR 2002 SC 576; AIR 2002 SCW 162; 2002 (4) ALD(SC) 84; 2002 (46) ALR 383; 2002 (1) AWC 509; 2002 (4) Bom.C.R. 263; 2002 (1) JhrCR(SC) 478; JT 2002 (1) SC 89; 2002 (1) MLJ(SC) 194; 2002 (130) PLR 568; 2002(1) SCALE 111; 2002 (1) SCJ 245; [2002] 1 S.C.R. 153; 2002 (1) SLT 153; 2002 (1) Supreme 123; 2002 (1) UC 338

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Women & Children

Keywords: Grounds For Divorce

Summary: The appellant is entitled to the decree for dissolution of marriage under Section 13(1)(ia) of the Hindu Marriage Act, if the relationship between the parties was irretrievably broken, and because of the non-cooperation and the hostile attitude of the respondent, the appellant was subjected to serious traumatic experience which can safely be termed as 'cruelty'.
