Bibhudatta Mohanty v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

20 March 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

S.S.M. Quadri, S.N. Variava

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 185; (2002) 4 SCC 16; AIR 2002 SC 1503; 2002 (2) CLR 11; 2002 (93) FLR 915; JT 2002 (3) SC 438; 2002 LabIC 1448; 2002(3) SCALE 64; [2002] 2 S.C.R. 613; 2002 (3) SLR 294; 2002 (2) SLT 669; 2002 (2) Supreme 524; 2002 (2) UPLBEC 1288

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Service

Summary: When, the selection is on the basis of merit, merely because the seniority in age of the candidate is also taken into consideration, it would not be right to invalidate the selection. Further, where any rule or guideline provide preference in respect of some higher qualification, it only means that all other requirements being equal, a person possessing higher educational qualification will be preferred, but it cannot, however, be considered as the sole criteria for preference in selection and appointment.
