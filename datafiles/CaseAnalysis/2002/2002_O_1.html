Union of India and Others v O. Chakradhar

Supreme Court of India

19 February 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

Brijesh Kumar, G.B. Pattanaik

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1200; (2002) 3 SCC 146; (2002) SCC (L&S) 361; AIR 2002 SC 1119; 2002 (2) AWC 1264; 2002 (1) CLR 975; 2002 (93) FLR 165; 2002 (2) JhrCR(SC) 85; JT 2002 (2) SC 191; 2002 LabIC 982; 2002 (2) LLN 386; 2002(2) SCALE 214; 2002 (2) SCJ 86; [2002] 1 S.C.R. 1091; 2002 (2) SLJ 275; 2002 (5) SLR 24; 2002 (2) SLT 109; 2002 (2) Supreme 50; 2002 (1) UC 485; 2002 (1) UPLBEC 924

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 311 - Termination - Railway Recruitment Board, issued an advertisement notice for recruitment to posts of junior clerk cum typist - In pursuance of selection held, respondent was appointed a junior clerk cum typist - Respondent preferred a petition before CAT challenging order of his termination - CAT while deciding case held that merely saying that serious irregularities were committed in conducting selection or that typing test was not held, such general allegations could not be enough to take a decision to cancel whole selection - Writ petition preferred by appellant against order of CAT was dismissed is based mainly on ground of violation of principles of natural justice and that notice which was given to candidates was vague which amounted to no notice at all - Appellants have impugned judgment and order passed by High Court, dismissing their writ petition, order passed by CAT by which tribunal had set aside termination of services of respondent - Court is of view that it is not one of those cases where it may have been possible to issue any individual notice of misconduct to each selected and seek his explanation in regard to large scale, wide spread and all pervasive illegalities and irregularities committed by those who conducted selection which may of course possibly be for benefit of those who have been selected but there may be a few who may have deserved selection otherwise but it is difficult to separate cases of some of candidates from rest even if there may be some - Decision in case of Krishna Yadav (supra) has been applied in present case - Held, thus, Railway Board's decision to cancel selection cannot be faulted with - Therefore, order passed by tribunal and HC are set aside and, order of termination of services of respondent is upheld - Appeal allowed.
