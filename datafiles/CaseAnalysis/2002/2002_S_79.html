National Council For Teacher Education and another v Shah Goverdhan Lal Kabra and another

Supreme Court of India

10 September 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, Ruma Pal

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 2357; (2009) 16 SCC 387; 2002 (49) ALR 640; JT 2002 (8) SC 289; 2002 (6) SLR 497; 2003 (1) UPLBEC 162

Case Digest

Subject: Education

Summary: Education - National Council for Teacher Education Act, 1993 - Vacational course - order of derecognition - Parliament enacted Act with object of achieving planned and coordinated development of teacher education system and for proper maintenance of norms and standards in teacher education system - Institution applied for recognition of courses to Council in which regularised recognition for regular course was granted but withdraws recognition for vacational course - Appeal was filed before HC in which HC held that order derecognizing institution was not tenable and same deserved to be quashed and also directed that appellant should recognize vacation course of institution - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether HC was right in issuing direction directing grant of certificate of recognition to vacational course of institution - Held, if course was provided for necessary training period and institution offered regular standard of education there should not be any justification not to grant recognition when correspondence course provided was required to be completed within five years - However, HC committed error in issuing direction in directing grant of certificate of recognition to vacational course of institution by Council - Further, if court was satisfied that order of derecognition was vitiated on some grounds appropriate course would be to strike down said order and remit matter to appropriate authority for reconsideration of matter - Thus, by interim order of Court institution was prevented from taking any admission into vacational course and Judgment of HC was set aside - Appeal disposed of.
