P. John Chandy and Company Private Limited v John P. Thomas

Supreme Court of India

29 April 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

Brijesh Kumar, D.P. Mohapatra

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 305; (2002) 5 SCC 90; AIR 2002 SC 2057; 2002 (2) AllCJ 1336; 2002 (47) ALR 694; JT 2002 (4) SC 444; 2002 (1) RCR(Rent) 569; 2002(4) SCALE 247; [2002] 3 S.C.R. 549; 2002 (3) SLT 542; 2002 (4) Supreme 393

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure; Rent Control

Summary: Kerala Buildings (Lease and Rent Control) Act 1965 - Section 11(4)(i) - eviction sought - appellant-tenant transferred his rights creating sub-leases without the consent of the landlord - Held, the consent as envisaged under Section 11(4)(i) of the Act would mean consent with some positive act, clear cut and without ambiguity which may lead to inference of conferring right on the tenant to sub-let the premises and mere inaction would not be sufficient to amount to implied consent on the part of the landlord.
