S.R. Ejaz v Tamil Nadu Handloom Weavers Co-operative Society Limited

Supreme Court of India

26 February 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

M.B. Shah, B.N. Agrawal

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 128; (2002) 3 SCC 137; AIR 2002 SC 1152; 2002 (1) AllCJ 388; 2002 (48) ALR 73; 2002 (1) ARC 471; 2002 (2) AWC 1337; 2002 (1) CRC 686; 2002 (2) JhrCR(SC) 100; JT 2002 (2) SC 317; 2002 (1) RCR(Rent) 331; 2002(2) SCALE 304; 2002 (2) SCJ 163; [2002] 2 S.C.R. 31; 2002 (2) SLT 202; 2002 (2) Supreme 128; 2002 (1) UC 499

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Land & Property; Trusts & Associations

Summary: When, the appellant was forcibly and illegally dispossessed from the premises by the tenant, then, in such circumstances the High Court was wrong in remanding the matter for reconsideration by the trial court, after lapse of 15 years, since the whole purpose of summary suit under Section 6 of the Specific Relief Act, 1963, for taking possession would be frustrated, and its object of preventing self-help and discouraging people to adopt any foul means to dispossess a person would be defeated.
