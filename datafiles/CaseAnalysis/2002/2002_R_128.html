Range Forest Officer v S. T. Hadimani

Supreme Court of India

15 February 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, B.N. Kirpal

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 843; (2002) 3 SCC 25; (2002) SCC (L&S) 367; AIR 2002 SC 1147; 2002 (2) AWC 1268; 2002 (18) CLR 922; 2002 (93) FLR 179; JT 2002 (2) SC 238; 2002 LabIC 987; 2002 (1) LLJ 1053; 2002 (2) LLN 391; 2002 (2) MLJ(SC) 137; 2002(2) SCALE 242; [2002] 1 S.C.R. 1080; 2002 (2) SLJ 316; 2002 (2) SLR 401; 2002 (2) SLT 154; 2002 (2) Supreme 58; 2002 (1) UPLBEC 920

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Dispute referred to Labour Court - Service terminated without paying retrenchment compensation - (A) Whether Tribunal correct in placing burden on Management to show that there was justification in termination of service? - Held, no - Tribunal was not right in placing onus on Management without first determining on basis of cogent evidence that respondent had worked for more than 240 days in year preceding his termination - It was for claimant to lead evidence to show that he had in fact worked for 240 days in year preceding his termination - (B) Whether that affidavit of workman was sufficient to prove that he had worked for 240 days in a year? - Further held, filing of an affidavit is only his own statement in his favour and that cannot be regarded as sufficient evidence for any Court or Tribunal to come to conclusion that a workman had in fact worked for 240 days in a year - No proof of receipt of salary or wages for 240 days or order or record of appointment or engagement for this period was produced by workman - On this ground alone, award is liable to be set aside - However department states that State is really interested in getting law settled and respondent will be given an employment on compassionate grounds on same terms as he was allegedly engaged prior to his termination - Appeals disposed of.
