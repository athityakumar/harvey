Mohd. Azeem v A. Venkatesh and Another

Supreme Court of India

16 August 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

D.M. Dharmadhikari, M.B. Shah

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1766; (2002) 7 SCC 726; 2003 (1) Bom.C.R. 524; [2003] 114 Comp Cas 25; 2002(8) SCALE 331; 2002 (7) SLT 433B

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Corporate; Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Banking & Finance - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, s. 138 - Criminal Procedure Code, s. 200 - Criminal complaint - Petitioner prosecuting complaint diligently - Attending court on all dates excepting one when he wrongly noted date for hearing - Dismissed complaint for non prosecution - Whether High Court by impugned order erred in dismissed appeal? - Held, there was one singular default in appearance on part of complainant - Also fact that in course of trial, complainant failed to examine witnesses could not be a ground to dismiss his complaint for his absence on one single day - Cause shown by complainant of his absence that he had wrongly noted date, has not been disbelieved and should have been held to be a valid ground for restoration of complaint - Impugned orders of Metropolitan Magistrate and of HC respectively, are set aside - Complaint is restored and learned Magistrate is directed to proceed with trial of case - Order accordingly.
