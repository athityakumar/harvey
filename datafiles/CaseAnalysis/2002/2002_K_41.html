 Kaiser-I-Hind Private Limited and others v National Textile Corporation Limited

Supreme Court of India

25 September 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

M.B. Shah, G.B. Pattanaik, Doraiswamy Raju, S.N. Variava, D.M. Dharmadhikari

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1156; (2002) 8 SCC 182; AIR 2002 SC 3404; 2003 (3) Bom.C.R. 392; JT 2002 (7) SC 339; 2003 (1) MLJ(SC) 129; 2002 (4) RCR(Civil) 445; 2002(7) SCALE 95; 2002 (4) SCJ 421; 2002 (5) SLT 555; [2002] Supp2 S.C.R. 555; 2002 (6) Supreme 608

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Administration of Evacuee Property Act, 1950, Existing Laws, Presidency Small Causes Courts Act, 1882, Essential Supplies Act, 1946, Kerala Essential Articles Control (Temporary Powers) Act, 1961, Bombay Rent, Hotel and lodging House Rates Control Act, 1947, Public Premises (Eviction and Unauthorized Occupants) Act, 1977, Punjab Common Lands (Regulation) Act, 1953, Bihar Buildings, (Lease, Rent and Eviction) Control (Amendment) Act, 1951, Electricity (Supply) Act, 1949, Indian Contract Act 1892, Kerala State Electricity Supply (Kerala Electricity Board and Licensees Areas) Surcharge Order, 1968

Summary: Constitution of India -- Art 254(2) -Repugnancy - Whether the "assent" given by the President under Article 254(2) of the the Constitution of India with regard to the repugnancy of the State legislation and the earlier law made by the Parliament or the existing law could only be qua the "assent" sought by the State with regard to repugnancy of the laws mentioned in the submission made to the President for his consideration before grant of assent? Held- (By majority) No question of giving supremacy to the State Act (Bombay Rent Act) qua the law made by the Parliament. Further, no exception could be taken to the High Court exercising its jurisdiction to verify if there was effective 'consideration by the President' as envisaged by the Constitution.
