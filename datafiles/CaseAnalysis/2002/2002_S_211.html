Tommy Thomas v State Of Goa

Supreme Court of India

04 September 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, Brijesh Kumar

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1031; (2007) 15 SCC 571

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Health & Drug

Keywords: Conviction, Non-compliance Of Provision, Possession of contraband

Summary: Criminal - Health & Drug - Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985, ss. 20(b)(ii), 50(i), 54 - Possession of contraband - Conviction - Non-compliance of provision - Appellant/accused was allegedly in possession of contraband - Trial Court convicted appellant u/s. 20(b)(ii) of the Act - On appeal, HC confirmed order of Trial Court - Hence, instant appeal - Appellant contended that mandatory provisions of s.50 of the Act has not been complied with.

Held, in the present case there is an evidence of PW4. PW4 has categorically stated that the accused had been informed of his right u/s. 50 of the Act. Both the special court as well as the HC has believed the evidence of PW4. No reason to disbelieve the testimony. No substance in the contention that the evidence of PW4 is contrary to the evidence of PW6 and/or PW3. There is evidence that during search 220 gms were found from the pocket of the pants worn by the appellant. Thus, even though 850 gms have been found from the bushes, recovery of 220 gms from the person of the appellant is sufficient to convict the appellant. No infirmity in the judgment of the courts below and no reason to interfere. Appeal dismissed.

Ratio - Asking accused whether he would like to be searched before Magistrate or Gazetted Officer is sufficient compliance of s.50 of the Act.
