Vinodan T. and others v University of Calicut and others

Supreme Court of India

26 April 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

Ruma Pal, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 262; (2002) 4 SCC 726; AIR 2002 SC 1885; 2002 (94) FLR 72; JT 2002 (Supp1) SC 352; 2002 LabIC 1688; 2002(4) SCALE 165; [2002] 3 S.C.R. 530; 2002 (3) SLT 429; 2002 (4) Supreme 60

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Indirect Tax; Service

Summary: The reason for cancellation of the Rank List prepared for the post of Assistant Grade -II by the University of Calicut cannot be termed to be arbitrary or unjust, when, there was a statutory ban with a limited relaxation allowing provisional appointment on the one hand, and the administrative and financial consequences of the creation of Kannur University on the other, and also when, the appellants had not been able to establish that there were any permanent vacancies which existed during that period which could have been filled by the University in accordance with law.
