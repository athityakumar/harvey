Collector of Central Excise, Bangalore v Gammon Far Chems Limited

Supreme Court of India

19 December 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhan, S.S.M. Quadri

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 2111; (2003) 11 SCC 693; 2003 (152) E.L.T. 28; 2003 (54) RLT 856

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - Central Excise Act, 1944, s. 11A - Whether assessee entitled to avail benefit of Notification No. 85/85, D/- 17-03-1985 - Case of Revenue was that by not disclosing fact that Freyssinet Prestressed Concrete Co. Ltd is a subsidiary of Gammon India Ltd of which assessee is a division, there has been a suppression of fact which entitled Collector to invoke proviso to s. 11A - On this aspect, finding recorded by Tribunal is not in accord with case of Revenue - Appeal - Held, it is not open to Revenue to make out a new case for invoking proviso to s. 11A - In view of fact that goods produced by assessee, a division of Gammon India Ltd, and Freyssinet Prestressed Concrete Company Ltd, a subsidiary of Gammon India Ltd, are for and on behalf of Gammon India Ltd, clubbing of clearance of goods cannot be said to be illegal, having regard to provisions of aforesaid notification - Assessee cannot, therefore, avail exemption under notification - Appeals filed by Revenue as well as assessee are dismissed.
