State of Bihar and Another v Dr. Radha Krishna Jha and Others

Supreme Court of India

22 July 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

Brijesh Kumar, D.P. Mohapatra

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1738; (2002) 6 SCC 308; (2002) SCC (L&S) 866; AIR 2002 SC 2755; 2002 (3) JhrCR(SC) 68; JT 2002 (5) SC 307; 2002 LabIC 2936; 2002(5) SCALE 301; 2002 (5) SLR 293; 2002 (4) SLT 487; [2002] Supp1 S.C.R. 269; 2002 (5) Supreme 112

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Whether or not lab assistants could be redesignated and treated as demonstrators and be entitled to all such benefits, pecuniary and otherwise, in matter of promotion? - As matter was examined by learned single judge in a writ petition had categorically held that division bench decision of Patna High Court and that of Supreme Court applied to case in hand and a direction was issued to decide representation in light of those decisions - If state wanted to take a stand that decisions of Supreme Court and that of Patna HC did not apply to facts of present case, they could not say so by means of an administrative order passed on their representation in teeth of judicial finding in judgment of learned single judge - A general direction could not be issued as qualifications and other relevant facts in respect of each lab Asst. may have to be examined by state Govt. while considering their representation - Appeals dismissed.
