Kamalnath v Sudesh Verma

Supreme Court of India

08 January 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, Y.K. Sabharwal

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 8; (2002) 2 SCC 410; AIR 2002 SC 599; JT 2002 (1) SC 15; 2002(1) SCALE 61; 2002 (1) SCJ 26; [2002] 1 S.C.R. 63; 2002 (1) SLT 111; 2002 (1) Supreme 48

Case Digest

Subject: Election; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Election Expenses

Summary: In an election petition alleging corrupt practice on the ground that expenses incurred by the candidate is more than the prescribed limit, it is necessary to aver the fact that whether the expenditure has been incurred by the candidate, his election agent or any other person authorized by him, and whether the candidate has undertaken the liability to reimburse.
