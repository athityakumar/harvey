National Insurance Company Limited, Chandigarh v Nicolletta Rohtagi and Others

Supreme Court of India

17 September 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

V.N. Khare, Ashok Bhan, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1606; (2002) 7 SCC 456; (2002) SCC (Cr) 1788; 2002 (3) ACC 292; AIR 2002 SC 3350; 2002 (6) ALD(SC) 1; 2003 (1) AWC 23; [2002] 112 Comp Cas 257; 2002 (3) JhrCR(SC) 201; JT 2002 (7) SC 251; 2003 (1) LCD 182; 2003 (1) MLJ(SC) 11; 2002 (132) PLR 621; 2002 (4) RCR(Civil) 464; 2002(6) SCALE 569; 2002 (4) SCJ 355; 2002 (5) SLT 363; [2002] Supp2 S.C.R. 456; 2002 (6) Supreme 362; 2003 (1) UC 33

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate

Summary: Miscellaneous - Motor Vehicles act, 1988, ss. 170 and 173 - Where an insured has not preferred an appeal u/s. 173 of Motor Vehicles Act, against an award given by Motor Accidents Claims Tribunal is it open to insurer to prefer an appeal against award by tribunal questioning quantum of compensation, as well as finding as regards negligence of offending vehicle? - Unless conditions precedent specified in s. 170 of 1988 Act are satisfied, an insurance Co. has no right of appeal to challenge award on merits - In any case where an application for permission is erroneously rejected insurer can challenge only that part of order while filing appeal on grounds specified in sub-section (2) of s. 149 of 1988 Act - Held, if no appeal is preferred u/s. 173 of 1988 Act by an insured against award of a tribunal, it is not permissible for an insurer to file an appeal questioning quantum of compensation as well as findings as regards negligence - Civil Appeal No. 5911 of 2002 allowed.
