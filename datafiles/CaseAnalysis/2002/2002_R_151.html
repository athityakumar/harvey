Registered Medical Practitioners Association v Registrar, Maharashtra Council of Indian Medicine and others

Supreme Court of India

08 October 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

V.N. Khare, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 2333; (2009) 16 SCC 315

Case Digest

Subject: Education

Keywords: Maharashtra Medical Practitioners Act, 1961, Bombay Homoeopathic and Biochemic Practitioner's Act, 1959, Maharashtra Medical Practitioners (Amendment) Act, 1978

Summary: Service - Administrative - Hyderabad Medical Act - Maharashtra Medical Practitioners Act, 1961, s.33 - Indian Medicine Central Council Act, 1970 - Right to practice - Amendment - Eligibly - Appellant was Association of Medical Practitioners registered under Act - Appellant claimed as qualified medical practitioners and entitled to practice in Indian system of medicine in district of Sholapur of Maharashtra - Maharashtra State passed 1961 Act and subsequently s.33 of Act was struck down by HC being void - Parliament passed 1970 Act - In view of amendment of Act appellant filed petition before Bombay HC which was dismissed - Hence instant appeal.

Held, members of appellant Association do not fall within any of cls. of amending Act and not entitled to practice in State of Maharashtra. State Govt. had power to relax any condition to enable persons to practice in State. State of Maharashtra submitted no objection if representations were filed. Members of appellant Association were directed to file representations individually within four weeks and State of Maharashtra was directed to consider in accordance with law. Appeal disposed of.

Ratio- Any person who possesses a recognized medical qualification and is enrolled on a State register or the Central register of Indian medicine, he shall be entitled to practice Indian system of medicine in any State
