Rupa Ashok Hurra v Ashok Hurra and Another

Supreme Court of India

10 April 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

U.C. Banerjee, S.S.M. Quadri, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1966; (2002) 4 SCC 388; AIR 2002 SC 1771; 2002 (3) ALD(SC) 54; 2002 (47) ALR 429; 2002 (3) G.L.R. 2138; JT 2002 (3) SC 609; 2002(3) SCALE 406; 2002 (3) SCJ 45; [2002] 2 S.C.R. 1006; 2002 (3) SLT 83; 2002 (3) Supreme 208

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Freedom Of Speech And Expression, Abuse Of Process Of Law

Summary: Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 32 and 124 - Supreme Court Rules, 1966, O. XL, r. 1 - Whether an aggrieved person is entitled to any relief against a final judgment/order of this Court, after dismissal of review petition, either u/art. 32 of Constitution, 1950, or otherwise? - Supreme Court Bar Association v. Union of India and another 1998 Indlaw SC 688 and M. S. Ahlwat v. State of Haryana and another 1999 Indlaw SC 794 and Antulay's case 1988 Indlaw SC 467 - Held, power of review can be exercised u/arts. 136 and 32 by filing curative petition if there is deprivation of fundamental rights but not entitled to second review - Application dismissed without any costs.
