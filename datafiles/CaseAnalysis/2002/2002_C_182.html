Commissioner of Police and others v Acharya Jagadishwarananda Avadhuta and another

Supreme Court of India

17 January 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

V.N. Khare, Ashok Bhan

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 2392; (2004) 12 SCC 809

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Tandava dance in public, Denial of permission

Summary: Constitution - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 25, 26 - Tandava dance in public - Denial of permission - Legality - Respondent sought permission of Commissioner of Police to perform Tandava dance in public - Commissioner accorded permission to take out Tandava dance without knife, live snake, trident or skull - HC held that it was correct that Tandava dance was essential and integral part of Ananda Margi faith based on revised edition of Carya Carya - Hence, instant petition by authority - Whether performance of Tandava dance in public was essential practice - Held, Ananda Margi order was founded in 1955 - Tandava dance was introduced as practice in 1966 - Even without practice of Tandava dance (between 1955 to 1966) Ananda Margi order was in existence - Therefore, Tandava dance was not core upon which Ananda Margi order was founded - Had Tandava dance been core of Ananda Margi faith, then without which Ananda Margi faith could not have existed - There was yet another difficulty in accepting the reasoning of HC that subsequent addition in Carya Carya could constitute Tandava dance as essential part of Ananda Margi faith - As matter of fact if in earlier litigations Court concluded of fact regarding the essential part or practice of religion it would create problematic situations if religion was allowed to circumvent decision of Court by making alteration in its doctrine - If subsequent alterations in doctrine could be allowed to create new essentials, judicial process would then be reduced into useless formality and futile exercise - Once there was finding of fact by competent Court, then all other bodies were estopped from revisiting that conclusion - On this count also decision of HC was liable to be set aside - Hence, practice of Tandava dance in public was not essential part of Ananda Margi faith - Appeal allowed.
