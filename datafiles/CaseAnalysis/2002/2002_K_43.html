Dhannalal v Kalawatibai and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 July 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, B.N. Agrawal

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1309; (2002) 6 SCC 16; AIR 2002 SC 2572; 2002 (5) ALD(SC) 70; 2002 (48) ALR 678; JT 2002 (5) SC 53; 2002 (2) RCR(Rent) 126; 2002(5) SCALE 42; 2002 (3) SCJ 268; 2002 (4) SLT 250; [2002] Supp1 S.C.R. 19; 2002 (4) Supreme 552

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Practice & Procedure; Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - Civil Procedure - Madhya Pradesh Accommodation Control Act, 1961, ss. 12, 23-A and 23 - J - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 6, rr. 14 and 15, O. 37 - Rent Controlling Authority (RCA) found claims under Chap. III-A of M.P. Accommodation Control Act, 1961 for eviction proved and directed to be evicted - High Court dismissed revision petitions - (A)Whether landlords were required to have recourse to jurisdiction of civil court by filing suits for eviction u/s. 12 of Act? - Held, respondents 2 and 3 being major sons of widow, respondent 1, such requirement clearly falls also within purview of s. 23-A(b) of Act therefore proceedings initiated before RCA do not suffer from want of jurisdictional competence - (B) Whether landlords have succeeded in making out a case of bona fide requirement of suit premises within meaning of s. 23-A(b) of Act? - Further held, RCA and HC both, have on a meticulous evaluation of evidence found requirement proved, none of landlords is possessed of any other suitable alternative accommodation of his or her own to satisfy requirement found proved - Appeal dismissed.
