Shanmugam @ Kulandaivalu v State of Tamil Nadu

Supreme Court of India

12 November 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Venkatarama Reddi, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1385; (2002) 10 SCC 4; (2003) SCC (Cr) 1501; AIR 2003 SC 209; 2002 (4) CCR 249; 2003 (1) Crimes 123; 2003 (2) CRJ 466; 2003 CRLJ 418; JT 2002 (9) SC 518; 2003 (1) RCR(Criminal) 753; 2002(8) SCALE 347; 2002 (6) SLT 482; 2002 (8) Supreme 243

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: IPC - Ss. 302, 300 and 304 Part I - conviction of the appellant on the basis of two dying declarations - Held, no good reason to discard the dying declaration since it was recorded by the Judicial Magistrate within a few hours after the victim was admitted in the hospital, in the presence of a Medical Officer of the hospital, while the victim was conscious and was in a position to make the statement- whether the offence falls under Section 300 IPC or the appellant can be convicted for a lesser offence? - Held, the medical opinion unmistakably point to the fact that the bodily injuries inflicted on the deceased were of such nature that they were likely to cause death. - Appellant liable to be punished under the first Part of Section 304 IPC.
