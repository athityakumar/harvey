P.U. Joshi and others v The Accountant General, Ahmedabad and others

Supreme Court of India

19 December 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

Doraiswamy Raju, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1524; (2003) SCC (L&S) 191; (2003) 2 SCC 632; AIR 2003 SC 2156; 2003 (5) ESC 78; 2003 (2) G.L.R. 1676; 2003 (2) JhrCR(SC) 65; 2003 LabIC 435; 2002(9) SCALE 738; 2003 (1) SCW 272; 2003 (1) SLJ 238; 2003 (1) SLT 146; [2002] Supp5 S.C.R. 573; 2003 (1) Supreme 501

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Constitution; Service

Keywords: Accounts And Audit

Summary: Service - Indian Audit and Accounts Department - Establishments therein - Service - Conditions of - Whether can be changed after selection of Govt. servant and whether Courts have jurisdiction to entertain such claims? - Held, questions relating to the constitution, pattern nomenclature of posts, cadres, categories, their creation/abolition, prescription of qualifications and other conditions of service including avenues of promotions and criteria to be fulfilled for such promotions pertain to the field of Policy and within the exclusive discretion and jurisdiction of the State, subject, of course, to the limitations or restrictions envisaged in the constitution It is not for the Statutory Tribunals, at any rate, to direct theGovt.to have a particular method of recruitment or eligibility criteria or avenues of promotion or impose itself by substituting its views for that the State - Further, there is no right in any employee of the State to claim that rules governing conditions of his service should be forever the same as the one when he entered service for all purposes and except for ensuring or safeguarding rights of benefits already earned, acquired or accrued at a particular point of time, a Govt.servant has no right to challenge the authority of the State to amend, alter and bring into force new rules relating to even an existing service - Appeals disposed of .
