Messrs Jaiprakash Industries Limited v Commissioner of Central Excise, Chandigarh

Supreme Court of India

22 November 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, M.B. Shah, D.M. Dharmadhikari

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1420; (2003) 1 SCC 67; AIR 2003 SC 349; 2002 (84) ECC 722; 2003 (106) ECR 617; 2002 (146) E.L.T. 481; JT 2002 (9) SC 419; 2002 (53) RLT 501; 2002(8) SCALE 559; 2002 (6) SLT 669; 2002 (8) Supreme 388

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Central Excise Act - S. 11-A - (a) whether the activity of crushing boulders into bajari amounts to manufacture and whether the "bajari" is a new product having a distinct name, character or use and (b) whether the Respondent could have invoked the extended period of limitation under Section 11-A for the demand under the Show Cause Notice dated 3rd May, 1993 - held, mere failure to pay duty and/ or take out a licence which is not due to any fraud, collusion or willful mis-statement or suppression of fact or contravention of any provision is not sufficient to attract the extended period of limitation - no evidence or proof that the licence was not taken out and/ or duty not paid on account of any fraud, collusion, willful mis-statement or suppression of fact - therefore, the demand under the show cause notice dated 3rd May, 1993 set aside - no reason to decide the other question, viz., whether crushing of stones amounts to manufacture and whether a new product has come into existence.
