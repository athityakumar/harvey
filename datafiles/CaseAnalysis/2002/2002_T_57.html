M/s. Supreme Washers Private Limited v The Commissioner of Central Excise, Pune

Supreme Court of India

04 December 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, B.P. Singh

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1446; (2003) 1 SCC 142; 2003 (85) ECC 9; 2003 (151) E.L.T. 14; JT 2002 (10) SC 27; 2003 (1) KHCACJ 248; 2003 (1) RCR(Civil) 289; 2002 (53) RLT 753; 2002(9) SCALE 76; 2002 (7) SLT 181; 2002 (8) Supreme 472

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - Central Excise Act - Whether the Central Excise & Gold (Control) Appellate Tribunal was justified in accepting the contention of the respondent Department that the three appellants herein are so inter-connected and have mutuality of interest among themselves, as to club their production for the purpose of either denying them the benefit of the exemption limit or to assess them as one unit for the purpose of levy of central excise duty? - Held, the reliance placed by the Tribunal on facts like the three companies having common management, having common procurement of raw material, having common stock accounting and planning, having inter-dependence in manufacturing operations, having common stock of raw materials and semi finished goods, having common use of machinery between the three units, having common marketing arrangements and free flow of finance between three units cumulatively indicates inter-dependence of the three units with each other as also inter-relationship, and cumulatively establishes the appellants inter-relationship and inter-dependence with each other - Appeals allowed.
