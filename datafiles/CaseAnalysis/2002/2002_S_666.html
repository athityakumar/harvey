West U. P. Sugar Mills Association and Others v State of Uttar Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 February 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

V.N. Khare, Ashok Bhan, S.N. Phukan

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1686; (2002) 2 SCC 645; AIR 2002 SC 948; AIR 2002 SCW 644; 2002 (1) AWC 701; 2002 (1) CRC 714; 2002 (2) JhrCR(SC) 49; JT 2002 (1) SC 619; 2002 (1) LCD 323; 2002(2) SCALE 59B; 2002 (1) SCJ 669; [2002] 1 S.C.R. 897; 2002 (1) SLT 749; 2002 (126) STC 190; 2002 (1) Supreme 537; 2002 (1) UC 474

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Trade

Summary: Trade - Practice & Procedure - Companies Act, 1956 - Uttar Pradesh Sugarcane Regulation of supply and Purchase Act, 1953, ss. 18, 28 - Uttar Pradesh Sugarcane Regulation of supply and Purchase Rules, 1954, r. 49 - Uttar Pradesh General Clauses Act, 1904, s. 6-C - Difference of Commission - Old Rule - New Rule - Challenged - Appellant nos. 4 to 22/Sugar Factories were companies incorporated the Act and were engaged in business of production and sale of sugar and raw material required for production of sugar was sugarcane which was purchased from respondents/sugarcane growers through sugarcane cooperative societies - As per representation by appellants before respondent/State Govt. reduced rate of society commission from 5 percent minimum statutory price of sugarcane to 2.69 percent of minimum statutory price of sugarcane and done by notification that existing rule 49 was deleted and in its place new rule 49 was substituted and remained operative from 1 -10-91 to 30 - 9 - 92 - After 30 - 9- 92, Cane Commissioner of Uttar Pradesh issued circular to effect that society commission after 30 - 9 - 92 should be charged at rate of 5 percent of minimum statutory price of sugarcane fixed by Central Govt. on premise that since substituted rule came to be inoperative after 30 - 9 - 92, and old rule 49 had revived - Aggrieved appellants filed writ petition which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether old rule would revive when substituted rule ceased to be operative - Held, relied on B.N. Tiwari v. Union of India and Ors., 1964 Indlaw SC 201, Indian Express Newspaper (Bom.) Pvt. Ltd. and Ors. v. Union of India and Ors., 1984 Indlaw, respondent/State Govt. exercise of power u/s. 28 r/w s.18 of the Act, amended r. 49 by deleting it and substituting same by new r.49 which provided society commission at rate of 2.69 percent of minimum statutory cane price fixed by Govt. of India - Therefore, notification dt. 24 -4-94 which had been extensively extracted above very clearly and in unambiguous terms provided that old rule should be substituted by new rule - In fact, by doing so, Govt. was very clear in its intention that it was substituting old rule by new one - Had Govt. ever intended that after 30 -9- 92 old rule would revive, it could had added proviso to old rule 49 providing for society commission at rate of 2.69 percent with effect from 1 -10-91 to 30 -9- 92 - Further, deliberate omission to provide what had been contained in new r. 49 by way of proviso to old rule 49 shows that State Govt. intended to repeal old rule and substitute it by new r. 49 - Since s. 6-C of has not been applied to statutory rule framed by State Govt. substituted rule after it became inoperative, old r. 49 would not revive - Thus, s. 6-C of 1904 Act had not been applied to statutory rule framed by respondent/State Govt. and substituted rule after it became inoperative, old r. 49 would not revive - Hence, set aside judgment and order and stated that appellant had paid society commission on basis of substituted rule or whatever they had paid towards society commission, as per respondents' demand, appellants would not claim any refund of same - Appeal allowed.
