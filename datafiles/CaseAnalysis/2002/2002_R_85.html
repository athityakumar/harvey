Ram Prasad Sarma v Mani Kumar Subba and others

Supreme Court of India

29 October 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

Brijesh Kumar, R.C. Lahoti

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1185; (2003) 1 SCC 289; AIR 2003 SC 51; JT 2002 (8) SC 517; 2003 (1) RCR(Civil) 58; 2002(8) SCALE 163; 2002 (6) SLT 225; [2002] Supp3 S.C.R. 473; 2002 (7) Supreme 426

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Election; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Corrupt Practices, Election Petition, Booth Capturing

Summary: Representation of the Peoples Act 1951 - S. 86, 82, 81 - prayer for dismissal of the election petition on ground of defect in the true copy of the petition - Any defect of whatever nature, in the true copy supplied to the respondent would not render the petition liable to be dismissed under Section 86 of the Act. The main consideration which would weigh is that the returned candidate must get a correct idea of the allegations of corrupt practices so that it may be possible to understand and meet the charges levelled against him. - Mere omission of indicating the name of the Oath Commissioner or an endorsement in the true copy that the affidavit was attested by on Oath Commissioner bearing his stamp and seal etc. will not be a vital or material deviation from the original nor it may in any manner mislead the returned candidate.
