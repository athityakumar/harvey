Government of A. P. and Others v J. Sridevi and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 April 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

K.G. Balakrishnan, Y.K. Sabharwal

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1242; (2002) 5 SCC 37; AIR 2002 SC 1801; 2002 (4) ALD(SC) 38; 2002 (47) ALR 685; JT 2002 (4) SC 139; 2002(3) SCALE 526; 2002 (3) SCJ 144; [2002] 2 S.C.R. 1147; 2002 (3) SLT 167; 2002 (4) Supreme 341

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Urban Land (Ceiling And Regulation) Act, 1976, ss. 2(h), 5(3), 6 and 28(a) - Whether lands owned by respondents were outside purview of Urban Land (Ceiling And Regulation) Act? - This Court in case of State of A.P. v. N. Audikesava Reddy [2001 Indlaw SC 20388] held that master plan prepared as per law in force even subsequent to enforcement of Act is to be taken into consideration to determine whether a particular piece of land is vacant land or not and view held in Atia Mohammadi Begum was partly overruled to this extent, further, when a statutory authority is vested with power to determine question as to applicability of provisions of Act, it is ordinarily desirable to leave question to be decided by such authority - Held, Division Bench was not justified in declaring that lands owned by these respondents were outside purview of provisions of Urban Land (Ceiling And Regulation) Act and also further directing HUDA to sanction forthwith layout and group housing scheme submitted by respondents, when applications were pending before Special Officer-cum-Competent Authority, High Court should have directed authority to take an appropriate decision - Appeal allowed.
