Jaskirat Datwani v Vidyavati and others

Supreme Court of India

03 May 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, S.S.M. Quadri

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 292; (2002) 5 SCC 647; AIR 2002 SC 2180; 2002 (48) ALR 327; 2002 (98) DLT 6; 2002 (3) JhrCR(SC) 45; JT 2002 (Supp1) SC 418; 2002 (1) RCR(Rent) 716; 2002(4) SCALE 394; 2002 (4) SLT 45; 2002 (4) Supreme 23; 2002 (2) UC 193B

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Ingress and egress - Deceased plaintiff filed against Respondent nos. 7 to 9 claimed that he became absolute owner of suit property - Trial Court held that plaintiff was not entitled to any injunction restraining respondent nos. 7 to 9 from dispossessing him from open space in front of and adjacent to annexe - On appeal filed by plaintiff, Appellate Court directed that 5 feet to 6 feet passage be provided to plaintiff from main gate to annexe after raising brick wall or perforated jalli in order to protect privacy of inhabitants of annexe and main building - Review sought by appellant was dismissed - Hence, instant appeals.

Held, the parties have time and again informed Court that they would file a Memorandum of Settlement. Yet on each occasion, one or the other of the party, raised some frivolous disputes. It is clear that no settlement is possible between the parties. If a wall is constructed as directed then the Appellant's access to the garage and the servant quarters gets completely blocked. At the same time, pending the final hearing of the suit, Respondent nos. 1 to 6 cannot be dispossessed from the annexe. They must also be given access to the annexe building through the main gate of the property. The walls/jallis constructed around the annexe block light and air and prevent ingress and egress to the annexe. Appellant and Respondent nos. 7 to 9 shall permit access to the Respondent nos. 1 to 6 and/or the guest through the main gate of the property. Appeals disposed of.

Ratio - Trial of suit cannot be brought to end merely because interest of party in subject matter of suit is devolved upon another during its pendency.
