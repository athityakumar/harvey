Jyoti K. K. and Others v Kerala Public Service Commission and Others

Supreme Court of India

13 March 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, P. Venkatarama Reddi

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 2267; (2002) 15 SCC 596; 2002 (93) FLR 1205; JT 2002 (Supp1) SC 85

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Kerala State and Subordinate Services Rules, 1956

Summary: Service - Kerala State and Subordinate Services Rules, 1956, r. 13(1) - Rejection of candidature - Legality of - Respondent/Commission invited application for the post of Sub-Engineers (Electrical) in the State Electricity Board - Appellant/Electrical Engineering Degree holders applied for the post - Respondent rejected appellants' candidature on the ground that they possessed higher qualification - Appellant challenged respondent's rejection before HC - HC dismissed appellants' writ petition holding that appointment of appellants would amount to fraud on public - Appellants contended that ever since the inception of the board, persons possessing higher qualifications have been considered and appointed in terms of r. 13(1) of the Rules and the board having accepted higher qualifications to be applicable in all such cases could not exclude appellants - Held, qualification of degree in electrical engineering presupposes the acquisition of the lower qualification of diploma in the subject prescribed for the post, should be considered to be sufficient for post of Sub-Engineers - Rules do not disqualify per se the holders of higher qualifications in the same faculty - When statutory rules have been published and those rules are applicable, it presupposes that everyone concerned with such appointments would be aware of such rules or make himself aware of the rules before making appropriate applications - HC, therefore, was not justified in holding that recruitment of appellants would amount to fraud on the public - However, diploma holders who had been selected by the respondent/Commission have already been appointed and, therefore, it would not be appropriate to disturb those appointments - Therefore, direction issued to the respondents to make appointment of those eligible degree holders who fulfil the qualifications and found suitable for the post on available vacancies - Appeals allowed.
