Deena (Dead) Through LRs v Bharat Singh (Dead) through LRs. and Others

Supreme Court of India

29 July 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Mohapatra, K.G. Balakrishnan

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1742; (2002) 6 SCC 336; AIR 2002 SC 2768; 2002 (5) ALD(SC) 27; 2002 (48) ALR 577; 2002 (4) AWC 2769; 2002 (2) CGLJ 192; 2002 (3) JhrCR(SC) 74; JT 2002 (5) SC 359; 2003 (1) MLJ(SC) 82; 2002 (3) RCR(Civil) 645; 2002(5) SCALE 352; 2002 (3) SCJ 404; 2002 (4) SLT 545; [2002] Supp1 S.C.R. 289; 2002 (5) Supreme 151

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil, 1908, ss. 100, O. 23, r. 1, O. 23, r. 2 - Limitation Act, 1963, s. 14 - Whether non impleadment of a necessary party can be a ground for condoning delay u/s. 14? - Held, no - Non-impleadment of a necessary party, in suit was a clear case of laches on part of plaintiffs - In such circumstances it could not be said that plaintiffs were prosecuting previous suit in good faith - Relied on Rabindra Nath Samuel Dawson 1972 Indlaw SC 660 in which it was held that a person who has registered objection regarding non-joinder of parties at initial stage and also at revisional stage and taken risk of proceeding with suit without impleading necessary parties cannot be said to have acted in good faith taking due care and attention - Consequently, such person will not be entitled to benefit of s. 14 of Act for excluding time spent by him in that proceeding in a fresh suit - Appeal allowed.
