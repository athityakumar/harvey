Prem Bakshi and Others v Dharam Dev and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 January 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Phukan, K.T. Thomas

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 11; (2002) 2 SCC 2; AIR 2002 SC 559; 2002 (1) AWC 484; 2002 (1) JhrCR(SC) 459; JT 2002 (1) SC 34; 2002 (1) MLJ(SC) 145; 2002 (130) PLR 558; 2002 (1) RCR(Civil) 613; 2002 (93) RD 104; 2002(1) SCALE 74; 2002 (1) SCJ 216; [2002] 1 S.C.R. 103; 2002 (1) SLT 124; 2002 (1) Supreme 40; 2002 (1) UC 318

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: The High Court under Section 115 (1) of CPC cannot vary or reverse any order made or any order deciding an issue, in course of a suit or other proceedings, except where (1) the order made would have finally disposed of the suit or other proceedings or (ii) the said order would occasion a failure of justice or cause irreparable injury to the party against whom it is made.
