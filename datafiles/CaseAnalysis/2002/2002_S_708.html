Mohd. Khalid v State of W. B.

Supreme Court of India

03 September 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, B.N. Kirpal, K.G. Balakrishnan

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1784; (2002) 7 SCC 334; (2002) SCC (Cr) 1734; 2002 (2) AllCriC 899; 2002 (3) CCR 303; 2002 (4) Crimes 160; 2002 (6) CRJ 452; JT 2002 (6) SC 486; 2002(6) SCALE 238; 2002 (5) SLT 126; [2002] Supp2 S.C.R. 31; 2002 (6) Supreme 132; 2002 (2) UC 714

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Socio-Economic; Tort

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 164 - Evidence Act 1872, ss. 10, 25 and 30 - Explosive Substances Act 1908, ss. 3, 4 and 5 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120-B, 302, 307, 326 and 436 - Terrorist and Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Amendment Act, 1985, ss. 3 and 15 - (A) Whether any confessional statement recorded u/s. 164, CrPC, 1973 comes within the ambit of s. 10 of the Evidence Act? - Confession of a coaccused for limited purpose can be used in evidence - Held, statements of PWs 40, 67 and 68 along with the confessional statements of the co-accused lend a definite assurance to the prosecution version - (B) Whether charges u/ss. 3(2)(1) and 3(3) of TADA, 1985 and s. 120-B IPC, 1860 justified? - Held, in view of our conclusions that charges u/ss. 3(2)(1) and 3(3) of TADA, 1985 and s. 120-B IPC, 1860 are clearly established, we do not think it necessary to go through a hair-splitting approach vis-a-vis ss. 3 and 4 of the Explosive Substances Act, 1908 - Even if it is accepted that s. 3 of the Act was not applicable and what was applicable is s. 4 of the Act yet it can only be the question of sentence which can be imposed - As the charge is for higher offence, conviction of lesser offence is permissible - We are upholding the award of life sentence for the offences u/s. 120-B IPC, 1860 and ss. 3(2)(1) and 3(3) of TADA, 1985 any reduction in sentence from 10 years to 7 years is really of no consequence - Appeal filed by the prosecution against the acquittal in respect of charges u/ss. 302/34 and 436/34 IPC, 1860 in our opinion the acquittal is justified - Appeals dismissed.
