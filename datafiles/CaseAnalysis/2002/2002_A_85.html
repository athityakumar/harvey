Supreme Court of India

27 November 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

Shivaraj V. Patil, Arijit Pasayat

Where Reported

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: CPC - S. 100 - appeal against the judgments of the High Court - the impugned judgments do not reflect or indicate as to what was the substantial question of law that arose for consideration between the parties, as required under Section 100 of the CPC - Held, in second appeals, substantial question or questions of law must arise for consideration and the appeals must be heard on the substantial question of law so formulated. - Further, the long delay in delivery of judgment is sufficient to set aside the judgment under appeal without expressing any opinion on the merits of the case - Matters remitted to the High Court for disposal in accordance with law.
