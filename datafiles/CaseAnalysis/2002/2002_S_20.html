Commissioner Of Central Excise, Jaipur-II v S.P.B.L. Limited

Supreme Court of India

17 September 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

M.B. Shah, D.M. Dharmadhikari

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1864; 2002 (146) E.L.T. 254; JT 2002 (8) SC 244; 2002 (53) RLT 644

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Evasion Of Duty, Contemporanea Expositio, Excisability of goods, Attorney-General

Summary: Excise - Hot Air Stenter Independent Textile Processors Annual Capacity Determination Rules, 2000 - Central Excise Act, 1944, s. 3 A - Determination of taxability of chambers of a hot air stenter - Whether Appellate Tribunal was justified in deciding that a float drying machine and any other equipment if attached would be deemed to be a chamber of stenter? - Held, Tribunal arrived at a conclusion that Explanation to r. 5 of Rules of 2000 makes it clear that a float drying machine or any other equipments mentioned therein must be for aiding process of heat setting or drying of fabrics - A float drying machine is aiding process of heat setting or drying of fabrics and other equipments contemplated by Explanation-I should also have like utility - Tribunal committed no error in relying upon doctrine of contemporanea exposition to remove ambiguity in understanding language of 1998 Rules - No interference - Appeal dismissed.
