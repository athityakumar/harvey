Jaipal v State of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

01 October 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, Brijesh Kumar

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1136; (2003) 1 SCC 169; (2003) SCC (Cr) 250; AIR 2002 SC 3447; 2002 (2) AllCriC 1197; 2002 (4) CCR 110; 2002 (4) Crimes 461; 2003 (1) CRJ 614; 2002 CRLJ 4703; JT 2002 (8) SC 29; 2002 (4) RCR(Criminal) 486; 2002(7) SCALE 154; 2002 (4) SCJ 544; 2002 (5) SLT 528; [2002] Supp2 S.C.R. 714; 2002 (7) Supreme 172

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: IPC -S 302 -Death by poisoning- Appellant husband convicted by concurrent courts for murdering his wife by poisoning her with Aluminium phosphide -Held, while dealing with a case of circumstantial evidence the Court has to be circumspect - Prosecution has utterly failed in proving such chain of circumstantial evidence as would fasten the guilt on the accused leaving no room for doubt- Appeal allowed
