Mor Modern Cooperative Transport Society Limited v Financial Commissioner and Secretary To Government of Haryana and Another

Supreme Court of India

09 July 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Singh, H.K. Sema, M.B. Shah

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1729; (2002) 6 SCC 269; 2002 (2) ACC 501; AIR 2002 SC 2513; 2002 (48) ALR 472; JT 2002 (5) SC 125; 2002 (3) RCR(Civil) 553; 2002(5) SCALE 145; 2002 (3) SCJ 290; 2002 (4) SLT 340; [2002] Supp1 S.C.R. 87; 2002 (5) Supreme 55

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Keywords: Writ Jurisdiction, Grant Of Stage Carriage Permit, Financial Interest

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, s. 68(2) - Notification D/- 20-02-2001 challenged - Whether Transport Commr. of State of Haryana has any financial interest within meaning of s. 68(2) of Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 in government undertaking known as Haryana Roadways so as to render him ineligible for appointment as Chairman of Regional Transport Authority? - Word 'undertaking' - An entity such as Haryana Roadways, which is engaged in business of providing transport service to people must be held to be an undertaking - Use of words 'any undertaking' also makes it abundantly clear that undertaking may be either a private undertaking or a govt. or public sector undertaking including a statutory corporation - State undertaking such as Haryana Roadways is within contemplation of sub-section (2) of s. 68 of the Act - S. 68(2) read with third proviso makes it explicit that an official of a state transport undertaking who is directly connected with management or operation of transport undertaking is debarred from being appointed a member of Regional Transport Authority - He is a person who has financial interest in transport undertaking within the meaning of that expression in s. 68(2) of the Act - Same can be said of Traffic Manager who functions under General Manager of Haryana Roadways - Transport Commr. is an official of Haryana Roadways and has a financial interest in that undertaking within the meaning of that expression in s. 68(2) of the Act, notification insofar as it relates to appointment of Transport Commr. as Chairman, Regional Transport Authority must be quashed - Appeal allowed.
