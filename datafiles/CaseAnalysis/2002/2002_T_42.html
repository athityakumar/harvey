State of Punjab v Tehal Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 January 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

V.N. Khare, B.N. Agrawal

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1; (2002) 2 SCC 7; AIR 2002 SC 533; JT 2002 (5) SC 40; 2002 (131) PLR 347; 2002(1) SCALE 18; 2002 (1) SCJ 177; [2002] 1 S.C.R. 27; 2002 (1) SLT 84; 2002 (1) Supreme 7

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Parliament & Legislature; Practice & Procedure

Summary: If an act is legislative in character, it is not subject to rule of natural justice, and therefore, if the legislature in its wisdom has not chosen to provide for any opportunity of hearing or observance of principle of natural justice before issue of a declaration, the same cannot be claimed for.
