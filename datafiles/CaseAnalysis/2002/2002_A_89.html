Union of India and Others v Aradhana Trading Company and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 April 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

Brijesh Kumar, B.N. Kirpal, K.G. Balakrishnan

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1940; (2002) 4 SCC 447; AIR 2002 SC 1626; 2002 (46) ARBLR 691; 2002 (1) CRC 754; JT 2002 (3) SC 442; 2002 (131) PLR 225; 2002 (2) RAJ 1; 2002(3) SCALE 248; [2002] 2 S.C.R. 843; 2002 (2) SLT 770; 2002 (3) Supreme 61; 2002 (2) UC 90

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Civil Procedure; Intellectual Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Ex Parte Decree

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration Act, 1940 - Govt. of India Act - Trade Marks Act, 1999 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 9, r. 13 - Whether High Court was justified in entertaining proceedings for making award Rule of Court? - Whether appeal before Division Bench against judgment and order of learned single Judge of Calcutta HC, rejecting application under O. 9, r. 13, CPC for setting aside ex parte decree making Award Rule of Court are maintainable? - Relied on cases of Neeilkantha and Mohindra Supply Co. - Held, by virtue of s. 41 of Arbitration Act shall have all other related powers of ordinary Civil Court subject to constraints contained in special Act itself - Normally, an appeal would be maintainable but there are two constrains as provided under Special Act, namely, it should not be a second appeal as provided u/s. 39(2) of Act which position is also clear in case of Mohindra Supply Company where it was held that second appeal u/s. 100, CPC or under Letters Patent against an appellate order was barred by virtue of sub-s. (2) of s. 39 - Appeals dismissed.
