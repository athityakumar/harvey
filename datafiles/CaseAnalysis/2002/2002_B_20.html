Shiromani Gurdwara Parbandhak Committee, Amritsar v Bagga Singh and Ors.

Supreme Court of India

03 December 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1468; (2003) 1 SCC 619; AIR 2003 SC 599; JT 2002 (10) SC 538; 2003 (1) RCR(Civil) 264; 2002(9) SCALE 92; 2002 (7) SLT 115; [2002] Supp4 S.C.R. 482; 2002 (8) Supreme 542

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land and Property - Sikh Gurdwaras Act, 1925, ss.8, 10, 16(2) -Property in question whether belonged to Gurudwara or to an individual? - Requisites before an institution can be declare as Gurudwara - Held, the property which Sarna Ram claimed, devolved upon him from his Guru Chet Ram, and they were Udasi Sadhus, HC was not in error in holding that the Sant Ram belonged to Udasi order - Sine qua non for an institution, to be treated as Sikh Gurdwara is that there should be established Guru Granth Sahib, and the worship of the same by congregation, and a Nishan Sahib Unless the claim falls within one or the other of the categories enumerated in s.16(2), the institution cannot be declared to be a Sikh Gurdwara - Appeals Dismissed.
