Sheela and others v Firm Prahlad Rai Prem Prakash

Supreme Court of India

04 March 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, Ruma Pal

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 134; (2002) 3 SCC 375; AIR 2002 SC 1264; AIR 2002 SCW 1065; 2002 (5) ALD(SC) 82; 2002 (2) AllCJ 1015; 2002 (47) ALR 415; 2002 (1) CGLJ 319; JT 2002 (2) SC 536; 2002 (131) PLR 66; 2002 (1) RCR(Rent) 351; 2002(2) SCALE 419; 2002 (2) SCJ 256; [2002] 2 S.C.R. 177; 2002 (2) SLT 328; 2002 (2) Supreme 203

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Land & Property; Rent Control

Keywords: Denial Of Title

Summary: To amount to denial of landlord's title or disclaimer of tenancy by tenant, as would entail forfeiture of tenancy rights and incur the liability to be evicted, the tenant should have renounced his character as tenant and in clear and unequivocal terms set up title of the landlord in himself or in a third party. However, a tenant bona fide calling upon the landlord to prove his ownership or putting the landlord to proof of his title so as to protect himself or to earn a protection made available to him by Rent Control Law but without disowning his character of possession over the tenancy premises as tenant cannot be said to have denied the title of landlord or disclaimed the tenancy.
