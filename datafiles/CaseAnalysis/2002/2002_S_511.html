Bhima @ Bhimarao Sida Kamble and Others v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

27 August 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, P. Venkatarama Reddi

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1651; (2002) 7 SCC 33; (2002) SCC (Cr) 1564; AIR 2002 SC 3086; 2002 (3) CCR 245; 2002 (4) Crimes 39; 2002 (6) CRJ 560; 2002 CRLJ 4293; JT 2002 (6) SC 322; 2002 (4) RCR(Criminal) 220; 2002(6) SCALE 140; 2002 (5) SLT 46; [2002] Supp1 S.C.R. 693; 2002 (5) Supreme 555

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 149, 302, 323 and 325 - Murder - Common Object - Whether appellants are liable to be convicted for sharing common intention and committing murder of deceased? - Large number of persons armed with sticks and pelted stones which they found nearby - No proof as to who specifically attacked whom - Evidence does not support that appellants intended to cause death of deceased but to assault him - It is more probable that intention was to give hard beating only - Reasonable inference to be drawn is that common object was to commit offences u/ss. 323 and 325 r/w ss. 147/149, IPC, 1860 and not u/s. 302/149 - Order accordingly.
