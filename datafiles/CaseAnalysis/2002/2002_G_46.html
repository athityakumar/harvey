Thankappan Nadar and Others v Gopala Krishnan and Another

Supreme Court of India

30 April 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Singh, H.K. Sema, M.B. Shah

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 2042; (2002) 9 SCC 393; 2002 (2) AllCriC 251; 2002 (3) AllCriR 2287; JT 2002 (Supp1) SC 474

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860 ss. 147, 148 and 307 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, s. 401 - Respondents convicted by Trial Court - On appeal, Sessions Judge acquitted accused - High Court in revision did not notice any manifest error of law or procedure and set aside acquittal order and conviction was upheld - Whether HC right in convicting accused and setting aside order of sessions judge? - Held, HC has merely re-appreciated evidence and has taken contrary view for setting aside acquittal order, which is not permissible while exercising revisional jurisdiction at instance of de-facto complainant against order of acquittal - Impugned order set aside - Appeal allowed.
