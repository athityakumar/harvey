P.H. Pujar v Kanthi Rajashekhar Kidiyappa and others

Supreme Court of India

05 March 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.K. Sabharwal, B.N. Kirpal, K.G. Balakrishnan

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 144; (2002) 3 SCC 742; AIR 2002 SC 1368; AIR 2002 SCW 1201; JT 2002 (2) SC 613; 2002(2) SCALE 449; 2002 (2) SCJ 275; [2002] 2 S.C.R. 205; 2002 (2) SLT 366; 2002 (2) Supreme 237

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Election

Summary: The recount of the votes cannot be ordered in a casual manner, only because the margin of defeat is meager, but for seeking recount, proper foundation must be laid in the pleadings by setting out material facts and later proving it by adducing requisite evidence so as to do complete justice between the parties.
