P. V. Hemalatha v Kattamkandi Puthiya Maliackal Saheeda and Another

Supreme Court of India

20 May 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

D.M. Dharmadhikari, Brijesh Kumar, D.P. Mohapatra

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1293; (2002) 5 SCC 548; AIR 2002 SC 2445; AIR 2002 SCW 2691; 2002 (3) JhrCR(SC) 34; JT 2002 (Supp1) SC 494; 2002 (1) KarLJ 665; 2002 (3) RCR(Civil) 406; 2002(4) SCALE 662; [2002] 3 S.C.R. 1098; 2002 (4) SLT 145; 2002 (4) Supreme 427

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure , 1908, ss. 96, 98 and 113 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - States Reorganisation Act, 1956, ss. 52, 54, 57 and 69 - Travancore Cochin High Court Act, s. 23 - Kerala High Court Act, 1958, ss. 6, 4 and 9 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898, ss. 307, 374 and 432 - Subordinate Judge dismissed suit filed for specific performance of agreement of sale of Sangam and Pushpa theatres - Appeals preferred to HC - Two Judges constituting Division Bench sharply differed on all issues of fact and mixed issues of fact and law - (A) whether judgment maintainable? - One dismissed appeals and other allowed it - In a case where Judges differ, there is no provision in Kerala HC Act, 1958, governing court to be adopted - Thus, in view of s. 98(2) CPC has to be ordered that all decrees challenged in appeals shall stand confirmed - Proviso to s. 98(2) CPC, states that where Bench is composed of two or other even number of Judges and Judges comprising Bench differ on a point of law, they may state point of law upon which they differ and appeal shall then be heard upon that point only by one or more Judges - (B) whether Letters Patent of Madras HC apply to Kerala HC? - Scheme of States Reorganisation Act, on reading of ss. 52, 54 and 57 makes it manifest that subjects practice and procedure and powers of Judges specifically covered by ss. 54 and 57 respectively are treated separately from jurisdiction dealt with in s. 52 of States Reorganisation Act, 1956 - New legislation for Kerala Act had an overriding effect and operation from date of enforcement of that Act - (C) whether s. 23 of Travancore-Cochin Act would apply? - Law contained in Travancore-Cochin Act and Kerala Act regulating practices, procedure and powers of Chief Justice and Judges of HC in relation to all cases appearing before them is a general law which cannot be made applicable to appeals from CPC regulated by special law that is contained in ss. 96 to 98 of Code - There is a clear conflict between provisions contained in s. 23 of Travancore-Cochin Act which allows reference by differing Judges who have delivered separate judgments or opinions to a third Judge on issues both on fact and law and provisions contained in proviso s. 98(2) CrPC which permits reference to one or more Judges only on difference of opinion on stated question of law , cl. 36 of Letters Patent of Madras HC on practice and procedure and powers of Judges is not applicable to any part of new territory of Kerala and to new HC of that State - S. 98(3) CPC gives an overriding effect to Letters Patent of any HC and if in it there is a provision indicating a procedure for resolving conflict between Judges of a Bench different from one provided in s. 98(2) provisions of Letters Patent of HC shall prevail - Held, in view of conflicting judgments delivered by them decree of subordinate court has to be confirmed strictly in terms of s. 98(2) - Appeals, therefore, could be decided finally in favour or against plaintiff on issues of fact alone without requiring Judges to express opinion on other mixed issues of law - Issues of facts arising between parties in suit and appeal were sufficient to decide cases for or against plaintiff, cleavage of opinion between two Judges on other mixed issues of law and fact, is inconsequential - Thus as opinion of one of Judges on issues of fact was decisive of appeal, it would not be in accordance with established practice of this court to interfere by grant of special leave to appeal - SLP dismissed.
