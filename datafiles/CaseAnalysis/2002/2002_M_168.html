Messrs Shrikrishna Oil Mill v Messrs Radhakrishan Ramchandra

Supreme Court of India

09 January 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Phukan, R.P. Sethi

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 17; (2002) 2 SCC 23; AIR 2002 SC 562; 2002 (3) Bom.C.R. 498; JT 2002 (1) SC 38; 2002 (1) MLJ(SC) 186; 2002 (1) RCR(Rent) 140; 2002(1) SCALE 84; 2002 (1) SCJ 212; [2002] 1 S.C.R. 98; 2002 (1) SLT 180; 2002 (1) Supreme 69

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Keywords: Yearly, Hyderabad Houses (Rent, Eviction and Lease) Control Act, 1954, Non-payment of rent

Summary: Rent Control - Non-payment of rent - Eviction - Hyderabad Houses (Rent, Eviction and Lease) Control Act, 1954 - Respondent-landlord filed application u/s. 15(2)(ii) of Act before Controller for eviction of appellant-tenant on ground of default in payment of rent - Rent Controller held that appellant was defaulter and ordered for his eviction, same was affirmed by Appellate Court - Revision filed by appellant before HC u/s. 26 of the Act was also rejected - Hence, instant appeal - Whether appellant was willful defaulter of rent on date of filing of application for eviction.

Held, arrears of rent were accepted by the landlord in the said civil suit without any protest. At the time of filing of the present eviction petition, landlord had no cause of action as the arrears of rent were paid and accepted by the landlord and, therefore, the petition became infructuous and liable to be rejected. For subsequent default also eviction cannot be ordered in view of the stated legal position and in absence of any legal provision in the Act. Impugned judgment and judgments of the Appellate Court and the Rent Controller set aside. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Suit for eviction filed by landlord only to penalise tenant for having defaulted in past cannot be entertained because once entire dues are paid to landlord, cause of action for filing of suit completely vanishes.
