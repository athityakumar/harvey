Collector of Central Excise, New Delhi v Hindustan Sanitaryware and Industries

Supreme Court of India

10 September 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, S.S.M. Quadri

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1797; (2002) 7 SCC 515; AIR 2002 SC 3162; 2002 (83) ECC 511; 2003 (106) ECR 614; 2002 (145) E.L.T. 3; JT 2002 (7) SC 17; 2002 (52) RLT 384; 2002(6) SCALE 380; 2002 (5) SLT 312; [2002] Supp2 S.C.R. 224; 2002 (6) Supreme 280

Case Digest

Subject: Excise

Summary: Excise - Exemption - Central Excise Tariff Act, 1985 - Whether exemption can be claimed on plaster of paris involved in making of moulds which are in turn used as inputs for manufacture of sanitaryware under Notification No. 217/86 (as amended by Notification No. 82/87-CE )? - Held, exemption valid - Proviso to notification postulates a situation where final product itself is exempted in which case alone exemption of plaster of paris under Notification No. 217/86 cannot be availed of - Exemption is given only to plastic moulds but they are only inputs and not final product in this case - This fact is further clarified by Central Govt. in Notification No. 221/86, as amended by Notification No. 89/89 - Item 6 thereof clearly mentions that plaster of paris moulds are inputs for ceramic products - Further held, plaster of paris which is used as inputs in relation to manufacture of sanitaryware (final product), is exempt under Notification No. 217/86 D\- 2-4-1986, as amended - Appeal dismissed.
