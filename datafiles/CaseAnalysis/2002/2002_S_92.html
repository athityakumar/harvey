Jai Singh v Shakuntala

Supreme Court of India

14 March 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

U.C. Banerjee, Brijesh Kumar

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 162; (2002) 3 SCC 634; AIR 2002 SC 1428; 2002 (3) ALD(SC) 8; 2002 (48) ALR 291; JT 2002 (3) SC 52; 2002 (2) MLJ(SC) 198; 2002(2) SCALE 627; 2002 (2) SCJ 435; [2002] 2 S.C.R. 431; 2002 (2) SLT 524; 2002 (2) Supreme 415; 2002 (1) UC 731

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Keywords: Hindu Adoptions And Maintenance Act, 1956.

Summary: The presumption under Section 16 of the Hindu Adoption and Maintenance Act, 1956 being a rebuttable presumption as the statute prescribes and on the state of evidence available on record question of decrying the order of the trial court as also of the two appellate courts on the fact situation of the matter in issue cannot be termed to be so perverse so as to authorise the Supreme Court to scan the evidence and reappreciate the same.
