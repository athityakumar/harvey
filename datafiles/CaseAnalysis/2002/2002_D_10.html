Commissioner of Income Tax v Damani Brothers and another

Supreme Court of India

11 February 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

Doraiswamy Raju, N. Santosh Hegde

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 814; (2002) 3 SCC 124; AIR 2002 SC 981; 2002 (173) CTR 78; [2002] 254 ITR 91; JT 2002 (5) SC 14; 2002(2) SCALE 113; 2002 (1) SLT 783; 2002 (1) Supreme 577

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 245 C(1) - Whether Special Bench of Settlement Commission right in holding in case of Om Metals and Minerals (P) Ltd., 1991 Indlaw KAR 182 that assessment order passed by AO before admission of settlement application subsisted and recovery proceedings continued even after admission of said application, especially after judgment of Supreme Court in case of CIT v. Express Newspapers Ltd. 1994 Indlaw SC 1975? - Held, decision in Express Newspapers Ltd. has been rendered by a Bench of three learned Judges - On conclusion arrived at that application filed before Settlement Commission in that case was not in compliance with first and foremost requirement of s. 245 C(1) very application was held to be not maintainable and ought to have been rejected in limine - S. 245 C has undergone a specific amendment of considerable significance w.e.f. 01-10-1984, under Taxation Laws (Amendment) Act, 1984 - An answer to questions raised may call for a detailed consideration of views expressed in earlier decisions in order either to further elaborate or to confine them in a manner that is desirable or permissible in terms of scheme, language used and purpose underlying various statutory provision - Therefore, it is more appropriate as well as proper to have matter referred for consideration of a Larger Bench - Matter referred to Larger Bench.
