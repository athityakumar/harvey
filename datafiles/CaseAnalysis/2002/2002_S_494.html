Dhanraj and Others v State of Maharashtra With Smt. Venubai Kelbaji

Supreme Court of India

16 September 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

Brijesh Kumar, R.C. Lahoti

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1659; (2002) 7 SCC 425; (2002) SCC (Cr) 1775; AIR 2002 SC 3302; 2002 (4) Crimes 280; 2003 (1) CRJ 291; 2002 CRLJ 4325; JT 2002 (7) SC 160; 2002 (4) RCR(Criminal) 157; 2002(6) SCALE 474; 2002 (5) SLT 271; 2002 (6) Supreme 374; 2003 (1) UC 118

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Bride Burning

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302 34 - Murder - Dying Declaration - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellants/accused persons murdered deceased by sprinkling kerosene on deceased body and lit to fire - Respondent/State approached Trial Court which convicted appellants u/s. 302 r/w s. 34 IPC - Aggrieved appellants filed appeal before HC which affirmed Trial Court's order - Hence, instant appeal - Whether lower Court's order was sustainable - Held, in Koli Chunilal Savji & Anr vs. State of Gujarat 1999 Indlaw SC 753 relied on case in hand did not solely rest upon dying declaration but eye-witness account was also available - Therefore, considered statement of eye-witnesses and found that Trial Court and HC committed no error in relying upon their statements - In dying declaration while mentioning about incident said to have taken place on 12 -3- 1992 had stated about her mother-in-law/appellant along with others having beat and poured kerosene oil on her but in regard to incident in question which took place on 17 -3- 1992, role of pouring kerosene oil was assigned only to one of appellant and there was no mention of name of appellant nor any role was assigned to her - Thus, Court viewed that said appellant entitles of benefit of doubt - Hence, some of appellants appeal was dismissed and one of appellants was given benefit of doubt and acquitted of all charges and released forthwith unless wanted in any other case - Appeal disposed of.
