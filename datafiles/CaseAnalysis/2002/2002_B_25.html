State of Orissa and Others v Balaram Sahu and Others

Supreme Court of India

29 October 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

Doraiswamy Raju, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1365; (2003) SCC (L&S) 65; (2003) 1 SCC 250; AIR 2003 SC 33; 2003 (1) AWC 273; 2002 (5) ESC 315; 2002 (95) FLR 954; 2003 (1) JhrCR(SC) 49; JT 2002 (8) SC 477; 2002 LabIC 3558; 2002 (3) LLJ 1115; 2002(8) SCALE 178; 2002 (6) SLR 542; 2002 (6) SLT 219; [2002] Supp3 S.C.R. 525; 2002 (7) Supreme 518; 2003 (1) UPLBEC 239

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Fair Wage

Summary: Labour - N.M.R. workers claim for payment of remuneration on the same scale and basis paid to regularly employed staff - principle of 'equal pay for equal work' - Held, equal pay would depend upon not only the nature or the volume of work, but also on the qualitative difference as regards reliability and responsibility as well and though the functions may be the same, but the responsibilities do make a real and substantial difference. - Further, to claim a relief on the basis of equality, is for the claimants to substantiate a clear-cut basis of equivalence and a resultant hostile discrimination before becoming eligible to claim rights on par with the other group vis-a-vis an alleged discrimination. - High Court was in serious error in directing that the respondents should be paid the same salary and allowances as were being paid to the regular employees holding similar posts.
