Central Bureau of Investigation v R. S. Pai and Another

Supreme Court of India

03 April 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

M.B. Shah, Brijesh Kumar, D.M. Dharmadhikari

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1241; (2002) 5 SCC 82; (2002) SCC (Cr) 950; AIR 2002 SC 1644; 2002 (2) CCR 63; 2002 (2) CGLJ 187; 2002 (2) Crimes 159; 2002 (4) CRJ 137; 2002 CRLJ 2029; 2002 (2) JCC 775; JT 2002 (3) SC 460; 2002 (2) KLT 149; 2002 (2) RCR(Criminal) 536; 2002(3) SCALE 262; 2002 (2) SCJ 697; [2002] 2 S.C.R. 889; 2002 (3) SLT 53; 2002 (3) Supreme 73; 2002 (2) UC 61

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Defence & Security Forces; Socio-Economic

Summary: Criminal - Defence & Security Forces - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 173 - Special Court rejected application of appellant for filing additional documents gathered during investigation after submitting charge-sheet - Challenged - Contended by appellant that arguments for framing of charge were not finally heard so no reason to reject application - Held, there is no specific prohibition, it cannot be held that additional documents cannot be produced subsequently - If some mistake is committed in not producing relevant document at time of filing charge-sheet then it is open to investigating officer to produce same with permission of Court - Further held, considering preliminary stage of prosecution and context in which police officer is required to forward to Magistrate all documents or relevant extracts thereof on which prosecution proposes to rely, word 'shall' used in s. 173(5) cannot be interpreted as mandatory, but as directory - Documents gathered during investigation upon which prosecution wants to rely are required to be forwarded to Magistrate, but if there is some omission, it would not mean that remaining documents cannot be produced subsequently - Application filed by appellant for production of additional documents allowed - Appeal allowed.
