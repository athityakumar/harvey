State of Punjab v Harnek Singh

Supreme Court of India

15 February 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

R.P. Sethi, B.P. Singh

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 807; (2002) 3 SCC 481; (2002) SCC (Cr) 659; AIR 2002 SC 1074; 2002 (2) CalLT(SC) 28; 2002 (1) CCR 167; 2002 (1) Crimes 337; 2002 (3) CRJ 340; 2002 CRLJ 1494; JT 2002 (2) SC 70; 2002 (1) RCR(Criminal) 778; 2002(2) SCALE 179; 2002 (2) SCJ 73; [2002] 1 S.C.R. 1060; 2002 (2) SLT 27; 2002 (1) Supreme 642; 2002 (1) UC 385B

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988 s. 5A(1) , 30(2), 6, 24, 30, 17(1) - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 482 - General Clauses Act, 1897 -- Bribe - Conviction - Sustainability - In Appeals FIRs and subsequent proceedings pending against respondents under provisions of the 1988 Act were quashed by HC in exercise of powers vesting in it u/s. 482 CrPC - Respondents had been apprehended while accepting bribe by laying trap under the 1988 Act - HC found that as investigations had not been conducted by authorized officers under the 1988 Act, same were vitiated and deserved to be quashed - Hence, instant appeals - Whether order passed by HC was justified - Held, it was evident that notifications issued by Sate Govt. in exercise of powers conferred u/s. 5A of the 1947 Act, empowering and authorizing Inspectors of Police posted in Special Inquiry Agency of Vigilance dept. to investigate cases registered saved under saving provision of re-enacted 1988 Act - Such notifications were not inconsistent with provisions of re-enacted Act and were deemed to continue in force as having been issued under re-enacted 1988 Act till aforesaid notifications were specifically superseded or withdrawn or modified under the 1988 Act - Investigation conducted by Inspectors of Police authorized in that behalf under the 1947 Act were held to be proper, legal and valid investigation under re-enacted Act and did not suffer from any vice of illegality or jurisdiction - HC had committed mistake of law in holding notifications as not saved under re-enacted 1988 Act - Quashing of proceedings on basis of FIR registered against respondent was held to be illegal and contrary to settled position of law -Hence, judgment of HC was set aside - Appeals allowed.
