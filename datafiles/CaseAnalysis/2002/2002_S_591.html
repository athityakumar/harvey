Kuldip Singh and Another v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

14 August 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Singh, N. Santosh Hegde

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1641; (2002) 6 SCC 757; (2002) SCC (Cr) 1534; AIR 2002 SC 3023; 2002 (3) CCR 174; 2002 (4) Crimes 79; 2002 (6) CRJ 263; 2002 CRLJ 3944; 2002 (3) JCC 1645; JT 2002 (6) SC 97; 2002 (4) RCR(Criminal) 51; 2002(5) SCALE 569; 2002 (4) SLT 759; 2002 (6) Supreme 30

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302 and 201 - Murder - Circumstantial Evidence - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellants/accused person allegedly murdered deceased by strangulation who was wife and daughter of informant - Out of said appellants one appellant/accused person was juvenile, and Competent Court acquitted him - Respondent/State approached Trial Court which convicted appellant/accused persons u/ss. 302 and 201 IPC and acquitted for charges framed against death of informer's daughter - Aggrieved appellant filed appeal before HC which upheld Trial Court's order - Hence, instant appeal - Held, dead body of deceased was recovered pursuant to disclosure statements made by the accused - There was also no scope for any confusion because accused being members of same family could make no mistake about courtyard of one or other - Therefore, it was not possible to hold that dead body of deceased was recovered pursuant to alleged disclosure statements made by appellants herein - Prosecution had therefore failed to establish second incriminating circumstance as well - In view of our finding that evidence regarding confessions made by appellants and recovery of dead body pursuant to disclosure statements made by them was unbelievable and unacceptable, there was no other evidence on record on basis on which conviction of appellants could be upheld - Thus, allowed appeal and set aside impugned judgment and order of HC and acquit appellants of charges leveled against them - appellant No.2 was on bail and bail bonds were discharged - Hence, appellant No.1 should be released forthwith unless required in connection with any other matter - Appeal allowed.
