Devender Pal Singh v State of N.C.T of Delhi and Another

Supreme Court of India

22 March 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, M.B. Shah, B.N. Agrawal

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 201; (2002) 5 SCC 234; (2002) SCC (Cr) 978; AIR 2002 SC 1661; 2002 (2) CCR 35; 2002 (2) Crimes 133; 2002 (4) CRJ 404; 2002 CRLJ 2034; 2002 (97) DLT 57; JT 2002 (3) SC 264; 2002 (2) RCR(Criminal) 515; 2002(3) SCALE 139; 2002 (2) SCJ 628; [2002] 2 S.C.R. 767; 2002 (2) SLT 702; 2002 (3) Supreme 9; 2002 (2) UC 171

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Foreigners

Keywords: Bride Burning, Asylum

Summary: A statement is voluntarily made, if it is intentional, intended, unimpelled by other influences, acting on one's own will, through his own conscience. Further, the essence of criminal conspiracy is an agreement to do an illegal act and such an agreement can be proved either by direct evidence or by circumstantial evidence or by both, and since it is a matter of common experience that direct evidence to prove conspiracy is rarely available, therefore, the circumstances proved before, during and after the occurrence have to be considered to decide about the complicity of the accused.
