Dr. Chandra Prakash and others v State of U.P. and another

Supreme Court of India

04 December 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

Ruma Pal, Brijesh Kumar

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1471; (2002) 10 SCC 710; (2003) SCC (L&S) 808; AIR 2003 SC 588; 2003 (2) ESC 124; 2003 LabIC 379; 2002(9) SCALE 159; 2002 (8) SLR 439; 2002 (7) SLT 288; [2002] Supp4 S.C.R. 574; 2002 (8) Supreme 577

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service

Summary: Service - Seniority of doctors in the Uttar Pradesh Medical Services - Claim to seniority from the date of their initial appointments on the principles laid down in Mathur's case - SC by its judgment dated 4th April 2002 set aside the decision in Tandon's case on the ground that it was in conflict with a larger Bench decision in Mathur's case - Held, a decision cannot be reopened on the principles of res judicata except by way of an application for review - There is however, no application for review to reopen the issues, which were concluded by a Bench of three-Judges of the SC about a decade ago - Further held, since there is no materially distinguishing factor between the circumstances of the writ petitioners in this case and of those in Mathur's case, they would be entitled to the same relief as was granted to the petitioners in Mathur's case and count their seniority from the date of their initial appointments - However, those doctors who were selected in 1972 and 1977-78-79 by the PSC and were not issued any orders of appointment and joined the service on the basis of Tandon's case, will be treated as having been appointed on the date that they actually joined the service and their seniority will be counted from that date - Petitions allowed.
