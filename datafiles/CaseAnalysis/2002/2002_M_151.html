Haryana Financial Corporation and Another v Messrs Jagdamba Oil Mills and Another

Supreme Court of India

28 January 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, B.N. Kirpal, K.G. Balakrishnan

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 61; (2002) 3 SCC 496; AIR 2002 SC 834; AIR 2002 SCW 500; 2002 (2) ALD(SC) 89; 2002 (46) ALR 717; 2002 (1) BC 568; [2002] 110 Comp Cas 20; 2002 (3) CompLJ 8; 2002 (1) CTC 503; 2002 (2) JhrCR(SC) 53; JT 2002 (1) SC 482; 2002 (2) MLJ(SC) 75; 2002 (1) RCR(Civil) 794; 2002(1) SCALE 481; 2002 (1) SCJ 480; [2002] 1 S.C.R. 621; 2002 (1) SLT 571; 2002 (1) Supreme 404; 2002 (1) UPLBEC 937

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Summary: The Central Industrial Financial Corporation is an independent autonomous statutory body having its own constitution and rules to abide by, and functions and obligations to discharge, and unless its action is mala fide, even a wrong decision by it is not open to challenge by the courts or a third party.
