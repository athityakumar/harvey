Epoch Enterrepots v M.V. WON FU

Supreme Court of India

29 October 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

U.C. Banerjee, Y.K. Sabharwal

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1367; (2003) 1 SCC 305; AIR 2003 SC 24; AIR 2002 SCW 4409; 2002 (3) ARBLR 683; JT 2002 (8) SC 546; 2003 (1) RCR(Civil) 65; 2002(8) SCALE 224; 2002 (6) SLT 258; [2002] Supp3 S.C.R. 507; 2002 (7) Supreme 415

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Indian Independence Act, 1947, Admiralty Court Act, 1861, Colonial Courts Of Admiralty Act, 1890, Colonial Courts of Admiralty (India) Act, 1891, Admiralty Act, 1890, Maritime Liens and Mortgages, Brussels, 1926, Brussels Arrest (of Seagoing Ships) Convention 1952, Brussels Maritime Liens Convention 1967

Summary: Colonial Courts of Admiralty Act, 1890 - applicability of the concept of Maritime Lien on the basis of the fixture note and alternatively as to whether the fixture note by itself would give rise to a right in rem thereby enabling the plaintiff to initiate proceedings under Admiralty Jurisdiction of the High Court at Madras - Held, even assuming the agreement has in fact been entered into by the disponent owner, unless sufficient evidence is laid that the charter was by demise, whereby the possession and control of the vessel was given to the disponent owner, question of pursuing the cause of action against the vessel would not arise.
