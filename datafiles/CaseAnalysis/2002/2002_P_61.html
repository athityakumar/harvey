Indian Telephone Industries Limited and Another v Prabhakar H. Manjare and Another

Supreme Court of India

30 October 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

Shivaraj V. Patil, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1382; (2003) SCC (L&S) 46; (2003) 1 SCC 320; AIR 2003 SC 195; 2003 (1) ALD(SC) 77; 2002 (95) FLR 1108; 2003 (1) JhrCR(SC) 83; JT 2002 (9) SC 183; 2002 LabIC 3746; 2002 (3) LLJ 1134; 2002(8) SCALE 235; 2002 (8) SLR 168; 2002 (7) SLR 187; 2002 (6) SLT 291; 2002 (8) Supreme 215

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Constitution; Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Ss. 32(2)(b), 33(2)(b) - Order of Dismissal - Validity of - the orders of dismissal held invalid by the National Industrial Tribunal for non-compliance of the provisions of Section 33(2)(b) of the Act in that wages for one month were not paid - orders of the Tribunal remained unchallenged and reached finality - Held, having not challenged the earlier order dated 1.9.1987, it was not open to the petitioners to make a second application seeking approval for the order of dismissal of the respondent, that too without paying full wages.
