Shrimant Shamrao Suryavanshi and Another v Pralhad Bhairoba Suryavanshi (Dead) By Lrs. and Others

Supreme Court of India

22 January 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

V.N. Khare, Ashok Bhan

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1942; (2002) 3 SCC 676; AIR 2002 SC 960; 2002 (2) AllCJ 477; 2002 (47) ALR 68; 2002 (1) ARC 493; 2002 (2) AWC 918; 2002 (1) CGLJ 313; 2002 (1) CRC 449; 2002 CRLJ 1006; JT 2002 (2) SC 24; 2002 (1) LCD 491; 2002 (2) MLJ 115; 2002 (2) MLJ(SC) 115; 2002 (1) RCR(Rent) 302; 2002(2) SCALE 118; 2002 (1) SCJ 425; [2002] 1 S.C.R. 393; 2002 (1) SLT 778; 2002 (1) Supreme 583; 2002 (1) UC 495

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Equitable Doctrine

Summary: Land & Property - Transfer of Property Act, 1882 - Whether in a suit brought by transferor for recovery of possession of suit property, defendant/transferee can defend or protect his possession over suit property obtained in pursuance of part performance on an agreement to sell u/s. 53 A, even if a suit for specific performance of an agreement to sell has barred by limitation? - Doctrine of part performance - Protection of possession u/s. 53 A - Held, essential conditions to be complied with is contract to transfer for consideration any immovable property - Limitation - Right of defendant to defend or protect his possession under part performance of contract laid down u/s. 53 A - If conditions under sections are complied with law of limitation does not come in way of defendant to protect his possession of suit property even though a suit for specific performance of contract is barred by limitation - Interpretation of statutes - Documents or reports preceding legislation can be legitimately taken into consideration while construing provisions of an Act - Appeals allowed.
