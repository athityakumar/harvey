Union of India and Others v Sonic Electrochem Private Limited and Another

Supreme Court of India

17 September 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, S.S.M. Quadri

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1808; (2002) 7 SCC 435; AIR 2002 SC 3288; 2002 (83) ECC 752; 2003 (107) ECR 10; 2002 (145) E.L.T. 274; JT 2002 (7) SC 192; 2002 (52) RLT 878; 2002(6) SCALE 548; 2002 (4) SCJ 370; 2002 (5) SLT 356; [2002] Supp2 S.C.R. 475; 2002 (6) Supreme 431

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Excise - Practice & Procedure - Central Excise Act, 1944, s. 11-A - Central Excise Rules, 1944, r. 8 - Central Excise Tariff Act, 1985 - Notification No. 160/86CE D/- 1-3-1986 - Whether plastic body, a part of electro mosquito repellent, and 'fragrant mat' are chargeable to excise duty u/cl. 5(f) of Notification No. 160/86-1986 and sub-heading 3307.49, respectively, of Act, 1985? - Essence of marketability is neither in form nor in shape or condition in which manufactured articles are to be found, it is commercial identity of articles known to market for being bought and sold - Plastic body, which is a part of electro mosquito repellent of respondents, is not 'goods' so as to be liable to duty as parts of electro mosquito repellent under para 5(f) of said exemption notification - From process of manufacture of fragrant mat, it cannot but be held that preparation in mat form is similar to that of 'agarbatti' - Held, fragrant mats are classifiable under sub-Heading 3307.41 and not under sub-Heading 3307.49 of Tariff Act - Appeal dismissed.
