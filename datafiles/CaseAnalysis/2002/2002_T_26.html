Hindustan Motors Limited v Tapan Kumar Bhattacharya and Another

Supreme Court of India

12 July 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Mohapatra, P. Venkatarama Reddi

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 2176; 2002 (3) AWC 2444; JT 2002 (5) SC 143; 2002 (3) MLJ(SC) 78; 2002 (8) SLR 78; [2002] Supp1 S.C.R. 127

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Industrial Disputes

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 10(2-A), 11-A and 33(2)(b) - Misconduct of assaulting his supervisor - Dismissed - Whether on facts and circumstances of case Tribunal and division bench of High Court were justified in passing order for payment of full back wages in favour of workman? - Held, u/s. 11-A as amended in 1971, Industrial Tribunal is statutorily mandated, while setting aside order of discharge or dismissal and directing reinstatement of workman to consider terms and conditions, subject to which relief should be granted or to give such other relief to workman including award of any other punishment in lieu of discharge or dismissal, as circumstances of case may require - From award passed by Industrial Tribunal confirmed by division bench of HC it is clear that order for payment of full back wages to workman was passed without any discussion and without stating any reason - Tribunal and HC proceeded on footing that since order of dismissal passed by management was set aside, order of reinstatement with full back wages was to follow as a matter of course - Appeal disposed of.
