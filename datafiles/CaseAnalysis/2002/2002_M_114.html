Delhi Administration (Now Nct of Delhi) v Manoharlal

Supreme Court of India

29 August 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

Doraiswamy Raju, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1652; (2002) 7 SCC 222; (2002) SCC (Cr) 1670; AIR 2002 SC 3088; 2002 (3) CCR 264; 2003 (1) CGLJ 11; 2002 (4) Crimes 122; 2002 (6) CRJ 639B; 2002 CRLJ 4295; 2002 (3) JCC 1718; JT 2002 (6) SC 325; 2002 (4) RCR(Criminal) 92; 2002(6) SCALE 147; 2002 (5) SLT 73; [2002] Supp2 S.C.R. 1; 2002 (6) Supreme 77

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, s. 433(d) - Prevention of Food Adulteration Act, 1954, ss. 2(ia)(a), 7 and 16 - Respondent convicted for an offence under Prevention of Food Adulteration Act, 1954 - Sentence imposed by Trial Court confirmed - High Court commuted sentence - Appeal - Whether HC could order commutation of sentence? - HC was swayed by considerations of judicial comity and propriety and failed to see that merely because this Court has issued directions in some other cases, to deal with fact situation in those other cases HC exercising statutory powers under criminal laws of land, could not afford to assume to itself powers or jurisdiction to do same or similar things - In certain cases Court merely proceeded to give certain directions to dispose of matter in special circumstances noticed by it - Same could not have been mechanically adopted as a general formula to dispose of, as a matter of routine, all cases coming before any or all courts as an universal and invariable solution in all such future cases also - HC had no justifying reason to disturb conclusion of first Appellate Court - S. 433, CrPC, 1973, enacts that appropriate Govt. may, without consent of person sentenced, commute a sentence of simple imprisonment for fine - Presently no discretion has been left with State Govt. to exercise powers u/s. 433(d), CrPC, 1973 - Not only has HC decided to commute but issued a mandatory direction to Govt. with no discretion or liberty left with it, except to 'formalise same', on payment of fine amount specified by Court - Held, HC has assumed powers where statute concerned specifically entrusts it to only appropriate Govt. - Appeal allowed.
