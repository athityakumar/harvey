 State Bank of India v Sarathi Textiles and others

Supreme Court of India

21 August 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, M.B. Shah, Doraiswamy Raju, S.N. Variava, D.M. Dharmadhikari

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 2373; (2009) 16 SCC 328; 2009 (2) BC 696

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance

Summary: Banking & Finance - Civil Procedure - Recovery of Debts Due to Banks and Financial Institutions Act, 1993, ss. 19, 19(20), 22 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 34 rr. 2, 3, 4, 11 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Recovery of debts - Grant of interest - Petitioner was bank which was involved in proceeding initiated against it before Tribunal for recovery of debts - Tribunal ordered in favour of petitioner granting interest at 19 ½% which was to be recovered by petitioner - Appeal was filed against order of Tribunal and Appellate Tribunal reduced interest to 10% - Hence instant petition.

Held, it was urged by petitioner that on interpretation of O. 34 rr. 2, 3, 4, 11 of CPC it was discretion of Court in matter of grant of interest, notwithstanding fact that there are several earlier decisions of Court with regard to same. Act clearly stipulates that provisions of CPC will have no application except certain provisions thereof mentioned in s.22 the Act. Procedure to be followed by the Tribunal on an application being filed by the banks and financial institutions is dealt with u/s. 19 of the Act and s.19 (20) of the Act deals with power of Tribunal in the matter of grant of interest. Special procedure having been provided in Act for recovery of debts due to banks and financial institutions, question of considering provisions of O. 34 rr. 2, 3, 4, 11 of CPC does not arise. Prima facie, s.19 (20) of the Act confers discretion on Tribunal to award interest on application being filed as it thinks fit to meet ends of justice. There is no justification for interference u/art.136 of Constitution against order of Appellate Tribunal. Petition dismissed.

Ratio - When procedure has been provided in special enactment for stated purpose, question of considering provisions of CPC do not arise when clearly stipulated in said enactment except where mentioned.
