Dr. (Mrs.) Renuka Datla and others v Commissioner of Income Tax Karnataka and another

Supreme Court of India

17 December 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

Ruma Pal, B.N. Srikrishna

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1565; (2003) 2 SCC 19; AIR 2003 SC 839; 2002 (179) CTR 218; [2003] 259 ITR 258; 2002(9) SCALE 643; 2003 (1) SCW 288; 2003 (1) SLT 209; [2002] Supp5 S.C.R. 166; 2003 (1) Supreme 515; 173 TAXATION 50; [2002] 126 TAXMAN 427

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax and Direct Taxes - Finance Act, 1998, ss.87(m), 95(1)(c) - Appeal against the order passed by the Commissioner, Income Tax rejecting the appellants' declarations which the appellants had filed under the 'Kar Vivad Samadhan Scheme, 1998'- HC held that the appellant's declaration was rightly rejected because there were no tax arrears as the demand had been conceded to and interest had been directed to be waived by the DGIT - Held, both the CIT(A) as well as the HC have proceeded upon an interpretation of the phrase 'tax arrears' de hors the definition u/s.87(m) - To hold that the modification must also be completed by 31st March, 1998 would mean, that in respect of a determination on 31st March 1998, the appellate order and consequent modification would all have to be completed on the same date - That, given the language, of s.87(m) would be practically impossible and, clearly could not have been intended - HC erred in holding that the entire demand raised on 31st December 1998 had been consented to by the appellant - Respondents directed to consider the declaration filed by the appellant u/s.88 of the Act within a period of eight weeks - Appeals allowed.
