GKN Driveshafts (India) Limited v Income Tax Officer and Others

Supreme Court of India

25 November 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

S.S.M. Quadri, Arijit Pasayat

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1429; (2003) 1 SCC 72; 2002 (179) CTR 11; 2003 (179) CTR 11; [2003] 259 ITR 19; JT 2002 (9) SC 574; 2002(8) SCALE 602; 2002 (6) SLT 693; [2002] Supp4 S.C.R. 359; 2002 (8) Supreme 322; [2002] 125 TAXMAN 963

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax Act, 1961 - Ss. 148 and 143(2) - notices issued under - validity of - the High Court took the view that the appellant could have taken all the objections in its reply to the notices and that, at that stage, the writ petition was premature - held, no justifiable reason to interfere with the order under challenge - when a notice under Section 148 of the Act is issued, the proper course of action for the noticee is to file return and if he so desires, to seek reasons for issuing notices. The assessing officer is bound to furnish reasons within a reasonable time. - On receipt of reasons, the noticee is entitled to file objections to issuance of notice and the assessing officer is bound to dispose of the same by passing a speaking order.
