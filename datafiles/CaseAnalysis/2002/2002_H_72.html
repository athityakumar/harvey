Inder Sain Mittal v Housing Board, Haryana and Others

Supreme Court of India

21 February 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Agrawal, M.B. Shah

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 825; (2002) 3 SCC 175; AIR 2002 SC 1157; 2002 (46) ARBLR 431; JT 2002 (2) SC 247; 2002 (131) PLR 554; 2002 (1) RAJ 396; 2002(2) SCALE 283; 2002 (2) SCJ 156; [2002] 2 S.C.R. 5; 2002 (2) SLT 160; 2002 (2) Supreme 64; 2002 (1) UC 460

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Arbitration and ADR - Arbitration Act, 1940, s. 30 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Whether by their conduct by participating in arbitration proceedings without any protest, parties would be deemed to have waived their right to challenge validity of proceedings and awards? - Held, it cannot be said that continuance of proceedings and rendering of awards therein by arbitrator after his transfer was in disregard of any provision of law much less a mandatory one but, at highest, in breach of agreement - Therefore, by their conduct by participating in arbitration proceedings without any protest, parties would be deemed to have waived their right to challenge validity of proceedings and awards, consequently, objections taken to this effect did not merit any consideration and High Court was not justified in allowing same and setting aside award - Appeal allowed.
