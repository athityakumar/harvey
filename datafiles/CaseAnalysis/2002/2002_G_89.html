State of Punjab v Gurmej Singh

Supreme Court of India

02 August 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

Brijesh Kumar, R.C. Lahoti

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1915; (2002) 6 SCC 663; (2002) SCC (Cr) 1460; AIR 2002 SC 2811; 2002 (3) CCR 124; 2002 (4) Crimes 11; 2002 (6) CRJ 142; 2002 CRLJ 3741; 2002 (2) JCC 1348; JT 2002 (6) SC 299; 2002 (3) RCR(Criminal) 869; 2002(5) SCALE 456; 2002 (4) SLT 639; [2002] Supp1 S.C.R. 427; 2002 (5) Supreme 506; 2002 (2) UC 555

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 326, 324, 450 - Rigorous Imprisonment - Grievous Hurt - Sustainability - Appeal had been preferred by State against judgment and order passed by HC declining to accept reference for confirmation of death sentence, as awarded against respondent Trial Court - Appeal, preferred by accused against his conviction was dismissed upholding his conviction - Sentence was commuted form one of death to imprisonment for life - Hence, instant appeal - Whether award of sentence was to be maintained as imprisonment for life or respondent be sentenced to death as ordered by Trial Court - Held, sentence of fine had been imposed - Out of fine, sum of Rs. 1000 each had been ordered to be given to three injured persons - Balance amount was to go to legal heirs of JS - Appellant submitted that GK lost both her parents as well as her brother in incident and now she was alone and would have become of marriageable age or may have to start some work of her own - She would need some money - In case she could not be compensated amount of fine may be enhanced - While declining to impose penalty of death in place of imprisonment of life on respondent as prayed on behalf of appellant and dismissed their appeal and upheld sentence of life imprisonment on all three counts u/s. 302 IPC, enhance amount of fine from Rs. 5000 to Rs.20000 on each count - Appeal dismissed.
