Haryana State F. C. C. W. Store Limited and Another v Ram Niwas and Another

Supreme Court of India

08 July 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Mohapatra, K.G. Balakrishnan

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1308; (2002) 5 SCC 654; (2002) SCC (L&S) 801; AIR 2003 SC 2495; 2002 (5) ALD(SC) 14; 2002 (3) LLN 746; 2002(5) SCALE 16; [2002] 3 S.C.R. 1151; 2002 (5) SLR 277

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 2(oo)(bb), 10(1) and 25-F - Whether on facts and circumstances of case termination of service of respondents is 'retrenchment" in terms of s. 2(oo) Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Whether s. 2(oo)(bb) has any application in case? - Respondents were appointed on contract basis on payment of daily wages till stocks were disposed of or for a period of three months - Engagement of both respondents was terminated, after stock lying in open was cleared - Industrial dispute raised - Labour Court came to conclusion that respondents were entitled to no relief in case - High Court set aside award passed by Labour Court and ordered reinstatement of writ petitioners in service - Appeals - Labour Court relying on oral and documentary evidence held that engagement/appointment of workmen concerned was for a specific purpose and for a particular period and since purpose for their engagement/appointment was over and period of appointment had expired, their disengagement was in terms of contract of service, and therefore, not a 'retrenchment' within meaning of s. 2(oo) - Held, since there exists a contract of service with terms and conditions, position is inescapable that case of disengagement/termination of workmen concerned did not amount to retrenchment - Labour Court rightly came to conclusion that workmen were entitled to no relief in case - HC was clearly in error in interfering with award passed by Labour Court - Appeals allowed.
