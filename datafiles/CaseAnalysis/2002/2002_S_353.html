Messrs Satnam Overseas (Export) through is v State of Haryana and another Etc

Supreme Court of India

24 October 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

S.S.M. Quadri, Ruma Pal

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1361; (2003) 1 SCC 561; AIR 2003 SC 66; JT 2002 (8) SC 573; 2003 (130) STC 107; [2002] Supp3 S.C.R. 271

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Keywords: Finance Act, 1982, Declared Goods, Tax On The Sale Or Purchase Of Goods, Tax Levied, Collected, Last Purchase, Levy Of Purchase Tax, Penultimate Sale, East Punjab General Sales Tax (Amendment) Act, 1958, East Punjab General Sales Tax (Amendment) Act, 1959

Summary: Haryana General Sales Tax Act, 1973 - S. 9(1)(b), 15-A, 17 - Punjab General Sales Tax Act, 1948 - S. 4-B - Constitution of India - Art. 286 - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956 - interpretation of the Punjab Act and Haryana Act in the light of the provisions of Article 286 of the Constitution and the CST Act - liability to pay purchase tax on raw material - Held, mere similarity between Section 9(1)(b) of the Haryana Act and Section 4-B of the Punjab Act would not relieve a dealer of the liability to pay purchase tax on paddy as the scope of charging sections under the said Acts are different.
