Shamim Ara v State of Uttar Pradesh and Another

Supreme Court of India

01 October 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, P. Venkatarama Reddi

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1133; (2002) 7 SCC 518; (2002) SCC (Cr) 1814; AIR 2002 SC 3551; 2002 (4) CCR 105; 2002 (4) Crimes 378; 2003 (1) CRJ 661; 2002 CRLJ 4726; 2002 (100) DLT 58; 2003 (1) G.L.R. 80; 2002 (3) JCC 1870; 2003 (1) JhrCR(SC) 278; JT 2002 (7) SC 520; 2002 (3) KLT 537; 2002 (4) RCR(Civil) 340B; 2002 (4) RCR(Criminal) 636; 2002(7) SCALE 183; 2002 (4) SCJ 517; 2002 (5) SLT 538; [2002] Supp3 S.C.R. 19; 2002 (7) Supreme 39; 2003 (1) UC 368

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Women & Children

Summary: Mulim Law - Talaq, when effective. Held, Talaq to be effective has to be pronounced. The mere plea taken in the written statement of a divorce having been pronounced sometime in the past cannot by itself be treated as effectuating talaq on the date of delivery of the copy of the written statement to the wife.
