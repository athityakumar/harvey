Mohd. Hoshan and Another v State of Andhra Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

16 September 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

Shivaraj V. Patil, U.C. Banerjee

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1658; (2002) 7 SCC 414; (2002) SCC (Cr) 1765; AIR 2002 SC 3270; 2002 (4) Crimes 356; 2003 (1) CRJ 92; 2002 CRLJ 4124; 2002 (3) JhrCR(SC) 224; JT 2002 (7) SC 238; 2002 (4) RCR(Criminal) 155; 2002(6) SCALE 488; 2002 (5) SLT 390; [2002] Supp2 S.C.R. 408; 2002 (6) Supreme 562; 2003 (1) UC 44

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 304-B, 306 and 498-A - (A) Whether High Court committed an error in reversing order of acquittal made by Trial Court? - Held, mental cruelty practised on deceased was a grave and serious provocation for an ordinary Indian women - Trial Court in acquitting appellants was not a reasonable and justifiable view which could have been taken looking to evidence keeping in view well settled principles - High Court was right and justified in reversing order of acquittal and convicting and sentencing appellants for offences u/s. 306 and 498-A IPC - (B) Whether HC was right in accepting dying declaration as rejected by Trial Court? - Further held, Trial Court committed manifest error in disbelieving dying declaration - Dying declaration was attested by doctor on duty - Deceased had made dying declaration in question-answer form in Urdu and he had translated version and recorded declaration in English - Thumb impression of deceased on her declaration - Appeal disposed of.
