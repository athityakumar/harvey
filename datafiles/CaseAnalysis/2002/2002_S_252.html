Abdul Waheed Khan Alias Waheed and Others v State of Andhra Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

27 August 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Ruma Pal

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1777; (2002) 7 SCC 175; AIR 2002 SC 2961; 2002 (2) AllCriC 815; 2002 (3) AllCriR 2667; 2002 (3) CCR 257; 2002 (4) Crimes 46; 2002 (6) CRJ 594; 2002 (3) JCC 1656; JT 2002 (6) SC 274; 2002 (4) RCR(Criminal) 265; 2002(6) SCALE 117; 2002 (5) SLT 65; [2002] Supp1 S.C.R. 703; 2002 (5) Supreme 562

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Defence & Security Forces

Summary: Criminal - Arms Act, 1959, s. 25 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 120B, 212, 299, 300, 302, 392 and 411 - Accused-appellants challenged their conviction - It is intention prevailing at time of assaults, which determines applicability of relevant provisions - Held, stab wounds as well as head injury are individually sufficient to cause death - Taking into account totality of circumstances conviction recorded by HC u/s. 302, IPC cannot be faulted - Appeal dismissed.
