C. V. Rajendran and Another v N. M. Muhammed Kunhi

Supreme Court of India

13 September 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, S.S.M. Quadri

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1805; (2002) 7 SCC 447; AIR 2003 SC 649; JT 2002 (7) SC 174; 2002 (3) KLT 461; 2002 (4) RCR(Civil) 512; 2002 (2) RCR(Rent) 453; 2002 (2) RCR(Rent) 488; 2002(6) SCALE 485; 2002 (4) SCJ 318; 2002 (5) SLT 388; [2002] Supp2 S.C.R. 390; 2002 (6) Supreme 409

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Rent Control - Kerala Buildings (Lease and Rent Control) Act, 1965, ss. 11 and 15 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 105 - Whether order of remand passed by Rent Control Appellate Authority, not barred by s. 15 of Act, can be permitted to be re agitated in proceedings arising from order passed by Rent Controller pursuant to order of remand? - Held, no - Principle of res judicata applies as between two stages in same litigation so that if an issue has been decided at an earlier stage against a party, it cannot be allowed to be re agitated by him at a subsequent stage in same suit or proceedings - Appeal dismissed.
