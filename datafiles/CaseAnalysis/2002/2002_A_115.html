General Finance Company and Another v Assistant Commissioner of Income Tax

Supreme Court of India

04 September 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, P. Venkatarama Reddi

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1714; (2002) 7 SCC 1; AIR 2002 SC 3126; 2002 (3) CCR 301; 2002 (6) CRJ 737; 2002 (176) CTR 569; [2002] 257 ITR 338; JT 2002 (6) SC 426; 2002 (4) RCR(Civil) 708B; 2002 (4) RCR(Criminal) 848; 2002(6) SCALE 198; 2002 (5) SLT 123; [2002] Supp2 S.C.R. 106; 2002 (6) Supreme 116; [2002] 124 TAXMAN 432; 2002 (2) UC 664

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Direct Tax Laws (Amendment) Act, 1987 - General Clauses Act, 1897 -

Income-tax Act, 1961 - General Clauses - Validity - Appellants sought for quashing of proceedings for prosecution u/s. 276DD of the Act by filing petition u/s. 482 CrPC and art. 227 of Constitution - HC held that provisions of the Act under which appellants had been prosecuted were in force during accounting year relevant to a/y 1986-87 and they stood omitted from statute book only from 1-4-1989 - HC dismissed writ petition - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order passed by HC was justified - Held, view taken by HC was not consistent with what had been stated by SC in principle underlying s. 6 of the 1897 Act as saving right to initiate proceedings for liabilities incurred during currency would not apply to omission of provision in 1897 Act but only to repeal, omission being different from repeal - In Income Tax Act, s. 276DD stood omitted but not repealed and hence, prosecution could not have been launched or continued by invoking s. 6 of the 1897 Act after its omission - Hence, order of HC was set aside - Appeal allowed.
