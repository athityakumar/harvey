B. Ramanjini and Others v State of Andhra Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

26 April 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1285; (2002) 5 SCC 533; (2002) SCC (L&S) 780; AIR 2002 SC 2023; 2002 (2) JhrCR(SC) 155; JT 2002 (4) SC 526; 2002 (35) LabIC 1697; 2002(4) SCALE 197; [2002] 3 S.C.R. 506; 2002 (4) SLR 9; 2002 (3) SLT 674; 2002 (3) Supreme 617; 2002 (3) UPLBEC 2006

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Re-Examination, Selection process, Mass copying

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 21 - Examination - Mass copying - Application was filed before Tribunal by respondent No.5 for declaration of results of District Selection Committee written test for declaration that it was arbitrary, illegal and violative of art. 21 of the Constitution and for direction to declare appropriate results - Tribunal noticed that inasmuch as Govt. already cancelled examinations did not consider it fit to order enquiry into various lapses and held that main relief to declare results had become infructuous and disposed of application - HC felt that there was no distinction between case of districts.

Held, not only that there was scope for mass copying and mass copying did take place in addition to leakage of question papers which was brazenly published in a newspaper and the photocopies of the question papers were available for sale at a price of Rs.2,000/- each. Further, even if it was not a case of mass copying or leakage of question papers or such other circumstance, it is clear in the conduct of the examination, a fair procedure has to be adopted. One cannot have an advantage either by copying or by having a fore-knowledge of the question paper or otherwise. It is clear that such persons would not be entitled to get relief at the hands of the court. Appeals allowed.

Service - Administrative Tribunals Act, 1985, s. 8 - Appointment - HC while dealing with appointment of teachers also dealt with Writ Petition and gave directions.

Held, it was not proper for the HC to have issued any of these directions, particularly directions relating to the scheme of the Act. The HC has been carried away by some kind of adventurism and virtually tried to overreach what this Court has stated which course should have been avoided at all costs. The orders of the HC set aside. Appeals allowed.

Service - Andhra Pradesh Education Act, 1982, ss. 78, 79 - Andhra Pradesh Panchayats Act, 1994, ss. 169(4), 195(4), 268 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 309 - Selection process - Re-examination - Appellants were selected and appointment orders were issued in proceedings of Chief Executive Officer - Since mass copying and leakage of question papers were reported Govt. having conducted enquiry ordered re-examination in that particular district alone - HC held notification to be illegal as same was not issued in exercise of rule making power u/ss. 78 and 79 of 1982 Act, ss. 169(4), 195(4) and 268 of 1994 Act or under the proviso to art. 309 of the Constitution.

Held, the persons selected were appointed pursuant to the selections made and had been performing their duties. However, the selected candidates had not been impleaded as parties to the proceedings either in their individual capacity or in any representative capacity. HC ought not to have examined any of the questions raised before it in the proceedings initiated before it. Order made by the HC shall stand set aside and the writ petitions filed by the petitioners shall stand dismissed. Appeals allowed.

Ratio - Ordinarily some time would be taken for most of members to get used to service-jurisprudence and when period is only five years, many would have to go out by time they are fully acquainted with law and have good grip over job.
