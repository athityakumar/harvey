Rajesh Gulati v Government of Nct of Delhi and Another

Supreme Court of India

29 August 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

Ruma Pal, Arijit Pasayat

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1653; (2002) 7 SCC 129; (2002) SCC (Cr) 1627; AIR 2002 SC 3094; 2002 (3) CCR 267; 2002 (4) Crimes 86; 2002 (6) CRJ 654; 2002 CRLJ 4299; 2002 (99) DLT 508; 2002 (83) ECC 281; 2002 (104) ECR 918; JT 2002 (6) SC 331; 2002 (4) RCR(Criminal) 89; 2002 (52) RLT 123; 2002(6) SCALE 142; 2002 (5) SLT 76; 2002 (6) Supreme 37; 2002 (2) UC 733

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Conservation of Foreign Exchange and Prevention of Smuggling Activities Act, 1974, s. 3(1) - Customs Act, 1962, ss. 108, 132 and 135(1)(a) - Appellant challenged his detention passed u/s. 3(1) - Supreme Court held that law permitting preventive detention must be meticulously followed both substantively and procedurally by detaining authority - Icchu Devi Choraria v. UOI, 1980 Indlaw SC 191 - Facts of this case show a breach of both - S. 3(1) of Act allows detention of a person only if appropriate detaining authority is satisfied that with a view to preventing such person from carrying on any of offensive activities enumerated therein, it is necessary to detain such person - Satisfaction of detaining authority is not a subjective one based on detaining authority's emotions, beliefs or prejudices - Held, it was not detaining authority's case that appellant was a die-hard smuggler - None of instances of smuggling by appellant as stated in impugned detention order describe appellant as having travelled without a passport for purpose of smuggling - Absence of his passport appellant could or would be able to continue his activities is based on no material but was a piece of pure speculation - Impugned order of detention is quashed - Appeal allowed.
