Chandra Prakash Tiwari and Others v Shakuntala Shukla and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 May 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

U.C. Banerjee, G.B. Pattanaik

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1720; (2002) 6 SCC 127; (2002) SCC (L&S) 830; AIR 2002 SC 2322; 2002 (4) AWC 2657; JT 2002 (Supp1) SC 159; 2002 LabIC 2168; 2002(4) SCALE 528; [2002] 3 S.C.R. 948; 2002 (7) SLR 241; 2002 (4) SLT 5; 2002 (4) Supreme 218

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces; Service

Summary: Service - Government of India Act, 1935 - Indian Independence Act, 1947 - Police Act, 1861, s. 46(2) - Civil Services (Classification, Control and Appeal) Rules - Whether selection as effected was to be made under specific police related order of 05-11-1965 or basis of seniority under General Uttar Pradesh Govt. Servants (Criterion for Recruitment by Promotion) Rules, 1994 framed u/art. 309 of Constitution? - Held, there is special conferment of power for framing of rules dealt with more fully hereinbefore, which would prevail over any other rule - Since no other rule stands formulated and Govt. order of 1965 being taken as existing rule pertaining to subject-matter presently under consideration with recent guidelines as noted above, its applicability cannot be doubted - Unless general rules specifically repeal effectiveness of special rules, question of latter rules becoming ineffective or inoperative would not arise - Appeal allowed.
