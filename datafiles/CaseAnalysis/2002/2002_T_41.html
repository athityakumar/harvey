Commissioner Of Central Excise, Cochin v Tata Tea Ltd

Supreme Court of India

05 May 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

Shivaraj V. Patil, N. Santosh Hegde

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1329; (2002) 9 SCC 17; AIR 2002 SC 2046; 2002 (81) ECC 225; 2002 (102) ECR 257; 2002 (142) E.L.T. 3; JT 2002 (4) SC 484; 2002 (50) RLT 126; 2002(4) SCALE 319; [2002] 3 S.C.R. 730; 2002 (3) SLT 566; 2002 (128) STC 331; 2002 (4) Supreme 114

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax; Agriculture & Agri. Products

Keywords: Brand Names, Attorney-General

Summary: Agriculture & Agri Products - Tea Act, 1953, ss. 3(n) and 25 - Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944 - Tea Waste (Control) Order, 1959 - Prevention of Food Adulteration Rules, 1955 - Levy of cess - Legality of - Whether 'instant tea' manufactured and exported by respondent is liable for levy of cess u/s. 25 of Tea Act? - Held, in order to satisfy definition of 'tea' u/s. 3(n), a product should be commercially known as tea and it should be made from leaves of plant of Camellia Sinensis (L) O Kuntze - 'Instant tea' satisfies both these conditions - By the very name, the product namely 'instant tea' conveys that it is a 'tea' - The term 'instant tea' is not the brand name of the product manufactured by the assessee but the name of the product itself - It is a variety of tea - Further, the term 'instant tea' gives a meaning that it is a 'tea', which can be prepared/used instantaneously - Merely because the product is known as 'instant tea', it does not cease to be known commercially as 'tea' - Whether tea is consumed as hot beverage or a cold beverage depending upon one's liking and taste, it does not make any difference in deciding whether it is a tea falling within the definition of s. 3(n) of the Act - The manner of preparation of tea and the process of manufacture of 'instant tea' powder cannot take away 'instant tea' out of definition of 'tea' under the Act - Once 'instant tea' falls within definition of s. 3(n), a cess can be levied on it u/s. 25 of Act - Appeals allowed.
