Neelakantan and Others v Mallika Begum

Supreme Court of India

29 January 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

Brijesh Kumar, R.C. Lahoti

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 62; (2002) 2 SCC 440; AIR 2002 SC 827; 2002 (1) CTC 439; 2002 (2) JhrCR(SC) 63; JT 2002 (1) SC 433; 2002 (1) MLJ(SC) 166; 2002 (1) RCR(Rent) 166; 2002(1) SCALE 512; 2002 (1) SLT 548; 2002 (1) Supreme 369

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Ex-Parte

Summary: The High Court was right in holding that so as to be entitled for relief for injunction as prayed, the plaintiff tenants were legally required to prove by legal and cogent evidence, the identity of the property in suit in reference to Door number, which may have been declared as slum area under Section 3 of the Tamil Nadu Slum Areas (Improvement and Clearance) Act, 1971.
