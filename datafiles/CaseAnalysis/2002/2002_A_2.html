Assistant Director of Inspection Investigation and another v A. B. Shanthi and another

Supreme Court of India

03 May 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

K.G. Balakrishnan, R.P. Sethi

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1724; (2002) 6 SCC 259; AIR 2002 SC 2188; 2002 (2) AllCriR 1495; 2002 (2) CCR 199; 2002 (2) Crimes 393; 2002 (174) CTR 513; [2002] 255 ITR 258; 2002 (3) JhrCR(SC) 112; JT 2002 (4) SC 565; 2002 (3) MLJ(SC) 42; 2002(4) SCALE 327; [2002] 3 S.C.R. 764; 2002 (3) SLT 552; 2002 (4) Supreme 53; [2002] 122 TAXMAN 574

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Finance Act, 1984

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Service - Legislations referred - Banking Regulation Act, 1949 - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 269 and 271-D - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Whether s. 269 of the Income Tax Act, 1961 is unconstitutional on the ground that it is draconian or expropriatory in nature and is violative of art. 14 of the Constitution? - The language of an entry should be given the widest meaning fairly capable to meet the need of the Govt. envisaged by the Constitution - Each general word should extend to all ancillary or subsidiary matters which can fairly and reasonably be comprehended within it - When the vires of an enactment is impugned, there is an initial presumption of its constitutionality. If there exists any difficulty in ascertaining the limits of the legislative power, it must be resolved in favour of the legislature, putting the most liberal construction on the legislative entry so that it is intra vires - The allocation of the subjects of the entries in the respective lists is not done by way of a scientific or logical definitions but it is a mere enumeration of broad and comprehensive categories - Held, s. 269 is not violative of art. 14 of the Constitution, or it was enacted without legislative competence. - Appeal is without any merit,dismissed.
