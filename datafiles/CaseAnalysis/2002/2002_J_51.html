Om Wati Gaur and others v Jitendra Kumar and others

Supreme Court of India

31 October 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, R.C. Lahoti

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1370; (2003) 1 SCC 42; (2003) 1 SCC 422; AIR 2003 SC 229; 2002 ALJ 2824; 2003 (1) AllCJ 568; 2002 (49) ALR 700; 2002 (5) AWC 3483; 2002 (2) CRC 658; JT 2002 (8) SC 607; 2002 (2) RCR(Rent) 695; 2002(8) SCALE 280; 2002 (6) SLT 450; [2002] Supp4 S.C.R. 17; 2002 (7) Supreme 579; 2003 (1) UC 81

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Summary: U.P. Urban Buildings (Regulation of Letting, Rent and Eviction) Act, 1972 - Ss. 39, 3(a) - challenge to the decree of eviction on the ground that the rent had been deposited at the rate of Rs. 80 per month - definition of a 'tenant' - Held, the definition of a 'tenant' as contained in Section 3(a) of the Act includes the heirs and legal representatives residing with the tenant at the time of his death. Therefore, on his death the Appellants who were residing with him at the time of his death would be tenants. - Further, the trial Court in its decree held that the rent was Rs. 100/- per month. This portion of the decree has not been challenged. Once it was held that the rent was Rs. 100/- there was no deposit of the full rent within one month of the coming into force the said Act as envisaged by Section 39. Thus on this ground the High Court was right in confirming the decree for eviction.
