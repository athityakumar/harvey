Delhi Development Authority v Skipper Construction Company Private Limited and

Supreme Court of India

13 November 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

U.C. Banerjee, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1390; (2003) 1 SCC 547; AIR 2003 SC 328; 2003 (3) AWC 2147; 2002 (100) DLT 617; JT 2002 (9) SC 225; 2002(8) SCALE 458; [2002] Supp4 S.C.R. 177; 2003 (1) UC 179

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Bank Guarantee - norms required to be followed when a bank guarantee is issued and the status in respect of its compliance - land of DDA allotted to Skipper Construction Company - the two Banks granted the facility of Bank Guarantee in violation of statutory norms as well as established practices and procedures - Report of Deputy Governors of Reserve Bank of India held responsible only one person i.e. the CMD of the New Bank of India for such a mess up and financial irregularity - held, the decision was not of one individual but of the body in its entirety. It is thus extremely significant as to how the responsibility stands foisted on to one individual rather than a body of persons who had taken up on themselves to sanction the limit or for issuance of the guarantee - the matter requires a further look by a functionary of the State - matter in issue be dealt with and considered by the Central Vigilance Commission.
