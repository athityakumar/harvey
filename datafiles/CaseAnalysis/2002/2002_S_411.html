Bodhraj Alias Bodha and Others v State of Jammu and Kashmir

Supreme Court of India

03 September 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Ruma Pal

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1786; (2002) 8 SCC 45; (2003) SCC (Cr) 201; AIR 2002 SC 3164; 2002 (3) CCR 291; 2002 (2) CGLJ 333; 2002 (4) Crimes 182; 2002 (6) CRJ 489; 2002 CRLJ 4664; 2002(6) SCALE 266; 2002 (5) SLT 111; [2002] Supp2 S.C.R. 67; 2002 (6) Supreme 154

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Evidence Act, 1872 - Indian Penal Code, 1860 s. 302 r/w s. 120-B - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Accused Nos. 1, 2 and 6 were convicted by Trial Court while accused Nos. 3 4,5,7 were acquitted by Trial Court, but HC set aside their acquittal and convicted them - Accused Nos. 8 and 9) were acquitted by Trial Court and their acquittal had been upheld by HC - Accused Nos. 1 and 2 and accused No. 2 having been convicted u/s. 302 r/w s. 120-B IPC were sentenced to suffer imprisonment for life and pay a fine - Similar was case with accused No. 6 - Accused Nos. 3, 4, 5 and 7 HC convicted and sentenced them at par with other three accused - Four appeals relate to DB Judgment of HC - While Criminal Appeal's were filed by accused and State - Hence, instant appeals - Whether order passed by HC was sustainable - Held, it was noticed by HC that PW 38 was investigating officer for day only - He had taken steps from 5.30 evening onwards to 9.00 p.m. on spot - Thereafter PW 42 was asked to investigate into matter - It was also noticed that road between Bari Brahamana and Samba where court was located was dosed - Though road was open it was closed - Day's delay FIR had reached Magistrate late could not be said to be unsual when proper explanation had been offered for delay - Plea of delayed dispatch had been rightly held to be without any substance - It had been recorded that there was valid reason for delayed examination - Such conclusion had been arrived at after analyzing explanation - It could not be laid down as rule of universal application that if there was any delay in examination of particular witness prosecution version becomes suspect - It would depend upon several factors - If explanation offered for delayed examination was plausible and acceptable and court accepts same as plausible, there was no reason to interfere with conclusion - Hence, appeal filed by State in respect of whom both Trial Court and HC recorded acquittal, was seen that there was no acceptable material - This aspect had been analysed in great detail by Trial Court and HC and there was no reason to interfere with conclusions - Appeals dismissed.
