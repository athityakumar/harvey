Ashok Kumar Pandey v State of Delhi

Supreme Court of India

15 March 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Agrawal, M.B. Shah

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 266; (2002) 4 SCC 76; (2002) SCC (Cr) 728; AIR 2002 SC 1468; 2002 (2) CCR 1; 2002 (2) Crimes 9; 2002 (3) CRJ 521; 2002 CRLJ 1844; 2002 (96) DLT 707; 2002 (1) JCC 526; JT 2002 (3) SC 103; 2002 (2) RCR(Criminal) 233; 2002(2) SCALE 647; 2002 (2) SLT 542; 2002 (2) Supreme 430; 2002 (1) UC 545B

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Since the prosecution has succeeded in proving its case beyond reasonable doubts the High Court was quite justified in upholding conviction of the appellant. However, since the case does not fall within the category of rarest of rare one, therefore, in the fitness of things, extreme penalty of death is not called for and the same must be commuted to life imprisonment.
