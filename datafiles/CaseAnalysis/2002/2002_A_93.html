New India Assurance Co. Ltd. v Asha Rani and others

Supreme Court of India

03 December 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1460; (2003) 2 SCC 223; (2003) SCC (Cr) 493; 2002 (3) ACC 753; AIR 2003 SC 607; AIR 2002 SCW 5259; 2003 (1) ALD(SC) 18; 2003 (1) AWC 719; 2003 (3) Bom.C.R. 765; [2003] 113 Comp Cas 520; 2002 (101) DLT 181; 2003 (2) G.L.R. 1001; 2003 (1) JhrCR(SC) 224; JT 2002 (10) SC 162; 2003 (1) KLT 165; 2003 (1) LCD 28; 2003 (1) RCR(Civil) 671; 2002(9) SCALE 172; 2002 (7) SLT 91; [2002] Supp4 S.C.R. 543; 2002 (8) Supreme 594

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Contractual Liability

Summary: Carriers and Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, s.147 (As stood prior to its amendment in 1994) - Whether the insurer is liable to pay compensation to the dependants of the deceased passenger, while the deceased passenger was traveling in a goods vehicle and that vehicle met with an accident, on account of which the passenger died or suffered bodily injury? - Held, prior to the amendment Act of 1994, even if widest interpretation is given to the expression 'to any person' it will not cover either the owner of the goods or his authorised representative being carried in the vehicle - Insurer will not be liable for paying compensation to the owner of goods or his authorised representative on being carried in a goods vehicle when that vehicle meets with an accident and the owner of goods or his representative dies or suffers any bodily injury - Appeals allowed.
