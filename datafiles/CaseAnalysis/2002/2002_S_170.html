Messrs Kanaka Gruha Nirman Sahakara Sangha v Smt. Narayanamma (Since Deceased) By LRs and Others

Supreme Court of India

03 October 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

M.B. Shah, D.M. Dharmadhikari

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 2001; (2003) 1 SCC 228; AIR 2002 SC 3659; 2002 (6) ALD(SC) 119; 2003 (1) AllCJ 646; 2003 (3) AWC 2046; 2003 (1) JhrCR(SC) 19; JT 2002 (8) SC 71; 2002 (4) RCR(Civil) 578; 2002(7) SCALE 257; 2002 (3) SCJ 622; 2002 (5) SLT 613; [2002] Supp3 S.C.R. 97; 2002 (7) Supreme 261; 2003 (1) UPLBEC 270

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property

Keywords: Society

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 3 (f)(vi), 4 and 6 - Land Acquisition (Mysore Extension and Amendment) Act, 1961 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 254 - Indian Penal Code, ss. 161, 468 and 471 - Prevention of Corruption Act, ss. 5(1)(d) and 5(2) - (A) Whether initiation of action by Special Deputy Commissioner u/s. 4 of Act for issuing notification is illegal as under Land Acquisition Act, appropriate Govt. is required to be satisfied that land is needed for public purpose? - Held, it would be difficult to hold that amendments made by 'Mysore Act' which are supplementary in nature, cannot stand together with amended Land Acquisition Act - It cannot be stated that amended Land Acquisition Act is so exhaustive and unqualified that only 'appropriate Govt.' has to be satisfied before issuing Notification u/s. 4 and it excludes empowering of other authority to exercise such powers by State Legislation - Only difference is, before issuing Notification, Deputy Commissioner is also empowered to decide whether land is needed or is likely to be needed for public purpose - It cannot be held that there is repugnancy between two provisions as both can co-exist without any conflict - (B) Whether finding given by HC with regard to non-compliance of s. 3(f)(vi) is, on face of it, illegal? - Further held, considering fact that State Govt. directed Assistant Registrar of Co-operative Societies of Bangalore to verify requirement of members of Society and also fact that matter was placed before Committee of three Members for scrutiny and thereafter State Govt. has conveyed its approval for initiating proceedings for acquisition of land in question by letter dated 14-11-1985, it cannot be said that there is lapse in observing procedure prescribed u/s. 3(f)(vi) - Appeals allowed.
