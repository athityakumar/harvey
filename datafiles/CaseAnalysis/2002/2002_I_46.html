H. S. Ahammed Hussain and Another v Irfan Ahammed and Another

Supreme Court of India

09 July 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Agrawal, R.C. Lahoti

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1723; (2002) 6 SCC 52; (2002) SCC (Cr) 1263; 2002 (2) ACC 575; AIR 2002 SC 2483; 2002 (4) ALD(SC) 103; 2002 (48) ALR 446; 2002 (3) AWC 2438; [2002] 111 Comp Cas 747; 2002 (94) FLR 851; 2002 (2) G.L.R. 1824; 2002 (2) G.L.R. 1825; JT 2002 (5) SC 118; 2002 LabIC 2630; 2002 (132) PLR 297; 2002 (3) RCR(Civil) 563; 2002(5) SCALE 99; 2002 (3) SCJ 284; 2002 (4) SLT 307; [2002] Supp1 S.C.R. 78; 2002 (4) Supreme 501

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Constitution; Practice & Procedure; Tort

Keywords: Tortfeasors

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Constitution of India, 1950 - Tort - Motor Vehicles Act 1939 - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 - (A) Whether High Court was not justified in entertaining and allowing joint appeals preferred by insured and insurer both challenging quantum of compensation awarded by tribunal? - Held, court do not find any merit in submission that joint appeal by insurer as well as insured was not maintainable - In such an eventuality, course which a court should adopt is as noticed in case of Narendra Kumar (1996 Indlaw SC 3379) to delete name of insurer from cause title and proceed with appeal of insured and decide same on merit - (B) Whether compensation has been awarded in accordance with Sch. 2? - Further held, according to Sch. 2, if age is above 40 years but not exceeding 45 years, multiplier applicable is 15 and if age is above 35 years but not exceeding 40 years, multiplier would be 16 but HC has taken multiplier as 13 and 14 instead of 15 and 16 respectively - HC was not justified in reducing compensation from Rs. 3,49,000 to Rs. 1,83,000 which should have been reduced to Rs. 2,07,000 - Appeal partly allowed.
