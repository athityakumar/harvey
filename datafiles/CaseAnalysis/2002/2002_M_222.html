Chandrakant Tukaram Nikam and others v Municipal Corporation of Ahmedabad and another

Supreme Court of India

06 February 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, R.P. Sethi, B.P. Singh

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 88; (2002) SCC (L&S) 317; (2002) 2 SCC 542; AIR 2002 SC 997; AIR 2002 SCW 710; 2002 (5) ALD(SC) 92; 2002 (1) CGLJ 248; 2002 (18) CLR 926; 2002 (92) FLR 1159; 2002 (2) G.L.R. 1257; JT 2002 (1) SC 578; 2002 (35) LabIC 794; 2002 (1) LLJ 842; 2002 (1) LLN 1153; 2002 (2) MLJ(SC) 106; 2002(2) SCALE 77; 2002 (1) SCJ 633; [2002] 1 S.C.R. 861; 2002 (2) SLJ 389; 2002 (1) SLT 697; 2002 (1) Supreme 529; 2002 (2) UPLBEC 1001

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Industrial Employment (Standing Order ) Act, 1946

Summary: The legality of order of termination passed by the employer is an industrial dispute within the meaning of Section 2(k) of the Industrial Disputes Act, and therefore, in such cases the jurisdiction of the Civil Court must be held to have been impliedly barred and the appropriate forum for resolution of such dispute is the forum constituted under the Industrial Disputes Act.
