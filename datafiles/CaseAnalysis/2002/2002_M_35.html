M.P. Electricity Board v Shail Kumari and others

Supreme Court of India

11 January 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, S.N. Phukan

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 18; (2002) 2 SCC 162; (2002) SCC (Cr) 315; 2002 (1) ACC 271; AIR 2002 SC 551; AIR 2002 SCW 129; 2002 (3) ALD(SC) 4; 2002 (46) ALR 448; 2002 (1) AWC 495; 2002 (1) JhrCR(SC) 375; JT 2002 (1) SC 50; 2002 (1) KarLJ 480; 2002 (1) KLT 480; 2002 (2) MLJ(SC) 9; 2002 (1) RCR(Criminal) 433; 2002(1) SCALE 119; 2002 (1) SCJ 253; [2002] 1 S.C.R. 164; 2002 (1) SLT 149; 2002 (1) Supreme 98; 2002 (1) UC 357

Case Digest

Subject: Tort

Summary: Torts-Damages-Strict liability-Cyclist entrapped on livewire and electrocuted instantaneously-Falling down and dying within minutes-High Court directed Electricity Board to pay compensation of Rs. 4.34 lakhs to claimants-Defence that stranger clandestinely taking wire from main supply line to siphon energy for his own use and line got unfastened from hook and fell on road-Doctrine of strict liability applicable-Exception of 'act of stranger' not available to Board-No merit in appeal.
