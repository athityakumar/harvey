Bharatbhai @ Jimi Premchandbhai v State of Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

03 October 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.K. Sabharwal, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1129; (2002) 8 SCC 447; (2003) SCC (Cr) 93; AIR 2002 SC 3620; 2002 (4) CCR 131; 2002 (4) Crimes 386; 2003 (1) CRJ 746; 2003 CRLJ 28; 2003 (1) G.L.R. 330; JT 2002 (7) SC 529; 2002 (4) RCR(Criminal) 445C; 2002(7) SCALE 201; 2002 (3) SCJ 562; 2002 (6) SLT 27; [2002] Supp3 S.C.R. 46; 2002 (7) Supreme 46

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Defence & Security Forces; Socio-Economic

Summary: TADA- Legality of the confessional statements. The defect of non-making and appending of memorandum, as required by Rule 15(3)(b) of the Terrorist and Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Rules, 1987, cannot be cured by oral deposition of the Superintendent of Police who recorded the confession, while appearing as a witness in court.
