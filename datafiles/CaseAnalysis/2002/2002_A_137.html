Sampath Kumar v Ayyakannu and Another

Supreme Court of India

13 September 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, Brijesh Kumar

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1997; (2002) 7 SCC 559; AIR 2002 SC 3369; AIR 2002 SCW 3925; 2002 (6) ALD(SC) 63; 2003 (1) AllCJ 1; 2002 (49) ALR 591; 2003 (1) AWC 18; 2002 (2) CRC 553; 2003 (1) JhrCR(SC) 283; JT 2002 (7) SC 182; 2002 (2) LCD 1186; 2002 (3) MLJ(SC) 160; 2002 (4) RCR(Civil) 566; 2003 (1) RD 148; 2002(6) SCALE 544; 2002 (4) SCJ 321; 2002 (5) SLT 360; [2002] Supp2 S.C.R. 397; 2002 (6) Supreme 424; 2003 (1) UC 1

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 6, r. 17 - Application for amendment - Whether it is permissible to convert through amendment a suit merely for permanent prohibitory injunction into suit for declaration of title and recovery of possession? - Held, basic structure of suit is not altered by proposed amendment only change is nature of relief sought for by plaintiff - Referred on Mst. Rukhmabai v. Lala Laxminarayan and others, (1959 Indlaw SC 207) - Appeal allowed.
