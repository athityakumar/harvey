Mauleshwar Mani and Others v Jagdish Prasad and Others

Supreme Court of India

23 January 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

V.N. Khare, Ashok Bhan

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 42; (2002) 2 SCC 468; AIR 2002 SC 727; 2002 (2) ALD(SC) 3; 2002 ALJ 403; 2002 (1) AWC 682; JT 2002 (1) SC 450; 2002 (1) MLJ(SC) 200; 2002 (93) RD 176; 2002(1) SCALE 303; 2002 (1) SCJ 439; [2002] 1 S.C.R. 423; 2002 (1) SLT 382; 2002 (1) Supreme 238; 2002 (1) UC 238

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Summary: (1) Hindu Succession Act, 1956-Section 30-U. P. Zamindari Abolition and Land Reforms Act, 1950-Sections 169, 172 and 174-Will-Husband first bequeathing his entire property to his wife with power of alienation-And after her death, 9 sons of daughters to inherit property-Whether wife obtained absolute estate?-Held, “yes”-Whether subsequent bequest in favour of sons of daughters invalid?-Held, “yes”-After death of wife whether sons would succeed to bhumidhari land?-Held, “no”-Succession to holding will be governed by Section 174 of U.P.Z.A.L.R. Act-Hence, after death of wife, her daughters and thereafter their sons to succeed to holding.

(2) Will - Construction - Later clauses in Will in conflict with earlier clause-Earlier clause to override later clauses.
