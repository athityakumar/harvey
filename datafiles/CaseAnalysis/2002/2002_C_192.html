Supreme Court of India

08 July 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

Brijesh Kumar, D.P. Mohapatra

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 617; (2002) 6 SCC 33; AIR 2002 SC 2487; 2002 (48) ALR 34; 2002 (3) AWC 2433; [2002] 111 Comp Cas 515; 2002 (4) CompLJ 329; 2002 (2) CPJ 7; 2002 (2) CPJ(Sc) 7; 2002 (3) CPR 91; 2002 (3) JhrCR(SC) 48; JT 2002 (5) SC 111; 2002 (3) MLJ(SC) 184; 2002 (132) PLR 221; 2002(5) SCALE 35; 2002 (3) SCJ 226; [2002] 3 S.C.R. 1167; 2002 (4) SLT 235; 2002 (4) Supreme 480

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection

Summary: Consumer Protection Act, 1986 - Section 13(2)(a) - The State Consumer Disputes Reddressal Commission can grant time to the respondent to file his reply, beyond a total period of 45 days under Section 13(2)(a) of the Consumer Protection Act, 1986 since the provision appears to be directory and not mandatory in nature, and is more by way of procedure to achieve the object of speedy disposal of such disputes.
