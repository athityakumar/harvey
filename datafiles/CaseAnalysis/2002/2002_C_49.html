Shaik Saidulu @ Saida v Chukka Yesu Ratnam and others

Supreme Court of India

23 January 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

R.P. Sethi, G.B. Pattanaik

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 43; (2002) 3 SCC 130; AIR 2002 SC 749; 2002 (2) ALD(SC) 7; 2002 (2) AllCJ 989; 2002 (47) ALR 221; JT 2002 (1) SC 247; 2002(1) SCALE 296; 2002 (1) SCJ 418; [2002] 1 S.C.R. 403; 2002 (1) SLT 377; 2002 (1) Supreme 338

Case Digest

Subject: Election; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Interpretation of ‘application’

Summary: Election - Municipalities & Local Governments - Hyderabad Municipal Corporation Act, 1955, ss. 671, 671(2), 71, 71(2) - Limitation Act, 1963, ss. 12, 13, 29(2), 4, 5 - Condonation of delay - Interpretation of 'application' - Appellant filed his election petition with application u/s. 5 of 1963 Act seeking condonation of delay of 42 days in filing election petition - Respondent No. 1 moved HC praying for dismissal of election petition of appellant on ground of being barred by limitation - HC held that petition filed by appellant was barred by time - Hence, instant appeals - Whether s. 5 of 1963 Act is applicable to election petitions filed under 1955 Act or not.

Held, the word 'application', as used in s. 671 of 1955 Act, would include within its ambit an election petition wherein a voter or the candidate makes the prayer to the court and seeks the redressal of his grievances regarding the conduct of the elections. Sub-s. (2) of S. 71 of 1955 Act specifically provides the period of limitation for filing the election petition for which sub-s. (1) of s. 671 of 1955 Act would be applicable to attract the sub-s. (2) of s. 71 of 1955 Act thereby applying the provisions of s. 5 of 1963 Act to the election petitions filed under 1955 Act. HC appears to have adopted a very technical approach by totally ignoring the purpose and object of 1955 Act and the conduct of the respondents in not constituting the Tribunal, with the result of preventing the aggrieved from approaching the Tribunal to challenge the election by way of election petition. Impugned orders set aside and the cases remitted back to the Election Tribunal for deciding the election petitions on merits. Appeals allowed.

Ratio - Word 'application' is synonymous with term 'petition' which means written statement of material facts, requesting Court to grant relief or remedy based on those facts.
