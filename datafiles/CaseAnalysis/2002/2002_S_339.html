Subash Parbat Sonvane v State of Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

24 April 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

M.B. Shah, B.P. Singh, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1629; (2002) 5 SCC 86; (2002) SCC (Cr) 954; AIR 2003 SC 2169; 2002 (2) CCR 181; 2002 (2) Crimes 267; 2002 CRLJ 2787; 2002 (3) G.L.R. 2245; 2002 (2) JCC 881; JT 2002 (4) SC 348; 2002 (3) RCR(Criminal) 188; 2003 (2) RCR(Criminal) 541; 2002(4) SCALE 40; 2002 (3) SCJ 507; [2002] 3 S.C.R. 359; 2002 (3) SLT 481; 2002 (3) Supreme 418

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 7, 13(1)(d) and 13(2) - Corruption - Conviction of appellant for offence u/s. 13(1)(d) - Whether mere acceptance of money without there being any other evidence would not be sufficient for convicting accused u/s. 13(1)(d)(i)? - In ss. 7 and 13(1)(a) and (b) of Act, Legislature has specifically used word 'accepts' or 'obtains'-as against this, there is departure in language used in cl. (1)(d) of s. 13 and it has omitted word 'accepts' and has emphasized word 'obtains' - Held, for convicting person u/s. 13(1)(d), there must be evidence on record that accused 'obtained' for himself or for any other person any valuable thing or pecuniary advantage by either corrupt or illegal means or by abusing his position as a public servant or he obtained for any person any valuable thing or pecuniary advantage without any public interest - Considering evidence on record, it is unreasonable to hold that appellant demanded money from complainant - Appeal partly allowed.
