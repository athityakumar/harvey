Sohan Lal v Babu Gandhi and others

Supreme Court of India

22 November 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, M.B. Shah, D.M. Dharmadhikari

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1421; (2003) 1 SCC 108; AIR 2003 SC 320; 2003 (3) AWC 2563; 2003 (1) JhrCR(SC) 164; JT 2002 (9) SC 474; 2003 (2) RCR(Civil) 731; 2002(8) SCALE 555; 2002 (6) SLT 682; [2002] Supp4 S.C.R. 333; 2002 (8) Supreme 408

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Keywords: Election Agents

Summary: M.P. Panchayat Raj Avam Gram Swaraj Adhiniyam, 1993 - S. 122 - Election Petition recounting - held, it is not correct to hold that, in an election petition, after the declaration of the result, the Court or Tribunal cannot direct recounting of votes unless the party has first applied in writing for recounting of votes. - There is no prohibition in the Act or under the rules prohibiting the Court or Tribunal to direct recounting of the votes. Even otherwise a party may not know that the recounting is necessary till after result is declared. His only remedy would be to file an Election Petition under Section 122. - in such a case, the Court or the Tribunal is bound to consider the plea and where case is made out, it may direct recount depending upon the evidence led by the parties - the ratio laid down in the case of Smt. Ram Rati vs. Saroj Devi and others (1997 AIR(SC) 3072) is not correct.
