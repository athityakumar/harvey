Corporation of Calicut v K. Sreenivasan

Supreme Court of India

03 May 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Agrawal, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 295; (2002) 5 SCC 361; AIR 2002 SC 2051; 2002 (4) ALD(SC) 70; 2002 (48) ALR 305; 2003 (51) ALR 721; 2002 (2) CGLJ 94; JT 2002 (4) SC 589; 2002 (2) KLT 291; 2002 (1) RCR(Rent) 614; 2002(4) SCALE 377; [2002] 3 S.C.R. 783; 2002 (4) SLT 38; 2002 (4) Supreme 35

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure; Rent Control

Summary: The Estate Officer was justified in initiating proceeding under the Kerala Public Buildings (Eviction of Unauthorised Occupants) Act, 1968 and passing eviction order therein, since, the expression 'unauthorised occupation' within the meaning of Section 2(f) of the Act would embrace within its ambit the case of licensee as well, after expiry of the period of licence or upon its determination for any reason whatsoever.
