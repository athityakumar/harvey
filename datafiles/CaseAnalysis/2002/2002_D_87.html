Dr. Anil Bajaj v Post Graduate Institute of Medical Education and Another

Supreme Court of India

21 January 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Kirpal, K.G. Balakrishnan, Arijit Pasayat

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 41; (2002) 2 SCC 240; (2002) SCC (L&S) 289; AIR 2002 SC 2414; 2002 (1) CLR 923; 2002 (1) JhrCR(SC) 447A; JT 2002 (1) SC 245; 2002 LabIC 2320; 2002 (2) LLJ 526; 2002(1) SCALE 302; [2002] 1 S.C.R. 375; 2002 (2) SLJ 356; 2002 (1) SLR 745; 2002 (1) SLT 375; 2002 (1) Supreme 230; 2002 (1) UPLBEC 659

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Indirect Tax; Service

Summary: A person, who gets an advantage, namely of a sanction to go abroad on service on the condition that he will come back within two years and if he does not come back, his lien will automatically be regarded as being terminated, cannot turn around and challenge the said condition on the basis of which sanction to go abroad was granted.
