State of West Bengal and Others v Vishnunarayan and Associates Private Limited and

Supreme Court of India

19 March 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Phukan, S.S.M. Quadri

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 183; (2002) 4 SCC 134; AIR 2002 SC 1493; JT 2002 (3) SC 166; 2002 (1) RCR(Rent) 425; 2002(3) SCALE 40; 2002 (2) SCJ 505; [2002] 2 S.C.R. 557; 2002 (2) SLT 604; 2002 (2) Supreme 476

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: The action of the State Government in taking possession of the suit premises by using force was unlawful and was destructive of the basic principle of rule of law, since, the State or its executive officers cannot interfere with the rights of others unless they can point to some specific provision of law, which authorises their acts.
