K. Shekar v V. Indiramma and Others

Supreme Court of India

27 February 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

Ruma Pal, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1951; (2002) SCC (L&S) 429; (2002) 3 SCC 586; AIR 2002 SC 1230; 2002 (93) FLR 669; JT 2002 (2) SC 435; 2002 LabIC 971; 2002 (2) LLN 451; 2002(2) SCALE 359; 2002 (2) SCJ 202; [2002] 2 S.C.R. 86; 2002 (2) SLJ 396; 2002 (2) SLR 662; 2002 (2) SLT 284; 2002 (2) Supreme 196; 2002 (2) UPLBEC 1213

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Service - Appellant was initially appointed as Lecturer - Post of Lecturer was re-designated as Asst. Professor pursuant to 4th Pay Commission - During pendency of proceedings, he has been promoted as Associate Professor and thereafter as Addl. Professor with National Institute of Mental Health and Neuro Sciences (NIMHANS) - Both single Judge and Division Bench of High Court, on an application u/art. 226 filed by respondent No. 1 have held that he had no such right - This decision has been impugned before Apex Court not only by appellant but also NIMHANS by way of a separate appeal - Whether appellant is entitled to continue as Addl. Professor in National Institute of Mental Health and Neuro Sciences (NIMHANS)? - Held, corrigendum in so far as it provided for 'reversion' of persons working at Centre to NIMHANS certainly could not operate to revert back appellant - Cadre and Recruitment Rules of NIMHANS provide that appointments to posts of Asst. Professor and Lecturer are to be made by direct recruitment - Besides, appellant was in fact serving as Research Officer with Indian Council of Medical Research (ICMR) Centre when he was appointed as a Lecturer - There was a distinction between appointment as a Lecturer at ICMR Centre and appointment to post of a Lecturer in NIMHANS - That is why appellant, even after having been issued letter of appointment as Lecturer in ICMR Centre, applied again pursuant to second advertisement for appointment as Lecturer in NIMHANS - He could not, in circumstances, be reverted back to NIMHANS - Appeals dismissed.
