Deccan Sugar and Abkari Company Limited v Commissioner of Excise

Supreme Court of India

13 February 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Kirpal, B.P. Singh, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 2080; (2004) 1 SCC 243

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - Demand of excise duty for loss of rectified spirit - Whether Excise Commissioner can order levying of duty on loss of rectified spirit? - Held, it is settled by Supreme Court in Synthetics and Chemicals Ltd v State of UP 1989 Indlaw SC 286 that State Legislature has no jurisdiction to levy any excise duty on rectified spirit - State can levy excise duty only on potable liquor fit for human consumption and as rectified spirit does not fall under that category State Legislature cannot impose any excise duty and, therefore, impugned order of Commissioner of Excise is set aside - Appeals allowed.
