Union of India and Another v Association for Democratic Reforms and Another

Supreme Court of India

02 May 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

M.B. Shah, B.P. Singh, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 308; (2002) 5 SCC 294; AIR 2002 SC 2112; 2002 (97) DLT 657; JT 2002 (4) SC 501; 2002 (3) MLJ(SC) 55; 2002(4) SCALE 297; [2002] 3 S.C.R. 696; 2002 (3) SLT 490; 2002 (4) Supreme 1

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Keywords: European Convention On Human Rights, Purity

Summary: Election - Held, (a) Freedom of speech and expression includes right to impart and receive information which includes freedom to hold opinions, and therefore, covers right to get material information with regard to a candidate who is contesting election for a post which is of utmost importance in the democracy; (b) The jurisdiction of the Election Commission is wide enough to include all powers necessary for smooth conduct of elections and Article 324 operates in areas left unoccupied by legislation and the words 'superintendence, direction and control' as well as 'conduct of all elections' are the broadest terms; (c) In case when the Act or Rules are silent on a particular subject and the Authority implementing the same has constitutional or statutory power to implement it, the Court can necessarily issue directions or orders on the said subject to fill the vacuum or void till a suitable law is enacted.
