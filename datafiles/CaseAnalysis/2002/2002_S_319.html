Saurashtra Oil Mills Association, Gujarat v State of Gujarat and Another

Supreme Court of India

19 February 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhan, V.N. Khare

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1949; (2002) 3 SCC 202; AIR 2002 SC 1130; 2002 (4) G.L.R. 2777; JT 2002 (2) SC 207; 2002 (3) RCR(Civil) 1; 2002(2) SCALE 263; 2002 (2) SCJ 93; [2002] 1 S.C.R. 1099; 2002 (2) SLT 116; 2002 (2) Supreme 41

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Consumer Protection; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Socio-Economic

Summary: Consumer Protection - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 14, 19(1)(g), 226, 251, 254 and 256 - Essential Commodities Act, 1955, s. 3 - Gujarat Essential Articles (Licensing, Control Stock Declaration) Order, 1981, cl. 24(1) - Whether the Government of Gujarat was justified in issuance of a notification under cl. 24 of the State Order of 1981 requiring compliance regarding stock limits from the dealers of Edible Oil and Edible Oilseeds despite the fact that the Central Government by its order D/- 10-11-1997 had directed omission of edible Oilseeds and edible oils from the purview of the Pulses and Edible Oils (Storage Control) Order of 1977? - The State Order of 1981 had been issued after the delegation of the power to the State Govts. by the Central Govt. and with prior concurrence of the Central Govt. Stock limits were provided in the tabulated form in cl. 3 and further State Govt. or its officers were authorised under cl. 24(1) of the State Order of 1981 to issue directions regarding maintenance of stock, storage, display of prices etc. - Appeals dismissed.
