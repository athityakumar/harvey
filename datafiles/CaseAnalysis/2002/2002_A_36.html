I.T.C. Limited v Agricultural Produce Market Committee and others

Supreme Court of India

24 January 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 53; (2002) 9 SCC 232; AIR 2002 SC 852; AIR 2002 SCW 523; 2002 (2) JhrCR(SC) 177; JT 2002 (1) SC 294; 2002(1) SCALE 327; [2002] 1 S.C.R. 441; 2002 (1) SLT 401; 2002 (1) Supreme 258; 2002 (1) UPLBEC 814

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Coffee Act, 1942, Karnataka Agricultural Produce Marketing (Regulation) Act, 1966, Tobacco Board Act, 1975, Uttar Pradesh Sugarcane (Purchase Tax) Act, 1961, Tea Act, 1953, Rubber Act, 1947, Coconut Development Board Act, 1979, Coir Industry Act, 1953, Bihar Agriculture Produce Markets Act, 1960, Tamil Nadu Regulation Act, 1959, Agricultural Market Committee Act, Bihar Agricultural Produce Markets Act, Central Silk Board Act, 1958, Central Silk Board Act, 1948

Summary: In the event there are two competing legislations, one by the Parliament and one by the State, the Court would make an endeavour if both the legislations could be allowed to be operated upon but, if it is found that the Central Legislation and the State legislation come in collision with each, then the Central legislation would prevail, provided the said legislation is otherwise constitutionally valid namely, the Parliament had the legislative competence to enact the legislation in question.
