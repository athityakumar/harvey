Pandurang Kalu Patil and Another v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

17 January 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, S.N. Phukan

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 38; (2002) SCC (Cr) 371; (2002) 2 SCC 490; AIR 2002 SC 733; 2002 (1) CCR 92; 2002 (1) Crimes 270; 2002 (3) CRJ 448; 2002 CRLJ 1007; 2002 (3) G.L.R. 2380; 2002 (1) JCC 380; JT 2002 (1) SC 229; 2002 (1) RCR(Criminal) 499; 2002(1) SCALE 290; 2002 (1) SCJ 378; [2002] 1 S.C.R. 338; 2002 (1) Supreme 233; 2002 (1) UC 435

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: The judges of the Division Bench of the High Court cannot set a locus classicus at nought without reference to any of the catena of judicial pronouncements rendered by High Courts as well as the Apex Court pertaining to the ratio in that decision. Further, the ban against admissibility of evidence under Section 27 of the Evidence Act would stand lifted only if the statement distinctly related to a discovery of fact.
