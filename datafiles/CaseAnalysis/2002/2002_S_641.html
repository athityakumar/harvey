Ezhil and others v State of Tamil Nadu

Supreme Court of India

24 April 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

Doraiswamy Raju, R.P. Sethi

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 271; (2002) 9 SCC 189; (2003) SCC (Cr) 1168; AIR 2002 SC 2017; AIR 2002 SCW 2060; 2002 (2) AllCriC 176; 2002 (2) AllCriR 1568; 2002 (3) CCR 1; 2002 (2) Crimes 366; 2002 (5) CRJ 499; 2002 CRLJ 2799; 2002 (2) JCC 957; 2002 (2) JIC 123; JT 2002 (4) SC 375; 2002 (3) RCR(Criminal) 775; 2002(4) SCALE 102; [2002] 3 S.C.R. 431; 2002 (4) SLT 170; 2002 (3) Supreme 589; 2002 (2) UC 353B

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Summary: Indian Penal Code - Sections 364, 392 and 302 r/w. S.34 and 120 - conviction and sentence under - Evidence Act - Section 114 Illustration (a) - application of. Held, when the appellants have miserably failed to explain their lawful possession of those articles with them that really belonged to and were in the possession of the deceased when he landed at the airport, then, it was legitimate for the courts below to draw the presumption not only of the fact that they were in possession of the stolen articles after committing robbery but also committed the murder of the deceased.
