Smt. Sushila Rani v Commissioner of Income Tax and Another

Supreme Court of India

04 February 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, Ruma Pal

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 85; (2002) 2 SCC 697; AIR 2002 SC 823; AIR 2002 SCW 485; 2002 (172) CTR 665; 2002 (96) DLT 64; [2002] 253 ITR 775; 2002 (2) JhrCR(SC) 81; JT 2002 (1) SC 573; 2002(2) SCALE 8; [2002] 1 S.C.R. 809; 2002 (1) SLT 649; 2002 (1) Supreme 501; [2002] 121 TAXMAN 343

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Kar Vivad Samadhan Scheme, 1998

Summary: A certificate issued under Section 90(1) of the Kar Vivad Samadhan Scheme, 1998 making a determination as to the sum payable under the KVSS, is conclusive as to the matters stated therein and cannot be reopened in any proceedings under any law for the time being in force, except on the ground of false declaration by any declarant.
