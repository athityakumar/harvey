Sant Prasad v Goverdhan Prasad and another

Supreme Court of India

08 March 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

S.S.M. Quadri, S.N. Variava

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 2323; (2009) 16 SCC 247

Case Digest

Subject: Trusts & Associations

Summary: Trusts & Associations - Practice & Procedure - U.P. Cooperative Societies Act,1965, s.70 - Loss - Recovery proceedings - Sustainability - Appellant was secretary of respondent/co-operative society which referred dispute to Registrar u/s.70 of Act with regard to loss occasioned to respondent while appellant was functioning as secretary thereof - Registrar passed award on 24-1-1995 directing recovery of loss of Rs.87,934.75/- from appellant - Tribunal upheld order passed by Registrar - HC dismissed petition of appellant and declined to interfere with order passed by Tribunal - Whether recovery of loss caused to society by employee of society can be subject-matter of dispute u/s.70 of Act.

Held, to appreciate contention that dispute of nature causing loss to society does not fall u/s.70 of Act, it will be useful to refer to provision of s.70(2)(c) of Act which is relevant. For purpose of s.70 (1) of Act, it shall be deemed to be included in dispute relating to constitution, management or business of a cooperative society, namely- a claim by a society for any loss caused to it by a member or employee including past or deceased member, officer, agent or employee, whether individually or collectively and whether such loss be admitted or not. From a perusal of this provision u/s.70 of Act it is clear that claim by a society for any loss caused to it by an employee, whether such loss is admitted or not falls within disputes contemplated u/s.70 of Act. In view of this position, there is no illegality in impugned order of HC. No interference is, therefore, warranted with order of HC. Appeal dismissed.
