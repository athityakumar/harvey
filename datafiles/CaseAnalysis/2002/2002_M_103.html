I.T.C. Limited Workers Welfare Association and v Management of I.T.C. Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

29 January 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Venkatarama Reddi, D.P. Mohapatra

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 82; (2002) SCC (L&S) 399; (2002) 3 SCC 411; AIR 2002 SC 937; AIR 2002 SCW 630; 2002 (18) CLR 965; 2002 (92) FLR 889; 2002 (2) JhrCR(SC) 1; JT 2002 (1) SC 511; 2002 (35) LabIC 780; 2002 (1) LLJ 848; 2002 (1) LLN 1144; 2002(1) SCALE 558; 2002 (1) SCJ 543; [2002] 1 S.C.R. 711; 2002 (2) SLR 42; 2002 (1) SLT 662; 2002 (1) Supreme 446; 2002 (1) UPLBEC 641

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Since the settlement arrived at was in the course of conciliation proceedings and was reached as a result of collective bargaining, it carries a presumption that it is just and fair and becomes binding on all the parties to the dispute as well as the other workmen in the establishment to which the dispute relates, and therefore, an individual employee cannot seek to wriggle out of the settlement merely because it does not suit him.
