Jagdish Jugtawat v Manju Lata and Others

Supreme Court of India

23 April 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

Brijesh Kumar, D.M. Dharmadhikari, D.P. Mohapatra

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1264; (2002) 5 SCC 422; (2002) SCC (Cr) 1147; 2002 (6) Bom.C.R. 189; 2003 (3) RCR(Criminal) 471

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Women & Children

Keywords: Criminal Procedure Code, 1963, Muslim Women (Protection of Rights on Divorce) Act

Summary: Family & Personal - Women & Children - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 125 - Hindu Adoptions and Maintenance Act, 1956, s. 20(3) - Muslim Women (Protection of Rights on Divorce) Act, 1986, s. 3(1)(b) - Right of maintenance of a minor girl - Single judge of High Court accepted legal position that u/s. 125 CrPC, 1973 a minor daughter entitled to maintenance from her parents only till she attains majority - But declined to interfere with order passed by Family Court taking cue from s. 20(3) of Hindu Act under which right of maintenance is given to a minor daughter till her marriage - Whether daughter entitled to maintenance? - In case of Noor Saba Khatoon v. Mohd. Quasim, 1997 Indlaw SC 1969 relating to claim of a muslim divorced woman for maintenance from her husband for herself and her minor children it was held that while accepting position that s. 125 CrPC, 1973 does not fix liability of parents to maintain children beyond attainment of majority, read said provision and s. 3(1)(b) of Muslim Act together and held that under latter statutory provision liability of providing maintenance extends beyond attainment of majority of a dependent girl - Applying principle to facts and circumstances of case in hand, manifest that right of a minor girl for maintenance from parents after attaining majority till her marriage is recognized in s. 20(3) of Hindu Act - Therefore, no exception can be taken to judgment passed by single judge for maintaining order passed by Family Court which is based on a combined reading of s. 125 CrPC 1973 and s. 20(3) of Hindu Adoptions and Maintenance Act, 1956 - Petition dismissed.
