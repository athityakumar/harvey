Secretary To Government, T. N. and Another v K. Vinayagamurthy

Supreme Court of India

26 August 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, K.G. Balakrishnan, Ruma Pal

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1774; (2002) 7 SCC 104; AIR 2002 SC 2968; JT 2002 (6) SC 361; 2002(6) SCALE 100; 2002 (5) SLT 41; [2002] Supp1 S.C.R. 683; 2002 (5) Supreme 535

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax

Summary: Indirect Tax - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14 and 19 - Tamil Nadu Liquor (Retail Vending) Rules, 1989, r. 14 - Licensing system for retail vending of Indian-made foreign liquor - Whether Govt. act of refusal to renew licences of existing licensees and holding fresh draw of lots is arbitrary, unreasonable u/art. 14? - Held, right of renewal has been unequivocally indicated in excise policy of 2001-02, as reflected in GOMs No. 115 for block period of 2001-04, State Govt. could not have annulled same - Hence, conclusion of Division Bench of Madras High Court that portions of GOMs dealing with non-renewal of privileges granted to existing licensees subject to their fulfilling other conditions of provisions of Act and Rules are arbitrary - Directions issued - Petition dismissed.
