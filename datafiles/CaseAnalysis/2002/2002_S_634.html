Chowdarapu Raghunanda v State of Tamil Nadu and others

Supreme Court of India

15 March 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

M.B. Shah, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 172; (2002) SCC (Cr) 714; (2002) 3 SCC 754; AIR 2002 SC 1460; 2002 (2) CCR 6; 2002 (2) Crimes 47; 2002 CRLJ 1836; 2002 (80) ECC 468; 2002 (102) ECR 1; 2002 (143) E.L.T. 26; 2002 (1) JCC 594; JT 2002 (3) SC 110; 2002 (2) RCR(Criminal) 241; 2002 (49) RLT 256; 2002(2) SCALE 638; 2002 (2) SCJ 469; [2002] 2 S.C.R. 486; 2002 (2) SLT 548; 2002 (2) Supreme 573

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Fema

Summary: An order of preventive detention passed under Conservation of Foreign Exchange and Prevention of Smuggling Activities Act, 1974 must be founded on a reasonable prognosis of the future behaviour of a person based on his past conduct consisting of one single act or of a series of acts and must be of such a nature that an inference can reasonably be drawn from it that the person concerned would be likely to repeat such acts so as to warrant his detention.
