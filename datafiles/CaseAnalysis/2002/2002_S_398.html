Sardul Singh with Jagtar Singh v State of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

27 September 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

Doraiswamy Raju, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1153; (2002) 8 SCC 372; (2003) SCC (Cr) 23; AIR 2002 SC 3462; 2002 (4) CCR 177; 2002 (4) Crimes 432; 2003 (1) CRJ 467; 2002 (3) JCC 1951; JT 2002 (7) SC 496; 2002 (4) RCR(Criminal) 560; 2002(7) SCALE 50; 2002 (5) SLT 635; 2002 (7) Supreme 105

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: IPC -- S 302, 148, 149 - Trial court finds one accused guilty while acquitting others -Acquittal of others challenged- In appeal, High Court convicts other accused u/s. 302 r/w. section 34 IPC -Held, Evidence of witnesses cannot be discarded totally merely on account of certain variations or infirmities pointed. There was manifestly erroneous evaluation of the evidence by the trial court, resulting in grave injustice - High Court has not committed any error or exceeded the parameters laid down for interfering with the verdict of acquittal recorded by the trial court.
