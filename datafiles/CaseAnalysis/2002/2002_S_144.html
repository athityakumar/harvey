Dr. J. J. Merchant and Others v Shrinath Chaturvedi

Supreme Court of India

12 August 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

M.B. Shah, B.P. Singh, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1757; (2002) 6 SCC 635; AIR 2002 SC 2931; 2002 (5) ALD(SC) 43; 2002 (4) AWC 2865; 2003 (1) Bom.C.R. 24; 2002 CPJ 8; 2002 (4) CPJ(Sc) 8; 2002 (3) JhrCR(SC) 137; JT 2002 (6) SC 1; 2002 (3) MLJ(SC) 133; 2002 (3) RCR(Civil) 700; 2002(5) SCALE 545; 2002 (4) SLT 714; [2002] Supp1 S.C.R. 469; 2002 (5) Supreme 337; 2002 (2) UC 597

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Grant Of Adjournment

Summary: Consumer Protection - Consumer Protection Act, 1986, ss. 2(1)(o), 3, 12, 13, 20 and 30-A - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, O. 8 rr. 1, 1-A, O. 7 r. 14, O. 18 r. 4 - (A) Whether delay in disposal of cases by Consumer Forum or Commission would be a ground for directing complainant to approach Civil Court? - Held, Consumer Forum is an alternative forum established under Act to discharge functions of a Civil Court therefore, delay in disposal of complaint would not be a ground for rejecting complaint and directing complainant to approach civil court (B) Whether National Commission ought to have directed complainant to approach Civil Court because case involves complicated question of facts? - Further held, under the Act, National Commission is required to be headed by a retired Judge of this court and State Commission is required to be headed by a retired High Court judge and they are competent to decide complicated issues of law or facts hence, it would not be proper in cases where negligence of experts is alleged, consumers should be directed to approach Civil Court - Whether complicated questions of facts cannot be decided in summary proceedings? - Held, yes - Under the Act, for summary or speedy trial, exhaustive procedure in conformity with principles of natural justice is provided - Appeal disposed of.
