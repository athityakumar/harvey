Mahant Chand Nath Yogi and Another v State of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

24 October 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

Shivaraj V. Patil, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1179; (2003) SCC (Cr) 312; (2003) 1 SCC 326; AIR 2003 SC 18; AIR 2002 SCW 4401; 2002 (4) CCR 220; 2003 (1) CGLJ 107; 2003 (1) Crimes 49; 2003 (1) CRJ 515; 2003 CRLJ 76; JT 2002 (8) SC 343; 2003 (1) RCR(Criminal) 764; 2002(7) SCALE 466; 2002 (6) SLT 182; 2002 (7) Supreme 381

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Defence & Security Forces

Summary: IPC - S. 120-B - whether the anticipatory bail granted to the appellants could be sustained or not? - Held, the judicial discretion exercised in granting anticipatory bail is neither perverse nor erroneous - the High Court committed a manifest and serious error in passing the impugned orders setting aside the anticipatory bail granted to the appellants. - Normally, very cogent and overwhelming grounds or circumstances are required to cancel the bail already granted. The High Court, it appears, did not bear this distinction in mind and cancelled the bail in a mechanical manner.
