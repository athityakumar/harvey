Ram Anup Singh and Others v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

07 August 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Singh, H.K. Sema, M.B. Shah

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1917; (2002) 6 SCC 686; (2002) SCC (Cr) 1466; AIR 2002 SC 3006; 2002 (3) CCR 131; 2002 (3) Crimes 71; 2002 (6) CRJ 273; 2002 CRLJ 3927; 2002 (3) JCC 1454; 2002 (3) JhrCR(SC) 128; 2003 (1) JhrCR(SC) 258; JT 2002 (5) SC 621; 2002 (3) RCR(Criminal) 756; 2002(5) SCALE 500; 2002 (4) SLT 663; 2002 (5) Supreme 209; 2002 (2) UC 623

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Arms Act, 1959, s. 27 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302/34, 302/109 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appeal had been preferred by three appellants - Appeal arises out of incident that occurred in which four innocent lives were lost - Appellants virtually annihilated entire family - Appellants were put up for trial before Trial Court and appellant RAS was found guilty of offence u/s. 302, r/w s. 34, 302/109 IPC and s. 27 of the 1959Act and sentenced appellant no. 1 to death u/s 302/34 IPC and 302/109 IPC - Appellant nos. 2 and 3were found guilty of offence u/s 302 IPC and s. 27 the 1959 Act - They were also sentenced to death u/s 302 of IPC On appeal, - HC affirmed sentence of death passed against appellant nos. 2 and 3 but refused to confirm sentence of death passed against appellant no. 1 and instead, sentenced him to life imprisonment -- Hence, instant appeal - Whether order passed by HC was justified - Held, Court had carefully considered evidence on record and were satisfied that conviction of appellants was fully justified in facts and circumstances of instant case - Further events took place over ten years, there was no instance where appellants ever resorted to violence - appellants were no doubt aggrieved but they did not resort to violence though they tried all other non violent methods including litigation and Panchayati -- HC was right in making observation because there was nothing on record to suggest that any untoward incident had taken place on earlier day or few days before occurrence which might have aggravated situation and motivated appellants to resort to drastic action of killing all members of family of deceased - Court further held that having regard to all facts and circumstances, and also having regard to fact that evidence does not disclose immediate cause of incident it would be unsafe to confirm sentence of death awarded by HC to appellant nos. 2 and 3 - Hence, death sentence awarded by Trial Court and confirmed by HC to appellants nos. 2 and 3 was set aside and instead sentenced them to suffer rigorous imprisonment for life - Appeal dismissed.
