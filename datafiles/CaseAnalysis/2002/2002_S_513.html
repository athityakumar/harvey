Dhananjay Shanker Shetty v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

31 July 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Agrawal, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1912; (2002) 6 SCC 596; (2002) SCC (Cr) 1444; AIR 2002 SC 2787; 2002 (3) CCR 75; 2002 (3) Crimes 59; 2002 (6) CRJ 49; 2002 CRLJ 3729; 2002 (2) JCC 1281; JT 2002 (5) SC 559; 2002 (3) RCR(Criminal) 819; 2002(5) SCALE 387; 2002 (4) SLT 566; [2002] Supp1 S.C.R. 352; 2002 (5) Supreme 148

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Murder - Circumstantial evidence - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34 and 302 - Appellant, a named history sheeter charged for murder - Conviction challenged - It is well settled that merely because motive is neither alleged nor proved, same would ipso facto not affect prosecution case but in case there are other circumstances to create doubt regarding veracity of the prosecution case, this may also become material - Held, no - Prosecution version is doubtful on material aspects - No direct evidence as no eye-witness to appellant assaulting deceased - Appellant was named history sheeter, hence his test identification parade was meaningless - Recovery of blood stained clothes and weapon from house of appellant does not support prosecution case as blood was not of deceased - Conviction set aside - Appeal allowed.
