Bhatia International v Bulk Trading S.A. and another

Supreme Court of India

13 March 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, G.B. Pattanaik, S.N. Phukan

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 265; (2002) 4 SCC 105; AIR 2002 SC 1432; AIR 2002 SCW 1285; 2002 (6) ALD(SC) 108; 2002 (47) ALR 747; 2002 (46) ARBLR 675; JT 2002 (3) SC 150; 2002 (2) MLJ(SC) 175; 2002 (7) RAJ 469; 2002(2) SCALE 612; 2002 (2) SCJ 420; [2002] 2 S.C.R. 411; 2002 (2) SLT 511; 2002 (2) Supreme 395; 2002 (1) UC 648

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; International; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Foreign Award, Domestic Award

Summary: A proper and conjoint reading of all the provisions of the Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996 indicates that Part I is to apply also to international commercial arbitrations which take place out of India, unless the parties by agreement, express or implied, exclude it or any of its provisions. Further, a construction that results in hardship, serious inconvenience, injustice, absurdity or anomaly or which leads to inconsistency or uncertainty and friction in the system which the statute purports to regulate has to be rejected and preference should be given to that construction which avoids such results.
