Zahirul Islam v Mohd. Usman & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

20 December 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

S.S.M. Quadri, Ashok Bhan, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1488; (2003) 1 SCC 476; 2003 (2) ALD(SC) 89; 2003 (1) AllCJ 728; 2003 (2) AWC 1664; 2003 (1) BC 555; 2003 (102) DLT 111; 2003 (1) DMC 192; 2003 (2) JhrCR(SC) 70; JT 2002 (10) SC 569; 2003 (2) LCD 780; 2003 (1) RCR(Civil) 465; 2002(9) SCALE 727; 2003 (1) SLT 7; [2002] Supp5 S.C.R. 712; 2003 (1) Supreme 444; 2003 (1) UC 379

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Ex-Parte

Summary: Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Order IX Rule 13 and Order XXII Rule 4 - stay of execution of the decree in proceedings sought - application dismissed by the High Court on the ground that the deceased had not chosen to appear before the trial Court and the matter proceeded ex-parte during his lifetime - appeal - Held, since no permission contemplated under sub-rule (4) of Order XXII Rule 4 was obtained from the court exempting the plaintiff from bringing on record the legal representative of deceased Defendant No.2, therefore, the order under challenge cannot be sustained. - The appellant was, therefore, entitled to be brought on record in the suit.
