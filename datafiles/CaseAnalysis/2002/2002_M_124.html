Tulsi Ram and Others v Mathura Sagar Pan Tatha Krishi and Another

Supreme Court of India

12 November 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

U.C. Banerjee, Y.K. Sabharwal

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1394; (2003) 1 SCC 478; AIR 2003 SC 243; 2003 (1) AllCJ 721; 2003 (3) AWC 2548; 2003 (1) CLR 235; 2003 (1) JhrCR(SC) 68; JT 2002 (9) SC 246; 2003 (2) LCD 950; 2002(8) SCALE 364; 2002 (7) SLT 69; [2002] Supp4 S.C.R. 104; 2003 (1) UC 245

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land and Property - Madhya Pradesh Abolition of Proprietary Rights Act, 1951 - Lost grant - Litigation between the Barai community and the fishing community called the Dhimars for right of fishing in tanks - Injunction to restrain the appellants from catching fish in the said tanks and also for damages - Defence of appellant that question of there being any permission for the catch and collection of fish or its removal, would not arise since such activities were within their own rights by reason of the grant - Suit decided in favour of defendants upholding the right in terms of the grant - Upheld by HC - Held, no reason to interfere with the order of HC since, the law seems to be well settled that if a right cannot be conferred, no grant can be presumed in favour of an indefinite body of persons and members of a particular community, though of a village, in such a body of persons - Further direction that the fishing rights be auctioned and the rights thereof be conferred on to the highest bidder - Appeal dismissed.
