Commissioner Of Income Tax v Sunil J. Kinariwala

Supreme Court of India

10 December 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

S.S.M. Quadri, Arijit Pasayat

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1467; (2003) 1 SCC 660; AIR 2003 SC 668; 2002 (179) CTR 15; 2003 (179) CTR 15; [2003] 259 ITR 10; JT 2002 (10) SC 277; 2002(9) SCALE 217; 2002 (7) SLT 270; [2002] Supp4 S.C.R. 650; 2003 (1) Supreme 241; [2002] 126 TAXMAN 161

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Partnership Firm, Diversion Of Income

Summary: Income Tax and Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961 - s. 256(1) - (A) Whether, assignment of 50 percent out of the assessee's ten per cent share creates over-riding title in favour of the Trust and whether the income accruing to the Trust can be treated as the income of the assessee? - Held, it is the total income of an assessee, computed under the provisions of the Act, that is assessable to income tax So much of the income which an assessee is not entitled to receive by virtue of an over-riding title created in favour of a third party would get diverted at source and the same cannot be added in computing the total income of the assessee - (B) When does the income attributable to an assessee get diverted by over-riding title? - Held, when a third person becomes entitled to receive the amount under an obligation of an assessee even before he could lay a claim to receive it as his income, there would be diversion of income by over-riding title; but when after receipt of the income by the assessee, the same is passed on to a third person in discharge of the obligation of the assessee, it will be a case of application of income by the assessee and not of diversion of income by over-riding title - (C) Whether, the sum of Rs 20,141 being the profits referable to 50 per cent, out of the assessee's right, title and interest of ten per cent in the partnership firm is not the real income of the assessee, but of the Trust and as such assessable only in the hands of the trust? - Held, it is not a case of a sub-partnership, though in view of s.29(1) of the Indian Partnership Act, the Trust, as an assignee, becomes entitled to receive the assigned share in the profits from the firm not as a sub-partner because no sub-partnership came into existence but as an assignee of the share of income of the assigner-partner - Appeals allowed.
