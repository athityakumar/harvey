Vashu Deo v Bal Kishan

Supreme Court of India

11 January 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, Brijesh Kumar

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 20; (2002) 2 SCC 50; AIR 2002 SC 569; AIR 2002 SCW 152; 2002 (46) ALR 440; JT 2002 (1) SC 97; 2002 (1) RCR(Rent) 343; 2002(1) SCALE 123; 2002 (1) SCJ 258; [2002] 1 S.C.R. 171; 2002 (1) SLT 184; 2002 (1) Supreme 102

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Land & Property

Summary: A sub-tenant inducted by a tenant in the premises governed by the provisions of rent control law, during the continuance of sub-tenancy and without vacating the premises, cannot attorn in favour of the owner of the premises and thereby refuse to discharge his obligations towards the tenant who admittedly inducted him in the premises.
