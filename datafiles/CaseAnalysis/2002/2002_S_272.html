Mohibur Rahman and Another v State of Assam

Supreme Court of India

21 August 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, Brijesh Kumar

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1990; (2002) 6 SCC 715; (2002) SCC (Cr) 1496; AIR 2002 SC 3064; AIR 2002 SCW 3523; 2002 (3) CCR 228; 2002 (3) Crimes 235; 2002 (6) CRJ 427; JT 2002 (6) SC 235; 2002(6) SCALE 49; 2002 (5) SLT 20; 2002 (5) Supreme 530; 2002 (2) UC 650

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302/34 and 201/34 - Indian Evidence Act, s. 3 - Circumstantial evidence - Reliability of - Whether circumstantial evidence sufficient to establish beyond reasonable doubt conviction of appellant? - Held, each of incriminating pieces of circumstantial evidence should be proved by cogent and reliable evidence and Court should be satisfied that proved pieces of circumstantial evidence taken together forge such a chain wherefrom no inference other than of guilt can be drawn against accused person or, and proved pieces of circumstantial evidence should not be capable of being explained on any hypothesis other than guilt of accused - There are cases where, on account of close proximity of place and time between event of accused having been last seen with deceased and factum of death, a rational mind may be persuaded to reach an irresistible conclusion that either accused should explain how and in what circumstances victim suffered death or should own liability for homicide but in present case there is no such proximity of time and place - Appeal partly allowed.
