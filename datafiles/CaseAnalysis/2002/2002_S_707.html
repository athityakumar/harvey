Hotel Sea Gull v State of W. B. and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 March 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhan, V.N. Khare, D.M. Dharmadhikari

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1952; (2002) 4 SCC 1; AIR 2002 SC 1506; JT 2002 (3) SC 195; 2002(3) SCALE 29; 2002 (2) SCJ 518; [2002] 2 S.C.R. 576; 2002 (2) SLT 613; 2002 (2) Supreme 490

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative

Summary: Administrative - West Bengal Town and Country (Planning and Development) Act (13 of 1979) - Construction plan - Sanction - Notices - Respondent no. 2 issued notice u/s. 54 of the Act to appellant requiring to discontinue unauthorised construction - Appellant did not stop - Respondent no. 2, issued notice u/s. 53 (1) of the Act to appellant to demolish construction of second floor - Appellant filed W.P. challenging said notices before HC - Single Judge passed interim order permitting appellant to complete construction of second floor on condition that it will not claim any equity what so ever and set aside notices - Respondent no. 2 filed appeal challenging the judgment of Single Judge - Governor of West Bengal accorded approval u/s. 37 (1) of the Act to Land Use and Development Control Plan (Development Plan) - By notice (Annexure A) u/s. 54 of the Act appellant was directed to discontinue further construction of building - DB of HC accepted appeal and set aside judgment of Single Judge - Whether construction should be discontinued as per direction which was upheld by DB - Held, if planning authority had asked appellant to demolish ground and first floor then it was liable to pay compensation to owner u/s. 51(2) of the Act, if at all, and not where notice has been issued asking owner to stop unauthorised construction which was being put up - After applicability of Act, any sanction obtained which ran contrary to interim provisions of development plan under preparation could not continue by reason only of fact that permission, approval or sanction under any other law for development had been obtained - Fresh approval was required to be taken - There was no conflict between s. 51 and s. 137(2) of the Act - Order accordingly.
