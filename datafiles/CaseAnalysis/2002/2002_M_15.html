State of Karnataka v M. Devendrappa and Another

Supreme Court of India

16 January 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, M.B. Shah, B.N. Agrawal

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 26; (2002) 3 SCC 89; (2002) SCC (Cr) 539; AIR 2002 SC 671; 2002 (1) CCR 83; 2002 (2) CHN 21; 2002 (2) CRJ 349; 2002 CRLJ 998; 2002 (1) JCC 214; JT 2002 (1) SC 213; 2002 (1) RCR(Criminal) 480; 2002(1) SCALE 176; 2002 (1) SCJ 331; [2002] 1 S.C.R. 275; 2002 (1) SLT 248; 2002 (1) Supreme 192; 2002 (1) UC 294

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Charge sheet set aside

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss.155(2), 156(1), 482 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120-B, 420, 465, 468, 471 - Forgery - Charge sheet set aside - Legality - 'X' sub-leased by way of General Power of Attorney to accused no. 1 to act as Excise Contractor - It was alleged that with fraudulent intention, fake bank guarantees, confirmation letters, extension letters were submitted and there was impersonation - Charge-sheet was submitted against respondents/accused nos. 1 and 2 alleging commission of offences punishable u/ss. 465, 468, 471 and 420 r/w s. 120-B of IPC - Single Judge held that there was no evidence to infer common intention to commit such offences - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, it would be erroneous to assess the material before it and conclude that the complaint cannot be proceeded with. If the allegations set out in the complaint do not constitute the offence of which cognizance has been taken by the Magistrate, it is open to the HC to quash the same in exercise of the inherent powers u/s. 482 of CrPC. It is not, however, necessary that there should be meticulous analysis of the case before the trial to find out whether the case would end in conviction or acquittal. The allegations of mala fides against the informant are of no consequence and cannot by itself be the basis for quashing the proceeding. The order of single Judge cannot be maintained. The same is set aside. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - It would not be proper for the HC to analyse the case of the complainant in the light of all probabilities in order to determine whether a conviction would be sustainable and on such premises, arrive at a conclusion that the proceedings are to be quashed.
