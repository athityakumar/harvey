Jageshwari Devi and others v Shatrughan Ram

Supreme Court of India

07 February 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Mohapatra, Brijesh Kumar

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 2295; (2007) 15 SCC 52

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Civil Procedure Code, 1906, O.7 r.11, O.6 r.17 - Amendment petition - Rejection of plaint - Non-disclosure of causes - Respondent/plaintiff filed petition under O.6 r.17 of CPC for amendment of earlier filed plaint before Trial Court - Petitioner/defendant also filed petition under O.7 r.11 of CPC and sought rejection of plaint - Trial Court allowed said petition filed by respondent as well as rejected petitioner’s petition which was confirmed by HC on appeal - Aggrieved petitioner filed appeal against order passed by HC - Hence, instant appeal - Whether petitioner is successful in proving grounds for rejecting said plaint.

Held, main ground on which rejection of plaint was sought was that plaint does not disclose cause of action which is ground specified under O.7 r.11 (a) of CPC. Trial Court on consideration of averments in plaint held, and in Court view rightly, that it could not be held that plaint does not disclose cause of action. It is relevant to state that there is difference between non disclosure of cause of action and defective cause of action that while former comes within scope of O.7 r.11 of CPC, latter is to be decided during trial of suit. Other petition which was filed by plaintiff was for amendment of plaint to introduce prayer for recovery of possession and averment to affect that ad valorem court fee is paid on plaint. This amendment was filed in view of objection taken by defendant that suit for declaration of title simplicitor was not maintainable. Trial Court considered matter on touchstone of settled principles of law regarding amendment of pleadings and took view that this was fit case for allowing prayer for amendment. Court finds no illegality in Lower Courts order. Appeals dismissed.

Ratio - In order to commence legal action, person should have some real grievance against person, which is foundation of any legal action otherwise plaint would liable to reject.
