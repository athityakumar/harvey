Khet Singh v Union of Indi

Supreme Court of India

20 March 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

K.G. Balakrishnan, R.P. Sethi

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 187; (2002) 4 SCC 380; (2002) SCC (Cr) 806; AIR 2002 SC 1450; 2002 (2) CCR 21; 2002 (2) Crimes 33; 2002 (3) CRJ 590; 2002 CRLJ 1832; 2002 (80) ECC 476; 2002 (103) ECR 1; 2002 (142) E.L.T. 13; 2002 (1) JCC 588; JT 2002 (3) SC 208; 2002 (2) RCR(Criminal) 277; 2002 (50) RLT 376; 2002(3) SCALE 58; 2002 (2) SCJ 530; [2002] 2 S.C.R. 598; 2002 (2) SLT 627; 2002 (2) Supreme 515; 2002 (1) UC 593

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Customs; Health & Drug; Indirect Tax

Summary: The appellant was rightly found to be in possession of opium, when, there was no allegation or suggestion that the contraband article was, in any way, meddled with by the officers when the mahazar was prepared at a later stage at the Office of the Customs Department while the accused persons were very much present throughout, though it was not prepared at the spot where the accused persons were found to be in possession of the contraband article.
