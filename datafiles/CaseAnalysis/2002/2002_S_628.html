S. Shanmugavel Nadar v State of Tamil Nadu and Another

Supreme Court of India

18 September 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, Brijesh Kumar

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1157; (2002) 8 SCC 361; AIR 2002 SC 3484; 2003 (185) CTR 593; [2003] 263 ITR 658; 2003 (1) JhrCR(SC) 26; JT 2002 (7) SC 568; 2003 (1) MLJ(SC) 1; 2002 (4) RCR(Civil) 299; 2002 (2) RCR(Rent) 404; 2002(7) SCALE 29; 2002 (4) SCJ 386; 2002 (6) SLT 127; 2003 (132) STC 284; [2002] Supp2 S.C.R. 498; 2002 (7) Supreme 191

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Constitution- Art. 141 -Application of Doctrine of merger - Held, not applicable w.r.t. cases of dismissal simpliciter by the Supreme Court as it is not a declaration of law by the Supreme Court under Article 141 of the Constitution. Held, It is speech, express or necessarily implied, which only is the declaration of law. As no law was declared by Supreme Court, the Full Bench was not precluded from going into the question of law arising for decision before it and in that context entering into and examining the correctness or otherwise of the law stated by the Division Bench in M Varadaraja Pillai's case
