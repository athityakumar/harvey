Commissioner, Central Excise, Nagpur v Messrs Wainganga Sahkari S. Karkhana Limited

Supreme Court of India

18 April 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, Shivaraj V. Patil, S.P. Bharucha

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 282; (2002) 5 SCC 415; AIR 2002 SC 2095; 2002 (82) ECC 457; 2002 (102) ECR 529; 2002 (142) E.L.T. 12; JT 2002 (4) SC 483; 2002 (50) RLT 125; 2002(4) SCALE 269; [2002] 3 S.C.R. 224; 2002 (4) SLT 73; 2002 (127) STC 12; 2002 (4) Supreme 392

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Central Excise - The Tribunal's order cannot be faulted, for following its earlier decision in the case of Aruna Industries, Vishakhapatnam vs. C.C.E., Guntur as it is applicable to a situation where the assessee is erecting the structures at the construction site and fabricating materials on the spot, and when, in the instant case, it has been found as a fact by the Collector that the assessee had undertaken fabrication work at site.
