Gananath Pattnaik v State of Orissa

Supreme Court of India

06 February 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

R.P. Sethi, B.P. Singh

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 71; (2002) SCC (Cr) 461; (2002) 2 SCC 619; 2002 (1) CCR 138; 2002 (1) Crimes 309; 2002 (1) JCC 430; JT 2002 (1) SC 609; 2002 (1) RCR(Criminal) 625; 2002(2) SCALE 36; 2002 (1) SCJ 622; [2002] 1 S.C.R. 845; 2002 (1) SLT 729; 2002 (1) Supreme 545; 2002 (1) UC 366

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Conviction, Death due to cruelty

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 313 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 32 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 304B, 498A - Death due to cruelty - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant/accused allegedly harassed and tortured his deceased wife - Appellant was charged for commission of offences punishable u/ss. 304B and 498A of IPC - Trial Court convicted appellant u/s. 498A of IPC - Aggrieved appeal filed by appellant was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Appellant contended that findings returned by Trial Court regarding cruelty within meaning of s.498A of IPC are not based on any legal evidence.

Held, the statement of the PW4 and does not find any mention of her having seen the appellant pushing the deceased which, in turn, could be held to be "cruelty" driving her to commit suicide. Again there is no evidence on the record to hold that the deceased had conceived the apprehension of the appellant having illicit relations with his sister-in-law which led her to end the life. It could not be held that the deceased had conceived an apprehension about the relationship of the appellant with his sister-in-law. There was no legal evidence tendered in the case which could be made the basis for returning a finding with respect to the alleged cruelty of the appellant with the deceased. The impugned judgment of the HC set aside as also of Trial Court. Giving him the benefit of doubt, the appellant is acquitted of the charge u/s. 498A of IPC. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Concept of cruelty and its effect varies from individual to individual also depending upon social and economic status to which such person belongs.
