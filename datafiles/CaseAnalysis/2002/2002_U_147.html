Union of India v Joginder Sharma

Supreme Court of India

30 September 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

Doraiswamy Raju, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1126; (2002) 8 SCC 65; (2002) SCC (L&S) 1111; 2002 (3) CLR 606; 2002 (100) DLT 50; 2002 (95) FLR 641; 2002 (3) JhrCR(SC) 188; JT 2002 (7) SC 425; 2003 (1) LLJ 501; 2003 (1) MLJ(SC) 67; 2002(7) SCALE 141; 2003 (3) SLJ 204; 2002 (4) SLR 65; 2003 (1) SLR 65; 2002 (5) SLT 514; 2002 (7) Supreme 83; 2003 (1) UPLBEC 444

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Service

Summary: Service - compassionate appointment - Held, where the question of relaxation is in the discretion of an authority in the Government and not even in the realm of any statute or statutory rules but purely administrative and that authority as a matter of policy declines to accord relaxation, there is hardly any scope for the Tribunal/court to compel the authority to exercise the grant of relaxation.
