Lakshmi Precision Screws Limited v Ram Bahagat

Supreme Court of India

13 August 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

U.C. Banerjee, K.G. Balakrishnan

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1760; (2002) 6 SCC 552; (2002) SCC (L&S) 926; AIR 2002 SC 2914; 2002 (95) FLR 43; JT 2002 (6) SC 162; 2002 LabIC 2968; 2002 (3) LLJ 516; 2002(5) SCALE 593; 2002 (5) SLR 612; 2002 (4) SLT 694; [2002] Supp1 S.C.R. 512; 2002 (5) Supreme 315

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure; Service; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Labour & Industrial Law - (A) Whether High Court has unlimited jurisdiction in issuing a writ of certiorari and can it interfere with findings of fact? - Held, no - A writ of certiorari can be issued for correcting errors of jurisdiction committed by inferior courts or tribunals: these are cases where orders are passed by inferior courts or tribunals without jurisdiction, or is in excess of it, or as a result of failure to exercise jurisdiction - Jurisdiction to issue a writ of certiorari is a supervisory jurisdiction and court exercising it is not entitled to act as an Appellate Court - This limitation necessarily means that findings of fact reached by inferior court or tribunal as result of appreciation of evidence cannot be reopened or questioned in writ proceedings - (B) Whether doctrine of natural justice should be read in as an inbuilt requirement of standing orders? - Further held, it should be - Arbitrariness is an antithesis to rule of law, equity, fair play and justice - Contract of employment there may be but it cannot be devoid of basic principles of concept of justice - Justice-oriented approach as is present trend in Indian jurisprudence shall have to read as an inbuilt requirement of basic of concept of justice, to with, doctrine of natural justice, fairness equality and rule of law - (C) Whether it can be said to be within jurisdiction of Supreme Court u/art. 136 of Constitution to intervene or interfere with an appraisal of evidence on record? - Further held, yes - In event of there being a misreading of law or applicability of law wrongly, intervention cannot but be said to be correct approach to matter - Appeal dismissed.
