Amar Nath Chowdhury v Braithwaite and Company Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 January 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

V.N. Khare, Ashok Bhan

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 28; (2002) 2 SCC 290; AIR 2002 SC 678; 2002 (1) AWC 517; [2002] 111 Comp Cas 707; 2002 (2) CompLJ 223; 2002 (93) FLR 850; 2002 (1) JhrCR(SC) 475; JT 2002 (1) SC 156; 2002 (35) LabIC 521; 2002 (1) LLJ 1048; 2002 (1) LLN 635; 2002(1) SCALE 173; 2002 (1) SCJ 268; [2002] 1 S.C.R. 184; 2002 (2) SLJ 433; 2002 (1) SLT 244; 2002 (1) Supreme 189; 2002 (1) UPLBEC 516

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Employment - Removal-Dismissal of appeal-Validity-Appellant removed from service by chairman-cum-managing director acting as disciplinary authority-Appeal to Board of Directors-Board presided over by chairman-cum-managing director (who passed order of removal) dismissed appeal by non-speaking order-Dismissal of appeal not justified-Fair play demanded that chairman-cum-managing director ought not to have participated in deliberations of Board deciding appeal-Board could have constituted committee for hearing appeal-Hence, doctrine of necessity did not apply-Orders of High Court and appellate authority set aside-Matter remitted to appellate authority to decide appeal by speaking order
