 Chandra Prakash and Others v State of Uttar Pradesh and Another

Supreme Court of India

04 April 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, Arijit Pasayat, R.C. Lahoti, Ruma Pal, S.P. Bharucha

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1213; (2002) 4 SCC 234; (2002) SCC (L&S) 496; AIR 2002 SC 1652; AIR 2002 SCW 1573; 2002 (2) AWC 1414; 2002 (93) FLR 608; 2002 (2) JhrCR(SC) 108; JT 2002 (3) SC 492; 2002 (2) LLJ 841; 2002(3) SCALE 311; 2002 (3) SCJ 1; [2002] 2 S.C.R. 913; 2003 (1) SLJ 222; 2002 (3) SLR 326; 2002 (3) SLT 26; 2002 (3) Supreme 105; 2003 (1) UC 598; 2002 (2) UPLBEC 1267

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Uttar Pradesh Public Commission Rules, 1979 - Uttar Pradesh Provincial Medical Services (PMS) - Ad-hoc Appointments - Regularization - Seniority - Challenged - Appellants were appointed as doctors on temporary basis - Respondent /State purported to regularize services of temporary doctors by promulgation of the Rulesand sought to give these appointees seniority only from date of their such regularized appointment under the Rules - Seniority arose between temporary doctors originally appointed and doctors appointed through public Service Commission - Aggrieved appellants claimed that their seniority should be considered from date of initial appointments which was rejected - Appellants filed writ petition before HC which was upheld and stated that appellant's seniority should be counted from date of their initial appointment in PMS cadre and that they were also entitled to all service benefits which were due to them after so fixing their seniority - Aggrieved respondent/state filed appeal before SC which was dismissed and affirmed HC's Order - Subsequently similar petitions were filed wherein judgment of HC was again confirmed by DB on 21 -1- 1993 - During pendency of some petitions, it was seen from record that some of selected doctors who were not given letter of appointment by respondent/State Govt., approached State Service Tribunal seeking direction that they be appointed in service in accordance with selections and recommendations made by Public Service Commission which was in favour of appellants - Hence, instant appeal -Whether there was any conflict between judgment of 2-Judge Bench and that of 3-Judge Bench and if so, what was effect of judgments dt. 23-3-1995 and 26 -7-1996 of 2-Judge Bench - Held, Court opined that it did not pass any orders on impleadment/ intervention applications pending in these petitions and those would be decided by Bench hearing these writ petitions on their merits - Hence, directed said conflicts between judgments should be listed for disposal before 3 Judges Bench - Order accordingly.
