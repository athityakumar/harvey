Essen Deinki v Rajiv Kumar

Supreme Court of India

29 October 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

U.C. Banerjee, Y.K. Sabharwal

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1184; (2002) 8 SCC 400; (2003) SCC (L&S) 13; AIR 2003 SC 38; 2003 (1) AllCJ 219; 2003 (1) AWC 240; 2003 (1) CGLJ 44; 2002 (5) ESC 266; 2002 (95) FLR 949; 2003 (1) JhrCR(SC) 4; JT 2002 (8) SC 471; 2002 (3) LLJ 1111; 2002(8) SCALE 188; 2002 (6) SLR 538; 2002 (6) SLT 235; [2002] Supp3 S.C.R. 499; 2002 (7) Supreme 393; 2003 (1) UPLBEC 302

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Constitution - Labour & Industrial Law - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 227 - High Court was approached u/art. 227 against order of Labour Court and High Court relying upon decision of Supreme Court in American Express (supra) came to a conclusion that workman in fact has completed 240 days of service and as such allowed writ petition and did set aside award of Labour Court with a direction that petitioner be reinstated in service with full back wages - Whether Respondent-workman had completed 240 days of service in terms of statutory provisions? - Exercise of jurisdiction u/art. 227 is limited and restrictive in nature - It is so exercised in normal circumstances for want of jurisdiction, errors of law, perverse findings and gross violation of natural justice - It is merely a revisional jurisdiction and does not confer an unlimited authority or prerogative to correct all orders or even wrong decisions made within limits of jurisdiction of Courts below - Filing of an affidavit is only his own statement in his favour and that cannot be regarded as sufficient evidence for any Court or Tribunal to come to conclusion that a workman had, in fact, worked for 240 days in a year - No proof of receipt of salary or wages for 240 days or order or record of appointment or engagement for this period was produced by workman - Thus, question of affirmance of impugned judgment cannot and does not arise more so by reason of fact that even Court searched in vain in regard to availability of such an evidence - HC, has thus committed a manifest error in reversing order of Labour Court - Finding of fact being within domain of inferior Tribunal except where it is a perverse recording thereof or not based on any material whatsoever resulting in manifest injustice, interference under art. 136 is not called for - Impugned order stands set aside and quashed and order of Labour Court stands restored - Appeal allowed.
