Yadarao Dajiba Shrawane (Dead) By Lrs v Nanilal Harakchand Shah (Dead) and Others

Supreme Court of India

02 August 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Mohapatra, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1750; (2002) 6 SCC 404; AIR 2002 SC 2849; 2002 (2) AllCJ 1547; 2002 (48) ALR 689; 2002 (6) Bom.C.R. 551; 2002 (2) CRC 475; 2002 (3) JhrCR(SC) 190; JT 2002 (5) SC 579; 2002 (2) LCD 1118; 2003 (1) RCR(Civil) 509; 2002(5) SCALE 461; 2002 (4) SLT 630; 2002 (5) Supreme 282

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 145 - Madhya Pradesh Public Trust Act, 1950, s. 32 - Disputes between two Jain communities regarding exclusive rights of management over two adjoining fields - Jurisdiction of High Court - Held, findings on documentary evidence placed on record and statements made by some witnesses which can be construed as admissions or conclusions - When judgment of final court of fact is based on misinterpretation of documentary evidence or on consideration of inadmissible evidence or ignoring material evidence HC in second appeal is entitled to interfere with judgment - Admission of parties or their witnesses are relevant pieces of evidence and should be given due weight age by courts - A finding of fact ignoring admissions or concessions is vitiated in law and can be interfered with by HC in second appeal - No interference - Appeal dismissed.
