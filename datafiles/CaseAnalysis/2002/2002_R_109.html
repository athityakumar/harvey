State of Uttar Pradesh and Others v Ramesh Chandra Mangalik

Supreme Court of India

04 March 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

Brijesh Kumar, D.P. Mohapatra

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 136; (2002) SCC (L&S) 413; (2002) 3 SCC 443; AIR 2002 SC 1241; AIR 2002 SCW 1033; 2002 (2) AWC 1203; 2002 (1) CLR 1101; 2002 (93) FLR 314; JT 2002 (2) SC 459; 2002 LabIC 978; 2002 (2) LLJ 539; 2002 (2) LLN 436; 2002(2) SCALE 413; 2002 (2) SLR 675; 2002 (2) SLT 322; 2002 (2) Supreme 191; 2002 (2) UPLBEC 1199

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Employment-Order withholding 4 increments permanently and re-covery of certain amount-Validity-Whether non-supply of documents to violate principles of natural justice as held by High Court?-Held, “no”-Documents not supplied to respon-dent related to personal file of other officers-And not at all relied upon by enquiry officer in support of any charge-Respondent not able to pin-point any particular document made use of by enquiry officer copies or in-spection whereof not given or allowed to respondent-Prejudice caused to respondent by non-supply of docu-ments-Not shown-High Court not going into question of relevance of documents-Finding of High Court that principles of natural justice vio-lated on account of non-supply of documents-Not sustainable-Cross-examination of witness sought for by respondent-But witness had died-And this fact brought to notice of re-spondent-Judgment of High Court set aside.
