Kanhaiya Lal Agrawal v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

29 July 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, P. Venkatarama Reddi

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1584; (2002) 6 SCC 315; AIR 2002 SC 2766; 2002 (2) ARBLR 616; 2002 (4) AWC 2774; 2002 (2) CRC 415; 2002 (3) JhrCR(SC) 72; JT 2002 (5) SC 383; 2002 (3) RAJ 74; 2002 (3) RCR(Civil) 655; 2002(5) SCALE 339; 2002 (4) SLT 549; [2002] Supp1 S.C.R. 284; 2002 (5) Supreme 145

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Civil Procedure - Contract - Tender - Rebate - Challenged - Respondent invited tenders for execution of five items of work including supply, delivery and stacking of 75,000 cubic meter Machine crushed track ballast as per specifications at its depot and loading it into railway wagons - Five tenders were received - Respondent No.1 accepted tender offered by appellant on rates subject to rebate and agreement was entered into by him on 19 -4-2001 - Respondent No.5 claiming that his tender should have been accepted, as rates offered by him were lowest and filed writ petition before Single Judge HC which made clear that offer made by respondent No. 5 after opening of tender was of no consequence and gave direction to respondent No.1 of taking fresh offers from appellant and respondent No. 5 - Aggrieved by said order respondent No.5 filed appeal before DB which, set aside order of Single Judge and directed fresh negotiations with parties - Further , directed that supply of material by appellant be stopped forthwith and balance material be taken from respondent No.5 at rate furnished by him - Hence, instant appeal - whether tender offered by appellant with rebate could have been accepted and such acceptance would affect interests of any other party - Held, appellant made his offer of concessional rates along with tender while respondent No.5 made such offer after opening of tenders - It was difficult to conceive that respondent No.5 who was prudent businessman would not be aware of commercial practice of giving rebate or concession in event of quick finalization of transaction - What appellant offered was part of tender itself while respondent No.5 made such offer separately and much later - There was nothing illegal or arbitrary on part of respondent No.1/Railway Administration in accepting offer of appellant, which was made at time of submitting tender itself - Hence, set aside orders made by HC both made by DB and Single Judge - Petition dismissed.
