Hindustan Petroleum Corporation Limited v Sriman Narayan and Another

Supreme Court of India

09 July 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Mohapatra, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1311; (2002) 5 SCC 760; AIR 2002 SC 2598; AIR 2002 SCW 2907; 2002 (2) AllCJ 1396; 2002 (2) ARBLR 619; 2002 (2) ARC 363; 2002 (3) AWC 2486; 2002 (2) CRC 327; 2002 (3) JhrCR(SC) 57; JT 2002 (5) SC 335; 2002 (3) RCR(Civil) 560; 2002(5) SCALE 132; 2002 (4) SLT 334; 2002 (4) Supreme 546

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 39, rr. 1 and 2, O. 43, r. 1(r) - Indian Easements Act, 1891 - Whether High Court was justified in granting relief of interim injunction in appellate jurisdiction, without considering question as to plaintiff-petitioner will suffer irreparable loss which cannot be adequately compensated by damages? - Dorab Cawasji Warden case, 1990 Indlaw SC 699 followed - HC not at all discussed considerations which weighed and reasons which persuaded Trial Court in rejecting prayer for interim mandatory injunction as prayed for by plaintiff - Held, order passed by HC granting prayer for interim injunction, in context of facts and circumstances of case, unsustainable - Appeals allowed.
