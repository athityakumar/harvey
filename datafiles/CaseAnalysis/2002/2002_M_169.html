State of Uttar Pradesh and others v Messrs Sitapur Packing Wood Suppliers etc

Supreme Court of India

23 April 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.K. Sabharwal, K.G. Balakrishnan

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 254; (2002) 4 SCC 566; AIR 2003 SC 2165; 2002 (2) AllCJ 1209; 2002 (48) ALR 4; 2002 (3) AWC 1834; JT 2002 (4) SC 341; 2002(4) SCALE 26; [2002] 3 S.C.R. 345; 2002 (3) SLT 453; 2002 (3) Supreme 479; 2002 (2) UC 339

Case Digest

Subject: Environment, Wildlife & Animal; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Uttar Pradesh Transit of Timber and other Forest Produce Rules, 1978

Summary: When, the transit fee under Rule 5 of U.P. Transit of Timber and Other Forest Produce Rules, 1978 framed under the Indian Forest Act, 1927, was regulatory, then, it was not necessary for the State to establish the factum of rendering of service as the question of quid pro quo is necessary only when a fee is compensatory.
