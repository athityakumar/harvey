Mata Gujri Memorial Medical College v State of Bihar and others

Supreme Court of India

12 December 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, K.G. Balakrishnan

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 2336; (2009) 16 SCC 309

Case Digest

Subject: Education

Keywords: All India Council For Technical Education Act, 1987, Dr. M.G.R. Medical University, Tamil Nadu Act, 1987, Establishment of Medical Regulation Act, 1999, Bihar State Universities Laws Act

Summary: Education - Indian Medical Council Act, 1956 - Bihar State Universities Laws Act, 1976, s. 21(2) - Establishment of medical college - Grant of affiliation - Provisional affiliation - Justifiability - Appellant/trust had set up medical college but failed to obtain necessary affiliation from university - Appellant approached HC by filing petition which resulted into dismissal stating that State Govt. rightly refused approval to grant of affiliation - During pendency of that petition, appellant filed several special leave petition in that HC directed appellant to approach central govt. for grant of recognition - Further, matter was directed before Constitution Bench of HC in which it was held that it was improper for Bench of two Judges to refer matter to Constitution Bench and Institution was granted provisional affiliation after due inspection by authorities of Medical Council of India(MCI) as well as Central Govt. - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether order passed by Larger Bench was justified - Held, purpose for which 1956 Act, was enacted and looking at procedure adopted by Medical Council before granting permission in question as engrafted in regulation, it was difficult to hold that those stringent provisions and requirements being complied with and Medical Council on being satisfied, State Govt. had its say in matter of affiliation of institution to University in exercise of its powers u/s. 21(2) of 1976 Act - However, both Medical Council as well as Central Govt. were satisfied with inspection of appellant Institution and were satisfied about, capability of Institution to impart MBBS course for students annually - Therefore, on basis of available material, State Govt. could prevent grant of affiliation to Institution in question and could not be denuded of its power even to derecognize/disaffiliate Institution - Thus, University should be granted permanent affiliation to Institution which was not granted so far as of pendency of matter - Appeal dismissed.
