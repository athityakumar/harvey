State Bank of Saurashtra v Messrs Ashit Shipping Services Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

12 April 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, S.S.M. Quadri

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1973; (2002) 4 SCC 736; AIR 2002 SC 1993; 2002 (3) ALD(SC) 83; 2002 (47) ALR 567; 2002 (2) BC 536; 2002 (3) CalLT(SC) 1; [2002] 110 Comp Cas 329; 2002 (2) CRC 1; 2002 (3) G.L.R. 2401; JT 2002 (4) SC 85; 2002 (132) PLR 547; 2002(3) SCALE 390; 2002 (3) SCJ 95; [2002] 2 S.C.R. 1074; 2002 (3) SLT 149; 2002 (3) Supreme 247; 2002 (2) UC 78

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Civil Procedure - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, O. 37 rr. 1 and 2 - Trial Court refused leave to defend - Both Appellants and 2nd Respondent filed revisions before High Court which dismissed them - Whether leave to defend could be granted? - Held, on face of it document appears to be an Indemnity and not a Guarantee therefore Court requires to consider nature and meaning of document and by itself necessitates granting of leave to defend - Further, leave to defend has been granted only to Appellants - Appeal disposed of.
