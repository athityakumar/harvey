Mohan Singh v Prem Singh and another

Supreme Court of India

01 October 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

D.M. Dharmadhikari, U.C. Banerjee

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1149; (2002) 10 SCC 236; (2003) SCC (Cr) 1514; AIR 2002 SC 3582; 2003 (1) CalLT(SC) 21; 2002 (4) CCR 123; 2003 (1) CHN 1; 2002 (4) Crimes 453; 2003 (1) CRJ 632; 2003 CRLJ 11; JT 2002 (8) SC 7; 2002 (4) RCR(Criminal) 842; 2002(7) SCALE 168; 2002 (4) SCJ 527; 2002 (6) SLT 17; [2002] Supp3 S.C.R. 5; 2002 (7) Supreme 163

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: IPC - S 34, 302, 324 - Statement under Section 313, Cr.P.C. - Whether substantive piece of evidence ? Held, Such statement can be used for appreciating evidence led by the prosecution to accept or reject it. It is, however, not a substitute for the evidence of the prosecution. Prosecution evidence does not inspire confidence to sustain the conviction of the accused
