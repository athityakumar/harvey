Hansraj and Sons v State of Jammu and Kashmir and Others

Supreme Court of India

17 July 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Mohapatra, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1693; (2002) 6 SCC 227; AIR 2002 SC 2692; JT 2002 (5) SC 242; 2002(5) SCALE 240; 2002 (3) SCJ 389; 2002 (4) SLT 444; 2002 (128) STC 203; [2002] Supp1 S.C.R. 199; 2002 (5) Supreme 46; 2002 (3) UPLBEC 2015

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Sales Tax

Summary: Direct Tax - Constitution - Levy of Tolls Act Samvat, 1995, s. 3 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 19(1)(g), 226 and 286 - Constitution of Jammu and Kashmir, s. 103 - Notification No. S R O 348 D/- 20-08-2982 - Toll tax - Manufacture - Legality and validity of levy of additional toll tax under said notification on dry fruits like almonds, walnuts and walnut kernels - Petition filed against impugned notification - Notification was quashed as impugned notification is ultra vires statute and that it also violates fundamental right of petitioner u/arts. 14 and 19(1)(g) and is otherwise ultra vires of art. 286(1) - Whether judgment of High Court is sustainable or not? - Position that emerges is that impugned notification in which additional toll tax was levied was clearly beyond purview of s. 3 of 1995 Act - Further, finding of HC that in context of facts and circumstances of case, processing of dry fruits like almonds, walnuts and walnut kernels did not come within expression 'manufacture' cannot be said to be erroneous - Held, judgment of HC upholding levy of additional toll tax in case is unsustainable - Therefore, judgment of HC is set aside - Appeal allowed.
