Pramodkumar Rasikbhai Jhaveri v Karmasey Kunvargi Tak and Others

Supreme Court of India

05 August 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

K.G. Balakrishnan, U.C. Banerjee

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1753; (2002) 6 SCC 455; (2002) SCC (Cr) 1355; 2002 (3) ACC 66; AIR 2002 SC 2864; 2002 (49) ALR 210; 2002 (4) AWC 3251; 2002 (2) CRC 374; 2002 (3) G.L.R. 2763; 2002 (3) JhrCR(SC) 83; JT 2002 (6) SC 380; 2002 (3) MLJ(SC) 180; 2002 (132) PLR 467; 2002 (3) RCR(Civil) 688; 2002(5) SCALE 493; 2002 (5) Supreme 198; 2002 (2) UC 565

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - High Court held that there was contributory negligence on part of appellant to extent of 30% and proportionate deduction was made from total compensation - Whether HC has erred in fixing total compensation? - Held, HC held that monthly income of appellant would have been Rs. 4100 per month and by applying multiplier of 8 years, claim should be Rs. 3,93,600 towards future loss of income - Multiplier adopted by HC is wrong or amount of compensation granted for future loss of income is inadequate - Court does not think that HC erred in fixing quantum of compensation - (B) Whether HC was justified in holding that there was contributory negligence on part of appellant? - Further held, HC found that there was contributory negligence on part of appellant for two reasons - Firstly, appellant who was driving car did not slow down his vehicle when he saw that truck coming at a high speed from opposite direction and, secondly, HC found that there was a three-feet width of road on left side of car of appellant could have swerved his vehicle to left side - Question of contributory negligence arises when there has been some act or omission on claimant's part, which has materially contributed to damage caused, and is of such a nature that it may properly be described as "negligence" - Court do not think that these two reasons given by HC fully justify accepted principles of contributory negligence - Appeal partly allowed.
