Neycer India Limited v Gmb Ceramics Limited

Supreme Court of India

07 February 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, S.N. Variava, S.N. Phukan

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 823; (2002) 9 SCC 489; 2002 AllCJ 454; 2002 (46) ARBLR 525; 2002 (1) CalLT(SC) 41; JT 2002 (2) SC 491; 2002 (2) RAJ 25

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Constitution

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration Act, 1940, ss. 41 and 31 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Forum Selection clause - 'City Civil Court, Madras shall be only Court, which shall have jurisdiction' - Pecuniary jurisdiction - Whether Calcutta High Court or Madras HC retains jurisdiction to entertain and decide legality of an award passed by arbitral tribunal considering clause 'City Civil Court Madras shall have jurisdiction' and Madras HC refused to entertain application on ground that Calcutta HC has already exercised jurisdiction and by invoking provisions of s. 31(4) Arbitration Act, 1940? - Held, since jurisdiction of Calcutta HC has already been decided by that very Court itself and also by Madras HC in rejecting appellant's application which appellant ever assailed in this Court, it would be futile to interfere with impugned order at this stage - No interference - Appeal dismissed.
