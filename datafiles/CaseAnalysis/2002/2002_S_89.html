Ram Narayan Sharma v Shakuntala Gaur

Supreme Court of India

29 April 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

Brijesh Kumar, D.P. Mohapatra

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 332; (2002) 5 SCC 184; AIR 2002 SC 2204; 2002 (2) AllCJ 1351; 2002 (48) ALR 230; 2002 (2) ARC 1; 2002 (4) AWC 2725; JT 2002 (Supp1) SC 423; 2002 (1) RCR(Rent) 564; 2002(4) SCALE 220; [2002] 3 S.C.R. 562; 2002 (3) SLT 422; 2002 (3) Supreme 632; 2002 (2) UC 327

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Keywords: Charitable or Religious entities

Summary: Uttar Pradesh Urban Buildings (Regulation of Letting, Rent; and Eviction) Act, 1972 - Section 21(1)(a) and Section 16(1)(a) - The Revisional Court and the High Court both erred in considering the appellant as a "prospective allottee" since his position was more akin to an allottee in possession. Further, the respondent could not be permitted to file for an eviction petition, when she failed to get the premises vacated under Section 21(1)(a) of the Act on the ground of her bona fide requirement within three years of purchasing the property.
