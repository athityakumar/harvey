Gauhati High Court and another v Kuladhar Phukan and another

Supreme Court of India

22 March 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, K.G. Balakrishnan

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 204; (2002) 4 SCC 524; (2002) SCC (L&S) 555; AIR 2002 SC 1589; 2002 (47) ALR 637; JT 2002 (3) SC 412; 2002 LabIC 1492; 2002(3) SCALE 175; 2002 (2) SCJ 658; [2002] 2 S.C.R. 808; 2002 (3) SLR 256; 2002 (2) SLT 687; 2002 (2) Supreme 600

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Indirect Tax; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: When, under Article 234 of the Constitution of India appointments of persons other than District Judges to the judicial services of a State are required to be made by the Governor of the State in accordance with the rules made by him in that behalf after consultation with the State Public Service Commission and with the High Court exercising jurisdiction in relation to such State, then, an invalidity caused by failure to comply with mandatory constitutional requirement, such as of consultation, cannot be cured by sheer inaction on the part of one or both of the functionaries between whom the requirement was to be fulfilled or by mere lapse of time.
