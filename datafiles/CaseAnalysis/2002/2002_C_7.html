India Automobiles (1960) Limited v Calcutta Municipal Corporation

Supreme Court of India

13 February 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

R.P. Sethi, G.B. Pattanaik, B.P. Singh

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1945; (2002) 3 SCC 388; AIR 2002 SC 1089; 2002 (2) CalLT(SC) 1; JT 2002 (2) SC 101; 2002(2) SCALE 192; 2002 (2) SCJ 8; [2002] 1 S.C.R. 961; 2002 (2) SLT 56; 2002 (2) Supreme 5

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Municipalities & Local Governments; Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - Municipalities & Local Governments - Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Calcutta Municipal Corporation Act, 1980,ss. 174, 194 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Delhi Rent Control Act, 1958 - Annual valuation - Assesement - Sustanability - Aggrieved by determination of annual valuation made in terms of s. 174 of the 1980 Act appellant of nine storeyed building, admittedly, used for commercial purposes, filed appeal before Tribunal who vide its order allowed appeal and fixed annual value on basis of rent actually received by appellant - Aggrieved respondent approached HC - HC vide order set aside order of Tribunal and directed it to hear appeals on merits keeping in mind that total amount paid by sub-tenants to tenant of appellant should also be taken into consideration in assessing annual valuation - Not satisfied with judgment of HC appellant of building filed appeal - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order passed by HC was justified - Held, basis for determination of annual rent value had to be standard rent where Rent Control Act was applicable and in all other cases reasonable determination of such rent by municipal authorities keeping in view various factors including rent which tenant was getting from his sub-tenant - In appropriate cases owner of property mightbe in position to satisfy authorities that gross annual rent of building of which annual valuation was being determined could not be more than actual rent received by such owner from his tenant - Municipal authorities were to keep in mind that various pronouncements of Court statutory provisions were made in specified Municipal Acts, keeping in mind applicability or non-applicability of Rent Act and peculiar circumstances of each case, to find out gross annual rent of building including service charges, if any, at which such land or building might, at time of assessment, be reasonably expected to let from year to year in terms of s. 174 of the 1980 Act - In present case Court opined that HC was right in remanding appeals to Tribunal for deciding on merits but was not justified to restrict consideration only on basis of rent being paid by sub-tenant to tenant for purposes of determining gross annual value - Tribunal shall consider appeals in light of Judgment by keeping in mind rent actually received by owner of building and rent being paid to tenant by his sub-tenant - Tribunal for determining gross annual rent at which building in controversy, at time of assessment, was reasonably expected to let from year to year, less allowances and other considerations referred to in s. 174 of the 1980 Act - If annual valuation determined was more than gross annual rent which appellant was actually receiving from his tenant appellant shall be at liberty to recover excess amount paid in terms of s. 194 of the 1980 Act - Appeal disposed of.
