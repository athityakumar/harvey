State of Uttar Pradesh and others v M. C. Chattopadhyaya and others

Supreme Court of India

27 February 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, S.N. Phukan, S.N. Variava

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 2395; (2004) 12 SCC 333

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Education - Uttar Pradesh Public Services (Reservation for Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribes and Other Backward Classes) Act, 1994, 3(5) - Appointment - Justifiability - DB held that there should not be any reservation in respect of post of Professor in university - Whether the order passed by DB was justifiable - Held, there should be reservation in respect of post of Professor and provisions of the Act would apply, but the same should not be applied taking all Professors as cadre and it had to be made subject wise - There should not be reservation for isolated post - In deciding question of reservation appropriate authority should follow roster as had been published in exercise of power u/s. 3(5) of the Act and then roster should be duly complied with in accordance with principles enunciated by SC in R. K. Sabharwal and Others v State of Punjab and Others, 1995 Indlaw SC 1756 - Impugned judgment on basis of earlier DB judgment in case of Ram Niwas Pandey (Dr.) v State of U.P. was possibly not correct reading of judgment - Advertisement that was issued in year 1995, which came up for consideration before SC in the case of State Of Uttar Pradesh v Dr. Dina Nath Shukla & Anr. 1997 Indlaw SC 3051 was judgment which was under consideration before HC in impugned judgment - SC held that law was declared and Vice-Chancellor would work out details and make fresh advertisement and have selection done in accordance with law and appointments made accordingly - Nothing survived for the High Court in the impugned judgment to give any direction contrary to the same - Vice-Chancellor of University should act accordingly - Appeals disposed of.
