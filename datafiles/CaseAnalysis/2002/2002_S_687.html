Ayyub and Others v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

28 February 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

K.G. Balakrishnan, R.P. Sethi

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 118; (2002) 3 SCC 510; (2002) SCC (Cr) 673; AIR 2002 SC 1192; AIR 2002 SCW 968; 2002 (1) CCR 213B; 2002 (2) Crimes 84; 2002 (3) CRJ 538; 2002 (1) JCC 532; JT 2002 (2) SC 371; 2002 (2) RCR(Criminal) 29B; 2002(2) SCALE 349; 2002 (2) SCJ 212; [2002] 2 S.C.R. 106; 2002 (2) SLT 246; 2002 (2) Supreme 156; 2002 (1) UC 552

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: TADA Act, Terrorist and Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Rules, 1987

Summary: The learned Designated Judge was not justified in rejecting the application for withdrawal from prosecution charges framed under the TADA Act, when, the State Government after proper discussion on the facts of the case and the evidence, reports, and letters available on the record had decided to waive the TADA Sections recorded in the enclosed list, and when, such a plea was not tainted with any mala fide intention. Further, under Section 15 of the TADA Act a confession statement is inadmissible in evidence when the statements of appellants were recorded not in accordance with law and there is nothing on record to show that the same was voluntarily made by the appellants.
