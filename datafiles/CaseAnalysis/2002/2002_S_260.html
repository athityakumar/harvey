Nallabothu Venkaiah v State of Andhra Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

20 August 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

H.K. Sema, Y.K. Sabharwal

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1920; (2002) 7 SCC 117; (2002) SCC (Cr) 1615; AIR 2002 SC 2945; 2002 (3) CCR 211; 2002 (3) Crimes 221; 2002 (6) CRJ 297; 2002 CRLJ 4081; 2002 (3) JCC 1582; JT 2002 (6) SC 213; 2002 (4) RCR(Criminal) 373; 2002(6) SCALE 26; 2002 (4) SLT 811; [2002] Supp1 S.C.R. 606; 2002 (5) Supreme 484; 2002 (2) UC 644

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Explosive Substance Act, ss. 3 and 5 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 141, 148, 149, 300(3), 302 and 326 - High Court acquitted accused Nos. 5, 7, 8 and 10 - However, confirmed conviction and sentence of appellant u/s. 302 - Appeal against conviction - Conviction u/s. 302 simpliciter without aid of s. 149 is permissible if overt act is attributed to accused resulting in fatal injury which is independently sufficient in ordinary course of nature to cause death of deceased and is supported by medical evidence - Charge u/s. 302 with aid of s. 149 could be converted into one u/s. 302 r/w. s. 34 if criminal act done by several persons - Held, from overt acts attributed to accused appellant by PWs 1 to 3, corroborated by medical evidence, it is apparent that appellant has caused murderous assault resulting in death of deceased - Eye-witness version is supported by medical evidence attributing specific overt acts to appellant - Ocular and medical evidence on record clearly establish guilt of appellant beyond reasonable doubt for causing death of deceased - Further held, acquittal of other accused, rendered by High Court was wrong and based on misappreciation of evidence therefore, contention that appellant could not have been convicted relying upon same evidence deserves to be rejected - Appeal dismissed.
