J.J. Lal Private Limited and others v M.R. Murali and another

Supreme Court of India

08 February 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, Brijesh Kumar

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 90; (2002) 3 SCC 98; AIR 2002 SC 1061; 2002 (4) ALD(SC) 90; 2002 (2) AllCJ 993; 2002 (47) ALR 230; JT 2002 (1) SC 657; 2002 (2) LCD 1235; 2002 (2) MLJ(SC) 63; 2002 (1) RCR(Rent) 263; 2002(2) SCALE 98; 2002 (1) SCJ 684; [2002] 1 S.C.R. 919; 2002 (1) SLT 762; 2002 (1) Supreme 594

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Land & Property

Summary: Under Section 10(2)(vii) of the Tamil Nadu Buildings (Lease and Rent Control) Act, a decree on the ground of denial of landlord's title by tenant and such denial being 'not bona fide' could not have been a ground for directing eviction of tenant, especially if, the application for eviction filed by the landlord does not plead such a cause of action, setting out material facts and as providing a ground for relief of eviction.
