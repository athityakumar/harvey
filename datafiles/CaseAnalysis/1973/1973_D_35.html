Prabhu Dayal Deorah and another v District Magistrate, Kamrup and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 October 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Hameedullah Beg, Kuttyil Kurien Mathew, A.K. Mukherjea

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 486; (1974) SCC (Cr) 18; (1974) 1 SCC 103; AIR 1974 SC 183; 1974 CRLJ 286; 1973 CrLR(SC) 729; [1974] 2 S.C.R. 12

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 22(5) and 32 - Maintenance Of Internal Security Act, 1971, ss. 3, 8 and 10 - Vague ground of detention - Charged of unauthorised milling of paddy and smuggling resultant for profit - No particular instances of unauthorised milling or smuggling rice was mentioned - (A) Whether ground of unauthorised milling of paddy in their Rice Mill and smuggling resultant rice to Meghalaya was valid? - Held, ground of unauthorized milling which was an independent ground referred to past activities of petitioners, and was vague for want of particulars so as to vitiate orders of detention u/s. 3 of Act of 1971, this could in no way be treated as a conclusion reached from fact of seizure of paddy and rice from unauthorised possession of petitioners - (B) Whether interference of Supreme Court can be sought even if one of grounds of detention are vague? - If one of grounds communicated to petitioners is found to be vague, detention orders must be pronounced to be bad - Held, the petitioners have every right in such a case to come to Supreme Court u/art. 32 complaining that their detention is bad as violating their fundamental right - Merely because advisory board was seized of matter when writ petitions were filed and could also consider contention of petitioner in their representations that grounds were vague, Supreme Court would not refuse to interfere with orders of detention - Appeal allowed.
