 State Of Tamil Nadu, Etc v Sitolakshmi Mills and Others

Supreme Court of India

21 December 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

Kuttyil Kurien Mathew, A.N. Ray, Hans Raj Khanna, A. Alagiriswami, P.N. Bhagwati

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 221; (1974) SCC (Tax) 258; (1974) 4 SCC 408; AIR 1974 SC 1505; 1974 (3) CTR 59; [1974] 3 S.C.R. 1; 1974 (33) STC 200; 1974 TAXLR 1995

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Sales Tax

Summary: Central Sales Tax Act, 1956, s. 8(2)(b) - Limitation Act, 1908 - Constitution, arts. 303(1), 301 - Constitutionality of s. 8(2)(b) of 1956 Act - Tax at higher rate - Object of - Held, not violative of arts. 301and 303 (1) of Constitution - Main reason for enacting s. 8(2)(b) was to canalize inter-State trade through registered dealers, over whom the appropriate Government has control and thus to prevent evasion of tax - Appeal Allowed.
