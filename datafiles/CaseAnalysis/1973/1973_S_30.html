 Shree Meenakshi Mills Limited v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

26 November 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Ray, D.G. Palekar, Y.V. Chandrachud, P.N. Bhagwati, V.R. Krishna Iyer

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 291; (1974) 1 SCC 468; AIR 1974 SC 366; [1974] 2 S.C.R. 398

Case Digest

Subject: Textiles; Trade

Keywords: Cotton Textile Cess Act, 1948, Tariff Act, 1934, Quotas, Essential Commodities Ordinance 1955, Iron and Steel ( Control of Production and Distribution) Order, 1941, Assam Foodgrains (Licensing and Control) Order, 1961, Cotton Textiles (Control) Amendment Order, 1972, Cotton Textiles Control of Movement Order, 1948, Cotton Textiles Control Order, 1948, Uttar Pradesh Coal Control Order, 1953

Summary: Textiles - Cotton Textiles (Control) Amendment Order, 1972 - Cotton Textiles Control of Movement Order, 1948 - Cotton Textiles Control Order, 1948 - Essential Commodities Act, 1955 - Essential Commodities Ordinance, 1955 - Essential Supplies (Temporary Powers) Act, 1946, s. 3 (1) (2) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 19(1)(f),(g) - Cotton Cloth and Yam Control Order, 1943 - Defence of India Rules, r. 81 - Whether cotton yarn is covered in cotton textile? - Held, the dictionary meaning of cotton textile are any material that is woven, a material, as a fibre or yarn, used in or suitable for weaving, woven or capable of being woven - The dictionary meanings show that cotton yarn is included in cotton textile - Cotton Cloth and Yam Control Order, 1943 was made in exercise of powers conferred by r. 81 of Defence of India Rules - The notification directed that no producer of yarn for civil consumption shall sell or deliver any such yarn produced by him except to such persons or persons and subject to such conditions as Textile Commr. might specify ss. 3(1) and (2) of 1946 Act empowered Central Govt. for maintaining or increasing supplies of essential commodities - 1955 Act contains power of Central Govt. to regulate or prohibit production, supply and distribution and trade and commerce in essential commodities - The validity of impugned orders is challenged under art. 19(1) (f) and (g) of Constitution on ground that it is a pre-emergency executive order which could have been challenged under art. 19(1) (f) and (g) before the proclamation of emergency - Appeals dismissed.
