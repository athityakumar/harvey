Lakshmi Kant Jha v Commissioner of Wealth Tax Bihar and Orissa

Supreme Court of India

16 April 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

Hans Raj Khanna, K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 101; (1974) 3 SCC 126; AIR 1973 SC 2258; 1973 (2) CTR 260; 1974 (2) ITJ 1; [1973] 90 ITR 97; [1973] 3 S.C.R. 973; 1973 TAXLR 955

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Wealth Tax Act, 1957, ss. 5(1)(viii), 5(1)(xv), 7(1), 2(e) and 4 - Exemption from wealth tax - Jewellery intended for personal use of assessee - Specially covered only by cl. (viii) of s. 5(1) - (B) Valuation of assets - Expenses in effecing sale of asset in open market - Deduction, if - (C) Assets, if - Compensation payable to assessee under Land Reforms Act - Exigibility is deferred - Held, it is exempted - Cannot be deducted - Compensation payable to assessee under Land Reforms Act even though its exigibility is deferred is an 'asset' in total wealth of assessee - Order accordingly.
