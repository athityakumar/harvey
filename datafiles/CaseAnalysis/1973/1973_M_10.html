Lt. Governor Of Delhi & Ors. v M/S. Ganesh Flour Mills Co. Ltd.

Supreme Court of India

09 January 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

Hans Raj Khanna, K.S. Hegde, P. Jaganmohan Reddy

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 128; (1973) 3 SCC 487; AIR 1973 SC 705; 1973 (2) CTR 145; [1973] 3 S.C.R. 211; 1973 (31) STC 354; 1973 TAXLR 1923; 1973 UJ 577

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Sales Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956 - Central Sales Tax(Registration and Turnover) Rules, 1957 s. 8(3), cl. (c) - Registered dealer - Amendment in registration certificate - Denial - Challenged - Respondent company was registered dealer under 1956 Act and was dealing in vegetable products - Respondent was granted certificate under 1957 Act and had applied for amendment of registration certificate to include tin plates or tin sheets u/s. 8(3) of 1957 Rules before Sales Tax Officer(STO) which was rejected - Respondent filed revision before Commissioner and further revision before Chief Commissioner which was dismissed - Aggrieved respondent filed writ before HC which was allowed and directed appellants to decide respondent's application for amendment of registration certificate - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, tin sheets and tin plates have to be subjected by the respondent to the process of cutting and moulding into tin containers would not take them out of the category of materials intended for being used for the packing of goods for sale.The cutting and moulding is essential for putting the tin sheets and tin plates into shape with a view to adapt them for actual user. The process of cutting and moulding does not alter the nature of the "materials intended for being used for packing of goods for sale"; it only facilitates the Actual user for packing. Thus, packing materials are necessary not only for solid articles but also for those in liquid and semiliquid form. It has further been observed that cans, both tin and aluminium, are, now used for ready-cooked products, brewery products, soft drinks, and many oils and semisolids. Where a reclosable pack is needed lever-lid or slip-lid cans are, used instead of sealed cans. As the tin sheets and tin plates purchased by the respondent were intended to be used for packing of vegetable products sold by the respondent, the respondent, was entitled to invoke the benefit u/s. 8(3) cl. (c) of 1957 Rules. Appeal dismissed.
