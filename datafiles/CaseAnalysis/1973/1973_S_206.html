Raja Jagadish Pratap Sahi v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

13 February 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Jaganmohan Reddy, Hans Raj Khanna, K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 248; (1973) 3 SCC 815; AIR 1973 SC 1059; 1973 (2) CTR 185; [1973] 88 ITR 443; [1973] 3 S.C.R. 528; 1973 TAXLR 898; 1973 UJ 616

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Uttar Pradesh Agricultural Income-tax Act, 1948, s. 32 (2) - Whether the mode of recovery of the arrears of tax or penalty due under the Act is possible only u/s. 32 of the Act or by a suit? - Once a notice of demand is served on the assessee for payment of tax due under the Act, and the assessee makes a default after the date for payment specified therein has expired, a debt is created in favour of the State - Held, once a debt is created, the State has the right to recover it by any of the modes open to it under the general law - In respect of mode of recovery no such prohibition is enacted in s. 32 - State can recover the debt by any other mode not provided in the referred Act but in accordance with the general law - Appeal dismissed.
