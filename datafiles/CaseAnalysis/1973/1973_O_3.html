Official Liquidator, Supreme Bank Ltd v P. A. Tendolkar (Dead) By L. Rs. And Ors

Supreme Court of India

19 January 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Hameedullah Beg, A.N. Grover, A.K. Mukherjea

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 337; (1973) 1 SCC 602; AIR 1973 SC 1104; [1973] 43 Comp Cas 382; 1973 (2) CTR 153; [1973] 3 S.C.R. 364

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Corporate; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Ex-Parte, Attachment Of Property

Summary: Companies Act, 1913 , s. 235 - Banking Regulation Act, 1949, s. 45-O - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Tort - Company under liquidation - Claim by liqudator - Misfeasance resulting from negligence of a Director - Is claim by liquidator is a claim by company and not barred u/s. 45-O of the 1949, Act - The applicability of maxim 'Actio personalis moritur cum persona' to case of breaches of fiduciary duties questioned - What is the effect of such misfeasence and what action can be taken against heirs of deceased Director - Held, claim is by th copany and s. 45-O confers new right of counting period of limitation - The maxim would not be applicable to actions based on contract or where a tort feasor's estate had benefited from a wrong done - Director liable as in case of fraud and only declaratory orders can be passed against heirs - Appeal No. 234 of 1967 Alowed ; Appeal no. 195-197, 300 Dismissed.
