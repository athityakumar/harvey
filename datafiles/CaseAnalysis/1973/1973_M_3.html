M. C. Chockalingam and Others v V. Manickavasagam and Others

Supreme Court of India

31 October 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

P.K. Goswami, P. Jaganmohan Reddy

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 298; (1974) 1 SCC 48; AIR 1974 SC 104; 1974 (2) MLJ 27; 1974 (2) MLJ(SC) 27; 1974 RCR(Rent) 242; [1974] 2 S.C.R. 143

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Rent Control

Summary: Land & Property - Rent Control - Transfer of Property Act, 1882 - Composite lease - Expiry - Notice - Renewal - Respondent obtained registered composite lease of cinema theatre for period of three years from absolute owners appellants/Lessors - Lease expired and notice was issued to respondent to deliver back possession - Respondent made application to Commissioner of Police renew license was allowed - Appellants appealed to Board of Revenue was allowed - Respondent filed application u/art.226 of Constitution in HC was dismissed - Respondent appealed to Division Bench was allowed - Court refused to grant leave to appeal - Hence instant S.L.P. - Whether respondent entitled to possession - Held, tenant on expiry of lease could not continue in lawful possession of property against wishes of landlord - Lawful possession could not be established without concomitant existence of lawful relationship between landlord and tenant - Lawful possession was not litigious possession and must have some foundation in legal right to possess property and it could not be equated with temporary right to enforce recovery of property - Possession of respondents on expiry of lease was not lawful possession within meaning of r.13 - Hence, HC was not correct in its interpretation of r.13 - Board of Revenue in appeal was, right in interfering with order of licensing authority - Single Bench of HC was rightly refused to interfere with order of Board u/art.226 of Constitution - There was no manifest error of law in the order of Board and there was no scope for interference by HC with tile order U/art.226 of Constitution - Decision of Division Bench of HC was set aside - Appeal allowed.
