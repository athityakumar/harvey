Santokh Singh v Izhar Hussain and Another

Supreme Court of India

25 April 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

I.D. Dua, Kuttyil Kurien Mathew

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 112; (1973) 2 SCC 406; (1973) SCC (Cr) 828; AIR 1973 SC 2190; 1973 CRLJ 1176; 1973 CrLR(SC) 473; [1974] 1 S.C.R. 78

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Octroi, Code of Criminal Procedure 1898

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 193, 195 and 211 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898, ss. 195(b), 195(3)and 439 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 9 - (A) Provision of s. 211 of IPC, 1860 - False charge of offence - (1) Statement on oath falsely supporting prosecution case - (2) Identification of accused at test parades - (B) Criminal Procedure - (1) Power of Court to direct prosecution for giving false evidence -Exercise of - (2) 'Subordination of Courts' - Court of Additional District Magistrate, if 'subordinate' to High Court within s. 195(3) of Code - (3) Revision - Order declining to direct prosecution for offence u/s 211, IPC, 1860 by ADM confirmed in appeal by Sessions Judge - HC directing filing of complaint - (A) Held, amounts to an offence u/ss 193 and 195 and u/s 211 - Does not amount to a 'false charge' within s. 211 - (B) Identification at test parades as not substantive evidence and it can only be used as corroborative of statement in court - Not 'subordinate' to HC within s. 195(3) - HC in revision, not justified in itself directing filing of complaint - Appeal allowed.
