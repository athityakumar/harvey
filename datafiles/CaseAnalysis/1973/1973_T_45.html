East India Hotels v Their Workmen and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 December 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Jaganmohan Reddy

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 72; (1974) SCC (L&S) 245; (1974) 3 SCC 712; AIR 1974 SC 696; 1974 LabIC 532; 1974 (1) LLJ 282; 1974 SLJ 175; 1974 UJ 45

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Standing Orders and Service rules of Hotel, cls. 17(2) and 17(19) - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 10 and 11 A - Dismissal - Domestic enquiry - Validity of - Whether dismissal of respondent is justified and to what relief if any, is he entitled? - Held, the Tribunal was in error in applying s. 11A of the Act - Because of the complaint, the enquiry, the report and the reference were all prior to the coming into operation of this section on 15-12-1971 - The Tribunal cannot, therefore, reappraise the evidence and arrive at a conclusion different from that arrived at by the domestic Tribunal - Even if no enquiry has been held by an employer or if the enquiry held by him is found to be defective, the Tribunal in order to satisfy itself about the legality and validity of the order, has to give an opportunity to the employer and employee to adduce evidence before it - No ground by which the enquiry is vitiated - In undoubted exercise of right of employer to take disciplined action and to decide upon quantum of punishment both of them are part of managerial functions - Enquiry and dismissal do not suffer from any defect and there is evidence by which dismissal of respondent is justified - Appeal allowed.
