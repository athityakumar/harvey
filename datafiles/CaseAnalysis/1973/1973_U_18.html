Patel India Private Limited v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

28 March 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Shelat, Y.V. Chandrachud

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 509; (1973) 1 SCC 745; AIR 1973 SC 1300; 1973 (2) CTR 242; 1990 (29) ECR 283; 1983 (13) E.L.T. 1495; [1973] 3 S.C.R. 811; 1973 TAXLR 2146

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Sea Customs Act, 1878, s. 40 - Refund of excess duty - Decision of Customs authorities levying excess duty against appellant company on part of imported items, refusing to treat invoice price as real value, reversed in revision by Govt., which directed re-assessment and refund of excess duty - Claim to refund of excess duty - Held, claim to refund of excess duty recovered by custom authorities on rest of items pending disposal of revision assessment of excess duty on those items was without authority of law - And s. 40 does not apply - Appellant entitled to refund - Appeal allowed.
