 Ramchandra Shankar Deodhar and Others v State of Maharashtra and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 November 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Bhagwati, A.N. Ray, D.G. Palekar, Y.V. Chandrachud, V.R. Krishna Iyer

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 210; (1974) SCC (L&S) 137; (1974) 1 SCC 317; AIR 1974 SC 259; 1991 (45) DLT 337; 1974 LabIC 165; 1974 (1) LLJ 221; [1974] 2 S.C.R. 216; 1974 (1) SLR 470

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 16, 32 and 309 - Government of India Act, 1935 - Police Act, 1861 - States Re-organisation Act, 1956, s. 115 - Whether Rules of 30-07-1959 which are admittedly statutory rules are unconstitutional? - Held, second proviso to r. 1 of Rules of 30-07-1959 is void as being violative of art. 16 of Constitution, 1950 and Procedure for Promotion to cadre of Deputy Collectors followed by State Govt. is invalid on ground that it denies equality of opportunity of promotion is therefore hit by art. 16 of Constitution, 1950 - (B) Whether seniority of petitioners vis-a-vis other Mamlatdars/ Tehsildars in recognised State of Bombay was liable to be determined according to principle laid down in Govt. Resolution D/- 21-11-1941? - Further held, inter se seniority of Tehsildars and Mamlatdars allocated from former States of Hyderabad, Madhya Pradesh, Bombay, Saurashtra and Kutch to reorganised States of Bombay as on 1-11-1956 would be governed by rr. 7, 8 and 9 of Rules of 1957, and neither Govt. Resolution D/- 21-11-1941 nor Govt. Resolution D/- 29-07-1963 would have any application - Petition disposed of.
