O. Rm. M. Sp. Sv. P. Panchanatham Chettiar v Commissioner of Income Tax, Madras

Supreme Court of India

06 March 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, P. Jaganmohan Reddy, Hans Raj Khanna

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 232; (1975) 4 SCC 834; AIR 1976 SC 909; [1975] 99 ITR 579

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Payment Of Interest, Legality

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 297(2)(i), 243 - Indian Income-tax Act, 1922, s. 66(7) - Payment of interest - Legality - Appellant (assesse) filed instant appeal - Whether assessee was entitled to claim interest on the refund which became due to him on the strength of decision of SC.

Held, assessment in instant case was completed long before 1961 Act came into force. Instant case clearly comes within the scope of s. 297(2)(i) of 1961 Act and not u/s. 66(7) of 1922 Act. Under the new Act, there is no provision for payment of interest unless the same comes within the scope of s. 243 of 1961 Act. It is not the case of the assessee that the facts of instant case come within the scope of s. 243 of 1961 Act. Appeal dismissed.
