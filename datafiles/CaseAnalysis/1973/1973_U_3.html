 Arati Ray Choudhury v Union Of India & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

11 October 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.V. Chandrachud, A.N. Ray, D.G. Palekar, P.N. Bhagwati, V.R. Krishna Iyer

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 141; (1974) SCC (L&S) 73; (1974) 1 SCC 87; AIR 1974 SC 532; 1974 LabIC 393; 1974 (1) LLJ 239; [1974] 2 S.C.R. 1; 1974 (1) SLR 659

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Panel

Summary: Constitution, arts. 14, 16, 32 and 226 - (A) Res judicata - Petition u/art. 226 not dismissed on merits - Whether petition u/art. 32 is barred by res judicata or any principles analogous to it - (B) Service Matter - Reservation of posts of SC and STs -Interpretation of 'Carry forward' rule as modified by Railway Board in 1964 - Constitutionality - Petition u/art. 32 held, is not barred by res judicata or by the application of any principles analogous to it - The carry forward Rule was not violative of arts. 14 and 16 - Rule Discharged.
