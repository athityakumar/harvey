State Of Uttar Pradesh & Anr v Anand Swarup

Supreme Court of India

06 November 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

P.K. Goswami, P. Jaganmohan Reddy, S.N. Dwivedi

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 124; (1974) 1 SCC 42; AIR 1974 SC 125; 1974 RCJ 96; 1973 RCR(Rent) 830; [1974] 2 S.C.R. 188; 1973 UJ 916

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Uttar Pradesh Government Premises (Rent recovery and Eviction) Act 1952

Summary: U.P. Government Premises (Rent Recovery and Eviction) Act, 1952, Pre., ss. 2(c), 4, 6 and 12 - Constitution, art. 136 - Defence of India Rules, 1939, r. 75A - Defence of India Act, 1939, s. 2(5) - Tenanacy - (A) Applicability of provisions of ss. 4, 6 and 12 - Premises let out before coming into force of the Act - (B)'Government Premises' - Premises requisitioned by District Magistrate - Effect of absence of notification containing requisition order of delegation - (C) Appeal by special leave - New plea - Regarding Decree of High Court - Held, arrears of rent and damages for unauthorised occupation can be recovered under the Act if conditions of ss. 4 and 6 are fulfilled - The premises requisitioned by the D.M. cannot be said to be 'Government premises' within s.2(c) of the Act and the Act will not apply to such premises and the Government will not be entitled to recover the arrears of rent as arrears of land Revenue - Respondent can support the decree of High Court on the ground not considered by it - Appeal Dismissed.
