Brij Kishore Prasad Singh and Others v Jaleshwar Prasad Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

02 March 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

Kuttyil Kurien Mathew, A.N. Grover, A.K. Mukherjea

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 79; (1973) 1 SCC 672; AIR 1973 SC 1130; [1973] 3 S.C.R. 562

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Keywords: Barred By Limitation, Suit for recovery of possession

Summary: Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 47 - Bihar Land Reforms Act, 1959, s. 5 - Suit for recovery of possession - Barred by limitation - Maintainability - Plaintiffs filed suit for recovery of possession of plaint property against defendants - Defendants alleged that 'K', while he was alive, executed a hukumnama in 1354 Fs. in their favour and they were put in possession of plaint property as lessees and so they were entitled to remain in possession - Trial Court found that K obtained possession of plaint property on basis of compromise, suit was not barred by limitation and decreed suit in favour of plaintiffs - Aggrieved, defendants filed appeal before Appellate Court, which was confirmed the order of Trial Court - Defendants filed appeal before HC - HC held that plaintiff should have paid stamps fee and got decree drawn up and executed it and so suit was barred by s. 47 CPC - HC, further held that since plaint property had vested under the 1959 Act, plaintiffs were not entitled to maintain the suit - HC allowed the appeal and dismissed suit - Aggrieved, plaintiffs preferred instant appeal - Whether the suit was barred by s. 47 CPC and whether under the provisions of the 1959 Act, plaint property had vested in Govt. and, plaintiffs were incompetent to maintain suit - Held, Trial Court found that definite case of defendants was that they were put in possession of property under hukumnama and, therefore, their possession was clearly that of lessees under an agricultural lease - Before compromise, K and defendants were in possession as tenants-in-common - Actual possession of defendants, of plaint property, was also constructive possession of K - So, when Appellate Court said that defendants continued in possession as before, it could only mean that after compromise defendants were in possession acknowledging title of K - Therefore, K was in constructive possession of property after compromise and, suit for recovery of khas possession was not barred by s. 47 CPC - HC dismissed suit was that plaint property had vested under the Act and plaintiffs had, no right to proceed with suit and obtain a decree for possession - However, suit was instituted on 7-3-1953, property vested in State on 26-1-1955, under the Act - There was no dispute that plaint property answers description of a homestead in s. 5 of the Act - HC was of view that K was not in possession at time property vested in State, he was not entitled to retain possession of plaint property u/s. 5 of the Act as a tenant under State free of rent - Constructive possession of K was sufficient to enable him to retain possession as a tenant under the section - On the date of vesting of property in Govt., K was, for purpose of s. 5 of the Act, in possession of plaint property - However, date of vesting of plaint property in State, K was in possession for purpose of s. 5 of the Act and that he became a tenant under State free of rent and that suit for recovery of possession was maintainable - Impugned order passed by HC was set aside and restored the order passed by Appellate Court - Appeal allowed.
