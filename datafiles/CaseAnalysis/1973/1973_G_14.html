Baburao Bagaji Karemore & Ors. v Govind & Others

Supreme Court of India

27 November 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Jaganmohan Reddy, P.K. Goswami

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 290; (1974) 3 SCC 719; AIR 1974 SC 405; [1974] 2 S.C.R. 429

Case Digest

Subject: Election; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Election Expenses, Statement Of Fact

Summary: Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 77, 78, 116A, 123(3), 123(4), 123(6) and rr. 86, 90 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 3 - Election petition - (A) Finding of fact and appreciation of evidence - Interference - (B) Corrupt practice - Burden of proof - Election of the returned candidate challenged - (C) Evidence Act, 1872, s. 3 - Appreciation of evidence - Witnesses - As to offending pamphlets, their interest - (D) Determination of corrupt practice u/s.123(6) r/w s. 77 and r. 90 of the Rules - (E) Compliance of ss. 77 and 78 r/ w r. 86 - (F) Onus - What petitioner has to prove - (G) Corroboration of evidence - Publication of a report of a meeting in a local newspaper - (H) Meaning of costs incurred u/s. 119 of the 1951 Act - Held, appellate Court should not lightly interfere with it merely because it takes a different view of the evidence - The onus on the petitioner who challenges the election on the ground is all the more heavy - Circumstances to be considered stated - On the facts of the case it was held that the candidate did not exceed the limit under the provision - Held, candidate is not obliged to file the account book unless the authenticity of the true copy of that account is challenged and notice has been given to produce the account book - Itself not sufficient to corroborate the testimony of the witnesses who depose that the impugned statement was made by a particular speaker at the meeting - The word 'incurred' in s.119 means actually spent. Thus when the election petition is dismissed u/s.98 (a), the responent will be entitled to the award of costs incurred including the counsel's fees provided he has already filed the fee Certificate of the counsel in the Court - Appeal dismissed.
