 State of Punjab and Another v Khan Chand

Supreme Court of India

17 December 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

Hans Raj Khanna, A.N. Ray, Kuttyil Kurien Mathew, P.N. Bhagwati, A. Alagiriswami

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 281; (1974) 1 SCC 549; AIR 1974 SC 543; [1974] 2 S.C.R. 768

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property

Keywords: Constitutionality, Bombay Tenancy and Agricultural Lands (Amendment) Act, 1956

Summary: Constitution - Land & Property - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - East Punjab Movable Property (Requisitioning) Act, 1947, s. 2 - Whether provisions of East Punjab Movable Property (Requisitioning) Act, 1947 violate art. 14 of Constitution and are therefore bad? - Act confers uncontrolled power on State Govt. or officers authorised by it to requisition any movable property - Only property excluded from purview of Act is one used for purpose of religious worship or an aircraft or anything forming part of an aircraft or connected with operation, repair or maintenance of aircraft - No guidelines have been laid down in Act regarding object or purpose, for which State Govt. or officers authorised by it may consider it necessary or expedient to requisition a movable property - It is not even requirement of Act that authority requisitioning movable property should specify in order purpose for which it has become necessary or expedient to requisition that property - There is no provision in Act that power of requisitioning movable property can be exercised under Act only for a public purpose nor is there any provision that powers under Act can be exercised only in an emergency or in some special contingency - It is open under provisions of Act for an officer authorised under Act to requisition movable property for any purpose whatsoever - Nothing in provisions of Act which makes it impermeable for a District Magistrate to requisition movable property for any purpose whatsoever for which he considers it necessary or expedient to do so - No suitable machinery is also provided in Act for determining compensation payable to owner of movable property nor does Act contains any guiding principles for determining amount of compensation - Held, provision of an enactment unconstitutional as it contravenes article of Constitution - Appeals dismissed.
