Sakal Deep Sahai Srivastava v Union Of India & Anr

Supreme Court of India

27 November 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Hameedullah Beg, Kuttyil Kurien Mathew

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 289; (1974) SCC (L&S) 158; (1974) 1 SCC 338; AIR 1974 SC 338; 1974 (28) FLR 144; 1974 LabIC 580; 1974 (1) LLJ 270; [1974] 2 S.C.R. 485; 1974 SLJ 123; 1974 (1) SLR 411; 1974 UJ 71

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Limitation Act, 1908, arts. 102 and 120 - Constitution, art. 141 - (A) Which provision of the 1908 Act is applicable to claim for arrears of salary - (B) Parliament did not alter the law at the time of its repeal - Rather enacting a new Act in its place with corresponding provision in it - Whether Supreme Court is bound by its own doubtful decision under a provision of an old Act - Held, claim for arrears of salary falls within the purview of art. 102 and not under the residue art. 120 - Held, yes and the correctness of the view therefore could not be re-examined by a Larger Bench - Appeal Partly Allowed.
