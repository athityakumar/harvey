 Pooran Mal Etc. v Director Of Inspection (Investigation) Of Income-Tax Mayur

Supreme Court of India

14 December 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

D.G. Palekar, A.N. Ray, Y.V. Chandrachud, A. Alagiriswami, P.N. Bhagwati

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 213; (1974) SCC (Tax) 114; (1974) 1 SCC 345; AIR 1974 SC 348; 1974 (3) CTR 25; 1974 (1) ITJ 406; [1974] 93 ITR 505; [1974] 2 S.C.R. 704; 1974 TAXLR 340

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Illegal Search

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14, 19(1)(f) and 19(g) - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 132 and 132A - Income Tax Rules, 1962, rr. 112 and 112A - Constitutional validity of s. 132(1) and (5) of 1961 Act and r. 112 A of 1962 Rules - Income Tax authorities made search u/s. 132 of the 1961 Act at the premises of the appellants and seized articles consisting of account books and documents, cash, jewellery and other valuables - Appellants filed writ petition challenging the validity of s. 132(1) and (5) of 1961 Act and also validity of r. 112 A of 1962 Rules on the ground that these provisions were violative of the fundamental rights guaranteed by arts 14, 19(1)(f)(g) and 31 of the Constitution - (A) Validity of s. 132(1) and (5) of 1961 Act and r. 112 A of 1962 Rules - Held, power to direct a search and seizure was given to the Director of Inspection or the Commissioner u/s. 132(1) of the Act - The authorisation for such search and seizure must be in favour of officers not below the grade of an Income-tax Officer - The power to authorise search and seizure can be exercised only when the Director of Inspection or the Commissioner has reason to believe (i) that in spite of the requisitions under the relevant provisions mentioned in s. 132(1)(a) of the 1961 Act the required books and documents have not been produced; (ii) that any person, whether requisition under the above provisions was made or not, will not, or would not, produce or cause to be produced, any books of account and other documents which will be useful for, or relevant to, any proceeding under the Income-tax Act; or (iii) that any person was in possession of any money, bullion, jewellery or any other valuable article or thing representing either wholly or partly undisclosed income or property - When the said authorisation was given by the Director of Inspection or the Commissioner, it must be limited to the five purposes mentioned in Sub-clauses (i) to (v) of Sub-section (1) of s. 132 of the 1961 Act - Rule 112 of the 1962 Rules, provides for the actual search and seizure being made after observing normal decencies of behaviour - The person in charge of the premises searched was immediately given a copy of the list of articles seized - Provision for the safe custody of the articles after seizure was also made in r.112 of the 1962 Rules - The safeguards were adequate to render the provisions of search and seizure as less onerous and restrictive as was possible under the circumstances - The provisions, therefore, relating to search and seizure in s. 132 of the 1961 Act and r. 12 of the Rules cannot be regarded as unconstitutional and violative of arts. 19(f) and (g) of the Constitution - (B) Whether s. 132(1) and (5) of the Act were violative of the fundamental right u/art. 14 of the Constitution? - Held, the provisions of s. 132(1) and (5) of the 1961 Act cannot be challenged on the ground that they make unjust discrimination between two sets of tax evaders in ordering search and seizure or retention of the seized wealth for recovering the tax, in some cases and not ordering the same in other cases - All evaders of taxes can be proceeded against u/s. 132 of the Act - Only in some cases, the search may be useful because of the information about the undisclosed income and wealth - Where there was no such information, search and seizure would be futile - Therefore, there was no substance in the contention that two different procedures for assessment were adopted and hence there was discrimination u/art. 14 of the Constitution - Petition dismissed.
