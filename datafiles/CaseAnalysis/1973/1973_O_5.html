Commissioner of Income Tax, Bombay v Onkarmal Meghraj (H. U. F.) (And Others Appeals)

Supreme Court of India

16 August 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

A. Alagiriswami, Hans Raj Khanna

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 242; (1974) 3 SCC 349; AIR 1973 SC 2585; 1973 (2) CTR 400; [1974] 93 ITR 233; [1974] 1 S.C.R. 391; 1973 TAXLR 1445

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Association Of Individuals

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1922, s.34 and 34(3), second Proviso - Interpretation of Statutes - (A) Escapement of assessment - Due to erroneous view taken by Income Tax Officer - Whether s.34(1)(a) or (b) applies? - (B) Construction of words 'any person' u/s 34(3), second Proviso - Partial partition of HUF - All members filing returns as individuals - One member filing appeal against order of assessment as he was assessed as HUF - Direction of appellate authority whether binding on other separated members - (C) Amendment in 1953 - Effect of amendment on Provision u/s 34(3), second proviso - Held, ss.34(1)(b), not (a) would apply - Further held, second proviso therefore, could be applied to appellant and not to others - Effect of amendment is not to enable ITO to take action u/s 34 where period mentioned therein has expired before 01-04-1952 - Appeal No. (other 9 Appeals ) dismissed; Appeal No. C A No 2264 Of 1969, 2268 Of 1969 and 2272 Of 1969 allowed.
