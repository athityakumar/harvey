Krishna Vithu Suroshe v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

30 August 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

Hans Raj Khanna, A. Alagiriswami

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 185; (1973) SCC (Cr) 969; (1974) 3 SCC 404; AIR 1974 SC 274; 1974 CRLJ 330; 1973 CrLR(SC) 626; [1974] 1 S.C.R. 567; 1973 UJ 837

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure 1898

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure,1898, s. 421 - Accused were convicted, by, Addl. Sessions Judge u/s 302 Indian Penal Code,1860 on allegation that they had caused death of one Bhaskar Narayan Suroshe - Each of them was sentenced to undergo imprisonment for life - Whether High COurt was justifies in dismissing appeal in limine? -U/s 421 CrPC, 1898, HC can dismiss an appeal in limine if on a perusal of petition of appeal and judgment appealed from it were to form view that there was no sufficient reason for its interference - It is now settled law, repeatedly laid down by this court in a series of decisions, that HC would not be justified in dismissing summarily and without a speaking order an appeal which raises arguable questions either on points of law or on points of fact - Held, appellant had an arguable case and HC was not justified in dismissing summarily appeal of appellant - Matter remitted to HC.
