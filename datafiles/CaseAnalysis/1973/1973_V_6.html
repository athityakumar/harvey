Hari Krishan Wattal v Vaikunth Nath Pandya

Supreme Court of India

18 July 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

D.G. Palekar, A. Alagiriswami

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 169; (1973) 2 SCC 510; AIR 1973 SC 2479; 1973 (2) CTR 391; [1974] 1 S.C.R. 259; 1973 UJ 760

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Arbitratin & ADR - Arbitration Act, 1940 - Enlargement of time for making award by consent of parties u/s 28(2) - Import and scope - Arbitration agreement itself need not embody stipulation that time can be extended by subsequent agreement - Arbitrator can enlarge time for making award if on initiation of arbitration, parties mutually agree to such enlargement - Said consent must be a post reference consent rendering provision in original agreement to that effect as null and void - Provision implies mutual consent for enlargement but such mutual consent initially expressed in original agreement does not save provision from being void - Matter remitted to High Court.
