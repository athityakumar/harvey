Inder Sain v State Of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

04 May 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

Kuttyil Kurien Mathew, I.D. Dua

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 322; (1973) 2 SCC 372; (1973) SCC (Cr) 813; (1973) SCC (Cr) 823; AIR 1973 SC 2309; 1973 CRLJ 1537; 1973 CrLR(SC) 536; [1974] 1 S.C.R. 215; 1973 UJ 694

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Health and Drugs - Opium Act, 1878, ss. 9(a) and 10 - Physical custody of opium - Meaning of the word possesses in s. 9(a) - Held, means having the knowledge of possession of opium - Accused in physical custody of opium - Failed to show that accused was not in the know about opium - Conviction proper - (B) Presumption - Shifting of burden of proof - Held, not necessary for the prosecution to establish conscious possession only show that the accused has dealt with the article, or has physical custody of the same or is directly concerned with it - Once it is so shown, the onus of proof is placed by virtue of s.10 of Opium Act, 1878,on the accused to prove by preponderance of probability that he did not knowingly possess the article -Appeal allowed.
