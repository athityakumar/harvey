 Purshottam Lal and Others v Union of India and Another

Supreme Court of India

21 February 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri, A.N. Ray, D.G. Palekar, S.N. Dwivedi, A.K. Mukherjea

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 11; (1973) SCC (L&S) 337; (1973) 1 SCC 651; AIR 1973 SC 1088; 1973 LabIC 604; 1973 (1) LLJ 407; 1973 SLJ 352; 1973 (1) SLR 633

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service

Summary: Service - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 16, 32 - Pay Commission report - Revision of pay scales - Entitlement of - Petitioners (employees) were research assistants and fall within class III of the non-Gazetted technical posts - Petitioners filed instant petition u/art. 32 of Constitution, complained that they were discriminated against by the Govt. in violation of their fundamental rights u/arts. 14 and 16 of the Constitution - Govt. contended that Pay Commission's Report did not deal with the case of petitioners.

Held, if any category of Govt. servants were excluded material should have been placed before Court. The Pay Commission has clearly stated that for the purposes of their enquiry they had taken all persons in the civil services of the Central Govt. or holding civil posts under that Govt. and paid out of the Consolidated Fund of India, to be Central Govt. employees. It is not denied by Govt. that the petitioners are paid out of the Consolidated Fund of India. Either the Govt. has made reference in respect of all Govt. employees or it has not. But if it has made a reference in respect of all Govt. employees and it accepts the recommendation it is bound to implement the recommendation in respect of all Govt. employees. If it does not implement the report regarding some employees only it commits a breach of arts. 14 and 16 of Constitution. This is what the Govt. has done as far as these petitioners are concerned. It is directed that the revised pay scales of the petitioners will have effect from 1-7-1959. Petitioners should be paid the amount payable to them as a consequence of the revision of the pay scales. Petition allowed.
