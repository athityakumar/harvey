Smt. Manibai and Another v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

10 August 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

Hans Raj Khanna, A. Alagiriswami

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 423; (1974) SCC (Cr) 236; (1974) 3 SCC 760; AIR 1974 SC 434; 1974 CRLJ 451; 1973 CRLR 584; 1973 UJ 788

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection; Health & Drug; Socio-Economic

Summary: Prevention of Food Adulteration Act, 1954, ss. 7(1) and 16(1)(a)(i) - Food - Adultration - Sale of adulterated item - (A) Sale of adulterated coconut oil at the shop - Determination of liability of co-licencee who is not in charge of nor actually conducting business at the shop - As licensee of shop or as partner of firm if business was owned by firm - (B) Prosecution - Necessity to show harmful effect of sold adulterated article on health - Held, not be liable for the sale u/s.17(1) - All that is required to be shown is that the article of food in question was adulterated - Appeal Allowed.
