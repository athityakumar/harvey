Krishnawati v Hans Raj

Supreme Court of India

29 November 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

D.G. Palekar, V.R. Krishna Iyer, R.S. Sarkaria

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 208; (1974) 1 SCC 289; AIR 1974 SC 280; 1975 RCJ 164; 1974 RCR(Rent) 163; [1974] 2 S.C.R. 524

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Criminal; Practice & Procedure; Rent Control

Summary: Tenancy - Sub-Let - Delhi Rent Control Act, 1958, ss. 14(4) and 39(2) - Landlord-respondent alleged that appellant had sub let premises - Appellant alleged that she and alleged sub-tenant were lawfully wedded husband and wife and they were running chemist shop - Whether on facts, a case of sub-letting is made out? - Held, no - Under sub-s. (4) of s. 14 premises could be deemed to have been sub-let by the tenant only when the Controller is satisfied that some person is let into possession ostensibly as a partner in business but really for the purposes of sub-letting - These provisions evidently have no application to the facts of the present case - Appellant and alleged sub-tenant to whom premises were let were living together as husband and wife to knowledge of respondent and right from time of letting, a chemists shop was run in premises - Further held, u/s. 39(2) of the Act the High Court could interfere in second appeal only if there was a substantial question of law - Question is not a substantial question of law - Rent Controller rightly held that appellant was lawfully wedded wife of sub-tenant and as such no question of sub-letting arises - HC wrongly disturbed findings of Rent Controller - Appeal allowed.
