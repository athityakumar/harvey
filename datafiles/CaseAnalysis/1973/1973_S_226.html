State of Haryana v Subash Chander Marwaha and Others

Supreme Court of India

02 May 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

D.G. Palekar, A. Alagiriswami

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 150; (1973) SCC (L&S) 488; (1974) 3 SCC 220; AIR 1973 SC 2216; 1973 LabIC 1212; 1973 (2) LLJ 266; [1974] 1 S.C.R. 165; 1973 SLJ 795; 1973 (2) SLR 137; 1973 UJ 723

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Government Servants-Punjab Civil service (Judicial Branch) , Service Rules (1951)

Summary: Service - Punjab Civil Services (Judicial Branch) Rules, 1961, rule 10(ii), Part C, rr.8 and 10 - Service Matter - (A) Appointment of Subordinate Judges - Whether existence of vacancies gives legal right to appointed post - (B) Whether Govt. can fix a higher score for selection than for eligibility - Held, does not give legal right to a candidate in select list to be appointed to post - Govt. is competent to fix a higher score for selection than one required for mere eligibility - Appeal allowed.
