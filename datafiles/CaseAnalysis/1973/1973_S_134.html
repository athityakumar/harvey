Bhagwan Tana Patil v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

09 October 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Sarkaria, Hans Raj Khanna

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 402; (1974) SCC (Cr) 11; (1974) 3 SCC 536; AIR 1974 SC 21; 1974 CRLJ 145

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 3(i) - CrPC, 1898, s. 367 - Appreciation of evidence - Evidence of prosecution witnesses - Fact that evidence not firm and safe against acquitted accused - Determination of its value against other accused - Applicability of maxim 'falsus in uno falsus in omnibus' - When thare is failure to explain injuries on person of accused - Held, mere fact that it is not firm and safe enough to be relied upon with regard to the part assigned to the acquitted accused in the occurrence, is no ground to reject it mechanically against the other accused also - Maxim is not to be blindly invoked - Appeal Dismissed.
