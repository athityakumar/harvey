Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Criminal Procedure Code, 1898 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Indian Penal Code, s. 302 r/w s. 34 - Murder - Conviction of - Held, acquitted accused should not be put in peril of conviction on appeal save where substantial and compelling grounds exist for such a course - There are no fetters on plenary power of Appellate Court to review whole evidence on which order of acquittal is founded and, it has a duty to scrutinise probative material de novo, informed, however, by weighty thought that rebuttable innocence, attributed to accused having been converted into an acquittal homage the Indian jurisprudence owes to individual liberty constrains higher court not to upset holding without very convincing reasons and comprehensive consideration - A balance has to be struck between chasing enhance possibilities as good enough to set delinquent free arid chopping logic of preponderant probability to, punish marginal innocents - Even if a witness is not reliable, he need not be false and even if Police have trumped up one witness or two or has embroidered story to give a credible look to their case that cannot defeat justice if there is clear and unimpeachable evidence making out guilt of accused - Accused, are reasonably proved to have murdered the deceased - Appeal dismissed.
