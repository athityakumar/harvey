R. Chandran v M. V. Marappan

Supreme Court of India

23 April 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

A. Alagiriswami, D.G. Palekar

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 450; (1973) 2 SCC 166; AIR 1973 SC 2362; 1974 (1) MLJ(SC) 130; [1974] 1 S.C.R. 46; 1973 UJ 659

Case Digest

Subject: Election; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Age limit, Panchayat election

Summary: Municipalities & Local Governments - Election - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 227, 326 - Madras Panchayats Act, 1958, ss. 20, 20(5) - Panchayat election - Age limit - Legality - Appellant secured 1256 votes as against 1015 secured by respondent in village panchayat elections and appellant was declared elected - Respondent filed an election petition Election Tribunal - Election Tribunal dismissed petition and held that it was not established that appellant was below 21 years of age - On revision, HC held that age of the appellant was not above 21 and was below 19 years - Hence, instant appeal - Whether once a person's name was found in the electoral roll of village panchayat it was open to Election Tribunal or any other authority to question the fact that appellant was above the age of 21.

Held, in regard to elections to village panchayats either of members or of the president there is no constitutional provision laying down any age limit. Art. 326 of Constitution, which lays down the principle of adult suffrage, lays down that all persons over the age of 21 shall be entitled to vote. But that is because the article specifically says so. That apart, the State Legislature is fully competent to legislate in respect of qualifications of voters and candidates for election to various local bodies in the State and there is no constitutional limitation on them so as to make adult suffrage a requisite for a valid provision of law. S. 20(5) of the Act itself does not lay down the qualification for a 'Voter', it only adopts the qualification laid down for persons to be included in the electoral roll of the Legislative Assembly constituency of which that village may be a portion. It follows, therefore, that all decisions of this Court holding that when once a person's name has been included in the electrol roll, his right to vote cannot be questioned would be applicable in interpreting s. 20 of the Act. once a name is found in the electoral roll its inclusion could not be questioned in any election petition must be followed. In instant case, therefore, it was not open either for the Election Tribunal or for the HC to go into the question regarding the appellant's age. Appeal allowed.
