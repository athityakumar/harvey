Shew Kissen Bhattar v Commissioner of Income Tax, Calcutta

Supreme Court of India

05 March 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, P. Jaganmohan Reddy, Hans Raj Khanna

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 118; (1973) 4 SCC 115; (1973) SCC (Tax) 401; AIR 1973 SC 2348; 1973 (2) CTR 293; 1974 (2) ITJ 203; [1973] 89 ITR 61; [1973] 3 S.C.R. 567; 1973 TAXLR 1286

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922 s. 9(1)(iv) - Principal amount- Interest paid - Claimed deduction - Denied - Challenged - Assessee claimed deduction of Rs. 38,221 towards interest payable on amount due by him for A/Y 1956-57 which was denied and ITO gave deduction of Rs. 18,000 only - Assessee filed appeal against this order before Appellate Assistant Commissioner and Tribunal which was dismissed - aggrieved assessee filed appeal before HC which was answered in favour of department - Hence, instant appeal - Whether assessee was entitled to claim deduction u/s. 9(1)(iv) of the Act.

Held, when interest payable is not paid, same became a part of the principal and thereafter, interest has to be paid not, only on the original principal but also on that part of the interest which had become a part of the principal. It cannot be said that the interest which became a part of the principal can be considered as the capital charge. What assessee is entitled to deduct is the interest payable by him on the capital charge and not the additional interest which because of his failure to pay the interest on the due date had been considered as a part of the loan. In fact, the real capital charge is that which was originally due. The other portion is merely an interest on which the assessee has agreed to pay interest. Hence, we are unable to accept the connection of the assessee that the interest is an interest paid on the capital charge. The compound interest is payable not on the capital charge but on that part of the interest on which he has agreed to pay interest. That is not the capital taken note of u/s. 9(1)(iv) of the Act. Thus, interpretation placed by the High Court is the correct interpretation and these appeals fail and they are dismissed. Appeals dismissed.
