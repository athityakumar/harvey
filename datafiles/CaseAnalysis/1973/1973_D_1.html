 Ramanlal Bhogilal Shah and Another v D. K. Guha and Others

Supreme Court of India

24 January 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri, A.N. Ray, D.G. Palekar, M. Hameedullah Beg, S.N. Dwivedi

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 78; (1973) SCC (Cr) 583; (1973) 1 SCC 696; AIR 1973 SC 1196; 1973 CRLJ 921; 1973 CrLR(SC) 454; [1973] 3 S.C.R. 438

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Keywords: Bombay Prohibition Act 1949

Summary: Constitution - Criminal - Constitution of India, 1950, art.20(3) - Whether a person against whom F.I.R in respect of an offence is lodged falls within expression 'person accused of an offence' under art.20(3) of Constitution? - Held, yes a 'person accused of an offence' refers to a person against whom an F.I.R is lodged alleging offences inter alia under Foreign Exchange Regulation Act. A person is entitled to protection under art.20 (3) and cannot be compelled to be a witness against himself - However, person cannot deny to give information regarding matters which do not tend to incriminate him in proceedings against him - Petition allowed.
