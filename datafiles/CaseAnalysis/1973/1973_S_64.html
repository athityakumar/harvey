Kali Ram v State Of Himachal Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

24 September 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

Hans Raj Khanna, A. Alagiriswami, R.S. Sarkaria

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 307; (1973) 2 SCC 808; (1973) SCC (Cr) 1048; AIR 1973 SC 2773; 1974 CRLJ 1; 1973 CrLR(SC) 705; [1974] 1 S.C.R. 722

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - CrPC, 1898, s.162 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 114 - Constitution, art. 136 - (A) Scope and applicability of s.162 - What is 'Statement made to a police officer' - (B) Findings based on appreciation of evidence - Interference with - Permissibility - (C) Criminal trial - Proof of guilt - Principles - (A) Held, restriction on the use of statement made during the course of investigation is in general terms relates to all statements made during the course of an investigation - Nothing in the section to show that the investigation must relate to any particular accused - Letter addressed by the constable to Station House Officer was in the nature of narration of what he had been told by the accused and constitute statement for the purpose of s.162 - (B) not permissible unless valid grounds are there to disturb them - (C) Principles indicated - Appeal allowed.
