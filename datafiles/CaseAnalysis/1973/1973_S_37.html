 Krishan Gopal v Shri Prakash Chandra & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

08 November 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

Hans Raj Khanna, A.N. Ray, Kuttyil Kurien Mathew, A. Alagiriswami, P.N. Bhagwati

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 296; (1974) 1 SCC 128; AIR 1974 SC 209; [1974] 2 S.C.R. 206

Case Digest

Subject: Election; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Constitution, arts. 216, 224A and 224 - Representation of the People Act, 1951, s. 80A - (A) Judge appointed u/s. 224-A - Jurisdiction to try election petitions - (B) Election - Powers of the Chief Justice - Held, can exercise the jurisdiction to try an election petition u/s. 80-A of the Act as such person sitting and acting as a Judge of the High Court u/art. 224-A, is a Judge of HC for the purpose of s. 80-A (2) of 1951 Act - Chief Justice can, after entrusting an election petition to one Judge, allocate it to another Judge - Appeal allowed.
