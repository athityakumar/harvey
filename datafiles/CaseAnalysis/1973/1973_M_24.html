Workmen v Management of Dunlop Rubber Company of India Limited

Supreme Court of India

04 May 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

C.A. Vaidyialingam, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 159; (1973) 2 SCC 492; (1973) SCC (L&S) 496; AIR 1973 SC 2394; 1973 (27) FLR 37; 1973 LabIC 1458; 1973 (2) LLJ 321; [1974] 1 S.C.R. 228

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Rehabilitation, Available Surplus

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Payment of Bonus Act, 1965, ss. 5 and 6 - Whether deductions of following is allowed while computation of available surplus - (A) Permissible deductions from gross profits - (B) Return on share premium - (C) Donations - (D) Return on reserves used as working capital - Permissibility - Held, commission received by Indian subsidiary Co. from parent Co. incorporated in a foreign country on sales effected by latter through Indian High Commission located there or on orders received by it directly from Indian importers as well as royalties received out of sales made by parent Co. to foreign countries can be deducted while computing available surplus - Return on share premium can be deducted - Donations cannot be deducted - Where claim rest merely on statement of profit and loss examined, its Deputy Accountant and Chaetered Accountant(auditor) and not cross-examined by union on those matters, return claimed could be validly allowed to be deducted - Appeal dismissed.
