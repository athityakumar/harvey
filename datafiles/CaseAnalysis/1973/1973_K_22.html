Katikara Chintamani Dora & Ors. v Guntreddi Annamnaidu & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

11 December 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Sarkaria, D.G. Palekar, V.R. Krishna Iyer

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 198; (1974) 1 SCC 567; AIR 1974 SC 1069; [1974] 2 S.C.R. 655

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Madras Estates (Abolition and Conversion into Ryotwari) Act, 1948, s. 2(7), 9(1), 9(2), 9-A - Estates Land Act, 1908, s. 3(2)(a) - Tenure of land - Consent decree - Legality - Settlement Officer (SO) made an enquiry as to whether village 'K' was an 'estate' or not within contemplation of s. 9(2) of 1948 Act and by an order held that it was not an 'inam estate' within meaning of s. 2(7) of 1948 Act - SO recorded a finding that village 'K' became an estate by virtue of 1936 Act - Aggrieved appellants filed a suit before Subordinate Judge against State Govt. for declaration that 'K' village was not an estate, which was decreed - Aggrieved State filed an appeal before HC, which was dismissed and attained finality - Thereafter, appellants filed a suit before Subordinate Court, against respondents for recovery of rent and damages in respect of their lands situated at 'K' village, which was disposed of in view of compromise that both parties filed a joint memo stating that they were abide by final decision in appeal or revision arising out of earlier suit filed by appellant - However, appeal pending against earlier suit was dismissed by HC by impugned judgment holding that Civil Court was not the forum for suit as framed by appellants and questions raised in suit including claim for arrears of rent or damages were outside Jurisdiction of Civil Court - Aggrieved appellant filed instant appeal - Whether impugned judgment passed by HC was justified - Held, there could be no doubt that as soon as Court accepted the compromise agreement between parties and passed a decree in terms thereof, to the extent of matter covered by it, was complete - Nothing further remained to be done by parties in pursuance of that agreement - Decree had become absolute and immediately executable on when HC in appeal finally decided that 'K' village was not an estate - Once it was held that determination of fact was not a matter of exclusive jurisdiction of Settlement Officer, appellants could not be debarred on the basis of any doctrine, of res-judicata from getting matter fully and finally adjudicated by a Court of competent jurisdiction - It was clear that under the law in force at material time, a suit for a declaration that decision of SO/Tribunal holding certain properties to be an 'estate' u/s. 3(2)(a) of the 1908 Act was void, was maintainable on the ground that suit property was not an 'inam village' - Thus, determination in appeal that 'K' village was not an 'estate' became as much binding on respondents - S. 9-A of 1948 Act inserted by Amending Act 20 of 1960 took in its retrospective sweep only those decisions of SO or Tribunal which at the commencement of the Amending Act 20 of 1960 were subsisting and had not been totally vacated or rendered non-est by a decree of a competent Court - Decision of SO in instant case, was not such a decision - It had ceased to exist as a result of the inter-linked decree in earlier suit and instant, passed before the enactment of Amending Act - Amending Act of 1960, thus, did not in anyway, affect the finality or the binding effect of those decrees - State had contested issue regarding 'K' village being an estate or not, right up to HC - It would, thus, operate as res judicata between State and land-owners - Same binding effect was produced by estoppel raised by consent decree in suit out of which instant appeal was aroused - Impugned judgment of HC was reversed and remanded case back to it for decision on remaining issues - Appeal disposed of.
