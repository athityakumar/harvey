Shamsher Singh v Rajinder Prashad and Others

Supreme Court of India

03 August 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

A. Alagiriswami, D.G. Palekar

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 320; (1973) 2 SCC 524; AIR 1973 SC 2384; [1974] 1 S.C.R. 322; 1973 UJ 784

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, ss. 2(2), 96, 100 and O. 7, r. 11, Court-fees Act, 1870, s. 7(iv)(c), sch II, art. 17 - (A) Constitution - Appeal by special leave - Competency - (B) Hindu Joint Family - Mortgage by father - Decree against father - Suit by son though couched in declaratory form in substance for setting aside decree or for consequential relief of injunction -fee payable is ad valorem u/s 7(iv)(c) of 1870 Act - (A) Held, order rejecting a plaint under O. 7, r. 11, CPC, 1908 for non-payment of additional court-fee demanded is appealable as a decree and when order is reversed in appeal by High Court a second appeal would lie u/s 100 CPC, 1908 on ground that decision of first appellate court on interpretation of s. 7(iv)(c) is a question of law - Furthermore, in a case in which Supreme Court has granted special leave question whether an appeal lies or not does not arise - (B) Court-fee payable is ad valorem u/s 7(iv)(c) - Appeal allowed.
