Commercial Tax Officer, Bangalore v Sri Venkateswara Oil Mills Limited and Another

Supreme Court of India

16 March 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, Hans Raj Khanna, P. Jaganmohan Reddy

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 526; (1974) 3 SCC 5; AIR 1973 SC 1325; 1973 (2) CTR 346; [1973] 3 S.C.R. 742; 1973 (32) STC 660; 1973 TAXLR 2150

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Sales Tax - Central Sales Tax (Amendment) Act, 1969,s. 10(1) - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956,s. 6 - Mysore Sales Tax Rules 1957,r. 38 - Whether decision of High Court holding that Sales Tax Officer had no jurisdiction to reassess assessee and he could not take any assistance from r. 38 of Mysore Sales Tax Rules, 1957, is correct? - Held, it is well settled that if a subsequent legislation is given retrospective effect and is deemed to have been in force at time when order to be rectified was made then law to be applied is amended law - HC had accepted this principal but it proceeded to rule that for finding out whether there was a mistake apparent on record or not, it is not permissible for Sales Tax Officer to take any evidence whatsoever as mistake to be rectified must be apparent on record - On that premise it held that because it is not permissible for assessee to adduce additional evidence to show that they have not collected tax, it is not open to assessing authorities to reopen assessments - This approach is neither logical nor sound in law s. 10 of Amendment Act mitigates rigor of amendment made to s. 6 of Principal Act - But for s. 10 of Amendment Act, every dealer would have had to pay tax on turnovers in question whether he had collected tax or not - If impact of s. 10 is ignored, as HC has done, then assessments in question are liable to be opened whether assessees had collected tax or not - Assessees cannot have benefits of s. 10(1), but not burden of proof placed on them u/s. 10(2) - R. 38 of Mysore Sales Tax Rules must be r/w s. 10 of Amendment Act - If so read, it is clear that assessing authorities before reassessing dealers should afford them reasonable opportunity to satisfy them that they have not collected tax - Appeals allowed.
