A. C. Sharma v Delhi Administration

Supreme Court of India

05 February 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

I.D. Dua, A. Alagiriswami, C.A. Vaidyialingam

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 334; (1973) SCC (Cr) 608; (1973) 1 SCC 726; AIR 1973 SC 913; 1973 CRLJ 902; 1973 CRLR 292; [1973] 3 S.C.R. 477; 1973 (1) SLR 779

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947, s. 5 A - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898, s. 537 - Corruption - Interpretation of Statutes - Delhi Special Police Establishment - Jurisdiction to investigate offence of bribery and corruption - Irregularity or illegality in investigation, effect - Held, legislature cannot be presumed to have intended to make any substantial alteration in existing law beyond what it expressly declares - Jurisdiction not exclusive - It does not by itself affect legality of trial by an otherwise competent Court unless miscarriage of justice has been caused thereby - Appeal dismissed.
