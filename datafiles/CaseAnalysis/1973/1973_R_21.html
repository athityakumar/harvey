Hindustan Lever Ltd. v Ram Mohan Ray & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

07 March 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

A. Alagiriswami, I.D. Dua, C.A. Vaidyialingam

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 330; (1973) 4 SCC 141; (1973) SCC (L&S) 309; AIR 1973 SC 1156; 1973 (26) FLR 408; 1973 LabIC 784; 1973 (1) LLJ 427; [1973] 3 S.C.R. 624

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Rationalisation, Standardisation

Summary: Labour and Industrial Laws - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, sch.4, items 8, 10, 11, ss. 9A, 33A, 33C, 2(oo) - (A) Applicability of sch.4 item 10 - (B) Applicability of sch 4 item 11- (C) Proof of condition of service - (D) Whether non-payment of wages amounts to alteration in condition of service - Held, retrenchment of workmen is essential - Workmen must be adversely affected - Whether any particular practice or allowance or concession had become a condition of service would always depend upon the facts and circumstances of each case - Amounts to alteration in condition of service - Appeals dismissed.
