Ram Jag And Others v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

21 December 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.V. Chandrachud, M. Hameedullah Beg

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 220; (1974) 4 SCC 201; (1974) SCC (Cr) 370; AIR 1974 SC 606; 1974 CRLJ 479; 1974 CrLR(SC) 116; [1974] 3 S.C.R. 9

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure; Tort

Summary: Constitution, art. 136 - CrPC, 1898, s. 154 - (A) Appeal against conviction by High Court -If and when reappraisal of evidence is permissible - (B) Practice and Procedure - Effect of delay in filing FIR - Held, it is implicit that if two views of the evidence are reasonably possible, the finding of acquittal ought not to be disturbed but on reappraisal of evidence, conviction was set aside and if after applying these principles, not by their mechanical recitation in the judgment, the High Court has reached the conclusion that the order of acquittal ought to be reversed, SC will not reappraise evidence in appeal u/art. 136 of Constitution - Throw a cloud of suspicion on the seeds of the prosecution case, depending upon a variety of factors - Appeal Allowed
