 E. P. Royappa v State of Tamil Nadu and Another

Supreme Court of India

23 November 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Ray, D.G. Palekar, V.R. Krishna Iyer, Y.V. Chandrachud, P.N. Bhagwati

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 66; (1974) 4 SCC 3; (1974) SCC (L&S) 165; AIR 1974 SC 555; 1974 LabIC 427; 1974 (1) LLJ 272; [1974] 2 S.C.R. 348; 1974 (1) SLR 497

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 32, 14, 16 and 314 - Indian Administrative Service (Pay) Rules, 1964, sch. III-A, rr. 9, 9(1) and 9(4) - Indian Administrative Service (Cadre) Rules, 1964, rr. 4 and 4(2) - Fundamental Rule of Indian Civil Service, rr. 56(f), 86(c), 13(d), 14, 56, 9(19), 86 and 86(a) - Tamil Nadu General Sales Tax Act - General Sales Tax Act, 1959 - (A) Whether appointment of petitioner to substantive post of Chief Secy. to State of Tamil Nadu is valid? - (B)Whether ambit and reach of arts. 14 and 16 are limited in cases where public servant's right has been affected to a post? - Held, neither post of Deputy Chairman, Planning Commission nor post of officer on Special Duty was a cadre post within meaning of r. 4 of Indian Administrative Service (Cadre) Rules, 1964 - R. 4(2) of Cadre Rules does not confer any power on State Govt. to alter strength and composition of cadre - Post of Deputy Chairman, Planning Commission as well as post of officer on Special Duty was not equivalent in status and responsibility to post of Chief Secy. to Govt. within meaning of r. 9(1) of Indian Administrative Service (Pay) Rules, 1964 - Fundamental r. 14 provides that no Govt. servant can be transferred substantively to or appointed to officiate in a post carrying less pay than pay of permanent post on which he holds a lien or will hold - Posts of Chief Secy. to Govt, Addl. Chief Secy. to Govt. and First Member, Board of Revenue should be in same category and interchangeable, it is necessary that there should be same pay for all three posts and State Govt. - Public servant can complain of violation of arts. 14 and 16 if he has been arbitrarily or unfairly treated or subjected to mala fide exercise of power by state machine - Petition dismissed.
