Rev. Fr. K. C. Alexander v State Of Kerala

Supreme Court of India

16 August 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Jaganmohan Reddy, S.N. Dwivedi

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 545; (1973) 2 SCC 737; AIR 1973 SC 2498; [1974] 1 S.C.R. 399

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Improvements, Travancore Land Conservancy Act, 1091, Land Assignment Act

Summary: (A)Land & Property - Conservancy Act 4 of 1091, s.9 - Dispossession from land and improvements - Inclusion of trees - Notice of forfeiture - Appellant was wrongfully dispossessed from disputed land along with improvements which were effected by him and State appropriated improvements without any right - Appellant filed suit for value of improvements as damages and filed suit for recovery of amount - Trial Court held that no notice of forfeiture of trees could be given u/s.9 of the Act and therefore no compensation or damages were payable for improvements and dismissed suit - On appeal, HC upheld order of Trial Court and dismissed appeal - Hence, instant Appeal- Whether trees are included within meaning of s.9 of the Act so as to entitle appellant to notice of forfeiture.

Held, s.9 of the Act provides for two notices to be given one notice is to be given to person who is in unauthorised occupation of Govt. land to vacate land within reasonable time and other notice is to forfeit any crop or other product raised on land or to remove any building or other structure erected or anything deposited therein within reasonable time as may be stated in notice. Words 'raised on land' qualify both 'crop' and 'other product', so words 'other product' have to be read in context of word 'crop' which precedes it. No doubt one of meanings given in Black's Law Dictionary does say that in more restricted sense word is synonymous with 'fructus industrials'. Term includes 'fructus industrials' and having regard to etymology of word it has been held to mean only products after they have been severed from soil. Even under definition of product it cannot mean anything which is attached to land like trees, it may, however, include fruit of trees. Thus, trees are not included within meaning of 'other products raised on, land' in s.9 of the Act and there is no obligation on Govt. to give notice of forfeiture u/s.9 of the Act. Appeal dismissed.

(B)Land & Property - Conservancy Act 4 of 1091, ss.5,6 - Damages for improvements - Entitled to claim - Whether appellant bonafide or not, is entitled to compensation or damages for improvements made by him on land.

Held, appellant was not bonafide trespasser and no authority has been cited to prove proposition that appellant nevertheless bonafide trespasser if he enters upon land with bona fide intention of improving land and if same is accepted, it would give validity to dangerous principle which will condone all acts of deliberate and wrongful trespass. However, Govt. had resolved to acquire disputed land and other lands and by arrangement with certain of sutidars it took possession of such land, including portion which was in occupation of appellant. Govt. was in position, by law at any rate, to regularize their possession by such notification fact which becomes material when it has to be considered what nature of trespass is. Further, it cannot be said that mere trespasser who has deliberately and wrongfully contrary to provisions of s.5 of the Act, entered 'upon another's land which makes such act even punishable u/s.6 of the Act, is entitled to compensation for trees planted by him on land. Thus, appellant deliberately entered upon lands knowing full well that he had no right, claim or title to lands and has been rightly evicted as trespasser, he cannot be entitled to compensation or damages for improvements made by him on land. Appeal dismissed.
