Commissioner of Income Tax, Madras v Madurai Mills Company Limited

Supreme Court of India

09 March 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

Hans Raj Khanna, K.S. Hegde, P. Jaganmohan Reddy

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 239; (1973) 4 SCC 194; (1973) SCC (Tax) 425; AIR 1973 SC 1357; 1973 (2) CTR 223; 1973 (2) ITJ 174; [1973] 89 ITR 45; 1973 (2) MLJ(SC) 40; [1973] 3 S.C.R. 662; 1973 TAXLR 680

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Association Of Persons, Excess Profits Tax, Dissolution Of Firm, Computation of Capital, Computation of Capital Income, Computation of Capital Reserves, Legality, Liquidation of company, Capital gain, A/y 1961-62, Transaction involved no sale

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 12B, 12B(2), 66(1) - A/y 1961-62 - Liquidation of company - Transaction involved no sale - Capital gain - Legality - Appellant (Revenue) filed by instant appeal against the judgment of HC whereby Court answered the question 'whether on the facts and circumstances of case, Tribunal was right in holding that the sum of Rs.95,944/- was liable to tax u/s. 12B(2) of the Act', referred to it u/s. 66(1) of the Act, in the negative in favour of the assessee (respondent) of A/y 1961-62.

Held, when a shareholder receives money representing his share on distribution of the net assets of the company in liquidation, he receives that money in satisfaction of the right which belonged to him by virtue of his holding the shares and not by operation of any transaction which amounts to sale, exchange, relinquishment or transfer. In the circumstances, we find it difficult to hold that the assessee company is liable to pay tax on capital gains as contemplated by s. 12B of the Act in respect of the amount of Rs.95,944. Distribution of the assets of the companies in liquidation does not amount to a transaction of sale, exchange, relinquishment or transfer so as to attract s. 12B of the Act. Appeal dismissed.
