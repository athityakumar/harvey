Controller of Estate Duty, Andhra Pradesh v Kancharla Kesava Rao

Supreme Court of India

04 April 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, Hans Raj Khanna

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 258; (1973) 2 SCC 384; AIR 1973 SC 2484; 1973 (2) CTR 337; [1973] 89 ITR 261; [1973] 3 S.C.R. 897; 1973 TAXLR 1314

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Family Property

Summary: Land and Property - Income Tax and Direct Taxes - Estate Duty Act, 1953, s. 24 - meaning of word 'disposition' - Partition deed of Joint Hindu Family - Widow gave up her right to her husband's share in the non-agricultural properties and right to be maintained from out of the family income - Other members of the family allotted certain acres of agricultural land to her (life interest ) - Same was to devolve on other members of the family as per stipulations in the deed of partition - Held, there was no disposition in favour of the widow within s. 24 under the deed - Held, it was only an adjustment of rights - Appeal allowed.
