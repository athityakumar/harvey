Santokh Singh v Delhi Administration

Supreme Court of India

20 February 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

I.D. Dua, A. Alagiriswami, C.A. Vaidyialingam

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 82; (1973) SCC (Cr) 577; (1973) 1 SCC 659; AIR 1973 SC 1091; 1973 CRLJ 846; 1973 CrLR(SC) 256; [1973] 3 S.C.R. 533

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Freedom Of Speech And Expression

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Punjab Security of State Act, 1953, s. 9 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 19(1)(a), 79(2) - Public meeting - Framing of charges - Legality - Appellant/accused, who addressed a public meeting of employees of Defence dept. had incited said employees to commit offences prejudicial to security of State - Magistrate framed charge against appellant punishable u/s. 9 of the Act - Aggrieved, appellant filed revision petition before HC - Single Judge dismissed revision petition - Hence, instant appeal - Whether impugned orders of Courts below were sustainable - Held, fundamental right guaranteed by art. 19(1)(a) of Constitution and interest of public protected by art. 79(2) of Constitution should be properly adjusted and reasonable balance struck between two - S. 9 of the Act was within legislative competence of State Legislature and it would be for Court in which appellant was tried to decide as to how far appellant's speech was covered by that section - Impugned judgment of HC did not show any serious legal infirmity resulting in failure of justice which should induce SC to interfere u/art. 136 of Constitution - However, after leave scope of appeal was not enlarged and even at that stage appellant could not as of right claim adjudication on merits 'if SC feels that there was no grave injustice done to appellant as a result of any serious legal, infirmity. We are unable find any such infirmity in the impugned judgment - Hence, Magistrate trying appellant's case would deal with all points raised before him oil the merits without being influenced by tentative view expressed by HC which appellant himself invited - Appeal dismissed.
