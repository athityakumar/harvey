 Fagu Shaw and Others v State of West Bengal

Supreme Court of India

20 December 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

Kuttyil Kurien Mathew, A.N. Ray, Y.V. Chandrachud, A. Alagiriswami, P.N. Bhagwati

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 481; (1974) 4 SCC 152; (1974) SCC (Cr) 316; AIR 1974 SC 613; 1974 CRLJ 486; 1974 CrLR(SC) 154; [1974] 2 S.C.R. 832

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Constitution, arts. 14, 22(4)(a), Proviso and 22(7)(b) - Maintenance of Internal Security Act, 1971, s. 13 - Detention - (A) Whether s.13 of 1971 Act has fixed 'maximum period' - (B) Power to determine maximum period of detention - If discriminatory - (C) Purpose of art. 22(4)(a) - To put curb on power by providing that no law shall authorize the detention of a person - Parliament, if bound to fix maximum period - Held, period fixed in s.13 of the 1971 Act is not 'the maximum period' as visualized by art. 22(7)(b) - Power of Govt. to determine the period of detention cannot be said to be discriminatory or arbitrary - Parliament not bound to fix maximum period in order that Proviso to art. 22 (4) (a) may operate - Petition Allowed.
