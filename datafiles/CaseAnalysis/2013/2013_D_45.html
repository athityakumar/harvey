Geetaben Ratilal Patel v District Primary Education Officer

Supreme Court of India

02 July 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 389; (2013) 7 SCC 182; AIR 2013 SC 3092; 2013 (5) AWC 4886; 2013 (5) Bom.C.R. 37; JT 2013 (9) SC 158; 2013 (6) MahLJ 88; 2013 (6) MLJ 73; 2013 (4) MPLJ 312; 2013(8) SCALE 171; 2013 (4) SLR 511; 2013 (3) UPLBEC 2147

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service

Keywords: Primary Education Act, Gujarat Panchayat Services (Discipline and Appeal) Rules, 1997

Summary: Service - Constitution - Persons with Disabilities (Equal Opportunities, Protection of Rights and Full Participation) Act, 1995, ss. 47, 47(1), 62, 64 - Termination - Challenged - Appellant, Primary Teacher, proceeded on medical leave from 21-6-1999 to 30-7-1999, and remained on leave upto 7-9-1999 - Thereafter, from 15-9-1999, appellant again remained absent unauthorisedly without producing any kind of leave report - Notice was issued to the appellant - Neither written nor oral explanation was received by respondent - Appellant was dismissed from service by respondent on the ground of carelessness towards duty, absence from duty, irregularity, breach of orders of the higher authorities and having badly affected the future of the children - For about three years, no action was taken by appellant - In the year 2007 appellant filed an application before the Commissioner u/s. 62 of the Act taking plea that the order of dismissal passed by the authorities while she was suffering from mental illness was in violation of s. 47(1) of the Act - Appellant requested for her reinstatement with full back-wages - Commissioner held that as appellant was suffering from 40 to 70% mental disability at the time of dismissal, the said order of dismissal was void - It was also held that if appellant was not in a position to work in the large educational interest of the students then an appropriate post should be created for her and her appointment to that post be made as per s. 47 of the Act - On appeal, Single Judge set aside the order passed by the Commission - DB dismissed appeal filed against said order - Hence, instant appeal - Whether the Commissioner u/s. 62 of the Act could look into the legality of the order of dismissal from service of a disabled person, if it comes to his notice that the said person with disabilities has been deprived of his rights -

Held, provisions of ss. 47 and 62 of the Act, empower the Commissioner, to look into the complaint with respect to the matters relating to deprivation of rights of persons with disabilities and non-implementation of laws, rules, bye-laws, regulations, executive orders, guidelines or instructions issued by the appropriate Governments or local authorities and to take up the matter with the appropriate authorities for the welfare and protection of rights of persons with disabilities including matter relating to dispensation with service or reduction in rank - Power of the Commissioner 'to look into the complaints with respect to the matters relating to deprivation of rights' as provided u/s. 62 of the Act was not an empty formality and the Commissioner was required to apply his mind on the question raised by the complainant to find out the truth behind the complaint - In instant case, Government Medical Board also held the appellant mentally disabled as she was suffering from 40 to 70% mental disability - Order of dismissal was passed during her mental disability in violation of s. 47(1) of the Act - In this background, the Commissioner having declared the order of dismissal as void, it was not open to the HC to interfere with such order and to restore the illegal order of dismissal - Moreover, show cause notices were issued to the appellant and charges were framed but there was nothing on the record to suggest that any departmental proceeding was initiated - Neither any inquiry officer was appointed, nor any notice was issued by any inquiry officer to the appellant to remain present in the departmental proceeding - No evidence was relied upon by respondent to bring home the charges - Aforesaid facts also show that the order of dismissal was passed in violation of rules of natural justice - Appeal allowed.
