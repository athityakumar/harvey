Ashrafi and others v State of Haryana and others

Supreme Court of India

11 April 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, J. Chelameswar

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 232; (2013) 5 SCC 527; AIR 2013 SC 3654; 2013 (99) ALR 45; 2013 (2) CLT(SC) 288; 2013 (4) CTC 228; JT 2013 (5) SC 643; 2013 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 2117; 2013 (2) RCR(Civil) 856; 2014 (124) RD 557; 2013(5) SCALE 686; [2013] 6 S.C.R. 148

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property

Keywords: Acquisition of lands

Summary: Land & Property - Administrative - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4 and 18 - Acquisition of lands - Compensation - Enhancement of compensation - Entitled to - State Govt. acquired lands of appellants/land owners measuring 184.66 acres for development of sector 45 - Notification was published u/s. 4 of the Act on 2-8-1989 - Land Acquisition Collector awarded compensation at rate of Rs. 3,50,000/- per acre for chahi lands and Rs.1,50,000/- per acre for other lands - Appellants filed reference to District Judge u/s. 18 of the Act, which was fixed the compensation at Rs.45/- per sq. yard - Appellants filed appeal before HC, which was fixed the compensation at Rs.220/- per sq. yard - Aggrieved, appellants filed instant appeals - Whether HC was justified in granting the compensation at Rs.220/- per sq. yard - Held, while deciding valuation of lands, HC applied a cut of 60% and also took into consideration that lands in question were small plots, value whereof was definitely higher than lands which were acquired which were much larger in area - HC was justified in taking into consideration size of plots, which were exhibited for purposes of comparison with size of plots acquired, but it could not be uphold the cut of 60%, which was imposed by HC, since acquired lands were already within developed municipal limits - In instant cases also, a cut of one-third value should be appropriate as in other cases - However, it was modified the valuation arrived at by HC upon imposing a cut of 60% and directed that amount of compensation be re-assessed upon imposing a cut of 331/3 per cent while re-assessing value of land - General cut imposed was at a flat rate of 40%, which was not warranted on account of fact that lands in question had lost their character and potentiality as agricultural lands and have more or less converted into lands which were ready for use for purpose of construction - However, a deduction of 331/3 per cent should be reasonable on account of passage of time and all round development in area which had made it impossible for lands to retain their original character - It was directed that wherever a deduction of 40% was made, same should be altered to 331/3 per cent and compensation awarded was modified - Resorting to belting system by HC was improper and that at best a standard cut of 1/3rd should sufficient to balance smallness - Appeals disposed of.
