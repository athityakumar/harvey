Erach Boman Khavar v Tukaram Shridhar Bhat and another

Supreme Court of India

12 December 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 825; (2013) 15 SCC 655; AIR 2014 SC 544; 2014 (1) Bom.C.R. 624; 2014 (1) CLT(SC) 125; [2014] 182 Comp Cas 84; 2014 (1) CompLJ 1; 2014 (1) RCR(Rent) 90; 2013(15) SCALE 224

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Keywords: Possession

Summary: Rent Control - Bombay Rent Act, 1947, s. 3(1)(b) - Companies (Court) Rules, 1959, r. 117 - Subletting - Eviction - Possession - Entitled to - Appellant/land lord entered into rental agreement with respondent no. 2/Company in respect of flat owned by him - A suit for eviction was instituted by appellant before Trial Court against respondent no. 2 and sub-tenant - Respondent no.1, owner of the company, filed application for impleading himself as party in suit contending that he was sub-tenant and same was allowed - When suit was pending, in a separate proceeding Company Judge passed winding up order against respondent no. 2 - Appellant filed application for granting leave to file eviction suit against respondent - Company Judge dismissed - Appellant filed second application wherein Company Judge granted liberty to file fresh suit - Respondent challenged said order before DB - DB held that second application was not maintainable - Hence, instant appeal - Held, SC were disposed to think, has erroneously opined that Company Judge in main part of order having rejected application could not have granted liberty to apply for filing of another application - Application for grant of leave was really not dealt with on merits and on basis of statement of respondent no. 3, Company Judge opined that it was not necessary for present and in that context liberty was granted - Principles stated in Arjun Singh v Mohindra Kumar and Others 1963 Indlaw SC 362, Satyadhyan Ghosal And Others v Sm. Deorajin Debi And Another.1960 Indlaw SC 413 and other authorities clearly spell out that principle of res judicata operates at successive stages in same litigation but, basic foundation of res judicata rests on delineation of merits and it has at least an expression of an opinion for rejection of an application - As it was evident, there was no advertence on merits and further Company Judge has guardedly stated two facets, namely, 'not necessary to grant present Judge's Summons' and 'liberty to applicant to apply, if necessary' - That same could not have been treated to have operated as res judicata as has been held by DB - Irresistible conclusion was that DB has fallen into serious error in dislodging order granting leave by Company Judge to file fresh suit - Order passed by DB was set aside and restore that of Company Judge - Appeal allowed.
