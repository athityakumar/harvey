Rumi Bora Dutta and another v State Of Assam

Supreme Court of India

24 May 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 349; (2013) 7 SCC 417; AIR 2013 SC 2422; 2013 CRLJ 3260; JT 2013 (9) SC 296; 2013(7) SCALE 535; [2013] 3 S.C.R. 801

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 r/w s. 34 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellants (wife and nephew of deceased) allegedly committed offences punishable u/ss. 34 and 302 IPC - Trial Court convicted the appellants u/s. 302 r/w s. 34 IPC and sentenced to undergo rigorous imprisonment of life - Appellants preferred appeal before HC, which affirmed the conviction and sentences - Hence, instant appeal - Whether HC and Trial Court were justified in convicting the appellants - Held, both appellants had led to discovery of knife and skipping rope used in crime - It was within their special knowledge - Medical evidence corroborated fact that deceased died because of strangulation and further there was a stab injury on his chest - Thus, weapon and other articles had direct nexus with injuries found in post mortem report - Prosecution witnesses had clearly deposed that deceased was lying on bed and they were told about arrival of miscreants and causing injury - It was also brought in evidence that apart from appellants old mother of deceased was in house - Trial Court as well as HC had rightly disbelieved attack by any miscreant - All probabilities thought to be covered by appellants gradually melted and their complicity in crime and criminality of mind stood revealed - Prosecution had proved guilty of appellants and circumstances were conclusive in nature to exclude every hypothesis but one proposed to be proved - Trial Court and HC were justified in convicting the appellants - Impugned orders passed by Trial Court and HC were affirmed - Appeal dismissed.
