State of Haryana v Janak Singh and Etc.

Supreme Court of India

10 May 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjana Prakash Desai, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 716; (2013) 9 SCC 431; AIR 2013 SC 3246; 2013 CRLJ 3317; JT 2013 (9) SC 51; 2013 (3) MLJ(Crl) 45; 2013(9) SCALE 697; [2013] 5 S.C.R. 1144

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 482 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 376, 506 and 511 - Reduction in sentence - Justifiability - Sessions Judge convicted respondent 'X' for offence punishable u/s. 376 of the IPC and sentenced him to undergo rigorous imprisonment (RI) for eight years and fine and he was also convicted u/s. 506 of the IPC - Respondent 'Y' was convicted u/s. 376 r/w s. 511 of the IPC and sentenced to undergo RI for four years and fine and he was also convicted u/s. 506 of the IPC - On appeal, HC confirmed conviction of respondents but reduced their sentence to the sentence already undergone by them - Hence, instant appeal - Whether reduction of sentence by HC was justified - Held, when the legislature provide for a minimum sentence and made it clear that for any reduction from the minimum sentence of seven years, adequate and special reasons have to be assigned in the judgment, the courts should strictly abide by this legislative command - While imposing sentence on persons convicted of rape, the court should be careful and should not overlook requirement of assigning reasons for imposing sentence below the prescribed minimum sentence - Reasons should contain extenuating circumstances which prompted the HC to reduce the sentence below the prescribed minimum - Further, sentence bargaining was impermissible in a serious offence like rape and such a course would be against the mandate of s. 376(1) of the IPC - In the instant case, once the respondents stated that they did not want to press the appeals and the HC was convinced that conviction should follow, then, it could not have reduced the sentence to the sentence already undergone by the respondents which was below the minimum prescribed by law - Impugned judgment was legally unsustainable and was liable to be set aside - Matter remanded to the HC for fresh disposal of the appeals filed by the respondents - Appeals partly allowed.
