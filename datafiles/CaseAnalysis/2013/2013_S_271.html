Sasidhar Reddy Sura v State of Andhra Pradesh and others

Supreme Court of India

05 December 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 799; (2014) 2 SCC 158; AIR 2014 SC 444; 2014 (1) ALD(SC) 122; 2014 (3) ALT(SC) 12; 2014 (140) FLR 396; JT 2014 (1) SC 215; 2014 (1) MLJ 250; 2014 (1) OLR 411; 2013(14) SCALE 593; [2013] 12 S.C.R. 985; 2014 (1) SLR 775; 2014 (2) WLN(SC) 125

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Post in question

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Practice & Procedure - Andhra Pradesh State Judicial Service Rules, 2007 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 233 - Appointment - Post in question - Justifiability - Respondent-HC invited applications for appointment to 18 posts of District and Sessions Judges (Entry Level) in State judicial service - Appellant applied for said post - Appellant was not appointed to post of District and Sessions Judge - Appellant filed petition before HC - HC dismissed petition - Hence instant appeal - Whether order recorded by HC was just and proper - Held, it did not say anything with regard to minimum age of candidate to be selected to post in question whereas Commission had expressed its view in its report that only after completion of 35 years of age person should be appointed as District and Sessions Judge but said recommendation had not been incorporated in Rules framed by HC for giving appointment to the post in question - Appellant, who had not completed 35 years of age at relevant time could not have been denied appointment to post in question simply because of his being under age as per recommendations of Commission especially when there was no provision in Rules that candidate should have completed 35 years of age for being appointed to post of District and Sessions Judge - Hence, HC was in error while giving undue weightage to recommendations made by Commission, especially when Rules did not provide for any minimum age for appointment to post in question - Further, art. 233 of the Constitution was also silent about minimum age for being appointed as District Judge - Therefore, impugned judgment was quashed and it was directed that appellant should be appointed to post in question with effect from date on which he ought to have been appointed, however, he should not be paid salary for period during which he had not worked as District and Sessions Judge - Appellant should be placed at appropriate place in seniority list of District Judges after considering his position in merit list - Appeal disposed of.
