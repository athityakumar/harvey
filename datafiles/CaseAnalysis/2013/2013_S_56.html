Satya Nand Munjal v Commissioner of Gift Tax

Supreme Court of India

22 January 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Madan B. Lokur, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 429; (2013) 14 SCC 360; JT 2013 (2) SC 125; 2013 (2) RCR(Civil) 448; [2013] 1 S.C.R. 492; [2013] 213 TAXMAN 437

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Revocable Transfer, Gift Tax Rules, 1958

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Gift Tax Act, 1958, s. 4(1)(c) - A/ys 1982-83, 1989-90 - Revocable transfer - Reassessment - Validity - Assessee was owner of 6000 fully paid up equity shares executed a deed of revocable transfer in favour of transferee for A/ys 1982-83 and 1989-90 - AO passed a reassessment order and held that transferee did not become owner of gifted shares until transfer was an irrevocable transfer - On appeal, Commissioner(Appeals) dismissed appeal and held that since there was no regular transfer of bonus shares transferee could not claim any ownership of shares and upheld reassessment order - On further appeal, Tribunal allowed appeal and quashed gift tax reassessment - Revenue preferred an appeal before HC - HC allowed appeal and set aside impugned order of Tribunal holding that assessee was liable to gift tax on value of bonus shares which were a gift made by assessee to transferee - Hence, instant appeals - Whether there was any perversity or illegality in impugned judgment and order of HC - Held, assessee had made a valid revocable gift of 6000 equity shares to transferee which was determined by HC - However, HC had not considered interpretation of s. 4(1)(c) of the Act - matter was remanded back to HC for de novo consideration - Impugned judgment and order of HC was set aside - Appeals allowed.
