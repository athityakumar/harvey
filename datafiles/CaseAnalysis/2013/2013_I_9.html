Swastik Gases Private Limited v Indian Oil Corporation Limited

Supreme Court of India

03 July 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, Madan B. Lokur, Kurian Joseph

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 414; (2013) 9 SCC 32; 2013(5) ALL MR 885; 2013 (4) AWC 4236; 2013 (3) CLT(SC) 154; 2013 (5) CTC 527; JT 2013 (10) SC 35; 2013 (171) PLR 789; 2014 (1) RCR(Civil) 52; 2013(8) SCALE 433; [2013] 7 S.C.R. 581

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Solicitor

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Contract & Commercial - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, ss. 2(e) and 11 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s.20(c) - Contract Act, 1872, s.23 - Dispute - Arbitral award - Sustainability - Whether, in view of cl.18 of the consignment agency agreement, the HC had exclusive jurisdiction in respect of the application made by the appellant u/s.11 of 1996 Act - Held, appellant did not dispute that part of cause of action has arisen in Kolkata - What appellant said was that part of cause of action had also arisen in Jaipur and, therefore, Chief Justice of that HC or the designate Judge had jurisdiction to consider the application made by the appellant for the appointment of an arbitrator u/s.11 of Act - Having regard to ss.11(12)(b) and 2(e) of 1996 Act r/w. s.20(c) of CPC, there remained no doubt that the Chief Justice or the designate Judge of HC had jurisdiction in the matter - Question was, whether parties by virtue of cl.18 of the agreement had agreed to exclude the jurisdiction of the courts at Jaipur or, in other words, whether in view of cl.18 of the agreement, the jurisdiction of Chief Justice of the HC had been excluded - HC had to see the effect of the jurisdiction clause in the agreement which provided that the agreement should be subject to jurisdiction of the courts at Kolkata - It was a fact that whilst providing for jurisdiction clause in the agreement the words like 'alone', 'only', 'exclusive' or 'exclusive jurisdiction' had not been used but this, was not decisive and did not make any material difference - Intention of the parties - by having cl. 18 in the agreement was clear and unambiguous that the courts at Kolkata should have jurisdiction which meant that the courts at Kolkata alone should have jurisdiction - It was so because for construction of jurisdiction clause, like cl.18 in the agreement, the maxim expressio unius est exclusio alterius came into play as there was nothing to indicate to the contrary - Legal maxim meant that expression of one was the exclusion of another - By making a provision that the agreement was subject to the jurisdiction of the courts at Kolkata, the parties had impliedly excluded the jurisdiction of other courts - Where the contract specified the jurisdiction of the courts at a particular place and such courts had jurisdiction to deal with the matter, HC thought that an inference may be drawn that parties intended to exclude all other courts - Cl. like cl.18 of agreement was not hit by s.23 of the 1872 Act at all - Such clause was neither forbidden by law nor it was against the public policy - It did not offend s.28 of 1872 Act in any manner - Appeal dismissed.
