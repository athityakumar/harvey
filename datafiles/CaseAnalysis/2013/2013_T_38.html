The Union Of India & Ors. v Anil Kumar Sarkar

Supreme Court of India

15 March 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, J. S. KHEHAR

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 153; (2013) 4 SCC 161; AIR 2013 SC 1661; 2013(2) ALL MR 954; 2013 (137) FLR 641; JT 2013 (4) SC 103; 2013 (3) MLJ 828; 2013(3) SCALE 542; [2013] 2 S.C.R. 396; 2013 (3) SLR 357

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Corruption charges

Summary: Service - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, s. 13(2) - Indian Penal Code, 1860, 420,120-B - Promotion - Corruption charges - Rejected - Validity - Respondent was appointed as Junior Clerk and was promoted to various post and was working as Senior AFA/ T-1 in office of the Financial Adviser and Chief Accounts Officer of Northeast Frontier (N.F.) - Departmental Promotion Committee was convened by UPSC to consider eligible Group 'B' officers for their substantive promotion to Group A of IRAS against vacancies - In DPC respondents name was considered, but since there were several corruption charges and FIR's pending against respondent due to which respondent was not promoted and his batch mates were promoted - Respondent made several representation but was rejected - Aggrieved respondent filed application before Tribunal, which dismissed his application - Appeal before HC, was allowed directing appellants to issue appropriate order for promotion with all consequential benefits - Hence instant appeal - Appellants contended that 4 charge sheet were issued against respondent and enquires were completed and notices to show cause had already been served upon respondent - Appellant pleaded that when his juniors were promoted he was not under suspension or any charge sheet was served upon him and he was not facing any criminal prosecution - Whether HC, was justified in directing appellants to promote respondents - Held, promotion etc. could not be withheld merely because some disciplinary/criminal proceedings were pending against employee, to deny said benefit, they must be at relevant time pending at stage when charge-memo/charge-sheet had already been issued to employee - In light of fact that no charge sheet was served on respondent-employee when DPC met to consider his promotion, yet sealed cover procedure was adopted - In such circumstances, SC, held that Tribunal had rightly directed authorities to open sealed cover and if respondent was found fit for promotion by DPC, to give him promotion from date of his immediate junior was promoted pursuant to order, hence no reason to interfere with order - Appeal dismissed.
