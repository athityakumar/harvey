State Bank of India v Gracure Pharmaceuticals Limited

Supreme Court of India

22 November 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Radhakrishnan, A.K. Sikri

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 770; (2014) 3 SCC 595; AIR 2014 SC 731; 2014 (2) ALD(SC) 113; 2014 (102) ALR 223; 2014 (1) ALT(SC) 25; 2014 (1) AWC 915; 2013 (4) CLT(SC) 301; 2013 (6) CTC 789; 2014 (4) MahLJ 20; 2013 (8) MLJ 535; 2014 (2) MPLJ 696; 2014 (1) OLR 446; 2014 (1) RCR(Civil) 889; 2014 (122) RD 280; 2014 (2) RLW 1765; 2013(14) SCALE 392; [2013] 12 S.C.R. 617

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Civil Procedure

Keywords: Letter Of Credit

Summary: (1) Code of Civil Procedure, 1908-Order II, Rule 2 and Order VII, Rule 11-Two suits filed by plaintiff (respondent), one in original side of High Court and another before district court-Applicability of Order II, Rule 2-Cause of action in both suits was same-Relief sought for in subsequent suit could have been claimed by plaintiff in earlier suit filed before High Court-When earlier suit for recovery of dues was filed for alleged relief, damages sought for in subsequent suit could have also been sought for-Facts on basis of which subsequent suit was filed, existed on date on which earlier suit was filed-No fresh cause of action arose in between earlier suit and subsequent suit-Plaintiff omitted certain reliefs which were available to it at time of filing of first suit and after having relinquished the same, it cannot file separate suit in view of provisions of Order II, Rule 2 (2)-Plaintiff not entitled to split cause of action into parts by filing separate suits-Subsequent suit filed before district court barred under Order II, Rule 2-High Court committed error in reversing order of district court, allowing application under Order VII, Rule 11-Impugned judgment of High Court-Set aside.(2) Code of Civil Procedure, 1908-Order II, Rule 2-Object of Order II, Rule 2-Is to avoid multiplicity of proceedings and not to vex parties over and again in litigative process-Object enunciated in Order II, Rule 2-Is laudable and it has larger public purpose to achieve by not burdening court with repeated suits.
