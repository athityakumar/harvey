Ni Pra Channabasava Deshikendra Swamigalu Matadhipathigalu Kannada Mutt v C.P. Kaveeramma And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

06 May 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 292; (2013) 16 SCC 565; JT 2013 (8) SC 227; 2013(6) SCALE 628; [2013] 8 S.C.R. 816

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Karnataka Certain Inams Abolition Act, 1977, Karnataka Village Offices Abolition Act, 1961

Summary: Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Karnataka Certain Inams Abolition Act, 1977, ss. 3 (2), 4 (2) (b), 10 - Karnataka Land Reforms Act, 1961, ss. 41 (2), 41, 126, 130 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 43 - Karnataka Village Offices Abolition Act, 1961, s. 8 - Karnataka Land Revenue Act, 1964 - Abolition of Inams - Grant of occupancy rights - Sustainability - By mortgage deed dt.1-3-1955, predecessor of 'M' stated to have mortgaged possession of 175.60 acres out of 197 acres of lands in favour of predecessor-in-title of respondents - By yet another mortgage deed dt.5-4-1967, a second mortgage deed in favour of very same parties in respect of 17 acres of land was stated to have been made - Mortgages were made for a period of 99 years - By virtue of s. 4 (2) (b) of 1977 Act all Inams that were existing on that date stood abolished w.e.f.1-3-1974, namely, effective date - After coming into force of 1977 Act, Mutt filed application on 25-6-1987 for grant of occupancy rights in respect of entire lands - By order dt.11-2-1993, appellant's application was allowed by Tribunal - Respondents, based on rights flowing from mortgage deeds, also claimed occupancy rights on ground that they were tenants of land in question - Their application was rejected - There was a challenge at instance of respondents to grant of occupancy rights in favour of appellant, as well as, rejection of their rights in W.P.No.6379 of 1993 - Writ petition was allowed by Single Judge and grant of occupancy rights in favour of appellant was set aside - Appeal filed by appellant was allowed and order of Single Judge was set aside and order of Tribunal granting occupancy rights in favour of appellant was restored - Appellant approached Tehsildar alleging that respondents were in unauthorised possession and having regard to coming into force of 1977 Act - Tehsildar allowed appellant's application and directed eviction of respondents - Aggrieved by said order, respondents filed writ petitions - Single Judge among other issues, considered question about jurisdiction of Tehsildar and held that Tribunal under 1964 Act had jurisdiction over lands in question and that Tehsildar was entitled to consider application of appellant filed in Form No.5 - Therefore, Single Judge set aside that part of order and remitted matter back for passing fresh orders - However, DB proceeded to hold that Tehsildar lacked jurisdiction since rights of respondents as mortgagee, prescribed a different status for respondents and consequently invocation of s. 41 or s. 130 of 1961 Act, could not have been invoked - Hence instant appeal - Whether order passed by Single Judge was justified - Held, power of Tehsildar to invoke s. 130 of 1961 Act for granting relief could not be held to be not sustainable - When once instant Court held that s. 43 of 1882 Act could not come to the aid of respondents, any right based on mortgages of year 1955 and 1967 no longer survived for consideration - Since s. 10 of the 1977 Act was also held to be not applicable to the case of respondents, only other question which could have been examined in all probabilities was one relating to manner in which earlier order came to be passed by Tehsildar, which was found to be not properly done as held by Single Judge in order dt.1-2-2005 in Writ Petitions, which disclosed that there were certain serious irregularities or malpractices in passing of order by Tehsildar dt.28-8-2004 and consequential Mahazar dt.31-8-2004 - In fact, Single Judge rightly set aside above orders of Tehsildar on that ground and remitted matter back to Tehsildar for passing fresh orders in accordance with law - SC also hold that Tehsildar, had every jurisdiction to deal with application of appellant for working out its remedy based on grant of occupancy rights in its favour in proceeding dt.11-2-1993, which had become final and conclusive - Since it was extensively pointed out by Single Judge in order dt.1-2-2005, as well as in impugned order highlighting malpractices indulged in by Tehsildar, while passing order directing possession in favour of appellant and while upholding the order of Single Judge referred to above, order of remand passed by Single Judge was restored - Appeals allowed.
