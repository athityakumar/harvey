Haryana Wakf Board v Mahesh Kumar

Supreme Court of India

21 November 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 765; AIR 2014 SC 501; 2014 (102) ALR 261; 2014 (2) AWC 1148; 2014 (1) RCR(Civil) 311; 2014 (122) RD 382; 2013(14) SCALE 199; [2013] 12 S.C.R. 596; 2014 (1) WLN(SC) 32

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Muslim Wakfs Act, 1954, Haryana Wakf Act, 1995, Wakf Act, 1985

Summary: Wakf Property-Suit for possession of property-Suit by Wakf Board-Question involved whether suit property is wakf property or not-Present suit instituted in year 2000, i.e., after Haryana Wakf Act, 1985 came into force-Wherever there is dispute regarding nature of property, namely whether suit property is wakf property or not-It is Tribunal constituted under Wakf Act, which has exclusive jurisdiction to decide same-If suit or proceeding already pending in civil court before commencement of Wakf Act in question-Then such proceedings before civil court would continue-And Tribunal would not have any jurisdiction-Return of plaint under Order VII of Rule 10, C.P.C. for want of jurisdiction of civil court-Justified-Appellate court taken right view in the matter-No fault with view taken by High Court in impugned judgment.
