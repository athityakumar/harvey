State Of Uttarakhand v Yogendra Nath Arora & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

18 March 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Chandramauli Kumar Prasad, V. Gopala Gowda

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 156; (2013) 14 SCC 299; AIR 2013 SC 1489; JT 2013 (4) SC 123; 2013 (2) MLJ(Crl) 628; 2013 (3) RCR(Criminal) 892; 2013(4) SCALE 531; [2013] 5 S.C.R. 233

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Bribery

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 7, 13(1)(d), 13(2), 19(1)(c) - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Illegal gratification - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant/accused was working as Deputy General Manager, allegedly accepting an illegal gratification of Rs.30,000 from complainant - Trial Court convicted accused guilty of offence punishable u/s. 7 r/w ss. 13(2), 13(1)(d) - On appeal, HC held that accused being an employee of an undertaking of State Govt. and having been repatriated to his parent dept., it was State Govt. which was competent to remove him and to grant necessary sanction - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order of HC was sustainable - Held, power to remove accused from service was with State Govt. and if contention that power to repatriate would mean power to remove from service did not find favour, it should be State Govt. which would be competent to grant sanction - However, office means a position which requires person holding it to perform certain duties and discharge certain obligations and removal from his office would mean to snap that permanently - By repatriation, person holding office on deputation might not be required to perform that duty and discharge obligation of that office, but nonetheless he continues to hold office and by virtue thereof performs certain other duties and discharge certain other obligations - Therefore, power to repatriate did not embrace within itself power of removal from office as envisaged u/s. 19(1)(c) of the Act - V.K. Sharma v. State (Delhi Admn.), 1975 Indlaw SC 155, relied on - Hence, in instant case with regard to merit of request made by State Govt. it should be decided by State Govt. on its own merit in accordance with law - Appeal dismissed.
