Sahib Hussain @ Sahib Jan v State of Rajasthan

Supreme Court of India

18 April 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, M.Y. Eqbal

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 256; (2013) 9 SCC 778; 2013 CRLJ 2359; 2013 (3) RCR(Criminal) 159; 2013(6) SCALE 219; [2013] 2 S.C.R. 1019

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Jail Manual

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 316, 366 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant (accused) allegedly committed offence u/s. 302 of IPC - Trial Court by its judgment convicted appellant and sentenced him to death - Thereafter Trial Court made death reference to HC u/s. 366 of CrPC for confirmation of death sentence - Aggrieved appellant filed an appeal before HC - HC by impugned judgment disposed of death reference and appeal filed by appellant by commuting the sentence of death to imprisonment for life and that appellant should not be released from prison unless he serve out at least 20 years of imprisonment - Aggrieved appellant filed instant appeals - Appellant contended that there was no direct eye witness to speak about incident and case of prosecution entirely rests upon circumstantial evidence - Whether impugned judgment passed by HC was justified - Held, in instant incident 5 persons were died and as per post mortem reports, deaths were due to multiple injuries on various parts of bodies - Even in absence of eye-witness to incident, if various circumstances prove that appellant was responsible and involved in gruesome murders, decision of Court based on such circumstances could not be faulted with - Perusal of entire evidence of PW1 (informant) and PW4 (to whom appellant mentioned the details of incident), though they did not witness occurrence, as rightly observed by HC, the manner in which they deposed before Court and details stated by them were acceptable and there was no valid reason to disbelieve their statements - Their evidence very clearly established that appellant was the person who involved in incident occurred - Evidence of PW4 supported stand took by PW1 - It was on record that PW4 was friend of appellant and residing in same area - Confession made by appellant to PW4 could be acted upon along with other material evidence - Statement of appellant recorded u/s. 313 of CrPC showed that appellant has neither denied nor stated about incriminating circumstances relied on by prosecution - In instant case 5 persons died and fact that HC commuted death sentence into life imprisonment imposing certain restrictions, decision of HC could not be faulted - Impugned judgment passed by HC was justified - Appeals dismissed.
