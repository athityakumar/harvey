Mohd. Yousuf v Director General Of Fire Services, A. P. & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

02 April 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Aftab Alam, Ranjana Prakash Desai

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 188; (2013) 4 SCC 265; 2014 (140) FLR 754; JT 2013 (9) SC 326; 2013(5) SCALE 248; 2013 (4) SLR 561

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Andhra Pradesh Revised Pension Rules, 1980, A.P.C.S. (CC & A) Rules, 1991

Summary: Service - Andhra Pradesh Civil Service (Classification, Control and Appeal) Rules, 1991 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 227 - Exparte proceedings - Dismissal from service - On grounds of unauthorized absence - Appellant was driver with fire station - Appellant allegedly committed offence of unauthorized absence - Appellant was punished with dismissal from service - Departmental appeals could not give desired relief to appellant - However, Tribunal by its order found and held that the enquiry was not conducted in accordance with Rules, and no enquiry report was furnished to the appellant - Further that on receipt of the Enquiry Officer's report, the Regional Fire Officer straightaway issued the order of punishment dismissing the applicant from service and, hence, his order was not in accordance with Rules - On filing of writ petition by respondents before HC, HC allowed petition in favour of respondents - Hence instant appeal - Whether order of dismissal from service was sustainable - Held, HC has taken the view that the ex parte enquiry held against the appellant could not be faulted as his whereabouts were not known and had also justified the non-supply of copy of the enquiry report to the appellant for the same reason - However, HC seemed to have overlooked that the notice with regard to the departmental enquiry was sent at the address of house No.147 but the correct address of the appellant was house No.177 and not No.147 - Thus, the ex parte enquiry and the order of dismissal passed on that basis were quite vulnerable and Tribunal had rightly held that the order of dismissal was passed on the basis of enquiry which was untenable in law - In the facts of the case, there was no occasion for HC to exercise its jurisdiction u/art. 227 of the Constitution - Impugned orders of the HC was set aside and order of Tribunal was restored - Appeals allowed.
