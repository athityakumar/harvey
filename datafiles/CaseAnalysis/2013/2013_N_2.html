Vathsala Manickavasagam and others v N. Ganesan and another

Supreme Court of India

02 July 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 386; (2013) 9 SCC 152; 2013(6) ALL MR 926; 2014 (102) ALR 881; 2013 (5) AWC 5230; 2013 (3) CLT(SC) 178; 2013 (5) CTC 884; JT 2013 (9) SC 483; 2013 (172) PLR 654; 2013 (4) RCR(Civil) 22; 2014 (122) RD 180; 2013(8) SCALE 522; [2013] 7 S.C.R. 320; 2014 (1) WLN(SC) 9

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Suit for partition-Plaintiffs claim 4/5th shares in respect of three items of suit properties-Trial court fully justified in holding that all three items of suit scheduled properties were joint family properties, in which plaintiffs and first respondent were entitled for equal share-Conclusions arrived by trial court based on contents of letter Ex. A-17-Cannot be found fault with-Contents of Ex. A-17 document not disputed by first respondent-First respondent not lead any evidence to disprove Ex. A-17 document-Ex. A-17 was very crucial piece of evidence, inasmuch as, it contains tacit admission voluntarily made by first respondent, while also establishing as to why veracity of it's nature was never questioned by him-Division Bench of High Court completely omitted to examine material piece of evidence which was considered in detail by trial court, while decreeing suit-Judgment of Division Bench cannot be sustained and set aside-Judgment and decree of trial court-Restored.
