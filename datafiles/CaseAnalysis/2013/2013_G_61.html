GAIL (India) Limited v Gujarat State Petroleum Corporation Limited

Supreme Court of India

17 September 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, V. Gopala Gowda

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 611; (2014) 1 SCC 329; 2013 (5) AWC 4942; 2013 (4) CLT(SC) 305; JT 2013 (12) SC 649; 2013 (7) MLJ 748; 2013(11) SCALE 518

Case Digest

Subject: Petroleum & Gas; Practice & Procedure; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Constitution of India-Article 226-Matter of fixation of price of gas supplied by appellant/G.A.I.L. (India)} Ltd.-Scope of judicial review-Contents of Gas Sale Agreement (G.S.A.), Price Side Letters and correspondence exchanged between appellant and respondent give clue of complex nature of price fixation mechanism-Therefore, High Court should have relegated respondent to effective alternative remedy of arbitration-Arbitral Tribunal could have decided complicated dispute between parties by availing services of experts-By invoking Article 226, power of judicial review cannot be exercised-Division Bench of High Court not justified in entertaining writ petition filed by respondent under Article 226.
