Gambhirsinh R. Dekare v Falgunbhai Chimanbhai Patel And Anr.

Supreme Court of India

11 March 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Chandramauli Kumar Prasad, V. Gopala Gowda

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 130; (2013) 3 SCC 697; AIR 2013 SC 1590; 2013 (6) ALT(SC) 58; 2013 CRLJ 1757; 2013 (2) CTC 579; 2013 (2) JCC 1324; JT 2013 (3) SC 570; 2013 (2) RCR(Criminal) 311; 2013(3) SCALE 302; [2013] 4 S.C.R. 719

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Media & Entertainment; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Media & Entertainment - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 500, 501, 502, 506, 507 and 114 - Press and Registration of Books Act, 1867, ss. 1, 5, 7 - Defamatory news - Publication - Cognizance - Sustainability - Appellant (complainant) filed a complaint against accused persons (editor and resident editor), who allegedly published false and defamatory news in a daily Newspaper and for alleged offences u/s. 500, 501, 502, 506, 507 and 114 of IPC - Trial Court took cognizance of said offences and issued process against accused - Aggrieved accused filed an application before HC for quashing of compliant, which was allowed by impugned judgment - Hence, instant appeal - Whether impugned judgment passed by HC was justified - Held, from s. 5 of the Act it was evident that every copy of every newspaper published in India was mandated to contain names of owner and Editor thereof - It was in the light of said obligation that name of accused no. 2 (editor) was printed as editor - S. 7 of the Act declaration to be prima facie evidence for fastening the liability in any civil or criminal proceeding on editor - It was evident that it was Editor who controls selection of matter that was published in a Newspaper - Once name of editor was shown, he should be held responsible in any civil and criminal proceeding - In instant case, complainant has specifically averred in complaint that news item was printed in newspaper as per the instructions and directions of accused persons - Complainant specifically alleged that accused nos. 1 and 2 have deliberately published the offending news and it was within their knowledge - At that stage, it was impermissible to go into truthfulness or otherwise of allegation and one has to proceed on a footing that allegation made was true - Thus, conclusion reached by HC that 'there was nothing in complaint to suggest that petitioner was aware of the offending news item being published or that he had any role to play in selection of such item for publication' was palpably wrong - Impugned judgment of HC was indefensible both on facts and law - Trial Court was directed to proceed with the trial - Impugned judgment was set aside - Appeal allowed.
