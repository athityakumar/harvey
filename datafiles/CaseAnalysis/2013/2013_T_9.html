Arunachal Pradesh Public Service Commission and another v Tage Habung and others

Supreme Court of India

01 May 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

M.Y. Eqbal, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 273; (2013) 7 SCC 737; AIR 2013 SC 1601; JT 2013 (6) SC 476; 2013(6) SCALE 502; [2013] 2 S.C.R. 1134; 2013 (4) SLR 200

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Kerala Judicial Service Rules, 1991, Arunachal Pradesh Public Service Combined Civil Service Examination Rules, 2001, Arunachal Pradesh Public Service Combined Competitive Examination Rules, 2001, Arunachal Pradesh Public Service Commission Examination Rules, 2001

Summary: Service - Arunachal Pradesh Public Service Combined Civil Service Examination Rules, 2001, r. 11 - Competitive exams - Cut off marks - Legality - Instant appeal was directed against the judgment and order passed by DB on a reference made to it pursuant to the order of Single Judge to answer the question as to whether the Office Memorandum dt. 7-1-2008 issued by the State Govt. and adopted by the Commission prescribing cut-off marks of 33 per cent or more to be secured in each written examination papers in Competitive Examination (Main) 2006-07 conducted by Commission for recruitment into various posts in Grade-A and Grade- B under the State Govt. was permissible after commencement of the recruitment process and applicable to the candidates who already took the Main Examination initiated in pursuance of the advertisement dt. 25-7-2006 for such recruitment - Held, there could not be any dispute that the merit of a candidate and his suitability was always assessed with reference to his performance at the examination - For the purpose of adjudging the merit and suitability of a candidate, the Commission had to fix minimum qualifying marks in the written examination in order to qualify in the viva voce test - Fixing the qualifying marks in the viva voce test after the commencement of the process of selection was not justified but fixing some criteria for qualifying a candidate in the written examination was necessary in order to shortlist the candidates for participating in the interview - Cut-off marks of 33 per cent fixed as qualifying marks in all subjects for the purpose of interview could not by any stretch of imagination be held illegal or unjustified merely because such criteria for securing minimum 33 per cent marks was notified for the Preliminary Examination and Main Examination - R. 11 of Rules empower the Commission to fix minimum qualifying marks for the purpose of shortlisting the candidates for interview - In SC's considered opinion, the power exercised by the Commission u/r. 11 of Rules fixing the qualifying marks in the written examination in the process of conducting the recruitment test could not be interfered with by SC - SC reiterate that there should be some yardstick to be followed by the Commission for the purpose of shortlisting the candidates after the written examination - Fixation of qualifying marks as 33 per cent in the written examination could not be held to be illegal or arbitrary action of the Commission merely because it was notified in the process of conducting recruitment tests - HC had lost sight of the fact that pursuant to the directions of Single Judge, result was declared applying the qualifying marks as notified in Office Memorandum dt. 7-1-2008 and the same was adopted by the Commission - Although it was desirable that the Commission should fix the minimum qualifying marks in each written examination, but in instant case the power exercised by the Commission in recruiting the candidates to secure qualifying marks could not be interfered with - Impugned order passed by DB was set aside - Appeal allowed.
