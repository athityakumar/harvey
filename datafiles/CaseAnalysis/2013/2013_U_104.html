Speaker, Orissa Legislative Assembly v Utkal Keshari Parida

Supreme Court of India

17 January 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, J. Chelameswar, Vikramajit Sen

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 27; (2013) 11 SCC 794; AIR 2013 SC 1181; 2013 (2) AWC 1934; 2013 (1) CTC 606; JT 2013 (1) SC 390; 2013(1) SCALE 403; [2013] 1 S.C.R. 348

Case Digest

Subject: Election; Parliament & Legislature

Keywords: Constitution (Fifty-Second Amendment) Act, 1985, Members of Orissa Legislative Assembly (Disqualification On Ground Of Defection) Rules, 1987

Summary: Parliament & Legislature - Practice & Procedure - Members of Orissa Legislative Assembly (Disqualification On Ground Of Defection) Rules, 1987, rr. 691), (2), para 8, 2(1)(a) - Constitution of India, 1950, sch. 10 - Political defection - Disqualification - Powers - Challenged - Respondent was unit president of a political party, filed petitions for disqualification of 4 elected members of State Legislative Assembly before appellant (Speaker of Assembly), in view of change of political party by said 4 members into other political party - Thereafter, respondent filed a petition before HC for direction to dispose pending disqualification petitions before appellant - HC held that if any member of house belonging to a political party had joined another political party, which was disqualification under para 2(1) of schedule 10 of Constitution, any person interested could make a reference to Speaker u/r. 6 of the Rules and it was not necessary that such reference had to be made by member of Legislative Assembly - Hence, instant appeal - Whether a petition for disqualification of a Member of House could be filed by any interested person - Held, 52nd amendment to Constitution, whereby 10th Schedule was added to Constitution indicated that evil of political defection had become a matter of national concern and if it was not checked, it could very well undermine the very foundation of democracy and principles which sustain the same - Provisions of rr. 6(1) and (2) of the Rules have to be read down to make it clear that not only a Member of House, but any person interested, would also be entitled to bring to notice of Speaker the fact that a Member of House had incurred disqualification under 10th Schedule to Constitution - On receipt of such information, Speaker of House would be entitled to decide under para 6 of 10th Schedule as to whether Member concerned had, in fact, incurred such disqualification and to pass appropriate orders on his findings - Impugned judgment of HC was upheld - Appeal dismissed.
