C.P. Subhash v Inspector Of Police Chennai & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

23 January 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, Gyan Sudha Misra

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 50; (2013) 11 SCC 559; AIR 2013 SC (Supp) 390; 2013 CRLJ 3684; JT 2013 (2) SC 270; 2013 (4) RCR(Criminal) 535; 2013(2) SCALE 19; [2013] 1 S.C.R. 545

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 468 and 471 - Forgery - Forged document - Complaint - Quashed - Sustainability - Respondent nos. 2, 3 and 4 (accused) were legal advisers/sr.Managers of a company, filed a declaration suit in respect of a piece of land, against appellant, who was general manager of another company, wherein respondents relied on 2 sale deeds, which are allegedly forged and crated one - Aggrieved appellant filed a complaint against said respondents for offences u/ss. 468 and 471 of IPC and FIR was registered - Thereafter, respondents filed a petition before HC to quash FIR, which was allowed holding that in entirety allegation did not prima facie constitute an offence and that u/s. 195 of CrPC there was a bar to take cognizance of offences in question except on a complaint in writing made by Court or public servant concerned - Appellant contended that s. 195 of CrPC was applicable to cases in which alleged fabrication of document taken place while same was in custody of court and that was not position in instant case - Hence, instant appeal - Whether impugned order quashing criminal proceedings against respondents was justified - Held, in instant case, it could not be said that allegations made in complaint did not constitute any offence or that same did not prima facie allege the complicity of persons accused of committing the same - Complaint filed by appellant sets out relevant facts and alleges that documents were forged and fabricated only to be used as genuine to make a fraudulent and illegal claim over land owned by complainant (appellant) - HC was wrong in quashing FIR on the said grounds - HC was wrong to hold that respondents were not makers of documents or that filing of a civil suit based on the same would not constitute an offence - Whether or not respondents had forged the documents and if so what offence was committed by respondents was a matter for investigation which could not be prejudged or quashed by HC in exercise of its powers u/s. 482 of CrPC or u/art. 226 of Constitution - Impugned order passed by HC was set aside - Appeal allowed.
