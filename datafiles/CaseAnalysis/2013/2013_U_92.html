S. Iyyapan v United India Insurance Company Limited and another

Supreme Court of India

01 July 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

M.Y. Eqbal, Surinder Singh Nijjar

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 377; (2013) 7 SCC 62; 2013 (3) ACC 19; 2013 ACJ 1944; AIR 2013 SC 2262; 2013 (5) ALD(SC) 62; 2013 (100) ALR 663; 2013 (4) AWC 4039; 2013 (5) Bom.C.R. 102; JT 2013 (10) SC 85; 2013 (6) MahLJ 1; 2014(5) M.P.H.T. 344; 2013 (4) MPLJ 259; 2013 (172) PLR 409; 2013 (3) RCR(Civil) 654; 2013 (4) RLW 2804; 2013(7) SCALE 637; [2013] 7 S.C.R. 45

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance

Keywords: Licensed Driver

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Insurance - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 - Accidental claim - Liability of insurance company - Whether an Insurance Company disown its liability on the ground that the driver of the vehicle although duly licensed to drive light motor vehicle but there was no endorsement in the licence to drive light motor vehicle used as commercial vehicle -

Held, heading 'Insurance of Motor Vehicles against Third Party Risks' given in Chap. XI of the Act itself showed the intention of the legislature to make third party insurance compulsory and to ensure that the victims of accident arising out of use of motor vehicles would be able to get compensation for the death or injuries suffered - Provision had been inserted in order to protect the persons travelling in vehicles or using the road from the risk attendant upon the user of the motor vehicles on the road - Further, as provisions of ss. 146 and 147 of the Act, it was evidently clear that in certain circumstances the insurer's right was safeguarded but in any event the insurer had to pay compensation when a valid certificate of insurance was issued notwithstanding the fact that the insurer may proceed against the insured for recovery of the amount - Hence, insurer could not disown its liability on the ground that although the driver was holding a licence to drive a light motor vehicle but before driving light motor vehicle used as commercial vehicle, no endorsement to drive commercial vehicle was obtained in the driving licence - In any case, it was the statutory right of a third party to recover the amount of compensation so awarded from the insurer - It was for the insurer to proceed against the insured for recovery of the amount in the event there had been violation of any condition of the insurance policy - Appeal allowed.
