Government of Andhra Pradesh and others v Ch. Gandhi

Supreme Court of India

19 February 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 99; (2013) 5 SCC 111; AIR 2013 SC 2113; 2013 (3) ALD(SC) 32; 2013 (137) FLR 143; JT 2013 (3) SC 131; 2013(2) SCALE 646; [2013] 2 S.C.R. 20; 2013 (2) SLR 241

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Andhra Pradesh Civil Services (Classification, Control and Appeal) Rules, 1991, Decision To Initiate, Punjab Public Service Commission (Conditions of Service) Regulations, 1958

Summary: Service - Andhra Pradesh Civil Services (Classification, Control and Appeal) Rules, 1991 - Misconduct - Imposition of punishment - Applicability of relevant rules - Disciplinary proceeding u/r. 5 of the Rules was initiated against respondent, a Senior Accountant in the Office of Sub Treasury on various charges - On basis of enquiry report, disciplinary authority, imposed penalty of reversion to post of Junior Accountant for 2years with the stipulation that there would be postponement of future increments - Aggrieved by said punishment, respondent filed application before Tribunal - Tribunal dismissed application and held that there was no illegality or irregularity in initiation of disciplinary proceeding, framing of charge or conduct of enquiry and further, regard being had to the gravity of the charge, punishment could not be treated to be disproportionate - On appeal, HC held that findings recorded by Tribunal on said scores were absolutely defensible and did not warrant any interference - As far as imposition of punishment was concerned, HC held that penalty imposed by disciplinary authority did amount to imposition of 2 penalties and, accordingly, set aside punishment which was concurred with by Tribunal and clarified that said overturning of orders would not preclude authorities to pass appropriate orders pertaining to punishment keeping in view the provisions of Rules - Hence instant appeals - Appellant/State contended that HC had erroneously opined that 2major penalties were issued in violation of the Rules though reversion to the lower post for a period of 2years with the stipulation of postponement of future increments on restoration to higher category did not tantamount to 2major penalties u/r. 9 of the Rules and, under no circumstances, it contravenes r.11 (27) of the Rules - Further contended that amended Rules permit imposition of such punishment but the same was not taken note of by HC, which made the order absolutely vulnerable - Whether punishment could be imposed in accord with the amended Rules or under un-amended Rules - Held, amended Rule despite having been substituted had no retrospective effect - That apart, the notification used the phraseology 'shall be substituted' which clearly indicated the fact that amended Rule was prospective - Therefore, fulcrum of controversy was whether respondent had vested or accrued right to be visited with particular punishment engrafted u/r. 9 of the un-amended Rules - Disciplinary proceeding were initiated under un-amended rules - Under un-amended r. 9(vii) of the Rules, punishment provided was reduction to a lower rank in the seniority list or to a lower stage in the seniority list or to a lower stage in the timescale of pay or to a lower time scale of pay not being lower than that to which he was directly recruited or to lower grade or post not being lower than that to which he was directly recruited - After the amendment, r. 9(vii) was bifurcated into two parts - It was luculent that though earlier r. 9(vii) provided for reduction to lower grade or post, yet it did not stipulate imposition of condition on restoration as regards his seniority and pay to the original grade or post - Instant case, disciplinary proceeding was initiated by serving a charge-sheet for the purpose of imposition of a major penalty and, therefore, the maximum punishment of dismissal could have been imposed on respondent - Further, under un-amended rule, there were, apart from stoppage of increment with cumulative effect and reduction in rank, grade, post or service, three major punishments, namely, compulsory retirement, removal and dismissal from service by which there was severance of service - Maximum punishment that could have been imposed on an employee after conducting due departmental enquiry was dismissal from service - As was evincible, chargesheet only referred to imposition of major penalty or to be dealt with under said rules relating to major penalty - In this backdrop, it would be difficult to say that employee had vested right to be imposed a particular punishment as envisaged under un-amended rules - Order of compulsory retirement is a lesser punishment than dismissal or removal as pension of a compulsorily retired employee, if eligible to get pension under the Pension Rules, is not affected - R. 9(vii) was only dealing with reduction or reversion but issuance of any other direction was not a part of it - It had come by way of amendment - Disciplinary authority had not committed any error by imposing said punishment, regard being had to the nature of charges - Reason behind it, was not to let off one with simple reduction but to give a direction about condition of pay on restoration and also not to impose a harsher punishment which may not be proportionate -Orders passed by HC was set aside and order of punishment imposed by Disciplinary Authority was restored - Appeal allowed.
