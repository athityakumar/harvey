Girraj Prasad Meena v State of Rajasthan and others

Supreme Court of India

30 September 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, Sharad Arvind Bobde

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 645; (2014) 13 SCC 674; 2013 (4) JCC 2519; 2013 (4) RCR(Criminal) 583; 2013(12) SCALE 275; [2013] 10 S.C.R. 393

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: United Provinces First Offender's Probation Act, 1938

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 323, 324, 365 - Probation of Offenders Act, 1958, ss. 3, 12 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 164 - Plea bargaining - Failure to give notice to victim - Private respondents allegedly kidnapped appellant's brother-in-law - Respondents filed application pleading guilty u/ss. 323, 324 IPC - Trial Court convicted appellants by imposing fine and granted benefit u/ss. 3 and 12 of 1958 Act - Appellants challenged Trial Court order before HC - HC dismissed appellant's application - Hence, instant appeal - Held, appellant had been raising grievance from very beginning that police was not investigating case properly - In statement recorded u/s. 164 CrPC before Magistrate, appellant gave a full version as to how victim was kidnapped - However, police filed a charge sheet only for offences punishable u/ss. 323 and 343 IPC - Had Trial Court applied its mind to material collected during investigation, charges could have been framed also u/s. 365 IPC - Further, before statements of witnesses could be recorded, respondents filed an application admitting their guilt - Trial Court proceeded in great haste and disposed of matter on the same date when application was filed - Trial Court adopted a procedure not known in law - HC failed to appreciate that before statement of appellant or any other witness could be recorded, Trial Court disposed of matter - If Trial Court wanted to entertain any issue of plea bargaining under Chapter 21-A of CrPC, then too the court was obliged thereunder to put victim to notice before extending any such benefits that had been given in instant case - Procedure was clearly violated - Appellant had no opportunity to raise any grievance before appropriate forum - Hence, impugned judgments of HC as well as of Trial Court were set aside - Appeal allowed.
