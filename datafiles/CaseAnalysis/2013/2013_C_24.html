Central Electricity Supply Utility of Odisha and another v Dhobei Sahoo and others

Supreme Court of India

01 November 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 725; (2014) 1 SCC 161; AIR 2013 SC 246; 2013 (6) AWC 6404; 2014 (1) ILR(Cut) 801; 2013(13) SCALE 477

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Recovery of honorarium amount

Summary: (1) Employment-Recovery of salary amount-Asking someone to work and when his appointment is nullified by issue of writ of quo warranto by Court-Neither employer can recover amount nor court can direct recovery of same-There has to be some other reason for denial of payment, recovery of salary or honorarium-While issuing writ of quo warranto there cannot be any direction for recovery of salary amount.

(2) Writ of quo warranto-Cautious approach is requisite while dealing with writ of quo warranto.

(3) Implementation of law-Role of courts-Courts while implementing law may not always be guided by total legalistic approach-But that does not necessarily mean to move on totally moralistic principle which has no sanction of law.

(4)Directions and guidelines by Court-In formulations of guidelines or issuing directions-Courts are bound to be within constitutional parameters.
