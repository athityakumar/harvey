The Commissioner, Bangalore Development Authority & Anr. v Brijesh Reddy & Anr. ....

Supreme Court of India

08 February 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, J. S. KHEHAR

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 73; (2013) 3 SCC 66; 2013 (97) ALR 474; 2013 (1) CLT(SC) 229; JT 2013 (3) SC 150; 2013 (4) KarLJ 66; 2013 (3) MahLJ 880; 2013 (2) MLJ 742; 2015(2) M.P.H.T. 41; 2013 (2) MPLJ 535; 2013 (171) PLR 136; 2013 (2) RCR(Civil) 444; 2013 (119) RD 274; 2013(2) SCALE 306; [2013] 1 S.C.R. 853; 2013 (4) WLN(SC) 92

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Land acquisition proceedings - Maintainability of suit - Notification was issued by the State Govt. proposing to acquire several lands including the suit land - After holding an enquiry, the Land Acquisition Officer passed the award - When the Bangalore Development Authority (BDA) tried to interfere with the possession of the suit property, plaintiff filed suit for injunction - In the meantime, the respondents purchased the suit land from the plaintiff under a registered sale deed and were put in possession - When the BDA tried to interfere with the possession of the plaintiff, they filed a petition, which was dismissed as withdrawn - Thereafter, suit was filed for permanent injunction, which was also dismissed as not maintainable - On appeal, HC by impugned order allowed the appeal and remitted the matter to the Trial Court - Hence, instant appeal - (A) Whether a Civil Court had jurisdiction to entertain a suit when the schedule lands were acquired under the land acquisition proceedings -

Held, Courts have jurisdiction to try all suits of a civil nature excepting suits of which their cognizance was either expressly or impliedly barred - Jurisdiction of Civil Court with regard to a particular matter could be said to be excluded if there was an express provision or by implication it could be inferred that the jurisdiction was taken away - Objection as to the exclusion of Civil Court's jurisdiction for availability of alternative forum should be taken before the Trial Court and at the earliest failing which the higher court may refuse to entertain the plea in the absence of proof of prejudice.

(B) Whether the HC was justified in remanding the matter to the Trial Court without examining the question with regard to the maintainability of the suit -

Held, HC committed an error in remanding the matter to the Trial Court on the ground that the plaintiffs were not given opportunity to adduce evidence to show that their vendor was in possession which entitled them for grant of permanent injunction from evicting them from the scheduled property without due process of law by the defendants - In the light of the specific assertion coupled with materials in the written statement about the acquisition of land long ago and subsequent events, suit of any nature including bare injunction was not maintainable, hence, HC was not right in remitting the matter to the Trial Court for fresh disposal - HC failed to take note of the fact that even in the plaint itself, the plaintiffs have stated that the suit land was acquired and yet they purchased the suit land in 1995 and undoubtedly have to face the consequence - Possession vested with the BDA way back in 1969 and 1978 and all the details have been asserted in the written statements, hence the remittal order could not be sustained - Impugned judgment passed by the HC remitting the matter to the Trial Court was set aside and the judgment of the Trial Court was restored - Appeal allowed.
