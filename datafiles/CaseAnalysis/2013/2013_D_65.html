Devendra Kishanlal Dagalia v Dwarkesh Diamonds Private Limited and others

Supreme Court of India

25 November 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya, V. Gopala Gowda

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 761; (2014) 2 SCC 246; AIR 2014 SC 655; 2013 ALL MR (Cri) 4434; 2013 (4) BC 663; 2014 (1) Bom.C.R. 547; 2015 (4) CGLJ 539; 2013 (6) CTC 780; 2014 (1) JCC (NI) 20; JT 2014 (1) SC 229; 2014 (1) MLJ(Crl) 82; 2014 (1) OLR 655; 2014 (174) PLR 707; 2014 (1) RCR(Civil) 222; 2014 (1) RCR(Criminal) 158; 2013(14) SCALE 397

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Legal Notice, Recall

Summary: (A)Criminal - Banking & Finance - Practice & Procedure - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, s. 138 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 201, 204, 482 - Dishonour of cheque - Summons - Recall - Legality - Appellant (complainant) filed a complaints u/s. 138 of the Act against respondents (accused/company) before Trial Court - Respondents filed an applications u/s. 201 of CrPC before Trial Court for return of complaint for want of jurisdiction and alleged that entire transactions took place at New Delhi and only the legal notice was issued from Mumbai - Trial Court by its order returned the complaints for want of jurisdiction - Aggrieved appellant filed a revision applications before Lower Appellate Court, which were allowed and matter was remitted back to Trial Court - However, HC by impugned judgment set aside the order Lower Appellate Court - Aggrieved appellants filed instant appeals - Respondents contended that issuance of notice would not by itself give rise to a cause of action for filing the complaint at Mumbai - Whether Magistrate after proceeding u/s. 204 of CrPC had jurisdiction to recall or review order by exercising its power u/s. 201 of CrPC - Held, once the decision was taken and summon was issued u/s. 204 CrPC, there was no question of going back following the procedure u/s. 201 of CrPC, in absence of a power of review including inherent power to do so, remedy lies before HC u/s. 482 of CrPC or u/art. 227 of Constitution and not before the Magistrate - Magistrate could not recall the summon in exercise of power u/s. 201 of CrPC - Appeals allowed.

(B)Criminal - Banking & Finance - Practice & Procedure - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, s. 138 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 201, 204, 482 - Dishonour of cheque - Jurisdiction - Maintainability - Whether petition u/s. 138 of the Act was maintainable at Mumbai - Held, business dealing was held at Mumbai; products were supplied from Mumbai to New Delhi, cheques were handed over at Mumbai and the cheques were dishounoured by the bankers of respondents at New Delhi, and legal notice was issued from Mumbai - Thus, at least one act out of 5 ingredients of s. 138 of the Act having committed at Mumbai, complaint preferred by the complainant before Magistrate at Mumbai was maintainable - Appeals allowed.
