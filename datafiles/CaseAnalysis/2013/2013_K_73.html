Jiju Kuruvila and another v Kunjujamma Mohan and another

Supreme Court of India

02 July 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 355; (2013) 9 SCC 166; 2013 (3) ACC 49; 2013 ACJ 2141; AIR 2013 SC 2293; 2013 (5) ALD(SC) 165; 2013(4) ALL MR 946; 2013 (4) AWC 4050; 2013 (4) Bom.C.R. 481; 2013 (4) CTC 252; 2013 (3) KLT 261; 2013 (5) MLJ 751; 2013 (2) OLR 1045; 2013 (3) RCR(Civil) 817; 2013 (4) RLW 3330; 2013(8) SCALE 722; [2013] 7 S.C.R. 276

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance

Keywords: Diplomatic and Consular Officers (Oaths and Fees) Act, 1948, Entitlement of, Ratio of contributory negligence

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Insurance - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, ss. 140, 166 - Diplomatic and Consular Officers(Oaths and Fees) Act, 1948, s. 3 - Motor accident - Compensation - Ratio of contributory negligence - Enhancement of compensation - Entitlement of - On account of death of deceased in a motor accident the appellants (dependents/claimants) filed an application u/ss. 140 and 166 of the Act and claimed compensation of Rs.57,25,000/- under different heads - Tribunal held that accident was caused due to rash and negligent driving of bus driver - Considering the contributory negligence on the part of deceased, Tribunal apportioned the liability for accident in ratio of 75:25 between the driver of bus and deceased and an awarded for of Rs.13,80,625/- with 12% interest was passed in favour of appellants - However, HC apportioned the contributory negligence at the rate of 50:50 for payment of compensation - While determining the compensation, HC took the figure of 1500$ US as the dependency benefit an exchange rate as was prevailed on the date of filing of claim petition and assessed total compensation of Rs.23,45,750/- with interest in favour the appellants - Aggrieved appellants and Insurance filed instant appeals - Whether there was any contributory negligence on the part of deceased and compensation awarded was just and proper - Hel, date of filing of claim petition (April, 1990) was the proper date for fixing the rate of exchange at which foreign currency amount was to be converted into currency of the country (INR) - Tribunal and HC rightly relied on Ext.-A7, to fix the rate of exchange as Rs.17.30 (as was prevailed in April, 1990) - There was no evidence on record to suggest any negligence on part of the deceased - 'Scene Mahazar' (Ext.B2) did not suggest any rash and negligent driving on the part of deceased - Mere position of the vehicles after accident, as shown in a Ex.B2, could not give a substantial proof as to the rash and negligent driving on the part of one or the other - In absence of direct or corroborative evidence, Court could not give any specific finding about negligence on the part of any individual - Ext.B2 and post mortem report (Ext.A5) could not take the place of evidence, when direct evidence like independent eye-witness (PW3), FIR, charge-sheet and F.I statement were on record - Tribunal and HC erred in concluding that accident occurred due to negligence on the part of deceased and said conclusion was not based on evidence but based on mere presumption and surmises - Ext.-A6 (certificate of employer) shows that annual salary of deceased was 30,000$, which was attested by Notary Public and counter signed by Consulate General of India, u/s. 3 of 1948 Act - Thus, total compensation amount come to Rs.57,49,500/- along with interest at the rate of 12% - Appeals disposed of.
