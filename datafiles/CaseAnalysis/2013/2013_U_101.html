Urmila Devi v Yudhvir Singh

Supreme Court of India

23 October 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla, T. S. Thakur

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 896; (2013) 15 SCC 624; 2014 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 2149

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: (A) Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 197, 198 - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Official duties - Prosecution of Magistrate - On complaint made by appellant, Trial by order dt.30-7-2001 summoned respondent and other accused to face trial for offences u/ss.323, 354, 389, 452,458,500 and 506, read with ss. 34 and 120-B of IPC - Aggrieved, respondent filed application to recall said order but the same was dismissed by Trial Court - However, on appeal, Sessions Judge set aside both orders of Magistrate holding that in view of bar enjoined u/s. 197 of CrPC, respondent could not be summoned to face trial - On appeal by appellant, HC upheld said orders of Sessions Judge - Hence, instant appeal - Whether acts complained against respondent and other accused could be construed as one in exercise of official duties so as to attract invocation of s. 197 of CrPC.

Held, when allegation of live-in relationship of appellant and another, could not have formed basis for prosecution of offence falling u/s. 198 of CrPC read with provisions of Chapter XX of IPC, none of actions alleged against respondent could have been carried out in his capacity as an Executive Magistrate. Hence, invocation of s. 197 of CrPC was uncalled for and consequently impugned orders of Sessions Judge and HC could not be sustained and summons issued by Trial Court dt.30-7-2001 and order dt.17-4-2007 of Trial Court declining to recall issuance of summons are restored. Appeal allowed.

(B) Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 200, 204, 397 - Issuance of summons - Interlocutory order - Whether order issuing summons is interim order or intermediate order.

Held, order issued by Magistrate deciding to summon accused in exercise of power u/ss. 200 to 204 of CrPC is an order of intermediatory or quasi-final in nature and not interlocutory in nature and aggrieved party could work out revisionary jurisdiction either with District Court or HC provided u/s. 397 of CrPC and it could always be subject matter of challenge under inherent jurisdiction of HC u/s. 482 of CrPC. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - For offences falling u/ss. 493 to 498 of IPC, an aggrieved person could be either husband or wife and none else other than those who would fall under proviso to s. 198(2) of CrPC.
