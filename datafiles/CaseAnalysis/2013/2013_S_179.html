Rajesh Patel v State Of Jharkhand

Supreme Court of India

15 March 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, Chandramauli Kumar Prasad

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 151; (2013) 3 SCC 791; AIR 2013 SC 1497; 2013 CRLJ 2062; 2013 (2) JCC 1260; JT 2013 (4) SC 110; 2013 (2) MLJ(Crl) 632; 2013 (2) OLR 118; 2013 (2) RCR(Criminal) 346; 2013 (2) RLW 1371; 2013(3) SCALE 550; [2013] 2 S.C.R. 411

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Consensual sex, Illegal confinement

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 376 - Rape - Illegal confinement - Consensual sex - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant-accused was convicted for offence u/s. 376 of IPC - Prosecution case was that appellant and prosecutrix had acquaintance with other and when prosecutrix went to house of appellant, he confined her and raped her - Trial court after perusing record and convicted accused - Aggrieved appellant filed appeal before HC, which dismissed appeal and concurred with HC - Hence instant appeal - Appellant contended that there was delay in lodging FIR, and that it was consensual sex and knew each other as they were class mates and that prosecutrix herself went to house of appellant - Whether conviction passed by Trial Court was sustainable - Held, contention urged on behalf appellant that it was consensual sex with prosecutrix is to be believed for reason that she herself has gone to house of appellant though her version is that she went there at request of appellant to take back her book which she had given to him, this was a strong circumstance to arrive at conclusion that defence case of appellant was a consensual sex - Further, prosecution case was that after offence was committed by appellant he had locked room from outside and left, after half an hour PW3 arrived and unlocked room, this story was improbable to believe and prosecutrix had not lodged complaint either immediately or within reasonable period from date of occurrence but complaint was undisputedly lodged after lapse of 11 days by prosecutrix - Hence, prosecution case was not natural, consistent and probable to believe to sustain conviction - Appeal allowed.
