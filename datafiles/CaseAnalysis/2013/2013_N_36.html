Pepsu Road Transport Corporation v National Insurance Company

Supreme Court of India

26 August 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Kurian Joseph, Gyan Sudha Misra

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 541; (2013) 10 SCC 217; 2013 (3) ACC 871; 2014 (2) ACC 18; 2013 ACJ 2440; AIR 2014 SC 305; 2014 (1) ALD(SC) 78; 2013(5) ALL MR 924; 2013 (100) ALR 647; 2013 (6) AWC 5736; 2013 (6) Bom.C.R. 292; 2013 (3) CLT(SC) 332; 2013 (4) CPJ(Sc) 84; 2013 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 3191; 2013 (6) MahLJ 913; 2013 (4) MPLJ 581; 2013 (172) PLR 750; 2013 (4) RCR(Civil) 273; 2013 (4) RLW 3237; 2013(10) SCALE 663; [2013] 9 S.C.R. 266; 2013 (4) WLN(SC) 87

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance

Keywords: Motor Accident Claim, Entitlement of

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, s. 149(2)(a) - Motor accident claim - Compensation - Entitlement of - Deceased met with an accident and succumbed to injuries - Claimants were widow and two minor sons of deceased - Claimants filed claim petition before Tribunal - Tribunal passed awarded compensation - Dissatisfied with compensation awarded, respondent no. 4/Insurance company took Local Commissioner to licensing authority, on verification of records, it was reported no such license was possessed by driver issued by licensing Authority - Aggrieved appellant/owner of vehicle filed instant appeal - Appellant contended that they had appointed respondent no. 3 as driver with PRTC in 1994, he was given proper training from driving school at Patiala and, having taken reasonable steps in verifying driving license and, thereafter, having trained driver by employer himself, it could not be said that respondent no. 4 was not liable -

Held, appellant employer had employed respondent no. 3 as driver in 1994 - In process of employment, he had been put to driving test and he had been imparted training also - Accident took place only after six years of his service in PRTC as driver - In such circumstances, it could not be said that insured was at fault in having employed person whose license was proved to be fake by insurance company before Tribunal - On scanning evidence of licensing authority before Tribunal, it could not also be absolutely held that license to driver had not been issued by said authority and that license was fake - Though appellant had also taken contention that compensation was on higher side, no serious attempt was made and according to us justifiably, to canvas that position - Respondent no. 4 was liable to indemnify appellant and, hence, there could be no recovery of compensation already paid to claimants - Appeal allowed.
