S. Subramaniam Balaji v Government of Tamil Nadu and others

Supreme Court of India

05 July 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, Ranjan Gogoi

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 415; (2013) 9 SCC 659; 2013(4) ALL MR 908; 2013 (6) AWC 5834; 2013 (6) Bom.C.R. 247; 2013 (3) CLT(SC) 203; 2013 (4) CTC 290; JT 2013 (10) SC 153; 2013 (6) MLJ 307; 2013 (3) RCR(Civil) 867; 2013(8) SCALE 249

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Election; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Comptroller and Auditor-General's (Duties, Powers and Conditions of Service) Act, 1971, Appropriation Act, 2012, Appropriation Bill, 2012, Solar Powered Green House Scheme, Marriage Assistance Scheme, Free Rice Scheme

Summary: Constitution of India-Article 324-Representation of People Act, 1951-Section 123-Election manifesto-Promise in election manifesto-Corrupt practice-Case relates to distribution of free gifts by political parties (Popularly known as 'freebies')-Dravida Munnetra Kazhagam (D.M.K.) while releasing election manifesto for Assembly Elections, 2006, announced scheme of free distribution of Colour Television Sets (C.C.T.Vs.) to each and every household which did not possess the same, if said party/its alliance were elected to power-Promises in election manifesto cannot be read into Section 123 for declaring it to be corrupt practice-Although promises in election manifesto do not constitute as corrupt practice under prevailing law-Reality cannot be ruled out that distribution of freebies of any kind, undoubtedly, influences all people-It shakes root of free and fair elections to large degree-No enactment that directly governs contents of election manifesto-Election Commission directed to frame guidelines for the same in consultation with all recognised political parties-Election Commission directed to take up this task as early as possible owing to its utmost importance-Need for separate legislation to be passed by Legislature in this regard for governing political parties-Recommended.
