Satya Jain (D) And Ors. v Anis Ahmed Rushdie (D) Th. Lrs. And Ors

Supreme Court of India

08 May 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjan Gogoi, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 315; (2013) 8 SCC 147; AIR 2013 SC 3784; 2013 (5) ALD(SC) 121; 2013 (98) ALR 910; 2013 (4) AWC 4022; JT 2013 (7) SC 422; 2013 (3) RCR(Civil) 281; 2013(6) SCALE 765; [2013] 3 S.C.R. 347

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Supreme Court Rules, 1966 - Specific performance - Decreed - Objections raised against decree - Sustainability - Applications were filed to sought impleadment/clarification/modification/ correction of the judgment dt. 3-12-2012 whereby SC had set aside decree passed by HC and the suit for specific performance filed by the plaintiffs 1 (since deceased), 2 and 3 was decreed - Whether modification/clarification of judgement passed by SC was sustainable - Held, application for modification/clarification of a final order passed by SC was not contemplated by the provisions of Rules which specifically provided the remedy of review and also laid down the procedure governing the consideration of a review application by SC - In fact, filing of such applications for modification was deprecated by SC in one case before it - Delhi Administration Vs. Gurdip Singh Uban & Ors. 2000 Indlaw SC 416, relied on and A.P. SRTC & Ors. Vs. Abdul Kareem 2007 Indlaw SC 38, relied on - It was in such backdrop that SC proceeded to examine the prayers made in applications filed - Insofar as first application was concerned, suffice it would be to note that the facts stated therein, on the basis of which the prayer for modification/clarification was made, were not before the Court at the time when the judgment dt. 3-12-2012 was rendered - In third application the reliefs sought were based on facts and events which occurred subsequent to the order of SC - Applicants sought to reopen concluded issues and alteration of the consequential directions which have attained finality - Such a course of action was not permissible and at best the parties might be left with the option of seeking such remedies as might be open in law to vindicate any perceived right or claim - SC, therefore, disposed of the first and third application - Insofar as second application was concerned, applicants had submitted that an application seeking review of SC's judgment dt. 3-12-2012, to the extent prayed for in the I.As., was filed - That apart, applicants had drawn SC's attention to some typographical errors in the judgment - SC, therefore, deemed it proper to consider the application on a slightly different footing - SC observed that the facts surrounding the alleged transfer of the suit property or the rights over the said property by the defendant/respondents to X Limited were not before SC at the time of hearing of the appeals in question or even at the time when the judgment was rendered - Though the aforesaid facts along with the supporting documents were filed by way of an additional paper book no specific order of the Court was sought or granted to the appellants to rely on the said documents - In such circumstances, the facts sought to be brought on record could not be a legitimate basis for any modification of our judgment even if applications in question were construed to be applications for review of SC's judgment dt. 3-12-2012 - Insofar as typographical errors and the suggested corrections mentioned applications were concerned, SC had examined the contents of the relevant paragraphs of the judgment dt. 3-12-2012 - On such consideration, SC found that the errors pointed out by the applicants indeed, had occurred - Consequently, such errors were rectified correct - Applications disposed of.
