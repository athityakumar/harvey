Shankar Kisanrao Khade v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

25 April 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan, Madan B. Lokur

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 264; (2013) 5 SCC 546; AIR 2013 SC (Supp) 947; 2013 CRLJ 2595; JT 2013 (6) SC 315; 2013 (3) RCR(Criminal) 588; 2013(6) SCALE 277; [2013] 6 S.C.R. 949

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Kidnap

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 363, 366-A, 376, 302, 201 r/w s. 34 - Kidnap - Rape - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant No.1 husband along with appellant No.2 wife allegedly kidnapped, raped and murdered the deceased minor girl in furtherance of common intention - Trial Court convicted appellant No.1 u/ss. 363, 366-A, 376, 302, 201 r/w s. 34 IPC and sentenced the appellant No. 1 to death - Trial Court convicted appellant No. 2 u/ss. 363, 366-A, 302, 201 r/w s. 34 IPC - Aggrieved, appellant preferred appeal before HC, which was dismissed and death sentence was confirmed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether the Courts below were justified in awarding death sentence - Held, victim was aged 11 years, on date of incident, a school going child totally innocent, defenseless and having moderate intellectual disability - Ex. P-4 was a certificate issued by President of Handicap Board General Hospital, which disclosed that girl was physically handicapped and was having moderate mental retardation - Evidence of PW 10, PW 12 and PW13 also corroborated the fact that she was a minor girl with moderate intellectual disability, an aggravating circumstance which goes against appellant - Vulnerability of victim with moderate intellectual disability was an aggravating circumstance - Appellant was a fatherly figure aged 52 years - PW3/Doctor, who conducted post mortem, had deposed as well as stated in report ghastly manner in which crime was executed - Rape was committed on more than one occasion and manner in which rape as well as murder was executed was elaborately discussed in oral evidence as well as in report which did not want to reiterate - However, action of appellant, not only was inhuman but barbaric - Ruthless crime of repeated actions of rape followed by murder of a young minor girl who was having moderate intellectual disability, shocks not only judicial conscience, but conscience of society - In instant case crime test had satisfied fully against appellant - Criminal test had fully satisfied against appellant and did not find any mitigating factor favouring appellant - Only mitigating circumstance stated was that appellant was having 2 sons aged 26 and 27 years and were dependent on him, was not a mitigating circumstance and 'criminal test' was fully satisfied against appellant - Both crime test and criminal test were, independently satisfied against appellant - Any other punishment other than life imprisonment should be completely inadequate and should not meet ends of justice - Even though all mentioned tests had satisfied in instant case, extreme sentence of Death penalty was not warranted since one of factors which influenced HC to award death sentence was previous track record of appellant - IO/Investigating Officer, during course of hearing of criminal appeal also stated that appellant was also figured as accused - However, mere pendency of criminal cases as such could not be an aggravating factor to be taken note of while granting appropriate sentence - Therefore, mere pendency of few criminal cases as such was not an aggravating circumstance to be taken note of while awarding death sentence unless accused was found guilty and convicted in those cases - High Court was in error in holding that those were relevant factors to be considered in awarding appropriate sentence - It was directed that convert death sentence awarded to appellant to rigorous imprisonment for life and that all sentences awarded would run consecutively - Appeals dismissed.
