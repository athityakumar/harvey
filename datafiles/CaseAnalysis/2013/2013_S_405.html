Mahanagar Telephone Nigam Limited v State of Maharashtra and others

Supreme Court of India

11 July 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, V. Gopala Gowda

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 587; (2013) 9 SCC 92; AIR 2013 SC 3568; 2013 (5) AWC 4659; 2013 (4) CLT(SC) 96; JT 2013 (10) SC 594; 2013 (4) RCR(Civil) 877; 2013(8) SCALE 739

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Socio-Economic

Keywords: Maharashtra Slum Areas (Improvement, Clearance and Redevelopment) Act, 1971

Summary: Socio-Economic - Practice & Procedure - Planning scheme - Rehabilitation of dwellers - Delay in filing petition - Condonation of delay - Notification was issued u/s. 4(1) of the Act for acquisition of four plots under Town Planning Scheme for Posts and Telegraph Offices - Special Land Acquisition Officer (respondent No.2) passed award and fixed market value of plots and deposited amount to officers of Telephones - Some portions of acquired land which were proposed for planning scheme were occupied by slum dwellers - Respondent no.2/appellant sent letters to officers of Bombay Telephones to rehabilitate hutment dwellers or pay rehabilitation compensation and deposit establishment and service charges - Officers of Bombay Telephones and Posts and Telegraph Department exchanged correspondence and sent communications to Bombay Housing and Area Development Board, for delivery of possession of two plots - Communications were sent to respondent no.2 and others to deliver possession of one plot - There was no mention about other plot as compensation deposited in respect of that plot was withdrawn - Further, as per order of SRA, respondent no.5 completed rehabilitation building Occupation Certificates was issued to slum dwellers for permanent residence - After pronouncement of award, respondent no.2 filed writ petition which was disposed of - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether appellant's prayer for condonation of delay should be entertained - Held, if it was not agreed to transfer of area by respondent no.5 to appellant appellant's counsel would not have written letter to her counterpart for execution of separate MOU and to indicate time limit for completion of construction and handing over of possession and officers of appellant would not have entered into correspondence with respondent no.5 for construction of separate building and providing independent access - If appellant was of view that HC had disposed of writ petition without examining its prayer then it would have filed petition for review - Appellant had not filed such petition - Reason for appellant's omission to adopt that course was not far to see - Appellant filed writ petition after almost three decades of pronouncement of award by respondent no.2 and there was no tangible explanation for delay - If appellant had pressed its prayer to official respondents to deliver possession of acquired land after evicting slum dwellers, HC would have non-suited it on ground of laches by taking cognizance of total inaction - HC would have also taken note of fact that while appellant was sleeping over its rights, Municipal Corporation had sanctioned slum rehabilitation scheme, Cooperative Society formed by slum dwellers had entered into development agreement with respondent no.5 and latter had constructed buildings and handed over units to slum dwellers for permanent residence - Delay of almost 3 decades stared in face of appellant - Appeal dismissed.
