Asha and another v State of Uttarakhand

Supreme Court of India

01 November 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 733; (2014) 4 SCC 174; 2013 (4) MLJ(Crl) 565; 2013 (4) RCR(Criminal) 1024; 2013(13) SCALE 368

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 304B, 306, 302 r/w s. 34 - Dowry death - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellants (accused) allegedly committed offence u/ss. 302/34, 304B and 306 of IPC - Trial Court found appellants guilty of offence u/s. 304B of IPC and sentenced them to undergo RI for 10 years - Aggrieved appellants filed appeals before HC, which were dismissed by impugned judgment - Hence, instant appeals - Appellants contended that there was no evidence to show that deceased was subjected to any cruelty or harassment by appellants in connection with demand of dowry soon before her death and conditions set forth for conviction u/s. 304B of IPC were not satisfied by prosecution - Whether impugned conviction judgment passed by Trial Court which was upheld by HC was justified - Held, Courts were required to scrutinise the evidence very cautiously and carefully in order to arrive at conclusion as to whether all the ingredients of offence with reference to conditions enumerated u/s. 304B of IPC to convict the accused were satisfied by prosecution - In instant case, charges of cruelty or harassment against appellants were not supported by legal evidence on record - There was no evidence of demand for dowry by appellants prior to alleged demand - Courts below failed to notice that prosecution failed to prove that dowry demand was made by appellants either at the time of marriage or subsequently as it has not produced convincing and cogent evidence in that regard - Evidence on record was not clear as to whether demand as alleged to was made by appellants was a demand for dowry with the deceased that could constitute cruelty or harassment by appellants - Courts below failed to consider the relevant fact that appellants were not in the house at the time of incident - Impugned judgment of HC was reversed by setting aside conviction of appellants - Appeals allowed.
