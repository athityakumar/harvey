Sondur Gopal and another v Sondur Rajini and another

Supreme Court of India

15 July 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Chandramauli Kumar Prasad, V. Gopala Gowda

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 438; (2013) 7 SCC 426; AIR 2013 SC 2678; 2013 (5) ALD(SC) 97; 2013(5) ALL MR 939; 2013 (100) ALR 260; 2013 (5) ALT(SC) 1; 2013 (6) AWC 5627; 2013 (5) Bom.C.R. 21; 2013 (3) CLT(SC) 169; 2013 (4) CTC 527; 2014 (2) G.L.R. 1175; JT 2013 (10) SC 535; 2013 (3) KLT 659; 2013(5) M.P.H.T. 1; 2013 (3) RCR(Civil) 979; 2013(9) SCALE 372; [2013] 10 S.C.R. 706

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Summary: Hindu Marriage Act, 1955-Sections 10, 1 (2) and 2 (1)-Judicial separation-Petition by wife for judicial separation and for custody of minor children-Maintainability-Domicile of origin and domicile of choice-Jurisdiction of Family Court in India-Application of provisions of Hindu Marriage Act-Plea of husband that both he and his wife are citizens of Sweden presently domiciled in Australia which is their domicile of choice and having abandoned domicile of origin, i.e., India, jurisdiction of Family Court, Mumbai is barred by provisions of Section 1 (2)-Case of wife is that both she and her husband are domiciled in India and, therefore, Family Court in Mumbai has jurisdiction to entertain petition filed by her seeking decree for judicial separation and custody of children-No material to endorse plea of husband's claim of being domicile of Australia-Person who alleges it has to prove that-No dispute that wife at time of presentation of petition was resident of India-No whisper at all as to how and in what manner husband had abandoned domicile of origin-Difficult to accept case of husband that he is domiciled in Australia and he shall continue to be domicile of origin, i.e., India-Question that wife shall follow domicile of husband is rendered academic-Held-Both husband and wife are domicile of India and, hence, shall be covered by provisions of Hindu Marriage Act-Both husband and wife are domicile of India-Hindu Marriage Act will apply to them-Other contentions raised on behalf of parties are rendered academic-Petition filed by wife for judicial separation and custody of children in Family Courts, Mumbai-Maintainable.
