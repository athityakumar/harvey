I. S. Sikandar (Dead) by Lrs. v K. Subramani and others

Supreme Court of India

29 August 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 862; (2013) 15 SCC 27; 2013 (6) AWC 6364; 2014 (1) OLR 504; 2014 (1) RCR(Civil) 237; 2014(1) SCALE 1

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: (1) Specific Relief Act, 1963-Sections 16 (c), 20 and 21 (2)-Suit for specific performance of agreement of sale in favour of plaintiff/respondent and for grant of decree of permanent injunction in respect to suit schedule property-Mandatory on part of plaintiff to prove his readiness and willingness to get decree for specific performance of suit schedule property in his favour-Plaintiff was defaulter-Plaintiff was not ready and willing to perform his part of contract to purchase suit schedule property by paying remaining sale consideration amount to defendants/appellants as per sale agreement as he had been seeking time without justification-Nothing on record to show that plaintiff could have made arrangement for payment of balance consideration amount to defendants-Even though agreement of sale is proved-In view of statutory provisions of Sections 16 (c), 20 (1) and (2) and 21 (2), plaintiff not entitled for decree of specific performance in respect of suit schedule property-Plaintiff had lost right to seek decree of specific performance-Grant of decree for specific performance by High Court in impugned judgment-Wholly unsustainable in law-Trial court came to right conclusions on contentious issues framed by it keeping in view law laid down by Apex Court in P. R. Deb and Associates v. Sunanda Roy, (1996) 4 SCC 423-Impugned judgment and decree of High Court passed in regular first appeal-Set aside-Judgment and decree of trial court-Restored.(2) Specific Relief Act, 1963-Section 16 (c)-Suit for specific performance of contract for sale-Readiness and willingness on part of plaintiff to perform his part of contract to get sale-deed executed in his favour-Mandatory requirement as per Section 16 (c)-Duty of court-Court to see conduct of party as well as attending circumstances of case regarding whether readiness and willingness of plaintiff can be inferred.
