Ranjit Singh v State of Madhya Pradesh and others

Supreme Court of India

27 September 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 640; (2013) 16 SCC 797; 2013 (4) RCR(Criminal) 600; 2013(12) SCALE 190; [2013] 11 S.C.R. 273

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 307, 302/34, 147, 148, 149, 120-B - Arms Act, 1959, ss. 25 and 27 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 439(2) - Bail - Grant of - Appellant was charged in a case u/ss. 307, 302/34, 147, 148, 149, 120-B IPC and ss. 25 and 27 of the Act - Appellant filed application for anticipatory bail - Single Judge directed appellant to apply for regular bail - Later, Trial Court granted bail with some condition - However, DB set aside regular bail granted by Trial Court - On appeal, SC set aside DB order - Respondent No. 3 informant filed fresh application for cancellation of bail u/s. 439(2) CrPC - Single Judge cancelled bail - Hence, instant appeal - Held, order read in entirety clearly reflected that Trial Court had an erroneous perception and fallacious understanding of earlier order passed by HC directing appellant to apply for regular bail - Regular bail was granted on the bedrock of order passed by HC - Trial Court absolutely misconstrued order - Thus, Trial Court order was totally unjustified and illegal - If order granting bail was a perverse one or passed on irrelevant materials, it should be annulled by superior court - Hence, order of Trial Court was set aside and impugned order of Single Judge was modified - However, liberty was granted to appellant to move an application for grant of regular bail - Appeal disposed of.
