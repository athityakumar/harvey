Veer Singh and others v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

10 December 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

C. Nagappan, Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 817; (2014) 2 SCC 455; 2014 CRLJ 1083; 2014 (1) MLJ(Crl) 45; 2014 (1) RCR(Criminal) 863; 2013(15) SCALE 162

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Cries

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 147,148, 307 r/w ss. 149 and 452 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 164 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 155, 157 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellants (accused) were tried for alleged offences u/ss. 147,148, 307 r/w s. 302 r/w ss. 149 and 452 of IPC - Trial Court found appellants guilty and convicted for charge u/s. 302 r/w s. 149 of IPC and sentenced them to death - Aggrieved appellants filed an appeal before HC - HC by common judgment allowed appeals filed by appellants and rejected the Reference regarding death penalty and acquitted appellants of all charges and dismissed appeal preferred by State - However, SC remitted the matter to HC for fresh hearing - HC by impugned common judgment commuted death sentence recorded against appellants to one of life imprisonment - Aggrieved appellants filed instant appeals - Whether impugned judgment passed by HC was justified - Held, from the testimony it becomes evident that PW4 (sole eye-witness) had witnessed the occurrence and also sustained grievous injuries - Immediately after the occurrence in the morning itself PW4 was admitted in hospital for treatment and information was sent to Magistrate for recording her dying declaration - Further it was not a dying declaration since she survived and it was only a statement u/s. 164 of CrPC which could be used u/s. 157 of 1872 Act for the purpose of corroboration and u/s. 155 of 1872 Act for the purpose of contradiction - PW4 had witnessed the brutal murder of all her family members by appellants and other accused during the occurrence and when she was in a state of shock in hospital she had given answer to the-question put by Magistrate - After regaining her health when she was examined by IO (PW18), she has stated the entire occurrence naming the assailants and attack made by them with weapons - There was intrinsic evidence available on record which lends credence to her testimony - Testimony of PW4 was cogent, credible and trustworthy and has a ring of truth and deserves acceptance - All 12 victims of occurrence died of homicidal violence was established by the oral testimony of doctors who conducted autopsies on their bodies and the certificates issued by them to that effect - HC rightly sustained the conviction on the appellants and sentence awarded to them was also proper - Appeals dismissed.
