Smt. K. Vijaya Lakshmi v Govt. Of Andhra Pradesh Represented By Its Secretary Home (Courts C1) Department And Another

Supreme Court of India

18 February 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Gokhale, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 96; (2013) 5 SCC 489; AIR 2013 SC 3589; 2013 (3) ALD(SC) 97; JT 2013 (3) SC 178; 2013(2) SCALE 557; [2013] 4 S.C.R. 364; 2013 (2) SLR 286

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Service

Keywords: Anganbari Sevika, Practicing, Resolutions

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Practice & Procedure - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 14, 234 - Right to Information Act, 2005 - Andhra Pradesh State Judicial Service Rules, 2007 - Appointment of Judges - Selection list - Appointment Letter - Prohibited Organizations - Sustainability - Respondent No.2-HC had invited applications for appointments to 105 posts of (Junior) Civil Judges, written examination was conducted and those qualified, were called for an interview appellant was one of those who were selected, and her name figured at S.No.26 in list of selected candidates from general category but she was not issued appointment letter - Appellant made application under provisions of Act, to find out the reason of her non-appointment - She received reply stating that her husband had close links with prohibited organization as such she was not appointed - Aggrieved appellant filed writ petition to declare non-inclusion of her name in list of Junior Civil Judges as illegal, arbitrary and in violation of art. 14 of Constitution, and consequently a direction be issued to respondents to forthwith issue an order of appointment to her - Respondents contested alleging that appellant too had close links with the CPI (Maoist) party - HC, Administration was informed through a letter that appellant had links with a prohibited organisation, Govt. at its own level had taken decision in this matter that candidature of appellant could not be considered due to adverse report, and conveyed it to HC, which was accepted, as it was, merely stating that it had no role to play since Respondent No 1 was appointing authority - Hence instant appeal - Appellant contended that respondents had changed their stand from time to time, they had stated that husband of appellant was having close links with prohibited organization, subsequently, it alleged that appellant was also having connection with same party, and lastly it was said that she was a member of CMS, which was named to be a Maoist Frontal Organization, but no material had been produced - Whether non-inclusion of name of appellant was arbitrary, illegal and in violation of art. 14 of Constitution and whether direction could be issued for issuing appointment letter - Held, in view of this constitutional and legal framework, SC, was clearly of view that HC had erred firstly on administrative side in discharging its responsibility u/art. 234 of Constitution, and then on Judicial side in dismissing writ petition, by drawing an erroneous conclusion - Court could not grant mandamus sought by appellant to issue an appointment order in her favour - Court could examine whether there was any infirmity in decision making process as final decision with respect to selection was however to be left with appropriate authority - Division Bench ought to have directed State Govt. to place all police papers before HC on administrative side, to enable it to take appropriate decision, after due consideration thereof - Accordingly, judgment and order rendered by DB was set-aside, respondent No. 1- State Govt. was directed to place police report for consideration of HC on administrative side - Appeal disposed of.
