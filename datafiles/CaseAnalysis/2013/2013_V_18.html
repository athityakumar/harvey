Singareni Collieries Company Limited v Vemuganti Ramakrishan Rao and others

Supreme Court of India

29 August 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

T. S. Thakur, Vikramajit Sen

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 548; (2013) 8 SCC 789; AIR 2013 SC (Supp) 167; 2014 (1) ALD(SC) 68; 2013(6) ALL MR 415; 2013 (100) ALR 674; 2014 (1) AWC 24; 2013 (4) CLT(SC) 31; 2013 (5) CTC 446; JT 2013 (11) SC 539; 2013 (6) MahLJ 860; 2013 (4) MPLJ 511; 2013 (121) RD 254; 2013(10) SCALE 627; [2013] 9 S.C.R. 658

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Administrative - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss.11A and 28 - Acquisition proceedings - Award of acquisition made beyond the period of two years - Validity - Appeals arose out of a judgment and order passed by HC whereby HC had dismissed the Writ Appeal and the review petition filed by the appellant holding that the LAO/Collector, Land Acquisition having made the award beyond the period of two years stipulated in s.11A of Act, the acquisition proceedings initiated by the authorities had lapsed - Petitioner contended that omission of a specific provision in s.11A of Act excluding the time taken in obtaining a copy of the order passed by the Court was casus omissus and that HC could while interpreting the said provision supply the unintended omission of the Parliament - There was no merit in that contention - HC said so because while applying the doctrine of casus omissus the Court had to look at the entire enactment and the scheme underlying the same -

Held, in the case at hand, HC found that Parliament had, wherever it intended, specifically provided for exclusion of time requisite for obtaining a copy of the order - For instance, u/s.28A of Act which provided for re-determination of the amount of compensation on the basis of the Award of the Court, the aggrieved party was entitled to move a written application to the Collector within three months from the date of the award of the Court or the Collector requiring him to determine the amount of compensation payable to him on the basis of the amount awarded by the Court - Proviso to s.28A of Act specifically excluded the time requisite for obtaining a copy of the award while computing the period of three months within which the application shall be made to the Collector - Absence of a provision analogous to proviso to s.28A of Act in the scheme of s.11A of Act militates against the argument that the omission of such a provision in s.11A of Act was unintended which could be supplied by the Court taking resort to the doctrine of casus omissus - In the instant case no ambiguity nor any apparent omission in s.11A of Act to justify application of the doctrine of casus omissus and by that route re-write s.11A of Act providing for exclusion of time taken for obtaining a copy of the order which exclusion was not currently provided by the said provision - Omission of a provision u/s.11A of Act analogous to the proviso u/s.28A of Act was obviously not unintended or inadvertent which was the very essence of the doctrine of casus omissus - SC, therefore, had no hesitation in rejecting the contention urged by appellant - HC was in the circumstances perfectly justified in holding that the award made by the Collector/Land Acquisition Officer was non est and that the acquisition proceedings had elapsed by reason of a breach of s.11A of Act - SC, however, made it clear that the declaration granted by HC and proceedings initiated by the Collector should be deemed to have elapsed only qua the writ petitioners - Appeals dismissed.
