Econ Antri Limited v Rom Industries Limited and another

Supreme Court of India

26 August 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjana Prakash Desai, Ranjan Gogoi, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 538; (2014) 11 SCC 769; AIR 2013 SC 3283; 2013 (3) BC 735; 2013 (5) Bom.C.R. 481; 2013 CRLJ 4195; 2013 (5) CTC 318; 2013 (202) DLT 193; 2014 (1) JabLJ 1; 2013 (4) JCC (NI) 193; JT 2013 (11) SC 582; 2013 (3) KLT 972; 2013(5) M.P.H.T. 438; 2013 (4) RCR(Criminal) 22; 2014 (1) RLW 446; 2013(10) SCALE 555

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Sheriff, Road Traffic Act, 1930, Limitation Act, 1939, Limitation Act, 1961, Bankruptcy Act, 1890

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, s. 142(b) - Limitation Act, 1963 - General Clauses Act, 1897, s. 9 - Cheque case - Limitation - Period to be reckoned - Instant matter was referred to larger Bench seeking reconsideration of law laid down by SC in Saketh India Ltd. & Ors. vs. India Securities Ltd., 1999 INDLAW SC 414 - Whether for calculating period of one month which was prescribed u/s. 142(b) of 1882 Act, the period had to be reckoned by excluding date on which cause of action arose - Held, reasoning of SC in Saketh case was based on various English decisions and SC decision in Haru Das Gupta vs. State of West Bengal, 1972 INDLAW SC 391, which aptly laid down and explained principle that where a particular time was given from a certain date within which an act should be done, the day of the date was to be excluded - As 1963 Act was held to be not applicable to 1882 Act in Tarun Prasad Chatterjee v Dinanath Sharma, 2000 INDLAW SC 2815, with the aid of s. 9 of 1897 Act, it could be safely concluded that while calculating period of one month which was prescribed u/s. 142(b) of 1881 Act, period should be reckoned by excluding date on which cause of action arose - Thus, it was not possible to hold that word 'of' occurring in s.138(c) and 142(b) of 1881 Act should be interpreted differently as against the word 'from' occurring in s. 138(a) of the same Act - Hence, for the purpose of calculating period of one month, which was prescribed u/s. 142(b) of 1881 Act, period should be reckoned by excluding date on which cause of action arose - Saketh case laid down correct proposition of law - SIL Import, USA vs. Exim Aides Silk Exporters, Bangalore, 1999 Indlaw SC 1405, overruled - Reference answered.
