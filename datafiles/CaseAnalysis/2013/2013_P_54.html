Bhadragiri Venkata Ravi v Public Prosecutor High Court Of A.P., Hyderabad

Supreme Court of India

29 May 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 345; (2013) 14 SCC 145; 2014 (2) CGLJ 183; 2013 CRLJ 3665; 2013 (4) JCC 2600; JT 2013 (8) SC 174; 2013 (3) MLJ(Crl) 181; 2013(7) SCALE 458; [2013] 6 S.C.R. 529

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Consistency in dying declarations

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 498-A and 302 r/w s. 201 - Murder - Consistency in dying declarations - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant (accused) and deceased (wife) were separated by a decree of divorce - Appellant allegedly killed his wife and was charge sheeted for offences u/ss. 302 and 498A of IPC along with his parents - Trial Court acquitted appellant and other accused holding that there prosecution could not prove case and that there was no evidence to show the involvement of accused - Aggrieved (State) filed an appeal before HC - HC by impugned judgment dismissed appeal against parents of appellant and convicted appellant for offence u/s. 302 and sentenced him to undergo imprisonment for life - Aggrieved appellant filed instant appeal - Appellant contended that it was a case of accident and that deceased was tutored by her mother and in dying declaration, appellant and his parents were enroped in the offence - Whether HC was justified in reversing acquittal judgment passed by Trial Court - Held, it was a settled legal proposition that in case there were apparent discrepancies in two dying declarations, it would be unsafe to convict the accused - In such a fact-situation, accused gets the benefit of doubt - In case there were inter-se discrepancies in depositions of witnesses given in support of one of dying declarations, it would not be safe to rely upon the same - In fact it was not the plurality of dying declarations but the reliability thereof that adds weight to the prosecution case - If dying declaration was found to be voluntary, reliable and made in a fit mental condition, it could be relied upon without any corroboration - But statements should be consistent throughout - In instant case, admittedly, there was a divorce between parties - Thus, question of demand of dowry or ill-treatment or harassment did not arise after 8 years of divorce decree by Court - First two dying declarations were made in Govt. Headquarter Hospital, and the Magistrate had reached there on being called by police - There was no inconsistency between the first two dying declarations and it was evident from the said dying declarations recorded on 15-4-2000 that both of them were recorded in the Govt. Headquarter Hospital - Third dying declaration made it evident that on 15-4-2000 deceased was not taken to Govt. Hospital and her in-laws were not available on 14-4-2000 - Her husband (appellant) was treating her at home and had also given her injections for two-three days - Her parents-in-laws reached on 15-4-2000 and then she was admitted to private hospital on 16-4-2000 - As she could not recover therein, then she was transferred to Govt. Headquarter Hospital, on that day - HC did not consider the matter in correct perspective nor observed the parameters laid down by SC to interfere against the order of acquittal - Impugned judgment of HC was set aside - Appeal allowed.
