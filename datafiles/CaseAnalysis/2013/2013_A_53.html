S. Kesari Hanuman Goud v Anjum Jehan and others

Supreme Court of India

10 April 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 218; (2013) 12 SCC 64; AIR 2013 SC (Supp) 265; 2013 (4) ALD(SC) 150; 2013(3) ALL MR 916; 2013 (98) ALR 241; 2013 (3) ALT 52; 2013 (3) CLT(SC) 114; JT 2013 (8) SC 200; 2014 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 2100; 2014 (2) RCR(Civil) 52; 2013 (119) RD 796; 2013(5) SCALE 520; [2013] 2 S.C.R. 750

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Civil Procedure - Urban Land Ceiling Act, 1976 - Specific performance of contract - Entitlement of - Appellant/plaintiff was carrying on business prior to 1-1-1978 in the appurtenant land as a tenant, and had made an offer to purchase the said premises, along with two other premises belonging to landlady respondent No.1/defendant No.1 - Parties entered into an agreement dt.15-10-1977, for the sale of land admeasuring 1200 square yards for a total consideration of Rs.1,70,070/- - Out of which a sum of Rs.25,000/- was paid as earnest money - After the execution of said agreement to sell, appellant/plaintiff paid non-agricultural assessment tax - A legal notice dt.18-6-1979 was received by appellant from Res. No.1 stating that she had obtained requisite permission from statutory authorities under the Act from income tax authorities, and also from the sub-divisional authorities - It was case of appellant that instead of executing sale-deed in favour of appellant/plaintiff, Respondent No.1 tried to sell suit property to other persons - Therefore, appellant/plaintiff got a public notice in local newspapers in respect of suit property - Despite said notice, GPA holder of respondent No.1 entered into 2different agreements to sell with respondent no. 2/defendant no.3 on 29/30-4-1980, for open land admeasuring 510 square yards - Appellant/plaintiff filed suit for specific performance of agreement to sell dt.15-10-1977, directing Respondent No.1 to execute a registered sale deed in favour of appellant/plaintiff - Respondent no.3/Defendant No. 7 filed suit for perpetual injunction, restraining appellant/plaintiff from interfering with construction of a theatre building, including compound wall of the same, which was in close proximity to his land - Trial court decreed the suit of appellant/plaintiff except for a small area admeasuring 65 square yards, which was purchased by defendant no.6, observing that said defendant had no knowledge of any agreement to sell between appellant/plaintiff and respondent No.1 - Trial Court also dismissed suit filed by respondent no.3/defendant No.7 - On appeals, HC disposed of said appeals and held that appellant/plaintiff was not ready and willing to perform his part of contract, thus, in view of the same, there was no occasion to decide issues regarding whether subsequent purchasers were in fact, bonafide purchasers for consideration without notice of agreement to sell between appellant/plaintiff and respondent No.1 - However, appellant/plaintiff would be entitled to get the sale deed executed in respect of said land, excluding land sold to defendant nos.3, 6 and 7 at the rate of Rs.750/- per square yard, adjusting amount that was already paid - Respondent no. 1 filed review before HC - HC dismissed said review - Hence instant appeals - Appellant contended that HC had committed error in appreciating evidence on record, and coming to conclusion that appellant/plaintiff was not ready and willing to perform his part of contract, as appellant/plaintiff was in fact issuing public notices, with intention of making other people aware of the fact that they should not indulge in any kind of transaction in respect of the suit property, as the same belonged to him - Held, so far as the notice of agreement between appellant and respondent No. 1 was concerned, Trial Court after taking note of the recital of said agreement in the agreement to sell and sale deed also, had held, that, so far as the land sold to respondents other than respondent no.6, the parties were fully aware of the same - Only respondent no.6 had no such notice - Further, appellant had not yet acquired any title over the land, he had no right to receive compensation to the tune of Rs. 29,47,112/- - However, he withdrew the said amount by giving an undertaking to return the said amount to respondent No. 1 in case any such order was passed by Court in this regard - Order of HC was set aside and appellant was entitled to get the sale deed executed and registered, with respect to all the suit land available - Appeals disposed of.
