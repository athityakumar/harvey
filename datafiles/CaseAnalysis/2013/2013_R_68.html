Venkatesan v Rani and another

Supreme Court of India

19 August 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjan Gogoi

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 514; (2013) 14 SCC 207; AIR 2013 SC 3320; 2014 (2) CGLJ 142; 2013 CRLJ 4208; 2013 (5) CTC 439; 2013 (4) JCC 2550; JT 2013 (11) SC 328; 2013 (4) RCR(Criminal) 919; 2013 (4) RLW 3402; 2013(10) SCALE 442; [2013] 9 S.C.R. 105

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 302B, 498A - Murder - Dowry death - Conviction - Sustainability - Deceased was married to appellant-accused - Deceased was inflicted with mental and physical cruelty by accused for demand of dowry - Accused maltreated deceased which caused her death - Accused was charged for offences punishable u/ss. 302, 498A, 304B IPC - Trial Court acquitted accused - Appeal before HC who held that order of acquittal passed by Trial Court suffered from certain inherent flaws which justified reversal and for remission of matter for fresh decision in accordance with law - Hence instant appeal - Whether principles governing the exercise of the aforesaid jurisdiction have been rightly determined by HC - Held, prosecution case was accused had poured kerosene on deceased and thereafter set her on fire and had gagged her mouth with piece of cloth to prevent her from screaming, which version had been unfolded by PWs 1, 2, 3 and 4, it was difficult to see as to how charge against accused u/s. 304B IPC could be sustained - Evidence of PW12 -IO was that investigation did not reveal any harassment and ill-treatment of deceased by accused prior to her death made prosecution case against accused u/s. 498A IPC wholly unsustainable - Offence u/s. 302 IPC was concerned, there was no eye witness to occurrence - PW-1 to PW-4 though examined as eye witnesses should not be understood to have actually witnessed any of events that would be crucial for determination of liability of accused - There was contradiction in evidence of PWs 1 and 2 on one hand and PW-12 on other - Evidence of PW10-doctor was that deceased herself had stated that she was injured due to bursting of stove while she was cooking casted further doubt on prosecution story - Absence of proof of seizure of material objects, made and contradiction between oral testimony and contents with regard to actual place of occurrence, was demolished credibility of prosecution version - Order of acquittal passed by Trial Court and reversed ordered by HC did not commend - Appeal allowed.
