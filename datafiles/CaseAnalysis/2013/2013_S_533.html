Subhasish Mondal @ Bijoy v State of West Bengal

Supreme Court of India

21 November 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 751; (2014) 4 SCC 180; 2015 ALL MR (Cri) 1219; 2014 CRLJ 1824; 2013(14) SCALE 166; [2013] 12 S.C.R. 435

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Double murder

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Double murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant (accused) was charged with murder of his brother and mother - Trial Court found appellant guilty of the murder u/s. 302 of IPC and sentenced him to life imprisonment - Aggrieved appellant filed an appeal before HC, which was dismissed by impugned judgment - Hence, instant appeal - Appellant contended that Court should acquit appellant on benefit of doubt and question of improbability of the involvement of appellant - Whether impugned conviction of appellant was justified - Held, from evidence it was apparent that appellant had a clear motive to have committed the brutal murder of his elder brother and his mother and the circumstances point to the guilt of appellant - Appellant held a strong grudge against his mother and elder brother as his mother had given the name of his brother for the job of his deceased father instead of his name - Motive of vengeance was established and in cases in which only circumstantial evidence was available, motive assumes a great importance - There was no attempt of explanation of circumstances or plea of alibi on the part of appellant - Appellant simply entered a plea of innocence - Appellant was seen loitering around after the occurrence and silver chain that he took from his sister (PW 2) was found at the site of murder all point to the guilt of appellant - Impugned conviction of appellant was justified - Appeal dismissed.
