Shahid Balwa v Union of India and others

Supreme Court of India

03 September 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 555; (2014) 2 SCC 687; AIR 2013 SC (Supp) 821; 2013(5) ALL MR 960; JT 2013 (12) SC 90; 2013 (3) MLJ(Crl) 725; 2013(11) SCALE 75; [2013] 11 S.C.R. 51

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: (A) Criminal - Constitution - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 136, 142, 226, 227 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 482 - 2G Spectrum Scam - Investigation monitored by SC - Violation of arts. 226. 227 - SC monitored investigation by Central Bureau of Investigation (CBI) into 2G Spectrum Scam - SC ordered that no other courts should pass any order, which impede investigation - CBI conducted investigation and submitted charge-sheet - SC ordered, by its vide order dt. 11-4-2011, to restrain other courts from entertaining any objection with regard to appointment of Special Public Prosecutor - In the meantime, large number of petitions filed before HC for staying trial proceedings - SC, by another order dt. 9-11-2012, stayed pending proceedings before HC - Hence, instant petitions seeking recall orders dt. 11-4-2011 and dt. 9-11-2012 - Petitioner contended that impugned orders would violate rights guaranteed to petitioners u/s. 482 of CrPC and arts. 226 and 227 of Constitution for moving HC - Held, in case petitioners had any grievance, they were free to approach SC so that progress of trial would not be hampered by indulging in cumbersome and time consuming proceedings in other Forums, thereby stultifying preemptory SC direction for day-to-day trial - Art.136 r/w art. 142 of Constitution enabled SC to pass such orders, which were necessary for doing complete justice in any cause or matter pending before it and, any order so made, should be enforceable throughout territory of India - Parties, in such a case, could not invoke jurisdiction u/arts. 226 or 227 of Constitution or u/s. 482 CrPC so as to interfere with those orders passed by SC, in exercise of its Constitutional powers conferred u/art. 136 r/w art. 142 of Constitution - If parties would move Courts inferior, it would defeat very purpose and object of various orders passed by SC in exercise of its powers u/arts. 136 r/w art. 142 of Constitution - Petitions dismissed.

(B) Criminal - Constitution - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 32, 136, 139A, 142, 226, 227 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 482 - 2G Spectrum Scam - Investigation monitored by SC - Public interest - Whether SC was justified in monitoring investigation in instant case on public interest - Held, public interest compelled SC to take up investigation in 2G related cases in exercise of its powers u/arts. 136 r/w art. 142 of Constitution, that too, on a request made by Central Govt. - When larger public interest was involved, it was responsibility of Constitutional Court to assure judicial legitimacy and accountability - Larger public interest should have precedence over prayers of petitioners, especially when SC had safeguarded their rights and given freedom to them to move SC, either u/art. 136 or art. 32 of Constitution - Art. 139A also reflected larger public interest, which enabled SC to transfer certain cases which involved substantial questions of law, from one HC to another or to SC, in such an event, it could not be contended that parties were deprived of their rights to adjudicate their grievances u/arts. 226, 227 or s. 482 CrPC, before HC - Petitions dismissed.

(C) Criminal - Constitution - Practice & Procedure - 2G Spectrum Scam - Monitoring of investigation by SC - Scope - Whether monitoring of investigation by SC would have the effect of monitoring trial proceedings - Held, once charge-sheet was submitted in proper Court, process of Court monitoring investigation would come to an end and it was for that Court to take cognizance of offence and deal with matter - In the instant case, charge-sheet was filed only in one among various 2G related cases - SC, while passing impugned order, only directed speedy trial and, that too, on a day-to-day basis which could not be termed as interference with trial proceedings - Hence, no basis was found in petitioners' contention that orders dt. 11-4-2011 and dt. 9-11-2012 had the effect of monitoring trial proceedings - No Court, other than Court seized with trial, had power to monitor proceedings pending before it - Order dt. 11-4-2011 only facilitated progress of trial by ordering that trial should proceed on a day-to-day basis - No error was found in orders passed by SC on 11-4-2011 and 9-11-2012 - Petitions dismissed.

(D) Criminal - Constitution - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 21 - 2G Spectrum Scam - Monitoring of investigation by SC - Guidelines - Requirement of - Whether SC ought to frame any guidelines in relation to monitoring of criminal investigation - Held, a superior court exercising appellate power or Constitutional power, if gives a direction to conduct trial on day-to-day basis or complete trial in a specific time by giving direction is not interfering with trial proceedings but only facilitating speedy trial, which was a facet of art. 21 of Constitution - Hence, no good reason was found to frame guidelines to be followed by a Constitutional court in relation to monitoring of criminal investigation - Petitions dismissed.
