M/S A.S. Motors Pvt. Ltd. v Union Of India & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

21 February 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, Gyan Sudha Misra

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 106; (2013) 10 SCC 114; 2013 (5) ALD(SC) 106; 2013 (2) AWC 1834; 2013 (1) CLT(SC) 341; 2013 (2) CTC 804; 2013 (2) JabLJ 163; JT 2013 (3) SC 316; 2013 (2) RCR(Civil) 323; 2013(3) SCALE 15; [2013] 4 S.C.R. 409; 2013 (4) WLN(SC) 77

Case Digest

Subject: Contract & Commercial

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Indian Contract Act, 1872 - Violation of contract - Invocation of bank guarantee - Validity - Respondent/National Highway Authority of India Ltd. (NHAI) invited tenders for award of a contract for collection of fee for use of National Highways - Appellant made an offer which was accepted by NHAI asking appellant to submit a demand draft for a sum of Rs.2,20,00,125/- towards performance security and a bank guarantee for a similar amount to be valid for a period of 15 months - Due to certain violations noticed by NHAI it terminated contract of appellant - Appellant filed writ petition against said order before HC - Single Judge held that termination of contract and forfeiture of performance security for breaches committed by appellant were perfectly justified in light of report submitted by agency deployed by respondent to collect material regarding overcharging of fee and other violations committed by appellant - On appeal, DB dismissed appeal - Hence, instant appeal - Whether action for termination of contract was done by competent authority and whether cancellation of contract was based on proof of breach committed by petitioner - Held, appellant had breached contractual stipulations, harassed innocent citizens to cough up more than what they were in law required to pay and thus undeservedly enriched itself before it turned to Court to claim relief in HC on equitable considerations - There was nothing on record whether any such estimation was made by Authority and if so basis on which that was done - Thus, failure of Authority to estimate accurately could jeopardise its claim for recovery by a simple invocation of bank guarantee, it was not open to respondent to invoke bank guarantee and recover entire amount of Rs.2,20,00,125/- covered by same - HC was, correct in holding that invocation of bank guarantee was not justified having regard to fact that Authority had already recovered penalty levied by it and also forfeited performance security amount of Rs.2,20,00,125/- in form of bank drafts furnished by appellant - Hence, appellant could ought to have sought its remedies in a proper civil action if it questioned reasonableness of amount recoverable by appellant in terms of contractual stipulations - Appeal dismissed.
