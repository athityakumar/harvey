State of Punjab v Madan Mohan Lal Verma

Supreme Court of India

12 August 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, Sharad Arvind Bobde

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 497; (2013) 14 SCC 153; AIR 2013 SC 3368; 2013 CRLJ 4050; 2013 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 3182; 2013 (3) MLJ(Crl) 565; 2013 (3) RCR(Criminal) 972; 2014 (1) RLW 438; 2013(10) SCALE 211; [2013] 7 S.C.R. 1130; [2013] 217 TAXMAN 402

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, ss.7, 13(1)(d), 13(2) - Demand of illegal gratification - Acquittal - Sustainability - Respondent Income-tax Inspector allegedly demanded illegal gratification from PW7 complainant - Trial Court convicted respondent u/s. 7 and 13(1)(d) r/w s. 13(2) of the Act - HC, on appeal, set aside Trial Court order and acquitted respondent - Hence, instant appeal by State - Held, demand of illegal gratification was sine qua non for constituting an offence under the Act - Mere recovery of tainted money was not sufficient to convict accused when substantive evidence in the case was not reliable, unless there was evidence to prove payment of bribe or to show that money was taken voluntarily as a bribe - Mere receipt of amount by accused was not sufficient to fasten guilt, in the absence of any evidence with regard to demand and acceptance of amount as illegal gratification - In the instant case, complainant went along with PW3 shadow witness to PW12, DCP and plan for trap was prepared and trap was laid - PW2 Executive Magistrate categorically stated that he had been directed by Deputy Commissioner in writing on 31-5-1994 to join trap party on 1-6-1994 - Hence, it was evident that in case complainant himself had gone to PW12 for having a trap on 1-6-1994, question of receiving a direction from Deputy Commissioner on 31-5-1994 could not arise - PW2 was a witness only of recovery and not of accepting the bribe money - This statement alone made it evident that prosecution had not disclosed genesis of case correctly - No cogent reasons found to interfere with the HC order - Appeal dismissed.
