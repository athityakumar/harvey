Gangabhavani v Rayapati Venkat Reddy and others

Supreme Court of India

04 September 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, Sharad Arvind Bobde

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 560; (2013) 15 SCC 298; AIR 2013 SC 3681; 2013 CRLJ 4618; JT 2013 (12) SC 117; 2014 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 281; 2013 (3) MLJ(Crl) 708; 2013 (4) RCR(Criminal) 853; 2013(11) SCALE 132

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Cries

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 148 - Explosive Substances Act, 1908, s. 3 - Murder - Conviction - Reversal by HC - Sustainability - Respondents accused allegedly caused the death of deceased - Trial Court convicted and sentenced respondents u/ss. 302, 148 IPC and s. 3 of the Act - On appeal, HC acquitted respondents - Hence, instant appeals by State and complainants - Whether reversal of conviction by HC was sustainable - Held, there was sufficient evidence on record that deceased was hacked with a hunting sickle - PW6 doctor clearly deposed that injury could have been caused by a hunting sickle - Evidence of PW6 stood fully corroborated by version given by PWs 1 to 3 - Evidence on record revealed that respondents came armed with deadly weapons whistling war cries and chased deceased - HC erroneously observed that eye-witnesses did not speak of explosion of bombs by certain accused and they could also not have seen incident because of smoke from explosion - There was nothing on record to show that eye-witnesses' visibility was impaired due to emanating of smoke and the said finding recorded by HC was illogical - There was no discrepancy in the testimony of eye-witnesses with respect to overt acts of accused - Further, since PW1 was illiterate and a rustic villager and did not know the niceties of law, his testimony could not be doubted - Delay in filing FIR fully explained by prosecution and there was no occasion for HC to take it to be fatal to prosecution case - There could be no reason for eye-witnesses i.e. PWs 1 to 3, who had lost two of their family members, to falsely implicate respondents and spare real assailants - Hence, findings recorded by HC were set aside being perverse - Appeals allowed.
