Christian Medical College Vellore And Ors v Union Of India And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

13 March 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Vikramajit Sen

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 307; 2013(7) SCALE 79

Case Digest

Subject: Education

Summary: Education - Indian Medical Council Act, 1956, s. 3 - Notification - Bar imposed on declaring of results - Challenged - Notification published and issued by the Medical Council, notifying a National Eligibility Entrance Test (NEET) for admission to Post-Graduate Medical Courses conducted in colleges all across the country - Challenge to the said notification gave rise to a wide range of submissions involving the competence of Medical Council, constituted u/s. 3 of the Act, to introduce such a test which denudes the different medical colleges across the country from having any control over their entrance examinations and admissions on the basis thereof - Whether bar imposed by SC on declaration of results could be lifted up - Held, students, who aspire to gain entry into the medical colleges at the MBBS and BDS and the Post-Graduate levels, were caught in the legal tangle for no fault of theirs and were the victims of policy decisions - In order to safeguard their interests, as also the interest of hospitals, SC considered it just and equitable to lift the bar imposed by it on 13-12-2012, for current year's entrance examinations and, to that extent, order of 13-12-2012, and allow the results of examinations already conducted to be declared to enable the students to take advantage of the same for the current year - Order accordingly.
