Essa @ Anjum Abdul Razak Memon (A-3) and others v State Of Maharashtra Through Stf, Cbi Mumbai; (2) Suleman Abdul Razak And Others

Supreme Court of India

21 March 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 169; JT 2013 (6) SC 1; 2013(4) SCALE 1

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Terrorist Act, Bombay bomb blasts, Possession of illegal arms

Summary: (A) Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120-B, 302, 307, 326, 324, 427, 435, 436, 201 and 212 - Terrorist and Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Act, 1987, ss. 3(3), 3(2)(i)(ii), 3(3)(4), 5 and 6 - Arms Act, 1959, ss. 3, 7 r/w ss. 25 (1A) and 25 (1B)(a) - Explosives Act, 1884, s. 9B (1)(a)(b)(c) - Explosive Substances Act, 1908, ss. 3, 4(a)(b), 5 and 6 - Prevention of Damage to Public Property Act, 1984, s. 4 - Bombay bomb blasts - Terrorist Act - Possession of illegal arms - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellants accused (A-3, A-4, A-8) allegedly acquired and possessed arms and ammunition illegally in notified area as a part of criminal conspiracy with intent to aid terrorists for carrying out Bombay serial blast - Appellants allegedly committed offences punishable u/ss. 3(3), 3(2)(i)(ii), 3(3)(4), 5, 6 of the 1987 Act and ss. 120-B, 302, 307, 326, 324, 427, 435, 436, 201, 212 IPC and ss. 3, 7 r/w. ss. 25 (1A), 25 (1B)(a) of the 1959 Act and s. 9B (1)(a)(b)(c) of the 1884 Act and ss. 3, 4(a)(b), 5, 6 of the 1908 Act and s. 4 of the 1984 Act -Trial Court convicted appellants and sentenced to rigorous imprisonment for life under TADA for Bombay Blast Case - Aggrieved, appellants preferred instant appeals - Whether Trial Court was justified in convicting the appellants - Held, confessional statement of A-11 u/s. 15 of TADA was recorded by DCP and revealed that on night of 7-3-1993, when A-11 went to house of AA (Absconding Accused), AA was having dinner with A-1, A-3 and other members of A-8 - DCP recorded that statement of A-46, driver of AA, revealed that A-3 used to drive one white Maruti 800 car owned by AA - Deposition of PW87 also corroborates with confession of A-46 which established that the A-3 owned a small white Maruti car in addition to other cars - PW245, Immigration Officer deposed that A-3 departed from India on 11-3-1993 and he also recognized embarkation card ('X-405') issued to A-3 - Prosecution submitted the recoveries made from flats and garages of A-3 at building establish that said building was nucleus of criminal conspiracy to carry out explosions in Bombay on 12-3-1993 and meetings were held to prepare plans and hold discussions and lastly to fill RDX in vehicles, which were eventually used as bombs - Evidence on record establishes that A-8 was aware that vehicle owned by her was used for terrorist acts by AA and his associates - Further, arms and explosives smuggled into India for purpose of conspiracy were also kept at said building and lastly RDX was filled in vehicles in and outside the garages allocated to A-8 at Al-Hussaini building which were used/planted as bombs at various places on 12-3-1993 by all conspirators - However, evidence established that white car driven by A-3 was used for terrorist activities by AA and other co-accused persons - It was clear that A-3, A-4 and A-8, used to reside together in Flat at Al-Hussaini building and were present when several conspiratorial meetings took place in said flats - It was clear that A-3, A-4 and A-8 knew about conspiracy and facilitated commission of acts pursuant to said conspiracy - Appellants arrived at International Airport, New Delhi on 25-8-1994 and were arrested on same day after preliminary investigation by PW674 - PW266 and PW267, Immigration Officers, stamped disembarkation cards of A-4, A-8 and A-3 - It was, clear that recovery of Pakistani identity cards and Pakistani passports from appellants at time of their arrest at New Delhi Airport clearly proved that appellants had relocated to Dubai from Bombay just prior to blasts on 12-3-1993 and, thereafter, to Pakistan - However, conduct of appellants after blasts further established that appellants did not intend to co-operate with investigation authorities in India and instead travelled on a holiday to Bangkok from Karachi which was evident from passport entries made in Pakistani passports recovered from A-1 at time of his arrest at New Delhi Railway Station - After travelling to Bangkok, appellants arrived at New Delhi Airport via Dubai where they were arrested on 25-8-1994 by PW674 - Appellants A-3, A-4, and A-8 resided jointly at flats where AA, A-1 and their associates hatched the criminal conspiracy to carry out multiple explosions in Bombay - Conduct of appellants in not reporting any of these activities to police and fact that A-3 and A-4 departed from India on 11-3-1993 in itself is an incriminating circumstance to be used against appellants - None of appellants responded to proclamation requiring presence issued by Trial Court, which was given wide publicity - Trial Court was justified in convicting appellants - However, conclusion arrived at by Trial Court with respect to all other offences by A-3, A-8 and A-4 was upheld - Appeals disposed of.

(B) Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Terrorist and Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Act, 1987 - Arms Act, 1959 - Possession of illegal arms - Acquittal - Sustainability - Whether Trial Court was justified in acquitting A-71 - Held, Trial Court recorded a finding that there was no evidence on record to establish that the recovered contraband was smuggled by AA and his associates - Trial Court also recorded that evidence brought in had failed to establish precisely period for which A-71 was in possession of said contraband material and further, there was nothing on record to suggest for what purpose appellant was in possession of such a huge quantity of contraband - Trial Court held that evidence of officer from BDDS could not be termed to be an interested witness as said investigation was not affected at his behest - Evidence establishing the nature of contraband articles, same would lead to conclusion that A-71 was in possession of such article which he had kept in unused lavatory - Prosecution failed to establish relevant materials, viz., contraband, being part of material smuggled into India by AA for commission of terrorist act - Appeals disposed of.
