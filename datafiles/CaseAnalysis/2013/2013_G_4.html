G. Ravindranath alias R. Chowdary v E. Srinivas and another

Supreme Court of India

03 July 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, V. Gopala Gowda

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 856; (2013) 12 SCC 455; 2013 (5) AWC 4674

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Keywords: Expectation Of Life, Loss Of Expectation Of Life

Summary: (1) Motor Vehicles Act, 1988- Sections 166 and 168- Compensation- Injury case- Enhancement- Appellant, 19 years old at relevant time and was assisting his father in agricultural operations, suffered grievous injuries in accident- Accident is of year 2000- As a result of accident appellant sustained pelvic and urethral injuries and has become impotent- Appellant already undergone multiple surgeries and will have to take treatment in institutes like Nizam's Institute of Medical Sciences, Hyderabad for at least 10 years- Tribunal awarded meagre compensation- High Court also did not adequately enhance compensation determined by Tribunal- Appellant entitled to R 2,20,000 towards expenses incurred in treatment, R 6,00,000 for future medical expenses, R 3,00,000 for pain, suffering and trauma, R 4,00,000 for loss of amenities and marriage prospects, R 5,00,000 for loss of expectation of life and loss of future earning- Appellant entitled to total compensation of R 20,20,000 with interest @ 6% p.a.- Impugned judgment set aside- Appeal allowed.(2) Motor Accident Compensation- Personal injury case- Determination of compensation.

It is settled law that compensation in personal injury cases should be determined under the following heads:

Pecuniary damages (Special damages)

(i) Expenses relating to treatment, hospitalisation, medicines, transportation, nourishing food and miscellaneous expenditure.

(ii) Loss of earnings (and other gains) which the injured would have made had he not been injured, comprising:

(a) Loss of earning during the period of treatment;

(b) Loss of future earnings on account of permanent disability.

(iii) Future medical expenses.

Non-pecuniary damages (General damages)

(iv) Damages for pain, suffering and trauma as a consequence of the injuries.

(v) Loss of amenities (and/or loss of prospects of marriage).

(vi) Loss of expectation of life (shortening of normal longevity).

In routine personal injury cases, compensation will be awarded only under heads (i), (ii) (a) and

(iv). It is only in serious cases of injury, where there is specific medical evidence corroborating the evidence of the claimant, that compensation will be granted under any of the heads (ii) (b), (iii), (v) and (vi) relating to loss of future earnings on account of permanent disability, future medical expenses, loss of amenities (and/or loss of prospects of marriage) and loss of expectation of life.

Judgment and order dated 17.1.2012 of High Court of Karnataka, Circuit Bench at Gulbarga in M.F.A. No. 2478 of 2004 (M. V.), reversed.
