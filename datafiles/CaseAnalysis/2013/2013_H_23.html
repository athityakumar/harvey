Jagdish Singh v Heeralal and others

Supreme Court of India

30 October 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan, A.K. Sikri

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 720; (2014) 1 SCC 479; AIR 2014 SC 371; 2014 (1) ALD(SC) 46; 2014 (102) ALR 253; 2013 (6) AWC 6233; 2013 (4) BC 744; 2014 (1) CompLJ 307; 2014 (1) CTC 652; 2014 (1) DRTC 1; 2014 (1) JabLJ 345; 2014 (3) MahLJ 588; 2013 (8) MLJ 84; 2014 (2) MPLJ 560; 2014 (174) PLR 649; 2014 (122) RD 375; 2013(13) SCALE 359; [2013] 12 S.C.R. 232

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Securitisation and Reconstruction of Financial Assets and Enforcement of Security Interest Act, 2002-Sections 13 (4), 17 and 34-Possession of secured assets-Jurisdiction of civil court to entertain any suit or proceedings in respect of matters which fall under Section 13 (4)-Due to non-payment of loan amount, Bank can always proceed against secured assets-In case borrower fails to discharge his liability, Bank can take measures provided in Section 13 (4) for recovery of loan amount-Civil court in such circumstances has no jurisdiction to entertain any suit or proceedings-D.R.T. and Appellate Tribunal has jurisdiction to deal with matters which fall under Section 13 (4)-Section 34 ousts civil court jurisdiction-Civil court jurisdiction completely barred, so far as “measures” taken by secured creditor (Bank) under Section 13 (4)-Aggrieved person has right of appeal before D.R.T. or Appellate Tribunal for determination of legality in “measures” taken-Aggrieved person affected by action taken under Section 13 (4) can invoke provisions of Section 17 in case secured creditor (Bank) adopt any measure including sale of secured assets-High Court in error in holding that only civil court has jurisdiction to examine as to whether “measures” taken by secured creditor (Bank) under Section 13 (4) were legal or not-Impugned judgment of High Court-Set aside.
