State Bank Of India And Ors. v Narendra Kumar Pandey

Supreme Court of India

14 January 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 20; (2013) 2 SCC 740; AIR 2013 SC 904; 2013 (2) AWC 1926; 2013 (136) FLR 704; JT 2013 (1) SC 409; 2013 (2) LLJ 1; 2013(1) SCALE 385; [2013] 3 S.C.R. 1109; 2013 (2) SLR 1; 2013 (1) UPLBEC 305

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: the Service Rules

Summary: Service - Practice & Procedure - State Bank of India Act, 1955,s. 43(1) - State Bank of India Officers Service Rules, rr. 50(3)(4)(9),60(2),68,67,69 - Deliberate Violation - Chargesheet - Disciplinary Authority - Dismissal - Sustainability - Charged officer, while functioning as Deputy Manager of Appellant Bank was served with a charge-sheet dt. 15-02-1995 by Joint Manager (Operations) [Disciplinary Authority] stating that while he was posted as officer JMGS-I at Government Business Branch, and Accountant and officiating Branch Manager from 21-5-1985 to 20-10-1987 and 21-10-1987 to 22-5-1991, he had failed to discharge his duties with utmost integrity, honesty, devotion and diligence and acted in a manner unbecoming of a Bank Official and highly prejudicial to Bank's interest in deliberate violation of rr. 50(3), 50(4), 50(9), 60(2) of Service Rules - Charged officer was charged with 12 charges and departmental inquiry in terms of r. 68(2)(ii) r/w. 67 of Service Rules was conducted - Inquiry authority gave ample opportunities but officer remained absent and IA concluded exparte, holding charges proved against charged officer and that he had failed to discharge his duties with utmost integrity, honesty, devotion and diligence and acted in a manner unbecoming of a bank official and highly prejudicial to Bank's interest - Dismissal order was passed by Inquiry Authority in terms of r. 67(j) r/w r. 68 of Service Rules - Charge officer without availing remedy of statutory appeal filed writ petition before HC, took view that presenting officer had failed to discharge his obligation of making available list of all documents and witnesses to charged officer, allowed writ petition quashing impugned order with liberty to hold a fresh inquiry, further direction to Bank to pay arrears of subsistence allowance treating period of his absence as deemed suspension - Hence instant appeal - Appellant was not justified in setting aside order especially when charged officer could have appealed to appellate authority u/r. 69 of Service Rules - Respondent pointed out that cogent reasons had been stated by HC in setting aside order of dismissal which was unassailable and further Inquiry Officer must ensure supply of list of documents and witnesses relied by Bank to support charges, there was nothing on record of proceeding which would show that presenting officer had produced list of documents before Inquiring Authority and hence no copy of same was made available to charged officer as well - SC, held that, HC, u/art. 226 of Constitution was not justified in interfering with order of dismissal passed by appointing authority after a full-fledged inquiry, especially when Service Rules provide for an alternative remedy of appeal - It was a well acceptable principle of law that HC, while exercising powers u/art. 226 of Constitution does not act as an appellate authority, its jurisdiction was circumscribed and confined to correct an error of law or procedural error, if any, resulting in manifest miscarriage of justice or violation of principles of natural justice - Inquiring Authority had elaborately considered charges leveled against charged officer and also materials produced by bank because some evidence was necessary to establish charges, in some cases, proof may only be documentary and in some cases oral - Requirement of proof depends on facts and circumstances of each case, Appellant-Bank in this case had succeeded in establishing charges leveled against delinquent officer and was rightly dismissed from service which called for no interference by HC u/art. 226 of Constitution - Appeal dismissed.
