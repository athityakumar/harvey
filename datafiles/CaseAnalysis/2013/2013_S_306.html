Pathan Mohammed Suleman Rehmatkhan v State of Gujarat and others

Supreme Court of India

22 November 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Radhakrishnan, A.K. Sikri

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 771; (2014) 4 SCC 156; 2014 (3) G.L.R. 1859; JT 2014 (1) SC 303; 2014 (122) RD 707; 2013(14) SCALE 385; [2013] 12 S.C.R. 446

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Administrative - Special Economic Zones Act, 2005 - Allotment of land - For Special Economic Zone - Maintainability - Petitioner in the year 2005 thought of developing an International Financial Services City at par with the globally benchmarked financial centres having offshore banking facilities - Respondent No.5/Govt., issued a notification under Act, for the area of 261 acres of land for development, operation and maintenance of the project - Petitioner filed a Public Interest Petition before HC primarily based on the report of (Comptroller and Auditor General of India)CAG seeking a declaration that the action of the State Govt. for allotting land in favour of the respondent company was illegal and void and sought for an investigation by the Central Bureau of Investigation and also for other consequential reliefs which was dismissed by HC - Whether court could interfere with financial policy decision of State - Held, salient features of the project referred to in the various orders passed by respondent No.5 and the resolutions allotting lands to respondent No.4 and also the notification issued under the Act, and SC was in agreement with the HC that it could not be said that respondent No.5 had acted against public interest - Respondent No.5 had noticed the development and the employment opportunities that the project would bring into the State - Decision taken by the Govt. was also transparent and that the Govt. had also got substantial stake in the Public-Private Partnership and had also taken care of its interests while entering into the various agreements - Petitioner submitted that he was not attributing any motives or stating that the decision was taken for extraneous reasons, but contended that the respondent No.5 had, without any application of mind, parted with a large tracks of land worth crores of rupees to the private party, which was not in the interest of the State - These were purely policy decisions taken by respondent No.5 and, while so, it had examined the benefits the project would bring into the State and to the people of the State - It was well settled that non-floating of tenders or absence of public auction or invitation alone was not a sufficient reason to characterize the action of a public authority as either arbitrary or unreasonable or amounted to mala fide or improper exercise of power - Courts had always held that it was open to the State and the authorities to take economic and management decision depending upon the exigencies of a situation guided by appropriate financial policy notified in public interest - Petition dismissed.
