V. K. M. Kattha Industries Private Limited v State of Haryana and others

Supreme Court of India

16 August 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, Ranjana Prakash Desai, Ranjan Gogoi

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 509; (2013) 9 SCC 338; AIR 2013 SC 3557; 2013 (100) ALR 407; 2013 (5) AWC 5053; 2013 (4) CLT(SC) 145; 2013 (6) CTC 216; JT 2013 (11) SC 334; 2013 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 3463; 2014 (1) MahLJ 762; 2014 (1) MPLJ 498; 2013 (4) RCR(Civil) 997; 2013 (121) RD 120; 2013(10) SCALE 346; [2013] 10 S.C.R. 33

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Notification

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4, 4(1), 5A, 6 - Land acquisition - Notification - Quashing of - Appellant/Company was industrial unit engaged in manufacturing of kattha for various tobacco and non-tobacco products - Govt. Industries Department issued notification for acquisition of lands for public purpose - Declaration was subsequently made and award was announced - Aggrieved appellant filed petition before HC and the same was dismissed - Hence instant appeal - Whether impugned order passed by HC was justified -

Held, if appellant had opportunity of participating in enquiry u/s. 5A of the Act, it would be open to Company to make representation for exclusion like others and there would be every possibility for State Govt. to accede to request since appellant was running an industry which was similar to public purpose for which lands were being acquired - During course of hearing, appellant brought in notice an approved sketch about excluded lands and location of appellant which was on extreme corner of acquired lands - Even if Govt. or authority concerned excludes lands of appellant, there would not be any difficulty in executing scheme - Said claim of appellant was acceptable - Impugned order of HC was set aside and land acquisition proceedings insofar as appellant was concerned was quashed - Appeal allowed.
