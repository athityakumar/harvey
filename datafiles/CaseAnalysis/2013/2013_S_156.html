Umesh Singh v State Of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

22 March 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, Chandramauli Kumar Prasad

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 174; (2013) 4 SCC 360; AIR 2013 SC 1743; 2013 CRLJ 2116; JT 2013 (4) SC 485; 2013 (3) MLJ(Crl) 201; 2013 (3) RCR(Criminal) 120; 2013(5) SCALE 83; [2013] 4 S.C.R. 797

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Murder, rigor mortis

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 r/w s. 34 - Arms Act, 1959, s. 27 - Murder - rigor mortis - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant (accused) and other co-accused persons allegedly committed offences u/s. 302 r/w s. 34 of IPC and u/s. 27 of the Act - Trial Court convicted appellant and other co-accused and sentenced them to undergo imprisonment for life - Aggrieved appellant and co-accused filed appeals before HC, which was dismissed by impugned judgment - Aggrieved appellant filed instant appeal - Whether impugned conviction judgment passed by Trial Court and affirmed by HC was justified - Held, if medical and ocular evidence was contrary then ocular evidence should prevail - Thus, Trial Court was right in recording finding on charge against appellant on proper appraisal of evidence of eye-witness PW2 supported by PW3 and 5 - Finding of fact on charge of s. 302 r/w s. 34 of IPC against appellant and others was seriously examined by HC and concurred with same and in view of evidence of PW2 and 9, informant who was eye-witness and IO's evidence regarding his evidence treating statement of PW2 as FIR was perfectly legal and valid - Trial Court held that faster the rigor mortis appeared, shorter the time it persist - Further rightly made observation that rigor mortis would be present in some parts of legs of dead body - According to medical officer (PW8) there was no question of time of death of deceased - It should have preceded more than 24 hours which was the maximum limit for disappearance of rigor mortis - Said view of PW8 was found fault with by Trial Court and held that PW8 had not correctly deposed in his cross-examination regarding time lapse of a dead person - PW8 has extended the time for rigor mortis to be 30 to 36 hours and Trial Court rightly held that PW8 has deposed in his evidence contrary to rule of medical jurisprudence - Appellants' allegation that he was falsely implicated in instant case was rightly rejected - HC rightly concurred with findings of Trial Court by assigning valid and cogent reasons in impugned judgment - Impugned judgment passed by HC was justified - Appeal dismissed.
