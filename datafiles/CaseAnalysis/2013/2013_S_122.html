Basawaraj and another v Special Land Acquisition Officer

Supreme Court of India

22 August 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, Sharad Arvind Bobde

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 532; (2013) 14 SCC 81; AIR 2014 SC 746; 2014 (1) ALD(SC) 33; 2013(5) ALL MR 934; 2013 (100) ALR 697; 2013 (6) ALT(SC) 43; 2013 (6) AWC 5829; 2013 (3) CLT(SC) 291; 2014 (1) KarLJ 34; 2014 (1) RCR(Civil) 603; 2013 (121) RD 276; 2013(10) SCALE 391; [2013] 8 S.C.R. 227; 2013 (4) WLN(SC) 72

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: (1) Land Acquisition Act, 1894-Section 54-Appeals to High Court-Condonation of delay-Sufficient cause-Appellants filed appeals under Section 54 before High Court with applications for condonation of delay-Application for condonation of delay stood rejected as High Court did not find any sufficient cause to condone delay-Whether impugned judgment and order of High Court calls for any interference?-Held, "no"-Admittedly, there was delay of 5-1/2 years in filing appeals under Section 54 before High Court-Only explanation offered for approaching court at such belated stage is that one of appellants was suffering from ailments and it was in itself good ground for condonation of delay-Difference between 'good cause' and sufficient cause-Court has no power to extend period of limitation on equitable ground-Statutory provision may cause hardship or inconvenience to particular party but Court has no choice but to enforce it giving full effect to the same-Inconvenience not decisive factor to be considered while interpreting statute-Appeals of appellants under Section 54 dismissed on ground of limitation-Held, justified-No interference required with impugned judgment and order of High Court.

(2) Interpretation of Statute-Inconvenience is not decisive factor to be considered while interpreting statute.

(3) Condonation of delay-Application for-To be decided only with parameters laid down by Apex Court in regard to condonation of delay-No Court could be justified in condoning inordinate delay by imposing any condition whatsoever.

(4) Condonation of delay-Sufficient cause-Expression "sufficient cause"-Law on the issue-Summarised.
