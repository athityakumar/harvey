Chatterjee Petrochem Company and another v Haldia Petrochemicals Limited and others

Supreme Court of India

10 December 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 812; (2014) 14 SCC 574; 2014 (2) AWC 1646; 2014 (3) CLT(SC) 49; [2014] 184 Comp Cas 1; 2014 (1) CompLJ 24; JT 2014 (1) SC 93; 2013(15) SCALE 45; [2013] 11 S.C.R. 1101

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; International

Keywords: Validity, Request for arbitration

Summary: Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996-Sections 45, 16 and 5-Arbitration-Foreign/International arbitration-Reference to arbitration in International Chamber of Commerce (I.C.C.)-Dispute arose between parties regarding allotment of shares-Appellant sought to invoke arbitration clause contain in agreement and made request for arbitration-Respondent No. 1 filed suit before High Court to restrain foreign arbitration for resolution of disputes between parties-Maintainability of arbitration of disputes between parties-Arbitration agreement held valid and appellant entitled to invoke arbitration clause for settling disputes as per Rules of I.C.C.-Non-signatory party to arbitration agreement does not jeopardise arbitration clause in any manner-Parties to directed to resolve their disputes through arbitration in accordance with Rules of I.C.C.-Appellants already initiated arbitration proceeding, in such case, parties to continue with arbitration proceeding-Suit filed for permanent injunction against arbitration proceeding by respondent No. 1-Unsustainable in law and set aside-Impugned judgment of High Court-Set aside.
