State of Andhra Pradesh and another v Md. Hussain and another

Supreme Court of India

13 September 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Gokhale, J. Chelameswar

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 597; (2014) 1 SCC 258; 2014 CRLJ 44; 2013 (4) JCC 2410; JT 2013 (12) SC 591; 2013 (4) RCR(Criminal) 353; 2013(11) SCALE 408; [2013] 11 S.C.R. 140

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: National Investigation Agency Act, 2008

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - National Investigation Agency Act, 2008, s. 6 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 439, 482 - Maharashtra Control of Organised Crimes Act, 1999, s. 3 - Explosive Substance Act, 1908 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120-B, 153-A, 302, 307, 326, 324, 427 - Indian Arms Act, 1959 ss. 3, 5 and 25 - Unlawful Activities (Prevention) Act, 1967, ss. 15,16,17, 18, 20, 23 - Applicant was accused No.1 in Special (MCOC) case pending before the NIA and MCOC Court - Said case aroused out of a bomb blast in Malegaon that occurred on 29-9-2008 - Charge-sheet was filed against the applicant and others, including 3 absconding accused, u/ss. 302, 307, 326, 324, 427, 153-A, 120-B of IPC, r/w ss. 3, 4, 5 and 6 of 1908, Act, ss. 3, 5 and 25 of 1959, Act, ss. 15,16,17, 18, 20 and 23 of 1967, Act, and ss. 3(1) (i), 3(1) (ii), 3(2), 3(4), and 3(5) of 1999 Act, before the Court of Special Judge (MCOCA) - National Investigation Agency was investigating the case - Applicant filed application for bail, before a Single Judge of the HC, under the provisions of s. 21(4) of the 1999, Act r/w s. 439 of the CrPC - In view of the order passed by SC, during the pendency of bail application, it was submitted that bail application was required to be placed before a DB of the HC - Hence, instant petitions - (A) Whether provisions of s. 21(2) of 2008, Act, could be applied only to those petitions, applications filed u/s. 21(1) of the 2008, Act, and order of SC did not apply to an appeal from an order of the Special Court refusing bail - Held, an appeal from an order of the Special Court under 2008, Act, refusing or granting bail would lie only to a bench of two Judges of the HC - (B) Whether in case where the 1999, Act applied, all bail matters would be governed by s. 21 of the 1999, Act, and not by s. 21 of the 2008, Act - Held, inasmuch as the applicant was being prosecuted for the offences under the 1999, Act, as well as 1967, Act, such offences were triable only by Special Court, and therefore application for bail in such matters would have to be made before the Special Court under the 2008, Act, and would not lie before the HC either u/s. 439 or u/s. 482 of the CrPC - Application for bail filed by the applicant in the instant case was not maintainable before the HC - Thus, where the NIA Act applied, the original application for bail would lie only before the Special Court, and appeal against the orders therein would lie only to a bench of two Judges of the HC - Petitions dismissed.
