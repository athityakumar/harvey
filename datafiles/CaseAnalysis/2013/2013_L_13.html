Kishan Gopal and another v Lala and others

Supreme Court of India

26 August 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 539; (2014) 1 SCC 244; 2013 (3) ACC 878; 2013 ACJ 2594; 2013 (6) ALD(SC) 59; 2013(5) ALL MR 899; 2013 (101) ALR 281; 2014 (1) AWC 150; 2013 (5) Bom.C.R. 499; 2013 (5) CTC 212; JT 2013 (11) SC 563; 2014 (3) MahLJ 560; 2015(2) M.P.H.T. 61; 2014 (2) MPLJ 569; 2014 (173) PLR 276; 2013 (4) RCR(Civil) 276; 2014 (1) RLW 411; 2013(10) SCALE 580; [2013] 10 S.C.R. 793

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance

Summary: (1) Motor Vehicles Act, 1988-Sections 163A, 166, 168 and 171-Compensation-Child death case-Appellants lost their affectionate 10 years old son in road accident-Tribunal ignored certain relevant facts and evidence on record while considering case of appellants-Approach of High Court to claim of appellants-Very casual-Legal principles laid down in Lata Wadhwa v. State of Bihar, (2001) 8 SCC 197 : 2003 (2) AWC 1486 (SC)-Applicable to facts and circumstances of instant case-Deceased was assisting appellants in their agricultural occupation-Deceased boy, had he been alive would have certainly contributed substantially to family of appellants by working hard-Just and reasonable to take his notional income at R 30,000-Taking young age of parents at time of accident-Multiplier of 15, applicable-Just and reasonable compensation to be awarded to appellants would come to tune of R 5,00,000-Compensation amount to carry interest @ 9% by applying law laid down in Municipal Corporation of Delhi v. Association of Victims of Uphaar Tragedy, (2011) 14 SCC 481 : 2012 (3) SCCD 1180 : 2012 (1) ACCD 400 : 2012 (2) AWC 1731 (SC)-Appeal allowed-Impugned judgments and award of Tribunal and High Court-Set aside. (2) Motor Vehicles Act, 1988-Section 171-Award of interest-Insurance company contesting claim of appellants from 1992-2013 without settling their legitimate claim for nearly about 21 years-If insurance company had awarded and paid just and reasonable compensation to appellants-Same could have been either invested or kept in fixed deposit-Then compensation amount could have earned five times more than what is awarded totally in instant case-Therefore, awarding of 9% interest on compensation awarded to appellants-Legally justified.
