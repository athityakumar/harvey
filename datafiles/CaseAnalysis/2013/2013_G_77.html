Gurvail Singh @ Gala & Another v State Of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

07 February 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 72; (2013) 2 SCC 713; AIR 2013 SC 1177; 2013 ALL MR (Cri) 1464; 2013 CRLJ 1460; 2013 (2) JCC 1025; JT 2013 (2) SC 436; 2013 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 872; 2013 (1) RCR(Criminal) 989; 2013(2) SCALE 271; [2013] 1 S.C.R. 783

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302,34 - Land disputes - Murder - Mitigating circumstances - Rarest of Rare case - Death Sentence - Sustainability - Appellants-accused were convicted for offence u/ss. 302,34 of IPC and were awarded death sentence - Prosecution case was that appellants due to land disputes had killed 4 members of family in front of PW1 lone survivor of family - Trial Court, after considering all oral and documentary evidence, found all accused guilty u/s. 302 r/w s. 34 IPC, and held that case would fall in category of 'rarest of rare cases', and awarded death sentence - Appeal preferred before HC, also concurred with views of trial Court - Hence instant appeal - Appellants contended that various mitigating circumstances were not properly addressed either by trial Court or HC and had wrongly awarded death sentence to both appellants treating case as 'rarest of rare cases' as there was possibility of their reformation and rehabilitation - State pleaded that appellants deserve no sympathy, since they were instrumental for death of four persons and had wiped off entire family in presence of PW1 and, therefore, appellants deserve no sympathy and case clearly called for extreme penalty of capital punishment - Whether appellants deserved death penalty and whether case fell under category of rarest of rare cases - Held, SC, held that age of appellants was a factor which could not be ignored, though not determinative factor in all fact situations, probability that accused persons could be reformed and rehabilitated was also a factor to be borne in mind - To award death sentence, aggravating circumstances (crime test) had to be fully satisfied and there should be no mitigating circumstance (criminal test) favouring accused, even if both tests were satisfied as against accused, even then Court had to finally apply 'Rarest of Rare Cases' test, which depended on perception of society and not 'judge-centric', that was whether society would approve awarding of death sentence to certain types of crime or not - While applying this test, Court had to look into variety of factors like society's abhorrence, extreme indignation and antipathy to certain types of crimes like rape and murder of minor girls, especially intellectually challenged minor girls, minor girls with physical disability, old and infirm women with those disabilities etc. examples were only illustrative and not exhaustive, Courts award death sentence, because situation demands, due to constitutional compulsion, reflected by will of people, and not Judge centric - SC, was of view, that extreme sentence of capital punishment was not warranted to this case - Appeal disposed of.
