Harivadan Babubhai Patel v State of Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

01 July 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 372; (2013) 7 SCC 45; 2013 CRLJ 3944; 2014 (2) G.L.R. 1057; 2013 (3) JCC 1958; JT 2013 (9) SC 274; 2014 (1) RCR(Criminal) 28; 2013(8) SCALE 17; [2013] 10 S.C.R. 889

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, 342, 346, 302, 120B, 201, 34 - Murder - Criminal conspiracy - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant and other accused allegedly murdered deceased - Charge sheet against appellant/A1, A2 to A4 was filed u/ss. 342, 346, 302, 120B and 201 r/w s. 34 IPC was filed - Trial Court acquitted A3 and 4 and convicted A1 and 2 for all the offences imposed against them - Hence instant appeal - Whether Courts below were justified in convicting appellant for the offences charged against them - Held, when the attention of accused was drawn to the circumstances that inculpated him in the crime and he fail to offer appropriate explanation or gives a false answer, the same could be counted as providing a missing link for building the chain of circumstances - In instant case though number of circumstances were put to the accused, yet he has made a bald denial and did not offer any explanation whatsoever - HC had taken note of the fact that A2 was not identified by anyone in the test identification parade - HC, also noticed number of material contradictions and omissions and, accordingly, acquitted A2 - As far as appellant was concerned, all the circumstances lead towards his guilt - As far as conspiracy u/s. 120B IPC was concerned, HC erred in not recording an order of acquittal u/s. 120B IPC as no other accused had been found guilty - Conviction u/s. 120B IPC could not be sustained when the other accused persons have been acquitted, for an offence of conspiracy cannot survive if there was acquittal of the other alleged co-conspirators - Thus, the conviction of appellant u/s. 120B IPC was set aside - Appeal dismissed.
