Commissioner of Central Excise, Jalandhar v Kay Kay Industries

Supreme Court of India

26 August 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 748; (2013) 14 SCC 94; 2013 (295) E.L.T. 177; JT 2013 (11) SC 554; 2013(10) SCALE 571; [2013] 9 S.C.R. 623; 2017 (48) S.T.R. 101

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Deemed Modvat Credit, Deemed Credit, Full duty liability

Summary: Excise - Practice & Procedure - Central Excise Rules, 1944, rr. 57A(1), (6), 96ZP - Central Excise Act, 1944, s. 3A - MODVAT credit - Deemed credit - Full duty liability - Respondent (company) availed deemed MODVAT credit on the strength of invoices issued by its supplier - However, supplier of respondent did not discharge full duty liability for the period covered by invoices - Adjudicating Authority, by its order disallowed the deemed MODVAT benefit earlier availed and ordered for recovery and imposed penalty - Aggrieved respondent filed an appeal before Commissioner, which was dismissed holding that credit of deemed duty paid by manufacturer u/s. 3A of the Act was available subject to condition that inputs were received directly from the factory of manufacturer under cover of an invoice declaring therein that appropriate duty of excise was paid on such inputs under the Act - Aggrieved respondent filed an appeal before Tribunal, which was allowed - Aggrieved appellant (Revenue) filed an appeal before HC, which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeals - Whether impugned judgment passed by HC was justified - Held, from cl. 4 and 5 notification no. 58/97-CE(NT) dt.30-8-1997, issued under the Rules, it was clear that there were 2 mandates to avail the benefit of said notification - One part was couched in affirmative language and other part was in negative - As per first part it was obligatory on the part of assesse (respondent) to produce the invoice declaring that the appropriate duty of excise was paid on such inputs u/s. 3-A of the Act - Second command, couched in negative, was that provisions of said notification should not apply to inputs where the manufacturer of the said inputs has not declared the invoice price of the said inputs correctly in the documents at the time of their clearance from his factory - In instant case, there was no dispute that a declaration was given by manufacturer of the inputs indicating that excise duty was paid on said inputs under the Act - There was no cavil over the fact that manufacturer of the inputs had declared the invoice price of inputs correctly in the documents - Only allegation was that at the time of MODVAT verification it was found that supplier of inputs had not discharged full duty liable for the period covered under the invoices - That lapse of the seller was different and not a condition or rather a pre-condition postulated in the notification - R. 57A(6) of the Rules postulates and requires 'reasonable care' and not verification from the dept. whether the duty stands paid by the manufacturer-seller - That would be practically impossible and would lead to transactions getting delayed - Impugned judgment of HC was justified - Appeals dismissed.
