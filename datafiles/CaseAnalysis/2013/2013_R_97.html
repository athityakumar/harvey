Integrated Finance Company Limited v Reserve Bank of India Etc.

Supreme Court of India

16 July 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Surinder Singh Nijjar, Pinaki Chandra Ghose

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 442; 2013 (4) BC 16; [2013] 179 Comp Cas 390; 2013 (4) CompLJ 1; JT 2013 (10) SC 283; 2013(9) SCALE 600

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate

Keywords: RBI (Amendment) Act, 1997, Sick Industrial Companies Act, 1982, Reserve Bank of India Amendment Act, 1963, RBI (Amendment) Ordinance 1997, Companies Act, 1983

Summary: Corporate - Reserve Bank of India Act, 1934, ss. 45N and 45QA - Companies Act, 1956, s. 391 - Approval to scheme - Appellant was incorporated as a Non-Banking Finance Company (NBFC) under 1956 Act and was engaged in the business of hire-purchase and leasing - RBI in exercise of its powers u/s. 45N of 1934 Act inspected the books of accounts of the appellant and disclosed violations of the provisions of the 1934 Act - RBI issued a circular to the appellant, prohibiting it from 'accepting deposits from any person, in any form whether by way of fresh deposits or renewal of the existing deposits or otherwise, until further orders' - Further, appellant was directed not to sell, transfer, create charge or mortgage, or deal in any manner with its properties, assets, without prior permission of the RBI - In order to overcome these problems, appellant proposed a Scheme of Compromise with its creditors, viz. the depositors and bond holders, which was approved by the Board of Directors of the appellant - Said scheme had been approved by majority of the bond holders and deposit holders - A petition was preferred before the HC u/s. 391(2) of 1956 Act, seeking sanction for the said scheme of compromise - Single Judge granted approval to said scheme - On appeal, DB set aside the judgment of Single Judge - Hence, instant appeal - (A) Whether DB rightly set aside the judgment of Single Judge - Held, HC had correctly concluded that even if no investigation was pending u/ss. 235-251 of 1956 Act, it was incumbent on the company to disclose the violations pointed out by the RBI on inspection of its books, which led to the issuance of the notice - This, would clearly reflect on the lack of bonafide of the appellant in proposing scheme of arrangement - Non- disclosure of the action taken and initiated by the RBI as apparent from the letter dt. 18-1-2005, amounted to non-disclosure of material facts which were required to be disclosed u/s. 391(1) r/w s. 393(1) of the 1956 Act - Company Court whilst examining the fairness and the bonafide of a scheme of arrangement does not act as a rubber stamp - It could not shut its eyes to blatant non-disclosure of material information, which could have a major influence/impact on the decision as to whether the scheme had to be approved or not - Hence, HC had not committed any error of jurisdiction in rejecting the submission of the appellant that the non-disclosure of the letter dt. 18-1-2005 was not material - No justification to interfere with the judgment and order passed by the HC - (B) Whether a scheme which does not comply with the provisions of s. 45QA of the 1934 Act could be sanctioned - Held, chapter IIIB of 1934 Act had been given an overriding effect over all other laws including 1956 Act by incorporating s. 45Q of 1934 Act with a clear intention to ensure that in a case of NBFC, a scheme u/s. 391 of the 1956 Act cannot be entertained unless it was in conformity with the provisions of s. 45QA of the 1934 Act - Appeals dismissed.
