Bhanwar Lal and another v Rajasthan Board of Muslim Wakf and others

Supreme Court of India

09 September 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 568; AIR 2014 SC 758; 2013 (101) ALR 261; JT 2013 (12) SC 300; 2013 (2) OLR 1009; 2013 (4) RCR(Civil) 758; 2014 (2) RLW 1697; 2013(11) SCALE 210; [2013] 9 S.C.R. 721; 2013 (4) WLN(SC) 1

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Andhra Pradesh Wakf Act, 1995, Rajasthan Wakf Act, 1995

Summary: Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Rajasthan Wakf Act, 1995, ss. 6, 7, 85 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 7 r. 10 - Dispute with regard to Wakf property - Jurisdiction of civil court - Respondent plaintiffs filed suit for possession of Wakf property in question - Later, respondents filed application stating that suit was not maintainable u/s. 85 of the Act - Trial Court returned plaint - Petitioner defendants challenged Trial Court order before HC - HC affirmed Trial Court order - Hence, instant appeal - Whether civil court lacked jurisdiction to entertain suit filed by respondent - Held, suit was for cancellation of sale deed, rent and for possession as well as rendition of accounts and for removal of trustees - In so far as relief of cancellation of sale deed is concerned, this should be tried by civil court for the reason that it was not covered by s. 6 or 7 of the Act - Relief of possession, which could be given by civil court, depended upon question as to whether sale deed was valid or not - Thus, issue of sale deed and possession and inextricably mixed with each other - Further, instant suit was instituted in the year 1980, i.e. much before the Act was enacted - Since the suit was filed much before the Act came into force, it was the civil court where suit was filed would continue to have jurisdiction over issue and civil court would be competent to decide the same - Hence, impugned judgment of HC was set aside and thereby dismissing application filed by respondent under O. 7 r. 10 of CPC with direction to civil court to decide the suit - Appeal allowed.
