Lokesh Kumar Jain v State of Rajasthan

Supreme Court of India

09 July 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 432; (2013) 11 SCC 130; JT 2013 (9) SC 360; 2013 (3) RCR(Criminal) 763; 2013 (3) RLW 2689; 2013(8) SCALE 455; [2013] 7 S.C.R. 519

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Quashing of

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 409 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 156(3), 482 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 21 - Criminal breach of trust - FIR - Quashing of - Appellant (accused/employee) was posted as LDC-cum-Cashier had allegedly committed financial irregularity and thereby committed offence u/s. 409 of IPC - FIR was registered against appellant and a report was submitted to Chief Judicial Magistrate (CJM) and u/s. 156(3) of CrPC matter was sent back matter to Police for further investigation - Thereafter, appellant filed a petition u/s. 482 of CrPC before HC for setting aside of FIR - However, in departmental inquiry appellant was exonerated from the charges by a report submitted by Inquiry Officer (IO) - HC by impugned order dismissed petition - Hence, instant appeal - Appellant contended that since the date of order u/s. 156(3) of CrPC passed by CJM, appellant was suffering the harassment of investigation for more than 13 years which was not completed till date due to lack of supply of documents - Whether HC was justified in not exercising its inherent powers u/s. 482 of CrPC - Held, at least for more than 9 years neither original records could be traced by Authorities nor any relevant document could be found to implicate appellant, as evident from Inquiry Report by IO whereby appellant was exonerated over the identical charges for which criminal case was lodged - Respondent inspite of repeated requests by IO failed to produce any records including originals from the Bank to establish the guilt of delinquent official, appellant - It was a fit case where HC should have exercised its power u/s. 482 of CrPC as the State was not sure as to whether a sanction for prosecuting the accused was required and if so, whether it was granted or not and that the case was pending for about 17 years and proceedings against appellant was quashed - Considering fact that delay in instant case was caused by respondent, the constitutional guarantee of a speedy investigation and trial u/art. 21 of Constitution was thereby violated and as the appellant was already exonerated in departmental proceedings for identical charges, keeping the case pending against appellant for investigation, was unwarranted and FIR was quashed - Appeal allowed.
