M.P. State Mining Corporation Ltd. v Sanjeev Bhaskar & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

02 July 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 359; (2013) 12 SCC 326; 2013 (5) AWC 4821; 2013 (3) CGLJ 525; 2013(8) SCALE 706; [2013] 8 S.C.R. 386; 2013 (3) WLN(SC) 129

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Mines & Minerals

Keywords: Delay and latches

Summary: Mines & Minerals - Administrative - Mines and Minerals (Development and Regulation) Act, 1957 - Mineral Concession Rules, 1960 - Mining lease - Renewal - Delay and latches - State Govt. granted mining lease to original lessee(deceased) for extraction of minerals under the Act r/w the Rules for a period of 20 years - Show cause notice was issued to original lessee as to why his mining lease should not be revoked on ground of certain breaches committed by him which were discovered during inspection made by Mining Inspector - Original lessee filed a revision application to Central Govt. which was dismissed - Original lessee challenged order of determination before HC - DB set aside orders with direction to State Govt. to decide afresh question of determination of lease in accordance with law - Meanwhile, legal heirs/respondents of original lessee preferred an application for grant of mining lease either by way of a fresh grant or by way of renewal but did not receive reply - Respondent no. 1 filed a contempt petition before HC which was dismissed on the ground of being time barred with an observation that it could hope and trust that Govt. would implement order passed in year 1986, if they had not implemented same - Hence, instant appeals - Whether there was any perversity or illegality in impugned judgment and decree of HC - Held, legal heirs including could not derive advantage of order which was inadvertently passed by HC in absence of knowledge of death of original lessee - However, respondent no. 1 had not explained delay of more than 14 years after death of original lessee and delay of 10 years after order passed by HC in earlier filed revision petition as to why they did not choose to move before any Court of Law - Even though revisional order was passed by Central Govt., Delhi HC ought to have asked respondent no. 1 to move before Madhya Pradesh HC for appropriate relief - Thus, HC ought to have dismissed case on the ground of delay and latches - Impugned orders passed by HC were set aside - Appeals allowed.
