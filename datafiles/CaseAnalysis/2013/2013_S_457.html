State of Rajasthan v Shambhu Kewat and another

Supreme Court of India

28 November 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan, A.K. Sikri

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 797; (2014) 4 SCC 149; 2014 ALL MR (Cri) 386; 2014 CRLJ 609; 2014 (1) RCR(Criminal) 199; 2013(14) SCALE 235; [2013] 12 S.C.R. 973

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 307, 323, 325, 427, 34 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 482 - Attempt to murder - Grievous hurt - Compromise - Acquittal - Sustainability - Respondents were charge-sheeted for the offences punishable u/ss. 307, 323, 325, 427 r/w s. 34 IPC - Trial Court convicted respondents u/s. 307 r/w s. 34 IPC - Aggrieved on appeal HC acquitted respondents as the matter was settled and compromised between the parties - Hence instant appeal - Whether HC was justified in acquitting respondents - Held, gravity of the injuries was taken note of by Trial Court and it had awarded the sentence of 10 years rigorous imprisonment for the offence punishable u/s. 307 IPC, but not by the HC - HC had completely overlooked the various principles laid down by SC in Gian Singh v. State Of Punjab & Another 2012 Indlaw SC 314, and has committed a mistake in taking the view that, the injuries were caused on the body of complainant in a fight occurred at the spur and the heat of the moment - It was categorically held by SC in Gian Singh 2012 Indlaw SC 314 that the Court, while exercising the power u/s. 482 CrPC, should have 'due regard to the nature and gravity of the crime' and 'the societal impact' - Both aspects were completely overlooked by HC - HC in a cursory manner, without application of mind, blindly accepted the statement of the parties that they had settled their disputes and differences and took the view that it was a crime against 'an individual', rather than against 'the society at large'- Impugned judgment of HC was set aside - HC was carried away by the settlement and had not examined the matter on merits, hence, HC was directed to take back the appeal to its file and decide the appeal on merits - Appeal allowed.
