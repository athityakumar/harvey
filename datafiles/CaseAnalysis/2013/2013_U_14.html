Union Of India & Ors. v Ex-Gnr Ajeet Singh

Supreme Court of India

02 April 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 187; (2013) 4 SCC 186; 2013 CRLJ 2215; 2013 (137) FLR 975; JT 2013 (4) SC 415; 2013 (2) RCR(Criminal) 639; 2013(5) SCALE 107; [2013] 5 S.C.R. 620

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Defence & Security Forces - Practice & Procedure - Army Rules, 1954, rr. 51, 72 and 79 - Dismissal - On grounds of theft - Defence of Juvenile - Sustainability - Respondent was enrolled in Army - Respondent allegedly committed offence of theft and unauthorized absence from duty - After due interrogation by Army recovery of stolen ammunition was made from possession of respondent - Respondent was awarded the punishment of dismissal from service and 7 years Rigorous Imprisonment(RI) by General Court Martial (GCM) - However, HC reversed the order on ground that few of the offences were committed by respondent when he was juvenile and therefore, the GCM proceedings stood vitiated - However, HC had given liberty to the appellant to hold fresh GCM, on the charges related to offences committed by the respondent after he attained the age of 18 years - Hence instant appeal - Whether order of HC was sustainable - Held, r. 51 of Rules required that the accused should raise the objection in respect of jurisdiction at early stage of the commencement of proceedings - Had the respondent raised the issue of juvenility at appropriate stage, authority conducting the GCM could have dropped the charges in respect of offences committed by him as juvenile - Further, r. 72 of Rules provided for mitigation of sentence in case of invalidity in framing of charges or on finding thereon - Respondent pleaded guilty to all the offences, though at belated stage - As member of the Indian Army, respondent was duty bound to protect the nation - However, respondent abused the nation instead of protecting it - Therefore, his conduct had been unpardonable and not worthy of being soldier - At the cost of repetition, it might be observed that after attaining 18 years of age, respondent committed four serious offences - Respondent could have been punished with 10 years' RI for the 2nd charge, 7 years' RI for the 6th charge and 3 years' RI on each count for the 4th and 5th charges - Further, there had been joint trial, and in view of the provisions of r. 65 of Rules, composite sentence of 7 years RI had been imposed - Respondent could have asked for separate trial of different charges as provided u/r. 79 of Rules - However, in that case the punishment would have been much more severe, as all the sentences could not run concurrently - In fact, respondent had benefited from the joint trial of all the charges and thus, by no means could he claim that his cause stood prejudiced by resorting to such course - HC ought to have taken cue from r. 72 of Rules for the purpose of deciding the case, as the same provided for mitigation of sentence in the event that charge or finding thereon was found to be invalid, as the respondent could not have been tried by GCM for the offences that had been committed by him as juvenile, keeping in view the provisions of r. 65 of Rules thereof - Thus, considering the nature of service of the respondent, gravity of offences committed by him after attaining the age of 18 years and the totality of the circumstances, SC was of the considered opinion that grant of relief to the respondent, even on the principles of "justice, equity, and good conscience"; was not permissible - Appeal allowed.
