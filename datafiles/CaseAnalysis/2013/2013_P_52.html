Shri Lal Mahal Limited v Progetto Grano Spa

Supreme Court of India

03 July 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, Kurian Joseph

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 413; (2014) 2 SCC 433; 2013 (6) AWC 6137; 2013 (3) CLT(SC) 295; 2013 (4) CTC 636; JT 2013 (11) SC 84; 2013 (4) RCR(Civil) 105; 2013(8) SCALE 489

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; International

Keywords: Foreign Awards (Recognition And Enforcement) Act, 1961, Arbitration (Protocol And Convention) Act, 1937, Geneva Convention Act, 1927, Grain and Feed Trade Association Rules

Summary: Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996-Sections 48 (1) (c) and 42 (2) (b)-Foreign award-Enforceability-Objections to enforcement of foreign awards - Maintainability-Enforcement of foreign award can be refused only if such enforcement found to be contrary to (1) fundamental Policy of Indian Law, or (2) interests of India or (3) Justice or morality-Procedural defects in course of foreign arbitration do not lead necessarily to excuse award from enforcement on ground of public policy-Objections raised to challenge enforceability of foreign awards by appellant do not fall in any of aforesaid categories-Therefore, foreign awards cannot be held to be contrary to public policy as contemplated under Section 48 (2) (b)-Nothing in awards which could be said to be against public policy of India-No substance in contention of appellant that awards cannot be enforced being contrary to Section 48 (1) (c)-Awards passed by Board of Appeal and affirmed by High Court of Justice at London in favour of respondent-Are enforceable under Section 48-Impugned judgment of High Court holding foreign award enforceable-Affirmed.
