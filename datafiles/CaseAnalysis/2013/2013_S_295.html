Dhrup Singh and others v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

08 April 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 213; (2013) 4 SCC 275; 2013 (2) KLT 396; 2013(3) M.P.H.T. 486; 2013 (4) RCR(Criminal) 228; 2013(5) SCALE 445

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Criminal charges, Issuance of summons

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34 and 302 - Arms Act, 1959, s. 27 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 319 - Criminal charges - Issuance of summons - Justifiability - Trial Court had taken cognizance against petitioners for offences u/ss. 34 and 302 IPC and s. 27 of the Act - Aggrieved, petitioners field writ petition, which was dismissed by HC - Hence, instant appeal - Petitioners contended that Trial Court was not justified in invoking s. 319 of CrPC since petitioners were not charge-sheeted by police after conducting investigation - Whether Trial Court was right in issuing summons to petitioners who were named in FIR, but not in charge-sheet - Held, petitioners were named in FIR and Trial Court after perusing FIR, case diary and death report came to a prima facie conclusion of involvement of all persons named in FIR in occurrence - Trial Court expressed the view that there were enough materials to initiate prosecution against them apart from charge sheeted accused persons - HC was justified in dismissing the petition preferred by petitioners - Petition dismissed.
