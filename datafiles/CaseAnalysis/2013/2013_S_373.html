Association for Environment Protection v State of Kerala and others

Supreme Court of India

02 July 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, Sharad Arvind Bobde

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 397; (2013) 7 SCC 226; AIR 2013 SC 2500; JT 2013 (10) SC 57; 2013 (3) KLT 201; 2013 (5) MLJ 604; 2014 (1) RCR(Civil) 14; 2013(8) SCALE 532; [2013] 7 S.C.R. 352

Case Digest

Subject: Environment, Wildlife & Animal

Keywords: Natural Resources, Kerala Protection Of River Banks And Regulation Of Removal Of Sand Act, 2001

Summary: Environment, Wild life & Animal - Public trust doctrine - Construction of restaurant on bank of river - Environmental impact - In 2005, Municipality reclaimed a part of Periyar river within its jurisdiction and District Tourism Promotion Council, decided to construct a restaurant on reclaimed land - Proposal was submitted for renovation and beautification of Manalpuram Park by District Tourism Promotion Council, which was accorded by State Govt. Vide order dt.20-5-2005 for implementation of project at an estimated cost of Rs.55,72,432/- - Appellant/petitioner filed writ petition for restraining respondents from constructing building (hotel/restaurant) on banks of river within the area of Municipality - HC dismissed said petition on ground that while appellant had questioned construction of a hotel, respondents were actually constructing restaurant as part of project for renovation and beautification of Park - Hence instant appeal - Whether impugned order was sustainable - Held, there was nothing in language of G.O. dt.20-5-2005 from which it could be inferred that while approving proposal forwarded by Director, Department of Tourism for renovation and beautification of Manalpuram Park at an estimated cost of Rs.55,72,432/-, State Govt. had amended G.O. dt.13-1-1978 or otherwise relaxed conditions embodied therein - Record also did not show that Department of Tourism had furnished a detailed comprehensive environmental impact statement for project so as to enable Committee to make appropriate review and assessment - Therefore, it should be held that execution of project including construction of restaurant was ex facie contrary to mandate of G.O. dt.13-1-1978, which was issued by State in discharge of its Constitutional obligation u/art.48-A of Constitution - Unfortunately, HC ignored this crucial issue and casually dismissed petition without examining serious implications of construction of a restaurant on land reclaimed by Municipality from river - Impugned order was set aside - Appeal allowed.
