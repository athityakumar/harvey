Life Convict Bengal @ Khoka @ Prasanta Sen v B.K. Srivastava & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

13 February 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, J. S. KHEHAR

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 86; (2013) 3 SCC 425; AIR 2013 SC 1163; 2013 CRLJ 1446; JT 2013 (3) SC 20; 2013(2) M.P.H.T. 470; 2013 (1) RCR(Criminal) 999; 2013(2) SCALE 467; [2013] 3 S.C.R. 392

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Jail Manual, Life Convict, West Bengal Correctional Services Act, 1992, West Bengal Correctional Services Act

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 57 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 432,401 - West Bengal Correctional Services Act, 1992, ss. 61,2(c) - Life Convict - Remission - Imprisonment - Premature release - Contempt - Validity - Petitioner-life Convict filed a writ of Habeas Corpus for his immediate release contending that he had undergone total sentence of imprisonment for a period of 22 years 2 months and 16 days including earned remission, according to him, even as per stand taken by respondents in their counter affidavits, he had undergone sentence for a period of 20 years 1 month and 17 days including remission and set off as on 31-12-2004, petitioner, had pleaded that he had already undergone full sentence of 20 years with remission - Petitioner further contended that inspite of order of this Court dt. 24-11-2010 and in view of Act, respondents had not released him due to which he had filed contempt petition - Respondent contended that after going through records and period of custody and provision of Act, it rejected prayer of petitioner for premature release, hence there was no violation of order dt. 24-11-2010 - Whether officials were liable to be punished for contempt of court - Held, it was clear that neither s. 57 IPC nor explanation to s. 61 of Act lays down that a life imprisonment prisoner had to be released after completion of 20 years - 20 years mentioned in explanation to s. 61 of Act was only for purpose of ordering remission, if State Govt. taking into consideration various aspects refused to grant remission of whole period then petitioner could not take advantage of above explanation and even s. 57 of IPC and seek for pre-mature release - Further question of remission of entire sentence or a part of it lies within exclusive domain of appropriate Govt. u/s. 401 of CrPC and neither s. 57 of IPC nor any rules or local Acts can stultify effect of sentence of life imprisonment given by Court under IPC - Petition dismissed.
