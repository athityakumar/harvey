Educare Charitable Trust v Union of India and another

Supreme Court of India

17 September 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, K.S. Radhakrishnan

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 613; (2013) 16 SCC 474; AIR 2014 SC 902; 2013(11) SCALE 569; [2013] 10 S.C.R. 167; 2014 (1) SLR 253

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Education; Health & Drug

Keywords: Dentists Act, 1948, Dental Council of India (Establishment of New Dental Colleges, Opening of New or Higher Course of Study or Training and Increase of Admission Capacity in Dental Colleges) Regulations, 2006, Kerala University of Health Science Act, 2010

Summary: Education - Constitution - Health & Drug - Dental Council of India (Establishment of New Dental Colleges, Opening of New or Higher Course of Study or Training and Increase of Admission Capacity in Dental Colleges) Regulations, 2006 - Increasing admission capacity - Denied - Challenged - Petitioner, Charitable Trust working in the field of education, had established a Dental College - In the year 2012, petitioner wanted to expand the size of Bachelor of Dental Surgery (BDS), being desirous of increasing the capacity from 50 to 100 seats - Said scheme was rejected by the Government on the ground that it did not fulfil the eligibility criteria for such an increase - Petitioner had approached the HC seeking quashing of the said order and for issuance of Writ of Mandamus commanding the Central Government to forward the application of the petitioner for intake of students, to the Dental Council of India (DCI) for technical scrutiny and further to direct the DCI to make appropriate recommendation to the Central Government for issuance of letter of permission during the Academic Year 2013-14 itself - Said Writ Petition had been dismissed by the HC - Hence, instant petition - Whether HC rightly dismissed the petition filed by the petitioner - Held, as per the aforesaid time-schedule, the petitioner-college desirous of increasing the admission capacity was to submit the application from 1st August to 30th September - This was done by the petitioner - However, what was found that the petitioner was not meeting the qualifying criteria as on that date because with respect to existing admission capacity, it had not been recognized so far - Applications were to be forwarded by the Central Government, once they were found to be in order and meeting the qualifying criteria laid down in regn.19 of Regulations, by 31st October in respect of BDS course - Said time was extended upto 31st December - After an application was forwarded to the DCI, DCI was supposed to evaluate the scheme for increasing admission capacity as per the procedure laid down in regn. 21 of Regulations which lays down that the DCI was required to ascertain the desirability and prima facie feasibility for increasing the admission capacity at the Dental College - As per the time-schedule, time upto 15th June was given for the DCI to make recommendation to the Central Government - Thereafter, Central Government was required to go into the said recommendation and if it was found that petitioner-college deserves the permission to increase the admission capacity, Letter of Permission was to be issued by 15th July - This time frame was to ensure timely admissions of students - It was not possible to accede to the request of the petitioner to change the time-schedule when the last date for admitting the students, which was 15-7-2013, expired long ago - If the Central Government forwards the application to the DCI at this juncture, DCI shall hardly have any time to look into the feasibility of the scheme as per the requirements contained in regn. 21 of Regulation - Hence, HC does not commit any error in holding that in the given circumstances mandamus could not be issued to the Central Government to exercise its discretionary powers in a particular manner to modify the time-schedule - Sanctity to the time-schedule had to be attached - Petition dismissed.
