Jagdish Prasad Sharma etc v State of Bihar and others

Supreme Court of India

17 July 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Surinder Singh Nijjar, J. Chelameswar

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 451; (2013) 8 SCC 633; 2013(9) SCALE 459; [2013] 11 S.C.R. 696

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Education

Keywords: Patna University Act, 1976, Kerala Public Service Commission Act, 1968, Bihar State Universities (Amendment) Act, 2006, Bihar State Universities (Amendment) Act, University Grants Commission (Minimum Qualifications required for the appointment and career advancement of teachers in Universities and institutions affiliated to it) Regulation, 2000, Kerala Service Rules, 1958

Summary: Constitution - Education - Constitution of India, 1950 - University Grants Commission Act, 1956, s. 26 - University Grants Commission (Minimum Qualifications required for the appointment and career advancement of teachers in Universities and institutions affiliated to it) Regulation, 2000 - Recommendation of Commission - Adoption by State Govts. - Discretionary - University Grants Commission (Commission) issued a Notification on revision of pay scales, minimum qualification for appointment of teachers in Universities, colleges and other measures for the maintenance of standards - Subsequently, letter was issued by the Central Govt. regarding a scheme of revision of pay of teachers and other equivalent cadres in all the Central universities and colleges and Deemed Universities, following the revision of pay scales of the Central Govt. employees on the recommendation of the Sixth Central Pay Commission, subject to all the conditions mentioned in the letter and the Regulations - State Govts. were given an option to adopt the scheme in its composite form - Pay Review Committee recommended that the age of superannuation throughout the country should be 65 years, whether in a State or Central University, as also in a college or in a University - Commission also recommended the report of the Pay Review Committee for acceptance by the Central Govt. - Pursuant to the said decision and recommendation of the Commission, the Ministry of Human Resource Development published a Scheme on 31-12-2008 - However, as no action was taken even thereafter, the appellants filed writ petitions - Single Judge held that the State Govt. had no discretion as they were statutorily bound by the decision of the Commission to enhance the age of superannuation - Appeal filed thereagainst was allowed by DB of HC - Hence, instant appeals - Whether the Regulations framed by the University Grants Commission u/s. 26 of the Act, were binding on the States and State-funded and other Universities and colleges being run therein - Held, authority of the Commission to frame Regulations with regard to the service conditions of teachers in the centrally- funded educational institutions was well established - Acceptance of the scheme in its composite form had been left to the discretion of the State Govts. and, therefore, there was no compulsion on the State and its authorities to adopt the scheme - Further, there was no ambiguity that the final decision to enhance the age of superannuation of teachers within a particular State would be that of the State itself - State Govts. were also entitled to legislate with matters relating to education under Entry 25 of List III of the Constitution - Hence, there being no compulsion to accept and/or adopt the said scheme, the States were free to decide as to whether the scheme would be adopted by them or not - Hence, there could be no automatic application of the recommendations made by the Commission, without any conscious decision being taken by the State in this regard, on account of the financial implications and other consequences attached to such a decision - No reason to interfere with the impugned judgment and order of the DB of the HC - Order accordingly.
