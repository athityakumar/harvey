Ram Deo Prasad v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

11 April 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Aftab Alam, Ranjana Prakash Desai

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 221; (2013) 7 SCC 725; 2013 ALL MR (Cri) 1876; 2013 CRLJ 2369; JT 2013 (7) SC 428; 2013 (4) RCR(Criminal) 198; 2013(5) SCALE 544; [2013] 6 S.C.R. 108

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Rape and murder

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 376 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 366 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 114 - Rape and murder - Death penalty - Sustainability - Appellant allegedly committed offences u/ss. 376 and 302 of IPC - Trial Court found appellant guilty of committing rape and causing injuries to child leading to her death and convicted appellant u/ss. 376 and 302 of IPC and awarded him death penalty - Trial Court made reference u/s. 366 of CrPC to HC, however appellant did not filed any appeal against conviction judgment - HC by impugned judgment affirmed the judgment passed by Trial Court - Hence, instant appeal - Whether impugned award of death penalty of appellant was justified - Held, in a case involving death sentence, Court could not afford to leave any detail, howsoever small and apparently insignificant, fully explored - SC sounded a note of caution that in a case where reliance was on circumstantial evidence, that factor had to be taken into consideration while awarding death sentence - Instant case was also a case purely on circumstantial evidence and in all cases of circumstantial evidence, death sentence could not be given - Prosecution case in 2 parts, first, soon after child was found missing, appellant was seen close to house of Informant, swiftly going eastwards in direction of fields/orchard carrying in his arms a girl child who was crying - That was at a point when child was lifted from verandah of her house and before she was subjected to sexual abuse - Second part of case appellant was seen carrying child and on seeing group of villagers coming in pursuit of him he threw down the child in wheat field and fled away - That was at a point after child was subjected to sexual abuse and brutality - First part did not form part of charge, which was based on solitary evidence of PW2, who was turned hostile - Second part of case was fully established by evidences of PW1 and PW3 (mother of child) - It was established against appellant that he was seen carrying the child soon after she was sexually abused and brutalized in most cruel manner and on seeing group of villagers coming after him he threw down child in wheat field and ran away - It was, thus, for him to explain how the child came in his possession and in absence of any explanation Court would be fully justified in invoking s. 114 of 1872 Act and to hold him guilty of causing injuries to her private parts leading to her death - No exception could, thus, be taken to appellant's conviction u/ss. 376 and 302 of IPC - There could be no doubt that offence committed by appellant was heinous and revolting but nature of offence alone might not in all cases be determining factor for bringing case in 'rarest of rare' category and to impose ultimate and irreversible punishment of death - There were deficiencies of investigation and from post-mortem report there was no medical evidence - There was not a scrap of forensic evidence of any kind - Even the torch in light of which appellant was said to have identified in cold wintry and foggy night was not produced before Court - Impugned conviction u/ss. 376 and 302 of IPC was confirmed, death sentence was substituted by imprisonment for life that should not be less than actual imprisonment for a period of 18 years - Appeal dismissed.
