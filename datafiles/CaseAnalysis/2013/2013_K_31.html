Kanhaiya Lal and others v State of Rajasthan and others

Supreme Court of India

22 April 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 259; (2013) 5 SCC 655; AIR 2013 SC 1940; 2013 CRLJ 2921; JT 2013 (6) SC 497; 2013 (3) RCR(Criminal) 215; 2013(6) SCALE 242; [2013] 6 S.C.R. 361

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Conversion of death sentence into life

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 147, 148, 302, 342, 427, 435, 460 and 149 - Murder - Conviction - Conversion of death sentence into life - Sustainability - Appellants accused attacked deceased persons with sharp edged weapons and succumbed to injuries - Charge sheet against appellants was filed u/ss. 147, 427, 435, 148, 302, 460 and 342 IPC and s. 435 r/w s. 149 IPC - Trial Court convicted appellants and acquitted other accused persons - On appeal HC partly allowed the appeal who were convicted u/ss. 302 and 460 IPC - As far as the other accused persons of the same category were concerned, sentence of death was converted to life sentence - Hence instant appeals by State and accused persons - Whether HC has fallen into error by commuting the death sentence to that of life imprisonment - Held, HC, while dealing with the Death Reference, has opined that when specific overt acts was not attributed and similarly placed accused persons was given life sentence and RN, who had engineered the incident, had breathed his last, it would not be appropriate to impose death sentence - HC observed that the three sons of RN was awarded death sentence and the other two were villagers and in the backdrop of the situation, there were mitigating factors for commutation of the sentence - Crime had taken place because RN had suspected that accused persons were responsible for extinguishing the life spark of his son - It was also seen that similarly placed persons have been imposed life sentence - Quite apart from that, all accused persons have almost spent thirteen years in custody - Regard being had to the totality of the circumstances, it could not be said that imprisonment for life was inadequate and the circumstances were so grave that it called for a death sentence - It was not a case which could be treated to be a case of extreme culpability and there was no other option but to impose death penalty - Thus, SC did not find any error in the decision of HC by which it has commuted the death sentence to life imprisonment - Appeals dismissed.
