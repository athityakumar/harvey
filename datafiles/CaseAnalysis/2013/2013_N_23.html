Thomson Press (India) Ltd. v Nanak Builders & Investors P.Ltd. & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

21 February 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

M.Y. Eqbal, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 111; (2013) 5 SCC 397; AIR 2013 SC 2389; 2013 (3) ALD(SC) 111; 2013(3) ALL MR 408; 2013 (98) ALR 280; 2013 (3) AWC 2442; 2013 (1) CLT(SC) 321; 2013 (2) CTC 104; JT 2013 (3) SC 289; 2013 (2) OLR 1; 2013 (171) PLR 26; 2013 (2) RCR(Civil) 875; 2013 (119) RD 825; 2013(3) SCALE 26; [2013] 2 S.C.R. 74

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Application For Impleadment

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 1 r. 10 - Specific Relief Act, 1963, s. 19 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 52 - Suit for specific performance - Sustainability - Appeal was directed against the order passed by HC affirming the order of the Single Judge and rejecting the petition filed by the appellant under O. 1 r. 10 of CPC for impleadment as defendants in suit for specific performance of contract filed by plaintiff/Respondent No.1 - Whether if the appellant who was the transferee pendente lite having notice and knowledge about the pendency of the suit for specific performance and order of injunction could be impleaded as party under O. 1 r. 10 on the basis of sale deeds executed in their favour by the defendants - Held, SC referred s. 19 of 1963 Act which read as under - Relief against parties and persons claiming under them by subsequent title - Except as otherwise provided by Chapter, specific performance of contract might be enforced against (a) either party thereto - (b) any other person claiming under him by title arising subsequently to the contract, except transferee for value who had paid his money in good faith and without notice of the original contract - (c) any person claiming under title which, though prior to the contract and known to the plaintiff, might have been displaced by the defendant - (d) when company had entered into contract and subsequently became amalgamated with another company, new company which arose out of the amalgamation - (e) when the promoters of company have, before its incorporation, entered into contract for the purpose of the company and such contract was warranted by the terms of the incorporation, the company - Provided that the company had accepted the contract and communicated such acceptance to the other party to the contract - From the bare reading of provision, it was manifest that contract for specific performance might be enforced against the parties to the contract and the persons mentioned in the said section - Cl. (b) of s. 19 of Act made it clear that suit for specific performance could not be enforced against person who was transferee from the vendor for valuable consideration and without notice of the original contract which was sought to be enforced in the suit - SC would examine some of the relevant facts in order to come to right conclusion - Even before the institution of suit for specific performance when the plaintiff came to know about the activities of defendants to deal with the property, public notice was published at the instance of the plaintiff in newspaper informing the public in general about the agreement with the plaintiffs - In response to the said notice the sister concern of the appellant served legal notice on the defendants- Defendants whereby had referred agreement to sell entered into between the plaintiffs and the defendants -

Even after the institution of the suit, defendants gave undertaking not to transfer and alienate the suit property - Notwithstanding the order passed by the Court regarding the undertaking given on behalf of the defendants and having full notice and knowledge of all these facts, sister concern of the appellant entered into series of transaction and finally the appellant got sale deed executed in their favour by defendants in respect of suit property - Taking into consideration all these facts, SC had no hesitation in holding that the appellant entered into clandestine transaction with the defendants and got the property transferred in their favour - Hence the appellant could not be held to be bonafide purchaser, without notice - On perusal of the two orders passed by single judge and HC it revealed that the HC did not go into the question as to whether if person who purchased the suit property in violation of the order of injunction, and having sufficient notice and knowledge of the Agreement, needed to be added as party for passing effective decree in the suit - Decree for specific performance of contract should be enforced against person claimed under the plaintiff, and title acquired subsequent to contract - There was no dispute that such transfer made in favour of the subsequent purchaser was subject to the rider provided u/s. 52 of 1882 Act and the restrain order passed by the Court - Hirachand Kundanmal v. Municipal Corporation of Greater Bombay & Ors. 1992 Indlaw SC 964 and Kafiladdin and others vs. Samiraddin and others, AIR 1930 Indlaw CAL 200, relied on - Appeal allowed.
