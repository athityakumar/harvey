Rana Girders Limited v Union of India and others

Supreme Court of India

16 August 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 513; (2013) 10 SCC 746; AIR 2013 SC 3422; 2014 (1) Bom.C.R. 332; 2013 (3) CLT(SC) 312; 2013 (2) DRTC 625; 2013 (295) E.L.T. 12; JT 2013 (11) SC 226; 2013 (172) PLR 721; 2013(10) SCALE 356

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: State Financial Corporations Act, 1951

Summary: Excise - Indirect Tax - Banking & Finance - State Financial Corporation Act, 1951, s. 29 - Recovery of excise dues - Challenged - X (Borrower) had taken loans/financial accommodation from the Uttar Pradesh Financial Corporation (UPFC) - Due to consistent default on the part of the X in re-paying the loans, the UPFC took possession of the land and building of the X which were mortgaged/kept as security with the UPFC - After taking physical possession of the unit, the UPFC held public auction on pursuant to advertisement - In the said public auction conducted by UPFC, the appellant was the highest bidder in respect of land and building as well as plant and machinery - Sale Deed was executed in favour of the appellant and appellant had become the owner, both of the land and building and also plant and machinery - X had not questioned the validity of the said auction which has attained finality - X had also to discharge the liability qua excise duty which had amounted to Rs.1,00,72,442/- - To recover that amount, the Commissioner of Customs and Central Excise was pressing the appellant to discharge this liability as purchaser and successor- in-interest of the land and building plus plant and machinery of the borrower - Appellant was resisting the demand with the posture that since the aforesaid properties have been purchased by the appellant in an open auction from the UPFC, free from all encumbrances, it was not the liability of the purchaser to make payment of the dues of excise department - Appellant filed the petition in the HC questioning the validity of the demands raised by the Revenue - HC held that in view of the covenants in the Sale Deed and Agreement it was the liability of the appellant to pay the excise duty - Hence, instant appeal - Whether excise department could recover the amount dues as excise duty from the appellant - Held, it was mentioned not only in the public notice but there was a specific clause inserted in the Sale Deed/Agreement as well, to the effect that the properties in question were being sold free from all encumbrances - At the same time, there was also a stipulation that 'all these statutory liabilities arising out of the land shall be borne by purchaser in the sale deed' and 'all these statutory liabilities arising out of the said properties shall be borne by the vendee and vendor shall not be held responsible in the Agreement of Sale' - HC had missed the true intent and purport of this clause - Expressions in the Sale Deed as well as in the Agreement for purchase of plant and machinery talks of statutory liabilities 'arising out of the land' or statutory liabilities 'arising out of the said properties' (i.e. the machinery) - Thus, it was only that statutory liability which arises out of the land and building or out of plant and machinery which was to be discharged by the purchaser - Excise dues were not the statutory liabilities which arise out of the land and building or the plant and machinery - Dues of the Excise Department became payable on the manufacturing of excisable items by the erstwhile owner, therefore, these statutory dues were in respect of those items produced and not the plant and machinery which was used for the purposes of manufacture - This fine distinction was not taken note at all by the HC - Judgment of the HC was unsustainable in law - Appeal allowed.
