State of Madhya Pradesh v Babulal and others

Supreme Court of India

12 August 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, Sharad Arvind Bobde

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 501; (2013) 12 SCC 308; 2013 (4) JCC 2363; 2013 (4) RCR(Criminal) 92; 2013(10) SCALE 230; [2013] 10 S.C.R. 21

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 148, 324, 326 and 149 - Rioting - Grievous hurt - Reduction of sentence - Justifiability - Respondents allegedly caused injuries to PW1 and PW5 - Trial Court convicted respondents u/ss. 148, 324, 326 and 149 IPC - Respondent's appeal before Sessions Court was dismissed - However, on appeal, HC reduced sentence from 3 years to 2 years - Hence, instant appeal by State - Whether reduction of sentence was warranted in the instant case - Held, one of the prime objectives of criminal law was imposition of adequate, just, proportionate punishment which was commensurate with gravity and nature of crime and manner in which offence was committed - Most relevant determinative factor of sentencing was proportionality between crime and punishment keeping in mind social interest and consciousness of society - Thus, it was the solemn duty of court to strike a proper balance while awarding sentence as awarding a lesser sentence encouraged a criminal and as a result of the same society suffered - In the instant case, four persons were injured and two of them had more than one head injury - There were too many injuries on their persons and some of them had been inflicted on vital parts of body - HC could not be justified in taking a lenient view which reduced administration of the criminal justice system to a mockery - Hence, HC order was set aside and that of the Trial Court restored - Appeal allowed.
