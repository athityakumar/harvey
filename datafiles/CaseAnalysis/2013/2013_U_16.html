C.V. Francis v Union Of India & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

03 July 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Ranjana Prakash Desai

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 363; (2013) 14 SCC 486; 2013 (5) AWC 5305; 2013 (138) FLR 784; JT 2013 (11) SC 132; 2013(8) SCALE 383; [2013] 8 S.C.R. 53; 2013 (5) SLR 424; 2013 (3) WLN(SC) 81

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Employment-Termination of service-Voluntary retirement-Deemed Acceptance of employee's application for voluntary retirement-Whether termination of service of petitioner was in violation of well-settled principles relating to acceptance of voluntary retirement laid down in Tek Chand v. Dile Ram, (2001) 3 SCC 290? Held, "no"-Without waiting for prayer for voluntary retirement to be accepted, petitioner joined American Company even before expiry of notice period-No such stipulation in Voluntary Retirement Scheme that even without acceptance of application for voluntary retirement it would be deemed that petitioner's voluntary retirement application had been accepted-Decision in Tek Chand's case nor applicable to facts of case, particularly when petitioner's application for voluntary retirement had not been accepted and he had been asked to rejoin his services-No reason to interfere with judgment and order of single Judge, as upheld by Division Bench of High Court, rejecting petitioner's prayer challenging termination of his services.
