Dr. Subramanian Swamy v Election Commission of India

Supreme Court of India

08 October 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, RANJAN GOGOI

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 668; (2013) 10 SCC 500; AIR 2014 SC 18; 2013(6) ALL MR 898; 2013 (6) AWC 6241; 2014 (1) CLT(SC) 223; 2013 (6) KarLJ 435; 2013 (4) KLT 216; 2013 (4) RCR(Civil) 793; 2013(12) SCALE 575

Case Digest

Subject: Election; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Constituency, Vote, Candidate, Political Parties

Summary: Constitution of India-Articles 32 and 226-Election-Writ of mandamus-For direction to Election Commission of India to incorporate system of “paper trail/paper receipt” in Electronic Voting Machines (E.V.Ms.) as convincing proof that E.V.Ms. rightly registered vote cast by voter in favour of particular candidate-”Paper trail” is indispensable requirement of free and fair elections-Confidence of voters in E.V.Ms. can be achieved only with introduction of “paper trail”-To have fullest transparency in voting system and to restore confidence of voters-Necessary to set up E.V.Ms. with V.V.P.A.T. System-Election Commission of India to introduce same in gradual stages or geographical-wise in ensuing general elections-Election Commission of India to implement in phased manner-Government of India directed to provide required financial assistance for procurement of units of V.V.P.A.T.-High Court not justified in not issuing writ of mandamus for the purpose aforesaid.
