ONGC Limited v Modern Construction and Company

Supreme Court of India

07 October 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, Sharad Arvind Bobde

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 670; (2014) 1 SCC 648; AIR 2014 SC 83; 2014 (104) ALR 36; 2013 (6) AWC 6429; 2014 (1) CLT(SC) 79; 2013 (6) CTC 77; 2013 (4) KLT 193; 2014 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1155; 2014 (1) RCR(Civil) 217; 2014 (122) RD 436; 2014 (123) RD 645; 2013(12) SCALE 520; [2013] 10 S.C.R. 466; 2014 (1) WLN(SC) 64

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Code of Civil Procedure Code, 1908-Order VII, Rule 10-Plaint-Return of plaint-Respondent initially instituted suit in civil court at Mehsana-Civil court at Mehsana admittedly had no jurisdiction to entertain suit-In spite of fact that civil suit stood decreed-High Court directed court at Mehsana to return plaint-Respondent presented plaint before civil court at Surat in 1999-Plaintiff/respondent claiming interest for period from initially filing suit in 1986 to 1999, i.e., for 13 years by taking advantage of its own wrong-Once plaint presented before civil court at Surat-It was fresh suit and cannot be considered to be continuation of suit instituted at Mehsana-Plaintiff/respondent cannot be permitted to take advantage of its own mistake instituting suit before wrong court-Impugned judgment and order of High Court-Not sustainable in law by holding that respondent was entitled to interest from date of institution of suit at Mehsana court-Judgment and orders of trial/executing court and appellate court-Restored-Judgment of High Court and impugned decree-Set aside.
