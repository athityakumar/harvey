Hema v State, through Inspector of Police, Madras

Supreme Court of India

07 January 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, Ranjan Gogoi, V. Gopala Gowda

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 11; (2013) 10 SCC 192; AIR 2013 SC 1000; 2013 CRLJ 1011; JT 2013 (1) SC 338; 2013 (1) RCR(Criminal) 869; 2013(1) SCALE 242; [2013] 3 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120-B r/w s. 420, 467, 468 and 471 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 313 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 13(2) r/w s. 13(1)(d) - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 27, 47 - Ante-dated passport applications - Conspiracy - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant (accused no. 5) allegedly along with other accused conspired to cheat Regional Passport Office, to obtain passports on the basis of creating ante-dated passport applications with duplicate file numbers, so as to make them appear as old cases - Appellant and other accused committed offences u/ss. 120-B r/w 420, 467, 468 and 471 of IPC and u/ss. 13(2) r/w s. 13(1)(d) of 1988 Act - Trial Court convicted appellant for offences u/ss. 120-B r/w s. 511, 465, and 471 of IPC and sentenced him to undergo 2 years imprisonment - Aggrieved appellant filed an appeal before HC, which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Appellant contended that parallel proceedings by State crime branch and Central Bureau of Investigation (CBI) were not permissible and that prosecution failed to exhibit FSL report with regard to impression of seals - Whether impugned conviction awarded by Trial Court and affirmed by HC was justified - Held, not only fair trial, but fair investigation was part of constitutional rights guaranteed u/arts. 20 and 21 of Constitution - Investigation should be fair, transparent and judicious and it was immediate requirement of rule of law - For certain defects in investigation, accused could not be acquitted - Even if there was negligence on the part of investigating agency or omissions, it was obligation on the part of Court to scrutinize prosecution evidence de hors such lapses to find out whether said evidence was reliable or not and whether such lapses affect object of finding out truth - Objection as to parallel proceedings as claimed by appellant, which objection was not raised either before Trial Court or before HC and even in grounds of appeal before Court - Evidence of witnesses were sufficient to establish that forged attested documents were created and enclosed for purpose of getting passports in support of false addresses given in applications by appellant, which fact was evident from evidence of Village Administrative Officer (PW-15), confessional statement given by accused no. 3 which was recorded u/s. 27 of 1872 Act - It was admitted by appellant u/s. 313 of CrPC that she had worked in travel agency of PW14 and evidence of PW14, who identified writings available on Exhs.P2 to 43 (application forms) as that of appellant, was admissible u/s. 47 of 1872 Act, charge against appellant as proved to have committed in pursuance of conspiracy - It was established by prosecution that filled up passport applications were submitted by appellant on behalf of her employer (accused no. 3) - In majority of passport applications bogus particulars were filled by appellant - Prosecution established that appellant has given false particulars regarding place of residence of applicants' in passport applications in view of her admission u/s. 313 of CrPC - Prosecution clearly established that false documents were made for the purpose of cheating and those documents were used as genuine for obtaining passports - Impugned conviction was confirmed and sentence was reduced to 6 months - Appeal disposed of.
