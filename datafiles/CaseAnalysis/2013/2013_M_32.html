Rajiv Thapar & Ors. v Madan Lal Kapoor

Supreme Court of India

23 January 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

J. S. KHEHAR, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 33; (2013) 3 SCC 330; AIR 2013 SC (Supp) 1056; 2013 ALL MR (Cri) 1107; 2013 (2) CalLT 48; 2013 CRLJ 1272; JT 2013 (2) SC 209; 2013 (1) RCR(Criminal) 911; 2013(1) SCALE 665; 2013(11) SCALE 3; [2013] 3 S.C.R. 52

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 304B, 496, 498A, 120-B - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 227, 228 and 482 - Administered poisoning - Discharge - Review - Quashing of - Challenged - Deceased (daughter of complainant) was wife of appellant no. 1 (accused), who was allegedly suffered a massive heart attack and died - Respondent (complainant) alleged that appellants (accused) allegedly poisoned deceased (daughter of complainant) and filed a private complaint against appellants before Magistrate - Thereafter, Trial Court based on inquest report and post-mortem report found no prima facie case against appellants u/ss. 304B or 498 of IPC and discharged appellants - Aggrieved respondent (complainant) filed a revision before HC, which was allowed by impugned order setting aside discharge order passed by Trial Court - Hence, instant appeal - Appellants contended that post-mortem report, report of Central Forensic Science Laboratory (FSL), inquest proceedings recorded by SDM, and letters addressed by family members of complainant (duly noticed in inquest proceedings), were a part of record of case, and were to be taken into consideration while passing orders contemplated u/ss. 227 and 228 of CrPC - Whether impugned order passed by HC was justified - Held, HC ought to have examined controversy in instant case and detailed order passed by Trial Court, before exercising discretionary powers vested to it u/s. 482 of CrPC - In instant case, complaint made by father of deceased (respondent/complainant) proceeds on assumption, that his daughter was administered poison - Said assumption was based on fact, that respondent, (his family members), found body of their daughter had turned blue when they laid their eyes on it for the first time after her death - Motive disclosed in complaint was non-cordiality of relations between deceased and appellants, on account of non-fulfillment of dowry demands - For allegations of poisoning, appellants relied on post-mortem report dt.28.9.1992, chemical analysis findings recorded in FSL dt.9.2.1993, inquest report dt.6.7.1993, and order passed by Trial Court - It was clear, that respondent was associated with investigative process from very moment the body of deceased arrived at 'D' - It was at his instance, post-mortem examination was conducted - Body of deceased was subjected to post-mortem examination, was handed over jointly to respondent and appellant no. 1 husband of deceased - FSL report negatived allegations of poisoning and samples did not indicate presence of any common poisoning substance - Appellant submitted that material in nature of post-mortem report, FSL report, inquest report, would be sufficient to exculpate appellants from allegations and accusations levelled in complaint and it was sufficient to demonstrate, that there was not remotest possibility, that trial against appellants would lead to their conviction - Thus, merely because deceased body had turned blue, when it arrived at 'D' was not a sufficient basis to infer that she was poisoned to death - In fact material relied upon by appellants was sufficient to condemn the factual basis of accusation as false - Death of deceased was not been shown to have caused by strangulation - Material relied upon by appellants would lead to indubitable conclusion that deceased had not died on account of having been strangulated - Respondent was himself not privy to facts - Impugned order passed by HC was set aside - Appeal allowed.
