Delhi Union of Journalist Cooperative House Building Society Limited and others v Union of India and others

Supreme Court of India

06 September 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, V. Gopala Gowda

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 564; (2013) 15 SCC 614; JT 2013 (12) SC 237; 2013(11) SCALE 177

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Municipalities & Local Governments - Land & Property - Amendment of Master Plan - Allotment of plot - Legality - Respondent authority amended Master Plan - Respondent allotted plot in question to respondent No. 4 for establishing a Nursery School - Appellants filed representation to respondent authority seeking to cancel allotment - Vice-Chairman of respondent authority rejected representation - Appellants challenged respondent's order before HC - HC dismissed appellants' writ petition and appeal thereafter - Hence, instant appeal - Held, writ petition filed by appellants for quashing notification by which Master Plan had been amended permitting use of Nursery School sites for other purposes was dismissed by HC and their challenge to allotment made in favour of respondent No. 4 was also rejected - Appellants carried matter to SC also but could not succeed - Therefore, representation made by appellants to respondent for withdrawing allotment made in favour of respondent No.4 was clearly misconceived and HC did not commit any error by refusing to entertain appellants' prayer for quashing allotment of site to respondent No. 4 - Appellants got an opportunity to indulge in another round of litigation because respondents' advocate before HC volunteered to make a statement that Vice-Chairman of respondent authority would take necessary decision in the light of note prepared by Minister of Urban Development - Undertaking given by respondents' counsel was totally uncalled for and order passed by Vice-Chairman did not entitle appellants to file fresh writ petition for questioning rejection of their representation or for quashing allotment made in favour of respondent No.4 - Further, note recorded by Minister did not have any legal sanctity and the same could not have been relied upon by appellants for seeking cancellation of allotment made in favour of respondent No.4 because no order was issued on the basis of that note and no notification was issued withdrawing amendment made in Master Plan - Appeal dismissed.
