Md. Iqbal and another v State of Jharkhand

Supreme Court of India

22 July 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, Sharad Arvind Bobde

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 462; (2013) 14 SCC 481; AIR 2013 SC 3077; 2013 ALL MR (Cri) 3319; JT 2013 (10) SC 53; 2013 (3) RCR(Criminal) 895; 2014 (1) RLW 299; 2013(9) SCALE 586

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Gang Rape

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 376(2) (g) - Gang rape - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellants-accused no.1 and accused no.2 allegedly raped prosecutrix-victim - Accused were charged for offences punishable u/s. 376(2) (g) IPC - Trial Court convicted and sentenced accused - Appeals were filed before HC - HC dismissed appeals - Hence instant appeals - Whether the impugned order recorded by HC was sustainable - Held, father of prosecutrix-PW4 had turned hostile - Statement of prosecutrix had been duly corroborated by medical evidence - Test identification parade could not be held as prosecutrix had fled away from her village and gone to reside with her sister after being threatened by accused, therefore, accused should not be allowed to take benefit of this circumstance - Trial Court had thoroughly appreciated facts of case and concluded that consent was not possible in case of gang rape - There was no prohibition in law to convict accused of rape on basis of sole testimony of prosecutrix and law did not require that her statement be corroborated by statements of other witnesses - In statements of accused made by both accused that they were innocent - No explanation had been furnished by either of them as to why prosecutrix had deposed against them and involved them in such heinous crime - Hence, there was no cogent reason to interfere with findings of fact recorded by Courts below - Appeals lack merit - Appeals dismissed.
