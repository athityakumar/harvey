Josphine James v United India Insurance Company Limited and another

Supreme Court of India

14 August 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 527; (2013) 16 SCC 711; 2013 (3) ACC 654; 2013 ACJ 2418; 2014 (1) ALD(SC) 111; 2014(7) ALL MR 815; 2013 (100) ALR 730; 2013 (5) AWC 5048; 2013 (5) CTC 681; 2013(10) SCALE 340

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance

Summary: (1) Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988-Sections 170 (b) and 168-Quantum of compensation-Appeal by insurance company for reduction of quantum of compensation-Maintainability-High Court reduced overall compensation from R 13,07,000 awarded by Tribunal to amount of R 8,12,000-Maintainability of appeal of insurance company on question of quantum of compensation in absence of permission to be obtained by it from Tribunal under Section 170 (b)-High Court should not have allowed appeal of insurance company as it has got limited defence as provided under Section 149 (2)-Impugned judgment and award of High Court vitiated in law and hence liable to be set aside-Impugned judgment and award of High Court-Set aside-Compensation awarded by Tribunal of R 13,07,000 restored with interest @ 9% p.a.-Direction to deposit 50% of awarded amount with proportionate interest in nationalized Bank of choice of appellant for period of 3 years.

(2) Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988-Section 171-Award of interest-Interest @ 6% p.a. on compensation awarded both by Tribunal and High Court-Bad in law-In Municipal Corporation of Delhi v. Association of Victims of Uphaar Tragedy, (2011) 4 SCC 481 : 2012 (1) ACCD 409 : 2012 (3) SCCD 1180 : 2012 (2) AWC 1731 (SC), Apex Court awarded interest @ 9% p.a. on compensation-No justification for not applying ratio of aforesaid case to fact situation of present case-Therefore, interest @ 9% p.a. granted on compensation amount awarded by Tribunal as insurance company contesting case without justifiable reason.
