State of Bihar and another v Lalu Singh

Supreme Court of India

29 October 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Chandramauli Kumar Prasad, Jagdish Singh Khehar

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 712; (2014) 1 SCC 663; 2013 (6) CTC 796; 2013 (4) RCR(Criminal) 888; 2013(13) SCALE 336; [2013] 10 S.C.R. 552

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Submission of investigation report, Power of Inspector of CID

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 34 - Indian Arms Act, 1959, s. 27 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 173, 173(2) - Submission of investigation report - Power of Inspector of CID - Case was registered u/ss. 320, 34 IPC and s. 27 of the Act against respondent-accused - Officer-in-charge of police station took up investigation, but before he could complete and submit report in terms of s. 173 Director General of Police entrusted investigation to CID and task for conducting investigation was assigned to Inspector - Inspector of CID conducted investigation and submitted charge-sheet against accused persons - Trial Court took offence and directed for issuance of process - Accused filed petition before HC for quashing of prosecution and held that only officer in-charge of police station had authority to do that and therefore, charge-sheet submitted by Inspector CID was fit to be quashed - HC though dismissed petition made observation and held that it was officer-in-charge only who should file charge-sheet - Hence instant appeal by State on observation - Whether Inspector of CID should be treated in law as officer-in-charge of police station for purpose of submitting report contemplated u/s. 173(2) CrPC - Held, according to r. 431(b) Inspector of CID could exercise power of officer-in-charge of police station and once it was held so, its natural corollary was that Inspector of CID was competent to submit report as contemplated u/s. 173 CrPC - Instant case not one of those cases where officer- in-charge of police station had deputed Inspector of CID to conduct some steps necessary during course of investigation - Rather, in instant case, investigation itself was entrusted to Inspector of CID by order of Director General of Police - In such circumstances, it should not be necessary for officer-in-charge of police station to submit report u/s. 173(2) CrPC - Formation of opinion as to whether or not there was case to forward accused for trial should always be with officer-in-charge of police station or officers superior in rank to them, but in a case investigated by Inspector of CID all these powers should be performed by Inspector himself or officer superior to him - Hence, observations made by HC in impugned judgment was erroneous and deserved to be set aside - Appeal allowed.
