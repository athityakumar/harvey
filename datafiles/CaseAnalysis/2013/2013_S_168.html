Satya Pal v State Of Haryana & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

13 March 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Patnaik, Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 149; (2014) 13 SCC 397; AIR 2013 SC 2015; 2013 CRLJ 2731; 2013 (2) MLJ(Crl) 638; 2013 (2) RCR(Criminal) 403; 2013(4) SCALE 527; [2013] 4 S.C.R. 745

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 302, 304B, 498A - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 113B - Demand of dowry - Death - Conviction - Challenged - Appellant/accused allegedly pushed his wife/deceased into well as result she died on account of non-fulfilling of his demand of dowry - Trial Court held that offence u/s. 302 r/w s. 34 of IPC was proved by prosecution beyond reasonable doubt and demand of dowry as well as harassment and cruelty by appellant or any of his relatives in connection with demand for dowry was not proved and hence presumption u/s. 113B of the Act was not attracted and appellant and his family member could not be held guilty u/s. 304B of IPC - On appeal, HC held that since prosecution was able to prove both fact of demand of dowry and fact of harassment or cruelty meted out to deceased soon before her death, the presumption u/s. 113B of the Act was attracted and appellant was not able to rebut the presumption and was thus guilty of offences u/s. 304B of IPC as well as u/s. 498A of IPC - Hence instant appeal - Appellant contended that view taken by Trial Court on the evidence of PWs 1 and 2 was not a correct view inasmuch as there were substantial improvements made by PWs 1 and 2 in Court over their statements made to the police u/s. 161 of CrPC - Further, findings of HC on the basis of evidence of PWs 1 and 2 that deceased was subjected to subsequent demand of television, fridge and cooler and also was subjected to cruelty soon before her death were not at all correct - Trial Court was right in taking a view that delay of 51 hours in lodging FIR by PW 1 was not properly explained and, therefore, prosecution story could not be believed - Held, considering evidence of PWs 1 and 2 in its entirety, HC was right in coming to the finding that deceased was not only subjected to subsequent demand of dowry but also subjected to cruelty and harassment in connection with such demand for dowry soon before her death and that Trial Court had not taken a correct view on evidence of PWs 1 and 2 - Further, HC had also rightly drawn presumption u/s. 113B of the Act that appellant had caused dowry death of deceased within meaning of s. 304B of IPC and appellant had failed to rebut presumption u/s. 113B of the Act that it was he who had caused dowry death of deceased within the meaning of s. 304B of IPC -Appeal dismissed.
