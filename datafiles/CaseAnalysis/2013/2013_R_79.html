Director Of Income Tax (Exemption), New Delhi v Raunaq Education Foundation

Supreme Court of India

07 January 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 328; (2013) 2 SCC 62; JT 2013 (1) SC 323; [2013] 213 TAXMAN 19

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income-tax Act, 1961, ss. 11, 12, 13(2)(h), 13(2)(b), 80G - Donation amount - Undue favor - Assessment - Legality - Respondent/assesse/trust received two cheques by way of donation for sum of Rs.40 lacks each from Apollo Tyres Ltd - One of the cheques was dated 22-4-2002 and yet it was given in accounting year 2001-2002 - The said cheque for donation was received by respondent before 31-3-2002 but was honored after 1-4-2002 in accounting year 2002-2003 - While assessment proceedings, AO came to conclusion that accepting a post-dated cheque and by giving receipt in earlier accounting year, respondent had done undue favour and passed order u/ss. 11, 12 of the Act had not been continued - Respondent preferred appeal before Commissioner and same was dismissed - Aggrieved by said order of dismissal, respondent filed appeal before Tribunal - Tribunal, came to conclusion that there was no violation of provisions of s. 13 (2) (b) & 13(2)(h) of the Act and respondent had not acted in improper and illegal manner and noted fact that amount of donation received by way of cheque dtd: 22-4-2002 was treated as donation receivable and accordingly accounting treatment was given to said amount and allowed the appeal - Aggrieved by said order, Revenue filed appeal before HC and same was dismissed - Hence instant appeal by appellant - Revenue contended that HC committed an error by dismissing appeal, as there was breach of s. 13(2)(b) and 13(2)(h), though cheque was dtd: 22-4-2002 it was given by way of donation in earlier accounting year for which respondent had issued receipt and as trustees of respondent trust and directors of M/s Apollo Tyres Ltd. were closely related, an effort was made by respondent to do undue favor to M/s Apollo Tyres Ltd - Whether impugned order passed by Tribunal was justified - Held, looking into undisputed facts, SC viewed that no irregularity had been committed by the respondent trust and there was no violation of provisions of s. 13(2)(b) or s. 13(2)(h) of the Act - Fact that most of trustees of respondent and directors of M/s Apollo Tyres Ltd. were related was absolutely irrelevant - Upon careful perusal of order passed by Tribunal, SC did not find any error therein - Therefore SC was in agreement with view expressed by Tribunal as well as HC - Appeal dismissed.
