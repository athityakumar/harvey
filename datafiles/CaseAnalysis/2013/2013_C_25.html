Centre for Environment Law, WWF-I v Union of India and others

Supreme Court of India

15 April 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan, Chandramauli Kumar Prasad

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 236; (2013) 8 SCC 234; 2013 (4) ALD(SC) 9; 2013(4) ALL MR 464; 2013 (3) CLT(SC) 130; JT 2013 (7) SC 450; 2013 (5) MLJ 169; 2013(5) SCALE 710; [2013] 6 S.C.R. 757

Case Digest

Subject: Environment, Wildlife & Animal

Keywords: Endangered Species, Ecosystems, Centrally Sponsored Scheme, 2009, Biological Diversity Rules, 2004

Summary: Environment, Wildlife & Animal - Constitution - Administrative - Wild Life (Protection) Act, 1972, ss. 2(36) - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 48A and 51A(g) - Forest (Conservation) Act, 1980, s. 2 - Asiatic Lion - Wildlife Sanctuary - Protection - Directions - Instant applications were filed to directed UOI and State Govts. to prepare a rescue plan and translocation of Asiatic lion to Kuno forest for its long term survival and preservation, an endangered species from extinction and to make resources available for said purpose and that to ensure the genetic purity of wild species was maintained and to scientifically monitor Asiatic lion and its habitat be made on long term basis by involving institutes - Whether Asiatic lion was an endangered species and required to be protected from its extinction - Held, State Govt. stated that Centrally Sponsored Scheme titled 'Integrated Development of Wildlife Habitats' incorporated additional components and activities for implementing the provisions of the Act, National Wildlife Action Plan (2002-2016), recommendations of Tiger Task Force, 2005, and National Forest Commission, 2006 and the necessities felt from time to time for conservation of wildlife and biodiversity in country - However, in 2003, the Act was amended and 2 more categories of Protected Areas (PAs) i.e Conservation Reserves and Community Reserves were recognized - U/art. 48A of Constitution imposed duty on State to protect and improve environment and to safeguard forest and wildlife of country - U/art. 51A(g) of Constitution it was duty of every citizen to protect and improve natural environment including wildlife and to have compassion for living creatures - Human-wildlife conflict was fast becoming a critical threat to survival of many endangered species, like Asiatic Lion and such conflicts affect not only its population but also has broadened environmental impacts on ecosystem equilibrium and biodiversity conservation - There was likelihood of anthropocentric bias towards man, and rights of wild animals often tend to be of secondary importance but in universe man and animal were equally placed, but human rights approach to environmental protection in case of conflict, was often based on anthropocentricity - Man-animal conflict often results not because animals encroach human territories but vice-versa - Endangered not because of natural causes alone but because man failed to preserve and protect them - Often, it was said such conflicts was due human population growth, land use transformation, species habitat loss, degradation and fragmentation, increase in eco-tourism, access to natural reserves, increase in livestock population - When Asiatic lion's eco-system was not maintained, they lose their home and either forced to adopt new surroundings or human habitat - State was directed to give effect fully Centrally Sponsored Scheme 'Integrated Development of Wildlife Habitats', to save Asiatic lions from extinction - To take immediate steps to undertake intensive research and monitor Asiatic Lion population in Kuno Wildlife Sanctuary and other areas, where Asiatic Lions, including preparing them their genetic profile for future reference - To initiate wildlife training programs for officials of State Forest Dept., especially for managing said sanctuary and other areas where Asiatic Lions were found - To submit Annual Plan of Operations to Central Govt. detailing the proposed course of action and effective steps should be taken by State to protect Asiatic lions - Order accordingly.
