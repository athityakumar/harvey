Ashok Kumar Aggarwal v Neeraj Kumar and another

Supreme Court of India

22 November 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, Sharad Arvind Bobde

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 790; (2014) 3 SCC 602; 2014 (102) ALR 265; 2014 (1) JCC 449; JT 2014 (1) SC 359; 2013(14) SCALE 317; [2013] 12 S.C.R. 457

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Misrepresentation Of Fact, Contempt proceedings

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 161, 164, 195, 340 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 215 - Contempt of Courts Act, 1971, s. 20 - Misrepresentation of fact - Contempt proceedings - Appellant was threatening 'V' and extorting money from him while seeking information in respect of dealings in foreign exchange - Case was registered against appellant - Appellant moved bail application before Special Judge and the same was dismissed - Respondent no. 2/CBI filed remand application seeking further judicial custody of appellant - Later appellant was granted bail - Appellant filed petition before HC to take action against respondents on the ground that they had misrepresented or concealed the correct information - HC disposed of said petition observing that appellant shoould seek relief before Special Judge - Appellant filed application u/s. 340 r/w s. 195 CrPC before Special Judge for taking action against respondents - Application was dismissed - Aggrieved appellant filed appeal before HC - HC remanded matter to Special Judge - Special Judge heard said application and dismissed the same - Appellant filed petition before HC u/art. 215 of the Constitution r/w provisions of 1971 Act - HC disposed said petition observing that suppression or misrepresentation was not deliberate - Hence instant appeal - Whether impugned order passed by HC was justified - Held, HC was required to examine as to whether proper procedure was adopted in bringing petition u/art. 215 of Constitution and as to whether limitation as prescribed u/s. 20 of 1971 Act was attracted in the case - HC did not advert to any of such issue of paramount importance - No reasoning was given to reach conclusion that no deliberate attempt was made by respondents to cause any prejudice to appellant - As both parties had raised issues on facts as well as on law, HC ought to have dealt with case adverting to all relevant issues, particularly when appellant had made an allegation that his liberty was jeopardized by respondents by interfering with course of justice by misleading court - Impugned order was set aside and case was remitted to HC to decide afresh answering all factual and legal issues raised by parties - Appeal disposed of.
