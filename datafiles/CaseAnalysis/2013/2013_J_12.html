State of Rajasthan v Jamil Khan

Supreme Court of India

27 September 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Kurian Joseph, Chandramauli Kumar Prasad

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 642; (2013) 10 SCC 721; 2013 (4) MLJ(Crl) 324; 2013 (4) RCR(Criminal) 484; 2013(12) SCALE 200

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Mitigating Factor

Summary: (A) Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 376, 201 - Rape and Murder - Death sentence - Commutation by HC - Legality - Respondent allegedly raped and murdered the deceased minor - Trial Court convicted respondents u/ss. 302, 376 and 201 IPC and sentenced him to death - However, on appeal, HC maintained conviction but death sentence was commuted to life imprisonment - Hence, instant appeal - Whether instant case would fall under the rarest of rare category - Held, crime in instant case was gruesome and rendered a loathsome shock to community, hence, mitigating factors qua criminal needed to be considered as per Bachan Singh vs. State of Punjab and Others, 1980 Indlaw SC 586 - Respondent was nineteen years of age and was a labourer - However, poverty should be understood in light of whether it was a factor influencing commission of offence - In the instant case, mitigating factor of crime was not poverty - Crime of rape and murder of a minor child had nothing to do with poverty, socio-economic background or other psychic compulsions of criminal - Further, in view of overwhelming and aggravating circumstances, mitigating factor of young age was declined to consider - Accused was under influence of alcohol at the time of commission of offence also was not a mitigating factor - Hence, punishment of life imprisonment was grossly inadequate - Further, HC failed to discuss on any of aggravating and mitigating circumstances - There was no consideration as to whether the case on facts would fall under the rarest of rare category - Since nine years passed after substitution of respondent's death sentence by life imprisonment, it would not be just and proper to alter the sentence from life imprisonment to death at this stage - Appeal disposed of.

(B) Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, Chapter 28 - Death reference by HC - Scope - What is the scope of consideration of Death Reference by HC under Chapter 28 of CrPC - Held, HC should refer to special reasons found by Trial Court for inclusion of the case in the rarest of rare category - It should be seen that Trial Court had already passed a sentence and what is required was only confirmation before execution - On the facts and circumstances of the case, HC should consider whether the case actually falls under the rarest of rare category - HC should see whether there was presence or absence of special reasons as per decision in Kunal Majumdar vs. State of Rajasthan, 2012 Indlaw SC 291 - If on such consideration, HC found that special reasons were available in the facts and circumstances of the case, HC should confirm death sentence - In the absence of such compelling special reasons, HC should award only imprisonment for life - Appeal disposed of.

(C) Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 432, 433A - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 201, 302, 376 - Remission and Commutation - Power and scope - Whether there was any restriction on exercise of power u/s. 432 CrPC for remission and s. 433 CrPC for commutation in cases of minimum sentence - Held, s. 433A of CrPC imposed a restriction with regard to period of remission or commutation - When a sentence of imprisonment of life, where death was also one of punishments provided by law, was remitted or commuted, such person should not be released unless he had served at least fourteen years of imprisonment - In the event of State invoking its powers u/s. 432 or s. 433 of CrPC, sentence u/s. 376 of IPC should not be remitted or commuted before seven years of imprisonment - In the instant case, respondent was awarded life imprisonment u/s. 302 of IPC - U/s. 376 of IPC also respondent was awarded life imprisonment - Third substantive sentence was u/s. 201 of IPC - All these sentences were ordered to run concurrently - Sentence of life imprisonment was till the end of one's biological life - However, in view of power of State u/ss. 432 and 433 of CrPC in the instant case, sentences should run consecutively, in case there was remission or commutation - However, any such remission or commutation, if considered in respondent's case, should be granted only after mandatory period of fourteen years in the case of offence u/s. 302 of IPC - Appeal disposed of.
