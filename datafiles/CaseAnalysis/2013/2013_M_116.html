Vidya Dhar and others v Multi Screen Media Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

03 May 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Vikramajit Sen

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 288; (2013) 10 SCC 145; 2013 (99) ALR 471; 2013 (4) AWC 3400; JT 2013 (8) SC 566; 2013 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 1934; 2014 (173) PLR 53; 2013 (2) RCR(Civil) 930; 2013(6) SCALE 547; [2013] 5 S.C.R. 510

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, s. 13 (2) - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 120B - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 389 - JBT Teachers Scam - Conviction - Pending appeal - Proposed to broad cast in television - Prejudice to Fair trial - Petitioner no. 3 was Chief Minister of State from 1999 to 2005 and during his tenure 3206 Junior Basic Trained Teachers were recruited in the year 2000 - During that time, one 'S' was Director, Primary Education, State Govt. - From 2000 onwards, upon certain facts being brought to the knowledge of State Govt., several disciplinary and vigilance inquiries were initiated against said 'S' - FIR was registered against him u/s. 13(2) r/w s. 13 (1) (d) of the Act - 'S' filed writ petition, holding himself out to be whistle blower and claiming that while he was functioning as Director, he was pressurized into altering lists for appointment of Junior Basic Trained Teachers - Since, 'S' had resisted and did not succumb to such pressure, he was being unfairly targeted by administration - SC directed CBI to inquire into allegations made therein - CBI filed charge sheet against various persons including 'S' and petitioners as accused in said case - Special Judge convicted petitioners and 'S' amongst others u/s. 120B of IPC and u/s. 13 (2) of the Act - Aggrieved by said order, petitioners filed appeal before HC, along with applications u/s. 389 of CrPC for suspension of conviction as well as grant of bail - During, pendency of appeal before HC, petitioners and their family member came to know that respondent was proposing to broad caste episodes in question, in which dramatized version of 'JBT Teachers Scam' was to be presented - Petitioners filed application before HC to restrain respondent to telecast proposed episodes - Single Judge by order, restrained respondent from broadcasting/telecasting said program till application for suspension of sentence u/s. 389 of CrPC was decided - On appeal, DB allowed appeal and set aside injunction order passed by Single Judge - Hence instant appeal - Petitioners contended that said proposed telecast of episode would have prejudicial impact on rights of petitioners, who were entitled for fair trial - Further, though petitioners might stand convicted in respect of charges framed against them, appeal from order of conviction was continuation of trial and even at appellate stage, there was every possibility of bias against petitioners, which would be against concept of free and fair trial - Held, once trial was completed and petitioners were convicted and, thereafter, arrested, there was no further possibility of any bias against them at the time of hearing of appeal - Without going into question of right of freedom of speech of maker of Television Episodes, SC was convinced that no interference was called for with order of DB - However, in order to safeguard interests of petitioners, SC was of the view that certain restrictions could be imposed at the time of screening of said Episodes - Petition dismissed.
