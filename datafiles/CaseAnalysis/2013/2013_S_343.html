Kavi Raj and another v State of Jammu and Kashmir and others

Supreme Court of India

09 January 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

J. S. KHEHAR, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 12; (2013) 3 SCC 526; AIR 2013 SC 767; JT 2013 (1) SC 526; 2013(1) SCALE 197; [2013] 3 S.C.R. 620; 2013 (2) SLR 37

Case Digest

Subject: Health & Drug; Service

Keywords: Jammu & Kashmir Medical Education (Gazetted) Service Recruitment Rules, 1979, Jammu & Kashmir Medical (Gazetted) Service Recruitment Rules, 1970

Summary: Service - Administrative - Practice & Procedure - Repatriation - Cadre post - Jurisdiction - Challenged - Appellants (Asst.Surgeons) were appointed through State Service Commission in respondent (health department) - Appellants were posted as senior/junior house officers in different departments of Govt. medical college, in consonance with a Govt. Order dt.17.7.1997 - Thereafter, appellants were posted against vacant posts of Govt. medical colleges, but within a week by an order dt.7.1.1998 appellants were reverted to their parent dept., Directorate of Health Service - Aggrieved appellants filed a petition before HC - Single Judge held that order dt.7.1.1998 was passed in capacity of head of dept. was without any jurisdiction and was not in consonance with factual/legal position - Aggrieved respondent (State) filed an appeal before DB, which was allowed - Hence, instant appeal - Appellants contended that they were not appointed to Govt. Medical College, by way of deputation and were appointed against posts created by Health and Medical Education dept. - Whether impugned repatriation order passed against appellants was justified - Held, it was not possible for to accept that appointment of the appellants was substantively made to a cadre under Director of Medical Education - Appointment of appellants in Directorate of Medical Education, was clearly by way of deputation - Their posting at Govt. Medical College (and/or at hospitals associated therewith) was most certainly beyond their parent cadre, and thus, by way of deputation - Rules governing the conditions of service of gazetted employees of Directorate of Medical Education, did not have posts of Asst. Surgeons - Cadre of Asst. Surgeons was only found in rules of recruitment applicable to gazettled employees of Directorate of Health Service - Posts of Senior/Junior House Officer were distinct and separate from posts of Asst. Surgeons - Posts of Senior/Junior House Officers, were included in cadre of posts in Directorate of Medical Education - Appellants posting as Senior/Junior House Officers exhibit, that their appointment was not within Directorate of Health Services, but was against posts outside Directorate of Health Services - Even impugned order dt.7.1.1998 noted, that appellants were being temporarily deployed to meet exigency of shortage of doctors at Govt. Medical College - Based on disbursement of salary to appellants from funds of Directorate of Health Services, appellants should be deemed to be substantive employees of cadre of Asst. Surgeons of Directorate of Health Services - There was therefore no room for any doubt, that appellants were substantively appointed to Directorate of Health Services, and not in Directorate of Medical Education - Reversion/repatriation of appellants to their parent dept. Directorate of Health Services, was affirmed - Appellants would be repatriated/reverted to Directorate of Health Services, should be relieved from their postings in Directorate of Medical Education -Impugned order passed by DB did not suffer from any factual or legal infirmity and same was affirmed - Appeal disposed of.
