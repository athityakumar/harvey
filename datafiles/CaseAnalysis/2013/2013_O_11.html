Indian Soaps and Toiletries Makers Association and another v Ozair Husain and another

Supreme Court of India

07 March 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 131; (2013) 3 SCC 641; AIR 2013 SC 1834; 2013 (99) ALR 870; 2013 (2) AWC 1783; 2013 (3) Bom.C.R. 603; 2013 (2) CTC 464; JT 2013 (4) SC 27; 2013 (4) MLJ 373; 2013 (2) RCR(Civil) 307; 2013(3) SCALE 251; [2013] 4 S.C.R. 675

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Health & Drug

Keywords: Cosmetic, Drugs Technical Advisory Board, Registered Medical Practitioner, Prescription, Prevention of Food Adulteration (Fourth Amendment) Rules, 2001

Summary: Constitution - Health & Drug - Drugs and Cosmetics Rules, 1945 - Disclosure of ingredients - Vegetarian/non-vegetarian - Maintainability - Respondent filed PIL claiming right of a consumer of cosmetics, drugs and articles of food to full disclosure of ingredients of such product whereby a clear indication as to its origin (vegetarian/non-vegetarian) was made - DB held that consumer had fundamental right to know whether food products, cosmetics and drugs available for human consumption were of non-vegetarian or vegetarian origin - Hence, instant appeals - Whether HC had jurisdiction to direct manufacturers of drugs and cosmetics to display a particular symbol in their packages to identify ingredients of 'non- vegetarian' or ' vegetarian' origin - Held, Without fruitful consultation with Advisory Board, no amendment should be made or suggested to change label of drugs and cosmetics by Central Govt. - Further, the Rules should be amended by Central Govt. after taking into consideration any suggestion which Advisory Board might make in relation to amendments of the Rules - Earlier on a reference, Advisory Board had already opined that labelling of drugs as 'vegetarian' or 'non- vegetarian' or 'from animal sources' was not desirable and such proposal was not accepted - HC had no jurisdiction to direct Executive to exercise power by way of subordinate Legislation pursuant to power delegated by Legislature to enact a law in a particular manner, as had been done in instant case - Thus, it was not open to HC to suggest any interim arrangement as had been given by impugned judgment - Hence, writ petition filed by respondent being not maintainable for issuance of such direction, HC ought to have dismissed writ petition in limine - Therefore, impugned order of HC was set aside - Appeals allowed.
