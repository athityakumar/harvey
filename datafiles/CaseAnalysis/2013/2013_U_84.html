T. S. R. Subramanian and others v Union of India and others

Supreme Court of India

31 October 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan, Pinaki Chandra Ghose

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 722; (2013) 15 SCC 732; AIR 2014 SC 263; 2014(1) ALL MR 480; 2013 (6) AWC 6018; 2013 (6) KarLJ 513; 2013 (8) MLJ 201; 2013(13) SCALE 340; [2013] 11 S.C.R. 991; 2014 (1) SLR 1

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service

Keywords: All India Services (Conduct) Rules, 1968, Maharashtra Government Servants Regulation of Transfers and Prevention of Delay in Discharge of Official Duties Act, 2005, Civil Services Performance Standards and Accountability Bill, 2010, Civil Service Act, Central Staffing Scheme, 1996

Summary: (1) Constitution of India-Article 32-Civil service reforms-Establishment of Civil Service Board-Writ petition by eminent retired civil servants-Highlighting necessity of various reforms for preservation of integrity, fearlessness and independence of civil servants at Centre and State levels in the country-Reliefs prayed for in writ petition are based on Hotta Committee Report, 2004, 2nd Administrative Reforms Commission (10th Report) 2008, 2nd Administrative Service Commission (15th Report), Report of Committee on Prevention of Corruption, Santhanam Committee Report etc.-Direction to constitute independent Civil Service Board at Centre and State level with high ranking serving officers, who are specialists in their respective fields-Parliament to bring in proper legislation in setting up Civil Service Board-Writ petition allowed with certain directions.
