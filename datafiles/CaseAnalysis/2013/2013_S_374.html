Mary v State of Kerala and others

Supreme Court of India

22 October 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Chandramauli Kumar Prasad, V. Gopala Gowda

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 687; (2014) 14 SCC 272; AIR 2014 SC 1; 2014 (1) ALD(SC) 1; 2014(1) ALL MR 471; 2014 (1) BC 180; 2014 (1) CLT(SC) 34; 2013 (4) KLT 466; 2014 (1) RCR(Civil) 288; 2013(13) SCALE 151; [2013] 9 S.C.R. 1126

Case Digest

Subject: Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Frustration, Impossibility, Kerala Abkari Shops (Disposal in Auction) Rules, 1974

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Kerala Abkari Shops (Disposal in Auction) Rules, 1974 - Security deposit - Forfeited - Challenged - Appellant was a successful bidder in an auction for sale of privilege to vend arrack in Shop - Being declared as auction purchaser, appellant deposited 30% of the bid amount i.e. Rs.7,68,600/- on the same date and executed a temporary agreement in terms of r. 5(10) of Rules - Kalady was the holy birth place of Adi Sankaracharya and adjoining thereto existed a Christian pilgrim centre associated with St. Thomas - Residents of those areas objected to the running of any abkari shop - A large number of people collected and offered physical resistance to the opening of the abkari shops and the law and order enforcing agency could not assure smooth conduct of business - Aforesaid circumstances led the appellant to believe that it was impossible for her to run the arrack shop in the locality in question - Appellant, informed Board of Revenue, District Collector and Asst. Commissioner of Excise that because of mass movement it was not possible for her to open and run the shops - Accordingly, appellant requested them not to confirm the sale in her favour - Appellant further reserved her right to claim refund of the security amount - Cause shown by the appellant does not find favour with the authority and the Asst. Excise Commissioner called upon the appellant to pay a sum of Rs.33,41,400/- towards the balance amount payable by her, together with interest at the rate of 18% thereon - Appellant filed writ petition praying for direction to the State authorities to refund an amount of Rs.7,68,600/- paid by her as initial deposit - Single Judge allowed said writ petition and directed for refund of an amount of Rs.7,68,600/- along with interest - On appeal, DB set aside the judgment of the Single Judge - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order of the DB could be upheld - Held, in a contract under the Abkari Act and the Rules made thereunder, the licensee undertakes to abide by the terms and conditions of the Act and the Rules made thereunder which were statutory and in such a situation, the licensee could not invoke the doctrine of fairness or reasonableness - Hence, Court negative the contention of the appellant - No merit in appeal - Appeal dismissed.
