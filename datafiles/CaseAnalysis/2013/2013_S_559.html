Surender Kaushik And Others v State Of Uttar Pradesh And Others

Supreme Court of India

14 February 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 88; (2013) 5 SCC 148; AIR 2013 SC 3614; 2013 CRLJ 1570; JT 2013 (3) SC 472; 2013 (2) RCR(Criminal) 861; 2013(2) SCALE 491; [2013] 1 S.C.R. 1053

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 406, 420, 467, 468, 471, 504 and 506 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 156(3) - Similar cause of action - Improvement of allegations - Registration of FIRs - Quashing of - Challenged - Appellants (accused) allegedly committed offences punishable u/ss. 406, 420, 467, 468, 471, 504 and 506 of IPC - A FIR was registered against appellants - Aggrieved appellant to quash the FIR filed a petition before HC, that when already a FIR was registered on similar and identical cause of action and allegation, second FIR could not be registered - HC, by impugned order opined that it could not be held that no prima facie case was disclosed - Aggrieved appellant filed instant appeal - Appellants contended that law prohibited lodgment of second FIR in respect of same cognizable offence - Whether impugned registration of second FIR and order passed by HC were justified - Held, lodgment of two FIRs was not permissible in respect of one and same incident - Concept of sameness was given a restricted meaning - It did not encompass filing of a counter FIR relating to same or connected cognizable offence - What was prohibited was any further complaint by same complainant and others against same accused subsequent to registration of case under CrPC, for an investigation in that regard would have already commenced and allowing registration of further complaint would amount to an improvement of facts mentioned in original complaint - In instant case, appellants lodged a FIR against 4 accused persons alleging that they had prepared fake and fraudulent documents - Second FIR was registered on the basis of direction issued by Judicial Magistrate in exercise of power u/s. 156(3) of CrPC at the instance of another person alleging, inter alia, that he was neither present in meetings nor had he signed any of resolutions of meetings and accused persons, 5 in number, including appellant no. 1, had fabricated documents and filed same before competent authority - Second FIR was registered because of an order passed by Magistrate - If involvement of number of accused persons and nature of allegations were scrutinized, it becomes crystal clear that every FIR had a different spectrum - Allegations made were distinct and separate - That might be regarded as a counter complaint and could not be stated that an effort was made to improve the allegations that found place in first FIR - It was impossible to say that principle of sameness get attracted and if attracted and investigation was scuttled by quashing FIRs, complainants in the other two FIRs would be deprived of justice - Appellants lodged FIR making allegations against certain persons, but that did not debar the other aggrieved persons to move the Court for direction of registration of an FIR as there were other accused persons including complainant in first FIR involved in forgery and fabrication of documents and getting benefits from statutory authority - It was up to Trial Court that how trial would commence and concluded - Appellants or any of other complainants or accused persons might move appropriate Court for a trial in one Court - But to say that it was a second FIR related to same cause of action and incident and there was sameness of occurrence and an attempt was made to improvise the case was not correct - Appeal dismissed.
