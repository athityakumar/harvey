Dr. Ram Tawakya Singh and another v State of Bihar and another

Supreme Court of India

19 August 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, V. Gopala Gowda

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 549; (2013) 16 SCC 206; JT 2013 (11) SC 470; 2013(10) SCALE 462; [2013] 9 S.C.R. 117; 2013 (5) SLR 586

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Constitution; Service

Keywords: Patna University Act, 1976, UGC (Affiliation of Colleges by Universities) Regulations, 2009, Nalanda Open University Act, 1995

Summary: Service - Constitution - Bihar State Universities Act, 1976 (BSU), ss.10(1),10(2),12(1) - Patna University Act, 1976 (PU), ss.11(1),11(2),14(1) - Constitution of India, 1950, art.14 - Appointments - Proper procedure - Eligibility - Violation of scheme - Chancellor issued notification for appointment of Vice-Chancellors and Pro Vice-Chancellors - Impugned notifications were challenged on ground that Chancellor had not consulted State Govt. - Single Judge of HC allowed petition and quashed notifications - Several appeals and petitions were filed by both parties - DB of HC allowed petition and quashed appointments of Vice-Chancellors and Pro Vice-Chancellors and directed that fresh appointments be made in consultation with State Govt., appellant no.1 was debarred from being considered for appointment as Vice-Chancellor or Pro Vice-Chancellor - Hence, instant Appeals - Whether mechanism adopted by Chancellor in making appointments was blatantly violative of scheme of BSU Act and PU Act and art. 14 of Constitution - Held, facts showed that Chancellor was consistently flouting mandate of law and making appointments of Vice-Chancellors and Pro Vice-Chancellors without effectively consulting State Govt. and completely disregarding requirement of academic excellence and experience - Chancellor prepared list of persons proposed to be appointed as Vice-Chancellors and Pro Vice-Chancellors and forwarded same to State Govt. without adopting any transparent method of making selection - Thus, entire exercise undertaken by Chancellor was ex- facie against mandate of ss.10(1), 10(2),12(1) of BSU Act and ss.11(1), 11(2),14(1) of PU Act because he made every possible effort to prevent State Govt. from providing inputs about candidates and conveying its opinion on their suitability to be appointed as Vice-Chancellors and Pro Vice-Chancellors - Further, Chancellor acted in contemptuous disregard to pronouncements made by HC in two rounds of litigation - Chancellor also selected two persons for appointment as Vice-Chancellors and one person as Pro Vice-Chancellor despite fact that they were facing prosecution - Hence, mechanism adopted by Chancellor in making appointments was blatantly violative of scheme of BSU Act and PU Act and also art. 14 of Constitution - Appeals allowed.
