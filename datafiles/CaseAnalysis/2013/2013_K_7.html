M. B. Ramesh (Dead) by Lrs. v K. M. Veeraje Urs (Dead) by Lrs. and others

Supreme Court of India

03 May 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Gokhale, Ranjana Prakash Desai

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 285; (2013) 7 SCC 490; AIR 2013 SC 2088; 2013 (4) ALD(SC) 104; 2013(3) ALL MR 962; 2013 (99) ALR 487; 2013 (5) ALT(SC) 40; 2013 (4) AWC 4027; 2013 (3) Bom.C.R. 893; 2013 (3) CLT(SC) 103; 2013 (3) CTC 650; 2014 (2) G.L.R. 970; JT 2013 (7) SC 94; 2013 (5) KarLJ 160; 2013(5) M.P.H.T. 239; 2013 (2) RCR(Civil) 932; 2013 (120) RD 438; 2013(6) SCALE 534; [2013] 8 S.C.R. 573

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Keywords: Suit for declaration of property, Execution of Will

Summary: Family & Personal - Civil Procedure - Indian Succession Act, 1925,s. 63(c) - Suit for declaration of property - Execution of Will - Validity - Respondent No. 1/plaintiffs were sons of cousin of N, who died on 21-11-1970 - Respondents alleged that N left behind a Will executed way back on 24-10-1943, and registered with Sub-Registrar - Respondents claimed that through said Will N had bequeathed her property in their favour - N was the widow of C, whereas appellant was son of C from his second wife - After death of N appellants and respondents, applied for entering their names in revenue records as owners of concerned lands - Mutation Registrar passed in favour of appellants - Appellants filed suit before Trial Court for declaration of their title to suit property, and for a permanent injunction restraining appellants from interfering with their possession - Trial Court accepted the submissions of appellants and held that respondents had failed to prove the Will since it had not come in evidence of PW-2 that N had executed the Will in presence of second witness M or that M had also signed the Will in her presence - Thus, requirement of s. 63 (c) of the 1925 Act was not fulfilled - Aggrieved, respondents filed appeal before Appellate Court which was dismissed - Respondents preferred appeal before HC, which was allowed - Aggrieved, appellants preferred instant appeal - Whether Will of N was validly executed, whether same was duly proved by respondent No.1 - Held, issue of validity of Will would have to be considered in context of facts - It was true that in instant case, there was no specific statement by PW2 that he had seen other attesting witness sign the Will in presence of testator, but he had stated that other witness had also signed the document - PW2 had proved his signature, and on top of it had has also stated in cross examination that other witness M, N himself and one S and writer of the Will were all present while writing Will on 24-10-1943 which was registered on very next day - However, that statement by implication and inference would have to be held as proving required attestation by other witness - Issue raised in instant matter was with respect to due execution of Will, and that same was decided by Trial Court, as well as by Appellate Court on basis of an erroneous interpretation of evidence on record regarding circumstances attendant to execution of Will - Property mentioned in Will was admittedly ancestral property of N and H had to face a litigation, initiated by her husband, to retain her title and possession over property - Appellant was N's stepson whereas respondents were sons of her cousin - N should definitely desire that her ancestral property protected by her in a litigation with her husband did not go to a stepson, but would rather go to relatives on her side - It was not ignored that context while examining validity of Will - Respondents had proved that N had duly executed a Will on 24-10-1943 in favour of respondents, and bequeathed suit properties to them - Finding of Trial Court and Appellate Court was clearly erroneous - HC was justified in passing the impugned order - Appeal dismissed.
