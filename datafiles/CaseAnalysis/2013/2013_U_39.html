People's Union for Civil Liberties and another v Union of India and another

Supreme Court of India

27 September 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, Ranjana Prakash Desai, Ranjan Gogoi

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 641; (2013) 10 SCC 1; 2013 (5) ALD(SC) 173; 2013 (101) ALR 476; 2014 (3) AWC 2652; 2014 (1) Bom.C.R. 642; 2013 (4) CLT(SC) 79; 2013 (5) KarLJ 545; 2013 (4) KLT 104; 2013 (7) MLJ 537; 2013 (4) RCR(Civil) 669; 2013(12) SCALE 165; [2013] 12 S.C.R. 283

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Election; Practice & Procedure

Summary: (1) Constitution of India-Article 19 (1) (a)-Representation of People Act, 1951 (R.P. Act)-Section 128-Conduct of Election Rules, 1961 (Rules 1961)-Rules 41 (2) and 3 and 49-O-Constitutional validity of Rules 41 (2) and 3 and 49-O of Rules 1961-Rules 41 (2) and 3 and 49-O of Rules 1961 ultra vires Section 128 of R.P. Act and Article 19 (1) (a) of the Constitution to the extent they violate secrecy of voting-Direction to Election Commission to provide necessary provision in ballot papers/E.V.Ms. and another button called “none of above” (NOTA) may be provided in E.V.Ms. so that voters, who came to polling box and decide not to vote for any of candidates in the fray, are able to exercise their right not to vote while maintaining their right of secrecy-Government of India to provide necessary help for implementation of direction.

(2) Election-Negative voting-Mechanism of negative voting serves very fundamental and essential part of vibrant democracy.

(3) Constitution of India-Articles 32 and 19 (1) (a)-Right to move before Supreme Court under Article 32-Casting of vote is facet of right of expression of individual-Said right is provided under Article 19 (1) (a)-Therefore, any violation of said right gives aggrieved person right to approach Supreme Court under Article 32-No merit in contention that since right to vote is not fundamental right but is merely statutory right, hence, writ petition under Article 32 is not maintainable.

(4) Constitution of India-Article 19 (1) (a)-Violation of Article 19 (1) (a)-Right not to vote-Decision taken by voter after verifying credentials of candidate either to vote or not is form of expression under Article 19 (1) (a)-Fundamental right under Article 19 (1) (a) read with statutory right under Section 79 (d) of Representation of People Act, 1951 is violated unreasonably if right not to vote effectively is denied and secrecy is breached.
