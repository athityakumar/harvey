Kulwant Singh and others v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

02 April 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Madan B. Lokur, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 199; (2013) 4 SCC 177; 2013 ALL MR (Cri) 1909; 2013 CRLJ 2199; 2013 (3) JCC 1594; JT 2013 (4) SC 616; 2013 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1291; 2013 (2) RCR(Criminal) 596; 2013(5) SCALE 329; [2013] 5 S.C.R. 604

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Family & Personal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Poisoning

Summary: Criminal - Family & Personal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 161 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 113B - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 304B, 498A - Dowry death - Conviction - Sustainability - Deceased was married to accused/appellant No. 1 - Deceased was harassed and maltreated by her appellants - Deceased died under suspicious circumstances - A case was registered against accused persons/appellants u/ss. 304B, 498A of IPC - Trial Court found appellants guilty and passed conviction order - Aggrieved by order passed by Trial Court, appellants preferred Appeal before HC and same was dismissed - Hence instant appeal - Whether there was reason to disturb conclusions arrived by Courts below - Held, deceased was harassed by her husband and in-laws for dowry and that she died under abnormal circumstances due to aluminum phosphide poisoning - There was sufficient evidence to hold appellants guilty of offences punishable u/s. 304-B, 498-A of IPC - There was no reason to disturb conclusions concurrently arrived at both Courts below - Considering age of appellant No. 2 and 3 and their physical disability, a sympathetic view should be taken in matter as far as they were concerned - Law prescribed minimum of seven years imprisonment for an offence u/s. 304-B of IPC - There was no provision for reducing sentence for any reason whatsoever nor had any exception being carved out in law - Not losing sight of fact that even though appellants No. 2 and 3 were now aged, they were responsible for death of deceased through aluminum phosphide poisoning - Deceased was young lady when she died and SC could only guess trauma that her unnatural death would have caused to her parents - Sympathizing with an accused person or a convict does not entitle to us to ignore feelings of deceased or immediate family of deceased - Appeal dismissed.
