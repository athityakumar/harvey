Sebastiao Luis Fernandes (Dead) Through Lrs. and others v K. V. P. Shastri (Dead) Through Lrs. and others

Supreme Court of India

10 December 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, G.S. Singhvi, C. Nagappan

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 814; (2013) 15 SCC 161; 2014 (2) ALD(SC) 103; 2015(2) ALL MR 930; 2014 (102) ALR 871; 2014 (1) ALT(SC) 51; 2014 (2) AWC 1570; 2014 (1) CLT(SC) 351; JT 2014 (1) SC 496; 2014 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 2168; 2014 (1) RCR(Civil) 607; 2014 (122) RD 646; 2013(14) SCALE 761; [2013] 11 S.C.R. 1076

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Keywords: Maintainability, Suit for declaration and ownership

Summary: (1) Evidence Act, 1872-Sections 101, 102 and 167-Code of Civil Procedure, 1908-Section 100-Suit for decree of declaration of title on basis of only oral evidence-Claim of original plaintiff regarding 1/3rd ownership of suit schedule property-Claim of plaintiff-appellants to suit schedule property is clearly in dispute-Plaintiff-appellants could not produce any document before trial court to prove their title regarding suit schedule property-Clear and specific denial of right of plaintiff over suit schedule property as well as right of ancestors of said plaintiff by defendant No. 1 (auction purchaser)-Plaintiff-appellants not produced on record any document of inscription of suit schedule property in their name-Lack of evidence in favour of plaintiff-appellants to prove their title to suit schedule property-In suit for disputed property-Burden to prove title to land squarely falls on plaintiff-Incumbent upon plaintiff to prove title as well as fact that suit schedule property was ancestral-Courts below erred in assuming certain facts which are not in existence to come to erroneous conclusion in absence of title document in justification of claim of plaintiff in respect of suit schedule property and ignored pleadings of defendants though they have specifically denied ownership right claimed by original plaintiff in respect of suit schedule property-Courts below committed serious error in holding that there was admission of defendants in pleadings with respect to ownership of 1/3rd of suit schedule property by original plaintiff-It was upon plaintiff-appellants to furnish proof regarding ownership of 1/3rd share of suit schedule property and discharge their burden of proof as per Sections 101 and 102 of Evidence Act-Grant of decree by courts below in favour of plaintiff-appellant-Erroneous in law-High Court rightly exercised its appellate jurisdiction under Section 100 of C.P.C. by framing correct substantial question of law with reference to legal position and applied same to fact situation of case-No error in impugned judgment and decree of High Court-No reason to interfere with impugned judgment and decree of High Court.(2) Decree-Settled law that for decree to be passed on admission-Admission should be clear and unambiguous.
