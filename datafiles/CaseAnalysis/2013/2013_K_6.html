Puttamma and others v K. L. Narayana Reddy and another

Supreme Court of India

09 December 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 855; (2013) 15 SCC 45; 2014 (1) ACC 3; 2014 ACJ 526; AIR 2014 SC 706; 2014 (3) AWC 2245; 2013 (4) CLT(SC) 353; JT 2014 (2) SC 201; 2014 (1) KLT 738; 2014 (1) MLJ 739; 2014 (1) RCR(Civil) 443; 2014 (1) RLW 244; 2013(15) SCALE 437

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Keywords: Fatal Accidents Act, 1846, Death Or Permanent Disablement, English Fatal Accidents Act, 1846, Motor Vehicles (Amendment) Bill, 2012, English Fatal Accident Act, 1896, Fatal Accident Act, 1846

Summary: (1) Motor Vehicles Act, 1988-Section 166-Motor accident claims under Section 166-Multiplier method-Split multiplier method-In absence of any specific reason and evidence on record, Tribunal or Court should not apply split multiplier in routine course and should apply multiplier as per decision of Supreme Court in case of Sarla Verma v. Delhi Transport Corporation, (2009) 6 SCC 121 : 2009 (2) ACCD 924 : 2009 (2) SCCD 937 : 2009 (3) AWC 2138 (SC) as affirmed in case of Reshma Kumari v. Madan Mohan, (2013) 9 SCC 65.

(2) Motor Vehicles Act, 1988-Section 163A (3), Second Schedule-As was enacted in 1994 now become redundant, irrational and unworkable, due to changed scenario including present cost of living, current rate of inflation and increased life expectancy-More than 19 years passed but no amendment made-Central Government required to amend Second Schedule.

(3) Motor Vehicles Act, 1988-Section 171-Award of interest-Under Section 171 no rate of interest fixed-Duty bestowed upon Tribunal to fix rate of interest-In Supe Dei v. National Insurance Co. Ltd., (2009) 4 SCC 513, Supreme Court held that proper interest would be 9% p.a.-In view of Section 171 and observation of Supreme Court-Tribunals and Courts to decide rate of interest after taking into consideration rate of interest allowed by Supreme Court in similar case and other factors such as inflation, change in economy, policy adopted by Reserve Bank of India from time to time and period since when case is pending.

(4) Motor Vehicles Act, 1988-Sections 166 and 168-Compensation-Enhancement-Death-Deceased 48 years old at time of death-Was working as Typesetting Assistant in Computer Section-Tribunal awarded sum of R 9,03,600 as compensation-High Court enhanced compensation amount to R 11,25,000-Income of deceased assessed as R 12,442-Deceased would have continued in service for another 12 years and he would have entitled for pension-Therefore, if increase in future income taken at 50% it will came to R 18,663-Four dependant family members-Deduction towards personal and living expenses of deceased should be 1/4th instead of 1/3rd-If 1/4th amount deducted from income of deceased it will come to R 13,998 (R 12,442 + R 6,221 - R 4,665)-High Court instead of deciding just compensation allowed meager enhancement of compensation-High Court introduced concept of split multiplier and departed from multiplier system generally used in light of decision in Sarla Verma case, (2009) 6 SCC 121 : 2009 (2) ACCD 924 : 2009 (2) SCCD 937 : 2009 (3) AWC 2138 (SC), without disclosing any reason-Multiplier of 13 applicable-Judgment of High Court perverse and contrary to evidence on record having not considered future prospects of deceased and for adopting split multiplier method against law laid down by Supreme Court-Impugned judgment of High Court-Set aside-Claimants entitled for total compensation of R 23,43,688 instead of R 11,25,000-Compensation amount enhanced by sum of R 12,18,688-Enhanced amount to carry interest @ 12% from date of filing of claim petition.
