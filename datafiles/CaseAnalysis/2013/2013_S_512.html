Md. Ishaque and others v State of West Bengal and Others

Supreme Court of India

03 May 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 281; (2013) 14 SCC 581; 2013 ALL MR (Cri) 2205; JT 2013 (8) SC 591; 2013 (3) RCR(Criminal) 58; 2013(6) SCALE 523; [2013] 5 S.C.R. 518

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 304 Part I - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellants (accused) allegedly committed offence u/s. 302 of IPC - Trial Court by its judgment convicted appellants u/s. 302 of IPC - Aggrieved appellants filed an appeal before HC, which was dismissed confirming sentenced awarded by Trial Court - Hence, instant appeal - Appellants contended that prosecution failed to establish the place of occurrence, time of alleged assault and manner of the alleged assault and there was no corroborative medical evidence to support various injuries alleged to have sustained by few of witnesses - Whether HC was justified in confirming conviction of appellants - Held, PW1 (injured and informant), in his statement, categorically stated that incident had occurred on 5-7-1983 at 'S' village and his evidence found full support from the evidence adduced by PW20 (IO) - Facts indicated that an incident took place on 4-7-1983 which resulted in death of 13 persons and due to that occurrence, there was an atmosphere of terror over the surrounding villages and also as a sequel of that massacre of village 'M', 'S' village was attacked - PW1 and 6 were directly affected by the incident that had occurred at 'S', in which involvement of appellants was clearly established - Prosecution succeeded in proving the place of occurrence, time of occurrence and manner of assault made on injured persons who were all examined by Court and their evidence fully corroborates the prosecution case - There was sufficient evidence to show that incident had happened on 5-7-1983, as projected by prosecution - Prosecution successfully proved that it was appellants and others who had committed the crime, so found by Trial Court and HC - Large number of persons were involved in the incident - Several injuries were caused by appellants on vital parts of deceased and injured persons, with dangerous weapons and the injuries were sufficient, as certified by doctor, in the ordinary course of nature to cause death and accused persons intended to inflict the injuries that were found on the person of deceased and injured persons - Appellants caused the injuries with deadly weapons, thus, intention could be presumed regarding causing injuries as were likely to cause death, which falls u/s. 304 Part I of IPC and conviction ordered by Trial Court u/s. 302 of IPC was converted to s. 304 Part I of IPC - Appellants were sentenced to undergo RI of 10 years - Appeal disposed of.
