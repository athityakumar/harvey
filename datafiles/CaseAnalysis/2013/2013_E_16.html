Essar Teleholdings Limited and others v Registrar General, Delhi High Cour and others

Supreme Court of India

01 July 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 381; (2013) 8 SCC 1; AIR 2013 SC 2300; JT 2013 (9) SC 236; 2013 (3) MLJ(Crl) 332; 2013 (3) RCR(Criminal) 709; 2013(7) SCALE 739; [2013] 7 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure; Telecom

Keywords: 2G scam case

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Telecom - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120B, 420 - 2G Scam case - Second charge sheet - Cognizance - Justifiability - CBI lodged FIR against 'unknown officers of Dept. of Telecommunications and unknown private persons/companies and others' for causing wrongful loss to Govt. by criminal misconduct and criminal conspiracy in distribution of UAS licenses in January, 2008 - Petitioners filed writ petition alleging that FIR lodged by CBI was not being investigated and directed CBI to investigate the same - HC dismissed writ petition - Petitioner filed SLP before SC against impugned order of HC wherein leave was granted - However, CBI filed second charge sheet against petitioners and others for alleged commission of offences u/ss. 420, 120B IPC - Special Judge by an order was pleased to take cognizance of second supplementary charge sheet and petitioners and others were summoned - Hence, instant writ petitions - Whether impugned order passed by Special Judge was sustainable - Held, 2G Scam case was triable by Special Judge against persons accused of offences punishable under IPC - However, petitioners were shown as co-accused in second supplementary charge sheet filed in 2G Scam case, it was open to Special Judge to take cognizance of offence u/ss. 120B and 420 IPC - Hence, in instant case there was nothing on record to suggest that petitioners would not get fair trial and might face miscarriage of justice - No interference against impugned order taking cognizance of offence against petitioners by Special Judge - Petitions dismissed.
