Vijay v Laxman & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

07 February 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Gyan Sudha Misra, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 82; (2013) 3 SCC 86; 2013 (1) BC 743; 2013 (2) CompLJ 107; 2013 (2) JabLJ 1; 2013 (2) JCC (NI) 103; JT 2013 (2) SC 562; 2013 (2) KLT 157; 2013 (3) MahLJ 869; 2013(4) M.P.H.T. 171; 2013 (2) MPLJ 615; 2013 (1) RCR(Civil) 980; 2013 (1) RCR(Criminal) 1028; 2013(2) SCALE 368; [2013] 4 S.C.R. 80; 2013 (2) WLN(SC) 28

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Criminal

Keywords: Banking Public Financial Institutions and Negotiable Instruments (Amendment) Act, 1988

Summary: Criminal - Banking & Finance - Banking Public Financial Institutions and Negotiable Instruments (Amendment) Act, 1988, ss. 118, 138 and 139 - Acquittal - Challenged - Instant appeal filed against the judgment and order passed by a Single Judge of the HC, whereby the conviction and sentence of one year along with fine imposed on the respondent for commission of an offence u/s. 138 of the Act had been set aside and the criminal revision was allowed - Whether judgment and order of the HC which reversed the concurrent findings of fact recorded by the Trial Court was justified -

Held, First Appellate Court misdirected itself by ignoring the defence version which succeeded in dislodging the complainant's case on the strength of convincing evidence and thus discharged the burden envisaged u/ss. 118 (a) and 139 of the Act - Further, absence of any detail of the date on which the loan was advanced as also the absence of any documentary or other evidence to show that any loan transaction had indeed taken place between the parties was a significant circumstance - Also the fact that the cheque was presented on the day following the altercation between the parties was a important circumstance - Version of the respondent that the cheque was not returned to him and the complainant presented the same to wreak vengeance against him was a circumstance that could not be easily rejected - In the totality of the circumstances, the HC was justified in its conclusion that the prosecution had failed to make out a case against the accused and in acquitting him of the charges - Appeal dismissed.
