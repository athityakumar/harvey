Jitendra Kumar Khan and others v Peerless General Finance and Investment Company Limited and others

Supreme Court of India

07 August 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 517; (2013) 8 SCC 769; 2013 (100) ALR 402; 2013 (6) ALT(SC) 48; 2013 (6) AWC 6359; 2014 (2) CLT(SC) 1; 2014 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 196; 2013 (4) RCR(Civil) 725; 2013 (121) RD 116; 2013(12) SCALE 21; [2013] 7 S.C.R. 1093

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: (1)Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908-Order VI, Rule 17 and Order VIII, Rules 6 and 6A-Amendment of pleadings-Amendment in written statement-Counter-claim or legal set-off not permissible at stage when application to amend written statement filed-Claim put forth in written statement cannot be regarded as Counter-claim or legal set-off-Claim of equitable set-off, as put forth-Tenable-Division Bench of High Court rightly allowed amendment on base that claim put forth could be treated as plea in nature of equitable set-off-No merit in plea that in garb of equitable set-off endeavour has been made to introduce claim which is really in nature of set-off as incorporated under Order VIII, Rule 6-Single Judge of High Court not justified in rejecting application for amendment.

(2) Code of Civil Procedure, 1908-Order VIII, Rule 6-Set-off-Equitable set-off is different than legal set-off-It is independent of provisions of C.P.C.
