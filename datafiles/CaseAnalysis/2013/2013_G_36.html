Gopal & Anr. v State Of Rajasthan

Supreme Court of India

18 January 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, J. S. KHEHAR

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 29; (2013) 2 SCC 188; 2013 CRLJ 1297; JT 2013 (1) SC 639; 2013 (1) MLJ(Crl) 617; 2013 (2) RCR(Criminal) 279; 2013(1) SCALE 445; [2013] 1 S.C.R. 385

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302 r/w s. 34 and 323 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 105 - Double murder - Right of private defence - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellants (accused) allegedly committed offences u/ss. 147, 148, 149, 302 and 323 of IPC - Trial Court convicted appellants u/ss. 302 r/w s. 34 and 323 of IPC and sentenced them to undergo RI for life - Aggrieved appellants filed appeals before HC - DB by impugned judgment modified order of Trial by acquitting other accused and confirmed conviction of appellants (accused nos. 1 and 3) - Hence, instant appeal - Appellants contended that members of complainant's party were aggressors and attacked on accused persons causing injuries to appellants and that they had a right of private defence - Whether right of private defence was available to appellants - Held, materials placed and relied on by prosecution show that deceased 'R' and PW7 and 8 had gone to the field of appellants and there was a fight between both groups - It was clear that appellants fought to repel the attack and in course of incident, both sides sustained injuries, as a result of which, 'R' died - In such circumstances, it would be possible for Court to accept claim of appellants that since they were defending themselves, they had a right of private defence - In fact, HC accepted said stand - U/s. 105 of the Act, burden of proof was on accused, who sets up plea of self-defence, and, in absence of proof, it was not possible for Court to presume the truth of plea of self-defence - Evidence of PW7, 8 and 10 clearly established that deceased 'P' was not present at the place where 'R' was assaulted - After inflicting injuries on person of 'R', appellants ran towards 'P', who was standing 10 steps away from the place of incident - Appellants have no right to invoke right of self defence by chasing 'P' and causing fatal injuries on him - Reasonable apprehension has disappeared when witnesses noticed that 'P' was running away from scene in order to escape, in such circumstances though appellants were entitled to plea of private defence insofar as death of 'R' was concerned, they were not justified in availing same for cause of death of 'P' - Appellants exceeded their limit and material revealed that appellants chased 'P' at some distance, pushed him and inflicted several blows with lathis due to which 'P' died - Trial Court and HC were fully justified in confirming conviction of appellants - Appeal dismissed.
