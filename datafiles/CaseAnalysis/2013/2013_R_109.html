Rushi Guman Singh v State of Orissa and others

Supreme Court of India

09 April 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Surinder Singh Nijjar, M.Y. Eqbal

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 228; (2013) 11 SCC 266; 2013 (137) FLR 753; JT 2013 (6) SC 394; 2013(5) SCALE 480; [2013] 4 S.C.R. 862; 2013 (4) SLR 108

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Deemed Suspension, Central Civil Services (CCA) Rules, 1965

Summary: Service - Central Civil Services (CCA) Rules, 1965, rr. 12(1), 12(3), 12(4) - Suspension - Disciplinary proceedings - Deemed suspension - Legality - Appellant (employee) was working with respondent (State Govt.) - Disciplinary Authority passed an order dt.14-2-2003 imposing punishment of removal on appellant - Aggrieved appellant challenged said order before Tribunal, which was dismissed - Aggrieved appellant filed a petition before HC, which was allowed directing Disciplinary Authority to provide reasonable opportunity to appellant before taking a final decision in the matter related to findings on charges framed against appellant - Thereafter, Disciplinary Authority issued show cause notice to appellant for his representation and informed that as per r. 12(4) of the Rules, appellants' suspension from the date of original order of removal dt.14-2-2003, should continue to remain until further orders - Aggrieved appellant moved Tribunal, which was dismissed - Aggrieved appellant filed a petition before HC, and challenged order passed by Govt., which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Appellant contended that respondent wrongly invoked r. 12(4) of the Rules - Whether appellant was deemed to be suspended w.e.f 14-2-2003 - Held, even though order of removal dt.14-2-2003 was set aside by HC on the ground that Disciplinary Authority had passed order dt.14-2-2003, in breach of rules of natural justice, it was not necessary for Govt. to pass an order of suspension of appellant u/r. 12(1) of the Rules - HC directed Disciplinary Authority to continue with disciplinary proceedings after giving an opportunity of hearing to appellant - R. 12(1) of the Rules enabled appointing authority or any authority to which it was subordinate to place a Govt. servant under suspension where a disciplinary proceeding against him was contemplated or was pending - Said stage in instant case came to an end when appellant was suspended for the first time on 12-6-1998, undoubtedly which was revoked on 20-7-1999 - Thereafter, appellant was removed from service on 14-2-2003 when Disciplinary Authority disagreed with findings of enquiry officer exonerating the appellant - It was that order of removal which was set aside by HC on 24-6-2008 - At that stage, dept. had no option but to pass an order u/r. 12(4) of the Rules directing that appellant should be deemed to have suspended w.e.f 14-2-2003 - Appeal dismissed.
