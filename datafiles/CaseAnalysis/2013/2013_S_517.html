Ajahar Ali v State of West Bengal

Supreme Court of India

04 October 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, Sharad Arvind Bobde

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 655; (2013) 10 SCC 31; AIR 2013 SC (Supp) 18; 2013 ALL MR (Cri) 4094; 2014 CRLJ 18; 2013 (4) JCC 2523; 2014 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 533; 2013 (4) MLJ(Crl) 379; 2013 (4) RCR(Criminal) 611; 2013(12) SCALE 410; [2013] 9 S.C.R. 911

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 354 - Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act, 2000 - Outrage modesty of woman - Conviction - Challenged - Prosecution alleged that appellant forcibly caught hold of X's hair and planted a kiss, resultantly, she suffered a cut over her lower lip and started bleeding - Trial Court convicted appellant guilty for the offence punishable u/s. 354 of IPC and sentenced him to suffer simple imprisonment for 6 months and further to pay a fine of Rs.1,000/- - On appeal, HC affirmed the order of the Trial Court - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order of the Trial Court as affirmed by the HC could be upheld - Held, as the appellant had been awarded only 6 months imprisonment, considering the matter under the Act would not serve any purpose at such a belated stage - HC had been of the opinion that appellant had been dealt with very leniently and it was a fit case where the HC wanted to enhance the sentence but considering the fact that the incident occurred long back, the HC refrained to do so - Appellant was directed to surrender within a period of 4 weeks to serve out the sentence - Appeal dismissed.
