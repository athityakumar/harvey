Mahadeo (Dead) and others v State of Uttar Pradesh and others

Supreme Court of India

08 April 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

M.Y. Eqbal, Surinder Singh Nijjar

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 217; (2013) 4 SCC 524; AIR 2013 SC 1628; 2013 (4) ALD(SC) 51; 2013 (3) AWC 2787; 2013 (2) CLT(SC) 78; JT 2013 (8) SC 430; 2013 (4) KarLJ 97; 2013 (4) MLJ 602; 2013 (3) RCR(Civil) 570; 2013(5) SCALE 457B; [2013] 2 S.C.R. 539; 2013 (4) UPLBEC 2708

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property

Keywords: De-requisition land

Summary: Land & Property - Administrative - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4, 4(1), 6, 9 and 18 - Acquisition of land - De-requisition land - Legality - State of UP issued notification dt.27-1-1990 u/s. 4(1) of the Act proposed to acquire 246.931 acres of land - Land was acquired for purpose of construction of residential/commercial building under planned Development Scheme by Meerut Development Authority/respondent No. 4/(MDA) - Respondent No.4 issued notice u/s. 9 of the Act - Appellants filed objections - Respondent No. 3/Special Land Acquisition Officer passed award - Appellants applied before Land Acquisition Officer for making a reference u/s. 18 of the Act - Respondent No. 3 referred the matter to District Judge - Appellants alleged that respondent No. 4 decided to withdraw acquisition of land except land measuring 42.018 acres for which compensation was paid - Respondent No. 4 decided to de-requisition land measuring 204.912 acres - State Govt. decided not to accede to decision of respondent No. 4 for de-requisition of land - Appellants filed writ petitions - HC disposed of writ petitions with a direction to respondent No. 4 to press its resolution if Authority was not in need of said land - Hence, instant appeal - Whether HC was justified in passing the impugned order - Held, appellants were not original owners of land at time when notifications u/ss. 4, 6 and 9 of the Act were issued - Some of appellants were purchasers of land from land owners after notification was issued u/s. 4 of the Act - However, internal correspondences between MDA and State that by resolution dt.17-9-1997 MDA took a decision to withdraw acquisition and to get approval from State Govt., a writ of mandamus could be issued directing State or MDA to de-notify or de-requisition land which was acquired after following due process of law and an award to effect was passed by Special Land Acquisition Officer - There was no dispute with regard to settled proposition of law that once land was acquired and mandatory requirements were complied with including possession had taken land vests in State Govt. free from all encumbrances - Land in question was acquired by State Govt. for purpose of expansion of city i.e. construction of residential/commercial building under planned Development Scheme by MDA and that major portion of land was already utilized by Authority - Some land was left at relevant time, that did not give any right to Authority to send proposal to Govt. for release of land in favour of land owners - HC was justified in passing the impugned order - Appeal dismissed.
