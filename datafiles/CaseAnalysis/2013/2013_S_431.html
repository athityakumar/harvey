Sham Lal and others v State of Punjab and others

Supreme Court of India

06 August 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, Gyan Sudha Misra

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 515; (2013) 14 SCC 393; 2014 (4) AWC 3711; JT 2013 (11) SC 102; 2013 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 3273; 2013 (4) RCR(Civil) 50; 2013(10) SCALE 67

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Withdrawal From Acquisition

Summary: Land & Property - Punjab Town Improvement Act, 1922, s. 36 - Land acquisition - Challenged - Appellants own small plots of land within the municipal limits of Bhatinda - Appellants have constructed houses on their respective plots - By resolution dt. 12-12-2000, Improvement Trust framed the scheme covering an area measuring 45.57 acres - In the resolution, it was specifically mentioned that 'A' class buildings be adjusted in the scheme and, as per the policy of the State Government, small plot holders may also be adjusted on 'as is where is' basis subject to the payment of development and exemption charges - Thereafter, District Town Planner inspected the area of the scheme and pointing out several deficiencies in the layout plan - State Government rejected the recommendation of the Trust to adjust the vacant plots coming within the boundaries of the scheme - Appellants filed writ petition for release of their plots by pointing out that while preparing the scheme and notifying the same, the Trust and the State Government ignored the fact that a large number of houses had already been constructed - DB dismissed said writ petitions - Appellants filed writ petition against said order submitting that the impugned order was liable to be set aside because HC had not assigned cogent reasons for negating their challenge to the acquisition of land - Whether the policy framed by the State Government to grant exemption only to 'A' class constructions was constitutional - Held, reasons assigned by the State Government for not excluding the appellants' land from acquisition, namely, the quality of construction was irrelevant and extraneous and the HC committed serious error by rejecting the appellants' plea that the respondents had discriminated them in the matter of grant of exemption from acquisition - An additional fact of which cognizance deserves to be taken was that the scheme framed by the Trust was not only for construction of ring road but also for development of commercial, residential and institutional plots - It was difficult, if not impossible, to fathom any reason why the Secretary to the Government rejected the recommendations made by the President for exemption of the land belonging to small plot holders despite the fact that the acquired land was intended to be utilised not only for construction of ring road but also for development of commercial, residential and institutional plots, which would have been ultimately allotted to other persons by draw of lots or by auction - Impugned order of HC was set aside and the State Government and the Trust were directed to exclude the appellants' land from the scheme except to the extent the same was required for constructing the ring road - Appeals partly allowed.
