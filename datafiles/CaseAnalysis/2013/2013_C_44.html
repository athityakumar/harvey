Pradip Kumar Maity v Chinmoy Kumar Bhunia and others

Supreme Court of India

01 July 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Vikramajit Sen, Altamas Kabir

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 857; (2013) 11 SCC 122; 2013 (5) AWC 5040; 2013 (138) FLR 769; JT 2013 (9) SC 509; 2013(8) SCALE 205; [2013] 7 S.C.R. 117

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: (1) Persons with Disabilities (Equal Opportunities, Protection of Rights and Full Participation) Act, 1995 (Disabilities Act)-Sections 2 (t), 38, 39 and 40-Employment-Appointment-Relaxation of age limit-Relaxation in maximum age as consequence of suffering from hearing disability to extent of sixty per cent-Entitlement-Appellant failed to disclose any legislation or Rules or Orders that would facilitate support or legitimise his claim for being conferred with advantage of age relaxation, which is presently available only to S.C./S.T./O.B.C. candidates-Disabilities Act explicitly postulate compulsory relaxation of age of candidate suffering from any of statutorily recognised disabilities-Failure to mandate age relaxation is lacuna in legislation-Disabilities Act does not in terms provide for age relaxation vis-a-vis persons suffering from disabilities-Keeping extant legislation and executive fiats in perspective, since age relaxation is not available post 1st November, 1999 to physically handicapped in State of W.B.-No succour can be extended to appellant who is indubitably suffering from disability-Since Disabilities Act postulates age relaxation only as directory or expectant-Government order will continue to hold field.

(2) Interpretation of Statutes-Word, "shall" and 'may' employed in statute-Where legislature uses words 'shall' and 'may' in close proximity of each other-There is no room to construe word 'may' as mandatory-When both words 'shall' and 'may' are used in same section, 'shall' imposes obligation or imperative whilst 'may' connotes directive or discretionary power
