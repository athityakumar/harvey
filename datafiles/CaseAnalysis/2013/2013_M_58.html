N. Manjegowda v Manager, United India Insurance Company Limited

Supreme Court of India

12 November 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Shiva Kirti Singh, G.S. Singhvi, C. Nagappan

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 871; 2014 (2) ACC 348; 2014 ACJ 617; 2013 (101) ALR 697; 2014 (1) AWC 20; 2014 (4) MahLJ 37; 2015(2) M.P.H.T. 81; 2014 (2) MPLJ 705; [2013] 12 S.C.R. 350

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance

Summary: Motor Vehicles Act, 1988-Sections 166 and 168-Compensation-Injury case-Enhancement-Appellant, advocate by profession at time of accident was aged about 36 years-Appellant sustained grievous injuries over his hands, legs and spinal cord-Appellant had no strength in hands and legs, there was full loss of sensation below neck and urinary track was blocked-Appellant sustained whole body disability of 50%-Appellant having partial sensory loss all over his limbs and lacked proper coordination in all four limbs-Appellant requires an assistant for daily routine work-Difficult for appellant to practice as advocate-Tribunal awarded total compensation of R 8,87,300 with interest @ 6% p.a.-High Court reduced compensation amount to R 4,67,500-High Court erred in reducing loss of income due to disability-Loss of earning to be treated as 70% and appropriate multiplier to be 16 in place of 13-Award made by Tribunal modified by adding R 4,00,000 towards head "loss of income due to disability"-Rest of order of Tribunal, confirmed-Total compensation payable to appellant R 12,87,300 in place of R 8,87,300 as awarded by Tribunal-Enhanced amount of R 4,00,000 to carry interest @ 6% p.a.-Impugned order of High Court-Set aside-Appeal allowed-Costs quantified at R 15,000.
