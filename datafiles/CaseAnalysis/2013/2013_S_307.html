State of Gujarat v Girish Radhakrishnan Varde

Supreme Court of India

25 November 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Gyan Sudha Misra, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 760; (2014) 3 SCC 659; AIR 2014 SC 620; 2014 ALL MR (Cri) 341; 2014 CRLJ 570; 2014 (1) JCC 611; 2014 (1) RCR(Criminal) 857; 2013(14) SCALE 360; [2013] 12 S.C.R. 930

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure 1973, ss. 154 and 211 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 120-B, 364, 365, 387, 386, 394, 398, 511, and 506(2) - Arms Act, 1959, s. 25 - Addition of section of IPC - Power of Magistrate - FIR was registered for the offences punishable u/ss. 365, 387, 511, 386, 34, 120-B and 506(2) of the IPC and u/s. 25(1)(A) of the Act - After completion of police investigation and on the basis of the FIR registered, a chargesheet was submitted by the police before the Magistrate which included ss. 365, 511, 387, 386, 34, 120-B and 506(2) of IPC as also u/s. 25(1)(A) of the Act - Complainant noticed that the offences punishable u/ss. 364, 394 and 398 of IPC were not included in the chargesheet which was filed against respondent and other accused persons - In order to rectify the said error, the complainant submitted an application before the Magistrate for adding other ss. 364, 394 and 398 of the IPC - Application was allowed - On revision, Additional District & Sessions Judge, quashed order passed by the Chief Judicial Magistrate - Application was filed thereagainst before HC - HC upheld the order passed by the Additional District & Sessions Judge - Hence, instant appeal - Whether the Magistrate by virtue of the powers conferred upon him under Chap. 15 of the CrPC under the Heading of 'Complaints to Magistrate' could be permitted to allow the complainant/informant to add additional sections of the IPC into the chargesheet after the same was submitted by the police on completion of investigation of the police case based on a FIR registered u/s. 154 of CrPC - Held, if a case was registered u/s. 154 of CrPC by the police based on the FIR and the chargesheet was submitted after investigation, the correct stage as to which sections would apply on the basis of the FIR and the material collected during investigation culminating into the chargesheet, would be determined only at the time framing of charge before the appropriate Trial Court - Instant case was based on the FIR lodged before the police, the correct stage for addition or subtraction of the Sections would have to be determined at the time of framing of charge - Order of the Chief Judicial Magistrate who permitted addition of three Sections into the chargesheet after the chargesheet was submitted not approved - Order accordingly.
