Manoj Manu and another v Union of India and others

Supreme Court of India

12 August 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 498; (2013) 12 SCC 171; 2013 (139) FLR 475; JT 2013 (11) SC 374; 2013(10) SCALE 204; [2013] 10 S.C.R. 8; 2013 (5) SLR 653; 2013 (4) UPLBEC 2740

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Limited Departmental Competitive Examination (LDCE) - Denial of promotion - Legality - Appellant Assistants appeared LDCE for next promotion to post of Section Officer's Grade - Out of successful candidates, 6 of them did not join - UPSC recommended names of 3 candidates from reserve list maintained by it - Appellants, secured same marks as of recommended candidates, challenged recommendation before Tribunal - Tribunal dismissed application - Appellant challenged Tribunal order before HC relying upon O.M. dt. 14-7-1967 - HC dismissed appellants' petitions - Hence, instant appeal - Whether HC order was sustainable - Held, after UPSC sent list of 184 persons/recommended by it to Govt. for appointment, 6 persons out of the said list did not join - It is not a case where Govt. decided not to fill up further vacancies - Govt. wanted to fill up all the notified vacancies - Requisition sent in this behalf was in consonance with its cl. 4(c) of O.M. dt. 14-7-1967 - Even when Govt. wanted to fill up post, UPSC chose to forward names of three candidates - Since requisition was within permissible notified vacancies, UPSC should have sent names of 6 candidates instead of 3 - It was not the case of UPSC that under no circumstances the names were sent by way of supplementary list, after sending names of candidates equal to vacancies - As per UPSC itself, names of 'repeat/common' candidates were sent and in the instant case itself, 3 names belonging to such category were sent - However, exclusion of appellants had clearly resulted in discrimination as one of those 3 candidates had also secured same marks and once he was appointed to the post in question, appellants with same marks were left out even when vacancies were available - Hence, decision of UPSC in forwarding 3 names against requisition for 6 vacancies was inappropriate - HC order as well as Tribunal were set aside - Direction issued to UPSC to forward names of next 3 candidates for appointment to post of Section Officer's Grade - Appeal allowed.
