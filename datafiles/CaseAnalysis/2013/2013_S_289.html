Shobha Sinha v State of Bihar and others

Supreme Court of India

23 October 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, Surinder Singh Nijjar

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 695; (2013) 16 SCC 456; AIR 2014 SC 862; 2014 (4) AWC 3826; 2013(13) SCALE 220; [2013] 11 S.C.R. 427; 2013 (8) SLR 744; 2014 (1) SLR 41; 2014 (2) WLN(SC) 1

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Non-Supply Of Documents, Bihar Government Servants (Classification, Control and Appeal) Rules, 2005

Summary: Service - Dismissal from service - Legality of procedure - Challenged - Appellant was charged on the allegation that she had made a proposal for allotment of Bitumen to X for around 1600 Metric Tonnes without disclosing the factum of misappropriation of 500 Metric Tonnes of Bitumen earlier allotted to the X and that an investigation was pending against the conduct of the X - On account of the said acts of the delinquent, the State Government had suffered a heavy loss - On basis of this report Government Resolution was passed whereby the appellant was dismissed from service - Appellant filed writ petition challenging her dismissal on the ground of legality of the procedure adopted in the departmental enquiry as well as the punishment imposed as a consequence thereto - - Said writ petition was allowed by the Single Judge on the ground that enquiry conducted was not proper inasmuch as the State Govt. had not supplied her the documents and also not examined the witnesses - Order of the Single Judge had been upset by the DB - Hence, instant appeal - Whether DB had rightly dismissed the order of the Single Judge - Held, Review Committee accepted the plea of the appellant that she had put up the proposal in a routine manner and that the main responsibility was that of Executive Engineer - Hence, it was not a case of lack of devotion to duty or any financial irregularities on the part of the appellant - State Govt. had not given any satisfactory explanation whatsoever as to why there was no consideration of the said report and whether there were any valid or cogent reason to ignore the same - Impugned order of DB was set aside - State Govt. was directed to pass penalty order on the basis of Review Committee report and also the observations of the Single Judge that it was the first case in her entire service career where the appellant had faced the departmental proceedings - Appeal allowed.
