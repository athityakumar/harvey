Manohar Lal Sharma v Medical Council of India and others

Supreme Court of India

12 September 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan, A.K. Sikri

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 588; (2013) 10 SCC 60; JT 2013 (12) SC 477; 2013(11) SCALE 294; [2013] 9 S.C.R. 325; 2015 (1) SLR 785

Case Digest

Subject: Education

Keywords: Indian Medical Council (Amendment) Act, 2010, Establishment of Medical College Regulations (Amendment) Act, 2010, Minimum Standard Requirements for the Medical College for 150 Admissions Annually Regulations, 1999

Summary: Education - Indian Medical Council Act, 1956, ss. 19A, 20 - Admission - Grant of renewal of permission - Deficiencies - Order of rejection - College was established during year 2011-12 and it admitted 150 M.B.B.S. students for said year - Renewal of permission for second batch was sought for academic year 2012-13 and Medical Council of India (MCI) carried out inspection and noticed various deficiencies and, in addition, one fake faculty was presented before inspection team - Board of Governors of MCI, after considering assessment report, nature of deficiencies and explanation submitted by College finally decided not to grant renewal of permission for admitting fresh batch - College filed petition before HC Single Judge who passed interim order directing MCI to conduct another inspection to assess deficiencies pointed out earlier - MCI filed appeal before DB which was disposed of nullifying decision of Board of Governors of MCI - MCI made Special Leave Petition before Board of Governors, so as to give effect to Court's order, recalled their earlier letter issued to College and, in obedience to directions of Court, issued Letter of Permission (LoP) granting permission for admission of batch of 150 MBBS students for academic year 2013-14 - Hence, instant Petition - Whether MCI was justified in passing order rejecting request for renewal of permission for 3rd batch of MBBS students for year 2013-14 - Held, MCI was body constituted under provisions of the Act and had power to supervise qualifications or eligibility standards for admission into medical institutions - MCI on basis of reports, regular and compliance, was legally obliged to form opinion with regard to capacity of college to provide necessary facilities in respect of staff, equipments, accommodation, training and other facilities to ensure proper functioning of medical college or for increase of admission capacity - It was legislative mandate that when new medical college was established or existing medical college seeks to increase admission capacity it would be in position to offer minimum standards of medical education as prescribed by MCI u/ss. 19A, 20 of the Act - By pointing out deficiencies, MCI was given opportunity to College to rectify deficiencies, if any noticed by inspection team and it was duty of College to submit compliance report - Thus, deficiencies pointed out were fundamental and very crucial, which could not be ignored in interest of medical education and in interest of student community - Therefore, infirmities pointed out by inspection team were serious deficiencies and Board of Governors of MCI rightly not granted approval for renewal of permission for 3rd batch of 150 MBBS students for academic year 2013-14 - Hence, MCI had rightly passed order rejecting approval for renewal of permission - Petition allowed.
