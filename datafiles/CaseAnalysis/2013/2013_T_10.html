Markio Tado v Takam Sorang

Supreme Court of India

10 May 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Gokhale, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 306; (2013) 7 SCC 524; AIR 2013 SC 3202; 2013(4) ALL MR 447; 2013 (5) AWC 4960; 2013 (2) CLT(SC) 269; JT 2013 (7) SC 383; 2013 (3) RCR(Civil) 274; 2013(7) SCALE 205; [2013] 6 S.C.R. 475

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Keywords: Corrupt Practices, Assembly elections

Summary: Representation of the People Act, 1951-Sections 100 (1) (d) (iii), 123 (8) and 135A-Election petition-Maintainability-Election to Arunachal Pradesh Legislative Assembly-Election petition filed on ground of booth capturing-Evidence on record clearly showed that apart from some allegations, there was no material evidence placed in support thereof-Petitioner tried to claim impersonation and double voting as facet of booth capturing-Impersonation and double voting would amount to deception and it will be facet of improper reception of votes and not booth capturing-Booth capturing involves use of force and that was not established-Election petition not filed on ground of improper reception of votes-Even if ground of improper reception of votes looked into, there is no direct evidence regarding casting of votes by impersonation-Election petition stands dismissed-Impugned judgment and order of High Court-Set aside.
