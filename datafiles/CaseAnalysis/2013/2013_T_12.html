Tamil Nadu Rural Development Engineers v Secretary to Government Rural Development Department and others

Supreme Court of India

27 September 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Surinder Singh Nijjar, M.Y. Eqbal

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 649; (2013) 15 SCC 380; AIR 2014 SC 159; JT 2014 (1) SC 245; 2013 (7) MLJ 263; 2013(12) SCALE 246; [2013] 9 S.C.R. 840; 2014 (1) SLR 788

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Previous Service

Summary: Service - Benefit of past service - Promotion - Members of the Tamil Nadu Rural Development Engineers' Association (appellants) were initially appointed as 'Overseers' by the Highways and Rural Works Dept. - By virtue of G.O. Rural Development Dept. (RD Dept.), the Govt. of Tamil Nadu decided to set up a separate 'Engineering Wing' for the RD Dept. so as to exercise adequate control over various Central and State sponsored Schemes and accordingly several new posts such as Assistant Engineers (AE). Assistant Executive Engineers (AEE), Executive Engineers (EE) and Superintending Engineers (SE), were created - By virtue of another G.O., the Govt. directed that the then Highways and Rural Works Dept. should cease forthwith from exercising control over the promotions and appointments in the RD Dept. - Govt. Order also recognised the rights of the Overseers, whose entire service was only in the RD Dept., for promotion to the posts of AEs and Junior Engineers (JEs). - Govt. framed Service Rules for various technical posts in the RD Dept. and notified the same in G.O. - Constitutional Validity of G.Os. were challenged before the Tribunal - Both the Govt. Orders were upheld by the Tribunal - Thereafter, appellants made representations to the respondent to fix a ratio of 1:1 among 'Assistant Engineers (AE)-direct recruits and AE-Promotees, for promotion to the post of AEE - Govt. rejected the request of the appellants to fix a ratio of 1:1, on the ground that the promotions of both the categories had to be made on the basis of the date of joining as Assistant Engineer, irrespective of the source - Writ petitions were filed thereagainst - Appellants claimed the benefit of the previous service on the lower post of Overseer for determining the seniority on the higher post of Assistant Engineer - Appellants also filed writ petitions seeking issuance of a writ of Mandamus directing the respondents to give retrospective effect to the promotions given to Overseers as Assistant Engineers from 25-5-1998, i.e., the date from which the Service Rules for 'AE-Promotees' as notified in G.O., came into effect - By the impugned judgment, the DB of the HC held that service of the appellants in the RD Dept. before absorption and immediately after the absorption was in a lower post, i.e., Overseer and therefore, they could not be equated with the direct recruits who joined the RD Dept. as Assistant Engineers - Post of Overseer was a feeder post for promotion on the post of Assistant Engineer - Hence, instant appeals - Whether appellants could claim that the services rendered by them in the Highways Dept. as Overseers for 20 years be taken into account for promotion in the RD Dept. - Held appellants could not now claim that the past service in the Highways Dept. should be recognised in the RD Dept. - Appellants having voluntarily opted to be absorbed in the RD Dept., without any protection of their previous service, could not now be permitted to make a grievance that they have not been treated at par with the Direct Recruits - Further, Rules provided a ratio on appointment to the post of Assistant Executive Engineer (RD) as 6:2:1 (promotion from AE (RD); JE; SDO) - Also, fixation of the quota/ratio was the prerogative of the executive - Claim of the appellants that the promotion on the post of Assistant Executive Engineer ought to be made in the ratio of 1:1 was also wholly devoid of any merit - In the absence of the appellants placing on the record material to establish that fixation of such a ratio was patently arbitrary, the action of the Govt. could not be nullified - Appeals dismissed.
