Rajeshwar Singh v Subrata Roy Sahara and others

Supreme Court of India

09 December 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 810; (2014) 14 SCC 257; AIR 2014 SC 476; 2014 (1) ALD(SC) 128; 2014(1) ALL MR 910; 2014 (1) AWC 197; 2014 (1) CLT(SC) 1; 2014 (1) RCR(Criminal) 445; 2013(14) SCALE 707; [2013] 11 S.C.R. 1051

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Investigation monitored by Court, Interference by accused

Summary: (1) Constitution of India-Articles 129 and 142-Contempt of Courts Act, 1971-Sections 12 and 15-Rules to Regulate Proceeding for Contempt of the Supreme Court, 1975-Rule 12-Contempt petition-Maintainability-Petitioner invoked Articles 129 and 142 of the Constitution to apprise Supreme Court of difficulties he faces while carrying on court monitored investigation by C.B.I. in respect to 2G Spectrum Scam case-Petitioner invoked jurisdiction and power conferred on Supreme Court under constitutional provisions-Whether consent of Attorney General of India is pre-requisite to initiate contempt of court proceedings?-Held, "no"-No sanction from Attorney General of India necessary when contempt proceedings initiated in exercise of powers conferred under Article 129 read with Article 142 of the Constitution-Jurisdiction of Supreme Court under Article 129 of the Constitution is independent of Contempt of Courts Act-Powers conferred under Article 129 of the Constitution-Cannot be denuded, restricted or limited by Contempt of Courts Act-Petitioner only expected to bring to notice of Supreme Court problems he confronts with while carrying on court monitored investigation-Supreme Court got constitutional obligation to examine truth of allegations as to whether respondents are attempting to derail investigation which is monitored by Supreme Court-Contempt petition filed under constitutional provisions without consent of Attorney General of India or other officer mentioned in Section 15 of Contempt of Courts Act-Perfectly maintainable-Notice issued to respondents to show cause why proceedings be not initiated against them for interfering with court monitored criminal investigation.

(2) Constitution of India-Articles 129 and 142-Contempt-Initiation of contempt proceedings in exercise of powers conferred under Article 129 read with Article 142-Power conferred on Supreme Court under Article 129 is constitutional power-Which cannot be circumscribed or delineated either by Contempt of Courts Act, 1971 or Rules or even Rules to Regulate Proceedings for Contempt of Supreme Court, 1975-Jurisdiction of Supreme Court under Article 129 of the Constitution-Is independent of the Contempt of Courts Act, 1971.
