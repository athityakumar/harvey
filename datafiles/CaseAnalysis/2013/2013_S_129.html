Subhash Chand v State (Delhi Administration).

Supreme Court of India

08 January 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjana Prakash Desai, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 322; (2013) 2 SCC 17; JT 2013 (1) SC 457; 2013 (1) RCR(Criminal) 1013; [2013] 1 S.C.R. 191; 2013 (1) WLN(SC) 59

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure (Amendment) Bill, 1994

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1908, ss. 378 (1) and (4) - Criminal Procedure (Amendment) Bill, 1994 - Prevention of Food Adulteration Act, 1954, ss. 16 (1) (1A), 7, 24 - Prevention of Food Adulteration Rules, 1955, rr. 32, 42(zzz) (i) and 47 - Complaint case - Appeal from order of acquittal - Complaint was filed against accused/vendor and appellant/supplier cum manufacturer alleging that he was not maintaining prescribed standards of food article namely Sweetened Carbonated Water - It also alleged that appellant had violated provisions of the Act and Rules and committed offence punishable u/s. 16 (1) (1A) r/w s. 7 of the Act and Rules - Appellant was acquitted by Magistrate - Aggrieved by said order, respondent/State filed appeal u/s. 378 (1) of CrPC to Sessions Court - Appellant raised preliminary objection on maintainability of appeal before Sessions Court and contended that appeal arising from order of acquittal in complaint case should lie to HC - Sessions Court rejected said objection - Appellant filed petition before HC against order of Sessions Court - HC held that Sessions Court had no jurisdiction to entertain appeal filed in complaint case and directed that appeal be transferred to HC - HC dismissed appeal filed by appellant and held that appeal filed by respondent/State against order of acquittal shall lie to Sessions Court u/s. 378(1) of CrPC and not u/s. 378(4) of CrPC to HC - Hence instant appeal - Whether in complaint case, appeal from order of acquittal of Magistrate would lie to Sessions Court u/s. 378(1) (a) of CrPC or to HC u/s. 378(4) of CrPC - Held, State Govt. could not present appeal against order of acquittal passed by Magistrate in respect of cognizable and non bailable offence - Complainant could file application for special leave to appeal against order of acquittal of any kind only to HC - Complainant could not file such appeal in Sessions Court - Complainant filed complaint alleging offences punishable u/s. 16 (1) (1A) r/w s. 7 of the Act and Rules, appellant was acquitted by Magistrate - Order of acquittal could challenge by filing application for special leave to appeal in HC and not in Sessions Court - Therefore, impugned order holding that instant case was not governed by s. 378(4) of CrPC was quashed and set aside - Appeal allowed.
