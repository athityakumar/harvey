Manjit Singh and another v State of Punjab and another

Supreme Court of India

13 September 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, Vikramajit Sen

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 598; (2013) 12 SCC 746; JT 2013 (12) SC 492; 2013 (4) MLJ(Crl) 200; 2013(11) SCALE 394; [2013] 11 S.C.R. 107

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 307, 148, 34 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellants-Accused (A1-A5) allegedly shot at complainant and his deceased brother - Accused were charged for offences punishable u/ss. 302, 307 r/w s. 34, 148 IPC - Trail Court convicted and sentences A1 to A3 for charges u/ss. 302, 307 r/w s. 34 IPC and acquitted them u/s. 148 IPC and A4 and A5 was acquitted of all charges - Appeal before HC , HC affirmed conviction of A1 and A2 and A3 was given benefit of doubt - Hence instant appeals - Whether order recorded buy Trial Court was sustainable - Held, on evidence A2 had accompanied A1 and was present at spot that he had carried weapon that it was established by prosecution that cartridges had been fired from his gun and that both accused were closely related - Thus, cumulative facts would clearly establish that A2 shared common intention with A1 - HC acquitted A3 on foundation that there was animosity between police officers and A3 had not carried any weapon - Thus, acquittal of A3 to A5 by Trial Court and HC was based on cogent reasoning and it was plausible view - Appeals dismissed.
