Saraswathy v Babu

Supreme Court of India

25 November 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya, V. Gopala Gowda

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 755; (2014) 3 SCC 712; AIR 2014 SC 857; 2013 ALL MR (Cri) 4486; 2014 CRLJ 1000; 2014 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 1970; 2013 (4) MLJ(Crl) 746; 2014 (1) OLR 432; 2014 (175) PLR 759; 2014 (1) RCR(Criminal) 167; 2014 (3) RLW 1901; 2013(14) SCALE 370; [2013] 12 S.C.R. 914

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Family & Personal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: domestic violence

Summary: Criminal - Family & Personal - Practice & Procedure - Protection of Women from Domestic Violence Act, 2005, ss. 18, 19, 20, 22 - Compensation - Domestic violence - Applicability - Appellant (wife) and respondent (husband) were married in year 2000- appellant filed a petition against respondent seeking relief u/ss. 18, 19, 20 and 22 of the Act - Trial Court partly allowed petition and directed respondent to allow appellant into shared household - Aggrieved respondent filed an appeal before Lower Appellate Court - Lower Appellate Court by its order set aside the order prohibiting respondent from committing acts of domestic violence as against the appellant by not allowing her to live in the shared household and order directing the respondent to reside in the house owned by respondent's mother and upheld the order granting maintenance of in favour of appellant by respondent - Aggrieved appellant filed a revision petition before HC - HC by impugned judgment and order held that although offending acts of respondent could be construed as offences under other enactments it could not be construed as acts of domestic violence under the Act until the Act came into force - Hence, instant appeal - Whether impugned judgment and order passed by HC was justified - Held, fact that even after order passed by Trial Court the respondent has not allowed the appellant to reside in shared household matrimonial house, there was a continuance of domestic violence committed by respondent against appellant - In view of such continued domestic violence, it was not necessary for courts below to decide whether domestic violence was committed prior to the coming into force of the Act and whether such act falls within definition of term 'Domestic Violence' as defined u/s. 3 of the Act - Act of respondent squarely comes within ambit of s. 3 of the Act - HC erred in holding that conduct of parties prior to the coming into force the Act could not be taken into consideration - Instant case was a case where respondent has not complied with order and direction passed by Trial Court and Appellate Court - Respondent mislead the Court by giving wrong statement before HC in contempt petition filed by appellant - Appellant having being harassed since 2000 was entitled for protection orders and residence orders u/ss. 18 and 19 of the Act along with maintenance as allowed by Trial Court u/s. 20(d) of the Act - Apart from those reliefs, appellant was entitled for compensation and damages for injuries, including mental torture and emotional distress, caused by the acts of domestic violence committed by respondent - Impugned order of HC was set aside - Appeal allowed.
