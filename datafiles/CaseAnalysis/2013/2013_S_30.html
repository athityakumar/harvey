Union of India v Sandur Manganese and Iron Ores Limited and others

Supreme Court of India

23 April 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, H.L. Dattu

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 260; (2013) 8 SCC 337; 2013 (4) AWC 3403; 2013 (3) CLT(SC) 45; JT 2013 (8) SC 275; 2013(6) SCALE 257; [2013] 2 S.C.R. 1045

Case Digest

Subject: Mines & Minerals

Summary: Mines & Minerals - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 47 rr. 1, 1(1) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 137 - Mines and Minerals (Development and Regulation) Act, 1957, ss. 11, 11(2), 11(4) - Mining lease - Grant - Illegality - Jurisdiction - Review petitioner filed petition seeking review of judgment and order wherein SC, had interpreted various provisions of the Act and had held that decision of State Govt. in not recommending mining lease to respondent and instead preferring two other Companies for grant of mining lease did not suffer from any irregularity, illegality, discrimination, arbitrariness, unreasonableness or violative of principles of natural justice - It was grievance of petitioner that review was instituted since Ministry of Mines, GOI, could not put forth its view on interpretation of provisions for reason that copy of SLP was not served upon review petitioner which was necessary and relevant party to subject-matter in issue/dispute and petitioner did not get an opportunity of being heard - Hence, instant review petition - (A) Whether impugned judgment had incorrectly reported 'Report of the Committee to Review Existing Laws and Procedures for Regulation and Development of Minerals' and whether it is erroneous in the eyes of law - Held, it was true that Expert Committee's Report had been misquoted to extent of adding four lines, which was originally not a part of report, thus, Court had power to modify impugned judgment to extent of deletion of misquoted statement under review jurisdiction - However, impugned judgment was not erroneous on face of law merely because Expert Committee Report was misquoted but, mere deletion of these lines along with removal of certain portion of impugned judgment would clarify mistake - (B) Whether ss. 11(2), 11(4) of the Act should be applicable to both virgin and previously held areas - Held, error contemplated in impugned judgment was not one which was apparent on face of record rather dispute was wholly founded on point of interpretation and applicability of ss. 11(2), 11(4) of the Act - In review jurisdiction, mere disagreement with view of judgment could not be ground for invoking same as long as point was already dealt with and answered, parties were not entitled to challenge impugned judgment in guise that an alternative view was possible under review jurisdiction - Hence, in review jurisdiction, Court should interfere only when there was a glaring omission or patent mistake or when a grave error had crept in impugned judgment, which SC, failed to notice in instant case - Review dismissed.
