Abhay Singh v State of Uttar Pradesh and another

Supreme Court of India

10 December 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, C. Nagappan

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 813; (2013) 15 SCC 435; AIR 2014 SC 427; 2014 (2) ALD(SC) 50; 2014 (103) ALR 248; 2014 (1) AWC 1; 2014 (4) CTC 53; 2014 (1) RCR(Civil) 714; 2013(15) SCALE 26

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Entitlement, Use of red lights and multi-toned horns by public servant

Summary: Motor Vehicles Act, 1988-Sections 109, 110 and 111-Motor Vehicles Rules, 1989-Rules 108, 108A and 119-Menace of unauthorised use of red lights and multi-toned horns on motor vehicles-Neither Central Government nor State Governments took any step for checking menace of unauthorised use of red lights and multi-toned horns on motor vehicles-Unauthorised use of red lights and multi-toned horns on motor vehicles deserves to be dealt with sternly and exemplary fine should be imposed on violators-State Governments and Administration of Union Territories to either amend existing rules or frame appropriate rules for imposing deterrent penalty on violators of rules containing prohibition against use of red lights and multi-toned horns or similar devices.
