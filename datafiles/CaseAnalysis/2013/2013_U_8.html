Udai Shankar Awasthi v State of Uttar Pradesh and another

Supreme Court of India

09 January 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, J. S. KHEHAR

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 13; (2013) 2 SCC 435; JT 2013 (1) SC 539; 2013 (1) MLJ(Crl) 462; 2013 (2) RCR(Criminal) 503; 2013(1) SCALE 212; [2013] 3 S.C.R. 935

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Continuous

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 403 and 406 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 482 - Criminal proceedings - Continuing offence - Quashing - Challenged - Respondent no. 2 (complainant) filed a complaint against appellant (accused) for alleged offences u/ss. 403 and 406 of IPC - Trial Court took cognizance and issued summons to appellant - Aggrieved appellant filed an application u/s. 482 of CrPC before HC for quashing of criminal proceedings, which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Appellant contended compliant case filed by brother of respondent no. 2 in regard to same subject matter were dismissed by Magistrate and that fresh complaint could not entertained during pendency of complaint case filed by respondent no. 2 and that there was suppression of fact that arbitration proceedings with respect to very same subject matter was sub-judice and that complaint made after a lapse of 15 years was barred by of s. 468 of CrPC -Whether impugned criminal proceedings were liable to be quashed - Held, law of limitation prescribed under CrPC, should be observed, but in certain exceptional circumstances, taking into consideration the gravity of the charge, the Court may condone delay, recording reasons for same, in the event that it was found necessary to condone such delay in the interest of justice - S. 472 of CrPC provided that in case of a continuing offence, a fresh period of limitation begins to run at every moment of the time period during which offence continues - Expression, 'continuing offence' was not defined in CrPC because it was one of those expressions which did not have a fixed connotation, and thus, formula of universal application could not be formulated in that respect - In case of a continuing offence, ingredients of offence continue, i.e., endure even after the period of consummation, whereas in an instantaneous offence, offence takes place once and for all i.e. when same actually takes place - In such cases, there was no continuing offence, even though damage resulting from injury might itself continue - It was evident that in complaint, no reference was made by complainant as regards the Arbitration proceedings - There was no disclosure of facts to show that earlier complaints in respect of same subject matter, was dismissed on merits by HC - In Award made by Arbitrator, issue no.13 which dealt with instant controversy, i.e. some material and documents were placed in premises of IFFCO and return of same was refused, which claim was rejected - There was no mention of such claim and its rejection by said concern, in either of writ petitions earlier or even for that matter, in application filed by said concern before IFFCO, for the purpose of making appointment of an arbitrator, or in application filed u/s. 11 of 1996 Act before HC - It was beyond imagination as to how offence involved could possibly be termed as a continuing offence - In fact, damage caused by virtue of non-payment of their dues, if any, was legally sustainable, might continue, but offence was most certainly not a continuing offence, as the same was not recurred subsequent to order dt.15.10.2001, even though effect caused by it might be continuous in nature - Impugned proceedings should be labeled as nothing more than an abuse of process of Court, in view of fact that, with respect to enact the same subject matter, various complaint cases had already filed by respondent no. 2 and his brother, which were all dismissed on merits - Thus, Magistrate concerned committed error by entertaining case and wrongly took cognizance against appellant - Impugned judgment was set aside - Kishan Singh (D) through L.Rs. v. Gurpal Singh and others, 2010 Indlaw SC 624, referred to - Appeals allowed.
