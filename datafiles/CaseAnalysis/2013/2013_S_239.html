Subodh Nath And Anr. v State Of Tripura

Supreme Court of India

19 March 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Patnaik, H.L. Gokhale

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 330; (2013) 4 SCC 122; AIR 2013 SC 3726; 2013 ALL MR (Cri) 1889; 2013 CRLJ 2308; 2013 (2) JCC 987; JT 2013 (9) SC 334; [2013] 4 S.C.R. 581

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Claim of juvenility

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 r/w s. 34 - Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act, 2000, ss. 2(k), 7A, 20 - Juvenile Justice Act, 1986 - Murder - Claim of juvenility - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellants (accused) allegedly killed the deceased and committed offences u/s. 302 r/w s. 34 of IPC - Trial Court convicted appellants and sentenced them to under RI for life - Aggrieved appellants filed an appeal before HC, which was dismissed by impugned judgment - Hence, instant appeal - Appellants contended that when offence was alleged to have been committed, appellant no. 2 was less than 18 years and juvenile u/s. 2(k) of 2000 Act, however Trial Court and HC held that provisions of 2000 Act would not apply to offence which was committed and instead provisions of 1986 Act - Further contended that conviction of appellant no. 1 was based on sole testimony of PW13, who was working as a daily labourer under deceased, was ran away from the place of occurrence and was a suspect - Whether impugned conviction of appellants was justified and appellant no. 2 was juvenile - Held, appellant no. 2 was below 18 years of age as per his birth certificate, impugned judgment of HC qua appellant no. 2 would have to be set aside and case would have to be remitted to concerned Juvenile Justice Board, for disposal of his case in accordance with provisions of 2000 Act - PW3 had left for 'C' because of his fear of the appellants who had threatened him with dire consequences if he disclosed the incident to anyone - At any rate, evidence of PW13 was supported by evidence of PW6 who has stated that on the date of incident he had found the deceased and appellants grazing cows, and evidence of IO(PW19) r/w inquest report (Ext.P2) prepared by him shows that there were injuries on dead body of deceased caused by an axe and a gun - IO also stated that he recovered handle of axe near the dead body of deceased and seized them after preparing a seizure list in presence of witnesses - Thus, evidence of PW13 was corroborated by material particulars by reliable testimony, direct and circumstantial - Once eye witness account of PW13 was corroborated by material particulars and was reliable, it could not discard only on the ground that there were some discrepancies in evidence of PW1, 2, 13 and IO - Appellant no. 1 was guilty of offence u/s. 302 of IPC and his conviction was justified - Appeal of appellant no. 2 was allowed setting aside impugned judgment of HC and Trial Court and matter was remitted to Juvenile Justice Board for disposal - Appeal disposed of.
