Mookkiah and another v State, Represented by the Inspector of Police, Tamil Nadu

Supreme Court of India

04 January 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, Ranjan Gogoi

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 7; (2013) 2 SCC 89; AIR 2013 SC 321; JT 2013 (1) SC 626; 2013 (1) MLJ(Crl) 361; 2013(1) SCALE 95; [2013] 2 S.C.R. 881

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302 and 34 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 378 - Murder - Common intention - Conviction - Sustainability - Accused persons attacked with deceased sharp weapons (bill hooks) with a common intention to kill him - Charge sheet against appellants was filed u/s. 302 r/w s. 34 IPC - Trial Court acquitted appellants - Aggrieved on appeal HC reversed the judgment of acquittal and sentenced appellants u/s. 302 r/w s. 34 IPC - Hence instant appeal - Whether HC was justified in convicting appellants for the offence charged against them - Held, SC noticed the motive as spoken to by PW1 both in his evidence as well as in complaint - It was pointed out that no blood stains were noticed in the M.Os I, II and III, namely, aruvals (bill hooks) and dress in the FSL report - It was explained that since these objects were lying on the earth and by efflux of time, no blood was found by the laboratory because of which the same could not be doubted when the same were duly recovered in the presence of witnesses - Trial Court failed to take note of relevant aspects and committed a grave error in rejecting the reliable materials placed by the prosecution - HC, analyzed the evidence as provided in s. 378 of the Code and rightly reversed the order of acquittal and found accused persons guilty of offence u/s. 302 r/w s. 34 IPC for murdering deceased in pursuance of their common intention and awarded sentence of life imprisonment - Appeal dismissed.
