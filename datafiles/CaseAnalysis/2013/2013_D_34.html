Standard Chartered Bank v Dharminder Bhohi and others

Supreme Court of India

13 September 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 630; (2013) 15 SCC 341; 2013 (101) ALR 465; 2013 (4) BC 407; 2013 (7) MLJ 171; 2013 (121) RD 732; 2013(12) SCALE 124; [2013] 9 S.C.R. 410

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Banking & Finance - Practice & Procedure - Recovery of Debts Due to Banks and Financial Institutions Act, 1993 - Securitisation and Reconstruction of Financial Assets and Enforcement of Security Interest Act, 2002 - Debt recovery proceedings - Delay in disposal - Appellant bank advanced home loan to respondent No.1 - Respondent No.1 defaulted repayment - Appellant brought hypothecated property for auction sale - Respondent No. 1 challenged sale before Debt Recovery Tribunal (DRT) - In the meantime, appellant effected sale to respondent No. 3 auction purchaser - DRT asked respondent No. 1 to deposit entire amount - Respondent No. 1 appealed to Debt Recovery Appellate Authority (DRAT) - DRAT passed order granting liberty to respondent No. 3 to file action against appellant for its omission - Appellant challenged DRAT order before HC - HC affirmed DRAT order - Hence, instant appeal - Whether DRAT was justified in granting liberty to respondent No.3 to take action against appellant - Held, intendment of 1993 Act was for speedy recovery of dues to bank - Tribunals were expected to act in quite promptitude regard being had to nature of the lis and see to it that an ingenious litigant did not take recourse to dilatory tactics - An action taken by bank under 2002 Act was subject to assail before DRT and a further appeal to DRAT - Neither the DRT nor the DRAT should afford to sit over matters as that would fundamentally frustrate the purpose of legislation - Presiding Officer of tribunal should be kept in mind that grant of an adjournment should be an exception and not to be granted in a routine and mechanical matter - Its duty was to see that disputes were disposed of quickly regard being had to larger public interest - Role of the tribunal should not be fettered by technicalities - Taking note of a submission of auction purchaser and then proceed to say that he was at liberty to file any action against bank for any omission committed by it had no sanction of law - Said observation was wholly bereft of jurisdiction, and was totally unwarranted - Hence, observation granting liberty to respondent No. 3 to file auction against appellant for any of its omission, was deleted - Impugned HC order was set aside - Appeal partly allowed.
