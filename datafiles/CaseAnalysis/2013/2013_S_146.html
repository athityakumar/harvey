Vipin Jaiswal(A-I) v State Of A.P. Rep.By Pub.Prosecutor

Supreme Court of India

13 March 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Patnaik, Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 148; (2013) 3 SCC 684; AIR 2013 SC 1567; 2013 CRLJ 2095; 2013 (3) G.L.R. 2510; JT 2013 (4) SC 188; 2013 (2) OLR 130; 2013 (2) RCR(Criminal) 342; 2013(3) SCALE 525; [2013] 3 S.C.R. 449

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 304B and 498A - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 45 and 113B - Dowry death - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant accused allegedly committed offences u/ss. 302, 304B and 498A IPC - Trial Court convicted appellant u/ss. 302 and 498A IPC - Aggrieved, appellant preferred appeal before HC, which was also dismissed - Whether HC was justified in convicting appellant - Held, from evidence of prosecution witnesses, and in particular PW1 and PW4, it was find that they made general allegations of harassment by appellant towards deceased and had not brought in evidence any specific acts of cruelty or harassment by appellant on deceased - It appeared from chit (D19) that deceased had written chit according to her free will saying that nobody was responsible for her death and that her parents and family members had harassed her husband and she was taking step as she was fed up with her life and because of her quarrels were taking place - Trial Court and HC would sought for an expert's opinion u/s. 45 of the Act on whether handwriting and signature were that of deceased - But, neither Trial Court nor HC resorted to these provisions of the Act and instead by their own imaginary reasoning disbelieved defence of appellant that D19 would not have written by deceased - Evidence of appellant and Ext. D19 cast a reasonable doubt on prosecution story that deceased was subjected to harassment or cruelty in connection with demand of dowry - Similarly, for Court to draw presumption u/s. 113B of the Act that appellant had caused dowry death as defined in s. 304B, IPC - Since prosecution had not proved beyond reasonable doubt of harassment or cruelty, neither of offences u/ss. 498A and 304B, IPC were made out by prosecution - Impugned orders passed by Trial Court and HC was set aside - Appellant was acquitted from offences - Appeal allowed.
