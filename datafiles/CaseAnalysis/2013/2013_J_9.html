State of Himachal Pradesh v Jai Chand

Supreme Court of India

03 July 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 423; (2013) 10 SCC 298; AIR 2013 SC 3349; 2013 CRLJ 4001; 2013 (3) JCC 2191; JT 2013 (9) SC 409; 2013 (3) MLJ(Crl) 318; 2014 (1) RLW 505; 2013(8) SCALE 591; [2013] 10 S.C.R. 646

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 304-B, 498-A - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 313 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 27 - Murder - Acquittal - Sustainability - Respondent (accused no. 1) along with 2 others were tried for offence punishable u/s. 302 r/w s. 34 and 498-A of IPC - Trial Court found respondent to be guilty u/s. 302 and 498-A of IPC and sentenced him to undergo imprisonment for life and other 2 accused were acquitted - Aggrieved respondent filed an appeal before HC - DB by impugned judgment set aside Trial Court and acquitted respondent - Aggrieved Appellant (State) filed instant appeal - Appellant contended that statement of respondent (husband of deceased) u/s. 313 of CrPC was self-explanatory that he had a hatred attitude towards his wife due to her illicit relation with his nephew and which resulted in motive and intention to kill her during night - Whether impugned judgment passed by DB acquitted respondent was justified - Held, HC was clearly in error, in formulating its own opinion based on conjectural premises and deciding the case on basis of that 'Doctor (PW10), might have acquired some experience as Medical Officer but was not a forensic expert to give the level of an expert witness examined in Court' discarding the opinion of medical experts regarding nature of injury and cause of death - That conclusions were not sustainable - Recovery of an incriminating article from a place which was open and accessible to others, alone could not vitiate such recovery u/s. 27 of the Act - Thus, in instant case, where there was no difficulty in holding that the bucket (Ex.P8) was the same which was used by respondent for drowning and strangulating his wife (deceased) - Opinion of PW10 was clear and definite that ligature mark in horizontal position could not be caused by hanging but could have caused by strangulation - Medical evidence, thus, completely falsify the case of respondent - Conduct of respondent was not natural - When he found his wife hanging, he neither made hue and cry nor called the villagers nearby - He along with others brought down the body of deceased even thereafter, did not report to police - Findings by DB rejecting the evidence of PW10 and other material witnesses PW3 and 5 were clearly unsustainable, whereas those given by Trial Court accepting the evidence of those witnesses were weighty and sound - Impugned judgment of acquittal passed by DB was set aside - Judgment of Trial Court was restored and respondent was convicted u/s. 302 of IPC and was sentenced to imprisonment for life - Appeal allowed.
