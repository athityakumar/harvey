Dulcina Fernandes and others v Joaquim Xavier Cruz and another

Supreme Court of India

08 October 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjan Gogoi, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 676; (2013) 10 SCC 646; 2013 (4) ACC 293; 2013 ACJ 2712; AIR 2014 SC 58; 2013 (6) ALD(SC) 152; 2013 (6) AWC 5485; 2014 (1) Bom.C.R. 213; 2013 (4) CGLJ 477; 2014 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 529; 2014 (2) MahLJ 510; 2014 (2) MPLJ 679; 2013 (4) RCR(Civil) 751; 2014 (2) RLW 968; 2013(12) SCALE 611; [2013] 10 S.C.R. 480; 2014 (1) WLN(SC) 1

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance

Summary: (1) Motor Vehicles Act, 1988-Sections 166 and 168-Compensation-Death case-Death of sole bread winner-Deceased at relevant time driving scooter with pillion rider-Pick-up van hit scooter driven by deceased as result of which deceased and pillion rider fell off and suffered injuries-On account of injuries sustained deceased died-C.W. 2 who at relevant time working as head constable specifically denied that scooter driven by deceased had dashed pick-up van which was stationary, i.e., parked on road-Accident occurred in year 1997-Evidence before Tribunal recorded after seven years, i.e., in 2004-While it is correct that pillion rider could have best unfolded details of accident-Taking into account sufficiently long period that has elapsed, Tribunal should not have treated non-examination of pillion rider as fatal to claim for compensation-Tribunal quantified entitlement of claimants to compensation at R 6,66,041.78-But withheld in view of its finding that accident occurred on account of negligence of deceased-Rejection of claim for compensation by Tribunal affirmed by High Court-High Court failed to notice lacunae in award of Tribunal-Accident occurred due to rash and negligent driving of pick-up van by first respondent-Impugned finding of Tribunal as affirmed by High Court-Set aside-Impugned finding of Tribunal-Reversed-Appellants-claimants entitled to compensation of R 6,66,041.78 as quantified by Tribunal-Amount awarded as compensation to carry interest @ 6% p.a.-Appeal of claimants-Allowed.

(2) Motor Vehicles Act, 1988-Section 166-Rash and negligent driving-Plea of rash and negligent driving-Required to be decided by Tribunal on touchstone of preponderance of probability and certainly not on basis of proof beyond reasonable doubt.
