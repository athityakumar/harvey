N. Anantha Reddy v Anshu Kathuria and others

Supreme Court of India

02 December 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, Shiva Kirti Singh

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 801; (2013) 15 SCC 534; 2014 (5) ALD(SC) 141; 2014 (1) AWC 775; 2014 (1) OLR 364; 2014 (1) OLR 642; 2014 (123) RD 344; 2013(14) SCALE 585; [2013] 12 S.C.R. 555; 2014 (1) WLN(SC) 86

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Summary: (1) Code of Civil Procedure, 1908-Order I, Rule 10 (2)-Suit-For declaration and perpetual injunction against Municipal Corporation and Assistant City Planner-Impleadment of third party to suit-Trial court and High Court in revisional jurisdiction allowed impleadment application of appellant-Respondent No. 1 made applications for review of impugned order of High Court passed under revisional jurisdiction-High Court by impugned order under review jurisdiction recalled its earlier order and directed trial court to consider impleadment application afresh-Course followed by High Court-Clearly flawed-High Court exceeded its review jurisdiction by reconsidering merits of its earlier order-High Court not at all justified to review its earlier order-Impugned order of High Court passed under review jurisdiction-Set aside.

(2) Review-Review jurisdiction -Extremely limited-Unless there is mistake apparent on face of record-Order/judgment does not call for review.

(3) Review-Review jurisdiction -Not appeal in disguise-Review does not permit rehearing of matter on merits.

(4) Review-Mistake apparent on record-Means that mistake is self evident-Needs no search and stares at its face.
