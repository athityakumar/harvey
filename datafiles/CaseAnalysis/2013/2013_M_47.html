Maharshi Mahesh Yogi Vedic Vishwavidyalaya v State Of M.P. & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

03 July 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 364; (2013) 15 SCC 677; AIR 2013 SC (Supp) 744; 2013(4) ALL MR 872; JT 2013 (10) SC 223; 2013(8) SCALE 541

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Education

Keywords: Maharshi Mahesh Yogi Vedic Vishwavidyalaya Adhiniyam, 1995

Summary: (A) Constitution - Education - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Maharshi Mahesh Yogi Vedic Vishwavidyalaya Adhiniyam, 1995 - Maharshi Mahesh Yogi Vedic Vishwavidyalaya Adhiniyam (Amendment) Act, 2000 - S. 4 (1) - Vires - Appellant was University, which was a creation by way of 1995 Act - There was amendment introduced to ss. 2, 4, 9 and 17, as well as insertion of ss. 31-A, 31-B, 31- C, 37-A, 37-B to the Act - Aggrieved by that writ petition was filed before DB - Writ petition was partly allowed by DB - Hence instant appeal - Whether proviso s. 4 (1) of the Act was ultra vires - Held, entire proviso to s. 4(1) had to be held to be ultra-vires - It was also necessary to note, as well as mention that after University was established for its initial establishment and for running institution, according to appellant, more than Rs.12 crores were spent by way of an investment and that nearly Rs.60 crores were spent for running University and its various centers throughout State Govt. - Recurring expenditure was stated to be Rs.11 crores - Therefore, when appellant University had proceeded to establish its institution for the purpose of imparting education by making huge investments, a major part of which would have definitely come by way of fees collected from students who had joined institution aspiring for improving their educational career, hence, it was the responsibility of State to ensure that such high expectation of students who joined appellant university was not impaired and that for whatever expenses incurred by students, appropriate returns should be provided to them by way of imparting education in respective fields which, they choose to associate themselves by getting themselves admitted in appellant University - Therefore, on this ground as well, it would have to be held that such expectations of students, as well as their parents could not be dealt with so very lightly by State, while considering for any change to be brought about in Constitution and functioning of appellant University - It could therefore be validly held that such expectations of students and their parents, as well as that of appellant University, could validly be held to be a legitimate expectation and considering challenge made to amendment introduced on various grounds raised at instance of appellant, legitimate expectation of appellant University, as well as student community, would also equally support contentions of appellant University, while challenging amendments in particular amendment introduced to s. 4(1), as well as addition of a proviso to said Section - Appeal partly allowed.

(B) Constitution - Education - Maharshi Mahesh Yogi Vedic Vishwavidyalaya Adhiniyam, 1995 - Maharshi Mahesh Yogi Vedic Vishwavidyalaya Adhiniyam (Amendment) Act, 2000 - Educational institution - Whether appellant University was deprived of its valuable right to hold very many programmes in conduct of course enumerated in its Ordinance 15, which consequently resulted in violation of its Constitutional, as well as Fundamental Rights in running of its educational institutions - Held, establishment of appellant University at repeated persuasion of Maharshi Mahesh Yogi was definitely to provide full-fledged education on Vedas and various intricate subjects, which were found in Vedas, as well as its practices, Ithihas, Puranas etc - In fact, there could be no 2 opinion that such an institution with such a laudable objective for imparting education in different fields based on teachings in Vedas, was very rare and it was said that appellant University was stated to be unique University created and established by founders of said institution - Therefore, when such a premium University, which was stated to be only one of its kind in whole of Country was successfully established based on 1995 Act, such well-established institution should be allowed to survive by enabling said University to conduct courses as was planned by it and introduced under Ordinance 15 and thereby, make appellant University a viable one - Such approach alone, ensure successful existence and continued running of University in further years and thereby, benefit very many aspirants from among younger generation who wish to learn more and more about very many subjects by understanding such subjects based on teachings that were found and established in Vedic learnings, its practices, Ithihas and Puranas etc. - Therefore, any attempt made from any quarters, which would disrupt running of appellant University, would only amount to interfering with its various Constitutional rights and fundamental rights enshrined in Constitution - Therefore, when such interference was brought to notice of HC, Court had to necessarily come to rescue of appellant University by saving it from any such onslaught being made on its continued existence - Appeal partly allowed.

(C) Constitution - Education - Maharshi Mahesh Yogi Vedic Vishwavidyalaya Adhiniyam, 1995 - Maharshi Mahesh Yogi Vedic Vishwavidyalaya Adhiniyam (Amendment) Act, 2000 - Appointment of Chancellor - Power of management after 2000 Act - Whether Management had power to appoint Chancellor after amendment to s. 9(2) of the Act - Held, under un-amended provision, after first Chancellor, Board of Management was empowered to appoint Chancellor from among persons of eminence and renowned scholar of Vedic education who could hold office for a term of 5 years and who would be eligible for reappointment - Under amended s. 9(2), it was stipulated that after first Chancellor, Board of Management should prepare and submit a panel of 3 persons to State Govt. and out of panel, one person should be appointed as Chancellor by Board of Management, after obtaining approval of State Govt. - As far as period of holding office was concerned, there was no change in its terms - DB while considering said amendment introduced under 2000 Act, held that even after amendment, Management had power of recommendation and they could recommend a person of eminence and renowned scholar of Vedic education and even if ultimate appointment had to be made with approval of State Govt., since any such appointment could be only from panel prepared by Board of management, such a stipulation contained in amendment did not in any way impinge upon any right, much less Constitutional Right or Fundamental Right of appellant University - Further, there was no wrong in conclusion reached by DB, and SC also hold that said provision did not in any way offend art. 14 of Constitution, nor it affect autonomy of appellant University - SC hold that amended s. 4(1) under 2000 Act inclusive of introduction of proviso to said Section was ultra-vires of Constitution and the same was liable to be set aside - In other respects, judgment of DB stood confirmed - Appeal partly allowed.
