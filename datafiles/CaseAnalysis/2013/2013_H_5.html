Hamza and another v Muhammadkutty @ Mani and others

Supreme Court of India

20 June 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Patnaik, Gyan Sudha Misra

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 892; (2013) 11 SCC 150; 2014 ALL MR (Cri) 1108; [2013] 10 S.C.R. 867

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Dress, Acquittal, Evidence of child witness

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code,1860, ss. 302, 201 r/w s. 34 - Acquittal - Evidence of child witness - Inconsistency - After two years of incident, brother of deceased lodged complaint before Magistrate that six accused persons committed offences punishable u/ss. 302, 201 r/w s. 34 of IPC - Magistrate took cognizance of case and committed case to Sessions Court - Trial Court held that there was nothing to suggest that A3 to 6 shared common intention of A1 and 2 to murder deceased, and thus acquitted A3 to 6 of offences - Aggrieved, A1 and 2 filed an appeal before HC, which was allowed setting aside their conviction and sentence - Hence, instant appeal - Whether HC should not have disbelieved evidence of PW1 merely because he was a child witness.

Held, from the evidence of PW1, it appears that PW1 did not tell anything about the incident to the police on the date of the incident, though the police had come to the house where the incident had taken place. Maternal aunt and maternal grandmother of PW1 has not corroborate the testimony of PW1. From the evidence of PW2, it is very clear that PW2 had developed animosity towards A 1 and 2 on account of what the deceased had told him. Further, from the deposition of PW3 and Ex. P11, it appears that there were two windows in the room in which the incident took place, one window opening towards the portico and the other window towards the road. None among the people from the road or from amongst the neighbours around the house have been examined on behalf of the prosecution to corroborate the evidence of PW1. However, in the absence of any corroboration of the oral testimony of PW1, the HC was right in taking the view that it is unsafe to convict A1 and 2 only on the evidence of PW1, who was a child witness and whose evidence did not inspire any confidence. The evidence of the medical experts cast a serious doubt on the reliability of the evidence of PW1. The opinions of the two medical experts do not lend assurance to the prosecution story that the death of the deceased was only homicidal. Therefore, on the evidence of PW1 read with the opinions of PW4 and DW1, the HC could not have held that the prosecution has been able to prove beyond reasonable doubt that A1 killed the deceased with the help of A2. The view taken by the HC that A1 and 2 were entitled to acquittal is not perverse or unreasonable on the evidence on record so as to call for interference. Appeals dismissed.

Ratio - On inconsistent evidence of child witness and others, it is extremely unsafe to convict the accused persons for the offence u/s. 302 r/w s. 34 of IPC.
