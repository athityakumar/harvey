KCP Limited v Commissioner of Central Excise, Chennai

Supreme Court of India

03 September 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Dattu

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 556; (2014) 1 SCC 597; 2013 (295) E.L.T. 353; JT 2013 (12) SC 101; 2013(11) SCALE 70

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - Indirect Tax - Central Excise Rules, 1944, r. 57Q - MODVAT credit - Entitled to - Appellant assessee manufactured sugar plant machineries in their factory and purchased some parts from local market - Appellant sent machineries to foreign country for setting up a sugar plant there - Appellant availed MODVAT credit u/r. 57Q of the Rules - Tribunal, on appeal of respondent Revenue, remanded matter back to original authority for computing amount of MODVAT credit irregularly availed by appellant and also for imposition of penalty - Hence, instant appeal - Whether appellant was entitled to avail MODVAT credit - Held, most important object concerning grant of MODVAT credit was to see that cascading effect of duty imposed on final product cleared at the time of sale was removed - If some duty was levied on inputs, raw materials etc. and if final product was also dutiable, then duty levied on inputs i.e. raw materials was to be reduced from duty ascertained on final product - In the instant case, no duty was paid by appellant on final product i.e. on sugar plant set up in foreign country - Hence, there would not be any question with regard to getting credit on duty paid on inputs, especially when appellant had not used machinery manufactured by other manufacturers in its factory premises while manufacturing machinery which had been transported along with machinery manufactured by appellant in a common container which had been sent to foreign country - Appellant purchased some machinery from others and such machinery had not even been unpacked by it and the same was transported along with machinery manufactured by it to foreign country - Thus, appellant did not use purchased machinery in its premises or in its factory and therefore, necessary condition incorporated in the Rules for availing credit of MODVAT had not been complied with - Appellant had only acted as a trader or as an exporter in relation to machinery purchased by it, which had been exported and used for setting up a sugar plant in a foreign country - Tribunal had come to a correct conclusion and conclusion so arrived at by Tribunal did not require any interference - Appeal dismissed.
