M/S. Deep Trading Company v M/S. Indian Oil Corporation And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

22 March 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, Madan B. Lokur, J. Chelameswar

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 183; (2013) 4 SCC 35; AIR 2013 SC 1479; 2013 (98) ALR 722; 2013 (3) AWC 2984; 2013 (3) CLT(SC) 97; [2013] 177 Comp Cas 475; 2013 (2) CompLJ 271; 2013 (2) CTC 888; JT 2013 (8) SC 159; 2013 (4) MahLJ 96; 2013 (3) MLJ 480; 2013 (3) MPLJ 51; 2013 (171) PLR 675; 2013 (2) RCR(Civil) 953; 2013(5) SCALE 96; [2013] 2 S.C.R. 470; 2013 (3) WLN(SC) 37

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Summary: (A)Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, s.11(6) - Appointment of arbitrator - Denied - Sustainability - Whether respondent No. 1 had forfeited its right to appoint the arbitrator having not done so after the demand was made and till the appellant had moved the court u/s. 11(6) of Act -

Held, if SC could apply the legal position exposited in one case to the admitted facts, it would be seen that the Corporation had forfeited its right to appoint the arbitrator - Datar Datar Switchgears Limited v Tata Finance Limited and Another (Referred) 2000 Indlaw SC 28, relied on - It was so for the reason that on 9-8-2004, the dealer called upon the Corporation to appoint the arbitrator in accordance with terms of Cl. 29 of the agreement but that was not done till the dealer had made application u/s. 11(6) of Act to the Chief Justice of HC for appointment of the arbitrator - Appointment was made by the Corporation only during the pendency of the proceedings u/s. 11(6) of Act - Such appointment by the Corporation after forfeiture of its right was of no consequence and did not disentitle the dealer to seek appointment of the arbitrator by the Chief Justice u/s. 11(6) of Act - Appeal allowed.

(B) Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, s.11(6) - Appointment of arbitrator - Denied - Sustainability - Whether the appointment of the arbitrator by respondent No. 1 in the course of the proceedings u/s. 11(6) of Act was of any legal consequence and the Chief Justice of the HC ought to have exercised the jurisdiction and appointed an arbitrator -

Held, Chief Justice ought to have exercised his jurisdiction u/s. 11(6) of Act in the matter for appointment of an arbitrator appropriately - Appointment of the arbitrator by the Corporation during the pendency of proceedings u/s. 11(6) of Act was of no consequence - Appeal allowed.
