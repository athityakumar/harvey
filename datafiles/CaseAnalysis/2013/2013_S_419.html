Geomin Minerals and Marketing (Private) Limited and others v State of Orissa and another

Supreme Court of India

10 May 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya, R.M. Lodha

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 304; (2013) 7 SCC 571; AIR 2013 SC 2438; 2013 (2) ILR(Cut) 517; JT 2013 (7) SC 156; 2013(7) SCALE 243; [2013] 8 S.C.R. 914

Case Digest

Subject: Mines & Minerals

Keywords: Grant of mining license, Preferential right

Summary: Mines & Minerals - Administrative - Mines and Minerals (Development and Regulation) Act, 1957, ss. 5(1), 11(3) and 11(5) - Grant of mining license - Preferential right - Legality - Appellant No. 3 made an application for prospecting licence for an area of 6828.54 hectares - Initially a recommendation was made to Central Govt. in favour of appellant No. 3 for an area of 6204.352 hectares by State Govt. - Revisional Authority set aside the recommendation of State Govt. as all mineral concession applications were not considered simultaneously and no orders were passed on those applications - Aggrieved, appellant No. 1 filed writ petition - State Govt. made impugned recommendation to Central Govt. in favour of appellant No. 1 u/s. 11(3) and (5) of the Act - HC held that appellant No. 1 had preferential right for grant of licence and lease and that recommendation made by State Govt. u/s. 11(5) in favour of appellant No. 3 was invalid - Hence instant appeals - Whether HC was justified in passing the impugned order - Held, it was not the stage to decide as to whether in instant case the pre-amended or amended s. 11(2) of the Act should be applicable and thereby priority should be assigned under pre-amended or amended s. 11(2) of the Act as matter had already considered by State Govt. and recommendation was required to be considered by Central Govt. u/s. 5(1) of the Act - It was well settled that no applicant had statutory or fundamental right to obtain prospecting licence or a mining lease - Therefore, HC before interfering with recommendation, ought to had looked into nature of recommendation - HC committed a grave error of law in deciding case on merits and deciding question of legality of recommendation made by State Govt. - In fact State Govt. should had left matter to Central Govt. to pass an appropriate order in accordance with law instead of entertaining a pre-mature writ petition - State Govt. by its recommendation had forwarded the tabulated chart showing inter se merit of each applicant, it was not for HC to sit in appeal to decide who amongst all was more meritorious and was entitled for preferential right - Impugned order passed by HC was set aside and remitted the matter to Central Govt. to consider the question of approval u/s. 5(1) of the Act taking into consideration recommendations made by State Govt. - Appeals allowed.
