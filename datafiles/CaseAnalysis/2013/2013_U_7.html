State of Rajasthan v Ucchab Lal Chhanwal

Supreme Court of India

22 October 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 700; (2014) 1 SCC 144; 2013(13) SCALE 272; [2013] 12 S.C.R. 20; 2014 (1) SLR 395; 2014 (1) WLN(SC) 5

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Seniority list

Summary: Service - Rajasthan Police Service Rules, 1954 - Seniority list - Promotion - Challenged - Respondent was appointed in Rajasthan Police Service - Seniority list was published wherein name of respondent was at serial number 51 in junior scale - On basis of recommendations of Departmental Promotion Committee/DPC persons junior to respondent were promoted - Aggrieved respondent filed writ petition before Single Judge for setting aside order whereby he had been superseded and his juniors were promoted - Single Judge allowed said petition and quashed the order - Appellant/state appealed to DB - DB affirmed order passed by Single Judge - Hence instant appeal - Whether impugned order passed by Single Judge and affirmed by DB was justified - Held, dispute was related to promotion which would have impact on inter se seniority - Respondents assiduously endeavored to convince that they were agitating grievance with regard to their promotion and it had nothing to do with persons junior to them who had been promoted - SC was not persuaded to accept aforesaid proponent, for once respondents were promoted, juniors who have been promoted earlier would become juniors in promotional cadre, and they being not arrayed as parties, an adverse order could not be passed against them as that would go against basic tenet of principles of natural justice - Directions issued by Single Judge as well as DB pertaining to grant of promotion to respondents were quashed - Conclusion of Single Judge relating circular was concerned, it was unexceptionable and SC concur with same - Order passed by DB as well as by HC was set aside to extent directions was issued granting benefit of promotion to respondents - Appeals partly allowed.
