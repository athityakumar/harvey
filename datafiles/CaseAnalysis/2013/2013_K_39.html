Karthi @ Karthick v State Represented by Inspector of Police, Tamil Nadu

Supreme Court of India

01 July 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

J. S. KHEHAR, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 382; (2013) 12 SCC 710; AIR 2013 SC 2645; 2013 CRLJ 3765; JT 2013 (9) SC 167; 2013 (3) MLJ(Crl) 458; 2013 (3) RCR(Criminal) 692; 2013(7) SCALE 777; [2013] 8 S.C.R. 1012

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 376, 417 - Cheating - Rape - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant (accused) allegedly committed rape of prosecutrix (PW1) - Trial Court convicted appellant u/ss. 376 and 417 of IPC, which was affirmed by Lower Appellate Court in appeal - Aggrieved appellant filed a revision before HC, which was dismissed by impugned judgment - Hence, instant appeal - Appellant contended that prosecutrix was a consenting party to sexual relationship which appellant had with her - Whether impugned conviction of appellant was justified - Held, prosecutrix confirmed in her deposition, appellant had promised to marry her, by placing his hand on her head, after having ravaged her - Subsequent acts of sexual intercourse, were actions of actively cheating her, by giving her the impression that he would marry her - Concurrent determination of Courts below, that appellant committed deceit with prosecutrix by promising to marry her - On the strength of said deception, in the first instance persuaded her not to disclose the occurrence to anyone, and thereafter, repeatedly had sexual intercourse with her - Thus, in facts and circumstances of case, it was not possible to accept contention of appellant, that sexual intercourse by appellant with prosecutrix was consensual - Obtaining consent by exercising deceit, could not be legitimate defence to exculpate an accused - As long as commitment of marriage subsisted, the relationship between the parties could not be described as constituting the offence of rape u/s. 376 of IPC - It was only after appellant declined to marry the prosecutrix, that a different dimension came to be attached to physical relationship, which had legitimately continued over the past 6 months - Things changed when appellant declined to marry prosecutrix - After the promised alliance was declined, prosecutrix without any delay disclosed the entire episode to her immediate family - It was only on refusal of apellant Karthick, to marry the prosecutrix, that the question of making a criminal complaint arose - Appeal dismissed.
