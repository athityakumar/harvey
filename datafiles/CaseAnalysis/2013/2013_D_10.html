Londhe Prakash Bhagwan v Dattatraya Eknath Mane

Supreme Court of India

10 September 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Pinaki Chandra Ghose, K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 592; (2013) 10 SCC 627; 2013(6) ALL MR 425; 2014 (1) Bom.C.R. 312; 2013 (139) FLR 301; 2014 (2) MahLJ 520; 2013(11) SCALE 383; [2013] 9 S.C.R. 775; 2014 (1) SLR 611

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Maharashtra Employees of Private Schools Rules

Summary: Service - Practice & Procedure - Maharashtra Employees of Private Schools (Conditions of Service) Regulation Act, 1977, s. 9 - Condonation of delay - Permissibility - Appellant was appointed as the Headmaster and same was subsequently approved by the Education Officer, after following due procedure - However, after a delay of 9 years and 11 months, application was filed before the School Tribunal challenging the appointment of the appellant - Application filed for condonation of delay was dismissed - School Tribunal held that respondent No.1 himself presided over the meeting of the Managing Committee and approved the appointment of the appellant as Headmaster of the said School - Writ petition was filed before HC - HC remanded the matter to the School Tribunal, holding that the provisions of limitation did not apply to appeals filed u/s. 9(1)(b) of the Act - Hence, instant appeal - Whether an application could be filed by an aggrieved party even long after 10 years, if no limitation was prescribed in the statute for filing an appeal before the appropriate forum - Held, if no time-limit had been prescribed in a statute to apply before the appropriate forum, in that case, he had to come before the court within a reasonable time - In the instant case, period of 9 years and 11 months, was inordinate delay to pursue the remedy of a person and without submitting any cogent reason therefore - Court had no power to condone the same in such case - Application should be rejected on the ground of inordinate delay - Appeal allowed.
