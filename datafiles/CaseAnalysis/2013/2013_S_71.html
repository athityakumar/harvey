Veer Pal Singh v Secretary, Ministry of Defence

Supreme Court of India

02 July 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, Sharad Arvind Bobde, Ranjana Prakash Desai

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 468; (2013) 8 SCC 83; AIR 2013 SC 2827; 2013 (5) AWC 5322; JT 2013 (10) SC 71; 2013 (6) MahLJ 876; 2013 (5) MLJ 841; 2013 (4) MPLJ 493; 2013(8) SCALE 686; [2013] 10 S.C.R. 579; 2013 (5) SLR 210; 2013 (3) UPLBEC 2103

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Armed Forces Tribunal Act, 2007

Summary: Defence & Security Forces - Practice & Procedure - Armed Forces Tribunal Act, 2007 - Discharge from service - Disability - Challenged - Appellant, Defence Personnel was admitted in Military Hospital for the treatment of 'Intestinal-Colic' - Appellant case was considered by the Invaliding Medical Board and on its recommendations, appellant was discharged from service - Appellant's claim for disability pension was rejected by Principal Controller of Defence Accounts (Pension) on the ground that the disease, i.e., Schizophrenic Reaction, which was the cause of his discharge was not attributable to the military service - Appellant challenged said order in writ petition and prayed that the respondents be directed to constitute a Review Medical Board to re-evaluate his disease - Said writ petition remained pending before the HC for 13 years - On the establishment of Lucknow Bench of the Tribunal under Act, the same was transferred to the Tribunal and was registered as Transferred Application - Tribunal dismissed said application - Review application and application filed by the appellant for grant of leave to appeal were dismissed by the Tribunal with a cryptic observation that the recommendations made by the Medical Board were binding and the same cannot be subjected to judicial review - Hence, instant appeal - Appellant submitted that mere irritability or quarrelsome nature could not lead to an inference that he was suffering from 'Schizophrenic Reaction' and the Tribunal committed grave error by declining his prayer for making a reference to the Review Medical Board - Whether order of the Tribunal could be upheld -

Held, at the time of enrolment in the Army, appellant was subjected to medical examination and Recruiting Medical Officer found that he was fit in all respects - Item 25 of the certificate issued by the Recruiting Medical Officer was quite significant - Therein it was mentioned that speech of the appellant was normal and there was no evidence of mental backwardness or emotional instability - It was, thus, evident that the doctor who examined the appellant did not find any disease or abnormality in the behaviour of the appellant - However, when the Psychiatrist examined the appellant, she noted he was quarrelsome, irritable and impulsive but he had improved with the treatment - Invaliding Medical Board simply endorsed the observation made by Psychiatrist that it was a case of 'Schizophrenic Reaction' - Tribunal did not even bother to look into the contents of the certificate issued by the Invalidating Medical Board and mechanically observed that it could not sit in appeal over the opinion of the Medical Board - Orders passed by the Tribunal were set aside and the respondents were directed to refer the case to Review Medical Board for reassessing the medical condition of the appellant and find out whether at the time of discharge from service he was suffering from a disease which made him unfit to continue in service and whether he would be entitled to disability pension - Appeal allowed.
