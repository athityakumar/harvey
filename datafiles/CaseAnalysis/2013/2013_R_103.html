Rohtash Kumar v State Of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

29 May 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 347; (2013) 14 SCC 434; AIR 2013 SC (Supp) 244; 2013 ALL MR (Cri) 2620; 2013 CRLJ 3183; JT 2013 (8) SC 181; 2013 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 1911; 2013 (3) MLJ(Crl) 31; 2013 (3) RCR(Criminal) 355; 2013(7) SCALE 472; [2013] 3 S.C.R. 884

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302 and 404 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant accused allegedly murdered the deceased - Appellant allegedly committed offence punishable u/ss. 302 and 404 IPC - Trial Court convicted the appellant u/ss. 302 and 404 IPC and sentenced him to undergo life imprisonment - Aggrieved, appellant preferred appeal before HC, which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether Courts below were justified in convicting the appellant - Held, PW8 was an independent witness and she had 'last seen together' appellant and deceased, just before her death - It was not see any reason to doubt veracity of PW8 statement - It was also on record that appellant had left alone from hostel - Appellant had not furnished any explanation with respect to what could have happened to deceased while she was with him, if he was not responsible for her death - No explanation was furnished by appellant as regards why he had stayed at Guest House, by providing a fictitious name and false address and nor was any explanation provided by him with respect to circumstances under which, mobile phone belonging to deceased, had come to be in his possession - Courts below were justified in convicting the appellant - Appeal dismissed.
