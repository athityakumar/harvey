Joydeb Patra & Ors. v State Of West Bengal

Supreme Court of India

06 March 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Patnaik, Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 136; (2014) 12 SCC 444; AIR 2013 SC 2878; 2013 CRLJ 2729; 2013 (3) JCC 1799; 2013 (2) RCR(Criminal) 338; 2013(3) SCALE 341; [2013] 4 S.C.R. 192

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 34 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Deceased was married to appellant no. 1/accused - Appellants/accused allegedly administered poison in food causing death of deceased - Trial Court convicted accused for offence punishable u/s. 302 r/w s. 34 IPC - On appeal, DB confirmed order of conviction by Trial Court - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order of conviction by Courts below were sustainable - Held, prosecution had not been able to discharge its burden of establishing beyond reasonable doubt that deceased died due to poisoning - Trial Court and HC should not have relied on evidence of PW 12 given in Court more than 2 years after deceased died to hold that poison was administered to deceased when there was nothing in evidence either in postmortem report or in report of Senior Chemical Examiner, Forensic Science Laboratory - Thus, very foundation of case of prosecution stood demolished - Hence, Trial Court and HC could not have held appellants guilty just because appellants were not able to explain under what circumstances deceased died - Vikramjit Singh Vs. State of Punjab 2006 Indlaw SC 856, relied on - Therefore, impugned orders of Courts below were set aside - Appeal allowed.
