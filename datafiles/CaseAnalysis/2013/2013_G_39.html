Gopal Singh v State Of Uttarakhand

Supreme Court of India

08 February 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 95; (2013) 7 SCC 545; AIR 2013 SC 3048; 2013 CRLJ 3872; 2013 (2) JCC 997; 2013 (4) RCR(Criminal) 458; 2013(2) SCALE 533; [2013] 4 S.C.R. 104

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 147, 148, 452, 307, 395, 324 and 380 - Assault - Injury - Gunshot - Recovery of Weapon - Conviction - Sentence - Sustainability - Appellant-accused were charged with offence u/ss. 147, 148, 452, 307, 395 of IPC, alleging that accused beat PW1 and took away money from his shop and pocket and also tried to shoot PW1 who sustained gunshot injury - Trial Court on basis of material on record convicted appellant u/ss. 307,324,380 of IPC - Aggrieved appellant preferred appeal before HC, which held that appellant was not guilty of offence u/s. 307, 380 but found that appellant had fired a gunshot and convicted him u/s. 324 of IPC and sentenced to undergo rigorous imprisonment for 3 years - Hence instant appeal - Appellant, contended that finding that appellant had fired a gunshot had not been proven beyond reasonable doubt inasmuch as Katta-pistol had not been seized, it was urged by him that regard being had to nature of injury, age of appellant at time of incident, evidence on record that there was no fracture and no injury barring a muscle injury, rigorous imprisonment of three years was excessive and it deserved to be reduced - Whether conviction and sentence of appellant was sustainable - Held, imposition of sentence, apart from illustrations which had been stated to be mitigating factors would depend upon many a other factors which would depend/vary from case to case - Legislature in respect of an offence punishable u/s. 124 of IPC had provided punishment which may extend to three years or with fine or with both - Legislative intent, was to confer discretion on judiciary in imposition of sentence in respect of such offence where it had not provided minimum sentence or made it conditional but discretion vested could not be allowed to roam in realm of fancy but was required to be embedded in rational concepts based on sound facts - Doctor had not stated injury to be grievous but on contrary, he had mentioned that there was no fracture and only a muscle injury, weapon used fits in to description as provided u/s. 324, occurrence had taken place almost 20 years back, parties are neighbours and there was nothing on record to show that appellant had any criminal antecedents, hence SC, found it appropriate that in obtaining factual score, sentence of rigorous imprisonment of one year u/s. 324 of IPC would be adequate - Appeal disposed of.
