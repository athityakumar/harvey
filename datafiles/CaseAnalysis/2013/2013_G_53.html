 Lalita Kumari v Government of Uttar Pradesh and others

Supreme Court of India

12 November 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, Balbir Singh Chauhan, Ranjana Prakash Desai, Ranjan Gogoi, Sharad Arvind Bobde

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 738; (2014) 2 SCC 1; AIR 2014 SC 187; 2013 ALL MR (Cri) 4444; 2014 CRLJ 470; 2013 (6) CTC 353; 2014 (1) JCC 1; 2013 (4) KLT 632; 2014 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 137; 2013 (4) MLJ(Crl) 579; 2013(5) M.P.H.T. 336; 2014 (1) OLR 5; 2013 (4) RCR(Criminal) 979; 2013(13) SCALE 559

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1882, Uttar Pradesh Police Regulations, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1861, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1872

Summary: Constitution - Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 154 - Registration of FIR - Interpretation of s. 154 - Whether 'a police officer was bound to register a FIR upon receiving any information relating to commission of a cognizable offence u/s. 154 of the CrPC or the police officer had the power to conduct a 'preliminary inquiry' in order to test the veracity of such information before registering the same - Held, registration of FIR was mandatory u/s. 154 of the CrPC, if the information disclosed commission of a cognizable offence and no preliminary inquiry was permissible in such a situation; if the information received did not disclose a cognizable offence but indicate the necessity for an inquiry, a preliminary inquiry to be conducted only to ascertain whether cognizable offence was disclosed or not; if the inquiry disclosed the commission of a cognizable offence, the FIR should be registered - Police officer could not avoid his duty of registering offence if cognizable offence was disclosed - Further, as to what type and in which cases preliminary inquiry was to be conducted would depend on the facts and circumstances of each case - Also, while ensuring and protecting the rights of the accused and the complainant, a preliminary inquiry should be made time bound and in any case it should not exceed 7 days; fact of such delay and the causes of it should be reflected in the General Diary entry - Order accordingly.
