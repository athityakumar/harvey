Mohinder Singh v State Of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

28 January 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 47; (2013) 3 SCC 294; AIR 2013 SC 3622; 2013 ALL MR (Cri) 1132; 2013 CRLJ 1559; JT 2013 (2) SC 182; 2013 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 689; 2013 (1) RCR(Criminal) 878; 2013(2) SCALE 24; [2013] 3 S.C.R. 90

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Murder Reference

Summary: P. Sathasivam, J.

(A) Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 376, 506 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 366, 432 - Double murder - Death sentence - Challenged - Appellant (accused) who was on parole, allegedly murdered his wife and daughter in the background of inimical relationship between them on account of criminal cases registered against him by his wife for committing rape of his minor daughter, wherein appellant was convicted - Appellant committed offence u/s. 302 of IPC - Trial Court convicted appellant and sentenced him to death - Aggrieved appellant filed an appeal before HC and respondent State filed reference u/s. 366 of CrPC for confirmation of death sentence - HC accepted murder reference confirmed the death reference imposed by Trial Court and dismissed appeal of appellant - Hence, instant appeals - Whether impugned death sentence awarded to appellant was justified and whether case falls under 'rarest of rare' categories - Held, apellant committed offence in presence of his youngest daughter, PW2 - It was proved that appellant had entered scene of occurrence to commit said offence carrying a deadly weapon 'Kulhara' (Axe) which was used in commission of both killings - Members present in house were his family members, wife and 2 daughters - HC did not find any mitigating circumstance in favour of appellant for the purpose of balancing aggravating against mitigating - In instant case, appellant had earlier committed rape on his deceased daughter and in that case, his deceased wife was a witness wherein appellant was convicted u/ss. 376 and 506 of IPC and sentenced to RI for 12 years - It was thirst for retaliation, which became motivating factor in instant case - Case of prosecution based on evidence adduced confirmed the commission of offence by appellant, however, still the case did not fall within four corners of 'rarest of rare' case - Appellant did not harm his other daughter, PW2, even though he had a good chance for same - Appellant being a poor man and unable to earn his livelihood since he was driven out of his house by his deceased wife - It was his claim that if he was allowed to live in house, he could easily meet both his ends and means, as the money which he was spending by paying rent would have been saved - It was a grievance of appellant that his deceased wife was adamant and he should live outside and should not lead a happy married life and that was reason that their relations were strained - That shows that appellant was frustrated because of attitude of his wife and children - Probability of offender's rehabilitation and reformation was not foreclosed in instant case, it could be seen from affidavit filed by sister of appellant that his family has not totally renounced as yet - Thus, there was a possibility for reformation in appellant - This case was not a case where death penalty should be imposed - Appellant, instead of being awarded death penalty, was sentenced to undergo rigorous imprisonment for life i.e., end of his life, but subject to any remission granted by Govt. satisfying conditions prescribed in s. 432 of CrPC - Appeals disposed of.

Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla, J. (Concurred)

(B)Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 376, 506 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 366, 432 - Double murder - Death sentence - Challenged - Whether impugned death sentence awarded to appellant was justified and whether case falls under 'rarest of rare' categories - Held, in instant case, offence of murder of appellant's wife and daughter was committed when appellant was on parole while undergoing RI of 12 years for conviction of offence of rape of his daughter committed in year 1999 - It was relevant to keep in mind that for holding appellant guilty of charge of murder of his wife and daughter apart from other evidence, evidence of his own 2nd minor daughter PW2 who was an eye-witness to occurrence weighed to very great extent along with evidence of his own son PW7 - Basic grievance of appellant was nothing but his desire to occupy his house which was occupied by none else than his own wife, daughters and son - Conduct of appellant in commission of earlier offence was not only bordering on immorality of the highest order but would be extremely difficult for anyone to lightly brush aside such a conduct by stating that either it was committed in a fit of anger or rage or such other similar situation - If such grotesque offence of rape was committed by anyone, other than father himself, victim would have had every opportunity to cry for solace in her father or mother - Conclusion as to 'rarest of rare' aspect with respect to a matter should entail identification of aggravating and mitigating circumstances relating both to crime and criminal - Expression 'special reasons' obviously means ('exceptional reasons') founded on exceptionally grave circumstances of particular case relating to crime as well as criminal - Appellant deserved to be sentenced to under RI for life till end of his life, subject to remission, u/s. 432 of CrPC, granted by Govt. - Bachan Singh v. State of Punjab and Others, 1980 Indlaw SC 586, Mohammed Ajmal Mohammad Amir Kasab v. UOI, 2012 Indlaw SC 258, Gopal Vinayak Godse v. State of Maharashtra & Ors, 1961 Indlaw SC 227 and Mohd. Munna v. UOI and Ors, 2005 Indlaw SC 558, referred to - Appeals disposed of.
