Rajendra Singh v State of Uttaranchal

Supreme Court of India

11 April 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Aftab Alam, Ranjana Prakash Desai

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 222; (2013) 4 SCC 713; 2013 ALL MR (Cri) 1870; 2013 CRLJ 3172; JT 2013 (7) SC 438; 2013(5) SCALE 568; [2013] 2 S.C.R. 783

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant allegedly committed offences u/s. 302 of IPC - Trial Court by its judgment acquitted appellant - Aggrieved respondent (State) filed an appeal before HC, which was allowed setting aside judgment of acquittal and appellant was found guilty of offence u/s. 302 of IPC and was sentenced to undergo RI for life - Aggrieved appellant filed instant appeal - Appellant contended that deposition of PW3 was unreliable as it contained certain statements that were either incorrect or inconceivable - Whether impugned judgment passed by HC reversing acquittal judgment of Trial Court was justified - Held, it was undeniable that both eye witnesses PW2 and PW3 (wife of deceased) fully supported prosecution case in regard to assault by appellant on deceased with a pair of scissors - PW3, deposed before Trial Court regarding genesis of occurrence i.e., quarrel between deceased and appellant that had taken place on evening before the date of occurrence over appellant's failure to return clothes given by deceased for stitching even after a number of days - Further, deposition of PW3 in regard to assault by appellant on the deceased was quite graphic - Even if it was assumed that depositions of PW2 and 3 were incorrect in regard to relationship between PW2 and deceased and in regard to PW2 living in house of deceased as a tenant at the time of occurrence, how that could be the ground to reject their deposition entirely even though it was perfectly sound in respect of main prosecution case - Testimonies of PW2 and 3 wholly reliable and there was no reason not to accept the same - HC rightly rejected view took by Trial Court as wholly untenable and rightly accepted evidences of PW2 and 3 in order to bring home the guilt of appellant - Impugned judgment passed by HC was justified - Appeal dismissed.
