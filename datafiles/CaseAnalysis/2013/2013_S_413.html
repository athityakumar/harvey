Gulabrao Baburao Deokar v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

17 December 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Gokhale, J. Chelameswar

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 853; (2013) 16 SCC 190; 2014 CRLJ 845; JT 2014 (2) SC 189; 2014 (1) RCR(Criminal) 459; 2013(15) SCALE 484

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Bail

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120-B, 406, 409, 411, 420, 465, 466, 468, 471, 109 r/w s. 34 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 13(2) r/w s. 13(1) (c) , 13(1)(d) - Criminal conspiracy -Cheating - Forgery - Bail - Entitled to - Accused was granted bail by Incharge Additional Adhoc District Judge No.1 and Additional Sessions Judge by common order of applications filed by accused nos. 31 to 50 - HC set-aside order of Session Judge - Hence instant appeals - Whether the order passé by HC was sustainable -

Held, accuse and other accused have been charged for offence which might result into punishment for imprisonment for life - It was serious charge supported by detailed charge-sheet - It was stated therein that Municipal Corporation had illegally given more than 30 contracts to Construction Company belonged to accused beneficiary in conspiracy - Prosecutor required time to interrogate accused and custodial interrogation in such situation, for at least two days, could not have been denied - Prosecutor pointed out to Court that role of the accused was no less than that of three others whose bail was rejected, Court ought to have considered these circumstances, justifying custodial interrogation, with due diligence - Attempts made by accused to pressurize witnesses and even IO was clearly placed on record through affidavit - On the ground it could be said that appellant will be pressurizing the witnesses if he is not restrained - Hence, there was no fault with order of HC cancelling the bail - Order did record cogent and overwhelming circumstances justifying cancellation of bail - Although appeal was not being entertained, accused along with 4 other accused who were denied bail, had made numerous attempts to intimidate witnesses, and even threatened IO - Some of witnesses were employees of Municipal Corporation, and accused and other 4 co-accused, though in jail, might still make every effort to influence them hereafter, and vitiate - Hence, in facts and circumstances of instant case Sc was in view that trial of case ought to be transferred outside that district - Order was passed on basis of material that was placed on record for that purpose - when trial was conducted, it would be decided on basis of evidence, which would be brought on record during course of trial - Appeal dismissed.
