Bhagwati Developers Private Limited v Peerless General Finance and Investment Company Limited and another

Supreme Court of India

15 July 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Chandramauli Kumar Prasad, V. Gopala Gowda

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 886; (2013) 9 SCC 584; 2013 (3) CompLJ 241; JT 2013 (10) SC 583; [2013] 7 S.C.R. 547

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Corporate

Summary: (A)Banking & Finance - Corporate - Securities Contracts (Regulation) Act,1956 - Nature of shares - Respondent no.2 took loan for purchasing equity shares from appellant wherein, transfer deeds were not properly filled and therefore appellant wrote to him to put his signature which was not accepted by respondent no.3 - Appellant filed suit in Court of Civil Judge wherein, respondent no.3 and appellant settled dispute out of Court and executed agreement and filed application before Trial Court who decreed suit - Appellant filed application before Company Law Board who dismissed same - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether provisions of the Act will apply to shares of public limited company which are admittedly listed on stock exchange.

Held, expression 'marketable' has been equated with word saleable. In other words, whatever is capable of being bought and sold in a market is marketable. The size of market is of no consequence. In other words, number of persons willing to purchase such shares would not be decisive. One cannot lose sight of the fact that there may not be any purchaser even for the listed shares. Further, what is required is free transferability. Subject to certain limited statutory restrictions, shareholders possess right to transfer their shares, when and to whom they desire. It is this right which satisfies requirement of free transferability. Therefore, shares of public limited company though not listed in stock exchange come within the definition of securities and hence, the provisions of the Act apply. Appeal dismissed.

(B) Banking & Finance - Corporate - Spot delivery contract - Determination of - Whether contract is spot delivery contract.

Held, according to definition, contract providing for actual delivery of securities and payment of price either on same day as date of contract or on next day means a spot delivery contract. It is found that contract is not a spot delivery contract. Further, letter written by respondent no.3 to appellant stated that formal agreement had been executed between them and as per agreement he is transferring entire 3530 shares purchased from loan amount and transfer is in its repayment. However, agreement between the two which formed part of compromise decree provides that sale of shares in consideration paid sum of Rs.10 lakhs and further dividend on entire shares was retained. In the face of it, plea of appellant that payment of Rs.10 lakh was made to buy peace, is not fit to be accepted and, in fact, that forms part of consideration for sale of shares. Therefore, plea of appellant that it is a spot delivery contract is fit to be rejected. Appeal dismissed.
