Bharat Bhushan v State of Himachal Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

26 April 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 271; (2013) 11 SCC 274; AIR 2013 SC 2018; 2013 ALL MR (Cri) 2199; 2013 CRLJ 2932; JT 2013 (7) SC 628; 2013 (2) MLJ(Crl) 773; 2013 (3) RCR(Criminal) 80; 2013(6) SCALE 456; [2013] 5 S.C.R. 1013

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 376 - Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act, 2000, s. 20 - Rape - Reversal of acquittal - Claim of protection under Juvenile Act - Sustainability - Appellants/accused allegedly raped upon girl/victim aged 11years - Trial Court acquitted appellants for offence punishable u/s. 376 of IPC - On appeal, HC partly allowed appeal and set aside acquittal of appellant and convicted for offence punishable u/s. 376 of IPC and confirmed order of acquittal passed in favour of 'D' - Hence instant appeals - (A) Whether HC could have at all recorded conviction against appellant who was a juvenile on date of commission of offence - Held, appellant was not a juvenile under 1986 Act as he had crossed the age of 16 years - This case was, however, pending before HC in appeal on the date the 2000 Act came into force and had, therefore, to be dealt with u/s. 20 of the Act which required HC to record a finding about guilt of accused but stop short of passing an order of sentence against him - Inasmuch as HC convicted appellant, it did not commit any mistake for the power to do so was clearly available to HC u/s. 20 of 2000 Act - What was not permissible was passing of a sentence for which purpose HC was required to forward juvenile to Juvenile Board constituted under the Act - Therefore, order of sentence was unsustainable - (B) Whether conviction recorded by HC was justified on merits and, if it was, whether SC ought to refer appellant to Juvenile Justice Board at this stage - Held, SC opined that HC had properly appreciated evidence on record especially deposition of prosecutrix, her companion PW2 and her aunt PW3 as also her parents - HC had also correctly appreciated medical evidence available on record especially deposition and report of PW8 and HC had rightly taken into consideration while finding appellant guilty - Therefore, there was no reason to interfere with order of conviction as recorded by HC on merits - Further, SC was of the view that reference to Juvenile Board was unnecessary at this distant point of time - Appellant was nearly 36 years old by now and a father of 3children and he had already undergone nearly 3years of imprisonment awarded to him by HC - In the circumstances, reference to Juvenile Justice Board at this stage of his life would serve no purpose - Appeals disposed of.
