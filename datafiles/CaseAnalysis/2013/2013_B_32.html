Balbir Singh Bedi v State Of Punjab & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

11 February 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, V. Gopala Gowda

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 78; (2013) 11 SCC 746; 2013 (136) FLR 1050; JT 2013 (3) SC 66; 2013 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 532; 2013 (3) MLJ 95; 2013(2) SCALE 381; [2013] 3 S.C.R. 376; 2013 (3) SLR 4; 2013 (3) WLN(SC) 113

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces

Keywords: Commander, Punjab Home Guard, Class-I Rules, 1988, 1988 Rules'

Summary: Defence & Security Forces - Practice & Procedure - Punjab Home Guard, Class-I Rules, 1988, r. 8 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. ss. 467, 468, 471 and 120-B - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 7 and 13(ii) - Promotion - Seniority-cum-merit list - Denied - Challenged - Appellant was District Commander in respondent (State force) - Appellant claimed to be eligible for substantive promotion to post of Battalion Commander, but respondent no. 5 (junior to appellant) was considered for promotion - Appellant filed representations in that regard, which were not considered - Aggrieved appellant filed a petition before HC, which was disposed of and directed concerned authorities to consider promotion ignoring executive instructions dt.29-12-2000 - However, appellant retired and filed a petition to quash said executive instruction - HC dismissed petition by impugned order - Hence, instant appeal - Respondent contended that State, even in absence of any executive instructions, could fix required benchmark - Whether appellant was entitled to promotion as claimed - Held, where a promotion was be given on principle of 'seniority-cum-merit', such promotion would not automatically be granted on the basis of seniority alone - Efficiency of administration could not be compromised with at any cost - Thus, in order to meet said requirements, all eligible candidates in the feeder cadre should be subject to a process of assessment to determine whether or not an individual in fact possesses the specified minimum necessary merit, and in the event that he did possess the same, his case should be considered giving due weight age to his seniority - Statutory authority should adopt a bonafide and reasonable method to determine the minimum necessary merit, as was required to be possessed by eligible candidate - Even in absence of executive instructions, State/employer has right to adopt any reasonable and bonafide criteria to assess merit, for the purpose of promotion on principle of 'seniority-cum-merit' - Executive instructions were nothing but codification of directions issued by HC - Thus, a challenge made to executive instructions on the ground that they were issued at a date subsequent to the date on which vacancy arose, was meaningless - In instant case, appellant was unable to achieve the benchmark set, as it was evident from record that his ACRs were average, and benchmark fixed by State was 'Good' - Appellant faced criminal prosecution a FIR was lodged against him u/ss. 7 and 13(ii) of 1988 Act and ss. 467, 468, 471 and 120-B of IPC, wherein appellant faced trial and acquitted as was evident from judgment and his acquittal took place after 5 years to his retirement - Appellant did not approach the Court with clean hands, clean mind and clean objective - Appeal dismissed.
