Nikhil Himthani v State of Uttarakhand and others

Supreme Court of India

06 August 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Patnaik, Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 750; (2013) 10 SCC 237; AIR 2013 SC (Supp) 39; JT 2013 (11) SC 300; 2013(10) SCALE 174

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Education

Summary: Education - Administrative - Constitution of India, 1950, arts.32 and 14 - Admission - Denied - Maintainability - In the writ petition u/art. 32 of the Constitution, the petitioner had made a grievance that his fundamental right to equality in the matter of admission in post-graduate medical course in the State quota in the medical colleges in the State of Uttarakhand guaranteed by art. 14 of the Constitution was violated by respondent nos. 1 and 2 -

Held, on a reading of cl. 5 of the Eligibility Criteria, SC found that eligible candidates who get elected through NEET PG-2013/NEET (MDS) -2013 would be given admission on available seats in post-graduate courses by Counselling Board according to their rank in State Merit List - Hence, u/cl. 5 of the Eligibility Criteria merit as determined through NEET was the basis for admission to the post-graduate medical courses - But when SC looked at cl. 1 of the Eligibility Criteria, SC found that the candidates who had passed MBBS examination from different colleges in the State of Uttarakhand were eligible for admission to the medical post-graduate courses in the State quota only if they were admitted through the Uttarakhand State PMT - Petitioner, who had studied the MBBS Course in the Govt. Medical College in Haldwani, was not admitted to the aforesaid course through the Uttarakhand State PMT examination, but it was admitted in the additional affidavit filed on behalf of the respondent nos. 1 and 2 on 22-7-2013 that the petitioner had qualified in the pre-medical examination in the year 2007 for admission in the All India Quota - It so happened that the Medical College at Haldwani was then not a Government College in 2007 and therefore in the Medical College at Haldwani, there was no All India Quota at that time - Fact however remained that the petitioner had the merit to be admitted in MBBS Course in 2007 when he was actually admitted in the Medical College at Haldwani - SC was thus of the considered opinion that to exclude the petitioner from consideration on the basis of his merit only on the ground that he was not admitted to the MBBS course through the Uttarakhand PMT would be to deny him equality of opportunity in matter of admission to the post-graduate medical course and to violate his right to equality u/art. 14 of the Constitution as explained by SC - Dr. Pradeep Jain and Others vs. Union of India and Others 1984 Indlaw SC 72, relied on - Petition allowed.
