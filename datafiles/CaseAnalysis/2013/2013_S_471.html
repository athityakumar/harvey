Mariappan v State of Tamil Nadu

Supreme Court of India

18 April 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, M.Y. Eqbal

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 258; (2013) 12 SCC 270; 2013 CRLJ 2334; 2013 (3) JCC 1579; JT 2013 (8) SC 522; 2013 (2) RCR(Criminal) 839; 2013(6) SCALE 185; [2013] 4 S.C.R. 273

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 84 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 105 - Murder - Plea of insanity - Conviction - Sustainability - There was a dispute between family of appellant/accused and deceased over a portion of land belonging to one 'C' - Appellant allegedly cut her neck with Aruval and gave repeated Aruval blows on her head as a result of which she died instantaneously - Appellant claimed plea of insanity u/s. 84 of IPC - Trial Court convicted appellant for offences punishable u/ss. 449 and 302 of IPC - On appeal, HC confirmed order of conviction passed by Trial Court and dismissed appeal - Hence instant appeal - Appellant contended that at the time of alleged incident, accused was suffering from 'Paranoid Schizophrenia' and, hence, he was entitled to the benefit of exception u/s. 84 of IPC - Whether at the time of incident, accused was suffering from unsoundness of mind - Held, though Doctor/DW1, who treated accused from 11-7-2001 to 8-8-2001 had stated that appellant-accused was suffering from paranoid schizophrenia, it was not in dispute that after 8-8-2001, there was no material or information on record that he was suffering from the same - Date of occurrence was 5-11-2001 i.e. nearly after 3months of the treatment by DW1 - As a matter of fact, DW2, father of accused/appellant had not stated anything about behaviour of deceased - DW2 had also not stated anything that he was a mentally ill person and not able to do his routine works properly - There was no evidence as to unsoundness of mind of appellant-accused at the time of occurrence and also taking note of the fact that accused failed to discharge burden as stated in s. 105 of the Act, hence order of Trial Court and HC affirmed - Appeal dismissed.
