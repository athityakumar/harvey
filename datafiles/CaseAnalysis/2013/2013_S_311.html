Khachar Dipu @ Dilipbhai Nakubhai v State of Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

04 April 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 204; (2013) 4 SCC 322; AIR 2013 SC (Supp) 466; 2013 CRLJ 3177; 2014 (1) G.L.R. 1; JT 2013 (6) SC 613; 2013 (3) RCR(Criminal) 188; 2013(5) SCALE 337; [2013] 5 S.C.R. 341

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 304 Part I - Murder - Culpable homicide - Conviction - Sustainability - Instant appeals were filed questioning, legal propriety of the judgment of conviction and order of sentence passed by HC - DB allowed the appeal of the State and converted conviction u/s. 304 Part-I of IPC recorded by Trial Court to that of an offence punishable u/s. 302 IPC - (A) Whether appellant was entitled to a judgment of complete acquittal or the conviction and sentence as recorded by Trial Court was absolutely justified in the obtaining factual matrix which did not warrant interference by the HC while entertaining the appeal by the State by converting the conviction u/s. 304 Part-I of IPC to s. 302 of IPC - Held, it appeared that there were injuries on the vital parts of body, the face was crushed and further there were marks of dragging which were found on the upper part of body and on the back, and the private part was crushed - Testimonies of the said two witnesses, it was seen that both of them categorically deposed that the motor vehicle involved in accident had dashed against the cycle of deceased as a result of which he had fallen down - In cross-examination by accused, they did not pave the path of variance in that regard - In SC's opinion, their evidence support the prosecution version that the motor vehicle had dashed against the cycle - Singular purpose of referring to the testimonies of witnesses was that the incident did occur and appellant had dashed the vehicle against the cycle - Trial Court had convicted accused u/s. 304 Part I of IPC as there was no previous deliberation or pre-meditation on the part of accused and there was no evidence that the dead body was dragged upto 10 kms. - HC, as was noticeable, accepted the prosecution version of murder, regard being had to the effective crushing of the body intentionally and dragging of the same to cause death - (B) Whether the HC was justified in saying that there was intention - Held, dashing of the motor vehicle and dragging was with the intention to inflict such bodily injury that was sufficient to cause death in the ordinary course of nature - To put it differently, the HC brought the case u/s. 300 'thirdly' - (C) Whether accused has done an act by doing which he has caused the death of another - Held, proof of such causal connection between the act of accused and the death, leads to the second stage for considering whether that act of accused amounts to 'culpable homicide' as defined in s. 299 - If the answer to the question was prima facie found in the affirmative, stage for considering operation of s. 300 IPC was reached - It was the stage at which the Court should determine whether the facts proved by prosecution bring the case within the ambit of any of four clauses of the definition of 'murder' contained in s. 300 IPC - If the answer to the question was in the negative, offence would be 'culpable homicide not amounting to murder', punishable under the first or the second part of s. 304 IPC depending, respectively, on whether the second or the third cls. of s. 299 IPC was applicable - If the question was found in the positive, but the case comes within any of the exceptions enumerated in s. 300 IPC, the offence would still be 'culpable homicide not amounting to murder', punishable under the first part of s. 304, IPC - (D) Whether the opinion expressed by the HC was correct and justified - Held, HC took note of the injuries and the conduct of accused persons and opined that it was a brutal murder - Accused had not taken the plea that there was an accident because of bad light or due to the negligence of deceased - Accused took the plea of complete denial - There was evidence that the body was dragged - Thus, it could safely be concluded that the intention to cause bodily injury was actually found to have been proved and such injuries were sufficient in the ordinary course of nature to cause death - When such injuries were inflicted, it would be travesty of justice to hold that it was an accident without the intention to cause death - SC did not find any flaw in the analysis made by the HC for reversing the conviction u/s. 304 Part I of IPC recorded by Trial Court to that of 302 of IPC - Appeals dismissed.
