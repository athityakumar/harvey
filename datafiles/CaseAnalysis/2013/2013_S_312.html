Pinakin Mahipatray Rawal v State of Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

09 September 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Radhakrishnan, Pinaki Chandra Ghose

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 569; (2013) 10 SCC 48; AIR 2014 SC 331; 2013 CRLJ 4448; JT 2013 (12) SC 357; 2013 (3) MLJ(Crl) 700; 2013 (2) OLR 867; 2013 (4) RCR(Criminal) 271; 2013(11) SCALE 198; [2013] 10 S.C.R. 306

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Family & Personal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: (A) Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 498A, 306 - Dowry death - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant husband, in consequence of his extra-marital relationship with A2, allegedly drove the deceased wife to commit suicide - Trial Court convicted and sentenced appellant u/ss. 498A, 306 IPC, however, acquitted A2 - On appeal, HC confirmed conviction of appellant but reduced sentence - Hence, instant appeal - Whether conviction recorded against appellant was sustainable - Held, appellant had not caused any physical or mental torture on deceased - Parents of the deceased also did not make any allegation against appellant of ill-treatment of wife or of dowry demand - It could not be said that A2 had in any way contributed or abetted the deceased in committing act of suicide, or had attempted to alienate affection of appellant towards his deceased wife - Alleged extra marital relationship was not of such a nature as to drive wife to commit suicide or that appellant had ever intended or acted in such a manner which under normal circumstances, would drive wife to commit suicide - Appellant might have developed some likings towards A2, his colleague, however, appellant had discharged his marital obligations towards the deceased - Deceased might have been under serious 'emotional stress' - Prosecution had not discharged burden that appellant had instigated, conspired or intentionally aided so as to drive the wife to commit suicide or that alleged extra marital affair was of such a degree which was likely to drive wife to commit suicide - Courts below had committed serious error in holding that it was due to extra marital relationship appellant had with A2 that led the deceased to take extreme step to commit suicide, and appellant was instrumental for the said act - Hence, order of conviction and sentence imposed on appellant was set aside and he was set at liberty - Appeal allowed.

(B) Criminal - Family & Personal - Practice & Procedure - Alienation of affection - Proof of - Held, for a successful prosecution of an action for alienation of affection, loss of marital relationship, companionship, assistance, loss of consortium, etc. as such should not be sufficient, but there should be clear evidence to show active participation, initiation or encouragement on the part of a third party that he/she should have played a substantial part in inducing or causing one spouse's loss of other spouse's affection - Mere acts, association, liking as such would not become tortuous - Appeal allowed.

(C) Criminal - Family & Personal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 498A - Cruelty - Meaning of - Held, mere fact that husband had developed some intimacy with another, during subsistence of marriage and failed to discharge his marital obligations, as such would not amount to 'cruelty', but it should be of such a nature as was likely to drive spouse to commit suicide to fall within the explanation to s. 498A IPC - However, harassment ought not be in the form of physical assault and even mental harassment also would come within purview of s. 498A IPC - Appeal allowed.
