Gopal Chawala and others v State of Madhya Pradesh and others

Supreme Court of India

30 October 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan, A.K. Sikri

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 873; (2014) 13 SCC 792; 2014 (1) AWC 983; 2015 (1) SLR 37

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Education Guarantee Scheme

Summary: Employment-Equal pay for equal work-Appointment of Shiksha Karmi/Guruji under Education Guarantee Scheme (E.G.S.)-Admittedly, petitioners were not appointed pursuant to any statutory rules but they were appointed pursuant to education programme-Their appointment and terms and conditions governed by Education Guarantee Scheme and not by statutory rules-Nothing to show that petitioner undergone any process of selection as such-Claim that they should be equated with teachers appointed following statutory rules-Cannot be accepted-Petitioners also could not establish that method of appointment, nature of work, quantum of work, quality of work, nature of responsibilities are identical to those teachers who are appointed following statutory rules-Order of granting of pay scale and other benefits available to post of Adhyapak to Shiksha Karmies working at various centres under E.G.S. withdrawn-Impugned order of Chief Education Officer granting benefits to petitioners which are available to Adhyapak cadre rightly cancelled-Impugned orders of single Judge and Division Bench of High Court requires no interference.
