Tofan Singh v State of Tamil Nadu

Supreme Court of India

08 October 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 679; (2013) 16 SCC 31; 2013 CRLJ 4990; 2013 (4) JCC (Narcotics) 146; 2013 (4) RCR(Criminal) 631; 2013 (4) RLW 3628; 2013(12) SCALE 552; [2013] 9 S.C.R. 962

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Health & Drug; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Health & Drug - Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985, ss. 8(c), 21(c), 29 and 67 - Statement by investigation officer - Referred to Larger Bench - 5.250 Kgs of heroin was seized from appellant which he was carrying and attempting to export out of India - Trial court concluded that the prosecution was able to prove the charges u/s. 8(c) r/w ss. 21(c) and 29 of the Act and convicted the appellant and sentenced him to undergo 10 years rigorous imprisonment and to pay a fine of Rs. 1 lakh - HC dismissed appeal filed against said order - Hence, instant appeal - Appellant challenged the conviction on the grounds that conviction was based solely on the purported confessional statement recorded u/s. 67 of the Act which had no evidentiary value - Further contended that no such confessional statement could be recorded u/s. 67 of the Act as this provision empowers to call for information and not to record such confessional statements - Whether the statement recorded by the investigating officer u/s. 67 of the Act can be treated as confessional statement or not, even if the officer was not treated as police officer - Held, registry was directed to place the matter before the Chief Justice for the decision of this appeal by a larger Bench after considering the issues specifically referred - Order accordingly.
