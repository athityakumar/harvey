Birendra Das and another v State of Assam

Supreme Court of India

01 July 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 416; (2013) 12 SCC 236; JT 2013 (9) SC 182; 2014 (1) RCR(Criminal) 272; 2013(8) SCALE 113; [2013] 7 S.C.R. 179

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 r/w s. 34 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellants (accused) allegedly murdered deceased by hacking him at various parts of the body in a brutal manner with deadly weapons and the injuries sustained by deceased were quite serious in nature - Trial Court convicted appellants u/s. 302 r/w s. 34 of IPC and sentenced them to undergo RI for life - Aggrieved appellants filed an appeal before HC, which was dismissed by impugned judgment affirming the verdict of Trail Court - Hence, instant appeal - Whether impugned conviction of appellants was justified - Held, there was no dispute that death of deceased was homicidal in nature - PW4 (doctor), who conducted the autopsy on dead body of deceased, opined that death was due to shock and hemorrhage resulting from the injuries sustained by deceased and all the injuries were ante mortem in nature - Factum that the dead body was seized from the courtyard - It was seen in evidence of others that appellants were armed with weapons and dragged the dead body of deceased to courtyard - Appellants were not onlookers - Their intention was clearly reflectible from their presence with weapons at the place of occurrence till the commission of crime and thereafter dragging dead body to the courtyard, thus s. 34 of IPC was attracted - Acceptation of direct evidence on record on proper scrutiny and analysis of proof of existence of motive or strength of motive did not affect the prosecution case - Different motives might come into operation in the minds of different persons, for human nature has the potentiality to hide many things and that was the realistic diversity of human nature and it would be well-nigh impossible for prosecution to prove the motive behind every criminal act - Thus, when appellants armed with lethal weapons were present and witnessed the occurrence and participated in dragging the deceased to courtyard, establishment of any motive was absolutely inconsequential - Appeal dismissed.
