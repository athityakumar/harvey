Kusti Mallaiah v The State Of Andhra Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

28 May 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 341; (2013) 12 SCC 680; AIR 2013 SC (Supp) 882; 2013 CRLJ 3098; JT 2013 (8) SC 166; 2013 (3) MLJ(Crl) 223; 2013(7) SCALE 442; [2013] 3 S.C.R. 815

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 404 r/w s. 34 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 161, 174 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant (accused no. 2) and co-accused allegedly stole gold and silver ornaments of deceased and brutally assaulted with stones, which resulted into death of deceased and thereby committed offences u/ss. 302, 404 r/w s. 34 of IPC - Trial Court convicted appellants and sentenced them to undergo RI for life - Aggrieved appellant filed an appeal before HC - DB by its impugned judgment affirmed conviction and dismissed appeal - Hence, instant appeal - Appellant contended that there were material contradictions in evidence of PW4, husband of deceased and PW5, daughter of deceased and that ocular testimony of PW6, eye witness to the occurrence could not be relied - Whether impugned conviction of appellant was justified - Held, chronology of events clearly shows that police, on the basis of report recorded u/s. 174 of CrPC, conducted the inquest and after the PW4 and 5 identified the photograph, commenced the investigation - During that time PW4 and his brother was searching for deceased - Omission in statement of PW4 recorded u/s. 161 of CrPC was not a significant omission so that it could be regarded as a contradiction so significant and glaring that prosecution case should be disbelieved - True it was, there were certain minor discrepancies in evidence of PW4 an 5 regarding the timing, the factum of meeting of accused no. 1 and the deceased in the market by the daughter, the quarrel between husband and wife (deceased) but they were absolutely minor - They even could not earn the status of minor contradictions - PW4 nor PW 5 had made any endeavor to make any attempt to materially improve their earlier statement in their deposition before Court to make their evidence acceptable - There were no such material contradictions which discredit the testimony of witnesses - Testimony of PW6 gets corroboration from medical evidence and from the factum of recovery - Nothing was suggested to him that he had any animosity with accused persons - Thus, cumulative nature and character of evidence of PW6 was difficult to ignore solely on the ground that he did not tell the incident to any one and only revealed after the police examined him - It was common knowledge that people react to situations in different manner - As was evincible, he had accompanied the accused persons along with deceased - PW6 categorically stated that he was able to see the assault by accused persons and removing gold and silver ornaments - Thus, there was no impediment to place reliance on his evidence as it was trustworthy and unimpeachable - Impugned conviction of appellant was justified - Appeal dismissed.
