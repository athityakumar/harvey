K. Srinivas Rao v D.A. Deepa

Supreme Court of India

22 February 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjana Prakash Desai, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 110; (2013) 5 SCC 226; AIR 2013 SC 2176; 2013 (3) ALD(SC) 11; 2013 (97) ALR 732; 2013 (3) AWC 2462; 2013 (3) Bom.C.R. 129; JT 2013 (3) SC 97; 2013 (5) MahLJ 10; 2013 (3) MLJ 85; 2013(4) M.P.H.T. 1; 2013 (3) MPLJ 567; 2013 (171) PLR 149; 2013 (2) RCR(Civil) 232; 2013(2) SCALE 735; [2013] 2 S.C.R. 126; 2014 (4) WLN(SC) 132

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Summary: Family & Personal - Hindu Marriage Act, 1955, s. 13 13(1)(i-a), (b) - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 498A - Respondent/wife filed a petition u/s. 9 of the Act for restitution of conjugal rights before the Family Court - Appellant/husband filed a counter-claim seeking dissolution of marriage on the ground of cruelty and desertion u/s. 13(1)(i-a) and (b) of the Act - Family Court dismissed the petition and allowed the counter claim - On appeal filed by respondent HC set aside the decree of divorce granted in favour of appellant - Hence instant appeal - Whether appellant could be granted a decree of divorce -

Held, SC was of the opinion that decree of divorce must be granted - SC, alive to the plight of respondent - Appellant was working as an Assistant Registrar in the Andhra Pradesh HC - Appellant was getting a good salary - Respondent fought the litigation for more than 10 years - Respondent appeared to be entirely dependent on her parents and on her brother, therefore, her future should be secured by directing appellant to give her permanent alimony - Appellant should be directed to pay a sum of Rs.15,00,000/- (Rupees Fifteen Lakhs only) to respondent as and by way of permanent alimony - Impugned judgment was quashed and set aside - Marriage between appellant and respondent was dissolved by a decree of divorce - SC issued directions, which the courts dealing with the matrimonial matters should follow - In terms of s. 9 of the Act, the Family Courts should make all efforts to settle the matrimonial disputes through mediation - Even if the Counsellors submit a failure report, the Family Courts should, with the consent of the parties, refer the matter to the mediation centre - Family Courts should set a reasonable time limit for mediation centres to complete the process of mediation because otherwise the resolution of the disputes by the Family Court may get delayed - In a given case, if there was good chance of settlement, the Family Court in its discretion, could always extend the time limit - Criminal courts dealing with the complaint u/s. 498-A of IPC should, at any stage and particularly, before they take up the complaint for hearing, refer the parties to mediation centre if they feel that there exist elements of settlement and both the parties were willing - All mediation centres should set up pre-litigation desks/clinics; give them wide publicity and make efforts to settle matrimonial disputes at pre-litigation stage - Appeal disposed of.
