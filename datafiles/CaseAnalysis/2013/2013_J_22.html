Registrar General High Court of Gujarat and another v Jayshree Chamanlal Buddhbhatti

Supreme Court of India

22 October 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Gokhale, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 699; (2013) 16 SCC 59; 2014 (4) AWC 3509; 2014 (140) FLR 130; 2014 (1) OLR 244; 2013(13) SCALE 230; [2013] 11 S.C.R. 395; 2014 (2) SLR 241; 2013 (4) WLN(SC) 117

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Constitution of India, 1950, art.311(2) - Termination of service because respondent raised voice against mis behavior of staff and bar members - Sustainability - Appeal raised the question with respect to the disputed termination of services of the respondent from Judiciary - Impugned judgment had accepted the contention of the respondent that the termination of respondent's services was a stigmatic one, and she was removed from her services after an inquiry in which she was not informed of the charges against her, nor was she given the opportunity of being heard in respect of those charges, which were the minimum requirements u/art. 311 (2) of the Constitution - As against that, the contention of the appellants was that the respondent's service was discontinued during the period of her probation, since she was not found suitable for the post she was holding, and it was not a stigmatic termination to attract art.311 (2) of the Constitution - Appellants contended that it was a case of termination simpliciter of the services of a probationer during the extended period of probation, without any stigma attached, and therefore, the High Court, on the judicial side, had no reason to interfere - Initial discreet inquiry, and the subsequent preliminary inquiry were both for the purpose of ascertaining the suitability of the respondent, and not for establishing any allegations against her - If that was so, appellants submitted, that there was no reason for the HC on the judicial side to interfere in the decision arrived at by the HC administration, to discontinue the services of the respondent on the ground of unsuitability - Held, respondent was a candidate who had obtained a high rank in the selection for the judicial service, and was given an independent posting in a rural area, where she was living all alone - Respondent's disposal of cases had been very good to say the least - Complaints made by respondent, regarding the mis behaviour of the staff, and the harassment to her by a section of the bar, were not heeded by the then District Judge, leave aside making an attempt to understand the difficulties faced by her - Instead, certain unjustified adverse remarks were made against respondent - Subsequently, the then District Judge conducted the preliminary inquiry against respondent, in his capacity as the vigilance officer, wherein without any justification he tried to connect her with the death of the wife of another judicial officer - It was the duty of the District Judge and also of the HC to protect the judicial officers against unjustified allegations - However, what SC found in instant case was that instead of doing the same, an investigation was conducted against the respondent without affording her any opportunity, though it contained allegations against her character, and the investigation was sought to be justified as determination of her suitability for the post which she was holding - SC would like to take this opportunity to emphasise that the HCs should see to it that the hostile work environment for junior judicial officers, particularly the lady officers, could be eliminated - It was necessary to encourage the young officers to put in good judicial work without fear or favour - SC was constrained to say that in instant case the HC administration had clearly failed in that behalf - In the circumstances, SC had no reason to interfere in the judgment and order of HC and confirmed the same - Appeal dismissed.
