Indrajit Sureshprasad Bind & Ors. v State Of Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

18 March 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Patnaik, Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 162; (2013) 14 SCC 678; 2013 (2) JCC 1356; 2013 (2) MLJ(Crl) 542; 2013 (2) OLR 109; 2013 (3) RCR(Criminal) 225; 2013(4) SCALE 569; [2013] 2 S.C.R. 931

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 304B, 498A and 306 r/w s. 114 - Dowry death - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellants allegedly harassed deceased for not bringing dowry and committed offences u/ss. 304B, 498A and 306 r/w s. 114 of IPC - Trial Court convicted appellants u/ss. 304B, 498A and 306 of IPC - Aggrieved appellants filed an appeal before HC, which was dismissed - Aggrieved appellants filed instant appeal - Appellants contended that deceased was in 'C' village of State 'B' with her brother's family and was not at 'A' from where letter dt.16-2-2004 (Ext.49) was said to have written by deceased to complainant/PW 3 (brother of deceased) and that deceased was a minor and wanted to remain with her parents due which deceased committed suicide - Whether impugned conviction of appellants was justified - Held, besides Ext. 49 there was no other evidence of a prosecution witness to establish that appellants had, in any way, subjected deceased to cruelty or harassment - Ext. 44 was an inland letter and bear postal stamp of not only post office of 'dispatch' in 'B' but also post office of 'receipt' in 'A' - Evidence of DW 1 (appellant no. 1) supported by Ext. 44 thus made it probable that deceased was not at 'A' but at 'C' village in 'B' State on 16-02-2004 when she was alleged to have written Ext. 49 alleging demand of dowry and ill-treatment by appellants towards her - from a reading of Ext. 49 which was in Hindi, it was found at many places the author of letter used words in 'puling' (masculine) instead of 'striling' (Feminine), which raised serious doubts as to whether letter was written by a woman or by a man - There were grave doubts as to whether Ext. 49 was actually written by deceased or not, conviction of appellants only on the basis of Ext. 49 was unsafe - Even if there was demand of dowry, mere 'demand of dowry' without proof of 'cruelty' or 'harassment' caused to deceased by appellants could not make the appellants liable for offences u/s. 304B, 498A or 306 of IPC - Prosecution was not able to prove beyond reasonable doubt that appellants have subjected deceased to any cruelty or harassment - No evidence to establish that appellants instigated deceased to commit suicide - Impugned judgment passed by HC was set aside - Appeal allowed.
