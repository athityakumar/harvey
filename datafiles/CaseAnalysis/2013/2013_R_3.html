R. K. Jain v Union of India and another

Supreme Court of India

16 April 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 240; (2013) 14 SCC 794; 2013 (4) ALD(SC) 33; 2013(3) ALL MR 932; 2013 (3) AWC 2573; 2013 (4) Bom.C.R. 76; 2013 (3) CLT(SC) 80; 2013 (291) E.L.T. 3; JT 2013 (10) SC 430; 2013 (2) RCR(Civil) 838; [2013] 5 S.C.R. 411; 2013 (2) UPLBEC 1481

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Constitution - Administrative - Practice & Procedure - Right to Information Act, 2005, ss. 6, 8(1)(j), 19(3), 11, 11(1) - Right to information - Public interest - Integrity - Appellant filed an application to Central Public Information Officer (CPIO) u/s. 6 of the Act seeking copies of all note sheets and correspondence pages of file relating to Member/CESTAT which was denied u/s. 8(1)(j) of the Act - On appeal, Appellate Authority disallowed the same - Appellant's further appeal before Central Information Commission(CIC) u/s. 19(3) of the Act was also rejected - Appellant filed a writ petition before HC - Single Judge dismissed petition holding that information sought by appellant was third party information wherein third party might plead a privacy defence and proper question would be as to whether divulging of such an information was in public interest or not and remitted matter back to CIC to consider issue after following procedure u/s. 11 of the Act - On appeal, DB dismissed appeal confirming order of Single Judge - Hence, instant appeal - Whether, in larger public interest information sought by appellant could be disclosed - Held, once information seeker was provided information relating to a third party, it was no longer in private domain - Such information seeker could then disclose in turn such information to whole World - Therefore, for providing information procedure outlined u/s. 11(1) of the Act could not be dispensed with - However, appellant sought for inspection of documents relating to ACR of Member, CESTAT, inter alia, relating to adverse entries in ACR and 'follow up action' taken therein on question of integrity - Girish Ramchandra Deshpande v. Central Information Commissioner and others, 2012 Indlaw SCO 578 relied on - Thus, no reason to interfere with impugned judgment passed by DB whereby order passed by Single Judge was affirmed - Appeal dismissed.
