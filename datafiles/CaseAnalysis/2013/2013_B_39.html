State of Rajasthan v Balveer @ Balli and another

Supreme Court of India

31 October 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Patnaik, Gyan Sudha Misra

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 728; (2013) 16 SCC 321; AIR 2014 SC 1117; 2014 CRLJ 314; 2013 (4) RCR(Criminal) 971; 2013(13) SCALE 429; [2013] 11 S.C.R. 557

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Approver, Former Statement

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss.302 and 376 r/w.s.34 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872 - Rape - Murder - Acquittal - Sustainability - Three Respondents allegedly committed offence of rape and murder - Two Respondents were convicted u/ss.302 and 376 IPC r/w.s.34 IPC by Trial Court and sentenced to death on the basis of evidence adduced by third respondent who had become approver of appellant/prosecution - By the impugned judgment, HC, therefore, declined to accept the death reference and allowed the three appeals of the respondents and set aside the judgment of the trial court and acquitted the respondents of the offences u/ss. 376(2)(g) and 302/34, IPC and directed that the respondents be released forthwith - Hence instant appeal - Whether acquittal of respondent Nos. 1and 2 was maintanable after approver adduced evidence of such crime - Held, testimony of PW-1 was corroborated by material particulars qua respondent No.1 and respondent No.2 and the only possible view on the evidence on record in the case was that both the respondents committed the rape and murder of the deceased and the Trial Court had rightly convicted them u/ss.376(2)(g) and 302 r/w. s.34 IPC - In the facts of the instant case, however, the view taken by the HC that the respondents were entitled to acquittal was not at all a possible view - Evidence on record, considered in the light of the provisions of the Act established beyond reasonable doubt that the respondents were guilty of the offences u/ss. 376(2)(g) and 302 r/w. s34 IPC - SC set aside the impugned judgment of the HC and held the respondents guilty of the offences u/ss. 376(2)(g) and 302 IPC - Appeal allowed.
