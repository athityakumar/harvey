Secretary To Government Of India v Sawinder Kaur And Another

Supreme Court of India

21 March 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, K.S. Radhakrishnan

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 181; (2013) 14 SCC 789; JT 2013 (4) SC 576; 2013 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1012; 2013 (3) MLJ 597; 2013(4) SCALE 619; [2013] 4 S.C.R. 788; 2013 (5) SLR 40; 2013 (2) UPLBEC 913

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Socio-Economic

Keywords: Freedom Fighters Pension Scheme, 1972

Summary: Administrative - Socio-Economic - Swatantrata Sainik Samman Pension Scheme, 1980 - Freedom Fighters Pension Scheme, 1972 - Freedom fighters pension - Entitlement of - Respondent No. 1' husband/deceased had applied for grant of freedom fighter pension on the basis that he had participated in freedom struggle and had joined Indian National Army (INA) or Azad Hind Fauj during 1941-42 in Singapore - Respondent No. 1' husband made claim for pension based on 1980 scheme - Prior to said Scheme, 1972 scheme was in vogue from 15-8-1972 - Husband of respondent No. 1 submitted application for grant of freedom fighters pension, army record showed that he was enrolled in army on 13-6-1939 and released from service on 14-2-1946 due to reduction of Indian Army, but not due to association with INA and was also paid service gratuity - Respondent No. 1' husband application was initially rejected on 16-8-1980 - After expiry of 9years, in 1989, respondent No. 1' husband claimed that he, being an ex-INA, was sent to New Guinea/New British Islands and had suffered immense hardships and, accordingly, sought pension in terms of Ministry of Home Affairs circular No. 8/4/83-FF(P) dt.31-1-1983 - His application was not entertained and prayer was not accepted - Aggrieved by the order of rejection, respondent No. 1' husband filed petition before HC and the same was disposed of with direction to respondent therein to pass speaking order in relation to his grievance within period of 6months - As his prayer was not accepted, respondent No. 1' husband filed petition before HC - HC issued direction to determine the issue afresh - Thereafter, Competent Authority, after due enquiry, accepted the prayer and directed that respondent No. 1' husband would be entitled to freedom fighters pension w.e.f.9-6-1994 - Being dissatisfied with determination of date of grant, respondent No. 1' husband filed petition in HC claiming that benefit should be extended to him from the date when the Scheme was made applicable, i.e., from 1-8-1980 - Single Judge directed petitioner therein was entitled to get the benefit of Freedom Fighters Pension Scheme from the date from which the original claim was filed i.e. 22-3-1973 along with interest @ 9 % p/a - On appeal by State, DB dismissed the appeal - Hence, instant appeal was filed by respondent no. 1/wife of deceased - From which date respondent no.1 would be entitled to get pension under the 1980 Scheme - Held, claim was not allowed on the basis of jail certificate produced by claimant but on the basis of oral statement of some other detenu - Competent Authority was not satisfied as regards fulfillment of conditions - There was no primary evidence available in the official records as required under the scheme to establish the claim of respondent-husband that he was an Ex- INA member and suffered in New Guinea/New Britain Islands to prove his eligibility for pension under the scheme - However, regard being had to the totality of circumstances, he was extended the benefit under the scheme as it was a case of benefit of doubt - As was evident from the orders passed by Single Judge as well as DB, there was no discussion in that regard but pension was granted from the date of application in an extremely mechanical manner - Appeal allowed.
