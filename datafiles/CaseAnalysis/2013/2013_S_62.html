Shrirampur Municipal Council, Shrirampur v Satyabhamabai Bhimaji Dawkher And Others

Supreme Court of India

01 April 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, H.L. Gokhale, Ranjana Prakash Desai

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 185; (2013) 5 SCC 627; AIR 2013 SC 3757; 2013(3) ALL MR 477; 2013 (3) Bom.C.R. 481; JT 2013 (4) SC 594; 2013 (5) MahLJ 492; 2013(5) SCALE 179; [2013] 2 S.C.R. 664

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Maharashtra Regional Town Planning (Amendment) Act, 1993

Summary: Land & Property - Administrative - Maharashtra Regional and Town Planning Act, 1966, 125, 126 and 127 - Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 300A - Steps for Acquisition were not taken by Govt. - Question of effectiveness of order of acquisition - Question which arose for consideration in appeals was whether reservation of the parcels of land owned by the respondents in the Regional plans/Development plans prepared under 1966 Act would be deemed to have lapsed because the same were not acquired or no steps were commenced in that respect within six months of the service of notice u/s. 127 of 1966 Act - Held, expression "no steps as aforesaid" used in s. 127 of 1966 Act had to be read in the context of the provisions of 1894 Act and mere passing of resolution by the Planning Authority or sending of letter to the Collector or even the State Govt. could not be treated as commencement of the proceedings for the acquisition of land under 1966 Act or 1894 Act - By enacting ss. 125 to 127 of 1966 Act, State Legislature had made definite departure from the scheme of acquisition enshrined in 1894 Act - But holistic reading of these provisions made it clear that while engrafting the substance of some of the provisions of 1894 Act in 1966 Act and leaving out other provisions, State Legislature had ensured that the landowners/other interested persons, whose land was utilized for execution of the Development plan/Town Planning Scheme, etc., were not left high and dry - That was the reason why time limit of ten years was prescribed in s. 31(5) of 1966 Act and also u/ss. 126 and 127 of 1966 Act for the acquisition of land, with stipulation that if the land was not acquired within six months of the service of notice u/s. 127 of 1966 Act or steps were not commenced for acquisition, reservation of the land would be deemed to have lapsed - Appellant's interpretation of the scheme of ss. 126 and 127 of 1966 Act, if accepted, would lead to absurd results and the landowners would be deprived of their right to use the property for an indefinite period without being paid compensation - That would tantamount to depriving the citizens of their property without the sanction of law and would result in violation of art. 300A of Constitution - SC had rightly observed in one case that steps towards the acquisition would really commence when the State Govt. took active steps for the acquisition of the particular piece of land which lead to publication of the declaration u/s. 6 of 1894 Act - Any other interpretation of the scheme of ss. 126 and 127 of 1966 Act would make the provisions wholly unworkable and leave the landowner at the mercy of the Planning Authority and the State Govt. - Girnar Traders v. State of Maharashtra, 2007 Indlaw SC 858, relied on - Appeals dismissed.
