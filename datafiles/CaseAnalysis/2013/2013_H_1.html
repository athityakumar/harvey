C. Keshavamurthy v H. K. Abdul Zabbar

Supreme Court of India

23 July 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Gokhale, J. Chelameswar

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 474; 2013 (4) BC 183; 2013 (4) JCC (NI) 190; JT 2014 (1) SC 370; 2013 (4) RCR(Civil) 15; 2013 (3) RCR(Criminal) 944; 2013 (3) RLW 2612; 2013(9) SCALE 676; [2013] 8 S.C.R. 429

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1888, s. 138 - Bouncing of cheques - Respondent had issued 4 cheques to the appellant, which had bounced - Appellant filed a complaint in the Court of Magistrate u/s. 138 of Act - Respondent contended that there was an agreement of sale between the parties, and that the appellant was a businessman dealing in lands, and it was in that transaction that the respondent had issued some cheques earlier, but since transaction did not fructify, he had issued a notice not to clear those cheques - Magistrate allowed said complaint on the ground that all the cheques, which had bounced were issued subsequent to the said notice - Addl. Sessions Judge partly allowed the appeal filed against said order - On appeal, HC held that the burden was always on the appellant to establish not only issuance of cheque, but existence of debt or legal liability - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order of the HC could be upheld - Held, disputed cheques were given subsequent to the notice not to clear the earlier cheques - There was no explanation as to why the subsequent cheques could not have been cleared - Agreement on the basis of which the submission was made was not produced in the courts below - That being so, on facts there was no error on the part of the Magistrate, as well as the Addl. Sessions Judge, in the view that they have taken - Impugned order of the HC was set aside and the order passed by the Addl. Sessions Judge was restored - Appeal allowed.
