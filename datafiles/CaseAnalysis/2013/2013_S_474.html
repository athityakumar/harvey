Mohit alias Sonu and Another v State of U.P. and Another

Supreme Court of India

01 July 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

M.Y. Eqbal, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 384; (2013) 7 SCC 789; AIR 2013 SC 2248; 2013 ALL MR (Cri) 3288; 2013 (3) JCC 2250; JT 2013 (9) SC 205; 2014(2) M.P.H.T. 271; 2013 (3) RCR(Criminal) 673; 2013(7) SCALE 620; [2013] 7 S.C.R. 86

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Power Of The Court, Revisional Jurisdiction, Summoning order, Exercise of inherent jurisdiction, Non-serving of notice, Efffect

Summary: (A)Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 147, 323, 504, 506, 304 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 319, 401, 397(2), 401, 482 - Summoning order - Revisional jurisdiction - Legality - Respondent no. 2 (complainant) lodged an FIR against appellants (accused) alleging that accused allegedly caused injuries to his uncle to him - Crime was registered against accused for offence u/ss. 147, 323, 504, 506, 304 of IPC, however IO submitted charge sheet against only against 5 accused leaving the names of two accused (appellants) - Complainant filed an application u/s. 319 of CrPC for summoning the appellants as accused in the case - Trial Court rejected application - Aggrieved complainant filed an application u/s. 482 of CrPC before HC, which was allowed by impugned order directed Lower Court to summon appellants - Aggrieved appellants filed instant appeal - Appellants contended that complainant ought to have challenged Trial Court order under revisional jurisdiction u/s. 397/401 of CrPC and that application u/s. 482 before HC was not maintainable and that impugned order was passed without giving notice and opportunity of hearing to appellants - Whether petition u/s. 482 of CrPC before HC challenging the order of Trial Court passed u/s. 319 of CrPC was maintainable - Held, order passed by Trial Court refusing to issue summons on application filed by complainant u/s. 319 of CrPC could not be held to be an interlocutory order within the meaning of s. 397(2) of CrPC - Admittedly, in instant case, before the Trial Court the complainant's application u/s. 319 of CrPC was rejected for second time holding that there was no sufficient evidence against appellants to proceed against them by issuing summons - Said order passed by Trial Court decide the rights and liabilities of appellants in respect of their involvement in case - Complainant ought to have challenged the order before HC in revision u/s. 397 of CrPC and not by invoking inherent jurisdiction of HC u/s. 482 of CrPC - Might be, in order to circumvent the provisions contained in ss. 397(2) or 401 of CrPC, the complainant moved the HC u/s. 482 of CrPC - In the event a criminal revision had been filed against the order of Trial Court passed u/s. 319 of CrPC, the HC before passing the order would have given notice and opportunity of hearing to appellants - Appeal allowed.

(B)Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 319, 401, 397(2), 401, 482 - Summoning order - Exercise of inherent jurisdiction - Without notice - Effect - Whether it would be justified for HC to entertain a petition u/s. 482 of CrPC and pass order to the prejudice of accused (appellants) without giving notice and opportunity of hearing to them - Held, a valuable right accrued to appellants by reason of the order passed by Trial Court refusing to issue summons on the ground that no prima facie case was made out on the basis of evidence brought on record - When Trial Court order was challenged, then it was incumbent upon the revisional Court to give notice and opportunity of hearing as contemplated u/s. 401(2) of CrPC - There was no reason why the same principle should not be applied in a case where such orders were challenged in HC u/s. 482 of CrPC - HC committed a grave error in passing impugned order - Impugned order was set aside and remand the matter back to HC to consider the matter afresh after giving an opportunity of hearing to appellants - Appeal allowed.
