Moti Lal Songara v Prem Prakash @ Pappu And Anr.

Supreme Court of India

16 May 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 321; (2013) 9 SCC 199; AIR 2013 SC 2078; 2013 ALL MR (Cri) 2657; 2013 CRLJ 2977; 2013 (3) JCC 1822; JT 2013 (7) SC 376; 2013 (3) MLJ(Crl) 50; 2013 (3) RCR(Criminal) 333; 2013 (3) RLW 1894; 2013(7) SCALE 320; [2013] 6 S.C.R. 496

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Suppression Of Facts, Attempt to murder, Powers of Magistrate

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 323, 324, 307 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 190, 190(1)(b) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 142 - Attempt to murder - Suppression of facts - Powers of Magistrate - Legality - Respondent no. 1 (accused) allegedly attacked son of appellant (informant) with knife and thereby allegedly committed offences u/ss. 323, 324, 307, 341, 379 of IPC - Addl. Chief Judicial Magistrate by order dt.19-11-2008 took cognizance against accused - Aggrieved accused filed revision before Addl. Sessions Judge, which was allowed on 14-10-2009 holding that offences were trialble by Court of Sessions and accordingly set aside order of Magistrate - Thereafter, Addl. Sessions Judge framed charges against accused u/ss. 307, 341 and 323, 324 r/w s. 34 of IPC - Aggrieved accused filed a revision before HC for quashing of charges, which was allowed by impugned order holding that order dt.19-11-2008 passed by Addl. Chief Judicial Magistrate taking cognizance and issuing summons was already set aside by Addl. Sessions Judge in a revision and, thus, principle 'when the infrastructure collapses, the superstructure was bound to collapse' got attracted - Hence, instant appeal - Whether impugned order passed by Single Judge discharging accused was justified in law - Held, anyone who takes recourse to method of suppression in a Court of law, was, in actuality, playing fraud with Court, and the maxim supressio veri, expression faisi, i.e., suppression of the truth was equivalent to the expression of falsehood, gets attracted - In instant case, order taking cognizance could not be found fault with - Addl. Chief Judicial Magistrate took cognizance on the basis of facts brought to its notice by informant and, thus, had, in fact, exercised power u/s. 190(1)(b) of CrPC - Though accused was fully aware about the fact that charges were framed against him by Trial Judge, yet he did not bring the same to the notice of revisional Court hearing the revision against order taking cognizance - It was a clear case of suppression - It was within the special knowledge of accused - Impugned order was obtained by practicing fraud and suppressing material fact before a Court of law to gain advantage, and impugned order could not be allowed to stand - Power u/art. 142 of Constitution was required to be invoked to do complete justice between parties - Cognizance of offences were rightly took by Magistrate and charges were correctly framed by Trial Judge - When an order of quashment of summons was obtained by suppression, SC had an obligation to set aside the said order and restore order framing charges - Impugned orders were set aside - Trial which was pending before Addl. Sessions Judges should proceed in accordance with law - Appeal allowed.
