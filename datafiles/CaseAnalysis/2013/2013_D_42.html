Amrit Lal Mehta v Director General of Revenue Intel. and Investigation and others

Supreme Court of India

12 November 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, Madan B. Lokur

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SCO 1705; 2017 (354) E.L.T. 4

Case Digest

Subject: Customs

Keywords: give, INDIA, jurisdiction, part, time, writ jurisdiction

Summary: Customs – Information on tax evasion – Non-payment of reward – Based on the information given by appellant, Customs Department was able to discover evasion of duty by a company and was able to recover customs duty to the tune of Rs.1.21 Crore and penalty of Rs.2.5 Crore – Appellant was aggrieved that respondent no.1 arbitrarily withheld reward amount which was due to him under Reward Policy framed by Govt. for the information supplied by him - Instant appeal by appellant against order of HC whereby matter was remanded to Reward Committee to consider afresh the rewards awarded vide minutes dt.26-3-2009 and 10-3-2010.

Held, considering quality, specificity and accuracy of the information provided, 7th Reward Committee had awarded appellant Rs.50 Lakh. However, in view of peculiar facts and circumstances of the case, said reward amount is increased to Rs.60 lakh and appellant is to be paid the same in full and final settlement of his claim. Appeal disposed of.
