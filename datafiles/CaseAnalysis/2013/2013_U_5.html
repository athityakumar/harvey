U.P. Power Corporation Limited and another v Virendra Lal (Dead) through Lrs.

Supreme Court of India

03 October 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 654; (2013) 10 SCC 39; AIR 2013 SC (Supp) 457; 2013(6) ALL MR 987; 2014 (2) AWC 1309; 2014 (140) FLR 531; 2013(12) SCALE 390; [2013] 11 S.C.R. 314; 2014 (3) SLR 199; 2014 (1) UPLBEC 30; 2014 (2) WLN(SC) 119

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Justifiability, Deduction in pension

Summary: U. P. State Electricity Board (Officers and Servants) (Conditions of Service) Regulations, 1975-Regulation 6-Employment-Pension-Reduction in pension-Delinquent employee, retired from service as Assistant Engineer which rank is lower than Superintending Engineer-Regulation 6 (4) clearly provides that Chairman is competent authority to pass order of punishment upto rank of Superintending Engineer-Hence, Chairman authorised to pass order of punishment-Under Regulations there is stipulation that appeal/ representation from order of Chairman shall lie to U. P. State Electricity Board (U.P.S.E.B.)-Not in dispute that disciplinary proceedings initiated against delinquent employee while he was in service and thereafter proceeding continued and eventually-U.P.S.E.B. passed order for deduction of 10% amount of pension-By virtue of order passed by U.P.S.E.B. remedy of appeal denied to delinquent employee-If appellate authority passes order as primary authority and there is provision for further appeal or revision or review it cannot be said that said order suffers from any illegality-Impugned order of High Court flawless and same upheld.
