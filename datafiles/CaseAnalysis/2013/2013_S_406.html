Mutha Associates and others v State of Maharashtra and others

Supreme Court of India

04 July 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, M.Y. Eqbal

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 874; (2013) 14 SCC 304; 2014 (1) AWC 984; JT 2013 (10) SC 328; 2014 (123) RD 184; [2013] 10 S.C.R. 1051

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: (1) Land Acquisition Act, 1894-Section 48-Acquisition of land-Withdrawal of acquisition-Denial of opportunity of hearing to beneficiary-Requirement of hearing to beneficiary before withdrawal of acquisition proceedings-Withdrawal of acquisition proceedings-Bad not only because it was arbitrary, lacked objectivity and ignored material on record-But also because order of withdrawal passed without offering fair and reasonable opportunity of being heard in the matter-Withdrawal of acquisition not notified in terms of Section 48-Withdrawal of acquisition not notified in official Gazette-Order of withdrawal of acquisition, ineffective and non-est in eye of law-High Court rightly quashed order of withdrawal of acquisition proceedings-No flaw in reasoning of High Court insofar it upheld validity of acquisition proceedings on merit.

(2) Malice in fact-Plea based on 'malice in fact' or mala fides-Law regarding pleading and proof of 'malice in fact' or mala fides.
