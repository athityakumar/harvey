Chhotan Sao and another v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

17 December 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

J. Chelameswar, Ranjana Prakash Desai

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 851; (2014) 4 SCC 54; AIR 2014 SC 907; 2014 ALL MR (Cri) 397; 2014 (1) MLJ(Crl) 208; 2014 (1) RCR(Criminal) 474; 2013(15) SCALE 338

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 328, 304B and 498A - Dowry Prohibition Act, 1961, ss. 3 and 4 - Cruelty - Dowry death - Conviction - Challenged - Accused were charged for the offences u/ss. 328, 304B and 498A of IPC and ss. 3 and 4 of the Act on the allegation that they harassed and were responsible for the unnatural death of deceased, the daughter of PWs1 and 6 - Accused were found guilty of the offences they were charged with by the Trial Court - Appeal filed thereagainst was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether conviction of accused persons for the offences u/ss. 304B and 498A of IPC was correct - Held, conviction of the accused u/s. 498A of IPC call for no interference as there was concurrent finding by both the courts below based on evidence that the accused (husband) and his relatives subjected deceased to cruelty as explained u/s. 498A of IPC - Also, prosecution adduced sufficient evidence to establish all other facts necessary to prove the offence u/s. 304B of IPC except the cause of death - There were no injuries on the body of the deceased - Conclusion recorded by both the Courts below that deceased died an unnatural death was not based on any legal material on record - None of the witnesses spoke to the factum of their witnessing deceased consuming poison either under compulsion or otherwise - Statement in the FIR by PW8 was based on hearsay evidence - Hence, accused should be acquitted of the offence u/s. 304B of IPC - Order accordingly.
