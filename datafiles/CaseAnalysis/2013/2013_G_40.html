Hari Prakash Agarwal and another v Gopi Krishan (Dead) Through Lrs. and others

Supreme Court of India

11 April 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 231; (2013) 11 SCC 296; 2013 (98) ALR 269; 2013 (4) AWC 3856; 2013 (3) CLT(SC) 121; 2013 (3) CTC 356; JT 2013 (8) SC 329; 2013 (119) RD 813; 2013(5) SCALE 670; [2013] 6 S.C.R. 127

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Keywords: Permissibility, Stanger to acquisition proceedings, Interference on award

Summary: Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 9 r. 13 r/w. s. 151 - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4, 18, 30 - Stanger to acquisition proceedings - Interference on award - Permissibility - Respondent No. 1 filed suit against J seeking relief of declaration stating that J was only a life estate holder in respect of properties and that further, she was not entitled to receive compensation or rehabilitation grant bonds with respect to village - Trial Court decreed the suit holding that J was in fact life estate holder of R property, she was also entitled to receive said compensation in respect of property in question - However, suit land was acquired by State Govt. for development of road scheme u/s. 4 of the Act - Respondent No. 1 approached Tribunal u/ss. 18 and 30 of the Act claiming compensation in respect of properties acquired by the State - In the meanwhile predecessor in interest of appellants, filed a reference u/s. 18 of the Act, for enhancement of compensation in respect of half share in suit land - Respondent No. 1 was not pleaded as party - Tribunal by order dt.22-5-2000 held that opposite parties were entitled to receive compensation relating to aforesaid property - Respondent No. 1 filed an application under O. 9 r. 13 r/w. s. 151 CPC, for purpose of setting aside the award dt.22-5-2000 - Tribunal rejected the application, on ground that an application under O. 9 r. 13 CPC could only be filed by a person who was a party to proceedings in which such an order was passed - Respondents filed writ petition before HC, which was allowed and held that while an application under O. 9 r. 13 CPC was not maintainable, the said award should set aside in exercise of its powers u/s. 151 CPC - Hence, instant appeal - Whether HC was justified in passing the impugned order - Held, permitting an application under O. 9 r. 13 CPC by a non- party, should amount to adding a party to case, which was provided for under O. 1 r. 10 CPC, or setting aside ex-parte judgment and decree, i.e. seeking a declaration that decree was null and void for any reason, which could be sought independently by such a party - In instant case, as fraud, if any, as alleged, was committed upon a party, and not upon Court, same was not a case where s. 151 CPC could be resorted to by Court, to rectify a mistake, if any was made - Inherent powers u/s. 151 CPC could be exercised by Court to redress only such a grievance, for which no remedy was provided for under CPC - In event that an order was obtained from Court by playing fraud upon it, it was always open to Court to recall said order on application of person aggrieved, and such power could also be exercised by Appellate Court - A person aggrieved might maintain an application before Land Acquisition Collector for reference u/s. 18 or 30 of the Act, but could not make an application for impleadment or apportionment before Reference Court - HC was not justified in passing the impugned order - Impugned order passed by HC was set aside - Appeal allowed.
