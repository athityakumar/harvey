Surya Baksh Singh v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

07 October 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Vikramajit Sen, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 673; (2014) 14 SCC 222; 2013 (4) KLT 493; 2014 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 511; 2013 (4) RCR(Criminal) 880; 2013(12) SCALE 492

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Overruled, Adjourn

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 482 - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Misuse of statutory right to appeal - Inherent Power of Court - Appeal filed against conviction was dismissed by HC - Hence, instant appeal - Whether it was essential for the HC to have appointed amicus curiae - Held, it was necessary for the Appellate Court which was confronted with the absence of the convict as well as his counsel, to immediately proceed against the persons who stood surety at the time when the convict was granted bail, as this would lead to his discovery and production in Court; if even this exercise failed to locate and bring forth the convict, the Appellate Court was empowered to dismiss the appeal - In the instant case, HC considered the case in all its complexities - HC discussed the evidence that have been led, and finding it of probative value, and concluded that the conviction was above appellate reproach correction and interference - HC had duly undertaken the curial responsibility that fastened upon the Appellate Court, and could not be faulted on the approach adopted by it - No error found - Hence, contention that it was essential for the HC to have appointed an amicus curiae was untenable - However, since the convict was not represented through counsel before the HC, appellant permitted opportunity to argue the appeal on its merits - Order accordingly.
