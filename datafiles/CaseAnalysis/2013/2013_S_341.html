State of Jammu and Kashmir and another v Lakhwinder Kumar and another

Supreme Court of India

25 April 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Chandramauli Kumar Prasad, Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 262; (2013) 6 SCC 333; AIR 2013 SC 3163; 2013 CRLJ 3307; JT 2013 (6) SC 264; 2013 (3) RCR(Criminal) 180; 2013(6) SCALE 332; [2013] 2 S.C.R. 1070

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Defence & Security Forces; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Active Duty, Criminal Courts and Court Martial (Adjustment of Jurisdiction) Rules, 1952, Jammu and Kashmir State Code of Criminal Procedure, Svt. 1989, Jammu & Kashmir Criminal Courts and Court Martial (Adjustment of Jurisdiction) Rules, 1983, Trial by Security Force Court, Discretion of Commanding Officer

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Defence & Security Forces - Jammu and Kashmir State Ranbir Penal Code, 1989, ss. 109, 201, 302 - Jammu and Kashmir State Code of Criminal Procedure, Svt. 1989, ss. 549 - Border Security Force Act, 1968, s. 80 - Jammu and Kashmir Criminal Courts and Court Martial (Adjustment of Jurisdiction) Rules, 1983, rr. 7, 41(1)(i), (ii), 41(2) - Criminal proceedings - Trial by Security Force Court - Discretion of Commanding Officer - Legality - Respondents (accused) allegedly committed offences u/ss. of 302, 109 and 201 RPC - Respondent security force personnel were arrested -Dy. Inspector General (DIG) of security force filed application u/s. 549 of CrPC stating that it was decided to institute proceedings against accused persons before Security Force Court and would be intimated to Court as required u/r. 7 of the Rules - Chief Judicial Magistrate (CJM) allowed application and handed over accused together with charge-sheet for trying the accused by Security Force Court - Aggrieved appellant State and uncle of deceased filed separate revisions before HC, which were dismissed by impugned judgment - Hence, instant appeals - Whether Commanding Officer had followed guidelines as required u/r. 41 of the Rules while exercising powers u/s. 80 of 1968 Act - Held, r. 41(1)(ii) of the Rules authorized officer competent to exercise its power u/s 80 of 1968 Act to decide as to whether or not it would be necessary in the interest of discipline to claim for trial by a Security Force Court - R. 41(2) of the Rules enumerated the factors which officer competent u/s. 80 of 1968 Act was to take into account for taking a decision for trial of an accused by a Security Force Court - None of the clauses of rr. 41(1)(i) and 41(2) of the Rules were applied in facts of instant case - Commanding Officer, exercised his power u/s. 80 of 1968 Act and excepting to say that said power was exercised in his discretion, there was not even a whisper as to why said discretion was exercised for trial of accused persons by a Security Force Court - Commanding Officer had nowhere stated that trial of accused by Security Force Court was necessary in the interest of discipline of Force - Once a statutory guideline was issued for giving effect to provisions of 1968 Act, exercise of discretion without adherence to those guidelines should render the decision vulnerable - Commanding Officer exercised his power ignorant of restriction placed on him under the Rules - In facts and circumstances of case, Director General of Force if came to conclusion that trial deserved to be conducted by Security Force Court, nothing would prevent him to make an appropriate application afresh before CJM- Impugned judgment and order of CJM and that of HC were set aside - Security Force Court should forthwith transmit record sent to it to CJM - Appeals allowed.
