Madhao and another v State of Maharashtra and another

Supreme Court of India

03 May 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, J. S. KHEHAR

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 284; (2013) 5 SCC 615; 2013 ALL MR (Cri) 2264; JT 2013 (9) SC 74; 2013 (2) RCR(Criminal) 975; 2013(6) SCALE 529; [2013] 5 S.C.R. 484

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 420, 419, 468 and 34 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 482 - Quashing of FIR - Sustainability - State Govt. published Govt. Resolution on 2-6-2004 wherein it was informed to public at large that percentage of educated un-employed amongst Scheduled Caste and neo- Buddhist were on higher side and those who were below poverty line were required to work under different schemes and their standard of living was consequently adversely affected - For said reason, it was resolved that land should be made available to such people to create a source of income for them - For the said purpose, a scheme was framed by name Karamveer Dadasaheb Gaikwad Sabalikaran and Swabhiman Yojana Samiti - As per the Scheme, a Committee was constituted and Scheme was made applicable with effect from 1-4-2004 - As per the Scheme, land was to be purchased by Govt. and was to be made available to persons belonging to Scheduled Caste and neo-Buddhist who were below poverty line - On 4-4-2005, State Govt. purchased agricultural land situated at village 'K' - Said land was jointly owned by 8persons - Appellants, after perusing revenue records of said land purchased it from Vendors by getting executed a registered sale deed - On 4-6-2005, A newspaper published article in which it was alleged that petitioners purchased agricultural land showing 'RR' as alive while he was dead - It was further alleged that one 'RR' had signed the sale deed as 'RJ' - Appellant No. 1 made an enquiry and recorded statements of said 8Executants and on 2-7-2005 lodged a report in PS against them for offence of impersonation and cheating - On 9-9-2005, one 'D', claiming himself to be a Social Worker, filed Criminal Complaint before Magistrate against appellants and few more persons - In said complaint it was alleged that accused had purchased land from a dead person, namely, 'RJ', while appellants were acting in their official capacity under said Scheme - Magistrate directed Police to investigate the matter u/s. 156 (3) of CrPC - Appellants filed application u/s. 482 of CrPC before HC seeking quashing of prosecution - HC dismissed said application and held that procedure adopted and power exercised by Magistrate ordering investigation u/s. 156(3) of CrPC was just and proper - Hence instant appeals - Whether Magistrate was justified in directing Police to investigate and submit a detailed report u/s. 156(3) of CrPC - Held, where Magistrate orders investigation by police before taking cognizance u/s. 156(3) of CrPC and receives report thereupon he could act on the report and discharge accused or straightaway issue process against accused or apply his mind to the complaint filed before him and take action u/s. 190 of CrPC - Devarapalli Lakshminarayana Reddy and Others v. Narayana Reddy and Others, 1976 Indlaw SC 591 and Tula Ram and Others v. Kishore Singh, 1977 Indlaw SC 397, wherein principles laid down - SC was satisfied that magistrate had not exceeded his power nor violated any of provisions contained in CrPC - Magistrate need not order any investigation if he pre-supposes to take cognizance of the offence and once he took cognizance of the offence, he had to follow procedure provided in Chap. 15 of CrPC - It was also settled position that any judicial magistrate before taking cognizance of the offence could order investigation u/s. 156(3) of CrPC - SC was of the view that procedure adopted and power exercised by Magistrate was acceptable and in accordance with the scheme of CrPC - Appeals dismissed.
