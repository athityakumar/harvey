Goudappa & Ors. v State Of Karnataka

Supreme Court of India

11 March 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Chandramauli Kumar Prasad, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 129; (2013) 3 SCC 675; AIR 2013 SC 1595; 2013 CRLJ 1762; JT 2013 (3) SC 562; 2013 (4) KarLJ 394; 2013 (2) MLJ(Crl) 641; 2013 (2) RCR(Criminal) 316; 2013(3) SCALE 309; [2013] 4 S.C.R. 547

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 109, 143, 148, 452, 341, 302, 427, 504, 506, 304 Part II, 34 - Murder - Common intention - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellants/accused and their brothers were put on trial for offence u/ss. 143, 148, 452, 341, 302, 427, 504 and 506 r/w s. 149 of IPC - Trial Court acquitted accused Nos. 1 and 2 of all the charges - Accused Nos. 3 and 4 were held guilty u/s. 304, Part II r/w s. 109 of IPC Accused No. 5, had been convicted u/s. 304, Part II of IPC - They however, been acquitted of all other charges - State/respondent aggrieved by order preferred appeal, HC by the impugned judgment and order set aside the judgment of acquittal and held all accused guilty u/ss. 143 and 148 of IPC and three found guilty u/s. 304, Part II r/w s. 109 or u/s. 304, Part II of IPC simplicitor have, instead, been convicted u/s. 302 and 34 - Hence instant appeal - Whether accused had the common intention to commit an offence of which they could be convicted - Held, SC without hesitation had come to the conclusion that deceased was done to death in furtherance of their common intention - All accused had assembled at one place and the moment deceased came out of the house to spit, one of accused started abusing him - They were armed with axe and jambia and by catching and immobilizing deceased these two accused facilitated the assault by accused no. 5 - Accused no. 5 stabbed the deceased with jambia over the left side of the chest and the blow was so severe that it penetrated into the heart and liver - Fact that these appellants held deceased and facilitated the other accused to give the fatal blow and made no effort to prevent him from assaulting the deceased leads to irresistible and inescapable conclusion that these two appellants shared the common intention with accused No. 5 - Intention of accused No. 5 was clear from the nature of weapon used and the severity of attack which was in the area of chest penetrating deep inside up to heart and liver which caused death of deceased - In Ramesh Singh v. State of A.P., 2004 Indlaw SC 226, relied on - Impugned judgement and order of HC was upheld - Appeal dismissed.
