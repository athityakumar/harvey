Noor Mohammed v Jethanand And Another

Supreme Court of India

29 January 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 51; (2013) 5 SCC 202; AIR 2013 SC 1217; 2013 (3) ALD(SC) 1; 2013 (97) ALR 528; 2013 (2) AWC 2101; 2013 (1) CLT(SC) 191; JT 2013 (2) SC 386; 2013 (2) RCR(Civil) 538; 2013(2) SCALE 94; [2013] 3 S.C.R. 1146

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Suit for Perpetual Injunction - Dismissed for Non-prosecution - Adjournment - Maintainability - Respondent-plaintiff had initiated civil action for injunction to restrain defendant-appellant therein from selling or otherwise transferring suit land and further to permanently injunct defendant from making any construction on land in dispute - Written statement was filed, a counter claim was put forth by defendant, Trial Court dismissed suit and decreed counter claim filed by defendant - Aggrieved respondent No. 1 preferred appeal before District Judge, who dismissed appeal - Respondent preferred appeal before HC, which was listed for admission - Matter was listed for several occasions but non appeared, HC, ordered notice to appellants and adjourned matter, but non appeared and appeal was dismissed for non-prosecution in 2003 - Appellant before HC, filed application for restoration in 2004 which was allowed in 2006, since than restoration appeal was listed and adjourned due to non-appearance of appellant counsel and finally in 2011, HC, admitted appeal and framing 2 substantial question of law and also granted stay on judgment passed by Courts below - Aggrieved instant SLP - Whether HC, was justified in granting stay of operation of Judgment and whether there existed substantial question of law to entertain appeal - Petitioner, had contended that there was no substantial question of law involved and HC, had no reason to entertain second appeal only on factual score - Held, Court should not have shown indulgence of such magnitude by adjourning matter when counsel for appellant was not present, it was difficult to envision why Court directed fresh notice to appellant when there was nothing suggestive for passing of such an order - Matter should had been dealt with taking a recourse to provisions in CPC, it was also astonishing that lawyers sought adjournments in a routine manner and court also acceded to such prayers - When matter stood dismissed, though an application for restoration was filed, yet it was listed after a long lapse of time, adding to misery, concerned official took his own time to put file in order - There was enormous delay in dealing with case, had timely effort been made and due concern bestowed, it could had been avoided. There may be cases where delay may be unavoidable, counsel sought adjournment after adjournment in a nonchalant manner and same were granted in a routine fashion, it was duty of counsel as officer of court to assist court in a properly prepared manner and not to seek unnecessary adjournments, getting an adjournment was neither an art nor science it had never been appreciated by courts - Though SC, dwelled upon issue, yet it restrained from issuing any directions, for HC as a constitutional Court had to carry burden and live up to requisite expectations of litigants - Petition disposed of.
