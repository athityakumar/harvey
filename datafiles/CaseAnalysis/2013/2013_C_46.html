Chironjilal Sharma Huf v Union of India and others

Supreme Court of India

26 November 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, Kurian Joseph, Madan B. Lokur

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 793; [2014] 360 ITR 237; 2013(14) SCALE 523; [2013] 12 S.C.R. 666; [2014] 222 TAXMAN 352

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Denied

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 132(5), 132B(4)(b) - Payment of interest - Denied - Justifiability - Tax liability of appellant (assessee) was calculated and cash was seized in a search from appellant's house and an order u/s. 132(5) of the Act was passed - Tribunal set aside the order passed by AO and appellant was refunded the seized amount - Appellant claimed that he was entitled to interest u/s. 132B(4)(b) of the Act, filed instant appeal - Whether appellant was entitled to interest as claimed - Held, interest for the post assessment period i.e. from (date of last of the regular assessments by the AO) until refund on the excess amount has already been paid by dept. to appellant - Dept. denied the payment of interest to appellant u/s. 132B(4)(b) of the Act, which was not right on the plain reading of s. 132B(4)(b) of the Act - Appellant was entitled to interest as claimed - Impugned order was set aside - Appeal allowed.
