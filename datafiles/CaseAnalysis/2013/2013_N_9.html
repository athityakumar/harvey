NOIDA Entrepreneurs' Association v New Okhla Industrial Development Authority and others

Supreme Court of India

27 November 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, V. Gopala Gowda

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 775; (2014) 13 SCC 335; 2014 (1) AWC 610; 2013(14) SCALE 475

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property

Keywords: Uttar Pradesh, Industrial Area Development Act, 1976

Summary: Constitution of India-Articles 14 and 32-U. P. Industrial Area Development Act, 1976-Allotment of land and plots-Illegal and irregular allotments-Major land scandals-Petitioner filed writ petition under Article 32 by way of public interest litigation and prayed that thorough probe be ordered into allotment of land and plots and abuse of power by functionaries of N.O.I.D.A.-C.B.I. found that allotment was end product of conspiracy-Direction to issue notices to all concerned persons to show cause as to why allotments/alternative allotments made in their favour may not be quashed
