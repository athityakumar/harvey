State Of Rajasthan v Bheru Lal

Supreme Court of India

28 May 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 350; (2013) 11 SCC 730; AIR 2013 SC 2288; 2013 CRLJ 3223; 2013 (3) JCC (Narcotics) 124; 2013 (3) RCR(Criminal) 292; 2013(7) SCALE 428; [2013] 6 S.C.R. 252

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Possession of Narcotics Drugs

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Narcotics Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985, ss. 8, 18, 42(1) - Possession of Narcotics Drugs - Acquittal - Sustainability - Respondent/accused was allegedly in possession of contraband articles - Accused was arrested and was booked u/ss. 8, 18 of the Act - Trial Court convicted accused for alleged offences u/ss. 8, 18 of the Act and sentenced him to undergo RI for 10 years - On appeal, HC reversed findings of Trial Court and acquitted accused - Hence, instant appeal - Whether acquittal recorded by HC was sustainable - Held, Sub Inspectors of Police, posted as Station House Officers, were authorised by State to exercise powers enumerated in s. 42 of the Act - However, there was cogent and reliable evidence on record that Station House Officer (SHO) had left police station for certain length of time and at that juncture, he had given charge of SHO to PW2 - Hence, search, seizure and arrest carried out by PW2/Station House officer would not make trial ab initio void - Thus, HC was in grave error by opining that s. 42(1) of the Act was not complied with as entire exercise was carried out by an officer who was not authorised - Impugned judgment passed by HC was set aside and judgment rendered by Trial Court was restored - Appeal allowed.
