Aresh @ Ashok J. Mehta (D) By Prop. Lrs. v Spl. Tahsildar, Balgaum Karnataka & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

11 March 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 144; (2013) 4 SCC 349; AIR 2013 SC 1527; JT 2013 (4) SC 87; 2013 (4) KarLJ 433; 2013 (3) MLJ 760; 2013 (120) RD 136; 2013(3) SCALE 450; [2013] 2 S.C.R. 280

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Punjab Requisitioning and Acquisition of Immovable Property Act, 1953, East Punjab Requisition of Immovable Property (Temporary Powers) Act, Claim of interest

Summary: Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Administrative - Karnataka Land Reforms Act, 1961, ss. 44, 47, 51 - Compensation - Delayed payment - Claim of interest - Entitled to - Appellant (landlord) claimed to be owner of land in question - Land was vested with State Govt. for grant in favour of a tenant u/s. 44 of the Act - Thereafter, special Tehsildar determined quantum of amount payable to appellant as compensation along with interest - Appellant filed an application and claimed interest on compensation @ 5½ % till payment of entire amount, which was rejected - Aggrieved appellant filed a petition before HC - Single Judge held that interest @5 ½% was payable, if compensation amount was paid through National savings certificates - Aggrieved appellant filed an appeal before DB, which was dismissed - Aggrieved appellant filed instant appeal - Appellant contended that owners of land could not be deprived of interest on compensation amount from the date principal amount become due and that appellant was entitled to interest amount even if principal amount was paid in cash and that - Whether appellant was entitled to interest for delayed payment of compensation - Held, every land-owner, landlord and all other persons interested in land were entitled for amount payable, for the extinguishment of their rights in lands vested in Govt. determined with reference to net annual income derivable from land in accordance with s. 47 of the Act - As per circular dt.24-11-1986, entire amount of compensation payable to ex-landlords along with interest was to be calculated taking 1-3-1974 as cut-off date up to 1-3-1984 - But if amount was paid earlier then upto the date of payment - Out of total amount Rs.2,000/- was paid in cash and rest through National Savings Certificates - If amount was not paid on or before 1-3-1984, provisions under the Act made to pay further interest @ 5 ½% on entire principal amount from 1-3-1984 till date of purchase of National Savings Certificates; that means authorities were required to either invest the amount in National Savings Certificates or pay interest till amount was invested - Substantive provision of 'mode of calculation' as prescribed u/s. 51 of the Act was clarified by circular dt.24-11-1986 - Example cited in said circular was merely an illustration - If illustration was conflicting with clarification of substantive law/provision or if illustration was vague, the clarification would prevail over the illustration - In such case, a person who was entitled for interest as per clarification could not be deprived of or denied his right relying on illustration - Appellant was entitled for interest w.e.f 1-3-1974 @ 5 ½% till total amount was paid to him - Respondent could not deny interest on amount of compensation to which appellant was entitled as a matter of general rule, and in the light of clarification made by circular dt.24-11-1986 - Respondents were directed to pay appellant interest @ 5 ½% p.a - Impugned judgments passed by Single Judge and DB were set aside - Appeal allowed.
