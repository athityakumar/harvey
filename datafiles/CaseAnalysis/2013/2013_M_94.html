Commissioner of Police, New Delhi and another v Mehar Singh and another

Supreme Court of India

02 July 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjana Prakash Desai, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 420; (2013) 7 SCC 685; AIR 2013 SC 2861; 2013(5) ALL MR 465; JT 2013 (9) SC 470; 2013 (5) MLJ 593; 2013(9) SCALE 444; 2013 (5) SLR 228

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Departmental Proceedings, Delhi Police (Appointment and Recruitment) Rules, 1980, Eve-teasing Act

Summary: Service - Candidature cancelled - Challenged - Appellants issued an advertisement for filling up the post of constables (Exe.) (male) - Respondent Nos. 1 and 2 applied for the same pursuant to the advertisement issued by the appellants - Respondent No. 1 disclosed his involvement in criminal case and his acquittal as both parties had entered into a compromise - Respondent No. 2 disclosed that a criminal case was registered against him however, he was acquitted in the said case by giving him benefit of doubt - After interview, respondents were declared provisionally selected, subject to verification of character and antecedents - Screening Committee does not recommend case of respondents for appointment to the post of constable - Deputy Commissioner of Police (Recruitment) cancelled the candidature of the respondents on the ground that respondents depicted their violent nature and that they have no respect for the law of the land - Respondents challenged said order before Tribunal who set aside said order of cancelling the candidature of the respondents - HC dismissed writ petition filed against said order holding that since respondents have been acquitted of the offences for which they have faced trial, the same cannot be held them - Hence, instant appeals - Whether the candidature of the respondents who have made a clean breast of their involvement in a criminal case by mentioning this fact in their application/attestation form while applying for a post of constable in Delhi Police; who were provisionally selected subject to verification of their antecedents and who were subsequently acquitted/discharged in the criminal case, could be cancelled by the Screening Committee of the Delhi Police on the ground that they are not found suitable for appointment to the post of constable - Held, in order to maintain integrity and high standard of police force, the Screening Committee may decline to take cognizance of a compromise, if it appears to it to be dubious - Screening Committee cannot be faulted for that - Respondents were trying to draw mileage from the fact that in their application and/or attestation form they have disclosed their involvement in a criminal case - Disclosure of these facts in the application/attestation form was an essential requirement - An aspirant was expected to state these facts honestly - Honesty and integrity were inbuilt requirements of the police force - Respondents should not, therefore, expect to score any brownie points because of this disclosure - Opinion formed by the Screening Committee in both these cases which was endorsed by the Deputy Commissioner of Police (Recruitment), Delhi, that both the respondents were not suitable for being appointed in the Delhi Police Force does not merit any interference - Tribunal and HC erred in setting aside the order of cancellation of the respondents' candidature - Appeals allowed.
