Mahendra Nath Das v Union of India and others

Supreme Court of India

01 May 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 277; (2013) 6 SCC 253; 2013 ALL MR (Cri) 2210; 2013 (3) CTC 894; JT 2013 (6) SC 463; 2013 (2) RCR(Criminal) 933; 2013(6) SCALE 591; [2013] 5 S.C.R. 1053

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Delay in disposal of mercy petition, Commutation of death sentence into life

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 72, 161 - Murder - Death sentence - Delay in disposal of mercy petition - Commutation of death sentence into life - Legality - Appellant (accused) allegedly committed offence u/s. 302 of IPC - While appellant was on bail, appellant allegedly killed another person - Appellant was tried separately for both offences - Trial Court convicted appellant and sentenced him to undergo imprisonment for life and for later offence, Trial Court sentenced appellant to death on the premise that murder was most foul and gruesome - Aggrieved from judgments of Trial Court appellant filed appeals before HC - HC dismissed appeals and confirmed death sentence - Aggrieved appellant filed an appeal before SC, which was dismissed - Thereafter, appellant filed a petition u/art. 72 of Constitution before President, which was accepted by an order dt.30-9-2005 and similar petition u/art. 161 of Constitution before Governor of State - However, recommendations made by Home Minister on 18-10-2010 were approved by President on 8-5-2011and thereafter, appellant was informed about rejection of his petition - Aggrieved appellant filed petition questioning rejection of his mercy petition, which was dismissed by DB - Hence, instant appeal - Whether 12 years delay in disposal of petition filed by appellant u/art. 72 of Constitution was sufficient for commutation of sentence of death into life imprisonment and DB committed an error by dismissing petition filed by appellant - Held, there was a long time gap of 12 years between submission of petition u/art. 72 of Constitution and rejection thereof - UOI tried to explain that time gap by citing correspondence between Central Govt. and State Govt., consideration of the matter in different levels in Ministry of Home Affairs - However, no explanation was given for time gap of 3 years between 20-6-2001, i.e., the date on which the then Home Minister made recommendation for rejection of mercy petition filed by appellant, and September, 2004, when the file again started moving within Ministry and 5 years between 30-9-2005, i.e., the date on which President opined that mercy petition of appellant be accepted and September, 2010, when the file was actually summoned back by Ministry of Home Affairs - In summary prepared by Home Ministry for President's consideration, which was signed by Home Minister on 18-10-2010, also no reference was made to order and note dt.30-9-2005 of the then President - Why that was done was not explained by respondents - Though, file containing petition filed by appellant and various notings recorded therein should be placed before President, omission to make a mention of the order passed by President's predecessor and said note from the summary prepared for consideration lead to an inference that President was kept in dark about the view expressed by predecessor and was deprived of an opportunity to objectively consider the entire matter - It was neither pleaded case of respondents nor any material was produced before SC to show that GOI had placed the file before the then President for review of order recorded by him on 30-9-2005 or the President who finally decided appellant's petition on 8-5-2011 was requested to reconsider the decision of predecessor - Thus, President was not properly advised and assisted in the disposal of petition filed by appellant - 12 years delay in disposal of appellant's mercy petition was sufficient for commutation of sentence of death and DB committed serious error by dismissing petition solely on the ground that appellant was found guilty of committing heinous crime - Rejection of appellant's mercy petition was illegal and quashed and impugned sentence of death awarded by Trial Court, which was confirmed by HC and SC was commuted into life imprisonment - Appeal allowed.
