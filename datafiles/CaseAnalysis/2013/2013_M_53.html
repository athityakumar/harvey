Mahinder Kumar and others v High Court of Madhya Pradesh Through Registrar General and others

Supreme Court of India

12 July 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Vikramajit Sen, Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 885; (2013) 11 SCC 87; JT 2013 (10) SC 194

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Constitution

Keywords: Kerala Judicial Service Rules, 1991, Madhya Pradesh Land Revenue Code, 1959

Summary: Altamas Kabir, J.

(A)Advocates & Judges - Constitution - Madhya Pradesh Higher Judicial Service (Recruitment & Conditions of Service) Rules, 1994,r.7 - Procedure for selection - Validity of - Jurisdiction - Advertisement inviting applications for filling up 20 posts in HC Madhya Pradesh Higher Judicial Service by direct recruitment from Bar was issued - Thereafter, HC made selection of in cadre of State Higher Judicial Service to post of entry level District Judge in selection process for year 2006-07 in which respondent no.3 alone came to be selected for 20 posts advertised - Petitioners and respondent nos.4 to 8 were all unsuccessful candidates who participated in said selection - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether HC was empowered to formulate its own procedure in matter of selection for post of State Higher Judicial Service and whether procedure evolved by HC, can be said to be in consonance with said power contained in r.7 of the Rules.

Held, nowhere in advertisement inviting applications for filling up 20 posts in State Higher Judicial Service by direct recruitment from Bar, has it been indicated as to what would be minimum percentage of marks, which would be required for final selection. All candidates who had obtained more than 40% marks in either of two papers were called for interview and were allotted separate marks in interview. Thereafter, on aggregate of marks obtained by candidates, only one person, placed at Serial no.1 of merit list, was found suitable to be recommended for appointment as District Judge on probation. However, problem was caused by note written by Registrar General of HC indicated at bottom of final Select List, wherein it was mentioned that one person, who had obtained more than 50% marks, was found suitable to be recommended for appointment. Said note is merely statement which indicates that selected candidate, had obtained more than 50% marks. Thus, same does not either indicate or pre-suppose that there was lower bench-mark for Petitioners to claim in Ground 'D' that bench-mark had been increased to 50%. Appeals dismissed.

Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla, J.

(B)Advocates & Judges - Constitution - Constitution of India,1950, art.233(1) - Madhya Pradesh Higher Judicial Service (Recruitment & Conditions of Service) Rules, 1994, r.7 - Procedure for appointment - Jurisdiction - Whether HC was empowered to formulate its own procedure in matter of selection for the post of State Higher Judicial Service and procedure evolved by HC, can be said to be in consonance with power contained in r.7 of the Rules.

Held, art.233(1) of the Constitution specifically states that appointment of persons to be made, as well as posting and promotion of District Judges in any state, can be made by Governor of state in consultation with HC exercising jurisdiction in relation to such a state. When such being the Constitutional mandate, which empowers respective HC to deal with the selection of District Judges, within its jurisdiction and the ultimate appointment to be made by the concerned Governor of such State, reading of art. 233(1) of the Constitution along with r.7 of the 1994 Rules, as amended in the year 2005, it will have to be held that respondent no.1-HC is invested with every power and authority to evolve appropriate procedure in matter of selection for direct recruitment and promotion of Entry Level District Judges and that depending upon the exigencies, such procedures can also be specified by the 1st respondent from time to time. Where nearly 3000 candidates appeared for written examination and the answer papers were evaluated by several District Judges, it cannot be held that there was every scope for variation in the assessment of the answers and the award of marks valued by different valuers. Thus, by virtue of r.7 of the Rules and there was enough prescription empowering HC to follow its own procedure in evaluating answer sheets initially by District Judges and subsequently by common evaluators, before holding interview. Thus, HC was empowered to formulate its own procedure. Appeals dismissed.

(C)Advocates & Judges - Constitution - Validity of - Whether procedure evolved by HC can be held to be in any manner vitiated or not in consonance with any Constitutional or statutory provisions.

Held, after advertisement was issued and after exam was conducted, HC constituted Selection Committee, consisting of 6 Judges of HC, with whom process of direct recruitment to post of Addl. District Judges, through competitive examination was entrusted. Said Selection Committee held its meeting, Committee passed necessary resolutions. Subsequently, Interview Committee of Seven Senior Judges interviewed short listed 15 candidates. Thereafter, Selection Committee again met and finalized selection in which, it resolved to recommend respondent no.3 for appointment to post of District Judge in Higher Judicial Service by direct recruitment. Based on resolution of Selection Committee, Registrar General forwarded recommendation of HC to Govt. of Madhya Pradesh for issuance of necessary appointment order in favour of respondent no.3. Any criteria of fixing 50% of aggregate marks as benchmark for making the selection, was neither resolved in any of the resolutions of Selection Committee or any other proceedings of HC, nor was there anything stated in so many words in the counter affidavit of the first respondent HC. Therefore, based on the said note, there is no scope to find fault with ultimate selection and appointment made by respondent no.1-HC. Appeals dismissed.

(D)Advocates & Judges - Constitution - Appointment - Method of selection - Validity of - Whether selection and appointment of respondent no.3 alone, as recommended by Selection Committee in its resolution, leaving out rest of 14 candidates as not suitable can be held to be justified.

Held, perusal of marks awarded by each of Seven members of Interview Committee disclose their identity of mind while awarding marks in interview to each of candidate interviewed by them. Interview committee's exercise, while interviewing 15 candidates can thus be held by board. Selection Committee's ultimate decision in their resolution in holding that in their fair assessment, respondent no.3 alone, who secured 55.46% marks in aggregate, as only suitable candidate for holding the post of entry level District Judge, cannot be assailed. No such minimum marks in interview, was prescribed for purpose of selection. None of aggrieved candidates have made any allegation of mala fides or lack of bona fides, as against any of the Selection Committee members or for that matter in manner in which the interview was held by the interviewing committee or with regard to the valuation of marks arrived at either by District Judges or in normalization of marks ultimately arrived at by the common evaluators. Therefore, in absence of any other attack to selection process made by HC by constituting Selection Committee and procedure followed by the said Selection Committee, which ultimately arrived at merit list of 15 shortlisted candidates for finalizing selection. Thus, no flaw can be found in said process adopted by HC. Appeals dismissed.
