Mahalaxmi Co-Operative Housing Society Ltd. & Etc. .. v Ashabhai Atmaram Patel (D) Th.Lrs And Others

Supreme Court of India

01 March 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 120; (2013) 4 SCC 404; AIR 2013 SC 961; 2013 (3) ALD(SC) 83; 2013 (99) ALR 18; 2013 (1) CLT(SC) 284; 2013 (3) G.L.R. 2149; JT 2013 (3) SC 530; 2013 (4) MahLJ 546; 2013 (3) RCR(Civil) 435; 2013 (120) RD 648; [2013] 6 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 23 r. 1, O. 23 r. 3, s. 24, - Possession - Declaration - Accepting pursis - Sustainability - Respondent nos. 1 to 5/purchasers filed suit for declaration restraining appellant/Society and respondent no. 6 from dealing with lands and for other consequential reliefs which was allowed by Trial Court - On appeal, Single Judge quashed orders and remanded matter to Court Trial Court - Hence, instant appeals -Whether impugned order would fall u/r. 3 of O. 23 r. 1 CPC - Held, pursis falls under O. 23 r. 3 since defendant had satisfied plaintiffs in respect of whole of subject-matter of suit - Cogent reasons were stated by Court while rejecting their objections and accepting pursis - However, transfer of suits from one court to another to be tried together would not take away right of parties to invoke O. 23 r. 3 and there was no prohibition under O. 23 r. 3 or s. 24 CPC to record a compromise in one suit - Suits always retain their independent identity and even after an order of consolidation, Court was not powerless to dispose of any suit independently once ingredients of O. 23, r. 3 had been satisfied - Therefore, in instant case there was no illegality in orders passed by Trial Court disposing of suit under O. 23, r. 3 CPC accepting pursis - Thus, HC was not right in upsetting orders of Trial Court and was set aside - Appeals allowed.
