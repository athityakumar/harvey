Chenda @ Chanda Ram v State of Chhatisgarh

Supreme Court of India

27 August 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Kurian Joseph, Chandramauli Kumar Prasad

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 547; (2013) 12 SCC 110; AIR 2013 SC (Supp) 156; 2014 CRLJ 172; 2013 (4) JCC 2703; JT 2013 (12) SC 28; 2013(10) SCALE 637

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 300 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant accused allegedly murdered deceased - Appellant was convicted and sentenced to life imprisonment u/s. 302 IPC by Trial Court - On appeal, HC concurred with findings of Trial Court - Hence, instant appeal - Whether conviction for murder by Trial Court in the instant case was sustainable - Held, there was no evidence that there was previous enmity between parties - Available evidence showed that there was no premeditation on the part of appellant and that it was a case of sudden fight - Scuffling parties being in motion, it could easily have happened that blow fell on the head of deceased unintentionally - There was no cruelty involved by inflicting any other injury - No conduct on the part of appellant so as to hold that he was taking any undue advantage of situation or that he behaved in a cruel or unusual manner - Thus, all four ingredients required for treating case under Exception 4 to s. 300 of IPC were satisfied in the instant case - Further, there was no criminal background or adverse history of appellant - Hence. it was a fit case to alter punishment of imprisonment for life to imprisonment for a period of 10 years with fine - Appeal partly allowed.
