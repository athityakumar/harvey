H.P. Scheduled Tribes Employees Federation and another v Himachal Pradesh S.V.K.K. and others

Supreme Court of India

13 September 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Surinder Singh Nijjar, Pinaki Chandra Ghose

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 602; (2013) 10 SCC 308; AIR 2013 SC (Supp) 70; 2013(11) SCALE 455; [2013] 9 S.C.R. 384; 2014 (2) SLR 3

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Scheduled Castes, Constitution (One Hundred Seventeenth Amendment) Bill, 2012 (48 of 2012) - Amendment of Article 16

Summary: Service - Promotion - Reservation - Amendment to impediment free reservation - Consequential seniority - Govt. of India issued instructions vide letter whereby provision was made for providing reservation in promotion for members of Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes - Respondent issued instructions vide Letter whereby reservation was provided for promotion of employees - Instructions were challenged by respondent No.1 by filing Original Application before Administrative Tribunal - Since Administrative Tribunal was thereafter abolished, O.A. was transferred to be heard and adjudicated by HC and was renumbered as Civil writ Petition - HC allowed writ Petition and quashed instructions - Judgment of HC was challenged in Civil Appeal @ SLP (Civil) filed by petitioner - Court disposed of S.L.P. (Civil) - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether amendment was with view to provide impediment free reservation in promotion to Scheduled-Castes and Scheduled-Tribes and to bring certainty and clarity in matter - Held, State had to await outcome of 117th Amendment as there was no relevance of amendment to implementation by State of its earlier decision making reservation in promotion - Therefore, statement was only to avoid decision on merits with regard to correctness of impugned judgment of HC - Having very deftly avoided decision on merits in SLP, State had totally failed to live up to solemn statement made to Court - However, it was hedged and hemmed and prevaricated, inspite of requisite data being available, policy of reservation already adopted by State had not been implemented - Therefore, applicants want State to implement its own decisions - Court was more than considerate to requests made by State for extension of time - Though, awaiting finalization of proposed Constitutional Amendment was proverbial last straw on camel's back and proposed Amendment would not adversely affect merits of clam of petitioner for grant of promotion with consequential seniority - Thus, amendment was with view to provide impediment free reservation in promotion to Scheduled-Castes and Scheduled-Tribes and to bring certainty and clarity in matter - Furthermore, proposed amendment was to be introduced with retrospective effect - Hence, there could be no impediment in way of State Govt. to implement policy of reservation which existed till issuance of various instructions prior to making of Statement before Court - Application allowed.
