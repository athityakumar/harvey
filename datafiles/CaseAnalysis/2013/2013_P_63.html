Putchalapalli Naresh Reddy v State of Andhra Pradesh and etc.

Supreme Court of India

18 October 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Sharad Arvind Bobde, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 685; (2014) 12 SCC 457; 2013 ALL MR (Cri) 4117; 2014 CRLJ 743; 2014 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 2137; 2014 (1) RCR(Criminal) 613; 2013(12) SCALE 691

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Attempt to murder

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 147, 148, 149, 302, 307, 326 - Murder - Attempt to Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant-A1 to A20 were charged for offences punishable u/ss. 147, 148, 302, 307, 326 IPC - A2, A3 and A12 were charged for offences u/s. 307 r/w s. 149 IPC and A1, A4 to A11 and A13 to A20 IPC were charged for offences punishable 326 r/w s. 149 IPC - Trial Court acquitted accused - Revision appeal before HC - HC found A1 to A12 guilty for offences punishable u/ss. 147, 148, 302 r/w s. 149, 324 r/w s. 34, 147, 147 IPC and A13 to A20 were acquitted for all the offences they were charged - HC partly allowed appeals and set aside judgment of Trial Court - Hence instant appeals by A1 to A12 - Whether conviction and sentence order recorded by of HC was sustainable - Held, deposition of PW6, who accompanied deceased fully corroborated version of PW3 who stated that PW3 and LW2 came to house of deceased and requested him to mediate dispute among brothers - Deceased promised to come on next day - On next day what transpired was narrated by him as above - There was no substance in contention that this witness supported prosecution side in some earlier litigation therefore, his testimony was liable to be discarded - PW7 was also present when deceased was requested to mediate dispute on earlier date and accompany him to field on next day, his narration of incident was similar to deposition of other witnesses and have been believed by HC - Prosecution case was fully supported by rest of witnesses and evidence - Prosecution had sufficiently established motive, which was political rivalry and land dispute between parties their preparation which consisted accused party going to field of PW3 and LW2 with arms and recovery of these arms at instance of A2, discovery of blood stains on knife and battle-axe and ante mortem injuries inflicted on deceased, as per inquest and post mortem report - Injuries were caused to deceased by accused - Injuries on deceased were completely relatable to injuries to attack as deposed to by prosecution witnesses - Alleged discrepancies and discrepancies and doubts were made their testimonies liable to be rejected, in view of deposition of PW3, 6 and 7, which was clear and consistent - HC was fully justified in reversing order of acquittal in respect of A1 to A12 - Witnesses stated that deceased received several injuries on his head, however, evidence of Medical Officer showed that X-ray of skull was taken and there was one simple injury on his head on date of offence - Witness should have been examined but for reasons best known to prosecution was not examined - In circumstances, conviction and sentence of A2, awarded by Trial Court and affirmed by HC was liable to be modified to s. 324 r/w s. 34 IPC - Appeals disposed of.
