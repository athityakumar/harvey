Orissa Power Transmission Corporation Limited and others v Asian School of Business Management Trust and others

Supreme Court of India

05 August 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, V. Gopala Gowda

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 525; (2013) 8 SCC 738; AIR 2013 SC (Supp) 48; JT 2013 (11) SC 25; 2013 (4) RCR(Civil) 61; 2013(10) SCALE 96

Case Digest

Subject: Electricity & Energy

Summary: Electricity & Energy - Electricity (Supply) Act, 1948, ss. 28 and 29 - Validity of scheme - Orissa State Electricity Board (Board) (predecessor of appellant No.1) framed 50 transmission schemes, which were notified on 30-5-1991 to enable the licensees and other interested persons to make representations - None of the licensees/other interested persons made representation against any of the schemes - Board executed the same without any objection - After 14 years and 6 months of publication of the schemes, Human Resources Development and Management Trust of India (Trust) (predecessor of respondent No.1), raised objection against execution of the scheme mentioned at item No.2 of notification dt. 30-5-1991 on the ground that two of the transmission towers proposed to be erected would cross the building constructed by it - Trust also requested appellant No.1 to shift the transmission towers and line to an alternative site - Appellant No.1 and its officers did not accept the objection/representation of the Trust - Suit was filed - Trial Court held that it was not possible for the appellant to erect any tower leaving the suit land and thereby compromise the public interest - Writ petition filed thereagainst was dismissed - Subsequently, another writ petition was filed wherein Single Judge held that the prayer made in the second writ petition did not merit consideration - On appeal, DB of the HC held that notifications dt. 30-5-1991 and 30-1-1996 were ultra vires the provisions of ss. 28 and 29 of the Act - Hence, instant appeals - Whether judgment passed by DB of HC was justified - Held, Board or the Generating Company could prepare one or more schemes for efficient performance of its duties under the Act - In the instant case, by notification dt. 30-5-1991, Board had notified almost 50 schemes and invited representations from interested persons - However, landowner(s) from whom the Trust purchased the land neither made any representation nor filed objection against the scheme - Therefore, neither the Trust nor respondent No.1, who constructed buildings knowing fully well that the Board had already framed scheme for the area in which its land was situated could object to erection of the remaining two towers or seek shifting of the scheme - Officers of appellant No.1 did not commit any illegality by refusing to entertain the request of respondent No.1 for shifting of the transmission towers by diverting 220 KV line from the existing alignment - DB of HC erred by ordaining appellant No.1 to shift the transmission towers from their present site - Finding recorded by the DB of HC that the scheme was not in consonance with ss. 28 and 29 of the Act was also erroneous - Impugned judgment was set aside - Appeal allowed.
