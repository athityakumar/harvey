Joginder Singh v State of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

24 October 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 703; 2013 ALL MR (Cri) 4104; 2014 (1) JCC 113; 2014 (1) RCR(Criminal) 248; 2013(13) SCALE 247; [2013] 11 S.C.R. 446

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302 and 307 - Arms Act, 1959, ss. 25 and 27 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant allegedly committed offence of murder and illegal possession of arms - Appellant was acquitted by Trial Court of the charges framed u/ss. 302 and 307 IPC and u/ss. 25 and 27 of Act - However, HC reversed the decision of Trial Court - Hence instant appeal - Whether conviction of appellant by HC was sustainable - Held, ballistic report, though referred to the mutilated pellets stated to have been recovered from the body of the deceased and also the two different leads pellets from the body of wife of PW3, but was not definite that .12 bore DBBL gun, that was seized from the appellant, was used for firing such gunshots - That fact was totally ignored by the HC in an extremely cryptic manner - Prosecution did not examine Chander, husband of the deceased, a relevant eye witness, three other injured witnesses - No explanation has been given by the prosecution - In the case at hand, non-examination of the material witnesses was of significance - It was so because PW11 was really an interested witness though the HC did not agree with the same - It appeared from the material brought on record that he had an axe to grind against the appellant - That apart, husband of deceased, who was present from the beginning, would have been in a position to disclose more clearly about the genesis of the occurrence - SC found no reason why the prosecution had withheld the said witness - Similarly, the other three witnesses who were said to be injured witnesses when available should have come and deposed - Therefore, in the obtaining factual matrix that their non-examination gains significance - In that regard, another aspect required to be taken note of - Case of the prosecution was that brother of appellant had snatched away the gun and fired at complainant and one injured person - Trial Court disbelieving the prosecution version had acquitted him - HC had given brother of appellant benefit of doubt - SC was of opinion that regard being had to the totality of evidence, both oral and documentary, there was no reason to extend the said benefit of doubt to the appellant - HC had fallen into error on that score - Appeal allowed.
