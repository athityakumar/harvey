Rajesh Kumar and others etc and another v State Of Bihar & Ors. Etc.

Supreme Court of India

13 March 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, Gyan Sudha Misra

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 138; (2013) 4 SCC 690; AIR 2013 SC 2652; 2013 (4) AWC 3772; 2013 (137) FLR 507; JT 2013 (4) SC 1; 2013 (3) MLJ 635; 2013(3) SCALE 393; [2013] 4 S.C.R. 753; 2013 (3) SLR 318

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Wrong answer key - Selection - Validity - State Staff Selection Commission invited applications from eligible candidate by advertisement dt.14-8-2006 for appointment against 2268 posts of Junior Engineer (Civil) out of which 1057 posts were in the open merit category - Respondent Nos. 6 to 8 were unsuccessful candidates assailed evaluation of answer scripts in instant appeals - Writ petition was still pending, 35 candidates were appointed as Junior Engineers - Single Judge of HC referred the 'Model Answer Key' to experts - Based on the report of said 2experts, Single Judge held that 41 model answers out of 100 were wrong and entire examination was liable to be cancelled and so also appointments made on basis thereof - Aggrieved by said order, appellants filed appeal before DB - DB modified order passed by Single Judge and declared that entire examination need not be cancelled as there was no allegation of any corrupt motive or malpractice in regard to other question papers -According to DB, a fresh examination in Civil Engineering Paper only was sufficient to rectify the defect and prevent injustice to any candidate - Appellants who happen to be beneficiaries of erroneous evaluation of answer scripts had assailed order passed by HC - Hence instant appeals - Held, if the key which was used for evaluating answer sheets was itself defective, result prepared on the basis of same could be no different - DB was, therefore, perfectly justified in holding that result of examination in so far as same pertained to 'A' series question paper was vitiated - This was bound to affect the result of entire examination qua every candidate whether or not he was a party to the proceedings - It also goes without saying that if the result was vitiated by application of a wrong key, any appointment made on the basis thereof would also be rendered unsustainable - HC was, in that view, entitled to mould the relief prayed for in writ petition and issue directions considered necessary not only to maintain purity of selection process but also to ensure that no candidate earned an undeserved advantage over others by application of an erroneous key - Further, appellants were innocent parties who had not, in any manner, contributed to the preparation of erroneous key or distorted result - There was no mention of any fraud or malpractice against appellants who had served State for nearly 7years now - In the circumstances, while inter-se merit position might be relevant for appellants, the ouster of latter need not be an inevitable and inexorable consequence of such a re-evaluation - Re-evaluation process might additionally benefit those who had lost the hope of appointment on basis of wrong key applied for evaluating the answer scripts - Such of those candidates as might be ultimately found to be entitled to issue of appointment letters on basis of their merit should benefit by such re-evaluation and should pick up their appointments on that basis according to their inter se position on the merit list - Order passed by HC was set aside - Appeals allowed.
