P. Sudhakar Rao and others v U. Govinda Rao and others

Supreme Court of India

03 July 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Madan B. Lokur, R.M. Lodha, Kurian Joseph

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 435; (2013) 8 SCC 693; AIR 2013 SC 2533; 2013 (6) ALD(SC) 15; 2013 (5) ALT(SC) 17; JT 2013 (9) SC 591; 2013(8) SCALE 504; 2013 (5) SLR 186

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Andhra Pradesh State and Subordinate Service Rules, Karnataka Public Works Engineering Department Service (Recruitment) (Amendment) Rules, 1985, Gujarat Engineering Service Rules, 1960, Andhra Pradesh Engineering (Research Laboratories) Service Rules, 2006

Summary: Service - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 16 - Recruitment - Retrospective seniority benefits - Govt. order - Validity - Aggrieved by the issuance of G.O.Ms No. 54 petitions were filed by aggrieved Junior Engineers in the Tribunal questioning its validity - Tribunal upheld the validity of the G.O.Ms. 54 - Aggrieved petitioners challenged the said order - HC held that impugned rule violate arts. 14 and 16 of the Constitution in so far as it takes away the vested right of seniority of Junior Engineers vis-à-vis Supervisors - Hence instant appeals challenging the validity of the rule by which retrospective seniority benefit was given to the Junior Engineers by G.O.Ms No. 54 - Whether G.O. Ms. 54 was violative of art. 14 of Constitution - Held, at least some of the Supervisors were given retrospective seniority on the date when they were not even eligible for appointment as Junior Engineers and it was impermissible - HC pointed out that there was no indication of the vacancy position, that was, whether the Supervisors could be adjusted in the grade of Junior Engineers from the date on which they were given notional retrospective seniority - There was also no indication whether the quota of vacancies for Supervisors was adhered to as on the date on which they were given notional retrospective seniority - Grant of retrospective seniority to Supervisors had adversely impacted on the promotion chances of Junior Engineers by bringing them down in seniority - That too was impermissible - To pass the scrutiny of art. 14 of Constitution, the seniority of Supervisors should be reckoned only from the date on which they satisfied all the real and objective procedural requirements of the Rules and the law laid down by SC - It was mentioned that by the time it came to be decided, impugned G.O.Ms No. 54 had already come into existence - Though it was brought to the notice of SC, its validity was neither examined nor determined - That was the first occasion when the Constitutional validity of the said G.O.Ms was considered - There was no occasion for interfering with the view taken by the HC to the effect that the grant of retrospective seniority to Supervisors on their appointment as Junior Engineers violate art. 14 of Constitution - Weightage of service given to the Supervisors could be taken advantage of only for the purpose of eligibility for promotion to the post of Assistant Engineer - Weightage could not be utilized for obtaining retrospective seniority over and above the existing Junior Engineers - Appeals dismissed.
