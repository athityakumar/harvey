Manga @ Man Singh v State of Uttarakhand

Supreme Court of India

03 May 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 286; (2013) 7 SCC 629; AIR 2013 SC (Supp) 829; 2013 CRLJ 3332; JT 2013 (7) SC 75; 2013 (3) RCR(Criminal) 16; 2013(6) SCALE 603; [2013] 3 S.C.R. 175

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Communal Tension

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 147, 148, 149, 302 and 307 - Murder - Attempt to murder - Unlawful assembly - Criminal trespass - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellants/accused armed with guns and pistols approached the house of complainant/PW2 - Appellants opened fire in which one of the family member of PW2 succumbed to injury on the spot and others were seriously injured - Charge sheet against appellants was filed u/ss. 147, 148, 149, 302 and 307 IPC - Trial Court convicted appellants - Aggrieved on appeal HC upheld the judgment of Trial Court - Hence instant appeals - (A) What interpretation to be placed on s. 141 'third' vis-a-vis s. 149 IPC - Held, conspectus reading of s. 40 made the position abundantly clear that for all offences punishable under IPC, the main clause of s. 40 IPC would straight away apply in which event the expression 'other offence' used in s. 141 'third', would have to be construed as any offence for which punishment was prescribed under the Code - Whomsoever was proceeded against for any offence punishable under the provisions of s. 40(1) would straight away apply for the purpose of construing what the offence was and when it comes to the question of offence under any other special or local law, the aid of cls. 2 and 3 would have to be applied for the purpose of construing the offence for which the accused was proceeded against - SC therefore, of the firm view that only such a construction would be in tune with the purport and intent of law makers while defining an unlawful assembly for commission of an offence with a common object, as specified u/s. 141 IPC - In instant case, since no special law or local law was attracted and the accuses were charged only for the offence under IPC, s. 40(1) gets attracted along with s. 141 'third' IPC - Having regard to such a construction of SC on s. 141, r/w s. 40 IPC, the offence found proved against appellants, namely, falling u/ss. 302 r/w s. 149, 307 r/w s. 149 along with ss. 147 and 148 of IPC for which the conviction and sentence imposed by the Court below could not be found fault with - (B) Whether the so-called delay in forwarding express report to the Magistrate after three days from the date of occurrence, on 24-11-2001 would vitiate the case of the prosecution - Held, there was no dearth in the process of investigation based on the factum of the alleged occurrence on 21-11-2001, as reported by PW2 and the mere delay in forwarding of the express report to the Magistrate had not caused any dent in the case of prosecution - SC had no difficulty in stating that FIR was factually recorded without delay and the investigation started on the basis of the FIR and in the absence of any other infirmity in that respect, the delay in forwarding the report to the Magistrate did not in any way vitiate the case of prosecution - (C) Whether the prevalence of communal riots at the time of occurrence merits acceptance in order to extricate appellants from the conviction imposed - Held, there was nothing brought out or placed either in the evidence of prosecution witness or by way of defence evidence before the court, as to what was the nature of communal tension, who were all communally and inimically disposed of and when such communal friction occurred - In fact, what all was stated in the s. 313 CrPC statement, was 'false implication' due to enmity and political reasons - Political difference and communal difference were two different factors and, therefore, it was not known why such a specific stand of communal tension was not taken in the s. 313 CrPC questioning - If really there was any communal tension in the village, there would have been any number of witnesses who would have come forward and stated the same before the Court, as none would have been prejudiced nor affected by making such a true statement before the Court - It was not even suggested to any of the witnesses that there was communal hatred as between those witnesses examined in support of the prosecution or that it was due to such communal tension they suffered such injuries, as well as casualties in their family - In fact, there were too many incongruities in the said submission, inasmuch as the said submission was made in desperation and did not deserve any consideration - Therefore, the said submission was rejected as meritless - (D) Whether there was any lacunae in the case of prosecution based on various points raised on behalf of appellants - Held, though PWs 1 to 4 were closely related to deceased, they also suffered fire-arm injuries at the hands of appellants and the injuries sustained by them were duly supported by medical evidence, both documentary as well as oral, through PWs 6 to 9 - There was nothing pointed out in the evidence of PWs 1 to 4, except stating that since because they were closely related, their version about the occurrence was not true in order to discredit their version - Even before the Courts below the only argument made was that the said witnesses were related to the deceased and that they falsely implicated appellants - In SCs considered opinion, merely based on such a flimsy submission as regards the credibility of those witnesses, evidence of those injured eye witnesses could not be discarded - Impugned judgment of conviction was upheld - Appeals dismissed.
