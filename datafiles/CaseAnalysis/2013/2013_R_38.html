Rukmini Amma and others v Rajeswary (Dead) through Lrs. and others

Supreme Court of India

22 March 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 461; (2013) 9 SCC 121; AIR 2013 SC 2428; 2013 (5) ALD(SC) 40; 2014(7) ALL MR 821; 2013 (98) ALR 728; 2013 (3) AWC 2584; 2013 (4) CLT(SC) 21; 2013 (2) DRTC 467; JT 2013 (8) SC 147; 2013 (3) KLT 119; 2014 (1) MahLJ 68; 2014 (1) MPLJ 30; 2013(5) SCALE 352; [2013] 5 S.C.R. 579

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Public Auction, Levy Of Tax, Redemption of mortgage, Recovery of arrears of income tax

Summary: Land & Property - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 76(c) - Revenue Recovery Act, 1890 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O.2 r. 2 - Public auction - Recovery of arrears of income tax - Redemption of mortgage - Levy of tax - Respondents were legal heirs and created mortgage related to plaint scheduled properties in favour of predecessor-in-interest of appellants - While mortgage was subsisting, for recovery of arrears of income tax payable by mortgagor, suit scheduled properties were attached under 1890 Act - Property was sold in public auction and son of mortgagee made highest bid in auction, pursuant to which he made payment and sale deed was executed in his favour - After lapse of more than 30 years, after mortgage, respondents filed suit before for redemption of mortgage and suit scheduled properties by directing defendants to put plaintiffs in possession on receiving mortgage - Trial Court held that suit was not hit by O. 2 r. 2 CPC - Appeal before Appellate Court, who allowed appeal and set aside judgment and decree of Trial Court and remanded back suit to Trial Court - Hence instant Appeal - Whether sale of property by way of public auction invoked provisions of 1890 Act for dues towards agricultural income tax payable by mortgagor -

Held, lands were in possession of mortgagee and was liable to pay Govt. dues when it came to question of payment of agricultural income tax - It could not be held that such liability would come within expression 'Govt. dues' in as much as tax liability was not qua land mortgaged but qua owner of land who was benefited by produce of lands which alone fell within definition of 'Agricultural Income' - After interpretation of statutory construction, some payments made towards either sales tax or agricultural income tax by mortgagee could not be held to had estopped appellants from raising plea purely based on legal and statutory construction - Appellate Court as well as HC failed to appreciate issue involved in proper perspective - However, there was no obligation on part of mortgagee to clear agricultural income tax liability of mortgagor u/s. 76(c) of 1882 Act - Though, such liabilities noted and contemplated to be cleared by mortgagee could not and would not include income tax liability of assessee which was purely personal and not of public nature - Thus, s.76(c) of 1882 Act could have limited application to specific Govt. dues of public nature as well as those which were referable to land and not to personal statutory dues of owner of land - Hence, sale effected to meet agricultural income tax liability of mortgagor had extinguished mortgagors right and suit was liable to be dismissed - Appeal allowed.
