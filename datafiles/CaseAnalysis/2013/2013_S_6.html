Bank of Baroda v S. K. Kool (Dead) Through Lrs. and another

Supreme Court of India

11 December 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Chandramauli Kumar Prasad, J. S. KHEHAR

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 820; (2014) 2 SCC 715; AIR 2014 SC 915; 2014 (2) AWC 1581; 2014 (1) DRTC 457; 2014 (140) FLR 910; 2014 (1) LLJ 373; 2013(15) SCALE 197; [2013] 12 S.C.R. 783; 2014 (1) SLR 771

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: the Regulation, Bank of Baroda (Employees) Pension Regulation, 1995

Summary: (1) Bank of Baroda (Employees) Pension Regulations, 1995-Regulation 22 (1)-Employment-Removal-Superannuation benefits-Respondent-employee (since deceased), clerk in Bank of Baroda-Respondent-employee visited with penalty of removal from service with superannuation benefits in terms of Bipartite Settlement-Regulation does not entitle every employee to pensionary benefits-Bipartite Settlement tends to provide punishment which gives superannuation benefits otherwise due-Employees who are otherwise eligible for superannuation benefits are removed from service in terms of Bipartite Settlement-Shall be entitled to superannuation benefits-Accordingly, respondent-employee's heirs entitled to superannuation benefits.(2) Interpretation of Statute-In case of apparent conflict between two provisions, they should be so interpreted that effect is given to both.
