Dayanand Anglo Vedic (Dav) College Trust And Management Society v State Of Maharashtra & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

22 March 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

M.Y. Eqbal, Surinder Singh Nijjar

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 173; (2013) 4 SCC 14; AIR 2013 SC 1420; 2013 (3) AWC 2608; 2013 (2) CLT(SC) 103; JT 2013 (8) SC 102; 2013(4) SCALE 624; [2013] 4 S.C.R. 821; 2013 (4) SLR 383

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Education; Practice & Procedure; Trusts & Associations

Keywords: Withdrawal, Entitlement of, Linguistic minority status, Claim

Summary: Trusts & Associations - Education - Administrative - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 30 - Societies Registration Act, 1860 - Bombay Public Trusts Act, 1950 - Linguistic minority status - Withdrawal - Claim - Entitlement of - Appellant (society) registered under 1860 and 1950 Acts, had established large number of schools and colleges and was running such institution all over India - Aim and object of appellant was to encourage study of languages - Appellant claimed to be a linguistic minority within meaning and purview of art. 30 of Constitution - Respondents issued a certificate on 11-7-2008 recognizing appellant at 'S' as a linguistic minority institution for academic year 2008-09 - However, respondent no. 2 by an order withdrawn linguistic minority status of appellant - Aggrieved appellant filed a petition before HC - DB by impugned judgment refused to interfere with order passed by respondent no. 2 - Hence, instant appeal - Appellant contended that order of withdrawal was erroneous and contrary to provisions of Govt. Resolution dt.4-7-2008 which prescribed procedure for granting a minority status and recognition certificate - Whether a member of a linguistic non-minority in one State could establish a Trust or Society in another State and claim minority status in that State - Held, Govt. Resolution dt.4-7-2008 prescribed a procedure for granting minority status - Resolution, permits persons of State of Maharashtra whose mother tongue was other Indian language than Marathi would be eligible to submit an application for recognition of their linguistic minority educational institution - The only rider put was that minimum 2/3rd trustees of Management Committee of Society/Institution should be from the concerned minority community - Rights conferred by art. 30 of Constitution to the minority were in 2 parts - 1st part was right to establish institution of minority's choice and 2nd related to right to administration of such institution - In order to claim minority/linguistic status for an institution in any State, authorities should be satisfied firstly that institution was established by persons who were minority in such State; and secondly, right of administration of said minority linguistic institution was also vested in those persons who were minority in such State - Right conferred by art. 30 of Constitution could not be interpreted as if irrespective of persons who established the institution in State for the benefit of persons who were minority, any person, be it non-minority in other place, could administer and run such institution - Admittedly, in instant case, appellant was registered at New Delhi and majority of trustees reside at New Delhi and, thus, those persons could not be treated as minority in State of Maharashtra and they could not claim protection of linguistic minority in State of Maharashtra - It was clear that appellant had not correctly furnished required information, inasmuch as it was not said that Trustees/Members of Board of Directors, who were looking after business of society, were non-minority - Obviously, the reason was that persons or trustees, who were managing the business of society were non-minority i.e. residing in New Delhi and not in State of Maharashtra - Thus, there was no doubt that view took by HC was justified - Appeal dismissed.
