Shanmugam and another v State Represented by Inspector of Police, Tamil Nadu

Supreme Court of India

11 September 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, Vikramajit Sen

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 575; (2013) 12 SCC 765; AIR 2013 SC (Supp) 258; 2013 CRLJ 4522; JT 2013 (12) SC 368; 2013(11) SCALE 304; [2013] 10 S.C.R. 99

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 304II, 34 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellants-accused no.1 to accused no.4 allegedly committed murder on deceased due to strained relations between accused and deceased , arose rivalry in smuggling of sandalwood - Accused were charged for offence punishable u/ss. 302, 34 IPC - Trial convicted and sentenced accused - Appeal before HC, which was dismissed and HC upheld conviction and sentences by Trial Court - Hence instant appeal - Whether conviction and sentence recorded by HC was sustainable - Held, deposition of PW1 had been found to be reliable by Trial Court and HC, no matter he was related closely to deceased - There was nothing in cross- examination of witness that could be said to have adversely affected credibility of PW1 witness nor was there anything to suggest that apart from his being relative of deceased he had any other reason to falsely implicate accused persons or any one of them - Version given by witness as to manner in which deceased was done to death by accused persons got support from medical evidence led in case - Doctor conducted post-mortem examination found death to be homicidal - It was noteworthy that other two witnesses PW4 and PW5 supported prosecution case, no matter only in part - Fact that deceased was present at cremation ground where occurrence took place was proved from their depositions - It was equally important to note that one of accused had, according to these 2 witnesses picked up stick and assaulted deceased on his head as result of which deceased had collapsed to ground - Rest of prosecution case, on role played by other accused persons in killing of deceased, had not been supported by these 2 witnesses who were declared hostile and cross-examined by prosecution - Hence, there was no room for altering conviction from s. 302 IPC to s. 304II IPC - Appeal dismissed.
