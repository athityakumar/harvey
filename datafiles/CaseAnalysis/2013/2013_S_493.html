Habib and another v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

01 May 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Radhakrishnan, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 276; (2013) 12 SCC 568; AIR 2013 SC 1764; 2013 (3) CGLJ 59; 2013 (2) MLJ(Crl) 863; 2013(6) SCALE 551; [2013] 5 S.C.R. 476

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 307, 302 r/w s. 34 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellants (accused) and other co-accused persons allegedly committed offence u/s. 307 and 302 r/w s. 34 of IPC - Trial Court by its judgment acquitted all accused persons - Aggrieved Respondent (State) filed an appeal before HC - HC by impugned judgment revered order of acquittal of appellants and appellant no. 1 convicted u/s. 302 of IPC and appellant no. 2 was convicted u/s. 302 r/w s. 34 of IPC - Hence, instant appeals - Whether impugned judgment passed by HC convicting appellants was justified - Held, it was settled legal position that if there was direct trustworthy evidence of witnesses as to the commission of offence, motive part loses its significance - Thus, if the genesis of the occurrence was proved, ocular testimony of witnesses could not be discarded only by the reason of absence of motive, if otherwise the evidence was worthy of reliance - In instant case, PW1 and 2 were son and brother of deceased, that itself was not a ground to disbelieve their evidence - Both PW1 and 2 categorically stated that first shot was fired by appellant no. 2 but missed his aim and it was appellant no. 1 who fired the fateful shot at the neck of the deceased and thereafter 3 culprits ran away from the spot - Prosecution placed reliance on testimony of PW3, who was a co-villager of informant and PW3 fully corroborated the testimony of other witnesses regarding part played by 3 accused persons in commission of crime - Nothing was brought out in corss-examination of PW1, 2 and 3 to discredit their statement - Mere fact that PW1 and 2 were interested witnesses being relatives was not a reason to discard their evidence, if the evidence was trustworthy - HC correctly appreciated oral and documentary evidence, including evidence of PW6 (Chief Medical Officer) and rightly concluded that Trial Court had committed an error in discarding their evidence - Appeals dismissed.
