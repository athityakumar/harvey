Selvi J. Jayalalithaa and others v State of Karnataka and others

Supreme Court of India

30 September 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, Sharad Arvind Bobde

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 646; (2014) 2 SCC 401; 2014 CRLJ 9; 2013 (4) MLJ(Crl) 139; 2013(12) SCALE 234

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Karnataka Judicial Service (Recruitment) Rules 2004

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Practice & Procedure - Criminal - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 21 and 235 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988 - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Prosecution was launched against the petitioners for having assets disproportionate to their known income in the year 1996-1997 in the State of Tamil Nadu - Respondent no- 5 was a political rival of the petitioner no-1 - Petitioners approached SC on 18-11-2003 for transferring the petitioners' trial to the neighbouring State of Karnataka in the interest of justice, on the ground that a fair trial was not possible in the State of Tamil Nadu - On 19-2-2005, the Govt. of Karnataka, after consultation with the Chief Justice of the HC, appointed, a former Advocate General, as Special Public Prosecutor (SPP) to conduct the prosecution - After his resignation, Govt. of Karnataka n initiated the process for appointment of a new SPP - Govt. of Karnataka issued a Notification appointing respondent no. 4 as SPP - After issuance of the notification dt. 2-2-2013, respondent no. 4 started working and 99 defence witnesses were examined and 384 defence exhibits were marked between 28-2-2013 and 29-7-2013 - On 26-8-2013, the Govt. of Karnataka issued a Notification withdrawing the appointment of respondent no-4 as SPP without assigning any reason and without consulting the Chief Justice of Karnataka HC - Subsequently, State Govt. withdrew the Notification dt. 26-8-2013 and simultaneously, vide letter of the same date, asked respondent no-4 not to appear in the matter before the Special Judge - Subsequently, State Govt. removed respondent no. 4 - Hence, instant petition - Whether directions issued by State Govt. was correct - Held, State Govt. was competent to appoint the Special Judge on contractual basis after his retirement for the period required to conclude the present trial, though with the consultation of the HC as required u/art. 235 of the Constitution - Further, such a course to be adopted in the manner prescribed and in view thereof, the matter required to be considered by the State Govt. with the consultation of the HC - In the instant case, order of removal of respondent no-4 was a product of mala fides and the impugned order was not sustainable in the eyes of law as such the same was quashed - Matter referred to the HC of Karnataka to decide on the administrative side as to whether, in order to conclude the trial expeditiously as guaranteed u/art. 21 of the Constitution required the extension of the services of the Special Judge - Petitions disposed of.
