Brihanmumbai Mahanagarpalika and another v Willingdon Sports Club and others

Supreme Court of India

18 November 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, V. Gopala Gowda

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 753; (2013) 16 SCC 260; AIR 2014 SC 505; 2014(1) ALL MR 400; 2014 (1) Bom.C.R. 571; 2013(14) SCALE 112

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Mumbai Municipal Corporation Act, 1888, NOC for eating house, Demand notice for payment

Summary: Municipalities & Local Governments - Administrative - Bombay Municipal Boroughs Act, 1925, ss. 172, 61(1)(b)(ii) - Food Safety and Standards Act, 2006, s. 3(1)(n) - Mumbai Municipal Corporation Act, 1888, ss. 3(ff), 394, 394(1)(e), 394(1)(e)(i), 471 - Retail Sales Tax Act - NOC for eating house - Demand notice for payment - Legality - Respondent no. 1 approached appellant no. 2 for grant of No Objection Certificate for eating house and permission to keep L.P. Gas Cylinders and same was allowed - Later appellant no. 2 sent demand notice to respondent no. 2 towards compounding fee, which respondent no. 2 did not accept - Respondent no. 1 and 2 challenged said demand notice before DB - DB held that respondent no. 1 was not required to take license u/s. 394 of Act - Hence appellant/municipality filed instant appeal - Whether respondent no. 1 was obliged to take license u/s. 394(1)(e) r/w Part IV of Sch. M of 1888, Act for catering services provided by it to members and their guests - Held, Licensing system prevents opening of establishments that pose threat to health of people - Licensing mechanism also provides for penalties in case of non-compliance with licensing conditions, which could lead to cancelling or suspension of license - Such fear created in minds of licensees also ensures that they comply with licensing conditions in order to continue enjoying benefits of license - Catering department of club which prepares and serves/supplies food to members of club was covered by definition of expression 'eating house' - It could not be denied members of club also fall within ambit of term 'public' - No doubt, primary activity of club was to provide sporting facilities to members, but supply of food was an integral part of such activity and catering department of club satisfies an essential component of facilities provided by club - DB was not right in relieving respondents of obligation to take licence u/s. 394(1)(e) of the Act - Impugned order was set aside and petition filed by respondents was dismissed - Appeal allowed.
