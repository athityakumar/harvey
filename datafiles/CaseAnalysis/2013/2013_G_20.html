Threesiamma Jacob and others v Geologist, Department of Mining and Geology and others

Supreme Court of India

08 July 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

J. Chelameswar, R.M. Lodha, Madan B. Lokur

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 436; (2013) 9 SCC 725; AIR 2013 SC 3251; 2013 (4) CTC 744; 2013 (3) KLT 275; 2014 (1) RCR(Civil) 20; 2013(9) SCALE 1; [2013] 7 S.C.R. 863

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Mines & Minerals

Keywords: Payment Of Royalty, Requirement of, Holder of jenmon rights, Excavation of minerals

Summary: Land & Property - Mines & Minerals - Holder of jenmon rights - Excavation of minerals - Payment of royalty - Requirement of - Appellants-owner of jenmom lands entered into lease agreement with department of Mining and Geology to conduct quarrying operations on suit property - Appellants realised that it was not necessary to pay any royalty to Govt. with regard to the property belonged to appellants - Appellant filed application to department for licence - Respondents failed to provide necessary permits to appellants - Thereafter, appellants was informed by department to renew lease - Appellants claimed that that holder of jenmom rights was not only proprietor of soil for which had jenmom rights, but owner of mineral wealth lying beneath soil and claim of royalty should be made only by owner of mineral against person who was excavating mineral with consent of owner - Appellants contended that they were holders of jenmom rights in lands in question and State had no legal authority to demand payment of royalties on minerals excavated by holder of jenmom right - HC dismissed contention of appellants - Hence instant appeal - Whether jenmi was entitled to rights of subsoil/ minerals lying beneath surface of the land - Held, there was nothing in law which declared that all mineral wealth sub-soil rights vest in State, on other hand, ownership of sub-soil/mineral wealth should normally follow ownership of land, unless owner of land was deprived of same by some valid process - In instant appeals, no such deprivation was brought forth - Therefore appellants were proprietors of minerals obtaining in appellants lands - However, it was not making any declaration regarding appellants liability to pay royalty to State as that issue stands referred to a larger Bench - Order accordingly.
