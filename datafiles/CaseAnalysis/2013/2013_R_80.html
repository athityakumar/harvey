Ravinder Singh v Sukhbir Singh & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

11 January 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, V. Gopala Gowda

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 25; (2013) 9 SCC 245; AIR 2013 SC 1048; 2013 CRLJ 1123; JT 2013 (1) SC 515; 2013 (1) MLJ(Crl) 487; 2013 (1) RCR(Criminal) 1020; 2013(1) SCALE 166; [2013] 1 S.C.R. 243

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Socio-Economic

Keywords: Second Writ Petition, Apology

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Schedule Casts & Schedule Tribes (Prevention of Atrocities) Act, 1989, ss. 3(1)(viii) - Criminal proceedings - Same set of facts - Estoppel - Challenged - Respondent no. 1 filed a criminal complaint u/s. 3(1)(v) of the Act against appellant (accused) for filing of false criminal writ petition in HC - Metropolitan Magistrate rejected complaint on the ground that HC closed contempt proceedings initiated against appellant and respondent no. 2, at the instance of respondent no. 1 - Aggrieved respondent no. 1 filed revision - Lower Appellate Court allowed revision - Aggrieved appellant challenged the same and filed a petition u/s. 482 of CrPC before HC, which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Appellant contended that said criminal complaint is barred by principle of issue estoppel and same issue was fully adjudicated by HC - Whether impugned judgment and order passed by HC was sustainable - Held, it was evident that appellant, after becoming aware of fact that a second writ petition was filed in his name, filed a complaint before Bar Council, against respondent no. 2 (Advocate) wherein it was stated that said second writ petition was filed without his instructions, using papers signed by him in good faith, in office of respondent no.2, at his instance - HC considered reply submitted by respondent no. 2, who tendered absolute and unconditional apology and disposed of said contempt proceedings - It was crystal clear that HC was satisfied that appellant was guided by his counsel and that he himself was not well-versed with English language and had filed his supporting affidavit in Hindi and further that it had accepted the unqualified apology tendered by respondent no.2, considering the facts even though both petitions were filed under signature of appellant, it had decided to drop proceedings, as respondent no. 1 did not wish to pursue his remedy any further and same was not pressed - In the event that appellant preferred an application for quashing of FIR lodged by respondent no.1, and was unsuccessful therein, same did not mean that appellant had filed a false case against respondent no. 1 - There was a difference between terms 'not proved' and 'false' - Merely because a party was unable to prove a fact, same could not be categorized as false in each and every case - In order to invoke rule of issue estoppel, not only parties in 2 trials should be same but also, the fact in issue, proved or not, as present in earlier trial, should be identical to what was sought to be re-agitated in subsequent trial - If cause of action was determined to exist, i.e., judgment was given on it, the same was said to be merged in judgment - Complaint in dispute filed by respondent no. 1 was based on ground that there was a false declaration by appellant while filing second writ petition as he suppressed truth that earlier for the same relief a petition was filed and it was done so to gain a legal advantage and thus, it was a false, vexatious and malicious one attracting provisions of s. 3(1)(viii) of the Act - In instant case, it was clear that respondent no. 1, became law unto himself and assumed jurisdiction to decide legal dispute himself to which he himself had been a party being son of a rank trespasser - Transfer by original allottee at initial stage, even if illegal, would not confer any right in favour of respondent no. 1 - Thus, respondent no.1 adopted intimidatory tactics by resorting to revenue as well as criminal proceedings against appellant without realising that even if initial transfer by original allottee 'A' was illegal, land might revert back to Govt. not to him merely because his father had encroached upon same - HC has dealt with issue involved herein and matter stood closed at the instance of respondent no. 1 himself - Thus, there could be no justification whatsoever to launch criminal prosecution on that basis afresh - Complaint filed by respondent no. 1 was quashed - Impugned judgment of HC was set aside - Appeal allowed.
