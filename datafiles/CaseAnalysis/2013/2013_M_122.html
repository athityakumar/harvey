Maharashtra Ekta Hawkers Union and another v Municipal Corporation, Greater Mumbai and others

Supreme Court of India

09 September 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, V. Gopala Gowda

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 586; (2014) 1 SCC 490; 2013(6) ALL MR 480; 2014 (2) AWC 1796; 2013 (6) Bom.C.R. 481; JT 2013 (12) SC 507; 2013 (2) OLR 830; 2013(11) SCALE 329; [2013] 9 S.C.R. 742

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Model, Street Vendors (Protection of Livelihood and Regulation of Street Vending) Bill, 2012, Street Vendors (Protection of Livelihood and Regulation of Street Vending) Bill, 2009

Summary: Constitution of India-Articles 19 (1) (g), 19 (6) and 21-Street trading-Right of street-vendors and hawkers-Problem of street-vendors and hawkers-Appropriate to lift embargo placed by Supreme Court on entertaining matters of street-vendors/hawkers by High Courts-Maharashtra Ekta Hawkers Union v. Municipal Corporation, Greater Mumbai, (2009) 17 SCC 151, stand modified-Street-vendors/hawkers, residents and other adversely affected by street-vending/hawking henceforth entitled to invoke jurisdiction of concerned High Courts for redressal of their grievance-Till Parliament enacts appropriate legislation for protecting rights of urban street- vendors/hawkers, court may ordain implementation of National Policy on Urban Street Vendors, 2009 with liberty to parties to approach appropriate judicial forums for redressal of their grievance-Aggrieved person free to file petition under Article 226 of the Constitution-Till needful is done, apposite for court to step in and direct that 2009 policy should be implemented throughout country-For facilitating implementation of 2009 Policy, certain directions issued-Directions to remain operative till appropriate legislation enacted by Parliament or any other competent Legislature and is brought into force.
