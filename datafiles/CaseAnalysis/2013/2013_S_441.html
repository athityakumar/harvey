Ranjit Singh v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

03 July 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 418; (2013) 12 SCC 333; AIR 2013 SC 2991; 2013 CRLJ 3959; 2013 (3) JCC 2173; 2013(9) SCALE 117; [2013] 7 S.C.R. 394

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal code, 1860, ss. 304-B, 498-A - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 113B - Dowry death - Cruelty - Conviction - Sustainability - Complainants daughter was married to appellant/accused - Appellant and his family members allegedly murdered the complainants daughter due to non-fulfillment of demand of dowry - Charge sheet against appellant was filed u/ss. 302, 498, 304B r/w s. 34 IPC - Trial Court convicted appellant and his mother u/ss. 304-B r/w s. 34 and s. 498-A IPC - On appeal HC acquitted appellants mother but affirmed the judgment of Trial Court for appellant - Hence instant appeal - Whether HC was justified in convicting appellant for the offences charged against him - Held, death of deceased was caused by bodily injury which was otherwise than under the normal circumstances - Death took place within few months of the date of marriage i.e. much before 7 years of marriage - It was shown that soon before her death she was subjected to cruelty and harassment by her husband in connection with the demand of dowry - Thus, instant case squarely fell within the meaning of dowry death for the purpose to attract s. 304-B IPC - S. 113-B of the Act deals with the presumption of 'dowry death' and proclaims that when the question whether a person has committed a dowry death of a woman and it was shown that soon before her death, such woman had been subjected by such person to cruelty or harassment, for or in connection with demand of a dowry, the Court should presume that such person had caused 'dowry death' - It could, therefore, be understood that irrespective of the fact whether the accused had any direct connection with the death or not, he should be presumed to have committed the 'dowry death' provided the other requirements were satisfied - Prosecution has successfully proved the ingredients necessary to attract the provision of s. 304B IPC - SC affirmed the conviction u/s. 304B IPC and 498-A IPC and reduce the sentence awarded u/s. 304B IPC - Appeal disposed of.
