Rajeev Kumar v State of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

31 October 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Patnaik, Gyan Sudha Misra

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 729; (2013) 16 SCC 640; AIR 2014 SC 227; 2014 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 274; 2013 (4) MLJ(Crl) 523; 2013 (4) RCR(Criminal) 964; 2013(13) SCALE 410; [2013] 12 S.C.R. 251

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 306, 304B and 498A - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s.164 - Dowry harassment - Committing of suicide by wife - Conviction u/s.304B IPC - Maintainability - Appellant allegedly committed offence of dowry harassment - Trial Court held appellant guilty of dowry death punishable u/s.304B after his wife-deceased committed attempt to suicide and gave details of harassment to judicial magistrate, u/s.164 CrPC in hospital before she succumbed to death - HC held that there was no ground to interfere with the orders of conviction and sentence passed by Trial Court and dismissed the criminal appeal of the appellant - Whether the prosecution has been able to prove beyond reasonable doubt that the appellant has committed the offence of dowry death under Section 304B, IPC - Held, it would be clear from the contents of the dying declaration that the deceased was fed up with the activities of her husband and deceased poured kerosene oil on herself and burnt herself - What those activities of the appellant were which prompted her to commit suicide were not stated, but she had stated that her husband used to get upset on petty issues and earlier her husband used to taunt her for dowry - When, however, SC scrutinized the evidence of PW-5, the father of the deceased, SC found that soon before the death of the deceased, the appellant had subjected the deceased to cruelty which was not in any way connected with the demand of dowry - SC on similar facts in another case had held that to attract the provisions of s.304B, IPC, one of the main ingredients of the offence, which was required to be established, was that "soon before her death" deceased was subjected to cruelty and harassment "in connection with the demand for dowry" and such ingredient of the offence was not there in that case - SC, however, held that it was not necessary to remit the matter to Trial Court for framing a charge u/s.306, IPC, and the accused also could not complain for want of opportunity to defend the charge u/s.306, IPC, if the facts found in evidence justify the conviction of the appellant u/ss. 498A and 306, IPC instead of the graver offence u/s.304B, IPC - In that case, the three-Judge Bench of HC held the appellant guilty of the offences u/ss.498A and 306, IPC instead of the graver offence u/s. 304B, IPC - K. Prema S. Rao and Another, etc. v. Yadla Srinivasa Rao and Others, etc. 2002 Indlaw SC 1363, relied on - In instant case also, SC held the appellant guilty of offences u/ss. 498A and 306, IPC - Appeal allowed.
