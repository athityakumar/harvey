Central Bureau of Investigation v Jagjit Singh

Supreme Court of India

01 October 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya, Ranjan Gogoi

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 651; (2013) 10 SCC 686; AIR 2013 SC (Supp) 152; 2013 (4) CGLJ 519; 2013 (4) JCC 2729; 2013 (4) RCR(Criminal) 607; 2013(12) SCALE 299

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Cheating, Quashing of proceedings, Allowed

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120B, 420, 467, 468, 471, 472 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, s. 13(1)(d) r/w s. 13(2) - Criminal conspiracy - Cheating - Quashing of proceedings - Allowed - Challenged - Respondent-accused entered into criminal conspiracy to cause wrongful loss to bank in the matter of term/demand loans - Case was registered by appellants-CBI against respondent u/ss. 120B, 420, 467, 468, 471, 472 IPC and u/s. 13(1)(d) r/w s. 13(2) of the Act - Matter was settled between respondent and bank and debt was paid by order of Tribunal - Application was filed by respondent for quashing of proceedings before HC - Application was allowed by HC - Appellants challenged impugned order passed by HC in instant appeal - Whether impugned order passed by HC was maintainable - Held, specific allegation made against the respondent was that he obtained loan on basis of forged document with aid of officers of Bank - On investigation, having found ingredients of cheating and dishonestly inducing delivery of property of bank and dishonestly using as genuine forged document , charge sheet was submitted u/ss. 420, 471 IPC against accused persons - Debt which was due to bank was recovered by bank pursuant to order passed by Tribunal - Therefore, it should not be said that there was compromise between offender and victim - Offences when committed in relation with Banking activities including offences u/ss. 420, 471 IPC have harmful effect on public and threaten wellbeing of society - These offences fall under category of offences involving moral turpitude committed by public servants while working in that capacity - Prima facie, one might state that bank as victim in such cases but, in fact, society in general, including customers of bank was sufferer - In instant case, there was neither allegation regarding any abuse of process of any Court nor anything on record to suggest that offenders were entitled to secure order in ends of justice - HC had not considered above factors while passing impugned order - Hence, opinion of HC erred in addressing issue in right perspective - Appeal allowed.
