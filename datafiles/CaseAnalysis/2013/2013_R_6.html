R. Shaji v State Of Kerala

Supreme Court of India

04 February 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, V. Gopala Gowda

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 62; (2013) 14 SCC 266; AIR 2013 SC 651; 2013 ALL MR (Cri) 1469; JT 2013 (2) SC 447; 2013 (1) KLT 493; 2013 (1) RCR(Criminal) 964; 2013(2) SCALE 186; [2013] 3 S.C.R. 1172

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 364, 302, 120-B - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 161, 164, 207 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant (accused no. 1) and other accused allegedly murder drivered of appellant, for alleged illicit relationship with wife of appellant - Appellants and co-accused allegedly committed offences u/ss. 302 r/w s. 120-B of IPC - Trial Court convicted appellant for offence u/ss. 302 r/w s. 120-B of IPC and sentenced him to undergo imprisonment for life and other co-accused were convicted u/ss. 364, 201 r/w s. 120-B of IPC - Aggrieved appellant filed an appeal before HC, which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Appellant contended that there were no eye-witnesses to actual incident of killing and that statements recorded u/s. 164 of CrPC before Magistrate were not put on record before Trial Court - Whether impugned conviction order passed by Trial Court and affirmed by HC was justified - Held, Cl. (iv) of s. 207 of CrPC clearly provided that any statement recorded u/s. 164 of CrPC should be made available to accused along with all other documents that were filed the charge sheet - Appellant had neither urged that statements recorded u/s. 164 of CrPC were not a part of such documents, before Trial Court, nor was any issue raised by him at the time of cross-examination of IO (PW77) - It appeared from documents on record that such documents, if same were in fact, a part of record, were not marked - Appellant raised that issue for the first time before HC - Object of statement of witnesses recorded u/s. 164 of CrPC was of two fold; first, to deter witness from changing his stand by denying the contents of his previously recorded statement, and secondly, to tide over immunity from prosecution by witness u/s. 164 of CrPC - A proposition to the effect that if a statement of a witness was recorded u/s. 164 of CrPC, his evidence in Court should be discarded, was not at all warranted - A failure by serologist to detect origin of blood due to dis-integration of serum, did not mean that blood stuck on axe could not have been human blood at all - Sometimes it was possible, either because stain was insufficient in itself, or due to haematological changes and plasmatic coagulation, that a serologist might fail to detect the origin of blood in question - However, in such a case, unless doubt was of a reasonable dimension, which a judicially conscientious mind might entertain with some objectivity, no benefit could be claimed by accused in that regard - It could not be accepted that in absence of a report regarding origin of blood, accused could not be convicted, for it was only because of lapse of time, that blood could not be classified successfully - Thus, no advantage could be conferred upon accused to enable him to claim any benefit, and report of disintegration of blood etc. could not be termed as a missing link, on the basis of which the chain of circumstances might be presumed to be broken - Evidence on record clearly established, appellant had adequate reason to harbour animosity towards deceased, as appellant might well have been unable to tolerate the intimacy that deceased had developed with his wife - Motive stood proved accused no. 2 and appellant were closely related and together they had hatched a conspiracy to eliminate deceased - Witnesses particularly PW8, 9, 11 and 12 made it clear that they were acquainted with appellant since he was posted in control room of their city - In view of fact situation holding/non-holding of a Test Identification Parade loses its significance - It was pertinent to note that defence did not put any question to PW77, IO in relation to why such Parade was not held - When a statement was recorded in Court, and witness spoke under oath, after understanding the sanctity of oath taken by him either in name of God or religion, it was then left to Court to appreciate his evidence u/s. 3 of the act - Judge should consider whether a prudent man would appreciate such evidence, and not appreciate the same in accordance with his own perception - However, Courts below have appreciated entire evidence meticulously - Impugned order passed by HC was upheld - Appeal dismissed.
