Asst. Engineer, Rajasthan Dev. Corp. & Anr. v Gitam Singh

Supreme Court of India

31 January 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 55; (2013) 5 SCC 136; 2013(2) ALL MR 440; 2013 (2) AWC 1977; 2013 (136) FLR 908; JT 2013 (2) SC 231; 2013 (2) LLJ 141; 2013 (5) MahLJ 1; 2013 (3) MPLJ 531; 2013(2) SCALE 126; [2013] 1 S.C.R. 679; 2013 (2) SLR 332

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Daily wager - Termination - Award - Sustainability - Respondent was engaged as a daily wager - Labour Court in its award recorded the findings that respondent had worked as technician (Mistri) under appellant for 240 days for the period and the termination of his service by an oral order was violative of s. 25-F of the Act - Hence instant appeal - Where the workman had worked for only eight months as daily wager and his termination has been held to be in contravention of s. 25-F of the Act and whether the direction to the employer for reinstatement with continuity of service and 25 per cent back wages was legally sustainable -

Held, workman was engaged as daily wager on 1-3-1991 and he worked hardly for eight months from 1-3-1991 to 31-10-1991, in SC's view, Labour Court failed to exercise its judicial discretion appropriately - Judicial discretion exercised by Labour Court suffered from serious infirmity - Single Judge as well as the DB also erred in not considering the above aspect at all - Award directing reinstatement of respondent with continuity of service and 25% back wages in the facts and circumstances of the case could not be sustained and has to be set aside and was set aside - Appeal partly allowed.
