Kallakkurichi Taluk Retired Official Association, Tamilnadu and others v State of Tamilnadu and another

Supreme Court of India

17 January 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

J. S. KHEHAR, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 28; (2013) 2 SCC 772; 2013 (136) FLR 764; JT 2013 (2) SC 42; 2013 (2) LLJ 9; 2013(1) SCALE 424; [2013] 4 S.C.R. 883

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Tamil Nadu Pension Rules, 1978, General Provident Fund Rules, University Pension Rules

Summary: Service - Practice & Procedure -Tamil Nadu Pension Rules, 1978, r. 30 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 16 - Dearness allowance - Dearness pay - Classification - Claim - Entitled to - Appellants (associations) was associations of retired employees) were retired employees, who were retired on or after 1.6.1988 - Respondent (Stat Govt.) issued an order (GO) dt.9.8.1989, pensionary benefits of an employee retired/retiring on or after 1.6.1988 were required to be computed by adding 'dearness allowance' to 'dearness pay' at a fixed percentage - By virtue of said determination, employees retiring on or after 1.6.1988 would be at a disadvantage, as against employees who had retired prior thereto - Aggrieved appellants filed an application before Tribunal and challenged said GO, which was transferred to HC - Single Judge allowed petition on 20.4.2006, holding that State Govt., in not extending benefits to members of appellant Association, had discriminated against them - Impugned GO dt.9.8.1989, to the extent that it did not confer same benefits (based on component of 'dearness allowance' treated as 'dearness pay'), for employees who retired on or after 1.6.1988, was held as unsustainable - Aggrieved State Govt. filed an appeal before DB - DB dismissed petitions preferred by employees (and employees' associations) and allowed appeal - Hence, instant appeals - Appellants contended that determination by HC to the effect, that employees who had retired prior to 1.6.1988 from a similar post, would 'get a little higher' pensionary emoluments, than those who retired afterwards, was clearly preposterous - Further contended that according to acknowledged position, respondent no. 1 in affidavit dt.15.12.2011, on proper calculations asserted, that in identical circumstances, a Deputy Collector retiring prior to 1.6.1988 would draw pension at a monthly rate of Rs.6,808/-, whereas, a Deputy Collector retiring after 30.6.1988 would get a monthly pension of Rs.4,287/-, merely on account of accident of retiring before or after 1.6.1988, one of pensioners would draw pension @ of about Rs.2,500/- pm more than the other -Whether appellants were discriminated by impugned GO dt. .8.1989 - Held, appellants claim was rejected by HC, merely on account of belief that carry home pension for employees who would retire after 1.6.1988, would be trivially lower than those retiring prior thereto, amounts to bagging issue pressed before HC - In a given situation, an employee retiring on or after 1.6.1988 could suffer a substantial loss, in comparison to an employee retiring before 1.6.1988 -HC clearly erred while determining issue projected before it - There could be no doubt, that 'dearness allowance' was extended to employees to balance the effects of ongoing inflation, so as to ensure that inflation did not interfere with enjoyment of life, to which an employee was accustomed - Likewise, objective of 'dearness pay' was to balance effects of ongoing inflation, so that a pensioner could adequately sustain the means of livelihood to which he was accustomed - Since component of inflation similarly affects all employees and pensioners (irrespective of date of their entry into service or retirement), it was not per se possible to accept different levels of 'dearness pay' to remedy the malady of inflation - Just like the date of entry into service (for serving employees) would be wholly irrelevant to determine 'dearness allowance' to be extended to serving employees, because same had no relevance to object sought to be achieved - Likewise, date of retirement (for pensioners) would be wholly irrelevant to determine 'dearness pay' to be extended to retired employees - In absence of any objective, projected in instant case, question of examining reasonableness to object sought to be achieved, did not arise - Pleadings of Govt. did not reveal any reason for classification, which was subject matter of challenge in instant appeal, except for introducing a cutoff date for differential treatment - It was evident that Govt. has not disclosed any object which was desired to achieve by cutoff date - Financial constraints of Govt. were not described as the basis/reason for classification made in impugned GO dt.9.8.1989 - Based on r. 30 of the Rules, it was clear that component of 'dearness pay' would be added to emoluments of an employee for calculating pension - Any classification without reference to object sought to be achieved, would be arbitrary and violative of protection afforded u/arts. 14 and 16 of Constitution - Thus, classification made by Govt. in impugned GO dt.9.8.1989 placing employees who had retired after 1.6.1988 at a disadvantage, vis-à-vis employees who retired prior thereto, by allowing them a lower component of 'dearness pay', was clearly arbitrary and discriminatory, and violative of arts. 14 and 16 of Constitution - Impugned GO and judgment passed by HC were set aside - Appeals allowed.
