State Of Orissa & Ors. v Sri Jagabandhu Panda

Supreme Court of India

27 February 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

M.Y. Eqbal, Surinder Singh Nijjar

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 115; (2013) 11 SCC 631; AIR 2013 SC (Supp) 291; 2013 (137) FLR 321; JT 2013 (3) SC 366; 2013(3) SCALE 93; [2013] 4 S.C.R. 1080; 2013 (4) SLR 427

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Orissa Mining and Geology Service Rules

Summary: Service - Administrative - Appointment - Vacancies - Permanent - Temporary - Challenged - Respondent was appointed post of Ore Dressing Engineer in the office of the Directorate of Geology was temporarily upgraded on 1-9-2008 as OSD-cum-Deputy Director - Later said post was termed as ex-cadre and the post of OSD cum-Deputy Director was to lapse on his retirement - Respondent filed application before the Tribunal with a prayer that he should be re-designated as OSC-cum-Deputy Director (Steel) instead of OSD-cum-Deputy Director (ex-cadre) - Respondent filed application before Tribunal and it was allowed - Aggrieved on appellant filed revision, HC dismissed the same - Hence instant appeal - Whether the post held by respondent was an ex-cadre post - Held, finding arrived by the HC that the post of Ore Engineer was for the first time treated as excadre post in the year 2005, was absolutely perverse and erroneous - Immediately after joining the post of Ore-dresser, respondent started filing representations - Respondent stated that only after joining he came to know that the post he was holding was an ex-cadre post - It was well within the knowledge of respondent that the post which he was holding was an ex-cadre post and, therefore, by series of representations he requested the Department to upgrade the said post and to open up the promotional avenues - Post of Ore-dressing Engineer was created and appointment was made outside the existing cadre of mining engineers - It was so understood by respondent that from the inception that he was holding an ex-cadre post there had been a special reason for recruiting an Ore-dressing engineer for a specific purpose temporarily outside the ex-cadre of mining engineer - A cadre might consist of permanent as well as temporary post and there might be permanent vacancies in permanent as well as temporary post, but it did not follow that appointment made outside the very service and outside the cadre should be considered to be made to temporary post borne on the cadre merely because, the post was likely to continue indefinitely - T.N. Administrative Service Officers Association and Another v. Union of India and Others, 2000 Indlaw SC 279, relied on - SC was of definite opinion that the direction issued by the Tribunal and the order of the HC affirming the order of the Tribunal was wholly without jurisdiction - Impugned orders passed were set aside - Appeal allowed.
