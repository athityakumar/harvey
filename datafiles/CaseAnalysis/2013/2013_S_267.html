Umesh Kumar v State of Andhra Pradesh and another

Supreme Court of India

06 September 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, Sharad Arvind Bobde

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 606; (2013) 10 SCC 591; AIR 2014 SC 1106; JT 2013 (12) SC 213; 2015 (1) RLW 391; 2013(11) SCALE 28

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Approver

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 216, 482 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120-B, 201, 468, 271 - Power of Court - Respondent no. 2 directed registration of First Information Report and that investigation be conducted by CID - As consequence, FIR was registered and one Deputy Sub Police was appointed as Investigating Officer - During course of investigation, one person/approver was arrested and but he did not named appellant - Report was forwarded by to State Govt. and approver was remanded to judicial custody in that he named appellant and made application to become approver - Meanwhile, Investigating Officer filed statement in court that unless approver was granted pardon, there would be no evidence against appellant - Trial Court accepted application and charge sheet was filed showing that offences punishable u/ss. 468, 471, 120-B, 201 of IPC had been committed by appellant - Appellant approached HC u/s. 482 of Cr.P.C. for quashing said charge sheet in which HC quashed charge sheet only in respect of offence u/s. 468 of and not quashed u/ss. 471, 120-B, 201 of IPC - Hence, instant Appeals - Whether Trial court could hold that applicant was required to be tried for offence u/s. 468 of IPC and Trial Court would be competent on said charge in exercise of its power u/s. of 216 CrPC - Held, charge sheet had been filed and cognizance had been taken by magistrate concerned, committal proceedings had not yet taken place and some of offences attracted were exclusively triable by Sessions Court - Appellant approached HC u/s. 482 CrPC and charge sheet had been partly quashed observing that provisions of s. 468 of IPC were not attracted - Appellant approached HC u/s. 482 of CrPC at premature stage and at said stage HC could examine charge sheet, case diary and other material in charge sheet which by no means could be termed as substantive evidence - Thus, order of HC impugned could not be termed as final decision and said order was subject to further order which could be passed by Trial court u/s. 216 of CrPC on basis of evidence to be led during trial - Appeals disposed of.
