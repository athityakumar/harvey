Kishorebhai Gandubhai Pethani v State of Gujarat and another

Supreme Court of India

20 September 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, Sharad Arvind Bobde

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 867; (2014) 13 SCC 539; 2013 (4) JCC 2669; JT 2013 (12) SC 601; 2014 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1180; 2013(12) SCALE 1; [2013] 10 S.C.R. 208

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Quashing Of Complaint

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 323, 324, 294A, 506(2), 463, 465, 468, 471 and 114 - Bombay Police Act, 1951, s, 135(1) - Forgery - Quashing of complaint - Entitled to - Complaint was filed against appellant accused u/ss. 307, 323, 324, 294A, 506(2)and 114 IPC r/w s. 135(1) of the Act by respondent - Further complaint was lodged u/ss. 463, 465, 468, 471 and 114 IPC for tampering with medical report - Accused filed application to quash the complaint - HC dismissed application - Hence instant appeal - Whether the order of HC was sustainable -

Held, documents were forged and fabricated - Manipulation if any, had been made prior to filing of those documents in Court - Hence, there was no fault found with impugned judgment rendered by HC and case did not warrant any interference - Instant appeal lacked merit - Ram Dhan v State Of U.P. & Anr. 2012 Indlaw SC 125; P. Swaroopa Rani v. M. Hari Narayana @ Hari Babu, 2008 Indlaw SC 333; Mahesh Chand Sharma v. State of U. P. & Ors., 2009 Indlaw SC 1058; C. Muniappan & Ors. v. State of T. N., 2010 Indlaw SC 686; Institute of Chartered Accountants of India v. Vimal Kumar Surana & Anr., 2010 Indlaw SC 1024; and C.P. Subhash v. Inspector of Police Chennai & Ors., 2013 Indlaw SC 50 relied on - Appeal dismissed.
