Central Bureau Of Investigation v V. Vijay Sai Reddy

Supreme Court of India

09 May 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, M.Y. Eqbal

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 313; (2013) 7 SCC 452; AIR 2013 SC 2216; 2013 CRLJ 3016; JT 2013 (8) SC 25; 2013 (3) RCR(Criminal) 252; 2013(7) SCALE 15; [2013] 8 S.C.R. 830

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Grant Of Bail, Continuation of investigation

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 120-B r/w ss. 420, 409 and 477-A - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, s. 13(2) r/w s. 13(1)(c) and (d) - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 437 - Economic offences - Continuation of investigation - Grant of bail - Justifiability - Pursuant to order of HC dt.10-8-2011, appellant, Central Bureau of Investigation (CBI) registered a case against respondent (accused no. 2), who was a charted accountant, director and chairman of a publication company owned by accused no.1, who allegedly floated said publication company with an objective of conducting media business with the ill-gotten wealth - Respondent and 73 others were charge sheeted for alleged offences u/s. 120-B r/w ss. 420, 409 and 477-A of IPC and u/s. 13(2) r/w s. 13(1)(c) and (d) of the Act - Respondent was arrested for his involvement and complicity in case and was in judicial custody - Respondent filed bail application u/s. 437 of CrPC, which was allowed - Aggrieved appellant filed a petition before HC for cancellation of bail granted to respondent - HC by impugned order dismissed petition - Hence, instant appeal - Appellant contended that out of 8 charge-sheets, 3 charge-sheets were yet to be filed for which CBI required interrogation and collection of materials through respondent for which his bail had to be cancelled - Whether bail granted to respondent was liable to be cancelled - Held, it was settled that if irrelevant materials were took into account or relevant materials was kept out of consideration, the order granting bail to accused could not be sustained - In same way, if there was specific allegation by prosecution that accused in question was a party to criminal conspiracy, neither Trial Court nor HC was justified in granting bail to said person - In instant case, in order to complete the investigation in respect of 3 more charge sheets, presence of respondent was required and could not be ignored by treating him as Chartered Accountant (CA) only - It was noted that on the basis of false rating of said publication company of which respondent was Director, many more companies and individuals were made to invest their money by threat, intimidation, cheating and inducement - It was specific stand of CBI that those ratings were falsely projected by respondent - It was true that Trial Court while granting bail imposed certain conditions and HC had also added some more additional conditions, however, taking note of few instances in which how respondent has acted, it could not be possible for investigating agency to collect the remaining materials for the remaining 3 charge sheets to be filed - In those circumstances, Trial Court took irrelevant materials for consideration for grant of bail and HC having arrived definite conclusion that several findings of Trial Court were unacceptable or irrelevant but ultimately affirmed the very same order of Trial Court granting bail - HC mistakenly took into account the irrelevant materials and kept out the relevant materials, which had to be considered for grant of bail - Cancellation of bail necessarily involved the review of a decision already made, it should always be exercised very sparingly by Court - Trial Court committed an error in granting bail and same was erroneously affirmed by HC - CBI was directed to complete all investigation relating to remaining 3 charge sheets within 4 months - Thereafter, respondent was free to renew his prayer for bail before Trial Court - Impugned orders passed by Trial Court and HC granting bail to respondent were set aside - Appeal allowed.
