State of Uttaranchal and another v Shiv Charan Singh Bhandari and others

Supreme Court of India

23 August 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 551; (2013) 12 SCC 179; 2013 (139) FLR 760; JT 2013 (12) SC 269; 2013(11) SCALE 56; [2013] 9 S.C.R. 609; 2013 (6) SLR 629; 2014 (2) SLR 688; 2013 (4) UPLBEC 2727

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Notional promotion - Entitlement - Respondents sought promotion from SAS Group III to SAS Group II from date of promotion of their juniors - Tribunal directed notional promotion to respondents - Appellant department's writ petition was dismissed by HC - Hence, instant appeal - Appellant contended that respondents' claim was barred by delay and laches - Held, anyone who slept over his right was bound to suffer - Neither Tribunal nor HC had appreciated these aspects in proper perspective and proceeded on the base that a junior was promoted and, therefore, seniors could not be denied promotion - A stale claim of getting promotional benefits definitely should not have been entertained by Tribunal and accepted by HC - Notional promotional benefits were granted but the same was likely to affect State exchequer regard being had to fixation of pay and pension - These aspects were not taken into consideration - Equality should be claimed at right juncture and not after expiry of two decades - There should not be any provision providing for limitation but a grievance relating to promotion could not be given a new lease of life at any point of time - Orders passed by HC and that of Tribunal were set aside - Appeal allowed.
