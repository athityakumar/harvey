Kusheswar Nath Pandey v State of Bihar and others

Supreme Court of India

05 August 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Gokhale, J. Chelameswar

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 849; (2013) 12 SCC 580; 2013 (5) AWC 5154; JT 2013 (12) SC 193; 2013(10) SCALE 227; [2013] 9 S.C.R. 593; 2014 (3) SLR 462

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Employment-Promotion-Time bound promotion-Cancellation-Time bound promotion granted to appellant-No fault or fraudulent act on part of appellant-Relevant rules relied upon and appellant also passed required examination-Some eleven years thereafter, decision of Government to cancel his promotion-Not proper-Approach of authorities of Government totally unjustified-Single Judge of High Court justified in setting aside order of cancelling his promotion-Impugned judgment of Division Bench of High Court affirming decision of Government to cancel his promotion-Set aside.The appellant was not at all in any way at fault. It was a time bound promotion which was given to him and some eleven years thereafter, the Authorities of the Bihar Government woke up and according to them the time bound promotion was wrongly given and then the relevant rules are being relied upon and that too after the appellant had passed the required examination. This approach of Authorities of State Government was totally unjustified
