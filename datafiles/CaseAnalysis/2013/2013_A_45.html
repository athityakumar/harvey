Ramesh Chandra Shah and others v Anil Joshi and others

Supreme Court of India

03 April 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, Kurian Joseph

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 200; (2013) 11 SCC 309; AIR 2013 SC 1613; 2013 (3) AWC 2197; 2013 (137) FLR 759; JT 2013 (4) SC 441; 2013(5) SCALE 397; [2013] 5 S.C.R. 687; 2013 (3) SLR 397; 2013 (2) UPLBEC 1241

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Adaptation and Modification Order 2002, Uttar Pradesh Procedure for Direct Recruitment for Group 'C' Posts (Outside the purview of the Uttar Pradesh Public Service Commission) Rules, 1998, Uttarakhand Procedure for Direct Recruitment for Group "C" Posts (Outside the purview of the Uttarakhand Public Service Commission) Rules, 2008, Uttar Pradesh Medical Health and Family Welfare Department Physiotherapist and Occupational Therapist Service Rules, 1998

Summary: Service - Adaptation and Modification Order, 2002 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 226, 309 - Selection process - Advertisement - Quashing of - Uttarakhand Board of Technical Education/Board issued advertisement - Appellants and private respondents submitted applications for posts of Physiotherapist - After written exam appellants were declared successful and became entitled to be appointed against advertised posts - Private respondents who failed to clear test filed petition before Single Judge for quashing advertisement and process of selection - Single Judge allowed said petition and quashed selection with direction that available posts be advertised afresh - Successful candidates appealed before DB and same was dismissed - Hence instant application - Whether Single Judge and DB were right in passing impugned order - Held, Board had been designated as recruiting agency and selection would be made in accordance with provisions of General Rules - They appeared in written test knowing that they would have to pass examination enumerated of advertisement - If they had cleared test, private respondents would not have raised any objection to selection procedure or methodology adopted by Board - They made grievance only after they found that their names do not figure in list of successful candidates - This conduct of private respondents clearly disentitles them from seeking relief u/art. 226 of Constitution - To put it differently, by having appeared in test and taken chance to be declared successful, private respondents would be deemed to have waived their right to challenge advertisement and procedure of selection - It was settled law that a person who consciously takes part in process of selection could not, thereafter, turn around and question method of selection and its outcome - By having taken part in process of selection with full knowledge that recruitment was being made under General Rules, respondents had waived their right to question advertisement or methodology adopted by Board for making selection and Single Judge and DB committed grave error by entertaining grievance made by respondents - Single Judge committed an error by holding that despite non obstante clause contained in r. 2 of General Rules, Special Rules would govern recruitment to post of Physiotherapist - We do not consider it necessary to express any conclusive opinion on this issue and leave question to be decided in an appropriate case - Impugned orders also one passed by Single Judge were set aside and petition filed by respondents was dismissed - Appeals allowed.
