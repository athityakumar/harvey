Rajasthan State Road Transport Corporation And Another v Satya Prakash

Supreme Court of India

09 April 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Gokhale, Ranjan Gogoi

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 352; (2013) 9 SCC 232; 2013 (5) ALD(SC) 88; 2013 (137) FLR 1035; JT 2013 (7) SC 121; 2014 (2) MahLJ 138; 2014 (1) MPLJ 530; 2013(6) SCALE 654; [2013] 2 S.C.R. 939; 2013 (4) SLR 40

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 33(2)(b), 33A - Misconduct - Dismissal - Reinstatement - Legality - Respondent was working as bus conductor on daily wages under appellant/Rajasthan State Road Transport Corporation - Due to misconduct respondent was dismissed from service - Respondent filed suit before Trial Court and the same was dismissed - Respondent filed complaint before Tribunal accordingly award was passed and directed reinstatement of respondent - Appellant filed appeal before Single Judge and the same was dismissed - On appeal before DB the same was dismissed - Hence instant appeal - Whether impugned order passed by DB was justified -

Held, respondent was employed as daily rated employee for period of three months, and thereafter was continued for few months more - There was no question of his being in service even for one continuous year, since he had obviously not completed 240 days of service - During this short span of service there were various allegations against him - Since there was an allegation of misconduct, they afforded him an opportunity to explain - At time of incident of checking of bus, respondent did not sign the way-bill, nor did he attend inquiry, wherein, he was called to explain his conduct - Which led to his dismissal from service - Respondent chose to file Civil Suit in wrong Court - Trial Court which heard suit held that misconduct was proved, and termination could not be faulted - However, very Court held that it did not have territorial jurisdiction to decide the suit - Therefore one might keep aside finding of that Court concerning misconduct - However, when respondent filed Complaint before Tribunal - Tribunal also returned same finding in its award that appellant had proved misconduct, this being position, finding would relate back and employer employee relationship between parties would be deemed to have ended from date of dismissal order passed by appellant - Judgment and order rendered by DB dismissing appeal filed by appellants against judgment and order rendered by Single Judge confirming award rendered by Tribunal was set-aside - Appeal allowed.
