Devendra Kumar v State of Uttaranchal and others

Supreme Court of India

29 July 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, Sharad Arvind Bobde

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 484; (2013) 9 SCC 363; AIR 2013 SC 3325; 2013(6) ALL MR 950; 2013 (6) AWC 6159; 2013 (139) FLR 284; JT 2013 (10) SC 566; 2013(9) SCALE 703; [2013] 8 S.C.R. 471; 2013 (5) SLR 536

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Termination

Summary: Employment - Termination-Validity-Employment obtained by suppressing material fact at time of appointment for post of constable-Courts below recorded finding of fact that appellant suppressed material information sought by employer as to whether he had ever been involved in criminal case-Suppression of material information sought by employer or furnishing false information itself amounts to moral turpitude and is separate and distinct from involvement in criminal case-Where applicant gets office by misrepresenting facts or by playing fraud upon competent authority-Such appointment order cannot be sustained in eyes of law-Fraud avoids all judicial acts, ecclesiastical or temporal-Information sought by employer if not disclosed as required-Would definitely amount to suppression of material information-In that eventuality, service becomes liable to be terminated, even it there had been no further trial or person concerned stood acquitted/discharged-Courts below correctly held termination of service of appellant by respondent authorities-Valid.
