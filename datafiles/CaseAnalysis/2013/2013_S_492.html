State of Uttar Pradesh v Rajeshwar and others

Supreme Court of India

16 April 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Aftab Alam, R.M. Lodha

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 238; (2013) 15 SCC 241; AIR 2013 SC (Supp) 218; 2013 CRLJ 3103; JT 2013 (10) SC 380; 2014 (2) RCR(Criminal) 504; 2013(6) SCALE 41

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 148, 302, 149, 307, 436, 120B - Rioting and arson - Murder - Reversal of conviction - Sustainability - Respondents/accused allegedly committed rioting and arson and also murdered 3people while committing the same - Trial Court convicted 14accused persons for offences punishable u/ss. 148, 302 r/w s. 149, 307 r/w s. 149 and u/s. 435 r/w s. 149 of IPC and acquitted other accused - On appeals, HC allowed appeals andset aside order of conviction passed by Trial Court - Hence instant appeal by State - Held, Old and bitter enmity between two sides was admitted by prosecution - As a matter of fact, that was said to be the cause behind the attack on members of prosecution party - From depositions of prosecution witnesses it also came to light that there were a number of killings on both sides - Though all accused were named in FIR and also in depositions of the prosecution witnesses, the witnesses appear to be careful not to attribute any specific role or any specific weapon to any of accused and all 14accused were mentioned in an ominous way - These were the facts and circumstances that seemed to have weighed with HC in appreciating the prosecution evidence and taking the view that prosecution witnesses did not appear natural and did not inspire confidence - HC examined prosecution witnesses in considerable detail and had observed that in the background of the long and very hostile enmity between 2sides and witnesses being highly interested, it would not be safe to rest the respondents' conviction on their evidences - Hence, SC was unable to hold that the view taken by HC was unreasonable or implausible -Appeal dismissed.
