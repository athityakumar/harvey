U.P. Power Corporation Ltd. & Ors. v Anis Ahmad

Supreme Court of India

01 July 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 368; (2013) 8 SCC 491; AIR 2013 SC 2766; 2013 (5) ALD(SC) 130; 2013 (100) ALR 736; 2013 (5) AWC 5238; 2013 (5) Bom.C.R. 76; 2013 (3) CLT(SC) 55; 2013 (3) CompLJ 366; 2013 (3) CPJ(Sc) 1; JT 2013 (10) SC 610; 2013 (6) MLJ 80; 2014(2) M.P.H.T. 390; 2014 (1) OLR 68; 2013 (3) RCR(Civil) 946; 2013(9) SCALE 334

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection

Keywords: Trader, U.P. Electricity (Consumers) Regulation, 1984

Summary: (1) Consumer Protection - Consumer Protection Act, 1986 (1986 Act)-Sections 2 (1) (c), 2 (1) (d), 2 (1) (e) and 3-Electricity Act, 2003 (2003 Act)-Sections 126, 135 to 140 and 153-Acts of indulgence in "unauthorised use of electricity"-Complaint against final order of assessment made by assessing officer under Section 126 or against offences committed under Sections 135 to 140 of 2003 Act before Consumer Forum-Maintainability-No allegation as stipulated under Section 2 (1) (c) of 1986 Act-Acts of "unauthorized use of electricity"-Do not fall within meaning of "complaint" as defined under Section 2 (1) (c) of 1986 Act-Therefore, complaint against assessment under Section 126 of 2003 Act-Not maintainable before Consumer Forum-Offences under Sections 135 to 140 of 2003 Act can be tried only by Special Court constituted under Section 153 of 2003 Act-In that view of matter also complaint against any action taken under Sections 135 to 140 of 2003 Act-Not maintainable before Consumer Forum-Impugned order of National Commission-Set aside.

(2) Consumer Protection - Consumer Protection Act, 1986-Sections 2 (1) (d) and 2 (1) (c)-"Consumer" within meaning of Section 2 (1) (d)-Complaint-Complainants/respondents enjoying electrical connections for industrial/commercial purpose-Do not come within meaning of "consumer" as defined under Section 2 (1) (d)-They cannot be treated as "complainant"-Nor they are entitled to file any "complaint" before Consumer Forum.

(3) Consumer Protection - Consumer Protection Act, 1986-Sections 2 (1) (d) and 2 (1) (c)-Supply of electrical or other energy-Complaint under Section 2 (1) (c)-Consumer within meaning under Section 2 (1) (d) may file valid complaint in respect of supply of electrical or other energy-If complaint contains allegation of unfair trade practice or restrictive trade practice ; or there is defective goods ; deficiency in services ; hazardous services or price in excess of price fixed by or under any law etc.

(4) Consumer Protection - Consumer Protection Act, 1986 (1986 Act)-Section 2 (1) (e)-Electricity Act, 2003 (2003 Act)-Section 126-'Consumer dispute'-Final decision by assessing officer on assessment of "unauthorised use of electricity" under Section 126 of 2003 Act-Is quasi-judicial decision-Does not fall within meaning of "consumer dispute" under Section 2 (1) (e).
