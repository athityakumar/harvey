Tripurari Sharan and another v Ranjit Kumar Yadav and others

Supreme Court of India

11 January 2018

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohan M. Shantanagoudar, Arun Mishra

Where Reported

2018 Indlaw SC 14

Case Digest

Subject: Education

Keywords: civil services, election, medical college

Summary: Education - Admission - Reserved category - Applicability - Appellants filed instant appeals challenging impugned order of HC whereby DB answered reference in the matter of validity or otherwise of admission process for MBBS/BDS and PG courses pursuant to a circular dt. 14-11-1995 of State Govt - Whether Meritorious Reserved Candidate (MRC) could opt for a seat earmarked for reserved category.

Held, in case of admission to PG medical institutions MRC who chooses to avail option of admission to a college with seats kept for the reserved category was deemed to have been admitted as an open category candidate and continues to be open category candidate. There was no migration into the reserved category even if a MRC opts for a seat earmarked for reserved category candidates. The lowest-ranking candidates who qualified in the reserved category, cannot hence have option for colleges/seats in reserved category on account of MRCs choice, might be adjusted against the choices of college seats then available in general category left over by MRC. However such reserved category candidates continue in reserved category, except for such option. Thus, by treating a MRC as a general category candidate, number of reserved seats remains the same and reservations do not exceed 50%. That was consistent with the principles of equity. To remedy the situation i.e. to provide the affected candidate a remedy, the 50th seat which would have been allotted to X - MRC, had he not opted for a seat meant for reserved category to which he belongs should be filled up by that candidate in the reserved category list who stands to lose out by the choice of MRC. That leaves the percentage of reservation at 50% undisturbed. SC could not find any reason to disagree with the conclusions reached by HC. Appeals dismissed.
