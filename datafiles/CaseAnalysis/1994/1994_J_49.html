Jilubhai Nanbhai Khachar and Others v State of Gujarat and Another

Supreme Court of India

20 July 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, N. Venkatachala

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 883; (1995) Supp1 SCC 596; AIR 1995 SC 142; JT 1994 (4) SC 473; 1994(3) SCALE 389; [1994] Supp1 S.C.R. 807

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Constitution - Land & Property - Mines & Minerals - Constitution (Seventh Amendment) Act, 1956 - Saurashtra Barkhali Abolition Act, 1951, ss. 2(7),(13), (15) and (18), 3 and 3(20), 4, 5 and 18 - Govt. of India Act, 1935, s. 299 - Constitution, 1950, arts. 14, 19 and 31 - Saurashtra Estates Acquisition Act, 1952, s. 2(c) - Urban Land (Ceiling and Regulation) Act, 1976, s. 11(6) - Requisitioning and Acquisition of Immovable Property Act, 1952, s. 8(3)(a) - Mines and Minerals (Regulation and Development) Act, 1957, s. 69-A - Land revenue - Land Tenure Abolition Laws - (A) Whether under Entry 54, of List I of the Seventh Schedule to the Constitution, since Regulation of Mines and Minerals Development Act, 1957 occupies the field of mines and minerals covered in s. 69-A of the Amendment Act, it is void and is ultra vires the Constitution? - Held, omitting the word 'alienated' from s. 69, it seeks to bring the mines, minerals or quarries situated in any land held by Girasdar or Barkhalidar etc. within the ambit of reservation u/s. 69 r/w. s. 69-A(1) of the Code, therefore in pith and substance, the predominant purpose of the Amendment Act is to extinguish the pre-existing rights, title and interests in the land which includes the mines, minerals and quarries held by Girasdars or Barkhalidars extinguished their rights and reserved and vested them in the State of Gujarat for public use - It would thereby fall within Entries 18 and 23 of List II (State List) read with Entry 42 of List III (Concurrent List) - (B) Whether Amendment Act though seeks to cover mines, minerals and quarries reserved u/s. 69 of the Code from lands engrafted in s. 69-A of the Code, without suitably amending the relevant provisions in the Acts, s. 69-A does not affect the rights of the appellants and the like? - Held, contention, therefore, that without amendment to the aforementioned four Acts the amendment becomes inoperative is devoid of substance - The analogy of special law prevails over Amendment Act and the Code, a general law, renders little assistance to the appellants - (C) Whether limited retrospective operation of ss. 69 and 69-A with effect from 01-05-1960 is illegal and ultra vires, lacks force? - Held, language in s. 2 of the Amendment Act, the express retrospective operation given to the Amendment Act, with effect from 01-05-1960 retrospectively affected vested rights of the Girasdar or Barkhalidars created by a grant or agreement etc - therefore retrospective operation cannot be faulted nor would it be declared ultra vires - (D) Whether s. 69-A of the Code though was included in the Ninth Schedule, is not a law relating to agrarian reforms and, therefore, it does not get the protection of art. 31-A? - Held, Amendment Act received its protective canopy of Ninth Schedule in Entry 219 thereof through the Constitution 66th Amendment Act, 1990, with effect from 07-06-1990 - Amendment Act is part of the scheme of agrarian reform envisaged under the Act falling within Entries 18 and 23 of List II (State List) and Entry 42 of Concurrent List of the Seventh Schedule to the Constitution. So it is saved by art. 31-A of the Constitution - Appeal disposed of.
