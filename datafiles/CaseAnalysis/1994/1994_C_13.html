Grauer & Weil (India) Ltd. v C.C.E.

Supreme Court of India

26 October 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

M.K. Mukherjee, R.M. Sahai

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1785; (1995) 1 SCC 77; AIR 1995 SC 543; AIR 1994 SCW 4808; 1995 (49) ECC 1; 1994 (55) ECR 420; 1994 (74) E.L.T. 481; JT 1994 (7) SC 284; 1995 (2) LLJ 648; 1994 (4) RLT 85; 1994(4) SCALE 701; [1994] Supp5 S.C.R. 168

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Excise - Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944, ss. 11 A(1), Proviso, ch. I, item 68 - Central Excise Rules, 1944, r. 8(1) - Factories Act, 1948, s. 2(m) - Excise Duty - (A) Notification No. 46/81 D/- 01-03-1981 - Whether goods manufactured in separate building forming a unit of factory premises, if exempt from duty - (B) Avoidance of tax - Manufacturer willfully and deliberately making mis-statements and suppressing material facts to avoid payment of excise duty - Applicability of s. 11 A(1) of Excise Act considering fact that it is not a case of simple inaction or failure to furnish material information - (C) Imposition of penalty on manufacturer claiming exemption from duty - But deliberately suppressing material facts to avoid payment of duty - Held, not exempt from duty even if less than ten workers are employed therein - Excise duty could be recovered for period exceeding six months prior to date of show ause notice by invoking Proviso to s. 1 A(1) - Liable to penalty - Appeal dismissed.
