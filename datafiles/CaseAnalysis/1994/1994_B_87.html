State of Mizoram v Biakchhawana

Supreme Court of India

07 October 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, N. Venkatachala

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 471; (1995) 1 SCC 156; AIR 1995 SCW 1497; 1995 (1) CCC 231; JT 1994 (7) SC 472; 1995 (1) RentLR 111; 1994(4) SCALE 948; [1994] Supp4 S.C.R. 421; 1995 (1) UJ 253

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Civil Procedure Code, 1908 - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4(1), 5, 11, 17, 18, 19 and 20 - Land acquisition - Respondent received compensation under protest and filed an application but Collector did not make any reference to Civil Court u/s. 18 of Act - Civil Suit by respondent decreed by DJ - In Appeal to High Court appellant challenged jurisdiction and validity of judgment and decree, which dismissed - Validity of HC order challenged - Held, at time of disposing of award proceeding, Land Acquisition Collector or court on reference u/s. 18 of Act is required by statutory commendations to follow substance of provisions of Land Acquisition Act - Further held, procedure prescribed in para 3 of notification is not in derogation of mandatory compliance u/ss. 18 to 20, and Civil Court does not get valid and legal jurisdiction to take cognizance of objection for higher compensation unless procedure prescribed in ss. 18, 19, 20 and 31 are complied with and adhered to - Appeal allowed.
