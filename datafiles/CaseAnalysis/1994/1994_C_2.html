C. Narayanaswamy and another v C. K. Jaffer Sharief and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 August 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

N.P. Singh, Kuldip Singh, P.B. Sawant

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 998; (1994) Supp3 SCC 170; AIR 1994 SCW 3799; JT 1994 (5) SC 136; 1994(3) SCALE 674; [1994] Supp2 S.C.R. 463

Case Digest

Subject: Election; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Election Expenses, Electorate, Incurred By The Candidate

Summary: Election - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 77, 83(1)(a)(b), 100(1)(d) and 123 - Corrupt Practices - Whether election of respondent could be held invalid on grounds of corrupt practices and improper counting of votes polled? - Held, pleading that 'mass feeding was either arranged by respondent or his election agent or his political party or other persons with consent of respondent or his election agent', is vague, does not give any particulars and does not meet requirements of s. 83(1)(a)(b) - If mass feeding had not been held against background of Rajiv Gandhi's assassination, SC could have examined as to whether arranging feasts before or during election amounted to corrupt practice - In special facts and circumstances of case, it is not possible to hold respondent guilty of charge of bribery, by arranging mass feeding - Anybody may spend any amount over election of any candidate in whom such person is interested, for which no account is to be maintained or to be furnished and any such expenditure is not deemed to be expenditure in connection with election, incurred or authorized by candidate or by his election agent for purpose of s. 77(1), so as to amount to a corrupt practice within meaning of s. 123(6) - Appellant, except stating details of expenditure over different advertisements etc, in connection with election, has not produced even prima facie evidence to establish that such expenditure had been actually incurred by respondent - Appellant has not discharged onus placed on him to produce materials in support of his assertion - Merely because in Form 16, instead of 'A', '1' has been mentioned as prefix to numbers of ballot papers beyond first 10 lakhs, it cannot be held that ballot papers beyond number of 999999 were spurious and were used by respondent in surreptitious manner, which has materially affected result of election - Appeals dismissed.
