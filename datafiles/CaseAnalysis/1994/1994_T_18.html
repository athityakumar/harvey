State of Tamil Nadu v T.V. Venugopala

Supreme Court of India

03 August 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, N. Venkatachala

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 612; (1994) 6 SCC 302; (1994) SCC (L&S) 1385; AIR 1994 SCW 3947; 1994 (28) ATC 294; 1994 (69) FLR 740; JT 1994 (5) SC 337; 1994 LabIC 2498; 1995 (1) MLJ(SC) 22; 1994(3) SCALE 667; 1994 (3) SLJ 233; 1994 (4) SLR 628; [1994] Supp2 S.C.R. 411

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Service - Practice & Procedure - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Date of birth - Alteration of - Whether the respondent can be allowed to seek alteration of the date of birth when he was due for superannuation? - Inordinate delay in making the application for correction of date of birth is itself a ground for rejecting of the application - Held, Tribunal has grossly erred in showing overindulgence in granting the reliefs even trenching beyond its powers of allowing him to remain in office for two years after his date of superannuation even as per his own case and given all conceivable directions beneficial to the employee - Appeal allowed.
