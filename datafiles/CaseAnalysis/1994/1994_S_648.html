A. K. Nigam and Others v Sunil Misra and Another

Supreme Court of India

13 May 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

Yogeshwar Dayal, Kuldip Singh

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1546; (1994) Supp2 SCC 245; (1994) SCC (L&S) 970; 1994 (27) ATC 665; 1994 (69) FLR 303; JT 1994 (4) SC 31; 1994(2) SCALE 1030; 1995 (1) SLJ 89; 1994 (4) SLR 20; [1994] Supp1 S.C.R. 126

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Seniority - Tribunal held that appellants could not be ranked senior to respondent 1 - Challenge before Tribunal was to grant of weightage of 5 years to appellants for purposes of seniority as per principle (vii) - Appeal against order of Tribunal - Held, it is settled law that appointing authority or appropriate Govt. can frame rules governing seniority which are reasonable keeping in mind divergent claims that can be put forward by various categories of members of service - However, there should be no discrimination, that is, persons placed in same group must be treated similarly and, further, that any principle which is made basis of determination of seniority should, if applicable to others, be applied to them also - Further held, seniority is to depend purely upon date of confirmation or date of appointment, that rule should be applicable to all; but if promotees and special recruits are being given weightage, principles applicable to members of service should be kept in mind while determining weightage to be given or while laying down rules for determination of seniority - Appeal allowed.
