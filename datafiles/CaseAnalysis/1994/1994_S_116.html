U. P. Bhumi Sudhar Nigam Limited v Shiv Narain Gupta

Supreme Court of India

11 July 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

Kuldip Singh, A.S. Anand

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1793; (1994) Supp2 SCC 541; (1994) SCC (L&S) 1146; 1994 (28) ATC 67; 1994 (2) CLR 411; 1994 (69) FLR 403; JT 1994 (4) SC 374; 1995 (2) LLJ 56; 1994 (2) LLN 384; 1994(3) SCALE 194; 1995 (1) SLJ 9; 1994 (4) SLR 461; [1994] Supp1 S.C.R. 317; 1994 (3) UPLBEC 1593

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950 - Recruitments - Selection criteria - Corporation advertised for the post of Financial Controller - As a result of the interviews held by the selection committee, a panel of three names was recommended to Corporation - Respondent in the appeal herein, was at number two - An appointment letter was issued to first candidate and he was asked to join the post, but he failed to join the post - Respondent represented before Corporation that he, being next on the merit panel, be considered for appointment to the post - When no action was taken by Corporation for considerable time, respondent filed a W.P. u/art. 226 of Constitution - W.P. was allowed by a learned Single Judge of the HC and a mandamus was issued to Corporation to appoint respondent to the post of Financial Controller - Hence, the present appeal - Whether Corporation was right in rejecting respondent - Held, on facts and circumstances of this case, the Corporation acted bona fide and was justified in not offering the appointment to respondent - HC fell into patent error in issuing the mandamus - This Court had authoritatively laid down that even if a vacancy was available and the employer bona fide declined to make an appointment, the candidate on the select list had no right whatsoever to claim appointment - Here, the post was abolished by the Board of Directors - Respondent in fact challenged before the HC the action of Corporation in abolishing the post - Neither the facts of this case nor the law on the subject warranted any interference by the HC in the W.P. filed by respondent - Appeal allowed.
