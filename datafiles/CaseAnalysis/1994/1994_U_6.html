State of Uttar Pradesh and Others v U. P. State Law Officers Association and Others

Supreme Court of India

25 January 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

P.B. Sawant, N. Venkatachala

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1289; (1994) 2 SCC 204; (1994) SCC (L&S) 650; AIR 1994 SC 1654; AIR 1994 SCW 1389; 1994 (26) ATC 906; 1994 (1) AWC 645; 1994 (68) FLR 894; JT 1994 (1) SC 225; 1994(1) SCALE 254; [1994] 1 S.C.R. 348; 1994 (1) SLJ 230; 1994 (1) SLR 533; 1994 (1) UJ 412; 1994 (1) UPLBEC 595

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Labour & Industrial Law; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Advocate & Judges - Service - Uttar Pradesh Legal Remembrance's Manual, chap. V, Para 5, chap. VI, Para 6.02 - Bombay Prohibition Act 1949 - (A) Whether termination of Law officers engaged by State Govt. to conduct cases in High Court is arbitrary - Stipulation that appointment terminable at any time without assigning reasons - (B) Validity of Govt. order abolishing system of brief holders - And granting power to Legal Remembrancer to appoint special counsel for special matters - Held, not arbitrary - Is proper - Appeal allowed.
