Ravi S. Naik v Union Of India

Supreme Court of India

09 February 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Agrawal, M.N. Venkatachaliah

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 371; (1994) Supp2 SCC 641; AIR 1994 SC 1558; AIR 1994 SCW 1214; JT 1994 (1) SC 551; 1994(1) SCALE 487; [1994] 1 S.C.R. 754

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Parliament & Legislature

Keywords: Disqualification On Grounds Of Defection, Court Of Limited Jurisdiction

Summary: Constitution of India, Sch.10, Para 2(1)(a); Goa Legislative Assembly (Disqualification on Grounds of Defection) Rules, 1986, rr.6 and 7 - Defection - Party membership - Disqualification Rules not followed - Finality of declaration upon disqualification - Subject to judicial review - 'voluntarily given up his membership' - Held, not synonymous with resignation - Has wider connotation - Rules being procedural cannot be equated with constitutional mandate - No ground for judicial review.
