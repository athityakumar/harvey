Tarapore And Co. v State Of M.P.

Supreme Court of India

16 February 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

B.L. Hansaria, B.P. Jeevan Reddy

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1334; (1994) 3 SCC 521; 1994 (1) ARBLR 341; 1995 (2) CCC 429; 1994 (68) FLR 816; JT 1994 (2) SC 162; 1994(1) SCALE 612; [1994] 1 S.C.R. 1012; 1994 (1) UJ 449

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration Act, 1940, ss. 16(1)(c), 20(1), 30(a) - Minimum Wages Act, 1948 - State approached District Judge to set aside award - District Judge did not set aside whole award and remitted same as contemplated u/s. 16(1)(c) of Act - State again approached District Judge to set aside award - District Judge set aside award - Appellant appealed to High Court which held that arbitrators ought to have given their finding insofar as primary question of liability was concerned which, however, was not done - Whether on face of two awards can it be said that arbitrators had acted beyond jurisdiction in awarding sum? - Held, Courts below was not correct view to take that award merited to be set aside on ground that award was beyond jurisdiction of arbitrators, insofar as increased payment on account of rise in rates of fair wages are concerned reverse this finding qua rise in rates of fair wages, while sustaining same as regards rise in rates of minimum wages - Appeal allowed.
