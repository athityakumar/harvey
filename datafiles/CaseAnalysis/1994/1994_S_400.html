 State of Haryana v Messrs Suman Enterprises and Others

Supreme Court of India

21 April 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, M.N. Venkatachaliah, P.B. Sawant, S. Mohan, Yogeshwar Dayal

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 2088; (1994) 4 SCC 217; 1994(2) SCALE 844

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Miscellaneous - Executive order promulgated by State of Tamil Nadu prohibiting sale of lottery tickets of other States - Order clearly implies "if it did not, it would have required order to be read down to mean that prohibition does not extend to sale of lottery tickets of lotteries 'organised' by other States" - Whether lottery claimed to have been 'organised' by State of Sikkim can be said to be a lottery 'organised' by State of Sikkim and not merely authorised by it authorising so-called 'Agents' themselves to organise lottery? - Basic functions characterising a State-organised lottery are - (1) Tickets which bear imprint and logo of State must be printed by or directly at instance of State Govt. so as to ensure their authenticity and genuineness and further to ensure that any possibility of duplication of tickets and sale of fake tickets is provided against and rendered impossible - (2) State itself must sell tickets though, if it thinks necessary or proper so to do, through a sole distributor or selling agent or several agents or distributors under terms and conditions regulated by agreement reached between parties - Sale proceeds of tickets either sold in retail or wholesale shall be credited to funds of Govt. - (3) Draws for selecting prize-winning tickets must be conducted by State itself, irrespective of size of prize money - (4) If any prize money is unclaimed or is otherwise not distributed by way of prize, it must revert to and become property of State Govt. - If basic and essential feature indicated above are ensured, it might be possible to raise a presumption that lottery is one that could be said to have been 'organised' by State itself of and not one merely authorised by State under which so-called 'agent' himself organises lottery - It will not be possible at interlocutory stage to hold that Sikkim Scheme is outside State power of regulation of 'Betting and Gambling' and does not attract ban contemplated by Tamil Nadu Govt. - Decision to be given on final hearing - Order accordingly.
