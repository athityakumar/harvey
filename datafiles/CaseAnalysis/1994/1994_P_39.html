C.S.T. v Pine Chemicals Ltd.

Supreme Court of India

24 October 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, M.N. Venkatachaliah, S. Mohan

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1140; (1995) 1 SCC 58; AIR 1995 SCW 1718; JT 1994 (7) SC 206; 1994(4) SCALE 711B; 1995 (96) STC 355; [1994] Supp5 S.C.R. 123

Case Digest

Subject: Electricity & Energy; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure; Sales Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Jammu and Kashmir General Sales Tax Act - Central Sales Tax Act, ss. 5 and 8(2-A) - Whether it was rightly held that benefit of s. 8(2-A) is available even where goods are exempted with reference to industrial unit and for a specified period, viz, period of five years from date relevant unit goes into production? - For attracting the exemption provided by the govt. order, it has to be established that (i) the goods, the sale or purchase of which is claimed to be exempt from tax, are manufactured by a large or medium scale industry and (ii) that the said goods are manufactured and sold within five years from the date the said industrial unit has gone into production - Idea behind s. 8(2-A) is to exempt sale/purchase of goods from Central sales tax where sale or purchase of such goods is exempt generally, under State sales tax law - So long as it is a large or medium scale industry and manufactures and sells goods within five years of its going into production, sale of such goods is exempt irrespective of nature or classification of goods - Held, exemption granted under the govt. order does not satisfy the requirements of s. 8(2-A) - Order accordingly.
