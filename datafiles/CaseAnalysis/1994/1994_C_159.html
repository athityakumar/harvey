Common Cause, A Registered Society v Union of India and others

Supreme Court of India

07 December 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, R.M. Sahai, N. Venkatachala

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 2216; (2006) 9 SCC 304

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Legality, Strike by legal profession

Summary: Advocated & Judges - Practice & Procedure - Bar Council of India (Conduct and Disciplinary) Rules - Strike by legal profession - Legality - Whether or not members of legal profession should resort to strike or abstain from appearing in cases in Court in which they were engaged - Held, Court might see working of interim arrangement and if that was found to be satisfactory it might perhaps not be required to go into wider question at this stage - Suggestions were emerged as an interim measure consistent with Bar Council of India's thinking that except in rarest of rare cases strike should not be resorted to and instead peaceful demonstration might be resorted to, to avoid causing hardship to litigant public - Senior Advocate representing Bar Council of India was good enough to state that he would suggest to Bar Council of India to incorporate cls. (1), (2), (3) and (4) in the Rules, so that it should have statutory support should there be any violation or contravention of aforementioned four clauses - Accordingly SC made cls. (1) to (4) mentioned above order of Court and directed further course of action in terms thereof - Same would operate prospectively - SC suggested to Bar Councils and Bar Associations that in order to clear pitch and to uphold high traditions of profession as well as to maintain unity and integrity of Bar they consider dropping action already initiated against their members who had appeared in Courts notwithstanding strike calls given by any Bar Council or Bar Association - Besides, members of legal profession should be alive to possibility of judges of different Courts refusing adjournments merely on ground of there being strike call and insisting on proceeding with cases - Order accordingly.
