C.W.S. (India) Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax

Supreme Court of India

01 March 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, B.L. Hansaria

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1887; (1994) Supp2 SCC 296; 1994 (118) CTR 118; [1994] 208 ITR 649; JT 1994 (3) SC 116; 1994(1) SCALE 840; [1994] 2 S.C.R. 257; [1994] 73 TAXMAN 174

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961 - Depreciation allowance - Provisions which were in force successively from 1-4-1963 to 31-3-1989 were enacted to discourage assessees from incurring expenditure which resulted directly or indirectly in provision of any benefit, amenity or perquisite to their employees beyond a particular limit - S. 40(a)(v) of 1961 Act was only an expanded version of s. 40(c)(iii) 1961 Act - S.40(c)(iii) of 1961 Act did not cover such allowances and this was sought to be remedied - Whether Parliament intended differently when it put in s. 40(a)(v)of the 1961 Act in the place of s. 40(c)(iii) of the 1961 Act - Held, controversy was limited only to s. 40(a)(v) and only because of use of words such employee interpretation of s. 40(a)(v) of 1961 Act - Mere use of the word such in cl.(ii) of s. 40(a)(v) of 1961 Act, should not have the effect of driving the court to place an interpretation upon said clause which was not only discriminatory but was highly incongruous - Appeal partly allowed.
