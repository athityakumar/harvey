Oil And Natural Gas Commission v Utpal Kumar Basu

Supreme Court of India

23 June 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, B.P. Jeevan Reddy, M.N. Venkatachaliah

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1574; (1994) 4 SCC 711; AIR 1994 SCW 3287; 1995 (1) CalLT(SC) 5; 1994 (4) CompLJ 203; 1994 (2) CTJ 577; JT 1994 (5) SC 1; 1994 (2) RentLR 198; 1994(3) SCALE 90; [1994] Supp1 S.C.R. 252

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Indirect Tax; Service

Keywords: Distribution Of Assets

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, s. 21 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 32 and 226 - EIL acting as consultants for ONGC invited tenders at New Delhi - NICCO, having its registered office in Calcutta, submitted their offer or bid in response to tender notice - NICCO's bid was rejected on experience criteria stipulated in tender - NICCO filed Writ Petition in High Court of Calcutta - Whether HC lack territorial jurisdiction? - Held, merely because it read advertisement at Calcutta and submitted offer from Calcutta and made representations from Calcutta and it sent fax messages from Calcutta and received a reply thereto at Calcutta would not constitute facts forming an integral part of cause of action - Therefore, it cannot be said that a part of cause of action arose within jurisdiction of Calcutta HC - Appeal allowed.
