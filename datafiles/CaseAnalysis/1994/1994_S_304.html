Shankar v State Of T.N.

Supreme Court of India

04 April 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Jayachandra Reddy, G.N. Ray

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1465; (1994) 4 SCC 478; (1994) SCC (Cr) 1252; AIR 1994 SCW 2083; 1994 (2) ALT(SC) 429; 1994 (2) CCR 469; 1994 (2) Crimes 1; 1994 CRLJ 3071; JT 1994 (3) SC 54; 1994(2) SCALE 435; [1994] 3 S.C.R. 298

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Tort

Keywords: Approver, Brothel, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120 B, 302, 201, 147, 34 and 404 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 133, 114 and 27 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, s. 313 - Appellants accused of conspiracy and murder - Convicted by Trial Court - Confirmed by High Court - A1-3 sentenced to death - (A) Whether evidence of approver is full of contradictions and is not corroborated in material particulars by independent evidence? - An accomplice namely a guilty associate in crime is a competent witness - S. 133 IE Act lays down that conviction based on uncorroborated testimony of an accomplice is not illegal, but rule of guidance indicted in Illustration (b) to s. 114 IE Act has resulted in settled practice to require corroboration of evidence of an accomplice which has now virtually assumed force of a rule of law - An accomplice by becoming an approver becomes a prosecution witness " Accomplice's" evidence must be reliable and his evidence should be sufficiently corroborated - Ordinarily approver's statement has to be corroborated in material particulars bridging closely distance between crime and criminal and furnishing need and assurance for acceptance of his testimony - Independent corroboration need not also cover whole of prosecution story or even whole of material particulars - So far as murder of D-1 is concerned, he did not participate in actual occurrence of causing death - So far as murders of D-2 to D-6 are concerned, PW 1 was a direct participant - There is overwhelming evidence which corroborates version of PW 1.
