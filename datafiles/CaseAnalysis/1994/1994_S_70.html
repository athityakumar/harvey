Bhanwari Lal v Satyanarain And Another

Supreme Court of India

04 October 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, S.C. Agrawal, N. Venkatachala

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 436; (1995) 1 SCC 6; AIR 1995 SC 358; 1995 (1) BLJR 57; 1994 (3) CCC 282; JT 1994 (6) SC 626; 1995 RD 144; 1995 (1) RentLR 95; 1994(4) SCALE 597; 1995 (1) SCJ 237; [1994] Supp4 S.C.R. 208

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Ground Of Res Judicata

Summary: Civil Procedure - Limitation Act, 1963 - CPC, 1908, O 21, rr 35(3) and 97(1) - Execution of decree for ejectment - Obstruction by person who was neither judgment-debtor nor claiming title from judgment-debtor - Removal of obstruction sought by decree-holder u/O 21, r 35(3) - Whether order dismissing second and third application filed by decree-holder as barred by limitation and by rule of res judicata respectively was erroneous - Held, it is duty of executing court to consider averments in petition and also consider applicability of relevant rule - Dismissal order was erroneous - Appeal allowed.
