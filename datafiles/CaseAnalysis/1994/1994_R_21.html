Transport Commr. v Radha K. Moorthy

Supreme Court of India

01 December 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, S.C. Sen

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 2029; (1995) SCC (L&S) 313; (1995) 1 SCC 332; AIR 1995 SCW 1555; 1995 (29) ATC 112; 1995 (1) CLR 377; JT 1994 (7) SC 744; 1995 LabIC 1749; 1995 (1) LLN 776; 1994(5) SCALE 59; 1995 (1) SLR 239

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Service - Termination - Legality - (A) Jurisdiction of the Tribunal in so far as the truth and correctness of charges - At the stage prior to the conclusion of the disciplinary enquiry - Held, when the matter comes to the Tribunal after the imposition of punishment, it has no jurisdiction to go into truth of the allegations/charges except in a case where they are based on no evidence and perverse - Jurisdiction of the Tribunal is akin to that of the High Court u/art. 226 of the Constitution - It is power of judicial review and only examines the procedural correctness of the decision making process - (B) Enquiry by an officer subordinate to appointing authority - Validity - Held, initiation can be by an officer subordinate to the appointing authority - Only the dismissal/removal shall not be by an authority subordinate to the appointing authority - (C) Vagueness of Charges - Charges being accompanied by a statement of particulars or other statement - Charges against the respondent are not specific and clear - Held, charges are vague - Appeal dismissed.
