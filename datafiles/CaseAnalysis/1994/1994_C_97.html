Cosmic Dye Chemical v Collector of Central Excise, Bombay

Supreme Court of India

06 September 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, S.C. Sen, K.S. Paripoornan

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 282; (1995) 6 SCC 117; 1995 (58) ECR 232; 1995 (75) E.L.T. 721; 1995 (6) RLT 333; 1994 (95) STC 604

Case Digest

Subject: Excise

Summary: Excise - Central Excises And Salt Act, 1944 - Exemption under Notification No. 71/78 dt. 1.03.1978 - Appellant-manufacturer of two kinds of dyes, claimed benefit of exemption notification no. 71/78 submitting that the value of the excisable goods manufactured by him during the year 1977-78 was Rs. 3,25,200 only - Asst. Collector rejected said claim on the ground that the appellant had submitted false declaration to the effect that his production of excisable goods was not in excess of Rs. 15 lacs, however, he had not included the income of one dye in the declaration filed by him for the purpose of Notification No. 71/78 - The Collector dismissed the appeal preferred by the appellant - Appellant filed appeal against said order submitting that he was under bona fide impression that inasmuch as Rapidogens dye was exempt from duty by virtue of Notification No. 180/61, value of Rapidogens dye manufactured by him need not be included in declaration filed by him for purpose of Notification No. 71/78 - Tribunal also dismissed appeal filed against said order and held that regardless of intent, a mere suppression of facts or misstatement in information statutorily required to be supplied to excise authorities attract larger period of limitation - Hence, present appeal - Whether tribunal was right in holding that regardless of intent, a mere suppression of facts or misstatement in information statutorily required to be supplied to excise authorities attract larger period of limitation? - Held, so far as misstatement or suppression of facts are concerned, they are clearly qualified by the word "wilful" preceding the words "misstatement or suppression of facts" which means with intent to evade duty - In the present case, appellant thought bona fide that he need not include the value of the Rapidogens dye in his declaration, for the reason that the said product was fully exempt from duty under Notification No. 180/61- Hence, misstatement of facts in the declaration filed by the appellant or the suppression of facts therein, as the case may be cannot be called wilful - Appeal allowed.
