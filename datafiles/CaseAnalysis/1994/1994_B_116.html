R.M. Investments and Trading Company Private Limited v Boeing Company

Supreme Court of India

10 February 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Agrawal, M.K. Mukherjee

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 378; (1994) 4 SCC 541; AIR 1994 SC 1136; 1994 (1) ARBLR 282; 1994 (1) CalLT 19; 1994 (1) CalLT(SC) 19; [1994] 80 Comp Cas 588; 1994 (54) DLT 667; JT 1994 (1) SC 615; [1994] 1 S.C.R. 837

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Corporate; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Foreign Award

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Foreign Awards (Recognition and Enforcement) Act, 1961, s. 3 - (A) Whether High Court was right in allowing Respondent's application u/s. 3 for stay of proceedings? - (B) Whether rendering of services by RMI for promoting a commercial transaction as consultant is a itself a 'commercial transaction'? - Act is designed to sub serve cause of facilitating international trade and promotion thereof by providing for speedy settlement of disputes arising in such trade through arbitration and construction of word 'commercial' should receive, consistent with its literal and grammatical sense, a liberal construction - Expression 'commercial' should be construed broadly having regard to manifold activities which are integral part of international trade - Held, HC was right in holding that agreement to render consultancy services by RMI is commercial in nature and that RMI and Boeing stand in commercial relation - SLP (Civil) No. 121 - HC was right in setting aside order of Single Judge allowing application for amendment of plaint and impleadment of Air India as defendant - No infirmity is found in HC's order - All three SLPs dismissed.
