A.P.State Of Financial Corpn v C.M. Ashok Raju

Supreme Court of India

12 July 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

Kuldip Singh, K. Jayachandra Reddy

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1707; (1994) 5 SCC 359; (1994) SCC (L&S) 1104; AIR 1995 SC 39; AIR 1994 SCW 4037; 1994 (27) ATC 878; 1994 (69) FLR 826; JT 1994 (5) SC 481; 1995 (2) LLJ 117; 1994 (2) LLN 1081; 1994(3) SCALE 149; 1995 (1) SLJ 37; 1994 (4) SLR 442; [1994] Supp1 S.C.R. 474

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Constitution - State Financial Corporations Act, 1951, s. 48 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 136 and 226 - Writ appeal - Special Leave Petitions against decision thereon - Supreme Court while rejecting petitions as withdrawn prima facie found contentions of petitioner to be plausible and, as such, granted liberty to raise same before High Court - HC heard parties at length and passed reasoned order - Criteria of promotion to post of Manager and above (A) Whether decision of HC in writ appeal has achieved finality? - (B) No written test was prescribed - Whether allocation of 25% marks for interview arbitrary or against law? - (C) Propriety of allocation of 50% marks for performance appraisal based on confidential reports? - (D) Allocation of 15% marks for length of service over a period of 5 years - Propriety of finding that allocation was on lesser side, not based on reasoning - Held, (A) in circumstances it cannot be said that decision of HC has achieved finality - (B) Not arbitrary or against law - C) Not improper - (D) Not proper - Appeal partly allowed.
