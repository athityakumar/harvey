Bachan Kaur and others v Bhagwan Kaur and others

Supreme Court of India

28 April 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

M.M. Punchhi, S.C. Agrawal, B.P. Jeevan Reddy

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 330; (1994) 5 SCC 135

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Family & Personal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Succession & Inheritance

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 63 and 68 - Indian Succession Act, 1925 - Registration Act, 1908 - Whether requirements of s. 63 of Indian Succession Act and s. 68 of Indian Evidence Act, 1872 had been satisfied as regards Will in question? - Held, statements of two witnesses being contradictory, should evidence of these two witnesses be discarded then due execution of will cannot be said to have been proved - Even requirements of s. 68 of Indian Evidence Act, 1872 are not met - Mere registration of will said to have been executed by Bachan Singh is of no consequence - Appeal allowed.
