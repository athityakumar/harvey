Anshad v State Of Karnataka

Supreme Court of India

22 April 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

A.S. Anand, Faizan Uddin

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 453; (1994) 4 SCC 381; (1994) SCC (Cr) 1204; JT 1994 (3) SC 324; [1994] 3 S.C.R. 642

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, ss. 235 and 354 - Evidence Act, s. 27 - Indian Penal Code, ss. 307, 395, 396 and 449 A-1 to 5 convicted for offences u/ss. 307, 395, 396 and 449, IPC - High Court enhanced sentence of 1,2 and 3 to death sentence - Question of conviction and sentence - Held, possession of a wrist-watch and a gold ring belonging to deceased not sufficient material to hold guilty of being participants in crime of murder 4 and 5 can only be held liable for being in possession of stolen property and, thus, guilty of an offence u/s. 411, IPC - Both Trial Court and HC failed to properly appreciate inherent infirmities in prosecution evidence regarding 4 and 5 and fell in error in convicting them for murder 4 and A-5 convicted u/s. 411, IPC - Further held, reasons given by HC to enhance sentence of life imprisonment to death, without taking into account all circumstances and balancing aggravating and mitigating circumstances, are neither, special reasons nor otherwise adequate and sufficient to impose death sentence - Conviction of A-1, A-2 and A-3 is altered to one u/ss. 302/34 IPC, 394/34 IPC and 379/34, IPC - Appeal partly allowed.
