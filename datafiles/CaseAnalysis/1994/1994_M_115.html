Selvarajan v Meenakshi Ammal

Supreme Court of India

05 October 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, N. Venkatachala

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 791; (1995) Supp2 SCC 51; 1995 (1) HLR 437; 1996 (1) RCJ 252; 1995 (2) RCR(Rent) 120; 1994(4) SCALE 937; 1995 (1) SCJ 116; [1994] Supp4 S.C.R. 283

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Security Bond

Summary: Land & Property - Tamil Nadu Agriculturists Relief Act, 1938 as amended in 1973 - Indian Contract Act, s. 128 - Respondent instituted a suit as heir of her husband for recovery of possession of certain properties trespassed upon by two persons and also for an injunction in relation to certain other items - During pendency of suit, a Receiver was appointed who granted a lease of properties in favour of one 'S', who agreed to pay rent - Father of appellant, executed a security bond in favour in Court - Lessee 'S' committed default in payment of rents - Respondent filed application for assignment of security bond, which was granted - Respondent filed petition for enforcement of security against surety and appellant - Whether decree is one for recovery of debt which could be scaled down under provisions of Relief Act? - Held, u/s. 128 of Indian Contract Act, liability of a surety was coextensive with that of 'S' - Rent has been expressly excluded from definition of debt and liability of interest which sought to be down under Relief Act is a debt whether secured or unsecured due from agriculturists whether payable under a decree or an order of civil or revenue court or otherwise - Since principal debtor committed default in payment of rent to Receiver appellant's father had undertaken to discharge that liability - In consequence, both appellant and tenant, had suffered decree which was sought to be executed - Appeal dismissed.
