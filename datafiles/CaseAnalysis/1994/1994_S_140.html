Special Land Acquisition Officer and Another v Sidappa Omanna Tumari and Others

Supreme Court of India

27 October 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Venkatachala, K. Ramaswamy, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1150; (1995) Supp2 SCC 168; AIR 1995 SC 840; 1994 (3) CCC 463; JT 1994 (7) SC 257; 1994 (2) RentLR 686; 1994(4) SCALE 782; [1994] Supp5 S.C.R. 207

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4(1), 11 and 18 - Reference - Compensation - Determination of - Compensation exceeding amount determined by Collector in award u/s. 11 - Whether can be determined by Court? - Whether Court can ignore award passed by Collector in respect of some land covered under Notification u/s. 4(1) while determining compensation payable for other acquired lands covered by same notification? - Whether Court is bound to determine market value of large extent of acquired land for determining compensation payable? - Held, (A) can be determined by Court only on recording finding that amount of compensation determined under award was inadequate for reasons that weighed with it - (B) award passed by Collector cannot be ignored by Court - (C) Court is not always bound to do so - Appeal allowed.
