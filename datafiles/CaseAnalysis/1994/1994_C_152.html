Bharat Hari Singhania and Others v Commissioner of Wealth Tax (Central) and Others

Supreme Court of India

16 February 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, A.S. Anand, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1335; (1994) Supp3 SCC 46; AIR 1994 SC 1355; 1994 (118) CTR 125; [1994] 207 ITR 1; JT 1994 (2) SC 6; 1994(1) SCALE 644; [1994] 1 S.C.R. 1033; [1994] 73 TAXMAN 3; 1994 (2) TLR 417

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Finance (No.2) Act, 1980, Finance Act, 1982, Unquoted Equity Shares, Preference Shares

Summary: Civil - Direct Taxation - Wealth Tax Act, 1957, s. 24 - Wealth Tax Rules, 1957, r. 1-D - Petitioner challenged the vires of r. 1-D of the Wealth Tax Rules, 1957 - Rule prescribes the manner in which the market value of unquoted equity shares of companies is to be determined - All the liabilities deducted from the value of assets and the net amount shall be divided by total amount of its paid-up equity share capital - The result multiplied by the paid up value of each equity share shall be the break-up value of each unquoted equity share - Whether the r. 1-D is ultra vires - Held, no - Method adopted leads to proper determination of market value of unquoted shares - Such rule bound to be applied in calculation of wealth tax - Rule is outcome of exercise of powers of delegated legislation - Petition dismissed.
