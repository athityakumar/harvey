Suresh Chandra Bahri v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

13 July 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

Faizan Uddin, A.S. Anand

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 354; (1995) SCC (Cr) 60; (1995) Supp1 SCC 80; AIR 1994 SC 2420; 1994 (2) BLJR 1147; 1994 (2) CCR 636; 1994 (2) Crimes 1027; 1994 CRLJ 3271; JT 1994 (4) SC 309; 1994(3) SCALE 197; [1994] Supp1 S.C.R. 483; 1994 (2) UJ 782

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 313, 306 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120, 201, 302 - Murder - Death sentence - Motive - No mitigating circumstances - X (deceased) was appellant's wife - Deceased and appellant had strained relations - Deceased was desirous of selling property and migrating to America with children - To avoid deceased's interference and intermeddling in property that belonged to appellant and thwart accomplishment and foil wishes of deceased of migrating to America with their children with sale proceeds, appellant murdered deceased and children - Thereafter, appellant absconded but he was arrested - Appellant denied guilt and pleaded false implication - Statement u/s. 313 of CrPC stated that deceased and their two children were not murdered and that they were still alive - Appellant was convicted u/s. 302 of IPC for causing murder of deceased and their two children - Appellant's defence was that prosecution had failed to prove factum of death of deceased and two children and that in any case prosecution had failed to bring home guilt against appellant for alleged murder, thus he was implicated based on suspicion - Appellant and two others were convicted u/ss. 302/120-B of the IPC for offence of criminal conspiracy to commit murder - Appellant with another was further convicted u/s. 201 of IPC for causing disappearance of evidence of murder - Appellant was awarded death sentence for offences u/ss. 302, 302, 120-B of IPC and rigorous imprisonment for period of seven years for offence u/s. 201 of IPC - HC, Patna dismissed appeal and affirmed sentences awarded and accepted death reference by judgment - Hence, instant appeal - Whether lower courts were justified in taking view that appellant had strong motive to hatch conspiracy with assistance of two others to commit murder of deceased and their two children - Held, prosecution stated motive behind murder of deceased and two children was strained relations and differences between deceased and appellant and his family, which had developed on account of deceased's firm determination to dispose of property and migrate along with two children to America where her parents were already settled, as her life and that of two children had become miserable due to mental and physical tortures - Prosecution had also produced documentary evidence to support allegation that relations of appellant and deceased was strained - Provisions of s. 306(4)(a) of CrPC was complied with, thus no prejudice or disadvantage was caused to appellant - Cold-blooded cruel murder of innocent children by their own real father/appellant showed enormous proportion with which crime was committed that eliminated almost all members of his family - No attempt was made to amicably sort out trivial matter - Rule of rarest of rare cases had to be applied as present case fell within category of rarest of rare cases - Crime of nature discussed above no other proper and adequate sentence except sentence of death as there were no mitigating circumstances - Appellant's case deserved no interference as regarded death sentence to awarded by lower courts - Hence, conviction and sentence of death awarded to appellant by HC was affirmed - Execution of death sentence u/s. 201 of IPC remained only of academic interest - Accordingly, Conviction and sentences awarded to appellant by lower courts was affirmed - Appeal dismissed.
