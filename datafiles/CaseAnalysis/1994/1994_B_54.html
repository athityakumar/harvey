Punjab S.E.B. v Bassi Cold Storage

Supreme Court of India

16 March 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

B.L. Hansaria, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1856; (1994) Supp2 SCC 124; AIR 1994 SC 2544; AIR 1994 SCW 3590; 1995 ARBLR 44; 1994 (2) CCC 18; JT 1994 (2) SC 682; 1994(2) SCALE 332; [1994] 3 S.C.R. 33

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Keywords: Arbitration Act 1899

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Electricity & Energy - Arbitration Act, 1940, s. 1 - Electricity Act, 1910, ss. 52, 13(2), 21(4), 22 A (2), 24 and 52 - (A) Whether matters not listed by Electricity act as arbitrable can be referred to Arbitration? - Held, no - Matters relating to which Electricity Act has directed that same would be determined by arbitration are only referable to arbitration - These matters are contained in ss. 13(2), 21(4) and 22 A(2) - (B)Whether Condition No. 29 of Conditions of supply would override arbitrable issues specified in Electricity Act - Cannot override Electricity Act Condition No. 29 of Abridged Conditions of supply applicable to consumers of Board in State of Punjab provides that any dispute arising between Board and Consumer in respect of 'any matter connected with supply' would be arbitrable under as per provisions of Arbitration Act - Dispute as to damages payable for disconnection cannot be subject-matter of arbitration being not covered by any of sections of Act dealing with arbitration - Appeal allowed.
