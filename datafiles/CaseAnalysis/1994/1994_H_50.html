Hindustan Lever Employees' Union v Hindustan Lever Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

24 October 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Sahai, S.C. Sen, A.M. Ahmadi

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 2011; (1995) Supp1 SCC 499; AIR 1995 SC 470; [1995] 83 Comp Cas 30; JT 1994 (7) SC 215; 1996 (1) LLN 555; 1994(4) SCALE 642; [1994] Supp4 S.C.R. 723

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate

Keywords: Deletion, Exchange Ratio, Capital Issues (Control) Act,1947

Summary: Companies Act, of 1956, ss.394, 226(3), M R T P Act, of 1969, s.12A - Amalgamation - Indian with foreign company - Approval of scheme and share exchange ratio by majority ( 95 % ) stake holders - Allotment of 51% shares to foreign company - Companies appoint valuer, a Director of amalgamating Company, to fix share exchange ratio - Refusal by MRTP Commission to pass - Order of injunction - Whether this amalgamation is - Prejudice to workers , against public interest , impropriety in valuation of shares or ignoring provisions of the Act - Held, not prejudice to workers -Not part of judicial process to examine entrepreneurial activities to ferret out flaws - Approving -95% of the shareholders - Best Judge of their interest - Better conversant with market trend - Agreed to the valuation - Refusal for Injunction - Not improper.
