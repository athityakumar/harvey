Quamarul Islam v S.K. Kanta And Others

Supreme Court of India

21 January 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

A.S. Anand, R.M. Sahai, S. Ratnavel Pandian

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1273; (1994) Supp3 SCC 5; AIR 1994 SC 1733; AIR 1994 SCW 1598; JT 1994 (1) SC 452; 1994(1) SCALE 129; [1994] 1 S.C.R. 210

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Keywords: Bombay Prohibition Act 1949

Summary: Civil Procedure - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 83, 87 and 123(4)- Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 19, r. 1, O. 6, r. 15 - Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 63 and 65 - Corrupt practices - Election petition - Trial - Second affidavit - After evidence is led and arguments over - Not on basis of 'belief' or 'information' - Proof - Newspaper reports - Held, averments on basis of 'Personal knowledge' only - Not permissible - Newspaper reports by themselves are not evidence of contents.
