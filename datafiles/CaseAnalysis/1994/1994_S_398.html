Virender Gaur and Others v State of Haryana and Others

Supreme Court of India

24 November 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, N. Venkatachala

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 532; (1995) 2 SCC 577; AIR 1995 SCW 306; JT 1997 (10) SC 600; 1994(5) SCALE 211; [1994] Supp6 S.C.R. 78; 1995 (1) UJ 529

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: &gt;Land & Property - Constitution (Forty-Second Amendment) Act, 1976 - Bangalore Improvement Act, 1945 - City of Bangalore Improvement Act, 1945 - Haryana Municipal Act, 1973 - Municipal Committee in Haryana State, framed Town Planning Scheme No. 5 - Govt. of Haryana had sanctioned that Scheme on 30-10-1975 - First appellant is an owner of a parcel in Scheme - By operation of s. 61 of Haryana Municipal Act, 1973, land stood vested in Municipality - Admittedly, in Scheme, land, subject-matter of lease for 99 years made in favour of Punjab Samaj Sabha (for short 'the PSS'), was earmarked for open spaces - PSS had paid price and had obtained sanction for construction of Dharmashala - Whether Govt. had no power to lease out land to PSS? - Held, action taken by Govt. is wholly without authority of law and jurisdiction and sanction of land by Municipality for different use defeats purpose and is in violation of law and Constitution - There is a constitutional imperative on State Govt. and municipalities, not only to ensure and safeguard proper environment but also an imperative duty to take adequate measures to promote, protect and improve both man-made and natural environment - Land is vested in Municipality and Govt. has no right and title or interest therein - They have no power to give either by lease to PSS or deal with property as if land vested in it - Therefore, grant of lease by Govt. in favour of PSS is clearly without authority of law and jurisdiction - Appeal allowed.
