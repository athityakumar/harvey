Bala Shankar Maha Shanker Bhattjee and Others v Charity Commissioner, Gujarat State

Supreme Court of India

17 August 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 966; (1995) Supp1 SCC 485; AIR 1995 SC 167; 1995 (1) G.L.R. 711; JT 1994 (5) SC 152; 1994(3) SCALE 796; [1994] Supp2 S.C.R. 687

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Trusts & Associations

Keywords: Charitable and Religious Trusts Act, 1920, Bihar Hindu Religious Trust, 1950

Summary: Trusts & Associations - Land & Property - Charitable and Religious Trusts Act, 1920 - Bombay Public Trust Act, 1950 - Registration of temples - Public trust - Appellants' predecessors, under protest, made an application u/s. 18 of the 1950 Act for registration of the temples as Public Trust - Asstt. Charity Commissioner after inquiry reiterated the temples and Haveli to be public trust properties and accordingly ordered their registration under the 1950 Act - District judge reiterated that all the five temples and Haveli were private properties and directed deletion of their registration under the 1950 Act - HC held that the temple was a public trust and a public temple within the meaning of s. 2(13) r/w s. 2(17) of the 1950 Act and set aside the contra declaration of the district judge - Hence, the instant appeal - Whether the temple was a public trust or a private property - Held, that the temple's sources of income were from cash allowance from the State Treasury, offerings by the devotees and visitors and income from immovable properties given over to and in the name of the deity - Properties attached to the temple had been shown in all Govt. records in the name of the deity, which led to the inference that the immovable properties standing in the name of the deity were gifted or donated to the deity - Origin of the temple, manner in which its affairs were managed, nature and extent of gifts received by it, rights exercised by the devotees in regard to worship, consciousness of the manager and the consciousness of the devotees themselves as to the public character of the temple were relevant factors which established that the temple was a public trust and a public temple within the meaning of s. 2(13) r/w s. 2(17) of the 1950 Act - Appeal dismissed.
