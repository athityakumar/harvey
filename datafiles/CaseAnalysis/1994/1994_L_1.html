L. Chandra Kumar v The Union Of India & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

02 December 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

Kuldip Singh, B.L. Hansaria, S.B. Majmudar

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 609; (1995) SCC (L&S) 321; (1995) 1 SCC 400; AIR 1995 SC 1151; 1995 (29) ATC 120; 1995 (71) FLR 27; JT 1995 (1) SC 454; 1995 (2) LLJ 640; 1994(5) SCALE 72; 1995 (2) SLJ 27; 1995 (1) SLR 10; [1994] Supp6 S.C.R. 261

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950 - Administrative Tribunals Act, 1985, s. 5(6) - Validity of s. 5(6) of the Act.

Held, SC case S.P. Sampath Kumar Etc v Union Of India And Ors 1986 Indlaw SC 193 do require a fresh look by a larger-Bench over all the issues adjudicated by SC in Sampath Kumar's case including the quest ion whether the Tribunal can at all have an Administrative Member on its Bench, if it were to have the power of even deciding constitutional validity of a statute or 309 Rule. Examination of this aspect would be necessary to install confidence in the minds of people (and litigants) which is the greatest prop of the judiciary. Matter referred to Larger Bench. Order accordingly.
