Union of India and Others v Rajiv Yadav, Ias and Others

Supreme Court of India

21 July 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

Kuldip Singh, K. Ramaswamy, M.M. Punchhi

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 892; (1994) 6 SCC 38; (1994) SCC (L&S) 1265; AIR 1995 SC 14; 1994 (28) ATC 228; 1994 (69) FLR 936; JT 1994 (5) SC 54; 1994(3) SCALE 617; 1995 (1) SLJ 13; 1994 (4) SLR 547; [1994] Supp2 S.C.R. 30; 1994 (2) UJ 558

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950 - Indian Administrative Service (Cadre) Rules, 1954 - Central Government is authority under Indian Administrative Service (Cadre) Rules, 1954 to allocate members of Indian Administrative Service (IAS) - Directly recruited - To various State cadre/Joint cadres under said Rules - Central Govt. has laid down broad principles of allocation called 'the Roster System' - Thereafter till 1984 allocations were done in accordance with procedure called The Limited Zonal Preferences System - Since 1985 batch onwards Central Govt. has reverted back to 'Roster System' with some modifications - Reservation to extent of 15% and 7 1/2% for Scheduled Caste/ Scheduled Tribes respectively has been provided in direct recruitment to IAS - 'Roster System' provides that while allocating Scheduled Caste/Scheduled Tribe candidates to their home States (insiders) vacancies shall be reversed for them in various cadres to extent reservation-percentage has been provided in direct recruitment to IAS - This reservation in process of allocation was challenged by Rajiv Yadav - A Full Bench of tribunal allowed application of Rajiv Yadav and held that no reservation can be provided for Scheduled Caste/Scheduled Tribes while allocating members of IAS to various cadres - Whether selected candidate has a right to be considered for appointment to IAS and also has a right to be allocated to a cadre of his choice or to his home State? - Held, a selected candidate has a right to be considered for appointment to IAS but he has no such right to be allocated to a cadre of his choice or to his home State Allotment of cadre is an incidence of service - A number of an all-India Service bears liability to serve in any part of India principles of allocation as contained in clause (2) of letter dated 31/5/1985, wherein preference is given to a Scheduled Castes/Scheduled Tribe candidate for allocation to his home State, do not provide for reservation of appointments or posts and as such question of testing said principles on anvil of art.16(4) of Constitution of India does not arise - It is common knowledge that Scheduled Caste/Scheduled Tribe candidates are normally much below in merit list and as such are not in a position to compete with general category candidates 'Roster System' ensures equitable treatment to both general candidates and reserved categories - In compliance with statutory requirement and in terms of art.16(4) of Constitution of India 22 1/2% reserved category candidates are recruited to IAS - Having done so both categories are to be justly distributed amongst States - But for 'Roster System' it would be difficult rather impossible for Scheduled Caste/Scheduled Tribe candidates to be allocated to their home States principles of cadre allocation, thus, ensure equitable distribution of reserved candidates amongst all cadres - Appeal allowed.
