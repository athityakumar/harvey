Smt. Kamla Devi v Shri Vasdev

Supreme Court of India

14 December 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Sen, S.P. Bharucha, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1841; (1995) 1 SCC 356; AIR 1995 SC 985; JT 1995 (1) SC 142; 1995 (2) RCJ 480; 1995 (1) RCR(Rent) 411; 1995 (2) RentLR 634; 1994(5) SCALE 295; [1994] Supp6 S.C.R. 603; 1995 (1) UJ 603

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - Delhi Rent Control Act, 1958, ss. 15(1) and 15(7) - M.P. Accommodation Control Act, 1961, ss. 12(3), 13(1), 13(5) and 13(6) - (A) Whether power of Rent Controller - striking out defence of tenant against eviction if the tenant fails to make payment or deposit as directed by an order passed u/s. 15 (1) discretionary - (B) Whether any material distinction between provisions of ss. 12 (1),(3) and s.13(1), (5) and (6) of Act of 1961 and corresponding provisions of ss. 14 (1), (2) and s. 15(1), (7) of Act of 1958 - Held, (A) power to strike out the defence against eviction is discretionary and must not be mechanically exercised without any application of mind to the facts of case - (B) No material distinction between provisions - Appeal dismissed.
