State of Tamil Nadu and Others v Ananthi Ammal and Others

Supreme Court of India

22 November 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Bharucha, K.S. Paripoornan, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 567; (1995) 1 SCC 519; AIR 1995 SC 2114; AIR 1995 SCW 355; JT 1995 (1) SC 247; 1994(4) SCALE 1106; 1995 (1) SCJ 607; [1994] Supp5 S.C.R. 666

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Constitution - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - State came in appeal against the order of the HC, whereby the 1978 Act was struck down, it being ultravirus the Constitution, as was violative of arts. 14, 19 and 300A of the Constitution - Hence, the present appeal - Whether the 1978 Act was ultra vires the Constitution - Held, that the provisions of the said Act was valid legislation except in so far as they provided for payment of the compensation amount in instalments - Act being valid legislation, its provisions precluded the State from acquiring land for the purpose of a Harijan Welfare Scheme under the 1894 Act - Appeal was allowed and the proceedings under the 1894 Act to acquire the appellant's land for the purpose of a Harijan Welfare Scheme are, therefore, was quashed and set aside - Appeal partly allowed.
