Director of Education and Others v Gajadhar Prasad Verma

Supreme Court of India

21 November 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, N. Venkatachala

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 2066; (1995) SCC (L&S) 327; (1995) 1 SCC 465; AIR 1995 SC 1121; 1995 (29) ATC 126; 1994(5) SCALE 338; [1994] Supp5 S.C.R. 617; 1995 (1) UJ 258

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Additional post created, No Previous approval taken

Summary: Service - Uttar Pradesh High Schools and Intermediate Colleges (Payment of Salaries of Teachers and Other Employees) Act, 1971, s. 9 - Additional post created - No Previous approval taken - Validity - Respondent was appointed as additional clerk during the leave vacancy of regular clerk - Managing committee passed resolution and continued respondent's services - It was stated that approval of District Inspector of Schools was obtained but no supporting material was placed - HC allowed petition and directed Govt. to create post and make payment of salary - Hence instant appeal - Whether the school of the respondent can claim reimbursement of the salary of such clerk from the Govt.

Held, s. 9 of the Act provides that no institution shall create a new post of teacher or other employee except with the previous approval of the Director or such officer as may be empowered in that behalf by the Director and admittedly no previous approval is taken for creation of additional post of clerk. Addl. Post of clerk is created by the Govt. pursuant to the impugned order passed by HC and stated subject to the result in the appeal. As per law 7, if there is any increase in the strength and sanction is needed then management has to take appropriate steps. Order of the HC is set aside. Appeals disposed of.
