Gujarat State Dy. Xen Assn. v State Of Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

10 May 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Sahai, Kuldip Singh, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1768; (1994) Supp2 SCC 591; (1994) SCC (L&S) 1159; 1994 (28) ATC 78; 1994 (2) CLR 29; 1994 (69) FLR 158; JT 1994 (3) SC 559; 1995 (1) LLJ 1047; 1994 (2) LLN 403; 1994(2) SCALE 866; [1994] 3 S.C.R. 983; 1994 (3) SLJ 5; 1994 (2) SLR 710

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Constitution - Gujarat Service of Engineers Class I Recruitment Rules, 1979, r. 4 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 309 - Seniority and quota violation between promotees and direct recruits - Service is the Engineering Service of State of Gujarat - Examinations were held for selection of Class I and Class 11 Engineers under Executive Engineers (Civil) 1979 'Recruitment Rules' framed u/art. 309 - R. 4 of the Recruitment Rules empowered the Gujarat Public Service Commission to fix qualifying marks in any or all the subjects - It appears that after the examinations were held the Commission for the first time fixed a minimum qualifying marks of 50%, (that is, 100 marks out of 200 of viva voce) for selection - Results were declared but one of such candidates had appeared in the examinations, but had not been selected even though he had secured good marks in the written examination and on aggregate had secured higher marks than those who had been selected, approached High Court by way of Writ Petition claiming that the fixing of qualifying marks was arbitrary - Whether the HC could issue a direction to 'appoint candidates from the waiting list to future vacancies as the quota of direct recruits had not been worked out by applying correct principles? - Held, a candidate from the waiting list appointed subsequently cannot claim appointment from a back date - Even otherwise appointment of a candidate operates from the date he is appointed and not from the date those from the select list are appointed - Same principle applies even amongst inter se appointees from the waiting list - Claim of the direct recruits is, therefore rejected and it is directed that they shall be given seniority from the date of their appointment and not from any back date - Thus, HC order is set aside subject to certain directions - Appeal allowed.
