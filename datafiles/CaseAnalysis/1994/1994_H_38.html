Hem Chand v State of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

06 October 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Jayachandra Reddy, P.B. Sawant, G.N. Ray

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 809; (1994) 6 SCC 727; (1995) SCC (Cr) 36; AIR 1995 SC 120; 1994 (4) CCR 814; 1994 (3) Crimes 608; 1995 (1) DMC 86; 1995 JCC 188; JT 1994 (6) SC 475; 1994(4) SCALE 401; 1995 (1) SCJ 102; [1994] Supp4 S.C.R. 295

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 304 B - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 113-B - Appellant's wife died within 7 yrs. of her marriage by strangulation - Charges of murder couldn't be framed due to lack of evidence - (A)Whether accused guilty of offence u/s. 304-IPC, 1860? - Second set of medical evidence shows that it is a case of strangulation - It should be accepted that it was an unnatural death - The plea set up by accused that it could be a case of suicide committed by deceased for unknown reasons is, under circumstances, wholly unacceptable - Held, though case rests on circumstantial evidence, presumption u/s. 113-B of Evidence Act, 1872 has rightly been drawn and appellant is convicted u/s. 304-B IPC, 1860 - (B) Whether extreme punishment of imprisonment for life is warranted in instance case? - In instant case no doubt prosecution has provided that deceased died an unnatural death namely due to strangulation, but there is no direct evidence connecting accused - No charge u/s. 302 IPC, 1860 - S. 304-B IPC, 1860 only raises presumption and lays down that minimum sentence should be seven years but it may extend to imprisonment for life - Therefore, awarding extreme punishment of imprisonment for life should be in rare cases - Further held, conviction of appellant u/s. 304-B, IPC, 1860 confirmed, sentence of imprisonment reduced from life to 10 years RI - Appeal dismissed.
