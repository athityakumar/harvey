Darshan Singh v Gurdev Singh

Supreme Court of India

05 September 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, N. Venkatachala

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 790; (1994) 6 SCC 585; AIR 1995 SC 75; 1994 (3) CCC 223; 1995 (1) MLJ(SC) 36; 1994 (2) RentLR 370; 1994(4) SCALE 143; 1995 (1) SCJ 207; [1994] Supp3 S.C.R. 95; 1995 (1) UJ 44

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Constitution - Limitation Act, 1963, ss. 6, 7 and 8 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - (A) Person under disability - Elongation of period of limitation by ss. 6 and 7 - Restricted to three years by s. 8 - Combined effect of s. 8 proviso to ss. 6 and 7 - (B) SLP filed against order decreeing suit for possession - All Courts found defendant to be stranger to plaintiff's family and to have wrongfully obtained possession - Suit barred by limitation - If order to be interfered with - Held, (A) Litigant is entitled to a fresh starting period of limitation subject to condition that in no case period extended shall exceed three years - (B) Supreme Court refused to interfere with order even though suit was barred by limitation - Appeal partly allowed.
