Subhash Muljimal Gandhi v L.Himingliana

Supreme Court of India

26 August 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

M.K. Mukherjee, P.B. Sawant

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 985; (1994) 6 SCC 14; (1994) SCC (Cr) 1580; AIR 1994 SCW 4975; 1994 (3) CCR 716; 1994 (3) Crimes 242; JT 1994 (5) SC 358; 1994(3) SCALE 856; [1994] Supp2 S.C.R. 785

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Tort

Summary: Criminal - , 1974, s. 3 (1) - Detention - Validity - Whether a detenu or anyone on his behalf is entitled to challenge an order of detention without detenu submitting or surrendering to it, and if so, what will be nature, scope and extent of such challenge? - Held, to contend that there was no breach of constitutional or legal provisions as petitioner was produced a competent court within 24 hours of his formal arrest, even if it is held that explanation offered by respondents for delayed production is not a satisfactory one and that Customs Officer have failed to comply with constitutional and statutory requirements, order of detention which has been made by detaining authority on basis of its satisfaction that petitioner was smuggling gold, would not be bad on that score - Entitlement to seek appropriate relief by way of compensation or otherwise by appellant in case he succeeds in proving that he was wrongfully and illegally detained - Appeal dismissed.
