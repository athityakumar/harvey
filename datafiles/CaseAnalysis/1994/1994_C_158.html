Common Cause, A Regd. Society v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 January 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, M.M. Punchhi, N.P. Singh

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1748; (1994) 5 SCC 557; 1995 (2) MLJ(SC) 1

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Constitution

Keywords: Attorney-General

Summary: Constitution - Advocates & Judges - Constitution of India, 1956 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Striking of court - Lawyers - Interference in judicial system - Petition brought u/art.32 of the Constitution - Petitioners raised questions in regard to duties and obligations of members of legal profession - Petitioner contended that striking of court by lawyers paralysed the judicial system and resulted in misery to litigants - Whether notice should be passed against strikes, among members of legal profession, in regard to their duties and obligations - Held, in light of SC judgments that cases should not ordinarily be adjourned on account of absence of lawyers unless there were cogent reasons to do so, Court directed a public notice under O. 1 r. 8, CPC and intimated all of pendency of petition - SC decision in Hussainara Khatoon v. Home Secy., State of Bihar, 1979 Indlaw SC 113, followed - Appeals allowed.
