Life Insurance Corporation of India v Vishwanath Verma and Others

Supreme Court of India

30 September 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mohan, G.N. Ray

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1094; (1994) Supp3 SCC 569; AIR 1995 SC 189; [1995] 82 Comp Cas 191; JT 1994 (6) SC 379; 1995 (1) LLN 434; 1994(4) SCALE 364; 1995 (1) SCJ 286; 1994 (5) SLR 630; [1994] Supp4 S.C.R. 87; 1995 (1) UJ 200

Case Digest

Subject: Insurance; Service

Keywords: Appointed Day, Annuities, Annuity insurance

Summary: Life Insurance Corporation Act, 1956, ss. 2(11) and 30 - Madhya Pradesh Municipal Corporation Act, 1956, s. 421 - Life insurance business - Scheme for benefit of its employees by Municipal Corporation - Financial help to family of Employee on death or retirement - Proportionate to monthly contribution made by employee - Held, scheme partakes character of life insurance business - Municipal Corporation cannot run scheme - No approval by Central Govt. - Exemption under s. 44(f) not available to Scheme.
