K.R.Shinivas v R.M. Premchand

Supreme Court of India

30 September 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

M.M. Punchhi, K. Jayachandra Reddy

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1765; (1994) 6 SCC 620; 1994(4) SCALE 436; 1995 (1) SCJ 113; 1994 (5) SLR 739; [1994] Supp4 S.C.R. 114

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Education - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Whether the University was competent to reevaluate the papers and whether there were procedural irregularities and the result of the appellant had been manipulated? - Respondent being a Research Scholar of the University, knew about the regulations that the answer books are destroyed within six months from the examination - It cannot be assumed that respondent was unaware about the destruction of the answer books of the appellant at the time when he moved the High Court - Respondent had no locus standi to move the HC in public interest - Held, order of the HC is set aside - Appeal is allowed.
