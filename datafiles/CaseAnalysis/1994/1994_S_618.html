R. K. Panda and Others v Steel Authority of India

Supreme Court of India

12 May 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

N.P. Singh, Kuldip Singh, P.B. Sawant

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1695; (1994) 5 SCC 304; (1994) SCC (L&S) 1978; AIR 1994 SCW 2460; 1994 (2) CLR 402; 1994 (69) FLR 256; JT 1994 (4) SC 151; 1997 LLJ 1202; 1994 (2) LLN 378; 1994(2) SCALE 910; [1994] 3 S.C.R. 1034; 1994 (3) SLJ 227; 1995 (6) SLR 665

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Contract, Labour (Regulation and Abolition) Act, 1970 - Whether petitioners are entitled to same pay which is being paid to regular employees and are entitled to be treated as regular employees of respondent and not as contract labourers? - The framers of the Act have allowed and recognised contract labour and they have never purported to abolish it in its entirety - The primary object appears to be that there should not be any exploitation of the contract labourers by the contractor or the establishment - For achieving that object, statutory restrictions and responsibilities have been imposed on the contractor as well as on the principal employer - Of course if any expenses are incurred for providing any amenity to the contract labourers or toward the payment of wages by the principal employer he is entitled to deduct the same from the bill of the contractor - The Act also conceives that appropriate Govt. may after consultation with the Central Board or the State Board, as the case may be, prohibit by notification in the Official Gazette, employment of contract labour in any process, operation or other work in any establishment, taking all facts and circumstances of employment of contract labour in such process, operation or the work into consideration - Held, whether contract labourers have become employees of principal employer in course of time and whether engagement and employment of labourers through a contractor is a mere camouflage and a smokescreen, as has been urged, is a question of fact and has to be established by contract labourers on basis of requisite material - At what point of time a direct link is established between contract labourers and principal employer, eliminating contractor from scene, is a matter which has to be established on material produced before court - Petition allowed.
