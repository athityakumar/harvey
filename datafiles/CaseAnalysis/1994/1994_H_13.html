Kartar Singh v Harbans Kaur

Supreme Court of India

21 January 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

B.L. Hansaria, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1270; (1994) 4 SCC 730; 1994 (23) ALR 393; JT 1994 (2) SC 196; 1994(2) SCALE 494

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Land & Property; Women & Children

Keywords: Succession & Inheritance

Summary: Land & Property - Hindu Succession Act, 1956 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882,s. 43 - Smt Harbans Kaur - Respondent executed sale deed on 19-04-1961, in favour of appellant of alienating lands on her behalf and on behalf of her minor son, Kulwant Singh - Kulwant Singh, on attaining majority, filed for a declaration that sale of his share in lands mentioned in schedule attached thereto by his mother was void and does not bind him - But before taking delivery of possession, Kulwant Singh died - Whether in view of finding that Harbans Kaur had succeeded by operation of law, appellant is entitled to interest acquired by Harbans Kaur by operation of s. 43 of Act? - Held, sale of half share of interest of Kulwant Singh made by his mother is void - S. 43 feeds its estoppel - Rule of estoppel by deed by transferor would apply only when transferee has been misled - When note in sale deed had put appellant on notice of limited right of mother as guardian, as a reasonable prudent man appellant is expected to enquire whether on her own mother as guardian of minor son is competent to alienate estate of minor - When such acts were not done first limb of s. 43 is not satisfied - Second limb of s. 43 is that contract must be a subsisting one at time of claim - A void contract is no contract in eye of law and was never in existence so second limb of s. 43 is not satisfied - Appeals dismissed.
