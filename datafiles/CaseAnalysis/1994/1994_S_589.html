Surya Prakash Sharma v State of Uttar Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 August 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

M.K. Mukherjee, P.B. Sawant

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 997; (1994) Supp3 SCC 195; (1994) SCC (Cr) 1691; AIR 1995 SCW 1841; 1995 ALJ 777; 1994 (3) CCR 701; 1994 (3) Crimes 228; 1995 CRLJ 2657; 1994 JCC 608; JT 1994 (5) SC 102; 1994(3) SCALE 710; [1994] Supp2 S.C.R. 486; 1994 (2) UJ 429

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Defence & Security Forces; Service

Keywords: Defence Regulations

Summary: Criminal - Detention - National Security Act, 1980 - Detention - Petitioner detained by order of Magistrate for preventing him from acting prejudicial to maintenance of public order - Whether detention order can be passed against a person who is already in custody? - Held, principles laid down in case of 'Rameshwar Shaw v. District Magistrate' followed - Detaining authority has not put forward any cogent reason to show how petitioner will engage himself in activities prejudicial to maintenance of public order - Detention order quashed - Petition allowed.
