Telangana Steel Industries v State Of A. P.

Supreme Court of India

04 March 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

B.L. Hansaria, B.P. Jeevan Reddy

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 2106; (1994) Supp2 SCC 259; AIR 1994 SC 1831; 1994 (73) E.L.T. 513; JT 1994 (7) SC 27; 1994(1) SCALE 884; [1994] 2 S.C.R. 324; 1994 (93) STC 187

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax; Land & Property; Sales Tax

Keywords: Declared Goods

Summary: Sales Tax - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956, ss. 14(iv)(xv) and 15(a) - Declared goods - (A) Sales tax paid on wire rods and then wires manufactured from them - Whether exigible to sales tax when iron wire rods and wires are part of sub-item (xv) - (B) Object of interdiction on imposing multi point tax - Held, not exigible in view of interdiction u/s.15(a) - Purpose is to minimise tax burden on declared goods - Appeal allowed.
