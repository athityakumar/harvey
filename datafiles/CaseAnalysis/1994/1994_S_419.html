Krishan Lal v State of Jammu and Kashmir

Supreme Court of India

25 February 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

B.L. Hansaria, B.P. Jeevan Reddy

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1379; (1994) 4 SCC 422; (1994) SCC (L&S) 885; 1994 (27) ATC 590; 1994 (1) CLR 990; 1994 (68) FLR 1175; JT 1994 (2) SC 619; 1995 (2) LLJ 718; 1994 (2) LLN 44; 1994 (2) MLJ(SC) 117; 1994(1) SCALE 848; [1994] 2 S.C.R. 149; 1994 (2) SLJ 64; 1994 (2) SLR 19; 1994 (1) UJ 573

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Socio-Economic

Summary: Criminal - Socio-Economic - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1962, ss. 17(5) and 20 - Suit challenging order of dismissal decreed by trail court - Upheld by District Judge - High Court allowed appeal of respondent-State - (A) Whether Civil Court had jurisdiction to entertain suit? - Held, finality given by s. 20 of Act could not have ousted jurisdiction of Civil Court because of violation of mandatory provision as contained in s. 17(5) of Act hence dismissal order being a nullity, Court's jurisdiction does not get barred - (B) Whether suit was barred by res judicata? - Held, there was no decision on merits as regards grievance of appellant and so, principle of res judicata had no application - Appeal allowed.
