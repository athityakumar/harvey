 Syndicate Bank and Another v K. Umesh Nayak and Others

Supreme Court of India

13 September 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

P.B. Sawant, Kuldip Singh, S. Mohan, G.N. Ray, N.P. Singh

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 206; (1994) 5 SCC 572; (1994) SCC (L&S) 1197; AIR 1995 SC 319; 1994 (2) CLR 75; JT 1994 (5) SC 647; [1994] Supp3 S.C.R. 491

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Strike - (A) Whether strike legal and justified? - (B) Whether workers entitled to wages for strike period? - Held, (A) legality and justifiability is to be primarily determined by adjudicator, it being industrial dispute within meaning of Act - (B) Workers not entitled to wages, unless strike is both legal and justified - Appeal allowed.
