Stae Of Rajastham v Umrao Singh

Supreme Court of India

29 September 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mohan, M.N. Venkatachaliah

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1087; (1994) 6 SCC 560; (1995) SCC (L&S) 10; 1994 (28) ATC 513; 1995 (70) FLR 552; JT 1994 (6) SC 372; 1995 (1) LLJ 908; 1996 (2) LLN 225; 1994(4) SCALE 372; 1995 (1) SLJ 132; 1994 (5) SLR 638; [1994] Supp4 S.C.R. 67; 1995 (1) UJ 15

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Rajasthan Recruitment of Dependants of Government Servants (Dying while in Service) Rules, 1975,r. 5 - Respondent's father died while he was serving as a Sub-Inspector, CID - Respondent applied to appellant for appointment on compassionate ground - Appointed as Lower Division Clerk on compassionate ground - Sought appointment as Sub-Inspector, which was denied - On application HC directed consideration of his candidature for appointment to post in accordance with Rules - Present appeal - Right to be considered for appointment on compassionate ground was consummated - No further consideration on compassionate ground would ever arise - Otherwise, it would be a case of endless compassion - Held, once right has consummated any further or second consideration for a higher post on ground of compassion would not arise - Appeal allowed.
