Ram Janam Singh v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

25 January 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

N.P. Singh, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 2112; (1994) 2 SCC 622; (1994) SCC (L&S) 716; AIR 1994 SC 1722; 1994 (23) ALR 451; 1994 (27) ATC 166; 1994 (1) CLR 691; 1994 (68) FLR 744; JT 1994 (1) SC 187; 1994 (1) LLJ 901; 1994 (2) LLN 68; 1994(1) SCALE 200; [1994] 1 S.C.R. 316; 1994 (2) SLJ 5; 1994 (1) SLR 510; 1994 (1) UJ 701; 1994 (1) UPLBEC 216

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Defence & Security Forces; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Armed Forces

Summary: Labour and Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, sch. III, item 5 - Payment of Bonus Act 1965, s. 34(3) - Determination of claim for customary bonus - Payment made at uniform rate for unbroken years before Puja festival under various Settlement between management and employees irrespective of profit or loss incurred by employer - Whether such customary bonus - Converts into ' bonus ' under the 1965 Act reference of s. 34(3) of Payment of Bonus Act in those settlements - Held, mere reference cannot alter nature of payment made and convert it into as 'Bonus' under that Act - Appeals allowed.
