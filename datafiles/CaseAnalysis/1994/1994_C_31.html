Shailesh Prabhudas Mehta v Calico Dyeing And Printing Mills Ltd.

Supreme Court of India

15 February 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Jayachandra Reddy, G.N. Ray

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1330; (1994) 3 SCC 339; [1994] 80 Comp Cas 64; 1994 (54) DLT 113; JT 1994 (1) SC 671; [1994] 1 S.C.R. 968

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Evacuee Property & Displaced Persons

Summary: Corporate - Practice & Procedure - Companies Act, 1956, s. 111 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Registration of shares - Limitation of - Whether powers of directors to refuse registration of shares u/s. 111 lapses on failure to give intimation of refusal within time provided therein? - In exercise of discretion Directors will act for paramount interest of company and for general interest of shareholders because Directors are in a fiduciary position both towards company and towards every shareholder - Held, mere failure to intimate refusal of registration of shares does not take away director's right u/s. 111 to refuse registration - Appeal dismissed.
