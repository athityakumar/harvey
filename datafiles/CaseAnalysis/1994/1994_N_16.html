Nageshwar Prasad and Others v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

28 July 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, S. Mohan

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 2048; (1995) Supp4 SCC 718; (1996) SCC (L&S) 230; 1996 (32) ATC 177; 1995 (8) SLR 118B

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14 and 16 - Promotion - Constitutionality - (A) Whether prescription of different periods of experience for degree holders and diploma holders is valid ? - Held, efficacy of diploma-holders has been recognised up to a particular stage in hierarchy and thereafter, it is realised that for manning higher posts a degree in engineering is a must - (B) Whether prescription of a quota of 50 percent for diploma-holders and 50 percent for degree? - Yes - Prescription of quote rule is obviously to ensure that in immediate promotion cadre there is a fair mix of both degree-holders because vertical movement from that stage and upwards in hierarchy is restricted to degree-holders and if they are not available in sufficient number in feeding channel said channel would be virtually dry and sufficient number of degree-holders would not be available for promotion to next higher cadre - Further held, not violative of arts. 14 and 16 - Appeal allowed.
