Gopal Chandra Ghosh v Renu Bala Majumdar

Supreme Court of India

12 January 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

B.L. Hansaria, B.P. Jeevan Reddy

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 2098; (1994) 2 SCC 258; 1994 (2) CalLT(SC) 31; JT 1994 (1) SC 80; 1994 (2) MLJ 48; 1994 (2) MLJ(SC) 48; 1994 (1) RCR(Rent) 328; 1994 (1) RentLR 579; 1994(1) SCALE 63; [1994] 1 S.C.R. 108

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Summary: Rent Control - Practice & Procedure - West Bengal Premises Tenancy Act, 1956, ss. 17(1), (3) and (4) - Limitation Act, 1963, s. 4 - Benefit of protection against eviction - Beneficial piece of legislation - Two days delay in deposit of rent by appellant/tenant - Case covered by provision of sub-s. (3) - Relied on Khemka's case - Held, courts below not justified to pass order for eviction merely because of two days delay - Merely because defence is struck off, order of eviction will not follow and plea of default will have to be established by landlord - Appeal allowed.
