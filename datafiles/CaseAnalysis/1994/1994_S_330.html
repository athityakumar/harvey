Sanjay Dutt v State Through C. B. I., Bombay (I)

Supreme Court of India

18 August 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, N.P. Singh

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 975; (1994) SCC (Cr) 1426; 1994 (31) ACC 702; 1995 (1) Bom.C.R. 181; 1994 (3) Crimes 207; 1994 CRLR 542; 1994 CrLR(SC) 542; JT 1994 (5) SC 225; 1994(3) SCALE 790; [1994] Supp2 S.C.R. 729; 1994 (2) UJ 474

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 -

Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Terrorist and Disruptive Activities Act, 1987,ss.2(h),3 - Arms Rules, 1962 - s.5 -Charge sheet - Confession - Possession - Investigation - Petitioner was one of accused in B blasts registered on file of Designated Court - Prosecution against petitioner, set out in charge-sheet - prosecution was relied upon statements of servant of petitioner and of police guard posted at his house - Petitioner made confession to police, that he remained unretracted and admitted receiving rifles al for purpose of self defence - Petitioner contends that possession of arms and ammunition specified in s.5 did not attract s.5 as it was for self-defence - HC opined to attract s. 5 there must be some interrelation between s. 5 and ss. 3 and 4 of Act - Whether petitioner entitled to released on bail - Held, investigation should be concluded within 180 days of arrest but in case it could not be concluded and public prosecutor reports that progress of investigation and specific reasons for detention of accused beyond period of 180 days - Court evidently satisfied with progress of investigation and reasons for continued detention of accused was empowered to extend period to one year - Petition was referred to Constitution Bench as it would be open to both parties to raise contentions - Hence, not inclined to accede release of petitioner on interim bail - Order accordingly.
