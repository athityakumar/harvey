Workmen v Kettlewell Bullen & Co. Ltd.

Supreme Court of India

06 January 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Agrawal, Kuldip Singh

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 249; 1994 (2) CalLT(SC) 19; 1994 (1) CLR 51; [1994] 1 S.C.R. 22; 1994 (1) UPLBEC 259

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Payment Of Bonus Act, 1965, s. 34(3) - Whether workmen are entitled to Customary Bonus at rate of 10.5 per cent of total annual salary or wages in respect of years 1974 to 1977? - Reason given by High Court for reference to s. 34(3) in various settlements, it may be stated that Act is confined, in its application, to profit bonus, and other kinds of bonus recognised in industrial law are not covered by provisions of Act - Reference to s. 34(3) must be regarded as having been made by way of abundant caution to exclude liability of appellant for bonus under Act, but that would not alter nature of payment - Held, in drawing an inference that bonus that was being paid by respondent company to appellants was customary bonus payable at rate of 10.5 per cent of salary or wages could be justifiably drawn by tribunal having regard to facts and circumstances of case, and HC was in error in setting aside award and holding that bonus that was paid was not in nature of customary bonus - Appeal allowed.
