M/S. Quality Steel Tubes (P) Limited v Collector Of Central Excise, U.P.

Supreme Court of India

09 December 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Sahai, S.B. Majmudar

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 695; (1995) 2 SCC 372; 1995 (56) ECR 209; 1995 (75) E.L.T. 17; JT 1995 (1) SC 99; 1995 (6) RLT 131; 1994(5) SCALE 183; [1994] Supp6 S.C.R. 439

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax

Summary: Customs - Central Excises & Salt Act, 1944, ss. 3 and 2(d) - Central Excise Rules, 1944, r. 174 - Notification No 31/76 dated 28-3-1976 - Whether tube mill and welding head erected and installed by appellant for manufacture of tubes and pipes out of duty-paid raw material was assessable to duty under residuary Tariff Item No 68 of Schedule being excisable goods within meaning of Act, 1944? - Basic test of levying duty under Act is twofold - One, that any article must be goods and second, that it should be marketable or capable of being brought to market - Goods which are attached to earth and thus becomes immovable and do not satisfy test of being goods within meaning of Act nor it can be said to be capable of being brought to market for being bought and sold - Both tests were not satisfied in case of appellant - Held, plant of tube mill and welding head erected by appellant and installed as a part of expansion programme was not exigibility to duty - Appeal allowed.
