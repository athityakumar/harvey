Subhash Desai v Sharad J. Rao

Supreme Court of India

31 March 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

N.P. Singh, A.M. Ahmadi, M.M. Punchhi

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1440; (1994) Supp2 SCC 446; AIR 1994 SC 2277; AIR 1994 SCW 2155; JT 1994 (3) SC 39; 1994(2) SCALE 391; [1994] 3 S.C.R. 271

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Election; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Freedom Of Speech And Expression

Summary: Election - Civil Procedure - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 83, 123(3) and 123(3 A) - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 2, rr. 1 and 7 - (A) Scope and object-Corrupt practice-Omission to state material facts or give full particulars - (B) Material facts and full particulars in respect of one corrupt practice not stated-However, same given in respect of another corrupt practice - Whether election petition be dismissed? - (C) Validity of ss. 123(3) and 123(3 A) of 1951 Act r/w art. 25 of Constitution - (D) Allegations relating to period prior to filing of nomination - Cannot be taken into consideration for judging legality or validity of election - S. 100(1)(d)(iii) of 1951 Act - Ground for declaring election void - Names of some persons included in voters' list and final publication made before filing of nominations - Plea that inclusion of their names was illegal - Held, (A) Not corrupt practice within s. 83 - (B) Cannot be dismissed - (C) sub-ss. (3) and (3 A) of s. 123 are not in conflict with art. 25 of Constitution - (D) Cannot be raised in election petition - Appeal dismissed.
