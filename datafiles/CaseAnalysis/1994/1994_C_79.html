Kerala Financial Corpn. v Cit

Supreme Court of India

12 May 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

B.L. Hansaria, Kuldip Singh

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1690; (1994) 4 SCC 375; AIR 1994 SC 2416; 1995 (1) BC 1; 1994 (119) CTR 164; [1994] 210 ITR 129; JT 1994 (7) SC 15; 1994 (2) KLT 303; 1994(2) SCALE 1026; [1994] 75 TAXMAN 573

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Debt Laws

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 5, 36 and 119 - Is interest on sticky advance to be treated as income of assessee and liable to be taxed? - Held, such an income was liable to be taxed - If it was established by assessee that such an advance had taken form of a bad debt it would be refundable if proved by assessee - S. 119 has empowered to issue orders for proper administration - Such an order cannot override provisions of Act - Appeals dismissed.
