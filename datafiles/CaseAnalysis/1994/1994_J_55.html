State Of Orissa v Johrimal Gajanand

Supreme Court of India

18 July 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

Kuldip Singh, A.S. Anand

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 866; (1994) 6 SCC 57; JT 1994 (4) SC 635; 1994(3) SCALE 377; 1994 (95) STC 93

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Sales Tax

Keywords: Resale

Summary: Sales Tax - Orissa Sales Tax Act, 1947, s. 5(2)(A)(a) - Purchase of certain goods from a registered dealer on basis of declaration that it is being intended for resale by him in Orissa - Violation of declaration filed by assessee by reselling goods interstate - Whether Tribunal was correct in holding that even sales in course of inter-State trade and commerce, can be sale inside State if goods are inside State of Orissa ? - A contract of sale by which goods are sold and are to be transported from one State to another cannot be made a lever for argument that though sale is in course of inter-State trade and commerce but nevertheless it is a sale within State of Orissa u/s. 2(g) of Act - In order that a sale or purchase might be inter-State, it is essential that must be transport of goods from one State to another under contract of sale or purchase - Held, member, Sales Tax Tribunal, was not correct in holding that even sales in course of inter-State trade and commerce can be sales inside State if goods were inside State of Orissa - Appeal allowed.
