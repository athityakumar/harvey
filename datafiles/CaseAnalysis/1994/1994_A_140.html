Printers (Mysore) Limited v Asstt. Commercial Tax Officer

Supreme Court of India

07 February 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, B.L. Hansaria

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1584; (1994) 2 SCC 434; 1994 (2) ALT 1; 1994 (53) DLT 662; JT 1994 (1) SC 692; 1994(1) SCALE 512; [1994] 1 S.C.R. 682; 1994 (93) STC 95

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Keywords: Resale, Processing

Summary: Sales Tax - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956, ss. 2(d) and 8 - Newspaper (Price And Page) Act, 1956 - Essential Commodities Act, 1955 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 19(1)(a) - Whether publishers of newspapers are entitled to benefit of s. 8(3)(b) r/w s. 8(1)(b) of Central Sales Tax Act, 1956? - Amendment in definition of expression 'goods' by 1958 (Amendment) Act did not intend to deprive newspapers of benefit of s. 8(3)(b) r/w s. 8(1)(b) - Held, appellants are entitled to benefit of said provisions - Expression 'goods' occurring in words 'for use by him in manufacture or processing of goods for sale' in s. 8(3)(b) of Central Sales Tax Act, 1956, does not exclude newspapers - Order accordingly.
