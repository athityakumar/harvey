Amar Singh and Others v Ajmer Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 July 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

Kuldip Singh, Yogeshwar Dayal

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1674; (1994) Supp3 SCC 213; JT 1994 (4) SC 307; 1994 (3) PLR 433; 1994 (2) RentLR 164; 1994(3) SCALE 103; [1994] Supp1 S.C.R. 312

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Haryana Ceiling on Land Holding Act, 1972

Summary: Land & Property - Haryana Ceiling on Land Holding Act, 1972, ss. 12 and 33 - Punjab Security of Land Tenures Act, 1953 - Whether land having been declared as surplus, rightly alloted to appellants in view of fact that possession of surplus land remained with respondent till 1981 though proceedings were finalised in year 1961/1962? - There is no provision under Haryana Act to reopen surplus determined under Punjab Act u/s. 12(3) of Haryana Act, surplus land declared under Punjab Act stood vested in State with effect from appointed day (24/01/1971) and simply because surplus land declared under Punjab Act was not utilised and it remained in possession of respondent would not make any difference so far as position in law is concerned - Held, in present case surplus proceedings under Punjab Act had been finalised as back as 1961/1962 and non-utilisation of surplus land till date of vesting (23-12-1972) is of no consequence - Appeal allowed.
