Sukalu Ram Gond v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

05 August 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, N. Venkatachala

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 632; (1994) 5 SCC 570; 1994 (2) ARBLR 254; 1994 (2) CCC 798; JT 1994 (5) SC 331; 1994(3) SCALE 764; [1994] Supp2 S.C.R. 421; 1994 (2) UJ 793

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Arbitration Act, 1940, ss. 8, 21 and 30 - Arbitration award - Whether a person not a party to a reference but who participated in the award proceeding with objection and continued to participate in the proceedings under protest is bound by the award? - Held, not bound by the contract - Award derives its force from the original contract - Unless, a proper reference being made the arbitrator does not get the authority and jurisdiction to make the award against a non-party to the contract - Award of the Arbitrator is clearly without jurisdiction and authority and is accordingly set aside - Order accordingly.
