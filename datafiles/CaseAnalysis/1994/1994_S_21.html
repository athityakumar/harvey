Sooraj and Others v S.D.O. Rehli and Others

Supreme Court of India

22 November 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, K.S. Paripoornan

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 28920; 1995 (58) DLT 453

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Summary: Family & Personal - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, s. 80 - Hindu Succession Act, 1956, ss. 8 and 4(2) - Madhya Pradesh Ceiling on Agricultural Holdings Act, 1960, ss. 2(gg)and 46 - Madhya Pradesh Land Revenue Code, s. 164 - Determination of surplus land - Validity of order challenged - Whether Bhoomiswami right governed by s. 164 of Madhya Pradesh Land Revenue Code, is subject to operation of provisions of Hindu Succession Act, 1956? - Held, appellant squarely comes within 3rd cl. of Sub-section (2) of s. 4, namely, when Madhya Pradesh Ceiling on Agricultural Holdings Act, 1960 seeks to determine holding of agricultural lands by operation of Sub-section (2) of s. 4 - Applicability of Act shall stand excluded and should have no effect on operation of Madhya Pradesh Ceiling on Agricultural Holdings Act, 1960 - It is seen that under provisions of Madhya Pradesh Ceiling on Agricultural Holdings Act, 1960, surplus land shall stand vested in State - State has not been impleaded nominee as party-defendant to suit nor notice u/s. 80 of CPC was issued to State - First defendant is only a statutory authority under Ceiling Act - Therefore, without impleading State Govt. or Collector and without issuing notice to Govt. as enjoined u/s 80, suit itself is not maintainable and is liable to be dismissed on this ground - Even otherwise also, s. 2 (gg) of Act defines family means husband, wife and their minor children, if any - Admittedly, appellants are major daughters and that, therefore, only person who satisfies definition of family is widow of Ratan Singh - Accordingly, that determination of holding as prescribed under Act has been made by a Competent Authority, namely, first defendant - Thereby, appellants cannot get any right de hors provisions under Madhya Pradesh Ceiling on Agricultural Holdings Act, 1960 by operation of Sub-section (2) of s. 4 of Hindu Succession Act - S. 46 of Madhya Pradesh Ceiling on Agricultural Holdings Act creates a bar to maintain any civil suit - Thus right of appeal and right of revision, and jurisdiction of Civil Court has been barred to impugn any question settled or decision made or matter dealt with by Competent Authority under Act - Thereby Civil suit also has been barred by operation of s. 46 - Thus suit is not maintainable- Appeal dismissed.
