Shri Mahinder Kumar Gupta Etc. v Union Of India, Ministry Of Petroleum And Natural Gas

Supreme Court of India

22 September 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, N. Venkatachala

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1064; (1995) 1 SCC 85; AIR 1994 SCW 5139; 1995 (25) ALR 160; 1994 (3) CCC 545; 1995 (57) DLT 284; JT 1995 (1) SC 11; 1995 (1) MLJ 64; 1995 (1) MLJ(SC) 64; 1994(4) SCALE 803; 1995 (1) UJ 76

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 19(1)(g) and (6), 39(b) and (c), 298 and 299 - Contracts of dealership or distributorship of petroleum products - Whether Govt. justified in imposition of eligibility restrictions in award of retail outlets (other than 2/3 wheeler ROs), SKO-LD dealerships and LPG distributorship guidelines? - Guidelines are based on public policy to give effect to Constitutional creed of part IV of Indian Constitution and there is no arbitrariness or unjustness in guidelines - Held, there is a reasonable nexus between object and prescription of eligibility criteria envisaged in guidelines - Petition dismissed.
