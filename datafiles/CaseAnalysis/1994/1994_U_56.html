Bhubaneshwar Singh v Union Of India

Supreme Court of India

14 July 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

N.P. Singh, Kuldip Singh, P.B. Sawant

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 846; (1994) 6 SCC 77; JT 1994 (5) SC 83; 1994(3) SCALE 334; [1994] Supp1 S.C.R. 639

Case Digest

Subject: Mines & Minerals; Socio-Economic

Summary: Mines & Minerals - Socio-Economic - Coking Coal Mines Nationalisation Act, 1972, s .3 - Coal Mines Nationalisation Laws (Amendment) Act, 1986 - Whether Coal Mines Nationalisation Laws (Amendment) Ordinance, 1986 is valid? - Validating legislation must remove cause of invalidity - Till such defect or lack of authority pointed out by court under a statute is removed by subsequent enactment with retrospective effect, binding nature of judgment of court cannot be ignored - Held, lacuna or defect has been removed by introduction of sub-section (2) in Section 10 of Act with retrospective effect - Sub-section (2) of Section 10 as well as s. 19, both have specified that amount which is to be paid as compensation mentioned in schedule shall be deemed to include and deemed always to have included, amount required to be paid to such owner in respect of all coal in stock on date immediately before appointed day - Further held, authority to introduce sub-section (2) in s. 10 of Act, 1986 with retrospective effect cannot be questioned - Once amendment has been introduced retrospectively, courts have to act on basis that such provision was there since beginning - Hence reading sub-section (2) of s. 10 along with s. 10, it has to be held that respondents are not required to take into account stock of coke lying on date prior to appointed day, for purpose of accounting during period when mine in question was under management of Central Govt., because it shall be deemed that compensation awarded to petitioner included price for such coal lying in stock on date prior to appointed day - Neither any compensation is to be paid for such stock of coal nor price thereof is to be taken into account for purpose of s. 22(1) of Act, 1972 - Application dismissed.
