Itc Bhadrachalam Paper Boards Limited v Collector of Central Excise, Hyderabad

Supreme Court of India

15 April 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

B.L. Hansaria, B.P. Jeevan Reddy

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1483; (1994) Supp2 SCC 322; 1994 (52) ECR 331; 1994 (71) E.L.T. 334; JT 1994 (3) SC 284; 1995 (6) RLT 913; 1994(2) SCALE 585; [1994] 3 S.C.R. 531

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax

Summary: Customs - Indirect Tax - Central Excise Rules, 1944. r. 8(1) - Notification Nos. 83 of 1984 Central Excises, D/-2-4-1984 - Notification Nos. 214 of 1984 Central Excise, D/-9-11-1984 - Whether the appellant is entitled to the exemption from such portion of excise duty as has been spelt out in Notification No. 108 of 1981 Central Excises, D/-24-4-1981 read with Notification Nos. 83 of 1984 Central Excises, D/-2-4-1984 and 214 of 1984 Central Excise, D/-9-11-1984? - The expression "this notification" appearing in the last notification has to refer to the notification of April 1981 - Even if these words were to relate to the notification of November 1984 that cannot help the appellant in claiming exemption as notification of November 1984 had not granted any exemption - Held, what had been stated in the notification of 9-11-1984 cannot relate to the notification of April 1984 - Appeal dismissed
