Gauri Shanker v Union Of India

Supreme Court of India

08 September 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Paripoornan, M.N. Venkatachaliah, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1173; (1994) 6 SCC 349; AIR 1995 SC 55; AIR 1994 SCW 4059; 1994 (3) CCC 436; JT 1994 (5) SC 634; 1994 (2) RCJ 391; 1994 (2) RCR(Rent) 474; 1994 (2) RentLR 283; 1994(4) SCALE 29; [1994] Supp3 S.C.R. 244; 1994 (2) UJ 679

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - Constitution - Delhi Rent Control Act, 1958, ss.2(i) 2(iii) - Constitution, arts. 14 and 21 - Houses and Rents - Rights of statutory tenants of residential premises - Validity of rights of restriction - (A) Difference of statutory tenants of residential premises from statutory tenancies of commercial premises - (B) Whether limitation / restriction placed by s. 2(i)(iii) of 1958 Act on rights of heirs of statutory tenants of residential premises are reasonable and violative of arts. 14 and 21 - Held, both are distinct and different - Restrictions placed are reasonable - Not violative of arts. 14 and 21 - Appeal dismissed.
