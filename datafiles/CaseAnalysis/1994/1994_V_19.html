Vasant Manga Nikumba and Others v Baburao Bhikanna Naidu (Deceased) By Lrs. and Another

Supreme Court of India

11 February 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, R.M. Sahai

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1647; (1995) Supp4 SCC 54; (1996) SCC (Cr) 27

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, s. 133 - Indian Penal Code, s. 268 - Order for demolition of 15 shops - Whether facts of this case would warrant to invoke s. 133 - Condition precedent to exercise power u/s. 133 is imminent danger to property and consequential nuisance to public - Exercise of power should be one of judicious discretions objectively exercised on pragmatic consideration of given facts and circumstances from evidence on pragmatic consideration of given facts and circumstances from evidence on record - Held, it is not so imminently dangerous as to require building to be demolished immediately exercising power u/s. 133 CrPC - There is no warrant in exercising power u/s. 133 CrPC directing demolition of 15 shops in question - Appeal allowed.
