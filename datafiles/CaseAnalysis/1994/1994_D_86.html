Juthika Mulick v Dr Mahendra Yashwant Bal

Supreme Court of India

28 October 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mohan, M.K. Mukherjee

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1156; (1995) 1 SCC 560; AIR 1995 SC 1142; JT 1994 (7) SC 291; 1995 (1) RCJ 158; 1995 (1) RCR(Rent) 81; 1994 (2) RentLR 674; 1994(4) SCALE 792B; 1995 (1) SCJ 124; [1994] Supp5 S.C.R. 249

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Rent Control - West Bengal Premises Tenancy Act, 1956, s. 2(h) - Transfer of Property Act, 1882 - Heritable tenancy - Determination of tenancy - Respondents leased out suit premises on lease under lease deed dt.11-7-1966 with covenant that lease was for lifetime of lessee and after his death, his heirs, executors, administrators, representative and assigns should deliver peaceful and vacant possession of premises to respondents without any objection - While so, lessee died on 16-12-1970 and his heirs refused to deliver possession to respondents - Hence, respondents filed suit for eviction of appellants but Trial Court dismissed the suit - However, on appeal, HC held that lease was not heritable and so appellants would not fall within s. 2(h) of 1956 Act and statutory protection would not be available and allowed appeal - Hence, instant appeal by appellants - Whether appellants fall within definition of tenant u/s. 2(h) of 1956 Act and lease is covered by proviso to s. 3 of 1956 Act.

Held, even though lease deed had not stipulated any term, yet, it was capable of being made certain because nothing is more certain than death. Further, as per s. 111 of 1882 Act, period of lease need not be certain on the date of lease. Either by the terms of actual contract or by implication of law it is enough if it could be made certain on a future date. Since, lessee died within period of three years of lease, proviso to s. 3 of 1956 Act will not apply and so, Act will govern rights of parties. Further, in view of language of s. 13(1) of 1956 Act, the parties have freedom to contract out of section. In the lease deed, it was stipulated that heirs of lessee will have no right to hold after the death of lessee and they have to deliver quiet, peaceful and vacant possession within three months after the demise of the original lessee which means that right has been specifically not heritable. Hence, appellants could not claim tenancy rights. Appeal dismissed.

Ratio - As per s. 13 of 1956 Act, only if anything is found contrary in any other law that an order or decree for the recovery of possession of any premises will be made by any Court in favour of the landlord against tenant.
