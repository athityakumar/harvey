Ali Mustafa Abdul Rahman Moosa v State Of Kerala

Supreme Court of India

28 September 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

A.S. Anand, Faizan Uddin

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1762; (1994) 6 SCC 569; (1995) SCC (Cr) 32; AIR 1995 SC 244; AIR 1994 SCW 4393; 1995 (1) CCR 57; 1994 (3) Crimes 456; JT 1994 (6) SC 326; 1994(4) SCALE 328; 1995 (1) SCJ 339; [1994] Supp4 S.C.R. 52; 1995 (1) UJ 85

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - NDPS, 1985, ss. 20 and 50 - Conviction -

Whether 'Unlawful possession' of the contraband is the sine qua non for conviction under the NDPS Act and that factor has to be established by the prosecution beyond a reasonable doubt? - Held, seized contraband is evidence but in the absence of proof of possession of the same, an accused cannot be held guilty under the NDPS Act - In view of the law laid down in Balbir Singh case I, Court hold that there has been violation of the provisions of s. 50 of NDPS Act and consequently the conviction of the appellant cannot be sustained - Therefore, set aside the conviction and sentence o f the appellant - Appeal allowed.
