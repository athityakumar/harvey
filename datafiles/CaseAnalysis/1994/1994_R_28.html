State Of Haryana v Raghubir Dayal

Supreme Court of India

10 November 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, N. Venkatachala

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1113; (1995) 1 SCC 133; AIR 1995 SCW 46; 1998 (33) ALR 737; JT 1998 (4) SC 507; 1994(4) SCALE 1084; 1995 (1) SCJ 332; [1994] Supp5 S.C.R. 448; 1995 (1) UJ 120

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Land & Property - Municipalities & Local Governments - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4, 5 A and 6 - Whether publication of substance of declaration u/s. 6(1) is mandatory and omission to do so renders declaration invalid? - Held, purpose of declaration u/s. 6 is to render land notified therein as that needed conclusively for public purpose - Opportunity is already given to persons having interest in land to raise their objections during enquiry u/s. 5 A, or otherwise in case of dispensing with enquiry u/s. 5 A, unless they show any grave prejudice caused to them in non-publication of substance of declaration u/s. 6(1) or omission to publish substance of declaration u/s. 6(1) in locality would not render declaration of s. 6 or notification u/s. 4 as invalid, word 'shall' used in sub-section (2) of s. 6 should be construed to be only directory but not mandatory - Appeal allowed.
