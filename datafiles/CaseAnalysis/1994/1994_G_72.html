Union of India v Vasudevan Pillay

Supreme Court of India

08 December 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

B.L. Hansaria, Kuldip Singh

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 142; (1995) 2 SCC 32; (1995) SCC (L&S) 396; 1995 (29) ATC 180; 1995 (1) CLR 592; 1995 (71) FLR 244; JT 1995 (1) SC 417; 1995 LabIC 1415; 1995(1) SCALE 9; 1995 (2) SLR 21; [1994] Supp6 S.C.R. 405; 1995 (1) UJ 295

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - (A) Disallowing of Dearness Relief on pension on re-employment - Held, it is permissible in law, to deny Dearness Relief on pension on re-employment to any post with Central Govt. - (B) Denial of Dearness Relief on Family Pension on employment of dependents of ex servicemen? - Held, decision to be sustained in view of what is stated above - Denial legal and just - (C) Reduction of enhanced pension from pay of ex servicemen who were holding civil posts on 01-01-1986 following their re-employment - Held, decision arbitrary and violates arts. 14 and 16 of the Constitution of India, 1950 - Appeals disposed of.
