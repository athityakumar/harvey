Madhya Pradesh Hasta Shilpa Vikas Nigam Limited v Devendra Kumar Jain & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

07 December 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

Faizan Uddin, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 141; (1995) SCC (L&S) 364; (1995) 1 SCC 638; 1995 (29) ATC 159; 1995 (1) CLR 26; 1995 (70) FLR 330; JT 1995 (1) SC 198; 1995 LabIC 1365; 1995 (1) LLN 185; 1994(5) SCALE 164; 1995 (2) SLJ 70; 1995 (1) SLR 272; [1994] Supp6 S.C.R. 344

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 14 - Whether Govt. approval is necessary for appointment of respondent and whether their services were terminated without giving them an opportunity of hearing? - Order of appointment says that appointment was made purely on temporary basis and their services were liable to be terminated at any time without notice or assigning any reason - Appointment on temporary basis does not acquire any substantive right to post - Held, Corporation being a Govt. company fully financed by State Govt. would be concerned that any project which is not feasible or incurring losses should not be given effect to - Govt. is justified in issuing instructions that no appointments of any staff will be made without approval of Board of Directors - Order of High Court cannot be sustained - Appeal allowed.
