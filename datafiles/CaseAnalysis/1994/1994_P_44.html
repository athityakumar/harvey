Porcelain Electrical Manufacturing Company v Collector of Central Excise, New Delhi

Supreme Court of India

29 November 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Paripoornan, R.M. Sahai

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 709; (1998) 9 SCC 637; 1998 (98) E.L.T. 583

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Customs - Excise - Central Excise Rules, 1944, rr. 11 and 173-J - Whether Customs, Excise and Gold (Control) Appellate Tribunal was justified in rejecting claim for refund for period which was beyond six months from date duty was paid? - Held, since appellant had filed an application u/r. 11 r/w r. 173-J and sought its remedy under statute, it was bound by limitation provided under therein - It was not open to appellant to claim that even though period of limitation was provided under statute for refund, application filed by it should be processed and considered under general law of limitation - Appeal dismissed.
