N. Jayalakshmi Ammal v R. Gopala Pathar

Supreme Court of India

09 September 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Paripoornan, N. Venkatachala

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1547; (1995) Supp1 SCC 27; AIR 1995 SC 995; 1994 (3) CCC 117; 1994 (2) DMC 631; 1995 (1) HLR 332; JT 1994 (6) SC 19; 1995 (1) MLJ 88; 1995 (1) MLJ(SC) 88; 1994(4) SCALE 50; [1994] Supp3 S.C.R. 310

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Land and Property - Family and Personal - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 108 - Hindu Women's Right to Property Act, 1937, s. 3 - Inheritance by survivorship - Acquisition of property by two brothers and one of them not heard thereafter for seven years - (A) Whether presumption that he is dead arises - (B) Whether surviving brother (coparcener) entitled to property in entirety - (C) Whether wife inherits undivided half share in property - Held, (A) presumption that he is dead arises and no proof that he died on or after coming into force of Act of 1937 - (B) Surviving brother entitled to property by way of survivorship - (C) it cannot be presumed that he must be deemed to have died on or after 14-4-1937 when Act of 1937 came into force and that his wife became entitled to undivided (half) interest in the property - His rights and interest in the joint family devolves on surviving coparcener by 'survivorship' - Wife does not inherit - Appeal dismissed.
