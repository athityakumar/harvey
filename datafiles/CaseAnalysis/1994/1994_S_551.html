Bheru Singh S/O Kalyan Singh v State of Rajasthan

Supreme Court of India

04 February 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

A.S. Anand, Faizan Uddin

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1451; (1994) 2 SCC 467; (1994) SCC (Cr) 555; 1994 (31) ACC 240; AIR 1995 SCW 2126; 1994 (1) ALT 695; 1994 (2) AWC 686; 1994 (2) CCR 342; 1994 (1) Crimes 630; JT 1994 (1) SC 501; 1994(1) SCALE 353; [1994] 1 S.C.R. 559; 1994 (1) UJ 401

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Murder - Confession - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, s. 164 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 25 to 30 - Appellant murdered his wife and his five children on suspicion of infidelity by his wife and that he was not father of children - Whole incident was witnesses by his brother's wife - Appellant himself went to police station and surrendered - He made a confessional statement in police station - Whether confessional statement of appellant is not admissible in evidence? - Held, no - First information report is not a wholly confessional statement, but only that part of it is admissible in evidence - First information report discloses motive for murder and manner in which appellant committed six murders and produced blood stained sword - Relationship of appellant with deceased, motive for commission of crime and presence of prosecution witness does not amount to confession - These statements are non-confessional in nature and can be used against appellant as evidence - Murder committed in a gruesome manner - Barbaric, gruesome and heinous type of crime which the appellant committed is a revolt against the society and an affront to human dignity - Death sentence justified - Appeal dismissed.
