State Bank Of Hydrabad v Rangachary

Supreme Court of India

12 January 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, B.L. Hansaria

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 399; (1994) Supp2 SCC 479; (1994) SCC (L&S) 1022; 1994 (27) ATC 837; 1994(1) SCALE 633; 1995 (4) SLR 789

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Service

Summary: Banking & Finance - Service - Appointing authority - Enquiry officer - Respondent was a clerk in appellant bank - he was promoted to the post of Officer Grade II - Thereafter he was working as Grade I Officer - Certain complaints were received and after obtaining his explanation and three charges were framed against him - Enquiry Officer was appointed to inquire into those charges - Enquiry Officer held that charges 1 and 2 were not proved but charge 3 was proved - Matter was placed before the disciplinary authority - Disciplinary authority agreed with the findings of the Enquiry Officer and since he was not competent to impose major penalty - He placed the entire record along with his recommendations before the appointing authority - Authority found charges 1 and 2 also proved - He was being imposed the punishment of compulsory retirement - Respondent approached the HC by way of W.P. - D.B. allowed the W.P. on the ground that the copy of the Enquiry Officer's report was not supplied to the respondent before imposing the punishment - Hence this appeal - Whether the HC was right in allowing the W.P. on the ground that the copy of the Enquiry Officer's report was not supplied to the respondent before imposing the punishment - Held, it was open to the appointing authority to disagree with the findings of the Enquiry Officer which may had been affirmed by the disciplinary authority and yet find that the articles of charge were not proved - Where the findings were in favour of the delinquent officer he can disagree with them and hold the officer guilty - This was the true construction of sub regulation (3) read as a whole - Appointing authority cannot be constricted by the opinions of either the Enquiry Officer or the disciplinary authority nor can he be reduced to a mere instrument for imposing higher punishment - Appeal allowed.
