Sukhvinder Singh and Others v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

12 May 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

A.S. Anand, Faizan Uddin

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1697; (1994) 5 SCC 152; (1994) SCC (Cr) 1376; 1994 (2) CCR 531; 1994 (2) Crimes 904; 1994 JCC 495; JT 1994 (4) SC 1; 1994(2) SCALE 877; [1994] 3 S.C.R. 1061; 1994 (2) UJ 580

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Socio-Economic

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120 B, 149 and 302 - Terrorist and Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Act, 1987, s. 3 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 27 and 73 - (A) Admissibility of statements before police - Held, s. 27 of Evidence Act is an exception to general rule that a statements made before police is not admissible in evidence is not in doubt - However, vide s. 27 of Evidence Act, only so much of statement of an accused is admissible in evidence as distinctly leads to discovery of a fact - Therefore, once fact has been discovered, s. 27 of Evidence Act cannot again be made use of to 'rediscover' discovered fact - It would be a total misuse even abuse of provisions of s. 27 of Evidence Act - (B) Applicability of s. 73 - Held, Court which can issue a direction to person to give his specimen writing can either by court holding enquiry under Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 or court trying accused person with a view to enable it to compare specimen writings with writings alleged to have been written by such a person - Court which is not holding an enquiry under CrPC, 1973, or conducting trial is not permitted, on plain language of s. 73 of Evidence Act, 1872, to issue any direction of nature contained in second paragraph of s. 73 of Evidence Act - Words 'any person present in court' in s. 73 has a reference only to such persons who are parties to a cause pending before court and in a given case may even include witnesses in said cause but where there is no cause pending before court for its determination, question of obtaining for purposes of comparison of handwriting of a person may not arise at all and therefore provisions of s. 73 of Evidence Act would have no application - Order accordingly.
