Veeramani v State of Tamil Nadu

Supreme Court of India

04 February 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Jayachandra Reddy, G.N. Ray

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1450; (1994) 2 SCC 337; (1994) SCC (Cr) 482; AIR 1995 SCW 1730; 1994 (2) CCR 286; 1994 (1) Crimes 617; 1995 CRLJ 2644; JT 1994 (1) SC 350; 1994(1) SCALE 363; [1994] 1 S.C.R. 616; 1994 (1) UJ 524

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Labour & Industrial Law; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Tamil Nadu Prevention of Dangerous Activities of Bootleggers, Drug Offenders, Forest Offenders, Goonndas, Immoral Traffic Offenders and Slum Grabbers Act, 1982, S. 3 - Constitution -art. 32 - Preventive detention - Challenging preventive detention imposed on petitioner by making a representation about same - Question as to whether an order of detention can be passed against a person who is in detention or in jail, will always have to be determined in circumstances of each case - From a catena of decisions clear that even in case of a person in custody, a detention order can validly be passed if authority passing order is aware of fact that he is actually in custody - If reason to believe on basis of reliable material that there is a possibility of his being released on bail and that on being so released, detenu would in all probabilities indulge in prejudicial activities and if authority passes an order after recording his satisfaction same cannot be struck down - Held, grounds in instant case disclosed that detaining authority was aware that detenu is in custody - So far as Tamil Nadu Act, 1982 is concerned, any detention order made by empowered officer shall cease to be in operation if not approved within 12 days - Therefore, it is clear that Act never contemplated that detaining in authority has specific power to revoke and it cannot be inferred that a of Constitution of India, 1950. provisions of Act are clear and lay down that detention order has to be approved within 12 days and where there is no such approval, it stands revoked - Therefore representation to be made by detenu, after earliest opportunity was afforded to him can be only to Govt. which has power to approve or to revoke - That being position question of detenu being informed specifically in grounds that had also a right to make a representation to detaining authority itself besides State Govt. does not arise - Petition dismissed.
