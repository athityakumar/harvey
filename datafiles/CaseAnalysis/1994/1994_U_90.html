Mohd. Aslam Alias Obhure, Acchan Rizvi v Union of India State of Uttar Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

24 October 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

G.N. Ray, M.N. Venkatachaliah

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1141; (1994) 6 SCC 442; AIR 1995 SC 548; AIR 1994 SCW 4897; JT 1994 (7) SC 245; 1994(4) SCALE 677; 1995 (1) SCJ 152; [1994] Supp5 S.C.R. 104

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Tort

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Contempt of Courts Act, 1971, s. 2 - Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - (A) Positive acts of violation of order of court and surreptitious aids to violation both are impermissible - State of Uttar Pradesh made representation before Supreme Court that it would implement orders of High Court as regards land acquired near Ramjanmabhoomi Babri Masjid - Despite of prohibition, massive construction - No steps taken by State to prevent large scale building material and constructional machinery entering sight - (B) Disobedience to order of Court by State Govt., Chief Minister and Ministers - If can be guilty of contempt - Held, Govt. cannot be heard to say that violation was by others (sadhus) - Further held, Govt. can be guilty of, and they can be liable for contempt both in official and personal capacity - Petition disposed of.
