State of Rajasthan v Puri Construction Company Limited and Anothers

Supreme Court of India

16 September 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

G.N. Ray, M.N. Venkatachaliah

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1035; (1994) 6 SCC 485; AIR 1994 SCW 5061; 1995 (1) ARBLR 1; 1994 (3) CCC 393; JT 1994 (6) SC 412; 1994(4) SCALE 419; [1994] Supp3 S.C.R. 616; 1995 (1) UJ 179

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Practice & Procedure - Arbitration Act, 1940, ss. 15, 17, 18, 20, 28 and 29 - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, s. 34 - Scope and ambit of power of interference by Court with an award made by an arbitrator in a valid reference to arbitration - Held, arbitrator is final arbiter for dispute between parties and it is not open to challenge award on ground that arbitrator has drawn his own conclusion or has failed to appreciate facts - Therefore, where reasons germane and relevant for arbitrator to hold in manner he did, have been indicated, it cannot be said that reasons are unreasonable - It is not necessary to indicate in award computation made for various heads and it is open to arbitrator to give a lump sum award - A court of competent jurisdiction has both right and duty to decide lis presented before it for adjudication according to best understanding of law and facts involved in lis by judge presiding over court - Such decision even if erroneous either in factual determination or application of law correctly, is a valid one and binding inter parties - It does not, therefore, stand to reason that arbitrator's award will be per se invalid and inoperative for simple reason that arbitrator has failed to appreciate facts and has committed error in appreciating correct legal principle in basing award - An award rendered by an arbitrator is open to challenge within parameters of several provisions of Arbitration Act - Challenge to validity of impugned award is to be considered with reference to judicial decisions on subject - Order accordingly.
