Pankaj Bhargava v Mohinder Nath

Supreme Court of India

02 September 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, S.C. Sen

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 759; (1994) 6 SCC 4; 1994 (3) CCC 443; 1994 (55) DLT 658; JT 1994 (5) SC 468; 1994 (2) RCJ 408; 1994 (2) RCR(Rent) 481; 1994 (2) RentLR 265; 1994(3) SCALE 946; 1994 (2) UJ 549

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Rent Control

Summary: Civil Procedure - Rent Control - Delhi Rent Control Act, 1958 - Contempt petition - Whether judgement debtor tenant has committed contempt by violating undertaking of vacating house? - Held, Ram Prakash executed and furnished an undertaking to this Court to vacate premises and to hand over same to landlords on or before 30-4-1991 - Respondents took up plea that a new tenancy was created between parties by mutual agreement on 15-4-1991 whereunder not only rent was enhanced but certain amounts were also paid to landlords by him - Petitioners denied any such agreement and receipt of monies - Characterised it as a forgery and fabrication - Court by its order dated 20-8-1991 directed District Judge to enquire into truth of claim - District Judge found that claim set up by respondent was false, that no such fresh tenancy was created on 15-4-1991 nor any amount paid by him to landlords as alleged by him - Contempt proceedings are summary proceedings - Respondent has violated order of this Court D\ 17-12-1990 and came forward with a false case and tried to support same by producing fabricated documents, he is held guilty of contempt of court - Contempt petition disposed of.
