G. Ramachandra Reddy v Chief Engineer

Supreme Court of India

29 April 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, N. Venkatachala

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1519; (1994) 5 SCC 142; AIR 1994 SC 2381; AIR 1994 SCW 2413; 1994 (2) ARBLR 61; 1994 (2) CCC 492; JT 1994 (4) SC 181; 1994(2) SCALE 1057; [1994] 3 S.C.R. 808

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration Act, 1940, s. 20 - Appellant's contract was terminated by the respondent and in consequence thereof, by notices dt. 23-7-1991 and 21-8-1991, the appellants exercising the option u/cl. 17 of General Condition of Contract, called upon the Engineer-in-Chief to appoint sole Arbitrator, in terms of the contract, to adjudicate the dispute that had arisen between them - Since no action was taken by the respondents, the appellant filed a suit u/s. 20 of the Act', requesting the Court to appoint an arbitrator - Single Judge appointed a retired Judge of the High Court as sole Arbitrator - On appeal, a DB agreed with the Single Judge that despite the issue of notice calling upon the respondent to appoint the Arbitrator in terms of the contract, no action was taken by the respondent - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, when the notice was given to the opposite contracting party to appoint an arbitrator in terms of the contract and if no action had been taken, it must be deemed that he neglected to act upon the contract. When no agreement was reached, even in the court between the parties, the court gets jurisdiction and power to appoint an arbitrator. In the instant case the respondent did not appoint an arbitrator, after the notice was received. The respondent averred in the written statement that it was under consideration. Even before Single Judge he did not even state that he was willing to appoint an arbitrator. Single Judge rightly exercised the power u/s. 20(4) of the Act and appointed the Arbitrator. DB, therefore, was not right in holding that the appellant has by giving option to the respondent to agree for appointment of an arbitrator out of the five named persons had left it to the respondent to appoint an arbitrator and allowing respondent to appoint an arbitrator. On the other hand, the appointment of an arbitrator made by the Single Judge must be deemed to have been approved by SC. The judgment of the Division Bench is set aside and that of the Single Judge is restored. Appeal allowed.
