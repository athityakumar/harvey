D. K. Jain and Others v State of Haryana and Others

Supreme Court of India

25 November 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

N.P. Singh, R.M. Sahai

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 702; (1995) Supp1 SCC 349; (1995) SCC (L&S) 491; 1995 (29) ATC 333; 1995 (1) CLR 401; 1995 (70) FLR 871; JT 1994 (7) SC 751; 1995 LabIC 722; 1994(4) SCALE 1121; 1995 (1) SCJ 250; 1995 (2) SLJ 9; 1995 (1) SLR 48; [1994] Supp6 S.C.R. 104

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Armed Forces, Punjab Reorganisation Act, 1966, Punjab Service Of Engineers, Class I, Pwd (Public Health Branch) Rules, 1961

Summary: Service - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 309 - States Reorganisation Act, 1956 - Punjab Service of Engineers, Class I, PWD (Public Health Branch) Rules, 1961, r. 9 - (A) Whether consideration of period spent in military service for purpose of seniority is valid? - Held, states have framed rules u/art. 309 of Constitution, in order to give benefit of seniority to persons, who were commissioned in Armed Forces at time of foreign aggression - They have been treated as a separate class - (B) Whether relaxation regarding holding of degree at stage of initial appointment as Assistant Engineers and at stage of promotion as Executive Engineers will enure while they are to be considered for promotion to Superintending Engineer? - Further Further held, on basis of certificate granted by appointing authority, person concerned shall be deemed to possess qualifications equivalent to qualifications prescribed for being appointed to service and post - It cannot be held that on basis of that certificate it shall be deemed that such a person possesses qualifications in question - Appeals allowed.
