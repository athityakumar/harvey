C.C.E. v Popular Cotton Covering Works

Supreme Court of India

22 August 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Bharucha, K.S. Paripoornan, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1387; (1994) 5 SCC 727; 1994 (54) ECR 305; 1994 (73) E.L.T. 264; JT 1994 (5) SC 292; 1994(3) SCALE 841B

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - Whether winding up of cotton or fibre-lass yarn results in manufacture of new product i.e., insulated wire, liable for excise duty under Tariff Item 33-B? - Excise authorities did not lead any evidence to establish that winding of cotton or fibreglass yam upon bare copper or aluminium electric wire constituted a process of manufacture or brought into existence a new commercially recognised article - Held, appellate authority's finding in this regard was justified and his order, as also order of Tribunal sustaining it, must be upheld - Appeal dismissed.
