State of Bihar and Others v Universal Hydrocarbons Company Limited and Another

Supreme Court of India

12 August 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mohan, M.N. Venkatachaliah

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 268; (1994) Supp3 SCC 621; AIR 1995 SC 991; JT 1994 (6) SC 118; 1994(4) SCALE 159; 1995 (96) STC 204; [1994] Supp2 S.C.R. 627

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Sales Tax

Keywords: Declared Goods, Bihar Finance Act, 1981, Bihar Sales Tax Rules, 1983, Central Sales Tax (Amendment) Act, 1972

Summary: Sales Tax - Constitution - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956, ss. 15(b) - Bihar Sales Tax Rules 1983, r. 35 - Finance Act, 1981 - Bihar Finance Act, 1981 - Sales tax - Claim for refund - Refusal - Entitlement - Sustainability - Respondent purchased raw petroleum product which resulted into different commodity, Calcined Petroleum Coke (CPC), after subjecting it to manufacturing process in factory - Respondent was entitled to refund of sales tax paid under the 1981 Act and stated that he missed to claim adjustment of sales tax paid on purchase of raw materials in returns filed for July and August, 1990 - While filing return for September, 1990, respondent did not pay admitted tax of Rs. 1,96,072 but claimed refund of Rs 5,22,728 which would be adjusted towards admitted tax of 1,96,072 and balance of Rs 3,26,656 was to be refunded on account of Bihar Sales Tax paid on direct raw materials purchased for July, August and September, 1990 - Assistant Commissioner considered application for refund but dismissed the claim as it was against law - By Order dt. 8-1-1990, penalty of Rs 9,852 was imposed and against this order, respondent preferred C.WJ.C No. 7549/90 - Later, respondent filed application for refund for Rs 19,22,340.12 for period 1985-86 and Rs. 17,65,987.01 for period of 1986-87 u/s. 15(b) of 1956 Act r/w with r. 35 of the 1983 Rules - While application was pending, respondent preferred C.W.J.C no. 5813/91 before HC - HC by order dt. 5-9-1991 directed authorities to consider his claim of refund - On consideration of the matter, Joint Commissioner by order dated 16-12-1991 rejected respondent's claim - Thereupon, respondent preferred C.WJ.C. 415 of 1992 -

By its impugned order, HC allowed both petitions, CWJC No. 7549/90 and CWJC 415 of 1992, and set aside findings of Joint Commissioner while holding that CPC was form of RPC and therefore petitioners would be entitled to exemption u/s. 15(b) of the Act - Hence, instant appeal - Held, when entry said 'coke in all its forms', there would be no possibility of bringing coke of different forms except under this entry - Joint Commissioner had clearly held that both Raw Petroleum Coke (RPC) and CPC, though different commercial commodities were 'declared goods' - However, Joint Commissioner held that by process of manufacture, RPC had lost its original identity and resulted in new product, namely, CPC - Therefore, according to him, benefit u/s. I5 (b) of the 1956 Act could be availed of only if same goods were subjected to Inter-State levy of tax - Joint Commissioner opined use of words 'such goods' u/s. 15 (b) of the 1956 Act were of significance - This line of reasoning was totally unacceptable - Once entry was 'Coke in all its forms' irrespective of RPC lost its original identity or in process of manufacture CPC was produced, should not take CPC out of purview of this entry - CPC was different commodity was of little consequence - In interpreting scope of Hides and Skins which fall u/s. 14(i), (iii) of the Act, SC held, 'it is hardly material that coloured leather may be form of leather or may even be said to represent different commercial commodity - Statutory entry was comprehensive enough to include products emerging from hides and skins until process of dressing or finishing was done' - India Carbon Ltd, v. Superintendent of Taxes, Gauhati and others, 1971 Indlaw SC 230, relied on - No merit in appeal - Appeal dismissed.
