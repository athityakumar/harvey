Ex-Capt. Ashwani Kumar Katoch v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

17 August 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, B.L. Hansaria

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 968; (1995) Supp4 SCC 715; 1996 (32) ATC 174; 1995 (8) SLR 645

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces

Keywords: Court Martial

Summary: Defence & Security Services - Army Act, 1950 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Enquiry - Misconduct - Evidence - Termination - Appellant was confirmed as Captain in Indian Army faced court of enquiry for alleged misconduct that he had committed irregularities - Appellant addressed letter to Law Minister alleged against his superiors - Authorities/Respondent contended that appellant had violated requirement of regn.557 as he had committed grave irregularity and was guilty of misconduct unbecoming of Army Officer - Appellant explanation to show cause notice did not impress authorities and he was removed from service - Appellant filed W.P. in HC was allowed - Respondent appealed to Division Bench of HC was allowed - Hence instant appeal - Whether service termination of appellant was justified - Held, appellant denied that he had written letter but Division Bench of HC found intrinsic evidence in letter betraying appellant as author - It would not possible to uphold that appellant had not written letter -Appellant had committed grave irregularity and was guilty of misconduct unbecoming of Army Officer - Appellant was let off leniently in hope that he would correct himself , hence it was difficult to hold, in requirement of army discipline in mind, that authorities had erred in terminating his services - It would be reasonable to award sum of compensation to appellant on termination of service - Appeal dismissed.
