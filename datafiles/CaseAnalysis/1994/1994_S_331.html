 Sanjay Dutt v State Through C. B. I., Bombay (Ii)

Supreme Court of India

09 September 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, N.P. Singh, P.B. Sawant, B.P. Jeevan Reddy, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1008; (1994) 5 SCC 410; (1994) SCC (Cr) 1433; AIR 1994 SCW 3857; 1995 (1) Bom.C.R. 186; 1994 (4) CCR 744; 1994 (3) Crimes 344; 1995 CRLJ 477; JT 1994 (5) SC 540; 1994(3) SCALE 1004; [1994] Supp3 S.C.R. 263

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Terrorist And Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Amendment Act, 1985, TADA Act, Arms Rules, 1962, Emergency Regulations, 1948, Inflammable Substances Act, 1952

Summary: (A) Criminal - Arms Act, 1959 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 167, 167(1) and 20 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 210 - Inflammable Substances Act, 1952 - Terrorist And Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Act, 1985 - Terrorist and Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Act, 1987 - Terrorist and Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Amendment Act, 1985, s. 20 - Matters relating to the grant of bail to the petitioner, an accused in the Bombay blast's case had been referred in regard to only questions of law - Hence, the present reference - Whether there was any nexus with terrorist activity requisite to prove an offence, u/s. 5 of the TADA Act - Held, that in the prosecution for an offence punishable u/s. 5 of TADA Act, the prosecution was required to prove that the accused was in conscious 'possession' , 'unauthorisedly', in 'a notified area' of any arms and ammunition or bombs, dynamite or other explosive substances, and no further nexus with any terrorist or disruptive activity was required to be proved by the prosecution in view of the statutory presumption - Accused in his defence was entitled to prove the non- existence of a fact constituting any of the ingredients - Accused could prove by adducing evidence, the nonexistence of facts constituting the third ingredient as indicated earlier to rebut the statutory presumption, that the purpose of his unauthorised possession of any such arms and ammunition etc. was wholly unrelated to any terrorist or disruptive activity - Order accordingly.

(B) Criminal - Arms Act, 1959 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 167, 167(1) and 20 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 210 - Inflammable Substances Act, 1952 - Terrorist And Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Act, 1985 - Terrorist and Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Act, 1987 - Terrorist and Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Amendment Act, 1985, s. 20 - Whether there was a requirement to give notice, u/s. 20(4)(bb), of the 1985 Act - Held, that s. 20(4)(bb) of 1985 Act only required production of the accused before the court in accordance with s. 167(1) of the CrPC, and this was how the requirement of notice to the accused before granting extension beyond the prescribed period of 180 days in accordance with the further proviso to cl. (bb) of sub-section (4) of s. 20 of 1985 Act had to be understood - Requirement of such notice to the accused before granting the extension for completing the investigation was not a written notice to the accused giving reasons therein - Production of the accused at that time in the court informing him that the question of extension of the period for completing the investigation was being considered, was alone sufficient for the purpose - Order accordingly.

(C) Criminal - Arms Act, 1959 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 167, 167(1) and 20 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 210 - Inflammable Substances Act, 1952 - Terrorist And Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Act, 1985 - Terrorist and Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Act, 1987 - Terrorist and Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Amendment Act, 1985, s. 20 - Whether the accused had a right for grant of bail - Held, that the indefeasible right accruing to the accused in such a situation was enforceable only prior to the filing of the challan and it did not survive or remain enforceable on the challan being filed, if already not availed of - If that right had accrued to the accused but it remained unenforced till the filing of the challan, then there was no question of its enforcement thereafter since it was extinguished the moment challan was filed because s. 167 CrPC would cease to apply - Accused person seeking bail u/s. 20(4), of the 1985 Act, had to make an application to the court for grant of bail on grounds of the 'default' of the prosecution and the court should release the accused on bail after notice to the public prosecutor uninfluenced by the gravity of the offence or the merits of the prosecution case - Order accordingly
