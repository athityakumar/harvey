Prakash Chand Sharma v Vimlesh Smt

Supreme Court of India

27 July 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, B.L. Hansaria

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 920; (1995) Supp4 SCC 642

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Summary: Family & Personal - Hindu Marriage Act, 1955, ss. 13 and 15 - Petition for divorce - On grounds of desertion, cruelty and unsoundness of mind - Trial court decreed divorce on desertion and cruelty rejecting unsoundness of mind - First Appellate Court, affirmed decree on cruelty only - Husband remarried in meantime - In second appeal High Court reversed decree holding that husband had failed to prove cruelty - Whether cruelty by wife towards husband proved in facts and circumstances of case? - Held, HC, therefore, was right in evaluation of evidence and was justified in taking view that it was wholly unwarranted to reach a conclusion that wife was guilty of causing mental cruelty, on such skeleton material there is no merit in appeal - Fact that husband has remarried and has a child through second marriage it cannot be said that marriage has irretrievably broken - From very beginning wife has been saying that she is ready and wiling to live with husband - It is husband who is denying her access husband has remarried in hot haste after institution of second appeal which was delayed by only three days and therefore he cannot take up this plea - That is clearly opposed to s. 15 of Hindu Marriage Act which in terms states that when a marriage is dissolved by a decree of divorce and there is no right of appeal against decree or where there is such a right, time for filing an appeal has expired or an appeal has been presented and has been dismissed, it shall be lawful for either party to marriage to remarry - In instant case no doubt second appeal was delayed by three days but fact is that it was instituted and was pending on date of second marriage - Therefore, husband acted in disregard of s. 15 and cannot get benefit of his own wrong - Wife expressed her willingness to go and live with husband notwithstanding presence of other woman but husband was not in a position to agree presumably because he has changed his position by remarriage - Be that as it may, a reconciliation was not possible - Appeal dismissed.
