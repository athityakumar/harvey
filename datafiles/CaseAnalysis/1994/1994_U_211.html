Union Of India v Upendra Singh

Supreme Court of India

17 February 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, B.L. Hansaria

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1342; (1994) 3 SCC 357; (1994) SCC (L&S) 768; AIR 1994 SCW 2777; 1994 (27) ATC 200; 1994 (2) AWC 919; 1994 (1) CLR 534; 1994 (68) FLR 762; [1994] 207 ITR 782; JT 1994 (1) SC 658; 1994 (1) LLJ 808; 1994 (1) LLN 895; 1994(1) SCALE 637; [1994] 1 S.C.R. 1070; 1994 (2) SLJ 77; 1994 (1) SLR 831; 1994 (1) UJ 434; 1994 (2) UPLBEC 966

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income-tax Act, 1961 - Assessing Officer - Review - Respondent was a member of the Indian Revenue Service and was working as Deputy Commissioner of Income Tax - Memorandum of charges was issued to him accompanied by a 'Statement of imputations of misconduct or misbehaviour in support of art.of charges' framed against him - Respondent approached Tribunal for quashing the charges - Tribunal admitted the original application and passed the interim order - Against the interim order, Union of India approached this court - Appeal was allowed by this Court by its order and Tribunal was directed to deal with the matter - When the matter went back to the Tribunal, it went into the correctness of the charges on the basis of the material produced by respondent and quashed the charges holding that the charges did not indicate any corrupt motive or any culpability on the part of respondent - Hence, the present appeal - Whether findings of Tribunal would ultimately be upheld - Held, disciplinary inquiry could be held even with respect to the conduct of an officer in discharge of his judicial or quasi- judicial duties - Therefore, this Court set out the situations in which disciplinary action could be taken with respect to the judicial/quasi-judicial conduct - The officer who exercised judicial or quasijudicial powers acted negligently or recklessly or in order to confer undue favour on a person was not acting as a Judge - Accordingly, the contention of respondent had to be rejected but case was concerned with the conduct of respondent in discharge of his duties as an officer - Respondent did not fall within any of the clauses of disciplinary action - Hence, the order of the Tribunal was set aside - Appeal allowed.
