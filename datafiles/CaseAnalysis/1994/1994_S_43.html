State Bank of India v Samaredra Kishore Endow

Supreme Court of India

08 January 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, B.L. Hansaria

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 359; (1994) 2 SCC 537; (1994) SCC (L&S) 687; AIR 1995 SCW 1465; 1994 (27) ATC 149; 1995 (1) BC 598; 1994 (68) FLR 754; JT 1994 (1) SC 217; 1994 (1) LLJ 872; 1994 (2) LLN 754; 1994(1) SCALE 206; 1994 (1) SLR 516; 1994 (2) UJ 53

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Service - State Bank of India (Supervising Staff) Service Rules, r. 51(2) - Respondent, Branch Manager in appellant-bank - Subjected to a disciplinary inquiry on five charges -- Penalty of removal - High Court set aside order of removal - (A) Whether findings of HC that non-supply of Inquiry Officer's report before imposing penalty vitiates order of punishment? - Held, this ground given by HC is not sustainable in law in view of recent decision of Constitution Bench of Supreme Court in Managing Director, ECIL, Hyderabad v B Karunakarl in as much as order of punishment in this case is prior to 20-11-1990 - (B) Whether findings of Inquiry Officer and Disciplinary Authority on charges 1 to 3 and 5 are based on no evidence and must therefore be characterised as perverse, is correct? - Held, it cannot be said that finding of Inquiry Officer was based on no evidence - Finding of Inquiry Officer is certainly based upon terms and conditions of loan contained in loan document and fact that entire amount of loan was disbursed in course of three days - So far as charge 5 is concerned there is no finding that account become irregular or that any loss was incurred by bank on account of irregularity committed by respondent - In circumstances it may be that punishment of removal imposed upon respondent is harsh but this is a matter which Disciplinary Authority or Appellate Authority should consider and not HC or Administrative Tribunal - Matter send back to Disciplinary Authority to impose appropriate punishment - Appeal allowed.
