Ram Prasad v Assistant Director Of Consolidation

Supreme Court of India

30 March 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, N. Venkatachala

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1437; (1994) Supp2 SCC 228; AIR 1994 SC 2733; JT 1994 (3) SC 519; 1994 RD 299; [1994] 3 S.C.R. 231

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Women & Children

Keywords: Female Heir, Succession & Inheritance

Summary: Rent Control - Family & Personal - Hindu Succession Act, 1956 - U.P. Tenancy Act, 1939, ss. 59 and 33(2), Proviso - Suit for declaration as to co-tenancy u/s. 59 - Ex parte decree - Decree found by Tribunals as collusive and fraudulent - Tenant was limited owner - No ratification of alleged joint tenancy rights of respondent was made by tenant as absolute owner on or after July 1, 1952 i.e., after she became absolute owner under U.P. Zamindari Abolition and Land Reforms Act or Succession Act on or after June 17, 1956 - Respondent, therefore, does not acquire any right as a joint tenant in disputed land - Whether finding by High Court that tenant being owner, has right of alienation, sustainable? - Held, absolutely unsustainable - Appeal allowed.
