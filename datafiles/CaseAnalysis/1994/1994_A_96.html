Chandra Shashi v Anil Kumar Verma

Supreme Court of India

14 November 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

B.L. Hansaria, Kuldip Singh

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 40; (1995) SCC (Cr) 239; (1995) 1 SCC 421; AIR 1994 SCW 4994; 1995 (78) E.L.T. 7; JT 1994 (7) SC 459; 1994(4) SCALE 944; 1995 (1) SCJ 84; [1994] Supp5 S.C.R. 465; 1995 (1) UJ 242

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Contempt of Courts Act, 1971, s. 2 - Respondent filed a forged and fabricated document to resist prayer of his wife to get matrimonial proceeding transferred on ground of poverty - Whether filing of forged and fabricated documents amounts to contempt of court? - Held, if publication be with intent to deceive court or one made with an intention to defraud, same would be contempt, as it would interfere with administration of justice - In case at hand, fabricated document was apparently to deceive court - Intention to defraud is writ large - Respondent, therefore, guilty to contempt - Thereafter, an offer of unconditional apology tendered by respondent - Whether respondent be set free on basis of apology tendered? - Further held, had contemner shown real contriteness and regret for act done, court would have perhaps accepted his apology, but as it cannot be used as a weapon of defence to get purged of guilt, which precisely contemner has sought to do as he desires to avoid worldly suffering which would follow if sentenced - Rejecting his offer and proceed to decide question of sentence - Imprisonment of two weeks awarded - Application disposed of.
