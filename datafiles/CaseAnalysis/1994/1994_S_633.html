Union of India and Another v Sudhir Kumar Jaiswal

Supreme Court of India

04 May 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

B.L. Hansaria, Kuldip Singh

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1471; (1994) 4 SCC 212; (1994) SCC (L&S) 925; AIR 1994 SC 2750; 1994 (27) ATC 561; 1994 (69) FLR 49; JT 1994 (3) SC 547; 1995 (1) LLJ 1173; 1994 (1) LLN 844; 1994(2) SCALE 808; [1994] 3 S.C.R. 886; 1994 (3) SLJ 1; 1994 (2) SLR 674; 1994 (2) UJ 314; 1994 (2) UPLBEC 1341; 1994 (2) UPLBEC 1385

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Keywords: Civil Services Examination

Summary: Service - Civil Services Examination Rules, 1992 - Whether fixation of 1st August as cut-off date for determining eligibility of applicants desirous of sitting in competitive examination for recruitment to Indian Administrative Service/Indian Foreign Service qua their age can be held to be arbitrary despite preliminary examination being conducted before that date? - Held, preliminary examination is only a screening order of merit, for which purpose marks obtained in main examination, which is still being held after 1st August, alone are material - In view of this, it cannot be held that continuation of treating 1st August as cut-off date, despite Union Public Service Commission having introduced method of preliminary examination which is held before 1st August, can be said to be 'very wide off any reasonable mark' or so capricious or whimsical as to permit judicial interference - Equity does not demand any favour to be shown to respondent - Respondent would not be treated or deemed to have passed examination in question and whatever benefit of same was given to him pursuant to Tribunal's directions shall stand cancelled - Appeal allowed.
