Arjun Marik v State Of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

02 March 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

Faizan Uddin, A.S. Anand

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1936; (1994) Supp2 SCC 372; (1994) SCC (Cr) 1551; AIR 2001 SCW 2322; 1994 (1) AWC 492; 1994 (2) CCR 445; 1994 (1) Crimes 777; 1994 JCC 388; JT 1994 (2) SC 627; 1994(1) SCALE 821; [1994] 2 S.C.R. 265; 1994 (1) UJ 610

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Tort

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 394 and 411 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, ss. 156, 157 and 159 - Appellants convicted u/s. 302 and sentenced them to death - Also convicted u/ss. 394 and 411 - Appeal - Held, from evidence led it is difficult to conclude that appellant could have entertained any idea or motive to do away with the deceased with a view to wash off alleged loans against him - Delay of filing FIR - No reasonable cause stated - Evidence led shows that appellant was man of means and status and he would have hardly resorted to such criminal act as has been alleged against him - Evidence of witness has not been found - Finding recorded by the trial court and High Court holding the appellants guilty of the offences charged with is erroneous and unsustainable - Appeals allowed.
