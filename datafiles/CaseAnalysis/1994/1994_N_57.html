Food Corpn. Of India v New India Assurance Co. Ltd

Supreme Court of India

15 February 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Sahai, S.P. Bharucha, N. Venkatachala

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1945; (1994) 3 SCC 324; AIR 1994 SC 1889; 1994 (1) CCC 596; [1995] 84 Comp Cas 214; JT 1994 (1) SC 703; 1995 (1) MLJ(SC) 1; [1994] 1 S.C.R. 939; 1994 (2) UJ 197

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Corporate; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Contract and Commercial - Limitation Act, 1908 - Contract Act ,1972, ss. 23 and 28 - Insurance Act ,1938 - Fidelity Insurance is different from contingency guarantee - FCI entered into such insurance agreement - It contain restriction that the insured corporation shall have no right under this bond after the expiry of (period) six months from the date of termination of principal contract - Suit to enforce claim after six months - The question arise whether such restiction is condition precedent for enforcement of claim or is curtailing the limitation period for filing the suit thereby in contrary to s.28 of 1872 Act - Held, restriction does not directly or indirectly curtail the period of limitation nor does it anywhere provide that the corporation shall be precluded from filing suit after expiry of six months therefore not in comntravention of s. 28 - Appeal dismissed., .
