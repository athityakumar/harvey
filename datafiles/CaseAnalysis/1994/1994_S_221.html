Shri P. G. Gupta v State Of Gujrat & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

14 December 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, S. Mohan, N. Venkatachala

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1845; (1995) Supp2 SCC 182; (1995) SCC (L&S) 782; AIR 1995 SCW 1540; 1995 (30) ATC 47; JT 1995 (2) SC 373; 1995 LabIC 1745; 1995(1) SCALE 653B; 1995 (2) SLR 72; [1994] Supp6 S.C.R. 628

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Gujarat Housing Board Act, 1961

Summary: Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950 -Gujarat Housing Board Act, 1961 - Houses constructed under Lower Income Group Housing Scheme were allotted to the to the Govt. employees on rental basis, the scheme was subsequently converted into hire purchase scheme for which the allotment to the Govt. employees was made on the basis of criteria indicated therein - When the Govt. of India had given sanction for converting lower income group houses from rental scheme to hire purchase scheme, respondents were in occupation of the respective allotted houses - It was seen that they had vacated the respective premises as they were allotted Govt. houses having better accommodation at other place with concessional rates - As on the date of the resolution passed by the Govt., admittedly, they were not in possession of the houses at instant place or some of them were in illegal occupation - HC had then quashed the entitlement to the allotment to category (iii) and (vi) - Hence, instant appeal by the employees covered under the said category - Whether the persons falling in categories (iii) and (vi) in the Govt. Resolution were entitled to priority in allotment of Govt. quarters under hire purchase scheme - Held, on the detailed facts and the circumstances of the instant case, HC was not appeared to be acted in arbitrary manner - so, no illegality was appeared to be done by HC in excluding employees of categories (iii) and (vi) for allotment under hire purchase scheme - Appeals dismissed.
