Uco Bank v Official Liquidator, High Court

Supreme Court of India

11 July 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

J.S. Verma, S.P. Bharucha

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1672; (1994) 5 SCC 1; 1995 (2) Bom.C.R. 371; [1994] 81 Comp Cas 780; JT 1994 (6) SC 350; 1994(3) SCALE 83; [1994] Supp1 S.C.R. 294; 1994 (2) UJ 470

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Corporate

Keywords: Debt Laws

Summary: Corporate - Companies Act, 1956, ss. 433 (e) and 529 - Scope and applicability - S. 529 (1) of Act -Decree obtained for realisation of security available to secured creditors - Held, provision is intended to protect interests of workmen in proceedings for winding up - Existence of security creates pari passu charge in favour of workmen only if security has been realised, pursuant to a decree - Prior that pari passu charge is not created, for there is no security upon which it can operate - Appeal dismissed.
