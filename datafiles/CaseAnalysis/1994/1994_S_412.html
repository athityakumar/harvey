Mool Raj Upadhyaya v State of Himachal Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 April 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

M.N. Venkatachaliah, S.C. Agrawal, S.P. Bharucha

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 176; (1994) Supp2 SCC 316; (1994) SCC (L&S) 990; 1994 (27) ATC 670; 1994 (2) CLR 6; 1994 (68) FLR 1257; JT 1994 (3) SC 453; 1994 (2) LLN 418; 1994(2) SCALE 630; 1994 (2) SLR 377; 1994 (1) UJ 570; 1994 (2) UPLBEC 1291

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Constitution - Municipalities & Local Governments - Labour & Industrial Law - Constitution of India, 1950 - Wages - Notification - Modification - Regularization - Petitioners in the W.P.s filed u/art. 32 of the Constitution were employed on daily-wage basis in the Irrigation and Public Health Wings of the Himachal Pradesh Public Works Department - They were Class III and IV employees who were being paid their daily wages as per the minimum wages prescribed by the Govt - A number of them had been thus employed for more than ten years - The wages that were being paid to petitioners who fall in the unskilled category were at Rs. 22 per day - In notification of Govt., new enhanced rates were provided - A Scheme for Betterment (Appointment) Regularization of Muster-Roll/Daily-Wagers had been prepared by the Govt. and the same had been placed on record along with the supplementary affidavit - Hence, the present appeal - Whether State should be burdened with the additional financial obligations pertaining to daily-wage workers - Held, along with written submissions filed on behalf of the State, figures had been given showing additional financial obligations resulting from the daily wage workers being paid the wages at the rates prescribed in the notifications issued by the State Govt. on account of the daily-wage workers who had completed 10 years' service being placed on work-charge cadre - With regard to the additional financial obligations that may be incurred by the proposed scheme for regularization, as modified, therefore, the State should not be burdened with the financial obligations arising out of payment of arrears for the period prior to 1-1-1994 and the said scheme, as modified, may be brought into operation with effect from 1-1-1994 - Petition dismissed.
