Liberty Oil Mills Private Limited v Commissioner of Central Excise

Supreme Court of India

13 December 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Paripoornan, R.M. Sahai

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1837; (1995) 1 SCC 451; 1995 (56) ECR 417; 1995 (75) E.L.T. 13; 1995 (6) RLT 121; 1994(5) SCALE 224; [1994] Supp6 S.C.R. 547

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - Indirect Tax - Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944, Item No. 13 - Notification No. 61/71 dated 29-5- 1971 - Notification No. 40/72 dated 17-3-1972 - Whether appellants entitled to benefit of exemption afforded by the aforesaid notification? - Under the notification benefit of exemption is available in respect of vegetable product produced out of rice bran oil when, according to condition (i) of the notification it is in admixture with til oil or with other vegetable product produced from other oils, or with both - Reading notification as a whole it is concluded that the expression, vegetable' product as is made from indigenous rice bran oil (which is exempted under the notification) has to be read as 'vegetable product as is made from indigenous rice bran oil' only - In the case of an ambiguity or doubt regarding an exemption provision in a fiscal statute, the ambiguity or doubt will be resolved in favour of the Revenue and not in favour of the assessee - Appellant not entitled to any relief - Appeals dismissed.
