Secretary To Government of India and Others v Shivram Mahadu Gaikwad

Supreme Court of India

12 January 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, M.M. Punchhi, N.P. Singh

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1756; (1995) Supp3 SCC 231; (1995) SCC (L&S) 1148; 1995 (30) ATC 635; 1995 (6) SLR 812

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Administrative Tribunals Act, 1985, s. 21 - Whether Application was barred by limitation? - Held, since order of discharge was of 7-10-1986 and application was filed in 1990, it was clearly barred by limitation unless an application for condoning delay was made u/s. 21(3) of Act - Even if it was the contention of the employee that he was suffering from schizophrenia, that could have been projected as a ground for condonation of delay under sub-s. (3) of s. 21 of the said stature - Even otherwise without insisting on the formality of an application u/s. 21(3) if the Tribunal had dealt with the question of limitation in the context of s. 21 the Court may have refrained from interfering with the order of the Tribunal u/art. 136, but it seems that the Tribunal totally overlooked this question which clearly stared in the face - Even the employee made no effort to explain the delay and seek condonation - The Court find no valid explanation on record for coming to the conclusion that the case for condonation of delay is made out - In the circumstances, there is no doubt that the application was clearly barred by limitation - Appeal allowed.
