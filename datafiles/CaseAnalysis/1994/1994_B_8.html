Union Of India v B.R. Bajaj

Supreme Court of India

18 January 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Jayachandra Reddy, G.N. Ray

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1251; (1994) 2 SCC 277; (1994) SCC (Cr) 477; AIR 1994 SC 1256; 1994 AllCriC 157; 1994 (1) CCR 56; 1994 CRLJ 2086; JT 1994 (1) SC 103; 1994(1) SCALE 89; [1994] 1 S.C.R. 138; 1994 (2) UJ 90

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Lottery

Summary: Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1974, ss. 482 and 154 - Quashing of F.I.R - Powers of Court under s. 482 were in question - F.I.R containing some important allegations which make out a cognizable offence at that stage and registration of F.I.R was only beginning of investigation - F.I.R was quashed by High Court - Held, not proper - Treating whole matter as though it was an appeal against order of conviction was not admissible in exercising inherent power u/s. 482 particularly at stage of F.I.R when same discloses commission of cognizable offence which had still to be investigated thoroughly by police.
