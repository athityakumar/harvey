Krishan Yadav and Another v State of Haryana and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 May 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mohan, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 220; (1994) 4 SCC 165; (1994) SCC (L&S) 937; AIR 1994 SC 2166; AIR 1994 SCW 2373; 1994 (27) ATC 547; 1994 (2) CLR 600; JT 1994 (4) SC 45; 1995 (2) LLJ 77; 1994 (2) LLN 371; 1994(2) SCALE 889; [1994] 3 S.C.R. 1045; 1994 (2) SLJ 192; 1994 (2) SLR 719; 1994 (2) UJ 594

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 16, 133 and 311 - Selection to posts - (A) setting aside of selection as entire process of selection was stinking, conceived in fraud and delivered in deceit - Tenability of plea of innocence of selectees - Held, plea not tenable, selectees however not required to repay salary and perks received - Costs to be paid by members from out of person for arbitrariness - Appeal allowed.
