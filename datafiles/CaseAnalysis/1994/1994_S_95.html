Bahadur Singh v Shangara Singh

Supreme Court of India

24 October 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, N. Venkatachala

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 518; (1995) 1 SCC 232; 1994(4) SCALE 1000; [1994] Supp5 S.C.R. 141

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - Land & Property - Punjab Occupancy Tenants (Vesting of Property Rights) Act, 1952, s. 2(a), 2(f) and 3 - Respondents 5 to 15 were tenants in occupation of demised lands from 16-12-1931 - Whether appellants had no right to obtain declaration as owners? - Held, on a perusal of s. 2(a) and (f) and s. 3, it is clear that occupancy tenant immediately before the commencement of the Act has been conferred with a right of ownership on the demised lands and the pre-existing right, title and interest held by the owner has been extinguished by operation of law - When pre-existing right, title and interest of the landlord has been divested by operation of law, the contract under which the tenants came into possession on 16-12-1931 and covenants contained there under no longer subsist and operate between erstwhile landlord and the tenant after the Act came into force on 15-06-1951 - Payment of rent by them does not have the effect of divesting the right, they had acquired under the statute - There is no estoppel against the statute - Respondents 5 to 15 had become absolute owners of the property as on 15-6-1951 when they had sold the property to Respondents 1 to 4 - Thereby, appellants had no right to obtain declaration as owners and recur for possession of the land as sought for - Appeal dismissed.
