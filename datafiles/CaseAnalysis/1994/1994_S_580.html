Joginder Kumar v State of Uttar Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

25 April 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

A.S. Anand, M.N. Venkatachaliah, S. Mohan

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1505; (1994) 4 SCC 260; (1994) SCC (Cr) 1172; AIR 1994 SC 1349; 1994 AllCriC 431; 1994 (2) BLJR 975; 1994 (2) CCR 401; 1994 (2) Crimes 106; 1994 CRLJ 1981; JT 1994 (3) SC 423; 1994 (1) KLT 919; 1994(2) SCALE 662; [1994] 3 S.C.R. 661

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Rights of the accused or detained person - The instant petition had been preferred for the release of the petitioner - Whether, respondent should be directed for the release of the petitioner - Held, directions for the release of petitioner were given to the respondent - However, after considering the facts of the case, in order to avoid the violation of the fundamental rights of the accused or detained person, the requirements were issued by the instant court giving directions to the police authority and declaring the rights of the detained person or accused - Instant court also pointed out the duty of the Magistrate, before whom the arrested person was to be produced, that he should satisfy himself that requirements issued had been complied with or not - It was also held in the instant case that requirements issued should be followed in all cases of arrest till legal provisions were made in that behalf - Appeal allowed.
