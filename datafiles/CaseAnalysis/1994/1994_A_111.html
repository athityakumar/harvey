State Of Rajasthan v Aruna Devi And Others

Supreme Court of India

08 November 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

B.L. Hansaria, Kuldip Singh

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 953; (1995) 1 SCC 1; 1994 (4) CCR 799; 1994 (3) Crimes 849; 1995 JCC 190; JT 1994 (7) SC 522; 1995 (1) SCJ 337; [1994] Supp5 S.C.R. 274; 1995 (1) UJ 216

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Cognizance by Magistrate - CrPC, 1973,ss.156(3), 173 - IPC, 1860, ss. 420, 467 - Complaint was filed against respondents under various sections of IPC - Magistrate directed an investigation to be made as contemplated by s. 156(3) of CrPC, 1973 - Case registered - Police report submitted stating that complaint was false - Report accepted by Magistrate - However, Superintendent of Police had independently ordered further investigation and a challan came to be filed by police against respondents, inter alia, u/ss. 420 and 467 IPC - Magistrate took cognizance - Challenge was made to this act of Magistrate before Sessions Judge who dismissed revision - HC set aside order of cognizance - Present appeal - Dismissal of a complaint u/s. 203 of CrPC is no bar to entertainment of a second complaint on same facts; but same could be done only in exceptional circumstances - In present case, as on further investigation being made, fresh materials came to light which led to filing of further report stating that a case had been made out - HC erred in quashing cognizance taken by Magistrate - Appeal allowed.
