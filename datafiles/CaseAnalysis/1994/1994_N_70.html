Pradeshiya Industrial And Investment Corpn. Of U.P. v North India Petrochemicals Ltd.

Supreme Court of India

09 February 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mohan, M.N. Venkatachaliah

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 369; (1994) 3 SCC 348; AIR 1994 SCW 2495; [1994] 79 Comp Cas 835; 1994 (2) CompLJ 50; 1994 (53) DLT 835; JT 1994 (1) SC 579; [1994] 1 S.C.R. 815

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Corporate

Keywords: Debt Laws

Summary: Corporate - Companies Act, 1956, ss. 433, 434 and 439 - Winding up petition - Legality - Whether refusal to pay debt is sufficient for ordering winding up of a company? - Wishes of creditors will be tested as to whether case of persons opposing winding up is reasonable - It is to be considered whether there are matters which should be inquired into and investigated if a winding-up order is made - Held, financial position of appellant is sound - It is largest financial corporation of State of Uttar Pradesh - It has rendered financial assistance of Rs. 1024.83 crores till March 1992 to more than 100 industrial units and has also promoted joint sector projects - No justification of winding up - Appeal allowed.
