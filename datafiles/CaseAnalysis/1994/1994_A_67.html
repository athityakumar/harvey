U.P. Public Service Commission v Alpana

Supreme Court of India

17 January 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, N. Venkatachala

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1901; (1994) 2 SCC 723; (1994) SCC (L&S) 742; AIR 1994 SCW 2861; 1994 (27) ATC 101; 1994 (2) AWC 675; 1994 (68) FLR 750; JT 1994 (1) SC 94; 1994(1) SCALE 98; [1994] 1 S.C.R. 131; 1995 (1) SLJ 183; 1994 (2) SLR 59; 1994 (1) UJ 330; 1994 (1) UPLBEC 242

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Constitution of India, 1950, Arts. 136, 226-PCS (J) Examination-Eligibility-LL.B, degree was the requisite educational qualification on last date fixed for receipt of application for-Respondent had appeared in LL.B Final Examination but her result was not declared-Hence, she was not qualified to apply-High Court directing PSC to interview respondent and, if found successful forward her name for appointment-Approach of the High Court not supported on any rule or practice nor on equitable consideration-Decision of the High Court in 1993 (2) AWC 1275, reversed-Writ petition dismissed-Appeal allowed -1993 (1) SLR 379, distinguished.
