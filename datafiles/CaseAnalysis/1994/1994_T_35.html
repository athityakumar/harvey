M.R. Gopalakrishnan v Thachady Prabhakaran and Others

Supreme Court of India

13 December 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

Faizan Uddin, A.S. Anand

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 583; (1995) Supp2 SCC 101; AIR 1995 SCW 156; 1995 (57) DLT 696; JT 1995 (1) SC 202; 1994(5) SCALE 192; [1994] Supp6 S.C.R. 460

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Keywords: Election Agents

Summary: Election - Representation of the People Act, 1951 - Election - Counting of votes - Result - Appellant filed Election Petition by challenging election of respondent No. 1 as a member of Kerala Legislative Assembly - Allegation was specific irregularities in counting votes and demand for recount - HC dismissed election petition - Whether HC was correct in rejecting demand for recount - Held, there was no error or infirmity much less grave or palpable which may call for any interference with the findings - By all means the respondent would get majority - Even after giving all possible discounts, election of respondent No. 1 could not be set aside or declared void - Hence, appeal failed and dismissed with costs - Appeal dismissed.
