P K. Narayanan v State Of Kerala

Supreme Court of India

27 October 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Jayachandra Reddy, M.K. Mukherjee, M.M. Punchhi

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1152; (1995) 1 SCC 142; (1995) SCC (Cr) 215; AIR 1994 SCW 5043; 1995 (1) CCR 39; 1994 (3) Crimes 850; 1994(4) SCALE 740

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Tort

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120-B and 302 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872 - Criminal conspiracy - Investigation - Infirmity of - Adverse remarks against IO - Strong reliance is placed on motive aspect - Held, in case of conspiracy there cannot be direct evidence - Assuming that A-1 was apprehensive that deceased might disclose his trade secrets, that by itself cannot prove conspiracy - When evidence relied upon by prosecution to prove conspiracy is wholly insufficient and on suspicion then an offence of conspiracy cannot be deemed to have been established on mere suspicion - Essence of criminal conspiracy is an agreement to do an illegal act and such an agreement can be proved either by direct evidence or by circumstantial evidence or by both and it is a matter of common experience that direct evidence to prove conspiracy is rarely available - Medical evidence is not conclusive - Possibility of suicide - Set aside judgment of High Court, adverse remarks and strictures passed against petitioner stand expunged - Appeal allowed.
