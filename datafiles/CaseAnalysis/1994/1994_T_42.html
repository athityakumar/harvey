Consolidated Coffee Limited & Anr. v The Coffee Board & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

22 November 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

N.P. Singh, B.P. Jeevan Reddy, S.B. Majmudar

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1806; (1995) 1 SCC 312; JT 1995 (1) SC 1; 1994(4) SCALE 1025; 1995 (97) STC 95; [1994] Supp5 S.C.R. 632

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax

Summary: Indirect Tax - Coffee Act, 1942 - Karnataka Sales Tax Act, 1957 - Appellants filed petitions for injuncting the respondent from making any payment under the head 'Purchase Tax' out of the Pool Fund maintained u/s. 30 of the 1942 Act - According to the appellants, the Board could not discharge its liability in respect of payment of 'Purchase Tax' to the State Govt., under the provisions of the 1957 Act out of the Pool Fund - Whether, the respondent was entitled to make payment of the 'Purchase Tax' out of the Pool Fund required to be maintained u/s. 30 of the 1942 Act and whether, appellants had any right against the said Pool Fund - Held, s. 25(6) of the 1942 Act specifically provided that after the coffee had been delivered for inclusion in the Surplus Pool, the registered owner i.e. in the instant case appellants, whose coffee had been so delivered should have no right in respect of such coffee except his right to receive the payments referred to in s. 34 of the Act -Proviso s. 32(2) of the 1942 Act provides that if after the requirements of provision had been met and there remains any excess in the Pool Fund, the Board could with previous sanction of the Central Govt., transfer the whole or any part of such excess to the credit of the General Fund which could be utilize for the payment of 'Purchase Tax' - However, directions were given to the respondent to perform its statutory duty in respect of payment for the coffee delivered to them by the appellants in accordance with the provisions of the 1942 Act and to make payment to the appellants at a rate which could be held to be just and reasonable and which should cover cost of production of the concerned coffee and reasonable percentage of profit thereon - Appeals disposed of.
