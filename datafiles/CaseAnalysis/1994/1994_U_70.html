N.K. Singh v Union Of India

Supreme Court of India

25 August 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

J.S. Verma, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 983; (1994) 6 SCC 98; (1994) SCC (L&S) 1304; AIR 1995 SC 423; 1994 (28) ATC 246; 1994 (69) FLR 729; JT 1994 (5) SC 298; 1995 (1) LLJ 854; 1996 (2) LLN 312; 1994(3) SCALE 845; 1994 (3) SLJ 37; 1994 (5) SLR 153; [1994] Supp2 S.C.R. 772; 1994 (3) UPLBEC 1900

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Administrative Tribunals Act, 1985, ss. 14, 19 - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 226 and 309 - Tenure Rules for IPS Officers, rr. 1 and 8 - (A) Alleging mala fides Tribunal rejecting allegations without requiring respondent to file counter-affidavit to rebut allegations - Validity - (B) When transfer of employee holding important and sensitive post can be prejudicial to public interest - (C) Tenure of deputation of IPS Officers in Central Police Organisation - (D) Proof of alleged malafides - Held, not proper - If it is avoidable and successor is not suitable to post - Tenure need not be in one Organisation but total period of deputation should be 5 years - Interference has to be drawn by reading record in between lines and by taking into account attendant circumstances - Appeal dismissed.
