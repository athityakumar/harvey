Messrs Neyvely Lignite Corporation Limited v Special Tahsildar(Land Acquisition) Neyvely and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 October 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, M.K. Mukherjee, S.C. Sen

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 2025; (1995) 1 SCC 221; AIR 1995 SC 1004; 1995 (1) ALT(SC) 24; 1994 (3) CCC 642; JT 1995 (1) SC 281; 1994(4) SCALE 1129B; [1994] Supp4 S.C.R. 567

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 3(b), 11 and 50(2) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 1, r. 10 - 'Person interested' - Determination of compensation - Rights of Beneficiary - Whether denial of right to a Person interested or Beneficiary is in negation of fair and just procedure offending art. 14? - Held, beneficiary, i.e., local authority or a cooperative society registered under relevant State law, or statutory authority is a person interested to determine just and proper compensation for acquired land and is an aggrieved person - If beneficiary is not made a party, it is entitled to seek leave of court and file appeal against enhanced award and decree of Civil Court and assail its legality or correctness - As per O. 1, r. 10, in absence of beneficiary who ultimately is to bear higher compensation, no complete, effectual determination of binding just, proper compensation to acquired land would be made - Beneficiary is concomitantly a proper party if not a necessary party to proceedings under O. 1, r. 10 - Therefore, denial of right to person interested is in negation of fair and just procedure offending art. 14 - Appeal allowed.
