Ashok Kumar Jain And Others v The State Of Bihar And Others

Supreme Court of India

08 December 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

G.N. Ray, B.L. Hansaria

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 547; (1995) SCC (L&S) 353; (1995) 1 SCC 516; 1995 (1) CCR 98; 1995 (1) CLR 207; 1995 (70) FLR 288; JT 1995 (1) SC 150; 1995 (2) LLJ 685; 1995 (1) LLN 400; 1994(5) SCALE 187; [1994] Supp6 S.C.R. 389

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Constitution of India, 1950 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 25-M, 25-O and 25-Q - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, ss. 202 and 482 - Whether appellants are liable to be prosecuted against u/s. 25-M r/w s. 25-O for not seeking prior permission before resorting to lay off? - Held, in facts of case it is more than evident that lay off was not motivated or unjustified but such lay off had to be resorted to for compulsive circumstances beyond Company's control and its management including appellants - Lay off without taking prior permission cannot be held to be per se illegal, unjustified and mala fide - S. 25-O was declared ultra vires in Excel Wear's case and constitutional validity of s. 25-M for reasons indicated in Excel Wear's case was seriously doubted - Criminal cases instituted against appellants for alleged violation of s. 25-M be quashed - Appeal allowed.
