Ajay Kumar Singh v State Of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

17 March 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, M.K. Mukherjee, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1403; (1994) 4 SCC 401; AIR 1994 SCW 2515; JT 1994 (2) SC 662; 1994(2) SCALE 302; [1994] 3 S.C.R. 57; 1994 (2) SLR 321; 1994 (2) UJ 689

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Education

Keywords: Indian Medical Council (Amendment) Act, 1993

Summary: Education - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 15(4), 29, Sch. 7, List I, Entry 66, List III, Entry 25 - Indian Medical Council Act, 1956 - Permissibility of providing reservations u/art. 15(4) in postgraduate medical course - Held, words 'any special provision' u/art. 15(4) are of wide amplitude and take in a provision reserving certain number of seats in educational institutions - Reservation is provided only at stage of entry and not at stage of exit - Pass marks are uniform for all - Even if a less meritorious student is admitted under reserved category, he has to improve his standard and has to acquire same proficiency as any other candidate while passing examination - A candidate who is seeking reservation at stage of admission to postgraduate course may not have availed benefit of reservation at stage of admission to MBBS, but because competition at postgraduate medical courses is acute, he may have to seek benefit of reservation - Assumption that a student seeking benefit of reservation at stage of admission to postgraduate medical course has already enjoyed benefit of reservation previously is not necessarily true - There is no rule u/art. 15(4) that a student cannot be given benefit of reservation at more than one stage during his educational career - Where to draw line is not a matter of law but a matter of policy for State to be evolved keeping in view larger interests of society - It cannot be said that admission to postgraduate medical course is a promotional post just because such candidate must necessarily pass MBBS examination before becoming eligible for admission to postgraduate medical course or for reason that some stipend is paid to postgraduate students - Power of Indian Medical Council to 'prescribe minimum standards of medical education' is only advisory in nature - Power conferred upon State by art. 15(4) is a constitutional power and it could not have been superseded by a Regulation made by Indian Medical Council - Regulation must be read consistent with art.15(4) and means that students shall be admitted to postgraduate training strictly on basis of merit in each of relevant classes or categories - While regulation of admission to these medical courses may be incidental to power u/Entry 66 List I, it is integral to power contained in Entry 25 List III - State which has established and is maintaining these institutions out of public funds must be held to possess power to regulate admission policy consistent with art. 14 - Appeals dismissed.
