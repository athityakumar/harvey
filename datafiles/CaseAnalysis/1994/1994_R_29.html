State of Madhya Pradesh and Others v Raghuveer Singh Yadav and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 August 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, N. Venkatachala

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 934; (1994) 6 SCC 151; (1994) SCC (L&S) 1317; 1994 (28) ATC 255; 1994 (2) CLR 638; 1994 (69) FLR 636; JT 1994 (5) SC 235; 1994(3) SCALE 766; 1995 (1) SLJ 11; 1994 (5) SLR 176; [1994] Supp2 S.C.R. 459; 1994 (3) UPLBEC 1849

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Madhya Pradesh Standard of Weights and Measurement (Enforcement) Rules, 1989 - Recruitment to posts of Inspectors, Department of Weights and Measures in State - Prescribed qualification was degree in Arts or Commerce or Science or Engineering or Diploma in Engineering - Written examinations - Results declared - Interview - Meanwhile, Govt. amended rules and altered eligibility qualification - Challenged - Held, it is settled law that State has got power to prescribe qualifications for recruitment - Candidates appeared for examination and passed written examination had only legitimate expectation to be considered of their claims according to rules then in vogue - Amended Rules have only prospective operation - Govt. is entitled to conduct selection in accordance with changed rules and make final recruitment - Further held, no candidate acquired any vested right against State - Therefore, State is entitled to withdraw notification by which it had previously notified recruitment and to issue fresh notification in that regard on basis of amended Rules - Appeal allowed.
