Buffalo Traders Welfare Association and Others v Maneka Gandhi and Others

Supreme Court of India

18 May 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

M.N. Venkatachaliah, S. Mohan

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1555; (1994) Supp3 SCC 448; 1994(3) SCALE 1

Case Digest

Subject: Environment, Wildlife & Animal

Summary: Environment, Wildlife & Animal - Constitution - Restriction on number of animals to be slaughtered in slaughtered in Idgah slaughter house - High Court had reduced number of animals to be slaughtered to 2500 per day - Whether restriction on number of animals to slaughtered is to be reconsidered ? - Held, order of High Court unexceptionable as it is to extent it goes, deals with only a part of problem - Prevalent conditions in Idgah Slaughter House were, apparently, appalling and HC has, very rightly, insisted on ensurement of certain minimal health and hygiene standards - But then courts do not possess any such expertise as to enable it to decide for itself conditions justifying increase in number of animals to be slaughtered - That has to be considered by an expert body, keeping in mind environmental aspect including treatment and outlet for effluents, proper cleaning, sewerage and water-supply facilities - Directions issued for constitution of committee for considering matter - Order accordingly.
