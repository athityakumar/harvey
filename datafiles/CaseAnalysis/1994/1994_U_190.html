Ex-Capt. Harish Uppal v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

30 March 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, B.L. Hansaria

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1864; (1994) Supp2 SCC 195; 1994 (23) ALR 574; JT 1994 (3) SC 126; 1994(2) SCALE 404; [1994] 3 S.C.R. 220; 1994 (2) SLJ 177; 1994 (2) SLR 359; 1994 (2) UJ 366

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Defence & Security Forces - Army Act, 1950 - Constitution of India, 1950 - A court-martial was held against petitioner and was found guilty - Petitioner was he was awarded the punishment of dismissal from service and 2 year's rigorous imprisonment and petition regarding same was dismissed - Yet another petition was filed by the petitioner on the ground that the order for rejection of earlier petition was not conveyed, the same was also dismissed directing the respondent to convey the order of rejection to the appellant - Hence, instant petition, in which he did not complained that he was not informed of the rejection of his earlier petition and merely sought for reconsideration of his case - Whether, appellant's petition was liable for consideration in the instant case - Held, petitioner had filed the petition almost after 10 years and hence, it was concluded that no person would have kept quiet for a period of more than 10 years without taking any steps if he had not really received the orders upon his petition - It was further concluded that petitioner was guilty of laches and his case was not liable for reconsideration since, there was no substance in the arguments - Petition dismissed.
