Bhajan Lal v Jindal Strips Ltd.

Supreme Court of India

08 August 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mohan, M.N. Venkatachaliah

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 935; (1994) 6 SCC 19; AIR 1994 SCW 3905; JT 1994 (5) SC 254; 1994(3) SCALE 703; [1994] Supp2 S.C.R. 445

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Principles of natural justice - Judicial process - Whether transfer of a case can be allowed on basis of allegation of personal bias? - Held, reasonable apprehension of bias which a reasonable person can entertain may be a good ground for transfer - A litigant has a right to ask for a change of Bench - Bias is second limb of natural justice - Prima facie no one should be a judge in what is to be regarded as 'sua causa', whether or not he is named as a party - Decision-maker should have no interest by way of gain or detriment in outcome of a proceeding - Further held, no ground to hold that there could be a reasonable suspicion of bias in this case - Petition dismissed.
