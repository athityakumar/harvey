Madras Fertilizers Limited v Assistant C.C.E

Supreme Court of India

20 January 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, B.L. Hansaria

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1264; (1994) 2 SCC 295; 1994 (51) ECR 337; 1994 (69) E.L.T. 625; JT 1994 (1) SC 150; 1994(1) SCALE 173; [1994] 1 S.C.R. 189

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - Central Excise Rules, rr. 8(1) and 173 B(5) - Notification No 25/70 - (A) Whether authorities could refuse benefit of exemption notification to appellant - Order deals only with one aspect, viz, that chemical reaction or transformation, if any, taking place on mixing of fertilizers is no ground to deny benefit of aforementioned Notification to mixture of fertilizers - Mixture manufactured by appellant does not satisfy all conditions prescribed by relevant Notification and that unless all conditions are satisfied, benefit does not flow - (B) Whether Assistant Collector has jurisdiction to revise or revoke order after it has been passed by him? - Under Sub-rule (5) to r. 173-B if Assistant Collector granted exemption contrary to law it was always open to him to rectify said error - Appeal dismissed.
