Collector of Central Excise v Fenoplast Private Limited (Ii)

Supreme Court of India

27 June 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, B.L. Hansaria

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 533; (1994) Supp2 SCC 678; 1994 (72) E.L.T. 513; JT 1994 (4) SC 242; 1994(3) SCALE 66; [1994] Supp1 S.C.R. 267

Case Digest

Subject: Excise

Summary: Excise - Textiles - Central Excise Act, 1944 - Tariff - Base fabric - Respondent company was manufacturer of rexine cloth - Department demanded excise duty as it was a tariff item - Tribunal held by a majority of 2:1 that it was not so - Department filed W.P. and Collector agreed that the item did fall within T.I. 19-111 and order was passed in favour of Department - Respondent's appeal allowed - Department aggrieved - Hence, instant appeal - Whether, 'rexine cloth' produced by the respondent fell under Tariff Item 19-111 of the Schedule to the 1944 Act - Held, that a coated fabric was expressly placed within the purview of the cotton fabric by Parliament and so it could not be argued that the rexine cloth or coated fabric was not cotton fabric - Parliament treated it to be cotton fabric for the purposes of the Act and had chosen to include it within the ambit of cotton fabrics for the purposes of levying excise duty - Predominance and percentages had to be in relation to the base fabric which were impregnated, coated or laminated - Base fabric in the case of respondent's product was 100 percent cotton and did fall within the Tarriff Item 19-111 - Appeals allowed.
