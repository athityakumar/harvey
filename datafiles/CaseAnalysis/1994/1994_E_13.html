Escorts Limited v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

17 February 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

B.L. Hansaria, B.P. Jeevan Reddy

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1350; (1994) Supp3 SCC 86; 1998 (97) E.L.T. 211

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Indirect Tax - Central Excise Act, s. 36 - Customs Act, 1962, ss. 17, 27, 36 and 47 - Revision petition u/s. 36 - Limitation of - Held, Act does not prescribe any particular form in which order of assessment is to be made - No formal order of assessment can be expected when there is no dispute as to classification or rate of duty - Appellant himself classified goods under tariff item No. 73.33/40 and paid duty applicable thereunder - He did not dispute either as to classification or as to duty applicable - There was no occasion for passing a formal order since there was no lis at that stage - Bill of entry presented by appellant was signed, signifying approval by assessing officer - That itself is an order of assessment in such a situation - Thus it cannot be said that there is no order of assessment and limitation u/s. 27 did not begin to run - Application made by appellant was u/s. 27 - Authorities must necessarily operate within four corners of Act - They had no option but to reject an application filed beyond six months - Appeals dismissed.
