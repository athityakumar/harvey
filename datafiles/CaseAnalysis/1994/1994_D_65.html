R. Kapur v Director of Inspection

Supreme Court of India

29 September 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mohan, M.N. Venkatachaliah

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1764; (1994) 6 SCC 589; (1995) SCC (L&S) 13; AIR 1995 SCW 272; 1994 (28) ATC 516; 1994 (2) CLR 885; 1994 (69) FLR 1137; JT 1994 (6) SC 354; 1995 (1) LLJ 884; 1994(4) SCALE 349; 1994 (5) SLR 616

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Administrative - Labour & Industrial Law - Service - Administrative Tribunals Act, 1985, s. 19 - Public Premises (Eviction of Unauthorised Occupants) Act, 1971 - Fundamental Rules, r. 48-A(iv)(c)(ii)(8) - Whether appellant's claim for gratuity could be withheld on account of his failure to vacate the government accomodation allotted to him and whether the appellant is entitled to 18% interest on the gratuity (DCRG) withheld due to non issuance of "No Demand Certificate" ? - The Tribunal having come to the conclusion that DCRG cannot be withheld merely because the claim for damages for unauthorised occupation is pending should have granted interest at the rate of 18% since right to gratuity is not dependent upon the appellant vacating the official accommodation - Held, the DCRG due to the appellant will carry interest at the rate of 18% per annum from 01-06-1986 till the date of payment, without prejudice to the right of the respondent to recover damages under Fundamental Rule 48-A - Appeal allowed.
