Babua Ram And Others v State Of Uttar Pradesh And Another

Supreme Court of India

04 October 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, N. Venkatachala

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 783; (1995) 2 SCC 689; AIR 1995 SCW 65; JT 1994 (7) SC 377; 1995 (1) RentLR 38; 1994(4) SCALE 843; 1995 (1) SCJ 573; [1994] Supp4 S.C.R. 148

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Land Acquisition, Date Of Award

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4, 26 and 28 - Land acquisition - Award - Enhanced compensation - Notifications issued u/s. 4(1) of the 1894 Act proposing acquisition of large extents of land - Compensation was awarded - Respondents received compensation without protest - Some received enhanced compensation with protest by order of District Judge - Respondents also demanded for same amount of compensation - State filed appeal against the order of District Judge - HC ordered to decide the matter u/s. 28(A) of the 1894 Act - Hence instant appeals - Whether application could be decided u/s. 28-A of the 1894 Act - Held, collector should await the final decision of HC or SC - Collector/L.A.O. committed grievous error of law in proceeding to make an award u/s. 28-A(2) of the 1894 Act - He should await the decision of HC in pending appeal - Order of HC and of collector were set aside - Cases were remitted to Collector to keep them back pending disposal of appeal by HC - In case decree of HC granting compensation was in excess to award of Collector/L.A.O., u/s. 11 of the 1894 Act, then he should enquire whether application satisfied requirements of s. 28A of the Act and whether application had been properly made and within limitation - If findings were in favour of applicants, he should redetermine compensation on basis of award u/s. 26, and award u/s. 28-A(2) of the 1894 Act and should follow procedure prescribed in Part III - Orders of Collector and that of HC of Punjab & Haryana in revisions were set aside - Appeals disposed of.
