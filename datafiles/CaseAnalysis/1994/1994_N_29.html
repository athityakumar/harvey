State of Haryana v Naresh Kumar Bali

Supreme Court of India

17 May 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mohan, M.N. Venkatachaliah, R.M. Sahai

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 643; (1994) 4 SCC 448; (1994) SCC (L&S) 909; AIR 1994 SCW 2539; 1994 (27) ATC 611; 1994 (2) CLR 399; 1994 (69) FLR 252; JT 1994 (4) SC 184; 1995 (2) LLJ 108; 1994 (2) LLN 458; 1994(2) SCALE 1105; 1994 (4) SLR 1; 1994 (2) UJ 226

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Whether High Court was right in giving orders for appointment of the respondent to the post of sub inspector? - Post of an Inspector is a promotional post and not by direct recruitment - Direction does not fall within the scope of mandamus - Judicial review is directed against the decision making process and not against the decision itself - HC went wrong in comparing the case of the respondent with others as their cases were considered on their merits - Held, the judgment under appeal set aside - Matter remitted to HC - Appeal allowed.
