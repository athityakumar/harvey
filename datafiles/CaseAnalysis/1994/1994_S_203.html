N.Nagendra Rao & Co. v State Of A.P.

Supreme Court of India

06 September 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Sahai, B.L. Hansaria

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 798; (1994) 6 SCC 205; (1994) SCC (Cr) 1609; AIR 1994 SC 2663; AIR 1994 SCW 3753; 1994 (3) CCC 87; 1996 (2) CCR 81; JT 1994 (5) SC 572; 1994(3) SCALE 977; [1994] Supp3 S.C.R. 144

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Socio-Economic; Trade

Keywords: Goods Seized

Summary: Constitution - Socio-Economic - Essential Commodities Act, 1955, ss. 6A(2)(A) and 6C(2) - Discretion vested in Collector -Seized commodity - Found to be subject to speedy decay or its disposal found to be expedient in public interest - Whether Collector has an option not to dispose of seized goods - Held, collector has no such option - (B) Seized goods - Interim arrangement for proper maintenance - Expression 'speedy and natural delay' - How to be understood - Expression 'it is otherwise expedient in public interest' - Connotation -Further held, to be understood in a common sense manner - To be understood so as to ensure that goods do not suffer either in quality or quantity - (C) Liability of Govt. to return seized goods or pay price thereof - Whether discharged by offering goods in whatever condition they are - Liability if cease in case of partial confiscation - Further held, neither by offering goods in any condition, nor in case of partial confiscation - (D) Sovereign immunity - Defence of - Limited only to primary and inalienable functions of constitutional Govt. - If suit filed by any person for negligence of officer of State in discharge of statutory duty referable to State functions - Whether liable to be dismissed on ground of sovereign immunity - Further held, not referable to primary State functions -Not liable to be dismissed - Appeal allowed.
