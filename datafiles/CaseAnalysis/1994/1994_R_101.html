Ravinder Sharma Smt. and Another v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

17 October 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mohan, M.N. Venkatachaliah, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 2228; (1995) 1 SCC 138; (1995) SCC (L&S) 228; AIR 1995 SC 277; 1994 (28) ATC 779; 1994 (2) CLR 1027; 1995 (70) FLR 43; JT 1994 (6) SC 531; 1995 (2) LLJ 589; 1994(4) SCALE 511; 1994 (5) SLR 773; 1995 (1) UJ 1

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Punjab Public Service Commission (State Service Class III) Regulations, 1967, Regn. 7 - Person appointed as clerk and later promoted as assistant - Whether appointment liable to be set aside on ground that he was not possessing requisite qualification at time of appointment - Whether doctrine of estoppel would apply? - Relaxation in qualification recommended by Commission but rejected by Govt. - Held, (A) appointment clearly against regn. 7 and therefore, liable to be set aside - (B) No question of estoppel would ever arise - Appeal dismissed.
