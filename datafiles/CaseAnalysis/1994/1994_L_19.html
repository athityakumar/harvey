Jai Bhagawan v Laxman Singh

Supreme Court of India

08 April 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

M.N. Venkatachaliah, A.S. Anand, N.P. Singh

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 476; (1994) 5 SCC 5

Case Digest

Subject: Tort

Summary: Criminal - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 - Compensation - Determination - Whether in the circumstance of personal injury amounted to amputation of appellant's left leg above the knee, entitled the appellant to get enhanced compensation? - Yes - In appeal for enhancement of compensation the finding of the Tribunal on appreciation of evidence assumes finality - Both pecuniary and non-pecuniary losses resulting from the injury are to be compensated - Assessment of damages is subject to rules of remoteness and mitigation - Held, having regard to the age of the appellant at the time of the accident and all the relevant facts and circumstances, the compensation enhanced to Rs. 80,000 from Rs. 45, 000, the amount being awarded by the Tribunal - Appeal disposed of.
