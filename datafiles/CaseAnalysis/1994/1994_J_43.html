Commissioner of Income Tax, Gujarat, Ahmedabad v Jayantilal Prem Chand Shah

Supreme Court of India

29 September 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Paripoornan, S.P. Bharucha, S.C. Sen

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1085; (1995) Supp3 SCC 208; [1995] 211 ITR 111

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Joint Hindu families - Minor - Contract - Valid partnership - Assessee was a Hindu Undivided Family of which A was Karta as a partner in firm of X Company and Firm had been originally constituted under a partnership deed - A had a share of 5 annas in a rupee in firm - Firm was reconstituted and B and C two minor sons of A were given benefits in firm - Share of A in firm was reduced to 2 annas in a rupee and B and C was given benefit of a share of 1-1/2 annas each - Income Tax Officer included shares of two minor sons of A in income of Hindu Undivided Family upon basis that beneficial ownership thereof remained with Hindu Undivided Family - HC found there had been no partition, partial or total, of Hindu Undivided Family and held it could not had foregone a part of share in firm in favor of minors - Decision of ITO was upheld by Appellate Assistant Commissioner - Assessee carried matter in appeal to Income Tax Appellate Tribunal - Income Tax Tribunal held there was no justification for clubbing income earned by minors with income of Hindu Undivided Family - Whether Tribunal was right in holding income earned by minors sons of assessee from partnership of X company cannot be included in income of assessee - Held, that Tribunal found minors were admitted to benefits of firm in shares stipulated in partnership deed as a result Hindu Undivided Family had agreed to a smaller share in firm - It was not necessary that there should be a partial partition or gift - There was no evidence to show that minors were benamidars of Hindu Undivided Family or beneficial owner of shares of minors was Hindu Undivided Family - Minor could not enter into a contract and he could not represent Hindu Undivided Family in partnership - Income of minors cannot be added to income of Hindu Undivided Family and assessed in hands of Hindu Undivided Family - Appeal dismissed.
