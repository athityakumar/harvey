Karnail Singh and Another v Darshan Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

16 December 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

G.N. Ray, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 2034; (1995) Supp1 SCC 760; 1995(1) SCALE 86; [1994] Supp6 S.C.R. 700; 1995 (1) UJ 613

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Gram Sabha

Summary: Municipalities & Local Governments - Punjab Gram Panchayat Act, 1952, ss. 3(q) and 4 - (A) Whether u/s. 4(1) r/w s. 3(q) of Act, for constitution of a gram sabha area, village must be a revenue estate? - Held, s. 4(2) gives power to State Govt. to amalgamate two or more than one sabha areas and to constitute, by a declaration u/s. 4(2) a single gram panchayat - Power to constitute a panchayat included power to amalgamate two or more than one sabha areas as one gram sabha - It would thus be clear that Govt. had exercised power u/s. 4(2) to amalgamate Harigarh and Bhorakh and also Madan was added by public notification - (B) Whether action of Govt. amalgamating two Gram Sabhas is vitiated by any arbitrary exercise of power? - Held, it is only an executive policy decision taken by Govt. to create separate panchayats or to amalgamate existing gram sabhas - It would not be for courts to evaluate and decide whether existence of two gram sabhas should be continued or further bifurcated or amalgamated - It must be left to Govt. to take a decision which unless it is vitiated by mala fides, court cannot substitute its view to that of Govt. in constituting two separate gram sabhas or equally amalgamating two existing gram panchayats situated in revenue estate or two contiguous villages with a population of not less than 500 - Notification upheld - Appeal allowed.
