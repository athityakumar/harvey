Novopan India Limited, Hyderabad v Collector of Central Excise and Customs, Hyderabad

Supreme Court of India

14 September 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, K.S. Paripoornan, S.C. Sen

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1028; (1994) Supp3 SCC 606; AIR 1994 SCW 3976; 1994 (54) ECR 505; 1994 (73) E.L.T. 769; JT 1994 (6) SC 80; 1994 (4) RLT 323; 1994(4) SCALE 122; [1994] Supp3 S.C.R. 549

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - Indirect Tax - Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944 - Central Excise Rule, 1944, r. 8(1) - Exemption - Notification No. 55 of 1979 - 'Melamine faced particle boards' (MFPBs) Appellant manufactured 'melamine faced particle boards' and claimed benefit of the exemption notification no. 55 of 1979 in respect of the same - Authorities rejected said claim on the ground that the impugned product fell and was dutiable under Tariff Item 68 - Accordingly the appellant filed the classification list and the Department approved it - Subsequently, Collector issued a notice u/s. 35A of 1944 Act proposing to revise the order of the Asst. Collector on the ground that that the melamine faced particle boards (MFPBs) were classifiable under Tariff Item 16-B but were not entitled to the benefit of exemption provided by the Notification No. 55 of 1979 - Tribunal confirmed the order of the Collector - Hence, present appeal - Whether MFPBs can be called 'unveneered particle boards' within the meaning of Item 6 of the table appended to Notification No. 55 of 1979? - Held, unveneered particle board is raw particle board - The melamine facing gives it a smooth polished surface - It looks as if the particle board has been laminated on both sides - It is thus difficult to say that the melamine faced particle boards can be described as 'unveneered particle boards' - Moreover, nobody in the trade circles or in the market would consider both the products as one and the same - Hence, the words 'unveneered particle boards' in Item 6 of the table appended to the exemption notification cannot and do not take in melamine faced particle boards - Appeal dismissed.
