State Bank of India and others v Samarendra Kishore Endow and another

Supreme Court of India

18 January 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, B.L. Hansaria

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 28919; (1994) 2 SCC 537; JT 1994 (1) SC 217; 1994(1) SCALE 206; [1994] 1 S.C.R. 154

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: State Bank of India (Supervising Staff) Service Rules

Summary: (A) Service - Reimbursement of expenses - Removal from service - Proof of charge - Appreciation of evidence - Respondent was appointed as Cashier in appellant-Bank and was promoted to rank of Branch Manager and was transferred - Respondent claimed certain amount by way of reimbursement for expenses incurred by him in shifting his belongings - Enquiry was made into correctness of receipts and other documents produced by him in that connection and into some other alleged irregularities committed by him - Charges against respondent were proved - Respondent was removed from service - Board dismissed Appeal preferred by respondent against impugned order - Further, respondent approached HC by way of Petition which was allowed - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether order of HC that findings of Enquiry Officer and disciplinary authority on charges against respondent were based on no evidence was liable to be set aside - Held, respondent had produced receipt in sum of Rs. 9, 500/- claiming that to be expenses incurred by him for transporting his belongings - When respondent came to know that certain enquiries were being made by Bank into correctness of receipts produced by him, he produced second receipt in sum of Rs. 2,755 - PW1-transporter, examined by Bank, supported respondent's case in full - However, Enquiry Officer refused to believe his evidence and found that 'there was no actual movement of household goods belonging to respondent on dates represented by documents' - Charge No. 2 related to claim of Rs. 120 towards loading and unloading charges, evidenced by two receipts - However, Enquiry Officer found that loading and unloading did not take place on dates mentioned therein which was again based upon documentary evidence and could not be said to be not supported by any evidence - As far as charge no. 5 was concerned, Enquiry Officer found that respondent acted in violation of instructions of bank that loan sanctioned to be disbursed in installments, should be released in installment/installments after verifying that previous installment/installments were properly utilized - Hence finding of Enquiry Officer was certainly based upon terms and conditions of loan contained in loan document - Hence, order of HC was liable to be set aside - Appeal disposed of.

(B) Service - Constitution - Constitution of India,1950, art.309 - Jurisdiction - Penalty - Whether HC or Administrative Tribunal had jurisdiction to entertain matter relating to question of penalty - Held, imposition of appropriate punishment was within discretion and judgment of disciplinary authority - Jurisdiction of Tribunal to interfere with disciplinary matters or punishment could not be equated with appellate jurisdiction - Tribunal could not interfere with findings of inquiry Officer or competent authority where they were not arbitrary or utterly perverse - Power to impose penalty on delinquent officer was conferred on competent authority either by Act of legislature or rules made under proviso to art. 309 of Constitution - Thus, if there was enquiry consistent with rules and in accordance with principles of natural justice what punishment would meet ends of justice was matter exclusively within jurisdiction of competent authority - Therefore, adequacy of penalty unless it was mala fide, was certainly not matter for Tribunal to concern with - Tribunal could not interfere with penalty if conclusion of Inquiry Officer or competent authority was based on evidence even if some of it was found to be irrelevant or extraneous to matter - Main charge against respondent was putting forward false claim for reimbursement of expenditure incurred for transporting his belongings - Thus, punishment of removal imposed upon respondent was harsh - However, it was matter which disciplinary authority or Appellate authority should consider and not HC or Administrative Tribunal - Appeal dismissed.
