Tata Cellular v Union Of India

Supreme Court of India

26 July 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mohan, M.N. Venkatachaliah, M.M. Punchhi

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 17; (1994) 6 SCC 651; AIR 1996 SC 11; AIR 1994 SCW 3344; 1995 (1) ARBLR 193; 1995 (1) CompLJ 40; JT 1994 (4) SC 532; 1994(3) SCALE 477; [1994] Supp2 S.C.R. 122

Case Digest

Subject: Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Solicitor, misa

Summary: Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14, 299 and 226 - Indian Contract Act, ss. 2 and 9 - Whether there has been bias in acceptance of tender by Govt. from a company where son of a Govt. employee is working? - Govt. has violated rule of natural justice by de-enlisting company from list of bidders without being heard - Held, tenderer must be heard before rejection or acceptance as required by art. 14 of Constitution.
