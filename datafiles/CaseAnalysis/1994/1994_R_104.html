Municipal Corporation of Delhi v Razak

Supreme Court of India

20 October 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Bharucha, K.S. Paripoornan, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 512; (1995) 1 SCC 235; (1995) SCC (L&S) 296; 1995 (29) ATC 93; 1995 (1) CLR 170; JT 1994 (7) SC 476; 1995 LabIC 330; 1995 (1) LLJ 395; 1995 (1) LLN 402; 1994(4) SCALE 967; 1995 (1) SCJ 177; 1995 (1) SLJ 168; 1994 (5) SLR 764; [1994] Supp4 S.C.R. 617

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Industrial Disputes (Appellate Tribunal) Act, 1950

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 33-C(2) and 36-A - Industrial Disputes (Appellate Tribunal) Act, 1950 - Respondents daily-rated/casual workers of Municipal Corporation claimed computation of arrears of their wages in accordance with s. 33-C(2) of Act and made applications made to Labour Court u/s. 33-C(2) of Act led to award in their favour - WPs by appellant-Municipal Corporation in High Court dismissed - Whether claim of daily-rated/casual workers are maintainable in proceedings u/s. 33-C(2) of Industrial Disputes Act, 1947? - Held, Labour Court has no jurisdiction to first decide workmen's entitlement and then proceed to compute benefit so adjudicated on that basis in exercise of its power u/s. 33-C(2) of Act, it is only when entitlement has been earlier adjudicated or recognised by employer - Therefore Labour Court as well as HC were in error in treating as maintainable applications made u/s. 33-C(2) of Act - Appeals allowed.
