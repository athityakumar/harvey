Raja Ram v State Of M.P.

Supreme Court of India

22 February 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

A.S. Anand, Faizan Uddin

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1364; (1994) 2 SCC 568; (1994) SCC (Cr) 573; 1994 (2) ALT(SC) 7; 1994 (2) CCR 317; 1994 (1) Crimes 734; 1994 JCC 263; JT 1994 (2) SC 36; 1994(1) SCALE 708; [1994] 2 S.C.R. 114; 1994 (1) UJ 424

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Supreme Court (Enlargement of Criminal Appellate Jurisdiction) Act, 1970

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, ss. 325 and 323 - Offence of grievous hurt and simple hurt - Held, analysis of evidence and trustworthy statements of prosecution witnesses show that conviction and sentence recorded by High Court u/ss. 325 and 323 is and case of appellants does not fall within ambit of any of four clauses of s. 300 IPC but u/s. 299 and punishable u/s. 304 Part II/149 - To meet ends of justice appellants are sentenced to suffer rigorous imprisonment and fine - Appeal partly allowed.
