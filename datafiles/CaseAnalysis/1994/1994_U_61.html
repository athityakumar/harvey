D.C.Vatia v Union Of India

Supreme Court of India

19 October 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Sen, M.N. Venkatachaliah, S.B. Majmudar

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1121; (1995) 1 SCC 104; AIR 1994 SCW 5011; JT 1994 (7) SC 114; 1994 (2) RCJ 595; 1995 (1) RCR(Rent) 24; 1994 (2) RentLR 610; 1994(4) SCALE 613; [1994] Supp4 S.C.R. 539

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law; Land & Property; Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - Delhi Rent Control Act, 1958, s. 3(c) - Standard Rent - Fixation of - Held, s. 3(c) speaks of premises 'whose monthly rent exceeds three thousand and five hundred rupees' - Scheme and purpose of Act are clear - Tenants who could afford to pay more than Rs. 3500 per month by way of rent, were being removed from protective umbrella of Rent Act - Only thing that has to be seen for purpose of deciding class of tenants, who were being excluded from ambit of Rent Act, was exact amount of monthly rent that was being paid on relevant date - There is no precondition of fixation of standard rent before application of provisions of s. 3(c) of Act - Appeal dismissed.
