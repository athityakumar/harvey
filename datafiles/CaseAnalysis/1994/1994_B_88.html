Bharat Coking Coal Limited v Bibhuti Kumar Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

16 September 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

M.K. Mukherjee, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1034; (1994) Supp3 SCC 628; (1995) SCC (L&S) 185; 1994 (28) ATC 594; 1994 (4) CCR 769; 1994 (2) CLR 1083; 1994 (69) FLR 1078; JT 1994 (6) SC 109; 1995 (2) LLJ 633; 1996 (2) LLN 451; 1994(4) SCALE 150; 1994 (5) SLR 534; 1994 (2) UJ 672

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947, ss. 5(1)(e) and 5(2) - Respondent 1 charged by CBI for acquiring asset disproportionate to his known source of income u/ss. 5(2) and 5(1)(e) of corruption Act and departmental investigation initiated - Whether HC's order of dismissing writ petition of appellant and confirmation of service of respondent 1 for not conducting inquiry within specified time, is correct in law? - Held, as regards the final report submitted by the appellant, it cannot be said that in the matter of misconduct, the allegations on which they are based, are not confined to only acquisition of assets disproportionate to the known source of income - Secondly, it cannot be said that the final report submitted by the CBI recommending discharge of respondent 1 on the ground of non-availability of sufficient evidence has to be considered in the context of the standard of proof for a successful prosecution vis-a-vis a departmental enquiry - While in the former onus is one of beyond all reasonable doubts in the latter it is one of probability - The enquiry has proceeded to some length in that six witnesses have already been examined - One of the reasons which weighed with the Single Judge in making the same was that Respondent 1 was suspended since the date the charge-sheet was issued, but that is admittedly incorrect - Considering the seriousness of the charges, the explanation offered by the appellant for the delay in concluding the enquiry, which cannot be said to be unsatisfactory and the fact that the enquiry has proceeded to some length the HC ought not to have rejected the reasonable prayer of the appellant for extension of time - Set aside the impugned orders of the HC so far as they refused to grant time to the appellant to complete the enquiry and direct the appellant to conclude the enquiry as expeditiously as possible, preferably within a period of six months - Appeal allowed.
