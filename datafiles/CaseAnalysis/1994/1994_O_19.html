Krishi Upaj Mandi Samiti and Others v Orient Paper and Industries Limited

Supreme Court of India

09 November 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

P.B. Sawant, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1015; (1995) 1 SCC 655; AIR 1994 SCW 5156; JT 1994 (7) SC 414; 1994(4) SCALE 914; [1994] Supp5 S.C.R. 392

Case Digest

Subject: Agriculture & Agri. Products; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Agricultural Produce Markets

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Agriculture & Agri. Products - Madhya Pradesh Van Upaj (Vyapar Viniyaman) Adhiniyam, 1969 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 110(2), 266 - Agricultural Produce Markets - Respondent purchased bamboos as raw material under a contract with the State Govt. which held monopoly in regard to bamboos as a forest produce in view of the provisions of the 1969 Act - Bamboos were supplied to respondent by the Forest Department at various forest depots established for that purpose - After taking delivery from the forest depots, respondent transported the same to its factory - Appellant-Committees levied fees on the sale and purchase of bamboos - Respondent challenged the said levy by way of a W.P. on the ground that the 1969 Act was ultra vires the Constitution, that the requirement of obtaining the licence u/s. 32 of the Act and of paying the market fee u/s. 19 of the Act was also unconstitutional - HC allowed the W.P. - Hence, the present appeal - Whether the finding of HC that the levy of the market fees was not justified because there was no direct or indirect benefit conferred by the market committee either on the purchasers or traders of bamboos as a class, was valid - Held, provision requiring the seller to collect the market fees in such cases was made for the convenience of collection of the fees - Collection made by the forest depots was for and on behalf of the Committee and was eventually to be handed over to the Committee - Provision was enabling and did not prevent the Committee itself from collecting the fee, if it so proposed - Therefore, HC was in error in holding that the levy of market fees was not legal - Appeal allowed.
