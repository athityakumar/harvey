Thiru A. Balakrishnan and Another v Government of T. N. and Others

Supreme Court of India

22 April 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

Kuldip Singh, S.C. Agrawal, Yogeshwar Dayal

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 2163; (1995) Supp4 SCC 108; 1995 (8) SLR 782

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Regularization - Notice in the review petition filed by petitioner contended that the view taken by court was contrary to the ratio in the judgment of State of T.N. v. E. Paripoornam 1991 Indlaw SC 218 : 1991 Indlaw SC 218 : 1991 Indlaw SC 218 - It was contended that service rendered by appellants prior to the date of regularization could not be counted towards seniority - According to him the period of service rendered after the actual date of regularization could only be counted towards the fixation of seniority - Hence, the present appeal - Whether there was any merit in the review petition - Held, the order of regularization itself denied the benefit towards the seniority of the period of service rendered prior to the date of regularization - There was no such condition in the order of regularization of appellants - However, since the seniority was initially fixed by department ignoring the temporary period, the condition should be impliedly read in the appointment order - Court did not agree with this contention - In the absence of any specific order denying seniority to appellant from the date of their initial appointment from which date they were regularized, they were entitled to count the whole of the period of service for the purpose of seniority - Therefore, no ground found to interfere with the court order under review - Review dismissed.
