State Of U.P. v Abhai Kishore Masta

Supreme Court of India

01 December 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, S.C. Sen

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 2175; (1995) SCC (L&S) 317; (1995) 1 SCC 336; 1995 (29) ATC 116; 1995 (1) CLR 398; 1995 (70) FLR 789; JT 1994 (7) SC 748; 1995 LabIC 1401; 1994(5) SCALE 57; 1995 (1) SCJ 199; 1995 (2) SLJ 1; 1995 (1) SLR 16; [1994] Supp6 S.C.R. 224; 1995 (1) UJ 213

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Fundamental Rule, r. 56J - Respondent working as an Executive Engineer was suspended pending enquiry into certain charges - Challenging said order by way of writ petition in High Court which was dismissed - Though enquiry commenced, it was not concluded when respondent filed another Writ Petition challenging continuation of order of suspension pending enquiry - HC suspended order of suspension pending enquiry - During pendency of said enquiry, respondent was retired compulsorily under Fundamental Rule 56-J by an order of Govt. - Respondent then filed Writ Petition questioning same - Whether opinion of HC that merely because order of compulsory retirement was passed during pendency of a disciplinary enquiry, it must be necessarily deemed to be penal in nature, is sustainable ? - It cannot be said as a matter of law nor can it be stated as an invariable rule, that any and every order of compulsory retirement made u/r. 56-J during pendency of disciplinary proceeding is necessarily penal - Held, it may be or it may not be, is a matter to be decided on a verification of relevant record or material - Merely because order of compulsory retirement is couched in innocuous language without making imputations against Govt. servant, it is not penal in nature - HC erred in its judgment and is set aside - Matter remitted to HC.
