Hindustan Sugar Mills Limited v State of Uttar Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

26 April 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

M.K. Mukherjee, S. Mohan

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1510; (1994) 4 SCC 149; 1994(2) SCALE 775

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Constitution - Illegal and invalid levy of vend fees - Court had decided on the 25-10-1989 in the Synthetics and Chemicals Limited and Others v State of Uttar Pradesh and Others 1989 Indlaw SC 286 that the respondent States were restrained from enforcing the disputed levy any further but the respondents would not be liable for any refund and the tax already collected and paid would not be refunded and it was declared that those imposts to be illegal and invalid, but did not affect any realisations already made - Whether, Sate had power to levy the vend fee prior to 25-10-1989 - Held, instant court had agreed with earlier courts view and opined that respondent State had no power to levy the vend fee prior to 25-10-1989 neither it had powers to move the steps for realisation of the vend fee for the period prior to 25-10- 1989 - However, instant court was of the view that same decision should be decided by an appropriate Bench in order that there should be uniformity in decision - And hence, papers were placed before the Chief Justice for appropriate orders - Order accordingly.
