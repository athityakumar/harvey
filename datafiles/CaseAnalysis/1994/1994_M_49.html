Mah. State Financial Corpn. v Suvarna Board Mills

Supreme Court of India

18 August 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

B.L. Hansaria, A.M. Ahmadi

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 976; (1994) 5 SCC 566; AIR 1994 SC 2657; 1995 (2) Bom.C.R. 369; [1995] 82 Comp Cas 364; JT 1994 (5) SC 280; [1994] Supp2 S.C.R. 754

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate

Summary: Corporate - State Financial Corporations Act,1951, s. 29 - Constitution, art. 14 - A public Finacial coarporation issued show cause notice to a chronic defaulter whose earlier cheques have bounced - Notice calling upon defaulter to repay dues by certain date - And failing which possession of his factory would be taken - Representation by defaulter - Action u/s. 29 - Whether show cause notice issued to defaulter sufficiently meets the requirement of natural justice - Held, action cannot be set at naught for want of independent notice - Yet Corporation being a public body should communicate its response to representation before deciding to take over possession - Appeal allowed.
