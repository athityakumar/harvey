Vee Kayan Industries v Commissioner of Central Excise, Chandigarh

Supreme Court of India

20 September 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Sahai, N.P. Singh

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 83; (1995) Supp3 SCC 605; 1996 (64) ECR 351; 1996 (83) E.L.T. 262

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - Customs and Excise Tariff, Item 52 - Whether value of sales of bright bars and bright bar scrap be aggregated in value of sales of nuts and bolts? - Appellant contended that sale of bright bars and bright bar scrap could not be subject to fresh duty as bars were purchased from market and made brighter only subsequently - Tribunal held that passing of round bars through slight narrow diameter resulted in change of form and shape and resulted in bringing out a distinct and different commodity - Therefore, appellant was liable to pay duty on it - In absence of any material to show that in commercial circle bars and bright bars are different, inference drawn by Tribunal cannot be upheld - Held, value of sales of bright bars and bright bar scrap could not be aggregated in value of sales of nuts and bolts - Appeals allowed.
