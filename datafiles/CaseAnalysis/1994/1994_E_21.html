C.I.T. v Express Newspapers Ltd.

Supreme Court of India

11 January 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, Yogeshwar Dayal, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1975; (1994) 2 SCC 374; AIR 1994 SC 1389; 1994 (116) CTR 496; [1994] 206 ITR 443; JT 1994 (1) SC 50; 1994(1) SCALE 39; [1994] 1 S.C.R. 64; [1994] 72 TAXMAN 438

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 245 C(1) - Maintainability of application u/s. 245 C(1) - Applicant assessee must disclose any income which was not disclosed before assessing officer - Mere offer of part of amount (claimed as losses) by assessee towards taxable income is not sufficient - Held, it has not been found by Commission that Income Tax authorities were aware of filing of application before commission - Even if they were aware, mere filing of application did not mean that they should fold their hands and stop their investigation and enquiries in their tracks - They were, in fact, entitled to rely upon evidence and material collected by them till date of submission of report to Commission - Appeals allowed.
