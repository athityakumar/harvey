V. Lakshmanan v B.R. Mangalagiri And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

13 December 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, N. Venkatachala

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 722; (1995) Supp2 SCC 33; 1995 (1) BC 315; 1995 (1) CCC 40; 1995 (1) DMC 213; 1995 (1) HLR 315; JT 1995 (2) SC 105; 1995(1) SCALE 51; [1994] Supp6 S.C.R. 561; 1995 (1) UJ 685

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Civil Procedure; Family & Personal; Women & Children

Keywords: Minority, Adoption & Guardianship

Summary: Civil Procedure - Hindu Minority & Guardianship Act, 1956, s. 8 (3) - Appellant entered into an agreement with respondents to purchase their land, and he undertook to have sale deed registered within six months - Appellant had taken possession of land and levelled land for purpose of sale to third parties, appellant failed to obtain sanction of layout plan as Gram Panchayat refused to sanction it - Whether respondents are entitled to forfeit entire amount? - Held, that a specific covenant under contract was that respondents are entitled to forfeit money paid under contract, and so when contract fell through by default committed by appellant, as part of contract, they are entitled to forfeit entire amount - Appeal dismissed.
