Indrani Bai v Union Of India

Supreme Court of India

21 April 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, N. Venkatachala

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 621; (1994) Supp2 SCC 256; (1994) SCC (L&S) 981; 1994 (27) ATC 755; 1994 (69) FLR 94; JT 1994 (3) SC 580; 1995 (1) LLJ 431; 1994 (2) LLN 391; 1994(2) SCALE 777; [1994] 3 S.C.R. 608; 1994 (2) SLR 672

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, arts.41, 21 and 311(2) - Termination of service - Without holding proper inquiry - Legality - Appellant allegedly committed theft in office - As per dept. inquiry charge memo was given - On 26-6-1980, the General Manager appointed the enquiry officer to conduct an enquiry against him - On 5-3-1981, appellant made another representation to recall the witnesses already examined for cross-examination and to give him a further opportunity to examine himself and his witnesses in rebuttal - Authorities directed the delinquent, by their order dt. 26-9-1981, to attend the enquiry when fixed and cooperate with the enquiry officer - Pursuant to the direction, as found from the record, that the enquiry officer, instead of reopening the matter and giving him an opportunity to cross-examine the witnesses already examined or to examine himself as a witness or his witnesses, by a letter dt. 26-11-1991 asked delinquent to submit written brief - Delinquent did not submit his written brief and an order of dismissal from service was passed and on appeal, it was confirmed - In the meanwhile, the delinquent died on 1-3-1985 - Delinquent's widow/appellant made a representation for payment of gratuity, pension and other retiral benefits and also sought alternative employment for her subsistence - As regards payment of gratuity, it was paid but the pension was not paid on the ground that the delinquent was dismissed from service - Whether nonpayment of pension and denial of compassionate appointment to appellant was sustainable in law - Held, after the direction was issued by the Director General in his letter dt. 26-9-1981, the enquiry officer did not recall the ex parte order dt. 14-12-1980 nor did he recall the witnesses already examined on 20-9-1981, 24-9-1981 and 30-9-1981 for cross-examination nor had given him an opportunity to adduce his evidence in rebuttal - Further, despite the direction issued by the higher authorities, the enquiry officer directed the delinquent to submit written brief, in other words, he proceeded from the stage where he last closed the proceedings - That was not the spirit of the order of the Director General - Thus, it was a clear case of the violation of principles of natural justice - It was seen that right through, the delinquent officer had entertained a doubt about the impartiality of the enquiry to be conducted by the enquiry officer - When delinquent made a representation at the earliest, requesting to change the enquiry officer, the authorities should have acceded to the request and appointed another enquiry officer, other than the one whose objectivity was doubted - Unfortunately, that was not done - Even after the Director General had given an opportunity to the delinquent to participate in the enquiry, the enquiry officer obviously was expected to recall the ex parte order and give the delinquent an opportunity to cross-examine the witnesses already examined and to adduce his evidence in rebuttal - However, the enquiry officer did not adopt the said procedure which would have been just, fair and reasonable - Under these circumstances, it was a clear case that the delinquent was not afforded a fair opportunity, much less a reasonable opportunity to defend himself - That had resulted in violation of the principles of natural justice and fair play offending arts.41, 21 and 311(2) of the Constitution - Orders of dismissal as confirmed by the appellate authority were accordingly quashed - Respondents were directed to grant to the appellant the pensionary benefits according to rules and also to consider her case for suitable appointment on any post to which she might be eligible for rehabilitation, on compassionate ground - Respondents were further directed to pay the full salary payable to the deceased delinquent to the appellant from the date on which he was kept under suspension till date on which he would have attained superannuation or 28-2-1985 - Appeal allowed.
