Tamil Nadu Housing Board v Commissioner of Central Excise

Supreme Court of India

28 September 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Sahai, N. Venkatachala

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 500; (1995) Supp1 SCC 50; 1994 (55) ECR 7; 1994 (74) E.L.T. 9; 1994 (4) RLT 526; 1994(4) SCALE 388; [1994] Supp4 S.C.R. 62

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Excise - Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944, s. 11-A - Central Excise Rules, 1944, r. 173 - What is scope of proviso to s. 11-A of Act? - Held, proviso is in nature of an exception to principal clause - Both, requisite situation and intension, must concur to enable Excise Officer to proceed under this proviso and invoke exceptional power - As proviso extends period of limitation from six months to five years it has to be construed strictly - Initial burden is on department to prove that situations visualised by proviso existed - Once department is able to so, burden shifts and then applicability of proviso has to be construed liberally - When law requires an intention to evade payment of duty then it is not mere failure to pay duty - Assessee must be aware that duty was leviable and it must deliberately avoid paying it - In circumstances of case presumption of reasonable doubt of appellant cannot be said to have been successfully rebutted - Appeal allowed.
