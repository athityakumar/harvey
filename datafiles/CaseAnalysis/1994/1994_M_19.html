Sanjay Ahlawat v M.D. University And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

24 November 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Sen, B.P. Jeevan Reddy

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1213; (1995) 2 SCC 762; AIR 1995 SCW 228; 1995 (70) FLR 291; JT 1995 (1) SC 26; 1994(4) SCALE 1115; 1995 (1) SLR 45; [1994] Supp6 S.C.R. 59; 1995 (1) UJ 138

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Education - Constitution of India, 1950, art.14 - Reservation of seats - Discrimination alleged by outstation candidates - Additional marks given to students of respondent college - Whether cl. 3(i) of Chapter IV of Prospectus for MD/MS/PG Diploma Entrance Examination, 1993 of Maharishi Dayanand University, which prescribes criteria for determining merit of candidates is constitutionally valid? - Held, no reservation has been made for boys graduating from respondent college at all - Students from other universities may apply for admission - There is no reservation of seats college-wise or university-wise - But some preference is given to candidates, based on domicile in Haryana or education at only medical college in Haryana - This does not have effect of shutting doors of admission to outstation boys - Appeal dismissed.
