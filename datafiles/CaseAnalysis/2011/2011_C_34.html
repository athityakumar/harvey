Chakas v State Of Punjab & Ors

Supreme Court of India

24 August 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Deepak Verma, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 522; (2011) 12 SCC 128; 2012 (3) ALD(SC) 105; 2011(6) ALL MR 443; 2011 (6) AWC 6119; 2011 (3) CLT(SC) 366; JT 2011 (10) SC 447; 2011 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 2988; 2012 (1) MLJ(SC) 183; 2011 (4) RCR(Civil) 211; 2011(9) SCALE 362; [2011] 10 S.C.R. 618

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Administrative - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4, 6, 18 - Land acquisition - Enhancement of compensation - Entitlement - Respondent State issued Notification u/s. 4 of the Act for acquisition of appellants' lands for setting up of an Industrial Focal Point - Respondent issued declaration u/s. 6 of the Act - Land Acquisition Officer passed award fixing different rates for the land acquired - Appellants preferred references u/s. 18 of the Act for enhancement of compensation - Reference Court enhanced the compensation, however, deducted 50% value towards development charges - Being dissatisfied with the quantum of compensation, appellants appealed to HC - HC, by impugned order, enhanced compensation further - What would be proper, adequate, just and reasonable compensation to be awarded to the appellant for the land acquired by the respondent State - Held, PW 31 (landowner) admitted that more than 80 to 85 industries near and adjoining the acquired land were already running and doing their business since long - Evidence of other government officials, who had appeared before the Reference Court, reflected that the land acquired had great Industrial potential as more than 80-85 big industries had already set up their factories in the close vicinity to the acquired land - Appellant produced (Exh.P8) sale deed for proving the market value of the property - Land so sold covered under (Exh.P8) sale deed neither belonged to any of the appellants nor they had any interest whatsoever in the said deed - Thus, it would safely be assumed that Exh.P8 was a genuine and bona fide transaction between two parties, who had nothing to do with the acquisition of appellants' lands - Exh.P8 was not executed for the purpose of creating evidence as agreement to sale for such transaction was executed before the issuance of Notification u/s. 4 of the Act - Therefore, whole transaction executed under the sale deed (Exh.P8) fully proved and established the case of the appellant - Reference Court committed a grave error in deducting 50% of the value assessed by it, towards development charges and further reduced the said amount for the reasons not assigned by it - HC, vide the impugned judgment, had enhanced the amount of compensation but committed an error in fixing the base price as 2,75,000/- per acre for the acquired land, applying the doctrine of reasonable cut to the average price worked out by it at Rs.3,42,527/- per acre - Reasoning adopted either by the Reference Court or by the HC was not approved - Further, once the industry was set up, it would be for the financial benefit and gain of 3rd respondent beneficiary year after year - Thus, looking to the matter from all angles, 3rd respondent would be a great beneficiary at the cost of depriving the appellant land owner of his sole livelihood of agriculture - Hence, base price was required to be fixed at the rate of Rs. 4,08,000/- per acre - Keeping in mind that more than 525 acres had been given to 3rd respondent, which in turn had set up its factory, a deduction of 10% on the base price would be reasonable - Further, appellant would be entitled for statutory benefits as mandated under the amended provisions of the Act - Direction issued to the Reference Court to recalculate the amount of compensation to be awarded to the appellants and all such other land owners whose lands had been acquired in the light of the directions contained in the instant judgment - Appeals disposed of.
