Jaipur Development Authority And Others v Vijay Kumar Data and another

Supreme Court of India

12 July 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 520; (2011) 12 SCC 94; 2011 (5) RCR(Civil) 351B; 2011 (3) RLW 2384; [2011] 7 S.C.R. 242

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Rajasthan Lokayukta and Up-Lokayuktas Act, 1973

Summary: Land & Property - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, o. 21 r. 99, ss. 144, 47 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 166, 226 - Jaipur Development Authority Act, 1982, s. 83 - Rajasthan Land Acquisition Act, 1953, ss. 31(3), 31(4), 4 -Rajasthan Lokayukta and Up-Lokayuktas Act, 1973, ss. 1, 10, 2(1) - Acquisition - Execution - Validity - - By notification issued u/s. 4 of the 1953 Act Sate Govt. proposed acquisition for planned development - Declaration u/s. 6 was issued , further notice was issued to land owners u/ss. 9(1) and (3) of the 1953 Act - - Land Acquisition Officer passed an unusual award where he not only determined amount of compensation payable to landowners and beneficiaries of illegal transfers, but also directed allotment of plots measuring 1000 to 2000 square yards to owners, their transferees and nominees/sub-nominees out of the acquired land - After passing of award GNG filed execution application and succeeded in getting an order for delivery of possession of land in Scheme - - - Members of Committee recommended that land be allotted to beneficiaries of illegal transactions at rate of Rs.8/- per square yard - Thereafter circular disguised as policy decision was issued for allotment of land to sub-awardees and their nominees/sub-nominees at the rate of Rs.8/- per square yard - In furtherance of policy decision, draw of lots was held for allotment of plots to awardees and beneficiaries of illegal transfers of acquired land and those who were successful were allotted plots - This exercise did not satisfy all and those who could not get plots filed writ petitions questioning draw of lots - DB held directions given by Land Acquisition Officer and Minister for allotment of plots were ex-facie illegal and had effect of defeating public purpose for which land was acquired - Notwithstanding this, HC granted relief to writ petitioners on ground of violation of equality cl enshrined in art. 14 of Constitution and directed that they should also be allotted plots as per their entitlement - Lokayukta made inquiry u/s. 10 of the 1973 Act and submitted report and no action was taken on recommendations contained in report - Hence, instant appeal - Whether DB could have granted relief to respondents by entertaining an altogether new case with reference to policy framed by State Govt. for regularization of illegal allotments / encroachments of acquired land in Schemes - Held, unless an order was expressed in name of President or Governor and was authenticated in manner prescribed by rules, same could not be treated as an order made on behalf of Govt. - Reading of letter showed that it was neither expressed in name of Governor nor it was authenticated manner prescribed by Rules - Letter merely speaks of discussion made by Committee and decision taken by it - By no stretch of imagination same could be treated as policy decision of Govt. within meaning of art. 166 of Constitution - It was further held that even if instructions contained in letter could be treated as policy decision of Govt., HC should have quashed same because said policy was clearly contrary to law declared by Court and was crude attempt by concerned political functionaries of State to legalise what had already been declared illegal by Court - Court was prima facie satisfied that execution of lease deeds by appellant in favour of some persons in 2002 and 2003 was clear indication of deep rooted malaise in functioning of appellant and was also indicative of sheer favouritism and nepotism, Court refrained from pronouncing upon legality of those transactions because beneficiaries were not parties to these appeals - Hence, impugned judgment was set aside - - Respondents should be entitled to recover price paid to GNG along with amount of cost by availing appropriate legal remedy - Since so called policy decision contained in letter was contrary to law declared by this Court, State Govt. and appellant were restrained from taking any action in future on basis of said letter - Appeal allowed.
