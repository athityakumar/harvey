Kolkata Metropolitan Development Authority and another v Gobinda Chandra Makal and another

Supreme Court of India

02 September 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 634; (2011) 9 SCC 207; JT 2011 (10) SC 573; 2011 (4) RCR(Civil) 758; 2011(10) SCALE 41; [2011] 14 S.C.R. 373; 2012 (2) UPLBEC 954

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Frontage, West Bengal Land (Requisition & Acquisition) Act, 1948

Summary: Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 11A, 4 (1), 6, 9 (3A), 9 (3B) - West Bengal Land (Requisition & Acquisition) Act, 1948, ss. 3(1), s. 4(1a) - Acquisition - Requisition - Frontage - Statutory interest - Lands belonging to respondent No. 1, were requisitioned by State Govt. u/s. 3(1) of the 1948 Act - Possession of land was taken by Collector in pursuance of such requisition and in anticipation of acquisition, value of land was assessed u/s. 8B of the 1948 Act and 80% of estimated compensation was paid to respondent No.1 - After Land the 1894 Act was amended by the 1997 acquisition proceedings under the 1948 Act were converted into acquisition proceedings under the 1948 Act - Fresh acquisition proceedings were initiated as no award was passed for a period of 2 years - Respondent no. 1 acquired report of experts for determining market value of lands - Reference Court on considering evidence was of view that valuation by expert value should be accepted - Aggrieved, Metropolitan Development Authority and State filed appeals - HC dismissed appeals - Hence, instant appeal - (A) Whether Reference Court had wrongly doubled value worked out with reference to these sale deeds, by applying thumb rule that value of sali lands were twice that of value of beel lands - Held, Beel lands when developed into residential plots, by draining, filling and leveling land, would cease to be Beel in nature - There was no evidence to show that these plots were drained, filled, levelled and made into plots similar to Sali plots - Sale deeds refer to these plots as Beel plots - Contention of appellant that value of these Beel plots should be treated on par with value of Sali plots and that should form basis for determining market value of Sali Plots was rejected - (B) Whether additions of 58% to value of plot no.62, 45% to value of plot no.42 and 58% to value of plot no. 272 was liable to be set aside - Held, unless difference between sale of transaction is more than 1 year, normally no addition should be made towards appreciation in value, unless there was special evidence to show some specific increase - Therefore, addition of 58% for plot nos.62 and 272 and addition of 45% for plot no.42 had to be deleted and were set aside - (C) Whether development cost with reference to Plot no. 192 ought to had been at least 50% instead of 33.33% - Evidence of expert valuer shows that sale price of Plot no. 192 furnished market value and submissions were made by appellants to Reference Court and HC and cut for development from market value of plot No.192 should be 33.33 - Thus, no reason to alter percentage of alteration of 33.33% - (D) Whether amount paid as advance to respondent no. 1 with appropriate interest thereon, should be adjusted against compensation - Held, compensation was paid u/s. 4(1) of the 1894 Act to respondent/land owner and statutory interest payable to respondent no. 1 would be calculated by adjusting aforesaid advance payment with interest therein towards compensation amount -- Appeals partly allowed.
