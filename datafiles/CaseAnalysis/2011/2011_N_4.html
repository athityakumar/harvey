Kodikunnil Suresh @ J. Monian v N.S. Saji Kumar, Etc. Etc

Supreme Court of India

12 May 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Patnaik, Altamas Kabir

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 372; (2011) 6 SCC 430; 2011 (2) CLT(SC) 386; JT 2011 (6) SC 83; 2011 (2) KLT 721; 2011 (6) MLJ(SC) 595; 2011 (3) RCR(Civil) 194; 2011(6) SCALE 70; [2011] 7 S.C.R. 640

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Keywords: House Of The People, Scheduled Caste, Reserved Constituency, Reserved For Scheduled Caste, Kerala Registration of Marriages (Common) Rules, 2008, Constitution (Scheduled Castes Order, 1950

Summary: Election - Constitution (Scheduled Castes) Order, 1950 - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 4(a), 100(1)(a), 100 (1)(d)(i) - Parliamentary Election - Reserved seat for Scheduled Caste - Caste of returned candidate - Determination - Appellant filed nomination paper for the seat reserved for SC in Parliament election - Respondent filed objection that appellant was not a member of SC in terms of 1950 Order - Returning Officer rejected objection and accepted appellant's nomination - Appellant subsequently won election - Respondents filed election petition challenging the candidacy of appellant on ground that appellant was a Christian and while under 1950 Order only a Hindu could be a scheduled caste and not being a scheduled caste, he was not qualified to be chosen to fill a seat in Parliament u/s. 4(a) of the Act and accordingly his election was void u/s. 100 (1) (a) of Act - HC, by impugned order, set aside appellant's election - Whether declaration of appellant's election as void by HC was justifiable - Held, appellant's father was a member of Cheramar caste which was admittedly a scheduled caste in the State - On conversion to Christianity, appellant's father had ceased to be a member of Cheramar caste - However, in 1978 appellant reconverted to Hinduism and continued to be a Hindu thereafter - Appellant had stated in para 13 of his affidavit (examination-in-chief) before the HC that in 1979 he was actively working for upliftment of Cheramar community and Kerala Cheramar Sangham issued a certificate thereof produced and marked before HC - Said certificate had been signed by the then President of Kerala Cheramar Sangham, and it stated that being a descendant of a scheduled caste convert and by conversion appellant was accepted and admitted into the fold of Hindu Cheramar Community by its members who were Cheramar Hindus and by that fact had become a member of Cheramar Community which was recognized as a scheduled caste community - Said certificate had been issued ten years prior to 1989 when appellant for the first time contested from a Parliamentary Constituency reserved for scheduled caste - In the years 1989, 1991, 1996 and 1999, appellant contested and got elected from same reserved Parliamentary Constituency - Circumstances clearly established that appellant after his reconversion to Hinduism in 1978 had been accepted by members of Cheramar caste - Appellant declared himself to be belonging to Cheramar caste in his nomination form - Returning Officer relying on caste certificate issued by Tehsildar had come to the conclusion that appellant belonged to Cheramar caste and had accordingly accepted his nomination - Findings of HC, therefore, that there was no acceptable evidence to prove that appellant was accepted as a member of Cheramar caste after his reconversion to Hinduism was contrary to evidence on record - Fact that appellant had been elected four times from same Parliamentary Constituency reserved for SC was a very strong circumstance to establish that he had been accepted by members of his caste after his reconversion to Hinduism - Appellant, therefore, was qualified u/s. 4(a) of Act to be chosen to fill seat in Parliamentary constituency reserved for SC and that his nomination was not improperly accepted by Returning Officer and accordingly his election was not void u/ss. 100 (1)(a) and 100 (1)(d)(i) of the Act - Hence, impugned HC order was set aside - Appellant would be entitled to amount deposited by respondents u/s. 117 of Act as security deposit towards costs - Appeals allowed.
