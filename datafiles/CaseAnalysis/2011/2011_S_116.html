Gridco Limited & Anr. v Sri Sadananda Doloi & Ors

Supreme Court of India

16 December 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 794; (2011) 15 SCC 16; AIR 2012 SC 729; 2012 (132) FLR 338; 2012 (2) LLJ 257; 2012 (2) MLJ(SC) 998; [2011] 16 S.C.R. 384; 2012 (1) SLR 755

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Grid Corporation Officers Service Regulations, 1996

Summary: Service - Contract & Commercial - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14, 16 and 226 - Grid Corporation Officers Service Regulations, 1996, - Contractual appointment - Renewal - Validity - Appellant-corporation issued an advertisement for the post of Sr. General Manager - Respondent appointed for the said post on contract basis for a period of 3 years, subject to renewal - Appellant-corporation re-designated respondent as Chief General Manager - Appellant-corporation extended employment of respondent on expiry of contractual period - Subsequently,respondent was terminated with 3 months salary in lieu of notice - Respondent filed writ petition before Single Judge - Single Judge dismissed writ petition holding respondent's employment was purely temporary and contractual in nature and had no legal right to claim continuance in service - DB allowed appeal filed against said order and held 'introduction of a contractual condition' on a regular appointment under state was opposed to principles of art. 14 and 16 of the Constitution, and freedom of contract was rendered illusory by an unequal bargaining power between a citizen seeking appointment to public service on the one hand and a giant employer like a State Corporation on the other - Hence, instant appeal - (A) Whether appointment of respondent was regular or simply contractual in nature - Held, there was no element of regular appointment in the offer made to respondent or any assurance that appointment was in the nature of a regular appointment or that he was on probation to be regularised on satisfactory completion of his probation period - Further, it was evident from the correspondence that parties were ad idem regarding the tenure of appointment - Regulations did not envisage regular appointment at E-10 level to which respondent was appointed on the terms of contract of employment - It was difficult to analyse the nature of appointment, treated to be a regular appointment when the Rules did not permit any such appointment - Hence, nature of appointment made by appellant-Corporation was contractual and not regular as held by DB - (B) If the appointment was contractual, was the termination vitiated any legal infirmity for interference u/art. 226 of the Constitution - Held, writ Court was entitled to judicially review and determine whether there was any illegality, perversity, unreasonableness, unfairness or irrationality that would vitiate the action, no matter the action was in the realm of contract - Judicial review cannot be extended to Court acting as an appellate authority - There was no material to show that there was any unreasonableness, unfairness, perversity or irrationality in the action taken by appellants-corporation - Regulations governing service conditions of employees of appellant-corporation, made it clear that officers in the category above E-9 had to be appointed only on contractual basis - Renewal of the contract of employment depended upon the perception of the management as to the usefulness of respondent and the need for an incumbent on the position held by him - Thus, there was no element of any unfair treatment or unequal bargaining power between appellant and respondent to call for an over-sympathetic or protective approach towards the latter - Impugned order of DB was set aside - Appeal allowed.
