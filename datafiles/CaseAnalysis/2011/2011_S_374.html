Dr. Puneet Gulati and others Etc. v State of Kerala and others Etc.

Supreme Court of India

17 August 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Cyriac Joseph, Surinder Singh Nijjar

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 545; (2011) 12 SCC 722; 2011(5) ALL MR 973; 2011 (88) ALR 692; JT 2011 (9) SC 353; 2011 (3) KLT 712; 2011(9) SCALE 283; [2011] 10 S.C.R. 279; 2012 (1) SLR 61

Case Digest

Subject: Education

Summary: Education - Admission to Super Speciality Course - Reservation to local candidates - Validity - Appellant challenged the condition stipulated in the prospectus for admissions to Super Speciality Courses provided that students who had completed MBBS or Post-graduate courses from Medical Colleges in Kerala and Doctors who had done Rural Service in Kerala, would be given preference for admission and students who were not from Kerala would get a chance for admission only if there were no students from the State of Kerala available for admission in the aforesaid courses - Single Judge dismissed appellants' writ petitions on the ground that after participating in the entrance examination they were not entitled to challenge the prospectus - Appellants filed appeal - DB agreed with the views expressed by the Single Judge, but while technically allowing the claim of the candidates who were from outside Kerala, on the ground that 100% reservation was unconstitutional, chose not to give any relief to the said students on the ground that the course had commenced more than 6 months prior to the matter being heard by the DB - Whether the appellant was entitled to a seat in the Super Speciality Course - Held, decision of the Single Judge and the DB as to the constitutional validity of the first and second prospectus reserving 100% of the seats in the Super Speciality Courses for students from Kerala alone were upheld - However, by an interim order, SC had stayed the admission process for the Super Speciality Courses for the year 2011-2012 in the Government Medical Colleges in Kerala - Subsequently, by another order, SC had modified the said order on the prayer made on behalf of the State of Kerala by directing that the admission process could continue but 5 seats were to be set apart for the appellants - Since 5 seats reserved in terms of SC order, although the appellant did not sit for the entrance examination for the year 2011-2012, on the strength of his marks in the entrance examination for the year 2010-2011, he should be given admission in one of the two seats available in the M.Ch. Genito Urinary Surgery course, which had been kept vacant in terms of subsequent SC order - Instant order had been passed in the special facts of the case and should not be treated as a precedent in future cases - Appeals disposed of.
