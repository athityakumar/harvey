Guru Dev Singh v State Of M.P.

Supreme Court of India

10 May 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Mukundakam Sharma, Anil R. Dave

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 360; (2011) 5 SCC 721; AIR 2011 SC 2088; 2011 ALL MR (Cri) 2395; 2011 CRLJ 3101; 2011 (2) JabLJ 276; 2011 (3) JCC 1829; JT 2011 (6) SC 76; 2011 (3) MLJ(Crl) 808; 2011 (3) RCR(Criminal) 30; 2011(6) SCALE 20; [2011] 6 S.C.R. 941; 2011 (3) WLN(SC) 50

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Kirpan

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 323, 34 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant accused allegedly murdered the deceased - Trial Court convicted and sentenced the appellant for murder that was upheld by the HC in appeal - Whether the conviction recorded by the Trial Court was sustainable - Held, PW1 eye-witness categorically stated that appellant and other accused persons caused serious injuries on the head and body of the deceased by using weapons which they were carrying with them - PW2 eye-witness also clearly stated that all the accused had inflicted injuries on the deceased - Statements of PW1 and PW2 were supported by the proved medical evidence of PW7 doctor - Nature of the injuries caused to the deceased would prove and establish that the injuries were caused with the intention of killing the deceased - It was also established from the records that weapons used during the incident, had been recovered at the instance of the accused persons and on the basis of the statements made by the accused persons leading to their discovery which were cogent and admissible evidence in the instant case - When the medical evidence of PW7 was read along with post mortem report and the statements of PWs 1 and 2, who were stated to be eye-witnesses, as also the statements of the accused persons leading to the discovery, which were admissible in evidence, it was clearly established that the deceased received serious injuries on account of the blows of the weapons used by the accused persons due to which the deceased died - PW7 doctor clearly stated in his evidence that the deceased died due to the head injuries and that the said injuries were sufficient to cause death in normal course of nature - PW4, who was a witness to the recovery of weapon, had clearly stated that on the basis of the statements made by the accused persons the weapons were recovered from the places shown by the accused persons - Hence, evidence of PW4 also proved the allegation made against the accused persons including the appellant - Entire evidence on record showed that the appellant was guilty of the offence committed u/s. 302/34 of the IPC as also under Section 323 /34 of the IPC and, therefore, order of conviction and sentence passed by the HC against him was justified - No merit in the appeal - Appeal dismissed.
