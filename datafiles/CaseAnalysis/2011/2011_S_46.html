Sec., U. P. S. C. and another v S. Krishna Chaitanya

Supreme Court of India

05 August 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

A.R. Dave, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 571; (2011) 14 SCC 227; 2011 (6) ALD(SC) 69; 2011 (5) AWC 4360; 2011 (4) CLT(SC) 359; 2011 (7) MLJ(SC) 836; [2011] 9 S.C.R. 842; 2012 (1) SLR 784; 2011 (4) UPLBEC 2988; 2011 (4) UPLBEC 2995

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Practice & Procedure

Summary: (1). Employment-Civil Services Pre-Examination, 2010-Respondent sent his application form to Union Public Service Commission through courier-He handed over his application form to courier on January 28, 2010-Courier intimated to respondent that application form was delivered to Commission on January 29, 2010-But courier did not give acknowledgment receipt issued by Commission in this behalf-Respondent made no enquiry from Commission within 30 days as required by advertisement-And awoke only on 20.4.2010 by sending representation to Commission-Commission informed that his application form not received-As respondent did not receive any admit card-He filed O.A. before Central Administrative Tribunal-By interim order Tribunal directed respondent to submit copy of his application form-And also directed Commission to issue admit card to respondent-Admit card issued to respondent-O.A. allowed by Tribunal with direction to Commission to declare result-And if respondent qualified-He should be permitted to take C.S. Examination (Mains), 2010-Said order of Tribunal challenged by Commission before High Court-High Court observing that respondent be permitted to take Mains Examination-And if he qualified-He should be permitted to appear for interview-Respondent appeared in Mains Examination-And also appeared for oral interview-But his result not declared and retained by Commission in sealed cover-But there is no indication in run sheet of courier that consignments were received on behalf of Commission-No proof could be submitted by respondent that application form was received by Commission/appellants-Even while giving final direction-Tribunal had not come to definite finding that application form of respondent was received by appellants-But misplaced-Appellants cannot be directed to declare final result of respondent-Orders of Tribunal and High Court quashed-I.A. filed by the respondent rejected.As the case involves a career of a young man, who can turn out to be a good civil servant, the Court had very meticulously gone through the record maintained by the appellants. Looking to the system which is being followed by the appellants, it is found that the said system is very comprehensive and flawless. It is very clear that if the application form of the respondent had been received by the appellants in the manner provided, it would have been recorded somewhere. Even the eight digit number of the application form of the respondent has not been recorded anywhere. Receipt of an application form through a courier is treated as 'hand delivery' by the appellants. In case of receipt of an application by hand delivery, on the spot, an acknowledgment card stamped with a distinct numerical mark is handed over to the person who delivers the application form. If the application form had been delivered by a representative of the courier agency to the office of the appellants, there was no reason for the appellants not to give a duly stamped acknowledgment card bearing a distinct numerical mark. No such acknowledgment card, duly stamped, could be produced by the respondent or by the courier agency. Thus, on perusal of the record and looking to the facts of the case, it is evident that no proof could be submitted by the respondent that the application form was received by the appellants.

Even while giving final direction to the appellants with regard to permitting the respondent to take the Civil Services Examination, the Tribunal had not come to a definite finding and specific conclusion that the application form of the respondent was in fact received by the appellants but the same had been misplaced by the appellants. Hence, in such a set of circumstances, it would not be proper to direct the appellants to permit the respondent to take the examination especially when there was nothing on record to show that the respondent had submitted his application form to the appellants. For the aforestated reasons, the appellants cannot be directed to declare the final result of the respondent, especially when his application form had not been received by the appellants within the period prescribed.

(2). Practice and procedure-Interim relief-Should not be of such nature that by virtue thereof-Petition or application is finally allowed or granted even at interim stage.
