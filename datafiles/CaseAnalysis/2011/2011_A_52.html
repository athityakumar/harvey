Amerika Rai & Ors. v State Of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

23 February 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

V.S. Sirpurkar, A.R. Dave

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 128; (2011) 4 SCC 677; AIR 2011 SC 1379; JT 2011 (5) SC 406; 2012 (5) RCR(Criminal) 746; 2011(2) SCALE 696; [2011] 3 S.C.R. 176

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302 r/w. 149 and 307 - Arms Act, 1959, s. 27 - Murder - Conviction - Challenged - Six accused persons were tried for forming themselves into an unlawful assembly and as a common object committing the murder of 'X' - All accused persons were convicted for the offence punishable u/ss. 302 r/w. 149 of IPC - The appeals of all accused persons were dismissed by HC - Hence, the instant appeals - Whether trial court rightly convicted accused persons u/ss. 302 r/w. 149 of IPC? - Held, there can be no dispute that the presence of the accused persons on the spot was well established by the 5 eye-witnesses - All the 5 eye-witnesses who supported the prosecution have in one voice deposed to the presence of all accused persons and the acts performed by them - However, the role of A-2, as attributed to by the eye-witnesses, should not make him vicariously liable - Therefore, benefit of doubt to be granted to the A-2 - Hence, all the accused persons were held guilty and their appeals were dismissed excepting that of A-2 whose appeal was accepted - Appeals dismissed
