M/S Dewan Chand Builders & Contractors v Union Of India & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

18 November 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

D.K. Jain, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 830; (2012) 1 SCC 101; 2012 (2) AWC 1355; 2012 (1) Bom.C.R. 342; 2012 (1) CTC 421; 2011 (274) E.L.T. 161; 2012 (1) MLJ(SC) 675; [2011] 13 S.C.R. 214; 2012 (1) UPLBEC 295

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Building and Other Construction Workers (Regulation of Employment and Conditions of Service) Central Rules, 1998

Summary: Constitution - Administrative - Labour & Industrial Law - The Building and Other Construction Workers (Regulation of Employment and Conditions of Service) Act, 1996 - The Building and Other Construction Workers Welfare Cess Act, 1996 - The Building and Other Construction Workers (Regulation of Employment and Conditions of Service) Central Rules, 1998 - The Building and Other Construction Workers Welfare Cess Rules, 1998 - Enactment of Act - Imposition of Cess - Collection of a Cess on cost of construction - Validity of Acts - Appellant was a contractor, engaged in building and other construction works in National Capital Territory - BOCW Act empowered Central Govt. and State Govt. to constitute Welfare Boards to provide and monitor social security schemes and welfare measures for the benefit of building and other construction workers - Thereafter, to generate revenues for making effective welfare provisions of the BOCW Act was through the Cess Act - Statement of Objects and Reasons to the Cess Act noted that intention was to 'provide for levy and collection of a Cess on cost of construction incurred by employers for augmenting resources of Building and Other Construction Workers' - Aggrieved, appellant challenged the validity of the Cess Act - Appellant contended that impost levied by the Cess Act was a compulsory and involuntary exaction, made for a public purpose without reference to any special benefit for payer of Cess - HC dismissed the petitions and held that the BOCW Act, the BOCW Rules, the Cess Act and the Cess Rules" were constitutionally valid and within competence of Parliament as levy under impugned enactments was a 'fee', - Hence, instant appeal - Whether the cess levied under the scheme of impugned Cess Act, was a 'fee' or a 'tax' - Held, principal object of the Cess Act, was to develop mineral areas in State and to assist in providing more efficient and extended exploitation of its mineral wealth - Cess levied did not become a part of consolidated fund and was not subject to an appropriation in behalf - It went into a special fund earmarked for carrying out purpose of the Cess Act and - Features of the Cess Act impressed upon levy character of a 'fee' as distinct from a 'tax' - However, reasons of the Cess Act, clearly spells out essential purpose, enactment sought to achieve i.e. to augment Welfare Fund under the BOCW Act - Levy of Cess on cost of construction incurred by employers on building and other construction works was for ensuring sufficient funds for Welfare Boards to undertake social security schemes and welfare measures for building and other construction workers - Fund, so collected, was directed to specific ends spelt out in the BOCW Act - Therefore, applying principle laid down, it was clear that said levy was a 'fee' and not 'tax' - Impugned order of HC was affirmed - Appeals dismissed.
