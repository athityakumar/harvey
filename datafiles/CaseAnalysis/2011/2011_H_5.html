Chatterjee Petrochem (I) Pvt. Ltd. v Haldia Petrochemicals Ltd.& Ors

Supreme Court of India

30 September 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 835; AIR 2012 SC 2753; [2011] 167 Comp Cas 373; 2012 (1) CompLJ 1; 2011 (8) MLJ(SC) 897; [2011] 15 S.C.R. 135

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate

Keywords: Depositories Act, 1996, SEBI (Depositories and Participants) Regulations, 1996, Companies Act, 1948

Summary: Corporate - Companies Act, 1956, ss. 397, 402 - Non-fulfillment of agreement - Winding up - Non-registration of shares - Agreement was entered into between appellant and Govt., represented by respondent no.8 wherein, it was indicated that Govt. would sell its entire shareholding in respondent-company and that price of shares would be determined by independent valuer selected by Govt. of West Bengal from amongst panel of firms - Respondent-company and Govt. of West Bengal had failed to fulfill their commitment to transfer their balance 36% shares to appellants - Appellants filed company petition which was disposed of - Govt. of West Bengal filed appeal before HC Single Judge it was held that appellant had no locus standi to maintain petition u/s. 397 of the Act - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether affairs of company were being conducted in manner oppressive to any member which was sufficient to justify passing of winding-up order and despite having transferred 155 million shares in favour of appellant it did not register same in name of appellant - Held, induction of Indian Oil Corporation (IOC) as member of company was part of conspiracy to deprive appellant of control of company as respondent-company never intended to keep its promise regarding transfer of its shareholdings in favour of appellant - If in first place, appellant had stood by its commitment to bring in equity and had subscribed to Rights Issue, which was decision taken by company to infuse equity in running of company, it would neither have been reduced to minority nor would it perhaps had been necessary to induct IOC as portfolio investor with possibility of same being converted into strategic investment - Failure of respondent-company to register 155 million shares transferred to appellant could not, be taken to be failure on part of company, but it was failure of one of parties to private arrangement to abide by its commitments - Remedy in such case was not u/s. 397 of the Act - It was not on account of any act on part of company that shares transferred to appellant were not registered in name of appellant Group - There was no occasion for the CLB (Company Law Board)to make any order u/ss. 397, 402 of the Act - Appellants had failed to substantiate either of two grounds canvassed by them for CLB to assume jurisdiction either u/ss. 397, 402 of the Act and it could not have given directions to respondent-company to transfer 520 million shares held by them in appellant - HC had rightly dismissed company petition - Appeal dismissed.
