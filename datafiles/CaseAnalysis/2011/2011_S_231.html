Binabai Bhate v State Of Madhya Pradesh And Ors

Supreme Court of India

04 July 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Mukundakam Sharma, A.R. Dave

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 399; (2011) 13 SCC 32; 2011 (3) JabLJ 333; JT 2011 (7) SC 510; 2011(6) SCALE 726; [2011] 7 S.C.R. 31

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Madhya Pradesh Nagar Tatha Gram Nivesh Adhiniyam, 1973

Summary: Land & Property - Madhya Pradesh Nagar Tatha Gram Nivesh Adhiniyam, 1973, ss. 14, 17A(2) and 19 - Land acquisition - Challenged - Respondent published draft development plan wherein it included some portion of appellant's land with the intention of making it available for Mela - Appellant filed objection to the acquisition of her land - Committee was constituted to consider the said objection who held that impugned land of the appellant was not required - Accordingly, a resolution dt. 26.05.2000 was passed by the committee in favour of the appellant - In spite of the resolution passed by the committee, the State Government had included land of the appellant in the modified development plan - Appellant filed review petition u/s. 23(A) of the Act before the State Government which was rejected stating that there was no provision for review of the order in the Act - Appellant filed writ petition against said order which was dismissed by Single Judge - DB upheld the order passed by the Single Judge - Appellant filed appeal against said order contending that the as Committee constituted accepted the objections of the appellant, the said resolution of the Committee should have been accepted by the Government as the same was binding - Whether order of the Single Judge as affirmed by DB could be upheld? - Held, the resolutions passed by the Committee cannot be said to be absolute, final and binding and the State Government possesses the final authority in the matter of giving approval to the development plan - In the instant case. the State Government approved the draft plan without any modification and therefore provisions of sub-sections (2) and (3) of s. 19 were not applicable - Moreover, despite the said legal provision, the State Government had issued a final plan and also invited objections from the persons who are likely to be affected by inclusion of their land - Even thereafter the appellant did not submit any objection and therefore the question of giving a hearing to the appellant at that stage did not arise - Hence, contentions of the appellant were not found to be worthy of acceptance - Impugned order upheld - Appeal dismissed.
