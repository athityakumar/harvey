Commissioner Of Customs(Import) v Stoneman Marble Industries

Supreme Court of India

21 January 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

D.K. Jain, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 54; (2011) 2 SCC 758; AIR 2012 SC (Supp) 163; 2011 (264) E.L.T. 3; 2011 (5) RCR(Civil) 1; 2011(1) SCALE 600; [2011] 1 S.C.R. 545

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Redemption Fine And Penalty

Summary: Customs - Indirect Tax - Customs Act, 1962, ss. 111(d), 112(a), 125 and 130A - ITC [HS] Classification of Export & Import Items 1997-2002 - Confiscation - Requirement of specific licence - Respondents-importers were engaged in the business of import of rough marble blocks, from various countries in the year 1999 - Said goods were covered by Exim Code No. 25151200 of ITC [HS] Classification of Export & Import Items 1997-2002, and required a specific licence for importation under the EXIM Policy-1997-2002 - Respondents did not possess the specific licence at the time of imports of said goods and also mis-declared the price of said goods in the bills of entry - Goods imported by the respondents were confiscated u/s. 111(d) of the Act - However, the respondents were given an option to redeem the confiscated goods on payment of redemption fine u/s. 125 of the Act and penalty levied u/s. 112(a) of the Act - Aggrieved, respondents approached the tribunal who partly allowed the appeal relying on the tribunal decision in Stonemann Marble Industries vs. CC, Mumbai 2002 INDLAW CEGAT 2768 and held that facts of this case was similar to Stoneman case - However, Tribunal reduced the redemption fine and penalty to 20% and 5% of the CIF value respectively - HC rejected the applications filed by the Revenue against order of the tribunal on the ground that no question of law arose from the orders of the Tribunal - Revenue filed appeal against said order contending that the Tribunal could not lay down a standard formula for the computation of redemption fine and penalty as facts and circumstances of each case have to be examined independently - Whether revenue submissions could be accepted and order of the HC could be set aside? - Held, contention of the revenue that a standard formula cannot be laid down for imposition of redemption fine and penalty and each case has to be examined on its own facts has some substance but when a final fact finding body returns a finding that the facts obtaining in each of the cases before it are similar, and such finding is not questioned, levy of redemption fine or penalty uniformly in all such cases cannot be construed as laying down an absolute formula, which is the case here - In the present case, Revenue did not discharge its burden u/s. 130A of the Act in as much as it did not specifically challenge the Revenue's aforestated finding as being perverse - Hence, HC was justified in declining to issue direction to the Tribunal to make a reference u/s. 130A of the Act - Impugned orders affirmed - Revenue's appeals dismissed.
