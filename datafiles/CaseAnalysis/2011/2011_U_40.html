Centre For Environment & Food Security v Union Of India & Ors

Supreme Court of India

12 May 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Swatanter Kumar, S.H. Kapadia, K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 382; (2011) 5 SCC 668; 2011 (4) AWC 4212; JT 2011 (6) SC 56; 2011(6) SCALE 212; [2011] 6 S.C.R. 744; 2011 (3) UPLBEC 2385

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: National Rural Employment Guarantee Act, 2005

Summary: Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950 - Mahatma Gandhi National Rural Employment Guarantee Act, 2005 - Implementation of - Petitioner filed PIL for issuance of appropriate directions to the respondents to ensure proper implementation of the Mahatma Gandhi National Rural Employment Guarantee Act, 2005 and the schemes framed thereunder - Petitioner also prayed that proper investigation was required to be conducted into cases of non-compliance with the provisions of the Act, schemes framed thereunder and the guidelines issued by the Central Government to prevent diversion of funds specifically allocated for implementation of the schemes framed under the Act - Whether PIL filed by the petitioner could be allowed? - Held, CBI was directed to conduct free and fair investigation in regard to the implementation of provisions of the Act and the schemes framed thereunder without any impediment - The State Government of Orissa, all the State Departments and concerned authorities of the Central and State Governments were directed to fully cooperate with the CBI so as to facilitate the expeditious completion of the investigation - Order accordingly.
