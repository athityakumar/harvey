Maloth Somaraju v State Of A.P.

Supreme Court of India

17 August 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

V.S. Sirpurkar, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 505; (2011) 8 SCC 635; 2011 ALL MR (Cri) 3906; 2012 (1) CGLJ 35; 2012 CRLJ 402; 2011 (4) MLJ(Crl) 670; 2011 (3) RCR(Criminal) 889; 2011(9) SCALE 174; [2011] 10 S.C.R. 349

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Murder - Conviction - Challenged - Appellant was tried for the offence punishable u/s. 302 of IPC on the allegation that he caused the death of his elder brother with an axe injuring his temporal region, nose and face - Trial Court acquitted appellant - On appeal, HC reversed the judgment of Trial Court and convicted appellant u/s. 302 of IPC - HC relied on the direct testimony of PW-1 (wife of deceased) for convicting the appellant - Whether HC erred in upsetting verdict of acquittal given by the Trial Court? - Held, PW-1 was cross-examined and nothing could be brought out in her cross-examination which would bring her testimony into suspicion - There were no contradictions in her evidence - Also, she lodged the FIR within few hours of the incident - FIR given by her was complete in all the details - She had also given reasons for the appellant to attack her husband - FIR completely corroborates her evidence - Further, evidence on record clearly showed that PW-1 was a truthful witness and her evidence alone was sufficient for the conviction of the appellant - PW-1 was a reliable witness - Hence, HC rightly found that she was a natural witness and her presence in her own household was also natural - HC also found that there could not have been any motive on the part of PW-1 to falsely implicate her husband's brother (appellant) - Judgment of the HC confirmed - Appeal dismissed.
