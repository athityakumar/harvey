B. A. Umesh v Regr. Gen. High Court of Karnataka

Supreme Court of India

01 February 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 101; (2011) 3 SCC 85; AIR 2011 SC 1000; 2011 (2) JCC 924; JT 2011 (2) SC 128; 2011 (6) KarLJ 1; 2011 (3) MLJ(Crl) 127; 2011 (1) RCR(Criminal) 803B; 2011(2) SCALE 111; [2011] 2 S.C.R. 367

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 376, 302 and 392 - Rape, Murder and Robbery - Conviction - Challenged - The Trial Court convicted appellant u/ss. 376, 302 and 392 of IPC and sentenced him to undergo 7 years rigorous imprisonment with fine for the offence punishable u/s. 376 of IPC, 10 years rigorous imprisonment with fine for the offence u/s. 392 of IPC and also sentenced to death by hanging for the offence punishable u/s. 302 of IPC - On appeal, HC confirmed the conviction and death penalty awarded by the trial court - Hence, the present appeal - Whether order of the trial court as affirmed by the HC could be upheld? - Held, the death of the victim was homicidal had been amply proved by the post-mortem report of the Doctor (P.W.26), who was of the opinion that the death was due to asphyxia as a result of smothering and evidence of violent sexual intercourse and attempted strangulation - Moreover, the minor son of the deceased clearly depicted the manner in which after returning from playing with his friends he found the appellant, coming out of the room in which he and his mother lived - He also identified the appellant in a test identification parade and also in the Court room while deposing - The evidence of the minor son of the deceased was corroborated by the evidence of PW 10 who lived in a rented house almost opposite to the rented house of the deceased - Hence, conviction of the appellant u/ss. 376, 392 and 302 of IPC was confirmed - The extreme depravity with which the offences were committed and the merciless manner in which death was inflicted on the victim, brings it within the category of rarest of rare cases which merits the death penalty, as awarded by the Trial Court and confirmed by the HC - Death sentence awarded to the appellant upheld - Appeals dismissed.
