P. V. Indiresan v Union of India and others

Supreme Court of India

18 August 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 600; (2011) 8 SCC 441; 2012 (3) AWC 2469; 2011 (3) CLT(SC) 250; 2011 (181) DLT 608; 2011 (4) ESC 571; JT 2011 (9) SC 415; 2011 (8) MLJ(SC) 956; 2011(9) SCALE 33; [2011] 10 S.C.R. 384; 2011 (6) SLR 776

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Constitution; Education

Keywords: Central Educational Institutions (Reservation in Admission) Act, 2006, Constitution (Ninety Third Amendment) Act, 2005, Haryana Service of Engineers Class I, PWD (Irrigation Branch) Rules, 1964

Summary: Constitution - Education - Administrative - Constitution of India, 1950 - Central Educational Institutions (Reservation in Admission) Act, 2006, Constitution (Ninety Third Amendment) Act, 2005, Haryana Service of Engineers Class I, PWD (Irrigation Branch) Rules, 1964 - Backward Classes-27% reservation - Entitlement - Petitioner made an application alleging that some central educational institutions were interpreting decision contrary to law laid down therein and sought directions/clarifications - Revised decision dt. 12-7-2010 of Deans Committee was challenged by two OBC students (respondents 3 and 4) in writ petition filed in High Court - Single Judge allowed writ petition holding that respondent no. 1 UOI/Universities were entitled to only fix minimum eligibility criteria for admission in the reserved category at maximum 10% below the minimum eligibility criteria fixed for the General (Unreserved) category - The OBC candidates to avail of reservation provided for them in the CEI Act, 2006, were not required to, in admission test or in the eligibility exam, secure marks within the bandwidth of 10% below the cut-off marks of the last candidate admitted in the General (Unreserved) category - Said order was challenged by appellant, a non party before HC with an application seeking leave to challenge order of Single Judge before SC, without filing a letter patent appeal - Said application was heard and disposed of by Order dt. 14-10-2008 relied on in P V Indiresan Vs. Union of India 2008 Indlaw SC 2689 - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order of HC was justifiable - Held, issue before HC was with reference to meaning of words cut-off marks - Submissions in regard to question whether OBC candidates who were selected on basis of their own merit without benefit of reservation, should be counted towards 27% reservation, was not subject matter of writ petition - However use of words 'cut-off-marks' in none of three places in order dt. 14-10-2008, refers to marks secured by last candidate to be admitted in general category or in any particular category, or to minimum marks to be possessed by OBC candidates, determined with reference to marks secured by last candidate to be admitted under general category - In regard to admissions for 2011-2012, if any Central Educational Institution had already determined 'cut-off marks' for OBCs with reference to marks secured by last candidate in general category, and had converted unfilled OBC seats to general category seats and allotted seats to general category candidates, such admissions should not be disturbed but where process of conversion and allotment was not completed, OBC seats should be filled by OBC candidates - If in any Central Educational Institution, OBC reservation seats remain vacant, such institutions should fill said seats with OBC students - Only if OBC candidates possessing minimum eligibility/qualifying marks were not available in OBC merit list, OBC seats should be converted into general category seats - If last date for admissions had expired, last date for admissions should be extended till dt. 31-8-2011 as a special case, to enable admissions to vacant OBC seats - Therefore words "maximum cut-off marks for OBCs be 10% below the cut off marks of general category candidates" in the order dated 14.10.2008 of the Constitution Bench meant that if the minimum eligibility/qualifying marks prescribed for general category candidates was 50%, the minimum eligibility/qualifying marks for OBCs should be 45% - Hence, decision of Single Judge was upheld - Appeal Disposed of.
