State of Madhya Pradesh and another v Medha Patkar and others

Supreme Court of India

02 August 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, J.M. Panchal, Deepak Verma

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 568; (2011) 8 SCC 55; 2011 (6) AWC 5549; 2012 (2) JabLJ 105; [2011] 9 S.C.R. 664

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Keywords: Panchayats (Extension of Scheduled Areas) Act, 1996, Madhya Pradeh Business Rules

Summary: Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Administrative - Environment (Protection) Act, 1986, s. 3(2) - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, s. 18 - Constitution of India,1950 - Environment (Protection) Rules, 1986, r. 5(3) - Panchayats (Extension of Scheduled Areas) Act, 1996,ss. 3, 4(i) - Appellant/ State Govt. submitted Indira Sagar and Omkareshwar project reports and got clearance from Ministry of Environment and Forest (MoEF) and planning commission - Dams were completed in 2005 and 2007 - Land acquisition proceedings were initiated in 2009 for setting up canals -Compensation paid for some lands acquired under the 1894 Act and further acquisition was under process - Respondents challenged acquisition by writ petition in HC - HC by order dt. 11-.11-.2009 granted stay of acquisition of lands under those projects till CAD plans were scrutinized by experts committee and MoEF clearance and to apply R and R policy for Narmada Valley Projects to canal affected persons/families and directed Narmada Control Authority (NCA) to ensure implementation of these directions - Aggrieved State Govt. filed instant appeal - Whether HC order was just and proper - Held , MoEF submitted that final decision would be taken in 4 weeks and time was consuming as following directions of SC dt. 11-.5-.2011 - SC by order dt.5-5-2010 granted certain interim reliefs to canal affected persons which were later modified regarding compensation and solatium -No specific direction for rehabilitation found in Indira Sagar project whereas Omkareshwar project contained rehabilitation scheme - R and R policy was amended on dt.14-8-2000 including persons who would loose their lands under submergence or for canal construction and based on SC in Narmada Bachao Andolan v. Union of India & Ors, 2000 Indlaw SC 3658 considered similar issue but made distinction between 'canal affected persons' and 'oustees' - After this judgment appellant again amended R and R policy on dt.1-9-2003 and deleted 'which is required for project related constructions of canal or government project colony' - As such appellant would not give same R and R package to canal affected persons - Canal affected persons could not be treated at par with submergence affected persons - In order to give more benefit to canal affected persons SC had accepted appellants suggestion that date of s. 4 notification in all cases related to this would be treated as this judgment dt. 2-8-2011 and market value would be re-determined and pass supplementary awards as early as possible and oustees could file reference u/s. 18 of the 1894 Act - SC issued directions to LAO - By this act of appellant reliefs would be much more than reliefs granted by SC dt. 5-5-2010 which was later clarified/modified - SC clarified that further canal work would be subject to clearance/direction from MoEF - Appeals disposed of.
