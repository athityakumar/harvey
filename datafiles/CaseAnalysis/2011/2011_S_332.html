Humanity and others v State Of West Bengal And Others

Supreme Court of India

26 May 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Ganguly, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 375; (2011) 6 SCC 125; AIR 2011 SC 2308; 2011 (88) ALR 261; 2011 (4) AWC 4286; 2011 (4) CalLT 9; 2011 (3) CLT(SC) 403; JT 2011 (6) SC 334; 2011 (5) RCR(Civil) 439; 2011(6) SCALE 378; [2011] 8 S.C.R. 653

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Constitution; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Administrative - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Allotment of land without advertisement - Legality - Respondent State issued an advertisement earmarking a plot for setting up of an integrated school - 2nd respondent Society (allottee) applied for allotment and Selection Committee selected 2nd respondent allottee - Allottee subsequently wrote a letter to 1st respondent stating that they would like to surrender the allotment and they required a bigger plot for getting affiliation from Council for Indian School Certificate Examination (ICSE) - 1st respondent allotted a bigger tract of land to allottee - Appellant association challenged the allotment before the HC - HC, by impugned order, upheld the allotment - Appellant contended that there was no advertisement for allotment of the subsequent plot, which was much bigger than the initial plot and allotment of that different and bigger plot, without any advertisement by the 1st respondent, only on the prayer of the allottee was arbitrary, discriminatory and violative of art. 14 of the Constitution - Whether the impugned order of the 1st respondent allotting bigger plot in a prime area without issuing any advertisement was in violation of art. 14 of the Constitution - Held, no advertisement was issued and no offer was sought to be obtained from the members of the public in respect of the new allotment of a much bigger plot - Subsequent allotment was not in exercise of the right of the Government in the first advertisement, where the Government reserved its right to change the location of the land - Second allotment was not only about a change in the location of the land, but the subsequent allotment was also of a much larger plot of land, brought about in terms of the request of the allottee for a bigger plot - Government was so anxious to oblige the allottee by giving bigger plot that too with no loss of time, the said allotment was made by the Government admittedly without verifying whether the allottee had surrendered the previous plot allotted to him - Government made allotment of the new plot to the allottee on terms which were even more generous than the ones suggested by the allottee in his letter - Such action of the Government was arbitrary and fell foul of art.14 of the Constitution - Further, constitution of the Society/Trust/Company running the school should be such that it should not vest control in a single individual or members of the same family as per the norms of ICSE - However, allottee Society which had been registered for running the proposed school under the name of 'Ganguly Education and Welfare Society' consisted of five members of a family residing in the same address - Hence, constitution of such allottee Society to run the school was clearly against the ICSE norms - HC fell into an error by holding that by allotting bigger plot without open advertisement and public offer the Government action was not illegal or arbitrary - Further, HC, in the impugned judgment, recorded a finding that the allottee was informed by ICSE that for obtaining affiliation for integrated educational institution, land should not be less than 60 kathas - However, letter of the allottee to 1st respondent State did not even whisper that he was informed of any objection by ICSE - Said letter stated that after going through the norms of ICSE, it was the allottee's own understanding that a plot of more than 60 kathas was necessary to take the school project forward - Hence, HC's recording of fact, that the allottee was 'informed' by the ICSE of any objection, was not substantiated by any material on record, that was a grave error on the part of the HC - Impugned allotment of a different and bigger plot by the Government in favour of the allottee without any advertisement, when initially advertisement was resorted to, and then it was given up and everything was rushed through in haste, was unreasonable and arbitrary, and the HC was wrong in upholding the same - Impugned HC order was set aside - Appeal allowed.
