Ivo Agnelo Santimano Fernandes & Ors v Government Of Goa & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

23 February 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Ganguly, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 355; (2011) 11 SCC 506; 2011 (87) ALR 483; 2011 (4) AWC 3983; 2011 (2) CLT(SC) 319; 2011 (5) MLJ(SC) 708; 2011 (113) RD 865; 2011(5) SCALE 252; [2011] 2 S.C.R. 1142

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, s. 4 - Land acquisition proceedings - Determination of compensation - Notification u/s. 4 of the Act was issued for acquisition of land for the construction of a sports complex - Land Acquisition Collector (LAC) awarded compensation at Rs.45 per sq. Meter - Aggrieved, landowners-appellants 1 to 3 and 'A' filed reference petitions against the order of the LAC u/s. 18 of the Act - District Judge passed an award wherein the rate of Rs.45 per sq. meter given by the LAC was upheld - Accordingly, pursuant to the award, the respondents prepared cheques in favour of the 'A' and appellant 1 - As the cheques were not collected by the claimants, respondents deposited the uncollected cheques in their Revenue Deposit by way of challan and utilized the same - Appellants filed an execution application for the recovery of the balance amount along with interest accrued thereon - District Judge directed that the amount of Rs.2,06,436 each for which the cheques had been drawn, be paid to the appellants 1 and 3, leaving the question of interest to be determined subsequently - Thus, fresh cheques for an amount of Rs.60,000 were issued by the respondents and deposited in court - District Judge held that there was a dispute as to apportionment of compensation, and in light of the judgment in the case of Prem Nath Kapur & Anr. v. National Fertilizers Corporation of India Ltd. & Ors., 1995 INDLAW SC 1987, held that the liability of the respondents to pay interest subsisted till the respondents had not deposited the amount in the court - Since, the respondents had deposited the amount of compensation in their Revenue account and had utilized the same instead of depositing it in Court, the respondents were liable to pay interest at 15% p.a. on compensation - Aggrieved by that order of the District Judge passed in the execution proceeding, the respondents preferred a revision before the HC - HC held that the amount was duly paid to the appellants but they did not come to collect the same - Therefore, judgment of the District Court could not be sustained and was set aside by way of impugned judgment of the High Court - Hence, instant appeal - Held, Act required that the interest be deposited in court, and the same was upheld in the case of Prem Nath Kapur - In the instant case, the respondents did not deposit the amount in court, but in their Revenue account and utilized the same - Even if the respondent State did pay the compensation to the claimants directly, and the same was not collected, the respondent State could not then keep the said money with itself and utilize it - In such cases, after a reasonable period, if the claimants did not come forward to collect compensation, then it should be deposited in court by the State - Allowing the State to keep the compensation with itself and utilizing it could not possibly be permitted being contrary to the provisions of the Act and the law laid down in Prem Nath Kapur - Hence, the judgment of the HC was erroneous and deserves to be set aside - Appeal allowed.
