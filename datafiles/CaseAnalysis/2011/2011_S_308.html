Dev Sharan and another v State Of U.P. & Ors

Supreme Court of India

07 March 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Ganguly, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 149; (2011) 4 SCC 769; 2011(2) ALL MR 932; 2011 (2) AWC 1697; 2011 (2) CalLT 83; 2011 (1) CLT(SC) 272; JT 2011 (3) SC 102; 2011 (3) RCR(Civil) 587; 2011(3) SCALE 369; [2011] 3 S.C.R. 728

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Land Acquisition (Amendment) Act, 1923

Summary: Land & Property - Administrative - Practice & Procedure - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4(1), 5A, 6, 17 - Land acquisition - Dispensation of enquiry u/s. 5A - Legality - Notification u/ss. 4(1) and 17 of Act were issued for acquisition of appellants' land for construction of jails wherein enquiry u/s. 5A of Act was dispensed with in view of pressing urgency of matter - Appellants challenged acquisition before HC, however, HC did not interfere with - Hence instant appeal - Whether or not State Government was justified in dispensing with inquiry, by invoking s. 17 (4) of Act, which was mandated to be conducted u/s. 5A of Act, especially when one year elapsed between notifications u/s. 4 and one u/s. 6 of Act - Held, concept of public purpose should also be read into provisions of emergency power u/s. 17 with consequential dispensation of right of hearing u/s. 5A of Act - If public purpose should be satisfied by not rendering common man homeless and by exploring other avenues of acquisition, Courts, before sanctioning an acquisition, should in exercise of its power of judicial review, focus its attention on concept of social and economic justice - Time which elapsed between publication of s. 4(1) and s. 17 notifications, and s. 6 declaration, in local newspapers was of 11 months and 23 days, i.e. almost one year - Such slow pace at which government machinery had functioned in processing acquisition, clearly evinced that there was no urgency for acquiring land so as to warrant invoking s. 17 (4) of Act - Construction of jail was certainly in public interest and for such construction land should be acquired, however, such acquisition should be made only by strictly following mandate of Act - In facts of instant case, such acquisition could not be made by invoking emergency provisions of s. 17 - Respondent State, therefore, was not justified in invoking emergency provision of s. 17(4) of Act and hence impugned notifications u/ss. 4 and 6 of Act in so far as they related to appellants' land were quashed - Appeal allowed.
