Lram Narayan Tiwari v Union Of India & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

21 February 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Mukundakam Sharma, Anil R. Dave

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 117; (2011) 4 SCC 684; 2011 (129) FLR 138; JT 2011 (2) SC 576; 2011(2) SCALE 639; [2011] 2 S.C.R. 1104; 2011 (2) SLR 304

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces; Service

Keywords: Air Force Act, 1950, Punishments

Summary: Service - Defence & Security Forces - Air Force Act, 1950, ss. 73, 157 - Dismissal from service - Commutation - Extent of - Appellant Corporal in the Indian Air Force was charge-sheeted for misconduct - Appellant was tried in the District Court Martial and found that the charges against him were proved - District Court Martial awarded the punishment of three months detention and reduction in rank - Findings of the District Court Martial were confirmed by the Confirming Authority, however, punishment of detention was commuted to dismissal from service - Appellant challenged the order of dismissal before the HC - Single Judge dismissed appellant's writ petition, which was confirmed by the DB - Hence the instant appeal - Appellant contended that the punishment of dismissal from the service was more severe and harsher than serving three months' detention and, therefore, the order passed by the Confirming Authority altering the punishment given by the District Court Martial was not permissible u/s. 157 of the Act - Whether the alteration of punishment from three months' detention to dismissal from service by the Confirming Authority was justifiable in the instant case - Held, according to the scale of punishment u/s. 73 of the Act, the most severe punishment was the punishment of death and, therefore, the same had been put on the top followed by imprisonment, detention, cashiering, dismissal from service and then other lesser punishments - Confirming Authority had commuted the punishment of three months detention and imposed the punishment of dismissal and, since the punishment itself was of dismissal from service there was no question of his reduction to the ranks at all - Further, charges against the appellant was serious and an offence of such nature the authority was justified in awarding him the punishment of dismissal from service - Scale of punishment provided in s. 73 of the Act clearly confirmed the position that dismissal from service was a lesser punishment than that of detention in prison - By commuting the punishment of three months detention and imposing the punishment of dismissal, the Confirming Authority had strictly followed the scale of punishment provided for in s. 73 of the Act and, therefore, there was no justification for any interference with the nature of punishment awarded to the appellant - Appeal dismissed.
