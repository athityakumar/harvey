Hari Ram v Jyoti Prasad & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

27 January 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Mukundakam Sharma, A.R. Dave

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 58; (2011) 2 SCC 682; AIR 2011 SC 952; 2011 (3) ALD(SC) 101; 2011(2) ALL MR 457; 2011 (85) ALR 480; 2011 (2) AWC 2006; 2011 (3) CTC 769; 2011 (1) JhrCR(SC) 191; JT 2011 (3) SC 173; 2011 (164) PLR 641; 2011 (2) RCR(Civil) 515; 2011 (113) RD 333; 2011(1) SCALE 749; [2011] 1 S.C.R. 1076

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 1 r. 8 - Unauthorized construction over public street - Respondents filed a suit submitting that earlier 'X' was the original owner of the entire area out of which he curved out a colony selling plots in favour of various parties - Respondents further submitted that at that time itself a 10 feet wide public street was left on the ground for the common use of all the plot holders of the colony but appellants from the time of possession of their plot have evil eye on the aforesaid disputed street and encroached upon substantial part of the same making the street narrowed down causing inconvenience to the users of the said street - Civil Judge decreed the suit and a permanent injunction was issued directing the removal of unauthorized construction from the ground - Appeal against said order was dismissed by Additional District Judge - HC also dismissed appeal filed against said order on the ground that there is no specific question of law involved in the aforesaid appeal - Hence, the present appeal - (A) Whether the appellants have made illegal/unauthorized construction over the public street by way of illegal encroachment? - Held, on appreciation of the evidence, all the three courts namely the HC, the First Appellate Court as also the trial court held that the aforesaid disputed suit land is a part of the public street where the appellant has encroached upon by constructing a part of the house - The aforesaid findings are therefore findings of fact - Public Officer namely Patwari was examined who had proved the report submitted by the BDO stating that part of the suit property is a public street - Hence, it is proved that the appellants have encroached upon the suit property consisting of the aforesaid street of 10 feet width - No infirmity in the order of the HC - (B) Whether suit was bad for non-compliance of the provisions of O. 1 r. 8 of the CPC? - Held, the suit was filed by an aggrieved person whose right to use public street of 10 feet width was prejudicially affected - Since affected person himself has filed a suit, therefore, the suit cannot be dismissed on the ground of alleged non-compliance of the provisions of O. 1 r. 8 of the CPC - Appeal dismissed.
