Itc Ltd. v State Of Uttar Pradesh & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

05 July 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, B. SUDARSHAN REDDY

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 429; (2011) 7 SCC 493; 2011 (5) AWC 5208; 2011 (3) CLT(SC) 89; JT 2011 (7) SC 602; 2011 (7) MLJ(SC) 170; 2011(7) SCALE 183; [2011] 7 S.C.R. 66

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Yearly, Uttar Pradesh, Industrial Area Development Act, 1976, Uttar Pradesh President's Acts (Re-enactment with Modifications) Act, 1974, NOIDA (Preparation and Finalisation of Plan) Regulations, 1991, NOIDA Building Regulations and Directions, 1986, NOIDA Building Regulations and Directions, 2006

Summary: Land & Property - Uttar Pradesh President's Acts (Re-enactment with Modifications) Act, 1974 - Uttar Pradesh Urban Planning and Development Act, 1973,ss. 4, 30, 32, 40, 41, 41(3), 43, 44, 45, 46, 47, 49, 50, 51, 53, 58 - Uttar Pradesh, Industrial Area Development Act, 1976, ss. 6, 9(2),12, 14, 18, 19 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 4 - NOIDA (Preparation and Finalisation of Plan) Regulations, 1991 - NOIDA Building Regulations and Directions, 1986 - NOIDA Building Regulations and Directions, 2006, regn. 3.12(e) - Registration - Violation of Regulation - Maintainability - NOIDA board approved proposal for increasing number of plots and reducing their size along with eligibility criteria was published in newspapers - Evaluation of applications and recommendations for allotment were made by an independent Screening Committee and recommendations for allotments were approved by Board of Directors of NOIDA and lease deeds have been executed and which were presented for registration - Writ petitions were filed challenging allotment of hotel sites by NOIDA - NOIDA cancelled allotments - State Govt. filed affidavit before HC in writ petitions challenging allotments - HC dismissed petitions - Aggrieved by same appellants filed writ petitions before DB which were allowed - Hence, instant appeals - State Govt. passed revisional orders during pendency of appeals - Whether lease could be cancelled and appellants suffer from any irregularity or illegality - Held, lease governed exclusively by provisions of the 1882 Act, could be cancelled by filing a civil suit for declaring that it was illegal, null and void until a court of competent jurisdiction grants such a decree, lease would continue to be effective and binding - Where grant of lease was governed by a statute or statutory regulations, it would be permissible for an Authority, as lessor, to cancel a duly executed and registered lease deed, even if possession had been delivered, on specific grounds of cancellation provided in statute - Further, If State Govt. in exercise of its revisional jurisdiction finds allotments were irregular or contrary to regulations or policies of NOIDA and directs cancellation, allotments become invalid and leases also become invalid thus NOIDA could resume possession, without intervention of a Civil Court in a civil suit - Further, allotment of plots situated in commercial areas earmarked for commercial use, to hotels did not violate any provisions of the Act or the Regulations and it was not necessary for NOIDA to change land use of plots to be allotted to hotels, from commercial to industrial use - Therefore, in absence of material to show that State had acted mala fide or out of improper or corrupt motive or in order to promote private interest of someone at cost of State, decision to grant licences was not open to interference - Further, where State was allocating resources for purpose of encouraging setting up of industries within State, State was not bound to advertise and tell people that it wants a particular industry to be set up in State or invite those interested to come up with proposals - Therefore, allotment of commercial plots by NOIDA to appellants for setting up hotels was valid there was no violation of regulations or policies of NOIDA in allotting commercial plots for hotels - Therefore cancellation of allotment was unsustainable - However there was violation of regulations and policies of NOIDA in making such allotment on fixed rate basis, instead of inviting sealed tenders or holding public auction - Mistake was found out by State Govt. in exercise of revisional jurisdiction but by then allotment was followed by payment of premium, execution of lease deed, and delivery of possession - HC had rightly felt that cancellation was unwarranted and matter required reconsideration by State Govt. - Thus, HC was directed reconsideration in light of its observations that allotments of commercial plots for hotels were not in violation of any regulations and allottees were not guilty of any objectionable conduct - Thus, in these cases allotment of commercial plots to appellants was valid and legal - Violation was in making such allotment on fixed allotment rate which was less than rate plots would have fetched by calling for tenders or by holding auctions - Therefore, equitable solution in these cases was to give an opportunity to lessees to pay difference thereby in consideration which arose on account of wrong interpretation instead of cancelling leases - Therefore, violation of guidelines in regard to disposal of commercial plots had resulted only in a loss of revenue by way of premium and if this could be made up, there was no reason why leases should not be continued - Appeals disposed of.
