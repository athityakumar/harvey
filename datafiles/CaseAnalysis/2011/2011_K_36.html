 C.B.I. & Ors v Keshub Mahindra Etc. Etc.

Supreme Court of India

11 May 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, Altamas Kabir, R.V. Raveendran, B. SUDARSHAN REDDY, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 331; (2011) 6 SCC 216; AIR 2011 SC 2037; 2011 ALL MR (Cri) 2337; 2011 (86) ALR 898; JT 2011 (5) SC 490; 2011 (2) KLT 740; 2011 (2) MLJ(Crl) 472; 2011 (2) RCR(Criminal) 906; 2011(5) SCALE 591; [2011] 6 S.C.R. 384

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Constitution - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 304, 324, 326, 429 r/w. s. 35 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 137 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 209, 228, 240 and 323 - Curative petition - Enhancement of sentences - On 2.12.1984, there was a massive escape of lethal gas from the MIC storage tank at Bhopal plant of the Union Carbide (I) Ltd. (UCIL) into the atmosphere causing the death of 5,295 people leaving 5,68,292 people suffering from different kinds of injuries - CBI filed charge sheets against UCIL and other accused alleging commission of offence u/ss. 304, 324, 326, 429 r/w. s. 35 of IPC - Additional Sessions Judge passed an order framing charges against the accused nos. 5 to 9 u/s. 304 (Part II), 324, 326 and 429 of IPC and against accused nos. 2, 3, 4 and 12 u/s. 304 (Part II) r/w. s. 35 of IPC - Accused challenged said order before HC who dismissed said appeal - On appeal, SC in Keshub Mahindra vs. State of Madhya Pradesh 1996 INDLAW SC 2410, quashed the charges framed by the Sessions Court on the ground that no charges could have been framed against the accused u/s. 304 (Part II) or u/ss. 324, 326, 429 of IPC with or without the aid of s. 35 of IPC and directed that on the material led by the prosecution the charge u/s. 304A IPC could be made out against accused nos. 5, 6, 7, 8 and 9 and u/s. 304A r/w. 35 of IPC against accused nos. 2, 3, 4 and 12 - CBI did not question said SC judgment or filed any review petition u/art. 137 of the Constitution - Subsequently, after 14 years, CBI filed curative petitions for recalling the said SC judgment on the ground that when SC quashed the charges framed against accused nos. 2 to 5, 7 to 9 and 12 u/ss. 304 (Part II), 324, 326 and 429 IPC and directed the trial court to frame charges u/s. 304A IPC, SC had before it adequate material to make out prima facie, an offence chargeable u/s. 304 (Part II) of IPC - CBI also submitted that SC vide earlier judgment barred Magistrate from exercising his judicial power u/s. 323 of the Code, even though the Code vested the jurisdiction in him to alter the charge or commit the case to the Court of Sessions as the case may be, on the basis of evidence that came on record during the trial - Whether curative petition filed by the CBI after 14 years of judgment could be allowed? - Held, in the earlier judgment, SC make it absolutely clear that its findings were based on materials gathered in investigation and brought before the Court till that stage - At every place in the judgment where the Court records the finding or makes an observation in regard to the appropriate charge against the accused, it qualifies the finding or the observation by saying 'on the materials produced by the prosecution for framing charge' - Earlier judgment was rendered at the stage of ss. 209/228/240 of the Code - Hence, it was wrong to assume that the earlier judgment was a fetter against the proper exercise of powers by a court of competent jurisdiction under the relevant provisions of the Code - Moreover, if the Magistrate failed to appreciate the correct legal position and misread the earlier SC decision as tying his hands from exercising the power u/s. 323 or u/s. 216 of the Code, it can certainly be corrected by the appellate/revisional court - Petitions dismissed.
