Booz Allen Andhamilton Inc v Sbi Home Finance Ltd. & Ors

Supreme Court of India

15 April 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, J.M. Panchal

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 283; (2011) 5 SCC 532; AIR 2011 SC 2507; 2011 (4) BC 216; 2012 (3) Bom.C.R. 865; 2011 (3) CLT(SC) 388; [2012] 173 Comp Cas 184; 2013 (5) CompLJ 21; JT 2011 (5) SC 198; 2011 (5) MLJ(SC) 373; 2011 (3) MPLJ 625; 2011 (5) RCR(Civil) 168B; 2011(5) SCALE 147; [2011] 7 S.C.R. 310

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, s. 8 - Scope of s. 8 of the Act - Capstone Investment Co. Pvt. Ltd. (second respondent) and Real Value Appliances Pvt. Ltd. (third respondent) had borrowed loans from SBI Home Finance Ltd., (first respondent) under two loan agreements by securing the two flats in favour of SBI - Under two leave and licence agreements, Capstone and RV Appliances permitted the appellant to use their respective flats - Also a tripartite deposit agreement was entered among RV Appliances and Capstone as the first party, appellant as the second party and SBI as the third party - Under the said agreement, the appellant paid a refundable security deposit of Rs.6.5 crores to Capstone and RV Appliances - As the loan amount due by RV Appliances was not repaid, SBI filed a mortgage suit - Appellant filed application u/s. 8 of the Act praying that the parties to the suit be referred to arbitration as provided in deposit agreement - Application was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - (A) Whether the subject matter of the suit fell within the scope of the arbitration agreement contained in clause 16 of the deposit agreement? - Held, all the parties to the suit were parties to an agreement which contained the provision for settlement of disputes by arbitration - Suit was filed by SBI to enforce the mortgage to recover the amounts due to it - In that context, SBI also sought delivery of vacant possession - Enforcement of the charge/mortgage over the flat, realisation of sale proceeds therefrom and the right of the appellant to stay in possession till the entire deposit was repaid, were all matters which were specifically mentioned in clause 16 as matters to be settled by arbitration - Therefore, the subject matter of the suit fall within the scope of the arbitration agreement - (B) Whether the application u/s. 8 of the Act was liable to be rejected as it was filed nearly 20 months after entering appearance in the suit? - Held, though s. 8 of the Act did not prescribe any time limit for filing an application under that section, and only stated that the application u/s. 8 of the Act should be filed before submission of the first statement on the substance of the dispute, the scheme of the Act and the provisions of the section clearly indicate that the application thereunder should be made at the earliest - In the facts of the instant case HC was not justified in rejecting the application on the ground of delay - (C) Whether the subject matter of the suit was 'arbitrable', that is capable of being adjudicated by a private forum (arbitral tribunal)? - Held, a decree for sale of a mortgaged property as in the case of a decree for order of winding up, required the court to protect the interests of persons other than the parties to the suit/petition and empowers the court to entertain and adjudicate upon rights and liabilities of third parties (other than those who are parties to the arbitration agreement) - Therefore, a suit for sale, foreclosure or redemption of a mortgaged property, should only be tried by a public forum, and not by an arbitral tribunal - Court where the mortgage suit was pending, should not refer the parties to arbitration - Hence, suit being one for enforcement of a mortgage by sale, it should be tried by the court and not by an arbitral tribunal - Appeal dismissed.
