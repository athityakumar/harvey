Ramesh Rout v Rabindra Nath Rout

Supreme Court of India

09 December 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, Jagdish Singh Khehar

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 809; (2012) 1 SCC 762; AIR 2012 SC 329; 2011 (4) CLT(SC) 526; 2012 (1) OLR 130; 2012 (1) RCR(Civil) 551; [2011] 16 S.C.R. 254

Case Digest

Subject: Election; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Dismissed, Authorisation, Political Parties, Substantial Character, Improper Rejection

Summary: Election - Practice & Procedure - Representation of the People Act, 1951 - Conduct of Elections Rules, 1961 - Election Symbols (Reservation and Allotment) Order, 1968 - Nominations - Rejection - Sustainability - Respondent filed nomination for General Assembly elections, as a candidate of registered political party - Returning Officer (RO) rejected nomination papers of proposed candidate (including appellant) on the ground that Form A and B filed by proposed candidate along with his first set of nomination paper were not duly signed in ink by authorized officer of registered political party - Appellant contested election as independent candidate and was elected to Assembly - Challenging election of appellant, two petitions were filed before HC - HC held that election of appellant was null and void and directed Election Commission (EC) for fresh election in constituency - Hence, instant appeals - Appellant contended that filing of original Forms A and B duly signed in ink by an authorised person of such political party was non-negotiable and non-filing of original Forms A and B signed in ink constitutes a defect of substantial nature - Respondent contended that failure to file original Form A and B signed in ink was not defect of a substantial character within the meaning of s. 36(4) of 1951 Act - (A) Whether it was mandatory for a candidate set up by a recognised political party, to file original ink signed Forms A and B appended to Para 13 of 1968 Order - Held, it was necessary for proposed candidate that Forms A and B referable to cls. (b), (c), (d) of Para 13 of 1968 Order were submitted to RO duly signed in ink by authorised person of recognised political party not later than given time and date - RO stated in his deposition that nomination papers filed by proposed candidate were examined by him only from technical stand point and it was not his duty to examine the correctness or validity of documents at the time of filing of the same - A careful consideration of evidence of RO left no manner of doubt that he had not distorted the facts nor withheld anything from court with regard to presentation of nomination papers by candidates including proposed candidate - HC was not wrong or unjustified in finding return - Authorised representative of appellant did not enquire into matter or requested, when RO raised objection that original Forms A and B were missing - (B) Whether respondent filed original Form-A and B being duly signed in ink by authorised person with first set of his nomination - Held, HC concluded that respondent had filed original Form-A and B duly signed in ink by authorised person of registered political party with first set of his nomination and, accordingly, decided in favour of respondent - (RO) had admitted in his evidence that Nominations along with all accompanying documents of all 8 candidates were Xeroxed outside and all 4 sets of Nomination papers of respondent were Xeroxed to display - Possibility that, in process, original ink signed Form A and B were inadvertently exchanged for Xerox copies, thereof, cannot be ruled out - Under such premises, respondent had filed original Form-A and B duly signed in ink by authorised person with 1st set of his Nomination - Conclusion of HC was affirmed - RO erred in acting in hot haste in rejecting nomination paper of respondent and not postponing the scrutiny to next day, particularly, when a request was made by authorised representative of respondent - Respondent was successful in proving the improper rejection of respondent's nomination paper - There was no ground to interfere with impugned judgment of HC - Appeals dismissed.
