Ramesh Kumar and another v Furu Ram and another etc.

Supreme Court of India

18 August 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 588; (2011) 8 SCC 613; 2012 (1) ALD(SC) 53; 2011 (3) CLT(SC) 336; JT 2011 (9) SC 505; 2011 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 2997; 2011 (8) MLJ(SC) 1121; 2012 (2) RCR(Civil) 720; 2011(9) SCALE 16; [2011] 10 S.C.R. 453

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Arbitration & ADR - Registration Act, 1908, s. 49 - Arbitration Act, 1940, ss. 14, 17, 32, 33 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 114 - Immovable Property - Registration - Sale - Justifiability - Appellants entered into agreement with respondent for sale of land for which a earnest money was paid by respondents - Respondents were asked to pay balance of sale consideration on failure they requested appellant to refund earnest money - Panchayat resolved dispute between parties by deciding that appellants should permit respondents to cultivate their lands for a period of one and half years without any rent in satisfaction for earnest money - Respondents reduced said settlement into writing and asked appellants to appear in Court and confirm the samewhich was done by appellants - Respondents obtained mutation in their favour on terms of arbitration award - Appellants filed suits against agreements and mutations in pursuance of said decrees in Trial Court which were decreed in favour of appellants - Respondents filed appeal in Appellate Court which was allowed - Aggrieved, by same appeal was made before HC which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether arbitration award was invalid for want of registration and orders passed by Courts below were justified - Held, awards were clearly documents which purport or operate to create and declare a right, title or interest in an immoveable property of value of more than Rs.100 which was not subject of dispute or reference to arbitration therefore awards were compulsorily registrable - Further, conditions for valid arbitration were not satisfied - However, arbitration award was only a ruse to avoid payment and registration then Court considered entire proceedings a sham - Thus, If all these facts which have a bearing on making of award and validity of award were suppressed before Court and Court was misled into making decrees in terms of awards proceedings were fraudulent and amounted to committing fraud on Court - Hence, decrees were invalid - Appeals allowed.
