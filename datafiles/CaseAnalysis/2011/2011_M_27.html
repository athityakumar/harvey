Union Of India v M/S Krafters Engineering & Leasing (P) Ltd.

Supreme Court of India

12 July 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 434; (2011) 7 SCC 279; AIR 2011 SC 2620; 2011 (87) ALR 902; 2011 (5) AWC 5102; 2011 (5) Bom.C.R. 509; 2011 (3) CLT(SC) 153; JT 2011 (7) SC 295; 2012 (1) MLJ(SC) 1191; 2011 (4) MPLJ 1; 2011 (4) RCR(Civil) 556; 2011(7) SCALE 405; [2011] 8 S.C.R. 196

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration Act, 1940 - Power of arbitrator to award interest - Respondent awarded a contract for the work of Provision of Signaling Arrangements by appellant - On completion of the contract, the respondent raised certain disputes/claims and demanded for adjudication through arbitration - HC directed the General Manager of the Central Railway to appoint an arbitrator and refer the disputes for adjudication - Award was passed - Challenging the award, appellant filed Arbitration Petition, which was dismissed - Appeal filed thereagainst was also dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - It was submitted that in view of the clause 1.15 in the contract, no interest was payable on the amount payable to the Contractor under the contract - Whether an arbitrator had jurisdiction to grant interest despite the agreement prohibiting the same? - Held, where the parties had agreed that no interest shall be payable, the arbitrator could not award interest for the amounts payable to the contractor under the contract - Hence, in view of the specific prohibition of contract contained in Clause 1.15, the arbitrator ceases to have the power to grant interest - Bar under clause 1.15 was absolute and interest could not be awarded without rewriting the contract - Award of the arbitrator granting interest in respect of the amount payable to the contractor under the contract as well as the order of the Single Judge and the Division Bench of the HC confirming the same set aside - Appeal allowed.
