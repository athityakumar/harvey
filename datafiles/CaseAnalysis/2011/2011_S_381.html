Narmada Bachao Andolan and others v State of Madhya Pradesh and another

Supreme Court of India

11 May 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, J.M. Panchal, Deepak Verma

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 332; (2011) 7 SCC 639; AIR 2011 SC 1989; JT 2011 (6) SC 379; 2011 (5) RCR(Civil) 397; 2011(5) SCALE 624; [2011] 6 S.C.R. 443

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Water

Keywords: Assistance, Punjab Security of Land Tenures Act, 1953, Inter-State Water Disputes Act, 1956, M.P. Land Revenue Code, Madhya Pradesh Pariyojana Ke Karan Visthapit Vyakti (Punahsthapan) Adhiniyam, 1985, Indian Railways Conference Association Rules

Summary: Water - Constitution - Environment, Wildlife & Animal - Land & Property - Electricity & Energy - Constitution of India, 1950 - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, s. 48 - Inter State Water Disputes Act, 1956, s. 2(c) - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 340 - Establishment of Omkareshwar Dam on Narmada river - Land acquisition - Challenged - Narmada Water Disputes Tribunal (NWDT) apportioned the water in the Narmada between Madhya Pradesh, Gujarat, Maharashtra and Rajasthan - The State of Madhya Pradesh approved the Omkareshwar Dam project for generating 520 mega watts of power, with an assessment that on the completion of the project, 30 villages would be submerged at the full reservoir level - The State of Madhya Pradesh framed a Rehabilitation and Resettlement policy in 1985 (R & R Policy) for the oustees of all the Narmada projects in the State - Construction of the said dam began in 2002 and stood completed in October, 2006 - However, the displaced persons were allegedly not offered the land under the R & R Policy as amended on 3.7.2003, rather compensation for their land was deposited in their accounts - Respondent No. 1, Narmada Bachao Andolan (NBA) filed PIL before HC who as an interim measure, had issued directions, inter-alia, for allotment of agricultural land to the displaced persons in lieu of the land acquired for construction of the said dam in terms of the R & R Policy as amended on 3.7.2003 and to treat a major son of the family whose land had been acquired as a separate family for the purpose of allotment of agricultural land - Hence, the instant appeals - (A) Allotment of land in lieu of land acquired - Held, in view of the provisions contained in R & R Policy, the State Authorities were under an obligation to allot the land to the oustees 'as far as possible' - In case an oustee had not accepted the compensation or had any grievance in respect of area/quality/location of land allotted or for any other entitlement, he may approach the Grievance Redressal Authority (GRA) and the GRA will adjudicate upon the issue and pass an appropriate order in individual cases - The person aggrieved by the order of GRA shall be entitled to approach the HC for appropriate relief - (B) Entitlement of major son to get the allotment of land as a separate family - Held, NWDT Award did not provide for allotment of agricultural land to the major sons of such oustees - The States of Gujarat and Maharashtra had given concessions/relief over and above the said Award - Thus, the Narmada Bachao Andolan-I had been decided with presumption that such a right had been conferred upon major sons by the NWDT Award and Narmada Bachao Andolan-II had been decided following the said judgment and interpreting the definition of 'family' contained in the R & R Policy - The view expressed earlier, inadvertently, on a wrong assumption may result in great public loss and would be against larger public interest - There was no prohibition under the law to locate the error and adopt a correct approach if the Court was convinced that the error exists and its avoidance was necessary to prevent any baneful effect on the general interest of the public or the State - Hence, the major son would not be entitled to anything additional as his separate share in the original holding - Direction given by the HC to allot agricultural land to major sons of the oustees in the impugned judgment was set aside - (C) Entitlement of land to the landless labourers - Held, as the landless labourers never had any land, they were not entitled to any compensation under the Act 1894, thus, the question of allotment of land to them would not arise - The R & R Policy itself provides that such persons were entitled to get Rs.49,300/- to buy productive employment creating assets etc., and such money can also be used for acquiring land - Such terms cannot be interpreted to mean that the landless labourers become entitled to allotment of land for agricultural purpose to the extent of 2 hectares - Hence, landless labourers were not entitled for land - (D) Applicability of NWDT award - Held, appellant submitted that under the provisions of Act 1956, a Tribunal was constituted and it had made the Award which provides for various reliefs to the oustees and all the benefits granted by the said Award to the oustees were applicable in case of the oustees of the Omkareshwar Dam Project - HC had rejected the said contention of the respondent on the ground that the Tribunal had been constituted to resolve the water dispute as defined u/s. 2(c) of the 1956 Act, for the reason that a dispute had arisen between various States - The matter was limited to resettlement and rehabilitation of 6147 oustee families spread over in 158 villages in the State of Madhya Pradesh as a consequence of Sardar Sarovar Project - Therefore, the HC rightly held that the Tribunal was considering only the resettlement of the aforesaid oustee families spread over 158 villages in the State of Madhya Pradesh and, therefore, the Tribunal was concerned only with those persons and it did not take in its ambit any other future plan or project - Hence, impugned award of NWDT did not give any benefit to the oustees of the Omkareshwar Dam - (E) Entitlement to land in lieu of submerged land - Held, HC decided the issue observing that as submerging of the 5 villages took place in view of the orders by the courts and the oustees had been paid compensation and SC had passed the order not to submerge the remaining 25 villages till the completion of rehabilitation took place, it was not proper for the HC to direct the respondents to restore the status quo ante for the 5 villages in issue - No person should suffer from an act of the Court and to ensure that the oustees of the 5 villages which have already been submerged under the orders of the Courts, do not face hostile discrimination at the hands of the authorities; they shall be entitled to the relief to which the other oustees were entitled - (F) Withdrawal of acquisition proceedings - Held, the State was entitled to abandon the land acquisition proceedings in exercise of its power u/s. 48 of the Act 1894 - However, it shall not apply to 167 dwelling units on the said land - Such persons whose dwelling units were acquired shall be entitled for the benefit of R & R Policy to the extent provided therein - Appeals disposed of.
