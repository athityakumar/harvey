The Municipal Corporation Of Greater Bombay & Anr. v Yeshwant Jagannath Vaity & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

17 March 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

V.S. Sirpurkar, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 253; (2011) 11 SCC 88; AIR 2011 SC 1916; 2011 (4) Bom.C.R. 431; JT 2011 (3) SC 603; 2011 (5) RCR(Civil) 375; [2011] 5 S.C.R. 653

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Bombay Municipal Corporations Act, 1888, Development Control Regulation for Greater Bombay, 1991

Summary: Land & Property - Municipalities & Local Governments - Development Control Regulation for Greater Bombay, 1991, regn. 3(7) - Development rights - Entitlement - Respondents' land was acquired by appellant/Municipal Corporation whereby appellant agreed to allow respondents for developing acquired land - Appellant further agreed that they would also allow the respondents to develop the additionally acquired land by issuing necessary Development Rights Certificate (DRC) and by granting Transfer Development Rights (TDR) - Respondents developed acquired land and asphalted courtyard for constructing a proposed export office and handed over the possession to appellant - Respondents sought issuance of DRC for TDR in respect of additional land, which was declined by appellant - Respondents filed writ petition, which was allowed by HC holding that respondents were entitled to obtain issuance of DRC for TDR - Appellant contended that asphalting courtyard could not be said to be an 'amenity' for proposed export office building, therefore, respondents were not entitled to get DRC for TDR in respect of additional land - Whether respondents were entitled to obtain DRC for TDR in respect of additionally acquired land since they had asphalted courtyard - Held, stance on the part of appellant to provide 15% of additional TDR for asphalting courtyard would contain an admission that asphalting of courtyard would amount to an amenity - Had it not been so, appellant could have conveniently said that it would not provide even 1% of additional TDR to respondents - Further, definition of 'amenity' u/regn. 3(7) of Regulations included open spaces, parks, recreational grounds, play grounds etc., therefore, asphalting courtyard would certainly amount to an amenity - Building offered to be constructed by respondents was an export office wherein overall situation prevailing in area, asphalting of whole courtyard and thus providing parking lot would certainly amount to an amenity - After all, office, by its very nature, would attract trucks and other vehicles and in absence of an asphalted large area, office could not be a feasible idea - Respondents, therefore, entitled to obtain DRC for TDR for additionally acquire land - Appeal dismissed.
