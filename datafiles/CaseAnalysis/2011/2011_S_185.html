State Of Haryana And Others v M/S. Malik Traders

Supreme Court of India

17 August 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Cyriac Joseph, V.S. Sirpurkar

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 502; (2011) 13 SCC 200; 2011(5) ALL MR 947; 2011 (5) AWC 5378; JT 2011 (11) SC 203; 2011 (4) RCR(Civil) 873; 2012 (1) RLW 284; 2011(9) SCALE 1; [2011] 10 S.C.R. 372

Case Digest

Subject: Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Letter Of Acceptance

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Indian Contract Act, 1872, s. 5 - Failure to pay bid security in time - Forfeiture of earnest money - Refund - Entitlement - Appellant State invited tenders for appointment of entrepreneur/agent for collection of toll at toll bridge - Respondent submitted bid which was the second highest bid - Since the highest bidder failed to deposit the bid security amount within 90 days as per the tender condition, appellant cancelled the bid in favour of highest bidder and issued letter of acceptance to respondent - However, respondent also failed to deposit the security amount - Appellant cancelled the letter of acceptance issued to the respondent and forfeited their earnest money - Respondent challenged the cancellation of bid and forfeiture of its earnest money by appellant before the HC - Appellant contended that before receipt of the letter of acceptance, the respondent had sent a letter informing the appellant that the respondent was not interested in the work and, therefore, the amount of bid security should be refunded - HC allowed respondent's writ petition directing the appellant to refund the earnest money to respondent - Whether the respondent was entitled to the refund of earnest money that they had deposited with the appellant - Held, in paragraph 8 of the written offer/bid, the respondent had agreed to keep the bid open for acceptance up to 90 days after the last date of receipt of bid - Respondent had also agreed that it should be bound by the communication of acceptance of the bid dispatched within the period of 90 days - Hence, the respondent could not have withdrawn the bid before the expiry of the period of 90 days - It was not disputed that the acceptance of the respondent's bid was communicated to the respondent within the said period of 90 days - Therefore, the respondent was bound by the said acceptance of the bid, despite its withdrawal by the respondent in the meanwhile - In paragraph 10 of the offer/bid, the respondent had also agreed that the full value of the bid security would be forfeited without prejudice to any other right or remedy available to the (appellant) Executive Engineer or his successor in office or his representative, should the respondent withdraw or modify its offer/bid during the period of bid validity (90 days) or extended validity period - Since the respondent withdrew its offer during the period of bid validity in violation of the agreement in paragraph 8 of the offer/bid, the full value of bid security was liable to be forfeited in terms of the agreement contained in paragraph 10 of the offer/bid - Thus, even though u/s. 5 of the Act a proposal should be revoked at any time before the communication of its acceptance was complete as against the proposer, the respondent was bound by the agreement contained in its offer/bid to keep the bid open for acceptance up to 90 days after the last date of receipt of bid and if the respondent withdrew its bid before the expiry of the said period of 90 days the respondent was liable to suffer the consequence (i.e. forfeiture of the full value of bid security) as agreed to by the respondent in paragraph 10 of the offer/bid - Under the cover of the provisions contained in s. 5 of the Act, the respondent could not escape from the obligations and liabilities under the agreements contained in its offer/bid - Right to withdraw an offer before its acceptance could not nullify the agreement to suffer any penalty for the withdrawal of the offer against the terms of agreement - A person may had a right to withdraw his offer, but if he had made his offer on a condition that the bid security amount should be forfeited in case he withdrew the offer during the period of bid validity, he had no right to claim that the bid security should not be forfeited and it should be returned to him - Forfeiture of such bid security amount did not, in any way, affect any statutory right u/s. 5 of the Act - Bid security was given by the respondent and taken by the appellants to ensure that the offer was not withdrawn during the bid validity period of 90 days and a contract came into existence - Such conditions were included to ensure that only genuine parties made the bids - In the absence of such conditions, persons who did not have the capacity or had no intention of entering into the contract would make bids - Purpose of such a condition in the offer/bid would be defeated, if forfeiture was not permitted when the offer was withdrawn in violation of the agreement - Hence, the HC was not justified in quashing the letter accepting the bid of the respondent and the forfeiting the bid security amount - Impugned HC order was set aside - Appeal allowed.
