Jawahar Singh v Bala Jain & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

09 May 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 - Liability to pay compensation - Contributory negligence - Deceased died in a motor vehicular accident - Claim petition was filed by the widow, two daughters and son of the deceased before the Motor Accident Claims Tribunal - Tribunal awarded a sum of 8,35,067 in favour of the claimants together with interest at 7% - Insurer was held liable to satisfy the Award and to recover the amount from the petitioner (owner of the vehicle) - Said Award was challenged before the HC, which disposed of the same, by upholding the judgment of the Motor Accident Claims Tribunal - Instant Special Leave Petition - Held, motorcycle belonging to the Petitioner was being driven by 'J', who had no licence to drive the same and was a minor on the date of the accident - Responsibility in causing the accident was found to be solely that of 'J' - However, since 'J' was a minor and it was the responsibility of the Petitioner to ensure that his motorcycle was not misused and that too by a minor who had no licence to drive the same, the Motor Accident Claims Tribunal rightly saddled the liability for payment of compensation on the Petitioner and, accordingly, directed the Insurance Company to pay the awarded amount to the awardees and, thereafter, to recover the same from the Petitioner - Special Leave Petition dismissed.
