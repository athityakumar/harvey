Durbal v State Of U.P

Supreme Court of India

25 January 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

B. Sudarshan Reddy, Surinder Singh Nijjar

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 64; (2011) 2 SCC 676; AIR 2011 SC 795; 2011 CRLJ 1106; 2011 (1) JCC 475; 2011 (2) RCR(Criminal) 124; 2011(1) SCALE 730; [2011] 1 S.C.R. 1062

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Supreme Court (Enlargement of Criminal Appellate Jurisdiction) Act, 1970

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant/2nd accused allegedly murdered the deceased - Trial Court acquitted the appellant - HC, however, in appeal, reversed the order of acquittal and convicted the appellant u/s. 302 IPC - Hence the present appeal - Whether the HC committed any error in relying upon evidence of PW1 (eye-witness) since the whole prosecution case rests upon his evidence - Held, PW1 was the partner of the deceased in his business and appellant had enmity only with PW1, however, there did not have any reason as to why the appellant should attack the deceased and leave the PW1 unscratched - PW1 was allegedly present at the scene of occurrence along with two other eye-witnesses, however, there was no explanation forthcoming as to why three of them put together could not resist the appellant in attacking the deceased - Lantern and torch lights were allegedly used by the eye-witnesses to identify the appellant, however, such lantern and torch lights, seized during the investigation, were not produced before the Court - Further, PW1 refused to state as to whether the assailants were covering their faces with mask, hence his evidence did not inspire any confidence - Trial Court rightly gave the benefit of doubt to the appellant - View taken by the Trial Court was plausible which could not be held to be a perverse one and such a view was taken after proper appreciation of the evidence - HC ought not to have interfered with the judgment of the Trial Court merely because there was a possibility of taking a different view other than the one taken by the Trial Court - Therefore, appellant is entitled to the benefit of doubt and hence, impugned HC order is set aside and Trial Court order is restored - Appeal allowed.
