National Council For Teacher Education And Others v Shri Shyam Shiksha Prashikshan Sansthan And Others Etc. Etc.

Supreme Court of India

31 January 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 73; (2011) 3 SCC 238; AIR 2011 SC 932; 2011(2) ALL MR 433; 2011 (5) Bom.C.R. 877; 2011 (3) MLJ(SC) 961; 2011 (1) RLW 665; 2011(2) SCALE 59; [2011] 2 S.C.R. 291; 2011 (2) SLR 81; 2011 (1) UPLBEC 320

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Education

Keywords: National Council for Teacher Education (Recognition, Norms and Procedure) Regulations, 2005, Special Courts Bill, 1978, National Council for Teacher Education (Form of application for recognition, the time limit of submission of application, determination of norms and standards for recognition of teacher education programmes and permission to start new course or training) Regulations, 2002, National Council for Teacher Education (Recognition, Norms and Procedure) Regulations, 2009, National Council for Teacher Education (Application for recognition, the manner for submission, determination of conditions for recognition of institutions and permissions to start new course or training) Regulations, 1995

Summary: Education - Constitution - National Council for Teacher Education (Recognition, Norms and Procedure) Regulations, 2007, regn. 5 - National Council for Teacher Education Act, 1993, s. 14 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Education course - Recognition - Grant of - Respondent/Educational Institutions submitted their applications for grant of recognition for starting B.Ed. course for the academic year 2008-2009 and also applied to the State Government for grant of No Objection Certificates (NOC) - Appellant/Council, in the meantime, amended the cut off dates for submitting applications specified in cls. (4) and (5) of regn. 5 of the 2007 Regulations by vide Notification dated 1-7-2008 - Appellant/Council inspected the premises of respondents and laid the inspection report before the Council meeting of Regional Committee for consideration, however, recognition was not granted to respondents apparently on the ground that the cut off date specified in the regulations was already over - Respondents filed writ petition before the HC - Respondents contended that similarly situated institutions had been granted recognition in the same Council meeting, however, respondents' applications were rejected, therefore, respondents' right to equality guaranteed u/art. 14 of the Constitution had been violated - HC allowed writ petitions holding that large number of similarly situated institutions were granted recognition despite the fact that their cases were considered in the meeting - HC further held that cut off date fixed vide notification dated 1-7-2008 was discriminatory, arbitrary and violative of art. 14 of the Constitution - Whether the cut off dates specified in cls. (4) and (5) of regn. 5 of the Regulations as amended by Notification dated 1-7-2008 for submission of application for recognition and disposal thereof are mandatory - Held, entire exercise of grant of recognition was time consuming and therefore, some date had to be fixed for submission of application and some time schedule had to be prescribed for taking final decision on the issue of recognition - For academic session 2008-2009, the cut off date was amended because the 2007 Regulations were notified on 27-12-2007 and going by the cut off dates specified in cls. (4) and (5) of regn. 5, no application could have been entertained and no institution could have been recognized for B.Ed. course - Hence, cut off dates specified in the two clauses of regn. 5 of the 2007 Regulations and notification dated 1-7-2008 were neither arbitrary nor irrational so as to warrant a conclusion that the same were violative of art.14 of the Constitution - Conclusion of the HC that cut-off date fixed vide notification dated 1-7-2008 was discriminatory and violative of art. 14 appears to have been influenced by the fact that some of the applicants, whose applications were considered in the meeting of the Regional Committee held after the cut off date were granted recognition while others like the writ petitioners were denied similar treatment on the pretext that decision in their case could not be taken before the cut off date - Further, Regional Committee could refuse to grant recognition based on the recommendation of State Government/Union Territory Administration on valid grounds and such refusal cannot be said that ulta vires of the Act or the Rules - Therefore, the cut off dates specified in cls. (4) and (5) of regn. 5 of the 2007 Regulations as also the amendment made in regn. 5(5) vide notification dated 1-7-2008 are not violative of art. 14 of the Constitution and HC was not right in recording a contrary finding qua the date specified in notification dated 1-7-2008 - Further, provisions contained in s.14 and the Regulations framed for grant of recognition including the requirement of recommendation of the State Government/Union Territory Administration are mandatory and an institution is not entitled to recognition unless it fulfils the conditions specified in various clauses of the Regulations - Direction issued to the appellant/Council to ensure that in future no institution is granted recognition unless it fulfils the conditions laid down in the Act and the Regulations and the time schedule fixed for processing the application by the Regional Committees and communication of the decision on the issue of recognition is strictly adhered to - Appeal disposed off.
