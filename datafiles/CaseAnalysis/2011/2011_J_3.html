Princl. Chief Conservator of Forest and another v J. K. Johnson and others

Supreme Court of India

17 October 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, Jagdish Singh Khehar

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 649; (2011) 10 SCC 794; 2011 (4) CLT(SC) 164; JT 2011 (12) SC 76; 2012 (5) KarLJ 145; 2012 (2) MLJ(SC) 257; 2012 (1) RCR(Civil) 125; 2011 (4) RCR(Criminal) 865; 2011(12) SCALE 50; [2011] 12 S.C.R. 1144

Case Digest

Subject: Environment, Wildlife & Animal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Assam Forest Regulation, 1891, Wild Life (Protection) Amendment Act, 2002, Bengal Municipal Act, Wild Life (Protection) Amendment Bill, 2002

Summary: Environment, Wildlife & Animal - Practice & Procedure - Wild Life (Protection) Act, 1972, s. 54 - Wild Life (Protection) Amendment Act, 2002, s. 54 (2) - Poaching of wild animals - Forfeiture of seized property - Power of Conservator of Forests - Determination - Respondents were arrested for poaching wild animals and a case was registered against them u/s. 9 of the 1972 Act - Respondents stated that the offence was done by them in ignorance and they were willing to pay money by way of composition of the offence - Conservator of Forests (Conservator) ordered that the offence be compounded for Rs. 30,000/- u/s. 54 of the 1972 Act and the vehicle and the weapons used in committing the offence be forfeited - Respondents appealed to the appellant Principal Chief Conservator of Forests - Appellant directed the Conservator to reduce the composition fee from Rs. 30,000/- to Rs. 25,000/- - Conservator passed a fresh order permitting the respondents to compound the offence for Rs. 25,000, however, he further ordered that seized items i.e., vehicle and two rifles be forfeited to the State Government - Respondents filed writ petition challenging the Conservator's order insofar as it relates to forfeiture of seized property - Single Judge set aside the order of forfeiture - Appellant filed writ appeal, which was dismissed by DB - Whether a specified officer empowered u/s. 54(1) of the 1972 Act as amended by the 2002 Act to compound offences had power, competence and authority, on payment of a sum of money by way of composition of the offence by a person who was suspected to have committed offence against the 1972 Act, to order forfeiture of the seized items - Held, by 2002 Act, the Parliament had consciously deleted from s. 54 the provision concerning release of seized property liable to be forfeited on payment of value of such property but the plain language that was retained in s. 54 (2) after amendment which read, 'on payment of such sum of money to such officer, the suspected person, if in custody, shall be discharged and no further proceedings in respect of the offence shall be taken against such person' did not show that the Legislature intended to empower the specified officer u/s. 54 to forfeit the seized property used by the suspected person in commission of offence against the Act - S. 54 substituted by 2002 Act did not speak of seized property at all and neither its return nor its forfeiture while providing for composition of offence - Property seized u/s. 50(1)(c ) and s. 50(3A) had to be dealt with by the Magistrate according to law - S. 50(4) provided that things seized should be taken before a Magistrate to be dealt with according to law - S. 54 substituted by 2002 Act did not empower the specified officer to deal with the seized property - Hence, a specified officer empowered u/s. 54(1) of the 1972 Act as substituted by 2002 Act to compound offences, had no power, competence or authority to order forfeiture of the seized items on composition of the offence by a person who was suspected to have committed offence against the Act - Order passed by the Conservator for forfeiture of the seized items to the State Government was de hors the provisions of the 1972 Act and hence unsustainable - HC was rightly set aside the impugned order of forfeiture - However, respondents were not entitled to release the seized items, which were to be dealt with by the Magistrate u/s. 50(4) of the 1972 Act - Appeal disposed of.
