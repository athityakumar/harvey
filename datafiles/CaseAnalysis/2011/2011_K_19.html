Kalyan Singh Chouhan v C.P. Joshi

Supreme Court of India

24 January 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 56; (2011) 11 SCC 786; AIR 2011 SC 1127; 2011 (3) ALD(SC) 90; 2011 (1) CLT(SC) 147; JT 2011 (2) SC 97; 2011 (1) RCR(Civil) 865; 2011(1) SCALE 718; [2011] 2 S.C.R. 216

Case Digest

Subject: Election; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Election - Practice & Procedure - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 30, 80, 81, 100(1)(d)(iii) and 100(1)(d)(iv) - Conduct of Election Rules, 1961, rr. 41 and 63 - Summoning of certain documents - Election to constitute 13th Legislative Assembly was announced on 10.11.2008 - Appellant as well as the respondent filed their nominations - Poll was held on 4.12.2008 - Counting took place and appellant secured 62216 votes and respondent secured 62215 votes - Respondent filed an election petition u/ss. 80, 81, 100(1)(d)(iii) and 100(1)(d)(iv) of 1951 Act alleging that 6 tendered votes cast in the election must be counted and the 6 votes originally polled against the tendered votes must be rejected - Subsequently, respondent filed application dt. 11.5.2010 praying for the summoning of certain documents in respect of other 4 tendered votes - Said application was rejected by the HC on the ground that it was not permissible to summon said documents as none of the parties had taken the pleadings nor an issue had been framed in respect of said 4 tendered votes - Hence, the present appeal - Whether HC rightly denied summoning of said documents? - Held, respondent has claimed only that there has been irregularity/illegality in counting of 6 tendered votes and the case squarely falls within the ambit of s. 100(1)(d)(iii) of the 1951 Act - Respondent has further pleaded that the result of the election stood materially affected because of improper receiving the 6 tendered votes and in absence of any Recrimination Petition in respect of other 4 tendered votes, respondent cannot be permitted to lead evidence on the fact which is not in issue as respondent has raised his doubts in respect of the aforesaid 6 tendered votes but has not taken any specific pleadings in respect of remaining 4 tendered votes - Hence, HC rightly denied summoning of said documents - Appeal dismissed.
