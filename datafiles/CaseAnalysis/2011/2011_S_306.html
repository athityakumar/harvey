Kesar Enterprises Ltd. v State Of U.P. & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

06 July 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

D.K. Jain, H.L. Dattu

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 423; (2011) 13 SCC 733; AIR 2011 SC 2709; 2011(4) ALL MR 897; 2011 (4) AWC 3909; JT 2011 (7) SC 397; 2011(7) SCALE 232; [2011] 9 S.C.R. 19

Case Digest

Subject: Excise

Keywords: Uttar Pradesh Excise Manual

Summary: Excise - Indirect Tax - Uttar Pradesh Excise Act, 1910, s. 28 - Uttar Pradesh Excise Manual, r. 633 - Penalty - Excise Commissioner issued an order authorising 9 distilleries in the State, including 'X', to export rectified spirit outside India - Since the export consignment was to be routed through the appellant, as handling agent, the appellant was required to furnish an indemnity bond - Appellant executed said indemnity bond wherein one condition was that if the said quantity of rectified spirit was not delivered at the warehouse at Kandla, the appellant shall indemnify the Governor for any loss of duty - Said consignment was dispatched through the Northern Railway to Kandla Port - However, out of 15 tank wagons only 14 tank wagons reached the Kandla port - Show cause notice was issued to the appellant alleging that since the pass in form PD-25, issued to the appellant by the concerned Collector in terms of r. 633 of the Excise Manual had not been received back along with certificate from the Collector for due delivery, they were liable to deposit Rs. 8,71,744/- along with interest at the rate of 18% per annum to the Government Treasury - Appellant pleaded that since the entire rake of 15 tank wagons were handed over to the Railway authorities, it was the responsibility of the railways to make safe delivery of the goods at the destination and, therefore, the appellant was in no way responsible for the disappearance of rectified spirit contained in one of the tank wagons - Excise Commissioner directed the District Excise Officer to issue recovery certificate against the appellant for the recovery of Excise duty amounting to Rs. 8,71,744/- and interest thereon - Appellant filed writ petition before HC seeking quashing of notice of demand - HC dismissed said writ petition and held that State Government had no authority to levy Excise duty u/s. 28 of the Act on rectified spirit in question but it could impose penalty on the appellant u/r. 633(7) of the Uttar Pradesh Excise Manual - Appellant filed appeal against said order contending that since an order u/r. 633 of Rules, entails serious consequences the elementary principles of natural justice and fair play were required to be observed and consequently, an opportunity of hearing has to be afforded before an order under the said Rule was made, which was admittedly not done in the instant case - Whether sub-rule (7) of r. 633 of the Excise Manual postulates the requirement of hearing before steps for recovery of penalty under the said Rule are initiated? - Held, the Commissioner could not demand Excise duty on rectified spirit contained in the tank wagon which, later on, was found to be empty, without returning a finding that the said spirit had been diverted/converted into potable alcoholic liquor fit for human consumption, on which the State was empowered to impose duty - It bears repetition that such a finding could not be recorded by the Commissioner without affording due opportunity to the appellant to explain its stand in this regard for which, the onus lay on them as transporter and the executant of the bond - However, in the absence of any reasonable explanation regarding disappearance of rectified spirit, the Commissioner would have reason to presume that the same has been disposed of otherwise than by way of export outside the country, for which purpose it was being transported - In the instant case, before imposing the impugned demand of penalty and interest, there was absolutely no adjudication by any authority as regards the breach committed by the appellant, except the allegation that the appellant had failed to furnish the PD-25 pass certified by the Collector - Therefore, the action of the respondents for the recovery of penalty and interest, being violative of principles of natural justice, was null and void - Hence, impugned order set aside, sustaining the demand by invoking r. 633 of the Excise Manual and matter remitted to the jurisdictional Excise Commissioner to decide the question of levy of Excise duty and/or penalty and interest on the subject consignment of rectified spirit, after affording adequate opportunity of hearing to the appellant - Appeal allowed.
