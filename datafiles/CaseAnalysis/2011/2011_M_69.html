NTPC Limited v Madhya Pradesh State Electricity Board and others

Supreme Court of India

28 September 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Gokhale, J.M. Panchal

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 754; 2012 (1) AWC 1060; 2012 (1) JabLJ 241; [2011] 11 S.C.R. 651

Case Digest

Subject: Electricity & Energy; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Electricity & Energy - Practice & Procedure - Electricity Act, 2003, ss. 2 (28), 61, 62(6), 111, 185 - Electricity Supply Act, 1948, ss. 43A (2) - Central Electricity Regulatory Commission Regulations, 2004 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 144 - Tariff - Interest - Differential amounts - Justified - Electricity Boards/respondent/appellant receive power generated from thermal power plants of NTPC ltd/appellant/respondent - Central commission determined tariff payable to NTPC by Boards through orders - Central commission determined tariff rate lesser than pre-existing tariff - NTPC collected excess amount which was adjusted by it in subsequent bills - Boards were claiming for differential amounts along with interest - Commission rejected claim of interest by Boards - Boards filed appeals against orders of Commission before Tribunal which were partly allowed and partly dismissed - Aggrieved by same both NTPC and Boards filed instant appeals - Whether Appellant Tribunal erred in denying interest on differential amounts to concerned Boards and Whether Appellant Tribunal was justified in allowing interest on differential amounts on basis of justice, equity and fair-play - Held, Tribunal had awarded interest at an average of prevailing lending rates (PLR) of RBI to Banks during relevant period - Thus, terms of supply agreement, governing regulation and notifications did not contain any provision for interest - Further, industry practice did not provide for it as well therefore interest could not be claimed either on basis of equity or on basis of restitution - It was not possible to accept submission that Appellate Tribunal erred in any way in declining to award interest u/s. 62 (6) of the 2003 Act - However, an error on its part in granting same under concept of equity, justice and fair-play - Appeals disposed of.
