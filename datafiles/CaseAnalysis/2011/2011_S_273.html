Jarnail Singh v State Of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

11 February 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Surinder Singh Nijjar, B. Sudarshan Reddy

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 136; (2011) 3 SCC 521; AIR 2011 SC 964; 2011 CRLJ 1738; 2011 (2) JCC (Narcotics) 86; JT 2011 (2) SC 120; 2011 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1491; 2011 (1) RCR(Criminal) 925; 2011(2) SCALE 401; [2011] 2 S.C.R. 888

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985, s. 18 - Conviction - Challenged - Trial Court convicted the appellant u/s. 18 of the NDPS Act and sentenced him to undergo rigorous imprisonment for 10 years and to pay a fine of Rs. 1 Lac - On appeal, HC confirmed the order of the Trial Court - Hence, the present appeal - Whether order of the trial court as affirmed by the HC could be upheld? - Held, the trial court as also the HC have meticulously examined and re-examined the entire evidence - On such close scrutiny, both the courts have concurrently found that the prosecution has proved its case beyond reasonable doubt - Hence order of the trial court as affirmed by the HC upheld - Appeal dismissed.
