Mahadev Govind Gharge and another v Special Land Acquisition Officer, Upper Krishna Project, Jamkhandi, Karnataka and another

Supreme Court of India

10 May 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Ganguly, Swatanter Kumar

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 356; (2011) 6 SCC 321; AIR 2011 SC 2439; 2011 (5) ALD(SC) 1; 2011(4) ALL MR 858; 2011 (87) ALR 219; 2011 (5) AWC 4704; 2011 (2) CLT(SC) 330; 2011 (4) CTC 620; JT 2011 (6) SC 100; 2011(5) M.P.H.T. 248; 2011 (5) RCR(Civil) 364; 2011 (113) RD 680; 2011 (4) RLW 3175; 2011(6) SCALE 1; [2011] 8 S.C.R. 829

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 41 r. 22 - Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - Land acquisition - Cross-objection - Limitation - Appellants' lands were acquired and award was also passed - Matter referred to Reference Court - Reference Court enhanced compensation - Respondent authority filed appeal before HC wherein appellants also filed cross-objections with delay condonation petition - HC dismissed appeal and cross-objection relying upon its earlier judgment - Whether limitation period of one month prescribed under O. 41 r. 22 (1) of CPC should run from date of filing of appeal by respondents or from date of service of notice of date of hearing of appeal fixed by court - Held, provisions of O. 41 r. 22 of Code were akin to provisions of Limitation Act, 1963, i.e. when such provisions barred a remedy, by efflux of time, to one party, it gave consequential benefit to opposite party - Before such vested benefit could be taken away, Court had to strike a balance between respective rights of parties on plain reading of statutory provision to meet ends of justice - If a cross-objector failed to file cross-objections within stipulated time, then his right to file cross-objections was taken away only in a limited sense - To that extent a benefit was granted to other party, i.e. appellant, of having their appeal heard without such cross-objections - Still, however, if Court was of opinion that it was just and proper to permit filing of cross-objection even after expiry of statutory limitation of one month, it was certainly vested with power to grant same, but of course, only after hearing other party - That was how rights of parties were to be balanced in consonance with scheme of O. 41 r. 22 of Code - Stipulated period of one month was to commence from date of service, on concerned party or his pleader, of notice of day fixed for hearing appeal - A cross-objection should also be filed within such further time as Appellate Court may see fit to allow - Language of O. 41 r. 22 of Code fixed period of limitation to be computed from date of service of notice of hearing of appeal upon respondent/cross- objector and within one month of such date he had to file cross objections - Thus, crucial point of time was date on which notice of hearing of appeal was served and that could be a notice for actual date of hearing or otherwise - Needless to notice that cross-objections were required to be filed within period of one month from date of service of such notice or within such further time as Appellate Court may see fit to allow depending upon facts and circumstances of given case - Since provisions of O. 41 r. 22 of Code itself provided for extension of time, Courts would normally be inclined to condone delay in interest of justice unless and until cross-objector was unable to furnish a reasonable or sufficient cause for seeking leave of Court to file cross-objections beyond statutory period of one month - Hence impugned HC order was set aside and direction issued to hear respondent's appeal and appellants' cross-objection afresh - Appeal disposed of.
