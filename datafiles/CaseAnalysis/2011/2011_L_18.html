State Of U.P. & Ors v Luxmi Kant Shukla

Supreme Court of India

19 August 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Patnaik, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 513; (2011) 9 SCC 532; 2011 (10) ADJ 9; 2011 (6) AWC 5691; 2011 (4) ESC 627; 2011 (130) FLR 11; JT 2011 (9) SC 204; 2012 (1) MLJ(SC) 522; 2012 (1) RLW 741; 2011(9) SCALE 80; [2011] 10 S.C.R. 531; 2011 (5) SLR 395; 2011 (4) UPLBEC 3413

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: U.P. Fundamental Rules, 1942

Summary: Service - Uttar Pradesh Fundamental Rules, 1942, r. 56 - Voluntary retirement - Acceptance - Respondent Special Secretary authored a book allegedly contained some defamatory remarks against national leaders and thereby hurt the religious sentiments of the people - Appellant State appointed enquiry officer and enquiry officer conducted the enquiry ex parte - HC, in respondent's writ petition, directed the enquiry officer to commence the proceedings afresh from the stage of charge-sheet - Respondent, in the meantime, requested to exonerate him from the charges against him and instead grant voluntary retirement from service u/r. 56 of the FR - Appellant rejected respondent's request - Enquiry officer subsequently submitted his report holding that the charges against the respondent were proved - Appellant dismissed the respondent from service - Respondent filed writ petition wherein HC, by impugned order, quashed the appellant's order rejecting respondent's request of voluntary retirement and direction issued to the appellant to reconsider the respondent's request afresh keeping in view the observations made in the impugned judgment - Whether the respondent stood voluntary retired from service before the order of dismissal was passed by the State Government - Held, disciplinary proceeding was initiated against the respondent on 19-2- 2008 and it was only after the initiation of a disciplinary proceeding that the respondent made a request in the copy of his reply dated 28-5-2009 to the appointing authority to accept his voluntary retirement u/cl. (c) of FR 56 - Thus, even if the request of the respondent made on 28-5-2009 was treated as the notice of voluntary retirement, on 28-5-2009 a disciplinary proceeding was pending against the respondent and as per the language of the proviso to FR 56, such notice of voluntary retirement would be 'effective only if it was accepted by the appointing authority' - Hence, until the appointing authority accepted the request of the respondent for voluntary retirement, the very notice dated 28-5-2009 for voluntary retirement would not be effective - Further, HC, however, had taken the view in the impugned judgment that it was incumbent upon the appointing authority to inform the respondent before the expiry of the notice period of three months that his request for voluntary retirement had not been accepted and the HC had therefore directed that a fresh decision be taken by the State Government on the request of the respondent for voluntary retirement after it found that the Chief Minister had not put her signature in the order rejecting the request of the respondent for voluntary retirement - However, that view taken by the HC, was contrary to the plain language of the proviso which stated that in the case of 'a contemplated disciplinary proceeding' the government servant should be informed before the expiry of his notice that it had not been accepted - In a case where a disciplinary proceeding was pending, the relevant proviso to FR 56(c) and (d) did not require the decision of the appointing authority to be communicated to the Government servant before the expiry of the period of notice of voluntary retirement - Hence impugned HC judgment was set aside - Appeal allowed.
