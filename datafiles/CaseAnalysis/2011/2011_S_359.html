State of Punjab v State of Haryana and others

Supreme Court of India

23 September 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Panchal, H.L. Gokhale

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 672; (2011) 12 SCC 726; JT 2011 (11) SC 403; 2012 (1) RCR(Civil) 26; 2011(10) SCALE 718

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure; Water

Summary: Land & Property - Water - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950 , art. 131 - Supreme Court Rules, 1966 - Restriction - Region - Sustainability - State of Punjab filed Suit and claimed decree of perpetual injunction restraining State of Haryana from proceeding with digging of channel and construction of embankment under project named Hansi Branch - During pendency Suit, State of Punjab filed interlocutory application, and prayed to grant ad interim injunction restraining Defendant - State of Haryana from proceeding with construction of concrete toe wall/providing concrete lining on outer slope of left embankment between RD 45000 and 57000 of Hansi Branch - Service of copy of interlocutory application State of Haryana had filed detailed reply to which State of Punjab had filed rejoinder - State of Harayan contended that if there were heavy rains, water in area between Mirapur Drain and Ghaggar River would go into Ghaggar River or spill over into Punjab territory because of Bandh, but that had always been so, and if Bandh was not strengthened and more breaches took place, water would flow down towards Haryana affecting 19 villages in immediate vicinity - Held, Court found that purpose of Bandh had been to prevent flooding of areas on southern side of Bandh, which was in Haryana - Extensive damage was caused to 19 villages of State of Haryana, which was due to breach of Bandh/canal at point RD 53000 - It was clear that damage in 19 villages in Haryana which were on southern side of River Ghaggar was clearly attributable to over flowing waters of River Ghaggar as well as water flowing through breach - Relief claimed in interlocutory application could not be granted on basis of hypothesis, that strengthening of Bandh would cause flooding in 70 villages - Submission made on behalf of State of Punjab that strengthening of Ghaggar Bandh would cause backwater formation in Punjab and thus, exacerbate nuisance of submerging of villages in Punjab to north of Ghaggar Bandh, was not correct because Ghaggar Bandh was constructed by State of Punjab in 1950s for purpose of preventing flood waters, entering and submerging areas to south of Bandh - It was constructed neither to guide course of River Ghaggar nor was it designed to be deliberately weak enough to give way in heavy floods - It was constructed to hold backwaters in heaviest of floods to prevent flood waters from ever submerging villages to south - Case of State of Punjab rests on premise that breach repaired area should be allowed to remain as it was without strengthening it so that it could breach again if there was flood once again, and this area could act as pressure release valve, which would cause less damage to State of Punjab - Such assertion of right was contrary to rights of Defendant-State of Haryana, which was entitled to protect its inhabitants from floods just as State of Punjab was entitled to protect its inhabitants to south of Bandh - State of Haryana was only ensuring that after two disastrous breaches of 1993 and 2010, a breach did not occur in future - SC opined that State of Punjab could not reasonably object to this course of action - Hence, in view of larger damage, which was caused in Haryana in year 2010, and which was likely to be caused in Haryana, if Bandh was not properly repaired as undertaken, balance of convenience was in favour of Defendant-State of Haryana - It was rightly pointed out by State of Haryana that if relief, as prayed for, was granted to State of Punjab, it was State of Haryana, which would suffer greater loss and irreparable injury - It could not as well be denied that State of Haryana had right to carry out necessary work in its territory and also duty to its citizens - Application dismissed.
