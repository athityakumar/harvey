Kusum Lata And Others v Satbir And Others

Supreme Court of India

02 March 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Ganguly, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 140; (2011) 3 SCC 646; 2011 (1) ACC 907; 2011 ACJ 926; AIR 2011 SC 1234; 2011 (2) AWC 1690; 2011 (3) MLJ(SC) 151; 2011 (3) MPLJ 18; 2011 (162) PLR 490; 2011 (2) RCR(Civil) 379; 2011(3) SCALE 74; [2011] 3 S.C.R. 480; 2011 (3) WLN(SC) 1

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 - Motor Accident Claim - Failure to mention details of offending vehicle in FIR - Compensation - Entitlement - Deceased met with an accident and succumbed to his injuries due to rash and negligent driving of respondent - Appellants (legal heirs of deceased) preferred claim petition - Tribunal rejected appellant's claim on ground that alleged involvement of offending vehicle had not been proved - HC, in appeal, affirmed Tribunal order on ground that number and name of offending vehicle and driver were not mentioned in FIR - Whether denial of compensation to appellants on ground of failure to mention name and number of driver and offending vehicle was proper - Held, when a person was seeing that his relative, being knocked down by a speeding vehicle, was suffering in pain and was in need of immediate medical attention, that person was obviously under a traumatic condition - Under such mental strain if brother of victim, who lodged FIR, forgot to take down number of offending vehicle it was natural - Further, Tribunal and HC discarded reliable evidence of an eye-witness only for reason that his name was not mentioned in FIR - In a case relating to motor accident claims, claimants were not required to prove case as it was required to be done in a criminal trial, therefore, approach of Tribunal and HC were not proper - Further, Tribunal erred by applying multiplier of 16 for assessment of compensation, however, considering age of victim, multiplier of 17 should be applied - Appellants, therefore, were entitled to compensation since victim was sole wage earner in family - Impugned Tribunal and HC orders were set aside - Appeal allowed.
