B. Kothandapani v Tamil Nadu State Transport Corporation Ltd

Supreme Court of India

12 May 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 334; (2011) 6 SCC 420; 2011 (2) ACC 707; 2011 ACJ 1971; 2011 (86) ALR 895; 2011 (4) AWC 3609; JT 2011 (6) SC 257; 2011 (164) PLR 646; 2011 (3) RCR(Civil) 206; 2011(6) SCALE 41; [2011] 6 S.C.R. 791

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Quantum of compensation - Appellant-claimant sustained grievous injuries in a motor vehicle accident for which he made a claim before the Motor Accident Claims Tribunal (Tribunal) for a sum of Rs. 12 lakhs as compensation - Tribunal, after finding that the accident was caused due to the negligence of the driver of the Tamil Nadu State Transport Corporation (Corporation), quantified the compensation and passed an award for Rs.5,05,053.45 - On appeal, HC allowed the appeal of the Corporation and reduced the compensation to the extent of Rs.1,00,000 which was awarded by the Tribunal for permanent disability - Hence, instant appeals - Whether the appellant was entitled to a sum of Rs. 1,00,000 towards 'permanent disability' in addition to the amount awarded under the head 'loss of earning capacity'? - Held, two doctors had explained the nature of injuries, treatment received and the disability suffered due to partial loss of eye-sight and amputation of middle finger in the right hand - Taking note of appellants' nature of work, partial loss in the eye sight, loss of middle finger of the right hand, it not only affects his earning capacity but also affects normal avocation and day-to-day work - Hence, Tribunal was fully justified in granting a sum towards permanent disability - Appeals allowed.
