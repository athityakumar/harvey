Deepak Agarwal and another v State Of Uttar Pradesh & Ors

Supreme Court of India

31 March 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Surinder Singh Nijjar, B. SUDARSHAN REDDY

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 212; (2011) 6 SCC 725; 2011 (8) ADJ 68; 2011 (4) AWC 4232; 2011 (2) ESC 309; JT 2011 (4) SC 423; 2011(4) SCALE 177; [2011] 4 S.C.R. 149; 2011 (2) SLR 739; 2011 (3) UPLBEC 2047

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Uttar Pradesh Government Criterion for Recruitment by Promotion Rules, 1994, Uttar Pradesh Servants Criterian for Recruitment by Promotion Rules, 1994, Uttar Pradesh Excise Group 'A' Service (5th amendment) Rules, 1999, Andhra Pradesh Registration and Subordinate Service Rules, 1976, Uttar Pradesh Promotion by Selection (on posts outside the purview of the Public Service Commission) Eligibility List Rules, 1986, U.P. Excise Group 'A' Service Rules, 1983

Summary: Service - Uttar Pradesh Excise Group 'A' Service Rules, 1983, rr. 2, 7 - Promotion - Denial - Legality - Appellant Technical Officers and Statistical Officers were denied promotion to post of Deputy Excise Commissioner (DEC) by virtue of Notification dated 17-5-1999 - Appellants challenged the denial of promotion before HC - Appellant contended that they were subjected to 1983 Rules and vacancies arouse prior to 17-5-1999, therefore, amendment brought out by Notification dated 17-5-1999 in 1983 Rules made Technical Officers and Statistical Officers ineligible for post of DEC was not sustainable - HC dismissed appellants writ petition - Hence the instant appeal - (A) Whether appellants had acquired any right to be considered for promotion at relevant time - Held, a candidate had right to be considered in light of existing rules, which implied 'rule in force' on date consideration took place, however, there was no rule of universal or absolute application that vacancies were to be filled invariably by law existing on date when vacancy arouse - Requirement of filling up old vacancies under old rules was interlinked with candidate having acquired a right to be considered for promotion - Right to be considered for promotion accrued on date of consideration of eligible candidates - In the instant case, there was no statutory duty cast upon respondents to either prepare a year-wise panel of eligible candidates or selected candidates for promotion - Proviso to r. 2 enabled State to keep any post unfilled, therefore, clearly there was no statutory duty which State could be mandated to perform under applicable rules - Requirement to identify vacancies in a year or to take a decision how many posts were to be filled u/r. 7 should not be equated with not issuing promotion orders to candidates duly selected for promotion - Appellants, therefore, had not acquired any right to be considered for promotion on relevant time - (B) Whether removal of posts of Technical Officers and Statistical Officers from feeder category of DEC was justifiable - Held, post of Technical Officers and Statistical Officers were deleted from feeder cadre for promotion to post of DEC for valid reasons - Government was of opinion that Technical Officers and Statistical Officers were not suitable to be promoted on post of DEC, which involved multifarious administrative responsibilities - Experience gained by officials working on post of Technical Officer and Statistical Officer was of no relevance for duties to be performed on the post of DEC - Government took a conscious decision to abolish feeder cadre consisting of Technical Officers and Statistical Officers for promotion to post of DEC - Removal of two posts from feeder cadre would lead to some stagnation for officers working on the said posts, however, Government recognized such a situation - It was perhaps for that reason that posts of Technical Officers and Statistical Officers had been upgraded, however, mere up-gradation of post may not be sufficient compensation for officers working on the said posts for loss of opportunity to be promoted on post of DEC - In such circumstances, Government was advised to have a re-look at promotion policy to provide some opportunity of further promotion to officers working on posts of Technical Officers and Statistical Officers - Impugned HC judgment was affirmed - Appeal dismissed.
