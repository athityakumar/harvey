Union Of India & Ors. v M/S. Master Construction Co

Supreme Court of India

25 April 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 314; (2011) 12 SCC 349; 2011 (86) ALR 752; 2011 (4) AWC 3629; 2011 (2) CLT(SC) 271; 2011 (2) CompLJ 520; JT 2011 (5) SC 244; 2011 (5) MLJ(SC) 835; 2011 (3) RCR(Civil) 526; 2011(5) SCALE 165; [2011] 5 S.C.R. 853

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Contract & Commercial - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996 - Arbitral dispute - Appellant awarded contract to the respondent (Contractor) for constructing and erecting technical buildings at Bhatinda - Respondent completed said work and furnished 'no-claim certificates' - Payment of final bill and bank guarantee was released to the respondent - Immediately after release of the bank guarantee, the respondent wrote to the appellant withdrawing 'no-claim certificates' and lodged certain claims - Appellant no. 3 declined to entertained said claims of the respondent on the ground that the final bill had been accepted by the respondent after furnishing the 'no-claim certificates' and no claim under the contract remained - Respondent made an application u/s. 11 of the Act before Civil Judge contending that 'no- claim certificates' were given by him under the financial duress and coercion as the appellant had arbitrarily withheld the payment - Civil Judge dismissed said application - Appellant challenged said order before HC - HC held that all disputes between the parties to the contract have to be referred to the arbitration - Hence, the instant appeal - Whether after furnishing 'no-claim certificates' and the receipt of payment of final bill, as submitted by the respondent, any arbitrable dispute between the parties survived or the contract stood discharged? - Held, mere allegation that no-claim certificates have been obtained under financial duress and coercion, without there being anything more to suggest that, does not lead to an arbitrable dispute - In the instant case, 'no-claim certificate' leave no manner of doubt that upon receipt of the payment, there has been full and final settlement of the respondent's claim under the contract - After receipt of the payment, no grievance was raised or lodged by the respondent immediately - The concerned authority, thereafter, released the bank guarantee - It was then that on that day itself, the respondent lodged further claims - The conduct of the respondent clearly shows that 'no claim certificates' were given by it voluntarily, the respondent accepted the amount voluntarily and the contract was discharged voluntarily - Impugned order of the HC set aside - Appeal allowed.
