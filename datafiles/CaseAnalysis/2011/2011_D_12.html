State of Rajasthan and others v Daya Lal and others

Supreme Court of India

13 January 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 37; (2011) 2 SCC 429; 2011 (8) ADJ 80; AIR 2011 SC 1193; 2011 (1) AllCJ 507; 2011 (4) AWC 3480; 2011 (1) ESC 97; 2011 (128) FLR 928; 2011 (1) JhrCR(SC) 177; JT 2011 (1) SC 457; 2011 (2) LLJ 193; 2011 (3) RLW 2161; 2011(1) SCALE 408; [2011] 1 S.C.R. 707; 2011 (1) SLR 646; 2011 (1) UPLBEC 606

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Government & Aided Hostels Management Rules, 1982

Summary: Service - Government & Aided Hostels Management Rules, 1982 - Regularisation - Entitlement - Respondent/ Superintendents/Cooks/Chowkidars were temporarily appointed in the aided hostels receiving aid from the Social Welfare Department of the State Government - Respondents sought regularisation of their service from the date of their initial appointment - Appellant/State rejected respondents' representations - Respondents filed writ petition contending that HC had earlier approved a scheme under which part time Superintendents/Cooks/Chowkidars who were working as on 1-5-1995 were regularized; and that as the respondents were all appointed subsequent to 1- 5-1995 and were not therefore covered under the said scheme, a fresh scheme should be framed to benefit them - Whether the respondents are entitled to regualrisation on their respective posts - Held, respondents were appointed under the provisions of 1982 Rules - 1982 Rules were not statutory rules framed by the State Government in pursuance of any power vested in the State by the legislature under any enactment - 1982 Rules were more in the nature of executive instructions and guidelines framed for administrative convenience - 1982 Rules were intended to apply to Government hostels run by the Social Welfare Department as also aided hostels which received any aid in the form of grant from the Social Welfare Department - Persons employed in the aided hostels were the employees of the respective organizations running those hostels and not the employees of the Government - Government has merely prescribed the eligibility conditions to be fulfilled by the private organizations to get grants to meet the food and education expenses of students staying in such hostels - Therefore, persons employed by the aided hostels could not be termed as persons employed by the State Government - Nor could the Government be held liable for respondents' service conditions, absorption, regularisation or salary of employees of private hostels - If the employees (either permanent or temporary) of the aided hostels are not the employees of the Government, but of the aided private charitable organizations which run such aided hostels, they could not maintain any writ petition claiming the status or salary on par with the corresponding post-holders in State Government service, nor claim regularization of service under the State Government - Hence, the writ petitions by persons employed in aided hostels for relief of regularization or parity in pay, were not maintainable and the decision of the HC granting any relief to respondents cannot be sustained - Impugned order is set aside - Appeal allowed.
