Shah Nawaz v State Of U.P. & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

05 August 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 479; (2011) 13 SCC 751; 2011 ALL MR (Cri) 3889; 2011 (3) KLT 926; 2011 (3) RCR(Criminal) 884; 2012 (2) RLW 1103; 2011(8) SCALE 423; [2011] 9 S.C.R. 859

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Uttar Pradesh Children Act, 1951

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302 and 307 - Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act, 2000 - Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Rules, 2007 - Determination of age of juvenile - Entry relating to date of birth made in the mark sheet of High School examination - FIR was lodged by wife of deceased, against the appellant and three others u/ss. 302 and 307 of the IPC - Mother of the appellant submitted an application before the Juvenile Justice Board (Board) stating that the appellant was a minor at the time of the alleged occurrence - Board declared the appellant juvenile under the provisions of the Act - On appeal, Additional Sessions Judge set aside the order passed by the Board - Revision filed thereagainst was dismissed by HC - Hence, instant appeal - Held, entry relating to date of birth entered in the mark sheet and School Leaving Certificate were valid proof of evidence for determination of age of an accused person - In the instant case, date of birth mentioned in the High School mark sheet produced by the appellant had duly been corroborated by the School Leaving Certificate of the appellant of Class X and had also been proved by the statement of the clerk of the School - Date of birth of the appellant had also been recorded as 18.06.1989 in School Leaving Certificate issued by the Principal of the School as well as the said date of birth mentioned in the school register of the said school - Appellant declared to be a juvenile on the date of commission of offence - Appeal allowed.
