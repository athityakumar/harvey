Mohan Singh v State Of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

26 August 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Ganguly, Deepak Verma

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 693; (2011) 9 SCC 272; 2011 CRLJ 4837; 2012 (1) MLJ(Crl) 648; 2011 (4) RCR(Criminal) 84; [2011] 12 S.C.R. 327

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: (A) Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120-B, 302, 387, 211 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s.225 - Murder - Criminal conspiracy - Conviction - Informant received call and asked demanded informant to send money - Informant on identification of voice of telephone caller as that of appellant - On basis of statements of informant which were supported by informant's father and deceased brother, case was registered - Sessions Court found appellant guilty of criminal conspiracy for murder u/s. 120B of IPC and of extortion u/s. 387 of IPC - Appeal filed before DB who upheld order of conviction holding that identification of voice and gait was risky, but where witness identifying voice was previous acquaintance with (caller) accused such identification could be relied upon and that case of conspiracy was made out - Hence instant Appeal - Whether appellant suffered prejudice in proceeding - Held, charge was cumbersome but in absence of any objection at proper time and in absence of any material from which Court could infer prejudice, such grievances was precluded by reason of provision of s. 225 of the CrPC - No prejudice was caused to appellant for non-mentioning of s. 302 of IPC in charge since all ingredients of offence were disclosed - Appellant had full notice and had ample opportunity to defend himself and no earlier stage appellant raised any grievance - Appellant was not suffered any prejudice nor there was any failure of justice - Accused-appellant had committed murder and by mentioning that all ingredients of charge was mentioned and requirement of s. 211(2) of IPC was complied with - Hence, conviction of appellant u/s. 120B of IPC for life imprisonment was affirmed - Appeal dismissed.

(B) Criminal - Identification of telephone call - Substantive evidence - Proof of conspiracy - Whether identification of voice call was substantive piece of evidence - Held, evidence of PW.4 that he knows voice of appellant was not challenged nor was it challenged that mobile number was not of appellant - Evidence of PW.8 was also not challenged that eight calls were recorded between mobiles of appellant and his conspirator accused - There was enough evidence that both appellant and accused had conspired together for committing offence - However, substantive evidence was placed to prove meeting of minds between appellant and accused about murder of victim - Evidence clearly showed that there was substantial piece of evidence to prove criminal conspiracy - Thus, no reason was found to interfere with concurrent finding in instant case - Appeal dismissed.
