Uttar Pradesh State Road Transport Corporation v Kulsum & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

25 July 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Deepak Verma, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 461; (2011) 8 SCC 142; 2011 (3) ACC 460; 2011 ACJ 2145; 2011(5) ALL MR 355; 2011 (89) ALR 724; 2011 (5) AWC 4682; 2011 (4) CPJ(Sc) 119B; JT 2011 (8) SC 322; 2011 (7) MLJ(SC) 375; 2011 (164) PLR 280; 2011 (3) RCR(Civil) 826; 2011(8) SCALE 28; [2011] 15 S.C.R. 618

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance

Keywords: Liabilities, Uttar Pradesh Amendment Act, 1993

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Insurance - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 - Motor accident claim - Contract carriage - Liability of insurer - Determination - Owner of a mini bus entered into an Agreement of Contract with the appellant Transport Corporation for allowing him to ply his mini bus, as per the permit issued in favour of appellant wherein except for the services of the driver, which were to be provided by the owner, all other rights of owner were to be exercised by the appellant - Mini bus met with an accident and four persons died in that accident - Claimants (legal heirs of deceased) filed claim petitions before the Tribunal - Tribunal awarded compensation to claimants by fastening the liability of payment on appellant on the ground that at the time of accident, the offending vehicle was being run by it under the contract - Appellant filed appeal contending that respondent insurance company was, in fact, liable to pay the compensation and not that of appellant - HC dismissed appeal holding that insurance company was not liable to make payment - If insured vehicle was plying under an Agreement of Contract with the appellant, on the route as per permit granted in favour of the appellant, in case of an accident, whether the insurance company would be liable to pay compensation or would it be the responsibility of the appellant or the owner - Held, appellant and the owner had specifically agreed that the vehicle would be insured and a driver would be provided by owner of the vehicle but overall control, not only on the vehicle but also on the driver, would be that of the appellant - Thus, the vehicle was given on hire by the owner of the vehicle together with its existing and running insurance policy to appellant - In view of the aforesaid terms and conditions, the insurance company could not escape its liability to pay the amount of compensation - Insurance company did not deny the fact that at the relevant point of time the vehicle in question was insured with it and the policy was very much in force and in existence - It was also not the case of the insurance company that the driver of the vehicle was not holding a valid driving licence to drive the vehicle - Tribunal also held that the driver had a valid driving licence at the time of accident - It had also not been contended by insurance company that there had been violation of the terms and conditions of the policy or that the driver was not entitled to drive the said vehicle - In the instant case, the driver was employed by the owner of the bus but evidently through cl 4.4. of the Agreement, driver was supposed to drive the bus under the instructions of conductor who was appointed by the appellant - Thus, it would be inferred that effective control and command of the bus was that of the appellant and for all practical purposes, for the relevant period, the appellant had become the owner of the vehicle for the specific period - If the appellant had become the owner even for the specific period and the vehicle having been insured at the instance of original owner, it would be deemed that the vehicle was transferred along with the Insurance Policy in existence to the appellant and thus insurance company would not be able to escape its liability to pay the amount of compensation - Further, insurance company had also failed to point out violation of any Act, Rules or conditions of the Insurance - Hence, insurance company had no legal justification to deny the payment of compensation to the claimants - Impugned HC order was set aside and held the respondent insurance company would be liable to pay compensation - Appeal allowed.
