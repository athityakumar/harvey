State Of Uttar Pradesh & Ors v J. P. Saraswat

Supreme Court of India

11 March 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Aftab Alam, R.M. Lodha

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 162; (2011) 4 SCC 545; 2012(2) ALL MR 40; 2011 (87) ALR 877; 2011 (87) ALR 879; 2011 (2) ESC 290; 2011 (130) FLR 319; JT 2011 (3) SC 198; 2011 (2) LLJ 625; 2011 (3) MPLJ 483; 2011(3) SCALE 510; 2011 (3) SLR 373

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Unauthorised absence - Termination from service - Legality - Appellant Surgeon was charge-sheeted for going abroad without permission from the Government and for unauthorised absence from duty - Appellant was terminated from service, however, he was not debarred from future employment - Appellant challenged termination order before HC - HC allowed appellant's writ petition - Hence instant appeal - Whether impugned HC order was proper - Held, charges against respondent were duly established, therefore, HC ought to have simply dismissed writ petition - Any interference on question of punishment was permissible in very rare cases where punishment was so disproportionate to established charge that it would appear unconscionable and actuated by malice - In instant case, punishment given to respondent was quite moderate and there was not even a whisper of any malice - In such circumstances, punishment of termination of service that would not debar from future employment was a perfectly reasonable and fair punishment and there was no occasion for HC to interfere with that order - HC was equally wrong in setting aside punishment order passed against respondent on ground that appellant State had not responded to respondent's applications for extension/grant of leave or that during long period of his absence Government had not sent him any notice asking to resume duties by a certain date - Impugned HC order, therefore, was set aside - Appeal allowed.
