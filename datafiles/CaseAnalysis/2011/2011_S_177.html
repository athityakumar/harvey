Jagat Singh v State Of H.P

Supreme Court of India

03 January 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, H.S. Bedi, Chandramauli Kumar Prasad

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 5; (2011) 2 SCC 234; 2011 ALL MR (Cri) 1685; 2011 CRLJ 949; 2011 (1) JCC 296; JT 2011 (1) SC 111; 2011 (1) MLJ(Crl) 284; 2011 (1) RCR(Criminal) 393; 2011(1) SCALE 25; [2011] 1 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 300, 302 and 323 - Murder - Conviction - Challenged - In a fight between two groups of persons, members of both sides got injured and one person succumbed to the injuries - Appellant and his brother also sustained injuries in the same commotion - Accused persons were charged for offence punishable u/ss. 302 and 307 r/w s. 34 of IPC - Trial Court held that accused persons had no requisite intention to kill the deceased as envisaged u/s. 300 of IPC and acquitted them - On appeal, HC reversed the order of acquittal and convicted them u/ss. 302 and 307 r/w s. 34 of IPC - Hence, present appeal - Whether the act of the appellant along with the other accused was deliberate and pre-planned to murder deceased or the offences alleged to have been committed have arisen from a free fight which had erupted at the spur of the moment? - Held, considering the medical evidence of the doctor with regard to the injuries sustained by the deceased, the complainant (PW-1) as well as the appellant and the evidence of doctor, the Trial Court rightly observed that they had no requisite intention to kill the deceased as envisaged u/s. 300 of IPC - Further, considering the nature of the injuries sustained by the deceased and the injuries sustained by the appellant, the appellant could be convicted u/s. 323 of IPC for causing hurt on the body of the deceased - Conviction altered from s. 302 to s. 323 of IPC - Appeal disposed of.
