Fuerst Day Lawson Limited and others v Jindal Exports Limited and others

Supreme Court of India

08 July 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Aftab Alam, R.M. Lodha

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 433; (2011) 8 SCC 333; AIR 2011 SC 2649; 2011 (3) CLT(SC) 189; JT 2011 (7) SC 469; 2011 (7) MLJ(SC) 557; 2011 (4) MPLJ 554; 2011 (4) RCR(Civil) 775; 2011(7) SCALE 513; [2011] 11 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Keywords: Arbitration (Protocol And Convention) Act, 1937, Arbitration Act, 1899

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, s. 50 - Exclusion of Letters Patent jurisdiction of HC - Whether an order, though not appealable u/s. 50 of the Act, would nevertheless be subject to appeal under the relevant provision of the Letters Patent of the HC? - It was submitted that even though the Act did not envisage or permit an appeal from the order, the party aggrieved by it can still have his way, by-passing the Act and taking recourse to another jurisdiction - Held, on matters pertaining to arbitration, the Arbitration Act, was held to be a self-contained code, which consolidates, amends and designs the law relating to arbitration to bring it, as much as possible, in harmony with the UNCITRAL Model - Hence, once it was held that the Arbitration Act was a self-contained code and exhaustive, then it must also be held, that it carried with it 'a negative import that only such acts as were mentioned in the Act were permissible to be done and acts or things not mentioned therein were not permissible to be done' - Therefore, a Letters Patent Appeal would be excluded by application of one of the general principles that where the special Act sets out a self-contained code the applicability of the general law procedure would be impliedly excluded - Hence, no letters patent appeal would lie against an order which was not appealable u/s. 50 of the Arbitration Act - Appeals disposed of.
