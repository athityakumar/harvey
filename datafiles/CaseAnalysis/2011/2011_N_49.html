Noida Entrepreneurs Association v Noida & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

09 May 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 324; (2011) 6 SCC 508; AIR 2011 SC 2112; 2011 (3) AllCriC 564; 2011 (4) AWC 3265; 2011 (3) JIC 122; JT 2011 (6) SC 223; 2011(5) SCALE 505; [2011] 8 S.C.R. 25; 2011 (3) UPLBEC 2407

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Keywords: All India Services (Death-Cum-Retirement Benefits) Rules, 1958, U.P. Urban Planning and Development Act, 1973, Uttar Pradesh, Industrial Area Development Act, 1976, New Okhla Industrial Development Area (Preparation and Finalisation of Plan) Regulations, 1991

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - U.P. Industrial Area Development Act, 1976 - All India Services (Death-cum-Retirement Benefits) Rules, 1958 - Criminal misconduct by a public servant - Initiation of disciplinary proceedings - It was alleged that officials responsible for managing the New Okhla Industrial Development Authority (NOIDA) were guilty of manipulation, nepotism and corruption - Allegations were leveled against some of the officials carrying out the responsibilities of implementing the Act and other statutory provisions - SC directed the CBI to conduct an investigation in respect of the irregularities in the matter of allotments and conversions of the plots in NOIDA - Respondent no.7 filed her affidavit with regard to irregularities committed by other officers, namely, respondent no. 4, 5 and 8 (IAS officer) - SC constituted a Commission to examine a large number of issues, including as to why disciplinary proceedings had been dropped by the State of U.P. against several officials who had been impleaded as respondents in this case - Complaint against some officers including respondent no.4 - (A) Whether any action was warranted against respondent no.4 and if so, whether it is permissible to initiate the disciplinary proceedings against him as he reached the age of superannuation and has retired and the alleged misconduct had been committed by him in 1993-94? - It was submitted that disciplinary proceedings could not be initiated against him in view of delay and latches as the statutory rules applicable did not permit such a course at such a belated stage - Held, services of respondent no.4 stood governed by All India Services (Death-cum-Retirement Benefits) Rules, 1958 - Rule 6(b), thereof, provides that in case the delinquent had already retired, the proceedings shall not be instituted against him without the sanction of the Central Government and shall be in respect of an event which took place not more than four years before the institution of such proceedings - Thus, law does not permit holding disciplinary proceedings against respondent no.4 at this belated stage - (B) Initiation of criminal proceedings - Held, initiation of criminal proceedings was governed by the provisions of Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - There was no limitation prescribed for an offence punishable with more than 3 years imprisonment - Question of delay in launching criminal prosecution may be a circumstance to be taken into consideration in arriving at a final decision, but it cannot itself be a ground for dismissing the complaint - More so, the issue of limitation was to be examined in the light of the gravity of the charge - In the instant case, allegations being of a very serious nature and as alleged, the respondent no.4 had passed orders in colourable exercise of power favouring himself and certain contractors, require investigation - Thus, CBI directed to make preliminary enquiry and in case the allegations were found having some substance warranting further proceeding with criminal prosecution, may proceed in accordance with law - Writ petition disposed of.
