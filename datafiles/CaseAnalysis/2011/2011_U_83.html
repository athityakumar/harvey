Union of India and others v J. D. Suryavanshi

Supreme Court of India

05 September 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 652; (2011) 13 SCC 167; 2011 (8) MLJ(SC) 1068; 2011(10) SCALE 689; [2011] 11 S.C.R. 158

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution

Summary: Civil Procedure - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art.226 - PIL - Directions issued - Maintainability - Respondent filed PIL in HC for issue of certain directions to appellant /railway administration - HC issued some directions - Some directions were complied and regarding other directions for more trains/coaches appellant informed that would not be feasible due to technical reasons - HC was not satisfied and passed directions by interim order dt. 5-7-2010 - Aggrieved, appellant filed instant appeal - Whether impugned directions issued by HC dt.5-7-2010 were valid and justified - Held, Railway administration was specialized field and caters needs of entire Country - Railway would have to distribute and utilize available resources and available rolling stock equally and appropriately to serve all sections of Country - Railways did not exist to cater a particular sector - It is for railway administration to decide where how and when trains or coaches should be added or timings should be changed - Courts didnot have data inputs, specialized knowledge or technical skills required for running Railways - HC could not direct introduction of trains or additional coaches etc. - SC by several judgments held that Courts should not interfere in matters of policy or in day-to-day functioning of any departments of Govt. or Statutory bodies - Railway had made all efforts to comply with requirements/earlier directions of HC - Courtesies extended by Railways should not be taken as readiness to comply with impractical suggestions and unreasonable directions - - Railways should have freedom and independence to grow, develop, improve and serve Nation - Appellants would take administrative decisions keeping larger public interest or public convenience and not convenience of public of particular town - Hence those administrative decisions should not be interfered by Courts as laid down by SC in Union of India v. Nagesh 2002 Indlaw SC 1778 - Every matter of public interest or curiosity could not be subject matter of PIL - Courts should interfere only when there was clear violation of constitutional or statutory provisions or non-compliance by State with its constitutional or statutory duties as laid down in Balco Employees Union (Regd.) vs. Union of India & Ors. 2001 Indlaw SC 20366 - Hence, interim order dt. 5-.7-.2010 of HC was set aside - Directions issued to HC to dispose of writ petition - Appeal allowed.
