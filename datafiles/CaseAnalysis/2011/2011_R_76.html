Prakash Kadam & Etc. Etc v Ramprasad Vishwanath Gupta & Anr

Supreme Court of India

13 May 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Markandey Katju, Gyan Sudha Misra

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 339; (2011) 6 SCC 189; AIR 2011 SC 1945; 2012 (1) CGLJ 322; 2011 CRLJ 3585; 2011 (2) JCC 1407; JT 2011 (5) SC 629; 2011 (3) RCR(Criminal) 1; 2011(6) SCALE 236; [2011] 6 S.C.R. 800

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 120-B, 302 and 364 - Cancellation of bail - Validity - Appellants were policemen accused of a contract killing in case pending before the Sessions Judge - Appellants have been charge-sheeted for offences punishable u/ss. 302/34,120-B, 364/34 of IPC and other minor offences - It was alleged that the appellants were engaged as contract killers by a private person to murder the deceased - Sessions Court granted bail to the appellants but that was cancelled by the HC by the impugned judgment - Hence, instant appeal - Whether there was a prima facie case against the appellants which disentitled them to bail? - Held, in considering whether to cancel the bail the Court had to consider the gravity and nature of the offence, prima facie case against the accused, the position and standing of the accused, etc. - If there were very serious allegations against the accused his bail may be cancelled even if he had not misused the bail granted to him - Instant case was a serious case wherein prima facie some police officers and staff were engaged by some private persons to kill their opponent and the police officers and the staff acted as contract killers for them - HC was perfectly justified in canceling the bail to the appellants - Appeals dismissed.
