Narmada Bai v State Of Gujarat And Others

Supreme Court of India

08 April 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 244; (2011) 5 SCC 79; AIR 2011 SC 1804; 2011 CRLJ 2651; JT 2011 (4) SC 279; 2011 (3) MLJ(Crl) 270; 2011 (3) RCR(Criminal) 12; 2011(4) SCALE 469; [2011] 5 S.C.R. 729

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 21, 22 and 32 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Transfer of investigation to CBI - Fake encounter case - Petitioner, mother of the deceased, who, according to her, was killed in a fake encounter by respondent Nos. 6 to 19, officials of Gujarat and Rajasthan Police, filed the instant writ petition u/art. 32 of the Constitution praying for issuance of a writ of mandamus directing the Central Bureau of Investigation (CBI) to register a First Information Report (FIR) and investigate into the fake encounter killing of her son and submit its report to SC - (A) Whether after filing of the charge-sheet by the State agency, the Court was precluded from appointing any other independent specialized agency like the CBI to go into the same issues if the earlier investigation was not done as per the established procedure? - Held, in an appropriate case, particularly, when the Court felt that the investigation by the State police authorities was not in the proper direction as the high police officials were involved, in order to do complete justice, it was always open to the Court to hand over the investigation to an independent and specialized agency like the CBI - (B) Whether the petitioner had made out a case for entrusting the investigation to the CBI? - Held, considering the nature of crime that was allegedly committed not by any third party but by the police personnel of the State of Gujarat, investigation conducted and concluded in the instant case by the State police could not be accepted - Hence, in view of various circumstances highlighted and in the light of the involvement of police officials of the State of Gujarat and police officers of two other States, i.e. Andhra Pradesh and Rajasthan, it would not be desirable to allow the Gujarat State Police to continue with the investigation, accordingly, to meet the ends of justice and in the public interest, CBI should be directed to take the investigation - Police Authorities of the Gujarat State were directed to handover all the records of the instant case to the CBI - Writ petition allowed.
