K.R. Madhusudhan & Ors. v Administrative Officer & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

18 February 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Ganguly, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 114; (2011) 4 SCC 689; 2011 (1) ACC 700; 2011 ACJ 743; AIR 2011 SC 979; 2011 (85) ALR 476; 2011 (2) AWC 1820; 2011 (3) MPLJ 486; 2011 (162) PLR 487; 2011 (2) RCR(Civil) 207; 2011(2) SCALE 511; [2011] 2 S.C.R. 1061

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, s. 166 - Motor accident claim - Quantum of compensation - Determination - Deceased died due to rash and negligent driving of the 2nd respondent - Appellant legal heirs of the deceased filed claim petition against respondents, owner and driver of the offending vehicle - Tribunal awarded compensation by adopting multiplier 11, however, HC, in appeal, reduced the compensation - Hence the instant appeal - Whether the appellants entitled to the enhanced compensation - Held, evidence on record was clear and incontrovertible that the deceased, who working as Senior Assistant in State Electricity Board, was entitled and in fact bound to get a rise in income in the future - PW3, who was also the Senior Assistant in the State Electricity Board, in his evidence stated that the deceased was 52 years of age at the time of his death and he was having six years of service left - PW3 further deposed that as per the Board Agreement for every five years their pay revision was compulsory - In view of that evidence the Tribunal should have considered the prospect of future income while computing compensation but the Tribunal had not done that - HC, in the appeal, however, instead of maintaining the amount of compensation granted by the Tribunal, reduced the same without giving any reason - HC introduced the concept of split multiplier and departed from the multiplier used by the Tribunal without disclosing any reason therefore - HC had also not considered the clear and corroborative evidence about the prospect of future increment of the deceased - When the age of the deceased was between 51 and 55 years the multiplier was 11, which was specified in the Column II in the Schedule II to the Motor Vehicles Act and the Tribunal had not committed any error by accepting the said multiplier - HC impugned order, therefore, was set aside as it was perverse and clearly contrary to the evidence on record - Appeals allowed.
