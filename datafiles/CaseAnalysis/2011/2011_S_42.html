State Of Madhya Pradesh & Ors. v Satyavrata Taran

Supreme Court of India

01 December 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Dattu, Chandramauli Kumar Prasad

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 802; (2012) 2 SCC 83; AIR 2012 SC (Supp) 147; 2012 (132) FLR 577; 2012 (1) JabLJ 341; [2011] 15 S.C.R. 469; 2012 (3) SLR 4

Case Digest

Subject: Education; Service

Keywords: Madhya Pradesh Educational Service (Collegiate Branch) Recruitment Rules, 1967

Summary: Education - Service - Recruitment Rules, 1967, r. 13(5) - Emergency Appointment - Regularization - Benefit claim - Senior/selection grade pay scale - Respondent was appointed as Assistant Professor on emergency basis on a condition of immediate termination without notice on availability of Public Service Commission's panel of selected candidates u/r. 13(5) of the Rules - Respondent cleared the exam and was regularized as per appointment order - State Government had issued a Circular for addition of period of service, prior to service in present College or University as Assistant Professor for conferring benefit of senior/selection grade pay scale but subject to certain conditions - Respondent made several representation to count his emergency appointment for grant of claim, however, didn't receive any reply from appellant department - Respondent filed a writ petition before HC - HC allowed writ petition - Aggrieved State Government filed writ appeal before HC - HC in its impugned order dismissed writ appeal - Hence the instant appeal - Whether Assistant Professors appointed through different means, modes and sources including emergency appointees in terms of r. 13(5) of the Rules, were entitled to claim benefit of the services rendered prior to their regularization for grant of senior/selection grade pay scales - Held, decisions of HC was that service rendered by Assistant Professor appointed on emergency basis requires to be counted for purpose of granting benefit of higher pay scale - Appointment of respondent were made after following due selection procedure hence, such appointments were not in nature of temporary or ad hoc appointments but emergency appointments in accordance with Rules, therefore, respondent was entitled to receive higher pay scale from the date of initial appointment as emergency employees - Respondent was qualified for the post and was getting annual increments for continuous service from the date of initial appointment - Respondent, as emergency appointee fulfilled all conditions envisaged in Clause 8(a) of the 1999 Circular and was regularly working without any artificial breaks and were paid UGC pay scale and was eligible for pensionary benefits - It was taken that past period of service rendered, was a 'stagnation removal device' and there was no reason for denial - Emergency appointments were made in view of non- availability of selected panel candidates , however respondents continued in service till regularization - Based on the observersation SC remanded matters back to HC for fresh disposal in accordance to law - Appeal allowed.
