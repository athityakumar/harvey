Balbir Singh v State Of Haryana & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

31 March 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

A.R. Dave, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 251; (2011) 15 SCC 277; 2011 (5) AWC 4772; JT 2011 (4) SC 4; 2011 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 3074; 2011(4) SCALE 154; 2011 (2) SLR 719

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Appointment - Challenged - Appellant filed writ petition submitting that he was wrongly not appointed as an Art and Craft teacher and certain candidates belonging to backward class 'A' category had been given appointment against the posts of general category and had it not been done so, the appellant would have got the appointment - Appellant also submitted that certain candidates who had secured same marks as secured by the appellant, were given appointment whereas he was not appointed to the post in question - HC dismissed said writ petition - Hence, the instant appeal - Whether HC rightly dismissed writ petition filed by the appellant? - Held, it was true that some persons belonging to the backward class 'A' category had been appointed along with the candidates of general category because they had secured more marks than the cut off marks determined for selecting the candidates of the general category and, therefore, on their merit they were given appointment - Said action of the appointing authority was in consonance with the law laid down by SC - Moreover, appellant had secured 62 marks whereas the last candidate of the general category who was selected had secured 71 marks - Hence, it cannot be said that any candidate who had secured lesser marks than the appellant had been given appointment as an Art and Craft teacher so far as the general category was concerned - Impugned order passed by the HC upheld - Appeal dismissed.
