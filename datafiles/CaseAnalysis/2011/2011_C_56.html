Union Of India v Col. L.S.N. Murthy & Anr

Supreme Court of India

23 November 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Patnaik, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 714; (2012) 1 SCC 718; 2012(1) ALL MR 963; 2012 (2) AWC 1591; 2011 (4) CLT(SC) 424; 2012 (1) CTC 329; 2012 (1) MLJ(SC) 819; 2012 (3) RCR(Civil) 629; 2011(13) SCALE 67; [2011] 13 S.C.R. 295

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Contract & Commercial - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, s. 34 - Arbitral award - Legality - Appellant entered into purchasing agreement with 2nd respondent contractor for supply of fruits to its troops - 2nd respondent supplied fruits for sometime but stopped supply due to price increase - Appellant rescinded the contract - Matter was referred to arbitration - Arbitrator passed award in favour of 2nd respondent - Appellant challenged the award before the Civil Court u/s. 34 of the Act - Civil Court dismissed appellant's application - Appellant appealed to HC and the same had been dismissed by HC - Appellant contended that the arbitration clause that was part of the contract could not be invoked since the arbitrator had held in Issue No. 4 of the award that the contract was void ab initio and thus, was not enforceable due to quoting of reasonable rates below 20% as against the instruction contained in letter dated 31-8-1990 issued by the Government of India - Whether the contract between the appellant and the 2nd respondent was legally enforceable - Held, unless the effect of an agreement resulted in performance of an unlawful act, an agreement which was otherwise legal should not be held to be void and if the effect of an agreement did not result in performance of an unlawful act, as a matter of public policy, the court should refuse to declare the contract void with a view to save the bargain entered into by the parties and the solemn promises made thereunder - Arbitrator was, therefore, not right in law in coming to the conclusion that the agreement between the appellant and the 2nd respondent was void and not enforceable as the consideration or object of the agreement was hit by the letter dated 31-8-1990 of the Government of India, Ministry of Defence - Such letter would be an instruction to the officers of the Defence Department to reject a tender where the rate quoted by the tenderor was more than 20% below the reasonable rates but the letter was not an Act of the legislature declaring that any supply made at a rate below 20% of the reasonable rates was unlawful - Finding of the arbitrator on Issue No.4 was thus patently illegal and opposed to public policy - Hence, award of the arbitrator and the judgments of the Civil Court and the HC were set aside and the matter was remitted to the arbitrator for deciding the claims of the appellant and the 2nd respondent - Appeal allowed.
