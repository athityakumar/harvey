A. Subash Babu v State Of A.P.& Anr.

Supreme Court of India

21 July 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Panchal, H.L. Gokhale

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 459; (2011) 7 SCC 616; 2011 ALL MR (Cri) 2931; 2011 CRLJ 4373; 2011 (3) JCC 2189; 2011 (4) MLJ(Crl) 88; 2011 (3) RCR(Criminal) 674; 2011(7) SCALE 671; [2011] 9 S.C.R. 453

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Bigamy, Code of Criminal Procedure (Andhra Pradesh Second Amendment) Act, 1992

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 482 - Indian Penal Code, 1860,ss. 417, 494, 495 and 498A - Quashing of criminal complaint - Legality - Respondent no. 2 (complainant) alleged that the appellant concealed the fact of former marriage from her - Feeling aggrieved respondent no. 2 lodged FIR against the appellant for alleged commission of offences u/ss. 498A and 420 of IPC - After investigation, charge sheet was filed against appellant for commission of offences punishable u/ss. 494, 495, 417, 420 and 498A of IPC - On receipt of the charge sheet the Magistrate took cognizance of the offences and summoned the appellant - Appellant filed Criminal Petition for quashing the criminal proceedings - HC held that taking cognizance of the offences punishable u/ss. 417, 420, 494 and 495 of IPC was in accordance with law, but the respondent no. 2 was second wife and therefore prima facie marriage between appellant and the respondent no. 2 was void and therefore, offence u/s. 498A of IPC was not made out against the appellant - HC partly allowed the petition by quashing proceedings insofar as offence punishable u/s. 498A of IPC whereas the proceedings relating to the offences punishable u/ss. 494, 495, 417 and 420 IPC were ordered to continue against the appellant - Hence, instant appeal - (A) Whether a woman with whom second marriage was performed was not entitled to maintain a complaint u/s. 494 of IPC? - Held, offence mentioned in s. 495 of IPC was an aggravated form of bigamy provided in s. 494 of IPC - S. 495 of IPC was extension of s. 494 of IPC and part and parcel of it - Concealment spoken of in s. 495 of IPC would be from the woman with whom the subsequent marriage was performed - Therefore, the wife with whom the subsequent marriage was contracted after concealment of former marriage, would also be entitled to lodge complaint for commission of offence punishable u/s. 495 of IPC - Further, where second wife alleged that the accused husband had married her according to Hindu rites despite the fact that he was already married to another lady and the factum of the first marriage was concealed from her, the second wife would be an aggrieved person within the meaning of s. 198 of CrPC - Hence, woman with whom second marriage was contracted by suppressing the fact of former marriage would be entitled to maintain complaint against her husband u/ss. 494 and 495 of IPC - In the instant case, complaint against the appellant for commission of offence punishable u/s. 494 and 495 of IPC was maintainable - (B) Whether amendment made in the First Schedule to the CrPC by the Code of Criminal Procedure (Andhra Pradesh Second Amendment) Act, 1992, shall prevail in the State of Andhra Pradesh? - Held, Yes - Once First Schedule to the CrPC was amended and offences punishable u/ss. 494 and 495 of IPC were made cognizable offences, those offences would have to be regarded as cognizable offences for all purposes of the CrPC including for the purpose of s. 198 of the CrPC - Hence, s. 198(1)(c), after the Amendment made by the Code of Criminal Procedure (Andhra Pradesh Second Amendment) Act, 1992 could not be interpreted in isolation without referring to the fact that offences u/ss. 494 and 495 of IPC have been made cognizable so far as the State of Andhra Pradesh was concerned - Therefore, the provision made in s. 198(1)(c) that no Court shall take cognizance of an offences punishable under Chapter XX of the IPC except upon a complaint made by some person aggrieved would have to be read subject to the amendment made by the Legislative Assembly of the State of Andhra Pradesh in 1992 - Amendment made in the First Schedule to the CrPC by the Code of Criminal Procedure (Andhra Pradesh Second Amendment) Act, 1992, shall prevail in the State of Andhra Pradesh, notwithstanding the fact that in the CrPC offences u/s. 494 and 495 are treated as cognizable offences - In the instant case, HC was not justified in quashing the proceedings initiated against the appellant u/s. 498A of the CrPC on the ground that the respondent no. 2 was not wife within the meaning of s. 498A of the IPC and was not entitled to maintain complaint under the said provision - Impugned Judgment quashing the complaint filed by the respondent no. 2 for alleged commission of offence by the appellant u/s. 498A of IPC, was set aside and the complaint lodged by the respondent no. 2 u/s. 498A of the IPC as well as charge sheet submitted by the Investigating Officer for the same shall stand restored/revived - Appeal disposed of.
