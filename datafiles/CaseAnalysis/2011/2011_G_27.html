Goa Housing Board v Rameshchandra Govind Pawaskar & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

11 October 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, P. Sathasivam, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 731; (2011) 10 SCC 371; AIR 2012 SC 193; 2012 (1) Bom.C.R. 433; 2012 (2) RCR(Civil) 267; [2011] 14 S.C.R. 735

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Goa, Daman and Diu Land Revenue Code, 1968, Goa Land Use (Regulations) Act, 1991, Goa, Daman and Diu Town & Country Planning Act, 1974

Summary: Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Goa, Daman and Diu Agricultural Tenancy Act, 1964, ss. 18A, 18C, 18D, 18H, 18K - Goa, Daman and Diu Town & Country Planning Act, 1974, ss. 13, 15, 17 - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 23, 23(1A), 23(2), 24(8), 28, 24(8) - Goa Land Use (Regulation) Act, 1991 - Public purpose - Acquisition - Compensation - Sustainability - Respondent was declared tenant under the 1964 Act and respondent land was acquired - Land Acquisition Officer (LAO) determined compensation payable to respondent - Reference was made to Civil Court for claiming higher compensation by respondent - Civil Court affirmed award of LAO - Respondent filed appeal before HC wherein compensation was awarded -- Hence, instant appeal - Whether acquired land should be valued only as agricultural land or as land with development potential for being used as building sites and Whether compensation awarded by HC was excessive- Held, it might not be possible for a tenant/purchaser to apply for conversion to non- agricultural use - Thus, land in question was agricultural land as on date whenthe 1991 ? Act came into force and when land was acquired under the 1894 Act and therefore contention that it was not agricultural land was rejected - Once Land Use Act came into force, notwithstanding anything contained in the 1974 Act or in any plan or scheme made thereunder, a land vested in a tenant under the 1964 Act could not be used or allowed to be used for any purpose other than agriculture - However, market value was determined Rs.75 per sq.m. as on dt. 23-3-1990 and increased it by Rs.35 to arrive at value as Rs.110/- after 4 years, as on dt. 16-6-1994 - In instant case, relevant date for purpose of determination of market value was dt. 26-9-1991, about one and half years after dt. 23-3-1990 - Thus, by applying same principle market value of land as on dt. 26-9-1991 would be Rs.90 per sq.m. - Value was with reference to land with potential for development - Land acquired was subject to prohibition under the 1991 Act, in first two appeals a deduction of 50% was made for to arrive at value of land with agricultural potential only - Market value of acquired land is determined as Rs.45/- per sq.m. - Hence, reduce compensation from Rs.140 per sq.m. to Rs.45 per sq.m. - Thus, respondent would be entitled to said compensation with all statutory benefits u/ss. 23(1A), 23(2), 28 of the 1894 Act - Appeals disposed of.
