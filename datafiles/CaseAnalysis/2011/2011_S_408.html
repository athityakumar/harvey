Chief Information Commissioner and another v State of Manipur and another

Supreme Court of India

12 December 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Ganguly, Gyan Sudha Misra

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 808; (2011) 15 SCC 1; AIR 2012 SC 864; 2012 (3) ALD(SC) 1; 2012 (92) ALR 494; 2012 (1) AWC 596; 2012 (286) E.L.T. 485; 2012 (2) MLJ(SC) 442; 2012 (1) RCR(Civil) 374; 2012 (116) RD 505; 2012 (1) RLW 517; [2011] 13 S.C.R. 505; [2012] 204 TAXMAN 151

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Central Information Commission (Appeal Procedure) Rules, 2005

Summary: Constitution - Practice & Procedure - Right to Information Act, 2005, ss. 18 and 19 - Central Information Commission (Appeal Procedure) Rules, 2005, r. 5 - Jurisdiction - Information - Refusal - Procedure - Appellant 2 filed application before state information officer u/s. 6 of Act relating to magisterial enquiries initiated by Government - Appellant 2 did not receive any response and therefore he filed complaint u/s. 18 of Act before State Chief Information Commissioner - Commissioner directed respondent 2 to furnish information within 15 days, said order was challenged by respondent by filing Writ Petition - Appellant 2 again filed complaint for obtaining similar information but did not receive any response - Complaint was filed by appellant u/s. 18 of Act, same was disposed of by directing disclosure of the information sought for within 15 days - Order was challenged by way of Writ Petition by respondents - Both Writ Petitions were heard together by single judge and were dismissed by a common order by upholding the order of Commissioner - Aggrieved by order, appellant filed appeal before DB - DB held that u/s. 18 of the act, Commissioner had no power to direct respondent to furnish information and such a power was already conferred on commissioner u/s. 19 (8) of the Act and also held that the direction to furnish information was without jurisdiction and directed the Commissioner to dispose of complaints in accordance with law - Hence, instant appeal - Respondent contended that u/s. 18 of the Act Central Information Commission or State Information Commission had no power to provide access to the information which was requested for by any person but which was denied to appellant 2 - Whether Commissioner had jurisdiction to pass an order providing for access to information u/s. 18 of Act - Held, Commissioner while entertaining a complaint u/s. 18 of Act had no jurisdiction to pass an order providing for access to the information and person who was aggrieved by refusal in receiving the information which he sought for could only seek redress in the manner provided in the statute i.e. procedure u/s. 19 of Act - Therefore, when the statute confers such right of appeal that must be exercised by a person who was aggrieved by reason of refusal to be furnished with the information - Hence, direction issued to file appeals u/s. 19 of Act in respect of requests by appellant 2 for obtaining information - Appeals disposed of.
