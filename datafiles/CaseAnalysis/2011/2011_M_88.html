Mangesh v State Of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

05 January 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 7; (2011) 2 SCC 123; 2011 ALL MR (Cri) 629; 2011 CRLJ 1166; 2011 (1) JCC 330; JT 2011 (1) SC 15; 2011 (2) MLJ(Crl) 106; 2011 (5) RCR(Criminal) 109; 2011(1) SCALE 57; [2011] 1 S.C.R. 72

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302 and 304 - Murder - Conviction - Challenged - Appellant on seeing deceased and his sister together stabbed the deceased - Appellant was charged u/s. 302 of IPC - Trial Court held that there was no material contradiction in both the dying declarations and the conviction could be based solely on the said dying declarations, and convicted appellant u/s. 302 of IPC - Conviction affirmed by HC - Hence, present appeal - It was submitted that as the act of the appellant was not pre-meditated and the incident happened because of sudden provocation, conviction could be only u/s. 304, Part I IPC and not u/s. 302 of IPC - Held, it was not the case even in any of the dying declarations that the appellant had premeditated or preplanned his actions or was having any information prior to the incident that the deceased would be found with his sister at the place of occurrence - Therefore, it was a clear case of loss of self control because of which appellant caused injuries to deceased - It cannot be held to be a case of premeditation - Moreover, appellant did not cause all the injuries on the vital part of the body - Nor the appellant caused the fatal injury with full force - Also, death of deceased was not instantaneous rather he died on third day of the incident - Appellant had not taken any undue advantage or acted in cruel or in unusual manner - Hence, conviction of the appellant altered from s. 302 of IPC to s. 304 Part-I of IPC - Appeal disposed of.
