Bar Council of Maharashtra and Goa and another v Manubhai Paragji Vashi And Others

Supreme Court of India

13 October 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Patnaik, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 741; (2012) 1 SCC 314; AIR 2012 SC 135; 2012(1) ALL MR 426; 2012 (1) AWC 424; 2012 (3) CTC 316; 2012 (2) MahLJ 574; 2012 (1) MLJ(SC) 982; 2012 (1) MLJ(SC) 989; 2012 (1) RCR(Civil) 870; 2012 (3) SLR 29

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Election

Keywords: Power To Make Rules, Cantonment Electoral Rules, 2007, Bar Council of India Rules, 1975, Bar Council of Maharashtra and Goa Rules

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Election - Constitution of India, 1950 - Advocates Act, 1961 - Bar Council of India Rules, 1975 - State Bar council Rules, r.40, r.6(h) - State Bar Council - Bar Elections - Vote - Electoral rolls - Candidate - Electoral roll to Bar council of Maharashtra and Goa were prepared - Names of Advocates on roll of State Bar Council who had not paid subscription as per r. 40, Chapter -II, Part VI of the 1975 Rules were deleted from Electoral Roll because as per r. 6 (h) of State Bar council Rules, if name of an Advocate appearing in State Bar Council Roll should not be on Electoral Roll if he had not paid subscription u/r. 40, Chapter - II, Part VI of State Bar council Rules and obtained receipt from State Bar Council - Aggrieved, respondent No.1 /Petitioner filed writ petition and prayed to allow advocates to vote and contest elections without being disqualified for non-payment of amounts as per r. 40, of the Rules - While writ petition remained pending, elections results declared - Respondent/ Petitioner name was elected - Further, respondent/ petitioner amended writ petition and prayed for setting aside of election - Hence, instant petition - Whether election to State Bar Council could be set aside and whether rr. 6 (h) and 32(g) of State Bar council Rules could be struck down and could be held as ultra vires powers of State Bar Council - Held, u/r. 31 of the Rules a voter should be entitled to mark his preferences to all candidates and r. 32 (g) of the Rules provided that a voting paper should be invalid in which preferences to less than ten candidates were communicated - In Bar Council of Delhi and Others v. Surjeet Singh and Others 1980 Indlaw SC 308 relied on - Hence, r. 32(g) held was ultra vires - Petition dismissed.
