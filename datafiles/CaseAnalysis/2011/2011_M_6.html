M. Nagabhushana v State Of Karnataka & Others

Supreme Court of India

02 February 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Ganguly, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 75; (2011) 3 SCC 408; AIR 2011 SC 1113; 2011 (2) ALD(SC) 117; 2011(2) ALL MR 423; 2011 (271) E.L.T. 481; JT 2011 (2) SC 23; 2011 (3) MLJ(SC) 982; 2012 (1) RCR(Civil) 807; 2011(2) SCALE 160; [2011] 2 S.C.R. 435; 2012 (27) S.T.R. 81

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Public Demand Recovery Act, Karnataka Acquisition of Land For Grant of House Sites Act, 1972

Summary: Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, s. 11A, 16 - Karnataka Industrial Areas Development Act, 1966, s. 28 - Land acquisition under 1966 Act - Failure to pass award u/s. 11A of 1894 Act - Legality - Appellant's lands were acquired pursuant to a notification issued u/ss. 28(1) and 28(4) of the 1966 Act - Appellant challenged the acquisition proceedings before the HC - Single Judge dismissed appellant's writ petition on the ground that the acquisition proceedings in question were challenged by the appellant in a previous writ proceedings, which was upheld by HC and by SC in respective appeals - Appellant filed writ appeal - DB dismissed writ appeals holding that the second round of litigation was misconceived inasmuch as the acquisition proceedings were upheld right up to SC - (A) Whether the instant appeal was barred by principles of res judicata - Held, attempt by the appellant to re-agitate the same issues which were considered by SC and were rejected expressly in a previous judgment, was a clear instance of an abuse of process of SC apart from the fact that such issues were barred by principles of res judicata or constructive res judicata and principles analogous thereto - (B) Whether notification for acquisition issued u/s. 28(4) of the 1966 Act stood vitiated in view of the provisions of s. 11A of the 1894 Act inasmuch as no award was passed within two years from the date of the notification - Held, appellant had not challenged the validity of the provisions of s. 28(4) and 28(5) of the 1966 Act - Further, on the publication of the notification u/s. 28(4) of the 1966 Act, the land in question vested in the State free from all encumbrances by operation of s. 28(5) of the 1966 Act, whereas the land acquired under the 1894 Act vested only u/s. 16 thereof - Further, land which was subject to acquisition proceeding under the 1894 Act got vested with the Government only when the Collector made an award u/s. 11 of the 1894 Act, and the Government took possession, however, u/ss. 28(4) and 28(5) of the 1966 Act, such vesting takes place by operation of law and it had nothing to do with the making of any award - Further, 1894 Act and 1966 Act were enacted to achieve substantially different purposes and in so far as 1966 Act was concerned, from its Statement of Objects and Reasons, it was clear that the same was enacted to achieve the purpose of planned industrial development of the State - Therefore, acquisition under 1966 Act would not be elapsed for alleged non- compliance with the provisions of s. 11A of the 1894 Act - No interference on HC order - Appeal dismissed.
