State of Punjab and another v Davinder Pal Singh Bhullar & Ors. Etc.

Supreme Court of India

07 December 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 843; (2011) 14 SCC 770; 2012 CRLJ 1001; 2012 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 513; 2012 (1) RCR(Criminal) 126; [2011] 15 S.C.R. 540

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Power Of The Court, Andhra Pradesh Forest Act

Summary: (A) Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 362 - Terrorist attempt - Arrest - Aggrieved - Applicability of provision - After completion of investigation, police charge sheeted eight persons to revealed that attempt was made by terrorists on life of SSP by using explosives - Trial Court vide its order acquitted three accused giving them benefit of doubt - Aggrieved, State preferred criminal miscellaneous before HC challenging said acquittal, however, appeal was dismissed - Hence, instant Appeals - Whether HC could pass order on application entertained after final disposal of criminal appeal or even suo motu particularly, in view of provisions of s. 362 of CrPC - Held, there was no power of review with Criminal Court after judgment had been rendered - HC could alter or review its judgment before it was signed - When order was passed, it could not be reviewed - S. 362 CrPC was based on acknowledged principle of law that once matter was finally disposed of by Court, said Court in absence of specific statutory provision becomes functus officio and was disentitled to entertain fresh prayer for any relief unless former order of final disposal was set aside by Court of competent jurisdiction in manner prescribed by law - Court becomes functus officio moment order for disposing of case was signed - Provided that judgment had been pronounced without jurisdiction or in violation of principles of natural justice or where order had been pronounced without giving opportunity of being heard to party affected by it or where order was obtained by abuse of process of Court which would really amount to its being without jurisdiction, inherent powers could be exercised to recall such order for reason that in such eventuality order becomes nullity and provisions of s. 362 of CrPC would not operate - Appeal allowed.

(B) Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 482 - Inherent jurisdiction - Investigation - Statutory provision - Whether in exercise of inherent jurisdiction u/s. 482 of CrPC HC could ask particular investigating agency to investigate case following particular procedure through exceptionally unusual method which was not in consonance with statutory provisions of CrPC - Held, inherent power u/s. 482 of CrPC was intended to prevent the abuse of process of Court and to secure ends of justice - Such power could not be exercised to do something which was expressly barred under CrPC - Provided that any consideration of facts by way of review was not permissible under CrPC and was expressly barred, it was not for Court to exercise its inherent power to reconsider matter and record conflicting decision - Inherent power of court u/s. 482 of CrPC was saved only where order had been passed by criminal Court which was required to be set aside to secure the ends of justice or where the proceeding pending before a court, amounts to abuse of the process of court - Court could direct CBI investigation only in exceptional circumstances where court was of view that accusation was against person who by virtue of his post could influence investigation - Hence, orders impugned being nullity, could not be sustained - Appeal allowed.
