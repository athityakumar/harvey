Commissioner Of Trade Tax, U.P. v Varun Beverages Limited

Supreme Court of India

11 April 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Mukundakam Sharma, Anil R. Dave

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 262; (2011) 11 SCC 308; AIR 2012 SC (Supp) 573; 2011 (267) E.L.T. 147; JT 2011 (4) SC 466; 2011(4) SCALE 625; [2011] 4 S.C.R. 803; [2011] 40 VST 240

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Keywords: Eligibility Certificate, Uttar Pradesh Trade Tax Act, 1948

Summary: Sales Tax - Indirect Tax - U.P. Trade Tax Act, 1948, s. 4A(4) - Exemption - Fixed capital investment - Bottles and creates - Assessee, engaged in manufacturing and sale of soft drink and beverages, applied for the grant of eligibility certificate u/s. 4A of the Act - Said eligibility certificate was granted to the assessee for a period of 10 years - Subsequently, assessee sought extension of the period from 10 years to 15 years and exemptions for fixed capital investment made by it in glass bottles and crates claiming that these items were essential for the manufacture of soft drinks and for running a beverage unit - Divisional level Committee allowed said claim sought by the assessee - Tribunal allowed appeal filed against said order and held that the bottles and crates were neither directly nor indirectly used in the manufacture of beverages and therefore the same cannot be treated as 'Apparatus' and cannot be exempted under explanation (4) to s. 4A of the Act - HC allowed revision petition filed against said order and held that for the manufacture of soft drink, the bottles and crates were essential apparatus, therefore, both bottles and crates were to be accepted as 'apparatus' within the meaning of Explanation (4) (b) (i) to s. 4-A of the Act - Hence, the instant appeal - Whether bottles and crates used by the assessee could be said to be essential apparatus or equipments or components under explanation (4) to s. 4A of the Act, for the establishment and running of the factory of the assessee? - Held, bottles were essential part of components and equipments necessary for the running of the factory, therefore, such value of the investment would form part of the fixed capital investment and would be entitled to exemption as provided for - But so far crates were concerned they were used by the assessee only for the purpose of marketing - Use of crates was necessary for taking out the bottled beverages out of the factory and while doing the marketing of the sealed bottled beverages - Therefore, crates have no user so far as running of the factory of the assessee - Hence, the value of crates cannot be deemed to be investment for the purpose of including it within the meaning of expression 'Fixed Capital Investment' as per sub-section (4) of s. 4-A of the Act - Revenue's appeal partly allowed.
