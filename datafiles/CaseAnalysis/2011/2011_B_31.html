Bhagaloo Lodh & Anr v State Of U.P.

Supreme Court of India

14 June 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, Swatanter Kumar

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 384; (2011) 13 SCC 206; AIR 2011 SC 2292; 2011 ALL MR (Cri) 2373; JT 2011 (6) SC 498; 2012 (1) MLJ(Crl) 298; 2011 (5) RCR(Criminal) 780; 2011 (3) RLW 2152; 2011 (4) RLW 2961; 2011(6) SCALE 505; [2011] 6 S.C.R. 1037

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34 and 302 - Murder - FIR - Delay in lodging of - Testimony of close relatives - Reliability of - FIR was lodged by PW.1 against the appellants and two other co-accused for the offence punishable u/s. 302 r/w s. 34 of IPC - Sessions Court convicted appellants u/s. 302 r/w s. 34 of IPC - Conviction was affirmed by HC - Hence, instant appeal - (A) Delay in lodging of FIR - Effect thereof - Held, in case there was delay in filing the FIR, the complainant must give explanation for the same - In absence of such an explanation, the delay may give presumption that allegations/accusations were false and had been given after thought or had given a coloured version of events - In the instant case, delay in lodging the FIR was satisfactorily explained by PW.1 - (B) Evidence of close relative - Reliability - Appellants contended that the eye-witnesses were very close relatives of the deceased, and thus, their testimonies could not be relied upon - Held, evidence of a close relation could be relied upon provided it was trustworthy - Such evidence was required to be carefully scrutinised and appreciated before resting of conclusion to convict the accused in a given case - Evidence could not be disbelieved merely on the ground that the witnesses were inter-related to each other or to the deceased - In the instant case, courts below held that evidence of both the eye-witnesses inspired confidence and was worth acceptance as both of them had given full version of the incident - No fault found with the evidence recorded by the courts below accepting the evidence of closely related witnesses - Appeal dismissed.
