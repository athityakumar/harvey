Trilok Sudhirbhai Pandya and others v Union of India and another

Supreme Court of India

13 October 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Patnaik, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 748; (2011) 10 SCC 203; AIR 2012 SC 1668; 2013 (1) AWC 126; [2011] 12 S.C.R. 1106; 2011 (8) SLR 726; 2011 (4) WLN(SC) 46

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Petroleum and Mineral Pipelines (Acquisition of Right of User in Land) Act, 1962, Petroleum Pipelines (Acquisition of Right of User in Land) Amendment Act, 1977

Summary: Service - Practice & Procedure - Petroleum and Mineral Pipelines (Acquisition of Right of User in Land) Act, 1962, ss. 2(a), 5, 6, 6(1), 10, 11, 12 - Petroleum Pipelines (Acquisition of Right of User in Land) Amendment Act, 1977 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Competent Authority - Compensation - Bias - Sustainability - State Govt. with approval of Central Govt. appointed Competent Authority for determination of compensation payable under the 1962 Act for acquisition of right of user in respect of properties - Expenses of pay and allowances and any other incidentals of Competent Authority was to be borne by respondent no.4-company to appellant from date of joining in respondent no.4-company - Competent Authority issued notices to appellants for acquisition of right of user of their properties - Objections raised by appellants to Competent Authority sitting at premises of respondent no. 4 as claim for compensation was in respect of acquisition of right of user for project of respondent no. 4 were rejected - Appellants filed writ petitions before HC challenging notification appointing Competent Authority which were dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether Competent Authority was likely to act with bias as his pay and allowances and all other incidentals were being borne by respondent no.4-company - Held, Ranjit Thakur v. Union of India and Others 1987 Indlaw SC 28438 relied on - SC held that test of likelihood of bias was whether there was reasonable apprehension in mind of party before Court or Tribunal that Court or Tribunal would not act with fairness and without bias on account of certain objective circumstances - Since salary, allowances, accommodation and transport were being borne by respondent no . 4-company directly there were grounds for appellants to entertain reasonable apprehension that Competent Authority would not act fairly and was likely to act with bias - Judgment which was result of bias or want of impartiality was nullity and trial coram non-judice - Thus, entire proceedings for determination of compensation before Competent Authority would be nullity - Hence, impugned orders were set aside - Appeals allowed.
