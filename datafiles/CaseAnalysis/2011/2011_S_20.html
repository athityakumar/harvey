Samittri Devi And Another. v Sampuran Singh And Another

Supreme Court of India

21 January 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Gokhale, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 55; (2011) 3 SCC 556; AIR 2011 SC 773; 2011 (4) ALD(SC) 6; 2011 (85) ALR 462; 2011 (3) AWC 2368; 2011 (1) CLT(SC) 196; 2011 (2) CTC 820; JT 2011 (1) SC 430; 2011 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 497; 2011 (5) MLJ(SC) 343; 2011 (3) MPLJ 1; 2011 (1) RCR(Civil) 860; 2011 (113) RD 500; 2011(1) SCALE 605; [2011] 2 S.C.R. 196

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Benami Transactions (Prohibition) Act, 1988, s. 4 - Benami transaction - Recovery - Entitlement - 1st appellant purchased the suit property in the names of 2nd appellant and 2nd respondent and the sale deed executed to that effect - 2nd respondent proposed to sell the suit property to 1st respondent - 1st appellant, on knowing about the proposal of sale, send a letter dated 8-4-1987 to the 1st respondent informing him that she was the real owner of the suit property - 2nd respondent, subsequently, sold the suit property to 1st respondent on 13-8-1987 without 1st appellant's consent - 1st appellant filed a suit for declaration and for permanent injunction - Trial Court held that prohibition u/s. 4 of the Act to recover the Benami property was applicable to suits, claims or action pending on the date of commencement of the 1988 Act by relying upon a decision of the SC in Mithilesh Kumari and Anr. v. Prem Behari Khare, 1989 INDLAW SC 736 - Since 1st appellant's suit was pending on the date of commencement of the 1988 Act, Trial Court dismissed the suit - 1st appellant filed appeal - First Appellate Court decreed the suit in favour of 1st respondent holding that since the appellant send a notice to 1st respondent prior to the alleged sale, 1st respondent was not a bona fide purchaser of the suit property - 1st respondent filed second appeal before the HC - HC, by impugned order, set aside the First Appellate Court order - Hence the present appeal - Whether the suit of the 1st appellant for the recovery of suit property filed prior to the 1988 Act coming into force could be considered to be prohibited by s. 4 of that Act - Held, SC, in R. Rajagopal Reddy v. Padmini Chandrasekharan, 1995 INDLAW SC 2016, overruled the Mithlesh Kumari case and held that suits filed prior to the application of the Act would not be hit by the prohibition u/s. 4 of the act - HC clearly erred in ignoring the three Judges Bench decision of SC in R. Rajagopal Reddy case - Further, HC committed a serious error of law in holding that the First Appellate Court had misread the evidence on record while coming to the conclusion that the suit property was the Benami Property of the 1st appellant and that her suit to enforce the right concerning the same should not lie - 1st appellant purchased the suit property out of her own funds -1st appellant on knowing about the clandestine sale of her property to 1st respondent sent a notice dated 8-4-1987 to 1st respondent - Agreement of purchase was signed by the 1st respondent five days thereafter i.e. 13-4-1987 - Appellant produced a copy of the notice along with postal certificate in evidence and there was no allegation that the postal certificate was procured - In such circumstances, it could certainly be presumed that the notice was duly served on 1st respondent before 13-4-1987 u/s. 114 of the Indian Evidence Act, 1872 - HC, therefore, erred in interfering in the findings rendered by the First Appellate Court that 1st respondent did receive the notice and, therefore, was not a bona fide purchaser for value without a notice - Impugned HC order is set aside - Appeal allowed.
