Kaushalya Devi Massand v Roopkishore Khore

Supreme Court of India

15 March 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 224; (2011) 4 SCC 593; AIR 2011 SC 2566; 2011 ALL MR (Cri) 2067; 2012 (1) BC 466; 2011 CRLJ 2593; 2011 (2) JabLJ 203; 2011 (3) JCC (NI) 165; 2011 (2) RCR(Civil) 390; 2011 (2) RCR(Criminal) 298; 2011 (4) RLW 3029; 2011(3) SCALE 574; [2011] 3 S.C.R. 879

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, s. 138 - Compensation in lieu of sentence - Respondent was convicted u/s. 138 of the Negotiable Instruments Act - Magistrate was of the view that imposition of a fine payable as compensation to the appellant was sufficient to meet the ends of justice in the instant case - Magistrate, accordingly, sentenced the respondent to pay a fine of Rs. 4 lakhs which was to be paid to the appellant as compensation - Whether a jail sentence for an offence u/s. 138 of the Negotiable Instruments Act was not mandatory and it was within the discretion of the Magistrate to award a sentence of fine only, as was done in the instant case? - Held, gravity of a complaint under the Negotiable Instruments Act could not be equated with an offence under the provisions of the Indian Penal Code or other criminal offences - No interference required with the order of the Magistrate, as confirmed by the HC, with an increased fine - Appeal party allowed.
