K.K.Velusamy v N.Palanisamy

Supreme Court of India

30 March 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 200; (2011) 11 SCC 275; 2011(3) ALL MR 455; 2011 (86) ALR 457; 2011 (3) AWC 3104; 2011 (2) CLT(SC) 21; 2011 (3) CTC 422; JT 2011 (4) SC 38; 2011 (7) MLJ(SC) 612; 2011 (2) OLR 13; 2011 (2) RCR(Civil) 875; 2011 (113) RD 414; 2011 (3) RLW 2457; 2011(4) SCALE 61; [2011] 4 S.C.R. 31

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Code of Civil Procedure, 1908-Section 151 and Order XVIII, Rule 17-Evidence Act, 1872-Section 8-Suit for specific performance of contract-Reopening of evidence-Recall of witness-Plaintiff alleging agreement for sale executed by defendant-But defendant not executing sale deed-Defence denying that amount advanced by plaintiff was as advance consideration for sale-But it was loan-When arguments in progress defendant/appellant filed two applications-One under Section 151 with prayer to reopen evidence for further cross-examination of plaintiff (P.W. 1) and attesting witness (P.W. 2)-Other application filed under Order XVIII, Rule 17 for recalling P.Ws. 1 and 2 for further cross-examination-Appellant wanted to cross-examine witnesses with reference to admissions made during some conversations-Recorded in compact disc (electronic record)-Rejection of application by trial court-Affirmed by High Court on revision-Electronic record can be received in evidence under Section 8 of Evidence Act-Scope and applicability of Section 151 and Order XVIII, Rule 17 delineated-In interest of justice and to prevent abuse of process of Court-Application under Section 151 ought to have been allowed-Order dismissing application under Section 151 set aside-Application to be considered afresh in accordance with law-However, rejection of application under Order XVIII, Rule 17 upheld.
