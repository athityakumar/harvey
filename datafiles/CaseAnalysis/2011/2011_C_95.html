Coromandel Indag Products (Private) Limited v Garuda Chit and Trading Company Private Limited and another

Supreme Court of India

16 August 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, H.L. Gokhale

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 576; (2011) 8 SCC 601; 2011 (6) AWC 5899; 2011 (3) CLT(SC) 355; 2011 (8) MLJ(SC) 112; 2011 (4) RCR(Civil) 677; [2011] 12 S.C.R. 115

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Keywords: Income-tax Act, 1981, Urban Land (Ceiling and Regulation) Ordinance, 1976

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Specific Relief Act, 1963, s. 16(c) - Urban Land (Ceiling and Regulation) Act, 1976 - Agreement of Sale - Specific performance - Validity- Appellant-company carrying on business in pesticides, chemicals required property for establishing Research and Development Centre - Respondent-Company, offered property measuring 12 grounds 33 sq. ft. with buildings for consideration of Rs.82 lakhs and respondent Nos. 2 & 3 accepted same - Agreement for Sale was executed between parties and sum of Rs. 2 lakhs was paid by way of cheque as advance - As respondents did not furnish required documents, appellant-company sent a notice calling upon them to perform their obligation under agreement dt. 28-8-1981 as also to fulfill their personal undertakings - In reply to said notice, respondent No.2 said that he was not personally liable for payment made by appellant- Company - Appellant-Company filed a suit for specific performance - Trial Court decreed suit and directed respondents to execute sale deed in favour of appellant-Company and granted 3 months' time to appellant-company to pay balance of sale consideration - Respondents challenged judgment before DB which was allowed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether decree granted by Trial Court for specific performance based on agreement for sale was sustainable, or DB was justified in concluding that appellant-plaintiff had not made out a case for a decree of specific performance in allowing appeal and setting aside decree passed by Trial Court by dismissing suit - Held, in agreement that there was no specific reference to production of order from competent authority under the 1976 Act with regard to exemption - Appellant-Company was not justified in calling for several documents when admittedly, their lawyers perused all relevant documents and on their advise, draft sale deed was prepared and that too after proper inspection of the site and building - In other words, production of clearance certificate from competent authority under the 1976 Act was not specifically intended at any point of time - Information sought for by appellant-company was only to delay transaction and it was not always ready and willing to perform in terms of s. 16(c) of the 1963 Act- Hence, though appellant relied on cl. 3 & 4 of Agreement contended that respondents failed to produce all required documents including documents pertaining to title and encumbrances and claims on or to property, there was no basis for such claim - Hence, in those circumstances, argument assailing judgment of DB was liable to be rejected - It was true that despite fact that there was no provision in the 1963 Act laying down process for seeking an exemption from its operation, respondent-Company wrote to Revenue and Govt. on 26-12-1979 seeking such exemption - As there was no response, it was understood that as proviso to s. 3 applies to land and no further exemption was needed - It was relevant to point out that appellant-Company made further applications on behalf of respondents but to no avail - Land was in enjoyment and possession of respondent-Company and no part of land was taken over by Govt. - Appeal dismissed.
