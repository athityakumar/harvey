Mrinal Das and others v State of Tripura

Supreme Court of India

05 September 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, H.L. Gokhale

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 640; (2011) 9 SCC 479; 2011 ALL MR (Cri) 3256; 2012 (1) RCR(Criminal) 247; 2011(10) SCALE 55; [2011] 14 S.C.R. 411

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Approver

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s.173 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 148,149,326, 302 and 307,r/w s. 34 - Arms Act, 1959, s. 27 - Indian Evidence Act , 1872, s. 133 - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellants/ accused murdered deceased by firing bullets on deceased - Investigating officer during investigation arrested 13 accused persons alleged u/s. 148,149,326,and 307 of IPC r/w s.27 of the 1959 Act - State /defendant approached Trial Court - A12 filed application praying for grant of 'pardon' and to treat him as 'approver' which was granted by Trial Court -Trial Court convicted A5 and A11 and acquitted other 10 accused persons - Aggrieved by judgment of Trial Court A5 and A11 filed appeal before DB and State also filed appeal against order of acquittal of 10 accused persons before DB - DB dismissed A5 and A11 appeal, and partly allowed State appeal by setting aside acquittal of4 persons, A4 , A9, A7 and A1 and convicted them u/s. 302 r/w. 34 and sentenced them with imprisonment for life with fine of Rs.3000/- each, in default, to suffer further term of simple imprisonment for three months - Hence, instant appeal - Whether lower Court's judgment was sustainable - Held , in matter of appreciation of evidence of witnesses, it was not number of witnesses but quality of their evidence - As rightly observed by HC, there were only six accused persons A5, A11, A9, A4, A7 and A1 identified by two or more eye witnesses while A5 and A11 were recognized by PWs 1, 4, 7 and 8 corroborated by PW-6 (approver) - All of them being corroborated by PW-6 (approver) - If PW-6 (approver) was included, there were 3 eye-witnesses who could identify 6offenders including two convicts /appellants - Therefore, entire evidence of above mentioned witnesses, Court fully endorsed view expressed by HC - Existence of common intention amongst participants in crime was essential element for application of s. 34 of IPC and it was not necessary that acts of several persons charged with commission of offence jointly should be same or identically similar and considered case of prosecution in light of disclosure made by approver (PW-6), coupled with statement of eye-witnesses - It was clear that 13 assailants had planned and remained present on shore of river to eliminate deceased - In view of these materials, HC was right in applying s.34 IPC and basing conviction of 6 accused persons including 2 convicted/appellants - Further, statement of approver (PW-6) inspires confidence including conspiracy part which gets full support from narration of occurrence given by eye-witnesses, more particularly, as to deployment of some of offenders for reporting to others about movement of victim - HC pointed out that , there was nothing wrong in accepting PW-6 's entire statement and true disclosure of incident coupled with corroboration of his evidence with eye witnesses - Hence, affirmed HC Judgment - Appeal dismissed.
