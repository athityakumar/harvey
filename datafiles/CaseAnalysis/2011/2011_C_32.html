Chairman-Cum-M. D., Coal India Limited And Others v Ananta Saha And Others

Supreme Court of India

06 April 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 240; (2011) 5 SCC 142; 2011 (8) ADJ 91; 2011(3) ALL MR 419; 2011 (5) AWC 4574; 2011 (2) ESC 351; 2011 (129) FLR 787; JT 2011 (4) SC 252; 2011 (3) LLJ 165; 2011 (5) MLJ(SC) 139; 2011(4) SCALE 398; [2011] 5 S.C.R. 44; 2011 (3) SLR 176

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Andhra Pradesh Public Employees (Regulation of Conditions of Service) Ordinance, 1983, Coal India Executives' Conduct Discipline and Appeal Rules, 1978

Summary: (A) Service - Coal India Executives' Conduct Discipline and Appeal Rules, 1978 - Misconduct - Dismissal from service - Legality - Respondent Medical Officer was charge-sheeted for misconduct - Enquiry Officer found that charge stood proved against respondent - Respondent was dismissed from service - Respondent challenged dismissal order before HC - Single Judge quashed dismissal order on the ground that competent authority under disciplinary rules was Chief Managing Director (CMD) of parent organization (CIL) who had not passed order of punishment but passed by CMD of subsidiary organization (ECL) - Single Judge further directed appellant authority to initiate proceedings afresh - Appellants appealed to DB, which was dismissed subsequently - (a) Whether any officer other than CMD of CIL could initiate disciplinary proceedings and issue charge-sheet - Held, respondent was an officer in E-2 Grade and was posted in subsidiary organization, i.e. ECL, therefore, there was no doubt that disciplinary proceedings could be initiated by CMD of CIL or by CMD of concerned subsidiary organization - As respondent was working in subsidiary organization, HC erred in holding that in such an eventuality CMD of concerned subsidiary organization was not competent to initiate proceedings - Statutory rules clearly stipulated that enquiry could be initiated either by CMD of CIL or by CMD of ECL - (b) Whether passing an ex parte order against respondent in domestic enquiry would vitiate disciplinary proceedings - Held, proceedings were held ex-parte against delinquent as he failed to appear in spite of notice - Respondent had not alleged that he did not receive notice and copy of enquiry report - Plea taken by respondent showed that he had adopted a belligerent attitude and kept litigation alive for more than two decades merely on technical grounds - Respondent waited till conclusion of purported fresh enquiry initiated, even though he could have challenged the same having been initiated by a person not competent to initiate proceedings - In such a fact-situation, HC ought to have refused to entertain respondent's writ petition - Further, writ petition could not have been proceeded with and heard on merit when statutory appeal was pending before Board of Directors of CIL - (c) Whether statutory appeal filed before Board of Directors of CIL was barred by limitation - Held, subsequent to disposal of writ petition and appeal by HC, statutory appeal filed by respondent after 15 months of imposition of punishment was entertained, though limitation prescribed under Rules was only 30 days and appeal had been disposed off on merit without dealing with issue of limitation - Appeal partly allowed.

(B) Service - Coal India Executives' Conduct Discipline and Appeal Rules, 1978 - De-novo enquiry - Meaning of - Respondent was dismissed from service for misconduct - HC directed appellant authority to conduct de-novo enquiry - Appellant proceeded afresh with existing charge-sheet - Whether appellant was justified in proceeding with existing charge-sheet in the context of HC's direction for conducting de-novo enquiry - Held, disciplinary proceedings commenced only when a charge-sheet was issued to delinquent employee - HC gave liberty to appellants to hold de-novo enquiry, meaning that entire earlier proceedings including charge-sheet issued earlier stood quashed - In such a fact- situation, it was not permissible for appellants to proceed on basis of charge-sheet issued earlier - Question of initiating a fresh enquiry without giving a fresh charge-sheet could not arise - Further, there was no application of mind by appellant authority while passing impugned order - Appellants, therefore, were granted liberty to initiate fresh disciplinary proceedings against respondent, i.e., issuing a fresh charge-sheet by competent authority as per Rules - Appeal partly allowed.
