Alamelu and others v State represented by Inspector of Police

Supreme Court of India

18 January 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Surinder Singh Nijjar, B. Sudarshan Reddy

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 50; (2011) 2 SCC 385; AIR 2011 SC 715; 2011 (1) JCC 239; JT 2011 (1) SC 265; 2011 (2) MLJ(Crl) 78; 2011 (1) RCR(Criminal) 498; 2011(1) SCALE 575; [2011] 2 S.C.R. 147

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 366 and 376 r/w. s. 109 - Kidnapping and rape - Trial court convicted A1 for offences punishable u/ss. 366 and 376 and A2 to A8 for offences punishable u/ss. 366 and 376 r/w. s. 109 IPC - The conviction as well as the sentence have been maintained in appeals by the HC - Hence the present appeals - Whether trial court rightly convicted A1 for offences punishable u/ss. 366 and 376 and A2 to A8 for offences punishable u/ss. 366 and 376 r/w. s. 109 IPC? - Held, HC erroneously concluded that the girl had not willingly gone with A1 - The conclusion could only be recorded by ignoring the entire evidence with regard to the conduct of the girl from the time of the alleged abduction till the time of the alleged recovery - Girl did not make any complaint on so many occasions when she had the opportunity to do so - However, even after the alleged marriage, the girl continued to be a willing partner in the entire episode - It is noteworthy that she made no protest even to known persons near the car, after her alleged abduction - She did not make any complaint to the sister of A1 - Again, there was no complaint on seeing her relatives allegedly assembled at the temple -The above behaviour would not be natural for a girl who had been compelled to marry - Hence, prosecution has failed to prove beyond reasonable doubt any of the offences with which the appellants had been charged - It appears that the entire prosecution story has been concocted for reasons best known to the prosecution - Appellants are entitled to the benefit of doubt - Appeals allowed.
