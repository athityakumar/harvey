Jagdish Parwani v Union Of India & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

15 July 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Mukundakam Sharma, A.R. Dave

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 438; 2011 (9) ADJ 5; AIR 2011 SC 2594; 2011(5) ALL MR 492; 2011 (6) AWC 6034; 2011 (4) ESC 554; 2011 (3) JabLJ 287; JT 2011 (11) SC 216; 2011(7) SCALE 627; 2012 (2) SLR 512

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Absorption of State service into Central service - Pay protection - Entitlement - Appellant Assistant Engineer resigned from the service of State Electricity Board and joined Military Engineering Service - Appellant sought pay protection, which was rejected by respondent authority - Appellant challenged respondent's order before Tribunal - Tribunal directed the respondent to fix the pay of appellant - However, in respondent's writ petition, HC held that appellant was not entitled to pay protection - Appellant's review petition thereafter was also rejected by HC - Whether the appellant was entitled to pay protection as per the notification dated 7-8-1989 - Held, since the appellant was selected and appointed to a post in Central Government on 23-2-1990 after working as an employee of the State Government Undertaking, the notification dated 7-8-1989 was not applicable to him and, therefore, he could not have legally claimed for any pay protection - Being fully aware of the aforesaid position the appellant accepted the appointment without any demur or protest on the issue of pay being given to him under the appointment order issued to him by the Military Engineering Service - Appellant accepted the said pay scale without raising any grievance and continued to receive the same till 11-9-1991, when for the first time he submitted his first representation for pay protection as per notification dated 7-8-1989 - Position with regard to the entitlement or otherwise of the appellant for getting pay protection was made clear by issuing the subsequent notification dated 28-2-1992 clearly stipulating therein that an employee of the State Government Undertaking selected for post in Central Government on direct recruitment basis would be entitled to pay protection upon appointment in Central Government only effective from 1-2-1992 - Appellant having joined the Military Engineering Service prior to the 1-2-1992 was not entitled to the benefit of the notification dated 7-8-1989 which was issued much after his joining date - Hence, HC was justified in setting aside the order of the Tribunal as the Tribunal had misread and misinterpreted the facts as also the legal principles in law - Appeals dismissed.
