Sanjay Chandra and others v Cbi

Supreme Court of India

23 November 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Dattu, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 775; (2012) 1 SCC 40; 2012 CRLJ 702; 2011 (4) RCR(Criminal) 898; [2011] 13 S.C.R. 309

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 420-B, 468, 471 and 109 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, s. 13 (2) r/w 13 (i) (d) - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, s. 439 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 21 - Non bailable offence - Grant of bail - Sustainability - Criminal case was registered against appellants' u/ss. 420-B, 468, 471 and 109 of IPC u/s. 13 (2) r/w 13 (i) (d), Act 1988 -Charges against appellants was that of cheating and dishonestly inducing delivery of property, forgery for the purpose of cheating using forged document - Special Judge, CBI rejected bail applications filed by appellants - Thereafter, appellants filed bail applications before HC - HC refused request for grant of bail on two grounds, primary ground was that offence alleged against appellants were serious, involving deep rooted planning in which, huge financial loss was caused to State exchequer; secondary ground was that possibility of appellants tempering with witnesses - Aggrieved by order, appellants filed instant appeal - Appellants contended that there was two principles for grant of bail, firstly, if there was no prima facie case and secondly, even if there was prima facie case and also if there was no reasonable apprehension of tampering with witnesses or evidence or absconding from trial, accused were entitled to grant of bail pending trial, since both conditions were satisfied in instant case, appellants should be granted bail - Whether appellants entitled for bail and what were the factors to be considered while granting bail in non-bailable cases - Held, while granting bail, seriousness of charge and severity of punishment should be taken into consideration and grant or refusal to grant bail lies within discretion of court - Also while deciding bail applications an important factor which should be taken into consideration by court was delay in concluding trial - In instant case, investigating agency completed investigation and charge sheet was already filed before Special Judge, CBI, therefore, presence of appellants in custody might not be necessary for further investigation - Further, appellants were already in custody for 66 days - Hence, no reason found to deny bail - It was ordered, appellants be released on conditional bail - Appeals disposed of.
