State Of U.P. v Mohd Iqram & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

13 June 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, Swatanter Kumar

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 383; (2011) 8 SCC 80; AIR 2011 SC 2296; 2011 ALL MR (Cri) 2344; 2011 CRLJ 3931; JT 2011 (6) SC 650; 2012 (1) MLJ(Crl) 291; 2011 (3) RCR(Criminal) 711; 2011(6) SCALE 497; [2011] 6 S.C.R. 1017

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 302 and 376 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 313 - Examination of the accused - Acquittal - Sustainability - On night of the murder of the deceased two respondents were apprehended while the third escaped - On interrogation the two respondents named the deceased's husband as the third accused - Deceased's husband was subsequently arrested - Trial court convicted and sentenced the two respondents but acquitted deceased's husband giving him benefit of doubt - Aggrieved, the two respondents appealed before the HC - HC convicted the deceased's husband and acquitted the respondents placing reliance on incriminating material against the accused without having put the same to him during his examination u/s. 313 of CrPC - Held, HC committed an error by taking into consideration inadmissible evidence for the purpose of deciding the criminal appeals and holding the person guilty who had already been acquitted by the trial court - No matter how weak or scanty the prosecution evidence was in regard to certain incriminating material, it was the duty of the Court to examine the accused and seek his explanation on incriminating material that has surfaced against him - S. 313 of CrPC was based on the fundamental principle of fairness - Attention of the accused must specifically be brought to inculpatory pieces of evidence to give him an opportunity to offer an explanation - Court was under a legal obligation to put the incriminating circumstances before the accused and solicit his response - Provision was mandatory in nature and casts an imperative duty on the court and confers a corresponding right on the accused to have an opportunity to offer an explanation for such incriminatory material appearing against him - Circumstances which were not put to the accused in his examination u/s. 313 of CrPC could not be used against him and have to be excluded from consideration - Findings of fact recorded by the HC are perverse, being based on irrelevant considerations and inadmissible material - Judgment of Trial Court restored - Appeals allowed.
