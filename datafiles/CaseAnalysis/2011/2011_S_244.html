Dattu S/O Namdev Thakur v State Of Maharashtra And Ors

Supreme Court of India

07 December 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Surinder Singh Nijjar, J. Chelameswar

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 720; (2012) 1 SCC 549; AIR 2012 SC 360; 2012(5) ALL MR 954; 2012 (2) Bom.C.R. 586; 2012 (3) MahLJ 179; 2011(13) SCALE 484; [2011] 13 S.C.R. 475

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Constitution - Practice & Procedure - Cast Certificates - Cancelled - Challenged - The Caste Certificates issued to the petitioners were invalidated on the ground that they were unable to satisfy the Caste Scrutiny Committee that they belong to the 'Thakur' tribe, which was recorded as a Scheduled Tribe of the Maharashtra Scheduled Tribes List - Petitioners challenged said order of the Caste Scrutiny Committee before HC who upheld the findings of the Caste Scrutiny Committee - Hence, the instant Special Leave Petitions - Whether HC rightly upheld the order of the Caste Scrutiny Committee? - Held, reference was made to the Caste Scrutiny Committee by the respondents in 2009, i.e. nine years after the certificates had been issued, and there was no proper explanation for such delay - On the other hand, the petitioners in the first writ petition has been allowed by the respondents to continue in service and also by virtue of orders passed by the HC - Hence, court directed that whatever advantage the three petitioners in the three Special Leave Petitions, may have derived on the basis of their 'Caste Certificates', shall not be disturbed and the cancellation of their respective 'Caste Certificates' will not deprive them of the benefits which they have already enjoyed - However, none of the three petitioners will be entitled to take any further advantage of reservation in future, either for studies or for employment - Petitioners were also directed that if the petitioners have obtained any concession by way of reduction in fees, as a reserved candidate, they will have to make good the same by paying the difference in fees that was being paid by general candidates - Order accordingly.
