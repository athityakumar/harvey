Shiji @ Pappu And Ors v Radhika And Anr

Supreme Court of India

14 November 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 791; AIR 2012 SC 499; 2011 ALL MR (Cri) 3915; 2012 CRLJ 840; 2012 (1) JCC 101; 2011 (4) KLT 682; 2012 (2) MLJ(Crl) 215; 2012(2) M.P.H.T. 123; 2012 (167) PLR 108; 2012 (1) RCR(Criminal) 9; [2011] 13 S.C.R. 135

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 320 and 482 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 354 and 394 -Inherent powers of HC - Quashing of criminal proceedings - Respondent filed complaint alleging that appellants snatched her purse and hit her - During pendency of criminal proceedings, the parties amicably settled matter among themselves - Appellants filed petition u/s. 482 CrPC before HC praying quashing of criminal proceedings against appellants - - HC declined said prayer and held that offences committed by appellants were not of a personal nature- Hence, instant appeal - Whether the criminal proceedings could be quashed having regard to the settlement that the parties had arrived at - Held, offence was not compoundable u/s. 320 of CrPC was no reason for HC to refuse exercise of its power u/s. 482 CrPC - Power could be exercised in cases where there was no chance of recording conviction against accused and entire exercise of trial was destined to be exercise in futility - There was subtle distinction between compounding of offences by parties before trial Court or in appeal on one hand and exercise of power by HC to quash prosecution u/s 482 Cr.P.C. on the other - Court trying accused or hearing appeal against conviction, might not be competent to permit compounding of offence based on settlement arrived at between parties in cases where offences were not compoundable u/s. 320 of CrPC, HC might quash prosecution even in cases where offences with which accused stood charged were non-compoundable - Inherent powers of HC u/s. 482 of CrPC. were not for that purpose controlled by s. 320 of CrPC - Power u/s. 482 of Cr.P.C. made it obligatory for the HC to exercise same with utmost care and caution - Width and nature of power demanded its exercise in cases where HC was, for reasons to be recorded, of clear view that continuance of prosecution would be nothing but an abuse of process of law -Impugned order of HC was set aside - Appeal allowed.
