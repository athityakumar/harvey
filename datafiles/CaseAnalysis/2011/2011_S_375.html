TGN Kumar v State of Kerala and others

Supreme Court of India

14 January 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

D.K. Jain, A.K. Ganguly, H.L. Dattu

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 39; (2011) 2 SCC 772; AIR 2011 SC 708; 2011 ALL MR (Cri) 600; 2011 (1) BC 612; 2011 (3) CalLT 27; 2011 (1) CTC 627; 2011 (2) G.L.R. 1570; 2011 (1) JCC (NI) 36; JT 2011 (3) SC 280; 2011 (1) KLT 362; 2011 (3) MLJ(Crl) 600; 2011(2) M.P.H.T. 92; 2011 (1) OLR 754; 2011 (1) RCR(Criminal) 507; 2011(1) SCALE 472; [2011] 1 S.C.R. 436

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Personal Presence

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 227 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 205 and 482 - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, s. 138 - Exemption to accused from personal appearance - Complaint was filed u/s. 138 of the Act - On being summoned by the Magistrate, the accused filed a petition before the HC u/s. 482 of the CrPC, inter alia, praying for dispensing with her personal appearance before the Magistrate - HC allowed the petition and also issued the 'rules of guidance', with a direction that these can and must certainly be followed by the court below - Present appeal - Whether HC exceeded its jurisdiction u/s. 482 of the CrPC and/or art. 227 of the Constitution by laying down the general directions? - Held, dispensation with the personal examination of an accused in terms of the s. 205 of CrPC is within the Trial Court's discretion, to be exercised keeping in view certain parameters, enumerated therein and not as a matter of course - Hence, impugned order was clearly erroneous in as much as the discretion of the Magistrate u/s. 205 of the CrPC cannot be circumscribed by laying down any general directions in that behalf - Thus, in the present case, HC exceeded its jurisdiction u/s. 482 of the CrPC and/or art. 227 of the Constitution by laying down the general directions, which were inconsistent with the clear language of ss. 205 and 313 of the CrPC - Impugned order containing general directions to the lower courts set aside - Appeal allowed.
