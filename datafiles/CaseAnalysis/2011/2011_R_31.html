Helios and Matheson Information Technology Limited and another v Rajeev Sawhney And Another

Supreme Court of India

16 December 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 757; (2012) 1 SCC 699; AIR 2012 SC 660; 2013 (3) CGLJ 189; 2012 CRLJ 1248; 2012 (1) JCC 513; 2012 (2) MLJ(Crl) 831; 2012 (1) RCR(Criminal) 354; 2012 (2) RLW 1669; 2011(13) SCALE 536; [2011] 16 S.C.R. 410

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: (A) Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 417, 420, 465, 467, 468, 471, 120B - Private complaint proceedings - Remand order - Legality - 1st respondent complainant lodged private complaint against petitioners alleging that they had entered into conspiracy to defraud him by fabricating 1st petitioner company's Board resolution - Trial Court recorded prima facie satisfaction about commission of offences punishable u/ss. 417, 420, 465, 467, 468, 471 r/w s. 120B of IPC, took cognizance and directed issuance of process against petitioners - Petitioner challenged Trial Court order before Sessions Court - Sessions Court set aside Trial Court order, however, HC, in complaint's revision petition, remanded matter to Trial Court - Whether HC was justified in remanding matter to Trial Court - Held, 1st respondent's complaint contained assertions with sufficient amount of clarity on facts and events which if taken as proved would culminate in an order of conviction against petitioners - No error or perversity was found in the view taken by HC that in a revision petition photocopies of documents produced by petitioners for first time for their defence, could not be entertained and made a basis for setting aside an order passed by Trial Court and dismissing a complaint which otherwise made out commission of an offence - Petitioner was doubtless entitled to set up his defence before Trial Court at proper stage, confront witnesses appearing before Court with any document relevant to controversy and have documents brought on record as evidence to enable Trial Court to take a proper view regarding the effect thereof - However, no such document, genuineness whereof was not admitted by parties to proceedings, could be introduced by petitioner in the manner it was sought to be done - No reason to interfere with impugned HC order - Petition dismissed.

(B) Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 202 - Lodging of private complaint - Suppression of previous complaint - Legality - Whether the instant private complaint would be sustainable in view of suppression of information regarding dismissal of previous complaint - Held, complaint previously filed was quashed by HC on account of filing of a comprehensive complaint out of which instant proceedings arose was a complete answer to charge of suppression - As on the date Trial Court took cognizance of offences in instant complaint filed before it no other complaint was pending in any other Court, previous complaint was quashed without a trial on merits - Mere filing of a previous complaint could not in be a bar to filing of another complaint or for proceedings based on such complaint being taken to their logical conclusion - HC was correct in holding that there was no violation of provision of s. 202 CrPC to warrant interference in exercise of revisional powers by Sessions Court - Petition dismissed.
