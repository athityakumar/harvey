Abdul Rehman & Ors. v K.M. Anees-Ul-Haq

Supreme Court of India

14 November 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 804; (2011) 10 SCC 696; AIR 2012 SC (Supp) 155; 2012 ALL MR (Cri) 362; 2012 CRLJ 1060; 2012 (1) CTC 184; 2012 (1) JCC 94; 2012 (1) RCR(Criminal) 150; 2012 (1) RLW 255; [2011] 14 S.C.R. 1033

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 211, 500 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 195 - Commission of offence in relation to court proceedings - Prosecution - Sustainability - Appellants lodged a complaint against respondent for offences u/s. 406 r/w s. 34 IPC - Respondent, in turn, filed a complaint against appellants u/ss. 211 and 500 r/w ss. 109, 114, 34 IPC - Magistrate passed order summoning appellants on the ground that there was sufficient material to show commission of offences punishable u/ss. 211 and 500 IPC - Appellant filed revision to Sessions Court - Sessions Court dismissed revision as barred by limitation - Appellant petitioned to HC u/s. 482 CrPC for quashing complaint against them - HC dismissed petition holding that since no judicial proceedings were pending in any Court at time when complaint u/ss. 211 and 500 IPC was filed by respondent, bar contained in s. 195 CrPC was not attracted nor was there any illegality in order passed by Magistrate summoning appellants to face trial - Whether complaint filed by respondent against appellants alleging commission of offences punishable u/ss. 211, 500, 109, and 114 r/w s. 34 IPC was barred by provisions of s. 195 of CrPC - Held, it was not in dispute that upon filing of complaint by appellants against respondent, latter had sought an order of anticipatory bail from Sessions Court, nor was it disputed that an order granting bail was indeed passed in favour of respondent - Bail proceedings conducted by Sessions Court in connection with case which appellants had lodged against respondents were judicial proceedings and offence punishable u/s. 211 IPC alleged to have been committed by appellants related to said proceedings - Hence, bar contained in s. 195 CrPC was clearly attracted to complaint filed by respondent - Magistrate and HC had fallen in error in holding that complaint filed by respondent was maintainable - However, allowing respondents to continue with prosecution against appellants for offence punishable u/s. 500 IPC would not sub-serve ends of justice and would result in appellants getting vexed twice on the same facts - Thus, any complaint u/s. 500 IPC would become time barred if complaint already lodged was quashed - Impugned orders of Magistrate and HC were quashed - Appeals allowed.
