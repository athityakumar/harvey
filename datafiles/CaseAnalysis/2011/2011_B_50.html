Anil Gilurker v Bilaspur Raipur Kshetria Gramin Bank and another

Supreme Court of India

15 September 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Patnaik, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 582; (2011) 14 SCC 379; AIR 2012 SC (Supp) 181; 2011 (6) AWC 5448; 2011 (131) FLR 369; JT 2011 (10) SC 373; 2012 (2) LLJ 20; 2012 (1) MLJ(SC) 978; 2011(10) SCALE 465; [2011] 12 S.C.R. 618; 2011 (5) SLR 737

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Employees Collection Service Regulations, Staff Service Regulations

Summary: Service - Issuance of vague charge - Removal from service - Legality - Appellant Branch Manager in the respondent bank was charge-sheeted for misappropriating the loan amount without disbursing fully to the borrowers - Inquiry Officer held that the charge of financial corruption against the appellant had not been proved - Disciplinary authority disagreed with the findings of Inquiry Officer and sent show cause notice proposing to remove the appellant and forfeiture of the contribution of the bank to the Provident Fund of the appellant u/s. 50(1) of the Staff Regulations of the bank - Appellant submitted his reply to the show cause notice, however, the disciplinary authority removed the appellant - Appellant's appeal thereafter was also dismissed by the appellate authority - Appellant challenged his removal before the HC - Single Judge quashed the orders of the disciplinary authority and the appellate authority and directed reinstatement of the appellant in service with continuity in service and without loss of seniority in the post to which he would be entitled to - Single Judge further directed that the appellant would be entitled to compensation in lieu of arrears of his salary - Respondent bank filed appeal - DB held that the charges against the appellant as described in the charge-sheet were not vague as on the basis of documents mentioned in the charge-sheet - DB, however, held that as the disciplinary authority had disagreed with the findings in the inquiry report, he should have furnished his reasons for the disagreement to the appellant before passing the order of punishment - Whether the charge-sheet filed against appellant was proper - Held, only vague allegations were made against the appellant that he had sanctioned loans to a large number of brick manufacturing units by committing irregularities, but did not disburse the entire loan amount to the borrowers and while a portion of the loan amount was deposited in the account of the borrowers, the balance was misappropriated by him and others - However, the details of the loan accounts or the names of the borrowers had not been mentioned in the charges - Amounts of loan which were sanctioned and the amounts which were actually disbursed to the borrowers and the amounts alleged to have been misappropriated by the appellant had not been mentioned - Along with the charge-sheet, no statement of imputations giving the particulars of the loan accounts or the names of the borrowers, the amounts of loans sanctioned, disbursed and misappropriated were furnished to the appellant, and yet the disciplinary authority had called upon the appellant to submit his written defence statement in reply to the charges - Appellant cannot submit his written statement in defence in respect of the charges and fair enquiry cannot be held unless he was furnished with the particulars of the loan accounts or the names of the borrowers, the amounts of loan sanctioned, the amounts actually disbursed and the amounts misappropriated were also furnished in the charge-sheet - Hence, the impugned order of the DB was set aside and the order of Single Judge was restored, however, order of compensation was set aside - Appeals allowed.
