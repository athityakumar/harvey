Dunlop India Limited v (1) A.A. Rahna Andanother; (2) A.A. Sulaiman Andothers

Supreme Court of India

04 May 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 306; (2011) 5 SCC 778; AIR 2011 SC 2198; 2011 (87) ALR 256; 2011 (2) DRTC 457; JT 2011 (5) SC 419; 2011 (2) KLT 682; 2011(5) SCALE 312; [2011] 5 S.C.R. 1080

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Keywords: Karnataka Rent Control Act

Summary: Rent Control - Kerala Buildings (Lease and Rent Control) Act, 1965, ss. 11(2)(b), 11(3), 11(4)(i), 11(4)(v) and 18 - Sick Industrial Companies (Special Provisions) Act, 1985 - Res judicata - Eviction Petition - 'X' (Predecessor of respondents) leased out the suit premises to the appellant for its godown and office for a period of 10 years - Appellant exercised the option for extension of the term of lease and did not vacate the premises at the end of extended period - After the death of 'X', respondents became owners of the property, filed petitions for eviction of the appellant on the ground that the appellant had ceased to occupy the premises from June, 1998 - Rent Control Court allowed said petitions and directed the appellant to vacate the premises - Appellate Authority allowed appeals filed against said order and the order of eviction was set aside - Revision petition filed against said order was dismissed by DB - Subsequently, respondents filed fresh rent control petitions for eviction of the appellant on the ground that the appellant had ceased to occupy the premises since September, 2001 without any reasonable cause - Appellant contended that the petitions filed by the respondents were liable to be dismissed as barred by res judicata - Appellant also pleaded that due to financial constraints, the appellant could not run its business effectively and profitably and it was declared sick under the Sick Industrial Companies (Special Provisions) Act, 1985 by Board for Industrial and Financial Reconstruction (BIFR) - Rent Control Court held that the petitions filed by the respondents were not barred by res judicata and s. 15 of the 1965 Act cannot be invoked for denying relief to them because two sets of rent control petitions were based on different causes - Rent Control Court also held that the appellant had ceased to occupy the suit premises since September, 2001 without any reasonable cause and, accordingly, directed it to vacate the premises - Appellate Authority and DB upheld the order of the Rent Control Court - Hence, the present appeals - (A) Whether the petition filed by the respondents were barred by res judicata and also u/s.15 of the Act? - Held, in the first set of petitions, the respondents had sought eviction on the ground that the appellant had ceased to occupy the premises from June, 1998 - However, in the second set of petitions the respondents had sought eviction on the ground that the appellant had ceased to occupy the premises since September, 2001 without any reasonable cause and continued till the filing of the eviction petitions - Both petitions were filed on different grounds, hence, not barred by res judicata and also u/s.15 of the Act - (B) Whether the petitioner ceased to occupy the petition schedule buildings continuously for six months and was entitled for an order of eviction u/s 11(4)(v) of the 1965 Act? - Held, the initial burden to show that the tenant had ceased to occupy the building continuously for 6 months was always on the landlord - Landlord had to adduce tangible evidence to prove the fact that as on the date of filing the petition, the tenant was not occupying the building - Once such evidence was adduced, the burden shifts on the tenant to prove that there was reasonable cause for his having ceased to occupy the tenanted premises for a continuous period of 6 months - In the instant case, respondents (Landlords) succeeded in proving that the appellant (tenant) had ceased to occupy the suit premises for a period exceeding 6 months, however, tenant could not prove that it was occupying the premises or that non occupation thereof was for a reasonable cause - Before the Rent Control Court, the appellant had neither pleaded nor any evidence was produced to show that due to financial stringency was due to the reasons beyond its control and on that account, the suit premises could not be used from September, 2001 onwards for the purpose specified in the lease deeds - Therefore, the so called financial stringency cannot be construed as reasonable cause within the meaning of s. 11(4)(v) of the Act - Hence, petitioner was entitled for an order of eviction u/s 11(4)(v) of the 1965 Act - Appeals dismissed.
