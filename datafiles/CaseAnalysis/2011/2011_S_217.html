Ummu Sabeena and others v State Of Kerala & Ors

Supreme Court of India

17 November 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Ganguly, J. S. KHEHAR

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 795; 2012 ALL MR (Cri) 317; 2011 (4) KLT 764; 2012 (2) MLJ(Crl) 429; 2012 (1) RCR(Criminal) 182; [2011] 13 S.C.R. 185

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Comments

Summary: Criminal - Constitution - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 22(5) - Conservation of Foreign Exchange and prevention of Smuggling Activities Act, 1974, s. 3 - Detention - Habeas corpus - Rejection - Delay Petitioners filed writ petition assailing orders of detention passed under the provisions of Act, 1974 submitting that representations filed on behalf of the detenus were not disposed of in accordance with the mandate of art. 22(5) of the - Said orders were rejected by HC - Hence, instant appeals - Whether the manner of consideration and rejection of representation by the Central Government was in accord with the principles laid down by SC on this aspect in several cases - Held, SC in K.M. Abdulla Kunhi and B.L. Abdul Khader vs. Union of India & Ors., State of Karnataka & Ors. 1991 Indlaw SC 473 held that neither under the Constitution nor under the relevant statutory provision, any time limit has been fixed for consideration for representation made by a detenu - SC also held that but the expression 'as soon as may be' in art. 22(5) of Constitution makes clear concern of framers of Constitution that representation should be very expeditiously considered and disposed of with a sense of urgency and without any avoidable delay - Following said principle, delay in impugned cases was for a much longer period and there was hardly any explanation - Orders of detention quashed on the ground of delay on the part of the Central Government in disposing of representation of detenus - Appeals allowed.
