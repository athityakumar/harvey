Registrar General, High Court of Madras v M. Manickam and others

Supreme Court of India

17 August 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Mukundakam Sharma, A.R. Dave

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 579; (2011) 9 SCC 245; 2011 (6) AWC 5710; 2011 (131) FLR 373; 2012 (2) MLJ(SC) 451; [2011] 10 S.C.R. 324; 2011 (6) SLR 765

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges

Keywords: Tamil Nadu State Judicial Service Rules

Summary: Advocate & Judges - Superannuation - Genuineness of claim - Validity - Respondent No. 1 joined State Subordinate Judicial Service as District Munsif-cum-Judicial Magistrate - Due to wrong entry of date of birth in service records, he would retire from his service 3 years, 8 months and 5 days before his actual date of superannuation - Respondent No. 1 submitted application to Registrar seeking change of his date of birth - Administrative Officer of HC asked for certain particulars and documents - Respondent No. 1 filed Suit before District Munsif which was allowed and directed mandatory injunction against respondent Nos. 2-4 to make change of date of birth - Aggrieved by decision of Munsif Court, respondent Nos. 2-4 filed appeal before Sub-Judge which was allowed - Hence, instant appeals - Whether alteration of date of birth should be permitted or application should be rejected - Held, respondent No. 1 had failed to discharge his onus in proving authenticity of horoscope on which reliance was placed - Since aforesaid horoscope was primary document on which reliance was placed for change of his date of birth, , same was required to be looked into very carefully and minutely so as to ascertain genuineness of claim of respondent No. 1 - Judgment and decree passed by Munsif Court which was affirmed by HC should not be sustained and was liable to be set aside - Respondent No. 1 had failed to prove that any change of date of birth was called for in instant case - Appeals allowed.
