Deepti Bhandari v Nitin Bhandari & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

14 December 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Surinder Singh Nijjar, J. Chelameswar

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 734; (2012) 1 SCC 725; AIR 2012 SC 326; 2012 ALL MR (Cri) 376; 2012 (92) ALR 477; 2012 (1) CGLJ 465; 2012 (2) JCC 970; 2012 (1) OLR 248; 2012 (1) RCR(Civil) 506; 2011(13) SCALE 506; [2011] 13 S.C.R. 547

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Summary: Family & Personal - Hindu Marriage Act, 1955, s. 9 - Guardians and Wards Act, 1890, s. 25 - Transfer petition - Visitation rights - Petitioner (Wife) and Respondent No.1 (Husband) were married to each other according to Hindu rites at Jaipur and a girl child was born to the couple - Disputes arose and petition for mutual divorce was filed by Petitioner - Respondent No.1 moved an application for visitation rights in respect of the minor child, which was dismissed by the Family Court - Appeal filed against said order was dismissed by the Upper District Judge - HC allowed an application for visitation rights and directed Petitioner to arrange for the meeting of Respondent No. 1 with Petitioner and their minor daughter at the office of the counsel for the Respondent No. 1 on every Saturday - Subsequently, petitioner's brother got admission with I.I.P.M., Delhi, which required him to shift to Delhi - Petitioner also decided to shift to Delhi to establish herself professionally - Petitioner filed an application for modification of order of HC and instead of Jaipur, to shift the place of visitation to Delhi - HC disallowed said application - Hence, instant petition - Whether order of the HC could be modified and visitation rights could be shift from Jaipur to Delhi? - Held, it was true that transfer to Delhi was likely to cause some inconvenience to Respondent No.1 and his family members, but it cannot be denied that it would be easier for Respondent No.1 to attend the proceedings in Delhi than for Petitioner to attend to the same in Jaipur, while staying in Delhi with her minor child - Therefore, there was no substance in the persistent demand of the Respondent No.1 that he should be allowed to meet the Petitioner and their minor child at Jaipur to enable him and his family members to meet the child on a regular basis - Hence, application for modification of the order filed by Petitioner before HC was allowed along with Transfer petition filed by Petitioner - Respondent No.1 and his parents will be entitled to meet the minor child on every Saturday in New Delhi - Petition be transferred from Family Court at Jaipur to a Family court of Delhi - Order accordingly.
