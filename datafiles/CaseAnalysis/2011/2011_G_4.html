G. Srinivas Rao v Union Of India & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

19 July 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Patnaik, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 449; (2011) 8 SCC 123; 2011 (9) ADJ 21; AIR 2011 SC 2756; 2011 (6) AWC 6047; 2011 (4) ESC 652; 2011(7) SCALE 723; [2011] 9 S.C.R. 313; 2011 (4) SLR 619

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: IPS (Cadre) Rules, 1954

Summary: Service - Indian Police Service (Cadre) Rules, 1954 - Indian Police Service (IPS) - Allotment of State cadre - Delay in challenging the allotment - Appellant, a general candidate, secured 95th rank appointed to IPS and was allocated to Manipur-Tripura Joint Cadre whereas 4th respondent, a OBC candidate, secured 133rd rank, was allocated to Andhra Pradesh Cadre - Appellant filed O.A. before the Tribunal seeking Andhra Pradesh Cadre - Tribunal dismissed application - Appellant challenged Tribunal order before HC - Appellant contended before HC that vacancy for a general candidate available in the Andhra Pradesh Cadre was converted to a vacancy for OBC candidate on the ground that relevant data for five years in respect of OBC was not available though actually such data was available - HC allowed writ petition and remanded the matter to Tribunal - Tribunal rejected appellant's contention - Appellant's second writ petition thereafter dismissed by HC holding that he was required to implead all the candidates of his batch of IPS, as respondents in the O.A. as well as in the Writ Petition but had not done so and thus relief could not be granted to him - HC further held in the impugned order that the allocation of the appellant to the Manipur-Tripura Joint Cadre was intimated to him by a letter dated 21-10-1999, but he filed the O.A. in 2001 and by the time the impugned order was passed, the officers would have undergone attachment training and a wholesale or extensive review of the cadre allocation at a belated stage would not be conducive to public interest - Whether the HC, by impugned order, refused to direct fresh allocation of cadre was proper - Held, cl. (2) of Para 3 of the letter dated 31-5-1985 by the Central Government with regard to the allocation of cadre was contemplated that a roster for each cadre, with vacancies earmarked for outsider and insider and for general candidates and reserved candidates was maintained and allocations of outsider, insider, general and reserved candidates were made to these earmarked vacancies - It would be further clear from cl. (2) of Para 3 that the vacancies for the reserved categories were not to exceed the prescribed percentage for the reserved category 'in the various cadres' - Further, data for 5 years in respect of allocation of OBC candidates was not relevant for making the allocation when cl. (2) of Para 3 of the letter dated 31-5-1985 - As held by SC in Union of India v. Rajiv Yadav, IAS and Others, 1994 INDLAW SC 892, the roster system ensured equitable treatment to both the general candidates and reserved candidates and hence the roster system should not be by-passed on some ground or the other which may result in unfair treatment to either general candidates or reserved candidates in violation of their right to equality u/arts. 14 and 16(1) of the Constitution - Further, appellant was allocated to the Manipur-Tripura Cadre on 27-7-1999 and was intimated about such allocation by letter dated 2-10-1999 - Instead of challenging the allocations made in 1999 at the earliest, the appellant filed the O.A. before the Tribunal only in 2001 by which time the 36 candidates including the 4th respondent, who had been selected and appointed to the IPS on the basis of Civil Services Examination, 1998 and had been allocated to different cadres, had already joined their respective cadres and undertaken training in their respective States - HC thus held in the impugned order that the wholesale or extensive review of the cadre allocation at a belated stage was not conducive to public interest - For granting relief to the appellant, the Tribunal or the Court would have to direct the 1st respondent to undertake afresh the exercise of allocation in accordance with the roster system as provided in the letter dated 31-5-1985 and allocate the 36 officers of the IPS appointed on the basis of the Civil Services Examinations, 1998 and such an exercise would disturb the allocation of several members of the IPS - Hence, HC was right in taking a view that no relief could be granted to the appellant on the ground of delay on the part of the appellant in moving the Tribunal - Appeal dismissed.
