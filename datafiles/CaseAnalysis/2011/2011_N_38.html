State Bank Of Bikaner & Jaipur v Nemi Chand Nalwaya

Supreme Court of India

01 March 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 202; (2011) 4 SCC 584; AIR 2011 SC 1931; 2011 (4) AWC 3385; 2011 (129) FLR 937; JT 2011 (4) SC 14; 2011 (3) LLJ 13; 2011 (3) MPLJ 493; 2011(4) SCALE 56; [2011] 3 S.C.R. 589; 2011 (4) SLR 458

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Dismissal from service - Challenged - Respondent, clerk in the appellant-Bank, was issued a charge sheet alleging that he had disclosed the balance of one in an in-operative account to an unidentified person - In preliminary enquiry charges against respondent were proved - Disciplinary authority dismissed respondent from service - On appeal, criminal court acquitted the respondent on the ground that the charges were not proved beyond doubt - Appellate Authority dismissed appeal filed against said order - Single Judge also dismissed writ petition filed against said order - However, DB set aside the order passed by the Single Judge and held that the case was not one where respondent had acted in wilful dereliction of duty and that in an increasing customer-friendly atmosphere in the Bank, the respondent had acted bona fide and allowed the person considered by him to be a valued customer to operate on the account not realising that such person was impersonating the account holder - DB also directed appellant bank to reinstatement the respondent with full backwages and consequential benefits - Hence, instant appeal - Whether DB rightly set the order passed by the Single Judge? - Held, the fact that the criminal court subsequently acquitted the respondent by giving him the benefit of doubt, would not in any way render a completed disciplinary proceedings invalid nor affect the validity of the finding of guilt or consequential punishment - The standard of proof required in criminal proceedings being different from the standard of proof required in departmental enquiries, the same charges and evidence may lead to different results in the two proceedings - The findings by the criminal court would have no effect on previously concluded domestic enquiry - Hence, DB was not justified in quashing the punishment and directing reinstatement of respondent with backwages and consequential benefits - Impugned judgment of DB set aside - Appeal allowed.
