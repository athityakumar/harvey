Chief General Manager, Calcutta Telephones District, Bharat Sanchar Nigam Limited & Ors. v Surendra Nath Pandey & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

03 November 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Surinder Singh Nijjar, Altamas Kabir, Gyan Sudha Misra

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 769; (2011) 15 SCC 81; 2012 (2) AWC 1255; 2012 (2) MLJ(SC) 241; [2011] 14 S.C.R. 840; 2012 (1) SLR 84

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Junior Accounts Officers Service Postal Wing (Group C) Recruitment Rules, 1977

Summary: Service - Junior Accounts Officers Service Postal Wing (Group C) Recruitment Rules, 1977, r. 14(4) - Ad-hoc Promotion - Re-examination - Sustainability - Respondents were employees in appellant department, appeared in examination for being promoted to Junior Accounts Officers - Names of respondents did not appear either in lists of successful or unsuccessful candidates - Respondents made representation before appropriate authority in request for being intimated of marks secured - Authority did not reply to representation - Respondents filed application before Tribunal seeking disclosure of marks and disposal of representation -Tribunal directed appellants to publish result and dispose representation and allow respondents to appear in examination on next year - Chief General Manager (CGM) disposed of respondents representation stating that their candidature was cancelled on account of some irregular practices having been noticed on their part - Respondent filed writ petition against the order passed by CGM - Single Judge allowed said writ petition - Aggrieved by the order passed by Single Judge appellants appealed before DB - DB dismissed said appeal holding that appellants' contention of being no scope for disciplinary action against erring employees could not be accepted, as respondents had been granted ad-hoc promotion and stated that respondent's decision not to appear in examination in subsequent year could not act as an estoppel for challenging the action of appellants - Hence, instant appeal - Respondent contended that appellants did not took any plea with regard to the cancellation of whole examination - Whether HC was justified in interfering with decision taken by appellants and holding that merely as respondents had been given ad-hoc promotion - Held, HC ought not to have interfered with decision taken by appellants requiring candidates, who appeared in cancelled examination, to reappear in subsequent examination, in order to qualify regular promotion - Candidate had to be subjected to disciplinary proceeding which had to be conducted on basis of report submitted u/r. 14(4) of Rules - Since it was mass-copying case, r. 14 of Rules would not, improve case of respondents as it merely enabled disciplinary authority to impose major penalty on a candidate who was found to have used unfair means - Merely because no disciplinary proceedings was initiated against respondents, would not be justified to hold that cancellation of result was in any manner, impermissible - HC was not justified in holding that merely because respondents had been given ad-hoc promotion, the previous alleged misconduct stood wiped out - Respondents were given equal opportunity to compete in examination subsequent to the cancellation of their examination result - It was matter of record that some candidates who were similarly placed took advantage of the order passed by Tribunal, and appeared in subsequent examination had been promoted in accordance with rule to next higher post - Respondents, chose not to appear in examination, at that stage they should not be permitted to complain that they had been treated unfairly - Judgments of Single Judge and DB were set aside - Appeal allowed.
