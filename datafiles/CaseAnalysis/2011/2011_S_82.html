High Court of Judicature, Patna and another v Shiveshwar Narayan and another

Supreme Court of India

22 September 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, J. S. KHEHAR

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 669; (2011) 15 SCC 317; 2012(1) ALL MR 431; 2012 (1) ALT 13; 2012 (1) AWC 886; 2011 (8) MLJ(SC) 1062; 2011(11) SCALE 369; [2011] 15 S.C.R. 51; 2013 (1) SLR 655; 2011 (4) WLN(SC) 55

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Constitution

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Age - Superannuation - Extension of service - Respondent/Judicial officer was required to be considered for extension of service as he was attaining age of 58 years - Committee on consideration of respondent service record and other relevant material including general reputation, efficiency, integrity and honesty, finally resolved that respondent was not fit for further continuance in service in public interest as he did not have potential for continued useful service - Committee evaluation was approved denying benefit of increase of retirement age to respondent - Respondent challenged communication in petition before HC and DB quashed communication and directed HC on its administrative side to re-evaluate his case for extension from 58 to 60 years - Hence, instant appeals - Whether DB was justified in its power of judicial review u/art. 226 of Constitution to interfere with unanimous administrative decision of Full Court - Held, committee on examination did not consider appellant fit for continued useful service after attaining age of 58 years - Full Court unanimously accepted and approved decision of Committee - DB failed to keep in mind distinction between judicial review and merit review and committed serious error in examining merits of decision of Full Court - In instant case where DB was examining each complaint and material against respondent to find out correctness of decision of Full Court which was legally not permissible - Weight of material was not capable of re-assessment while sitting in judicial review over such decision - Even if, some other view was possible on material that was considered by Committee and Full Court to evaluate respondent's case for extension of superannuation age to 60 years, it was opined, that did not justify interference in decision of Full Court which was founded on material and relevant considerations - Further, there was no allegation of bias or mala fides- nor it could had been against decision making authority - Hence, DB was in error in interfering with decision of HC on administrative side in not extending benefit of enhancement of retirement age of respondent from 58 to 60 years - Appeals disposed of.
