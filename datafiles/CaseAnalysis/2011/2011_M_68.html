State of Uttar Pradesh and others v Madhav Prasad Sharma

Supreme Court of India

10 January 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 45; (2011) 2 SCC 212; 2011 (1) ESC 144; 2011 (128) FLR 915; JT 2011 (1) SC 326; 2011 (3) MLJ(SC) 596; 2011(1) SCALE 209; [2011] 1 S.C.R. 266; 2011 (1) SLR 655; 2011 (1) UPLBEC 595

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Absence From Duty, Allahabad High Court Rules, 1952, Uttar Pradesh Subordinate Police Officers/Employees (Punishment and Appeal) Rules, 1991

Summary: (A) Service - Practice & Procedures - Uttar Pradesh Subordinate Police Officers/Employees (Punishment and Appeal) Rules, 1999 - Allahabad High Court Rules, 1952, r. 5 - Order of Single Judge - Special Appeal before the DB - Maintainability - Respondent/Police Constable was absent from the duty for 101 days without permission - Disciplinary proceedings initiated against respondent, however, respondent failed to appear before the Enquiry Officer - Appointing Authority, consequently, terminated the service of respondent after accepting the findings of enquiry report - Respondent preferred departmental appeal, which was dismissed by Appellate Authority - Respondent filed writ petition before the HC - Single Judge allowed respondent's writ petition on the ground that the respondent had been sanctioned leave without pay and subsequently his service was terminated on the same ground and as such two punishments were inflicted for one charge which was not permissible in law - Appellant/department challenged the Single Judge order before the DB - DB dismissed appellant's special appeal on the ground of maintainability - Whether the special appeal preferred by the appellant/department before the DB against the order of the Single Judge allowing the writ petition filed by the respondent is maintainable - Held, delinquent availed departmental appeal to the Appellate Authority against the order of termination - After the order of the Single Judge no further appeal by way of special appeal before the DB would lie - Appellant Authority passed the order after considering the materials placed on record and order of the Disciplinary Authority - Single Judge, thereafter, passed the order impugned in the writ petition in exercise of appellate jurisdiction in terms of the r. 5 of the High Court Rules - Therefore, the conclusion arrived at by the DB in the impugned order holding the special appeal filed by the appellant was not maintainable, is proper - Appeal partly allowed.

(B) Service - Uttar Pradesh Subordinate Police Officers/Employees (Punishment and Appeal) Rules, 1999, rr. 4, 7, 8 - Unauthorised absence - Termination of service - Legality - Whether the order of the Single Judge quashing the order of termination of the respondent is sustainable - Held, r. 4 of the Rules clearly indicated that 'sanctioning leave without pay' is not one of the prescribed punishments - Disciplinary authority is competent to impose appropriate penalty from those provided in r. 4 of the Rules which deals with the major penalties and minor penalties - Denial of salary on the ground of 'no work no pay' cannot be treated as a penalty in view of statutory provisions contained in r. 4 defining the penalties in clear terms - R. 7 empowers the Government or any Officer of the Police to award the punishment mentioned in r. 4 - Thus the punishment of dismissal from the service is the punishment which has been awarded to the respondent in accordance with rr. 4 and 8 of the Rules and hence, there is no question of awarding two punishments in respect of one charge - Further, Doctrine of double jeopardy enshrined in art. 20(2) of the Constitution of India has no application in the event of there being only one punishment awarded to the respondent under the Rules on charges being proved during the course of disciplinary enquiry - Therefore, conclusion of the Single Judge that the delinquent had suffered two punishments cannot be sustained and hence set aside - Matter remitted back to Single Judge for fresh disposal - Appeal partly allowed.
