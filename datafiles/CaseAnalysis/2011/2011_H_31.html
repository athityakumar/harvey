State Of H.P. & Ors. v Himachal Pradesh Nizi Vyavsayik Prishikshan Kendra Sangh

Supreme Court of India

20 April 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 274; (2011) 6 SCC 597; 2011 (2) CLT(SC) 180; 2011 (3) ESC 502; JT 2011 (5) SC 153; 2011(4) SCALE 801; [2011] 5 S.C.R. 533; 2011 (3) SLR 376

Case Digest

Subject: Education

Summary: Education - Interference in decision of cabinet - Government of Himachal Pradesh, in consonance with National Policy of Education (NPE) decided to adopt a policy for developing manpower in conventional as well as in emerging areas of Engineering and Technology and in other professional disciplines - State Government framed Technical Education Policy and the Department of Technical Education issued guidelines for Vocational Training Centres (VTCs) in Himachal Pradesh - In the year 2004, the State Government invited private parties/institutions to open Vocational Training Centres (VTCs) within the State of Himachal Pradesh - Subsequently, decision was taken in the meeting of State Council for Vocational Training (SCVT) to wind up certain courses - Despite the endeavour of the State Government to promote and encourage the participation of the private sector, it had not accorded permission to the institutions to run the vocational courses for the academic Session 2007-08 - Members of the respondent's Association (unregistered association of Vocational Training Centres) made representations to the State Government with regard to the same - Thereafter, in the meeting, it was decided that all the issues raised in the meeting including cancellation of affiliation, permission for fresh admissions and starting of fresh courses in different VTCs would be examined by a Sub-Committee - Sub-Committee, so constituted, submitted its report to the Government and the matter was taken up in the Cabinet meeting - Effect of the decision of the Cabinet was that for the academic session 2007-08 there would be no admission for the courses which were being taught by the respondent association - Respondent associations filed writ petition - HC, by the impugned order, allowed the writ petition and quashed cabinet decision discontinuing the three courses - Hence, instant petition - Held, with regard to the importance of human resources, especially manpower requirement in various professional and technical fields, the Government is free to frame its policy, alter or modify the same as to the needs of the society - In such matters, the Courts could not interfere lightly - In the instant case, respondent-association had not sought for quashing of any policy or scheme or decision or order of the State Government but only prayed for certain directions for admission of students in SCVT courses for the session 2007-08 - HC without reopening the case and hearing both sides about the matter as to the subsequent development, simply quashed and set aside the same by issuing various directions - Conclusion of the HC quashing the Cabinet decision and as a consequence issuing several directions was unacceptable and contrary to the well established principles - Further, quashing of the Cabinet decision without analyzing the pros and cons in the manner seeks to restrict the State's constitutional authority and powers to frame policy especially in such vital areas like imparting technical education was not acceptable - HC was not justified in interfering with the Cabinet decision which was not the issue or challenge in the writ petition - Impugned order of the HC quashing the Cabinet decision and issuing various directions was set aside - Appeal allowed.
