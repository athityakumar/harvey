Chandu Khamaru v Nayan Malik and others

Supreme Court of India

02 September 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Patnaik, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 618; (2011) 12 SCC 314; 2011 (6) AWC 5441; 2012 (1) CGLJ 170; 2011 (3) CLT(SC) 431; 2012 (1) RCR(Civil) 151; [2011] 11 S.C.R. 112

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Electricity & Energy

Summary: Electricity & Energy - Administrative -Electricity Act, 2003, ss. 42(1), 43(1), 67, 67(1), 67(2) - Supply of Electricity - Location of Passage - Entitlement - Distribution Licensee had provided electricity to house of appellant by electric line taken through passage located on Dag Nos. 406, 407 and 409 which belonged to respondents nos. 1 to 3 and not appellant - Respondent nos. 1 to 3 filed petition in HC praying for writ prohibiting distribution licensee to give electric connection in favour of appellant through passage situated on Dag Nos. 406, 407 and 409 - Single Judge held that appellant was not entitled to get supply through land in Dag Nos.406, 407 and 409 until appellant had established his right over land in Civil Court - DB also dismissed appeal filed by appellant by holding that until private dispute between appellant and respondent nos. 1 to 3 was resolved in Civil Court, distribution licensee should not supply electricity to house of appellant through disputed land - Hence, instant appeal - Whether appellant had statutory right to apply for and obtain supply of electricity from distribution licensee and distribution licensee had corresponding statutory obligation to supply electricity to appellant - Held, by s. 42(1) of the 2003 Act, every distribution licensee had duty to develop and maintain efficient and economical distribution system in his area of supply and to supply electricity in accordance with provisions contained in the 2003 Act - S. 43(1) of the 2003 Act had provided that every distribution licensee, should, on application by owner or occupier of any premises, give supply of electricity to such premises, within one month after receipt of application requiring such supply - S. 67(1) of the 2003Act had provided that licensee might, from time to time, but subject always to terms and conditions of his licensee, within area of supply carry out works mentioned in clauses (a) to (f) therein - S. 67(1)(d) of the 2003 Act had provided that licensee might lay down and place electric lines, electrical plant and other works - By s. 67(2)(b) of the 2003 Act, appropriate Govt. might, by rules, specify authority which might grant permission in circumstances where owner or occupier objects to carrying out of works - Respondent nos. 1 to 3 also did not object to supply of electricity by distribution licensee to appellant - Hence, distribution licensee would have to find out whether there was any other way in which electric line could be drawn for supply of electricity to house of appellant, other than disputed passage - Moreover, if there was no other way to supply electricity to house of appellant, distribution licensee would have to follow provisions of s. 67(2) of the 2003 Act for carrying out work for supply of electricity to house of appellant - Appeal allowed.
