Rasiklal Manickchand Dhariwal & Anr v M/S. M.S.S. Food Products

Supreme Court of India

25 November 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 798; (2012) 2 SCC 196; 2012 (3) ALD(SC) 67; 2012(1) ALL MR 968; 2012 (1) AWC 717; 2012 (4) Bom.C.R. 730; 2011 (4) CLT(SC) 449; 2012 (1) CTC 741; 2012 (2) MLJ(SC) 595; 2012 (2) RCR(Civil) 307; [2011] 14 S.C.R. 1141; 2012 (2) WLN(SC) 8

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Intellectual Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Intellectual Property - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 9 r. 7 - O. 18 rr. 2(2), 2(3A), 15 and O. 20 r. 1 - Exparte decree - Appeal - Set aside - Validity - Suit for declaration was filed by respondents - Due to non-appearance of appellants their right to cross examine respondents were closed and appellants were set exparte - Application was filed by appellant for setting aside the said exparte order - Trial court dismissed said application and decreed the suit against appellants - Against the ex parte decree appellants preferred appeal which was dismissed by DB - Appellant filed appeal against said order contending that judgment passed by Presiding Officer of trial court and affirmed in appeal by HC was null as it was delivered by a Judge who never heard the matter - Appellant also contended that predecessor Judge had recorded evidence ex parte and heard respondent reserved judgment and fixed the date for pronouncement of judgment but she never delivered judgment - Appellant also contended that successor Presiding Officer though heard various applications made by appellants but never heard parties insofar as suit was concerned and delivered judgment which apparently was not in conformity with legal mandate that one who hears the matter must decide the case - (A) Whether the Code empowers a judge to proceed with suit from the stage at which his predecessor left it - Held, the expression 'from the stage at which his predecessor left it' was wide and comprehensive enough to take in its fold all situations and stages of suit - No category or exception deserves to be carved out while giving full play to O. 18 r. 15 of CPC which amply empowers successor Judge to proceed with suit from the stage at which his predecessor left it - (B) Whether hearing of suit was confined to oral hearing - Held, 'hearing of the suit' as understood was not confined to oral hearing - Hearing of the suit begins when evidence in suit begins and was concluded by pronouncement of judgment - Code contemplates that at various stages of hearing of suit - Judge may change or he may be prevented from concluding trial and in that situation, successor Judge must proceed in suit from stage the predecessor Judge had left it - Appeal dismissed.
