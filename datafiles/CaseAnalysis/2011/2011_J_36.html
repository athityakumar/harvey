Mahatma Gandhi University & Anr. v Jikku Paul & Ors. Etc.Etc.

Supreme Court of India

08 April 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 305; (2011) 15 SCC 242; 2012 (132) FLR 856; 2011(4) SCALE 578; 2012 (3) SLR 350

Case Digest

Subject: Education

Keywords: All India Council for Technical Education (Grant of Approvals for Technical Institutions) Regulations, 2010

Summary: Education - Lateral Entry Scheme (LES) - Eligibility prescribed by State Government - Legality - State Government, by G.O. dated 13-11-2002, accorded sanction for admitting diploma-holders in the State, directly to second year of engineering degree (B-Tech) course, subject to concurrence of All India Council for Technical Education (AICTE) and Universities concerned - State Government, by G.O. dated 28-5-2008, subsequently provided that candidates who had passed diploma in engineering, having a minimum of 60% marks and who had appeared in State level entrance test and secured a minimum of 20% marks in entrance test, would be admitted to second year/third semester of B-Tech course under LES - Respondent students in self-financing colleges challenged the eligibility condition set by G.O. 28-5-2008 that students required to get 20% marks in State level entrance test - HC allowed respondents' writ petitions holding that State Government could prescribe minimum marks in entrance test as an additional qualification only for admission to seats in colleges run by it or under its control - Appellant University contended that self-financing engineering colleges were not exempted from additional requirement prescribed by State Government and HC committed a serious error in holding it was not essential to secure minimum 20% marks in LES for admission to second year of Engineering course - Held, as per scheme of AICTE to seek lateral entry to an engineering degree, candidate should have passed diploma in engineering in relevant branch with a minimum of 60% marks in aggregate - Said clause also provided that selection of candidates would be based on entrance test, merit ranking in test being basis of admission - As per LES of State Government, additional requirement was that candidates should also secure minimum of 20% marks in entrance test - In view of decision in Visveswaraya Technological University & Anr. v. Krishnendu Halder & Ors., 2011 INDLAW SC 191, contentions of appellant were accepted and decision of HC was set aside - However, instant judgment would be applicable only on next academic year (2010-2011) and students who were admitted for 2008-2009 and 2009-2010, even if they did not possess minimum qualifying marks in entrance test, should not be disturbed and should be allowed to complete their course - Appeal allowed.
