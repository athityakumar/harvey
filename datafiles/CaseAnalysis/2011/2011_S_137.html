Asmathunnisa v State Of A.P. Represented By The Public Prosecutor, High Court Of A.P., Hyderabad & Another

Supreme Court of India

29 March 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Dalveer Bhandari, Deepak Verma

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 208; (2011) 11 SCC 259; AIR 2011 SC 1905; 2011 CRLJ 2594; 2011 (2) JCC 1318; JT 2011 (4) SC 195; 2011 (4) MLJ(Crl) 121; 2011 (1) OLR 1088; 2011 (2) RCR(Criminal) 571; 2011(4) SCALE 101; [2011] 3 S.C.R. 1116

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 482 - Scheduled Castes and the Scheduled Tribes (Prevention of Atrocities) Act, 1989, s. 3(1)(x) - Quashing of criminal proceedings - Complaint was filed against appellant and her husband u/s. 3(1)(x) of the Act alleging that offending words were spoken by husband of the appellant against complainant - Appellant was also implicated because she had accompanied her husband to the house of the complainant - Appellant filed a petition before the HC u/s. 482 of the CrPC for quashing the proceedings for an offence u/s. 3(1)(x) of the Act - Petition was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether in view of the fact that the appellant did not utter the offending words and she merely accompanied her husband, s. 3(1)(x) of the Act was not attracted? - Held, relied on SC judgment in M. Mohan v. State 2011 INDLAW SC 138 wherein it was held that the if all the facts mentioned in the complaint were accepted as correct in its entirety and even then the complaint did not disclose the essential ingredients of an offence, in such a case the HC should ensure that such frivolous prosecutions were quashed under its inherent powers u/s. 482 of the CrPC - In the instant case, HC ought to have exercised its jurisdiction u/s. 482 of the CrPC and quashed the complaint qua the appellant only to prevent abuse of the process of law - Complaint qua the appellant quashed - Appeal disposed of.
