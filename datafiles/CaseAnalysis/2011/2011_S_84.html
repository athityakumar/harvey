Shradhha Aromatics Private Limited v O.L. Of Global Arya Industries Limited And Others

Supreme Court of India

24 May 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 368; (2011) 6 SCC 207; [2011] 164 Comp Cas 396; 2011 (2) DRTC 609; 2012 (1) G.L.R. 74; JT 2011 (6) SC 445; 2011(6) SCALE 330B

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate

Keywords: Adjourned

Summary: Corporate - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Acceptance of revised bid - Challenged - Company Judge ordered winding up of the company and appointed the Official Liquidator who proposed sale of the assets of the company - Advertisement was issued for sale of the assets of the company showing reserve price of the land etc. as Rs. 64 Lakhs - Company Judge approved the highest offer of Rs.127 lakhs given by respondent no.2 vide order dt. 30.08.2007 - Subsequently, appellant filed application for recall of said order by stating that it was interested in making an offer of Rs. 141 lakhs - Company Judge dismissed said application on the ground that there was no tangible reason to review the earlier order - Appellant filed another application for recall of earlier order dt. 30.08.2007 and also revised his offer - Company Judge allowed the second application - Respondent no. 2 challenged said order before DB who held that the confirmed auction sale cannot be set aside merely because subsequently a higher price was offered by one of the bidders - Hence, the instant appeal - During the pendency of the appeal, intervenor-cum-promoter (Third Party) made an application submitting that he was willing to give a better offer of Rs. 7.60 crores - Whether confirmed auction sale can be recalled on the ground that the another bidder had offered better price? - Held, ordinarily the court was loathe to accept the offer made by any bidder or a third party after acceptance of the highest bid/offer given pursuant to an advertisement issued or an auction held by a public authority - However, in the peculiar facts of instant case, court was inclined to make a departure from said rule as if the order of the DB was sustained, the creditors of the Company were bound to suffer because the amount available for repayment of the dues of the creditors would be a paltry sum of Rs.127 lakhs - As against this, if the offer made by the intervenor-cum-promoter was accepted, the Official Liquidator will get an additional amount of more than Rs.4.25 crores - The availability of such huge amount will certainly be in the interest of the creditors - Therefore, it was not possible to approve the order passed by the DB - Hence, impugned order was set aside - The offer of the intervenor-cum-promoter was accepted - Appeal disposed of.
