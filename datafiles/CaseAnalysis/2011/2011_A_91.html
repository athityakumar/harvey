Keshavadas Shridharao Savakar & Others v Assistant Commnr. & Land Acquisition Officer & Another

Supreme Court of India

28 January 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Ganguly, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 66; (2011) 11 SCC 476; 2011 (2) AWC 1973; 2011 (2) CLT(SC) 299; JT 2011 (2) SC 203; 2011(2) SCALE 4

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4(1), 6, 8 and 23 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 142 - Land acquisition proceedings - Quantum of market value of land acquired - Land was acquired for Karnataka Housing Board (Board) - Land Acquisition Officer determined the market value at Rs.35,000 per acre and passed an award to that effect - Reference was filed thereagainst before Reference Court - Reference Court fixed the market value of the acquired land at Rs.14,500 per gunta - On appeal, HC quashed the judgment under appeal and remanded the matter to the Reference Court with a direction to the parties to lead additional evidence - Hence, present appeal - Held, having regards to fact that about 20 years have elapsed since the initiation of acquisition proceedings and non-payment of the compensation to the landowners till date, the proposal of the landowners of agreeing to 75% deduction on the market value fixed by the Reference Court would meet the ends of justice - Board directed to pay to the appellants the amount of compensation as fixed by the Reference Court after imposing a deduction of 75% - Appeals disposed of.
