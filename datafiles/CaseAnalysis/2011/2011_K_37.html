Ketan V. Parekh and others v Special Director, Directorate of Enforcement and another

Supreme Court of India

29 November 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 814; (2011) 15 SCC 30; AIR 2012 SC 683; 2012 (3) ALD(SC) 119; 2011 (4) CLT(SC) 473; 2012 (275) E.L.T. 3; 2012 (3) MLJ(SC) 141; 2012 (1) RCR(Civil) 234; [2011] 14 S.C.R. 1204; 2012 (28) S.T.R. 195

Case Digest

Subject: Exchange Control & FDI; Fema; Practice & Procedure

Summary: FEMA - Exchange Control & FDI - Practice & Procedure - Foreign Exchange Management Act, 1999 - Limitation Act, 1963 - Special Court (Trial of Offences relating to Transactions in Securities) Act, 1992 - Penalty Dispensation of pre-deposit - Appeal - Jurisdiction - Limitation - Appellants purchased shares of a company through illegal source of income - Enforcement Directorate (ED) issued show cause notices and found appellants contravened ss. 3(d), 6(3)(e) of 1999 Act and imposed penalty - Appellants filed appeal u/s. 9 of 1999 Act, and application u/r. 10 of Foreign Exchange Management (Adjudication Proceedings and Appeal) Rules, 2000 (FEMR) r/w s. 19(1) of 1999 Act for dispensing with deposit of penalty amount - Appellant was notified person u/s. 3(2) of 1992 Act - Appellant contended that Debt Recovery Tribunal (DRT) in pursuant to notification of Government, ordered attachment of all their movables and immovables - SEBI debarred appellants from associating with securities market for 14 years - By orders of DRT and SEBI, appellants were suffering from grave financial hardship and were not in a position to deposit penalty amount and payment of pre-deposit would cause undue hardship - Appellate Tribunal dismissed appeal and directed to deposit 50% of penalty imposed by ED - Appellant filed appeal u/s. 35 of 1999 Act and an application to condone delay in filing appeal before HC, Delhi - DB dismissed application holding that delay could not be condoned as delay was more than 60 days apart from limitation period in filing appeal - DB further held that relief sought by appellants was appealable u/s. 35 of FEMA and not u/art. 226 of Constitution- Appellants thereafter filed a writ petition before HC, Bombay, along with application for condonation of delay - Appellant contended that they were prosecuting remedy before a wrong forum i.e., HC, Delhi - DB, Bombay held that there was no power to condone delay beyond 120 days - DB of Bombay HC further held that appellants deliberately invoked jurisdiction of Delhi HC despite fact that appellants were residents of Bombay and had contested other matters including proceedings of ED at Bombay -- Hence, instant appeal - (A)Whether delay in filing appeal could be condoned u/s. 5 of 1963 Act, when there was a remedy available u/s. 35 of Act - Held, orders of Appellant Tribunal were appealable u/s. 35 of 1992 Act and there was no remedy available to appellants u/art. 226 of Constitution - Bombay HC had exclusive jurisdiction to entertain an appeal u/s. 35 of 1999 Act - Parties should not be allowed to indulge in forum shopping - - Manner in which appellants prosecuted writ petitions before the Delhi HC left no room for doubt that they had done so with sole object of delaying compliance of direction given by Appellate Tribunal and it could not be said that they were bona fide prosecuting remedy before a wrong forum - When any special statute prescribed certain period of limitation as well as provision for extension up to specified time-limit, on sufficient cause being shown, then period of limitation prescribed under special law would prevail and to that extent provisions of Limitation Act should stand excluded - (B) Whether Appellants were entitled for relief of dispensation of pre-deposit of penalty - Held, appellants failed to disclose factual details of their properties before appellate tribunal and failed to make out case for dispense of pre-deposit - Appellate Tribunal was justified in not relieving appellants from statutory obligation to deposit the amount of penalty - Appellants had exclusive knowledge of their financial condition/status and it was their duty to candidly disclose all their assets, movable and immovable including those in respect of which orders of attachment was ordered by judicial and quasi-judicial forums, instead to come clean, appellants tried to paint a gloomy picture about their financial position, which Appellate Tribunal rightly refused to accept - A mere assertion about undue hardship would not be sufficient - Undue hardship was caused when the hardship was not warranted by the circumstances - It was for tribunal to impose such conditions as were deemed proper to safeguard interests of Revenue - Order of Appellant Tribunal was upheld - Appeals dismissed.
