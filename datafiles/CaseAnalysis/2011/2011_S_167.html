Sarojben Ashwinkumar Shah Etc. v State Of Gujarat & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

10 August 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 496; (2011) 13 SCC 316; 2011 ALL MR (Cri) 2924; 2011 (3) BC 543; 2012 CRLJ 430; 2011 (3) RCR(Criminal) 852; 2011(8) SCALE 542; [2011] 9 S.C.R. 1138

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, ss. 138, 141 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 420, 114 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 319 - 2nd respondent advanced a sum to partnership firm in question consisting of accused persons - Accused persons (partners) issued a cheque in favour of 2nd respondent - Since accused persons failed to make payment, 2nd respondent presented the cheque for encashment - Cheque in question dishonoured with remark 'account closed' - 2nd respondent lodged a complaint alleging offence u/s. 138 of the 1881 Act and u/ss. 420, 114 IPC against partners - 2nd respondent subsequently filed an application before the Trial Court to implicate appellants as accused in the case since they were joined as new partners in the firm, which evidenced the filing of copy of registration of the firm by existing partners - Trial Court directed the appellants be joined as accused in the case and HC maintained such direction - Whether the HC was justified in allowing the appellants be added as accused in the instant case - Held, HC failed to consider whether Trial Court had addressed to the essential aspects before invoking his power u/s. 319 of the Code - Further, HC did not advert to the question whether or not filing of copy of registration of the firm by other existing partners would be covered by expressions 'in the course of any inquiry into or trial' and 'evidence' occurring in s. 319 of the Code and also the aspect as to whether such document could be treated as an evidence to show that the appellant (newly added accused) had committed an offence of cheating u/s. 420 IPC - As regards the criminal liability of a partner in the firm, in light of the provisions contained in s. 141 of the Act, there had to be evidence that at the time the offence was committed, the partner was in-charge of and was responsible to the firm for the conduct of the business of the firm - Impugned order showed that all these relevant aspects had not been considered by the HC at all and the petitions u/s. 482 of the Code were dismissed - Matter ought to be considered by the HC afresh - Appeals allowed.
