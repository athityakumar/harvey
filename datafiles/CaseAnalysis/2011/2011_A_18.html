Mig Cricket Club v Abhinav Sahakar Education Society and others

Supreme Court of India

05 September 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Chandramauli Kumar Prasad, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 556; (2011) 9 SCC 97; 2011(6) ALL MR 436; 2011 (6) Bom.C.R. 546; JT 2011 (10) SC 394; 2012 (1) RCR(Civil) 715; 2011(10) SCALE 8; [2011] 11 S.C.R. 141

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Land & Property - Municipalities & Local Governments - Maharashtra Regional and Town Planning Act, 1966 - Allotment of land - Change of user - 1st respondent Society was allotted land in question for its school - However, 1st respondent brought to the notice of 4th respondent Development Authority and 5th respondent Municipal Corporation that the area in question had been reserved for a playground in the draft development plan - 1st respondent sought change of user of the land - In exercise of the powers u/s. 37(2) of the Act, a notification dated 10-4-1985 came to be issued and published in the Government Gazette on 25-4-1985 excluding the site reserved for the playground and the land so released to 1st respondent was earmarked for the 'school and cultural centre' in the development plan of the area - Appellant Cricket Club subsequently approached the State Government for change of the user of the plot in question for 'cricket playground' - Notification dated 24-4-1992 published in the Gazette which revealed that State Government in exercise of the powers conferred u/s. 31(1) of the Act, had modified the user of the land in question and instead of land being shown reserved for 'school and cultural centre' it was shown as a 'playground' - 1st respondent challenged the Notification dated 24-4-1992 before the HC - HC allowed 1st respondent's writ petition holding that the impugned notification dated 24-4-1992 had been issued without consideration of the notification dated 10-4-1985 which rendered the same illegal - Whether the notification dated 24-4-1992 issued in exercise of the powers u/s. 31(1) of the Act was legal, valid and complied with the provisions of the Act - Held, State Government was conferred with the power to make minor modification to the final development plan - Thus, under the scheme of the Act, a development plan sanctioned by the State Government prior to the commencement of the Act, should be deemed to be the final development plan and there could be minor modification in such development plan by the State Government in exercise of power conferred u/s. 37(2) of the Act - Development plan existing prior to the commencement of the Act showed that the area in question was reserved for 'playground' which was modified to 'school and cultural society' in exercise of power u/s. 37(2) of the Act and earmarked for the 'school and cultural centre' by notification dated 25-4-1985 and such a course was permissible under law - It was the 1st respondent's plea that the Corporation informed it that in the proposed development plan the area in question had been shown as 'cricket club and playground' - Had the notification dated 25-4-1985 been a sanction of final Development plan, the area in question ought not to have figured in the draft development plan submitted to the State Government - Draft plan submitted to the State Government was considered by it and the development plan dated 24-4-1992 was sanctioned - It was not the modification of the development plan but sanction of the same in exercise of the power u/s. 31(1) of the Act - HC misdirected itself by considering the notification dated 10-4-1985 to be the sanction of the Development plan u/s. 37(2) of the Act and the notification dated 24-4-1992 to be the modification of the final Development plan which had rendered its order illegal - It is trite that the validity of the order did not depend upon the section mentioned in the order - Wrong provision mentioned in the order itself did not invalidate the order, if it was found that order could be validly passed under any other provision - However in the instant one, contrary to what had been mentioned in the notifications the Court should not say that such powers were not exercised to render the notification illegal if in fact such power existed - In the facts of the instant case, power had been exercised in accordance with law and there was no arbitrariness in the same - Impugned judgment of the HC was set aside - Appeal allowed.
