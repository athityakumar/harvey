Collector, Bilaspur v Ajit P. K. Jogi & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

13 October 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, H.L. Dattu

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 742; (2011) 10 SCC 357; AIR 2012 SC 44; 2012(1) ALL MR 395; 2012 (5) AWC 4737; 2012 (1) MLJ(SC) 357; [2011] 12 S.C.R. 1045

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Election; Socio-Economic

Summary: Election - Socio-Economic - Constitution - Persons With Disabilities (Equal Opportunities, Protection of Rights and Full Participation) Act, 1995 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 338 - Scheduled caste - Scheduled Tribe - Election - Caste certificate - Deprivation - Respondent no. 1 on basis of caste certificates claimed that he belonged to particular scheduled tribe and also contested parliamentary elections - After State of Chattisgarh came into existence respondent no. 1 became first chief minister and served term - Further, complaint was filed before National Commission for Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes by respondent no. 6, alleging that respondent no. 1 was Christian and that he did not belong to Scheduled Tribe - He had contested elections from a constituency reserved for Scheduled Tribes based on false certificates - Commission made an order and called upon State Govt.to verify genuineness of ST certificate - Respondent no. 1 challenged said order and filed a writ petition in HC - HC ,held that entire exercise was politically motivated and directed State Govt. and commission to pay back actual costs incurred by respondent no. 1 -- Hence, instant appeal - Whether commission had jurisdiction to entertain complaints about genuineness of caste certificate and pronounce upon its validity and whether HC was justified in holding that proceedings before Commission at instance of respondent no. 6 were politically motivated - Held, u/art. 338 of Constitution, it should be duty of Commission to enquire into specific complaints and if any particular person had made a false claim of being a person belonging to a scheduled tribe, Commission was duty bond to enquire with respect to deprivation of rights and safeguards to scheduled castes and scheduled tribes - After examining material collected, found that respondent no. 1 was a Christian, Commission had directed State Govt.to conduct verification of genuineness of ST certificate and initiate action for cancellation of certificate - No justification was found for HC to either term application given by respondent no. 6 to Commission as politically motivated -- Appeals partly allowed.
