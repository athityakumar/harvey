A. P. Industrial Infrastructure Corporation Limited v Chinthamaneni Narasimha Rao and others

Supreme Court of India

15 September 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

A.R. Dave, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 586; (2012) 12 SCC 797; 2012 (1) ALD(SC) 104; 2011 (89) ALR 253; 2012 (2) AWC 1109; 2011 (3) CLT(SC) 445; JT 2011 (11) SC 42; 2011 (4) RCR(Civil) 831; 2011 (114) RD 843; 2011(10) SCALE 460

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Administrative - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4, 6 - Land acquisition - Issuance of declaration u/s. 6 of the Act - Delay - Respondents' lands were acquired by the State for industrial purpose - Land Acquisition Officer passed award - Respondents challenged the land acquisition proceedings before the HC on the ground that declaration u/s. 6 of the Act was passed beyond the prescribed period - Single Judge dismissed respondents' writ petitions - DB, however, allowed respondents' writ appeal and held that the declarations u/s. 6 was made by the State beyond the stipulated period - Appellant beneficiary filed review petition before the DB - DB dismissed review by impugned order - Whether the HC was right in setting aside the acquisition proceedings on the ground of delay in issuing the declaration u/s. 6 of the Act - Held, notification u/s. 4 of the Act was issued on 27-8-1993 and it was lastly published, in the locality where the land was situated, on 29-9-1993 - After some litigation, ultimately the land in question had been acquired - Declaration u/s. 6 was made on 7-8-1996 and an award was made on 7-1-1998 - Though there was some dispute with regard to the fact of taking possession of the land, it was an admitted fact that at least paper possession of the land in question was taken on 9-3-1998 and possession was handed over to the appellant on 16-7-1998 - Thus, the declaration u/s. 6 of the Act was made on 7-8-1996 and the award was made on 7-1-1998 - A petition challenging the validity of the declaration u/s. 6 of the Act on the ground that it was declared beyond the period specified u/s. 6 of the Act was filed in November, 1998 - Petition challenging the declaration u/s. 6 of the Act had been filed at a belated stage - If the land owners were really aggrieved by the declaration u/s. 6 of the Act, they ought to have challenged the same immediately after the declaration u/s. 6 was made - Award was made on 7-1-1998 and even possession was taken on 9-3-1998 and on 16-7-1998, the possession was also handed over to the appellant so that it could use the land in question for a public purpose for which it had been acquired - No reason for the land owners to wait for a few years for challenging the declaration made u/s. 6 of the Act on the ground of delay - If the land owners had been really aggrieved, they ought to have challenged the proceedings immediately after declaration made u/s. 6 of the Act - Hence, no interference with the acquisition proceedings in question warranted - Appeal allowed.
