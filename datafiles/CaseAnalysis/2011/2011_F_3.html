Fee Regulatory Committee v Kalol Institute Of Management, Etc

Supreme Court of India

11 October 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Patnaik, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 732; (2011) 10 SCC 592; AIR 2012 SC 203; 2011 (5) ESC 756; 2012 (1) MLJ(SC) 529; [2011] 11 S.C.R. 829; 2012 (1) SLR 257

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Education

Keywords: Gujarat Professional Technical Educational Colleges or Institutions (Regulation of Admission Fixation of Fees) Act, 2007

Summary: Education - Administrative - Regulation of Admission Fixation of Fees, Act, 2007, ss. 10(1) (3) and 11(1) - Revision of Fees - Entitlement - Respondents /institutions ,colleges in State - S.10(1) of the Act provides that Fee Regulatory Committee Should determine fee structure and s.10(3) of the Act states that so determined fee should be binding on respondents/institutions, colleges for period of 3 years and applicable to student and it should not be revised and it was determined for 3 academic years 2008-2009, 2009-2010 and 2010-2011 - When state Govt. accepted recommendations of Sixth Pay Commission for revision of pay and allowances of employees w.e.f 1-1-2006 - Respondents sought revision of fees for students admitted in their colleges on ground that respondents had to pay their teaching and non-teaching staff revised pay and allowances as per recommendations of Sixth Pay Commission - But petitioner/ Fee Regulatory Committee declined to revise fees - Aggrieved, respondent filed writ petitions before HC which set aside orders of petitioner/Fee Regulatory Committee and remitted back to fresh consideration - Hence, instant appeals - Held, in Islamic Academy of Education and Another v. State of Karnataka and Others 2003 Indlaw SC 611 relied on - Further, fee fixed by Committee should be binding for period of 3 years and at end of 3 years, institution would be at liberty to apply for revision - Despite statutory provision in s. 10(3) of the Act, HC had directed to revise fees for academic years 2008-2009, 2009-2010 and 2010-2011, which had already been determined by petitioner/Fee Regulatory Committee and which could not be revised for period of 3 years - Hence, set aside impugned orders of HC and directed that increase in cost suffered by respondents/colleges, institutions on account of higher pay and allowances payable to teaching and non-teaching staff on basis of recommendations of Sixth Pay Commission would be taken into consideration by petitioner/Fee Regulatory Committee while determining fees for academic years 2011-2012, 2012-2013 and 2013-2014 and subsequent period of 3 years in accordance with provisions of the Act - Appeals allowed.
