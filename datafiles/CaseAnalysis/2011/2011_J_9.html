Haryana State Warehousing Corporation and another v Jagat Ram & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

23 February 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 122; (2011) 3 SCC 422; 2011 (130) FLR 360; JT 2011 (2) SC 398; 2011(2) SCALE 598; [2011] 2 S.C.R. 1151; 2011 (2) SLR 283

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Warehousing Corporations Act, 1962, Haryana State Warehousing Corporation (Officers and Staff) Regulations, 1994

Summary: Service - Haryana Warehousing Corporation (Officers & Staff) Regulations, 1994 - Promotion - Entitlement - 3rd respondent was promoted to the post of Assistant Manager (Administration) in the 1st appellant Corporation on account of his excellent service record in comparison to that of 1st respondent - 1st respondent filed writ petition challenging the promotion of 3rd respondent - Single Judge dismissed the writ petition holding that the service record of 3rd respondent was superior to that of 1st respondent and that the 1st appellant Corporation had not committed any error in granting promotion to 3rd respondent - 1st respondent filed writ appeal before the DB - DB allowed 1st respondent's appeal holding that since the criterion for promotion to the post of Assistant Manager (Administration) was seniority-cum-merit and not merit-cum-seniority, the promotion given to 1st respondent was not sustainable since such promotion had been made predominantly on the principle of merit, in contravention of the provisions of the Regulations - Whether the 3rd respondent entitled to the promotion over the 1st respondent - Held, in applying the principle of granting promotion on the basis of seniority-cum-merit, what is important was that the inter se seniority of all candidates who were eligible for consideration for promotion should be identified on the basis of length of service or on the basis of the seniority list as prepared, inasmuch as, it was such seniority which gave a candidate a right to be considered for promotion on the basis of seniority-cum-merit - Consequently, the candidate had to be fit to discharge the duties of the higher post and if his performance was assessed not to meet such a requirement, he could be passed over and those junior to him could be promoted despite his seniority in the seniority list - However, there was nothing on record to indicate that 1st respondent was not capable of discharging his functions in the promoted post of Assistant Manager (Administration) - 1st respondent was denied promotion only on the ground of the superior assessment that had been made in favour of 3rd respondent, which ran contrary to the concept of seniority-cum- merit - DB, therefore, was right in held that in cases of seniority-cum-merit, the comparative assessment was not contemplated and was not required to be made - Petitions dismissed.
