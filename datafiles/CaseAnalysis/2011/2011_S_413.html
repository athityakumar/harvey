Inderjit Singh Grewal v State of Punjab and another

Supreme Court of India

23 August 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 671; (2011) 12 SCC 588; 2012 ALL MR (Cri) 369; 2012 (2) CGLJ 595; 2012 CRLJ 309; JT 2011 (11) SC 177; 2011 (4) MLJ(Crl) 1048; 2011 (2) OLR 875; 2011 (4) RCR(Criminal) 1; 2011(9) SCALE 295; [2011] 10 S.C.R. 557

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Family & Personal

Summary: Family & Personal - Criminal - - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 468, 482 - Guardians and Wards Act, 1890 - Hindu Marriage Act, 1955, ss. 13B, 23(2) - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 107, 120B, 191, 193, 376, 40, 406, 43, 498A - Protection of Women from Domestic Violence Act, 2005, ss. 12, 2(f), 28, 32 - Divorce - Maintenance - Validity - Appellant and respondent no. 2 were married and had a son- Due to differences they decided to get divorce and proceedings were adjourned for period of 6 months - Parties appeared before Court on second motion, petition was allowed and marriage was dissolved - Respondent no. 2 filed complaint before SSP against appellant alleging that decree of divorce obtained was a sham transaction - Complaint was sent to SP and after enquiry was conducted it was reported that parties had been living separately after divorce and no case was made out against appellant - Aggrieved by order of Court application was filed by appellant u/s. 482 CrPC for quashing complaint - Respondent no. 2 filed Civil Suit in HC seeking declaration that decree of divorce, was null and void as it had been obtained by fraud - HC with impugned judgment and order dismissed application filed by appellant - Hence, instant appeal - Whether judgment and decree of competent Trial Court could be declared null and void in collateral proceedings, that too, criminal proceedings - Held, if a decree was void ab initio, declaration to that effect had to be obtained by person aggrieved from competent court such declaration could not be obtained in collateral proceedings - Respondent no.2, who did not change her stand in second motion and obtained a sham decree of divorce as alleged by her asked Criminal Court to sit in appeal against judgment and decree of competent Trial Court - Complaint was filed before Magistrate while decree of divorce had been granted by District Judge of another district - Therefore, it was incorrect that subordinate Criminal Court could sit in appeal against judgment and order of superior Trial Court, having different territorial jurisdiction - - Evidence adduced by respondent no. 2 could not be taken into consideration so long as judgment and decree of Trial Court subsists - Permitting Magistrate to proceed further with complaint under provisions of the 2005 Act was not compatible and in consonance with decree of divorce which still subsists and thus, process amounts to abuse of process of Court -- In instant case, permitting Court to proceed with complaint would be travesty of justice - Thus, impugned judgment and order was set aside - Appeal allowed.
