Saraswati Sugar Mills v Commissioner Of Central Excise, Delhi-Iii

Supreme Court of India

02 August 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Dattu, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 492; 2011 (270) E.L.T. 465; JT 2011 (8) SC 547; 2011(8) SCALE 203; [2011] 13 S.C.R. 579

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Exemption Notification

Summary: Excise - Indirect Tax - Central Excise Act, 1944 - Central Excise Rules, 1944, r. 57Q - Central Excise Tariff Act, 1985 - Exemption - Notification No. 67/95-CE dt. 16.03.1995 - Assessee, manufacturer of sugar and molasses was availing MODVAT credit facility on the excise duty paid for the capital goods used in the factory for manufacturing process u/r. 57A of the Rules - Assessee in order to modernize the manufacturing process of sugar and molasses, had installed new machineries by replacing the old one - However, certain machineries like cane milling plant, clarification plant, evaporator and pan boiling plant, power generation plant etc., required the support of structural items for their installation - In view of this, the assessee started the manufacturing of iron and steel structures, after purchasing excise duty paid iron and steel sheets, angles, nuts and bolts etc. for the installation of the said machineries - Thereafter, assessee filed a declaration u/r. 57Q of the Rules declaring iron and steel structures under sub-heading 7308.90 of Chapter 73 of CETA, 1985 as capital goods and claimed exemption under notification no. 67/95-CE - Asst. Commissioner denied said exemption to the assessee on the ground that the said notification was not applicable to the iron and steel structures as they were neither inputs used in relation to the manufacture of final product nor capital goods as per sub-rule (1) of r. 57Q of the Rules and confirmed the duty demand and imposed a penalty of Rs. 5,00,000/- - Commissioner confirmed the order of the Asst. Commissioner and held that the said iron and steel structures form the part of the building - On appeal, Tribunal reduced the amount of the penalty to Rs. 1,00,000/- and affirmed the demand of duty - Hence, the instant appeal - Whether the iron and steel structures manufactured and used captively in the factory for installation of the sugar manufacturing plant by the assessee can be classified as capital goods u/r. 57Q of the Rules? - Held, in the light of the meaning of the expression 'component parts' that the iron and steel structures were not essential requirements in the sugar manufacturing unit - Anything required to make the goods a finished item can be described as component parts - Iron and Steel structures would not go into the composition of vacuum pans, crystallizers etc. - If an article was an element in the composition of another article made out of it, such an article may be described as a component of another article - Thus, structures in question do not satisfy description of 'components' - Therefore, order of the tribunal upheld - Appeal dismissed.
