Union Of India And Others v Tantia Construction Pvt. Limited

Supreme Court of India

18 April 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 311; (2011) 5 SCC 697; 2011 (5) AWC 4568; 2011 (2) CLT(SC) 173; JT 2011 (5) SC 59; 2011 (3) RCR(Civil) 821; 2011(4) SCALE 745; [2011] 5 S.C.R. 397

Case Digest

Subject: Contract & Commercial

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Alteration in work contract - Petitioners, East Central Railways invited Risk and Cost Tender for the work of construction of a Rail Over-Bridge - Contract work was to be completed within 15 months from the date of issuance of the letter of acceptance - Contract was awarded to the Respondent Company and a letter of acceptance was issued - On account of changes in the design, Petitioner requested the Respondent Company to convey its consent for execution of the complete work, including the revised work - Respondent Company refused to give their consent for the execution of the complete work at the revised cost - On such refusal the Railways floated a separate Tender for the additional work of the extended portion - While the Tender process for the extended contract was going on, Respondent Company agreed to execute the varied contract at the same rate, terms and conditions of the contract agreement, but on condition that the price increase, due to the Price Variation Clause, would be payable to the company - Same was accepted and the time for the execution of the left-over work was extended - However, the Railways once again asked the Respondent Company to carry out the complete work, including the additional work of the Viaduct, at an approximate cost of Rs. 36.11 crores - Aggrieved by the stand taken by the Railways, the Respondent Company filed a Writ Petition challenging the directions given by the Railway Authorities for completion of the entire work, including the extended work - Respondent Company contended that having failed to get any suitable response to the fresh Tender floated in respect of the additional work, it was not open to the Petitioners to compel it to complete the same at an arbitrarily low price, particularly when the additional work was not part of the original Tender - Single Judge held that there was no breach of the agreement entered into between the Petitioners and the Respondent Company, since it was the Petitioners themselves who had altered the agreement by separately tendering the extended work - Writ Petition was allowed - Appeal filed thereagainst was dismissed - Hence, instant Special Leave Petition - Held, Respondent Company satisfactorily explained their position regarding their offer being confined only to the balance work of the original Tender and not to the extended work - Delay occasioned in starting the work was not on account of any fault or lapses on the part of the Respondent Company, but on account of the fact that the project design of the work to be undertaken could not be completed and ultimately involved change in the design itself - No reason to interfere with the views expressed by the HC - Special Leave Petition dismissed.
