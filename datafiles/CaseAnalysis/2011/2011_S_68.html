Sheelkumar Jain v The New India Assurance Co. Ltd. & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

28 July 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Patnaik, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 466; (2011) 12 SCC 197; 2011(5) ALL MR 380; 2013 (1) AWC 100; 2011 (131) FLR 307; JT 2011 (8) SC 179; 2011(8) SCALE 96; [2011] 9 S.C.R. 574; 2012 (1) SLR 305; 2011 (4) UPLBEC 3440

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: General Insurance (Termination, Superannuation and Retirement of Officers and Development Staff) Scheme, 1976, General Insurance (Employees') Pension Scheme, 1995, Reserve Bank of India Pension Regulations, 1990, UCO Bank (Employees') Pension Regulations, 1995

Summary: Service - Resignation - Pension under Pension Scheme - Entitlement - Appellant Administrative Officer in the respondent company submitted his resignation by giving three months notice as per cl. 5(1) of Pension Scheme, 1976 - Competent authority accepted the resignation and relieved the appellant from duty on 16-12-1991 after the completion of three months - Respondent introduced General Insurance (Employees) Pension Scheme, 1995 whereby respondent provided an option to employees who had retired before the first day of November, 1993 to opt for the new pension scheme with a condition that they had to refund the entire contribution of Provident Fund made by the respondent with interest - Appellant sought to avail the pension scheme by reimbursing respondent's contribution towards Provident Fund, however, his request was rejected by respondent - Appellant filed writ petition - Single Judge dismissed writ petition, thereafter, appellant's writ appeal also dismissed - Whether the determination of service of the appellant u/cl. 5(1) of the Pension Scheme, 1976 amounted to resignation in terms of cl. 22 or voluntary retirement in terms of cl. 30 of the Pension Scheme, 1995 - Held, cl. 22 of the Pension Scheme, 1995 stated that resignation of an employee from the service of the Corporation or a Company should entail forfeiture of his entire past service and consequently should not qualify for pensionary benefits, but did not define the term 'resignation' - Under cl. 30(1) of the Pension Scheme, 1995, an employee, who had completed 20 years of qualifying service, may by giving notice of not less than 90 days in writing to the appointing authority retire from service and u/cl. 30(2) of the Pension Scheme, 1995, the notice of voluntary retirement should require acceptance by the appointing authority - Since 'voluntary retirement' unlike 'resignation' did not entail forfeiture of past services and instead qualified for pension, an employee to whom cl. 30 of the Pension Scheme, 1995 applied should not be said to have 'resigned' from service - In the instant case, appellant had completed 20 years qualifying service and had given notice of not less than 90 days in writing to the appointing authority of his intention to leave service and the appointing authority had accepted notice of the appellant and relieved him from service - Hence, cl. 30 of the Pension Scheme, 1995 applied to the appellant even though in his relieving letter to the General Manager of respondent he had used the word 'resign' - Further, cls. 22 and 30 of the Pension Scheme, 1995 should not be so construed as to deprive of an employee of an Insurance Company, such as the appellant, who had put in the qualifying service for pension and who had voluntarily given up his service after serving 90 days notice in accordance with cl. 5(1) of the Scheme, 1976 - Hence, impugned HC judgment was set aside and direction issued to the respondents to consider the claim of appellant for pension in accordance with the Pension Scheme, 1995 - Appeal allowed.
