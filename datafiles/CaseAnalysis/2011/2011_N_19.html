Shanker Singh v Narinder Singh & Ors

Supreme Court of India

15 December 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Gokhale, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 760; 2012 (5) ALD(SC) 108; 2012 (90) ALR 698; 2012 (2) AWC 1826; 2012 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 591; 2012 (1) OLR 190; 2012 (165) PLR 650; 2012 (1) RCR(Civil) 576; 2012 (117) RD 608; [2011] 16 S.C.R. 351

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Specific Relief Act 1963, ss. 12, 14 and 20 - Suit for specific performance - Appellant entered into an agreement to sell certain property with 1st respondent and paid earnest money - On agreed date of registration appellant did not turn up for registration - Respondent gave a notice to appellant to execute sale deed - Appellant did not respond, and therefore 1st respondent filed suit for specific performance - Trial Court decreed the suit - On appeal, HC set aside decree of Trial Court - DB allowed second appeal - Hence, instant appeal - (A) whether HC erred in applying the provisions of ss. 12, 14 and 20 of the Act - Held, inspite of the offer of relinquishment made by respondents, specific performance of agreement could not be granted, solely on the ground that it was incapable of being performed - HC erred in applying provisions of ss. 12, 14 and 20 of the Act, to the facts of the instant case and in exercising its discretion, since it was not a case for specific performance - (B) whether agreement in question being vague in nature was incapable of being performed - Held, it could not be ignored that inspite of agreement, land remained with appellant all throughout in view of orders passed by courts from time to time, due to which he had benefited - Specific performance of agreement was denied basically because of finding that the agreement was incapable of being performed inspite of the offer of relinquishment - Impugned HC judgement was set aside - Appeal allowed
