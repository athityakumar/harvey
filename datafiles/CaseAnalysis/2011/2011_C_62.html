Commissioner Of Central Excise, Chandigarh v M/S Doaba Steel Rolling Mills

Supreme Court of India

06 July 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

D.K. Jain, H.L. Dattu

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 421; JT 2011 (10) SC 456; 2011(7) SCALE 282; [2011] 7 S.C.R. 934

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Central Excise Tariff Act, 1944, Hot Re-Rolling Steel Mills Annual Capacity Determination Rules, 1997

Summary: Excise - Indirect Tax - Central Excise Act, 1944, s. 3A - Hot Re-rolling Steel Mills Annual Capacity determination Rules, 1997 - Annual capacity of production - Assessee was engaged in the manufacture of hot re-rolled steel products of non-alloy steel in a hot steel rolling mill - Commissioner determined the annual capacity of production of the assessee at 7683.753 MT, as per the formula laid down in sub-section (3) of r. 3 of 1997 Rules - However, keeping in view r. 5 of Rules, the annual capacity was finally fixed at 11961.135 MT on the basis of actual production of the mill during the financial year 1996-97 - Assessee requested the Commissioner for re-determination of annual production capacity of their unit in terms of r. 4(2) of the 1997 Rules on the ground that they have changed some of the parameters of their mill - Commissioner, applied the formula as laid down u/r. 3(3) of 1997 Rules, determined the annual capacity of the mill at 7328.435 MT but relying on r. 5 of 1997 Rules and again computed the annual capacity at 11961.135 MT, being equal to the actual production of the mill during the financial year 1996-97 - Tribunal allowed appeal filed against said order and held that r. 5 of 1997 Rules cannot be applied in view of change in technical parameters of the rolling mill - HC held that the provisions of r. 5 of Rules cannot be invoked in a case where the annual capacity of the mill was to be determined in terms of r. 4(2) of the 1997 Rules on account of change in parameters - Hence, the instant appeal - Whether r. 5 of the 1997 Rules will apply in a case where a manufacturer proposes to make some change in the installed machinery or any part thereof and seeks the approval of the Commissioner in terms of r. 4(2) of the said Rules? - Held, s. 3A was inserted in the Act to enable the Central Government to levy Excise duty on manufacture or production of certain notified goods on the basis of annual capacity of production to be determined by the Commissioner in terms of the Rules - The charging s. 3A of the Act and being in nature of a non obstante provision, the provisions contained in the s. 3A of the Act override those of s. 3 of the Act - Rule 3 of 1997 Rules framed in terms of s. 3A (2) of the Act lays down the procedure for determining the annual capacity of production of the factory - Sub-rule (2) of r. 4 of Rules, permits a manufacturer to make a change in the installed machinery or part thereof which tends to change the value of either of the parameters, referred to in sub-rule (3) of r. 3 of Rules - In the absence of any other Rule, providing for any alternative formula or mechanism for re-determination of production capacity of a factory, on furnishing of information to the Commissioner as contemplated in r, 4(2) of the 1997 Rules, such determination has to be in terms of sub-rule (3) of r. 3 of 1997 Rules - Where annual capacity was determined/ redetermined by applying the formula prescribed in sub-rule (3) of r. 3 of 1997 Rules, r. 5 springs into action and has to be given full effect to - Hence, r. 5 of the 1997 Rules will be attracted for determination of the annual capacity of production of the factory when any change in the installed machinery or any part thereof is intimated to the Commissioner in terms of r. 4(2) of the 1997 Rules - Impugned orders set aside - Appeals allowed.
