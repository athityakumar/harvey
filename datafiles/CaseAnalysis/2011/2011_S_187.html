Raghbir Singh Sehrawat v State Of Haryana And Others

Supreme Court of India

23 November 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 805; (2012) 1 SCC 792; AIR 2012 SC 468; 2012(1) ALL MR 905; 2012 (90) ALR 469; 2012 (2) AWC 1596; 2012 (1) CLT(SC) 111; 2011 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 3777; 2012 (3) MahLJ 81; 2012 (1) MLJ(SC) 808; 2012 (1) OLR 436; 2012 (2) RCR(Civil) 698; [2011] 14 S.C.R. 1113; 2012 (2) UPLBEC 934

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property

Summary: Administrative - Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4, 5(A), 6(1) - Notification - Public Purpose - Hearing rejected - Sustainability - Notification was issued u/s. 4(1) of Act proposing acquisition of land for development of Industrial Sector - Appellant filed objections u/s. 5A(1) along with other land owners and pleaded that his land should not be acquired because same was being used for agricultural purposes and was only source of income for his family - District Revenue Officer-cum- Land Acquisition Collector heard objectors and made recommendations for acquisition - State Govt. issued declaration u/s. 6 (1) and Land Acquisition Collector passed award - Aggrieved appellant and other land owners filed writ petition before HC contending that notifications issued u/ss. 4(1) and 6(1) had not been duly published and that they were not given opportunity of hearing by Land Acquisition Collector - HC dismissed writ petition - Appellant filed SLP before SC, but withdrew and filed review before DB, which was rejected - Aggrieved instant appeal - Whether respondent-Govt. was justified in acquiring land by way of compulsory acquisition - Held, it was necessary to remember that rules of natural justice had been ingrained in scheme of s. 5A with a view to ensure that before any person was deprived of his land by way of compulsory acquisition, he should get an opportunity to oppose decision of State Govt. and/or its agencies/instrumentalities to acquire particular parcel of land - Objector could make an effort to convince Land Acquisition Collector at the time of hearing to make recommendation against acquisition of his land by pointing out that land proposed to be acquired was not suitable for purpose specified in notification issued u/s. 4(1) - Objector could even produce evidence to show that another piece of land was available and same could be utilized for execution of particular project or scheme - Collector should give a fair opportunity of hearing to objector and objectively consider his plea against acquisition of land, only thereafter, he should make recommendations supported by brief reasons as to why particular piece of land should or should not be acquired and whether or not plea put forward by objector merits acceptance - Recommendations made by Collector should reflect objective application of mind to objections filed by landowners and other interested persons - Hence, it was wholly unjust, arbitrary and unreasonable to deprive agriculturists of their houses by way of acquisition of land in name of development of infrastructure or industrialization - If acquisition of private land became absolutely necessary, then too, concerned authorities should strictly comply with relevant statutory provisions and rules of natural justice - Impugned orders were set aside - Appeals allowed.
