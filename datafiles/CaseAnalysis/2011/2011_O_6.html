Oil And Natural Gas Corporation Ltd. v Off-Shore Enterprises Inc.

Supreme Court of India

23 February 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 321; (2011) 14 SCC 147; 2011 (2) CLT(SC) 255; 2011(4) SCALE 499

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitral award - Validity - Under a contract between appellant and the respondent, the respondent agreed to supply the equipment and engineering drawings and provide project management and construction supervision to 'P' to assist with the design, building, equipping and launching of a drillship purchased by the appellant - Appellant had simultaneously entered into an agreement with 'P' for purchase of the said drillship - Appellant took delivery of the Drill-ship - Appellant found some defects in the drill-ship and therefore withheld the payment to the respondent - Respondent initiated arbitration proceedings in respect of the dispute relating to nonpayment of the balance price - Award was made in regard to the balance price due to the respondent - During the pendency of the said arbitration, appellant sought to raise the issue of defective stability of the Drill-ship - Separate reference was made in regard to dispute relating to the stability/defects of the drillship - Arbitrators made an Award for Rs. 36,38,50,000 in favour of appellant, with interest at the rate of 12% per annum on the said sum from the date of award - Said award was challenged by the respondent - Single Judge of HC held that the issue being a technical matter, the same was not arbitrable and that the Arbitral Tribunal exceeded its jurisdiction in deciding a issue - Appeal filed thereagainst was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether the interference with the award by the HC justified? - Appellant stated that the claim for bringing the drillship to the desired state and condition, would be Rs. 3.5 crores - Claim for US$ 1,80,000 as compensation towards 'non-use' of the drillship during the 'repair period' was also final - Held, appellant did not reserve the liberty to modify the claim of Rs. 3.5 crores plus US S 1,80,000 - Further, the appellant did not amend the claim before the Arbitral Tribunal nor did they seek to amend the claim - Claim continued to be Rs. 3.5 crores plus US$ 1,80,000 - Therefore, any award by the Arbitrators in excess of 3.5 crores plus U S 1,80,000 was wholly without jurisdiction as not being the subject matter of reference to the Arbitral tribunal and being an award beyond the pleadings - Further, as the award was a non-reasoned award, it was not possible to ascertain how the Arbitrators awarded a sum of Rs. 3638.50 lakhs which was more than the claim in the Statement of Claim and the claim in the affidavit evidence - Entire award was arbitrary, whimsical and beyond the jurisdiction of the Arbitrators - Appeal dismissed.
