Padal Venkata Rama Reddy @ Ramu v Kovvuri Satyanarayana Reddy and others

Supreme Court of India

29 July 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, H.L. Gokhale

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 680; (2011) 12 SCC 437; 2011 CRLJ 4340; JT 2011 (8) SC 646; 2012 (2) MLJ(Crl) 259; 2011(8) SCALE 128; [2011] 9 S.C.R. 623

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure (Amendment) Act, 1923

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure (Amendment) Act, 1923 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 482 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120B, 147, 148, 149, 201, 307, 427 - Criminal Conspiracy - Attempt to Murder - Quashing of Criminal Proceedings - Sustainability - Respondent Nos. 1 to-3 had been charged u/ss. 120-B, 147, 148, 427, 307, 201 r/w s. 149 IPC for conspiring and attempt to murder appellant - During pendency of trial, respondents filed petition u/s. 482 CrPC to quash criminal proceedings against them - HC allowed petition and quashed criminal proceedings - Hence, instant petition - Whether HC was justified in quashing criminal proceedings against respondents by invoking jurisdiction u/s. 482 CrPC - Held, Court could always take note of any miscarriage of justice and prevent same by exercising its powers u/s. 482 CrPC - Powers u/s. 482CrPC should be exercised only when no other remedy was available to litigant and not in situation where specific remedy was provided by statute - Power to quash criminal complaints should be used sparingly and with abundant caution - Criminal proceedings should not be quashed, merely, on ground that few ingredients had not been stated in detail - From averments in FIR, it should not be claimed that complainant had not highlighted incident said to have been taken place on 7-11-2007 at around 7:00 p.m. - In exercising jurisdiction u/s. 482 CrPC, HC would not ordinarily embark upon enquiry whether evidence in question was reliable or not or whether on reasonable appreciation of it accusation would not be sustained -There was no need to analyse each and every aspect meticulously before trial to find out whether case would end in conviction or acquittal - Statement of witnesses made on oath to be verified in full and materials put forth in charge-sheet ought to be taken note of as whole before arriving any conclusion - Hence, HC had committed error in assuming that with materials available, prosecution could not end in conviction - Appeal allowed.
