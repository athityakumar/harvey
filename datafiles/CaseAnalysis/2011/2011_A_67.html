State of Rajasthan v Arjun Singh and others etc.

Supreme Court of India

02 September 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, H.L. Gokhale

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 614; (2011) 9 SCC 115; 2011 CRLJ 4943; 2012 (1) MLJ(Crl) 516; 2011 (4) RCR(Criminal) 270

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302,307, 149 and 34 - Arms Act, 1959, ss. 3 and 27 - Murder - Acquittal - Justifiability - Respondents allegedly murdered deceased - Trial Court found accused guilty and convicted u/ss. 148, 302, 149, 307 , 149 IPC - Respondents filed appeal before DB - DB altered conviction and sentence u/ss. 307, 34 IPC of 3 respondents and acquitted other 6 accused - Hence, instant appeal - Appellant contended that mere non-recovery of pistol or cartridge did not detract case of prosecution where clinching and direct evidence was acceptable - Whether DB was justified in acquitting and altering conviction and sentence of respondents - Held, motive was emotion which impels a man to do a particular act - In instant case, even in absences of evidence as to motive, 2 persons had been killed and one sustained injuries due to fire Arms and prosecution could be thrown out on this ground - However, DB had rightly observed that presence of eye-witnesses, namely , PWs 3,4, and 6 at place of occurrence on date and time as pleaded by prosecution was highly doubtful - Further, PW 2 in categorical terms had asserted that he saw 5 to 7 persons were standing on roof of house and only 3 respondents name was mentioned in parchabayan - Hence judgment and order of DB was upheld - Appeals dismissed.
