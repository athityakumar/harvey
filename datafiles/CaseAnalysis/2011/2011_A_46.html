Allied Motors Ltd. v Bharat Petroleum Corp. Ltd.

Supreme Court of India

16 December 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Dalveer Bhandari, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 841; (2012) 2 SCC 1; 2012 (3) AWC 2458; 2012 (3) MLJ(SC) 123; [2011] 16 S.C.R. 422

Case Digest

Subject: Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Motor Spirit and High Speed Diesel (Regulation of Supply and Distribution and prevention of Malpractices) Order, 1999

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Cancellation of dealership - Violation of natural justice - Appellant had maintained highest standards and norms of excellent petrol pump - Yet, respondent-Corporation, in clandestine manner, terminated its dealership on basis of sample collected by respective authority in most arbitrary manner and in total violation of principles of natural justice - It was alleged by appellant that its dealership was terminated without even issuing any show cause notice - Division Bench of HC uphold judgment passed by Single Judge in Petition filed by petitioner/appellant against him - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether respondent/Corporation violated provisions of law and principles of natural justice in terminating appellant's dealership - Held, samples were taken without even giving show-cause notice and respondent-Corporation terminated dealership of appellant even without giving them opportunity of being heard - appellant had been operating petrol pump for respondent for last 30 years and was given 10 awards declaring its dealership as best petrol pump in entire State and during that period, on number of occasions, samples were tested by respondent and were found to be as per specifications - Further, haste in which 30 years old dealership was terminated even without giving show-cause notice clearly indicated that entire exercise was carried out by respondent Corporation non-existent, irrelevant and on extraneous considerations - There had been total violation of provisions of law and principles of natural justice and as samples were collected in complete violation of procedural laws and in non-adherence of guidelines of respondent Corporation - Thus, it became imperative in interest of justice to quash and set aside termination order of dealership so same was quashed - Hence, respondent-Corporation were directed to handover possession of petrol pump and restore dealership of petrol pump to appellant - Appeal allowed.
