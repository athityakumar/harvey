Hindustan Coca-Cola Beverage Pvt. Ltd. v Sangli Miraj & Kupwad Municipal Corporation & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

04 July 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Mukundakam Sharma, Anil R. Dave

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 397; (2011) 7 SCC 605; 2011(4) ALL MR 890; 2011 (4) Bom.C.R. 507; JT 2011 (7) SC 70; 2011(6) SCALE 750; [2011] 7 S.C.R. 766

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax

Keywords: Uttar Pradesh Municipalities Act, 1916

Summary: Indirect Tax - Bombay Provisional Municipal Corporation Act, 1949, s. 2(42) - Levy of octroi duty - Reusable glass bottles of soft drinks - Appellant's products were distributed and sold in returnable and reusable glass bottles which were stored in plastic crates - Appellant never sold glass bottles and plastic crates to any distributor and once the product in the glass bottles kept in crates was consumed, glass bottles along with crates were returned to the appellant for filling after cleaning and washing them - Appellant paid octroi levied on the aerated beverages when they entered octroi limits of Municipal Corporations and the bill had the effect of levying octroi separately on the glass bottles and plastic crates utilized by the appellant to pack and transport the aerated beverages manufactured by them - Respondent (Corporation) alleged that the cost of the glass bottles and crates of appellant was amortized and included in the retail sale price of the aerated beverages, hence, octroi cannot be levied on the value of the glass bottles and crates and the impugned bills were, therefore, illegal and arbitrary - Appellant filed writ petition before HC challenging said demand of octroi on glass bottles and crates - DB relied on SC decision in Acqueous Victuals Private Limited vs. State of Uttar Pradesh and Others 1998 INDLAW SC 803 and dismissed petition filed by the appellant - However, DB granted liberty to the appellant to claim refund by filling appropriate application, in case, the bottles and crates were not sold, used, or consumed in the Municipal limits of the respondent - Appellant filed appeal against said order contending that as per the definition of the term octroi as found in s. 2(42) of the Act, 'octroi' means a cess on the entry of goods into the limits of a city for consumption, use or sale therein and as in the instant case there was no consumption, use or sale as plastic crates and glass bottles were durable and reusable, hence, the levy of octroi was unjustified - Whether the bottles which were filled in with beverages imported for sale within the Municipal limits could be said to have been consumed or used within the Municipal limits and octroi could be levied on it? - Held, instant case was squarely covered by the SC decision in Acqueous Victuals case wherein it was held that in case the appellant was sending out the same bottles for recycling and if the bottles and crates were not sold, used or consumed in the Municipal limits of the respondent and if they have not finally rested in the Municipal limits of the respondent in which they were imported, the appellant can always make an application for refund under the said Rules - On the appellant making an application for refund, the concerned authority will consider it in its proper perspective and if a case was made out shall grant refund - Appeals dismissed.
