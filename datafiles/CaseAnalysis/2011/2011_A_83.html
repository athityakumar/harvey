State Of Jharkhand And Others v Ashok Kumar Dangi And Others

Supreme Court of India

04 July 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Chandramauli Kumar Prasad, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 457; (2011) 13 SCC 383; 2011 (130) FLR 973; JT 2011 (8) SC 203; 2011(7) SCALE 420; [2011] 7 S.C.R. 748; 2011 (6) SLR 541

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Bihar Primary School Teachers Amendment Appointment Rules, 1993, Jharkhand Primary Schools Appointment Amendments Rules, 2003, Bihar Primary School Teacher Appointment Rules, 1991, Jharkhand Primary Teachers' Appointment Rules, 2002

Summary: Service - Jharkhand Primary Teachers' Appointment Rules, 2002, rr. 2, 3 - Jharkhand Primary Schools Appointment Amendments Rules, 2003, r. 2(b)(iii) - Apportion of vacancy - Permissibility - State Public Service Commission (SPSC) invited applications for the post of Primary School Teachers wherein eligibility criteria specified for the post was prescribed in terms of rr. 2 and 3 of the Rules - Appellant State subsequently amended the r. 2 which inter alia provided that those candidates having qualification of C.P.Ed/Dip.P.Ed. should be eligible only for Physical Trained Teachers (PTT) - SPSC issued corrigendum to the recruitment notification to that effect of amendment - Respondent candidates, having qualification of C.P.Ed/Dip.P.Ed., applied for the vacancies and appeared for the examination - However, respondents' results were withheld and their candidature confined only to the posts of PTT - Respondents filed writ petition, which was dismissed by the Single Judge - DB, however, allowed respondents writ appeals directing that appellant should make appointment of PTT at least on the 5% posts of the total vacancies of the primary teachers - Whether the amendment to r. 2(b)(iii) made after the recruitment notification, which inter alia provided that candidates having C.P.Ed/Dip.P.Ed should be eligible for PTT only, could be applied retrospectively and respondents cases should be governed by the un-amended Rules - Held, at no point of time the respondents had challenged the amendment of Rules which provided that the Physical Trained Candidates should be eligible only for the appointment to the PTT as also corrigendum issued by the SPSC confining their eligibility for the PTT only - SPSC conducted the examination on the basis of such amendment and the respondents without making any challenge to the same, participated in the selection process and appeared in the examination without any demur - It was only after the result was published and respondents' candidature not considered against the entire vacancy of the Primary School Teachers that they had chosen to file the writ petition - Any direction to consider the candidature of the respondents against the entire vacancy of Primary School Teachers would unsettle settled matter and should result into chain reaction, affecting the appointment of a large number of persons - Hence, HC erred in directing the appellants to fill-up 5% vacancies of Primary School Teachers from Physical Trained candidates - Hence impugned HC order was set aside - Appeal allowed.
