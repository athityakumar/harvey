Commissioner Of Police, Delhi & Ors v Jai Bhagwan

Supreme Court of India

10 May 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Mukundakam Sharma, A.R. Dave

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 342; (2011) 6 SCC 376; 2011 (179) DLT 578; 2011 (3) ESC 401; 2011 (129) FLR 1094; JT 2011 (6) SC 63; 2011 (6) MLJ(SC) 305; 2011(5) SCALE 696; [2011] 7 S.C.R. 558; 2011 (3) SLR 728

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Delhi Police (F & A) Rules, 1980

Summary: Service - Delhi Police (F & A) Rules, 1980, r. 16 (iii) - Demand of illegal gratification - Dismissal from service - Legality - Respondent Constable allegedly demanded illegal gratification from a passenger (complainant) during airport security check up - Disciplinary proceedings were initiated against respondent and enquiry officer found that respondent was guilty of charges drawn up against him - Disciplinary authority dismissed respondent from service - Respondent's appeal before the appellate authority and original application thereafter before the Tribunal were dismissed consecutively - Respondent filed writ petition - HC, by impugned order, set aside the order of dismissal - Whether dismissal of respondent from service was proper - Held, place where security check was carried out was an open place and there should have been many other persons, besides police officers, present at that time but none of them had been examined during the departmental proceedings against the respondent to prove the alleged fact of demanding and receiving illegal gratification by him - Although there was some evidence that an amount received from complainant was returned by the respondent but there was no such direct and reliable evidence produced by the appellants in the departmental proceedings which clearly proved and established that the respondent demanded and received an illegal gratification - Further, it also seemed quite impracticable to presume that in the presence of so many passengers, the respondent could have extorted money - Allegation of receiving money as illegal gratification was framed on suspicions and possibilities while trying to link it up with the instance of returning back money by the respondent to the complainant - Further, statement of complainant was not recorded by the investigating officer and the investigating officer also did not take down in writing and also attest the complaint made by her - Non-examination of the complainant and important witnesses during the departmental proceeding had denied the respondent of his right of cross-examination and thus caused violation of r. 16 (iii) of the Rules - In the absence of such a definite/clear proof supporting the case of the appellants it was difficult to draw a finding of taking illegal gratification by the respondent from the complainant - Hence, HC was rightly held that the instant case was a case of no evidence - Appeal dismissed.
