State Of Kerala & Anr v C.P. Rao

Supreme Court of India

16 May 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Ganguly, Deepak Verma

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 369; (2011) 6 SCC 450; AIR 2012 SC (Supp) 393; 2012 ALL MR (Cri) 3068; 2012 CRLJ 2607; JT 2011 (3) SC 641; 2011 (2) KLT 812; 2011 (4) MLJ(Crl) 723; 2011 (3) RCR(Criminal) 688; 2011(6) SCALE 358; [2011] 6 S.C.R. 864

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 7, 13(1)(d) and 13(2) - Non-examination of complainant - Acquittal - Challenged - Respondent was convicted u/ss. 7 and 13(2) r/w s. 13(1)(d) of the Act by the Special Judge - On appeal, HC held that in view of the fact that the complainant was not examined and the only explanation given was that he was not available in the country but no details were given as to where the complainant was, acquitted respondent - HC relied on the decision of SC in Panalal Damodar Rathi v. State of Maharashtra 1979 INDLAW SC 505 wherein it was held that when there was no corroboration of testimony of the complainant regarding the demand of bribe by the accused, it had to be accepted that the version of the complainant was not corroborated and, therefore, the evidence of the complainant could not be relied on - Instant appeal challenging acquittal - Held, in the absence of semblance of explanation by the investigating officer for the non-examination of the complainant, it was not open to the courts below to find out their own reason for not tendering the complainant in evidence - Further, best evidence to prove the demand was not made available before the court - Judgment of HC upheld - Appeal dismissed.
