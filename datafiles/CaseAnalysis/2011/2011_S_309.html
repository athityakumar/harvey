Devender Kumar Tyagi and another v State Of U.P. & Ors

Supreme Court of India

23 August 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Dattu, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 514; (2011) 9 SCC 164; 2011 (8) ADJ 173; 2011 (89) ALR 275; 2011 (6) AWC 6338; 2012 (1) CGLJ 497; 2011 (3) CLT(SC) 316; JT 2011 (9) SC 390; 2011 (8) MLJ(SC) 385; 2011 (4) RCR(Civil) 321B; 2011 (114) RD 824; 2011(9) SCALE 310; [2011] 15 S.C.R. 641; 2011 (4) UPLBEC 2867

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property

Keywords: U.P. Urban Planning and Development Act, 1973, National Capital Region Planning Board Act, 1985

Summary: Land & Property - Administrative - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4, 5A, 6, 17 - Land acquisition -Notification u/ss. 4 and 6 - Invocation of urgency clause - Validity - Respondent authority issued Notification u/s. 4 r/w s. 17(4) of the Act for acquisition of petitioners' lands for the planned development of Leather City Project - Respondent invoked urgency provision and thereby dispensed with the inquiry u/s. 5A of the Act - Respondent subsequently issued Notification u/s. 6 r/w s. 17(1) of the Act whereby it directed the Collector to take possession of the acquired land - Hence, petitioner landowners filed the instant writ petition challenging the validity of Notifications issued u/ss. 4 and 6 of the Act - (A) Whether the Notification dated 18-12-2007 issued by the respondents u/s. 6 r/w s. 17 (1) of the Act was within the period of limitation as contemplated by proviso (ii) to s. 6 (1) of the Act - Held, publication of the Notification in two newspapers having circulation in the locality where the land was situated and where people were well conversant with Hindi amounted to ample compliance with the requirement of the publication u/s. 4(1) of the Act - In view of that, the subsequent publication of English translation of the said Notification u/s. 4 in two newspapers on 5-1-2007 was unnecessary and would not assist the respondents to extend the period of limitation envisaged in the proviso to s. 6(1) of the Act - Hence, the last date of publication for the purpose s. 4(1) of the Act, which should be treated as date of publication, was the date on which, the second Notification u/s. 4 was published in the newspaper, that is, 4-7-2006 - Therefore, the period of limitation commenced from 4-7-2006, which was the date of publication of the Notification u/s. 4(1) of the Act - If the declaration u/s. 6 of the Act was made before the expiry of the period of one year starting from 4-7-2006, then, only such declaration would be considered as valid for the purpose of the acquisition of land - However, in the instant case, the declaration u/s. 6 was issued on 18-12-2007 which was clearly beyond the period of limitation of one year as mandated by the proviso to s. 6(1) of the Act - Therefore, the declaration of Notification u/s. 6 and its subsequent publications were clearly beyond the period of limitation of one year starting from the date of publication of Notification u/s. 4 of the Act - (B) Whether the respondent was justified in invoking the urgency provision u/s. 17(1) and excluding the application of s. 5-A in terms of s. 17(4) of the Act for acquisition of the land for the development of the Leather City Project - Held, in the instant case, SC, by vide its order dated 17-8-2004, had issued a direction to the respondents to relocate the bone mills and allied industries causing environment pollution and health hazards as per the recommendations of the Central Pollution Control Board and, inter alia, respondents were also directed to identify the area for relocation- Subsequently, it was only after the lapse of two years, the respondent State Government had issued a Notification u/s. 4 on 3-7-2006 and the same was published on 4-7-2006 - Thereafter, the State Government took more than 17 months in order to make a declaration of the Notification u/s. 6 from the date of publication of the Notification u/s. 4 of the Act - It was crystal clear that the government functionary had proceeded at very slow pace at two levels, that is, prior to the issuance of the Notification u/s. 4 and post the issuance of the Notification u/s. 4, for acquisition of the land for construction of the Leather City Project, which undoubtedly was a public purpose - Therefore, the series of the events amply exhibited the lethargical and lackadaisical attitude of the State Government - In the light of the above circumstances, the respondents were not justified in invoking the urgency provisions u/s. 17 of the Act, thereby, depriving the appellants of their valuable right to raise objections and opportunity of hearing before the authorities in order to persuade them that their property may not be acquired - Hence, impugned Notification u/s. 4 and Notification u/s. 6 of the Act were quashed - Petitions allowed.
