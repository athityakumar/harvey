Suba Singh & Anr. v Davinder Kaur & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

06 July 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Aftab Alam, R.M. Lodha

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 432; (2011) 13 SCC 296; 2011 ACJ 2161; 2011 (6) ALD(SC) 24; 2011(4) ALL MR 940; 2011 (4) CGLJ 490; 2011 (3) CLT(SC) 17; 2011 (3) JCC 1911; JT 2011 (7) SC 156; 2012 (1) MLJ(SC) 824; 2011 (4) RCR(Criminal) 109; 2011 (4) RLW 2785; 2011(7) SCALE 225; [2011] 7 S.C.R. 217; 2011 (3) WLN(SC) 67

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Keywords: Fatal Accidents Act, 1846, Part B States (Laws) Act, 1951, Indian Short Titles Act, 1897

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 357 - Fatal Accidents Act, 1855 - Compensation - Murder - Plaintiffs (Wife and daughter of deceased) filed suit, claiming a sum of Rs. 3 lakhs as damages from the defendants for causing the death of 'X' (deceased) by their wrongful act - Civil Judge, decreed the suit and awarded compensation of Rs. 3 Lakhs to the plaintiffs alongwith interest @ 12% per annum from the date of the filing of the suit - On appeal, the District Judge reduced the amount of compensation from Rs. 3 lakhs to Rs. 2,32,700/-, leaving the rate of interest unchanged - HC dismissed appeal filed against said order - Defendants filed appeal against said order contending that they were being punished twice over for the same offence as they were already punished for the same offence in criminal proceedings - Defendants referred s. 357 of CrPC and submitted that there being a specific provision there for payment of compensation, a suit for damages would not be maintainable - Whether contentions of the defendants could be accepted and order of the District Judge as affirmed by the HC could be set aside? - Held, the contention made on behalf of the defendants was fully answered by clauses (b) and (c) of sub-section (1) and sub-section (5) of s. 357 of the CrPC - In those provisions there was a clear and explicit recognition of a civil suit at the instance of the dependents of a person killed, against his/her killers - In sub-section (1)(c) of s. 357 of CrPC there was clear indication that apart from the punishment of fine, the person convicted of any offence of having caused the death of another person or of having abetted the commission of such an offence may also be liable to face a civil action for damages under the Fatal Accidents Act, 1855 in a suit for damages and sub-section (5) of s. 357 of the CrPC makes it all the more clear by stipulating that at the time of awarding compensation in a subsequent civil suit relating to the same matter the court shall take into account any sum paid or recovered as compensation under that section - Hence, no scope for any interference - However, courts below have awarded interest at the rather higher rate of 12% per annum, hence, simple interest at the rate of 6% per annum from the date of the filing of the suit till payment would meet the ends of justice - Rate of interest reduced to 6% - Appeal dismissed.
