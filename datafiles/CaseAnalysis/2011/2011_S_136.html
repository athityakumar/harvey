Lanka Venkateswarlu (D) By Lrs v State Of A.P. & Ors

Supreme Court of India

24 February 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Surinder Singh Nijjar, B. SUDARSHAN REDDY

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 126; (2011) 4 SCC 363; AIR 2011 SC 1199; 2011 (86) ALR 59; 2011 (2) ALT 55; 2011 (3) AWC 2295; 2011 (5) Bom.C.R. 857; 2011 (1) CLT(SC) 220; JT 2011 (2) SC 540; 2011 (5) MLJ(SC) 167; 2011 (3) MPLJ 135; 2011 (2) RCR(Civil) 880; 2011(2) SCALE 703; [2011] 3 S.C.R. 217

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Land & Property - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Limitation Act, 1963, s. 5 - Condonation of delay of 3703 days - Appellants filed suit for the declaration of his title as the absolute owner of the suit property and for permanent injunction restraining respondents from interfering with his peaceful possession - Appellants submitted that they have purchased the impugned property from 'X' (original plaintiff) by a registered sale deed dt. 15.07.1961 - Respondents contended that appellants were not the owner of the suit property - Respondents also contended that 'X' was never in possession and enjoyment of the impugned property - Trial court decided the suit in favour of the appellants - Respondents challenged said order of trial court before HC - In the meanwhile 'X' died - HC directed the government pleader to take steps to bring on the record the legal representatives of the 'X' - No actions were taken by the respondents to comply with the order passed by the HC - HC dismissed said appeal - Subsequently, respondents filed application for condonation of delay of 3703 days to bring the legal representatives on record - HC had allowed the said application and restored the appeal filed by the respondents - Appellants filed appeals against said order contending that the impugned order of the HC cannot be justified on legal ground as HC having itself recorded the utter negligence of the respondents in pursuing the appeal at every stage, without any justification, condoned the delay - Whether the HC was justified in condoning the said delay? - Held, HC graphically narrated the clear dereliction of duty by the concerned government pleaders in not pursuing the appeal before the HC diligently - HC had set out the different stages at which the government pleaders had exhibited almost culpable negligence in performance of their duties - HC found the justification given by the government pleaders to be unacceptable - Having recorded such conclusions, inexplicably, the HC proceeds to condone the unconscionable delay - Impugned order of the HC was set aside - Appeals allowed.
