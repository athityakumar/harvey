Mohammad Ahmad & Anr v Atma Ram Chauhan & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

13 May 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Deepak Verma, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 350; (2011) 7 SCC 755; 2011 (7) ADJ 296; AIR 2011 SC 1940; 2011 (87) ALR 475; 2011 (2) ARC 433; 2011 (4) AWC 3741; 2011 (4) Bom.C.R. 74; 2011 (4) CTC 218; 2011 (179) DLT 532; JT 2011 (6) SC 189; 2011 (2) LCD 1419; 2011 (162) PLR 711; 2011 (2) RCR(Civil) 972; 2011(6) SCALE 265; [2011] 6 S.C.R. 822

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - Uttar Pradesh Urban Buildings (Regulation of Letting, Rent and Eviction) Act, 1972 - Bona fide requirement for business purpose - Eviction - Sustainability - Respondent landlords filed eviction petition against respondent tenants on ground of bona fide requirement of demised premises for business purpose - Rent Control Court (RCC) directed respondents to release a part of their holdings to appellants and directed appellants to release a part of demised premises to respondent - Both parties filed appeal before Appellate Authority - Appellate Authority modified RCC order to the extent of demised premises released in favour of respondents was maintained - Appellants filed writ petition wherein Single Judge, by an interim order, enhanced rent payable by respondent to appellant - Whether HC was justified in enhancing rent payable by appellants to respondents during the pendency of proceedings - Held, respondents made an offer to appellants which they agreed, only thereafter rent was enhanced - Single Judge applied his own yardstick in working out rent but only after both parties' contentions were taken into account and said yardstick was absolutely correct and perfect method of working out present market rental of premises - Even though, report of valuation was not taken into consideration as there was none but assessment and judgment of Single Judge should not be disallowed, even though detailed reasons had not been assigned by Single Judge for enhancing rate of rent because ultimate conclusion arrived at by him did not suffer from any infirmity, illegality or perversity - Hence, impugned interim order of Single Judge was upheld - Appeals dismissed.
