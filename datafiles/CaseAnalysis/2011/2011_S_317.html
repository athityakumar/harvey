Allahabad High School Society,Allahabad & Another v State Of U.P.

Supreme Court of India

12 May 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 348; (2011) 6 SCC 118; 2011 (8) ADJ 78; AIR 2011 SC 1969; 2011 (87) ALR 277; 2011 (4) AWC 4006; 2011 (2) ESC 390; JT 2011 (6) SC 250; 2011 (2) LCD 1659; 2011(6) SCALE 45; [2011] 6 S.C.R. 759; 2011 (2) UPLBEC 1504

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Trusts & Associations

Keywords: Rules of Allahabad High Schools Society, 1952, U.P. Societies Registration Act, 1860

Summary: Trusts & Associations - Practice & Procedure - Uttar Pradesh Societies Registration Act, 1860, s. 12D(b) - Rules of Allahabad High Schools Society, 1952, r. 38 - Appellant Educational Society amended its constitution and bye-laws and communicated to Assistant Registrar for registration of proceedings - Assistant Registrar cancelled registration proceedings u/s. 12D(b) of the Act holding that amendments made were arbitrary and illegal - Assistant Registrar consequently directed appellant to convene a General Body Meeting, after informing all Members about current situation and circumstances and reasons thereof and to form a Governing Body and present the same - Appellant challenged Assistant Registrar order before HC - Single Judge and thereafter DB upheld Assistant Registrar order - Whether HC was correct in upholding order of Assistant Registrar cancelling the amendments made by appellant Society - Held, no quorum was there in the meeting held in which amendments had been carried out - Appellant Society consisted of 14 members, however, in Special General Meeting for carrying out amendment only nine members were present - Hence, according to r. 38 of Rules, at least 10 members were required to be present at Special General Meeting - In the absence of quorum laid down by r. 38, neither amendments could be passed in Special General Meeting of Society nor could amendments made be registered by Assistant Registrar - Further, manipulations/manoeuvrings was writ large that concerned administrators, in order to perpetuate themselves in Society had made amendments for their benefit and to disadvantage of the Society - Single Judge correctly concluded that in such a situation and in that background, any interference with order of Assistant Registrar would amount to perpetuating illegality and subscribing to apparent illegality committed - Basic feature of appellant Society along with its primary object had been altered by way of amendments to constitution and bye-laws - DB had accepted that Assistant Registrar had jurisdiction not only to deal with validity of convening of meeting but also to examine import of resolution regarding amendments of the Rules - DB also accepted that meetings in which amendments were carried out had not been validly convened - In the light of factual findings by Assistant Registrar, affirming the same by Single Judge and DB, it was impermissible for the SC to exercise jurisdiction u/art. 136 of Constitution - No valid ground for interference on impugned order - Appeal dismissed.
