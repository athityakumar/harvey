State Of Goa v Praveen Enterprises

Supreme Court of India

04 July 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 428; (2012) 12 SCC 581; 2013 (1) ALD(SC) 29; 2011(5) ALL MR 446; 2011 (87) ALR 889; 2011 (4) AWC 3918; 2011 (3) CLT(SC) 164; JT 2011 (8) SC 359; 2011 (7) MLJ(SC) 775; 2011(7) SCALE 131; [2011] 10 S.C.R. 1026

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Contract & Commercial - Arbitration Act, 1940, s. 20 - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, ss. 11, 11(4), 11(5), 11(6), 11(9), 2(9), 21, 23, 23(3), 25(a)(9), 32(2)(a), 34, 43, 8 - English Arbitration Act, 1996 - Limitation Act, 1963, ss. 3, 3(2)(b) - Contract - Arbitration Award - Maintainability - Appellant entrusted construction work to respondent - Contract was terminated by appellant on ground of non completion beyond extended date - Respondent filed application for appointment of Arbitrator which was allowed -- Arbitrator awarded appellant for counter claims made by appellant and rejected other claims of respondent and appellant - Aggrieved by same application was filed challenging award - Trial Court disposed of said challenge - Appellant challenged said judgment by filing arbitration appeal before HC which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether respondent in an arbitration proceedings was precluded from making a counter-claim - Held, s. 11 of the Act requires Chief Justice or his designate to either appoint Arbitrator/s or take necessary measures with regard to appointment procedure contained in arbitration agreement and where arbitration agreement provides for referring all disputes between parties , Arbitrator would have jurisdiction to entertain any counter claim, even though it was not raised at a stage earlier to stage of pleadings before Arbitrator - However, arbitration agreement requires specific disputes to be referred to arbitration and provides that arbitrator would have jurisdiction to decide only disputes referred - Further, arbitrator's jurisdiction was controlled by specific reference and he could not travel beyond reference, nor entertain any additional claims or counter claims which are not part of disputes specifically referred to arbitration - Hence, all disputes being referred to arbitration by a sole arbitrator refers to an Appointing Authority whose role was only to appoint Arbitrator - Further, arbitration clause requires party invoking arbitration to specify dispute/s to be referred to arbitration, it did not require appointing authority to specify disputes or refer any specific disputes to arbitration nor requires Arbitrator to decide only referred disputes - Thus, it did not bar Arbitrator deciding any counter claims on contrary in absence of agreement counter claims by appellant were maintainable and arbitrable with regard to ss. 23 r/w s. 2(9) of the Act - Appeal allowed.
