Dharmatma Singh v Harminder Singh & Ors

Supreme Court of India

10 May 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Patnaik, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 353; AIR 2011 SC 2094; 2011 ALL MR (Cri) 2917; 2011 CRLJ 3591; JT 2011 (6) SC 68; 2011 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 3126; 2011 (4) MLJ(Crl) 525; 2011 (3) RCR(Criminal) 38; 2011 (3) RLW 2719; 2011(5) SCALE 552; 2011(6) SCALE 102; [2011] 6 S.C.R. 355

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 190, 173 and 482 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 452, 324, 323, 506, 326 r/w. s. 34 - Quashing of criminal proceedings - FIR was registered against the appellants u/ss. 452, 324, 323, 506, 326 r/w. s. 34 of IPC - After investigation, the police filed two challans before Judicial Magistrate one against the appellants and the other one against the respondents - Respondents submitted an application to the Judicial Magistrate for permission to investigate further in the case - Judicial Magistrate granted such permission to the respondents - After further investigation, the Superintendent of Police submitted his report to the Deputy Inspector General of Police submitting that respondents gave some injuries to the appellants and others for his self- defence and such injuries come under the definition of right of private defence and, therefore, no proceedings could be initiated against respondents - The Deputy Inspector General of Police referred the matter to the Additional Director General of Police who was of the opinion that as the challans had already been filed against the respondents, the decision of the case should be left to the Court - However, respondents filed an application u/s. 482 CrPC before HC for quashing of challan filed against them in the Court of Judicial Magistrate - HC quashed the criminal proceedings initiated against respondents - Appellants filed appeal against said order contending that the HC should not exercised the power granted u/s. 482 of CrPC for quashing of the criminal proceedings and quashed the criminal proceedings against the respondents when the Magistrate was yet to exercise his judicial mind u/s. 190 of the CrPC to the police reports filed u/s. 173 of the CrPC - Whether contentions of the appellants could be accepted and impugned order of the HC could be set aside? - Held, s. 482 of the CrPC saves the inherent powers of the HC to make such orders as may be necessary to give effect to any order or to prevent abuse of the process of any court or otherwise to secure the ends of justice - However, in the instant case, Magistrate had not applied his mind to the merits of the reports filed u/s. 173 CrPC - The exercise of power by the HC u/s. 482, CrPC, was at an interlocutory stage and was not warranted in the facts of instant case - Hence, impugned order of the HC set aside - Appeal allowed.
