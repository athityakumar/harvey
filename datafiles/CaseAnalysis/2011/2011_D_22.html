Union of India and another v Deepak Electric and Trading Company and another

Supreme Court of India

20 October 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Patnaik, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 665; (2012) 12 SCC 509; AIR 2012 SC 41; 2012(2) ALL MR 46; 2012 (2) AWC 1613; 2011 (4) CLT(SC) 187; 2011 (6) CTC 452; 2011 (183) DLT 475; 2012 (3) MLJ(SC) 815; 2012 (2) RCR(Civil) 332; 2012 (1) RLW 962; 2011(12) SCALE 139; [2011] 12 S.C.R. 1198; 2011 (4) WLN(SC) 20

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Practice & Procedure - Arbitration Act, 1940, ss. 14, 17 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 79 - Objections to arbitral award - Limitation - 1st respondent entered into a construction contract with appellant Union of India (UoI) - Dispute arose between the parties and matter referred to arbitration - Arbitrator published award and respondent filed petition before the HC u/ss. 14, 17 of the Act for making the award a rule of the Court - Respondent's petition was registered and numbered as a Suit and notice was served on UoI - Executive Engineer addressed a letter to the Registrar of HC stating that he had not received a formal notice from the Court - Court directed the respondent to issue fresh notice to Executive Engineer - While service of notice on the Executive Engineer was awaited, UoI filed objections to the award - Respondent contended that objections were not filed within the period of limitation, i.e. 30 days from the date of service of the notice of filing of the award - UoI contended that service was not complete since notice was not served upon the Executive Engineer - Single Judge held that u/s. 79 of CPC when suits were filed against the Central Government, only the UoI had to be arrayed as a party and the Executive Engineer could not be taken to be a party in such proceedings - Single Judge further held that since the UoI failed to file objections within the period of limitation, the objections filed were time barred - DB, in appeal, held that the Executive Engineer was a necessary party to the proceedings, however, Executive Engineer in the instant case had acquired the knowledge of filing of award on 21-5-1997 but he did not file objection within the period of limitation i.e., 30 days counted from the date of 21-5-1997 - Whether the DB had taken a correct view in the impugned order that the objections to the award were time barred - Held, cl. (b) of art. 119 of the Schedule to the Limitation Act, 1963 that an application for setting aside of an award should be filed within 30 days from 'the date of service of the notice of the filing of the award' - Thus, the starting point of limitation was the date of service of the notice of the filing of the award and not the date of knowledge of the filing of the award - DB had taken a view in the impugned order that as the Executive Engineer was looking after the arbitration proceedings, he was the one who could have filed the objections to the award on behalf of the UoI and thus notice of the filing of the award on the Executive Engineer was mandatory and the starting point of limitation for filing the application for setting aside the award would be the date of service of notice on the Executive Engineer as provided in art. 119(b) of the Schedule to the Limitation Act - HC, therefore, was not right in coming to the conclusion that as the Executive Engineer had knowledge of the filing of the award on 21-5-1997 and as the objections were filed beyond the period of 30 days counted from 21-5-1997, the objections to the award were barred by time - Relied on the decision of SC in Deo Narain Choudhury v. Shree Narain Choudhaury, 2000 INDLAW SC 2562 - Hence, impugned orders of Single Judge as well as DB were set aside and matter remanded to Single Judge for fresh disposal - Appeal allowed.
