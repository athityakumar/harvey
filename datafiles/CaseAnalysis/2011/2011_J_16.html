National Fertilizers Ltd. v Jagga Singh (Deceased) Through L.Rs. & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

15 November 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Patnaik, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 767; (2012) 1 SCC 74; AIR 2012 SC 2535; 2012 (2) ALD(SC) 30; 2012 (92) ALR 272; 2012 (1) AWC 33; 2012 (5) Bom.C.R. 454; 2011 (4) CLT(SC) 418; 2011 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 3702; 2012 (2) MahLJ 591; 2012 (117) RD 595; [2011] 15 S.C.R. 809

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Land Acquisition Act 1894, ss. 4 and 18 - Land acquired - Unregistered Sale Agreements - Compensation - Validity - Appellants, government undertaking was engaged in business of manufacturing fertilizers - Government issued notification u/s. 4 of the Act to acquire land required for dwelling houses for appellant's employees - Land Acquisition officer awarded compensation at 50% for each classes of land - Aggrieved by award, respondents filed an appeal u/s. 18 of the Act before civil court -District Judge determined compensation for all the three classes of land at a uniform rate of Rs.32.50 per sq. yard after considering two unregistered sale agreements - Aggrieved by order of District Judge, two appeals were filed by both parties before HC - Single Judge sustained determination of compensation and dismissed appeals - On appeal DB held, agreements were unregistered and did not bear any date, those documents should not be considered for determination of compensation and assessed Rs.120/- per sq. yard as market value for land acquired - Whether compensation for lands acquired as determined by District Judge and as upheld by Single Judge was correct assessment of the market value of acquired land or compensation determined by DB in impugned order was an accurate assessment of the market value of land acquired - Held, sale agreement should not have constituted the basis for determination of market value of land by District Judge - Reliance on order of HC by District Judge and Single Judge was not correct because from the site plan it appears that land acquired was located adjacent to the colony of appellants and other colonies - DB was rightly held that unregistered sale agreements cannot constitute the basis for determination of the market value of acquired land - Further, DB applied a cut to the rate and determined rate of Rs.120/- per sq. yard considering location and potentiality of acquired land - DB had taken into consideration the fact that land acquired was slightly away from the heart of the town and was located adjacent to the existing colony of appellants and other colonies and reduced market value of land acquired - Thus, there was no merit on appellant's contention that a cut of 60% should have been applied considering larger size and lower quality of land acquired - Impugned order of DB holding cut applied so as to reduce value from Rs.176/- per sq. yard to Rs.120/- per sq. yard was just and reasonable - Appeals dismissed.
