 K. T. Plantation Private Limited and another v State of Karnataka

Supreme Court of India

09 August 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan, S.H. Kapadia, Mukundakam Sharma, Swatanter Kumar, Anil R. Dave

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 598; (2011) 9 SCC 1; 2011(6) ALL MR 387; 2011 (3) CLT(SC) 279; JT 2011 (9) SC 65; 2011 (7) MLJ(SC) 1185; 2012 (5) RCR(Civil) 878; 2011(8) SCALE 583; [2011] 13 S.C.R. 636

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Constitution (Seventeenth Amendment) Act, 1964, Land Acquisition (Madras Amendment) Act, 1961, Central Educational Institutions (Reservation in Admission) Act, 2006, Banking Companies (Acquisition and Transfer of Undertaking) Act, 1969, Canadian Constitution, U.S. Constitution, Bombay Town Planning Act, 1958, Karnataka Land Reforms amendment Act, 1973, Roerich and Devika Rani Roerich Estate (Acquisition & Transfer) Act, 1996, Roerich and Devika Rani Roerich Estate (Acquisition and Transfer) Bill, 1996, Travancore-Cochin Land Tax Act, West Bengal Great Eastern Hotel (Acquisition of Undertaking) Act, 1980, Roerich and Devika Rani Roerich Estate (Acquisition and Transfer) Ordinance, 1992

Summary: (A) Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Karnataka Land Reforms Act, 1961, ss. 107(1)(vi), 110, - Surplus land - Surrendered to State - Withdrawing exemption - Validity - X and Y had owned estate covering 470.19 acres out of which 100 acres were granted to them by State Govt. for Linaloe cultivation - 'X' had sold 141. 25 acres of to appellant no. 1/company and 'y' also sold extent of 223 acres 30 guntas to Company by way of unregistered sale deed - 'X' and 'Y' alleged that some of persons who were associated with couple, had an eye on their properties, including land used for linaloe cultivation, valuable paintings, jewellery, artefacts etc., and began to create documents to grab those properties - Chief Secretary of State noticing facts and convened meeting, for that purpose a letter was handed over to 'X' on behalf of State Govt. expressing Govt.'s willingness to purchase paintings and other valuables - Deputy Commissioner reported that 'X' had sold extent of land to Company - Commissioner and Secretary to Govt. sought legal opinion of Department of Law and Parliamentary Affairs as to whether valuable lands held by 'X' could be resumed by State before lands changed hands, by withdrawing exemption given to lands used for linaloe cultivation - Department of Law and Parliamentary Affairs in their note opined that exemption given u/s. 107 of the 1961 Act could be withdrawn by Govt. by issuing notification as per s. 110 of the 1961 Act - Consequently Govt. issued notification dt. 8-3-1994 in exercise of powers conferred by s. 110 of the 1961 Act, withdrawing exemption granted for lands used for cultivation of linaloe u/s. 107(1)(vi) of the 1961 Act - Hence, instant appeals - Respondent contended that exemption u/s. 107(1)(vi) was granted to 'X' for cultivation of linaloe, while Company was statutorily disentitled to hold land and, hence, claim for exemption from provisions of Land Reforms Act was opposed to policy of the Act - Whether s. 110 of the 1961 Act and validity of notification dt. 8-3-1994 was maintainable - Held, s. 110 of the 1961 Act and notification dt. 8-3-1994 were valid, and there was no excessive delegation of legislative power on State Govt. - Relied on in Quarry Owner's Association v. State of Bihar and Others 2000 Indlaw SC 2839 - Hence, non-laying of notification dt. 8-3-1994 u/s. 140 of the 1961 Act before State Legislature was curable defect and it would not affect validity of notification or action taken there under - Appeals dismissed.

(B) Land & Property - Constitution - Roerich and Devika Rani Roerich Estate (Acquisition & Transfer) Act, 1996, s. 66 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 19, 31A, 300A - Validity of Acquisition Act - Enhanced compensation - Succeed - Respondent contended that procedure and principle for acquisition of land as well as determination of compensation, etc., under both Acts were contrary to each other and hence impugned Act could be saved only if Presidential assent was obtained u/art. 254(2) of Constitution - Whether Constitutional validity of the 1996 Act and claim for enhanced compensation and scope of art. 300A of Constitution was succeed - Held, the 1996 Act was enacted in public interest, to preserve and protect land used for linaloe cultivation and its tree growth as part of agrarian reforms which was its dominant purpose - Hence, there was no repugnancy between provisions of the 1984 Act and the 1961 Act- Hence, no assent of President was warranted u/art. 254(2) of Constitution - Further, public purpose was pre-condition for deprivation of person from his property u/art. 300A of Constitutionand right to claim compensation was also inbuilt in that Article and when person was deprived of his property State had to justify both grounds which should be depended on scheme of statute, legislative policy, object and purpose of legislature and other related factors - Thus, directed notified authority under the 1996 Act to disburse amount of compensation fixed by the 1996 Act - Appeals dismissed.
