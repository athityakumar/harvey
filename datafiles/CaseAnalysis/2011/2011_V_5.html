Vaithi @Vaithianathan v State Of Tamil Nadu

Supreme Court of India

14 November 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 801; 2012 (1) AWC 18; 2012 CRLJ 669; 2012 (1) RCR(Civil) 356; 2012 (1) RCR(Criminal) 246

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Theft of electricity - Conviction - Confirmation by SC - Recall of order - SC, in instant appeal, upheld Trial Court order convicting appellant of an offence of theft of electricity and sentencing him to undergo rigorous imprisonment for a period of one month - Appellant's counsel contended that appellant had already undergone eight months of imprisonment and yet continued to be in jail even when sentence awarded to him was only for a period of one month - SC directed an enquiry into matter by HC and called for a report on subject - HC reported that appellant had undergone a sentence of 13 days only and that allegation that he had been in jail for 8 months was factually incorrect - Held, instant appeal was filed through Supreme Court Legal Service Committee (Committee) - One of the documents, that was furnished to appellant's counsel by Committee and formed part of SLP paper book, created an impression that appellant was taken into custody on 26-7-2004 and released only on 7-5-2005 i.e., 8 months - Appellant's counsel was, therefore, misled into making a statement that appellant had undergone eight months' imprisonment on a bona fide assumption - Further, appellant had already undergone 13 days' sentence as against 1 month's imprisonment awarded to him - In view of fact that offence in question was committed nearly 10 years back period already undergone by appellant should suffice - Order accordingly.
