Delhi Development Authority and others v S. S. Aggarwal and another

Supreme Court of India

02 August 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 480; (2011) 12 SCC 533; 2011 (6) AWC 5543; 2011 (3) CLT(SC) 272; 2011 (181) DLT 557; JT 2011 (11) SC 536; 2012 (5) RCR(Civil) 537; 2011(8) SCALE 178; [2011] 9 S.C.R. 756

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Assignees

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - Land acquisition - Entitlement to compensation - Land in question was acquired by Government - Landowners filed three claim petitions stating that keeping in view the prevailing market rates, they be paid compensation at the rate of Rs.4,000 per square yard - During the pendency of the matter before the Land Acquisition Collector, landowners executed Assignment Deed in favour of appellants and others (assignees) - However, assignees neither sought impleadment in the award proceedings nor produced the assignment deeds to show that the landowners had transferred the right to receive compensation - Award was passed fixing market value of the acquired land at the rate of Rs. 98 per square yard - Appellant and 5 others filed an application u/s. 18 of the Act for re-fixation of market value of the acquired land at the rate of Rs.10,000 per square yard by asserting that they fall in the category of interested persons - Collector did not make any inquiry on the issue of locus of appellant and others to claim compensation and referred the matter to the Court - Reference Court also did not inquire about the entitlement of appellant and others to claim compensation and disposed of the reference by fixing market value of the acquired land at the rate of Rs.102 per square yard - Appeal was filed thereagainst u/s. 54 of the Act - After four and a half years of filing the appeals, appellant and others filed petition for amendment of the memo of appeal so as to enable them to claim compensation at the rate of Rs.7,000 per square yard, but no order was passed by HC granting or refusing the prayer for amendment - Thereafter, appeals were disposed of by the HC and market value of the acquired land was enhanced from Rs.102 to Rs.7,390 per square yard and amendment application was also allowed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether HC erred by entertaining the amendment application filed after a long time gap of four and a half years? - Held, HC erred by entertaining and allowing the amendment application filed by appellant and others without even adverting to the issue of unexplained delay of 4 and half years - Also, Union of India and the DDA were deprived of an opportunity to make a request to the HC to remit the case to the Reference Court or to allow them to adduce evidence on the issue of correct market value of the acquired land - Hence, in the facts and circumstances of the case, it would be just and proper to set aside the impugned judgment and remit the case to the Reference Court for fresh determination of the amount of compensation payable to the landowner and/or assignee after giving them reasonable opportunity of adducing evidence in support of their respective cases - Matter remitted to the Reference Court - Further, in view of the law laid down in Delhi Development Authority v. Bhola Nath Sharma 2010 INDLAW SC 1063, the DDA would be entitled to participate in the proceedings of the Reference Court and raise objections against the claim made by the assignees for payment of compensation - Appeals disposed of.
