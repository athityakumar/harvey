Rakesh and another v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

19 September 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 596; (2011) 9 SCC 698; 2011 ALL MR (Cri) 3900; 2012 (1) JabLJ 1; JT 2011 (10) SC 525; 2011 (4) RCR(Criminal) 355; 2011(10) SCALE 584; [2011] 15 S.C.R. 34

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Natural Witness

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellants allegedly murdered the deceased - Trial Court convicted and sentenced the appellants u/s. 302 IPC - Appellants filed appeal - HC dismissed appeals - Whether the conviction and sentence recorded by the Trial Court and subsequent confirmation by the HC, was sustainable - Held, overt acts of the appellants accused and their motive were clearly mentioned in the FIR - It was improbable that the appellants had been enroped falsely as promptness in lodging the FIR showed that there was no time for manipulation - Prompt and early reporting of the occurrence by the informant with all its vivid details gave an assurance regarding truth of its version - It did not appeal to reasons as to why the witness would falsely enrope the appellants and other accused in such a heinous crime and spare the real culprits to go scot-free - Further, arrest of the accused and recovery of weapons on their disclosure statements proved the prosecution case - Trial Court had appreciated the evidence on record, and reached the conclusion to the effect that PW11 was a trustworthy witness and had been an eye-witness of the incident - Even if there were minor discrepancies between the narrations of witnesses when they spoke on details, unless such contradictions were of material dimensions, the same should not be used to discard the evidence in its entirety - Hence, courts below reached the correct conclusion in accepting the prosecution case as PW11 was a natural witness and his testimony inspired confidence and was, thus, worth acceptance - Facts and circumstances of the instant case did not warrant any interference by SC - Appeal dismissed.
