Radheshyam Kejriwal v State Of West Bengal & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

18 February 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Chandramauli Kumar Prasad, P. Sathasivam, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 106; (2011) 3 SCC 581; [2011] 163 Comp Cas 509; 2011 CRLJ 1747; 2011 (266) E.L.T. 294; [2011] 333 ITR 58; 2011 (2) JCC 943; JT 2011 (2) SC 443; 2011 (3) MLJ(Crl) 394; 2011 (2) RCR(Criminal) 756; 2011(2) SCALE 540; [2011] 4 S.C.R. 889; [2011] 40 VST 11

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Exchange Control & FDI; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Exchange Control & FDI - Foreign Exchange Regulation Act, 1973, ss. 9(1)(f)(i), 8(2), 35 and 64(2) - Criminal prosecution - Show cause notice was issued to appellant alleging contravention of the provisions of s. 8, 9(1)(f)(i) and s. 8(2) r/w s. 64(2) of the Act - Meanwhile, the Enforcement Directorate on the same allegation which was the subject matter of adjudication proceeding laid complaint against the appellant for prosecution u/s. 56 of the Act before the Metropolitan Magistrate - Subsequently, Adjudication officer held that the allegation made against the appellant of contravention of the provisions of s. 8, 9(1)(f)(i) and s. 8(2) r/w s. 64(2) of the Act cannot be sustained - After the issuance of process and exoneration in the adjudication proceeding appellant filed application for dropping the proceedings, which rejected - Revision application filed thereagainst was also dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether continuance of the criminal prosecution permissible? - Held, the yardstick would be to judge as to whether allegation in the adjudication proceeding as well as proceeding for prosecution was identical and the exoneration of the person concerned in the adjudication proceeding was on merits - In case it was found on merit that there was no contravention of the provisions of the Act in the adjudication proceeding, the trial of the person concerned shall be in abuse of the process of the court - In the instant case, in view of said finding by the Enforcement Directorate in adjudication proceeding that there was no contravention of any of the provisions of the Act, it would be unjust and an abuse of the process of the court to permit the Enforcement Directorate to continue with the criminal prosecution - Appeal allowed.
