Anil Sachar &Anr. v M/S. Shree Nath Spinners P.Ltd. & Ors. Etc.

Supreme Court of India

19 July 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Anil R. Dave, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 468; (2011) 13 SCC 148; AIR 2011 SC 2751; 2011 ALL MR (Cri) 2664; 2011 (3) BC 508; [2011] 165 Comp Cas 178; 2011 (3) CompLJ 654; 2011 CRLJ 4611; 2011 (3) JCC (NI) 186; JT 2011 (8) SC 586; 2011 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 2513; 2012 (2) MLJ(Crl) 377; 2011 (2) OLR 693; 2011 (3) RCR(Criminal) 698; 2011 (4) RLW 3442

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, s. 138 - Dishonour of cheques - Acquittal - Challenged - Accused no. 4, a Director of 'A' company had given in cheques to Complainant in consideration of supply of goods to 'B' company - Cheques were dishonoured - Complainant issued notice - In spite of the said notice, the complainant was not paid the amount covered under the cheques and, therefore, complaints were filed against the accused persons - It was alleged that 'A' company was a sister concern of 'B' company and the cheques were given by Accused no. 4 towards dues of 'B' company as a Director of 'A' company - Trial Court acquitted the accused persons for the reason that the goods had been supplied by the complainants to 'B' company but cheques had been given by 'A' company and as 'B' company and 'A' company were two different legal entities and there was nothing on record to show that the cheques were given by 'A' company in consideration of goods supplied by the complainants to 'B' company, there was no liability of 'A' company and, therefore, dishonour of the cheques would not make signatory of the cheques from the account of 'A' company liable under the provisions of the Act - Acquittal was upheld by HC - Hence, instant appeals - (A) Whether Judicial Magistrate and HC committed an error by acquitting the accused simply because the goods had been supplied to 'B' company whereas the cheques were given by 'A' company? - Held, complainants had established before the Trial Court that there was an understanding among the complainants and the accused persons that in consideration of supply of goods to 'B' company, 'A' company was to make the payment - the understanding was on account of the fact that directors in both the companies were common and the companies were sister concerns - Hence, it was proved that in consideration of supply of goods to 'B' company, 'A' company had made the payment - In view of the above fact, Trial Court was not right in concluding that there was no reason for 'A' company to give the cheques to the complainants - (B) Presumption in favour of holder - Held, according to the provisions of the s. 139, there was a presumption with regard to consideration when a cheque was paid by the drawer of the cheque - In the facts of the instant case, all the four cheques were presumed to have been given for consideration - Presumption u/s. 139 of the Act had not been rebutted by the accused persons - Hence, Trial Court erred while coming to a conclusion that in criminal law no presumption could be raised with regard to consideration as no goods had been supplied by the complainants to 'A' company - Trial Court wrongly acquitted the accused persons by taking a view that there was no consideration for which the cheques were given by respondent no. 4 to the complainants - Acquittal order set aside - Accused no. 4 who had signed all the cheques for 'A' company held guilty of the offence u/s. 138 of the Act - Order accordingly.
