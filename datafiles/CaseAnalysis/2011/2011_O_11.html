Phulchand Exports Ltd v Ooo Patriot

Supreme Court of India

12 October 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, Jagdish Singh Khehar

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 737; (2011) 10 SCC 300; 2012 (2) ALD(SC) 133; 2012 (90) ALR 36; 2011 (6) AWC 6285; 2011 (4) CLT(SC) 339; 2011 (6) CTC 673; [2011] 15 S.C.R. 1129; 2012 (2) WLN(SC) 49

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, ss. 34, 47, 48, 48(2)(b) - Indian Contract Act, 1872, ss. 23, 73, 74 - Sale of Goods Act, 1930, s. 26 - Contract for Goods - Non-Delivery - Arbitral Award - Sustainability - Contract to supply 1000 tons of long-grain rice was entered between appellant-seller and respondent-buyer - Vessel could not deliver goods due to break-down on high-seas and consequent detention of vessel at intermediary port - Liability of insurers was not established - Respondents made claim in Arbitral Tribunal for reimbursement of transferred amount - Appellants contended that risk in goods and property in goods passed to respondents upon shipment of goods - Arbitral Tribunal held that appellants shipped goods 16 days later of stipulated time and vessel freighted by appellants left 38 days later than time of departure stipulated in contract however, respondents neither did pass insurance certificate/cargo documents to appellants nor did demand reimbursement of transferred amount immediately after expiration of 180 days from appellants - Award of Arbitral Tribunal was to split amount of losses between parties - Single Judge upheld arbitration petition filed by respondents - Appellants contested order of Single Judge on ground that award was against public policy - DB dismissed appeal - Hence, instant appeal - Whether enforcement of award given by Arbitral Tribunal in favour of respondent was contrary to public policy u/s. 48(2)(b) of the 1996 Act - Held, parties to contract were free to by-pass prima facie rule provided in s. 26 of the 1930 Act by making agreement otherwise - Delay in shipping goods, delayed departure of vessel from port of loading, and goods were shipped in vessel having no firm commitment to reach port of contractual destination as first port of discharge breached terms of contract - Appellants' failure to discharge primary obligation under contract regarding shipment of goods should be held to have rebutted s. 23 of the 1930 Act - There was no delivery of goods due to fault of appellants in shipment of goods hence goods continued to be at risk of appellants - Stipulation for reimbursement in event stated in last para of cl. 4 of contract was not in nature of penalty - Clause of reimbursement or repayment in event of delayed delivery/arrival or non-delivery should not to be regarded as damages - Appellants and respondents in instant case were business persons having no unequal bargaining powers hence clause for reimbursement or repayment in circumstances provided therein was neither unreasonable nor unjust - Arbitral Tribunal had only awarded reimbursement of half price paid by respondents to appellants - Hence, arbitral award should not be held to be unconscionable or contrary to public policy - Appeal dismissed.
