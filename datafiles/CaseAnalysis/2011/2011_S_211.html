Vasanth Sreedhar Kulkarni and another v State Of Karnataka And Others

Supreme Court of India

14 October 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 749; (2012) 1 SCC 138; 2013 (1) KarLJ 389

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Keywords: Karnataka Improvement Boards Act, 1976, Karnataka (Amendment) Act, 1961

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Karnataka Urban Development Authorities Act, 1987, ss. 3, 16, 17, 17(1), 18, 18(3), 19, 19(7), 19(1) - Karnataka Improvement Boards Act, 1976 - Karnataka (Amendment) Act, 1961 - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 3(c), 4, 5, 5A, 6, 12(2), 16(2), 36, 48 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908,s. 151 - Land acquisition - Denotification - Validity - Appellants land was acquired and award was approved by State Govt. - Notice was issued to landowners u/s. 12(2) of the 1894 Act and possession of land was taken and name of BDA was mutated in revenue records - Appellants filed writs questioning notifications issued u/ss. 17(1) and 19(1) of the 1987 Act and all writ petitions were dismissed by Single Judge - Possession of land comprised in survey Nos. 533/1 and 534/B was also taken by authority and entries were made in the record of rights in name of BDA after petitions were dismissed - It was notices issuance of notifications u/ss. 17(1) and 19(1) of the 1987 Act, landowners entered into some clandestine transactions with'X', who was described as their General Power of Attorney and created large number of documents on ten rupees stamps showing sale of small parcels of land to 'Y'and others - At that juncture, 'X'and others made representation to Commissioner, BDA for release of land comprised in survey Nos. 533/1, 534/A and 534/B by stating that 120 persons belonging to weaker sections of society had constructed houses after taking loan and even scheme sanctioned by State Govt. envisages allotment of 52% plots to the persons belonging to backward classes and weaker sections of the society - Single Judge also declared that erstwhile landowners did not have locus to challenge allotment of sites because writ petitions filed by them questioning notifications issued u/ss. 17(1) and 19(1) of the 1987 Act had been dismissed and acquired land had vested in BDA -

Whether HC committed serious error by nullifying notification dt. 24-.-3-.1999 at the instance of those to whom sites were allotted by BDA - Held, there was no merit in argument of appellants - Documents produced before HC and SC showed that possession of land comprised in was taken on 1-.1-.1996 and possession of land comprised in survey Nos. 533/1, 534/B was taken after dismissal of Writ petitions - After taking of possession, name of BDA was entered in record of rights - Further, appellants had not produced any evidence before Court to show that Panchnamas evidencing takeover of possession were fabricated by Special Land Acquisition Officer and entries in record of rights were manipulated by the concerned revenue authorities - Therefore, bald statement made by the landowners that they continued to be in possession of acquired land could not be relied upon for recording a finding that denotification of acquired land was valid - Hence, possession of acquired land had been taken by Special Land Acquisition Officer in accordance with law - It also appears to us that both, BDA and State Govt. laboured under a mistaken impression that power u/s. 19(7) of the 1987 Act could be exercised by latter - Appellants were awarded cost of Rs.1,00,000/- each to BDA for thrusting unwarranted litigation upon it - Appeals dismissed.
