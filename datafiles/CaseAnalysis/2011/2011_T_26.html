Varghese K. Joseph v The Custodian & Ors. ..

Supreme Court of India

31 January 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Gyan Sudha Misra, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 68; (2011) 3 SCC 394; AIR 2011 SC 832; [2011] 162 Comp Cas 451; 2011 (2) CompLJ 211; JT 2011 (2) SC 11; 2011 (1) RCR(Criminal) 862; 2011(2) SCALE 48; [2011] 2 S.C.R. 269

Case Digest

Subject: Capital Market

Keywords: Custodian, Transaction of Sale of Securities Act, 1956, Securities (Control) Regulation Act, 1956

Summary: Capital Market - Special Courts (Trial of Offences Relating to Transactions in Securities) Act, 1992 - Securities Contracts (Regulation) Act, 1956 - Certification of tainted securities - Denial on ground of delay - Legality - Appellant, investor purchased 100 equity shares of the respondent No.2 (Reliance Industries Ltd.) and payment of the same was made through his share broker (respondent No.4) - However, when appellant approached respondent No.2 seeking dividend and other consequential benefits, it was informed to the appellant by the respondent No.2 that the shares of the appellant on which dividend was claimed, were found to be tainted and hence it was unable to consider the request of the appellant for payment of dividends - Appellant filed application before the Special Court under the provisions of Act, 1992 wherein he prayed for certification of the shares by the respondent No.1-Custodian and its release and payment of accruals but Special Court intimated appellant that his application could not be entertained as it was filed after cut-off date - Hence, present appeal - Whether the Special Court was right in rejecting the application of the appellant seeking certification of the tainted shares on the ground of delay due to violation of cut off date in spite of absence of a statutory provision to that effect as also the fact that the appellant admittedly had no role or involvement in treatment of the alleged equity shares as tainted which required certification before payment of dividends on the same? - Held, Act, 1992 was enacted for speedy trial of offences relating to transactions in securities and disposal of properties attached - Further, cut off date sought by the custodian and accepted by the Special Court cannot be construed so as to have a binding effect of statutory nature under the provisions of the Act, 1956, wherein there is no fixed time limit for encashment of shares nor there is prescribed procedure for certification which emerged only on account of extraordinary situation when certain shares were found to be tainted - Therefore, Special Court had the duty to ensure that in absence of a statutory time limit prescribed for certification of shares under the Act, 1956, r/w Act, 1992, the Special Court was duty bound to guard the interest of the investors through the Custodian at least in case of those investors who had bonafide purchased the shares of a notified company which for reasons beyond the control of investors, was held to be tainted - Hence, appellant under the facts and existing circumstances of the case could not have been denied his due on the ground of delay in filing the application for certification - Impugned order of the Special Court set aside - Appeal allowed.
