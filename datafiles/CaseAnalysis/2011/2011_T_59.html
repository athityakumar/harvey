Thirumalai Chemicals Limited v Union Of India & Ors

Supreme Court of India

11 April 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 294; (2011) 6 SCC 739; AIR 2011 SC 1725; 2011 (5) AWC 4585; 2012 (1) Bom.C.R. 790; 2012 (1) CLT(SC) 60; [2011] 163 Comp Cas 380; 2011 (3) CompLJ 46; 2011 (268) E.L.T. 296; JT 2011 (4) SC 453; 2011 (3) RCR(Civil) 20; 2011(4) SCALE 642; [2011] 4 S.C.R. 838

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Fema

Summary: FEMA - Constitution - Foreign Exchange Regulation Act 1973, ss. 8(3), 8(4) and 52(2) - Foreign Exchange Management Act 1999, ss. 19(2) and 49 - Limitation Act, 1963 - General Clauses Act, 1956 - Jurisdiction - Petitioner imported various consignments for home consumption and forwarded Exchange Control Copies (ECC) of bills of entry to the authorized dealer (Bank) for submission to the Reserve Bank of India (RBI) - Authorized dealer did not forward said ECC of bills of entry to RBI - Directorate of Enforcement issued show cause notices to the petitioner alleging that it had contravened the provisions of ss. 8(3) and 8(4) of FERA r/w. s. 49 of FEMA as petitioner failed to furnish the required bills/ information/documents to the RBI and held that an appeal would lie before the Appellate Tribunal after depositing the amount of penalty imposed within 45 days from the date on which the order was served - Petitioner informed Enforcement Directorate that it was not due to the mistake of the petitioner that the ECC of bills of entry were not forwarded to the Directorate of Enforcement in time, but due to the mistake of the authorized dealer - Petitioner also informed Enforcement Directorate that RBI had subsequently carried out necessary corrections and deleted the entries from their records and regularized the transactions and requested to drop the proceedings initiated against the petitioner - Subsequently, petitioner filed appeals against orders of Enforcement Directorate with an application u/s. 5 of Limitation Act, 1963 r/w. ss. 19 and 49(5) (a) of FEMA for condonation of delay - Tribunal dismissed said appeals on the ground of delay by applying the first proviso to sub section (2) of s. 52 of FERA - Petitioner filed writ petition against said order which was also dismissed by the HC - Hence, the instant appeals - Whether the Tribunal was right in dismissing the appeals preferred u/s. 19(1) of FEMA, by applying the first proviso to sub section (2)of s. 52 of FERA holding that it had no power to condone the delay beyond 90 days from the date on which the order was served on the person committing the contravention? - Held, u/s. 19 of FEMA, there was no ceiling in regard to the period of delay that could be condoned by the appellate tribunal - If sufficient cause was made out, delay beyond 45 days can also be condoned - The tribunal and the HC misdirected themselves in assuming that the period of limitation was governed by s. 52(2) of FERA - Moreover, on a combined reading of s. 49 of FEMA and s. 6 of General Clauses Act, it was clear that the procedure prescribed by FEMA only would be applicable in respect of an appeal filed under FEMA though cause of action arose under FERA - The time limit prescribed under FERA was taken away under the proviso to sub-section (2) of s. 19 of FEMA and the Tribunal had been conferred with wide powers to condone delay if the appeal was not filed within 45 days prescribed, provided sufficient cause is shown - Therefore, the findings rendered by the Tribunal as well as the HC was not correct, hence, set aside - Matter remanded back to the tribunal for fresh consideration - Appeals disposed of.
