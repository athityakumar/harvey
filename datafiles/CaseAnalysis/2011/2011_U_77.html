Hardev Singh v Union of India and another

Supreme Court of India

14 September 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

A.R. Dave, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 656; (2011) 10 SCC 121; AIR 2012 SC 286; 2013 (1) AWC 121; 2012 (1) MahLJ 557; 2012 (1) MPLJ 341; 2012 (7) SLR 1

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces; Service

Keywords: Armed Forces, Armed Forces Tribunal Act, 2007

Summary: Defence & Security Forces - Service - Armed Forces Tribunal Act, 2007, s. 30 - Promotion - Service tenure - Sustainability - Appellant was not promoted to rank of Lieutenant General - Policy with regard to promotion had been changed after Special Selection Board (SSB) had been constituted for considering cases of officers of 1973 batch for promotion to rank of Lieutenant General - After SSB started process of selecting officers for promotion Govt. had promulgated a new policy dt. 31-12-2008 with regard to promotion - Formerly policy with regard to promotion was based on 'value-judgment' and on that basis SSB was to consider overall profile of all concerned officers - Policy was changed w.e.f 1-1-2009 and instead of 'value judgment', new policy described as 'quantification method' had been adopted for giving promotion - As per new policy, while considering cases for promotion, 92% weightage was to be given to confidential reports, 3% to courses, honours and awards and 5% weightage was to be given to value judgment - Appellant had not been promoted filed non-statutory and statutory complaints but said complaints were not properly considered and were disposed of without assigning any reason - Further, appellant approached Tribunal by filing application - Application was transferred to Principal bench of Tribunal and was rejected - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order passed by Principal Bench of Tribunal was justified - Held, appellant's case was duly considered by SSB for promotion - Appellant and others were never considered by SSB in 2008 or prior to 1-1-2009, cases were considered as per new policy and, therefore, appellants claim that policy was changed after process of selection had been started were incorrect and were discarded - Relevant service record of appellant was placed before SSB on two different occasions and appellant failed to get himself empanelled in list of selected officers for reason that there were better officers available - It was further held that it was always open to employer to change its policy in relation to giving promotion to employees and Court would not interfere in policy decisions - Relying upon judgment made in B alco Employees' Union (Regd.) v. Union of India and Others 2001 Indlaw SC 20366 it was held that Court could not strike down policy decision taken by Govt. merely because it feels that another policy would have been fairer or wiser or more scientific or logical - It was not within domain of Court to weigh pros and cons of policy or to test degree of its beneficial or equitable disposition - Hence, no injustice had been caused to appellant as case was duly considered for promotion by SSB twice but as other officers were found better than appellant, he could not be promoted - Appeal dismissed.
