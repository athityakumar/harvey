Urviben Chiragbhai Sheth v Vijaybhai Shambhubhai Joranputra & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

26 April 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Ganguly, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 291; (2011) 12 SCC 582; 2011 (2) ACC 379; 2011 ACJ 1445; AIR 2011 SC 2502; 2011 (5) ALD(SC) 55; 2011 (86) ALR 890; 2011 (4) AWC 3617; 2011 (3) G.L.R. 1871; JT 2011 (5) SC 396; 2011 (7) MLJ(SC) 823; 2011 (2) OLR 135; 2011 (2) RCR(Civil) 871; 2011(5) SCALE 225; [2011] 5 S.C.R. 897

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Insurance - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 - Motor accident claim - Award based on consent of parties - Sustainability - Appellant met with an accident due to rash and negligent driving of 1st respondent driver - Appellant filed claim petition - Tribunal awarded compensation on basis of consent of parties - Appellant filed appeal before HC for enhancement of compensation - HC dismissed appeal - Appellant contended that there was no consent before Tribunal, and the same was wrongly recorded by Tribunal - Held, HC refused to take into consideration the affidavit filed by appellant's advocate stating that appellant had not given any consent with regard to computation of compensation - HC ignored said affidavit on ground that such affidavit being placed before it was an afterthought and no ground had been taken in memorandum of appeal to that effect - Tribunal held that in facts of case, amount granted by it was just proper and reasonable and also held that same was based on consent of parties - HC, as last court of fact and law, should have examined whether Tribunal's finding that compensation granted was proper, just and reasonable in facts of case - In absence of any leave obtained from Tribunal to compromise matter, when an affidavit was filed by appellant's counsel contending that no such settlement was ever entered into by consent of parties, HC fell into an error by discarding same only on ground that such affidavit was filed belatedly before HC and was an afterthought - Further, dependence of children and running of family was to be shouldered by appellant even though she was infirm and bedridden due to accident - Appellant also required someone to help her in her daily life and to meet her medical expenses - Just because appellant was a homemaker was no reason why courts should be miserly in fixing compensation for her - Compensation, therefore, was enhanced - Appeal allowed.
