Sesa Industries Limited v Krishna H. Bajaj and others

Supreme Court of India

07 February 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

D.K. Jain, H.L. Dattu

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 88; (2011) 3 SCC 218; AIR 2011 SC 1070; 2011(3) ALL MR 483; 2011 (3) Bom.C.R. 768; [2011] 162 Comp Cas 119; 2011 (1) CompLJ 481; JT 2011 (2) SC 266; 2011(2) SCALE 248; [2011] 3 S.C.R. 317

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate

Keywords: Dissenting

Summary: Corporate - Companies Act, 1956, ss. 209A and 391 - Scheme of amalgamation - Appellant-company (SIL) was incorporated as a subsidiary of Sesa Goa Limited (SGL) with the latter holding 88.85% of the shares in the former - A resolution was passed by the Board of Directors of SIL to amalgamate SIL with SGL - In pursuance thereof, SIL and SGL filed respective company applications in the HC seeking permission to convene a general body meeting for seeking approval of shareholders for the said amalgamation - Respondent No. 1, a share holder in SIL, filed an affidavit intervening in the company petitions - Ignoring the objections raised by respondent No.1, HC allowed SIL and SGL to convene meetings for seeking approval of shareholders for the said amalgamation, and directed the companies to disclose, as part of the Explanatory Statement to be sent with individual notices - Single Judge of HC sanctioned the scheme of amalgamation between SGL and SIL - Respondent No.1 filed an intra-court appeal before a DB of the HC - DB of HC set aside the order of the Single Judge and revoked the sanction to the amalgamation scheme - Hence, instant appeals - (A) Whether once a scheme of amalgamation had been approved by a majority of the shareholders after sufficient disclosure in the explanatory statement regarding the pendency of an inspection u/s. 209A of the Act, court called upon to sanction a scheme of amalgamation should not act as a court of appeal? - Held, s. 394 of the Act casts an obligation on the Court to be satisfied that the scheme of amalgamation or merger was not prejudicial to the interest of its members or to public interest - Thus, even though approved by a majority of the concerned members or creditors, the Court had to be satisfied that the company or any other person moving such an application for sanction under sub-s. (2) of s. 391 had disclosed all the relevant matters mentioned in the proviso to the said sub-section -Therefore, Court before whom the scheme was placed, was not expected to put its seal of approval on the scheme merely because the majority of the shareholders have voted in favour of the scheme - It was manifest that before according its sanction to a scheme of amalgamation, the Court had to see that the provisions of the Act have been duly complied with; the statutory majority had been acting bona fide and in good faith - (B) Whether the DB was correct in holding that the affidavit filed by the Official Liquidator was vitiated on account of non-disclosure of all material facts? - Held, for examining the questions as to why the transferor-company came into existence; for what purpose it was set up; who were its promoters; who were controlling it; what object was sought to be achieved by dissolving it and merging with another company, by way of a scheme of amalgamation, the report of an official liquidator was of seminal importance and in fact facilitates the Company Judge to record its satisfaction as to whether or not the affairs of the transferor company had been carried on in a manner prejudicial to the interest of the minority and to the public interest - Hence, Official Liquidator was obliged to incorporate in his affidavit the contents of the inspection report - However, in the instant case, Official Liquidator had not examined and applied its mind to the findings contained in the inspection report u/s. 209A of the Act - Official liquidator failed to discharge the statutory burden placed on him under the second proviso to s. 394(1) - (C) Whether sanction of a scheme of amalgamation could be held up merely because the conduct of an Official Liquidator was not found to be satisfactory? - Held, effect of misdemeanour on the part of the official liquidator on the scheme as such would depend on the facts obtaining in each case and ordinarily the Company Judge should be the final arbiter on that issue - In the instant case, the Company Judge had, before him, all material facts which had a direct bearing on the sanction of the amalgamation scheme, despite the lapse on the part of the Official Liquidator - Hence, Company Judge, having examined all material facts, was justified in sanctioning the scheme of amalgamation, particularly when the current investigation u/s. 235 of the Act was initiated pursuant to a complaint filed by respondent No.1 subsequent to the order of the Company Judge sanctioning the scheme - Impugned judgment was set aside - Order passed by the Company Judge sanctioning the scheme of amalgamation restored - Appeals allowed.
