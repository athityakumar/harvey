Gosu Jairami Reddy & Anr. v State Of A.P.

Supreme Court of India

26 July 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, V.S. Sirpurkar

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 469; (2011) 11 SCC 766; 2011 CRLJ 4387; 2011 (3) JCC 2160; JT 2011 (8) SC 263; 2012 (1) MLJ(Crl) 793; 2011 (4) RCR(Criminal) 540; 2011(8) SCALE 58; [2011] 9 S.C.R. 503

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 147, 148, 149 and 302 - Explosive Substances Act, 1908, ss. 3 and 5 - Rioting - Murder - Conviction - Challenged - Trial Court convicted A1 to A5 for commission of offences punishable u/ss. 147, 148 and 302 r/w s. 149 of IPC and ss. 3 and 5 of the Explosive Substances Act - On appeal, HC affirmed the conviction and sentence awarded to the appellants and dismissed the appeal - It was held that prosecution had established the existence of political rivalry as the motive for the murder of the deceased - Hence, instant appeals - (A) Motive - Political rivalry - Held, there evidence on record in the form of depositions of PW1 and PW2 regarding the alleged political rivalry between the two sides and same was also mentioned in the FIR lodged by PW1 - Complaint and FIR attributed the reason for the murder of the deceased to his refusal to support the candidature of A1 and A3 in the elections - It was not, therefore, a case where motive was introduced as an improvement in the prosecution story - Prosecution case that the appellants had a motive for the commission of the offence alleged against them thus stood satisfactorily proved - (B) Delay of one hour in lodging FIR - Held, credibility of a report could not be judged only by reference to the days, hours or minutes it had taken to reach the police station concerned - It was the totality of the circumstances that would determine whether the delay long or short had in any way affected the truthfulness of the report lodged in a given case - In the instant case, a report regarding the commission of a cognizable offence, lodged within an hour of the incident could not be said to be so inordinately delayed as to give rise to a suspicion - (C) Evidence of witnesses - Creditability - Held, depositions of PW 2 and PW3 have fully supported the prosecution case and the narrative given by PW1 - Version of these eye witnesses to the occurrence was accepted as truthful by the Trial Court as also the HC in appeal - In the absence of any material contradiction in the version given by these witnesses and in the absence of any other cogent reason rendering the depositions unacceptable, there was no reason why the said version should not be accepted as truthful - Further, there was no material contradiction between the ocular evidence on the one hand and the medical evidence - (D) Non-examination of one of the witness 'D' - Held, simply because more than one witnesses have been cited to establish the very same fact was no reason why the prosecution must examine all of them - Prosecution in the instant case examined three eye-witnesses to prove the incident in question - There was no particular fact that could be proved only by the deposition of 'D' and not by other witnesses - Further, once the deposition of the eye-witnesses examined at the trial was accepted as trustworthy the non-examination of other witnesses would become inconsequential - View taken by courts below upheld - Appeals dismissed.
