Lalrinvenga (Dead) Through L.Rs. and another v State of Mizoram and others

Supreme Court of India

13 September 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, H.L. Dattu

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 592; (2011) 13 SCC 190; 2011(6) ALL MR 477; 2011 (89) ALR 249; 2011 (6) AWC 5452; 2012 (2) CalLT 59; 2011 (4) CLT(SC) 36; JT 2011 (11) SC 18; 2012 (2) RCR(Civil) 641; 2011 (114) RD 839; 2011(10) SCALE 431

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property

Keywords: Mizo District (Land Revenue) Act, 1956, Mizo District (Agricultural Land) Rules, 1971

Summary: Administrative - Land & Property - Mizo District (Agricultural Land) Rules, 1971, r. 6 - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4, 6(1) - Land acquisition - Cancellation of Land Settlement Certificates (LSCs) - Legality - Appellants purchased the land in question and submitted applications u/r. 6 for the issuance of LSC - 2nd respondent Land Revenue Director issued LSC to appellants - In the meanwhile, State Government issued notification u/s. 4 of the Act for acquiring the land in question for Assam Rifles - State Government subsequently issued declaration u/s. 6(1) of the Act and Land Acquisition Collector passed an award - State Government directed 2nd respondent to cancel the LSC issued to the appellant by vide order dated 14-6-1985 - 2nd respondent accordingly cancelled the LSC - Appellants filed writ petition seeking to quash the cancellation order of 2nd respondent and to issue a mandamus to the respondents to pay full compensation with interest and solatium in lieu of the acquisition of their land - Single Judge allowed writ petitions - Respondents filed appeal - DB allowed respondents' appeal - Whether the appellants were entitled to compensation and other benefits in lieu of the acquisition of their land - Held, notification issued by an appropriate Government proposing the acquisition of land should be published in the Official Gazette and two daily newspapers having wide circulation in the locality of which at least one should be in the regional language - Collector was under an obligation to ensure that public notice of the substance of such notification was given at convenient places in the locality - DB upheld the acquisition of land by assuming that notification dated 14-5-1985 issued u/s. 4(1) would have been published in the Official Gazette because the declaration issued u/s. 6 was published in the Official Gazette, however, that approach of the DB was clearly erroneous - Question whether the notification issued u/s. 4(1) was published in the Official Gazette was a question of fact and such question should not be decided on assumptions and conjectures or inferences - Whenever the acquisition of land was challenged on the ground that the notification had not been published as per the mandate of the statute, the authority defending the acquisition was under an obligation to produce evidence in the form of documents to prove that the requirement of publication had been complied - In the absence of such evidence, the Court should not decide challenge to the acquisition proceedings by assuming that the particular notification had been published as per the requirement of law - In the instant case, no material was produced before the HC and none had been produced before the SC to show that notification dated 14-5-1985 issued u/s. 4(1) of the Act had been published in the Official Gazette - Therefore, the HC was not justified in declining relief to the appellants by assuming that the said notification should have been published in the Official Gazette because other notifications including the one issued u/s. 6 was published in the Official Gazette - It was also not in dispute that the appellants had purchased land in 1984 and the Settlement Officer had issued the LSC in 1987 after duly scrutinizing the applications made for that purpose - Action of the concerned officer was not in violation of order dated 14-6-1985 whereby allotment of land to private individuals was restricted/freezed - It was neither the pleaded case of the respondents before the HC nor any evidence was produced to show that the land in question had been allotted to the appellants by any public authority - Impugned judgment of DB was set aside and the order passed by the Single Judge was restored - Directions issued to the respondents to pay the amount of compensation to the appellants in terms of the order of the Single Judge - Appeal allowed.
