State of Jharkhand and another v Pakur Jagran Manch and others

Supreme Court of India

12 January 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, H.L. Gokhale

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 30; (2011) 2 SCC 591; AIR 2011 SC 675; JT 2011 (3) SC 84; 2011 (5) MLJ(SC) 685; 2011 (5) RCR(Civil) 41; 2011(1) SCALE 309; [2011] 1 S.C.R. 687

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property

Keywords: Santhal Parganas Tenancy (Supplementary Provisions) Act, 1949, Bihar and Orissa General Clauses Act, Santhal Parganas Settlement Regulations, 1872

Summary: Land & Property - Administrative - Santhal Parganas Settlement Regulations, 1872, regn. 24 - Santhal Parganas Tenancy (Supplementary Provisions) Act, 1949, s. 38 - Settlement Officer notified and published a record- of-rights u/s. 24 of the 1872 Regulations whereby land in dispute was recorded as village grazing land for the village - State Health Department, in pursuance of a HC direction, identified the disputed land as suitable land for constructing a hospital - Appellant/State, consequently, commenced the construction after obtaining the consent from village headman - 1st respondent filed a Public Interest Litigation before the HC challenging the construction in the village grazing land (disputed land) - Appellant/State, in the meantime, de-notified the disputed land from record-of-rights vide notification dated 31-5-2007 and declared another tract of land as village grazing land u/s. 38(2) of 1949 Act - HC, by impugned order, set aside the de-notification on the ground of bar contained in s. 38(1) r/w ss. 67, 69 of the 1949 Act - Hence the present appeal by the appellant/State - Whether s. 38(1) of the 1949 Act was violated by the State Government, in using the village graze land for constructing a hospital, after de-reserving it from its status as village graze land - Held, HC erroneously assumed that as there was no provision in the 1949 Act for de-reserving village graze land for other uses - HC also erroneously assumed that once a land was recorded as village graze land, such land should forever be village graze land - Prohibition u/s. 38(1) of the 1949 Act in regard to settlement, cultivation or utilization for non-grazing purposes is applicable only to land recorded as village grazing land - If the land is not recorded as village grazing land, or if the land ceases to be shown as village grazing land in the record-of- rights for valid reasons, then the bar u/s. 38(1) will not apply - Manner of recording a land as village grazing land or the manner of de-reserving any land recorded as village grazing land is not governed or regulated by s. 38 of the 1949 Act - If the State Government has the power to de-reserve or de-notify village grazing land under any other law, and such power is validly exercised, then the land will cease to be village grazing land and the prohibition u/s. 38(1) of the 1949 Act in regard to non-grazing use will not apply - In the instant case, Deputy Commissioner is the authority empowered to reopen the record-of-rights for the purpose of de-reserving the land recorded as village grazing land by altering its use - Deputy Commissioner made a proposal seeking the sanction of the appellant/State for de-reserving the land in question and the appellant/State by the impugned notification dated 31-5-2007 granted such approval by passing an order of de-reservation - By the very same notification, it ensured that s. 38(2) of the 1949 Act was also fulfilled by earmarking alternative land as village grazing land - Further, de-reservation was effected by the Deputy Commissioner in pursuance of the approval granted by the appellant/State, by making appropriate entry in the record-of-rights of the village - Therefore, the notification in question has to be read as an order granting reopening of the final record-of-rights of the village for the purpose of de-reserving the disputed land for constructing a hospital with the consent of the village headman and at the same time instructing and directing the Deputy Commissioner to ensure that appropriate suitable land is earmarked for grazing u/s. 38(2) of the Act - In the present case the urgent need for de-reserving the disputed land and diversion of its use for the public purpose of hospital was not in dispute - Village headman and all the concerned interest parties had consented to the de-reservation and use of the land in question for hospital - Land in question was found to be most suitable for housing the hospital and alternative land was immediately notified as village grazing land - Therefore, notification dated 31-5-2007 of the Government de-notifying the disputed land is upheld and hence the impugned HC order is set aside - Appeals allowed.
