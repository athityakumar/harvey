Ganduri Koteshwaramma & Anr v Chakiri Yanadi & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

12 October 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, Jagdish Singh Khehar

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 739; AIR 2012 SC 169; 2012 (2) ALD(SC) 50; 2012(1) ALL MR 443; 2011 (89) ALR 453; 2012 (1) ALT 29; 2011 (6) AWC 6305; 2012 (4) Bom.C.R. 821; 2012 (1) CGLJ 268; 2011 (4) CLT(SC) 1; 2011 (6) CTC 102; 2011 (6) KarLJ 307; 2012 (1) MahLJ 613; 2012 (1) MLJ 333; 2012 (1) MLJ(SC) 175; 2012 (1) MPLJ 333; 2011 (4) RCR(Civil) 916; 2012 (115) RD 9; [2011] 12 S.C.R. 968

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Family & Personal; Land & Property

Summary: Civil Procedure - Family & Personal - Land & Property - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 20 r. 18, s. 97 - Hindu Succession (Amendment) Act, 2005 - Hindu Succession Act, 1956, s. 6 - Registration Act, 1908 - Coparcenary Property - Partition - Preliminary Decree - Amendment to Parent Act - Applicability - Respondent filed suit for partition of joint-family property - Father of respondent/ defendant no. 1 died during pendency of suit - Trial Court passed preliminary decree declaring 1/3rd share in schedule A, C and D properties and 1/5th share of schedule B property and also declared 1/4th share in 1/3rd share left by defendant no. 1 - Respondent made application before Trial Court for passing final decree and for determination of mesne profits - Appellants made an application under the 2005 Act, for passing preliminary decree in their favour for partition of schedule properties A, C and D into four equal shares - Trial Court allowed appellants application and held that appellants were entitled to 1/4th share each and separate possession in schedule properties A, C and D - Aggrieved, respondent filed appeal before HC, which set aside decree of Trial Court - Hence, instant appeal - Whether benefits of the 2005 Act were available to appellants and partition had been effected before 20-12-2004 either by registered instrument of partition or by decree of Court - Held, except categories to which new s. 6 of the 1956 Act was not applicable were two, namely, (i) where disposition or alienation including any partition had taken place before 20-12-2004; and (ii) where testamentary disposition of property had been made before 20-12-2004 - In instant case, partition had not been effected before 20-12-2004 either by registered instrument of partition or by decree of Court - Suit filed by respondent no. 1 had reached stage where determination of shares vide preliminary decree dt. 19-3-1999 which came to be amended on 27-9-2003 - Suit for partition would not be disposed of by passing of preliminary decree - Unless partition of property was effected by metes and bounds, daughters should not be deprived of benefits conferred by the 2005 Act - CPC created no impediment for even more than one preliminary decree if after passing of preliminary decree events had taken place necessitating readjustment of shares as declared in preliminary decree - Hence, suit for partition would continue after passing of preliminary decree and proceedings in suit get extinguished only on passing of final decree - Thus, impugned judgment of HC was set aside and restored order of Trial Court - Appeal allowed.
