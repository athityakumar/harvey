Pradeep Oil Corporation v Municipal Corporation Of Delhi Andanr

Supreme Court of India

06 April 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Mukundakam Sharma, A.R. Dave

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 261; (2011) 5 SCC 270; AIR 2011 SC 1869; 2011 (86) ALR 703; 2012 (186) DLT 1; JT 2011 (5) SC 94; 2011 (5) MLJ(SC) 193; 2011 (4) RCR(Civil) 313; 2011 (5) RCR(Civil) 582; 2011 (2) RCR(Rent) 285; 2011(4) SCALE 422; [2011] 4 S.C.R. 764

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Municipalities & Local Governments - Delhi Municipal Corporation Act, 1957, ss. 20(2) and 119 - Government Grant Act, 1895 - Property Tax - Lease or License - The President of India under the Government Grant Act, 1895, granted separate and distinct licenses to the appellant for maintaining depot for storage of petroleum products at a yearly license fee - Appellant had also given the right to erect/construct 'petroleum installation buildings' consisting of petroleum tanks, buildings and other conveniences for receiving and storing petroleum in bulk, and consequently possession of land had been given - Appellant raised various constructions comprising of an administration block etc. along with huge petroleum storage tanks for storing petroleum products - MCD (Respondent) vide its order dt. 17.08.1984 passed an assessment order with regard to the property tax qua the aforesaid property and confirmed the rateable value proposed by it - Tribunal set aside the order passed by the MCD and held that the appellant was only a licensee in the property, therefore, no property tax can be levied on the appellant u/s. 20(2) of the 1957 Act - Single Judge dismissed writ petition filed against said order - Full Bench of HC held that the petroleum storage tanks were a building and the petitioner was a lessee and not a licensee in the impugned property - Hence, the instant appeal - Whether an agreement entered into between appellant and the President of India, for erection of oil storage tank together with pump house, chowkidar cabins, switch room, residential rooms and verandah for storing oil decanted from the railway tankers, would amount to lease or license? - Held, whether a particular document will constitute 'lease' or 'license' would depend upon certain factors like whether a document creates a license or lease, the substance of the document must be preferred to the form, the real test was the intention of the parties, if the document creates an interest in the property, it was a lease but if it only permits another to make use of the property, of which the legal possession continues with the owner, it was a license and if under the document a party gets exclusive possession of the property, prima facie, he was considered to be a tenant; but circumstances may be established which negative the intention to create a lease - In the instant case, appellant was in possession of the buildings in question since 1958 - They have been permitted to raise huge constructions and the nature of construction was of wide range - Appellant was in exclusive possession over the impugned land along with construction thereon without any let or hindrance from the Administration - Moreover, the appellant had been continuously carrying on their business without any interference from any quarter whatsoever since 1962 - Hence, it was the appellant to show that despite the right to possess the demised premises exclusive, a right or interest in the property had not been created - The aforesaid burden was not discharged by the appellant - Hence, agreement in question constitutes lease in favour of the appellant and accordingly, appellant liable to pay taxes - Appeal dismissed.
