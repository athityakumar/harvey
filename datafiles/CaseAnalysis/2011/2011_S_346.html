Rajender Singh Pathania and others v State of (NCT of Delhi) and others

Supreme Court of India

12 August 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 543; (2011) 13 SCC 329; 2011 ALL MR (Cri) 3287; 2012 CRLJ 609; JT 2011 (10) SC 294; 2011 (4) MLJ(Crl) 1028; 2011 (2) OLR 797; 2011 (3) RCR(Criminal) 827; 2011(9) SCALE 124; [2011] 10 S.C.R. 260

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 107 and 151 - Appellant nos. 2 to 4 (constables) while patrolling in the area found that respondent nos.3 and 4 were fighting with each other in an intoxicated condition - Respondents were taken to the hospital for medical examination wherein they misbehaved with the Doctor and other staff of the hospital - Respondents were charged u/ss. 107 r/w 151 of the CrPC and were produced before the Special Executive Magistrate (Magistrate) - Magistrate had passed the release order of the said respondents, however, they could not be released because they failed to furnish the personal bond with a surety in the like amount - However, on the next day, they submitted the bail bonds voluntarily for a sum of Rs.15,000 each, and thus, they were released - Said respondents filed writ petition for quashing of the proceedings u/ss. 107 r/w 151 of the CrPC and further asked to initiate criminal proceedings against the appellant nos.2 to 4 and award them compensation for illegal detention - HC quashed the criminal proceedings u/ss. 107 r/w 151 of the CrPC; awarded a token compensation and further directed the Central Bureau of Investigation (CBI) to investigate the cases against the police officials who had allegedly misused their powers, and directed the police administration to initiate proceedings against such officials - Hence, instant appeals - (A) Appellants submitted that they had not been served personal notices and had no opportunity to defend themselves and order impugned was passed in violation of the principle of natural justice - Held, there was nothing on record to show that the standing counsel had any communication with appellants and the Magistrate - Thus, none of them had an opportunity of appearing before the HC - Hence, judgment and order impugned was passed in violation of the principles of natural justice and, thus, liable to be set aside - (B) Investigation by CBI - Whether HC was correct in directing CBI to investigate? - Held, in the instant case, the grievance of the respondents had been against the two Constables and one Head Constable - It was not a case where it could be held that the State authorities were interested or involved in the incident - Thus, it was not a fit case where investigation could be handed over to the CBI - (C) Award of compensation - Violation of fundamental rights of a person - Held, though the HCs and SC in exercise of their jurisdictions u/arts. 226 and 32 could award compensation for such violations but such a power should not be lightly exercised - Before awarding any compensation there must be a proper enquiry on the question of facts alleged in the complaint - Court may examine the report and determine the issue after giving opportunity of filing objections to rebut the same and hearing to the other side - Awarding of compensation was permissible in case the court reaches the same conclusion on a re-appreciation of the evidence adduced at the enquiry - In the instant case, HC erred in awarding even token compensation to the tune of Rs.25,000 as the HC did not hold any enquiry and passed the order merely after considering the status report submitted by the appellant no.1 without hearing any of the persons against whom allegations of abuse of power had been made - Impugned order set aside - Appeals allowed.
