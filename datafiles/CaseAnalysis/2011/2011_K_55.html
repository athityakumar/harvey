Visveswaraya Technological University and another v Krishnendu Halder and another

Supreme Court of India

18 February 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 191; (2011) 4 SCC 606; AIR 2011 SC 1429; 2011 (3) AWC 2945; JT 2011 (3) SC 187; 2011 (4) MLJ(SC) 878; 2011(3) SCALE 359; [2011] 2 S.C.R. 1007; 2011 (6) SLR 1

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Education

Keywords: Visveswaraya Technological University Act, 1994, Karnataka Selection of Candidates for Admission to Government Seats in Professional Educational Institution Rules, 2006, AICTE Regulations

Summary: Education - Constitution - Visveswaraya Technological University Act, 1994, s. 20(1) - Admission in engineering courses - Eligibility criteria - Respondents secured marks which were more than what was prescribed by All India Council for Technical Education (AICTE) norms, but less than what was prescribed by the University Regulations - Respondents were admitted to the Bachelor of Engineering Course but when the list of admissions were submitted by the said colleges to the University for approval of admissions, the University refused to approve their admissions on the ground that they had secured less than the minimum percentage required for being eligible to admissions - Respondents filed writ petition against said order - Single Judge dismissed said writ petitions - On appeal, DB directed the university to approve the admissions of the respondents as they fulfilled eligibility criteria fixed by AICTA - Hence, instant appeal - Whether the eligibility criteria for admission to the Engineering courses stipulated under the Statutory Rules and Regulations of the State Government/University could be relaxed or ignored, and candidates who do not meet with such eligibility criteria can be given admission, on the ground that such candidates possess the minimum eligibility prescribed under the norms of the central body (AICTE)? - Held, no - University fixed a marginally higher eligibility criteria, that was 40% for candidates belonging to schedule castes and schedule tribes and 45% for others, as against 35% and 40% respectively suggested by the AICTE norms - Therefore, it cannot be said that the higher standards fixed by the State or University were abnormally high or unattainable by normal students - Hence, a student whose marks fall short of the eligibility criteria fixed by the State/University, or any college which admits such students directly, cannot contend that the admission of students found qualified under the criteria fixed by AICTE, should be approved even if they did not fulfil the higher eligibility criteria fixed by the State/University - Impugned order of the DB Set aside - Appeal allowed.
