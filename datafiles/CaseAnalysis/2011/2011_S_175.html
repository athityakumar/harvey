Yomeshbhai Pranshankar Bhatt v State Of Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

19 May 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Ganguly, Deepak Verma

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 370; (2011) 6 SCC 312; AIR 2011 SC 2328; 2011 CRLJ 3125; 2011 (3) G.L.R. 1833; JT 2011 (6) SC 591; 2011 (4) MLJ(Crl) 726; 2011 (4) RCR(Criminal) 7; 2011(6) SCALE 346; [2011] 6 S.C.R. 958

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Murder - Conviction - Challenged - Instant appeal against the finding of both the courts below convicting the appellant u/s. 302 of IPC - Case was entirely based on circumstantial evidence and the statement of the deceased in three dying declarations - First dying declaration was recorded when PW 2 took the deceased to hospital wherein the doctor said that the deceased was fully conscious and had informed the doctor that the appellant had sprinkled kerosene on her at her residence - Second was recorded by PSI and the third by the Executive Magistrate - Whether the appellant was guilty for commission of an offence under any of the parts of s. 304 of the IPC and not u/s. 302 of IPC? - Held, in a case relating to circumstantial evidence, the Court should see the circumstances very carefully before arriving at a finding of guilt of the person concerned and yet if there was any doubt which was inconsistent with the innocence of the accused, the benefit should go to the accused - In the instant case, appellant had no pre-meditation to kill the deceased or cause any bodily harm or injury to the deceased - Everything happened on the spur of the moment - Appellant must have lost self-control on some provocative utterances of the deceased - However, the fact that kerosene was sprinkled on the deceased by the appellant possibly could not be disputed, in view of concurrent finding by both the courts and having regard to the materials on record - Hence, having regard to the facts and circumstances of the case and in the light of defence of the deceased, that the case falls u/s. 304 Part II of IPC and the appellant had already suffered imprisonment for 11 years 2 months, sentence already undergone by the appellant was sufficient u/s. 304 Part II of IPC - Appeal disposed of.
