Amitava Banerjee @ Amit @ Bappa Banerjee v State of West Bengal

Supreme Court of India

17 August 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, V.S. Sirpurkar

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 587; (2011) 12 SCC 554; 2012 CRLJ 390; JT 2011 (9) SC 371; 2011(9) SCALE 185; [2011] 12 S.C.R. 160

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 161, 164 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 201, 302, 364 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant was alleged that he killed deceased - Accused was presented before Trail Court - Trail Court found appellant guilty and convicted him u/s. 302IPC - Appellant preferred an appeal before HC which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether decision by lower Courts was sustainable - Held, circumstances established left no manner of doubt, that crime was committed by appellant - Deceased having been last seen with appellant around time he was killed was a circumstance which together with other circumstances proved case and are explainable only on one hypothesis that appellant was guilty of killing deceased - Further, fact that appellant had borrowed spade, tide it with 'Sutil' after wrapping wooden part with newspaper was fully established by statement of PW6 - However, deceased and appellant were both wards of police officials and there was no room for favouring one over other - Thus, there was neither any illegality, nor any miscarriage of justice - Further, Court took into consideration all circumstances incriminating appellant and also evidence of police officials - Thus, there was neither any illegality, nor any miscarriage of justice in judgments and orders under appeal to call for our interference - Appeal dismissed.
