The Joint Action Committee Of Airlines Pilots Associations Of India & Ors. v The Director General Of Civil Aviation & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

03 May 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 302; (2011) 5 SCC 435; AIR 2011 SC 2220; 2011 (3) Bom.C.R. 511; JT 2011 (6) SC 24; 2011 (5) RCR(Civil) 558; 2011(5) SCALE 284; [2011] 5 S.C.R. 1019

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Air Law; Carriers & Transportation

Keywords: Statutory Authority, Business Rules, 1961, Indian Audit and Accounts Department (Administrative Officers, Accounts Officers and Audit Officers) Recruitment Rules, 1964

Summary: Air Law - Administrative - Carriers & Transportation - Aircraft Act, 1934 - Aircraft Rules, 1937, rr. 133A, 29C - Civil Aviation Requirements (CAR) - Suspension - Legality - 1st respondent Director General of Civil Aviation (DGCA) issued CAR 2007 fixing rest period of pilots, which substantially provided greater benefits to them - Various airlines made large number of representations to 1st respondent and Central Government to the effect that it was practically not possible for them to ensure compliance of CAR 2007 - Respondents, by impugned circular dated 29-5-2008, kept CAR 2007 in abeyance and by subsequent order, revived AIC 28/92 (an earlier CAR) - Appellant (Association of pilots) challenged the validity of impugned circular before HC - HC dismissed appellant's writ petition - Appellant contended that AIC 28/92 could not be revived when CAR 2007 kept in abeyance and respondent authority was not competent to pass impugned order and there was a flagrant violation of principles of natural justice - Held, CAR 2007 was an executive instructions which could be termed as 'special directions' - Executive instructions which were issued for guidance and to implement scheme of the Act and did not have force of law, could be issued by competent authority and altered, replaced and substituted at any time - It was not a case of automatic revival of AIC 28/92, but there was a specific order by competent authority in exercise of statutory powers whereby AIC 28/92 had been revived - Since instructions which had been issued for revival of AIC 28/92 were merely in form of interim measures, question of applicability of principles of natural justice did not arise - Suspension of CAR 2007 had created a vacuum, and it was, therefore, necessary for 1st respondent to take an appropriate decision during finalisation of CAR, pursuant to report to be submitted by a Committee constituted by Government - Appellants did not challenge revival order, by virtue of which AIC 28/92 came into force which had also been nothing but special directions and remained in force from 1992 to 2007 - Impugned circular was neither issued illegally nor without any authority - DGCA was competent to issue special directions and impugned circular had been issued by him, though may be with consultation of some other authorities - Authority which had been in consultation with DGCA had been provided for under business rules and it should not be held by any stretch of imagination that Ministry of Civil Aviation was not an authority concerned with safety measures involved therein - Further, whole exercise had been done to bring a new CAR into existence for which process had already been initiated and a draft CAR was put on DGCA website giving opportunity to all concerned to submit their objections/suggestions within a period of 30 days and a new CAR was likely to come into existence very soon - HC held that DGCA was directly under control of Civil Aviation Ministry and considering rules of business, Government being appropriate authority to formulate necessary policy in relation to subject matter in issue, and Government in its wisdom having decided after taking into consideration all representations made from various sections, had appointed a Committee to formulate CAR in relation to matters in question and on that count, DGCA in exercise of its power u/r. 133A r/w r. 29C of Rules issued impugned circular, and therefore, no fault could be found with the same - Findings recorded by HC was therefore upheld - No merit in appeal - Appeal dismissed.
