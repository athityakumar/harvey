Khatri Hotels Private Limited and another v Union of India and another

Supreme Court of India

09 September 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, H.L. Dattu

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 645; (2011) 9 SCC 126; 2012 (2) ALD(SC) 9; 2011 (182) DLT 597; JT 2011 (10) SC 541; 2011 (8) MLJ(SC) 369; 2012 (1) RCR(Civil) 676; 2011(10) SCALE 190; [2011] 15 S.C.R. 299

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Land & Property - Administrative - Municipalities & Local Governments - Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, o.II, r.2 - Delhi Municipal Corporation Act, 1957, s. 507(a) - Delhi Land Reforms Act, 1954, s. 150(3) - Delhi Development Act, 1957, s. 22(1) - - Title - Encroachment - Justifiability - Suit land belonged to Gaon Sabha of village and formed part of revenue estate of that village - By notification dt. 28-5-1966 issued u/s. 507(a) of the 1957 Act, with previous approval of Central Govt. declared that localities mentioned in Schedule forming part of rural areas should ceased to be rural areas - By virtue of s. 150(3) of the 1954, Act suit land stood automatically vested in Central Govt. - After 8 years same was transferred by Central Govt. to Delhi Development Authority (DDA) - DDA vide notification dt. 20-8-1974 issued u/s. 22(1) of the 1957 Act for purpose of development and maintenance as Green - Appellant No. 2 and his brothers claimed that they had purchased suit land comprised in khasra Nos.2728/1674/2 and 2728/1674/3 through registered sale deed and encroached upon suit land, raised construction and started restaurant named as Sahara Restaurant - Defendants / Officials of Corporation and DDA came to suit premises along with Tahsildar on 10-8-1990 without serving any notice and threatened to demolish superstructure - Aggrieved, appellant No.2 filed suit before HC for grant of permanent injunction against Corporation and DDA - HC granted interim injunction and suit was transferred to District Judge for disposal - Which was dismissed by observing that appellant had failed to prove that he and his brothers were owners of suit land and also held that appellant was not entitled to relief of injunction because suit filed for determination of title of disputed land was pending adjudication - Aggrieved, appellant challenged Trial Courts order before HC which was dismissed and upheld Trial Court's order - Hence, instant appeal - Whether lower courts judgment and order was sustainable - Held, further HC did not examine issue in detail as was done by Trial Court- Single Judge relied on Rajinder Kakkar v. Delhi Development Authority 1993 Indlaw DEL 463 held that by virtue of s. 150(3) of the 1954 Act suit land was automatically vested in Central Govt. and same was transferred to DDA u/s. 22(1) of the 1957, DD Act - Further conclusion recorded by Trial Court that appellants had failed to prove that suit land formed part of khasra Nos. 2728/1674/2 and 2728/1674/3 did not suffer from any error because they did not adduce any evidence to establish that suit land on which restaurant was being run formed part of those khasra numbers - Therefore, approved findings and conclusions recorded by Trial Court that appellants had not approached Court with clean hands inasmuch as they withheld Aks Sijra, site plan and demarcation report and award - Not only this, appellants raised illegal construction despite of injunction order passed by HC and that too without obtaining permission from competent authority - Court did not considered that suit was filed by appellants was barred by o.II, r.2 CPC - Hence, stated that appellants had not only made encroachment on public land but also abused process of Court were saddled with cost which quantified Rs. 5 lacs - Appellants were directed to be deposited Rs. 2.5 lacs with SC Legal Services Committee and balance Rs. 2.5 lacs to be deposited with Delhi State Legal Services Committee within 2 months failing which Secretaries of two Legal Services Committees should be entitled to recover same as arrears of land revenue - Appeal dismissed.
