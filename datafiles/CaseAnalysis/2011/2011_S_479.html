Dr. Shehla Burney Andothers v Syed Ali Mossa Raza (Dead) By Lrs. & Ors

Supreme Court of India

21 April 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Ganguly, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 286; (2011) 6 SCC 529; 2011 (4) ALD(SC) 101; 2011 (86) ALR 716; 2011 (4) AWC 3624; 2011 (2) CLT(SC) 267; JT 2011 (5) SC 137; 2011 (4) MPLJ 40; 2011 (164) PLR 127; 2011 (3) RCR(Civil) 345; 2011 (113) RD 456; 2011(4) SCALE 838; [2011] 5 S.C.R. 841

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 100 - Title by adverse possession - Entitlement - Plaintiff filed suit for recovery of possession against defendants - Defendants contended that they acquired title by adverse possession of suit property since their predecessor-in-title had been in possession even before filing the instant suit - Trial Court dismissed suit - Single Judge, in appeal, held that plaintiffs were entitled to decree of possession - Defendants filed Letters Patent Appeal that was dismissed by DB as non maintainable -Whether instant suit was liable to be dismissed - Held, no prayer for decree of possession was either in original plaint or amended plaint against original 2nd defendant - In a case where prayer was not made against a particular defendant, no relief possibly could be granted against him - Since no relief having been claimed against 2nd defendant, who was predecessor-in-title of defendants, no relief could be granted against defendants - Defendants had been in peaceful possession of disputed property till property was transferred by her to predecessor-in-title of defendants - After such transfer construction started on property and defendants had been residing there since 1964 and suit came to be filed only in 1975 - Judgment of HC, therefore, was set aside and that of Trial Court was affirmed - Appeal allowed.
