Jamaluddin v State of Jammu and Kashmir and others

Supreme Court of India

29 September 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Gokhale, J.M. Panchal

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 639; (2011) 14 SCC 725; AIR 2012 SC 291; JT 2011 (11) SC 333; 2011(11) SCALE 226; [2011] 11 S.C.R. 690; 2012 (5) SLR 572

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Service

Keywords: Constitution of Jammu and Kashmir, Jammu and Kashmir Civil Services (Classification, Control and Appeal) Rules, 1956, Jammu and Kashmir Schedule Castes and Backward Classes Reservation Rules 1970, Jammu and Kashmir Civil Services (Judicial) Recruitment Rules 1967, Jammu and Kashmir Higher Judicial Service Rules 1983, Civil Services (CC & A) Rules of 1956

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Service - Jammu and Kashmir Civil Services (Judicial) Recruitment Rules 1967 - Appointment of Munsif - Relaxation of age - Entitlement - Appellant, adhoc Munsif in the Jammu and Kashmir Judicial Service, belonged to a Scheduled Tribe, applied for the post of Munsif in the Scheduled Tribe category when a notification was issued by the Jammu and Kashmir Public Service Commission for the regular appointments - Notification required the person to be of not more than thirty five years of age as on the 1st January of the year in which the notification was issued - In view thereof, the Commission informed appellant by communication dated 21-5-2002 that his application was rejected since he was overage by eleven months - Appellant filed writ petition challenging the notification - Single Judge dismissed the writ petition holding that by the time the matter was heard, the appellant had crossed the age of 37 years which he claimed as the permissible age for the Scheduled Tribe candidates - DB heard the Letters Patent Appeal also accepted the view taken by the Single Judge, and therefore dismissed the appeal - Whether the Full Court resolution passed by the HC in February 1982 relaxing the age of Scheduled Tribe candidate for Munsif post would be applicable in the instant case - Held, advertisement of the Public Service Commission issued in the year 2002, required the persons concerned to be of less than thirty five years of age at the relevant time and that age limit applied to all the candidates - No age relaxation was specified in favour of the candidates belonging to the Scheduled Castes or Scheduled Tribes, though there was a quantum of reservation provided for them - Resolution of the Full Court of the HC passed in February 1982, would therefore, have to be read as providing only for the quantum and not for any age relaxation - If there was no age relaxation in the rules, the same should not be brought in by any judicial interpretation - In the circumstances, no error was found in the judgment of the Single Judge or that of the DB - Although, it was not inclined to interfere with the order passed by the High Court on the judicial side, the HC should on its administrative side should examine the issue as to whether age relaxation should be provided to the candidates belonging to Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribes and Other Backward Classes appearing for the Judicial Service Examination at the Munsif level as was provided to the candidates appearing for the Higher Judicial Service Examination - Appeal dismissed.
