A. Shankar v State Of Karnataka

Supreme Court of India

09 June 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, Swatanter Kumar

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 380; (2011) 6 SCC 279; AIR 2011 SC 2302; 2011 ALL MR (Cri) 2357; JT 2011 (6) SC 659; 2011 (5) KarLJ 439; 2012 (1) MLJ(Crl) 529; 2011(6) SCALE 478; [2011] 6 S.C.R. 999

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Supreme Court (Enlargement of Criminal Appellate Jurisdiction) Act, 1970

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302 and 307 - Appellant was put to trial u/ss. 302 and 307 of IPC - Trial Court held that prosecution failed to prove beyond reasonable doubt that the appellant had murdered the deceased or made an attempt to kill the complainant - Appellant was acquitted of the charges u/ss. 302 and 307 of IPC - On appeal, HC convicted the appellant u/s. 302 IPC for committing the murder and also u/s. 324 of IPC for causing injuries to the complainant - Hence, instant appeal - Whether HC committed an error in interfering with judgment of acquittal by the Trial Court and relying upon the evidence on record while ignoring the material inconsistencies between the evidence of the witnesses; and medical and ocular evidence? - Held, as per the medical evidence, injuries found on the person of deceased had been fatal and could be caused with a knife or scissors - However, complainant was not sure as to whether injuries were caused by knife or scissors - No explanation was given as to whether the complainant was capable of understanding the distinction between knife and scissors - Further, non-production of the FSL report in the court by the prosecution was fatal as in absence thereof it was difficult for the court to reach the conclusion whether the offence was committed with scissors - Hence, HC committed an error in recording the finding of fact that the prosecution succeeded in proving the case beyond reasonable doubt - HC failed to meet the grounds pointed out by the Trial Court discarding the case of prosecution and thus, the findings of fact recorded by the HC remain perverse - Appeal allowed.
