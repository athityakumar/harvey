A. B. Bhaskara Rao v Inspector of Police, CBI Visakhapatnam

Supreme Court of India

23 September 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 675; (2011) 10 SCC 259; 2012 (2) CalLT 77; JT 2011 (11) SC 498; 2012 (3) MLJ(Crl) 403; 2011 (4) RCR(Criminal) 290; 2011(10) SCALE 740; [2011] 12 S.C.R. 718

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Illegal Gratification

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Constitution - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 12, 13(1)(d), 13(1)(d)(ii), 13(2), 7, 7(2) - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 482 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 142 - Illegal Gratification - Conviction - Sustainability -Appellant/accused allegedly demanded and accepted a sum of Rs.200/- as illegal gratification from complainant with regard to release of transfer order - Trial Court convicted appellant for an offence punishable u/ss. 7, 13(1)(d)(ii), r/w s. 13(2) of the Act and sentenced him to undergo 6 months RI - On appeal, HC dismissed appeal confirming conviction and sentence of Trial Court - Hence, instant appeal - Appellant contended that alleged incident took place on 14-11-1997 and 14 years had elapsed since then, amount of Rs. 200/- said to have been received by appellant was trivial in nature and also of fact that due to said conviction and sentence he lost his job, leniency might be shown and sentence be reduced to period already undergone - Held, long delay in disposal of appeal or any other factor might not be a ground for reduction of sentence, particularly, when statute prescribes minimum sentence - In other cases where no such minimum sentence was prescribed, it was open to Court to consider delay and its effect and ultimate decision - However, in a case of corruption by public servant, quantum of amount was immaterial and depends on conduct of delinquent and proof regarding demand and acceptance established by prosecution - Though jurisdiction of SC, u/art. 142 of Constitution was not in dispute, it was clear that exercise of such power would, however, depend on facts and circumstances of each case - HC, in exercise of its jurisdiction, u/s. 482 CrPC, u/art. 142 of Constitution, would not ordinarily direct quashing of a case involving crime against society particularly, when both Trial Court as also HC had found that charge leveled against appellant under the Act had been made out and proved by prosecution by placing acceptable evidence - Hence, impugned order of Courts below were upheld - Appeal dismissed.
