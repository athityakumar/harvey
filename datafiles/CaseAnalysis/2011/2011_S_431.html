State of Uttar Pradesh and others v Mohan Meakin Breweries Limited and another

Supreme Court of India

23 September 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 692; (2011) 13 SCC 588; AIR 2012 SC 2674; 2012 (1) AWC 891; [2011] 14 S.C.R. 98

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Beverage, Uttar Pradesh Excise Act, 1910, Punjab Breweries Rules, 1932, U.P. Brewery Rules, 1961, U.P. Bottling of Foreign Liquor Rules, 1969

Summary: Indirect Tax - Excise - Uttar Pradesh Excise Act, 1910, ss. 17(1)(d),18(c), 19, 28, 28A,29(e)(i), 31 - Punjab Excise Act, 1914, s. 32 - Income tax Act, 1961, ss. 44AC, 206C - Uttar Pradesh Brewery Rules, 1961, rr. 37, 41, 53- Uttar Pradesh Bottling of Foreign Liquor Rules, 1969, rr.7, 7(11), 7(11)(a) - Manufacturing wastage - Levy of Tax - Validity - Respondent manufactures beer and bottling beer in bond - Excise Inspector(EI)would check stock of beer and submits report to Excise Commissioner(EC) if deficiency exceeded 9% - EI found excess deficiency and reported to EC -EC issued show cause notice and as explanation of respondents was not satisfactory issued orders with regard to excess 'manufacturing wastage' during September 1963 to March 1973 and levied and demanded in all Rs.81,94,310/- as excise duty and an equal amount as additional duty in regard to the deficiency in excess of 9% of the total stock of beer - Revision was filed by respondent no.1 to State Govt. which by order dt.12-4-1978 dismissed revision and upheld EC orders - Aggrieved, respondent no.1 filed writ in HC and DB allowed writ petition with other connected petitions by common order dt.15-3-2002 - Hence instant appeal by State Govt. -At what stage beer manufactured was exigible to duty and procedure adopted by appellants for ascertaining excess manufacturing wastage was proper - Held, process of brewing beer involves malting, mashing, boiling, fermentation, separation of yeast from beer, ageing and finishing - Beer comes into existence on completion of process of fermentation - Filtration, ageing and finishing were processes to remove impurities, improve clarity, taste and increase shelf life- levy or impost of excise duty would be only on alcoholic liquor ready for human consumption - Thus, final product of beer was exigible to duty when beer was received in bottling tank - State had legislative competence to levy excise duty on beer either after completion of process of fermentation and filtration, or after fermentation - Standard procedure to levy excise duty was at time when beer would be removed from brewery - S. 28A of the 1910 Act and r. 53 of the 1961 Rules and r. 7 of the 1969 Rules had levied double amount of excise duty only in those months when difference exceeded 9% and quantity in excess to 9% would be treated as excess wastage and excise duty and an equal amount as additional duty would be charged for such excess - Procedure followed by appellant to determine excess manufacturing wastage was in accordance to law - Sardar Baldev Singh v Commissioner of Income Tax, Delhi and Ajmer 1960 Indlaw SC 336 was relied - Appeals by Breweries regarding imposition of duty and additional duty on excess bottling wastage - S. 28A of the 1910 Act provides allowance of 10% to cover losses due to evaporation, sullage and other contingencies within brewery and also covered losses in bottling and storage - r.53 of 1961 Rules was amended on 19-7-1975 and provided allowance of 9% of total stock of beer in month to cover losses due to evaporation, sullage and other contingencies within the brewery - r.7(11)(a) of the 1969 Rules provided allowance up to 1% of total stock of spirit during month for actual loss in bottling and storage - Neither r.53 of 1961 Rules nor r. 7(11) of 1969 Rules was under challenge- Breweries having obtained bottling licence subject to special conditions in r. 7(11) of the Rules could not ignore said rule - r.53 of 1961 Rules and 7(11) of 1969 Rules when read conjointly showed they were supplementary to each other and together implement s. 28A of the 1910 Act - Appeals disposed of.
