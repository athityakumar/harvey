M/S. Grasim Industries Ltd v Union Of India

Supreme Court of India

13 October 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Dattu, Chandramauli Kumar Prasad

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 747; (2011) 10 SCC 653; AIR 2012 SC 161; 2011 (273) E.L.T. 10; [2011] 12 S.C.R. 1013

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Manufacturing Process

Summary: Indirect Tax - Excise - Central Excise Act, 1944, ss. 11A and 11 AC - Central Excise Rules, 1944, r. 173 Q (1) (a) - Metal scrap - Excise duty - Penalty - Sustainability - Assessee, manufacturer of white cement, repaired worn out machineries or parts of cement manufacturing plant in which process certain metal scrap or waste was generated - In inspection conducted by officials it was found that assessee had cleared various types of metal scrap and waste without payment of excise duty - Show cause notice was issued to assessee demanding duty u/ s. 11A of the Act along with equal amount of penalty u/s. 11AC of the Act - Penalty u/ r. 173 Q of the Rules, for non-payment of excise duty on clearance of said metal scrap and waste was also imposed - Assessee filed appeal before Commissioner (Appeals) which set aside demand of duty along with equal amount of penalty pertaining to scrap and waste arising out of dismantling of used capital goods and packing materials and demand of penalty u/ r. 173Q(1)(a) of the Rules, and upheld demand of duty and equal amount of penalty u/s. 11AC of the Act - On appeal, Tribunal set aside demand of duty and penalty confirmed by Commissioner (Appeals) - Revenue, aggrieved by this Order, filed appeal u/s. 35G of the Act before HC - HC set aside Order of Tribunal and restored Order of Commissioner (Appeals) - Hence, instant appeal by assessee u/s. 35L of the Act - Whether metal scrap or waste generated whilst repairing of worn out machineries or parts of cement manufacturing plant amounts to manufacture and is thereby excisable to excise duty - Held, process of repair and maintenance of machinery of cement manufacturing plant, in which scrap and Iron scrap arose, had no contribution or effect on process of manufacturing of cement, which was excisable end product and since welding electrodes, mild steel, cutting tools etc. which were used in process of repair and maintenance were not raw material used in process of manufacturing of cement, which end product, issue of getting new identity as M.S. Scrap and Iron Scrap as an end product due to manufacturing process did not arise - Repairing activity in any possible manner could not be part of manufacturing activity in relation to production of end product - Thus, M.S. scrap and Iron scrap could not be said to be by-product of final product - Therefore, impugned Judgment and Order of HC was set aside and Order of Tribunal was restored - Appeal allowed.
