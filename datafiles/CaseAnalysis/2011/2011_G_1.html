Royal Orchid Hotels Limited and another v G. Jayarama Reddy and others

Supreme Court of India

29 September 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 684; (2011) 10 SCC 608; 2011 (9) ADJ 505; 2012 (1) AWC 916; 2012 (1) CalLT 66; 2011 (4) CLT(SC) 131; JT 2011 (11) SC 346; 2011 (4) RCR(Civil) 613; 2011(11) SCALE 239; [2011] 11 S.C.R. 701

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4(1), 6 - Acquired land for public purpose - Transferred to private individuals - Sustainability - On requisition sent by Corporation, State Govt. issued notification u/s. 4(1) of the Act for acquisition of 39 acres 27 guntas land comprised in various survey numbers including Survey No. 122 and makes it clear that public purpose for which land was sought to be acquired was to establish Golf-cum-Hotel Resort by Corporation - However instead of utilizing acquired land for purpose specified in notification, Corporation transferred same to private parties and agreement was executed in favour of respondent no. 5 conveying him 12 acres 34 guntas of acquired land likewise 6 acres 8 guntas land was transferred to Bangalore International Centre and 5 acres including 2 acres 30 guntas land belonging to respondent no. 1 and his brothers was leased out to 'X' - Respondent no. 5 filed petitions questioning acquisitions proceedings before Single Judge, which was dismissed on ground of delay but DB reversed order of Single Judge and quashed acquisition proceedings qua land of appellants in those cases - DB relied on in Industrial Development & Investment Company Private Limited v. State of Maharashtra 1988 Indlaw MUM 327 - DB further held, appellants were entitled to return of land subject to condition of deposit of amount of compensation together with interest at rate of 12% per annum - Hence, instant appeals - Appellants contended that petition was highly belated and that having compensation determined by Special Land Acquisition Officer, respondent no. 1 would be deemed to have waived his right to challenge acquisition proceedings - Whether HC was justified in directing restoration of land to respondent no. 1 - Held, respondent no. 1 had not only prayed for quashing of acquisition proceedings, but also prayed for restoration of acquired land on ground that instead of using same for public purpose specified in notifications issued u/ss. 4(1), 6 of the Act Corporation had transferred same to private persons - Respondent no. 1 and other landowners should not be having any serious objection to acquisition of their land for public purpose and therefore some of them not only accepted compensation - However, it was discovered that acquired land had been transferred to private persons, they sought intervention of Court and in 3 cases DB nullified acquisition on ground of fraud and misuse of provisions of the Act - Hence, in Om Parkash v. Union of India 2010 Indlaw SC 86 relied on that respondent no. 1 independently questioned acquisition proceedings and succeeded in convincing DB that action taken by Corporation to transfer his land was wholly illegal, arbitrary and unjustified - Appeals dismissed.
