Bharat Sanchar Nigam Limited v Ghanshyam Dass and another

Supreme Court of India

17 February 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Patnaik, R.V. Raveendran, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 105; (2011) 4 SCC 374; 2011 (8) ADJ 5; 2011 (3) AWC 3027; 2011 (2) ESC 200; 2011(2) SCALE 479; [2011] 4 S.C.R. 380; 2012 (4) SLR 711; 2011 (1) UPLBEC 617

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Seniority based on basic grade - Promotion - Entitlement - Appellant organization introduced Biennial Cadre Review (BCR) Scheme to those employees, who were on regular service as on 1-1-1990 and had completed 26 years of satisfactory service in the basic grades, were to be screened by a duly constituted Committee to assess their performance and determine their suitability for advancement and if they were found suitable they were to be upgraded in the higher scale - Some officers of Grade III, who were senior in the basic grade but had lost their seniority in Grade III because of their later promotions and who were not considered for up-gradation to Grade IV under the BCR Scheme, filed petitions before the Tribunal - Tribunal in its order dated 7-7-1992 directed that promotions of 10% posts in Grade IV scale would have to be based on seniority in the basic grade subject to fulfilment of other conditions in the BCR Scheme - Appellant challenged the Tribunal order before the SC - SC upheld the Tribunal order - Respondents subsequently filed applications before the Tribunal contending that employees who were juniors to them in the basic grade but otherwise senior in Grade-III, had been given promotion to Grade-IV earlier to the dates when the respondents were given such promotion - Tribunal allowed the applications and directed the appellant to consider promoting respondents to Grade IV with effect from the dates their immediate juniors in the basic grade seniority were so promoted - Appellant challenged the Tribunal order before the HC - HC dismissed writ petitions holding that the Tribunal, while allowing the applications, had directed the Government to follow its own circular dated 13-12-1995 which had been issued pursuant to the order of the Tribunal dated 7-7-1992 which had attained finality after dismissal of the appeals by the SC - Whether the respondents entitle to the promotions based on the seniority in basic grade - Held, directions in Tribunal order dated 7-7-1992 clearly stated that the appellant was directed to consider only those applicants who were the parties to applications before the Tribunal for promotion to 10% in Grade-IV scale on the basis of seniority in the basic cadres from the due dates with consequential benefits - Respondents were not the applicants in that case before the Tribunal and there was no direction to the appellant to consider the respondents in the two appeals for promotion to Grade-IV scale on the basis of seniority in the basic cadre as per the BCR Scheme - Hence, the respondents were not entitled to claim any promotion to Grade-IV on the basis of their seniority in the basic grade on the basis of the Tribunal order dated 7-7-1992 as affirmed by the SC in appeal - Further, language of the circular dated 13-12-1995 made it crystal clear that the Government took a fresh decision in supersession of earlier instructions that promotion to Grade-IV should be given from amongst officials in Grade-III on the basis of their seniority in the basic grade - Hence, the decision of the Government to make promotions to Grade-IV on the basis of their seniority in the basic grade could take effect only from 13-12-1995 and not from a prior date and the respondents could not claim any promotion to Grade-IV on the basis of their seniority in the basic cadre with effect from any date prior to 13-12-1995 - Tribunal was, therefore, not right in allowing respondent's applications directing the Government to consider promoting the applicants to Grade-IV with effect from the dates their immediate juniors in the basic grade seniority were so promoted subject to their being found fit with consequential benefits of seniority - Further, HC ought to have interfered with the decision of the Tribunal and hence the impugned orders of the HC and Tribunals were set aside - Appeal allowed.
