R.S. Mishra v State Of Orissa & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

01 February 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Gokhale, J.M. Panchal

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 71; (2011) 2 SCC 689; AIR 2011 SC 1103; 2011 CRLJ 1654; JT 2011 (2) SC 291; 2011 (3) MLJ(Crl) 741; 2011 (1) RCR(Criminal) 872; 2011(2) SCALE 98; [2011] 2 S.C.R. 338

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 227 and 228 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Adverse remarks - Expunging of - Appellant, a retired Additional Sessions Judge, filed an appeal challenging judgment and order passed by HC in suo motu criminal revision arising out of Session Trial to the extent the HC had made certain observations against the appellant who had decided that session case - These remarks were made on account of the appellant not framing the charge u/s. 302 of the IPC against the accused in that case, when the material on record warranted framing of that charge - The impugned order also led to denial of the selection grade to appellant - Whether HC was justified in making these observations against the appellant? - Held, while discharging an accused, the Judge concerned had to consider the record of the case and the documents placed therewith, and if he was so convinced after hearing both the parties that there was no sufficient ground to proceed against the accused, he shall discharge the accused, but he had to record his reasons for doing the same - Charge was to be framed 'after such consideration' and therefore, that consideration must be reflected in the order - Hence, the Judge was to look into the material placed before him and if he was of the view that no case was made out for framing of a charge, the order ought to be clear and self-explanatory with respect to the material placed before him - In the instant case, the appellant only stated in his order that on consideration of the material available in the case diary, he had found that there was no sufficient material to frame the charge u/s. 302 of IPC - Appellant had not stated in his order as to why he was of the opinion that the material available in the case diary was insufficient - Order did not explain as to why a charge u/s. 304 was being preferred to one u/s. 302 IPC - However, as per the material on record there was a prima facie case to proceed to frame the charge u/s. 302 of IPC - Hence, the reason given for dropping the charge u/s. 302 was totally inadequate and untenable, and showed a non-application of mind by the appellant to the statements in the charge-sheet and the medical record - There was no reason to interfere in the impugned order making certain observations and suggestions which were necessary in the facts and circumstances of the case - Appeal dismissed.
