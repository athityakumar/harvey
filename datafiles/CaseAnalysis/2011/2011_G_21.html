Union Of India v Giani

Supreme Court of India

17 February 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Mukundakam Sharma, A.R. Dave

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 127; (2011) 11 SCC 480; AIR 2011 SC 977; 2011 (85) ALR 739; 2011 (2) AWC 1903; 2011 (1) CLT 215; JT 2011 (2) SC 479; 2011 (3) MLJ(SC) 1006; 2011 (3) RCR(Civil) 341; 2011 (113) RD 350; 2011(2) SCALE 500; [2011] 2 S.C.R. 978; 2011 (1) UPLBEC 593

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4, 6 and 23(1A) - Benefit of s. 23(1A) - Entitlement to - Acquisition proceeding commenced with the notification u/s. 4 issued on 06.03.1965 - Award was passed by the Collector on 09.07.1980 - Meanwhile, s. 23(1A) of the Act was inserted, w.e.f., 24.9.1984, by way of amendment to the Act which was made applicable to proceedings pending on or after 30.04.1982 - The said sub-section (1A) provides that in additional to the market value of the land, the Court would in every case award an amount calculated at the rate of twelve per centum per annum on such market value for the period commencing on and from the date of the publication of the notification u/s. 4(1), in respect of such land to the date of the award of the Collector or the date of taking possession of the land, whichever is earlier - Whether respondents (owners of land) were not entitled to the benefit of s. 23(1A) of the Act? - Held, in the instant case the acquisition proceeding commenced with the notification u/s. 4 issued on 06.03.1965 and it culminated in passing of the award by the Collector on 09.07.1980, i.e., before 30.04.1982, the date from which the amending Act was made applicable to the pending and subsequent proceedings - Therefore, in terms of the law laid down by the SC in the case of K.S. Paripoornan 1994 INDLAW SC 1227, the respondents were not entitled to the benefit of s. 23(1A) of the Act - Appeal partly allowed.
