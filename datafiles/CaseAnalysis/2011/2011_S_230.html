Haricharan & Anr v State Of Madhya Pradesh & Ors

Supreme Court of India

09 March 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Surinder Singh Nijjar, B. Sudarshan Reddy

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 187; (2011) 4 SCC 159; 2011 (2) JabLJ 334; 2011 (2) JCC 1068; JT 2011 (3) SC 493; 2011 (2) RCR(Criminal) 330; 2011(3) SCALE 462; [2011] 3 S.C.R. 769

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 304, 330 and 342 - Third degree torture - Conviction - Challenged - FIR was registered against appellants, police officers, for offences punishable u/s. 343, 330 and 304 Part II of IPC - It was alleged that deceased unlawfully detained in the police station and was subjected to third degree, resulting in death - Trial Court acquitted all the appellants of all the charges - On appeal, HC reversed the findings of acquittal recorded by the Trial Court and convicted all the appellants - Hence, instant appeal - Whether the HC erred in reversing-the judgment of acquittal as recorded by the Trial Court? - Held, prosecution proved beyond reasonable doubt that deceased was taken to the police station - Whilst at the police station, he was subjected to third degree torture and was given electric shocks - Such torture was inflicted on deceased merely for the purpose of extracting a confession that he was guilty of the offence of theft fact that deceased was tortured and subjected to electric shock whilst in police custody was well established by the medical evidence - Hence, evidence on the record showed that death of deceased was a direct consequence of the inexcusable and inhuman torture by the police - Appeals dismissed.
