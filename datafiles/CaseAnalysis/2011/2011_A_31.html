Air India Cabin Crew association and others v Union of India and other

Supreme Court of India

17 November 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 846; (2012) 1 SCC 619; 2012 (2) AWC 1371; 2012 (1) LLJ 305; [2011] 15 S.C.R. 843; 2012 (2) SLR 337

Case Digest

Subject: Air Law; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Equal Remuneration Act, 1976, Air India Limited, Indian Airlines Limited

Summary: Service - Practice & Procedure - Air Law - Air India Employees' Service Regulations - Air Corporation (Transfer of Undertakings and Repeal) Act 1994, ss. 8, 9 - Equal Remuneration Act, 1976, s. 5 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 15 and 16 - Merging of Cadre - Policy decision - Alteration - Challenged - Appellants (employees) working with respondent (Airlines Corporation) - Respondent issued promotion policy for all cabin crew members, but treated pre and post-1997 crew separately - When revised promotion policy for Cabin crew was brought into effect from 7.6.1997, few Air Hostesses association unsuccessfully challenged binding effects of Settlement dt.5.6.1997, before HC, despite opposition from appellant Association - HC accepted conditional proposal of merger of cadres of male and female members of Cabin Crew and held that Air Hostesses were entitled to retire at the age of 58 years from flying duties on par with Flight Pursers and other members of cabin crew - Further held that age of retirement from flying duties of Air Hostesses at and up to age of 50 years with option to them to accept ground duties after 50 and up to age of 58 years amounted to discrimination against them based on sex, which was violative of arts. 14, 15 and 16 of Constitution and s. 5 of 1976 Act - It was further held that 2 cadres of male and female Cabin Crew members came to be merged only after 1997 and merger applied to fresh recruits and conditions of service and distinction between 2 cadres would continue with regard to existing Cabin Staff up to year 1997 - Claim of respondents that posts of Flight Supervisors was abolished by promotion policy of 1997 and that male and female cadres of Cabin Crew recruited prior to 1997, was merged - An administrative order bringing female Cabin crew and male Cabin Crew at par in respect of age of retirement was passed an ir Hostesses were permitted to fly up to age of 58 years - in said order it was indicated that after promulgation of order, Executive Female Cabin Crew would be eligible to be considered for position of In-Flight Supervisor along with Executive Male Cabin Crew - Appellants challenged the same before HC of Delhi - DB dismissed the petitions by impugned judgment, wherein constitutional validity of s. 9 of 1994 was question - DB held that 'In-Flight Supervisor' was a description of a job and was not a post exclusively reserved for male cabin crew - Aggrieved appellants filed instant appeals - Whether management of Air India was entitled to alter service conditions of Flight Pursers and Air Hostesses, despite several bilateral agreements - Held, on 26.5.1993, Management of Air India and appellant Association signed a Memorandum of Settlement providing for payment of interim relief during period of wage settlement for period commencing from 1.9.1990 - It was indicated that settlement was in supersession of all previous Agreements, Record Notes, Understandings, Awards and past practices in respect of matters specifically dealt with or amended or modified - It was stipulated that Settlement would be implemented after the same was approved by Board of Directors of Air India Limited - Result of said Settlements and Agreements was that designation of Air Hostesses and Flight Pursers were discontinued and all were designated as 'Cabin Crew' - It was apparent from judgments delivered in Nergesh Meerza's case, 1981 Indlaw SC 165 and Yeshaswinee Merchant's, 2003 Indlaw SC 506, cases that the same were rendered in the context of bringing parity between cadre of In-Flight Supervisors and cadre of Air Hostesses - It was, in fact, prerogative of Management to place an employee in a position where he would be able to contribute the most to Company - Thus, notwithstanding decision in Nergesh Meerza's case and in Yeshaswinee Merchant's case, Air India was at liberty to adopt revised promotion policy which was intended to benefit all employees - Admittedly appellants were not concerned with post-revised promotion policy, but with separate cadre of In-Flight Pursers, as distinct from cadre of Air Hostesses, with regard to their channel of promotion - It was agreed that there was a category of Cabin Crew referred to as In-Flight Supervisors, which was confined to In-Flight Pursers alone and did not concern the Air Hostesses - However, it was unable to agree that to treat duties discharged by In- Flight Supervisors to indicate that 'In-Flight Supervisor' was a separate post - Duties discharged by persons designated as In-Flight Supervisors did not create any separate post and post remained that of In- Flight Pursers - Appellants were not discriminated against in any way on account of decision in Nergesh Meerza's and Yeshaswinee Merchant's cases - Position since the decisions rendered in said cases, underwent a change with adoption of revised promotion policy agreed to between parties and which replaced all earlier agreements - Management of Air India was always entitled to alter its policies with regard to their workmen, subject to consensus arrived at between parties in supersession of all previous agreements - It could not be accepted that those workmen who were promoted to Executive category would continue to be governed by Settlements arrived at when they were workmen and were represented by Association -Once an employee was placed in Executive cadre, he ceased to be a workman and ceases to be governed by Settlements arrived at between Management and workmen through the concerned Trade Union - It was not a question of an attempt made by such employees to wriggle out of Settlements which was arrived at prior to their elevation to Executive cadre, which, by operation of law, cease to have any binding force on employee so promoted by Management - Impugned order passed in several writ petitions out of instant appeals aroused were dismissed - Yeshawinee Merchant and Others v. Air India Limited and Others, 2003 Indlaw Sc 506 and Air India v. Nergesh Meerza and Others, 1981 Indlaw SC 165, referred to - Appeals dismissed.
