Kailash Gour & Ors v State Of Assam

Supreme Court of India

15 December 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, Dalveer Bhandari, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 793; (2012) 2 SCC 34; AIR 2012 SC 786; 2012 (1) JCC 519; 2012 (1) MLJ(Crl) 807; 2012 (1) OLR 263; 2012 (5) RCR(Criminal) 548; [2011] 16 S.C.R. 318

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 323, 324, 326 and 448 r/w s. 34 - Murder - Mob - Communal Violence - Benefit of Doubt - Accused persons were charged with offences punishable u/ss. 302, 326, 324, 323, and 448 r/w s. 34, IPC - Trial Court convicted 8 out of 14 persons for offence of murder and sentenced them to undergo imprisonment for life - HC upheld conviction of appellants while acquitting 3 of the accused giving them benefit of doubt - Hence, the instant appeal - Whether prosecution established beyond reasonable doubt that appellants were perpetrators of crime - Held, fundamental principles of criminal jurisprudence states that accused to be presumed innocent till proven guilty - There was indeed long distance between accused 'may have committed the offence' and 'must have committed the offence' which should have been traversed by prosecution by adducing reliable and cogent evidence - Presumption of innocence to be recognised as human right which could not be wished away - In the instant case, 3 innocent persons including 2 young children were done to death in the incident in question which needed to be deprecated in strongest terms but unless proved to be the perpetrators of the crime beyond a reasonable doubt, appellants not to be convicted and sentenced for same - Benefit of doubt had to survive - Appeal allowed.
