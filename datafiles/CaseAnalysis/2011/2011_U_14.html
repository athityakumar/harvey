Union Of India & Anr. v Pradip Kumar Kedia Etc.

Supreme Court of India

17 November 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Patnaik, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 774; (2012) 1 SCC 432; 2011 (5) ESC 819; [2011] 13 S.C.R. 196; 2012 (1) SLR 505; [2012] 204 TAXMAN 71; 2012 (1) UPLBEC 331

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Income-tax Appellate Tribunal (Recruitment and Conditions of Service) Rules 1963

Summary: Service - Income Tax Act, 1961 - Income-tax Appellate Tribunal (Recruitment and Conditions of Service) Rules 1963 - Appointment - Wait-list candidates - Rules - Applications were invited for filling up of posts in Income Tax Appellate Tribunal (ITAT) by Government of India - Selection Board selected 18 candidates out of whom 2 did not get vigilance clearance and remaining 16 candidates were approved for appointment by Appointments Committee, which decided not to make any further appointment till amendment of Rules, 1963 - Writ Petition was filed by Revenue Bar Association for a mandamus to give effect to the selection list with regard to the posts of Judicial and Accountant Members in the Income Tax Appellate Tribunal - HC disposed of Writ Petition with direction to place matter before Appointments Committee and also directed Appointment Committee to give effect to Selection List as approved by Selection Board in the light of the decisions in R.S. Mittal v. Union of India, 1995 Indlaw SC 607 - On appeal, SC directed Union of India to complete formalities and to give effect to Selection List - Thereafter, Appointments Committee approved names of all selected candidates and appointed them till date of retirement - Appointments Committee decided that appointment of members of ITAT in future would be taken up only after recruitment rules 1963, were amended - Aggrieved by decision, Appellants, who were in wait-list of candidates for respective posts filed OA before Central Administrative Tribunal (CAT) for directions for their appointment - Applications were transferred to Principal Bench of CAT - Principal Bench allowed 3 OA's by passing common order and directed Union of India to consider 3 wait-listed candidates for filling up advertised vacancies existing in posts of Judicial Member and Accountant Member in unreserved category - Appellants approached HC, contending that vacancies in ITAT could be filled up only after recruitment Rules, 1963 were amended as decided by Appointments Committee - HC held that recruitment Rules, 1963 were already amended and an amendment was inserted in r. 4 (a), Rules, 1963 but there was nothing in amendment which disqualify any of 3 wait-listed candidates from being appointed as members of ITAT - Further, selection was conducted by a high-power Selection Board presided over by a sitting Judge of SC and no one could doubt recommendation of Selection Board which deserved to be given due weightage and consideration and directed Union of India to process appointment of 3 wait-listed candidates - Hence, instant appeal - Whether selection list was given effect to by appellants in the light of decisions of this Court in R.S. Mittal v. Union of India and A.P. Aggarwal v. Govt. of NCT of Delhi and another - Held, r. 4 of Rules, 1963 was considered by SC in R.S. Mittal V. Union of India and it was held that a person in the select panel had no vested right to be appointed to the post for which he had been selected, but he has a right to be considered for appointment - In instant case wait list of candidates recommended by Selection Board, however, was not given effect to, u/r. 4 (3), Rules 1963, Central Government after taking into consideration recommendations of Selection Board make list of persons selected for appointment as members - Thus, until Appointments Committee approved list of wait-listed candidates, such wait-listed candidates were not persons selected for appointment - Appointments Committee in its meetings took view that any further appointment after 16 selected candidates could be made after amendment of Rules, 1963 - Central Government was having both rule making authority as well as appointing authority of any member of ITAT under Act 1961 - Hence, if Central Government took decision through Appointments Committee of Union Cabinet to undertake appointments in future after amendment of rules, it was difficult for Court to hold that reason given by Central Government in not making any further appointments because of proposed amendments to rules was not a justifiable or proper reason and that decision of Central Government in not approving wait list of candidates recommended by Selection Board was not in accordance of SC decisions in R.S. Mittal v. Union of India and A.P. Aggarwal v. Govt. of NCT of Delhi and Another - Further, after Appointments Committee approved appointments of 16 selected candidates found suitable for appointment as members of ITAT, Court should not compel Central Government to make appointments from wait-listed candidates recommended by Selection Board by writ - Hence, set aside impugned judgment of HC and common judgment of Principal Bench, CAT - Appeals allowed.
