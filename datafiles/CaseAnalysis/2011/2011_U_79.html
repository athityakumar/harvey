State of Madhya Pradesh v Union of India and another

Supreme Court of India

17 August 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 578; (2011) 12 SCC 268; AIR 2012 SC 2518; 2011 (5) AWC 5382; 2011 (3) JabLJ 167; [2011] 12 S.C.R. 140

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution

Keywords: Constitutionality, Constitution (Forty-Third Amendment) Act, 1977

Summary: Civil Procedure - Constitution - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 6 r. 17 - Constitution (Forty-Second Amendment) Act, 1976 - Constitution (Forty-Third Amendment) Act, 1977 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 131, 14, 226, 32 - Madhya Pradesh Reorganisation Act, 2000, ss. 58(3), 58(4) - Violation of provisions - Validity - Plaintiff/State filed application for amendment of plaint seeking, inter alia, amendment to effect that ss. 58(3) and 58(4) of the 2000 Act were violative of art. 14 of Constitution - Defendant No. 1/UOI apart from disputing its maintainability on delay and laches also contested on merits - Defendant No. 2 had objected to amendment on ground that same was totally misconceived and untenable in law and that no recourse whatsoever could be permitted to challenge validity of Central law under exclusive jurisdiction of SC u/art. 131 of Constitution- Defendant No. 2 contended that plaintiff was seeking that defendant No. 1 should perform its duty in accordance with Statute and, on other hand, was challenging validity of Statute and, therefore, it was liable to be dismissed - Held, it was not clear and not explained to SC why such recourse was not adopted when MPEB itself had approached SC by way of a writ petition to challenge vires of those provisions and, ultimately, SC dismissed said writ petition filed by Board - It was also noted that Court did not find any infirmity in manner of exercise of power by Central Govt u/ss. 58(3) and 58(4) of the 2000 Act while upholding notifications as being constitutional and not suffering from any vice of arbitrariness as claimed by plaintiff and MPEB - - By way of present amendment, plaintiff was seeking to challenge validity of Central law in proceeding (suit) initiated u/art. 131 of Constitution - Original plaint proceeded that exercise of power by Central Govt. by passing impugned Notifications u/ss. 58(3) and 58(4) of the 2000 Act was arbitrary, unjust and unfair and had resulted in serious anomalies in apportionment of assets and liabilities - It was further held that if amendment as sought for by plaintiff was allowed and plaintiff was permitted to challenge vires of said provisions, then very basis on which plaintiff was claiming its right to apportionment of assets, rights and liabilities of undivided Board would cease to be in existence and entire suit of plaintiff would be rendered infructuous - Moreover, it was settled principle of law that leave to amend would be refused if it introduces totally different, new and inconsistent case or challenges fundamental character of suit - In spite of conclusion, Court held that plaintiff might be given opportunity to put forth its stand that Central Govt. issued impugned Notifications/Orders without proper guidelines and affording opportunity to parties concerned - Thus, plaintiff was permitted to raise such objections at time of trial by placing acceptable materials - Application disposed of.
