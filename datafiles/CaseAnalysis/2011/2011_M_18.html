Board of Trustees of the Port of Mumbai and another v M/S Byramjee Jeejeebhoy Pvt.Ltd. & Anr

Supreme Court of India

08 April 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Aftab Alam, R.M. Lodha

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 246; (2011) 5 SCC 128; AIR 2011 SC 1793; 2011(3) ALL MR 431; 2011 (3) Bom.C.R. 563; JT 2011 (4) SC 473; 2011 (5) MLJ(SC) 712; 2011(4) SCALE 454; [2011] 5 S.C.R. 157

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - Bombay Rents, Hotel and Lodging House Rates Control Act, 1947, ss. 13, 15 and 22 - Subletting - Protection u/s. 15(2) of the Act - Plaintiff-respondent no.1 filed a suit in the court of Small Causes at Bombay, seeking a decree of eviction, against the defendants - Suit land was given to defendant no.1 on lease - In terms of the lease deed, defendant no.1, the lessee had the right to renewal but it had no right to assign the leased out land to any third party - Plaintiffs sought eviction of the defendants on grounds of breach of the terms and conditions of the lease the condition against assignment of any portion of the lease hold land to any third party - Trial court held that the plaintiff had failed to establish the breach of the covenant against assignment of the leasehold property to a third party - Appeals filed thereagainst was dismissed - Revision was filed before HC wherein HC affirmed the findings of the courts below that the execution of the sub-lease by defendant no 1 in favour of defendant no.2 without obtaining the permission in writing from the plaintiff was in breach of clause 4 of the lease deed - (A) Whether defendant No. 2 was entitled to the protection of s. 15(2) of the Act? - Held, as per s. 15(2) of that Act any sub-letting, assignment, transfer of interest in any other manner or licensing made by the tenant after February 1, 1973 without there being any sanctioning clause in the contract or without the express consent of the landlord would constitute a ground for eviction under section 13(1)(e) of the Act - In the instant case, defendant No. 2 was in occupation of the suit land long before February 1, 1973 and had continued to be in its possession on that date - The sub-letting by defendant No. 1 in favour of defendant No. 2, thus, clearly fell within the protective ambit of s. 15(2) of Act - (B) Whether to claim protection u/s. 15(2) of the Act, it was incumbent upon the claimant to show that there was a sub-lease, assignment or transfer in his favour prior to 1973 and it was in pursuance of such sub-lease, assignment or transfer that it came in possession and continued to be in possession of the demised property and was actually in possession of the demised property on February 1, 1973? - Held, s. 15(2), used the expression 'transfer of interest in any other manner' which was sufficiently wide to include even an oral arrangement pursuant to which the sub-lessee might enter upon the land and continue in its possession - Hence, initial induction of defendant No. 2 on the suit land was covered by s. 15(2) of the Act - (B) It was submitted that the execution of the sub-lease by defendant no.1 in favour of defendant No. 2 on June 17, 1978 and the continued possession of the suit land by defendant no.2 on the basis of that sub-lease would not come under the protection of section 15(2) of the Act - Held, s. 22 of the Act provided for the landlord to have full information concerning the sub-lessee/licensee who might be in occupation of the demised premises on February 1, 1973, including the rent charged from him by the tenant - Provisions of s. 22 suggested that after the cut off date, i.e., February 1, 1973 there should be no material change, to the detriment of the landlord in the terms and conditions on which the sub-tenant was in possession of the demised premises on that date and in case after that date, any material change is brought about in the status of the sub-tenant, to the prejudice of the landlord that might not have the protection of section 15(2) but may come within the mischief of s. 13(1)(e) - In the facts of the instant case, there was no material change in the status of defendant No. 2 or in the terms and conditions on which it was in possession of the suit land on February 1, 1973 or in the inter se relationship between the two defendants - Execution of the sub- lease on June 17, 1978 by defendant No. 1 in favour defendant No. 2 would not, therefore, militate against the protection offered by s. 15(2) of the Act - Execution of the lease would not constitute a ground for eviction against defendant no.1 in terms of s. 13(1)(e) of the Act - Judgments and orders passed by the HC and the two courts below unsustainable and accordingly, set aside - Appeals allowed.
