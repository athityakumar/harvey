Rajesh Kumar Srivastava v State Of Jharkhand & Ors

Supreme Court of India

10 March 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Mukundakam Sharma, A.R. Dave

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 158; (2011) 4 SCC 447; 2011 (3) CGLJ 93; 2011 (2) ESC 281; 2011 (129) FLR 252; JT 2011 (3) SC 99; 2011 (3) LLJ 305; 2011 (4) MLJ(SC) 894; 2011(3) SCALE 435; [2011] 3 S.C.R. 823; 2011 (3) SLR 231

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Service

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Service - Non-suitability to post - Discharge from service - Violation of principles of natural justice - Appellant was appointed as Probationer Munsif - Full Court meeting of HC, on basis of a complaint against appellant, recommended that appellant's service was no longer required - Respondent State issued order of discharge - Appellant challenged discharge order before HC - HC dismissed appellant's writ petition - Hence instant appeal - Appellant contended that order of discharge was an order of removal and same having been passed without holding an enquiry amounts to, not only violation of principles of natural justice but also amounts to casting a stigma in appellant's career - Whether impugned order of discharge was passed in violation of principles of natural justice - Held, in course of adjudging suitability of appellant's candidature it was found that appellant's performance was not satisfactory and therefore he was not suitable for job - Decision of discharge from service was taken by respondents considering his overall performance, conduct and suitability for job - While taking such decision neither any notice was required to be given to appellant nor he was required to be given any opportunity of hearing - Instant case was not a case of removal as sought to be made out by appellant, but was a case of simple discharge from service, hence it was only a termination simpliciter and not removal from service on grounds of indiscipline or misconduct - While adjudging appellant's performance, conduct and overall suitability, his performance record as also report from higher authorities were called for and they were looked into before any decision was taken as to whether officer concerned should be continued in service or not - Impugned order of termination passed in instant case was a fall out of his unsatisfactory service adjudged on basis of his overall performance and manner in which he conducted himself - Such decision should not be said to be stigmatic or punitive, therefore, there was no infirmity in impugned judgment and order passed by HC - Appeal dismissed.
