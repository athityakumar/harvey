Ashiwin S. Mehta & Anr v Union Of India & Others

Supreme Court of India

08 November 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

D.K. Jain, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 790; (2012) 1 SCC 83; 2012 (2) AWC 1242; 2011 (4) BC 636; 2011 (6) Bom.C.R. 674; [2011] 168 Comp Cas 251; 2012 (1) CompLJ 140; 2012 (1) DRTC 305; 2012 (5) RCR(Civil) 861; [2011] 14 S.C.R. 1000

Case Digest

Subject: Capital Market

Keywords: Unregistered, Custodian, Presidency Insolvency Act

Summary: Capital Market - Special Court (Trial of Offences Relating to Transactions in Securities) Act, 1992 - Unregistered shares - Custodian - Recovery - Appellants purchased more than 90 lakh shares from respondents company - Appellants holding was attached by a notification u/s. 3(2) of the Act - Appellants handed over 39.16 lakh unregistered shares to custodian - Custodian filed an application before Special Court seeking registration of unregistered shares on the name of Custodian and for recovery of lapsed benefits that accrued on unregistered shares - Special court allowed registration of un-registered shares on the name of Custodian - On suo motto, Custodian was directed to draft a scheme for sale of shares of appellants, which constituted bulk of the attached assets to Special court for approval - Special Court approved scheme of Custodian for sale of Controlling Block of Shares in toto and ordered sale of all registered shares, except shares of respondents because their objection regarding registration of unregistered shares on the name of Custodian-appellants, was pending - Custodian drafted the terms and conditions for sale of 54,88,850 shares of respondents - Custodian submitted report to Special Court for consideration - Special Court directed sale of 54,88,850 shares to respondents - Hence, instant appeal - Whether or not impugned order of Special Court was in breach of principles of natural justice, thus, vitiating to sell subject shares to respondents and companies managed by their promoters - Held, Special Court had exercised its discretion in complete disregard to its own scheme and 'terms and conditions' approved by it for sale of shares and passed order in violation of principles of natural justice - When 36.90 lakh shares of respondents were claimed to have been extinguished, relief sought by appellants to rescind the entire sale of 54,88,850 shares would be impracticable and fraught with grave difficulties - Thus, relief should be confined to 4.95% of the shares - Impugned order was set aside and directed Special Court to recover the 4.95% shares from respondents - Appeal partly allowed.
