Sri Radhy Shyam (Dead) Through L.Rs. Andothers v State Of U.P. Andothers

Supreme Court of India

15 April 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 282; (2011) 5 SCC 553; 2011(3) ALL MR 920; 2011 (4) AWC 3280; 2011 (2) CLT(SC) 114; 2011 (7) MLJ(SC) 1361; 2011 (3) RCR(Civil) 96; 2011(4) SCALE 677; [2011] 8 S.C.R. 359; 2011 (3) UPLBEC 1793

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Uttar Pradesh, Industrial Area Development Act, 1976, English Lands Clauses Consolidation Act, 1845

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 17(1) and 17(4) - Invoking of urgency clause - Legality - Appeal against order passed by the HC whereby the writ petition filed by the appellants questioning the acquisition of their land for planned industrial development by invoking ss. 17(1) and 17(4) of the Act was dismissed - Appellants challenged the acquisition of their land on the grounds: (i) land could not be used for industrial purposes because in the draft Master Plan of Greater NOIDA (2021), the same was shown as part of residential zone; (ii) that they had already constructed dwelling houses and as per the policy of the State Government, the residential structures were exempted from acquisition; (iii) That the State Government arbitrarily invoked s. 17(1) r/w s. 17(4) of the Act and deprived them of their valuable right to raise objections u/s. 5-A of the Act - Whether the appellants had succeeded in prima facie proving that there was no justification to invoke the urgency clause and to dispense with the inquiry envisaged u/s. 5-A of the Act? - Held, s. 17(1) r/w s. 17(4) conferred extraordinary power upon the State to acquire private property without complying with the mandate of s. 5-A - Before excluding the application of s. 5-A, the concerned authority must be fully satisfied that time of few weeks or months likely to be taken in conducting inquiry u/s. 5-A would, in all probability, frustrate the public purpose for which land was proposed to be acquired - If land was acquired for the benefit of private persons, the Court should view the invoking of s. 17(1) and/or s. 17(4) with suspicion and carefully scrutinize the relevant record before adjudicating upon the legality of such acquisition - In the instant case, there was no legally acceptable justification for the exercise of power by the State Government u/s. 17(1) of the Act because the acquisition was primarily meant to cater private interest - It was neither the pleaded case of the respondents nor any evidence was produced before the Court to show that the State Government and/or agencies/instrumentalities of the State were intending to establish industrial units on the acquired land either by itself or through its agencies/instrumentalities - Apprehension of the respondents that delay in the acquisition of land would lead to enormous encroachment was totally unfounded - There was no real and substantive urgency which could justify invoking of the urgency provision u/s. 17(1) and in any case, there was no warrant to exclude the application of s. 5-A of the Act - Impugned judgment of HC set aside - Appeal allowed.
