Jagdish Prasad v State Of Rajasthan And Others

Supreme Court of India

07 July 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Swatanter Kumar, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 431; (2011) 7 SCC 789; 2011 (130) FLR 784; JT 2011 (7) SC 384; 2011(7) SCALE 552; [2011] 12 S.C.R. 1; 2011 (4) SLR 651

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Clubbing, Rajasthan Transport Service Rules, 1979

Summary: Service - Rajasthan Transport Service Rules, 1979 - Selection/promotion process - Legality - Appellant, belonging to a Scheduled Caste, appointed as a Motor Vehicle Sub-Inspector, was promoted to post of Motor Vehicle Sub Inspector through Departmental Promotion Committee (DPC) on the principle of seniority-cum-merit - Subsequently, a notification was issued by the respondents amending 1979 Rules; by this amendment, Schedule 1 and Schedule 2 of the 1979 Rules to the existing Rules were amended and requirement of candidates to pass the qualifying examination for the post of District Transport Officer was deleted - Thereafter, seniority list of Motor Vehicle Inspectors was issued, on the basis which, a number of persons, including the appellant, were promoted to the post of District Transport Officer - Respondent No.2, who was holding the post of Motor Vehicle Inspector filed appeal before the Rajasthan Civil Services Appellate Tribunal (Tribunal) against said order contending that he was senior to the persons who were promoted by that order and this was a supercession, contrary to the 1979 Rules - It was also contended that the State of Rajasthan violated rr. 10 and 24 of the 1979 Rules and clubbing of the vacancies from the years 1983-84 to 1993-94 was for the total 21 vacancies, which was improper as the respondents were obliged to consider the vacancies in each given year in terms of r. 10 - State replied that though the vacancies had been determined year wise no one had passed the qualifying examination and nobody had been found eligible for promotion upto 1st April, 1994 as per Schedule II of the 1979 Rules - Thereafter, qualifying examination was done away with and appointment/promotion to the post of DTO was made simply by promotion - Tribunal directed the State Government to hold review DPC to determine yearwise vacancies afresh - Writ petitions and appeals filed thereagainst were dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - (A) Whether merely by the amendment to Schedule I and the deletion of Schedule II of the 1979 Rules the effect of the statutory provisions like rr. 6, 10, 24 of the 1979 Rules read in their plain language would stand diluted? - Held, Schedule of the 1979 Rules had to be in conformity with, and was required to advance the object of the primary statutory provision - Thus, a schedule could not in any way wipe out the statutory provisions of the Act in effect and spirit - Once these provisions stand in the statute book, then respondents could not escape from complying with them in the appropriate manner and without defeating the object of these Rules - (B) How the 50% promotions were to be filled up by the respondents - Held, r. 24(6) of the 1979 Rules mandates that selection for promotion to all other higher posts or higher categories of posts in the Service shall be made on the basis of merit and on the basis of seniority-cum-merit in the proportion of 50:50 - 50% vacancies are to be filled up on the basis of merit while the remaining 50% vacancies in the promotion quota were to be filled up by seniority-cum-merit - Eligibility requirements have been specified under the 1979 Rules, which candidates must satisfy to be considered under the seniority-cum-merit category - Further, even if Schedule II of the 1979 Rules did not exist, it was obligatory on the part of the respondent to evolve a methodology to make promotions purely on merit - Hence, once the framers of the Rules have intended to provide merit as the sole criteria of promotion, the appointing authority was not vested with the jurisdiction to waive the same or completely wipe out the same, on a flimsy excuse such as the one proposed in the instant case - In the instant case, process of selection adopted by the State Government could not be accepted - Preparation of seniority list, method of selection and clubbing of vacancies were in violation of the statutory Rules - Selection/promotion order set aside - Directions issued to hold fresh process of selection in accordance with Rules and that the selection by promotion for the yearwise vacancies shall be filled in by compliance to r. 24(6) of the 1979 Rules - Appeals disposed of.
