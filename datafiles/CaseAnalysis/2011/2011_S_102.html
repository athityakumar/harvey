Shaik Imambi v Special Deputy Collector (Land Acquisition), Telegu Ganga Project

Supreme Court of India

02 February 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 130; (2011) 11 SCC 639; 2011 (2) CLT(SC) 296; 2011 (3) RCR(Civil) 333; 2011(3) SCALE 17

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - Determination of compensation - Appellant's lime orchard consisting of 761 lime trees and the adjoining lands were acquired - Land Acquisition Officer determined the market value of the said land by income capitalisation method and valued the income from each lime tree as Rs.80 per annum - He assessed the age of the trees as six years and accordingly awarded compensation - However, not being satisfied with the said award, the appellant sought reference to the Civil Court, which upheld the award and affirmed the compensation determined by the Land Acquisition Officer - On appeal, HC assessed the annual income as Rs.100 per lime tree, however, reduced the multiplier to 10 for determining the market value by capitalisation method - Instant appeal - (A) Whether HC ought to have maintained the multiplier of 14 adopted by the Land Acquisition Officer instead of reducing it to 10 in regard to the increased income adopted by it? - Held, HC was right in holding that the multiplier should be 10 - (B) Whether income per lime tree ought to have been taken as Rs.200 per annum, instead of Rs.100 per annum, having regard to the expert opinion and evidence let in by the parties? - Held, there was no specific documentary evidence in regard to the actual income from the orchard; as the reports of experts of the state government assessed the gross annual income from each tree as Rs.150-200, it would be appropriate to take the average thereof, namely Rs.175 as the annual income per tree in instant case - After deducting Rs.35 towards the cost of cultivation and other expenses, the net annual income would have been Rs.140 per tree - Thus it was increased to Rs. 60 per tree per annum - Appeal partly allowed.
