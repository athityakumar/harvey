Gayathri Womens Welfare Association v Gowramma and another

Supreme Court of India

11 January 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Surinder Singh Nijjar, B. SUDARSHAN REDDY

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 42; (2011) 2 SCC 330; AIR 2011 SC 785; 2011 (84) ALR 786; 2011 (2) AWC 2161; 2011 (4) CLT(SC) 256; JT 2011 (1) SC 315; 2011 (4) MLJ(SC) 195; 2011 (2) MPLJ 477; 2011 (3) RCR(Civil) 325B; 2011 (112) RD 511; 2011 (1) RLW 588; 2011(1) SCALE 379; [2011] 2 S.C.R. 47

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Belated counter-claim - Appellants filed a suit for permanent injunction against respondents alleging that respondents were interfering with the appellant's possession and enjoyment of the schedule property - Trial Court decreed the suit of the appellants for permanent injunction - On appeal, HC remanded the matter to the Trial Court - After remand, respondents amended their written statement and incorporated counter claim to direct the appellants to demolish the structures put up subsequent to passing of the status quo order by the Trial Court on the schedule property - After remand and framing of additional issues, Trial Court again decreed the suit of appellants and dismissed the counter claim filed by the respondents - During the pendency of the appeal before the HC, the respondents filed an application seeking amendment of the written statement to include the additional prayer in the counter claim, which was allowed by HC - HC also set aside judgment and decree of the Trial Court dismissing the counter claim of the respondents - Present appeal - Whether the HC was justified in permitting the respondents to raise the counter claim at a stage after the issues had been framed by the Trial Court? - Held, Trial Court upon a detailed appreciation of the evidence led by the parties concluded that on the basis of the material on record, it can be said that the possession of the appellant in respect of the plaint schedule property as against the respondents was long, settled and uninterrupted - In the facts of the present case, the Trial Court held that the appropriate remedy available to the respondents was to sue for possession - Further, evenafter the matter was remanded back, the Trial Court again decreed the suit of the appellants, the counter claim was dismissed - Moreover, respondents failed to establish any factual or legal basis for modification/nullifying the decree of the Trial Court - HC while allowing the claims of the respondent to include the prayer for possession in the counter claim, failed to appreciate that the order passed by the Trial Court did not cause any prejudice to the respondents - Trial Court had merely held that the remedy of an independent suit was available to the respondents - Hence, HC committed a serious error of jurisdiction in allowing the appeal filed by the respondents - Appeal allowed.
