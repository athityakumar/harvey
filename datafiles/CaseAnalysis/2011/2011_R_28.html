Haryana State Agricultural Marketing Board & Anr. v Raj Pal

Supreme Court of India

10 February 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 170; (2011) 13 SCC 504; AIR 2011 SC 1394; 2011 (3) CPJ(Sc) 20; 2011 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 3355; 2011 (3) RCR(Civil) 514; 2011(4) SCALE 209B

Case Digest

Subject: Trade; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Haryana State Agricultural Marketing Board (Sale of Immovable Property) Rules, 1997

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Trade - Haryana State Agricultural Marketing Board (Sale of Immovable Property) Rules 1997 - Payment of instalments - First appellant was Haryana State Agricultural Marketing Board (Board) and second appellant was the Nigdhu Market Committee (Market Committee) - The Market Committee established a New Grain Market and issued public notice of a scheme for the open auction of plots, booths and commercial places in the market - Respondent (auction purchaser) was the highest bidder in respect of the plot for which he gave the bid - In pursuance of it, respondent was issued a letter of allotment - Allottees/auction purchasers committed default in paying the instalment of even the auction price - Market Committee sent Demand Notices to all the allottees calling upon them to pay the balance sale price and interest on the instalments at 15% per annum, as also the penal interest - Allottees/auction purchasers filed writ petitions for quashing the demand notices contending that the Market Committee had not provided the basic amenities and facilities in the market - HC allowed the writ petitions - Instant appeal - Whether allottees could postpone the payment of instalments merely on the ground that some of the amenities were not ready? - Held, allottees were liable to pay the instalments and simple interest thereon in terms of the letters of allotment - Allottees could not postpone the payment of instalments merely on the ground that some of the amenities were not ready - However, having regard to the fact that the Rules did not contemplate compound interest and penal interest and the Market Committee was yet to complete certain infrastructural work like water, sewerage disposal, Market Committee would not be entitled to claim any compound interest or penal interest - Appeals partly allowed.
