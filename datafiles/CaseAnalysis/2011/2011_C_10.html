K.N. Govindan Kutty Menon v C.D. Shaji

Supreme Court of India

28 November 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, J. Chelameswar

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 799; (2012) 2 SCC 51; 2012 (3) ALD(SC) 28; 2012 (90) ALR 454; 2012 (2) AWC 1606; 2011 (4) CLT(SC) 442; 2012 (1) CTC 96; 2012 (1) JCC (NI) 36; 2011 (4) KLT 857; 2012 (3) MahLJ 490; 2012 (2) MLJ(SC) 307; 2012 (2) MPLJ 482; 2012 (2) OLR 244; 2012 (165) PLR 546; 2012 (1) RCR(Criminal) 102; 2012 (3) RLW 2423; [2011] 15 S.C.R. 447; 2012 (2) WLN(SC) 1

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Banking & Finance - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, s. 138 - Legal Services Authorities Act, 1987, s. 21 - Instalment - Execution - Settlement - Decree - Whether criminal case filed u/s 138 of the N.I Act referred by Magistrate Court to Lok Adalat could be settled by the parties and an award passed in Lok Adalat recording the settlement could be considered as decree of civil court and thus executable - Held, according to s. 21 of the Act, 1987, every award of Lok Adalat should be deemed to be decree of civil court and executable by that Court - The 1987 Act did not make out distinction between reference made by civil court and criminal court - Moreover, no restriction on power of Lok Adalat to pass award based on compromise arrived at between the parties in respect of cases referred to by various Courts (both civil and criminal), Tribunals, Family court, Rent Control Court, Consumer Redressal Forum, Motor Accidents Claims Tribunal and other Forums of similar nature - Impugned order passed by Principal Munsiff Judge and HC was set aside - Appeal allowed.
