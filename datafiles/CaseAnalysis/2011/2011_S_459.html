Bakshi Dev Raj and another v Sudhir Kumar

Supreme Court of India

04 August 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, H.L. Gokhale

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 584; (2011) 8 SCC 679; 2011 (6) ALD(SC) 1; 2011 (88) ALR 463; 2011 (5) AWC 4392; 2011 (4) CLT(SC) 109; JT 2011 (8) SC 520; 2011 (7) MLJ(SC) 584; 2011 (4) RCR(Civil) 258; 2011 (114) RD 357; 2011(8) SCALE 259; [2011] 9 S.C.R. 815

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, o. 23 r. 3, o. 47 r. 1(1), o. 47 r. 1(a) - Indian Contract Act, 1872, s. 23 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 420 - Maintainability - Review - Adjustment - Validity - Respondent filed petition for declaratory decree to effect that he was owner and in possession of suit land that of appellant No. 1 and sought decree for permanent injunction restraining appellants - Appellants filed joint written statement - Trial Court dismissed suit - Aggrieved respondent filed appeal - Appellate Court allowed appeal in favor of respondent - Appellants filed Appeal before HC - Appeal was disposed of by HC and modified decree with consent of both parties - Petition was filed by appellants in SC and same was dismissed - Hence, instant appeals - Whether orders passed by HC were sustainable - Held , Court should insist upon parties to reduce terms into writing - - It was clear that there was no permission or reservation or liberty for taking further action - Dismissal of SLP was not bar for filing review before Court - Even after dismissal of SLP aggrieved parties were entitled to move Court concerned by way of review - In instant case, though appellants moved SLP in Court against order of HC in Appeal SLP was dismissed as withdrawn without leave of Court - Even after dismissal of an SLP with or without reasons, aggrieved party was entitled to file review - Hence, language used in o. 47 r. 1(a) CPC which related to 'review', present Review Petition could not be dismissed on ground of maintainability - It was further held that review petition filed by appellants was maintainable in view of O. 3 rr. 1 and 4, Chapter related to role of pleaders and in view of conduct of appellants in not raising any objection to act of their counsel except filing review petition Court was not inclined to accept claim of appellants - Appeals dismissed.
