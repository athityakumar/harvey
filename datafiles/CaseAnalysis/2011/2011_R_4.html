R. Ramachandran Nair v The Deputy Superintendent Vigilance Police & Anr

Supreme Court of India

28 March 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 192; (2011) 4 SCC 395; 2011 ALL MR (Cri) 2911; 2011 (2) KLT 135; 2011 (4) MLJ(Crl) 687; 2011 (2) RCR(Criminal) 564; 2011(4) SCALE 14; [2011] 3 S.C.R. 1054

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Sree Sankaracharya University of Sanskrit Act, 1994

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 13(1)(d) and 13(2) - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120-B and 463 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 239 - Sree Sankaracharya University of Sanskrit Act, 1994, s. 50 - Discharge - Criminal proceedings - Allegedly appellant while functioning as the Vice-Chancellor of the University obtained a pecuniary advantage thereby causing corresponding wrongful loss to the University - FIR was registered against the appellant u/ss. 13(2) r/w s. 13(1)(d) of the Prevention of Corruption Act and ss. 120-B and 463 of IPC - After 8 years a charge-sheet was also filed in the Court of the Enquiry Commissioner however, without obtaining the previous sanction of the Syndicate of the University u/s. 50(2) of the Act, 1994 - Meanwhile, State Government directed Vigilance and Anti Corruption Bureau to withdraw the cases against the appellant as he was eligible for the protection u/s. 50(3) of the Act, 1994 - Appellant filed an application u/s. 239 of the CrPC for discharge, which was dismissed on the ground that the appellant was not entitled to get the protection of s. 50 of the Act as being the Vice-Chancellor, the appellant was a public servant - Revision petition filed thereagainst was also dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether prosecution could be allowed to proceed in the face of s. 50(2) of the Act, 1994 without the sanction of the Syndicate of the University? - Held, appellant, being Vice-Chancellor of the University, was one of the Officers of the University in terms of s. 23 of the Act, 1994 - Hence, as per s. 50(2) of the Act, no prosecution would lie against the appellant without the previous sanction of the Syndicate - Also, allegations against appellant related to actions which he had taken while he was discharging his duties as an Officer of the University, namely, the Vice-Chancellor of the University - Further, prosecution could not be allowed to proceed, even otherwise, he being a Vice-Chancellor, acted diligently by following the procedure, no action could be initiated after a period of 8 years from the initiation of the complaint - Appellant had made a case for discharge from the criminal proceedings - Appellant was discharged from all the allegations leveled against him - Appeal allowed.
