Bachni Devi & Anr. v State Of Haryana Through Secretary, Home Department

Supreme Court of India

08 February 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 83; (2011) 4 SCC 427; AIR 2011 SC 1098; 2011 ALL MR (Cri) 964; 2011 CRLJ 1634; 2011 (2) G.L.R. 1557; 2011 (1) JCC 418; JT 2011 (2) SC 1; 2011 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 665; 2011 (3) MLJ(Crl) 769; 2011 (1) RCR(Criminal) 868; 2011(2) SCALE 265; [2011] 2 S.C.R. 627

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 304B - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 113B - Dowry Death - Conviction - Challenged - Appellants were convicted for the offence punishable u/s. 304B of the IPC - Trial Court held that the prosecution had been able to establish that the death of deceased was within seven years of her marriage and otherwise than under normal circumstances; that before her death she was subjected to cruelty and harassment by A-1 and A-2 in connection with the demand of motorcycle and that A-1 and A-2 were guilty of causing dowry death - On appeal, HC upheld the order of conviction passed by Trial Court - Hence, instant appeal - Whether conviction of A-1 and A-2 was correct? - Held, it was clearly established that deceased died otherwise than under normal circumstances within seven years of her marriage - It was established that unlawful demand of motorcycle was made by A-1 and A-2 from PW-8 and deceased was harassed on account of his failure to provide the motorcycle and that led the deceased to commit suicide - There was no merit in the contention that the demand of motorcycle did not qualify as a 'demand for dowry' - Hence, all the essential ingredients to bring home the guilt u/s. 304B of IPC were established against the appellants by the prosecution evidence - Further, presumption u/s. 113B of the Evidence Act was fully attracted in the facts and circumstances of the present case; the appellants failed to rebut the presumption u/s. 113B of the Evidence Act - Appeal dismissed.
