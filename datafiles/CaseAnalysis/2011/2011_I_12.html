Indra Das v State of Assam

Supreme Court of India

10 February 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Markandey Katju, Gyan Sudha Misra

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 92; (2011) 3 SCC 380; 2011 (3) ACR 2530; 2011 (2) ADJ 702; 2011 (2) AllCriC 351; 2011 ALL MR (Cri) 945; 2011 CRLJ 1646; 2011 (2) JCC 1165; JT 2011 (2) SC 153; 2011 (2) MLJ(Crl) 522; 2011 (2) RCR(Criminal) 64; 2011(2) SCALE 312; [2011] 4 S.C.R. 289

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Bihar Sales Tax Act, 1947, Punjab Municipal Corporation Act, 1976, Rajasthan Nathdwara Temple Act, 1959

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Terrorist and Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Act, 1987, s. 3(5) - Being a member of banned organization - Extra judicial confession - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant was charged for the death of deceased, however, his name was not named in the FIR - No prosecution witness had attributed any role to the appellant - Charge sheet in the case was filed after a gap of nine years from the date of the commission of the offence, and charges were framed more than four years after filing of the charge sheet - Appellant allegedly made confession to a police officer on a subsequent occasion, for which he was charged under the provisions of 1987 Act, however, appellant retracted the confession subsequently - Whether the appellant could be convicted on the basis of his confession - Held, there was no evidence against the appellant except the confessional statement - SC, in Arup Bhuyan v. State of Assam (decided on 3-2-2011 in Criminal Appeal No.889 of 2007), held that mere membership of a banned organization could not incriminate a person unless he was proved to have resorted to acts of violence or incited people to imminent violence, or did an act intended to create disorder or disturbance of public peace by resort to imminent violence - In the instant case, even assuming that the appellant was a member of a banned organization, there was no evidence to show that he did acts of the nature mentioned in Arup Bhuyan case - Even if appellant was a member of banned organization it had not been proved that he was an active member and not merely a passive member, hence the decision in Arup Bhuyan's case squarely applicable in the instant case - Impugned judgement of conviction, therefore, was set aside - Appeal allowed.
