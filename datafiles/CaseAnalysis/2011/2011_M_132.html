Nandiesha Reddy and another v Mrs.Kavitha Mahesh

Supreme Court of India

08 July 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Chandramauli Kumar Prasad, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 518; (2011) 7 SCC 721; AIR 2011 SC 2639; 2012 (2) CLT(SC) 224; 2012 (1) KarLJ 353; 2011 (3) RCR(Civil) 709; [2011] 8 S.C.R. 154; 2012 (2) WLN(SC) 38

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Election

Keywords: Presidential and Vice-Presidential Elections Act, 1952

Summary: Civil Procedure - Election - Presidential and Vice-Presidential Elections Act, 1952, ss. 5(B)(1)(a) - Representation of the People Act, 1951, ss. 33, 33(1), 33(4), 34, 81, 81(3), 83, 83(1)(a), 86, 87, 100(1)(c) - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 6 r. 16, r. 2, O. 7 r. 11 - Nomination - Rejection - Sustainability - Appellant got elected to Assembly in general election from Assembly Constituency - Respondent in election petition before HC challenged election of appellant - Appellant filed two applications under O. VI r. 16 CPC for striking out pleading from election petition and another u/ss. 83, 86 of the 1951 Act r/w O. VII r. 11 CPC for dismissal of election petition - HC by impugned orders dismissed aforesaid applications - Hence, instant appeals - Whether respondent/petitioner could be said to be candidate so as to maintain election petition and further Returning Officer was right in refusing to accept nomination - Held, when nomination paper was presented it was bounden duty of Returning Officer to receive nomination, peruse it, point out defects, if any, and allow candidate to rectify defects and when defects were not removed then alone question of rejection of nomination would arise - Further, s. 33(4) of the 1951 Act casts duty on Returning Officers to satisfy himself that names and electoral roll numbers of candidates and their proposers as entered in nomination paper were same as in electoral rolls - Therefore, respondent/petitioner for purpose of maintaining an election petition should be deemed to be candidate, if Election Petitioner wants to put forth plea that nomination was improperly rejected to declare an election to be void it was necessary to set out averments for making out said ground - Further, reason given by Returning Officer for refusal to accept nomination and facts necessary to show that refusal was improper was required to be set out in election petition - However, it could not be said that election petition contains material facts to make out a cause of action and s. 83(1)(a) of the 1951 Act inter alia provides that election petition should contain concise statement of material facts - Further, s. 87 of the 1951 Act provides that subject to provisions of the Act and the Rules framed thereunder every election petition should be tried in accordance with procedure applicable under CPC to trial of suits - In an election petition, which did not contain material facts, no relief could be granted - Material fact as used in s. 83(1)(a) of the Act or O. VI r. 2 CPC had not been defined in the Act or CPC -- Appeals dismissed.
