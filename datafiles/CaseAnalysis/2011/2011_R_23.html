Raghubir Singh v State of Rajasthan and others

Supreme Court of India

29 August 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

H.S. Bedi, Gyan Sudha Misra

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 681; (2011) 12 SCC 235; 2012 (1) MLJ(Crl) 682; 2011 (4) RCR(Criminal) 113; 2011(9) SCALE 472

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 149, 302, 307, 304 Part II - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Accused allegedly attacked appellants/PWs and caused blows to them with their weapons and where as one of appellant died on spot and some of appellants sustained serious injuries - PW 1 filed FIR and on completion of investigation, accused persons were charged u/ss. 302, 307, r/w 302/149, 307/149 IPC - Trial Court convicted 7 accused persons out of 9 u/ss. 302, 302/149, 307 and 307/149 PC and sentenced them various terms of imprisonment under those provisions, however Trial Court acquitted 2 accused persons - Aggrieved by Trial Court judgment 7 accused persons filed appeal before DB - Where as PW 1 assailed acquittal of 2 accused persons filed revision before DB during pendency of appeal in HC - HC held, appeal of appellants were partly allowed and conviction of appellant under provisions of IPC was set aside, instead he was convicted u/s. 304 IPC and revision was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Accused contended that dispute between parties with regard to land had resulted in sudden fight between two groups and as such observations of HC was fully justified on evidence - Whether HC judgment was sustainable - Held, injury No.1 had been caused with a cultivator was clear from medical evidence and extent and gravity of injury shows that accused had intention to cause one of appellant's death - However, accused was concerned, his conviction u/s. 304 IPC even on findings recorded by HC was erroneous and allowed these appeals to the extent that accused was guilty u/s. 302 IPC for having caused murder of one of appellant and restore judgment of Trial Court to such limited extent - Hence, in Gajanand and Others v State of Uttar Pradesh 1954 Indlaw SC 202 relied on - Appeals disposed of.
