C. M. Thri Vikrama Varma and another v Avinash Mohanty & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

12 July 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Patnaik, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 435; (2011) 7 SCC 385; 2011 (9) ADJ 19; AIR 2011 SC 2627; 2011 (6) ALD(SC) 81; 2011(5) ALL MR 400; 2011 (6) AWC 6039; 2011 (4) ESC 644; JT 2011 (8) SC 1; 2011(7) SCALE 542; [2011] 8 S.C.R. 212; 2011 (4) SLR 601; 2011 (4) UPLBEC 3430

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Indian Police Service (Recruitment) Rules, 1954, Indian Administrative Service (Cadre) Rules, 1954

Summary: Service - Indian Administrative Service (Cadre) Rules, 1954 - Appointment - Challenged - Petitioner and respondent both were selected for appointment to the Civil Services Examination, 2004 and were offered appointments to the Indian Police Service (IPS) in 2005 - By notification dt. 19.01.2006, respondent, who had secured the 45th rank was allocated to the Chhattisgarh cadre, whereas petitioner, who had secured 201st rank was allocated to the Andhra Pradesh cadre - Respondent filed original application before Tribunal contending that the guidelines and norms of the letter dt. 31.05.1985 issued by the Secretary, Government of India, have not been followed while making the allocations and the allocation of petitioner to the Andhra Pradesh cadre was arbitrary and in his place he should have been allocated to the Andhra Pradesh cadre - Tribunal dismissed said application - Respondent filed writ petition before HC who allowed said writ petition and quashed the allocation of the petitioner to the Andhra Pradesh cadre and directed the Union of India to reconsider the allocation of respondent and petitioner in accordance with law - Petitioner filed appeal against said order contending that SC in Union of India and Others vs. Rajiv Yadav, IAS and Others 1994 INDLAW SC 892, held that u/r. 5 of Rules, the Central Government was under no obligation to have options or preferences from the officers concerned and this Rule made the Central Government the sole authority to allocate the members of the service to various cadres and therefore a person appointed to an All India Service, having various State cadres, had no right to claim allocation to a State of his choice or to his home State - Whether order of the HC could be upheld? - Held, admittedly, respondent had secured a higher rank than petitioner in the Civil Services Examination, 2004 and both respondent and petitioner were insiders - Para 3 of the letter dt. 31.05.1985 states that allocation of insiders, both men and women, will be strictly according to their ranks, subject to their willingness to be allocated to their home States - Hence, respondent was required to be considered for allocation to the Andhra Pradesh cadre if he had given his willingness for being allocated to his home State, Andhra Pradesh, before petitioner could be considered for such allocation - However, the vacancy for which consideration was being made was a vacancy for an insider OBC candidate in the 30 point roster, petitioner would have preference over respondent - But the HC had come to a finding that the number of vacancies in the 30 point roster filled up by OBC candidates from Civil Services Examinations 1999-2003 were 9 and had exceeded the 27% reservation for OBC candidates and hence there could not be an insider OBC vacancy in which petitioner could have been allocated - The HC was, therefore, right in coming to the conclusion that allocation of petitioner to the Andhra Pradesh cadre was in violation of the guidelines contained in the letter dt. 31.05.1985 and was clearly arbitrary and not equitable - Impugned order upheld - Appeals dismissed.
