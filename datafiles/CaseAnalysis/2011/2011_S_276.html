M/S. Msk Projects (I) (Jv) Ltd. v State Of Rajasthan & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

21 July 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 845; 2011 (5) AWC 5076; 2011 (3) CompLJ 481; 2011 (6) MLJ(SC) 985

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Keywords: Indian Tolls Act, 1851, Rajasthan Motor Vehicles Taxation (Amendment) Act, 1994, Notification dated 1.9.2000

Summary: Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996 - Sections 34 and 37 (1A) - Indian Tolls Act, 1851 - Interest Act, 1978 - Section 3 - Rajasthan Motor Vehicles Taxation (Amendment) Act, 1994 - Arbitral award - Works contract for construction of road on basis of Build, Operate and Transfer (B.O.T.) - Collection of toll from vehicles - Dispute referred to arbitration - Arbitrator cannot be allowed to assume jurisdiction over question not referred to him - In view of Section 3 of Interest Act, Court has power to vary rate of interest agreed by parties - State can levy/collect toll fee only for period stipulated under statute - Or till actual cost of project with interest etc. recovered - However, it cannot be source of revenue for State - Toll fee cannot be collected to recover amount never spent by contractor - On facts, matter remitted to arbitral Tribunal to reconsider certain points.The arbitral Tribunal cannot travel beyond terms of reference ; however, in exceptional circumstances where a party pleads that the demand of another party is beyond the terms of contract and statutory provisions, the Tribunal may examine by the terms of contract as well as the statutory provisions. In the absence of proper pleadings and objections, such a course may not be permissible.

As the notification had been issued, and it was not the responsibility of the State to establish a police chowki etc. to implement the notification, there was no occasion for the Tribunal to proceed further. Therefore, any award in favour of the private appellant in that respect for non-issuance of notification beyond the date of the notification, cannot be held to be justified and the same is liable to be set aside.

The State authority has decided to establish a toll road as it was not having sufficient funds. In case the claim of the private appellant is allowed and as the State is not in a position to grant further facility to collect the toll fee at such a belated stage, the purpose of establishing the toll road itself stands frustrated. More so, the toll fee cannot be collected to recover the amount never spent by the contractor. It is evident from the discourse in pre-bid meetings of the parties that it had been decided that compensation would be worked out on the basis of investment made by concerned contractor. More so, the statutory notification dated 10.2.1997 provided to recover the cost of construction and maintenance including interest thereon. Therefore, the question of non-execution of work of second phase of the contract becomes very material and relevant to determine the real controversy. The State authorities for the reasons best known to them, did not make reference to the arbitration proceedings for non-execution of the work of the second phase of the contract. However, the relief claimed by the private appellant would prove to be a "windfall profit" without carrying out the obligation to execute the work just on technicalities. It has already been held that the arbitrator cannot proceed beyond the terms of reference and, therefore, the question of considering the non-execution of work of second phase of the work was neither permissible nor possible as it had arisen subsequent to the date of award in the arbitration proceedings.

However, in order to do complete justice between the parties and protect the public exchequer, the matter requires adjudication and reconsideration on the following points by the arbitration Tribunal :

(i) What amount could have been recovered by the private appellant for Bharatpur-Deeg part of the road from the vehicles using the road?

(ii) What could be the effect on the contract as a whole for non-executing the work of the second phase?

The private appellant shall be entitled only for a sum of R 26.34 lacs awarded by the Tribunal for delay in issuing the notification with 10% interest, if not paid already or it could be adjusted in the final accounts bills.
