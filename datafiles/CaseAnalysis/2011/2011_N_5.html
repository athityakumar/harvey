Interglobe Aviation Ltd. v N.Satchidanand

Supreme Court of India

04 July 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 426; (2011) 7 SCC 463; 2012 (2) ALD(SC) 72; 2011 (88) ALR 289; 2011 (3) CLT(SC) 26; 2011 (3) CPJ(Sc) 1; JT 2011 (8) SC 106; 2011 (3) KLT 295; 2011 (7) MLJ(SC) 619; 2011 (3) RCR(Civil) 666; 2011(7) SCALE 159; [2011] 6 S.C.R. 1116

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Keywords: Carrier

Summary: Air Law - Arbitration & ADR - Practice & Procedure - Carriage By Air Act, 1972 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872 - Legal Services Authorities Act, 1987 - ss. 19, 22B(1), 22C - Deficiency in service - Compensation - Sustainability - Respondent opted for a flight from Delhi to Hyderabad - Flight got delayed due to dense fog in airport for which respondent was made to stay in airport - Respondent filed complaint against appellant before permanent Lok Adalat for Public Utility Services claiming compensation - Permanent Lok Adalat granted award to respondent - Aggrieved by same a writ petition was filed before HC which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether Permanent Lok Adalat have jurisdiction and Whether compensation awarded by lower Court was sustainable - Held, Permanent Lok Adalats were authorized to deal with and decide only disputes relating to service rendered by notified public utility services provided value did not exceed Rupees Ten Lakhs and dispute did not relate to non-compoundable offence - S. 22D of the 1987 Act, provides that Permanent Lok Adalat while conducting conciliation proceedings or deciding a dispute on merit under the 1987 Act, be guided by principles of natural justice, objectivity, fair play, equity and other principles of justice and not be bound by CPC and the 1872 Act. - Further, part of cause of action also arose at Hyderabad as respondent clearly alleged as one of causes for claiming compensation, his illegal detention for an hour and half at Hyderabad Airport by security staff of appellant when flight landed therefore Courts and Tribunals at Hyderabad had jurisdiction to entertain claims/disputes - Further, there could be no doubt that respondent was forced to sit which was considerable physical hardship and agony on account of delay, but which was not as a consequence of any deficiency in service, negligence or want of facilitation by appellant. - Thus, if delay was due to reasons beyond control of airline and if appellant and its crew have acted reasonably and in a bona fide manner, appellant could not be made liable to pay damages even if there had been some inconvenience or hardship to a passenger on account of delay - Appeal allowed.
