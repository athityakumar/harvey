Dedicated Freight Corridor Corporation Of India v Subodh Singh & Ors

Supreme Court of India

30 March 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 203; (2011) 11 SCC 100; 2011 (86) ALR 473; 2011 (3) AWC 3112; 2011 (2) CLT(SC) 13; 2011 (5) RCR(Civil) 217; 2011(4) SCALE 71; [2011] 3 S.C.R. 1160

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Railways Act, 1989, ss. 20E(1) and 20F(2) - Acquisition - Compensation - Challenged - Respondent's land was acquired for a railway project - A declaration regarding impugned land to be acquired was published on 12.12.2008 in the official gazette - Thereafter, an order dt. 8.2.2010 was made by the competent authority determining the compensation payable to the respondent u/s. 20F(1) of the Act - Respondent filed writ petition for quashing the impugned award on the ground that the award was not made within 1 year from the date of publication of the date of declaration dt. 12.12.2008 - HC quashed the impugned award and declared that the acquisition proceedings stood lapsed - Appellant filed appeal against said order contending that impugned award was validly made within 1 year from the date of declaration, as it was made within 1 year from 20.2.2009, the date on which public notice of the said notification dt. 12.12.2008 was published in the newspapers - (A) Whether the period of 1 year, stipulated u/s. 20F(2) of the Act, for making the award, has to be reckoned from the date of publication of the declaration in the official gazette or from the date of any subsequent publication of the declaration in newspapers? - Held, the publication of the declaration u/s. 20E(1) of the Act was complete when it was published in the official gazette - The publication of the notification u/s. 20E(1), or its substance, in any newspaper, was not a requirement under the Act - Even if it was published in any newspaper, such publication will be only for general information and will not serve any purpose under the Act - Hence, what was relevant for the purpose of reckoning the period of 1 year was the date of publication of notification of declaration in the official gazette and nothing else - (B) Whether an award made within 6 months after the expiry of 1 year from the date of publication of the declaration, was valid u/s. 20F(2) of the Act, even if reasons were not recorded by the competent authority in writing to show that he was satisfied that the delay had been caused due to unavoidable circumstances? - Held, sub-section (2) of s. 20F of the Act provides that if the award was not made by the competent authority within 1 year from the date of publication of the declaration, the entire proceedings for acquisition of land shall lapse, the proviso thereto enables the competent authority to make the award within an extended period of 6 months if he was satisfied that the delay had been caused due to unavoidable circumstances and reasons therefor are recorded in writing - In the instant case, competent authority proceeded under the bona fide impression that publication of the public notice in the two newspapers on 20.2.2009 was also part of the process of publication of the declaration u/s. 20E(1) of the Act - The aforesaid reasoning in the award can be treated as the reason for the delay in making the award - Therefore, the acquisition did not lapse - However, as per second proviso to s. 20F(2) of the Act, the land owners would be entitled to additional compensation for the delay in making of the award at a rate not less than 5% of the value of the award for each month of delay - Hence, respondent shall be entitled to additional compensation - Appeal allowed.
