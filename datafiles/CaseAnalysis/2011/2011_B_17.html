Ravi v Badrinarayan & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

18 February 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Deepak Verma, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 108; (2011) 4 SCC 693; 2011 (1) ACC 704; 2011 ACJ 911; AIR 2011 SC 1226; 2011 (85) ALR 471; 2011 (2) AWC 1823; 2011 (2) G.L.R. 1216; JT 2011 (2) SC 472; 2011 (4) MLJ(SC) 315; 2011 (3) MPLJ 255; 2011 (162) PLR 493; 2011 (2) RCR(Civil) 190; 2011(2) SCALE 618; [2011] 4 S.C.R. 400

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance

Summary: Insurance - Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, ss. 140 and 166 - Dismissal of claim petition - Delay in lodging FIR - Legality - Appellant filed claim petition u/ss. 140 and 166 of Act before the Motor Accident Claims Tribunal (Tribunal) - Claim petition was dismissed on the grounds that formal FIR of the incident was lodged belatedly and appellant failed to establish that the said vehicle was involved in a motor road accident causing injuries to him - Appeal filed thereagainst before HC was also dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - (A) Whether delay in lodging the FIR of the accident could prove fatal so as to result into dismissal of the claim petition filed by the claimant? - Held, delay in lodging the FIR cannot be the ground to deny justice to the victim - In cases of delay, the courts are required to examine the evidence with a closer scrutiny and in doing so; the contents of the FIR should also be scrutinized more carefully - If court finds that there is no indication of fabrication, then even if there is a delay in lodging the FIR, the claim case cannot be dismissed merely on that ground - Hence, although lodging of FIR is vital in deciding motor accident claim cases, delay in lodging the same should not be treated as fatal for such proceedings, if claimant was able to demonstrate satisfactory and cogent reasons for it - In the instant case, it was amply proved that the vehicles was involved in the road accident, which had caused injuries to appellant - Also the delay in lodging the FIR was explained satisfactorily and it inspired confidence as cogent and valid reasons were assigned therein - (B) Quantum of compensation - Held, in the instant case, the appellant had sustained permanent disability to his own body to the extent of 50% and which have not been cured till date despite several surgeries - Hence, a total amount of Rs. 2,50,000 to be awarded to the appellant payable by respondents jointly and severally, would meet the ends of justice - Appeal allowed.
