Booz-Allen and Hamilton Inclusive v State Bank of India Home Finance Limited and others

Supreme Court of India

15 March 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, J.M. Panchal

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 823; 2011 (4) AWC 3656

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Summary: (1) Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996 -Section 8 -Reference to arbitration -Suit for enforcement of charge/mortgage over flat -Realisation of sale proceeds therefrom -And right of appellant to stay in possession till entire deposit repaid -And all matters specifically mentioned in Arbitration Clause to be settled by arbitration -Hence, subject-matter of suit falling within scope of arbitration agreement.

(2) Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996 -Section 8 -Reference to arbitration -Suit -Filing of first statement on substance of dispute before filing of application under Section 8 -Scope of Section 8 -Filing of detailed objection by appellant to application for interim relief -Cannot be considered to be submission of statement on substance of dispute -Resulting in submitting oneself to jurisdiction of Court.

(3) Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996 -Section 8 -Reference to arbitration -Application therefor under Section 8 -No time limit for filing application -In circumstances of case, application filed nearly 20 months after entering appearance in suit -High Court not justified in rejecting application on ground of delay.

(4) Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996 -Sections 8 and 11 -Reference to arbitration -Suit for enforcement of mortgage by sale -Should be tried by Court and not by arbitral Tribunal -Dismissal of application under Section 8 upheld -Though for different reasons.
