Shivlal and another v State of Chhattisgarh

Supreme Court of India

19 September 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 663; (2011) 9 SCC 561; AIR 2012 SC 280; 2011 ALL MR (Cri) 3922; 2012 CRLJ 616; 2012 (1) MLJ(Crl) 536; 2012 (1) OLR 98; 2011 (4) RCR(Criminal) 286; 2011(10) SCALE 577

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Madhya Pradesh Police Regulations

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 148, 149, 302 -Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s.157 (1) - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellants/accused and others allegedly murdered deceased - Trial Court found appellants/accused guilty and awarded punishment u/ss. 149, 302 IPC - Appellants filed appeal before HC - HC confirmed conviction and sentence - Hence, instant appeal - Appellant contended that Courts below erred in convicting appellants on basis of evidence on which a large numbers of accused had been acquitted - There had been material irregularities in trial itself as no report had been sent to Magistrate which was mandatory - Once HC came to conclusion that recovery of weapons itself was doubtful, appellants were equally entitled for benefit of doubt - Whether conviction of appellants by Trial Court was sustainable - Held, object of statutory provision was to keep Magistrate informed of investigation so as to enable him to control investigation and, if necessary, to give appropriate direction - In instant case, copy of FIR was not sent to Magistrate at all as required u/s. 157 (1) Cr.P.C - and in absence of any explanation furnished by prosecution to that effect, would definitely cast shadow on case of prosecution - Therefore, appellants were entitled to benefit of doubt - Hence, appeal impugned judgments and orders of Courts below were set aside and appellants were acquitted - Appeal allowed.
