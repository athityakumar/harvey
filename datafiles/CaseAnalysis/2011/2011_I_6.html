State Of Tamil Nadu & Anr v India Cements Ltd. & Anr

Supreme Court of India

21 April 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

D.K. Jain, H.L. Dattu

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 277; (2011) 13 SCC 247; JT 2011 (5) SC 143; 2011 (5) RCR(Civil) 432; 2011(4) SCALE 843; [2011] 7 S.C.R. 395; [2011] 40 VST 225

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Keywords: Assessment Year, Eligibility Certificate

Summary: Sales Tax - Indirect Tax - Tamil Nadu General Sales Tax Act, 1959 - Sales tax deferral scheme - Entitlement of - Respondent, engaged in the manufacture and marketing of cement, proposed to set up an expanded unit to avail the benefit of sales tax deferral scheme under Government Order dt. 13.04.1994 - State Industries Promotion Corporation of Tamil Nadu (SIPCOT) issued the requisite eligibility certificate to the respondent - Respondent entered into an agreement with the Zonal Asst. Commissioner undertaking to comply with the Base Production Volume (BPV) and Base Sales Volume (BSV) - Respondent submitted its return claiming the deferral of tax on the sale in excess of BSV - Asst. Commissioner issued a notice to the respondent that they could avail of deferral of sales tax after reaching the BSV/BPV for all the units whichever was later and then they can avail deferral for expansion unit, accordingly, directed the respondent to pay a sum of Rs. 5873.51 lakhs which had been availed, in excess, as deferral of sales tax - Respondent filed petition before tribunal against said order who dismissed said petition - On appeal, HC held that the respondent was entitled to the benefit of deferral of sales tax under the interest free sales tax deferral scheme - Hence, the instant appeal - Whether the respondent was entitled to the benefit of sales tax deferral for the sales made in that year in excess of the BSV as soon as they exceed the BSV or only when their production also exceeds the BPV in that year? - Held, a conjoint reading of clauses 3(i) and (ii) of Government Order dt. 13.04.1994, and paragraph 5.3 of eligibility certificate shows that the object of the conditions with reference to reaching of BPV was to ensure that the concerned unit achieves the highest production and sale of the existing unit in the last 3 years prior to the commencement of the commercial production in the expansion unit, resulting in higher revenue on higher sales - The benchmark for availing the benefit of the sales tax deferral scheme having been fixed both with reference to the production as also to the sales, it was immaterial whether the unit concerned reaches BPV or the BSV earlier - The intention behind the schemes, clause 3(ii) of the Government Order dt. 13.04.1994 cannot be construed to mean that the benefit would flow only from the date of reaching the BPV and not from the date of reaching the BSV, particularly when the main object of the schemes was to increase the productivity without compromising with the revenue of the State - Any other interpretation of the said Government Order would frustrate the object of the scheme - Hence, respondent was entitled to the benefit of deferral of sales tax under the interest free sales tax deferral scheme for the sales made in excess of the BSV - Impugned order of the HC upheld - Appeal dismissed.
