Bhagwan Dass v State (Nct) Of Delhi

Supreme Court of India

09 May 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Markandey Katju, Gyan Sudha Misra

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 328; (2011) 6 SCC 396; AIR 2011 SC 1863; 2011 ALL MR (Cri) 2028; 2011 CRLJ 2903; JT 2011 (6) SC 345; 2011 (4) MLJ(Crl) 746; 2011 (2) OLR 47; 2011 (2) RCR(Criminal) 920; 2011(5) SCALE 498; [2011] 6 S.C.R. 330

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: (A) Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Honour killing - Conviction - Challenged - Appellant strangulated his daughter as he felt that he was dishonoured by the conduct of his daughter - Trial Court convicted the appellant and this judgment was upheld by the HC - Hence, instant appeal - Held, there was overwhelming circumstantial evidence to show that the appellant committed the crime as he felt that he was dishonoured by his daughter - Trial Court and HC have given very cogent reasons for convicting the appellant, and there was no reason to disagree with same - Appeal dismissed.

Obiter Dicta

(B) Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Honour killing - Held, honour killings, for whatever reason, come within the category of rarest of rare cases and hence deserve death punishment - Appeal dismissed.
