Senior Intelligence Officer v Jugal Kishore Samra

Supreme Court of India

05 July 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Aftab Alam, R.M. Lodha

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 406; (2011) 12 SCC 362; 2011 (270) E.L.T. 147; 2011 (3) JCC (Narcotics) 178; JT 2011 (7) SC 165; 2012 (2) MLJ(Crl) 567; 2011 (3) RCR(Criminal) 519; 2011(7) SCALE 3; [2011] 7 S.C.R. 889

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 20 and 21 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 438(2) - Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985, ss. 21 and 29 - Presence of advocate at time of interrogation - Permissibility - Officers of the Directorate of Revenue Intelligence (DRI) raided the premises of a company and found a shortage of 250kgs of Dextropropoxyphene Hydrochloride (DPP HCL), a manufactured narcotic drug - Accused no.1 and accused no.2, the Managing Director and the Production Manager, of said company admitted that the drug was clandestinely cleared to company, of which the respondent and his brother (accused no.3) were partners - Case was registered against the accused persons for the offences punishable u/ss. 21 and 29 of the NDPS Act - DRI officials summoned the respondent and accused no.3 - Respondent filed an application for anticipatory bail, which was allowed - Thereafter, respondent filed another application u/s. 438(2) of the CrPC for modification of the order of anticipatory bail to the extent that the interrogation and examination of the respondent be conducted in the presence of his advocate and a cardiologist - Application was allowed - Order was upheld by HC - Hence, instant appeal - Whether respondent was entitled, as of right, to the presence of his lawyer at the time of his interrogation in connection with the case? - Held, relied on decision in Poolpandi & Ors v. Superintendent, Central Excise & Ors., 1992 INDLAW SC 474, wherein it was held that in case persons concerned were not accused, there was no justification for 'expanding' the right reserved by the Constitution of India in favour of accused persons to be enjoyed by them - It was also held that refusal to allow presence of lawyer in such case would not be violative of protection u/art. 20(3) nor could it be violative u/art. 21 - In the instant case, respondent applied for and got anticipatory bail on the premise that he was not an accused in the case - There was no change in his position or status since the grant of bail till he was summoned to appear before the DRI officers - On the facts of the case, therefore, it was futile to contend that the respondent was entitled, as of right, to the presence of his lawyer at the time of his interrogation in connection with the case - Order of the HC, affirming the direction given by the Sessions Judge was unsustainable - However, having regard to the special facts and circumstances of the case, advocate or the person authorized by the respondent may watch the proceedings from a distance or from beyond a glass partition but he will not be within the hearing distance - Appeal allowed.
