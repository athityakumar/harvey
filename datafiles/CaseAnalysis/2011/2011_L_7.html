Chandrashekar (D) by LRs. and another v Land Acquisition Officer and another

Supreme Court of India

22 November 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

J. S. KHEHAR, R.M. Lodha

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 796; (2012) 1 SCC 390; AIR 2012 SC 446; 2012 (90) ALR 5; 2012 (2) AWC 1579; 2011 (4) CLT(SC) 406; 2012 (4) KarLJ 18; 2012 (3) MahLJ 8; 2012 (115) RD 302; [2011] 15 S.C.R. 414

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: City Improvement Trust Board Act, 1976

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act 1894, s. 18(3)(b) - City Improvement Trust Board Act, 1976 - Land Acquisition - Compensation - Enhancement of Compensation - Entitlement - Respondents issued land acquisition notification u/s. 15(1) of 1976 Act and acquired land falling in revenue estate of appellants - Respondents awarded market value of land for two villages at Rs.4,100 and Rs.13,500 per acre respectively, as compensation - Appellants filed an application u/s. 18(3)(b) of the 1894 Act for enhancement of compensation before Reference Court - Reference Court enhanced compensation to Rs. 1,46,000 per acre - Respondents filed writ appeal against award of Reference Court - HC held, deduction was inappropriate and remanded the matter for re- determination - Reference Court re-determined market value of the acquired land at Rs.1,45,000 per acre - Respondents filed another appeal before HC for reduction of quantum of compensation as awarded by Reference Court - HC partly allowed appeal and reduced quantum of compensation from Rs.1,45,000 to Rs.65,000 per acre - Hence, instant appeal - Appellants contended that deduction of 10% by way of de-escalation by HC was arbitrary and did not record any reasons, compensation determined by Reference Court should be restored - Whether HC was justified in deducting towards development of land, out of the market value determined on the basis of exemplar sale deed - Held, range for deductions, for issues other than developmental costs, must be 8 % or even furthermore, if deductions did not exceed upper benchmark of 75 % - Absence and presence of developmental components, should constitute the basis for arriving at an appropriate percentage of deduction, necessary to balance the differential factors between exemplar land and acquired land - Further, fault cannot be found with the determination rendered by HC on deduction of 10% under de-escalation, especially when period in question exceeded one year - Thus, there was no infirmity on the quantum of accumulated deductions applied by HC during assessment of the market value of acquired land - Hence, the final compensation determined by HC at Rs.65,000 per acre, even for land acquired from revenue estate of two villages was fully justified - Appeal dismissed.
