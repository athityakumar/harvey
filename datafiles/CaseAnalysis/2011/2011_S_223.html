Glodyne Technoserve Ltd v State Of M.P. & Ors

Supreme Court of India

04 April 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 215; (2011) 5 SCC 103; AIR 2011 SC 2574; 2011 (2) CLT(SC) 51; 2011 (2) JabLJ 167; JT 2011 (4) SC 404; 2011(4) SCALE 229; [2011] 15 S.C.R. 930

Case Digest

Subject: Contract & Commercial

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Rejection of bid - Validity - Government of Madhya Pradesh issued a Request for Proposal (RFP) for the appointment of a vendor for District Mechanism for Public Distribution System (PDS) - RFP, as it stood at the time when the bids were invited, included s. 3.1 which provided that the bidder/one partner in the consortium must possess a valid certification in the Capability Maturity Model - In addition, the bidder/all partners of consortium (in case of consortium) should have an active (valid at least till June, 2010) ISO 9001:2000 certificate which had to be submitted as qualifying documents - Subsequently, pre-qualification (Eligibility Criteria) provided in the RFP was changed, s. 3.1 was amended so that the bidder/one partner in the consortium had to possess a valid certification in the Capability Maturity Model - Vide the corrigendum, s. 7 which provided for the Bidder Check List, was also altered and provided that the bidder/one partner in the consortium must possess a valid certification in the Capability Maturity Model, in case of consortium the partner developing the software application was required to have CMM level 3 certification - Appellant was, disqualified from consideration, in view of the fact that along with the Tender documents it had filed a copy of the ISO 9001:2000 certificate of the previous year, instead of the current year - Whether appellant's bid documents was rightly rejected? - Appellant submitted that on the date of submission of the Bid, it had a valid and active ISO 9001:2000 certification, but that through inadvertence the expired certification of the previous year had been filed along with the bid papers - Held, introduction of the Corrigendum completely changed the provision in the Bidder's Response Form relating to submission of the Quality Certificate in the form of an active ISO 9001:2000 certification - As per Clause 9 of s. 7.1.1 of the RFP a tenderer was required to produce along with the bid document a copy of the Quality certificate which was valid and active on the date of submission of the bid and it did not enable a bidder to withhold the copy of such Quality Certificate - Appellant claimed to had a valid and active ISO 9001:2000 certification which it did not submit along with the Bid documents, may be due to inadvertence, but whether such explanation was to be accepted or not lay within the discretionary powers of the authority inviting the bids - Decision taken to reject the Technical Bid of the appellant could not be said to be perverse or arbitrary - Appeal dismissed.
