Arun Kumar Aggarwal v State of Madhya Pradesh and others

Supreme Court of India

02 September 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Dattu, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 613; (2014) 13 SCC 707; 2011 CRLJ 4935; 2012 (1) JabLJ 166; JT 2011 (10) SC 167; 2011 (4) RCR(Criminal) 137; 2011(9) SCALE 608; [2011] 10 S.C.R. 850

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Anti Corruption Act

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 120B - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 19, 13(1d), 13(2) - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 169 and 482 - Conspiracy by Govt. Servants - Prior Sanction to Prosecute - Obiter Dicta - Order -Justifiability - Respondent nos.1 and 2, had allegedly entered into conspiracy with 'X' and purchased land at higher rates causing financial loss to Govt. - Appellant filed complaint against respondents with Lokayukta police, which in turn, submitted closure report u/s. 169 CrPC after investigation to Reference Court - Trial Court rejected closure report and expressed opinion that matter may be taken seeking necessary sanction to prosecute respondents/accused -Respondent filed revision petition before HC - HC allowing petition quashed order on ground that it was illegal and without jurisdiction - Hence, instant appeals - Appellant contended that order of Trial Court could not be treated as direction issued to prosecute respondents as it no where addresses sanctioning authority nor directs to do any affirmative action or abstain from doing anything - Whether HC was justified in treating operative portion of order as direction issued to sanction prosecution of respondents/accused - Held, it was settled that obiter dictum was a mere observation or remark made by Court by way of aside while deciding actual issue before it , which did not form part of judgment of Court and had no authoritative value - In instant case refusal of Trial Court to accept final report by Lokayukta Police was only ratio decidendi of order - Other part of order which deals with initiation of challan proceedings could not be treated as direction issued - Therefore, there was no occasion for HC to interfere with order of Trial Court - Hence, directed and impugned order of Trial court was restored - Appeals allowed.
