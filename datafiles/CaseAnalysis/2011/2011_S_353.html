Iqbal Moosa Patel v State of Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

12 January 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 29; (2011) 2 SCC 198; 2011 (3) CGLJ 361; 2011 CRLJ 1142; 2011 (2) G.L.R. 905; 2011 (1) JCC (Narcotics) 35; 2011 (1) JhrCR(SC) 241; JT 2011 (1) SC 157; 2011 (3) MLJ(Crl) 948; 2011 (1) RCR(Criminal) 473; 2011(1) SCALE 318; [2011] 2 S.C.R. 121

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Smuggling and interstate trafficking of narcotic substances - Conviction - Challenged - Appellants were convicted for offences punishable u/s. 8(c), r/w ss. 21 and 29 of the NDPS Act - Conviction upheld by HC - Hence, present appeal - Appellants submitted that the prosecution had failed to prove its case beyond a reasonable doubt and the evidence adduced by it suffered from serious contradictions which made it unsafe to place any reliance upon the same - Held, prosecution is required to establish its case beyond a reasonable doubt, but that does not mean that the degree of proof must be beyond a shadow of doubt - In the present case, Trial Court and the HC have held that the recovery of the narcotic substance from the vehicle driven by appellant no.4 in which the appellant no.3 was also travelling was clearly established - Recovery of the narcotic substance from the house of the appellant no. 1 was also held by both the Courts below to have been proved beyond a reasonable doubt - Hence, there was no error or perversity in the view taken by the Trial Court or the HC for that matter to warrant interference u/art. 136 of the Constitution - Appeal dismissed.
