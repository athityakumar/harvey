State Of Delhi v Ram Avtar @ Rama

Supreme Court of India

07 July 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Swatanter Kumar, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 504; (2011) 12 SCC 207; AIR 2011 SC 2699; 2011 CRLJ 4231; 2011 (180) DLT 629; 2011 (3) JCC (Narcotics) 146; 2011 (4) RCR(Criminal) 191; 2011 (4) RLW 3500; 2011(7) SCALE 428; [2011] 7 S.C.R. 1129

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985, ss. 50, 21 - Illegal search - Non-compliance - Conviction - Sustainability - Secret informer met ASI and informed him if accused was searched and raid was conducted, smack could be recovered from him - Based on this ACP conducted raid immediately, resultantly, accused was taken into custody and charge-sheet for committing an offence u/s. 21 of the Act was filed against him - Trial Court convicted accused and sentenced him to undergo 10years RI and fine of Rs. 1,00,000/- - Aggrieved by this accused filed appeal before HC - HC held, there was no compliance with provisions of s. 50 of the Act, therefore accused was acquitted of the charge - Hence, instant appeal - Whether HC judgment was sustainable - Held, an illicit article seized from person of an accused during search conducted in violation of safeguards provided in s. 50 of the Act could not be used as evidence of proof of unlawful possession of contraband on accused, though any other material recovered during that search might be relied upon by prosecution in other proceedings, again accused, notwithstanding recovery of that material during illegal search - Hence, an illegal recovery could not take colour of lawful possession even on basis of oral evidence but in no event illegal recovery could be foundation of successful conviction u/s. 21 of the Act - Appeal dismissed.
