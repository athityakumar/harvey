Ramesh and others v State Of Rajasthan

Supreme Court of India

22 February 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

V.S. Sirpurkar, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 189; (2011) 3 SCC 685; 2011 (3) JCC 1599; JT 2011 (2) SC 331; 2011 (4) MLJ(Crl) 104; 2011 (2) RCR(Criminal) 732; 2011(2) SCALE 669; [2011] 4 S.C.R. 585

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 120-B, 302, 201, 404, 414, 457 and 460 - Murder - Robbery - Conviction - Death sentence - Challenged - Chargesheet was filed against accused persons for offences punishable u/ss. 120-B, 302, 201, 404, 414, 457, 460 r/w s. 34 of the IPC as also for the offence punishable u/s. 4 r/w s. 25 of the Arms Act against A-3 - Trial Court convicted all accused persons and against A-3 the case was treated to be the rarest of rare case and he was awarded death sentence - On reference, HC confirmed the death sentence and conviction - Hence, instant appeal - (A) Evidence of discovery and recovery - Held, HC correct in believing the recoveries and discoveries also, particularly, as against A-3 - The recovery was also supported by the evidence of PW-13 - Discovery made by the accused and the recovery of the ornaments in pursuance of that were completely credible - Hence, merely because the recoveries were made from the same place which was already visited by the police, that would itself not dispel the evidence of discovery and recovery - Further, if the accused agreed to discover different things on different dates and those things were actually found in pursuance of the information given by the accused, the discoveries could not be faulted for only that reason - Conviction upheld - (B) Whether the death sentence awarded to accused A-3 was justified? - Held, in the instant case, it was not clear from the evidence as to who inflicted the injuries on deceased persons though all the three were participants of the crime - There was no definite evidence about the acts on the part of each of the accused - Hence, it could not be said that it was A-3 alone who committed the murder only because he was the one who discovered the murder weapon - Further, it was not established that alternative punishment of life imprisonment would be futile and would serve no purpose - Hence, death sentence awarded to A-3 would not be justified and instead same modified to life imprisonment - Conviction for the other offences as also sentences awarded were confirmed - Appeals dismissed.
