Bhushan Kumar Meen v State of Punjab and others

Supreme Court of India

02 September 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Cyriac Joseph, Surinder Singh Nijjar

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 555; (2011) 8 SCC 438; JT 2011 (10) SC 107; 2012 (1) MLJ(Crl) 165; 2012 (1) OLR 39; 2011 (4) RCR(Criminal) 144; 2011(9) SCALE 617; [2011] 10 S.C.R. 844

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 406 and 498-A - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 482 - Quashing of criminal proceedings - Respondent No.2 filed a complaint to the Senior Superintendent of Police requesting that a criminal case be registered against the appellant u/ss. 406 and 498-A IPC - Said complaint was forwarded to the Women's Cell - Women's Cell found the entire complaint had been exaggerated - Subsequently, a further inquiry was held by the Superintendent of Police, who concluded that the respondent No.2 had been harassed by the appellant and her father-in-law and mother-in-law for not meeting the demand of dowry and suggested action to be taken u/ss. 406, 498-A of IPC and ss. 3 and 4 of the Dowry Prohibition Act, 1961 - Appellant filed application u/s. 482 of CrPC for quashing the FIR, which was dismissed - It was held that in view of the specific allegations contained therein, no ground for quashing the same had been made out - Hence, instant appeal - Held, complaint made by the respondent No.2 did not made out a prima facie case u/s. 498-A of IPC and appeared to have been filed by the respondent No.2 based on misunderstandings between the parties - No offence u/s. 498-A of IPC had been made out against the appellant and the complaint was, therefore, liable to be rejected - FIR was quashed - Appeal allowed.
