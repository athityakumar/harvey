Jahid Shaikh & Ors. v State Of Gujarat & Anr

Supreme Court of India

06 July 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 410; (2011) 7 SCC 762; 2011 ALL MR (Cri) 2673; 2011 CRLJ 3944; JT 2011 (7) SC 220; 2011 (3) RCR(Criminal) 608; 2011(7) SCALE 327; [2011] 10 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Transfer petition - Transfer Petition filed u/s. 406 of the CrPC for transfer of Sessions Case pending before the Special Judge, Ahmedabad, for trial outside the State of Gujarat - Cases were in connection with the series of bomb blasts that occurred in 2008 all over the country in major cities like Delhi, Mumbai, Jaipur, Ahmedabad and Bengaluru, killing many and injuring several others - As a response to the aforesaid blasts which were declared to be acts of terrorism by the State Government, a large number of young men were arrested both from within and outside the State of Gujarat - It was submitted that the local police authorities, jail authorities and the public prosecutor had conducted themselves in a manner which reflected total bias and prejudice against the accused (petitioners) and the same had created more than a reasonable apprehension in their mind that they would not get a fair and free trial in the State of Gujarat - Held, facts involved in the case were of a sensitive nature, but that by itself could not be a ground for transfer of the trial outside the State of Gujarat - Offences with which the accused were charged were of a very serious nature, but except for an apprehension that justice would not be properly administered, there was nothing to suggest that the charged atmosphere which existed at the time when the offences were alleged to have been committed, still exist and was likely to prejudice the little petitioners during the trial - Communally surcharged atmosphere which existed at the time of the alleged incidents, had settled down considerably and was no longer as volatile as it was previously - Hence, conditions in Gujarat today were not exactly the same as they were at the time of the incidents, which would justify the shifting of the trial from the State of Gujarat - Transfer Petition disposed of.
