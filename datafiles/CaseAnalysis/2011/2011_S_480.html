Syed Maqbool Ali v State Of Uttar Pradesh & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

04 April 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 304; (2011) 15 SCC 383; AIR 2011 SC 2542; 2011(3) ALL MR 905; 2011 (3) CLT(SC) 429; 2011 (2) RCR(Civil) 845; 2011(4) SCALE 766; [2011] 4 S.C.R. 238

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, s. 18 - Certain lands were acquired for construction of a road and compensation was paid to the land owners - After 12 years of acquisition, appellant filed complaint before Lokayukta alleging that his plots were illegally and unauthorisedly used for constructing the road - On enquiry by the Lok Ayukta, the Addl. District Magistrate (Land Acquisition) informed that there was a possibility of the acquired lands being left out and the road being constructed in the adjoining lands which were not acquired - Complaint was closed as time barred, in view of the delay of 12 years in seeking relief - Writ petition was filed seeking a direction to the respondents to pay compensation in regard to the extra land used and occupied by respondents by diverting the road from its original alignment - Writ petition was dismissed on the ground that appellant could have recourse to s. 18 of the Act, if he wanted enhancement of compensation - Whether HC could have dismissed a writ petition seeking a direction to acquire the land and pay compensation (on the ground that his land has been taken over without acquisition) by holding that the remedy lies u/s. 18 of the Act? - Held, application seeking reference to court u/s. 18 of the Act would lie only where the land-holder was aggrieved by the award made by the Land Acquisition Collector in regard to land acquired under the provisions of the Act - Application u/s. 18 of the Act could not be filed in regard to a land which was not acquired at all - Remedy of a land holder whose land was taken without acquisition was either to file a civil suit for recovery of possession and/or for compensation, or approach the HC by filing a writ petition if the action could be shown to be arbitrary, irrational, unreasonable, biased, malafide or without the authority of law, and seek a direction that the land should be acquired in a manner known to law - Appellant chosen to follow the second course - HC was not therefore, justified in dismissing the writ petition on the ground that the remedy was u/s. 18 of the Act - Matter required to be reconsidered by HC, on merits - Appeals allowed.
