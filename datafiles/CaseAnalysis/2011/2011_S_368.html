Sheo Shankar Singh and another v State of Jharkhand and another

Supreme Court of India

15 February 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, V.S. Sirpurkar

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 100; (2011) 3 SCC 654; AIR 2011 SC 1403; 2011 ALL MR (Cri) 1689; 2011 CRLJ 2139; JT 2011 (2) SC 367; 2011 (4) MLJ(Crl) 145; 2011 (2) RCR(Criminal) 634; 2011 (3) RLW 2342; 2011(2) SCALE 449; [2011] 4 S.C.R. 312

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120B and 302 r/w. 34 - Arms Act, 1959, s. 27 - Murder - Conviction - Challenged - The Trial Court found the accused (A1 and A2) guilty of the charges u/s. 302/34 of IPC - Accused A-2 was further held guilty of the charge u/s. 27 of 1959 Act - Out of the remaining six accused persons, the trial court found A3, A4 and A5 guilty of the charge u/s. 302 r/w. s. 120B of the IPC - However, the Trial Court acquitted accused A6, A7 and A8 for insufficiency of evidence against them - The Trial Court awarded rigorous imprisonment for life to accused A1 and A2 - Accused A2 was in addition sentenced to undergo rigorous imprisonment for 3 years u/s. 27 of the Arms Act - Similarly, accused A3, A4 and A5 were sentenced to undergo rigorous imprisonment for life u/ss. 302/120B of IPC - On appeal, HC upheld the conviction of the accused A1 and A2 and the sentence imposed upon them enhanced to the sentence of death by hanging - However, the HC acquitted accused A3, A4 and A5 and allowed criminal appeals to that extent - Hence, the present appeal - Whether the HC rightly convicted accused A1 and A2 u/s. 302 of IPC? - Held, the instant case rests upon the deposition of the eyewitnesses to the occurrence which suggested that on the date of occurrence and proximate in point of time the accused A1 and A2 were seen together riding a black coloured motor cycle, without a registration number - Evidence also established seizure of the motor cycle on which the deceased was riding from the place of occurrence and that which was being driven by accused A1 - Medical evidence, also supported the version of PW 16, that he sustained injuries when he fell from the motor cycle being driven by him after the deceased who was on the pillion was shot at by accused A2 - Hence, the HC rightly convicted accused A1 and A2 u/s. 302 of IPC - (B) Whether the present is one of those rare of rarest cases in which the HC could have awarded to the appellants the extreme penalty of death? - Held, the appellants were not professional killers - They were a part of the coal mafia active in the region and indulging in theft of coal from the collieries - The deceased, being opposed to such activities, appears to have incurred their wrath and got killed - Moreover, the murder was not politically motivated - Hence, the death sentence awarded to the appellants deserves to be commuted to life imprisonment - Appeals partly allowed.
