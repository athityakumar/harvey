T. C. Thangaraj and another v V. Engammal & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

29 July 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Patnaik, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 531; (2011) 12 SCC 328; 2011 CRLJ 4348; 2011 (3) RCR(Criminal) 751; 2011(8) SCALE 120; [2011] 9 S.C.R. 647

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 409, 420 and 471 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 482 - Complainant filed a complaint against appellants for offence punishable u/ss. 409, 420, 471 r/w s. 34 of the IPC alleging that she was cheated in a loan transaction of Rs.3 lakh by the three accused persons - However, as there was no progress in the investigation on the complaint, the complainant filed petition u/s. 482 of the CrPC with a prayer to entrust the case to the CBI for proper investigation - Complainant submitted that her complaint was not being investigated properly because one of the accused persons was an Inspector of Police - HC held that as the accused No.1 was an Inspector of Police, the investigating agency had not done its duty properly and under the circumstances, relief claimed by the complainant should be granted and accordingly ordered that Crime be entrusted to the CBI for investigation - Whether reasons given by the HC in the impugned order that the accused No.1 was an Inspector of Police and therefore the investigating agency had not done its duty properly, could be held to be good reasons for entrusting the investigation to the CBI? - Held, power of the HC u/art. 226 of the Constitution to direct investigation by the CBI was to be exercised only sparingly, cautiously and in exceptional situations and an order directing to CBI was not to be passed as a matter of routine or merely because a party had levelled some allegations against the local police - This was not one of the exceptional situations calling for exercise of extra-ordinary power of the HC to direct investigation into the complaint by the CBI - Impugned order of the HC quashed - Appeals allowed.
