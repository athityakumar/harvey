Rekha v State Of T.Nadu Tr.Sec.To Govt. & Anr

Supreme Court of India

05 April 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Markandey Katju, Surinder Singh Nijjar, Gyan Sudha Misra

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 233; (2011) 5 SCC 244; 2011 ALL MR (Cri) 2014; 2011 (3) CTC 222; JT 2011 (4) SC 392; 2011 (3) MLJ(Crl) 422; 2011 (2) OLR 147; 2011 (4) RCR(Criminal) 21; 2011(4) SCALE 387; [2011] 4 S.C.R. 740

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Tamil Nadu Prevention of Dangerous Activities of Bootleggers, Drug- offenders, Forest Offenders, Goondas, Immoral Traffic Offenders, Sand Offenders, and Slum Grabbers and Video Pirates Act, 1982 - Detention - Legality - Detenu was detained by a detention order passed under the Act, 1982, on the allegation that he was selling expired drugs after tampering with the labels and printing fresh labels showing them as non-expired drugs - Habeas corpus petition filed by the wife of the detenu challenging the said detention order was dismissed by the impugned order - Hence, instant appeal - Whether Detaining Authority was correct in observing that since there was possibility of detenue coming out on bail in the similar cases, there was a necessity to detain him in order to prevent him from indulging in such further activities in future? - Held, a mere ipse dixit statement in the grounds of detention could not sustain the detention order and had to be ignored - In the instant case, detention order in question only contains ipse dixit regarding the alleged imminent possibility of the accused coming out on bail and there was no reliable material to said effect - In the absence of details, statement was mere ipse dixit, and could not be relied upon - Further, the charge against the detenu was of selling expired drugs after changing their labels - Relevant provisions in the Indian Penal Code and the Drugs and Cosmetics Act were sufficient to deal with said situation - Hence, detention order in question was illegal on said ground also - Detention order in question could not be sustained and quashed - Appeals disposed of.
