Koka Suryanarayana Rao & Ors. v Landacquisition Offr. & Rev. Div. Offcr, A.P.

Supreme Court of India

17 March 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

V.S. Sirpurkar, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 258; (2011) 4 SCC 186; 2011 (4) AWC 3389; 2011 (2) CLT(SC) 450; JT 2011 (4) SC 123; 2011 (5) RCR(Civil) 207; 2011(3) SCALE 660; [2011] 5 S.C.R. 639

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, s. 18 - Lands belonging to the appellants were acquired for the purpose of construction of bus station - This was done on the basis of a requisition made by Andhra Pradesh State Road Transport Corporation (APSRTC) - Possession was taken and award was passed - Reference was made u/s. 18 of the Act by the appellants - Reference Court enhanced the compensation - In appeal, HC confirmed the said judgment - Land Acquisition Officer deposited the amount as awarded by the Reference Court - Claimants had filed execution petitions claiming compensation - In the execution petitions, the calculations made by the appellants regarding the decretal amount were accepted and the Land Acquisition Officer was directed to deposit the amounts in terms of the calculations made by the appellants (decree holders) - APSRTC filed civil revisions against the order of the Executing Court - Revision petitions were dismissed by the HC - Special Leave Petition was filed thereagainst was also dismissed - Thereafter, Land Acquisition Officer filed review petitions, which were allowed - Instant appeal - Appellants contended that HC was in complete error in allowing the civil revisions inasmuch as nothing was left in the said executions and the said execution had become final against the APSRTC - Held, there was no fault with the calculation memo which were approved by the HC - All the questions regarding the correctness of the calculation memos had also been finally closed - Also, once the APSRTC had chosen to challenge the calculation memos and had failed in that exercise, it was not permissible to the Land Acquisition Officer to challenge the very same orders, as Land Acquisition Officer was a party to all the proceedings - Appeals allowed.
