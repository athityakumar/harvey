Mustkeem @ Sirajudeen v State Of Rajasthan

Supreme Court of India

13 July 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Deepak Verma, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 436; (2011) 11 SCC 724; AIR 2011 SC 2769; 2011 CRLJ 4920; 2011 (3) JCC 2117; JT 2011 (7) SC 304; 2012 (1) MLJ(Crl) 151; 2011 (2) OLR 526; 2011 (3) RCR(Criminal) 766; 2011(7) SCALE 481; [2011] 9 S.C.R. 101

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302/34 - Arms Act, 1959, ss. 4/25 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 161 - Murder - Conviction - Challenged - Trial court convicted appellants guilty for offence punishable u/ss. 302/34 of IPC and u/ss. 3/25 of Act and awarded life imprisonment with fine of Rs. 1,000/- u/ss. 302/34 of IPC and 1 year rigorous imprisonment and fine of Rs. 500/- u/ss. 4/25 of Act - On appeal, HC upheld the order of the trial court - Hence, the instant appeals - Whether the trial Court and HC were justified in holding the appellants guilty for commission of the offences punishable u/ss. 302/34 of IPC and u/ss. 4/25 of the Act? - Held, where the case rests squarely on circumstantial evidence the inference of guilt can be justified only when all the incriminating facts and circumstances were found to be incompatible with the innocence of the accused or the guilt of any other person - In the impugned case, main material witnesses (PW1, PW2 and PW3) were declared hostile as they have retracted their statements made u/s. 161 CrPC during examination - Moreover, the recovery of the weapons on disclosure of the appellants itself becomes doubtful as recovery memos also reflect that there were overwriting on the same which had not been explained by the PW16 (Investigating Officer) - Hence, the evidence was vitiated by serious errors and if appellant's conviction was upheld on the basis of those evidences then it would amount to miscarriage of justice - Impugned order of trial court as affirmed by the HC set aside - Appeals allowed.
