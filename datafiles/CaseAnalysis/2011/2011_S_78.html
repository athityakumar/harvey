Shish Ram v Union Of India & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

23 November 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Patnaik, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 787; (2012) 1 SCC 290; 2012 (4) AWC 3291; 2012 (132) FLR 1; [2011] 13 S.C.R. 289; 2012 (1) SLR 653; 2012 (1) UPLBEC 18

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces

Keywords: Commander, Pension Regulations, 1961, Defence Services Regulations, 1961

Summary: Defence & Security Forces - Army Act, 1950, s. 20(3) - Defence Services Regulations, 1961, regn. 206 - Pension Regulations, 1961, regn. 113(a) - Dismissal - Pension Eligibility - Appellant, Army Personnal was transferred to reserve establishment wherein he was required to attend reservist training held from time to time -An intimation was sent to appellant to attend the biennial reservist training but he failed to attend the same - Respondent learnt that appellant was employed as a driver in Delhi Transport Corporation, letters were sent to appellant for furnishing the required exemption certificate exempting him from training but there was no response - Appellant was declared as a deserter and was eventually dismissed from service - Appellant filed Writ Petition challenging order of dismissal and claiming pension - HC dismissed said writ petition - Appellant filed appeal against said order contending that he was dismissed from service by brigade commander who had no authority to dismiss appellant from service - Appellant further contended that Authority who could dismiss appellant was the officer-in-charge of reservists u/regn. 206 of Defence Services Regulations, 1961 - (A) whether a brigade commander could dismiss appellant from service - Held, regn. 206 of Defence Services Regulations, 1961, show that a man, who was transferred to reserve, comes under administration and disciplinary orders of the Officer-in-Charge reservists -However, there was no mention in regn. 206 of Defence Services Regulations, 1961, that Officer-in-Charge reservists had power to either remove or dismiss a reservist from service - S. 20(3) of Act 1950 shows that an officer having power not less than a brigade or equivalent commander or any prescribed officer may dismiss or remove from the service any person serving under his command other than an officer or a Junior Commissioned Officer - Regn. 206 of Defence Services Regulations, 1961, could not take away the power vested under the Act 1961, in brigade commander to dismiss or remove any person working under him Hence, HC rightly held in impugned judgment that Brigade Commander had power to dismiss appellant from service - (B) Whether HC was right in rejecting claim of appellant for pension - Held, regn. 113(a) of Pension Regulations, 1960 was clear that an individual, who was dismissed under the provisions of Army Act, was ineligible for pension or gratuity in respect of all previous service - As appellant was dismissed from service under the provisions of Army Act, 1950, he was not eligible for pension and gratuity and HC was right in rejecting the claim of appellant for pension - Impugned judgement of HC confirmed - Appeal dismissed.
