Shiv Shankar Singh v State Of Bihar & Anr

Supreme Court of India

22 November 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 797; (2012) 1 SCC 130; 2012 ALL MR (Cri) 354; 2012 (1) OLR 165; 2012 (1) RCR(Criminal) 14; [2011] 13 S.C.R. 247

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 395 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 156 (3) and 173 - Dacoity - Protest Petition - Dacoity was committed at appellant's house and his nephew was killed and valuable properties were looted by dacoits - FIR was lodged u/ss. 396 and 398 of IPC and charge sheet was filed - 'X' (Appellant's brother) approached the court and filed a case u/s. 156(3) of Cr.P.C alleging that appellant killed his son with intention to grab his immovable property - While both FIR were pending, appellant filed Protest Petition, court did not pass any order - Appellant filed a second protest petition -Magistrate dismissed second petition - On appeal, HC set aside order of Magistrate - Appellant filed appeal against said order contending that HC failed to appreciate that the so-called first Protest Petition having been filed prior to filing the Final Report was not maintainable and just has to be ignored - Whether First Protest Petition filed prior to Final Report was maintainable or not or just to be ignored - Held, appellant along with others was facing criminal trial, proceedings cannot be initiated against respondent no.1 at his behest as registration of 2 FIRs in respect of same incident was not permissible in law - An order of dismissal u/s. 203 of Cr.P.C. was however, no bar to entertain second complaint on same facts but only in exceptional circumstances - In the instant case, HC without evidence set aside order of Magistrate on a technical ground that second Protest Petition was not maintainable without considering the fact that first Protest Petition was filed prior to filing of the Final Report was not competent - Impugned order of HC was set aside and order of Magistrate was restored - Directed respondent no.1 to appear before Magistrate - Appeal allowed.
