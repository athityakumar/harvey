Waman & Ors. v State Of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

29 June 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 463; (2011) 7 SCC 295; 2011 CRLJ 4827; JT 2011 (7) SC 24; 2011 (3) MLJ(Crl) 999; 2011 (4) RCR(Criminal) 503; 2011(6) SCALE 594; [2011] 6 S.C.R. 1072

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 147, 148, 149 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant accused allegedly formed an unlawful assembly and murdered the deceased - Trial Court convicted and sentenced the appellant u/s. 302, 147, 148, 149 IPC - HC upheld the Trial Court conviction and sentence - Hence the instant appeal - Whether the conviction and sentence recorded by the Trial Court against appellants was sustainable - Held, Trial Court on thorough analysis held that the prosecution had made out a case against the accused- appellants not only u/s. 302 r/w s. 149, the prosecution had very well established offences punishable u/s. 147, 148 and the accused A-1 to A-6 including A-12 used force and violence being members of unlawful assembly in prosecution of common object of causing death of deceased - Deadly weapons in the hands of appellants were axes, farshas, sticks, iron pipe etc - Though there was no recovery of weapon from A-12 but weapons had been recovered from other accused and prosecution witnesses had asserted that A-12 gave blow of iron pipe on deceased - Said iron pipe was recovered from the house of an accused which also proved that A-12 had participated in the offence with such weapon and therefore he was rightly punished along with other accused nos. 1-6 u/s. 148 for committing offence of rioting armed with deadly weapons - Prosecution established long standing land and water dispute among the deceased and the accused, the evidence of eye-witnesses PWs.1-4 were acceptable, contradictions were trivial in nature and medical evidence corroborated the assertion of prosecution witnesses - All those materials were correctly analysed and accepted by the Trial Court and affirmed by the HC - No error or infirmity or valid legal ground was found for interference in the order passed by the courts below - Appeal dismissed.
