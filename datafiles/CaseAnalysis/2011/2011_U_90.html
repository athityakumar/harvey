Sanjay Kumar Singh v Union of India and others

Supreme Court of India

06 September 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Mukundakam Sharma, A.R. Dave

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 621; (2011) 14 SCC 692; AIR 2012 SC 1783; 2012 (90) ALR 498; 2012 (3) AWC 2677; 2011 (131) FLR 1095; [2011] 11 S.C.R. 182; 2012 (1) SLR 98; 2012 (1) UPLBEC 668

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces; Service

Keywords: Central Reserve Police Force Rules, 1955

Summary: Service - Defence & Security Forces - Dismissal from Service - Departmental Proceedings - Validity - Appellants were dismissed from service by respondents on charges of disobedience of orders, committed gross misconduct and displayed cowardice in execution of their duties and in their capacity as members of CRPF - Single Judge dismissed appeal by holding that appellants were given all reasonable opportunity to defend themselves - On appeal, DB passed order confirming order of Single Judge - Hence, instant appeal - Appellants contended that violation of principles of natural justice in departmental proceedings as appellants were not given list of witnesses and that some witnesses were examined who were not even cited as witnesses in said list - Whether appellants were given all opportunities to defend themselves against dismissal from service - Held, appellant-escort made statement before party from battalion headquarter that appellant was provided with 40 rounds but appellant could fire only 14 rounds during said attack - Appellant-Head Constable left his wireless set in vehicle and took shelter in banana grove - Appellant-Driver had not been able to prove that his request for ammunition from Unit was placed before concerned authorities - Charge Sheet was issued much before 7 days as required to be done prior to holding of trial - Reading out of charge sheet could have been read out only when trial began, in order to find out whether appellants pleaded guilty to charges or not - Each one of appellants had specifically stated in inquiry proceedings about non-requirement of Defence Assistant - List of witnesses was also supplied to appellants along with charge sheet issued to them - Hence, no violation of principles of natural justice and that charges had been established against all appellants hence punishment awarded was not disproportionate to offences alleged - Appeal dismissed.
