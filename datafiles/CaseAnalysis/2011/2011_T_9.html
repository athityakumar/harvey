Deutsche Post Bank Home Finance Ltd v Taduri Sridhar & Anr

Supreme Court of India

29 March 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 197; (2011) 11 SCC 375; AIR 2011 SC 1899; 2011 (86) ALR 758; 2011 (3) AWC 3120; 2011 (2) CLT(SC) 7; 2011 (2) CompLJ 542; 2011 (4) CTC 472; JT 2011 (4) SC 45; 2011 (7) MLJ(SC) 385; 2011 (4) RCR(Civil) 655; 2011(4) SCALE 33; [2011] 5 S.C.R. 674

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, s. 11 - Second respondent (Developer) entered into a development agreement with the owners of lands for constructing certain buildings - Landowners, the developer and the first respondent who wanted to buy an apartment therein entered into an agreement for sale dated 16.10.2006 - Appellant (BHW Home Finance Ltd.) sanctioned a housing loan to the first respondent for purchase of the said apartment in terms of a loan agreement dated 21.12.2006 entered into between the first respondent as the borrower and the appellant as the lender - Land-owners and the developer also executed a registered sale deed dated 21.2.2008 - Subsequently, first respondent entrusted the construction of the unfinished flat to the developer under a construction agreement dated 21.2.2008 containing an arbitration clause - First respondent made a demand for damages against the developer - As the developer refused to comply, first respondent invoked the arbitration clause and sought reference of the disputes between them to arbitration - There was no response from the developer - Thereafter, the first respondent filed a petition u/s. 11 of the Act for appointment of an Arbitrator - In the said petition, the appellant was also impleaded as a respondent along with the developer - Appellant contended that the petition u/s. 11 of the Act was not maintainable against appellant, as the dispute was between the first respondent and the developer and it was not a party to the arbitration agreement invoked by the first respondent - HC allowed the application u/s. 11 - Hence, instant appeal - Whether appellant could be made a party to the arbitration, even though the appellant was not a party to the construction agreement containing the arbitration agreement dated 21.2.2008? - Held, existence of an arbitration agreement between the parties to the petition u/s. 11 of the Act and existence of dispute/s to be referred to arbitration were conditions precedent for appointing an Arbitrator u/s. 11 of the Act - If a person who was not a party to the arbitration agreement was impleaded as a party to the petition u/s. 11 of the Act, the court should either delete such party from the array of parties, or when appointing an Arbitrator make it clear that the Arbitrator was appointed only to decide the disputes between the parties to the arbitration agreement - In the instant case, appellant was not a party to the arbitration agreement which was relied upon by the first respondent to seek appointment of arbitrator - Further, disputes referred to in the petition u/s. 11 of the Act related to the claims of the First respondent against the developer - Hence, first respondent could not involve the appellant as a party to an arbitration in regard to his disputes arising out of the claims made by him against the developer which were covered by clause (7) of the construction agreement - Further, first respondent did not issue any notice or demand making any claim against the appellant nor did he issue any notice claiming that the appellant was liable for the consequences of non-performance by the developer, of its obligations - Nor did the first respondent issue any notice to the appellant seeking reference of any disputes to arbitration - Hence, it could not be said that any dispute existed between the first respondent and appellant, when the petition u/s. 11 of the Act was filed - Petition u/s. 11 of the Act against the appellant was therefore misconceived as the appellant was not a party to the construction agreement dated 21.2.2008 - Appeal allowed.
