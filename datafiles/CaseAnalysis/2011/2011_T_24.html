Rbf Rig Corporation, Mumbai v The Commissioner Of Customs (Imports), Mumbai

Supreme Court of India

08 February 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Dattu, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 82; (2011) 3 SCC 573; AIR 2012 SC (Supp) 176; 2011 (264) E.L.T. 486; JT 2011 (2) SC 489; 2011(2) SCALE 271; [2011] 2 S.C.R. 691

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax

Summary: Customs - Indirect Tax - Customs Act, 1962, s. 27 - Refund - The Appellant-Importer pursuant to contract with Oil and Natural Gas Corporation Ltd. (ONGC), had imported 3 consignments of spares and duly filed bills of entry in respect of the same - Said imported goods were covered under Notification No. 21/2002, Cus, which requires the importer to produce Essentiality Certificates issued by Director General of Hydrocarbons (DGH) - The DGH issued the Essentiality Certificates only on the strength of recommendatory letters issued by the ONGC - The appellant had requested ONGC to issue recommendatory letters in order to enable the DGH to issue the Essentiality Certificates, which were not granted - The appellant had cleared the said 3 consignments of the imported goods on full payment of the customs duty - The appellant filed a writ petition challenging the refusal of the ONGC to issue the requisite recommendatory letters and also the refusal of the DGH to issue the Essentiality Certificates - The High Court directed ONGC to take a final decision in the matter within a fixed time frame - Subsequently, ONGC issued recommendatory letters and on the strength of these recommendatory letters, the DGH issued Essentiality Certificates to the appellant - Accordingly, the appellant filed refund claim in respect of the customs duty paid on the import of the said 3 consignments, which was rejected by the Deputy Commissioner on the ground of unjust enrichment and failure to challenge the assessment of the bills of entry - The Commissioner rejected appeal filed against said order - The Tribunal also dismissed appeal filed against said order - Hence, the instant appeal - Whether the adjudicating authority was justified in rejecting the appellant's claim for refund of the duty paid under the Customs Act, 1962 without considering Essentiality Certificates, produced on a later date? - Held, the refund claim of the appellant had been erroneously rejected by the Deputy Commissioner ignoring the specific directions issued by the HC to the customs authorities to dispose of the appellant's claim of refund by taking into consideration the Essentiality Certificates issued by the DGH - The Deputy Commissioner had rejected the refund claim of appellant on the ground of unjust enrichment and failure to challenge the assessment of the bills of entry at the appellate stage, without even considering the Essentiality Certificates in the light of specific and binding directions of the HC - Impugned order was set aside - Appeal allowed.
