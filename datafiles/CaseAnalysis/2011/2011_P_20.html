Parmender Kumar & Ors. v State Of Haryana & Ors

Supreme Court of India

14 November 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Cyriac Joseph, Surinder Singh Nijjar

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 826; (2012) 1 SCC 177; 2012 (2) AWC 1228; 2011 (4) CLT(SC) 399; 2012 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 961; 2012 (1) MLJ(SC) 961; [2011] 14 S.C.R. 1065; 2012 (1) SLR 628

Case Digest

Subject: Education

Summary: Education - Admission - Change in eligibility criteria - Authority to issue instruction - Common entrance examination was held and results were declared and all appellants stood admitted along with similar candidates - Thereafter, Govt. of Haryana issued instruction, and changed eligibility conditions - Petitions were filed by appellant and others wherein Single Judge of HC passed interim order - Appellants filed Letters Patent Appeal, before DB of HC upheld order of Single Judge on ground that it was evident that State had every right to prescribe policy for grant of NOC - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether State Govt. has any authority to alter conditions relating to admission in Post-Graduate which had earlier been indicated in prospectus.

Held, once the process of selection had started on the basis of the terms and conditions included in the prospectus, was it within the competence of the State Govt. to effect changes in the criterion relating to eligibility for admission, when not only had the process in terms of the prospectus been started, but also when counselling was to be held on the very next day, which had the effect of eliminating many of the candidates from getting an opportunity of pursuing the Post-Graduate or Diploma Courses in the reserved HCMS category. However, it is no doubt true that benefits of admission in reserved category are many, but same is result of policy adopted by State Govt. to provide for candidates from reserved category and since appellants had been selected on basis of merit, in keeping with results of written examination, appellants have shown their competence by being selected on basis of their results in written examination. Since appellants were candidates in respect of reserved category of HCMS. Hence, judgment and order of DB of HC is set aside. Thus, Appellants shall be admitted in Post-Graduate or Diploma Courses, for which they have been selected, for new academic year without any further test or selection. Appeals allowed.
