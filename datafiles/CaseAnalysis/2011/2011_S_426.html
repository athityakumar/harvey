Murugan and others v State of Tamil Nadu and another

Supreme Court of India

06 May 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 320; (2011) 6 SCC 111; AIR 2011 SC 1691; 2011 ALL MR (Cri) 2023; 2011 (3) CGLJ 554; 2011 CRLJ 2948; JT 2011 (6) SC 602; 2011 (4) MLJ(Crl) 166; 2011 (3) RCR(Criminal) 35; 2011(5) SCALE 464; [2011] 5 S.C.R. 1189

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 109, 366 and 376 - Kidnapping - Rape - Conviction - Challenged - Appellant (A.1) was convicted u/ss. 366 and 376 of the IPC and other appellants stood convicted u/ss. 366 r/w s. 109 of IPC - On appeal, HC upheld that conviction of A.1 but set aside conviction of other appellants u/ss. 366 r/w s. 109 IPC and convicted them u/s. 363 r/w s. 109 of IPC - Hence, instant appeal - Appellants contended that the prosecutrix PW. 4 was major on the date of incident and therefore their conviction was liable to be set aside - Held, in the instant case, there was no reason to doubt the veracity of the birth certificate issued by the Municipality which showed that prosecutrix, PW.4 was a minor on the date of incident - Also the school certificate was issued by the Head Master on the basis of the entry made in the school register corroborated the contents of the certificate of birth issued by the Municipality - Both these entries in the school register as well, as in the Municipality came much before the criminal prosecution started and those entries stand fully supported and corroborated by the evidence of mother of the prosecutrix - No reason to hold that prosecutrix, PW.4 was major on the date of incident - Conviction and sentence upheld - Appeals dismissed.
