Jiten Kumar Sahoo & Ors v Chief General Manager Mahanadi Coalfields Ltd. & Ors

Supreme Court of India

27 January 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 59; (2011) 11 SCC 520; 2011 (8) ADJ 3; 2011 (88) ALR 3; 2011 (3) AWC 3041; 2011 (2) ESC 193; 2011 (130) FLR 321; 2011(1) SCALE 746; [2011] 1 S.C.R. 572; 2011 (7) SLR 615

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Appointment - Entitlement - Fresh vacancies of Mazdoor Category-I (ITI) were occurred in 1st respondent/Government Company - 1st respondent requested the local employment exchange for their permission to fill up fresh vacancies from amongst the candidates who had qualified in the written test and the trade test conducted by it on earlier occasion for the same post as per the recommendation of employment exchange - Since there was no response from the local employment exchange, respondent filled up the fresh vacancies by giving employment to those candidates who had already undergone the apprenticeship with them - Respondent/candidates, who were in the earlier merit list, filed writ petitions challenging the appointment of candidates (including appellant) who had undergone apprenticeship with the 1st respondent - HC, by impugned order, held that 1st respondent ought to have filled up the fresh posts from the merit list prepared earlier strictly in the order of merit and no preference could have been given to those who had undertaken apprenticeship with 1st respondent - Hence the present appeals - Whether the appellants are entitled to continue in their posts - Held, appellants impleaded as respondents before the HC after ten years of filing the writ petition, however, in the meantime, they got promoted from Mazdoor Category-I to Mazdoor Category-II and then to Mazdoor Category-III and thereafter to the posts of Fitter - HC unfortunately failed to take into consideration the material aspects stated in the counter affidavit filed by the appellants - On promotion of the appellants to the higher posts, other candidates were appointed to the posts of Mazdoor Category-I in place of the appellants - If the order of the HC is allowed to stand, it would not only affect the appellants who, during the continuation of their service, had got three promotions, but also will seriously affect the persons who have been appointed in their place and were not impleaded before the HC - Impugned HC order, therefore, is set aside - Appeals allowed.
