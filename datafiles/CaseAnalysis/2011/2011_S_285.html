Ghure And Ors v State Of Rajasthan

Supreme Court of India

17 June 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, Swatanter Kumar

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 387; (2011) 11 SCC 694; AIR 2011 SC 2268; 2011 CRLJ 3941; 2011 (5) RCR(Criminal) 768; 2011(6) SCALE 570; [2011] 6 S.C.R. 1062

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 395, 396, 397, 120B and 412 IPC - Arms Act, 1959, ss. 3/25 and 3/27 - Dacoity - Conviction - Challenged - Trial court convicted all the accused u/ss. 395, 396 and 397 of IPC and awarded them life imprisonment and a fine of Rs. 1,000/- - u/ss. 395 and 396 of IPC and rigorous imprisonment for 7 years and a fine of Rs.500/- u/s. 397 of IPC - Trial court also convicted accused nos. 1, 2, and 3 u/ss. 3/25 and 3/27 of the Arms Act and awarded sentence of 3 years rigorous imprisonment and a fine of Rs. 500/- - On appeal, HC acquitted the accused nos. 4 and 5 though maintained the conviction and sentence in respect of other accused - Hence, the instant appeal - Whether order of the HC could be upheld? - Held, there were concurrent findings of fact so far as the involvement and participation of all the accused were concerned - They had been rightly identified in the Test Identification Parade as well as in the Court of the witnesses - More so, recoveries made at the instances of the accused stood proved by examining the panel witnesses - No cogent reason to take a contrary view taken by the courts below - Moreover, accused could not point any material discrepancy in the statements of the witnesses which goes to the root of the case - Nor could they point out that how the judgment impugned requires any interference - Impugned order of the HC upheld - Appeal dismissed.
