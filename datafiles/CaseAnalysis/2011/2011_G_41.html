Reva Electric Car Company Private Limited v Green Mobil

Supreme Court of India

25 November 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Surinder Singh Nijjar

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 813; (2012) 2 SCC 93; AIR 2012 SC 739; 2012 (1) AWC 754; 2011 (4) CLT(SC) 547; 2012 (3) MahLJ 523; 2012 (2) MPLJ 497; 2012 (4) RCR(Civil) 574; [2011] 13 S.C.R. 359

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Contract & Commercial - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, ss. 11(4) and (6) and 16(1) - Appointment of arbitrator - Contractual relationship - Termination - Damages - Sustainability - Petitioner/ manufacturer and respondent/ distributor entered into an MOU for supply of Cars - Disputes arose between parties - Petitioner terminated MOU and requested respondent to immediately cease sales and marketing activities on its behalf - Respondent initiated legal proceedings claiming damages on account of termination of MOU - Petitioner issued notice invoking clause 11 of MOU referring all disputes to arbitration, respondent replied denying contractual relationship between parties on date of termination - Petitioner filed suit in Principal City Civil and Sessions Judge for injunction restraining respondent from proceeding with legal proceedings - Thereafter, petitioner filed instant application for appointment of arbitrator - Petitioner contended that MOU was effective for a period of 3 to 6 months from date of arrival of cars and parties were to mutually decide to continue marketing, sales and service of work hours by respondent and it was sole discretion of petitioner to extend MOU if he believes additional time was required to complete trial period - Respondent had admitted contractual relationship and also about non-existence of subsequent agreement contending that MOU had come to an end by efflux of time and there was no question of any termination and notice invoking arbitration was sent only as counterblast to proceedings initiated by respondent - Whether valid arbitration agreement existed between the parties - Held, there was no dispute that parties had entered into a legally valid and enforceable MOU and also no dispute that Clause 11 provides disputes arising between parties, at any time, in relation to MOU, shall be referred to arbitration and clause (2) provides that petitioner may unilaterally decide to extend the MOU, if it considers necessary - Correspondence between parties shows that petitioner had proposed a draft distribution agreement to the respondent for discussion, but making it apparent that no final consensus was reached, therefore the MOU was duly extended till it was terminated - Disputes that had arisen between parties clearly relate to subject matter of relationship between parties which came into existence through MOU, therefore disputes raised needs to be referred to arbitration - Former Judge of SC was appointed as sole arbitrator to adjudicate disputes - Petition disposed of.
