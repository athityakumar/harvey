Valliyammal and another etc. v Special Tahsildar (Land Acquisition) and another etc.

Supreme Court of India

01 August 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, H.L. Dattu

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 583; (2011) 8 SCC 91; 2011 (6) ALD(SC) 13; 2011(5) ALL MR 933; 2011 (6) AWC 5556; 2011 (5) Bom.C.R. 284; 2012 (4) CLT(SC) 449; 2011 (7) MLJ(SC) 761; 2011 (3) RCR(Civil) 903B; 2011(8) SCALE 139; [2011] 10 S.C.R. 293

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Keywords: Valuation Of Land

Summary: Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 41 r. 7 -

Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 18(1), 4(1), 54 - Valuation of land - Potential value - Land acquired - Validity - Govt. issued notification for acquisition of land comprised in different survey numbers of village for construction of houses by Board - Land Acquisition Officer fixed market value of acquired land at rate of Rs.50,000/- per acre - Appellants filed applications u/s. 18(1) of the Act and claimed compensation by asserting that acquired land was situated near residential colonies and was having potential for being used for housing and business purposes - Collector made reference to Court for determination of compensation payable to appellants - Reference Court concluded that appellants were entitled to compensation - Appellants and respondents filed appeals u/s. 54 of the Act - HC allowed applications and directed Reference Court to give opportunity to parties to adduce additional evidence and make fresh determination of compensation payable to appellants and remit its findings along with documents - Reference Court opined that appellants were entitled to compensation - HC reduced market value fixed by Reference Court from Rs.19.28 to Rs.12/- and from Rs.20/- to Rs.8/- per square feet - Hence, instant appeal - Whether Court determination of market value of acquired land was justified - Held, HC erred related to deduction of 40% towards development charges and ignored that acquired land was situated in vicinity of residential colonies developed by Board and other establishments and that respondents had not produced any evidence to show that they would have to start development work from scratch - Court had repeatedly held that exercise undertaken for fixing market value and determination of compensation payable to landowner should involve consideration of escalation in land prices - HC did not add 10% escalation per annum in land prices - Sustained 20% deduction keeping in view smallness of plots which were sold it would be unjust to allow deduction - Majority of appellants were deprived of their entire landholding and they had waited for 14 to 20 years for getting compensation - It appeared that in compliance of interim orders passed by Court, appellants did get 25% and one of them got 35% of compensation, but majority of them had not received single penny towards compensation and at distant point of time, it would be unjust to deprive appellants of their legitimate right by approving 20% deduction made by HC - Appeal allowed.
