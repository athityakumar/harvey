Jalpat Rai and others v State of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

06 July 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 541; (2011) 14 SCC 208; AIR 2011 SC 2719; 2011 ALL MR (Cri) 2898; 2011 CRLJ 4295; JT 2011 (8) SC 55; 2011 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 2463; 2011(7) SCALE 348; [2011] 7 S.C.R. 1037

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Supreme Court (Enlargement of Criminal Appellate Jurisdiction) Act, 1970

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss.148,149,302 and 307 - Arms Act, 1959, s.27 - Supreme Court ( Enlargement of Criminal Appellate Jurisdiction ) Act, 1970, s. 2 - Conviction - Sustainability - Deceased was fired by appellants/convicted persons with fire arms - State/ respondent approached Trial Court which convicted A2 u/s.302 IPC and acquitted A1, A3, A4 and A5 - Aggrieved, by Trial Court's order 2 appeals and revision were filed before HC which allowed appeal of State/respondent and convicted A1, A3, A4, A5 and A6 u/ss. 148, 302 r/w s. 149 IPC - A5 was also convicted u/s. 323 IPC these five accused had been sentenced to undergo imprisonment for life and fine of Rs. 10,000/- with default stipulation was also imposed on them - Insofar as A2 was concerned, HC modified his conviction from s. 302 r/w. s. 149 IPC while maintaining sentence awarded to him by Trial Court- Hence, instant appeal - Whether Lower Court's judgment was sustainable - Held, ballistic evidence was clearly in conflict with evidence of PW1, PW4 and PW8 and shatters their evidence completely vis appellants and testimony of PW1, PW4 and PW8 about role of appellants was not corroborated by medical and ballistic evidence - Their evidence also did not get support from collateral circumstances that had come on record - SLP preferred by A2 was no nest since he had right of appeal u/s. 2 of the 1970 Act - Therefore, order of Court dismissing SLP preferred by A2 was also no nest - Case against A2 stands on different footing that ballistic evidence was conclusive against him and leaves no manner of doubt about his involvement in crime - Further in this regard as SLP preferred by A2 against his conviction had already been dismissed - Judgment of HC as regards present appellants was set aside and judgment of acquittal passed in their favour by Trial Court was restored - Hence, A1 and A6 were already on bail and accordingly their bail bonds were discharged and other appellantsA3,A4 and A5 be released forthwith, if not required in any other case - Appeals allowed.
