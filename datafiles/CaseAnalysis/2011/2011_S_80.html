The Secretary, Sh. A. P. D.Jain Pathshala & Ors. v Shivaji Bhagwat More & Ors

Supreme Court of India

04 July 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 417; (2011) 13 SCC 99; 2011(5) ALL MR 460; JT 2011 (7) SC 534; 2011(7) SCALE 83; [2011] 6 S.C.R. 1173; 2011 (4) SLR 687

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Shikshan Sevak Scheme

Summary: Service|Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 9 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 162, 233, 234, 247, 311 - Maharashtra Employees of Private Schools (Conditions of Services) Regulation Act, 1977 - Constitution - Service - Education - Govt. by Resolution accorded sanction for implementation of Shikshan Sevak scheme in recognized private secondary/higher secondary schools/Junior colleges/B.Ed. colleges, in State - HC disposing various writs recommending modifications to said scheme, in its order, issued specific directions making significant changes in constitution and functioning of committee - Appellants appointed respondent no. 1 as Shikshan Sevak and services of respondent no.1 came to end as his appointment was not approved due to lack of prescribed qualifications - Respondent challenged his termination by filing appeal before Grievance Committee - Appellants raised various preliminary objections about maintainability of complaint and as Grievance Committee did not consider them, appellants filed writ seeking direction to Grievance Committee to decide preliminary issues - HC did not stay proceedings before Grievance Committee - Therefore, Committee proceeded and allowed appeal and quashed termination and directed appellants to reinstate respondent no.1 - Appellants filed challenging order and HC refused to stay order of Grievance Committee - Respondent no.1 filed writ petition seeking direction to appellants to implement order dated passed by Grievance Committee - HC while issuing notice directed Education officer to ensure compliance by appellants, of order passed by Grievance Committee forthwith, unless said order was challenged and stay obtained - Under Scheme, as originally formulated by State Government by Government Resolution Grievance Committee was merely mechanism to hear grievances of Shikshan Sevaks and give its recommendation to Education Department, so that department could take appropriate action - Grievance Committee was not intended to be quasi-judicial forum - HC while recommending various modifications to said scheme, in its order dated, issued specific directions making significant changes in constitution and functioning of committee - Appellants contend that constitution of such quasi judicial tribunal, by judicial fiat to state government, was without authority of law and invalid, and consequently, decisions by such forum were void and unenforceable - Hence, instant appeals - (A) Whether HC could direct State Govt. to create quasi judicial forum; and whether creation of such forum by executive order, by State Govt., in pursuance of such a direction, was valid - Held, neither Constitution nor any statute empowers HC to create or constitute quasi judicial Tribunals for adjudicating disputes - It had no legislative powers nor could it direct executive branch of State Govt. to create or constitute quasi judicial Tribunals, otherwise than by legislative Statutes - Therefore, it was not permissible for HC to direct State Govt. to constitute judicial authorities or Tribunals by executive orders, nor permissible for State by executive order or resolution create them for adjudication of rights of parties - (B) Whether HC could, by judicial order, exclude jurisdiction of Civil Courts to entertain any suits or applications in respect of disputes raised by Shikshan Sevaks - Held, s. 9 CPC provides that Courts should, subject to provisions of Code, had jurisdiction to try all suits of civil nature excepting suits of which their cognizance was either expressly or impliedly barred - Express or implied bar necessarily refers to bar created by Code itself or by any statute made by Legislature - Therefore, HC in exercise of power of judicial review, could not issue direction that Civil Courts should not entertain any suit or application in regard to a particular type of disputes (in this case, disputes relating to Shikshan Sevaks) nor create exclusive jurisdiction in a quasi judicial forum like the Grievance Committee will be entitled to deal with them - HC, could not by judicial order, nullify, supersede or render ineffectual express provisions of an enactment - Therefore any such Grievance Committee created by executive order, either on direction of HC or otherwise, can only be fact finding bodies or recommending bodies which could look into grievances and make appropriate recommendations to Govt. or its authorities, for taking necessary actions or appropriate reports to enable judicial Tribunals to render decisions - Grievance Committee cannot be public quasi-judicial forum nor can its decisions be made final and binding on parties, in disputes relating to Shikshan Sevaks - Hence, any order or opinion of Grievance Committee on complaint or grievance submitted by a Shikshan Sevak were only recommendations to State Government (Education Department) for taking further action and nothing more - Appeals allowed.
