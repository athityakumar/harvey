Chandigarh Administration through the Director Public Instructions (Colleges), Chandigarh v Usha Kheterpal Waie and others

Supreme Court of India

02 September 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 617; (2011) 9 SCC 645; 2011 (5) ESC 799; 2012 (2) MLJ(SC) 468; [2011] 12 S.C.R. 398; 2011 (6) SLR 645

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Service

Keywords: Chandigarh Educational Service (Group A Gazetted) Government Arts and Science College Rules, 2000, Punjab Educational Service (College Grade) (Class I) Rules, 1976

Summary: Service - Administrative - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 309 - Chandigarh Educational Service (Group A Gazetted) Government Arts and Science College Rules, 2000 - Appellant had notified the 2000 Rules, vide notification dt. 29-3-2000 - Recruitment Rules prescribed educational qualification of Ph.D. degree for appointment to post of Principal by direct recruitment - Appellant issued advertisement for post of Principal based on qualifications prescribed under Recruitment Rules - Respondents 1 to 4, who were serving as lecturers in Govt. Arts and Science Colleges, challenged Recruitment Rules before Central Administrative Tribunal ('Tribunal') as unconstitutional and prayed for direction to consider them for promotion to said post - Tribunal allowed application and quashed advertisement dt. 14-7-2001 inviting applications for post of Principal - Order of Tribunal was challenged by appellant before HC - HC affirmed findings of Tribunal - Hence, instant appeal - (A) Whether appellant was entitled to prescribe educational qualifications for post of Principal in advertisement as per Recruitment Rules - Held, appellant had clear intention to enforce Recruitment Rules in future as they had been made in consultation with UPSC, in accordance with UGC guidelines and Rules had been sent to Central Govt. for being notified by President and matter was pending consideration for few months when advertisement was issued - In exercise of its executive power, appellant could issue administrative instructions from time to time in regard to all matters which were not governed by any statute or rules made under Constitution or statute - Recruitment Rules made by Administrator were duly notified - Hence, advertisement issued in terms of said Recruitment Rules was valid - (B) Whether appellant could have prescribed Ph.D. degree as eligibility qualification for direct recruitment of Principals - Held, Recruitment Rules were made in consultation with UPSC, to give effect to UGC guidelines which prescribed Ph.D. degree as eligibility qualification for direct recruitment of Principals - When said qualification was not unrelated to duties and functions of post of Principal and was reasonably relevant to maintain high standards of education, there was absolutely no reason to interfere with provision of said requirement as eligibility requirement - Courts and Tribunals could neither prescribe qualifications nor entrench upon power of concerned authority so long as qualifications prescribed by employer was reasonably relevant and had rational nexus with functions and duties - Posts of Principal were filled by UT cadre lecturers without Ph.D. degree in earlier occasions would not authorise subsequent notifications on same qualifications - No dispute regarding notification dt. 13-1-1992 issued by appellant, adopting corresponding Punjab Rules governing conditions of service of its employees - Hence, prescription of Ph.D. degree for post of Principal was not unreasonable - Appeal allowed.
