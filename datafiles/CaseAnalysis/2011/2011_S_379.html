State of M. P. v Ramesh and another

Supreme Court of India

18 March 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 847; 2011 (3) JCC 1550

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Child Witness, Rape, FIR, Indian Evidence Act, 1872, Age, Place Of Occurrence, Version, Corroboration, Reliance, Oath, Dealing, Deposition, Haemorrhage, Indian Oaths Act, 1873, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Husband, Constitution of India, 1950, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120B, 302, 376 - Murder - Acquittal - Sustainability - Respondents/accused allegedly committed murder of deceased - Trial Court by judgment and order convicted accused u/s. 302 r/w s. 120B IPC and sentenced them to life imprisonment - On appeal, HC allowed appeal and acquitted respondents - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, an inference as to whether child was tutored or not could be drawn from the contents of his deposition. Further, statement of PW1 was affirmed by the statements of other witnesses, proved circumstances and medical evidence. Her deposition being precise, concise, specific and vivid without any improvement or embroidery was worth acceptance in toto. So far as the deposition of DW2 father of accused was concerned he was 80 years of age at the time of examination and not the resident of same village. He has deposed only on the basis of the information he had received from his daughter and was not of any help to the defence no reason to believe the theory put forward by defence. All the witnesses examined by prosecution including those who have turned hostile were neighbours of deceased. Thus, they were the most natural witnesses and Trial Court has rightly placed reliance on their testimonies. HC has completely ignored the most material incriminating circumstances which appeared against respondents. The findings recorded by HC are contrary to the evidence on record and was perverse. Impugned judgment of HC was set aside and that of Trial Court was restored. Appeal allowed.
