Tej Bir And Another v State Of Haryana And Another

Supreme Court of India

14 February 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Ganguly, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 344; (2011) 11 SCC 556; AIR 2012 SC 943; 2012 ALL MR (Cri) 2110; 2012 CRLJ 1488; JT 2011 (6) SC 376; 2011 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 3267; 2012 (1) MLJ(Crl) 510; 2012 (3) RCR(Criminal) 308; 2011(5) SCALE 250

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120B and 307 - Arms Act, 1959, s. 27 and 30 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 401 - Quashing of charges - Legality - Charges were framed against 'K' are, inter alia, u/s. 120B r/w s. 307 r/w s. 34 of IPC and ss. 27 r/w s. 30 of the Arms Act - 'K' preferred an application u/s. 401 of the CrPC before the HC, whereby the HC quashed the order of Additional Sessions Judge with respect to the charges framed against 'K' inter alia on the ground that there was no evidence against him - Hence, instant appeal - Held, FIR could not represent the entire evidence of the case - Further, at the stage of framing of charges the HC should not exercise its power of revision by way of quashing the charges by confining its attention only to the recitals in the FIR - In the instant case, even though in the FIR, a reference was made to 'K' as masterminding of the conspiracy, the HC should have refrained itself from quashing the charges by just referring to the recitals in the FIR - Judgment of the HC in quashing the charge was set aside and charge framed was restored - Appeal allowed.
