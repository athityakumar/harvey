Dhanraj Singh Choudahry v Nathulal Vishwakarma

Supreme Court of India

08 December 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, H.L. Gokhale

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 789; (2012) 1 SCC 741; AIR 2012 SC 628; 2012 (91) ALR 246; 2012 (2) ALT 1; 2012 (1) AWC 579; 2012 (1) CLT(SC) 103; 2012 (1) KLT 16; 2012 (2) MLJ(SC) 474; 2012 (2) MPLJ 263; 2012 (1) OLR 199; 2012 (1) RCR(Civil) 594; 2012 (115) RD 777; [2011] 16 S.C.R. 240; [2012] 204 TAXMAN 124

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Practice & Procedure - Professional misconduct - Attestation - Sale deed - Complainant filed complaint against appellant (Advocate) alleging professional misconduct - Complainant alleged that he had already filed suit for specific performance of agreement which was pending and was within the knowledge of appellant - Complainant also alleged that appellant on the contrary had instituted suit on behalf of vendor's father against complainant for eviction of shop showing complainant as tenant - Disciplinary Committee of the State Bar Council held the appellant guilty of professional mis-conduct and awarded punishment of reprimand to appellant - On appeal, Disciplinary Committee of the Bar Council of India ordered appellant suspension from practice for 1 year - Cross-appeal preferred by appellant was dismissed - Appellant filed appeal against said order contending that he had compromised with complainant so appeals should be disposed of accordingly Whether argument of appellant could be accepted - Held, settlement with complainant would not mitigate or wipe out professional misconduct and would not prevent adequate punishment to advocate - Moreover, punishment for professional misconduct had twin objectives i.e. - deterrence and correction - Hence, appellant be suspended from practice for period of 3 months - Appeals dismissed.
