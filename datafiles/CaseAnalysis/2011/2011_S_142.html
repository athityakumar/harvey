M/S Hyderabad Engineering Industries v State Of Andhra Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

04 March 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Dattu, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 146; (2011) 4 SCC 705; AIR 2012 SC (Supp) 201; JT 2011 (3) SC 117; 2011(3) SCALE 268; [2011] 3 S.C.R. 546; [2011] 39 VST 257

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Keywords: Finding of fact, Assessment Year, Supply Of Goods, Transfer Of Documents Of Title, Indian Sale Of Goods Act, 1930, Manufacturing Unit

Summary: Sales Tax - Indirect Tax - Andhra Pradesh General Sales Tax Act, 1957 - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956, s. 3(a) - A/y 1981-82 - Taxability - Inter-State Trade - Assessee engaged in the manufacture and sale of electrical fans, sewing machines, fuel injection parts and accessories etc., entered into an agreement with 'A' under which 'A' has to organize the sale and distribution of the products of the assessee and to arrange for sale promotion measures of the products and to provide after sales service - Said agreement also envisaged that A' would purchase the said products as an independent principal and maintain adequate stocks and sell the same as such - Assessee filed annual return under 1956 Act claiming exemption on a turnover of Rs. 8,87,75,643/- towards goods transported to out-of-state depots - Said claim was negatived by the Assessing Authority - Said order of the Assessing authority was confirmed by the Tribunal and the HC - Hence, the present appeal - Whether the sale or purchase of goods can be said to have taken place in the course of inter-State trade or commerce and thereby exigible to tax under the Central Sales Tax Act, 1956? - Held, when the sale or agreement for sale causes or has the effect of occasioning the movement of goods from one State to another, irrespective of whether the movement of goods is provided for in the contract of sale or not, or when the order is placed with any branch office or the head office which resulted in the movement of goods, irrespective of whether the property in the goods passed in one State or the other, if the effect of such a sale is to have the movement of goods from one State to another, an inter-State sale would ensue and would result in exigibility of tax u/s. 3(a) of the 1956 Act on the turnover of such transaction - In the instant case, the assessing authority and the Tribunal have recorded a finding of fact that there were prior contracts between 'A' and the assessee and in pursuance of those contracts, the goods moved from the assessee's factory to its Branch offices to be delivered to 'A' or their nominees - Moreover, the assessing officer, in his detailed and well considered order, had looked into nearly 378 documents and voluminous correspondence between the assessee and 'A' and had discussed and co-related the documents to prove on facts that the disputed transaction was inter-State sales - Therefore, the impugned transactions were inter-State sales taxable under the Central Act - Appeal dismissed.
