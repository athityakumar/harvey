Krishan Lal Gera v State Of Haryana & Ors

Supreme Court of India

04 July 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, H.L. Gokhale

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 414; (2011) 10 SCC 529; 2011(5) ALL MR 959; 2011 (6) AWC 5787; 2011 (4) RCR(Civil) 115B; 2011(7) SCALE 105; [2011] 7 S.C.R. 722

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Stadium - Illegal activities - PIL - Maintainability - Appellant filed PIL before HC alleging that instead of using leased premises part of stadium complex, for sports and sports related activities, it was being used for illegal activities, lease deed in favour of club barred subletting or transfer of premises in violation thereof, premises had been sub-let under guise of a licence that licensee Modern Tent House in violation of lease terms constructed several permanent structures including pandals and rooms (for godown, generator etc.) - Swimming pool constructed by DCA Club had been given on 15 year lease at a throwaway rent implying that other amounts were received by the committee members, which was not being accounted - Further, that funds were misused by corrupt members of Executive Committee who were least interested in fulfilling objects of club - Appellant prayed for direction to respondents 1 to 3 to cancel the sub-lease/licence and the open area in front of it and also sub-lease/licence of swimming pool and to stop usage of premises for purposes of private functions, gambling and illegal activities etc - Respondents on denied allegation that illegal activities were carried on in premises - DB dismissed petition by single line order 'No public interest was involved in instant petitionrelying on decision in Jayalalitha v. Government of Tamil Nadu 1998 Indlaw SC 1231, appellant contends that public interest litigation was in fact maintainable in event of a stadium intended for public use, meant for sports activities was misused or not properly maintained - Hence, instant appeal - Whether HC was justified in dismissing PIL as there was no public interest involved - Held, Govt. stadium, being prime land in heart of city meant for developing sports and athletics was misused or illegally allowed to go into private hands, it could not be said that no public interest was involved - HC were bound to interfere and protect public interest when blatant misuse was brought to their notice - HC should direct concerned authorities to perform their duties and take action in regard to irregularities, omissions and negligence, so that interest of public, particularly human resources development, could be protected - Development of sports infrastructure did not mean spending hundreds of crores for infrastructure for some international event and then allowing entire infrastructure to go waste, but to ensure continuous and effective use of those facilities and provide adequate maintenance and upkeep - Hence, set aside order of HC, remand PIL to HC to dispose of matter in accordance with law, in particular with reference to issues enumerated - Appeal allowed.
