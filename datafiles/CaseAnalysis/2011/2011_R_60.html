Delhi Development Authority v Ram Prakash

Supreme Court of India

15 March 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 167; (2011) 4 SCC 180; AIR 2011 SC 1399; JT 2011 (3) SC 401; 2011 (3) KarLJ 155; 2011 (3) RCR(Civil) 511; 2011(3) SCALE 563; [2011] 5 S.C.R. 606

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Municipalities & Local Governments - Land & Property - Misuser charges - Demand - Legality - Respondent purchased demised premises from petitioner Development Authority in an open auction and entered into a lease agreement with it - Petitioner sent several show-cause notice to respondent alleging that basement of demised premises was being misused for office instead of storage, hence cl. II(13) of lease agreement had been violated - Respondent denied allegation of change of user and informed petitioner that they had referred the matter to tenants using demised premises - Petitioner demanded misuser charges from respondent - Respondent challenged demand before HC - HC quashed demand - Hence instant appeal - Whether demand of misuser charges raised by petitioner was legal - Held, respondent took prompt steps against tenants for their misuse of demised property and tenants had already vacated portion of premises occupied by them - Further, petitioner did not take any follow-up action after issuance of show-cause notice, however, after a lapse of 25 years, it set up a claim on account of charges for entire period - It would be inequitable to allow petitioner which had sat over the matter to take advantage of its inaction in claiming misuser charges - What would construe a reasonable time depending upon facts and circumstances of each case, but it would not be fair to respondent if such demand was allowed to be raised after 25 years on account of inaction of petitioner - No interference on impugned HC order - Petition dismissed.
