Suresh Dhanuka v Sunita Mohapatra

Supreme Court of India

02 December 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Surinder Singh Nijjar, Gyan Sudha Misra

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 758; (2012) 1 SCC 578; AIR 2012 SC 892; 2012 (3) ALD(SC) 41; 2012 (1) ALT 49; 2011 (4) CLT(SC) 492; 2012 (49) PTC 417; [2011] 15 S.C.R. 512

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Intellectual Property

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Intellectual Property - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, s. 9 - Trade Marks Act, 1999, ss. 134 and 135 - Specific Relief Act, 1963, s. 42 - Indian Contract Act, 1872, s. 27 - Trade mark - Cancellation - Arbitration clause - Appellant and respondent entered into an agreement to jointly carry on business - Respondent applied to Registrar of trade marks for registration of title of company - Respondent executed a deed assignment assigning 50% of her right, title and interest in trade mark under terms and conditions - Appellant and his son floated a company under similar title - Appellant resigned from company despite the fact that company was not started manufacturing activities - Respondent filed suit u/ss. 134 and 135, the 1999 Act before District Court - District Court passed an exparte order restraining appellant company from selling, distributing, manufacturing and marketing any of products under title in dispute - Respondent filed application u/s. 9 of 1996 Act before District Court - Appellant also filed an application u/s. 9 of 1996 Act, meantime respondent cancelled agreement and deed of assignment - Appellant application was dismissed on account of earlier application filed by respondent - Appellant filed fresh application u/s. 9 of 1996 Act, before District Judge - District Judge passed interim order restraining respondent from selling products in question - Respondent preferred appeal before HC, while matter was pending, respondent filed a letter with Trade Mark Authority praying for cancellation of order allowing request of appellant resulting in issuance of Corrigendum in Trade Mark Journal - Assistant Registrar of Trade Marks cancelled Corrigendum without notice to appellant and same was published in Trade Mark Journal - HC allowed appeal filed by respondent - Appellant filed review application against HC order, which was rejected by HC - Hence, instant appeal - (A) Whether HC was justified in interfering with order passed by District Judge and Whether, pending arbitration proceedings, an order could have been passed by which right acquired by appellant under deed of assignment could have been suspended and restrained from objecting to use of said Mark by respondent - Held, terms of deed of assignment clearly indicated that respondent had of her own volition parted with 50% of her right, title and interest in Trade Mark with proportional goodwill of business from date of deed - Further, in view of provisions of deed of assignment, appellant was no longer entitled to benefits of Trade Mark which was transferred to him to the extent of 50% absolutely and forever, hence, order passed by District Judge, restraining respondent from marketing products through any person, other than appellant was more apposite and as the rights of both parties stood protected till such time as a final decision could be taken in arbitral proceedings - (B) Whether HC was justified in relying upon affidavit filed on behalf of respondent after hearing was concluded and judgment was reserved in appeal, without giving opportunity to appellant - Held, it was inappropriate on part of HC to allow respondent to file affidavit, on which reliance was placed, after hearing was concluded and judgment was reserved, without giving appellant an opportunity of dealing with same it would have been prudent on part of HC to be given an opportunity to appellant to deal with same before allowing it to be taken on record - (C) Whether invocation of s. 42, 1963 Act to enforce negative covenant contained in deed of assignment was contrary to provisions of s. 27, 1872 Act and was, therefore, void - Held, injunction sought for by appellant was not to restrain respondent from carrying on trade or business, but from using Trade Mark which was subject matter of dispute, accordingly provisions of s. 27, 1872 Act would not be attracted to facts of instant case - Further, it was not a case where money could be an adequate compensation, since appellant was apparently acquired 50% interest in Trade Mark in question, together with goodwill of business in which Trade Mark was used - Hence, Single Judge order was set aside and District Judge order was restored - Appeal allowed.
