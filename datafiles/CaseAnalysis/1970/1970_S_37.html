Shivappa and Others v State of Mysore

Supreme Court of India

19 February 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, A.N. Ray, I.D. Dua

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 108; (1970) SCC (Cr) 215; (1970) SCC (Cr) 235; (1970) 1 SCC 487; AIR 1971 SC 196; 1973 CRLJ 260; [1970] 3 S.C.R. 720

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Evidence Act, 1872 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 395, 411, 412 - Theft by dacoity - Accused were convicted on sole evidence of having in their possession pieces of cloth which were later identified to belong to traders - High Court, and Court below drew conclusion that appellants were themselves dacoits, and convicted them accordingly u/s. 395 of IPC and sentenced them to '5 years' rigorous imprisonment - Appellants only found in possession of stolen property, whether sufficient to convict for dacoity? - Held, appellants found in possession only within short period of offence, presumption of dacoity can be inferred - It is legitimate therefore to raise presumption in this case that persons with whom goods were found were dacoits themselves - Sentence reduced to 3 yrs - Appeal partly allowed.
