Jadunath Singh and Another v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

07 December 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri, Vishishtha Bhargava, I.D. Dua

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 397; (1970) 3 SCC 518; (1971) SCC (Cr) 124; AIR 1971 SC 363; 1971 CRLJ 305; [1971] 2 S.C.R. 917

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34 and 302 - Murder - Conviction - Death sentence - Challenged - Instant appeal filed against the judgment of the HC confirming conviction of the two appellants by the Sessions Judge, u/s. 302 r/w s. 34 of the IPC - It was alleged against the appellants that in furtherance of their common intention, they murdered deceased by repeatedly stabbing him to death.

Held, the absence of test identification in all cases is not fatal and if the accused person is well-known by sight it would be waste of time to put him up for identification. In the instant case, witness knew the accused persons for about four. On the facts of instant case, the trial was not vitiated because the accused persons were denied identification. Medical evidence is not in conflict with the prosecution case. Appeal dismissed.
