Malegaon Electricity Company Private Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax, Bombay

Supreme Court of India

11 August 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, J.C. Shah, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 252; (1970) 2 SCC 431; AIR 1970 SC 1982; [1970] 78 ITR 466; [1971] 1 S.C.R. 761

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act,1922, ss. 10(2)(Vii), 34(1)(b), 34(1)(a) - A/y.1952-53 - Assessment - Depreciation - Sale price more than written down value - Proceedings for non disclosure - Challenged - Validity - Assessment for 1952-53 was completed by Income-tax Officer and after setting off unabsorbed depreciation determined assessee's total income as 'Nil' - In course of assessment proceedings appellant/assessee informed ITO that its company was sold to 'X' company and submitted relevant documents - Entire sale consideration was paid in cash and profits earned by assessee for period of six months ended on dt.30-9-1951 were paid over to 'X'company - Consideration received by appellant/assessee was much more than their written down value and neither it was disclosed in its return u/s. 10(2)(vii) of the Actnor mentioned in Part I of s. 'D' of Return - Subsequently it was identified by ITO and proceedings u/s. 34(1)(a) of the Act were initiated after obtaining sanction from Commissioner - After hearing appellant ITO reassessed assessee on 26-8-1957 and determined its total income for A/y 1952-53 at Rs. 4,48,893 based on profits earned by assessee u/s. 10(2)(vii) of the Act were Rs. 4,88,386 and failure of assessee to disclose its profits brought case within scope of s. 34(1)(a) of the Act - Aggrieved appellant/assessee filed appeal and was dismissed by Appellate Assistant Commissioner - Aggrieved appellant/assessee filed appeal in Tribunal and was allowed holding that appellant had not suppressed any material information during original assessment and as such action u/s. 34(1)(a)of the Act could not be sustained - At instance of Revenue reference was made to HC and HC answered them in favour of Revenue and against appellant/assessee - Hence instant appeal - Whether judgment of HC was justified - Held, tribunal erred in declining to decide question whether any portion of sale price came within scope of s. 10(2) (vii)of the Act and that question should have been examined for considering whether assessee had placed before ITO all material facts necessary for purpose of its assessment - Admittedly price realized at sale in excess of written down value of assets sold had not been mentioned as profits either in return or in s. 'D' of Part I of return - Assessee had not mentioned written down value of assets sold either in its return or in any other documents submitted to ITO - Law casts duty on assessee to 'disclose fully and truly all material facts necessary for his assessment for that year' - HC should not have answered questions referred to it without first deciding whether part of sale price received by appellant/assessee amounts to profits u/s. 10(2)(vii) of the Act - Tribunal must first decide that question and thereafter decide other questions of law arising for decision on basis of its decision whether there was any profits falling within s. 10(2)(vii) of the Act - HC answers were set aside and matter remitted back to Tribunal for fresh disposal - Appeal allowed.
