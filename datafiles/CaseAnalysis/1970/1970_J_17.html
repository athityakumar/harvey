Jugraj Singh and Another v Jaswant Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

16 March 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, A.N. Ray, I.D. Dua

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 83; (1970) 2 SCC 386; AIR 1971 SC 761; 1971 (73) PLR 314; [1971] 1 S.C.R. 38; 1971 UJ 379

Case Digest

Subject: Tort

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Punjab Redemption of Mortgages Act, 1913, ss. 9, 12 - Mortgage of property - Power of attorney - Ratification - X mortgaged certain lands to Y in the year 1923 - On 6-9-1961 son of X executed a power of attorney authorising SKS to sell the property and execute the sale deed and present it for registration - On the strength of the document SKS executed the sale deed in favour of respondents and presented it for registration and the document was registered - Vendees thereupon sought to redeem the mortgage and applied u/s. 9 of the 1913 Act - Vendees deposited the entire amount due under the mortgage in the Collector's court and the amount was still lying there, because of the later proceedings from instant appeal arose - Collector ordered the redemption of the mortgage - Appellants who were the sons of the original mortgagee thereupon filed a suit under s. 12 of the Act - While suit was still pending and because of the challenge to the power of attorney on the ground that it had not been properly authenticated under the law, a fresh power of attorney was executed by son of X in favour of SKS - Second power of attorney was subscribed and sworn to before the Notary Public - Clerk of the Court as required by the laws appended a certificate that the Notary Public had duly given the certificate in acknowledgement of the execution of the power of attorney by son of X - Second power of attorney was produced in the suit and the court of first instance ordered the dismissal of the suit, because it was of opinion that the transfer in favour of the redeeming mortgagors by son of X was thereafter flawless - Appeal in the District Court and a second appeal filed in HC failed - Hence instant appeal - Whether the second power of attorney was effective to render valid the transaction of sale and the registration of the document both earlier than the power of attorney - Held, second power of attorney states in express terms that the first power of attorney was defective and was being ratified - Son of X also stated in the second power of attorney that the act of SKS would be his act which included not only the making of the document but also the presentation of that document - SC was satisfied that there was proper execution of the document and registration - SC were also satisfied that appellants were not entitled to a declaration - It would be noticed that they neither asked for the cancellation of the order of the Collector nor for any injunction, two of the reliefs which they were entitled to ask in the case in addition to the declaration - Suit was obviously an unmeritorious on the part of the mortgagees - They were after all mortgages and redemption was offered to them by a person who was the son of the original mortgagor and who resorted to the quick remedy of the 1913 Act, for getting the mortgage redeemed - It was obvious that over the years the property should have appreciated considerably in value - Intention of the mortgagees was to retain the property as long as they could by raising technical objections in the way of the mortgagors seeking to redeem it - Further again appellants as plaintiffs in the case joined the transferees from son of X but made no attempt to join the son of original mortgagor - Suit could be only properly framed with all the parties before the Court - In SC's opinion, even if was not a necessary party, he was at least a proper party - If he had been brought before the court, SC would have known from him whether he had given the authority to execute the document and he could have adopted the act of SKS by ratifying it again - Suit was not properly framed - Appeal dismissed.
