rem Lata Agarwal v Lakshman Prasad Gupta and Others

Supreme Court of India

23 April 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Ray, I.D. Dua

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 60; (1970) 3 SCC 440; AIR 1970 SC 1525; 1971 (2) MLJ(SC) 1; [1971] 1 S.C.R. 364

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Family & Personal; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Family & Personal - Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - U.P. Encumbered Estates Act, 1934 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 48 - Limitation Act, 1963 - Stay of execution proceedings - Benefit of exclusion of time under limitation - Entitlement of - Suit for partition was filed by 'L' before Trial Court - Trial Court passed preliminary decree - Aggrieved by said order, appeal was filed, which was dismissed - Amicable settlement was arrived at in partition suit on 13-1-1931 for partition of properties into 5equal lots and allotment of shares - Commissioner was appointed to go into accounts and prepare 5lots - Inter se disputes were raised as to liability for loans alleged against joint family - Commissioner prepared his report on 18-5-1936 - Final decree was passed - On appeal, HC at Allahabad disposed of said appeal - 'P' and six others filled suit and obtained decree on 20-7-1938 against 'B' and other members of joint family to which appellant's father belonged - Decree-holder commenced execution proceedings and prayed for attachment and sale of 'J' belonged to joint family - HC stayed execution proceedings and after stay order was vacated execution proceedings were revived on 13-5-1950 - 'J' was attached and was sold - Sale was set aside on 31-5-1955 pursuant to objections of judgment-debtors that 'J' could not be sold because of provisions of the Act - Decree-holders made application for transfer of decree - Decree was transferred to Madras HC - Decree-holders filed application in HC for attaching properties of joint family - Master of HC dismissed said application for execution was barred by limitation - On, Single Judge held that application was not within mischief of bar of limitation - On appeal, DB uphold judgment of Single Judge - Hence instant appeal - Whether order of Madras HC was right in holding that decree holder was entitled to the benefit of exclusion of time during which execution proceedings were stayed by order of Allahabad HC - Held, there was stay of execution proceedings and on 13-5-1950 execution proceedings were revived - Judgment debtors did not challenge order dt.13-5-1950 - Judgment debtors impeached the sale only on a ground covered by the Act - Judgment debtor further in impeaching the sale of 'J' did not advance before Trial Court any contention that any of orders of Trial Court reviving execution proceedings, attaching 'J' and directing sale of 'J' was barred by limitation -Principle of res judicata applies to execution proceedings - Judgment debtors did not raise any objection as to limitation in regard to execution of decree before Trial Court - On contrary judgment debtors asked for setting aside sale on basis of revival of execution proceedings - Revival of execution was not challenged and judgment debtors were thereby barred by principle of rem judicate from questioning directly or indirectly order dt.13-5-1950 reviving execution proceedings - Further, Madras HC upheld order of Single Judge entitling decree holder to exclusion of period spent in prosecuting prior infructuous execution proceedings before Trial Court at Allahabad - Decree holder was allowed to proceed with execution proceedings and Madras HC remitted matter to Master to consider questions indicated in judgment and judgment debtors were allowed to raise objections to executability of decree apart from that of limitation as indicated in judgment of Single Judge - SC was of opinion that Madras HC was right in holding that decree holder was entitled to the benefit of exclusion of time during which execution proceedings were stayed by order of Allahabad HC and decree holder proceeded with said execution proceedings in good faith and with diligence - Appeal dismissed.
