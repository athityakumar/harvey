Jijabai Vithalrao Gajre v Pathankhan and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 September 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

C.A. Vaidyialingam, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 312; (1970) 2 SCC 717; AIR 1971 SC 315; [1971] 2 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Women & Children

Summary: Constitution, art. 227 - Hindu Minority and Guardianship Act, 1956, s. 6 - Bombay Tenancy and Agricultural Lands (Vidarbha Region) Act, 1958, ss. 39 and 38 - (A) Constitution - Powers of High Court - Not greater than those u/art. 226 - Power limited to seeing that tribunals function within limits of their authority - HC cannot sit in appeal against order of Tribunal - HC while agreeing with Revenue Tribunal on all material findings, differing in two material aspects which affect jurisdiction of Revenue Tribunal to grant necessary relief - (B) Hindu Law - Natural guardians - Mother comes only after death of father - Position is same under Hindu Law - Father though alive not taking any interest in minor and as good as non-existent - (C) Tenancy - Lease prior to 01-04-1957 - As s. 39 does not apply as one of essential conditions of maintainability of application u/s. 39 is that lease should not be one created before 01-04-1957 - Held, HC commits no error of jurisdiction - Mother treated as natural guardian - HC held was right in treating application as one u/s.36 r/w s. 38 and granting relief of possession of half area of lands leased, u/s. 38(4)(a), proviso (i) with requisite direction to Revenue Court - Appeal dismissed.
