Ratilal Shakarabhai and Others v State of Gujarat and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 March 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, J.C. Shah, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 541; (1970) 2 SCC 264; AIR 1970 SC 984; 1970 UJ 451

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4, 6, 40, 41 and 42 - (A) Whether proceedings were vitiated as no steps were taken u/ss. 40 to 42 of Land Acquisition Act, 1894? - Held, both notifications u/ss. 4 and 6 say that proposed acquisition was for a public purpose namely for a housing scheme - Therefore, if proposed acquisition is not for a company but for a public purpose then there was no need to comply with ss. 40 to 42 - (B) Whether a housing scheme for a limited numbers of persons cannot be considered as a public purpose? - Declaration u/s. 6 shall be conclusive evidence that land proposed to be acquired is needed for a public purpose - Held, ordinarily, Govt. is best authority to determine whether purpose in question is a public purpose or not - Appeal dismissed.
