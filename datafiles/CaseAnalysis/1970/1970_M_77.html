Khushal Khemgar Shah and Others v Mrs. Khorshed Banu Dadiba Boatwalla and Another

Supreme Court of India

12 February 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, K.S. Hegde, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 573; (1970) 1 SCC 415; AIR 1970 SC 1147; [1970] 3 S.C.R. 689; 1970 UJ 348

Case Digest

Subject: Contract & Commercial

Summary: Partnership Act, 1932, ss. 14, 55, 39, 42 and 46 - (A) Property of firm - Whether covers goodwill of the business of the firm - (B) Death of partner - Partnership to continue under agreement - Whether s. 55 bars LRs to claim share in goodwill (as one of assets of firm) - (C) Provisions deal with consequences ensuing after general dissolution - They do not abrogate terms of contrat under which partnership is to continue between surviving partners after death of one - (D) Contract to contrary - Partnership to continue under agreement between surviving partners - Share of deceased partner - Devolution upon LR in absence of contrary conract - Held, expressly included - The s. 55 not a bar to LR claiming share in goodwill - Act does not extinguish rights of deceased partner in assets of firm - Share of deceased partner in assets including good-will will devolve on his LR in absence of anything to contrary in agreement - Appeal Dismissed.
