Talib Hussain v State of Jammu and Kashmir

Supreme Court of India

07 July 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

I.D. Dua

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 485; (1971) 3 SCC 118; AIR 1971 SC 62; 1970 UJ 709

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Administrative - Practice & Procedure - Jammu Kashmir Preventive Detention Act, 1964, ss. 13-A (2)(b)(ii), 8(1), 13-A (2)(c), 10 - Preventive detention - Legality - Petitioner (detenue) filed instant application for a writ of habeas corpus, alleged that due to personal grudges of 2 persons, he was arrested on 4-9-1968, but the warrant for his detention was dt.10-9-1968 and that op to 12-12-1969, he was neither shown the warrant of detention nor were the grounds of his detention communicated to him.

Held, it is clear that by virtue of the provisions of ss. 13-A (2)(b)(ii), 8(1) of the Act, and by virtue of ss. 13-A (2)(c), 10 of the Act, were both inapplicable to instant case. These provisions merely serve to demonstrate that the question of security of the State is of paramount importance and is placed above the right to personal liberty of an individual whose activities are a danger to such security. Activities prejudicial to the security of the State always pose a threat to the public peace and safety and in the event of a crisis arising out of such activities, the rights of individuals have to be postponed to the larger considerations of State. In habeas corpus proceedings the Court has to consider the legality of the detention on the date of hearing. If on the date of hearing it cannot be said that the aggrieved party has been wrongfully deprived of his personal liberty and his detention is contrary to law, a writ of habeas corpus cannot issue. Petition dismissed.
