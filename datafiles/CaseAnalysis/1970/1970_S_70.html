Sahadu Gangaram Bhagade v Special Deputy Collector, Ahmednagar and Another

Supreme Court of India

30 March 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, A.N. Grover, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 289; (1970) 1 SCC 685; AIR 1971 SC 1887; [1971] 1 S.C.R. 146

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Arbitration Act

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Arbitration Act, 1940, ss. 7 and 11 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Bombay Court-fees Act, 1959, art. 3, Sch 1, 13, Sch II - Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - Requisitioning and Acquisition of Immovable Property Act, 1952, ss.8 and 11 - (A) Nature of Court-fee payable on memorandum of cross-objection filed in an appeal against Award made - Held, it is ad valorem Court-fee as prescribed by sch I, art. 3 and not a fixed Court fee under sch II, art. 13 of Bombay Court-fees Act, 1959 court-fee that is payable on memorandum of cross-objection filed in an appeal against award made u/s. 8 of Requisitioning and Acquisition of Immovable Property Act - (B) Scope of s.7 of Arbitration Act, 1940 - Meaning of 'petition' and 'Appeal' - Held, Arbitration Act, 1940, s. 7 apply to an appeal filed u/s. 11 - Petition in Article includes a cross-objection filed in an appeal - Words 'including memorandum of appeal' in art. 3 refer to word 'petition' - Cross-objection filed in an appeal is a memorandum of appeal in substance though not in form and hence it would be covered by term petition - Appeal dismissed.
