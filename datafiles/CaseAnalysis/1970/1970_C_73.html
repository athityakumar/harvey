N. V. Shanmugham and Company v Commissioner of Income Tax, Madras

Supreme Court of India

23 April 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, A.N. Grover, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 58; (1970) 2 SCC 139; AIR 1970 SC 1707; [1971] 81 ITR 310; 1971 (1) MLJ(SC) 51; [1971] 1 S.C.R. 340

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961 - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922 - Partnership - Dissolution - Appointment of receiver - Association of persons - Plaintiff/firm was carrying on business in manufacture and sale of snuff under deed of partnership dt. 20-4-1955 and its partners were 'A', 'B', 'C' and 'D'/minor been admitted to benefits of partnership his share in net profits being 1/6th - Deed of partnership provided that partnership could not be dissolved before dt. 31-8-1955 and it was open to partners to continue partnership or enter into fresh partnership on fresh terms and conditions - On 17-9-1956, 'A' filed suit for dissolution of partnership with effect from dt. 31-8-1956 and for taking of accounts and for appointment of receiver to take charge of business - Income Tax Officer concluded that business was carried on by 'association of persons' and assessing individual partners on their share of income at tile rate applicable to them would arise as contended by receivers - Appellate Assistant Commissioner rejected the appeal of assessees and confirmed the order of Income Tax Officer - On appeal Tribunal concluded that profits earned should be assessed to tax in hands of individual partners at rates applicable to them - Whether the profits earned in business should be considered as profits earned by association of persons and whether persons whom receivers represented constituted association of persons - Held, tax may be levied and recovered from Receivers u/s.41 (1) of Act and to borrow expression from 1961 Act they are only representative assessees - Three receivers did not make association of receivers and jointly represented real owners and wholly irrelevant for purpose of assessment - None of heirs of deceased owner of business wanted to break unity of business or its continuity and business was of nature not be carried on without consensus and continuance of business by receivers was rightly considered as continuance of business by heirs of deceased - Business was continued with consent of all owners - Import and distribution of cloth was done on joint basis purchasers were joint so were sales and profits were ascertained on joint basis and distributed according to capital contributed by each member of group - Group was association of persons and could be assessed on its profits as such to income tax and excess profits tax - Business was carried on because Deputy Commissioner of district had appointed members constituting group to import and distribute cloth - Members of group did not voluntarily join group - Business carried on by association of persons - Appeal dismissed.
