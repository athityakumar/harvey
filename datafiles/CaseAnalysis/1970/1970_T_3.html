State of West Bengal v Indian Iron and Steel Company Limited

Supreme Court of India

16 April 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 334; (1970) 2 SCC 39; AIR 1970 SC 1298; [1971] 1 S.C.R. 275

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax

Summary: W.B. Cess Act, 1880, ss. 75 and 102 - CPC, 1908, s. 9 - (A) Cess - (1) Determination of annual net profits for assessment - Owner of coal mine supplying coal to his own workshops and factories - (2) Finality of valuation - Mode of computation of net profits - Assessing authorities to consider except where it is in violation of law - Mistake in computation has to be rectified by following precise and self-contained procedure under the Act - Assessee failing to do so - But filed suit - (B) Civil Procedure - Courts to try all civil suits unless barred - Matter of levy of tax - Finality given to orders of special Tribunal - Jurisdiction of Civil Court when excluded - (D) Exclusion of jurisdiction of Civil Court - Readily inferred - Held, value of coal supplied can be taken into account in determining profits of mine - Assessee failing to do so cannot be allowed to challenge it as excessive by way of suit in view of s. 102 - Excluded if there is adequate remedy to do what the civil court would normally do in a suit - Cannot be readily inferred but must either be explicitly expressed or clearly implied and even in cases of such exclusion Civil Court can examine cases where statutory provisions have not been complied with or statutory tribunal has not acted in conformity with fundamental principles of judicial procedure - Appeal Allowed.
