R. C. Chandiok and Another v Chuni Lal Sabharwal and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 October 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Grover, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 195; (1970) 3 SCC 140; AIR 1971 SC 1238; [1971] 2 S.C.R. 573; 1971 UJ 35

Case Digest

Subject: Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Special Relief Act, 1963

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Specific Relief Act, 1877, s. 22 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 115 - Special Relief Act, 1963, ss. 21 and 22 - (A) Whether appellants defaulted regarding payment and hence respondents could cancel agreement with them? - Held, respondents were to obtain sanction from Ministry of Rehabilitation before appellants were to complete their part of agreement concerning payment due - Sanction was given in November, 1956 and even then respondents did not inform appellants about it so as to enable them to perform their part of agreement of sale - Appellants promptly filed suit shows their keenness and readiness in matter of acquiring plot by purchase - Readiness and, willingness cannot be treated as a straight jacket formula - These have to be determined from entirety of facts and circumstances relevant to intention and conduct of party concerned - Appellants were carrying on business and there is nothing to indicate that they were not in a position to arrange for remaining sum to make up total of Rs. 15, 000/- - It is not possible to accept that appellants, who had all along been trying their utmost to purchase plot, did not have necessary funds or could not arrange for them when sale deed had to be executed and registered after sanction had been obtained - (B) Whether appellant could have accepted a part of judgment but appealed against yet another part contesting it to be against him? - Held, a party cannot approbate and reprobate at same time - These propositions are so well known that no possible exception can be taken to them - Further held, however, above rule cannot apply because appellants had, by consistent and unequivocal conduct, made it clear that they were not willing to accept judgment of Trial Court as correct - The relief is discretionary but not arbitrary and discretion must be exercised in accordance with sound and reasonable judicial principles - It is notpossible to hold that conduct of appellants, which is always an important element for consideration, was such that it precluded them from obtaining a decree for specific performance - Appeal allowed.
