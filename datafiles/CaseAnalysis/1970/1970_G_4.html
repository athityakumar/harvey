Ganga Prasad and others v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

02 April 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

G.K. Mitter, Mohammad Hidayatullah, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 615; (1970) 3 SCC 92

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Essential Commodities Act, 1955, ss. 3, 7 and 12-A - Bihar Food grains Licensing Order, 1963, cls. 7 - Failure to update stock register - Seizure - Manipulation of official record - Effect - District Supply Officer noticed a cart loaded with six bags of rice being taken out of a godown whereof appellants were partners of it - Officer entered the godown and checked the stock register and other relevant papers and obtained a statement in writing Ex. 1 from appellant of food grains received by appellant on that day - Registers and all material papers and stock of food grains in godown was seized - More than a month thereafter a complaint was lodged by the District Supply Officer in the Court of Sub-Divisional Officer - District Supply Officer, did not file copy of seizure list nor the books of account seized nor original statement in writing of appellant, showing the arrival of food grains at the godown on that day - As the complaint was limited to the item of 72 bags of rice alleged to have been found in the stock in excess of the quantity shown on the register, appellants filed a petition before the Magistrate for release of all other food grains - Magistrate passed an order of release of all the food grains including 949 bags of rice but excluding 72 bags - Magistrate held that prosecution had proved its case with regard to 72 bags and convicted appellants - On revision, HC dismissed revision petition - Hence, instant appeal - Appellants contended that although Ex. 1 and attested copy thereof Ex. 15/2 appear to contain manipulations these could not have been done by appellants or for their benefit - Whether Ex. 1 was manipulated by the District Supply Officer or department while document was in custody - Held, evidence pointed to the manipulations having been made while document was in custody of the Supply Officer or his department and the filing of a copy of Ex. 1 as interpolated before attestation by a Magistrate was with a sinister motive - Withholding of books of account of appellants for a space of more than two months was not justified on facts of the case - It appeared that a false case was deliberately launched against appellants and that the manipulation in Ex. 1 accounts for delay in filing complaint after seizure of stock - Appeal allowed.
