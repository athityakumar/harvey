Commissioner Of Income-Tax, Madras v S. S. Sivan Pillai And Others

Supreme Court of India

29 April 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, K.S. Hegde, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 71; (1970) 2 SCC 180; AIR 1970 SC 1667; [1970] 40 Comp Cas 726; [1970] 77 ITR 354; 1971 (1) MLJ(SC) 59; [1971] 1 S.C.R. 434

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 15C(1) and 15C(4) - Exemption under provisions of s. 15C - Availability - Shareholder claiming exemption u/cl. (4) - For it a condition precedent that company claiming exemption u/cl. (1) is there - Profits - After allowing for depreciation - But loss after setting off unabsorbed depreciation - Paid dividends out of profits - Held, shareholders cannot claim exemption u/s.15 of the Act - Appeal Allowed.
