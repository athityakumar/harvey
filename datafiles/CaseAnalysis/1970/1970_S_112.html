 Ashoka Marketing Limited v State of Bihar and Another

Supreme Court of India

30 January 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Mohammad Hidayatullah, K.S. Hegde, A.N. Grover, A.N. Ray, I.D. Dua

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 104; (1970) 1 SCC 354; AIR 1971 SC 946; [1970] 3 S.C.R. 455; 1970 (26) STC 254

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Keywords: Collected, Railway Freight

Summary: Sales Tax - Indirect Tax - Bihar Sales Tax Act, 1947 - Bihar Sales Tax Act, 19 of 1959 - Act repealed - Sections ultra vires the state legislature - Legality - Assessee returned for the year 1956-57 under the 1947 Act, an amount of Rs. 2, 46, 67, 517-1-6 as its turnover from sale of cement and other commodities - Superintendent of Sales Tax brought to tax an additional amount of Rs. 7, 67, 70213-0 being the railway freight paid in respect of the goods supplied by assessee - By order Appellate Authority set aside the order directing inclusion of the railway freight in the turnover, and ordered that the assessment be revised - In the meantime 1947 Act, was repealed and was replaced by the Act, 19 of 1959 - Hence instant appeal - It was contended that the power to legislate in respect of recovery of the amount collected by a dealer which in law he was not entitled to collect, falls within Entries 6, 7 and 13 of List III - Held, SC failed to appreciate how power to legislate in respect of Entries 6, 7 and 13 would authorise the State Legislature to legislate in respect of recovery from the dealer of an amount which the dealer was in law not entitled to collect, but which he has collected - Power to legislate in respect of s. 20A (3), (4) and (5) did not fall under Entries 6, 7 and 13 of List III expressly, nor could it be said that the power to legislate was necessarily incidental to the power contained in Entries 6, 7 and 13 List III - State had no power to legislate for recovering amount which was collected by the tax-payer in order to recoup himself for payment of tax which under the law he was not bound to pay - Even though the competence of the State to legislate was not sought to be supported under Entries 6, 7 and 13 of List III, the decision of the Court plainly implies that the State has no such power under any Entry in the third List - It was declared that of s. 20A (3), (4) and (5) were ultra vires the State Legislature - As a corollary thereto s. 20A (6) and (8) should be deemed invalid - Appeal allowed.
