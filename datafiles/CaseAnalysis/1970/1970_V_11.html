 State Of Kerala, Etc v Very Rev. Mother Provincial, Etc

Supreme Court of India

10 August 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, K.S. Hegde, A.N. Grover, A.N. Ray, I.D. Dua, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 318; (1970) 2 SCC 417; AIR 1970 SC 2079; 1970 KLT 630; [1971] 1 S.C.R. 734

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Education; Indirect Tax; Service

Summary: Education - Constitution - Minority educational institutions - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 132(1), 13, 3(1)(c), 31, 30(1) and 19 - Kerala Education Bill, 1957 - Kerala University Act, 1957, ss. 50(1), 48(2), 49(4), 53 (1), (2), (3), (9), 56(2), (4) and 63 - Kerala University Act, 1969 - Whether provisions of Kerala University Act, 1957 are ultra vires art. 30(1) of Constitution? - Held, ss. 48(2), 49(4), 48(6) and 49(6) of Act, 1957 are ultra vires art. 30(1) - Also agree that sub-ss. (1), (2), (3), (9) of s. 53 and sub-ss. (2) and (4) of s. 56 are ultra vires as they fail with ss. 48 and 49 - (B) Whether provisions of Act are violative of arts. 14 and 19(1)(f)? - Further held, provisions of s. 63 affect both kinds of institutions alike and must be declared ultra vires in respect of both majority and minority educational institutions - Appeal allowed.
