State of Punjab v Dewan Chuni Lal

Supreme Court of India

16 February 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

G.K. Mitter, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 348; (1970) 1 SCC 479; AIR 1970 SC 2086; 1971 (1) SCJ 238; [1970] 3 S.C.R. 694; 1970 SLR 375

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service

Summary: Service - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, Art. 311 - Punjab Police Rules - Departmental Enquiry - Defense Witness - Reasonable Opportunity -Dismissal - Respondent/Sub Inspector of Police was called upon to answer his inconsistent conduct and reputation of charge framed, while in service - An enquiry was made against him and DIG(Deputy Inspector General) passed an order, dismissing him from service - Respondent filed suit before Subordinate Judge, which hold that charge framed against him was vague, indefinite - Respondent submitted that oral and documentary evidence sought was withheld and no reasonable opportunity of defense was afforded to him u/art. 311 of Constitution which was violated - Hence order of dismissal was made inoperative by HC - Hence instant appeal - Whether reasonable Opportunity was given to respondent by punishing authority - Held, though case was governed by art. 311 of Constitution, it stood prior to amendment in 1963 - Hence respondent could not be deprived of an effective right to make representation against action of dismissal - Court opined refusal of right to examine witnesses who had made general remarks against his character and they were available for examination at inquiry amounted to denial of reasonable opportunity of showing cause against action - Court viewed it was impossible to hold that respondent had been given reasonable opportunity of conducting his defense before enquiry officer - Court hold that HC's conclusion was right in retention of respondent's dismissal - Appeal dismissed.
