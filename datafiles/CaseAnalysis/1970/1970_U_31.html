Messrs Ashok Construction Company v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

13 February 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, K.S. Hegde, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 294; (1971) 3 SCC 66; 1970 UJ 367

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, s. 115 - Limitation Act, 1963 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Arbitration Act, 1940, ss. 8 and 14 - Appointment of arbitrator - Application u/s. 8 - D appointed as arbitrator - This order was challenged before High Court in a revision petition, and later in appeal to Supreme Court, without success - Meanwhile D entered upon reference - Appellants submitted a claim for Rs. 4,41,440.20 under terms of contract - Arbitrator made an award directing UOI to pay to appellants Rs. 1,79,843.80, and filed it in Court of Deputy Commr. - Notice of award was given u/s. 14(2) of Act to parties - The application filed by UOI for setting aside award was rejected - In appeal against order, Deputy Commr. set aside award - Appellant preferred a revision application u/s. 36 of Rules for Administration of Justice in Khasi and Jaintia Hills and u/s. 115 of CPC, 1908 - HC dismissed petition - Whether arbitrator in making award acted in violation of principles of natural justice and exceeded his jurisdiction; and that claim made by appellants was barred by law of limitation? - Held, arbitrator did not act in a manner not consonant with general principles of equity and good conscience which governed his conduct as an arbitrator - Arbitrator not acted in violation of principles of natural justice - In refusing to show any more indulgence arbitrator did not act improperly, nor did he act in a manner contrary to rules of natural justice - Grounds given by Deputy Commr. in setting aside award proceed upon a complete misconception as to what constitutes 'misconduct' on part of arbitrator - HC declined to enter upon an enquiry whether arbitrator acted improperly in making an award after UOI had failed to file its 'written arguments' - HC did not record a finding on plea of UOI that arbitrator exceeded jurisdiction in making award - Claim made by appellants before arbitrator for Rs. 4,41,440.20 only Rs. 1,79,843.80 were awarded - Arbitrator has given no reasons for accepting claim of appellants for sum awarded by him - In view of Deputy Commr. under cl. 25 of arbitration agreement, arbitrator was not empowered to go into 'questions of loss of goodwill, or damages by way of interest, and that arbitrator could not award interest by way of damages for detention of money and that is really what claim for 'damages' amounts to' - Appellants made a claim for interest on amount withheld after due date and arbitrator was competent to decide that claim - In present case work of construction was completed on 12-10-1957 - Bill was prepared on 20-03-1958, and appellants had demanded of Superintending Engineer that a reference be made by letter, D/- 29-10-1960 - Request was repeated in subsequent letter, D/- 30-08-1961- First demand cannot on account of second request be wiped out - No objection was raised at time when D was appointed arbitrator by order of Court of Deputy Commr. that claim was barred by law of limitation, nor was contention raised before HC - Even before arbitrator no contention appears to have been raised that claim was barred by law of limitation - From date of settlement of claim three years had not elapsed before a notice asking for a reference was made - Prima facie, claim was not barred by law of limitation - Appeal allowed.
