State of Gujarat and Another v Acharya D. Pandey and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 October 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, S.M. Sikri

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 194; (1970) 3 SCC 183; (1971) SCC (Cr) 1; AIR 1971 SC 866; 1971 CRLJ 760; [1971] 2 S.C.R. 557; 1971 UJ 16

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Reserve Bank of India Act

Summary: CrPC, 1898, s. 342 - Bombay Public Trusts Act, 1950, ss. 35(1) and 66 - IPC, 1860, s. 40 - (A) Criminal Procedure - Statement of accused u/s. 342 of Code - Court cannot split the statement into various parts and accept a portion only - Statement of accused - Accused pleading not guilty - (B) Penal Code - Mens rea - Requirement of mens rea is not a necessary ingredient of s. 35(1) of 1950 Act - Offence u/s. 35(1) is an absolute one - Held, statement taken as a whole cannot show that he was guilty of any offence - Held, s. 35(1) creates a quasi-criminal offence - Appeal Dismissed.
