State of Assam and Another v Mahendra Kumar Das and Others

Supreme Court of India

18 March 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

C.A. Vaidyialingam, S.M. Sikri, Vishishtha Bhargava

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 525; (1970) 1 SCC 709; AIR 1970 SC 1255; 1970 LabIC 1056; [1971] 1 S.C.R. 87; 1970 SLR 444

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Police Act, 1861, s. 7 - Respondent, a police officer was dismissed from service - Respondent filed writ petition challenging disciplinary proceedings initiated against him and orders of dismissal passed on basis of enquiry conducted by Enquiry Officer - High Court was of view that Enquiry Officer had taken into consideration materials gathered from records of Anti-Corruption Branch - HC set aside order of dismissal - (A) Whether order of dismissal of respondent by subordinate authority, viz, Superintendent of Police, was illegal and void? - Held, no - Respondent was appointed permanent Sub-Inspector of Police not by Inspector-General of Police but by Superintendent of Police - (B) Principles of natural justice - Compliance of - Rules of natural justice can be considered to have been violated only if authority concerned acts upon information collected by it and said information has not been disclosed to party against whom material has been used - Held, in instant case Enquiry Officer has made a note that he consulted Deputy Superintendent of Police, Anti-Corruption Branch and perused records relating to charges - But enquiry report does not show that materials, if any, collected by Enquiry Officer on those two days, have been utilised against respondent - Further held, punishing authority and Appellate Authority, Superintendent of Police and Deputy Inspector-General of Police, respectively, have independently considered matter and found respondent guilty on evidence on record, it must be held that in circumstances of this case there has been no violation of principles of natural justice when order of dismissal was passed - Appeal allowed.
