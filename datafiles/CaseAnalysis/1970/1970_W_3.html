Western India Match Company Limited v Western India Match Company Workers Union and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 January 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Shelat, C.A. Vaidyialingam, P. Jaganmohan Reddy

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 355; (1970) 1 SCC 225; AIR 1970 SC 1205; 1970 (20) FLR 297; 1970 LabIC 1033; 1970 (2) LLJ 256; 1970 (2) MLJ(SC) 72; [1970] 3 S.C.R. 370

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Keywords: Espousal

Summary: U.P. Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 2(1) - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 2(k), 10 - (A) Meaning of expression 'industrial dispute' u/s. 2(1) - Espousal of individual dispute by Union - Concerned individual whether was member of Union when cause arose - Relevency of - (B) Exercise of Govt's power to make reference - Existence of industrial dispute is condition precedent - Words 'at any time' u/s.4(k) - Earlier refusal by Govt. to make reference - Validity of reference at later stage - Held, is immaterial - Reference at later stage upon reconsideration of the matter, is still competent - Appeal Dismissed.
