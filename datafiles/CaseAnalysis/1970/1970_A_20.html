Aligarh Municipal Board and Others v Ekka Tonga Mazdoor Union and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 August 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

I.D. Dua, S.M. Sikri

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 482; (1970) 3 SCC 98; (1970) SCC (Cr) 570; AIR 1970 SC 1767; 1970 CRLJ 1520; 1970 UJ 729

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Contempt of Courts Act, 1952, s. 2 - Contempt of court - Realization of stand fees from tongawallah by Municipal Corporation - Dispute - Proceedings initiated by Association - Court granting interim stay over collection - Copy of order conveyed by Associations secretary - Delay in execution of stay by Demand Inspector - Whether municipal corporation and its employees can be held liable for contempt of court for delay in execution of stay order? - In order to justify action for contempt of court for breach of a prohibitive order it is not necessary that order should have been officially served on party against whom it is granted if it is proved that he has notice of order aliunde and he knew that it was intended to be enforced - Official communication is not a condition precedent, provided here is no valid reason to doubt authenticity of order conveyed to him - Held, clear instructions from Executive Officer - Delay in instruction for stay - Other subordinates duty bound to obey orders of superior (Demand Inspector) - Unqualified apology - Corporation liable to be punished for disobeying orders of competent courts - Held, Corporation and Demand Inspector guilty of contempt of court and others not guilty - Appeal allowed.
