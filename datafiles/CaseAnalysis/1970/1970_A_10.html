State of Assam v Abdul Noor and Others

Supreme Court of India

13 March 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Ray, Mohammad Hidayatullah, I.D. Dua

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 605; (1970) 3 SCC 10; (1970) SCC (Cr) 360; AIR 1970 SC 1365; 1970 CRLJ 1264; 1970 (6) DLT 313; 1970 (72) PLR 585

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898, ss. 156(3), 190 and 202 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 134(1)(c) - Quashing of complaints pending before Addl. District Magistrate and other before Court of Magistrate - High Court quashed both complaints on ground that Magistrate sent complaint petitions to officer-in-charge of police station for investigation without examining complainant - Challenged - Held, no useful purpose can be served by allowing these cases to be proceeded with - Both cases appear to typify tale of a woman who is lawfully married and complaints are baseless and do not disclose any offence - Appeal dismissed.
