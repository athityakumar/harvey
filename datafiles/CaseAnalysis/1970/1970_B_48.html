Commissioner of Income Tax (Central), Calcutta v Birla House Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

23 April 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Grover, K.S. Hegde, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 219; (1970) 2 SCC 88; AIR 1970 SC 1531; [1970] 77 ITR 751; [1971] 1 S.C.R. 357

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Vocation

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922 - Bad Debts - Assessment - Income - Deduction - Assessee was a private limited company and carried on business of banking and financing as also of managing agency - Company was managed by assessee - Assessee claimed to have stood guarantee for a loan of Rs. 6 lakhs which was advanced to U - U failed to pay loan - Assessee treated U as debtor for amount - Company went into liquidation and as assessee could not recover sum was written off in books of assessee company - Claim was not entertained either by Income Tax Officer or Appellate Assistant Commissioner - Tribunal did not agree with finding of Appellate Assistant Commissioner that loss was not directly incidental to the assessee's business - Tribunal and HC assumed that assessee's claim for loss of Rs. 5,60,199 was an admissible deduction - Hence instant appeal - Whether allowance was admissible deduction in computing business profits of assessee u/s.10(2)(xi) of Act - Held, there was no privity of contract or any legal relationship between assessee and selling agent - Neither under customer nor under any statutory provision or any contractual obligation was assessee bound to guarantee loan advanced by Bank to selling agent - There was no material established that managed company was under any legal obligation to, finance selling agent or to guarantee any loans advanced to selling agent by a third party - Allowance claimed did not fall within s.10(2)(xi) of Act - No attempt was made nor indeed it could be usefully made to claim any allowance u/s.10(2)(xv)of Act - Hence view of Appellate Tribunal and HC was based on a complete misapprehension of true legal position - Appeals disposed of.
