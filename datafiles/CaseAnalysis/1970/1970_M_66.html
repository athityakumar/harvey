Bansidhar Shankarlal v Mohd. Ibrahim and Another

Supreme Court of India

25 September 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 436; (1970) 3 SCC 900; AIR 1971 SC 1292; [1971] 41 Comp Cas 21; [1971] 2 S.C.R. 476; 1971 TAXLR 740

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate

Summary: Corporate - Companies Act, 1913, ss. 171 and 179(1)(a) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Company - Winding up order during pendency of second appeal - Execution of decree against company by successful party - Liquidators prosecuting second appeal - Whether it can be supposed that they did not obtain sanction of Court u/s. 179 (1) (a) to prosecute second appeal? - Whether leave of Court u/s. 171 of Companies Act is a condition precedent to execution of decree against such company? - Appeal against order of High Court refusing to certify certain appeal u/art. 133(1)(b) and (c) - Held, in absence of any evidence to contrary, it could not be supposed that they did not obtain sanction of Court u/s. 179(1)(a) - Fresh sanction u/s. 171 held not necessary - View taken by HC found to be true - Appeal dismissed.
