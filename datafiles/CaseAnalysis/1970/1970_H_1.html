 H. H. Maharajadhiraja Madhav Rao Jiwaji Raoscindia Bahadur v Union Of India

Supreme Court of India

15 December 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.C. Shah, S.M. Sikri, J.M. Shelat, Vishishtha Bhargava, G.K. Mitter, C.A. Vaidyialingam, K.S. Hegde, A.N. Grover, A.N. Ray, I.D. Dua

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 300; (1971) 1 SCC 85; AIR 1971 SC 530; [1971] 3 S.C.R. 9

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Indian Bar Councils Act, 1926, Indian Independence Act, 1947, Schedules, Defence of India Act, 1962, Titles, Government of India Act 1935

Summary: Constitution - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Constitution (Seventh Amendment) Act, 1956 - Constitution of India, 1950 - After independence rulers of various states were integrated and was guaranteed of payment of some amount known as privy purse along with 'gaddi of state' - Order of derecognising such rulers passed by President - Whether order passed by president is valid? - Held, contention of petitioners that order is violative of arts. 291 and 362 so it is unconstitutional and void - Respondents contended on ground that power of derecognising rulers is sovereign power so can be exercised by the President when he wants - President have no power to withdraw recognition of persons as ruler - Doctrine of paramountcy cannot be used to validate illegal act of derecognition of rulers as it affects rights of citizens - Right to receive privy purse is absolute right of rulers which cannot be taken away - Right to receive privy purse taken by order of President so it can be said that there is infringement of arts. 19 and 31 so order passed by President is unconstitutional and void - Appeal allowed.
