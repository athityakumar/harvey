 A. Sanjeevi Naidu Etc v State of Madras and Another

Supreme Court of India

05 February 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, J.C. Shah, Mohammad Hidayatullah, A.N. Grover, A.N. Ray, I.D. Dua

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 297; (1970) SCC (Cr) 196; (1970) 1 SCC 443; AIR 1970 SC 1102; 1970 (2) MLJ(SC) 98; [1970] 3 S.C.R. 505

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Defence & Security Forces; Indirect Tax; Service

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Madras Govt. Business Rules, r. 23(A) - Motor Vehicles Act, 1939, s. 68(C) - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 154, 163, and 166 - Whether Draft-Scheme which has been prepared for nationalisation of routes is constitutionally valid? - Held, yes - It is challenged on ground that opinion requisite u/s. 68(C) was not formed by State Govt. but by Secretary to Govt. acting in pursuance of powers conferred on him u/r. 23(A) - Under Indian Constitution, Governor is essentially a constitutional head, administration of State is run by Council of Ministers - In very nature of things, it is impossible for Council of Ministers to deal with each and every matter that comes before Govt. - For more convenient transaction of business of Govt. and for allocation amongst its Ministers, business of Govt. have to be allocated to one or other of Ministers on advice of Chief Minister - That Minister can, not only allocate various subjects amongst Ministers but may go further and designate a particular official to discharge any particular function - Where functions entrusted to a Minister are performed by an official employed in Minister's department there is in law no delegation because constitutionally the act or decision of official is that of Minister - Appeals dismissed.
