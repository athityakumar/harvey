Naresh Chandra Sanyal v Calcutta Stock Exchange Association Limited

Supreme Court of India

25 September 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 306; (1971) 1 SCC 50; AIR 1971 SC 422; [1971] 41 Comp Cas 51; [1971] 2 S.C.R. 483

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate

Summary: Calcutta Stock Exchange Articles of Association, arts. 33, 29, 24, 22, 26 and 27 - Companies Act, 1956, sch.1, Table A, Regn.31 - Contract Act, 1872, s. 74 - Corporate laws - Forfeiture of fully paid share of member - (A) Separate resolutions expelling him and declaring him defaulter - Need of - (B) Forfeiture in certain events, including failure to carry out engagement - Obligation on the part of the Company to re-allot or dispose it of - Articles - Validity - (C) Forfeiture for failure to carry out engagement - Balance remaining on hand after satisfying liabilities and engagements of defaulting shareholder to Exchange and its members in hands of Exchange, must be returned to defaulting shareholder - And art. 29 of Exchange is to be read subject to art. 33 - Article giving power to company to appropriate balance in hand would be contrary to intendment of s. 74, Contract Act - Separate resolutions held, not required - Articles are intra vires and valid - Held, forfeiture of share is in the nature of imposition of penalty - Case remanded - Appeal Allowed.
