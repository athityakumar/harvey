 Shiv Kirpal Singh v Shri V. V. Giri

Supreme Court of India

14 September 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri, Vishishtha Bhargava, G.K. Mitter, C.A. Vaidyialingam, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 308; (1970) 2 SCC 567; AIR 1970 SC 2097; [1971] 2 S.C.R. 197

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Keywords: Statement Of Fact, Electorate, Connivance, Presidential and Vice-Presidential Elections Act, 1952, Union Territories, Circulation, Election Rules, 1952, Definition of undue influence

Summary: Election - Constitution - Presidential a Vice-Presidential Election Act (XXXI of 1952), ss. 14, 18 and 21 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 21, 71 and 239A - Presidential and Vice-Presidential Election Rules, 1952, rr. 4, 6 - Representation of the People Act, 1951, s. 123 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 171 - Whether offence of undue influence was committed by others without his connivance? - Held, no commission of offence of undue influence by anybody with connivance of respondent and result of election was not materially affected as a result of any commission of offence of undue influence - Petition dismissed.
