Joint Commercial Tax Officer, Harbour Div. Ii Madras v Young Men's Indian Association (Reg.) Madras and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 February 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Grover, Mohammad Hidayatullah

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 349; (1970) 1 SCC 462; AIR 1970 SC 1212; 1970 (2) MLJ(SC) 103; [1970] 3 S.C.R. 680; 1970 (26) STC 241; 1970 UJ 342

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Keywords: Companies Act 1913

Summary: Sales Tax - Indian Sale of Goods Act, 1930 - Companies Act 1913, s. 26 - Madras General Sales Tax Act, 1959, ss. 2(b), 2(g) and 2(n) - Whether the supply of the various preparations by each club to its members involved a transaction of sale within the meaning of the Sale of Goods Act 1930 - If the club even though a distinct legal entity is only acting as an agent for its members in the matter of, supply of various preparations to them no sale would be involved as the element of transfer would be completely absent - Held, there is no transaction of sale involved - Appeal dismissed.
