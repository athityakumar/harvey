Madhowji Dharamshi Manufacturing Company Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax, Bombay

Supreme Court of India

29 April 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, A.N. Grover, K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 226; (1970) 2 SCC 229; AIR 1970 SC 1811; [1970] 78 ITR 62; 1970 UJ 614

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 7, 10(2)(xv) and 10(5 A) - Whether amount paid as compensation on premature termination of managing agency and on breach of contract was rightly disallowed? - Finding of ITAT was that the appointments of the selling agents and the managing agent, and the agreements to pay compensation for determining their contracts formed a chain of sham or colourable transactions - Circumstances in which another company was appointed as managing agent and the various events that took place in quick succession confirms that it was a part of design to withdraw a large sums of money tax free from the assessee's tax - Held, High Court was right in refusing call for a statement of case - Appeal dismissed.
