 Adi Pherozshah Gandhi v H. M. Seervai, Advocate-General of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

21 August 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, G.K. Mitter, C.A. Vaidyialingam, A.N. Ray, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 313; (1970) 2 SCC 484; AIR 1971 SC 385; [1971] 1 S.C.R. 863

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Law Officer, Professional Conduct And Etiquette, Solicitor General, State Bar Councils, Indian Bar Councils Act, 1926, Legal Practitioners Act, 1879, Criminal Law Amendment Act, 1908

Summary: Advocates Act, 1961, s. 37(1) - Govt. of India Act, 1935 - CPC, 1908, s. 11 - (A) 'Person aggrieved' - Advocate General appearing in pursuance of notice u/s. 35 (2) - (B) Practice and Procedure - Res judicata - Provision of s. 11 of CPC, 1908 - Applicability - Civil proceeding Decision of a criminal Court - Held, is not a person aggrieved - In a civil proceeding the decision of a criminal Court is not res judicata - Appeal Allowed.
