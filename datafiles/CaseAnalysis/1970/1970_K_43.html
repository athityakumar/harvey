 Management of Safdarjung Hospital, New Delhi v Kuldip Singh Sethi

Supreme Court of India

01 April 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, K.S. Hegde, A.N. Grover, A.N. Ray, I.D. Dua

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 77; (1970) 1 SCC 735; AIR 1970 SC 1407; 1970 (20) FLR 399; 1970 LabIC 1172; 1970 (2) LLJ 266; 1978 (1) LLN 376; [1971] 1 S.C.R. 177

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Research, Air Force Act, 1950

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Air Force Act, 1950 - Army Act, 1950 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1949, ss. 10(1)(d) and 33 C(2) - 'Industry' - Definition of - Whether a hospital can be described as an industry? - Held, hospital is not embarked on an economic activity which can be said to be analogous to trade or business - There is no evidence that it is more than a place where persons can get treated - This is a part of functions of Govt. and Hospital is run as a Department of Govt. - Hospital is wholly charitable and is a research institute dominant purpose of Hospital is research and training, but research and training cannot be given without beds - Treatment is thus a part of research and training - In these circumstances a Hospital cannot be described as an Industry - Appeals allowed.
