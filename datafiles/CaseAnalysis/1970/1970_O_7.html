Orient Paper Mills Ltd v Union Of India

Supreme Court of India

10 March 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Grover, J.C. Shah, K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 594; (1970) 3 SCC 76; AIR 1970 SC 1498; 1978 (2) E.L.T. 382

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Attorney-General

Summary: Indirect Tax - Administrative - Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944, ss. 35, 36 - Excessive assessment - Refund of sum - Valid assessment - Appellant carried on business of manufacturing and selling various kinds of paper and boards at its factory - Deputy Superintendent, Central Excise, had collected excise duty on creamwove paper in accordance with Item 17(1) of tariff - Appellant lodged claim with Assistant Collector of Central Excise for refund being sum assessed was excess - Assistant Collector rejected claim on ground that no overcharge had been made - Appellant then appealed u/s. 35 of the Act to Collector of Central Excise and Customs which was rejected by him - Central Govt. approached u/s. 36 of the Act on revisional side which was dismissed - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether assessment made by subordinate officer could be called valid assessment - Held, when assessment was to be made by Deputy Superintendent or Assistant Collector, Collector, to whom appeal lie against his order of assessment, could not control or fetter his judgment in matter of assessment - If Collector issued directions by which Deputy Superintendent of Assistant Collector was bound no room was left for exercise of his own independent judgment - In previous decision appeal and revision had been rejected by Collector and Central Govt. on ground that direction had been issued by Central Board or Revenue to effect that paper in question be treated as belonging to particular classification - Direction given by Collector was invalid and proceedings before Deputy Superintendent or Assistant Collector were vitiated - That position obtained in all appeals although type and quality of paper were different - Central Govt. merely affirmed order made by Collector in each case and did not give any independent reasons for upholding levy of duty made in accordance with directions of Collector - Appeals must succeed on ground that impugned orders were vitiated for reasons given and deserved to be set aside - Hence, Deputy Superintendent or Assistant Collector should make fresh assessment of duty in accordance with law - Appeal allowed.
