 In The Matter Of:Rustom Cawasjee Cooper v Union Of India

Supreme Court of India

05 May 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, K.S. Hegde, A.N. Grover, A.N. Ray, I.D. Dua, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 76; (1970) 2 SCC 298; AIR 1970 SC 1318; 1970 KLT 354; [1971] 1 S.C.R. 512; 1970 UJ 674

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Fair Criticism

Summary: Constitution, art. 129 - Contempt of Courts Act, 1952, s. 1 - Practice and Procedure - Contempt of Supreme Court - Limits of criticism of SC judgment - Held, while fair and temperate criticism even if strong, may not be actionable, attributing improper motives, or tending to bring Judges or Courts into hatred and contempt or obstructing directly or indirectly with the functioning of Courts is serious contempt of which notice must and will be taken - Respect is expected not only from those to whom the judgment of the Court is acceptable but also from those to whom it is repugnant -Order Accordingly.
