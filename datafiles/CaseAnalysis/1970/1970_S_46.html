 Shri N. Sri Rama Reddy Etc v Shri V. V. Giri

Supreme Court of India

27 April 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

C.A. Vaidyialingam, S.M. Sikri, J.M. Shelat, Vishishtha Bhargava, G.K. Mitter

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 64; (1970) 2 SCC 340; AIR 1971 SC 1162; [1971] 1 S.C.R. 399; 1970 UJ 604

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Former Statement

Summary: Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 63, 146, 153, 155, 157, 155(3) - Election Petition - (A) Evidence -Tape-recorded statement - Admissibility - (B) Meaning of expression 'Which is liable to be contradicted' u/s. 155(3) - (C) Construction of ss.153 and 155 - The provisions of any statute, must be properly and strictly construed - (D) Construction of ss. 146 and 153 - Held, is admissible u/s. 146 (1), Exception 2 to s. 153, and 155 (3) and also for corroboration - Does not mean merely 'which is relevant to the Issue' - Held statement in certain specific calcutta High Court case was too broadly laid down - Held, ss. 153 and 155 must be strictly construed - Held, ss. 146 not to be r/w s. 153 in its entirety - Order Accordingly.
