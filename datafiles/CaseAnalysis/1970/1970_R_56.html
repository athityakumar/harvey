Ratanlal v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

17 December 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri, Vishishtha Bhargava, I.D. Dua

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 200; (1970) 3 SCC 533; (1971) SCC (Cr) 139; AIR 1971 SC 778; 1971 CRLJ 654; [1971] 3 S.C.R. 251

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 84 - Mischief by fire - Accused of unsound mind - Pleading for same - Burden of proof on accused - Evidentiary value of related witness - Defence witness corroborated by medical evidence - Whether evidence of accused is to be disbelieved merely because they related to accused? - Held, that defence witnesses should not be disbelieved merely because they are relation of accused as they are one who are likely to remain in intimate contact of accused - Thus accused is insane within meaning of s. 84, IPC, 1860 and should be released henceforth - Appeal allowed.
