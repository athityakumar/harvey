Sunil Kumar Roy v Messrs Bhowra Kankanee Collieries Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

15 December 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Grover, K.S. Hegde, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 299; (1970) 3 SCC 565; AIR 1971 SC 751; [1971] 3 S.C.R. 232

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Indian Registration Act, 1908 - Changes in terms of rent - Registration of lease deed - Appellant entered into lease deed with its lessor and as per terms of lease, royalty at rate of Rs. 1 per ton on dispatches of coke had to be paid to lessor and rate was subjected to revision by mutual agreement based on market condition - Later, lessor of appellant sold colliery to respondent no. 1 who claimed royalty on all dispatches of coke at rate of Rs. 1 per ton - However, appellant paid amount at rate of 8 As. per ton and on breeze coke at 2 As. per ton on ground that as per revised mutual agreement entered in December, 1951 royalty on hard coke was to be paid at 8 As. per ton - Hence, respondent no. 1 filed suit against appellant claiming amount at rate of Rs. 1 per ton but the same was dismissed - On appeal, HC held that appellant failed to prove that reduction in rate of royalty had been given effect to from july and allowed appeal - Hence, instant appeal by appellant - Whether plea of admissibility of deed entered in December, 1951 could be entertained.

Held, finding of HC that it was not proved that there was any change in the market condition in July or in December 1953 to call for a reduction in the rate of royalty or that there was any mutual arrangement between the lessor or the lessee for such a reduction which was to become effective from July 1952 could not be reversed. Further, even on assumption that there was arrangement between appellant and its lessor for change in rent, yet, any reduction in the rate of royalty effected by means of deed made in December, 1951 which had not been registered under the provisions of the Act could not be accepted as registration of an agreement which reduces the rent of an existing registered lease is necessary. Appeal dismissed.

Ratio - Document which varies essential terms of existing registered lease, such as the amount of rent, should be registered.
