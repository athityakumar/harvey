Bholanath Amritlal Puroihit v State Of Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

14 August 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, S.M. Sikri, I.D. Dua

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 316; (1970) 2 SCC 414; (1970) SCC (Cr) 469; AIR 1971 SC 194; 1971 CRLJ 258; [1971] 1 S.C.R. 817

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Indian Post Office Act, 1898, s. 55 - Conviction - Challenged - Appellant was convicted by the Judicial Magistrate 1st Class, 1st Court, Broach u/s. 55 of the Act - In appeal that conviction was affirmed by the Sessions Judge - In revision petition before the HC, the principal contention taken by appellant was that the Magistrate was not competent to take cognizance of the case against him as there was no complaint as required by s. 72 of the Act - HC dismissed said revision petition - Hence, instant appeal -

Held, as per s. 72 of the Act there was admittedly no complaint' against the appellant which means that the Magistrate was incompetent to take cognizance of the case. From that it follows that the trial of the case was an invalid one and that the appellant was convicted without the authority of law. Appeal allowed.
