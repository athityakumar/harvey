Century Spinning and Manufacturing Company Ltd. and Another v Ulhasnagar Municipal Council and Another

Supreme Court of India

27 February 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, K.S. Hegde, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 341; (1970) 1 SCC 582; AIR 1971 SC 1021; [1970] 3 S.C.R. 854

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate

Keywords: Companies Act, 1913

Summary: Constitution, art. 226 - Companies Act, 1913 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 115 - (A) Exercise of jurisdiction is in judicial discretion of High Court - Petitioner prima facie entitled to have his grievance tried - Dismissal of petition u/art. 226, in limine without giving reasons - (B) Representation of existing fact and representation as to promise to do something in future - Distinction -Other party acting on such representation - (C) Writ petition u/art. 226 - Alternative remedy of civil suit open - Qestions of fact are raised - Held, is improper - It may give rise to estoppel in former case but in latter it may result in contract or obligation ex contractu enforceable in equity and is equally applies to obligations of public bodies like municipality - Petitioner not to be relegated to remedy of suit merely because questions of fact are raised - Appeal Dismissed.
