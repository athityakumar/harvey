Bhaiya Ram Munda v Anirudh Patar and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 August 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Vishishtha Bhargava

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 362; (1970) 2 SCC 825; AIR 1971 SC 2533; [1971] 1 S.C.R. 804

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Prohibition Act

Summary: Constitution, art. 342 - Constitution (Scheduled Tribes) Order 1950, Part III - Prohibition Act - Order u/art. 342 - (A) Express or implied admission - Made by a person that he is not member of Scheduled Tribe - Conclusiveness - (B) Scheduled Tribes of Bihar - Nonmention of 'Patars' as sub-tribe of Mundas in schedule - Inference that they are not Mundas - Held, not conclusive - Inference that they are not Mundas cannot be drawn and Patars of Tamar District in Bihar held are sub-tribe of Munda - Appeal Dismissed.
