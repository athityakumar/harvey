Bihar School Examination Board v Subhas Chandra Sinha, and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 March 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, A.N. Ray, I.D. Dua

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 100; (1970) 1 SCC 648; AIR 1970 SC 1269; [1970] 3 S.C.R. 963

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Bihar School Examinations Board Act 1952

Summary: Bihar School Examinations Board Act, 1952, ss. 6(2) and 9(3) - Constitution, art. 226 - Education - (A) Cancellation of examinations at one Centre by Chairman reported by him to the Board which endorsed it - (B) Writ petition u/art. 226 - Principles of natural Justice - Secondary School Examination - Adoption of unfair means by vast majority of examinees at particular centre - Board cancelling examination at that centre - Need of opportunity to represent to all - Held, cancellation must be treated as an order of he Board and cannot, therefore, be challenged on the ground that it was incompetently made - Opportunity to represent their cases to all candidates not necessary before action was taken - Appeal Allowed.
