State of West Bengal v Dalhousie Institute Society

Supreme Court of India

05 August 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

C.A. Vaidyialingam, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 481; (1970) 3 SCC 802; AIR 1970 SC 1778; 1970 UJ 737

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Indian Limitation Act, 1963, arts. 64 and 65 - Title of land - Acquisition of land by State Govt. - Given as grant to Institute - Invalid grant - Adverse possession by Institute for 60 years - Compensation for land - Whether compensation will go to State Govt. as title to land belongs to it due to invalid grant or to institute due to adverse possession? - Grant, whether valid or invalid, made in favour of institute - Possession of institute held was on basis of grant though invalid - Open, continuous and uninterrupted possession and enjoyment of site for over 60 years - Institute perfected its title by adverse possession - Persons in such possession acquires title by adverse possession - Held, compensation will go to institute - Appeal dismissed.
