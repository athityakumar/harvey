D.F.O., South Kheri and Others v Ram Sanehi Singh

Supreme Court of India

15 January 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 585; (1971) 3 SCC 864; AIR 1973 SC 205; 1970 UJ 290

Case Digest

Subject: Contract & Commercial

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Writ Petition - (A) Matter arising out of contract - Maintainability of writ - (B) New point - Objection not raised before High Court - However objection prominently mentioned in writ petition not traversed by other party - (C) Administrative order affecting property - Nature - Held, where action of a public authority invested with statutory powers is challenged, petition is maintainable even if right to relief arises out of an alleged breach of contract - In appeal by other party, respondent can be allowed to rely upon contention in support of order - Order has to be made in a manner consonant with rules of natural justice - Appeal dismissed.
