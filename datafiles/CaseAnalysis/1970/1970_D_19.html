Dev Kanta Barooah v Golok Chandra Baruah & Ors

Supreme Court of India

12 February 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

Vishishtha Bhargava, S.M. Sikri

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 350; (1970) 1 SCC 392; AIR 1970 SC 1231; [1970] 3 S.C.R. 662; 1970 UJ 352

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Keywords: Statement Of Fact, Leaflet

Summary: Election - Representation of the People Act, 1951, S. 123(4) - Leaflet - False Statements - Personal Character - Corrupt Practice - Appellant was declared elected in General Elections to Legislative Assembly, defeating respondents 1 to 4/rival candidates - Respondent No. 1 challenged election of appellant, on various grounds including charge of false statements as to personal character of respondent No. 1 - Trial court held that statements of fact made in leaflet did not relate to personal character or conduct of respondent No. 1 - High Court accepted ground and election of appellant was set aside - Hence instant appeal - Whether HC was right in setting aside election of appellant on ground of corrupt practice committed u/s. 123(4) of Act - Held, corrupt practice was alleged by respondent No. 1 to had been committed by appellant by publication of leaflet - Opportunity of official to misappropriate money occurred, only because an order passed earlier by respondent No. 1 as Chairman of Municipality and there was no personal corruption by him - HC was correct in recording same as not false statements, and they could not constitute corrupt practice under S. 123(4) of Act - Only statements, which related to personal character of respondent No. 1, was found to be true - Hence, material contained in leaflet did not require to be revised on the basis of evidence - Decision of HC was set aside and election petition dismissed - Appeal allowed.
