Khedu Mohton and Others v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

17 August 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, I.D. Dua

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 315; (1970) 2 SCC 450; (1970) SCC (Cr) 479; AIR 1971 SC 66; 1971 (1) ALT(SC) 288; 1971 CRLJ 20; [1971] 1 S.C.R. 839

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 379,149, 143 - The appellants were prosecuted for dishonestly cutting and removing the paddy crop - Instant appeal by special leave was directed against the decision of HC setting aside the acquittal of the appellants and convicting them u/ss. 379,149, 143 of IPC - Whether, the appellants were falsely implicated - Held, on the facts and the circumstances of the case and after considering all witnesses, instant court was of the view that, the complaint by the respondents was appeared imaginary one and hence, reached to the conclusion that HC erred while setting aside the order of appellant's conviction - Appeal allowed.
