Shree Gopal Paper Mills Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax, Central, Calcutta

Supreme Court of India

21 April 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, J.C. Shah, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 216; (1970) 2 SCC 80; AIR 1970 SC 1750; [1970] 77 ITR 543; [1971] 1 S.C.R. 323

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Assessment Year, Capital Gains, Bonus Shares

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Finance Act, 1956,Sch.1 Part II-D (i)(b), Sch. 1 Part II-D (II)(i)(a) - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922 - Income tax Act, 1961 - Finance Act, 1957 - A/y 1956-67 - Bonus Shares - Capital gains - Challenged - Appellant passed resolution in its general body meeting that Rs. 50,07,500/- from undivided profits of appellant company in General Reserve as on 30-6-1954 be capitalised and distributed to holders of ordinary shares in Company - For A/y 1956-67 relevant accounting period ending on 31-12-1955 AO by order dt.29-9-1958 determined total income of appellant at Rs. 42,73,176 - In computing tax AO had reduced rebate to which appellant company was entitled on two counts firstly in accordance with Sch.D part II (1) (a) of the 1956 Act and secondly excluded bonus shares from paid up capital of the company as on 1-1-1955 for determining excess dividends under Sch.D Part II (1) (b) of the 1956 Act - Appellant filed appeal - Appeal was disposed of holding that Sch.D part II (1) (a) would apply and that bonus shares were part of paid up capital as on 1-1-1955 one in favour of Revenue and another infavour of assessee - Aggrieved both filed appeals - Tribunal held infavour of Revenue and against assessee - HC answered Reference in favour of Revenue - Hence, instant appeals - Whether judgment of HC was justified - Held, it was clear from resolution that ordinary shareholders became owners of bonus shares from date of resolution - Authorities under the Act and HC placed undue emphasis on cls. (b) and (c) of resolution which laid down procedure to be adopted in carrying decision of General Meeting embodied in cl. (a) - HC and Tribunal were under erroneous impression that share could not be held to have been issued to person until share certificate is given to him - SC considered meaning of "allotment" in Sri Gopal Jalan and Co. v. Calcutta Stock Exchange Association Ltd,1963 Indlaw SC 375 - 'distribute' found in cl. (b) of resolution means distribution of shares in books of company - By resolution dt. 30-12-1954 portion of accumulated undivided profits were converted into capital and that capital was divided into bonus shares and allotted to ordinary shareholders on basis of their share holdings - Shares so allotted became property of shareholders from that date subject to getting dividends on those shares only from 1-1-1955 - Certain directions were given to Directors regarding implementation of that resolution u/cls. (b) and (c) of the resolution - Hence, AO order was not justified - Tribunal and HC judgments were set aside - Answered in favour of assessee and against Revenue - Appeal allowed.
