 State Of Mysore v H. Papanna Gowda & Anr. Etc

Supreme Court of India

24 November 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

G.K. Mitter, K.S. Hegde, A.N. Grover, A.N. Ray, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 191; (1970) 3 SCC 545; AIR 1971 SC 191; 1970 (2) LLJ 683; [1971] 2 S.C.R. 831; 1971 (2) SLR 48

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Education; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Research, University of Agricultural Sciences Act, 1963

Summary: Administrative - University of Agricultural Sciences Act, 1963 s. 7(5) - Service - Regularisation - Transferred - Enactment -Challenged - Respondent was appointed as agricultural demonstrator and later on his services were regularized when he was selected by Public Service Commission - On 04-04-1964 he was transferred and posted as Chemical assistant in department of agriculture - During his service, State had enacted law as 'University of Agricultural Sciences Act, 1963' - Aggrieved with such enactment respondents challenged vires of s.7(5) of the Act before HC which was decreed in favour of respondents - Hence, instant appeal - Whether prospects of respondents were prejudicially affected if he was to become an employee of the University.

Held, Act provides u/s. 7(5) that the terms and conditions of the Govt. employees immediately before the appointed day or the date specified in the notification were to be such as might be determined by the State Government in consultation with the Board.

Hence, appeals dismissed.
