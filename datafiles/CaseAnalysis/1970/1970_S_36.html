Shivamurthy Swami Inamdar and another v Agadi Sanganna Andanappa and another

Supreme Court of India

11 February 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 574; (1971) 3 SCC 870; 1970 UJ 334

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Keywords: Khadi and Village Industries Commission Act, 1956

Summary: Election - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 9(a), 116-A, 123(1), 123(3) and 123(7) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 102 - Election petition - High Court rejected allegations made on behalf of appellants and dismissed election petitions on ground of corrupt practice - Challenged - There is material difference between case pleaded and that sought to be proved - In appellants pleadings, none of necessary particulars of grounds alleged were given - Neither dates and time on which public meetings were held nor places where those meetings were held were mentioned therein - It would have been extremely difficult for returned candidates to rebut evidence adduced on behalf of appellants - Allegations were made in election petitions without ascertaining facts and thereafter evidence was gathered to support those allegations - Trial Court rightly held that no value can be attached to evidence adduced to show that returned candidates had contravened provisions of Act - Appeal dismissed.
