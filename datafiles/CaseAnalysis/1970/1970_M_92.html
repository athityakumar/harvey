T. N. Raghunatha Reddy v Mysore State Transport Authority

Supreme Court of India

24 February 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri, Vishishtha Bhargava, C.A. Vaidyialingam

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 344; (1970) SCC (Cr) 232; (1970) 1 SCC 541; AIR 1971 SC 1662; [1970] 3 S.C.R. 780

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Nationalisation

Summary: Motor Vehicles Act 1939, ss. 63, 68D(3), 68F(1), 63 and 68B, 68C - (A) Inter-State agreement regarding counter signature of inter-state permit - Kolar Scheme for Nationalisation of Road Transport approved and published in Mysore Gaz., D/-01-03-1968 - Total exclusion of private operators from nationalised routes except existing permit holders - Crucial date of determining existing permit holders within cl. (d) of scheme is date of publication or at least date on which Mysore State Transport applied for permits - Subsequent counter signature - (B) Inter-state agreement for counter-signature of inter-state permits envisaged in Proviso to s. 63 is not law - (C) Constitution, art.136 - New plea - Scheme imposing total exclusion of private operators whether offends art. 301 - Point raised before Supreme Court - Subsequent counter signature of inter-state permit by Mysore STA cannot make its holder existing permit holder - It cannot override provisions of chap. IV-A in view of specific provisions in s.68-B - Point not allowed to be raised before SCas it was not raised before HC - Appeal dismissed.
