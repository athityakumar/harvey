 Management Of Advance Insurance Co. Ltd v Shri Gurudasmal & Ors

Supreme Court of India

04 March 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, A.N. Grover, A.N. Ray, J.M. Shelat, C.A. Vaidyialingam

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 91; (1970) 1 SCC 633; AIR 1970 SC 1126; [1970] 3 S.C.R. 881

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Defence & Security Forces; Indirect Tax; Service

Summary: Constitution, sch. 7, List 1, entry 80, arts. 367, 372 and 372A - General Clauses Act, s. 3(58) - Delhi Special Police Establishment Act, 1946, ss. 2, 3, 5 and 6 - CPC, 1908, Pre. - Service matter - (A) Constitution, sch.7, List 1, entry 80 - (1) Belonging to any State - After Adaptation Order No. 1 of 1956, word 'State' includes 'Union Territory' - Members of Police force belonging to Union Territory - Jurisdiction, if extended to other States - (2) Meaning of expression 'police force belonging to any State' - (B) CPC, 1908, Pre. - Interpretation of Statutes - Provisions - (A) Held, members of Police force belonging to UT can have their jurisdiction extended to other States , art. 372A conferred fresh power on President to adapt - Expression means a police force constituted to function in an area - (B) Provisions must be read as far as possible with a view to their validity and not to render them invalid - Appeal Dismissed.
