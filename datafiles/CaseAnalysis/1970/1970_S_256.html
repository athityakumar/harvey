State Of Punjab & Ors v Sukh Deb Sarup Gupta

Supreme Court of India

29 April 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Grover, K.S. Hegde, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 72; (1970) 2 SCC 177; AIR 1970 SC 1641; [1971] 1 S.C.R. 442; 1970 (26) STC 303

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Keywords: East Punjab General Sales Tax Act, 1948

Summary: Indirect Tax - Punjab Excise Act, 1914 ss. 3(6)(c), 6 - Medicinal and Toilet Preparations(Excise Duties) Act (Central Act) - General Clauses Act, 1897, s.8 - Medicinal preparations - Levy of Excise duty - Enactment of new Act - Exemption claimed - Challenged - Respondent was running manufacturing spirituous and medicinal preparations and excise duty was payable u/s. 3(6)(c)of the Act - In 1955 Union Parliament had enacted Medicinal and Toilet Preparations(Excise Duties) Act/Central Act, to bring uniformity in all states in imposition of excise duty on alcoholic preparations - Respondent was assessed to sales tax on alcoholic preparations and excise duty was levied under Central Act by State authorities for A/Y's 1959-60, 1960-61,1961-62, that respondent could not claim exemption under Entry 37 of Sch. B of the Act - Aggrieved respondent filed petition before HC challenging levy of sales tax under the Act which was allowed by virtue of s.8 of 1897 Act under Entry 37and in appeal affirmed by DB - Hence, instant appeal by State - Whether respondent could claim exemption under Entry 37 of Sch. B of the Act notwithstanding that exemption expressly related only to goods on which duty could be levied under 1914 Act.

Held, meaning of that provision is that enactment would include any Act or provision contained therein passed by the Union Parliament or the State Legislature. HC, was right in saying that there' was nothing in s. 8 to indicate that the words 'former enactment' meant only a Central enactment and not a State enactment and that the courts would not be justified to read in that section words which were not there and to place a narrow and limited construction on the words 'former enactment.' Therefore, it has not been disputed that if s. 8 is applicable, respondent would be exempt from payment of tax under the Act on the alcoholic preparations on which excise duty is being levied under the provisions of the Central Act. Appeal dismissed.
