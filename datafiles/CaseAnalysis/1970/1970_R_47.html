Ramaphupala Reddy and Others v State of Andhra Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

28 September 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, S.M. Sikri

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 435; (1970) 3 SCC 474; (1971) SCC (Cr) 80; AIR 1971 SC 460; 1970 (2) ALT(SC) 300; 1971 CRLJ 422; 1971 UJ 6

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302 and 149 - Murder - Dispute between two factions - Witnesses reporting matter to police - District Court disbelieving witnesses acquitted all accused - High Court convicted appellants (Accused 1 to 5) under various charges including one u/ss. 302/149, IPC - Whether conviction for murder justified? - All points taken into consideration by Trial Court to arrive at conclusion of acquittal was considered by HC and it gave reasons for differing from conclusions reached by Trial Court - There was nothing wrong in approach adopted by HC - HC considered evidence on record on basis of human probabilities - Tested evidence given by witnesses by various methods known to law - After doing so it came to conclusion that evidence of P.W.s 1 and 3 is acceptable - Held, no reason to interfere with conclusions - Appeal dismissed.
