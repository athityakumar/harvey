Abdul Ghani v State of Jammu and Kashmir

Supreme Court of India

18 December 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

Vishishtha Bhargava, S.M. Sikri, I.D. Dua

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 201; (1970) 3 SCC 525; (1971) SCC (Cr) 131; AIR 1971 SC 1217; 1971 CRLJ 21; [1971] 3 S.C.R. 275

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Criminal - Jammu and Kashmir Preventive Detention Act, 1964, ss. 3(2) r/w 5, 8 and 13-A - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 32(4), 35(c) and 22 - Preventive Detention - Order of detention - Direction u/s. 8 proviso - Order of detention was D/- 09-05-1970 - Actual detention made on 22-05-1970 and direction u/s. 8 proviso also made on said date - Information conveyed to detenue that it is against public interest to disclose to him grounds on which detention order was made in pursuance of direction made by District Magistrate - Order of detention challenged on different grounds - Application of Constitution of India, 1950 to State of Jammu and Kashmir - Modification in art. 35(c) - Contention raised that application of modified art. 35(c) has abridged fundamental right of detenue to seek remedy against detention in Supreme Court u/art. 32, thus amounted to contravention of art. 32(4) of Constitution, 1950 and thus void - Contention whether acceptable? - Held, no - Constitutional validity of s. 8 proviso and s. 13-A, which has been enacted in pursuance of modified art. 35(c), has been upheld in decision of Court in 'Sampat Prakash v State of J and K Indlaw SC 192' - Effect of that amendment was only that, when approaching Supreme Court, detenue could not challenge validity of Act on ground that any provision of it contravened provisions of art. 32(4) - Held, modification in Constitution, 1950 had, therefore no bearing at all on art. 32(4) - Rest of contentions raised were also found to be without merit by Court - Order of detention upheld - Petition dismissed.
