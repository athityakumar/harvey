 R. L. Butail v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 September 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Shelat, G.K. Mitter, C.A. Vaidyialingam, A.N. Ray, Mohammad Hidayatullah

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 146; (1970) 2 SCC 876; 1970 (2) LLJ 514; [1971] 2 S.C.R. 55; 1970 SLR 926

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Keywords: Right To Promotion

Summary: Service - Indian Railway Establishment Code, 1951, rr. 148(3) and 149(3) - Central Civil Services (Classification, Control and Appeal) Rules, 1965, r. 11 - Civil Services Rules, r.16 - Whether order of compulsory retirement and non promotion of petitioner was justified? - Held, on question of non-promotion, appellant had demanded disclosure of proceedings before promotion committee, which demand was resisted by respondents by claiming privilege - It is not necessary to go into vexed question of privilege, firstly, because demand for disclosure was in nature of a fishing inquiry into papers relating to proceedings of that committee, and secondly, because adverse confidential reports, which, according to appellant, were responsible for committee's refusal to recommend his name, were communicated to appellant - Selection was made on merits with due regard to seniority and not seniority alone - Though petitioner has accused officials of alleged irregularities but there is no material on record to justify allegation - Appeal dismissed.
