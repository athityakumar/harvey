New Central Jute Mills Company Limited v Assistant Collector of Central Excise, Allahabad and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 September 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Grover, J.C. Shah, K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 456; (1970) 2 SCC 820; AIR 1971 SC 454; 1978 (2) E.L.T. 393; [1971] 2 S.C.R. 92

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax

Summary: Customs - Central Excise and Salt Act, 1944, s. 12 - Sea Customs Act, 1878, s. 12 - Customs Act, 1962, s. 105(1) - General Clauses Act, 1897, s. 8 - Search Warrant of Asst. Collector - Validity of - (A) Power of the Central Govt. - U/s. 12 of the Customs Act, 1962 to modify or alter the provisions of the Sea Customs Act, 1878 - Scope of - Held, Notification D/- 01-03-1964 issued u/ss. 105(1) and 110 inter alia - No such changes have been made to amounting 'alterations' - Power to restrict and modify does not import the power to make essential changes - Alterations of minor character, not a principle change - Word 'alterations' employed by the legislature legitimately in a constitutional manner - No question of delegation either of any essential legislative functions or any change of legislative policy - (B) Power of Central Govt. - U/s. 12 to apply s. 105(1) of Customs Act, 1962 when Sea Customs Act, 1878 having been repealed - Held, by virtue of s. 8(1) of General Clauses Act, s. 12 can be read in place of Sea Customs Act, 1878 - S. 12 only empowers the Central Govt. to apply the provision with necessary modification for the implementation of s. 3 of the Act - S. 12 containing limited delegating power - (C) Notification u/s. 12 - Empowering Asst. Collector instead of Magistrate to issue warrant for search and seizure - Validity of - Held, the Notification issued u/s. 12 of the Act superseded the previous notification under the Sea Customs Act, 1878 - No question of validity survives - (D) Issuance of warrant for seizure by Asst. Collector - Contention of not applied the mind to relevant facts - Held, warrant in which the documents have not been particularised or specified but the words 'certain documents' have been used - Single Judge repelled the contention - No merit - Appeal dismissed.
