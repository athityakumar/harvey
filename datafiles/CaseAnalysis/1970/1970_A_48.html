Uttam Bala Ravankar v Assistant Collector of Customs and Central Excise, Goa and Another

Supreme Court of India

03 August 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri, I.D. Dua

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 319; (1970) 2 SCC 396; (1970) SCC (Cr) 466; AIR 1970 SC 1765; 1970 CRLJ 1369; [1971] 1 S.C.R. 714

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898, s. 435 - Goa, Daman and Diu (Laws) Regulation, 1962, regns. 7 and 8 - Order passed by Governor that proceedings for offences committed prior to coming into force of CrPC, 1898 would be according to law in force in territory on date of offence - Complaint was filed against appellant - Sessions judge held that by virtue of impugned order procedure to be followed in case is one laid down by Portuguese Code of Criminal Procedure and not by (Indian) CrPC, 1898 - On a revision, Judicial Commr. came to conclusion that impugned order was not in conformity with 1961 order passed by Central Govt. - Question regarding validity of order passed by Governor? - Effect of s. 7, was that offenses committed prior to coming into force of CrPC, 1898 were to be investigated, inquired into under provisions of corresponding law in force in Goa, Daman and Diu - Held, yes - If investigations had been done under Portuguese Code of Criminal Procedure, unless there was some clear provision to deem that investigation as investigation under CrPC, fresh investigations under CrPC would have to be undertaken - Be that as it may, whatever difficulties which impelled Lt. Governor to act, he was competent to make provisions to remove difficulties - Impugned order is valid and prosecution must be conducted in accordance with its provisions - Appeal allowed.
