Mahabir Prasad Santosh Kumar v State Of U.P. And Ors

Supreme Court of India

02 April 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 337; (1970) 1 SCC 764; AIR 1970 SC 1302; [1971] 1 S.C.R. 201

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950 - Sugar Dealers' Licensing Order, 1962 - Detection of Irregularities - Essential Commodities - License Cancelled - Opportunity of Being Heard - Appellants were holding license under Order, 1962 to deal in sugar as "wholesale distributors" under Order 1964 - As irregularities were detected on inspection of appellants shop and directed to hand over all their stocks of sugar and flour to Marketing Society - District Magistrate, cancelled dealers licenses of appellants and were deprived of their right to carry on business without opportunity to explain alleged irregularities, as appeal was rejected - Appellants petition before HC for quashing orders of District Magistrate was rejected - Hence instant appeal - Whether proceedings of authorities exercising power under Order, 1962 and Order, 1964, were maintainable - Held, Court viewed that HC erred in holding that appellants had right not only to had an opportunity to make a representation which implicated nature of jurisdiction exercised by appellate authority - Nature of proceeding required that Respondent/State Govt. should give adequate reasons to disclose that an attempt was made to reach conclusion according to law and just - Orders passed by District Magistrate and Respondent cancelling licenses of appellants were quashed - Order accordingly.
