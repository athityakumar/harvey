Commissioner of Income Tax, West Bengal, Calcutta v Shri Prem Bhai Parekh and Others

Supreme Court of India

20 April 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, A.N. Grover, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 54; (1970) 1 SCC 784; AIR 1970 SC 1518; [1970] 77 ITR 27; [1971] 1 S.C.R. 308

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act,1922, s. 16(3) - Accounting year - Reconstitution of firm - Benefits of partnership - Transfer of asset - Ultra vires - Assesses was partner of firm having annas share and continued to be partner of firm till dt. 1-7-1954 of accounting year of firm relevant for assessment year 1955-1956 - On 1-7-1954, assesses retired from firm and he gifted to each of his four sons Rs., 75,000/- Out of his four sons, three were minors at that time - There was reconstitution of firm with effect from dt. 2-71954 as evidenced by partnership deed dt. 5-7-1954 - Major son of assesses became partner of reconstituted firm and his minor sons were admitted to benefits of partnership in reconstituted firm and major son had 2 annas share and three minor brothers were admitted to benefits of partnership having 2 annas share - In assessment year 1956-1957, Income Tax Officer ordered that income arising to minors by virtue of their admission to benefits of partnership came within purview of s.16(3) (a) (iv) of 1922 Act and included that income in total income of assesses for that year - Tribunal upheld the decision of Appellate Assistant Commissioner - On appeal, on an interpretation of S. 16(3) (a) (iv), HC opined that the answer to the question must be in favour of the assesses - Instant appeal - Whether benefits of partnership of firm was rightly included in total income of assesses u/s.16(3) (a) (iv) of 1922 Act was ultra vires - Held, s.16(3) of 1922 Act created artificial income and it must be proved to have arisen directly or indirectly from transfer of assets made by assesses in favour of his wife or minor children - Connection between transfer of assets and income must be proximate - Before an income could be held to come within the ambit of s. 16(3), it must be proved to have arisen-directly or indirectly-from a transfer of assets made by the assesses in favour of his wife or minor children - Income in question must arise as a result of the transfer and not in some manner connected with it - In the instant case, connection between gifts mentioned and income in question was remote one - Income of minors arose as result of their admission to benefits of partnership and they were admitted to benefits of partnership because of contribution made by them - No nexus between transfer of assets and income that income arose directly or indirectly from transfer of assets referred to earlier - Appeal dismissed.
