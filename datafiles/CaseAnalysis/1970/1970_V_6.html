Union of India v Vasant Jayaram Karnik and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 September 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, K.S. Hegde, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 244; (1970) 3 SCC 658; AIR 1970 SC 2092; [1970] 78 ITR 243; 1970 SLR 813; 1970 UJ 822

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 16(1) - List of seniority of Asst. CIT - Prepared by Union Public Service Commission, by modifying the relevant rules - Alleging violation u/art. 16(1) of the Constitution - Praying for the quashing of list - Whether High Court was justified interpreting that provision (b) to r. iii of Union Public Service Commission modified Rules was to neutralize effect of minimum service rule in determining seniority in grade of Asst. Commr.? - Held, HC was right in observing that proviso (b) to r. iii of Union Public Service Commission modified Rules was intended to neutralise effect of minimum service rule in determining seniority in grade of Asst. Commr. - R. (iii) of Union Public Service Commission modified Rules is not intended, contrary to all notions of justice and fair-play, to confer upon an officer, who was junior in list of ITO's and who could not be considered for promotion on an earlier occasion, a right to be placed in list of Asst. Commr.s above an officer senior to him in list of ITO and who was promoted before him - Appeal dismissed.
