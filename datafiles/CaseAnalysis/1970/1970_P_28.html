State of Assam and Others v Premadhar Baruah and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 May 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Ray, I.D. Dua

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 327; (1970) 2 SCC 211; AIR 1970 SC 1314; 1970 LabIC 1067; [1971] 1 S.C.R. 503; 1970 SLR 529

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Constitution, arts.14 and 309 - Fundamental Rule, r. 6(a) - Indian Railway Establishment Code, 1951 - Service matter - (A) Rule enabling Govt. to retain Govt. servant in service after age of 55 years - In special circumstances - Notification allowing retention of civil servants beyond superannuation upon exigencies of public service and consideration of physical fitness and efficiency - Efffect - Constitutionality - (B) Government notification making 58 years as age of compulsory retirement - Subsequent notification deciding age of compulsory retirement to be 55 years as laid down in r. 56 (a) - Whether rules u/art. 309 of Constitution - Held, no right to continue in service after 55 years' of age can be founded upon this notification and notification does not infringe art. 14 - Both notifications are executive instructions and are not rules u/art. 309 - Appeal Allowed.
