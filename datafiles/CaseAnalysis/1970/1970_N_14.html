Narayan Nathu Naik v State Of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

25 March 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 89; (1970) 2 SCC 101; (1970) SCC (Cr) 316; AIR 1971 SC 1656; [1971] 1 S.C.R. 133

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Evidence Act, 1872, s. 8 - IPC, 1860, s. 300 - Murder Case - (A) Court satisfied about accused being assailant of victim - Question of motive - (B) Evidence - Appreciation of - Occurrence in midnight at door of cottage of deceased - There was sufficient light from lantern - Three out of five eye-witnesses close relative of deceased - Remaining two resiling - Their evidence corroborating that of three eye witnesses except as to identity of assailant - (C) Evidence - Appreciation of - Circumstantial evidence - Absence of blood marks at scene of offence - Deceased stabbed on spot receiving wound in heart - Court held, need not consider question of motive - Evidence of three eye-witnesses even though close relations of deceased held to be acceptable - Fact that decease might have bleeded internally is sufficient explanation for this circumstance, however, blood was in fact found there though source of blood could not be ascertained - Appeal Dismissed.
