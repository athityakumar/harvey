Raj Kumar Singh Hukam Chandji v Commissioner of Income Tax, M. P.

Supreme Court of India

11 August 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, A.N. Grover, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 249; (1970) 2 SCC 436; AIR 1971 SC 1454; [1970] 78 ITR 33; [1971] 1 S.C.R. 748; 1970 UJ 855

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 66 - Remuneration received by Managing director of HUF - Assessbility - Whether the managing directors remuneration received by 'X' was assessable in his individual hands and not in the hands of the assessee HUF - Assessee was a HUF and the a/y was 1954-55.

Held, whether the remuneration received by the coparcener in substance though not in form was but one of the modes of return made to the family because of the investment of the family funds in the business or whether it was a compensation made for the services rendered by the individual coparcener. If it is the former, it is an income of the HUF but if it is the latter then it is the, income of the individual coparcener. If the income was essentially earned as a result of the funds in- vested the fact that a coparcener has rendered some service would not change the character of the receipt. But if on the other hand it is essentially a remuneration for the services rendered by a coparcener, the circumstance that his services were availed of because of the reason that he was a member of the family which had invested funds in that business or that he had obtained the qualification shares from out of the family funds would not make the receipt, the income of the HUF. In the instant case, income in question was the individual income of 'X'. There is no material to hold that he was elected managing director on behalf of the family. The income in question cannot be treated as the income of the assessee. The managing director's remuneration received by 'X' was assessable as his individual income and not as the income of his HUF. Appeal allowed.
