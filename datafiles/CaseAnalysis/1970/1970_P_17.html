Perumal Nadar (Dead) By LRs v Ponnuswami

Supreme Court of India

17 March 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, K.S. Hegde, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 530; (1970) 1 SCC 605; AIR 1971 SC 2352; 1970 (2) MLJ(SC) 121; [1971] 1 S.C.R. 49

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Prohibition Act

Summary: Family & Personal - Madras Hindu (Bigamy Prevention and Divorce) Act, 1949, ss. 3 and 4(1) - Respondent filed suit for separate possession of a half share in properties of joint family held by his father - Petitioner denied being married to mother of respondent - Trial Court decreed suit - Challenged - Whether there was valid marriage between appellant and respondent's mother 'N'? - Held, marriage ceremony was performed according to Hindu rites and customs - It is in evidence that marriage between Hindu males belonging to Nadar community and Christian females are common and wife after marriage is accepted as a member of Hindu Nadar community - A person may be a Hindu by birth or by conversion - But a bona fide intention to be converted to Hindu faith, accompanied by conduct unequivocally expressing that intention may be sufficient evidence of conversion - No formal ceremony of purification or expiation is necessary to effectuate conversion - On evidence there can be no doubt that 'N' bona fide intended to contract marriage with appellant - (B) Whether respondent was legitimate child - Conclusive presumption u/s. 112 of Indian Evidence Act can only be displaced if it is shown that parties to marriage had no access at any time when child could have been begotten - Held, there is no evidence to establish that appellant living in same village as 'N' had no access to 'N' during time when respondent could have been begotten - Appeal dismissed.
