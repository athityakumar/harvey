 Tata Engineering & Locomotive Co. Limited v The Assistant Commissioner Of Commercialtaxes & Another

Supreme Court of India

02 March 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Grover, K.S. Hegde, A.N. Ray, I.D. Dua, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 106; (1970) 1 SCC 622; AIR 1970 SC 1281; [1970] 3 S.C.R. 862; 1970 (26) STC 354

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Keywords: Stock Transfer

Summary: Central Sales Tax Act, 1956, ss. 2(g), 3 and 6 - (A) Term 'Sale' as defined in s. 2(g) is different from contract of sale - To be exigible to tax movement of goods from one Sate to another - It ought to be under a covenant or an incident of contract - Movement of vehicles from works site to various stock-yards - Appropriation to contract made only from stock-yards - Exigibility to tax - (B) Sale of motor vehicles - Determination of whether movement of vehicles from manufacturing site to stock-yards in different States was occasioned by covenant or incident of the contract of sale - Assessing authority should examine each transaction to see whether it was a completed contract of sale - Assessment for a period based on examination of specimen transaction - Legality -Held, movement from works site to stock-yards is not a 'sale' exigible to sakes tax - Assessment , even if it is by consent of party, is illegal - Appeal Allowed.
