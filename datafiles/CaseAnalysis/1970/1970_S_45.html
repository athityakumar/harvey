Dalmia Dadri Cement Limited v Shri Murari Lal Bikaneria

Supreme Court of India

19 August 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

G.K. Mitter

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 30; (1970) 3 SCC 259; AIR 1971 SC 22; 1970 (21) FLR 201; 1971 LabIC 1; 1970 (2) LLJ 416; 1970 UJ 795

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, sch. 2, item 6 - Dismissal of workman after domestic enquiry - Bias therein - (A) Enquiry officer being junior advocate and at times appearing on behalf of management - (B) Enquiry officer being lawyer and his report bearing stamp of a lawyer's work - If he he acted unduly hastily - (C) Whether Works Manager applied his mind to facts before ordering dismissal - Mere fact that Works Manager referred to opinion of others did not exclude his having formed his own opinion - (D) Act of indiscipline - Worker blowing whistle at the instance of another worker leading to stoppage of work in factory - Such act of indiscipline cannot be tolerated by employer - Held, no bias to be inferred - Circumstances do not justify conclusion that he acted unduly hastily - His view being same as that of Director no exception could be taken to his having referred to such opinion - Such act of indiscipline cannot be tolerated by employer - Appeal Allowed.
