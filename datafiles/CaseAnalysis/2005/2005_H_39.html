Union of India v Harinder Pal Singh and others

Supreme Court of India

26 October 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Ashok Bhan

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 920; (2005) 12 SCC 564; AIR 2006 SC 447; 2006 (62) ALR 472; 2006 (1) AWC 26; JT 2005 (9) SC 280; 2005 (4) RCR(Civil) 638; 2005(8) SCALE 741; 2005 (Supp) RD 574; [2005] Supp4 S.C.R. 669

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, s. 4 - Acquisition of land for extension of Cantonment at Amritsar - Awards passed by Reference Court modified to extent that claimants of five villages would be entitled to compensation at rate of Rs. 40,000/-, per acre irrespective of nature of land and would also be entitled to statutory interest and solatium at rate of 30% - Appealed against - Plea that HC erred in abandoning belting system and taking recourse to a uniform rate for all lands comprised in five different villages on account of fact that all lands were not similar in nature and did not enjoy same privileges - Held, entire area is in a stage of development and different villages are capable of being developed in same manner as lands comprised in Kala Ghanu Pur where market value of acquired lands was fixed at a uniform rate of Rs. 40,000/- per acre - Appeals dismissed.
