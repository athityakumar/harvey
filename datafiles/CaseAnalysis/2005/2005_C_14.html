C.S. Krishnamurthy v State of Karnataka

Supreme Court of India

29 March 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Mathur, P. Venkatarama Reddi

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 217; (2005) 4 SCC 81; (2005) SCC (Cr) 923; AIR 2005 SC 2790; 2005 CRLJ 2145; 2005 (2) JhrCR(SC) 249; JT 2005 (11) SC 114; 2005 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 466; 2005 (2) RCR(Criminal) 409; 2005(3) SCALE 408; [2005] 2 S.C.R. 1163

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Prevention of Corruption Act 1947, s.5(2) r/w s.5(1)(e) - Single Judge set aside order of acquittal - Whether sanction granted by authority is a valid sanction or not? - Held, sanction order itself discloses facts that incumbent is being prosecuted for accumulating moveable and immovable assets worth Rs. 4,01,454 58 paise which is disproportionate to his known source of income and he has failed to give satisfactory account for same - Facts mentioned in sanction order are eloquent for constituting prima facie offence under s.5(2) r/w s.5(1)(e) - There is due application of mind by sanctioning authority and sanction is valid - No ground to interfere - Appeal dismissed.
