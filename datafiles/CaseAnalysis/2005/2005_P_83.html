Punj Lloyd Limited v Petronet MHB Limited

Supreme Court of India

29 April 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, D.M. Dharmadhikari, B.N. Srikrishna

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 1825; (2006) 2 SCC 638; 2006 (143) PLR 813; 2006 (3) RCR(Civil) 836; 2005(3) SCALE 299

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, s. 11 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Appointment of arbitrator - Contract was executed - Disputes and differences arose between the parties - Appellant served a 30 days' notice on the respondent demanding appointment of an arbitrator and reference of disputes to him - Respondent failed to act - Arbitrator u/s. 11(6) of the Act was appointed - However, respondent had not made appointment consistently with the arbitration clause - Judge designated by the Chief Justice of the HC refused to appoint the arbitrator holding that the only remedy available to the appellant was to move in accordance with cl. 14.1, whereupon the Functional Director would adjudicate upon the disputes as sole arbitrator - Appellant filed a petition u/art. 226 of the Constitution, which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, in cases arising u/s. 11(6) of the Act, if the opposite party has not made an appointment within 30 days of demand, the right to make appointment is not forfeited but continues, but an appointment has to be made before the former files application u/s. 11 of the Act seeking appointment of an arbitrator. Only then the right of the opposite party ceases. The order of the designated Judge and the order of the DB both set aside. The application filed u/s. 11(6) of the Act shall stand restored before the Chief Justice of the HC and taken afresh for consideration and appointment of arbitrator in accordance with s. 11(6) of the Act. Appeal allowed.
