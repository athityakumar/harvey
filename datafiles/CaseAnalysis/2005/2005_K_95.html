Karnataka State Election Commission v H. C. Yatheesh Kumar and others

Supreme Court of India

21 November 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.K. Sabharwal, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 1794; (2006) 9 SCC 181

Case Digest

Subject: Election; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Karnataka Panchayat Raj (Reservation df Seats in Taluk Panchayats and Zila Panchayats) Rules, 1998

Summary: Election - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 243-E, 243-O - Karnataka Panchayat Raj Act, 1993, s. 308 - Karnataka Panchayat Raj (Reservation of Seats in Taluk Panchayats and Zila Panchayats) Rules, 1998 - Taluk Panchayat Election - Ultra vires - Vacation of order - Panchayat elections under 1998 Rules were held in 2000 by appellant/State Commission and respondents questioned said election and 1998 Rules before HC which held that 1998 Rules were ultra vires and void - Aggrieved appellant challenged order of HC before SC on SLP - Leave was granted by SC which was opposed by respondents - Appellant prayed for grant of interim relief which was allowed on 3-10-2005 on ground that elections ought to be held and same cannot be kept in abeyance indefinitely - Aggrieved respondents sought vacation of said interim order with amend- Hence instant petitions.

Held, SC observes that in view of order dt. 3-10-2005, appellant State Commission has on 14-10-2005, issued calendar of events to conduct elections to Taluk Panchayats and Zila Panchayats in ‘K’ State u/s. 308 of 1993 Act. Calendar of events sets out the schedule for various steps to be taken including the dates of polling and counting of votes. Elections were last held in July 2000, 1998 Rules held to be ultra vires by HC were made in 1998 and Writ petitions were filed in 2005. order of SC which has led to preparations being made and huge amounts spent for conduct of elections was passed on 3-10-2005, calendar of events published on 14-10-2005 and present applications have been filed on 16-11-2005. U/art. 243-E of Constitution, Panchayat elected in 2000 will continue for five years from date appointed for its first meeting and no longer. Question is also about applicability and interpretation of art. 243-O of Constitution which bars interference by Courts in electoral matters. SC is of view that question requires deeper consideration and that is reason that leave was granted, as above noted. Having regard to totality of circumstances, SC finds no ground to vacate order dt. 3-10-2005 which, if vacated, would result in staying the elections which have already been directed to be held subject to result of present appeal. SC is of view that application seeking vacation of order dt. 3-10-2005 is dismissed. Petitions disposed of.

Ratio - Elections ought to be held and same cannot be kept in abeyance indefinitely due to certain irregularities because in elections lot of public money is involved.
