Haldia Refinery Canteen Employees Union and others v M/s. Indian Oil Corporation Limited and others

Supreme Court of India

29 April 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhan, A.K. Mathur

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 345; (2005) 5 SCC 51; (2005) SCC (L&S) 593; AIR 2005 SC 2412; 2005 (105) FLR 1051; JT 2005 (5) SC 62; 2005 (2) LLN 971; 2005(4) SCALE 487; [2005] 3 S.C.R. 972; 2005 (5) SLR 317; 2005 (2) UPLBEC 2021

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: [A.] Labour & Industrial Law - Factories Act, 1948, ss. 2(1), 46 - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 16(4) - Employees working in canteen - Management refused to grant status of regular employees to appellants and treated them as employees of canteen contractor - Whether appellants were entitled to be regularized? - Held, no power vests in management either to make appointment or to take disciplinary action against erring workmen and their dismissal or removal from service - Management is not reimbursing to contractor wages of workmen - It cannot be concluded that contractor was nothing but an agent or a manager of respondent working completely under supervision and control of management - Appellants did not become workers of management for a purpose other than Factories Act - Appeal dismissed.
