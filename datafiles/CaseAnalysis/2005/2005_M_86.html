N.K. Rajgarhia v M/s. Mahavir Plantation Limited and others

Supreme Court of India

16 December 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, P.K. Balasubramanyan

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 957; (2006) 1 SCC 502; 2006 (62) ALR 491; 2006 (1) AWC 857; 2006 (2) JhrCR(SC) 13; 2006 (2) JhrCR(SC) 332; JT 2006 (1) SC 70; 2006 (2) MLJ(SC) 456; 2006 (1) RCR(Civil) 129; 2006 (100) RD 371B; 2005(10) SCALE 194; 2006 (1) SCJ 308; [2005] Supp5 S.C.R. 882

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Summary: CPC Interpretation of a consent order Compromise in terms whereof Appellant was to receive a sum of Rs. 41,69,110/- Interpretation of words 'balance decree' Default in making payment by respondent Contention that once a default is committed by Respondent, Appellant would be entitled to execute balance decree which was waived by him Held, sum which was waived by Appellant did not form part of consent decree It was merely a claim Such a claim never fructified into any decree and in that view of matter plea of Respondent being liable to pay said amount to Appellant despite fact that no decree in relation thereto was passed cannot be countenanced Appeal dismissed.
