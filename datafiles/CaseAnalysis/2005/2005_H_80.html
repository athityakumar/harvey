Hindustan Steel Works Construction Limited and v Hindustan Steel Works Construction Limited

Supreme Court of India

11 August 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 460; (2005) 6 SCC 725; (2005) SCC (L&S) 899; 2006 (3) ALD(SC) 29; 2005 (6) AWC 5494; 2005 (106) FLR 998; 2005 (4) JhrCR(SC) 75; JT 2005 (7) SC 273; 2005(6) SCALE 430; 2005 (8) SLR 307; [2005] Supp2 S.C.R. 540; 2005 (3) UPLBEC 2923

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Keywords: City Compensatory Allowance, Defence Service

Summary: [A.] Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 9-A, 10 - Withdrawal of construction allowance which was being earlier allowed - High Court in writ petiton held that withdrawal is violative of s.9-A - Appealled herein - Held, DB did not discuss basic issues about applicability of s.9-A and whether on facts of case s.9-A has really any application - Directed appropriate Govt. to refer question for adjudication by appropriate Tribunal - Appeal allowed.
