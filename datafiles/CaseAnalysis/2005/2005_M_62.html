Global Energy Limited and Another v M/s. Adani Exports Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

03 May 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

G.P. Mathur, R.C. Lahoti

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 384; (2005) 4 SCC 435; AIR 2005 SC 2653; 2005 (5) AWC 4759; 2005 (3) JhrCR(SC) 143; JT 2005 (5) SC 121; 2005(4) SCALE 551; [2005] 3 S.C.R. 1108; 2005 (2) UPLBEC 2030

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Electricity & Energy; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Electricity & Energy - Electricity Act, 2003 - Sale of surplus power - Notice inviting tenders - Condition requiring deposit of Rs. 30 lakhs as earnest money - Whether condition is discriminatory or contrary to express mandate of the Act? - Held, terms of invitation of tender are not open to judicial scrutiny and Courts cannot whittle down terms of tender as they are in realm of contract unless they are wholly arbitrary, discriminatory or actuated by malice - No ground has been made out by appellants, which may warrant interference by this Court with decision taken by West Bengal State Electricity Board in not awarding contract to Appellant No. 1 as price offered cannot be sole criteria in matter of trading of power - Appeals dismissed.
