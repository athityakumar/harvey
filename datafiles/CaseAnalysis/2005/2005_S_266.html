Damodaran Pillai and Others v South Indian Bank Limited

Supreme Court of India

08 September 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Ashok Bhan

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 546; (2005) 7 SCC 300; AIR 2005 SC 3460; 2005 (61) ALR 205; 2005 (4) AWC 3160; 2006 (1) CGLJ 12; 2006 (2) JhrCR(SC) 145; JT 2005 (8) SC 197; 2005 (4) KLT 192; 2005 (4) MLJ(SC) 163; 2005 (4) RCR(Civil) 132; 2005 (99) RD 657; 2005(7) SCALE 229; [2005] Supp3 S.C.R. 117; 2005 (3) UC 1793

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Ex-Parte, Ex-Parte Order

Summary: [A.] Civil Procedure - CPC, 1908, o. 21 rr. 106(3), 105, s. 151 - Limitation Act, 1963, s. 5 - Interpretation of o. 21, r.106(3) - Execution Petition dismissed for default on 01-11-1990 - Restoration application filed on 04-04-1998 allowed - Appealed herein - Contention that restoration application was filed beyond prescribed period of limitation - Held, when an application is dismissed for default in terms of r.105, starting period of limitation would be date of order and not knowledge thereabout - Restoration must be filed only within a period of thirty days from date of order - Application u/s. 5 of Limitation Act was not maintainable - Appeal allowed.
