Mrs. Sanjana M. Wig v Hindustan Petro Corporation Limited

Supreme Court of India

15 September 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 556; (2005) 8 SCC 242; AIR 2005 SC 3454; 2005 (6) ALD(SC) 110; 2005 (61) ALR 786; 2005 (6) AWC 5153; 2006 (1) Bom.C.R. 689; 2005 (4) JhrCR(SC) 249; JT 2005 (8) SC 334; 2005 (4) RCR(Civil) 160; 2005(7) SCALE 290; [2005] Supp3 S.C.R. 190

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Contract & Commercial - Specific Relief Act, 1963, s. 14 - Scope of judicial review vis-a-vis availability of alternative remedy - Whether a discretionary jurisdiction would not be exercised solely on ground of existence of an alternative remedy which is more efficacious? - Held, a writ will be entertained when it involves a public law character or involves a question arising out of public law functions on part of respondent or if an action of the party is de'hors the terms and conditions contained in an agreement and is beyond the scope and ambit of domestic forum created therefor- Appellant committed a default in payment of dues towards supplies made and having regard to fact that dealership agreement has come to an end, it is not a fit case where we would set aside impugned order of HC - Appeal dismissed.
