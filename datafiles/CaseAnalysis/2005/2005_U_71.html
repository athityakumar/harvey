Messrs Ashoka Smokeless Coal Industries Private v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 December 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, P.K. Balasubramanyan

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 797; (2006) 9 SCC 228; AIR 2006 SC 696; 2006 (2) JhrCR(SC) 88; JT 2005 (10) SC 508; 2005(10) SCALE 136; 2006 (1) SCJ 686; [2005] Supp5 S.C.R. 718

Case Digest

Subject: Mines & Minerals; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Mines & Minerals - Scheme of selling coal through E-Auction which meant that industrial concerns which had linkage with Coal India Ltd and its subsidiaries for supply of coal to them had to pay more for coal compared to what they were paying earlier or what was known as notified price- Some of HCs directed linked Undertakings to furnish bank guarantees for difference in prices or to pay cash or to furnish security for difference while seeking supply of coal, based on linkage - Held, there is substance in argument that mere furnishing of security or bank guarantee would not enable Coal India Ltd and its subsidiaries to carry on their business which it was necessary to do in interests of whole Nation - Directed to pay 33 1/3% of enhanced price by petitioners - Tranfer petitions of Coal India Ltd allowed - Order accordingly.
