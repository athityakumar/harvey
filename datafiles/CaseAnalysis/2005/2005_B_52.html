Banhisikha Roy (Now Sengupta) v Somnath Roy and others

Supreme Court of India

25 July 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

D.M. Dharmadhikari, B.N. Srikrishna

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 1618; (2005) 12 SCC 500; AIR 2006 SC 685; 2005(6) SCALE 6

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Summary: Criminal – Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code,1860, ss.34,498A,406 - Demand of dowry – Quashing of proceeding – Validity of - On demand of dowry and cruelty complaint was filed by petitioner against respondent before Magistrate – However, HC quashed said criminal proceedings initiated against respondent u/ss.498A,406 r/w s.34 of IPC – Hence, instant Appeal.

Held, on perusal of the contents of the complaint filed before Magistrate it was clear that on several occasions both the accused persons assaulted complainant mercilessly as she did not ask her father for making payments of rs.50,000 as demanded by accused no.1 and all accused persons after assaulting her forcefully took her to uncle's house on 4-6-1995 saying that if she did not bring money she would have no place in their house. On said date accused no. 1 also stated that he would not accept the complainant as his wife anymore. That on 4-6-1995 the complainant was forced to come back in her uncle's house with accused nos.1,2 and since then in spite of several requests accused no.1 has never come to take complainant in their house. Thus, these are clear allegations of cruelty meted out to appellant wife on demand of dowry. Therefore, HC is clearly in error in coming to conclusion that prima facie no case for offence u/s.498A of IPC is made out against the respondent. Therefore, the impugned order of the HC is quashed. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Where there is clear case of cruelty made out by evidence then proceeding initiated for same cannot be quashed.
