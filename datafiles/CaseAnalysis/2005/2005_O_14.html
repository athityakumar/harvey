U.P.State Road Transport Corporation Through Its Chairman v Omaditya Verma & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

05 April 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Mathur, Ashok Bhan

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 1834; (2005) 4 SCC 424; 2005 (2) ACC 400; 2005 (3) AWC 2343; JT 2005 (4) SC 62; 2005 (4) MLJ(SC) 43; 2005(3) SCALE 636; [2005] 3 S.C.R. 166

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Carriers & Transportation - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, s. 68F(2) - Notified route - Respondents filed writ petitions submitting that petitioner be directed to issue permit to all grantees on the basis of resolution 14.06.1993 - HC allowed said writ petitions and directed petitioner to issue permit to all grantees who have not been issued permits on the basis of the resolution dt. 14.6.1993 forthwith and without any delay - Petitioner granted 38 permits for notified route - Subsequently, petitioner filed appeal against said order of the HC contending that the directions of the HC was against the notification dt. 3.9.1994 - Whether appeal filed by the petitioner against order of the HC could be allowed? - Held, impugned route was notified way back in 1951, no permit could have been issued in pursuance of the resolution dt. 14.6.1993 and likewise under notification dt. 3.9.1994 when the route from Muzaffarnagar to Bijnore had been notified, no permit could have been granted on the aforesaid route as both schemes were of total exclusion - Therefore, the impugned question no more remains res integra as it was settled principle of law that no private operators could be permitted to operate on a notified route except by modifying scheme and after making provisions for the same - Hence, view taken by the HC cannot be sustained and set aside - Appeals allowed.
