Hari Chand and Others v Faridabad Complex Administration and Others

Supreme Court of India

26 April 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

C.K. Thakker, Ruma Pal

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 323; (2005) SCC (L&S) 513; (2005) 4 SCC 592; AIR 2005 SC 2429; 2005 (2) ESC 252; 2005 (105) FLR 765; JT 2005 (4) SC 608; 2005 (3) LLN 64; 2005(4) SCALE 419; [2005] 3 S.C.R. 903; 2005 (3) SLR 752; 2005 (4) Supreme 595; 2005 (2) UPLBEC 1994

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: [A.] Service - Faridabad Complex (Regulation and Development) Act, 1971 - Constitution, arts. 14, 19, 226 - Punjab Civil Services Rules, r. 3.16 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 11 - Petitioners were employees of Faridabad Development Board which was converted into Faridabad Complex Administration - Prayer to declare that petitioners were entitled to pensionary benefits as admissible to employees of Haryana Govt. - Held, Rule 3 .16 Note 1 of Punjab Civil Services Rules which excluded Municipal employees from payment of pensions, DB was right in holding that appellants were not entitled to pension - Action of respondent-Corporation, cannot be described as arbitrary, discriminatory or unreasonable, violative of art.14 or 19 of Constitution - Appeals dismissed.
