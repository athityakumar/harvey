Pramod Kumar Jaiswal And Others v Bibi Husn Bano & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

03 May 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

P.K. Balasubramanyan

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 347; (2005) 5 SCC 492; AIR 2005 SC 2857; 2005 (60) ALR 173; 2005 (2) AWC 1697; 2006 (2) Bom.C.R. 855; 2005 (3) JhrCR(SC) 4; 2005 (3) JhrCR(SC) 153; JT 2005 (5) SC 79; 2006 (2) MLJ(SC) 372; 2005 (141) PLR 27; 2005 (2) RCR(Civil) 629; 2005 (1) RCR(Rent) 570; [2005] 3 S.C.R. 1119

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure; Rent Control

Summary: [A.] Land & Property - Rent Control - Bihar Buildings (Lease, Rent and Eviction) Control Act - Transfer Of Property Act, 1882, ss. 105, 108(q), 109, 111(d), 44 - Appeal against a direction to tenant to deposit rent in arrears - Effect of assignment of rights of certain co-owner by tenants of building, on the lease originally taken by them and on basis of which they held building - Lease or tenancy does not get determined, by the tenant acquiring the rights of a co-owner landlord and a merger takes place and the lease gets determined only if the entire reversion or the entire rights of the landlord are purchased by the tenant - Held, u/s. 111(d) a lessee who has taken assignment of rights of a co-owner lessor, cannot successfully raise plea of determination of tenancy on ground of merger of his lessee's estate in that of estate of landlord - Position of appellants as tenants continue and they are bound to comply with requirements of Rent Control Act under which order for deposit has been passed - Appeal dismissed.
