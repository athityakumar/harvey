M/s. A.B.C. (India) Limited v State of Assam and another

Supreme Court of India

04 August 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

AR. Lakshmanan, S.N. Variava

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 497; (2005) 6 SCC 424; AIR 2005 SC 3215; JT 2005 (7) SC 197; 2005(6) SCALE 95; 2005 (142) STC 88; [2005] Supp2 S.C.R. 36

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate; Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Summary: [A.] Constitution - Sales Tax - Assam General Sales Tax Act, 1993, ss. 42, 44, 44(3), 44(5), 44(5)(b), 44(6), 44(7), 46A, 61, 62, 72(1) (the Assam Act)- Assam General Sales Tax Rules, 1993, r.12A - Haryana General Sales Tax Act, 1973, s. 38 - Tripura Sales Tax Act, 1976, ss. 29, 36A, 38B (the Tripura Act) - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 14, 19(1)(g), 21, 300A, 32 - Validity of ss. 42, 44 of Assam Act challenged as ultra vires the Constitution - Penalty on transporters, dealer and agents on non furnishing of particulars or not obtaining certificate of registration - Whether ultra vires? - Held, following Tripura Goods Transport Association Anr. Vs. Commissioner of Taxes Ors.(1998 Indlaw SC 423) - ss. 42 and 44 of the Assam Act do not impose any liability upon the transporter, carriers etc. to pay any sales tax - Provisions basically meant to check the tax evasion - Requirement of maintenance of document and the certificate of registration by a transporter or any such agent is only for similar purpose as incorporated u/ss. 36A and 38B of the Tripura Act which has been held to be a valid piece of legislation - Similarly insertion of s. 46A and r. 21A no way infringe fundamental rights of the petitioners - Appeal dismissed.
