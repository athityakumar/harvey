Rajendra Construction Company v Maharashtra Housing and Area Development

Supreme Court of India

12 August 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

C.K. Thakker, P.K. Balasubramanyan

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 467; (2005) 6 SCC 678; 2005 (2) ARBLR 637; 2006 (1) Bom.C.R. 374; JT 2005 (7) SC 388; 2005 (4) RCR(Civil) 16; 2005(6) SCALE 535; [2005] Supp2 S.C.R. 582

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Arbitration & ADR - Maharashtra Housing & Area Development Act, 1976, s. 173 - Arbitration Act, 1940, ss. 16, 17, 20, 21, 30, 31(3), 34 - Arbitration And Conciliation Act, 1996 - HC set aside award made by sole Arbitrator on ground that awards were not 'speaking' awards - Whether sole Arbitrator was required to record reasons in support of awards made by him? - Held, awards are under old Act and therefore they could not have been set aside by High Court on ground that they were not supported by reasons and were not speaking awards - Emphasis on recording reasons is to facilitate the appellate function or exercise the power of judicial review in adjudging the validity of the decision by the courts - The court does not exercise appellate jurisdiction and cannot substitute its decision for the decision of the arbitrator in considering the legality of an award made by an arbitrator with the consent of parties, which is otherwise legal and valid - Interest rate modified - Appeals partly allowed.
