Saibanna v State of Karnataka

Supreme Court of India

21 April 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Srikrishna, K.G. Balakrishnan

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 311; (2005) 4 SCC 165; (2005) SCC (Cr) 1094; JT 2005 (5) SC 564; 2005 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 801; 2005 (2) RCR(Criminal) 708; 2005(4) SCALE 262; [2005] 3 S.C.R. 760

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Bride Burning

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 303, 307, 309 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 235(2), 313, 354(3), 427(2) - Appellant was convicted for murder of wife - Murder committed during the period he was out on parole - Previoulsy convicted for murder of first wife - Sessions Court took the view that the case belonged to the category of "rarest of rare cases"- Death penalty imposed - Appeal against conviction and sentence - Whether HC right in imposing death penalty? - Held, HC was right in coming to conclusion that appellant's case bristles with special circumstances requisite for imposition of death penalty - Appeal dismissed.
