Hans Raj Banga v Ramchander Aggarwal

Supreme Court of India

29 April 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhan, A.K. Mathur

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 770; (2005) 4 SCC 572; AIR 2005 SC 2384; JT 2005 (5) SC 38; 2005 (141) PLR 185; 2005 (2) RCR(Civil) 591; 2005(4) SCALE 518; [2005] 3 S.C.R. 994

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Displaced Persons (Compensation and Rehabilitation) Act, 1954 - Administration of Evacuee Property Act, 1950 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882 - Displaced Persons (Compensation and Rehabilitation) Rules, 1955, r. 2(d) - Premises in dispute is an evacuee property, a Govt-built property in terms of r. 2(d) of Displaced Persons (Compensation and Rehabilitation) Rules, 1955 and forms part of compensation pool within meaning of s. 14 of 1954 Act - Bhagwan Dass father and predecessor-interest of defendant-respondent who was not a displaced person, filed a petition seeking transfer of Shop No. 114, in his favour on ground that same was allotted to him with effect from 10-5-1956 by Department - An order D/- 21-09-1960 was passed wherein it was agreed by Department that if Department decides to sell shop in question by auction, whether public or private, or by calling tenders, occupier of shop will also be given equal opportunity to give his bid or tender, as case may be, and bid or tender of occupier will be considered on merits along with other bidders or tenderers, if any - An advertisement was issued on 28-12-1960 in papers inviting tenders for sale of various shops - Sale certificate duly confirmed under Rules was issued in favour of appellant - Appellant filed suit for possession and recovery of damages for unauthorised use and occupation of property against respondent - Appellant was held to be owner of suit property - On appeal, High Court held that as per compromise arrived at in CWP No. 438-D of 1958 filed by Bhagwan Dass Department could sell property only by way of public auction and not by any other mode - Since in present case, sale was made in favour of appellant by inviting tenders same was not valid - Whether HC erred in holding so? - Held, it is clear from reading of order that Department had option to sell suit property either by auction, public or private, or by calling tenders - Bhagwan Dass did not file a tender in response to advertisement - Finding recorded by HC is factually incorrect - HC has erred in holding that sale was bad in law having been made in contravention of order passed by HC in CWP No 438-D of 1958 - HC erred in holding that issue of ownership was not concluded in earlier proceedings instituted by respondents before Rehabilitation Authorities, writ petition in HC and appeal in this Court - Appeal allowed.
