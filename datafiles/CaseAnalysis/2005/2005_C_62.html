New India Assurance Company Limited v Charlie and Another

Supreme Court of India

29 March 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 252; (2005) 10 SCC 720; (2005) SCC (Cr) 1657; AIR 2005 SC 2157; 2005 (59) ALR 626; 2005 (3) AWC 2221; 2005 (3) G.L.R. 2343; 2005 (3) JhrCR(SC) 65; JT 2005 (11) SC 264; 2005 (3) KLT 227; 2005 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 517; 2005 (3) MLJ(SC) 118; 2005 (140) PLR 661; 2005 (2) RCR(Civil) 550; 2005(3) SCALE 541; [2005] 2 S.C.R. 1173

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance

Keywords: Future Pecuniary Benefit, Computation Of Compensation, Assessment Of Damage

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Motor Accidents Claims Amount of compensation - Multiplier of 16 - Held, claimant was nearly 37 years of age and was married therefore 1/3 rd deduction has to be made for personal expenditure - Highest multiplier has to be for age group of 21 years to 25 years when an ordinary Indian Citizen starts independently earning and lowest would be in respect of a person in age group of 60 to 70, which is normal retirement age - Choice of the multiplier is determined by the age of the deceased and by the calculation as to what capital sum, if invested at a rate of interest appropriate to a stable economy, would yield the multiplicand by way of annual interest - Normal rule about the deprivation of income is directly not applicable to cases where agricultural income is the source of deceased's or injured's income - Quantum of compensation fixed at Rs. 3,50,000/- Appeal allowed.
