Messrs Saci Allied Products Limited, Uttar Pradesh v Commissioner of Central Excise, Meerut

Supreme Court of India

26 April 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

AR. Lakshmanan, S.N. Variava, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 319; (2005) 7 SCC 159; 2005 (183) E.L.T. 225; JT 2005 (4) SC 577; 2005(4) SCALE 405; [2005] 3 S.C.R. 881

Case Digest

Subject: Excise

Keywords: Sale Price, Normal Price, Central Excise Valuation Rules, 1975, Place Of Removal

Summary: [A.] Excise - Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944, ss. 4(1)(a), 4(4)(c) - Customs Tariff Act, 1975, s. 3 - Central Excise Valuation Rules, 1975, rr. 5, 7 - Whether appellants are liable to pay excise duty on basis of sale price of buyer to its dealers in Uttar Pradesh or based on appellants' sale price to independent dealers? - Held, Collector could not have confirmed demand u/s.4(1)(b) when there are significant sales at factory gate to independent buyers throughout India - Tribunal having accepted sale as a genuine sale and having accepted that price to independent dealers is available u/s.4(1)(a) appellate Tribunal ought not to have rejected acceptance of price to independent dealers - Appeal allowed.
