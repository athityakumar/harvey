Indian Oil Corporation Limited v Saroj Baweja and another

Supreme Court of India

16 February 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhan, A.K. Mathur

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 1817; (2005) 12 SCC 298

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Keywords: Sustainability, Recovery of mesne profits

Summary: Rent Control - Suit for eviction - Recovery of mesne profits - Sustainability - Respondent landlord filed suit against appellant tenant for eviction, possession and mandatory injunction - Suit was decreed - Appellant accepted judgment and decree passed in said suit and vacated suit premises - Respondents filed another suit against appellant for recovery of mesne profits and claimed Rs.40,000/- per month as the mesne profits for use and occupation of the suit premises - Trial Court fixed mesne profits at Rs.17,516.80 per month with 10% interest p.a - Aggrieved against order passed by Trial Court fixing mesne profits at Rs.17,516.80, respondents filed appeal before Appellate Court - Appellate court modified judgment and decree passed by Trial Court and fixed mesne profits at Rs.35,000/- per month instead of Rs.17,516.80 per month - Appellant filed appeal before HC which was dismissed - Hence instant appeal - Whether the order passed by HC was sustainable - Held, respondent in their plaint stated that market rent of suit premises was approximately Rs.40,000/- per month - They deposed to same effect in their testimony in Court - Appellant did not cross-examine or produce any evidence to counter statement made by respondents - Since appellant failed to produce any evidence or even to cross-examine respondents regarding market rent existing on date of terminating tenancy, order passed by Appellate Court fixing mesne profits at Rs.35,000/- per month, which was affirmed by HC was right - Appeal dismissed.
