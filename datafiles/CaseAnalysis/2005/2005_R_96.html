Ram Bhual v Ambika Singh

Supreme Court of India

29 September 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

P.K. Balasubramanyan, R.C. Lahoti, G.P. Mathur

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 1375; (2005) 12 SCC 121; 2005 (4) AWC 3786; JT 2005 (12) SC 49; 2005 (4) RCR(Civil) 331; 2005(7) SCALE 669; [2005] Supp3 S.C.R. 693

Case Digest

Subject: Election; Parliament & Legislature

Summary: Election - Parliament & Legislature - Representation of the People Act, 1951, ss. 33(4), 36(4) and 100(1)(c) - Rejection of nomination papers - Appellant was declared elected in the elections of the Uttar Pradesh Legislative Assembly - Election of the appellant was challenged by the respondent (Defeated candidate) on the ground that the Returning Officer, while scrutinizing the nominations, had wrongly rejected the nomination of an independent candidate - Appellant contested said election petition by questioning the right of the respondent to file the election petition based on the rejection of the nomination of another candidate, who had not come forward to challenge that rejection - HC allowed the said election petition on the ground that the wrongful rejection of the nomination of any candidate can be taken as a ground for challenging an election in an election petition by the defeated candidate - HC further held that the rejection of the nomination of the independent candidate was improper and declared the election of the appellant as void u/s. 100(1)(c) of the Act - Hence, the instant appeal - Whether HC rightly allowed election petition filed by the respondent ? - Held, it was clear that the rejection of the nomination of the independent candidate by the Returning Officer was only on the ground that out of ten proposers, the ninth proposer was described as the voter at Serial No. 392 in Part 91 of the Voters' list - It was not disputed, that the name of the ninth proposer was actually included in the Voters' list, but as Serial No. 352 in Part 91 of the Voters' list - Both Serial No. 352 and Serial No. 392 of Part 91, were printed on the same page of the Voters' list - Therefore, it required no detailed search by the Returning Officer to find out or to satisfy himself that the ninth proposer was a voter in the constituency - It was a minor defect which obviously should have been got corrected by the Returning Officer even while accepting the nomination and certainly he could not have rejected the nomination on that ground in the light of s. 36(4) of the Act - Hence, HC rightly held that the rejection of the nomination of the independent candidate was improper - Appeal dismissed.
