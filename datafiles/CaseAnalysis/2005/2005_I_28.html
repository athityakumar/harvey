M/s. Makhija Construction and Engineering Private v Indore Development Authority and others

Supreme Court of India

19 April 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Ruma Pal, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 308; (2005) 6 SCC 304; AIR 2005 SC 2499; 2005 (4) ALD(SC) 81; 2005 (3) BC 519; JT 2005 (4) SC 427; 2005(4) SCALE 210

Case Digest

Subject: Education; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Competition - Land & Property - Madhya Pradesh Nagar Tatha Gram Nivesh Adhiniyam 1973 - Tender - Res judicata - Appellant's tender for allotment of land reserved for educational use was not accepted by respondent-authority - There was a conflict of interest between two co-respondents in Crescent's appeal, namely between Jagriti and appellants - Appellant was not competent to participate in tender - Appellant is entitled to succeed on ground that order of DB disposing of Crescent's appeal operated as res judicata to bind not only Crescent but also Jagriti and appellant - Principle of res judicata has been held to bind co-defendants if relief given or refused by earlier decision involved a determination of an issue between co-defendants (or co-respondents as case may be) - It was necessary for Appellate Court to decide whether appellant was entitled to participate.
