National Housing Coop. Society Limited v State of Rajasthan and others

Supreme Court of India

28 March 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Ruma Pal, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 1439; (2005) 12 SCC 149

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Rajasthan Land Acquisition Act, 1953, s. 18 - Acquisition - Compensation - Implement - Appellant buy certain land by respondent nos. 5 and 6 - Later said land had acquired without payment of compensation and respondent nos. 5 and 6 filed an application u/s. 18 of 1953 Act before Trial Court - Trial Court held in favour of respondent nos. 5 and 6 - Being aggrieved, State/respondent no.1 filed an appeal before HC while same was pending before HC, appellant moved an application for implement him to appeal but same was rejected by HC - Appellant filed review application and during pendency of said review application appellant filed special leave petition which was dismissed without any reasons - Hence instant appeal.

Held, while review application was pending, appellant sought to file an appeal from order before Court by way of a special leave petition. Incidentally, State as well as development authority also order of HC disposing of appeal. As far as their special leave petitions are concerned, leave has been granted and appeals are pending. As far as appellant's special leave petition was concerned, it was dismissed without giving any reasons at all. After dismissal of its special leave petition, the appellant sought to revive its review application before HC. HC by order in this appeal held that review petition was not maintainable in view of fact that special leave petition from order sought to be reviewed had been dismissed. When a special leave petition is dismissed by a non-speaking order, HC could be moved by way of a petition for review. It is clear that person not impleaded as a party has a right to file a special leave petition as well as to file an application for review. In these circumstances, appeal is allowed and decision of HC is set aside. Matter is remanded back to HC and HC will now hear review application of appellant on merits. As far as application for implement is concerned, we pass no order. It is however open to applicant to make appropriate application if it is otherwise so entitled in law before HC. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - It is clear that a person not impleaded as a party has a right to file a special leave petition as well as to file an application for review.
