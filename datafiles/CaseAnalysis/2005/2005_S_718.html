State of Uttar Pradesh and Another v Brijpal Singh

Supreme Court of India

27 September 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

AR. Lakshmanan, Ruma Pal, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 1248; (2005) 8 SCC 58; (2005) SCC (L&S) 1081; AIR 2006 SC 3592; 2005 (6) ALD(SC) 91; 2005 (6) AWC 5980; 2005 (4) ESC 18; 2005 (107) FLR 604; JT 2005 (12) SC 498; 2005(7) SCALE 555; 2005 (6) SLR 38; [2005] Supp3 S.C.R. 633; 2005 (6) Supreme 692

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 10, 33C(2) - Undetermined claim - Adjudication - Jurisdiction of Labour Court - Appellant appointed respondent as Clerk on temporary/ad hoc basis - Appellant terminated respondent's service - Respondent challenged the termination order before HC - HC, by its interim order, stayed the operation of appellant order - Respondent filed a petition u/s. 33C(2) of the Act before the Labour Court claiming the arrears of salary - Labour Court directed appellant to pay the arrears - Appellant challenged the Labour Court order before the HC - HC dismissed appellant's petition - Whether the Labour Court has jurisdiction to entertain and decide the undetermined claim - Held, workman can proceed u/s. 33C(2) only after the Labour Tribunal has adjudicated on a complaint u/s. 33A or on a reference u/s. 10 that the order of discharge or dismissal was not justified and has set aside that order and reinstated the workman - Labour Court cannot arrogate to itself the functions of an Industrial Tribunal and entertain the claim made by the respondent which is not based on an existing right but which may appropriately be made the subject matter of an industrial dispute in a reference u/s.10 of the Act - Therefore, Labour Court has no jurisdiction to adjudicate the claim made by the respondent u/s. 33C(2) of the Act in an undetermined claim and until such adjudication is made by the appropriate forum, the respondent-workman cannot ask the Labour Court in an application u/s. 33C(2) of the Act to disregard his dismissal as wrongful and on that basis to compute his wages - Labour Court committed a manifest error of law in passing the order in question which was rightly impugned before the HC and HC erroneously dismissed the same - HC also equally committed a manifest error in not considering the scope of s. 33C(2) of the Act - Impugned orders of Labour Court and HC are set aside - However, direction issued to the appellant to regularise the salary and emoluments of respondent - Appeal allowed.
