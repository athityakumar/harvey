Messrs Pahwa Chemicals Private Limited v Commissioner of Central Excise, New Delhi

Supreme Court of India

24 February 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, AR. Lakshmanan, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 122; (2005) 2 SCC 720; AIR 2005 SC 1532; 2005 (181) E.L.T. 339; [2005] 274 ITR 87; JT 2005 (11) SC 21; 2005(2) SCALE 358; [2005] 2 S.C.R. 380

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Indirect Tax; Trade

Keywords: Central Excise Officer, Excisability of goods

Summary: Excise - Central Excise Tariff Act, 1985 - Central Excise and Salt Act, 1944, ss. 11A, 2(b) - Demand & Penalty - Suppression of facts - Benefit of Notification No. 1 of 1993 as amended and Notification No 16 of 1997 denied on the ground that they were using logo of 'ATR' belonging to one M/s ATR St Moritz A G , Switzerland - Whether Superintendent had jurisdiction to issue show-cause-notices and that Deputy Commissioner had jurisdiction to adjudicate?; whether Board can override provisions of Act by issuing directions? - Held, Act permits any Central Excise Officer to issue show-cause notices even in cases where there are allegations of fraud, collusion, willful misstatement and suppression of facts - If Act vests in Central Excise Officers jurisdiction to issue show-cause-notices and to adjudicate, Board has no power to cut down that jurisdiction - Superintendent had jurisdiction to issue show-cause-notice and Deputy Commissioner had jurisdiction to adjudicate - Appeals Dismissed.
