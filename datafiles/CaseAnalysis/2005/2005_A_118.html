Manicka Poosali (Dead) By Lrs. and Others v Anjalai Ammal and Another

Supreme Court of India

17 March 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhan, A.K. Mathur

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 200; (2005) 10 SCC 38; AIR 2005 SC 1777; 2005 (3) ALD(SC) 27; 2005 (59) ALR 646; 2005 (2) AWC 1430; 2005 (3) JhrCR(SC) 16; JT 2005 (3) SC 443; 2005 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 268; 2005 (2) MLJ(SC) 116; 2005 (2) RCR(Civil) 190; 2005 (99) RD 241; 2005(3) SCALE 345; [2005] 2 S.C.R. 1027

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Family & Personal; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Ex-Parte, Coparcener

Summary: Civil Procedure - Family and Personal - Land and Property - Partition suit - HC granted preliminary decree of partition and separate possession to respondents setting aside judgment and decree of Trial Court as well as that of Lower Appellate Court - Whether HC right in setting aside judgment of trial court? - Held, HC could not go into question regarding due execution and validity of settlement deed or genuineness of will which had not been challenged by respondents either in their pleadings or in their evidence or in memorandum of grounds of second appeal - Findings recorded by HC regarding validity and genuineness of will are vitiated and cannot be sustained - Appeal allowed.
