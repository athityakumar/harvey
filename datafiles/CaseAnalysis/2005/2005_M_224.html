New India Assurance Company Limited v Meera Bai and others

Supreme Court of India

11 November 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Singh, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 1750; (2006) 9 SCC 174; 2007 (3) ACC 665

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Insurance - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 - Compensation - Challenged - Appellant (Insurance company) filed appeal against order of the HC submitting that HC was in error in awarding a sum of Rs 1,99,500 by way of compensation along with interest @ 9% p.a., in view of the fact that the policy of insurance did not cover the risk of the owner of the vehicle, who was the insured - Whether submissions of the appellant could be accepted and impugned order of the HC could be set aside? - Held, the insurance policy does cover the risk to the driver of the vehicle and not the owner of the vehicle - The owner, who was himself driving the vehicle, was not covered under the policy - Impugned order of the HC set aside - Appeal allowed.
