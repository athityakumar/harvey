Shree Subhlaxmi Fabrics Private Limited v Chand Mal Baradia and Others

Supreme Court of India

29 March 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

G.P. Mathur, D.M. Dharmadhikari

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 251; (2005) 10 SCC 704; AIR 2005 SC 2161; 2005 (3) ALD(SC) 93; 2005 (1) ARBLR 623; 2005 (2) AWC 1420; [2005] 124 Comp Cas 811; 2005 (4) CompLJ 549; JT 2005 (11) SC 155; 2005 (2) RCR(Civil) 363; 2005(3) SCALE 393; [2005] 2 S.C.R. 1138; 2005 (2) UC 807

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Civil Procedure - CPC, s. 151, Order 39 Rule 1 and 2 - Injunction - Arbitral tribunal - Jurisdiction - Whether HC right in granting an injunction in favour of plaintiff and staying proceedings before defendant No 2? - Held, trial court having held that it had no territorial jurisdiction to try suit, HC should have gone deeper into matter and until a clear finding was recorded that court had territorial jurisdiction to try suit, no injunction could have been granted in favour of plaintiff - HC erred in reversing its order and granting an injunction in favour of the plaintiff - Appeal allowed.
