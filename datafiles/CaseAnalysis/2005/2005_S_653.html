Jagjit Singh @ Jagga v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

18 January 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Singh, P. Venkatarama Reddi

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 34; (2005) 3 SCC 689; (2005) SCC (Cr) 893; AIR 2005 SC 913; 2005 (1) CGLJ 397; 2005 CRLJ 955; 2005 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 297; 2005 (3) RCR(Criminal) 647; 2005(1) SCALE 368; [2005] 1 S.C.R. 559

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 161, 313 - IPC, ss. 302, 460, 458 r/w 34 - Child witness - Appellant found guilty - Death sentence affirmed by HC - Appeal against conviction - Held, no charge was framed against appellant u/s. 376 - There is no evidence suggesting that appellant had committed offence of rape - There is nothing on record to suggest that appellant had any animus against his own uncle - Nor is there any evidence on record to suggest any possible motive for appellant to commit such a heinous crime - Not safe to sustain conviction of appellant on sole testimony of child witness - Appellant is entitled to benefit of doubt - Appellant is acquitted of all charges levelled against him - Appeal allowed.
