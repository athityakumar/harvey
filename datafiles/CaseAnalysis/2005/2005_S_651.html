Gurpreet Singh and another v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

09 November 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Agrawal, A.K. Mathur

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 716; (2005) 12 SCC 615; AIR 2006 SC 191; 2006 CRLJ 121; 2006 CRLJ 126; JT 2005 (9) SC 465; 2005 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 829; 2005(9) SCALE 189; [2005] Supp5 S.C.R. 90

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Human Rights; Practice & Procedure; Women & Children

Summary: [A.] Criminal - IPC, 1860, ss. 148, 149, 302, 304-B, 307, 34 - CrPC, 1973, ss. 154, 157, 313, 464(1), 465 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 113-B - Juvenile Justice Act, 1986, s. 2(h) - Punjab Police Rules, r. 24.1 - Conviction and sentence to undergo imprisonment for life and to pay a fine of Rs. 2, 000/- each - Appeal against conviction and sentence - Held, non-disclosure of name of witnesses in daily dairy does not affect prosecution's case - Accused persons not prejudiced due to errors and omissions in charges - Prosecution succeeded in proving its case beyond reasonable doubt and conviction of appellants u/s.302 is liable to be altered to one u/s.302 r/w s.34 as fatal injury could not be attributed to him - Upheld conviction - Call for a report from trial court in relation to one of appellant about his age on date of occurrence - Appeals disposed of.
