Canara Bank v V.K. Awasthy

Supreme Court of India

31 March 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 261; (2005) 6 SCC 321; (2005) SCC (L&S) 833; AIR 2005 SC 2090; 2005 (5) AWC 4410; 2005 (4) CompLJ 249; 2005 (2) ESC 225; 2005 (105) FLR 630; 2005 (4) JhrCR(SC) 124; JT 2005 (4) SC 40; 2005 (2) LLN 996; 2005(3) SCALE 600; [2005] 3 S.C.R. 81; 2005 (3) SLR 421; 2005 (3) Supreme 492

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: [A.] Service - Dismissal from service - Principles of Natural Justice - Single Judge came to hold that the quantum of punishment was disproportionate to the misconduct proved - DB held that the order directing respondent's dismissal from service was in violation of the principles of natural justice - Held, proved charges clearly established that respondent-employee failed to discharge his duties with utmost integrity, honesty, devotion and diligence and his acts were prejudicial to interest of bank - Order of dismissal as passed by Appellant-Bank does not suffer from any infirmity - Appeal allowed.
