Madhya Pradesh Electricity Board v Jagdish Chandra Sharma

Supreme Court of India

04 March 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

P.K. Balasubramanyan, N. Santosh Hegde, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 180; (2005) 3 SCC 401; (2005) SCC (L&S) 417; AIR 2005 SC 1924; 2005 (1) CLR 1074; 2005 (105) FLR 155; JT 2005 (3) SC 102; 2005 (2) KLT 147; 2005 (2) LLN 67; 2005(3) SCALE 1; [2005] 2 S.C.R. 559; 2005 (3) SLR 304; 2005 (2) UPLBEC 1935

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Madhya Pradesh Industrial Relations Act, 1962

Summary: Labour and Industrial Law - Madhya Pradesh Industrial Relations Act, 1962 - Charge of hitting a superior officer with an implement and causing him injury and absence from duty without intimation - Termination - Whether in face of charges proved, it was proper for Labour Court or for HC to interfere with punishment imposed by employer? - Held, when an employee breaches discipline and employer terminates his services, it is not open to a Labour Court or an Industrial Tribunal to take view that punishment awarded is shockingly disproportionate to charge proved - Punishment of termination awarded to employee will stand - Appeals dimissed.
