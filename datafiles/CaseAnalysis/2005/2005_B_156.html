Commissioner of Customs, Mumbai v Bureau Veritas and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 February 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, C.K. Thakker, Mrs. Justice Ruma Pal

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 780; (2005) 3 SCC 265; AIR 2005 SC 1292; 2005 (181) E.L.T. 3; JT 2005 (2) SC 348; 2005 (66) RLT 877; 2005(2) SCALE 158; [2005] 2 S.C.R. 118

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax

Summary: Customs - Customs Act, 1962, ss. 14, 28AB, 111(m), 112 and 113(a) - Territorial Waters, Continental Shelf, Exclusive Economic Zone and Other Maritime Zones Act, 1976 - Customs Valuation (Determination of Price of Imported Goods) Rules, 1988, rr. 3, 4, 5, 6, 7 and 8 - Whether the tribunal had erred in holding that there was enough evidence to justify the view that sale of rig in 1999 was uninfluenced by the relationship between buyer and the seller - After having agreed on some point as recorded, it is not open to the appellant to turn round or take a plea that the position is different - Tribunal has referred to various materials which can by no stretch of imagination be termed irrelevant - Held, no interference is called for in these appeals - Appeal dismissed.
