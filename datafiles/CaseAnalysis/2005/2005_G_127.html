Guru Nanak Dev University v Saumil Garg and others

Supreme Court of India

24 August 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.K. Sabharwal, B.N. Srikrishna, P.P. Naolekar

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 1960; (2005) 13 SCC 749

Case Digest

Subject: Education

Summary: Education - Entrance test - incorrect key answers - Re-evaluation - Legality - Entrance test was conducted by appellant university - Respondents students filed writ petition alleging that the key answers to 21 questions were incorrect - HC directed appointment of experts for the re-examining the key answers - Hence instant appeal - Whether HC was justified in passing the impugned judgment - Held, SC directed that for the students who attempted those questions or some of those questions, insofar as they were concerned, the said questions should not be treated to be part of the question paper - To illustrate, if a student answered all the 7 vague questions, insofar as that student was concerned, total marks would be counted out of 772 i.e. 800 less 28 and likewise depending upon number of such questions, if any, answered by the student - 7 vague questions were question 4 in Physics, Questions 76 and 89 in Chemistry, Questions 147 and 148 in Botany and Questions 156 and 163 in Zoology of Question Paper Code A - Directions contained in the impugned judgment of the HC modified and directed appellant to re-evaluate the answer-books in terms of the aforesaid directions and, on that basis, prepare the ranking of the students - Appeals disposed of.
