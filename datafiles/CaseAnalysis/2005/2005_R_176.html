Research Foundation For Science Technology and Natural Resources Policy v Union of India and Another

Supreme Court of India

05 January 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.K. Sabharwal, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 9; (2005) 13 SCC 186; 2005 (3) CompLJ 193; 2005 (192) E.L.T. 8; 2005 (2) JhrCR(SC) 294; JT 2005 (11) SC 135; 2005(1) SCALE 87; [2005] 1 S.C.R. 115

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Environment, Wildlife & Animal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Absolute Liability, Pollution Control Boards, Sustainable Development, Degradation, Hazardous Wastes (Management and Handling) Rules, 1988

Summary: Dumping of hazardous waste - Whether directions shall be issued for destruction of consignments with a view to protect environment and, if not, in what other manner consignments may be dealt with? - Held, precautionary principles are fully applicable to facts and circumstances of the case and only appropriate course to protect environments is to direct destruction of consignments by incineration as recommended by Monitoring Committee - Order accordingly.
