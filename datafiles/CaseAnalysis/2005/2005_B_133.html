Bombay Dyeing and Manufacturing Company Limited v Bombay Environmental Action Group and others

Supreme Court of India

11 May 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, N. Santosh Hegde

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 409; (2005) 5 SCC 61; 2005 (6) Bom.C.R. 565; JT 2005 (11) SC 366; 2005(5) SCALE 147; 2006 (2) SCJ 705; [2005] Supp1 S.C.R. 282

Case Digest

Subject: Environment, Wildlife & Animal; Municipalities & Local Governments; Textiles

Summary: [A.] Municipalities & Local Governments - Maharashtra Regional & Town Planning Act, 1966, s. 37 - Development Control Regulations, 1991, reg. 58 - Challenge to this regn. 58 - Appeal against Interim orders passed by HC - Held, interest of justice would be sub-served if National Textile Corporations are permitted to complete transactions in terms of scheme framed by BIFR but same shall be subject to conditions that in event, writ ultimately succeeds, vacant land available from other mills, if necessary, shall be offered by way of adjustment - Since called upon to deal with an interim order, no purpose would be served in keeping matters pending - Appeals disposed of.
