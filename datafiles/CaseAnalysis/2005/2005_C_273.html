Britannia Industries Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax, West Bengal, Kolkata

Supreme Court of India

05 October 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, B.P. Singh, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 741; (2006) 1 SCC 646; AIR 2005 SC 4333; 2005 (198) CTR 313; [2005] 278 ITR 546; JT 2005 (12) SC 98; 2005(8) SCALE 148; [2005] Supp4 S.C.R. 1; [2005] 148 TAXMAN 468

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: [A.] Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 30, 31, 32, 33, 34, 35, 36 - Finance Act, 1964, s. 37 - Finance Act, 1997 - Finance Act 1970 - Dispute regarding disallowance of sum of Rs. 31,38,017 for assessment year 1994-1995 claimed by assessee as expenses towards rent, repairs, depreciation and maintenance of guest house purportedly used in connection with business of company - Divergent views expressed by different HCs - Issue in consideration whether expression 'premises and buildings' referred to in ss. 30 and 32 and used for purposes of business or profession would include within its scope and ambit expression 'residential accommodation including any accommodation in nature of guest house' used in s. 37(3), (4) and (5) - Intention of Legislature clear and unambiguous and intended to exclude expenses towards rents, repairs and also maintenance of premises/accommodation used for purposes of a guest house of nature indicated in s. 37(4) - If Legislature had intended that deduction would be allowable in respect of all types of buildings/accommodations used for purposes of business or profession, then it would not have felt need to amend provisions of s. 37 so as to make a definite distinction with regard to buildings used as guest houses as defined in s. 37(5) and ss. 31 and 32 would have been sufficient for said purpose - Appeal dissmissed.
