National Insurance Company Limited v Bommithi Subbhayamma and others

Supreme Court of India

21 February 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, AR. Lakshmanan

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 1412; (2005) 12 SCC 243; 2005 (3) ACC 423; 2005 (141) PLR 546; 2005 (4) RCR(Civil) 829

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance

Keywords: Motor Vehicles (Amendment) Act, 1994

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Insurance - Motor Vehicles (Amendment) Act, 1994, s. 147 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 137 - Gratuitous passengers - Compensation - Entitlement - Deceased was traveling in lorry which met with accident resulting in his death - Claim application filed by heirs of deceased before Additional District Judge who awarded compensation with interest - In appeal Motor Accidents Claims Tribunal, held that appellant/insurance company was not liable to pay amount of compensation as deceased was traveling in lorry as gratuitous passenger - Petition before SC which was dismissed - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether insurance company was liable to pay compensation for gratuitous passenger - Held, although owner of goods or his authorized representative would be covered by policy of insurance in respect of goods vehicle, it was not intention of legislature to provide for liability of insurer with respect to passengers, especially gratuitous passengers, who were neither contemplated at time contract of insurance was entered into, nor was any premium paid to extent of benefit of insurance to such category of people - Thus, Insurance Company was not liable for payment of any compensation for death of gratuitous passenger traveling in goods vehicle - Therefore, claimant would be entitled to recover amount of compensation from owner of vehicle - Hence, judgment of HC was set aside - Appeal allowed.
