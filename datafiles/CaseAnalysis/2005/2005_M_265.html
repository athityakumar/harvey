Commissioner of Sales Tax, Delhi and Others v Messrs Shri Krishna Engineering Company and Others

Supreme Court of India

25 January 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

AR. Lakshmanan, S.N. Variava, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 45; (2005) 2 SCC 692; AIR 2005 SC 958; 2005 (117) DLT 147; JT 2005 (1) SC 563; 2005(1) SCALE 561B; [2005] 1 S.C.R. 825; 2005 (139) STC 457

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Practice & Procedure; Sales Tax

Summary: [A.] Sales Tax - Delhi Sales Tax Act, 1975, ss. 2, 3, 4(2)(a)(v), 5, 71, 8 - Delhi Sales Tax Rules, 1975, rr. 7, 8(4)(c), 8(4)(c)(ii) - Interpretation - Whether rules are ultra vires provision of s. 4(2)(a)(v) of Act? - Whether selling dealers can canvass that Department has no authority to refuse to issue sales tax forms to purchasing dealers or allow deduction to selling dealers and if r. 8 empowers it to do so, if there is such a power? - Held, Sales Tax Department is neither privy to nor is it concerned with any assurances that might have been exchanged inter se between selling and purchasing dealers in matter of furnishing ST-1 Forms - r. 8(4)(c) is not ultra vires to provisions of s. 4(2)(a)(v) of Delhi Sales Tax Act - Appeal dismissed.
