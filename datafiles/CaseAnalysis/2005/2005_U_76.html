R.C. Tobacco Private Limited and Another Etc v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 September 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Ruma Pal, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 685; (2005) 7 SCC 725; 2005 (188) E.L.T. 129; JT 2005 (12) SC 281; 2005(7) SCALE 436; 2005(7) SCALE 455A; [2005] Supp3 S.C.R. 342

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Excise

Keywords: Additional Duties of Excise (Textiles and Textile Articles) Act, 1978, Enactment, Constitutionality, Maharashtra Tax Laws (Levy Amendment and Repeal) Act, 1989, Central Excise Rules, 1982

Summary: [A.] Constitution - Excise - Central Excise Act, 1944, ss. 11A, 5A - Finance Act, 2003, s. 154 - Central Excise Tariff Act, 1985, ss. 11A, 11B - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 13, 14, 16, 19, 19(1)(f), 19(1)(g), 265, 31(1) - Rescinding of benefit of Exemption Notification - Validity of - Petitioners job workers for large tobacco companies, set up their units under agreements with large tobacco companies and admittedly produced cigarettes with brand names of those companies - Industrial policy for north eastern region announced by Govt. of India giving incentives including exemption from excise duties - Issuance of Excise Notifications Nos 32/99 and 33/99 giving diverse benefits - Benefit of notification allowed to petitioners for first few months - Since refund of excise duty was not made to some petitioners - Writ was filed - Claims for refund rejected - Enactment of s.154 of Finance Act, 2003 - Due to Amendment of Notification No 32/99 excluding cigarettes, exemptions available to manufacturers of cigarettes were rescinded retrospectively - Retrospective operation of s.154 challenged herein - Competence of parliament and State legislatures to repeal, amend or supersede an exemption notification is unquestionable - Power to do so retrospectively cannot be challenged - A law cannot be held to be unreasonable merely because it operates retrospectively - Whether s.154 was discriminatory or unreasonable or confiscatory thus violated arts.14 and 19 - Parliament could not be blamed for having at least awaited decision of HC, nor could statutory provision be questioned as being unreasonably retrospective - Since reasons for retrospective enactment of s.154 were given, held that, those reasons were factually plausible - Writ petitions dismissed.
