C. K. Jidheesh v Union of India and others

Supreme Court of India

27 October 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, P.P. Naolekar

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 921; (2005) 13 SCC 37; AIR 2006 SC 444; 2005 (199) CTR 212; [2005] 279 ITR 118; JT 2005 (10) SC 297; 2005 (4) KLT 770; 2005 (71) RLT 505; 2005(8) SCALE 784; 2006 (144) STC 322; 2006 (1) S.T.R. 3; [2005] Supp4 S.C.R. 862; [2005] 149 TAXMAN 446; [2006] 3 VST 1

Case Digest

Subject: Service Tax

Summary: [A.] Service Tax - Constitution - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 14, 19(1)(g), 366(29A) - Finance Act, 1994, s. 67 - Challenge to a letter issued by Ministry of Finance as being arbitrary and discriminatory being in violation of arts.14 and 19(1)(g), and also violative of Finance Act - Issuance of letter by Ministry of Finance clarifying that service tax would be levied on entire amount recovered by persons like petitioner - Held, a mere challenge to classificatory letter was not enough - Challenge has to be to the provisions of the Finance Act - Provisions of Finance Act had been challenged by Kerala Colour labs Association and had been repelled by Kerala HC - No infirmity found in judgment of Kerala HC - Following the 2 cases viz. Kerala Colour labs Association case and Rainbow Colour Lab case (2000 Indlaw SC 605) held that contracts of type entered into by persons like petitioner were nothing else but service contracts pure and simple in which there was no elements of sale of goods - Hence held that question of directing respondent to bifurcate receipts into an element of goods and element of service could not and did not arise - Petition dismissed.
