Saroop Singh v Banto and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 October 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 610; (2005) 8 SCC 330; AIR 2005 SC 4407; 2005 (61) ALR 807; 2006 (1) JhrCR(SC) 36; JT 2005 (9) SC 200; 2005 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 699; 2005 (4) RCR(Civil) 599; 2005(8) SCALE 415; [2005] Supp4 S.C.R. 253

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Family & Personal; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Declaratory Suit, Ancestral Immovable Property

Summary: [A.] Land & Property - Family & Personal - Hindu Succession Act, 1956 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 107, 108 - Limitation Act, 1963 - Punjab Limitation (Customs) Act, 1920 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Suit for possession and permanent injunction - Deed of gift in favour of appellant - Legality of gift deed - Held, deed of gift to be valid only so long as owner was alive, as the donor only had life interest - On her death succession reopened having regard to provisions of Hindu Succession Act - Respondent, being daughter, inherited interest - Plea of adverse possession not raised by appellant - Limitation will start from date when his possession becomes adverse -Appeal dismissed.
