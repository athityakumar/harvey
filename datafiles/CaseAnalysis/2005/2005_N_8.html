Hari Rao v N. Govindachari and Others

Supreme Court of India

15 September 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

P.K. Balasubramanyan, B.N. Srikrishna

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 555; (2005) 7 SCC 643; AIR 2005 SC 3389; 2005 (5) ALD(SC) 134; 2005 (61) ALR 597; JT 2005 (8) SC 305; 2005 (4) KLT 244; 2005 (4) MLJ(SC) 115; 2005(7) SCALE 280; [2005] Supp3 S.C.R. 217

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Rent Control

Summary: [A.] Rent Control - Tamil Nadu Buildings (Lease and Rent Control) Act, 1960, ss. 10(2)(ii)(b), 10(2)(iii), 21 - Transfer Of Property Act, 1882, s. 108(o) - Eviction petition on grounds of usage for purpose other than for which it was let and acts of waste as are likely to impair materially value and utility of building - Appeal against eviction - Held, merely because a tenant, alters commodity in which he was trading when he took building on lease or trades in other commodities also, he could not be held to be using premises for a purpose other than purpose for which it was let - Fixing of racks inside premises even by drilling holes in walls or beams cannot be said to be acts which are themselves acts of waste as are likely to impair materially value and utility of building - Appeal allowed.
