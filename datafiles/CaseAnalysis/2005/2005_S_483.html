I. I. S. Employees' House Building Coop. Society Limited v State of Karnataka and others

Supreme Court of India

04 August 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhan, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 1648; (2005) 12 SCC 483

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Proper - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4,5-A, 6 - Land Acquisition- Appeal was filed against the judgment passed by the Lower Courts for quashing of notifications issued u/s. 4 of the Act and declaration issued u/s. 6 of the Act - Hence instant appeal - Notification was issued in the name of the Respondent No.1 landlord, who had already died by then - However, Respondent No. 2 was the owner of the said land - Final notification released did not include the respondent No. 2's land - Appellant contended that it was due to a typographical error or printing mistake which was rejected by the HC by giving cogent reasons - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, there was non-compliance with s 5-A of the Act as no notice had been issued to Respondent No. 2. Notice had been issued to Respondent No. 1 only who had already died, depriving the Respondent No. 2 of his valuable right to file objections u/s 5-A of the Act. In the notification issued u/s 4, the land sought to be acquired belonged to the Respondent No.2, whereas the same does not find mention in the final notification issued u/s 6 of the Act. The appellant contended that Survey No. 91/3 instead of 90/3 has been mentioned due to a typographical error or printing mistake which was rejected by the HC by giving cogent reasons as no corrigendum was issued by the State correcting the issue. SC agreed upon the HC reasoning. Appeal dismissed.
