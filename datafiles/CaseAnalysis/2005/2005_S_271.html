Spun Casting and Engineering Company Private v Dwijendra Lal Sinha (Dead) through Lrs. and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 April 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhan, A.K. Mathur

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 264; (2005) 6 SCC 265; AIR 2005 SC 2054; JT 2005 (4) SC 328; 2005 (3) RCR(Civil) 398; 2005(3) SCALE 818; [2005] 3 S.C.R. 395

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Land & Property; Rent Control

Summary: [A.] Land & Property - Rent Control - Madhya Pradesh Accommodation Control Act, 1955, s.3A - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s.116 - Transfer Of Property Act, 1882, s.106 - West Bengal Premises Tenancy Act, 1956, s.2(f) , s.13(6) , s.17(i) - Uttar Pradesh (Temporary) Control of Rent and Eviction Act, 1947, s.2(1)(d) - West Bengal Premises Tenancy Act, 1956 - Relationship of landlord and tenant - Suit for recovery of possession of 'B' and 'C' Schedule properties and for mesne profits - Appeal against eviction - Held, in absence of any pleadings and evidence on record that appellant committed a default in payment of rent, HC erred in passing a decree for eviction on that ground - Ground that what was let out to appellant was not premises within meaning of s.2(f) but business housed in a building along with machinery which was not covered under provision of Act is correct - It is evident from terms of settlement that dominant intention of parties was to create a lease for running business of an iron casting foundry - No infirmity in the HC judgment - Appeal dismissed.
