Arjunan v State Represented By Inspector of Police and others

Supreme Court of India

15 July 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Singh, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 1905; (2005) 13 SCC 454

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 148, 149, 302, 307, 324 and 364 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellants/accused allegedly caused murder of deceased - Trial Court found accused guilty and awarded punishment u/ss. 148 and 302 IPC - Appellants filed appeal before HC - HC found appellants guilty u/s. 148 IPC and upheld conviction - Hence, instant appeals - Whether conviction of appellants u/s. 148 IPC was justifiable - Held, in order to convict appellants of offence u/s. 148 IPC , it was necessary for HC to first record a finding that they were members of an unlawful assembly - HC had not recorded such findings , in fact, A-5 to A-7 had been acquitted of charge u/s. 307, 149 IPC and A-3 and A4 were not even charged of such offences - In instant case there was neither any finding nor any material on record on basis which appellant had been convicted u/s. 148 IPC - Therefore, conviction was set aside - Appeals allowed.
