Chairman, Life Insurance Corporation and others v Rajiv Kumar Bhasker

Supreme Court of India

28 July 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Ashok Bhan

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 572; (2005) 6 SCC 188; AIR 2005 SC 3087; 2005 (4) AWC 3210; 2006 (1) Bom.C.R. 882; 2005 (12) CCC 356; [2005] 126 Comp Cas 809; 2005 (4) JhrCR(SC) 34; JT 2005 (6) SC 416; 2005 (4) MLJ(SC) 194; 2005 (141) PLR 429; 2005(5) SCALE 668; [2005] Supp1 S.C.R. 867; 2005 (3) UPLBEC 2539

Case Digest

Subject: Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Life Insurance Corporation of India (Agents) Regulation, 1972

Summary: [A.] Contract & Commercial - Consumer Protection - Insurance - Life Insurance Corporation Act, 1956, s. 49 - Consumer Protection Act, 1986 - Indian Contract Act, 1872, ss. 182, 185, 186 - Life Insurance Corporation of India (Agents) Regulation, 1972 - Salary Saving Scheme - Responsibility for collection of premium by deducting same from salary of employee and making over same to Corporation was of employer - Held, even in case of non-payment of premium for any reason whatsoever, in view of object Scheme seeks to achieve, it was duty of insurer to inform employee about consequences of non-receipt of such premium from employer - Corporation cannot be permitted to take advantage of its own wrong as also wrong of its agent - Corporation liable - Civil Appeal No. 2357 of 2003 dismissed - Civil Appeal No. 2357 of 2003 allowed.
