Ghanashyam Das v State of Assam

Supreme Court of India

27 July 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Venkatarama Reddi, D.M. Dharmadhikari

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 1932; (2005) 13 SCC 387

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s.302 - Evidence Act, 1872, ss.8, 27 - Murder - Conviction - Dying declaration - Appellant/accused was charged for offence of killing ‘G’ with sharp cutting weapon - On basis of evidence, Trial Court convicted appellant u/s. 302 of IPC - On appeal, HC confirmed conviction order of lower Courts - Hence instant appeal.

Held, most incriminating evidence in appellant case is dying declaration made by ‘G’ to PW 4. After uttering words that accused appellant ‘cut him’ victim became unconscious. It may be recalled that PW4 was with ‘G’ till they parted company to go to their respective houses and within a few minutes thereafter, incident had happened. There is absolutely no reason why PW4 would come forward to give a false version to implicate accused. Oral dying declaration made to PW 4 was believed by Trial Court as well as by HC. In FIR lodged without delay, oral dying declaration was specifically mentioned. Another incriminating circumstance which corroborates case of prosecution is that appellant led PWs 10, 12 to Kharbhanga river side and pointed out place where he had thrown away khukri. According to evidence of PWs 12, 10 and 6, khukri was recovered from river with help of a diver. Courts eschewed circumstance from consideration on ground that no information was recorded by PWs 12 and 10 so as to attract s.27 of Act. Evidence of PWs12 and 6 to effect that appellant led them to spot and pointed out place where khukri was thrown, which fact stands confirmed by its recovery, can be looked into to throw light on conduct of appellant u/s.8 of Act. Possibility of ‘G’ saying a few words before he became unconscious cannot be ruled out especially when there is no cross-examination of medical officer on this aspect. Findings recorded by both Courts that prosecution case has been established beyond reasonable doubt cannot be faulted. Appeal dismissed.
