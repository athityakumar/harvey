Messrs Transmission Corporation of A.P. Limited v Messrs Lanco Kondapalli Power Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

15 December 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, P.P. Naolekar

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 808; (2006) 1 SCC 540; 2006 (1) ARBLR 1; JT 2005 (10) SC 542; 2006 (1) RCR(Civil) 122; 2005(10) SCALE 233; 2006 (1) SCJ 435; [2005] Supp5 S.C.R. 790

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Constitution; Electricity & Energy

Summary: [A.] Arbitration & ADR - Electricity & Energy - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, ss. 11(3), 11(4), 9 - Andhra Pradesh Electricity Reform Act, 1998, ss. 28, 37(1), 50 - Electricity Act, 2003, ss. 174, 86(1)(f) - Electricity Regulatory Commissions Act, 1998, s. 185 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 226, 14(2), 136, 14(1) - Whether High Court committed a serious error in passing an order of injunction? - Whether jurisdiction of the Civil Court is barred and any dispute and difference between the two licensees and/or two generating companies can be referred to an arbitration of the Commission only, as envisaged u/s. 86(1)(f) of the 2003 Act - Prima facie s. 50 of the 1998 Act, which bars the jurisdiction of the Civil Court , is required to be read with Section 37 thereof - Resolution of the disputes between the parties rests upon the proper interpretation of the said Power Purchase Agreement and in particular the definition of 'installed capacity' - Who would arbitrate in respect of the said dispute is the principal question - In the event if it ultimately be held that the arbitration clause contained in the contract stood superseded by the provisions of the 1998 Act and the 2003 Act, arguably, the question of Civil Court's granting of an order of injunction in terms of s. 9 or the High Court to determine the question as to who should be appointed as an arbitrator, may not arise - As to whether s. 86(1)(f) of the 2003 Act confers an exclusive jurisdiction to decide all disputes and differences between a licensee and a generating company is open to question - It may or may not be that the said provision may have to be read with other provisions contained in the power of the Commission to resolve disputes between various parties - It would be matter of construction of the relevant provisions as to whether by reason of s. 86(1)(f) of the 2003 Act, the Commission derives a power so as to enable it to arbitrate also in relation to a dispute arising out an agreement although the Commission may not have any role - Not a fit case for interference with the High Court's judgment - Appeal dismissed.
