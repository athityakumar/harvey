P. C. Agarwala v Payment of Wages Inspector, M. P., and Others

Supreme Court of India

26 September 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 849; (2005) 8 SCC 104; (2005) SCC (L&S) 1089; AIR 2005 SC 3576; [2005] 127 Comp Cas 787; 2005 (107) FLR 826; JT 2005 (8) SC 544; 2005(7) SCALE 519; 2005 (6) SLR 401; [2005] Supp3 S.C.R. 514

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Employed Person, Personality, Industrial Disputes (Madhya Pradesh Amendment) Act, 1983

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Payment of Wages Act, 1936 - Industrial Disputes (Madhya Pradesh Amendment) Act, 1983 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 25(O) - Sick Industrial Companies (Special Provisions) Act, 1985, s. 15 - Factories Act, 1948 - Whether directors of a company are personally liable for payment of wages? - Held, u/s. 3 of Act as amended by M P, Amendment, liability is cast on a person who has been named as manager of factory and employer jointly - Therefore, in order to find out whether director had a responsibility for making payment, two different things have to be established; (i) he was employer or (ii) he was a person who has been named as manager of factory - As High Court has proceeded to hold directors liable by introducing expression 'occupier', which expression is used in Factories Act and not in Act, basic premises on which High Court proceeded are clearly untenable - Appeal allowed.
