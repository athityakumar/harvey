U.P.S.R.T.C. v Imtiaz Hussain

Supreme Court of India

12 December 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 952; (2006) 1 SCC 380; AIR 2006 SC 649; 2006 (1) AWC 414; 2006 (2) CalLT(SC) 72; 2006 (1) CLR 256; JT 2005 (10) SC 496; 2006 (1) LLJ 714; 2005(10) SCALE 149; 2006 (1) SCJ 191; 2006 (1) SLR 409; [2005] Supp5 S.C.R. 725; 2006 (1) UPLBEC 851

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: [A.] Service - CPC, 1908, s. 152 - Uttar Pradesh Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 6(6) - Respondent a conductor did not issue tickets to passengers - Removal from service - Reinstatement with out back wages directed by Labour Court - Labour Court corrected judgment as continuity of service with full back wages - HC held that respondent was entitled to 50% of back wages with 9% interest - Appeal against - Held, after passing of judgment, decree or order, same becomes final subject to any further avenues of remedies provides in respect of same and very Court or tribunal cannot, on mere change of view, be entitled to vary terms of judgments, decrees and orders earlier passed except by means of review - Order of labour court modifying award and impugned judgment of HC are indefensible and are set aside - Appeal allowed.
