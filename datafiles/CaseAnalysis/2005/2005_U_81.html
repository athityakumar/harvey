Sushil Kumar Sharma v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 July 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 425; (2005) 6 SCC 281; (2005) SCC (Cr) 1473; AIR 2005 SC 3100; 2005 CRLJ 3439; JT 2005 (6) SC 266; 2005 (3) KLT 611; 2005 (3) RCR(Criminal) 745; 2005(5) SCALE 523; [2005] Supp1 S.C.R. 730

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Constitution - Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 304-B, 306, 498A - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 113-B - Criminal Law (Second Amendment) Act, 1983 - Prayer to declare s.498 A to be unconstitutional and ultra vires in alternative to formulate guidelines so that innocent persons are not victimized by unscrupulous persons making false accusations - Held, if a statutory provision is otherwise intra-vires, constitutional and valid, mere possibility of abuse of power in a given case would not make it objectionable, ultra-vires or unconstitutional - Directed that investigating agencies and courts should take effort to see that an innocent person is not made to suffer on account of unfounded, baseless and malicious allegations - Writ Petition disposed of.
