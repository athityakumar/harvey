Uttar Pradesh State Spinning Co. Limited v R. S. Pandey and another

Supreme Court of India

26 September 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 1250; (2005) 8 SCC 264; 2005 (6) AWC 6059; 2005 (107) FLR 729; JT 2005 (12) SC 242; 2005(7) SCALE 634; 2005 (6) SLR 359; [2005] Supp3 S.C.R. 603

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service

Keywords: U.P. Industrial Disputes Act, 1947

Summary: Service - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - U.P. Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Termination - Respondents while working in the appellant's concern made claims of 15% of the basic pay as an interim relief as was being paid to the officers and clerical staff at the Headquarters of the appellant's concern- Appellant issued show cause notice to respondent but terminated his services before receipt of the reply to the show cause notice - Respondents filed writ petition against said order - Appellant contended that writ petition filed by the respondents should not be entertained as they had efficacious, alternative and statutory remedy provided under the Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 r/w. U.P. Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - HC rejected the plea relating to existence of alternative remedy and allowed the ground that the final order was passed before the receipt of the show cause reply, quashed the proceedings - Hence, present appeal - Whether order of HC could be upheld? - Held, writ petition u/art. 226 of the Constitution should not be entertained when the statutory remedy is available under the Act, unless exceptional circumstances are made out - However, when the HC had entertained a writ petition notwithstanding existence of an alternative remedy SC while dealing with the matter in an appeal should not permit the question to be raised unless the HC's reasoning for entertaining the writ petition is found to be palpably unsound and irrational - In present case respondents were terminated before receipt of the reply to the show cause notice - Hence, impugned order of HC set aside - Fresh enquiry shall be held from the stage of service of show cause notice and consideration of the reply, if any, filed in accordance with the standing orders holding the field - Respondent No. 1 shall be re-instated to service but without any back wages and other service benefits and his re-instatement shall be solely for the purpose of completing the departmental proceedings - His entitlements, if any, would be adjudicated by the authorities depending upon the result of the disciplinary proceedings - Appeal allowed.
