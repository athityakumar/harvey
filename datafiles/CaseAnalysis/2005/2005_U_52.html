Pattammal and Others v Union of India and Another

Supreme Court of India

08 November 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Ashok Bhan

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 717; (2005) 13 SCC 63; AIR 2006 SC 291; 2006 (1) AWC 216; 2006 (1) CTC 232; JT 2005 (9) SC 324; 2006 (1) MLJ(SC) 177; 2006 (1) RCR(Civil) 14; 2005(9) SCALE 182; 2005 (Supp) RD 641; [2005] Supp5 S.C.R. 41

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 23(1A), 4(1), 51A - Land Acquisition Collector classified acquired lands as 'Wet' lands and fixed market value - Reference Court classified lands as having potential value as house sites and increased value - HC reduced market value - Appealed herein - Held, Reference Court adopted correct procedure upon examination of location and potential value of acquired lands in its detailed award, as also contemporaneous sale deeds which indicated that there had been a steep escalation of price of lands in immediate vicinity of acquired lands - No justification for Madras HC to have reduced market value of acquired lands - Appeals allowed.
