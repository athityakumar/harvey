State of Himachal Pradesh v Asha Ram

Supreme Court of India

17 November 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

H.K. Sema, P.P. Naolekar

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 731; (2005) 13 SCC 766; (2006) 2 SCC (Cr) 296; AIR 2006 SC 381; 2006 CRLJ 133; 2006 CRLJ 139; JT 2005 (9) SC 574; 2006 (1) RCR(Criminal) 139; 2005(9) SCALE 371; 2006 (1) SCJ 324; [2005] Supp5 S.C.R. 280

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure; Women & Children

Summary: (A) Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 376 - Rape - Acquittal by HC - Sustainability - Respondent father allegedly raped PW1 daughter - Trial Court convicted respondent for rape - However, HC, in appeal, acquitted the respondent from charges - Whether the order of acquittal recorded by the HC was sustainable - Held, statement of the PW1 was well corroborated in all material particulars by PW2, sister of the PW1 - PW1 was also subjected to lengthy cross-examination but nothing could be elicited to dislodge the creditworthiness of her testimony in the examination-in-chief - Further, testimony of PWs 1 and 2 was also corroborated by the statement of PW3, the mother of PW1 - PW 11, doctor, examined the respondent and opined that there was nothing to suggest that the respondent was incapable of performing sexual intercourse - Testimony of PW1 was well corroborated by the testimony of PW2 and PW3 corroborated by the medical evidence of PWs 4, 11 and 12 inspired confidence and the Trial Court had rightly convicted the respondent u/s. 376 IPC - HC erred in law as well as on facts and thereby committed grave miscarriage of justice in acquitting the respondent by reversing the conviction of the respondent recorded by the Trial Court u/s. 376 IPC - Hence, impugned HC order was set aside and the Trial Court order convicting the respondent u/s. 376 was restored - Appeal allowed.

(B) Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 376 - Committing of rape by father on daughter - Quantum of sentence - Determination - Held, offence of rape was grave by its nature, however, when the perpetrator of the crime was the father against his own daughter it was more graver and the rarest of rare, which warranted a strong deterrent judicial hand - Even in ordinary criminal terminology a rape was a crime more heinous than murder as it destroyed the very soul of a hapless woman - A father was the fortress, refuge and the trustee of his daughter - In the instant case, respondent father jeopardised PW1 daughter's future prospect of getting married, enjoying marital life, had been totally devastated - Not only that, PW1 carried an indelible social stigma on her head and deathless shame as long as she lived - Hence, while maintaining the conviction recorded by the Trial Court, sentence of 5 years' rigorous imprisonment awarded by the Trial Court was enhanced to imprisonment for life with enhanced amount of fine - Appeal allowed.
