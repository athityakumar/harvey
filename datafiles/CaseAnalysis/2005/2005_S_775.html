Hafiz v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

04 October 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 861; (2005) 12 SCC 599; AIR 2006 SC 632; 2006 CRLJ 522; 2005(8) SCALE 138; [2005] Supp3 S.C.R. 952

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Criminal - IPC, 1860, ss. 302, 304, 326, 34 - CrPC, 1973, ss. 161, 313 - Trial Court convicted appellant and sentenced to undergo rigorous imprisonment for life - HC found appellant guilty for commission of offence u/s. 304, Part II and sentenced to undergo rigorous imprisonment for a period of seven years - Appeal against - Held, purported exercise of right of private defence cannot also be accepted in view of fact that even FIR lodged by Hafiz has not been brought on record - Other accused persons-respondents guilty of offence u/s. 326, 34 - Appellant found guilty u/s. 304 part II - Appeals disposed of.
