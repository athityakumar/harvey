(1) Md. Munna; (2) Kartick Biswas v (1) Union of India and Others; (2) State of West

Supreme Court of India

16 September 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

K.G. Balakrishnan, B.N. Srikrishna

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 558; (2005) 7 SCC 417; (2005) SCC (Cr) 1688; AIR 2005 SC 3440; 2005 CRLJ 4124; 2005 (123) DLT 388; 2006 (1) G.L.R. 114; JT 2005 (8) SC 348; 2005 (4) KLT 256; 2005(7) SCALE 305; [2005] Supp3 S.C.R. 233

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Human Rights; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 32 - IPC, 1860, ss. 302, 34, 53, 53A, 55, 57, 60 - West Bengal Correctional Services Act, 1992, s. 61 - Code Of Criminal Procedure (Amendment) Act, 1955 - CrPC, 1973, ss. 383, 386, 401, 53(4) - Prisoners Act, 1900, s. 32 - Offence of murder u/s. 302 r/w s.34 IPC - Sentence of imprisonment for life - Undergone more than 21 years imprisonment - Writ of habeas corpus sought herein - Contention raised that duration of imprisonment for life is 20 years u/s. 61 of W.B. Act - Detention beyond that illegal - And 'imprisonment for life' can not be treated as 'rigorous imprisonment for life' unless commuted u/s. 55 of the IPC to 'rigorous imprisonment for a term not exceeding 14 years' - Petitioner is liable to be released from detention - Held, it was unnecessary for Legislature to specifically mention that imprisonment for life would be rigorous imprisonment for life as it is imposed as punishment for grave offences - Life imprisonment is not equivalent to imprisonment for fourteen years or for twenty years - Petitioner is not entitled to be released on any of grounds so long as there is no order of remission passed by appropriate Govt. in his favour - Petition dismissed.
