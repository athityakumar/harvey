State of Madhya Pradesh v Babbu Barkare Alias Dalap Singh

Supreme Court of India

13 May 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 764; (2005) 5 SCC 413; (2005) SCC (Cr) 1335; AIR 2005 SC 2846; 2005 CRLJ 3117; JT 2005 (11) SC 257; 2005 (3) RCR(Criminal) 18; 2005(5) SCALE 227; [2005] Supp1 S.C.R. 381

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art.136 - Criminal Law (Amendment) Act, 1983, ss. 375, 376 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 376 - Rape - Conviction - Sustainability - Respondent-accused was sentenced to undergo rigorous imprisonment for period of 7 years with a fine of Rs. 2,000/- for alleged commission of offences punishable u/s. 376 IPC - On appeal by respondent, HC directed sentence to be reduced to period already had undergone, on ground that respondent was illiterate labourer aged about 20 years at time of commission of offence - Appellant contended that reduction of sentence as done by HC was contrary to law as laid down by SC in several cases - Hence, instant appeal - Whether reduction of sentence on ground that respondent was illiterate labourer aged about 20 years at time of commission of offence, was sustainable - Held, sentences prescribed for offences relatable to s. 376 IPC were imprisonment for life or up to period of 10 years - Ss. 375 and 376 IPC had been substantially changed by the 1983 Act, and several new sections viz. ss. 376-A, 376-B, 376-C and 376-D IPC were introduced by the 1983 Act, which reflected legislative intent to curb with iron hand, offence of rape which affected dignity of woman - Undue sympathy to impose inadequate sentence would do more harm to justice system to undermine public confidence in efficacy of law and society could not long endure under such serious threats - In Dhananjoy Chatterjee v. State of W.B. 1994 Indlaw SC 1743 Court should not only keep in view rights of criminal, but also rights of victim of crime and society at large, while considering imposition of appropriate punishment - If Court did not mention reasons for imposing minimum sentence in judgment, there was no scope for awarding sentence lesser than prescribed minimum - Only reason indicated by HC was that accused belonged to rural areas, same could by no stretch of imagination be considered either adequate or special - Hence, HC's order was clearly unsustainable and was set aside - Appeal allowed.
