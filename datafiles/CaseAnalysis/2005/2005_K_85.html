Kapila Hingorani v State Of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

13 January 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 16; (2005) SCC (L&S) 206; (2005) 2 SCC 262; AIR 2005 SC 980; 2005 (2) CompLJ 59; 2005 (104) FLR 869; JT 2005 (1) SC 440; 2005 (1) LLN 1084; 2005(1) SCALE 267; [2005] 1 S.C.R. 456; 2005 (2) UPLBEC 1490

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Application for clarification to order dated 9th May, 2003 - Whether State of Bihar or State of Jharkhand or both would be required to pay unpaid salary to employees of BHALCO - Held, they are bound to pay salaries of employees of public sector undertakings on the ground that employees have a human right as also a fundamental right under art. 21 which States are bound to protect - Directions, which have been issued on 9th May, 2003 as also which are being issued herein, are in furtherance of human and fundamental rights of employees concerned and not by way of an enforcement of their legal right to arrears of salaries - Applications disposed of.
