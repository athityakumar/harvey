State Through Central Bureau of Investigation v Amarmani Tripathi and another

Supreme Court of India

26 September 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhan, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 1225; (2005) 8 SCC 21; AIR 2005 SC 3490; 2005 CRLJ 4149; 2005 (123) DLT 362; JT 2005 (8) SC 517; 2005(7) SCALE 489; [2005] Supp3 S.C.R. 454

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Extra Judicial Confession, Due Course Of Justice, Close Range

Summary: Criminal - CrPC, 1973, ss. 161, 173(8), 437, 439 - IPC, 1860, s. 201 - Appeal against granting of bail - Offence of murder - Held, in this case conduct of accused from day of murder in trying to interfere, detract and mislead investigation and to threaten and coerce witnesses is very relevant - High Court has failed to deal with vast material placed by CBI which clearly indicated that accused has, at all material times, tried to interfere with course of investigation, tamper with witnesses, fabricate evidence, intimidate or create obstacles in path of investigation officers and derail case - Bail cancelled.
