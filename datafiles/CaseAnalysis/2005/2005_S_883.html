National Insurance Company Limited v Swaroopa and others

Supreme Court of India

17 March 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.K. Sabharwal, D.M. Dharmadhikari

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 1530; (2005) 11 SCC 419; 2007 (1) ACC 207; AIR 2005 SC 2472

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Motor Vehicle Act,1988 - Goods vehicle - Gratuitous passenger - Liability to pay compensation - Respondent nos. 1 to 6/claimants were legal representatives of deceased who died in road accident on 28-1-1996 - Respondent no.7 was owner of vehicle in dispute which insured with appellant Insurance Company - Claimants filed claim petition on 9-7-1996 under 1988 Act before Tribunal which granted compensation both against Insurance Company and owner of vehicle - Insurance Company filed appeal against its liability to pay compensation on ground that deceased was gratuitous passenger being carried in goods vehicle before HC which was dismissed - Insurance Company filed appeal against order of HC and raised question with regard to rate of interest granted by Tribunal - Hence instant appeals.

Held, as regards liability to pay compensation by Insurance Company to claimants, SC set aside judgment of HC and dismissed claim petition against appellant. SC clarifies that amount of compensation, if any that may have been paid to claimants shall be recoverable by Insurance Company from owner of vehicle and not from legal representatives of deceased. As regards rate of interest, accident took place in 1988. Tribunal decided claim petition in 1990 awarding compensation along with interest at rate of 12% per annum. HC has maintained rate of interest granted by Tribunal and only challenge of Insurance Company before HC was on question of rate of interest. Claimants are unrepresented having been served by publication in newspaper. Though Insurance Company may have point on issue of interest but having regard to facts and circumstances of case and that accident took place seventeen years ago, SC is not inclined to interfere with order of Tribunal in exercise of powers u/art. 136 of Constitution. Order accordingly.

Ratio - Insurance Company will not be liable to pay compensation in respect of gratuitous passenger being carried in goods vehicle if vehicle meets with accident.
