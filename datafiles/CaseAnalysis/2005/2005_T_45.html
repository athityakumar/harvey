S.D.O. Grid Corporation of Orissa Limited and v Timudu Oram

Supreme Court of India

28 July 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhan, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 441; (2005) 6 SCC 156; 2005 (3) ACC 508; 2005 (61) ALR 168; 2005 (4) AWC 3304; 2006 (2) JhrCR(SC) 161; JT 2005 (6) SC 476; 2005 (141) PLR 543; 2005 (3) RCR(Civil) 804; 2005(5) SCALE 686; [2005] Supp1 S.C.R. 891

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Electricity & Energy; Tort

Keywords: Act Of God

Summary: [A.] Constitution - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 226 - Compensation awarded to respondents for death due to electrocution after snapping of electric wires - Whether HC was justified in exercising its power u/art. 226 and award compensation to respondent even though appellants had denied liability on ground that deaths had not occurred as a result of their negligence but because of negligence of respondent themselves or of an act of God or because of an act of some other persons? - Held, appellants had disputed negligence attributed to it and no finding has been recorded by HC that GRIDCO was in any way negligent in performance of its duty - Awarding of compensation by HC in exercise of its jurisdiction u/art.226 cannot be justified - Appeals disposed of.
