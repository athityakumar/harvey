State of Rajasthan v Ram Chandra

Supreme Court of India

12 April 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 276; (2005) 5 SCC 151; (2005) SCC (Cr) 1010; AIR 2005 SC 2221; 2006 (1) CalLT(SC) 20; 2006 (1) CGLJ 44; 2005 CRLJ 2201; JT 2005 (4) SC 390; 2005 (3) KLT 601; 2005 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 540; 2005 (2) RCR(Criminal) 586; 2005(4) SCALE 60; [2005] 3 S.C.R. 496

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Health & Drug; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Narcotic Drugs And Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985, ss. 18, 41, 41(2), 42, 50, 8 - Mandatory requirement u/s.50 - Search of persons - Whether there is non-compliance with mandatory requirements of s.50? - Held, PW-3 arrived at spot after person was detained and search was proposed to be done by officer authorized - Respondent-accused was given option as to whether he would like to be searched in presence of PW-3 or nearest magistrate - He exercised his option to be searched in presence of PW-3 - HC's judgment is indefensible and is set aside and that of trial court is restored - Appeal allowed
