Uttar Pradesh State Textile Corporation Limited v P.C. Chaturvedi and others

Supreme Court of India

03 October 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 858; (2005) 8 SCC 211; (2005) SCC (L&S) 1108; AIR 2006 SC 87; 2005 (6) AWC 5986; 2005 (4) ESC 15; JT 2005 (12) SC 90; 2005(8) SCALE 46; 2005 (6) SLR 32; [2005] Supp3 S.C.R. 849; 2005 (6) Supreme 612

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Voluntary Retirement

Summary: [A.] Service - Administrative - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 21 - Uttar Pradesh State Textile Corporation Conduct, Control and Disciplinary Rules, 1992, r. 41 - Natural justice - Alleged misconduct - Punishment of removal from service - Writ Petition by employee allowed holding that departmental proceedings were non-est and in gross violation of principles of natural justice also non-payment of subsistence allowance also vitiated departmental proceedings - Appealed here - Held, it has not been shown as to how non-supply of list caused any prejudice - It cannot be held as an absolute position in law that non-payment of subsistence allowance amounts to denial of opportunity and vitiates departmental proceedings - HC's judgment is, therefore, clearly unsustainable - Appeals disposed of.
