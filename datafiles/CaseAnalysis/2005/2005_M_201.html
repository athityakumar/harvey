Govindaraju v Mariamman

Supreme Court of India

04 February 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhan, A.K. Mathur

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 100; (2005) 2 SCC 500; AIR 2005 SC 1008; 2005 (2) ALD(SC) 90; 2005 (59) ALR 133; 2005 (1) AWC 787; 2005 (3) Bom.C.R. 87; 2005 (2) JhrCR(SC) 267; JT 2005 (2) SC 107; 2005 (2) MLJ(SC) 122; 2005 (2) RCR(Civil) 105; 2005 (98) RD 731; 2005(2) SCALE 3; [2005] 1 S.C.R. 1100; 2005 (1) UC 557

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Ex-Parte

Summary: Civil Procedure - CPC, 1908, s. 100 - Suit for declaration of title and permanent injunction - Scope of hearing of Second Appeal by HC - Whether HC right in setting aside findings of fact recorded by courts below on re-appreciation of evidence in Second Appeal? - Held, even if HC was of view that findings of fact recorded by courts below were wrong, these findings of fact could not be disturbed without coming to conclusion that findings recorded were perverse i.e. based on misreading of evidence or based on no evidence - HC did not come to such a conclusion - Single Judge also did not come to conclusion that appeal involved other substantial questions of law or formulate the same - Substantial question of law does not arise in appeal - Appeal allowed.
