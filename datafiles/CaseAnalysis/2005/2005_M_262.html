Messrs Sanjeev Woolen Mills v Commissioner of Income Tax, Mumbai

Supreme Court of India

24 November 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

P.P. Naolekar, AR. Lakshmanan

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 742; (2005) 13 SCC 307; AIR 2006 SC 500; 2005 (199) CTR 441; [2005] 279 ITR 434; JT 2005 (10) SC 83; 2005(9) SCALE 519; 2006 (1) SCJ 158; [2005] Supp5 S.C.R. 459; [2005] 149 TAXMAN 431

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Best Judgment Assessment

Summary: [A.] Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 144, 145, 145(1), 80HHC - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 13 - Method of valuation of closing stock - Assessing Officer found that method of valuing closing stock at market value resulted in a distorted picture and assessee had artificially inflated profits in order to get benefit u/s. 80 HHC of Act, which amounted to tax planning with intent to defraud Revenue - Appealed herein - Held, income which has not been derived by assessee cannot be said to be income chargeable for income and, therefore, rejection of accounts maintained by assessee for valuation of closing stock by assessing officer and confirmed by HC is in accordance with law - Appeal dismissed.
