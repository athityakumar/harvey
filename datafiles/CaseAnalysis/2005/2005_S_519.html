Rama Subramanian @ Mani v State of Kerala

Supreme Court of India

07 September 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

K.G. Balakrishnan, Ashok Bhan

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 1266; (2007) 12 SCC 801; (2008) 3 SCC (Cr) 396; AIR 2006 SC 639; JT 2005 (8) SC 495

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 380, 392, 449 - Murder - Conviction - Maintainability - Appellant was found guilty of offences punishable u/ss. 302, 392, 449 of IPC by Sessions Judge for having caused death of one lady and her three children - Sessions Judge imposed capital punishment and death sentence was confirmed however, conviction and sentence of appellant u/s. 392 of IPC was set aside and in its place appellant was found guilty of offence punishable u/s. 380 of IPC by HC and his sentence was reduced - HC re-appreciated entire evidence and confirmed conviction and sentence - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether HC was justified in conforming conviction of appellant - Held, evidence adduced by prosecution satisfactorily proved that appellant and none else had committed crime under IPC - Appellant was working as servant in house of deceased for about 8 to 9 years prior to incident and it showed that appellant had enjoyed full freedom in house and there was evidence to effect that services of appellant were terminated prior to incident - There was reliable evidence to show movement of appellant immediately after incident - Scientific evidence adduced by prosecution was very clear and convincing to prove guilt of appellant - Appellant was arrested eight months later and his finger prints were taken and finger prints taken from appellant tallied with chance finger prints taken from bedroom of deceased - Evidence was very crucial and convincing to show that appellant was in room of deceased - Recovery of various gold ornaments pursuant to information given by appellant was satisfactorily proved by various witnesses - Therefore, all that proved guilt of appellant beyond reasonable doubt and Sessions Judge rightly found him guilty for offence and HC was fully justified in confirming same - Hence, sentence of death imposed on appellant was commuted to life imprisonment and to that extent appeal was allowed and however, appellants conviction and sentence as regards on all other counts were maintained - Appeal allowed.
