Shakuntla Devi v Kamla and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 April 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, D.M. Dharmadhikari, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 265; (2005) 5 SCC 390; 2005 (3) ALD(SC) 118; 2005 (59) ALR 599; 2005 (2) AWC 1599; 2005 (3) JhrCR(SC) 91; JT 2005 (4) SC 315; 2005 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 634; 2005 (2) LCD 1659; 2005 (3) MahLJ 578; 2005 (3) MLJ(SC) 56; 2005 (3) MPLJ 254; 2005 (141) PLR 351; 2005 (2) RCR(Civil) 668; 2005(4) SCALE 21; 2005 (Supp) Bom.C.R. 787; 2005 (Supp) RD 759

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Family & Personal; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Civil Procedure - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908, s.11 - Hindu Succession Act, 1956, s.14(1) CPC - Suit for possession of properties on basis of a declaratory decree which is not a lawful decree as per law prevailing - Whether finding of HC in impugned judgment that earlier decree obtained by appellant being declaratory in nature would not operate as res judicata in favour of appellant and would not enable her to obtain possession through court of law by filing a suit for possession, is correct in law or not ? - Held, principles of res judicata u/s.11 of CPC will not be attracted and it is open to defendant in such suits to establish that decree relied upon by plaintiff is not a good law or court granting such decree did not have jurisdiction to grant such decree - Appeal dismissed.
