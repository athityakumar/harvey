State of Madhya Pradesh v Munna Choubey and Another

Supreme Court of India

24 January 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 50; (2005) SCC (Cr) 559; (2005) 2 SCC 710; AIR 2005 SC 682; 2005 (3) CalLT(SC) 62; 2005 CRLJ 913; JT 2005 (2) SC 122; 2005(1) SCALE 602; [2005] 1 S.C.R. 781

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Women & Children - Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 109(1), 375, 376(1), 376-A, 376-B, 376-C, 376-D, 450 - Whether Single Judge right in reducing respective sentence as imposed on each of respondents? - Held, only ground indicated by HC for reducing the sentence was that accused belonged to rural areas - This reason can by no stretch of imagination be considered either adequate or special - Requirement in law is cumulative in that respect - HC's order unsustainable and is set aside - Appeal allowed.
