Jagdish and Another v State of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

12 May 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Venkatarama Reddi, P.P. Naolekar

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 390; (2005) 11 SCC 146; AIR 2005 SC 2576; 2005 CRLJ 3073; JT 2005 (5) SC 579; 2005 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 307; 2005 (3) RCR(Criminal) 1; 2005(5) SCALE 258

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Criminal - IPC, 1860, ss. 307, 323, 34 - Appellants were convicted and sentenced - Appeal against conviction - Held, it is highly doubtful whether Appellant no. 2 shared his common intention with Appellant no. 1 and both of them wanted to kill PW-8 - Conviction and sentence of Appellant no. 2 is set aside - There are no compelling grounds to discard testimony of injured eye witness who was brutally assaulted by Appellant no. 1 with a dangerous weapon - Affirm conviction of Appellant no. 1 - Since Appellant no. 1 came forward to pay compensation of Rs. 1 lac awarded by HC, sentence reduced to eight years rigorous imprisonment instead of ten years - Appeal partly allowed.
