Tata Teleservices Limited v Commissioner of Customs

Supreme Court of India

13 December 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Ruma Pal, Arijit Pasayat, B.N. Srikrishna

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 989; (2006) 1 SCC 746; 2006 (194) E.L.T. 11; 2006 (72) RLT 624

Case Digest

Subject: Excise

Summary: Excise - Customs Tariff Act - Whether telephone LSP 340 imported would be entitled to benefit of exemption granted by Customs Circular No. 57/2003 dated June, 2003 to Cellular Telephones classified under Tariff Heading 8525-17? - Contrary decisions taken by different Tribunals - This Court observed that reasoning of Bombay Bench of Tribunal as well as that of Andhra Pradesh High Court must be affirmed and decision of Delhi Tribunal set aside insofar as it relates to eligibility of LSP 340 to benefit of exemption notification - Andhra Pradesh HC was correct in coming to conclusion that Board had, in impugned circular, pre-determined issue of common parlance that was a matter of evidence and should have been left to department to establish before adjudicating authorities - Bombay Bench was also correct in its conclusion that circular sought to impose a limitation on exemption notification which exemption notification itself did not provide - It was not open to Board to whittle down exemption notification in such a manner - Exemption notification merely reproduced language of Entry 852520-17 and since exemption notification merely reproduced tariff entry, limitation sought to be imposed by Board would tantamount also to reading limitation into classification itself - Since issue would be ultimately a question of evidence, onus was on department to prove by appropriate evidence that goods were classifiable under 8525-19 being residuary entry - This department could have done by negativing claim of importers that goods were classifiable under Tariff Entry 8525-17 and by establishing that imported goods could not reasonably be classified under any other head - In this particular case onus had not been discharged by revenue - Only evidence on record was opinion sought for by Ministry of Finance itself and given by Department of Telecommunications to effect that Model LSP 340 was in fact covered by phrase 'cellular telephone' - Held, since there is no dispute that technology used in LSP 340 and hand held mobile phone is same, there is no warrant to limit either tariff entry or exemption notification to hand held cellular phones - Neither range nor size would make any difference.
