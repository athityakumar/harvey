Sangeeta Singh v Union Of India And Ors

Supreme Court of India

23 August 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 506; (2005) 7 SCC 484; AIR 2005 SC 4459; 2005 (4) AWC 3645; JT 2005 (7) SC 545; 2005(6) SCALE 632; [2005] Supp2 S.C.R. 823

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Petroleum & Gas; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Trade - Constitution - Petroleum & Gas - Dealership and distributorship of various petroleum products - Eligibility of appellants - Appellants relatives already hold letters of intent for dealership or distributionship - HC held that if father-in-law holds dealership then daughter-in-law is disqualified - Appealed herein - Held, court cannot read anything into a statutory provision or a stipulated condition which is a plain and unambiguous - A statute is an edict of Legislature - HC's judgment set aside - Remitted matter to HC for consideration of other issues raised - Appeal allowed.
