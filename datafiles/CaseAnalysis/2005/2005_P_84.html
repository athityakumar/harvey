Ramlal and Another v Phagua and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 October 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

AR. Lakshmanan, Arijit Pasayat

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 722; (2006) 1 SCC 168; 2005 (61) ALR 613; 2006 (1) JhrCR(SC) 82; 2005 (4) RCR(Civil) 514; 2005 (99) RD 796; 2006 (2) RLW 1506; 2005(8) SCALE 427; [2005] Supp4 S.C.R. 331

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Land & Property - Transfer Of Property Act, 1882, s. 53 - Sale deed executed for obtaining a loan and also executed an agreement stating therein that in case respondent returns Rs. 400/- within 3 years, property shall be reconveyed to him - Whether respondent has title over suit land and on his paying a sum of Rs. 400/- and shall he have right to get possession of suit land? - HC rightly come to conclusion that sale deed in question was not in fact a real sale deed but was by way of surety and thus did not pass any right, title or interest in favour of vendee - Appeal dismissed.
