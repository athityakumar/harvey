State Of U.P. & Anr v Pawan Kumar Tiwari & Ors

Supreme Court of India

04 January 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, G.P. Mathur, A.K. Mathur

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 1; (2005) 2 SCC 10; (2005) SCC (L&S) 193; AIR 2005 SC 658; 2005 (1) AWC 363; 2005 (1) ESC 86; 2005 (104) FLR 582; 2005 (1) JhrCR(SC) 147; JT 2005 (1) SC 151; 2005(1) SCALE 17; [2005] 1 S.C.R. 21; 2005 (1) SLR 637; 2005 (2) UPLBEC 1285

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: Service - Judiciary - Posts of Civil Judge (Junior Division) - UP Judicial Service - U.P. Public Service (Reservation for Physically Handicapped, Dependants of Freedom Fighters and Ex- Servicemen) Act 1993 - U.P. Public Service (Reservation for Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribes and Other Backward Classes) Act 1994 - Percentages of reservation - Application of percentage against total number of posts - Whether respondent is entitled to appointment? - Held, total number of vacancies was 93 - Allocation of reservation and calculation done by appellants, number of reserved seats would be 47, leaving only 46 available for general category candidates - Reservation would exceed 50% which would be unconstitutional - Total number of reserved seats could not have been more than 46 out of 93 - If 47 candidates would have been considered for selection in general category, respondent was sure to find a place in the list of selected meritorious candidates and hence entitled to appointment - Appeal dismissed.
