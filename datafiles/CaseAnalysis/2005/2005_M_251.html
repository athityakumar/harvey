Dhariwal Industries Limited and Another v Messrs M.S.S. Food Products

Supreme Court of India

25 February 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

P.K. Balasubramanyan, B.P. Singh

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 125; (2005) 3 SCC 63; AIR 2005 SC 1999; 2005 (3) AWC 2934; 2005 (2) BLJR 929; 2005 (1) CGLJ 358; JT 2005 (2) SC 505; 2005 (2) MLJ 139; 2005 (2) MLJ(SC) 139; 2005 (30) PTC 233; 2005 (2) RCR(Civil) 112; 2005(2) SCALE 407; 2005 (2) UPLBEC 1111

Case Digest

Subject: Intellectual Property

Summary: Intellectual Property - Trade Marks Act, 1999, s. 27 - CPC, 1908, O. 39, r. 1 and 2 - Appeal against interim injunction - Trade mark 'Malikchand' and 'Manikchand' - Passing-off action - Whether plaintiff established a prima facie case for interim injunction? - Held, courts below cannot be said to have erred in thinking that balance of convenience was in favour of grant of interim injunction in favour of plaintiff - Appeal dismissed.
