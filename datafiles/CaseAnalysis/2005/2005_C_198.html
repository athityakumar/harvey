Messrs Dabur India Limited v Commissioner of Central Excise, Jamshedpur

Supreme Court of India

01 April 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, S.N. Variava, AR. Lakshmanan

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 234; (2005) 4 SCC 9; 2005 (182) E.L.T. 290; JT 2005 (4) SC 57; 2005(3) SCALE 617; [2005] 3 S.C.R. 144

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - Central Excise Tariff, Chapter 30, 33 , TI 33.04 - - Lal Tail and Janam Ghunti - Classification - (A) Whether Tribunal right in holding that Lal Tail ('red oil') is classifiable under heading 33.04 - Held, product has a Drug Controller's Licence - Evidence by way of prescriptions of Ayurvedic Doctors, who have prescribed these for treatment of rickets - Product Lal Tail would be a medicament and classifiable as such under Chapter 30 - (B) Classification of product 'Janam Ghunti' - - Held, Tribunal correctly held that if 'Janam Ghunti' is an aqueous distillates or aqueous solutions of essential oils - Classification would fall under Chapter 33 even though it may have therapeutic or prophylactic properties - Appeals partly allowed.
