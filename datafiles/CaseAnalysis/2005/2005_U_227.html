Gujarat Ambuja Cements Limited v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

17 March 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Ruma Pal, Arun Kumar

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 1012; (2005) 4 SCC 214; AIR 2005 SC 3020; 2005 (4) CompLJ 440; 2005 (182) E.L.T. 33; [2005] 274 ITR 194; JT 2005 (3) SC 389; 2005 (67) RLT 469; 2005(3) SCALE 307; [2005] 2 S.C.R. 1038; 2006 (3) S.T.R. 608; [2005] 144 TAXMAN 512

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Finance Act, 1998, Constitutionality, Service Tax (Amendment) Rules, 1998, Finance Act,2000

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Constitution - Parliament & Legislature - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Finance Act, 1994, ss. 65, 65(41)(j), 66, 70 - Finance Act, 1997, s. 84 - Finance Act, 1998 - Finance Act, 2003, s. 158 - Finance Act,2000, ss. 116, 117 - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, s. 2(14) - Punjab Passengers and Goods Taxation Act, 1952, s. 3(3) - Service Tax (Amendment) Rules, 1998 - Service Tax Rules, 1994, r. 2(l)(d) - Service Tax - Levy - Enactment - Validity - Petitioners were customers or clients of goods transport operators and forwarding and clearing agents - Petitioners contended that basis of decision rendered in Laghu Udyog Bharati & Anr. Vs. Union of India & Ors. 1999 Indlaw SC 101 had not been removed or displaced by impugned sections and could not overruled, replaced or overridden SC's decision , moreover, Parliament could not by resorting to residuary Entry 97 of List 1 of Schedule VII circumvented Entry 56 of List II and in guise of levying service tax in fact levy tax on transport of goods - Petitioners also challenged constitutional validity of imposition on ground that it operated in discriminatory manner by singling out only customers of goods transport operators and clearing and forwarding agents to pay tax - Hence, instant petition - Whether ss. 116 and 117 of the 2000 Act and s. 158 of the 2003 Act, by which decision of SC in Laghu Udyog Bharati & Anr. Vs. Union of India & Ors. 1999 Indlaw SC 101, striking down rr. 2(1)(d), (xii) and (xvii) of the 1994 Rules was sought to be overcome, were constitutionally valid - Held, it was apparent from s. 116 of the 2000 Act that all material portions of two sections which were found to be incompatible with the 1994 Rules were themselves amended so that now in body of the 2000 Act by virtue of amendment to word 'assessee' in s. 65(5) and amendment to s. 66(3), liability to pay tax was not on person providing taxable service however, as far as service provided by clearing and forwarding agents and goods transport operators were concerned, on person who paid for services - By s. 68(1A) of the 2000 Act, persons availing of services of goods transport operators or clearing and forwarding agents had explicitly been made liable to pay service tax - R. 2(1)(d) (xii) and (xvii) had been held to be illegal in Laghu Udyog Bharati & Anr. Vs. Union of India & Ors. 1999 Indlaw SC 101 only because charging provisions of the 2000 Act provided otherwise, however, charging section itself had been amended so as to make provisions of the 2000 Act and the 1994 Rules compatible, hence, criticism of earlier law upheld by SC could no longer be availed of - It was not case of petitioners that the 2000 Act was referable to any other entry apart from Entry 56 of List II, hence, negation of petitioners submission perforce led to conclusion that the 2000 Act fell within residuary power of Parliament under Entry 97 of List I - By ss. 116 and 117 of the 2000 Act, tax was sought to be levied from recipients of services, hence, petitioners should not claim that petitioners were not connected with service since service was rendered to them - Hence, claim of petitioners that by amendments brought by ss. 116 and 117 of the 2000 Act, decision in Laghu Udyog Bharati & Anr. Vs. Union of India & Ors. 1999 Indlaw SC 101 had been legislatively overruled, was rejected - Petitions dismissed.
