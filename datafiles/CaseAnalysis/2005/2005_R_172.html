Gurcharan Singh v Registrar, Co-Operative Societies, Himachal Pradesh And Ors

Supreme Court of India

15 September 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 1732; (2005) 7 SCC 565; 2005 (61) ALR 204; 2005 (4) AWC 3536; JT 2005 (8) SC 423; 2005(7) SCALE 457; [2005] Supp3 S.C.R. 230

Case Digest

Subject: Trusts & Associations

Summary: Trusts & Assiciations - Consitution of India, 1950, art. 12 - Maintainablity of writ petition -Instant appeal filed against the judgment of the HC, whereby HC held that it had no jurisdiction to entertain the writ petition - It was further held that the respondent was a Cooperative Society and was, therefore, not covered within the meaning of art. 12 of the Constitution - Whether HC was right in holding that the HC had no jurisdiction to entertain the writ petition.

Held, the basic factual aspects were not placed before the HC to determine the question whether the respondent-Society was 'State'' within the meaning of Art. 12 of the Constitution. In view of this, it would be appropriate for the HC to examine the question regarding the maintainability. Appeal disposed of.
