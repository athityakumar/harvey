Kapra Mazoor Ekta Union v Management of M/s. Birla Cotton Spinning and

Supreme Court of India

16 March 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Singh, N. Santosh Hegde, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 247; (2005) 13 SCC 777; (2006) SCC (L&S) 1635; AIR 2005 SC 1782; 2005 (2) AWC 1075; 2005 (3) JhrCR(SC) 38; 2005 (2) KLT 978; 2005 (2) LLJ 271; 2005(3) SCALE 218; [2005] 2 S.C.R. 888; 2005 (4) SLR 655

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Whether Industrial Tribunal was justified in recalling earlier Award made on June 12, 1987 and in framing an additional issue for adjudication by Tribunal? - Held, recall of Award of Tribunal was sought not on ground that in passing Award Tribunal had committed by procedural illegality or mistake of nature which vitiated proceeding itself and consequently Award, but on ground that some matters which ought to have been considered by Tribunal were not duly considered - Apparently recall or review sought was not a procedural review, but a review on merits - Such a review was not permissible in absence of a provision in Act conferring power of review on Tribunal either expressly or by necessary implication - Appeal dismissed.
