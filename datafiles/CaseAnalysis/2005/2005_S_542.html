State of Madhya Pradesh v Bala @ Balaram

Supreme Court of India

03 October 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

G.P. Mathur, R.C. Lahoti

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 649; (2005) 8 SCC 1; (2005) SCC (Cr) 1947; AIR 2005 SC 3567; 2005 CRLJ 4371; 2006 (3) JhrCR(SC) 219; JT 2005 (12) SC 386; 2005(8) SCALE 53; [2005] Supp3 S.C.R. 859

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Summary: [A.] Criminal - IPC, 1860, ss. 363, 366, 376(2)(g) - CrPC, 1973, ss. 384, 385, 386 - Reduction of sentence in an offence of Rape - Powers of appellate Court to dismiss appeals summarily - Conviction of accused u/ss. 363, 366, 376(2)(g) by Trial Court awarding him sentence of 10 years R.I. - HC partly allowing appeal, while upholding conviction of accused reduced sentence to period already undergone of nine and half months - State appeals on ground that sentence imposed by HC was wholly inadequate being contrary to minimum punishment prescribed by law - s.385 CrPC lays down procedure for hearing appeal not dismissed summarily and s. 385(2) thereof casts an obligation to send for records of case and to hear parties - Non consideration of evidence adduced by parties - No Satisfactory reason much less adequate and special reasons assigned by HC for reducing sentence to a term far below prescribed minimum - Appeal disposed of by HC in most unsatisfactory manner exhibiting complete non-application of mind - Judgment of HC set aside - Matter remitted back to HC for decision afresh - Appeal allowed.
