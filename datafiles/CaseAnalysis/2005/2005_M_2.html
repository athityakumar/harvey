NGEF Limited v Chandra Developers Private Limited and Another

Supreme Court of India

29 September 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 1062; (2005) 8 SCC 219; 2005 (4) BC 406; [2005] 127 Comp Cas 822; 2005 (2) DRTC 657; JT 2005 (12) SC 29; 2005 (4) MLJ(SC) 168; 2005(7) SCALE 715; [2005] Supp3 S.C.R. 747

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate

Keywords: Disposition, English Companies Act

Summary: Corporate - Sick Industrial Companies (Special Provisions) Act, 1985 - Companies Act, 1956 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Foreign Exchange Regulation Act, 1973 - Urban Land (Ceiling and Regulation) Act, 1976 - English Companies Act - Companies (Court) Rules, 1959 - Whether a company judge can issue an order regarding sale of company assets when assets are in control of Board for Industrial and Financial Reconstruction? - Held, Board for Industrial and Financial Reconstruction had admittedly power to sell assets of company but High Court until a winding up order is issued does not have same - Board for Industrial and Financial Reconstruction might have made an observation to effect that company may approach HC in case it intended to dispose of its property by private negotiation but same would not mean that Board for Industrial and Financial Reconstruction could delegate its power in favour of HC - Board for Industrial and Financial Reconstruction being a statutory authority in absence of any provision empowering it to delegate its power in favour of any other authority had no jurisdiction to do so - Company Court could exercise its power in accordance with law and not de hors it - If Company Court had no jurisdiction to pass impugned order, it could not derive any jurisdiction only because Board for Industrial and Financial Reconstruction said so - Appeals allowed.
