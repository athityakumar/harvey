Anandwardhan and another v Pandurang and others

Supreme Court of India

24 January 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Singh, Arun Kumar

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 1656; (2005) 11 SCC 195

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure,1973, s.156 - Failure to investigation - Jurisdiction - Due to allegation of affairs of two societies, respondents filed petitions before HC to take immediate cognizance of complaints made by him from time to time - HC disposed of said petitions and observed that it was not satisfied with pace of investigation and manner in which IO’s concerned were dealing with matter - HC directed Commissioner of Police to withdraw investigation of case pending with police stations concerned and allegations made in petitions be treated as FIR and allegations may be investigated on said basis - HC further directed that investigation be entrusted to Crime Branch and that it should be done directly under control and supervision of Additional Commissioner of Police - Hence instant appeals.

Held, in pursuance of order passed by HC, it appears that at stage of admission itself HC disposed of petitions filed by respondents. Court fails to understand how HC could have interfered in writ jurisdiction in a case of said nature. Court finds from material on record that several cases have been registered against respondent himself. It appears that he has sent some complaints to police station by post. There is also material to show that when police required his presence in connection with investigation of case, he did not appear before police. Police after investigating his complaint in one case has closed case finding it to be matter involving civil dispute. Court does not wish to make any comments about investigation of case or result of investigation. If grievance of respondent was that police was not properly investigating his case, or that report made by police was wrong or based on no investigation whatsoever, it was open to him to move Magistrate concerned. Petition should not have been entertained by HC when remedy is provided to aggrieved party under CrPC in accordance with procedure established by law. Appeals allowed.

Ratio - When police fails to investigate a case arising from FIR lodged before it disclosing commission of a cognizable offence, it is open to complainant to move Magistrate concerned for appropriate order u/s.156 of CrPC.
