Shanmughasundaram and Others v Diravia Nadar (D) by Lrs. and Another

Supreme Court of India

11 March 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

D.M. Dharmadhikari, G.P. Mathur

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 239; (2005) 10 SCC 728; AIR 2005 SC 1836; 2005 (1) ARBLR 401; 2005 (3) AWC 2942; JT 2005 (3) SC 186; 2005 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 216; 2005 (141) PLR 670; 2005 (2) RCR(Civil) 342; 2005(3) SCALE 62; [2005] 2 S.C.R. 649

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Summary: Arbitration and ADR - Arbitration Act, 1940 - Specific Releif Act, s. 12 - Enforceability of award - Review - Whether first award was set aside merely on ground of breach of principles of natural justice or it was set aside also on ground that sisters were not parties to arbitration agreement? - Held, Award, which is found unenforceable and invalid on date it is sought to be made a rule of court, cannot be upheld on possibilities and eventualities which might occur in future - An award cannot be upheld on guess and speculations - HC was fully justified in reversing order of civil court and coming to conclusion that arbitration could not have been revived - Appeal dismissed.
