Bangalore Development Authority and others v R. Hanumaiah and others

Supreme Court of India

03 October 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhan, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 860; (2005) 12 SCC 508; JT 2005 (12) SC 64; 2005(8) SCALE 80; [2005] Supp3 S.C.R. 901

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Equitable Doctrine

Summary: [A.] Land & Property - Bangalore Improvement Act, 1945, s. 3 - Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - Bangalore Development Authority Act, 1976, s. 65 - General Clauses Act, 1897, s. 21 - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 166, 26 - Transfer Of Property Act, 1882 - Power of Bangalore development authority to reconvey the land in favour of owner - Property once aquired could be reconveyed in favour of owner only in event possession thereof had not been taken - Once possession is taken, State cannot direct reconveyance of property - Notification for acquisition of land of respondent for formation of a scheme to set up a layout - Award made, compensation paid and possession of land was taken - Resolution passed by Bangalore board for reconvenyance of 8 acres and 21 guntas of land out of total land acquired - However resolution passed by authority to reconvey to 6 acres and 20 guntas of land was not given effect to - Writ petition - Since no provision in act or rules enables Authority to reconvey land - Hence no direction could be issued to Bangalore development authority for recoveyance of land - Principle of promissory estoppels held not applicable - Appeal allowed.
