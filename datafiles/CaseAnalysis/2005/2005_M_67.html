Srikantha S.M. v M/s. Bharath Earth Movers Limited

Supreme Court of India

07 October 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

C.K. Thakker, B.N. Srikrishna

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 684; (2005) 8 SCC 314; (2005) SCC (L&S) 1119; 2006 (1) ALD(SC) 98; 2005 (3) CLR 806; JT 2005 (12) SC 465; 2005(8) SCALE 407; [2005] Supp4 S.C.R. 156

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: [A.] Service - Bharath Earth Movers Limited Service Rules - Letter of resignation on 04-01-1993 - Appellant was informed that his casual leave had been sanctioned from 05-01-1993 to 13-01-1993 and he would be relieved by close of working hours on 15-01-1993 - Withdrawn resignation on 08-01-1993 - Not accepted by company - Appealed herein - Held, since letter of resignation was as per Company rules, it was to become effective after one month - Vinculum juris, continued and relationship of employer and employee did not come to an end on 04-01-1993 - It was, therefore, incumbent on Company to give effect to withdrawal of resignation - HC was in error in not granting relief to appellant - Directed to treat appellant in continuous service upto age of superannuation - Appeal allowed.
