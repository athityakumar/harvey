Jayendra Saraswathi Swamigal v State of Tamil Nadu

Supreme Court of India

10 January 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

G.P. Mathur, R.C. Lahoti, P.P. Naolekar

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 11; (2005) 2 SCC 13; (2005) SCC (Cr) 481; AIR 2005 SC 716; 2005 CRLJ 883; 2005 (116) DLT 354; 2005 (2) G.L.R. 1133; JT 2005 (1) SC 361; 2005 (1) KLT 471; 2005 (1) RCR(Criminal) 629; 2005(1) SCALE 114; [2005] 1 S.C.R. 160

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - CrPC, s. 439 - Bail - Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 10 and 30 - Whether bail can be granted to the petitioner? - Held, there should first be a prima facie evidence that the person was a party to conspiracy before his acts or statements can be used against his co-conspirators - No prima facie evidence apart from alleged confessions have been brought to show that petitioner along with A-2 and A-4 was party to a conspiracy - Prima facie a strong case has been made out for grant of bail to petitioner - Bail granted - Appeal allowed.
