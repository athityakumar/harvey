Iridium India Telecom Limited v Motorola Inc

Supreme Court of India

05 January 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Srikrishna, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 7; (2005) 2 SCC 145; AIR 2005 SC 514; 2005 (2) ALD(SC) 34; 2005 (2) AWC 1872; 2005 (3) Bom.C.R. 781; 2005 (2) CalLT(SC) 59; 2005 (118) DLT 216; JT 2005 (1) SC 50; 2005 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 211; 2005 (1) MahLJ 678; 2005 (2) MLJ(SC) 97; 2005 (140) PLR 280; 2005(1) SCALE 42; [2005] 1 S.C.R. 73

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Civil Procedure; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Civil Procedure - CPC, 1882, s. 682 - CPC, 1908, o. 8, r. 1, ss. 100, 116, 120, 122, 129, 148 - Punjab Courts Act, 1918, s. 41 - Amended proviso of o. 8, r. 1 - Whether amended provision can apply to suits on Original Side of High Court? - Whether such suits continue to be governed by HC Original Side Rules? - Held, non-obstante clause used in s. 129 is not merely declaratory, but indicative of Parliament's intention to prevent application of CPC in respect of civil proceedings on Original Side of HCs - s.97 of Amending Act does not, affect special hierarchial status given to proceedings before Chartered HCs on its Original Side - No fault found with impugned judgment of HC - Appeal dismissed.
