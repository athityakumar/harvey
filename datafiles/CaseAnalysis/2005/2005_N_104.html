State Farm Corporation of India Limited v Nijjer Agro Foods Limited and others

Supreme Court of India

21 July 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.K. Sabharwal, B.N. Srikrishna, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 1595; (2005) 12 SCC 502; AIR 2006 SC 679; 2007 (3) BC 513; 2006 (2) RLW 1512

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Challenged

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, s, 138 - Dishonour of Cheque - Challenged - Appellant filed complaint against respondents u/s. 138 of the Act - Magistrate issued summons to the respondents - Application was filed by respondents for dropping the proceedings, which was dismissed - Revision petition before HC - HC allowed the petition and reversed the order of Magistrate - Hence instant appeals.

Held, according to the respondents, they made payment of Rs 40 lakhs by six bank drafts after the issue of some of the cheques. Whether the said payment has been made or it is towards some of the amounts covered by the cheques are all the questions which can be decided only at the trial of the complaint cases u/s. 138 of the Act and could not have been made the basis of allowing the revision petition. The approach of the HC is clearly erroneous. Thus, impugned judgment of the HC is set aside and restores the complaint cases to be tried on merits in accordance with law. Appeals allowed.

Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881
