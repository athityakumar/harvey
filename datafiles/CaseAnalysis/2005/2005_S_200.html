Commissioner of Central Excise, Chandigarh v Shivalik Agro Poly Products Limited

Supreme Court of India

19 April 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

AR. Lakshmanan, S.H. Kapadia, S.N. Variava

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 841; (2005) 11 SCC 464; 2005 (184) E.L.T. 124; 2005 (68) RLT 719

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Ad Valorem

Summary: Excise - Indirect Tax - Manufacturer - Lay flat tubes - sold as final product - Also input for protective cover/tarpaulins-cases where lay flat tubing used as input for protective cover/tarpaulins, different excise procedures for sales of protective covers/tarpaulins to two different parties - Show cause notices - Whether the respondent entitled to benefit of notification No. 217/86-CE, D/- 02-04-1986? - Held, the mere fact that respondent did not show lay flat tubes as input or that choose to take Modvat credit of duty paid in respect of some material does not dis-entitle them to claim benefit of the notification, no infirmity in the judgment of the tribunal - The respondent entitled to benefit of notification No. 217/86-CE, D/- 02-04-1986 - Appeals accordingly stand dismissed
