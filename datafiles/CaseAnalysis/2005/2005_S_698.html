Kuldeep Singh and another v State of Tamil Nadu and others

Supreme Court of India

31 March 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 289; (2005) 11 SCC 122; AIR 2005 SC 2106; 2005 (60) ALR 769; 2005 (2) AWC 1757; JT 2005 (11) SC 80; 2005 (2) KLT 505; 2005 (3) MLJ(SC) 80; 2005 (2) RCR(Civil) 417; 2005(3) SCALE 503; [2005] 3 S.C.R. 67

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Health & Drug

Summary: [A.] Health & Drug - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 252, art. 32 - Transplantation Of Human Organs Act, 1994 - Transplantation of Human Organs Rules, 1995 Transplantation of Human Organs Act, 1994; Transplantation of Human Organs Rules, 1995 - Kidney transplantation - Application for issuance of 'No Objection Certificate' - Petitioners belong to State of Punjab - Transplantation intended to be done in State of Tamil Nadu - Authorisation Committee of which state can issue such a certificate? - Held, Authorisation Committees of State to which donor and donee belong have to take exercise to find out whether approval is to be accorded - Such Committee shall be in a better position to ascertain true intent and purpose for authorisation to remove organ and whether any commercial element is involved or not - It was Authorisation Committee of State of Punjab which is required to examine claim of petitioners - Petition disposed of.
