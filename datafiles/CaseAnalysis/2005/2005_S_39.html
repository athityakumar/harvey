Sahakari Khand Udyog Mandal Limited v Commissioner of Central Excise and Custom

Supreme Court of India

09 March 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

C.K. Thakker, Ruma Pal, Arijit Pasayat

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 1242; (2005) 3 SCC 738; AIR 2005 SC 1897; 2005 (181) E.L.T. 328; JT 2005 (3) SC 296; 2005 (4) MLJ(SC) 13; 2005(3) SCALE 39; [2005] 2 S.C.R. 606

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Excise; Indirect Tax; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Quasi-Contract, Doctrine Of Unjust Enrichment, Uttar Pradesh Tax on Luxuries Act, 1995, Doctrine Of Restitution

Summary: Excise - Indirect Tax - Central Excise Act, 1944, s. 11B - Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 226, 299, 38, 39 - Indian Contract Act, 1872, s. 70 - Punjab Agricultural Produce Markets Act, 1961, s. 23A - Uttar Pradesh Tax on Luxuries Act, 1995 - Central Excise Rules, 1944 - Excise duty - Exemption - Validity - Appellants had claimed rebate of on basis of Notification No. 108/78 lodged with Range Forest Officer - Under said Notification, sugar factory was entitled to exemption from excise duty on excess production of sugar of corresponding period of preceding three years - Notification also provided that such exemption would be on sale of sugar as specified in columns (3) and (4) as levy sugar and free sale sugar - Assistant Collector held that u/s. 11B of the Act, Mandal could not claim said amount from Government - He in circumstances reduced claim but transferred amount to Consumer Welfare Fund set up by Government of India - Being aggrieved by said order passed by Assistant Collector, appellant preferred appeal but appeal was dismissed by Collector of Customs - Aggrieved appellant then approached CEGAT - CEGAT also dismissed appeal - Hence, instant appeal - Whether claim of appellants was time barred and orders passed by authorities and refusal to grant benefit was arbitrary, unreasonable or inequitable. - Held, appellant was not entitled to any relief - Therefore, could not have been invoked by authorities for denying benefit of exemption from payment of excise duty and in refusing to pay amount to which appellant was held entitled by diverting it to Consumers Welfare Fund set up by Govt. - Burden was on petitioner to prove that tax/duty paid by him was not passed on to customers or third party and that he was entitled to restitution - Therefore, irrespective of applicability of s. 11B of the Act, doctrine can be invoked to deny benefit to which a person was not otherwise entitled - Before claiming a relief of refund, it was necessary for petitioner/appellant to show that he had paid amount for which relief was sought he has not passed on burden on consumers and if such relief was not granted, he would suffer loss - In present case, not only no such case has been made out by appellant-Mandal, position was just contrary - All authorities below have expressly recorded finding that appellant-Mandal has recovered amount from consumers and as such excise duty was passed on to consumers/customers - In view of specific finding, in SC opinion, conclusion was inescapable that appellant-Mandal was not entitled to claim any amount - Allowing exemption or refund of amount would result in 'unjust enrichment' by appellant which could not be permitted - Therefore, even on that count, orders passed by authorities and refusal to grant benefit cannot be held arbitrary, unreasonable or inequitable - Appeals dismissed.
