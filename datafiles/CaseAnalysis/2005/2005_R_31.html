U.P. Madhyamik Shiksha Parishad and Others v Raj Kumar Agnihotri

Supreme Court of India

21 April 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

AR. Lakshmanan, Ashok Bhan

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 315; (2005) 11 SCC 465; AIR 2005 SC 2491; 2005 (3) AWC 2212; 2005 (3) ESC 323; 2005 (105) FLR 969; JT 2005 (4) SC 516; 2005 (3) LLN 43; [2005] 3 S.C.R. 770; 2005 (3) SLR 580; 2005 (4) Supreme 316; 2005 (2) UPLBEC 1413

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: [A.] Service - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 226, 309 - Births, Deaths And Marriages Registration Act, 1886 - Uttar Pradesh Recruitment Service (Determination of the Date of Birth) Rules, 1974, r. 2 - Uttar Pradesh Recruitment of Service (Determination of Date of Birth) (First Amendment) Rules, 1980 - Limitation - Respondent's claim for change in date of birth from 30/07/1941 to 16/10/1945 - Whether time barred?; - Evidence was not of such a nature as to lead to conclusive proof of date of birth of respondent - r.2 stipulate that no application or representation shall be entertained for correcting any date or age record and entry made in service book shall be deemed to be correct date of birth? - Held, any application for correction is to be made within two years of issuing of certificate - Respondent has not moved any application within two years of this certificate being issued through concerned Principal - Respondent's suit is liable to be dismissed - Appeal allowed.
