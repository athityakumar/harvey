Vishnu Dutt and Others v State of Rajasthan and Others

Supreme Court of India

15 December 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

C.K. Thakker, Ashok Bhan

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 810; (2005) 13 SCC 592; 2006 (1) ACC 18; 2006 (1) AWC 417; JT 2005 (10) SC 584; 2005(10) SCALE 222; 2006 (1) SCJ 666; [2005] Supp5 S.C.R. 773

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Civil Procedure; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, s. 88 - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 136, 226 - CPC, 1908, s. 151, o. 31, r. 5 - Bombay Police Act, 1951 - Difference between 'Stay' and 'Injunction' Effect of order of Road Transport Authority (RTA) - HC was right and justified in ignoring directions issued by STAT and directing grant of permits by RTA in favour of appellants - Division Bench was right in observing that even if it is held that RTA was not aware of interim order passed by the single Judge and hence it could consider the applications submitted by the appellants and other applicants, when the petitions were allowed and the order of STAT remitting the matter to RTA for reconsideration was quashed and set aside, the action taken by RTA had no effect in the eye of law - Appeal dismissed.
