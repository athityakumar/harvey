Bank of India v Abhay D. Narottam and others

Supreme Court of India

03 May 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Ruma Pal, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 1464; (2005) 11 SCC 520

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance

Summary: Banking & Finance - (Trial of Offences Relating to Transactions in Securities) Act, 1992 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, ss. 58(a), 58(b), 100 - Loan - Defaults committed - Suit for recovery - Maintainability - Defaults were committed by respondent No. 2 in repayment of moneys under both the accounts - Two separate suits were filed by appellant/Bank for recovery of the amounts from respondent No. 2 - Respondent No. 1 was declared a notified party under the Special Court under the Act - Respondent No. 1 filed suit before the Special Court for recovery of an amount of approximately Rs 23 crores from respondent No. 2 - An interim application moved by respondent No. 1, a Court Receiver was appointed over the assets of respondent No. 2, including the flat and the land - Appellant sought to intervene in the proceedings before the Special Court and the same for intervention was rejected - Notice of motion taken out by respondent No. 1 was ultimately disposed of by appointing a Court Receiver as prayed for therein - Hence instant appeal - Whether appellant Bank had a prior charge in respect of properties claimed to belong to respondent No. 2 Company - Held, it was not necessary to determine the import of s. 125 of the Act as SC was of the opinion that the appeal should be dismissed on a much more basic ground - 'Mortgage' was defined in s. 58(a) of the 1882 Act as a transfer of an interest in specific immovable property for the purpose of securing the payment of money advanced or to be advanced by way of loan, etc. - Without a transfer of interest there was no question of there being a mortgage - Same principle would apply to a charge u/s. 100 of the 1882 Act - S. 100 of the 1882 Act provide that all the provisions which apply to a simple mortgage shall, so far as may be, apply to such charge - Definition of simple mortgage in s. 58(b) of the 1882 Act merely speaks of the procedure and describes that species of mortgage - As far as the land was concerned, a mere undertaking to create a mortgage was not sufficient to create any interest in any immovable property - As far as the flat was concerned, it needs no authority to say that a contract for sale of immovable property does not of itself create any interest in or charge over such property - In instant case, the agreement for sale which was deposited by respondent No. 2 with appellant was not an agreement by which respondent No. 2 agreed to sell the property to a third party, but an agreement to sell the flat to respondent No. 2 - No interest was created in favour of respondent No. 2 by virtue of agreement for sale which could have been transferred by way of security to appellant - There was as such no question of the appellant Bank having any charge over such non existent interest - Appeals dismissed.
