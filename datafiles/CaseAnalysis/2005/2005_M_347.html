Municipal Committee, Sirsa v Munshi Ram

Supreme Court of India

04 February 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 70; (2005) SCC (L&S) 277; (2005) 2 SCC 382; AIR 2005 SC 792; 2005 (1) AWC 793; 2005 (21) CLR 809; 2005 (105) FLR 66; JT 2005 (2) SC 117; 2005 (1) LLN 1091; 2005(1) SCALE 750; 2005 (2) SLR 288; 2005 (1) UPLBEC 934

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Service - Whether termination of respondent was punitive or is a discharge simplicitor? - Whether Labour Court justified in setting aside order of discharge made by appellant? - Held, mere fact that there was a misconduct on part of respondent which was not enquired into ipso facto does not lead to conclusion that order of termination is colourable and in fact is a punitive order - Respondent having been appointed as a probationer and his working having been found not to satisfaction of employer, it was open to management to terminate his services - Uphold order of discharge made by appellant - Appeal allowed.
