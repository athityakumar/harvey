Supreme Court of India

02 August 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.K. Sabharwal, D.M. Dharmadhikari, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 592; (2005) 6 SCC 344; AIR 2005 SC 3353; 2005 (5) ALD(SC) 1; 2005 (3) ARBLR 81; 2005 (3) AWC 2996; 2005 (6) Bom.C.R. 839; 2006 (2) G.L.R. 1312; JT 2005 (6) SC 486; 2005 (3) RCR(Civil) 530; 2005(6) SCALE 26; [2005] Supp1 S.C.R. 929

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Keywords: Family Courts (Amendment) Act, 1991

Summary: Civil Procedure - Legislation - CPC, 1908, o. 10, rr. 1A, 1B, 1C, o. 18, rr. 19, r. 4, o. 19 rr. 1, 2, o. 6 rr. 15, 17, ss. 115(1), 80(1), 89 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 154 - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 14, 226, 227 - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996 - Legal Services Authorities Act, 1987, s. 20(1) - Constitutional validity of amendments made to CPC by Amendment Acts of 1999 and 2002 - Challenge dismissed noticing that modalities have to be formulated for manner in which s.89 of Code and, for that matter, other provisions which have been introduced by way of amendments, may have to be operated - Committee was constituted so as to ensure that amendments become effective and result in quicker dispensation of justice - Report of the committee filed and is considered herein - Draft Civil Procedure - Alternative Dispute Resolution and Mediation Rules and Model Case Flow Management Rules framed - Registrar Generals, Central Govt. and State/Union Territories shall file progress report in regard to action taken within a period of four months - Petition disposed of.
