V. Subramani And Anr v State Of Tamil Nadu

Supreme Court of India

03 March 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 169; (2005) 10 SCC 358; (2005) SCC (Cr) 1521; AIR 2005 SC 1983; 2005 CRLJ 1727; JT 2005 (3) SC 82; 2005 (2) RCR(Criminal) 77; 2005(2) SCALE 546; [2005] 2 S.C.R. 536

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice and Procedure - IPC, 1860, s.302 - Appellants were sentenced to undergo rigorous imprisonment for life - Appeal against conviction - Whether there is exercise of right of private defence - Held, right of private defence is essentially a defensive right circumscribed by governing statute - Accused persons had not established that they were exercising right of private defence - It would be proper to alter conviction from s.302 to s.304 Part I - Appeal disposed of - Appeal disposed of.
