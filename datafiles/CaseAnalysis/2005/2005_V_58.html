M/s. Sait Nagjee Purushotham and Company Limited v Vimalabai Prabhulal and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 October 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Mathur, Arun Kumar

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 596; (2005) 8 SCC 252; AIR 2006 SC 770; JT 2005 (12) SC 225; 2005 (4) KLT 452; 2005(8) SCALE 235; [2005] Supp3 S.C.R. 973

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Keywords: Indian Companies Act, 1930, United Kingdom Companies Act

Summary: [A.] Rent Control - Corporate - Kerala Buildings (Lease and Rent Control) Act, 1965, ss. 11(17), 11(3), 11(4)(i), 11(4)(ii) - Indian Companies Act, 1930 - Eviction granted u/s. 11(3) but denied under ss.11 (4) (i), 11(4)(ii) - Appeal by tenants - Whether landlords who are owners of portion of building have substantiated allegation with regard to bona fide need or not? - Whether appellant is entitled to protection u/s. 11(17)? - Held, view taken by first appellate court as well as by High Court appears to be justified and there is no reason to take a contrary view of matter - Barring one exception, if there is voluntary transfer by the company to a newly incorporated company then in that case one has to plead and prove that all the members of the old firm continued in the new firm and it is essentially the same - Appellant-Tenant failed to substantiate that private limited company which was formed in 1948 carried same partners on Board of Directors as were there prior to 1948 - Benefit of s.11(17) cannot be extended to appellant - Appeal dismissed.
