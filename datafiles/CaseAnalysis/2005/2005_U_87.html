Union of India and another v Major Bahadur Singh

Supreme Court of India

22 November 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 828; (2006) 1 SCC 368; 2006 (5) AWC 4349; 2006 (1) ESC 4; JT 2005 (10) SC 127; 2005(9) SCALE 459; 2006 (1) SLR 66; [2005] Supp5 S.C.R. 385; 2006 (1) UPLBEC 459

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: Constitution of India-Articles 16 and 226-Employment-Promotion-Denial - Legality - Respondent petitioner not promoted as Lt. Colonel from rank of Major in Indian Army-Filing writ petition in High Court-High Court finding adverse elements in ACRs of respondent for 1988-90 and respondent ought to have been given performance counselling-High Court quashing ACRs for 1988-90-And remanding case to appellants for reconsideration -Whether High Court justified?-Held, “no” - According to instructions on subject, there are different stages-First is counselling, second is guidance and third is consequences of officer failing to show desired improvement-Only on failure of officer to show desired improvement adverse / advisory remarks included in ACR-High Court overlooking these aspects-Petitioner made grievance of non-communication of adverse remarks-But High Court quashed entries in ACRs for 1988-90-Order of High Court unsustainable-Matter remitted to High Court for decision afresh.
