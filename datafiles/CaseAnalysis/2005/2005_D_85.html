Panchanan Mishra v Digambar Mishra and Others

Supreme Court of India

17 January 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

AR. Lakshmanan, Ashok Bhan

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 21; (2005) 3 SCC 143; (2005) SCC (Cr) 660; AIR 2005 SC 1299; 2005 CRLJ 1721; 2005 (1) JhrCR(SC) 216; JT 2005 (2) SC 341; 2005 (1) KLT 673; 2005 (1) RCR(Criminal) 712; 2005(1) SCALE 338; [2005] 1 S.C.R. 484

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Defence & Security Forces; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Constitution - Criminal - Defence and Security Forces - Practice and Procedure - Bail - Cancellation of bail - Appeal against granting bail by HC - Whether appeal right in granting in bail? - Held, HC not considered material evidence available on record against accused which prima facie establish criminal liability of respondents - HC also failed to see that Sessions Judge, after taking into consideration, seriousness and gravity of crime convicted accused for crime - HC did not take into proper account grave apprehension of prosecution that there was a likelihood of accused persons tampering with prosecution witnesses - Order passed by HC in granting bail stands set aside - Appeals allowed.
