Commissioner of Central Excise, Hyderabad-Ii v Aldec Corporation

Supreme Court of India

29 September 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, B.P. Singh

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 760; (2005) 7 SCC 700; 2005 (188) E.L.T. 241; JT 2005 (8) SC 574; [2005] Supp3 S.C.R. 536

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax; Trade

Summary: [A.] Excise - Central Excise Act, 1944, ss. 2(f), 35L(b), 6 - Central Excise Tariff Act, 1985 - Central Excise Rules, 1944, r. 174 - Whether Commissioner was right in holding that fragmented activities of M/s Aldec Corporation, M/s Vitthaleshwara Painting Industries (VPI) and M/s Srinivasa Rolling and Engineering Works (SREW) taken individually or jointly resulted in 'manufacture' of a separate, independent and distinct identifiable product namely, Painted Aluminium Slat (PAS) for Venetian blinds classifiable under chapter sub-heading 76.6.90 ? - Commissioner has not discussed the difference between the old tariff items 27(6) and 68 vis-a-vis Chapter Heading 76.06 and sub-heading 7616.90 of the Tariff Act as also many other relevant aspects - Dept. has accepted the decisions of the Commissioner (Appeals) and Additional Collector passed in 1986 and 1987 holding that each of the above process do not constitute 'manufacture' - Respondent has acted on that basis for at least ten years - No intention to evade duty on the part of the respondent can be found - Appeal dismissed.
