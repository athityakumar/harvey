State of Karnataka v Laxuman

Supreme Court of India

25 October 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

P.K. Balasubramanyan, R.C. Lahoti, G.P. Mathur

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 962; (2005) 8 SCC 709; AIR 2006 SC 24; 2006 (62) ALR 368; JT 2005 (9) SC 89; 2006 (1) KarLJ 129; 2006 (1) MLJ(SC) 111; 2005 (4) RCR(Civil) 631; 2005(8) SCALE 768; 2005 (Supp) RD 295; 2006 (Supp) RD 166; [2005] Supp4 S.C.R. 535

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - Limitation Act, 1963 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Whether u/s. 18 of Land Acquisition Act, as in Karnataka, on expiry of said period of three years and 90 days, right of Deputy Commr. to make a reference and that of claimant to move court, get extinguished? - Held, just as party loses right to reference if no application is made within 90 days in terms of s. 18(2), party, who had made an application within 90 days loses right to secure a reference if he fails to make an application within three years after expiry of 90 days from date of reference application and consequently power of Deputy Commr./ Land Acquisition Officer to make reference comes to an end - Date of cessation of Deputy Commr. to make a reference also constitutes date of cessation of power of Deputy Commr. - Therefore, neither party can seek a reference nor Deputy Commr. can make reference after expiry of 3 years and 90 days from date of reference application - Appeals allowed.
