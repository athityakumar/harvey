Pushpraj Sachdeva v State of Uttar Pradesh and others

Supreme Court of India

07 November 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Agrawal, A.K. Mathur

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 1709; (2006) 9 SCC 758

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code,1860, ss.323,506,498-A,307,504 - Dowry Prohibition Act,1961, ss.3,4 - Order denying arrest - Payment of compensation - Justifiability - Accused persons moved HC in petition for quashing their prosecution u/ss.323,506,498-A,307,504 of IPC besides ss.3,4 of the Act - HC rejected plea for quashing prosecution but directed that accused would not be arrested till conclusion of trial, provided compensation paid to wife - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether order of HC not to arrest accused on payment of compensation is justifiability.

Held, no such direction was given by SC in case on which HC is relied for passing impugned order that upon payment of such compensation, accused shall not be arrested. Thus, HC was not justified in directing that accused persons shall not be apprehended till conclusion of trial as if similar direction was given by SC. Impugned order has to be set aside. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Unless specific direction is given by SC denying arrest of accused in lieu of payment of compensation, said direction cannot be passed by HC.
