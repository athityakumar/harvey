Board Of Control For Cricket, India And Another v Netaji Cricket Club And Others

Supreme Court of India

10 January 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, N. Santosh Hegde

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 1243; (2005) 4 SCC 741; AIR 2005 SC 592; 2005 (2) AWC 1965; JT 2005 (1) SC 235; 2005(1) SCALE 121; [2005] 1 S.C.R. 173; 2005 (1) UJ 334

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Election

Keywords: Election Dispute, Ex-Parte, Equality Of Votes, Appeals Disposed Of, Review Application, Casting Of Votes, Jurisdiction Of The High Court

Summary: Trusts and Association - Board of Control for Cricket India - Election to the post of President - Disqualification - 1st respondent filed suit before Single Judge to not disqualify them from contesting the election to the post of president of appellant Board on the basis of their residential status - Single Judge appointed Administrator to conduct the election - Appellant filed Letters Patent Appeal before DB - Counsel for appellant gave an undertaking before DB that they would not disqualify anyone from participating in the election - DB disposed off the appeal in terms of that undertaking - Maharashtra Cricket Association was not permitted to take part in election and voting, therefore, they sought the review of the DB order - DB reviewed its order by observing that the appellant had misled them by giving an undertaking which was not observed in its letter and spirit - DB ordered interim injunction against appellant from disqualifying any member of the Board and preventing them from voting - Whether HC has the jurisdiction in entertaining a review application - Held, application for review would be maintainable not only upon discovery of a new and important piece of evidence or when there exists an error apparent on the face of the record but also if the same is necessitated on account of some mistake or for any other sufficient reason - DB accepts its own mistake in understanding the nature and purport of the undertaking given by the appellant's counsel - Subsequent event may also be taken into consideration by DB for the purpose of rectifying its own mistake - Order is not wholly without jurisdiction so as to warrant interference of SC at the present stage - DB of HC had jurisdiction to admit the review application and examine the contention as to whether it can have a re-look over the matter - Appeals disposed of.
