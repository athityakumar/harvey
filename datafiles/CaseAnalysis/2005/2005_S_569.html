Ranjitsing Brahmajeetsing Sharma v State of Maharashtra and Another

Supreme Court of India

07 April 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, N. Santosh Hegde, B.P. Singh

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 272; (2005) 5 SCC 294; (2005) SCC (Cr) 1057; AIR 2005 SC 2277; 2005 CRLJ 2533; JT 2005 (4) SC 123; 2005(3) SCALE 774; [2005] 3 S.C.R. 345

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Maharashtra Control Of Organised Crime Act, 1999, s. 20, s. 21(4), s. 24, s. 3(2) - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 107, s. 108, s. 120-B, s. 146, s. 153A, s. 249, s. 255, s. 260, s. 263(a), s. 263(b), s. 34, s. 472, s. 474, s. 478 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 268 - Interpretation and application of Act - Applicant a police officer - Offence of help and support to member of an organised crime syndicate - Refusal of bail - Held, every little omission or commission, negligence or dereliction may not lead to a possibility of his having culpability in matter which is not sine qua non for attracting provisions of MCOCA - HC, considered matter from a wrong perspective - Only because Appellant had power, same would not by itself lead to a conclusion that he was a privy to crime - Interim bail granted should continue.
