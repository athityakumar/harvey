Commnr. Of Central Excise, Indore v M/S S. Kumars Ltd. & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

21 November 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Ruma Pal, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 1973; (2005) 13 SCC 266; JT 2005 (10) SC 104; 2005(9) SCALE 434; [2005] Supp5 S.C.R. 370

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Normal Price, Additional Duties of Excise (Amendment) Act, 1980

Summary: Excise - Indirect Tax - Central Excise Act, 1944 - Central Excise Valuation Rules, 1975, rr. 3, 4, 5 and 7 - Assessable value - Related persons - Assessee (Respondent no.1) processed grey fabric, sometimes by its own account and sometimes which it received on job-charge basis from merchant manufacturers - Assessee paid excise duty on the fabrics processed for the period 01.09.1985 to 28.02.1989, calculating the duty payable by treating the value of the processed fabrics as being that at which the merchant manufacturer was selling the processed goods - However, on the fabrics processed by assessee which had been received from the merchant manufacturers', the assessee valued the processed goods on the basis of the cost of grey fabrics plus the processing charges as well as its manufacturing expenses and profits - Department issued show cause notice to the assessee proposing to recover differential duty of excise alleging that assessee and merchant manufacturers were related persons as they were all firms and companies having a common management - Commissioner held that the assessee and merchant manufacturers were related persons and upheld the demand for duty to the extent of Rs 38,24,153 from assessee - Tribunal allowed the appeal filed by the assessee relying on the formula laid down by SC in Ujagar Prints and Others (III) vs. Union of India and Others 1989 INDLAW SC 909 - However, the Tribunal did not go into the question whether the assessee and the merchant manufacturers were related persons as alleged in the show cause notice - Hence, the instant appeal - Whether order of the tribunal could be upheld? - Held, formula laid down by SC in Ujagar Prints (III) would not apply to a processor who was not independent - As in Ujagar Prints (III) case, the processor is not a mere processor but also a merchant manufacturer who purchases/manufactures the raw material, processes it and sells it himself in the wholesale market - In such a situation, the profit was not of a processor but of a merchant manufacturer and a trader - If the transaction is between related persons, the profit would not be "normally earned" within the meaning of r. 6(b)(ii) of the Rules - If it was established that the dealings were with related persons of the manufacturer, the sale of the processed fabrics would not be limited to the formula prescribed by Ujagar Prints (III) - Hence, impugned order to be set aside - Appeals allowed.
