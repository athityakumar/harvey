State of Bihar and Others v Shree Baidyanath Ayurved Bhawan Private Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 January 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, S.N. Variava, AR. Lakshmanan

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 19; (2005) 2 SCC 762; AIR 2005 SC 932; 2005 (1) JhrCR(SC) 190; JT 2005 (1) SC 314; 2005(1) SCALE 320; [2005] 1 S.C.R. 334

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Excise; Health & Drug; Parliament & Legislature

Keywords: Pith And Substance, Competence Of The Legislature

Summary: Constitution - Health and Drug - Parliament and Legislature - Excise - Bihar Excise Act, 1915, s. 2(12a) - Legislative competence of State Act (as amended) to license and regulate "medicinal and toilet preparations" containing alcohol as alcoholic beverages- Validity of Bihar Excise Act, 1915, 2(12a)(iv) - Legislative competence of State Legislature in redefining word 'intoxicant'- Whether State Legislature entitled to regulate use and possession of substances which are capable of being used as alcoholic beverages under Bihar Act, 1915 as amended?; - Whether fees levied under impugned notifications violated art.301 of Constitution? - Held, so long as alcoholic preparation can be diverted to human consumption, States shall have power to legislate as also to impose taxes on such diversion - State as well as Board was competent to issue impugned notifications/ communications under ss 5, 19(4), 38, 39 and 90 of 1915 Act (as amended) to license and regulate use of such preparations as alcoholic beverages - Upheld validity of Bihar Excise Act, 1915 and validity of impugned notifications/communications - Appeals allowed.
