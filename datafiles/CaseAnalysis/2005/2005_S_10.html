Manager, R.B.I., Bangalore v S. Mani and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 March 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, N. Santosh Hegde, B.P. Singh

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 216; (2005) 5 SCC 100; (2005) SCC (L&S) 609; AIR 2005 SC 2179; 2005 (6) Bom.C.R. 455; 2005 (2) CLR 3; 2005 (105) FLR 1067; 2005 (3) JhrCR(SC) 110; JT 2005 (3) SC 248; 2005 (2) LLJ 258; 2005 (2) LLN 952; 2005 (3) MahLJ 758; 2005(3) SCALE 202; [2005] 2 S.C.R. 797; 2005 (3) SLR 333; 2005 (2) UPLBEC 1656

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Central Civil Services (Classification, Control and Appeal) Amendment Rules, 2008

Summary: Labour and Industrial Law - Service - Industrial Disputes Act, s. 25F - Ticca Mazdoors - Re-employment - Misconduct of producing false certificates - Respondents submitted fresh school transfer certificates and requested the Appellant herein to reemploy them - Request for reemployment was not accepted - Industrial Tribunal held that the Respondents having completed 240 days of service; and their terminations having been brought about without complying with the provisions of s.25F of ID Act and, thus being illegal were entitled to be reinstated in the Bank's services as per the prevailing rules and conditions of the service with full back wages - In appeal DB allowed the appeal in part modifying the award of the Tribunal as also the learned Single Judge to the effect that the back wages be paid from 23rd July, 1993 instead of their respective dates of retrenchment - Whether respondents entitled to reinstatement of service with full back wages? - Held, DB order by no stretch of imagination, can be said to have been passed with consent of parties - Plea of abandonment of service by respondents in facts and circumstances of case was wholly misconceived - Impugned judgments, cannot be sustained - Appeals allowed.
