Commissioner Hindu Religious and Charitable v P. Shanmugama and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 January 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Srikrishna, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 15; (2005) 9 SCC 232; AIR 2005 SC 770; 2005 (2) ALD(SC) 87; 2005 (58) ALR 658; 2005 (2) AWC 1869; 2005 (2) CalLT(SC) 25; 2005 (2) JhrCR(SC) 88; JT 2005 (1) SC 201; 2005 (98) RD 620; 2005(1) SCALE 154

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure; Trusts & Associations

Summary: Civil Procedure -Trusts and Associations - Hindu Religious and Charitable Endowment Act - Second appeal - Whether HC is right in entertaining second appeal? - Held, HC has proceeded as if it were deciding a first appeal against a decree in original proceedings - On this ground alone judgment is liable to be interfered with - It was not open to HC to reverse findings of facts as it has done - Findings recorded by first appellate court were justified and there was no scope for interference - Appeal allowed.
