M.B.Ramachandran v Gowramma & Ors

Supreme Court of India

28 July 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Singh, Arun Kumar

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 1301; (2005) 10 SCC 25

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property

Keywords: Mysore (Personal and Miscellaneous) Inams Abolition Act, 1954, Karnataka Inams Abolition (Amendment) Act, 1979

Summary: Land & Property - Administrative - Mysore (Religious and Charitable) Inams Abolition Act, 1955 - Karnataka Inams Abolition (Amendment) Act, 1979 - Adjudication of occupancy right - Jurisdiction of Land Tribunal - Appellants claimed occupancy rights in respect of lands in dispute - Land Tribunal granted occupancy rights to appellants - Respondents challenged Tribunal order before HC - Single Judge dismissed appeals, however, DB set aside Tribunal order and Single Judge order by remanding matters to Deputy Commissioner on ground that Tribunal had no jurisdiction to pass order granting occupancy right since 1979 Act was declared as ultra vires in an earlier HC judgment - Whether Land Tribunal had jurisdiction to dispose of appellant's application - Held, earlier HC judgment insofar as it declared 1979 Act void in its entirety was not correct - HC could have declared amendments brought about by s. 3 of Act to 1955 Act as ultra vires, since question of validity of amendments to 1955 Act was not in issue - Jurisdiction should be exercised by Land Tribunal including matters pending before Deputy Commissioner after the instant judgment, however, that would not prevent parties from challenging vires of 1979 Act insofar as it related to 1955 Act - Impugned HC order, therefore, was set aside and matter remanded back to HC for fresh disposal - Appeal allowed.
