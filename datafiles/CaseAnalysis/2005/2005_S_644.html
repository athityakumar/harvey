State of Punjab v Hakam Singh

Supreme Court of India

31 August 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Mathur, B.N. Agrawal

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 510; (2005) 7 SCC 408; (2005) SCC (Cr) 1679; 2005 CRLJ 4111; JT 2005 (8) SC 68; 2005(7) SCALE 43; 2005 (3) UC 1938

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Self Defence, Ballistics

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 307, 34, 449 - Appellant was convicted by Sessions Judge but High Court set aside conviction - Appeal against acquittal - Held, HC has committed an error in discarding testimony of P.W.3, wife of the deceased Harbans Singh on technical grounds de hors factual statement given by her - Conviction of appellant under ss. 302 and 307 r/w s.34 by trial court for causing death was correct and it should not have been reversed by HC - Testimony of PW-5, son of deceased Mohinder Singh is not convincing as he could witness whole incident only from long distance - Charge u/s. 302 r/w s. 34 can not be upheld for causing death of Mohinder Singh - Appeal partly allowed.
