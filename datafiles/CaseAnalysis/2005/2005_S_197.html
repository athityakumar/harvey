State of Punjab v Shiv Ram and Others

Supreme Court of India

25 August 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, C.K. Thakker, P.K. Balasubramanyan

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 495; (2005) 7 SCC 1; 2005 (3) ACC 717; AIR 2005 SC 3280; 2005 (5) ALD(SC) 85; 2006 (62) ALR 173; 2005 (4) AWC 3868E; 2006 (1) Bom.C.R. 338; 2006 (1) CCC 1; 2009 (2) CompLJ 398; 2005 (4) CPJ(Sc) 14; JT 2005 (7) SC 606; 2005 (4) KLT 669; 2005 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 161; 2005 (4) MLJ(SC) 132; 2005 (141) PLR 782; 2005 (4) RCR(Civil) 100; 2005 (4) RLW 2494; 2005(6) SCALE 770; [2005] Supp2 S.C.R. 991

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Tort

Keywords: Risk, Negligent

Summary: [A.] Tort - Civil Procedure - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 80 - Medical Termination Of Pregnancy Act, 1971, s. 3 - Recovery of damages - Female child born in spite of wife-respondent No 2 having undergone a tubectomy operation - Compensation granted - Appeal against - Whether State or Surgeon were liable to compensate for consequences of operation having failed? - Held, merely because a woman having undergone a sterilization operation became pregnant and delivered a child, operating surgeon or his employer cannot be held liable for compensation on account of unwanted pregnancy or unwanted child - Appeal allowed.
