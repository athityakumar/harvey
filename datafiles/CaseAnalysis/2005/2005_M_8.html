M. B. Ramachandran v Gowramma and others

Supreme Court of India

28 April 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Singh, Arun Kumar

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 2008; 2005(4) SCALE 546; [2005] 3 S.C.R. 964

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Karnataka Inams Abolition (Amendment) Act, 1979, Mysore (Personal and Miscellaneous ) Inams Abolition Act, 1954, Mysore (Religious and Charitable ) Inams Abolition Act, 1955

Summary: Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Karnataka Inams Abolition (Amendment) Act, 1979, s.3 - Mysore (Religious and Charitable ) Inams Abolition Act, 1955, s. 3 - Amendment act - Ultra vires - Validity - Jurisdiction - There were two Acts in State, viz; 1954 Act, (Act 1 of 1955) which related to abolition of personal 'Inams', and religious and charitable, 1955 Abolition Act., which related to abolition of religious and charitable Inams - 1979 Act was enacted by State Legislature whereby s. 2 amended some provisions of 1955 Act relating to personal Inams - S. 3 of 1979 Act amended some of provisions of Act 18 of 1955 which related to abolition of religious and charitable Inams - HC declared entire Amendment Act of 1979 as ultra vires - State preferred appeals before Court and were dismissed by order of Court without expressing any opinion on validity of Amendment Act of 1979 - Civil Appeals were disposed of only considering compensation payable to 'S' and validity of Amendment Act was left open to be considered in appropriate case - Thus, Amendment Act of 1979 which was declared to be ultra vires by HC continued to be so since, Court did not set aside said declaration of HC - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether entire Amendment Act of 1979 was ultra vires was justifiable - Held, dispute before HC in petitions preferred by 'S', related to religious and charitable 'Inams' - Therefore, in that context, provisions of Amendment Act of 1979 related to amendment of Mysore Act 18 of 1955, related to abolition of religious and charitable 'Inams' were challenged - Yet, HC declared entire Amendment Act to be ultra vires which was wholly unnecessary - There was, really no justification for HC to quash entire Amendment Act - Thus, HC had exceeded its jurisdiction in quashed entire Amendment Act of 1979 - HC ought to have confined its declaration to amendment of Act 18 of 1955 which was amended by s. 3 of 1979 Act - HC's judgment in 'S' in so far as it declared 1979 Act, void in its entirety was not correct - At best, HC could have declared amendments brought about by s. 3 of 1979 Act to Mysore Act 18 of 1955 as ultra vires, since question of validity of amendments to Mysore Act 1 of 1955 was not in issue - Hence, impugned judgment and order in appeals was set aside - Appeal allowed
