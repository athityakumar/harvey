Gayatri Devi and Others v Shashi Pal Singh

Supreme Court of India

09 March 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Srikrishna, D.M. Dharmadhikari

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 189; (2005) 5 SCC 527; AIR 2005 SC 2342; 2005 (3) ALD(SC) 40; 2005 (2) AWC 1072; 2005 (2) CGLJ 308; 2005 (118) DLT 108; JT 2005 (3) SC 170; 2005 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 344; 2005 (2) RCR(Civil) 1; 2005 (1) RCR(Rent) 311; 2005(9) SCALE 36; 2005 (2) UC 954

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure; Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - Delhi Rent Control Act - Eviction - Revision petition against execution proceedings - Revision allowed by HC - Whether HC right in allowing petition? - Held, Revision Petition was entertained at a stage of an interlocutory proceedings - Revision petition before HC was wholly incompetent in view of amended provision of s.115 of CPC - An order interim in nature or which does not finally decide lis, cannot be challenged by way of a revision under s.115 - Impugned judgment of HC is quashed and set aside - Appeal allowed.
