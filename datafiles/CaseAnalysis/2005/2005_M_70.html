Karnataka State Industrial Investment and v M/s. Cavalet India Limited and others

Supreme Court of India

30 March 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.K. Sabharwal, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 250; (2005) 4 SCC 456; 2005 (59) ALR 609B; 2005 (2) BC 443; [2005] 124 Comp Cas 797; 2005 (4) CompLJ 513; 2005 (2) DRTC 1; JT 2005 (3) SC 570; 2005 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 590; 2005 (3) RCR(Civil) 235; 2005 (99) RD 233; 2005(3) SCALE 414; [2005] 2 S.C.R. 1183

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Constitution; Corporate; Trusts & Associations

Summary: Banking & Finance - State Financial Corporation Act, 1951, s. 29 - (A) Whether Karnataka State Industrial Investment and Development Corporation acted in a bona fide manner in sale of properties of borrower exercising its right u/s.29? - Held, on failure of borrower to comply with directions of Single Judge, action of KSIIDC to sell unit in favour of Vinpack was valid and legal - Impugned judgment directing KSIIDC to redo entire sale process cannot be sustained - (B) Maintainability - HC while exercising its jurisdiction under Article 226 of the Constitution does not sit as an appellate authority - Doctrine of fairness does not convert the writ courts into appellate authorities over administrative authorities - In commercial matters courts should not risk their judgments for the judgments of the bodies to which that task is assigned - (C) Principles for sale of public property - Public Auctions - Purpose is to secure the best price by inviting maximum public participation - Reasonableness is to be tested against the dominant consideration to secure the best price - Appeals allowed.
