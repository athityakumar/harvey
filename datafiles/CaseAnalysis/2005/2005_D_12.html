D.S.P. Chennai v K. Inbasagaran

Supreme Court of India

07 December 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Mathur, B.N. Agrawal

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 762; (2006) 1 SCC 420; AIR 2006 SC 552; 2006 CRLJ 314; 2006 CRLJ 319; 2006 (200) CTR 624; [2006] 282 ITR 435; 2006 (1) JCC 125; JT 2005 (10) SC 332; 2006 (44) MLJ 399; 2006 (1) RCR(Criminal) 107; 2005(10) SCALE 1; 2006 (2) SCJ 104

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Socio-Economic

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Prevention Of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 13(1)(e), 13(2) - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 132(4) - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947, s. 5(3) - Respondent was found guilty, convicted and sentenced by Special Judge - HC acquitted accused of charges - Appeal against acquittal - When accused provided satisfactorily explanation that all money belonged to his wife and she has owned it and Income-tax Department has assessed in her hand, then in that case, whether he could be charged under Prevention of Corruption Act? - Held, since crucial question in this case was of possession and premises in question was jointly shared by wife and husband and wife having accepted entire recovery at her hand, it will not be proper to hold husband guilty - There are no compelling circumstances to reverse order of acquittal - Appeal dismissed.
