Air India Employees Self Contributory Superannuation Pension Scheme v Kuriakose V. Cherian And Others

Supreme Court of India

03 October 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.K. Sabharwal, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 1261; (2005) 8 SCC 404; (2005) SCC (L&S) 1188; AIR 2006 SC 3716; 2005(8) SCALE 109; 2005 (6) SLR 13; [2005] Supp3 S.C.R. 867

Case Digest

Subject: Insurance; Service

Keywords: Superannuation Fund, Scheme Of Insurance, Annuities, Air India Employees Self-Contributory Superannuation Pension Scheme, Air India Employees Self-Contributing Pensionary Scheme Rules

Summary: Service - Insurance - Air India Employees Self-Contributing Pensionary Scheme Rules, r. 14 - Deficit in the Pension Scheme - Additional contribution by retirees - Requirement of - Appellant/Air India introduced a Pension Scheme for its employees wherein each permanent employees had to contribute for a minimum period of 15 years and for those who did not have sufficient number of years of service from the date of the commencement of the Scheme up to their superannuation, an amount was calculated based on the total number of years in deficit and the member was required to make payment of the entire sum so calculated either in lump sum or to pay the said amount in monthly instalment along with interest on the total sum due - Further, amount was to be calculated according to the Scheme on superannuation of an employee and annuity was required to be purchased from Life Insurance Corporation of India (LIC) so as to ensure payment by LIC of a fixed monthly sum to the retired employee and on his demise the payment of the annuity amount to his legal representatives - Existing employees questioned the Scheme as defective inasmuch as large amounts were given to the retiring employees without having regard to the contributions made by them towards the Scheme and resultantly the old employees by making smaller contributions received disproportionately larger amount of benefits - Appellant, subsequently, amended the Scheme and amendment required pensioners to make payment of additional contribution towards annuities purchased from LIC - Further, amendment stipulated that the employees who had retired up to 31-10-2001 should contribute the deficit amount so as to make up the difference between cost of annuity purchased for them from the pension fund from LIC and the total contribution made by them till date of retirement - Respondent/retired employees challenged the amendment before the HC on the ground that rights in their favour crystallized on purchase of annuities at the time of their superannuation and the same cannot be subjected to any alteration or amendment - Respondents further contended that the trustees of the Scheme could only effect amendment to the Scheme for future benefits of existing employees and had no right to effect any amendment which adversely affects vested rights of the pensioners in regard to the pension payable to them as per the amended Scheme - HC by the impugned judgment held that the impugned amendment of the Scheme to the extent it applies in future was legal and valid but the amendment could not apply to the employees who had retired before the date of amendment and such employees should continue to receive pensionary benefits as before, namely, the benefits which existed at the time of amendment - Hence the present appeal by the trustees of Pension Scheme and existing employees - Whether there was any requirement to make the impugned amendments in view of depletion of the fund amount which warranted additional contribution by retired employees for making good the deficit in the fund - Held, existence of several contributory factors resulted in the fund position became quite bad - Factors included the non-receipt of huge funds in time from Air India, lack of proper investment by the trust resulting in loss of interest in addition to there being a gap between the contribution and the amount required for purchase of annuity to ensure return of specified amount to the retirees - Said factors contributed most in the depleted financial position of the fund requiring the trustees to make the impugned amendments in the scheme, but the original scheme was a 'Benefit Defined Scheme' as opposed to a 'Contribution Defined Scheme' - Further, appellants admitted that there was no fraud in formulation or implementation of the scheme - Retirees received what was receivable by them according to the existing scheme on the date of retirement - Pension Scheme, as originally conceived and formulated, was a rolling scheme postulating outgoing employees on retirement and their place being taken by induction of new employees whose contributions would add to the fund - However, retirees, as a result of amendment, were being asked to pay to make good the gap between the amount of annuity and the contributions made by them and, if not, either their monthly pension would be reduced or their heirs would not get the annuity amount at the relevant stage - Amounts already taken by the retirees had also been taken into consideration while working out the figures, therefore, it cannot be said that the amendment was not retrospective - Liability of the retiring member to make any further contribution ceases on retirement - It is nobody's case that after the retirement any contribution is made or required to be made by retired employees - Retired employees are not claiming any interest in the master policy of the Scheme but are claiming right flowing from the annuity purchased on their retirement - Rights of the employees to receive the annuity and quantum of the annuity get crystallized at the time of purchase of the annuity - After retirement retirees are not liable for any deficit in the fund which is sought to be made good by recovery from them which is the effect of retrospective amendment - Further, Scheme in question was a benefit and rolling scheme as opposed to a contributory scheme - None of the clauses of Trust Deed nor r. 14 has any applicability on question of retrospective operation of amendment to the retired employees - Appellants are bound by the consequences flowing from purchase of annuity and LIC having accepted the annuity and having effected monthly payments can neither reduce the annuity amount nor refund it to the trust to the detriment of the retirees since the annuity has already crystallized and no change can be made in such annuity as stipulated by the impugned amendments - Therefore, impugned amendment seeking additional contribution by the retirees for making good the deficit in the fund would not be sustained and HC was rightly rejected such contention - Impugned HC order is confirmed - Appeal dismissed.
