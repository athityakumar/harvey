Harshad Chiman Lal Modi v Dlf Universal and Another

Supreme Court of India

26 September 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

C.K. Thakker, Arijit Pasayat

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 582; (2005) 7 SCC 791; AIR 2005 SC 4446; 2005 (6) ALD(SC) 1; 2005 (61) ALR 647; 2006 (3) JhrCR(SC) 222; JT 2005 (8) SC 561; 2005 (4) RCR(Civil) 260; 2006 (100) RD 27; 2005 (4) RLW 2459; 2005(7) SCALE 533; [2005] Supp3 S.C.R. 495

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure; Contract & Commercial

Summary: [A.] Contract & Commercial - Civil Procedure - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908, o. 21 rr. 32, 34, 35, o. 6, r. 17, ss. 15, 16, 20 - Indian Contract Act, 1872, s. 28 - Suit for declaration, specific performance of agreement, for possession of property and for permanent injunction - Order that Delhi Court has no jurisdiction to try suit and plaint should be returned to plaintiff for presentation to proper court - Appealed herein - In which court a suit for specific performance of agreement relating to immovable property would lie? - Held, s.16 recognizes a well established principle that actions against res or property should be brought in forum where such res is situate - A court within whose territorial jurisdiction property is not situate has no power to deal with and decide rights or interests in such property - Delhi Court has no jurisdiction since property is not situate within jurisdiction of that court - Trial court was, therefore, right in passing an order returning plaint to plaintiff for presentation to proper court - Appeal dismissed.
