Secretary, O.N.G.C., Limited and Another v V.U. Warrier

Supreme Court of India

20 April 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

C.K. Thakker, Ruma Pal

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 1040; (2005) 5 SCC 245; (2005) SCC (L&S) 676; AIR 2005 SC 3039; 2005 (5) AWC 4913; 2005 (5) Bom.C.R. 251; 2005 (106) FLR 63; JT 2005 (4) SC 489; 2005 (3) LLN 74; 2005 (2) MahLJ 985; 2005(4) SCALE 251; [2005] 3 S.C.R. 696

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Air Force Act, 1950, Navy Act, 1957, Meritorious, Oil and Natural Gas Commission Act, 1959, Oil and Natural Gas Commission (Transfer of Undertaking and Repeal) Act, 1993

Summary: Service - Oil and Natural Gas Commission Act, 1959, s. 32 - Public Premises (Eviction of Unauthorised Occupants) Act, 1971 - Payment of Gratuity Act, 1972 - (A) Whether High Court was clearly wrong in allowing petition and in directing Commission to pay amount of gratuity to respondent with interest at rate of 6% per annum? - Held, yes - HC was wholly wrong in granting relief to respondent and not reviewing order passed ex parte observing that no 'sufficient cause' had been made out for condonation of delay - Appeal deserves to be allowed by quashing and setting aside order of HC and by upholding order passed by commission - (B) Whether cl. (e) of s. 2 of Payment of Gratuity Act is not applicable to respondent? - Held, definition of 'employee' was no doubt amended subsequently and provision as to amount of wages had been deleted - But amendment was made in 1994 and was not retrospective in nature and thus was not applicable in case of respondent who retired in 1990 - Respondent continued to occupy quarter ignoring warning by commission that if he would not vacate latest by June 30, 1990, penal rent would be charged from him - Appeal allowed.
