Smt. Ram Sakhi Devi v Chhatra Devi and others

Supreme Court of India

12 July 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 412; (2005) 6 SCC 181; 2005 (60) ALR 742; 2005 (3) AWC 2497; 2006 (1) Bom.C.R. 466; 2005 (197) CTR 602; 2005 (3) JhrCR(SC) 201; JT 2005 (6) SC 167; 2005 (4) MahLJ 289; 2005 (4) MPLJ 48; 2006 (1) RCR(Civil) 436B; 2005 (99) RD 399; 2005(5) SCALE 430; [2005] Supp1 S.C.R. 544

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Civil Procedure - CPC, 1908, ss. 100, 100(5) - HC interfered with judgment and decree of First Appellate Court without framing a substantial question of law - Whether HC right in interfering? - Held, HC can only exercise its jurisdiction u/s.100 in Second Appeal on basis of substantial question of law framed at time of admitting appeal - Remitted matter to HC for disposal in accordance with law - Appeal disposed of.
