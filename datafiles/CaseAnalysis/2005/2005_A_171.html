R.M. Yellatti v Assistant Executive Engineer

Supreme Court of India

07 November 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, S.N. Variava, AR. Lakshmanan

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 712; (2006) 1 SCC 106; AIR 2006 SC 355; 2005 (6) AWC 5900; 2006 (2) JhrCR(SC) 5; JT 2005 (9) SC 340; 2006 (2) KarLJ 447; 2005(9) SCALE 139; 2005 (6) SLR 738; [2005] Supp4 S.C.R. 1010; 2006 (1) UPLBEC 213

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Madhya Pradesh Industrial Relations Act

Summary: [A.] Service - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 10(I)(c), 2(j), 2(oo), 2(s), 25-B, 25-F - Indian Evidence Act, 1872 - Appellant was appointed as a daily wage earner by Asst. Executive Engineer - Terminated - Reinstatement with 50% back wages from date of award till date of reinstatement awarded by Labour Court - HC set aside award - Burden of proof as to completion of 240 days of continuous work in a year - Appealed herein - Held, this is not a case where allegations of workman are founded merely on an affidavit - He has produced cogent evidence in support of his case - Management was duty bound to produce before labour court nominal muster rolls for relevant period, particularly when it was summoned to do so - Division Bench of HC had erred in interfering with concurrent findings of fact - Appeal allowed.
