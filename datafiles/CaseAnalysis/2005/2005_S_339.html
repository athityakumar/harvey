State of Andhra Pradesh v Kanda Gopaludu

Supreme Court of India

27 September 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

H.K. Sema, P.P. Naolekar

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 1021; (2005) 13 SCC 116; (2006) 2 SCC (Cr) 121; AIR 2005 SC 3616; JT 2006 (2) SC 412; 2005(7) SCALE 677; 2006 (2) SCJ 467; [2005] Supp3 S.C.R. 643

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 24, 25 and 26 - (A) Whether High Court was right in recording acquittal on ground that P W 1 and P W 2 before whom accused made extra-judicial confession are strangers? - Held, it is now well established principle of law that judicial decision is based on reasons - Extra judicial confession is admissible if it inspired confidence and made voluntarily - Reasoning that accused has made a confession statement before a stranger is totally perverse - Relied upon Gum Singh v. State of Rajasthan 2000 Indlaw SC 2738 - (B) Whether in facts and circumstances HC has committed a grave error in law as well as in facts in recording acquittal of accused? - Further held, HC has committed a grave error in law as well as in facts in recording acquittal - Circumstances established in case complete chain of circumstances to prove charge of murder - Appeal allowed.
