Godfrey Phillips India Limited and another v State of Uttar Pradesh and others

Supreme Court of India

20 January 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Ruma Pal, Arun Kumar, G.P. Mathur, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 66; (2005) 2 SCC 515; AIR 2005 SC 1103; 2005 (194) CTR 257; JT 2005 (5) SC 587; 2005(1) SCALE 465; [2005] 1 S.C.R. 732; 2005 (139) STC 537

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax

Keywords: Constitution (Seventeenth Amendment) Act, 1964, Expenditure Tax Act, 1987, Tobacco Board Act, 1975, Andhra Pradesh Tax on Luxuries Act, 1987, Uttar Pradesh Tax on Luxuries Act, 1995, Uttar Pradesh Tax on Luxuries Act, 1955, West Bengal Tax on Luxuries Act, 1994, Uttar Pradesh Tax on Luxuries Act 1995, Uttar Pradesh Tax on Luxuries Act 1994, Karnataka Cinemas (Regulations) Act, 1964

Summary: [A.] Indirect Tax - Uttar Pradesh Tax on Luxuries Act, 1955 - Andhra Pradesh Tax on Luxuries Act, 1987 - West Bengal Tax on Luxuries Act, 1994 - Uttar Pradesh Tax on Luxuries Act 1995 - Uttar Pradesh Tax on Luxuries Act 1994 - Imposition of luxury tax on tobacco and tobacco products treating them as 'luxuries' challenged - Luxury tax - Tobacco and tobacco products - Ambit of Entry 62 of List-II - Held, Entry 62 of List II does not permit levy of tax on goods or articles - As none of impugned statutes seek to tax any activity and seek to tax goods described as luxury goods, they are legislatively incompetent - Following Somaiya Organics (India) Ltd vs State of U P (2001) 5 SCC 519 while striking down impugned Acts it is not appropriate to allow any refund of taxes already paid under impugned Acts.
