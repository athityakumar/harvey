Commissioner of Central Excise, Trichy v M/s. Grasim Industries Limited

Supreme Court of India

12 April 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, AR. Lakshmanan, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 274; (2005) 4 SCC 194; 2005 (183) E.L.T. 123; JT 2005 (4) SC 270; 2005(4) SCALE 85; [2005] 3 S.C.R. 466

Case Digest

Subject: Excise

Keywords: Tariff Item, Medicinal and Toilet Preparations (Excise Duties) Act, 1955

Summary: [A.] Excise - Central Excise Act, 1944, s. 5A(1) - Central Excise Tariff Act, 1985 - Trade And Merchandise Marks Act, 1958 - Medicinal And Toilet Preparations (Excise Duties) Act, 1955, s. 2 - Central Excise Rules, 1944, r. 173Q - Benefit of Notification No 5/98 CE - Whether use of name of a company amount to using a brand name or trade name of some other company?; whether respondent entitled to benefit of notification? - Held, any name or mark or writing, even name of a company is sufficient so long as it is used for purpose of indicating a connection between product and that Company - Respondent clearly have intention of indicating a connection between product i e cement and M/s Grasim Industries Ltd - Respondents were using a trade name of some other company with purpose of indicating a connection in course of trade between product and that person - Respondents were therefore clearly not entitled to benefit of Notification - Appeals disposed of.
