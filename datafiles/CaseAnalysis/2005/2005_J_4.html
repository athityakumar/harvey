Government of Andhra Pradesh and Another v J.B. Educational Society and Another, Etc

Supreme Court of India

23 February 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

K.G. Balakrishnan, B.N. Srikrishna

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 123; (2005) 3 SCC 212; AIR 2005 SC 2014; 2005 (2) ALD(SC) 69; 2005 (2) ESC 184; JT 2005 (2) SC 521; 2005 (2) KLT 154; 2005(2) SCALE 337; [2005] 2 S.C.R. 302; 2005 (2) SLR 823; 2005 (2) Supreme 210; 2005 (2) UPLBEC 1647

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Education; Parliament & Legislature

Summary: Constitution - Education - Andhra Pradesh Education Act, 1982, s. 20(3)(a)(i) - All India Council of Technical Education Act, 1987, s. 10 - Private educational institutions - Permission to establish institution - Whether s. 20(3)(a)(i) of A.P. Act is void and inoperative in view of All India Council of Technical Education Act, 1987?; whether State Govt.had legislative competence to pass such a legislation? - Held, State has certainly legislative competence to pass legislation in respect of education including technical education and s. 20 of Act is intended for general welfare of citizens of State and also in discharge of constitutional duty enumerated under art. 41 of Constitution - s. 20(3)(a)(i) is not in any way repugnant to s. 10 of AICTE Act and it is constitutionally valid - Appeals allowed.
