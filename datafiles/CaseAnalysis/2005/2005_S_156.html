Shanti Prasad Devi and Another v Shankar Mahto and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 July 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

D.M. Dharmadhikari, B.N. Srikrishna

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 401; (2005) 5 SCC 543; AIR 2005 SC 2905; 2005 (4) ALD(SC) 116; 2005 (60) ALR 744; 2005 (3) AWC 2537; JT 2005 (6) SC 6; 2005 (3) RCR(Civil) 402; 2005(5) SCALE 337; [2005] Supp1 S.C.R. 418

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Rent Control - Transfer Of Property Act, 1882, ss. 106, 116 - Lease deed - Renewal of lease deed - Suit for ejectment of lessee and counter suit seeking specific performance of alleged agreement of renewal of lease - Whether on expiry of original period of lease, acceptance of rent shows an 'assent' on part of lessor to continue lease? - Whether courts below erred in holding that lease, which stood renewed as an effect of 'holding over' u/s. 116 was not required to be terminated by statutory notice u/s. 106 as a pre-condition for filing a suit for ejectment? - Held, there is a renewal clause in contract prescribing a particular period and mode of renewal which was 'an agreement to contrary' within meaning of s.116 - Option of renewal was exercised not in accordance with terms of renewal clause that is before expiry of lease - Mere acceptance of rent for subsequent months in which lessee continued to occupy lease premises cannot be said to be a conduct signifying 'assent' to continuance of lessee - Appeals dismissed.
