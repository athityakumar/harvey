Ashok Lanka & Anr v Rishi Dixit & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

11 May 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, N. Santosh Hegde

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 1832; (2005) 5 SCC 598; JT 2005 (5) SC 395; 2005(5) SCALE 202; [2005] Supp1 S.C.R. 299

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Excise

Keywords: Code of Civil Procedure (Amendment) Act, 2002, Bihar Legislative Council Members (Disqualification on ground of Defection) Rules, 1994, Chhattisgarh Excise Settlement of Licences for Retail Sale of Country/Foreign Liquor Rules, 2002

Summary: (A) Excise - Administrative - Chhattisgarh Excise Act, 1915 - Chhattisgarh Excise Settlement of Licences for Retail Sale of Country/Foreign Liquor Rules, 2002. rr. 7, 9 - Grant of excise license - Dilution of eligibility criteria by Commissioner of Excise - Permissibility - Notice inviting applications for grant of license under the Act and the Rules were issued - Appellant filed Public Interest Litigation challenging the selection process, namely, from manual to computer - Appellants further contended that selection process adopted by the State was vitiated, inter alia, on the premise that no affidavit was filed by the applicants for license as was mandatorily required by r. 9 of the Rules - HC disposed off writ petitions holding that: (i) circular letter dated 14-2-2005 issued by the Commissioner of Excise was contrary to the Rules insofar as eligibility criteria laid down in r. 9 thereof were dispensed with; (ii) applications filed by the applicants were not properly scrutinized, except the requirement of r. 9(c), namely, whether the applicants were blacklisted or otherwise not eligible; (c) While holding that the application fees to the extent of Rs 77 crores earned by the State need not be refunded, it directed scrutiny of about 2.65 lakh applications by the respective district-level committees for their satisfaction that all eligibility requirements stand satisfied whereafter only the draw of lottery may take place - Whether the Commissioner of Excise had the jurisdiction to dispense with the eligibility criteria laid down by r. 9 of the Rules through the issuance of circular dated 14-2-2005 - Held, Commissioner of Excise issued a circular letter dated 14-2-2005 which power evidently he did not possess in terms of s. 7 of the Act - Although the State may delegate its power to the Commissioner of Excise, such a delegation should not be made in relation to the matters contained in the rule-making power of the State - Matters which were, therefore, outside the purview of the Rules only could be the subject-matter of delegation in favour of the Commissioner of Excise - Commissioner of Excise was a statutory authority and was bound to exercise his power only within the four corners of the Act or the Rules framed thereunder and not dehors the same - By reason of the amendment carried out in the Rules in terms of the notification dated 9-3-2004, the Commissioner of Excise was empowered to prescribe a format of the application form and affidavit - Such an application or affidavit could be filed within the date and time stipulated by him but the same would not mean that while prescribing a format in respect of an application form or affidavit, he became authorized to dilute the statutory requirements or dispense with the same - Appeal disposed of.

(B) Excise - Administrative - Chhattisgarh Excise Act, 1915 - Chhattisgarh Excise Settlement of Licences for Retail Sale of Country/Foreign Liquor Rules, 2002. r. 11 - Selection of licensee - Non-compliance of rules - Legality - Whether the selection of eligible candidates for the grant of license was vitiated due to non-observance of statutory rules - Held, cl. (b) of r. 11 presupposed that before a licence was granted the requirements contemplated therein should be complied with - A candidate before selection should be found to be eligible therefore - R. 11 postulated that before the actual licence was granted, the conditions precedent as contained therein were required to be fulfilled, failing which the Committee should take steps for resettlement of shops or group of shops - Further, in terms of r. 11, an affidavit was required to be filed by the applicant which was fortified by the fact that r. 23(1)(c) contemplated that if the affidavit submitted by the licensees at the time of application was found incorrect and assertions made therein were found to be false, the licensing authority would be empowered to suspend or cancel the license - Further, very fact that a circular was issued by the Commissioner of Excise asking the applicants to file an affidavit after the selection process was over itself was a pointer to the fact that filing of such an affidavit along with the application was considered by all concerned to be necessary - Advertisement was also required to be issued in consonance with the Rules and not in derogation thereof - Further, filing of an affidavit in the prescribed format was a statutory requirement under the Rules - Filing of such an affidavit was necessary as in the event the same on verification was found to be incorrect, not only the deponent could be proceeded against but his licence would also be liable to be cancelled - Filing of an affidavit under the Rules was, therefore, mandatory in character - In the instant case, selected candidates had not been asked to submit affidavits in the prescribed format - It was not expected of the statutory functionaries to ask the selected candidates to comply with the requirements orally - Different persons belonging to different communities had filed different applications showing the same address and even persons having the same name had filed more than one application - Even if r. 9 was held to be directory, substantial compliance thereof was necessary - A mandatory statute required strict compliance whereas a directory statute required substantial compliance - Even if a statute was directory, the State should not say that the requirements contained therein did not envisage compliance thereof - Authorities of the State could not raise a plea that they would not even notice the inherent defects contained in the application - Authorities could not proceed on a presupposition, for which there was no legal sanction, that contents of the affidavit would be correct - Rules postulated that each and every application should be examined carefully - Mere fact that a large number of applications had been filed, as a result whereof the State had been able to obtain crores and crores of rupees by itself did not entitle the State to dispense with the statutory requirements - Application fees were not meant to be utilized for the purpose of earning revenue but to meet the administrative charges required thereof - Hence, direction issued to the Member-Secretary to scrutinize all the applications of successful candidates afresh and prepare a summary report - Appeal disposed of.
