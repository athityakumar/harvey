Ranvir Singh and Another v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

07 September 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Ashok Bhan

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 547; (2005) 12 SCC 59; AIR 2005 SC 3467; 2005 (6) ALD(SC) 63; 2005 (61) ALR 771; 2005 (4) AWC 3152; 2005 (123) DLT 252; JT 2005 (8) SC 253; 2005 (4) MLJ(SC) 147; 2005 (4) RCR(Civil) 277; 2005(7) SCALE 238; 2005 (Supp) RD 352; [2005] Supp3 S.C.R. 31

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, s. 18, s. 4(1), s. 51A - Indian Evidence Act, 1872 - Compensation - Enhanced by High Court - Appealed herein - Held, HC was required to consider deeds of sale in their proper perspective for determining market values of acquired land - Market value of fully developed land cannot be compared with wholly underdeveloped land although they may be adjoining or situated at a little distance - For determining market value nature of land plays an important role - Matters remitted to HC for consideration of matter afresh - Appeal disposed of.
