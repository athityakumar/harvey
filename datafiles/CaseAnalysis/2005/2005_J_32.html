Jaipur Municipal Corporation v C.L. Mishra

Supreme Court of India

27 October 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

G.P. Mathur, R.C. Lahoti, P.K. Balasubramanyan

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 700; (2005) 8 SCC 423; 2006 (1) AWC 89; 2006 (2) Bom.C.R. 91; 2006 (3) JhrCR(SC) 231; JT 2005 (9) SC 195; 2006 (1) RCR(Civil) 588; 2006 (1) RCR(Criminal) 774; 2005(8) SCALE 726

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Practice & Procedure - Rajasthan Public Trust Act - Contempt of Courts Act - Review petition for reviewing order wherein one month's time was given to comply with order in a Contempt Petition - Petition dismissed holding that since contempt proceedings were initiated against contemnors in their individual capacity and Municipal Corporation, Jaipur was not a party in contempt proceedings, petition by Municipal Corporation, Jaipur is not maintainable - Appealed herein - Held, cannot have any independent existence and cannot survive once contempt petition itself was withdrawn and was dismissed - As such there is no occasion for review of order - Appeal disposed of.
