Shyama Devi v Union of India and another

Supreme Court of India

04 February 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

D.M. Dharmadhikari, B.N. Srikrishna

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 1470; (2005) 12 SCC 217; 2006 (4) ACC 622

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Challenged, Rejected, Death compensation

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Workmen's Compensation Act, 1923, s. 4-A(3) - Death compensation - Rejected - Challenged - Appellant claimed compensation n the ground that her husband died during the course of his employment with respondent - Commissioner rejected appellants claim - Appeal before HC - The order of Labour Court was upheld and HC by impugned order dismissed the appeal - Hence instant appeal - Whether the order recorded by HC was sustainable.

Held, there was no justification for Labour Court for coming to the finding that the death of the deceased workman did not arise during the course of his employment. Merely because after his duty period, he had gone to his quarters and come out only after hearing a commotion to pacify the mob, it cannot be said that his death was not in the course of his employment. As the facts show, he was courageous enough to come out of his quarters even after his duty hours to save railway property. Thus, there is no ground to deny to his dependants compensation under the Act. As far as quantum of compensation is concerned, on the basis of his last wages and age, according to Schedule IV of the Act, a total sum of Rs 1,05,560/- was payable as compensation on the death of the deceased but since the claim was made for Rs 84,448/-, Court will restrict the award for the aforesaid sum as has been claimed in the claim petition. Apart from the above quantum of compensation, the appellant would be entitled to statutory interest payable on this sum. Accordingly, appellant is awarded compensation in the sum of Rs 84,448/- with statutory interest u/s. 4-A(3) of the Act. Appeal allowed.
