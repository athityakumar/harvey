Rajender Singh v Lt. Governor, Andaman & Nicobar Islands and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 October 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

AR. Lakshmanan, Ruma Pal

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 865; (2005) 13 SCC 289; (2006) SCC (L&S) 1057; AIR 2006 SC 75; JT 2005 (12) SC 109; 2006 (1) MLJ(SC) 60; 2005(8) SCALE 159; 2005 (6) SLR 1; [2005] Supp3 S.C.R. 1042

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Andaman and Nicobar

Summary: [A.] Civil Procedure - Power of Judicial Review - Application by appellant working as a lecturer for regularization of his service - Respondent authority directed to regularize services of petitioner - Appeal by appellant on ground of denial of award of senior scale and selection grades - Allowed by Tribunal - Appeal by respondent - Allowed by HC setting aside order of Tribunal - Review application rejected by HC - Grievance of appellant was that though several vital issues were raised and documents placed, HC did not consider same in its review jurisdiction - Validity - HC was not justified in ignoring materials on record which on proper consideration would have justified claim of appellant - Explanation offered by appellant as to why he could not place before Division Bench some of documents which were not in his possession at time of hearing of case HC, was not correct in overlooking documents relied on by appellant and respondents Review jurisdiction was available since impugned judgment was a clear case of an error apparent on face of record and non- Consideration of relevant documents - Courts should not hesitate to review its own earlier order when there exists an error on face of record and interest of justice so demands in appropriate cases -Impugned order of HC set aside for fresh consideration - Appeal allowed.
