 Pratap Singh v State of Jharkhand and Another

Supreme Court of India

02 February 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

H.K. Sema, N. Santosh Hegde, S.N. Variava, B.P. Singh, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 81; (2005) 3 SCC 551; (2005) SCC (Cr) 742; AIR 2005 SC 2731; 2005 CRLJ 3091; 2005 (3) JhrCR(SC) 244; JT 2005 (2) SC 271; 2005 (1) RCR(Criminal) 836; 2005(1) SCALE 763; [2005] 1 S.C.R. 1019

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Women & Children

Keywords: Disposition, Date Of Occurrence, Juvenile Delinquency, Rehabilitation Of Neglected

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Women & Children - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120B, 201, 302 and 364A - Juvenile Justice Act, 1986 - Children Act, 1960 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 106 - Whether date of occurrence will be reckoning date for determining age of alleged offender as Juvenile offender or date when he is produced in Court/competent authority? - Whether Act of 2000 will be applicable in case a proceeding initiated under 1986 Act and pending when Act of 2000 was enforced with effect from 01-04-2001? - Held, reckoning date for determination of age of juvenile is date of an offence and not date when he is produced before authority or in Court - 2000 Act would be applicable - Appeal disposed of.
