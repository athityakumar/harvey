V.M. Salgaocar and Bros. v Board of Trustees of Port of Mormugao

Supreme Court of India

31 March 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhan, A.K. Mathur

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 253; (2005) 4 SCC 613; 2005 (5) Bom.C.R. 374; JT 2005 (3) SC 607; 2005(3) SCALE 524; [2005] 3 S.C.R. 27

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Bombay Port Trust Act, 1879

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Major Port Trust Act, 1963- Limitation Act, s.3 - (A) Limitation - Denial of rebate - Maintainability of suit - Whether suit is maintainable? - Held, s.120 provides that suit has to be filed within six months of accrual of cause of action and it has to be preceded by one month notice - Major Port Trust Act, 1963 is a special Act - - Formal notice u/s.120 had not been issued - Suit was filed much beyond period of six months provided for filing suit - (B) Constitution of India,1950, Art. 14 - Vires of s. 120 - Held, provision of the Act providing for a shorter period of limitation cannot be declared to be unconstitutional simply because in some of the Statutes a longer period of limitation has been prescribed for the redressal of the litigants grievances - Appeals dismissed.
