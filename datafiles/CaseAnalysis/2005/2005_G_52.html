Union of India and Others v Ghulam Mohd. Bhat

Supreme Court of India

20 October 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, AR. Lakshmanan

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 680; (2005) 13 SCC 228; (2006) SCC (L&S) 1215; AIR 2005 SC 4289; 2006 (1) CLR 247; 2006 (1) JhrCR(SC) 109; JT 2005 (9) SC 212; 2005(8) SCALE 509; 2005 (8) SLR 4; [2005] Supp4 S.C.R. 367

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: [A.] Service - Defence & Security Forces - Central Reserve Police Force Act, 1949, ss. s.10(m), 11(1) - Central Reserve Police Rules, 1955, r. 27 - Misconduct on account of overstay beyond sanctioned leave for 315 days without prior permission or sanction from competent authority - Removal from service - HC held that order of removal from service passed by departmental authorities is without jurisdiction - Appealed herein - Held, a punishment of dismissal can certainly be awarded thereunder even if delinquent is not prosecuted for an offence u/ss. 9 or 10 - Period is more than 300 days and that too without any justifiable reason - That being so order of removal from service suffers from no infirmity - Appeal allowed.
