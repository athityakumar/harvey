State of Haryana and Another v National Consumer Awareness Group and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 May 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Srikrishna, K.G. Balakrishnan

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 767; (2005) 5 SCC 284; (2005) SCC (L&S) 530; AIR 2005 SC 2356; 2005 (59) ALR 29; 2005 (2) AWC 1691; 2005 (5) CompLJ 390; 2006 (3) CPJ(Sc) 8; 2005 (3) JhrCR(SC) 127; JT 2005 (5) SC 151; 2005 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 315; 2005 (3) LLN 103; 2005(4) SCALE 624; [2005] 3 S.C.R. 1158

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Consumer Protection

Summary: Consumer Protection Act, 1986, ss. 16(1)(a) and 16(1)(b) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 217 - What is content of statutory consultation with Chief Justice of High Court postulated u/s. 16(1)(a) of Consumer Protection Act, 1986? - No conflict between provisions of s. 16(1)(a) and 16(1-A) of Act as they could effectively operate in their respective fields, which did not overlap - It is inconceivable that sub-s. (1-A) is intended for appointment of President of State Commission itself - In first place, cannot accede to contention that Chairman of State Commission, who is or has been a Judge of HC, can be selected by a Selection Committee comprising two Secretaries of State Govt. - Nothing could be more erosive of judicial independence than such interpretation of sub-s. (1-A) - Procedure contemplated under sub-s. (1-A) can apply only in respect of appointment of members falling within contemplation of cl (b) of sub-s. (1) of s. 16 - Appeal dismissed.
