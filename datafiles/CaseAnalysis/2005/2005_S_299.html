State Bank's Staff Union Madras v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

15 September 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 554; (2005) 7 SCC 584; (2005) SCC (L&S) 994; AIR 2005 SC 3446; 2005 (6) AWC 5172; JT 2005 (8) SC 315; 2005(7) SCALE 296; 2005 (5) SLR 731; [2005] Supp3 S.C.R. 200

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Banking Laws (Amendment) Act, 1984

Summary: [A.] Banking & Finance - Banking Laws (Amendment) Act, 1984 - State Bank Of India Act, 1955, s. 43-A(1), s. 43-A(2), s. 43-A(3), s. 7(1) - State Bank Of India (Subsidiary Banks) Act, 1959 - Banking Companies (Acquisition and Transfer of Undertakings) Act, 1970 - Banking Companies (Acquisition and Transfer of Undertakings) Act, 1980, s. 12-A - Payment Of Bonus Act, 1965, s. 2(13) - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 14 Banking Laws (Amendment) Act, 1984 - DB held that customary bonus was not payable by State Bank of India - Appealed herein - Held, in every case exercise by legislature by introducing a new provision or deleting an existing provision with retrospective effect per se does not amount to violation of art.14 of Constitution - Act in clear and unambiguous terms prohibits grant of bonus to employees of public Sector Banks, except in accordance with Bonus Act, and also limits such payment only to those eligible under Act - HC's judgment does not suffer from any infirmity to warrant interference.
