Romesh Lal Jain v Naginder Singh Rana and Others

Supreme Court of India

28 October 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 703; (2006) 1 SCC 294; AIR 2006 SC 336; JT 2005 (9) SC 393; 2006 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 889; 2005 (4) RLW 3074; 2005(8) SCALE 810; [2005] Supp4 S.C.R. 868

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: [A.] Criminal - IPC, 1860, ss. 167, 218, 406, 409, 419, 420, 465, 468, 471 - Prevention Of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 13, 5(2) - CrPC, 1973, ss. 173, 197, 197(1), 482 - Essential Commodities Act, 1955, s. 7 - How far a sanction against a public servant for commission of an offence punishable u/s. 13(2) of 1988 Act and ss 409, 167, 218, 419, 420, 465, 468 and 471 of IPC is essential? - Held, no order of sanction to prosecute First Respondent u/s. 197 CrPC was necessary to be obtained from State - Question as to whether an order of sanction would be found essential would depend upon the facts and circumstances of each case - HC was, not right in passing impugned order particularly in view of fact that a valid order of sanction was granted in relation to offences committed by First Respondent under 1988 Act - Appeal allowed.
