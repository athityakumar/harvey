General Manager, Haryana Roadways v Rudhan Singh

Supreme Court of India

14 July 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

G.P. Mathur, R.C. Lahoti, P.K. Balasubramanyan

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 416; (2005) 5 SCC 591; (2005) SCC (L&S) 716; 2005 (3) AWC 2492; 2005 (3) ESC 484; 2005 (106) FLR 607; 2005 (3) JhrCR(SC) 209; JT 2005 (6) SC 137; 2005 (3) LLN 754; 2005 (141) PLR 397; 2005(5) SCALE 433; 2005 (5) SLR 51; [2005] Supp1 S.C.R. 569; 2005 (4) Supreme 726; 2005 (3) UPLBEC 2418

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: [A.] Service - Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 10(1)(c), 2(oo)(bb), 25-B, 25-F - Termination - Whether order directing reinstatement with continuity of services sustainable? - Held, Industrial Tribunal-cum-Labour Court has recorded a finding that respondent has worked for 264 days and this finding has not been challenged before HC - Provisions of s. 25-F are clearly applicable - Services terminated without notice - Termination invalid - Respondent is not entitled to payment of any back wages - Appeal partly allowed.
