Kishun @ Ram Kishun (Dead) Thru. Lrs v Bihari (D) By Lrs

Supreme Court of India

05 August 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

P.K. Balasubramanyan, R.C. Lahoti, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 454; (2005) 6 SCC 300; 2005 (60) ALR 879; 2005 (2) ARC 735; 2005 (3) AWC 2783; 2005 (6) Bom.C.R. 913; 2005 (4) JhrCR(SC) 87; JT 2005 (6) SC 614; 2005 (4) MahLJ 1; 2005 (3) RCR(Civil) 782; 2005 (99) RD 468; 2005(6) SCALE 173; [2005] Supp2 S.C.R. 383; 2005 (3) UC 1899

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Family & Personal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Civil Procedure - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908, o. 23, r. 3, o. 43, r. 1(m) - Gift deed - Whether HC was right in holding that appeal against decree of trial court accepting a compromise which was disputed by the Appellant, was not maintainable? - Held, when one of parties sets up a compromise and other disputes it and court is forced to adjudicate on whether there was a compromise or not and to pass a decree, it could not be understood as a decree passed by court with consent of parties - Bar u/s. 96(3) of Code could not have application - Remanded suit to trial court for a fresh decision - Appeals allowed.
