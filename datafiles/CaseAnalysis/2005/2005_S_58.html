Divisional Manager, United India Insurance v Samir Chandra Chaudhary

Supreme Court of India

14 July 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 443; (2005) 5 SCC 784; 2005 (3) AWC 2501; 2006 (1) Bom.C.R. 870; 2005 (11) CCC 352; 2005 (3) CPJ(Sc) 2; 2006 (2) JhrCR(SC) 175; JT 2005 (6) SC 289; 2006 (143) PLR 148; 2005 (3) RCR(Civil) 371B; 2005(5) SCALE 470; [2005] Supp1 S.C.R. 613

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection; Criminal; Insurance

Summary: [A.] Consumer Protection - Consumer Protection Act, 1986 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 31 - Tree under which car was parked fell on car and extensive damage was caused - Claim for damage suffered - Whether exclusion cl. of special perils applies? - Held, Contemporaneous documents clearly show that complainant right from beginning had accepted position that branch had got knocked off tree because of storm - Onus was on him to adduce material to show contrary - Such material has to be of clinching nature so as to outweigh admission - National Commission did not consider these aspects - Remitted matter to National Commission for hearing matter afresh - Appeal disposed of.
