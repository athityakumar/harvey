U.P.S.C v K. Rajaiah and Others

Supreme Court of India

03 May 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Venkatarama Reddi, P.P. Naolekar

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 379; (2005) 10 SCC 15; (2005) SCC (L&S) 738; AIR 2005 SC 2853; 2005 (106) FLR 214; JT 2005 (11) SC 1; 2005 (4) LLN 78; 2005(4) SCALE 558; 2006 (4) SLR 81

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Defence & Security Forces; Service

Keywords: Eligibility - Seniority - Cadre

Summary: [A.] Constitution - Administrative - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 14, 226 - Indian Police Service (Appointment By Competitive Examination) Regulations, 1955, regns. 5(4), 5(5) - Notifications issued by Central Govt. for selecting and appointing certain police officers of Andhra Pradesh State cadre to IPS - Prayer to convene fresh Selection Committee for re-assessment of comparative merit on basis of service records - Whether non-selection of 1 st respondent to IPS against vacancies pertaining to A P State for year 1999 is on account of non-adherence to relevant rules or arbitrariness in process of selection? - Held, 1 st respondent could not be selected for reason that he did not get gradation of 'outstanding' for four years in a block of five years that was taken into account for purpose of evaluating merits of candidates - Appeal allowed.
