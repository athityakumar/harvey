S.K. Shukla and Others v State of Uttar Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 November 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Mathur, B.N. Agrawal

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 796; (2006) 1 SCC 314; AIR 2006 SC 413; 2006 CRLJ 139; 2006 CRLJ 148; JT 2005 (9) SC 592; 2006 (1) RCR(Criminal) 25; 2005(9) SCALE 268; 2006 (2) SCJ 678; [2005] Supp5 S.C.R. 172

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Uttar Pradesh Gangster and Anti-social Activities (Prevention) Act, 1986

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Prevention Of Terrorism Act, 2002, s. 60 - Unlawful Activities (Prevention) Act, 1967 - Explosive Substances Act, 1908, s. 2 - Uttar Pradesh Gangster and Anti-social Activities (Prevention) Act, 1986, ss. 2, 3 - Petition against withdrawal of POTA order by State Govt. - Whether order passed by Review Committee is sustainable in law or not? - Held, there is prima facie case for prosecuting accused persons u/s. 3(3) r/w s.4 (a)(b) - Set aside order of Review Committee and hold that respondents can be prosecuted u/s. 3(3) and s.4(b) - Criminal case No.3/2003 in crime case No.10/03 under Sections 3 and 4 of POTA Act transfered as sought - Order accordingly.
