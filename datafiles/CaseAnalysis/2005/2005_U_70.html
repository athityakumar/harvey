Major General J.K. Bansal v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

23 August 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

G.P. Mathur, R.C. Lahoti, P.K. Balasubramanyan

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 484; (2005) 7 SCC 227; (2005) SCC (L&S) 932; AIR 2005 SC 3341; 2005 (122) DLT 562; 2005 (107) FLR 37; 2005 (4) JhrCR(SC) 49; JT 2005 (8) SC 11; 2005 (4) LLN 65; 2005 (4) RLW 2550; 2005(6) SCALE 669; 2006 (1) SLR 103

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Defence & Security Forces; Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Court Martial

Summary: [A.] Service - Defence & Security Forces - Defence Research Development Service Rules (DRDS), 1979 - Scope of interference by courts in regard to members of armed forces - Transfer order - Whether malafide? - Held, it is for higher authorities to decide when and where a member of armed forces should be posted - It was Vigilance Branch of Army Head Quarters, which had taken decision to proceed against appellant - Contention that transfer order has been passed on account of malafide reasons is devoid of merit - Appeal dismissed.
