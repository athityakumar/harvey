Messrs Xerox Modicorp Limited v State of Karnataka

Supreme Court of India

24 August 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 490; (2005) 7 SCC 380; AIR 2005 SC 3336; JT 2005 (7) SC 539; 2005(6) SCALE 691; 2005 (142) STC 209; [2005] Supp2 S.C.R. 895

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Summary: [A.] Sales Tax - Constitution - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 366(29A)(b) - Karnataka Sales Tax Act, 1957, s. 5B - Karnataka Sales Tax Rules, 1957, r. 6(4) - Taxable Turnover - Service Maintenance Agreement - Xerox machines - Toners - Spare parts and goods supplied under Service Agreements whether amounted to sale? - Held, there is transfer of title in movables for a price - Mere fact that it is not known in beginning whether or not a part will have to be replaced is irrelevant - Even in absence of any such Agreements, if a part was required to be replaced and was replaced there would be a sale of that part - Same position remains even under Agreements - Transfer of property takes place before the goods are consumed - Transfer takes place in respect of tangible goods - Appeal dismissed.
