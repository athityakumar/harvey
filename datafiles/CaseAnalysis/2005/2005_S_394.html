Ahmed Noormohmed Bhatii v State of Gujarat and Others

Supreme Court of India

16 March 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Singh, N. Santosh Hegde, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 245; (2005) 3 SCC 647; (2005) SCC (Cr) 794; AIR 2005 SC 2115; 2005 CRLJ 2157; 2005 (2) G.L.R. 1402; JT 2005 (3) SC 484; 2005 (2) KLT 769; 2005 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 274; 2005 (2) RCR(Criminal) 320; 2005(3) SCALE 300; [2005] 2 S.C.R. 879

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Summary: Constitution - Criminal - CrPC - Prayer for quashing of criminal proceedings initiated and for a declaration that s.151 of CrPC is unconstitutional and ultra vires. - Whether prayer can be allowed? - Held, a provision cannot be held to be unreasonable or arbitrary and, therefore, unconstitutional, merely because authority vested with power may abuse his authority - HC examined matter and has come to conclusion that those proceedings do not deserved to quashed under art.226 of Constitution or u/s.482 of CrPC - No ground for interference - Appeal dismissed.
