Harinagar Sugar Mills Limited and another v State of Bihar and others

Supreme Court of India

09 November 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhan, Arun Kumar

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 1690; (2006) 1 SCC 509

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure; Agriculture & Agri. Products

Summary: Agriculture & Agri. Products - Practice & Procedure - Constitution - Bihar Agricultural Produce Markets Act, 1960, ss. 27, 4B - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 142 - Issuance of notification - Applicability of provision - Payment of market fee - Review jurisdiction - State issued notification deleting sugar from commodity to which the Act was applicable - Later on, said notification was revived and HC took view that on revival of notification on commodity, notification could not be treated to be attracted provisions of the Act - Thereafter, the Act was again amended and s.4-B of the Act was inserted deleting earlier notification deleting sugar from Schedule of the Act - While writ applications were pending in HC challenging that provisions, it was brought to notice of HC that other sugar mills were paying market fee to some extent but appellants were not paying market fee - HC ordered appellants to deposit fee before Registrar of HC subject to further orders to be passed by Court and upheld amended provisions - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether sugar factories engaged in purchasing sugarcane and selling sugar and sugar molasses were liable to pay market fee under provisions of the Act - Held, Court gave directions in exercise of its powers u/art. 142 of the Constitution that decision could be prospective in operation, meaning thereby that after pronouncement of judgment all future transactions of purchase of sugarcane by sugar factories concerned in market areas as well as sale of manufactured sugar and molasses produced there from by utilizing purchased sugarcane by these factories could not be subjected to levy of market fee u/s. 27 of the Act by Market Committees concerned - So far as past transactions up to date of judgment were concerned which had already suffered levy of market fee, it was observed that levy of market fee could not be covered by judgment and collected market fee of these transactions prior to date of judgment could not be required to be refunded to any of sugar mills which might have paid these market fees - Thus, review could be granted only if there was mistake apparent on face of record - However, there was no such apparent mistake on face of record - HC had taken view that amount deposited in Court by appellants was in lieu of market fee for benefit of Market Committee, and therefore Market Committee was entitled to receive same - Appeal dismissed.
