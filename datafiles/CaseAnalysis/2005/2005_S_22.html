Karnataka State Road Transport Corporation and v S.G. Kotturappa and Another

Supreme Court of India

03 March 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, N. Santosh Hegde

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 144; (2005) 3 SCC 409; (2005) SCC (L&S) 484; AIR 2005 SC 1933; 2005 (2) AWC 1064; 2005 (2) CLR 59; 2005 (3) ESC 311; 2005 (105) FLR 274; 2005 (3) JhrCR(SC) 21; JT 2005 (3) SC 20; 2005 (1) LLN 1095; 2005(2) SCALE 493; [2005] 2 S.C.R. 520; 2005 (2) SLR 804; 2005 (2) Supreme 477; 2005 (2) UPLBEC 1632

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Labour and Industrial Law - Karnataka State Road Transport Corporation (Cadre and Recruitment) Regulations, 1982 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts.14, 16 and 311 - Temporary workers - Badli worker - Respondents were terminated by appellant as he was not found suitable for the job - Labour Court directed reinstatement with full back wages - Held, history-sheet of Respondents show that opportunities after opportunities had been given to them to improve themselves but they did not avail same - Badli workers did not acquire any legal right to continue in service - Not even entitled to the protection under the Industrial Disputes Act nor the mandatory requirements of Section 25-F of the Industrial Disputes were required to be complied with before terminating his services - Order of termination simpliciter cannot be said to be violative of Articles 14, 16 and 311 of the Constitution - No fault can be found with action of Appellant - Appeals allowed.
