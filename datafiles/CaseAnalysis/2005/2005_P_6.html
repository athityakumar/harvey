K. Prabhakaran v P. Jayarajan

Supreme Court of India

11 January 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, B.N. Srikrishna, G.P. Mathur, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 1999; (2005) SCC (Cr) 451; (2005) 1 SCC 754; AIR 2005 SC 688; JT 2005 (1) SC 173; 2005 (1) KLT 510; 2005 (2) MLJ(SC) 27; 2005 (1) RCR(Civil) 667; 2005(1) SCALE 166; [2005] 1 S.C.R. 296

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Keywords: Date Of Nomination

Summary: Election - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 191 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, s. 31 - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 8(1), 8(2), 8(3), 8(4), 36(2)(a), 67-A and 100(1)(a) - Question of qualification or disqualification of a returned candidate within meaning of s. 100(1)(a) - Election - (A) Whether an appellate judgment of a date subsequent to date of election and having a bearing on conviction of a candidate and sentence of imprisonment passed on him would have effect of wiping out disqualification from a back date if a person consequent upon his conviction for any offence and sentenced to imprisonment for not less than 2 years was disqualified from filing nomination and contesting election on dates of nomination and election? - Held, question of qualification or disqualification of a returned candidate within meaning of s. 100(1)(a) of RPA has to be determined by reference to date of his election which date, as defined in s. 67-A of Act, shall be date on which candidate is declared by returning officer to be elected - Whether a nomination was improperly accepted shall have to be determined for purpose of s. 100(1)(d)(i) by reference to date fixed for scrutiny of nomination, expression, as occurring in s. 36(2)(a)? - Such dates are focal point for purpose of determining whether candidate is not qualified or is disqualified for being chosen to fill seat in a House - It is by reference to such focal point dates that question of disqualification under sub-ss. (1),(2) and (3) of s. 8 shall have to be determined - Factum of pendency of an appeal against conviction is irrelevant and inconsequential - So also a subsequent decision in appeal or revision setting aside conviction or sentence or reduction in sentence would not have effect of wiping out disqualification which did exist on focal point dates referred to here in above - Decisive dates are date of election and date of scrutiny of nomination and not date of judgment in an election petition or in appeal there against - (B) What is meaning to be assigned to expression a person convicted of any offence and sentenced to imprisonment for not less than 2 years in sub-s. (3) of s. 8 of RPA? - Held, for purpose of attracting applicability of disqualification within meaning of a person convicted of any offence and sentenced to imprisonment for not less than two years expression as occurring in s. 8(3) of RPA, what has to be seen is total length of time for which a person has been ordered to remain in prison consequent upon conviction and sentence pronounced at a trial - Word 'any' qualifying word 'offence' should be understood as meaning nature of offence and not number of offence/offences - (C) What is purport of sub-s.(4) of S. 8 of RPA? - Held, sub-s.(4) of S. 8 of RPA is an exception carved out from sub-sections (1),(2) and (3) - Saving from disqualification is preconditioned by person convicted being a member of a House on date of conviction - Benefit of such saving is available only so long as House continues to exist and person continues to be a member of a House - Saving ceases to apply if House is dissolved or person ceases to be a member of House - Order accordingly.
