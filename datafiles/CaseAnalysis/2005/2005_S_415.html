Bijender Singh v State of Haryana and Another

Supreme Court of India

28 March 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 219; (2005) 3 SCC 685; (2005) SCC (Cr) 889; AIR 2005 SC 2262; 2005 CRLJ 2195; JT 2005 (4) SC 1; 2005 (2) KLT 743; 2005 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 331; 2005 (2) RCR(Criminal) 406; 2005(3) SCALE 467; [2005] 2 S.C.R. 1131

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Human Rights; Practice & Procedure; Women & Children

Summary: Criminal - Human Rights - Women and Children - IPC - Whether respondent No 2, who was admittedly more than 16 years of age on 17/11/1999 when he purportedly committed offences punishable under ss 302, 364, 201 r/w ss 34, 120 B IPC would be given benefits of Juvenile Justice Act, 2000? - Held, provisions of 2000 Act would be applicable to those cases initiated and pending trial/inquiry for offences committed under 1986 Act provided that person had not completed 18 years of age as on 17/11/1999 - Respondent No 2 accused had completed 18 years of age before 17/11/1999 - Trial court has to deal with case of respondent no 2- Accused keeping in view law laid down by Constitution Bench in Pratap Singh v State of Jharkhand and Anr - Appeal disposed of.
