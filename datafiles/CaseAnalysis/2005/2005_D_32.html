State of Madhya Pradesh v Dayal Sahu

Supreme Court of India

29 September 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

H.K. Sema, G.P. Mathur

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 1235; (2005) 8 SCC 122; (2005) SCC (Cr) 1988; AIR 2005 SC 3570; 2005 CRLJ 4375; JT 2005 (12) SC 150; 2005 (4) KLT 426; 2005(7) SCALE 663; [2005] Supp3 S.C.R. 703

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Self-Inflicted

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 376 - Complaint - Committed rape in guise as husband - Acquitted - Sustainability - Accused/respondent relative of complainant entered into room of prosecutrix and had sexual intercourse with her in guise of her husband and committed rape - PW1 registered a complaint and prosecution was initiated before Trail Court - Trial Court convicted accused u/s. 376 of 1860 Act - Aggrieved by same appeal was preferred before HC which was allowed and accused was acquitted - Hence, instant appeal - Whether acquittal of accused was sustainable - Held, SC relied upon State of Rajasthan vs. N.K, 2000 Indlaw SC 240 and also relied upon State of Punjab vs. Gurmit Singh, 1996 Indlaw SC 1087 where it was said that corroboration of testimony of prosecutrix as a condition for judicial reliance was not a requirement of law but a guidance of prudence under given facts and circumstances - Further, it was also noticed that minor contradictions or insignificant discrepancies should not be a ground for throwing out an otherwise reliable prosecution case and non-examination of doctor and non-production of doctor's report would not cause fatal to prosecution case even if the statements of the prosecutrix and other prosecution witnesses inspire confidence - Therefore, it was also noticed that Court while acquitting accused on benefit of doubt should be cautious to see that doubt should be a reasonable doubt and it should not reverse findings of guilt on the basis of irrelevant circumstances or mere technicalities - Thus, HC was totally erred in law in recording acquittal of accused by giving him benefit of doubt for non-examination of doctor, thereby committed grave miscarriage of justice - Appeal allowed.
