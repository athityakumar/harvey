Aniglase Yohannan v Ramlatha and Ors.

Supreme Court of India

23 September 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 574; (2005) 7 SCC 534; AIR 2005 SC 3503; 2005 (6) ALD(SC) 11; 2005 (61) ALR 766; 2005 (4) AWC 3776; JT 2005 (8) SC 499; 2005 (4) KLT 280; 2005 (4) RCR(Civil) 563; 2005 (4) RLW 2428; 2005(7) SCALE 471; 2005 (Supp) RD 361; [2005] Supp3 S.C.R. 440

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure; Trusts & Associations

Summary: [A.] Contract & Commercial - Specific Relief Act, 1963, s. 16(c) - Non compliance of requirement of s.16(c) - Willingness to perform essential terms of contract - Trial Court found non-compliance - High Court allowed specific performance - Appealed herein - Held, an averment of readiness and willingness in plaint is not a mathematical formula which should only be in specific words - If averments in plaint as a whole do clearly indicate readiness and willingness of plaintiff to fulfil his part of obligations fact that they are differently worded will not militate against readiness and willingness of plaintiff in a suit for specific performances of contract for sale - Appeal dismissed.
