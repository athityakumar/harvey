Messrs K. Raheja Development Corporation v State of Karnataka

Supreme Court of India

05 May 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, AR. Lakshmanan

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 350; (2005) 5 SCC 162; AIR 2005 SC 2350; JT 2005 (5) SC 161; 2005 (2) KLT 822; 2005 (3) MLJ(SC) 151; 2005(4) SCALE 668; [2005] 3 S.C.R. 1210; 2005 (141) STC 298

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Indirect Tax; Land & Property; Sales Tax

Summary: [A.] Sales Tax - Land & Property - Indirect Tax - Karnataka Ownership Flats (Regulation of Promotion of Construction, Sales, Management and Transfer) Act, 1974 - Karnataka Sales Tax Act, 1957, ss. 2(1)(k)(viii), 2(1)(u1), 5(3C) - Transfer Of Property Act, 1882, s. 53A - Turnover tax - Appellants undertake to build as developers for prospective purchaser - Whether Appellants are dealers and are liable to pay turnover tax ? - Held, even if the Appellants are owners to the extent that they have entered into Agreements to carry out construction activity on behalf of somebody else for cash, deferred payment or other valuable consideration, they would be carrying out a works contract and would become liable to pay turnover tax on the transfer of property in the goods involved in such works contract - If agreement is entered into after flat or unit is already constructed, then there would be no works contract - But so long as agreement is entered into before construction is complete it would be a works contract - Appeal dismissed.
