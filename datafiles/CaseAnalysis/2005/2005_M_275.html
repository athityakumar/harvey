Messrs Master Marine Services Private Limited v Metcalfe and Hodgkinson Private Limited and

Supreme Court of India

19 April 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

G.P. Mathur, R.C. Lahoti

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 293; (2005) 6 SCC 138; AIR 2005 SC 2299; 2005 (2) BC 574; 2005 (119) DLT 139; JT 2005 (4) SC 408; 2005(4) SCALE 216; [2005] 3 S.C.R. 666

Case Digest

Subject: Insurance

Keywords: Instructions

Summary: [A.] Carriers & Transportation - Contract & Commercial - Insurance Act, 1938, s. 64-UM - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 2 r. 2 - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 14 - Professional services for survey of containers and cargo - Contract given by second respondent, Container Corporation of India in favour of appellant quashed - Whether HC right in quashing contract on ground that appellant have no license? - Held, CONCOR have power to relax tender conditions at any stage, if considered necessary, for purpose of finalizing contract in overall interest of CONCOR and trade - Contract is of data entry of a container and for this kind of work no license under IRDA is required - No public interest was involved which may warrant interference by HC - Appeal allowed.
