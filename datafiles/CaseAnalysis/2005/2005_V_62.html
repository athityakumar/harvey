Vinayaka Dev Idagunji and others v Shivaram and others

Supreme Court of India

28 July 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Arun Kumar, B.P. Singh

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 571; (2005) 6 SCC 641; AIR 2005 SC 3081; 2005 (3) AWC 3055; JT 2005 (6) SC 429; 2005(5) SCALE 691; [2005] Supp1 S.C.R. 855

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Keywords: Madras Endowment Act, Hindu Religious and Endowments Act

Summary: [A.] Trusts & Associations - Bombay Public Trusts Act, 1950, ss. 50, 51, 79, 80 - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908, o. 14 r. 2, ss. 151, 9, 92 - Madras Endowment Act - Suits falling u/s. 50 of the Act, to be either filed by the Charity Commissioner or after obtaining consent in writing of the Charity Commissioner - Whether a suit to establish a right to be hereditary 'archaks' (Pujaris) in a temple and a share in offerings made to deity, is a suit in relation to personal/private right of archaks or it is a suit in nature of exercising a public right in a public trust? - Held, there is no public interest involved - Only interest is that of plaintiffs and their families - Right of archakship is claimed on basis of inheritance - It is a hereditary personal right which they want to establish - s.50 of Bombay Public Trusts Act is not attracted - Appeal dismissed.
