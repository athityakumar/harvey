Uttar Pradesh State Electricity Board, Agra and another v Natwar Singh

Supreme Court of India

26 April 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 1366; (2005) 11 SCC 552

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - U.P Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 6 - Termination of service - Reinstatement - Fixation of backwages - Respondent workman was terminated from service by appellant Department - On industrial dispute, Industrial Tribunal came to conclusion that respondent was to reinstated in service, which was confirmed by HC - Appellant disputed the order of Tribunal and HC, on various grounds - Hence instant appeal.

Held, Labour Court considered evidence led by workman vis-a-vis that of management and came to that conclusion, which finding has been confirmed by HC and Court find no reason to interfere with said question. Respondent workman is entitled to reinstatement in same post as he was working on the date of his termination. However, Tribunal in operative portion of order, while directing reinstatement, held that workman is entitled to reinstatement with full back wages and all service benefits from 16-12-1989 as if he was in continuous service. Court cannot accept said directions given by Tribunal in regard to scale of pay to be drawn and regularization and benefits directed by said Tribunal. It is the case of workman himself that his appointment was purely on ad hoc basis and he was being paid consolidated salary of Rs.600 per month. It is while so serving as an ad hoc employee, his services were terminated without following requirement of law u/s. 6-N of Act. If that be case, then on being found that the ad hoc employee is entitled to reinstatement in same post on same pay scale as was being drawn by him on date of his termination other questions like management putting him on a particular pay scale and further regularization of his services will not arise. By doing this Tribunal has gone beyond scope of dispute. HC, however, has reduced back wages payable for period during which workman was not in service to 50% as against 100% back wages granted by Tribunal. Court is in agreement with HC so far as quantum of back wages to be paid to workman, which shall be 50% of back wages drawn by him on date of his termination. Court set aside order of Tribunal in regard to the fixation of pay scale and regularization of service since that was beyond the scope of dispute before it. While upholding order of reinstatement of workman concerned and entitlement of scale as drawn on date of his retrenchment, Court set aside order directing fixation of pay scale and regularization of his service as done by Tribunal. Appeal partly allowed.
