Haryana State Cooperative Land Development Bank v Neelam

Supreme Court of India

28 February 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, N. Santosh Hegde

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 175; (2005) 5 SCC 91; (2005) SCC (L&S) 601; AIR 2005 SC 1843; 2005 (2) AWC 1245; 2005 (2) ESC 192; 2005 (105) FLR 114; 2005 (2) JhrCR(SC) 108; JT 2005 (2) SC 600; 2005 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 748; 2005 (1) LLJ 1153; 2005 (2) LLN 653; 2005(2) SCALE 434; [2005] 2 S.C.R. 424; 2005 (2) SLR 784; 2005 (2) Supreme 314; 2005 (2) UPLBEC 2044

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour and Industrial Law - Whether HC right in ordering reinstatement of respondent? - Held, respondent was not in a position to establish her legal right so as to obtain a writ of or in nature of mandamus directing Appellant to reinstate her in service - Conduct of Appellant in approaching Labour Court after more than seven years had, therefore, been considered to be a relevant factor by Labour Court for refusing to grant an relief to her - Such a consideration on part of Labour Court cannot be said to be an irrelevant one - Labour Court cannot be said to have exercised its discretionary jurisdiction injudiciously arbitrarily and capriciously warranting interference at hands of HC in exercise of its discretionary jurisdiction under art.226 of Constitution - Appeal allowed.
