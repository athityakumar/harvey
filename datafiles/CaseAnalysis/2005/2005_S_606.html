Vaijayantl v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

22 September 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 1867; (2005) 13 SCC 134

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34 and 302 - Murder - Common intention - Conviction - Challenged - Appellant, a lady, had been convicted for alleged commission of an offence u/s. 302 r/w s. 34 of the IPC - Hence, instant appeal - Whether in view of the facts and circumstances of instant case, the prosecution could not be said to have made out a case of sharing common intention on the part of the appellant with the other accused persons - Held, appellant had not caused any injury nor any weapon had been recovered from her - No evidence had been brought on record by the prosecution to show that the accused persons met prior to the date of occurrence for the purpose of commission of the said offence - Appellant reached after the other accused persons reached the spot and had been assaulting the deceased by gupti and knife - HC, therefore, was not correct in arriving at a finding that the appellant had shared the common intention with other accused for commission of the crime - Impugned judgments could not be sustained and were set aside - Appeal allowed.
