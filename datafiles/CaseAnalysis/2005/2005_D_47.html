K.C. Sharma v Delhi Stock Exchange and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 April 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Srikrishna, K.G. Balakrishnan

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 232; (2005) 4 SCC 4; (2005) SCC (L&S) 496; AIR 2005 SC 2884; 2005 (5) AWC 4347; 2005 (118) DLT 618; 2005 (2) ESC 215; 2005 (105) FLR 538; JT 2005 (4) SC 18; 2005 (2) LLN 667; 2005(3) SCALE 561; 2005 (3) Supreme 168; 2005 (3) UPLBEC 2650

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: Constitution - Service - Maintainability of WP - State - Appellant joined service in Delhi Stock Exchange - Termination - Whether Delhi Stock Exchange is amenable to writ jurisdiction? Held, respondent no 1 is amenable to writ jurisdiction - Termination of services of appellant illegal - Although termination of appellant's service was illegal and unjustified, totality of circumstances of case render it improper and unjust to direct relief of reinstatement with full back wages - Compensation granted - Appeal partly allowed.
