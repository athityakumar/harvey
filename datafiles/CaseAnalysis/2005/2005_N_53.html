Mangal Prasad Tamoli (Dead) By Lrs v Narvedshwar Mishra (Dead) By Lrs. and Others

Supreme Court of India

24 February 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Srikrishna, K.G. Balakrishnan

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 134; (2005) 3 SCC 422; AIR 2005 SC 1964; 2005 (4) ALD(SC) 53; 2005 (59) ALR 440; 2005 (1) ARC 642; 2005 (2) AWC 1305; JT 2005 (11) SC 229; 2005 (2) MLJ(SC) 171; 2005 (3) RCR(Civil) 171; 2005 (99) RD 177; 2005(2) SCALE 384

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Summary: Land and Property - Suit for redemption of a mortgage - Whether HC right in allowing continuation of proceedings? - Held, trial court and first appellate court had held that suit for redemption brought by plaintiff was premature and rightly dismissed it - HC took an erroneous view that because of plaintiff's advocate had stated that he would not seek delivery of possession before stipulated time (26/1/1968), suit could be continued - Original judgment of trial court and first appellate court dismissing the suit as pre-mature is reaffirmed - Appeal allowed.
