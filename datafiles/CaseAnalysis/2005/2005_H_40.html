National Insurance Copany Limited v Harjeet Rice Mills

Supreme Court of India

25 July 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

P.K. Balasubramanyan, R.C. Lahoti

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 428; (2005) 6 SCC 45; 2005 (60) ALR 752; 2005 (4) AWC 3309; 2005 (12) CCC 367; [2005] 126 Comp Cas 804; 2005 (3) CPJ(Sc) 6; JT 2005 (6) SC 372; 2005 (4) MLJ(SC) 58; 2005 (141) PLR 285; 2005 (3) RCR(Civil) 413; 2005(5) SCALE 606; [2005] Supp1 S.C.R. 794

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection; Insurance; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Consumer Protection - Jammu & Kashmir Consumer Protection Act - Insurance Act, 1938, s. 64UM(c) - Deficiency in service - Claim allowed by HC - Held, HC failed to exercise its appellate jurisdiction properly - It failed to see that it had duty as Appellate Authority to satisfy itself that no fraud was involved and that claim was genuine and sustainable - Adequate prima facie material was available to warrant a proper enquiry on that question - Remanded claim of respondent to State Commission for an investigation de novo - Appeal allowed.
