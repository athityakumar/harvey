Dhampur Sugar Mills Limited v Bhola Singh

Supreme Court of India

08 February 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, N. Santosh Hegde

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 73; (2005) SCC (L&S) 292; (2005) 2 SCC 470; AIR 2005 SC 1790; 2005 (3) ALD(SC) 35; 2005 (5) AWC 3987; 2005 (21) CLR 799; 2005 (3) ESC 319; 2005 (104) FLR 999; JT 2005 (2) SC 98; 2005 (1) LLJ 1084; 2005 (1) LLN 1069; 2005(2) SCALE 15; [2005] 1 S.C.R. 1123; 2005 (2) SLR 312; 2005 (1) Supreme 948; 2005 (1) UPLBEC 929

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: [A.] Service - Uttar Pradesh Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 6N - Apprentices Act, 1961 - Appointment as a trainee in terms of a scheme sponsored by State Govt. for training cane growers - Termination as scheme had come to an end - Whether appellant adopted unfair labour practice? - When a workman is appointed in terms of a scheme on daily wages, he does not derive any legal right to be regularized in his service - Completion of 240 days of continuous service in a year may not by itself be a ground for directing regularization particularly in a case when workman had not been appointed in accordance with extant rules - Appellant is not guilty of commission of unfair labour practice - Appeal allowed.
