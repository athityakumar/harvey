State of Uttar Pradesh and others v Sukhpal Singh Bal

Supreme Court of India

02 September 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, B.P. Singh

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 526; (2005) 7 SCC 615; 2005 (3) ACC 785; AIR 2005 SC 3324; 2005 (4) AWC 3073; JT 2005 (8) SC 115; 2005 (4) RCR(Civil) 96; 2005(7) SCALE 106; [2005] Supp2 S.C.R. 1135

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Legislative Powers, Evasion Of Tax, Constitutional Validity of a Provision, Freedom Of Speech And Expression

Summary: [A.] Indirect Tax - Constitution - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 14, 19(1)(g) - Uttar Pradesh Motor Vehicles Taxation Act, 1997, ss. 10(3), 5(1)(b) - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, ss. 88(12), 88(9) - Imposing ten time penalty - Declaration by High Court that s. 10(3) of Taxation Act as ultra vires art.14 and 19(1)(g) of Constitution - Appealed herein - Held, object of legislature in levying such penalty is to provide deterrent against tax evasion and to put a stop to a practice which legislature considers to be against public interest - It is not possible to say that s 10(3) infringes arts.14 and 19(1)(g) of Constitution, as held in impugned judgment - Appeals allowed.
