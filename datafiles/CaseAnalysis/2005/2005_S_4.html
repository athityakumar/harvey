B.P. Achala Anand v S. Appi Reddy and Another

Supreme Court of India

11 February 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, G.P. Mathur, P.K. Balasubramanyan

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 92; (2005) 3 SCC 313; AIR 2005 SC 986; 2005 (59) ALR 122; 2005 (4) Bom.C.R. 823; 2005 (117) DLT 354; 2005 (2) JhrCR(SC) 21; JT 2005 (2) SC 233; 2005 (1) KLT 904; 2005 (2) MLJ(SC) 80; 2005 (2) RCR(Civil) 80; 2005 (1) RCR(Rent) 286; 2005(2) SCALE 105; [2005] 2 S.C.R. 3; 2005 (2) UC 770

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Keywords: Karnataka Rent Control, Act, 1961

Summary: [A.] Rent Control - Karnataka Rent Control, Act, 1961, ss.18, 21(1)(a), 29, 30 - Hindu Adoptions and Maintenance Act, 1956, s. 3(b) - Limitation Act, 1963, art. 128 - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 142 - - Eviction petition - Whether wife of tenant can be allowed to prosecute appeal and defend her right against claim for eviction made by landlord? - Held, during pendency of these proceedings and while matter was pending in HC a decree for dissolution of marriage by divorce based on mutual consent has been passed - It is not case of appellant that she is entitled to continue her residence in tenanted premises by virtue of an obligation incurred by her husband to provide residence for her as a part of maintenance - Appellant cannot be allowed to prosecute appeal and defend her right against claim for eviction made by landlord - Appeal dismissed.
