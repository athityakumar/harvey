State of Karnataka through CBI v C. Nagarajaswamy Respondent with M.K. Vijayalakshmi

Supreme Court of India

07 October 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 880; (2005) 8 SCC 370; AIR 2005 SC 4308; JT 2005 (12) SC 349; 2005(8) SCALE 280; [2005] Supp4 S.C.R. 169

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure; Socio-Economic

Summary: [A.] Criminal - CrPC, 1973, ss. 300, 313, 403, 482 - Prevention Of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 13(2), 7 - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 20, 21 - IPC, 1860, s. 161 - Interpretation of - Order of discharge by HC holding that Trial Court was bound to record either a judgment of conviction or acquittal, even after holding that sanction was not valid - Appeal against - Held, even if a judgment of conviction or acquittal was recorded, same would not make any distinction for purpose of invoking provisions of s.300 as even then, it would be held to have been rendered illegally and without jurisdiction - Set aside judgments of HC - Trial court is requested to dispose of matters at an early date - Appeal disposed of.
