Baldev Singh Bajwa and Others v Monish Saini and Others

Supreme Court of India

05 October 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

P.P. Naolekar, K.G. Balakrishnan

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 1052; (2005) 12 SCC 778; AIR 2006 SC 59; JT 2005 (12) SC 442; 2005 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 561; 2005 (4) RCR(Civil) 492; 2005(8) SCALE 338; [2005] Supp4 S.C.R. 26

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Keywords: Restoration Of Possession, Residential Purpose, Foreign Country, Summary Trial, Non Residential Building

Summary: Rent Control - East Punjab Urban Rent Restriction Act, 1949, ss. 2(dd), 13-B & 18-A(5) - Properties of Non Resident Indian's - Eviction of Tenant - Refusal to Grant Permission to Leave - Justifiability - Appellants were tenants in properties of respondents/landlords - Respondents filed ejectment petitions before Rent Controller u/s. 13-B of the Act claiming to be non-resident Indian as per 2(dd) of the Act - Rent controller ordered for eviction and refused to grant permission to contest petitions of respondents/landlords, as claimed by appellants u/s. 18-A(5) of the Act - Appellants filed revision petitions before HC, which upheld order of rent controller and dismissed petitions - Hence, instant appeals - Appellant contended that to bring case within four corners of ss. 2 (dd) and 13-B of the 1949, it was necessary that NRI had to return to India permanently - Held, time and again SC had laid down that legislature intent had to be ascertained according to plain language used in enactment and basic rule of statutory construction should be preferred which advances purpose and object of legislation and not which leads to anomalies, injustice or absurdities - Legislature wants decision of Controller to be final and does not provide any appeal or second appeal against order of eviction, it was only HC which could exercise power of consideration of case, whether decision of Controller was in accordance with law - Provisions makes it explicitly clear that right to eject tenant, u/s. 13-B of the Act, was available to that landlord only who has a particular legal status or character namely, that he was non-resident Indian landlord, he was owner of the suit building for five years before he availed of that right. - Landlord had also to prove his requirement in respect of that building - Thus proceedings taken up u/s. 13-B of the Act by NRI landlords for ejectment of tenant, Court would presume that landlord's need pleaded in petition was genuine and bonafide - Provisions of ss. 18-A(4) and (5) of the Act, concede to tenant's right to defend proceedings initiated u/s. 13-B of the Act showing that requirement of landlord was not genuine or bona fide - Mere assertion on part of tenant would not be sufficient to rebut strong presumption in landlord's favour that his requirement of premises was real and genuine - Thus, HC was justified in upholding order of rent controller - Appeals dismissed.
