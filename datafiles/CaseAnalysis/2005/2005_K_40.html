The Managing Director, TNSTC Limited v K.I. Bindu and others

Supreme Court of India

05 October 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Arun Kumar

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 886; (2005) 8 SCC 473; 2005 (4) ACC 350; AIR 2005 SC 4425; 2005 (61) ALR 623; 2006 (1) AWC 19; 2006 (1) JhrCR(SC) 45; JT 2005 (10) SC 501; 2006 (1) RCR(Civil) 85; 2005(8) SCALE 173; 2006 (Supp) Bom.C.R. 631; [2005] Supp3 S.C.R. 1089

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Future Pecuniary Benefit, Computation Of Compensation, Assessment Of Damage

Summary: [A.] Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, s. 166 - Tribunal awarded compensation - Monthly income was taken to be Rs. 5,843 - Multiplier of 17 was applied - HC confirmed - Appeal against - Plea of contributory negligence - Whether quantum as awarded is on higher side as claimed by appellant-Corporation? - Held, there was no definite material to infer that deceased by his negligent acts contributed to accident - Taking into account relevant factors and age of deceased it would be appropriate to apply multiplier of 13 - Appeal partly allowed.
