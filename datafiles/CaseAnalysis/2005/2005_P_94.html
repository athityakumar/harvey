Ponnusamy v Karuppanna Mudaliar (Dead) By Lrs. and others

Supreme Court of India

25 January 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhan, A.K. Mathur

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 1612; (2005) 12 SCC 477

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s.100 - Indian Easements Act, 1882, s.60 - Appellant/plaintiff filed suit against defendant/respondent which was decreed in favour of appellant - On appeal, HC set aside findings recorded by Lower Courts and dismissed suit filed by appellant - Whether Lower Appellate Court was right in holding that s.60 of 1882 Act will not apply to case.

Held, HC had failed to take notice of question of law framed at time of admission of second appeal and decided same as if it was hearing first appeal and judgment under appeal was unsustainable. Same was set aside and remitted back HC for fresh decision in accordance with law. HC would be at liberty to either decide appeal on question of law already framed or reframe question(s) of law if same arise from findings recorded by Lower Courts. HC was directed to take up appeal on priority basis and dispose of it as expeditiously as possible. Order accordingly.

Ratio - HC can exercise its jurisdiction u/s.100 of CPC to entertain second appeal only substantial question of law framed at time of admission or at subsequent stage and while deciding same confine itself to question(s) of law framed.
