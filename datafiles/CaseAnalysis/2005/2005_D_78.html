Dharampal Satyapal v Commissioner of Central Excise, New Delhi

Supreme Court of India

21 April 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, S.N. Variava, AR. Lakshmanan

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 355; (2005) 4 SCC 337; 2005 (183) E.L.T. 241; JT 2005 (5) SC 95; 2005(4) SCALE 344; [2005] 3 S.C.R. 746

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: [A.] Excise - Central Excise Act, 1944, ss. 11A(1), 11AB, 11AC, 35-L(b) - Central Excise Rules, 1944, rr. 173Q, 9(2) - Whether, in facts and circumstances of this case, Tribunal was justified in upholding order of commissioner with respect to (a) excisability of kimam and classification thereof under sub-heading 2404.49 prior to 23/7/1996 and under sub-heading 2404.40 w e f 23/7/1996 - (b) rationale for invoking extended period of limitation under proviso to s. 11a(1) - (c) Eligibility for benefit of proforma and modvat credit in respect of chewing tobacco kimam? - Held, no infirmity found in the impugned judgment of the tribunal which has held that the compound (kimam) was marketable and classifiable as chewing tobacco or a preparation for chewing tobacco under chapter sub-heading 2404.49/2404.40 - Unexplained concealment of existence of two units - As the burden to prove the defence of bonafides was on the assessee and the assessee in this case has failed to prove its bonafides, the Department was justified in invoking extended period of limitation under proviso to s.11A(1) - Appeals dismissed.
