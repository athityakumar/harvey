V. Ramana v A.P.S.R.T.C. and Others

Supreme Court of India

05 September 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 653; (2005) 7 SCC 338; AIR 2005 SC 3417; 2005 (4) AWC 3079; 2005 (3) CLR 410; 2005 (4) ESC 611; 2005 (107) FLR 262; JT 2005 (8) SC 134; 2005 (4) LLN 50; 2006 (2) MLJ(SC) 162; 2005(7) SCALE 121; 2005 (5) SLR 752; [2005] Supp2 S.C.R. 1149; 2005 (6) Supreme 183

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: [A.] Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 11-A - Andhra Pradesh State Road Transport Corporation (APSRTC) Employees (Conduct) Regulations, 1963 - Termination of Services on alleged misconduct - Scope of interference with quantum of punishment - Initiation of disciplinary proceedings against appellant conductor working in APSRTC - Charges related to non-issuance of tickets to passengers - Order of removal from services passed by enquiry officer after giving opportunity of hearing to appellant - Writ Petition filed - Reference to a larger bench as regards effect of acquittal in criminal case and smallness of amount involved - HC observing that acquittal was really of no consequence and small amount of discrepancy was equally inconsequential held that termination order was justified - Validity - Unless punishment imposed by Disciplinary Authority or Appellate authority shocks conscience of Count/Tribunal, there is no scope for interference - Scope of judicial review is limited to deficiency in decision making process and not decision - Hence impugned judgment of HC does not suffer from any infirmity - Appeal dismissed.
