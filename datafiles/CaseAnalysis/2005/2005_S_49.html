State of Madhya Pradesh v Saleem @ Chamaru and Another

Supreme Court of India

13 July 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 413; (2005) 5 SCC 554; (2005) SCC (Cr) 1329; 2005 CRLJ 3435; JT 2005 (6) SC 293; 2005 (3) RCR(Criminal) 749; 2005(5) SCALE 462; [2005] Supp1 S.C.R. 562

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Criminal - IPC, 1860, ss. 34, 294, 307, 325, 330, 333, 506 - HC reduced sentence to period already undergone - Appeal by state - Held, it is to be noted that alleged offences are of very serious nature - Undue sympathy to impose inadequate sentence will undermine public confidence in efficacy of law - An accused charged u/s. 307 cannot be acquitted merely because injuries inflicted on victim were in nature of a simple hurt - Remitted matter to HC to hear matter only relating to sentence - Appeal disposed of.
