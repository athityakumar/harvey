D. Ganesh Rao Patnaik and others v State of Jharkhand and others

Supreme Court of India

06 October 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

G.P. Mathur, R.C. Lahoti, P.K. Balasubramanyan

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 878; (2005) 8 SCC 454; (2005) SCC (L&S) 1171; AIR 2005 SC 4321; 2006 (2) JhrCR(SC) 91; JT 2005 (10) SC 261; 2005(8) SCALE 180; 2005 (6) SLR 425; [2005] Supp4 S.C.R. 102

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Uttar Pradesh Higher Judicial Service Rules, 1975, Bihar Superior Judicial Service Rules, 1946, Uttar Pradesh Forest Service Rules, 1952

Summary: [A.] Service - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 14, 233, 233(2), 309 - Bihar Superior Judicial Service Rules, 1946, rr. 1(2), 15, 16, 2, 3, 5, 6 - Uttar Pradesh Higher Judicial Service Rules, 1975, rr. 22(3), 22(4) - Issue raised is that of seniority between direct recruits and promotees in Bihar Superior Judicial Service, who are currently serving in State of Jharkhand - Promotion order passed in favour of contesting respondents on 30-04-1991 - Held, as no vacancy either permanent or temporary in quota meant for promotees was available on 30-04-1991, period of officiation/service rendered by contesting respondents till a vacancy in their quota became available to them, cannot be counted towards their seniority - Direct recruits senior to promotees - Respondents can under no circumstances claim seniority over appellants - Appeal allowed.
