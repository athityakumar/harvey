Bombay Electric Supply and Transport Undertaking v Laffans (India) Private Limited and Another

Supreme Court of India

21 April 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, AR. Lakshmanan, G.P. Mathur

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 312; (2005) 4 SCC 327; AIR 2005 SC 2486; 2005 (3) AWC 2133; 2005 (4) Bom.C.R. 587; 2005 (2) CPJ 6; 2005 (2) CPJ(Sc) 6; 2005 (3) JhrCR(SC) 76; JT 2005 (4) SC 538; 2005 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 702; 2005 (2) RCR(Civil) 587; 2005(4) SCALE 312; [2005] 3 S.C.R. 785

Case Digest

Subject: Electricity & Energy; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Apparatus, Supply Of Energy

Summary: [A.] Electricity & Energy - Indian Electricity Act, 1910, ss. 26(6), 26(7) - Indian Electricity Rules, 1956, r. 57 - Meter not correct - Notice of demand and disconnection - Whether applicability of s.26(6) is attracted to facts and circumstances of present case and, if so, to what extent? - Held, according s.26(4), licensee cannot take off or remove any such meter as to which difference or dispute of nature described in sub-section (6) has arisen until matter has been determined by Electrical Inspector - Appellant cannot now be allowed to raise an additional demand over and above demand raised through bills which were issued for that period and paid by first respondent - Appeal disposed of.
