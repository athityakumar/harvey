Umabai and Another v Nilkanth Dhondiba Chavan (D) By Lrs. and Another

Supreme Court of India

13 April 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 1038; (2005) 6 SCC 243; 2005 (60) ALR 463; 2005 (3) AWC 2948; 2005 (3) CalLT(SC) 87; JT 2005 (4) SC 292; 2005 (4) MahLJ 306; 2005 (4) MLJ(SC) 24; 2005 (141) PLR 341; 2005 (2) RCR(Civil) 659; 2005 (99) RD 338; 2005(4) SCALE 142; [2005] 3 S.C.R. 521; 2005 (Supp) Bom.C.R. 746

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Conditional Sale, Purchase-Money, Mortgage By Conditional Sale, Maharashtra Debt Relief Act, Special Relief Act, 1963

Summary: Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Maharashtra Debt Relief Act, ss. 4(e) and 7(f) - Transfer Of Property Act, 1882, s. 58(c) - Special Relief Act, 1963, s. 16(c) - Whether High Court was justified in holding that respondents were ready and willing to perform their part of contract, and thereby setting aside order passed in first appeal? - Inconsistent plea raised by respondent no. 1, categorically taken a standing that debt stood discharged, such a plea was irreconcilable with plea that he had all along been ready and willing to perform his part of contract - Normally, a court of appeal would not interfere with a concurrent finding of fact which is based on appreciation of oral evidence - Held, finding of Division Bench, cannot be accepted, as pleas of specific performance of contract and cancellation of debt and/or a decree for accounting are inconsistent - Appeal allowed.
