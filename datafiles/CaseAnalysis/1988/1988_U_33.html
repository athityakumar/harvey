Jain Exports (P) Limited And Anr. v Union Of India And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

05 May 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranganath Misra, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 936; (1988) 3 SCC 579; 1988 (2) CompLJ 188; 1988 CRLR 361; 1988 CrLR(SC) 361; 1988 (36) DLT 257; 1988 (17) ECC 49; 1988 (17) ECR 631; 1992 (61) E.L.T. 173; JT 1988 (2) SC 602; 1988(1) SCALE 1123; [1988] 3 S.C.R. 952; 1988 (71) STC 173; 1988 (2) UJ 125

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Customs; Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Summary: Customs - Constitution of India, 1950 - Customs Act, 1962, ss. 111(d) and 112 - Imports and Exports (Control) Act, 1947 - Imports (Control) Order, 1955, r. 3 - Whether at relevant time, import of coconut oil had become canalised through State Trading Corporation? - If 'coconut oil' of industrial variety was covered by paragraph 5 of Appendix 9, then it would not have been included in Appendix 10 and, therefore, could not have been imported under O.G.L. - Held, no breach in compliance of statutory procedure - Appeal dismissed.
