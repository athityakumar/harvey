Piyush Kantilal Mehta v Commissioner Of Police, Ahmedabad City And Another

Supreme Court of India

16 December 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

M.M. Dutt, S. Natarajan

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 48; (1989) Supp1 SCC 322; (1989) SCC (Cr) 438; AIR 1989 SC 491; 1989 CRLJ 956; JT 1988 (4) SC 703; [1988] Supp3 S.C.R. 1081

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Gujarat Prevention of Anti-Social Activities Act, 1985, ss. 2(b), 3, 3(2) and 12 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 22(5) and 32 - (A) Representation of detenu pending before Advisory Board - Writ petition u/art. 32 before Supreme Court by detenu - Maintainablity - (B) Detention order - Legality - Detenu signing his name as given in order of detention, when order was served on him - Detenu wrongly described in it - (C) Preventive detention - Public order - Activities prejudicial to - Proof, necessary - Bootlegger indulging in using force and beating people - (D) Grounds of detention vague - Statements of witnesses also vague and general thus not removing defect of vagueness of grounds - Validity of order - Held, (A) Maintainable - (B) Generally not create insecurity or panic in general public - (C) Detention not proper - (D) Order illegal - Petition allowed.
