State Of U.P. And Ors. v Renusagar Power Co. And Others

Supreme Court of India

28 July 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, S. Ranganathan

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 534; (1988) 4 SCC 59; AIR 1988 SC 1737; [1991] 70 Comp Cas 127; 1988 (3) CompLJ 1; JT 1988 (3) SC 141; 1988(2) SCALE 238; [1988] Supp1 S.C.R. 627; 1988 (2) UJ 572; 1990 (3) UPLBEC 1467

Case Digest

Subject: Electricity & Energy

Summary: Electricity and Energy - Industries (Development And Regulation) Act, 1951 - U.P. Electricity (Duty) Act, 1952, s.3(4), 3(1)(c) - Electricity duty - (A) Levy - Whether electricity energy was consumed by a Company from its 'own source of generation' - Lifting of corporate veil - Permissibility - (B) Rates - Fixation of different rates for different classes of consumers or granting exemption - Power is to be exercised by Govt. in public interest - (C) Nature of power of State Govt. to fix rates and grant exemption - Public interest is paramount factor to be taken into consideration - Price fixation - Held, is permissible and consumption of energy by the Company from its subsidiary was from its 'own source of generation' - Does not amount to excessive delegation - Prior opportunity of hearing need not be given - Appeal disposed of.
