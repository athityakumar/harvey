Maiku and Others v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

29 September 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

G.L. Oza, K. Jagannatha Shetty

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 243; (1989) Supp1 SCC 25; (1989) SCC (Cr) 117; AIR 1989 SC 67; 1988 BBCJ 168; 1988 (3) Crimes 638; 1989 CRLJ 860; 1989 CRLR 10; JT 1988 (4) SC 73; 1988(2) SCALE 1150

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Tort

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 147, 149, 300, 325 and 354 - Unlawful object - While pursuing investigation, police party resorted to violence - Suspect tried to escape - Beating - Death on spot - None of injuries sufficient to cause death in ordinary course of nature - No evidence to indicate individual acts - Provisions of ss. 147 and 149 of IPC, if could be invoked - Whether they could be entitled to acquittal? - Held, not pursuing unlawful object - They are entitled to acquittal - Appeal allowed.
