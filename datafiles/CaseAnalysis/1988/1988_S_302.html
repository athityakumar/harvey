Collector Of Customs, Bombay v Swastic Woollen (P) Ltd. And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

10 August 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, S. Ranganathan

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 161; (1988) Supp SCC 796; AIR 1988 SC 2176; 1989 CRLR 38; 1989 CrLR(SC) 38; 1988 (18) ECC 352; 1988 (18) ECR 373; 1988 (37) E.L.T. 474; JT 1988 (3) SC 558; 1988(2) SCALE 479; 1989 (72) STC 201; [1988] Supp2 S.C.R. 370; 1988 (2) UJ 604

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Summary: Customs - Imports and Exports (Control) Act, 1947, s.3 - Customs Act, 1962, ss. 2, 13E(b), 112, 130E, 111(d), 111(m) - Appeal u/s.130E of the Customs Act before the Supreme - (A) Ascertainment of nature of goods in absence of statutory definition - (B) Tribunal came to the conclusion that are classed as 'wool wastes' - The propriety and the validity of this finding is under challenge - Possibility of two views - Understanding of goods in opinion of those dealing with goods, held is safest guide - Interference is not warranted - Appeal rejected.
