Food Corporation Of India And Anr. v Great Eastern Shipping Co. Ltd.

Supreme Court of India

28 March 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, S. Ranganathan

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 419; (1988) 3 SCC 291; AIR 1988 SC 1198; 1988 (1) ARBLR 392; 1988 (4) Bom.C.R. 46; JT 1988 (2) SC 143; 1988(1) SCALE 786; [1988] 3 S.C.R. 366; 1988 (2) UJ 9

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration Act, 1940 - Charter Party Agreement - Joint Arbitration - A party appointed one its arbitrator with a specific condition that he shall give reasons for award - Award passed - Non-speaking award - Award filed in Bombay High Court - Objections raised by a party - (A) Whether court has jurisdiction to entertain award, as there was no cause of action arose in Bombay? - Held, specific finding made by single judge that agreement was signed at Bombay which was affirmed by division bench - No material to impeach finding - (B) Whether award is liable to be set aside for reasons of misconduct, irregularity and lack of competence? - Further held, no mandate given by both parties to arbitration agreement to both arbitrators to state reasons in award - Arbitrators cannot act on mandate of one of parties - Unreasoned award is bad - No legal misconduct as such in not giving reasons - Appeals disposed of.
