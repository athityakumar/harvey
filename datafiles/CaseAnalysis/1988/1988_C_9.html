Petron Engineering Construction Private Limited and Another v Central Board of Direct Taxes and Others

Supreme Court of India

13 December 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

M.M. Dutt, S. Natarajan

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 298; (1989) Supp2 SCC 7; AIR 1989 SC 501; 1989 (1) CompLJ 43; 1988 (75) CTR 20; 1989 (75) CTR 20; [1989] 175 ITR 523; JT 1988 (4) SC 666; 1988(2) SCALE 1556; [1988] 3 S.C.R. 1058; [1988] Supp3 S.C.R. 1058; [1988] 41 TAXMAN 294

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Finance Bill, Finance Act

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 80-O - Services to terminal project - Payment of fee - Approval of agreement - Refusal - Legality - Appellant Company and Toyo Engineering India Ltd.(T) entered into agreement - Appellant agreed to render technical services in respect of Iraqi Storage Terminal Project Installations in consideration of payment to it by way of fees payable under said agreements - Appellant company agreed to render technical services in respect of Iraqi Storage Terminal Project Installations in consideration of payment to it by way of fees payable under said agreements - Appellant requested respondent No. 1 for approval of said agreements u/s. 80-O of the Act - Respondent No. 1 refused to approve the said agreements - Appellant filed writ petition before HC - Single Judge dismissed the petition on ground inter alia that payment was not received by appellant-company from the Govt. of a foreign State or a foreign enterprise and, as such, it was not entitled to any relief u/s. 80-O of the Act - On appeal, DB upheld the judgment of Single Judge - Hence, instant appeal - Whether HC was justified in passing the impugned order - Held, it was manifestly clear from provision of s. 80-O of the Act that agreement should be entered into by and between assesse and Govt. of a foreign State or a foreign enterprise - In instant case no such agreement was entered into by appellant with Govt. of a foreign State or a foreign enterprise - In that respect also appellant did not fulfill another condition of s. 80-O of the Act which was also very material - Agreements which had not approved by Central Board of Direct Taxes had as already noticed entered into between appellant and T which was not a foreign enterprise but an Indian Company - There was no defect in provision of s. 80-O of the Act - It might be that Legislature had not provided for canalization but that could not be said to be a lacuna or a defect in provision - However, s. 80-O of the Act as providing canalization that was to say income by way of royalty commission etc. need not be received directly from Govt. of a foreign State or a foreign enterprise but through another Indian company - Appeal dismissed.
