Messrs Bakhtawar Singh Bal Kishan v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 February 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

M.P. Thakkar, N.D. Ojha

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 652; (1988) 2 SCC 293; AIR 1988 SC 1003; 1988 (2) AWC 894; 1988 (34) DLT 377; JT 1988 (1) SC 467; 1988(1) SCALE 527; 1988 (1) UJ 556

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Civil Procedure; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Code of Civil Procedure 1908

Summary: Arbitration and ADR - Arbitration Act, 1940 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Limitation Act, 1908 - CPC, 1908, s.20 - Institution of suit - Costruction contract with the MES (Military Engineering Services) for making some additional construction in the ordnance factory, entered into in U. P. - Work executed in U. P. - Award passed by Arbitrator - Proceedings instituted in Delhi High Court for making award a rule of Court - Jurisdiction - Held, Delhi High Court had no jurisdiction because the Union of India was not carrying on any 'business' in Delhi so as to attract s. 20 of the CPC, 1908 - Appeal disposed of.
