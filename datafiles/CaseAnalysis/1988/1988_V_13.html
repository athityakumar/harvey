Vidya Dhar Pande v Vidyut Grih Siksha Samiti And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

10 October 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

B.C. Ray, M.P. Thakkar

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 248; (1988) 4 SCC 734; (1989) SCC (L&S) 30; AIR 1989 SC 341; 1988 (8) ATC 778; 1989 (58) FLR 513; JT 1988 (4) SC 84; 1989 (1) LLJ 81; 1989 (1) LLN 210; 1988(2) SCALE 916; [1988] 3 S.C.R. 442; 1988 (5) SLR 709; [1988] Supp3 S.C.R. 442; 1989 (1) UJ 275

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Madhya Pradesh Madhyamik Shiksha Adhiniyam, 1965, Oil and Natural Gas Commission, Rajasthan Medical Service (Collegiate Branch) Rules, 1962

Summary: Service - Madhya Pradesh Madhyamik Shiksha Adhiniyam, 1965, s. 28(2)(d) - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 14, 16, 226 and 227 - Termination of service - (A) Whether regulations framed u/s. 28(2)(d) of Act have statutory force behind it - (B) Higher Secondary School - Received 100% grant from Govt. (despite of being run by private trust) - Writ jurisdiction over, if - Held, they have statutory force - It is amenable to writ jurisdiction - Appeal allowed.
