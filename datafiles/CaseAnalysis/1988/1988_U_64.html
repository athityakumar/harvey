Doypack Systems Private Limited and Others v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 February 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, G.L. Oza

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 695; (1988) 2 SCC 299; AIR 1988 SC 782; [1989] 65 Comp Cas 1; 1988 (1) CompLJ 225; 1988 (36) E.L.T. 201; JT 1988 (1) SC 304; 1988(1) SCALE 273; [1988] 2 S.C.R. 962; 1988 (2) UJ 54

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate

Keywords: Swadeshi Cotton Mills Company Limited (Acquisition and Transfer of Undertakings) Act, 1986

Summary: Textiles - Companies Act, 1956 - Swadeshi Cotton Mills Co. Ltd. (Acquisition and Transfer of Undertakings) Act, 1986, ss. 2(k), 3, 4, 7 and 8 - Constitution, art. 74(2) - Evidence Act,1872, s. 123 - Interpretation of statutes - Certain privileged cabinet notes u/art. 74(2) which lead to the preparation of official documents - For rendering advice to the President before enactment of act - Not relevant in deciding the issue - (A) Whether the Govt. can be asked to produce such documents in Court - (B) Whether in the light of ss. 3, 4, 7, 8, 2(k) - it can be understood that shares held by the company in other companies vest in Central Govt. - (C) Whether the doctrine of contemporanea expositio can be applied to ss. 3 and 4 of the Act - Govt. held, cannot be asked to produce such documents in the Court - Shares held by the company vest in Central govt. - The doctrine applies only to construction of ambiguous language in old statutes , hence refused - Order accordingly.
