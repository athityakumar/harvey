Bharat Singh and Others v State Of Haryana And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

13 September 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

M.M. Dutt, K.N. Singh

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 619; (1988) 4 SCC 534; AIR 1988 SC 2181; 1988 BBCJ 184; JT 1988 (4) SC 91; 1988(2) SCALE 890; [1988] Supp2 S.C.R. 1050

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, s.4(1) - Acquisition of agricultural land - Entitled to - In these appeals and writ petitions, the appellants challenged the validity of the acquisition of their land by the State/respondent under the Act for a public purpose, namely, for the development and utilisation of land for industrial purpose - - Appellant contended that non-publication of the substance of the notification u/s.4(1) of the Act in the locality could not be sustained - Appellant also contended that before starting the proceedings for acquisition, the Govt. had not applied its mind to its policy decision wherein it was stated that in the matter of State's need for land for its development activities, utmost restraint should be exercised in the acquisition of land - It was submitted that as the land was agricultural, it should not have been acquired in view of the said policy decision of the Govt. - Appellants further contended that they were discriminated inasmuch as the land of other persons in the village was not acquired - Held, with regard to first contention of appellant, in counter-affidavit filed by the Land Acquisition Collector, it was averred that the substance of the notification was published and out of 22 appellants 16 filed their objections pursuant to the publication of the notification in the locality - In the circumstances, there was no substance in the contention of appellants that the substance of the notification u/s. 4(1) of the Act was not published in the locality - With regard to second contention in welfare State, it was the duty of the Govt. to proceed with the work of development and took steps for the growth of industries which were necessary for the country's progress and prosperity and for solving the question of unemployment - It was true that agricultural land was necessary and should not ordinarily be converted to non-agricultural use, but keeping in view the progress and prosperity of the country, respondent had to strike a balance between the need for development of industrialisation and the need for agriculture - Allegation that before initiating the acquisition proceedings, Govt. did not apply its mind to the need for agricultural land was a very vague allegation without any material in support - As far as last contention was concerned Govt. would acquire only that amount of land which was necessary and suitable for the public purpose in question - Land belonging appellants were acquired obviously considering the same as suitable for the public purpose - Appellants could not complain of any discrimination because the land of other persons was not acquired by the Govt. - Appeals dismissed.
