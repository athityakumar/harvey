M. Veerappa v Evelyn Sequeira and Others

Supreme Court of India

13 January 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Natarajan, A.P. Sen

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 313; (1988) 1 SCC 556; AIR 1988 SC 506; 1988 (1) CCC 400; JT 1988 (1) SC 120; 1988 (1) KLT 450; 1988(1) SCALE 107; [1988] 2 S.C.R. 606; 1988 (1) ShimLC 251; 1988 (1) UJ 720

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Civil Procedure

Keywords: Code of Civil Procedure 1908, Legal Practitioners Act, 1879, Legal Practitioners (Fees) Act, 1926, Succession Act 1925

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 22 r. 3(1) - Professional misconduct - Suit for damages - Substitution - Legality - Respondent filed suit for damages against appellant (advocate) for professional misconduct and negligent in rendering professional services by filing appeal after considerable delay and withdrawing appeal instead of canvassing for its admission - Suit was laid against appellant for sum of Rs.6,000/- by way of damages and compensation - During pendency of suit respondent died and his legal representatives filed petition under O. 22 r. 3(1) CPC seeking their substitution in suit for prosecuting - District Judge dismissed the suit as abated - Respondents filed revision before HC for substitution in the suit - HC declared the legal representatives to be entitled to get impleaded and continue suit - Aggrieved, appellant filed instant appeal - Whether a suit for damages already instituted against a counsel had abated or not consequent on death of plaintiff - Held, legal practitioner who acted or agreed to act for any person might settle with said person terms of his engagement and fee to be paid for his professional services, that legal practitioner would be entitled under law institute and maintain legal proceedings against his client for recovery of any fee due to him under agreement or as per costs taxed by Court where there was no pre-settlement of fee - No legal practitioner who had acted or agreed to act should merely by reason of his status as a legal practitioner be exempt from liability to be sued in respect of any loss or injury due to any negligence in conduct of his professional duties - Therefore, a legal practitioner could not claim exemption from liability to be sued in respect of any loss or injury suffered by client due to any negligence in conduct of his professional duties merely by reason of his being a legal practitioner - Whether suit had abated or not could be answered only after nature of suit was determined on basis of materials placed and evidence adduced by parties - Appeal dismissed.
