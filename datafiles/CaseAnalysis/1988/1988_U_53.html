Srinivasa Gopal v Union Territory Of Arunachal Pradesh (Now State)

Supreme Court of India

18 July 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, S. Ranganathan

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 518; (1988) 4 SCC 36; (1988) SCC (Cr) 889; 1988 (2) ACC 462; 1988 (2) ACJ 1079; AIR 1988 SC 1729; 1988 BLJR 602; 1988 (2) Crimes 950; 1988 CRLJ 1803; 1988 CRLR 506; 1988 CrLR(SC) 506; JT 1988 (3) SC 342; 1988(2) SCALE 113; [1988] Supp1 S.C.R. 477; 1988 (2) UJ 593

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Constitution of India, 1950 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Rash and negligent driving - Offence of - Delay of 9 1/2 years for taking cognizance - Trial vitiated - With regard to nature of offence, enormous delay in proceeding with criminal prosecution by appellant - 9 1/2 years for a trial for rash and negligent driving, is too long a time - Quick justice is a sine qua non of art. 21 of Constitution - Keeping a person in suspended animation for 9 1/2 years without any cause at all cannot be with spirit of procedure established by law - Just and fair and in accordance with equity to direct that trial or prosecution of appellant to proceed no further - Trial would be vitiated and it could not be saved.
