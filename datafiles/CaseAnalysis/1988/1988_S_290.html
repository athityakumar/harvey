Dr. M. K. Salpekar v Sunil Kumar Shamsunder Chaudhari And Others

Supreme Court of India

10 August 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

L.M. Sharma, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 940; (1988) 4 SCC 21; AIR 1988 SC 1841; JT 1988 (3) SC 356; 1988 MahLJ 760; [1988] Supp2 S.C.R. 339

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Rent Control

Keywords: Central Provinces and Berar Letting of Houses and Rent Control Order, 1949

Summary: Land & Property - Rent Control - Central Provinces and Berar Letting of Houses and Rent Control Order, 1949, cl. 13(3)(v) - Whether sub-cl. (v) of cl. 13(3) of Order, 1949, applied to all buildings whether residential or non-residential, or was confined only to residential houses? - Held, expression 'house' used in Control Order in wider sense is retained and envisages two situations in which landlord becomes entitled to possession, namely, (i) where tenant secures alternative accommodation, and (ii) where he leaves area for a continuous period of four months - It cannot be reasonably suggested that by addition of Explanation, which is confined to cases dealing with residential buildings, a non-residential building is excluded even where tenant leaves area for a period of four months and does not need house - If position in regard to second category of cases remained unaffected, Explanation cannot be construed to narrow down scope of first category of cases where tenant secures alternative accommodation - Main sub-clause is not restricted to cases where tenant is owner of alternative building - It also applies where tenant gets alternative accommodation in another capacity, e.g., under a lease, or acquires right of residence for life under a bequest - It is, therefore, manifest that Explanation operates within a very narrow area and does not cover entire field governed by main sub-clause - Without Explanation there was some scope for controversy about precise meaning of expression 'has secured' in sub-clause, which by inclusive nature of Explanation is set at rest - It cannot be legitimately suggested that since majority of State legislatures have followed a particular policy, same must be presumed to be intention of author of Control Order in question before Court - When a Court is called upon to decide whether another building available to tenant can be treated as alternative accommodation, it has to consider whether other building is capable of reasonably meeting requirements of tenant on his vacating disputed premises - Appeal dismissed.
