Messrs Oswal Spinning and Weaving Mills Limited v Collector of Customs and Another

Supreme Court of India

19 April 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranganath Misra, B.C. Ray, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 448; (1988) 3 SCC 310; (1988) SCC (Cr) 670; 1988 CRLR 286; 1988 CrLR(SC) 286; 1988 (36) DLT 250; 1988 (17) ECC 74; 1988 (16) ECR 424; 1988 (35) E.L.T. 244; 1989 (14) ETR 106; JT 1988 (2) SC 135; 1988(1) SCALE 762; [1988] 3 S.C.R. 601; 1988 (2) UJ 98

Case Digest

Subject: Customs

Keywords: Sea Customs Act 1962, Sen Customs Act 1962

Summary: Customs - Customs Act, 1962, ss. 42, 43, 125 and 130-E - Confiscation of goods - Imposition of fine - Not traceable of goods - Legality - Appellants imported bales of woollen rags through State Trading Corporation from Canada - Customs authorities called upon appellants to show cause as to why same might not be confiscated under provisions of the Act - Collector of Customs directed confiscation of goods in exercise of power u/s. 111(d) and (m) of the Act but in lieu of confiscation, appellants were given option u/s. 125 of the Act to clear goods on payment of redemption fine of Rs. 50,000 - Department which sustained order of confiscation but reduced redemption fine to Rs.20,000 - Appellants filed revision before Appellate Tribunal - Appellate Tribunal dismissed the revision - Appellant filed appeal u/s. 130-E of the Act, before SC - SC directed waive of redemption fine - Appellants agreed to pay duty, as directed, when delivery was to be taken of goods - Appellants complained that a part of goods was not traceable - Appellants issued notice to Port Trust authorities/respondent No. 2 - Hence, instant appeal - Whether respondent dept. was liable for not traceable goods - Held, imported goods were not handed over to the custody of Port Trust - It was the Customs authorities who ware in possession and control of said imported bales of woollen rags and they could not shirk their responsibility for loss and damage of said goods and they were liable to pay value of goods to appellant as damages in order to re-compensate appellant - However, under the provisions of ss. 42 and 43 under the Act, Board of Trustees would be liable to recompense loss or damages in respect of goods which had took charge of by Board - In instant case as not a single document was produced before Court by Dept. Authorities showing that goods were handed over to custody and possession of Board of Trustees and that Board issued any receipt for that as required u/ss. 42 and 43 of the Act - Therefore, under no circumstances could Board of Trustees be held responsible for loss or destruction of said imported goods - Imported goods were kept unloaded in customs area and were confiscated and as such respondent No. 1 was liable for loss or damages that was caused to appellant by destruction of imported goods from their custody and possession - It was not possible for Court while hearing appeal u/s. 130-E of the Act against order of Appellate Tribunal to ascertain and determine money value of imported goods which were lost or destroyed from possession and custody of dept. authorities - Appellant might ay take appropriate proceedings for determination of damages and for recovery of same in accordance with law - Appeal disposed of.
