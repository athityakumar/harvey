Neelakantan And Bros. Construction v Superintending Engineer, National Highways,Salem And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

16 August 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, S. Ranganathan

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 556; (1988) 4 SCC 462; AIR 1988 SC 2045; 1989 (1) ARBLR 34; 1988 (2) CCC 492; JT 1988 (3) SC 743; 1988(2) SCALE 586; [1988] Supp2 S.C.R. 462; 1988 (2) UJ 625

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Arbitration Act, 1940 - Evidence Act, 1872 - Party acquiescing in appointment of arbitrator subsequently - Parties to reference either agree beforehand to method of appointment, or afterwards acquiesce in appointment made, with full knowledge of all circumstances - No legal proposition was made basis of award - Held, precluded from challenging appointment as invalidating proceedings - Precluded from objecting to such appointment as invalidating subsequent proceedings - Court could not review, award and correct any mistake in adjudication by arbitrator.
