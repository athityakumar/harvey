Burmah Shell Oil Distributing Now Known As Bharat Petroleum v Khaja Midhat Noor and Others

Supreme Court of India

03 May 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, S. Ranganathan

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 925; (1988) 3 SCC 44; AIR 1988 SC 1470; JT 1988 (2) SC 429; 1988 (1) RCR(Rent) 613; 1988 (2) RentLR 140; 1988(1) SCALE 1074; [1988] 3 S.C.R. 811; 1988 (2) UJ 148

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Rent Control - Land & Property - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, ss. 106, 107 and 116 - Lease deed - Sub lease - Notice to lessee terminating lease and for giving vacant possession of land - No separate notice was given terminating lease - Whether there was a valid termination of lease and as such sub lessee, petitioner was bound to deliver vacant possession? - Harmonious construction - Held, tenancy was automatically determined on expiry of ten years which was stipulated - If all paragraphs of notice are read together in harmony it would be manifest that lessee was directed to handover lease hold property - There was a valid notice of termination of lease of lessee - Petition dismissed.
