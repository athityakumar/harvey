Messrs Larsen and Toubro Limited, Club House v Trustees of Dharmamurthy Rao Bahadur Calavala Cunnan, Chetty's Charities By Its Trustees

Supreme Court of India

31 August 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Ranganathan, S. Mukharji

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 593; (1988) 4 SCC 260; JT 1988 (3) SC 684; 1989 (1) MLJ(SC) 15; 1988 (2) RCR(Rent) 395; 1988 (2) RentLR 695; 1988(2) SCALE 605; [1988] Supp2 S.C.R. 755; 1988 (2) UJ 702

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Land & Property - Tamil Nadu City Tenants' Protection Act, 1922, s. 9 - (A) Whether the lessee company is entitled to protection u/s. 9 of the Act and whether the lease was of of a building or of land? - Before a right of purchase can be exercised u/s. 9, the tenant must be a tenant of land, not comprising of buildings or lands appurtenant thereto; definition of 'building' in s. 2(1) means any structure whatever put up on land 'and includes the appurtenance thereto - Held, in the present case, the building is itself a substantial one occupying as many as two grounds, the use of the land was incidental to the enjoyment and beneficial use of the building and squarely fell within the definition and the lessee is not entitled to the rights conferred by s. 3 or s. 9 of the Act - (B) Whether the lease deed in the present case should be treated as creating two separate leases, one of the building and the other of the land and that the appellant would be entitled to exercise rights under the Act in respect of portion comprising of vacant land ? - Held, it is impossible to consider the document as comprising of two leases because the extent of land which has to go with the building as appurtenance and the extent of land which can be treated as separately demised cannot be defined; this was a composite lease of a building with appurtenant land - Appeal dismissed.
