West Bengal Hosiery Association And Ors. v State Of Bihar And Anr.

Supreme Court of India

11 August 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

M.H. Kania, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 919; (1988) 4 SCC 134; AIR 1988 SC 1814; 1989 (1) CalLT(SC) 9; JT 1988 (3) SC 347; 1988(2) SCALE 280; 1988 (71) STC 298; [1988] Supp2 S.C.R. 378; 1988 (2) UJ 614

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Sales Tax - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 301 and 304(b) - Bihar Finance Act, 1981, ss. 7 and 11(1) - Exemption from sales tax granted to hosiery goods manufactured in State - But hosiery goods manufactured outside and sold in State of Bihar are subjected to levy of sales tax - Constitutionality - Held, that such discrimination would restrict free flow of goods in state and will violate provisions of art. 301 r/w art. 304(b) of Constitution - Rule is made absolute.
