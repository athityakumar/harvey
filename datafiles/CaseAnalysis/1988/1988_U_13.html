Vidya Prakash v Union Of India & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

10 February 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

B.C. Ray, A.P. Sen

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 651; (1988) 2 SCC 459; (1988) SCC (L&S) 603; AIR 1988 SC 705; 1989 CRLJ 1205; 1988 (34) DLT 337; 1988 (56) FLR 505; JT 1988 (1) SC 284; 1989 LabIC 1205; 1988(1) SCALE 313; [1988] 2 S.C.R. 953; 1988 (1) SLR 605; 1988 (1) UJ 588

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Defence & Security Forces - Army Act, 1950, ss. 116, 39(a) and 71(e) - Army Rules, 1954, r. 39(2) - Dismissal from service - Appellant absent without sufficient reason - Was booked earlier for same reason - Summary Court-Martial against him - Whether Summary Court Martial having been convened by Commanding Officer of corps was illegal? - Commanding Officer of Corps, Department or Detachment of Regular Army to which appellant belongs, is quite competent in accordance with provisions of s. 116 of Act of 1950 and as such constitution of summary court martial by Commanding Officer of Corps cannot be questioned as illegal or incompetent - Held, in such circumstances, Summary Court Martial having been convened by Commanding Officer of corps according to provisions of Army Act, 1950 and is thus legal - (B) Whether infliction of punishment of dismissal from service is disproportionate with charge of absenting himself from duty without taking any leave from lines as required under Act of 1950? - After considering evidences it has been found appellant guilty of alleged charge and awarded said punishment in accordance with provisions of Army Act - Further held, said order of dismissal cannot be challenged as disproportionate to charge or as one tainted with illegality - (C) Whether at time of taking evidence of witnesses there was violation of principles of natural justice? - Further held, it appears from summary of evidences recorded that appellant in fact cross-examined prosecution witnesses - It is also evident from judgment of Delhi High Court that appellant admitted his guilt of absenting from duty without taking any leave - Further held, plea of violation of natural justice is without substance - Petition dismissed.
