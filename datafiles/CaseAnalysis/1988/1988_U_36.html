Ramesh.S/O Chotalal Dalal v Union Of India And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

16 February 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, S. Ranganathan

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 370; (1988) SCC (Cr) 266; (1988) 1 SCC 668; AIR 1988 SC 775; 1989 (1) Bom.C.R. 239; 1988 CRLR 276; 1988 CrLR(SC) 276; JT 1988 (1) SC 361; 1988(1) SCALE 327; 1988 (2) SCJ 70; [1988] 2 S.C.R. 1011; 1988 (2) UJ 107

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Media & Entertainment; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Examining Committee, Board Of Film Censors, Film Censor

Summary: Media and Entertainment - Cinematograph Act, 1952, s. 5B - Writ petition by an advocte seeking restrain on telecast or screening the serial titled 'Tamas' and to enforce petitioner's fundamental rights u/arts. 21 and 25 of the Constitution and declaring the screening or televising of 'Tamas' as violative of s. 5B of the Act - Prior approval for exhibition of T.V.serial by Film Censor Board - Doordarshan authorities scrutinising it, before screening - High Court also found that the picture viewed in its entirety, is capable of creating a lasting impression of this message of peace and co-existence and that people are not likely to be obsessed, overwhelmed or carried away by the scenes of violence or fanaticism shown in the film - Constitutionality of exibition - Held, would not affect public order or incite into commission of any offence - So, the telecasting or screening could not be restrained - Hence , exhibition is not violative of arts. 21 and 25 - Petition dismissed.
