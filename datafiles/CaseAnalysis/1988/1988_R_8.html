 A. R. Antulay v R. S. Nayak and Another

Supreme Court of India

29 April 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, S. Ranganathan, M.N. Venkatachaliah, Ranganath Misra, G.L. Oza, B.C. Ray

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 467; (1988) SCC (Cr) 372; (1988) 2 SCC 602; AIR 1988 SC 1531; 1988 (2) Crimes 753; 1988 CRLJ 1661; 1988 CRLR 366; 1988 CrLR(SC) 366; JT 1988 (2) SC 325; 1988 SCALE 52; [1988] Supp1 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Prevention Of Corruption Act, 1947, s. 6 - Indian Penal Code , 1860, ss. 384,420, 161,165,109,120(B) and 5 - Issues raised - (A) whether the case which is triable under the 1952 Act only by a Special Judge appointed under s. 6 of the said Act could be transferred to the High Court for trial by itself or by this Court to the High Court for trial by it? Held, yes - S. 406 of the Code deals with transfer of criminal cases and provides power to this Court to transfer cases and appeals whenever it is made to appear to this Court that an order under this section is expedient for the ends of justice - The law provides that this Court may direct that any particular case or appeal be transferred from one High Court to another High Court - (B) Whether this Court under Section 406 of the Code could have transferred a case which was triable only by a Special Judge to be tried by the High Court? - It was contended by Shri Rao that the jurisdiction to entertain and try cases is conferred either by the Constitution or by the laws made by Parliament - He referred us to the powers of this Court under arts. 32, 131, 137, 138, 140, 142 and 145(1) of the Constitution - He referred us to the previous order of this Court directing the transfer of cases to the High Court and submitted that it was a nullity because of the consequences of the wrong directions of this Court
