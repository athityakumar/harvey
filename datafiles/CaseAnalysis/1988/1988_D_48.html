Dr. Ajay Pradhan and another v State Of Madhya Pradesh & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

09 August 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

A.P. Sen, L.M. Sharma

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 607; (1988) 4 SCC 514; AIR 1988 SC 1875; JT 1988 (3) SC 295; 1988(2) SCALE 532; [1988] Supp2 S.C.R. 281; 1988 (2) UJ 485

Case Digest

Subject: Education; Health & Drug; Indirect Tax; Service

Summary: Constitution of India, 1950, art. 162 - Madhya Pradesh Selection for Post-Graduation Courses (Clinical), Para-clinical and Non-clinical Courses) in Medical Colleges of Madhya Pradesh Rules, 1984, r. 10 - Admission to PG medicine course - Seat falling vacant 'in midst of' or 'towards end' of academic year - State Govt. transferring seat reserved for one college to another, but not empowered to do so - (A) Right to admission - (B) Fulfillment of seats becoming vacant - There is no right to admission - Seat becoming vacant in 1986-87 cannot be filled subsequently in terms of r. 10 - Appeal dismissed.
