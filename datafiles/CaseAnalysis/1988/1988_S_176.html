J. R. Raghupathy and Others and Others v State of Andhra Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

28 July 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

A.P. Sen, B.C. Ray

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 536; (1988) 4 SCC 364; AIR 1988 SC 1681; 1988 (38) E.L.T. 225; JT 1988 (3) SC 313; 1988(2) SCALE 218; [1988] Supp1 S.C.R. 694

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 32 and 73 - Andhra Pradesh Districts (Formation) Act, 1974, s.3(5) - (A) Interference with administrative instructions by the Court - Govt.'s decision to locate Mandal head quarters at particular places - Interference by High Court on ground of breach of guidelines - Validity - (B) Scope of Executive powers of State and Union u/arts. 73 and 162 of Constitution of India in comparison to prerogative powers in England - Held, not warranted - Much wider than prerogative powers in England - Appeals allowed.
