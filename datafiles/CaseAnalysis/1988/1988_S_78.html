Union Of India & Ors. v Somasundram Viswanath & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

22 September 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

E.S. Venkataramiah, N.D. Ojha

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 640; (1989) SCC (L&S) 150; (1989) 1 SCC 175; AIR 1988 SC 2255; 1989 (9) ATC 263; JT 1988 (3) SC 724; 1989 LabIC 1228; 1988(2) SCALE 823; [1988] 3 S.C.R. 146; 1988 (5) SLR 174; [1988] Supp3 S.C.R. 146

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Keywords: Defence Service

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 73, 162, 309 and 311 - Indian Defence Accounts Service (Recruitment) Rules, 1958, rr.16, 18 - Office Memo D/-30-12-1976 - Service Rules - Quorum - Whether absence of one of the member of the Departmental Promotion Committee can vitiate a meeting held for recommending candidates for promotion - Held, proceeding not vitiated solely on account of this reason vis., that Secretary, Ministry of Defence, one of its members was not present - No repugnancy between provisions of 1958 Rules and office memo. - Appeal disposed of.
