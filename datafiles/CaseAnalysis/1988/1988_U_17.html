 Kehar Singh and Another v Union Of India And Anr.

Supreme Court of India

16 December 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Pathak, E.S. Venkataramiah, M.N. Venkatachaliah, N.D. Ojha, Ranganath Misra

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 47; (1989) SCC (Cr) 86; (1989) 1 SCC 204; AIR 1989 SC 653; 1989 BLJ 463; 1989 (1) Crimes 238; 1989 CRLJ 941; 1989 CrLR(SC) 112; JT 1988 (4) SC 693; 1988(2) SCALE 1565; [1988] 3 S.C.R. 1102; [1988] Supp3 S.C.R. 1102

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Separation Of Powers

Summary: Parliament & Legislature - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 21, 32, 72, 74, 161, 162, 134 and 226 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120 B, 121, 132, 302, 307 and 396 - Constitution - (A) Power to pardon - (1) Life and personal liberty - Attributes of civilised society - Order threatening/denying them - Power to scrutinise validity - In whom vested? - (2) Nature of and scope regarding scrutiny of evidences - (3) Order of President granting pardon - Justiciability - (4) Exercise of - Whether condemned person is not entitled to oral hearing from President - (5) Exercise of art. 72 provides sufficient guidelines - Need for Court to give certain guidelines - (B) Death penalty - Imposition, is exception rather than rule - Reconsideration of constitutional validity of provisions in IPC providing for sentence of death - Held, (A) (1) Belongs to people and is reposed in President of India - (2) Different from judicial power, granting pardon does not amount to amending or modifying or superseding judicial record and President can scrutinise evidence on record of case - (3) Not justiciable on its merits, nor reason can be asked, but area and scope of President's power u/art. 72 can be examined by Court - (4) Matter lies entirely within President's discretion - (5) No specific guidelines should be spelled out by Court - (B) Supreme Court declined to reconsider - Order accordingly.
