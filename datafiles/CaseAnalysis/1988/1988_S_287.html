Collector of Customs, Calcutta v Sun Industries

Supreme Court of India

11 April 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, Ranganath Misra

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 762; (1988) Supp SCC 342; 1988 (2) CompLJ 194; 1988 CrLR(SC) 326; 1988 (17) ECC 69; 1988 (17) ECR 6; 1988 (35) E.L.T. 241; JT 1988 (2) SC 131; 1988(1) SCALE 758; [1988] 3 S.C.R. 500; 1988 (71) STC 149

Case Digest

Subject: Customs

Keywords: Sea Customs Act 1962, Excise Duties Act Drawback Rules 1971

Summary: Customs - Customs Act, 1962, s. 75 - Sea Customs Act 1962 - Undelivered goods - drawback - dismissal order - Legality - Respondent/Sun Industries had shipped plywood panels for tea chests - Shipment of said goods was intended for delivery at Colombo under claim for drawback on said goods u/s. 75 of the Act against shipping bill - Due to some trouble in the ship, it returned back and ran aground in Indian territorial waters - Respondent applied for drawback u/s. 75 of the Act before Customs - Assistant Collector of Customs rejected the claim of respondent - Respondent appealed to Appellate Collector of Customs and same was rejected - Aggrieved by said order respondent preferred revision petition and same was transferred to Tribunal - Tribunal held that respondent was entitled to benefit of drawback scheme - Hence instant appeal - Whether Tribunal was right in passing impugned order - Held, expression 'place' would depend for its connotation on context in which it was used - In clause of charter party requiring charterer to procure safe 'place' for discharge of cargo, quoted word meant spot selected to drop anchor plus area over which tanker might swing on tide and charter's duty was not fulfilled merely by selecting area containing both safe and unsafe berths - Word 'place' as used in statute relating to searching for stolen goods in any store, shop, warehouse, or other building or place in a town, includes steam-boat or vessel moored at wharf - There was export of goods in terms of s. 75 of the Act - Fact that ship was brought back to India because of damages in ship does not, affect the position - Tribunal was right in view it took and respondent was entitled to benefit of s. 75 of the Act - Appeal dismissed.
