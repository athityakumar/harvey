V.K. Agarwal, Assistant Collector Of Customs v Vasantraj Bhagwanji Bhatia And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

07 April 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

M.P. Thakkar, K.N. Singh

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 501; (1988) 3 SCC 467; (1988) SCC (Cr) 679; AIR 1988 SC 1106; [1988] 64 Comp Cas 664; 1988 (2) Crimes 6; 1988 CRLJ 1106; 1988 CrLR(SC) 263; 1988 (16) ECC 164; 1988 (35) E.L.T. 26; JT 1988 (2) SC 39; 1988(1) SCALE 648; [1988] 3 S.C.R. 450; 1988 (2) UJ 115

Case Digest

Subject: Customs

Summary: Criminal - Customs Act, 1962 - Gold Control Act, 1968 - Constitution of India, 1950 - (A) Offence under Gold (Control) Act, 1968 - Does the acquittal of an accused charged with having committed an offence punishable u/s.111 r/s. with s.135 of the Customs Act, 1962 create a legal bar to the said accused subsequently being prosecuted u/s.85 of the Gold (Control) Act, 1968 - (B) CrPC, 1898 - Whether matter can be dropped due to inordinate delay in criminal trial - When period of 20 years elapsed since date of offence and High Court holding that subsequent trial was barred - Held, not barred, s. 403 of CrPC, 198 not attracted and accused sought to be tried in subsequent trial could not have been tried on same facts at former trial u/s.236 of CrPC, 1898 - Held, no ground for dropping the matter - Appeal allowed.
