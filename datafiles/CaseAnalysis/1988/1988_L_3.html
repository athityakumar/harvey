Scooters India Limited, Lucknow v Labour Court, Lucknow and Others

Supreme Court of India

30 September 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Pathak, S. Natarajan

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 247; (1989) Supp1 SCC 31; (1989) SCC (L&S) 180; AIR 1989 SC 149; 1988 (57) FLR 719; JT 1988 (4) SC 29; 1989 LabIC 1043; 1989 (1) LLJ 71; 1989 (1) LLN 303; 1988(2) SCALE 1266; 1988 (5) SLR 634; 1988 (2) UJ 532

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Uttar Pradesh Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 6(2 A) - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 11 A - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Bombay Industrial Disputes Act, 1938 - Company - Punishment for misconduct - Termination of service after disciplinary enquiry - Enquiry fair and lawful and findings not vitiated - Reformative approach - Despite that Labour Court interfere and ordered reinstatement - Whether illegal - Held, not illegal and arbitrary - Petition dismissed.
