Reliance Petrochemicals Limited v Proprietors of Indian Express News-Papers, Bombay Private Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

23 September 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, S. Ranganathan

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 642; (1988) 4 SCC 592; AIR 1989 SC 190; 1988 (2) CCC 546; [1989] 66 Comp Cas 577; 1988 (3) CompLJ 179; 1988 CRLR 778; 1988 CrLR(SC) 778; JT 1988 (3) SC 749; 1988(2) SCALE 748; [1988] 3 S.C.R. 212; [1988] Supp3 S.C.R. 212; 1989 TAXLR 66

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Freedom Of Press, Punitive Action, Broadcasting

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Practice & Procedure - Constitution (Forty-Fourth Amendment) Act, 1978 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Contempt of Courts Act, 1971 - Criminal contempt - News item published in 'Indian Express' - Article was related to subject matter of proceeding in Transfer Petition which was sub judice - Respondent contended that pre-stoppage of newspaper article or publication on matters of public importance was uncalled for - Held, publication pre-judging issue and thereby interfering with due administration of justice amounts to contempt - Order accordingly.
