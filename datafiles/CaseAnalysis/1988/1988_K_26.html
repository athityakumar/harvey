Kamla Devi and Others v Mool Raj

Supreme Court of India

15 April 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Ranganathan, A.P. Sen, L.M. Sharma

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 442; (1989) 2 SCC 722; (1991) SCC (Cr) 230; AIR 1990 SC 471; 1991 CRLR 357; 1989(2) SCALE 768A; 1990 (1) UJ 417

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Constitution - Uttar Pradesh Zamindari Abolition and Land Reforms Act, 1950, s. 20(b)(i) - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 133 and 226 - Sirdari or Adhivasi rights - Person recorded as sub-tenant in 1357 F - Status - Writ petition inter alia raising principal question, whether sub-tenant can claim Sirdari rights - Allowed by High Court - But question whether sub-tenants could not become Sirdar as objector was disabled landholder, not considered - Decision of HC reversed in appeal - Held, entitled to claim Adhivasi or Sirdari rights u/s. 20 - Supreme Court directed that writ petition be reheard by HC on this point - Appeal allowed.
