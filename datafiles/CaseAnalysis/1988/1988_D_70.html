Collector Of Central Excise, Chandigarh v Messrs Doaba Co-Operative Sugar Mills Limited

Supreme Court of India

16 August 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, S. Ranganathan

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 554; (1988) Supp SCC 683; AIR 1988 SC 2052; 1989 CRLR 36; 1989 CrLR(SC) 36; 1988 (18) ECC 157; 1988 (19) ECR 273; 1988 (37) E.L.T. 478; JT 1988 (4) SC 28; 1988(2) SCALE 477; [1988] Supp2 S.C.R. 458; 1988 (2) UJ 603

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Customs; Indirect Tax; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Excise - Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944, s.11A - Limitation Act, 1908 - Statutory appeal against the decision of CEGAT, u/s. 35-L(b) of the 1944 Act - Scope of s. 11A: provision would come into operation only when the demand is on account of central excise duty short levied or not levied or refunded erroneously - Departmental authorities seeking to recover excess amount paid to dealer - Held, authorities functioning under the Act are bound by the provisions of limitation prescribed in the Act - Appeal dismissed.
