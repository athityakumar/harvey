Madhu Gopal v Vi Additional District Judge And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

26 September 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, S. Ranganathan

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 233; (1988) 4 SCC 644; AIR 1989 SC 155; 1988 (14) ALR 713; 1988 (2) AWC 1387; JT 1988 (4) SC 106; 1988 (2) RCJ 627; 1988 (2) RCR(Rent) 488; 1988(2) SCALE 1273; [1988] 3 S.C.R. 276; [1988] Supp3 S.C.R. 276

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 136 - Uttar Pradesh Urban Buildings (Regulation of Letting and Eviction) Act, 1972, ss. 16(5) and 18 - Application is by tenant-petitioner - Premises in question had five co-owners - Thereafter, Rent Controller allowed application of respondent 3 and cancelled allotment order - Review application filed by tenant dismissed - Question arose about maintainability of review application u/s. 16(5) of Act - Held, High Court was right, s. 16(5)(a) speaks of 'where landlord or any other person' - Section has used expression "or" and so expression "or" is disjunctive of these two categories to be treated separately - Hence, requirement to be in lawful occupation, is not there in case of an application by landlord - Further held, it is a well settled principle of construction that unless clearly indicated, a proviso would not take away substantive rights given by section or sub-section - A landlord has a right to property - Section should not be so constructed as to defeat right to possession of property in appropriate cases unless intention of legislature is manifest - No such clear intention in facts of this case - HC came to correct conclusion that a landlord, even though not in actual physical possession at time of possession of property, can ask for review of order of release or allotment - Application dismissed.
