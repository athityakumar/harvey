Laxmi Raj Shetty And Anr. v State Of Tamil Nadu

Supreme Court of India

26 April 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

A.P. Sen, L.M. Sharma

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 69; (1988) 3 SCC 319; (1988) SCC (Cr) 633; AIR 1988 SC 1274; 1988 (2) BankCLR 223; 1988 (2) Crimes 107; 1988 CRLJ 1783; 1988 CrLR(SC) 305; JT 1988 (2) SC 180; 1988(1) SCALE 931; [1988] 3 S.C.R. 706

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Criminal; Trade

Summary: Criminal - CrPC, 1973, ss. 313, 366 - IPC, 1860, ss. 302, 392 and 449 - Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 3, 57, 78(2) and 81 - (A) Determination of extent of proof required when conviction on basis of circumstantial evidence - (B) Judicial notice of facts stated in newspaper - Held, circumstances should be fully established, consistent only with the hypothesis of the guilt of the accused, conclusive - And a statement of fact contained in a newspaper is merely hearsay and therefore inadmissible in evidence in the absence of the maker of the statement appearing in Court and deposing to have perceived the fact reported - Appeal dismissed.
