State Of Kerala v Attesee (Agro Industrial4L, Trading Corporation)

Supreme Court of India

27 October 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Ranganathan, S. Mukharji

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 258; (1989) SCC (Tax) 536; (1989) Supp1 SCC 733; AIR 1989 SC 222; 1989 (19) ECC 60; 1988 (38) E.L.T. 720; JT 1988 (4) SC 250; 1988(2) SCALE 1597; 1989 (72) STC 1; [1988] Supp3 S.C.R. 601

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Keywords: Additional Duties of Excise (Textiles and Textile Articles) Act, 1978, Declared Goods, Rate, Finance Act, 1961, Finance Act, 1969, Finance (No.2) Act, 1977, Finance (No2) Act, 1977

Summary: Sales Tax - Excise - Kerala General Sales Tax Act, 1963, ss. 9, Chap. 3, item. 7 - Central Excise and Salt Act, 1944, Sch. 1, item. 19 - Cotton Fabrics - Which definition would be applicable in order to be eligible for exemption? - Held, 'cotton fabrics' would be as defined in item. 19 of Sch. to Central Excises and Salt Act 1944 as amended in 1969 would be applicable - Scope of exemption available under item. 7 of Sch. 3 to 1963 Act will vary according to scope of corresponding entry in Schedule to 1944 Act as it stands at relevant time - Correct to say that 1963 Act brings in definitions of 1944 Act by way of reference or citation and not by way of incorporation - (B) Whether failure by High Court to specify substantial question of law would occasion failure of appeal? - Further held, no, It would not be rejected as defective as matter involves a substantial question of law - Appeal dismissed.
