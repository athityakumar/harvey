Bakulabai and Another v Gangaram and Another

Supreme Court of India

27 January 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

L.M. Sharma, Ranganath Misra

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 349; (1988) SCC (Cr) 189; (1988) 1 SCC 537; 1988 (1) AWC 389; 1988 (1) Bom.C.R. 537; 1988 (1) Crimes 587; 1988 CRLR 97; 1988 CrLR(SC) 97; 1988 (1) DMC 210; 1988 (1) HLR 381; JT 1988 (1) SC 197; 1988 (1) KLT 413; 1988 MahLJ 330; 1988(1) SCALE 188; [1988] 2 S.C.R. 787; 1988 (1) UJ 405

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Family & Personal; Practice & Procedure; Women & Children

Summary: (A)-Criminal P. C., 1973, Secs. 397 (3) and 125-Revision by wife for enhancement of rate of maintenance granted by Magistrate-Sessions Judge reversed the finding of Magistrate and dismissed the application for maintenance-Order challenged by wife before High Court by a revision application-Revision maintainable and not barred by Sec. 397 (3).

The main judgment of the Judicial Magistrate upholding the appellant's claim for maintenance was in her favour and there was no question of her challenging the same. Her challenge before the Sessions Judge was confined to the part of the order assessing the amount of maintenance, and this issue could not have been raised again by her. Subject to this limitation she was, certainly entitled to invoke the revisional jurisdiction of the High Court. The decision on the merits of her claim went against her for the first time before the Sessions Judge, and this was the subject matter of her revision before the High Court. She could not, therefore, be said to be making a second attempt when she challenged this order before the High Court. The fact that she had moved before the Sessions Judge against the quantum of maintenance could not be used against her in respect of her right of revision against the Sessions Judge's order. Held that the revision petition of the appellant before the High Court, except the prayer for enhancing the amount was maintainable.

(B)-Criminal P. C., 1973, Sec. 125.(1), Clauses (b) and (c)- Illigitimate child is entitled to maintenance.

(C)-Criminal P. C., 1973, Sec. 397-Findings on disputed question of fact were recorded by Magistrate after a full consideration of evidence-No error discovered in his judgment-Revisional court not justified in making a reassessment of evidence and substitute its own views for those of the Magistrate.

(D)-Criminal P. C., 1973, Sec. 125-Benefit of-Marriage of a Hindu woman with a Hindu male found to be null and void as the first marriage of Hindu male was subsisting-Such woman cannot claim the benefit of Sec. 125.
