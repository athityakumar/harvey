National Insurance Co. Limited, New Delhi v Jugal Kishore And Others

Supreme Court of India

09 February 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

N.D. Ojha, E.S. Venkataramiah

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 845; (1988) SCC (Cr) 222; (1988) 1 SCC 626; 1988 ACC 327; 1988 (1) ACJ 270; AIR 1988 SC 719; 1988 (1) CCC 691; [1988] 63 Comp Cas 847; 1988 CRLR 139; JT 1988 (1) SC 265; 1988(1) SCALE 268; [1988] 2 S.C.R. 910

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Corporate

Keywords: Unlimited

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1939, ss. 95(2)(b), 95 and 110 A - Motor vehicle accident - (A) Limit of liability of insurer - It is open to insurer to take policy covering higher risk - Essentials - (B) Claim filed - Insurance Company, to produce policy - Held, for this purpose specific agreement and payment of separate premium is necessary - Further held, insurance company must produce policy for doing justice and being instrumentality of State, it has to act fairly - Appeal allowed.
