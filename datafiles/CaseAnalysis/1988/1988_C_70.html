Commissioner of Income Tax, Allahabad v Dr. Nandlal Tahiliani

Supreme Court of India

10 March 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 656; [1988] 172 ITR 627

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income tax-Act, 1961, s. 132 - Held, articles and money seized have now been released and are no longer in the possession of the petitioner, no useful purpose will be served by entertaining instant petition. It is open to income-tax authorities to take such other proceedings against the respondent as are available under the Act. For that purpose, if it is otherwise permissible in law, it will be open to the income-tax authorities to take into account any information derived from the inventories prepared in consequence of the search and seizure made u/s. 132 of the Act and impugned in instant case. Petition dismissed.
