Smt. Sudha Devi v M.P. Narayanan And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

26 April 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

L.M. Sharma, A.P. Sen

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 462; (1988) 3 SCC 366; AIR 1988 SC 1381; 1988 (14) ALR 444; 1988 BBCJ 26; 1988 (2) CalLT(SC) 30; JT 1988 (2) SC 217; 1988 (1) RCR(Rent) 541; 1988 (1) RentLR 786; 1988(1) SCALE 952; [1988] 3 S.C.R. 756; 1988 (2) UJ 70

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice and Procedure - CPC 1908,O. 9 r. 13, O. 19 rr.1, 2 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 3 - Affidavits - Evidentiary value of - Whether witness (now deceased) was husband of plaintiff-appellant and thus he was fully conversant with relevant facts? - Held, no - Plaintiff cannot be allowed to fill up lacuna in evidence belatedly at SC stage - Besides, affidavit are not included in definition of 'evidence' in s. 3 of Evidence Act and can be used as evidence only if for sufficient reason court passes an order under O 19, r.1 or.2 of CPC - Appeal allowed.
