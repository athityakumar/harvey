Tarsem Lal Gautam And Another v State Bank Of Patiala And Others

Supreme Court of India

11 October 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

M.N. Venkatachaliah, Ranganath Misra

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 272; (1989) SCC (L&S) 139; (1989) 1 SCC 182; AIR 1989 SC 30; 1989 (1) BankCLR 1; 1989 (1) CLR 110; 1988 (57) FLR 746; JT 1988 (4) SC 184; 1989 LabIC 1138; 1989 (1) LLJ 39; 1989 (1) LLN 314; 1988(2) SCALE 924; [1988] 3 S.C.R. 479; 1989 (5) SLR 386; [1988] Supp3 S.C.R. 479

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: State Bank of Patiala (Officers) Service Regulations 1979

Summary: Constitution - Service - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - State Bank of India (Subsidiary Banks) Act, 1959, s. 63 - State Bank of Patiala (Officers) Service Regulations, 1979, Regn. 6 and 7, sch. 1 item 3 - Revision of pay - Violation of art. 14 - Bifurcation of officers of Grade A - Principle of classification amongst existing officers Grade A for purpose of fitment in new dispensation brought about by statutory regulations - This is not an instance to which principle of 'equal pay for equal work' could straightway be applied - Qualitative differences in regard to degrees of reliability and responsibility cannot be put aside as irrelevant - Not unreasonable and arbitrary requiring to be struck down as violative of art. 14 - Particular date 31-12-1975 was not sole basis for bifurcation of Officers of Grade A into two groups - For purpose of fitment in revised pay scales 'Regulations' did not bring about a mere revision of pay - Petition dismissed.
