Indru Ramchand Bharvani and Others v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

22 July 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, S. Ranganathan

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 526; (1988) 4 SCC 1; (1988) SCC (Cr) 867; [1989] 65 Comp Cas 227; 1989 (3) Crimes 445; 1988 CrLR(SC) 529; 1988 (18) ECC 1; 1988 (19) ECR 295; 1992 (59) E.L.T. 201; JT 1988 (3) SC 732; [1988] Supp1 S.C.R. 544; 1988 (2) UJ 565

Case Digest

Subject: Customs

Keywords: Customs Officer, Currency, Fair Hearing, Collector Of Customs, Indian Currency, Smuggled Goods, Sea Customs Act 1962

Summary: Customs - Customs Act, 1962, ss. 110, 123, 112 - Possession of large quantity of diamonds - Confiscation - Sustainability - Petitioner No. 3 was a firm engaged in the business of diamonds and precious stones - Customs officers received secret information that smuggled diamonds were kept by petitioner Nos. 1 and 2 in said premises - On search, Customs officers found over 2,800 carats of rough diamonds and over 400 carats of cut and polished diamonds in addition to a lot of other items of precious stones, pearls, gold manufactures etc. - On being asked to produce evidence of legal acquisition, import and possession of diamonds, petitioners Nos. 1 and 2 showed their inability to produce any such documents - Customs officers formed a prima facie belief u/s. 110 r/w s. 123 of the Act that unaccounted diamonds were smuggled goods - They seized various goods including cut and published diamonds and rough diamonds in all valued at Rs.54,42,882,02 u/s. 110 of the Act on reasonable belief that goods were smuggled into India - They also seized Indian currency of Rs.1.40 lakhs and some other incriminating documents found in premises - Show-cause notice was issued on 9-5-1980 - Subsequently, Collector of Customs by his order directed release of jewellery but ordered absolute confiscation of various other goods including diamonds in question and also imposed a penalty of Rs.65 lakhs on petitioners u/s. 112 of the Act - A penalty of Rs.25 lakhs each was imposed on the firm and petitioner No. 1 and a penalty of Rs. 15 lakhs was imposed on petitioner No. 2 - On appeal, Collector of Customs dismissed the appeal - On appeal, Tribunal confirmed order of confiscation in respect of both diamonds and held that seizure of diamonds was in the reasonable belief that these were smuggled goods and consequently onus of proof, according to s. 123 of the Act, was on petitioners and they had failed to discharge it in respect of seized diamonds - Aggrieved by said order, petitioners filed writ petition before HC - HC by impugned judgment dt.16-5-1988 dismissed petition - Hence instant application - Whether there was material for forming an opinion as to reasonable belief u/s. 110 r/w s. 123 of the Act - Held, HC was rightly found that there was evidence to presume that goods in question were smuggled - A large quantity of diamonds was found in possession of the petitioners - No trustworthy evidence, documentary or oral, was produced in favour of petitioners as to its legal acquisition/importation or possession - HC came to a conclusion that there was reasonable belief that diamonds were smuggled and there was nothing on record to show that profit element was lacking - Further, there was ample material, their existence could not be disputed - There was certainly a nexus between these materials and formation of the belief that goods were liable to confiscation - In the light of the above s. 110 r/w s. 123 was fully complied with - There was no denial of opportunity, the proceedings followed excluded the possibility of denial of opportunity - Proceedings taken were in order and in consonance with natural justice - HC was right in answering that Tribunal was justified in holding that seizing Customs officer had adequate material to form reasonable belief as contemplated u/s. 110 r/w s. 123 of the Act and it rightly held that appellants had failed to discharge the onus - Reasonable and fair hearing was afforded to petitioners, hence, it could not be accepted that there was legitimate cause of grievance - Application dismissed.
