Hiralal Kapur v Prabhu Choudhury

Supreme Court of India

19 February 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Ranganathan, Ranganath Misra, S. Mukharji

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 342; (1988) 2 SCC 172; AIR 1988 SC 852; 1988 (34) DLT 297; JT 1988 (1) SC 371; 1988 (2) RCJ 1; 1988 (1) RCR(Rent) 240; 1988 (1) RentLR 319; 1988(1) SCALE 360; [1988] 2 S.C.R. 1058; 1988 (1) UJ 475

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - (A) Delhi Rent Control Act, 1958 - (1) Provision of s. 4(1) Proviso (e) - Inference of creation of separate tenancy - Original tenancy in favour of tenant only but tenant choosing to pay rent by two cheques, one drawn by himself and another drawn in name of a trust - Landlord aware of certain activities of trust being carried from premises - His letter refused to recognise trust as tenant - Held, trust cannot be considered tenant of premises or any part thereof - (2) Provision of s. 25 B(8) - Revisionary powers of High Court - As to findings of fact - Further held, findings of fact should not be reversed - (3) Provision of s. 14(1) Proviso (e) - Bona fide need of residence for lawyer - Need of a room for use as office - Further held, not a consideration extraneous to scope and content of proviso - (B) Constitution of India, 1950, art. 133 - Appeal before Supreme Court - New plea - Point that premises were used by a trust and, hence for non-residential purpose - Plea not taken before HC - Special leave petition against order of HC dismissed - Further held, party cannot raise plea in question - Appeal allowed.
