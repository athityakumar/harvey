Tata Tea Limited And Anr. Etc. v State Of West Bengal And Ors. Etc.

Supreme Court of India

05 May 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

M.H. Kania, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 779; (1988) Supp SCC 316; AIR 1988 SC 1435; 1988 (70) CTR 99; [1988] 173 ITR 18; JT 1988 (2) SC 299; 1988(1) SCALE 867; [1988] 3 S.C.R. 961; 1988 TAXLR 1299; [1988] 39 TAXMAN 76

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Bengal Agricultural Income Tax Act, 1944, Bengal Agricultural Income Tax (Amendment) Act 1980, Kerala Agricultural Income Tax Act, 1950, Kerala Agricultural Income Tax (Amendment) Act 1980

Summary: Income Tax and Direct Taxes - Bengal Agricultural Income Tax (Amendment) Act 1980 - Bengal Agricultural Income Tax Act, 1944 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Government of India Act, 1935 - Income Tax Act, 1961 - Kerala Agricultural Income Tax (Amendment) Act 1980 - Kerala Agricultural Income Tax Act, 1950 - Uttar Pradesh Sales Tax Act , 1948 - Income Tax Act and Rules - Agricultural income tax - (A) On sale of tea grown and manufactured - Levy of - (B) Determination of meaning of term 'agricultural income' in art. 366(1) - (C) Legislative competency as to r.8 of the Income Tax Rule, 1962 - Held, it can be levied only on 60% of income derived by assessee from sale of tea notwithstanding amendments effected by State legislatures of Bengal and Kerala in 1980 - Provision of r. 8 can be taken into account in considering meaning of the term - Rule not beyond scope of rule making power - Order accordingly.
