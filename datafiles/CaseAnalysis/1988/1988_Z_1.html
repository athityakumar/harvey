Roshan Singh and Others v Zile Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

24 February 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

A.P. Sen, B.C. Ray

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 939; AIR 1988 SC 881; 1988 (34) DLT 317; 1989 (2) MLJ(SC) 18; [1988] 2 S.C.R. 1106

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Family & Personal - Registration Act, 1908, ss. 17(1)(b) and 49 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 145 - Appeal for declaration and injunction and an alternative for partition - (A) Whether document exhibit P-12 is an instrument of partition and requires registration u/s. 17 of Act? - Held, s. 17(1)(b) lays down that a document for which registration is compulsory should, by its own force, operate or purport to operate to create or declare some right in immovable property - Therefore, a mere recital of what has already taken place cannot be held to declare any right and there would be no necessity of registering such a document - Exhibit P-12 is a mere list of properties allotted at a partition and it cannot be construed to be an instrument of partition - Hence, document exhibit P-12 was a mere list of properties allotted to shares of parties - (B) Whether plaintiffs were owners in possession of portion marked B2 as delineated in plaint map? - Held, plaintiffs' ancestor on his part got a smaller house called baithak marked B1, also got ghers marked B2 to B5 - Plaintiff's claim that parties have remained in separate exclusive possession of their respective properties - However in February, 1971 plaintiffs wanted to raise construction over gher marked B2 and started constructing a boundary wall - Defendants Nos. 1-3, however, demolished wall - Taking an overall view it is clear that gher marked B2 belonged to plaintiffs - Appeal dismissed.
