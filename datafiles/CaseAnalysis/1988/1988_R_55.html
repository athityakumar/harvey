Smt. Yamunabai Anantrao Adhav A v Ranantrao Shivram Adhav And Another

Supreme Court of India

27 January 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

L.M. Sharma, Ranganath Misra

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 13; (1988) SCC (Cr) 182; (1988) 1 SCC 530; AIR 1988 SC 644; 1988 (1) AWC 350; 1988 (1) Bom.C.R. 541; 1988 (1) Crimes 594; 1988 CRLJ 793; 1988 CrLR(SC) 112; 1988 (1) DMC 205; 1988 (1) HLR 375; JT 1988 (1) SC 193; 1988 (1) KLT 416; 1988 MahLJ 335; 1988(1) SCALE 184; [1988] 2 S.C.R. 809

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Keywords: Legality

Summary: Family & Personal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 125 - Maintenance - Dismissal - Legality - Appellant was married to respondent no. 1 - Appellant filed application for maintenance before Trial Court and the same was dismissed - On appeal before HC the same was dismissed - Hence instant appeal.

Held, it is only where an applicant establishes her status on relationship with reference to personal law that an application for maintenance can be maintained. Once the right under the section is established by proof of necessary conditions mentioned, it cannot be defeated by further reference to the personal law. The issue whether section is attracted or not cannot be answered except by reference to appropriate law governing the parties. For purpose of extending benefit of section to a divorced woman and an illegitimate child Parliament considered it necessary to include in section specific provisions to that effect, but has not done so with respect to women not lawfully married. It was urged that appellant was not informed about respondent's marriage with 'L' when she married respondent who treated her as his wife, and, therefore, her prayer for maintenance should be allowed. There is no merit in point either. Appellant cannot rely on principle of estoppel so as to defeat provisions of the Act. So far as respondent treating her as his wife is concerned, it is again of no avail as issue has to be settled under the law. It is intention of the legislature which is relevant and not attitude of the party. Marriage of a woman in accordance with Hindu rites with a man having a living spouse is a complete nullity in eye of law and she is not entitled to the benefit of s. 125 of CrPC. Appeal dismissed.
