Tata Iron And Steel Co. Limited v Union Of India And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

06 May 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

M.H. Kania, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 493; (1988) SCC (L&S) 381; (1988) 3 SCC 403; AIR 1988 SC 1269; 1988 (17) ECC 62; 1988 (17) ECR 401; 1988 (35) E.L.T. 605; JT 1988 (2) SC 581; 1988(1) SCALE 985; [1988] 3 S.C.R. 1023; 1988 (71) STC 112; 1988 (2) UJ 308

Case Digest

Subject: Excise

Keywords: Tariff Item, Medicinal and Toilet Preparations (Excise Duties) Act, 1955

Summary: Excise - Medicinal And Toilet Preparations (Excise Duties) Act, 1955 - Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944, sch.1 items 26AA(ia) and 68, s 11A - Excise Duty - Forged steel products - (A) When weight for purpose of levy of duty to be taken - (B) Supplied to railways - After manufacturing , polishing and machining but in rough machined condition - Products put to use only after precision machined by Railway - No new commercial commodity arise - If liable to duty under Item 68 also - (C) Non-levy of exicse duty - On goods manufactured by assessee under certain Item - Not attributable to any fraud or misstatement - Demand - Period for - Hel, to be taken after machining and polishing of product is done to remove excess surface skin - Held, not liable - Period for which demand could be raised is only 6 months prior to notice - Appeal allowed.

.
