Vijay Kumar v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

24 February 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

M.M. Dutt, K. Jagannatha Shetty

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 63; (1988) 2 SCC 57; (1988) SCC (Cr) 293; AIR 1988 SC 934; [1989] 65 Comp Cas 568; 1988 (3) Crimes 50; 1988 CRLJ 951; 1988 (34) DLT 306; 1988 (17) ECC 82; 1988 (35) E.L.T. 15; JT 1988 (1) SC 448; [1988] 3 S.C.R. 42

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Comments, Continuous

Summary: Criminal - Detention - Conservation of Foreign Exchange and Prevention of Smuggling Activities Act, 1974,ss. 3 and 10 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 22 - Customs Act, 1962,s. 135 - Appellant allegedly engaged in receipt, storage and disposal of smuggled gold on a large scale - Advisory Board and Central Govt. ordered detention of appellant - Appellant was already in detention u/s. 135 of Customs Act, 1962 - High Court dismissed challenge to order of detention - (A) Whether order of detention was justified? - Held, yes - When a detenu is under detention for an offence, whether bailable or non-bailable, detaining authority will take into his consideration fact of detention of detenu - Compelling reasons to justify his preventive detention in spite of his detention for a criminal offence - (B) Whether it is necessary for concerned authority to give special reasons for directing detention for maximum period prescribed under Act? - If Advisory Board reports that there is in its opinion sufficient cause for detention of person, concerned authority may confirm and continue detention of person for such period as it thinks fit - Authority is not required to give any special reason either for fixing a shorter period or for fixing maximum period prescribed u/s. 10 - Opinion of Advisory Board and grounds of detention are only basis for confirming and continuing detention, for any period, even up to maximum period prescribed - S. 11 provides for revocation of detention order - Detention order may at any time be revoked or modified - When power to revoke order of detention could be exercised at any time, it is not necessary for authority to articulate special reasons for continuing detention for any period much less for maximum period prescribed under Act - Order accordingly.
