Registrar. High Court Of Madras Etc. v R. Rajiah And K. Rajeswaran

Supreme Court of India

11 May 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

M.M. Dutt, R.S. Pathak, L.M. Sharma

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 809; (1988) 3 SCC 211; (1988) SCC (L&S) 743; AIR 1988 SC 1388; 1988 (57) FLR 161; JT 1988 (2) SC 567; 1988 LabIC 1643; 1988 (2) LLN 571; 1989 (1) MLJ(SC) 49; 1988(1) SCALE 1034; 1988 (3) SLR 113; [1988] Supp1 S.C.R. 332; 1988 (2) UJ 294

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Constitution

Keywords: Power and control of HC

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 226, 235 - Fundamental Rules, r. 56(d) - Subordinate judicial service - Compulsory retirement - Power and control of HC - Legality - Respondents (petitioners) were District Munsif - HC by passed an order compulsorily retiring them from service r/r. 56(d) of Fundamental Rules, which was communicated to them by appellant (Registrar) - Aggrieved respondents filed petitions u/art. 226 of Constitution - DB by impugned judgment quashed orders of compulsory retirement of respondents - Hence, instant appeals - Whether impugned judgment passed by DB was justified - Held, u/r. 56(d) of Fundamental Rules, State Govt. or the Governor being the appointing authority, has the absolute power to retire a District Munsif - Test of control was not the passing of an order against a member of subordinate judicial service, but decision to take such action - It might be that so far as the members of subordinate judicial service were concerned, it was the Governor, who being appointing authority, had to pass an order of compulsory retirement or any order of punishment against such a member - But passing or signing of such orders by Governor would not necessarily take away the control of HC vested in it u/art. 235 of Constitution - An action against any Govt. servant consisted of 2 parts - Under first part, a decision would have to be made whether an action will be taken against the Govt. servant Under the second part, the decision would be carried out by a formal order - Power of control envisaged u/art. 235 of Constitution related to power of making a decision by HC against a member of subordinate judicial service - Such a decision was arrived at by holding an enquiry by HC against the member concerned - After the HC come to the conclusion that some action either in nature of compulsory retirement or by imposition of a punishment, had to be taken against the member concerned, HC would make a recommendation in that regard to Governor and Governor would act in accordance with such recommendation of HC by passing an order in accordance with the decision of HC - Governor could not take any action against any member of a subordinate judicial service without, and contrary to, recommendation of HC - In instant case, admittedly, impugned orders of compulsory retirement were passed by HC u/r. 56(d) of Fundamental Rules - U/r. 56(d) of Fundamental Rules right of compulsory retirement was conferred on appropriate authority which, under Explanation I, means the appointing authority, that was, the Governor - While HC decided to compulsorily retire respondents, it did not communicate the recommendations to Governor for passing formal orders of compulsory retirement - Instead, HC passed orders of compulsory retirement itself - Absolute right to compulsorily retire a Govt. servant conferred on Governor by r. 56(d) of Fundamental Rules should be subject to power of control of HC, so far as members of subordinate judicial service were concerned - DB was perfectly justified in quashing impugned orders of compulsory retirement - Appeals dismissed.
