D.K. Agarwal v High Court Of Judicature At Allahabad

Supreme Court of India

11 May 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

M.M. Dutt

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 810; (1988) 3 SCC 764; (1988) SCC (L&S) 886; AIR 1988 SC 1403; 1988 (14) ALR 650; 1988 (2) AWC 852; 1988 (57) FLR 135; JT 1988 (2) SC 465; 1988 LabIC 1658; 1988 (2) LLN 903; 1988(1) SCALE 1043; 1988 (2) SLR 558; [1988] Supp1 S.C.R. 317; 1988 (2) UJ 290

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Land & Property; Service

Keywords: Uttar Pradesh Higher Judicial Service Rules, 1975

Summary: Service - Constitution, arts. 136 and 227 - UP Higher Judicial Service Rules, 1975, rr. 27A and 4B - Service Rules - Member of Higher Judicial Service - (A) Grant of super time scale - Matter exclusively within administrative jurisdiction of HC - SC's interference sought - (B) Communication of adverse remarks - Allegations made in adverse entry found to be baseless by Chief Justice - On facts of the case, SC interfere and directed grant of super time scale to appellant - Such entry should not be communicated to concerned judicial officer - Appeal allowed.
