U.P.Bhoodan Yagna Samiti,U.P. v Braj Kishore And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

09 September 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

G.L. Oza, K. Jagannatha Shetty

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 616; (1988) 4 SCC 274; AIR 1988 SC 2239; JT 1988 (3) SC 640; 1988(2) SCALE 571; [1988] Supp2 S.C.R. 859; 1988 (2) UJ 744

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land and Property - U.P. Bhoodan Yagna Act, 1953, ss. 14, 15 - Interpretation of Statutes - Determination of scope of words 'landless persons' - Whether the settlement made by the Bhoodan Yagna Samiti in favour of the respondents was in accordance with law or which was not in accordance with law and therefore Additional Collector was right in setting aside those allotments - Rule of interpretation which had been generally accepted in later part of 19th century and the first half of 20th century was that the word should be given its plain ordinary dictionary meaning - Purpose of the movement of Bhoodan Yagna which was started by late Acharya Vinoba Bhave and followed by J.P. Narain and in the light of the Act - Held, do not cover persons who admittedly do not have land in village but are traders and have properties in cities - Appeal allowed.
