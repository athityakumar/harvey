Mahalakshmi Oil Mills and Others v State of Andhra Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 September 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Ranganathan, S. Mukharji

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 622; (1989) 1 SCC 164; AIR 1989 SC 335; 1988 (38) E.L.T. 714; JT 1988 (4) SC 161; 1988 (71) LabIC 285; 1988(2) SCALE 838; 1988 (71) STC 285; [1988] Supp2 S.C.R. 1088

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Andhra Pradesh General Sales Tax Act, 1957 - Exemption for goods specified in fourth schedule - Entry 7 reads as 'tobacco and all its products' - Whether tobacco seed oil and tobacco seed cake are entitled to exemption under Act? - Definition requires that item in question should be a form of tobacco seed that is manufactured - While leaves, stalks and stems even after manufacture retain form of tobacco, complete metamorphosis of seed on its manufacture renders it impossible to describe oil and cake as a form of tobacco seed - Categories of unmanufactured tobacco enumerated in entry in Schedule include 'stalks' but not 'seeds' - Held, High Court was right in holding that tobacco seed once it is separated from plant, is an item entirely different from tobacco and does not fall within expression 'tobacco or any form of tobacco' - Appeals and petitions dismissed.
