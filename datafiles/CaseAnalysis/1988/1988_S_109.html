Hare Krishna Singh and Others v State Of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

24 February 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

M.M. Dutt, K. Jagannatha Shetty

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 865; (1988) 2 SCC 95; (1988) SCC (Cr) 279; AIR 1988 SC 863; 1988 (2) AWC 776; 1988 (2) Crimes 927; 1988 CRLJ 925; 1988 CrLR(SC) 203; JT 1988 (1) SC 423; 1988(1) SCALE 405; [1988] 3 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: All India Services (Death-Cum-Retirement Benefits) Rules, 1958

Summary: Criminal - IPC, 1860, ss. 300, 34, 379 - Murder - (A) Whether it is an invariable rule that whenever an accused sustains an injury in the same occurrence, the prosecution is obliged to explain the injury and on the failure of the prosecution to do so, the prosecution case should be disbelieved - (B) Validity of conviction of an accused whose name was not stated in F.I.R. - Only a description that accused B's nephew who is in military service - No evidence produced as to him being in military or that no other nephew in such service- His complicity in crime also not proved - (C) Common intention - Attack by group - Eye-witnesses named the persons who had fired - And no over act attributed to other accused - Held, not necessary in each and every case - His conviction is unsustainable - Held, it could not be said that other accused had shared common intention to murder with accused who had opened fire and conviction set aside - Appeal allowed..
