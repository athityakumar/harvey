Union Of India And Ors. v K.S. Subramanian

Supreme Court of India

15 December 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Jagannatha Shetty, B.C. Ray, G.L. Oza

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 299; (1989) Supp1 SCC 331; (1989) SCC (L&S) 404; AIR 1989 SC 662; 1989 (10) ATC 513; 1989 (58) FLR 199; JT 1988 (4) SC 681; 1989 LabIC 1005; 1989 (1) LLN 310; 1988(2) SCALE 1546; [1988] 3 S.C.R. 1074; 1989 (3) SLR 713; [1988] Supp3 S.C.R. 1074; 1989 (1) UJ 165

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Service

Keywords: Civil Services (Classification, Control and Appeal) Rules, Central Civil Services (Classification, Control and Appeal) Rules 1965

Summary: Civil - Service - Constitution of India - Arts. 310 and 311 - Respondent appointed as ordinary industrial labourer but his services were terminated without assigning reason - Whether respondents case is to governed by Article 311 of Constitution - Held, No - Respondent was working in the Defence Department - Respondent not a civil servant and is deprived from protection conferred by Art. 311 of the Constitution - Appeal dismissed.
