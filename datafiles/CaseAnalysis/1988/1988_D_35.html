Dilbagrai Punjabi v Sharad Chandra

Supreme Court of India

08 August 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

L.M. Sharma, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 803; (1988) Supp SCC 710; AIR 1988 SC 1858; 1988 (2) CalLT(SC) 34; 1988 (2) CCC 441; JT 1988 (3) SC 308; 1988 (2) RCR(Rent) 261; 1988(2) SCALE 523; [1988] Supp2 S.C.R. 276; 1988 (2) UJ 512

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Madhya Pradesh Accommodation Control Act, 1961 - Eviction - Legality - Appellant/tenant was in possession of shop - Respondent/landlord filed suit before Trial Court for his eviction on ground of personal necessity - Trial Court dismissed the Suit - Respondent appealed to Appellate Court and same was dismissed - Aggrieved by said order respondent filed appeal before HC - HC had reversed decision and passed a decree in favor of respondent - Hence instant appeal - Whether HC was justified in passing impugned order - Held, respondent also produced counter foil receipts signed by appellant in which respondent was described as 'the owner of property' - It was not case of an isolated single receipt quite a number of such documents were produced before Trial Court - HC was right in pointing out that Lower Courts had seriously erred in not considering entire evidence on record - It was true that HC while hearing appeal had no jurisdiction to re-appraise evidence and reverse conclusion reached by Appellate Court, but at same time its power to interfere with finding could not be denied if when lower Appellate Court decides an issue of fact substantial question of law arises - Court was under duty to examine entire relevant evidence on record and if it refuses to consider important evidence having direct bearing on disputed issue and error which arises was of magnitude that it gave birth to substantial question of law, HC was fully authorized to set aside finding - Therefore SC did not discover any defect in judgment of HC - Appeal dismissed.
