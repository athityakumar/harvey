Shroff and Company and another v Municipal Corporation Of Greater Bombay Andanother, Etc.

Supreme Court of India

12 August 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, S. Ranganathan

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 701; (1989) Supp1 SCC 347; AIR 1989 SC 347; 1988 (3) CompLJ 146; 1989 (21) ECC 24; 1989 (20) ECR 10; 1988 (38) E.L.T. 243; JT 1988 (3) SC 406; 1988(2) SCALE 348; 1989 (72) STC 150; [1988] Supp2 S.C.R. 406; 1988 (2) UJ 738

Case Digest

Subject: Excise

Keywords: Warehouse, Maharashtra Foreign Liquor (Import and Export) Rules 1963, Maharashtra Foreign Liquor (Storage in Bond) Rules 1964

Summary: Excise - Maharashtra Foreign Liquor (Import and Export) Rules, 1963 - Maharashtra Foreign Liquor (Storage in Bond) Rules, 1964 - Bombay Municipal Corporation Levy of Octroi Rules, 1965 - Countervailing duty - Demanding payment of octroi - Appellants were carrying business of dealing in wines and spirits and were licensed to import and store liquors in their bonded warehouse at 'M', Bombay - Appellants were also holders of licence issued under 1963 Act - Under 1964 rules, the importer could import liquor and store the same in warehouse without payment of countervailing duty - Octroi Rules were amended time and again on 28-7-1976 and 30-6-1983 to impose octroi on assessable value which included customs duty paid on import of liquor - Aggrieved by that, appellants filed writ petition before HC challenging inclusion of countervailing duty in assessable value for octroi on the ground that said duty was not incurred 'till the date of removal of goods from the place of import - Single Judge allowed writ petition and held that countervailing duty was neither incurred nor was it liable to be incurred until after bonded liquor was removed from the place of import - On appeal, DB reversed judgment of Single Judge and held that countervailing duty was includible in assessable value for imposition of octroi - In pursuance of the same Deputy Assessor and Collector (Octroi), issued notice demanding payment of octroi amounting to Rs.76,70,308.71 - Hence instant petition - Whether countervailing duty was includible in octroi - Held, countervailing duty did form part of the incidental charges - Countervailing duty was clearly contained in r. 2(7)(a) of 1965 Rules - S. 105 of 1949 Act itself talks of excise duties so as to include both excise duty as well as countervailing duty - Therefore, normal connotation of the words 'excise duties' would take in countervailing duty also - Apart from that charges include taxes - Hence, countervailing duty was an incident of importation and, as such, it was includible even prior to 28-6-1983 as an octroi -DB was right in the view it took - Appeal dismissed.
