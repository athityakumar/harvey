Smt. Indira Kaur and Others v Sheo Lal Kapoor

Supreme Court of India

28 March 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

M.P. Thakkar, N.D. Ojha

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 421; (1988) 2 SCC 488; AIR 1988 SC 1074; JT 1988 (1) SC 652B; 1988(1) SCALE 598; 1988 (1) UJ 650

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Constitution; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Debt Laws

Summary: Land and Property - Contract and Commercial - Constitution - Transfer of Property Act, 1882 - (A) Transfer of Property Act, 1882, ss.54, 58, 58(c) - (1) Whether sale or mortgage - A person selling property to another, who entered into a contemporaneous agreement to resale property to former person, for same consideration - Stipulation as to period of 10 years for claiming re-sale - Possession to remain with former on payment of rent - (2) Sale deed and contemporaneous agreement to resale property for same sum executed between parties - Original seller whether could claim relief of redemption u/s. 58(c) - (B) Contract - Time whether of essence - Sale of immovable property - (C) Constitution of India - Appeal u/art. 136 - Scope - Concurrent finding of fact - (D) Specific Relief Act - Suit for specific performance of agreement to sell Readiness and willingness of plaintiff - Proof - Plaintiff stating that he has deposited sum more than sale consideration in bank - Held, (1) Defendant neither enquiring about the name of bank nor he nor Court asking plaintiff to produce pass book - Adverse inference - (2) No question put by lower Courts as to how plaintiff could have saved the sum or as to his expenses and income of his sons - Finding yet recorded by lower Courts that plaintiff could not have saved the sum is improper - Evidence showing that defendant did not attend Registrar's office to perform his part despite notice - Held : (A) Transaction is in reality a mortgage - Question left open, concern expressed by SC on increasing tendency in recent years to enter into such transactions in order to deprive the debtor of his right of redemption within the prescribed period of limitation - (B) Time is not essence of contract - (C) Not that SC can in no case upset such finding - (D) Adverse inference on account of non-production of pass book cannot be drawn against plaintiff - Finding of lower Courts that plaintiff was not ready and willing to perform his part, is liable to be set - Appeal allowed.
