Nand Lal Agarwal v Ganesh Prasad Sah and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 August 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Natarajan, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 611; (1988) 4 SCC 215; AIR 1988 SC 1821; 1988 BBCJ 128; 1988 BLJR 567; JT 1988 (3) SC 402; 1988 (2) RCJ 294; 1988 (2) RCR(Rent) 265; 1988 (2) RentLR 671; 1988(2) SCALE 387; [1988] Supp2 S.C.R. 303; 1988 (2) UJ 504

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Bihar Buildings (Lease, Rent and Eviction) Control Act, 1977, s. 8(2) - Suit for eviction - On the grounds: (1) bona fide requirement of premises for opening a shop; (2) default in payment of rent for five months; and (3) wrongful conversion of leased premises from a residential house to a grocery shop - Trial court and appellate court decreed suit for eviction on first two grounds but not on third ground - High Court held that appellant's prayer for adjustment of excess payment can be sustained only if he had exercised his right of option u/s. 8 (2) as section lays down that any payment made in excess of fair rent fixed for a building has to be refunded to person by whom it was paid or at option of such person it can be adjusted towards arrears of rent - Whether it is valid in law? - Held, an increase of rent by way of return for additional amenities provided by respondent was not permissible under Act - S. 4 does not permit any increase being made in rent except in accordance with provisions of Act - Sub-s. 2 of s. 8 inter alia provides that if any rent has been collected in excess of fair rent determined for a building, then tenant is entitled to a refund of excess amounts paid by him unless he opts for a different course of action viz adjustment of excess amounts paid by him towards rent past or future - Further, even if rent for building leased out to appellant had not been determined by Controller but had been fixed by parties themselves - Right to seek adjustment for excess payments made by appellant has to be in accordance with principle set out in s. 8 (2) - HC has not therefore committed any error in holding that without appellant exercising his option and calling upon respondent to adjust excess payments towards arrears of rent he cannot seek an automatic adjustment of excess payments made by him and contend that he was not liable to be evicted for non-payment of rent - Appeal dismissed.
