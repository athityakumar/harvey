Surajmal v Radheyshyam

Supreme Court of India

26 April 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

L.M. Sharma, A.P. Sen

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 461; (1988) 3 SCC 18; AIR 1988 SC 1345; 1988 BBCJ 96; JT 1988 (2) SC 210; 1988 (2) RCJ 20; 1988 (1) RCR(Rent) 511; 1988 (1) RentLR 784; 1988(1) SCALE 971; 1988 (2) UJ 233

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - CPC, 1908, s. 100 - Suit for eviction - Nature of rquirement - Whether decision of HC is correct? - Held, yes - HC in paragraph 4 of its judgment pointed out that nature of requirement pleaded in earlier suit was different from that in present suit - First appellate court while deciding issue against defendant observed that bona fide need must be considered with reference to time when a suit for eviction is filed and it cannot be assumed that once question of necessity is decided against plaintiff it has to be assumed that he will be not have a bona fide and genuine necessity ever in future - Appeal dismissed.
