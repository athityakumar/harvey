State Of Punjab And Ors. v Om Prakash Baldev Krishan

Supreme Court of India

23 August 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, L.M. Sharma

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 568; (1988) Supp SCC 722; AIR 1988 SC 2149; 1988 (2) CCC 817; JT 1988 (3) SC 616; 1988(2) SCALE 420; [1988] Supp2 S.C.R. 536; 1988 (2) UJ 657

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Government of India Act 1935

Summary: Criminal - Customs Act, 1962 - CrPC, 1974 - Sentencing - (A) Consecutive and concurrent sentences - If can be imposed - When transaction relating to offences not same or facts constituting two offences, quite different - (B) Maximum sentence - Enormity of the crime - Accused pleading guilty in both cases - When 18 other accused facing the trial in the same case - Maximum sentence awarded in first case and also in the second case, making it non consecutive - Legality - Held, consecutive sentences may be imposed - Not proper as in consecutive sentences, the court cannot afford to be blind to imprisonment which the accused is already undergoing - Appeal allowed.
