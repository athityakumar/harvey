Rural Litigation And Entitlement Kendra v State Of U.P.

Supreme Court of India

30 August 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranganath Misra, M.M. Dutt

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 586; (1989) SCC 5; (1989) Supp1 SCC 504; AIR 1988 SC 2187; JT 1988 (3) SC 787; [1988] Supp2 S.C.R. 690

Case Digest

Subject: Environment, Wildlife & Animal; Mines & Minerals

Keywords: Estate Duty (Amendment) Act, 1958, Forest (Conservation) Act, 1980 (With Amendments Made In 1988)

Summary: Mines & Minerals - Environment, Wildlife & Animal - Forest (Conservation) Act, 1980 - Mining activities - Regulations - Committee appointed - Legality - Petitioners filed instant writ petitions alleging of unauthorised and illegal mining in Mussoorie-Dehradun belt which adversely affected ecology of area and led to environment disorder - Held, mining in that area had to be stopped - Further, if any decree or order had already obtained from any Court relating to renewal of those leases, same should stand vacated and similarly any appeal or other proceeding taken to obtain a renewal or against orders/decrees renewal should also become no nest - Maintenance of environment and ecological balance was obligation of State and Central Govts. and unless there was any real objection to opinion of UOI as to continuing or closing down of mining activity, it should have been taken in proper light and little modified - However, question of foreign exchange component did not seem to be very material as required type of mineral was indigenously available and import might not be necessary when production in Rajasthan area increases - Most of those mines were either within reserved forests or in forest lands as covered by the Act - Once importance of forests was realised and as a matter of national policy and in interests of community, preservation of forests was accepted as goal nothing which would detract from that end should be permitted - Thus, mining activities could be regulated and simultaneously reafforestation should be activised - In regard to mines closed for more than 3 years, labour was sitting idle and mine owner was paying them - They should have got employed elsewhere of they have lost their service and have taken to alternate engagement - Therefore, allowing those three on-going mines to operate for their initial period of lease was most appropriate direction that could be given during switch over from present position to one of complete closing down of mining operation - Hence, permitted those three mines to continue mining operation subject to compliance with all legal requirements and additional conditions - Committee was directed to set up with representatives of UOI and State Govts. while effecting rehabilitation by giving alternate mining sites, ecology and environment would have to be considered - Petitions disposed of.
