Madan And Co. v Wazir Jaivir Chand

Supreme Court of India

28 November 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Ranganathan, S. Mukharji

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 291; (1989) 1 SCC 264; AIR 1989 SC 630; JT 1988 (4) SC 520; 1989 (1) RCJ 29; 1988 (2) RCR(Rent) 654; 1989 (1) RentLR 97; 1988(2) SCALE 1408; [1988] 3 S.C.R. 983; [1988] Supp3 S.C.R. 983; 1989 (1) UJ 98

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Rent Control - Civil Procedure - Jammu and Kashmir Houses and Shops Rent Control Act, 1966, ss. 11 and 12 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 106 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 5, rr. 10 and 20 - General Clauses Act, 1897, s. 27 - Notice to terminate tenancy - Validity - Arrears of rent - Registered post on correct address - Letter returned for non-availability of addressee - Held, valid - No responsibility on sender or postman to arrange that notice is served - Appropriate amendment to s. 11 of 1966 Act suggested - Appeal dismissed.
