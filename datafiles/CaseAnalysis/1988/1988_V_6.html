Vaidya Harishankar Laxmiram Rajyaguru Of Rajkot v Pratapray Harishankar Rajyaguru Of Rajkot

Supreme Court of India

13 April 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, Ranganath Misra

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 712; (1988) 3 SCC 21; 1988 (1) ARBLR 416; JT 1988 (2) SC 224; 1988(1) SCALE 955; [1988] 3 S.C.R. 534; 1988 (2) UJ 74

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Keywords: Code of Civil Procedure 1908

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Arbitration Act, 1940, s. 2(a) - By impugned judgment, High Court dismissed civil revision application which challenged award made - Main objection was that there was no written agreement signed by both parties to refer disputes to arbitration - Held, in fact parties had agreed to refer dispute to arbitrator - Award was signed by both parties - All these are in writing and signed by all parties - A proper arbitration agreement in terms of s. 2(a) can be construed - Petition dismissed.
