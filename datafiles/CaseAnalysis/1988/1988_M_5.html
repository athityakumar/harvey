State Of Maharashtra v M.H. Mazumdar

Supreme Court of India

24 February 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

K.N. Singh, E.S. Venkataramiah, N.D. Ojha

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 378; (1988) 2 SCC 52; (1988) SCC (L&S) 436; AIR 1988 SC 842; 1988 (6) ATC 876; 1988 (3) Bom.C.R. 257; 1988 (56) FLR 764; JT 1988 (1) SC 432; 1988 LabIC 991; 1988 (2) LLJ 62; 1988 (1) LLN 886B; 1988(1) SCALE 402; [1988] 3 S.C.R. 31; 1988 (3) SLJ 67; 1988 (1) SLR 747; 1988 (1) UJ 550

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Service - Bombay Civil Services Rules, 1959, rr. 188, 189 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 309 and 311 - Bombay Civil Services (Conduct, Discipline and Appeal) Rules, 1932, rr. 33, Note I - Service matter - Misconduct, negligence or financial irregularities - Acts of - Whether Govt. is competent to withhold or reduce pension of Govt. servant for such Act committed by retired servant during period of his service? - Held, Govt. is competent, after giving opportunity of defence to him - Appeal allowed.
