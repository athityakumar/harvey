Bharat Steel Tubes Limited and Another v State of Haryana and Another

Supreme Court of India

04 May 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranganath Misra, R.S. Pathak, M.M. Dutt

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 705; (1988) 3 SCC 478; JT 1988 (2) SC 320; 1988(1) SCALE 842; [1988] 3 S.C.R. 895; 1988 (70) STC 122; 1988 (2) UJ 204

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Indirect Tax - Punjab General Sales Tax Act, 1948 - Haryana General Sales Tax Act, 1973 - A/Ys 1968-69, 1969-70, 1970-71, 1971-72, 1972-73 and 1973-74 - Order of assessment - Limitation - Petitioner No. 1 was manufacturer of electric resistance, welded steel tubes and pipes, and also dealer registered under 1973 Act as also under Central Sales Tax Act - Petitioner no. 1 filed returns for all quarters as prescribed by 1948 Act till 31-3-1973 and under 1973 Act for quarters of remained years in question as 1973 Act came into force w.e.f.5-5-1973 - On receipt of notice relating to A/Ys 1968-69, 1969-70, 1970-71, 1971-72, 1972-73 and 1973-74 in prescribed form u/s. 14(2) of 1948 Act and u/s. 28(2) of Act relating to year 1974-75, petitioner appeared before respondent No. 2 and complied with requirements of such notice by production of documents, books and other papers - While matter was thus proceeding, second respondent again issued notice on 24-9-1982 requiring petitioner company to produce certain further records and documents - Petitioner challenged that notice as also the vires of s. 28A of 1973 Act - Whether order of assessment u/s. 11(3) of 1948 Act or s. 28(3) of 1973 Act could be completed or would that be barred by limitation - Held, assessment proceedings were very delayed - Though notices were issued u/ss. 11(2) of 1948 Act or 28(2) of 1973 Act within a reasonable period from the filing of returns for respective quarters in assessment years under consideration, further action was not taken by AO to complete the assessments - However, in absence of any prescribed period of limitation, assessment had to be completed within reasonable period - What such reasonable period would be, would depend upon facts of each case - One view could be that it should be a period not exceeding 5 years as Legislature had fixed limitation of 5 years for completing assessments in case of escaped turnover - Unless then be an assessment made soon after period to which such assessment relates, question of consideration of escapement would indeed become difficult to consider and examine - Assessing authority intended to complete assessments u/s. 11(3) of 1948 Act, SC saw no prejudice to assessee if assessing authority was permitted to complete assessment now - Petitions dismissed.
