G. Ramegowda, Major and Others v Special Land Acquisition Officer, Bangalore

Supreme Court of India

10 March 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

M.N. Venkatachaliah, S. Natarajan

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 655; (1988) 2 SCC 142; AIR 1988 SC 897; 1988 (1) CCC 970; JT 1988 (1) SC 524; 1989 (2) MLJ(SC) 14; 1988(1) SCALE 479; [1988] 3 S.C.R. 198; 1988 (1) UJ 666

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Limitation Act 1953

Summary: Land and Property - Practice and Procedure- Limitation Act 1953 - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss.54 - Land Acquisition case - Appeals against award - Delay , cause alleged inaction on part of Government counsel - High Court condoning delay - Appeal to Supreme Court against order - (A) Interference Sought - (B) High Court disposing of main appeals on merit finally - Whether it put a bar on consideration of condonation order by SC - Held, on facts discretion of HC in condoning delay could not be interfered with - It does not bar consideration by SC of correctness of order of HC condoning delay - Appeals dismissed.
