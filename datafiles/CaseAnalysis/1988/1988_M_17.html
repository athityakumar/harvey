Union of India and Others v Madhumilan Syntex Private Limited and Another

Supreme Court of India

03 May 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

M.H. Kania, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 395; (1988) SCC (Tax) 341; (1988) 3 SCC 348; AIR 1988 SC 1236; 1988 CRLR 356; 1988 CrLR(SC) 356; 1988 (17) ECC 39; 1988 (17) ECR 1; 1988 (35) E.L.T. 349; JT 1988 (2) SC 255; 1988(1) SCALE 979; [1988] 3 S.C.R. 838; 1988 (2) UJ 130

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Customs; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Short Levy

Summary: Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944 - Excise duty - Short payment by Company - Notice of demand - Prior show cause notice not served on Company - Notice of demand was violative of provisions of s. 11 A - Post facto show cause notice - Held, that before any demand was made on any person chargeable in respect of non levy or short levy or under payment of duty, a notice requiring him to show cause why he should not pay amounts specified in notice must be served on him - Post facto show cause notice could not be regarded as adequate in law.
