Vijay Prakash D. Mehta And Anr. v Collector Of Customs (Preventive), Bombay

Supreme Court of India

16 August 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, S. Ranganathan

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 821; (1988) 4 SCC 402; AIR 1988 SC 2010; 1988 BBCJ 178; 1988 (4) Bom.C.R. 91; [1989] 66 Comp Cas 30; 1989 CRLR 75; 1988 (18) ECR 369; 1989 (39) E.L.T. 178; [1989] 175 ITR 540; JT 1988 (3) SC 435; 1988(2) SCALE 367; 1989 (72) STC 324; [1988] Supp2 S.C.R. 434; [1988] 41 TAXMAN 147; 1988 (2) UJ 609

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Summary: Customs - Indirect Tax - Customs Act, 1962, ss. 111, 112, 113, 114, 118, 129A and 129E - Foreign Exchange Regulation Act, 1973 - Reduction in penalty - Addl. Collector of Customs (Preventive) imposed a penalty of Rs. 3,00,000 on each of the appellants as appellants were allegedly caught red-handed with foreign exchange to the tune of Rs.11,90,648/- - Against said order appellants preferred appeals to the Appellate Tribunal u/s. 129A of the Act - Tribunal reduced the amount of penalty to Rs. 1 lakh for each of the appellants - Appellants prayed for further reduction in penalty submitting that neither of the appellants were in any financial position to deposit even Rs. 1 lakh - Appellate Tribunal declined to do so and dismissed the appeals for non-compliance with the provisions of s. 129E of the Act - Appellants filed present appeal contending that they had not gone out of India and had no assets in India - Their passports were impounded at the time of arrest and they had no money, hence, the right of appeal could be illusory unless they are permitted to deposit only Rs.60,000 each which they contend they are able to procure with the assistance of their father - Whether further reduction in penalty could be granted to the appellants? - Held, right given u/s. 129A of the Act as controlled by s. 129E of the Act, is with a condition and thus a conditional right - Petitioner has no absolute right of stay - He could obtain stay of realisation of tax levied or penalty imposed in an appeal subject to the limitation of s. 129E of Act - The proviso gives a discretion to the authority to dispense with the obligation to deposit in case of 'undue hardships' - That discretion must be exercised on relevant materials, honestly, bona fide and objectively - Once that position is established it cannot be contended that there was any improper exercise of the jurisdiction by the Appellate Authority - Order of the Tribunal was passed honestly, bona fide and having regard to the plea of 'undue hardship' as canvassed by the appellant - No error of jurisdiction or misdirection - Appeals dismissed.
