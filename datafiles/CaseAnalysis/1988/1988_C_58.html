Chern Taong Shang And Anr., Etc., Etc. v Commander S.D.Baijal And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

13 January 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

B.C. Ray, K. Jagannatha Shetty

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 60; (1988) SCC (Cr) 162; (1988) 1 SCC 507; AIR 1988 SC 603; 1988 (1) Crimes 524; 1988 CRLJ 855; 1988 CrLR(SC) 150; JT 1988 (1) SC 202; 1988(1) SCALE 69; [1988] 2 S.C.R. 641

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Poaching - Maritime Zones of India (Regulation of Fishing by Foreign Vessels) Act, 1981,ss. 10, 11, 12, 13 - Violation of fishing conditions and permissions in exclusive economic zones - (A) Whether conviction of masters of trawlers was justified - Held, Yes - Fish found on board trawler which leads to conclusion that appellants were liable for offence of poaching of fish without relevant permission - (B) Whether confiscation of vessel was proper? - Maritime Zones of India (Regulation of Fishing by Foreign Vessels) Act has been enacted with object of preventing illegal poaching of fishes by foreign vessels and includes foreign vessels chartered by Indian parties by providing deterrent punishment to protect Indian fishermen - Not open to Court to consider graveness of offence and then make order for confiscation of offending vessel concerned - S. 13 is mandatory and it is not open to Court as soon as master of vessel is convicted for an offence u/s. 12 and is awarded penalty to refrain from making an order confiscating offending vessel - Confiscation of vessel is immediate statutory consequence in case an offence either under Act has been proved - Appeals dismissed.
