Deputy Commissioner of Sales Tax (Law) Board of v Thomas Stephen And Co . Ltd. Quilon.

Supreme Court of India

14 March 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, S. Ranganathan

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 913; (1988) SCC (Tax) 190; (1988) 2 SCC 264; AIR 1988 SC 997; 1988 (34) E.L.T. 412; JT 1988 (1) SC 631; 1988 (1) KLT 568; 1988(1) SCALE 569B; [1988] 3 S.C.R. 248; 1988 (69) STC 320

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Excise; Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Kerala General Sales Tax Act, 1963, s. 5A - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Scope of s.5 of the Act - Manufacturing - (A) Whether material used as fuel (cashew shells) in kiln and for maintenance of kiln and factory exigible from purchase Tax - Contention that purchases in question were not liable for levy of tax since none of the conditions prescribed in clause (a), (b) or (c) of s. 5A of the Act were satisfied - (B) Whether there is any disposal of impugned goods in any manner otherwise than by way of sale within the State - Held, not exigible to purchase tax - Further held that as there was no evidence of any transfer at all, therefore, there was no disposal of the goods as known to law - Petition rejected.
