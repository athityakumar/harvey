Navnit R. Kamani & Ors. v R.R. Kamani

Supreme Court of India

19 September 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

M.P. Thakkar, A.P. Sen, S. Natarajan

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 629; (1988) 4 SCC 387; AIR 1989 SC 9; [1989] 66 Comp Cas 132; 1988 (3) CompLJ 162; JT 1988 (3) SC 700; 1989 LabIC 1; 1989 (1) LLJ 47; 1989 (1) LLN 602; 1988(2) SCALE 721; [1988] 3 S.C.R. 123; [1988] Supp3 S.C.R. 123; 1990 (3) UPLBEC 1456

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Debt Laws

Summary: Corporate - SICA,1986, ss. 1, 2 and 18(4) - Constitution, arts. 129 and 142 - Companies Act, 1956, s. 522 - Sick industrial unit - BFIR to sanction scheme for revival in accordance with s. 18(4) of SICA, 1986 - Shareholders and workers presented their respective schemes - Since for the first time the legislative intent reflected in the provisions of SICA, 1986 and workers encouraged to run the sick unit - Shares transferred to the employees at reduced value of Re. 1/- per share - (A) Directions sought from the court in regard to schemes - (B) Validity of SICA, 1986 , ss. 1 and 2 - Challenged - Held, (i) scheme presented by the shareholder was liable to be rejected (ii) scheme presented by the workers was liable to be stamped with the imprimatur of the Court. it did not suffer from any infirmity - Board allowed to consider the alternative Scheme, if presented within the time limit - Provisions of SICA, 1986 found to be immune from challenge by virtue of declaration contained in s. 2 of Act attracting application of Art. 31C of Constitution - Order accordingly.
