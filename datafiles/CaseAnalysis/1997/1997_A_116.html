State of Gujarat v Anirudhsingh and Another

Supreme Court of India

10 July 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Wadhwa, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 2662; (1997) 6 SCC 514; (1997) SCC (Cr) 946; AIR 1997 SC 2780; AIR 1997 SCW 2758; 1997 (4) CCR 53; 1997 (3) Crimes 82; 1997 CRLJ 3397; JT 1997 (6) SC 236; 1997(4) SCALE 724; [1997] Supp2 S.C.R. 234; 1997 (6) Supreme 435

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: TADA Act

Summary: Criminal - Arms Act, 1959 - Bombay Police Act, 1951 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 114, 302 - Terrorist and Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Act, 1987, ss. 3,5, 25 - Supreme Court Rules, 1966 - Possession - Unauthorized fire arms - Murder - Conviction - Acquitted - 'P'/ deceased a Member of Legislative Assembly was done to death in public gaze when full ceremonial Independence Day function was in progress - Appeal u/s. 25 of 1987 Act arises out of a charge laid against two accused u/ss. 3, 5 of 1987 Act, s.114 of IPC for causing terror for having in possession unauthorized fire arms and for causing death of deceased - Designated Court acquitted respondents of all charges through special Court - Whether prosecution had proved case as against Accused No.1/ respondent No.1 beyond reasonable doubt - Held, prosecution had established that respondent No.1, had committed murder of deceased - Court had not correctly appreciated evidence in proper perspective - Prosecution had proved its case beyond reasonable doubt that respondent No.1 was in possession of a fire arm which was not proved to be licensed - Therefore, it was an unauthorized weapon - Respondent No.1 had committed offence u/s. 5 of 1987 Act and also offence of murder punishable u/s.302 of IPC - Judgment and order of acquittal passed by Designated Court stand set aside - Respondent No.1 committed the offence of murder convicted u/s. 302 of IPC and u/s.5 of 1987 Act sentenced to undergo imprisonment for three years - Both sentence were directed to run concurrently - Appeal disposed of.
