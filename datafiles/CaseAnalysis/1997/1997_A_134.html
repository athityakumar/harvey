State of West Bengal and Another v Arun Kumar Basu and Another

Supreme Court of India

04 April 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, G.B. Pattanaik

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 874; (1997) 5 SCC 317; AIR 1997 SC 2645; 1997 (2) CalLT(SC) 52; 1997 (2) CLT 382; JT 1997 (5) SC 106; 1997(3) SCALE 720; [1997] 3 S.C.R. 653; 1997 (5) Supreme 505

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land and Property - W.B. Estates Acquisition Act, 1954, ss. 4(1), 6(1)(c) and 6(1)(j) - (A) Acquisition of land - Vesting of right, title and interest free from encumbrances in State - Whether affected by inaction on part of Collector in not taking possession of land - (B) Non-agricultural land belonging to company - Whether all of said land including tenancy rights, if any, held by third parties would vest in State - What is company entitled to -

(C) Applicability of s. 6(1)(j) of 1954 Act - Exemption from Acquisition - Availability to agricultural land held by company engaged in farming - Land belonging to Railway Company not engaged in farming - Would exemption u/s. 6(1)(j) be available -

(D) W.B. Estates Acquisition Act, s. 4(1) - Vesting of land - Abolition of proprietary rights by virtue of s. 4(1) - Would s. 3A of Land Reforms Act dealing with vesting of tenancy rights in non-agricultural land apply - Held, (A) Not affected by mere inaction -

(B) Would stand vested in State - Entitled only to retain title and possession up to 15 acres of land - (C) Would not be available - (D) Would not apply - Appeal allowed.
