Vellore Electric Corporation Limited Etc v Commissioner Of Income Tax, Madras

Supreme Court of India

08 July 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Agrawal, D.P. Wadhwa

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 2601; (1997) 6 SCC 705; [1997] 227 ITR 557; JT 1997 (6) SC 412; 1997(4) SCALE 447; [1997] Supp1 S.C.R. 586; 1997 (7) Supreme 180

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure; Trusts & Associations

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 261 - Electricity (Supply) Act, 1948, s. 57 - Taxes - (A) Whether the assessee entitled to claim deduction in respect of sums transferred to the Contingencies Reserve, Development Reserve and Tariffs and Dividend Control Reserve? - (B) Whether the assessee entitled to deduction under s. 80-I on the interest received on the amounts transferred to the Contingencies Reserve which were invested in Govt. securities? - Held, no difference between Development Reserve and Contingencies Reserve and the High Court, in the impugned judgment, has rightly held that the amount appropriated towards Development Reserve could not be deducted - The income earned by way of interest on the sums appropriated to the Contingencies Reserve which have been invested in securities can be said to be profits and gains attributable to the business of the assessee for the purpose of s. 80-I of the Act - Question 3 in Civil Appeals Nos. 2613-14 of 1984 is, therefore, answered in the affirmative, i.e., in favour of the assessee and against the Revenue - Civil Appeals Nos. 3333-3334 of 1981 are dismissed and Civil Appeals Nos. 2613-14 of 1984 are partly allowed to the extent that Question 3 is answered in the affirmative, in favour of the assessee and against the Revenue and the impugned judgment of the HC is reversed to this extent.
