K.I. Pavunny v Assistant Collector (Head Quarter), Central Excise Collectora

Supreme Court of India

03 February 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, S. Saghir Ahmad, G.B. Pattanaik

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 859; (1997) SCC (Cr) 444; (1997) 3 SCC 721; 1997 (2) CCR 1; 1997 (1) Crimes 210; 1998 CRLJ 4018; 1997 (69) ECR 209; 1997 (90) E.L.T. 241; JT 1997 (2) SC 120; 1997 (1) KLT 489; 1997 (18) RLT 641; 1997(1) SCALE 640; 1997 (1) SCJ 341; [1997] 1 S.C.R. 797; 1997 (2) Supreme 524

Case Digest

Subject: Customs

Keywords: Evidence Act, Accused Of An Offence

Summary: Customs - Customs Act, 1962, ss. 108 and 135 - Gold Control Act, 1968, ss. 85 and 86 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 24 - On gold biscuits of foreign making were recovered from compound of appellant's house in his presence PW-2, Superintendent of Customs, and PW-5 Inspector and two panch witnesses, PW-3 and another - Same were kept concealed in a wooden box buried in ground visible through window of his bed-room - Appellant gave, in his own handwriting, confessional statement on said date before Customs officials - In proceedings for confiscation, 200 gold biscuits, since unclaimed, were confiscated - Appellant was apprehended and was released on bail - Prosecution case hinges upon retracted confessional statement - Magistrate acquitted appellant of charges u/s. 135 (1)(i) of Act and ss. 85 (1)(a) and 86 of Gold (Control) Act, 1968 - On appeal, Single Judge of High Court by impugned judgment set aside acquittal and convicted appellant - Whether retracted confessional statement, Ex P-4, by appellant is inadmissible in evidence u/s. 24 of Act and what is scope for its consideration? - Held, there is no prohibition under Evidence Act to rely upon retracted confession to prove prosecution case or to make same basis for conviction of accused - Practice and prudence require that Court could examine evidence adduced by prosecution to find out whether there are any other facts and circumstances to corroborate retracted confession - It is not necessary that there should be corroboration from independent evidence adduced by prosecution to corroborate each detail contained in confessional statement - Court is required to examine whether confessional statement is voluntary, in other words, whether it was not obtained by threat, duress or promise - If Court is satisfied from evidence that it was voluntary, then it is required to examine whether statement is true - If Court on examination of evidence finds that he retracted confession is true, that part of inculpatory portion could be relied upon to base conviction - However, prudence and practice require that Court would seek assurance getting corroboration from other evidence adduced by prosecution - Therefore, it was held that 'the confession, though retracted, is an admission and binds petitioner - So there is no need to call Panch witnesses for examination and cross-examination by petitioner' - As noted, object of Act is to prevent large-scale smuggling of precious metals and other dutiable goods and to facilitate detection and confiscation of smuggled goods into, or out of country - U/s. 86, whoever fails to make a declaration enjoined under sub-s. (12) of s. 16 without any reasonable cause, is liable to punishment of imprisonment prescribed for purpose - Offences are proved from evidence - Appeal partly allowed.
