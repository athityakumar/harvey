State of Rajasthan and Another v Amrit Lal Gandhi and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 January 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Kirpal, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 954; (1997) 2 SCC 342; (1997) SCC (L&S) 512; AIR 1997 SC 782; 1997 (2) CLT 234; 1997 (2) ESC 1037; JT 1997 (1) SC 421; 1997 LabIC 420; 1997 (2) LLN 596; 1997(1) SCALE 168; [1997] 1 S.C.R. 121; 1997 (1) SLR 528; 1997 (1) Supreme 270

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Service

Keywords: Jodhpur University Act, 1962

Summary: Service - Administrative - Jodhpur University Act, 1962 - Revised pension - Effective date - Instant case was related to issue regarding date with effect from which the revised pension was to be paid to the teachers of the Jai Narayan Vyas University and Mohan Lal Sukhadia University who had retired between 1-1-1986 and 1-1-1990 - Whether HC was in error substituting the date of 1-1-1986 for 1-1-1990.

Held, the University could, in 1991, validly frame Pension Regulations to be made applicable prospectively. It, however, chose to give them limited retrospectively so as to cover a larger number of employees by taking into account the financial impact of giving retrospective operation to the Pension Regulations. It was decided that employees retiring on or after 1-1-1990 would be able to exercise the option of getting either pension or provident fund. Financial impact of making the Regulations retrospective can be the sole consideration while fixing a cut-off date. It cannot be said that this cut-off date was fixed arbitrarily or without any reason. The HC was clearly in error in allowing the writ petitions and substituting the date of 1-1-1986 for 1-1-1990. The policy decision of the Universities, making the Pension Regulations applicable w.e.f. 1-1-1990 has not been shown to be arbitrary or invalid. Appeal allowed.
