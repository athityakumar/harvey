M/s Hyderabad Abrasives and Minerals Private Limited v State of Andhra Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 July 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 2704; JT 1997 (6) SC 634

Case Digest

Subject: Environment, Wildlife & Animal; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Municipalities And Local Governments, Andhra Pradesh Forest Act, 1967, Mining, Tribals, Lease

Summary: Environment, Wildlife & Animal - Municipalities & Local Governments - Andhra Pradesh Forest Act, 1967, s. 24 - Andhra Pradesh Scheduled Areas Land, Transfer Regulation, 1959, s. 3 - Forest (Conservation) Act, 1980, s. 2 - SAKTI, a voluntary social organisation for the upliftment of tribals in East Godavari District filed the writ petition in the Andhra Pradesh High Court praying therein that the mining activities which are carried on by the respondents in the said writ petition should be immediately stopped as the grant of mining leases in their favour is in contravention of s. 3 of the Andhra Pradesh Scheduled Areas Land, Transfer Regulation, 1959 as well as s. 2 of the Forest (Conservation) Act - The public officers of the state Govt. supported the case of the petitioner and took the stand that a joint inspection report had been conducted after surveying the area over which the mining activities are being carried on by the respondents - The decision of this court in the case of STATE OF BIHAR v. BANSHI RAM - It was also contended that s. 3 of the regulation has no application to a transfer by the Govt. in respect of its land in favour of a non-tribal and the word 'person' in s. 3 of the said regulation will not include the Govt. - Appeal dismissed.
