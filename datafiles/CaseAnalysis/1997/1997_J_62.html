Malkiat Singh & Anr. v Joginder Singh & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

02 December 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

A.S. Anand, V.N. Khare

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1010; (1998) 2 SCC 206; AIR 1998 SC 258; 1998 (1) CLT 44; JT 1997 (9) SC 642; 1998 (1) MLJ 76; 1998 (1) MLJ(SC) 76; 1997(7) SCALE 398; 1998 (2) SLT 74; [1997] Supp6 S.C.R. 139

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 O. 9, r. 13 - Exparte decree - Application for setting aside - Maintainability - Respondents/plaintiffs filed suit against appellants/defendants in Trial Court claiming damages for deprivation of income on account of murder of deceased by appellants - After 2 witnesses for plaintiffs were examined, on 18-11-1991, counsel for appellants pleaded 'no instructions' before Court - Therefore, appellants were proceeded ex-parte and ex-parte decree against appellants was passed on 8-2-1992 - Application for setting aside order dt. 18-11-1991 and ex-parte judgment and decree dt. 8-2-1992 was dismissed by trial court - Appeal before District Judge failed - HC dismissed civil revision petition - Hence, instant appeal - Whether impugned orders were justified - Held, perusal of record also reveals that appellants were neither careless nor negligent in defending suit, they had engaged counsel and were following proceedings - In this fact situation, Trial Court, which had admittedly not issued any notice to appellants after their counsel had reported no instructions, should have, in interest of justice, allowed that application and proceeded in case from stage when counsel reported no instructions - Appellants could not, in facts and circumstances of case, be said to be at fault and they should not suffer - Therefore, order of Trial Court dt. 18-11-1991 and ex-parte decree dt. 8-2-1991 were set aside - SC also set aside order of District Judge and that of HC dismissing civil revision petition - Case was remanded to Trial Court for its disposal in accordance with law, Trial Court should proceed with case from stage, where case was on 18-11-1991 - Order accordingly.
