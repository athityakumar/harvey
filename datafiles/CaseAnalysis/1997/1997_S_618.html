Sandeep Kumar Sharma v State of Punjab and Others

Supreme Court of India

24 February 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, M.M. Punchhi

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 2448; (1997) 10 SCC 298; (1997) SCC (L&S) 1682; AIR 1997 SC 1194; 1997 (1) CLR 651; 1997 (76) FLR 798; JT 1997 (3) SC 14; 1997 LabIC 1489; 1997(2) SCALE 318; [1997] 2 S.C.R. 405; 1997 (2) Supreme 467; 1997 (1) UJ 671

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Keywords: Punjab Police Service Rules, 1959

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 16 and 309 - Punjab Police Service Rules, 1959, r. 7 - Appointment to post of Deputy Superintendent of Police - Whether relaxation in respect of a candidate in exercise of power u/r. 7 in matter of height considering useful service rendered by candidate's brother in tackling terrorists menace discriminatory - Held, no discrimination - Govt. may have to act on some occasion for chalking out particular policy - Appeal dismissed.
