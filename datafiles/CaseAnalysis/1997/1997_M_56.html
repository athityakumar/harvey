M/S L.M.L. Limited v Collector Of Central Excisekanpur

Supreme Court of India

21 August 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Kirpal, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 582; (1997) 7 SCC 588; AIR 1997 SC 3516; 1997 (94) E.L.T. 273; JT 1997 (7) SC 468; 1997 (22) RLT 1; 1997(5) SCALE 542; [1997] Supp3 S.C.R. 507; 1997 (7) Supreme 618

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - Indirect Tax - Manufacture - Central excise license - Credit Scheme - Set off - Appellant engaged in manufacture & sale of two-wheeler vehicles - It was granted central excise license by respondent for manufacture of scooters with parts and accessories - Appellant purchase duty paid steel sheets on which duty of excise at rate of Rs. 715 per ton - Modvat Credit Scheme introduced to give credit to manufacturers and allow them benefits of set off of such credit of duty already paid against duty payable them - Some portions used by appellant for manufacture of small parts - Appellant pleaded that, off-cuts portions could not be classified as sheets and their correct classification would be that of waster as scrap - Plea rejected by authorities - Hence instant appeal - Whether portions of cut sheets which were used in manufacture of ancillary items could be regarded as waste and scrap - Held, no - Those portions of cut sheets which were used in manufacture of ancillary items could not be regarded as waste and scrap - Appellant entitled to clear off-cuts of steel sheets by paying duty under tariff entry 72.10 at rate of Rs. 365/- per ton - Orders of Tribunal and Assistant Collector and Collector's order set aside - It was clear that off-cut which were odd shaped were used by appellant do not answer to this description of sheet but on contrary could only be regarded as 'shapes' falling under tariff entry 7210.10 - It was this duty of Rs. 365/- per ton which has to be paid by appellant on those pieces of steel or off-cuts which were cleared by appellant for use in manufacture of ancillary items and not Rs. 715/- per ton as demanded by respondent - Appeal allowed.
