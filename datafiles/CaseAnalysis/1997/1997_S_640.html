Shri Digvijay Cement Company, Etc v State of Rajasthan and Others

Supreme Court of India

21 March 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

G.T. Nanavati, S.P. Kurdukar

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1100; (1997) 5 SCC 406; AIR 1997 SC 2609; JT 1997 (4) SC 340; 1997(3) SCALE 150; [1997] 3 S.C.R. 184; 1997 (106) STC 11; 1997 (5) Supreme 165

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Summary: Sales Tax - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 301 and 303 - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956, (CST Act), s. 8(5) - Notifications D/- 27-06-1990 and 07-03-1994 issued by State of Rajasthan - (A) Whether, impugned notification under Act were issued in public interest? - Held, no - Public interest as sought u/s. 8(5) not justified by state - Object of relevant provisions contained in Central Sales Tax Act Act is to canalize inter state trade and prevent evasion of tax - Public interest defined in s. 8(5) will have to be interpreted in context of Central Sales Tax Act Act and arts. 301 to 304 of Constitution - (B) Whether, impugned notifications were violative of arts. 301 and 303 of Constitution? - Held, Parliament to impose restriction on freedom of trade, commerce and intercourse in public interest - No discrimination to be made or preference to be given to one state over another - Impugned notifications had direct and immediate adverse effect on free flow of trade thus violating arts. 301 and 303 - Appeals allowed.
