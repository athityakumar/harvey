Dhanwanti Joshi v Madhav Unde

Supreme Court of India

04 November 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Jagannadha Rao, S.B. Majmudar

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1353; (1998) 1 SCC 112; 1998 (4) Bom.C.R. 308; 1998 (1) DMC 1; JT 1997 (8) SC 720; 1997(6) SCALE 624; [1997] Supp5 S.C.R. 30; 1997 (9) Supreme 220

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; International

Summary: Family & Personal - Women & Children - Guardians and Ward Act, 1890 - Respondent, who was in USA came to Bombay and filed Habeas Corpus for the custody of the child and said Writ petition was dismissed and custody was granted to appellant by High Court - Respondent filed Case in Family Court, Bombay afresh for custody of child - That petition was clubbed with MJ Petition filed earlier by appellant in City Civil Court - Family Court passed an order allowing custody of child to respondent and dismissed appellant's MJ Petition filed to declare her marriage with respondent as null and void - Appellant preferred appeal to HC but it was dismissed on ground of non prosecution - Could Family Court and HC have ignored orders passed in favour of appellant in habeas corpus case and ex parte order in Guardians and Wards Act case and orders of refusal of HC or Supreme Court to set aside latter orders and could respondent file a fresh case in Family Court to claim custody, and if so whether there is proof of changed circumstances between 1990 and 1993 or 1997 warranting shifting of custody to respondent and whether capacity of respondent to give education to child in USA could alone be sufficient ground to shift custody? - Held, it is clear that HC was clearly in error in giving sole or more importance to superior financial capacity of husband - Assuming that his financial capacity is superior to that of his wife, that cannot be sole ground for disturbing child from his mother's custody - There is no substantial change in circumstances between 1990 and 1993 or 1997 which can justify shift over permanent custody of child from appellant to respondent - Respondent was based upon fact that appellant had, during 1984, removed child from US to India violating court orders passed in that country - It is said she has also not produced child before HC and this conduct disqualified appellant from having custody of child - Facts of case are that when respondent moved courts in India and in proceedings for habeas corpus and under Guardians and Wards Act, courts in India thought it best in interests of child to allow it to continue with mother in India, and those orders have also become final - So, prima facie find any willful disobedience on part of appellant in not producing child before HC warranting shifting of custody to father - Contention of respondent based on violation of earlier orders of US courts or of HC for production of child, is rejected - Appeal allowed.
