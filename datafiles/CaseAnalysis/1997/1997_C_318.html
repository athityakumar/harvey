Allied Motors Private Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax, Delhi

Supreme Court of India

10 March 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, Sujata V. Manohar, K. Venkataswami

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 2600; (1997) 3 SCC 472; AIR 1997 SC 1361; 1997 (139) CTR 364; 1997 (66) DLT 464; [1997] 224 ITR 677; JT 1997 (3) SC 418; 1997(2) SCALE 575; [1997] 2 S.C.R. 780; 1997 (3) Supreme 255; [1997] 91 TAXMAN 205; 1997 (2) TLR 403

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Income Tax and Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 43B - Finance Act, 1987 - Computation of business income - When the deduction is allowed - First proviso to s. 43B should be read with second proviso of the 1961 Act - Memorandum of reasons makes note of the combined effect of s.43B and the first clause inserted by the act - Held, first clause removes the difficulties of tax payers and the second clause was inserted to remove any ambiguity about the term 'any sum payable' - And the explanation is made retrospective - Further both the explanations are complementary - Thus ambit of s. 43-B becomes unduly wide - Appeal allowed.
