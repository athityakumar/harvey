Commissioner of Income Tax v Amalgamations Private Limited (Vice Versa)

Supreme Court of India

25 April 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Agrawal, K.S. Paripoornan

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1971; (1997) 5 SCC 33; AIR 1997 SC 2404; 1997 (140) CTR 313; [1997] 226 ITR 188; JT 1997 (5) SC 50; 1997(3) SCALE 614; [1997] 3 S.C.R. 1005; 1997 (5) Supreme 382; [1997] 92 TAXMAN 132; 1997 (2) TLR 679

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 10(2)(XV) - Remuneration - Allowable Deductions - Nexus of Amount Spent - Assessee/appellant paid excess amount to its managing directing for purpose of its business and claimed allowable deduction which was not allowed by respondent - HC on appeal affirmed action of respondent treating there is no nexus for amount spent for remuneration of manager and functioning of company - Hence, instant appeal - Whether amounts paid by assessee-company to directors of its subsidiary companies can be admissible as a deduction u/s. 10(2)(XV)of the 1922 Act - Held, yes - Very important nexus between assessee company and managed company which necessitated assessee company making payment to employees of managed company taken again it would be possible for assessee company to satisfy expenditure was one which fell within the ambit of s. 10(2)(XV) of the Act - Excess amount paid by assessee to its managing directing was expended wholly and exclusively for purpose of its business and was an allowable deduction u/s. 10(2)(XV) of the Act - Appeal allowed.
