Malkhan Singh v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 January 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

Sujata V. Manohar, A.M. Ahmadi

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 918; (1997) 2 SCC 33; (1997) SCC (L&S) 315; AIR 1997 SC 538; 1997 (75) FLR 786; JT 1997 (1) SC 363; 1997 LabIC 286; 1997 (1) LLN 561; 1997(1) SCALE 132; [1997] 1 S.C.R. 64; 1998 (1) SLJ 187; 1997 (1) SLR 537; 1997 (1) Supreme 283

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Keywords: Roster Points, Brochure On Reservation For Scheduled Castes And Scheduled Tribes

Summary: Service - Advocates & Judges - Brochure on Reservation for Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes - Petitioner, practising advocate, applied for the post of an Addl. District and Sessions Judge in the Delhi Higher Judicial Service - One vacancy reserved for a scheduled caste candidate - Petitioner, scheduled caste - Petitioner not appointed - Both in 1989 and in 1983 the vacancy for a scheduled tribe candidate remained unfilled and the reservation was carried forward while other candidates were recruited - Held, considerable merit in the contention of the petitioner that 1985 was the third recruitment year for the reservation meant for a scheduled tribe candidate and in that year the reservation could have been exchanged for a scheduled caste candidate, especially in view of the definition of "recruitment year" in note (1) to para 11.1 - Benefit of such exchange was not given to the petitioner because of the erroneous view taken by the High Court that the post was not exchangeable in 1985 - Petitioner has by now attained the age of fifty years and he has very fairly accepted that it would not now be appropriate to appoint him as an Addl. District Judge, and he will be content if his stand is vindicated - No order on the Special Leave Petition - Petition disposed of.
