India Carbon Limited and Others v State of Assam

Supreme Court of India

16 July 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Bharucha, M. Jagannadha Rao

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 2083; (1997) 6 SCC 479; AIR 1997 SC 3054; JT 1997 (7) SC 4; 1997(5) SCALE 51; 1997 (106) STC 460; [1997] Supp3 S.C.R. 1; 1997 (7) Supreme 20

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Keywords: Delay In Payment Of Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Indirect Tax - Constitution of India, 1950 - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956 - Assam Sales Tax Act, 1974, s. 35A - Assam Sales Tax Rules, 1974 - Charging of interest - Validity - Appellants manufacture and sell petroleum coke, which were goods declared by s. 14 of the 1956 Act - For the assessment years 1974 to 1980 the appellants were required by the respondents to pay interest at the rate of 24% p.a. thereon, in purported exercise of the provisions of s. 35A of the 1947 Act - Petitions were filed by the appellants challenging the imposition of such interest - There was a difference of opinion - Hence, instant appeal - Whether s. 9(2) of the 1956 Act did not visualise any payment of interest.

Held, there is no substantive provision in the 1956 Act requiring the payment of interest on Central sales tax. The substantive law that the States' sales tax authorities must apply is the 1956 Act. In such application, for procedural purposes alone, the provisions of the State Act are available. The provision relating to interest in the latter part of s. 9(2) of 1956 Act can be employed by the States' sales tax authorities only if the 1956 Act makes a substantive provision for the levy and charge of interest on Central sales tax and only to that extent. There being no substantive provision in the 1956 Act requiring the payment of interest on Central sales tax the States' sales tax authorities cannot, for the purpose of collecting and enforcing payment of Central sales tax, charge interest thereon. The requirement of the respondent's sales tax authorities that the appellants should pay interest at the rate of 24% p.a. on delayed payments of Central sales tax under the provisions of s. 35(A) of the 1974 Act must, therefore, be held to be bad in law. Appeals allowed.
