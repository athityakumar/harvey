Punjab State Electricity Board and Another v Ashwani Kumar

Supreme Court of India

14 March 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, G.B. Pattanaik

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1072; (1997) 5 SCC 120; 1997 (3) CLT 306; JT 1997 (5) SC 182; 1998 (1) MLJ(SC) 1; 1997(3) SCALE 360; [1997] 2 S.C.R. 1143; 1997 (3) Supreme 615

Case Digest

Subject: Electricity & Energy

Summary: Electricity & Energy - Indian Electricity Act, 1910 - Indian Electricity (Supply) Act, 1948 - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, s. 9 - Meter tampering - Allegation - Installation of new meter - Bill raised - Suit filed for permanent injunction restraining Board or its Officers from collecting and recovering amount - Maintainability of suit - Provisions of Acts, machinery provided there under, relevant circulars by Board for subject matter in hand, as to tackling tampered meters, reporting, enquiry proceedings etc. perused and looked for - Held, suit not maintainable - Civil Court shall not be justified in entertaining suit and giving declaration without directing party to avail remedy provided under Acts and instructions issued by Board in that behalf from time to time - Appropriate authority under Act should hear parties, considers objections and pass reasoned order - Departmental appeal there against can be availed of - Appropriate directions issued - Appeal allowed.
