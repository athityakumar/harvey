Bandhua Mukti Morcha v Union Of India & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

21 February 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, S. Saghir Ahmad

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1024; (1997) 10 SCC 549; AIR 1997 SC 2218; 1997 ALJ 1186; JT 1997 (5) SC 285; 1997 LabIC 2107; 1997(3) SCALE 755; [1997] 2 S.C.R. 379; 1997 (4) Supreme 609

Case Digest

Subject: International; Labour & Industrial Law; Municipalities & Local Governments; Tort

Keywords: Hazardous, Personality, Parents, Child Labour, Dignity, Bombay Prohibition Act 1949

Summary: Women and Children - Constitution, arts. 24, 28, 32 - Bombay Prohibition Act 1949 - Child labour - Prohibition - (A) Various directions given to implement constitutional mandate in Previous case - Need for their speedy implementation - (B) Prohibition evolving principles and policy for progressive elimination of children below age of 14 years in various employments - Held, need reiterated - SC directed govt. to convene meeting of concerned Ministers of respective State govts. and their principles secretaries holding concerned Departments - Petition disposed of.
