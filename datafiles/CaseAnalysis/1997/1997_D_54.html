Harapal Singh v Devinder Singh and Another

Supreme Court of India

09 July 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, M.K. Mukherjee

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 2475; (1997) 6 SCC 660; (1997) SCC (Cr) 981; AIR 1997 SC 2914; 1997 (3) CCR 31; 1997 (3) Crimes 23; 1997 CRLJ 3561; JT 1997 (6) SC 10; 1997(4) SCALE 459; 1997 (2) SCJ 475; [1997] Supp1 S.C.R. 648; 1997 (6) Supreme 274

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Delay In Recording Fir, Terrorist Act

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 326 and 302 - Terrorist and Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Act, 1987, ss. 6 and 19 - Terrorist act - Acquittal - Sustainability - Respondents accused allegedly committed offences punishable u/s. 6 of the Act - Trial Court acquitted all the respondents - Aggrieved, appellant/PW 3 preferred instant appeals u/s. 19 of the Act - Whether Trial Court was justified in acquitting the respondents - Held, appellant was resident of hostel in precincts of which incident happened - PW 1 was who gave first information statement to police in which also he claimed to have seen occurrence and in which he narrated incident with all details - PW 1 was one of those who helped injured persons to reach hospital at earliest point of time - Normally, these broad circumstances should ensure that he should ensure that he should certainly have seen occurrence - But Trial Court which declined to place reliance on him noted some flaws in his testimony - One such flaw was failure of police to collect clothes worn by appellant which were smeared with blood during rescue operation - But no investigating agency should take trouble to seize clothes worn by witnesses at time they saw occurrence merely because their clothes too had collected stains of blood during any post event activities - Another reason which Trial Court highlighted against appellant was delay in recording FIR - Trial Court took a pedantic view in instant matter - Little delay in lodging FIR with magistrate should not be viewed from an unrealistic angle - Trial Court omitted to refer to a very important item of evidence while dealing with testimony of appellant - It was the evidence of PW-7 who was a research scholar in University - When PW 7 was proceeding to hostel during lunch recess, he saw appellant and R helping the injured persons to get into a rickshaw and then PW-7 also helped them to reach hospital soon - On the way to hospital, appellant gave an account of occurrence to PW-7 - However, testimony of victim S stands fully corroborated by other eye witness appellant - Trial Court was not justified in acquitting the respondents - Set aside the order of acquittal as against A-1, A-5, A-6 and A-9 - Convicted the A-1 and A-6 u/ss. 34 and 302 IPC and sentenced each to imprisonment for life - Conviction order was issued against A-5 and A-9 u/s. 326 IPC and sentence them to rigorous imprisonment for 5 years - Appeals allowed.
