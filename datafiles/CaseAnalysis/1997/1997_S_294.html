Sahib Singh v State Of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

28 July 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Saghir Ahmad, M.K. Mukherjee

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1937; (1997) 7 SCC 231; (1997) SCC (Cr) 1049; 1997 (35) ACC 355; AIR 1997 SC 3247; 1997 (3) CCR 40; 1997 (3) Crimes 114; 1997 CRLJ 3956; JT 1997 (7) SC 42; 1997(5) SCALE 226; [1997] Supp3 S.C.R. 95; 1997 (7) Supreme 26

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Hallucination

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860, s.300 - Murder - Enmity between parties - Already dead person along with accused and terrorists alleged to have fired gun-shots indiscriminately but no injuries caused to eye-witnesses - Deliberate delayed FIR - Held, case sought to be proved through highly interested witnesses, instead of independent witnesses - Confessional statement of accused is not truthful and is part of the hallucination with which prosecution and its witnesses were suffering - Conviction of accused liable to be set aside.
