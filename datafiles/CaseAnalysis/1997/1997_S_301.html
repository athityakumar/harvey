Ajit Savant Majagavi v State Of Karnataka

Supreme Court of India

14 August 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Saghir Ahmad, M.K. Mukherjee

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 574; (1997) 7 SCC 110; (1997) SCC (Cr) 992; AIR 1997 SC 3255; 1997 (3) CCR 135; 1997 (3) Crimes 204; 1997 CRLJ 3964; JT 1997 (7) SC 414; 1997(5) SCALE 496; 1998 (5) SLT 494; [1997] Supp3 S.C.R. 444; 1997 (7) Supreme 370

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Tort

Summary: Criminal - IPC,1860, s. 300 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 73 - Murder - Circumstantial evidence - Death by strangulation in lodge occupied by the couple - Accused husband informing the room boy that her wife died of heart attack, left the lodge with his 4 months child on pretext of calling relatives - Instead of returning till evening he got him self shaved - Proof of his suspecting infidelity of his wife and was last seen in hercompany before she died - Setting aside of acquittal of accused - (A) Interference sought - (B) Permissibility of comparison of signature as proof u/s. 73 of the Evidence Act - Held, no interference - Power is clearly available u/s. 73 - Appeal dismissed.
