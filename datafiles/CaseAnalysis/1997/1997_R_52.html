Rajasthan State Road Transport Corporation v Kailash Nath Kothari and Others

Supreme Court of India

03 September 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

A.S. Anand, K. Venkataswami

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1312; (1997) 7 SCC 481; AIR 1997 SC 3444; 1998 (1) AWC 508; 1998 (1) Bom.C.R. 466; JT 1997 (7) SC 673; 1997(5) SCALE 680; 1997 (3) SCJ 81; [1997] Supp3 S.C.R. 724; 1997 (8) Supreme 171; 1997 (2) UJ 669

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Practice & Procedure; Tort

Summary: Insurance - Motor Vehicles Act, 1939, ss. 110 B and 2(19) - Compensation - Liability of Rajasthan State Road Transport Corporation (RSRTC) - Hirer in actual possession and control of vehicle at time of accident - Held, owner had not merely transferred services of driver to RSRTC but actual control - Driver was to act under instructions, control and command of conductor and other officers of RSRTC - That being so, RSRTC would be vicariously liable for tort committed by driver - Appeal dismissed.
