Commissioner of Income Tax v Bankipur Club Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 May 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Paripoornan, S. Saghir Ahmad

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 3256; (1997) 5 SCC 394; AIR 1997 SC 2312; 1997 (2) CLT 500; 1997 (140) CTR 102; [1997] 226 ITR 97; JT 1997 (5) SC 191; 1997(4) SCALE 146; 1998 (109) STC 427; [1997] Supp1 S.C.R. 263; 1997 (5) Supreme 208; [1997] 92 TAXMAN 278; 1997 (2) TLR 634

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 5 - Whether assessees', mutual clubs extending various facilities to its members as part of usual privileges, advantages and convenience attached to membership be termed as a 'trading activity'? - Held, since services offered by assessees were not done, with any profit motive, and were not tainted with commerciality - Thus, receipts for various facilities extended by assessees to its members cannot be said to be 'a trading activity' - Appeal dismissed.
