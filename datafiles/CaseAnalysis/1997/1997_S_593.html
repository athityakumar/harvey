State of Orissa and Sri Jagannath Temple Puri Managementcomm v Chintamani Khuntia and Others

Supreme Court of India

17 September 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Sen

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 618

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Family & Personal

Summary: Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 25, 26 and 300 A - Andhra Pradesh Charitable and Hindu Religious Institutions and Endowments Act, 1966, s. 144 - Shri Jagannath Temple (Administration) Act, 1954, ss. 21, 28-(B)(C) and 30 - Religion - (A) Whether right of temple attendants to get a portion of the offerings made to the deity in a temple is a religious right or the manner of collecting and getting a share of the offerings is a religious rite of the temple within arts. 25, 26 and 300 A of constitution - Relied on Tilkayat Shri Govindlalji Maharaj v. The State of Rajasthan &Ors., [1963 Indlaw SC 305] - Held, no question of violation of arts. 25, 26 and 300 A and offerings made to deities are not properties of sevaks - (B) Whether amendment of s. 28-B and 28-C of Jagnnath Temple Act contravenes arts. 25(1), 26 or 300 A - Held, no violation of constitution - Appeal allowed without any costs.
