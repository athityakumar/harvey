State of Punjab and Others v G.S. Gill and Another

Supreme Court of India

27 March 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, G.B. Pattanaik

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1123; (1997) 6 SCC 129; (1997) SCC (L&S) 1475; AIR 1997 SC 2324; AIR 1997 SCW 2209; JT 1997 (4) SC 750; 1997 LabIC 2292; 1997 (4) LLN 104; 1997(3) SCALE 686; [1997] 3 S.C.R. 412; 1997 (4) SLR 18; 1997 (4) Supreme 375

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14 and 16 - Reservation - On single cadre post - Validity of - Whether, judgement of Division Bench that since it is a single post cadre, there could not be any reservation at all, is correct in law? - Held, reservation provided to single post on basis of rule of rotation is not unconstitutional - Promotions in accordance with roster are valid and constitutional - Reservation for dalits and tribes is as a class but not as individuals and, therefore, such a reservation is not violative of art. 14 or art. 16(1) of Constitution - High Court is clearly in error in holding that reservation in promotion to a single post and application of carry forward rule and of roster is unconstitutional - Judgments of Division Bench and of Learned Single Judge are set aside - Appeal allowed.
