Black Diamond Beverages and Another v Commercial Tax Officer, Central Section, Assessment Wing, Calcutta and Others

Supreme Court of India

16 September 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Jagannadha Rao, S.P. Bharucha

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1515; (1998) 1 SCC 458; AIR 1997 SC 3550; JT 1997 (8) SC 128; 1997(6) SCALE 123; 1998 (6) SLT 74; 1997 (107) STC 219; [1997] Supp4 S.C.R. 133; 1997 (8) Supreme 321

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Indirect Tax - Bengal Finance (Sale Tax) Act, 1941 - West Bengal Sales Tax Act, 1954 , S. 2(d) - Freight charges - Handling charges - Money transaction - Sale price - Appellants 'freight charges' could not be included in 'money transaction" in definition of 'sale price' - Material on record and conduct of parties and in particular shows that delivery charges were separately collected as distinct from cost of goods was evidence that they were not part of 'sale price' - Other materials for packing of notified commodities and in absence of a similar inclusion of 'freight' such charges must, by implication be treated as outside 'sale price' - Respondent pointed out that majority of Tax Tribunal had detained that obligation of paying 'freight charges' was on appellant /sellers and therefore it must be treated as included in e 'sale price' u/s. 2(d) of Act - Bengal Taxation Tribunal dismissed these applications - Hence, instant appeal - Whether freight charges and handling charges were to be treated as included within "money consideration' in s.2(d) of Act - Held, 'freight and handling charges' represented expenditure incurred by dealer in making goods, those charges would contribute an addition to cost of goods to dealer and would clearly be a component of price charged from purchaser - Amount of 'freight and handling charges' would be payable under any statutory or other liability but as part of consideration for sale of goods and would form part of 'sale price' - Court did not intend that freight expenses up to point of delivery were not to be included in 'sale price' - Case was clearly distinguishable that payment of 'freight charges' was not obligation of purchasers - HC erred in interfering with orders of Sale Tax Appellate Tribunal it was found on identical facts that 'freight charges' were not part of sale-price - Appeal disposed of.
