High Court of Judicature At Bombay through Its Registrar v Shirish Kumar Rangrao Patil and Another

Supreme Court of India

30 April 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1174; (1997) 6 SCC 339; (1997) SCC (L&S) 1486; AIR 1997 SC 2631; 1998 (1) AWC 146; 1998 (1) Bom.C.R. 309; 1997 (76) FLR 659; 1997 (1) GLH 997; JT 1997 (5) SC 337; 1997 (2) LLN 470; 1997(4) SCALE 181; 1997 (2) SCJ 320; [1997] 3 S.C.R. 1131; 1997 (4) SLR 321; 1997 (5) Supreme 71; 1997 (2) UJ 152

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: (1) Employment-Dismissal-Judicial Officer-Dismissal by Government on recommendations of High Court-Division Bench of High Court setting aside dismissal-Charge of demand by respondent P (Probationer Civil Judge and Judicial Magistrate First Class) of illegal gratification-Order of Governor acting upon recommendation of High Court-Not vitiated by any manifest error of law-Tendency to corrupt activity-More serious and deleterious than actual catch of corrupt judicial officer while demanding and accepting illegal gratification-In absence of demanding of illegal gratification-Different view might be possible-But Supreme Court cannot embark upon appreciation of evidence and reach its own conclusion-Allegation of bias against Enquiry Officer-Afterthought attempt to get over his report-Charge No. 1 standing proved from evidence on record-Not necessary to go into other charges-Appeal allowed.

(2) Constitution of India-Article 235-Control of subordinate judiciary under Article 235-Vested in High Court-Disciplinary action against members of subordinate judiciary-Power of High Court-Delegation to Committee of Five Judges by resolution-Four Judges unanimously recommending to Governor to impose punishment of dismissal of respondent from service-It constitutes quorum-Competent to transact administrative business of High Court.

(3) Judiciary-Independence of judiciary-Is essential attribute of rule of law-Constitution securing independence to judiciary-Heart of judicial independence-Is judicial individualism-Judges are not employees-As members of judiciary-They exercise sovereign judicial power of State.
