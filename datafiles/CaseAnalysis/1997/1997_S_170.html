United India Insurance Co. Ltd. v Shri Gian Chand And Others

Supreme Court of India

02 September 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Majmudar, B.N. Kirpal

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 2747; (1997) 7 SCC 558; 1997 ACC 437; AIR 1997 SC 3824; AIR 1997 SCW 3742; [1998] 94 Comp Cas 418; JT 1997 (7) SC 736; 1998 (1) MLJ(SC) 68; 1997 (3) SCJ 42; [1997] Supp3 S.C.R. 691; 1997 (8) Supreme 208; 1997 (2) UJ 676

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Corporate; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Motor Vehicles Act 1939, s.96(2)(b) - Insurance Company - Exoneration from liability - Breach of term of policy - Driving by unlicensed driver - Finding of fact, insured had given vehicle for driving to unlicensed driver - Held, no liability of Insurance Company.
