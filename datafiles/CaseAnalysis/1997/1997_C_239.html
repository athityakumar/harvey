G. Radhakrishna Murthy and Co. v Commercial Tax Officer-IVB, Vijayawada and Others

Supreme Court of India

20 February 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Sen, Sujata V. Manohar

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 2418; (1997) 8 SCC 37; JT 1998 (4) SC 426; 1999 (113) STC 7

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Sales Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Andhra Pradesh General Sales Tax Act - Item 36 of First Schedule - Classification of Agarbatti - Whether agarbattis were liable to sales tax under Item 36 of First Schedule to Act? - Department's stand is that 'perfumes' would include agarbattis and this stand found favour with High Court - Agarbattis cannot be construed as either cosmetics or toilet preparations - Having regard to nature of goods included in entry, 'perfumery' could not have been given an extended meaning to include agarbattis - No special definition was provided in Act by which agarbattis were included in perfumery - Held, neither in common parlance nor by dictionary meaning nor having regard to context of Item 36 of First Schedule of Act can it be said that 'perfumes' would include agarbattis for purpose of imposition of sales tax - Appeal allowed.
