Commissioner of Income Tax v Electric Control Gear Manufacturing Company

Supreme Court of India

08 July 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Agrawal, G.B. Pattanaik

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 2844; (1997) 6 SCC 502; 1997 (141) CTR 302; [1997] 227 ITR 278; JT 1997 (6) SC 233; 1997(4) SCALE 558; [1997] Supp1 S.C.R. 570; 1997 (6) Supreme 330; [1997] 93 TAXMAN 384

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 41(2) - Applicability of s. 41(2) - Consideration received for transfer of business was not attributed to assets like machinery, plant or building separately - Nothing to indicate price attributable to assets like machinery, plant or building out of consideration amount - Merely because a sum of Rs. 3,32,863 had been allowed as depreciation to assessee-firm, it could not be said, that was excess amount between price and written down value - Question answered against Revenue - Referred section not applicable - Appeal partly allowed.
