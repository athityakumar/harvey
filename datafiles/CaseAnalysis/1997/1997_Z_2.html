Satish Kumar v Zarif Ahmed and Others

Supreme Court of India

20 February 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, S. Saghir Ahmad

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1016; (1997) 3 SCC 679; 1997 (30) ALR 189; 1997 (3) AWC 1841; 1997 (2) CLT 138; JT 1997 (3) SC 457; 1997 (2) MLJ(SC) 125; 1998 (89) RD 373; 1997 (1) RentLR 762; 1997(2) SCALE 554; [1997] 2 S.C.R. 336; 1997 (3) Supreme 404; 1997 (1) UJ 576

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, ss. 3 and 53(A) - Registration Act, 1908, ss. 49, 107 and 17(1)(d) - Indian Stamp Act, 1899, s. 2(14) - (A) Whether, the unregistered lease deed is admissible in evidence? - (B) Whether, a lease of immoveable property from month to month or for 11 months is a compulsorily registrable document, through it was reduced to writing as an instrument defined u/s. 2(14) of the Stamp Act? - Held, the combined effect of all the provisions is that an unregistered leases deed executed from month to month for a period not exceeding 11 months, though reduced to writing and possession is delivered thereunder to a tenant, is not a compulsorily registrable instrument - Therefore, the prohibition contained in s. 49 of the Registration Act is inapplicable - The document is admissible in evidence to consider the effect of the immovable property contained therein or to receive as an evidence of any transaction vis-a-vis such property - Appeal allowed.
