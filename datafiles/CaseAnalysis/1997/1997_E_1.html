Superintendent of Post Offices and Others v E. Kunhiraman Nair Muliyar

Supreme Court of India

10 September 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

G.T. Nanavati, Sujata V. Manohar

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1575; (1998) 9 SCC 255; (1998) SCC (L&S) 956; 1999 (1) CLR 18; JT 1998 (7) SC 393; 1999 (3) LLJ 1215

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service

Keywords: Termination

Summary: Service - Constitution - Constitution of Indian, 1950, arts. 14, 16 - Extra Departmental Agents (Conduct & Service) Rules, 1964, r. 6 - Appointment - Termination -Sustainability - Respondent was temporarily and provisionally appointed as extra departmental branch postmaster with appellant department for period which was extended later - Respondents services was then terminated with immediate effect - Order of termination by memo on 23-9-1978 was not on record - Respondent made representation against termination but Director of Postal Services upheld order of termination - Respondent filed petition before HC - Petition was allowed and directed his reinstatement with consequential benefits - Hence instant appeal - Whether order of HC was sustainable - Held, terms and conditions of employment as contained in order of appointment provided for termination without assigning any reason and without notice - In instant case, termination was within 9 months of initial appointment and was on administrative grounds which was in accordance with terms and conditions of service applicable to respondent and r. 6 - Respondents order of termination was in breach of arts. 14 and 16 of the Constitution and hence it was arbitrary because his juniors were retained - There was no material on record which showed whether any junior of respondent was retained or not - When termination of services of employee was on administrative grounds, question of any other person being retained was not really germane to retention or discharge of employee concerned - If there were administrative difficulties relating to particular individual, he should not be retained and his junior discharged - Therefore, there should be no presumption of arbitrariness simply because somebody junior to employee concerned remains in service - Only reason why HC had held termination of service as arbitrary was because, according to HC, reasons for termination were not set out in order of termination - Amended order of termination mentioned that termination was on administrative grounds - Order was in accordance with r. 6 - It was in accordance with respondent's terms of appointment - Such termination of services of temporary employee should not be termed as arbitrary - Impugned order of HC was set aside - Appeal allowed.
