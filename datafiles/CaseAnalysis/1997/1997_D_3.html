M/S. Faridabad Ct Scan Centre v D. G. Health Services & Ors

Supreme Court of India

15 September 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

Sujata V. Manohar, B.N. Kirpal

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 612; (1997) 7 SCC 752; AIR 1997 SC 3801; 1997 (4) CLT 16; 1997 (72) ECR 801; 1997 (95) E.L.T. 161; JT 1997 (8) SC 171; 1997(6) SCALE 148; 1997 (6) SLR 540; [1997] Supp4 S.C.R. 122; 1998 (5) Supreme 287

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950 - Benefit - Petition for special leave was dismissed by an order passed by a bench of Two Judges - In view, however, of a judgement of another Bench of two Judges, in a similar matter Mediwell hospital & Health Care Pvt. Ltd. vs. Union of India & Ors. 1996 Indlaw SC 2044, the order was recalled - Whether order was rightly recalled.

Held, the court, granted relief to the appellant in matter Mediwell hospital & Health Care Pvt. on the ground that several other individual diagnostic centres not attached to any hospital had been granted the exemption under the notification in question and hence there should not be any discrimination against the appellant u/art. 14 of Constitution. The relief was granted entirely on the basis of art. 14 of Constitution. However, wrong orders cannot be perpetuated with the help of art. 14 of Constitution on such wrong orders were earlier passed in favour of some other persons and, therefore, there will be discrimination against them. The benefits of the exemption notification, in the instant case, cannot, therefore, be extended to the petitioner on the ground that such benefit has been wrongly extended to others. Petition dismissed.
