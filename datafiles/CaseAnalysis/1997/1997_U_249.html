All India Judges' Assn. v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

29 September 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

G.N. Ray, B.N. Kirpal, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1558; (1997) 8 SCC 520

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Administrative - Revision of pay scales - Status Report - Solicitor General suggested Central Govt. that terms of reference of Shetty Commission be enlarged - Object of reference was to include revision of pay scales and other service conditions of staff attached to Subordinate Courts - Exercise before Commission was not confined merely to examining matter relating to Subordinate Judges alone - Solicitor General added question of enlargement of terms of reference of Commission will be considered shortly - Members of Subordinate Judiciary made grievance that Commission was not empowered to make any interim recommendations - Hence instant petition.

Held, all State Govt. to file comprehensive Status Report of action taken by each of them to implement directions and also to implement other decisions taken by Govt. on basis of any other recommendations for providing the facilities needed by members of subordinate judiciary to discharge their duties effectively and properly. Status Report with regard to provision of residential accommodation and library at residence of each of Subordinate Judge, which is of paramount importance to enable Subordinate Judges to discharge their duties, must be indicated by each State Govt. in statement required to be filed by each of them. Personal involvement of Advocate General of each State who, by virtue of his Office, is naturally bound to be concerned with proper administration of justice in the State is essential. It is directed to issue notice to Advocates General of States requesting them to appear personally in these proceedings to ensure proper attention being given by their State Govt. emphasising on all persons concerned the great significance of the subject-matter of these proceedings. Order accordingly.
