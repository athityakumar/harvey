Messrs Modern Tailoring Hall v Shri H.S. Venkusa and Others

Supreme Court of India

24 April 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Venkataswami, M.M. Punchhi

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1153; (1997) 5 SCC 315; AIR 1997 SC 2453; JT 1997 (5) SC 92; 1997 (1) RCR(Rent) 624; 1997 (1) RentLR 707; 1997(3) SCALE 705; [1997] 3 S.C.R. 984; 1997 (5) Supreme 198; 1997 (2) UJ 64

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Keywords: Right to re-entry

Summary: Rent Control - Karnataka Rent Control Act, 1986, ss. 21(1)(h), (1)(j) - Eviction - Right to re-entry - Respondent landlord sought eviction of tenants u/s. 21(1)(h) of the Act on ground that he bona fide required premises for his own use and occupation but after demolition and reconstruction - Whether after demolition and reconstruction of building, appellants-tenants have right of re-entry - Held, s. 21(1)(h) and s. 21 (1)(j) were mutually exclusive and that demolition and reconstruction of premises by landlord for his own use and occupation, after getting order of eviction, clearly falls under Cls. (h) of s. 21(1) and not under cls. (j) - It had been viewed that plea of landlord for bona fide requirement, for his own use and occupation of premised u/s. 21(1)(h), would include occupation of premises after making any alteration or new construction on securing order of eviction - Provisions relating to demolition and reconstruction saddled with obligation to provide to tenant re-entry could not possibly apply to case where landlord reasonably and bona fidely required premises for his own occupation even if he had to priorly demolish premises and erect new building on it - And further it was viewed that qualitatively eviction u/s. 21 (1)(j) of the Act would apply to cases where landlord did not require premises for his own occupation, but required it for erecting new building to be let out to tenants - Appeal dismissed.
