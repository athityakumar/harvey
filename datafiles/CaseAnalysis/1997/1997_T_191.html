Process Technicians and Analysts' Union v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 March 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, Sujata V. Manohar

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 2578; (1997) 10 SCC 142; (1997) SCC (L&S) 1415; AIR 1997 SC 1288; JT 1997 (4) SC 44; 1997 LabIC 1500; 1997 (1) LLJ 1217; 1997 (2) LLN 534; [1997] 2 S.C.R. 798; 1997 (2) SLR 434

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Public Sector, Parity, Life Insurance Corporation (Modification of Settlements) Act, 1976, Payment Of Bonus (Amendment) Act, 1976, Bharat Petroleum Corporation Limited (Determination of Conditions of Service of Employee) Act, 1988

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Bharat Petroleum Corporation Ltd. (Determination of Conditions of Service of Employees) Act, 1988 - S. 3 - Burmah Shell (Acquisition of Undertakings In India) Act, 1976 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Life Insurance Corporation (Modification of Settlements) Act, 1976 - Constitution - (A) Whether those who are governed by a statutory scheme can compare themselves with employee who entered into a negotiated settlement with their employer? - And the charge of discrimination under art. 14, can be sustained in this regard? - Held, no - (B) Whether the amended scheme of 1996 is valid exerise of power under the Act of 1988? - Held, yes - The employees who are members of the appellant union being the only set of employees who have not entered into a settlement with their employer, have necessarily to be provided for under a statutory scheme - They cannot expect a statutory scheme to give them the benefits of the settlements which the other employees have entered into with the employer - The amended scheme of 1996 is not framed by the employer - It is framed by the Central Govt. under the statutory provisions of the 1988 Act - The amended scheme of 1996 gives substantial additional benefits to the employees - The amended scheme of 1996 is also a valid exercise of power under the Act of 1988 - Appeal dismissed.
