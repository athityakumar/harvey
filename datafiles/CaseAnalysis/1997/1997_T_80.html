State of Tamil Nadu and Others v A. Gurusamy

Supreme Court of India

17 February 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, G.T. Nanavati

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 832; (1997) 3 SCC 542; AIR 1997 SC 1199; JT 1997 (3) SC 346; 1997 (2) MLJ(SC) 49; 1997(2) SCALE 344; [1997] 2 S.C.R. 104; 1998 (1) SLJ 179; 1997 (2) Supreme 758; 1997 (1) UJ 668

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Constitution - Civil Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 341(1) and 366(24) - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 9 - (A) Jurisdiction of Civil Court - Declaration of lists of Scheduled and Scheduled Tribes - Held, no - Declaration of President of India, u/arts. 341 and 342 of Constitution, with respect of lists of SC and ST in relation to a State, that a particular caste or tribe is defined in arts. 366(24) or 366(25) respectively, is conclusive subject to an amendment by Parliament u/arts. 341(2) and 342(2) of Constitution - By necessary implication, jurisdiction of Civil Court to take cognizance of and give a declaration stands prohibited - Declaration issued by courts below is unconstitutional and without jurisdiction - (B) Caste certificate - Cancellation of - Respondent was scheduled caste - Subsequently he obtained certificate of being Scheduled Tribe - Whether respondent has been given an opportunity to establish his case before authorities canceled his community certificate obtained by him? - Held, yes - Order of District Collector clearly mentions that an opportunity was given to respondent and he himself had examined him - Authority had, therefore, given an opportunity to respondent to establish his status - (C) Whether principle of estoppel applies to respondent? - No promise made by State that State would protect perpetration of fraud defeating constitutional objective, no promise was made that his false certificate will be respected and accepted by State - Appeal allowed.
