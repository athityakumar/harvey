Steel Authority Of India Ltd. v Collector Of Central Excise, Bolpur

Supreme Court of India

20 February 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Majmudar

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 3313; (1997) 3 SCC 34; AIR 1997 SC 1103; 1997 (69) ECR 503; 1997 (90) E.L.T. 287; 1997 (2) ESC 887; JT 1997 (2) SC 716; 1997 (19) RLT 23; 1997(2) SCALE 285; 1997 (2) Supreme 489

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944, s. 35-L - Central Excise Rules, 1944 - Whether CEGAT erred in upholding demand of excise department for duty on iron in crude? - CEGAT had failed to appreciate that though duty on input of pig iron at prior stage was not paid, whole of that duty got paid on clearance of steel ingots which were main final product and emergence of a minor by-product like steel melting scrap which might have been cleared on payment of nil duty had no impact whatsoever for enabling department to once again bring to tax same input of pig iron - Judgment and order of CEGAT set aside - Appeal allowed.
