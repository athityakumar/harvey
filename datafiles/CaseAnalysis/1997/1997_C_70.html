General Court-Martial and Others v Col. Aniltej Singh Dhaliwal

Supreme Court of India

12 December 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Srinivasan, M.M. Punchhi

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1216; (1998) SCC (Cr) 496; (1998) 1 SCC 756; AIR 1998 SC 983; 1998 (1) CCR 66; 1998 (1) Crimes 62; 1998 CRLJ 1402; 1998 (78) FLR 414; JT 1997 (10) SC 110; 1998 (1) RCR(Criminal) 404; 1997(7) SCALE 717; 1998 (1) SLT 463; [1997] Supp6 S.C.R. 470; 1998 (1) UPLBEC 242

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Army Act, 1950, ss. 154 and 191 - Army Rules, 1954, r. 182 - Appeal - Accused an army officer - Held guilty by court martial - High Court quashing order of court martial - On basis of report of Lt. Col. fixing responsibility to junior of accused - No omission by court martial to consider on said report - Validity - Held, interfering and setting aside finding of court martial by HC - Improper and erroneous.
