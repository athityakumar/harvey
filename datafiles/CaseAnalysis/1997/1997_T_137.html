M/S. Jonas Woodhead & Sons (India) Ltd. Madras v The Commissioner Of Income-Tax, Tamil Nadu Iii, Madras

Supreme Court of India

11 February 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 968; (1997) 10 SCC 119; AIR 1997 SC 1105; 1997 (138) CTR 275; 1997 (138) CTR 283; [1997] 224 ITR 342; JT 1997 (2) SC 485; 1997(2) SCALE 67; [1997] 1 S.C.R. 1145; 1997 (3) Supreme 283; [1997] 91 TAXMAN 1; 1997 (2) TLR 285

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 256(1) - A/Ys 1967-68, 1968-69 - Income Tax - Royalty - Capital expenditure - Enduring benefit - Disallowance - Challenge - Appellant/assessee, to carry on business of manufacture of automobiles springs, entered into agreement with firm based in United Kingdom (UK) - Under terms and conditions of agreement between parties, foreign firm would provide assessee technical know-how relating to setting up of plant, appellant made payments of Rs. 24,000/- and Rs. 47,000/- respectively to foreign firm for A/ys 1967-68 and 1968-69 as royalty - In assessment proceedings Income Tax Officer (ITO) disallowed 1/4th of said payments on ground that such payment represented consideration for service provided by foreign company of enduring nature and therefore would come under capital receipt - Assessee preferred appeal before Appellate Assistant Commissioner who dismissed appeal - Further appeal was dismissed by Tribunal which referred question of law, u/s. 256(1) of the Act, to HC following application by appellant - HC by its impugned Orders answered question of law in favour of revenue and against appellant - Hence instant appeals - Whether Tribunal was right in holding that 25 per cent of amount paid by appellant as royalty was capital expenditure and therefore not allowable as revenue expenditure under provisions of the 1961 Act, for A/ys 1967-68 and 1968- 69 - Held, no single definitive criterion by itself could be determinative and, therefore, bearing in mind changing economic realities of business and varieties of situational diversities, various clauses of agreement were to be examined - In instant case Tribunal having considered different clauses of agreement and having come to conclusion that under agreement with foreign firm what was set up by assessee was new business and foreign firm had not only furnished information and technical know-how but rendered valuable services in setting up of factory itself and even after expiry of agreement there was no embargo on assessee to continue to manufacture the product in question, it would be difficult to hold that entire payment made was revenue expenditure merely because payment was required to be made on certain percentage of rates of gross turnover of products of income as royalty - On the facts and circumstances of instant case, HC was fully justified in answering reference in favour of revenue and against assessee - Alembic Chemical Works Co. Ltd. v. Commissioner of Income-Tax, Gujarat, 1989 Indlaw SC 559, relied on - Appeals dismissed.
