Pardeep Aggarbatti, Ludhiana v State of Punjab and Others

Supreme Court of India

23 October 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Bharucha, S.C. Sen

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 2993; (1997) 8 SCC 511; AIR 1998 SC 171; 1997 (96) E.L.T. 219; JT 1997 (8) SC 581; 1997(6) SCALE 569; 1998 (5) SLT 460; 1997 (107) STC 561; [1997] Supp4 S.C.R. 692; 1997 (9) Supreme 215

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Summary: Indirect Tax - Sales Tax - Punjab General Sales Tax Act, 1948 - Dhoop and aggarbatti - Assessment to sales tax - Whether dhoop and aggarbatti fell within the ambit of the said Entry No.16.

Held, word 'perfumery' in the said Entry No.16 draws colour from the words 'cosmetics' and 'toilet goods' therein and that, so read, the word 'perfumery' in the said Entry No.16 can only refer to such articles of perfumery as are used, as cosmetics and toilet goods are, upon the person. The word 'perfumery' in the context in which it, is used has, therefore, no application to 'dhoop' and 'aggarbatti'. Appeal allowed.
