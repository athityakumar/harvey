Mahendra Raghunathdas Gupta v Vishvanath Bhikaji Mogul & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

10 April 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Wadhwa, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 2208; (1997) 5 SCC 329; AIR 1997 SC 2437; 1998 (1) Bom.C.R. 304; JT 1997 (5) SC 363; 1997 (1) RentLR 723; 1997(4) SCALE 166; 1997 (2) SCJ 501; [1997] 3 S.C.R. 840; 1997 (5) Supreme 62

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Rent Control - Land & Property - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, ss. 106 and 108 - Bombay Rents, Hotel and Lodging House Rents Control Act, 1947, s. 12 - (A) Whether appellant committed any default or was in arrears in payment of rent? - (B) Whether tender of rent by cheque is a valid tender? - After receipt of notice rent was tendered to advocate and advocate refused to accept - Landlord shall not be entitled to recovery of possession of demised premises as long as tenant pays or is ready and wiling to pay amount of standard rent - Two conditions must be satisfied for eviction of a tenant on ground of default in payment of rent or permitted increases - Held, (A) merely because tenant has made payment of rent by cheque, it cannot be assumed that tenant was not ready or willing to pay arrears of rent - (B) tendering amount within 30 days to agent on behalf of principal, is a legal tender of amount of arrears of rent - Thereby, by operation of s. 12(3)(a) appellant has tendered amount - Thereby, he has not committed any default in payment of rent on account of which he is not liable to be ejected from demised premises - It cannot be said that tenant was not ready and willing to pay arrears of rent or that he neglected to make payment thereof in terms of notice - Attornment by tenant is not necessary to confer validity of transfer of landlord's rights - Tendering amount within 30 days to agent on behalf of principal, is a legal tender of amount of arrears of rent - Appeal allowed.
