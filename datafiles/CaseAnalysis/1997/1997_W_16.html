Management of Dandakaranya Project, Koreput v Workman Through Rehabilitation Employees Union and Another

Supreme Court of India

07 January 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 919; (1997) 2 SCC 296; (1997) SCC (L&S) 434; AIR 1997 SC 852; 1997 (2) AWC 1128; 1997 (75) FLR 357; JT 1997 (1) SC 331; 1997 LabIC 858; 1997 (1) LLJ 833; 1997 (2) LLN 39; 1997(1) SCALE 199; 1997 (1) SCJ 120; [1997] 1 S.C.R. 55; 1997 (1) SLR 533; 1997 (1) Supreme 390; 1997 (1) UPLBEC 281

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Closure, Public Sector, Rehabilitation

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 10 and 25(fff) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Whether, the demands raised by Rehabilitation Employees Union of the management of Dandakaranya Project, Koraput, are justified, if so, to what relief the concerned workmen are entitled to and from what date? - Held, even after coming to the conclusion that the project has been wound up and there are no employment facilities for these NMR workers for regular absorption yet the Tribunal issued the direction requiring the project authorities to find out work for the NMR workers - It may be stated that the assets and liabilities have been transferred to the State of Orissa and State of MP, yet on account of an interim order passed by this court the 425 NMR workers are sitting idle and getting wages to the tune of Rs 1.50 lacs per month which is undoubtedly an unnecessary financial strain on the public exchequer - In lieu of the said circumstances any direction to pay compensation will not be in the interests of justice - Appeal allowed.
