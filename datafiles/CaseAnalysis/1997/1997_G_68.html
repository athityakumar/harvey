Jagdish A. Sadarangani v Government of India

Supreme Court of India

27 November 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Kirpal, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 2865; (1998) 8 SCC 409; 1998 (146) CTR 105; [1998] 230 ITR 442; JT 1998 (9) SC 241; 1998(1) SCALE 1; 1998 (4) SLT 212; [1998] 98 TAXMAN 59

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Constitution - Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Finance Act, 1995 - Income-tax Act, 1961, s. 269UC - Transfer of Property Act, 1882 - Immovable Property - Appellant entered into an agreement with the executors for the purchase of urban property in the City of Madras for certain amount - On the same date, an application was filed before the Appropriate Authority, Madras - The Appropriate Authority addressed a letter seeking certain clarifications - Appellant submitted his reply to the said objections but the Appropriate Authority, by order, held that in view of the prohibition in s. 6 of the Urban Ceiling Act the agreement entered into between the parties to transfer the entire land, shall be deemed to be null and void - It was advised to petitioner, to file a fresh Form for transfer of the balance land or after finality was reached with regard to the excess vacant land - Feeling aggrieved by the said order, appellant filed a W.P. in the Madras HC which was allowed by a learned single judge by his judgment - The learned single judge directed respondents to reconsider the matter and pass appropriate orders keeping in view the said judgment - Respondent filed an appeal against the said judgment which had been allowed by the Division Bench of HC by the impugned judgment - HC had held that keeping in view of s. 269UC(4), the order passed by the Appropriate Authority was valid since the agreement was unenforceable and void being in violation of the provisions of s. 6 of the Urban Ceiling Act - Hence, the present appeal - Whether appeal relates to the interpretation of the provisions of s. 269UC(4) of the 1961 Act - Held, sub-s. (4) was inserted in s. 269UC by the 1995 Act - The learned judges had held that sub-s. (4) of s. 269UC enables the appropriate authority to return the statement in Form No. 37-I that was submitted u/s. 269UC(2) if it was found that the agreement suffers from a basic defect and was void or unenforceable - The learned judges had referred to the objects and reasons appended to the Bill which was enacted as the 1995 Act - Court had carefully perused the said reasons given by the learned judges of HC in the impugned judgment as well as the objects and reasons of the Bill which was enacted as the 1995 Act - Order accordingly.
