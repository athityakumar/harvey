Food Corporation of India Etc v State of Kerala

Supreme Court of India

06 January 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Venkataswami, Sujata V. Manohar

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 904; (1997) 3 SCC 410; AIR 1997 SC 1252; JT 1997 (2) SC 243; 1997(1) SCALE 70; [1997] 1 S.C.R. 24; 1997 (105) STC 4; 1997 (3) Supreme 653

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Keywords: Oil and Natural Gas Commission, Food Corporations Act, 1964, Market Fee

Summary: Sales Tax - Indirect Tax - Essential Commodities Act, 1955, s. 3 - Uttar Pradesh Sales Tax Act, 1948, s. 2(h) - Food Corporations Act, 1964 - Food grains - Procurement by FCI - Sales tax - Liability - Legality - Food Corporation of India (FCI) was incorporated under the 1964 Act - Different States should make their contributions to enable FCI to perform one of its functions of maintaining national pool of food grains - State Govt issued different orders under the 1955 Act for purchasing part of produce or stocks of food grains from farmers or millers - Procurement was made through different agencies - On obtaining required quantity of food grains, it was purchased by FCI from State Govts for maintaining national pool of food grains - Sales Tax Department of UP Govt sought to levy purchase tax upon FCI on point it made purchases from State Govt to which FCI denied its liability to pay tax - DB of Allahabad HC had taken view that FCI was liable to pay purchase tax under the provisions of the 1948 Act - Hence, instant appeal by FCI - Appellant contended that levy procurement was compulsory acquisition and therefore, would fall outside purview of Entry 54 of List II of 7th Schedule to Constitution of India and levy procurement was not at all taxable under the 1948 Act - Whether FCI was liable to pay sales/purchase tax to States while purchasing food grains or in distributing fertilizers pursuant to orders issued u/s. 3 of the 1955 Act - Held, it was shown that FCI reserved right to accept or reject offer of State - On facts, SC was satisfied that some area of consensual arrangement and some field for volition was left untouched by legislation in all disputed transaction - Such disputed transactions were sales, might be, under compulsion of statute and nevertheless they were sales exigible to tax - Hence, levy procurement was sale/purchase and therefore, would fall within purview of Entry 54, List II of Seventh Schedule to Constitution - States were competent to levy sales tax on such transactions - View taken by full bench of Allahabad HC in Commissioner of Sales Tax v. Ram Bilas Ram Gopal, 1969 Indlaw ALL 25, case was correct, and HC (Lucknow Bench) was right in applying same in the judgment under appeal - It was also held that view of Punjab and Haryana HC challenged in instant case taking different view had not laid down correct law and decision, in M.s Chitter Mai Narain Das v. Commissioner of Sales Tax,1970 Indlaw SC 321 holding that levy procurement was compulsory acquisition and not sale, would be no longer good law in the light of later larger Bench decisions of SC -

Further, as transactions falling under Levy Orders would amount to sales there should be no difficulty in approving view taken by HC that the activity of distribution of fertilizers would amount to sale exigible to sale tax - Appeals disposed of.
