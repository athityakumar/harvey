Lakshman Swarup Om Prakash v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

06 May 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Wadhwa, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 2017; (1997) 7 SCC 245; AIR 1998 SCW 4072; [1998] 229 ITR 662; JT 1997 (10) SC 683; [1998] 97 TAXMAN 354; 1999 (2) TLR 161

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Civil Procedure; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Debt Laws

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, s. 73 - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 226(4) - Various persons held decrees against M/s John Mills - In proceedings for execution of those decrees, decree-holder, sought to attach various sums of money which were lying to credit of said judgment-debtor in Court of Civil Judge - Decree-holder moved an application for rateable distribution u/s. 73 of CPC, 1908 - UOI, moved an application u/s. 226(4) of Act stating that large sums of money were due as tax against judgment-debtor and UOI has a priority over claims of other creditors - In said application it was asserted that UOI had a right to attach and realize amounts which are lying in Court to credit of judgment-debtor - Said application was dismissed by Court - UOI filed revision petition before High Court which has been allowed - Whether on 31-8-1973 when application u/s. 226(4) was moved by respondent any money belonging to judgment-debtor was in custody of Executing Court? - Held, u/s. 73, CPC, 1908, money lying in Executing Court continues to belong to judgment-debtor till it is disbursed among decree-holders or other creditors of judgment-debtor - On 31-8-1973 date on which application u/s. 226(4) of Act was filed by Respondent 1, cheques sent by Civil Judge to Executing Court were lying with Executing Court - Payment was actually made to appellant on 23-4-1974, i.e., after passing of order D/- 4-3-1974, rejecting application u/s. 226(4) filed by Respondent 1 - HC was, therefore, right in holding that on date of filing of application u/s. 226(4) of Act money had not been distributed to appellant decree-holders and was lying with Executing Court - Executing court was, therefore, in error in rejecting application filed by UOI u/s. 226(4) - Appeal dismissed.
