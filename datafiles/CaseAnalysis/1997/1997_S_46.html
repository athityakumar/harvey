Union Public Service Commission v S. Papaiah and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 September 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

A.S. Anand, K. Venkataswami

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 2997; (1997) 7 SCC 614; (1997) SCC (Cr) 1112; AIR 1997 SC 3876; 1998 (1) AWC 676; 1997 (4) CCR 17; 1998 (3) Crimes 40; 1997 CRLJ 4636; JT 1997 (8) SC 24; 1997(6) SCALE 39; [1997] Supp4 S.C.R. 56; 1998 (5) Supreme 329

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120-B, 420, 381, 468 and 478 - Code of Criminal Procedure, s. 173 - IFS exam - Use of unfair means - Complaint - Investigation - Closure report - Challenge - Appellant/Union PUblic Service Commission (UPSC) on 27-9-1993 sent complaint to Joint director, CBI alleging use of unfair means, at Urdu Hall sub-centre at Hyderabad, by respondent no. 1 in collusion with respondent no. 2 in charge of said centre - Appellant requested CBI to investigate case on priority basis and intimate results of investigation to it - CBI registered case and after investigation filed closure report before magistrate - CBI intimated appellant about closure of case upon being insisted by magistrate - Appellant addressed letter dt. 13-1-1995, pointing out serious issues, to CBI director stating that it was informed that CBI decided to close case and pointed out that investigation was not carried out properly - Despite said letter, CBI resubmitted final report and same was accepted by magistrate, on 16-3-1995, and closed the case - Appellant by its letter dt. 6-4-1995 reminded CBI about its letter following which CBI replied that case was closed - On receipt of communication from CBI, Appellant filed petition before magistrate at Hyderabad and requested for high level investigation as case involved alleged cheating during All India Service Examination - Court rejected petition - Revision petition filed by Appellant was also dismissed on ground that final report was accepted following proper procedure and it did not require any interference - Hence instant appeal - Appellant contended that observations of revisional court that notice had been issued to appellant 'as directed by lower court' and inspite that notice appellant had not filed any objections was clearly erroneous and against record and omission to issue notice before accepting final report vitiated order of court accepting final report - Held, issuance of notice by Magistrate to informant at time of consideration of final report would be 'must' - This binding precedent, law of land, had not been followed by magistrate and wrongly ignored by revisional court too - In first place, issuance of notice by CBI to appellant was not substitute for notice which was required to be given by Magistrate in terms of judgment in Bhagwant Singh's case - CBI also did not issue any fresh notice to appellant before it resubmitted final report to Magistrate on 24-2-1995 - Magistrate could not in any event 'delegate' to investigating agency its function of issuing notice - Further, when law required particular thing to be done in particular manner, it must be done in that manner and in no other manner - Bhagwant Singh v. Commissioner of Police & Anr., 1985 Indlaw SC 48, relied on - Matter remitted back to Magistrate who should issue directions to CBI to further investigate case and collect further evidence keeping in view points raised by appellant and then proceed further in the matter - It was further held that, CBI should be directed to complete investigation expeditiously and proceed in matter in accordance with law - Appeal allowed.
