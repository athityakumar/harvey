Union of India and Others v Barmalt (India) Limited, Gurgaon

Supreme Court of India

18 February 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, S.B. Majmudar

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 838; (1997) 5 SCC 748; 1997 (66) DLT 762; 1997 (90) E.L.T. 281; JT 1997 (2) SC 738; 1997(2) SCALE 225; [1997] 2 S.C.R. 125; 1997 (2) Supreme 282

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Evacuee Property & Displaced Persons

Summary: Excise - Whether malt and malt extract produced by respondent, Barmalt (India) Pvt. Limited, falls within expression 'food product' in Exemption Notification No. 55 of 1975 D/- 01-03-1975? - Held, malt and malt extract do quality as food products and therefore respondent has been rightly held entitled to benefit of aforesaid notification - No duty was payable on clearance/removal of malt and malt extract by virtue of Exemption Notification - Whether respondent is entitled to refund of excess duty paid by him pending decision of High Court? - Held, in view of admitted fact that it has passed on burden of duty to its purchasers that barmalt should refund to state amount received by them by way of refund - Appeal allowed.
