U.P. Secretariat U.D.A. Associationthrough Its Joint Secreta v State Of Uttar Pradesh And Others

Supreme Court of India

27 January 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

G.T. Nanavati, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 2328; (1999) 1 SCC 278; (1999) SCC (L&S) 289; 1997 (75) FLR 810; JT 1997 (2) SC 461; 1997 LabIC 1021; 1997 (1) LLJ 918; 1997(1) SCALE 752; [1997] 1 S.C.R. 586; 1997 (1) SLR 507; 1997 (2) Supreme 651; 1997 (1) UPLBEC 592

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Rules, 1942, r. 21 - Whether initial appointment of promotees on officiating basis was in accordance with Rules, 1942? - Merely because temporary appointment or promotion came to be made, seniority cannot be counted from date of officiation except when appointment was made in accordance with rules - Mere inaction cannot be made ground to contend that quota rule was broken down - Held, appointments are not according to Rules and within quota - Division Bench rightly held that direct recruit is to be treated from date on which he actually joined service, though vacancies did exist prior to that - Petition dismissed.
