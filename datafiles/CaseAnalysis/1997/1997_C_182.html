Pravara Pulp and Paper Mills v Collector of Central Excise

Supreme Court of India

04 November 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Wadhwa, Sujata V. Manohar

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1351; (1998) 9 SCC 573; 1998 (75) ECR 511; 1997 (96) E.L.T. 497; JT 1998 (9) SC 201; 1997 (23) RLT 890

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Justifiability, Exemption of duty

Summary: Excise - Indirect Tax - Practice & Procedure - Exemption of duty - Denial - Justifiability - Appellant manufacturers of craft paper - Appellant filed their price lists for approval of prices for their product 'paper' claiming partial exemption from excise duty, which was approved - Appellants paid concessional rate of excise duty at 50% of effective applicable rate of duty - On verification of sale invoices, it was found that they had recovered from their customers full amount of excise duty - Show-cause notice was issued to appellants - Appellants contended that since exemption notification did not contain condition that its benefit should be passed on to customers, appellants could retain for themselves benefit of exemption notification - Assistant Collector negatived these defences and confirmed demand under demand notice, for Rs. 2, 29, 981.07 - Appeal before Collector - Collector confirmed the finding of the Assistant Collector - Said order was confirmed by Tribunal - Hence instant appeal - Whether order of Tribunal was just and proper - Held, appellants were entitled to exclude from value of excisable goods was effective duty of excise payable by them on these goods after taking into account exemption notification - Appellant should exclude only 50% of duty which they have paid, being the effective duty of excise, from the value of the excisable goods - Balance amount if they have charged it from customers, would form part of value of goods for purpose of calculation of excise duty - This explanation was inserted by the Act with retrospective effect from 1-10-1975 and it applied to instant case - Hence, order of Tribunal upholding order of Collector was upheld - Appeal dismissed.
