State of Orissa and Others v Shri Ramanath Patnaik

Supreme Court of India

02 April 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, D.P. Wadhwa

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 818; (1997) 5 SCC 181; (1997) SCC (L&S) 1141; AIR 1997 SC 2452; 1997 (1) CLR 1096; 1997 (76) FLR 865; JT 1997 (4) SC 660; 1997 (2) LLJ 1022; 1997 (2) LLN 1034; 1997 (2) SCJ 287; [1997] 3 S.C.R. 528; 1997 (2) SLR 799; 1997 (4) Supreme 351

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Govt. servant - Date of birth - Correction of - It is well known that service record would be opened after Govt. servant enters service record would be countersigned by Govt. servant - Date of birth as entered in school record is source of material for making entry in service record - Held, when entry was made in service record and when he was in service, he did not make any attempt to have service record corrected - Therefore, any amount of evidence produced subsequently would be of no avail - Date cannot be corrected - Appeal allowed.
