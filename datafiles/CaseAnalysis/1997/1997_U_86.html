People's Union for Civil Liberties v Union of India and Another

Supreme Court of India

05 February 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, S.C. Sen

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 2526; (1997) 3 SCC 433; (1997) SCC (Cr) 434; AIR 1997 SC 1203; 1997 (1) CCR 187; 1997 (1) Crimes 190; JT 1997 (2) SC 311; 1997(1) SCALE 706; 1997 (1) SCJ 480; [1997] 1 S.C.R. 923; 1997 (2) Supreme 429

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; International; Tort

Summary: Constitution of India, arts. 21 and 300 - Doctrine of constitutional tort - Custodial death - Whether tort committed? - If yes, what relief? - Respondent/People's Union for Civil Liberties filed writ petition u/art. 32 for judicial enquiry into fake encounters by Imphal Police on 03-04-1991 in which two persons of Lunthilian Village were killed, for directions against culprit police officials and for award of compensation - Affidavits, by persons who were taken up by police alongwith deceased persons and, by wives of deceased filed and counter affidavit filed by appellant - Direction issued by Court, for enquiry to be conducted by District and Sessions Judge into alleged incident - Enquiry report gave finding as to that police shot dead the deceased persons while in their custody - Territorial integrity of Nation and terrorists and separatists activities, noticed and deliberated upon by Court - Court said that in instant case two persons along with some others were just seized from a hut, taken to a long distance away in a truck and shot there and this type of activity cannot certainly be countenanced by the courts even in the case of disturbed areas - Proper course is to deal as per law and not 'administrative liquidation' - (A) Held, constitutional tort committed - Defence of sovereign immunity, vicarious liability of Govt. servants and reading international covenants and conventions into national law to what extent permissible, discussed and deliberated upon - Precedents on constitutional tort referred and quoted - (B) Compensation of Rs. 1,00,000 (Rupees one lakh only) to the families of each of the deceased awarded - Appropriate directions issued - Petition disposed of.
