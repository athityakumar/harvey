Sun Export Corporation and Another v Board of Trustees of The Port of Bombay

Supreme Court of India

23 October 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, K. Venkataswami

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1619; (1998) 1 SCC 142; AIR 1998 SC 92; 1998 (1) CLT 1; 1997 (96) E.L.T. 3; JT 1997 (8) SC 530; 1997(6) SCALE 553; [1997] Supp4 S.C.R. 682; 1997 (9) Supreme 264; 1997 (2) UJ 786

Case Digest

Subject: Customs

Keywords: Warehouse

Summary: Custom - Major Port Trust Act, 1963, ss. 2(o) and 63 - Bombay Port Trust Act, 1879, s. 3(5) - Customs Act, 1962, s. 111(d) - Imports and Exports (Control) Act, 1947, s. 3(2) - Port Trust - Import - Demurrage charges - Goods - Non - clearance by consignee's agent from dock/warehouse - Confiscation by Customs Authority - (A) Interpretation of expression 'owner' to determine liability to pay demurrage charges by agent - (B) Sale of goods to off - set said charges - Legality - Held, liable to pay demurrage charges - Goods are not available to Port Trust for appropriation towards their dues/demurrage charges - Appeal dismissed.
