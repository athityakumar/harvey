State of Uttar Pradesh and Others v Rajendra Kumar Singh and Another

Supreme Court of India

17 March 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, G.N. Ray

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1827; (1997) 10 SCC 682

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Uttar Pradesh Temporary Government Servants (Termination of Services) Rules, 1975 - Whether termination of services of respondent was bad in law? - Respondent was given temporary appointment and continued serving for more than four years - Held, simply by completing period of probation an employee cannot claim to be made permanent until and unless his service record is taken into consideration and a positive decision is taken by appointing authority for making him permanent - Records of service of respondent are not good and if on consideration of such adverse records, a decision to terminate temporary service of respondent was taken and order was passed without attaching any stigma, such order would be held as legal - Appeal allowed.
