Braj Kishore Thakur v Union of India and others

Supreme Court of India

24 February 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, M.M. Punchhi

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 2441; (1997) 4 SCC 65; (1997) SCC (Cr) 514; AIR 1997 SC 1157; 1997 (1) CCR 287; 1997 (1) Crimes 199; JT 1997 (3) SC 26; 1997(2) SCALE 312; [1997] 2 S.C.R. 420; 1997 (3) SLJ 73; 1997 (2) Supreme 471

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Expunge

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985 - Single Judge of High Court castigating District Judge for granting bail in proceedings under NDPS, 1985 observing that Judge was ignorant of law and latest rulings on issue - Held, Single Judge while so observing ignored law laid by DB of that very HC on that very subject indicating that District Judge did not exceed his jurisdiction - In application for expunction of remarks possibility of extraneous considerations cannot be ruled out - Supreme Court expunged all offending remarks in interest of justice - Appeal allowed.
