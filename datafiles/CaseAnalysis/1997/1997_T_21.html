Maddi Venkataraman and Company Private Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax

Supreme Court of India

02 December 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Sen, S. Saghir Ahmad

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1009; (1998) 2 SCC 95; AIR 1998 SC 563; 1998 (144) CTR 214; [1998] 229 ITR 534; JT 1997 (9) SC 546; 1997(7) SCALE 327; 1998 (2) SLT 116; [1997] Supp6 S.C.R. 67; 1998 (1) Supreme 20; [1998] 96 TAXMAN 643; 1998 (2) TLR 130

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Debt Laws

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 28 and 40 A(3) - Whether, the penalty levied on violation of provisions in Foreign Exchange (Regulation) Act, 1973 can be allowed as deduction? - The expenditure incurred for evading the provisions of the Act and also the penalty levied for such evasion cannot be allowed as deduction - It was not enough that the disbursement was made in the course of trade - It must be for the purpose of the trade - It will be against public policy to allow the benefit of deduction under one statute of any expenditure incurred in violation of the provisions another stature or any penalty imposed under another statute - Held, deduction is cannot be allowable - Appeal dismissed.
