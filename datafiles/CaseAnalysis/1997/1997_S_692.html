A. P. Sareen and Others v State of Uttar Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

13 January 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

G.T. Nanavati, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 2242; (1997) 9 SCC 359; AIR 1997 SC 1284; 1997 (2) CLT 240; 1997(1) SCALE 357; [1997] 1 S.C.R. 210; 1997 (1) Supreme 488

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Land & property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 5A, 4(1), 6 and 17(1) - Land acquisition for planned development of town - Urgency clause - Urgency continues as long as scheme is not initiated, action taken and process completed - Sufficient time lapsed after publication of notification, to publish declaration u/s. 6 of Act - If on basis of fact it can be said that urgency has ceased to exist and notification dispensing with enquiry u/s. 5A can be quashed - Held, cannot be ground to say so - Consequently notification cannot be quashed on that count - Appeal disposed of.
