Victoria v K.V. Naik & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

09 May 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Wadhwa, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 2186; (1997) 6 SCC 23; 1997 (2) CLT 487; JT 1997 (5) SC 606; 1997(4) SCALE 318; [1997] Supp1 S.C.R. 374; 1997 (6) Supreme 55

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Keywords: Sustainability, Suit for redemption

Summary: Civil Procedure - Kerala Land Reforms Act , 1963, ss. 4(1)(10) (b), 13, 2(25), 125(3) - Suit for redemption - Sustainability - Respondents-mortgagor filed suit for redemption of mortgage - Petitioner-mortgagee claimed fixity of tenure in respect of entire extent of land u/s. 4(1)(10) (b) and s. 13 of the Act; in the alternative, she claimed to be entitled to deemed Kudikidappu rights over 3 cents of the total extent of 8 cents, by operation of Explanation IV to section 2(25) of Act - Trial Court negatived the decree - Final decree was passed - Petitioner foiled petition which arose for consideration only at time of execution thus, in instant case, prima facie, they were not entitled to reference u/s. 125(3) of the Act - Petitioner prayed before HC and HC held that- it operated as constructive res judicata, since that question was raised at time when final decree was passed, petitioner raised this point in execution - Hence instant petition - Whether the order passed by HC was sustainable - Held, deposit of mortgage money u/s. 83did not ipso facto extinguished mortgage where mortgagee had refused to accept deposit that's if deposit was not accepted, mortgage did not get extinguished; that means patties continue to have relationship of mortgagor and mortgagee - When ss. 2,(25) and 125(3) of the Act were to be considered in suit of redemption claiming right of Kudikidappu as found, was not barred by principle of constructive res judicata in executing proceedings - Later part of view taken therein was not correct for reason that if plea had not been raised, it operated as constructive res judicataon the principle of might and ought - If it was taken and rejected, it operated as res judicata and same should not be raised in execution - Even if it was left open, inequity, justiceand good conscience, it should not be extended to mortgagee - Even if it was left open, in equity, justice and good conscience, it should not be extended to the mortgagee - After all, mortgagee, money-lender came into possession of property as mortgagee and always remained as mortgagee unless limitation snaps off link - He should not be permitted in good sense of law to eat away cake as Kudikidappu, It would be abhorrence to good conscience and playing upon property of indigent mortgagor's own property - Since in suit, plea had been raised and negatived and preliminary decree had been passed followed by final decree, it was not open to petitioner to raise plea after passing of final decree that he was entitled to 3 cents of land as kudikidappu - Petition dismissed.

.
