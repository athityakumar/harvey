D.V. Shanmugham and Another v State of Andhra Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

25 April 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

G.N. Ray, G.B. Pattanaik

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1163; (1997) 5 SCC 349; (1997) SCC (Cr) 691; AIR 1997 SC 2583; 1997 (34) AllCriC 810; 1997 (2) CCR 36; 1997 (2) Crimes 41; 1997 CRLJ 3129; JT 1997 (5) SC 4; 1997(3) SCALE 638; 1997 (2) SCJ 177; [1997] 3 S.C.R. 988; 1997 (4) Supreme 267; 1997 (1) UJ 751

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 148, 149, 307, 324 - Unlawful assembly - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellants/accused persons allegedly formed themselves into an unlawful assembly and attacked complainant and deceased with deadly weapons due to some incidents happened between 2groups - Trial Court convicted all accused u/s. 302 r/w s. 149 of IPC and also convicted for offences punishable u/ss. 148, 307, 324 r/w s. 149 of IPC except accused no. 2 - Trial Court convicted accused no. 2 for offences punishable u/ss. 302, 307, 324 r/w s. 149 of IPC - On appeal, HC by impugned order set aside conviction of accused nos. 3, 4, 5 and acquitted them of all charges - HC also set aside conviction of accused no. 1 for offences punishable u/ss. 148, 307, 324 r/w s. 149 of IPC and modified conviction u/s. 302 r/w s. 149 to s.302 r/w s. 34 of IPC and conviction u/s. 324 was maintained - In the case of accused no. 2, HC confirmed conviction passed by Trial Court for offence u/ss. 302 and 307 and set aside conviction u/ss. 148 and 324 r/w s. 149 - Hence instant appeal - Whether impugned order of conviction passed by HC was justified - Held, prosecution had not explained grievous injury on the head of accused/appellant No.1 and such non-explanation persuaded to draw inference that prosecution had not presented true version at least so far as role played by accused appellant No.1 and the witnesses who was examined and who ascribed a positive role to appellant No.1 that he caught hold of deceased when appellant No.2 stabbed deceased was not true on material point and their evidence thus had become vulnerable - Even though accused-appellant No.2 also sustained some injuries as indicated earlier but those injuries being simple and superficial, prosecution might not be obliged to offer explanation to the same but the same principle would have no application when an injury of such grievous nature as was sustained by accused-appellant No.1 was not explained by prosecution witnesses who were grossly interested in prosecution being all related to one another - Further, prosecution had not offered any explanation for grievous injuries on accused-appellant No. 1which prosecution was obliged to explain and such omission on the part of prosecution to explain injuries on accused appellant No.1 assumed greater significance since all eye-witnesses to the occurrence were related to deceased and thus were interested in prosecution - On account of such infirmities in that prosecution case and more particularly when prosecution had failed to offer any explanation for grievous injuries sustained by accused No.1 on his head and HC had already found that said injury was caused in course of the incident, SC had no hesitation to held that accused-appellant No. 1 was entitled to the benefit of doubt and order of conviction of accused/appellant no. 1 u/s. 302 r/w s. 34 and s. 324 of IPC - With regard to appellant No.2, injuries on accused-appellant No.2 were all simple and superficial in nature and prosecution was not bound to explain such minor and superficial injuries - That apart where evidence was absolutely, clear, cogent and consistent coming from an independent source that it far outweighs the effect of omission on part of prosecution to explain injuries on accused, in such a case a conviction could be based notwithstanding injury was not being explained - Prosecution case of accused appellant No.2 consistently stated by all the eye-witnesses to the occurrence that accused No. 2 stabbed deceased on his abdomen with knife and stabbed deceased 's' also on the abdomen with knife - Their evidence also gets corroborated from medical evidence as well as from the post-mortem examination of dead bodies of 2deceased persons - In view of clinching evidence so far as role ascribed to accused appellant No. 2, it should be held that prosecution case as against appellantNo.2 was proved beyond reasonable doubt and therefore conviction against accused - appellant No.2 as affirmed by HC did not warrant any interference - Appeal disposed of.
