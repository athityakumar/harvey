Harshad J. Shah and Another v L.I.C. of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 April 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Agrawal, G.B. Pattanaik

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 289; (1997) 5 SCC 64; AIR 1997 SC 2459; [1997] 89 Comp Cas 109; 1997 CPJ(Sc) 9; 1997 (5) CTJ 377; 1997 (2) G.L.R. 1577; JT 1997 (4) SC 505; 1997(3) SCALE 423; [1997] 3 S.C.R. 617

Case Digest

Subject: Insurance

Summary: Insurance - Indian Contract Act, 1872, ss. 186, 237 - Life Insurance Corporation Act, 1956, s. 49 - Life Insurance Corporation of India [Agents] Regulations, 1972, regn. 8 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Insurance policy - Double accident benefit - Payment of premium - Grace period - Lapse of policy - Death - Claim for benefit - Legality - Insured/husband of appellant no. 2 took four insurance policies for Rs. 25,000/- each with double accidental benefits on 6-3-1986 through respondent no. 3, general agent of LIC/respondent no. 1 - Premium under said policies was payable on half yearly basis - Insured paid first half yearly premium and second half yearly premium fell due on 6-3-1987 but it was not deposited within prescribed period - On 4-6-1987, respondent no. 3 met insured and obtained from him cheque dt. 4-6-1987 for Rs 2,730 drawn on Union Bank of India towards half yearly premium on all four policies - Cheque was encashed by son of respondent no. 3 on 5-6-1987 - Said amount of premium was deposited by respondent no. 3 with LIC on 10-8-1987 after death of insured in fatal accident on 9-8-1987 - Appellant no. 2, widow of insured as nominee under policies submitted claim to LIC on basis of four policies but claim was repudiated by LIC on ground that policies had lapsed on account of non-payment of half-yearly premium which fell due on 6-3-1987 within period of grace - Appellant no. 2 along with appellant no. 1/Consumer Education & Research Society filed complaint before State Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission wherein claim was made for payment of Rs 4,32,000 to appellant no. 2 - State Commission by judgment dt. 5-6-1992 directed LIC to settle claim in respect of four policies within 30 days from receipt of order and to pay amount of claim to appellant no. 2 after deducting amount of interest, if any, necessary to treat policies as surviving - On appeal preferred by both appellants and respondents, National Commission allowed appeals of respondents and held that insurance agent in receiving bearer cheque from insured towards payment of insurance premium was not acting as agent of LIC - Hence, instant appeals -Whether payment of premium in respect of a life insurance policy by insured to general agent of LIC could be regarded as payment to insurer so as to constitute discharge of liability of insured - Held, in disclaiming its liability, LIC was acting in accordance with provision in regulations/rules framed by it whereby agents had been prohibited from collecting moneys on behalf of LIC - Said provision had been made in public interest in order to protect Corporation from any fraud on part of agent - It should not be said that in making such provision in regulations/rules and in acting in accordance with same LIC had not acted fairly or in consonance with its obligations under Part III of Constitution - Hence, claim of appellants could not be upheld - No ground was made out for interfering with decision of National Commission that respondent no.3 in receiving bearer cheque for Rs.2,730/- from insured was not acting as agent of LIC - In view facts and circumstances of instant case LIC was directed to refund entire amount of premium paid to it on four insurance policies to appellant no. 2 along with interest at 15 per cent per annum - Interest would be payable from date of receipt of amounts of premium - Having regard to fact that appellants had succeeded before State Commission and questions raised by them were of sufficient importance requiring decision by this Court respondent no.1 should pay appellants sum of Rs. 10,000/- as costs - Appeals disposed of.
