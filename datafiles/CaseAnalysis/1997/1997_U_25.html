Vineet Narain & Others v Union Of India & Another

Supreme Court of India

18 December 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

J.S. Verma, S.P. Bharucha, S.C. Sen

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1247; (1998) 1 SCC 226; (1998) SCC (Cr) 307; AIR 1998 SC 889; 1998 (1) CCR 190; 1998 (1) Crimes 12; 1998 CRLJ 1208; JT 1997 (10) SC 247; 1998 (1) RCR(Criminal) 357; 1997(7) SCALE 656; 1998 (1) SLT 518; [1997] Supp6 S.C.R. 595; 1997 (10) Supreme 476

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: - Constitution Of India, 1950 - Delhi Special Police Establishment Act, 1946 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Police Act, 1861 - Prevention Of Corruption Act, 1947 - Prevention Of Corruption Act, 1947; Delhi Special Police Establishment Act, 1946; Police Act, 1861

(A) Constitution of India, Art.21 - Fair trial - Public hearing in Court - Requirement of, is subject to need of in-camera proceedings to safeguard public interest and avoid prejudice to accused -

(B) Constitution of India, Art.226 - Mandamus - Continuing mandamus - Inertia of investigating agencies to investigate into offences involving persons holding high offices - Issuance of mandamus directing agencies to perform would have been futile - Court therefore forged out a new tool of continuing mandamus - The matter was kept pending requiring the agencies to report the progress of investigation so that monitoring by the Court could ensure continuance of the investigation -

(C) Delhi Special Police Establishment Act, ss.3, 4 - C.B.I. - Superintendence by Central Government - Scope - Word 'superintendence' cannot include supervision of actual investigation of an offence by C.B.I. - Whether Govt. can issue any directive under s. 4 curtailing or inhibiting C.B.I. to investigate an offence notified under s. 3 -

(D) Constitution of India, Art.141, 32 - Supreme Court - Power to issue directions, guidelines to fill the void in the absence of suitable legislation to cover the field - Has now become well settled and well recognised practice in India -

(E) Words and Phrases - Public life - Standards - Enumerated - Conduct of Public servant to be scrutinised by keeping these standards in mind.

(F) Constitution of India, Art.141,32 - Delhi Special Police Establishment Act, s.2 - Central Bureau of Investigation - Insulation from extraneous influences - Central Vigilance Commission (CVC) directed to be given statutory status - C.V.C. responsible for efficient functioning of C.B.I. - Methodology for appointment of C. V. Commissioner laid down.

(H) Constitution of India, Art.141,32 - Delhi Special Police Establishment Act, s.2 - CBI - Director - Transfer - Procedure laid down - Director to have freedom for allocation of work within CBI - Selection, extension, premature retirement of officers of CBI up to level of Joint Director - Procedure for, indicated.

(I) Delhi Special Police Establishment Act, s.2 - C.B.I. - Professionals from the revenue, banking and security sectors to be inducted into the CBI, for strengthening CBI's in house expertise - CBI Manual to be scrupulously followed in relation to its investigative functions -

(J) Delhi Special Police Establishment Act, s.2 - CBI - Director - Shall be responsible for timely filing of charge sheets in Courts - Shall conduct regular appraisal of personnel to prevent corruption and/or inefficiency in the agency.

(K) Constitution of India, Art.32,141 - Enforcement Directorate - Director - Appointment - Mode and tenure laid down - Other directions issued to ensure efficient functioning.

A Selection Committee headed by the Central Vigilance Commissioner and including the Home Secretary, Secretary (Personnel) and Revenue Secretary, shall prepare a panel for appointment of the Director, Enforcement Directorate. The Appointment to the post of Director shall be made by the Appointments Committee of the Cabinet (ACC) from the panel recommended by the Selection Committee.

(L) Constitution of India, Art.32,141 - Investigation and prosecution of cases having politico-bureaucrat criminal nexus - Nodal agency directed to be constituted for co-ordinated action -

(M) Constitution of India, Art.141,32 - Police Act, s.4 - Chief of the State police - Appointment tenure, transfer etc. - Mechanism similar to that of CBI directed to be set up. Directive No. 4.7(3) of the Single Directive struck down and the above directions issued. Petition Disposed Of .
