Employees' State Insurance Corporation v Harrisons Malayalam Limited

Supreme Court of India

04 November 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Jagannadha Rao, S.B. Majmudar

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 3186; (1998) 9 SCC 74; (1998) SCC (L&S) 1174; 1998 (2) CLR 1082; 1998 (80) FLR 835; JT 1998 (7) SC 278; 1999 (1) KLT 145; 1999 (1) LLJ 28; 1999 (1) LLJ 284

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Employees State Insurance Act, 1948 - Issue - Whether the appellant-corporation should have been remitted employees' contribution as well as employer's contribution amounting - Since the workmen were engaged by the contractor to execute the work in question the respondent-company should pay the contribution, it was further observed that it cannot be said that because the employees had gone away there is no liability to contribute - Held, the appeal is allowed with no costs.
