Dr. K. Ramulu and Others v Dr. S. Suryaprakash Rao and Others

Supreme Court of India

15 January 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, K. Ramaswamy, S. Saghir Ahmad

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 2260; (1997) 3 SCC 59; (1997) SCC (L&S) 625; AIR 1997 SC 1803; 1997 (75) FLR 709; JT 1997 (2) SC 80; 1997 LabIC 1451; 1997 (2) LLN 451; 1997(1) SCALE 613; [1997] 1 S.C.R. 287; 1997 (2) SLR 195; 1997 (3) Supreme 215

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Husbandry

Summary: Service - Andhra Pradesh State and Subordinate Service Rules, (1966), r. 4 - Asst. Surgeons in Animal Husbandry Department - Promotion as Asst. Director - Whether tribunal was right in directing Govt. to prepare and operate panel for promotion to post Asst. Directors of Animal Husbandry Department in accordance with repealed rules and to operate same? - Not right and correct - A.P. Animal Husbandry Service Rules, 1996 made in GOMs No. 54 of Animal Husbandry and Fisheries Department D/- 06-06-1996 came into force with effect from 12-06-1996 - Govt. had taken a decision not to fill up any of vacancies until repealed rules were duly amended - In Shankarsan Dash case [1991 Indlaw SC 188] it was held that when Govt. have taken a conscious policy decision not to fill up vacancies, decision reasonable and not arbitrary - Held, since it was a policy decision it could not be interfered with - If decision of Govt. is supported by valid reasons, it cannot be stated that decision taken by Govt. was arbitrary - Govt. is entitled to take a decision not to fill up existing vacancies as on relevant date - Tribunal was not right in giving those directions - Appeals allowed.
