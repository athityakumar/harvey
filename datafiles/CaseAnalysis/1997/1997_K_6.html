Post Graduate Institute of Medical Education and Research, Chandigarh and Others v K. L. Narasimhan and another

Supreme Court of India

02 May 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, S. Saghir Ahmad, G.B. Pattanaik

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1007; (1997) 6 SCC 283; (1997) SCC (L&S) 1449; AIR 1997 SC 3687; 1997 (3) AWC 1972; JT 1997 (5) SC 313; 1997 LabIC 2317; 1997 (2) LLN 626; 1997(4) SCALE 75; 1997 (4) SLR 701; 1997 (5) Supreme 37; 1997 (2) UJ 74

Case Digest

Subject: Education; Service

Keywords: Post-Graduate Institute Of Medical Education And Research, Chandigarh, Act, 1966, Carry-forward rule, Uttar Pradesh Public Services (Reservation for Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribes and OBCs) Act, 1994, Post- Graduate Institute of Medical Education and Research, Chandigarh Rules, 1967

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14, 15(1), 15(4), 16, 16(1), 335 - Post-Graduate Institute of Medical Education and Research, Chandigarh, Act, 1966, ss. 31, 32 - Recruitment - Reservation - Legality - Appellant/post Graduate Institute furnished advertisement for recruitment for the post of Assistant Professor reserved for Scheduled Castes/Dalits and Scheduled Tribes/Tribes - Appellants had called for application for appointment - Respondents/General Candidates and Faculty Association challenged sais advertisement before Single Judge - Single Judge held that post of Assistant professor in various disciplines was single post cadre - Reservation for Dalits and Tribes would amount to 1005 reservation, it was unconstitutional and reservation in recruitment for the post of Doctoral Courses undermines efficiency detrimental to excellence for which purpose appellant was established and thereby it was unconstitutional - Aggrieved by said order respondents appealed before DB and same was dismissed - Hence instant appeals - Whether reservation in appointment to posts of Assistant professors in various disciplines in appellant institute was violative of arts. 14, 16 (1) of Constitution - Held, authorities should implement executive/legislative/constitutional policy or principle in their true spirit, honestly and sincerely to effectuate policy, no mandamus or direction should be issued to de-reserve carry forward vacancies reserved for appointment of Dalits and Tribes nor should direction be given to fill up reserved posts with general candidates - It was settled legal position that application of roster to single post cadre and appointment by promotion to carry forward post was valid and constitutional - With a view to given adequate representation in public service to reserved category candidates, opportunity given to them was not violative of arts. 14, 16(1) of Constitution nor was it unconstitutional - View of HC that reservation in post-graduation specialties or super-specialties were detrimental to high degree of efficiency and violative of art. 14 were clearly incorrect, erroneous, illegal and unconstitutional - Thus, reservation in post-graduation specialty or super- specialty was valid under arts. 14, 15(1) and 15(4) of Constitution - Appellant institute should follow reservation an make appointment to posts of Assistant professors and other posts in accordance with Regulation 32(2) and in other faculties as well as admission to various courses in appellant institute by applying rule of reservation - Appeals allowed.
