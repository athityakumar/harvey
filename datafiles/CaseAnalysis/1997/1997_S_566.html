Dattu Ramrao Sakhare and Others v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

08 May 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Kurdukar, G.T. Nanavati

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 2059; (1997) 5 SCC 341; (1997) SCC (Cr) 685; 1997 (35) AllCriC 100; 1997 (2) CCR 54; JT 1997 (5) SC 370; 1997 (2) MPLJ 267; 1997(4) SCALE 121; 1997 (2) SCJ 80

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Child witness - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 118 - Witness in the present case happened to be a child aged 10 years - She witnessed her father being assaulted by the appellants - Reliance placed upon her testimony - Whether conviction can be based upon testimony of such child witness - Held, yes - Child is a competent witness if the child is able to understand the questions put to her and give answers thereof - Courts should ensure that such child was not tutored - Courts should further seek corroboration of testimony of such child from independent witness - Conviction upheld - Appeal dismissed.
