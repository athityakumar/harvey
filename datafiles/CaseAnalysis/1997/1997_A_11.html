Indian Council of Agricultural Research v A. N. Lahiri

Supreme Court of India

29 April 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Majmudar, M. Jagannadha Rao

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1993; (1997) 10 SCC 691; AIR 1997 SC 2259; JT 1997 (5) SC 18; 1997 LabIC 2287; 1997 (2) LLJ 661; 1997(3) SCALE 699; 1998 (1) SLJ 24; 1997 (4) SLR 459; 1997 (4) Supreme 511; 1997 (2) UJ 38

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Constitution of India, 1950, art. 39(d) - Equal pay for equal work - Respondent a scientist in Indian Council for Agricultural Research (ICAR) - ICAR constituting a separate service, Agricultural Research Service(ARS) for its scientists - Respondent not joining ARS as he was Head Of Department and there was no financial gain - Subsequent revision of pay scale of scientist in ARS - Held, respondent entitled to revised pay scale - Since respondent and ARS personnel have same work, qualifications, initial source of recruitment and even employer.
