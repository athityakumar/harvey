Amarnath Ashram Trust Society and Another v Governor of Uttar Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

03 December 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

G.T. Nanavati, G.B. Pattanaik

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1063; (1998) 1 SCC 591; AIR 1998 SC 477; 1998 ALJ 196; JT 1997 (9) SC 659; 1998 (2) MLJ 44; 1998 (2) MLJ(SC) 44; 1997(7) SCALE 376; 1998 (1) SLT 324; [1997] Supp6 S.C.R. 143; 1997 (10) Supreme 246

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss.48 and 39; Constitution of India, art.14 - Withdrawal from acquisition by State Government - Land proposed to be acquired for company - Agreement between company and Government as to cost executed and s.6 Notification issued - Held, Withdrawal by State Government thereafter misconceived view that since entire cost was borne by company acquisition was not for public purpose - Arbitrary and would affect company prejudicially - Act confers upon the State wide discretion but not permits to act arbitrarily.
