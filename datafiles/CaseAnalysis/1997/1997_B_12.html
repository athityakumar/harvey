B. S. Bajwa and Another v State of Punjab and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 December 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

V.N. Khare, B.N. Kirpal, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1198; (1998) 2 SCC 523; (1998) SCC (L&S) 611; AIR 1999 SC 1510; 1998 (2) AWC 809; JT 1998 (1) SC 57; 1998 (3) SLJ 28; [1997] Supp6 S.C.R. 451

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Seniority - Placement in gradation list - 'X' and 'Y' joined the Army and were granted Short Service Commission in the year 1963 - On being released from the Army 'X' jointed the PWD (B & R) on 4-5-1971 and 'Y' joined the same dept. on 12-5-1971 - Here position in the gradation list was shown throughout with reference to these dates of joining the dept. - It was only in the year 1984 that 'X' and 'Y' filed the writ petition in the HC claiming a much earlier date of appointment in the department - Petition was allowed - Appeal filed thereagainst was allowed - However, DB granted certain benefits to 'X' and 'Y' which had the effect of making them senior to the others by giving them a much earlier date of appointment in the dept. - Hence, instant appeals.

Held, in service matters the question of seniority should not be re-opened in such situations after the lapse of a reasonable period because that results in disturbing the settled position which is not justifiable. There was inordinate delay in the instant case for making such a grievance. This alone was sufficient to decline interference u/art. 226 of the Constitution and to reject the writ petition. The writ petition was wrongly entertained and allowed by the Single Judge and, therefore, the judgments of the Single Judge and the DB have both to be set aside. Appeals allowed.
