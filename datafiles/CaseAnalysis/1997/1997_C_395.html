Suryanarayan Sahu and Others v Council of Scientific and Industrial Research

Supreme Court of India

25 November 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Wadhwa, Sujata V. Manohar

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 3009; (1998) 2 SCC 162; (1998) SCC (L&S) 514; AIR 1998 SC 548; JT 1997 (9) SC 307; 1998 LabIC 828; 1997(7) SCALE 202; 1998 (1) SLR 21; 1998 (2) SLT 1; [1997] Supp5 S.C.R. 440; 1998 (1) Supreme 12

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Service - Pay scale - Council of Scientific and Industrial Research (CSIR) - Whether rules laid by Third Pay Commission's Report which Central Govt. decided to implement from 01-01-1973 applicable to employees working in CSIR? - CSIR is not bound to adopt all recommendations of Third Pay Commission and it is not material if Central Govt. accepted recommendations of Third Pay Commission - CSIR is an independent body - Further held, principle of law which has been settled by Supreme Court in a string of judgments is that different scales of pay can be granted based on experience and merit to employees working in same grade - Classification based on experience is valid - An employee having more length of service is certainly better equipped to perform his duties of an office than a relatively new employee - CSIR had not violated principle of 'equal pay for equal work' when it resolved that 50% of members of cadre of senior draughtsmen would be placed in higher scale of pay leaving other 50% for lower scale of pay - Order accordingly.
