Naga People's Movement of Human Rights v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

27 November 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Agrawal, S.P. Bharucha, J.S. Verma, M.M. Punchhi

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1720; (1998) 2 SCC 109; (1998) SCC (Cr) 514; AIR 1998 SC 431; JT 1997 (9) SC 431; 1997(7) SCALE 210; 1998 (1) SLT 238; [1997] Supp5 S.C.R. 469; 1997 (10) Supreme 169

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Defence & Security Forces

Keywords: Explosive, Commissioned Officer, Disturbed Area, Commander, Armed Forces (Assam and Manipur) Special Powers Act, 1958, Assam Preventive Detention Act, 1980

Summary: Armed Forces (Special Powers) Act, 1958 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 248, Sch. 7, List 1, Entry 2A - Assam Disturbed Areas Act, 1955, ss. 4 and 5 - 'Central act' and 'State Act' - Whether Central Act enacted for maintenance of public order is ultra vires? - Constitutionality - Held, is enacted for deployment of Armed Forces 'in aid of civil power' in State - Pith and substance - State Act is a law enacted in exercise of powers under Entry 1 of List II - Valid and not ultra vires.
