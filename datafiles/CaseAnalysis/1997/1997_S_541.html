Madru Singh and Others v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

09 September 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Kurdukar, M. Jagannadha Rao, M.M. Punchhi

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1499; (1997) 11 SCC 506; (1997) SCC (Cr) 1232; AIR 1997 SC 3527; 1997 (4) CCR 45; 1997 (3) Crimes 255; 1997 CRLJ 4398; JT 1997 (7) SC 757; 1997 (8) Supreme 27

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 148 and 300 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 154 - (A) FIR - Evidence of witness lodging FIR very emphatically stating that she saw entire incident and she herself went to police station in matador along with dead body of victim and lodged FIR on same day - Mere fact that copy of FIR was received by concerned Magistrate three days after incident - On basis of this whether conclusion can be drawn that FIR was not lodged on same day after incident or it was antedated - (B) Murder Presence of eye witness at place of occurrence - Proof - Injuries on person of witness - Medical evidence denying that such injury could be self-inflicted injury - Eye witness giving all necessary details regarding assault, weapons and role played by each accused - witnesses also corroborated from evidence of other witnesses though they were hostile witnesses Evidence of eye witness and her presence at place of occurrence, whether true - Whether accused liable to be convicted u/s. 300 of IPC, 1860? - (C) Murder - Unlawful assembly - witness that one of accused directed other accused persons to punish deceased and severe his head - Other accused persons laying murderous assault on deceased in compliance of such direction and causing as many as fourteen incised injuries on his person - Accused persons then chasing deceased until a field nearby and then again assaulting him with deadly weapons - Held that, accused persons formed unlawful assembly and object of said assembly was to commit murder of deceased - Whether conviction of accused u/s. 300, r/w s. 148 proper? - Held, (A) No such conclusion can be drawn on such ground - (B) Cannot be doubted on basis of some trivial contradictions - Liable to be convicted - (C) Accused persons formed unlawful assembly whose object was to commit murder of deceased - Conviction confirmed - Appeal dismissed.
