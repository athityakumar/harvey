Union of India v Bajaj Tempo Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

29 July 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Kirpal, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1412; (1998) 9 SCC 281; 1997 (94) E.L.T. 285; JT 1998 (9) SC 138

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - Quashing of show-cause notices issued to assessees - Exigibility to duty - Process by which end-product on which duty is demanded came into existence - Held, no finding of fact on which question of exigibility to excise duty on any of items or goods can be decided - Appropriate course for assessee in each case was to reply to show-cause notice enabling authorities to record their findings of fact in each case - Trade notice was not decisive of question either before tribunal or in this court - Matters remitted to Asst. Collector for decision of questions of fact - Appeal allowed.
