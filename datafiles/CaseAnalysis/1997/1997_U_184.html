Major General Inder Jit Kumar v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

20 March 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

Sujata V. Manohar, A.M. Ahmadi

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1861; (1997) 9 SCC 1; (1997) SCC (L&S) 1177; AIR 1997 SC 2085; 1997 (3) AWC 1500; 1997 (3) CLT 441; JT 1997 (4) SC 8; 1997 LabIC 2080; 1997(3) SCALE 111; 1997 (2) SLJ 115; 1997 (2) SLR 35; 1997 (3) Supreme 423

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Keywords: Court Martial

Summary: Defence & Security Forces - Service - Army Act, 1950, ss. 109 and 191 - Army Rules, 1954, rr. 22, 23 and 184 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Court martial - Dispute relating to supply of documents to accused - Whether there was non-compliance of principle of natural justice in court-martial proceedings against accused? - Held, accused was given sufficient time to engage defence counsel of his choice but despite opportunity accused keep on changing defending officer and was seeking adjournments - Cross-examination of witnesses not done when offered - Accused well versed in Army Law and capable of arguing case and cross-examining witnesses but did not do so though present throughout - Court-martial proceedings cannot be faulted on ground of non-compliance with principles of natural justice - Appeal dismissed.
