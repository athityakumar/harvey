Power Finance Corporation Limited v Pramod Kumar Bhatia

Supreme Court of India

17 March 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, G.T. Nanavati

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1082; (1997) 4 SCC 280; (1997) SCC (L&S) 941; 1997 (66) DLT 573; 1997 (2) ESC 1221; JT 1997 (4) SC 300; 1997 (2) LLJ 819; 1997 (2) LLN 5; 1997(3) SCALE 347; 1997 (2) SCJ 10; [1997] 2 S.C.R. 1170; 1997 (2) SLR 519; 1997 (3) Supreme 445

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Whether, respondent acquired a vested right after acceptance of the voluntary retirement by proceedings D/- 20-12-1994? - Order is a conditional order in that until the dues are paid by the respondent, the order will not become effective - Unless the employee is relieved of the duty, after acceptance of the offer of voluntary retirement or resignation, jural relationship of the employee and the employer does not come to an end - Before the conditions could be complied with, appellant withdrew the scheme - Order accepting voluntary retirement did not become effective - Held, no vested right has been created in favour of the respondent - Appeal allowed.
