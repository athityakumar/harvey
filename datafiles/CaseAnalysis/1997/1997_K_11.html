Commissioner of Wealth Tax, T. N. and another. v K. N. Shanmugasundaram

Supreme Court of India

06 May 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Wadhwa, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 2027; (1997) 11 SCC 252; 1998 (149) CTR 306; [1998] 232 ITR 354

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 261 - Wealth Tax Act, 1957, s. 29 - Gift Tax - Whether the gifts of immovable properties belonging to the joint family made by the karta to his minor daughters were not invalid and therefore, the income from the settled properties should not be included in the assessment of the assessee-family? - Held, the gifts which were made by the assessee in favour of the three minor daughters were accepted as valid gifts by the Gift Tax Officer and the order of the Gift Tax Officer was upheld in appeal by the Appellate Asst. Commr. and the said order has become final - The Revenue has already treated these gifts as valid gifts for the purpose of gift tax, it is not open to the Revenue to assail the said gifts in connection with the income tax and wealth tax assessment - Appeals dismissed.
