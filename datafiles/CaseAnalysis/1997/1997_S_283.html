Mansukhlal Vithaldas Chauhan v State Of Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

03 September 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Saghir Ahmad, M.K. Mukherjee

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1311; (1997) 7 SCC 622; (1997) SCC (Cr) 1120; (1997) SCC (L&S) 1784; AIR 1997 SC 3400; 1997 (3) CCR 169; 1997 (3) Crimes 301; 1997 CRLJ 4059; JT 1997 (7) SC 695; 1997(5) SCALE 667; 1997 (3) SCJ 19; 1998 (1) SLJ 244; 1997 (8) Supreme 178

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Anti- Corruption Laws (Amendment) Act, 1964

Summary: Criminal - Anti-corruption Laws (Amendment) Act, 1964 - Companies Act, 1956 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947, ss. 5(2) and 6 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 21, 161, 164 and 165 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 21 and 226 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 197 - Validity of sanction granted u/s. 6 of Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947 - Held, High Court put Secy. in a piquant situation while Act gave him discretion to sanction or not to sanction prosecution of appellant, judgment gave him no choice except to sanction prosecution as any other decision would have exposed him to an action in contempt for not obeying mandamus issued by HC - Sanctioning authority, in instant case, was left with no choice except to sanction prosecution and in passing order of sanction, it acted mechanically in obedience to mandamus issued by HC by putting signature on a pro forma drawn up by office - Appeal allowed.
