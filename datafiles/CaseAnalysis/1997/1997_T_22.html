S.P. Jaiswal Etc v Commissioner of Income Tax

Supreme Court of India

06 March 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1372; (1997) 10 SCC 81; AIR 1997 SC 2155; 1997 (139) CTR 436; [1997] 224 ITR 619; JT 1997 (3) SC 460; 1997(2) SCALE 548; [1997] 2 S.C.R. 746; 1997 (3) Supreme 48; [1997] 91 TAXMAN 99; 1997 (2) TLR 615

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Advisory Jurisdiction

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 61 - Whether loan by father (assessee) to partnership firm constituted by his children is a device liable to be taxed? - Held, there is no bar for a father to advance loan to his children for carrying on their business and such loan or income arising from cannot be liable to be taxed in hands of father - Appeal dismissed.
