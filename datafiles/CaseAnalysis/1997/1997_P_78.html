Achutananda Baidya v Prafullya Kumar Gayen

Supreme Court of India

08 April 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

G.N. Ray, S.C. Sen

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 2141; (1997) 5 SCC 76; AIR 1997 SC 2077; 1997 (2) CLT 345; JT 1997 (5) SC 75; 1998 (1) MLJ(SC) 59; 1997(3) SCALE 475; 1997 (2) SCJ 703; [1997] 3 S.C.R. 709; 1997 (Supp) AWC 556; 1997 (3) Supreme 673

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Constitution of India, 1950, art. 227 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 60 - Oral evidence - Deposition by witness - That verbal discussions for oral agreement between parties held in their presence - Finding of fact - Finding by Lower Court without taking into consideration evidence on record - Interference by High Court u/art. 227 - Justification - Held, too hypertechnical, to say that witness should not only state that in their presence negotiation for oral agreement was held but that they had heard talk between parties - Interference by HC - On Finding by Lower Court without taking into consideration evidence on record - Justified.
