Commissioner of Income Tax v Trilok Nath Mehrotra and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 October 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Sen, S. Saghir Ahmad

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 2801; (1998) 2 SCC 289; 1998 (149) CTR 304; [1998] 231 ITR 278; JT 1998 (4) SC 246; [1998] 98 TAXMAN 462

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 2(31) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 141 - Whether on facts and in circumstances of case, tribunal was justified in law in holding that salary could not be assessed in hands of HUF? - Held, law is well-settled that if a member of a hindu undivided family(HUF), joins a partnership and he is given a salary for managing firm or rendering special services to firm, salary will be his individual income - But if his salary is really a part of return of investments made by HUF, in partnership firm, salary income would be added to income of HUF - Appellate Asst. Commr. held that salary could not be assessed in hands of HUF as it was separately given to managing partners individually for their services - This finding was on basis that salary was found to have been paid for services rendered by partners to firm - On further appeal, tribunal sustained finding of Appellate Asst. Commr. - Therefore, on basis of finding of fact, it must be held that tribunal had come to a right decision - Appeals dismissed.
