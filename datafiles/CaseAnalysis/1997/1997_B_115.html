State of Andhra Pradesh v Bimal Krishna Kundu and Another

Supreme Court of India

03 October 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, M.K. Mukherjee

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1318; (1997) 8 SCC 104; (1997) SCC (Cr) 1245; AIR 1997 SC 3589; 1997 (4) CCR 168; 1997 (4) Crimes 40; 1997 CRLJ 4056; JT 1997 (8) SC 382; 1998 (1) MLJ(SC) 31; 1997(6) SCALE 347; [1997] Supp4 S.C.R. 412; 1997 (8) Supreme 446

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Anticipatory bail - Grant of - Sustainability - Respondent nos. 1 and 2 were allegedly committed offences punishable u/ss. 420, 468 and 406 IPC - Respondent nos. 1 and 2 filed application for anticipatory bail before HC - Single Judge exercised discretion envisaged in s. 438 of CrPC and granted anticipatory bail - Hence instant appeal by State - Held, it was not a case for exercising discretion u/s. 438 of CrPC in favour of granting anticipatory bail to respondent - It was disquieting that implications of arming respondent, when they were pitted against this sort of allegations involving well-orchestrated conspiracy, with a pre-arrest bail order, though subject to some conditions, were not taken into account by Single Judge - SC had absolutely no doubt that if respondents were equipped with such an order before they were interrogated by police it would greatly harm the investigation and would impeded prospects of unearthing all ramifications involved in conspiracy - Public interest also would suffer as a consequence - Having apprised himself of nature and seriousness of criminal conspiracy and adverse impact of it on 'the career of millions of students', Single Judge should not have persuaded himself to exercise discretion which Parliament had very thoughtfully conferred on Sessions Judges and HC through s. 438 of CrPC, by favouring respondents with such a pre-arrest bail order - Order of HC was quashed - Appeals allowed.
