M. P. Oil Extraction v State of Madhya Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 July 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

G.N. Ray, G.T. Nanavati

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 2198; (1997) 7 SCC 592; AIR 1998 SC 145; JT 1997 (6) SC 97; 1997(4) SCALE 515; [1997] Supp1 S.C.R. 671; 1997 (2) UJ 438

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Environment, Wildlife & Animal

Summary: Socio Economic - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14 and 226 - (A) Maintainability of plea against renewal clause, when appellant earlier failed to challenge validity of agreement including renewal clause - (B) Validity of Govt. policy of setting and giving special treatment to oil extracting units in backward and its further renewal - (C) Whether rates of royalties fixed through arbitration for Industries set up at instance of Govt., can be interfered by Courts? - Held, subsequent challenge not be entertained unless it can be clearly demonstrated that fact situation has undergone such changes that discretion in matter of renewal of agreement should not be exercised by State - Further held, policy not per se illegal and arbitrary - Court cannot interfere with exercise of Govt. decision, as royalties are rationally fixed - Appeal dismissed.
