Bharat Ram Meena v Rajasthan High Court At Jodhpur And Others

Supreme Court of India

29 January 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Sen

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 810; (1997) 3 SCC 233; (1997) SCC (L&S) 797; AIR 1997 SC 896; 1997 (2) ESC 1357; 1997 (75) FLR 670; JT 1997 (2) SC 16; 1997 LabIC 874; 1997 (2) LLJ 804; 1997 (1) LLN 540; 1997(1) SCALE 596; [1997] 1 S.C.R. 673; 1997 (1) SLR 678; 1997 (1) Supreme 667; 1997 (1) UPLBEC 477

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Constitution, arts. 16 and 226 - Constitution - Judicial Magistrate seeking quashing of adverse remarks in Annual Confidential Report - Representation against - Judged on strength of his work and his conduct - Assessment of his merit cannot be said to be arbitrary or without any factual basis - (A) Refusal for quashing adverse remarks - Interference sought - (B) Jurisdiction of Court - Examination of facts in detail is a matter of appreciation of evidence - Validity of summary dismissal of writ petition by High Court - No interference - Held was proper - Appeal dismissed.
