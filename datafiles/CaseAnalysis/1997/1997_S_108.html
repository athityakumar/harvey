Secretary To Government, Home Deptt. and Others v Srivaikundathan

Supreme Court of India

06 November 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Wadhwa, Sujata V. Manohar

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1394; (1998) 9 SCC 553; (1998) SCC (L&S) 1249; 1999 (81) FLR 257; JT 1998 (8) SC 470; 1998 (2) LLJ 629; 1998 (3) LLN 591

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Respondent working as a Police Constable was assigned along with Naik, duty of escorting two prisoners from Central Jail to Court of Judicial Magistrate - One of prisoners escaped - Charge sheet filed - Departmental inquiry - Found guilty of charges - Dismissal - Govt. modified punishment to that of compulsory retirement in case of Naik and removal of service of respondent - Application before tribunal - Tribunal remitted matter to Superintendent of Police to reconsider extent of guilt of respondent with stipulation that no punishment involving termination of service should be imposed on respondent - Appeal - Re-examination of entire evidence by tribunal led before Enquiry Officer and concluding that Enquiry Officer erred in holding respondent guilty without examining exact role of respondent in respect of escape of prisoner - Held, tribunal was not sitting in appeal over findings of Enquiry Officer, nor was it required to examine nature of evidence which was led as if it were a criminal trial - Further held, tribunal could not have set aside findings of Enquiry Officer merely by expressing dissatisfaction with evidence which was led - In present case, there was a clear evidence pointing to guilt of two employees who had not merely allowed prisoner who was entrusted to their custody to escape, but had also lodged a false complaint in that connection - Therefore, tribunal was not justified in setting aside findings of Enquiry Officer and remitting matter as it did - State of Haryana v. Rattan Singh 1977 Indlaw SC 2 relied on - Appeal allowed.
