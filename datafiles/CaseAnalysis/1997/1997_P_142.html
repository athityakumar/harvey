Income Tax Officer Jodhpur v Purushottam Das Bangur and Another

Supreme Court of India

22 January 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

G.T. Nanavati, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 2305; (1997) 3 SCC 253; AIR 1997 SC 1372; 1997 (139) CTR 32; [1997] 224 ITR 362; JT 1997 (2) SC 419; 1997(2) SCALE 16; [1997] 1 S.C.R. 480; 1997 (3) Supreme 142; [1997] 90 TAXMAN 541; 1997 (2) TLR 409

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Gift Tax

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Wealth Tax Act, 1957 - Income Tax Act, s. 147(b) - Claim by assessee that he had suffered long term capital loss on sale of shares of certain company - Assessment on basis of such claim - Whether notice for reassessment on basis of letter received by ITO about prospering financial condition of company during relevant period and that quotation of rate of shares was as result of manipulated transactions between group liable to be quashed? - Held, notice for reassessment u/s. 147(b) on basis of such information was not liable to be quashed - Appeal dismissed.
