Belwal Spinning Mills Limited v U.P.State Electricity Board and Another

Supreme Court of India

10 July 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

G.N. Ray, G.B. Pattanaik

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 2694; (1997) 6 SCC 740; AIR 1997 SC 2793; 1997 ALJ 1557; JT 1997 (6) SC 277; 1997 (Supp) AWC 333; [1997] Supp2 S.C.R. 197

Case Digest

Subject: Electricity & Energy; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure; Tort

Summary: Electricity Act, 1910-Sections 20 and 26-Defective meter-Original meter and check meter installed by U.P.S.E.B. in premises of appellant-Both found to be defective by Electrical Inspector-Appellant liable to pay estimated amount as determined by Electrical Inspector under Section 26 (6) for statutory period under Section 26 (6)-For earlier period, since no allegation of fraud against appellant-Appellant liable to pay only on basis of reading in installed meter/check meter in view of statutory protection of conclusive proof of consumption of electricity for such period on basis of reading in meter-U.P.S.E.B. precluded from raising any demand contrary to aforesaid liability-And not entitled to disconnect electricity in premises of appellant for non-payment for consumption of electricity beyond quantity registered by installed meter-Proper amendment of law felt desirable to protect large number of licensees from suffering huge loss of revenue.
