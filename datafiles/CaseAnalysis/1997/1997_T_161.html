Kattite Valappil Pathumma & Ors. v The Taluk Land Board & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

19 February 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Paripoornan, S.P. Kurdukar

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 846; (1997) 4 SCC 114; AIR 1997 SC 1115; AIR 1997 SCW 1142; JT 1997 (2) SC 624; 1997 (1) KLT 576; 1997(2) SCALE 200; [1997] 2 S.C.R. 175; 1997 (2) Supreme 358

Case Digest

Subject: Agriculture & Agri. Products

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land and Property - Kerala Land Reforms Act, 1963, s. 82 - Constitution, art. 133 - Ceiling area - Determination of - Declarant having plurality of wives - (A) Whether land owned by declarant and family chosen by him alone can be considered or land held by his other wives and members of their families can also be considered - (B) Whether interference with a decision on appeal on ground that different view is possible - Held, (A) land owned by declarant and family chosen by him alone ca be considered and not land held by his other wives and members of their family - (B) interference only if decision is manifestly wrong or unfair can be considered otherwise no interference possible - Appeal dismissed.
