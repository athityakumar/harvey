State of Punjab and Others v Bakhshish Singh

Supreme Court of India

05 May 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Wadhwa, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 2010; (1997) 6 SCC 381; (1997) SCC (L&S) 1510; AIR 1997 SC 2696; 1998 (1) AWC 57; 1997 (76) FLR 849; JT 1997 (5) SC 633; 1998 (1) LLJ 348; 1997 (2) LLN 1019; 1997(4) SCALE 360; 1998 (1) SLJ 148; 1997 (4) SLR 590; [1997] Supp1 S.C.R. 91; 1997 (6) Supreme 43

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Whether High Court is justified by holding that lack of finding about misconduct being of requisite type makes order of dismissal of respondent arbitrary in nature and is liable to be quashed? - It is for disciplinary authority to pass appropriate punishment, civil court cannot substitute its own view to that of disciplinary as well as appellate authority on nature of punishment to be imposed upon delinquent officer - Held, Appellate Court ought not to have interfered with decree of Trial Court - Dismissal order of the HC is without application of mind and has ignored settled legal principles - Appeal allowed.
