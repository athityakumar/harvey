State Of U.P. v Yamuna Shanker Misra & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

21 February 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, S. Saghir Ahmad

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1025; (1997) 4 SCC 7; (1997) SCC (L&S) 903; AIR 1997 SC 3671; 1997 ALJ 1166; 1997 (30) ALR 384; 1997 (3) AWC 1496; 1997 (76) FLR 170; JT 1997 (4) SC 1; 1997 (2) LLJ 1; 1997(3) SCALE 80; 1997 (1) SCJ 546; [1997] 2 S.C.R. 371; 1997 (2) SLJ 121; 1997 (2) SLR 311; 1997 (3) Supreme 187; 1997 (2) UPLBEC 1054

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Constitution, art. 16 - Adverse remarks in confidential report of employee when vigilance enquiry pending - High Court found that remarks were made by officer due to malice and were arbitrary - Validity - Held, not warranted as unless employee was exonerated from vigilance enquiry the officer could not have clear his conduct - Appeal allowed.
