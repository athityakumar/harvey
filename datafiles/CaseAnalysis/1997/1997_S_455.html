Jagdish Lal and Others v State of Haryana and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 May 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, G.B. Pattanaik, S. Saghir Ahmad

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 2052; (1997) 6 SCC 538; (1997) SCC (L&S) 1550; AIR 1997 SC 2366; JT 1997 (5) SC 387; 1997 LabIC 2301; 1997 (4) LLN 22; 1997(4) SCALE 202; 1997 (2) SCJ 251; 1997 (4) SLR 333; 1997 (5) Supreme 1

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Bombay Prohibition Act 1949

Summary: Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14 and 16 - Fundamental Rules, r. 14 A - Determination of Seniority - Seniority gained by SC/ST candidate over a general candidate due his accelerated promotion through reservation - Lien, Govt. servant - Held, seniority to be counted from date of initial appointment and not from date of confirmation - Seniority, does not in all event get wiped out on promotion of general candidate - Protective discrimination itself is a facet of art. 14 - Govt. servant cannot hold lien on two posts.
