Rajesh Kumarstate Of Haryana v Dharamvir & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

12 March 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

M.K. Mukherjee, B.N. Kirpal

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 2235; (1997) 4 SCC 496; (1997) SCC (Cr) 591; AIR 1997 SC 3769; 1997 AllCriR 486; 1997 (2) AWC 972; 1997 (1) CCR 281; 1997 (1) Crimes 281; 1997 CRLJ 2242; JT 1997 (3) SC 525; 1997(2) SCALE 602; 1997 (2) SCJ 492; [1997] 2 S.C.R. 1012; 1997 (3) Supreme 80

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 96, 105 and 300 - Limitation Act, 1908 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 11 - Murder - Private defence - Alibi - (A) High Court accepting plea of private defence, recording observations counter to case put up by accused - And defence witness appearing to be procured witness - (B) When right of private defence can be exercised - (C) Mischief u/s. 425 - Availability of right of private defence as to property in light of HC's finding that accused came to place of occurrence and attacked complainant after latter had damaged outer door of his house - And accused pleaded that he sustained injuries due to assault by complainant with knife about which evidence of two doctors varying - (D) Plea of alibi - Claim by accused that he had gone to see certain advocate for consultation - No contemporaneous document produced - Held, plea of private defence wrongly accepted - Can be exercised only to repel unlawful aggression and not to retaliate - Further held, accused had no right of private defence - Plea cannot be accepted - Appeal allowed.
