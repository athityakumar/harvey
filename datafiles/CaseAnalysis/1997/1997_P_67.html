Commissioner of Income Tax v Podar Cement (P) Ltd.

Supreme Court of India

27 May 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Venkataswami, B.N. Kirpal, K.S. Paripoornan

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 2014; (1997) 5 SCC 482; AIR 1997 SC 2523; 1997 (141) CTR 67; [1997] 226 ITR 625; JT 1997 (5) SC 529; 1997(4) SCALE 271; [1997] Supp1 S.C.R. 394; 1997 (5) Supreme 259; [1997] 92 TAXMAN 541; 1997 (2) TLR 690

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Finance Bill, 1988

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 22 and 56 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882 - Income from house property - Legal owner for purpose of s. 22 - Assessee purchased flats and took possession after payment of full consideration - However, ownership of said flats not transferred in the name of the assessee - Said flats were let out to various persons - Assessee included rental income from these flats as income from other sources u/s. 56 of IT Act in the return for the a/ys 1975-76 and 1976-77 - Income Tax Officer rejected the claim of the assessee that the income from the flats should be assessed u/s. 56 of the Act and has taken the annual letting value of those flats at Rs. 1, 31, 268 on the basis of rent receivable in respect of flats from an adjoining building - Commissioner upheld the order of the Income Tax Officer - Tribunal held that the income from the flats should be assessed as income from other sources and not as income from house property - Hence, present appeal - Whether the Tribunal was justified in law in holding that the income derived by the assessee Company from flats is taxable under the head 'income from other sources' u/s. 56 of the Income Tax Act and not income from 'house property' u/s. 22 of the IT, Act, 1961? - Held, u/s. 22 of IT Act "owner" is a person who is entitled to receive income from the property in his own right, therefore, where property is handed over by contractor/builder to the purchaser, the purchaser would be treated as "owner" of that property irrespective of the fact that no registered document was executed under section 54 of Transfer of Property Act or Registration Act - Hence, rental income from said flats is taxable under heading 'income from house property' and not under head 'income from other sources' - Appeal disposed of.
