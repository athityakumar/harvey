State of Bihar and Others v Shyam Yadav and Others Etc

Supreme Court of India

14 January 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Agrawal, G.T. Nanavati

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 759; (1997) 2 SCC 507; (1997) SCC (L&S) 530; AIR 1997 SC 776; JT 1997 (1) SC 520; 1997 LabIC 612; 1997(1) SCALE 309; 1997 (1) SCJ 72; [1997] 1 S.C.R. 249; 1997 (2) SLR 47; 1997 (3) Supreme 751

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Census Act,1948

Summary: Constitution, arts. 16, 226 and 311 - Census Act, 1948, s. 4 - Absorption of retrenched Census employees of 1991in Government service - Petition for absorption - (A) No document indicating state goverment's decision to absorb them - Direction that they should be given preference in appointment to State service - Challenged - (B) Whether employees of State or Central Government - Specific averment in petition that they had applied in response to Advertisement issued by UOI in Census Deptt. and had been appointed by Central Govt. in Home Ministry - submissions made by such appointees that they were employees of the State Govt., therefore, it is the obligation of the State Govt. to absorb them - Held, such appointment liable to be set aside ,but these mployees however held entitled to relaxation in age limit and priority in forwarding of names by employment exchange - Plea has no basis - Appeal allowed.
