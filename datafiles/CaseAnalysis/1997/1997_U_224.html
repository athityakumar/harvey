T. N. Godavarman Thirumulkpad v Union of India and others

Supreme Court of India

08 May 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

J.S. Verma, B.N. Kirpal, S.P. Kurdukar

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 3344; (1997) 5 SCC 760

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Summary: Environment, Wildlife & Animal - Collection of forest produce - Sale of rounded timber - In State, it was permitted that Principal Chief Conservator of Forest (PCCF) might on a case-to-case basis consider grant of permission to existing licensed saw mill to relocate itself - Relocated site was not within 10 Kms of any existing forest - Petitioners filed writ petitions seeking directions in regard to forest produce - Hence, instant petitions.

Held, to alleviate unintended hardship caused to ordinary populace in hill areas, it was clarified that nothing contained in orders passed by SC would prevent Forest Corporation from directly undertaking exercise of collecting forest produce to extent strictly necessary and distributing same ex depot to people living in hill areas. Forest Corporation might with prior permission of PCCF remove dead trees for supply in same manner ex depot to people residing in those areas. In relation to seven North-Eastern States, it was further directed that ban on felling and transportation of trees and timber as already imposed should continue. After inventory of felled timber gathered at depots was complete, High Powered Committee (HPC) might permit sale of such rounded timber for utilisation within State. State Govt. was permitted to consider grant of permission/licence to District Leprosy Committee to run their band saw mill. Order accordingly.

Ratio - No person other than a local inhabitant, a Forest Officer or Police Officer or any other personnel on official duty shall be permitted to enter reserved forests except in accordance with permission in writing issued by PCCF.
