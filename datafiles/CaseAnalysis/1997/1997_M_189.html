Collector of Central Excise v Messrs Wood Polymers Limited Etc

Supreme Court of India

17 December 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Agrawal, D.P. Wadhwa

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 728; (1998) 1 SCC 665; AIR 1998 SC 1493; 1998 (74) ECR 241; 1998 (97) E.L.T. 193; JT 1997 (10) SC 194; 1997(7) SCALE 748; 1998 (2) SLT 76; [1997] Supp6 S.C.R. 528; 1998 (5) Supreme 275

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Customs; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Central Excise, Finance Bill, Finance Act

Summary: Finance Act, 1997 ; Central Excise Tariff Act 1986, Sch., Heading 3920.21 and Heading 3920.37 ; Central Excise Tariff Act 1986, Sch., Entry 4818.90 and Entry 4923.90 - Paper based decorative laminated sheets and cotton fabric based laminated boards are classified under which sub-heading - Which category do paper based decorative laminated sheets or boards fall under - Held, classifiable under SH. 3920.21 and not under SH. 4818.90 and 3922.90 till February 28, 1988 and under SH. 3920.37 and not under SH. 4823.90 and 3926.90 on and after March 1988 - Do not fall in Chap. 48 but fall in Chap. 39 - Cannot be classified under sub-heading 4818.90 till February 28, 1988 and under sub-heading 4923.90 on or after March 1988 -They cannot claim benefit of concessional rate of duty.
