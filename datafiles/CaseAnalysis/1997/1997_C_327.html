Messrs South India Steel Rolling Mills, Madras v Commissioner of Income Tax, Madras

Supreme Court of India

25 February 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Agrawal, G.B. Pattanaik

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1040; (1997) 9 SCC 728; 1997 (139) CTR 353; [1997] 224 ITR 654; JT 1997 (3) SC 707; 1997(2) SCALE 747; [1997] 2 S.C.R. 488; 1997 (3) Supreme 250; [1997] 91 TAXMAN 196

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Companies Act, 1956 - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922 - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 261 and 263 - Partnership - Whether, on the facts and circumstances of the case the revision of assessment u/s. 263 by the Commr. for withdrawing the development rebate granted for assessment years 1962- 63, 1963-64, 1967-68 and 1968-69 is proper and justified? - Held, as regards his taking into consideration an event which had occurred subsequent to the passing of the order by the ITO's, it may be stated that in explanation (b) in s. 263 there is an express provision wherein it is prescribed that ''record shall include and shall be deemed always to have included all records relating to any proceeding under this act available at the time of examination by the Commr.''. The death of one of the two partners resulting in the dissolution of the assessee firm on account of such death took place prior to the passing of the order by the Commr. and it could therefore be taken into consideration by him for the purpose of exercising his powers u/s. 263 of the Act - Appeals dismissed.
