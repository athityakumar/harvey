Government of Tamil Nadu and Another v K. Jayaraman

Supreme Court of India

03 February 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, G.T. Nanavati

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 853; (1997) 9 SCC 606; (1997) SCC (L&S) 1208; AIR 1997 SC 1332; 1997 (76) FLR 709; JT 1997 (2) SC 526; 1997 LabIC 1523; 1997 (1) LLJ 920; 1997 (2) LLN 591; 1997(2) SCALE 61; 1997 (1) SCJ 492; [1997] 1 S.C.R. 848; 1997 (1) SLR 710; 1997 (2) Supreme 456

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Voluntary resignation - Entitlement to pensionary benefits - Computation of period of service - Whether respondent is eligible in accordance with appropriate procedure to pensionary benefits? - Amended rules provided that Govt. may, be giving him notice of not less than three months in writing or three months pay and allowances in lieu of such notice, after he has attained age of fifty years on after he has completed twenty five years of qualifying service retire any Govt. servant - Any Govt. servant who has attained age of 50 years or who has completed 25 years of qualifying service may also likewise retire from service by giving notice of not less than three months in writing to be appropriate authority - Held, Govt. is at liberty and is accordingly directed to consider his case whether he is eligible in accordance with appropriate procedure to pensionary benefits or relaxation may be given and pass appropriate orders within a period of four months from date of receipt of order - Appeal disposed of.
