Mahmood Hasan and Others v State of Uttar Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 January 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, G.T. Nanavati, S.P. Bharucha

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 3071; (1997) 3 SCC 138; (1997) SCC (L&S) 657; AIR 1997 SC 800; JT 1997 (1) SC 353; 1997 LabIC 621; 1997 (2) LLN 574; 1997(1) SCALE 243; 1997 (1) SCJ 287; [1997] 1 S.C.R. 88; 1997 (1) SLR 724; 1997 (3) Supreme 524; 1997 (3) UPLBEC 1807

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 16, 32, 142 and 309 - Ad-hoc Promotion - Interim directions given granting benefit of interim promotions - Causing serious imbalance inasmuch as inexperienced junior manned higher posts than seniors - And seniors denied promotion because No. of juniors promoted occupies total promoted cadre - Held, Supreme Court issued directions to set aside all promotions made by State and HC - And further directed that promotion be made according to State level seniority list - Petition disposed of.
