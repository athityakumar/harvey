M. Hara Bhupal v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

24 February 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

G.T. Nanavati, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 3165; (1997) 3 SCC 561; (1997) SCC (L&S) 833; JT 1997 (3) SC 397; 1997 (2) LLJ 231; 1997(2) SCALE 543; [1997] 2 S.C.R. 455; 1997 (2) SLR 387; 1997 (3) Supreme 681

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Central Administrative Tribunal Stenographers' Services Recruitment Rules, 1989, r. 5(1) - Central Administrative Tribunal (Group B and C Miscellaneous Posts) Recruitment Rules, 1989 - Whether Tribunal has rightly pointed out that method of recruitment to two categories of posts are different and distinct and, therefore, both posts cannot be treated to be analogous? - When candidates are governed by two different sets of rules in deputation service, even if they are analogous posts, one cannot be transposed from service of one set of rules into another set of rules - If adoption of transposition by analogous situation is given acceptance, one can, by manipulation, get into another service and seek promotional avenues or recruitment by back-door method - Held, Tribunal was right in not accepting contention of petitioner - Petition dismissed.
