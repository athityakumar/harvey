Mohan Lal and Others v State of Himachal Pradesh Through its Secretary,Excise

Supreme Court of India

17 March 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

G.T. Nanavati, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 3013; (1997) 4 SCC 416; (1997) SCC (L&S) 969; AIR 1997 SC 3775; 1997 (1) CLR 846; JT 1997 (4) SC 197; 1997 LabIC 1921; 1997(3) SCALE 349; [1997] 2 S.C.R. 1160; 1998 (1) SLJ 9; 1997 (2) SLR 610; 1997 (4) Supreme 370

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Constitution of India, 1950, art. 16 - Seniority - Determination of - Direct recruits - Probationers have to pass departmental examination within two years - Two sets of officers - One who passed examination within two years and others who sat for examination within two years but passed examination beyond two years - Held, their seniority will be reckoned under Rules from date of their passing examination and not from date of appointment.
