Associated Cement Company Limited v Keshvanand

Supreme Court of India

16 December 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, M.K. Mukherjee

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1230; (1998) SCC (Cr) 475; (1998) 1 SCC 687; AIR 1998 SC 596; [1998] 91 Comp Cas 361; 1998 (1) Crimes 88; 1998 CRLJ 856; JT 1998 (4) SC 268; 1998 (1) KLT 179; [1997] Supp6 S.C.R. 500

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Corporate; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, s. 256 - Non-appearance of complainant - Complaint for dishonour of cheque filed by attorney - Holder of Company - (A) Applicability of provisions of s. 256 to a Co. - Discretion as to whether personal attendance of complainant should be dispensed with or not - (B) Whether resorting to step of axing down complaint if presence of complainant on that day was quite unnecessary a proper exercise of power envisaged in section? - Held, (A) Provisions of s. 256 is applicable even where complainant is Co. or any other juristic person - (B) If presence of complainant is quite unnecessary axing down complaint is not proper - Appeal allowed.
