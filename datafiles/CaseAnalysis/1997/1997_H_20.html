Haryana State Electricity Board and Another v Hakin Singh

Supreme Court of India

30 September 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, A.S. Anand

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 636; (1997) 8 SCC 85; AIR 1997 SC 3887; 1997 (77) FLR 768; JT 1997 (8) SC 332; 1998 LabIC 115; 1997(6) SCALE 325; 1999 (1) SLJ 114; 1997 (5) SLR 598; [1997] Supp4 S.C.R. 377; 1997 (8) Supreme 518; 1997 (3) UPLBEC 2047

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Compassionate appointment - Limitation - Widow with minor children - Circular issued by Electricity Board extending period for submitting application from one to three years, provided request is made by widow within one year of death of employee - Held, scope of circular cannot be stretched to mean that period of three years is applicable from date minor child becomes major - Application made by widow after 14 years of death of her husband - Not maintainable - If family members of deceased employee can manage for fourteen years after his death one of his legal heirs cannot put forward a claim as though it is a line of succession by virtue of a right of inheritance - Object of provision is to give succour to family to tide over sudden financial crisis on account of untimely demise of earning family member - Supreme Court has observed time and again that object of providing such ameliorating relief should not be taken as opening an alternative mode of recruitment to public employment - Appeal allowed.
