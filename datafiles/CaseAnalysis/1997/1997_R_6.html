P.N. Kohli and Ors., Union of India and Another Etc v R. Iyyaswamy and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 September 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

G.T. Nanavati, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 597

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Constitution - Service - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14 and 16 - Asst. Engineers (Akashwani and Doordarshan Group 'B' Posts) Recruitment (Amendment) Rules, 1985, r. 2(1)(a) - Asst. Engineers (Akashwani and Doordarshan Group 'B' Posts) Recruitment Rules, 1972 - Amendment Rules of 1985 were challenged by the graduate Senior Engineering Asst./Asst. Engineers - Contention of the applicants was that till 1972 all the higher posts were reserved for educationally better qualified persons and thereafter a certain percentage was reserved for them but, as a result of the 1985 Amendment, the reservation in favour of educationally better qualified persons has been completely done away with the result that they will now lose initiative obtaining higher educational qualification - Tribunal rejected the contention raised on behalf of the applicants that r. 2(1)(b) of the Amendment Rules providing for filling up of 75 per cent of the posts in the promotion quota by selection on the basis of departmental competitive examination was either discriminatory or arbitrary merely because it provided for competitive examination and made the diploma-holder eligible for competing with the degree holders - Tribunal held, by permitting both the degree and diploma-holders to compete for 75 per cent promotion quota, the Govt. cannot be said to have made unequal's equal, more particularly in view of the purpose and reason behind the said rule - Whether r. 2(1)(a) of 1985 Rules is discriminatory and, therefore, violative of arts. 14 and 16? - Held, a degree in Engineering is a better educational qualification than a diploma in Engineering and, therefore, while competing for the post of Asst. Engineers, Graduate Engineers will have an edge over the diploma-holders - By adopting the new policy contained in rr. 2 (1)(a) and (b) of the Amendment Rules, the Govt. appears to have made an attempt to balance the advantages arising out of merit and experience based on long service - Per cent promotion quota rule enables those Senior Engineering Asst. who have rendered long years of service but due to certain reasons like age, etc do not desire to appear for the competitive examination but are otherwise fit for being promoted to such higher post - Policy underlying r. 2 (1)(a) and (b) of the Amendment Rules of 1985, therefore, cannot be regarded either as discriminatory or arbitrary - It is also not correct to say that by providing for promotion by selection by Department Promotion Committee against the 25 per cent promotion quota what the Govt. has done is to make unequal's equal - In view of N Abdul Basheer's case, there is clear pronouncement of law, the contrary view taken by the Tribunal has to be regarded as bad - Thus, order passed by the Tribunal is set aside - R. 2(1)(a) of the Amendment Rules of 1985 is held to be valid - Appeals allowed.
