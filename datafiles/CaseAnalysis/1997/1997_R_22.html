R.C. Tiwari v M.P. State Co-Operative Marketingfederation Ltd. & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

09 April 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, D.P. Wadhwa

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 950; (1997) 5 SCC 125; (1997) SCC (L&S) 1128; AIR 1997 SC 2652; JT 1997 (5) SC 95; 1997 (2) LLJ 236; 1997(3) SCALE 741; [1997] 3 S.C.R. 795; 1998 (1) SLJ 42; 1997 (4) SLR 688; 1997 (5) Supreme 186

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Trusts & Associations - Industrial Dispute Act, 1947, s. 10(1) - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, s. 11 - M.P. Co-operative Societies Act, ss. 55 and 64 - (A) Whether, reference u/s. 10(1) of Industrial Dispute Act, 1947 is maintainable in view of provisions contained in Societies Act, 1960? - Dispute relating to management or business of society is very comprehensive - Held, special procedure has been provided under Act, 1960 - Necessarily reference u/s. 10 of Industrial Dispute Act, 1947 excluded - (B) Res judicata - CPC, 1908, s. 11 - Applicability - Finding recorded by Registrar upholding misconduct of petitioner - That constitutes res judicata - Held, High Court is well justified in holding that Labour Court has no jurisdiction to decide dispute once over and reference itself is bad in law - Special leave petition dismissed.
