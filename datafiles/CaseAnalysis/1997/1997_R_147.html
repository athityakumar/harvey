Brahmdeo Choudhary v Rishikesh Prasad Jaiswal and Another

Supreme Court of India

22 January 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Majmudar, A.S. Anand

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 2298; (1997) 3 SCC 694; AIR 1997 SC 856; JT 1997 (1) SC 641; 1997 (1) MahLJ 817; 1997 (1) MLJ 817; 1997 (2) MLJ(SC) 128; 1997 (1) RCR(Rent) 332; 1997 (1) RentLR 96; 1997(1) SCALE 437; [1997] 1 S.C.R. 463; 1997 (2) Supreme 660

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Ground Of Res Judicata

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 21, rr.35, 97 and 99 - Limitation Act, 1908 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Obstruction by stranger in execution of decree for possession - (A) If he can get his claim adjudicated only after losing possession - (B) Noted by executing court and decree - holder and also re-issue warrant for possession with police assistance - Held, stranger can get his claim adjudicated even prior to losing possession to decree-holder - Cannot be re-issued - Appeal allowed.
