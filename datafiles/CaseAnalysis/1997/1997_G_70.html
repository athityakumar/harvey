Government of Orissa Through Secretary, Commerce and Transport Department, Bhubaneswar v Haraprasad Das and Others

Supreme Court of India

24 November 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

G.T. Nanavati, S.P. Kurdukar

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1692; (1998) SCC (L&S) 382; (1998) 1 SCC 487; AIR 1998 SC 375; 1998 (78) FLR 166; JT 1997 (9) SC 246; 1998 LabIC 591; 1998 (1) LLN 5; 1997(7) SCALE 137; 1998 (1) SLR 47; 1998 (1) SLT 83; [1997] Supp5 S.C.R. 338; 1998 (1) UPLBEC 526

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Constitution - Service - Constitution of India, 1950. arts. 16 and 309 - Orissa Govt. Press Industrial Employees Classification, Recruitment, Promotion, Conditions of Service and Appeal Rules (1978), rr. 9, 10 and 11 - Notification of vacancies for appointment - Select list prepared - Policy decision not to fill posts - (A) Whether Court can interfere and direct Govt. to make appointments? - Held, no - Tribunal cannot direct Govt. to fill up those posts even though it had good reasons not to do so even though there were some vacant posts of copy holders and director of press had recommended to Govt. to fill up those posts? - No right to appointment arises upon a mere empanelment or inclusion of one's name in selection list - If Govt. decides not to make further appointments for a valid reason, it has not acted arbitrarily by not appointing those whose names are included in selection list - Decision to fill up a post is a policy decision and is not open to Tribunal to interfere with such decision of Govt. and direct it to make appointments - (B) Whether appointment is to be through direct recruitment? - Further held, in case of direct recruitment there is no role for appointment and promotion committee - Committee need not approve selection list prepared for direct recruitment - One year life span for selection list runs from date it is drawn and not from date it is approved by appointment and promotion committee - Appeal allowed.
