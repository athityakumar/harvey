Ram Sakal Singh v Mosamat Monako Devi (Dead) & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

21 February 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, S. Saghir Ahmad

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1030; (1997) 5 SCC 192; AIR 1998 SC 277; AIR 1997 SCW 4251; 1997 (3) CLT 185; JT 1997 (3) SC 504; 1997(2) SCALE 508; [1997] 2 S.C.R. 357; 1997 (3) Supreme 340

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land and Property - Constitution - Bihar Consolidation of Holdings and Prevention of Fragmentation Act, 1956, s. 4(1)(c) - CPC, 1908, O. 22, r. 4 - Consolidation of land holdings - (A) Abatement of suit based on document relating to land - (B) Death of one of the defendants holding interest jointly and indivisiblly - Counsel seeking deletion of name of deceased defendant - Instead of seeking transposition of LR in place of deceased defendant - Held, would abate if document is void but would not abate if document is voidable and requires to be so declared by Court - Mistake of counsel condoned and existing defendant transposed as legal representatives of the deceased defendant - Appeal dismissed.
