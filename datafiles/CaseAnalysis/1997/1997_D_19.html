State Of U.P. v Dan Singh And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

03 February 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Kirpal, M.K. Mukherjee

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 856; (1997) SCC (Cr) 469; (1997) 3 SCC 747; AIR 1997 SC 1654; 1997 ALJ 647; 1997 (1) CCR 234; 1997 (1) Crimes 121; 1997 CRLJ 1150; JT 1997 (2) SC 149; 1997(1) SCALE 626; 1997 (1) SCJ 461; [1997] 1 S.C.R. 764; 1997 (2) Supreme 67

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Third Judge

Summary: Penal Code,1860, ss.300, 147, 149 and 436; Criminal Procedure Code, 1974, s.392 - Multiple murder - Communal riot - Assaulted by villagers - Some of them burnt alive inside the house of Scheduled Caste - Accused persons identified by four eye-witnesses - Held, testimony of eye-witnesses - Cannot be rejected just because some inconsequential contradiction or exaggeration - Order of Division Bench becomes non est - Order which follows opinion of third Judge is the final order - Liable to be convicted.
