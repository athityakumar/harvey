Surinder Singh v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

27 August 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Wadhwa, Sujata V. Manohar

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1482; (1997) 8 SCC 488; (1998) SCC (L&S) 65; AIR 1998 SC 18; 1997 (31) ALR 496; 1997 (77) FLR 453; JT 1997 (7) SC 537; 1998 LabIC 142; 1998 (1) LLN 86; 1997(5) SCALE 567; 1999 (1) SLJ 75; 1997 (5) SLR 269; [1997] Supp3 S.C.R. 538; 1997 (8) Supreme 1; 1997 (2) UJ 630

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Constitution - Service - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 16 - Advertisement to fill up certain posts of teachers - Whether circumstances existed for deviating from principle of limiting appointment number or appointments so advertised? - Held, it was not proper on part of state to deviate from principle of limiting number of appointments so advertised - There was neither any exceptional circumstance nor was there any emergent situation - Appointments of teachers over and above those advertised to be set aside - Order is valid - Appeal dismissed.
