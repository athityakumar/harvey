Johnson and Johnson Limited v Commissioner of Central Excise, Aurangabad

Supreme Court of India

11 March 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, S.P. Kurdukar

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 3036; (1997) 9 SCC 681; 1997 (92) E.L.T. 23; JT 1997 (10) SC 737

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - Central Excise Tariff - Appellants manufactured highly specialised 'Cardio Sutures' used in Vascular Surgery and 'Atraumatic Needled Sutures' used in Ophthalmic Surgery - Whether goods of appellant fall within Entry 3005.90 or they fall within scope of Entry 90.18 within meaning of respective terms 'others' and instruments and appliances used in surgical sciences? - Court observed that items produced by Appellant would fall within Entry 90.18 as terminology surgical appliances has a broader compass than terminology suturing appliances of Chapter 30 of Excise Tariff - Intention of authorities was to grant exemption to certain life - saving and sight - saving articles manufactured in country - Intention is clear from subsequent notifications issued u/s. 5-A of Act of 1995 - Therefore, no reason why a narrow view should be taken to confine two items produced by Appellants to Entry 3005.90 rather than place them in wider connotation of surgical appliances in Entry 90.18 of Chapter 90 - Held, appellants entitled to exemption from whole of duty payable on said two items - Appeals allowed.
