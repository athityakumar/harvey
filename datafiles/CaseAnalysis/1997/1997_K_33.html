Physical Research Laboratory v K.G. Sharma

Supreme Court of India

08 April 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

G.T. Nanavati, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 941; (1997) 4 SCC 257; (1997) SCC (L&S) 1057; AIR 1997 SC 1855; 1997 (4) ALD(SC) 1; 1997 (76) FLR 212; JT 1997 (4) SC 527; 1997 LabIC 1912; 1997 (2) LLJ 625; 1997 (2) LLN 668; 1997 LLR 401; 1997(3) SCALE 479; 1997 (2) SCJ 696; [1997] 3 S.C.R. 733; 1997 (2) SLR 593; 1997 (3) Supreme 695

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Research

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 2 (j) - Bombay Public Trust Act, 1950 - Whether, Physical Research Laboratory (PRL), the appellant, is an 'industry' within the meaning of s. 2(j) of the Industrial Disputes Act, 1947? - Held, Physical Research Laboratory is not an industry even though it is carrying on the activity of research in a systematic manner with the help of its employees as it lacks that element which would make it an organisation carrying on an activity which can be said to be analogous to the carrying on of a trade or business because it is not producing and distributing services which are intended or meant for satisfying human wants and needs, as ordinarily understood - Appeal allowed.
