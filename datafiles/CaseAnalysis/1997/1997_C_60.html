Smt. Ram Kali v Choudhri Ajit Shankar and Others

Supreme Court of India

28 February 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

A.S. Anand, K. Venkataswami

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1047; (1997) 9 SCC 613; 1997 (3) AWC 1639; 1997 (2) CLT 216; 1997 (1) HLR 212; JT 1997 (3) SC 158; 1997(2) SCALE 417; 1997 (3) Supreme 10; 1997 (1) UJ 605

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Practice & Procedure; Women & Children

Keywords: Succession & Inheritance, Marriage & Divorce

Summary: Transfer of Property Act, s. 19 - Hindu Women's Rights to Property Act, 1937 - Hindu Succession Act, 1956, s. 14(1) - Land & Property - (A) Whether, the limited Estate (including the suit house) given by the father-in-law under a registered will D/- 05-01-1921 to his widowed daughter-in-law, is absolute estate u/s. 14(1) of the Hindu Succession Act, 1956? - Held, in view of s. 14(1) of the Hindu Succession Act, the property given to Kamlawati was in recognition of her pre-existing right to maintenance and that property she was to hold absolutely notwithstanding the restrictions placed in the will on her right to alienation - Kamlawati had a pre-existing right and that she was in possession of the suit house when the Hindu Succession Act came into force and in view of s. 14(4) of the said Act, her limited estate enlarges into an absolute one. The reversioners have no right in the property till it comes to them by reversion because the widow is not a trustee of the interests of the reversioner after the coming into force of the 1956 Act - Appeal allowed.
