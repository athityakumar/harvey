Surendra Narain Alias Munna Pandey v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

07 November 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Srinivasan, M.M. Punchhi

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1398; (1998) SCC (Cr) 14; (1998) 1 SCC 76; AIR 1998 SC 192; 1998 (3) CCR 31; 1997 (4) Crimes 255; 1998 CRLJ 359; JT 1997 (9) SC 72; 1997(6) SCALE 675; 1998 (5) SLT 375; [1997] Supp5 S.C.R. 97; 1997 (9) Supreme 337

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s.302 - Murder - Conviction - Identification parade - Substantive evidence - Appellant shot the victim with a pistol and sped away - Witnesses proceeded to police station and lodged a complaint - Victim was taken to hospital where he was declared dead - Order challenged in Court of Session - Identification parade was not held - Trial Court accepted prosecution and found accused guilty of murder punishable u/s.302 of IPC - Accused had voluntarily accepted risk being identified in a parade - HC confirm order of conviction passed by Additional Sessions court charge u/s. 302 IPC against appellant - Hence, instant appeal - Whether Court made enquiry or holding trial should stay its proceedings and Whether conviction order passed by HC was justified - Held, provisions of Code which confers a right on accused to demand that investigation should be conducted in a particular way - Statements made at an identification parade were not substantive evidence at the trial - Trial was not vitiated because accused persons were denied identification - No doubt that failure to hold a test identification parade in spite of an order passed by sessions court was not fatal to prosecution - Reasoning of HC was unassailable - There was ample evidence on record to show that there was a dispute between appellant and deceased which remained unsettled - Appellant had intention to commit offence of murder and accordingly carried out - Appeal dismissed.
