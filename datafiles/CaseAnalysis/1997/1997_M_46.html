M.C. Mehta v Union Of India & Ors. Etc.

Supreme Court of India

20 November 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

V.N. Khare, B.N. Kirpal

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1681; (1997) 8 SCC 770; AIR 1998 SC 186; 1998 (1) JCC 25; JT 1997 (9) SC 237; 1997(7) SCALE 97; [1997] Supp5 S.C.R. 303; 1997 (9) Supreme 418

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Constitution

Summary: Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 21 - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, chaps 4, 5, 8 and 13 - Ample legal power but inadequacy of personnel and other infrastructure for implementation of strict measures for management and control of traffic - Issued of non implementation raised before Supreme Court by way of writ - Supreme Court held, empowered existing authorities to delegate their authority to responsible persons including persons chosen even from public for urgent implementation of measures - Notwithstanding any administrative orders imposing any impediment - Further held, entire scope of matter and particularly control and regulation of traffic is a matter of paramount public safety and, therefore, is evidently within ambit of art. 21 of Constitution - Order accordingly.
