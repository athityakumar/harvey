The United Planters Associationof Southern India v K.G. Sangameswaran And Another

Supreme Court of India

06 March 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Saghir Ahmad, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 2564; (1997) 4 SCC 741; (1997) SCC (L&S) 1404; AIR 1997 SC 1300; 1997 (3) AWC 1853; 1997 (1) CLR 598; 1997 (75) FLR 927; JT 1997 (3) SC 379; 1997 LabIC 1513; 1997 (1) LLJ 1104; 1997 (2) LLN 73; 1997 (1) MLJ 144; 1997 (1) MLJ(SC) 144; 1997(2) SCALE 559; [1997] 2 S.C.R. 756; 1997 (2) SLR 205; 1997 (3) Supreme 436; 1997 (1) UJ 690

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Practice & Procedure - Bihar Shops and Commercial Establishments Act, s. 26(1) - Bombay Industrial Disputes Act, 1938 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 s. 11 A - Tamil Nadu Shops and Establishments Act, 1947, ss. 41(1), (2), 2(3) and (12) - Respondent employed with appellant as an accountant - Misconduct - Dismissed from service - Criminal proceedings were also initiated - Order challenged - Appellate Authority allowed appeal - (A) Whether order was an innocuous order? - Held, this order, ex facie, is punitive in nature as respondent has been held guilty of misconduct - Order is not an innocuous order and cannot be treated as an order by which services of respondent were simply terminated - He was, in fact, dismissed from service - (B) Whether Appellate Authority should have allowed appellant to lead evidence in support of charges leveled against respondent? - Jurisdiction of Appellate Authority to record evidence and to come to its own conclusion on questions involved in appeal is very wide - Appellate Authority has jurisdiction to take evidence at appellate stage and to come to its own conclusion about guilt of delinquent employee - Appellate Authority did not decide application of appellant for recording evidence - Held, Appellate Authority, therefore, committed grave error in exercise of its jurisdiction by not disposing of application of appellant for additional evidence and proceeding to dispose of appeal on ground that order of dismissal having been passed without holding a domestic enquiry, was bad in law - (C) Whether principles of natural justice were observed and whether defect is curable at appellate stage? - Held, since Appellate Authority has to come to its own conclusion on basis of evidence recorded by it, irrespective of findings recorded in domestic enquiry opportunity of hearing which is being provided to respondent at appellate stage will sufficiently meet his demands for a just and proper enquiry - Appeal allowed
