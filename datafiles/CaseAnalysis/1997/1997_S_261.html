K. Krishnamacharayulu & Ors. v Sri Venkateswara Hindu College of Engineering and

Supreme Court of India

21 February 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, S. Saghir Ahmad

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1026; (1997) 3 SCC 571; (1997) SCC (L&S) 841; AIR 1998 SC 295; AIR 1997 SCW 4271; 1997 (3) AWC 1795; 1997 (2) ESC 1229; JT 1997 (3) SC 455; 1998 LabIC 405; 1997 (3) LLN 304; 1997(2) SCALE 558; [1997] 2 S.C.R. 368; 1997 (2) SLR 448; 1997 (3) Supreme 433

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Labour and Industrial Law - Constitution, arts. 21, 39(d) and 226 - Bombay Industrial Disputes Act, 1938 - Private educational institution not receiving grant-in-aid - Catering to public interest - Executive instructions issued by Govt. but no statutory rules issued - (A) Employees claim equal pay for equal work, if entitled - (B) It remedy under writ jursdiction is available to teachers in private educational institutions - Held, remedy would be available u/art. 226 and not under Industrial Disputes Act - They would be entitled to pay parity with Govt. employees - Teachers can avail remedy under art. 226 - Appeal allowed.
