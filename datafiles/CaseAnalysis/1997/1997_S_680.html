Shambhoo Nath Misra v State of U. P.

Supreme Court of India

14 March 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

G.T. Nanavati, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1818; (1997) 5 SCC 326; (1997) SCC (Cr) 676; AIR 1997 SC 2102; 1997 ALJ 1089; 1997 (3) CCR 63; 1997 (2) Crimes 26; 1997 CRLJ 2491; JT 1997 (4) SC 203; 1997 (2) RCR(Rent) 539; 1997(3) SCALE 286; 1997 (1) SCJ 645; [1997] 2 S.C.R. 1139; 1997 (3) Supreme 538

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 109, 409, 420, 465, 468, 477A - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 197(1) - Complaint of forgery and misappropriation - Dismissal - Sustainability - Appellant filed private complaint against respondent No. 2, for offences u/ss. 409, 420, 465, 468, 477A and 109 IPC - Alleging that respondent No. 2 and the Cashier had fabricated his signatures, drawn and misappropriated and amount of Rs. 443.90 which was due and payable to him - Trial Court dismissed the complaint holding that the sanction u/s. 197 of CrPC was not obtained - Aggrieved on appeal HC upheld the order of Trial Court - Hence instant appeal - Whether view taken by Trial Court and upheld by HC for the reason was correct in law - Held, public servant's act was in furtherance of the performance of his official duties - If the act/omission was integral to performance of public duty, the public servant was entitled to the protection u/s. 197 (1) of CrPC - Without previous sanction, the complaint/charge against him for alleged offence could not be proceeded with in the trial - Sanction of appropriate Govt. or competent authority would be necessary to protect a public servant from needless harassment or prosecution - View expressed by HC as well as by Trial Court on the question of sanction was clearly illegal and could not be sustained - Impugned order of dismissing the complaint set aside - Complaint restored - Appeal allowed.
