Heeralal v Kalyan Maland Others

Supreme Court of India

19 November 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Majmudar, M. Jagannadha Rao

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1673; (1998) 1 SCC 278; AIR 1998 SC 618; 1998 (32) ALR 442; 1998 (32) ALR 582; 1998 (1) CalLT 45; 1998 (1) CalLT(SC) 45; 1998 (1) GLH 605; JT 1997 (9) SC 267; 1998 (1) MLJ 101; 1998 (89) RD 140; 1997(7) SCALE 196; 1998 (5) SLT 395; 1998 (Supp) RD 347; [1997] Supp5 S.C.R. 277; 1997 (10) Supreme 56

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution

Summary: Family & Personal - Civil Procedure - Partition - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 15, r. 1 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Appellant had filed a civil suit for partition of 10 items of immovable properties mentioned in Sch. A and also for partition of other properties listed in Sch. B of plaint - In Written Statement a definite stand was taken by defendants that out of listed properties in Sch. A only three properties at Items 4, 9 and 10 were exclusively belonging to contesting defendants and were not joint family properties of plaintiff and Defendants - Meaning thereby that other seven properties listed in Sch. A were admitted to be joint family properties - Not only that but in para 11 of written statement it was submitted that 'the plaintiff is only entitled for partition regarding properties of Sch. A except Items 4, 9 and 10 and all properties mentioned in Sch. B' - On basis of aforesaid stand taken by defendants issues were framed by Trial Court - The issue No. 2 was whether property mentioned in Items 4, 9 and 10 of Sch. 'Aa' attached with plaint is property of Hindu Undivided Family - In the later stage of suit defendant 1 came forward with an amendment application to amend his written statement - In application it was submitted that because of incomplete information supplied by him to his counsel Written Statement came to contain so-called admissions regarding 5 out of 7 items of properties in Schedule A and that he had suffered a heart attack and therefore when Written Statement was moved this error crept in Defendant 1 also wanted to insert a further averment in written statement regarding Schedule B properties that they had ceased to remain in possession of Defendant I and were in possession of trespassers - Trial Judge took view that application for amendment was not a bona fide one and it was moved only with a view to protract proceedings as suit was at stage of trial by then - Judge was not inclined to accept reasons put forward for moving such an amendment application at such a late stage and dismissed application - Defendant 1 carried matter in revision u/s. 115 of CPC, 1908 before High Court - This application was allowed by HC - Whether the order of HC is sustainable in law? - Held, order passed by HC cannot be sustained - Reason is so far as Sch. A properties were concerned, a clear admission was made by Defendants 1 and 2 in their joint written statement that 7 properties out of 10 were joint family properties wherein plaintiff had 1/3 rd share and they had 2/3rds undivided share - Once such a stand was taken, naturally it must be held that there was no contest between parties regarding 7 items of suit properties in Schedule A - Trial Judge, therefore, was perfectly justified in framing Issue No. 2 concerning only remaining three items for which there was dispute between parties - In such a situation under O. 15, r. 1 of CPC, 1908 plaintiff even would have been justified in requesting Court to pass a preliminary decree forthwith qua these 7 properties - Therefore, on facts of this case no case was made out by respondents, contesting defendants, for amending written statement and thus attempting to go behind their admission regarding 5 out of 7 remaining items out of 10 listed properties in Schedule A of plaint - However, so far as Schedule B properties are concerned, from very inception defendants' case qua those properties was that plaintiff had no interest therein - By proposed amendment they wanted to introduce an event with reference to those very properties by submitting that they had been in possession of trespassers - Such amendment could not be said to have in any way adversely or prejudicially affected case of plaintiff or displaced any admission on their part qua Sch. B properties which might have resulted into any legal right in favour of plaintiff - Therefore, so far as Sch. B properties were concerned, amendment could not be found fault with - Appeal partly allowed.
