Commonwealth Trust Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax

Supreme Court of India

30 July 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Wadhwa, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 2842; (1997) 7 SCC 319; AIR 1997 SC 3580; 1997 (142) CTR 214; [1997] 228 ITR 1; JT 1997 (7) SC 479; 1997 (2) KLT 584; 1997(5) SCALE 551; [1997] Supp3 S.C.R. 179; 1998 (5) Supreme 357; [1997] 94 TAXMAN 137; 1997 (2) TLR 934

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 48, 50 and 55(2) - (A) Whether Tribunal is right in law in deleting Rs. 1, 260 towards house rent u/s. 40(a)(v) of 1961 Act? - High Court answered this issue in favour of Revenue and against assessee - Impugned judgment in so far as it answers this question has been overruled by this court in CIT v. Mafatlal Gangabhai and Co. (P ) Ltd. - Accordingly, Court answered in favour of assessee and against Revenue - (B) Capital asset is depreciable and assessee availed deduction on account of depreciation - Determination of cost of acquisition - Held, cost of acquisition shall have to be determined in terms of provisions of s. 50 r/w s. 48 of Act - Because s. 50 absolutely provides for fixing cost of acquisition in case of depreciable assets - (C) Whether Tribunal was justified in holding that assessee did not have right of substituting market value as on January 1, 1954, in respect of depreciable assets? - While Revenue gets support from decisions of High Courts of Gujarat, Allahabad and Calcutta for upholding impugned judgment, assessee gets support from decision of Bombay HC - Gujarat, Allahabad and Calcutta HC decisions are reported, respectively, in Rajnagar Vaktapur Ginning, Pressing and Manufacturing Co Ltd v. CIT - Further held, Court uphold views of Gujarat, Allahabad and Calcutta HCs and of Kerala HC in impugned judgment - Impugned judgment of HC whereby has been answered in favour of Revenue is, therefore, upheld - Appeal No. 2978 of 1982 is dismissed and Civil Appeal No. 2979 of 1982 is allowed.
