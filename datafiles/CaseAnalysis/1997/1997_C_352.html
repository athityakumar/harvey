National Rayon Corporation Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax

Supreme Court of India

29 July 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Sen, K.T. Thomas

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 2841; (1997) 7 SCC 56; AIR 1997 SC 3487; 1997 (3) CLT 367; 1997 (142) CTR 202; [1997] 227 ITR 764; JT 1997 (7) SC 372; 1997(5) SCALE 415; [1997] Supp3 S.C.R. 140; 1997 (7) Supreme 396; [1997] 93 TAXMAN 754

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Corporate - Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Super Profit Tax Act, 1963, sch. II - Companies (Profits) Surtax Act, 1964 - Whether a sum of Rs. 79 lakhs representing debenture redemption reserve was includable in computing the capital of the assessee-Co. for the purpose of the Companies (Profits) Surtax Act, 1964? - Perusal of the balance-sheet shows that the assessee-Co. had floated and actually issued 6 1/2 per cent secured redeemable mortgage debentures, against the security of land, buildings and machinery of the company and a floating charge on the undertaking - None of the debentures appear to have been redeemed during the previous years - The debenture redemption reserve must be regarded as a provision made by the assessee-Co. to enable it to redeem the said debentures when they became due for redemption - Since the aggregate amount of debentures is much larger than the amount of the debenture redemption reserve, it cannot be said that there was any excess which could be taken as reserve - An amount set aside to meet a future liability, which was certain to come into existence, must be regarded as a provision and not as a reserve - The High Court has come to the correct conclusion - Held, appeal is dismissed.
