Shri Dinesh Trivedi, M.P. and Others v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

20 March 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, Sujata V. Manohar

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1093; (1997) 4 SCC 306; 1997 (3) CLT 21; 1997 JIC 1105; JT 1997 (4) SC 237; 1997(3) SCALE 117; 1997 (1) SCJ 697; [1997] 3 S.C.R. 93; 1997 (3) Supreme 294

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Practice & Procedure; Service; Socio-Economic

Keywords: Freedom Of Speech And Expression

Summary: Socio-Economic - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947, s. 6 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, s. 197 - Criminal - Whether, the supporting material placed before the Vohra Committee can be disclosed for the benefit of the general public - Held, no the disclosure of the supporting material placed before the Vohra Committee to the public at large would, instead of aiding the interest of the public, be severely and detrimentally injurious to it - There is no necessity for us to express ourselves on the constitutionality of s. 5 of the Official Secrets Act, 1923 - We recommend that a high level committee be appointed by the President of India on the advice or the Prime Minister, and after consultation with the Speaker of the Lok Sabha - The Committee shall monitor investigations involving the kind of nexus referred to in the Vohra Committee Report and carry out the objectives described earlier - In the case of public servants, sanctions are required, for instance, u/s. 197 of the Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 and u/s. 6 of the Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947 - The Nodal Agency, in its present form, may not command the confidence of the people in this regard this is a serious handicap for, in such matters, people's confidence is of the essence - Writ Petition disposed off.
