Mohmoodkhan Mahboobkhan Pathan v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

03 February 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, M.M. Punchhi

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 2476; (1997) 10 SCC 600; (1997) SCC (Cr) 894; AIR 1998 SC 2360; AIR 1998 SCW 2334; 1998 CRLJ 3635; JT 1997 (2) SC 232; [1997] 1 S.C.R. 830; 1998 (1) SLJ 122

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Illegal Gratification

Summary: Criminal - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947, ss. 4(1)and 5 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, s. 20(1) - Act of keeping amount of money, called along with application by sub-registrar, in his pocket instead of drawer before being rounded up - (A) Whether presumption u/s. 4(1) of 1947 Act can be raised in light of meaning of 'gratification'? - (B) Whether act of putting into pocket is decisive to conclude that it was intended for himself? - Considering possibilities of issue of receipt or collection of lawful charges - Held, court cannot raise presumption - Not decisive to conclude, accused acquitted - Appeal allowed.
