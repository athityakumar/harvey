C. K. Damodaran Nair v Government of India

Supreme Court of India

08 January 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

M.K. Mukherjee, S.P. Kurdukar

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 2597; (1997) 9 SCC 477; (1997) SCC (Cr) 654; AIR 1997 SC 551; 1997 (1) CCR 138; 1997 (1) Crimes 75; 1997 CRLJ 739; JT 1997 (2) SC 494; 1997(1) SCALE 126; 1997 (1) SCJ 142; [1997] 1 S.C.R. 107; 1997 (2) SLJ 55; 1997 (1) Supreme 478

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Illegal Gratification

Summary: Criminal - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947, ss. 4 and 5(2) - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 161 - Bribery - Accused, public servant alleged to have obtained bribe from hospital authorities - Currency notes recovered from possession of accused - Evidence of independent witnesses that they saw complainant handing notes to accused, and accused putting them in his pocket - (A) Whether presumption u/s. 4 of Act of 1947 attracted? (B) Whether conviction of accused correct? - (C) Whether testimony of prosecution witnesses that accused demanded bribe can be disbelieved? - (A) Yes, presumption u/s. 4 of Act of 1947 attracted (B) Conviction of accused proper - (C) testimony of witnesses cannot be disbelieved - Appeal dismissed.
