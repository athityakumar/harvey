Commissioner of Income Tax v Artex Manufacturing Company

Supreme Court of India

08 July 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Agrawal, G.B. Pattanaik

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 2846; (1997) 6 SCC 437; AIR 1997 SC 2970; 1997 (141) CTR 290; [1997] 227 ITR 260; JT 1997 (6) SC 261; 1997(4) SCALE 548; [1997] Supp1 S.C.R. 608; 1997 (7) Supreme 130; [1997] 93 TAXMAN 357; 1997 (2) TLR 864

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 41(2) and 45 - Surplus - Taxable - Assessment year 1967-68 - Whether surplus as a result of difference between written down value and sale consideration for plant, machinery and dead stock transferred by assessee is taxable u/s. 41(2) of 1961 Act - Held, surplus is taxable u/s. 41(2) and not u/s. 45 - Liability u/s. 41(2) is limited to amount of surplus to extent of difference between written down value and actual cost - If surplus amount exceeds difference then it is taxable under Capital Gain - Appeal partly allowed.
