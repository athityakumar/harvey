Panchugopal Barua and Others v Umesh Chandra Goswami and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 February 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

A.S. Anand, S.B. Majmudar

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 978; (1997) 4 SCC 713; AIR 1997 SC 1041; AIR 1997 SCW 1035; 1997 (2) CLT 253; JT 1997 (2) SC 554; 1997 (2) MLJ(SC) 27; 1997 (1) RCR(Rent) 370; 1997(2) SCALE 145; 1997 (1) SCJ 452; [1997] 2 S.C.R. 12; 1997 (2) Supreme 152; 1997 (1) UJ 523

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Indian Easements Act, 1882, s. 60(b) - Second appeal - Maintainability - (A) Whether, High Court was justified in entertaining second appeal on an altogether new point, neither pleaded nor canvassed in Subordinate Courts? - Held, no new case can be submitted in second appeal or raise a new issue, not supported by pleadings or evidence on record - Unless appeal involves a substantial question of law, a second appeal shall not lie to HC - (B) Whether, relief u/s. 60(b) can be extended to respondent on grounds of 'justice, equity and good conscience? - Further held, person seeking relief must come to court with clean hands - Respondent not entitled to relief - Appeal allowed.
