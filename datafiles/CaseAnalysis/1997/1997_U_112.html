Union of India and Others v K. V. Baby and Another

Supreme Court of India

21 August 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Wadhwa, Sujata V. Manohar

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 3190; (1998) 9 SCC 252; (1998) SCC (L&S) 539; 1999 (1) AWC 703; 1999 (1) CLR 377; JT 1998 (7) SC 435; 1999 (1) LLJ 1290

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Whether Tribunal has rightly held that respondents are entitled to regular salaried bearers/waiters of appellant? - Persons who are engaged on basis of individual contracts to work on a commission basis cannot, by very nature of their engagement, be equated with regular employees doing similar work - Their appointment and mode of selection, qualifications cannot be compared with regular employees - Recruitment Rules and service conditions of Southern Railways do not apply to respondents, since they are not regular employees on cadre of Railway Catering Service - Held, their responsibilities cannot be equated with responsibilities of regular employees - Thus, impugned judgment of Tribunal is set aside - Appeals allowed.
