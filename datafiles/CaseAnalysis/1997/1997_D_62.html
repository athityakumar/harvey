Regional Provident Fund Commissioner and Another v Dharamsi Morarji Chemical Company Limited

Supreme Court of India

09 December 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Majmudar, M. Jagannadha Rao

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 373; (1998) 2 SCC 446; (1998) SCC (L&S) 584; 1998 (2) CLR 151; 1998 (80) FLR 561; 1998 (1) LLJ 1060; 1998 (3) LLN 932

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Employees' Provident Funds and Miscellaneous Provisions Act, 1952, ss. 2A and 16(1)(b) - Infancy benefit - Refusal to grant - Whether new factory established by respondent was an infant industry and entitled for exemption under Act? - Held, products manufactured at two factories are distinct and different - Workers of two factories are also separate and are not transferable from one factory to another - Factories separately registered under Factories Act - Maintain and draw up separate profit and loss accounts - No interconnection in matter of supervisory, financial or managerial control - Could not be said, that Roha factory, only because it is owned by respondent company, was not a new establishment or could be treated to be a branch or department of Ambarnath factory - High Court was not in error in granting infancy benefit u/s. 16(1)(b) to Roha factory in first two years - Appeal dismissed.
