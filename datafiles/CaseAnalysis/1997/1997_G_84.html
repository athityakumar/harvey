Union Of India v Gujarat State Export Corporation

Supreme Court of India

24 January 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, S.C. Sen

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 3310; 2000 (121) E.L.T. 15

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Customs - Indirect Tax - Modvat credit - Violation of condition - Issuance of notice - Respondent/State received notice from UOI/petitioner regarding illegal imports and exports - It was alleged that respondent violated conditions of non-availment of Modvat Credit - Special Commissioner was appointed to inquire as well as show-cause notice was issued to respondent - Whether issuance of notice as well as appointment of Special Commissioner was legal in nature.

Held, two things are brought to Court’s notice. One is that Special Commissioner, who was appointed to inquire into and consider all cases of alleged illegal import, has quashed all 12 show cause notices holding that there is no substance therein. It is stated that CBEC is considering that to question said orders by way of review/appeal. Other circumstance is that Govt. has since issued orders on 3-1-1997 stating that any exporter, who has violated conditions of non availment of Modvat Credit, reverses Modvat Credit along with specified interest on or before 31-1-1997, no action will be taken against him and import licenses shall be deemed to be in order. In this view of matter, Court does not think that any purpose will be served in considering with this petition. Matter is dropped at this stage. Let law takes its own course. Order accordingly.

Legislation Cited

Central Excise Rules, 1944

Central Excise Rules, 1944 r. 57A
