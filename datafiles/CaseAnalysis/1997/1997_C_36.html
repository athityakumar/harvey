Ashok Kumar Sharma and Others v Chander Shekhar and Another

Supreme Court of India

10 March 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, S.B. Majmudar, S. Saghir Ahmad

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 2621; (1997) 4 SCC 18; (1997) SCC (L&S) 913; 1997 (2) CLT 104; JT 1997 (4) SC 99; 1997 (1) LLJ 1160; 1997 (1) LLN 1036; 1997(2) SCALE 707; [1997] 2 S.C.R. 896; 1997 (2) SLJ 131; 1997 (2) SLR 409; 1997 (4) Supreme 320; 1997 (1) UJ 766

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Public Service Commission Rules, r. 37 - Review Petition - Whether majority was right in extending the principle of r. 37 and in taking the view that it is enough for a candidate to be qualified by the date of interview even if he was not qualified by the last date prescribed for receiving the applications, is correct in law? - All the three learned Judges are unanimous in holding that seniority given to the 33 candidates not to be disturbed - Matter within the discretion of the court - Bench, sitting in review jurisdiction, not to interfere with the said unanimous opinion of three learned Judges - Not proper to disturb the inter-se seniority between the two groups of selected candidates - Petitions dismissed.
