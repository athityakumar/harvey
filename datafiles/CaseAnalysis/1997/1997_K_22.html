The Executive Engineer(State Of Karnataka) v K. Somasetty & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

02 May 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, D.P. Wadhwa

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 996; (1997) 5 SCC 434; (1997) SCC (L&S) 1229; AIR 1997 SC 2663; 1997 (2) CLR 387; 1997 (76) FLR 851; JT 1997 (5) SC 611; 1997 LabIC 2651; 1997 (2) LLJ 698; 1997 (3) LLN 69; 1997 (4) SLR 582; [1997] Supp1 S.C.R. 46; 1997 (6) Supreme 13

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Labour and Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 2(j) and 11 - Industry - State - Function of public welfare carried by State - (A) Whether state is 'industry' - State is under constitutional mandate to bring about welfare State - It is sovereign function of State - Daily wager appointed on project undertaken by State - Discharged on closure of project - (B) Whether order of labour court reinstating him with backwages is without jurisdiction - (C) Whether the daily wager had right to post - Held, (A) Does not make it industry - (B) Is without jurisdiction - (C) On closure of project - (C) No right to post - Appeal allowed.
