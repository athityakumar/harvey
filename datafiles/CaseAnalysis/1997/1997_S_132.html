Inspecting Assistant Commissioner, Bombay and Others v Sharat Narayan Parab

Supreme Court of India

12 November 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

Sujata V. Manohar

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 341; (1998) SCC (L&S) 316; (1998) 1 SCC 484; 1998 (78) FLR 79; JT 1997 (9) SC 123; 1998 (2) LLJ 653; 1997(7) SCALE 4; 1998 (1) SLR 8; 1998 (6) SLT 27; 1997 (9) Supreme 381

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 147, 149, 332 and 506 - Dismissal - Validity - Whether order of dismissal of respondent was in violation of principles of natural justice? - Held, inquiry was properly conducted by Inquiry Officer - Respondent participated in inquiry and had been given proper opportunity to defend himself - Tribunal has relied upon decision in case of Union of India v. Mohd Ramzan Khan [(1991-I-LLJ-29) (SC)] - Present inquiry was completed long prior to Ramzan Khan case - Reasoning of Tribunal cannot be sustained - Punishment imposed cannot be considered as unreasonable - Appeal allowed.
