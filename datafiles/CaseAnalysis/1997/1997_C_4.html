Government of A.P v C. Muralidhar

Supreme Court of India

22 July 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Agrawal, G.T. Nanavati

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 2096; (1997) 6 SCC 594; (1997) SCC (L&S) 1746; AIR 1997 SC 3005; JT 1997 (7) SC 59; 1997 LabIC 2884; 1997(5) SCALE 57; 1998 (1) SLJ 210; 1997 (4) SLR 756; 1997 (7) Supreme 193; 1997 (2) UJ 641

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service; Socio-Economic

Summary: Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 309 and 311 - Andhra Pradesh Civil Services (Classification, Control and Appeal) Rules 1963, r. 20 - Disciplinary proceedings for charges related to holding of assets disproportionate to known sources of income and for having acquired assets without permission of Rules, 1964 - Criminal case pending against delinquent officer for possessing disproportionate assets - Held, disciplinary proceedings for very same charge cannot take place but Disciplinary Authority could proceed with charges relating to acquisition of assets without permission of department.
