Karan Singh & Others v Union Of India

Supreme Court of India

24 September 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

V.N. Khare, A.S. Anand

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 2989; (1997) 8 SCC 186; AIR 1997 SC 3889; 1997 (4) CLT 35; 1997 (68) DLT 900; JT 1997 (8) SC 257; 1997(6) SCALE 255; 1997 (Supp) AWC 675; [1997] Supp4 S.C.R. 237; 1997 (8) Supreme 377

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Land Acquisition

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4, 6 - Land acquisition - Notification - Compensation - Enhancement - Land which included appellants' land was notified for acquisition by notification dt. 17-11-1980 u/s. 4 of the Act for purpose of planned development of Delhi - After issual of declaration u/s. 6 of the Act, Collector by award dt. 2-7-1983 assessed market value of entire land at Rs. 9,000/- per bigha and assessed other portions of land in the village at lower rate of compensation - Not content with award, appellants sought reference for determination of compensation - Trial Court determined market value at rate of Rs. 23,000/- per bigha but awarded lower rate of compensation for other portions of land - On appeal, HC granted compensation at uniform rate of Rs. 76,550 per bigha to all - Hence, instant appeals - Appellants contended that exhibits relied upon by them for enhancement of compensation for acquired land were erroneously rejected by HC on ground that evidences related to post notification - Held, market value of piece of land for determining compensation u/s. 23 of 1894 Act would be price at which vendor and vendee were willing to sell or purchase land - Consideration in terms of price received for land under bonafide transaction on date of notification issued u/s. 4 or few days before or after issue of notification u/s. 4 of the Act would generally show market value of acquired land and market value of acquired land should be assessed in terms of those transactions - In absence of any evidence of sale of land on date of issue of notification u/s. 4 of the Act, under certain conditions post notification transactions of sales of land could be relied upon in determining market value of acquired land - One of conditions being that it should be shown before Court by reliable evidence that there was no appreciation of value of land during period of issue of notification and date of transaction of sale - In instant case, it was found that except filing of exhibits, no effort was made by claimants to establish before Court that there was no upward rise in price or increase in price of land in the village during period between issue of notification and date of execution of exhibits sought to be relied upon - HC did not commit any error in rejecting said exhibits while arriving at market value of acquired land - No merit in appeals - Appeals dismissed.
