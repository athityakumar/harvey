Messrs Gujarat State Fertilizers Company v Collector of Central Excise

Supreme Court of India

28 February 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Bharucha, S.B. Majmudar

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1049; (1997) 4 SCC 140; AIR 1997 SC 3620; 1997 (69) ECR 475; 1997 (91) E.L.T. 3; JT 1997 (3) SC 307; 1997 (19) RLT 605; 1997(2) SCALE 407; [1997] 2 S.C.R. 561; 1997 (5) Supreme 240

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Exemption Notifications

Summary: Indirect Tax - Excise - Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944, s. 3 - Central Excise Rules 1944, r. 8 - Notification No. 75 of 1984 and Notification No. 40 of 1985 - Exemption to Raw naptha and Ammonia - Availability - Raw naphtha utilised by manufacturer to produce Ammonia - Ammonia in turn utilised to produce Molten urea - Molten urea captively consumed to produce Malamine - Whether exemption to Raw Naptha and Ammonia can be refused on ground that molten urea the intermediate product or malamine the final product was not soil fertilizer - Held, exemption cannot be refused on ground - Appeal allowed.
