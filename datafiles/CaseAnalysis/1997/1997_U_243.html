Union of India v Tata Engineering and Locomotive Company Limited

Supreme Court of India

10 November 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Jagannadha Rao, S.C. Sen

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1577; (1997) 8 SCC 730; AIR 1998 SC 287; 1997 (73) ECR 725; 1997 (96) E.L.T. 209; JT 1997 (9) SC 81; 1997(7) SCALE 2; 1998 (5) SLT 298; [1997] Supp5 S.C.R. 109; 1997 (9) Supreme 317

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - Practice & Procedure - Assessment of value of excisable goods - Judicial review - Instant appeal filed by UOI against the judgment and order of the DB of HC directing the Assistant Collector of Central Excise to pass without delay appropriate final order on all the price lists already submitted by the Company - HC also held that the price at which the Company sold the vehicles at the factory gate at Jamshedpur should be the normal price for the purpose of computation of the value of the excisable goods - Court also held that the Assistant Collector of Central Excise could only call for relevant documents for the purpose of assessment of the value of the excisable goods.

Held, writ petition should not have been entertained by the HC at all. The Assistant Collector is entitled to complete the assessment as he thinks fit in exercise of his judgment and according to his understanding of the law and facts. The Court should not try to control the mode and manner in which an assessment should be made. HC was in error in entertaining this writ petition. Therefore, the appeal is allowed and the judgment of the HC under appeal is set aside. Appeal allowed.
