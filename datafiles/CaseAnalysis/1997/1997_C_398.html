State of Madhya Pradesh and others v Mahesh Kumar and others etc

Supreme Court of India

29 April 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, S.C. Sen

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1990; (1997) 10 SCC 558; 1997 (2) CLT 464; 1997 (70) ECR 225; 1997 (92) E.L.T. 9; JT 1997 (5) SC 82; 1997 (20) RLT 243; 1997(3) SCALE 731; [1997] 3 S.C.R. 1088; 1997 (5) Supreme 67

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Customs - Banking & Finance - Customs Act, 1962 - (A) Whether importer was entitled to relief under Entry No. 84.66 of Customs Tariff regarding customs duty payable at time of clearance of imported commodities? - Appellant got import licence on 22-10-1980 still he did not make any application for registration for almost six months thereafter - Letter which he claims to be prescribed application was sent only a month before clearance of goods from port - Held, no legal foundation for both appellants to secure reliefs prescribed in Entry No. 84.66 of Customs Tariff - Appeals dismissed.
