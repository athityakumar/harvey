Commissioner of Income Tax v Tata Iron and Steel Company Limited

Supreme Court of India

17 December 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

S.S.M. Quadri, S.C. Sen

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1239; (1998) 2 SCC 366; 1998 (150) CTR 209; [1998] 231 ITR 285; 2001 (2) SLT 461; 2001 (4) Supreme 441; [1998] 98 TAXMAN 459

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 43-A - Whether, on facts and in circumstance of case, and having regard to fact that net gain was made by assessee-Co. from fluctuations in rate of foreign exchange while repaying installments of foreign loan for Assessment Year 1960-61, appropriate part of said gain (i.e., after excluding that portion of it which is attributable to element of interest) was gain on capital account which went to reduce 'actual cost' of depreciable assets for computing depreciation for Assessment Year 1960-1961? - Held, what is actual cost must depend on amount paid by assessee to acquire asset - Amount may have been borrowed by assessee - But even if assessee did not repay loan it will not alter cost of asset - If borrower defaults in repayment of a part of loan, cost of asset will not change - What has to be borne in mind is that cost of an asset and cost of raising money for purchase of asset are two different and independent transactions - Even if an asset is purchased with non-repayable subsidy received from Govt., cost of asset will be price paid by assessee for acquiring asset - Manner or mode of repayment of loan has nothing to do with cost of an asset acquired by assessee for purpose of his business - Appeals dismissed.
