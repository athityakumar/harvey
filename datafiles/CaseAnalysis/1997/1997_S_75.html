Samatha v State of Andhra Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 July 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, S. Saghir Ahmad, G.B. Pattanaik

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 3331; (1997) 8 SCC 191; AIR 1997 SC 3297; JT 1997 (6) SC 449; 1997(4) SCALE 746; [1997] Supp2 S.C.R. 305

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property; Mines & Minerals

Keywords: Internal Revenue Code, 1954, Constitutionality, Andhra Pradesh Forest Act, 1967, Schedules, Government of India Act, 1919, Madras Cultivating Tenants Protection Act, 1955, Chota Nagpur Tenancy Act, 1908, Law of property Act, 1925, Madras Forest Act, 1882, Bihar Scheduled Areas Regulations, 1969, Mineral Concession Rules, Agency Tracts and Land Transfer Act, 1917, Agricultural Land and Tenancy Act, 1950, Andhra Pradesh (Ceiling on Agricultural Holdings) Act, 1961, Andhra Pradesh (Provision of the Panchayats Extension to Scheduled Areas) Act, 1966, Andhra Pradesh Co-operative Societies Act 1964, Assam Land and Revenue Regulation Act, 1964, Bombay Province Land Revenue Code, 1879, Calcutta Municipality Act, Central Forest Act, Constitution (First Amendment) Bill, Constitution of India Act, 1935, Forest (Conservation) Ordinance, 1980, Ganjam and Vizagapatnam Act, 1839, Government of India (Adoption of Indian Laws) Order, 1937, H.P. Transfer of Land (Regulation) Act, 1968, Kerala Scheduled Tribes (Restriction of Transfer of Land and Restoration of Alienation Lands) Act, 1975, M.P.L.P. Code, 1959, Constitution 1948, Maharashtra (Restoration of Lands to Scheduled Tribes) Act, 1974, Maharashtra Land Revenue Code and Tenancy Laws (Amendment) Act, 1974, Manipur Land Reforms and Land Revenue Act, 1970, Santhal Pargana Tenancy (Supplementary Provisions) Act, 1959, Scheduled Areas (Part 'A' States) Order, 1950, Scheduled Areas (Part 'B' States) Order, 1950, Sherman Anti Trust Act, Sikkim Agricultural Land Ceiling and Reforms Act, 1977, Sikkim Revenue Order, 1977, Tamil Nadu State Carriages and Contract Carriages (Acquisition) Act, U.P. Jamindari Abolition and Land Reforms Act, U.P. Land Laws (Amendment) Act, 1982, Andhra Pradesh Scheduled Area Land Transfer Regulation, 1959, AP Tribe Area Regulation, Mysore Land Revenue (Amendment) Rule, 1960, Orissa Scheduled Areas (Transfer of Immovable Property) Regulation

Summary: K. Ramaswamy, J.

(A) Constitution - Mines & Minerals - Land & Property - Andhra Pradesh Scheduled Area Land Transfer Regulation, 1959 - Forest Conservation Act, 1980 - Mines and Minerals (Regulation and Development) Act, 1957, s. 11(5) - Borra reserved forest area alongwith its environs consisting of 14 villages, notified scheduled area - State Government granted mining leases in this area to several non-tribal persons - Respondent No. 13, was granted mining lease in that reserved forest area - Appellant-Society filed writ petitions challenging power of Government to transfer Government land situated in tribal area to non-tribals for mining purpose - DB of HC, held that Regulation does not prohibit transfer of Government land by way of lease to non-tribals - In appeal filed by Hyderabad Abrasives and Minerals, another DB, earlier had taken opposite view and held mining leases illegal and word 'person' used in s. 3 of Regulation includes Government - It was held that mining lease in a forest area to non-forest purpose or renewal thereof, without prior approval of Central Government, is in violation of s. 2 of FC Act - Hence, present appeals directed to resolve mutually inconsistent law adumbrated by two DB of HC - (A) Whether word 'person' u/s. 3(1) of Regulation would include State Government? - Held, State, by Cabinet form of Government, is a persona ficta a Corporate sole - Word "person" in s. 3(1)(a) would, therefore, be construed to include not merely natural persons, in context of tribal and non tribal who deal with land in Scheduled Areas by transfer inter vivos but all juristic persons in generic sense - It must be construed in a broader perspectivity, unless statute, either expressly or by necessary implication, exempts State from operation of Act as against State and would include "State Government" - (B) Whether grant of mining leases lands in Scheduled Areas belonging to Government is outside purview of Regulation? - Held, transfer of land in Scheduled Area by way of lease, for mining purpose in favour of non-tribals stands prohibited by para 5(2) (b) of Fifth Schedule read with S. 3 of Regulation - Government stands prohibited to transfer mining leases to Corporation aggregate etc. except to its instrumentality - Lease being a transfer of an interest in land or a right to enjoy such property during subsistence of lease, its transfer stands prohibited - Further, s. 11(5) of Act, 1957 brought by State Amendment Act prohibits grant of mining lease in Scheduled Areas in favour of non-tribals - (C) Whether leases are in violation of FC ACT OR EP ACT? - Held, it is, necessary for State Government to obtain prior permission of Central Government for (1) dereservation of forest; and (2) use of forest land for non-forest purpose - Prior approval of Central Government was a condition precedent for such permission - It would, therefore, be mandatory that even renewal of mining leases without prior approval of Central Government, is void - In present case, mining leases were granted by State Government or were transferred and re-transferred with sanction of State Government from private individuals to juristic persons, partnership firms or companies - Therefore, all mining leases or renewals thereof are in violation of Fifth Schedule - Equally, mining leases/renewals of mining leases by State Government are in violation of Regulation 3(1)(a) read with 3(2) of Regulation and FC Act and are all void - Appeals of Samatha allowed - Appeal of Hyderabad Abrasives and Minerals (P) Ltd. dismissed since their licence has already expired and grant of renewal prohibited under FC Act and s. 11(5) of Mining Act - Order accordingly.

S. SAGHIR AHMAD, J. Assenting judgment

(B) Constitution - Andhra Pradesh Scheduled Area Land Transfer Regulation, 1959 - Forest Conservation Act, 1980 - Held, prohibition that no person, whether he is a member of Scheduled Tribe or not, shall transfer his immovable property to a non-tribal must, therefore, in its scope cover Government, as well, which, if it possesses land in Agency Tracts, cannot transfer it either by sale, allotment, lease or otherwise to a non-tribal - To this limited extent, Government has to be treated as a "person" within meaning of s. 3(1)(A) of Regulations - Order accordingly.

CA. No. 4601-4602/97. G. B. Pattanaik, J. Dissenting judgment

(C) Constitution - Andhra Pradesh Scheduled Area Land Transfer Regulation, 1959 - Forest Conservation Act, 1980 - Interpretation of word 'Person' in S. 3(1)(A) whereby transfer of land in scheduled area in favour of non tribal became prohibited and non tribals holding land in scheduled area were prohibited from transferring land in favour of persons other than tribals - Held, changes brought about by amended Regulation were essentially intended to facilitate effective enforcement of 1959 Regulation and object of amended regulation cannot be held to be total prohibition of alienation of all land including a government land within scheduled area in favour of a non-tribal - Expression 'person' used in s. 3(1)(a) of regulation should have its natural meaning throughout Section to mean 'natural person' and it does not include State - Word 'person' as amended in 1970 has to be construed to convey and same meaning throughout S. and said expression does not include State Government - State is not denuded of its power in matter of exploiting its mineral resources within scheduled area by a grant or renewal of lease even in favour of non-tribal persons and restrictions and embargo contained in s. 3(1)(a) is not applicable to State in dealing with land belonging to State - Appeals disposed of.

(D) Land & Property - Mines and Minerals (Regulation and Development) Act, 1957, s. 11(5) - Whether leases in question are contrary to provisions of Act? - Held, on and after coming into force s. 11(5) of Act no mining leases can be granted or renewed within Scheduled Area to any person who is not a member of Scheduled Tribe within State of AP - Only exception being as contained in proviso, namely, an undertaking owned or controlled by State or Central Government or a society registered or deemed to be registered under Andhra Pradesh Co-operative Societies Act which is composed of members of Scheduled Tribes are excluded from rigours of Sub-s. (5) of S. 11 - Provisions is prospective in operation and would be applicable to any or renewal of a lease subsequent to enactment of S. 11(5) of Act - Leases of respondents being prior to aforesaid enactment these are not hit by said provisions - Appeals disposed of.

C.A. No. 4603 of 1997. G. B. Pattanaik, J.

(E) Mines & Minerals - Land & Property - Andhra Pradesh Scheduled Area Land Transfer Regulation, 1959 - Forest Conservation Act, 1980 - Writ petition was filed before Andhra Pradesh HC praying therein that mining activities which are carried on by respondents 6 to 10 should be immediately stopped as grant of mining leases in their favour is in contravention of s. 3 of Regulation as well as s. 2 of FC Act - HC held that transfer of any land in scheduled area to a non-tribal was void under s. 3 of Regulation, and therefore, lease in favour of respondent no. 6 within scheduled area was void - Further, that word 'person' in s. 3 of Regulation includes Government, and therefore, leases granted by State Government in scheduled area to a non-tribal was void - Held, in view of conclusion that word 'person' used in s. 3(1)(a) of Regulation does not include State Government, and therefore, prohibitions contained in said Regulation with regard to transfer of land in favour of a non-tribal will not apply to transfer of land made by government for purpose of mining lease, conclusion of HC on this score erroneous - However, as question of applicability of Conservation Act, conclusion of HC in impugned judgment to be sustained - Appeal dismissed.
