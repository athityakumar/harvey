The Agriculture Produce Marketcommittee-Gondal & Ors. v Shri Gridharbhai Ramjibhaichhaniyara & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

05 May 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Wadhwa, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 2012; (1997) 5 SCC 468; AIR 1997 SC 2674; 1997 (30) ALR 613; 1998 (1) AWC 37; 1997 (3) CLT 33; JT 1997 (5) SC 591; 1998 (89) RD 781; 1997(4) SCALE 298; [1997] Supp1 S.C.R. 74; 1997 (6) Supreme 165

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Gujarat Agricultural Produced Markets Act, 1963 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 39 r. 2 and O. 1 r. 8 - Interim relief - Legality - Market Committee was constituted under the provisions of the Act - Advertisement was published inviting offers from the interested persons for allotment of shops in new market yard vide notification dt. 23-11-1991 - After the advertisement so made, respondents No.1 and 2 filed suit for perpetual injunction in a representative capacity under O. 1, r. 8 of CPC restraining the Market Committee from making allotment of the shop and ad interim injunction under O. 39 of CPC was sought for and was granted by the Trial Court - On appeal, it was confirmed by HC with a further rider - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, HC has stated in the order as if there is a concluded right between the parties. Since the right of the respondents is still in embryo even O. R. 2 39 of CPC is inapplicable and the enforcement thereof is also unthinkable. The HC has further enlarged the scope of the relief which was not even sought for in trial. HC has committed not only manifest error of law, but crossed the limitation of O. 43 r. 1 of CPC in granting the relief. The order of the court below set aside. Appeal allowed.
