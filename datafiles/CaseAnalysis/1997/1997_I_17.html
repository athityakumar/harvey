Oriental Insurance Company Limited v Inderjit Kaur and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 December 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Bharucha, J.S. Verma, S.C. Sen

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1440; (1998) 1 SCC 371; (1999) SCC (Cr) 148; AIR 1998 SC 588; [1998] 91 Comp Cas 306; JT 1997 (9) SC 760; 1998 (1) MLJ(SC) 78; 1997(7) SCALE 488; 1998 (1) SLT 341; [1997] Supp6 S.C.R. 255; 1997 (10) Supreme 289

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Corporate; Insurance

Summary: Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, ss. 147(5) and 149(1) - Insurance Act, 1938, s. 64VB - Liability of insurer - Third party risks policy - Without premium covers bus - Motor accident - Held, insurer liable to indemnify third parties in respect of liability which policy covers and satisfy awards of compensation - Provisions u/s. 64VB of Insurance Act does not absolve insurer of his liability.
