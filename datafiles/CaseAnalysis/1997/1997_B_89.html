State of Madhya Pradesh and Others v Bharat Heavy Electricals

Supreme Court of India

14 August 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Kirpal, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 573; (1997) 7 SCC 1; AIR 1997 SC 3285; 1998 (99) E.L.T. 33; JT 1997 (7) SC 427; 1998 (25) RLT 327; 1997(5) SCALE 471; 1997 (106) STC 604; [1997] Supp3 S.C.R. 435; 1997 (7) Supreme 365

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Customs; Indirect Tax

Summary: Constitution Of India, 1950, art.246; M.P. Sthaniya Kshetra Me Mal Ke Pravesh Par Kar Adhiniyam, 1976, s.7(5) - Presumption - Under s.7(5) of the Act - Not rebuttable - Plea, it leaves no discretion to reduce or waive penalty on ground of absence of mala fide - Held, not ultra vires - Penalty of ten times is only the maximum amount which could be levied to lesser amount depending upon the facts and circumstances of each case.
