Smt. Sathyaprema Manjunatha Gowda v Controller of Estate Duty

Supreme Court of India

03 April 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Wadhwa, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1798; (1997) 10 SCC 684; 1997 (3) CLT 44; 1997 (143) CTR 415; [1997] 227 ITR 1; JT 1997 (6) SC 51; 1997(4) SCALE 309; [1997] 3 S.C.R. 586; 1997 (6) Supreme 108; [1997] 92 TAXMAN 617

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Indirect Tax; Women & Children

Keywords: Succession & Inheritance

Summary: Family & Personal - Estate Duty Act, 1953, s. 64(1) - Hindu Law Women's Rights Act, 1933, s. 8(1)(d) - Hindu undivided family property - Received on partition - Deceased having no other coparcener - Whether estate left by Manjunatha Gowda was obtained by survivorship applying s. 8 (1)(d) of Act? - Held, s. 8, cl. (d) applies to facts in this case - When joint family property passes to a single coparcener, by survivorship, it shall so pass subject to rights to share of classes of females enumerated in cl. (a) of sub-s. (1) of section 8 - Classes of females have been mentioned in cl. (a), namely, his mother, his unmarried daughters, widows and unmarried daughters of his predeceased undivided sons and brothers who have left no male issues, they are all entitled to a share with him - A Hindu undivided family and a coparcenary for purposes of taxation, under Act, as in other tax measures, like Income Tax Act, are treated as one and same concept - Therefore it is important to know whether ,Manjunatha Gowda had received property at partition between coparceners, by survivorship - Primary meaning of word 'survive' is to live beyond life or extent of, or to outlive, but it also has a secondary meaning, namely, to live after - Survivorship, therefore, is living of one of two or more persons after death of others having interest to succeed in property by succession - Shares in coparcenary property change with death or birth of other coparceners - However, in case of survivorship it is not of same incidence - He received property at partition without there being any other coparcener - It is an individual property and, therefore, he did not receive it by survivorship but by virtue of his status being a coparcener of Hindu joint family along with his father and with brothers - Under these circumstances, conclusion reached by HC that since it is by partition, not by survivorship, cl. (d) of sub-s. (1) of s. 8 does not get attracted is not correct - Thus family members enumerated u/s. 8(1)(d) are not entitled to a share in estate left by deceased - Appeal dismissed.
