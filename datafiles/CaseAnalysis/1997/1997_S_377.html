Phool Chand Gupta v State of Andhra Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

21 January 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, Sujata V. Manohar

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 2292; (1997) 2 SCC 591; AIR 1997 SC 914; JT 1997 (1) SC 700; 1997(1) SCALE 419; [1997] 1 S.C.R. 420; 1997 (104) STC 601; 1997 (2) Supreme 18

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Keywords: Subsequent Sale, Transfer Of Documents Of Title

Summary: Sales Tax - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956, ss. 3(b), 6(2), 8 and 13 - Central Sales Tax (Registration and Turnover) Rules, 1957, r. 12(1) - Central Sales Tax (Andhra Pradesh) Rules, r. 12(3)(ii) - (A) Whether appellant entitled to exemption u/s. 6(2) of Central Act? - Unless subsequent sale to a registered dealer in course of inter - State trade or commerce of goods of description referred to in s. 8(3) is shown to have been effected by transfer of documents of title to such goods, there could be no question of grant of exemption from payment of tax - Held, appellant is not entitled to exemption - (B) Whether r. 12(3)(ii) of Central Sales Tax (Andhra Pradesh) Rules is ultra vires Central Act or Central Rules? - Requirement of production of Form 'C' in addition to requirement of production of Form 'E - I' cannot be said to be inconsistent with Act or Central Rules - Further held, r. 12(3)(ii) of State rules is not ultra vires Act or Central rules - Appeal dismissed.
