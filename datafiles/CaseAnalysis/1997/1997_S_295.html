Jai Dev Gupta v State Of Himachal Pradesh And Another

Supreme Court of India

03 September 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Venkataswami, V.N. Khare

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1314; (1997) 11 SCC 13; AIR 1998 SC 2819; 1997 (77) FLR 410; JT 1997 (7) SC 650; 1998 LabIC 1400; 1998 (1) LLJ 63; 1998 (1) LLN 405; 1997(5) SCALE 697; 1999 (1) SLJ 110; 1997 (5) SLR 400; 1997 (8) Supreme 148; 1997 (3) UPLBEC 1713

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Service - Administrative Tribunals Act, s. 21 - Whether, difference in back wages should be paid from the date of appellant posting as Lecturer in Commercial Arts? - Tribunal granted relief of difference in back wages from May 1988 only on ground that u/s. 21 of Administrative Tribunals Act - Period of one year is prescribed for redressal of grievances - Tribunal fell into patent error in brushing aside question of limitation by observing that respondent has been making representations from time to time and as such limitation would not come in his way - Held, appellant is entitled to get difference in back wages only for three years - Appeal disposed of.
