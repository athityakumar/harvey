Shri Arvind Dattatraya Dhande v State of Maharashtra and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 July 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, D.P. Wadhwa

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 2693; (1997) 6 SCC 169; (1997) SCC (L&S) 1437; AIR 1997 SC 3067; 1998 (1) AWC 342; JT 1997 (6) SC 229; 1997 LabIC 2899; 1997 (4) LLN 109; 1997(4) SCALE 742; 1998 (1) SLJ 162; 1997 (4) SLR 779; [1997] Supp2 S.C.R. 231; 1998 (5) Supreme 167; 1997 (3) UPLBEC 1647

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 16 - Transfer - Excise Officer filed complaints against a contractor for selling adulterated toddy and as a consequence licence of contractor was cancelled - Reviewing authority certified performance of officer to be excellent but despite this fact officer was transferred at behest of aggrieved contractor - Whether transfer of officer was mala fide? - Held, transfer of officer was mala fide exercise of power to demoralise honest officer and was liable to be quashed - Petition allowed.
