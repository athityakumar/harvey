Bilal Ahmed Kaloo v State of Andhra Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

06 August 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, A.S. Anand

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1481; (1997) 7 SCC 431; (1997) SCC (Cr) 1094; AIR 1997 SC 3483; AIR 1997 SCW 3574; 1997 (3) CCR 76; 1997 (3) Crimes 130; 1997 CRLJ 4091; JT 1997 (7) SC 272; 1997(5) SCALE 399; [1997] Supp3 S.C.R. 327; 1997 (7) Supreme 122

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Socio-Economic

Keywords: Ballistics

Summary: Criminal - IPC, 1860, ss. 124A, 153A and 505 - Arms Act, 1959, s. 25 - (A) S.124A of IPC - Sedition - Whether charge framed against accused containing no averment that he did anything as against Govt. - (B) Promoting feeling of enmity or hatred - Proof - Publication of words or representation, whether necessary - Whether merely inciting feeling of one group without any reference to another an offence - (C) Arms Act, s. 25 - Illegal possession of arms - Proof - Revolver and cartridges alleged to be seized from possession of accused - Particulars of weapons in seizure memo tallied with weapon on examination by Ballistic Expert - No challenge to seizure memo prepared at time of recovery of weapons - Said revolver and cartridges found to be in working condition - Whether conviction of accused u/s. 25 of Arms Act proper - Held, (A) Offence u/s. 124A not made out - (B) Is necessary u/s. 505 but not u/s. 153-A - Neither attract provision of ss. 153-A nor s. 505 - (C) Conviction, proper - Appeal allowed.
