Sardar Mohar Singh Through Power of Attorney Holder, Manjit Singh v Mangilal Alias Mangtya

Supreme Court of India

15 January 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, K. Ramaswamy, S. Saghir Ahmad

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 2266; (1997) 9 SCC 217; 1997 (2) CLT 213; JT 1997 (2) SC 94; 1997 (2) MLJ 88; 1997 (2) MLJ(SC) 88; 1997 (88) RD 153; 1997(1) SCALE 611; 1997 (1) SCJ 365; [1997] 1 S.C.R. 297; 1997 (1) Supreme 846

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Limitation Act, 1963, s. 5 - Conditional decree for specific performance - Extension of time - Validity of - Whether judgment of Learned Single Judge of Madhya Pradesh High Court, made on 04-10-1996 in Civil Revision No. 394 of 1996 to extension of time for compliance is correct? - Court has discretion to extend time for compliance of conditional decree as mentioned in decree for specific performance - It is true that respondent has not given satisfactory explanation of every day's delay - It is not, unlike s. 5 of Limitation Act, 1963 an application for condonation of delay - It is one for extension of time - Under these circumstances, executing court as well as HC had exercised discretion and extended time to comply with conditional decree - There is no valid and justifiable reason to interfere with order passed by HC confirming order of executing court when in particular, HC has further enhanced a sum of Rs.16,000 to compensate petitioner for loss of enjoyment of money - Special Leave Petition dismissed.
