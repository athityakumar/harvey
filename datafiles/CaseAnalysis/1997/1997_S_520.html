K. Gopinathan Nair and Others v State of Kerala

Supreme Court of India

21 March 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Majmudar

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 3164; (1997) 10 SCC 1; AIR 1997 SC 1925; JT 1997 (4) SC 369; 1997(3) SCALE 252; [1997] 3 S.C.R. 226; 1997 (105) STC 580; 1997 (5) Supreme 420

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Trade

Summary: Trade - Constitution of India, 1950 - Import and Export (Control) Act, 1947 - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956, s. 5(2) - Kerala General Sates Tax Act, 1963 - Whether purchases of African raw cashewnuts made by assessees from Cashew Corporation of India (CCI) are in course of import and therefore, immune from liability to tax under impugned act? - Held, sale and import must have an integrated and intertwined connection - If no nexus it would not be a sale in course of import but it would be a sale by import or because of import - Court laid down the preposition to decide whether the concerned sale or purchase of goods can be deemed to take place in the course of import as laid down by s. 5(2) of the Central Sales Tax, 1956 - Direct and inseverable link between transaction of sale and import of goods - Appeals allowed.
