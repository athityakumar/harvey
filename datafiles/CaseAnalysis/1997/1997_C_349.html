Meera Company, Ludhiana v Commissioner of Income Tax

Supreme Court of India

11 March 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Sen, B.P. Jeevan Reddy, G.T. Nanavati

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 2695; (1997) 4 SCC 677; AIR 1997 SC 1973; 1997 (2) BC 550; 1997 (3) CLT 102; 1997 (139) CTR 442; [1997] 224 ITR 635; JT 1997 (3) SC 692; 1997(2) SCALE 628; [1997] 2 S.C.R. 991; 1997 (5) Supreme 219; [1997] 91 TAXMAN 219; 1997 (2) TLR 566

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Artificial Juridical Person, Corporate Body

Summary: Income Tax & Direct - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 2(31), 4 and 161 - Business continued by widow and minor children of deceased sole proprietor - Finding that organised activity jointly carried on produce income is evident - Whether income assessable as Body Of Individuals(BOI)? - Held, business was inherited and carried on by wife and minor children of deceased levy of tax would be on body so formed - Wife cannot insist that assessment should be made separately even when a joint business is carried on u/ss. 4 and 161 of 1961 Act - Appeal dismissed.
