Commissioner and Secretary To Government and Ors v C. Shanmugam

Supreme Court of India

09 December 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Venkataswami, V.N. Khare

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 371; (1998) 2 SCC 394; (1998) SCC (L&S) 562; 1998 (79) FLR 739; JT 1998 (4) SC 236; 1998 (2) LLJ 290; 1998 (3) LLN 603

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Tamil Nadu Civil Services (CCA) Rules, 1953, r. 17-B - Misconduct - Suspension - Compulsory retirement - Sustainability - Respondent was basic servant who was placed under suspension on account of his misbehavior with his superior officers and charges were framed against him u/r. 17-B of the Rules - After regular departmental enquiry, order of compulsory retirement was passed against respondent by disciplinary authority - Departmental appeal and review were not successful - Respondent moved Tribunal by filing application where Tribunal set aside order of compulsory retirement and allowed application - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether Tribunal was right in reappreciating evidence and holding that in absence of independent evidence enquiry report could not be accepted without appreciating place of incident and circumstances - Held, disciplinary authority taking lenient view and having regard to interest of family members of delinquent imposed punishment of compulsory retirement which was one of punishments that could be awarded after enquiry - Instead of compulsory retirement, if disciplinary authority had imposed either removal or dismissal order, consequences would have been more serious to delinquent - View of Tribunal that requirement of Fundamental Rules 56 should be satisfied before imposing punishment of compulsory retirement on facts of case, was not sustainable and both dealt with different situations - Under Fundamental Rules 56 even without enquiry order of compulsory retirement could be passed provided requirements were complied with - In case of imposing punishment of compulsory retirement after enquiry, requirements were totally different - Tribunal was not right in holding that requirement of Fundamental Rules 56 ought to be satisfied even for imposing punishment after full-fledged enquiry - Hence, none of grounds by Tribunal to set aside order of compulsory retirement was sustainable in law - Appeal allowed.
