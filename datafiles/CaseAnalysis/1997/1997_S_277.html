Sangar Aboina Sreena v State Of Andhra Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

23 April 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

M.K. Mukherjee, S.P. Kurdukar

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1151; (1997) 5 SCC 348; (1997) SCC (Cr) 690; AIR 1997 SC 3233; 1997 AllCriC 818; 1997 (2) CCR 45; 1997 (2) Crimes 55; 1997 CRLJ 3955; 1997 JIC 671; JT 1997 (5) SC 47; 1997(3) SCALE 611; 1997 (2) SCJ 512; [1997] 3 S.C.R. 957; 1997 (4) Supreme 214; 1997 (2) UJ 1

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - IPC, 1860, ss. 302 and 306 - Powers of Court, u/s. 222 of CrPC, 1973 - Conviction of accused of u/s. 306 despite of the fact that charge was framed u/s. 302 of the Penal Code - Legality of conviction - Held, not legal - Cannot be said so - It is true that s. 222, CrPC, 1973 entitles a Court to convict a person of an offence which is minor in comparison to the one for which he is tried but s. 306 of Penal Code cannot be said to be a minor offence in relation to an offence u/s. 302 of penal Code within the meaning of s. 222, CrPC, 1973 - Appeal allowed.
