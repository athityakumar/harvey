Satpal and Others v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

24 September 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

V.N. Khare, A.S. Anand

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 2988; (1997) 11 SCC 423; AIR 1997 SC 3882; 1997 (31) ALR 456; 1998 (32) ALR 283; 1997 (4) CLT 38; JT 1997 (8) SC 213; 1998 (89) RD 4; 1997(6) SCALE 220; 1997 (8) Supreme 296; 1997 (2) UJ 664

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, s. 4 - Land acquisition - Market value of land - Determination of - Whether High Court erroneously rejected Ex. AW 1/7 to Ex. AW 1/10 while determining market value of land acquired? - Held, there is no explanation whatsoever for such a sharp increase in price of land in such a short time - Rise in price is more than 100 per cent within a very short period which is not usual prevailing trend in rise of prices in area where acquired land is situated - Court below found that sale consideration shown in Ex. AW 1/1 to Ex. AW 1/6 is more realistic and therefore, relied on those instances for assessing market value of disputed land - HC was justified in rejecting Ex. AW 1/7 to Ex. AW 1/10 while assessing market value of claimants' acquired land - Judgment of HC is neither perverse nor illegal and does not call for any interference, since it is based on correct appreciation of evidence on record and proper application of law to established facts - Appeal dismissed.
