Union of India and Others v A. Nagamalleshwar Rao

Supreme Court of India

18 November 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

G.T. Nanavati, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 3185; (1998) SCC (L&S) 363; (1998) 1 SCC 700; AIR 1998 SC 111; 1998 (1) AWC 714; 1998 (1) CLR 188; 1998 (78) FLR 68; JT 1997 (9) SC 164; 1998 LabIC 389; 1998 (1) LLN 361; 1997(7) SCALE 34; 1998 (1) SLR 18; 1998 (6) SLT 17; [1997] Supp5 S.C.R. 169; 1997 (9) Supreme 443; 1997 (2) UJ 824; 1998 (1) UPLBEC 253

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Constitution of India, 1950, art. 311 and 309 - Central Civil Services (Conduct) Rules, 1964, r.(1) - Administrative Tribunals Act, 1985, s. 14 - Dismissal from service - Telephone operator - Disclosing wrong percentage of marks for appointment - Non production of original certificate on several demands - Inquiry - Found to have obtained much less marks than last person appointed - Held, entry of high marks could not have been made but by mistake or fraud - Dismissal from service proper - Tribunal cannot examine evidence produced before inquiry officer being Appellate Court.
