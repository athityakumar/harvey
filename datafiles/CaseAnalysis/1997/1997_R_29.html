Union Of India v R.V. Swamy @ R. Vellaichamy

Supreme Court of India

31 March 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, D.P. Wadhwa

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1128; (1997) 9 SCC 446; AIR 1997 SC 2069; JT 1997 (4) SC 579; 1997(3) SCALE 492; 1997 (2) SCJ 140; [1997] 3 S.C.R. 458; 1997 (2) SLR 577; 1997 (4) Supreme 33

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Socio Economic - Central Freedom Fighters Pension Scheme, r. 4 - Constitution, art. 226 - Proof for pension scheme for freedom fighter - Applicant not being a proclaimed offender, so no proof that he was actually sentenced - But certificates issued by prominent freedom fighters on which applicant 's claim relying on them - Govt. rejecting his claim - A matter of appreciation of evidence - HC could not direct grant of pension to applicant - Govt. directed to frame guide lines for freedom fighters issuing certificates - Appeal allowed.
