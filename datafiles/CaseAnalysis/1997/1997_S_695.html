Ashok Kumar Gupta, Vidya Sagar Gupta and Others v State of Uttar Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

21 March 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, S. Saghir Ahmad, G.B. Pattanaik

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1096; (1997) 5 SCC 201; (1997) SCC (L&S) 1299; AIR 1997 SCW 2257; 1997 (3) ESC 1549; 1997 (1) GLH 893; JT 1997 (4) SC 251; 1997 (3) LLN 19; 1997(3) SCALE 289; [1997] 3 S.C.R. 269; 1997 (2) SLR 656; 1997 (4) Supreme 450; 1997 (2) UPLBEC 1417

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Backward Classes, Affirmative Action, Equal opportunity, Equality of opportunity

Summary: Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 16(1) and 142 - (A) Whether, the prospective over-ruling of Rangachari's case, to be operative after five years from the date of Mandal's case, amounts to judicial legislation and will it be void ab initio u/art. 13(2) of the Constitution? - (B) Whether, it is violative of the fundamental rights of the appellant-petitioners and whether the exercise of the power by this Court u/arts. 32(4) and 142 of the Constitution is inconsistent with and derogatory to the fundamental rights of the appellants-petitioners and, if so, what would be the consequence? - The directions given are not violative of rights u/art. 14 r/w art. 16(1), not ultra vires the power nor void, nor incompatible to or inconsistent with the doctrine of equality enshrined u/art. 14 r/w art. 16(1) of the Constitution - On the other hand, the power was exercised by this Court u/art. 142 r/w art. 32 and the direction postponing the operation of the decision for a period of five years is a law of the land u/art. 141 - The rule of reservation in promotions was in vogue in the State of UP right from 1973 and the promotions came to be made from 1981 onwards to the respondents 2 to 10 - The U.P. Act saves the existing policy of reservation in promotions - This court upheld the previous promotions, though in excess of the roster system, as constitutional and valid - Therefore, we hold that the promotions of the respondents are legal and valid and they do not become void or unconstitutional as contended - Both, the appeal and the writ petition are accordingly dismissed
