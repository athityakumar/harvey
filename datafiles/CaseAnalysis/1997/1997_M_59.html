Employees State Insurancecorporation v M/S. Apex Engineering Pvt. Ltd.

Supreme Court of India

06 November 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Majmudar, M. Jagannadha Rao

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 2774; (1998) 1 SCC 86; (1998) SCC (L&S) 178; 1998 (1) CalLT 31; 1998 (1) CalLT(SC) 31; 1997 (77) FLR 878; JT 1997 (9) SC 54; 1998 (1) LLJ 274; 1998 (1) LLN 330; 1998 (1) MahLJ 12; 1998 (2) MLJ 33; 1998 (2) MLJ(SC) 33; 1997(6) SCALE 652; 1998 (1) SLR 78; 1998 (5) SLT 382; [1997] Supp5 S.C.R. 57; 1997 (9) Supreme 362

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Employees State insurance Corporation Act, 1948, ss. 75 and 76 - Companies Act, 1956, s. 2(12) - Factories Act, 1948, s. 2 - Apprentices Act, 1961 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Constitution - (A) Whether Managing Director could be said to be principal employer within the meaning of s. 2 (17) of the Act as he could be said to be occupier within the meaning of s. 2(15) of the Act r/w s. 2(n) of the Factories Act, 1948? - (B) Whether the Managing Director of a Co. can be said to be a servant of the Co. whose remuneration could be treated to be salary assessable to income tax - A Managing Director may have a dual capacity - He may both be a Director as well as employee, depending upon the nature of his work and the terms of his employment - Whether or not a Managing Director is a servant of the Co. apart from his being a Director can only be determined by the articles of association and the terms of his employment - Thus even assuming that Shri Dhanwate was a principal employer even though in the light of the aforesaid discussion it has to be held that he could have a dual capacity both as a Managing Director on the one hand and as an employee of the Co. on the other - Appeal allowed.
