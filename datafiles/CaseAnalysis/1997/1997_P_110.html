Ved Prakash Garg v Premi Devi and Others

Supreme Court of India

25 September 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Majmudar, V.N. Khare

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 414; (1997) 8 SCC 1; 1998 ACJ 1; AIR 1997 SC 3854; [1997] 90 Comp Cas 405; JT 1997 (8) SC 229; 1998 (1) LLJ 363; 1998 (1) MLJ(SC) 17; 1997 (3) PLR 606; [1997] Supp4 S.C.R. 250; 1997 (3) UPLBEC 2156

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Insurance - Workmen's Compensation Act, 1923, ss. 3 and 4-A(3) - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, s. 147 - Employee receives a personal injury in a motor accident - Arising out of and in course of his employment while working on motor vehicle of employer - The said vehicle has been insured against third-party accident claims under Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 - Whether Insurance Co. is liable to pay compensation in respect of claims for compensation arising out of proceedings under Workmen's Compensation Act, 1923? - Held, Insurance Co. carried matter in appeals before High Court and contended that Insurance Co. would be liable under contract of insurance - But it could not have been made liable to pay amounts of penalties with interest thereon as ordered by Workmen's Commr. as these amounts of penal nature were awarded against insured owner on account of his personal default as per s. 4-A(3) of Compensation Act, 1923 - The Insurance Co. was liable because of his own fault and negligence will have to bear entire burden of said penalty amount with proportionate interest thereon if imposed by Workmen's Commr. - Liability to pay compensation under Workmen's Compensation Act, 1923 - It is shown that workman concerned suffered personal injury, arising out of and in course of his employment - Such an accident is also covered by s. 147 of Motor Vehicles Act 1988, r/w identical provisions under very contracts of insurance which would make insurance company liable to cover all such claims for compensation for which statutory liability is imposed on employer u/s. 3 r/w s. 4-A of Compensation Act, 1923 - Once compensation falls due and within one month it is not paid by employer then as per s. 4-A(3)(a), interest at permissible rate gets added to said principal amount of compensation - No element of penalty is involved therein - It is a liability of employer to make good principal amount of compensation within permissible time limit - Appeal partly allowed.
