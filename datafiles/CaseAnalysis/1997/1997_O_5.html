New India Civil Erectors Private Limited v Oil and Natural Gas Corporation

Supreme Court of India

17 February 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, Sujata V. Manohar

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 2374; (1997) 11 SCC 75; AIR 1997 SC 980; 1997 (1) ARBLR 292; 1997 (2) CLT 141; JT 1997 (2) SC 633; 1997(2) SCALE 176; [1997] 2 S.C.R. 86; 1997 (2) Supreme 265

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration Act, 1940, s. 30 - Bombay Prohibition Act, 1949 - Validity of arbitral award - No formal construction contract - Interference sought as - (A) Arbitrator construed stipulations in schedule to tender notice and correspondence and awarded amounts - (B) Contractor's own letter and tender condition stipulating exclusion of balcony area at time of measurement - Still amount calculated by including balcony area - (C) Express stipulation between parties as to price being firm and not subject to any escalation till completion of work - Award granting escalated amount - Held, no interference is warranted - Not justified, arbitrator cannot award any amount ruled out by terms of agreement - Beyond authority of arbitrator - Appeal allowed.
