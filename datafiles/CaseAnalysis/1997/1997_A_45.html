Aditanar Educational Institution v Additional Commissioner of Income Tax

Supreme Court of India

05 February 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Paripoornan, B.P. Jeevan Reddy

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 899; (1997) 3 SCC 346; AIR 1997 SC 1436; 1997 (139) CTR 7; [1997] 224 ITR 310; JT 1997 (2) SC 284; 1997(1) SCALE 758; [1997] 1 S.C.R. 948; 1997 (3) Supreme 269; [1997] 90 TAXMAN 528; 1997 (2) TLR 419

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 10(22) - Income not included in total income - Educational society running educational institution solely for educational purpose and not for purpose of profit - Can such an institution be exempted from paying income tax under Act, 1961? - Held, u/s. 10(22) of Act availability of exemption should be calculated every year - Decisive or acid test is that institution exists solely for educational or profit purposes is also involved - One should also bear in mind distinction between corpus, objects and powers of concerned entity - Appeal dismissed.
