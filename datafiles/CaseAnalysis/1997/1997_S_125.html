Agricultural Market Committee v Shalimar Chemical Works Ltd.

Supreme Court of India

07 May 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Saghir Ahmad, K.S. Paripoornan

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 2065; (1997) 5 SCC 516; AIR 1997 SC 2502; JT 1997 (5) SC 272; 1997(4) SCALE 93; [1997] Supp1 S.C.R. 164; 1997 (4) Supreme 575

Case Digest

Subject: Agriculture & Agri. Products

Keywords: Andhra Pradesh (Agricultural Produce and Livestock) Markets Act, 1966

Summary: Indian Sale of Goods Act, 1930 - Andhra Pradesh Agricultural Produce and Live Stock Markets Act of 1966, ss. 12 and 33, Andhra Pradesh Agricultural Produce and Live Stock Market Rules 1969, r. 74(2), Bye Law 24 (5) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 245 - Validity of Bye-law (5) Explanation (2) - Whether market fee can be levied under act on sales and purchase of notified agricultural produce within notified area? - Held, Bye Law 24(5) explanation is ultra vires act and mere weighment of notified produce in notified area does not determine that sale took place in that area - Sale has taken place in State of seller and not in State of purchaser.
