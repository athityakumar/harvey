Sun Export Corporation, Bombay v Collector of Customs, Bombay and Another

Supreme Court of India

07 July 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Venkataswami, S.C. Sen, V.N. Khare

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 2592; (1997) 6 SCC 564; AIR 1997 SC 2658; 1997 (71) ECR 524; 1997 (93) E.L.T. 641; JT 1997 (5) SC 750; 1998 (21) RLT 111; 1997(4) SCALE 394; 1998 (111) STC 69; [1997] Supp1 S.C.R. 434; 1997 (7) Supreme 54

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax

Summary: Customs - Indirect Tax - Customs Tariff Act, 1975 - Central Excise Tariff Act, 1985 - Refund of countervailing duty - Appellant-Corporation imported six consignments of goods - Consignments were assessed to duty under the heading 29:01/45(17) of the 1975 Act r/w Item 68 of 1985 Act - Corporation paid the duty - Later, it claimed refund of the duty paid as countervailing duty contending inter alia that the goods imported were classifiable under item 23:01/07 as Animal Feed' and as per Notification 234/82 CE dated 1-11-1982, those goods were exempted from levy of duty - Claim was rejected on ground that the goods imported were assessable to duty under the heading 29.01/45(17) of the then prevailing First Schedule to the 1975 Act r/w Item 68 of 1985 Act and therefore, the exemption notification dt. 1-11-1982 was of no avail to the corporation - Whether the 'animal feed' supplement would fall under the Exemption Notification dt. 1-11-82.

Held, if two views possible, it is well settled, that one favourable to the assessee in matters of taxation has to be preferred. The appellant is entitled to the refund under the relevant Exemption Notification. Appeals allowed.
