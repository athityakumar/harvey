Surjit Singh and Others v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 May 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Wadhwa, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 2190; (1997) 10 SCC 592; AIR 1997 SC 2693; 1997 (77) FLR 41; JT 1997 (6) SC 32; 1997(4) SCALE 314; 1997 (4) SLR 697; [1997] Supp1 S.C.R. 382; 1997 (6) Supreme 1; 1997 (2) UJ 245

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Central Secretariat Service Rules

Summary: Service - Constitution - Central Secretariat Service Rules, 1962, r. 13 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 16 and 309 - Appointment - Vacancies in Section Officers' grade - Ratio fixed for direct recruits and promotees - Provision for carry forward of unfilled post due to non-availability of promotee or direct recruit for two years introduced by amendment in 1984 - Whether it means that preceding date of amendment Govt. has power to carry forward all unfilled vacancies to direct recruits and that all these vacancies are meant to be thrown open to promotees? - Held, cannot be understood to mean that - Appeal allowed.
