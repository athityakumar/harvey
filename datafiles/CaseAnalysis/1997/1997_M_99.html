M.V. Javali v Mahajan Borewell & Co. & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

26 September 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

M.K. Mukherjee, M. Jagannadha Rao

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 630; (1997) 8 SCC 72; (1997) SCC (Cr) 1239; AIR 1997 SC 3964; 1997 (4) CCR 131; [1998] 91 Comp Cas 708; 1997 (4) Crimes 63; 1997 (143) CTR 320; [1998] 230 ITR 1; JT 1997 (8) SC 386; 1997(6) SCALE 343; 1998 (6) SLT 80; [1997] Supp4 S.C.R. 320; 1997 (8) Supreme 471; [1997] 95 TAXMAN 306; 1997 (2) TLR 1061

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax and Direct Taxes - Interpretation of Statutes - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 276B and 278B - Gold Control Act, 1968 - Punishment for failure to pay Income Tax deducted at source ( TDS ) - Mandatory sentence of imprisonment and fine provided under 1961 Act - Interpretation of s. 276-B of the Act - Because offender is a Company - Held, only sentence of fine could be imposed - Harmonious construction to s. 276-B has t o be given - Appeal allowed.
