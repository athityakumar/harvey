R. M. Arunachalam v Commissioner of Income Tax

Supreme Court of India

09 July 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Agrawal, D.P. Wadhwa

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 2203; (1997) 7 SCC 698; AIR 1997 SC 2905; 1997 (141) CTR 348; [1997] 227 ITR 222; JT 1997 (6) SC 431; 1997(4) SCALE 608; [1997] Supp2 S.C.R. 64; 1997 (7) Supreme 207; [1997] 93 TAXMAN 423; 1997 (2) TLR 754

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Claim, Permissibility, A/ys 1966-67, 1970-71, 1971-72, Deduction in income

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income-tax Act, 1961, s.48 - Cost of Acquisition - Deduction - Capital Gains - Estate Duty - Asseessee/appellant paid estate duty of A and B from whom he acquired property and claimed cost of deduction which was not allowed by revenue/respondent - HC on appeal found in favor of revenue - Hence, instant appeal - Whether estate duty paid in respect of estate of A and B to the extent such duty related to assets in question can be claimed as a deduction as cost of acquisition or as cost of improvement - Held, no - Estate duty paid by assessee/ appellant should not be treated as cost of acquisition to previous owner and, therefore could not qualify for deduction as cost of acquisition of asset - Further, it could not also be treated as cost of improvement thereto as expression thereto would appear to cover a case where amount in expended on asset itself - Assessee could not claim deduction of amount paid by him to discharge a mortgage on assets and cost of improvement of asset sold u/s. 48 of the Act - Appeals dismissed.
