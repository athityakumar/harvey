State of Rajasthan and Another v Prem Raj

Supreme Court of India

14 February 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 2372; (1997) 10 SCC 317; (1997) SCC (L&S) 1688; AIR 1997 SC 1081; JT 1997 (2) SC 546; 1997 LabIC 1295; 1997(2) SCALE 75; [1997] 2 S.C.R. 60; 1997 (1) SLR 691; 1997 (3) Supreme 566; 1997 (1) UJ 466

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Keywords: Computation Of Pension

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 16 and 309 - Rajasthan Service Rules, 1951, rr. 250 C, 255 A and 256 B - Liberalised pension scheme - (A) Decision in previously decided D.S. Nakara case (1982 INDLAW SC 158) has not been completely accepted in subsequent decisions of Supreme Court - (B) Amended pension Rules fixing cut off dates for applicability - Challenged as arbitrary by pre 1979 retiree - Govt. in meantime introduced revised pension formula - Formula extended to pre-1979 retirees by notification - Striking earlier liberalisation order (amendments to rules in 1971, 1974) without consideration notification, extending revised pension formula to pre 1979 retiress - If improper - Held, extent of acceptability of D.S. Nakara's case has been explained in later decisions indicated - Proper and, therefore, validity need not be decided - Appeal allowed.
