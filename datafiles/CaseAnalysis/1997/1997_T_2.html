Thiagarajar Charities v Additional Commissioner of Income Tax and Another

Supreme Court of India

24 April 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Paripoornan, B.N. Kirpal, K. Venkataswami

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 3258; (1997) 4 SCC 724; AIR 1997 SC 2541; 1997 (140) CTR 295; [1997] 225 ITR 1010; JT 1997 (4) SC 728; 1997(3) SCALE 337; [1997] 3 S.C.R. 965; 1998 (1) SLJ 217; 1997 (2) SLR 512; 1997 (3) Supreme 552; [1997] 92 TAXMAN 152; 1997 (2) TLR 706

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Charitable or Religious Purposes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 11 and 2(15) - Whether appellant - assessee - trust is entitled to exemption u/s. 11 r/w s. 2(15)? - Held, main objects of trust as stated in Trust Deed are education, medical relief and poor relief - Business so started was held under trust - Business is only a 'means' of achieving 'object' of trust - Facts available in records go to show that profits or amounts earned in business fed charitable purposes specified in trust deed - It cannot be said that trust is carrying on (business) an activity for profit - Answered in favour of assessee.
