Union of India and Others v Major A. Hussain

Supreme Court of India

08 December 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Wadhwa, Sujata V. Manohar

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1439; (1998) SCC (Cr) 437; (1998) 1 SCC 537; AIR 1998 SC 577; JT 1997 (9) SC 676; 1998 (1) RCR(Criminal) 300; 1997(7) SCALE 477; 1998 (1) SLR 343; 1998 (1) SLT 298; [1997] Supp6 S.C.R. 218; 1997 (10) Supreme 377; 1998 (1) UPLBEC 330

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Defence & Security Forces; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Keywords: Court Martial

Summary: Defence & Security Forces - Criminal - Constitution - Army Act, ss. 118 and 191 - Army Rules, 1954, rr. 149 and 184 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 226 and 227 - (A) Powers of court - General court - martial under Army Act - Reasonable opportunity to defend - Denial - Petition against - Finding by High Court in a petition, that respondent was denied reasonable opportunity to defend himself - Whether examination of record of court martial minutely by HC permissible? - (B) General court martial - Providing reasonable opportunity to accused to defend - Evidence showing no irregularity or illegality - Whether conviction and sentence liable to be set aside? - (C) General court - Whether accused is entitled to copy of report of court of enquiry? - Held, (A) Examination of record of court martial minutely by HC as if it was sitting in appeal not permissible - (B) Setting aside conviction and sentence - Illegal - (C) R. 184 does not postulate that accused is entitled to copy of report of court of enquiry - Appeal allowed.
