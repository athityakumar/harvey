Messrs Kanoria Chemicals and Industries Limited v U.P. State Electricity Board And Others

Supreme Court of India

10 March 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, K.S. Paripoornan

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 2560; (1997) 5 SCC 772; 1997 (3) AWC 1433; 1997 (2) CLT 191; JT 1997 (3) SC 545; 1997(2) SCALE 719; 1997 (1) SCJ 666; [1997] 2 S.C.R. 844; 1997 (3) Supreme 229; 1997 (1) UJ 620

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Electricity & Energy

Keywords: Revised electricity rates, Non-payment of additional charges, Admissibility

Summary: Electricity & Energy - Administrative - Electricity (Supply) Act, 1948, s. 49 - Revised electricity rates - Non-payment of additional charges - Admissibility - Respondent issued notification dt.21-4-1990, revised electricity rates/tariffs u/s. 49 of the Act - Notification inter alia provided for payment of interest in case bill amount was not paid within specified period - Appellant alleged that, it deposited difference amount between pre-revised and revised electricity rates - It did not deposited 'additional charges'/ 'late payment surcharge' - Respondent issued demand notice - Appellants filed writ petition questioning the notices demanding late payment surcharge u/cl. 7(b) of notification - HC by order dt. 25-7-1990 stayed operation of notification issued by respondent - No late payment surcharge could be levied on amount withheld by appellants - Appellant contended that where the operation of very notification was stayed which meant that from date of stay order, cl. 7(b) of notification did not operate and was not effective till dismissal of writ petitions - HC opined that consumers were liable to pay late payment surcharge u/cl. 7(b) of notification even for period covered by aforementioned order dt. 25-7-1990 - Hence, instant appeal - Whether HC was justified in passing the impugned order - Held, petitioner might ask for injunction, another might ask for stay of demand notice, third on might ask for stay of collection of amount demanded and fourth one might ask for stay of very notification - Such distinctions were bound to occur where a large number of writ petitions were filed challenging same notification - Interim orders made by Court might also vary in their phraseology in such a situation - Take this very case while consumers had asked for stay of operation of Govt. Order revising rates, those very consumers asked for an injunction at SC - Furthermore, as pointed out rightly by HC, order of stay granted by HC in writ petitions questioning validity of notification dt. 21-4-1990 were not uniform - It was not agreed that rate of late payment surcharge provided by cl. 7(b) of notification wass penal, but having regard to particular facts and circumstances of instant case and having regard to fact that petitioners would possibly have understood decision in Adoni Ginning as relieving them of their obligation to pay interest/late payment surcharge for period of stay - It reduced rate of late payment surcharge payable u/cl. 7(b) of notification to eighteen percent - However, direction was confined only to period covered by stay orders in writ petitions filed challenging notification dt. 21-4-1990 and limited to 1-3-1993 on those writ petitions were dismissed - Appeal dismissed.
