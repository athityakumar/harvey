State of Rajasthan v R. Dayal and Others

Supreme Court of India

17 February 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

G.T. Nanavati, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 3053; (1997) 10 SCC 419; (1997) SCC (L&S) 1631; JT 1997 (3) SC 198; 1997(2) SCALE 341; [1997] 2 S.C.R. 108; 1998 (1) SLJ 119; 1997 (2) SLR 68; 1997 (3) Supreme 333; 1997 (1) UJ 479

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Service - Rajasthan Service of Engineers (Building and Roads Branch) Rules, 1954, r. 24 A - Selection - Validity - (A) Whether appointment of respondents, is made in accordance with Rules? - Determination of vacancies is required to be done u/r. 9 and selection has to be made in accordance with criteria prescribed u/r. 23 - Procedure and criteria prescribed by r. 23-A was also to be followed - Held, while selecting officers, minimum requisite qualifications and experience for promotion specified in relevant column, should be taken into consideration against vacancies existing as on 1st April of year of selection - But since Rules came to be amended and amendment became effective with immediate effect and cl. (11-B) of r. 24 A indicates that options have been given to Govt. or Appointing Authority, as case may be, to revise select list as existing as per law as on date of appointment or as may be directed by a competent court, selection is required to be made by concerned DPC accordingly - An appointment made, after selection as per procedure, to vacancies existing prior to amendment, is valid - (B) Whether selection would be made, in case of appointment to vacancies which admittedly arose after amendment of Rules came into force, according to amended Rules or in terms of r. 9 r/w rr. 23 and 24-A? - Further held, vacancies that arose subsequent to amendment of Rules are required to be filled in in accordance with law existing as on date when vacancies arose - Selection came to be made prior to amendment of Rules in accordance with law then existing since anticipated vacancies also must have been taken into consideration in light of r. 9 - But after amended Rules came into force, necessarily amended Rules would be required to be applied for and given effect to - Appeal disposed of.
