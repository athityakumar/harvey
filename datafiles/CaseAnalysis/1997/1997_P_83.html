States of Rajasthan v Prakash Chand and Others

Supreme Court of India

02 December 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

A.S. Anand, M.K. Mukherjee, K. Venkataswami

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1015; (1998) 1 SCC 1; AIR 1998 SC 1344; AIR 1998 SCW 1219; 1998 (1) CCR 88; 1998 CRLJ 2012; 1998 (2) G.L.R. 1149; JT 1997 (9) SC 492; 1998 (2) KLT 22; 1998 (1) RCR(Criminal) 322; 1997(7) SCALE 411; 1998 (2) SLT 39; [1997] Supp6 S.C.R. 1; 1997 (10) Supreme 122

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Constitution

Keywords: Rajasthan High Court Ordinance, 1949, High Court Judges Travelling Allowances Rules, 1956

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Practice & Procedure - Contempt proceeding against chief justice - Sustainability - Petitioner filed a petition with regard to allocation of accommodation to HC Judges - Matter was in Single Judge bench, however, it was placed before the DB since it involved constitutional questions - DB dismissed writ petition as infructuous - Subsequently, criminal revision petition was filed by respondent No.1 challenging his conviction and sentence for an offence u/s. 304 A of IPC - Single Judge directed Registry to list those cases 'on a separate board' - Since, Registry could not create a 'separate board' for said Single Judge without obtaining directions from Chief Justice, matter was placed before Chief Justice - Chief Justice directed that there would be no roster for Single Judge for sitting in Single Bench on 5-9-1997, those part heard matters should be listed on some other day sometime next week as the business of Court would permit with his specific order - However, while still sitting in DB on a mention made by advocate for revision petitioner, single Judge directed that Criminal Revision along with 'other part-heard cases' should be listed before him' on a separate board on 9-9-97, knowing fully well that on that date also he was to continue to sit in DB and that no cases could be listed before him without appropriate directions of Chief Justice - Registry could not act on the directions of Single Judge and therefore Registry once again sought directions of Chief Justice - Chief Justice directed that criminal revision petition and 'other part-heard cases' be listed before him on a separate board - However while hearing preliminary arguments in criminal revision petition filed by respondent no. 1 for admission and bail, Single Judge and in a detailed order, caustic comments and observations were made regarding ( and unrelated to) that writ petition and an exception was taken to its disposal by DB - Single Judge opined that Chief Justice had prima facie committed criminal contempt of court - Respondent No.2 Rajasthan HC called in question the notice directed to be issued to him to show cause why contempt proceedings be not initiated against him - Whether transferring a partly heard matter from Single Judge bench to DB bench by Chief Justice would constitute a contempt of court of Single Judge - Held, administrative control of HC vested in Chief Justice alone - Chief Justice was only the first amongst the equals - Chief Justice was master of the roster, he alone had prerogative to constitute benches of Court and allocate cases to the benches so constituted - Puisne Judges could only do that work as was allotted to them by Chief Justice or under his directions - Chief Justice could take cognizance of an application laid before him u/r. 55 of the Rules and refer a case to the larger bench for its disposal and could exercise this jurisdiction even in relation to a part-heard case - Further, puisne judges could not 'pick and Choose' any case pending in HC and assign the same to himself or themselves for disposal without appropriate orders of Chief Justice - No Judge or judges could give directions to the Registry for listing any case before him or them which would run counter to the directions given by Chief Justice - In instant case, Singe Judge had no authority or jurisdiction to send for record of the disposed of writ petition and made comments on the manner of transfer of writ petition to DB or on merits of that writ petition - All comments, observations and findings recorded by Single Judge in relation to the disposed of writ petition were not only unjustified and unwarranted but also without jurisdiction and make Judge coram-non-judice - 'Allegations' and 'comments' made by Single Judge against Chief Justice of HC, Advocate of petitioner in the writ petition and Judges constituting DB which disposed of writ petition were uncalled for, baseless and without any legal sanction - Finding recorded by Single Judge against Chief Justice that he had 'illegally' drawn a daily allowance of Rs. 250/- while sitting at jaipur and had thereby committed 'criminal' misappropriation of public funds' lacked procedural propriety, factual accuracy and legal authenticity - Finding was wholly incorrect and legally unsound and made motive of author not above personal pique so wholly taking away dignity of judicial process - Disparaging and derogatory comments made in most intemperate language in order under appeal did not credit to high office of HC Judge - Direction of Single Judge to issue notice to Chief Justice of HC to show cause why contempt proceedings be not initiated against him, for transferring a part-heard writ petition from his bench to DB for disposal, was not only subversive of judicial discipline and illegal but was also wholly misconceived and without jurisdiction - Hence, all observations, comments, insinuations, allegations and orders made by Single Judge in connection with and relating to disposed of writ petition in impugned order, were illegal, misconceived and without jurisdiction and same were quashed - Further, direction to issue show cause notice to Chief Justice of HC Respondent No.2, being wholly unwarranted, unjustified and legally unsustainable was quashed and set aside - Appeal allowed.
