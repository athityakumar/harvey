Harinarayan Srivastav v United Commercial Bank and Another

Supreme Court of India

31 March 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, D.P. Wadhwa

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 466; (1997) 4 SCC 384; (1997) SCC (L&S) 965; AIR 1997 SC 3658; 1997 (2) CLT 341; 1997 (76) FLR 268; JT 1997 (4) SC 595; 1997 LabIC 2076; 1997 (2) LLJ 620; 1997 (3) LLN 56; 1997 LLR 497; 1997(3) SCALE 536; 1997 (2) SCJ 122; [1997] 3 S.C.R. 465; 1997 (2) SLR 574; 1997 (3) Supreme 739

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14 and 16 - Misconduct - Charge sheet - Domestic enquiry - Permission to engage an advocate - Not obligatory on bank as per r. 19.12 of bi-partite settlement - Allegations in charge sheet not complicated but are very simple - Failure to permit employee to engage an advocate - Whether violative of principles of natural justice? - Held, not violative of principles of natural justice - Petition dismissed.
