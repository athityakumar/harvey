Narhari Shivram Shet Narvekar v Pannalal Umediram

Supreme Court of India

16 January 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, R.S. Sarkaria

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 337; (1976) 3 SCC 203; AIR 1977 SC 164; [1976] 3 S.C.R. 149

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Civil Procedure; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Ex Parte Decree

Summary: Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908, ss. 13, 38 and 39, o. 41, r. 33 - Can the money decree passed by Bombay High Court can be sent to Goa court for execution - Held, decree passed by Bombay HC is not a nullity - The right of judgment-debtor to pay up decree passed against him cannot be said to be a vested right - Decree passed by Bombay HC having been passed by a Court of competent jurisdiction and not being a nullity because judgment-debtor had appeared and participated in proceedings of Court to some extent.
