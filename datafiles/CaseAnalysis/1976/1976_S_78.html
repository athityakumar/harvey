Sridhar Suar & Anr. v Shri Jagan Nath Temple & Others

Supreme Court of India

21 April 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

Jaswant Singh, A.N. Ray, M. Hameedullah Beg

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 148; (1976) 3 SCC 485; AIR 1976 SC 1860; 1976 RCR(Rent) 660; [1976] Supp S.C.R. 101

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 105 - Hindu Law - (A) Whether 'Sanand' executed by Shebait is lease or licence - (B) Religious endowments - (1) Permanent-alienation of temple debutter property by shebait - Permissibility - (2) Powers of mohunt - Validity of permanent lease for fixed rent - Sanand' could not be held to be any more than a licence and could not clothe the grantee with the status of a lessee - Permissible only in a case of a need or for the benefit of the estate - Apart from unavoidable necessity to create a new and fixed rent for all time, though adequate at the time, in lieu of giving the endowment the benefit of an augmentation of a variable rent from time to time would be a breach of duty in the mohunt - Appeal Allowed.
