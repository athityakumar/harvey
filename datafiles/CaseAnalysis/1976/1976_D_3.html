D. R. Venkatachalam and Others v Dy. Transport Commissioner and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 December 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

V.R. Krishna Iyer, A.N. Ray, M. Hameedullah Beg

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 425; (1977) 2 SCC 273; AIR 1977 SC 842; 1977 (2) MLJ(SC) 6; [1977] 2 S.C.R. 392

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Motor Vehicles Act, 1939, ss. 47(1), Proviso and 68 - T.N. Motor Vehicles Rules, 1940 - Prohibition Act - (A) Interpretation of Statutes - Public Law - Rule of construction - (B) Whether r. 155A(3)(D)(i) of the Rules is obnoxious to public interest, and if contrary to Proviso to s. 47(1) of 1939 Act - Public law, in the piebald economy and pluralist society, responds to societal challenges and constitutional changes and to miss the ideological thrust of the Constitution and the economic orientation of the nation while construing legislation relating to public law and scanning them for their validity is to fail in understanding the social philosophy that puts life and meaning into the provisions of the Act - Held, rule is valid - Appeal Dismissed.
