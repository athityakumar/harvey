Tea Estate India Private Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax, West Bengal Ii

Supreme Court of India

26 April 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

Hans Raj Khanna, P.K. Goswami

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 306; (1976) 4 SCC 446; AIR 1976 SC 1790; 1976 CTR 256; [1976] 203 ITR 785; [1976] Supp S.C.R. 145

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922 - Accumulated profits - Agricultural income - Dividend - Taxable - Assessee company held shares in DDT Co. and TT Co. - Companies sold their tea estates including all its assets - As a result of sales, companies received surplus over book value of assets - Companies went into voluntary liquidation - Assessee became entitled to receive total distributable assets of companies - Assessee contended that apart from capital gain u/s. 12B of the Act no other amount was to be included in computation of accumulated profits under s. 2(6A)(c) of the Act - ITO rejected contention - AAC allowed further deduction and rejected all other contentions - Tribunal held that balance left over after making deduction from total distributable pool was accumulated profits of companies and share received by assessee on distribution of such accumulated profits was dividend within meaning of s. 2(6A)(c) of the Act - HC held to be wholly includible in accumulated profits within meaning of s. 2(6A)(c) of the Act - Whether balances in accounts were includable in accumulated profits within meaning of s. 2(6A)(c) of the Act and if so to what extent - Held, amount distributed to assessee company which was attributable to accumulated profits would constitute dividend in hands of assessee and whole amount so received would be liable to be taxed - Income which was realised by sale of tea by company which grew tea on its land and subjected it to manufacturing process in factory was integrated income - Sixty per cent of income was taken to be agricultural income and forty per cent represented non-agricultural income - Forty per cent of income was to be treated as income liable to income tax - Sixty per cent of income was deemed to be agricultural income within meaning of expression in the Act and distribution of such amount on liquidation of companies did not take character of dividend - Capital gains was not liable to be taxed as dividend u/s. 12 of the Act - SC decision in Anglo-American Direct Tea Trading Company Limited v Commissioner of Agricultural Income Tax, Kerala, 1968 Indlaw SC 258, followed - SC decision in Karimtharuvi Tea Estates Limited, Kottayam and Another v State of Kerala and Others, 1962 Indlaw SC 474, followed - SC decision in Bacha F. Guzdar v Commissioner of Income Tax, Bombay, 1954 Indlaw SC 8, followed - Appeals dismissed.
