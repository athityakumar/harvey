Mannalal Khetan and Others v Kedar Nath Khetan and Others

Supreme Court of India

25 November 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Ray, M. Hameedullah Beg, Jaswant Singh

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 435; (1977) 2 SCC 424; AIR 1977 SC 536; [1977] 47 Comp Cas 185; [1977] 2 S.C.R. 190; 1977 TAXLR 1638; 1977 TLR 1638; 1976 UJ 1017

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Corporate; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Corporate - Municipalities & Local Governments - Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Whether a company having been served with notice to attachment of shares register transfer of shares in contravention of the order of attachment? - A separate prohibitory order was issued to the company in Form 18 of the First Schedule of the Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Therefore, the company by registering the transfer of shares was obviously permitting the transfer and such action on the part of the company being in violation of the prohibition is contrary to law - Shares which had not been attached but had been surrendered to the Receiver appointed by the Collector of Bombay came from the possession of the Receiver in the partnership suit - The Receiver in the partnership suit took possession of the shares along with blank transfer forms in the year 1953 - When the Receiver held the scrips and the transfer forms it was not open to the persons in whose names the shares originally stood to exercise rights of ownership in respect thereof or to transfer their ownership to anyone else - Impugned order is set aside
