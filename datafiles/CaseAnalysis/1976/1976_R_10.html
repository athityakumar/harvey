Commissioner Of Income-Tax, Madras v R.M. Chidambaram Pillai Etc.

Supreme Court of India

17 November 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

V.R. Krishna Iyer, Hans Raj Khanna

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 272; (1977) SCC (Tax) 188; (1977) 1 SCC 431; AIR 1977 SC 489; 1977 (6) CTR 71; [1977] 106 ITR 292; 1977 (1) MLJ 50; 1977 (1) MLJ(SC) 50; [1977] 2 S.C.R. 111; 1977 TAXLR 214

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 10(4)(b) and 16(1)(b) - Income Tax Rules, 1922, r. 24 - Constitution, art. 141 - CPC, 1908, s. 35 - (A) Tea estates - Firm deriving agricultural as well as business income - Taxability of salary received by partner for services rendered to firm - Taxability - (B) Declaration of law u/art. 141 of Constitution - Rule of Supreme Court - (C) Costs - Clarification of confused juridical situation by court - If parties are fair, costs of litigation must come out of national exchequer and not out of party's purse - Held 40 per cent, of salary can only be taken in computation of total income of the partner - When SC, as the apex adjudicator declaring the law for the country and invested with constitutional credentials u/art. 141 clarifies a confused juridical situation, its substantial role is of legal mentor of the nation - In the instant case both parties were directed to bear their costs throughout - Appeal Dismissed.
