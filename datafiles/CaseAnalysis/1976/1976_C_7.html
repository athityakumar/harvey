State of Maharashtra v Central Provinces Manganese Ore Company Limited

Supreme Court of India

29 October 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Hameedullah Beg, A.N. Ray, P.N. Shingal

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 353; (1977) SCC (Tax) 211; (1977) 1 SCC 643; AIR 1977 SC 879; 1977 (6) CTR 56; [1977] 1 S.C.R. 1002; 1977 (39) STC 340; 1977 TAXLR 1861

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Indian Sale Of Goods Act, 1930

Summary: Constitution, arts. 245, 246 and 286 - Indian Sale of Goods Act, 1930 - C.P. and Berar Sales Tax Act, 1947, s. 2(g), expln. II - (A) Provision of art. 286 - No retrospective operation - Assessment under 1947 Act - For period prior to commencement of Constitution - If violative of art. 286 - (B) Effect of unconstitutionality of the substituted expln. II, s.2(g) - (C) Words and Phrases - 'Manufacture' - Mere giving of a new name to product - (D) Whow repeal of legislative provision and executive instruction is effected - Although assessment order was made after the commencement, art.286, held, was not contravened - Law as it stood before amendment is left unaltered - The mere giving of a new name by the seller to what is really the same product is not the 'manufacture' of a new product - Held, repeal as well as displacement are capable of being achieved and inferred from a bare issue of fresh instructions on the same subject - Appeals dismissed.
