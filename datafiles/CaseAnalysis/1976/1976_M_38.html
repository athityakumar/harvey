Mayenghoan Rahamohan Singh v The Chief Commissioner (Admn.) Manipur Andothers

Supreme Court of India

01 November 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Ray, M. Hameedullah Beg, P.N. Shingal

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 203; (1976) 4 SCC 709; (1977) SCC (L&S) 32; AIR 1976 SC 2581; 1976 LabIC 173; [1977] 1 S.C.R. 1022; 1977 SLJ 65; 1977 (1) SLR 234; 1976 UJ 950

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Fundamental Rules, r. 56(j) - Constitution, arts. 309 and 311 - Govt. decision No. 23 below r. 56 ( Compulsory retirement without assigning reasons ) - Decision No. 23 substituted by r. 56 (i) on 21-07-1965 (providing for compulsory retirement in public interest ) - Notice of retirement served on 30-06-1966 under Decision No. 23 i.e. when Decision was not subsisting - Validity of order of retirement - Held, compulsory retirement is not a punishment - There is no stigma in compulsory retirement - Whether the order was correct or not is not to be gone into by the Court - Appeal Dismissed.
