D. Venkata Reddy v R. Sultan & Others

Supreme Court of India

24 February 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, P.K. Goswami

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 104; (1976) 2 SCC 455; AIR 1976 SC 1599; [1976] 3 S.C.R. 445

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Elections - Representation of People Act, 1951 ss.116-A, 123 - Corrupt practices - Whether order of Single Judge declaring election of appellant void on grounds of indulging in a large number of corrupt practices as envisaged by s. 123 of Act, thereby setting aside same is justified? - Considering entire evidence and circumstances of case, by applying standard of proof by virtue of benefit of doubt or that of preponderance of probabilities conclusion is inescapable that respondent has failed to prove his allegations regarding payment of bribe and distribution of pamphlet by appellant - Contesting respondent has not adduced any satisfactory evidence on this point whereas appellant has through his evidence shown that probabilities were that appellant had not distributed this pamphlet - Learned Judge overlooked certain fundamental features, inherent improbabilities, intrinsic infirmities, weak and interested nature of evidence and other facts - Held, appellant was not guilty of any corrupt practices - Further held, order of single Judge declaring election of appellant void and setting aside same is quashed - Appeal allowed.
