I.N. Subba Reddy v Andhra University and Others

Supreme Court of India

05 April 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

Jaswant Singh, A.N. Ray, M. Hameedullah Beg

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 152; (1977) SCC (L&S) 178; (1977) 1 SCC 554; AIR 1976 SC 2049; [1976] 3 S.C.R. 1013

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Implementation of resolution, Validity of resolution

Summary: Service - Andhra University Act 1926, s. 19(c) (iii) - Implementation of resolution - Termination - Validity of resolution - Appellant Professor of Anthropology entered into agreement with university as required by conditions of service and s. 24 of Chap. 5 of the Administration Manual of University - Appellant was confirmed as Professor of Anthropology w.e.f. 1-10-1968 - Syndicate passed a resolution dt.28-10-1973 determining aforesaid agreement which appellant had entered into with University on his promotion and appointment as Professor of Anthropology - Intimation of determination of agreement was communicated to appellant - Appellant filed suit before Musiff Court questioning the validity of resolution dt.28-10-1973 of syndicate - Munsiff granted the ex-parte temporary injunction, restraining respondents from implementing resolution passed by Syndicate - Respondents filed appeal before District Court, which was dismissed - Respondents filed revision before HC, which was allowed and vacated the injunction - Appellant filed petition before DB, challenging the validity of resolution of syndicate and vires of s. 24 of Chap. V of Administration Manual - Appellant alleged that action of the syndicate terminating his services was mala fide, and that it was with ulterior object of circumventing procedure prescribed by ss. 9 and 11 of Chap. 29 of the University Code - DB dismissed appeal holding that impugned action had neither taken as a measure of punishment for any misconduct on part of appellant nor it involved a breach of any mandatory statutory obligation or any principle of natural justice - Hence, instant appeal - Whether DB was justified in passing the impugned order - Held, Syndicate was invested with untrammelled power to define terms and conditions of service of teachers of University - S. 24 of Chap. V of the Administration Manual being undoubtedly a condition of service of University teachers, however, it was unable to understand how it was ultra vires the powers of Syndicate - Case of termination of service simpliciter with out attaching any stigma which was governed by conditions of service specified in contract of employment which Syndicate was empowered to lay down u/s. 19(c) (iii) of the Act - Terms and conditions of service specified in contract of employment entered into between appellant and University u/s. 34 of the Act contained an express provision for termination of his services by 6 months notice on either side - DB was justified in passing the impugned order - Appeal dismissed.
