Seth Beni Chand (Since Dead) Now By L.Rs v Smt. Kamla Kunwar and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 September 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.V. Chandrachud, V.R. Krishna Iyer, N.L. Untwalia

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 189; (1976) 4 SCC 554; AIR 1977 SC 63; 1977 HLR 1; [1977] 1 S.C.R. 578

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Family & Personal; Women & Children

Keywords: Succession & Inheritance

Summary: Civil Procedure - Family & Personal - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Will - Execution - Validity - Testrarix/deceased executed a Will by bequeathing her property in respondents and disinheriting appellant and his progeny - Respondent filed petition in HC for probate of the Will - HC dismissed the petition on the ground that the propounder of the will had failed to explain the suspicious circumstances surrounding the execution of Will - On appeal DB reversed the judgment of Single Judge and upheld the validity of Will - Hence instant appeal - Whether the execution of the Will by Testratix was proved satisfactorily - Held, execution of the will were shrouded in suspicion, it was the duty and the function of propounder to remove that suspicion by leading satisfactory evidence - Testatrix was advanced in age being past eighty years of age, the Will contains provisions which were prima facie unnatural since the only son was disinherited under it and the testatrix died five days after making the Will - There could be no dispute that these were gravely suspicious circumstances - But the propounder had, in SC's opinion, offered an explanation of these circumstances which ought to satisfy a prudent mind - Ultimately, that was the test to adopt for one could not insist on mathematical proof even where the circumstances attendant on execution of the Will raise a suspicion as regards its due execution - Burden in testamentary cases was of a different order than in other cases in the sense that an attesting witness should be called, wherever possible, to prove execution, propounder should remove the suspicion, if any, attaching to the execution of the Will and if there be any doubt regarding the due execution, he should satisfy conscience of the Court that the testator had a sound and disposing state of mind and memory when he made the Will - Property included in the Will was for the time being in the possession of a Receiver appointed by the Court - Since Court had upheld the Will, the Receiver should have to hand over the property to the executrix/respondent No.1 to instant appeal - SC directed receiver should continue in possession Of the property for a period of 4 months from today and hand it over to respondent No. 1 on the expiry of that period - Appeal dismissed.
