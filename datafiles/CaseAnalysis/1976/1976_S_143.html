Sharda Prasad Sinha v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

08 December 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Bhagwati, A.C. Gupta, P.N. Shingal

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 238; (1977) SCC (Cr) 132; (1977) 1 SCC 505; AIR 1977 SC 1754; 1977 CRLJ 1146; 1977 CrLR(SC) 337; [1977] 2 S.C.R. 357

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Orissa Excise Rules

Summary: Excise - Indirect Tax - Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code Of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 482 - Bihar And Orissa Excise Act, 1915, ss. 25(2), 54(1)(a), 57(c) - Orissa Excise Rules - Charges of offences under s. 54(1)(a) and s. 57(c) of Bihar and Orissa Excise Act, 1915 - Ingredients - Cognizance taken by Sub-Divisional Magistrate, Patna - Application under s. 482, CrPC, for quashing rejected by High Court - Scope of s. 482, CrPC, 1973 - Held, allegations in complaint or charge-sheet not constituting any offence - No offence made out either under s. 54(1)(a) r/w s. 25(2) or s. 57 (c) of Bihar and Orissa Excise Act 1915 - Order taking cognizance can be quashed by HC in exercise of inherent powers - Appeal allowed, order of HC set aside and proceedings pending before Executive Magistrate quashed - Appeal allowed.
