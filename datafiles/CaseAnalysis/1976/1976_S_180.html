State of Kerala v General Manager, Southern Railway, Madras

Supreme Court of India

30 August 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

Hans Raj Khanna, N.L. Untwalia, Jaswant Singh

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 373; (1976) 4 SCC 265; AIR 1976 SC 2538; 1977 (1) MLJ(SC) 60; [1977] 1 S.C.R. 419; 1976 UJ 750

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Keywords: Railway Act 1890

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, ss. 77 and 80 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 31(a) and 131 - Indian Railway Act, 1890, ss. 3 (6), 72, 74, 76 and 80 - Whether suit against railway administration was not maintainable as UOI had not been impleaded as a defendant to suit? - Held, yes - The definition of 'railway administration' as given in s. 3(6) is comprehensive and deals with both types of railways. i.e., railways administered by the Govt. as well as those administered by railway companies - The words 'railway administration' have been used in ss. 72 to 80 because those sections pertain to rights and liabilities of the parties in both types of cases, that is, cases where liability is incurred by Govt. administered railways as well as cases in which liability is incurred by railway administered by railway company - The Act, however, does not deal with, the question as to who should be impleaded as a defendant when a suit is brought against the railway administration - This is essentially a matter relating to the frame of suits, and is dealt with by the CPC, 1908 - According to s. 79 of the Code, in a suit by or against the Govt., the authority to be named as plaintiff or defendant, as the case may be, shall be (a) in the case of a suit by or against the Central Govt., the Union of India, and (b) in the case of a suit by or against a State Govt., the State - This section is in accordance with art. 300 of the Constitution of India, 1950 according to which the Govt. of India may sue or be sued by the name of the UOI and the Govt. of a State may sue or be sued by the name of the State - Further, Act makes provision for liability of railway administration, but from that it does not follow that railway administration is a separate legal entity having a juristic personality capable of being sued as such - There is distinction between owner of railway, namely, UOI, and authority which actually runs railway and to whom duties have been assigned for this purpose by Act - Manager of railway is such authority - When, however, liability is sought to be fastened on railway administration and a suit is brought against it on that account, suit would have to be brought against UOI because it is Union who owns railway and who would have funds to satisfy claim in case decree is awarded in such suit - A suit against a State railway should be brought against Govt. - Appeals dismissed.
