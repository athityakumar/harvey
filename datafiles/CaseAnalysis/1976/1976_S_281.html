Municipal Corporation Of City Of Hubli v Subha Rao Hanumatharao Prayag & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

24 March 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Bhagwati, Hans Raj Khanna, A.C. Gupta

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 127; (1976) 4 SCC 830; AIR 1976 SC 1398; [1976] 3 S.C.R. 883; 1976 TAXLR 1720; 1976 UJ 464

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Bombay Municipal Boroughs Act, 1925, ss. 81(4), 82 and 84 - Constitution, art. 136 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - (A) When assessment list is effective in levying tax - (B) Interpretation of Statutes - If statute to be read as a whole - (C) Appeal by special leave - Interpretation of State statute prevailing in State - Interference - Held, in order to be effective in levying, tax must be authenticated before expiry of official year for which it is prepared, otherwise it would be void and inoperative - The statute must be read as a whole and every provision in the Statute must be construed with reference to the context and other clauses in the statute so as, as far as possible, to make a consistent enactment of the whole statute - The SC is ordinarily loathe to interfere with the interpretation of a statute which has prevailed in the State for a long number of years and which the State Legislature has chosen not to disturb by legislative amendment - Appeal Dismissed
