Jayaraj v State of Tamil Nadu

Supreme Court of India

30 March 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Sarkaria, N.L. Untwalia

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 530; (1976) SCC (Cr) 293; (1976) 2 SCC 788; AIR 1976 SC 1519; 1976 CRLJ 1186; 1976 CRLR 236

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Evidence Act, 1872, s. 32(1) - Penal Code, 1860, ss. 299, 300 Thirdly, 304 and 302 - (A) Evidentiary value of dying declaration - (B) Ingredients of offence u/s. 299 of IPC, 1860 - Connotion of 'Intent' and 'knowledge' - Act of accused falling u/s. 299 (b) - Provision of s. 300 Thirdly, if attracted - The very brevity of dying declaration, in the circumstances of the case, far from being a suspicious circumstance, was an index of its being true and free from the taint of tutoring, more so when the substratum of the dying declaration was fully consistent with the ocular account given by the eye-witnesses - Offence held would fall under First Part of s.304 and not u/s.302 - Appeal Partly Allowed.
