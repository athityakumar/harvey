K. Kapen Chako v Provident Investment Company Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

01 November 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Ray, M. Hameedullah Beg, P.N. Shingal

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 449; (1977) 1 SCC 593; AIR 1976 SC 2610; [1977] 1 S.C.R. 1026

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure; Rent Control

Summary: Land & Property - Rent Control - Practice & Procedure - Kerala Land Reforms Act, 1964, ss. 2(44), 3(1)(vii), 3(1)(c), 13(2)(3), 50-A, 52, 73, 108, 125(3) and 132(3) - Amendment Act, 1969 - Amendment Act, 1971 - Amendment Act, 1972 - Malabar Tenancy Act, 1930, ss. 13 and 23 - (A) Whether appellant, by effect of proviso to s. 3(1)(vii) of 1964 Act as amended in 1969, was entitled to fixity of tenure and was not liable to be evicted u/s. 23 of Malabar Tenancy Act, 1930? - Held, appellant had no fixity of tenure on 21-01-1961 warranting grant of any benefit, or right against eviction; further, respondent in present case is a corporation owned or controlled by Govt. of M P and, therefore, is a Govt. company; appellant is disentitled to claim fixity of tenure u/s. 13 of Act inasmuch as under 1964 Act and under amendment in 1969 leases of lands owned by Govt. owned companies are by s. 3(1)(i) of 1964 Act specifically exempted from provisions of Chap. II of Act, therefore, s. 13 which is in Chap. II cannot be invoked by appellant; appellant is, therefore, not entitled to claim fixity of tenure under 1964 Act as amended - (B) Whether or not appellant's holding is a plantation in context of s. 3(1)(viii) of 1964 Act? - Beenachi Estate in present case does not come within definition of 'plantation' in s. 2(44) of 1964 Act as amended in 1969 because extent of coffee cultivated area has been found by court to have dwindled to a little over 110 acres in extent out of total extent of a little over 550 acres; tapioca has been grown by receiver in areas other than coffee planted area - Held, estate ceased to be a property principally planted with coffee even before 01-01-1970; appellant is disentitled to take advantage of his own wrongs so as to claim statutory benefits which are also not available to appellant - (C) Whether decree could be reopened by invoking provisions contained in s. 108(2) and (3)? - S. 108(3) of Act as amended in 1971 will permit reopening of decree only if a person has claims to right, benefit or remedy which has been conferred on him under Act - Held, in present case neither under 1964 Act nor under 1969 Amendment Act appellant can claim benefit of s. 50-A of 1964 Act as amended in 1969 because appellant wilfully misused holding and caused acts of waste causing loss to lessor and damage and destruction to holding - (D) Whether s. 125(3) of 1964 Act as amended in 1969 provides that all questions regarding rights of tenants could be decided only by Land Tribunal to which a reference has to be made? - Provisions contained in s. 125(1) are prospective; effect of proviso to s. 125(1) is to carve out by way of exception what would otherwise have fallen within provision to which it is a proviso - Held, proceedings which were pending at commencement of Amendment Act on 10-01-1970 are saved from operation of s. 125(1) and are to be determined by civil court; it is not that pending proceedings which are excepted from application of s. 125(1) will yet fall within s. 125(1) by reference to s. 125(3), therefore, provisions of s. 125 are not applicable in present case - (E) Whether provisions of ss. 50 A, 52 and 73 as inserted by 1969 amendment, are applicable in present case? - It is a general rule that when legislature alters rights of parties by taking away or conferring any right of action, its enactments, unless in express terms they apply to pending actions, do not affect them - Held, appellant is disentitled from invoking provisions in ss. 50-A, 52 and 73 by reason of Chap. II of 1964 Act not being applicable to lease where lessor is a Govt. company; further these sections came into effect on 01-01-1970 and are not retrospective but prospective in operation - Appeal dismissed.
