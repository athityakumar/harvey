Performing Right Society Limited and Another v Commissioner of Income Tax and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 August 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

A.C. Gupta, Jaswant Singh

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 501; (1976) 4 SCC 37; AIR 1976 SC 1973; 1976 CTR 429; [1977] 106 ITR 11; [1977] 1 S.C.R. 171; 1976 UJ 745

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 5(2)(b) and 9 - Assessment - Non-resident - Income accuring in India - Relevency of question as to source of income - If s. 9 attracted - Applicability of rule of diversion - Held, question not relevant - And no question arose whether u/s. 9 it should be 'deemed' to accrue or arise in India - Held, to be a case where the assessee having received the income applied it in a particular way; it was not a case of diversion of income by an overriding charge - Appeal Dismissed.
