Maina Singh v State of Rajasthan

Supreme Court of India

17 March 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Shingal, R.S. Sarkaria

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 113; (1976) SCC (Cr) 332; (1976) 2 SCC 827; AIR 1976 SC 1084; 1976 CRLJ 835; 1976 CrLR(SC) 158; [1976] 3 S.C.R. 651

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 149 and 302 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Offence u/ss. 302, 149 and 34, IPC, 1860 - Charge related to commission of offence of unlawful assembly by appellant along with other named four co-accused, and with no other person - Trial went on that basis throughout - Whether accused is guilty of offence u/s. 302, IPC, 1860? - Held, as evidence on record does not show that appellant was responsible for any injury on deceased as could have resulted in deceased's death but it however proves that he inflicted gunshot injuries on deceased and that one of them was grievous - Appellant was guilty u/s. 302 r/w s. 34, IPC, 1860 is altered to one u/s. 326, IPC, 1860 and sentence was reduced to 10 years rigorous imprisonment - Appeal partly allowed.
