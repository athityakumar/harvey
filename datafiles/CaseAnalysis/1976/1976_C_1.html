Puzhakkal Kuttappu v C. Bhargavi and Others

Supreme Court of India

22 September 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

P.K. Goswami, Y.V. Chandrachud, A.C. Gupta

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 364; (1977) 1 SCC 17; AIR 1977 SC 105; [1977] 1 S.C.R. 696; 1976 UJ 853

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Agriculture & Agri. Products

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - Mortgage - Lease - 'Otti' right in land was transferred to predecessors-in-interest of appellant and respondents by daughter of janman and other heirs by registered document dt.30-12-1894 - While so, Govt. acquired property under the Act and awarded compensation of Rs.2859.88 including solatium - Appellant claimed entire compensation while making allowance for small sum in favour of respondents and on other hand, respondents claimed entire compensation while making allowance for small sum in favour of appellant - Trial Court held that document to be mortgage whereas HC held it to be lease - Whether document in question is that of mortgage or lease.

Held, from the contents of the document it is clear that the consideration was predominantly past debt and only a small sum of cash was received on the date of its execution and possession of the land was made over to the transferee giving him the right to 'enjoy' the land. The transferee was required to appropriate the income of the property to the interest on the amount advanced. The transferee was also to pay the land revenue to the credit of the transferor. There is nothing in the recitals to show that the land was given as security for the amount of loan and there is no right of sale of the land delivered to the transferee in case the debt is not discharged. On contrary, there is a clear recital about the payment of annual rent by the transferee to the transferor. Further, feature of payment of rent, fits the balance in favour of construing the document as a lease, coupled with the fact that the essence of a mortgage being the transfer of immovable property as security for the debt is conspicuous by its absence in the detailed enumeration of the terms. Hence, said document is mortgage deed and not lease deed. Appeal dismissed.

Ratio - In case of mixed elements in an instrument disclosing features of mortgage as well as of lease, then Court should find out the predominant intention of the parties executing the document viewed from the essential aspect of the reality of the transaction. Further, one essential feature in mortgage that is absent in lease, is, that the property transferred is a security for the repayment of debt in a mortgage whereas in a lease it is a transfer of a right to enjoy the property.
