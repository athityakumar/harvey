Syed Mohd. Salie Labbai (Dead) By L.Rs. and Others v Mohd. Hanifs (Dead) By L.Rs. and Others

Supreme Court of India

22 March 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, P.K. Goswami

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 403; (1976) 4 SCC 780; AIR 1976 SC 1569; [1976] 3 S.C.R. 721

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Trusts & Associations

Summary: Trusts & Associations - Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - s.92- Muslim Wakfs Act, 1954 - s.55(2) - Agreement - Grave-yard - Possession - Mismanagement - Defendant properties were situated on lands sold to their ancestors of by sale deeds - Plaintiffs approached defendants for permission to build mosque on part of land and agreement was executed and vacant land was used as grave-yard on payment of certain fees to defendants - Defendants constructed few shops on part of grave-yard - Plaintiffs alleged that properties were public trusts dedicated to God and defendants could not claim right of ownership - Plaintiffs initiated suits alleged that defendants had mismanaging wakf properties as Dargah was public property dedicated to God and defendants had no individual interest in it - Defendants contested that it was their private property - Trial Court declared possession of properties to defendants - Plaintiffs filed appeals to HC was allowed - HC remanded case to Trial Court for framing scheme for administration of trust properties and suit regarding Dargah was dismissed - Hence instant appeals - Whether defendant's entitled to possession of properties - Whether mismanagement of defendants was justified - Held, there was evidence to show that constructions were used by Mahomedan community for purpose of offering their prayers in mosque on special occasions - Adjuncts of mosque were meant for purely religious purpose connected with offering of prayers in mosque - There was no evidence for defendants to show that constructions was their private property - It was inescapable that mosque and adjuncts constitute one single unit as public wakf - It was evident that no attempt was made by defendants to repair and maintain it properly - Hence, it was evident that defendants were guilty of grave mismanagement and clear for formulating scheme under s. 92 of 1908 Act - Appeals disposed of.
