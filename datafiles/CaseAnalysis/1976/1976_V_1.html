Devarapalli Lakshminarayana Reddy and Others v V Narayana Reddy and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 May 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Sarkaria, Jaswant Singh, P.N. Shingal

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 591; (1976) 3 SCC 252; (1976) SCC (Cr) 380; AIR 1976 SC 1672; 1976 AllCriC 230; 1976 CRLJ 1361; 1976 CrLR(SC) 268; [1976] Supp S.C.R. 524

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, ss. 156(3), 482, 202, 200 and 190-199 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 147, 148, 149, 307, 342, 392, 395, 448, 378 and 342 - Respondent made a private complaint against appellants to Magistrate who directly sent complaint to police for investigation - Whether in view of Cl. (a) of the first Proviso to s. 202(1), CrPC, 1973 a Magistrate who receives a complaint, disclosing an offence exclusively triable by the Court of Session, is debarred from sending the same to the police for investigation u/s. 156(3) of the CrPC? - Object of an investigation u/s. 202 is not to initiate a fresh case on police report but to assist the Magistrate in completing proceedings already instituted upon a complaint before him - S. 202 comes in at a stage when some evidence, has been collected by the Magistrate in proceedings under Chap. XV, but the same is deemed insufficient to take a decision as to the next step in the prescribed procedure - In such a situation, the Magistrate is empowered u/s. 202 to direct, within the limits circumscribed by that section, an investigation - Held, Magistrate sending complaint for investigation cannot be said to have taken cognizance - Magistrate has to first examine complainant and his witnesses u/s. 200, CrPC, 1973 which is first step of procedure and he cannot directly send complaint to police for investigation - Further held, stage at which s. 202 could become operative was never reached in this case - Appeal dismissed.
