Karam Chand Thapar and Bros. (Coal Sales) Limited v State of Uttar Pradesh and Another

Supreme Court of India

21 July 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

A.C. Gupta, Jaswant Singh

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 384; (1976) 4 SCC 257; AIR 1976 SC 2101; 1976 (5) CTR 365; [1977] 1 S.C.R. 25; 1976 (38) STC 593; 1976 TAXLR 1921; 1976 UJ 681

Case Digest

Subject: Environment, Wildlife & Animal; Land & Property

Summary: Sales Tax - Indirect Tax - Uttar Pradesh Sales Tax Act, 1948, s. 22 - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956, ss. 9(1), 14 - (A) Whether s. 9 (1) of Central Act was applicable, enabling State of Uttar Pradesh to levy, collect Central sales tax on sales of coal effected by Company to consumers in Uttar Pradesh? - (B) Whether order rectifying assessment order made was barred by limitation u/s. 22(1) of U P Act? - Held, the dispute relates to amount of Rs. 5, 59, 172/32 which could not be taxed in State of Uttar Pradesh - S. 2(c) of Central Act defines 'declared goods' as goods declared u/s. 14 as important in inter-State trade or commerce mentions coal as well - S. 6(1),provides; every dealer shall be liable to pay tax under this Act on sales of goods - S. 6(2), states that notwithstanding what is provided in sub-s. (1), any subsequent sale of goods effected by a transfer of documents of title to goods, to Govt, or to a registered dealer other than Govt, if goods are of description referred to in sub-s. (3) of s. 8, shall be exempt from tax under this Act - S. 7(1) requires every dealer liable to pay tax under this Act to apply for registration - S. 7(3), provides if application is in order, shall be registered, grant him a certificate of registration - S. 9 of U P Act gives right of appeal to 'any dealer objecting to any order made by assessing authority, other than an order mentioned in s. 10-A', within thirty days from date of service of copy of order - In this case Company was not affected by order u/s. 22 being communicated to it after expiry of three years from date of order because limitation for an appeal from that order did not begin to run before communication of order provisions of s. 9 of U P Act make that clear - Appeal dismissed.
