State of Madhya Pradesh v Ratan Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

05 May 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, A.C. Gupta

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 590; (1976) SCC (Cr) 428; (1976) 3 SCC 470; AIR 1976 SC 1552; 1976 CRLJ 1192; 1976 CrLR(SC) 280; 1977 (1) MLJ(SC) 19; [1976] Supp S.C.R. 552

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 57 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898, s. 401 - Respondent was convicted by Sessions Judge u/s. 302, IPC - Awarded life imprisonment - Transferred to Amritsar Central Jail on request - Respondent contended that he had served more than 20 years in jail - Petition accepted by Punjab and Haryana High Court - (A) Whether a prisoner can be released automatically on expiry of 20 years of confinement in jail - Question of remission of entire sentence or a part of it lies within exclusive domain of appropriate Govt. u/s. 401 of CrPC, 1898 and s. 57 of IPC, 1860 - Any Rules or local Acts cannot stultify effect of sentence of life imprisonment given by court under IPC - Held, the prisoner cannot be released automatically on expiry of 20 years - (B) Whether HC can interfere with state Govt.'s decision in case of request of release of a prisoner on completing 20 years in jail is rejected? - A perusal of s. 402(3)(f) CrPC clearly reveals that test to determine appropriate Govt. is to locate State where accused was convicted and sentenced and Govt. of that State would be appropriate Govt. within meaning of s. 401 of CrPC - Held, where transferee State feels that accused has completed a period of 20 years it has merely to forward request of prisoner to concerned State Govt., that is to say, Govt. of State where prisoner was convicted and sentenced and even if this request is rejected by State Govt. order of Govt. cannot be interfered with by a HC in its writ jurisdiction - Appeal allowed.
