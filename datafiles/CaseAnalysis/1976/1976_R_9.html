Commissioner of Income Tax Gujarat-Ii, Ahmedabad v R.M. Amin

Supreme Court of India

26 November 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

Hans Raj Khanna, Jaswant Singh

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 281; (1977) SCC (Tax) 234; (1977) 1 SCC 691; AIR 1977 SC 999; 1977 CTR 27; [1977] 106 ITR 368; [1977] 2 S.C.R. 220; 1977 UJ 31

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Finance Act, 1949 - Income Tax And Excess Profits Tax (Amendment) Act,1947 - Income-tax Act,1961 - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922 - Liquidation - Relinquishment - Capital gains - Acquisition - Respondent company went into voluntary liquidation and liquidators sold assets of company and as per account assessee became entitled to receive amount per share as return of capital and was received by assessee - Appellant treated amount of Rs.1,84,326 as capital gains as liable to tax within meaning of s. 45 of Act of 1961 and contended that it was not company within meaning of s. 2(17) of Act of 1961 and shareholders could not be said to be entitled to benefit provided u/s.46(2) of Act of 1961 and entire amount was liable to be taxed - Appeal made to Appellate Assistant Commissioner was dismissed and held that amount was liable to be taxed to capital gains tax - Appeal made to Tribunal was accepted - Appellant appealed to HC was in favour of respondent - Hence, instant appeal - Whether respondent was liable to tax under capital gains - Held, shares assumed by assessee in company constituted capital asset - No dispute that amount received by assessee in shares of company assumed by him constituted profits - There was no transfer contemplated by law as to attract levy of tax on capital gains - It would not had been possible to charge tax under head Capital gains on assets of company received by its shareholder on its liquidation - S.46(2) apply only to distribution of assets by companies in liquidation - Hence, it was difficult to sustain levy of tax on capital gains when companies distribute assets on liquidation to share-holders - Appeal dismissed.
