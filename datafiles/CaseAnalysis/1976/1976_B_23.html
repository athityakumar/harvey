Bechan Pandey and Others v Dulhin Janki Devi and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 March 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

Hans Raj Khanna, P.K. Goswami

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 185; (1976) 2 SCC 286; AIR 1976 SC 866; [1976] 3 S.C.R. 555; 1976 UJ 320

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Remand of case - Breach of Peace - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 145 - Civil Suit filed by appellants for declaration of title to land along with possession and mesne profits - Appellants claim to land was purchase in an auction - Suit decreed in favour of appellants and defendants preferred an appeal denying title of appellants - Whether suit should be remanded back to Trial Court for decision on merits as regards possession? - Held, no - Remanding suit would have effect of prolonging long litigation by another round of legal battle - Final decision should be rendered by Apex Court to end long course of litigation between parties - Appeal dismissed.
