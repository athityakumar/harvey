P. Venkataswami and Another v D. S. Ramireddy and Another

Supreme Court of India

27 February 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

A.C. Gupta, P.K. Goswami

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 551; (1976) 3 SCC 665; AIR 1976 SC 1066; 1977 (1) MLJ(SC) 1

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Administrative - Madras Estates (Abolition and Conversion into Ryotwari) Act, 1948, ss. 13(b)(iii), 15(1) - Ryotwari patta - Assessed waste - Suo moto proceedings were initiated u/s. 15(1) of the Act in respect of subject lands and from order of Assistant Settlement Officer, respondent no. 1 went on appeal to Tribunal and appellants also preferred appeal challenging finding that land should be treated as assessed waste - Tribunal dismissed appeal of respondent no. 1 and allowed appeal of appellants - Hence, respondent no. 1 filed appeals before HC challenging rejection of claim for ryotwari patta and also challenging order of tribunal allowing appeal of appellants - HC passed common judgment and set aside orders of Tribunal and directed Tribunal to dispose of appeals afresh in light of observations made in its judgment - Hence, instant appeal by appellants - Whether HC was right in holding that the requirements of s. 13(b)(iii) of the Act is satisfied if the landholder is able to show that there was an intention to cultivate or resume the land for cultivation.

Held, s. 13(b)(iii) of the Act requires as a condition that landholder has cultivated such lands and the words 'has cultivated' could not imply mere intention to cultivate. Hence, in view of clear terms of s. 13(b)(iii) there is no warrant for reconsideration of the question by Tribunal. Hence, order of HC is set aside and that of Tribunal is restored. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - As per s. 13(b)(iii) of the Act, landholder should prove that he has cultivated the land himself or by his own servants or hired labour from 1-7-1945 and has been in direct and continuous possession of the land from the date in order to be entitled for ryotwari patta.
