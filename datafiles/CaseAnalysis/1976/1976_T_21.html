Khadya Peya Vikarete Malak Sangh v The Chief Officer, Sangli Municipal Council & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

19 November 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, Y.V. Chandrachud, P.K. Goswami

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 276; (1977) SCC (Cr) 113; (1977) 1 SCC 455; AIR 1977 SC 527; [1977] 2 S.C.R. 139; 1977 TAXLR 1629

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Maharashtra Rules 1962, Licence Fees, Chargeability

Summary: Municipality & Local Governments - Practice & Procedure - Prevention of Food Adulteration Act, 1954, s. 24 - Licence Fees - Chargeability - Legality - Appellant was an association of hoteliers and restaurant keepers doing business within local limits of respondent (Municipality) - Appellant association were required to take a licence for the business conducted by them - Municipality insisted that members of appellant/Association should pay 2 sets of fees on the ground that members were both manufacturers and retail dealers and were liable to pay fees in both those capacities - Aggrieved appellant filed a suit for declaration and injunction before Extra Asst. Judge, which was allowed - However on appeal, HC by impugned judgment dismissed suit holding that that case of appellant was covered both by item 1, 3 to 8 of Appendix (1) to schedule of the Rules framed under the Act and reversed judgment of Extra Asst. Judge - Hence, instant appeal - Whether impugned judgment passed by HC was justified - Held, item 1, 3 to 8 of Appendix (1), postulated 2 different contingencies - Item 1 takes within its fold wholesale dealer or manufacturer or both - There was no mention of a retail dealer in that item - Thus, before a trader fall within the purview of item 1 of Appendix (1), it should be shown that he was either a wholesale dealer or a manufacturer or both - Where dominant nature of trading activity of a person was neither that of a manufacturer nor as a wholesale dealer, but he was engaged in retail sale, item 1 would have no application - Mere fact that trader prepared the articles for the purpose of selling same to his customers would not make him either a wholesale dealer or a manufacturer - Appellant's members could not be wholesale dealers because there was nothing to show that they deal in articles for the purpose of re-sale - On the other hand, nature of their trading activity was one of retail sale - Thus, appellant clearly fall within items 3 to 8 of Appendix (1) - HC erred in taking view that case of appellant was covered both by item 1 and items 3 to 8 of Appendix (1) and was not justified in reversing judgment of Extra Asst. Judge - In instant case, members of appellant who were mostly restaurant-keepers conducting business of retail sale, the preparation of articles being merely an ancillary activity, were liable to pay licence fee under items 3 to 8 of Appendix (1) and not under item 1 of Appendix (1) to the Schedule - Appellant was entitled to declaration sought for and was entitled to refund - Impugned judgment was set aside - Appeal allowed.
