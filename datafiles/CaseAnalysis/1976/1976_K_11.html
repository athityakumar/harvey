State Of Andhra Pradesh v K. Venkata Reddy & Others

Supreme Court of India

26 March 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Sarkaria, P.N. Shingal

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 132; (1976) SCC (Cr) 448; (1976) 3 SCC 454; AIR 1976 SC 2207; 1976 CRLJ 1723; 1976 CrLR(SC) 210; [1976] 3 S.C.R. 929

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34 and 302 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 9 - (A) Prosecution of several accused persons u/s. 302 r/w s. 34 - Legality of conviction of one alone - (B) Identification - Evidence of - Prosecution of A along with other 12 accused persons - Two eye-witnesses identifying accused at test identification parade - Fact that these witnesses while identifying him did not say anything with regard to specific part played by him in commission of crime - Held, permissible to come to the conclusion that they acted conjointly with one of the charged accused if there was other evidence to lead to the conclusion, but not otherwise - Held it did not render evidence of such identification inadmissible - Appeal Allowed.
