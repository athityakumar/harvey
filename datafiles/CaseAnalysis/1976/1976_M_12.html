Commissioner of Sales Tax, U. P. v Madan Lal Das and Sons, Bareilly

Supreme Court of India

22 September 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

Hans Raj Khanna, N.L. Untwalia, Jaswant Singh

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 415; (1976) 4 SCC 464; AIR 1977 SC 523; 1976 (5) CTR 434; [1977] 1 S.C.R. 683; 1976 (38) STC 543; 1977 TAXLR 1626; 1976 UJ 935

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Limitation Act, 1963, ss. 12(2) and 29(2) - Indian Limitation Act, 1908, s. 12(2) - Uttar Pradesh Sales Tax Act, 1940, s. 10(1) - Limitation - Whether time taken by dealer in obtaining other copy of impugned appellate Order could be excluded for purpose of limitation of filing revision? - Held, time spent in obtaining that copy would necessarily have to be excluded u/s. 12(2) - Expression time requisite in s. 12 (2) cannot be understood as time absolutely necessary for obtaining copy of Order - What is deductible u/s. 12 (2) is not minimum time within which copy of Order appealed against could have been obtained - Appeal dismissed.
