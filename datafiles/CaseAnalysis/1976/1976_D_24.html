Kale and Others v Deputy Director of Consolidation Others

Supreme Court of India

21 January 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, V.R. Krishna Iyer, R.S. Sarkaria

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 409; (1976) 3 SCC 119; AIR 1976 SC 807; 1976 (2) ALR 173; 1978 HLR 441; [1976] 3 S.C.R. 202

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Land & Property; Women & Children

Keywords: Succession & Inheritance

Summary: Land & Property - Uttar Pradesh Consolidation of Holdings Act, 1953 - Uttar Pradesh Tenancy Act, 1939 - Registration Act, s. 17 (2) - Uttar Pradesh Zamindari Abolition and Land Reforms Act, 1950 - Whether a distinction should be made between a document containing terms and recitals of a family arrangement made under document and a mere memorandum prepared after family arrangement had already been made either for purpose of record or for information of court for making necessary mutation? - Held, the memorandum itself does not create or extinguish any rights in immovable properties and does not fall within mischief of s. 17(2) of Registration Act and not compulsorily registrable - In Sahu Madho Das and others v Pandit Mukand Ram and another Court appears to have amplified doctrine of validity of family arrangement - In Tek Bahadur Bhujil v Debi Singh Bhujil and others Court held that family arrangement could be arrived at even orally and registration would be required only if it was reduced into writing - Family settlement did not contravene any provision of law but was a legally valid and binding settlement in accordance with law, the view that it was against provisions of law was wrong and could not be sustained - Appeal allowed.
