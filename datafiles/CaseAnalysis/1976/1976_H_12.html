Chhotan Prasad Singh and Others v Hari Dusadh and Others

Supreme Court of India

24 November 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Shingal, P.N. Bhagwati, A.C. Gupta

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 438; (1977) SCC (Cr) 35; (1977) 1 SCC 102; AIR 1977 SC 407; 1977 (3) ALR 67; 1977 CRLJ 249; 1977 CRLR 9; [1977] 2 S.C.R. 174; 1977 UJ 46

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, ss. 145(1), 510-A, 539, 539-A and 539-AA - Oaths Act, 1873, s. 4(a) - Evidence Act, 1872 - General Clauses Act, 1897, s. 3(3) - Whether affidavits sworn or affirmed before magistrates who are not in seisin of case u/s. 145 of CrPC, 1973 could be read in evidence under that section? - An affidavit u/s. 145 is now however of a formal character because it is meant to prove or disprove competing claims of parties as respects fact of actual possession of subject of dispute; there is no provision in Code specifying courts or persons before whom affidavits referred to in s. 145 have to be sworn and affirmed, therefore, this is to be done according to general provisions relating to affidavits - U/s. 4 of Oaths Act, 1873 all courts and persons having by law or consent of parties authority to receive evidence are authorised to administer oaths and affirmations, but they can do so only where they are otherwise acting 'in discharge of duties or in exercise of powers imposed or conferred upon them respectively by law', thus, court or person mentioned in cls. (a) of s. 4 of Oaths Act, 1897 can administer oath or affirmation to deponent in an affidavit only if court or person is acting 'discharge of duties or in exercise of powers imposed or conferred upon them respectively by law' - Held, magistrates concerned with proceeding u/s. 145 were discharging duties imposed and exercising powers conferred by Code, and they alone could administer oaths and affirmations to persons who made affidavits, and not magistrates who were not discharging any such duty or exercising any such power; as affidavits were admittedly not sworn or affirmed before magistrates who were dealing with disputes u/s. 145, they were not proper affidavits and did not constitute evidence for purpose of s. 145 - Appeals dismissed.
