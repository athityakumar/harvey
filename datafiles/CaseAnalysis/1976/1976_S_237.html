Santa Singh v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

17 August 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Bhagwati, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 169; (1976) 4 SCC 190; (1976) SCC (Cr) 546; AIR 1976 SC 2386; 1976 CRLJ 1875; 1976 CrLR(SC) 375; [1977] 1 S.C.R. 229

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006), Penology

Summary: Criminal - Sentence - Indian Penal Code, s.302 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 235 and 465 - Appellant was convicted for murder of his mother and his foster father - Appellant was awarded sentence of death in absence of his lawyer - No hearing was provided to appellant on question of sentence - Whether provisions of s. 235 of CrPC have been committed and death sentence liable to be set aside? - Held, yes - Penology and sentencing procedures provide that when an accused is convicted he has to be given an opportunity to be heard in regard to sentence - Court can proceed to pass sentence only after hearing him - Non-compliance with the requirement of the section cannot be described as a mere irregularity curable u/s. 465 - Such deviation constitutes disobedience of an express provision of the CrPC as to t he mode of trial and hence cannot be regarded as a mere irregularity - Matter sent back to Trial Court for giving an opportunity to appellant to make a representation regarding sentence proposed - Appeal allowed.
