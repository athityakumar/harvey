Natha Singh & Ors. v Financial Commissioner, Taxation, Punjab and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 March 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

Jaswant Singh, A.C. Gupta

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 200; (1976) 3 SCC 28; AIR 1976 SC 1053; [1976] 3 S.C.R. 620; 1976 UJ 334

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Administrative - Punjab Security of Land Tenures Act, 1953, 2(8) - Holding land to be tenant permissible area - Sustainability - Appellant No. 1 was recorded in revenue records as land-owner in respect of 39 standard acres and 9 3/4 units of land in X village, 53 standard acres and 5 1/2 units in Y village and 4 standard acres and 2 units in Z village - By his order, the then Collector, acting under the provisions of Act declared an area of 63 standard acres and 1 1/4 units out of land aggregating 93 standard acres and 1 1/4 units as surplus in the hands of appellant No.1 - Appellant Nos. 2 and 3 who were the sons of appellant No. 1, went up in appeal against the said order of the Collector to the Commissioner who vide his order allowed the appeal, set aside order of the Collector and remanded the case for fresh determination of the 'Surplus Area' - After re- examination of the case on remand, the Collector overruled the plea raised by appellants Nos. 2 and 3 that the area comprised in khasra Nos. 296, 297, 517, 519, 285, 293 and 206 which was in their cultivating possession as tenants under appellant No.1 before the commencement of the Act should be treated 'Tenants Permissible Area' and excluded from the surplus pool and held that the entries in khasra girdawaries on which the claim of the said appellants was grounded could not be relied upon as they had been tampered with - Hence instant appeal - Appellant contended that the writ petition filed by the appellants could not, in the facts and circumstances of the case, be dismissed in limine by the High Court - Appellant urged that the orders passed by the revenue authorities could not be sustained as they did not, while computing the 'Surplus Area', left out the permissible area which even according to the khasra girdawaries and Roznamcha Waqaiti which was maintained for the purpose of recording changes in cultivation was being cultivated by appellants Nos. 2 and 3, as tenants of appellant No. 1 since 1951-52 that 30 bighas of land which was recorded as 'banjar' at the time of the commencement of Act and did not fall within the definition of land as contained in s.2(8) of Act had not been taken into account while evaluating and assessing the 'Surplus Area', and that appellant Nos. 2 and 3 were not afforded proper and adequate opportunity by the Collector to prove the claim put forth by them - Held, contention of the appellants based on the revenue record was concerned, it might be remarked that it had been concurrently found by the Collector and the Commissioner who examined the original khasra girdawaries that they had been tampered with by the revenue staff in collusion with the appellants - In the circumstances, it would not be safe to place any reliance on them - Reliance sought to be placed on 'Roznamcha Waqaiti' was also an after thought - No authenticated copy of the 'Roznamcha Waqaiti' with reference to which HC invited to verify the entries in the khasra girdawaries was included in the record - It was also significant that no reliance either before the Collector or before the Commissioner or even before the Financial Commissioner seemed to have been placed upon the 'Roznamcha Waqaiti' - It was also to be noted that even in the application for leave to adduce additional evidence, no mention had been made of any entry in 'Roznamcha Waqaiti' - Even if the entries in khasra girdawaries were treated as genuine, they could be of little assistance to the appellants as they did not at all, as observed by the Collector, appeared to show that any rent was being paid by the appellant Nos. 2 and 3 to appellant No. 1 - In the absence of payment of rent or in the absence of material to show that there was contract between appellant No. 1 and appellant Nos. 2 and 3 absolving the latter of the liability to pay rent, it was difficult to uphold the claim of appellant Nos. 2 and 3 that they were tenants of appellant No. 1 - Appeal dismissed.
