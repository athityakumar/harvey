Narayan Govind Gavate and Others v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

11 October 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Hameedullah Beg, A.N. Ray, Jaswant Singh

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 363; (1977) SCC (Cr) 49; (1977) 1 SCC 133; AIR 1977 SC 183; [1977] 1 S.C.R. 763

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 101, 102 and 106 - Burden of proof - Whether orders governed by same judgment of a Division Bench of High Court of Maharashtra disposing of Writ Petitions relating to four groups of lands is correct? - Held, HC correctly applied provisions of s. 106 of Evidence Act, 1872 to place burden up on State to prove those special circumstances although it also appears that HC was not quite correct in stating its view in such a manner as to make it appear that some part of initial burden of petitioners u/s. 101 and 102 of Evidence Act, 1872 had been displaced by failure of State, to discharge its duty u/s. 106 of Act - Correct way of putting it would have been to say that failure of State to produce evidence of facts especially' within knowledge of its officials, which rested upon it u/s. 106 of Evidence Act, taken together with attendant facts and circumstances, including contents of recitals, had enabled petitioners to discharge their burdens u/s. 101 and 102 of Evidence Act, 1872 - There is no force in either appeals by owners of land or in those preferred by State of Maharashtra - Appeals dismissed.
