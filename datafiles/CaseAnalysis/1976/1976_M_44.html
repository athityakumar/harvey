Mumbai Kamgar Sabha, Bombay v Messrs Abdulbhai Faizullabhai and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 March 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

V.R. Krishna Iyer, N.L. Untwalia

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 194; (1976) SCC (L&S) 517; (1976) 3 SCC 832; AIR 1976 SC 1455; 1976 (32) FLR 291; 1976 LabIC 1012; 1976 (2) LLJ 186; 1976 (1) LLN 502; [1976] 3 S.C.R. 591

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Implied Term, Festival, Full Bench Formula, Coal Mines Provident Fund and Miscellaneous Provisions Act, 1948

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Payment of Bonus Act, 1965, Pre. and ss. 17 and 34 - Constitution of India, 1950, Pre. and Part IV - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 11 - Interpretation - Civil Procedure - Bonus - (A) Scope of 1965 Act - (B) Interpretation of Pre. and Part IV of Constitution - (C) Res judicata, case of customary bonus or contract bonus not urged before Arbitration Board - Held, act deals only with profit bonus and connected matters, customary bonus is not annihilated - Where two judicial choices are available, construction in conformity with social philosophy of Part IV has preference - That ground is not barred by general principles of res judicata in later years - Appeal allowed.
