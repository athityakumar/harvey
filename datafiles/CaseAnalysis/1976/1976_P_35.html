D. C. Roy v Presiding Officer, Madhya Pradesh Industrial Court, Indore

Supreme Court of India

23 March 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.V. Chandrachud, V.R. Krishna Iyer

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 124; (1976) SCC (L&S) 484; (1976) 3 SCC 693; AIR 1976 SC 1760; 1976 (32) FLR 285; 1976 LabIC 1142; [1976] 3 S.C.R. 801; 1976 UJ 401

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Madhya Pradesh Industrial Employment (Standing Order) Rules 1963

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Madhya Pradesh Industrial Employment (Standing Orders) Rules, 1963, r. 7 - Madhya Pradesh Standard Orders, cl. 12(b) - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, Sch. 2, item 3 - Industrial Dispute - Standing order - Major misconduct - State transport - Collection of fare but tickets not issued by conductor - Non detection by ticket examiner - Domestic enquiry, defective - Dismissal justified - (A) Liability of examiner - (B) Claim for back wages from order of dismissal till judgment of labour court, maintainability - Held guilty of 'major misconduct' - Claim held not maintainable - Appeal dismissed.
