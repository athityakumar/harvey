State Of Kerala And Ors. v K.A. Gangadharan

Supreme Court of India

27 October 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Ray, M. Hameedullah Beg, Jaswant Singh

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 332; (1977) 1 SCC 208; AIR 1977 SC 311; [1977] 1 S.C.R. 960; 1976 UJ 918

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Kerala Land Reforms Act 1964

Summary: Land & Property - Administrative - Kerala Land Reforms Act 1964 ss. 82(1), 83, 85 - Family unit - Ceiling - Notified - Respondent was holding land under family unit comprised of himself, his wife and children - Ceiling area allowed u/s. 82(1) of the Act was 10 standard acres which should not be less than 12 and not more than 15 - Respondent was found to have total holding of 28.38 acres of land and 3.87 acres were exempted u/s. 81 of the Act - Statement showing determination was served on him and his wife by concerned authorities and asked to file objections - Respondent filed objection stating that he and his wife does not hold more than ceiling area available to family and was not liable to surrender any excess land - Land Board recognized gift to eldest daughter and required to surrender 8.78 acres of land - Aggrieved respondent filed revision in HC and challenged said order of Land Board which was allowed and held that respondent was not liable to surrender lands specified in order of Land Board - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, prohibition against ceiling area u/s. 83 of the Act and the surrender of the excess land u/s. 85 of the Act are both to be determined with reference to the position as on the notified date u/s. 83 of the Act. Therefore, crucial date for determining and surrendering the surplus land is 1-1- 1970 and not any earlier date and transfers which have been effected between 15-8-1963 and 1-1-1970 will be treated as valid provided they come within the excepted categories enumerated u/s. 84 of the Act. Thus, lands covered by such valid transfers will be treated as properties belonging to the transferors on the notified date for purposes of determining a ceiling area and the extent of excess land to be surrendered by him. However, in respect of transfers effected after 1-1-1970, the ceiling area applicable to a person and the extent of his liability to surrender, which became crystallized on 1-1-1970, will determine the excess land to be surrendered. Hence, obligation to surrender the excess land owned or possessed by person as on 1-1-1970 cannot be affected by voluntary transfers even of the excepted varieties mentioned u/s. 84 of the Act, subsequent to the notified date and transferor will continue to be liable to surrender to the Govt. the full extent of the excess land that was in his possession as on 1-1-1970. HC erred in holding that the respondent was not liable to surrender the land and for the foregoing reasons the judgment is set aside. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - provisions in the Act establish the dominant legislative intent of the imposition of the ceiling on land holdings and the consequential obligation to surrender lands owned or held in excess of the ceiling area on the notified date, namely, 1-1-1970.
