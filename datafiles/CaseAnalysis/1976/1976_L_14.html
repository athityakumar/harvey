Lonankutty v Thomman and Another

Supreme Court of India

15 April 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.V. Chandrachud, V.R. Krishna Iyer, N.L. Untwalia

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 145; (1976) 3 SCC 528; AIR 1976 SC 1645; [1976] Supp S.C.R. 74; 1976 UJ 550

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 11 - Resjudicata - Applicability - Maintainability - Appellant filed suit against respondents for a perpetual injunction restraining them from taking water from survey No. 673, from discharging the water back through survey No. 673 and for a mandatory injunction directing them to demolish the bund and close the sluice gates - Appellant disputed the right claimed by respondents in its entirety, contending that they had no right to the flow of water either way for either purpose-fishing or agriculture - Respondents also filed suit for an injunction restraining appellant from trespassing on the bund constructed by them and for preventing appellant from interfering with their right to take water from Survey No. 673 and to discharge the water back through that land - Respondents claimed this prescriptive right for fishing as well as for agricultural purposes - Trial Court decreed appellants suit partly and dismissed the suit filed by respondent - On appeal Appellate Court dismissed the appeals - Aggrieved further respondent filed appeal before HC - HC set aside the decrees passed by Appellate Court and remanded the appeal for a fresh hearing - On remand Appellate Court proceeded to disposed the matter on merits and dismissed the appeal - On appeal by respondent DB allowed the appeal - Hence instant appeal - Whether resjudicata was applicable in instant case - Held, appellate Court disposed of 4 appeals by a common judgment could not affect the application of s. 11 of CPC because as observed in earlier decision even where 2 appeals arose out of one proceeding and even if the appeals were disposed of by a common judgment, the decision in that judgment may amount to 2 decisions and not to one if the subject-matter of each appeal was different - Instant case was stronger still for the application of s. 11 CPC because appeals filed in the Appellate Court arose not out of one proceeding but out of 2 different suits, one by appellant and the other by respondents - Failure of respondents to challenge the decision of the Appellate Court in so far as it pertained to their suit attracts the application of s. 11 CPC because to the extent to which the Appellate Court decided issues arising in respondents suit against them, that decision would operate as res judicata since it was not appealed against - In view of SC's decision that the contention raised by respondents was barred by res judicata, it should be held that the HC was in error in allowing respondents appeal and accepting his contention - Impugned judgment of the HC was set aside and restore that of the District Court. In the circumstances, there will be no order as to costs.
