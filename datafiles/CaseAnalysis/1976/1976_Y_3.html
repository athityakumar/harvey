 Principal, Guntur Medical College, Guntur v Y.Mohan Rao

Supreme Court of India

06 April 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Bhagwati, A.N. Ray, A.C. Gupta, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, Jaswant Singh

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 317; (1976) 3 SCC 411; AIR 1976 SC 1904; 1978 HLR 473; [1976] 3 S.C.R. 1046; 1976 (2) SLR 35; 1976 UJ 471

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Education - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 15 and 29 - Reconversion - Embracing Hinduism - Earlier a scheduled caste member - Benefit of reservation - Admission to medical college - Held, that since caste is a social combination of persons governed by its rules and regulations, and if person is accepted as a member of that caste by other members of caste he would be eligible for benefit of reservation of seats for scheduled castes in matter of admission to a medical college - Appeal dismissed.
