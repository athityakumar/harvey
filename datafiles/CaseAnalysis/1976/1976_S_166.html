Indian Aluminium Cables Limited v State of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

02 April 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Ray, M. Hameedullah Beg, Jaswant Singh

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 396; (1976) 4 SCC 27; (1976) SCC (Tax) 437; AIR 1976 SC 1711; 1976 (5) CTR 356; [1976] 3 S.C.R. 992; 1976 (38) STC 108; 1976 TAXLR 1839; 1976 UJ 445

Case Digest

Subject: Electricity & Energy; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Land & Property

Summary: Central Sales Tax Act, 1956, s. 8(2A) - Punjab General Sales Tax Act, 1948, ss. 5(2)(a)(iv) and 6 - Indian Electricity Act, 1910 - Inter-State sales - Exemption from sales tax - Meaning of expression 'exemption from tax generally' in s. 8(2A) of CST Act - Sales of poles and cables to State Electric Supply undertaking - Interpretation of words 'in the generation or distribution of such energy' in s. 5(2)(a)(iv) of 1948 Act - Sales held were not exempt from tax generally within s. 8(2A) of Central Act r/w s. 5(2)(a)(iv) of State Act - Appeal dismissed.
