H.C. Sarin v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

14 April 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

N.L. Untwalia, Y.V. Chandrachud, V.R. Krishna Iyer

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 392; (1976) 4 SCC 765; (1977) SCC (L&S) 65; AIR 1976 SC 1686; 1976 (32) FLR 392; 1976 LabIC 1128; 1976 SLJ 455; 1976 (2) SLR 248; [1976] Supp S.C.R. 39

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Indian Railway Establishment Code, 1951

Summary: Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 226 and 311 - Railway Establishment Code, Vol. 1, rr. 1730, Note 3 - Natural Justice - Taking bribery - Departmental enquiry - Delinquent demand particular lawyer's service - Denied - Constitutionality - Held, no entitlement of taking services of a professional Lawyer by rules - May have wide field of choice - Not violative of natural justice.
