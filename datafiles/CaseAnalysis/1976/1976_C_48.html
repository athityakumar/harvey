Dalmia Cement Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax, New Delhi

Supreme Court of India

10 September 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Shingal, A.N. Ray, M. Hameedullah Beg

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 370; (1976) 4 SCC 614; AIR 1976 SC 2150; 1976 CTR 442; [1976] 105 ITR 633; [1977] 1 S.C.R. 554

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Adventure In The Nature Of Trade

Summary: Income Tax & Direct taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922 - Machinery - Assessment - Consideration - Permission - Appellant owned cement factories and placed an order for supply of four complete units of cement manufacturing machinery - Appellant transferred machinery meant for 'D' factory to new company - New company paid sum of Rs.7 lakhs, in lieu of which 70,000 fully paid up ordinary shares of Rs.10/each to appellant - Income-tax Officer treated that amount as income earned by appellant and taxed it - Appeal made to Assistant Appellate Commissioner favoured respondent - Appellant appealed to Income-tax Appellate which remanded case to Income-tax Officer - Appellant appealed to HC was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether appellant was liable for tax in sum of Rs. 7 lakhs received from new Company in nature of trade under 1922 Act - Held, appellant knew that it machinery could not be used in D's factory so he resold it to new company - Appellant was not inclined to make it gratuitous sale, but agreed setting up of new factory that price of machinery had gone up - It was therefore real transaction by way in nature of trade and was as such business transaction - Hence, department had succeeded in proving that transaction of sale was in nature of trade and fail within definition of business in cl. (4) of s.2 of Act - Appeal dismissed.
