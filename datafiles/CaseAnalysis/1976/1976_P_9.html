Padma Uppal and Others v State of Punjab and Others

Supreme Court of India

23 August 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

Jaswant Singh, A.N. Ray, M. Hameedullah Beg

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 377; (1977) 1 SCC 330; AIR 1977 SC 580; [1977] 1 S.C.R. 329; 1976 UJ 775

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Constitution of India, 1950 - Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - Notification - Acquisition - Compensation - Enhancement - Appellants land was acquired by Govt. for public purpose through notification u/ss. 4, 6 of 1894 Act - Collector, fixed compensation into two categories and awarded Re. 1/per sq. yd. for potential building area and Re. 6/per sq. yd. for agricultural land - Appellants dissatisfied with award, approached Collector to make, references to District Court u/s. 18 of Act - District enhanced compensation of agricultural land to Re. 1/per sq. yd. and of potential building area to Rs. 1.50 per sq. yd - Appellants appealed to HC - HC awarded Rs. 3/per sq. yd. for agricultural land and Rs. 4.50 per sq. yd. for potential building area - Hence instant appeals - Whether appellants entitled compensation to agricultural land at rate of potential building - Held, according to provision, compensation had to be awarded on basis of market value prevalent on date of publication of notification u/s. 4(1) 1894 Act - Compensation could not be awarded treating entire land as potential building area - It was true that land constitutes one block but it could not be overlooked that entire area possess same advantages and benefits - Appellants could not justifiably granted compensation for agricultural land at rate of potential building - Appellants had claimed compensation at rate of Rs. 4.00 per sq. yd. hence, they could not had compensation exceeding that rate - Appeals disposed of.
