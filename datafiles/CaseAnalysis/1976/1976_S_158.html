Prem Kakar v State of Haryana and Another

Supreme Court of India

05 April 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Ray, Jaswant Singh

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 153; (1976) 3 SCC 433; (1976) SCC (L&S) 450; AIR 1976 SC 1474; 1976 (32) FLR 343; 1976 LabIC 1028; 1976 (1) LLN 494; [1976] 3 S.C.R. 1010; 1976 UJ 535

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 10(1), 12, 12(4) and 12(5) - Writ of mandamus - Issue of - Maintainability - Whether State can be asked by a writ of mandamus to make a reference u/s. 10(1) of Industrial Disputes Act? - Held, s. 12 of Act deals with duties of Conciliation officers - If Conciliation officer cannot arrive at a settlement of dispute he sends a report to appropriate Govt. - Under s. 12(5) of Act if, on a consideration of report referred to in sub-s. (4), appropriate Govt. is satisfied that there is a case for reference, it may make such reference - Where appropriate Govt. does not make such a reference it shall record and communicate to parties concerned its reasons therefore - If it appears that reasons given show that appropriate Govt. took into account any consideration irrelevant or foreign, then Court may in a given case, consider case on a writ of mandamus - Appeal dismissed.
