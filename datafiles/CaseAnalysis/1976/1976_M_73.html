Molu and Others v State of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

23 August 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, P.N. Bhagwati, N.L. Untwalia

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 487; (1976) 4 SCC 362; (1976) SCC (Cr) 636; AIR 1976 SC 2499; 1976 CRLJ 1895; 1976 CRLR 387

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Supreme Court (Enlargement of Criminal Appellate Jurisdiction) Act, 1970

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 302, 325 - Supreme Court (Enlargement of Criminal Appellate Jurisdiction) Act, 1970, s. 2 A - (A) Murder - Conviction - Challenged - PW 7 and PW 8 have come out with straightforward and consistent facts and their evidence contains a ring of truth; they were family members and closest relations of deceased persons and it was quite natural for them to rush to scene of occurrence after hearing alarm given by deceased and this by itself does not detract from testimony of these witnesses - Held, prosecution has been able to prove case against appellants beyond reasonable doubt; since multiple injuries received by deceased persons were caused by blunt weapons like lathis and are of minor character - Nothing to show that accused intended to cause deliberate murder of two deceased persons - However, as all accused appear to have acted together and under a preconceived plan which developed at spot which is clear from fact that they suddenly pounced on deceased and went away together, they must be deemed to have possessed a common intention to assault deceased with knowledge that injuries caused by them were likely to cause death of deceased - Accused have committed an offence u/s. 304 Part II IPC, 1860 and not u/s. 302 - (B) Acquittal of some accused persons - Effect thereof - Held, it is well settled that mere fact that out of many accused some are acquitted is not sufficient to entitle rejection of entire prosecution case; if evidence of eyewitnesses is credit worthy and is believed by court which has placed implicit reliance on them, question whether there is any motive or not becomes wholly irrelevant - Order accordingly.
