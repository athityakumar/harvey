Union of India and Another v Majur Mahajan Mandal and Others

Supreme Court of India

16 December 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

P.K. Goswami, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 603; (1977) SCC (L&S) 172; (1977) 1 SCC 548; AIR 1977 SC 714; 1977 (34) FLR 1; [1977] 106 ITR 412; 1977 LabIC 363; 1977 (2) LLJ 266; 1977 (1) LLN 193; [1977] 2 S.C.R. 472; 1977 UJ 67

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Constitution - Additional Emoluments (Compulsory Deposit) Act 1974, s. 2(b) - Constitution of India, 1950 - Respondent no. 1 had applied to the HC for a writ of mandamus or other suitable order to permanently restrain the respondent no. 2 from effecting any deduction from the arrears of dearness allowance (D.A.) and to permanently restrain the respondent no. 2 from treating the base for calculation of additional D.A. at a rate less than the agreed 100% of the Ahmedabad Rate and to direct the respondent no. 2 not to deduct or deposit 21/2% of D.A. per month payable to each employee treating the same as not being additional D.A. within the meaning of s. 2(b) of the Act and lastly there was a prayer for refund of the amount already deducted by the respondent - The HC allowed the writ application and also granted certificate to appeal to instant court - Appellants claim against that was, since the increased D.A. to the workers was sanctioned after the appointed day, only when the awards were made, the difference between the increased D.A. at 100% of the Ahmadabad Rate and the prevailing rate payable in arrears would be additional D.A. in terms of s. 2(b) of the Act and was, therefore, subject to deduction of 50% of the same and secondly, for future deductions of additional D.A., after the appointed day, the base for calculation of additional D.A. should be 95% of the Ahmadabad Rate of D.A. - Whether, appellant's contentions were sustainable - Held, after considering the facts and the circumstances of the case and provisions of s. 2(b) of the Act along with the other provisions of the Act, instant court was of the view that contentions of the appellants, failed on the solitary ground, namely, that the particular sanction of additional D.A. in instant case was not after the appointed day - Appeal dismissed.
