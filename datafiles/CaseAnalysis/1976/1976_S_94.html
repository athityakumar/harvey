Mani Subrat Jain Etc. v State Of Haryana And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

09 December 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Ray, M. Hameedullah Beg, Jaswant Singh

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 240; (1977) SCC (L&S) 166; (1977) 1 SCC 486; AIR 1977 SC 276; 1977 LabIC 52; [1977] 2 S.C.R. 361; 1977 SLJ 144; 1977 (1) SLR 272; 1977 UJ 89

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Constitution, arts. 226 and 233 - (A) Who can apply for wtit of Mandamus - (B) Appointment of District Judges is exclusive jurisdiction of Govt. - Whether Govt. is bpound to accept recommendations given by High Court - Held, one suffering a legal grievance can ask for a mamdamus and a person can be said to be aggrieved only when a person is denied a legal right by some one who has a legal duty to do something or to abstain from doing something - Government not bound to accept recommendations of HC - Appeals Dismissed.
