State Bank of India v N. Sundaramoney

Supreme Court of India

16 January 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

V.R. Krishna Iyer, Y.V. Chandrachud, A.C. Gupta

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 4; (1976) SCC (L&S) 132; (1976) 1 SCC 822; AIR 1976 SC 1111; 1976 (32) FLR 197; 1976 LabIC 769; 1976 (1) LLJ 478; [1976] 3 S.C.R. 160; 1976 UJ 215

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial laws - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 2(oo) and 25 F - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 133(1) and 136 - (A) Certificate u/art. 133 (1) - Scope of art. 136 - Held, a certificate can be granted only if case involves a question of law - While exercising wider power under art. 136 this Court must have due regard to constitutional limitations on art. 133(1) - (B) Retrenchment - Validity of - Held, s. 25 F (b) provides that no workman employed in any industry who has been in continuous service for not less than one year under an employer shall be retrenched by that employer until he has been paid, at time of retrenchment, compensation which shall be equivalent to fifteen days' average pay for every completed year of service or any part thereof in excess of six months - Word 'terminate' or 'termination' is by itself ambiguous, it can refer to either of two things-either to termination by notice or to termination by efflux of time - Further held, respondent should be put back where he left off, but his new salary will be what he would draw where he to be appointed in same post today de novo - As for benefits, if any, flowing from service he will be ranked below all permanent employees in that cadre and will be deemed to be a temporary hand upto now - Respondent will not be allowed to claim any advantages in matter of seniority or other priority inter se among temporary employees on ground that his retrenchment is being declared invalid by Court - Appeal dismissed.
