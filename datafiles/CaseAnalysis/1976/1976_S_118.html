Shaw Wallace & Co. Ltd. v State Of Tamil Nadu

Supreme Court of India

23 March 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

Jaswant Singh, A.N. Ray

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 614; (1976) 3 SCC 17; AIR 1976 SC 1437; 1976 (5) CTR 299; 1977 (1) MLJ(SC) 11; [1976] 3 S.C.R. 795; 1976 (37) STC 522; 1976 TAXLR 1765; 1976 UJ 397

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Sales Tax - Tamil Nadu General Sales Tax Act, 1959, s. 3 - Exemption from tax - Fertilizers - Assessee claimed exemption on turnover relating to sales of fertilizer mixtures for A/Y 1969-70 and 1970-71 but AO disallowed the same for A/Y 1969-70 and allowed exemption on turnover which represented mixture sold after 6-8-1970, date when amended item 21 of Schedule to the Act came into force - On appeal, appellate authority allowed exemption for turnover which already suffered tax but again assessee filed appeals before Tribunal for both years and State also filed enhancement petitions - Tribunal cancelled relief granted to assessee by appellant authority and HC also dismissed applications of assessee for revision - Hence, instant appeals by special leave - Whether fertilizer mixtures in question could be treated as the same article as chemical fertilizers composing them.

Held, fertilizer mixture is not the same article as the ingredients composing it. It is sold as a different commercial product and is put to different use and has different chemical properties and as such has to be treated as a different article from its component parts. Hence, mixtures produced by assessee are different from their component parts, their properties and uses are also different and they are sold as different commercial products, assessee was not entitled to exemption claimed by it. Appeals disposed of.

Ratio - Only when a chemical fertilizer specified in item no. 21 (1) to (15) of First Schedule is sold in the same condition in which it is purchased that it is not subject to a fresh levy of tax.
