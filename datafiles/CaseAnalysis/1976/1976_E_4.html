Excise Commissioner. U.P., Allahabad v Ram Kumar

Supreme Court of India

05 May 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

Jaswant Singh, A.N. Ray, R.S. Sarkaria, P.N. Shingal

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 217; (1976) SCC (Tax) 360; (1976) 3 SCC 540; AIR 1976 SC 2237; [1976] Supp S.C.R. 532; 1976 UJ 656

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure; Sales Tax

Keywords: Collected, Uttar Pradesh Excise Act, 1910

Summary: Sales Tax - Uttar Pradesh Excise Act, 1910 - Uttar Pradesh Sales Tax Act, 1948, ss.18(d), 24, 31, 29, 40 and 41 - (A) Whether condition incorporated in licenses of respondents that they would lift fixed minimum quantity of liquor and sell same at their allotted shops and in case of their default or failure, they would be liable to compensate amount of excise duty leviable on unlifted quantity is valid and enforceable? - Held, neither s. 28 nor s. 29 nor any other provision of Act authorises levy of amounts sought to be recovered from respondents - Demand made by appellants though disguised as compensation, is in reality a demand for excise duty on unlifted quantity of liquor which is not authorised by provisions of Act - (B) Whether fact that sales of country liquor had been exempted from sales tax vide Notification No. ST 1149/X-802(33)-51 D/- 6-04-1959 could not operate as an estoppel against State Govt.? - There can be on question of estoppel against Govt. in exercise of its legislative, sovereign or executive powers - Held, High Court erred by ignoring that Govt. cannot divest itself of right incidental to its office by conduct which, in case or a private person, would amount to estoppel and in characterizing demand for sales tax made by appellants as illegal - Further held, demand made by appellants from respondents in these in respect of sales tax on turnover of sales of country spirit was valid and could not be struck down - Appeals partly allowed.
