McDowell and Company Limited and Others v Commercial Tax Officer and Others

Supreme Court of India

25 October 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

Jaswant Singh, Hans Raj Khanna

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 601; (1977) SCC (Tax) 198; (1977) 1 SCC 441; AIR 1977 SC 1459; 1977 (6) CTR 79; [1977] 1 S.C.R. 914; 1977 (39) STC 151

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax

Summary: Indirect Tax - Andhra Pradesh General Sales Tax Act, 1957 - Andhra Pradesh Excise Act, 1968 - Excise duty - Paid by buyers - Taxable turnover - Appellants were into business of manufacture of Indian liquors - Buyers of liquor obtained distillery pass for release of liquor after making payment of excise duty and presented same at concerned distillery whereupon bill of sale or invoice was prepared - Said bill did not include excise duty paid by buyer - Appellants' books of accounts also did not contain any reference regarding excise duty paid by purchasers - Appellants paid sales tax in full - After completion of assessments of sales tax under the Act, CTO felt that there was failure to include excise duty paid on aforesaid liquors vended by appellants in their taxable turnover - CTO issued notices to appellants to show cause why assessments to be not reopened - Appellants filed petitions in HC challenging notices - HC dismissed petitions - Whether excise duty or countervailing duty paid directly to excise authorities of State in respect of Indian liquor by buyers could be said to form part of taxable turnover of appellants u/s. 5 of the Act - Held, excise duty or countervailing duty was not charged or received by dealer but was charged by excise authorities and deposited directly by buyers in state exchequer - It could not be said that excise duty or countervailing duty was charged by appellants - Sales tax authorities were not competent to include excise duty and countervailing duty in turnovers of appellants - Excise duty and countervailing duty did not constitute part of turnovers of appellants - SC decision in A.V. Fernandez v. The State of Kerala, 1957 Indlaw SC 18, followed - Appeals allowed.
