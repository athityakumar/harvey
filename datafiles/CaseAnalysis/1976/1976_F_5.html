Wilfred D'Souza v Francis Menino Jesus Ferrao

Supreme Court of India

26 October 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

Hans Raj Khanna, Jaswant Singh

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 357; (1977) 1 SCC 396; AIR 1977 SC 286; [1977] 1 S.C.R. 942; 1976 UJ 999

Case Digest

Subject: Election; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Election - Practice & Procedure - Representation of the People Act, 1951 - Election petition - Dismissed - Challenged - Appellant filed election petition to declare the election of respondent to the Goa Legislative Assembly to be void and to declare instead the appellant to be duly elected - Appellant submitted that the scrutiny and counting of the tendered votes was absolutely necessary in this case, considering the fact that the respondent had been declared the returned candidate after securing in his favour only 2 votes more than the petitioner and the fact that the tendered votes were 10, and that the non-counting of such votes may materially affect the result of the election, in so far as it concerns the respondent, by the improper reception of votes originally polled by persons other than those who tendered their votes - Appellant, therefore, submitted that the votes initially and improperly received should be removed and the tendered votes should be accepted and counted instead - Judicial Commissioner dismissed said petition - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, appellant had adduced prima facie proof in respect of two of the tendered ballot papers, the Judicial Commissioner should call upon the respondent to adduce his evidence. The evidence of the respondent would be confined not merely to the 2 tenders ballot papers in respect of which the appellant had adduced evidence but can also relate to some or all of the other eight tendered ballot papers in respect of which the appellant has not adduced any evidence After the said evidence was examined, Judicial Commissioner would decide the matter in the light of the legal position relating to tendered ballot papers. Impugned order of the Judicial Commissioner was set aside. Appeal allowed.
