Smt. Kasturi Devi v Deputy Director Of Consolidation And Others

Supreme Court of India

04 November 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, Y.V. Chandrachud, P.K. Goswami

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 214; (1976) 4 SCC 674; AIR 1976 SC 2595; 1977 HLR 609; [1977] 2 S.C.R. 25; 1976 UJ 970

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Women & Children

Summary: Family & Personal - U.P. Consolidation of Holdings Act, 1953- Hindu Succession Act, 1956, s. 6 Class I - Inheritance - Re marriage - Divested - Challenged - Appellant along with her son inherited property of her husband after his death - After death of her son, brothers of her husband 'X' and 'Y' claimed share in property as reversioners and appellant also had claimed his son's share and alleged that appellant had remarried to 'Y' and excluded from inheritance and property should pass to next reversioner - Consolidation Officer had negatived claim of appellant and directed mutation in name of 'X' under provisions of 1953 Act - Aggrieved appellant filed appeal before Settlement Officer who reversed finding of Consolidation Officer and held appellant was entitled to be recorded in revenue papers - Revision was filed by 'X' before Deputy Director of Consolidation who set aside order of Consolidation Officer and held appellant was excluded from inheriting property as remarriage with 'Y' was proved - Appellant had filed writ before HC which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether appellant would be disinherited or divested the property even after having acquired an absolute interest under 1956 Act.

Held, appellant as wife might be divested of her interest on her remarriage with 'Y' and dispute arises over the property of her son and appellant's property - Appellant claimed inheritance not as a widow of her husband but as the mother. Deputy Director of Consolidation seemed to think that the bar of inheritance would apply to a mother as such as to a widow and on this ground he refused to accept the claim of the appellant. Therefore unable to agree with the view taken by the Deputy Director of Consolidation which appears to be contrary to the written text of the Hindu Law of Mulla in his 'Hindu Law', 14th Edn, as ' Unchastity and remarriage Unchastity of a mother is no bar to her succeeding as heir to her son, nor does remarriage constitute any such bar.' Thus, application of bar of inheritance to the Hindu widow is based on the special and peculiar, sacred and spiritual relationship of the wife and the husband. After the marriage, the wife becomes an absolute partner and an integral part of her husband and the principle on which she is excluded from inheritance on re-marriage is that when she relinquishes her link with her husband even though he is dead and enters a new family, she is not entitled to retain the property inherited by her. The same, however, cannot be said of a mother. The mother is in an absolutely different position and that is why the Hindu Law did not provide that even the mother would be disinherited if she remarried. Hence, view of the Deputy Director of Consolidation is legally erroneous and HC erred in not interfering with it even though a pure question of law was involved and has failed to exercise jurisdiction vested in it by law. Therefore, the orders of the HC and the Deputy Director of Consolidation are set aside, and the order of the Settlement Officer directing the mutation of the name of appellant is restored. Appeal allowed.
