Veera Ibrahim v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

18 March 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Sarkaria, N.L. Untwalia

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 539; (1976) SCC (Cr) 278; (1976) 2 SCC 302; AIR 1976 SC 1167; 1976 CRLJ 860; 1976 CRLR 165; [1976] 3 S.C.R. 672

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax; Municipalities & Local Governments; Trade

Keywords: Prohibition Act, Import and Export (Control) Act. 1947

Summary: Customs - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 20(3) - Customs Act, 1962, ss. 108, 123(2) and 135 - Import And Export (Control) Act 1947 - Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 21 and 24 - Confession - Contraband goods - Prosecution u/s. 135 - Statement u/s. 108, if hit by art. 20(3) of Constitution and its admissibility u/s. 21 of Evidence Act - Admission of incriminating facts in absence of notification u/s. 123(2), when accused knowingly concurred in a fraudulent attempt at evasion of duty - Held, not attracted, as person is not an 'accused of any offence' and such statements are not barred u/s. 24 as confession but are admissible u/s. 21 as an admission of incriminating facts - Offence u/s. 135, held proved although prosecution could not invoke presumption u/s. 123 - Appeal dismissed.
