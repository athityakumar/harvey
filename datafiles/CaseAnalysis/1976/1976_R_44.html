State of Andhra Pradesh v Rayavarapu Punnayya and Another

Supreme Court of India

15 September 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Sarkaria, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 192; (1976) 4 SCC 382; (1976) SCC (Cr) 659; AIR 1977 SC 45; 1977 CRLJ 1; 1976 CrLR(SC) 485; [1977] 1 S.C.R. 601

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Tort

Keywords: Risk

Summary: Criminal - Murder - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 299, 300, 302, 304, 323, 324 and 325 - Deceased and appellants were members of two rival factions - Respondent pounded hands and legs of deceased - Deceased succumbed to his injuries - Whether respondent is liable to be convicted for murder of deceased? - Held, yes - Murder of deceased was premeditated and respondents had a motive to beat deceased - Grievous injures were inflicted with dangerous weapons and assault on helpless deceased was in most brutal manner - Injuries inflicted were intentional and homicidal - Respondents did not pay any heed to protests of by-standers - Conviction for murder justified - Appeal allowed.
