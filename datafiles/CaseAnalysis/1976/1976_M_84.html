Tara Chandkhatri v Municipal Corporation of Delhi and Others

Supreme Court of India

26 November 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

Jaswant Singh, A.N. Ray, M. Hameedullah Beg

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 346; (1977) SCC (L&S) 151; (1977) 1 SCC 472; AIR 1977 SC 567; 1977 LabIC 55; 1977 (1) LLJ 331; 1977 (1) LLN 428; [1977] 2 S.C.R. 198; 1977 (1) SLR 752

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Municipalities & Local Governments; Service

Keywords: Court Martial

Summary: Municipalities & Local Govts - Delhi Municipal Corporation Act, 1957, ss. 92(1)(b), 95(1) and 491 - Delhi Municipal Corporation service (Control, Appeal) Regulations, 1959, Regn. 7 - Delhi Road Transport Authority Act, 1950 - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 226 - Service Rules - Deputy Commissioner appointed assistant Teacher u/s. 92 (1)(b) as per power delegate to him u/s. 491, by Commr. - Validity of dismissal by Deputy comissioner challengd by writ petition u/art. 226 of Constitution, alleging mala fides - Quasi-judicial authority - Necessary particulars not given in petition - Held, not invalid as Regn. 7 and sch. conferring such power in Deputy Commissioner is not invalid as being inconsistent with s. 95(1) - If should record findings on charge and give reasons for passing order - Dismissal in limine is justified - Appeal dismissed.
