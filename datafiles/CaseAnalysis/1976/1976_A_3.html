Board of Revenue v A. M. Ansari

Supreme Court of India

17 March 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

Jaswant Singh, A.N. Ray, M. Hameedullah Beg

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 59; (1976) SCC (Tax) 350; (1976) 3 SCC 512; AIR 1976 SC 1813; [1976] 3 S.C.R. 661; 1976 (38) STC 577

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Andhra Pradesh Court Fees and Suits Valuation Act, 1956, Transfer of Prisoners Act, 1950, Transfer of Property (Amendment) Supplementary Act, 1929, Indian Stamp (Andhra Pradesh Extension and Amendment) Act (19 of 1959)

Summary: Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 105 - A.P. General Sales Tax Act, 1957, ss. 2(e), 2(bbb) and 5 - Stamp Act, 1899, sch. I, art.35(c) and sch. IA, art.31(c), s. 2(17) - Easements Act, 1882, s. 52 - (A) Lease and Licence - Distinction - In regards to agreement granting right to pluck, cut, carry away and appropriate forest produce - (B) Govt. holding auction sales only annually of its forest produce - Not at frequent intervals - Determination of taxability as business of sale (C) Stamp Act - Mortgage bond - Essential conditions - Sale notice not indicating that any right over or in the security deposit was created in favour of State Govt. - Held, to be a licence and not a lease so as to attract sch. I-A art. 31(c) Stamp Act - Government is not carrying on business of sale of forest produce and is not liable to pay sales tax - As such security deposits are not liable to stamp duty u/art. 35(c) - Appeals dismissed.
