State of Uttar Pradesh v Lalai Singh Yadav

Supreme Court of India

16 September 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

V.R. Krishna Iyer, P.N. Bhagwati, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 585; (1976) 4 SCC 213; (1976) SCC (Cr) 556; AIR 1977 SC 202; 1977 CRLJ 186; 1976 CrLR(SC) 510; [1977] 1 S.C.R. 616

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Question Of Fact And Law

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898, ss. 99A and 99C - Forfeiture of a book captioned 'Ramayan : A True Reading' - Govt. not stating grounds - Whether Govt. order for forfeiting book was justified? - Triple facets of a valid order of forfeiture u/s. 99A are : (i) that book or document contains any matter, (ii) such matter promotes or is intended to promote feelings of enemity or hatred between different classes of citizens of India, (iii) a statement of grounds of Govt's opinion - When grounds are self-evident, silence is whispered speech and law does not demand their separate spelling out as a ritualistic formality - Held, that not stating ground where it's required statutory is lethal sin, this vital ingredient missing, has voided order - Appeal dismissed.
