Controller of Estate Duty, Kerala v R. V. Vishwanathan and Others

Supreme Court of India

21 September 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

Hans Raj Khanna, N.L. Untwalia, Jaswant Singh

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 467; (1977) 1 SCC 90; (1977) SCC (Tax) 142; AIR 1977 SC 463; [1976] 105 ITR 653; [1977] 1 S.C.R. 649

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Property Deemed To Pass

Summary: Estate Duty Act, 1953 - Scope and applicability of s.10 of the Act - Conditions precedent - Where deceased retained only 1/6 share in business and tranferred the all other in favour of his six sons - Held, no question arise for the inclusion 6/7 share or amount in the estate - In order to attract s.10, it is necessary that the possession of the donor of the gifted property must be referable to some contractual or other arrangement enforceable in law or in equity - Court explained the scope, object and applicability of s.10 - Appeal Dismissed.
