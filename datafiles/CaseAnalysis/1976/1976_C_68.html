Cumbum Roadways Private Limited v Balaguru Bus Service Private Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 December 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

V.R. Krishna Iyer, A.N. Ray, M. Hameedullah Beg

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 252; (1977) 1 SCC 440; AIR 1977 SC 1563; 1978 (1) MLJ(SC) 7; [1977] 2 S.C.R. 407; 1977 UJ 63

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1939 - Permit - Appellant and respondent were operators of stage carriers and applied for permit and based on marks secured permit was awarded to respondent - Road Transport Authority(RTA) had preferred candidate with lesser marks on compassionate ground - On appeal Tribunal had set aside this award and granted permit to one who had more merit and affirmed in further appeals - Aggrieved appellant filed writ before HC and contended that his permit should not have been set aside as respondent had got earlier permit on part of route which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, permits cannot be equated with distribution of patronage and must remember that public interest is at stake when public transport services are operated. Thus, scheme of the statute, viz., the Motor Vehicles Act is that he who can serve the travelling public best, is to be chosen as the permit holder. Hence, considerations of grace, charity and compassion at the expense of public interest are an act of unfairness to the Act and concluded that the appellant's claim was rightly rejected and the respondent's award was rightly made. Appeal dismissed.
