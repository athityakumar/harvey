Bindeshwari Prasad Singh v Kali Singh

Supreme Court of India

05 August 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, P.N. Bhagwati

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 588; (1977) SCC (Cr) 33; (1977) 1 SCC 57; AIR 1977 SC 2432; 1978 CRLJ 187; 1977 CrLR(SC) 42; [1977] 1 S.C.R. 125

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 95 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898, ss. 202, 203, 253, 403 and 561A - Slight harm - Criminal proceeding - Case dismissed as complainant was absent and didn't show any interest - Respondent asked court to re-call his order - Transferred to another magistrate - Inquiry recalled and transferred to another magistratre - Whether magistrate can recall a case disposed of by judicial order? - Held, recall of case disposed of by judicial order not possible for any Magistrate- There is no provision in CrPC empowering a Magistrate to review or recall a judicial order passed by him - Appeal allowed.
