Hindustan Steel Limited v Presiding Officer, Labour Court, Orissa and Others

Supreme Court of India

15 September 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

A.C. Gupta, Y.V. Chandrachud, P.K. Goswami

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 190; (1976) 4 SCC 222; (1976) SCC (L&S) 583; AIR 1977 SC 31; 1976 (33) FLR 257; 1976 LabIC 1766; 1977 (1) LLJ 1; [1977] 1 S.C.R. 586; 1977 (1) SLR 199; 1976 UJ 829

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Closure

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 2(oo) and 25 F - Whether three respondents had been retrenched by their employer as found by Labour Court vitiated s. 25F, is correct in law? - Held, s. 25 F(a) lays down that no workman who has been in continuous service for not less than one year under an employer shall be retrenched by that employer unless he has been given one month's notice or wages in lieu of such notice, has a proviso which says that 'no such notice shall be necessary if retrenchment is under an agreement which specifies a date for termination of service' - Clearly, proviso would have been quite necessary if retrenchment as defined in s. 2(oo) was intended not to include termination of service by efflux of time in terms of an agreement between parties - Appeal dismissed
