Lourdes Society Snehanjali Girls Hostel and another v H and R Johnson India Limited and others

Supreme Court of India

02 August 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, T.S. Thakur, R. Banumathi

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 575; (2016) 8 SCC 286; AIR 2016 SC 3572; 2016 (118) ALR 222; 2016 (6) ALT(SC) 13; 2016 (5) Bom.C.R. 30; 2016 (3) CLT(SC) 209; 2016 (3) CPJ(Sc) 27; JT 2016 (7) SC 475; 2016 (3) RCR(Civil) 991; 2016(7) SCALE 546

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection

Keywords: Consumer, District Forum, Consumer Protection Act, 1986, Defective, State Commission, National Commission, Manufacturing, Societies Registration Act, 1860, Complainant

Summary: Consumer Protection - Consumer Protection Act, 1986, s.2(d) - Consumer - Commercial purpose - Appellant-society filed consumer complaint before District Forum against respondents claiming Rs.4,27,712.37 on account of damage caused due to supply of defective tiles by respondent no.1 - District Forum held that tiles supplied by respondents had manufacturing defect and that they committed unfair trade practice and directed respondents to pay Rs. 2,00,000 along with interest - Aggrieved, respondents filed First Appeal before State Commission but the same was dismissed confirming orders of District Forum - Aggrieved, respondents filed Revision Petition before National Commission and appellant-Society made application in said revision petition for awarding sufficient amount of compensation in addition to amount already awarded by District Forum - National commission vide impugned orders reversed findings of District Forum and State Commission by holding that appellant-Society failed to establish that it was a consumer within the meaning of the Act - Hence, instant appeal by appellant-Society - Whether appellant-society is a consumer within the meaning of the Act.

Held, National Commission failed to appreciate the fact that the appellant-society is not a commercial establishment rather a registered society helping adivasi students in their education by providing hostel facilities. The charges, if any, for accommodation in hostel are for maintaining the hostel and not for making profit. Thus, the appellant-society is consumer within the meaning of the term 'consumer' u/s. 2(d) of the Act. Further, National Commission exceeded its jurisdiction by setting aside concurrent finding of fact recorded in the order passed by State Commission which is based upon valid and cogent reasons. Hence, impugned order of National Commission is set aside and order of District Forum as affirmed by State Commission is restored. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - National Commission has to exercise jurisdiction vested in it only if the State Commission or the District Forum has either failed to exercise their jurisdiction or exercised when the same was not vested in them or exceeded their jurisdiction by acting illegally or with material irregularity.
