Harijan Paniben Dudabhai v State of Gujarat and others

Supreme Court of India

01 July 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Uday Umesh Lalit, V. Gopala Gowda

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 430; AIR 2016 SC 3066; JT 2016 (6) SC 216

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Panchayats, Gujarat Panchayats Act, 1961, Gram Panchayat Service (Classification and Recruitment) Rules, 1967, Gujarat Panchayat Service (Appointing Authorities) Rules, 1967, Gujarat Panchayat Service (Conditions of Service) Rules, 1977

Summary: Municipalities & Local Governments - Gujarat Panchayats Act, 1961, s. 203 - Gram Panchayat Service (Classification and Recruitment) Rules, 1967 - Gujarat Panchayat Service (Appointment Authorities) Rules, 1967 - Members of Panchayat Service - Eligibility for family pension and gratuity - Upon conversion of Okha District Municipality into Okha Gram and Nagar Panchayat, existing staff of municipality was allocated to Gram Panchayat and treated as part of Panchayat service - While so, husband of appellant, who was employed in Gram Panchayat, died in harness and appellant claimed family pension and gratuity but the same was rejected - Hence, appellant moved HC but Single Judge of HC dismissed the same - On appeal, DB of HC dismissed appeal along with other connected matters observing that only those employees who had been appointed following procedure laid in s.203 of the Act and rules framed there under, would alone be members of Panchayat Service, apart from allocated employees from municipality to the Panchayats at the time of formation of the Panchayats or such other employees who had been recognized as members of Panchayat Service by State Govt. or by the DPSC - Hence, instant appeal - Whether deceased could be treated as member of Panchayat service.

Held, in the year 1964 when deceased came to be appointed, there were no rules governing the appointment in question. The rules regulating 'Superior Panchayat Service' and 'Inferior Panchayat Service' in form of Gram Panchayat Service (Classification and Recruitment) Rules, 1967 came on the statute book in the year 1967 and as per 1967 Rules, Gram Panchayat is the appropriate authority in respect of posts included in the Local Cadre. Hence, there is no infraction in the appointment of deceased, who was appointed pursuant to a resolution passed by Panchayat. Further, as per reply affidavit of respondent no.5, deceased was holding post within the sanctioned set up of Safai Kamdars and was full time employee getting regular salary. At no stage, while he was in service any objection or even doubt was raised that he was not validly appointed. Hence, deceased was regularly appointed and there is no infirmity or illegality in his appointment so as to disentitle the family of the benefits of family pension and gratuity. Hence, orders of HC are set aside and Civil Application filed by appellant is allowed. All other appeals filed by other appellants are also allowed. Appeals disposed of.
