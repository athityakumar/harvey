Hindustan Unilever Limited v State of Rajasthan and another

Supreme Court of India

12 April 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Shiva Kirti Singh, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 417; (2016) 7 SCC 474; AIR 2016 SC 2121; 2016 CRLJ 2676; 2016 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 1868; 2016 (2) RCR(Criminal) 807; [2016] 2 S.C.R. 401

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Prevention of Food Adulteration Act, 1954, Prayer, Public Analyst, Prevention of Food Adulteration Rules, 1955, Analyst, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Health & Drug - Prevention of Food Adulteration Act, 1954, ss. 13,16 - Prevention of Food Adulteration Rules, 1955, r. 50(1) - Re-analysis of sample - Benefit to all accused - Complaint was filed against all accused including appellant u/s.16 of the Act and r.50(1) of the Rules alleging adulteration of products - While so, one co-accused exercised his right u/s. 13(2) of the Act and so, order was passed to send second sample for re-analysis - However, CFL gave report that sample was deteriorated and not fit for analysis and so, directions were passed to prosecution to produce third sample - While so, appellant moved HC for quashing of complaint but the same was dismissed by HC vide impugned order - Hence, instant appeal - Whether complaint could be quashed.

Held, when once right is exercised u/s.13(2) of the Act by one of co-accused leading to certificate from CFL, then benefit of suppression of earlier report of Public Analyst enure to all co-accused. Further, in the prevailing situation, it would be waste of time and an empty formality to get the third sample also declared as deteriorated by CFL. Furthermore, in cases where there are more than one accused, there is no possibility of complying with individual prayer of all the co-accused to send different samples for re-analysis by CFL because Statute requires preparation of only 3 samples. Hence, view taken by HC is erroneous and contrary to law and so, impugned order of HC is set aside and prayer of appellant before HC for quashing of criminal complaint is allowed. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - In cases where there are many accused, once right is exercised u/s. 13(2) of the Act by any of accused leading to a certificate from CFL, the consequence would be suppression of the report given earlier by Public Analyst u/s.13(1) of the Act and such suppression enure to the benefit of all the co-accused.
