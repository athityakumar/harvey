Brij Lal v State of Rajasthan

Supreme Court of India

17 August 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Jagdish Singh Khehar, Arun Mishra

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 614; 2016 (10) ADJ 82; AIR 2016 SC 3875; 2016 CRLJ 4832; 2016 (4) JCC 2485; JT 2016 (8) SC 183; 2016 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 3416; 2017 (1) MLJ(Crl) 290; 2016 (3) RCR(Criminal) 1044; 2016(8) SCALE 83; [2016] 7 S.C.R. 176

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Presumption, Indian Evidence Act, 1872, Proof, Spot, Place Of Occurrence, Harm, Version, Reliance, Blood, Firing, Arms Act, 1959, Private Defence, Employed, Right Of Private Defence, Distance, Lathi, Villagers, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, Plea

Summary: (A) Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Arms Act, 1959, ss. 25, 27 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 313 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 105, 96 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 300, 302, 307, 324, 34, 96 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant/accused along with co-accused who was armed with pistol allegedly fired at deceased persons who died on the spot - Appellant was charged u/ss. 302, 307 and 324 r/w s. 34 IPC and ss. 25 and 27 of the Act - Trial Court acquitted appellant by accepting the plea of self-defence raised by him by invoking the second exception u/s. 300 IPC - Aggrieved, State filed writ petition before HC challenging impugned judgment of Trial Court - HC allowed writ petition and found appellant guilty of having committed the offence punishable u/s. 302 IPC and awarded sentence of life imprisonment to appellant - Hence, instant appeal - Whether appellant had fired gunshots at the mob of villagers only as a matter of self-defence, when appellant and co-accused, had been attacked.

Held, no benefit can be derived by appellant on the legal position expressed by SC with reference to the plea of self-defence. Herein, there is no evidence to demonstrate, that accused- and co-accused were actually attacked and it was as a matter of self-defence that they fired at the crowd, with their pistols. Therefore SC find no merit in the contention that appellant had fired gunshots at the mob of villagers only as a matter of self-defence, when appellant and co-accused, had been attacked. Appeal dismissed.

(B) Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Arms Act, 1959, ss. 25, 27 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 313 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 105, 96 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 300, 302, 307, 324, 34, 96 - Murder - Prosecution version - Intention - Whether entire prosecution version discloses that alleged intention of appellant was to murder PW15.

Held, reason why neighbours and co-villagers had gathered at the place of occurrence was to protect PW15, by dissuading accused from insisting on to carry out their objective. Consequent upon their being angered by villagers, they retaliated by firing indiscriminately at the gathering. Since it was not disputed by accused that three fatal (besides other) injuries, were caused by accused and his co-accused, onus lies on appellant to demonstrate the reason and justification for their action. The evidence produced by prosecution demonstrates that accused had fired gunshots indiscriminately, on being angered by gathering, which was trying to persuade them from carrying out their singular objective - to cause harm to the person of PW15. Having accepted, that they had actually fired at neighbours and villagers, who had gathered at the place of occurrence, it does not lie in their mouth to raise such a plea. Appeal dismissed.

(C) Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Arms Act, 1959, ss. 25, 27 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 313 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 105, 96 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 300, 302, 307, 324, 34, 96 - Murder - Recovery of weapon - Evidenced - Whether recovery of weapon, namely, gun, with which accused had shot at the crowd was not proved to have been recovered from appellant.

Held, one of the recovery witnesses had deposed, that gun recovered at the instance of accused was found wrapped when it was dug out. First and foremost, such a plea could have been raised only if appellant had been in denial and had adopted the stance that he had not fired at the crowd at the time of occurrence. Since that is not his plea, the instant submission is wholly misconceived. Secondly, the factum of recovery has been substantiated by the prosecution through the statements of PW1 and PW15. Even the signatures of accused were obtained on the "mazhar" prepared at the time of recovery. In such view of the matter, whether or not recovered gun was found without any covering, or in a wrapped condition, when the same was dug out, at the instance of accused makes no difference, whatsoever. Appeal dismissed.

(D) Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Arms Act, 1959, ss. 25, 27 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 313 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 105, 96 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 300, 302, 307, 324, 34, 96 - Murder - Acquittal - Sustainability - Whether acquittal of co-accused who was separately tried was valid.

Held, in instant case most of prosecution witnesses had resiled and did not identify co-accused as the person involved in occurrence. The position in the present case is just reverse. All the relevant prosecution witnesses, duly identified appellant. It is therefore not possible for SC to accept, that appellant deserves to be acquitted because of acquittal of co-accused in the separate trial conducted against him. Appeal dismissed.

(E) Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Arms Act, 1959, ss. 25, 27 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 313 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 105, 96 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 300, 302, 307, 324, 34, 96 - Murder - Place of occurrence - Validity - Whether as a consequence of aggressive attitude of neighbours and co-villagers who had gathered at the place of occurrence accused and co-accused were pushed back to a distance of about 200 feet from the house of PW1.

Held, prosecution has clearly demonstrated through the testimony recorded on oath, that none of the persons gathered at the place of occurrence was armed in any manner. It is also apparent that crowd gathered at the place of occurrence was comprised of men, women and children. The fact, that there was a distance of about 17 to 18 feet between accused and villagers shows that there was no real threat to him when he opened firing at unarmed gathering including women and children. It was only because of their desire to retaliate against crowd, consequent upon crowd having gathered to protect PW15 cannot be a satisfactory reason for appellant to fire gunshots indiscriminately. Appeal dismissed.

(F) Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Arms Act, 1959, ss. 25, 27 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 313 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 105, 96 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 300, 302, 307, 324, 34, 96 - Murder - Self-defense - Sustainability - Whether PW15 was also a part of crowd which accused and co-accused were facing and as such, he ought to have fired at him, rather than at the other members of crowd.

Held, accused did not even make the above suggestion to prosecution witnesses when they were being cross-examined on his behalf. Moreover, the actual suggestion given was, that accused had come to a general merchant shop to buy "biris" (traditional cigarettes), and that, they never come to the place of occurrence, or that, they had any intention to harm PW15. HC relied upon cogent evidence to set aside the order of acquittal passed by Trial Court. SC was also satisfied in recording that Trial Court had overlooked vital evidence recorded on behalf of prosecution, specially during cross-examination of prosecution witnesses, whereupon, the position of there being any second way of viewing the facts was absolutely out of question. SC was of considered view that statements of two prosecution witnesses, namely, PW1 and PW15, along with testimony of other witnesses would clearly and unequivocally lead to inference that accused was guilty of having committed the offence u/s. 302 IPC, insofar as his having caused the murders of deceased were concerned. There is absolutely no question of extending the benefit of any doubt to accused in instant case. Appeal dismissed.
