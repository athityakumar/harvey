Delhi Development Authority v Prithi Pal Singh and others

Supreme Court of India

18 February 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Kurian Joseph, R.F. Nariman

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 146; JT 2016 (2) SC 313; 2016 (2) RCR(Civil) 458

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property

Keywords: Constitution of India, 1950, Land Acquisition Act, 1894, Right to Fair Compensation and Transparency in Land Acquisition, Rehabilitation and Resettlement Act, 2013

Summary: Land & Property - Administrative - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, s. 48 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 148 - Land acquisition - Denotification - Appellant authority acquired respondents' land - Respondent No. 1 requested appellant to denotify his land u/s. 48 of the Act - Appellant turned down respondent No. 1's request - Respondent filed writ petition - HC remitted matter to appellant for reconsideration - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, the award is of 1979. History of the litigation spanning over to more than three decades. The disputed land which has been lying unused for last around 40 years would continue to be so while the land is badly required if not for the housing scheme for many other public purposes. The concerned Municipal Corporation has also submitted on the need for some part if not whole of the land. Therefore, to balance equities between the parties and to put an end to the entire litigation, be it for the compensation, be it for de-notification or for that matter anything touching on road pertaining to the disputed land, and thus for doing complete justice, to invoke art. 142 of the Constitution.

Direction issued to the appellant to limit its claim to 3000 square meters and leave the rest to respondent No. 1. However, while leaving the balance to the respondent No. 1, the appellant should also see that the respondent No. 1 gets access to the main road and that he is in a position to utilize the property for any of the purposes as permitted under law. Appeal dispose of.
