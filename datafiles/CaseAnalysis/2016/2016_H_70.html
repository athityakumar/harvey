R. Rachaiah v Home Secretary, Bangalore

Supreme Court of India

05 May 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, R.K. Agrawal

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 972; 2016 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 2705; 2016(5) SCALE 692; [2016] 3 S.C.R. 379

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Suicide, Marriage, Dead Body, Prosecutor, Alternative, Registered, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 216, 217 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss.34, 302, 306, 364 - Alteration of charge - Cross examination of witnesses - Accused appellants were convicted by Trial Court for offences punishable u/ss. 302 and 364 read with s. 34 of IPC and on appeal HC affirmed conviction and sentence recorded by Trial Court - Hence, instant appeals - Whether conviction of accused appellants is sustainable.

Held, the accused persons were initially charged for an offence u/s.306 i.e., abetting suicide allegedly committed by deceased and after investigation, it was alleged by prosecution that all three accused aided and abetted said suicide. On such specific charge, 26 witnesses were examined and also cross-examined by appellants but at the end of the trial, the charge is altered with alternative charge with framing of charge u/s.302 of IPC. However, such addition and substitution of charge is bound to create prejudice to appellants and so, it has to be treated as original charge. Further, it was incumbent upon prosecution to re-call the witnesses, examine them in the context of the charge u/s.302 of IPC and allow the accused persons to cross-examine those witnesses. However, nothing of that sort happened and only one official witness was examined on the day when alternative charge was framed. The case was not even adjourned as mandatorily required u/s.216 of CrPC. Hence, trial which is conducted and on the basis of which conviction is recorded u/s.302 of IPC is clearly vitiated as the same is in violation of the mandatory procedure prescribed u/ss.216 and 217 of CrPC. Further, as the accused have already suffered incarceration for more than eight years, sentence is reduced to the period already undergone and appellants are directed to be released. Appeals disposed of.

Ratio - As per s.216(3) of CrPC, with the alteration or addition to a charge if any prejudice is going to be caused to the accused in his defence or the prosecutor in the conduct of the case, the Court has to proceed with the trial as if it altered or added the original charge by terming the additional or alternative charge as original charge. Further, u/s.217 of CrPC, whenever a charge is altered or added by Court after the commencement of the trial, the prosecutor as well as the accused could be allowed to recall or re-summon or examine any witnesses who have already been examined with reference to such alteration or addition.
