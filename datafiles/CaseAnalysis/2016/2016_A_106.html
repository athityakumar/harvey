President/Secretary, J. K. Synthetics Mazdoor Union (CITU), Indira Gandhi Nagar, Kota and others v Arfat Petrochemicals Private Limited and others

Supreme Court of India

18 November 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

L. Nageswara Rao, Anil R. Dave

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 838; (2017) 1 SCC 427; AIR 2016 SC 5261; 2017 (120) ALR 296; 2017 (1) BC 146; [2017] 200 Comp Cas 80; 2017 (1) DRTC 305; 2017 (1) DRTC 609; JT 2016 (11) SC 172; 2017 (2) MPLJ 275; 2016(12) SCALE 111

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate

Keywords: BIFR, Jurisdiction, Meeting, Dispose, Industrial Company, Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, Sick Industrial Companies (Special Provisions) Act, 1985, Sick Industrial Company, Rehabilitation Scheme, Revival

Summary: Corporate - Sick Industrial Companies (Special Provisions) Act, 1985, s. 22A - Sanctioned scheme - Implementation - Jurisdiction - Appellants filed instant appeals challenging impugned order of HC wherein HC allowed writ petition of respondent no. 1 holding that Board and Appellate authority do not have jurisdiction to issue directions to a company which was not a sick industrial company u/s. 22 A of the Act.

Held, Board could issue a direction not to dispose of assets only to a sick industrial company. There was no dispute that respondent no. 1 was not a sick industrial company and that it purchased the assets from a sick industrial company in accordance with the Sanctioned Scheme. Board was not correct in passing an order of status quo and directing respondent no. 1 not to alienate/transfer the assets by its orders. SC agree with the findings of HC in impugned judgment that Board does not have competence to issue directions to a company which was not a sick industrial company u/s. 22A of the Act. It was open to Board to review the implementation of Sanctioned Scheme and pass suitable directions. Appellate Authority held that respondent no. 2 has no liability in respect of Kota units which have been sold to respondent no. 1. The said findings were not challenged by respondent no. 1 in writ petition filed in HC. HC set aside the entire order without taking note of findings in favour of respondent no. 2. The petition filed for clarification by respondent no. 2 was also dismissed by HC. HC ought not to have disturbed the findings in favour of respondent no. 2 as they were not in challenge in writ petition filed by respondent no. 1. Appeals disposed of.
