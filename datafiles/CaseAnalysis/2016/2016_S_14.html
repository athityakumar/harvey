V. L. S. Finance Limited v S. P. Gupta and another

Supreme Court of India

05 February 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, N.V. Ramana

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 152; (2016) 3 SCC 736; AIR 2016 SC 721; 2016 ALL MR (Cri) 1300; 2016 CRLJ 1296; 2016 (2) CTC 547; 2016 (2) JCC 926; JT 2016 (3) SC 6; 2016 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1370; 2016 (2) MLJ(Crl) 111; 2016 (1) RCR(Criminal) 934; 2016(2) SCALE 164; [2016] 1 S.C.R. 623

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: FIR, Jurisdiction, Production, Single Judge, Permission, Communication, Review, Summons, CONSTITUTION, Challenge, Secretary, Recommendation, Dealing, Assistant Public Prosecutor, Interfere, Screening, Screening Committee, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Constitution of India, 1950, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 321- Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120B, 406, 409, 420, 424, 467, 468, 471, 477A - Withdrawal of application for withdrawal from prosecution - Authority of Assistant Public Prosecutor - Determination - Appellant filed FIR u/ss. 406, 409, 420, 424, 467, 468, 471, 477, 120B of IPC - Magistrate took cognizance of offences in question and summoned accused persons - Order of issuing summons was assailed before HC along with prayer for quashing of FIR - Further, Committee was formed which recommended for withdrawal of cases which included case against respondent accused person - Further, recommendation of Committee was approved by Lt. Governor and application was filed seeking withdrawal of cases by Assistant Public Prosecutor averring that facts of case clearly showed that it was indicating commercial transaction between parties but same had culminated into criminal offence - However, Director of Prosecution communicated to Principal Secretary, Home Ministry, stating that on further perusal of charge-sheet in case of accused it was found that there was sufficient evidence on record to establish charges against accused persons and public prosecutor should be requested accordingly - Lt. Governor agreed with proposal not to press applications for withdrawal of cases and issued letters - Petition was filed by respondent against order passed by Lt. Governor - Further, lower Court held that there was no basis for petitioners to contend that decision of Assistant Public Prosecutor to file application u/s. 321 of CrPC was taken independently by him - Assistant Public Prosecutor filed application for withdrawal of earlier application for withdrawal of prosecution - Lower Court vide impugned order has set aside order granting withdrawal of application u/s. 321 of CrPC - After remand of matter, Magistrate declined permission to withdraw application - Whether Assistant Public Prosecutor is entitled under law to file application for withdrawal of application for withdrawal of application preferred u/s.321 of CrPC and not to press application for withdrawal.

Held, Writ Court had not found any fault with instructions given by Govt. not to press application for withdrawal. Writ Court had not opined with regard to role of Public Prosecutor in not pressing application. Further, HC disposing of appeal opined that decision by Lt. Governor directing to withdraw application was justified and said order had attained finality after SLP's assailing same stood dismissed. Further, HC on earlier occasion had only observed that accused persons had right to pursue matter further and to raise all issues available to them in appropriate proceedings. Further, SC held that vide impugned order lower court held that it is Public Prosecutor who has to take decision and not Govt. or Lt. Governor and so that dismissal of petition against grant of consent by Lt. Governor to withdrawal of application u/s. 321 of CrPC which is erroneous particularly when right to pursue remedies before criminal courts was preserved while deciding petition. SC held that Public Prosecutor having been satisfied, as application would show, had filed application and said application was not taken up for hearing. Magistrate had not passed any order granting consent for withdrawal. Further, SC held that Assistant Public Prosecutor has re-appreciated facts, applied his mind to totality of facts and filed application for not pressing application preferred earlier u/s. 321 of CrPC filing of application not to press application cannot be compared with any kind of review of order passed by court. Further, court cannot say that Public Prosecutor has no legal authority to file application for not pressing earlier application. Further, SC held that if anyone is aggrieved for case instituted against accused persons on his FIR is sought to be withdrawn and accused persons have no role, hence, HC could not have quashed orders permitting prosecution to withdraw application and granting such liberty to accused persons. Hence, impugned order passed by lower Courts and consequentially order passed by Magistrate is set aside. Appeals allowed.
