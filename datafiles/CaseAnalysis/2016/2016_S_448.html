Mukarrab etc v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

30 November 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

R. Banumathi, A.K. Sikri

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 870; (2017) 2 SCC 210; 2017 CRLJ 1013; JT 2016 (11) SC 345; 2017 (1) RCR(Criminal) 103; 2016(12) SCALE 379; [2016] 8 S.C.R. 557

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Presumption, Wrist, Forensic, Birth Certificate, Juvenile, Medicine, Proof, Age, Medical Examination, Medical Opinion, Two Views, Medical Report, Birth, Juvenile Justice (Care And Protection Of Children) Act, 2000, Ossification Test, Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Rules, 2007, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Inquiry, Plea

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 148, 149 - Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act 2000 - Reliability on Medical opinion as to age of accused - Ossification test - Appellants along with four others quarreled with deceased and later, attacked deceased with guns and knives and killed him - Upon perusal of evidence, Trial Court convicted all accused persons u/s.302 read with ss.148, 149 of IPC - Aggrieved, accused persons filed appeals before HC but HC disposed of all three appeals vide common judgment and order dt.27-5-2014 affirming conviction of accused persons and sentence imposed thereof - Aggrieved, accused persons filed SLP before SC but SC dismissed SLP of other accused and issued notice qua appellants as appellants raised claim of juvenility before SC - SC directed Trial Court to examine aspect of juvenility of appellants and submit report and accordingly, inquiry was conducted and report was submitted wherein, claim of juvenility raised by appellants was rejected - However, as certain doubts were raised on said report, ossification test was conducted on appellants wherein it was opined that age of appellants ranges between 35-40 yrs on date of examination - Whether the opinion of the Medical Board of AIIMS determining the age of the appellants between 35-40 years, could be accepted.

Held, appellants have certainly crossed the age of thirty years when ossification test was done which is an important factor to be taken into account as age could not be determined with precision. Further, ossification test could not be regarded as conclusive when it comes to ascertaining the age of a person. Babloo Pasi v. State of Jharkhand and another, 2008 INDLAW SC 1590 and State of Madhya Pradesh v. Anoop Singh, 2015 INDLAW SC 454, followed. Further, in medical reports of appellants, it was stated that there was no indication for dental x-rays since both the accused were beyond 25 years of age. As such, the age determination based on ossification test though useful is not conclusive. An X-ray ossification test could by no means be so infallible and accurate a test as to indicate the correct number of years and days of person's life. Further, appellants were involved in commission of various offences, appellant-Mukarrab between years 1988 - 1995 and appellant-Arshad between years 1993 - 2003 and proceedings are still pending against appellants in those cases. Relying on medical report, had appellants been born in 1979, then in the years when first offence was committed by them, appellant-Mukarrab would have only in age of 9,10,11 and appellant-Arshad only 14 years and when appellants were produced in those cases then they would have been considered as children by the very appearance. They would have been dealt with accordingly by concerned juvenile court and the matter would not have been kept pending till date. Hence, under this reason also, opinion of Medical Board determining age of appellant as 35-40 years in May, 2016 could not be relied upon. As such, considering the facts and circumstances of the case, the opinion of medical board in determining the age of appellants could not be relied upon so as to give benefit under the provisions of the Act. In the absence of other cogent evidence, the plea of juvenility of the appellants is rejected and appeals are dismissed. Appeals disposed of.

Ratio - Medical evidence as to the age of a person though a very useful guiding factor is not conclusive and has to be considered along with other circumstances.
