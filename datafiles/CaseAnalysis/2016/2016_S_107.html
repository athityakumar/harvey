Ajay Arjun Singh v Sharadendu Tiwari and others

Supreme Court of India

15 March 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

J. Chelameswar, A.M. Sapre

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 224; (2016) 6 SCC 576; AIR 2016 SC 1417; 2016(6) ALL MR 450; 2016 (3) AWC 2637; 2016 (2) CLT(SC) 24; 2016 (2) JabLJ 99; JT 2016 (3) SC 164; 2016 (2) MLJ 667; 2016 (2) RCR(Civil) 528; 2016(3) SCALE 310; [2016] 4 S.C.R. 555

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Keywords: Dismissed, PARLIAMENT & LEGISLATURE, Candidate, Representation Of The People Act, 1951, Conduct of Elections Rules, 1961, High Court of Madhya Pradesh Rules, 2008, Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, Constitution of India, 1950, People (Conduct of Elections and Election Petitions) Rules, 1956

Summary: Election - Representation of the People Act, 1951, s.83(1)(c) - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O.7 r.11 - Filing of affidavit - Election petitioner filed election petition before HC challenging election of Returned Candidate as MLA on ground that said Returned Candidate was guilty of commission of two corrupt practices as defined u/ss. 123(a) and 123(6) of the Act - While so, Returned Candidate filed application under O.7 r.11 of CPC for dismissal of election petition but HC dismissed said application and aggrieved by the same, Returned Candidate filed application for review of said order but the same was also dismissed by HC - Aggrieved by said orders of HC, SLPs were preferred by both Election Petitioner and Returned Candidate - Whether election petition was accompanied by affidavit complying with requirement of statute under proviso to s. 83(1)(c) of the Act.

Held, Election Petitioner in his reply to O.7 r.11 petition took a stand by way of abundant caution that if the Court comes to a conclusion that his affidavit is found to be defective for any reason, then he is willing to file further affidavit to cure the defect. However, HC without examining question whether affidavit at pg nos.394-395 satisfied the requirement of Form 25 and also without recording definite finding in that regard, simply recorded conclusion that the defect is curable and the same could be cured by filing affidavit in Form 25. Further, Returned Candidate could not shift his case from stage to stage and could not be permitted to raise such question of fact in the absence of appropriate pleading and contend that election petitioner is precluded from arguing that he had filed 2nd affidavit along with election petition by pressing into service rule of estoppel. Hence, appeals of Returned Candidate are dismissed and that of Election Petitioner is allowed. Appeals disposed of.
