Amanullah and another v State of Bihar and others

Supreme Court of India

12 April 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, Uday Umesh Lalit

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 266; (2016) 6 SCC 699; AIR 2016 SC 1871; 2016 (4) CalLT 13; 2016 CRLJ 2346; 2016 (3) JCC 1915; JT 2016 (4) SC 92; 2016 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 1870; 2016(4) SCALE 119; [2016] 2 S.C.R. 1027

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: FIR, Jurisdiction, Limitation, Inherent Power, Right Of Appeal, Subordinate, Criminal Proceedings, Reliance, Abuse, Quash, Cognizance, Private Party, Arms Act, 1959, Registered, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Husband, Constitution of India, 1950, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, Complainant

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Arms Act, 1959, ss. 25, 26 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120B, 302, 34 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 482 - Cognizance of offence for murder - Criminal proceeding quashed - Justification - It is stated that informant deceased's husband had gone to stay at his relatives place when he was informed by respondent's uncle that his wife was found lying in field with her mouth and nose tied and she died on way to hospital - FIR was lodged by informant against respondent persons u/ss. 302, 120B read with s. 34 of IPC - Further, it is alleged that informant accused threatened appellant witness with weapon - Lower court took cognizance against informant and other accused-persons, however, HC vide impugned order set aside cognizance order passed by lower Court and quashed criminal prosecution - Respondent accused persons contended that appellant has no locus standi to file instant appeal.

Held, with respect to locus standi of appellant to file appeal, appellants have connection with matter at hand as appellant no.1 was threatened by informant and appellant no.2 was falsely implicated by informant in case of murder of his wife. Hence, appellant have locus to maintain instant appeal. Further, with respect to impugned order, SC held that at stage of taking cognizance, court should not get into merits of case made out by police and court's duty is limited to extent of finding out whether from material placed before it, offence alleged therein against accused is made out or not with view to proceed further with case. SC held that HC ignored important facts, that appellant was threatened by accused and FIR was registered against informant under Act. Further, there are statements of various witnesses to effect that deceased has been murdered by informant. SC held that evidence collected by IO was duly considered by lower Court before taking cognizance, hence, HC exceeded its jurisdiction u/s. 482 of CrPC. Impugned order passed by HC is set aside and matter is remitted to lower Court for proceeding further in accordance with law. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - HC is not justified to exercise its inherent jurisdiction for quashing of criminal proceeding when allegations made against accused person show a prima-facie case.
