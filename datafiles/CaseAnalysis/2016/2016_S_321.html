Dr. Rini Johar and another v State of Madhya Pradesh and others

Supreme Court of India

03 June 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, Shiva Kirti Singh

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 405; AIR 2016 SC 2679; 2016 ALL MR (Cri) 3113; 2016 CRLJ 3156; 2016 (2) JabLJ 320; 2016 (3) JCC 1673; JT 2016 (6) SC 20; 2016 (3) KLT 502; 2016 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 2504; 2016 (3) MLJ(Crl) 501; 2016 (2) OLR 486; 2016 (3) RCR(Criminal) 300; 2016(5) SCALE 780; [2016] 5 S.C.R. 579

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: FIR, Machine, Preliminary Enquiry, Seizure Memo, Doctor, Diary, Interrogation, Case Diary, Information Technology Act, 2000, Code of Criminal Procedure (Amendment) Act, 2008, Registered, Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Constitution of India, 1950, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, Inquiry, Seizure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 41, 41-A, 482 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 420 - Information Technology Act, 2000, s.66-D - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 21 - Quashing of proceedings - Arrest and Seizure - Validity - Respondent no.8 filed complaint before IG, Cyber Cell alleging that petitioner no.1 and another had committed fraud of US $ 10,500 and basing on said complaint, FIR u/ss. 420 and 34 of IPC and u/s. 66-D of the Act was registered against petitioners and petitioners were arrested and later released on bail - While so, petitioners filed application for discharge and Trial Court passed order discharging petitioners for offence punishable u/s.66-D of the Act but opined that there is prima facie case for offence punishable u/s.66-A(b) of the Act read with ss. 420 and 34 of IPC - Hence, instant writ petition - Whether arrest of petitioners was valid and legal.

Held, report clearly shows there have been number of violations in the arrest and seizure and the arrest of the petitioners was not made by following the procedure of arrest. Further, s. 41-A of CrPC as interpreted by SC was also not followed. Hence, dignity of petitioners, a doctor and a practicing advocate was seriously jeopardized. The two ladies were arrested without following the procedure and put in the compartment of a train without being produced before the local Magistrate from Pune to Bhopal. Further, there was violation of art.21 of Constitution and petitioners were compelled to face humiliation. They have been treated with an attitude of insensibility. There are violation of mandate of law enshrined u/ss.41 and 41-A of CrPC. Hence, petitioners are granted Rs.5,00,000 each towards compensation to be paid by State. Further, dispute is purely of a civil nature, but a maladroit effort was made to give it a criminal colour. No ingredient of s.420 of IPC is remotely attracted and even if it is wrong, the complainant has to take recourse to civil action. The case does not fall in the categories where cognizance of offence could be taken by court and accused could be asked to face trial. The entire case projects a civil dispute and nothing else. Hence, proceedings are quashed which are initiated at instance of respondent no.8 and order negativing prayer for discharge of accused persons is set aside and prosecution initiated against petitioners quashed. Petition allowed.
