State of Gujarat and another v Lal Singh @ Manjit Singh and others

Supreme Court of India

29 June 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, Shiva Kirti Singh

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 501; (2016) 8 SCC 370; AIR 2016 SC 3197; 2016 (5) ALT(SC) 11; 2017 (1) G.L.R. 89; JT 2016 (6) SC 519; 2017 (1) MLJ(Crl) 28; 2016 (3) RCR(Criminal) 505; 2016(6) SCALE 105; [2016] 4 S.C.R. 816

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Life Imprisonment, Habeas Corpus, Parole, Jurisdiction, Review, President, Prayer, Imprisonment For Life, Jail, Appropriate Government, Conservation Of Foreign Exchange And Prevention Of Smuggling Activities Act, 1974, Dealing, Prisoner, Remission, Jail Manual, Arms Act, 1959, Explosive Substances Act, 1908, Commutation, Terrorist And Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Act, 1987, Transfer of Prisoners Act, 1950, Prisons Act, 1894, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Constitution of India, 1950, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Arms Act, 1959 - Terrorist and Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Act, 1987, ss. 3(3) - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 432, 433 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 120B(1) - Grant of Parole - Respondent no.1 along with 20 other accused were tried in TADA cases and Trial Court convicted respondent no.1 and others for offences punishable u/ss. 3(3), 5 of 1987 Act, u/s. 120B(1) of IPC and s.25(1A) of 1959 Act and imposed sentence of punishment - Respondent no.1 filed appeal before SC but the same was dismissed confirming conviction and sentence imposed by Trial Court - While so, pending appeal before SC, respondent no.1 sought transfer from Central Prison, Ahmedabad to Central Prison, Jalandhar and State Govt. consented to such transfer - Later, respondent no.1 sought premature release u/s. 432 of CrPC but the same was rejected by State - Aggrieved, respondent no.1 filed writ petition before HC which was disposed of with directions to State Govt. to reconsider the case of respondent no.1 for premature release considering applicability of s. 433 of CrPC, s. 3 of 1987 Act - However, State Govt. again rejected application of respondent no.1 - Later, in pursuance of directions of HC, State Govt. reconsidered case of respondent no.1 but again rejected proposal for release of respondent no.1 - Aggrieved, respondent no.1 again approached HC wherein directions were passed to reconsider representation of respondent no.1 and further to release him forthwith on parole for period of three months - Hence, instant appeal - Whether impugned orders of HC could be upheld.

Held, it was clearly stated that the convict was involved in disruptive activities, criminal conspiracy, smuggling of arms, ammunitions and explosives and further he had also been involved in various other activities. It was also mentioned that the prisoner under disguise of common name used to purchase vehicles for transportation and his conduct showed that he had wide spread network to cause harm and create disturbance to National security and that because of said reasons remission was declined. In such a fact situation, the view expressed by HC to consider the case on the basis of observations made by it in judgment is not correct. Further, in impugned order, an abrupt direction was issued to release respondent no.1 on parole for three months forthwith. However, the constitutional court before directing the temporary release where the request is made to be released on parole for a specified reason and for a specified period, should form an opinion that request has been unjustifiably refused or where the interest of justice warranted for issue of such order of temporary release. However, the impugned order is silent on such aspects. Hence, impugned judgment and order of HC is set aside. However, liberty is granted to respondent no.1 to submit representation before competent authority of UOI for premature release. Appeal allowed.
