Manager, VKNM Vocational Higher Secondary School v State of Kerala and others

Supreme Court of India

27 January 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla, Sharad Arvind Bobde

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 65; (2016) 4 SCC 216; AIR 2016 SC 1031; 2016 (2) AWC 1951; 2016 (1) CLT(SC) 339; JT 2016 (3) SC 51; 2016 (1) KLT 489; 2016 (1) MLJ 818; 2016(1) SCALE 590; [2016] 1 S.C.R. 343

Case Digest

Subject: Education; Service

Keywords: Vacancy, Permanent, Kerala Education Rules

Summary: Service - Education - Kerala Education Rules, rr. 1, 2A - Preferential appointment - Respondent no.5 challenged appointment of respondent no.6 in appellant's school relying upon rule which provided for preferential appointment to some categories of qualified teachers who had the fortune of working earlier in school - However, appellant rejected claim of respondent no.5 and aggrieved by the same, approached respondent no.2 but again claim of respondent no.5 was rejected - Revision was preferred before respondent no.1 wherein, directions were issued to respondent no.4-DEO to issue necessary formal orders appointing respondent no.5 as high school assistant in appellant school w.e.f. 1-6-2010 - Aggrieved, appellant filed writ petition before HC and respondent no.5 also filed writ petition for implementation of order of respondent no.1 dt.26-11-2011 and another writ petition was also filed by another claimant relying on r.51A and all writ petitions were referred to Full Bench wherein impugned judgment was passed by Full Bench of HC - Whether respondent no.5 is entitled for preferential appointment.

Held, in view of interpretation of amended r.7A(3) read along with r.51A, if one were to be brought under category of qualified teacher relieved on account of termination of services, the amended r.7A(3) required to be satisfied. However, respondent no.5 not having satisfied the said requirement there was no scope to allow her to press her claim u/r.51A for a preferential appointment. Having regard to said legal consequence, the relief granted by respondent no.1 in order dt.26-11-2011 could not be sustained and consequently directions issued by HC in impugned judgment could not be sustained. Further, answers to questions made by Full Bench of HC are also set aside and interpretation made by SC in State of Kerala and Others v. Kandath Distilleries, 2013 INDLAW SC 109, would alone prevail. Hence, appeals are allowed and impugned judgment is set aside and order of respondent no.1 is also set aside and order of appointment of respondent no.6 is restored. Appeals disposed of.

Ratio - After amendment of r. 7A(3), in order for a qualified teacher to claim preferential appointment under the category 'on account of termination of vacancies' as mentioned in r.51A, earlier appointment in such vacancies should have been for a duration of one full academic year, i.e., from June 1st of previous year till last day of March of subsequent year.
