Bhagwan Jagannath Markad and others v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

04 October 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Adarsh Kumar Goel, V. Gopala Gowda

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 706; (2016) 10 SCC 537; AIR 2016 SC 4531; 2017 CRLJ 578; JT 2016 (9) SC 594; 2016 (4) MLJ(Crl) 477; 2016 (4) RCR(Criminal) 590; 2017 (1) RLW 853; 2016(9) SCALE 610; [2016] 5 S.C.R. 452

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Common Object, FIR, Indian Evidence Act, 1872, Assembly, Benefit Of Doubt, Appreciation Of Evidence, Proof, Appreciation, Version, Innocent, Credibility, Unlawful Assembly, Panchayat, Discrepancies, Enmity, Attack, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, Complainant, Plea

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 147,149, 302, 324, 326 - Evidence of eye witnesses - Deceased was murdered and six other persons were injured in attack by accused persons and FIR was registered accordingly - However, Trial Court rejected prosecution version and acquitted all accused of charges levied - State preferred appeal before HC wherein HC observed that acquittal by Trial Court was based on omissions and contradictions which were not material and did not affect the veracity of prosecution case and though upheld acquittal of accused nos. 8,9 and 13 to 16, yet, found appellants guilty and passed order of conviction against them u/ss. 147,149, 302 read with ss.149, 324, 326 of IPC and awarded punishment - Hence, instant appeal by appellants - Whether HC was justified in reversing the acquittal of appellants on basis of evidence available on record.

Held, Trial Court adopted perverse approach in rejecting the entire evidence comprising of injured eye witnesses where one person has been killed and six others have been injured. Further, since rejection of eye witnesses account is uncalled for, other reasons given by Trial Court are not sufficient to reject the prosecution case. Even if recoveries or Chemical Analyzer's report are disregarded, the same have only corroborative value, as prosecution case is established by credible eye witness account. Mere fact that some of the witnesses have not been examined is also of no consequence when credible evidence to prove the case has been produced. Hence, HC rightly reversed judgment of Trial Court. Further, all the five eye witnesses have named accused nos. 1 to 7 and other accused have not been named by PWs 11 and 18. As such, benefit of doubt is given to accused nos. 10 and 11 for the reason that they have not been named by PWs 11 and 18 and also for the reason that PW10 has attributed specific role only to accused nos. 1 to 7 and all five witnesses have consistently named them only and assigned specific role to them only in assaulting the deceased. Hence, conviction of accused nos. 1 to 7 u/s. 302 read with s.149 of IPC is upheld and accused nos. 7 and 8 are given benefit of doubt and are acquitted. Appeal partly allowed.
