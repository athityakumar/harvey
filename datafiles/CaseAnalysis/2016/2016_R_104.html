State of Uttar Pradesh and others v Ravindra Kumar Sharma and others

Supreme Court of India

03 February 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Arun Mishra, M.Y. Eqbal

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 89; (2016) 4 SCC 791; AIR 2016 SC 690; 2016 (3) ALD(SC) 78; 2016(2) ALL MR 975; 2016 (2) AWC 1733; 2016 (2) CGLJ 93; 2016 (1) CLT(SC) 196; 2016 (1) CLT(SC) 335; 2016 (148) FLR 969; JT 2016 (1) SC 548; 2016 (1) MLJ 834; 2016 (1) OLR 676; 2016(2) SCALE 159; [2016] 2 S.C.R. 845; 2016 (1) UPLBEC 609; 2016 (1) WLN(SC) 81

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Service

Keywords: Verification, Bad Faith, Persons With Disabilities (Equal Opportunities, Protection Of Rights And Full Participation) Act, 1995, Persons with Disabilities (Equal Opportunities, Protection of Rights and Full Participation) Rules, 1996, Fraud, Training, Disability certificate

Summary: Service - Administrative - Disability certificate - Verification - Respondents applied for BTC training course under the physically handicapped category on the basis of certificates issued under the aforesaid Rules - Respondents completed training an and were offered appointment - Complaint was received that illegal usurpation of the quota reserved for handicapped persons on the basis of fraudulently procured certificates without suffering from the disability certified under the Rules of 1996 - Medical board after assessing the disability concluded that 21% of the candidates were not handicapped - HC dismissed application and held that the certificate issued was not final - Appeal before DB - Appeal was allowed - Hence instant appeal.

Held, DB and HC ignored and overlooked the material fact that verification has already been done by the Medical Board and it has been found that certificates of 21% were fraudulently obtained. The HC has issued a direction in the impugned order for physical verification of the candidate by the authorities and in case he does not suffer from disability so certified candidate can be subjected to fresh medical test. The HC has overlooked that on mere physical verification it may not be possible to know various kinds of disabilities such as that of eyes, ear impairment etc. That can only be done by the medical examination and particularly when HC itself has observed that in case there is genuine suspicion and fraud has been committed medical certification can be reopened. Direction issued in this regard has not been questioned by the respondents and in fact process of re-verification was already over when HC issued aforesaid directions. The peculiar facts of the case of such a fraud and genuine suspicion raised in the representation lodged by the Viklang Sangh and when 21% of such certificates have been found to be fraudulently obtained there was no scope for the Division Bench to interfere and issue order to perpetuate fraud, writ is to be declined in such a scenario and no equity can be claimed by the respondents. Thus, impugned judgment and order passed by DB of the HC is set aside. Appeal allowed .
