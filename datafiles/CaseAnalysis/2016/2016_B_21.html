Baijnath and others v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

18 November 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Amitava Roy, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 823; (2017) 1 SCC 101; AIR 2016 SC 5313; 2017 CRLJ 179; JT 2016 (11) SC 138; 2016 (4) RCR(Criminal) 896; 2016(12) SCALE 102

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Presumption, Suicide, Cruelty, Harassment, Marriage, Indian Evidence Act, 1872, Proof, Dowry, Matrimonial, Dowry Prohibition Act, 1961, Valuable Security, Sarpanch, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Husband, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 313 - Dowry Prohibition Act, 1961, s. 2 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 113B - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 201, 302, 304B, 34, 498A - Abetment of suicide - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellants (husband and in-laws)/accused inflicted mental and physical cruelty and harassed deceased/wife with beatings for dowry and drove deceased to commit suicide - Charge-sheet was filed against appellants u/ss. 302, 304B, 498A, 201 r/w s. 34 IPC - Trial Court acquitted accused persons of charges leveled against them - On appeal, HC reversed impugned order of Trial Court and convicted accused u/ss. 498A and 304B IPC - Hence, instant appeal - Whether there was any perversity or illegality in impugned judgment of HC.

Held, prosecution has failed to prove beyond reasonable doubt, cruelty or harassment to deceased for or in connection with any demand for dowry as contemplated in either of the two provisions of Code under which accused persons were charged. Further, alleged demand centers around a motorcycle, which as the evidence of prosecution witnesses would evince, did not surface at the time of finalization of marriage. PW5/mother of deceased conceded that there was no dowry demand at that stage. Evidence of defence witnesses was consistent to the effect that no demand as imputed was made as the family of husband was adequately well-off and appellant no.1 was living separately from before marriage. Significant was also the testimony of DW3/sister-in-law of deceased who indicated abandonment of matrimonial home by her with son of Sarpanch of village for which she understandably had incurred displeasure of in-laws. DW4/ father of DW3 who had given his daughter in marriage in the same family had deposed that he did not ever encounter any demand for dowry. The testimony of prosecution witnesses PW3 and PW7 fully consolidate defence version. The factum of unnatural death in matrimonial home and that too within 7 years of marriage was ipso facto not sufficient to bring home charge u/ss. 304B and 498A IPC against them. Analysis of evidence by Trial Court was in proper perspectives, factual and legal and findings recorded by it were valid. HC based its determination substantially on presumptive inferences taking the aid of s. 113B of the Act, divorced from attendant facts and evidence with regard thereto. Impugned judgment of HC was set aside. Appellants in view of evaluation made were entitled to benefit of doubt. Appeal allowed.
