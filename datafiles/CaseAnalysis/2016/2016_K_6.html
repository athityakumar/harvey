Narendra v K. Meena

Supreme Court of India

06 October 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Anil R. Dave, L. Nageswara Rao

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 713; (2016) 9 SCC 455; 2016 (10) ADJ 275; AIR 2016 SC 4599; 2017 (1) ALD(SC) 1; 2016 (119) ALR 494; 2016 (6) ALT(SC) 1; 2016 (6) AWC 5441; 2016 (6) Bom.C.R. 553; 2017 (1) CGLJ 425; 2016 (4) CLT(SC) 1; 2016 (6) CTC 440; 2016 (233) DLT 149; 2016 (2) ILR(Cut) 930; 2016 (4) JCC 2682; 2016 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 2993; 2017 (1) MahLJ 754; 2016 (7) MLJ 726; 2017 (1) MPLJ 306; 2016 (2) OLR 928; 2016 (4) RCR(Civil) 706; 2017 (1) RLW 624; 2016(9) SCALE 681; [2016] 5 S.C.R. 840

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Keywords: divorce, Hindu Marriage Act, 1955, Mental Cruelty

Summary: Family & Personal - Hindu Marriage Act, 1955, ss. 13(1)(ia), 28(1) - Decree for divorce - Dismissal - Validity - Appellant/husband filed decree for divorce before Family Court against respondent/wife contenting that respondent's cruelty as she made serious allegations about moral character of appellant to the effect that he was having an extra-marital affair - Family Court allowed petition and passed decree for divorce - Aggrieved, respondent filed appeal u/s. 28(1) of the Act before HC - HC allowed appeal and set aside impugned judgment of Family Court - Hence, instant appeal - Whether there is any perversity or illegality in impugned judgment of HC.

Held, no son would like to be separated from his old parents and other family members, who are also dependent upon his income. The persistent effort of respondent to constrain appellant to be separated from the family would be torturous for the husband and Trial Court was right when it came to the conclusion that this constitutes an act of 'cruelty'. Further, unsubstantiated allegations levelled by respondent and the threats and attempt to commit suicide by her amounted to mental cruelty and therefore marriage deserves to be dissolved by a decree of divorce on ground stated in s. 13(1)(ia) of the Act. Taking an overall view of the entire evidence and judgment delivered by Trial Court, SC firmly believes that there was no need to take a different view than the one taken by Trial Court. The behaviour of respondent appears to be terrifying and horrible. One would find it difficult to live with such a person with tranquility and peace of mind. Such torture would adversely affect the life of husband. It is also not in dispute that respondent had left the matrimonial house more than 20 years back. Though not on record till date respondent was not staying with appellant. The daughter of appellant and respondent has also grown up and she is working in an IT company. Thus, no reason to disbelieve the facts because with the passage of time daughter would have grown up and separation of appellant and the wife should have also become normal for her and at this juncture it would not be proper to bring them together especially when appellant was treated so cruelly by respondent. Impugned judgment of HC was quashed and set aside and that of Family Court was restored. Appeal allowed.
