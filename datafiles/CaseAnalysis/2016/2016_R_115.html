Reliance General Insurance Company Limited v Shashi Sharma and others

Supreme Court of India

23 September 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Khanwilkar, Ranjan Gogoi, Prafulla C. Pant

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 1016; 2017 (120) ALR 256

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance

Keywords: Claims Tribunal, Computation Of Compensation, Period, Insurance Company, Motor Vehicles Act, 1939, Age, Just Compensation, Quantum Of Compensation, Compensation Amount, Amount Of Compensation, Compensation Payable, Motor Accident, Assistance, Pecuniary Advantage, Computation, Fatal Accidents Act, 1855, English Fatal Accidents Act, 1846, Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, Constitution of India, 1950, Defendants of Deceased Governments Employees Rules, 2006

Summary: Insurance - Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 - Dependants of Deceased Government Employees Rules, 2006 - Computation of compensation - As deceased succumbed to injuries sustained in motor accident, respondents filed claim petition before Tribunal for award of compensation - Tribunal partly allowed claim and awarded Rs.4,50,000 as compensation payable by appellant-Insurance company to respondents - However, aggrieved by quantum of compensation awarded by Tribunal and more so, deduction of compensation amount received by them from other source, respondents filed appeal before HC and the same was allowed and were awarded Rs.89,60,604 as compensation - HC disallowed deduction of compensation amount received by claimants as per Rules in respective companion cases listed for analogous hearing - Hence, instant appeals by appellants-insurance companies contending that HC erred in law in disallowing deduction of amount received by concerned claimants under the Rules, from the quantum of compensation amount payable to claimants under the Act - Whether appeals could be allowed.

Held, Tribunal has to adjudicate the claim and determine the amount of compensation which appears to it to be just. The amount receivable by dependents towards the head of pay and allowances in the form of ex-gratia financial assistance could not be paid for the second time to the claimants. The harmonious approach for determining just compensation payable under the Act is to exclude the amount received or receivable by dependents of the deceased Govt. employee under the Rules towards the head financial assistance equivalent to 'pay and other allowances' that was last drawn by deceased Govt. employee in the normal course. Further, other benefits extended to the dependents of the deceased Govt. employee in terms of rr.5(2) to 5(5) including family pension, Life Insurance, Provident Fund etc., are to remain unaffected and could not be allowed to be deducted, which would be paid to dependents of deceased Govt. employee, applying principle expounded in Helen and Patricia 's cases. Appeals disposed of.

Ratio - Under the Act, the two cardinal principles for determination of compensation are: firstly, the measure of compensation has to be just and adequate and secondly, no double benefit is to be passed on to the claimants in the matter of award of compensation.
