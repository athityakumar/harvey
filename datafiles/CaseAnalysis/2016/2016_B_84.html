Bunga Daniel Babu v Vasudeva Constructions and others

Supreme Court of India

22 July 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, N.V. Ramana

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 554; (2016) 8 SCC 429; AIR 2016 SC 3488; 2016 (118) ALR 901; 2016 (5) ALT(SC) 18; 2016 (5) AWC 5266; 2016 (6) Bom.C.R. 28; 2016 (4) CompLJ 97; 2016 (3) CPJ(Sc) 1; 2016 (4) CTC 548; JT 2016 (7) SC 268; 2016 (3) RCR(Civil) 924; 2016(7) SCALE 267; [2016] 3 S.C.R. 293

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection

Keywords: Consumer, District Forum, Consumer Protection Act, 1986, State Commission, National Commission, Deficiency, Hires, Flats, Consumer Protection (Amendment) Act, 1993, Consumer Protection (Amendment) Act, 2002, Income-tax Act, 1961, Complainant

Summary: Consumer Protection - Consumer Protection (Amendment) Act, 1993 - Consumer Protection (Amendment) Act, 2002 - Consumer Protection Act, 1986, ss. 2(1)(d), 2(1)(d)(ii) - Disputes between parties - Joint venture - Challenged - Appellant/land owner entered into a Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) with respondents for development of his land - Under MOU, apartments constructed were to be shared in proportion of 40% and 60% between appellant and respondent no. 1 - Appellant sent notice to respondents for delay in occupancy certificates and deviations from sanction plans and non-completion of various other works which he claimed a sum of Rs.19,33,193/- - Respondents repudiated those claims - Aggrieved, appellant approached District Forum for redressal of his grievance - District Forum held that transaction between the parties could not be termed as a joint venture, in order to exclude it from the purview of the 1993 Act directed respondents to pay the amount with interest - On appeal, State Commission held that appellant did not come within the ambit of "consumer" under the 1986 Act and reversed view of District Forum that complainant is a "consumer" within the definition u/s. 2(1)(d) of the 1986 Act as agreement of appellant with respondents was not a joint venture - Appellant invoked revisional jurisdiction before National Commission which concurred with the view expressed by State Commission - Hence, instant appeal - Whether appellant falls within the definition of "consumer" u/s. 2(1)(d) of the 1986 Act.

Held, use of words 'joint venture' or 'collaboration' in the title of an agreement or even in body of the agreement will not make the transaction a joint venture, if there are no provisions for shared control of interest or enterprise and shared liability for losses. If there is a breach by land owner of his obligations, builder will have to approach a Civil Court as the land owner is not providing any service to builder but merely undertakes certain obligations towards builder, breach of which would furnish a cause of action for specific performance and/or damages. Appellant was neither a partner nor a co-adventurer. He has no say or control over construction. He does not participate in business. He is only entitled to, as per MOU, a certain constructed area. The extent of area as held in Faqir Chand Gulati v. Uppal Agencies Pvt. Ltd. & Anr, 2008 Indlaw SC 1032, does not make a difference. Therefore, appellant is a consumer under the 1986 Act. Thus, there has to be appropriate adjudication with regard to all the aspects except status of appellant as a consumer by appellate authority. Impugned judgments and orders passed by National Commission and State Commission were set aside and matter is remitted to State Commission to re-adjudicate treating appellant as a consumer. Appeal allowed.
