State of Assam v Ramen Dowarah

Supreme Court of India

11 January 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Arun Mishra, Kurian Joseph

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 28; (2016) 3 SCC 19; AIR 2016 SC 341; 2016 (2) CalLT(SC) 67; 2016 CRLJ 1125; JT 2016 (1) SC 195B; 2016 (1) MLJ(Crl) 373; 2016 (1) RCR(Criminal) 799; 2016 (1) RCR(Criminal) 835; 2016(1) SCALE 258; [2016] 1 S.C.R. 179

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Kerosene

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 454, 376(G) and 302 - Consensual sexual intercourse - Rape - Accused were convicted for commission of offences u/ss. 454, 376(G) and 302 of IPC and were sentenced - On appeal, HC set aside conviction of accused u/s. 376 of IPC and altered conviction u/s. 302 to s. 304 of IPC - Hence, instant appeal by State - Whether impugned order of HC is sustainable.

Held, victim made hue and cry on commission of rape on her and also on being threatened that she would narrate the incident to her mother, accused-Ramen had set her ablaze after pouring kerosene over her body. Hence, HC erred in upsetting finding of Trial Court which was based on circumstances and evidence on record which clearly makes out that it was not a case of consensual sexual intercourse and HC erred in law in acquitting accused R from commission of offence u/s. 376 of IPC. Further, HC erred in holding that accused had not intended to cause death. The facts and circumstances proved indicate that accused wanted to get rid of the victim by causing her death and the same makes him liable for punishment u/s. 302 of IPC. Hence, judgment and order of HC is set aside and that of Trial Court is restored. Appeal allowed.
