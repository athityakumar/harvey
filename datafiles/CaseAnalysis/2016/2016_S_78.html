Commissioner of Income Tax, Gujarat Central and another v Saurasthra Cement and Chemical Industries Limited and another

Supreme Court of India

02 May 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, Rohinton Fali Nariman

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 1065; AIR 2016 SC 2918

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: INDIRECT TAX, Income-tax Act, 1961

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income-tax Act, 1961, ss. 123, 125, 125(2)(a), 125A, 125A(1), 125A(4), 130A, 143, 143(3), 144, 144A, 144B, 153 - Draft assessment order - Time barred - Validity - Appellants/revenue and assesse filed instant appeals challenging impugned order of HC whereby HC dismissed appeal of Revenue affirming order of Tribunal holding that assessment order passed by AO in respect of A/Y 1981-82 was time barred - Revenue contended that conferment of powers of AO upon Inspecting Assistant Commissioner (IAC), was in the same position as Income Tax Officer (ITO) and draft assessment order could not be sent to him who was brought at par with ITO.

Held, in instant case, SC found that it was not IAC who exercises the powers or performs functions of ITO, even when such a power was conferred upon him concurrently with ITO. The significant feature of s.125A of the Act was that even when IAC was given the same powers and functions which were to be performed by ITO in relation to any area or classes or person or income or classes of income or cases or classes of cases, on conferment of such powers, ITO does not stand denuded of those powers. With conferment of such powers on IAC gives him "concurrent" jurisdiction which means that both, ITO as well as IAC were empowered to exercise those functions including passing assessment order. It was still open to ITO to assume the jurisdiction and pass the order in case IAC does not exercise those powers in respect of a/y. Provisions of s. 144B of the Act would not apply only if IAC exercises powers or performs functions of an ITO. What was important is the Actual exercise of powers and not merely conferment of the powers that were borne out from the bare reading of s. 125(B)(4). Therefore, no such power was exercised or function of an ITO was performed by IAC in instant case. SC agree with the view taken in CIT v. Shree Digvijay Woollen Mills Ltd.,1993 Indlaw GUJ 228. Appeals disposed of.
