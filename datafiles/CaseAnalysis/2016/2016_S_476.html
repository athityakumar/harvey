Kala @ Chandrakala v State, through Inspector of Police

Supreme Court of India

12 August 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Arun Mishra, Jagdish Singh Khehar

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 581; (2016) 9 SCC 337; AIR 2016 SC 3912; 2017 CRLJ 1167; 2016 (4) JCC 2380; JT 2016 (8) SC 111; 2016 (4) RCR(Criminal) 141; 2016(7) SCALE 735; [2016] 3 S.C.R. 797

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Vehicle, Motor, Indian Evidence Act, 1872, Gas, Chain, Cause Of Death, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 313 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 106 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 201, 302, 34 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - It was alleged that appellant/accused along with her father and nephew committed murder of deceased by strangulating him with a saree - FIR was lodged against accused - Trial Court convicted appellant and her father for commission of offence u/ss. 302 /w ss. 34, 201 IPC and sentenced them to undergo imprisonment for life - On appeal, HC allowed appeal in respect of father of accused, but conviction and sentence of appellant was affirmed - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, prosecution has also not led evidence that appellant was ever required to identify the articles of deceased. There is nothing on record indicating that they were shown to her for the purpose of identification and she had refused to identify them. Further, in instant case, when the body has not been recovered at the instance of accused, recoveries of moped and piece of nylon saree which were made are not proved to be related to commission of offence, they are not proved to be incriminating materials. The extra-judicial confession made by appellant to PW4 is prima facie unusual and doubtful and is not corroborated by other evidence on record. The fact that deceased had left the house as per version of appellant, cannot be used as a circumstance against her so as to fasten guilt. The prosecution has not been able to complete the chain of circumstances so as to fasten guilt and to prove the commission of offence by appellant beyond periphery of doubt. The father of appellant has also been extended benefit of doubt. Therefore, appellant is acquitted giving her benefit of doubt in view of evidence which has been adduced by prosecution. Impugned judgments and orders of Courts below were set aside. Appeal allowed.
