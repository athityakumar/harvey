Union of India v Rajasthan High Court and others

Supreme Court of India

14 December 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

D.Y. Chandrachud, T.S. Thakur, L. Nageswara Rao

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 890; (2017) 2 SCC 599; AIR 2017 SC 101; 2017 (2) CLT(SC) 34; JT 2016 (12) SC 262; 2016(12) SCALE 763; [2016] 8 S.C.R. 705; 2017 (1) WLN(SC) 99

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Jurisdiction, Review, Arms Act, 1959, Aircraft Act, 1934, Constitution of India, 1950, Aircraft Rules, 1957

Summary: Civil Appeal Nos. 717 of 2006

Practice & Procedure - Administrative - Aircraft Act, 1934, s. 5(e) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136, 226 - Aircraft Rules, 1957, r. 8(a) - Pre-embarkation security checks - Exemption - Respondents/DB by its judgment dt. 13-5-2005 issued a direction to appellants/UOI and to its Secretaries in Ministries of Civil Aviation and Home Affairs "to include the Chief Justices and the judges of HC in the list of persons exempted from pre-embarkation security checks" at airports and to amend a circular dt. 1-5-2002 of Bureau of Civil Aviation Security (BCAS) within 30 days - HC further directed that certain suggestions formulated by it for laying down a 'National Security Policy' should be considered by appellants - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, appellants adopted the position that the issue as to whether pre-embarkation security exemptions should be granted does not depend only on warrant of precedence. Among the factors which were borne in mind was that the person who was exempted from pre-embarkation security checks should according to govt. be secured by such a level of govt. security on a 24x7 basis, which would virtually preclude the possibility of any prohibited or dangerous items being introduced on board an aircraft through his or her baggage. The security perception of appellants was that no exemption could be granted to a dignitary if he/she was not under effective govt. security coverage on a 24x7 basis. Heads of foreign missions in Country were exempted from pre-embarkation security checks on a reciprocal basis. SC was not called upon to decide the legality or justification for inclusion of the name of any particular individual in the list of exempted persons in those proceedings. What SC have said was to emphasise that the view of appellants was based on a considered assessment of security perceptions and ought not to have been interfered with in the manner that HC did in the exercise of its jurisdiction u/art. 226 of Constitution. Impugned judgment and order of HC was set aside. Appeals disposed of.

T.P.(C) No. 75 of 2012

Practice & Procedure - Administrative - Judgment of HC - Challenged - Instant transfer petition was instituted by Commissioner of Security (Civil Aviation), BCAS challenging order of DB.

Held, record of transfer petition indicates that HC in the course of special appeal has made certain observations while issuing a notice to Director General of BCAS. Since, HC has made those observations in a matter which was unrelated to the issue involved in special appeal, SC draw the attention of HC to the principles enunciated above while disposing of appeal filed by appellants against the judgment of HC. A copy of the above judgment should be placed on record of special appeal filed before HC. In the event that the special appeal still remain on file of HC, HC should proceed to hear and dispose of special appeal accordingly. Appeals disposed of.
