Bhagwan Sahai and another v State of Rajasthan

Supreme Court of India

03 June 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Shiva Kirti Singh, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 402; AIR 2016 SC 2714; 2016 (3) CGLJ 256; 2016 CRLJ 3154; 2016 (3) JCC 1913; JT 2016 (6) SC 6; 2016 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 2514; 2016 (3) MLJ(Crl) 366; 2016 (2) OLR 468; 2016 (3) RCR(Criminal) 322; 2016(5) SCALE 762; [2016] 3 S.C.R. 533

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Blunt, Free Fight, Indian Penal Code, 1860

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 147,148,149,323,324,326,327 - Right of Private defence - Free fight - Appellants were tried for commission of offences u/ss. 147,148,323,324,326 and 327 read with s. 149 of IPC and upon conclusion of trial, appellants were convicted by Trial Court and on appeal, HC upheld conviction of appellants though under different sections - Hence, instant appeal assailing conviction - Whether conviction of appellants could be upheld.

Held, when Court came to a finding that prosecution suppressed the genesis and origin of occurrence and also failed to explain the injuries on the person of the accused including death of father of the appellants, then, under such circumstances, Court were to grant benefit of doubt to appellants. Further, appellants could legitimately claim right to use force once they saw their parents being assaulted and when actually it was shown that due to such assault and injury their father subsequently died. Under such circumstances, adverse inference needs to be drawn against prosecution for not offering any explanation much less a plausible one. Hence, appellants are acquitted of all charges. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Drawing of adverse inference could not be made in case of free fight mainly because occurrence in such case could take place at different spots and in such a manner that a witness could not reasonably be expected to see and therefore explain the injuries sustained by defence party.
