Amin Merchant v Chairman, Central Board of Excise and Revenue and others

Supreme Court of India

22 July 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

N.V. Ramana, Madan B. Lokur

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 544; (2016) 9 SCC 191; AIR 2016 SC 3920; 2017(1) ALL MR 486; 2016 (338) E.L.T. 164; 2016 (4) RCR(Civil) 76; 2016(7) SCALE 336; [2016] 4 S.C.R. 488

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Writ Of Mandamus, Customs Act, 1962, Rate Of Duty, Part, Customs Tariff Act, 1975, Review, Issued, India, Give, Right To Information Act, 2005, Finance Act, 1993, Constitution of India, 1950

Summary: (A) Customs - Parliament & Legislature - Customs Act, 1962, ss. 159, 25, 25(1), 25(2) - Customs Tariff Act, 1975 - Right to Information Act, 2005 - Finance Act, 1993 - Import - Customs tariff - Exemption - Appellant imported eight consignments of goods falling under Tariff Sub-Heading 2208.10 of Customs Tariff during financial years 1993-94 and 1994-95 - Respondent/customs authorities assessed goods imported and prescribed rate of duty of Rs.300/- per liter or 400% whichever is higher - Appellant deposited amount of duty provisionally assessed on assessable value declared in eight bills of entry - Subsequently, appellant addressed several communications to respondents claiming that rate which has been prescribed for goods falling under Tariff Sub-Heading 2208.10 is higher than that was authorized in budget proposals during financial years 1993-94 and 1994-95 - Appellant took recourse to provisions of the 2005 Act in order to procure relevant information from concerned authorities however, no information was furnished by authorities - Appellant filed writ petition before HC seeking to direct respondents to issue a notification u/s.25(2) of the 1962 Act for granting exemption from customs duty for goods falling under Tariff Sub-Heading 2208.10 which was dismissed - Aggrieved, appellant filed review petition before HC which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeals - Whether budget proposals alleged by appellant are duly passed and approved by Parliament and whether tariff rates fixed by respondents are contrary to the legislative mandate.

Held, law as enacted is what contained in the 1993 Act. In instant case, as per the finance bill, legislative will of Parliament is that for commodities falling under Tariff Head 2208.10, tariff is Rs.300/- per litre or 400% whichever is higher. Even assuming that amount of tax is excessive in matters of taxation laws, Court permits greater latitude to discretion of legislature and it is not amenable to judicial review. SC unable to concur with contention of appellant that budget proposals are duly passed and approved by Parliament. However, if appellant is aggrieved by particular tariff prescribed under the 1993 Act and the same is contrary to approved budget proposals he ought to have questioned the same if permissible. Appeals dismissed.

(B) Customs - Parliament & Legislature - Customs Act, 1962, ss. 159, 25, 25(1), 25(2) - Customs Tariff Act, 1975 - Right to Information Act, 2005 - Finance Act, 1993 - Notification - Legislative discretion - Legality - Whether SC can direct Central Govt. to issue a notification u/s. 25(1) of the 1962 Act.

Held, as per s. 159 of the 1962 Act, any notification issued u/s. 25 of the 1962 Act shall be placed before Parliament and Parliament might amend or reject the same. This clearly demonstrates that ultimate law making power is vested with Legislature. Hence, allegation of appellant that notifications are issued basing on whims and fancies of respondent no. 2 is misconceived. Whereas, notifications are issued generally in larger public interest, Legislature has given power to exempt duty to respondent no. 2 subject to amending power. Therefore, it is not appropriate to issue any orders directing respondents to issue a notification u/s. 25(2) of the 1962 Act except on grounds of discrimination. In the matter of taxation, Court gives a greater latitude to legislative discretion. Appeals dismissed.

(C) Customs - Parliament & Legislature - Customs Act, 1962, ss. 159, 25, 25(1), 25(2) - Customs Tariff Act, 1975 - Right to Information Act, 2005 - Finance Act, 1993 - Compound alcoholic - alcoholic beverage - Applicability - Whether the compound alcoholic preparations of a kind used for manufacturing of beverages fall under the category of alcoholic beverage.

Held, appellant has relied on a judgment of HC which was confirmed by SC and respondents made contentions relying on some judgments. Thus, it is not for SC to do such exercise. It is always open to the parties to settle dispute before appropriate forum if they choose to do so. Appeals dismissed.

(D) Customs - Parliament & Legislature - Customs Act, 1962, ss. 159, 25, 25(1), 25(2) - Customs Tariff Act, 1975 - Right to Information Act, 2005 - Finance Act, 1993 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 19(1)(g), 265, 300 - Notification - Discrimination - Sustainability - Whether there is any discrimination on part of Central Govt. in issuing a notification u/s. 25(1) of the 1962 Act in respect of other goods and contrary to art. 14 of Constitution.

Held, when appellant alleges discriminatory action on part of respondents, he has to establish that there is no rational basis for making classification between goods which are notified and goods of appellant which are not notified. It is also a firmly established principle that legislature understands and appreciates needs of its people. A Taxing Statute can be held to contravene art. 14 of Constitution if it purports to impose certain duty on the same class of people differently and leads to obvious inequality. Such a material is not placed before us to come to a just conclusion that action of respondents is discriminative. Hence, the same is held against appellant. Appeals dismissed.
