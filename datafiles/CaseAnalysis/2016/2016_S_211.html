Amal Kumar Jha v State of Chhatisgarh and another

Supreme Court of India

26 April 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Arun Mishra, V. Gopala Gowda

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 318; (2016) 6 SCC 734; AIR 2016 SC 2082; 2016 CRLJ 2634; 2016 (3) JCC 1760; JT 2016 (4) SC 374; 2016 (2) MLJ(Crl) 631; 2016(4) SCALE 378B; [2016] 2 S.C.R. 1060

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Vehicle, Omission, Official Duty, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 197(1) - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 304A - Negligence in conducting official duties - Prosecution with sanction - Discharge - It is alleged that appellant who is in-charge of Govt. hospital failed to provide ambulance with in time for shifting of patient from one place to other which resulted in her death - FIR was lodged against appellant and other accused person u/s. 304-A of IPC - Appellant filed application for discharge u/s. 197 of CrPC on ground that sanction to prosecute was required - Lower court rejected application of appellant for discharge which is confirmed by HC - Whether appellant could be prosecuted without previous sanction.

Held, allegation against appellant is of omission in discharge of official duty in not providing Govt. vehicle for shifting patient from one place to other, whereas he himself travelled in vehicle in question for attending monthly official meeting at District Headquarters. Hence, SC held that it is act or omission in discharge of official duty and sanction to prosecute was necessary. SC held that accused was acting in discharge of his official duty when he refused to provide official vehicle. Further, refusal is directly and reasonably connected with his official duty, hence, sanction is required for prosecution as provided u/s. 197(1) of CrPC. Hence, appellant could not have been prosecuted without sanction. Hence, SC directed competent authority to consider question of grant of sanction in accordance with law. Impugned order passed by lower courts is set aside. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - In case accused were discharging public duties while performing duty, prosecution was not maintainable without sanction.
