Indo Burma Petroleum Corporation Limited v Commissioner VAT Delhi and others

Supreme Court of India

13 May 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Uday Umesh Lalit, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 364; AIR 2016 SC 2716; 2016(5) SCALE 591

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; VAT

Keywords: Delhi Value Added Tax Act, 2004, Delhi Value Added Tax (Amendment) Act, 2006

Summary: Indirect Tax - VAT - Delhi Value Added Tax Act, 2004, s.2(1)(zd), 32 - VAT - Variation in prices - Appellants filed VAT Returns on footing that by continued operation of first proviso to s.2(1)(zd), they were permitted to recover VAT only on amount of sale price currently charged, i.e., even after partial roll back and subsequent, complete roll back of prices, appellants continued to deduct amounts from prevailing sale price and charged/recovered VAT in respect of sale price so reduced by Rs.4 and Rs.2 - Later, notices of default u/s. 32 of the Act were issued to appellants calling upon them to pay VAT and penalty - On appeal, Appellate Tribunal dismissed appeals and aggrieved, appellants preferred appeals before HC - HC opined that upon partial roll back and subsequent complete roll back, benefit of proviso ceased to be partly and fully applicable - Hence, instant appeals by appellants contending that benefit in terms of the proviso in question was to the extent of VAT chargeable and payable in respect of the amount of increase and the benefit so quantified is to be made available regardless of any variation or decrease in the rates of Petrol and High Speed Diesel - Whether impugned judgment of HC could be upheld.

Held, on the element of increase no additional ad valorem VAT was payable and according to the proviso the increased component was not to be part of sale consideration. Consequently, VAT was not to be charged in respect of such increased component, as per definition of term 'sale price' which came to be controlled by introduction of the proviso. The idea was to grant benefit only in respect of that element of VAT respecting increase in rates and not beyond. If that component of increase ceased to be in existence, the benefit of proviso also ceased to be in operation. Hence, view taken by HC and appellate authority are affirmed and appeals are dismissed. Appeals disposed of.

Ratio - First proviso to s.2(1)(zd) is to be given normal and natural meaning considering context, object and reasons for its enactment and incorporation and the idea was to protect the interest of the consumers by giving exemption in respect of enhanced ad valorem VAT payable on account of increase in prices of diesel and petrol from 6-6-2006.
