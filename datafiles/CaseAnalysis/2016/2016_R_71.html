Ramakant Dwivedi v Rafiq Ahmad and others

Supreme Court of India

04 January 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Adarsh Kumar Goel, Anil R. Dave

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 3; (2016) 3 SCC 352; AIR 2016 SC 235; 2016 (114) ALR 698; 2016 (2) AWC 1100; JT 2016 (1) SC 75; 2016 (131) RD 13; 2016(1) SCALE 187; [2016] 1 S.C.R. 571; 2016 (3) WLN(SC) 156

Case Digest

Subject: Mines & Minerals

Summary: Mines & Minerals - Cancellation of lease - Justification - Govt. order was issued on 31-5-2012 with respect to mining lease - HC in earlier case passed judgment on 29 -1-2013 where it was held that all pending applications as on 31-5-2012 stood rejected and further lease would be granted through e-auction - Appellant was granted lease on 17-10-2013 - HC vide impugned order held that that lease granted to appellant was in violation of its judgments dt. 29-1-2013 - Whether impugned order passed by HC was justified and in accordance with law.

Held, HC has rightly held that renewal was in pursuance of Govt. order dt. 26-2-2013 which itself was in conflict with order of HC in earlier case. In view of order of HC dt. 29-1-2013, all pending applications as on 31-5-2012 stood rejected. SC held that in instant case, environmental clearance was granted to appellant on 21-9-2012 and renewal was granted on 27-4-2013. SC held that orders of HC in earlier case which are not under challenge clearly debarred grant of lease under Chapter II after 31-5-2012. Hence, SC held that lease was granted in violation of judgment of HC and subsequent withdrawal of Govt. order dt. 31-5-2012 could not benefit appellant as on date of grant of lease in favour of appellant, said Govt. order was operative. Hence, impugned order passed by HC is justified and in accordance with law. Appeal dismissed.

Ratio - Lease granted/ renewed in violation of judgment of court is not justified and same is liable to be quashed.
