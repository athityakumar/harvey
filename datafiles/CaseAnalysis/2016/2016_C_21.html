HCL Infosystem Limited and another v Central Bureau of Investigation and another

Supreme Court of India

09 August 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Adarsh Kumar Goel, V. Gopala Gowda

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 588; (2016) 9 SCC 281; AIR 2016 SC 3705; 2016 CRLJ 4400; 2016 (4) JCC 2579; JT 2016 (8) SC 26; 2016 (3) KLT 722; 2016 (2) OLR 730; 2016 (3) RCR(Criminal) 1012B; 2016(7) SCALE 674; [2016] 8 S.C.R. 755

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Conspiracy, FIR, CBI, Jurisdiction, Final Report, Challenge, Special Judge, Cognizance, Successor, Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Constitution of India, 1950, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, Corruption

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 173(8), 220, 223, 26 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120B, 409, 420 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988 ss. 13(1)(d), 13(2), 3(1) - Non corruption cases - Jurisdiction of Special Judge -Challenged - Respondents/CBI conducted investigation in "National Rural Health Mission Scam"(NRHM Scam) - CBI found that NRHM funds of Rs. 9,000 crores, which were allocated to State for the period 2005-2006 by Ministry of UOI were diverted and allegedly misappropriated on a large scale - CBI filed charge sheet in respect of offences not covered by the Act against appellant and others - Appellants were charged for offence of conspiracy along with a public servant under the Act - Appellants filed petition before HC contending that Special Judge has no jurisdiction to deal with the case against appellants after the death of public servant - HC dismissed petition relying on judgment of SC in Essar Teleholdings Limited v. Registrar General, Delhi High Court and Others, 2013 Indlaw SC 381, wherein it was held that Special Judge having been appointed to deal with "all 2G scam" cases, could also deal with cases involving other offences under the Act - Hence, instant appeals - Whether Special Judge can try non corruption cases under the Act when his appointment is to try all cases of the category which covers instant case.

Held, there is nothing on record to suggest that appellants will not get fair trial and may face miscarriage of justice. In absence of any such threat & miscarriage of justice, no interference is called for against impugned order taking cognizance of offence against appellants. In instant case Special Court in question has been constituted not only to deal with the cases of the Act but also other cases relating to NRHM scam. Procedure of CrPC is applicable to trial before Special Judge and there is no prejudice to trial that is taking place before Special Judge duly appointed to deal with non-corruption cases when the object of doing so was to try connected cases before same Court. Therefore, while Special Judge alone could deal with cases under the Act, non-corruption cases under the Act could also be allowed to be tried by Special Judge u/s. 26 CrPC. There is no legal bar to do so, as held by SC in Essar Teleholdings Limited, 2013 Indlaw SC 381. Appeals dismissed.
