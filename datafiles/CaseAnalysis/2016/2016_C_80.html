ITC Limited, Gurgaon v Commissioner of I. T. (TDS), Delhi

Supreme Court of India

26 April 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

R.F. Nariman, Kurian Joseph

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 344; (2016) 6 SCC 652; AIR 2016 SC 2127; [2016] 384 ITR 14; JT 2016 (4) SC 385; 2016 (4) MLJ 305; 2016(4) SCALE 331; [2016] 3 S.C.R. 23; [2016] 239 TAXMAN 372

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Contract, Principal Officer, Hotel, Resident, Person Responsible For Paying, Employees State Insurance Act, 1948, Hotel Receipts Tax Act, 1980, Income-tax Act, 1961, English Income Tax Act, 1918, Catering Wages Act, 1943, U.K. Income Tax Act

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income-tax Act, 1961, ss. 192, 201(1) - Tip paid to employees - Liability of employer to deduct tax at source - Determination - Assessees are engaged in business of owning, operating, and managing hotels, however, assessees had been paying tips to its employees but not deducting taxes, hence, AO treated receipt of tips as income under head 'salary' in hands of various employees and held that assessees were liable to deduct tax at source from such payments u/s. 192 of Act - However, appellant authority held that assessees could not be treated as assessees-in-default u/s. 201(1) of Act for non-deduction of tax on tips collected by them and distributed to their employees - Tribunal affirmed order passed by appellant authority - However, HC declared to be assessees-in-default u/s. 201 of Act - Whether assessee is liable to deduct tax at source for payment of tips to employees.

Held, amount of tip paid by employer to employees has no reference to contract of employment at all. Tips are received by employer in fiduciary capacity as trustee for payments that are received from customers which they disburse to their employees for service rendered to customer. Hence, there is no reference to contract of employment when these amounts are paid by employer to employee. SC held that as income from tips would be chargeable in hands of employees as income from other sources, such tips being received from customers and not from employer, hence, s. 192 of Act would not get attracted. SC held that since appellant is outside s. 192 of Act, appellants cannot be stated to be assessees-in-default and hence no question of interest therefore arises. Hence, impugned order passed by HC is set aside. Appeal filed by assessee is allowed. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Employer is not liable to deduct tax at source for tip, since payment is gratuitous, and assessees act as mere trustees in collecting tips charged to customers and then pass over same to employees and it has no connection with contract of employment between employer and employee.
