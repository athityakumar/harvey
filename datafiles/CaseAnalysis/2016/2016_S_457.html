Chaman and another v State of Uttrakhand

Supreme Court of India

19 April 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Amitava Roy, Sharad Arvind Bobde

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 273; AIR 2016 SC 1912; 2016 CRLJ 2330; 2016 (3) JCC 1496; JT 2016 (4) SC 187; 2016(4) SCALE 188

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Presumption, FIR, Omission, Indian Evidence Act, 1872, Description, Proof, Search, Cause Of Death, Dead Body, Inference, Oath, Informant, Identification, Discrepancy, Doctrine, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Husband, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, Seizure

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 364, 34 - Alleged Abduction and Murder - Conviction - Justification - FIR was lodged against appellant u/ss. 302, 364, 34 of IPC - Trial Court convicted appellant accused /ss. 302, 364, 34 of IPC which was confirmed by HC - Whether impugned order passed by HC convicting appellant was justified and in accordance with law.

Held, FIR clearly stated that there were dual visits of appellants to house of deceased in search of his son. Further, contents prove that on date of incident, complainant's husband was taken by appellant in presence of complainant PW1 and her daughter PW2. Further, PWs 1 and 2 identified appellant accused persons. Further, fact of recovery of rope, being led thereto by appellant is admissible in evidence against appellants. Further, motive for offence is also proved from facts stated by complainant. SC held that as prosecution had succeeded in establishing that deceased had been abducted by accused, they knew what happened to him until he was with them and if he was found murdered in short time, after abduction, hence, court is justified to draw presumption that accused had murdered him. Further, PW6 doctor conducting post-mortem examination stated that cause of death is asphyxia. Further, PW 6 that death of victim was during interval between abduction of deceased and detection of his dead body. Hence, SC held that conviction order passed by lower Courts is justified and in accordance with law. Appeals dismissed.

Ratio - When prosecution has proved guilt of accused beyond reasonable doubt, conviction of accused was justified and in accordance of law.
