State of Bihar and others v Sanjay Kumar and another

Supreme Court of India

06 January 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

R. Banumathi, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 10; (2016) 3 SCC 33; AIR 2016 SC 931; 2016(2) ALL MR 451; 2016 (148) FLR 588; JT 2016 (1) SC 84; 2016(1) SCALE 209; 2016 (2) SLR 198

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Bihar State Panchayat Raj Act, 2006, Recruitment Rules, 2006

Summary: Service - Recruitment Rules, 2006, r. 4(k)(vii)(a) - Failure to consider for appointment - Validity of amended rule - Determination - State govt. framed Manual 2006 and Rules - R. 4(k)(vii)(a) of Rules was amended which prescribed essential qualification for appointments of teachers and librarians in schools and also regulating appointments - Advertisement was issued by State Govt. for appointment to post of Librarian and Teacher in different schools - Order was issued by Department containing exhaustive list of twenty eight colleges/universities/degrees that were not then recognized by State Govt. for purpose of Rules and advertisement dt. 25-8-2008 and those degrees were not valid for employment of teachers - Further, Department issued another order declaring that degree of Sahityaalankar awarded by Deoghar Vidyapeeth was not valid for employment - Respondents applied for post of librarian and possessed degree of Sahityaalankar awarded by Deoghar Vidyapeeth - Respondent challenged validity, legality and correctness of amendment of r. 4(k) (vii) (a) of Rules as amended in 2008 - HC vide impugned order allowed petitions filed by respondents directing State to redo entire selection process - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, as per direction of HC, State of Bihar examined matter and by order dt. 25-11-2008 held that degree of Sahityaalankar cannot be valid degree for appointment as teacher. Further, it is further submitted by state that validity of Sahityaalankar degree from Hindi Vidyapeeth Deoghar and its equivalence with graduation degree has been considered in detail by HC in subsequent decisions filed by State and several other connected matters. SC held that having regard to stand of appellants and reliance placed upon order dt. 25-11-2008 and Press Note dt. 5-5-1988 and subsequent decision in CWJC 13343/2011 etc. and in interest of justice, SC deemed it necessary to remit matter back to HC for consideration of matter afresh after affording sufficient opportunity to both parties. Liberty is granted to parties to file additional documents/pleadings. Impugned order passed by HC is set aside. Matter remanded to lower Court. Order accordingly.

Ratio - Failure to consider candidate for appointment is not justified unless same is based on reasonable and just grounds.
