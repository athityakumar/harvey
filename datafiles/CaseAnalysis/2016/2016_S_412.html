Jagdish Narain Shukla v State of U. P. and others

Supreme Court of India

26 September 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Khanwilkar, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 1017; 2016 (4) RCR(Civil) 713; 2016(9) SCALE 308

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Foreign Exchange Management Act, 1999, Prevention of Money Laundering Act, 2002, Lokayukta, Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Constitution of India, 1950, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, Uttar Pradesh Lokayukta and Upa-Lokayuktas Act, 1975

Summary: Administrative - Practice & Procedure - Report of Lokayukta - Upon complaint made by complainant against MLC and then Cabinet Minister of UP, Lokayukta submitted report to Competent Authority for taking necessary action and recommended that investigation is to be entrusted to CBI or ED and further action to be taken in accordance with the result of investigation - However, as appellant was of the view that Competent Authority was not taking any steps to comply with said recommendations of Lokayukta, writ petition was filed praying for implementation of report of Lokayukta dt.22-2-2012 - However, DB of HC held that opinion of Lokayukta in the report could not be construed to be final or conclusive as it was a fact finding enquiry and a detailed enquiry is yet to be made after affording opportunity of hearing to the persons against whom complaint is made and that there was no element of public interest in the grievances made by appellant and with said findings, dismissed petition in limine - Hence, instant appeal - Whether impugned orders of HC are sustainable.

Held, writ petition was limited to directing Competent Authority to act upon recommendations made by Lokayukta and relief has worked out in view of the direction issued by Competent Authority to investigate into the factual matrix noticed in Lokayukta's report. Further, law enforcement agencies have moved into action and have collected information and material including with reference to representations and affidavits received in the course of the said investigation. As such, request of law enforcement agencies to give them some more time to complete the investigation/enquiries in relation to the acts of commission and omission of respondent nos.5 and 6 or any other persons privy thereto is acceded. Appeal disposed of.
