State of Maharashtra and others v Anita and another

Supreme Court of India

12 July 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

R. Banumathi, T.S. Thakur, Uday Umesh Lalit

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 508; (2016) 8 SCC 293; AIR 2016 SC 3333; 2016(6) ALL MR 494; 2016 (6) Bom.C.R. 390; 2016 (151) FLR 614; JT 2016 (7) SC 452; 2016(6) SCALE 807; [2016] 4 S.C.R. 882; 2016 (4) WLN(SC) 49

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Service

Keywords: Selection, Pay, Permanent, Terms And Conditions, Posts, Regular, Policy, Resolutions, Constitution of India, 1950, Appointment

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Service - Contractual appointment - Regularisation - Challenged -Appellants/State Govt. by a Resolution dt. 21-08-2006 approved creation of 471 posts in various cadres including Legal Advisors, Law Officers and Law Instructors - By a subsequent Resolution dt. 15-9-2006, Govt. maintained that 471 posts created shall be filled up on contractual basis by payment of consolidated pay - Aggrieved, respondents approached Tribunal - Tribunal partly allowed claim of respondents however, no direction was issued to appellants to regularise respondents - Respondents filed writ petitions before HC seeking for a direction to State Govt. to regularise their services - State Govt. also filed writ petition challenging impugned order of Tribunal - HC dismissed petitions and held that 471 posts created by State Govt. in various cadres are permanent posts and appointments should be permanent however, respondents cannot claim regularization - Hence, instant appeals.

Held, conditions of respondents engagement is governed by the terms of agreement. After having accepted contractual appointment, respondents were estopped from challenging the terms of their appointment. Further, respondents are not precluded from applying for said posts afresh subject to satisfaction of other eligibility criteria. HC did not keep in view various clauses in Govt. Resolutions dt. 21-08-2006 and 15-09-2006 and terms of agreement entered by respondents with appellants. Creation of posts was only for administrative purposes for sanction of amount towards expenditure incurred but merely because the posts were created they cannot be held to be permanent in nature. When Govt. has taken a policy decision to fill up 471 posts on contractual basis, Tribunal and HC ought not to have interfered with the policy decision to hold that appointments were permanent in nature. Impugned judgment of HC was set aside. Appeals allowed.

Ratio - After having accepted contractual appointment, respondents were estopped from challenging the terms of their appointment for regularization.
