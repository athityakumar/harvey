State of Kerala v P. B. Sourabhan and others

Supreme Court of India

04 March 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjan Gogoi, Prafulla C. Pant

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 193; (2016) 4 SCC 102; AIR 2016 SC 1194; 2016 CRLJ 1833; JT 2016 (2) SC 636; 2016 (1) KLT 988; 2016 (2) RCR(Criminal) 316; 2016(3) SCALE 118

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Jurisdiction, Assistant Commissioner, Two Cases, Further Investigation, Kerala Police Act, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 173(8), 36 - Kerala Police Act, s. 18 - Appointment of Superior police officer by State Police chief - Alleged jurisdiction exceeded - Determination - It is stated that over matrimonial disputes between parties, two police cases were registered - Complainant filed representation before State Police Chief for further investigation by competent/ neutral officer - Assistant Commissioner of Police was entrusted with further investigation of case - Further, Trial Court vide order granted permission for further investigation u/s. 173(8) of CrPC - Petitioner contended that impugned order passed by Trial Court are in excess of powers vested by s. 36 of CrPC Act and s. 18 of Act cannot override provisions of s. 36 of CrPC - HC passed order in favor of petitioner - Whether State Police Chief/Director General of Police is empowered to appoint superior police officer to investigate crime case registered outside territorial jurisdiction of such officer.

Held, s. 36 of CrPC does not debar exercise of powers by State Police Chief to appoint any superior officer who, in his opinion, would be competent and fit to investigate particular case keeping in view circumstances thereof. Further, SC held that s. 18 of Act does not confer any such power and merely recognizes State Police Chief as head of police force in State. SC held that impugned actions were not challenged before HC on grounds of malafide or being without justification and reasonable cause. Further, SC held that representation on basis of which appointment of special officer was made by State Police Chief intended for appointment of neutral and impartial police officer to conduct further investigation in fair and unbiased manner without specifically naming of any particular officer in said application. Hence impugned order passed by HC is set aside. Appeals allowed.

Ratio - Order passed by authority within its jurisdiction is justified and in accordance with law and cannot be challenged as set aside unless malafide intention of authority is proved.
