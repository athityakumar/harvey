Union of India and another v Indusind Bank Limited and another

Supreme Court of India

15 September 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

R.F. Nariman, C. Nagappan

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 653; (2016) 9 SCC 720; AIR 2016 SC 4374; 2016 (6) ALD(SC) 86; 2017(2) ALL MR 898; 2016 (118) ALR 842; 2016 (6) AWC 5521; 2016 (5) CTC 674; JT 2016 (12) SC 240; 2017 (1) MahLJ 783; 2017 (2) MPLJ 1; 2016 (4) RCR(Civil) 469B; 2017 (1) RLW 300; 2016(8) SCALE 779

Case Digest

Subject: Trade; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Legal Proceedings, Contract, Limitation Act, 1963, Limitation, Indian Contract Act, 1872, CONSTITUTION, Imports and Exports (Control) Act, 1947, Benami Transactions (Prohibition) Act, 1988, Interest On Delayed Payments to Small Scale and Ancillary Industrial Undertakings Act, 1993, Income-tax Act, 1961

Summary: Trade - Contract & Commercial - Imports and Exports (Control) Act, 1947 - Indian Contract Act, 1872, s. 28 - Interest on Delayed Payments to Small Scale and Ancillary Industrial Undertakings Act, 1993 - Furnishing documents failure - Bank guarantee invoked - Obligation absolved - Textile Commissioner invited applications vide notification dt. 9-1-1996 for export of staple cotton - Pursuant to notification, four sale contracts were executed between private party and exporters on January, 1996 - On 31-1-1996, exporters made an application with a bank guarantee of even date - On February, exporters were permitted to export the total quantity of 9175 bales vide an Allocation-cum-Registration Certificate dt. 6-2-1996 within a validity period of shipment up to 31-7-1996 - Textile Commissioner vide letters dt. 15-5-1997, invoked bank guarantees as exporters failed and neglected to furnish documents - Respondent/bank refused to pay under the said guarantees stating that the same could be invoked only within the extended period of 3 months i.e. up to 30-4-1997 and not later - Appellants and Textile Commissioner filed suits against exporters and Bank before HC - Single Judge held that invocation of bank guarantees being without time constraint was valid and the said suits were decreed in favour of appellants - On appeal, DB reversed judgment of Single Judge holding that bank guarantees were not invoked within time prescribed - Hence, instant appeals - Whether change made in s. 28 of the 1872 Act could be said to be clarificatory or declaratory of the law and hence retrospective.

Held, amendment does not purport to be either declaratory or clarificatory. It seeks to bring about a substantive change in the law by stating for the first time, that even where an agreement extinguishes the rights or discharges the liability of any party to an agreement, so as to restrict such party from enforcing his rights on the expiry of a specified period, such agreement would become void to that extent. These are substantive changes in the law which are remedial in nature and cannot have retrospective effect. S. 28 of the 1872 Act being substantive law, operates prospectively as retrospectivity is not clearly made out by its language. Being remedial in nature, and not clarificatory or declaratory of the law, by making certain agreements covered by s. 28(b) of the 1872 Act void for the first time, it is clear that rights and liabilities that have already accrued as a result of agreements entered into between parties are sought to be taken away. SC was of view that both Single Judge and DB were in error in holding that amended s. 28 of the 1872 Act would apply. Parliament has to a large extent redressed any grievance that may arise qua bank guarantees in particular, by adding an exception (iii) by an amendment made to s. 28 of the 1872 Act in 2012 w.e.f 18-1-2013. Since SC is not directly concerned with such amendment, suffice it to say that stipulations like the present would pass muster after 2013 if the specified period is not less than 1 year from the date of occurring or non-occurring of a specified event for extinguishment or discharge of a party from liability. Appeals dismissed.

Ratio - Presumption against retrospective operation is not applicable to declaratory statutes.
