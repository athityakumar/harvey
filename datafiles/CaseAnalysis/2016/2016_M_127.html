Muthuramalingam and others v State Represented by Inspector of Police

Supreme Court of India

09 December 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Pinaki Chandra Ghose, Amitava Roy

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 883; (2017) 1 SCC 477; AIR 2017 SC 50; 2017 CRLJ 1040; 2017 (1) MLJ(Crl) 79; 2017 (1) RCR(Criminal) 159; 2016(12) SCALE 671; [2016] 8 S.C.R. 594

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Life Imprisonment, Common Intention, Common Object, Assembly, Criminal Liability, CONSTITUTION, Arms Act, 1959, Unlawful Assembly, Attack, Rigorous Imprisonment, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Constitution of India, 1950, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 31 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 147, 148, 149, 302, 307, 324, 34, 506(ii) - Unlawful assembly - Murder - Conviction - Appellants/accused persons assembled unlawfully with deadly weapons and murdered 8 persons including one who succumbed to injuries in hospital - Trial Court convicted accused u/ss. 147, 148, 324, 307, 506(ii), 307 & 302 r/w s. 34 IPC and sentenced them to undergo RI for 10 years - On appeal, HC modified conviction and sentence imposed by Trial Court and convicted accused u/s. 302 r/w s. 149 IPC instead of 302 r/w s. 34 IPC and sentenced to undergo imprisonment for life for each count - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, in instant case motive was seen in the collective testimony of eye-witnesses PW1 to PW3 when accused came out from the bushes shouting "kill them", "hack them", "fire them", as also mentioned in complaint. A child was also mercilessly attacked in the incident with a spear on his chest. Accused no. 7 snatched away the child from her mother and killed her too with velstick.

Prosecution case was well established by the testimonies of eye-witnesses PW1 to PW3 and corroborated by PW4, wherein factum of unlawful assembly was proved. Appellants did cause the death of 8 persons in a barbaric and brutal manner wherein merciless killing of a child of only 1½ years was also involved. Therefore, accused in instant case do not deserve any sympathy. SC uphold the judgment passed by HC as far as awarding of sentences was concerned. However, the sentences should run in conformity with the observations made by Constitution Bench of SC. Impugned judgment passed by HC was modified to the above extent. Appeals disposed of.
