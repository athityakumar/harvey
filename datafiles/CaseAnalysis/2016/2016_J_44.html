Vatsala Shenoy v Joint Commissioner of Income Tax (Assessment), Mysore

Supreme Court of India

18 October 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, N.V. Ramana

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 736; (2017) 5 SCC 104; AIR 2016 SC 5299; [2016] 389 ITR 519; JT 2016 (10) SC 300; 2016(10) SCALE 147; [2016] 243 TAXMAN 152

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Partnership, Capital Asset, Capital Gains, Computation of Capital, Winding Up, Goodwill, Registration, Companies Act, 1956, Market Value, Assessing Officer, Partners, Partnership Firm, Partnership Deed, Finance Act, 1999, Chargeable To Tax, Income-tax Act, 1961

Summary: (A) Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income-tax Act, 1961, ss. 2(42C), 2(14), 45 - Sale of going concern - Capital gains - All assessees were partners of partnership firm which was sold to three other partners, AOP-3, as a going concern, but after dissolution of partnership firm, consideration received as a result thereof were treated as capital gains, and AO charged income tax thereon - Assailing the same, appeals were preferred before appellate authority, Tribunal and assessees also approached HC, but orders of AO were upheld by all - Hence, instant appeals by assessees contending that since it was sale of an ongoing concern, it had to be treated as a slump sale within the meaning of s.2(42C) of the Act - Whether there is capital gain on sale of ongoing concern.

Held, firm had not filed income tax returns after the date of dissolution as it stood dissolved and was no more in existence and for that reason, the income that was generated from the business, after the dissolution, was assessed by authorities in hands of such erstwhile partners as an AOP. Further, AOP was filing the returns and getting the same assessed in that capacity and paying income tax thereupon. Further, in orders passed by HC in company petition, from time to time, firm was always described as 'the dissolved partnership firm'. Thus, the assets which were sold ultimately on 20-11-1994 were of a dissolved partnership firm, though as a going concern. Further, the business was carried on by partners with controlling interest as an interim arrangement; the income was assessed in their hands as AOP and not in hands of the firm which had already been dissolved; assets of the company were put to sale in accordance with cl.16 of Partnership Deed of a dissolved firm, though as a going concern, and outgoing partners received their net share of the value of the assets of the firm out of the amount received by way of sale of the assets of the firm as per cl.16 of the Partnership Deed. Hence, assets of the firm that were sold were the capital assets within the meaning of s. 2(14) of the Act and gain there from is to be treated as capital gain within the meaning of s. 45 of the Act. Hence, when Official Liquidator distributed amount among the nine partners, including the assessees, after deducting the liability of each of the partners, HC had rightly held that the amount received by them is value of net assets of the firm which would attract capital gain. Further, partnership firm had dissolved and thereafter winding up proceedings were taken up in HC. The result of those proceedings was to sell the assets of the firm and distribute the share thereof to erstwhile partners. Thus, the transfer of the assets triggered the provisions of s.45 of the Act and making the capital gain subject to the payment of tax under the Act. Appeals disposed of.

(B) Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Association of persons - Income of firm - Whether income of the firm in A/Y 1995-96 could be taxed at hands of assessees.

Held, it is admitted case that 40% of income was allowed by HC to be retained by successful bidder, AOP-3, precisely for reason that 40% represented the tax which was to be paid on income generated by ongoing concern being run by Association of Persons, as authorized by HC. Secondly, in previous years, Department had taxed the AOP and the procedure had to continue in A/Y 1995-96 as well. However, HC had not considered said aspects. Hence, appeals of assessees are partly allowed to extent that business income/revenue income in A/Y in 1995-96 is to be assessed at hands of AOP-3, as AOP-3 in terms of orders of HC, retained tax amount from the consideration which was payable to assessees and it is AOP-3 which was supposed to file the return in that behalf and pay tax on the said revenue income. Appeals disposed of.
