Cheviti Venkanna Yadav v State of Telangana and others

Supreme Court of India

24 October 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, Shiva Kirti Singh

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 813; (2017) 1 SCC 283; AIR 2016 SC 4982; 2017 (1) ALD(SC) 193; JT 2016 (11) SC 310; 2016(10) SCALE 385; [2016] 7 S.C.R. 689

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Constitution; Trade; Agriculture & Agri. Products

Keywords: Retrospective Effect, Coking Coal Mines (Nationalisation) Act, 1972, Andhra Pradesh (Agricultural Produce and Livestock) Markets Act, 1966, Coking Coal Mines (Emergency Provisions) Act, 1971, Constitution of India, 1950, Andhra Pradesh Reorganisation Act, 2014, Andhra Pradesh (Agricultural Produce and Livestock) Markets (Amendment) Act, 2015, Coal Mines Nationalisation Laws (Amendment) Ordinance, 1986, Coal Mines Nationalisation Laws (Amendment) Act, 1986

Summary: (A) Constitution - Trade - Andhra Pradesh (Agricultural Produce and Livestock) Markets (Amendment) Act, 2015, ss. 5, 5(1), 5(2), 5(3), 6(1) - Andhra Pradesh (Agricultural Produce and Livestock) Markets Act, 1966, ss. 11, 11(1), 33(2)(a), 5, 5(1), 5(2), 5(3), 6 - Andhra Pradesh Reorganisation Act, 2014 - Ordinance passed - Challenged - Petitioners/appellants were appointed as Chairmen of Market Committees by State for a term of 3 years - Governor of State/respondents promulgated Ordinance No. 1 of 2015 and by virtue of said Ordinance s. 5 of the 2015 Act underwent two major changes through which total number of members in market committee was reduced from 18 to 14 and term of market committee was reduced from 3 to 1 years - Aggrieved, appellants challenged the same before HC - HC by impugned judgment and order declined to interfere and dismissed writ petitions - Hence, instant appeals - Whether State Legislature could have legislated for the period prior to coming into existence of State.

Held, the 2014 Act came into force on 02-06-2014. Submission was prior to the said date legislature that was not in existence as an entity could not have legislated relating to some aspect that covers the prior period. In M/s. Rattan Lal and Co. and another etc v. The Assessing Authority, Patiala and another, 1968 Indlaw SC 198, Court was dealing with competence of State pertaining to a legislation enacted by State by way of an amendment prior to reorganisation of State. In other words, the legislature of one of the States seeks to amend a law passed by composite State. Thus, after legislature came into existence, it has the competence to enact any law retrospectively or prospectively within the constitutional parameters. Appeals dismissed.

(B) Administrative - Agriculture & Agri. Products - Andhra Pradesh (Agricultural Produce and Livestock) Markets (Amendment) Act, 2015, ss. 5, 5(1), 5(2), 5(3), 6(1) - Andhra Pradesh (Agricultural Produce and Livestock) Markets Act, 1966, ss. 11, 11(1), 33(2)(a), 5, 5(1), 5(2), 5(3), 6 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Appointment - Substituted to nomination - Validity - Whether base of earlier judgment was removed to erase the effect of judgment.

Held, HC in its earlier judgment had struck down the amended provision on foundation that there was discrimination between existing appointees and future appointees to office of members, Vice-Chairmen and Chairmen. HC had opined that classification between two categories was not reasonable and it caused discomfort to art. 14 of Constitution. It had given emphasis on statutory safeguards meant for removal. Legislature after the decision of HC has amended the provision. By such amendment, it has removed the distinction between the existing members and the members who are to come in future. The members were meant to be members for the purpose of composition of market committee. SC made it clear that competent authority of State Govt. still can remove member or Vice-Chairman or Chairman taking recourse to other provisions prior to expiry of period. The status of members have been changed by amending the word "appointed" by substituting it with the word "nominated". Thus, legislature has retrospectively changed the meaning. In SC considered opinion, by virtue of amendment, the term which was reduced for a nominated member stands on a different footing. In Om Narain Agarwal and others v. Nagar Palika, Shahjahanpur and others, 1993 Indlaw SC 909, it was held that if an appointment was made initially by nomination, there can be no violation of any provision of Constitution in case legislature authorised State Govt. to terminate such appointment at its pleasure and to nominate new members in their place. It is because the nominated members do not have the will or authority of any residents of Municipal Board behind them as may be present in the case of an elected member. Appeals dismissed.

(C) Constitution- Agriculture & Agri. Products - Andhra Pradesh (Agricultural Produce and Livestock) Markets (Amendment) Act, 2015, ss. 5, 5(1), 5(2), 5(3), 6(1) - Andhra Pradesh (Agricultural Produce and Livestock) Markets Act, 1966, ss. 11, 11(1), 33(2)(a), 5, 5(1), 5(2), 5(3), 6 - Vested rights - Amendment - Whether by virtue of the amendment vested rights have been affected.

Held, the word "appointment" has been substituted by "nomination". It was an appointment by nomination. It is from certain categories for the purpose of representation. It was not appointment as the word ordinarily connotes. Legislature, in its wisdom, has substituted the word "appointment" and made it "nomination with retrospective effect". To enable it to curtail or reduce the term, procedure for removal remains intact. A nominee can go from office by efflux of time when the period was over. That was different than when he was removed. A nominated member, in present, can also be removed by adopting the procedure during the period. Otherwise, he should continue till his term was over and the term was one year. Appeals dismissed.
