Securities and Exchange Board of India and others v Gaurav Varshney and others

Supreme Court of India

15 July 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Jagdish Singh Khehar, C. Nagappan

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 528; [2016] 198 Comp Cas 86; 2016 (3) CompLJ 321; JT 2016 (8) SC 287; 2017 (1) MLJ(Crl) 170; 2016(7) SCALE 4; [2016] 7 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Securities Exchange Board of India

Keywords: Board Of Directors, Directorship, Companies, Winding Up, Jurisdiction, Meeting, Registration, Companies Act, 1956, Contract, Mandate, Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, Limitation, Particulars, Certificate Of Registration, Annual Return, Legislation, Secretary, Repayment, Securities, Resignation, Annual General Meeting, Incorporated, Securities And Exchange Board Of India Act, 1992, Incorporation, Investors, Collective Investment Schemes, Securities and Exchange Board of India (Collective Investment Schemes) Regulations, 1999, Securities Laws (Amendment) Act, 1995, Constitution of India, 1950, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973

Summary: Appeal Nos. 827-830 of 2012, 251 of 2015, 833-836 of 2012 and 832 of 2012

Practice & Procedure - Securities Exchange Board of India - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 200, 251, 460, 461, 462, 463, 464, 465, 465(2), 466, 468, 468(2)(b), 482 - Companies Act, 1956, ss. 108, 159, 159(1)(g), 303, 303(2) - Securities and Exchange Board of India Act, 1992, ss. 12, 12(1B), 24, 24(1), 26, 27, 30, 32 - Securities and Exchange Board of India (Collective Investment Schemes) Regulations, 1999, regns. 4, 5, 5(1), 68, 68(1), 68(2), 69, 70, 71, 72, 73, 74 - Collective investment - Forbidden - Validity - Appellant/Board framed Collective Investment Regulations u/s. 30 of the 1992 Act - Board filed a complaint against respondent nos. 1 and 2 u/s. 200 CrPC r/w ss. 24(1) and 27 of the 1992 Act alleging that they had breached the bar created by s. 12(1B) of the 1992 Act which had forbidden sponsoring or carrying on of a collective investment initiative without obtaining a certificate of registration from Board - Aggrieved, respondent nos. 1 and 2 filed criminal application before HC - HC by an order quashed complaint as well as order issued by Trial Court summoning respondent nos. 1 and 2 - Hence, instant appeal - Whether respondent nos. 1 and 2 had violated s. 12(1B) of the 1992 Act, by incorporating collective investment enterprise under the provisions of the 1956 Act in capacity of its first directors and promoters.

Held, bar created through s. 12(1B) of the 1992 Act forbidding new entrepreneurs from commencing activities concerning collective investment without obtaining a certificate of registration was strict and mandatory. Further, by incorporating company and immediately on its incorporation by sponsoring or carrying on a collective investment enterprise, without obtaining a certificate of registration from Board in accordance with Collective Investment Regulations, respondents had clearly breached bar created by s. 12 (1B) of the 1992 Act. Thus, proceedings initiated by Board against respondent nos. 1 and 2 calls for no interference, for reason that they relate to an alleged breach by incorporation of the Collective Investment Regulations by treating them as existing collective investment undertaking. By the time Collective Investment Regulations were notified, respondent nos. 1 and 2 had already severed their relationship with the company. Therefore, proceedings which were initiated against respondent nos. 1 and 2 and were quashed by HC call for no interference. Order accordingly.

Criminal Appeal No. 252 of 2015

Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 200, 251, 460, 461, 462, 463, 464, 465, 465(2), 466, 468, 468(2)(b), 482 - Companies Act, 1956, ss. 108, 159, 159(1)(g), 303, 303(2) - Securities and Exchange Board of India Act, 1992, ss. 12, 12(1B), 24, 24(1), 26, 27, 30, 32 - Securities and Exchange Board of India (Collective Investment Schemes) Regulations, 1999, regns. 4, 5, 5(1), 68, 68(1), 68(2), 69, 70, 71, 72, 73, 74 - Collective investment - Forbidden - Justifiability -Appellant/accused was inducted as director and his involvement in functioning of company was limited to tendering advice - Appellant resigned from company and submitted the same with Registrar of Companies in Form-32 - Board/respondent filed a complaint against appellant u/s. 200 CrPC r/w ss. 24(1) and 27 of the 1992 Act alleging that she has breached the bar created by s. 12(1B) of the 1992 Act, which had forbidden sponsoring or carrying on of a collective investment initiative without obtaining a certificate of registration from Board - Respondent filed criminal application before HC - HC by an order upheld complaint as well as order issued by Trial Court summoning appellant - Hence, instant appeal - Whether proceedings initiated against appellant was valid and justified.

Held, since Board had not denied fact that company did address the letter dt. 7-2-2000 to Board, making its intentions clear, that it was not desirous of continuing its activities any further, because of the stringent conditions postulated under Collective Investment Regulations notified on 25-1-1995, question of refund would arise only after intimation was furnished by Board u/regn. 73(2) of the 1999 Regulations to company or to appellant. Since details of such intimation by Board were not brought to the notice of SC on behalf of Board, SC is of the view that it was not open to Board to initiate action against company or its directors, till the expiry of 2 months from the date of receipt of intimation from Board. Therefore, proceedings initiated against appellant were wholly misconceived as it has not been established that appellant either violated regn. 5 r/w regns. 68 to 72, or regns. 73 and 74 of the 1999 Regulations. Thus, conviction and sentence imposed on appellant were set aside. Order accordingly.
