Star Sports India Private Limited v Prasar Bharati and others

Supreme Court of India

27 May 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, Prafulla C. Pant

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 394; AIR 2016 SC 2586; 2016 (4) AWC 3265; JT 2016 (5) SC 501; 2016 (5) MLJ 182; 2016(5) SCALE 661; [2016] 3 S.C.R. 118

Case Digest

Subject: Media & Entertainment

Keywords: Agency, Radio, Sports Broadcasting Signals (Mandatory Sharing with Prasar Bharati) Act, 2007, Constitution of India, 1950, Broadcasting organisation

Summary: Media & Entertainment - Sports Broadcasting Signals (Mandatory Sharing with Prasar Bharati) Act, 2007, ss. 3, 3(1), 4 - Sports Broadcasting Signals (Mandatory Sharing with Prasar Bharati) Rules, 2007, rr. 2(b), 5 - On-Screen credits - Sharing of revenue - Appellant filed writ petition, seeking declarations and reliefs, inter alia, for declaration that live broadcasting signals of sporting events shared by appellant with respondent no.1 u/s.3 of the Act is to be the best feed as received from site with all features inclusive of any commercials of event owner and without insertion of any commercial advertisements by appellant - However, HC held that s.3 of the Act casts obligation on broadcaster to share live broadcasting signals without its advertisements with Prasar Bharati and disposed of petition - Hence, instant appeal - Whether the logos of the advertisers contained in the 'world feed' shared by appellant with Prasar Bharati amounts to advertisement.

Held, logos of event sponsors, known as 'On-Screen Credits' contained in 'world feed' shared by appellant with Prasar Bharati and appearing at time of featuring replays etc., are the advertisements of sponsors like Pepsi, LG etc., and they have entered into arrangement for showing their logo on said occasions and sponsors pay for such On-Screen Credits. Further, sponsors advertise their company names with commercial motives in mind and so, such commercials of sponsors are commercial advertisements. Further, s.3(1) of the Act, mandates that sharing of live broadcasting signals with Prasar Bharati has to be 'without its advertisements'; however, exception is that, subject to sharing of revenue in the proportion prescribed in s.3(2) of the Act, broadcasting service provider could share contents along with advertisement. Hence, sharing of signals has to be without any advertisements and if advertisements are also to be included in signals, then there has to be sharing of revenue. Appeal dismissed.

Ratio - S.3 of the Act prohibits a TV broadcasting organisation from carrying the live television broadcast of a sporting event of national importance on cable or Direct-to-Home (DTH) networks in India, unless it simultaneously shared it, without its advertisements, with the Prasar Bharati
