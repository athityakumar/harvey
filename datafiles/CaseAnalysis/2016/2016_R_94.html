Ranveer Singh v State of U.P., Through Secy. and others

Supreme Court of India

22 July 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Shiva Kirti Singh, A.M. Khanwilkar

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 533; AIR 2016 SC 3753; 2016 (118) ALR 240; 2016 (118) ALR 474; 2017 (3) AWC 2566; 2016 (3) CLT(SC) 85; JT 2016 (7) SC 280; 2016 (3) RCR(Civil) 1015; 2016 (133) RD 338; 2016(7) SCALE 347; [2016] 4 S.C.R. 130; 2016 (3) UPLBEC 2116

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property

Keywords: Jurisdiction, Registration Act, 1908, Protest, Land Acquisition Act, 1894, Uttar Pradesh Land Acquisition (Determination of Compensation and Declaration of Award by Agreement) Rules, 1997

Summary: Land & Property - Administrative - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 11, 11(1), 17, 18, 23, 34, 4, 6 - Uttar Pradesh Land Acquisition (Determination of Compensation and Declaration of Award by Agreement) Rules, 1997 - Land acquisition - Compensation - Interest claimed - Appellant's land was acquired by authorities of respondent/State under the provisions of the Act on the basis of an agreement for compensation dt. 27-2-2003 followed by payment of such compensation - Appellant subsequently claimed interest u/s. 34 of the Act from the dt.15-2-2001 till 27-2-2003 - Trial Court rejected the same - Aggrieved, appellant filed writ petition before HC which was dismissed - On appeal, DB dismissed appeal - Hence, instant appeal - Whether, after entering into an agreement under the Rules and accepting the agreed amount without any protest or demand for further interest appellant can claim interest on ground of s. 34 of the Act for the period that had already lapsed between taking of possession and signing of agreement/payment of compensation.

Held, not only remedy under the Act of seeking enhancement is lost but substantive cause of action also vanishes when land owner agrees for a consent award and amount of compensation is accepted without any protest. Equitable considerations also cannot help appellant because agreed amount was paid without any delay, on the date of agreement itself. Notably, award passed on the basis of agreement with appellant stipulates amount of compensation at Rs. 329.76 per Sq.Yd. However, in the case of other claimants under the same notification who had not entered into such agreement rate was fixed at Rs. 50.57 per Sq.Yd. with 30% solatium and 12% interest from date of taking possession. Thus, agreement with appellant was a package with regard to compensation amount voluntarily accepted by appellant without any demur. The argument of equitable consideration is misplaced and ill-advised. In view of judgments of SC in the case of State Of Gujarat Etc. v. Daya Shamji Bhai Etc. 1995 Indlaw SC 1183 and in State of Karnataka and Another v. Sangappa Dyavappa Biradar and Others 2005 Indlaw SC 237, no error in the order of HC rejecting claim of appellant. Appeal dismissed.

Ratio - The right to seek reference for enhancement itself gets lost by accepting compensation without protest especially when there is an acceptance of agreement by land owner.
