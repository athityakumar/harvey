Centre For Public Interest Litigation v Union of India and others

Supreme Court of India

08 April 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, T.S. Thakur, R. Banumathi

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 256; (2016) 6 SCC 408; AIR 2016 SC 1777; 2016 (3) AWC 2378; 2016 (3) CompLJ 278; JT 2016 (4) SC 17; 2016 (3) MLJ 516; 2016(3) SCALE 712; [2016] 2 S.C.R. 699

Case Digest

Subject: Telecom

Keywords: Licensee, Societies Registration Act, 1860, Telecom Regulatory Authority of India Act, 1997, Civil Procedure, Constitution of India, 1950

Summary: Telecom - Alleged grant of license at lower fees - Migration of licenses - Alleged illegality - It is stated that respondent no.1 issued notice inviting applications for auction of 3G and 4G licenses which was were granted to respondent - Petitioner contended that amount at which license for voice telephony is granted to respondent No. 2 is less and in normal course grant of this license would have fetched more amount, hence, it is alleged that undue favour is given to respondent no.2 - Further, petitioner stated that respondent no.1 allowed migration of license to UL (Unified License) regime which is illegal and there should be two different auction for different spectrum - Hence, present appeal.

Held, with respect to migration of licenses to UL, view of various Department were taken into consideration which show that there was discussion on issue wherein pros and cons of migration of telecom licenses to UL regime were discussed; various apprehensions expressed were considered; and ultimately consensus emerged for switching over to UL regime. Hence, decision of Govt. permitting migration of telecom licenses to UL regime is valid, legal and without any blemish. Further, with respect to undue advantage to respondent no.2, SC held that once policy decision is taken to allow migration to all those who were holding spectrum and decision was not taken only for respondent No.2 individually, respondent No.2 also became entitled to avail benefit of said decision, hence, it cannot be said that any favoritism was granted to respondent no.2. Further, with respect to fixation of fees, SC held that TRAI, in its recommendations, had not prescribed any additional fee to be charged for migration of spectrum operators to UL regime. Further, respondent no.2 has paid spectrum price along with amount for migration to UL and entry fees which is prescribed fee. Hence, SC held that fees charged from respondent no.2 cannot be said to be less or that it has caused any wrongful loss to Govt. and wrongful gain to respondent no. 2. Hence, SC held that grant of license to respondent no.2 by authority is justified and in accordance with law. Petition dismissed.

Ratio - Policy decisions taken by authority after due deliberations and taking into consideration views of various departments cannot be quashed unless same is illegal and with malafide intention.
