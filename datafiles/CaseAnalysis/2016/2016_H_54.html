Vijay Kumar Mishra and another v High Court of Judicature at Patna and others

Supreme Court of India

09 August 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

J. Chelameswar, A.M. Sapre

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 572; (2016) 9 SCC 313; AIR 2016 SC 3698; 2016 (5) AWC 4546; 2017 (1) CalLT(SC) 65; 2016 (3) CLT(SC) 108; 2016 (231) DLT 633; 2017 (152) FLR 811; 2016 (2) ILR(Cut) 461; JT 2016 (8) SC 14; 2017 (2) MahLJ 9; 2017 (1) MPLJ 537; 2016 (2) OLR 753; 2016(7) SCALE 646; [2016] 3 S.C.R. 806

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Advocates & Judges

Keywords: Consultation, Pleader, Indian Bar Councils Act, 1926, Constitution of India, 1950

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Administrative - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 16, 233(2) - Indian Bar Councils Act, 1926 - Selection process - Bar of art. 233 - Advertisement was issued inviting applications from eligible Advocates for direct recruitment for vacancies - Petitioners appeared in Preliminary as well as in the Mains Examination pursuant to such advertisement - Meanwhile, petitioners were qualified for the Subordinate Judicial Service of the State of Bihar and joined Subordinate Judicial Service of State - Subsequently, result of the Mains Examination of the District Judge Entry Level (Direct from Bar) was published and both the petitioners qualified in the Mains Examination - HC published detail of interview schedule and issued Call Letters for interview to petitioners imposing condition in Interview Letter was 'No-Objection Certificate of Employer' - Therefore, petitioners filed representation before Registrar General, Patna HC to appear in the interview which was declined - Aggrieved, petitioners filed writ petition claiming that since they were eligible on date of inviting applications, action of HC in not permitting them to appear in interview is illegal - HC dismissed petitions holding that to permit appellant to participate in interview would be breaching the mandate of art. 233(2) of Constitution - Hence instant appeal - Whether the bar u/art. 233(2) of Constitution is only for appointment or even for participation in selection process.

Held, text of art. 233(2) only prohibits the appointment of a person as a District Judge, if such person is already in the service of either the Union or the State. It does not prohibit the consideration of the candidature of a person who is in the service of the Union or the State. A person who is in the service of either of the Union or the State would still have the option, if selected to join the service as a District Judge or continue with his existing employment. Compelling a person to resign his job even for the purpose of assessing his suitability for appointment as a District Judge, is not permitted either by the text of art. 233(2) nor contemplated under the scheme of the Constitution as it would not serve any Constitutionally desirable purpose. There is no bar for a person to apply for the post of district judge, if he otherwise, satisfies qualifications prescribed for the post while remaining in service of Union/State. Order accordingly.

Ratio - There is no bar for a person to apply for the post of district judge, if he otherwise, satisfies the qualifications prescribed for the post while remaining in service of Union/State.
