R. Rajashekar and others v Trinity House Building Co-operative Society and others

Supreme Court of India

15 September 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, Adarsh Kumar Goel

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 649; AIR 2016 SC 4329; 2016 (119) ALR 505; 2016 (5) AWC 5315; 2016 (4) CLT(SC) 9; JT 2016 (9) SC 178; 2016 (4) RCR(Civil) 786; 2017 (134) RD 145; 2016(8) SCALE 843; [2016] 4 S.C.R. 763; 2016 (4) WLN(SC) 81

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Verification, Jurisdiction, Specific Relief Act, 1963, Limitation Act, 1963, Acquisition Of Land, Agent, General Clauses Act, 1897, Public Purpose, Land Acquisition Officer, Societies, Karnataka Co-operative Societies Act, 1959, Constitution of India, 1950, Land Acquisition Act, 1894, Inquiry, Right to Fair Compensation and Transparency in Land Acquisition, Rehabilitation and Resettlement Act, 2013, Land Reforms Act, 1952

Summary: (A) Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 11A, 3(f)(vi), 39, 4(1), 41, 5A(2), 6(1) - Land Reforms Act, 1952 - Right to Fair Compensation and Transparency in Land Acquisition, Rehabilitation and Resettlement Act, 2013, s. 24(1)(a) - Notification - Land acquisition - Validity - Respondent/society entered into an agreement with outside agency for getting lands acquired in favour of respondent on 12-03-1985 - State Level Coordination Committee cleared the case of respondent for acquisition of lands in its favour on 25-09-1987 - State Govt. issued notification u/s. 4(1) of the 1894 Act dt. 09-06-1988, proposing to acquire the lands in question in favour of respondent - Landowners filed objection petitions before Land Acquisition Officer which were rejected - Aggrieved, landowners filed writ petitions before HC challenging validity of impugned notification - Single Judge quashed impugned notifications and acquisition proceedings holding that lands could not have been acquired by State Govt. in favour of a society for the purpose of providing residential sites to its members without following the procedure provided under Part VII of the 1894 Act - On appeals, DB allowed appeals and restored acquisition proceedings holding that case of respondent was considered by State Level Co-ordination Committee on 25-09-1987 and on the basis of clearance granted by Committee State Govt. granted approval to acquire lands in question - Hence, instant appeals - Whether acquisition of lands in favour of respondent in impugned acquisition proceedings is covered under the definition of the term public purpose, as defined u/s. 3(f)(vi) of the 1894 Act.

Held, acquisition proceedings in instant case cannot be said to be one for 'public purpose' u/s. 3(f)(vi) of the 1894 Act, especially in the light of the fact that not only was there no scheme formulated by respondent and approved by State Govt. for the said purpose before initiating acquisition proceedings, but that evidence on record clearly indicates that respondent paid consideration to proprietor of outside agency to act as agent between it and State Govt. to ensure that lands of original land owners are acquired in its favour. Further, State Govt. did not even take into consideration finding of Committee report, which found that respondent was issuing membership to persons fraudulently, including to those persons who were not even residing within the area of operation of jurisdiction of society. The acquisition of lands of two different villages by issuing impugned notifications by State Govt. in exercise of its eminent domain power at the instance of a middleman amounts to a mala fide exercise of power by State Govt. The stand of State Govt. clearly goes to show seriousness in which it has exercised its eminent domain power in such important case of acquisition of lands of poor agricultural workers in blatant violation of the provisions of the 1894 Act and law laid down by SC. Therefore, impugned notifications issued u/ss. 4(1) and 6(1) of the 1894 Act are bad in law as the same suffer from not only legal mala fides but also legal malice, which amounts to colourable exercise of power by State Govt. and same are liable to be quashed. The acquisition proceedings in respect of acquired lands are liable to be quashed and there is no need for SC to render an opinion on this question of law in these cases, as interesting as it is. Appeals allowed.

(B) Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 11A, 3(f)(vi), 39, 4(1), 41, 5A(2), 6(1) - Land Reforms Act, 1952 - Right to Fair Compensation and Transparency in Land Acquisition, Rehabilitation and Resettlement Act, 2013, s. 24(1)(a) - Acquisition proceedings - Quash - Relief entitled to - Whether appellants were entitled for the reliefs as prayed by them.

Held, acquisition proceedings in instant case are liable to be quashed. Appellants contended that said question has already been answered by SC in H.M.T. House Building Co-Op. Society v. Syed Khader & Ors., 1995 Indlaw SC 1559, wherein it was held that once the proceedings are void ab initio in law for non-compliance with mandatory statutory requirement of prior approval of the Scheme, the original owners cannot be shut out from Court in challenging the acquisition proceedings and therefore, they are entitled to challenge the same at any point of time even in the collateral proceedings. SC has accepted the above contention as the same is well founded and tenable in law. Thus, the transactions of alleged sales made during the pendency of proceedings are wholly illegal and void ab initio in law and therefore the same cannot be allowed to sustain in law. The said sale transactions are also contrary to law which is in operation in respect of agricultural lands. Impugned judgment and order of DB was set aside and restore judgment and order of Single Judge, wherein acquisition notifications and proceedings in respect of lands of some of original landowners have been quashed at their instance. Dpty. Commissioner was directed o to extend all such protection and assistance to landowners to ensure that SC judgment and order is implemented properly. Appeals allowed.
