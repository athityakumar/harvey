Shyam Pal v Dayawati Besoya and another

Supreme Court of India

28 October 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Amitava Roy, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 801; (2016) 10 SCC 761; AIR 2016 SC 5021; 2016 (4) BC 592; 2017 CRLJ 166; JT 2016 (10) SC 379; 2017 (2) MPLJ 56; 2017 (1) OLR 101; 2016 (4) RCR(Civil) 892; 2016 (4) RCR(Criminal) 790; 2016(10) SCALE 441

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, Duration, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, ss. 138, 139 - Single transaction - Concurrent running of substantive sentence - Complainant filed two complaints against appellant as cheques issued by him were dishonored with remarks 'funds insufficient' and upon trial, Trial court convicted appellant u/s.138 of the Act in both proceedings and on appeal, appellate court affirmed conviction recorded by Trial Court in both proceedings - Aggrieved, appellant filed two criminal revision petitions before HC and HC disposed of said petitions maintaining substantive sentence of simple imprisonment for 10 months and fine of Rs. 6,50,000 as compensation awarded by Trial Court but reduced default sentence from six months Simple imprisonment to that of three months - Further, HC passed two orders dt.22-2-2016 declining prayer for directing release of appellant, having already served the sentence in all being in custody from 25-2-2015 - Aggrieved, appellant filed SLP before SC but the same were dismissed and conviction and sentence awarded to appellant had attained finality - However, instant appeal by appellant contending that sentence awarded ought to run concurrently but HC failed to appreciate the same.

Held, the facts pleaded and proved unassailably demonstrate that loans advanced had been in the course of a series of transactions between the same parties on same terms and conditions. Further, in both proceedings, following the conviction of the appellant u/s. 138 of the Act, the same sentences as well have been awarded. There is thus an overwhelming identicalness in the features of both the cases permitting, the two transactions, though undertaken at different points of time, to be deemed as a singular transaction or two segments of one transaction. Hence, considering nature of the transactions between the parties thereto, the offences involved, the sentences awarded and the period of detention of the appellant as on date as evidenced by certificate to that effect, appellant is entitled to the benefit of the discretion contained in s.427 of CrPC. As such, substantive sentences of 10 months simple imprisonment awarded to appellant in two complaint cases would run concurrently. Appeals disposed of.

Ratio - Single transaction is the decisive factor that enables the Court to direct subsequent sentence to run concurrently with the previous one. However, the direction for concurrent running of sentence would be limited to the substantive sentence alone.
