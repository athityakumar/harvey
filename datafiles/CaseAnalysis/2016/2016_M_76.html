Union of India and another v Meghmani Organics Limited and others

Supreme Court of India

07 October 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Shiva Kirti Singh, J. Chelameswar, A.M. Sapre

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 794; (2016) 10 SCC 28; AIR 2016 SC 4733; 2016 (340) E.L.T. 449; JT 2016 (11) SC 214; 2016(9) SCALE 723; [2016] 9 S.C.R. 353

Case Digest

Subject: Anti-Dumping; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Natural Justice, Designated Authority, INDIRECT TAX, Customs Tariff Act, 1975, Normal Value, Customs Tariff (Identification, Assessment and Collection of Anti-dumping Duty on Dumped Articles and for Determination of Injury) Rules, 1995, CONSTITUTION, Right To Information Act, 2005, Constitution of India, 1950, Provisional Duty

Summary: Anti-dumping - Practice & Procedure - Customs Tariff (Identification, Assessment and Collection of Anti-Dumping Duty on Dumped Articles and For Determination of Injury) Rules, 1995, r. 7 - Confidentiality of information - In view of judgment of DB of HC recording views of UOI and Designated Authority (DA) that Reliance Industries case needed fresh look, instant reference on whether the interpretation placed upon r.7 of the Rules is correct insofar as it diminishes the rule of confidentiality statutorily provided for u/r.7.

Held, facts and issues involved in Reliance Industries case were entirely different and the Court was not examining the provisions of r.7 in any detail but made rather scathing observations against DA because the DA claimed confidentiality not in respect of any information but in respect of its findings based upon information supplied by the same party who was aggrieved by non-supply of the findings. The observations in Reliance Industries case were to be understood in the fact situation of that case. Hence, observations made in case of Reliance Industries are fully justified and do not require any review. Further, r.7 does not postulate that DA could claim confidentiality and that too not in respect of any information supplied by a party but in respect of its reasons or findings derived from information supplied by the same very party. Reference answered to the affect that - Reliance Industries case had not got into the details of the relevant Rules including r.7 and the observations made therein in respect of rule of confidentiality as spelt out in r.7 of the Rules does not diminish the scope of r.7 as provided. Further, the reasons or findings could not be equated with the information supplied by a party claiming confidentiality in respect thereto and r.7 does not empower the DA to claim any confidentiality in respect of reasons for its finding given against a party. Directions are issued to post the cases before appropriate Bench for disposal on merits. Order accordingly.

Ratio - Ratio Decidendi consists in the reasons formulated by the court for resolving an issue arising for determination and not in what could logically appear to flow from observations on non issues.
