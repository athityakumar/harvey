Anant Prakash Sinha @ Anant Sinha v State of Haryana and another

Supreme Court of India

04 March 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, Shiva Kirti Singh

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 213; AIR 2016 SC 1197; 2016 CRLJ 1836; 2016 (2) CTC 767; 2016 (2) JCC 1154; JT 2016 (3) SC 105; 2016 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1345; 2016 (2) MLJ(Crl) 294; 2016 (2) RCR(Criminal) 327; 2016 (3) RLW 2024; 2016(3) SCALE 107; [2016] 2 S.C.R. 128

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: FIR, Jurisdiction, Defect, Misappropriation, Reliance, Prosecutor, Public Prosecutor, Informant, Dowry, Interfere, Terrorist And Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Act, 1987, Explosives Act, 1884, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Husband, Constitution of India, 1950, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, Complainant

Summary: (A) Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 216 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34,323, 406, 498A - Addition of Charge - Jurisdiction of Trial Court - Determination - Respondent no.2 filed FIR against appellant husband and her mother-in-law u/ss. 498A,323, 34 of IPC alleging that appellant husband was insistent upon getting mutual divorce and on her resistance, he had physically assaulted her and deprived her of basic facilities of life - Further, it is alleged that accused person demanded dowry and non-meeting of same by family members of wife resulted in physically assaulted by accused persons - Further, appellant was charge sheeted appellant husband u/ss. 498A and 323 IPC - Trial court vide order framed charges against husband for commission of said offences - Further, when matter was pending, application dt. 31-7-2014 u/s. 216 of CrPC was filed by informant-wife for framing additional charge u/s. 406 IPC against husband and mother-in-law - Trial Court in view of specific allegations regarding misappropriation of her entire stridhan by husband and other statements recorded during investigation, prima facie case for criminal breach of trust was made out and, accordingly, allowed application u/s. 216 of CrPC against husband and mother-in-law - Further, revisional court dwelt upon law pertaining to alteration and addition of charges and came to hold that framing of charge against mother-in-law was unsustainable but framing of additional charge u/s. 406 of IPC against husband appellant herein, could not be faulted - HC vide order held that Trial Court has spelt out reasons that have necessitated for addition of charge and hence, impugned order did not warrant any interference - Whether without evidence being adduced another charge could be added.

Held, Harihar Chakravarty v State of W.B, 1953 Indlaw SC 124, relied on, SC held that court can change or alter charge if there is defect or something is left out, however, it must be founded on material available on record. Further, it can be on basis of complaint or FIR or accompanying documents or material brought on record during course of trial. It can also be done at any time before pronouncement of judgment. SC held that it is not necessary to advert to each and every circumstance. SC held that if court has not framed charge despite material on record, it has jurisdiction to add charge and alter charge. SC held that charge so framed by Trial Court is in accord with materials produced before him or if subsequent evidence comes on record. Further, it is obligatory on part of court to see that no prejudice is caused to accused and he is allowed to have fair trial. Appeal dismissed.

(B) Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Application to add charge - Application to be filed by public prosecutor - Justification - Appellant contended that Trial court could not have entertained application preferred by respondent no.2 wife for such application is incompetent because it has to be filed by public prosecutor - Whether application made my respondent no.2 is maintainable.

Held, instant case does not pertain to trial or any area by which private lawyer takes control of proceedings. Further, application was filed by respondent no.2 to add charge u/s. 406 of IPC as there were allegations against husband about criminal breach of trust as far as her stridhan is concerned. SC held that it was, in way, bringing to notice of Trial Court about defect in framing of charge. Court could have done it suo motu and in such situation, it could not be held that there is fault on part of Trial Court in entertaining said application. Further, Trial Court has referred to materials and recorded his prima facie satisfaction. There is no error in said prima facie view. Further, SC found no error in revisional order by which it has set aside charge framed against mother-in-law. Hence, impugned order passed by HC is justified and in accordance with law. Appeal dismissed.

Ratio - Trial Court could issue direction for alteration of charge if there were materials before it in complaint or any evidence to justify such action.
