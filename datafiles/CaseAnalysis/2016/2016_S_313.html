Jamnadas and another v State of M. P.

Supreme Court of India

29 June 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Prafulla C. Pant, D.Y. Chandrachud

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 438; 2016 (2) JabLJ 364; 2016 (4) JCC 2263; JT 2016 (6) SC 189; 2017 (1) MLJ(Crl) 158; 2016 (3) RCR(Criminal) 924; 2016(6) SCALE 1; [2016] 5 S.C.R. 599

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Burden Of Proof, Common Intention, Bone, Indian Evidence Act, 1872, Proof, Reasonable Doubt, Head Constable, Marks, Reliance, Abdomen, Blood, Chain, Dead Body, Hair, Autopsy, Kidney, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Husband, Constitution of India, 1950, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, Plea

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1960, ss. 201, 302, 304B, 34, 498A - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 161(2), 313, 313(2), 315(1) - Murder - Conviction Challenged - Appellants/accused nos. 1-3 were held guilty of murder of 'X' by Trial Court - Trial Court convicted appellant nos. 1-3 u/s. 302 of IPC and sentenced to suffer imprisonment for life with fine of Rs. 5000 and in default to under further RI for one year - Trial Court further convicted accused no.3 u/s. 201 of IPC and sentenced to suffer RI for 3 years with fine of Rs. 5000 and in default to under RI for 3 months - HC dismissed appeals of accused - Hence, instant appeals.

Held, in a case of circumstantial evidence charge can be said to have been established on fulfillment of following conditions (i) The circumstances from which the conclusion of guilt is drawn should be fully established. The accused must be, and not merely may be guilty, before a court can convict and the mental distance between 'may be' and 'must be' is long and divides vague conjectures from sure conclusions; (ii) The facts so established should be consistent only with the hypothesis of the guilt of the accused, that is to say, they should not be explainable on any other hypothesis except that the accused is guilty; (iii) The circumstances should be of a conclusive nature and tendency; (iv) They should exclude every possible hypothesis except the one to be proved; and (v) There must be a chain of evidence so complete as not to leave any reasonable ground for the conclusion consistent with the innocence of the accused and must show that in all human probability the act must have been done by the accused. Further, when the accused offers an explanation and that explanation is found to be untrue, then the same offers an additional link in the chain of circumstances, to complete the chain. In instant case, there is consistent view taken by both the courts below that the appellants had acted in common intention with co-accused no.3 in commission of murder of the deceased. Chain of circumstances against the appellants is complete, and the defence theory that they were in the shop cannot be accepted. Hence, impugned order does not warrant any interference. Appeals dismissed.
