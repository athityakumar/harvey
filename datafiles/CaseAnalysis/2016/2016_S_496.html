Sukh Ram v State of Himachal Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

25 July 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

R. Banumathi, V. Gopala Gowda

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 546; AIR 2016 SC 3548; 2016 ALL MR (Cri) 3561; 2016 CRLJ 4146; 2016 (4) JCC 2368; JT 2016 (7) SC 397; 2016 (3) RCR(Criminal) 919; 2016(7) SCALE 354; [2016] 3 S.C.R. 254

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Conspiracy, Indian Evidence Act, 1872, Expert, Handwriting Expert, Signatures, Handwriting, Prospective, Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, Plea, Identification of Prisoners Act, 1980

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 311A - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 73 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120B, 409, 420, 467, 468, 471 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, s. 13(2) - Misappropriation of loans - Conviction - Sustainability - During the period 1983-1986, there was a govt. scheme for providing loans at cheaper interest rates to poor persons living below poverty line for running small businesses and for development of land etc - Bank disbursed loans to beneficiaries - Appellant/accused was a Gram Sewak during said period - Appellant while submitting applications on behalf of villagers for loans was involved in misappropriation of loan amounts by forging their signatures and thumb impressions on applications and acknowledgement receipts - Charges were framed against appellant u/ss. 409, 467, 468, 471, 120-B and 420 IPC and u/s. 13(2) of the 1988 Act - Trial Court acquitted accused holding that in absence of legal evidence that appellant and others have forged loan documents it cannot be concluded that accused had entered into conspiracy of committing forgery and cheating - On appeal, HC reversed judgment of acquittal and convicted him for offences punishable u/ss. 468 and 471 of IPC - Hence, instant appeals.

Held, occurrence was of the year 1983-1986 and therefore, authority of Magistrate to take specimen signatures of PW5 and PW7 during the course of investigation cannot be disputed. In any event, even dehors opinion evidence of handwriting expert there is clear oral evidence of PW5 and PW7 denying their signatures in loan application and other documents. Affirming evidence of PWs 5 and 7 and analysis of evidence, HC has rightly reversed judgment of acquittal and found appellant guilty of offences u/ss. 468 and 471 IPC. Further, appellant is more than 75 years of age and is suffering from severe ailments he has prayed for reduction of sentence of imprisonment. Considering the facts and circumstances of the case and that innocence of villagers has been misused to siphon public money, SC was not inclined to reduce the sentence of imprisonment of appellant. HC rightly reversed judgment of acquittal of accused. Appeals dismissed.
