Malati Sardar v National Insurance Company Limited and others

Supreme Court of India

05 January 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Adarsh Kumar Goel, Anil R. Dave

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 1; (2016) 3 SCC 43; 2016 (1) ACC 1; 2016 ACJ 542; AIR 2016 SC 247; 2016 (2) ALD(SC) 99; 2016 (114) ALR 701; 2016 (1) AWC 972; 2016 (1) CalLT(SC) 61; 2016 (3) CTC 332; 2016 (2) JCC 921; JT 2016 (1) SC 4; 2016 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 833; 2016 (5) MahLJ 488; 2016 (1) MLJ 525; 2016 (4) MPLJ 17; 2016 (1) RCR(Civil) 908; 2016 (2) RLW 898; 2016(1) SCALE 133; [2016] 1 S.C.R. 601; 2016 (1) WLN(SC) 162

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance

Keywords: Armed Forces Tribunal Act, 2007

Summary: Insurance - Carriers & Transportation - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 21 - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, s. 166 - Rejection of Claim - Lack of jurisdiction - Justification - Deceased aged 26 years was hit by bus belonging to respondent company at Hoogly, in State of West Bengal and died - Appellant filed application u/s. 166 under Act for compensation before Tribunal - Tribunal granted compensation - Appeal filed before HC on ground of lack of territorial jurisdiction of the Tribunal - Respondent contended that accident took place at Hoogly and claimant resided at Hoogly hence, office of respondent being at Kolkata did not attract jurisdiction of Kolkata Tribunal - HC upheld objection of respondent and allowed appeal of respondent company and directed refund of amount paid to appellant - Hence, instant appeal Whether Tribunal at Kolkata had jurisdiction to decide claim application u/s. 166 of Act when accident took place outside Kolkata jurisdiction and claimant also resided outside Kolkata jurisdiction, but respondent being juristic person carried on business at Kolkata.

Held, Mantoo Sarkar v Oriental Insurance Company Limited and Others, 2008 Indlaw SC 2001, followed wherein it was held that jurisdiction of Tribunal was wider than civil court and Tribunal could follow provisions of CPC. In the instant case, HC was not justified in setting aside award of Tribunal in absence of any failure of justice even if there was merit in plea of lack of territorial jurisdiction. Further, respondent insurance company which was main contesting respondent had its business at Kolkata. SC held that there is no bar to claim petition being filed at place where insurance company, which is main contesting parties has its business. SC held that there is no prejudice to any party and there is no failure of justice. SC held that HC failed to notice provision of s. 21 of CPC. Impugned order passed by HC is set aside and award passed by Tribunal is restored. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Provision for territorial jurisdiction has to be interpreted consistent with the object of facilitating remedies for the victims of accidents.
