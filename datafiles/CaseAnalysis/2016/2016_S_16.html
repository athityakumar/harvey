S. P. S. Rathore v C. B. I. and another

Supreme Court of India

23 September 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

R.K. Agrawal, V. Gopala Gowda

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 665; (2017) 5 SCC 817; AIR 2016 SC 4486; 2017 CRLJ 537; 2016 (4) JCC 2687; JT 2016 (9) SC 316; 2017 (1) MLJ(Crl) 63; 2016 (4) RCR(Criminal) 454; 2016(9) SCALE 125; [2016] 5 S.C.R. 335

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Direct Evidence, Modesty, FIR, CBI, Value, Indian Evidence Act, 1872, Signature, Proof, Expert, Handwriting Expert, Age, Secretary, Corroboration, Chain, Implicate, Signatures, Contents, Handwriting, Admissible, Deposition, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, Complainant, Association

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 473 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 157, 45 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 306, 354, 509 - Molestation - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant/accused allegedly molested deceased/victim due to which deceased committed suicide by consuming poison - Charge sheet was filed against accused u/ss. 306, 354 IPC - Trial Court by its judgment and order held appellant guilty of offence u/s. 354 IPC and sentenced him to suffer RI for 6 months with fine - On appeal, Appellate Court dismissed appeal and enhanced sentence of RI for 1 1/2 years - Aggrieved, accused filed revision petition before HC which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether there was any perversity or illegality in impugned judgment of Courts below.

Held, sole evidence of a handwriting expert is not sufficient for recording a definite finding about the writing being of a certain person or not. It is opinion evidence and it can rarely, if ever, take the place of substantive evidence. Before acting on such evidence, it is usual to see if it is corroborated either by direct evidence or by circumstantial evidence. In instant case there is nothing on record on the basis of which it can be said that the tender age of victim was exploited for the benefit of PW1. HC rightly held that adverse inference against prosecution can be drawn only if it withholds certain evidence and not merely on account of its failure to obtain certain evidence. SC was of opinion that they were not in any way connected with the actual commission of offence and even in their absence commission of the offence of molestation by accused stands well proved by unimpeachable testimony of eye witness PW13 to the incident. PW13 withstood her testimony from beginning till the end and her deposition was found reliable and corroborative with other prosecution witnesses and both Courts below were right in upholding conviction of accused u/s. 354 IPC. However, keeping in view the old age and physical condition of appellant SC do not think it expedient to put him back in jail. While SC uphold the findings as to the guilt of appellant, SC were of the opinion that cause of justice would be best sub-served when the sentence of accused would be altered to the period already undergone. Therefore, sentence of appellant was reduced to the period already undergone by him as a special case considering his very advanced age. Impugned conviction of appellant u/s. 354 IPC while modifying the sentence to the period already undergone. Appeal disposed of.
