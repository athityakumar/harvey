Videocon Industries Limited and another v State of Maharshtra and others

Supreme Court of India

19 April 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, Shiva Kirti Singh

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 481; AIR 2016 SC 2843; 2016 ALL MR (Cri) 3124; 2016 (337) E.L.T. 3; 2016 (3) JCC 1661; JT 2016 (6) SC 33; 2016 (3) RCR(Criminal) 261; 2016(5) SCALE 753; [2016] 2 S.C.R. 741

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Foreign Exchange Regulation Act, 1973, Constitution of India, 1950, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 245 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Foreign Exchange Regulation Act, 1973, ss. 18, 18(2),18(3),56(1)(i) - Exoneration - Applicability - Appellants-firm/accused were charged for offence punishable u/ss. 56(1)(i) of the Act - Meanwhile, an application was filed u/s. 245 CrPC for discharge of accused before Tribunal - Tribunal dislodged finding of Adjudicating authority - On appeal, Single Judge dismissed appeal holding that Tribunal had not given any finding on merits of the case and its findings were based on technical grounds - Hence, instant appeals - Whether allegation in adjudication proceedings as well as proceeding for prosecution is identical and exoneration of person concerned in adjudication proceedings is on merits.

Held, there is no contravention of provisions of the Act in adjudication proceedings. The trial of person concerned shall be an abuse of process of Court. Further, when there is a finding by Enforcement Directorate in adjudication proceeding that there is no contravention of any of provisions of the Act, it would be unjust and an abuse of process of Court to permit Enforcement Directorate to continue with criminal prosecution. HC without an assail to order passed by Tribunal, has adverted to the same and opined that it does not subscribe to view expressed by Tribunal that ss. 18(2) and 18(3) of the Act were not applicable to transaction in question. HC could not have done that. Thus, judgment of Tribunal is on merits, inasmuch as findings were recorded after analysis of facts and arrived at conclusion that appellants had not violated the provisions of the Act. Therefore, it cannot be said that it is a judgment rendered on technical grounds. HC has totally erred in law. Impugned judgment and order of HC was set aside. Appeals allowed.

Ratio - In case of exoneration, where allegation is found to be not sustainable and person held innocent, criminal prosecution on same set of facts cannot be allowed, underlying principle being higher standard of proof in criminal cases.
