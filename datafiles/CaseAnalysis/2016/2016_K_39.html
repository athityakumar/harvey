Veerendra Kr. Gautam and others v Karuna Nidhan Upadhyay and others

Supreme Court of India

15 July 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla, Sharad Arvind Bobde

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 537; AIR 2016 SC 3373; 2016 (118) ALR 344; 2017 (3) AWC 2938; JT 2016 (7) SC 367; 2016 (6) MLJ 686; 2016(7) SCALE 82; [2016] 7 S.C.R. 223; 2016 (3) UPLBEC 2311

Case Digest

Subject: Education; Service

Keywords: Experience, Award, Selection, Vacancies, Interview, Pay, Procedure, Qualification, Teacher, Rule, Date, Merit, Posts, Recruitment, Validity Of, Appeal, Public Service Commission, Criteria, Advertisement, Grounds, Uttar Pradesh State Universities Act, 1973, Uttar Pradesh Higher Education Services Commission Act, 1980, Reservation, Universities, Uttar Pradesh Higher Education Services Commission (Procedure for Selection of Teachers) Regulations, 1983, Uttar Pradesh Higher Education Services Commission (Procedure and Conduct of Business) Regulations, 1983, Writ Petition, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Constitution of India, 1950, Post, Appointment

Summary: (A) Service - Education - Uttar Pradesh Higher Education Services Commission Act, 1980, ss. 31, 4(2)(a)(g), 4(2)(e) - Uttar Pradesh State Universities Act, 1973, s. 50 - Uttar Pradesh Higher Education Services Commission (Procedure and Conduct of Business) Regulations, 1983 - Uttar Pradesh Higher Education Services Commission (Procedure for Selection of Teachers) Regulations, 1983, reg. 6(1) - Writ petition - Estopped - Appellants challenged the selection dt. 15-05-2007 to the post of Principals in Degree Colleges in State and selection dt. 30-06-2008 and 02-07-2008 to the post of Principals in different Post Graduate Colleges also in the State before HC - DB set aside the select list dt. 30-06-2008 and 02-07-2008 as well as the select list dt.15-05-2007 in respect of Principals to PG and degree colleges - Hence, instant appeals - Whether petitioners were estopped from filing writ petition before HC.

Held, withdrawal of earlier writ petition without any reservation did not cause any prejudice in as much as at the time when the earlier writ petition was filed, select list was not announced and therefore when at the stage prior to the interview, those writ petitions were filed there was nothing wrong in the writ petitioners moving HC subsequent to the declaration of the results by raising a challenge to the select list. SC do not find anything wrong with such a conclusion arrived by DB. That apart, in instant case there were as many as 17 writ petitions pending before HC therefore, withdrawal of earlier two petitions by petitioner could not have in any manner prevented HC from examining correctness of challenge made in other writ petitions. Therefore do not find any merit on the ground of principle of estoppel. Appeals dismissed.

(B) Service - Education - Uttar Pradesh Higher Education Services Commission Act, 1980, ss. 31, 4(2)(a)(g), 4(2)(e) - Uttar Pradesh State Universities Act, 1973, s. 50 - Uttar Pradesh Higher Education Services Commission (Procedure and Conduct of Business) Regulations, 1983 - Uttar Pradesh Higher Education Services Commission (Procedure for Selection of Teachers) Regulations, 1983, reg. 6(1) - Selection - Interference - Justifiability - Whether HC was justified to apply de facto doctrine on finding that members of Commission/respondents 4 to 9 in writ petition ceased to hold office it ought not to have interfered with the selection.

Held, DB was able to note serious allegations as against respondents 4 to 9 who were inducted as members of Commission and whose qualification to be appointed as members was seriously put to challenge. Further, in the course of making selection it was pointed out that Commission did not adopt a definite course in making selection instead it varied its norms on different dates, certainly conclusion of DB in having frowned upon such approach made by Commission cannot be found fault with. DB was well justified in holding that changing of norms while applying regn. 6(1) of the 1983 Regulations for initial screening thrown considerable doubt about genuineness in selection process adopted by Commission. Therefore, in the said circumstance conclusion of DB that when greater faith and trust was invested with Commission and when Commission breached its own criteria and thereby acted contrary to the standards laid by it which resulted in an arbitrary selection made by it has to be held as well justified. SC found full justification in said approach made by DB in impugned judgment. Appeals dismissed.

(C) Service - Education - Uttar Pradesh Higher Education Services Commission Act, 1980, ss. 31, 4(2)(a)(g), 4(2)(e) - Uttar Pradesh State Universities Act, 1973, s. 50 - Uttar Pradesh Higher Education Services Commission (Procedure and Conduct of Business) Regulations, 1983 - Uttar Pradesh Higher Education Services Commission (Procedure for Selection of Teachers) Regulations, 1983, reg. 6(1) - Selection by Commission - Sustainability - Whether selection made by Commission is in accordance with regn. 6(2) of the 1983 Regulations, that there were guidelines both for initial screening as well as for evaluating the merits at the time of interview.

Held, since guidelines provided for sufficient criteria to assess the merit of a candidate selection made based on such guidelines cannot be interfered with. DB having noted the above ruling of SC held that what is prescribed in the guideline dt. 06-10-1983, would at best only enable Interviewing Committee to grade candidates based on their performance in the interview and that it did not provide sufficient guidelines as was required to be provided u/regn. 6(2) of the 1983 Regulations. As far as the guideline dt. 06-10-1983 was concerned, it merely showed as to what will be the effect of percentage of marks that were to be allotted to each individual candidate and there were no such specific factors determined by way of guideline as was required to be done by Commission u/regn. 6(2) of the 1983 Regulations. SC found full justification in said approach made by DB in impugned judgment. Appeals dismissed.

(D) Service - Education - Uttar Pradesh State Universities Act, 1973, s. 50 - Uttar Pradesh Higher Education Services Commission (Procedure and Conduct of Business) Regulations, 1983 - Uttar Pradesh Higher Education Services Commission (Procedure for Selection of Teachers) Regulations, 1983, reg. 6(1) - Selection - Minimum qualification - Justifiability - Whether DB committed a serious factual error in respect of certain candidates with reference to whom it gave a finding that they were permitted to participate in the interview even though they did not fulfill minimum qualification and on that ground held that their selection was unjustified.

Held, after finding serious flaw in the matter of selection made by Commission, where DB noted serious allegations as against those who were inducted as members of Commission who ceased to hold office by the time writ petitions came to be heard. DB was obliged to apply de facto doctrine and consider whole selection with little more care and caution, in which process, DB was able to find out that regn. 6 of the 1983 Regulations was seriously breached. Further, there were serious flaws in preliminary screening as well as in permitting ineligible candidates to participate apart from lack of necessary guidelines as prescribed u/regn. 6(2) of the 1983 Regulations for holding the interview in order to assess merits of candidates. DB took note of certain instances where it pointed out how the various factors noted by it did show serious lacuna in overall selection made by Commission. Therefore, individual cases cannot be isolated to state that setting aside of selection should be restricted to only those individual cases and entirety of selection cannot be affected. SC found full justification in said approach made by DB in impugned judgment. Appeals dismissed.

(E) Service - Education - Uttar Pradesh Higher Education Services Commission Act, 1980, ss. 31, 4(2)(a)(g), 4(2)(e) - Uttar Pradesh State Universities Act, 1973, s. 50 - Uttar Pradesh Higher Education Services Commission (Procedure and Conduct of Business) Regulations, 1983 - Uttar Pradesh Higher Education Services Commission (Procedure for Selection of Teachers) Regulations, 1983, reg. 6(1) - Vacancy - Reservation - Applicability - Whether against the post of Principal which is single post in an institution, horizontal reservation for physically handicapped and dependent of freedom fighter is applicable and selection made of reserved categories candidates is valid who otherwise could not have been included in select list.

Held, it is not that single instance which weighed with DB to interfere with selection. DB after finding serious flaw in the whole of selection process found that there was serious breach of regulations governing selection process and consequently set aside whole selection. DB also found that in spite of clear dictum of SC in State Of U.P. & Ors. v. Bharat Singh & Ors 2011 Indlaw SC 150, Commission applied the rule of reservation and quoted those instances while setting aside selection. Therefore, non-joining or resignation of some of the candidates or likelihood of retirement of one other candidate will be of no consequence when whole process of selection was affected by serious illegalities. SC found full justification in said approach made by DB in impugned judgment. Appeals dismissed.
