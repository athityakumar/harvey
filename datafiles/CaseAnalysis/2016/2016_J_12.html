Jagdish Narain Shukla v State of U.P. and others

Supreme Court of India

26 September 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Khanwilkar, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 671; (2016) 10 SCC 433; AIR 2016 SC 4871; JT 2016 (9) SC 362; [2016] 5 S.C.R. 360; 2016 (4) UPLBEC 2887

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Constitution

Keywords: Foreign Exchange Management Act, 1999, Prevention of Money Laundering Act, 2002, Lokayukta, Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Constitution of India, 1950, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, Uttar Pradesh Lokayukta and Upa-Lokayuktas Act, 1975

Summary: Constitution - Administrative - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 136, 226 - Uttar Pradesh Lokayukta and Upa-Lokayuktas Act, 1975, s. 12(3) - Lokayukta recommendation report - Implementation - Applicability - Appellant filed PIL before HC seeking opposite parties for implementation of recommendation/report of Lokayukta dt. 22-2-2012 - DB dismissed PIL holding that opinion of Lokayukta in the report cannot be construed to be final or conclusive as it was a fact finding enquiry and a detailed enquiry is yet to be made after affording opportunity of hearing to the person against whom complaint is made - DB further observed that HC ought not to entertain petition for implementation of recommendations/orders of Lokayukta as there is sufficient provision under the 1975 Act to get the same implemented - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, relief in the writ petition was limited to directing Competent Authority to act upon the recommendations made by Lokayukta. That relief has worked out in view of direction issued by Competent Authority to investigate/enquire into the factual matrix noticed in Lokayukta's report. Further, law enforcement agencies have moved into action and have collected information and material including with reference to representations and affidavits received in the course of said investigation/enquiry. Therefore, SC accede to the request of law enforcement agencies to give them some more time to complete the investigation/enquiries in relation to the acts of commission and omission of respondent nos. 5 and 6 or any other persons privy thereto. Considering the fact that law enforcement agencies are on their job for quite some time, SC express a sanguine hope that they would complete the investigation/enquiry at the earliest and not later than 6 months and take the same to its logical end in accordance with law. SC is not expressing any opinion on the merits of matters under investigation/enquiry or the defence that may be available to respondent nos. 5 and 6 in any proceedings to be instituted against them in relation to the said matters. Appeal disposed of.
