Mukund Dewangan v Oriental Insurance Company Limited and etc.

Supreme Court of India

11 February 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Arun Mishra, Kurian Joseph

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 153; (2016) 4 SCC 298; 2016 ACJ 1008; JT 2016 (2) SC 174; 2016(2) SCALE 287; [2016] 3 S.C.R. 1075; 2016 (2) WLN(SC) 198

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance

Keywords: Registration, Carrier, Insurer, Licensing Authority, Licensing, Central Motor Vehicles Rules, 1989, Valid Licence, Goods Carriage, Licensed Driver, Light Motor Vehicle, Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, Administrative

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Practice & Procedure - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, ss. 10(2), 10(2)(d), 2(21), 2(41), 3 - Driver holding license of light motor vehicle to drive transport vehicle - Competency - Determination - Matter was placed before SC for determination of question that whether for drivers having licence to drive light motor vehicles there is necessity of obtaining endorsement to drive transport vehicle when transport vehicle is of class of light motor vehicle.

Held, as per s. 10(2) of Act, classes of vehicles have been separately provided. Further, transport vehicles were included under Act vide amendment in 1994 under category of 'light motor vehicle', 'heavy motor vehicle' etc. as per gross vehicle weight or unladen weight, as case may be, is apparent from bare reading of classification given in s. 10(2) of Act read with definition of light motor vehicle as defined in s. 2(21) of Act. Further, vide amendment no change had been brought about with respect to transport vehicles of class of light motor vehicle as defined in s. 2(21) of Act. However, SC held that there is difference between opinion and different views are taken by SC in various case with respect to competency of driver holding license of light motor vehicle to drive transport vehicle. Hence, there appears conflict in decisions of SC with respect to pre-amended position and also after amendment has been effected. Questions framed that are required to be referred to larger Bench. SC held that matter be place before Chief Justice of India to constitute larger Bench in order to resolve conflict in views expressed by different Benches of SC. Matter referred to larger Bench. Order accordingly.

Ratio - In case of conflict between decisions by difference benches, matter has to be placed before larger bench to lay down binding precedent on subject matter.
