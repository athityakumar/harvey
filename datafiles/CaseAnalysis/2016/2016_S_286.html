L. Narayana Swamy v State of Karnataka and others

Supreme Court of India

06 September 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, N.V. Ramana

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 638; (2016) 9 SCC 598; AIR 2016 SC 4125; 2017 CRLJ 1084; 2016 (4) JCC 2465; JT 2016 (9) SC 100; 2016 (6) KarLJ 177; 2016 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 3617; 2016 (2) OLR 869; 2016 (4) RCR(Criminal) 315; 2017 (1) RLW 163; 2016(8) SCALE 560; [2016] 5 S.C.R. 683

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Jurisdiction, Opportunity, Corrupt, Special Judge, Abuse, Cognizance, Police Report, Further Investigation, Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947, Misconduct, Registered, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Constitution of India, 1950, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, Complainant

Summary: (A) Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 156(3), 173(2), 190, 202, 397, 401, 482 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120B, 34, 420, 427, 447, 467, 468, 471, 506 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947, ss. 13(1)(d), 13(2), 19, 19(1) - Misconduct in investigation - Complaint filed - Investigation performed - Accused no.6 filed a suit for specific performance based on said agreement to sell in which ex-parte decree came to be passed - The fact that out of 27.25 acres of land, in respect of which accused no.6 obtained sale deed 10 acres of land had been acquired by Govt. - Accused no.6 applied for conversion of use of land and accused no.1/ Revenue Inspector, had conducted spot inspection, accused no.3/Tehsildar had recommended case for conversion on the same day and accused no.5/Assistant Commissioner, had given an endorsement to accused no. 6 on the very next day to the effect that property in question is not the subject matter of acquisition - Respondent no.2/complainant filed a complaint against accused nos. 3 and 5 seeking to secure presence of appellants/accused persons and complaint be referred to Lokayukta Police for investigation u/s. 156(3) CrPC - Trial Court passed an order on said complaint thereby referring the same to Lokayukta Police for further investigation u/s. 156(3) CrPC - On the basis of which a case has been registered against appellants/accused nos. 3 and 5 for the offences punishable u/s.13(1)(d) r/w s. 13(2) of the Act and u/ss. 120(b), 427, 447 and 506 r/w s. 34 IPC - Aggreived, appellants filed writ petitions before HC which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeals - Whether an order directing further investigation u/s. 156(3) CrPC can be passed in relation to public servant in the absence of valid sanction and contrary to the judgments of SC in Anil Kumar & Ors. v. M.K. Aiyappa & Anr., 2013 Indlaw SC 648 and Manharibhai Muljibhai Kakadia and Anr. v. Shaileshbhai Mohanbhai Patel and Ors., 2012 Indlaw SC 333.

Held, when a complaint is received, Court records preliminary evidence of complainant on the basis of which it satisfies itself as to whether sufficient evidence is placed on record which may prima facie constitute such offence. Likewise, Police report is filed u/s. 173(2) CrPC on completion of investigation and on perusal thereof, Magistrate satisfies himself about the facts which constitute such offence. Similar is the position in the third contingency. On this basis, HC has opined that since prior sanction is required only at the time of taking cognizance which stage comes much after the investigation is ordered u/s. 156(3) CrPC at the stage of giving direction to investigate into the complaint, such a sanction is not required. HC has taken the view, in the impugned judgment, that bar is from taking cognizance which would not apply at the stage of investigation by investigating officer. It is observed that sanction is required only after investigation and that too when, after investigation, it is found that there is substantial truth in the investigation report as to what amounts to cognizance of offence an order directing further investigation u/s. 156(3) CrPC cannot be passed in the absence of valid sanction. Appeals dismissed.

(B) Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 156(3), 173(2), 190, 202, 397, 401, 482 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120B, 34, 420, 427, 447, 467, 468, 471, 506 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947, ss. 13(1)(d), 13(2), 19, 19(1) - Different post - Cognizance - Transferred - Whether a public servant who is not on the same post and is transferred (whether by way of promotion or otherwise to another post) loses the protection u/s. 19(1) of the Act, though he continues to be a public servant, albeit on a different post.

Held, in case of appellants there was no question of appellants' getting any protection by a sanction. HC was absolutely right in relying on decision in Parkash Singh Badal And Anr v. State Of Punjab And Ors, 2006 Indlaw SC 941 to hold that appellants in both the appeals had abused entirely different office or offices than the one which they were holding on the date on which cognizance was taken and, therefore, there was no necessity of sanction u/s. 19 of the Act. Where the public servant had abused the office which he held in the check period but had ceased to hold "that office" or was holding a different office, then a sanction would not be necessary. Where the alleged misconduct is in some different capacity than the one which is held at the time of taking cognizance, there will be no necessity to take the sanction. It would be a matter of evidence on the basis of which culpability of the appellants shall be judged. Thus, judgment of HC though on the issue of obtaining the sanction at the time of taking cognizance may not be correct and is rightly decided. Effect thereof would be to hold that sanction was not needed as appellants at the time of taking cognizance were not holding the post which is alleged to have been misused. Appeals dismissed.
