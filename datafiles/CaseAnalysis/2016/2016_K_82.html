Hiral P. Harsora and others v Kusum Narottamdas Harsora and others

Supreme Court of India

06 October 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

R.F. Nariman, Kurian Joseph

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 834; (2016) 10 SCC 165; 2016 (10) ADJ 293; AIR 2016 SC 4774; 2017 (2) ALD(SC) 95; 2016 ALL MR (Cri) 4930; 2016 (119) ALR 462; 2016 (6) AWC 5830; 2016 (6) Bom.C.R. 505; 2016 (4) CLT(SC) 86; 2017 CRLJ 509; 2016 (233) DLT 154; 2016 (4) JCC 2659; JT 2016 (9) SC 629; 2016 (4) KLT 268; 2017 (2) MahLJ 147; 2017 (1) MLJ(Crl) 348; 2017 (2) MPLJ 20; 2016 (2) OLR 995; 2016 (4) RCR(Civil) 750; 2016 (4) RCR(Criminal) 433; 2017 (1) RLW 689; 2016(9) SCALE 776; [2016] 9 S.C.R. 515; 2016 (4) WLN(SC) 19

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Women & Children

Keywords: Jurisdiction, Central Excise Act, 1944, Delhi Rent Control Act, 1958, Equal Protection, Hindu Succession Act, 1956, Delhi Special Police Establishment Act, 1946, Severability, Protection of Women from Domestic Violence Act, 2005, Calcutta Municipal Act, 1951, Andhra Pradesh Buildings (Lease, Rent and Eviction) Control Act, 1960, Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, Constitution of India, 1950, Income-tax Act, 1961, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, Sexual Harassment of Women at Workplace (Prevention, Prohibition and Redressal) Act, 2013, Protection From Domestic Violence Bill, 2002

Summary: (A) Women & Children - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 19(6) - Protection of Women from Domestic Violence Act, 2005, ss. 2(a), 2(f), 2(q) - Complaint filed - Adult male persons - Discharged - Respondents (mother and daughter) filed complaint under the Act against their brother/son, his wife and two sisters/daughters alleging various acts of violence against them - Meanwhile, an application was filed before Trial Court for discharge of respondent nos. 2 to 4 stating that as complaint was made u/s. 2(a) r/w s. 2(q) of the Act and respondents not being adult male persons were required to be discharged - Trial Court passed an order in which such discharge was refused - Aggrieved respondents 2 to 4 filed writ petition before HC wherein HC discharged respondents 2 to 4 from complaint - Aggrieved, respondents filed writ petition before DB challenging Constitutional validity of s. 2(q) of the Act which was allowed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether the object sought to be achieved by the 2005 Act was valid.

Held, in the instant case, there was no necessity to rebut the presumption, since appellant was aware that respondent was a married person even before the commencement of their relationship, hence the status of appellant was that of a concubine or a mistress, who cannot enter into relationship in the nature of a marriage. However, SC was afraid that the Act does not take care of such relationships which might perhaps call for an amendment of definition of s. 2(f) of the Act which was restrictive and exhaustive. The microscopic difference between male and female, adult and non-adult, regard being had to the object sought to be achieved by the Act was neither real or substantial nor does it have any rational relation to the object of legislation. Further, as per the principle settled in Dr. Subramanian Swamy; (2) Centre for Public Interest Litigation v. (1) Director, Central Bureau of Investigation and another; (2) Union of India, 2014 Indlaw SC 332, judgment, the words "adult male person" were contrary to the object of affording protection to women who have suffered from domestic violence "of any kind". Therefore SC strike down the words "adult male" before the word "person" in s. 2(q) of the Act as those words discriminate between persons similarly situate and far from being in tune with were contrary to the object sought to be achieved by the Act. Appeal disposed of.

(B) Women & Children - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 19(6) - Protection of Women from Domestic Violence Act, 2005, ss. 2(a), 2(f), 2(q) - Adult male - Struck down - Effect of - Whether the rest of the Act could be implemented without the two words i.e. words "adult male" before the word "person".

Held, having struck down the expression "adult male" in s. 2(q) of the Act, rest of the Act was left intact and could be enforced to achieve the object of legislation without the offending words. U/s. 2(q) of the Act, while defining 'respondent', a proviso was provided only to carve out an exception to a situation of "respondent" not being an adult male. Once SC strike down 'adult male', the proviso has no independent existence having been rendered otiose. Therefore, SC declare that the words "adult male" in s. 2(q) of the Act would stand deleted since those words do not square with art. 14 of Constitution. Further, the proviso to s. 2(q) of the Act being rendered otiose also stands deleted. Therefore, SC record the statement of counsel and it becomes clear that nothing survives in aforesaid complaints of October, 2010. Impugned judgment of HC was set aside. Appeal disposed of.
