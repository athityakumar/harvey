State of Haryana v Eros City Developers Private Limited and others

Supreme Court of India

19 January 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Prafulla C. Pant, Ranjan Gogoi

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 36; AIR 2016 SC 451; 2016 (3) ALD(SC) 1; 2016 (115) ALR 437; 2016 (2) AWC 1605; 2016 (1) CLT(SC) 36; JT 2016 (1) SC 310; 2016 (1) MLJ 795; 2016 (1) RCR(Civil) 904; 2016 (131) RD 559; 2016 (3) RLW 1806; 2016(1) SCALE 356; [2016] 1 S.C.R. 242

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Punjab Scheduled Roads and Controlled Areas Restriction of Unregulated Development Rules, 1965, Land Acquisition Act, 1894

Summary: Headnote:

Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4, 5A, 6 - Promissory estoppel - Legitimate expectation - Instant appeals against judgment and order of HC whereby notifications issued u/ss.4 and 6 of the Act by State regarding acquisition of land were quashed - Further, HC also quashed Award passed by respondent no. 4 in respect of land owned by respondent no.1, which was acquired through said notifications - Whether impugned judgment and order of HC could be upheld.

Held, HC erred in quashing the acquisition of land by applying doctrine of promissory estoppel and legitimate expectation. The purpose, i.e., expansion and systematic development of Surajkund Tourist Complex is a public purpose which included development of parking area adjacent to Surajkund Tourist Complex near annual Surajkund Fair. Further, HC is incorrect in holding that State has not acted bonafide, after 1992 acquisition proceedings were dropped as earlier proceedings were dropped in the light of orders passed in M.C.Mehta's case, 1996 Indlaw SC 1441, in 1996, restraining construction in the area, and after modification in the said order in the year 1998, the State took fresh decision to acquire the land for public purpose and there is no illegality in the same. Hence, both appeals are allowed and impugned judgment of HC is set aside. Appeals allowed.

Ratio - As per decisions of SC, protection of legitimate expectation does not require the fulfillment of the expectation where an overriding public interest requires otherwise i.e., personal benefit has to give way to public interest and the doctrine of legitimate expectation could not be invoked which would block public interest for private benefit.
