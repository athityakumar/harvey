Babita Lila and another v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

31 August 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Amitava Roy, Pinaki Chandra Ghose

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 633; (2016) 9 SCC 647; AIR 2016 SC 4061; [2016] 387 ITR 305; JT 2016 (8) SC 494; 2016 (3) KLT 1006; 2017 (1) MLJ(Crl) 456; 2016 (2) OLR 968; 2016 (4) RCR(Criminal) 246; 2016(8) SCALE 453; [2016] 7 S.C.R. 604; [2016] 243 TAXMAN 258

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: False Evidence, Jurisdiction, Locker, Territorial Jurisdiction, Forum, Judicial Proceeding, Subordinate, CONSTITUTION, Search, Central Boards of Revenue Act, 1963, False Statement, Challenge, Rank, Cognizance, Proviso, Oath, Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, Dealing, Judicial Proceedings, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898, Punjab Courts Act, 1918, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Constitution of India, 1950, Income-tax Act, 1961, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, Inquiry

Summary: (A) Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 177, 178, 178(b), 178(d), 179, 190, 194(4), 195, 195(1)(b), 195(1)(c), 195(3), 195(3)(b), 195(4),195(g), 476(A), 476B, 482 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 109, 120B, 147, 191, 193, 196, 200, 34, 406, 420, 471, 498A - Income-tax Act, 1961, ss. 116, 118, 120, 124, 131, 132, 136, 142(2A), 246, 246(1), 246A, 277, 39 - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 37 - Complaint filed - Territorial jurisdiction - Legality - Appellants have residences at two different places and filed their returns from first place - On 28-10-2010, search operations u/s. 132 of the 1961 Act were conducted simultaneously at both the places - Authorities after investigation revealed that appellants did hold a locker in bank which was operated by appellant no. 1 - Dy. Director filed Complaint before Trial Court stating that by making such false statement in course of search operations appellants had committed offence u/ss. 109, 191, 193, 196, 200, 420, 120B, 34 IPC - Trial Court allowed petition and issued process against appellants - Aggrieved, appellants filed writ petition before HC seeking annulment of impugned order on ground that search operations were undertaken by I.T.Os. of different places, complaint could not have been lodged by Dy. Director who was not appellate authority u/s. 195(4) CrPC and that no part of alleged offence was committed within the territorial limits of Trial Court - HC declined to interfere on either of those contentions - Hence, instant appeal - Whether the proceeding before ITO u/s. 37 of the 1922 Act could be construed to be a proceeding in any Court within the meaning of s. 195(1)(b) CrPC.

Held, it is impermissible to acknowledge Dy. Director to be the forum to which an appeal would ordinarily lie from an order/decision of an assessing officer/I.T.O. The complaint filed by Dpty. Director on an overall analysis of the facts of case and law involved has to be held as incompetent. Generally, every offence ought to be inquired into and tried by a Court within whose local jurisdiction it had been committed as is mandated by s. 177 CrPC. S. 178 CrPC however marks a departure contingent on the eventualities as listed in cls. (a),(b), (c) and (d) of s. 178 CrPC to identify the Court that would have jurisdiction to try the offences as contemplated. Though the concept of "cause of action" identifiable with a civil action is not routinely relevant for the determination of territoriality of criminal Courts as had been ruled by SC in Dashrath Rupsingh Rathod v. State of Maharashtra and Another, 2014 Indlaw SC 494, their Lordships however were cognizant of the word "ordinarily" used in s. 177 CrPC to acknowledge the exceptions contained in s. 178 CrPC. S. 179 CrPC also did not elude notice. Further, a single and combine search operation had been undertaken simultaneously at both places for the same purpose alleged offence can be tried by Courts otherwise competent at both the places. To confine the jurisdiction within the territorial limits to the Court at one place would amount to impermissible and illogical truncation of the ambit of ss. 178 and 179 CrPC. The objection with regard to the competence of Trial Court was rejected. The inevitable consequence of the determination in its entirety is that complaint is unsustainable in law having been filed by an authority, incompetent in terms of s. 195 CrPC. Impugned proceeding and the order assailed were set-aside. Respondent is left at liberty to take appropriate steps in the matter as available in law. Appeal allowed.
