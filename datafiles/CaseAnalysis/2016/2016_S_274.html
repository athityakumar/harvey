State of Himachal Pradesh v Sanjay Kumar @ Sunny

Supreme Court of India

15 December 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, A.M. Sapre

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 895; (2017) 2 SCC 51; AIR 2017 SC 835; 2017 CRLJ 1443; JT 2016 (12) SC 66; 2017 (1) RCR(Criminal) 261; 2016(12) SCALE 831; [2016] 9 S.C.R. 237

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Accomplice, Rape, FIR, Medicine, Reasonable Doubt, Age, Version, Corroboration, Blood, Girl, Intercourse, Fear, Deposition, Birth, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 376, 376(2)(f), 506 - Rape - Acquittal - Challenged - Respondent/accused was charged for commission of offence of rape of victim 'X' - Trial Court convicted and sentenced accused u/ss. 376(2)(f) and 506 of IPC - HC in appeal set aside conviction order passed by Trial Court for want of failure to prove case beyond reasonable doubt by prosecution, thereby acquitted respondent - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, it is well settled that the testimony of a victim in cases of sexual offences is vital and unless there are compelling reasons which necessitate looking for corroboration of a statement, the courts should find no difficulty to act on the testimony of the victim of a sexual assault alone to convict the accused. No doubt, her testimony has to inspire confidence. Seeking corroboration to a statement before relying upon the same as a rule, in such cases, would literally amount to adding insult to injury. The deposition of the prosecutrix has, thus, to be taken as a whole. Needless to reiterate that the victim of rape is not an accomplice and her evidence can be acted upon without corroboration. She stands at a higher pedestal than an injured witness does. If the court finds it difficult to accept her version, it may seek corroboration from some evidence which lends assurance to her version. Thus, whenever such charge of rape is made, where the victim is a child, it has to be treated as a gospel truth and the accused person has to be convicted. In instant case, evidence on record contains positive proof, credible sequence of events and factual truth linking the respondent with rape of the prosecutrix and had criminally intimidated her. Hence, judgment of HC is set aside and respondent is convicted u/s. 376(2)(f) of IPC and sentence to suffer RI for 12 years with fine of Rs.50,000 i/d to undergo further sentence of one year. Further convicted u/s. 506 of IPC and sentenced to suffer RI for 2 years. Appeal allowed.
