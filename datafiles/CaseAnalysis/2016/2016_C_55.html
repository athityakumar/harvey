Chief Executive Officer, Krishna District Co-Operative Central Bank Limited and another v K. Hanumantha Rao and another

Supreme Court of India

09 December 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, A.M. Sapre

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 881; (2017) 2 SCC 528; 2017 (152) FLR 131; 2017 (152) FLR 757; JT 2016 (11) SC 548; 2016(12) SCALE 717

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Punishment, Jurisdiction, Appellate Authority, Review, Judicial Review, Appeal, Dismissal, Grounds, Charges, Interference, Employee

Summary: Service - Charges of misconduct - Dismissal - Challenged - Departmental inquiry was conducted against respondent no.1/employee of appellant/bank for certain charges of misconduct - Charges were proved and disciplinary authority inflicted the punishment of dismissal from service on respondent no.1 - Respondent no. 1 filed writ petition before HC challenging impugned order - Single Judge held that there were no grounds to interfere with the punishment imposed by disciplinary authority - On appeal, DB by impugned judgment has altered the said penalty of dismissal to that of stoppage of two increments for a period of 3 years - Hence, instant appeal - Whether it was permissible for DB to do so in the facts of instant case.

Held, HC failed to appreciate that the duties of Supervisor were not identical and similar to that of top management of Bank. No such duty by top management of Bank was spelled out to show that it was similar to the duty of respondent no. 1. It was only in exceptional circumstances, where it was found that the punishment/penalty awarded by disciplinary authority/employer was wholly disproportionate, that too to an extent that it shakes the conscience of Court that Court steps in and interferes. No such finding was arrived at by HC to the effect that the punishment awarded to respondent no. 1 was shockingly disproportionate. SC do not find it to be so having regard to the fact that respondent no.1 did not perform his duties with due diligence and his negligence in performing the duties as a Supervisor has led to serious frauds in number of accounts by the subordinate staff. It was for disciplinary authority to consider as to whether respondent no. 1 was fit to continue in the post of Supervisor. Therefore, punishment imposed was not shockingly disproportionate, no question of remitting the case to disciplinary authority arises. Impugned judgment of DB was unsustainable and the same was set aside. Appeal allowed.
