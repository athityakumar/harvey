Bangalore Development Authority v N. Jayamma

Supreme Court of India

10 March 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, R.K. Agrawal

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 219; AIR 2016 SC 1294; 2016 (115) ALR 902; 2016 (2) AWC 2051; 2016 (1) CLT(SC) 199; JT 2016 (3) SC 27; 2016 (5) KarLJ 1; 2016 (3) MLJ 31; 2016 (2) RCR(Civil) 402; 2016(3) SCALE 233; [2016] 4 S.C.R. 583

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Adverse Possession, Limitation Act, 1963, Limitation, Bangalore Development Authority Act, 1976, Constitution of India, 1950, Land Acquisition Act, 1894, Right to Fair Compensation and Transparency in Land Acquisition, Rehabilitation and Resettlement Act, 2013

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4, 6 - Alleged adverse possession - Title to property - Determination - Respondent stated that she had purchased suit property in question on 22-6-1994 from its previous owner and had constructed building thereupon - Further, part of suit property was acquired by State Govt. for appellant authority - Appellant authority issued notification u/s. 4 of Act was issued on 15-12-1984 followed by declaration u/s. 6 of Act on 29-10-1986 - Further, possession thereof was handed over to appellant authority on 30-8-1988 vide Mahazar - However, actual possession of suit property remained with original owner who then sold it to respondent in year 1994 - Hence, respondent filed suit on ground that she was in possession of said property for more than 12 years even after acquisition thereof by State Govt. and, she had perfected her title by adverse possession - Hence, relief claimed in suit was for declaration that respondent had become owner thereof - Trial Court passed order in favor of respondent and it was declared that she is owner in possession of suit property having perfected her title by way of adverse possession - Further, decree of permanent injunction was passed restraining appellant - Further, HC vide impugned order affirmed order passed by Trial court - Whether impugned order passed by lower Courts in favor of respondent is justified and in accordance with law.

Held, Venkatesh and others; (2) T. R. Ramadas others v Commissioner, Bangalore Development Authority, 2015 Indlaw SC 839, followed, where it was held that where fact that plaintiffs had not possibly establish their adverse possession over suit property, suit should be dismissed for unauthorised occupant had no right to claim relief. SC held that findings of court below that only paper possession was taken and actual possession was not taken also becomes meaningless as manner of taking possession could not be proved by respondent. Further, respondent in plaint had herself admitted that officials of appellant authority had come to suit property on 24-4-2001 and demolished existing structure. Hence, act of appellant authority demonstrate that there was no unhindered, peaceful and continuous possession of suit land. Impugned order passed by lower courts in favor of respondent is quashed and is set aside. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Party claiming adverse possession must prove that his possession is, peaceful, open and continuous and possession must be adequate in continuity, in publicity and in extent to show that their possession is adverse to true owner.
