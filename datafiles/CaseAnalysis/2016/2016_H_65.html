Commissioner of Central Excise, Pune v Hindustan National Glass and Industries Limited

Supreme Court of India

14 January 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, N.V. Ramana

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 43; (2016) 4 SCC 675; AIR 2016 SC 547; 2016 (332) E.L.T. 193; JT 2016 (2) SC 206; 2016(1) SCALE 443; [2016] 1 S.C.R. 250

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Assessable Value, Sale Price

Summary: Excise - Indirect Tax - Central Excise Act, 1944, ss.11A(1), 11AC - Notional interest on advance payments - Depression of assessable value - Adjudicating authority passed order making demand u/s. 11A(1) of the Act being duty payable on additional consideration received by assessee from customers in form of notional interest accrued on advance payments and also imposed penalty u/s. 11AC of the Act and also confirmed other demands - Aggrieved, respondent-assessee preferred appeal before Tribunal and by impugned order, Tribunal held that revenue had not been able to discharge onus by adducing cogent material evidence that the advances obtained from buyer had readily been instrumental in depression of price - Hence, instant appeal by revenue - Whether there was depression of sale price on account of receipt of advance.

Held, there should be connect and link between the two i.e., the money advanced it should be established was a consideration paid which could form the basis for depression of sale price. Evidence and material to establish the said factual matrix has to be uncovered and brought on record to connect and link the sale price paid on paper and the 'other' consideration, not gratis, but by way of interest free advances. Hence, there has to be application of mind by Tribunal regard being had to the amount of money paid by purchasers and the effect of sales made to two companies in percentile terms, whether it had effect of depressing sale price and onus would be on revenue. Hence, liberty is granted to revenue to produce documents to discharge onus and so, matter is remitted to Tribunal for fresh disposal and impugned order of Tribunal is set aside. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Sale price agreed between two competing parties could get depressed, when substantial and huge advances are periodically extended and given with the objective and purpose that the sale price paid or charged would be lowered, to set off the consideration paid by grant by advances.
