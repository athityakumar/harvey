State of Rajasthan v Jag Raj Singh @ Hansa

Supreme Court of India

29 June 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhushan, A.M. Sapre

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 436; 2016 CRLJ 3336; 2016 (3) JCC (Narcotics) 153; JT 2016 (6) SC 202; 2016 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 2526; 2016 (4) MLJ(Crl) 214; 2016 (3) RCR(Criminal) 539; 2016(6) SCALE 32; [2016] 4 S.C.R. 451; 2016 (3) WLN(SC) 106

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Gazetted Officer, Vehicle, Reason To Believe, Drug, Motor Vehicles Act, 1939, Opportunity, Transport, Belief, CONSTITUTION, Search, Believe, Secret, Special Judge, Chain, Opium, Escape, Proviso, Narcotic Drugs And Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985, Station House Officer, Forfeiture, Search Warrant, Forthwith, Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, Rigorous Imprisonment, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, Seizure

Summary: Criminal - Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substance Act, 1985, ss. 15, 42(1), 42(2), 43, 50, 8 - Possession of Contraband Drugs - Acquittal Challenged - Contraband drugs were recovered from possession of respondents/accused on basis of information from informant - Trial Court convicted accused u/s. 8/15 of the Act and sentenced to suffer RI for 12 years with fine of Rs. 1,20,000 each and in default of payment of fine, further RI of one year - HC in appeal acquitted accused from charges for want of failure to comply provisions and set aside conviction order of Trial Court - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, the object of the Act, is to make stringent provisions for control and regulation of operations relating to those drugs and substances. At the same time, to avoid harm to the innocent persons and to avoid abuse of the provisions by the officers, certain safeguards are provided which in the context have to be observed strictly. S. 42(1) of the Act, indicates that any authorised officer can carry out search between sun rise and sun set without warrant or authorisation. The scheme indicates that in event the search has to be made between sun set and sun rise, the warrant would be necessary unless officer has reasons to believe that a search warrant or authorisation cannot be obtained without affording the opportunity for escape of offender which grounds of his belief has to be recorded. In instant case, there is no case that any ground for belief as contemplated by proviso to sub-section (1) of s. 42 or Sub-section (2) of s. 42 was ever recorded by Station House Officer who proceeded to carry on search. Further, if search is conducted in violation of s. 50 it may not vitiate the trial but that would render the recovery of illicit articles suspect and vitiates the conviction and sentence of the accused. Non-compliance of requirement of Sections 42 and 50 is impermissible, however, delayed compliance with satisfactory explanation will be acceptable compliance of s.42 of the Act. The HC has rightly held that non compliance of s. 42(1) and s. 42(2) of the Act were proved on the record. Hence, impugned order does not suffer from infirmity to warrant interference. Appeal dismissed.
