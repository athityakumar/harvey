Raj Kumar v Director of Education and others

Supreme Court of India

13 April 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, Amitava Roy

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 275; (2016) 6 SCC 541; AIR 2016 SC 1855; 2016 (4) AWC 4168; 2016 (229) DLT 66; 2016 (149) FLR 941; JT 2016 (4) SC 58; 2016 (2) LLJ 257; 2016(4) SCALE 150; [2016] 1 S.C.R. 697

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Evidence, Wages, Back Wages, Educational Institutions, SERVICE, Compensation, Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, Retrenchment, Relief, Pay, Industrial Disputes (Central) Rules, 1957, Permanent, Teacher, Employment, Rule, Terms And Conditions, Date, Appeal, EDUCATION, Delhi School Education Act, 1973, Dismissal, Employer, Surplus, Industrial Disputes (Amendment) Act, 1964, Misconduct, U.P. Intermediate Education Act, 1921, Intermediate Education (Amendment) Act, 1958, Writ Petition, Notice, Employee, Constitution of India, 1950, Post, Income-tax Act, 1961

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Delhi School Education Act, 1973 - Industrial Disputes (Central) Rules, 1957, r. 76 - Retrenchment - Notice and prior approval - Respondent-Managing Committee passed resolution to retrench services of two surplus drivers, appellant and another, as respondent-Managing Committee could not purchase new buses due to shortage of funds and the same resulted in surplus drivers on account of non-availability of jobs - Later, notice was sent to appellant stating that appellant would be retrenched from service on expiry of notice period of one month and that he would be paid retrenchment compensation - Appellant approached HC and in pursuance of orders of HC, appellant filed appeal before Tribunal against retrenchment notice dt.7-1-2003 - However, Tribunal dismissed appeal holding that respondent-Managing Committee had right to retrench surplus drivers of school and aggrieved by the same, appellant filed writ petition before HC but the same was dismissed vide impugned judgment and hence, instant appeal - Whether retrenchment of appellant from services could be upheld.

Held, even though notice of retrenchment was served on appellant on 7-1-2003, no evidence was adduced on behalf of respondents to show that notice of retrenchment has been sent to appropriate authority and thus, mandatory conditions of s. 25F of 1947 Act to retrench a workman have not been complied with. Further, respondent-Managing Committee had not obtained prior approval from Govt. as required u/s. 8(2) of 1973 Act for order of termination passed against appellant and thus, order of termination passed against appellant is bad in law. Further, the respondent-School has not produced any evidence on record to show that the retrenchment of the appellant was necessary as he had become 'surplus'. Hence, retrenchment of appellant is bad in law for non-compliance with the mandatory provisions of s. 25F of 1947 Act and also s. 8(2) of 1973 Act and for said reasons, impugned judgment and order of HC is set aside and respondent-Managing Committee is directed to reinstate appellant at his post. Further, termination of appellant was ordered in the year 2003 and was unemployed till date and respondents had not adduced any evidence to show that appellant was gainfully employed during the period. Hence, relief of back wages is awarded to appellant along with all consequential benefits from the date of termination of his services. Deepali Gundu Surwase v. Kranti Junior Adhyapak Mahavidyala (D.Ed.) & Others 2013 INDLAW SC 579, followed. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - As per r.76(a) of the Rules, notice has to be sent to appropriate authorities within three days from the date on which notice is served on the workman.
