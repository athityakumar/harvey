Umesh Goel v Himachal Pradesh Cooperative Group Housing Society Limited

Supreme Court of India

29 June 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla, C. Nagappan

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 443; AIR 2016 SC 3116; 2016(6) ALL MR 918; 2016 (117) ALR 709; 2016 (5) AWC 4479; 2016 (5) Bom.C.R. 112; 2016 (3) CLT(SC) 4; 2017 (1) CLT(SC) 75; 2016 (4) CTC 419; JT 2016 (11) SC 108; 2016 (5) MLJ 587; 2016 (2) OLR 431; 2016 (4) RCR(Civil) 54; 2017 (1) RLW 16; 2016(5) SCALE 844

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Partnership, Jurisdiction, Contract, Limitation Act, 1963, Indian Contract Act, 1872, Arbitration Act, 1940, Arbitration And Conciliation Act, 1996, Indian Partnership Act, 1932, Arbitration, Arbitration Proceedings, Provincial Insolvency Act, 1920, Interest Act, 1978, Insolvency, Presidency-Towns Insolvency Act, 1909, Code of Civil Procedure, 1908

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Contract & Commercial - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, ss. 9, 11(5), 2(1)(e), 34, 35, 36 - Indian Partnership Act, 1932, ss. 69, 69(1), 69(3) - Arbitral Award - Interim Order Challenged - Respondent/society awarded contract of construction to appellant/unregistered firm, however construct work of units could not be completed for failure to obtain sanctioned plan - Arbitrator allowed claim of appellant to extent of Rs. 1,36,24,886 with interest at rate of 12% and further interest from date of award till its payment at rate of 18% - Single Judge confirmed award of counter claim in application by respondent - DB in appeal by respondent, reversed order of Single Judge by virtue of s. 69 of 1932 Act, thereby passed interim order directing respondent to deposit 50% decreetal amount - Hence, instant appeal - Whether s. 69(3) of 1932 Act was attracted to Arbitral Proceedings and ultimate award passed therein by construing the same as falling under the expression 'other proceedings'.

Held, s. 69(1) of 1932 Act, primarily imposes a ban on any person as a partner of a firm from filing any suit to enforce a right arising from a contract or a right conferred under the Partnership Act, in any Court by or on behalf of an unregistered firm or a person suing as a partner of a firm against the said firm or against any person alleged to be or to have been a partner in that firm. U/s. 69(2) of 1932 Act ban in the same form of a suit in the Court will also operate against any third party at the instance of such an unregistered firm. Further, in order to invoke s. 69(3) of 1932 Act, and for the ban to operate either the firm should be an unregistered one or the person who wants to sue should be a partner of an unregistered firm, that its / his endeavour should be to file a suit in a Court, in which event even if it pertains to a claim of set off or in respect of 'other proceedings' connected with any right arising from a contract or conferred by the Partnership Act, which is sought to be enforced through a Court by way of a suit then and then alone the said sub-section can operate to its full extent. S. 3 6of 1996 Act, only creates a statutory fiction which is limited for the purpose of enforcement of the Award. Therefore, it cannot be held that the entire Arbitral proceeding is a Civil Court proceedings for purpose of s. 69(3) of 1932 Act. Arbitral Proceedings will not come under the expression 'other proceedings' of s. 69(3) of 1932 Act, the ban imposed under the said can have no application to Arbitral proceedings as well as the Arbitration Award. Hence, impugned order passed by DB is set aside and the order that of Single Judge is restored. Appeal allowed.
