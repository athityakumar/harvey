Sarla Performance Fibers Limited and Ect. v Commissioner of Central Excise, Surat-II

Supreme Court of India

03 June 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, Shiva Kirti Singh

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 401; AIR 2016 SC 2697; 2016 (4) AWC 4100; 2016 (336) E.L.T. 577; JT 2016 (6) SC 471; 2016 (5) MLJ 824; 2016(5) SCALE 793; [2016] 7 S.C.R. 201

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Show Cause Notice, Central Excise Duty, Excise Duty, Duty Of Excise, Central Excise Act, 1944, Companies Act, 1956, Yarn, Excisable Goods, Customs Tariff Act, 1975, Central Excise Rules, 1944, Finance Act, 2001, Constitution of India, 1950

Summary: Excise - Indirect Tax - Central Excise Act, 1944, ss. 3(1) and 11AC - Demand - Penalty - Appellant, 100% EOU had procured partial oriented yarn (POY) without payment of duty for the manufacture of various types of yarn - A show cause notice was issued by the Commissioner requiring the appellant to explain why central excise duty of Rs.32,92,854/-should not be recovered on the texturised yarn allegedly removed by the appellants without payment of duty - After the show cause notice was issued, the appellants made payment aggregating to Rs.14,89,349.00 as against the duty payable u/s. 3(1) of the Act after taking into account the cum-duty benefit and Rs.11,19,775.00 payable in the event the benefit of Notification No. 2/05 was allowed - Commissioner by his order confiscate the seized nylon covered yarn, demanded Rs.55,202.96 as differential duty on the confiscated goods which were released provisionally before the adjudication, confirmed the central excise duty amounting to Rs.32,92,854/- and imposed a penalty of Rs.33,48,060/- on the appellants - On appeal, Tribunal held that the goods cleared by the 100% EOU and sold in India whether with or without permission of the Development Commissioner, the assessment shall be made u/s. 3(1) of the Act and exemption under Notification No. 125/84 shall not be applicable - HC upheld the order of the Tribunal - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, following SC judgment Commissioner of Central Excise, Visakhapatnam-Ii v. M/S NCC Blue Water Products Ltd. 2010 Indlaw SC 786 in which SC held that expression 'allowed to be sold in India' used in proviso to s. 3(1) of the Act would be applicable only to sales made in DTA of the production by 100% EOUs, which are allowed to be sold into India as per the provisions of the Exim Policy. Judgment and order passed by the tribunal and that of the adjudicating authority are set aside. The appellant shall be liable to pay the excise duty as per s. 3(1) of the Act. The competent authority is directed to compute the duty accordingly and proceed thereafter as per law. Appeals allowed.
