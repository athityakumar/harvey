Electrotherm (India) Limited v Patel Vipulkumar Ramjibhai and others

Supreme Court of India

02 August 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Uday Umesh Lalit, T.S. Thakur, R. Banumathi

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 574; (2016) 9 SCC 300; AIR 2016 SC 3563; 2016 (3) RCR(Civil) 1002; 2016(7) SCALE 536

Case Digest

Subject: Environment, Wildlife & Animal

Keywords: Environment, Environment (Protection) Act, 1986, Environment (Protection) Rules, 1986, Eia

Summary: Environment, Wildlife & Animal - Environmental Clearance - Public Hearing - Respondent no.1 filed application before HC, in public interest, seeking revocation of Environment Clearance granted to appellant for expansion of its plant on ground that as a result of expansion, the proposed capacity and activities of appellant were to increase substantially and that the Environmental Clearance granted for expansion of plant was not in conformity with EIA Notification of 2006 - HC by its judgment allowed said application on ground that Environmental Clearance dt.27-1-2010 was granted without there being public consultation or public hearing which was mandatory requirement under 2006 Notification and set aside Environmental Clearance dt.27-1-2010 and directed appellant to stop operations of entire plant - Hence, instant appeal - Whether impugned order of HC could be upheld.

Held, minutes of the meetings show that the request of the appellant for exemption from the requirement of public hearing was accepted by Committee. The decision making process in doing away with or in granting exemption from public consultation/public hearing, was not based on correct principles and as such the decision was invalid and improper. However, in pursuance of Environmental clearance dt.27-1-2010, the expansion of the project has been undertaken and as reported by CPCB in its affidavit filed on 7-7-2014, most of the recommendations made by CPCB are complied with. As such, it is not appropriate to order closure of entire plant as directed by HC. Hence, decision exempting public consultation/public hearing is set aside and matter is relegated to concerned Authorities to effectuate public consultation/public hearing. However, if the public consultation/public hearing results in a negative mandate against the expansion of the project, the Authorities could direct and ensure scaling down of activities to level that was permitted by Environmental Clearance dt.20-2-2008. Appeal disposed of.

Ratio - Public consultation/public hearing is one of the important stages while considering the matter for grant of Environmental Clearance and as per decision of SC, public consultation/public hearing is considered as mandatory requirement of Environmental Clearance process.
