V. Venkata Prasad and others v High Court of A.P. and others

Supreme Court of India

29 June 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, Shiva Kirti Singh

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 498; AIR 2016 SC 3159; 2016 (4) ALT 33; 2016 (6) AWC 5767; JT 2016 (7) SC 15; 2016 (5) MLJ 672; 2016(6) SCALE 71; [2016] 3 S.C.R. 834

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges

Keywords: Andhra Pradesh State Higher Judicial Service Rules, 1958, Constitution of India, 1950, Andhra Pradesh State Higher Judicial Service Special Rules, Andhra Pradesh State Judicial Service Rules, 1962

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Andhra Pradesh State Higher Judicial Service Special Rules for Adhoc Appointments, 2001 - Seniority of judges - Direct recruits - Appellants were appointed as District Munsifs in Andhra Pradesh Judicial Service in the years 1985-1987 - Ministry of Law and Justice, Government of India sanctioned 86 additional posts to be established as Fast Track Courts - After the posts were sanctioned, the Registrar, Vigilance communicated to the State Government for appointment of District & Sessions Judges Grade II by transfer - Appellant were posted against the vacancies to Fast Track Courts temporarily - Respondent nos. 3 to 7 were appointed as District & Sessions Judge, Grade II by direct recruitment - Final seniority list was published which featured the names of respondent nos. 3 to 7 above the appellants - Appellants challenged publication of final seniority list before HC and DB of HC after analyzing rule position held that appellants could not be conferred benefit of seniority on the basis of continuous length of service - Further, it was also held that officers who were appointed by promotion/transfer from Civil Judges to the cadre of District and Sessions Judges, Grade - II could not claim seniority from the date of initial appointment but could only claim so from the date when they were regularly appointed in the cadre vacancies as they could only be appointed in the said vacancies as and when the vacancies arose and dismissed writ petition - Hence, instant appeal - Whether impugned orders of DB of HC could be upheld.

Held, appellants got promotion because of the introduction of the Fast Tract Court Scheme and under the 2001 Rules framed by HC. They were the beneficiaries of a Scheme. While continuing in the post under the Scheme, the regular posts in the cadre fell vacant and they were regularized but prior to that, the respondents were appointed as direct recruits in respect of substantive posts in their quota. Hence, appellants are not entitled seniority over respondents who were direct recruits in regular vacancies. Appeal dismissed.
