Punjab State Warehousing Corporation v Bhushan Chander and another

Supreme Court of India

29 June 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, Shiva Kirti Singh

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 496; AIR 2016 SC 3014; 2016 CRLJ 3579; 2016 (3) JCC 1684; JT 2016 (6) SC 149; 2016 (4) MLJ(Crl) 345; 2016 (3) RCR(Criminal) 869; 2016(6) SCALE 84; [2016] 4 S.C.R. 537

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Breach Of Trust, Criminal Breach Of Trust, Customs Act, 1962, Misappropriation, Omission, Single Judge, Official Duty, Reliance, Cognizance, Employed, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Constitution of India, 1950, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, Madras Village Panchayats Act

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 409, 467, 468, 471 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s.197 - Public servant - Breach of trust - Sanction to prosecution - MD of Corporation written letter to police authorities to register case against respondent no.1 for offences punishable u/ss. 409, 467, 468 and 471 of IPC or any other law; and police authorities filed charge-sheet against respondent no.1 for said offences before Trial Court - Upon perusal of evidence, Trial Court held prosecution proved guilt of respondent no.1 and passed order of conviction and sentence - On appeal, appellate authority upheld conviction but modified sentence imposed - Aggrieved, respondent no.1 filed revision before HC contending that requisite sanction u/s. 197 of CrPC was not obtained and so, trial was vitiated - Single Judge of HC held that respondent no.1 was a public servant and so, sanction was necessary and as the same was not obtained, trial was vitiated and so, set aside conviction of respondent no.1 - Hence, instant appeal - Whether the HC has correctly accepted the submission advanced on behalf of the respondent No 1, that he being an employee of the Corporation is a public servant and the trial had commenced without obtaining sanction u/s. 197 of CrPC and hence, the trial in entirety was invalid and as a result the conviction and sentence deserved to be set aside..

Held, there has to be reasonable connection between the omission or commission and the discharge of official duty or the act committed was under the color of the office held by the official. Further, Single Judge of HC based his conclusions on assumption that the allegation is that while being a public servant, the alleged criminal breach of trust was committed while he was in public service. However, no official could put forth a claim that breach of trust is connected with his official duty and the reasoning of HC could not be appreciated. Hence, in view of settled principles, it could not be said that in the obtaining factual matrix, sanction u/s. 197 of CrPC was necessary. Hence, respondents are not entitled to have the protective umbrella of s. 197 of CrPC and hence, HC has erred in setting aside the conviction and sentence on ground that the trial is vitiated in the absence of sanction. Hence, impugned judgment and order of HC is set aside and matter is remitted to HC to decide revision in accordance with law. Order accordingly.

Ratio - If the acts omission or commission is totally alien to the discharge of the official duty, question of invoking s. 197 of CrPC does not arise.
