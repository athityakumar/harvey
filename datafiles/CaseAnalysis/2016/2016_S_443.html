Sulekhan Singh and Company and others v State of Uttar Pradesh and others

Supreme Court of India

04 January 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Adarsh Kumar Goel, Anil R. Dave

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 4; (2016) 4 SCC 663; AIR 2016 SC 228; 2016 (2) AWC 1102; 2016 (3) CTC 758; JT 2016 (1) SC 50; 2016 (131) RD 20; 2016(1) SCALE 190; [2016] 1 S.C.R. 91

Case Digest

Subject: Mines & Minerals

Keywords: Minor Minerals, U.P. Minor Minerals (Concession) Rules, 1963

Summary: Mines & Minerals - Cancellation of lease - Failure to comply with govt. order - Determination - Appellants were granted mining lease - However, respondent challenged mining lease and sought cancellation of mining lease on ground that it was not in accordance with Govt. order dt. 31-5-2012 which stated that lease should be granted by public auction for transparency - Appellant contended that govt. order passed cannot be applicable to applications already made prior to 31-5-2012 - HC held that pendency of application did not create any vested right for application being considered otherwise than by way of order dt. 31-5- 2012 - HC vide impugned order quashed mining lease granted in favour of appellants vide orders dt. 24-5-2014 and 26-5-2014 on ground that said leases were granted in violation of Govt. order dt. 31-5-2012 - Whether impugned order passed by HC against appellant is justified and in accordance with law.

Held, in the instant case, plea of the appellants that they had acquired a vested right prior to G.O. dt. 31-5-2012 cannot be accepted. Order dt. 31-5-2012 was issued by the State to bring about transparency and to safeguard the Govt. revenue and was consistent with the decisions of SC. The validity thereof was upheld by the HC in, 2013 Indlaw ALL 1074. The said judgment applied to the mineral in question as specifically laid down by the HC. The HC upheld the stand of the State that pendency of application did not create any right in favour of the appellants. All applications pending as on dt. 31-5-2012 stood rejected including the application of the appellants. The appellants did not make an application after the changed policy dt. 22-10-2014 and thus the said G.O. had no application to the instant case. Hence, SC held that impugned order passed by HC is justified and in accordance with law as appellant failed to comply with Govt. order. Appeals dismissed.

Ratio - It is justified to cancel lease granted to allottes when they do not comply with Govt. order passed by authority.
