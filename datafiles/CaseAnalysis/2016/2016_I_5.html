In Re : Inhuman Conditions In 1382 Prisons (II)

Supreme Court of India

03 October 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Madan B. Lokur, R.K. Agrawal

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 705; (2016) 10 SCC 17; AIR 2016 SC 4527; JT 2016 (9) SC 521; 2016 (4) RCR(Criminal) 616; 2016(9) SCALE 503; [2016] 7 S.C.R. 1001; 2016 (4) WLN(SC) 211

Case Digest

Subject: Human Rights

Keywords: Narcotic Drugs And Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985, WOMEN & CHILDREN, Constitution of India, 1950, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act, 2015

Summary: Human Rights - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act, 2015 - Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985 - Prison reforms - Over-crowding of prisons - Instant petition regarding inhuman conditions in prisons.

Held, Ministry of Women and Child Development of GOI had set up Committee for drafting Manual concerning issues pertaining to juveniles in custody either in Observation Homes or Special Homes or places of Safety in terms of 2015 Act. Further, SC vide order dt.6-5-2016 expanded mandate of under-trial Review Committee to examine cases of under trials falling under categories such as those eligible for release on bail under 1985 Act; those imprisoned for offences which carry maximum punishment of 2 years; those detained under Chapter VIII of CrPC etc. and also required States and IG of prisons to prepare Plan of Action either to reduce over-crowding or to augment infrastructure so that there is more space available for each prisoner. However, even though more than sufficient time has elapsed, Manual for juveniles in custody has not yet been prepared by Ministry of Women and Child Development. Hence, directions are issued to said Ministry to expedite the preparation of Manual and ensure that it is ready positively on or before 30-11-2016. Further, no State or UT had prepared Plan of Action and brought to notice of SC or learned Amicus. Hence, directions are issued to State and IG of Prisons to prepare to Plan of Action as already directed on 6-5-2016 for reducing prison population. Further, viable plan of action is to be prepared by 31-3-2017 and information is to be passed on to Additional Solicitor General and Amicus. Further, UOI through Ministry of Home Affairs is directed to obtain status of compliance of orders of SC passed on 5-2-2016 and 6-5-2016 as on 30-9-2016 and information is to be collated by Ministry of Home Affairs and shared with Additional Solicitor General and Amicus so that even the rights of prisoners, whether convicts or under trials are given due importance. Matter listed - Order accordingly.
