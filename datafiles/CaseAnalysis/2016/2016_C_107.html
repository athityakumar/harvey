Board of Control for Cricket in India v Cricket Association of Bihar and others

Supreme Court of India

21 October 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

D.Y. Chandrachud, T.S. Thakur, A.M. Khanwilkar

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 809; (2016) 10 SCC 231; AIR 2016 SC 4911; 2016 (6) AWC 6475; JT 2016 (10) SC 243; 2016(10) SCALE 237; [2016] 9 S.C.R. 819

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Constitution

Summary: Administrative - Constitution - Implementation of reforms - Timeline preferred - Validity - Appellants filed instant appeals contending that though in judgment of SC dt. 18-7-2016, it was hoped that process of implementing reforms suggested by Committee "should be completed within a period of 4 or at best 6 months, Committee has hastened the process by indicating timelines for completion even within the said period and further process of implementation requires a continuous process of monitoring and supervision and it would be reasonable to assume as did Committee, that the process could not be completed in one installment and laid down timelines for implementation.

Held, SC directed that appellants should forthwith cease and desist from making any disbursement of funds for any purpose whatsoever to any state association until and unless the state association concerned adopts a resolution undertaking to implement the recommendations of Committee as accepted by SC in its judgment dt. 18-7-2016. After such a resolution was passed and before any disbursement of funds takes place to State association concerned, a copy of resolution should be filed before Committee and SC together with an affidavit of President of State association undertaking to abide by the reforms contained in the report of Committee, as modified by SC. Committee appointed by SC was requested to appoint an independent auditor to scrutinise and audit the income received and expenditure incurred by appellants. Committee would be at liberty to formulate the terms of engagement and reference to auditors having regard to the above directions. Appellants should defray the costs, charges and expenses of auditors. President and Secretary of appellants association should within 2 weeks file a statement on affidavit indicating compliance made by appellants of those of recommendations of Committee which were complied with, the manner of compliance and steps adopted for securing compliance with remaining recommendations. They should appear before Committee to explain the manner of compliance. An affidavit of compliance should be filed before SC on or before 3-12-2016 by President and Secretary to appellants association. Appellants should cooperate with Committee and auditors by granting, in particular, full access to records, accounts and other information as required to facilitate implementation of these directions. Order accordingly.
