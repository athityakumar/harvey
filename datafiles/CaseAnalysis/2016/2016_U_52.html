Common Cause and another v Union of India and others

Supreme Court of India

04 April 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Jagdish Singh Khehar, C. Nagappan

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 250; AIR 2016 SC 1672; 2016 (4) ALD(SC) 86; 2016 (5) ALT(SC) 13; 2016 (3) AWC 2561; JT 2016 (4) SC 217; 2016 (4) KarLJ 516; 2016 (3) MLJ 467; 2016 (3) RCR(Civil) 518; 2016(3) SCALE 562; [2016] 2 S.C.R. 243

Case Digest

Subject: Mines & Minerals

Keywords: Minerals, Mineral Concession Rules, 1960, Mining Leases, Renewal Application, Mining Operations, Mineral Development, Interest Of Mineral Development, Mines and Minerals (Development and Regulation) Act, 1957, Mines and Minerals (Development and Regulation) Amendment Act, 2015

Summary: Mines & Minerals - Mines and Minerals (Development and Regulation) Act, 1957, ss. 8A, 8A(1), 8A(5), 8A(6) - Mines and Minerals (Development and Regulation) Amendment Act, 2015 - Mineral Concession Rules, 1960, rr. 24A(1) - Suspension of mining operation - Revocation of suspension on ground of valid mining lease - Determination - SC vide order in earlier case restrained mining leaseholders from carrying on any mining operations on ground that none of leaseholders were in possession of clearances required for carrying on mining operations -Applications are filed by leaseholders seeking revocation of order of suspension, wherein concerned applicants asserted, that they had obtained all clearances - However, respondent contended that question of granting permission to leaseholders to recommence mining operations would arise, only if leaseholders have subsisting mining lease - Whether applicants have subsisting right to carry on mining operation, under valid lease so as to cancel order of suspension passed by SC.

Held, taking into consideration 1957 Act, 2015 Amendment Act and amended r. 24(A)(1) of Rules, SC held that leaseholder would have subsisting mining lease, if period of original grant was still in currency on 12-1-2015,however, leaseholder whose original lease has since expired, would still have subsisting lease, if original lease having been renewed, renewal period was still in currency on 12-1-2015 and such leaseholder, would be entitled to benefit of s. 8A of amended Act of 2015. Further, leaseholder who has moved application for renewal of mining lease, at least twelve months before existing lease was due to expire, and on consideration, such application has been rejected, will be considered as not valid leaseholder and provisions of amended s. 8A of 1957 Act will not enure to benefit of such leaseholder. However, where application is not rejected it will be considered to be valid leaseholder having subsisting right to carry on mining operations, till expiry of two years after 18-7-2014, i.e., up to 17-7-2016, as per unamended and amended r. 24A of Rules and such leaseholder would have benefit of s. 8A(5), (6) of 2015 Act. Further, leaseholder who had moved second (third or subsequent) renewal application at least twelve months before renewed lease was due to expire, and whose application had not been considered and rejected up to 12-1-2015 would still have benefit of s. 8A (5), (6) of 2015 Act. Further, SC held that unless order is passed by State Govt. declaring, that mining lease has lapsed, mining lease would be deemed to be subsisting, up to date of expiry of lease period provided by lease document. Petition disposed of.

Ratio - In order to cancel suspension order passed against mining lease holder, it is necessary that leaseholder have subsisting right to carry on mining operation under valid lease.
