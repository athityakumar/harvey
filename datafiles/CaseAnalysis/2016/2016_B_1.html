B. A. Umesh v Registrar General, High Court of Karnataka

Supreme Court of India

03 October 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Prafulla C. Pant, Ranjan Gogoi

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 1006; (2017) 4 SCC 124; 2017 CRLJ 762; JT 2016 (10) SC 8; 2016 (4) RCR(Criminal) 575; 2016(9) SCALE 600; [2016] 5 S.C.R. 438

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Rape, Opportunity, Review, Death Sentence, History, Robbery, Aggravating, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 376, 302, 392 - Confirmation of Death sentence - Deceased was smothered after commission of sexual assault and upon completion of investigation, charge sheet was filed against petitioner for trial in respect of offences punishable u/ss. 376, 302 and 392 of IPC - After hearing parties, Trial Court convicted petitioner u/ss. 302, 376 and 392 of IPC and, after hearing parties on quantum of sentence, and considering gravity of offence and criminal history of petitioner and other aggravating and mitigating circumstances, petitioner was imposed sentence of imprisonment and also sentenced to death u/s. 302 of IPC - Later, record was sent to HC for confirmation of death sentence and same was registered as Reference and petitioner also filed appeal before HC against conviction and sentence recorded by Trial Court - HC disposed of both reference and appeal vide common judgment whereby, conviction of petitioner was affirmed u/ss. 302,376 and 392 of IPC and also sentence of death was confirmed - Aggrieved, appeals were filed before SC but the same were dismissed and review petitions were also dismissed - Instant miscellaneous petitions were filed but confined to point of sentence of death only - Whether death sentence awarded to petitioner could be upheld.

Held, petitioner's age of 30 years, at the time of incident, in instant facts and circumstances of the case could not be a ground to show any kind of leniency on sentence to him. Further, petitioner committed number of crimes and also escaped from lawful custody before commission of crime in instant matter. Further, petitioner committed crimes not only before the incident in question, but also within two days, subsequent to the incident in question, another robbery was committed in connection with which he was apprehended by public and handed over to police. When considered all together, petitioner is a menace and has become threat to society. Further, upon overall analysis of facts and circumstances of the case, gravity of the offence, and the manner in which the crime is committed read with antecedents of petitioner who is an ex-police official, there are no sufficient reasons to review or modify the order of affirmation of death sentence. Further, aggravating circumstances are grave and far more serious as against mitigating circumstances pointed out on behalf of petitioner. Hence, even after open hearing, review petitions could not be allowed and judgment and orders of SC in appeals could not be modified. Petitions disposed of.
