Pramod Jain and others v Securities and Exchange Board of India

Supreme Court of India

07 November 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Adarsh Kumar Goel, Anil R. Dave

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 793; (2016) 10 SCC 243; 2016 (4) BC 551; 2016 (6) Bom.C.R. 707; [2016] 199 Comp Cas 440; 2017 (1) CompLJ 161; JT 2016 (10) SC 505; 2016 (8) MLJ 102; 2016(10) SCALE 513; [2016] 9 S.C.R. 178

Case Digest

Subject: Securities Exchange Board of India

Keywords: Board Of Directors, Company Law Board, Stock, Companies Act, 1956, Dispose, Indian Contract Act, 1872, Variation, Undue Delay, Settlement, Subsequent, Securities, Securities And Exchange Board Of India, Private Limited, Promoters, Shareholders, Securities And Exchange Board Of India Act, 1992, Mismanagement, Investors, Letter Of Offer, Securities And Exchange Board Of India (Substantial Acquisition Of Shares And Takeovers) Regulations, 1997, Constitution of India, 1950

Summary: (A) Securities Exchange Board of India - Securities and Exchange Board of India (Substantial Acquisition of Shares and Takeovers) Regulations, 1997, regn. 23, 27(1)(d) - Delay by SEBI - Comments of SEBI on draft letter of offer - Acquirers along with PAC made Public Announcement (PA) through merchant banker to acquire 25% of the target company from its equity shareholders at a price of Rs. 101 per equity share and later, acquirers and PAC through merchant banker, filed draft letter of offer (DLO) with SEBI on 26-11-2009 - While so, acquirers vide application dt.8-10-2011 sought permission to withdraw the offer u/regn.27(1)(d) of the Regulations on ground that SEBI had not taken any decision on DLO in two years and during said period, management of target company had systematically siphoned off its coffers, depleted its valuable fixed assets and eroded its net worth substantially with intention of making it shell company - However, SEBI by order dt.13-4-2012 rejected application of acquirers for withdrawal of public offer to acquire shares of target company in terms of public announcement dt.12-11-2009 under the provisions of the Regulations - On appeal, Appellate Tribunal affirmed order of SEBI and hence, instant appeal - Whether timeline laid down under Takeover Regulations are required to be adhered to by SEBI.

Held, even though, timeline prescribed under the Regulations could not be adhered to when SEBI justifiably takes time in dealing with the complaints, yet, in instant case, the stand of SEBI was that it could not go, into the complaints as the right forum was CLB. Nevertheless, SEBI could have promptly proceeded with the matter and as such, there was undue delay on the part of SEBI in dealing with DLO. However, even though there was delay on part of SEBI, still acquirers are not entitled to withdrawal of public offer. The withdrawal has to be dealt with u/regn. 27 of the Regulations as held by SC. Hence, though SEBI was not justified in causing delay in giving its comments on public offer, that by itself is not enough to justify withdrawal from public offer so long as the case does not fall u/regn.27. Appeal dismissed.

(B) Securities Exchange Board of India - Withdrawal of public offer - Prejudice to acquirers - Whether decision of SEBI and Appellate Tribunal in not permitting withdrawal of public offer is justified.

Held, SEBI as well as Appellate Tribunal have concurrently held that public offer is capable of being carried out and that it has not become impossible and also assets are available with the target company. Further, finding has also been recorded about the circumstances preceding the public offer and the conduct of the acquirer which is based on record. The steps for development of the Vile Parle property had already been initiated and the acquirer had taken remedies before CLB against the decision of the target company and had settled the matter with the target company. Further, there is no absolute bar for the target company to take decision about its assets, subject to compliance with statutory procedure and subject to the decision being otherwise valid. Furthermore, against any mala fide, illegal or unjustified decision of the target company, remedies at appropriate fora are available to aggrieved parties. Under such circumstances, there is no justification for automatic withdrawal from public offer without clear prejudice to the acquirer to the extent of rendering the carrying out of public offer impossible. Hence, decision of SEBI and Appellate Tribunal could not be interfered with. Appeal dismissed.

Ratio - The general principle is that public offer once made could not be withdrawn. Exception to the rule is the specified situations under Regulations as laid down by SC.
