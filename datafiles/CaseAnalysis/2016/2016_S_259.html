Casio India Company Private Limited v State of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

29 March 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, Shiva Kirti Singh

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 246; (2016) 6 SCC 209; AIR 2016 SC 1690; JT 2016 (3) SC 393; 2016 (3) MLJ 748; 2016(3) SCALE 441; [2016] 2 S.C.R. 791; [2016] 91 VST 231

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Keywords: Tribunal, Exemption From Payment Of Tax, Levy, Period, Central Sales Tax Act, 1956, Registered Dealer, Tax Payable, Sale Of Goods, Turnover, Central Act, Sales Tax Law, Rate, Requirement, Entitlement, Haryana General Sales Tax Act, 1973, Haryana General Sales Tax Rules, 1975

Summary: Sales Tax - Indirect Tax - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956, ss. 5,8(2A), 8(5) - Haryana General Sales Tax Rules, 1975, rr. 28A, 28A(4)(c) - Intra-State sales - Exemption from sales tax - Appellant claimed exemption relying on notification dt. 4-9-1995 issued u/s. 8(5) of the Act read with r.28A(4)(c) of the Rules but the claim of exemption was not accepted by AO - Aggrieved, appellant preferred appeal before Commissioner and it being dismissed, approached Tribunal which also dismissed appeal - Appellant filed writ petition before HC seeking directions to Tribunal to make reference and in pursuance of orders of HC, Tribunal made reference to HC seeking opinion on whether notification dt.4-9-1995 is relatable to exemption of goods or the person selling it and whether in view of said notification, inter-state sales of the goods manufactured by an 'exempted unit' even by any other dealer, is exempted from levy of the Act - HC concluded that successive sales of goods manufactured by dealer holding valid exemption certificate were exempt from payment of sales tax so long as they were inter-state sales but in respect of sale of goods by a dealer not holding exemption certificate u/r.28A in the course of inter-state trade, the benefit of exemption envisaged under notification was not available to such dealer and accordingly, answered reference against appellant and in favour of revenue - Hence, instant appeals - Whether impugned orders could be upheld.

Held, the purport and impact of r.28A is with reference to eligible industrial unit, is not only clear from definition clauses which define eligibility certificate, exemption certificate, etc. but also from r.28A(4)(a) which stipulates that the benefit of tax exemption or deferment would be given to an eligible industrial unit holding exemption or entitlement certificate for the period specified. Further, r.28A(2)(c) postulates that goods manufactured by eligible industrial unit availing of exemption under the Rule would be exempt from levy of tax on all successive stage/stages of sale or purchase, subject to the dealer affecting the said purchase or sale furnishing a certificates in the form of ST-14A obtained from assessing authority and the clause extends the benefit granted u/cl. (n)(ii) which relates to inter-state trade or commerce to intra-state sale or purchase. Such sales could be one or successive and tax at all stages is exempt. The exemption, hence, is good specific, subject to other conditions being satisfied. Further, the basic objective and purpose is to exempt the goods manufactured in the State when they are further transferred in the course of inter-State or intra-State trade or commerce. Hence, reference is made to eligible industries and the goods manufactured by said industries, which are entitled to exemption. The exemption notification refers to sale of goods manufactured by a dealer holding a valid exemption certificate. The emphasis is on the goods manufactured. However, it is confined by condition that the said manufacture should be within the exemption period and by a dealer holding exemption certificate. Further, r.28A(4)(c) exempts all intra-state sales including subsequent sales. The intention is to exempt all subsequent stages in the State of Haryana and the eligible product could be sold a number of times, without payment of tax. Thus, r.28A(2)(4)(ii) refers to inter-state trade or commerce and the notification does not refer to subsequent sales as in case of r.28A(4)(c). Whether or not tax should be paid on subsequent sales/purchase in the other State could not be made subject matter of r.28A or the notification. Inter-State sale from the State of Haryana would be only once or not a repeated one. Hence, there is no requirement of reference to subsequent sale. Hence, appellants would reap benefit of notification dt.4-9-1995. All the appeals are allowed and impugned orders are set aside. Appeals disposed of.

Ratio - Ss.8(2A) and 5 of the Act enable State Govt. to exempt the tax payable under the Act in public interest by issuing appropriate notification and for said purpose, the State Govt. has to be satisfied and is also entitled to impose conditions which have to be specified in the notification.
