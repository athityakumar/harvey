State of H.P. and others v Rajesh Chander Sood etc.

Supreme Court of India

28 September 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Jagdish Singh Khehar, C. Nagappan

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 691; (2016) 10 SCC 77; AIR 2016 SC 5436; JT 2016 (9) SC 439; 2016 (4) LLJ 334; 2016(9) SCALE 178

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Estoppel, Evidence, Wages, Gratuity, Civil Services, Equity, Conditions Of Service, provident fund, Transfer, Retrospective Effect, Sales Tax, SERVICE, Limitation, Review, Computation Of, CONSTITUTION, General Clauses Act, 1897, Violation Of, Age, Relief, Expenditure, Superannuation, Leave, Pay, Salary, Pension, Representation, Employment, Pay Scale, Rule, Date, Pensionary Benefits, Posts, Qualifying Service, Validity Of, Revision Of Pay, Regular, Abolition Of Post, Delay, Interest, Policy, Government Servants, Amendment, Option, Relationship, Scheme, Allowances, Pension Rules, Civil Servants, Employer, Central Civil Services (Pension) Rules, 1972, Cut-Off Date, Pension Scheme, Classification, Karnataka Civil Services Rules, 1958, Arrears, Banks, Running Allowance, Discrimination, Corporations, Employees Provident Funds and Miscellaneous Provisions Act, 1952, Writ Petition, Notice, Employee, Constitution of India, 1950, Post, Right to, Training, Himachal Pradesh Corporate Sector Employees Pension (Family Pension, Commutation of Pension and Gratuity) Scheme, 1999, Rajasthan Civil Services (Commutation of Pension) Rules, 1981, Employees' Provident Funds Scheme, 1995

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Administrative - Employees Provident Funds and Miscellaneous Provisions Act, 1952 - Repeal of Scheme - Cut-off date for extension of benefits - Taking into consideration the report of high level committee on viability of '1999 Scheme', State Govt. took decision to repeal '1999 Scheme' and decided that regular employees who had retired from corporate bodies, during the period of subsistence of '1999 Scheme' from 1999 to 2004, would not be affected and accordingly issued notification dt.2-12-2004 repealing '1999 Scheme' - Aggrieved by repeal, employees who were deprived of benefit of '1999 Scheme' challenged repeal notification before HC through writ petitions wherein HC by impugned common order dt.19-12-2013, allowed all writ petitions - Hence, instant appeals by State - Whether State Govt. was justified in postulating a cut-off date, by which some of the employees governed by '1999 Scheme' were entitled to draw pension, whereas others, who had not retired by the time the repeal notification was issued on 2-12-2004, were deprived of such benefits.

Held, SC repeatedly upheld a cut-off date, for extending better and higher pensionary benefits, based on the financial health of the employer. A cut-off could therefore legitimately be prescribed for extending pensionary benefits, if the funds available could not assuage the liability, to all the existing pensioners. Hence, it is well within the authority of the State Govt., in exercise of its administrative powers to fix a cut-off date, for continuing the right to receive pension in some, and depriving some others of the same. Further, original action of the State Govt. was bonafide, and for the welfare of the respondent-employees. The State Govt. could not be accused of having misrepresented to respondent-employees in any manner. As per the provisions of '1999 Scheme', pension scheme would be self-financing, and would be administered from the corpus fund created out of the employer's contribution to their CPF account. However, when the said foundational basis for introducing the pension scheme, was found to be an incorrect determination or calculation the same was withdrawn. Under such circumstances, it could not be inferred, that the State Govt., induced the respondent-employees, to move to 'the 1999 Scheme'. Hence, principle of estoppel/promissory estoppel is not applicable. Further, the calculations projected at the behest of the State Govt., to demonstrate that scheme was not financially viable were not disputed. Hence, as the respondent-employees were unable to demonstrate, that the foundational basis for withdrawing 'the 1999 Scheme', was not premised on any arbitrary consideration, or alternatively, was not founded on any irrelevant consideration, contention of respondent-employees that withdrawal of '1999 scheme' was not based on due consideration or that, it was irrational or arbitrary or unreasonable could not be accepted. Further, action of State Govt. in allowing those who had already started earning pensionary benefits under '1999 Scheme,' was based on a legitimate classification, accepted in law. Hence, legality and constitutionality of the impugned notification is upheld. As such, appeals are allowed and impugned order of HC is set aside. Appeals disposed of.
