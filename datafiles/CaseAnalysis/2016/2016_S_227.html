Vipinchandra Vadilal Bavishi (D) by Lrs. and another v State of Gujarat and others

Supreme Court of India

28 January 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

M.Y. Eqbal, C. Nagappan

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 79; (2016) 4 SCC 531; AIR 2016 SC 626; 2016 (2) AWC 1543; 2016 (3) CalLT 66; 2016 (1) CLT(SC) 126; 2016 (2) CTC 470; 2016 (3) G.L.R. 2629; JT 2016 (3) SC 308; 2016 (1) MLJ 759; 2016 (131) RD 761; 2016(1) SCALE 620; [2016] 1 S.C.R. 283

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Res Judicata, Particulars, General Clauses Act, 1897, Letters Patent, Dispossession, Failure, Urban Land (Ceiling and Regulation) Repeal Act, 1999

Summary: Land & Property - Urban Land (Ceiling and Regulation) Act, 1976 - Urban Land (Ceiling and Regulation) Repeal Act, 1999 - Land in excess of ceiling limit - Appellant filed petition before HC for declaration that respondent nos. 1 and 2 have no powers or authority to take possession of land in question and prayed for permanent injunction against respondent nos. 1 and 2 for dealing or disposing of land pending petition - Further, appellants also challenged legality and validity of order of allotment of land to respondent no.3-Society - HC observed State Govt. was not legally justified in disposing land in question under 1999 Act and as said land was disposed of without observing settled norms for disposal of public property, quashed and set aside order allotting land to respondent no.3- Society and also dismissed petition of appellants - Aggrieved, appellants filed appeal before DB of HC assailing dismissal of their petition and respondent no.3-Society also filed appeal challenging cancellation of allotment of land - However, DB of HC held that appellants are not entitled to benefit of 1999 Act and dismissed appeal of appellants but allowed appeal of respondent no.3- Society - Hence, instant appeal by appellants - Whether impugned order of HC could be upheld.

Held, in the draft statement, neither the lands comprised within plot nos. 36 to 43 were shown as excess land nor objection was invited from appellants and also in final statement prepared u/s. 9 of 1976 Act, land of plot nos. 36 to 43 was not shown as excess land beyond ceiling limit. Further, even the map attached to the letter dt.26-6-1989 shows that the possession of plot nos. 1 to 16 were taken and not of plot nos. 36 to 43. Hence, neither the notifications u/ss. 10(1), 10(2), 10(3) and 10(5) of 1976 Act were issued in respect of plot nos. 36 to 43 nor possession of said plots have been taken over by respondents. On contrary, notification was issued u/s. 10(3) of 1976 Act declaring land of plot nos. 1 to 16 deemed to have been acquired by State. Further, no notice was adduced by State to show that appellants were asked to surrender or deliver possession of plot nos.36 to 43 nor there is any evidence to show that appellants have refused or failed to comply with any notice issued u/s. 10(5) of 1976 Act. Further, substituting different lands in place of the lands which had been notified by a statutory Notification u/ss.10(1), 10(3) and 10(5) of 1976 Act could not and should not be done by issuing a corrigendum unless the mandatory requirements contained in said sections is complied with. Hence, appeal is allowed and impugned judgment of HC is set aside and appellant are entitled to retain possession of land comprised within plot nos.36-43 as the same is not vested in the State. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - The provisions contained in ss.8, 9 and 10 of 1976 Act have to be mandatorily complied with before the land is declared in excess of ceiling limit. Further, a land holder could not be divested from his land on the plea of clerical or arithmetical mistake liable to be corrected by issuing corrigendum.
