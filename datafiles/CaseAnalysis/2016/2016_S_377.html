Harpal Singh @ Chhota and another v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

21 November 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Amitava Roy, A.K. Sikri

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 829; (2017) 1 SCC 734; AIR 2016 SC 5389; 2017 CRLJ 551; JT 2016 (11) SC 194; 2016 (4) RCR(Criminal) 936; 2016(12) SCALE 206; [2016] 8 S.C.R. 334

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Conspiracy, Currency Notes, FIR, Computer, Omission, Indian Evidence Act, 1872, Opportunity, Signature, Expert, Secret, False Implication, Ransom, Disclosure Statement, Oath, Revolver, Identification, Pistol, Discovery, Arms Act, 1959, Inadmissible, Registered, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, Plea

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Arms Act, 1959, ss. 25, 27, 54, 59 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 161, 164, 313 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120B, 34, 364, 364A, 392, 395, 397, 412, 465, 467, 468, 471, 474 - Abduction - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellants/accused allegedly kidnapped victim son of complainant at gun point for extracting ransom - Charge-sheet u/ss. 364A, 392, 395, 397, 412, 465, 467, 468, 471, 474, 120B IPC and ss. 25/27 of the Act was filed against accused - Trial Court convicted accused u/ss. 364A, 395, 412, 471, 120B IPC and u/s. 25 of the Act and awarded sentences ranging from 1 year to imprisonment for life and fine - On appeal, HC upheld conviction of Trial Court - Hence, instant appeals.

Held, PWs 10, 11, 12, 14, 16 were those who on oath, stated to have lent different amounts to father of victim to meet the ransom demand. Of those witnesses, PW14 in particular, claimed to have put his identification marks on the packets of currency notes and on basis thereof he identified at the trial, the same when shown to him. Evidence adduced vis-a-vis the stage wise recovery of currency notes, fire-arms, car etc. from successive disclosures made by accused do establish their complicity in offence. The witnesses examined by prosecution as lenders of different amounts not only were referred to by witness in his deposition, to reiterate they also endorsed to have responded to his clarion call. Further, disclosures made by accused persons leading to the recoveries and seizures were indeed relevant facts in support of the charge levelled against them.

Charges against accused stand proved beyond reasonable doubt even sans the call details. Prosecution was able to prove the charges levelled against appellants. Therefore the encompassing sweep of culpability of an offence of conspiracy, if proved, even from the established attendant circumstances. Both the Courts below have analysed evidence in correct perspectives and in the face of the conclusions recorded on different aspects of imputations levelled against them SC was of opinion that no interference was called for with impugned judgment of conviction and sentence recorded against them. Appeals dismissed.
