Pepsu Road Transport Corporation, Patiala, Through its Managing Director and another v S. K. Sharma and others

Supreme Court of India

08 August 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Shiva Kirti Singh, R. Banumathi

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 569; (2016) 9 SCC 206; AIR 2016 SC 3662; 2016 (5) AWC 4809; 2016 (150) FLR 930; JT 2017 (1) SC 314; 2016 (3) LLJ 513; 2016(7) SCALE 611; [2016] 4 S.C.R. 24

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Merger, Gratuity, Transfer, Companies Act, 1956, Limitation, Pension, Rule, Terms And Conditions, Date, Posts, Appeal, Absorption, Delay, Retiral Benefits, Government Servants, Option, Relationship, Scheme, Temporary, Pension Scheme, Road Transport Corporations Act, 1950, States Reorganisation Act, 1956, Lien, Writ Petition, Notice, Employee, Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, Constitution of India, 1950, Post, Punjab Service Rules, Corporation framed PRTC Employees Pension/Gratuity and General Provident Fund Regulations, 1992, PEPSU Road Transport Corporation Regulations, Mysore Civil Services Regulations, PEPSU Services Regulations

Summary: Service - Corporation framed PRTC Employees Pension/Gratuity and General Provident Fund Regulations, 1992 - Employment Regulations 1957 - Temporary basis appointment - Retiral benefits - Denial - Respondents were appointed in dept. of appellants/corporation on temporary basis - Subsequently, after the retirement of respondents, w.e.f. 15-06-1992 Corporation framed the 1992 Regulations wherein pension was introduced in Corporation - Respondents who had received their retiral benefits under the 1957 Regulations filed writ petition before HC seeking that the 1992 Regulations have not been made retrospective that they continued to be employees of State in the dept. of appellants till the date of reorganization with right to pension as available to Govt. servants - Single Judge allowed petition on ground that respondents had simply been transferred from parent dept. to serve in Corporation and therefore they continued to be Govt. servants because there was no order passed for their absorption in Corporation - On appeal, DB dismissed appeal affirming order of Single Judge - Hence, instant appeal - Whether claim of respondents that in spite of transfer of parent dept. to Corporation they continue to be Govt. servants and therefore entitled to retiral benefits instead of CPF (Govt. Contribution) is acceptable or not.

Held, HC erred in ignoring conduct of respondents, effect of Chief Secretary's letter dt. 16-10-1956 containing decision of parent dept. and its acceptance by Corporation reflected by order dt. 30-11-1956. HC further erred in relying upon law which is applicable when there is no merger of Govt. concern with private concern but only individual employees are transferred on deputation or on foreign service to other organizations/services. If there are no such stipulations like in instant case then transferee concern like Corporation has no obligation to ask for options and to issue letters of options to individual employees who become employees of transferee organization simply by virtue of order and action of transfer of whole concern leading to merger. However, if the employees choose to accept the transition of their service from one concern to another and acquiesce then after decades and especially after their retirement they cannot be permitted to turn back and challenge the entire developments after a gap of decades. Respondents had accepted to continue as employees of Corporation pursuant to order of merger/transfer of parent dept. w.e.f. 16-10-1956 and on completing their service under Corporation and reaching the age of retirement they were entitled to receive only the benefits of CPF and gratuity as admissible to them under then prevailing regulations of Corporation. Since they accepted those retiral benefits there is no relationship left between Corporation and respondents and in such a situation further claim against Corporation that it should treat respondents to be Govt. servants and adjust their retiral benefits accordingly was totally untenable and wrongly allowed by HC. Impugned judgment of HC granting relief to respondents is set aside. Appeal allowed.
