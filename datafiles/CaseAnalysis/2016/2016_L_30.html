United India Insurance Company Limited v Leisure Wear Exports Limited Etc.

Supreme Court of India

29 June 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Sapre, Ashok Bhushan

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 451; AIR 2016 SC 3145; 2016 (117) ALR 899; 2016 (6) AWC 6348; 2016 (3) CPJ(Sc) 11; JT 2016 (7) SC 431; 2016 (5) MLJ 529; 2016 (3) RCR(Civil) 698; [2016] 4 S.C.R. 799

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection; Insurance

Keywords: Consumer, Consumer Protection Act, 1986, Insurance Policy, Marine Insurance Act, 1963

Summary: Insurance - Consumer Protection - Consumer Protection Act, 1986 - Marine Insurance Act, 1963, ss. 17, 52, 79 - Short Delivery of Goods - Order Challenged - Respondent/complainant obtained marine policy from appellant/insurance company against export of goods, however, loss occurred due to short delivery of goods - State Forum awarded compensation to respondent in both complaints, which included value of loss of goods, loss of earning and interest - National Commission upheld order of State Forum, thereby dismissed appeals of insurance company - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, s. 17 of the Act, in terms, recognizes and permits the insured to make assignment of their contract of insurance policy in favour of an assignee and at the same time allows the insured even after making an assignment to retain all those rights which are available to them under the contract of insurance with the Insurer (appellant). Hence, in terms of s. 17, even after making an assignment by the insured of their contract of insurance policy, the rights of insured under the contract of insurance policy are not assigned in favour of assignee by the deed of assignment but they are continued to remain with the insured. The respondent was, therefore, legally entitled and had the locus to file a complaint against the appellant on the strength of contract of insurance policy for enforcement of their all contractual rights available to them under the insurance policy for claiming compensation for the loss caused from the appellant and the complaint so filed by the respondent could not be dismissed as not maintainable on the ground of locus. It was thus rightly held as maintainable. Hence, appeals devoid of any merit and the same are dismissed. Appeals dismissed.
