Madina Begum and another v Shiv Murti Prasad Pandey and others

Supreme Court of India

01 August 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Madan B. Lokur, R.K. Agrawal

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 567; AIR 2016 SC 3554; 2016 (118) ALR 465; 2016 (4) AWC 4235; 2016 (6) Bom.C.R. 49; 2016 (3) CGLJ 523; 2016 (3) CLT(SC) 342; 2017 (4) CTC 99; 2016 (3) JabLJ 1; JT 2016 (7) SC 457; 2016 (6) MLJ 123; 2016 (2) OLR 780; 2016 (3) RCR(Civil) 952; 2016 (133) RD 436; 2016(7) SCALE 478; [2016] 5 S.C.R. 92

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Contract, Limitation Act, 1963, Limitation, Agricultural Land, Agreement To Sell, Code of Civil Procedure, 1908

Summary: (A) Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 39 r. 1, O. 39 r. 2, s. 96 - Limitation Act, 1963, art. 54 of Schedule - Specific performance suit - Limitation -Sustainability - Deceased entered into an agreement to sell agricultural land being disputed property with appellant - Appellant filed suit for specific performance of agreement before Trial Court - Trial Court decided issue of limitation in appellant's favour and dismissed suit - On appeal, HC dismissed appeal holding that suit was barred by limitation and Trial Court committed a grave error in recording a finding that suit was within limitation - Hence, instant appeal - Whether suit filed by appellant was barred by limitation in terms of art. 54 of Schedule 1 of the Act.

Held, agreement between deceased and appellant did not specify a calendar date as the date fixed for performance of agreement. Consequently, view expressed in Ahmmadsahab Abdul Milla (Dead) By Proposed Lrs v. Bibijan And Ors, 2009 Indlaw SC 423, on the first part of art. 54 of Schedule 1 of the Act apply to facts of instant case. In taking a contrary view, ignoring absence of a specified date for performance of agreement and reversing Trial Court, HC has fallen in error. Appeal allowed.

(B) Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 39 r. 1, O. 39 r. 2, s. 96 - Limitation Act, 1963, art. 54 of Schedule - Appeal - Barred by limitation - Validity -Whether HC ought to have decided first appeal filed by appellant not only on preliminary issue of limitation but also on all other issues.

Held, HC only considered issue of limitation and did not consider other issues in appeal. This was impermissible. SC do not agree with view taken by HC on the issue of limitation, there is no option but to set aside view expressed by HC and following decisions of SC, remand the matter to HC to decide remaining issues in first appeal filed u/s. 96 CPC. Impugned order of HC was set aside. Appeal allowed.
