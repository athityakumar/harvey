Sahyadri Co-operative Credit Society Limited v State of Maharashtra and others

Supreme Court of India

28 March 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Prafulla C. Pant, Ranjan Gogoi

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 238; AIR 2016 SC 1580; 2016(3) ALL MR 481; 2016 (3) AWC 3038; 2016 (3) Bom.C.R. 547; JT 2016 (3) SC 353; 2016 (3) MLJ 599; 2016(3) SCALE 434; [2016] 2 S.C.R. 347

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance

Keywords: Sugarcane (Control) Order, 1966, Multi-State Co-Operative Societies Act, 2002, Maharashtra Land Revenue Code, 1966, Godown, Civil Procedure, Land & Property

Summary: Banking & Finance - Maharashtra Land Revenue Code, 1966, s. 218 - Secured Loan granted - Priority in repayment - Determination - It is stated that respondent nos. 5 and 6 availed loan from appellants Co-operative Credit Societies and amount was credited in account of erstwhile creditor of respondent for which charge was created in favor of appellant - However, respondent suffered losses and respondent no.2 Collector passed order attaching stock and godown of respondent and auction of stock for release of unpaid amount which was objected by appellant - HC passed order for auctioning of goods and held that appellants would have first right over amount of pledged sugar - However, authority rejected claim of appellant and directed payment of amount to creditor and workers - Further, vide impugned order dismissed petition of appellant on ground that appellant have alternative remedy available to them to file suit u/s. 218 of Code - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, in earlier round of litigation, HC accepted that stock goods of respondent was pledged in favour of appellants. HC disposed of petition directing Collector to consider entitlement and priority of appellants, sugarcane farmers and workers. SC held that HC while passing earlier order heard contesting respondent in present round of litigation, hence, it is not open for contesting respondents now to challenge genuineness of pledge made in favour of appellants, as order in earlier round has attained finality. Further, SC held that resolutions were passed by bank granting 'No Objection' to respondent for raising working capital loan from other financial institutions and for pledging stock along with godown in favor of appellant, hence, SC held that documents prove that there are transactions of pledge in question in favour of appellants. Hence, impugned order passed by HC is set aside and authorities are directed to disburse amount to appellant over dues payable to workers and sugarcane farmers. Appeals allowed.

Ratio - Party being secured creditors have right of precedence in repayment of dues outstanding against other creditors.
