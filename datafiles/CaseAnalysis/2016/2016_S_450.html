Nankaunoo v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

19 January 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

R. Banumathi, T.S. Thakur, A.K. Sikri

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 38; (2016) 3 SCC 317; AIR 2016 SC 447; 2016 (2) JCC 882; JT 2016 (1) SC 423; 2016 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1156; 2016 (1) MLJ(Crl) 525; 2016 (2) RCR(Criminal) 774; 2016(1) SCALE 344; [2016] 4 S.C.R. 627

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Pistol

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 313 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 304 - Murder - Nature of injury sufficient to cause death - Conviction - Challenged - Trial Court convicted appellant for commission of offence u/s. 302 of IPC and awarded sentence of imprisonment for life and by impugned judgment, HC dismissed appeal against said order of Trial Court and upheld conviction - Hence, instant appeal assailing judgment and order of HC - Whether conviction of appellant u/s. 302 of IPC is sustainable.

Held, prosecution has not elicited from doctors that the gunshot injury on the inner part of left thigh caused rupture of any important blood vessel and that it was sufficient in the ordinary course of nature to cause the death. Hence, in view of situs and nature of injury and in absence of evidence elicited from the doctor that the said injury was sufficient in the ordinary course of nature to cause death, it is fit case where conviction of appellant is to be u/s. 304 Part I of IPC. Hence, conviction of appellant u/s. 302 of IPC is modified as conviction u/s. 304 Part I IPC and appellant is sentenced for 10 years rigorous imprisonment. Appeal partly allowed.

Ratio - Depending on the nature of weapon used and situs of the injury, in some cases, the sufficiency of injury to cause death in the ordinary course of nature has to be proved and could not be inferred from the fact that death has, in fact, taken place.
