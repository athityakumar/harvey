Devinder Singh and others v State of Punjab, through CBI

Supreme Court of India

25 April 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Arun Mishra, V. Gopala Gowda

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 316; AIR 2016 SC 2090; 2016 CRLJ 2658; JT 2016 (4) SC 340; 2016 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1785; 2016 (2) MLJ(Crl) 682; 2016(4) SCALE 348

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Good Faith, CBI, Jurisdiction, Misappropriation, Omission, Opportunity, Gold (Control) Act, 1968, CONSTITUTION, Criminal Proceedings, Prosecution Evidence, Version, Official Duty, Cognizance, Torture, Dealing, Public Duty, Official Secrets Act, 1923, Atomic Energy Act, 1962, Encounter, Kerala Police Act, Taxation on Income (Investigation Commission) Act, 1947, Punjab Disturbed Areas Act, 1983, Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, Complainant, Inquiry, Plea

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 197 - Punjab Disturbed Areas Act, 1983, s. 6 - Act done in discharge of official duty - Failure to obtain sanction - Discharge of accused - It is stated that appellant conducted fake encounters in view of terrorist attacks, hence, compliant was lodged against appellant - Appellant contended encounter was conducted in view of discharge of official duty, hence, sanction from Central Govt. is necessary - However, HC held prosecution case is of fake encounter, hence, sanction is not required as same could not be said to be act in discharge of official duties - Whether prosecution relating to Police officers can be instituted without prior sanction of Central Govt. u/s. 6 of Act.

Held, in case assault made is connected with to performance of official duties sanction would be necessary u/s. 197 of CrPC, but such relation to duty should not be pretended or fanciful claim. SC held it is open to appellant to adduce evidence in defence and to submit such other materials on record indicating that incident has taken place in discharge of their official duties. SC held that Trial Court has prima facie to proceed on basis of prosecution version and can re-decide question afresh in case from evidence adduced by prosecution or by accused or in any other manner it comes to notice of court that there was reasonable nexus of incident with discharge of official duty, Court shall re-examine question of sanction and take decision in accordance with law. Appeals disposed of.

Ratio - Whether sanction is necessary or not may have to be determined from stage to stage and material brought on record by accused depending upon facts of each case.
