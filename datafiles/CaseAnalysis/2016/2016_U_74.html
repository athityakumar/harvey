Rajeev Kumar Gupta and others v Union of India and others

Supreme Court of India

30 June 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

J. Chelameswar, A.M. Sapre

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 472; AIR 2016 SC 3228; 2016 (5) AWC 4686; 2016 (150) FLR 403; JT 2016 (11) SC 473; 2016 (3) KLT 263; 2016 (5) MLJ 311; [2016] 3 S.C.R. 407

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service

Keywords: Backward Classes, Promotion, Employment, Rule, Posts, Recruitment, Prasar Bharati (Broadcasting Corporation of India) Act, 1990, Policy, Persons With Disabilities (Equal Opportunities, Protection Of Rights And Full Participation) Act, 1995, Classification, Reservation, Constitution (Seventy-Seventh Amendment) Act, 1995, Writ Petition, Constitution of India, 1950, Post

Summary: Service - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts.14,16,16(4) - Persons With Disabilities (Equal Opportunities, Protection of Rights and Full Participation) Act, 1995 ss. 2(a), 2(k), 2(t), 32, 33 - Prasar Bharati (Broadcasting Corporation of India) Act, 1990 - Notification for posts - Reservation - Denial - Appellants who were disabled (PWD) were employed with Prasar Bharati Corporation of India (Prasar Bharati) - Appellants filed instant appeal aggrieved by two office memoranda (impugned memorandum I and II) issued by respondent/dept. that deprived them of statutory benefit of reservation under the 1995 Act with regard to Group A and Group B posts in Prasar Bharati.

Held, objective behind the 1995 Act is to integrate PWD into society and to ensure their economic progress. PWD cannot be equated with backward classes contemplated u/art.16 (4) of Constitution. Thus, low numbers of PWD (much below three per cent) are in Govt. employment long years after the 1995 Act. Barriers to their entry should be scrutinized by rigorous standards within legal framework of the 1995 Act. Further ss. 32 and 33 of the 1995 Act explicates a fine and designed balance between requirements of administration and imperative to provide greater opportunities to PWD. Therefore, identification exercise u/s. 32 of the 1995 Act was crucial. Once found to be so capable, reservation u/s. 33 of the 1995 Act to an extent of not less than three per cent should follow. Once the post is identified, it should be reserved for PWD irrespective of mode of recruitment adopted by State for filling up of the said post. Thus, impugned memoranda are illegal and inconsistent with the 1995 Act. Respondents were directed to extend three percent reservation to PWD in all identified posts in Group A and Group B, irrespective of mode of filling up of such posts. Petition allowed.

Ratio - Government shall appoint in every establishment such percentage of vacancies not less than three per cent for persons or class of persons with disability.
