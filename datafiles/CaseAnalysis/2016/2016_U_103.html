Greaves Cotton Limited v United Machinery and Appliances

Supreme Court of India

14 December 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Prafulla C. Pant, J. Chelameswar

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 888; (2017) 2 SCC 268; AIR 2017 SC 120; 2017 (2) Bom.C.R. 344; JT 2016 (12) SC 62; 2017 (1) RCR(Civil) 737; 2016(12) SCALE 723

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Keywords: Jurisdiction, Extension Of Time, Arbitration Act, 1940, Arbitration And Conciliation Act, 1996, Arbitration Clause, Conciliation

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, ss. 5, 8,8(1) - Waiver of right to seek arbitration - Plaintiff/respondent filed suit seeking decree towards breach of contract against defendant/appellant - Appellant filed an application before HC seeking extension of time for 8 weeks to file written statement and invoked arbitration clause contained in agreement - HC dismissed application on ground that appellant by moving application for extension of time to file written statement waived its right to seek arbitration - Hence, instant appeal - Whether filing of an application for extension of time to file written statement before a judicial authority constitutes 'submitting first statement on the substance of the dispute' or not.

Held, SC found it difficult to agree with HC that in instant case merely moving an application seeking further time of 8 weeks to file written statement would amount to making first statement on the substance of dispute. Further, filing of an application without reply to the allegations of plaint does not constitute first statement on the substance of dispute. It does not appear from the language of s. 8(1) of the Act that Legislature intended to include such a step like moving simple application of seeking extension of time to file written statement as first statement on the substance of dispute. Therefore, SC was unable to hold that appellant by moving an application for extension of time of 8 weeks to file written statement had waived right to object to jurisdiction of judicial authority. From impugned order it also reflects that before disposing of application u/s. 8 of the Act, HC has not looked into questions as to whether there was an agreement between the parties, whether disputes which were subject-matter of suit fall within the scope of arbitration and whether reliefs sought in suit were those that could be adjudicated and granted in arbitration. HC was directed to decide the application afresh in the light of law laid down by SC in Booz Allen and Hamilton Inc. v. SBI Homes Finance Limited and others, 2011 Indlaw SC 283. Impugned order passed by HC was set aside. Appeal allowed.
