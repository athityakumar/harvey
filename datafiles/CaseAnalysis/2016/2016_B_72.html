Board of Control For Cricket v Cricket Association of Bihar and others

Supreme Court of India

18 July 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 553; (2016) 8 SCC 535; JT 2016 (6) SC 573; 2016 (5) MLJ 774; 2016(7) SCALE 143; [2016] 8 S.C.R. 606

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative

Keywords: Natural Justice, Right To Information Act, 2005, Tamil Nadu Societies Registration Act, 1975, Ombudsman, Indian Council of World Affairs Ordinance, 2001, ICC Rules, Constitution of India, 1950, IPL Regulations, National Sports Development Code of India, 2011, Operational Rules, IPL Rules

Summary: (A) Administrative - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts.12, 14, 19, 19(1)(c), 19(1)(d)(5), 19(1)(g)(6), 226, 32 - Indian Council of World Affairs Ordinance, 2001 - National Sports Development Code of India, 2011 - Right to Information Act, 2005 - Recommendation of Committee - Violation - Validity - Instant appeals are a sequel to SC order in case of BCCI v. Cricket Association of Bihar and Ors., 2015 Indlaw SC 44 - SC held that even when Board of Control for Cricket in India (BCCI)/appellants was not "State" within meaning of art. 12 of Constitution it was amenable to writ jurisdiction of Court u/art. 226 of Constitution - Further, SC set up a Committee comprising former Chief Justice as Chairman with former Judges of SC as members to determine and award punishment considered appropriate on those found guilty by Committee - Committee heard individuals and Franchisees found guilty and awarded punishments - Committee by a separate report has recommended several steps and measures that would streamline working of BCCI and possibly prevent any aberrations or controversies in which it has been embroiled in the past - Whether the recommendations of Committee have tendency to violate art. 19(1)(c) of Constitution or any other provisions thereof.

Held, right guaranteed u/art. 19(1)(c) of Constitution cannot be claimed by an association or union or a co-operative Society as is sought to be done in the instant case, even when the right to form an association or union or cooperative society extends to the continued existence of such association or union or cooperative society with its original voluntary composition. Further, initial voluntary composition of respondent/Associations who are complaining of breach of their right u/art. 19(1)(c) of Constitution remains unaffected there is no violation of what is guaranteed by u/art. 19(1)(c) of Constitution. The recommendations made by Committee in instant case do not interfere with or alter composition of State Associations. Individual citizens who came together to form State Associations have not been asked to discontinue their association nor do recommendations impose upon their members an obligation to associate with others with whom they do not wish to associate. Composition of the State Cricket Associations remain unaffected, and so does right of those forming such Associations u/art. 19(1)(c) of Constitution. Thus, no difficulty in rejecting contention opposing recommendation of Committee that BCCI shall have Cricket Association from each State as a full member. Order accordingly.

(B) Administrative - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts.12, 14, 19, 19(1)(c), 19(1)(d)(5), 19(1)(g)(6), 226, 32 - Indian Council of World Affairs Ordinance, 2001 - National Sports Development Code of India, 2011 - Right to Information Act, 2005 - One State One vote - Legality - Whether "One State One vote" recommended by Committee suffers from any legal or other infirmity sufficient for SC to reject the same.

Held, this was a measure which has been recommended with a view to structurally streamlining BCCI to make it more responsive and accountable having regard to the aspiration of different regions for an equal opportunity to participate in the growth and promotion of game in the country. The conversion notwithstanding shall continue to be associated with growth and promotion of game, right to vote remaining confined to full members, representing definite geographical regions or territories. The recommendation made by Committee regarding conversion of status of clubs and associations were sound and was accepted. Needless to say that right of association to field teams as before will remain unaffected subject to any changes that BCCI may make in its wisdom over a period of time. BCCI shall decide order in which membership will rotate among three associations in those two states. Therefore, arrangement of rotational membership shall continue till such time clubs/associations come together to form a single entity, if such a unification was to ever become a reality. There is nothing irrational about view taken by Committee and contention that individuals should be eligible to hold two posts one each in State Association and BCCI does not stand scrutiny in the light of reasons given by Committee which do not suffer from any perversity to call for SC interference. Order accordingly.

(C) Administrative - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts.12, 14, 19, 19(1)(c), 19(1)(d)(5), 19(1)(g)(6), 226, 32 - National Sports Development Code of India, 2011 - Tamil Nadu Societies Registration Act, 1975 - Recommendation for cap - Justifiability - Whether contention that recommendations have no rationale or that same are contrary to the provisions of the 1975 Act was valid.

Held, recommendation before Committee for a cap on number of terms for which an officer bearer can serve and optimum period for which one can be a member of apex council. Further, under present dispensation office bearers could continue for any number of terms. It was also noticed that no grounds for disqualifying an office bearers were prescribed. Committee found both of these to be unacceptable and in SC's opinion, rightly so. Given the problems that often arise on account of individuals holding office for any number of consecutive terms, Committee was justified in recommending length of a term in office. A 3 year term recommended by Committee was reasonable. So also, the prescription of cooling off period between two terms cannot be faulted. Grounds for disqualification like unsoundness of mind, member becoming a minister or holding a membership in any sporting body also meet requirement of reasonableness and do not call for interference from Court. Therefore, contention that recommendations have no rationale or that same are contrary to the provisions of the 1975 Act deserve notice was rejected. Order accordingly.

(D) Administrative - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts.12, 14, 19, 19(1)(c), 19(1)(d)(5), 19(1)(g)(6), 226, 32 - Indian Council of World Affairs Ordinance, 2001 - National Sports Development Code of India, 2011 - Right to Information Act, 2005 - Committees of BCCI - Overhaul - Sustainability - Whether recommendation that overhaul of existing Committees of BCCI on ground that they do not have clearly defined terms of reference was valid.

Held, Committee has on the basis of a thorough consideration and deliberation with all concerned recommended that BCCI ought to adopt an approach that would institutionalize management of its administrative affairs rather than such affairs being run on an ad-hoc basis. Committee has on that premise, recommended an administrative set up which it has evolved on the basis of its interactions with people who have necessary expertise and insight into the needs of BCCI and its associations. Therefore, no compelling reason for SC to reject recommendation made by Committee, especially when objective underlying said recommendation is not only laudable but achievable through the medium of change recommended by Committee. Order accordingly.
