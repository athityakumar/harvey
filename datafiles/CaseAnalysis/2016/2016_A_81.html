Collector of Land Acquisition and others v Andaman Timber Industries and others

Supreme Court of India

22 February 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, C. Nagappan

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 149; (2016) 4 SCC 406; AIR 2016 SC 1025; 2016 (116) ALR 475; 2016 (2) Bom.C.R. 735; 2016 (1) CLT(SC) 319; JT 2016 (3) SC 214; 2016 (2) RCR(Civil) 212; 2016 (132) RD 115; 2016(2) SCALE 478

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property

Keywords: Companies Act, 1956, Acquisition Of Land, Review, Timber, Displaced Persons (Compensation and Rehabilitation) Act, 1954, Indian Easements Act, 1882, Trees, Crops, Andaman and Nicobar Islands Land Revenue and Land Reforms Regulation, 1966, Land Acquisition Act, 1894

Summary: Land & Property - Administrative - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 17(1), 4(1), 4(2) - Andaman and Nicobar Islands Land Revenue and Land Reforms Regulation, 1966, regns. 141, 146(ii), 162, - Acquisition of land vested with Govt. - Compensation - Right of licensee - Lease deed was executed with respect to property in question in favor of respondent no.1 - Further, licensing rights were granted in favor of respondent no.1 under Regulations - Further, it is stated that land property in question leased was acquired by appellant, however compensation was computed by authority and paid for part of land - SC vide impugned order dismissed appeal filed by appellant with direction to appellants to make and publish award in respect of remaining suit land within four months from date of impugned judgment and pay compensation to respondent no.1 - Appellant contended that Land Acquisition Collector erred in not noticing fact that acquisition notifications specifically mention that respondent no.1 is licensee and not owner of land, hence, erred in determining market value of land as same is not legally permissible in law for reason that land in question belongs to Govt., on which licensing rights have been granted in respect of land in question for purpose of establishing timber industry, therefore, no interest upon said land has been created in favour of respondent No.1 - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, license has been granted u/regn. 146 (ii) of Regulations and it does not stipulate period of licensing right. In absence of stipulation of period, contention urged on behalf of respondent no.1 that it is perpetual license in respect of acquired land is tenable contention, particularly having regard to classes of tenants defined u/regn. 146 (ii) of Regulations. SC held that respondent no.1 has acquired interest in land in dispute, in terms of notifications u/ss. 4(1), 4(2) read with s. 17(1) of Act, wherein plots and parcels of land along with building structures, trees and crops have been clearly mentioned and such interest could only be extinguished in terms of u/regn. 162 (ii) of Regulations i.e. right of respondent no.1 could be extinguished only in terms of notification to acquire land which was done by authority who issued notifications to acquire land, building structures including standing trees and crops and award has been passed. Further, SC held that award on remaining 6 hectares of land was not passed by Land Acquisition Collector, even though entire land including building structures, trees and crops had been acquired for purpose of establishing industry. Not awarding compensation as provided under Act amounts to deliberate omission in discharge of statutory duty by Collector, despite statutory right vesting in respondent no.1 for award of compensation in lieu of losing holding rights over land on account of acquisition of same by Govt. Hence, respondent no.1 rightly approached HC praying that writ be issued to appellants to pass award of compensation in its favour in respect of remaining land. Saraswati Devi (D) By Lr. v Delhi Devt. Authority & Ors., 2013 Indlaw SC 52, relied on , SC where it was held that even if land absolutely vests with State, interest of private individuals can certainly be created on same by executing conveyance in their favour. Hence, SC held that said interest of respondent no.1 has rightly been recognised by Collector of Land Acquisition after issuing notifications. Hence, SC found no reason to review its earlier order passed giving directions to appellant. Review disposed of.

Ratio - If land absolutely vests with State, interest of private individuals can be created on same by executing conveyance in their favour.
