Narayanappa (Dead) by Lrs. v B. S. Ramaswamy (Dead) by Lrs. and others

Supreme Court of India

08 August 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Madan B. Lokur, R.K. Agrawal

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 570; AIR 2016 SC 3635; 2016 (5) AWC 5226; JT 2016 (8) SC 22; 2016 (6) KarLJ 1; 2016 (132) RD 630; 2016(7) SCALE 599; [2016] 3 S.C.R. 783; 2016 (4) WLN(SC) 57

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Karnataka Land Reforms Act, 1961, Occupancy Rights, Karnataka Land Reforms Rules, 1974

Summary: Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Karnataka Land Reforms Act, 1961, ss. 48A, 48A(3), 48A(6) - Karnataka Land Reforms Rules, 1974 - Occupancy right - Amendment - Sustainability - Appellant/deceased (represented by his legal representatives) claimed occupancy right by filing an application in Form 7 under the Rules and invoking provisions of s. 48A of the Act - Tribunal dismissed application and held that since appellant was not tenant in land in question but was a kathedar, question of granting occupancy rights in his favour did not arise - Appellant after cut-off date moved for an amendment in Form 7 which was allowed by Tribunal - Aggrieved, respondent filed writ petition before HC - Single Judge dismissed petition holding that Tribunal was not only entitled to permit amendment but in view of s. 48A(6) of the Act it was empowered to suo motu rectify any error in application - On appeal, DB allowed appeal - Hence, instant appeal - Whether HC was correct in holding that appellant was not entitled to claim occupancy rights in the land in question under the provisions of the Act.

Held, Tribunal having adjudicated upon application, it could only correct clerical or arithmetical errors as permitted by s.48A of the Act. The amendment sought by appellant was not in the nature of a clerical or arithmetical error. What he sought was not only a change in the survey number but also a change in the village and also a change in the area of the land for which occupancy rights were claimed. This was clearly beyond the ambit of a clerical or arithmetical error. That apart, order of Tribunal passed had attained finality since appellant did not challenge its correctness before any forum. Therefore, proposed amendment sought by appellant was not in the nature of an amendment to original application in Form 7 but a fresh claim made by him for a different parcel of land after cut-off date. Thus, appellant sought to circumvent provisions of the Act by making a fresh claim after cut-off date by styling it as an amendment to original application in Form 7. This was clearly impermissible and was an attempt to do so something in an indirect manner which could not have been done by him directly. Impugned judgment of DB was upheld. Appeal dismissed.
