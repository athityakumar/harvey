State of Gujarat and another v Shree Ratnakar Enterprise

Supreme Court of India

19 January 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Uday Umesh Lalit, V. Gopala Gowda

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 46; 2016 (332) E.L.T. 389; JT 2016 (1) SC 308; 2016(1) SCALE 386

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property

Summary: Administrative - Land & Property - Delay and Latches - Initially respondent made application before appellant-State for grant of lease of land for manufacture of salt but the same was rejected on ground that land was not available - Respondent challenged rejection in Revision and matter was sent back to Collector - However, Collector again rejected application and assailing said order, respondent filed Revision after more than five years but Revisional authority affirmed order of Collector - Later, after six years of disposal of revision, respondent filed application before HC inter-alia praying for quashing of said order of Revisional authority and for appropriate directions to allot land to respondent - Single Judge of HC rejected application of respondent but on appeal, DB of HC allowed application and directed Collector to ensure that land for salt cultivation be allotted to respondent - Hence, instant appeal by appellant-State - Whether impugned order of DB could be upheld.

Held, application having been rejected by Collector on 18-12-1999, Revision was preferred more than five years later and same was rejected on ground of delay and taken up in challenge before HC again after delay of five years. Hence, Single Judge of HC was right in observing that respondent had remained indolent in pursuing its remedy and that because of delay and latches on its part, no indulgence could be shown. DB was not justified in reversing judgment and order passed by Single Judge of HC and was not right in directing Collector to allot to respondent land for salt production from any other survey number. Hence, judgment and order of DB of HC is set-aside and that of Single Judge of HC is restored. Appeal allowed.
