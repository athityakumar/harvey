Anita International and another v Official Liquidator and others

Supreme Court of India

04 July 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Jagdish Singh Khehar, Adarsh Kumar Goel

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 483; (2016) 9 SCC 44; 2016 (118) ALR 484; 2017 (4) AWC 3535; 2017 (1) BC 560; [2016] 198 Comp Cas 194; 2016 (3) CompLJ 129; 2016 (2) DRTC 490; JT 2016 (7) SC 517; 2016 (8) MLJ 39; 2016 (133) RD 510

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate

Keywords: Liquidator, Official Liquidator, Secured Creditors, Winding Up, Jurisdiction, Liquidation, Companies Act, 1956, Appellate, Dispose, Immovable, Financial Institutions, Person Aggrieved, Subsequent, Receiver, Unsecured, Reserve, Recovery of Debts Due to Banks and Financial Institutions Act, 1993, Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, Income-tax Act, 1961

Summary: (A) Corporate - Banking & Finance - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 21 r. 58 - Companies Act, 1956, ss. 446, 446(1), 529A - Recovery of Debts Due to Banks and Financial Institutions Act, 1993, ss. 18, 19, 30, 34 - Winding up - Recovery proceedings - Challenged - Appellants/Company filed petitions for winding up before HC - HC directed respondent/official liquidator to take possession of properties - Respondent no. 1 approached before HC seeking leave to proceed with recovery proceedings before DRT - HC passed an order imposing conditions firstly, Official Liquidator would have to be impleaded by bank in recovery proceedings before DRT - Secondly, no coercive steps would be taken against assets of company during or after conclusion of proceedings before Tribunal - Recovery Officer issued a proclamation of sale under the 1993 Act and auction of appellants properties was fixed - Aggrieved, appellants filed writ petition before HC challenging recovery proceedings - Single Judge directed that sale made by Recovery Officer would be subject to final outcome of petition - On appeal, DB set aside sale of appellants properties by Recovery Officer and confirmation of sale by Recovery Officer on 12-9-2015 - Hence, instant appeals - Whether an order passed by Company Court was binding on Recovery Officer.

Held, SC relied on case in The Official Liquidator, U.P. And Uttarakhand vs Allahabad Bank And Others 2013 Indlaw SC 146. It was not open to Recovery Officer to confirm sale of properties at his own, and such a sale and confirmation of movable or immovable properties made by Recovery Officer without the permission of Company Court were liable to be set aside. Therefore, above issue had nothing to do with proposition, whether an Official Liquidator can approach a Company Court to seek setting aside of auction and sale conducted by Recovery Officer. Appeals dismissed.

(B) Corporate - Banking & Finance - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 21 r. 58 - Companies Act, 1956, ss. 446, 446(1), 529A - Recovery of Debts Due to Banks and Financial Institutions Act, 1993, ss. 18, 19, 30, 34 - Winding up petition - Jurisdiction - Whether an order passed by a Court which has no jurisdiction with reference to a controversy could be termed as a void.

Held, for recovery of a debt due to a bank or a financial institution, concerned bank or financial institution can legitimately initiate proceedings by filing a winding up petition before jurisdictional Company Court or intervene in a pending winding up petition. Since there is no bar restraining a bank or a financial institution from approaching a Company Court, by filing a winding up petition, it is not possible to conclude, that the jurisdictional Company Court is not possessed with determinative authority/competence to entertain a claim raised by such bank or financial institution. Thus, it is not possible to accept that order passed by Company Court lacked jurisdictional authority. Appeals dismissed.

(C) Corporate - Banking & Finance - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 21 r. 58 -Companies Act, 1956, ss. 446, 446(1), 529A - Recovery of Debts Due to Banks and Financial Institutions Act, 1993, ss. 18, 19, 30, 34 - Recovery proceedings - Binding effect - Validity - Whether the proceedings conducted on 11-8-2005 and confirmation thereof on 12-9-2005 was valid.

Held, sale made by Recovery Officer on 11-8-2005 and its confirmation on 12-9-2005, were in utter violation of the order dt. 10-3-2000. Therefore, concerned parties were justified in approaching HC. Impugned order of HC had a clear and binding effect on proceedings permitted to be initiated before DRT. Further, that it was equally binding on Recovery Officer. The same would also be binding on those claiming through sale proceedings conducted by Recovery Officer. Thus, impugned order dt. 10-3-2000 was binding on appellant before SC. Appeals dismissed.

Ratio - When an order passed by a Court subsists, the same is liable to be complied with till it is set aside.
