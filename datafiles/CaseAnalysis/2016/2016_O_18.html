Commissioner of Commercial Tax, U. P. v Oswal Greentech Limited

Supreme Court of India

28 October 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, Shiva Kirti Singh

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 800; AIR 2016 SC 5075; JT 2017 (1) SC 325; 2016 (8) MLJ 632; 2016(10) SCALE 432; [2016] 5 S.C.R. 851; [2017] 97 VST 399

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax

Keywords: Tribunal, Sales Tax, Trader, Stock Transfer, Rate Of Tax, Concessional Rate, Concessional Rate Of Tax, Processing, Uttar Pradesh Trade Tax Act, 1948, Fertilizer (Movement Control) Order, 1973

Summary: Indirect Tax - Uttar Pradesh Trade Tax Act, 1948, ss. 3B, 4B(6) - Purchase of raw material against concessional rate of tax - Stock transfer of Notified goods - Upon noticing that respondent made purchase of natural gas against Form III-B at concessional rate of tax, and that after manufacture of notified goods out of said natural gas, some of the finished goods were transferred outside the State, revenue issued show cause notice to respondent for A/Y 2005-06 - Later, penalty of Rs. 10,46,98,335 was imposed vide order dt.28-3-2009 - Aggrieved, respondent filed appeal but the same was dismissed by appellate authority by observing that assessee after availing the benefit at concessional rate, has violated the provisions contained in s.4B(2) of the Act and making stock transfers very often - Respondent thereupon filed second appeal before Tribunal and basing upon opinion of nominated Judicial Member, it was observed that no action could be taken u/s.3B of the Act on ground that products utilizing the natural gas purchased against issue of Form III-B were sent through stock transfer without selling those directly, because s.4B of the Act could not be extended to determine the responsibility u/s.3B - Further it was held that s.4B has nothing to do with the fact as to how the notified goods are to be disposed of because the provision of s.3B is not applicable in case the raw material is used for production of the notified goods mentioned in the recognition certificate - With such observations, Tribunal allowed appeal and penalty imposed was annulled - Assailing orders of Tribunal, revenue filed revision before HC but the same was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether impugned orders of Tribunal could be upheld.

Held, s.3B is to be read harmoniously with s.4B(6) of the Act and s.3B is applicable when a false and wrong certificate or declaration is made but s.4B(6) deals with cases were the dealer is unable to comply with the intendment i.e., for some reason unable to sell the goods within the State, export them or sell them in the course of inter-State trade or commerce. Further, s.4B(6) specifically deals with cases where the dealer manufactures or packs the notified goods and has taken benefit of lower/concessional or nil rate of tax on the raw material but is unable to fulfill the intendment, i.e., he has not been able to sell the notified goods by way of sale within the State or in course of inter-State state or commerce or by way of export. In such cases, the dealer is liable to pay the amount of difference on the amount of sale or purchase of such goods on which concession or nil rate of tax was paid on account of issue of the requirement certificate and the amount of tax calculated @ 4%. Further, s.4B(6) is a particular and a specific section which deals with and specifies the consequences when the dealer is unable to meet and comply with intendment and thus, same is applicable to instant case. Hence, view expressed by Tribunal and as concurred by HC could not be interfered with. Appeal dismissed.
