Greater Noida Industrial Development Authority v Savitri Mohan and others

Supreme Court of India

29 June 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Adarsh Kumar Goel, Anil R. Dave

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 458; AIR 2016 SC 4823; 2017 (2) ALD(SC) 2; 2016 (117) ALR 868; 2016 (5) AWC 4415; 2016 (3) CLT(SC) 199; 2016 (3) RCR(Civil) 873; 2016 (133) RD 147; 2016(6) SCALE 699; [2016] 3 S.C.R. 187; 2016 (4) WLN(SC) 186

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property

Keywords: Urgency, Restoration, Additional Compensation, Land Acquisition Act, 1894

Summary: Land & Property - Administrative - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 17(4), 4(1) - Land acquisition - Additional compensation - Quash - Appellants issued notification to acquire lands of respondents - Award was declared but respondents did not receive compensation - Respondents filed writ petition before HC on ground that urgency clause could not be invoked so as to deprive land owners of their right to file objections - DB allowed petitions holding that invocation of urgency was not justified - Hence, instant appeal - Whether respondents would be entitled to relief of higher compensation and allotment of land instead of quashing of acquisition proceedings.

Held, consideration for not granting relief of quashing acquisition is overall development on substantial part of acquired land. Filing of prompt petitions by an individual is not the only consideration for grant of relief of quashing acquisition when almost entire land has already been developed. SC quashed acquisition only where substantial part of land had not been developed. The category of judgment where acquisition has not been quashed covers the entire village where land of respondents was located. Thus, respondents were entitled to be treated at par with other similarly placed persons. Respondents were entitled to relief as per judgment of SC in 2015 Indlaw SC 520. Therefore, allotment of 10% of acquired land to concerned land owners is subject to maximum of 2500 sq. meters. Impugned judgment of HC was set aside. Appeal allowed.
