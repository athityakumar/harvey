Prem Nath Khanna and others v Narinder Nath Kapoor (Dead), Through Lrs. and others

Supreme Court of India

01 March 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, Uday Umesh Lalit

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 406; 2016 (2) CLT(SC) 195; JT 2016 (3) SC 223; 2016 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1778; 2016 (2) RCR(Civil) 273; 2016(3) SCALE 76

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Adverse Possession, Benami Transactions (Prohibition) Act, 1988, Code of Civil Procedure, 1908

Summary: Land & Property - Benami Transactions (Prohibition) Act, 1988 - Mutation in land records - Adverse possession - Appellants purchased suit lands vide registered sale deeds dt.17-10-1996 and 7-1-1967 and prior to execution of said sale deeds, land was leased to respondent no.1 by previous owners of land - Later, aggrieved by ex-parte decree passed by Trial Court in suit filed by respondent no.1, appellants filed suit before Trial Court for declaration of title and joint possession over the suit property as well as declaration to the effect that the judgment and order passed in suit filed by respondent no.1 is not binding upon them as defendants in said suit were no more owners of suit land as the same was purchased by them vide registered sale deeds dt.17-10-1966 and 7-1-1967 - However, Trial Court dismissed suit inter alia holding that respondent no.1 perfected title over suit land by prescription and adverse possession - However, on appeal, First Appellate Court held that respondent no.1 could not be held to have acquired legal right to claim ownership over suit property by adverse possession as mere mutation entry in his name in record does not create or confer title in immovable property - Aggrieved, respondent no.1 filed appeal before HC and vide impugned judgment and order, HC allowed appeal and set aside judgment and order of First Appellate Court - Hence, instant appeal - Whether impugned judgment and order of HC is sustainable.

Held, HC should have noticed that appellants are the owners of suit land by way of registered sale deed. The non-application of mind on part of HC on said vital aspect of the case is erroneous in law as it is not based on correct appreciation of facts and evidence on record. Further, respondent no.1 has no right to claim ownership over the suit property on ground of adverse possession by taking plea of sham transaction as the same is not only prohibited by the Act but makes the appellants absolute owner. Hence, impugned judgment and order of HC is erroneous of law and suffers from infirmity and so, the same is set aside and judgment of First Appellate Court is restored. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Mutation of a property in revenue record does not create or extinguish title nor has it any presumptive value on title.
