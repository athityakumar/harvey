Alagaapuram R. Mohanraj and others v Tamil Nadu Legislative Assembly, Represented by its Secretary and another

Supreme Court of India

12 February 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

J. Chelameswar, A.M. Sapre

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 154; (2016) 6 SCC 82; (2016) 6 SCC 105; AIR 2016 SC 867; 2016 (2) ALD(SC) 192; 2016 (3) AWC 2911; 2016 (2) CTC 450; JT 2016 (2) SC 605; 2016 (2) MLJ 148; 2016 (2) RCR(Civil) 87; 2016(2) SCALE 340; 2016 (1) WLN(SC) 146

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Parliament & Legislature

Keywords: Legislative Assembly, Tamil Nadu Payment of Salaries Act, 1951, Constitution of India, 1950, Tamil Nadu Assembly Rules

Summary: Parliament & Legislature - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 105, 14, 19, 19(1)(2), 19(1)(a), 19(1)(g), 194, 21 - Suspension of members of Legislative Assembly - Alleged violation of fundamental rights - Justification - On 19-2-2015, petitioners allegedly resorted to unruly conduct while parliament session was in progress and when Speaker directed Marshalls to evict petitioner no.1 from House because of alleged unruly conduct, remaining petitioners ran to Speaker's podium allegedly to attack Speaker - Further, Speaker passed order suspending 19 members of Legislative Assembly belonging to different Constituencies - Privileges Committee, after inquiry, recorded conclusion that conduct of six petitioners was in breach of privileges of House, hence, recommended to House that six petitioners be removed from Assembly for 10 days from commencement of next session of Legislative Assembly and also that during said period, petitioners be not paid salary and be given other benefits to which members of House are entitled - Petitioners contended that their fundamental rights guaranteed u/arts. 19(1)(a), 19(1)(g), 14 and 21 of Constitution have been violated by impugned resolution - Hence, instant petition.

Held, with respect to violation of fundamental rights u/art. 19(1)(a) to 19(g) of Constitution, SC held that provisions of art. 194(2) of Constitution indicated that freedom of speech referred to in sub-article (1) thereof was different from freedom of speech and expression guaranteed u/art. 19(1)(a) of Constitution and could not be cut down in any way by any law contemplated by art. 19(2) of Constitution. SC held that scope and amplitude of freedom of speech inhering in citizen and available to member of legislative body are totally different. No citizen has right to enter legislative body and exercise his freedom of speech unless he first gets elected to such legislative body in accordance with law. No legislator would continue to enjoy freedom of speech contemplated u/arts. 105 and 194 of Constitution after cessation of membership of legislative body. Further, SC held that though there is curtailment of petitioners' right of free speech in Legislative Assembly of Tamil Nadu to which they are entitled u/art. 194 of Constitution by virtue of impugned order, said impugned order does not, in context, violate fundamental rights of petitioners guaranteed u/art. 19(1)(a) of Constitution. Further, SC held that right to contest election to legislative bodies established by Constitution is held not to be fundamental right. Hence, it would be difficult to accept that right to participate in proceedings of legislative bodies can be fundamental right falling u/art. 19(1)(g) of Constitution. Further, SC held that economic footings of 'occupation' u/art. 19(1)(g) of Constitution and transient and incidental nature of economic benefits flowing from office of legislator lead to conclusion that member of legislative assembly cannot be treated as pursuing 'occupation' u/art. 19(1)(g) of Constitution, hence, issue at hand does not involves rights of petitioners u/art. 19(1)(g) of Constitution. Further, with respect to violation of fundamental right u/art. 21 of Constitution as petitioners are deprived of their salaries and other benefits incidental to membership of legislative assembly during period of suspension, SC held that no clear authority is cited nor any tenable submission is made to demonstrate that there is violation of art. 21 of Constitution except bare assertion. Further, salary and other benefits to which members of legislative body are entitled to during their tenure are purely incidental to membership and they don't even create independent and indefeasible Constitutional right. Hence, question that deprivation of such benefits amounted to deprivation of fundamental right u/art. 21 of Constitution does not arise. Further, with respect to violation of art. 14 of Constitution on grounds that there was violation of principles of natural justice, SC held that there was considerable correspondence between Privileges Committee and petitioners. SC held that perusal of minutes of privileges committee meeting dt. 20-2-2015, it is evident that viewing of video recording of incident dt. 19-2-2015 formed basis for application of mind by members of privileges committee. Further, incident happened inside assembly chamber, speaker and other present members were eye-witnesses to incident, and that nature of incident was known to all members in house, including members of privileges committee. However, SC held that principles of natural justice require that petitioners ought to have been granted opportunity to see video recording and Privileges Committee should have necessarily offered this opportunity, in order to make process adopted by it compliant with requirements of art. 14 of Constitution. SC held that failure to supply copy of video recording or affording opportunity to petitioners to view video recording relied upon by committee clearly resulted in violation of principles of natural justice i.e. denial of reasonable opportunity to meet case. Hence, impugned resolution dt. 31-3-2015 passed in Legislative Assembly is set aside. Further, salary and other benefits incidental to membership of assembly stand restored to six petitioners. Petition allowed.

Ratio - Order passed against party is set aside if authority has failed to grant reasonable opportunity accused person to be heard as there is violation of fundamental rights u/art. 14 of Constitution i.e. violation of principles natural justice
