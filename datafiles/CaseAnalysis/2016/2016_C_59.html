Ludovico Sagrado Goveia v Cirila Rosa Maria Pinto and others

Supreme Court of India

06 September 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

R.F. Nariman, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 639; (2016) 9 SCC 615; AIR 2016 SC 4248; 2016 (6) ALD(SC) 78; 2017(1) ALL MR 457; 2016 (118) ALR 864; 2016 (5) AWC 5124; 2016 (4) CLT(SC) 50; JT 2016 (9) SC 26; 2017 (1) MahLJ 608; 2017 (1) MPLJ 271; 2016 (2) OLR 1040; 2016 (4) RCR(Civil) 369; 2016(8) SCALE 549

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Auction, Principal Debtor, Liquidator, Indian Companies Act, 1913, Registration, Agent, Arbitration And Conciliation Act, 1996, TRUSTS & ASSOCIATIONS, Settlement, Arbitration, Surety, Past Member, Multi-State Co-Operative Societies Act, 2002, Multi-State Cooperative Societies Act, 1984, Multi-State Co-operative Societies Rules, 2002, Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, Income-tax Act, 1961, Madhya Pradesh Suhayata Upkaram (Vishesh Upbandh) Adhiniyam, 1978, Multi State Co-operative Society Rules, 1985

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996 - Madhya Pradesh Suhayata Upkaram (Vishesh Upbandh) Adhiniyam, 1978, s. 5 - Multi-State Co-operative Societies Act, 2002, ss. 126(6), 84, 94 - Multi-State Cooperative Societies Act, 1984, ss. 74, 76, 85, 85(c) - Multi State Co-operative Society Rules, 1985, r. 22 - Multi-State Co-operative Societies Rules, 2002, rr. 37(13), 37(14) - Loan payment default - Arbitral award - Quash - Respondent no. 3/borrower/defaulter obtained a loan of Rs. 40 lacs from Bank for the purpose of construction of a bungalow and a deed of mortgage was executed - Respondent no. 3 failed to repay the loan installments - Recovery proceedings were initiated u/ss. 74 and 76 of the 1984 Act by Bank against respondent no. 3 and two sureties - Assistant Registrar of bank passed an ex-parte award holding opponents liable to pay the loan dues amounting to Rs 55,04,583/- with interest at 21% - Subsequently, a demand notice was issued by Bank u/r. 22 of the 1985 Rules against defaulters for principal amount of Rs. 60,59,646/- with interest at 19% p.a.- Defaulters failed to pay amount towards bank dues and bank referred the said award for execution to Recovery Officer - Recovery Officer issued a tender notice dt. 18-3-2007 calling for sealed tenders - Appellant was found to be the highest and paid the entire bid amount of Rs. 86 lacs, a Sale Certificate in favour of appellant was issued on 23-4-2007- Respondent no. 3 filed a writ petition before HC seeking to quash both award and certificate of sale accorded in favour of appellant - HC allowed writ petition and set aside award proceedings holding that the 1984 Act was repealed by the 2002 Act which came into force on 19-8-2002 and award would be liable to be executed only in the manner provided by the 1996 Act - Hence, instant appeal - Whether proceedings initiated under the old Act could continue under the said Act.

Held, the scheme of the 2002 Act which replaces the 1984 Act is a little different. S. 84 of the 2002 Act corresponds to ss. 74 and 76 of the 1984 Act. With this difference that disputes that have been referred to arbitration are now to be settled or decided by Arbitrator to be appointed by Central Registrar and the provisions, therefore, of the 1996 Act shall apply to such arbitration as if the proceedings for arbitration were referred for settlement or decision under the provisions of the 1996 Act. Thus it can be seen that s. 84 (4) and (5) of the 1984 Act provide for a different scheme. Equally, s. 94 of the 1984 Act which provides for execution of certain decisions and orders made under the 2002 Act, mentions various Sections, but s. 84 of the 1984 Act is conspicuous by its absence. This is obviously for the reason that the entire proceedings have now to be conducted under the 1996 Act, including execution of the arbitration Award made under the said Act. The proceeding in execution initiated u/s. 85(c) of the 1984 Act and pending before the authorities under the 1984 Act prior to 19-08-2002, would continue unhindered by the repeal of the 1984 Act by the 2002 Act. SC found that at no stage were respondents were ready to pay back the entire money borrowed by them as far back as in 1997. No relief can be given in writ petition so as to circumvent the statutory provisions contained in r. 37(13) and (14) of the 2002 Rules. A charge of malafides has to be made out with great clarity and particularity. Therefore, it is clear that judgment under appeal is incorrect and would have to be set aside. Order accordingly.
