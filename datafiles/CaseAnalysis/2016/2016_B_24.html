Bakshi Security and Personnel Services Private Limited v Devkishan Computed Private Limited and others

Supreme Court of India

26 July 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

R.F. Nariman, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 549; (2016) 8 SCC 446; AIR 2016 SC 3585; 2016 (6) AWC 6512; JT 2016 (7) SC 342; 2016(7) SCALE 425; [2016] 3 S.C.R. 310

Case Digest

Subject: Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Contract, Constitution of India, 1950

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Tender invitation - Minimum wage figure - Validity - Commissioner of Transport invited tender seeking bids for services at RTO check-posts - Appellant bid for a total amount of Rs.2,92,93,944/- and respondent no.1 bid for Rs.2,77,68,000/- - Technical Evaluation Committee arrived at a minimum wage figure of Rs.3,00,92,346/- - Both appellant and respondent no.1 were held to be ineligible and tender was awarded to third bidder - Respondent no.1 filed writ petition before HC - HC allowed petition directing Commissioner to give an opportunity to all three tenderers to resubmit their bids after being appraised of minimum wage figure given by Labour Dept. - Aggrieved, respondent no.1 filed second writ petition before HC which was allowed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether the process adopted or decision made by authority is mala fide or intended to favour someone.

Held, HC was not correct in treating respondent no.1's offer as matching with revised minimum wage calculation, as that would make a new contract between parties that parties have not made themselves. Further, present tender has not gotten off the ground since May 2015, and one year's precious time has been wasted due to litigation between parties. State Govt. was partly to blame for this inasmuch as it arrived at a minimum wage figure and did not disclose the same to tendering parties twice. Even in second round of litigation, Govt. did not disclose newly arrived at minimum wage figure of Rs.2,91,00,000/- to two persons in fray before SC. Therefore, Govt. should disclose second figure of minimum wage and restart the tendering process. Respondent no.1 has violated strict terms of tender condition on every occasion and hence cannot be given relief. Secondly, SC found that due to litigation, present tender has not taken off for over one year. In absence of malafides, and judgment of HC has found that malafides did not vitiate calculation of minimum wage by Labour Dept., SC cannot accept submission that figure of Rs.2,91,00,000/- was tailor made to suit the bid offered by appellant. Thus, impugned orders of HC was set aside and allow Govt. to proceed further in finalizing tender in favour of appellant. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - When a decision is taken by appropriate authority on due consideration of tender document and if it is found that successful bidders complied with purport and object for which essential conditions were laid down, the same may not be interfered by Courts.
