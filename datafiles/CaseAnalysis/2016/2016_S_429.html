Vivek Singh v State of Uttar Pradesh and another

Supreme Court of India

26 August 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Sharad Arvind Bobde, Ashok Bhushan

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 617; (2016) 9 SCC 295; AIR 2016 SC 4081; 2016 (6) AWC 6289; 2016 (151) FLR 51; JT 2016 (8) SC 391; 2017 (2) MahLJ 41; 2017 (1) MPLJ 546; [2016] 4 S.C.R. 276; 2016 (3) UPLBEC 2301

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Vacancy, Selection, Vacancies, Seniority, Posts, Recruitment, Public Service Commission, Advertisement, Quota, Persons With Disabilities (Equal Opportunities, Protection Of Rights And Full Participation) Act, 1995, Reservation, Writ Petition, Post, Appointment

Summary: Service - Persons With Disabilities (Equal Opportunities, Protection of Rights and Full Participation) Act, 1995 - Appointment - Reservation - Retrospective effect - On 29-01-2001, State Public Service Commission (Commission) invited applications for filling up 800 posts - Appellant applied for selection under physically handicapped category - Commission recommended appellant's appointment as a Trade Tax Officer under the quota reserved for physically handicapped candidates - Appellant filed a writ petition before HC seeking for appointment on post of Dpty. Collector - However, pending writ petition, appellant joined as a Trade Tax Officer in November, 2004 - HC passed an interim order directing State Govt. to not fill up any vacancy unless a reservation of 3% was provided to physically handicapped candidates from initial stage - Appellant in pending writ petition modified his prayer and claimed that since 1 out of the 5 posts of Dpty. Collector was to be filled up under the special recruitment drive for physically handicapped category, included the post for the year 2001-2002 in which appellant appeared and was selected, he is entitled to be selected - HC declined relief to appellant on ground that many recruitments have taken place between 2001-2002 till the date of decision of HC and if appellant is given appointment with retrospective effect from 2001-2002, the entire seniority position of recruitment of that year as well as the subsequent years will get disturbed - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, since the exercise was undertaken in pursuance of the Order of HC and the year 1997-1998 was taken as the starting point since that is the first year after the Act came into force on 01-01-1996. The reservation which must be provided for, as a matter of law has been duly provided by State which has in fact determined the roster point which was calculated for the number of posts that ought to have been reserved from the year 1997-1998 to 2009-2010 and have accordingly made appointments. It is another matter that appellant has not been appointed thereto. In any case, SC agree with the observation of HC that a direction to accommodate appellant in the selection year 2001-2002 would create difficulties in the seniority of those who have been appointed every year since then, as observed earlier some of Deputy Collectors who have been appointed may have got promoted. Therefore, in any case the appellant could not have been considered for the 9 vacancies which arose in the year 2000-2001. Appeal dismissed.

Legislation Cited

Persons With Disabilities (Equal Opportunities, Protection of Rights and Full Participation) Act, 1995
