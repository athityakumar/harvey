Joint Secretary, Political Department, Government of Meghalaya, Main Secretariat, Shillong v High Court of Meghalaya, Through Its Registrar, Shillong

Supreme Court of India

18 March 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, Shiva Kirti Singh

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 227; AIR 2016 SC 1467; 2016 (3) AWC 2209; JT 2016 (3) SC 203; 2016 (3) MLJ 42; 2016(3) SCALE 351; [2016] 2 S.C.R. 376

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Advocates & Judges

Keywords: Prevention of Food Adulteration Rules, 1955, Lokayukta, Constitution of India, 1950, Lokpal and Lokayuktas Act, 2013, Meghalaya Lokayukta Act, 2014

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Administrative - Lokpal and Lokayuktas Act, 2013 - Meghalaya Lokayukta Act, 2014 - Suo motu powers of HC - Appointments to Lokayukta - Instant appeal challenging orders of HC on issue of appointments to Lokayukta contending that there had been no assail to Constitutional validity of provisions and so, HC could not have suo motu taken up the same, especially when language employed is also similar to 2013 Act passed by Parliament - Whether HC could proceed suo motu to deal with appointment of Lokayukta.

Held, HC compared provisions pertaining to appointment of Chairperson and Members under 2014 Act with the provisions of other Acts enacted by different legislatures. There was no challenge to Constitutional validity of the provisions of the Act and suo motu petition was registered for giving effect to 2014 Act by bringing institutions into existence. However, Court could not raise the issue relating to any particular provisions and seek explanation in exercise of jurisdiction u/art.226 of the Constitution. DB of HC with an erroneous understanding of fundamental principle of law, scanned anatomy of provision and passed order in relation to it as if it is obnoxious or falls foul of any Constitutional provision and the same is impermissible. A person aggrieved or with expanded concept of locus standi some one could have assailed provisions though in such event there are certain requirements and need for certain compliances. Hence, HC could not have proceeded as if it was testing validity of provision and granted stay and the approach is totally fallacious. Hence, part of order dealing with provisions of 2014 Act is set aside and directions pertaining to stay of the provisions of 2014 Act are set aside and writ petition initiated by HC is disposed of. Appeal partly allowed.

Ratio - Suo motu public interest litigation could be initiated to ameliorate conditions of a class of persons whose Constitutional or otherwise lawful rights are affected or not adequately looked into.
