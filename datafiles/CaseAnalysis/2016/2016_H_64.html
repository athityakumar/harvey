Deputy Commissioner of Commercial Taxes (Vigilance) v Hindustan Lever Limited

Supreme Court of India

30 June 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, N.V. Ramana

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 500; AIR 2016 SC 3085; 2016 (5) MLJ 396; [2016] 5 S.C.R. 635; [2016] 93 VST 452

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Keywords: Finding of fact, Tribunal, Collection Of Tax, Levy, Appellate Authority, Companies Act, 1956, Central Sales Tax Act, 1956, Registered Dealer, Tax Payable, Sale Price, Exemption Notification, Standards Of Weights And Measures Act, 1976, Standards of Weights and Measures (Packaged Commodities) Rules, 1977, Rate, Requirement, Karnataka Sales Tax Act, 1957, Collected, Tamil Nadu General Sales Tax Act, 1959, Constitution of India, 1950, Madhya Pradesh General Sales-tax Act, 1958

Summary: Sales Tax - Indirect Tax - Component of tax included in sale price - Premises belonging to respondent-assessee were visited by Assistant Commissioner of Commercial Taxes (Intelligence) and later, concluded that respondent-assessee was not entitled to benefit of exemption, as Explanation III(e) to the Notification dt.19-6-1991 was violated - On basis of said findings, assessment orders were passed and claim of exemption on turnovers of Dharwad tea based on notifications was rejected - Assessment orders were assailed before appellate authority but appellant authority upheld view of assessing authority by rejecting claim of exemption advanced by respondent-assessee on ground that there was collection of tax by considering tax component in determination of sale price, though the same was not distinctly shown as tax and collected as such - On appeal, Tribunal held that though respondent-assessee had considered local tax element in price fixed, but it could not be stated that they had collected local taxes from consumers and as such, accepted contentions raised by assessee - Aggrieved, said orders of Tribunal were challenged before HC in revision petition but HC concurred with opinion expressed by Tribunal - Hence, instant appeal - Whether impugned orders of HC could be upheld.

Held, an assessee is entitled to carry on and conduct business, fix the maximum retail price of its products. In the instant case in spite of the multiple units both exempted and non-exempted, the respondent had adopted and followed uniform market price throughout India. The respondent is entitled and can fix a uniform price meant for whole of India. The uniform market price does not differ in spite of differences in sales-tax payable at the end point, i.e., at the point of sale. This is a matter of business policy and cannot be taken exception to. Uniform pricing cannot be a ground to hold that the respondent was charging sales tax on a sale price of the goods manufactured in the exempt unit. Cost of production in different units of the respondent assessee can vary. Cost of production has various components and is computed with reference to revenue expenditure, rate of return on the capital expenditure, etc. These are complex commercial and business considerations which cannot be decided with reference to a single factor, i.e., the uniform market retail price. A market retail price stating that it is inclusive of all taxes could be the starting point, but would not prove and establish that the sales-tax has been collected. Further, when the respondent was not liable to pay tax and had not passed on the tax liability, then, sale consideration received should not be bifurcated and divided on the basis of any assumption that the sale price received could have included the tax. There is neither such principle nor any precept in law. Hence, appeal is dismissed with costs of Rs. 1,00,000. Appeal dismissed.
