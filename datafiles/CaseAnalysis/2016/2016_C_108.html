State of Jharkhand and others v Cwe-soma Consortium

Supreme Court of India

12 July 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

R. Banumathi, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 495; AIR 2016 SC 3366; 2016 (5) AWC 4614; 2016 (4) BC 464; JT 2016 (7) SC 508; 2016(6) SCALE 743; [2016] 4 S.C.R. 157

Case Digest

Subject: Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Arbitrariness, CONSTITUTION, WATER, Constitution of India, 1950, Right to Fair Compensation and Transparency in Land Acquisition, Rehabilitation and Resettlement Act, 2013

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Right to Fair Compensation and Transparency in Land Acquisition, Rehabilitation and Resettlement Act, 2013 - Tender invitation - Cancellation - Challenged - Appellants/State issued a Notice Inviting Tender (NIT) for construction of Dam as per Standard Bidding Documents (SBD) - Respondent was found responsive and other two bidders were found unresponsive - Tender committee took a decision to cancel tender and go for retender to make tender process more competitive - Tender committee re-affirmed this decision in a meeting - Aggrieved respondent filed a writ petition before HC - Single Judge allowed petition holding action of appellants as arbitrary and against public interest - On appeal, DB dismissed appeal holding that re-tendering at later stage would further enhance estimated value causing excessive loss to state exchequer which may not be in public interest - Hence, instant appeal - Whether there is any infirmity in decision making process of HC.

Held, when authority took a decision to cancel tender due to lack of adequate competition and in order to make it more competitive to invite fresh tenders, it cannot be said that there is any mala fide or want of bona fide in such decision. Right to refuse lowest or any other tender is always available to govt. Respondent had neither pleaded nor established mala fide exercise of power by appellant. Further, decision of tender committee ought not to have been interfered with by HC. Therefore, HC erred in sitting in appeal over decision of appellant to cancel tender and float a fresh tender. Equally, HC was not right in going into financial implication of a fresh tender. Appellants were directed to invite fresh tenders and process the same, but no allotment shall be made without permission of SC. Impugned judgment of HC is set aside. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - While exercising judicial review in govt. contracts Court should see if there is any infirmity in decision-making process or is vitiated by mala fide, unreasonableness or arbitrariness.
