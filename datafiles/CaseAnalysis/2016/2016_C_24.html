Mohd. Jalees Ansari and others v Central Bureau of Investigation

Supreme Court of India

11 May 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Uday Umesh Lalit, Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 423; AIR 2016 SC 2461; 2016 CRLJ 2879; 2016 (4) MLJ(Crl) 18; 2016 (3) RCR(Criminal) 181; 2016(5) SCALE 326; [2016] 8 S.C.R. 247

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Life Imprisonment, Commission Of An Offence, Conspiracy, FIR, CBI, Jurisdiction, Books, Production, Value, Passport, Signature, Confessional Statement, Expert, Endorsement, Handwriting Expert, Search, Commissioner Of Police, Entries, Post Office, Seizure Memo, Corroboration, Police Custody, Diary, Signatures, Contents, Cognizance, Innocent, Interrogation, Torture, Oath, Infirmity, Handwriting, Fax, Pistol, Arms Act, 1959, Admissible, Deposition, Terrorist And Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Act, 1987, Passenger, Bombs, Explosion, Revenge, TADA Rules, Rigorous Imprisonment, Registered, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, Investigating Officer, Seizure, Association

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 120B - Terrorist and Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Act, 1987 - Terrorist and Disruptive Activities Rules, r. 15 - Invocation of provisions of the Act - Terrorist activities - Serial bomb blasts occurred during night intervening 5-12-1993 and 6-12-1993 in various trains of the country and after investigation, originally 16 accused persons were tried by Designated Court for having committed offences under the Act and other enactments - Upon perusal of evidence, Designated Court found prior approval u/s.20A(1) of the Act and orders of sanction u/s. 20A(2) to be valid and in accordance with law and in view of confessional statement and other prosecution evidence, it found that it was proved beyond reasonable doubt that accused nos.1 to 5 and 7 to 16, had conspired and caused terrorist acts by causing bomb blasts in six trains on 5-12-1993 and 6-12-1993 and found accused nos. 1 to 5, 7 to 14 and 16, guilty of offences punishable u/s. 3(3) of the Act while accused no.15 was found guilty of the offence punishable u/s. 3(3) of the Act read with s. 120-B of IPC and accordingly convicted and sentenced appellants - Hence, instant appeal on grounds inter alia that there is no valid invocation of provisions of the Act as required u/s. 20A(1) of the Act - Whether impugned judgment of Designated Court could be upheld.

Held, in case of bomb blast occurred at Moula Ali Railway Station in A.P.Express, SHO sought permission of SP to register case under the Act and SP dictated proceedings invoking provisions of the Act. The registration of FIR itself under the provisions of the Act was thus quite prompt. Further, contemporaneous documentation clearly showed invocation of provisions of the Act right at the inception. Hence, registration of crime under the provisions of the Act in Hyderabad Crime is valid and proper and there was correct exercise of power. Further, in respect of other bomb blasts, endorsement at foot of report in handwriting of PW62 which document was exhibited at Ext.P246 is valid exercise of power invoking the provisions of the Act. Further, there is no inconsistency or infirmity in invocation of provisions of the Act and as such invocation vide Ext.P.No.s 450, 246, 247 and 160 are correct. Hence, prosecution established case against accused nos. 1 to 5, 7, 11, 14 to 16 and so, said accused are guilty of offences with which they were charged and so, conviction and sentence awarded by Designated Court to said accused persons is upheld. However, conviction and sentence recorded against accused nos.8 to 10, 13 are set aside. Appeal disposed of.
