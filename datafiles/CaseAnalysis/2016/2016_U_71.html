Pooja Pal v Union of India and others

Supreme Court of India

22 January 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Amitava Roy, V. Gopala Gowda

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 64; (2016) 3 SCC 135; AIR 2016 SC 1345; JT 2016 (1) SC 430; 2016 (2) MLJ(Crl) 37; 2016 (1) RCR(Criminal) 880; 2016 (3) RLW 2166; 2016(1) SCALE 534B

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Fundamental Right, Conspiracy, Fair Trial, Right To Life, Jurisprudence, CBI, Vehicle, Deficiency, Assembly, Incomplete, CONSTITUTION, Secretary, Criminal Trial, Denial, Further Investigation, Speedy Trial, Fear, Credibility, Assignment, Explosive Substances Act, 1908, Restoration, National Security Act, 1980, Pendency, Intervention, Judiciary, Delhi Special Police Establishment Act, 1946, Entrustment, Threats, Uttar Pradesh Gangsters and Anti-Social Activities (Prevention) Act, 1986

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 311, 391 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 165 - Investigation by CBI - Deceased, who was sitting MLA on day of incident, was travelling in car along with others when he was intercepted and surrounded by eight persons, and was shot and in course of shoot-out, deceased along with two others were seriously injured and later succumbed to injuries - Instant appeal by appellant seeking investigation by Central Bureau of Investigation (CBI) into incident of murderous attack on deceased, which HC declined to grant by impugned judgment - Whether CBI could be entrusted with investigation into attack and murder of deceased.

Held, notwithstanding pendency of the trial, and the availability of the power of the lower courts u/ss.311 and 391 of CrPC read with s. 165 of the Act, it is of overwhelming and imperative necessity so as to rule out any possibility of denial of justice to the parties and more importantly to instill and sustain the confidence of the community at large, for CBI to undertake a de novo investigation in the incident. Hence, CBI is entrusted with task of undertaking a de novo investigation in the incident of murder of deceased. Further, only on receipt of report by CBI, Trial Court could proceed in accordance with law and conduct and conclude trial. Appeal allowed.
