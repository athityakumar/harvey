Anil Hoble v Kashinath Jairam Shetye and others

Supreme Court of India

07 October 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Khanwilkar, T.S. Thakur, D.Y. Chandrachud

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 795; (2016) 10 SCC 701; AIR 2016 SC 5293; 2017(1) ALL MR 466; 2016 (6) Bom.C.R. 735; 2016 (4) CLT(SC) 140; JT 2016 (10) SC 36; 2017 (2) MahLJ 514; 2017 (2) MPLJ 91; 2016(10) SCALE 195

Case Digest

Subject: Environment, Wildlife & Animal

Keywords: Environment, National Green Tribunal Act, 2010, National Green Tribunal (Practices and Procedure) Rules, 2011

Summary: Environment, Wildlife & Animal - Demolition of unauthorized structures - Applicants filed application before Tribunal complaining about degradation of environment on account of unauthorized construction on plot of land falling within CRZ(III) alleging that appellant was responsible for construction of commercial building on plot of land without obtaining necessary permission from concerned authorities - Upon analyzing documentary evidence, Tribunal rejected plea of appellant that structure as it exists at present was constructed prior to 19-2-1991 and issued directions inter alia for demolition of all structures including restaurant and bar/pub and allied structures standing in land survey no.65/1-A, or in survey no.83/2-A, of Morambio Grande village - Aggrieved, appellant filed review petition before Tribunal but the same was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal by appellant challenging both judgments on original application and review application - Whether impugned judgments of Tribunal are sustainable.

Held, when appellant purchased the subject plot vide registered sale deed dt.3-8-1992, only a small structure at the corner of the said plot was in existence and was used as a garage and the same was indisputably within 100 meters from High Tide Line. On such finding, it necessarily follows, that the structure as it exists at present is quite different - both in shape, size and location being in the middle of the plot. As such, it is an unauthorized structure constructed after 19-2-1991. Hence, as the structure directed to be demolished by Tribunal was obviously erected after 19-2-1991, that being an unauthorized structure within the meaning of the Notification, could not be used for any purpose whatsoever and is required to be demolished. Hence, the finding recorded by Tribunal and consequential directions given in that behalf are unassailable. Appeal dismissed.

Ratio - The CRZ policy dt.19-2-1991 prohibits any construction upto 200 metres from High Tide Line. It is to be treated as 'No Development Zone', except for repairs of existing 'authorized structures' not exceeding specific permissible FSI, plinth area and other norms for permissible activities including facilities essential for such activity under the Notification.
