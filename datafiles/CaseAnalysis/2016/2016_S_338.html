Chandrakant Adinath Utture v State of Maharashtra and others

Supreme Court of India

15 February 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Kurian Joseph, R.F. Nariman

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 121; (2016) 6 SCC 150; AIR 2016 SC 893; 2016(3) ALL MR 475; 2016 (3) AWC 2521; JT 2016 (2) SC 249; 2016 (6) MahLJ 34; 2016 (2) MLJ 161; 2016(2) SCALE 423; [2016] 2 S.C.R. 193

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property

Keywords: Maharashtra Project Affected Persons Rehabilitation Act, 1986, Land Acquisition Act, 1894

Summary: Land & Property - Administrative - The Maharashtra Project Affected Persons Rehabilitation Act, 1986, s. 13, 13(1)(a),(b),(c), 13(2) - Acquisition - Slab - In the gazette notification originally published, the slab under Part II was Slab III whereunder there is no acquisition in case the holding in the benefitted zone is not more than 3 Hectares and 23 Ares - Under all slabs, larger the holding, proportionately larger is the acquisition - After publishing the notification u/s. 13 of the Act with Slab III on 4-3-1994, another notification was published on 26-7-1995 changing the slab under Part II from Slab III to Slab I wherein the exemption from acquisition is only where the size of holding is not more than 1 Hectare and 61 Ares - HC in the impugned judgment, has taken the view that the public notice for hearing u/s. 13 of the Act need only be in respect of the lands in the affected zone and benefitted zone and there is no requirement under the Act or under law, for notice when there is a change in the slab - Hence instant appeals - In case there is a change in slab or area to be acquired, after the publication of the first notification, should there be a notice to those whose lands will be acquired on account of the change in the slab.

Held, passing of the award by itself does not mean that any illegality should not be addressed. In the instant case, the writ petitions were filed when the declaration u/s. 6 of the Act was published, and in any case, it is submitted by the appellants that they have not been dispossessed so far and no compensation also has been paid. Therefore, the HC is not right in holding that the requirement of notice on objections and suggestions need only be in respect of the lands coming u/s. 13(1)(a) of the Act (the affected zones) and s. 13(1)(b) of the Act (the benefitted zones). The HC is right, however, in holding that no notice is required in respect of the lands belonging to the slab u/s. 13(1)(c) of the Act, when it is issued for the first time. However, in case there is a change in the slab reducing the area of exemption from acquisition in the benefitted zone, the procedure u/s. 13 (2) of the Act is required to be followed. These appeals are disposed of permitting the appellants to prefer their objections or submit their suggestions on the change of slab from Slab III to Slab I before the Collector. Appeals allowed.
