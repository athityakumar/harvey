Vikas Yadav v State of U. P. and others

Supreme Court of India

03 October 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, C. Nagappan

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 702; (2016) 9 SCC 541; AIR 2016 SC 4614; 2017 CRLJ 1111; JT 2016 (10) SC 172; 2017 (2) KLT 266; 2016 (4) RCR(Criminal) 546; 2016(9) SCALE 549; [2016] 8 S.C.R. 872

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Life Imprisonment, Pardon, Implied, Undertrial, FIR, Jurisdiction, Precedent, Enhancement, Restriction, President, Two Accused, Death Sentence, Rarest Of Rare, Imprisonment For Life, Jail, Appropriate Government, Criminal Justice, Narcotic Drugs And Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985, Expiry, Dealing, Stamp, Identification, Prisoner, Remission, Interfere, Commutation, Caste, Antecedents, Judiciary, Judicial Power, Representation Of The People Act, 1951, Rigorous Imprisonment, Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Constitution of India, 1950, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, Corruption

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 364, 201, 34 - Honour killing - Imposition of fixed term sentence - Trial Court vide order dt.30-5-2008 sentenced accused Vikas Yadav and Vishal Yadav to life imprisonment and fine u/s. 302 of IPC and sentenced to undergo SI for ten years and fine for conviction u/ss. 364 /34 of IPC and RI for five years and fine u/s.201/34 of IPC and all sentences were directed to run concurrently - Further, other accused Sukhdev Yadav was also convicted and was awarded life imprisonment and fine for offence u/s.302 and RI for seven years and fine for offence u/s. 364 and RI for three years and fine for conviction u/s. 201 of IPC and all sentences were directed to be concurrent - On appeal by State for enhancement of sentence of imprisonment of life to one of death for offence u/s. 302 of IPC, HC awarded fixed term sentence without consideration of remission - Hence, instant appeal challenging propriety of sentence as HC imposed a fixed term sentence, i.e., 25 years for offence u/s. 302 and 5 years for offence u/s. 201 of IPC with stipulation that both the sentences would run consecutively - Whether imposition of fixed term sentence is valid.

Held, from the evidence brought on record as well as the analysis made by HC, it is demonstrable about the criminal proclivity of accused persons, for they have neither the respect for human life nor did they have any concern for the dignity of a dead person. They had deliberately comatosed the feeling that even in death a person has dignity and when one is dead deserves to be treated with dignity. The brutality that has been displayed by accused persons clearly exposes the depraved state of mind. Further, conduct during the trial was also emphasized by HC as it is not an effect to protect one-self, but the arrogance and the impunity shown in which they set up false defense and instilled shivering fear in the mind and heart of witnesses with the evil design of defeating the prosecution case. Further, accused Vikas Yadav committed instant crime when he was on released on bail, being convicted u/s. 201/120-B of IPC in Jesica Lal Murder case. Under such circumstances, imposition of fixed term sentence on appellants by HC could not be found fault with. However, appeals are disposed of with singular modification in sentence i.e., sentence u/s. 201/34 of IPC are to run concurrently and all other sentences and directions would remain intact. Appeals disposed of.
