V. Vasanthakumar v H. C. Bhatia and others

Supreme Court of India

13 July 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, R. Banumathi, Uday Umesh Lalit

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 512; (2016) 7 SCC 686; 2016 (6) AWC 5779; 2016 (3) CLT(SC) 140; 2016 (6) CTC 549; JT 2016 (6) SC 544; 2016(6) SCALE 834; [2016] 5 S.C.R. 51

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Jurisdiction, Substantial Question, Tribunals, Constitution of India, 1950

Summary: Constitution - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 136, 145(3) - Representation - Authoritative pronouncement - Legality - Petitioner/advocate filed instant PIL seeking direction to respondents to consider his representation and to take steps for implementation of suggestion of the Constitution Bench of SC in Bihar Legal Support Society, v. The Chief Justice Of India And Anr., 1986 Indlaw SC 32, by establishing a National/Regional Courts of appeal.

Held, dismissal of an overwhelming number of cases does not discourage litigants or member of Bar from filing cases. It is common knowledge that huge backlog of cases in SC not only attracts criticism from the litigant public but also from independent observers of judicial systems. To add to the woes of Court there are a number of new legislations which provide for a first appeal to SC, a role which SC was never intended to play in Constitutional scheme. The only difference in that situation would be that in place of SC Court of Appeal would look into the matter and correct whatever needs to be corrected in the judgment impugned before it. The pronouncement of SC sounding notes of caution against liberal grant of special leave to appeal or exercise of restraint in the matter of entertaining cases have lead to no meaningful improvement in situation. Matter was referred to Constitutional Bench for an authoritative pronouncement. Order accordingly.
