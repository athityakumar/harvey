Dilbagh Singh v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

28 November 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Amitava Roy, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 861; 2017 (2) JCC (Narcotics) 49; JT 2016 (11) SC 336; 2017 (1) RCR(Criminal) 88; 2016(12) SCALE 357; [2016] 7 S.C.R. 781

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Gazetted Officer, Investigating Agency, Search, Narcotic Drugs And Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, Seizure

Summary: Criminal - Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985 - Seizure of contraband - Conviction - Appellant and other accused, while travelling in car and were intercepted by police and upon search of car, six bags stuffed with contraband, Poppy Husk was recovered - Upon completion of investigation, appellant and other accused were put on trial u/ss.15 and 25 of the Act - Trial Court held charge to be proved and convicted and sentenced both appellant and other accused u/s.15 of the Act and awarded punishment of rigorous imprisonment for 10 years and fine - On appeal, HC confirmed judgment and order of Trial Court in toto by impugned judgment - Hence, instant appeal by appellant on ground that investigating agency had contravened mandatory prescriptions of ss.50 and 57 of the Act and so, conviction is illegal and non est in law - Whether conviction of appellant could be upheld.

Held, contraband was recovered from inside the car in which appellant and other accused were travelling at the relevant point of time and not in course of the search of their person. Hence, s.50 had no application to support cause of the defence. Further, lower courts have concurrently concluded that IO at the site, had after the arrest of the accused persons and seizure of the contraband forwarded the information with regard thereto to his higher officer without any delay and the related FIR with necessary endorsements had reached Magistrate on the same date. There was no evidence by appellant to either contradict or decimate said finding based on records. Hence, assertion of non-compliance of s.57 of the Act could not be accepted and in fact, requirements of s.57 of the Act were duly complied with. Further, testimony of the PWs 4, 5 fully substantiate the recovery of the contraband i.e, Poppy Husk from conscious possession of the accused person and it was also established that samples were properly sampled, sealed and forwarded to FSL. Further, fact that contraband was recovered from the car while the same was being driven by one of the accused person in the company of the other also authenticate the charge of their conscious possession thereof. The haul of six bags of Poppy Husk is substantial so much so that it negates even the remote possibility of the same being planted by the police. Furthermore, no evidence with regard to bias or malice against Investigative Agency was adduced. As such, prosecution proved charge against accused persons beyond all reasonable doubt and the courts appreciated materials on record in correct legal and factual perspectives and the findings recorded do not merit any interference. Appeal dismissed.

Ratio - Application of s.50 of the Act is comprehended and called for only in the case of search of a person as distinguished from search of any premises etc.
