Saddik @ Lalo Gulam Hussein Shaikh and others v State of Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

03 October 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Pinaki Chandra Ghose, Amitava Roy

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 790; (2016) 10 SCC 663; AIR 2016 SC 5101; 2017 (1) CGLJ 563; 2017 CRLJ 149; 2016 (4) JCC 2752; JT 2016 (10) SC 74; 2017 (1) RCR(Criminal) 171; 2016(9) SCALE 492; [2016] 5 S.C.R. 421

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Common Object, Assembly, Quarrel, Interfere, Knife, Unlawful Assembly, Altercation, Concurrent, Scheduled Castes and the Scheduled Tribes (Prevention of Atrocities) Act, 1989, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Constitution of India, 1950, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, Complainant

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 209 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 143, 147, 148, 149, 299, 300, 302, 304, 323, 504 - Scheduled Castes and the Scheduled Tribes (Prevention of Atrocities) Act, 1989, ss. 3(1)(x), 3(2)(v) - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant/accused no. 1 allegedly caused knife injuries to deceased while other accused persons started beating complainant and prosecution witnesses with sticks - Charge sheet u/ss. 143, 147, 148, 149, 302, 323 and 504 IPC and ss. 3(1)(10) and 3(2)(5) of the Act were framed against accused - Trial Court convicted accused for offence punishable u/s. 302 r/w ss. 143, 147, 148, 323 IPC and sentenced them to RI for life with fine however, acquitted accused of offences punishable u/s. 504 IPC and ss. 3(1)(10) and 3(2)(5) of the Act - On appeals, HC dismissed appeals and confirmed judgment of conviction passed by Trial Court - Hence, instant appeals - Whether concurrent findings of fact call for interference in the facts and circumstances of the case.

Held, in instant case there were concurrent findings as to the guilt of accused persons by both Courts below. In upholding the judgment and order of conviction of Trial Court, HC had primarily relied upon the evidence of eye-witnesses, namely, PW1, PW2 and PW3, who were found to be trustworthy and their statements corroborated each other. HC held that accused were sharing the common object of causing injuries to deceased and prosecution witnesses. Further, looking to the evidence given by PW15/doctor who performed post-mortem of deceased, HC found that there were three stab injuries on chest, stomach and intestine which were sufficient in the ordinary course of nature to cause death of deceased, thereby attracting cl. "thirdly" of s. 300 r/w s. 149 IPC. However, accused no.1 had arrived at the scene of occurrence with nine armed men out of which three were equipped with knives and rest were equipped with sticks. Sufficient amount of time had elapsed between the initial altercation at the restaurant of accused no.1 and subsequent arrival of accused persons at the spot of crime. Further, it was also established from the evidence on record that accused no.1 had inflicted knife injury of such a nature, upon the unarmed deceased that was sufficient in ordinary course of nature to cause death. Thus, SC was not inclined to grant the benefit of Exception cl. to accused no.1 in instant case. No ground to interfere with the judgment passed by HC. Appeals dismissed.

Ratio - If direct evidence establishes the commission of crime, then, absence of motive is of no consequence.
