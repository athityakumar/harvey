Ram Naresh Rawat v Ashwini Ray and others

Supreme Court of India

15 December 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, N.V. Ramana

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 894; (2017) 3 SCC 436; 2017 (153) FLR 601; JT 2016 (12) SC 225; 2017 (1) LLJ 1; 2017 (4) MahLJ 12; 2016(12) SCALE 816; [2016] 9 S.C.R. 141

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Jurisdiction, Industrial, CONSTITUTION, Minimum Wages, Regularisation, Labour Court, Standing Order, Seasonal, Labour, Madhya Pradesh Industrial Employment (Standing Orders) Act, 1961, Madhya Pradesh Industrial Employment (Standing Orders) Rules, 1963

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Madhya Pradesh Industrial Employment (Standing Orders) Act, 1961- Madhya Pradesh Industrial Employment (Standing Orders) Rules, 1963 - Regularization of Employees - Non-compliance of Order Challenged - Petitioner's/employees initially were engaged by respondent/ state govt. as daily wage earners - Labour Court on reference of dispute held petitioners as permanent employees and entitled to regular pay scale - Industrial Court and HC dismissed appeal and writ petition of respondent respectively - Respondents by virtue of order passed by HC, fixed minimum of pay scale - Hence, instant contempt petitions.

Held, when the conditions of service are governed by two statutes; one relating to selection and appointment and the other relating to the terms and conditions of service, an endeavour should be made to give effect to both of the statutes. Further, a daily-wager does not hold a post as he is not appointed in terms of the provisions of the Act and the Rules framed there under and in that view of the matter he does not derive any legal right and only because an employee had been working for more than 240 days that by itself would not confer any legal right upon him to be regularised in service. Hence, if an appointment has been made contrary to the provisions of the statute the same would be void and the effect thereof would be that no legal right was derived by the employee by reason thereof. In common parlance, normally, a person who is known as 'permanent employee' would be treated as a regular employee but it does not appear to be exactly that kind of situation in the instant case, that merely after completing six months' service an employee gets right to be treated as 'permanent employee'. Though a 'permanent employee' has right to receive pay in the graded pay-scale, at the same time, he would be getting only minimum of the said pay-scale with no increments. It is only the regularisation in service which would entail grant of increments etc. in the pay-scale. Hence, contempt petitions are devoid of any substances and the same are dismissed. Petitions dismissed.
