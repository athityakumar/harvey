Ashok Kumar and another v State of Bihar and others

Supreme Court of India

21 October 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Dr. D.Y. Chandrachud, T.S. Thakur, A.M. Khanwilkar

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 1041; (2017) 4 SCC 357; AIR 2016 SC 5069; 2017 (3) ALD(SC) 92; 2017(1) ALL MR 914; 2017 (2) AWC 1475; 2016(10) SCALE 320; [2016] 8 S.C.R. 815

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Selection, Interview, Promotion, Procedure, Rule, Examination, Merit, Posts, Recruitment, Appeal, Civil Court, Writ Petition, Post, Appointment, Bihar Civil Court Staff (Class III and Class IV) (Amendment) Rules, 1998, Bihar Civil Courts Staff (Class III and Class IV) (Amendment) Rules, 2001

Summary: Service - Bihar Civil Courts Staff (Class III and Class IV) (Amendment) Rules, 2001 - Principle of Estoppel - Office of District and Sessions Judge, Muzaffarpur issued General Order inviting applications for promotion to six Class III posts from amongst Class IV employees of Civil Court at Muzaffarpur and for selection based on written test and interview, 85 marks were fixed for written examination and 15 marks for interview - Later, appointment committee selected six candidates and submitted list to HC - However, HC declined to approve the list on ground that marks allotted for written examination were not in accordance with Court's general letter and Rules - Further, HC issued directions to hold fresh examination fixing 90 marks for written examination - In pursuance of said directions, fresh General Order was issued and written test was held and all appellants also participated in process of fresh selection - Later, respondent nos.9 to 14 were appointed on Class III posts on 31-12-2004 - However, two appellants and four other unsuccessful candidates filed writ petition before HC challenging General order and order of appointmentt on ground that appointment process was vitiated, since under relevant rules, the written test was required to carry 85 marks and interview, 15 marks - Single Judge of HC allowed petition and quashed appointments and held that u/r.6 of 2001 Rules, the written examination was to carry 85 marks and the interview 15 marks - As such, six persons i.e, respondent nos. 9 to 14, who had been appointed to Class III posts were reverted to their original Class IV posts - Aggrieved, said persons filed appeal and meantime, six persons who were successful in first selection process were promoted to Class III posts and the candidates included two appellants and four other candidates - While so, DB of HC allowed appeal filed by respondent nos. 9 to 14 and restored original order of appointment dt.31-12-2004 - Further, DB also opined that appellants had in pursuance of the notification issued by District and Session Judge participated in the selection process without any protest. Having failed to raise any objection to the selection process it was held that appellants were estopped from turning around and challenging the selection once they were declared unsuccessful - Hence, instant appeal - Whether appellants are estopped from challenging selection process.

Held, appellants were clearly on notice, when the fresh selection process took place that written examination would carry 90 marks and the interview, 10 marks and if appellants were aggrieved by decision to hold a fresh process, they had not espoused their remedy but instead participated in the selection process. Further, it was only upon being unsuccessful that appellants challenged the result in writ petition but the option is not available to appellants as principle of Estoppel would operate. Hence, DB could not held to be in error in coming to conclusion that it was not open to appellants after participating in the selection process to question the result, once they were declared to be unsuccessful. However, to meet ends of justice, directions are issued that in the event that there is any existing vacancy, appellant who has still continued in service, could be allowed to continue to work on Class III post in which he is currently working on a provisional basis until the next round of selection process takes place and appellant is at liberty to participate in the selection process. Subject to said direction, judgment of DB of HC is affirmed. Appeal disposed of.
