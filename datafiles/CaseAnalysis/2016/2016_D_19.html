Shyam Lal v Deepa Dass Chela Ram Chela Garib Dass

Supreme Court of India

05 July 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjan Gogoi, Arun Mishra, Prafulla C. Pant

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 432; (2016) 7 SCC 572; AIR 2016 SC 3243; 2016 (118) ALR 253; 2016 (4) AWC 4043; JT 2016 (6) SC 394; 2016 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 2272; 2016 (5) MLJ 389; 2016 (3) RCR(Civil) 812; 2016 (133) RD 406; [2016] 5 S.C.R. 893

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Transfer Of Property Act, 1882, Registration Act, 1908, Leases, Punjab Tenancy Act, 1887, Punjab Security of Land Tenures Act, 1953, Kerala Buildings (Lease and Rent Control) Act, 1965

Summary: Rent Control - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 107 - Punjab Security of Land Tenures Act, 1953, ss. 14A, 2(6), 9, 9(8) - Punjab Tenancy Act, 1887, ss. 39, 4(5), 40 - Expiry of Lease Period - Eviction Order Challenged - Appellant/tenant was self-cultivating lessee of suit land for fixed period of term - Respondent/landlord on expiry of lease period claimed eviction of tenant - Lower Court passed eviction order against tenant and HC in appeal affirmed order of lower Court on expiry of lease period - Hence, instant appeal - Whether after expiry of fixed term tenancy in respect of an agricultural lease under 1953 Act, tenancy get automatically terminated and person occupying leased premises ceases to be a tenant.

Held, s 107 of the 1882 Act, which has been made applicable to the State of Punjab (including Haryana) by the notifications require annual leases of immovable property to be made by a registered instrument. Further, retention/continuance of possession after expiry of the duration of the lease with the consent of the landlord will continue to vest in the erstwhile tenant the same status on the principle of holding over. Such continuance even after expiry of the deemed period of the lease u/s. 106 of 1882 Act, as in the instant case, would clothe the occupant with the status of a tenant under the Act in view of s. 116 of 1882 Act, which deals with the consequences of holding over. The operation of s. 116 of 1882 Act, would confer legitimacy to the possession of the tenant even after the termination or expiration of the deemed period of the lease so as to confer on him a status akin to that of a statutory tenant and hence protection from eviction as envisaged by the provisions of 1953 Act. Hence, order of HC is set aside. Appeal allowed.
