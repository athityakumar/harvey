Ram Rati v Mange Ram (Dead) Through Lrs. and others

Supreme Court of India

23 February 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Kurian Joseph, R.F. Nariman

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 186; AIR 2016 SC 1343; 2016 (115) ALR 880; 2016 (5) CTC 555; JT 2016 (3) SC 477; 2016 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 2071; 2016 (2) RCR(Civil) 464; 2016 (131) RD 717; 2016 (3) RLW 2328; 2016(3) SCALE 219; [2016] 2 S.C.R. 9; 2016 (3) WLN(SC) 75

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Keywords: Consolidation, Code of Civil Procedure, 1908

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 18 r. 17, s, 151 - Consolidation of suits - Application for discharge of witness and re-examination - Justification -Two suits were filed by respondent and appellants before Trial Court in respect to same property in question for grant of injunction, hence, suits were consolidated - However, before consolidation of suit, appellant was examined as PW 1 and respondent had also cross- examined PW1 - Further, evidence was closed - Further, after consolidation of two suits, respondents filed application for discharging of statement of PW 1 and examination of witness PW 1 afresh under O.18 r. 7 read s. 51 of CPC - Whether it is justified to allow application filed by respondent for discharging statement of witness and re-examining witness afresh.

Held, only ground taken up in application filed under O. 18 r. 7 of CPC is that after consolidation of suits, respondent in suit should get opportunity to cross examine PW1 . Further, in order passed by Trial Court dt. 24-2-2010, it has been noted by respondent in no more party of suit and earlier order of consolidation of suits dt. 8-12-2007 was maintained, further clarifying that past evidence of respondent which has already been recorded, to be treated as main suit. However, SC held that Trial Court and HC failed to consider such curtail aspects, hence, impugned order passed by lower Courts is set aside. SC held that during pendency of appeal, evidence has been closed and what only final order is to be passed. Hence, SC directed that Trial Court should disposed of suits expeditiously. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Impugned order passed for re-examination of witness in favor of party is not sustainable when evidence has been closed and applicant is no more party to suit.
