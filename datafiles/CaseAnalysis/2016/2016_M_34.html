Government of (NCT of Delhi) and another v Mahender Singh and others

Supreme Court of India

22 September 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Kurian Joseph, R.F. Nariman

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 777; AIR 2016 SC 4895; 2017 (3) ALD(SC) 64; 2017 (121) ALR 218; JT 2016 (11) SC 23; 2016 (2) OLR 1102; 2017 (135) RD 255; 2016(10) SCALE 37; 2016 (4) UPLBEC 2912

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Land Acquisition Act, 1894, Right to Fair Compensation and Transparency in Land Acquisition, Rehabilitation and Resettlement Act, 2013

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4(1), 6, 11 - Right to Fair Compensation and Transparency in Land Acquisition, Rehabilitation and Resettlement Act, 2013, ss. 11, 24(2) - Acquisition proceedings - Physical possession - Validity - Notification u/s. 4(1) of the 1894 Act was issued for initiation of land acquisition proceedings on 04-03-2003 - U/s. 6 of the 1894 Act declaration was published and award was passed u/s. 11 of the 1894 Act on 03-02-2006 - HC rendered a declaration that the land acquisition proceedings have elapsed in terms of s. 24(2) of the 2013 Act - Hence, instant appeal - Whether the stay granted in one of the items covered by s. 4(1) of the notification could have the effect on other lands covered by same notification.

Held, under the scheme of acquisition, an award u/s. 11 of the 1894 Act has to be passed in respect of each land owned by a person/persons interested. Therefore, what was lapsed u/s. 11A of the 1894 Act was not the entire land as declared u/s. 6 of the 1894 Act but the particular land belonging to person/persons in whose favour an award u/s. 11 of the 1894 Act had to be passed within 2 years of declaration u/s. 6 of the 1894 Act. Land acquisition proceedings referred u/s. 24(2) of the 2013 Act would include the steps for taking physical possession of the land and payment of compensation, as held by SC in Delhi Development Authority v. Sukhbir Singh, 2016 Indlaw SC 650. S. 24(2) of the 2013 Act has to operate and the acquisition proceedings in respect of respondents' lands where award u/s. 11 of the 1894 Act was passed have lapsed. However, the declaration as above and the consequent dismissal of appeal was without prejudice to the liberty available to appellants to initiate proceedings afresh for the acquisition of subject land under the provisions of the 2013 Act. Thus, appellants were given a period of 1 year to exercise its liberty granted u/s. 24(2) of the 2013 Act for initiation of acquisition proceedings afresh. Appeal disposed of.
