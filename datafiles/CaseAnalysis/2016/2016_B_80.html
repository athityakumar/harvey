Jaya Biswal and others v Branch Manager, Iffco Tokio General Insurance Company Limited and another

Supreme Court of India

04 February 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, Uday Umesh Lalit

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 112; 2016 (1) ACC 601; 2016 ACJ 721; AIR 2016 SC 956; 2016 (2) ALD(SC) 141; 2016 (2) AWC 1296; 2016 (1) CLT(SC) 326; 2016 (148) FLR 875; 2016 (2) JCC 1427; JT 2016 (2) SC 6; 2016 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1354; 2016 (2) LLJ 132; 2016 (1) OLR 710; 2016 (1) RCR(Civil) 1003; 2016 (3) RLW 1771; 2016(2) SCALE 138; [2016] 1 S.C.R. 299

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance

Keywords: Labour & Industrial Law, Challenged, In The Course Of, Insured, Claim Petition, Payment Of Wages Act, 1936, Arising Out Of And In The Course Of, Employee's [Workmen's] Compensation Act, 1923

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Insurance - Payment of Wages Act, 1936 - Claim petition - Challenged - Deceased met with accident and died - Appellants claimants filed claim petition of Rs. 20,0000/- before the Commissioner for workmen commission - Accordingly Commissioner allowed compensation of Rs. 10,75,253/- - Respondent Insurance company filed appeal before HC - HC allowed the appeal and set aside the order of Commissioner -Hence instant appeal - Whether the order recorded by HC was just and proper.

Held, HC did not give any reason on which basis it interfered with the finding recorded by the Commissioner on the aspect of monthly wages earned by the deceased. The impugned judgment does not even mention what according to the HC, the wages of the deceased were at the time of the accident. Such an unnecessary interference on part of the HC was absolutely uncalled for, especially in light of the fact that the appellant Nos.1 and 2 are old and have lost their elder son and they have become destitutes. Further, under the 1936 Act the onus is on the employer to maintain the register and records of wages. Thus, judgment and order of HC suffers from gross infirmity as it has been passed not only in ignorance of the decisions of SC referred to supra, but also the provisions of the 1923 Act. Accordingly monthly wage of the deceased arrived at by Commissioner was Rs.10,000/-. The date of birth of the deceased according to the Driver's License produced on record is 1-7-1984. The date of death of the deceased is 19-7-2011. Thus, according to Schedule IV of the 1923 Act, the 'completed years of age on the last birthday of the employee immediately preceding the date on which the compensation fell due', is 27 years, the factor for which is 213.57. Hence, the amount of compensation payable to the appellants is calculated accordingly. Hence, total amount of compensation payable thus comes to Rs.10,92,850/- with 12% p.a. and another Rs. 25,000/- was awarded - Appeal allowed .
