Vikas Sankhala and others v Vikas Kumar Agarwal and others

Supreme Court of India

18 October 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, Mala Kumari Sharma

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 1038; 2017 (2) AWC 1363; 2017 (1) CLT(SC) 219; [2016] 7 S.C.R. 639; 2016 (4) UPLBEC 2917

Case Digest

Subject: Education

Keywords: Affirmative Action, CONSTITUTION, Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribes, Migration, Constitution (Eighty-Sixth Amendment) Act, 2002, Right of Children to Free and Compulsory Education Act, 2009, Constitution of India, 1950

Summary: Education - Appointment of teachers - Relaxation in qualification marks - Writ petitions were filed before HC challenging selection of candidates by contending that all those candidates belonging to reserved categories who secured less than 60% in TET could not be declared as having passed TET since minimum percentage for passing TET was 60% and as such ineligible to participate in selection process - Single Judge of HC passed common judgment in all petitions and held that order dt.23-3-2011 of State Govt. could not be allowed to stand as the relaxation/concession in qualifying marks was not legal or valid - Aggrieved, State Govt. and also persons belonging to reserved categories who were selected but whose selection was set aside by Single Judge of HC, preferred appeals before DB of HC - However, DB of HC though not agreed with some of the reasons of Single Judge, yet, dismissed all appeals giving its own reasons - Hence, instant appeals by State Govt. and also by selected candidates belonging to reserved category - Whether NCTE notification dt.29-7-2011 amending paragraph 3 of its earlier guidelines dt.11-2-2011, provides 5% relaxation to reserved category for passing TET.

Held, qualifying marks for TET are prescribed in para 9 of the guidelines dt.11-2-2011 and there is no amendment to said para. The amendment is incorporated in para 3 of the said notification and the same provided for two aspects - training and minimum marks in graduation or B.Ed. It was in that context, second aspect of amended provision in para 3(ii) mentioned about 'Reservation Policy' and allowed relaxation upto 5% in qualifying marks. The relaxation is, therefore, relatable to marks in graduation and B.Ed. Qualification, meaning thereby insofar as reserved category candidates such as SC/ST/OBC/PH are concerned, that they would be treated as qualified to undergo the training in case they pass graduation with minimum 45% marks and B.Ed. qualification with minimum 40% marks. However, such relaxation of 5% does not relate to TET at all. Hence, NCTE has not provided any provision for relaxation in TET examination for reserved category candidates but has left it to State Govt. to do the needful as per para 9 of guidelines dt.11-2-2011 which remained unaltered. Hence, view of HC on said specific aspect is affirmed. However, reserved category candidates who secured pass marks on the application of relaxed standards as contained in the extant policy of the Govt. in its communication dt.23-3-2011 are to be treated as having qualified TET examination and thus, eligible to participate in the selection undertaken by State Govt. Further, migration from reserved category to general category would be admissible to those reserved category candidates who secured more marks obtained by last unreserved category candidates who are selected, subject to the condition that such reserved category candidates did not avail any other special concession. Further, concession of passing marks in TET would not be treated as concession falling in said category. Appeals allowed to such extent only. Appeals disposed of.
