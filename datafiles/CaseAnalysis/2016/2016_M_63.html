Madhya Pradesh State Road Transport Corporation v Manoj Kumar and another

Supreme Court of India

29 August 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, R.K. Agrawal

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 605; (2016) 9 SCC 375; 2017 (3) AWC 2371; 2016 (151) FLR 60; 2016 (3) JabLJ 122; JT 2016 (8) SC 470; 2016 (4) LLJ 1; 2016(8) SCALE 292; [2016] 4 S.C.R. 225

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Voluntary Retirement, Indian Contract Act, 1872, Resignation, Leave, Employment, Rule, Date, Validity Of, Appeal, Option, Relationship, Scheme, Employer, Banking Companies (Acquisition and Transfer of Undertakings) Act, 1970, State Bank of India Act, 1955, Banks, Employee, Constitution of India, 1950, Right to, Employees Voluntary Retirement Scheme

Summary: (A) Service - Practice & Procedure - Banking Companies (Acquisition and Transfer of Undertakings) Act, 1970, ss. 19(1), 19(4) - Indian Contract Act, 1872, s. 2(b) - Employees Voluntary Retirement Scheme - Withdrawal of VRS - Denial - Challenged - Appellant/Corporation undertakes work of carrying passengers from one place to another within and outside State - Appellant was running into losses, State Govt. obtained permission for winding up of appellant Corporation - Managing Director, of Corporation by an order introduced a Scheme called as Voluntary Retirement from Service (VRS) for employees of Corporation - The said Scheme was to come into force from 07-01-2005 - Respondents/employees had submitted their applications for voluntary retirement within the span of original period fixed under the Scheme, i.e. between 07-01-2005 and 08-01-2005 - However, requests for withdrawal of options were made on 08-01-2005 after expiry of original Scheme - Appellants did not accept respondents requests for withdrawal and on contrary applications for VRS submitted by employees were accepted - Respondents filed writ petition before HC challenging action of Corporation - Single Judge dismissed petitions holding that applications for withdrawal of VRS could only be moved within the validity period of Scheme and in those cases where applications for withdrawal was submitted after 08-01-2005, this could not be done by concerned employees - On appeals, DB allowed appeals holding that it is always permissible for an employee to withdraw the option under VRS before it is accepted - Hence, instant appeals - Whether an employee opting for voluntary retirement under the said Schemes was precluded from withdrawing that offer.

Held, VRS Scheme floated by employer would be treated as invitation to offer and application submitted by the employees pursuant thereto is an offer which does not amount to resignation in praesenti and offer can be withdrawn during the validity period. This would be the position even when there is a clause in the Scheme that offer once given cannot be withdrawn at all. However, exception to this principle is that in such cases offer is to be withdrawn during the validity period of the Scheme and not thereafter even when if it is not accepted during the period of Scheme. In such situation, if employees are allowed to withdraw from Scheme at any time even after its closure, it would not be possible to work out the Scheme as all calculations of employer would fail. In instant case Corporation had floated Scheme because of reason that it has virtually stopped transport business and the purpose of Scheme was to benefit itself by shrinking the strength of employees as with no transport business need for such employees is not there. Further, Scheme provided that once option is given, the same cannot be withdrawn. Employees had a right to withdraw the offer during validity period but not thereafter. This legal principle is even taken note of by HC in impugned judgment. HC however, held that though Scheme was valid up to 08-01-2005, but validity was extended up to 07-31-2007, employees could withdraw their offers before 07-31-2007. Further, as in all these cases where the offer was withdrawn before 07-31-2007, HC dismissed appeals of Corporation. Appeals disposed of.

(B) Service - Practice & Procedure - Banking Companies (Acquisition and Transfer of Undertakings) Act, 1970, ss. 19(1), 19(4) - Indian Contract Act, 1872, s. 2(b) - Employees Voluntary Retirement Scheme - Voluntary retirement - Withdrawal - Permissibility - Whether validity of Scheme was extended up to 07-31-2007 and the employees could withdraw their offer before this date or the date on which initial scheme expired and withdrawal thereafter was not permissible.

Held, there was no VRS Scheme in operation from 08-02- 2005 to 10-11-2006. It is only on 10-12-2006 another opportunity was given to the rest of employees to submit their applications and period during which such an application for voluntary retirement could be submitted was from 10-12-2006 to 10-28-2006. This small window was opened for a period of 17 days for those employees who had not submitted their applications and they were afforded another chance. At the same time, main reason was to attract more such employees to opt for VRS as Corporation had decided to close down its operations and wanted its employees to take an honorable exit with 'golden handshake'. In fact, instead of promulgating VRS Scheme all over again, easy way was found by making amendment in a particular clause stating that application presented after 10-28-2006 shall not be considered. In view thereof, insofar as first set of employees are concerned, they could withdraw their option, before it was accepted, by 08-01-2005 and not thereafter. Likewise, those who submitted their options in second phase could withdraw the same before 10-28-2006. A chart was submitted before SC which indicates that some of the employees belonging to first group had withdrawn their offer before 08-01-2005 they had right to do so. Acceptance of their offer after withdrawal would be of no consequence. However, those employees who withdrew their offers after 08-01-2005 could not do so and, therefore, Corporation was within its right to accept their offers. Similarly, those employees belonging to second category who had withdrawn their offers before 10-28-2006 were entitled to withdraw their offers as those were not accepted by that date. However, the withdrawal after 10-28-2006 when Scheme was closed would be of no consequence. Appeals disposed of.
