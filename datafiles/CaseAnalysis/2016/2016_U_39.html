Union of India and others v Saleena

Supreme Court of India

29 January 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, Prafulla C. Pant

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 158; (2016) 3 SCC 437; AIR 2016 SC 641; 2016 CRLJ 1189; JT 2016 (2) SC 424; 2016 (2) MLJ(Crl) 749; 2016(1) SCALE 682; [2016] 1 S.C.R. 373

Case Digest

Subject: Human Rights; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: COFEPOSA, Speaking Order, Communication, Sufficient Ground, Subordinate, Foreign Exchange Regulation Act, 1973, Detenu, Secretary, Comments, Advisory Board, Detaining Authority, Preventive Detention, Subjective Satisfaction, Appropriate Government, Conservation Of Foreign Exchange And Prevention Of Smuggling Activities Act, 1974, Dealing, National Security Act, 1980, Communicate, Maintenance of Internal Security Act, 1971, Jammu & Kashmir Public Safety Act, 1978, Constitution of India, 1950, Criminal

Summary: Human Rights - Practice & Procedure - Conservation of Foreign Exchange and Prevention of Smuggling Activities Act, 1974, s. 11 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 22(5) - Failure to consider rejection of representation - Alleged Illegal detention - Determination - Order of detention was issued on 8-2-2013 u/s. 3(1) of Act - Further, grounds of detention were communicated to detenu vide communication dt. 8-2-2013 and detenu was informed of his right to make representation against his detention to Detaining Authority - Further, detenu was detained on 25-2-2013 and lodged in prison - Further, representation filed by detenu was rejected and same was communicated to detenu vide memorandum dt. 29-4-2013 - Further, Central Govt. confirmed order of detention and directed that detention of detenu for period of one year commencing from date of his detention - Wife of detenu field petition before HC seeking writ of habeas corpus on ground that decision of competent authority was not communicated to detenu and there were various irregularities while passing order of detention against detenu - Lower court passed order in favor of detenu and held that art. 22(5) has been violated, hence, order of detention is set aside and detenu was set free - Order passed by lower court was affirmed by DB - Whether non-communication of order rejecting representation in effective manner would invalidate order of detention.

Held, order of rejecting representation has been communicated by Under Secretary on 29-4-2013 which indicates that representation submitted by detenu had been carefully considered by competent authority. SC held that competent authority while considering representation is not required to pass speaking order but it must reflect that there has been real and proper consideration of representation. Further, HC held that competent authority of appropriate government has passed order on basis of material produced before it. It cannot be said that there is no subjective satisfaction. Further, when material, file, representation and comments on representation were produced before authority and he had mentioned in order that he had gone through representation and not found sufficient ground for exercising power u/s. 11 of Act, it cannot be said that there has been no subjective satisfaction. Hence, impugned order granting writ of habeas corpus and directing detenu to be set at liberty is totally vulnerable and same is set aside. Further, SC held that detenu was initially detained for one year and he remained in incarceration from 25-2-2013 to 24-10-2013, hence, detaining authority should re-examine matter keeping in view principle laid by SC in earlier cases. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - It is not justified to set aside detention order, when representation of detenu is considered by authority after taking into consideration material on record and have such representation is rejected which is communicated to detenu.
