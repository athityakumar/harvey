Commissioner of Commercial Taxes and others v Bajaj Auto Limited and another

Supreme Court of India

28 October 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

R.K. Agrawal, Shiva Kirti Singh

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 868; AIR 2016 SC 5014; JT 2016 (10) SC 417; 2016(10) SCALE 424; [2016] 7 S.C.R. 1008; [2017] 97 VST 24

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Keywords: Entry Tax, Levy, Tax Payable, Set Off, Importer, Turnover, Orissa Sales Tax Act, 1947, Rate, Taxable Turnover, Addition, Surcharge, Orissa Entry Tax Act, 1999, Orissa Entry Tax Rules, 1999, Computation, Bihar Sales Tax Act, 1959, Bihar Entry Tax Act

Summary: Sales Tax - Indirect Tax - Orissa Sales Tax Act, 1947 - Orissa Entry Tax Act, 1999 - Orissa Entry Tax Rules, 1999 - Surcharge - Entry tax - State Govt. by letter dt.20-11-2001, stated that surcharge under 1947 Act is to be calculated on the payable amount of tax due on taxable turnover instead of on reduced Sales Tax amount after setting off of entry tax - Accordingly, Sales Tax Officer passed order u/s.12(4) of the Act wherein surcharge was levied u/s.5A of the Act on the gross sales tax payable by respondent-Company - Aggrieved, respondent-company and also similarly placed other respondents filed writ petitions before HC - DB of HC vide common judgment and order dt.5-1-2007, allowed petitions filed by respondents - Hence, instant appeals - Whether 'Surcharge' u/s.5A of 1947 Act is to be computed on gross amount of sales tax or the net amount of sales tax after setting of or deducting the amount of entry tax.

Held, s.5A of 1947 Act is a self-contained provision and the surcharge, is leviable at the specified per centum of tax payable under 1947 Act. Tax payable under 1947 Act is independent of the provisions of 1999 Act. The assessment or quantification or computation of surcharge has to be made in accordance with the provisions of 1947 Act. Further, as per r.18 of the Rules, the tax payable under 1999 Act was to be determined after deduction there from the entry tax paid by a dealer importing vehicle into the State. Hence, on a conjoint reading of s.5 of the 1947 Act, s.4 of 1999 Act and r.18 of the Rules, the amount of surcharge u/s.5A of 1947 Act is to be levied before deducting the amount of entry tax paid by a dealer. As such, impugned judgment and order of HC is not sustainable and same is set aside. Appeals disposed of.

Ratio - S.5A of 1947 Act creates a charge and imposes liability on every dealer under 1947 Act to pay surcharge @ 10% on the amount of tax payable by him under 1947 Act. Further, s.4(1) of the 1999 Act, prescribes for reduction of the tax amount payable by the dealer to the extent of entry tax already paid for the same article for which sales tax is payable. However, the section, does not specifically contemplate anything, which would indicate that the provisions of 1999 Act or the Rules have to be taken into consideration while assessing the Sales tax or surcharge.
