Laljibhai Kadvabhai Savaliya and others v State of Gujarat and others

Supreme Court of India

05 October 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Uday Umesh Lalit, V. Gopala Gowda

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 792; (2016) 9 SCC 791; AIR 2016 SC 4715; 2017 (2) ALD(SC) 118; 2017(2) ALL MR 460; 2017 (1) G.L.R. 758; JT 2016 (10) SC 149; 2016(9) SCALE 649

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Jurisdiction, Railways Act, 1989, Indian Telegraph Act, 1885, Minerals, Reports, National Highways Act, 1956, Metro Railways (Construction of Works) Act, 1978, Petroleum and Natural Gas Regulatory Board Act, 2006, Petroleum and Minerals Pipelines (Acquisition of Right of User in Land) Act, 1962, Constitution of India, 1950, Land Acquisition Act, 1894

Summary: Land & Property - Petroleum and Minerals Pipelines (Acquisition of Right of User in Land) Act, 1962, ss. 3,6 - Right of user in land - Compensation - In exercise of powers conferred by s.3 of the Act, Central Govt. issued notification on 7-1-2006, declaring its intention to acquire the right of user in respect of certain lands including lands in question - After considering objections received from land-owners and hearing those who were present including appellant no.1, Competent Authority forwarded its Report dt. 7-8-2007 to Central Govt. and after considering the same, Govt. of India vide notification dt.17-11-2007, directed that right of user in respect of land would stand vested in Reliance Gas Transportation Infrastructure Ltd. (RGTIL) free from all encumbrances - While so, owners/occupiers of Block Nos.331, 342 and 364 filed applications before HC seeking declaration that RGTIL had no authority to enter lands of said owners/occupiers and that it be restrained and RGTIL challenged non-agricultural permissions by filing applications before HC and HC stayed operation of non-agricultural permissions and assailing the same, appeals were preferred - DB of HC permitted RGTIL which had started laying pipelines - While so, challenging notification issued by Central Govt. u/s.6 of the Act, owners/occupiers of Block nos. 342 and 364 filed applications and HC heard said applications along with earlier applications and held that balance of convenience was in favour of RGTIL and rejected relief sought - Later, DB of HC disposed of other applications observing that petitioners therein, if aggrieved, could approach District Judge for compensation - Hence, instant appeals by owners/occupiers assailing said orders of DB of HC.

Held, Block Nos. 331,342 and 364 of village Gothan were agricultural lands and recorded so in the revenue records on the day the Notification u/s.3(1) was issued. The permission to convert Block Nos.342 and 364 to non-agricultural use was granted after such Notification. Anyways, the compensation including damages was arrived at by consent in the meeting held with the land owners/occupiers and determined at the level of Rs.181 per sq.mtr. for lands other than those falling in industrial zone and in respect of those falling in industrial zone, the compensation was at Rs.201 per sq.mt. If the average rate at which the lands were sold in preceding five years was taken into account, compensation was not inadequate. Further, while the matter was pending in HC, provisional compensation of Rs.300 per sq.mt. was deposited with the competent authority. Under such circumstances, the compensation awarded, for acquisition of right of user could not be called inadequate or illusory on any count. Appeals disposed of.
