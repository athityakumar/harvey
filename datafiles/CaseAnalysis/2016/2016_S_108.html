Saheb Reddy v Sharanappa and others

Supreme Court of India

16 November 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Anil R. Dave, L. Nageswara Rao

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 821; (2017) 1 SCC 142; AIR 2016 SC 5253; 2017 (2) ALD(SC) 10; 2017 (120) ALR 300; 2016 (4) CLT(SC) 204; JT 2016 (11) SC 453; 2017 (134) RD 628; 2016(12) SCALE 79

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Second Appeal, Hindu Adoptions and Maintenance Act, 1956, Hindu Succession Act, 1956

Summary: Family & Personal - Land & Property - Hindu Adoptions and Maintenance Act, 1956, ss. 12, 12(c) - Hindu Succession Act, 1956, s. 15 - Suit for partition - Adoption deed basis -Validity - Plaintiff/respondent no. 1 filed suit for partition and separate possession of his share and for a declaration that registered adoption deed dt. 9-2-1971 was null and void - Trial Court decreed suit and ordered that plaintiff was entitled to 1/16th share in suit property - On appeal, Appellate Court dismissed appeal - On further appeal, HC allowed appeal by setting aside judgment of Appellate Court and restored judgment and decree of Trial Court - Hence, instant appeal - Whether HC has rightly allocated share of properties among family members in accordance with the Act.

Held, Appellate Court was correct to the effect that plaintiff would inherit not only property of his mother along with his three sisters but he would also have share in properties of his grandmother. Plaintiff's grandmother had not prepared any Will and as she had died intestate, her property would be divided among her adopted son i.e. defendant no. 1 and heirs of her three daughters, who had predeceased their grandmother. Further, grandmother was having 1/4th share in entire property, which she had inherited from her husband. One of the daughters being plaintiff's mother heirs would inherit 1/4th share of property of grandmother and plaintiff being one of the four heirs of her mother would get 1/64th share from the property of his grandmother. Therefore, plaintiff would be getting 1/16th share in property of his mother and 1/64th share upon death of his grandmother and plaintiff would be getting 5/64th share in suit property, whereas defendant no. 1 would get 1/16th share of suit property. Trial Court found that adoption was valid because that was by virtue of a registered adoption deed and said deed was duly proved. SC confirm the view of Appellate Court that the adopted son i.e. defendant no. 1 would not divest any person in whom the property was vested prior to adoption. However, property which was vested in widow and three daughters would not be disturbed because of adoption of defendant no. 1, which had taken place on 9-2-1971. Thus, grandmother had become absolute owner of 1/4th share and mother of plaintiff had also become an owner of 1/4th share of suit property. HC had committed an error by setting aside the judgment and decree of Appellate Court and the same aws set aside and restore judgment and decree of Appellate Court. Appeal allowed.
