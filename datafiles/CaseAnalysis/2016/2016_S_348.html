Gajanan Dashrath Kharate v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

26 February 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

R. Banumathi, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 183; (2016) 4 SCC 604; AIR 2016 SC 1255; 2016 ALL MR (Cri) 1317; 2016 CRLJ 1900; 2016 (2) MLJ(Crl) 141; 2016 (2) RCR(Criminal) 346; 2016 (3) RLW 2280; 2016(2) SCALE 641; [2016] 2 S.C.R. 852

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Life Imprisonment, Blood, Clothes, Strangulation, Intervene, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Murder - Conviction Challenged - Appellant/accused was held guilty of murder of deceased 'X' by Trial Court - Trial Court convicted accused u/s. 302 of IPC and sentenced to suffer imprisonment for life - HC in appeal confirmed conviction and sentence imposed by Trial Court - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, when an offence like murder is committed in secrecy inside a house, the initial burden to establish the case would undoubtedly be upon the prosecution. S. 106 of the Act, there will be a corresponding burden on the inmates of the house to give cogent explanation as to how the crime was committed. In instant case, accused failed to offer any explanation as to the homicidal death of his father, it is a strong circumstance against the accused that he is responsible for the commission of the crime. Further, when the prosecution has explained the delay in lodging the complaint, prosecution case cannot be doubted on the small delay between the time of occurrence and in registration of first information report. The Trial Court and the HC rightly convicted the accused u/s. 302 of IPC. Hence, impugned conviction and sentence is confirmed. Appeal dismissed.
