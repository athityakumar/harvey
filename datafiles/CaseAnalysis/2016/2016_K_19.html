Bharat Aluminium Company v Kaiser Aluminium Technical Services Incorporation

Supreme Court of India

28 January 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Kurian Joseph, Anil R. Dave, Amitava Roy

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 78; (2016) 4 SCC 126; AIR 2016 SC 1285; 2016 (1) CLT(SC) 103; 2016 (2) CompLJ 503; JT 2016 (2) SC 211; 2016 (2) MLJ 139; 2016(2) SCALE 60; [2016] 1 S.C.R. 364

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Keywords: Arbitration Agreement, Land Acquisition Act, 1894

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration Act, 1940 - Applicable law for arbitration between parties - Parties entered into agreement dt.22-4-1993 with relation to supply of equipment and modernization and up-gradation of production facilities of appellant and when disputes arose, matter was referred to arbitration - Arbitration proceedings were held in England and Tribunal made two awards in favour of respondent and aggrieved by awards, appellant filed applications u/s. 34 of the Act before Trial Court which were dismissed - Aggrieved, appellant filed appeals before HC but the same were dismissed - Hence, instant appeals - Whether parties by agreement, express or implied, have excluded wholly or partly, Part I of the Act.

Held, parties have agreed in expressed terms that the law of arbitration would be English Arbitration Law. Further, u/art. 22 of the Arbitration agreement, it is agreed between the parties that the proper law of contract would be governed by prevailing law of India and in the case of arbitration English law would apply. Further, when once it is found that law governing the arbitration agreement is English Law, then Part I of the Act stands impliedly excluded. Hence, view taken by HC that applications filed by appellant u/s. 34 of the Act are not maintainable against two foreign awards is upheld and appeals are dismissed. Appeals disposed of.

Ratio - As per art.17 of the Arbitration agreement, disputes or claims arising out of or relating to agreements, if not amicably settled by negotiation, the same would be settled by arbitration pursuant to English Arbitration Law and subsequent amendments thereto.

Further, approach in analyzing terms of agreement should be straight and plain but at the same time cohesive and logical.
