T. S. Das and others v Union of India and another

Supreme Court of India

27 October 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Khanwilkar, D.Y. Chandrachud

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 787; JT 2016 (12) SC 38; 2017 (1) MLJ 135; 2016(12) SCALE 46; 2016 (4) UPLBEC 3177

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces

Keywords: Armed Forces, Enrolled, Chief Of The Naval Staff, Indian Navy, Naval Service, Navy Act, 1957, Constitution of India, 1950, Indian Naval Reserve Forces (Discipline) Act, 1939, Indian Fleet Reserve Regulations, Fleet Reserve Act, Naval Ceremonial Conditions of Services and Miscellaneous Regulations, 1964, Navy Ceremonial, Conditions of Service and Miscellaneous Regulations, 1963

Summary: (A) Defence & Security Forces - Navy Act, 1957, ss. 11, 12, 16, 184A, 9 - Indian Fleet Reserve Regulations, regn. 6 - Naval Ceremonial Conditions of Services and Miscellaneous Regulations, 1964, regns. 92, 92(2), 95 - Navy Ceremonial, Conditions of Service and Miscellaneous Regulations, 1963, regns. 268, 269, 279 - Reservist pension -Eligibility - Appellants were appointed as Sailors in Indian Navy before 1973 - Appellants continued for a brief period beyond initial term of 10 years in active service/engagement and discharged without drafting them to Fleet Reserve Services - Appellants filed writ petitions before HC to issue direction to competent Authority to grant special pension u/regn. 95 of the 1964 Regulations - HC directed competent Authority to examine the claim of appellants for grant of special pension - Competent Authority rejected the claim holding that appellants were discharged from service after completion of their initial engagement and were not drafted to the Fleet Reserve, as they were not required - Aggrieved, appellants approached Tribunal, Tribunal held that regn. 95 of the 1964 Regulations was not applicable to appellants who were discharged from service after completion of 10 years of engagement - Hence, instant appeals - Whether appellants were entitled to claim Reservist Pension.

Held, quintessence for grant of Reservist Pension, as per regn. 92 of the 1964 Regulations was completion of prescribed Naval and Reserve qualifying service of 10 years "each". Merely upon completion of 10 years of active service as a Sailor or for that matter continued beyond that period, but falling short of 15 years or qualifying Reserve Service, the concerned Sailor cannot claim benefit u/regn. 92 of the 1964 Regulations for grant of Reservist Pension. None of the appellants were drafted to the Fleet Reserve Service after completion of their active service.Tribunal committed manifest error in overlooking the statutory provisions in the Act and the relevant Regulations framed thereunder, governing the conditions of service of Sailors. The fact that on completion of 10 years of active service, the Sailor could be taken on the Fleet Reserve Service for a further period of 10 years cannot be interpreted to mean that the concerned Sailor had acquired a legal right to join the Fleet Reserve Service or had de jure continued on Fleet Reserve Service for a further 10 years after expiration of the initial term of active service/engagement. There was no provision either in the Act or the Regulations framed thereunder as pressed into service by appellants to suggest that drafting of such Sailors on Fleet Reserve Service was "automatic" after expiration of their active service/enrolment period. However, original appellants might be entitled to get a Special Pension u/regn. 95 of the 1964 Regulations being a separate dispensation for such Sailors, unless discharged by way of punishment u/regn. 279 of the 1963 Regulations. Thus, none of the appellants before Tribunal were entitled for Reservist Pension in terms of regn. 92 of the 1964 Regulations. Appeals disposed of.

(B) Defence & Security Forces - Navy Act, 1957, ss. 11, 12, 16, 184A, 9 - Indian Fleet Reserve Regulations, regn. 6 - Naval Ceremonial Conditions of Services and Miscellaneous Regulations, 1964, regns. 92, 92(2), 95 - Navy Ceremonial, Conditions of Service and Miscellaneous Regulations, 1963, regns. 268, 269 - Special pension - Entitled to - Whether Sailors appointed before 1973 were entitled for a Special Pension, in terms of regn. 95 of the 1964 Regulations.

Held, discretion was vested in Central Govt. to grant Special Pension to such Sailors, who fall within the excepted category. In instant case, cl. (i) of regn. 95 of the 1964 Regulations should come into play, in the backdrop of the policy decision taken by Govt. as enunciated in the notification dt. 3-7-1976. Indisputably, Sailors appointed prior to 3-7-1976, had the option of continuing on Fleet Reserve Service after expiration of their active service/empanelment period. The option to continue on the Fleet Reserve Service could not be offered to appellants and similarly placed Sailors, by dept. after expiration of their empanelment period of 10 years or less than 15 years as the case might be. It is for that reason, such Sailors were simply discharged on expiration of their active service/empanelment period. In other words, on account of discontinuation of the Fleet Reserve establishment of the Indian Navy, in terms of policy dt. 3-7-1976 it had entailed in reducing the strength of establishment of the Indian Navy to that extent. Thus, all Sailors appointed prior to 3-7-1976 and whose tenure of initial active service/empanelment period expired on or after 3-7-1976 might be eligible for a Special Pension u/regn. 95 of the 1964 Regulations, subject, however, to fulfilling other requirements. In that, they had not exercised the option to take discharge on expiry of engagement (as per s. 16 of the 1957 Act) and yet were not and could not be drafted by competent Authority to the Fleet Reserve because of the policy of discontinuing the Fleet Reserve Service w.e.f. 3-7-1976. The cases of such Sailors should be considered by Competent Authority within 3 months for grant of a "Special Pension" from 3 years prior to the date of application made by respective Sailor and release payment after giving adjustment of Gratuity and Death-cum-Retirement-Gratuity (DCRG) already paid to them from arrears. They shall be entitled for interest @ 9% p.a. on arrears till the date of payment. Appeals disposed of.
