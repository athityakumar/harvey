Commissioner of Income Tax v Meghalaya Steels Limited

Supreme Court of India

09 March 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

R.F. Nariman, Kurian Joseph

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 218; (2016) 6 SCC 747; AIR 2016 SC 1309; [2016] 383 ITR 217; JT 2016 (3) SC 117; 2016 (3) MLJ 147; 2016(3) SCALE 192; [2016] 1 S.C.R. 952; [2016] 238 TAXMAN 559

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Export Promotion, Industrial Undertaking, Total Income, Subsidy, Finance Act, 1999, Profits And Gains, Import Entitlements, Expenditure, Exports, Such Profits, Tax Holiday, Income-tax Act, 1961

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income-tax Act, 1961, ss. 80IB, 80IB(4), 80IC - Amount received as subsidy - Deduction disallowed - Justification - Respondent engaged in manufacturing business and on 9-10-2014, respondent submitted its return of income for year 2004-2005 disclosing income after claiming deduction u/s. 80-IB of Act on profits and gains of business of respondent's industrial undertaking - Further, respondent had received subsidies - AO vide order held that amounts received by assessee as subsidies were revenue receipts and did not qualify for deduction u/s. 80-IB(4) of Act, hence, respondent's claim for deduction of amount of on account of three subsidies were disallowed - Further, respondent's appeal before appellant authority was dismissed - However, Tribunal vide order allowed appeal of respondent - HC vide impugned order confirmed order passed by Tribunal and passed order against Revenue - Appellant Revenue contended that subsidies that are allowed to assessee have no close and direct nexus with business of assessee but have close and direct nexus with grants from Govt., hence, respondent did not qualify for deductions u/ss. 80-IB and 80-IC of Act - Whether respondent was eligible for deduction u/ss. 80-IB and 80-IC of Act.

Held, all four subsidies in present case are revenue receipts which are reimbursed to assessee for elements of cost relating to manufacture or sale of their products have direct nexus between profits and gains of industrial undertaking or business, and reimbursement of such subsidies. SC held that so long as profits and gains emanate directly from business itself, fact that immediate source of subsidies is Govt. would make no difference, as said subsidies are only in order to reimburse, wholly or partially, costs actually incurred by assessee in manufacturing and selling of its products. SC held that 'profits and gains' stated u/ss. 80-IB and 80-IC of Act have reference to net profit and net profit can only be calculated by deducting from sale price of article all elements of cost which go into manufacturing or selling it. Hence, it is clear that profits and gains are derived from business of assessee, namely profits arrived at after deducting manufacturing cost and selling costs reimbursed to assessee by Govt. concerned. SC relied on earlier HC held that subsidies were inseparably connected with business carried on by assessee, hence, assessee is entitled to claim deduction u/ss. 80-IB and 80-IC of Act. Appeals dismissed.

Ratio - In order to claim u/ss. 80-IB and 80-IC of Act, there should be direct nexus between profits and gains and industrial undertaking or business and such nexus cannot be only incidental.
