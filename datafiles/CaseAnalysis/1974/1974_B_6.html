Babubhai Muljibhai Patel v Nandlal Khodidas Barot and Others

Supreme Court of India

17 September 1974

Case Analysis

Bench

Hans Raj Khanna, M. Hameedullah Beg, V.R. Krishna Iyer

Where Reported

1974 Indlaw SC 251; (1974) 2 SCC 706; AIR 1974 SC 2105; [1975] 2 S.C.R. 71

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 226 - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, s. 141 - Gujarat Municipalities Act, 1964, s. 36 - Petition u/art. 226 - Power of High Court to go into disputed questions of fact and applicability of s. 141, CPC, 1908 - Procedure to be followed while summoning of parties for cross-examination - Necessity to mention ground for no confidence motion - HC is not deprived of its jurisdiction to entertain a petition u/art. 226 merely because in considering petitioner's right of relief, questions of fact may fail to be determined - Can summon, if necessary, as per its discretion - Nothing in language of s. 36 which makes it necessary to specify a ground - Appeal dismissed.
