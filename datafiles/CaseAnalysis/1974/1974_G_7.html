Ganpat v Returning Officer & Ors

Supreme Court of India

04 December 1974

Case Analysis

Bench

A. Alagiriswami, R.S. Sarkaria

Where Reported

1974 Indlaw SC 76; (1975) 1 SCC 589; AIR 1975 SC 420; [1975] 2 S.C.R. 923

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Election; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Untouchability, Scheduled Caste

Summary: Representation of the People Act, 1951, ss. 5(a), 36 and 100 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 115 - Constitution, arts. 330 and 332 - Reservation of seats for Scheduled Castes - (A) Members of SC converted to Buddhism, position of -They cease to be members of SC - Proof of conversion - Absence of - (B) Election questioned on ground that the elected candidate was not a member of a SC as he had ceased to be a Hindu and had become a Buddhist - (1) Failure to object at time of scrutiny of nomination papers - (2) Evidence and proof of candidate's religion - Born Hindu - Held, in absence of such proof they continue to be members of Scheduled Castes and their election to reserved seats cannot be questioned - No bar to raise objection m election petition , but such failure would considerably weaken the objection - Proof of change of religion necessary -

Appeal Dismissed.
