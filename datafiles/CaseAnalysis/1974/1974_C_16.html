Phiroze Bamanji Desai v Chandrakant N. Patel and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 February 1974

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Bhagwati, P.K. Goswami

Where Reported

1974 Indlaw SC 282; (1974) 1 SCC 661; AIR 1974 SC 1059; [1974] 3 S.C.R. 267

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Bombay Rents, Hotel and Lodging House Rates Control Act, 1947, ss.13(1)(g), 13(2), 29(3) - CPC, 1908 - Tenancy - (A) Scope of revisional powers of High Court u/s. 29(3) of 1947 Act - Finding of lower Court u/s. 13(1)(g) as to bona fide requirement and greater hardship - Interference by HC, by reappreciating evidence - (B) Reasonable and bona fide requirement - Whrether juridical possession of other premises by landlord can be considered in determining need of landlord - Held, HC cannot reassess the value of the evidence and interfere with a finding of fact merely because it thinks that the appreciation of the evidence by the lower Court is wrong - Wwhat is necessary to be considered is not whether the landlord is juridically in possession of other premises, but whether they were available to him for occupation - Appeal allowed.
