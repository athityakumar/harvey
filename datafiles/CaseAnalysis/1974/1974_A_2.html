General Manager, South Central Railway v A. V. R. Siddhanti and Others

Supreme Court of India

30 January 1974

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Sarkaria, V.R. Krishna Iyer

Where Reported

1974 Indlaw SC 3; (1974) SCC (L&S) 290; (1974) 4 SCC 335; AIR 1974 SC 1755; 1974 (28) FLR 231; 1974 LabIC 587; 1974 (1) LLJ 312; [1974] 3 S.C.R. 207

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Equal opportunity, Equality of opportunity

Summary: Constitution, arts. 14, 16, 136 and 226 - CPC, 1908, O. 1, r. 9 - (A) Grain shop department of Railway - Personnel drawn from 3 sources - Subsequent integration into single cadre - Personnel from category (i) always treated as distinct unit while personnel from categories (ii) and (iii) becoming members of same class and governed by same conditions of service - Winding up of deptt. - Staff employed in other permanent departments - Constitutional validity of classification of grain-shop staff for seniority etc. in permanent departments - Resurrection of abandoned plea in Supreme Court - Permisssibility - (B) Plea of laches - (C) Procedure - Parties - Partition challenging policy decisions of Railway regarding seniority - Held, differential treatment of the personnel belonging to category (i) rested on a sound rational basis but to categories (ii) and (iii) directions were arbitrary and violative of arts. 14 and 16 - Appellants cannot be permitted to resurrect in the SC the same objection which they had apparently abandoned in the HC - Employees were at the most proper parties but not necessary parties - Appeal Dismissed.
