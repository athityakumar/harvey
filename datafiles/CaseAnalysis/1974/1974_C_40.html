Commissioner of Wealth Tax, U.P., and Another v Kundan Lal Behari Lal

Supreme Court of India

05 September 1974

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Jaganmohan Reddy, P.K. Goswami

Where Reported

1974 Indlaw SC 202; (1975) SCC (Tax) 469; (1975) 4 SCC 844; AIR 1976 SC 1150; [1975] 99 ITR 581

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Failure to file returns, Interpretation, Word issued u/s. 18(2A) of the Act

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Wealth-Tax Act, 1957, ss. 18(2A) - Indian Income-tax (Amendment) Act, 1959 - Word issued u/s. 18(2A) of the Act - Interpretation.

Held, question on which the HC decided and which was the only question contended for admitting the instant petition was that the word 'issued' occurring in s. 18(2A) of the Act means 'served' - In Banarsi Debi v. Income-tax Officer, Calcutta, SC observed that the expressions 'issued' and 'served' are used as inter-changeable terms and in the legislative practice of our country they are sometimes used to convey the same idea. Accordingly, it was held that the word 'issued' was not used in the narrow sense of 'sent' but that the said expression had received, before the 1959 Act, 1959, a clear judicial interpretation. Petition dismissed.
