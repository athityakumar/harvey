Bhagwan Dutt v Kamla Devi And Anr.

Supreme Court of India

17 October 1974

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Sarkaria, Y.V. Chandrachud, A.C. Gupta

Where Reported

1974 Indlaw SC 243; (1975) 2 SCC 386; (1975) SCC (Cr) 563; AIR 1975 SC 83; 1975 CRLJ 40; 1974 CrLR(SC) 621; 1978 HLR 742; 1975 MLJ 81; [1975] 2 S.C.R. 483

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Family & Personal

Summary: Family and Personal -Hindu Adoptions And Maintenance Act, 1956, s.23 -Cr PC, 1898, s.488 -Maintenance -Power of magistrate -To consider Income of wife in determining the maintenance payable to her -(A)Whether a wife can file an application for maintenance u/s.488,CrPC,1973 even if she can maintain herself? -In granting maintenance u/s.488,CrPC,1973 to a wife 'her personal income also can be considered' may Prima-facie appear to be against the language of the section because the condition "unable to maintain itself" appearing therein attaches itself only to child and not to wife -But that condition has application only in considering the maintainability of a petition filed u/s.488, CrPC,1973 - Held, A wife can file a petition under that section irrespective of the question whether she is able or unable to maintain herself. But on her application at the time of the granting of monthly allowance to her there is nothing prohibiting the Court from considering whether she can maintain herself with her own income and if she can, granting her nothing by way of allowance - (B) Whether there is any thing in s.488,CrPC,1973 which is inconsistent with s .23 or any other provisions of the Act of 1956? - s.488,CrPC,1973 is intended to serve a social purpose - It provides a machinery for summary enforcement of the moral obligations of a man towards his wife and children so that they may not, out of sheer destitution become a hazard to the well-being of orderly society - As against this, s. 23 and other provisions of the Act relating to fixation of the rate of allowance, provide for the enforcement of the rights of Hindu wives or dependents under their personal law- Held, there is nothing in s.488,CrPC,1973 which is inconsistent with s .23 of the Act of 1956 - appeal allowed.
