State of Punjab v Bhagat Ram

Supreme Court of India

09 October 1974

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Ray, Kuttyil Kurien Mathew, A. Alagiriswami

Where Reported

1974 Indlaw SC 141; (1975) SCC (L&S) 18; (1975) 1 SCC 155; AIR 1974 SC 2335; 1974 LabIC 1442; [1975] 2 S.C.R. 370; 1975 SLJ 88; 1975 (1) SLR 2; 1974 UJ 664

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 311 - Respondent filed suit for a declaration that his dismissal was illegal as statement of copies not served to him - Whether State gave respondent a reasonable opportunity as contemplated by art. 311? - Meaning of a reasonable opportunity of showing cause against action proposed to be taken is that Govt. servant is afforded a reasonable opportunity to defend himself against charges on which inquiry is held - Unless statements are given to Govt. servant, he will not be able to have effective and useful cross-examination - It is unjust and unfair to deny Govt. servant copies of statements of witnesses examined - Held, a synopsis does not satisfy requirements of giving Govt. servant a reasonable opportunity of showing cause against action proposed to be taken - Appeal dismissed.
