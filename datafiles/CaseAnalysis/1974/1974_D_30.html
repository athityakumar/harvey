Dhondiba Gundu Pomaje & Ors. v State Of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

17 September 1974

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Jaganmohan Reddy, P.K. Goswami

Where Reported

1974 Indlaw SC 144; (1975) SCC (Cr) 93; (1975) SCC (Cr) 793; (1976) 1 SCC 162; AIR 1976 SC 1151; 1976 CRLJ 856; [1975] 2 S.C.R. 66

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure 1898, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898 s. 421 - Constitution of India, 1950 art. 361 - Conviction - Dismissal - Challenged - Appellant was convicted by Trial court and appeal before HC had been summarily dismissed u/s. 421 of CrPC - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, HC does vest a power u/s. 421 of CrPC to dismiss an appeal summarily but it can do so only on a perusal of the petition and the copy of the judgment. Under Constitution any person aggrieved by an order of the HC can petition to instant court u/art. 136 for special leave, it is not only necessary but having regard to the long series of decisions beginning as far back as 1953 onwards which discourages this practice of dismissal by one word 'dismissed', the HC should at least have given some reasons why no arguable case is made out on a perusal of those documents. Therefore, it is contended before us that arguable points do arise in this case in support of which the statement made in instant petition has been read to us and we are not in a position to say that an arguable case does not arise. Thus, we would have been able to do so even if we had the slightest inkling in the order of the HC and in the absence of any reasons what has been happening in many cases is that special leave is admitted, and after hearing the appeal if this Court has come to the conclusion that the conviction is valid, it has held that the dismissal by the HC is justified, but this method, reverses the process and imposes unnecessary burden on this Court. Hence, order of HC is set aside and appeal is remanded back to HC for hearing for admission and disposal in accordance with law. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Long avoidable delay thus ensues during which the person convicted entertains a doubt about his conviction and has to suffer the anxiety caused thereby
