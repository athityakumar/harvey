Bava Hajee Hamsa and Others v State of Kerala

Supreme Court of India

21 February 1974

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Sarkaria, M. Hameedullah Beg, Y.V. Chandrachud

Where Reported

1974 Indlaw SC 437; (1974) 4 SCC 479; (1974) SCC (Cr) 515; AIR 1974 SC 902; 1974 CRLJ 755; 1974 CRLR 317

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: CrPC, 1898, ss. 286 and 423 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 3 - (A) Criminal procedure - (1) Powers of High Court in appeal against acquittal - To review evidence and arrive at its own conclusion - Tests to be applied for determination - (2) Duty of prosecution - Examination of eye-witnesses to occurrence - (B) Appreciation of evidence - (1) Duty of trial Court - (2) Applicability of maxim 'Falsus in uno falsus in omnibus' - (A) Held, to consider every matter on record having a bearing on the question of fact and the reasons given by the Court below and HC must express its reasons as to why acquittal is not justified and if two alternative conclusions , it should not disturb there reasonably possible findings of trial court - Held, unsound to lay down as a general rule that every witness must be examined even though his evidence may not be very material or even if it is known that he has been won over or terrorised - (B) To be guided by the compass of probabilities along the rock-ribbed contours of the case converging on the heart of the matter - Maxim should not be mechanically applied in India - Appeal Dismissed.
