Smt. Bela Das and Others v Samarendra Nath Bose

Supreme Court of India

11 December 1974

Case Analysis

Bench

N.L. Untwalia, Kuttyil Kurien Mathew, P.N. Bhagwati

Where Reported

1974 Indlaw SC 512; (1975) 1 SCC 644; AIR 1975 SC 398; 1975 RCJ 141; [1975] 2 S.C.R. 1004

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - Bihar Buildings (Lease Rent and Eviction) Control Act, 1947, ss. 11-A and 2(d) - Suit for eviction - On ground of non-payment of rent, breach of conditions of tenancy and on account of their bona fide personal requirements of suit premises - Whether hearing of suit ex-parte was legal and valid under act, when landlord-tenant relationship had been denied? - Held, High Court had fallen into an error of law in applying ratio of Full Bench decision of HC, Mahabir Ram v Shiva Shankar Prasad, to facts of instant case - Defence set up by defendant that he was not tenant but tenant was Liberty and Co. was a mere pretence - Defendant was carrying on business in assumed name of Liberty and Co. which was not any legal entity or a person different from defendant - In instant case plea of defendant has been that plaintiffs being landlords of suit premises for a moiety of share could not alone claim a decree for eviction against him - Such a plea set up by defendant to resist suit for eviction was a plea qua-tenant and not de-hors - Defendant's statements in his written statement as also in his rejoinder to plaintiffs petition u/s. 11-A of Act that defendant had admitted that he was paying rent to plaintiffs and had recognised them to be their landlords - Suit for eviction was rightly decreed - Order set aside - Appeal allowed.
