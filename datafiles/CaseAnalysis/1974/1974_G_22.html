Government Of Mysore & Ors v J. V. Bhat, Etc. Etc

Supreme Court of India

14 October 1974

Case Analysis

Bench

A. Alagiriswami, P. Jaganmohan Reddy, M. Hameedullah Beg

Where Reported

1974 Indlaw SC 217; (1975) 1 SCC 110; AIR 1975 SC 596; [1975] 2 S.C.R. 407

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Mysore Slum Areas (Improvement and clearance) Act 1958

Summary: Land & property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894 ss. 3, 9, 12, 12(1) (a) - Constitution of India, 1950 art. 19(1)(f) - Notification - challenged - Notifications were made u/s. 3 and declaration u/s.9 of the Act and notification by Govt. u/s. 12 of the Act for acquisition of certain lands - Respondents/appellants filed writ before HC challenging notifications which struck down said notifications as violating art.19(1)(g) of Constitution and s. 12(1)(b) of the Act was violating art.14 of Constitution - Hence, instant appeals.

Held, power u/s. 12(1) (a) of the Act would be relatable to this purpose, but that section also enables acquisition of lands to rehabilitate slum dwellers. Such rehabilitation may not be in the original slum area itself and by its very nature a slum area is likely to be over-crowded and in redeveloping it not enough land may be available to house all persons formerly living in the cleared area. Therefore, it may be necessary to acquire other lands for the purpose of rehabilitating them and the power u/s. 12(1)(b) of the Act is relatable to the purpose of slum clearance itself. Furthermore, that power is nothing more than the power available to a State Govt. under the Act. As the section itself provides for calling upon the owner or any other person interested in the land to show cause why it should not be acquired, and validity u/s. 12(1)(a) of the Act has been upheld by the HC and instant court agree with the HC. Appeals allowed.
