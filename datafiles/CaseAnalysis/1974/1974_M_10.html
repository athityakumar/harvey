Ajantha Transports (P) Ltd. Coimbatore Etc. v M/S. T. V. K. Transports, Pulampattti, Coimbatore,Distt.

Supreme Court of India

24 September 1974

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Hameedullah Beg, Hans Raj Khanna, V.R. Krishna Iyer

Where Reported

1974 Indlaw SC 147; (1975) 1 SCC 55; AIR 1975 SC 123; 1975 (2) MLJ(SC) 16; [1975] 2 S.C.R. 166

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Civil Procedure; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Civil Procedure - Constitution - Practice & Procedure - Motor Vehicles Act, 1939, s. 47 - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 115 - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 14 and 19(1)(g) - (A) Is recent grant or possession of another permit for a stage carriage permit, either by itself or in conjunction with other facts and circumstances, a relevant consideration in either refusing or granting a permit? - (1) If so what weight to be attached to it in assessment of comparative merits of rival claims? - Held, relevance of previous possession or grant of a permit appear only when other facts and circumstances, connecting it with and showing either adverse or beneficial effects of its impact, in a particular case, on interests of public, are shown to exist - Unless and until these other facts and circumstances, indicating nexus with public interest appear, such a fact by itself should not affect grant - Weight to be attached to such a consideration will depend upon totality of all such facts and circumstances viewed in a proper perspective - (i) Does High Court's judgment or order in any of cases dealt with by it call for interference u/art. 136? - Held, these cases do not justify interference in exercise of its power u/art. 136 - Powers conferred by Act, including those u/s. 47, are limited by Constitutional guarantees to citizens - Manner in which a grant would affect fundamental rights of citizens to be judged by that standard - Where powers to be exercised by organs of State are not clearly defined, Constitutional guarantees contained in art. 14 and 19 (1) (g) would limit scope and regulate exercise of such powers - Exercise of permit-issuing power u/s. 47 must confirm with question of public interest - Consideration of matters which are not relevant to or a existing relevant ground if unjustifiably ignored will vitiate grant of a permit u/s. 47 and will be covered by s. 115, CPC - Power to grant permits u/s. 47 of Act is limited to purposes for which it is meant to be exercised - Considerations which are relevant for applying arts. 14 and 19(1)(g) could not be foreign to scope of s. 47(1) (a) which is fairly wide - By itself, a recent grant or possession of permit is neither a qualification nor a disqualification - In cases where everything else is absolutely equal as between two applicants an application of principle of equality of opportunity may enable a person who is not a fresh grantee to get a preference - Relevancy or otherwise of one or more grounds of grant or refusal of a permit could be a jurisdictional matter - Civil Appeal 1402 of 1974 allowed - Civil Appeal 2254 of 1968 dismissed - Civil Appeals 1481-83 of 1970 dismissed.
