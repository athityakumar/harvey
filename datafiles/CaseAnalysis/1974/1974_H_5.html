Hans Raj Kehar and Others v State of Uttar Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 December 1974

Case Analysis

Bench

Hans Raj Khanna, A.N. Ray, P. Jaganmohan Reddy, P.K. Goswami

Where Reported

1974 Indlaw SC 77; (1975) 1 SCC 40; AIR 1975 SC 389; [1975] 2 S.C.R. 916

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14 and 19(1)(g) - Motor Vehicles Act, 1939, s. 43 A - Whether s. 43 A, inserted in Motor Vehicles Act, 1939 by U P Act No. 25 of 1972, and notification issued under that section is valid? - Held, yes - S. 43 A shows that object of legislature in inserting it in Motor Vehicles Act, 1939 was to make it easier to secure permits in respect of non-nationalized routes - It seeks to simplify procedure for this purpose - It has accordingly been provided that in case of non-nationalized routes if State Govt. is of opinion that it is in public interest to grant permits to all eligible applicants, it may by notification in Gazette issue a direction accordingly - No valid basis for the inference that if there is no limit on the number of permits for intra-region routes, limit on the number of permits for inter-region routes would have to be imposed - The object of the impugned notification is to liberalize the issue of permits and the Court fail to see as to how such a liberal measure can have the effect of introducing strictness or stringency in the matter of grant of permits for inter-region routes - Assuming that a different rule is applicable in the matter of inter-region routes, the differentiation is based upon reasonable classification - It is nobody's case that the impugned provision brings about discrimination in the matter of grant of permits between applicants belonging to the same class - The argument about the impugned provision being violative of art. 14 is wholly untenable - Contention that impugned notification is violative of rights of appellants u/art. 19(1)(g) is devoid of force - There is nothing in notification which prevents appellants from acquiring, holding and disposing of their property or prevents them from practising any profession or from carrying on any occupation trade or business - No right is guaranteed to any private party by art. 19 of Constitution of carrying on any trade and business without competition from other eligible persons - Appeal dismissed.
