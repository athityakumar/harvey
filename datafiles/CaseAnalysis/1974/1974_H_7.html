 Haradhan Saha and another v State of West Bengal and Others

Supreme Court of India

21 August 1974

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Ray, P. Jaganmohan Reddy, Kuttyil Kurien Mathew, M. Hameedullah Beg, A. Alagiriswami

Where Reported

1974 Indlaw SC 360; (1974) SCC (Cr) 816; (1975) 3 SCC 198; AIR 1974 SC 2154; 1974 CRLJ 1479; [1975] 1 S.C.R. 778

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Constitution - Maintenance of Internal Security Act, 1971 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 19, 21, 22 (5) - Preventive Detention - Validity - Instant petitions were filed challenging constitutional validity of the Act on ground of violation of arts. 14, 19, 21 of the Constitution and also that the Act had not laid down just procedure for giving effect to art. 22(5).

Held, Act does not suffer from any constitutional infirmity and detention orders in instant cases came to be passed to prevent the likelihood of such acts prejudicial to the maintenance of supplies essential to the community. Hence, petitions are dismissed. Petitions disposed of.

Ratio - Art.14 of the Constitution is inapplicable because preventive detention and prosecution are not synonymous and their purposes, authorities, nature of proceedings are different.
