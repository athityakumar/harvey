Kanwar Lal Gupta v Amar Nath Chawla and Others

Supreme Court of India

03 October 1974

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Bhagwati, R.S. Sarkaria

Where Reported

1974 Indlaw SC 247; (1975) 3 SCC 646; AIR 1975 SC 308; [1975] 2 S.C.R. 259

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Keywords: Election Expenses, Carbon Copies

Summary: Election - Representation of the People Act, 1951 - Corrupt practice - Excess expenditure - Prescribed limit - Invalidate - Five candidates contested in election along with petitioner and respondent - Respondent was declared elected - Petitioner contended that respondent was guilty of corrupt practice set out in s. 123(6) of Act on excessive expenditure incurred - Petitioner filed election petition challenging validity of election of respondent was dismissed as grounds sought to be invalidated was not established - Hence instant appeal - Whether respondent was proved of corrupt practice set out in s. 123(6) of Act - Held, no doubt that respondent did not maintain cash-book or ledger - Carbon copies of bills show names of parties ,materials and hire charges - Respondent failed to show expenditure in connection with public meeting - Absence of sales tax in bill was clear indication that cost of paper was not included in amount of bill - No doubt that affidavit was made by witness voluntarily to support case of first respondent and it was clear from evidence of witness that Press carried out printing work for respondent - Total expenditure proved to be incurred by respondent was in excess of prescribed limit and it would be sufficient to invalidate election on ground of corrupt practice defined in s. 123(6) of Act - Appeal allowed.
