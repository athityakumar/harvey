Shanti Kumar R. Canji v Home Insurance Company of New York

Supreme Court of India

24 July 1974

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Ray, Kuttyil Kurien Mathew

Where Reported

1974 Indlaw SC 379; (1974) 2 SCC 387; AIR 1974 SC 1719; 1974 (29) FLR 239; [1975] 1 S.C.R. 550; 1974 (2) SLR 538; 1974 UJ 456

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Letters Patent of Bombay High Court, cls. 15, 12 and 13 - An order allowing amendment - Whether it is a judgment? - Held, 'Judgment' means a decree in a suit by which right of parties in suit are determined - An order of transfer of a suit filed in High Court to be tried in its extra ordinary original civil jurisdiction was a judgment within meaning of cl. 15 of Letters Patent - Judgment must be final pronouncement which puts an end to proceeding so far as court dealing with it is concerned - Secondly, judgment must involve determination of some right or liability though it may not be necessary that there must be a decision on merits - An amendment, if allowed will relate to date of institution of suit - HC relied on case where held, substitution of a company for a firm would be a change of a substantial character affecting right of a party - Right to claim that an introduction of a cause of action by amendment is barred by limitation is founded on immunity from a liability - Amendment order is not purely of discretion - Even with regard to discretionary orders Appellate Court can interfere where order is insupportable in law or is unjust - High Court rightly found that there were no special circumstances to entitle the appellant to introduce by amending to introduce new claim - Appeal dismissed.
