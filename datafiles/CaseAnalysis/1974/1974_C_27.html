Amrit Lal Berry and another v Collector of Central Excise, New Delhi and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 December 1974

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Hameedullah Beg, V.R. Krishna Iyer, P.K. Goswami

Where Reported

1974 Indlaw SC 227; (1975) SCC (L&S) 412; (1975) 4 SCC 714; AIR 1975 SC 538; 1975 LabIC 363; 1975 (1) LLJ 144; [1975] 2 S.C.R. 960; 1975 (1) SLR 153

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Constitution, arts. 16, 16(1), 32 and 226 - (A) Petition u/art. 32 - Alleged violation of fundamental right u/art. 16 (1) - Evidenceand proof - (B) Laches - Effect - Promotions made in 1959 on basis of rules made m 1959 , challenged in 1971- (C) Mandamus - Matter relating to promotion - No assertion that any representation was made against violation of petitioner's right u/art.16(1) - Held, petitioner must disclose how his fundamental right has been infringed by a particular rule or decision or its application - Petition is liable to be dismissed on ground of inordinate delay - Rule that a demand for justice and its refusal must precede the filing of petition asking for a writ of mandamus would operate against the petitioner - Petition Dismissed.
