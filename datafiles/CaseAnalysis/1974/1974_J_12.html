Jainal Sk v District Magistrate, West Dinajpur, and Others

Supreme Court of India

26 November 1974

Case Analysis

Bench

N.L. Untwalia

Where Reported

1974 Indlaw SC 319; (1974) SCC (Cr) 929; (1975) 3 SCC 321; AIR 1975 SC 229; 1975 CRLJ 275; 1975 CRLR 75; 1975 UJ 27

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Criminal - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 32 - Detention - Validity of - Held, In view of statement made in counter-affidavit, it is manifest that mere absence of petitioner's name in FIR cannot justify inference that ground of his detention is non est - In a case of this kind, investigation as to truthfulness of facts forming basis of grounds of detention cannot be embarked - Exercising peripheral jurisdiction in matters of preventive detention this Court is unable to accept contention of non-naming of detenu in FIR - Appeal dismissed.
