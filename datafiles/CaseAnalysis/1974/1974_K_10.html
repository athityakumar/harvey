Kamal Pathak v District Magistrate, Burdwan and Others

Supreme Court of India

20 December 1974

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Bhagwati, Kuttyil Kurien Mathew, N.L. Untwalia

Where Reported

1974 Indlaw SC 301; (1975) SCC (Cr) 224; (1975) 3 SCC 763; AIR 1975 SC 1145; 1975 CRLJ 937; 1975 CRLR 127; 1975 UJ 115

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Prejudicial to maintenance of supplies and services essential

Summary: Administrative - Practice & Procedure - Maintenance of Internal Security Act, 1971, ss. 3(1), (32) - Detention order - Prejudicial to maintenance of supplies and services essential - Legality - Petitioner filed instant challenging the validity of an order of detention passed u/s. 3(1) r/w s. 3(2) of the Act - Petitioner contended that he was denied an opportunity of making effective representation.

Held, there is nothing on the record to show that there was before the District Magistrate any material other than the grounds of detention served on the petitioner. District Magistrate did not rely on the circumstance that the petitioner was a dangerous person for the purpose of reaching his subjective satisfaction as regards the necessity for the detention of the petitioner. District Magistrate referred to the fact of the petitioner being a dangerous person in his affidavit in reply merely by way of giving a reason why the witnesses were not willing to come forward to give evidence against him. That was not a circumstance taken into account by him for the purpose of arriving at this subjective satisfaction. In any event, the 2 incidents set out in the grounds of detention clearly suggested that the petitioner was a dangerous person. No inference could, therefore, be drawn that there was any other material before the District Magistrate which was not disclosed to the petitioner. Petition dismissed.
