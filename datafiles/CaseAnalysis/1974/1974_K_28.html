Rahim Khan v Khurshid Ahmed and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 August 1974

Case Analysis

Bench

V.R. Krishna Iyer, D.G. Palekar, P.N. Bhagwati

Where Reported

1974 Indlaw SC 372; (1974) 2 SCC 660; AIR 1975 SC 290; [1975] 1 S.C.R. 643

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Keywords: Purity, Circulation

Summary: Representation of the People Act, 1951, ss. 83, 87, 99(a)(ii), 123, 123(1), 123(2),123(3) and 123(4) - Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 3, 47 and 60 - (A) Election - (1) Powers of appellate Court in election cases - Principles laid down in 1973 INDLAW SC 131 and 1973 INDLAW SC 290 reiterated - (2) Setting aside election - Corrupt practice - Nature and burden of proof - (3) Corrupt practice of bribery u/s.123(1) - What constitutes - (4) Precedents - Decisions on credibility of witness - How far binding - (5) Particulars of corrupt practice - Names of all witnesses if should be mentioned - (6) Copies of the offending hand-bill - Appellant got printed and distributed among his constituents exhorting the Muslim voters to support his candidature for his Islamic way - Applicability of provisions of s.123 - (7) Duty of High Court in naming all collaborators in corrupt practice and procedure to be followed before naming them in judgment - (B) Evidence Act, 1872 - (1) Opinion as to handwriting when relevant - (2) Credibility of witness - Negative evidence is ordinarily no good to disprove the factum of meetings - Held, (A) Trial Court's finding will be reversed only when it is shown to be perverse or wrong - The burden is heavy on him who assails an election, which has been concluded - Held, financial help given by a candidate to a fellow candidate must be to induce the latter not to withdraw from the election - Precedents on legal propositions are useful and binding, but the variety of circumstances and peculiar features of each case cannot be identical with those in another and judgments of Courts on when and why a certain witness has been accepted or rejected can hardly serve as binding decisions - The name of every witness need not be mentioned in the particulars except where his name becomes a necessary item of particulars - Held that offending hand-bills amounted corrupt practice falling u/s-ss.(3) and (4) but not u/s-s.(2) of s.123 - Duty of HC pointed out - (B) Opinion evidence is hearsay and becomes relevant only if the condition laid down in s. 47 of the Evidence Act is first proved - Held, to disbelieve a witness because he came without summons as the trial Court has done is altogether wrong - Appeal Dismissed.
