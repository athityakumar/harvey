Balak Ram and another v State Of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

16 August 1974

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.V. Chandrachud, Hans Raj Khanna

Where Reported

1974 Indlaw SC 121; (1974) SCC (Cr) 837; (1975) 3 SCC 219; AIR 1974 SC 2165; 1974 CRLJ 1486; 1974 CRLR 530; [1975] 1 S.C.R. 753

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Defence & Security Forces; Indirect Tax; Service

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code- Ss. 302 and 337 r/w. 149 and 148 - Criminal Procedure Code - S. 144 - a) Whether the High Court was justified in upholding the death sentence passed by the Sessions Court - Held, HC was justified in upholding the conviction by the Sessions Court - The HC must however must examine the entire evidence independent of the Sessions Court - b) Whether the HC had sufficient reason to interfere with the order of acquittal passed by the Sessions Court? - c) If two views of evidence are reasonably possible, the HC ought to interfere with the order of acquittal passed by the Trial Court - Appeal dismissed - All convictions confirmed.
