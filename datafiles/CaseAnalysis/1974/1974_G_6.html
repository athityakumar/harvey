Ganga Bai v Vijay Kumar and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 April 1974

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.V. Chandrachud, M. Hameedullah Beg

Where Reported

1974 Indlaw SC 138; (1974) 2 SCC 393; AIR 1974 SC 1126; [1974] 3 S.C.R. 882

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, O. 34, r. 4, O. 41, r. 2, s. 96(1) - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Right of suit and right of appeal - Whether appeal filed by defendants 2 and 3 was maintainable as being mere finding? - Appeal filed by defendant 2 and 3 was directed not against any part of preliminary decree but against mere finding by Trial court - Distinction between right to suit and right to appeal - Inherent right in every person to bring suit of civil nature unless barred by statute - Right to appeal inheres in no one so it should have clear authority of law - Held, appeal lies only as against a decree or as against an order passed under rules from which an appeal is expressly allowed by O. 43, r. 1 - No appeal can lie against mere finding so appeal filed by defendants 2 and 3 against that finding was not maintainable - Appeal dismissed.
