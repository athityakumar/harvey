Firm Of Pratapchand Nopaji v Firm Of Kotrike Venkatta Setty & Sons Etc

Supreme Court of India

12 December 1974

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Hameedullah Beg, V.R. Krishna Iyer, P.K. Goswami

Where Reported

1974 Indlaw SC 182; (1975) 2 SCC 208; AIR 1975 SC 1223; [1975] 3 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Wager, Gaming Act, 1845, Bombay Forward Contracts Control Act, 1947, Oil Seeds (Forward Contract Prohibition) Order 1943

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Contract Act, 1872, ss. 23, 30, 222 and 224 - Bombay Forward Contracts Control Act, 1947 - Oil Seeds (Forward Contracts Prohibition) Order, 1943 - Badla transactions by agent with third parties - Agent sued principal for recovery of losses incurred on such transactions - (A) Determination of maintainability of suit in light of s. 23 of Contract Act - (B) Whether agent incurring losses on such contracts can claim indemnification from principal u/s. 222 as per s. 224 of Contract Act? - Held, mere agreement to engage in speculation on rise and fall in prices of goods is not a wagering contract - Cannot claim indemnification and further held that in as much as both sides to unlawful agreements are in 'pari delicto', decrees for costs awarded to defendants were set aside - Appeal dismissed.
