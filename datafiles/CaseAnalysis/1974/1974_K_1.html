K. Balasubramania Chetty v N. M. Sambandamoorthy Chetty

Supreme Court of India

20 December 1974

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Bhagwati, Kuttyil Kurien Mathew, N.L. Untwalia

Where Reported

1974 Indlaw SC 513; (1975) 1 SCC 242; AIR 1975 SC 818; [1975] 3 S.C.R. 91

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act 1939, ss. 64B - Motor Vehicle Rules, (1940), r. 155A - Regional Transport Authority, after considering applications, made an order granting permit to respondent, though on marking according to provisions contained in cl. (3) of r. 155A, respondent obtained only 7.40 marks as against 9.00 marks obtained by appellant - State Transport Authority preferred respondent to appellant was that respondent was a single bus operator, while appellant was a multi bus operator - Respondent thereupon preferred a revision application to High Court u/s. 64B of Act and held that State Transport Appellate Tribunal had acted with material irregularity in exercise of its jurisdiction in preferring appellant to respondent for grant of permit - Whether it is valid? - Held, Judge was in error in holding that same standard was not applied by State Transport Appellate Tribunal in comparing history sheets of appellant and respondent - History sheet of appellant related only to his performance as stage carriage operator and entire history sheet was before State Transport Appellate Tribunal and it showed that appellant had a clean record - On other hand, respondent had two history sheets, one relating to his performance as stage carriage operator and other relating to his performance as lorry operator and both history sheets showed adverse entries - It can hardly be disputed that this comparison with reference to past performance of appellant and respondent was relevant to question as to who between two should be selected for grant of permit - It may be that history sheet of respondent as lorry operator related to a period of ten years while that of appellant as a stage carriage operator covered a shorter period, but that cannot be helped - Comparison has to be made on basis of available material and if history sheet of respondent, which may be for a longer period, shows that past performance of respondent was not satisfactory while history sheet of appellant, though for a shorter period, shows that he has had a clean record of performance, that would certainly be a relevant circumstance to be taken into account - Further, Judge overstepped limits of his revisional jurisdiction and treated revision application before him as if it were an appeal - That was clearly impermissible as revisional jurisdiction of HC u/s. 64B is as severely restricted as that u/s. 115 of Civil Procedure Code, 1908 and it is only where there is a jurisdictional error or illegality or material irregularity in exercise of jurisdiction that HC can interfere u/s. 64B with an order made by State Transport Appellate Tribunal - Set aside judgment of Judge - Appeal allowed.
