Assistant Custodian, E.P. and Others v Brij Kishore Agarwala and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 October 1974

Case Analysis

Bench

A. Alagiriswami, Kuttyil Kurien Mathew

Where Reported

1974 Indlaw SC 111; (1975) 1 SCC 21; AIR 1974 SC 2325; [1975] 2 S.C.R. 359

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Evacuee Property & Displaced Persons

Summary: Land & Property - Administration Of Evacuee Property Act, 1950, ss. 2(d), 2(f), 7, 8 and 58(2) - Uttar Pradesh Administration of Evacuee Property Ordinance, 1949, ss. 2(c)(i) and 2(c)(ii), 5 and 6 - Displaced Persons (Compensation And Rehabilitation) Act, 1954, s. 12 - (A) Vesting of evacuee property - Effect on validity of acquisition by notification, of such property u/s. 12 of 1954 Act - (B) Certain person migrated to Pakistan in 1948 - She came back in 1962 to India and sold her property - Whether she was an evacuee and disputed property was evacuee property - Held, an evacuee property automatically vests in Custodian u/s. 5, notification u/s. 6 of 1949 Ordinance is not a necessary condition for such vesting - Held, that is, 2(c)(ii) and not s. 2(c)(i) of Ordinance would apply in instant case - Appeal allowed.
