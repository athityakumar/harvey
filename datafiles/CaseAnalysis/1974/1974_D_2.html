D. Bhuvan Mohan Patnaik and Others v State of Andhra Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 September 1974

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.V. Chandrachud, Hans Raj Khanna, P.K. Goswami

Where Reported

1974 Indlaw SC 140; (1974) SCC (Cr) 803; (1975) 3 SCC 185; AIR 1974 SC 2092; 1975 CRLJ 556; 1974 CRLR 605; [1975] 2 S.C.R. 24

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Criminal; Defence & Security Forces; Land & Property

Keywords: Defence Regulations

Summary: Criminal - Constitution - Practice & Procedure - Code Of Criminal Procedure, 1898, ss. 44 and 46(3) - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 19, 21 and 42 - Right to life and personal liberty of prisoners - Posting of police-guards outside jail installation of live-wire mechanism - Held, no prisoner can be deprived of his 'life' or 'personal liberty' except according to procedure established by law - Convicts have not been deprived of their fundamental rights - No fundamental freedom to escape from lawful custody - Wire mechanism does not offend against command of s. 46(3) of CrPC - Jail reforms - Art. 42 needs to be extended to prisoners - Petition dismissed.
