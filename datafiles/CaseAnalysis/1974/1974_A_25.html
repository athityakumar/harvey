Anil Kumar Bose v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

10 April 1974

Case Analysis

Bench

P.K. Goswami, Hans Raj Khanna

Where Reported

1974 Indlaw SC 150; (1974) 4 SCC 616; (1974) SCC (Cr) 652; 1975 (12) ACC 35; AIR 1974 SC 1560; 1974 CRLJ 1026; 1974 CrLR(SC) 457; [1974] 3 S.C.R. 902; 1974 UJ 405

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Acquaintance

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 420 - Cheating - Accused were accountant and cashier of a Medical College Hospital - Fact was that fictitious pay bills were prepared by bill clerk, amounts of pay bills were drawn from treasury and disbursed of fictitious persons - Whether accused can be charged with offence u/s. 420, IPC, 1860 ? - For purposes of holding accused guilty u/s. 420, IPC, 1860 evidence adduced must establish beyond reasonable doubt mens rea on their part - Held, that it could not be said that requisite mens rea had been established against accused - It was a case of an error of judgment of breach of performance of duty which, per se, could not be equated with dishonest intention which was one of essential ingredients of offence of cheating u/s. 420, IPC, 1860 - Appeal allowed.
