Govindaraju @ Govinda v State By Sriramapuram P.S. & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

15 March 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Swatanter Kumar, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 94; (2012) 4 SCC 722; AIR 2012 SC 1292; 2012 ALL MR (Cri) 1385; 2012 (4) CalLT 20; 2012 CRLJ 1991; 2012 (3) JCC 1714; JT 2012 (3) SC 404; 2013 (1) KarLJ 591; 2012 (2) MLJ(Crl) 644; 2012 (3) RCR(Criminal) 881; 2012(3) SCALE 414; [2012] 5 S.C.R. 67

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 307 and 302 - Murder - Sole Witness - Beyond reasonable doubt - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant allegedly murdered the deceased - Trial Court acquitted appellant and another accused of charges u/s. 302/34 of IPC, against which respondent sought leave to appeal which was granted against appellant - HC, convicted appellant for offence u/s. 302 of IPC - Whether HC was justified in overturning judgment of acquittal in favour of appellant passed by Trial Court on merits of case - Held, there was no justification on record as to why official witnesses were not examined despite their availability without justification, hence, inference against prosecution could be drawn - Cumulative reading and appreciation of entire evidence on record, it was considered that Trial Court had not fallen in error of law or appreciation of evidence in accordance with law - HC interfered with judgment of acquittal only on basis that 'there was a possibility of another view' - Prosecution should prove its case beyond any reasonable doubt - HC had acted on certain legal and factual presumptions which could not be sustained on basis of record before court and principle of 'adverse inference' - Case of prosecution, thus, sufferred from proven improbabilities, infirmities, contradictions and the statement of the sole witness, Police Officer, PW1, was not reliable and worthy of credence - Appeal allowed.
