Sandeep and another v State Of U.P.

Supreme Court of India

11 May 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 505; (2012) 6 SCC 107; JT 2012 (5) SC 268; 2012 (3) MLJ(Crl) 56; [2012] 5 S.C.R. 952

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 316, 307, 326, 324, 328 and 302 r/w s. 34 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 25 - Murder - Conviction - Death sentence - Sustainability - Appellants (accused) allegedly committed murder of deceased and committed offences u/ss. 307, 326, 324 and 328 of IPC - Trial Court convicted appellants for offences u/ss. 316, 302 r/w s. 34 and sentenced them to death - Thereafter, reference was made to HC and aggrieved appellants filed an appeal before HC - DB confirmed death penalty of appellant no. 1 and altered conviction of appellant no. 2 into life imprisonment - Aggrieved appellants filed instant appeals - Appellants contended that dying declaration of deceased was not proved and that there were very many missing links in chain of circumstances and that there DNA test result could not be relied on - Whether impugned conviction of appellants was justified - Held, relationship of appellant no. 1 with deceased, prior to date of occurrence was his girlfriend and deceased was carrying foetus of 6 months old in her womb - As per post-mortem certificate, there were as many as 17 injuries which were caused by blunt weapons like jack and pana (spanner), shaving blades and chemical acid - Constable (PW-3), noticed acid injuries in inner mouth of deceased - Whatever injuries sustained by the deceased were borne out by medical record, post-mortem certificate and evidence of doctor (PW6), who issued said certificate - 17 injuries were noted in post-mortem and injury Nos. 4 and 17 alone were stated to have caused by acid - Evidence of Doctor, specifically expressed an opinion that he was not in a position to state whether after receipt of injury on body of deceased, she would have been in a position to speak or not and did not rule out possibility of deceased making any statement irrespective of injuries sustained - Condition of deceased, even after sustaining multiple injuries, was such that she was alive, conscious and her death was not instantaneous - Deceased made a statement about sequence of occurrence was really made as spelt out by witnesses PW Nos. 1 to 5 - Appellants could not demonstrate as to any prejudice that was caused by non-examination of Constable PW3 and jeep driver in order to find fault with case of prosecution - Witnesses who were examined were able to unfold the narration of events in a cogent and convincing manner and non-examination of PW3 and jeep driver was, thus, not fatal to case of prosecution - Failure of appellant no. 1 in not having taken any steps to prove that he was not present at the place of occurrence strikes at very root of defence and case of prosecution as demonstrated before Court stood fully established - Statement of appellant no. 1's revealed only fact of relationship developed with deceased 6 months prior to occurrence and physical relationship developed by him with her - Accepting said statement could not be held to straightway implicate appellant no. 1 into crime and consequently it could not be construed as a confessional statement in order to reject same by applying s. 25 of 1872 Act - FIR, Ex.P42, was not a wholly confessional statement, but only that part of it was admissible in evidence which did not amount to a confession and was not hit by provisions of s. 25 of 1872 Act - Relationship of appellant with deceased; motive for commission of crime and presence of his sister-in-law PW11 did not amount to confession of committing any crime - Those statements were non-confessional in nature and could be used against appellant as evidence u/s. 8 of the 1872 Act - PW10 Junior Scientific Officer confirmed that appellant no. 1 was biological father of foetus and that when sample on second time was received, same was preserved in ice separately which they were able to test in their laboratory for finding out result - Submission that procedure was not followed with regard to preservation of samples for DNA test, was not supported by any relevant material on record and appellant was not able to substantiate said argument with any other supporting material - Report of DNA concluded that appellant no. 1 was biological father of recovered foetus of deceased was one other relevant circumstance to prove guilt of appellant no. 1 - Chain of circumstances alleged against appellants was conclusively proved without any missing link - Instant case did not fall within four corners of principle of 'rarest of the rare case' - Impugned death sentence was set aside - Appellants should serve 30 years of jail - Swamy Shraddananda @ Murali Manohar Mishra v. State of Karnataka, 2008 Indlaw SC 1128 and Ramraj @ Nanhooo @ Bihnun v. State of Chhattisgarh, 2009 Indlaw SC 2087, referred to - Appeals disposed of.
