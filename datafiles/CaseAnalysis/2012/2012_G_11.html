Raju Jhurani v Germinda Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

16 August 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, J. Chelameswar

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 370; (2012) 8 SCC 563; AIR 2012 SC 3191; 2013 (96) ALR 262; 2012 (6) Bom.C.R. 191; 2012 (3) CLT(SC) 198; [2013] 176 Comp Cas 1; 2012 (2) OLR 669; 2012 (4) RCR(Civil) 949; 2013 (118) RD 671; [2012] 7 S.C.R. 279; 2012 (3) WLN(SC) 54

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Corporate

Summary: Corporate - Rent Control - Civil Procedure - West Bengal Premises Tenancy Act, 1956, s. 13(6) - Companies Act, 1956, ss. 433, 434 and 439 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 2 r. 2 - Default in payment of rent - Winding up - Dismissal - Challenged - Appellant landlord filed suit for eviction against respondent company on ground of default in making payment of rents and also on grounds of reasonable requirement, under provisions of the Act - Trial Court decreed the suit only on ground of default, but only upon recording that notice u/s. 13(6) of the Tenancy Act - Respondent did not handed over vacant possession of suit premises, appellant issued notice to respondent company demanding payment of arrears of rent, corporation taxes, etc but without yielding any result - Appellant filed winding-up petition before Company Court for payment of arrears of rent - Single Judge (Company Affairs) dismissed winding-up petition on ground of alleged bar of O. 2 r. 2 CPC as well as observations made that appellant could approach any other appropriate forum with regard to claim raised by him in winding-up petition - Appellant filed appeal before DB, which was also dismissed - Hence instant appeal - Whether Courts below were justified in passing the impugned order - Held, O. 2 CPC deals with frame of suits and various rules contained therein also refer to suits for obtaining reliefs of civil nature - However, proceeding u/ss. 433, 434 and 439 of the Companies Act, was not a suit, but petition which did not attract provisions of O. 2 r. 2 CPC, which deals with suits - There were various stages involved in deciding amount of rents payable and periods of default and also amount to be ultimately calculated on account of such default and same could not be tried in summary way, without adducing proper evidence - It was, necessary that such issues be heard and tried in properly constituted suit for recovery of such dues, in which issue relating to actual dues payable by respondent-tenant to appellant-landlord could be decided - Hence set aside the findings of Single Judge, as also DB, in regard to application of provisions of O. 2 r. 2 CPC to winding-up proceeding under Companies Act that might be filed for recovery of dues payable by respondent-tenant to appellant-landlord - Appeal allowed.
