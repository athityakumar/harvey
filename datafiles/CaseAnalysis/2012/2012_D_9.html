Deepa Thomas and others v Medical Council Of India & Ors

Supreme Court of India

25 January 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Cyriac Joseph, Gyan Sudha Misra

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 50; (2012) 3 SCC 430; 2012 (5) AWC 4610; JT 2012 (1) SC 557; 2012 (3) MLJ(SC) 310; 2012(1) SCALE 738; [2012] 3 S.C.R. 11; 2012 (2) SLR 47

Case Digest

Subject: Education

Keywords: MCI Regulations, Kerala Professional Colleges or Institutions (Prohibition of Capitation Fee, Regulation of Admission, Fixation of Non-exploitative Fee and Other Measures to ensure Equity and Excellence in Professional Education) Act, 2006, Gujarat Professional Medical Educational Colleges or Institutions (Regulation of Admission and Payment of Fees) Rules, 2008

Summary: Education - Admission to Medical course -Violation of MCI Regulation -Effect - Appellants had secured more than 50% marks in qualifying examination, however, they could secure only less than 50% marks in Competitive Entrance Exam (CEE) - Without being aware of difference between MCI Regulations and Prospectus in respect of eligibility criteria, appellants took admission in medical colleges - MCI did not have objection but later directed colleges concerned to discharge appellants on the ground that they were not eligible for admission as they had secured only less than 50% marks in the CEE - Appellants were constrained to approach HC for redressal - Writ petitions filed by appellants were dismissed by HC holding that regulations framed by MCI were mandatory in nature - Hence, instant appeal - Appellants contended that they were eligible for admission as per criteria laid down in Prospectus, but they were not eligible for admission as per criteria laid down in MCI Regulations, as they secured only less than 50% marks in Physics, Chemistry and Biology taken together in competitive examination - Whether HC was right in holding that admission of appellants was irregular and MCI was justified in directing colleges to discharge appellants - Held, appellant had continued their studies for 4 years on basis of interim orders, it would be quite unjust and unfair to discharge appellants at this stage - Instant case was an eminently fit case for invoking SC's powers u/art. 142 of Constitution to permit appellants to continue and complete MBBS course to which they were admitted - Irregularity in admissions occurred due to an inadvertent and bona fide mistake or omission on part of Colleges while issuing Prospectus even before applications were invited - It was not possible to attribute any malafides on part of respondent-Colleges as it did not appear to be a deliberate act to violate MCI Regulations and since irregular admissions had not resulted in any pecuniary gain for management - There was no attempt to favour appellants, as Colleges could not have anticipated that appellants would apply and fail to secure 50% marks in CEE - Respondent-Colleges in-spite of bonafide lapse were adequately punished as they were directed to surrender equal number of seats from management quota in coming years - Appeal disposed of.
