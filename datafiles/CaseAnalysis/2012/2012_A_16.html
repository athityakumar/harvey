I.T.C. Limited v Adarsh Coop. Housing Soc. Ltd

Supreme Court of India

27 August 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjan Gogoi, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 266; (2013) 10 SCC 169; AIR 2012 SC (Supp) 3; 2012 (6) ALD(SC) 139; 2012 (94) ALR 682; 2012 (5) AWC 5356; 2012 (3) CLT(SC) 411; JT 2012 (8) SC 183; 2012 (7) MLJ(SC) 62; 2012 (117) RD 562; 2013 (4) RLW 2977; 2012(8) SCALE 11

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Specific Relief Act, 1963 - Specific Relief - Dispossession from immovable property - Challenged - Plaintiff had filed a suit u/s. 6 of the Act praying for delivery of possession of the of the suit property from which plaintiff claimed to have been illegally dispossessed by the defendant - Plaintiff submitted that the impugned land was jointly owned by 'X' and 'Y' - 'Y' has sold his portion to the plaintiff - On the basis of the aforesaid sale made by a registered deed, the revenue records were corrected and necessary entries were made showing the name of the plaintiff against the share of 'Y' - Subsequently 'Y' had entered into an agreement of sale of the same land with the defendant - Defendant filed a suits for specific performance of the agreement to sell and for an order of injunction restraining 'Y' from raising any construction on the suit land and from transferring/ alienating the same - As the property involved in both the suits had already been transferred to the plaintiff , the plaintiff was impleaded as a party in both the suits - Court refused the prayer of injunction and appeal against such refusal was dismissed - Defendant forcible took possession of the impugned land from the plaintiff - Defendant contended that in the absence of any formal partition between the co-sharers, and also in the absence of any mutual agreement between the parties with regard to possession of any specific share of the land, no exclusive right in the suit land had vested in 'Y' so as to confer legitimacy to the sale deed executed by 'Y' in favour of the plaintiff - Trial court decreed the suit in favour of the plaintiff - The Revisional Court reiterated the findings of the Trial Court - On appeal, HC affirmed the order of the Revisional court - Hence, the instant appeal - Whether order of the Revisional court as affirmed by the HC could be upheld - Held, in a civil proceeding, the issues that may arise were required to be decided by balancing the claims and counter-claims of the parties before the Court and on the basis of a preponderance of probabilities - The conclusion that the defendant could not have been in possession, as claimed, was necessary to be reached in order to answer the question that was before the Court in the present case - The findings recorded by the Trial Court and affirmed by the Revisional Court as well as by the HC were essentially findings on question of fact which have been arrived at on the basis of the evidence and materials adduced by the parties - SC, therefore, find no reason whatsoever, to disturb the said findings and the same were hereby affirmed - Appeal dismissed.
