Radhakrishna Nagesh v State of Andhra Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

13 December 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Swatanter Kumar, Gyan Sudha Misra

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 545; (2013) 11 SCC 688; 2013 ALL MR (Cri) 711; 2012 (2) DRTC 616; JT 2013 (4) SC 68; 2013 (1) RCR(Criminal) 659; 2012(12) SCALE 506; [2012] 11 S.C.R. 1114

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 363 and 376 (2) (f) - Schedule Castes and the Schedule Tribes (Prevention of Atrocities) Act, 1989, s. 3(2)(v) - Rape - Acquittal - Revisal by HC - Sustainability - Appellant/accused allegedly committed rape on minor girl/victim - Trial Court acquitted appellant for offences punishable u/ss. 363 and 376 (2)(f) of IPC and for offence u/s. 3 (2) (v) of 1989 Act - On appeal, HC convicted appellant for offences punishable u/ss. 363 and 376 (2) (f) of IPC and set aside the order of Trial Court - Hence instant appeal - Appellant contended that there was a clear conflict between medical evidence and ocular evidence which created a serious doubt in the case of prosecution - Further, contended that there was no direct evidence connecting the accused to the commission of crime - Held, in order to establish a conflict between ocular evidence and medical evidence, there had to be specific and material contradictions - Merely because, some fact was not recorded or stated by doctor at a given point of time and subsequently such fact was established by expert report, the FSL Report, would not by itself substantiate plea of contradiction or variation - Absence of injuries on the body of prosecutrix would not be of any advantage to accused - Further, there was no reason as to why SC should disbelieve the statements of PW nos. 1, 2, 3, 5 and 11, particularly when they stood the lengthy cross-examination without any material damage to the case of prosecution - Evidence of PW9, sufficiently indicate that victim was subjected to rape - Presence of the element of mens rea on part of accused could not be denied - There was a direct link of accused with the commission of crime, such conclusion can well be established by the statement of witnesses, the recoveries made, the Medical Report and the FSL Report - Statement of PW no.2 was fully corroborated by statements of PW nos. 1 and 3, they were independent witnesses and had no personal interest or motive of falsely implicating the accused or supporting the case of prosecution - Order of HC was upheld - Appeal dismissed.
