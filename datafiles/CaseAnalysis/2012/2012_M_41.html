 State Of Kerala & Ors. v M/S. Mar Appraem Kuri Co. Ltd. & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

08 May 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, D.K. Jain, Surinder Singh Nijjar, Ranjana Prakash Desai, Jagdish Singh Khehar

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 162; (2012) 7 SCC 106; AIR 2012 SC 2375; [2012] 174 Comp Cas 339; JT 2012 (5) SC 51; 2012 (2) KLT 639; 2012 (4) MLJ(SC) 946; 2012 (2) RCR(Civil) 985; 2012(5) SCALE 266; [2012] 4 S.C.R. 448

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Constitution

Keywords: Extent Of Laws Made By Parliament, State Laws, Kerala Chitties Act, 1975, East Punjab Refugees (Registration of Land Claims) Act, 1948, Uttar Pradesh Civil Laws (Reforms and Amendment) Act, 1976, East Punjab Refugees (Registration of Land Claims) Ordinance of 1948, Mines and Minerals (Development and Regulation) Act 1948

Summary: Constitution - Banking & Finance - Kerala Chitties Act, 1975, s. 4(1a) - Central Chit Funds Act, 1982, s. 1(3) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 254(1), 254(2) and 367 - General Clauses Act, 1897 - Whether the 1975 Act became repugnant to the 1982 Act u/art. 254(1) of Constitution upon making of the 1982 Act (i.e. on 19-8-1982 when the President gave his assent) or whether the 1975 Act would become repugnant to the 1982 Act as and when notification u/s. 1(3) of the 1982 Act bringing the Central Act into force in the State of Kerala was issued - Held, repugnancy arises on the making and not commencement of the law - On the enactment of the 1982 Act , on 19-8-1982, which covered the entire field of 'chits' under entry 7 of List III of the Constitution, the 1975 Act, on account of repugnancy as enshrined in art. 254(1) of Constitution, became void and stood impliedly repealed - On the occupation of the entire field of 'chits', the Kerala Legislature could not have enacted the State Finance Act, 2002, inserting s. 4(1a) into the 1975 Act, particularly on the failure of the State in obtaining Presidential assent u/art. 254(2) of Constitution - The 1982 Act though not brought in force in the State of Kerala was still a law made, which was alive as an existing law - By reason of art. 367 of the Constitution, the 1897 Act applies to the repeal - S. 6 of 1897 Act was, therefore, relevant, particularly ss. 6(b) and 6(c) of 1897 Act and consequently, the previous operation of the 1975 Act was not affected nor any right, privilege, obligation or liability acquired or incurred under that repealed 1975 Act -Thus, after 19-8-1982, the 1975 Act stands repealed except for the limited purposes of s. 6 of 1897 Act - If and when the Central Government brings into force the 1982 Act by a notification in State of Kerala, u/s. 1(3), s. 90(2) of 1982 Act will come into play and thereby the 1975 Act shall continue to apply only to chits in operation on the date of commencement of the 1982 Act in the same manner as the 1975 Act applied to chits before such commencement - Reference answered.
