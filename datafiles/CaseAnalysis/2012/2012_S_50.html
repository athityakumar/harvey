Sayed Mohd. Ahmed Kazmi v State, Gnctd and others

Supreme Court of India

19 October 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Surinder Singh Nijjar, J. Chelameswar

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 432; (2012) 12 SCC 1; AIR 2013 SC 152; 2013 CRLJ 200; 2013 (1) JCC 269; JT 2012 (11) SC 81; 2012 (4) RCR(Criminal) 875; 2013 (1) RLW 750; [2012] 9 S.C.R. 836

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: National Investigation Agency Act, 2008

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 307,427,120-B - Unlawful Activities (Prevention) Act, 1967 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 167(2) - FIR registered - Chargesheet - Statutory Period - Bail refused - Sustainability - FIR against appellant-accused was registered u/ss. 307, 427, 120-B of IPC in connection with an explosion involving an Embassy vehicle carrying the wife of Diplomat - Appellant was arrested and sent to police custody and later to judicial custody - Prosecution after 90 days sought further custody of appellant and CMM granted custody which was appealed by appellant before Sessions Judge, which held that CMM was not competent to even extend judicial custody of accused and entertain case of such nature - Appellant filed application u/s. 167(2) of CrPC before CMM seeking default bail as no charge sheet was filed, but same was dismissed - Session Judge on reference extended custody of appellant against which appellant moved HC, questioning validity of orders passed by Sessions Judge - Application for bail was not taken up as reference was pending - Sessions Court in reference matter held custody of appellant to be illegal, immediately appellant filed application u/s. 167(2) of CrPC before and prosecution had filed application for extension of custody before CMM, which was allowed - Aggrieved appellant filed revision before Sessions Judge, which was adjourned till 12-10-2012 - Appellant filed application before HC for early hearing which was rejected on ground that on account of heavy board of Court it was not possible to accommodate - Hence instant appeals - Appellant challenged that once period of 90 days, as stipulated u/cl. (a) (i) of proviso to s. 167(2) of Cr.P.C., came to an end, right of a person arrested in connection with commission of offence to be released on statutory bail commenced and could not be extinguished by a subsequent application for extension of period of custody and further Sessions Judge had declared custody of appellant to be illegal - Whether appellant was legally eligible for bail after statutory period of 90 days and whether CMM was justified in extending custody of appellant - Held, SC, was unable to appreciate procedure adopted by CMM, which had been endorsed by HC and was of view that Appellant acquired right for grant of statutory bail on 17-7- 2012, when his custody was held to be illegal by Additional Sessions Judge since his application for statutory bail was pending at time when application for extension of time for continuing investigation was filed by prosecution - SC, held that right of Appellant to grant of statutory bail remained unaffected by subsequent application and both CMM and HC erred in holding otherwise - Appeal allowed.
