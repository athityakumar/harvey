Sampath Kumar v Inspector Of Police, Krishnagiri

Supreme Court of India

02 March 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, Gyan Sudha Misra

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 89; (2012) 4 SCC 124; AIR 2012 SC 1249; 2012 (2) JCC 1185; JT 2012 (2) SC 661; 2012 (2) MLJ(Crl) 550; 2012 (2) RCR(Criminal) 231; 2012 (2) RLW 1371; 2012(3) SCALE 271; [2012] 2 S.C.R. 289

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302 - Murder - Circumstantial evidence - Conviction - Life Imprisonment - Sustainability - Appellant accused was convicted by Trial Court for offence u/s. 302/34 IPC on the basis of circumstantial evidence and sentenced him to life imprisonment - Appellant appealed to HC which was dismissed by impugned order - Whether prosecution was able to establish motive, chain of events leading to involvement of appellant in alleged crime - Held, motive to harm deceased was important circumstance, in a case based on circumstantial evidence, but should not take place of conclusive proof that person concerned was author of crime - One could even say that presence of motive in facts and circumstances of case created a strong suspicion against appellant but suspicion, howsoever strong, also could not be a substitute for proof of guilt of accused beyond a reasonable doubt - Prosecution had not proved its case against appellants who were, entitled to acquittal giving them benefit of doubt - Appeal allowed.
