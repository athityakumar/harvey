Kamalbai Sinkar v State of Maharashtra and others

Supreme Court of India

20 July 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 216; (2012) 11 SCC 754; AIR 2012 SC 2960; 2012 (4) CGLJ 83; 2012 (134) FLR 761; 2013 (1) RCR(Civil) 229; 2012(6) SCALE 545; [2012] 6 S.C.R. 1011; 2012 (5) SLR 359

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Constitution - Freedom Fighter Pension - Underground Freedom Fighter - Denied - Challenged - Resolution of Freedom Fighter Pension was issued - Pursuant to the said resolution dt. 4-7-1995, the husband of the appellant forwarded his application through the Collector of Amravati - Husband of the appellant also enclosed certain documents relating to his claim - The Collector of Amravati informed the appellant that her husband's claim for grant of Freedom Fighters' Pension was submitted to the State Govt. along with recommendation of the Gaurav Samiti dt. 20-12-1996 - State Govt. communicated to the Collector of Amravati, that there was no concrete evidence in proof of the participation of the freedom fight movement by the husband of the appellant and his claim for grant of Freedom Fighters' Pension was, therefore, rejected - The Collector was directed to communicate the same to the appellant - Hence, the instant appeal - Whether State Govt. rightly rejected the claim of the appellant's husband regarding Freedom Fighter Pension - Held, appellant's husband made a genuine effort to collect all credentials in his support as required under the Resolution of the State Govt., and forwarded them to the State Govt. along with his application - When the Collector, Amrawati forwarded his letter dt. 20-12-1996 and reiterated his recommendation in his subsequent communications there was no reason for the State Govt. to simply reject the application without assigning any reason - A perusal of the documents enclosed by the appellant's husband along with his application disclosed that the appellant's husband made out a case for grant of Freedom Fighters' Pension under the category 'Underground Freedom Fighter' - The claim of the appellant's husband cannot be held to be a fraudulent one or without any supporting material - Impugned orders were set aside and the State Govt. was directed to grant Freedom Fighters' Pension in favour of the appellant's husband - Appeal allowed.
