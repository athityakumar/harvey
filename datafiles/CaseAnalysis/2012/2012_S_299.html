Kishan Chand v State of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

13 December 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Swatanter Kumar, Madan B. Lokur

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 553; (2013) 2 SCC 502; AIR 2013 SC 357; 2013 ALL MR (Cri) 774; 2013 CRLJ 723; JT 2013 (1) SC 222; 2013 (1) KLT 634; 2013 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 205; 2013 (2) RCR(Criminal) 67; 2012(12) SCALE 429; [2012] 11 S.C.R. 1010

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Narcotics Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985, ss. 18, 42(1), (2), 50 - Secrete Information - Contraband - Recovery - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant (accused) and another allegedly smuggled opium in a scooter - Trial Court acquitted another accused and convicted appellant u/s. 18 of the Act and awarded punishment to undergo RI of 10 years - Aggrieved appellant filed an appeal before HC - DB dismissed appeal holding that appellant was in motion at the time when secret information was received, from reliable source, and PW7 (IO) acted swiftly and arrested appellant, in such circumstances, secret information report was not recorded by IO - Hence, instant appeal - Appellant contended that there was no compliance of ss.42 (1) and (2) of the Act as they were mandatory and not directory and that appellant was entitled to acquittal and that PW5 (Tehsildar) was never present at the site - Whether impugned order convicting appellant was justified - Held, question of substantial compliance of ss.42 (1) and (2) of the Act would amount to misconstruction of those relevant provisions - It was a settled canon of interpretation that the penal provisions, particularly with harsher punishments and with clear intendment of legislature for definite compliance, ought to be construed strictly - Doctrine of substantial compliance could not be called in aid to answer such interpretations - Principle of substantial compliance would be applicable in the cases where the language of provision strictly or by necessary implication admits of such compliance - In instant case, IO (PW7) in his statement while referring to story of prosecution did not state in examination-in-chief that he had made the report immediately upon receiving the secret information and had informed his senior officers -Trial Court and HC proceeded on the basis of substantial compliance and there being no prejudice to the accused, though clearly recording that it was an admitted case of total non-compliance - Statement of IO put the matter beyond ambiguity that there was 'total non-compliance of statutory provisions of s. 42 of the Act - Once, there was total non-compliance and those provisions being mandatory in nature, prosecution case should fail - Total non-compliance of s. 42 of the Act, non-involvement of any independent witness at any stage of investigation and presence of PW5 at the spot so very doubtful, thus, compel to hold that prosecution failed to prove its case beyond reasonable doubt - Trial Court and HC erred in law and that of appreciation of evidence - Appellant was acquitted of offence u/s. 18 of the Act - Appeal allowed.
