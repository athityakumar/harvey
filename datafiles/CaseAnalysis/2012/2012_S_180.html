Bhajju @ Karan Singh v State Of M.P.

Supreme Court of India

15 March 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Swatanter Kumar, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 86; (2012) 4 SCC 327; AIR 2012 SC (Supp) 792; 2012 CRLJ 1926; 2012 (2) JabLJ 410; 2012 (2) JCC 1192; JT 2012 (3) SC 377; 2012 (3) MLJ(Crl) 261; 2012 (2) RCR(Criminal) 539; 2012(3) SCALE 438; 2012(3) SCALE 452; [2012] 5 S.C.R. 37

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 307 and 302 - Murder - Dying Declaration - Contradictions - Admissibility - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant was convicted for offence u/s. 302 of IPC - Initially case u/s. 307 of IPC was registered, later it was altered to s. 302 of IPC - Appellant doubting chastity of his wife-deceased, questioning paternity of children born out of wedlock, poured kerosene on deceased and had burnt her - Deceased in her dying declaration recorded before executive magistrate accusing appellant of burning her - Trial Court concluding that prosecution had been able to prove its case beyond reasonable doubt, convicted appellant u/s. 302 of IPC - Aggrieved appellant filed appeal before HC - HC affirmed conviction of Trial Court - Hence instant appeal - Whether conviction of Trial Court on basis of dying declaration was sustainable - Held, if dying declaration was recorded in accordance with law, was reliable and gave a cogent and possible explanation of occurrence of events, then the dying declaration could certainly be relied upon by Court and could form sole piece of evidence resulting in conviction of the accused - Instant case was not a case where dying declaration, was only evidence against appellant or that whatever was stated in it, was not partially or otherwise supported by other evidence given fact that there was no dispute to occurrence in question, statements of doctor, PW9 and Investigating Officer, PW10 and Exhibits including site plan, post-mortem report etc., which were admissible pieces of substantive evidence, fully corroborated dying declaration - Contradictions between statements of DW1 and DW2 were not reliable and their statements were not trustworthy - No ground was found to interfere in concurrent judgments of conviction and order of sentence - Appeal dismissed.
