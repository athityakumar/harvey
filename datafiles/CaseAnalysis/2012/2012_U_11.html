Krushnakant B. Parmar v Union Of India & Anr

Supreme Court of India

15 February 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 39; (2012) 3 SCC 178; AIR 2012 SC (Supp) 42; 2012(4) ALL MR 442; 2012 (2) AWC 2047; 2012 (132) FLR 1023; JT 2012 (2) SC 352; 2012 (4) MLJ(SC) 595; 2012(2) SCALE 545; [2012] 3 S.C.R. 484; 2012 (3) SLR 321

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Absence From Duty

Summary: Service - Central Civil Services (Conduct) Rules, 1964, r. 3(1)(ii) - Unauthorised absence - Departmental Inquiry - Dismissed - Sustainability - Appellant was issued charge-sheet for unauthorized absence, during pendency of departmental proceedings appellant was transferred - Appellant challenged transfer before CAT alleging bias against superior officer - CAT set aside order holding transfer vitiated due to malice in law and fact which was affirmed by HC - Inquiry officer after 7 years held that charge had been proved against beyond all reasonable doubt holding him guilty - Appellant submitted objections against Inquiry report - Appellant challenged dismissal order before CAT contending that controlling officer prevented applicant from performing duty and to sign attendance register - CAT refused to entertain application and allowed appellant to avail alternative remedy of appeal - Appellant preferred appeal before Director, Intelligence Bureau - Appellate authority without discussing objections raised rejected appeal - CAT dismissed appeal and same was affirmed by HC - Hence instant appeal - Appellant contended that inquiry officer failed to consider relevant evidence produced and misdirected himself in finding him guilty - Respondents contended departmental inquiry was conducted in accordance with law, and after providing full opportunity to appellant, on appreciation of evidence, as Inquiry Officer held appellant guilty - whether unauthorised absence from duty amounts to failure of devotion to duty or behaviour unbecoming of a Government servant cannot be decided without deciding question whether absence is wilful or because of compelling circumstances - Held, disciplinary authority failed to prove that absence from duty was willful, no such finding has been given by Inquiry Officer or Appellate Authority - Appellant had taken a specific defense that he was prevented from attending duty and to sign attendance register and also brought on record 11 defense exhibits in support of his defense but such defense and evidence were ignored and on the basis of irrelevant fact and surmises, Inquiry Officer held appellant guilty - Appeal allowed.
