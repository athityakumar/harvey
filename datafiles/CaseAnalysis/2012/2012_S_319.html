Shudhakar v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

24 July 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Swatanter Kumar, Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 218; (2012) 7 SCC 569; AIR 2012 SC 3265; 2012 ALL MR (Cri) 2682; 2012 CRLJ 3985; 2012 (3) JabLJ 72; 2012 (4) JCC 2344; 2012 (5) RCR(Criminal) 16; 2013 (1) RLW 290; 2012(6) SCALE 587; [2012] 7 S.C.R. 128

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Murder - Conviction - Multiple dying declaration - Challenged - Accused (Husband) poured kerosene oil on deceased (Wife) and put her ablaze by lighting a match stick due to which deceased died - Trial Court found that the prosecution had been able to prove its case beyond reasonable doubt and, thus, held the accused guilty of an offence punishable u/s. 302 IPC - On appeal, the HC affirmed the judgment of conviction and order of sentence passed by the trial court - Hence, the instant appeal - Appellant contended that deceased had made various dying declarations - The first dying declaration had completely absolved the accused - Recording of subsequent dying declarations could not be made the basis of conviction - Whether Trial court and HC rightly convicted appellant u/s. 302 of IPC - Held, where the version given by the deceased as dying declaration was supported and corroborated by other prosecution evidence, there was no reason for the courts to doubt the truthfulness of such dying declaration - In the instant case, the second and third dying declarations were authentic, voluntary and duly corroborated by other prosecution witnesses including the medical evidence - These dying declarations, read in conjunction with the statement of the prosecution witnesses, can safely be made the basis for conviction of the accused - Moreover, deceased herself stated that the reason for not implicating her husband in her first dying declaration was that there was every likelihood that accused would lose the job - No infirmity in the appreciation of evidence and law in the concurrent judgments of the courts - Appeal dismissed.
