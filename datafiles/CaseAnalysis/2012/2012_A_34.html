JIK Industries Limited and others v Amarlal V. Jumani and another

Supreme Court of India

01 February 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Ganguly, Jagdish Singh Khehar

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 583; (2012) 3 SCC 255; [2012] 170 Comp Cas 223; 2012 CRLJ 1649; 2012 (3) CTC 428; 2012 (2) JCC (NI) 68; JT 2012 (2) SC 1; 2012 (4) MahLJ 568; 2012 (2) MLJ(Crl) 703; 2012 (3) MPLJ 349; 2012 (1) RCR(Criminal) 822; [2012] 3 S.C.R. 114

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Criminal

Keywords: Criminal Procedure Code, 1861, Statement of Objects and Reasons of Negotiable Instrument (Amendment) Bill 2001, Banking Financial Institutions and Negotiable Instruments (Amendment) Act 1988

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, s. 138,141,147 - Companies Act, 1956 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 482 - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Dishonour of Cheque - Company Petition - Compounding - Inapplicable - Sustainability - appellant company came out with a scheme and agreed that they should be revived and thereafter payments would be made to creditors and filed petition u/s. 391 of Act before HC, meeting was convened by appellant company and same was attended by several creditors respondent No. 1 opposed scheme, but despite opposition, appellant succeeded in getting scheme approved by statutory majority as required under law - On 17-11-2005 another company petition with a fresh scheme was filed and all proceedings which were initiated by different companies against appellant(s) came to be stayed by HC - Appellant company filed application for compounding u/s. 147 of Act, r/w s. 320 of CrPC and s. 391 of Act, respondents opposed prayer of petitioner and rejected application filed by appellant for termination of proceedings inter alia on ground that Magistrate had no power to quash or terminate proceedings - Aggrieved appellant filed writ petitions before HC, which was rejected and appellants were given opportunity to file petition u/s. 482 of CrPC - Aggrieved instant appeal - Appellants contended that s. 391 of Act was not mere agreement but it had statutory binding even on dissenting creditors and shareholders and a creditor was bound by it - Respondent pleaded that HC, was justified in formulating principle of compounding by holding that act of compounding involved an element of mutuality and it had to be bilateral and not unilateral - Whether Trial Court and HC, were justified in rejecting application for compounding filed by appellants - Held, in our country when CrPC, was enacted it was silent about compounding of offence, subsequently, next Code of 1872 was introduced it mentioned about compounding in s. 188 by providing mode of compounding, however, it did not contain any provision declaring what offences were compoundable - Subsequent Code provided s. 345 indicating offences which were compoundable but said Section was only made applicable to compounding of offences defined and permissible under IPC - Present Code, which repealed 1898 Code, contains s. 320 containing comprehensive provisions for compounding, perusal of s. 320 makes it clear that provisions contained in s. 320 and various sub-sections was a Code by itself relating to compounding of offence - It provides for various parameters and procedures and guidelines in matter of compounding - If contention of appellant is upheld as a result of incorporation of S. 147 in NI Act, entire gamut of procedure of s. 320 of CrPC were made inapplicable to compounding of an offence under NI Act, in that case compounding of offence under NI Act would be left totally unguided or uncontrolled - There was no other statutory procedure for compounding of offence under NI Act, therefore, s. 147 of NI Act must be reasonably construed to mean that as a result of Section offences under NI Act, were made compoundable, but main principle of such compounding, namely, consent of person aggrieved or person injured or complainant could not be wished away nor could same be substituted by virtue of s. 147 of NI Act - Hence though scheme u/s. 391 of Act, was sanctioned, that will not automatically compound offences u/s. 138 of NI Act, without consent of complainant - Appeals dismissed.
