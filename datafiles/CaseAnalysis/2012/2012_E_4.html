Maria Margarida Sequeria Fernandes And Others v Erasmo Jack De Sequeria(Dead) Through L.Rs.

Supreme Court of India

21 March 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Dalveer Bhandari, H.L. Dattu, Deepak Verma

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 100; (2012) 5 SCC 370; AIR 2012 SC 1727; 2012 (4) ALD(SC) 1; 2012 (92) ALR 251; 2012 (4) AWC 3645; 2012 (4) Bom.C.R. 75; 2012 (2) CLT(SC) 31; JT 2012 (3) SC 451; 2012 (3) MLJ(SC) 1202; 2012 (1) OLR 945; 2012 (2) RCR(Civil) 441; 2012 (116) RD 318; 2012 (2) RLW 1765; 2012(3) SCALE 550; 2012 (3) SCJ 518; [2012] 3 S.C.R. 841

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Civil Procedure Rules, 1998, Central Provinces Court of Wards Act, 1899

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Land & Property - Specific Relief Act, 1963, s. 6 - Mandatory Injunction - Eviction - Due process of Law - Sustainability - Appellant purchased property through suit proceedings by depositing amount and had been regularly paying house tax and other Cess since then - Respondent, brother of appellant, requested and lived in property for some time as care taker - Appellant was married to naval officer who was transferred from time to time -Respondent handed over property to appellant who started living in property - Respondent filed suit for permanent and mandatory injunction against appellant u/s. 6 of Act - Trial Court passed decree and judgment in favor of respondent, which was affirmed by HC - Aggrieved instant appeal - Appellant contended that respondent, had handed over possession to appellant and that records clearly showed that appellant was lawful owner of property as such suit u/s. 6 of Act was not maintainable - Whether Trial Court judgment and decree granting injunction to respondent was sustainable - Held, no one acquired title to property if he or she was allowed to stay in premises gratuitously, even by long possession of years or decades such person would not acquire any right or interest in said property - Caretaker, watchman or servant could never acquire interest in property irrespective of his long possession, caretaker or servant had to give possession forthwith on demand - Courts were not justified in protecting possession of a caretaker, servant or any person who was allowed to live in premises for some time either as a friend, relative, caretaker or as a servant - Hence, impugned judgment of HC as also of Trial Court were set aside - Appeal allowed.
