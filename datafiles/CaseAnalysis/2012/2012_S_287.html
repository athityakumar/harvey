Ashraf khan and others v State of Gujarat and others

Supreme Court of India

26 September 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Chandramauli Kumar Prasad, H.L. Dattu

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 317; (2012) 11 SCC 606; AIR 2013 SC 217; 2013 CRLJ 226; 2013 (2) G.L.R. 1364; JT 2012 (9) SC 661; 2012 (5) KarLJ 353; 2012 (4) RCR(Criminal) 642; 2012(9) SCALE 413; [2012] 12 S.C.R. 1033

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Curable, Deem, Terrorist and Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Amendment Act, 1993

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120B, 121A, 122, 123 and 188 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 157, 465 - Terrorist and Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Act, 1987, ss. 3, 5, 20-A(1) - Explosive Substances Act, 1908, ss. 4 to 6 - Arms Act, 1959, ss. 7 and 25(1A) - Illegal weapons - Possessing of - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellants (accused) allegedly were found in possession or involved in transporting or facilitating transportation of illegal weapons and committed offences u/ss. 3 and 5 of 1987 Act, u/ss. 4 to 6 of 1908 Act and u/ss. 7 and 25(1A) of 1959 Act - Designated Court (Trial Court) convicted appellants u/ss. 3 and 5 of 1987 Act, u/ss. 4 to 6 of 1908 Act and u/ss. 7 and 25(1A) of 1959 Act to undergo rigorous imprisonment for 5 years - Aggrieved appellants filed instant appeals against their conviction and respondent (State) preferred instant appeals against the inadequacy of sentence and acquittal of other accused - Hence, instant appeals - Appellants (accused) contended that FIR under 1987 Act was registered without approval of District Superintendent of Police (DSP) as contemplated u/s. 20-A(1) of 1987 Act and that itself vitiated their conviction - Respondent (State) contended that appellants (accused) did not challenge the sanction granted by Commissioner of Police u/s. 20-A(2) of 1987, they could not assail their conviction - Whether impugned conviction order passed by Designated Court was justified - Held, PW10 (IO) in cross-examination admitted that letter asking for approval to investigate and report u/s. 157 of CrPC was lost while producing the same in SC - According to DSP (PW65) original letter of approval was not found, the Xerox copy thereof was produced - Communication of Assistant Commissioner of Police (ACP) (Exh.1173) did not refer to any approval granted by Dy. Commissioner earlier and, not only that, Dy. Commissioner while giving approval on 11-8-1994 had nowhere whispered that earlier he had already granted the approval - No explanation was forthcoming from prosecution side that when Dy. Commissioner had already granted approval 9-6-1994, what was the occasion to write to him for grant of another approval and Dy. Commissioner granting the same - Case of prosecution that prior approval was granted on 9-6-1994 was fit to be rejected - Omission to grant prior approval for registration of the case under 1987 Act by Superintendent of Police (SP) was not the kind of omission which was covered u/s. 465 of CrPC - It was a defect which goes to the root of matter and it was not one of curable defects - The 1987 Act mandated that no information about commission of an offence under 1987 should be recorded by Police without prior approval of DSP - Further, safeguard was provided and restriction was put on Court not to take cognizance of any offence without previous sanction of Inspector-General of Police or Commissioner of Police - Even in a case which was not fit to be tried by Designated Court but it decides to do the same instead of referring the case to be tried by a Court of competent jurisdiction, it would not prevent appellants (accused) to challenge trial or conviction later on - Conviction of appellants was vitiated on account of non-compliance of s. 20-A(1) of 1987 Act and thus, it might be permissible in law to maintain conviction under 1959 and 1908 Acts but that should only be possible when there were legally admissible evidence to establish those charges - Designated Court relied only on confessions recorded under 1987 Act to convict appellants (accused) for offences under 1959 and 1908 Acts, the confessions recorded could not be looked into to establish the guilt under said Acts - Mohd. Yunus v. State of Gujarat, 1997 Indlaw SC 1590 and Kalpnath Rai v. State, 1997 Indlaw SC 3281, referred to - Appeal of respondent (State) was dismissed - Appeal of appellant (accused) was allowed - Impugned order was set aside - Appeals disposed of.
