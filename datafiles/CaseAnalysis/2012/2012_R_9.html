Ratnagiri Gas and Power Private Limited v RDS Projects Limited and others

Supreme Court of India

18 October 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, Gyan Sudha Misra

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 408; (2013) 1 SCC 524; AIR 2013 SC 200; 2012 (4) BC 469; 2012 (4) CLT(SC) 431; 2012 (193) DLT 762; JT 2012 (10) SC 626; 2012(10) SCALE 410; [2012] 9 S.C.R. 690

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Practice & Procedure; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Administrative - Practice & Procedure - Notification - Tender - Eligibility - Cancellation of Tender - Sustainability - Govt. of India had entrusted task of reviving and restructuring of 'D' Project to GAIL and NTPC, both Govt. undertakings who had in turn formed a joint venture company in name and style of appellant was charged with duty of completing balance work at LNG Terminal and of commissioning and operating same - Appellant for completion of Breakwater at LNG had invited tenders with conditions and respondent was successful in getting project work - One of the bidder who was not found techno-commercially qualified filed writ petition in HC, alleging that he had been wrongly disqualified and that respondent had been wrongly held qualified - Appellant called for documents from respondent to substantiate his claim and appellant had also received CAG report which did not support claim of respondent - Appellant in exercise of its power passed resolution annulling tender and further called for fresh tenders and further did not permit respondent to participate in tender - Respondent challenged annulment resolution in writ petition - HC, held that action taken by the appellant on both counts was vitiated by mala fides especially when the fresh tender notice issued by the appellant made an attempt to exclude RDS from competing for the works in question - Aggrieved instant appeals - Appellants contended that tender was categorically open for those who have done bid as single bidders and have experience of successfully completing it - Whether resolution passed by appellant cancelling tender and calling for new tender and rejecting tender of respondent was sustainable and whether HC, was justified in allowing petition of respondent - Held, there was considerable merit in case of appellants, a concession even if made by one of parties could not prevent other parties from arguing that it did not bind them or that same was contrary to facts - HC ought to have examined issue on merits, rather than taking a short cut - HC had incidentally taken support from certificate dt. 5-4-2008 and clarification issued on 5-6-2010 to hold that RDS had indeed executed qualifying project - SC, had in course of hearing asked Central Govt., to disclose basis on which certificate and clarification had been issued by officers concerned, but no satisfactory answer to was given to query, parties were asked to produce relevant record including Govt. files, so that SC, could answer question regarding eligibility of respondent but in absence of any conclusive evidence, and in absence of a specific finding from HC, on question, SC, remained handicapped and a remand to HC, therefore, became inevitable and matter was remanded back to HC - Appeal allowed.
