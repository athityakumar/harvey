Subulaxmi v M. D., Tamil Nadu State Transport Corporation and another

Supreme Court of India

01 November 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 390; (2012) 10 SCC 177; 2012 (4) ACC 438; 2012 ACJ 2863; 2013 (1) ALD(SC) 68; 2012(6) ALL MR 954; 2012 (95) ALR 914; 2013 (1) AWC 3; 2012 (4) CGLJ 607; JT 2012 (11) SC 135; 2013 (2) MahLJ 20; 2012 (8) MLJ(SC) 363; 2013 (1) MPLJ 536; 2012 (4) RCR(Civil) 945; 2013 (1) RLW 128; 2012(10) SCALE 617; [2012] 9 S.C.R. 962

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, s. 166 - Accidental claim - Enhancement of compensation - Appellant as claimant filed an application u/s. 166 of the Act before the Motor Accidents Claims Tribunal (Tribunal) and claimed a sum of Rs.6,50,000/ as compensation for the injuries sustained by her in a motor vehicle accident - Tribunal granted Rs.2,00,000/ as compensation - On appeal, HC, enhanced amount to Rs.2,75,000/ - Hence, instant appeal for enhancement of the amount of compensation - Whether amount of compensation awarded was justified -

Held, in the instant case, Tribunal awarded a sum of Rs.86,000/ towards the permanent disability - HC deleted it - Deletion was impermissible - Further, as claimant suffered 86 percent permanent disability, the future earning should be computed at 14 percent less and accordingly it was estimated that the multiplicand should be Rs.15,480/ p.a. - At the time of accident, she was 30 years of age, and hence, the multiplier of 18 would be applicable - Thus, the loss of future earning by multiplying the multiplicand of Rs.15,480/ by multiplier of 18, the amount would come to Rs.2,78,640/ - Rs.1,00,000/ granted for pain and suffering and loss of amenities - Appeal allowed.
