Brij Mohan Lal v Union of India (UOI) and others

Supreme Court of India

19 April 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Swatanter Kumar, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 319; (2012) 6 SCC 502; 2012 (2) CLT(SC) 90; JT 2012 (4) SC 212; 2012 (5) MLJ(SC) 689; 2012(4) SCALE 450; [2012] 5 S.C.R. 305

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Constitution

Keywords: Finance Commission, Practicing, Punjab Superior Judicial Service Rules, 1963, Bar Council of India Rules, 1975, Andhra Pradesh State Higher Judicial Service Special Rules, 2001, Fast Track Courts (Sponsored by Central (Govt.) for elimination of arrears Rule, 2001, Gujarat Judicial Services Recruitment Rules, 1961, Haryana Additional District and Sessions Judges Ad hoc Services Rules, 2001, Orissa Judicial Service (Special Scheme) Rules, 2001, Orissa Judicial Service Rules, 1994, Orissa Superior Judicial Services and Judicial Service Rules, 2007, Orissa Superior Judicial Services Rules, 1963, Punjab Superior Judicial Services Rules, 2007, Rajasthan Higher Judicial Service Rules, 2010, Rajasthan Higher Judicial Services Rules, 1969, Rajasthan Judicial Services Rules, 2010

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Constitution - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 21, , 142, 216, 280, 281, 308,309, 310 and 311 - Appointment of adhoc Judges for FTC - Violation of provisions of Constitution - Sustainability - Petitioners filed writ petition praying to stop scheme and policy of appointment of retired District and Sessions Judges as ad hoc Judges of Fast Track Courts - Petitioners contended that Constitutional scheme contained u/arts. 233 to 235 and 308, 309 of Constitution do not contemplate and permit appointment of retired judges as ad hoc District and Sessions Judges, even otherwise, there was no constitutional provision which empowered authorities concerned to make such appointments - Petitioners purpose of filing petition was to ensure that only members of Bar are appointed by direct recruitment to post of adhoc District and Sessions Judges - Respondent filed transfer petitioners for transfer of petitioner to SC - Hence this Petitions - Whether appointment of retired judges as ad hoc District and Sessions Judges for FTC courts was sustainable with reference to provisions of constitution - Held, recommendations of 13th Finance Commission that there were over 3 crores pending cases in various courts and there was enormous delay in disposing of cases resulting in immense hardship, including those borne by large number of under-trials, if FTC ad hoc direct recruits who had over the years gained a lot of judicial experience are regularised and absorbed in regular cadre of Additional District Judges in different States, problem of arrear of cases could be handled to some extent - Without any intent to interfere with policy decision taken by Govt. but, unmistakably, to protect guarantees of art. 21 of Constitution, improve Justice Delivery System and fortify independence of judiciary, while ensuring attainment of constitutional goals as well as to do complete justice to lis before us, in terms of art. 142 of Constitution, SC, passed orders with directions - Petitions disposed of.
