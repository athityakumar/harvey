Pratapbhai Hamirbhai Solanki v State of Gujarat and another

Supreme Court of India

12 October 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 355; (2013) 1 SCC 613; 2013 (2) G.L.R. 1035; JT 2012 (10) SC 286; 2012 (4) MLJ(Crl) 532; 2013 (1) RCR(Criminal) 592; 2012(10) SCALE 237; [2012] 9 S.C.R. 561

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 120-B - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 439 - Arms Act, 1959, ss. 25(1)(b) and 27 - Bail - Sustainability - Deceased was shot dead by two people and disappeared form scene of occurrence - Appellant/accused was arraigned for the offences u/s. 120-B IPC, u/ss. 25(1)(b) and 27 of the 1959 Act - During pendency of investigation appellant filed an application for grant of bail and contended that his name was not found in FIR that he had no nexus with the connection of crime - Trial Court dismissed the bail application - An application before HC was filed and HC granted temporary bail for a period of 21 days - Thereafter appellant preferred bail application u/s. 439 CrPC - HC declined to admit appellant to bail - Hence instant appeal - Held, it was demonstrable that the HC had expressed its dissatisfaction with regard to the investigation conducted by the investigating agency - It had called it perfunctory - After ascribing reasons, HC directed the CBI to expeditiously undertake further investigation - SC hasten to add that the legal propriety of the said order was not the subject matter of challenge in instant appeal - It had only been brought to notice that CBI had been directed to conduct a comprehensive investigation - Needless to state, it was open to appellant to challenge the legal substantiality of the said order - Suffice it to say, as there was a direction for fresh investigation, it should be inapposite to enlarge the appellant on bail - Order for reinvestigation was annulled by instant Court, it would be open for appellant to file a fresh application for bail before the competent Court - Appeal disposed of.
