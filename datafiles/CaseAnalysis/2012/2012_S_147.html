Rohtash v State Of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

22 May 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 143; (2012) 6 SCC 589; AIR 2012 SC 2297; 2012 CRLJ 3189; JT 2012 (5) SC 451; 2012 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 1924; 2012 (2) OLR 351; 2012 (4) RCR(Criminal) 539; 2012 (5) RCR(Criminal) 799; 2012 (4) RLW 3745; 2012(5) SCALE 578; [2012] 6 S.C.R. 62

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 304-B, 498-A - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 161, 313 - Dowry death - Conviction - Challenged - Appellant and other accused persons were charged for offence punishable u/ss. 304-B and 498-A of the IPC - Trial Court held that there were material inconsistencies in the depositions PWs on the issue of demand of dowry as they could not exactly point out the amount of demand and payment - Hence, Trial Court acquitted all the accused persons of all the charges - On appeal, HC held that there was nothing on record to show that deceased, died of fits - There was sufficient evidence on record to show demand of dowry by the appellant from his father-in-law and HC convicted the appellant u/s. 304-B of IPC, however, in respect of other accused persons the order of acquittal passed by the Trial Court was maintained - Hence, instant appeal - Whether there was a dowry demand by the appellant and for that purpose the deceased had been ill-treated to the extent that she had to take a drastic step of committing suicide - Held, in the instant case there had been major improvements/embellishments in the prosecution case and demand by the appellant did not find mention in the statements u/s. 161 of CrPC - Further, the chemical analysis report falsified the theory of suicide by deceased taking any pills - In such a fact-situation, the defence taken by the appellant in his statement u/s. 313 of CrPC that deceased was suffering from fits, as a result of which she died could be plausible - Appellant was given the benefit of doubt - Impugned judgment of the HC was set aside - Appellant acquitted of all the charges - Appeal allowed.
