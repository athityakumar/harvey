Hukam Chand Gupta v Director General, I.C.A.R. and others

Supreme Court of India

25 September 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Surinder Singh Nijjar, H.L. Gokhale

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 411; (2012) 12 SCC 666; AIR 2013 SC 547; 2012(6) ALL MR 971; 2013 (1) AWC 226; 2013 (136) FLR 1; JT 2012 (11) SC 314; 2013 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 950; 2012(10) SCALE 479B; [2012] 8 S.C.R. 831; 2013 (1) SLR 771

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Service

Summary: Service - Administrative - Revised pay - Denial - Challenged - Appellant was initially appointed as Laboratory Assistant later he got further promotions - Subsequently, he was promoted as Assistant Administrative Officer on the basis of seniority-cum-fitness - Respondent revised the pay scale of Assistants from Rs.1400-2600 to Rs.1640-2900 - However, the pay scale of Superintendent was not revised - Appellant submitted representation requesting that his pay scale may be revised on the ground that in Headquarters of Indian Council of Agricultural Research (ICAR), the post of Superintendent was a promotional post from that of Assistant which carries the pay scale of Rs.1640-2900/ - Representation not having been decided, appellant filed OA before Tribunal and Tribunal declined to entertain the claim of appellant - Screening committee of respondent recommended the case of X who was junior to appellant, for financial service in the scale of Rs. 8000-13500/- - Appellant reached the age of superannuation and retired from service - Appellant filed an OA and claimed that X being junior to him could not be put in a higher pay scale - Tribunal dismissed the OA - Aggrieved appellant filed Writ petition before HC and the same was also dismissed - Hence instant appeal - Held, appellant failed to establish that the action of respondents was either discriminatory or beyond the purview of rules - There was no comparison between appellant and X - Appellant had duly earned promotion in his cadre from the lowest rank to the higher rank - Having joined in Group D, he retired on the post of AAO - X had been working in the same pay scale till his promotion on the post of AAO - Therefore, he was held entitled to the second upgradation after 24 years of service - There could not be any straitjacket formula for holding that two posts having the same nomenclature would have to be given the same pay scale - Prescription of pay scales on particular posts was very complex exercise - It required assessment of the nature and quality of duties performed and responsibilities shouldered by incumbents on different posts - Even though, two posts might be referred to by same name, it would not lead to the necessary inference that the posts were identical in every manner - Those were matters to be assessed by expert bodies like the employer or the Pay Commission - Tribunal or the Writ Court would lack the necessary expertise undertake complex exercise of equation of posts or the pay scales - In expressing the aforesaid opinion, instant Court fortified by the observations made in State of Punjab v. Surjit Singh, 2009 Indlaw SC 1054, upon review of a large number of judicial precedents relating to the principle of 'equal pay for equal work', relied on - Appeal dismissed.
