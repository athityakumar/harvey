Union Of India v Mohanlal & Anr

Supreme Court of India

03 July 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, Gyan Sudha Misra

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 191; (2012) 7 SCC 712; AIR 2012 SC 2653; 2012 CRLJ 3644; 2012 (3) JabLJ 36; 2013 (1) RCR(Criminal) 623; 2012(6) SCALE 106; [2012] 6 S.C.R. 823

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985, ss. 8/18(b) r/w. s. 29 - Destruction of seized contraband - Challenged - Petitioner filed appeal against order of the HC whereby the HC has acquitted the respondents of the charges framed against them u/ss. 8/18(b) r/w. s. 29 of Act, primarily for the reason that no evidence regarding the destruction of the 3.36 Kgs. of opium allegedly seized from the respondents had been provided by the prosecution - In the absence of any evidence to show that the seized contraband was destroyed as per the prevalent procedure, the contraband should have been, according to the HC, produced before the Trial Court - HC further held that the failure of the prosecution to do so, therefore, implies a failure to prove the seizure of the contraband from the possession of the respondents - Held, while fight against production, sale and transportation of the NDPS was an ongoing process, it was equally important to ensure that the quantities that were seized by the police and other agencies do not go back in circulation on account of neglect or apathy on the part of those handling the process of seizure, storage and destruction of such contrabands - There cannot be anything worse than the society suffering on account of the greed or negligence of those who were entrusted with the duty of protecting it against the menace that was capable of eating into its vitals - Hence, SC directed collection of the information from the police heads of each one of the States through the Chief Secretary concerned on aspects like what narcotic drugs and psychotropic substances (natural and synthetic) have been seized in the last 10 years and in what quantity - What were the steps, if any, taken by the seizing authorities to prevent damage, loss and pilferage of the narcotic drugs and psychotropic substances (natural and synthetic) during seizure/transit - What were the circulars /notifications /directions /guidelines, if any, issued to competent officers to follow any specific procedure in regard to seizure of contrabands, their storage and destruction - Was there any specified/notified store for storage of the seized contraband in a State, if so, was the storage space available in each district or taluka - If a store/storage space was not available in each district or taluka, where was the contraband sent for storage purposes - What were the steps taken at the time of storage to determine the nature and quantity of the substance being stored and what were the measures taken to prevent substitution and pilferage from the stores - Was there any check stock-register maintained at the site of storage and if so, by whom - What was the condition of the storage facilities at present - Have any circulars/notifications/directions/guidelines been issued to competent officers for care and caution to be exercised during storage - What narcotic drugs and psychotropic substances (natural and synthetic) have been destroyed in the last 10 years and in what quantity - Who was authorised to apply for permission of the Court to destroy the seized contraband - Was any action taken against the person who should have applied for permission to destroy the drugs or should have destroyed and did not do so - What were the steps taken at the time of destruction to determine the nature and quantity of the substance being destroyed - What were the steps taken by competent authorities to prevent damage, loss, pilferage and tampering/substitution of the narcotic drugs and psychotropic substances (natural and synthetic) during transit from point of storage to point of destruction - Was there any specified facility for destruction of contraband in the State - If a facility was not available, where was the contraband sent for destruction purposes - Was any record, electronic or otherwise prepared at the site of destruction of the contraband and by whom - Was any inspection done by the District and Sessions Judge of the store where the seized drugs were kept - Was any report of the inspection conducted, submitted to the Administrative Judge of the HC or the Registry of the HC - Were there any pending applications for destruction of drugs in the district concerned, if so, what was the reason for the delay in the disposal of such application - What level officers including the judicial officers were associated with the process of destruction - At what stages were the magistrates/ judicial officers/ any other officer of the Court associated with seizure/storage/destruction of drugs - What was the average time for completion of trial of NDPS matters - The Chief Secretaries of the States shall ensure that a questionnaire on the above lines was served upon the Director General of Police of the State for a report and on receipt of the report forward the same to the Registrar General of the State HC - The Registrar General of the HC in each State shall be the Nodal Officer and shall ensure collection of the reports from the Chief Secretary of the State concerned, scrutinise the same, get clarifications and further information wherever necessary and submit the report to this Court containing a summary of the information so collected, as early as possible but not later than 3 months from the date of a copy of this order being received by him - The Registrar Generals shall independently secure from the concerned District and Sessions Judges, in their respective States, answer to the queries specified under the head 'Judicial Supervision' within the same period -Chiefs of Central Government agencies viz. Narcotics Control Bureau, Central Bureau of Narcotics, Directorate General of Revenue Intelligence and Commissionerates of Customs and Central Excise including the Indian Coast Guard shall issue similar questionnaire to the concerned officers and submit a report detailing the information required in terms of this order within 3 months - Order accordingly.
