Salauddin Ahmed & Anr v Samta Andolan

Supreme Court of India

29 August 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, J. Chelameswar

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 263; (2012) 10 SCC 235; AIR 2012 SC 3891; 2013 (1) ALD(SC) 9; 2012 (5) AWC 5171; JT 2012 (8) SC 539; 2012(8) SCALE 56; [2012] 7 S.C.R. 402; 2013 (3) SLR 110; 2012 (3) UPLBEC 2445

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Civil Contempt, Constitution (Eighty-Fifth Amendment) Act, 2001, Constitution (Seventy-Seventh Amendment) Act, 1995, Rajasthan Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribes, Backward Classes, Special Backward Classes & Economically Backward Classes (Reservation of Seats in Educational Institutions in the State and of Appointments & Posts in Services under the State) Act of 2008

Summary: Constitution - Administrative - Service - Constitution of Indian, 1950, art. 16(4-A) - Reservation of Seats in Educational Institutions in the State and of Appointments & Posts in Services under the State Act, 2008, ss. 3, 4 - Reservation - Promotion - Seniority - State issued notification for reservation for Scheduled Castes/Scheduled Tribes (SC/ST) candidates to extent of 15% for Scheduled Castes and 7.5% for Scheduled Tribes - Subsequently, reservation was increased to 16% and 12% - Later rules for promotion based on criteria of seniority- cum-merit were introduced in Constitution (Seventy Seventh Amendment) u/art. 16(4-A) was inserted in Constitution to provide for reservation for SC/ST candidates in promotional posts - Parliament passed Constitution (Eighty Fifth Amendment) inserted words consequential seniority for members of reserved category - HC held that even if person in reserved category was promoted earlier than general category candidate due to operation of roster, and subsequently general category candidate was also promoted, candidates in general category would regain their seniority as existing in cadre prior to promotion - Catch-up principle was introduced in Constitution (Eighty Fifth Amendment) - General category employees filed petition against Chief Secretary for not implementing order passed by HC - HC restrained State from giving effect to u/ss. 3 and 4 of the 2008 Act and directed against reservation Special Backward Classes & Economically Backward Classes and had nothing to do with reservation in respect of promotion for SC/ST candidates - Hence instant appeals - Whether such inaction was on account of deliberate intention of State and its authorities - Held, in view that despite fact that there had been delay on the part of State and its authorities in giving effect to observations made in 2 cases, there was no willful or deliberate intention on part of State to defy orders of Court - Fact that Bhatnagar Committee was appointed indicated that State and its authorities had every intention to implement observations, though progress of implementation had been tardy - Accordingly, it was unable to sustain impugned judgment and order of HC holding appellants guilty of contempt of Court for purported violation of order by DB of HC on 05-02-2010 - Hence judgment and order was set aside and directed State and its authorities to act in terms of the report of Bhatnagar Committee - Appeals allowed.
