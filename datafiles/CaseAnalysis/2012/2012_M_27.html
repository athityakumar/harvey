M/S Thakker Shipping P. Ltd. v Commissioner Of Customs (General)

Supreme Court of India

30 October 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 629; (2012) 12 SCC 189; AIR 2012 SC (Supp) 786; 2014 (34) S.T.R. 899

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Customs House Agents Licensing Regulations, 2004

Summary: Customs - Practice & Procedure - Customs Act, 1962, ss.129D (4), 129A (5) - Custom House Agent Licencing Regulations, 2004 regn. 23 - Seizure of custom goods - Pending enquiry - Condonation of delay - Container was intercepted by Commissioner of Customs and found to contain assorted goods of foreign origin - Clearance of goods was handled by appellant referred to as Custom House Agent (CHA) - Appellant's CHA license was placed under suspension pending inquiry u/regn. 23 of Regulations but suspension order was set aside by Customs, Excise and Service Tax Appellate Tribunal (Tribunal) and CHA license was restored - Inquiry proceeded against CHA on diverse charges - Commissioner dropped proceedings u/regn. 23 of Regulations by rejecting findings of inquiry officer - Committee of Chief Commissioners of Customs directed Commissioner to apply to Tribunal for determination - Commissioner made application u/s 129D(4) of the Act, before Tribunal with application for condonation of delay - Tribunal rejected application for condonation of delay and dismissed appeal - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether order passed by Tribunal to pass to reject application for condonation of delay was valid - Held, provisions relating to appeals to Tribunal was made applicable to application made u/s 129D(4) of the Act, and such application should be heard as if it was appeal made against decision or order of adjudicating authority - Any delay in presentation of appeal u/s 129A of the Act, was condonable by Tribunal by virtue of sub-s. (5) - Tribunal was invested with same power for consideration of applications u/s 129D(4) of the Act if it was satisfied that there was sufficient cause for not presenting application within prescribed period as provisions relating to appeals to Tribunal was made applicable to such applications - It was competent for Tribunal to invoke s. 129A(5) of the Act, where application u/s 129D(4) of the Act, was not made within prescribed time and condone delay in making application if it was satisfied that there was sufficient cause for not presenting it within period - Appeal dismissed.
