Praveen Pradhan v State of Uttranchal and another

Supreme Court of India

04 October 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 350; (2012) 9 SCC 734; 2012 ALL MR (Cri) 4124; 2012 CRLJ 4925; JT 2012 (10) SC 478; 2012 (4) RCR(Criminal) 724; 2012(9) SCALE 745; [2012] 8 S.C.R. 1129

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 306 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 482 -Abetment of suicide - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant/accused(bother of deceased) inflicted mental and physical cruelty and harassed and insulted deceased in front of staff of entire factory, and at regular intervals - Accused drove deceased to commit suicide - Accused was charged u/s. 306 IPC - Trial Court convicted accused u/s. 306 IPC - On appeal, HC granted stay of proceedings, initiated on basis of charge-sheet, as an interim measure - However, vide impugned judgment and order said application was then dismissed Hence, instant appeal - Whether impugned judgments of Courts below were sustainable - Held, in instant case, alleged harassment had not been a casual feature, rather remained a matter of persistent harassment - However, it was not a case of a driver or a man having an illicit relationship with a married woman and therefore, could not be compared to situation of deceased - Further, deceased who was a qualified graduate engineer and still suffered persistent harassment and humiliation and additionally, also had to endure continuous illegal demands made by appellant, upon non-fulfillment of which, he would be mercilessly harassed by appellant for a prolonged period of time - Thus, he had also been forced to work continuously for a long durations in factory, but other employees which often even entered to 16-17 hours at a stretch - Such harassment, coupled with utterance of words to the effect, that, 'had there been any other person in his place, he would have certainly committed suicide' was what makes instant case distinct from other cases - Hence, impugned judgment and order of courts below were upheld - Appeal dismissed.
