Super Cassettes Industries Ltd. v Music Broadcast Pvt. Ltd.

Supreme Court of India

04 May 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Surinder Singh Nijjar, J. Chelameswar

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 578; (2012) 5 SCC 488; 2012 (4) ALD(SC) 175; 2012 (4) AWC 3275; 2012 (4) Bom.C.R. 258; JT 2012 (4) SC 627; 2012 (4) MLJ(SC) 974; 2012 (2) RCR(Civil) 961; 2012 (4) RLW 3110

Case Digest

Subject: Intellectual Property; Media & Entertainment

Keywords: Copyright Board, Copyright Act, 1911, Licences, Indian Copyright Act, 1914, Punjab State Election Commission Act, 1994, Compulsory Licence, Karnataka Appellate Tribunal Act, 1976, Armed Forces Tribunal Act, 1985

Summary: Intellectual Property - Civil Procedure - Copy Right Act, 1957, ss. 31 and 31(1)(b) - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O.39, rr. 1 and 2, s. 151 - Compulsory licence - Grant - Challenged - Appellant/Super Cassettes alleged that on 16-5-2008, SC decided 2 cases, in which it upheld the setting aside of compulsory licence granted against appellant by Copyright Board/Board in relation to Entertainment Network India Ltd(ENIL) a radio broadcaster - In other set of matters, where appellant was not a party, SC upheld the grant of compulsory licence in relation to works administered by Phonographic Performance Ltd. (PPL), and remanded the matter to Board to fix rates at which compulsory licences, in relation to works administered by PPL, were to be granted - Pursuant to decision of SC, Board passed order dt. 25-8-2010, fixing the rates, not just for PPL, but for all music providers, including appellant - Music Broadcast Pvt. Ltd.,(MBPL) wrote to appellant informing it that MBPL proposed to broadcast the works in which copyright was owned by appellant Super on the terms fixed in aforesaid order of Board dt. 25-8-2010 - MBPL submitted that it had existing licence from appellant, which had subsisted since 25-3-2002, and was amended and renewed number of times since then - Appellant filed writ petition questioning the order passed by Board dt. 25-8-2010 - HC passed an interim order to effect that order dt. 25-8-2012, passed by the Board should not be relied upon by any of respondents or any other party for a compulsory licence against appellant - MBPL filed application for compulsory licence u/s. 31(1)(b) of the Act, relying solely on rates fixed by the Board for PPL by its order dt. 25-8-2010 - Appellant responded to said offer made by MBPL, on same terms as were prevalent under expired voluntary licence agreement - However, said proposal made by appellant was rejected by MBPL, while other broadcasters continued to broadcast the work of appellant on existing mutually agreed terms which were different from terms set out in order of Board dt. 25-8-2010 - Hence instant appeal - Whether during the pendency of an existing licence granted u/s. 30 of the Act, dispute could be raised with regard to the fees charged u/s. 31(1)(b) of the Act - Held, in instant case, power being sought to be attributed to the Board involves the grant of final relief, which was only relief contemplated u/s. 31 of the Act - However, even in matters under O. 39, rr. 1 and 2 and s. 151 of CPC, an interim relief granting final relief should be given after exercise of great caution and in rare and exceptional cases - In instant case, such power was not even vested in the Board and hence question of granting interim relief by grant of an interim compulsory licence could not arose - To grant an interim compulsory licence during stay of proceedings should amount to granting final relief at interim stage, although power to grant such relief had not vested in the Board - However, such incidental powers could at best be said to exist in order to preserve status-quo, but not to alter the same, as would no doubt happen, if an interim compulsory licence is granted - If legislature had intended that Board should had powers to grant mandatory injunction at interim stage, it should have vested Board with such authority - Impugned Order passed by HC was set aside - Appeals allowed.

Chelameswar, J.

Intellectual Property - Copy Right Act, 1957, s. 31 - Compulsory licence - Grant - Challenged - Whether the Board was a Court or a Tribunal while exercising the jurisdiction u/s. 31 of the Act, or any other Section - Held, both Courts and Tribunals were adjudicatory bodies to whom Legislature entrusts the authority to resolve the disputes falling within jurisdiction conferred upon each of such bodies and brought before them - Jurisdiction was, normally, defined with reference either to - Copyright in a 'work', undoubtedly, was a valuable legal right subsisting in the 'owner' or somebody claiming through the owner - Such right had more than one dimension - It might have a commercial value depending upon quality, nature and public demand of work - It might also have aesthetic value - It was, further, right of owner to decide on what terms and conditions (which need not necessarily be related to money alone), he should part with copyright of his work if ever he decides to part with it - However, s. 31 of the Act, created an exception to abovementioned principle of right of owner of copyright -In substance - the Section deprived the 'copyright' of the 'owner' against his volition - In other words, by s. 31 of the Act, State was authorised, by its coercive powers, to deprive owner of his copyright in work, which was his property and right to enjoy such property in manner as owner of the property pleases - Power u/s. 31 of the Act, to grant a compulsory licence meant for avoiding withholding of republication or refuse to allow performance in pubic of some 'work' - was, essentially, for benefit of the public - Appeal disposed of.
