Rakhal Debnath v State Of West Bengal

Supreme Court of India

04 September 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla, Swatanter Kumar

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 306; (2012) 11 SCC 347; 2012 ALL MR (Cri) 3750; 2012 (4) JCC 2510; JT 2012 (8) SC 655; 2012 (4) RCR(Criminal) 322; 2012(8) SCALE 497; [2012] 7 S.C.R. 513

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 306 and 498A - Abetment to commit suicide - Conviction - Challenged - Prosecution alleged that deceased was instigated by the appellant to take the extreme decision of committing suicide by pouring kerosene on herself and set herself on fire - Trial Court acquitted the appellant from the offence punishable u/ss. 306 and 498A of IPC - On appeal, HC reversed the judgment of the Trial Court and held the appellant guilty on both the charges - Hence, instant appeal - Whether HC rightly reversed the judgment of the Trial court - Held, no inconsistency in the evidence of PW3 and other witnesses who had the advantage of hearing from the mouth of the deceased about the conduct of the appellant relating to his demand, as well as, his illicit intimacy with the 'X' - Moreover, deceased committed suicide within 35 days from the date of marriage, coupled with the untrammelled evidence before the court below about the cruelty meted out to the deceased, fully established the guilt of the appellant of abetment to the deceased to commit suicide as well as the cruelty u/s. 498A of IPC - Impugned order of the HC upheld - Appeal dismissed.
