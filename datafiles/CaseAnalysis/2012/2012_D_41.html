State of Rajasthan v Dr. Rajkumar Agarwal and another

Supreme Court of India

17 August 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjana Prakash Desai, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 371; (2012) 8 SCC 616; AIR 2013 SC 847; 2012 ALL MR (Cri) 3001; 2012 CRLJ 4754; 2012 (4) JCC 2763; 2012(5) M.P.H.T. 138; 2012 (4) RCR(Criminal) 192; 2013 (2) RLW 1475; [2012] 7 S.C.R. 319; 2012 (348) WLN(SC) 75

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 482 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 7, 13 (1) (d) (2) - Illegal gratification - Quashing of FIR - Challenged - Respondent no. 1 allegedly demanded illegal gratification for performing the operation of 'S' and for better treatment - Respondent no. 1 filed petition u/s. 482 of CrPC for quashing of FIR before HC - HC quashed complaint filed against respondent no. 1 - Hence instant appeal - Appellant contended that HC had fallen into a grave error in quashing FIR, HC misinterpreted the ratio of judgment of SC in State of Haryana v. Bhajan Lal, 1990 Indlaw SC 91 - FIR and other material collected by prosecution prima facie made out a strong case against respondent no.1 - Held, it was true that complainant was not related to 'S' but nothing was brought on record to even prima facie establish that complainant holds any grudge against respondent no.1 because respondent no.1 had knowledge about alleged irregularities in respect of his Chemist shop - In statements made u/s. 161 of CrPC, 'S' and her husband stated that complainant was treating 'S' as his aunt and he had admitted her to the hospital - Further, they stated that respondent no.1 did not demand any money from them and they were not aware whether respondent no. 1 demanded any money from complainant - Thus, these statements support complainant's case that he was treating 'S' as his aunt; that he had admitted her to hospital and that he had dealt with respondent no.1 - Respondent no.1 was relying on 3affidavits - Affidavits were filed by 'S', her husband and another patient - Surprisingly, in these affidavits, 'S' and her husband had given totally contrary version - They had gone on to say that complainant had lodged a false complaint against respondent no.1 - Another patient in his affidavit stated that respondent no. 1 had never taken any money from him for treatment - Hence, at this stage, SC did not want to give any final opinion on these affidavits but SC found it difficult to quash the complaint on the basis of these affidavits - Further, it was not possible to agree with HC that transcription did not corroborate FIR - It was also pertinent to note that when complaint was lodged, 'S' was still in hospital, probably because after the money was handed over, she was to be discharged, and in fact, her discharge card was found on the table of respondent no. 1 - In view of contents of FIR and nature of evidence collected by investigating agency, this was certainly not a case where FIR could be quashed - Impugned order was set aside - Appeal disposed of.
