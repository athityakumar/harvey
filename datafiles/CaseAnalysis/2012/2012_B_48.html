Shree Om Enterprises Private Limited v Bses Rajdhani Power Limited

Supreme Court of India

22 November 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjan Gogoi, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 476; (2013) 10 SCC 130; 2012 (4) CLT(SC) 483; JT 2012 (11) SC 351; 2014 (3) MahLJ 134; 2014 (2) MPLJ 481; 2013 (2) RCR(Civil) 242; 2012(11) SCALE 243

Case Digest

Subject: Electricity & Energy

Keywords: Conduit

Summary: Electricity & Energy - Practice & Procedure - Maximum Permissible Sanctioned Load - Surcharges - Change of Category - Sustainability - Appellant-plaintiff was engaged in business of printing of calendars, diaries, stationery items, packing materials - Respondent issued 2 separate electrical connections for running printing press - Respondent issued letter to appellant stating that during inspection it was found that connected load was found to be beyond maximum permissible sanction load for small industries and same had effect of placing plaintiff in category of Large Industrial Power Consumers and that he would be billed higher tariff and further that it was liable to pay surcharge at prevailing rate - Appellant filed suit for injunction restraining respondents from enforcing disconnection notice - Respondent pleaded that during inspection it was revealed that total connected load was excess of limit for SIP consumers - Suit was dismissed by all courts, hence instant appeal - Appellant contended that no inspection was conducted in premises and that respondent could not substantiate that respondent was manufacturing PVC pipes - Whether Trial Court was justified in dismissing suit of appellant - Held, plaintiff in its reply dt. 6-1-1992 to notices issued by Defendant had only asserted that it was engaged in manufacture and printing of calendars etc. and that it had not exceeded sanctioned load, there was no positive stand taken with regard to findings of inspection as mentioned in report, neither any evidence had been led by plaintiff to establish that machinery mentioned in inspection note to have been found installed in its premises were not so installed or that such machinery was not used or utilized by plaintiff for manufacture of PVC conduit pipes - It was incumbent on part of plaintiff, who had claimed in suit that report of inspection was incorrect, to prove said facts by means of legally acceptable evidence, no such evidence was forthcoming, perhaps, because plaintiff had taken stand that no inspection at all was carried out, mere inability of DW 1 to specify status of plaintiff's representative present at site would not, in any way, affect credibility of fact that an inspection was, infact, carried out - No infirmity or illegality was disclosed in any of actions of defendant infringing any known right of plaintiff so as to entitle it to a decree of perpetual injunction as prayed for - Appeal dismissed
