Bipromasz Bipron Trading Sa v Bharat Electronics Limited (Bel)

Supreme Court of India

08 May 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Surinder Singh Nijjar

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 606; (2012) 6 SCC 384; 2012 (4) AWC 3977; JT 2012 (5) SC 214; 2012 (5) MahLJ 534; 2012 (4) MPLJ 89; [2012] 5 S.C.R. 743

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, ss. 3(2), 11(4), 11(6) - Appointment of arbitration - Maintainability - Certain disputes arose between petitioner and respondent related to some business transaction - Petitioner seeks reference of disputes to independent and impartial sole Arbitrator u/s. 11(6) of the Act r/w. paragraphs 2, 3 of appointment of Arbitrators by Chief Justice of India Scheme, 1996 - In terms of arbitration agreement, petitioner had issued necessary notice and respondent had not agreed for such appointment of independent arbitrator - Hence, instant Petition - Whether instant petition was maintainable in view of claim made by respondent - Held, order passed by authority could not be said to take effect unless same was communicated to party affected and order passed by competent authority or by appropriate authority and kept with itself, could be changed, modified, cancelled and thus denuding such order of characteristics of final order - Such un communicated order could neither create any rights in favour of party, nor take away rights of any affected party, till it was communicated - Further, s. 3(2) of the 1996 Act was mere reiteration of general principle of law - Hence, in exercise of powers u/ss. 11(4), 11(6) of the Act, r/w. Para 2 of the Scheme appointment of arbitrator was done as sole arbitrator, to adjudicate disputes that had arose between parties, on such terms and conditions as sole arbitrator deemed fit and proper - Thus, sole arbitrator should decide all disputes arising between parties without being influenced by any prima facie opinion expressed in instant order - Petition disposed of.
