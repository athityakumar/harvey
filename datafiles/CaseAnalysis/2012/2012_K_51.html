Kunal Majumdar v State Of Rajasthan

Supreme Court of India

12 September 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 291; (2012) 9 SCC 320; 2012 ALL MR (Cri) 3764; 2012 CRLJ 4635; JT 2012 (8) SC 586; 2012 (4) RCR(Criminal) 363; 2012 (4) RLW 3333; 2012(8) SCALE 641; [2012] 8 S.C.R. 706

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 376 and 511 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 366(1), 367, 368, 369, 370 and 371 - Rape and murder - Death sentence - Confirmation of - Prosecution alleged that appellant raped and murdered deceased - Trial Court held that the appellant was guilty of the charges falling u/ss. 302 and 376/511 of IPC and imposed the punishment of death sentence for the offence found proved u/s. 302 of IPC and sentence of 7 years' rigorous imprisonment for the offence u/s. 376/511 of IPC - Since death sentence was imposed, the case was referred for confirmation u/s. 366 (1) of CrPC to the HC and ordered to await for the confirmation of the HC before its execution - HC altered death sentence to the one of life imprisonment u/s. 302 of IPC while maintaining the sentence awarded for offences u/s. 376 r/w s. 511 of IPC - Hence, instant appeal - Whether HC rightly altered death sentence awarded by trial court to life imprisonment - Held, in a case for consideration for confirmation of death sentence u/s. 366 (1) CrPC, the HC was bound to examine the Reference with particular reference to the provisions contained in ss. 367 to 371 CrPC - Under s. 367 of CrPC, when Reference was submitted before the HC, the HC, if satisfied that a further enquiry should be made or additional evidence should be taken upon, any point bearing upon the guilt or innocence of the convict person, it can make such enquiry or take such evidence itself or direct it to be made or taken by the Trial court - In the instant case, HC merely recorded to the effect that the appellant pleaded for sympathy to commute the death sentence into one for life for the offence falling u/s. 302 of IPC and that there was no opposition from the Public Prosecutor - HC on that sole ground and by merely stating that there was no use of force of severe nature on the deceased at the hands of the appellant and that the commission of offence of murder cannot be held to be brutal or inhuman and consequently the death sentence was liable to be altered as one for life for the offence u/s. 302 of IPC - HC did not bother to exercise its jurisdiction vested in it u/s. 366(1) r/w ss. 368 to 370 and 392 of CrPC in letter and spirit - Impugned order of the HC was set aside and matter was remanded back to the HC for deciding the reference u/s. 366 of CrPC in the manner it ought to have been decided - Appeal disposed of.
