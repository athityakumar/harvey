Rameshkumar Agarwal v Rajmala Exports Private Limited and others

Supreme Court of India

30 March 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, J. Chelameswar

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 437; (2012) 5 SCC 337; AIR 2012 SC 1887; 2012 (4) ALD(SC) 67; 2012 (93) ALR 215; 2012 (4) AWC 3624; 2012 (4) Bom.C.R. 95; JT 2012 (4) SC 12; 2012 (4) MLJ(SC) 326; 2012 (2) RCR(Civil) 739; 2012 (116) RD 774; [2012] 3 S.C.R. 992

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 7, r. 17 - Amendment of plaint - Impleadment of parties - Sustainability - Respondent no. 1/plaintiff-company filed suit for specific performance of agreement before HC against appellant/defendants alleging that appellant had agreed to sell his 50% share in the suit property to company for a consideration of Rs.1,85,00,000/ - Further alleged that appellant ensured that respondent No. 4/brother of appellant would sell his 50% undivided share in the property to Company for Rs.3,00,00,000/ and represented him as an agent of respondent No. 4 - On 6-9-2007, respondent No.1 took out Notice of Motion in which an ex-parte ad interim order was passed in their favour - Appellant sought for vacating ex-parte order, HC vacated the exparte ad interim order - Respondent no. 1 took out Chamber Summons with a prayer to amend the plaint by impleading other parties - HC partly allowed said chamber summons - Aggrieved by said order, appellant filed appeal before DB - DB dismissed said appeal - Hence instant appeal - Appellant contended that proposed amendment altered the cause of action - Further contended that in said agreement for sale dt.2-2-2006, the parties being only appellant and respondent No. 1 and other proposed parties, particularly, plaintiff Nos. 2 & 3 had nothing to do with the contract, and Courts below committed an error in entertaining the amendment application - Held, Single Judge himself agreed with the objection as to proposed defendant Nos. 3-5 and found that they were not necessary parties to the suit, however, inasmuch as the main object of the amendment sought for by respondent no. 1 was to explain how the money was paid, permitted the other reliefs including impleadment of plaintiff Nos. 2 & 3 as parties to the suit - Further, it was clear that while deciding application for amendment ordinarily the Court should not refuse bona fide, legitimate, honest and necessary amendments and should never permit mala fide and dishonest amendments - Purpose and object of Order 7, r. 17 of CPC was to allow either party to alter or amend his pleadings in such manner and on such terms as may be just - Normally, amendments were allowed in the pleadings to avoid multiplicity of litigations - In instant case, amendment application came to be filed immediately after filing of the suit i.e. before commencement of trial and taking note of the fact that Single Judge confined the relief only to a certain extent and also that in the proposed amendment respondent no. 1 wanted to explain how the money was paid, though necessary averments in the form of foundation was already laid in the original plaint, hence, by this process respondent no. 1 was not altering the cause of action and in any way prejudice appellants - Respondent no. 1 furnished more details about the mode of payment of consideration - Hence, there was no inconsistency and amendment sought for was not barred by limitation - Appeal dismissed.
