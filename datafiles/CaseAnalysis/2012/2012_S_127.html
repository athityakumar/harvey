Jeetu @ Jitendera & Ors. v State Of Chhattisgarh

Supreme Court of India

04 December 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 516; (2013) 11 SCC 489; 2013 ALL MR (Cri) 345; 2013 (1) CGLJ 323; 2013 (1) JCC 794; JT 2012 (12) SC 94; 2013 (1) OLR 807; 2013 (1) RCR(Criminal) 524; 2012(11) SCALE 630; [2012] 13 S.C.R. 161

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 147,327,364-A,323,34, 149 329 - Falsely Implicated - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant-accused were convicted for offence u/ss. 147,327,149 of IPC - Prosecution case was that appellant and other accused had demanded informant money for liquor and when informant refused they took him near railway station and assaulted him - Case u/ss. 327,366, 323, 34 was registered and chargesheet for offence u/ss. 147,327,364-A,323,34 was filed - Trial Court appreciating evidence on record Trial Court did not find accused guilty of offences u/ss. 148,329,149,364,149 but convicted appellant and other accused for offence u/ss. 147,327,149 of IPC - Appeal before HC, was disposed of by granting relief with regard to sentencing - Hence instant appeal - Appellant contended that they were falsely implicated in case and that conviction u/s. 327 was not sustainable inasmuch as no charge was framed u/s. 383 of IPC - Whether conviction of appellant by Trial Court was sustainable - Held, it was luminescent that HC had not made any effort to satisfy its conscience and accepted concession given by appellant in a routine manner , when a convicted person preferred an appeal, he had legitimate expectation to be dealt with by Courts in accordance with law, he had intrinsic faith in criminal justice dispensation system and it was sacred duty of adjudicatory system to remain alive to faith, he had embedded trust in his counsel that he shall put forth his case to best of his ability assailing conviction and to do full justice to case - Counsel was expected to assist Courts in reaching a correct conclusion, therefore, it was obligation of Court to decide appeal on merits and not accept concession and proceed to deal with sentence, for mode and method defeats fundamental purpose of justice delivery system - SC, was compelled to note that in many cases where HC, after recording non-challenge to conviction, had proceeded to dwell upon proportionality of quantum of sentence, SC, clearly stated that same being impermissible in law should not be taken resort to, it should be borne in mind that a convict who had been imposed substantive sentence was deprived of his liberty, stem of life that should not ordinarily be stenosed, and hence, it was duty of Court to see that cause of justice was sub-served with serenity in accordance with established principles of law - Hence, judgment and order passed by HC were set aside and appeal was remitted to HC to be decided on merits in accordance with law - Appeal allowed.
