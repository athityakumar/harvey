Tehri Hydro Dev. Corpn. Ltd.& Anr. v Jai Prakash Asso. Ltd.

Supreme Court of India

25 September 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjan Gogoi, R.M. Lodha, Anil R. Dave

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 325; (2012) 12 SCC 10; AIR 2013 SC 920; 2012 (6) AWC 5441; 2012 (4) CLT(SC) 213; 2016 (3) CompLJ 79; JT 2012 (9) SC 418; 2013 (1) RCR(Civil) 262; 2012(9) SCALE 433; [2012] 8 S.C.R. 813

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Uttar Pradesh Civil Laws (Reforms and Amendment) Act, 1976

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Practice & Procedure - Arbitration Act of 1940, s. 29 - Uttar Pradesh Civil Laws (Reforms and Amendment) Act, 1976, para 7A - Pendente lite interest - Award of - Challenged - Maintainability - Appellant (Corporation) and respondent (Contractor) entered for completion of a work - Respondent's final bill was not paid and arbitration clause was invoked by parties - Claim of respondent was proved and an award was passed holding that respondent was entitled to Rs.10,17,461/- with 6% p.a, from the date of invocation of claim till date of award and 12% p.a from the date of award till payment or till award was made Rule of Court - However, for claim of Rs.12.50 Lakhs, which refundable security deposited made by respondent with appellant, respondent was left with option of settling said claim in an amicable manner or by resorting to a civil suit for recovery of same - Aggrieved respondent and appellant approached Trial Court, which upheld claim of respondent and further held that respondent was entitled to Rs.12.50 Lakhs - Aggrieved appellant field appeal before HC - By impugned order HC, partly allowed appeal holding that claim of Rs.12.50 lakhs could not have awarded by Trial Court as said entitlement was not gone into by Arbitrators and remanded matter to be settled by an Arbitrator appointed by it - Hence, instant appeal - Whether impugned order of HC was justified - Held, arbitration proceeding in respect of specific claims of respondent stood barred in view of earlier arbitration proceeding between parties - From arbitrators' award dt.15-1-1994, appeared that arbitrators recorded that both parties agree that arbitrators should adjudicate both disputes relating to refund of deposit of Rs.12.50 lakhs and payment of final bill of Rs.10,17,461/- - Award in respect of claim of Rs.10,17,461/- made by Arbitrator and affirmed by Courts below would not require any further scrutiny - In respect of claim of Rs.12.50 Lakhs, which was not adjudicated upon by Arbitrators, Trial Court was patently wrong in decreeing said claim - There was no reasonable basis for HC that entitlement of respondent to Rs.12.50 Lakhs should be determined by Arbitrator nominated by it - Rather, that issue should have left for determination in accordance with procedure agreed upon by parties, if parties were, at all, inclined to go into a further round of adjudication at that stage - Thus, parties were left to work out their remedies as might be considered best and most appropriate in facts and circumstances of case - Clauses of contract agreement between parties clearly revealed that no interest was payable to respondent for delay in payment, either, interim or final, for works done or on any amount lying in deposit by way of guarantee - Consequence contemplated under agreement would be applicable both to a situation where withholding of payment was on account of some dispute or difference between parties or even otherwise - Provisions of 1976 Act amending First Schedule to 1940 Act did not assist respondent in any manner to sustain claim of award of interest pendente lite - Para 7A to First Schedule of 1976 Act, was only an enabling provision which would have no application to a situation where there was an express bar to entertainment or payment of interest on delayed payment either of an amount due for work done or of an amount lying in deposit as security - Thus, grant of pendente lite interest on claim of Rs.10,17,461/- was not justified - Grant of interest on amount of Rs.10,17,461/-from the date of award till date of decree or date of payment, was upheld - Rate of interest should be 12% p.a as determined in arbitration proceeding between parties - Award and orders of Courts below were modified - Appeal partly allowed.
