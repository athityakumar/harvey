Purbanchal Cables and Conductors Private Limited v Assam State Electricity Board and another, (2) Shanti Conductors Private Limited and another

Supreme Court of India

10 July 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Dattu, A.R. Dave

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 405; (2012) 7 SCC 462; AIR 2012 SC 3167; 2012 (2) DRTC 305; 2012 (6) MahLJ 24; 2012 (4) MPLJ 282; 2012 (4) RCR(Civil) 6; 2013 (118) RD 18; [2012] 6 S.C.R. 905

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Conductors

Summary: Civil Procedure - Contract & Commercial - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Interest on Delayed Payments to Small Scale and Ancillary Industrial Undertakings Act, 1993 - Delayed payments - Interest levied - Validity - Challenged - Supplier was the manufacturer of Aluminium Conductors Steel Reinforced for various specifications - Respondent/Board had placed orders for supply of ACSR of different specifications in three (3) quarterly phases - In pursuance to the said supply order, supplier had initially made delivery of goods with respect to three bills on 16-9-1992, but did not receive payment from respondent - Supplier had completed the entire supply by 12-10-1993 and received the payment for such supplies from respondent - In pursuance to such supplies, supplier raised demand for interest on delayed payment made by respondent - Supplier instituted Money Suit for the payment of interest to the tune of '24,57,927.28/-, on delayed payment of principal amount by respondent, under the 1993, Act - Said suit was decreed in favour of supplier - Aggrieved by the said order, respondent filed appeal before HC - DB allowed the appeal and dismissed the suit on the ground that suit was not maintainable as no amount was due on the date of institution of the suit - In another similar matter Board had placed two supply orders for the manufacture and supply of KM ACSR Penther Conductors, and supplier completed the supplies and after the receipt of the entire amount, supplier filed a suit for interest on delayed payment by the Board in terms of the provisions of the 1993 Act - However, suit filed by supplier was decreed and awarded a sum of '51,60,507.42 by way of interest for the delayed payment - Aggrieved by the said order, the Board preferred an appeal DB - DB referred the matter to Full Bench to determine whether suit was maintainable only for interest and whether the provisions of the Act was applicable to contracts concluded prior to its commencement, where the delayed payment was made after its commencement - Full Bench came to the conclusion that suit for only interest was also maintainable and the matter was remanded back to DB - Aggrieved instant appeals - (A) Whether a suit for interest alone was maintainable under the provisions of the Act - Held, supplier whose payment was delayed by buyer prior to the commencement of the Act, could file a suit for payment of the principal amount along with the interest - Supplier, thus, had the vested right to claim principal amount along with interest thereon in case of a delay in payment by buyer and it was the discretion of the Court to award this interest - Hence, interest awarded was a discretion exercised by the Court, on the principal amount claimed, in case of a suit for recovery of payment by the supplier if such payment was delayed by the buyer - With the commencement of the Act, a new vested right existed with supplier, that being, if there was delay in payment after the acceptance of goods by buyer, supplier could file a suit for claiming interest at a higher rate, as prescribed by the Act - (B) Whether the Act would be applicable to contracts that have been concluded prior to the commencement of the Act - Held, It had been approved by SC in Modern Industries v. Steel Authority of India Limited 2010 Indlaw SC 268 - There was no doubt about the fact that the Act was a substantive law as vested rights of entitlement to a higher rate of interest in case of delayed payment accrued in favour of the supplier and a corresponding liability was imposed on the buyer - Question regarding the applicability of the Act to contracts concluded prior to coming into force of the Act was no longer res integra - Said question was answered by SC in the case of Assam Small Scale Industries Development Corpn. Ltd. V. J.D. Pharmaceuticals 2005 Indlaw SC 609 - Ratio of the aforesaid decision in Assam Small Scale Industries case was clearly applicable and would squarely govern the facts of instant case as well - Said decision was rendered by instant Court after appreciating the entire facts as also all the relevant laws on the issue and therefore, SC did not find any reason to take a different view than what was taken by instant Court in the aforesaid judgment - There was no dispute with regard to the fact that the supply order was placed with the respondents on 16-7-1992 for supply of the pipes which date was admittedly prior to the date on which this Act came into effect - There was no ambiguity in the aforesaid judgment passed by SC - Intent and the purpose of the Act, as made in para 37 of the judgment, were quite clear and apparent - When SC said 'transaction' it meant initiation of the transaction i.e. placing of the supply orders and not the completion of the transactions which would be completed only when the payment was made - SC in Shakti Tubes Ltd. v. State of Bihar 2009 Indlaw SC 913 - Constitution Bench of SC in Keshav Mills Co. Ltd. v. CIT 1965 Indlaw SC 164 crystallized the position with regard to what the Court should do when a plea for consideration of an earlier judgment was made - SC was in full agreement with the view expressed in Keshav Mills Co. Ltd. v. CIT 1965 Indlaw SC 164 - In light of this dictum, and the factum that no case had been made out for reconsideration by suppliers, SC did not see any reason much or less good reason to doubt the correctness of the decision in Assam Small Scale Industries or Shakti Tubes - When there were four decisions of instant Court with regard to the applicability of the Act for contracts entered into prior to the commencement of the Act, and when the plea for reconsideration had been expressly rejected in the past, it would be against the spirit of doctrine of stare decisis for SC to take any view in divergence with same - Appeals dismissed.
