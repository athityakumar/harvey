State Of Gujarat & Ors v Arvindkumar T. Tiwari & Anr

Supreme Court of India

14 September 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 293; (2012) 9 SCC 545; AIR 2012 SC 3281; 2012 (135) FLR 560; JT 2012 (11) SC 521; 2013 (1) MahLJ 555; 2012 (7) MLJ(SC) 133; 2013 (1) MPLJ 256; 2012(8) SCALE 664; [2012] 7 S.C.R. 1072; 2013 (1) SLR 1; 2012 (4) WLN(SC) 161

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Compassionate appointment - Entitlement of - Father of respondent No.1 who was working in the Police Department, State of Gujarat as the Assistant Sub-Inspector of Police, died in harness on 9-4-1999 - Immediately thereafter, respondent No.1 filed an application for employment on compassionate ground, for the post of Peon - Application was declined by department - However, on appeal HC allowed application of respondent No.1 - Appellant contended that HC erred in observing that the new policy/rules did not apply retrospectively, and that the case of respondent No.1 should be considered in light of the then existing rules, i.e., the rules which were in force prior to 2005 - Earlier, employment on compassionate ground in the Department of Police was governed by way of Circular dt. 16-12-1991, which provided that employment in Class III or Class IV posts, should be accorded on compassionate ground to deserving candidates on the basis of their educational qualification -

Held, person who did not possess the requisite qualification could not even apply for recruitment for the reason that his appointment would be contrary to the statutory rules was, and would therefore, be void in law - Lacking eligibility for the post could not be cured at any stage and appointing such a person would amount to serious illegibility and not mere irregularity - Such a person could not approach the court for any relief for the reason that he does not have a right which could be enforced through court - Prit Singh v. S.K. Mangal and Ors., 1992 Indlaw SC 404, relied on - Claim of the respondent was earlier rejected on the ground that, the family had adequate financial status and the amount of pension being given was actually over and above the limit fixed by the appellant issuing the guidelines - Subsequently, when the case was reconsidered upon the direction of the court, it was found that the respondent did not meet the requisite eligibility criteria i.e., 10th standard certificate - Admittedly, the respondent was 8th standard fail, and thus, he could be considered only as 7th standard pass and SC should therefore consider, whether he could have been offered appointment to a Class IV post - Cl. 9 thereof, provided that no relaxation in educational qualification(s) for the purpose of giving compassionate appointment to the dependant(s) of a deceased employee, would be permissible - However, such relaxation could be granted if there exists some requirement of minimum qualification(s) with respect to the said post - Cl. 11 thereof, provided that a dependant could, in fact, be given appointment on compassionate ground, on the basis of the pass marks obtained by him in the new Secondary School Certificate and in view thereof, as respondent No.1 was admittedly only 8th standard (fail), he was therefore, ineligible for the post - Even otherwise, if the direction of HC was complied with and the case is considered as per the un-amended provisions in existence prior to 2005, the financial limits fixed therein, would automatically be applicable - Respondent No.1's application dt. 11-5-1999 revealed that his date of birth was 1-3-1976, and further that he has studied only upto the 8th standard (fail) - SC was of the considered opinion that since 1991, the eligibility criteria for a Class IV post was set as, the passing of the 10th standard, and as the said respondent had been unable to pass even the 8th standard, he was most certainly, not eligible to apply for the said post - It was neither desirable, nor permissible in law, for HC to issue direction to relax the said eligibility criteria and appoint respondent No.1 merely on humanitarian grounds - Thus, the question framed by HC with respect to whether the application for compassionate employment was to be considered as per existing rules, or under the rules as existing on the date of death of the employee, was not required to be considered - Appeal allowed.
