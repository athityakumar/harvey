M/S Michigan Rubber (India) Ltd. v The State Of Karnataka & Ors

Supreme Court of India

17 August 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, Ranjan Gogoi

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 449; (2012) 8 SCC 216; AIR 2012 SC 2915; 2012 (4) BC 177; 2013 (4) KarLJ 231; 2013 (1) RCR(Civil) 202; [2012] 8 S.C.R. 128

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Public Procurements Act, 1999, Public Procurements Rules, 2000

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Practice & Procedure - Karnataka Transparency in Public Procurements Act, 1999 - Karnataka Transparency in Public Procurements Rules, 2000 - Tender prequalification - Criterion - Public interest - Challenged - Respondent (Corporation) floated a tender for supply of Tyres, Tubes and Flaps with certain pre-qualification criteria - Appellant (company) was supplier, challenged pre-qualification before HC - Thereafter, respondent modified pre-qualification criteria and issued tender wherein a new pre-qualification criterion was specified - Aggrieved appellant filed petition before HC, which was dismissed - Aggrieved appellant filed an appeal before HC - DB dismissed appeal - Hence, instant appeal - Appellant contended that specified conditions in prequalification was unreasonable, arbitrary, discriminatory and opposed to public interest in general and the impugned pre-qualification was included to exclude appellant from tender bidding process - Whether impugned inclusion of prequalification for bidding was justified - Held, it was explained that after going into various complaints, in order to achieve good results, new Tyre mileage and safety of public etc., and after noting that vehicle/chassis manufacturers have strict quality control system, it was thought fit to incorporate similar criteria as a pre-qualification for procurement of tyres - According to respondent Corporation and State that conditions were aimed at sole purpose of obtaining good quality and reliable supply of materials and there was no ulterior motive in stipulating said conditions - Impugned conditions were incorporated in tender notice pursuant to a decision of Contract Management Group (CMG of Corporation, which was an institutional mechanism for the purpose of devising proper method in matter, of procurement of materials to Corporation - CMG consisted of various high level officials representing different departments of Corporation - CMG deliberated on question of conditions to be incorporated in matter of calling of tenders for supply of tyres, tubes and flaps and it was felt by CMG that impugned 2 conditions should be essential qualifications of any tenderer - Said policy decision was taken in best interest of Corporation and members of traveling public to whom it was committed to provide best possible service - It was seen from records that pursuant to tender notice 7 bids were received including that of appellant and accordingly, contracts were entered into with successful bidder by Corporation - Court would not normally interfere with policy decision and in matters challenging the award of contract by State or public authorities - Appellant failed to establish that the same was contrary to public interest and beyond pale of discrimination or unreasonable - Impugned conditions did not, in any way, could be classified as arbitrary, discriminatory or mala fide - Impugned judgment and order of HC was upheld - Appeal dismissed.
