Avinash Sadashiv Bhosale (D) through Lrs. v Union of India and others

Supreme Court of India

25 September 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Surinder Singh Nijjar, H.L. Gokhale

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 318; (2012) 13 SCC 142; AIR 2012 SC (Supp) 755; 2013 (1) AWC 387; 2013 (1) Bom.C.R. 55; 2012 (135) FLR 564; JT 2012 (9) SC 566; 2013 (1) LLJ 1; 2012(9) SCALE 505; [2012] 9 S.C.R. 86; 2012 (6) SLR 749; 2012 (4) UPLBEC 2634

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: State Bank of India Officers Service Rules, 1992

Summary: Service - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120(B), 420, 467, 468, 471 and 201 - State Bank of India Officers Service Rules, 1992, rr. 50(4) , 68(2)(iii) - Fraudulent transactions - Criminal prosecution - Dismissal - Challenged - Appellant (bank manager) was suspended from service for alleged fraudulent transactions and was prosecuted for having committed offences u/ss. 120(B), 420, 467, 468, 471 and 201 of IPC - However, appellant along with other co-accused were acquitted with all criminal charges - Thereafter, inquiry officer in his report, held all charges against appellant - Disciplinary Authority was dismissed from service - Aggrieved appellant field a statutory appeal before Appellate Authority, which was dismissed - Aggrieved appellant filed a petition before HC - DB dismissed the same - Hence, instant appeal - Appellant contended that appellant was acquitted with all criminal charges by Criminal Court and that continuation of departmental proceedings after his acquittal was in violation of principles u/art. 20(2) of Constitution - Whether impugned order of HC was justified - Held, charges against appellant in criminal trial related to commission of criminal offences u/ss. 120(B), 420, 467, 468, 471 and 201 of IPC - Proof of criminal charges was depended upon prosecution producing proof beyond reasonable doubt relating to culpability of appellant along with other persons - In departmental proceedings, basic charge was that appellant whilst posted as a branch Manager of, failed to discharge his duties with utmost integrity, honesty, devotion and diligence to ensure and protect interest of Bank and acted in a manner unbecoming of a bank officer - Said charge clearly related to manner in which appellant performed duties as manager of branch of Bank - It had nothing to do with any criminal liability attaching to such conduct -Standard of integrity required of the Bank officials should be above suspicion - Appellant failed to maintain such high standards of integrity and acted in violation of r. 50(4) of the Rules - Failure of prosecution in producing necessary evidence before Trial Court could not have any adverse impact on evidentiary value of material produced by Bank before Inquiry Officer in departmental proceedings - Before Inquiry Officer, record and relevant documents which clearly established that appellant had exceeded his discretionary powers in purchasing cheques and issuing demand drafts to show undue favour to 3 construction companies named in charge sheet - Inquiry Officer could not be said to be based on no evidence - Findings of Inquiry Officer could not be nullified so long as there was some relevant evidence in support of conclusions recorded by Inquiry Officer - In instant case, all relevant documents were produced in Inquiry to establish the charges leveled against appellant and appellant did not doubt the authenticity of documents produced by Bank - Appellant merely stated that signature on documents were not his, which statement was nullified by a witness, produced by Bank - Witness clearly stated that he recognized the signature of appellant as he had been working as his subordinate - Appellant could not take any advantage of the findings of innocence recorded by Criminal Court - 'clean chit' given by Trial Court was influenced by failure of prosecution to lead necessary evidence - No advantage of same could be taken by appellant in departmental proceedings - Ajit Kumar Nag v. G.M.(P.J.)Indian Oil Corporation Limited Haldia and Others, 2005 Indlaw SC 585 and Depot Manager, A.P. State Road Transport Corporation v Mohd. Yousuf Miya and Others, 1996 Indlaw SC 1923, referred to - Impugned order of HC was justified - Appeal dismissed.
