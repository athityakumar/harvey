Dr. Mohammad Khalil Chisti and another v State of Rajasthan and others

Supreme Court of India

12 December 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, Ranjan Gogoi

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 537; (2013) 2 SCC 541; 2013 (2) CGLJ 339; 2013 CRLJ 637; JT 2012 (12) SC 405; 2013 (1) MLJ(Crl) 198; 2013 (3) RCR(Criminal) 514; 2012(12) SCALE 254; [2012] 13 S.C.R. 777

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 148, 302 and 324 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant allegedly committed offence of rioting, House- trespass, murder and assault - Appellant was convicted u/ss. 148, 302 And 324 of IPC - Hence instant appeal - Appellants contended by pointing out that since the complainant's were the aggressors, armed with sword, hockey sticks and pelted stones, appellants/accused were entitled to avail the right of private defence - Held, from the evidence of PWs 4 and 5 who were police constables attached to Police Station, and the materials abundantly showed that the deceased and the complainant's party were also armed with sword and hockey sticks - In the absence of evidence of fire shot from the revolvers of A 1 and A 3 and in view of the statement of PWs 3, 6, 13 and 18 alleging against the present appellants, in order to bring the matter within free fight both sides have to come armed and prepared to do battle should be applied in instant case with the result that each accused would be liable for his individual act alone - Even if HC accepted the evidence of prosecution witnesses that A 2 was having sword and PW 3 sustained injuries at his instance, considering his individual act, he could only be convicted u/s. 324 of IPC and taking note of his age and of the fact that he was in custody from 14-4-1992 till 9-5-1992 during the trial and again from 31-1-2011 to 12-4-2012 (roughly one year and four months), HC felt that the ends of justice would be met by altering the sentence to the period already undergone - Appeals disposed of.
