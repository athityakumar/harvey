Baliya@ Bal Kishan v State Of M.P.

Supreme Court of India

05 October 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjan Gogoi, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 343; (2012) 9 SCC 696; 2012 ALL MR (Cri) 4129; JT 2012 (10) SC 145; 2013 (2) RCR(Criminal) 98; 2012(10) SCALE 12; [2012] 8 S.C.R. 1154

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120-B r/w 302 - Criminal conspiracy - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellants (accused) were allegedly committed offence u/s. 120B r/w s. 302 of IPC - Trial Court convicted appellants u/s. 120B r/w 302 of IPC to sentenced to undergo, inter alia, rigorous imprisonment for life - Aggrieved appellants filed appeals before HC, which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeals - Whether impugned conviction of appellants was justified - Held, naturally in evaluating proved circumstances for the purposes of drawing any inference adverse to accused, benefit of any doubt that might creep in should go to the accused - In instant case, evidence of PW4 (eye witness) indicated that though she could not identify any of alleged assailants in police custody, she had identified appellants in Court - Not much reliance could be placed on testimony of PW4, which was suffered from material discrepancies if read with evidence of PW1 and 5, particularly, in respect of role of appellant - PW3 another eye witness was declared hostile - While PW1 (informant) and PW5 (brother of deceased) did not mention about presence of appellant at the place of occurrence, PW4 had identified appellant in Court as one of assailants - PW4, however, could not identify any of accused while they were in police custody - Thus, it would not be safe and prudent to place any reliance on evidence of PW4 - Prosecution evidence shows that far charge of criminal conspiracy u/s. 120-B of IPC, prosecution has sought to establish that a pamphlet authored/published by deceased was in circulation casting doubt on character of 'S' and her relationship with appellant - Said pamphlet though seized in course of investigation was not exhibited in trial - From evidence of PW8 (another brother of deceased) and PW11 (witness), conversation between 'S' and appellants and statements attributed to appellants along with instructions to appellants that deceased should be killed was proved by prosecution - Though prosecution sought to prove that deceased was seen in company of appellants shortly before his death there was some amount of discrepancy in evidence of PW1 and 5, in that regard - Essential meeting of minds between appellants to commit murder of deceased, no evidence was forthcoming as to what was the response of appellants to the statement made by another appellant to the effect that author of pamphlet should be done away with - In absence of any material to establish said fact vital chain or link to enable Curt to satisfy with regard to an agreement or meeting of minds amongst appellants to commit murder of deceased was lacking - Alleged participation of appellants in commission of actual act of murder could not be evidence of conspiracy as the commission of murder should be the result of conspiracy already hatched - If evidence of eye witnesses (PW4) was excluded, appellant was not implicated, by the circumstances that prosecution had sought to establish by examining PW1 and 5 - PW1 and 5 nowhere mentioned that appellant was present at any point of time or at place when occurrence took place - PW1 and 5 implicated appellant as being saw with deceased along with accused 'M' shortly before incident - There was a serious discrepancy in evidence of PW1 and 5 with regard to presence of appellant in company of deceased immediately before the crime - Version of last seen together even if it was hypothetically accepted in its entirety, would establish only a solitary incriminating circumstance against appellant, which could not give rise to conclusion that appellant should be held liable for murder - Impugned conviction order was set aside - Appeals allowed.
