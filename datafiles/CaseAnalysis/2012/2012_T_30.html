Rajendra Pralhadrao Wasnik v The State Of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

29 February 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Swatanter Kumar, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 76; (2012) 4 SCC 37; AIR 2012 SC 1377; 2012 ALL MR (Cri) 1375; 2012 CRLJ 1917; JT 2012 (2) SC 560; 2012 (2) MLJ(Crl) 557; 2012 (2) RCR(Criminal) 696; 2012(3) SCALE 182; [2012] 2 S.C.R. 225

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 376, 377, 302 - Rape - Murder - Circumstantial evidence - Conviction - Death sentence - Sustainability - Appellant accused allegedly raped and murdered deceased minor - Trial Court convicted appellant u/ss. 376(2)(f), 377 and 302 IPC on the basis of circumstantial evidence and sentenced him to death - Appellant appealed to HC which was dismissed by impugned order - (A) Whether prosecution was able to establish chain of events leading to involvement of appellant in the alleged crime - Held, testimony of PW1 (mother of deceased) remained uncontroverted or nothing material came in her cross-examination - Further, evidence of PW1 corroborated statements of other material witness also - PW12 (father of deceased) provided complete chain of events, right from time he got information that his daughter had been taken away till time when her dead body was recovered from fields - Appellant admitted documents relating to seizure of material objects of the case - Once crucial pieces of documentary evidence were admitted by accused and other factual links in story of prosecution were duly proved by witnesses by circumstantial or direct evidence, there was no occasion to doubt that prosecution had not been able to prove its case beyond reasonable doubt - Cumulative effect of entire prosecution evidence was that it pointed unmistakably towards guilt of accused - It was not only a case of circumstantial evidence simpliciter but also the 'last seen together' principle since number of witnesses who had seen accused at house of PW2 with deceased minor girl - Hence, prosecution was able to establish not only by substantial evidence but clearly by medical evidence as well - (B) Whether instant case was a case of rarest of rare cases where awarding death sentence was justified - Held, crime committed by appellant was not just heinous simplicitor, but was a brutal and inhuman crime - On the basis of 'last seen together' theory and other direct and circumstantial evidence, prosecution was able to establish its case beyond any reasonable doubt - Pain and agony that appellant should have caused to deceased minor girl was beyond imagination and was limit of viciousness - No mitigating circumstances favouring accused was available in the instant case - Accused was holding deceased child in a relationship of 'trust-belief' and 'confidence', in which capacity he took child from house of PW2 - Appellant, by his conduct, had belied human relationship of trust and worthiness - Act of appellant left deceased in a badly injured condition in open fields without even clothes, reflected most unfortunate and abusive facet of human conduct - Trial Court was fully justified in law and on facts, in awarding extreme penalty of death for an offence u/s. 302 IPC along with other punishments for other offences - No interference called for - Appeal dismissed.
