Pravin Gada And Another v Central Bank Of India And Others

Supreme Court of India

03 December 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 495; (2013) 2 SCC 101; AIR 2013 SC 4; 2013 (97) ALR 496; 2013 (1) AWC 17; 2013 (1) BC 3; 2013 (1) Bom.C.R. 299; [2013] 176 Comp Cas 101; 2013 (1) CompLJ 1; 2013 (2) G.L.R. 1567; JT 2012 (12) SC 17; 2013 (119) RD 344; 2012(11) SCALE 746; [2012] 13 S.C.R. 611

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Corporate

Keywords: Recovery Officer

Summary: Banking & Finance - Corporate - Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Suit for recovery - Auction - Irregularity - Validity - ICICI Bank instituted a suit before HC for recovery of its dues against Company - Single Judge appointed a Receiver who was granted liberty to sell assets by public auction or by private treaty and to apply net sale proceeds as between ICICI Bank and Central Bank/respondent no. 2 - Subsequently, suit was transferred to DRT - DRT, by order allowed application filed by ICICI Bank Ltd. for a sum of Rs.1.12 crores together with future interest at 12% per annum - Further directed that on failure on part of borrower to repay amount within 6 months, immoveable property would be sold and net sale proceeds would be paid to applicant bank and Central Bank in proportion to their respective charges - Being grieved by aforesaid order, respondent nos. 1 and 2 filed appeal before DRT which set aside sale - On appeal, DRAT allowed appeal and directed restoration of confirmation of sale in favour of respondent nos. 1 and 2 - Aggrieved, by order appellants filed writ petition before HC - DB quashed said order of DRAT wherein DRAT had set aside order of DRT and restored confirmation of sale conducted by way of public auction in favour of appellants - Hence, instant appeals - Whether procedure that was followed in sale of property was fair and proper or whether there was any fraud and material irregularity - Held, official liquidator had appeared before recovery officer on number of dates - However, DRT had returned a finding that he had restricted role which had been found fault with HC - HC was absolutely correct in setting aside sale on foundation that a fair and transparent procedure had not been adopted - SC had undertaken exercise to have an auction conducted through competent authority of DRT by adopting a fair, competitive and transparent procedure but that did not mean that conclusion arrived by HC in that regard was erroneous - In A. P. State Financial Corporation v. Official Liquidator 2000 Indlaw SC 456 was relied on - Appeals disposed of.
