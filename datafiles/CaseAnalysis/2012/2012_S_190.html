Vilas Pandurang Pawar & Anr v State Of Maharashtra & Ors

Supreme Court of India

10 September 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, Ranjan Gogoi

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 288; (2012) 8 SCC 795; AIR 2012 SC 3316; 2012 ALL MR (Cri) 3743; 2012 CRLJ 4520; 2012 (5) CTC 460; 2013 (1) G.L.R. 512; JT 2012 (9) SC 390; 2012 (4) RCR(Criminal) 761; 2012 (4) RLW 3487; 2012(8) SCALE 577; [2012] 8 S.C.R. 270

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Scheduled Castes and the Scheduled Tribes (Prevention of Atrocities) Act, 1989, ss. 3(1)(10), 18 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 438 - Anticipatory bail - Bar - Challenged - Petitioners (accused) allegedly abused respondents (complainant) on their caste and assaulted - FIR was registered against petitioners and other co-accused - Thereafter, petitioners filed an application for anticipatory bail u/s. 438 of CrPC, before Trial Court, which was dismissed - Aggrieved petitioners filed appeal before HC - HC allowed anticipatory bail of other co-accused and dismissed appeal of petitioners - Hence, instant petition - Whether accused charged with provisions of the Act, was entitled for anticipatory bail u/s. 438 of CrPC - Held, scope of s. 18 of the Act r/w s. 438 of CrPC was such that it created a specific bar in grant of anticipatory bail - When an offence was registered against a person under provisions of the Act, no Court should entertain application for anticipatory bail, unless it prima facie finds that such an offence was not made out - However, a duty was cast on Court to verify averments in complaint and to find out whether an offence u/s. 3(1) of Act was prima facie made out - Perusal of complaint shows that petitioners and other accused persons abused complainant and her husband by calling their caste 'Mahar' and assaulted them for their action of letting rain water to their field - S. 18 of Act was applicable to instant case and petitioners were not entitled to anticipatory bail u/s. 438 of CrPC - Petition dismissed.
