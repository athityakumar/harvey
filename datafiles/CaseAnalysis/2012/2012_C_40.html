Commissioner Of Central Excise, Bangalore-Ii v M/S Osnar Chemical Pvt. Ltd.

Supreme Court of India

13 January 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

D.K. Jain, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 610; (2012) 2 SCC 282; AIR 2012 SC (Supp) 643; JT 2012 (1) SC 284; 2012 (2) RCR(Civil) 587; [2012] 2 S.C.R. 1035

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Tariff Item, Tissue Paper

Summary: Excise - Indirect Tax - Central Excise Tariff Act, 1944, s. 2(f) - Central Excise Tariff Act, 1985 - Petroleum bitumen - Improvement in quality - Levy of excise duty - Challenged - Respondent (company) was engaged in supply of Polymer Modified Bitumen (PMB) and Crumbled Rubber Modified Bitumen (CRMB) - Appellant (Revenue) issued show cause notice demanding duty in respect of PMB falling under sub-heading 271500.90 of 1985 Act and passed order holding that process of PMB carried by respondent amounted to manufacture of PMB in terms of s. 2(f) of 1944 Act and was dutiable - Aggrieved respondent filed appeal before Tribunal, which was allowed holding that PMB could not be bought and sold in market as it was fit for use only in a molten condition and that process carried by respondent did not amount to manufacture - Hence, instant appeals u/s. 35L(b) of 1944 Act - Appellant contended that PMB and CRMB satisfy the test of marketability which was one of essential conditions for the purpose of levy of excise duty - Whether addition and mixing of polymers and additives to base bitumen results in manufacture of a new marketable commodity - Held, in order to bring a process in relation to any goods within ambit of s. 2(f) of 1944 Act, same was required to be recognised by legislature as manufacture in relation to such goods in Section notes or Chapter notes of First Schedule to 1985 Act - Thus, in order to bring petroleum bitumen, falling under CSH 27132000, within extended or deemed meaning of expression 'manufacture', so as to fall under CSH 271500900, process of its treatment with polymers or additives or with any other compound was required to be recognised by legislature as manufacture under the Chapter notes or Section notes to Chapter 27 - In instant case, a plain reading of Schedule to 1944 Act made it clear that no such process or processes was specified in Section notes or Chapter notes in respect of petroleum bitumen falling under Tariff Item 27132000 or even in respect of bituminous mixtures falling under Tariff Item 27150090 to indicate that said process amounts to manufacture - Thus, it was evident that said process of adding polymers and additives to heated bitumen to get a better quality bitumen, viz. PMB or CRMB, could not be given an extended meaning under the expression manufacture in terms of s. 2(f)(ii) of 1944 Act - Mere improvement in quality did not amount to manufacture - It was only when change or a series of changes take the commodity to a point where commercially it could no longer be regarded as original commodity but was instead recognized as a new and distinct article that manufacture could be said to have taken place - Process of mixing polymers and additives with bitumen did not amount to manufacture - Both Lower authorities found as a fact that said process merely resulted in improvement of quality of bitumen - Bitumen remained bitumen - There was no change in characteristics or identity of bitumen and only its grade or quality was improved - Said process did not result in transformation of bitumen into a new product having a different identity, characteristic and use - End use remained same, namely for mixing of aggregates for constructing the roads - PMB or CRMB could not be treated as bituminous mixtures falling under CSH 27150090 and should continue to be classified under CSH 27132000 pertaining to tariff for petroleum bitumen - Impugned order passed by Tribunal was upheld - Appeal dismissed.
