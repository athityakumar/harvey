Director General of Posts and others v K. Chandrashekar Rao

Supreme Court of India

13 December 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Swatanter Kumar, Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 549; (2013) 3 SCC 310; 2013(1) ALL MR 476; 2013 (4) AWC 3783; JT 2013 (1) SC 163; 2012(12) SCALE 538; [2012] 12 S.C.R. 795; 2013 (1) SLR 721; 2013 (1) UPLBEC 334

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Central Civil services (Temporary services) Rules, 1965, r. 5 - Compassionate appointment scheme, 1998 - Appointment in excess of quota - Termination - Challenged - Respondent was appointed to group D post under compassionate appointment scheme - Respondent contended that notice of termination u/s. 5 of the Rules was issued and services of 48 persons, whose names were recommended in excess of quota were terminated - Respondent challenged said order of termination before Tribunal - Tribunal allowed application of respondent and held that appointment of respondent was not liable to be terminated - Aggrieved by order of Tribunal, appellant filed writ petition before HC - HC upheld the order of Tribunal - Hence instant appeal - Whether respondent were liable to be terminated - Held, from compassionate scheme and Office Memorandum dt.16-5-2001, it was clear that where on the one hand, the state had formulated a welfare scheme for compassionate appointments, there on the other, because of limitations of its financial resources it decided to take economic measures by reducing extent of appointment by direct recruitment from financial year 2001-2002 - Both these matters falling in domain of Govt. and being matters of policy, Court was hardly called upon to comment upon either of them - These were acts which fall in the domain of State and do not call for any judicial interference - Respondent was admittedly appointed to the post, in furtherance to 1998 scheme, in the year 2002 (while others were appointed during the period of 2001-2003) - Instructions which specifically dealt with the compassionate appointments were issued by office memorandum dt.4-7-2002 - Neither Memorandum dt.16-5-2001 nor Memorandum dt.4-7-2002 stated that restrictions sought to be imposed were applicable retrospectively or even retroactively - Rights of these persons were settled, respondent and others were appointed to the posts and they had already worked in their respective posts before notice of termination were issued to them at the end of year 2004 - No data or material was placed by government before SC even to support contention that under effect of instructions of the year 1998, these persons were appointed in excess of posts provided under the scheme - Both these office memorandums were expected to operate prospectively and thus rights which were settled could not be re-settled - It was also undisputed that appointments of respondent and others were made on the basis of vacancies existing against year 2000 when instructions of 1998 were in operation, free of any restriction - In the meanwhile, another office memorandum came to be issued on 14-6-2006 amending the restrictions placed by office memorandum dt.16-5-2001 - Memorandum of 14-6-2006 in fact requires as to how vacancies available for making direct recruitment were to be calculated - HC clearly observed that authorities have to reconsider the matter in the light of instructions issued in memorandum dt.14-6-2006 - In view of settled position of law and fact that memorandums could not be given retrospective effect, spirit of scheme was to provide relief to family members of deceased persons and thus on yardstick of social justice, such relief could not be withdrawn on the ground of some alleged discrepancy which was not supported by any data, was unreasonable and therefore, even unsustainable - Appeals dismissed.
