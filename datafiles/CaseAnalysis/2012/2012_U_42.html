Syed Intesar Mehdi and others v Union of India and another

Supreme Court of India

08 May 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Aftab Alam, Ranjana Prakash Desai

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 243; (2012) 6 SCC 265; AIR 2012 SC 2453; JT 2012 (5) SC 1; 2012 (2) RCR(Civil) 935; 2012(5) SCALE 217; [2012] 5 S.C.R. 715

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; International

Keywords: Maritime Zones of India (Regulation of Fishing By Foreign Vessels) Act, 1981, Haj Committee Act, 2002

Summary: International - Administrative - Hajj Committee Act, 2002 - Private Tour Operators (PTO) - 250 Sq.ft. area - Registration - Eligibility - Hajj pilgrims in India were allowed to perform Hajj under bilateral agreement signed between Govt. of India and Kingdom of Saudi Arabia every year - Later Govt. assigned fixed number of pilgrims who were permitted to perform Hajj - Out of fixed number, a small portion was specified for registered PTOs and rest for Hajj Committee of India - To establish PTOs, Govt. of India and as per Hajj policy, PTO was required minimum office area of 250 sq.ft for registration and ferrying pilgrims for Hajj - Aggrieved PTOs filed writ petition before HC - HC rejected challenge and gave directions to Govt. of India to allocate certain seats to some of writ petitioners - Hence, instant petitions - Whether PTO required 250 sq.ft office area for registration and ferrying pilgrims for Hajj and allotment of Hajj pilgrims as per Hajj policy 2012 to PTOs was justifiable - Held, as per Saudi Regulations, PTO should be allotted minimum of 50 pilgrims - No arbitrariness and unreasonableness found in requirement of minimum office area of 250sq.ft. and annual turnover of Rs. 1 crore to face any unforeseen situation aroused during Hajj - Further, none of the conditions could be said to be arbitrary or unreasonable and conditions prescribed in Govt. of India 2012 Hajj Policy did not warrant any interference - 2012 Hajj Policy for registration of PTO filed on behalf of Union of India was, accordingly, approved for 2012 Hajj - Govt. of India gave subsidy in air fares to Hajj committee pilgrims to cover additional burden resulting from stringent regulation imposed by Saudi Arbian Authorities - Direction issued to UOI to file greater detail in regard to quota of 11,000 seats was being allocated in 201 Hajj and directed Hajj Committee of India and State Hajj committees to file full details of process of selection of pilgrims and examine functioning of Hajj Committees where number of applicants exceed quota allotted for State and what amounts were charged from each pilgrim and what facilities provided to them - Petitions disposed of.
