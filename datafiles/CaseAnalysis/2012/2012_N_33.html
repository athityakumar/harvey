K. Suresh v New India Assurance Company Limited and another

Supreme Court of India

19 October 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 433; (2012) 12 SCC 274; 2012 (4) ACC 395; 2012 ACJ 2694; 2013 (1) AWC 453; 2013 (169) PLR 337; 2013 (1) RCR(Civil) 312; [2012] 11 S.C.R. 414; 2012 (4) WLN(SC) 53

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Insurance - Practice & Procedure - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, s. 168 - Rash and Negligent - Permanent Disability - Compensation - Validity - Appellant was hit from behind by auto which was driven in rash and negligent manner due to which appellant sustained injuries with 75% permanent disability and filed claim for compensation - Tribunal awarded Rs. 25,00,000/- under various heads - Appeal before HC, HC reduced compensation claim and determined amount to Rs. 9,78,000/- and even reduced interest granted by tribunal - Hence this appeal - Appellant contended that HC, had erroneously held that there could not be grant of compensation under two heads, namely, 'permanent disability' and 'loss of earning power' - Whether analysis made by HC, in not granting compensation under certain heads and further reducing amount on certain scores, were justified - Held, it was perceptible that HC, had deleted amount awarded under head of pain and suffering by family members of claimant and amount granted towards loss of marital life, there was no iota of evidence with regard to loss of marital life, hence, SC, did not find any error in said deletion, as far as grant of compensation on score of pain and suffering suffered by family members of claimant was concerned, same was not permissible, therefore, SC, held that finding of HC on that score was totally justified and does not call for any interference - Appeal partly allowed.
