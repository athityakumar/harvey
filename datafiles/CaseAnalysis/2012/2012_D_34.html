Rebeka Minz & Ors v Divisional Manager,United India Insurance Co. Ltd. & Anr

Supreme Court of India

23 August 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 255; (2012) 8 SCC 145; 2012 (3) ACC 759; 2012 ACJ 2328; AIR 2012 SC 3263; 2013 (1) ALD(SC) 27; 2012(5) ALL MR 905; 2012 (94) ALR 715; 2012 (5) AWC 5215; 2012 (4) CLT(SC) 250; 2012 (168) PLR 784; 2013 (1) RCR(Civil) 220; 2012(7) SCALE 514; [2012] 7 S.C.R. 381

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Insurance - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 - Compensation - Enhancement of - Denied - Challenged - Deceased (Husband of Appellant No. 1) died in an accident - Appellants ( Wife and children of deceased) filed the claim for compensation - Tribunal awarded a sum of Rs. 10,08,000/- as compensation along with interest at the rate of 7% per annum - Appellants filed appeal against said order contending that Tribunal applied the multiplier 12 instead of 17, having regard to the fact that the deceased at the time of his death was 35 years - Appellants also contended that Tribunal has also not granted the interest for certain period - HC reduced the compensation awarded by the Tribunal and also the rate of interest - Hence, the instant appeal - Whether compensation awarded to appellants was just and reasonable or were they entitled to enhanced compensation - Held, applying the formula stated in SC in Santosh Devi v. National Insurance Company Ltd. & Ors. 2012 INDLAW SC 151 and Sarla Verma & Ors. v. Delhi Transport Corporation & Anr. 2009 INDLAW SC 488, the multiplier would be 16 for calculating the compensation as the deceased was 35 years old - The Tribunal after examining the materials before it, found that after deducting 1/3rd of personal expenses, the monthly income of the deceased was Rs.7,000/- and the net contribution to the family was ascertained at Rs. 84,000/- per annum - Applying the multiplier of 16, the compensation works out to Rs. 13,44,000/- - Impugned order of the HC was set aside - The compensation payable to the appellants in a sum of Rs. 13,44,000/- [84,000/- x 16] - The said sum of Rs. 13,44,000/- also carried interest at the rate of 7% per annum from the date of application till the date of realization - Appeals allowed.
