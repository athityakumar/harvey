Kishor Kumar & Ors v Pradeep Shukla & Ors

Supreme Court of India

29 February 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, J. Chelameswar

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 79; (2012) 4 SCC 103; AIR 2012 SC 1864; 2012 (3) AWC 3147; 2012 (133) FLR 151; JT 2012 (3) SC 107; 2012(3) SCALE 267; [2012] 2 S.C.R. 251; 2012 (4) SLR 287; 2012 (3) SLR 844; 2012 (1) UPLBEC 836

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: U.P. Pharmacists Service Rules, 1980, U.P. Procedure for Direct Recruitment of Group 'C' Posts (Outside the Purview of Public Service Commission) Rules, 2000

Summary: Service - U.P. Procedure for Direct Recruitment of Group 'C' Posts (Outside the Purview of Public Service Commission) Rules, 2000 - U.P. Pharmacists Service Rules, 1980 - Vacancy - Seniority - Eligibility - Special Leave Petitions are directed against judgment and order passed by DB affirming order of Single Judge about appointment of Pharmacists in State - 766 vacancies were advertised for being filled up by diploma holders in Pharmacy as per 2000, Rules - Respondents interpreted r. 15(2) 1980, Rules, that required diploma holders to be appointed against vacancies which became available in each recruitment year, by first appointing those Pharmacists who had obtained their diplomas earlier - Further contended that they were entitled to be selected and appointed first in respect of vacancies advertised, as they belonged to previous batches and had been denied appointment by the State Government earlier - Questioning interpretation of r. 15(2) of 1980, Rules writ petitions were filed for quashing advertisement and to command concerned authorities to effect recruitment to post of pharmacist in accordance with r. 14 and 15 of 1980, Rules - HC orders were challenged before BD which disposed appeals with direction that case of appellants would be considered in accordance with pre-existing practice by considering their appointment on basis of their merit, but that said process would be available only for appellants - It was directed that they would be accommodated if they were otherwise found eligible and remaining vacancies would be filled up by following r. 15(2) of 1980 Rules - State challenged order of DB through SLP which was dismissed upholding decision of DB - Select list was prepared which was challenged through writ petitions with direction not to make appointments - Contempt petitions were filed for not following orders of DB - Instant SLP is filed by candidate on ground that despite better merit they had not been selected - Whether instant SLP is maintainable - Held, fact that matter had already been decided and it had been directed by this Court, following decision of DB, that candidates could be appointed against vacancies in order of their inter-se seniority as per vacancies available in each year, being so and having regard to earlier decision of this Court, there is no reason to interfere with order of DB - Petitions dismissed.
