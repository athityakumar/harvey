Shobhan Singh Khanka v The State Of Jharkhand

Supreme Court of India

30 March 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, J. Chelameswar

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 107; (2012) 4 SCC 684; 2012 CRLJ 2213; 2012 (2) JCC 1396; JT 2012 (3) SC 570; 2012 (3) MLJ(Crl) 569; 2012 (2) RCR(Criminal) 535; 2012(4) SCALE 78; [2012] 2 S.C.R. 663

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 420, 423, 424, 467, 468, 469, 471, 477A, 120-B, 109 and 201 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, s. 13(2)/ 13(1) (c) (d) - Expert Committee - Undue Favor - Anticipatory Bail - Dismissed - Appellant was charged u/ss. 420, 423, 424, 467, 468, 469, 471, 477A, 120-B, 109 and 201 of IPC and s. 13(2)/ 13(1) (c) (d) of 1988, Act - Allegation was that appellant had provided highest marks to candidates whom they desired to be selected by giving undue favour in Interview Board to JPSC - Appellant filed petition before Special Court, which was dismissed, HC confirmed order of Special Court - Aggrieved instant appeal - Whether Anticipatory bail should be granted to appellant - Held, though HC had concluded that on ground of parity and on similar footing that other co-accused declined to grant anticipatory bail, SC held that inasmuch as all other Members of Board including Chairman belonged to Jharkhand and some of their relatives participated in selection and considering fact that present appellant had no connection with JPSC and hails from a different State, said observation/conclusion is not acceptable - Perusal of FIR also shows that appellant was not acquainted with or related to any of candidates interviewed by panel of which he was a Member - SC held that appellant had made out a case for anticipatory bail - Appeal allowed.
