Winston Tan & Anr. v Union Of India & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

04 October 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, A.R. Dave

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 330; (2012) 10 SCC 222; AIR 2013 SC 604; 2012(6) ALL MR 429; 2012 (6) AWC 5690; 2012 (6) CTC 355; JT 2012 (10) SC 167; 2012 (4) RCR(Civil) 719; 2012 (4) RCR(Criminal) 717; 2012(9) SCALE 705; [2012] 8 S.C.R. 1107

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Customs - Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Smugglers and Foreign Exchange Manipulators (Forfeiture of Property) Act, 1976, ss. 3, 11, 6(1), 19(1), 7(1), - Conservation of Foreign Exchange and Prevention of Smuggling Activities Act, 1974 - Detenues/appellants were detained under the 1974 Act - Detention order was passed and appellnats premises were searched wherein authorities seized Indian Currency of Rs. 13,50,000/- along with incriminating materials showing illegal transfer of money from abroad - Notices were served on detenues u/s. 6(1) of the 1976 Act - Competent Authority, passed an order u/ss. 7(1) and (3) of the 1976 Act forfeiting subject flat and declaring that forfeited property stands vested in Central Govt. free from all encumbrances - Subsequently, a further order u/s. 19(1) of the 1976 Act was passed directing appellants to surrender/deliver possession of forfeited flat within 30 days of receipt of order - Appellants filed a writ petition before HC for quashing order forfeiting subject flat and not to interfere with their peaceful possession and enjoyment in respect of subject flat - Single Judge held that sale in favour of appellants had taken place before order of forfeiture was passed by Competent and quashed same and remitted matter to Competent Authority for fresh consideration - On appeal, DB held that sale transaction in favour of appellants was subsequent to issuance of notice u/s. 6 and, was null and void u/s. 11 of the 1976 Act and appellants were not entitled to any notice and non-issuance of notice to them had not vitiated action taken by Competent Authority - Hence, instant appeal - Whether appellants who purchased subject flat during pendency of forfeiture proceedings were entitled to an opportunity to prove that they were transferees in good faith for adequate consideration - Held, in Parvathi Bai 2011 Indlaw MAD 437 relied on - Further, sale in appellants favour was effected after notices u/s. 6(1) of the 1976 Act were issued to vendors such sale had no legal sanction - Thus, sale was null and void u/s. 11 of the 1976 Act and it was not protected so as to enable purchaser to prove that he was transferee in good faith for adequate consideration - Hence, no title came to be vested in appellants by virtue of sale-deed as vendors could not have transferred property after service of notice u/s. 6(1) and during pendency of forfeiture proceedings under the 1976 Act - Therefore, title in subject flat was deemed to have vested in Central Govt. when first notice u/s. 6(1) of the 1976 Act was issued and served on one of vendors and they ceased to have any title in subject flat on date of transfer and had no transferable right - Thus, appellants could not claim any right in flat and opportunity to appellants to prove that they were transferees in good faith with adequate consideration did not arise - Appeal dismissed.
