Satish Batra v Sudhir Rawal

Supreme Court of India

18 October 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 430; (2013) 1 SCC 345; AIR 2012 SC (Supp) 684; 2013 (1) ALD(SC) 73; 2013 (96) ALR 242; 2013 (1) AWC 245; 2012 (6) CTC 121; JT 2012 (11) SC 70; 2013 (2) MahLJ 596; 2013 (2) MPLJ 1; 2013 (1) OLR 593; 2012 (4) RCR(Civil) 890; 2013 (118) RD 657; [2012] 9 S.C.R. 662; 2012 (4) WLN(SC) 47

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Practice & Procedure - Forfeiture of earnest money - Permissibility - Whether the seller was entitled to forfeit the earnest money deposit where the sale of an immovable property fell through by reason of the fault or failure of the purchaser -

Held, to justify the forfeiture of advance money being part of 'earnest money' the terms of the contract should be clear and explicit - Earnest money was paid or given at the time when the contract was entered into and, as a pledge for its due performance by the depositor to be forfeited in case of non-performance, by the depositor - There could be converse situation also that if the seller failed to perform the contract the purchaser could also get the double the amount, if it was so stipulated - Part payment of purchase price could not be forfeited unless it was a guarantee for the due performance of the contract - Hence, if the payment was made only towards part payment of consideration and not intended as earnest money then the forfeiture clause would not apply - Appeal allowed.
