Voltas Limited v Tehsildar, Thane and others

Supreme Court of India

08 November 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 417; (2012) 13 SCC 165; 2012 (95) ALR 871; 2013 (2) Bom.C.R. 628; JT 2012 (11) SC 195; 2013 (1) RCR(Civil) 944; 2012(11) SCALE 57; [2012] 12 S.C.R. 514

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property

Summary: Administrative - Land & Property - Land Acquisition (Companies) Rules, 1963 - Maharashtra Land Revenue Code, 1966 - Urban Land (Ceiling and Regulation) Act, 1976 - Urban Land Ceiling Act - Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - Allotment of Land - Violation of Terms - Cancellation of allotment - Demand of Unearned income - Sustainability - Appellant-Company was allotted land by Govt. with specific condition that Company shall not in any way whatsoever, alienate land or any portion thereof howsoever except with prior permission in writing, of Govt. - Appellant after about 24 years, violated terms of allotment due to which Govt. issued show cause to appellant as to why land should not be forfeited and amount of Rs.14,11,45,851/- towards unearned income be not charged as it violated the terms and conditions of the order of allotment by granting rights to developers for construction of houses and selling them after development, thereby benefiting to a large extent - Collector issued letter dt. 15-2-1989 staying scheme of appellant and passed order dt 18-2-2002 holding that Company breached terms and conditions of order of allotment and thereby was liable to pay 50% of difference amount of unearned income amounting to Rs. 14,11,45,851 - Aggrieved appellant filed writ petition before HC, which held that there was a breach of terms and conditions of order of allotment and, therefore, it was open to respondents to take appropriate proceedings in accordance with law, including recovery of unearned profit - (A) Whether Appellant-Company breached any of terms and conditions of order of allotment - Held, Company decided to develop land as per Housing Scheme dt. 11-1-1984, through Developer this was intimated to State Govt. which agreed to proposal, as was clear from State Govt.'s letter dt. 2-12-1989 issued from Department of Housing and Special Assistance, State at request of Developer, also extended period of completion for housing scheme, vide letter dt. 25-6-1991 - State Government allowed Company to change use of land and to develop surplus land for purposes other than that for which said land was originally allotted and such permission was in accordance with terms and conditions as mentioned in order of allotment dt. 20-1-1969 - (B) whether notice of demand of 50% of unearned income is legal and valid - Held, order of allotment dt. 20-1-1969 and scheme dt. 11-1-1984 did not stipulate any charge on unearned income of Company, respondents had failed to show provision under which Company was required to pay 50% of its unearned income - (C) Whether Company was required to be heard before passing of impugned orders - Held, no hearing was given to Company before passing impugned orders, there was nothing on record to suggest basis on which respondents determined unearned income - It was a settled law that no Penal order could be passed without giving any notice and hearing to affected person, admittedly, impugned orders were passed without giving such notice and hearing to company and were passed in violation of Rules of Natural Justice - Appeals allowed.
