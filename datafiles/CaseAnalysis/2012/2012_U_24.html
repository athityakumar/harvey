Ram Dhari Jindal Memorial Trust v Union Of India And Others

Supreme Court of India

21 March 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, H.L. Gokhale

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 118; (2012) 11 SCC 370; AIR 2012 SC 1878; 2012 (4) ALD(SC) 55; 2012 (92) ALR 285; 2012 (4) AWC 3435; 2012 (2) CLT(SC) 1; 2012 (188) DLT 345; JT 2012 (3) SC 595; 2012 (2) RCR(Civil) 594; 2012 (116) RD 341; 2012(4) SCALE 101; [2012] 2 S.C.R. 525

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property

Summary: Administrative - Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4(1), 17(1), 6, 7, 5A - Land Acquisition - Notification - Dispensation of Enquiry - Sustainability - On requisition of development authority for construction of residential scheme, notification u/s. 4(1) of Act was issued and enquiry s. 5A of Act was dispensed off by Governor u/s. 17(4) of the Act - - Govt. made declaration u/s. 6 of Act stating that land mentioned was acquired for public purpose, another notification u/s. 7 of Act directing Land Acquisition Collector to take orders for acquisition and take possession of land - Appellant land owners aggrieved by notification, challenged acquisition before HC, - HC dismissed writ petitions being devoid of merit - Aggrieved instant appeal - Whether HC was justified in holding that Governor had right to direct that all provisions of s. 5A of Act would not apply - Held, if government sought to invoke its power of urgency, it had to first form opinion that land for stated public purpose was urgently needed - Such opinion had to be founded on need for immediate possession of land for carrying out purpose for which land was sought to be compulsorily acquired - Use of power of urgency u/s. 17(1) and (4) of Act ipso facto did not result in elimination of enquiry u/s. 5A and, therefore, if Govt. intended to eliminate enquiry, then it had to apply its mind on aspect that urgency was of such nature that necessitated elimination of such enquiry - Power of urgency by Govt. u/s. 17 for a public purpose like Residential Scheme should not be invoked as a rule but had to be by way of exception - No material was available on record that justified dispensation of enquiry u/s. 5A of Act - HC was clearly wrong in holding that there was sufficient urgency in invoking provisions of s. 17 of Act - Appeal allowed.
