Jitu Patnaik v Sanatan Mohakud & Ors

Supreme Court of India

02 March 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, H.L. Gokhale

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 69; (2012) 4 SCC 194; AIR 2012 SC 913; 2012 (92) ALR 233; 2012 (1) CLT(SC) 372; JT 2012 (3) SC 297; 2012 (1) OLR 880; 2012 (2) RCR(Civil) 566; 2012(3) SCALE 107; [2012] 2 S.C.R. 258

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Keywords: Contesting Candidate, Order For

Summary: Election - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 6 r. 16, O. 7 r. 11 - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 86(1), 100(1)(d)(iii) (iv) - Election petition - Failure to provide material facts - Effect - 1st respondent challenged appellant's (returned candidate) election by filing election petition - Appellant filed written statement and an application under O. 6 r. 16 r/w s. 151 and O. 7 r. 11 CPC r/w s. 86(1) of the Act for striking off certain paras in election petition - Election Tribunal/HC struck out paras from election petition as sought by appellant, however, directed respondents to proceed with remaining paras nos. 7A and 7D in pleadings - Hence instant appeal - Appellant contended that remaining two paragraphs did not set out material facts to constitute cause of action u/s. 100(1)(d)(iii) and/or (iv) of the Act - (A) Whether para 7A of pleadings, stating that votes polled by an independent candidate sharing common ideology had died after expiry of withdrawal date and whose name was displayed in Electronic Voting Machine (EVM) should be counted in respondent's favour, set out material facts constitute cause of action - Held, s. 52 took care of situation in case of death of a candidate of recognized political party before poll - However, s. 52 did not provide any obligation on returning officer if a candidate of a registered political party other than recognized political party or an independent candidate died after list of contesting candidates as defined in s. 38 was published - Number of candidates' buttons which should be visible on EVM should be equal to number of candidates as published in list of validly nominated candidates who had not withdrawn candidature within period prescribed and whose nominations were included in list published u/s. 38 of the Act - No duty was imposed on returning officer to mask name of independent candidate who died after publication of list of validly nominated candidates being a contesting candidate as defined in s. 38 of the Act - Hence, averments made in para 7(A) of election petition did not furnish any cause of action for declaring election of returned candidate to be void u/s. 100(1)(d)(iv) of the Act - HC seriously erred in holding otherwise and ordering trial of election petition on pleadings set out in para 7(A) of petition - (B) Whether pleadings in para 7(D) stating that counting personnel had committed deliberate illegality while recording number of votes in Form 17C and Form 20, set out material facts constitute cause of action - Held, no averment was in election petition that respondent or any of his polling agents had perused register of voters maintained in Form 17A - Basis of knowledge that register of voters maintained in Form 17A recorded that 1091 voters came to vote was not disclosed at all - Further, there was no pleading that 1091 voters who came to vote at concerned booth in fact voted - No averment was in election petition that election petitioner or his agents challenged part II of Form-17C or Form 20 before authorities - Pleading of material facts with regard to allegation of suppression of 319 votes in para 7(D) was also incomplete - Further, there was no express pleading as to how result of election had been materially affected by less counting of 319 votes - Hence, averments made in para 7(D) did not set out all material facts and did not constitute cause of action for declaring election of appellant to be void - Election petition rejected in its entirety - Appeal allowed.
