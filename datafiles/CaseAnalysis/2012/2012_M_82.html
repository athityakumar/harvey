Medical Council Of India v Rama Medical College Hospital &Research Centre, Kanpur & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

04 July 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, J. Chelameswar

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 194; (2012) 8 SCC 80; AIR 2012 SC 3063; 2012 (5) AWC 4830; 2012 (5) Bom.C.R. 511; 2012 (3) CLT(SC) 71; 2012(6) SCALE 219; [2012] 6 S.C.R. 449; 2012 (5) SLR 266

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Education; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Karnataka Capitation Fee Act, Opening of a New or Higher Course of Study or Training (including Post-Graduate Course of Study or Training) and Increase of Admission Capacity in any Course of Study or Training (including a Post- Graduate Course of Study Or Training) Regulations, 2000

Summary: Administrative - Education - Practice & Procedure - Indian Medical Council Act, 1956, ss. 10,11 - Establishment of Medical College Regulations, 1999 - Establishment of Medical College Regulations, 2000 - Establishing Medical College - Permission - Recognition - Sustainability - Respondent-College was recognised Medical College with approval by petitioner and previous permission by Central Govt. - Respondent had approached HC, and filed writ petition for renewal of recognition and to increase number of seats in MBBS course - SJ, was pleased to allow petition granting permission to respondent, appeal before DB, by petitioner was dismissed and DB, concurred with SJ - Aggrieved instant Appeal - Petitioner pleaded that as per Act, no person would be entitled to establish a Medical College except in manner provided in Section 10A, which, in addition provides that no medical college shall open a new or higher course of study or training, including a post-graduate course of training, which would enable a student of such course or training to qualify himself for award of recognised medical qualification, except with previous permission of Central Govt., prohibition also extended to increase in admission capacity in any course of study or training, including post-graduate study or training, except with such previous permission of the Central Govt. - Whether orders passed by HC, that once permission granted u/s. 10A of Act, would amount to grant of recognition and, thereafter it was free to enhance number of seats without permission either of Council or Central Govt. was sustainable - Held, position was quite clear that in terms of scheme of Act and Regulations framed by Medical Council of India, it was Central Government which was empowered to grant recognition to a medical college or institution on recommendation made by Medical Council of India, role of Medical Council of India in grant of recognition to a medical college/institution was recommendatory and Council has no power to grant recognition to a medical institution - Such power lied with Central govt., no provision was available under Act relating to grant of recognition of a medical college/institution, since s. 10A speaks only of permission and not recognition, same had been supplemented by provisions of 1999 and 2000 Regulations for purpose of s. 10A(7)(g) of Act - Appeal disposed of.
