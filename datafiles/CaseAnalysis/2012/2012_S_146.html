Munish Mubar v State Of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

04 October 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 341; (2012) 10 SCC 464; AIR 2013 SC 912; 2012 ALL MR (Cri) 4135; 2013 CRLJ 56; JT 2012 (10) SC 41; 2012 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 3393; 2012 (4) MLJ(Crl) 546; 2012 (4) RCR(Criminal) 705; 2012(9) SCALE 736; [2012] 9 S.C.R. 193

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302,34,201, 120-B - Murder - Circumstantial Evidence - Chain of Events - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant and other co-accused were convicted for offence u/ss. 302, 34,201,120-B of IPC and were sentenced to undergo life imprisonment - Trial Court relying on circumstantial evidence as there were no independent witnesses convicted appellant - Appellant filed appeal before HC, same was dismissed - Aggrieved instant Appeal - Appellant contended that police had planted each of alleged recoveries made by them and that jewellery recovered, actually belonged to him, he contended that he did not knew deceased at all, and all alleged details of calls etc., were supported by way of fabricated documents - State pleaded that present case is one of circumstantial evidence, and chain of events were complete, and every link , was a pointer towards guilt of appellant and that appellant had failed to furnish any explanation in relation to incriminating circumstances put to him - Whether trial court was justified in convicting appellant basing on circumstantial evidence - Held, there was nothing on record to doubt existence of illicit relationship of co-accused with deceased, and also with appellant, as this fact had been fully established from evidence provided by several witnesses, it had further been proved that Car belonging to appellant was parked on 26-12-2002, at Airport for a duration of 3 hours, when flight by which deceased was to arrive, was scheduled to land, and car left after arrival of such flight - Telephone call records reveal presence of the appellant in place of occurrence, at relevant time of incident, recoveries were made upon disclosure statement of appellant and some articles found, had human blood on them and same connected appellant to crime - Evidence indicating guilt of accused became untrustworthy and unreliable, because most often it was only perpetrator of crime alone, who had knowledge of circumstances that prompted him to adopt a certain course of action, leading to commission of crime, therefore, if evidence on record suggested sufficient/necessary motive to commit a crime, it may be conceived that accused had committed same - It was evident that in spite of fact that in case there was no independent witness of recoveries and panch witnesses were only police personnel, it may not affect merits of the case - SC, found no force in appeal - Appeal dismissed.
