Vinod Kapoor v State Of Goa & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

03 October 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Patnaik, Swatanter Kumar

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 320; (2012) 12 SCC 378; AIR 2012 SC 3722; 2012(6) ALL MR 411; 2012 (6) Bom.C.R. 13; 2012 (4) CLT(SC) 201; 2012 (5) CTC 877; JT 2012 (10) SC 51; 2012 (7) MLJ(SC) 707; 2012 (4) RCR(Civil) 748; 2012(9) SCALE 657; [2012] 8 S.C.R. 1089

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Town and Country Planning Act, 1974

Summary: Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Municipalities & Local Governments - Town and Country Planning Act, 1974, s. 44 - Environment (Protection) Act, 1986 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 47 r. 7 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Review application - Dismissal - Appeal - Maintainable - Appellant filed a petition before HC for alleged illegal construction structure carried out by respondent No. 8 and contravention of provisions of notification issued under 1986 Act - HC dismissed petition holding that construction was not within 100 Mtrs of High Tide Line (HTL) - Aggrieved appellant filed SLP before SC, which was dismissed as withdrawn - Thereafter, appellant filed a review petition, which was rejected - Hence, instant Appeal - Respondent No. 8 contended that appeal against order of HC rejecting review application of appellant was not maintainable in view of O. 47 r. 7 of CPC - Whether order of HC rejecting an application for review was appealable - Held, order dt.22-11-2000 of SC would show that no liberty was took by appellant to file a fresh SLP u/art. 136 of Constitution against order of HC and SLP was withdrawn by appellant saying that he had filed a review petition before HC - Hence, SC appeared to have permitted appellant to pursue his remedy by way of review before HC - Appellant had not taken liberty from SC to challenge order dt.29-1-2000 afresh by way of special leave in case he did not get relief in review application - Appellant was precluded from challenging order dt.29-1-2000 of HC by way of SLP to appeal u/art. 136 of Constitution - Further, on HC rejecting application for review of appellant, order rejecting application for review was not appealable by virtue of principle in O. 47 r. 7 of CPC - Appeals dismissed.
