State Of Gujarat & Another v Manoharsinhji Pradyumansinhji Jadeja

Supreme Court of India

04 December 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 510; (2013) 2 SCC 300; AIR 2012 SC (Supp) 983; JT 2012 (12) SC 156; 2013 (1) RLW 449; 2012(11) SCALE 670; [2012] 11 S.C.R. 507

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Keywords: Constitution (Twenty-Sixth Amendment) Act, 1971, Authorization, Grass, Village Site, Gujarat Agricultural Lands Ceiling Act, 1960, Saurashtra Land Reforms Act, 1951, Saurashtra Estates Acquisition Act, 1952, Andhra Pradesh Land Reforms (Ceiling on Agricultural Holdings) Act, Saurashtra Barkhali Abolition Act, Gujarat Agricultural Lands Ceiling (Amendment) Act, 1972

Summary: Administrative - Land & Property - Gujarat Agricultural Lands Ceiling Act, 1960, ss. s. 2(1), 2(17), 2(17)(i), 2(17)(ii)(d), 35, 38 - Gujarat Agricultural Lands Ceiling (Amendment) Act, 1972 - Land Ceiling - Agricultural Land - Excess Land - Amendment of Act - Sustainability - Appellant No. 2 had initiated proceedings under, Act and after hearing interested party, order was passed holding that land to an extent of 587 acres 35 Gunthas was in excess of ceiling limit and respondent was entitled to retain only balance land i.e. 51 acres - Respondent preferred appeal u/s. 35 of Act before Deputy Collector - DC, dismissed appeal, against order of DC, revision was preferred before Tribunal, which partly allowed by remanding matter back to appellant No. 2 - Aggrieved respondent filed writ petition before HC, contending that his lands were covered by 1976, Act and was not governed by 1960, Act - HC, accepted stand of respondent and set aside orders passed by Tribunal - Appellants preferred LPA, which was dismissed - Hence instant appeal - Appellants contended that when once amending legislation was passed in Assembly in year 1974 subsequent notification though was made in year 1976 for bringing into force amendments, relevant date would be date when Act was passed and not date when it was notified - Respondent contended that even assuming that Amendment Act of 1974 would apply to case on hand, since respondent did not fall under definition of 'Ruler' as stipulated in s. 2(17)(ii)(d) of Amended Act, 1960, Act could not be applied to respondent case - Whether HC was justified in holding that lands were covered by 1976, Act and was not governed by 1960, Act - Held, a fortiori if ratio decided by Constitution Bench of this Court was applied, there would be no difficulty in holding that as held earlier, since as per unamended Act of 1960, 'Bid land' held by respondent fell within definition of 'agriculture' u/s. 2(1) and consequent definition of 'land' as defined in s. 2(17) of Act, 1960, determination of holding of such excess agriculture land under Act of 1960 prior to coming into force of Act, 1976 should be operated upon - Having regard to legal position, SC, held that action of appellants in having passed orders impugned before HC, were fully justified and interfering with same by SJ and DB of HC, by impugned order in this Appeal were liable to be set aside - Appeal allowed.
