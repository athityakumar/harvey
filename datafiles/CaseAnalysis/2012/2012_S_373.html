Dahari and others v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

11 October 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 347; (2012) 10 SCC 256; AIR 2013 SC 308; 2013 ALL MR (Cri) 783; JT 2012 (10) SC 1; 2013 (1) RCR(Criminal) 131; 2013 (2) RLW 1307; 2012(10) SCALE 160; [2012] 8 S.C.R. 1219

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 149, 148, 34 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellants (accused) were allegedly committed offence u/s. 302, 149, 148 of IPC - Trial Court convicted appellants u/s. 302, 149, 148 of IPC and sentenced them to undergo rigorous imprisonment for life - Aggrieved appellants filed appeals before HC, which was partly allowed acquitting 3 co-accused out of 7 - Hence, instant appeal - Appellants contended that total number of accused were only 4 and s. 149 of IPC did not attract and that HC disbelieved testimonies of witnesses and acquitted 3 among accused persons - Whether impugned conviction of appellants was justified - Held, in instant case, FIR was lodged within a period of 1 hour, that proved that witness (PW1 and 2), who were real brother of deceased, were present at the place of occurrence and were in a position to see accused from close quarters and all were known to witnesses - There was nothing in the cross-examination of eye-witnesses to cast a doubt upon veracity of their testimony or discredit it in anyway - It was evident from record that during cross-examination of IO (PW4), none of said accused voiced their concerns or raised any apprehension regarding non-examination of pillion rider of motor cycle, which deceased was riding - Appellants could not be permitted to advance an argument stating that since most material witness was withheld by prosecution therefore, adverse inference should be drawn against them - There was undisputedly, prior ill-will existing between parties, as criminal cases were pending between them and son of PW2 was in jail in connection with same - Thus, there was sufficient motive for appellants to kill deceased - It was not the case of prosecution that there were in fact, some other accused who were not yet put to trial - In a case where prosecution fail to prove that number of members of an unlawful assembly were 5 or more, Court could simply convict the guilty persons with the aid of s. 34 of IPC, provided that there was adequate evidence on record to show that such accused shared a common intention to commit crime in question - It not probable that real brothers of deceased who had eye-witnesses would implicate appellants falsely sparing real assailants, though false implication of some of persons might not be ruled out - Appeal dismissed.
