Rev. Mother Marykutty v Reni C. Kottaram and another

Supreme Court of India

12 October 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 351; (2013) 1 SCC 327; 2012 (4) BC 322; 2012 (4) CompLJ 590; JT 2012 (10) SC 280; 2013 (2) MahLJ 590; 2012 (4) MLJ(Crl) 526; 2013 (2) MPLJ 163; 2013 (1) OLR 548; 2012 (4) RCR(Civil) 797; 2012 (4) RCR(Criminal) 729; 2013 (1) RLW 12; 2012(10) SCALE 149; [2012] 9 S.C.R. 530

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Banking & Finance - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, s. 138 - Dishonour of cheque - Respondent alleged that appellant entrusted the work of construction of an Old Age Home and a Chapel based on an agreement between the appellant and the respondent - Appellant issued a post dated cheque for Rs.25 lakhs in favour of the respondent towards the outstanding amount due to him for the work done by him - When the cheque was presented by the respondent with his bankers, the same was dishonoured due to insufficiency of funds in the account of the appellant - Appellant contended that no amount was due and that the respondent stealthily removed 2 cheques from the custody of the appellant of which the present one was forged and presented for clearance - Trial Court held that the appellant was able to rebut the presumption and that there was no circumstance warranting the execution cheque in favour of the respondent - Trial Court found the appellant not guilty of the offence u/s. 138 of the Act and acquitted her - On appeal, HC found the appellant guilty of the offence and sentenced her to pay a fine of Rs.30 lakhs - Hence, instant appeal - Whether HC rightly convicted appellant guilty of the offence u/s. 138 of the Act -

Held, there was no evidence to conclude that any measurement of the work was done and the accounts were settled - As regards the variation in the stand of the appellant, namely, the one in the reply notice and the other in the written statement the same did not materially affect the stand of the appellant in the light of the overwhelming evidence in support of her stand - Cheque was not in the handwriting of the appellant strengthens the defence version that it was not executed in favour of the respondent - Hence, judgment of the trial Court in having drawn the conclusions to the effect that the appellant sufficiently rebutted the initial presumption as regards the issuance of the cheque u/ss. 138 and 139 of the Act, was perfectly justified - Consequently, the conviction and sentence imposed in the judgment impugned was set aside - Appeal allowed.
