Dipak Kumar Mukherjee v Kolkata Municipal Corporation And Others

Supreme Court of India

08 October 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 337; (2013) 5 SCC 336; AIR 2013 SC 927; JT 2012 (10) SC 354; 2012 (8) MLJ(SC) 107; 2012(10) SCALE 29; [2012] 10 S.C.R. 757

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Municipalities & Local Governments - Practice & Procedure - Kolkata Municipal Corporation Act, 1980, ss. 396, 400(1), 400(8) - Kolkata Municipal Corporation Building Rules, 1990, r. 25(2) - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 1 r. 8 - Unauthorised constructions - Demolition of - Challenged - Respondent No. 8 was owner of a building and respondent No. 7 was developer of said building - Respondent (Corporation) sanctioned permission for construction of multi storied building, on 11-4-1990 - Thereafter, when building in question was inspected by officials of respondent, found construction in deviation of sanctioned plan - Notice was issued u/ss. 400(1) and 401(A) of the Act, to stop construction and a report was submitted by Deputy Chief Engineer (Building) for demolition of unauthorised construction - Issue was considered by Mayor-in-Council on 14-1-2010 and was decided to demolish unauthorised construction - Accordingly, part of total constructed area was demolished on 4.1.2010 - Meanwhile, appellant filed a petition before HC for issue of direction to respondent (Corporation) to demolish illegal construction by respondent No. 7, which was disposed of directing competent authority to decide issue of illegal construction - However, respondent No. 7 continued construction of building in question - Appellant filed fresh writ petition before HC - Single Judge directed competent authority to demolish unauthorised construction forthwith u/s. 400(8) of the Act - Aggrieved respondent No. 7 and 8 filed appeal before DB - DB suo-motu directed issue of notice under O. 1 r. 8 of CPC Civil in daily newspapers to enable other purchasers of unauthorised portions of building to present their cause before Court, but none of occupants had come forward to espouse their cause - DB disposed of appeal, directing competent authority of Corporation to take appropriate decision in accordance with law - Hence, instant appeal - Appellant contended that respondent No. 7 had defied 'stop work notice', decision took by Mayor-in-Council and continued with construction of building even after demolition of unauthorised portion thereof and that DB committed serious error by interfering with direction gave by Single Judge for demolition of construction which was in violation of sanctioned plan and by showing total contempt for notices issued by respondent (Corporation) u/ss. 400 and 401 of the Act - Respondent (Corporation) contended that respondent No. 7 admitted construction of building in violation of sanctioned plan and construction could not be regularised under r. 25(2) of the Rules - Whether unauthorised construction raised by respondent No. 7 was liable to be demolished - Held, respondent No. 7 did not disputed that building was constructed in violation of sanctioned plan and Mayor-in-Council passed order dt.14-1-2010 for demolition of disputed construction - Direction gave by DB to competent authority of respondent (Corporation) to pass appropriate order after giving opportunity of hearing to respondent No. 7 could not be sustained - It appeared that attention of DB was not drawn to notices issued by competent authority of Corporation u/ss. 400, 401 and 401A of the Act and order dt.14.1.2010 passed by Mayor-in-Council, else it would not have decided appeal by assuming that competent authority had not passed an order for demolition of illegal construction - Respondent No. 7 had raised construction in violation of plan sanctioned u/s. 396 of the Act and continued with that activity despite order of Mayor-in-Council - Respondent No. 7 could not take benefit of r. 25 of the Rules because disputed construction was in clear violation of sanctioned plan and notices issued by competent authority of Corporation and because application for regularization was made after completion of construction - Respondent No.7 was guilty not only of violating sanctioned plan and relevant provisions of the Act and Rules framed there under but also of cheating those who purchased portions of unauthorized construction under a bona fide belief that respondent No. 7 had constructed the building in question as per sanctioned plan - With the demolition of unauthorized construction some of such persons would become shelter-less - Respondent No. 7 was directed to compensate them by refunding cost of flat with interest - Respondent No. 7 should pay for raising construction in violation of sanctioned plan -Unauthorized construction of buildings not only destroys the concept of planned development which was beneficial to public but also places unbearable burden on basic amenities and facilities provided by public authorities - It was imperative for concerned public authorities not only to demolish unauthorized construction but also impose adequate penalty on wrongdoer - Appeal allowed.
