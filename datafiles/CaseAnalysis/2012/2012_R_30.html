Jegannathan v Raju Sigamani & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

02 April 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, H.L. Gokhale

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 115; (2012) 5 SCC 540; AIR 2012 SC 3788; 2012 (5) ALD(SC) 8; 2012(3) ALL MR 441; 2012 (92) ALR 457; 2012 (3) ALT 54; 2012 (3) AWC 3037; 2012 (4) Bom.C.R. 28; 2012 (3) CLT(SC) 133; 2012 (3) CTC 410; 2012 (279) E.L.T. 328; JT 2012 (4) SC 91; 2012 (5) MahLJ 1; 2012 (4) MLJ(SC) 314; 2012 (3) MPLJ 502; 2012 (3) RCR(Civil) 233; 2012 (116) RD 389; 2012 (2) RLW 1722; 2013 (1) RLW 840; 2012(4) SCALE 228; [2012] 3 S.C.R. 1003

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 43 r. 1(u), s. 100 - Appeal against Remand order - Maintainability -- Appellant-plaintiff No. 2 filed suit for declaration, permanent injunction and mandatory injunction against respondent No. 1 and Trial Court, after recording evidence and hearing parties, decreed suit - Respondent No. 1 preferred appeal whereby appellate court allowed appeal and set aside judgment of Trial Court and remanded suit back to Trial Court with a direction to give an opportunity to both parties to let in evidence oral and documentary and then decide suit afresh on merits - Order of remand was challenged by appellant and respondent No. 2 before HC under O. 43 r. 1(u) of Code - HC dismissed appeal on ground that appeal was not maintainable - Review petition was filed before HC, which was dismissed - Aggrieved instant appeal - Whether HC was justified in dismissing appeals against order of remand as non-maintainable - Held, an appeal under O. 43 r. 1 cl. (u) should be heard only on ground enumerated in s. 100 of Code - Constraints of s. 100 continued to be attached to an appeal under O. 43 r. 1(u) - Appeal under O. 43 r. 1(u) could only be heard on grounds a second appeal was heard u/s. 100 - There was a difference between maintainability of an appeal and scope of hearing of an appeal - HC failed to keep in view this distinction and wrongly held that miscellaneous appeal preferred by appellant was not maintainable - Impugned order of HC was set aside - Appeals allowed.
