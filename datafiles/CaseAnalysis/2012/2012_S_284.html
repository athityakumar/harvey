Sangeetaben Mahendrabhai Patel v State of Gujarat and another

Supreme Court of India

23 April 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, Jagdish Singh Khehar

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 175; (2012) 7 SCC 621; AIR 2012 SC 2844; 2013 ALL MR (Cri) 358; 2012 (3) Bom.C.R. 537; 2012 CRLJ 2432; 2012 (3) G.L.R. 1841; 2012 (3) JCC (NI) 160; JT 2012 (4) SC 338; 2012 (2) RCR(Civil) 839; 2012 (2) RCR(Criminal) 757; 2012(4) SCALE 549; [2012] 3 S.C.R. 1155

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Gold (Control) Act, 1968, Second Trial, Import and Export (Control) Act, 1947, Autrefois Acquit, Rajasthan Sati (Prevention) Act, 1987

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Banking & Finance - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 482- Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, s. 138 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 406, 420, r/w s. 114 - Dishonour of Cheque - Insufficient Funds - Double jeopardy - Respondent No. 2 filed complaint u/s. 138 of the Act on the ground that appellant had taken hypothecation loan and did not repay - Appellant issued cheque on being presented for collection, cheque had been dishonoured - Respondent no. 2 filed an FIR u/s. 406/420, r/w s. 114 IPC for committing offence of criminal breach of trust, cheating and abetment - Trial court convicted appellant u/s. 138 of the Act - Aggrieved, appellant file appeal 2006, before DJ and had been acquitted - Against order of acquittal, respondent no. 2 file appeal before HC which was pending - Appellant stood acquitted in criminal case u/s. 138 of the Act - Thus, appellant should not be tried again for same offence - Doctrine of double jeopardy was attracted and HC dismissed application - Hence instant appeal - Whether the scope and application of double jeopardy was applicable - Held, appellant had been tried for offences punishable u/s. 138 of the Act and case was sub judice before HC - Appellant was involved u/ss. 406, 420 r/w s. 114 IPC, prosecution u/s. 138 of the Act, mens rea that fraudulent or dishonest intention at time of issuance of cheque was not required to be proved - Offence punishable u/s. 420 IPC was serious and sentence of 7 years would be imposed. In the case under N.I. Act, there was legal presumption that the cheque had been issued for discharging antecedent liability and that presumption should be rebutted only by person who draws cheque, which was not required for offences under IPC - Under the Act, if fine was imposed, it was to be adjusted to meet legally enforceable liability which was not required for offences in IPC - Case under the Act should only be initiated by filing complaint, However, under IPC such condition was not necessary - There was some overlapping of facts in both cases, but ingredients of offences were entirely different. Thus, subsequent case was not barred by any of statutory provisions - Appeal dismissed
