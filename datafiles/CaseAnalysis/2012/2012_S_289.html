Laxmi Dyechem v State of Gujarat and others

Supreme Court of India

27 November 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, Gyan Sudha Misra

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 500; (2012) 13 SCC 375; 2014 ALL MR (Cri) 721; 2013 (1) BC 140; 2013 (1) Bom.C.R. 813; 2012 (4) CLT(SC) 506; 2013 (1) CompLJ 137; 2013 CRLJ 3288; 2012 (6) CTC 690; 2012 (195) DLT 111; 2013 (1) JCC (NI) 41; JT 2012 (12) SC 65; 2013 (1) KLT 167; 2013 (1) MLJ(Crl) 336; 2013 (1) RCR(Civil) 353; 2013 (1) RCR(Criminal) 260; 2012(11) SCALE 365; [2012] 11 S.C.R. 466

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Criminal

Keywords: Dishonour of cheques, Stop Payment, Banking, Public Financial Institutions and Negotiable Instruments Laws (amendment) Act, 1988

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, ss. 138,139 - Cheque Dishonour - Signatures not complete - Holder of Cheque - Presumption - Discharge - Sustainability - Appellant-complainant supplied chemical to respondent-company-accused who in lieu of goods supplied issued 117 cheques, several cheques when presented were dishonoured by bank for reason drawers signature were incomplete or that no image was found or signatures did not match - 40 different cases were filed and direction was issued for service of summons to respondents - Aggrieved accused No. 5 filed petition for quashing of proceedings contending that signature were incomplete or that no image was found or signatures did not match could not constitute offence u/s. 138 of Act - HC, allowed petition holding that endorsement that signature were incomplete or that no image was found or signatures did not match does not amount to dishonour of cheque - Hence instant appeal - Whether HC, was justified in holding that cheque returned due to signature were incomplete or that no image was found or signatures did not match did not amount of dishonour of cheque - Held, dishonour of cheques simpliciter for reasons stated in s. 138 of Act although was sufficient for commission of offence since presumption of law on this point was no longer res integra , category of 'stop payment' instruction to bank where account holder had sufficient funds in his account to discharge debt for which cheque was issued, category of cases would be subject to rebuttal as this question being rebuttable - Offence u/s. 138 although would be made out, same will attract s. 139 leaving burden of proof of rebuttal by drawer of cheque, thus, in cases arising out of 'stop payment' situation, ss. 138, 139 will have to be given a harmonious construction as in that event s. 139 would be rendered nugatory - Instant matter however does not relate to a case of 'stop payment' instruction to bank as cheque in question had been returned due to mismatching of signatures but more than that petitioner having neither raised nor proved to contrary as envisaged u/s. 139 of Act that cheques were not for discharge of a lawful debt nor making payment within fifteen days of notice assigning any reason as to why cheques had at all been issued if amount had not been settled, plea of rebuttal envisaged u/s. 139 does not come to his rescue so as to hold that same would fall within realm of rebuttable presumption envisaged u/s. 139 of Act - In view of legal position that presumption in favour of holder of a cheque u/s. 139 of Act had been held by Act as also by SC, to be a rebuttable presumption to be discharged by accused/drawee of cheque which may be discharged even at threshold where magistrate examines a case at stage of taking cognizance as to whether a prima facie case had been made out or not against drawer of cheque - Order accordingly.
