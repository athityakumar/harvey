State Of Rajasthan v Darshan Singh @ Darshan Lal

Supreme Court of India

21 May 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 141; (2012) 5 SCC 789; AIR 2012 SC 1973; 2012 ALL MR (Cri) 2407; 2012 CRLJ 2908; JT 2012 (5) SC 229; 2012 (3) MLJ(Crl) 506; 2012(3) M.P.H.T. 1; 2012 (3) RCR(Civil) 152; 2012 (2) RCR(Criminal) 987; 2012(5) SCALE 570

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 201, 302, - Evidence of deaf and dumb person - Reliability - Chargesheet was filed against the respondent u/ss. 302 and 201 of IPC - PW.16 was the sole eye-witness of the occurrence, being deaf and dumb, her statement was recorded in sign language with the help of her father, PW.1, as an interpreter - Trial Court placed reliance upon the evidence of PW.16 and recovery etc., and convicted the respondent u/s. 302 of IPC - Appeal filed thereagainst was allowed - HC held that deposition of PW.16 could not be relied upon as it was not safe for the court to embark upon the examination of deaf and dumb witness, on her information without the help of an expert or a person familiar of her mode of conveying ideas to others in day to day life - Hence, instant appeal - Whether deposition of PW.16 could be relied upon - Held, deaf and dumb person was a competent witness - Such a witness, if able to read and write, it was desirable to record his statement in writing - In case the witness was not able to read and write, his statement could be recorded in sign language with the aid of interpreter, if found necessary - In case the interpreter was provided, he should be a person of the same surrounding but should not have any interest in the case and he should be administered oath - In the instant case, there was sufficient material on record that PW.16 was able to read and write and this fact stood proved in the Trial Court - However, her statement had been recorded with the help of her father as an interpreter, who for the reasons given by the HC, being an interested witness who had assisted during trial, investigation and was examined without administering oath, made the evidence unreliable - Hence, HC rightly given the benefit of doubt and acquitted the respondent - Appeal dismissed.
