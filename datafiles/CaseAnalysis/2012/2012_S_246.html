Kishore Samrite v State Of Uttar Pradesh And Others

Supreme Court of India

18 October 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Swatanter Kumar, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 627; (2013) 2 SCC 398; AIR 2012 SC (Supp) 699; [2012] 9 S.C.R. 733

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Human Rights; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Rajasthan High Court Ordinance, 1949, Allahabad High Court Rules, 1952

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Human Rights - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 21 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 376 - Rape - Detention - Infringement of rights - Petitioner was came to know from certain websites which contained news items stating that while on tour of his parliamentary constituency respondent no.6, along with six of his friends committed rape on victim who was daughter of congress worker - Incident was reported to various authorities, including Chief Minister, Home Minister, Chief Secretary of State, Governor and other authorities of State - Only communication he received was from office of Governor wherein it was said that his application had been sent to State Govt. for proper action - No action was being taken on basis of said report - Congress men had threatened to kill victim if they raised issue - Though offence u/s. 376 of IPC was made out, yet no FIR was being registered by authorities - Ex-member of legislative assembly filed petition which was dismissed - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether there was infringement of right to life and liberty as enshrined u/art. 21 of the Constitution and victim was illegally detained - Held, victims had been used by both petitioners and they were ones whose reputation had suffered serious setback and were exposed to inconvenience of being dragged to courts for no fault of their own - There was hardly any attributes of Police except performance of their duties in normal course so as to entitled them to exceptional rewards - Reputation of respondent no.6 had been damaged - Writ petition was based upon falsehood, was abuse of process of Court and was driven by malice and political vendetta - While dismissing there was imposition of exemplary costs upon next friend, costs being payable to respondent no.6 - Further, next friend in petition had approached Court with unclean hands, without disclosing complete facts and misusing judicial process - Petitioner filed petition without any proper authority, in fact and in law - On basis of affidavit filed by Director General of Police statement of three petitioners in writ Petition, CBI's stand before Court, its report and contradictory stand taken by next friend in writ Petition were of view that allegations against respondent no.6 in regard to alleged incident of rape and alleged detention of petitioners, were without substance - There was not iota of evidence before Court to validly form opinion to contrary - As per petitioners they were never detained by any person at any point of time - Court of competent jurisdiction or CBI would not in any way be influenced by observations made or even judgment of HC - Appeal disposed of
