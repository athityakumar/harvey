Alister Anthony Pareira v State Of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

12 January 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, Jagdish Singh Khehar

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 12; (2012) 2 SCC 648; 2012 (1) ACC 104; AIR 2012 SC 3802; 2012 CRLJ 1160; 2012 (1) JCC 641; JT 2012 (1) SC 100; 2012 (1) MLJ(Crl) 627; 2012(1) SCALE 189; [2012] 1 S.C.R. 145

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Reckless

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 304 Part II, 338 and 337 -Culpable homicide - Rash and Negligent driving - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant was convicted by Trial Court for offence u/ss. 304 A 337 of IPC - State preferred appeal challenging acquittal of appellant u/ss. 304 Part II and 338 and also sought enhancement of sentence - HC set aside acquittal of appellant u/s. 304 IPC and convicted him u/ss. 304 Part II, 338 and 337 of IPC and enhanced his sentence- Aggrieved by HC order, appellant preferred instant appeal - Appellant contended that it was legally impermissible and mutually destructive and two charges cannot co-exist - Further no question was put to appellant about his drunken condition or that he was under influence of alcohol which resulted causing death - Respondent contended that drunken condition of appellant was fully proved with recovery of liquor bottle -- (A) Whether indictment on two charges, namely, offence punishable u/s. 304 Part II IPC and offence punishable u/s. 338 IPC was mutually destructive and legally impermissible or whether it was permissible to try and convict a person for offence punishable u/s. 304 Part II IPC and the offence punishable u/s. 338 IPC for a single act of same transaction - Held, if a person doing an act of rash or negligent driving, if aware of risk that particular consequences was likely to result and that result occurred may be held guilty - Appellant drove in rash and negligent on public road with knowledge of dangerous character likely effecting act and resulting in death - No impediment in law for an offender being charged and convicted for two charges which could legally co-exist in single case of rash and negligent Act - (B) Whether by not charging appellant of 'drunken condition' and not putting to him entire incriminating evidence let in by prosecution, particularly evidence relating to appellant's drunken condition, at time of his examination u/s. 313 of Code, trial and conviction of appellant got affected - Held, police after completing investigation had reported that rash and negligent driving of appellant by consuming alcohol caused deaths - Copy of report was given to appellant along with other materials - Examination u/s. 313 of code showed that appellant was fully aware of prosecution evidence relating to rash and negligent driving in drunken condition - (C) Whether prosecution evidence established beyond reasonable doubt commission of offences by appellant u/s. 304 Part II, IPC, s. 338 IPC and s. 337 IPC - Held, appellant had admitted the accident and his explanation was that accident occurred due to mechanical failure and defect that was developed in vehicle - Court did not find explanation improbable and unacceptable - (D) Whether sentence awarded to appellant by HC for offence punishable u/s. 304 Part II IPC required any modification - Held, facts and circumstances of case had been proved bringing home guilt of accused u/s. 304 Part II IPC undoubtedly despicable aggravated offence warranting punishment proportionate to crime - Sentence of three years awarded by HC was too meagre and not adequate - It would be travesty of justice and highly unjust, unfair, improper and disproportionate to gravity of crime - HC was quite considerate and lenient in awarding sentence - However, appellant was directed to surrender for undergoing remaining sentence as awarded by HC - Appeal dismissed.
