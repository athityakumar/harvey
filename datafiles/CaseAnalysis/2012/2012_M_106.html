State of Uttar Pradesh v Munesh

Supreme Court of India

12 October 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, Ranjan Gogoi

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 352; (2012) 9 SCC 742; AIR 2013 SC 147; 2013 CRLJ 194; JT 2012 (10) SC 120; 2012 (4) MLJ(Crl) 540; 2012 (4) RCR(Criminal) 712; 2012(10) SCALE 141; [2012] 9 S.C.R. 545

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 376, 302, 511 - Rape - Murder - Acquittal -Sustainability - Respondent accused raped and murdered deceased who was minor - Accused was charged for offences punishable u/ss. 376, 302 and 511 IPC - Trial Court convicted and sentenced to death - Accused filed appeal before HC - Accused was acquitted by HC - Hence instant appeal by State - Whether the order of HC was sustainable - Held, deceased death was caused due to asphyxia by strangulation and due to pre-mordial injuries - PW4/doctor supported prosecution case that deceased was raped before strangulation - Prosecution case should not be doubted about rape, medical evidence proved that victim was raped before her death - Prosecution story was fully corroborated with medical evidence on which HC failed to give importance - In light of material objects evidence of prosecution witnesses, statement of PW4 who conducted post mortem proved prosecution case - Acceptable materials in form of oral and documentary evidence led by prosecution eye-witnesses PW2 and PW3 who were independent witnesses coupled with evidence of PW4 were acceptable - Analysis and ultimate conclusion of HC was contrary to acceptable and reliable material placed by prosecution and held that accused first committed offence of rape and then murdered deceased - Prosecution established both charges u/ss. 376 and 302 IPC - Impugned order of HC was set - Since incident occurred in year 2002, sentence of death was modified to rigorous imprisonment for life - Appeal dismissed.
