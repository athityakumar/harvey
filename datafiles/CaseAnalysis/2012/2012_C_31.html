Cesc Ltd. v Chief Post Master General & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

11 May 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya, R.M. Lodha

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 156; (2012) 6 SCC 286; AIR 2012 SC 2027; 2012 (5) ALD(SC) 61; 2012 (4) AWC 3422; 2012 (2) CLT(SC) 290; JT 2012 (5) SC 190; 2012 (6) MLJ(SC) 121; 2013 (1) RCR(Civil) 196; 2012 (4) RLW 3168; 2012(5) SCALE 492; [2012] 5 S.C.R. 1055; [2012] 207 TAXMAN 340

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative

Keywords: Indian Post Office Rules, 1933

Summary: Administrative - Indian Postal Act, 1898, s. 11 - Demand notice - Validity - Appellant was conducting the business of supplying electricity and had about 26 lakh of registered consumers - Consumption bills were sent by the Appellant to its consumers, every month through the Post Office - Later, Director of Postal Service informed the appellant about the revised postal tariff w.e.f 1-6-1997 - Accordingly, from June 1997 to October, 1998, the appellant sent a total of 1,63,60,121 Bills, based on the said communication, treating the posts as 'book post', affixing Rs.1/, per postal articles - Posts were cleared by the postal Dept. without any objection and were also delivered to the respective addressee consumers - However, subsequently, appellant was informed that the 'Monthly Consumption Bill' did not come under the category of 'book post'/ 'book packets' and that such type of 'bills' could be posted by affixing postage stamps as applicable to the 'letter mail' with immediate effect - Appellant started posting the consumption bills affixing Rs.3/ stamps under protest and without prejudice - Dept. of Post made additional claim for Rs. 1,83,89,410/ for the period from June 1997 to October, 1998 during which a total of 1,69,60,121 bills were dispatched by the appellant affixing franking stamp of Rs.1/per bill - As the Postal Authority continued to make the demand, the appellant filed writ petition on the ground that the demand notice asking the appellant to deposit Rs.1,83,89,410/, was contrary to s. 11(2) of the Act - Single Judge allowed the writ petition affirming that the demand notice was contrary to s. 11(2) of the Act - On appeal, DB held that the Postal Authority through the Post Master General, West Bengal Circle was completely empowered by Cl. 11.5(xv) and 34 of the Post Office Guide r/w s. 12 of the Act to recover the outstanding sum remaining, due by the licensee Appellant to the Postal Authority - Hence, instant appeal - Whether Post Master General was empowered by Cl. 11.5 (xv) and 34 of the Post Office Guide to recover the sum from appellant - Held, Cl. 30(iv) of Post Office Guide reminds the office of the Postal Authority to check the bundles to ensure proper check of franking articles - In the instant case, due to a wrong intimation given by the Postal Authority, the appellant affixed the postal stamp of Rs.1/per bill, treating it as 'book post' and the staff of the Postal Dept. without any objection cleared and delivered to the respective addressees - There was failure on the part of the office of the Postal Authority and for that the sender appellant could not be made liable - Mistake having been committed by the Postal Authority and there being failure on the part of office of the Postal Authority to check the postal articles and postage for recovering the amount from the addressee, it was not open for the Postal Authority to pass on such liability on the appellant or to recover the same from the appellant - Demand notice being not proper was rightly held to be illegal by the Single Judge - Demand notice and the order passed by the DB set aside - Appeals allowed.
