IFB Industries Limited and another v State of Kerala and another

Supreme Court of India

27 February 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Aftab Alam, A.R. Dave

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 68; (2012) 4 SCC 618; AIR 2012 SC 1468; 2013 (295) E.L.T. 186; JT 2012 (3) SC 110; 2012(3) SCALE 12; [2012] 4 S.C.R. 802; [2012] 49 VST 1

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Keywords: Sale Price, Trade Discount, Practice

Summary: Sales Tax - Indirect Tax - Kerala General Sales Tax Rules, 1963, r. 9(a) - A/y 2001-02 and 2002-03 - Computation - Deduction of trade discounts - Assessing Authority (AA) accepted appellant's claim for deduction of trade discount given by appellant to its dealers through credit notes u/r. 9(a) of Rules and it was only a dispute over computation - Appellant had objection to computation made by AA contending that though in principle it allowed deduction for trade discount AA actually denied any deduction by subtracting amount of trade discount only after first adding it to turnover - Further contended that in the computation made by the AA the amount of trade discount, thus, got neutralized and appellant does not actually get any deduction of the trade discount from its turnover - Deputy Commissioner accepted case of appellant and observed that in effect appellant's claim was disallowed even though it was allowed in order of AA, accordingly, directed AA to verify whether it was a computation mistake and to modify order accordingly - Appellate Authority allowed appeal of revenue filed against order of Deputy Commissioner holding that since there was no assessment on trade discount, direction of AA to verify whether there was a mistake in this computation was without any basis - Appellant's rectification application was rejected - Appellant preferred revision before HC - HC, observed that in order to be eligible for deduction in terms of r. 9(a) of the Rules the discount must be granted in the invoices itself - HC set aside orders passed by Revenue authorities and remitted case to AA for fresh assessment - Aggrieved instant appeal - How far deductions were allowable u/r. 9(a) of the Rules - Held, claim for exemption of amounts of trade discount could not be denied solely on ground that discount amounts were not shown in sale invoices - Rules does not speak of invoices but stipulates that the discount must be shown in the accounts - Provision states that exemption is allowable subject to 2 conditions; first, discount is given in accordance with regular practice in trade and accounts should show that purchaser had paid only sum originally charged less discount, and secondly, nothing in r. 9(a) of Rules to read it in restrictive manner to mean that a discount in order to qualify for exemption under its provision must be shown in invoice itself - Orders of HC coming under appeal were unsustainable, cases of appellants for respective assessment periods were remitted to AA with a direction to make assessments and pass fresh orders in accordance with law and in light of this judgment - Appeals allowed.
