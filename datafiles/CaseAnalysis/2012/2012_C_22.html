Chandra Kumar Chopra v Union Of India And Others

Supreme Court of India

11 May 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 446; (2012) 6 SCC 369; 2012 (5) AWC 4470; 2012 (2) JCC 1464; JT 2012 (5) SC 504; 2012 (3) MLJ(Crl) 251; 2012 (3) RCR(Criminal) 419; [2012] 5 S.C.R. 1029

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Defence & Security Forces - Army Act, 1950, s. 164 - Punishment - Challenged - Instant appeal was directed against the order passed by the HC wherein Single Judge had declined to interfere with the order whereby the confirming authority u/s. 164 of Act had passed order of confirmation as regards the sentence of cashiering but reduced the rigorous imprisonment from five years to six months as imposed by the Competent Authority of General Court Martial - Whether punishment of six months awarded to appellant required to be reduced - Held, appellant was Major in the Army and all the charges levelled against him fundamentally pertained to commission of illegal acts in fiscal sphere - Acts done by him were intended to gain pecuniary advantage - Primary obligation of member of Armed Forces was to maintain discipline in all aspects - Discipline in fiscal matters had to be given top priority as that mirrors the image of any institution - That apart, appellant was Major in the Army - Irreproachable conduct, restrained attitude, understanding of responsibility and adherence to discipline in apple pie order were expected of him - Proven charges luminously project that the said aspects should be given total go by - It was well-nigh impossible to hold that the punishment was harsh or arbitrary - Regard being had to the nature of rank held by the appellant and the disciplined conduct expected of him, HC found that the doctrine of proportionality was uninvocable and, accordingly, SC was also compelled to repel the said preponement advanced by appellant without any hesitation - Appeal dismissed.
