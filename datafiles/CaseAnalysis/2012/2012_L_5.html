United India Insurance Company Limited v Laxmamma and others

Supreme Court of India

17 April 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, H.L. Gokhale

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 401; (2012) 5 SCC 234; 2012 (4) ACC 801; 2012 ACJ 1307; AIR 2012 SC 2817; 2012 (4) ALD(SC) 165; 2012 (92) ALR 723; 2012 (3) AWC 3046; 2012 (4) CLT(SC) 539; JT 2012 (4) SC 273; 2012 (6) KarLJ 529; 2012 (5) MahLJ 506; 2012 (4) MLJ(SC) 429; 2012 (4) MPLJ 29; 2012 (2) RCR(Civil) 834; [2012] 5 S.C.R. 261

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Insurance - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, ss. 147(5),149(1) - Compensation Claim - Cancellation of Insurance - Non-payment of Premium - Award - Sustainability - Husband-deceased of respondent No. 1 was travelling in bus and due to negligent application of brake by bus driver, back door of bus opened and deceased fell down and died - Respondents filed claim compensation petition for Rs. 15 lakhs against appellant-respondent No. 2 and owner of vehicle-respondent No. 1 - Appellant-insurer had contended that insurance premium was paid through cheque and cheque got dishonored, therefore there was no liability on it to cover third party risk - Tribunal held that cancellation of policy due to non-payment of premium was done by insurer after accident and held insurer was liable to claimants and passed award for an amount of Rs - Appellant filed appeal before HC, which was dismissed - Aggrieved instant appeal - Whether Tribunal was justified in awarding compensation to claimant which was upheld by HC - Held, where policy of insurance was issued by an authorized insurer on receipt of cheque towards payment of premium and such cheque was returned dishonoured, liability of authorized insurer to indemnify third parties in respect of liability which that policy covered subsists and it has to satisfy award of compensation by reason of provisions of ss. 147(5), 149(1) of Act, unless policy of insurance was cancelled by authorized insurer and intimation of such cancellation has reached insured before accident - In present case, owner of bus obtained policy of insurance from insurer for period 16-4-2004 to 15-4-2005 for which premium was paid through cheque on 14-4-2004, accident occurred on 11-05-2004, it was only thereafter that insurer cancelled insurance policy by communication dt. 13-5- 2004 on the ground of dishonour of cheque which was received by the owner of the vehicle on 21-5-2004 - Cancellation of policy been done by insurer after accident, insurer became liable to satisfy award of compensation passed in favour of claimants - Appeal dismissed.
