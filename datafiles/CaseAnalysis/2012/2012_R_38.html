Ram Dhan v State Of U.P. & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

10 April 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, Jagdish Singh Khehar

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 125; (2012) 5 SCC 536; AIR 2012 SC 2513; 2012 CRLJ 2419; 2012 (2) JCC 1400; 2012 (3) MLJ(Crl) 714; 2012 (2) RCR(Criminal) 751; 2012(4) SCALE 259; [2012] 3 S.C.R. 1059

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 364, 149, 177, 181, 182, 195, 420 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 239, 195, 340, 482 - Kidnapping - Convicted - Discharge - Sustainability - Petitioner filed complaint against respondent No. 2 alleging that he had kidnapped his son - Chargesheet was filed and respondent No. 2 and other accused were convicted u/s. 364, 149 of IPC - Petitioner's son came back and had informed that he was not kidnapped rather had voluntarily gone - Respondent No. 2, realizing that he was wrongly enroped and convicted, filed FIR u/ss. 177, 181, 182, 195, 420 of IPC against petitioner, chargesheet was filed u/ss. 177, 181, 182, 195 of IPC - Petitioner filed application u/s. 239 of CrPC contending that FIR at the behest of respondent No. 2 was not maintainable in view of s. 195 r/w s. 340 of CrPC - Magistrate rejected application of petitioner - Petitioner filed revision before HC which was dismissed - Aggrieved instant petition - Whether Courts below were justified in not discharging petitioner of charges - Held, after investigation, chargesheet had been filed against petitioner and others u/ss. 177, 181, 182, 195 of IPC - Petitioner had suppressed material fact and had not disclosed anywhere in petition that he had approached HC u/s. 482 of CrPC for quashing of chargesheet, which stood rejected and said HC order attained finality as had not been challenged any further - Petitioner was guilty of suppressing material fact, and filing of successive petition before court amounted to abuse of process of court - Petition dismissed.
