Rajesh Bhatnagar,Mukesh Bhatnagar v State Of Uttarakhand

Supreme Court of India

10 May 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Swatanter Kumar, Ranjan Gogoi

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 245; (2012) 7 SCC 91; AIR 2012 SC 2866; 2012 CRLJ 3442; 2012 (2) JCC 1424; JT 2012 (5) SC 91; 2012 (3) MLJ(Crl) 73; 2012 (3) RCR(Criminal) 854; 2012(5) SCALE 311; [2012] 5 S.C.R. 895

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss.173, 313 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 304B - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 113B - Dowry death - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant accused persons, alleged to have committed offence u/s. 304B, by pouring kerosene, on deceased wife, as she and her parents failed to satisfy their demands of dowry - Trial Court convicted and sentenced all appellants to undergo life imprisonment - HC dismissed appeal of all accused and confirmed conviction and order of sentence passed by Trial Court - Hence instant appeals - Whether order of conviction was sustainable - Held, there was definite ocular, expert and documentary evidence to show that there was persistent demand of dowry by accused persons, deceased was subjected to cruelty and ill-treatment - There was demand of dowry on specific items like refrigerator, television and cooler and they had killed her by sprinkling kerosene on her and putting her on fire deceased died an unnatural death and she died within seven years of her marriage - Thus, ingredients of s. 304B were fully satisfied - Hence, there was no mitigating circumstances in favour of accused which would persuade to take any view other than view taken by Trial Court on question of quantum of sentence - Appeals dismissed.
