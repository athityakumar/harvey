Ashok Kumar v State of Uttarakhand and others

Supreme Court of India

13 December 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 542; (2013) 3 SCC 366; JT 2013 (9) SC 143; 2013(2) M.P.H.T. 362; 2013 (1) RCR(Criminal) 961; 2012(12) SCALE 338; [2012] 13 S.C.R. 971; 2013 (1) WLN(SC) 93

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Land & Property - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 145,146(1) - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 39 r. 1&2 - Temporary Injunction - Attachment of Property - Breach of Peace - Sustainability - Respondent No. 2 filed suit for temporary injunction restraining appellant from interfering with her peaceful possession, along with interim application of interim orders - Judge ordered notice to respondents and also ordered for local inspection by Amin - Respondent No. 2 filed application u/.s 145 of CrPC, before SDM for attachment of property, enquiry was conducted and report was that house of appellant was situated in land in dispute and that there was possibility of breach of peace - SDM ordered for attachment of property u/s. 146(1) of CrPC - Aggrieved appellant filed application before HC, which was affirmed by HC - Hence instant appeal - Whether SDM and HC, were justified in passing order of attachment u/s. 146(1) of CrPC - Held, respondent had filed a civil suit for injunction before Civil Judge and an application for interim injunction was also pending, on which civil court had issued notice, an Amin report was called for and Amin submitted its report - Civil suit was filed prior in point of time, it was for civil court to decide as to who was in possession on date of filing of suit, there was nothing to show that there was an emergency so as to invoke powers u/s. 146(1) to attach property, specially, when civil court was seized of matter, under such circumstances, SC, was inclined to set aside order passed by SDM dt. 25-11-2009 and order of HC - Hence, no ground exists to attach property u/s. 146 of CrPC - Appeal disposed of.
