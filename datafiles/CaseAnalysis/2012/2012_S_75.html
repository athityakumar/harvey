Sheikh Jumman v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

12 September 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 399; (2012) 12 SCC 486

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 498-A - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant (accused) allegedly murder his wife (deceased) and committed offences u/ss. 302 r/w 498-A of IPC- Trial Court convicted appellant u/s. 302 r/w s. 498-A of IPC and sentenced him to undergo imprisonment for life - Aggrieved appellant filed appeal before DB, which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Respondent (State) contended that if really it was PW7 (nephew of PW3 (mother of deceased)) who was responsible for causing attack on deceased, there was no reason why appellant did not took any step after the alleged assault on deceased - Whether impugned conviction of appellant was justified - Held, incident occurred at about 4.45 a.m and eye witness PW3 who was residing separately in same house, heard the cries of deceased, and then when she rushed to room where deceased and the appellant were living, she found the appellant causing severe injuries on the chest of the deceased with a knife and that she could clearly see the appellant withdrawing the knife on the chest of deceased after the last blow - PW3 stated that after inflicting injuries on deceased, appellant rushed out while PW3 brought PW 7 for help who was stated to have immediately rushed to spot and thereafter proceeded to Police station for lodging the FIR - Story of prosecution as narrated was true and there was nothing brought out in cross-examination of either PW 3 or PW 7 to dislodge their version - Medical evidence of PW4 confirmed that manner in which injuries were inflicted upon deceased were so serious and severe that resulted in instantaneous death of deceased - Evidence of PW 3 read along with PW4, post morterm report fully confirm the involvement of appellant in the murder of deceased - Appellant examined as DW1 admitted that he did pull out knife from the chest of deceased though he would claim that the knife was thrust on his wife by PW7, which could not be accepted for simple reason that if said version was true there was no reason why appellant did not took any steps to either apprehend the assailant then and there or took steps to save the life of deceased by getting necessary medical aid or by reporting the incident to Police to book the culprit - Conduct of appellant was found loitering in the street with blood stained knife in his hand for several hours after the occurence till Police party arrived at the scene to arrest him and recover blood stained clothes and knife show that it was appellant who was responsible for causing injuries on the deceased - Impugned conviction was justified - Appeal dismissed.
