Tejas Constructions & Infrastructure Pvt. Ltd. v Municipal Council, Sendhwa & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

04 May 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, Gyan Sudha Misra

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 443; (2012) 6 SCC 464; 2012 (5) AWC 4596; 2012 (3) BC 74; 2012 (2) JabLJ 364; JT 2012 (4) SC 574; [2012] 4 S.C.R. 190

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Municipalities & Local Governments - Contract & Commercial - Practice & Procedure - Integrated Water Supply Scheme - Technical Bid - Eligibility - Allotment - Sustainability - Respondent No. 1 invited tenders from eligible contractors for the construction of an Integrated Water Supply Scheme, several applications were received only six applicants eventually participated in pre-bid meeting out of six bidders only four were eventually found to be eligible - Appellant's case, was that on request to show technical bid received from respondent No.2, appellant had raised an objection as to eligibility to participate in bid process on the ground that it did not have requisite experience of executing a single integrated water supply scheme of requisite value further respondent No.2 was said to have claimed eligibility to offer a bid on basis of clubbing of different water supply scheme projects which was impermissible and that respondent No.2 had not submitted certified copies of audited balance-sheets for last five years and that net-worth certificate produced from a Chartered Accountant for financial year, did not satisfy requirement - Aggrieved by allotment of work to respondent No. 2 appellant filed writ petition before HC, which was dismissed holding that respondent No.2 was eligible to offer a bid in as much as it had substantially complied with requirement - Whether allotment of work to respondent No. 2 by respondent No. 1 was sustainable - Held, nature of work executed by respondent No.2, satisfied requirement of tender notice was in no way irrational or absurd because certificate relied upon by respondent No.2 sufficiently demonstrated that respondent No.2 had designed, and executed an integrated water supply scheme which included raw water transmission from intake wells and transmission of treated clear water from WTP including providing, supplying and laying of pipelines, construction of E.S.R.s, Sumps, Pump houses and providing erecting pumping machinery - It was also noteworthy that in matter of evaluation of bids and determination of eligibility of bidders Municipal Council had advantage of aid & advice of an empanelled consultant, a technical hand, who could well appreciate significance of tender condition regarding bidder executing single integrated water supply scheme and fulfilling that condition of tender by reference to work undertaken by them, therefore, SC, found no reason to interfere with view taken by HC, of allotment of work made in favour of respondent No.2 - Appeal dismissed.
