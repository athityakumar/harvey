Union Of India & Ors. v Madhu E.V. & Anr

Supreme Court of India

26 April 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, H.L. Gokhale

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 199; (2012) 5 SCC 474; 2012 (4) AWC 3451; 2012 (133) FLR 867; JT 2012 (4) SC 555; 2012 (2) KLT 558; 2012 (5) MLJ(SC) 885; 2012(5) SCALE 11; [2012] 5 S.C.R. 470; 2012 (3) SLR 795; 2012 (2) UPLBEC 1251

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces

Summary: Defence & Security Forces - Border Security Force Rules, 1969, r. 19 - Central Civil Services (Pension) Rules, 1972, r. 49(2) - Pensionary benefits - Challenged - Respondents, Constables in the BSF, tendered resignation on completion of 10 years service - Their resignation was accepted by the Commandant - However, claim of respondents regarding pensionary benefits were denied on the ground that no pensionary benefits were admissible to those who have proceeded on resignation u/r. 19 of 1969 Rules - Respondents challenged said order by writ petitions - Petitioners contested said writ petitions contending that respondents were governed by the 1972 Rules and as per these rules the minimum qualifying service for pension was 20 years and, therefore, they were not entitled to any pension - Single Judge held that when the respondents were allowed to resign with pensionary benefits u/r. 19 of 1969 Rules, then their claim for pension must be worked out u/r. 49(2)(b) of 1972 Rules - On appeal, DB upheld the order of the Single Judge - Hence, the instant appeals - Whether order of Single Judge as affirmed by the DB could be upheld - Held, the 1972 Rules do not provide that a person who has resigned before completing 20 years of service was entitled to the pensionary benefits - R. 49 of Rules only prescribes the procedure for calculation and quantification of pension amount and not the minimum qualifying service - In the instant case, the respondents have resigned from BSF service immediately after completion of 10 years service and, therefore, they were not entitled to any pensionary benefits - The view taken by the Single Judge as affirmed by the DB cannot be upheld and have to be set aside - Appeals allowed.
