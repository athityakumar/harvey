Purno Agitok Sangma v Pranab Mukherjee

Supreme Court of India

11 December 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

J. Chelameswar

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 535; (2013) 2 SCC 239; JT 2013 (1) SC 1; 2012(12) SCALE 194; [2012] 11 S.C.R. 585

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Keywords: Office Of Profit Under, Parliament (Prevention of Disqualification) Act, 1959, House Of The People, Presidential and Vice-Presidential Elections Act, 1952, Code of Civil Procedure, 1859, Jharkhand Area Autonomous Council Act, 1994, Indian Statistical Institute Act, 1959, Karnataka Legislature (Prevention of Disqualification) Act, 1956

Summary: Constitution - Election - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 101,102,103,14,145,191,58,71,73,84, - Presidential and Vice-Presidential Elections Act, 1952, ss. 14,5 - Representation of the People Act, 1951, ss. 10,87 - Parliament (Prevention of Disqualification) Act, 1959, s. 3 - Nomination - Office of profit - Disqualification - Sustainability - Petitioner filed election petition challenging election of respondent as President of India and for disqualification of his election as President on ground that respondent was not eligible to contest in view of provision of art. 58 of Constitution as he did not resign from 2 posts of Office of profit which he was holding prior to filing of nomination papers - Returning officer had rejected petitioners objection and accepted nomination papers of respondent - Aggrieved petitioner questioning election of respondent as President of India u/art. 71 of Constitution r/w O. 31 of Rules and r. 13 - SC, holding that petition deserved hearing as contemplated by r. 20 of O. 31 listed matter for hearing - Petitioner contended that respondent was holding office of Chairman of Council of Indian Statistical Institute, and Respondent was also Leader of House in Lok Sabha which had been declared as an office of profit as such election of respondent as President of India, was liable to be declared as void - Respondent pleaded that on date of filing of nominations, he was neither holding Office of Chairman of Institute nor was he Leader of House in Lok Sabha, as he had tendered his resignation on 20-6-2012, as such petition did not deserve a regular hearing, as contemplated in r. 20 of O. XXXIX of Rules, and was liable to be dismissed - Whether petition deserved regular hearing as contemplated in r. 20 of O. XXXIX of Rules and whether petitioner had made out case for declaring election of respondent as President of India as void - Held, issue was not whether petitioner would eventually be able to establish his case or not but issue was whether petitioner was entitled to a rational procedure of law to establish his case - Stake in case for parties was enormous, nothing but Presidency of this country, Constitution creates only one forum for adjudication of such disputes, all other avenues were closed by holding that petition does not deserve a regular hearing contemplated u/r. 20 - Court would not be consistent with requirement that justice must not only be done but it must also appear to had been done - Adjudication of rights of parties under Anglo-Saxon jurisprudence, which we follow, required establishment of relevant facts which constitute cause of action necessary for party claiming a relief from court, such facts were to be established by adducing evidence either oral or documentary - Recognizing possibility party making an assertion of fact may not have within its control, all evidence necessary for proving such a fact, courts of civil judicature were empowered to order discovery, inspection, production etc. of documents and also summon persons in whose custody such relevant documents were available but such empowerment was a part of a rational procedure designed to serve ends of justice - Hence accepting statement of respondent that he did not derive any pecuniary benefit by virtue of his having had been Chairman of Statistical Institute without permitting petitioner to test correctness of that statement by cross-examining respondent or confronting respondent with such documents which petitioner might discover if such a discovery was permitted would be a denial of equality of law to petitioner guaranteed u/art. 14 of Constitution, hence, Judge did not subscribe to view that election petition does not deserve regular hearing - Order accordingly.
