Head Master, Lawrence School Lovedale v Jayanthi Raghu & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

16 March 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 99; (2012) 4 SCC 793; AIR 2012 SC 1571; 2012(3) ALL MR 422; 2012 (133) FLR 359; JT 2012 (3) SC 282; 2012 (4) MLJ(SC) 128; 2012(3) SCALE 491; [2012] 2 S.C.R. 492; 2012 (3) SLR 799; 2012 (2) UPLBEC 910

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Punjab Civil Services (Punishment and Appeal) Rules, 1952, Punjab Educational Service (Provincialised Cadre) Class III Rules, 1961, Madhya Pradesh Government Servants' General Conditions of Service Rules, 1961, Punjab Civil Services (Judicial Branch) Rules, 1951, U. P. Co-operative Societies Employees Service Regulations, 1975, Madhya Pradesh Judicial Service (Classification, Recruitment and Conditions of Services) Rules, 1955, Rules of Lawrence School, Lovedale (Nilgiris)

Summary: Service - Rules of Lawrence School, Lovedale, r. 4.9 - Probation - Confirmation - Termination - Sustainability - Respondent no. 1 was appointed on post of a Mistress and was on probation for a period of two years which extended for another one year - Appellants terminated first respondent for some allegations against him - Termination was challenged by Respondent no. 1 before HC, HC held that order of termination, being stigmatic in nature and had been passed without an enquiry warranted quashment was dislodged - DB held that order of termination did not cast any stigma, but concurred with ultimate conclusion on base that she was a confirmed employee and hence, holding of disciplinary enquiry before passing an order of termination was imperative - Whether termination of First respondent without holding enquiry was sustainable - Held, language employed u/r. 4.9 was scrutinised, it can safely be concluded that entitlement to continue till age of superannuation, i.e., 55 years, was not absolute - Power and right to remove was not obliterated, status of confirmation had to be earned and conferred, had rule making authority intended that there would be automatic confirmation, r. 4.9 would have been couched in a different language, that being not so, wider interpretation cannot be placed on Rule to infer that probationer gets status of a deemed confirmed employee after expiry of three years of probationary period as that would defeat basic purpose and intent of Rule which clearly postulates "if confirmed" - An order of confirmation was required to be passed, DB had clearly flawed by associating words 'if confirmed' with entitlement of age of superannuation without appreciating that use of said words was a fundamental qualifier negatives deemed confirmation - Appeal allowed.
