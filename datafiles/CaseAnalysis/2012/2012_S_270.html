Pathan Hussain Basha v State of Andhra Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

16 August 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Swatanter Kumar, Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 382; (2012) 8 SCC 594; AIR 2012 SC 3205; 2012 CRLJ 4230; 2012 (4) RCR(Criminal) 202; [2012] 7 S.C.R. 290

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure; Women & Children

Keywords: Woman

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Women & Children - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 304-B, 498-A - Dowry harassment - Dowry death - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant (accused) allegedly harassed his wife (deceased) and subjected her to cruelty - Appellant's wife unable to bear cruelty to which she was subjected by appellant and his family members, committed suicide by hanging herself in house of appellant - Appellant and other accused allegedly committed offences u/ss. 304-B and 498-A of IPC - Trial Court convicted appellant and other accused and sentenced to undergo life imprisonment - Aggrieved appellant filed an appeal before HC, which was allowed in part acquitting accused no. 3 and confirmed conviction of appellant and another - Hence, instant appeals - Whether impugned conviction of appellants was justified - Held, expression 'soon before her death' could not be given a restricted or a narrower meaning - They should be understood in their plain language and with reference to their meaning in common parlance - Once the prosecution proves its case with regard to basic ingredients of s. 304-B of IPC, Court would presume by deemed fiction of law that the husband or the relatives complained of, has caused her death - Such a presumption could be drawn by Court keeping in view the evidence produced by prosecution in support of substantive charge u/s. 304-B of IPC - In instant case, there was no dispute to the fact that deceased died of hanging - LW13 (Doctor), who performed the post-mortem over body of deceased, stated that cause of death was to be asphyxia, as a result of hanging - LW7 stated on oath that he was present at the time of giving of dowry to appellant by the family of deceased - LW7 confirmed the fact that Rs.15,000/- was given at the time of marriage and Rs.10,000/- was to be given within some time, which the father of deceased failed to provide - According to LW7, accused persons used to harass the deceased primarily for non-payment of amount of dowry, as a result of which, she was forced to commit suicide - Statement of LW1 (father of deceased) was corroborated by LW3 and LW7 - Period intervening between the marriage and death of deceased was very small - Appellant and deceased were married in year 2002 and deceased committed suicide by hanging on 15.2.2003 - It was expected of accused to explain as to how and why his wife died, as well as his conduct immediately prior and subsequent to the death of deceased - Maintaining silence could not be equated to discharge of onus by accused - In instant case, prosecution by reliable and cogent evidence has established the guilt of accused - Impugned quantum of sentence was reduced to 10 years - Appeals partly allowed.
