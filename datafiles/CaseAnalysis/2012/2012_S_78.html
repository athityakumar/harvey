Ash Mohammad v Shiv Raj Singh @ Lalla Babu & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

20 September 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 301; (2012) 9 SCC 446; 2012 ALL MR (Cri) 3756; 2012 CRLJ 4670; 2012 (4) JCC 2909; JT 2012 (9) SC 155; 2013 (1) OLR 30; 2013 (1) RCR(Criminal) 277B; 2012(9) SCALE 165; [2012] 7 S.C.R. 584

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Gangster Act, United Province Special Power Act, 1936

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 364, 365, 506 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 439 - Abduction - Bail - Challenged -Respondent (accused) allegedly committed offences u/ss. 364, 365, 506 of IPC - Appellant (complainant-informant) had lodged a FIR against accused for said alleged offences - Thereafter, accused filed a bail application before Trial Court, which was dismissed - Aggrieved accused filed bail application u/s. 439 of CrPC before HC, which was allowed granting bail to accused - Hence instant petition for cancellation of bail - Appellant contended that HC did not considered the statement recorded u/s. 164 of CrPC, gravity of offences and criminal antecedents of accused - Whether bail granted to accused by impugned order of HC was justified - Held, affidavit by competent authority of the prosecution had brought to the notice of HC about cases pending against accused - HC recorded the submission of complainant that accused was involved in 52 cases - From the perusal of said affidavit it was perceptible that it was categorically stated that accused was a history-sheeter; that he was the pivotal force in getting kidnapping done; that victim was in captivity for 8 days - Role of accused was clearly stated - HC ignored the criminal antecedents of accused and what has weighed with the HC was that accused had spent 7 months in custody - That might be one of the factors but that could not be the whole and sole factor in every case - Period of custody was a relevant factor but simultaneously the totality of circumstances and criminal antecedents were to be weighed in the scale of collective cry and desire - In instant case, victim was kidnapped under threat, confined and abused - Sole reason for kidnapping was because the victim had shown some courage to speak against the accused - That might be the purpose for sustaining of authority in the area by accused and his criminal antecedents, speak eloquently in that regard - Thus, criminal antecedents of accused could not have totally ignored - HC failed to consider relevant material factors which made the impugned order perverse - Impugned order of HC was set aside - Appeal allowed.
