Mukut Bihari & Anr. v State Of Rajasthan

Supreme Court of India

25 May 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 146; (2012) 11 SCC 642; AIR 2012 SC 2270; 2012 ALL MR (Cri) 2421; 2012 CRLJ 3370; JT 2012 (5) SC 517; 2012 (3) MLJ(Crl) 716; 2012 (3) RCR(Criminal) 980; 2012(5) SCALE 651; [2012] 6 S.C.R. 710

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 7, 13, 20 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 120-B - Demand and acceptance of illegal gratification - Conviction - Challenged - Appellant were convicted for the offences punishable u/ss. 7 and 13(1)(d) r/w s. 13(2) of the Act and u/s. 120-B of IPC - Appeal filed against conviction before HC was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Held, there were concurrent finding of facts that appellant asked for bribe - Acceptance had duly been corroborated by PW.3 - Trap stood proved by the depositions of witnesses - All the witnesses narrated fully how the trap was conducted from the very beginning till the seizure of the tainted money including the making of seizure memos - There was no contradiction in the deposition of the witnesses - Their version was without any embellishment and improvement - Further, courts below considered the facts properly and appreciated the evidence in correct perspective and then reached the conclusion that the charges stood fully proved against the appellants - Explanation furnished by the appellants that they had falsely been enroped due to enmity could not be proved for the reason that no evidence was brought on record indicating any previous enmity between the complainant and the appellants - Conviction upheld - Appeal dismissed.
