Sanjeev Kumar Samrat v National Insurance Company Limited and others

Supreme Court of India

11 December 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 533; (2014) 14 SCC 243; 2012 (4) ACC 334; 2013 ACJ 1; AIR 2013 SC 1125; 2013 (2) ALD(SC) 105; 2013(1) ALL MR 402; 2013 (97) ALR 459; 2013 (2) AWC 1260; 2012 (4) CLT(SC) 597; JT 2012 (12) SC 350; 2013 (1) KLT 41; 2013 (1) LLJ 513; 2013 (1) RCR(Civil) 877; 2013 (1) RLW 366; 2012(12) SCALE 77; [2012] 13 S.C.R. 174

Case Digest

Subject: Insurance

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Insurance - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, s. 146, 147, 149, 166, 167 - Workmen's Compensation Act, 1923 - Compensation - Owners Liability - Insurer - Sustainability - Deceased-Labourers and owner of goods were travelling in truck carrying iron rod and cement when they met with accident and sustained injuries and eventually succumbed - Legal Heirs of deceased filed separate petitions u/s. 166 of Act for compensation before Tribunal - Tribunal held that legal representatives of labourers were covered as per insurance policy, as policy covered six employees and fixed liability on insurer and as far as legal representative of owner of goods was concerned, tribunal treated him as owner of goods who was travelling along with goods and accordingly saddled liability on respondent No. 3 - Aggrieved insurer preferred appeal before HC, who concurred with finding recorded by tribunal that deceased-owner of goods was travelling along with goods, therefore, insurer was liable to pay compensation to his legal representatives - Hence instant appeal - Whether insurer was obliged under law to indemnify owner of a goods vehicle when employees engaged by hirer of vehicle travel with owner of goods on foundation that they should be treated as 'employees' covered under policy issued - Held, contextual reading of provision, schematic analysis of Act and 1923 Act, it was quite limpid that statutory policy only covers employees of insured, either employed or engaged by him in a goods carriage - It does not cover any other kind of employee and therefore, someone who travels not being an authorised agent in place of owner of goods, and claims to be an employee of owner of goods, could not be covered by statutory policy and to hold otherwise would tantamount to causing violence to language employed in Statute, therefore, SC, concluded that insurer would not be liable to indemnify insured - Appeals dismissed.
