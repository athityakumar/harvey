Bhupendra Nath Hazarika and another v State Of Assam And Others

Supreme Court of India

30 November 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 501; (2013) 2 SCC 516; AIR 2013 SC 234; 2013 (136) FLR 161; JT 2012 (12) SC 44; 2012(11) SCALE 469; [2012] 12 S.C.R. 587; 2013 (1) SLR 465

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces; Service

Keywords: U.P. Civil Service (Judicial Branch) Rules, 1951, Maharashtra Service of Engineers (Regulation of Seniority and Preparation and Revision of Seniority Lists for Specified Period) Rules, 1982, Bihar Superior Judicial Service Rules, 1946, Assam Administrative Tribunals Act, 1977, Punjab Food and Supplies Department (State Service Class II) Rules, 1966, Assam Police Service Rules, 1966

Summary: Service - Assam Police Service Rules, 1966, r. 5(1) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Appointment - Challenged - In these appeals, the challenge was to judgment passed by DB of HC whereby stamp of approval was given to the judgment and order passed by single judge whereunder single Judge had maintained the order passed by Tribunal and dismissed petitions which were preferred directly for issuance of mandamus commanding the respondent authorities to consider the previous services rendered by the petitioners therein prior to their appointments in Police Service (Junior Grade) in the year 1993 and to determine their inter se seniority in the promotional cadre accordingly and further disposed of petition preferred by Superintendent of Police for quashing of the appointment to the promotional post of the private respondents therein on the foundation that they had been promoted in violation of the provisions of Rules - Whether selection in excess of the quota provided in the Rule was sustainable - Held, on scanning of r. 5(1) of Rules, it was evident that various methods had been stipulated for recruitment - In the case at hand, the direct recruits were recruited by way of competitive examination conducted by the Commission - Special batch was selected u/r. 5(1)(c) of Rules - In that context, the proviso to r. 5(1) of Rules was significant - It clearly laid postulate that the number of posts filled up under cl. (c) should not, at any time, exceed five per cent of the total number of posts in the cadre and one post in any particular year - There was requisition for 20 posts to be filled up by special drive - On query being made during hearing, it was fairly conceded before HC that five per cent in the cadre could not have exceeded four posts - Thus, there was selection in excess of the quota provided in the Rule and nothing had been shown to justify the departure since nothing really could have been demonstrated as the commission had already recommended the names of the candidates meant for direct recruits - HC referred to r. 18 of Rules which dealt with seniority - Two facets which emerged from the scanning of Rule were that the seniority of member of the service was to be determined on the basis of the date of appointment to the service and the seniority had to follow particular order as was stipulated - Other significant aspect was that power was conferred on the Governor to consider the previous service of incumbent and fix deemed date of appointment for the purpose of seniority by adopting specific method - As far as the first part was concerned, Tribunal as well as the HC had not accepted the stipulation that in instant case the seniority should be determined on the basis of the date of appointment as the same was made in flagrant violation of the rules and HC had already concurred with the same - As far as the computation of the previous service was concerned, single Judge as well as the DB, after adequate ratiocination, had expressed the view that the appointments had been made in contravention of the rules, question of conferment of the benefit under the second proviso to r. 18(1) did not arise - Said conclusion was absolutely defensible for the simon pure reason when the infrastructure was founded on total illegal edifice, endeavour to put forth claim for counting the previous service to build pyramid was bound to founder - Another specious contention was urged that power was vested with the Governor to dispense with or relax any rule and in the case at hand, it should be treated that the authority by its conduct had relaxed the rules - It was appropriate to refer to r. 23 of Rules - As was observed by single Judge which was accepted by the DB, there was no decision to relax the rules in favour of the special batch recruits - That apart, whenever there had to be relaxation about the operation of any of the rules, regard had to be given to the test of causation of undue hardship in any particular case - That apart, authority was required to record satisfaction while dispensing or relaxing the requirements of any rule to such extent and subject to such conditions as he might consider necessary for dealing with the case in just and equitable manner - Language of the Rule really casted number of conditions - It provided guidance - It could not be exercised in arbitrary manner so as to dispense with the procedure of selection in entirety in respect of particular class, for it had to be strictly construed and there had to be apposite foundation for exercise of such power - It was to be borne in mind that if particular rule empowered the authority to throw all the rules overboard in all possibility, it might not withstand close scrutiny of art. 14 of Constitution - Be that it might, no decision was taken to relax the rules and, the concept of deemed relaxation was not attracted and, therefore, the relief claimed by the special batch recruited had no legs to stand upon - From the analysis, there could be no scintilla of doubt that the selection of the special batch recruited was totally de hors the Rules - Appeals dismissed.
