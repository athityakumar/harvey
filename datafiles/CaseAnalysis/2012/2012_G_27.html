Tara Chand and others v Gram Panchayat Jhupa Khurd and others

Supreme Court of India

06 November 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 415; (2012) 13 SCC 269; JT 2012 (11) SC 209; 2013 (1) RCR(Civil) 972; 2013 (118) RD 326; 2012(11) SCALE 21; [2012] 10 S.C.R. 974

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property

Keywords: Punjab Occupancy Tenants (Vesting of Proprietary Rights) Act, 1952

Summary: Administrative - Land & Property - Punjab Tenancy Act, 1887 - Punjab Village Common Lands (Regulation) Act, 1961, ss. 4(3),7,8 - Tenancy Act 1887, s. 10 - Occupancy Rights - Joint Owners - Uninterrupted possession - Dismissed - Sustainability - Appellants/their predecessors-in-interest had been in cultivatory possession of the land in dispute and were shown as co-sharers in revenue records - Appellants had filed a suit before Assistant Collector for declaration of their occupancy rights u/ss. 5,8 of Act - Suit was contested by State and Gram Panchayat and was dismissed - Appellants filed appeal before District Collector which was allowed and matter was remanded back to be decided afresh - Assistant Collector allowed suit after remand and held that appellants were in uninterrupted possession of land cultivating, paying nominal rent as such provisions of s. 7 of 1961, Act was not attracted and appellants were entitled for declaration as occupants - Appeal before DC, was allowed on ground that predecessors-in-interest of appellants, were in possession of the land which was always shown as 'shamilat deh', and all revenue records showed status of appellants/their predecessors-in-interest as co-sharers, owing to which, they could not be termed as tenants - Appeal by appellants-predecessors-in-interest before Divisional Commissioner was allowed holding that as appellants had been in cultivatory possession for more than 12 years, as per s. 4(3)(ii) of 1961, Act, now making any distinction between a tenant or co-owner of 'shamilat deh' and therefore, right of occupancy would be available to tenants, as well as to co-sharers - Revision before Financial Commissioner was allowed in favor of respondents holding that it was a settled legal proposition that occupancy rights could not be acquired in shamilat deh by a joint owner - Appellant challenged judgment before HC, which was dismissed on ground that appellants had been joint-owners prior to 1953 and till date, revenue record depicts them as joint-owners, s. 10 of Tenancy Act puts an embargo on joint owners to claim occupancy rights - Hence present appeals - Whether appellants were entitled for occupancy rights certificate - Held, provisions u/s. 10 of Tenancy Act, do not attract and thus, facts herein become distinguishable, however, HC found them non-suited on anvil of s. 10 of Tenancy Act, observing that expression 'any person', contained in s. 8, does not include a joint-owner, it had been admitted by parties that appellants and their ancestors were joint owners/co-sharers in shamilat deh from a period prior to even 1935-36 - S. 10 of Tenancy Act puts a complete embargo on joint-owner to claim occupancy rights, there was no agreement between appellants and Gram Panchayat creating any tenancy in their favour - Granting relief to appellants would amount to ignoring existence of s. 10 itself and it would be against all norms of interpretation which required that statutory provisions must be interpreted in such a manner as not to render any of its provision otiose unless there were compelling reasons for court to resort to that extreme contingent - SC, did not see any cogent reason to interfere with well-reasoned judgment of HC - Appeal dismissed.
