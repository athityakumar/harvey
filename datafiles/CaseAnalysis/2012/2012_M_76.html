Haryana State Industrial Development Corporation Limited v Mawasi and others Etc

Supreme Court of India

02 July 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 203; (2012) 7 SCC 200; AIR 2012 SC 3874; 2012 (95) ALR 229; 2012 (4) AWC 3843; 2012 (3) CLT(SC) 38; 2012 (4) CTC 554; 2012 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 2544; 2013 (118) RD 122; 2012(6) SCALE 60; [2012] 6 S.C.R. 237

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 47 r. 1 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 137 - Review petition - Challenged - Petitioner has filed petitions for review of judgment dt. 17-8-2010 passed in Civil Appeal No. 6515 of 2009 whereby the appeals filed by it against the judgments of the Single Judge were dismissed, those filed by the landowners were allowed and a direction was given for payment of compensation at the rate of Rs. 20 lakhs per acre with all statutory benefits - Respondent contended that petitioner cannot seek review of judgment dt. 17-8-2010 on the pretext of discovery of facts relating to composition of the 2 companies because no evidence was adduced before the Reference Court to prove that the sale deed 'A' was not a bona fide transaction or that vendee had paid exorbitant price for extraneous reasons - Respondent further contended that after dismissal of Review Petition Nos. 2107-2108 of 2010, the petitioner cannot revive its prayer because there was total absence of diligence on the part of its officers - Whether the petitioner's prayer for review should be entertained by ignoring the dismissal of similar petitions by SC vide order dt. 13-1-2011 - Held, a careful reading of order dt. 13-1-2011 shows that in Review Petition Nos. 2107-2108 of 2010, the petitioner had sought reconsideration of judgment dt. 17-8-2010 on the premise that the vendor and the vendee had common management and that the price mentioned in the sale deed 'A' had been manipulated with an oblique motive - The Court declined to entertain said plea by observing that the petitioner had not produced any material to substantiate its assertion - Along with the present batch of review petitions, the petitioner has placed on record the search reports prepared by 'X', Certificate of Incorporation, Memorandum of Association and Articles of Association of 'Y', mutations showing the purchase of land by 'Y' vide sale deeds dt. 16-8-1993 and 18-8-1993, annual return of 'Z', but these documents neither singularly nor collectively support the petitioner's plea that management of the 2 companies, i.e., the vendor and the vendee, was under the control of the same set of persons or that the vendee had paid unusually high price with some oblique motive - Petitioner has not offered any explanation as to why it did not lead any evidence before the Reference Court to show that sale deed was not a bona fide transaction and the vendee had paid unusually high price for extraneous reasons - The parties had produced several sale deeds, majority of which revealed that the price of similar parcels of land varied from Rs. 6 to 7 lakhs per acre - A reading of the said sale deeds would have prompted any person of ordinary prudence to make an enquiry as to why 'Z' (vendee) had paid more than Rs.2,42,00,000/- for 12 acres land, which have been purchased by the vendor only a year back at an average price of Rs.6 lakhs per acre - However, the fact of the matter was that neither the advocate for the petitioner nor its officers/officials, who were dealing with the cases made any attempt to lead such evidence - This may be because they were aware of the fact that at least in 2 other cases such parcels of land had been sold in 1993 for more than Rs.13 lakhs and Rs.15 lakhs per acre and in 1996, a sale deed was executed in respect of the land of 'B' village at the rate of Rs.25 lakhs per acre - This omission coupled with the fact that the petitioner's assertion about commonality of the management of 2 companies was ex-facie incorrect leads to an irresistible inference that judgment dt. 17-8-2010 does not suffer from any error apparent on the face of the record warranting its review - In guise of seeking review, the petitioner cannot ask for de novo hearing of the appeals - While deciding the review petitions, SC cannot make roving inquiries into the validity of the transaction involving the sale of land by 'Y' to 'Z' or declare the same to be invalid by assuming that the vendee had paid higher price to take benefit of an anticipated joint venture agreement with a foreign company - Review petitions dismissed.
