Arjun v State Of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

03 May 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 134; (2012) 5 SCC 530; AIR 2012 SC 2181; 2012 (3) CGLJ 408; 2012 CRLJ 2641; JT 2012 (4) SC 447; 2012 (3) MLJ(Crl) 275; 2012 (2) RCR(Criminal) 850; 2012 (4) RLW 3236; 2012(5) SCALE 52; [2012] 5 S.C.R. 661

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Facts:

Appellant/accused allegedly committed an offence punishable u/ss. 302 and 326 - Trial Court convicted appellant for the offence punishable u/ss. 302 and 326 of IPC - Aggrieved by said order appellant filed appeal before HC - HC affirmed the conviction passed by Trial Court - Hence instant appeal

Issues/Contentions:

Whether right of private defence was available to appellant

Held:

Mere fact that other 7accused were acquitted or that some of the prosecution witnesses were also convicted would not be sufficient to hold that appellant was not aggressor - There was nothing to show that the deceased, his wife, or his son or others had attacked appellant, nor surrounding circumstances would indicate that there was a reasonable apprehension that death or grievous hurt was likely to be caused to appellant by them or others - Therefore, plea of private defence was rejected - However, there was no premeditation and the act was committed in a heat of passion and that the appellant had not taken any undue advantage or acted in a cruel manner and that there was a fight between the parties, which would come under exception 4 to s.300 of IPC - Hence, it was just and proper to alter conviction from s. 302 of IPC to s. 304 part I of IPC

Result:

Appeal disposed of.
