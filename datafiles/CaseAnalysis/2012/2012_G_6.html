N.C. Das v Gauhati High Court Thr. Registrar & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

11 January 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, H.L. Gokhale

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 11; (2012) 2 SCC 321; 2012 (132) FLR 853; JT 2012 (4) SC 52; 2012 (7) MLJ(SC) 89; 2012(1) SCALE 288; [2012] 1 S.C.R. 128; 2012 (3) SLR 1

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges

Keywords: Tripura Judicial Service Rules, 2003, Tripura Judicial Service Rules, 1974

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Tripura Judicial Service Rules, 2003 - Promotion - Judicial Officer - Superannuation - Evaluation - Validity - Petitioner holding post of Civil Judge prayed for diverse reliefs seeking direction to HC to incorporate 'Court suitability test' in Rules and consider him for promotion to post of Grade I - During pendency of Writ Petition, petitioner retired from service - Petitioner filed IA praying to quash of Memo dt. 7th June 2005 issued by HC and for consideration of petitioner for benefits of Assured Career Progress in accordance with recommendations of Shetty Commission Report - Petitioner contended that he was wrongly denied promotion although his juniors were accorded promotion - Petitioners grievance was that he was made to retire on attaining age of 58 years although superannuation age stood enhanced to 60 years - Whether HC ought to have considered extension of petitioner's service under the Rules - Held, cl. (B) of amended r. 20 left no manner of doubt that HC was empowered to assess and evaluate record of a judicial officer for continued utility in service upto 60 years - Cl. (B) had overriding effect over cl. (A) of r. 20 - Mode and manner of assessment and evaluation of potential of continued utility was prescribed in r. 20(B)(I) of the Rules - No legal flaw was pointed out to the exercise undertaken by HC in respect of assessment and evaluation of petitioner's service for continued utility in service upto 60 years - Hence, petitioner was not entitled to relief claimed - Petition dismissed.
