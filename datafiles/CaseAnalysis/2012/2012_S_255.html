Abuzar Hossain @ Gulam Hossain v State Of West Bengal

Supreme Court of India

10 October 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, Anil R. Dave, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 345; (2012) 10 SCC 489; AIR 2013 SC 1020; 2013 (1) CalLT 60; 2012 (4) JCC 2725; JT 2012 (10) SC 453; 2012 (4) MLJ(Crl) 334; 2012 (4) RCR(Criminal) 796; 2012(10) SCALE 101; [2012] 9 S.C.R. 244

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Uttar Pradesh Children Act, 1951, United Nations Standard Minimum Rules, West Bengal Children Act, 1959, Bihar Children Act, 1970

Summary: Lodha J

(A) Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Juvenile Justice Act, 1986 - Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act, 2000, ss. 2(k),3 , 49, 68(1), 7A - Juvenile Plea - Appellate stage - Maintainability - Conviction - Sustainability - Petitioner-accused was convicted by Trial Court, which was concurred by Lower Appellate Court and HC - Appeal was filed before SC, wherein petitioner had contended that at time of offence petitioner was a minor as such Conviction passed by Trial Court and concurred by appellate courts was not sustainable - Juvenile plea was raised for first time before SC, as such matter was referred to larger bench - Hence instant petition - Whether Juvenile plea raised by petitioner was maintainable at appellate stage and whether conviction passed by Trial Court was sustainable - Held, claim of juvenility may be raised at any stage even after final disposal of case, it may be raised for first time before this Court as well after final disposal of case - Delay in raising claim of juvenility could not be a ground for rejection of such claim, claim of juvenility could be raised in appeal even if not pressed before trial court and could be raised for first time before this Court though not pressed before trial court and in appeal court - Claim with regard to juvenility after conviction, claimant must produce some material which may prima facie satisfy court that an inquiry into claim of juvenility was necessary - Court where plea of juvenility was raised for first time should always be guided by objectives of 2000 Act and be alive to position that beneficent and salutary provisions contained in 2000 Act were not defeated by hyper-technical approach and persons who were entitled to get benefits of 2000 Act get such benefits - Claim of juvenility lacking in credibility or frivolous claim of juvenility or patently absurd or inherently improbable claim of juvenility must be rejected by court - Reference answered.

T. S. Thakur, J.

(B) Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Juvenile Justice Act, 1986 - Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act, 2000, ss. 2(k),3 , 49, 68(1), 7A - Juvenile Plea - Appellate stage - Maintainability - Conviction - Sustainability - Whether Juvenile plea raised by petitioner was maintainable at appellate stage and whether conviction passed by Trial Court was sustainable - Held, accused setting up plea of juvenility was unable to produce any one of documents referred to in r. 12(3)(a) (i) to (iii) of Rules, under Act, not necessarily because, he was deliberately withholding such documents from court, but because, he did not have good fortune of ever going to a school from where he could produce a certificate regarding his date of birth, an affidavit of a parent or a sibling or other relative would not ordinarily suffice, to trigger an enquiry into question of juvenility of accused, unless circumstances of case were so glaring that court was left with no option except to record a prima facie satisfaction that a case for directing an enquiry was made out - Approach at stage of directing enquiry had of necessity to be more liberal, lest, there was avoidable miscarriage of justice, suffice it to say that while affidavits may not be generally accepted as a good enough basis for directing an enquiry, that they were not so accepted was not a rule of law but a rule of prudence - Reference answered.
