Medha Kotwal Lele And Others v Union Of India And Others

Supreme Court of India

19 October 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, A.R. Dave, Ranjan Gogoi

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 450; (2013) 1 SCC 297; AIR 2013 SC 93; 2013 (2) ALD(SC) 26; 2012 (4) CLT(SC) 567; 2013 (3) KarLJ 562; 2012 (4) MLJ(Crl) 599; 2013 (1) OLR 481; 2012 (4) RCR(Criminal) 972; [2012] 9 S.C.R. 895; 2013 (8) SLR 303

Case Digest

Subject: Women & Children

Keywords: Woman, Sexual Harassment, Industrial Employment (Standing Orders) Rules, Protection of Women against Sexual Harassment at Workplace Bill, 2010, Mumbai Industrial Employment (Permanent Orders) Rules, 1959, Maharashtra Civil Services (Conduct) Rules, 1974

Summary: Constitution - Women & Children - Practice & Procedure - Women Harassment - Harassment at workplace - Guidelines - Service Rules - Validity - SC, in Vishaka 1997 Indlaw SC 2304 had laid down guideline for prevention and redressal of sexual harassment and their due compliance u/art. 141 of the Constitution of India until such time appropriate legislation was enacted by the Parliament, but many women still struggled to have their most basic rights protected at workplaces - Present public interest litigations were filed principally with a grievance that women continued to be victims of sexual harassment at work places and guidelines in Vishaka were followed in breach in substance and spirit by State functionaries and all other concerned - Respondent-States filed their counters contending that they have made changes in the service conduct rules and that they have implemented guidelines as instructed - Whether States have implemented recommendations given by SC, in Vishaka Case - Held, SC, held that as a largest democracy in world, we have to combat violence against women, and existing laws, if necessary, be revised and appropriate new laws be enacted by Parliament and State Legislatures to protect women from any form of indecency, indignity and disrespect at all places, prevent all forms of violence domestic violence, sexual assault, sexual harassment at workplace, etc. and provide new initiatives for education and advancement of women and girls in all spheres of life and that guidelines in Vishaka 1997 Indlaw SC 2304 should not remain symbolic and following further directions were necessary until legislative enactment on subject was in place - Appeals disposed of.
