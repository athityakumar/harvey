Sri Marcel Martins v M. Printer and others

Supreme Court of India

27 April 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, Gyan Sudha Misra

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 132; (2012) 5 SCC 342; AIR 2012 SC 1987; 2012 (5) ALD(SC) 11; 2012 (93) ALR 228; 2012 (4) AWC 3614; 2012 (3) CTC 416; JT 2012 (4) SC 371; 2013 (2) KarLJ 101; 2012 (5) MLJ(SC) 645; 2013 (169) PLR 168; 2012 (2) RCR(Civil) 922; 2012 (117) RD 281; [2012] 5 S.C.R. 480; [2012] 207 TAXMAN 103

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Family & Personal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Family & Personal - Benami Transactions (Prohibition) Act, 1988, s. 4 - Transfer of Tenancy Rights - Rights of co-owners - Determination - Mother of appellant-defendant and respondents-plaintiffs was holding tenancy rights of property with City Corporation and after her death with consent of all parties, tenancy rights were transferred in favor of appellant - Corporation offered to sell property and demanded sale consideration, appellant raised bank draft and got property transferred in his favor - Respondents filed suit praying for declaration that they were co-owners of property and sought injunction restraining appellant from interfering with their possession - Respondents contended that their father had bequeathed property equally to all his children and that property was purchased out of contribution of their father - Trial Court dismissed suit filed by respondents - Respondents preferred appeal before HC - HC reversed findings recorded by Trial Court and decreed suit holding that appellant had not succeeded in proving that he had paid entire amount of consideration for purchase of property - Aggrieved instant appeal - Whether HC was justified in reversing order of Trial Court - Held, it was reasonable to assume that transfer of rights in favour of appellant was not because others had abandoned their rights but because Corporation required transfer to be in favour of individual presumably to avoid procedural complications in enforcing rights and duties qua in property at a later stage - Other legal representatives of the deceased-tenant neither gave up their tenancy rights in property nor did they give up benefits that would flow to them as legal heirs of deceased tenant consequent upon decision of Corporation to sell property to occupants - Further, parties had made contributions towards sale consideration paid for acquisition of suit property which they would not have done if intention was to concede property in favour of appellant - Parties were closely related to each other which too lent considerable support to case of respondents that appellant held tenancy rights and ostensible title to suit property in a fiduciary capacity vis-a-vis his siblings who had by reason of their contribution and contribution made by their father continued to evince interest in property and its ownership - Reposing confidence and faith in appellant was in facts and circumstances of case not unusual or unnatural especially when possession over suit property continued to be enjoyed by respondent who would in law and on a parity of reasoning be deemed to be holding same for benefit of appellant as much as appellant was holding title to property for benefit of respondents - No question of fact beyond what had been found by HC was essential for answering plea raised by appellant nor was there any failure of justice to call for interference at present stage - Appeal dismissed.
