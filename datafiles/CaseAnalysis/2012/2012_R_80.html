Roy Fernandes v State Of Goa And Others

Supreme Court of India

01 February 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 44; (2012) 3 SCC 221; AIR 2012 SC 1030; 2012 CRLJ 1542; JT 2012 (2) SC 457; 2012 (1) RCR(Criminal) 943; 2012(2) SCALE 68; [2012] 1 S.C.R. 477

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 143, 148, 323, 325 and 302 r/w s. 149 - Murder - Unlawful assembly - Prolonged Trial - Sustainability - Appellant was convicted for offence u/ss. 143, 148, 323, 325 and 302 r/w s. 149 IPC - Prosecution story was that X had raised objection against those engaged in digging pit works, appellant along with 4 accused assaulted persons digging pit and one of the accused stabbed deceased - Prosecution completed investigation, recovered weapon of offence on disclosure of A2 and lodged charge sheet - Trial Court found all accused guilty of offence u/ss. 143, 148, 323, 325 and 302 r/w s. 149 IPC and convicted, aggrieved accused persons appealed, HC upheld conviction of appellant while set aside conviction and sentence awarded to remaining accused giving them benefit of doubt - Aggrieved by order of HC, appellant filed instant petition questioning, whether conviction and sentence awarded to appellant for offences with which he stood charged, was in facts and circumstances of case, legally sustainable - Appellant contended that acquittal of other three accused persons should be sufficient to negative theory of there being an unlawful assembly of which appellant was a member - Further contended that court could reduce sentence to period already undergone by appellant having regard to fact that incident in question had taken place nearly 15 years back and appellant had not only suffered trauma of a prolonged trial and uncertainty but his life had also suffered a setback, in as much 'X' had divorced him - Whether appellant and his companions at all constituted an unlawful assembly and if they did whether murder of deceased by appellant who was member of the unlawful assembly would in facts and circumstances of case attract provisions of s. 149 of IPC so as to make appellant also responsible for the act - Held, appellant was totally unarmed for even according to prosecution witnesses that he had pushed, slapped and boxed those on spot using his bare hands, equally notable circumstance was that neither cycle chain nor belt allegedly carried by two other members of unlawful assembly was put to use by them - Further, there was no evidence to show that appellant knew that in prosecution of common object of preventing putting up of fence around chapel members of assembly or any one of them was likely to commit murder of deceased, indeed no evidence to even show that appellant knew that one co-accuse was carrying a knife with him, which he could use - Conduct of members of assembly especially appellant also did not suggest that they intended to go beyond preventing laying of fence, leave alone committing a heinous offence of murder of a person who had fallen to ground with a simple blow and who was being escorted away from spot by his companions - Courts below fell in error in convicting appellant for murder with aid of s. 149 of IPC - Hence, conviction and sentence awarded to appellant u/s. 302 r/w s. 149 of IPC was set aside and he was acquitted of that charge - However, conviction of appellant for offences punishable u/ss. 323 and 325 of IPC was affirmed and appellant was sentenced to period of imprisonment already undergone by him - Appeal partly allowed.
