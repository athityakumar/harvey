Sukhdev Singh v State of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

13 December 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Swatanter Kumar, Madan B. Lokur

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 565; (2013) 2 SCC 212; AIR 2013 SC 953; 2013 ALL MR (Cri) 764; 2013 CRLJ 841; 2013 (2) JCC (Narcotics) 41; JT 2013 (1) SC 212; 2013 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 546; 2013 (1) MLJ(Crl) 395; 2013 (2) RCR(Criminal) 232; 2012(12) SCALE 699; [2012] 11 S.C.R. 964

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Narcotics Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985, ss. 15, 18, 42(1), (2), 50 - Secrete Information - Contraband - Recovery - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant (accused) was allegedly in possession of contraband and recovery was made from him - Trial Court held the appellant was guilty of offence u/s. 15 of the Act and sentenced him to undergo 10 of RI - Aggrieved appellant filed appeal before HC, which was dismissed - Hence instant appeal - Appellant contended that there was no compliance of s. 42 of the Act - Whether impugned order convicting appellant was justified - Held, ss. 42(2) of the Act was amended vide Act 9 of 2001 w.e.f 2.10.2001 and in instant case, information was received by empowered officer on 4.2.1994 when the un-amended provision was in force - U/s. 42(2) of the Act, empowered officer was obliged to furnish the information to his superior officer forthwith, without any delay - But there could be cases where IO instantaneously, for special reasons to be explained in writing, was not able to reduce the information into writing and send the said information to his superior officers but could do it later and preferably prior to recovery - Compliance of s. 42 of the Act was mandatory and there could not be an escape from its strict compliance - It was clear from statement of PW1 (IO) that he, upon receiving the secret information, neither reduced the same in writing nor communicated to his senior officer about receiving the secret information as required u/s. 42 of the Act - PW1 in his cross-examination, admitted that he had received secret information at about 11:30 am and did not know from where the secret information was received - He was in a jeep and the distance between the house of appellant accused and spot where he was at the time of receiving secret information was merely 6 kilometers, but he reached the house of appellant only at 2 p.m - House of appellant was situated in middle of village in a busy locality, and yet appellant did not call anybody from the neighbourhood at the time of effecting recovery - IO had more than sufficient time at his disposal to comply with provisions of s. 42 of the Act- There was not an iota of evidence, either in statement of PW1 or in any other documentary form, to show what IO was doing for those hours and what prevented him from complying with provisions of s. 42 of the Act - There was patent illegality in case of prosecution and such illegality was incurable - Instant case was of total noncompliance, thus question of substantial compliance would not even arouse for consideration of Court - There was provision like s. 57 which the empowered officer was mandatorily required to comply with - That itself some extent would minimize the purpose and effectiveness of s. 42 of the Act - Director General of Police concerned of all States were directed to instruct directing IOs to duly comply provisions of s. 42 of the Act - Impugned order was set aside - Appeal allowed.
