M/S Topman Exports v Commissioner Of Income Tax, Mumbai

Supreme Court of India

08 February 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Patnaik, S.H. Kapadia, Swatanter Kumar

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 32; (2012) 3 SCC 593; AIR 2012 SC (Supp) 579; 2012 (277) E.L.T. 10; JT 2012 (2) SC 390; 2012(2) SCALE 382; [2012] 4 S.C.R. 684; [2012] 205 TAXMAN 119

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Cash Assistance

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 28(iiid) and 80HHC - A/y 2002-2003 - Deduction - Assessment - Entitlement - Sustainability - Civil Appeal against Judgment and orders of HC holding that entire amount received by an assessee on sale of Duty Entitlement Pass Book [DEPB] represents profit on transfer of DEPB u/s. 28(iiid) of Act - Assesse for A/y 2002-03 claimed deduction of amount u/s. 80 HHC of Act - AO held, that if profit on transfer of export incentives was deducted from profits of assessee, figure would be a loss and there will be no positive income of assessee from its export business and assessee will not be entitled to any deduction under Section 80HHC of the Act - Assessee aggrieved filed appeal before Commissioner of IT, than before Income Tax Appellate Tribunal, but were dismissed - Aggrieved filed appeal in HC - HC answered 2 substantial questions of Law - (A) Whether the Tribunal is justified in holding that the entire amount received on the sale of the DEPB does not represent profits chargeable under Section 28(iiid) of the Income Tax Act, 1961 and that the face value of the DEPB shall be deducted from the sale proceeds - Held, HC held that Tribunal was not justified in holding that entire amount received on sale of DEPB does not represent profits chargeable u/s. 28(iiid) of Act and in holding that face value of DEPB shall be deducted from sale proceeds of DEPB On the second question of law formulated under - (B) Whether the Tribunal is justified in holding that the face value of the Duty Entitlement Passbook is chargeable to tax under Section 28(iiib) at the time of accrual of income i.e. when the application for Duty Entitlement Passbook is filed with the competent authority pursuant to the exports made and that the profits on the sale of Duty Entitlement Passbook representing the excess of the sale proceeds over the face value is liable to be considered under Section 28(iiid) at the time of sale - Held, HC in its judgment did not agree with Tribunal that face value of DEPB is chargeable to tax as income of assessee u/s 28(iiib) of Act and instead held that entirety of sale consideration for transfer of DEPB would fall within purview of s. 28(iiid) of Act - Appellant contended that contention of revenue that DEPB would be income chargeable to tax only on transfer and would be covered under clause (iiid) of s. 28 of Act is not correct - Revenue contended that profit on transfer of DEPB would represent entire sale value realized by assessee on transfer of DEPB - Whether HC was justified in concluding that appellant was not entitled for deduction - Held, provisions of s. 28 that under cl. (iiib) cash assistance received or receivable by any person against exports under any scheme of Government of India is by itself income chargeable to income tax under head 'Profits and Gains of Business or Profession' - DEPB is a kind of assistance given by Government of India to an exporter to pay customs duty on its imports and it is receivable once exports are made and an application is made by exporter for DEPB - Therefore, there is no doubt that DEPB is 'cash assistance' receivable by a person against exports under scheme of Government of India and falls under cl. (iiib) of s. 28 and is chargeable to income tax under head 'Profits and Gains of Business or Profession' even before it is transferred by assessee - High Court, therefore, was not right in coming to conclusion that as assessee did not have export turnover exceeding Rs.10 crores and as assessee did not fulfill conditions set out in third proviso to s. 80HHC (iii), assessee was not entitled to a deduction u/s. 80HHC on amount received on transfer of DEPB - Appeal allowed.
