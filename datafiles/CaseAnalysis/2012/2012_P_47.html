 Purno Agitok Sangma v Pranab Mukherjee

Supreme Court of India

05 December 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, P. Sathasivam, Surinder Singh Nijjar, Ranjan Gogoi, J. Chelameswar

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 514; AIR 2013 SC 372; 2012 (4) CLT(SC) 546; JT 2012 (12) SC 296; 2013 (1) RCR(Civil) 334; 2012(11) SCALE 509

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Election; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Office Of Profit Under, Parliament (Prevention of Disqualification) Act, 1959, House Of The People, Presidential and Vice-Presidential Elections Act, 1952, Bengal Municipal Act, 1932, Parliament (Prevention of Disqualification) Amendment Act, 2006, Leaders and Chief Whips of Recognised Parties and Groups In Parliament (Facilities) Act, 1998, Mysore Town Municipal Act, 1951

Summary: Chief Justice Altamas Kabir

Constitution - Election - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 101,102,103,14,145,191,58,71,73,84, - Presidential and Vice-Presidential Elections Act, 1952, ss. 14,5 - Representation of the People Act, 1951, ss. 10,87 - Parliament (Prevention of Disqualification) Act, 1959, s. 3 - Nomination - Office of profit - Disqualification - Sustainability - Petitioner filed election petition challenging election of respondent as President of India and for disqualification of his election as President on ground that respondent was not eligible to contest in view of provision of art. 58 of Constitution as he did not resign from 2 posts of Office of profit which he was holding prior to filing of nomination papers - Returning officer had rejected petitioners objection and accepted nomination papers of respondent - Petitioner approached Election Commission praying for directions to Returning officer to re-scrutinize nomination papers of respondent - EC, rejected petition as not maintainable before commission, since all dispute relation to Presidential election could be inquired and decided only before SC - Aggrieved petitioner questioning election of respondent as President of India u/art. 71 of Constitution r/w O. 31 of Rules and r. 13 - SC, holding that petition deserved hearing as contemplated by r. 20 of O. 31 listed matter for hearing - Petitioner contended that respondent was holding office of Chairman of Council of Indian Statistical Institute, and Respondent was also Leader of House in Lok Sabha which had been declared as an office of profit as such election of respondent as President of India, was liable to be declared as void - Further contended that original Jurisdiction of SC, provided for in O. XXII of Rules, entire procedure for institution and trial of a suit had been set out, providing for all different stages in respect of a suit governed by CPC - Petition contended that the making of procedure for trial of Election Petitions akin to that of Original Jurisdiction of SC, was a clear indication that matter must be tried as a suit, if u/r. 13 of O. XXXIX, Court consisting of 5 Judges was satisfied at a preliminary inquiry that matter deserved a regular hearing, as contemplated in r. 20 of Order - S. 87 of 1951, Act, laid down procedure for trial of Election Petitions and providing that every Election Petition shall be tried by HC, as nearly as may be, in accordance with procedure applicable under CPC to trial of suits - Respondent pleaded that nomination papers of candidates had been scrutinized by Returning Officer in accordance with provisions of s. 5A of 1952, Act - As per s. 5E (3) RO, after completing all formalities indicated in Sub-Section (3), RO had accepted nomination papers of Respondent as valid, which, gave Respondent right to contest election - SS. 14, 14A of Act, specifically vest jurisdiction to try Election Petitions under 1952, Act with SC, in manner prescribed and any challenge to a Presidential election would have to be in compliance with provisions of O. XXXIX of Rules, 1966, which deals with Election Petitions under Part III of 1952 Act - R. 13 of O. XXXIX of Rules, therefore, becomes applicable and it enjoins that upon presentation of an Election Petition, and same has to be posted before a Bench of Court consisting of five Judges, for preliminary hearing to satisfy itself that the petition deserves a regular hearing, as contemplated in r. 20 - Further pleaded that Chairman of Indian Statistical Institute, was not an office of profit as post was honorary and there was no salary or any other benefit attached to post and holding of post of Leader of House, was normally a Cabinet Minister of Govt. and was certainly not an appointee of Govt. so as to bring him within bar of Cl. (2) of art. 58 of Constitution - Respondent pleaded that on date of filing of nominations, he was neither holding Office of Chairman of Institute nor was he Leader of House in Lok Sabha, as he had tendered his resignation on 20-6-2012, as such petition did not deserve a regular hearing, as contemplated in r. 20 of O. XXXIX of Rules, and was liable to be dismissed - Whether petition deserved regular hearing as contemplated in r. 20 of O. XXXIX of Rules and whether petitioner had made out case for declaring election of respondent as President of India as void - Held, SC, was not inclined to accept that once a person was appointed as Chairman of Institute, Rules and Bye-laws of Society did not permit him to resign from post and that he had to continue in post against his wishes, there was no contractual obligation that once appointed, Chairman would have to continue in such post for full term of office - There was no such compulsion under Rules and Bye-laws of Society either, since holder of post of Chairman of Institute had been excluded from disqualification for contesting Presidential election, by 2006 amendment to s. 3 of 1959, Act - Hence in facts and circumstances of the case, SC, held that Election Petition did not deserve a full and regular hearing as contemplated u/r. 20 of O. XXXIX of Rules, 1966 and also that applicability of s. 141 of CPC for trial of Election Petition was of no avail as it was not required to be incorporated into a proceeding taken u/ O. XXXIX of Rules r/w Part II of Act, 1952, which includes ss. 14 to 20 of 1952, Act and art. 71 of Constitution - Petition disposed of.

Ranjan Gogoi, J.

Constitution - Election - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 101,102,103,14,145,191,58,71,73,84, - Presidential and Vice-Presidential Elections Act, 1952, ss. 14,5 - Representation of the People Act, 1951, ss. 10,87 - Parliament (Prevention of Disqualification) Act, 1959, s. 3 - Nomination - Office of profit - Disqualification - Sustainability - Whether petition deserved regular hearing as contemplated in r. 20 of O. XXXIX of Rules and whether petitioner had made out case for declaring election of respondent as President of India as void - Held, disqualification incurred by a Presidential candidate on account of holding of an office of profit was not removed by provisions of Act, which deals with removal of disqualification for being chosen as, or for being a Member of Parliament, therefore, it was assumed that office of Chairman, ISI was an office of profit and Respondent had held said office on material date(s) consequences adverse to Respondent, in so far as result of election was concerned, were likely to follow - Therefore, be required to be proved by election Petitioner, no conclusion that a regular hearing in present case would be a redundant exercise or an empty formality could be reached so as to dispense with same and terminate Election Petition at stage of its preliminary hearing under O. XXXIX r. 20 - Hence, Election Petition, deserves a regular hearing under O. XXXIX, r. 20 in accordance with what was contained in different provisions of Part III of 1966, Rules - Petition disposed of.

J. Chelameswar, J.

Constitution - Election - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 101,102,103,14,145,191,58,71,73,84, - Presidential and Vice-Presidential Elections Act, 1952, ss. 14,5 - Representation of the People Act, 1951, ss. 10,87 - Parliament (Prevention of Disqualification) Act, 1959, s. 3 - Nomination - Office of profit - Disqualification - Sustainability - Whether petition deserved regular hearing as contemplated in r. 20 of O. XXXIX of Rules and whether petitioner had made out case for declaring election of respondent as President of India as void - Held, inability to agree with the conclusion recorded by the learned Chief Justice that the instant Election Petition does not deserve a regular hearing, shall pronounce my reasons for such disagreement - Petition disposed of.
