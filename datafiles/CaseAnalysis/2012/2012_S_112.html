Lavesh v State (Nct Of Delhi)

Supreme Court of India

31 August 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, Ranjan Gogoi

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 274; (2012) 8 SCC 730; 2012 ALL MR (Cri) 3300; 2012 (192) DLT 352; 2013 (1) G.L.R. 553; 2012 (4) JCC 2330; JT 2012 (8) SC 336; 2012 (3) KLT 876; 2012 (4) RCR(Criminal) 240; 2012(8) SCALE 303; [2012] 7 S.C.R. 469

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 438 - Abetment to suicide - Anticipatory bail - Denied - Challenged - Appellant was the elder brother of the husband of the deceased who has committed suicide - The mother of the deceased lodged a complaint against the family members of the husband of the deceased - The husband and mother-in-law of the deceased were arrested - Appellant moved an application for anticipatory bail - Trial court dismissed said application - Appellant moved an application for anticipatory bail before the HC who also dismissed said application - Hence, the instant appeal - Whether appellant has made out a case for anticipatory bail in terms of s. 438 of the CrPC - Held, when the accused was 'absconding' and declared as a 'proclaimed offender', there was no question of granting anticipatory bail - In the instant case, appellant was not available for interrogation and investigation and declared as 'absconder' - Unless free hand was given to the investigating agency, particularly, in the light of the allegations made against the appellant and his family members, the truth will not surface - Appellant has not made out a case for anticipatory bail - Appeal dismissed.
