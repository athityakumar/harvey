Mano Dutt & Anr v State Of U.P.

Supreme Court of India

29 February 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Swatanter Kumar, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 63; (2012) 4 SCC 79; JT 2012 (2) SC 573; 2012 (2) MLJ(Crl) 836; 2012 (2) RCR(Criminal) 798; 2012(3) SCALE 219; [2012] 3 S.C.R. 686

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 323 and 34, Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, s. 313 - Murder - Self-defence - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellants were convicted for offence u/ss. 323/34 and 302/34 of IPC by Trial court - Initially case u/s. 308 of IPC was registered, but after death of deceased it was altered to 302/34 of IPC - Appellants also had filed complaints against deceased and his relatives contending that fight was started by deceased and his relatives and they had exercised their right of private self-defence, to protect themselves - Trail Court convinced by evidence placed by prosecution, convicted appellants - On appeal HC partially accepted appeal by acquitting accused of offence u/s. 323/34 of IPC - Aggrieved instant appeal - Whether conviction of appellants was sustainable - Held, once plea of self-defence was disbelieved, then statement of co-accused u/s. 313 CrPC could not be of any advantage to co-accused, as prosecution had been able to establish its case beyond any reasonable doubt - In instant case, in chain of events, nowhere did plea of self-defence as sought to be raised by appellant or other accused - Defence had miserably failed to prove any fact or any need for resorting to commission of offence in self-defence - Conviction was fully corroborated by oral evidence and on basis of documentary and ocular evidence - Prosecution was able to prove its case beyond reasonable doubt and had brought home guilt of accused u/s. 302/34 of IPC - Appeal dismissed.
