Dr. Sunil Clifford Daniel v State Of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

14 September 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 294; (2012) 11 SCC 205; 2012 CRLJ 4657; JT 2012 (8) SC 639; 2012 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 3229; 2012 (4) MLJ(Crl) 240; 2012 (4) RCR(Criminal) 349; 2012(8) SCALE 670; [2012] 7 S.C.R. 1100

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 201 and 302 - Murder - Circumstantial evidence - Conviction - Challenged - Prosecution alleged that appellant (Husband) has murdered deceased (Wife) - Trial court convicted appellant u/ss. 302 and 201 of IPC - HC affirmed the order passed by the trial court - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order of the trial court as affirmed by the HC could be upheld - Held, instant case was a case of blind murder and was based entirely on circumstantial evidence, as there was no eye-witness to the said incident - The nature of the injuries mentioned in the post-mortem report makes it crystal clear that the deceased died of strangulation i.e. asphyxia, and she also had several injuries to her head, which could have been caused by a dumb bell, which was one of the materials recovered and found to have blood stains on it - As the appellant had a strained relationship with his wife, he no doubt wanted to get rid of her - Although he has claimed that the petitions for divorce by mutual consent were pending before the court, he has never submitted any documents with respect to this before the court - Thus, inference may be drawn that the appellant did in fact wish to get rid of his wife - As the recoveries of the blood stained gunny bag, dumb bell, tie etc. were made on the basis of the disclosure statement of the appellant himself, the chain of circumstances was therefore, complete - No reason to interfere with the concurrent findings recorded by the courts below - Impugned order of the trial court as affirmed by the HC to be upheld - Appeal dismissed.
