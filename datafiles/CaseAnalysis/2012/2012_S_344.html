Jagroop Singh v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

20 July 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 217; (2012) 11 SCC 768; AIR 2012 SC 2600; 2012 ALL MR (Cri) 2727; 2012 (3) JCC 2137; 2012 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 2576; 2013 (1) RCR(Criminal) 516; 2012(6) SCALE 512; [2012] 7 S.C.R. 91

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 201 and 302 - Murder - Circumstantial Evidence - Conviction - Challenged - Deceased was allegedly murdered by the accused persons - Trial Court, on appreciation of the evidence brought on record, held that the prosecution had been able to prove the case against the accused persons beyond reasonable doubt and accordingly recorded the conviction of the accused persons for the offences punishable u/s. 302 r/w. ss. 34 and 201 of the IPC and imposed the sentence - On appeal, HC affirmed the conviction and confirmed the sentence passed by the Trial court - Hence, the instant appeal - Whether order of the Trial court as affirmed by the HC could be upheld - Held, all the 3 circumstances i.e. last seen with the accused persons, recovery of the weapon and the disclosure statement, have been established by the prosecution complete the chain - Moreover, SC in Sucha Singh and Another v. State of Punjab 2003 INDLAW SC 578 held that prosecution was not required to meet any and every hypothesis put forward by the accused persons - A reasonable doubt was not an imaginary, trivial or merely possible doubt, but a fair doubt based upon reason and common sense - It must grow out of the evidence in the case - If a case was proved perfectly, it was argued that it was artificial; if a case has some inevitable flaws because human beings were prone to err, it was argued that it was too imperfect - The present case was one where there was no trace of doubt that all circumstances complete the chain and singularly lead to the guilt of the accused persons - No infirmity in the judgment of conviction and order of sentence recorded by the Trial Judge which has been affirmed by the HC - Appeal dismissed.
