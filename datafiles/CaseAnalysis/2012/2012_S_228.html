Oma @ Omprakash & Anr v State Of Tamil Nadu

Supreme Court of India

11 December 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 526; (2013) 3 SCC 440; AIR 2013 SC 825; 2013 CRLJ 997; JT 2013 (1) SC 122; 2013 (1) OLR 822; 2013 (2) RCR(Criminal) 176; 2012(12) SCALE 112; [2012] 13 S.C.R. 740; 2013 (1) WLN(SC) 76

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Notions

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, ss. 395, 396 and 397 - Decoity with Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellants and 9 other absconding accused persons entered the house of PW 2 with intention of committing burglary with iron rods in their hands and burgled gold and cash - It was alleged that appellants had strangulated husband of PW 2 with rope and thereby killed him - It was alleged that accused assaulted PW 2, PW 5 and PW 6 - Trial Court convicted accused persons for offences u/ss. 395, 396 and 397 IPC - Trial Court granted life imprisonment u/s. 395 IPC and they were sentenced to death for offence u/s. 396 IPC - Appellants were also sentenced for RI for 7 years u/s. 397 IPC - Aggrieved appellants preferred appeal before HC - HC converted the sentence of death to life imprisonment u/s. 396 IPC and rest of sentence on other heads were confirmed - Hence instant appeal - Whether Courts below were justified in passing the impugned order - Held, Trial Court had primarily guided by some kind of notion and connected them with civilized world and democracy which, should not have at all referred to - Trial Court should remember language of art. 302 IPC and precedents that govern field for imposition of death penalty - In that event, perception might have wrong but it could not said that it was based on some kind of personal philosophy - Thus, view expressed did not sustain concept of law and rather, on contrary, exhibits sanctuary of errors - It can be stated with certitude that in criminal trial, while recording sentence, Trial Court should have guided and governed by established principles and not by personal notions or even ideas of eminent personalities Binding judgments should be Bible of Judge and there should not be any deviation - Trial Court judges were appositely guided and refrain themselves from engaging in innovative creativity or 'borrowed creativity' which has no sanction in Law - Judgment of conviction and order of sentence were set aside - Appeal allowed.
