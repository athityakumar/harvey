Faiza Choudhary v State Of Jammu & Kashmir & Another

Supreme Court of India

06 September 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 273; (2012) 10 SCC 149; AIR 2013 SC 1115; 2012 (95) ALR 867; JT 2012 (10) SC 554; 2013(1) M.P.H.T. 369; 2012(8) SCALE 481; [2012] 7 S.C.R. 528; 2012 (6) SLR 737

Case Digest

Subject: Education

Keywords: J & K Board of Professional Entrance Examination Act 2002, Jammu & Kashmir Reservation Act, 2004

Summary: Education - Jammu & Kashmir Reservation Act, 2004 - Admission - Denied - Challenged - Whether an MBBS seat which fell vacant in the year 2010 could be carried forward to the year 2012 so as to accommodate a candidate who was in the merit list published in the year 2010 - Held, a medical seat has life only in the year it falls that too only till the cut-off date fixed by SC i.e. 30th September in the respective year - Carry forward principle was unknown to the professional courses like medical, engineering, dental etc. - No rule or regulation has been brought to SC knowledge conferring power on the Board to carry forward a vacant seat to a succeeding year - If the Board or the Court indulges in such an exercise, in the absence of any rule or regulation, that will be at the expense of other meritorious candidates waiting for admission in the succeeding years - Hence, a seat which fell vacant in a particular year cannot be carried forward or created in a succeeding year, in the absence of any rule or regulation to that effect - Appeal dismissed.
