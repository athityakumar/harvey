Dr. Subramanian Swamy v Dr. Manmohan Singh And Another

Supreme Court of India

31 January 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 509; (2012) 3 SCC 64; 2012 (4) ALD(SC) 109; 2012 (2) Bom.C.R. 495; 2012 CRLJ 1519; JT 2012 (2) SC 203; 2012 (1) MLJ(Crl) 484; 2012 (2) MLJ(SC) 86; 2012 (1) RCR(Criminal) 720; [2012] 3 S.C.R. 52

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Constitution - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Central Vigilance Commission Act, 2003, s. 8(d) - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 173, 173(2), 190, 196(1A), 197 - Criminal Law Amendment Act, 1952,s. 6, 8(1) - Indian Penal Code, 1860,s. 120B - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947, ss. 5(1)(e), 5(2), 5A, 6(1) - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 11, 13(1)(d), 13(2), 19, 19(1) - Illegal gratification - Sanction to Prosecute - Timeline - Validity - Appellant had been vigorously pursuing in public interest cases alleged involving loss of thousands of crores of rupees to Public Exchequer due to arbitrary and illegal grant of licences at behest of respondent No. 2 - Appellant made details representation to respondent No. 1 to accord sanction for prosecution of respondent No. 2 for offences under Act, alleging that respondent No. 2 had allotted new licences in 2G mobile services on 'first come, first served' basis to novice telecom companies which was in clear violation of Cl. 8 of Guidelines for United Access Services Licence issued by Ministry of Communication and Information Technology vide letter No.10-21/2005-BS.I(Vol. II)/49 dt. 14-12-2005 and, thereby, caused loss of over Rs. 50,000 crores to Govt. and further alleged that appellant pointed out how respondent No. 2 ignored recommendations of TRAI and gave totally unwarranted benefits to two companies and thereby caused loss to Public Exchequer - Appellant did not receive any response from respondent No.1, he sent letter and reiterated his request/demand for grant of sanction to prosecute respondent No. 2 - After 1 year and 4-1/2 months of the first letter written by him, Secretary, Department of Personnel and Training, Ministry of Personnel sent letter dt. 19-3-2010, to appellant mentioning therein that CBI had registered a case on 21-10-2009 against unknown officers of Dept. of Telecommunications (DoT), unknown private persons/companies and others and that issue of grant of sanction for prosecution would arise only after perusal of evidence collected by investigating agency and other material provided to Competent Authority and that it would be premature to consider sanction for prosecution at that stage - Appellant filed Civil Writ Petition before HC, issue of a mandamus to respondent No.1 to pass an order for grant of sanction for prosecution of respondent No. 2 - HC, dismissed petition on ground that it was not permissible to grant or refuse the sanction because that may affect investigation - Hence instant appeal - Appellant contended that respondent No.2 resigned from Council of Ministers but appellant pleaded that issues relating to his right to file a complaint for prosecution of respondent No.2 and grant of sanction within time specified in judgment in Vineet Narain's case should be decided and argued that embargo contained in s. 19(1) of 1988 Act, operates only against taking of cognizance by Court in respect of offences punishable u/ss. 7, 10, 11, 13, 15 committed by a public servant, but there was no bar to filing of a private complaint for prosecution of concerned public servant and grant of sanction by Competent Authority, and that respondent No. 1 was duty bound to take appropriate decision on his representation within time specified - Respondent No. 1 pleaded that Attorney General argued that question of grant of sanction for prosecution of a public servant charged with any of offences enumerated in s. 19(1) arises only at stage when Court decides to take cognizance and any request made prior to that was premature - Whether authority competent to sanction prosecution of a public servant for offences under 1988 Act was required to take an appropriate decision within time - Held, right of private citizen to file a complaint against a corrupt public servant must be equated with his right to access Court in order to set criminal law in motion against a corrupt public official - This right of access, a Constitutional right should not be burdened with unreasonable fetters, when a private citizen approaches a court of law against a corrupt public servant who was highly placed, what was at stake was not only a vindication of personal grievance of that citizen but also question of bringing orderliness in society and maintaining equal balance in rule of law - S. 19 of Act, which bars a Court from taking cognizance of cases of corruption against a public servant u/ss. 7, 10, 11, 13 and 15 of Act, unless Central or State Govt., as case may be, had accorded sanction, virtually imposes fetters on private citizens and also on prosecutors from approaching Court against corrupt public servants, these protections were not available to other citizens - Public servants were treated as a special class of persons enjoying protection so that they could perform their duties without fear and favour and without threats of malicious prosecution - However, said protection against malicious prosecution which was extended in public interest could not become a shield to protect corrupt officials, these provisions being exceptions to equality provision of Art. 14 are analogous to provisions of protective discrimination and these protections must be construed very narrowly, art. 14 must be construed as a guarantee against uncanalized and arbitrary power, therefore, absence of any time limit in granting sanction in s. 19 of Act was not in consonance with requirement of due process of law which had been read into our Constitution - SC, concluded that Parliament should consider Constitutional imperative of art. 14 enshrining rule of law wherein 'due process of law' had been read into by introducing a time limit in s. 19 of Act 1988 for its working in a reasonable manner and proposed few guidelines - Appeal disposed of.
