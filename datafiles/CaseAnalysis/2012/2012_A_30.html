Parshavanath Charitable Trust and another v All India Council for Technical Education and others

Supreme Court of India

13 December 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Swatanter Kumar, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 568; (2013) 3 SCC 385; JT 2013 (1) SC 557; 2013 (3) MahLJ 915; 2013 (3) MPLJ 291; 2012(12) SCALE 219; [2012] 11 S.C.R. 1057; 2013 (2) SLR 110; 2013 (1) UPLBEC 314

Case Digest

Subject: Education; Trusts & Associations

Keywords: Shifting college, New premises

Summary: Education - Trusts & Associations - Shifting college - New premises - Legality - Appellant/College applied for an occupation certificate for shifting college to new premises - College shifted its location to new site without receiving No Objection Certificate from University and State Govt - Appellant received show cause notice on ground that college had shifted to another location without obtaining prior approval of AICTE - Appellant filed petition before HC and the same was allowed During pending proceedings before HC Municipal Corporation had stated that Occupancy Certificate had not been granted to appellant - HC passed order withdrawing approval granted to appellant - Cancellation of approval was challenged by appellant before HC and the same was dismissed - Hence instant appeals - Whether impugned order passed by HC was justified -

Held, both grant/refusal of approval and admission schedule, as afore stated, should be strictly adhered to by all authorities concerned including the AICTE, University, State Govt. and any other authority directly or indirectly connected with grant of approval and admission - No person or authority should have power or jurisdiction to vary Schedule prescribed - While dealing with application for grant of approval to new colleges or additional seats, AICTE should inform applicant within time framed, shortcomings/defects, who, in turn, should remove such shortcomings/defects within time framed - Process of grant of approval has to be transparent and fair - AICTE or University or State Govt. should take disciplinary action against person who commits default in adherence to Schedule and performance of his duties in accordance therewith - Reports submitted by Expert Committee visiting college should be unambiguous and clear, and should bear date and time of inspection and should be sufficiently comprehensive and inspection be conducted in presence of representative of institute - Students of appellant/college should be re-allocated to recognized and affiliated colleges in terms of judgment of HC, and AICTE and concerned University should ensure that academic courses of these students were completed within balance period of academic year in all respects - If extra classes were required to be held, concerned institute, University and AICTE were directed to ensure holding of such extra classes - Even if appellate authority grants approval after 30th April, it would not be operative for current academic year - All colleges which have been granted approval/affiliation by 10th or 30th April, as case might be, should alone be included in brochure/advertisement/website for purpose of admission and none thereafter - Appeals dismissed.
