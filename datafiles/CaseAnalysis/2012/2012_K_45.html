State of Madhya Pradesh and others v Ku. Sandhya Tomar and another

Supreme Court of India

13 December 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 560; (2013) 11 SCC 357; 2013(1) ALL MR 485; 2013 (96) ALR 811; 2013 (3) AWC 2540; 2013 (136) FLR 300; 2013 (3) JabLJ 99; JT 2013 (9) SC 139; 2012(12) SCALE 561; [2012] 11 S.C.R. 839; 2013 (4) SLR 501

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Women & Children

Summary: Service - Temporary appointment - Termination - Permission - Entitlement - Central Govt. introduced scheme for elimination of child labour - In order to implement the said Scheme, a society, namely, the Child Labour Elimination & Rehabilitation Society was formed - In order to appoint the Project Director, respondent No. 1 was selected and appointed temporarily - Respondent No. 1 joined a post in the Panchayat & Rural Development Department, later her services were not required, and she was repatriated to her parent department - However, respondent No. 1 was not permitted to join the Society - Post of Project Director was advertised thus, respondent No. 1 filed a writ petition, challenging the advertisement dated 16-5-2005, claiming her right to join the said post - Single Judge allowed the petition - Aggrieved, on appeal DB dismissed the appeal - Hence instant appeal - Whether respondent No. 1 was entitled for the post of Project Director in the society - Held, it was not the case of respondent No. 1 that she had any lien with respect to the post - Respondent No. 1 voluntarily abandoned her job in the Society and joined another post, in another department - Therefore, her temporary employment in the Society came to an end automatically, she had chosen better employment under the State Govt. as opposed to continuing her employment in the Society on a project - Her employment in the State Govt. was terminated after serving therein for a period of eight months - In such a fact-situation, the Society was not bound to permit Respondent No. 1 to join the post of Project Director - As a consequence thereof, she had no right to challenge the advertisement dt. 16-5-2005 - At the most, if respondent No. 1 was eligible for appointment as per the said advertisement, she could apply for fresh appointment - In case respondent No. 1 felt that she had a right to join the services of the State Govt. and that her service from there was wrongly terminated, she could have challenged the said order which had in fact, never been challenged by her, for reasons best known to her - Impugned order of Court below were set aside - Appeal allowed.
