Sadhupati Nageswara Rao v State Of Andhra Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

03 August 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, Ranjan Gogoi

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 238; (2012) 8 SCC 547; AIR 2012 SC 3242; 2012 ALL MR (Cri) 3034; 2012 CRLJ 4317; 2012 (4) JCC 2682; 2012 (3) RCR(Criminal) 922; 2012(7) SCALE 63; [2012] 6 S.C.R. 1143

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 409 - Criminal breach of trust - Conviction - Challenged - Appellant was the Fair Price Shop dealer of 'X' village and also in-charge dealer of Fair Price Shop at 'Y' - Appellant was entrusted with the task of distribution of rice at free of cost under 'Food For Work Scheme' (FFWS) to the workers on production of coupons, to maintain proper accounts and to handover the said coupons to the Revenue Office to that effect - 'A' filed a complaint against the appellant alleging irregularities committed in the distribution of essential commodities to the public - On inspection of the Fair Price Shop, the Revenue officials could not find the records/Stock Registers, pursuant to the goods/stocks lying therein, they made inventory of the goods lying in the shop and seized the same - Trial court found the appellant guilty for the offence punishable u/s. 409 of IPC - The Appellate Court, dismissed the appeal filed against said order and confirmed the order passed by the Trial court - HC dismissed the revision filed by the appellant and confirmed the conviction and sentence imposed upon him u/s. 409 of IPC by the Trial court - Hence, the instant appeal - Whether HC rightly affirmed the order of the Trial court - Held, the Trial Court, came to the conclusion that the evidence of PWs 1 to 6 was reliable and trustworthy in relation to the offence in proving entrustment of property of the Government to the appellant - In the instant case, the appellant, was entrusted with the distribution of rice under the 'FFWS' to the workers on production of coupons, was charged with misappropriation of 67.65 quintals of rice - The evidence also proves that there was entrustment of property to the appellant - All these aspects have been rightly considered by the Trial Court and found the appellant guilty of the offence punishable u/s. 409 of IPC - The Appellate Court and HC, on appreciation of the materials placed by the prosecution and defence, confirmed the same - Impugned order was upheld - Appeal dismissed.
