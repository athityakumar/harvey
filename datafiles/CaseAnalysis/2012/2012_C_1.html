Mrudul M. Damle and another v C. B. I. New Delhi

Supreme Court of India

10 May 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, Gyan Sudha Misra

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 642; 2012 (2) JCC 1406

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Criminal Case, CBI, Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, Corruption

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 13(1)(e), 13(2) - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 109 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 406 - Transfer of proceedings - Petitioners were facing prosecution in CC No.45/2008 for offences punishable u/ss.13(1)(e) and 13(2) of the Act read with s.109 of IPC before Special Judge, CBI Cases, Rohini Courts, New Delhi - While so, instant petition is filed for transfer of said case to Court of Special Judge, CBI Cases, Thane, Maharashtra on ground of convenience of the parties and witnesses cited in charge sheet by prosecution - Whether proceedings could be transferred to Court at Maharashtra.

Held, the trial in Rohini Court at Delhi would be inconvenient not only to the accused persons but also to almost all the witnesses cited by prosecution except 4 who were in or around Delhi. Further, the case is not Delhi centric in the true sense inasmuch as the only reason the FIR was registered in Delhi was the fact that petitioner no.2 was posted in Delhi during a part of the check period. Further, CBI is fully equipped with an office at Bombay and a Court handling CBI cases is established at Thane also and so, transfer of case would not cause any hardship to prosecution especially when searches relied upon by prosecution have been conducted at Thane in which the prosecution claimed to have discovered a part of the assets allegedly acquired y petitioners. Hence, case is transferred to Special Judge, CBI Cases, Thane, Maharashtra. Petition allowed.
