Paras Nath Rai And Others v State Of Bihar And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

05 October 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 329; (2012) 12 SCC 642; AIR 2013 SC 1010; JT 2012 (10) SC 16; 2013 (118) RD 736; 2012(9) SCALE 757; [2012] 10 S.C.R. 732

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Family & Personal; Land & Property

Keywords: Orissa Consolidation of Holdings and Prevention of Administration of Land Act, 1972

Summary: Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Family & Personal - Bihar Consolidation of Holdings and Prevention of Fragmentation Act, 1956, ss. 3, 4(c) - Uttar Pradesh Consolidation of Holdings Act, 1953, ss. 5 - Title appeal and suit - Abatement - Dismissal - Challenged - Appellant's father and others (plaintiffs) filed a partition suit before Trial Court - Trial Court dismissed suit holding that suit was defective for non-joinder of parties and that stand of appellant that 'U' was daughter of 'AR' did not appeared to be correct - Aggrieved appellants filed title appeals, wherein a petition was filed u/s. 4(c) of 1956 Act appeal and suit had abated by statutory operation of law, but Appellate Court held that appeal could not be allowed to proceed as one of respondents had died during pendency of appeal and application for substitution was rejected and further held that appellants could not be allowed permission to file a fresh suit and allowed appeal as withdrawn - Aggrieved appellants filed revision, which was allowed by Single Judge and Lower Appellate Court was directed to decide matter afresh - Thereafter, Lower Appellate Court disposed appeal holding that appellants were not interested to contest appeal and that title appeals stood abated - Meanwhile, Consolidation Officer (CO) by order dt.23-3-1974 directed entry in khata No. 142 (revisional survey), which was in the name of father of appellant, would remained in operation - Thereafter, revisions were filed, which were decided ex-parte on 1-9-1978 and confirmed orders passed by CO and Deputy Director Consolidation (DDC) - Aggrieved challenged said order before HC - Single Judge quashed said order and directed the Additional Director (AD) to decide revision petitions - After the remand, revision petitions were dismissed by DDC holding that 'U' was daughter of 'DR' and not of 'AR' - Single Judge concurred with view of Appellate Authority and Revisional Authority and dismissed petition - Aggrieved by Single Judge, an appeal was filed before DB - DB dismissed appeal holding that appeal had abated for non-prosecution by appellants and consolidation authorities took note of findings of Civil Court - Hence, instant appeal - Appellant contended that Courts below and forums below did not took note of fact that application for abatement was filed u/s. 4(c) of 1956 Act to the effect that title appeal had abated after issue of notification u/s. 3 of 1956 Act and that HC erred by holding that appeal had abated because of non-substitution of legal representative (LR) - Whether appeal filed by appellants was abated in view of 1956 Act - Held, once a notification was published u/s. 3 of 1956 Act, every suit and proceeding in respect of declaration of rights or interest in any land lying in areas or for declaration or adjudication of any other rights in regard to which proceeding could or ought to be taken under 1956 Act pending before any Court or Authority whether of first instance or of appeal, reference or revision, should, on order being passed in that behalf by Court or Authority before whom such suit or proceeding was pending should stand abated with a view to ensure jurisdiction of Authorities under 1956 Act remained unhampered and said Authorities were not obstructed by proceedings in Civil Courts and their decisions were not impeded by decisions of Civil Courts - There was conceptual difference between statutory abatement and abatement under CPC - On the basis of a statutory abatement, whole proceeding from its inception stood abated because local law had provided an effective alternative remedy to be perused before an exclusive forum to remedy the grievance raised before Court - Thus, view expressed by HC in judgments that appeal might abate but decree would not abate was not correct, more so, when preliminary decree was under challenge in appeal - In instant case, judgment and decree passed by Trial Court was assailed in title appeal - Though a petition was filed u/s. 4(c) of 1956 Act no order was passed thereon, yet appeal was permitted to be withdrawn - Challenge was made in civil revision, HC had remanded matter directing appeal to be restored to file with a further direction that matter would be dealt with on merits including competence of Court to hear appeal - Despite remit Trial Court did not took note of petition filed by appellant u/s. 4(c) of 1956 Act, but observed that they were not interested to contest appeal and accordingly directed appeal stood abated because of non-substitution - That order shows total non-application of mind - As was evincible consolidation proceedings had continued and at one stage the authorities were relying on findings of Civil Court and at some other ignoring same - Eventually matter travelled to HC in writ petition - Single Judge ruled that consolidation authorities were justified in relying on findings of Civil Court - In instant case, title appeal was pending against preliminary decree and an application u/s. 4(c) of 1956 Act was preferred - It would have advisable on the part of Appellate Court to record a finding that entire proceeding of civil suit stood abated - But, Appellate Court directed abatement because of non-substitution of LRs of one of respondents - Thus, suit and appeal abated and resultantly the very commencement of civil proceeding come to a naught, and findings recorded in said proceeding become extinct - Single Judge and DB did not appreciate the lis in proper perspective and held that reliance on findings recorded by Civil Court by revisional Authority under 1956 Act could not be faulted, which finding was wholly erroneous and overturned - None of findings recorded by Civil Court should be taken aid of - Impugned orders of Single Judge and DB were set aside and matter was remanded to Single Judge to decide matter on merits on the basis of material brought before Consolidation Authorities - Appeal allowed.
