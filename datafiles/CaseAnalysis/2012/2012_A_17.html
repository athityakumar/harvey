Adarsh Shiksha Mahavidyalaya and others v Subhash Rahangdale and others

Supreme Court of India

06 January 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 575; (2012) 2 SCC 425; 2012 (3) AWC 2846; JT 2012 (1) SC 213; 2012 (2) MLJ(SC) 741; [2012] 2 S.C.R. 1; 2012 (2) SLR 86

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Education

Keywords: National Council for Teacher Education (Recognition Norms and Procedure) Regulations, 2005, NCTE Regulations, 2005, National Council for Teacher Education (Application for recognition, the manner for submission, determination of conditions for recognition of institutions and permissions to start new course or training) Regulations, 1995, State Education Board Act, National Council for Teacher Education (Form of Application for Recognition, the Time-Limit of Submission of Application, Determination of Norms and Standards for Recognition of Teacher Education Programmes and Permission to Start New Course or Training) Regulations, 2002, M.P. B.Ed. Examination Rules, 2007

Summary: Education - Administrative - National Council for Teacher Education Act, 1993 - Educational institutions - Recognition denied - Challenged - Cases filed by these institutions, many of whom were not granted recognition due to non- fulfilment of the conditions specified in Act and the Regulations framed there under and by the students who had taken admission in such institutions with the hope that at the end of the day they would be able to get favourable order by invoking sympathy of the Court, have choked the dockets of various HCs and even SC - Hence instant appeals - Whether the students who had taken admission in unrecognized institutions or the institutions which had not been granted affiliation by the examining body had the right to appear in the examination - Held, SC in one case, where one Professor, who was signatory to the Memorandum of Association of the appellant-society created fake documents for starting medical college for Christian students - When the appellant sought affiliation with X University, the latter made some queries and asked for certain documents - Appellant did not furnish the requisite information and documents - In the meanwhile, 60 students were admitted in the first year MBBS course - In July 1985, the Govt. informed the appellant that permission to start private medical college was not granted in view of the policy of the Govt. and Medical Council of India - Appellant then filed writ petition before the HC, which was dismissed - Before SC, it was contended that the appellant was minority institution and, as such, it was not required to take permission for starting medical college - SC negatived the appellant's plea and confirmed the order passed by the HC - While dealing with the question, whether the students who had already been admitted by the appellant should be allowed to participate in the examination conducted by the University, SC observed that it did not think that it could possibly accede to the request of the students - Any direction of the nature sought students would be in clear transgression of the provisions of the University Act and the regulations of the University - SC could not by its fiat direct the University to disobey the statute to which it owed its existence and the regulations made by the University itself - HC could not imagine anything more destructive of the rule of law than direction by the court to disobey the law - A.P. Christians Medical Educational Society v. Govt. of Andhra Pradesh, 1986 Indlaw SC 304, relied on - Thus, SC held that the impugned orders did not suffer from any legal infirmity warranting interference by it - Appeals dismissed.
