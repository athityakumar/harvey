Acc Limited (Formerly Known As The Associated Cement Company Limited) v Global Cements Limited

Supreme Court of India

11 June 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan, J. S. KHEHAR

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 149; (2012) 7 SCC 71; AIR 2012 SC 3824; AIR 2012 SC (Supp) 400; 2012 (5) ALD(SC) 170; 2012(4) ALL MR 475; 2012 (93) ALR 676; 2012 (4) AWC 3971; 2012 (1) CLT 57; 2012 (3) CompLJ 56; 2012 (3) CTC 871; 2012 (6) MahLJ 17; 2012 (4) MPLJ 311; 2012 (168) PLR 203; 2013 (1) RCR(Civil) 248; 2012(5) SCALE 778; [2012] 6 S.C.R. 215

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Keywords: Chota Nagpur Tenancy Act, 1908, Andhra Pradesh (Talangana Area) Tenancy and Agricultural Lands Act, 1950

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, s. 11 - Appointment of arbitrator - Whether an arbitration agreement would survive on the event of death of a named arbitrator - Held, ss. 14 and 15 of Act provided the grounds for termination of mandate of arbitrator on the ground of incapability of arbitrator to act or if he withdrew from his office or when parties agreed to termination of mandate of arbitrator - S. 15(2) of Act stated that a substitute arbitrator should be appointed as per the rules that were applicable to appointment of arbitrator being replaced - S. 15(2) of Act was required to be given a liberal interpretation so as to apply to all possible circumstances under which mandate would be terminated - In the instant case, cl. 21 of agreement did not prohibit or debar parties in appointing a substitute arbitrator in place of named arbitrators and, in the absence of any prohibition or debarment, parties could persuade the court for appointment of an arbitrator u/cl. 21 of agreement - HC was justified in entertaining such application and appointing a former Judge of SC as a sole arbitrator under the Act to adjudicate dispute and difference between parties - Petition dismissed.
