State of Uttar Pradesh and others v Ashok Kumar Nigam

Supreme Court of India

13 December 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Swatanter Kumar, Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 548; (2013) 3 SCC 372; 2013(1) ALL MR 488; 2013 (2) AWC 1427; JT 2013 (1) SC 153; 2013 (1) MLJ 538; 2012(12) SCALE 551; [2012] 13 S.C.R. 265; 2013 (1) SLR 790; 2012 (4) UPLBEC 3218; 2014 (1) WLN(SC) 70

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 16 - Appointment - Renewal of term - Sustainability - Respondent was appointed as District Govt. Counsel on 17-9-2004 vide a notification issued by State Govt./appellant - Later, term of respondent was renewed on 3-3-2006 for a period of 1 year and his term came to an end on 5-3-2007 - Subsequently, respondent submitted his application for renewal of his term on 19-1-2007 - Appellant by order refused his renewal which resulted in cancellation of engagement of respondent - Aggrieved, respondent filed writ petition before HC - HC allowed writ petition and set aside order of appellant - Hence, instant appeals - Whether renewal/extension beyond 60 years should be granted to respondent or not - Held, right of consideration for renewal for specified period was legitimate right vested in an applicant and he could be deprived of such right and be declined renewal where his work was unsatisfactory and was reported by specified authorities - Further, Govt. had an absolute right to terminate appointment at any time without specifying any reason, it would be violative of arts. 14 and 16 of Constitution and such rule should be arbitrary, thus not sustainable in law - Thus, it was not permissible for Govt. to take recourse to Para 7.06 (3) in manner in which it had done and in any case, said rule could hardly be sustained in law - Hence, SC could not find any fault with reasoning of HC - However, those were considerations which had been weighed by competent authority in State Govt. to examine whether renewal/extension beyond 60 years should be granted or not but that did not ipso facto means that there was a right to appointment upto age of 60 years irrespective of work, conduct and integrity of counsel - Thus, rule provides due safeguards as it calls for report of District Judge and District Officer granting renewal - Therefore, impugned judgment of HC was upheld - Appeals dismissed.
