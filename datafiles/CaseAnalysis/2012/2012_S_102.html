Sabhia Mohammed Yusuf Abdul Hamid Mulla (D) Byl.Rs. And Others v Special Land Acquisition Officer And Others

Supreme Court of India

02 July 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 185; (2012) 7 SCC 595; AIR 2012 SC 2709; 2012 (6) ALD(SC) 26; 2012 (95) ALR 219; 2012 (4) AWC 3860; 2012 (4) Bom.C.R. 519; 2012 (3) CLT(SC) 12; 2012 (6) MahLJ 510; 2012 (4) MPLJ 589; 2012 (3) RCR(Civil) 844; 2013 (118) RD 112; 2012(6) SCALE 34

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4(1), 18, 54 - Land acquisition - Determination of compensation - Appellants/landowner's land was acquired for project by respondent/Special Land Acquisition Officer (AO) in 1970 - AO passed different awards for different parcels of land and fixed market value of acquired land in range of Rs. 1.75 per square meter to Rs. 2.50 per square meter - Appellants filed application u/s. 28A(1) for redetermination of market value of acquired land - AO held that landowners were entitled to compensation at rate of Rs. 1.20/- per square meter to Rs. 2/- per square meter - Appellants filed application for award of compensation at rate of Rs. 100/- per square meter - AO controverted claim of appellants and pleaded that on date of s. 4(1) notification appellants' land was undeveloped and was being used only for purpose of agriculture - Reference Court considered evidence produced by appellants and held that acquired land had non-agricultural potential - State Govt. filed appeal u/s. 54 of the Act and prayed for reduction in amount of compensation - Appellants also filed appeal and prayed that amount of compensation be enhanced keeping in view judgment in LAR Nos. 168/86 and 172/86 - DB took cognizance of earlier judgments and held that appellants were not entitled to compensation in excess of what was awarded to other landowners - Review petition filed by appellants was partly allowed by HC - Hence instant appeals - Whether compensation awarded was adequate in the instant case - Held, while fixing market value of acquired land, Land Acquisition Collector was required to keep in mind existing geographical situation of land, existing use of land, already available advantages, like proximity to National or State High Way or road and/or developed area, market value of other land situated in same locality/village/area or adjacent or very near acquired land - Thus, while determining amount of compensation Reference Court had taken notice of fact that acquired land was in proximity of National Highway no. 4 and construction of important connecting bridges - Reference Court also noted that civic amenities were available prior to 1970 and industrial estates had been developed and concluded that acquired land was available for non-agricultural use - HC did not advert to factors noted by Reference Court and reduced amount of compensation by mechanically applying distance criteria, i.e., distance of acquired land from Bombay-Pune Highway adopted in earlier judgments - Therefore, impugned judgment and order could not be sustained - Order passed by Reference Court for payment of compensation to appellants at rate of Rs.25/- per square meter was restored - Appeals allowed.
