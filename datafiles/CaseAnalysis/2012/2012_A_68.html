M/S Hotel Ashoka (Indian Tour.Dev.Cor.Ltd.) v Assistant Commissioner Of Commercial Taxes & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

03 February 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

A.R. Dave, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 22; (2012) 3 SCC 204; AIR 2012 SC 982; 2013 (1) CTC 95; 2012 (276) E.L.T. 433; JT 2012 (2) SC 66; 2012(2) SCALE 290; [2012] 1 S.C.R. 808; [2012] 48 VST 443

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax; Sales Tax; VAT

Keywords: Customs Frontiers Of India, Transfer Of Documents Of Title, Customs Frontiers

Summary: Sales Tax - VAT - Customs - Indirect Tax - Karnataka Value Added Tax Act, 2003 - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956, s. 5 - Sales in duty free shops - Taxability - Appellant assesse was engaged in business of selling liquor to passengers through its duty free shops at airports - Appellant contented that no tax was payable as goods which were sold at duty free shops were sold directly to passengers and even delivery of goods at duty free shops was made before importing goods or before goods had crossed customs frontiers of India - Revenue, by assessment order, directed appellant to pay sales tax - Appellant challenged assessment order before HC - Single Judge dismissed appellant's writ petition - Appellant's writ appeal was also dismissed thereafter - Hence, instant appeal - (A) Whether goods in question had crossed customs frontiers of India and thereby, attracting sales tax on impugned transaction - Held, goods brought from foreign countries by appellant was kept in bonded warehouses and they were transferred to duty free shops situated at airport as and when stock of goods lying at duty free shops was exhausted - When goods were lying in bonded warehouses, they were deemed to have been kept outside customs frontiers of country - No tax on sale or purchase of goods could be imposed by any State when transaction of sale or purchase took place in course of import of goods into or export of goods out of territory of India - Thus, if any transaction of sale or purchase took place when goods were being imported in India or they were being exported from India, no State could impose any tax thereon - Hence, State had no right to tax any such transaction which took place at duty free shops owned by appellant which were not within customs frontiers of India - (B) Whether transactions of sale, which was not taken place in course of import, would be not exempted u/s. 5 of the 1956 Act - Held, at duty free shops, which were admittedly outside customs frontiers of country, goods were sold to customers by giving physical delivery - Goods were sold by giving physical possession at duty free shops to customers - Simply because sales were not affected by transfer of documents of title to goods and sales were effected by giving physical possession of goods to customers, it would not mean that sales were taxable under the 1956 Act - Hence, impugned assessment order of Revenue was quashed - Appeal allowed.
