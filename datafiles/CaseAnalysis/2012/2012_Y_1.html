Y. K. Singla v Punjab National Bank and others

Supreme Court of India

14 December 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

J. S. KHEHAR, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 552; (2013) 3 SCC 472; 2013 (2) AWC 1436; 2013 (136) FLR 1087; JT 2013 (1) SC 363; 2013 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 587; 2013 (2) LLJ 417; 2012(12) SCALE 646; [2012] 13 S.C.R. 1189; 2013 (1) SLR 778; 2013 (4) WLN(SC) 98

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Punjab National Bank (Employees) Pension Regulations, 1995

Summary: Service - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 120B - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 5(2) r/w. 13(1)(d) - Gratuity Act, 1972, s. 7(3A) - Punjab National Bank (Employees) Pension Regulations, 1995, regn. 46(2) - Fraud - Nonpayment of gratuity - Challenged - Appellant was employee of respondent/Bank - Appellant allegedly committed offence of criminal conspiracy and public servant committing fraud - Appellant was charged with s. 120B of IPC and 13(1)(d) r/w. 5(2) of 1988 Act - However, Trial Court acquitted appellant - During course of Trial, appellant retired from service - However, respondent did not reimburse leave encashment benefit amount and gratuity - Appellant filed petition before HC after which appellant was allowed interest on the amount withheld @ 8% from the date retiral benefits had became due to the appellant, till the actual payment thereof to him - Dissatisfied, respondent filed appeal before DB claiming that appellant was not entitled to any interest on delayed payment of gratuity - DB allowed the appeal of respondent to that extent - Hence instant appeal - Whether the provisions of the Gratuity Act could be extended to the appellant, so as to award him interest u/s. 7(3A) of 1972 Act - Held, insofar as the instant aspect of the matter was concerned, it was the vehement contention of appellant that the provisions of the 1972 Act were extendable to the appellant, and as such, he would be entitled to disbursement of interest u/s. 7(3A) of 1972 Act - Plea at the behest of respondent, however, was to the contrary - Contention of respondent was that the respondent having adopted Regulations, claim of the appellant could only be determined under the provisions of Regulations - It was pointed out, that denial of payment of gratuity in instant case, was valid and justified u/regn. 46(2) of the Regulations - Furthermore, it was pointed out, that Regulations, did not make any provision for the award of interest in case of delayed payment of gratuity - HC agreed with contention of respondent - However, taking into consideration that the appellant had to fight the case upto instant level, Rs. 50000/- was granted to him which respondent was made liable to pay - Appeal disposed of.
