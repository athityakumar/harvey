State of Uttar Pradesh and another v Santosh Kumar Mishra and another

Supreme Court of India

03 August 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Surinder Singh Nijjar

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 223

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: U.P. Pharmacists Service Rules, 1980, Educational Regulation Act, 1991, U.P. Procedure for Direct Recruitment of Group 'C' Posts (Outside the Purview of Public Service Commission) (First Amendment) Rules, 2003, U.P. Procedure for Direct Recruitment of Group 'C' Posts (Outside the Purview of Public Service Commission) Rules, 2000

Summary: Service - Administrative - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Uttar Pradesh Procedure for Direct Recruitment of Group 'C' Posts (Outside the Purview of Public Service Commission) (First Amendment) Rules, 2003 - Uttar Pradesh Procedure for Direct Recruitment of Group 'C' Posts (Outside the Purview of Public Service Commission) Rules, 2000 - Uttar Pradesh Pharmacists Service Rules, 1980, rr. 14, 15(2), 15, 3g - Recruitment - Vacancies - Diploma course in pharmacy - Advertisement - Age relaxation - Respondents/petitioners were diploma holders in Pharmacy registered with State Pharmacy Council - Controversy was triggered by an advertisement dt. 12-11-2007, whereby 766 vacancies were advertised for being filled up by diploma holders - Said advertisement provided that recruitment would be made in accordance with 2000 Rules as amended (First Amendment) Rules, 2003 and relevant service rules in force with regard to educational qualifications and other conditions of service - According to respondents, on an interpretation of r. 15(2) of the 1980 Rules by State Govt. respondents were entitled to be selected and appointed first on vacancies advertised, as they belonged to previous batches and were denied appointment by State Govt. earlier on plea that notwithstanding their merit being superior to those of some of diploma holders, who had obtained diploma prior in point of time, latter candidates were to be given appointment first - Such practice was continued till 2002 - However, when fresh vacancies were declared and respondents were to be appointed on same principle and practice, they were denied benefit of same citing the 1980 Rules r/w 2003 Amendment Rules - Respondents filed several writ petitions before HC for quashing advertisement in question - HC disposed of petitions - Respondents filed several writ appeals before DB - DB disposed of appeals - Hence instant petitions - Whether order passed by DB was sustainable - Held, practice which had been consistently followed and had deprived some of diploma-holders in pharmacy earlier, was discarded against them to deprived them of an opportunity of employment - HC while deciding various writ petitions had allowed benefit of relaxation of age, erred in directing that selections of said respondents were to be made strictly on criteria of merit, irrespective of batch in which incumbents had obtained their diplomas in Pharmacy - Said error was rightly corrected by DB in appeals, which was reflected on extract of impugned judgment - Therefore, on facts of case no interference called for with decision of DB impugned - Respondent/petitioners should be entitled to same benefits as those diploma holders governed by the 1980 Rules, had obtained their diplomas in pharmacy prior to 1998 - Petitions disposed of.
