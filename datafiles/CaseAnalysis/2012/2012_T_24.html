P. Sanjeeva Rao v The State Of A.P.

Supreme Court of India

02 July 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, Gyan Sudha Misra

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 161; (2012) 7 SCC 56; AIR 2012 SC 2242; 2012 (3) JCC 1867; 2012 (4) MLJ(Crl) 311; 2012 (2) OLR 477; 2012 (3) RCR(Criminal) 653; 2012 (3) RLW 2635; 2012(6) SCALE 9; [2012] 6 S.C.R. 787

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 7 and 13 (1) r/w. s. 13(1)(D) - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 242 and 311 - Recall of prosecution witnesses - Denied - Challenged - Appellant, a Sub Divisional Officer in BSNL, was being prosecuted for offences punishable u/ss. 7 and 13 (1) r/w. s. 13(1)(D) of Act - Appellant filed applications u/ss. 242 and 311 of CrPC for recall of prosecution witnesses No. 1 and 2 for cross-examination - Said applications were dismissed by trial court who also held that recall of PWs 1 and 2 for cross- examination more than 3 and ½ years after they had been examined in relation to an incident that had taken place 7 years back, was bound to cause prejudice to the prosecution - HC dismissed revision petitions filed against said order - Hence, the instant appeal - Whether Trial court and HC rightly denied appellant to recall the prosecution witnesses No. 1 and 2 for cross examination - Held, recall of the witnesses was being directed nearly 4 years after they were examined in chief about an incident that was nearly 7 years old - Delay takes a heavy toll on the human memory apart from breeding cynicism about the efficacy of the judicial system to decide cases within a reasonably foreseeable time period - To that extent the apprehension expressed by respondent, that the prosecution may suffer prejudice on account of a belated recall, may not be wholly without any basis - On a parity of reasoning and looking to the consequences of denial of opportunity to cross-examine the witnesses, Court would prefer to err in favour of the appellant getting an opportunity rather than protecting the prosecution against a possible prejudice at his cost - Fairness of the trial was a virtue that was sacrosanct in the judicial system and no price was too heavy to protect that virtue - Impugned orders passed by Trial Court as affirmed by the HC to be set aside - Court directed that the prosecution witnesses No. 1 and 2 to be recalled and an opportunity to cross-examine the said witnesses to be awarded to the appellant - Appeals allowed.
