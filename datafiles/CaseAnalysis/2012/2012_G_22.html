Lalita Kumari and others v Government of Uttar Pradesh and others

Supreme Court of India

27 February 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Dalveer Bhandari, T.S. Thakur, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 609; (2012) 4 SCC 1; JT 2012 (3) SC 119; 2012 (2) MLJ(Crl) 525; 2012 (2) RCR(Criminal) 188; [2012] 1 S.C.R. 1066

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 32 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 154(1) - Registering of FIR - Mandatory - Officer Incharge - Validity - Father of petitioner No. 1 had filed writ petition u/art. 32 of Constitution alleging that police inspite of lodging a complaint that he daughter was kidnapped failed to register FIR and instead demanded money from him for initiating investigation and to arrest accused persons - Aggrieved instant petition - Court had issued notice to all the States inducting them in petition - Petitioner contended that whenever information regarding cognizable offence was brought to notice of SHO, he had no option but to register FIR - UOI contended that s. 154 (1) provides that every information relating to commission of a cognizable offence if given orally, to an officer incharge of a police station shall be reduced in writing by him or under his directions - Provision is mandatory, use of word 'shall' by legislation was indicative of statutory intent - In case information was given in writing or was reduced in writing on being given orally, it was required to be signed by persons giving it, further provided that substance of commission of a cognizable offence as given in writing or reduced to writing 'shall' be entered in a book to be kept by such officer in such form as State Govt. may prescribe in this behalf - Sub-section (2) provides that a copy of such information as recorded in sub-section (1) shall be given forthwith free of cost to informant - Another State contended that before registering an FIR u/s. 154 CrPC it was open to SHO to hold a preliminary enquiry to ascertain whether there was prime facie case of commission of cognizable offence or not - Whether u/s. 154 of CrPC, a police officer was bound to register an FIR when a cognizable offence was made out or he had some latitude of conducting some kind of preliminary enquiry before registering FIR - Held, it was quite evident from ratio laid down in cases that different Benches of this Court had taken divergent views in different cases - In present case after Court's notice, UOI, States and Union Territories had also taken or expressed divergent views about interpretation of s. 154 CrPC - Analysis of various judgments delivered by this Court in last several decades, SC, clearly discerned divergent judicial opinions of this Court on main issue whether under Section 154 CrPC, a police officer was bound to register an FIR when a cognizable offence was made out or he (police officer) had an option, discretion or latitude of conducting some kind of preliminary enquiry before registering FIR - In view of divergent opinions in a large number of cases decided by this Court, it had become extremely important to have a clear enunciation of law and adjudication by a larger Bench of this Court for benefit of all concerned, courts, investigating agencies and citizens, hence Hon'ble Chief Justice may refer these matters to a Constitution Bench of at least five Judges of this Court for an authoritative judgment - Order accordingly.
