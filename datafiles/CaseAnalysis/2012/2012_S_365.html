Maulana Mohd. Amir Rashadi v State of Uttar Pradesh and another

Supreme Court of India

16 January 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, J. Chelameswar

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 394; (2012) 2 SCC 382; 2012 (2) ADJ 409; 2012 CRLJ 1444; 2012 (2) JCC 818; JT 2012 (1) SC 159; 2012 (1) RCR(Criminal) 586; [2012] 1 S.C.R. 311

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 307, 325 - Respondent No. 2 (accused) was allegedly committed offences u/ss. 302, 307 of IPC and was charge sheeted for offences u/ss. 302, 307 and 325 r/w s. 34 of IPC - Pending trial, respondent No. 2 filed an application for bail, before HC, which was allowed by impugned order - Aggrieved appellant (complainant) filed instant appeal - Appellant contended that he had received threats from respondent No. 2 and his supporters warning him not to pursue case against him - Whether HC was justified in enlarging respondent No. 2 (accused) on bail - Held, in instant case, respondent No. 2 was arrested and in jail since 24-8-2009 and after filing of charge-sheet, prosecution examined 2 important witnesses as PWs 1 and 2 - Based on representations of appellant, adequate protection was provided to him - State had assured that trial would not be prolonged and conclude within a reasonable time - Fact that HC while granting bail, imposed several conditions for strict adherence during the period of bail, and it was clear that in case of breach of any of conditions, Trial Court would have liberty to take steps to send respondent No. 2 to jail again - Further made clear that if appellant receives any fresh threat from the respondent No. 2 or from his supporters, he was free to inform Trial Court and in such event Trial Court was free to take appropriate steps as observed by HC - Trial Court was directed to complete trial within a period of 4 months - Impugned order of HC was justified - Appeal dismissed.
