Burdwan Central Cooperative Bank Ltd. & Anr v Asim Chatterjee & Ors

Supreme Court of India

18 January 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 49; (2012) 2 SCC 641; 2012 (1) CalLT 36; 2012 (132) FLR 713; JT 2012 (1) SC 404; 2012 (2) LLJ 548; 2012(1) SCALE 455; [2012] 1 S.C.R. 390; 2012 (4) SLR 805

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: West Bengal Co-operative Societies Rules, 1987

Summary: Service - West Bengal Co-operative Societies Rules, 1987, r. 69(2)(b) - Disciplinary Proceedings - Dismissal - Validity - Respondent no. 1 was an employee of Samity, a Co-operative Society affiliated to appellant-bank - Appellant-bank appointed respondent no. 1 as Grade III Staff u/r. 69(2)(b) of the Rules - Registrar, lodged complaint with appellant-bank stating respondent no.1 had committed various financial irregularities in maintaining accounts - Appellant-bank dismissed respondent no. 1 on the basis of enquiry report submitted by disciplinary authority - Aggrieved by order of dismissal, respondent no. 1 filed writ petition before HC - Single Judge allowed and held that order of disciplinary authority was vitiated as respondent no.1 was not served with a copy of enquiry report, nor was any opportunity given to him by way of show cause notice to offer his explanation - Appellant-bank filed writ appeal against order of Single Judge - DB disposed of appeal holding that appellant-bank was not entitled to proceed against respondent no.1 in law - Hence instant appeal - Whether an employer could take disciplinary action against an employee in regard to acts purported to have been done by him in his previous employment in an affiliated society - Held, even though respondent no.1 might not was under the direct administrative control of appellant-bank at the relevant point of time when the defalcation was alleged, on account of affiliation of Samity with appellant-bank under the provisions of the Rules, appellant-bank had jurisdiction over respondent no.1 after he joined the employment of appellant-bank - Thus, it was natural for appellant-bank to proceed departmentally against respondent no.1 - Both Single Judge and DB were not justified in interfering with the action taken by disciplinary authorities of appellant-bank - Impugned orders of Single Judge and DB were set aside - Appeal allowed.
