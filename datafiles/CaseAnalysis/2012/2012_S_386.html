Sudevanand and others v State through CBI

Supreme Court of India

19 January 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Aftab Alam, Ranjana Prakash Desai

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 431; (2012) 3 SCC 387; 2012 CRLJ 1320; JT 2012 (2) SC 40; 2012 (3) MLJ(Crl) 82; 2012 (1) OLR 536; 2012 (1) RCR(Criminal) 662; [2012] 2 S.C.R. 139

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Approver

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 115, 307 and 120B - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 164,306,311,391 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 145,80 - Explosive Substance Act, 1908, s. 4(b) - Attempt to Murder - Approver's evidence - Additional evidence - Sustainability - Petition filed to recall witnesses at appellate stage before HC was dismissed - Appellants were convicted u/ss. 115, 307/120B attempting to kill Chief Justice - Appellants were charged for the offence on basis of statement made by co-accused/ approver - Appellants were undergoing sentence - In 1978, Chief Minister wrote a letter to Prime Minister of India stating that statement of approver were obtained by CBI subjecting him to great mental and physical torture and under coercion - Approver had deposed in another case where he deposed that statement made by him was not voluntary but was under coercion of CBI - Appellants were released on bail pending appeals - Appellants filed applications praying to call for and taking on record statement of approver made in another case, affidavit of officials, transfer petitions and enquiry report and for further cross examination of approver in terms of s. 145 of Evidence Act - HC dismissed application on ground that it was filed after lapse of 20 years - Hence, instant appeal - Appellant contended that denying cross-examination of approver in light of statement made by approver would cause grave prejudice to appellants and would lead to miscarriage of justice - CBI contended that approver had voluntarily made statements on 3 different occasions where he was subjected to lengthy cross-examination, he did not make any statement that it was obtained under coercion or threats - Whether HC was justified in dismissing application to recall approver for cross-examination - Held, that one of 2 statement of approver is false and is difficult to say which is true and which statement is made under influence - When appeal is pending and Approver is found blabbering and boasting among his friends that he was able to take Court for a ride and settled his personal score with the convict by sending them to jail - Such a statement would also be completely beyond legal framework but can it be said that in light of such a development, it may be admissible on its own without any help from s. 311 or s. 391 of Cr.P.C - It is only such statement or development which is otherwise not within legal framework that would need exercise of Court's jurisdiction to bring it before it as part of legal record. Appeals allowed.
