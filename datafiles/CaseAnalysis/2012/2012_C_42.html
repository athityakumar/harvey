Commissioner Of Central Excise, Mumbai v M/S. Fiat India (P) Ltd. & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

29 August 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Dattu, A.R. Dave

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 453; (2012) 9 SCC 332; AIR 2012 SC (Supp) 814; 2012 (283) E.L.T. 161; [2012] 12 S.C.R. 975

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Levy Of Duty, Sale Price, Normal Price, Place Of Removal, Central Excise Valuation (Determination of Price of Excisable Goods) Rules, 2000

Summary: Excise - Practice & Procedure - VAT - Central Excise Tariff Act, 1985, Central Excise Act, 1944, ss. 11A, 14, 2(h), 3, 4, - Central Excise Rules, 1944, ss. 4, 4(1)(b) - Exercisable goods - Wholesale price - Manufacturing Cost - Penalty - Sustainability - Respondents-assessees were manufacturer of motor cars, and goods were excisable under chapter sub-heading No. 8703.90 of Act - Business was initially managed M/s Premier Automobiles Ltd. who surrendered its central excise registration on 6-4-1998, thereafter, M/s Ind Auto Ltd.- now M/s Fiat India Ltd. carried on business after obtaining fresh central excise registration - Assessees had filed several price declarations in terms of r. 173C of Rules declaring wholesale price of their cars for sale through whole sale depots during period commencing from 27-05-1996 to 04-03-2001 - Authorities under Act had made enquiries on 20-12-1996 and 31-12-1996, u/r. 173C(3) of 1944 Rules r/w s. 14 of Act and prima facie found that wholesale price declared by assessees was much less than cost of production and, therefore, price so declared by them could not be treated as a normal price for purpose of quantification of assessable value u/s. 4(1)(a) of Act and for levy of excise duty as it would amount to short payment of duty - Preventive and Intelligence Branch sometime in year 1997-98 had conducted investigation into affairs of respondents, whereby it was found that respondents were importing all kits in CKD/SKD condition for manufacturing cars - Superintendent of Central Excise, issued 11 show cause notices to assessees for period from June 1996 to February 2000, inter alia, making a demand of differential duty on assessable value calculated on basis of manufacturing cost plus manufacturing profit minus MODVAT availed per car, and duty which respondents were actually paying on assessable value, it was alleged in show cause notices that respondents had failed to determine and pay correct duty cars while clearing them - Assessees were required to show cause as to why correct duty due on goods along with interest should not be recovered from u/r. 9 of 1944 Rules r/w ss. 11A, 11AB of Act, goods should not be confiscated and penalty imposed u/r. 9 r/w rr. 52-A, 173Q of Rules, and further, penalty equal to amount of duty should not be imposed u/s. 11AC of Act - Assessees replied that they had declared assessable value or normal price in terms of s. 4(1)(a) of Act - Assessees further submitted that since they had launched new models of cars which required import of cars in kit-form (CKD and SKD), thereafter they were assembled and sold, cost of imports, assembly and overheads lead to increase in overall cost of production of their cars, they were facing intense competition from Maruti car manufacturers which required them to keep price of their cars at a lower price, therefore, they were forced to sell their cars at a loss in order to compete and attract buyers in market - Superintendent of Central Excise confirmed show cause-cum-demand notices issued and, thereby, had directed respondents to pay difference in duty - Assessee preferred appeal before appellate authority which dismissed - Second appeal before Tribunal, reversed findings on ground that there was no allegation that wholesale price charged by assessee was for extra commercial consideration and that dealing of assessees and their buyers was not at arms length or that there was a flow back of money from buyers to assessees and, therefore, price declared by assessees was ascertainable normal price in view of previous decision of this Court - Hence instant appeals - Petitioner contends that assessees were not fulfilling conditions enumerated in s. 4(1)(a) of Act and therefore, valuation had to be done in accordance with s. 4(1)(b) of Act r/w 1975 Valuation Rules and that price fixed by assessees did not reflect true value of goods as manufacturing cost and profit was much higher than sale price - Respondent pleaded that charging Section and computation Section were independent to each other and should not be mixed up - Whether Tribunal was justified in reversing order of assessment authority, which directed respondents to pay duty - Held, u/s. 4(1)(b) of Act, 1944, any goods which did not fall within ambit of s. 4(1)(a) i.e. if 'normal price' could not be ascertained because goods were not sold or for any other reason, 'normal price' would have to be determined in prescribed manner i.e. prior to 1-7-2000, in accordance with Rules, 1975 and after 1-7-2000, in accordance with Rules, 2000 - R. 7 was in nature of residuary clause and provides that if value of excisable goods could not be determined u/rr. 4, 5, 6 of Rules, adjudging authority shall determine value of such goods according to best of his judgment and while doing so, he may have regard to any one or more methods provided under Rules - Bare reading of these rules does not give any indication that adjudging authority while computing assessable value of excisable goods, had to follow rules sequentially, rules only provides for arriving at assessable value under different contingencies - R. 7 of Valuation Rules provides for best judgment assessment and gives an indication that assessing authority while quantifying assessable value under Rules, may take assistance of methods provided u/rr. 4, 5 or 6 of Valuation Rules, therefore, contention that assessing authority before invoking r. 7 of 1975, Valuation Rules, ought to have invoked rr. 4, 5 and 6 of Rules could not be accepted - Assessing authority could not do valuation with help of other rules, had resorted to best judgment method and while doing so, had taken assistance of report of 'Cost Accountant' who was asked to conduct special audit to ascertain correct price that required to be adopted during relevant period, therefore, it could not take exception of assessable value of excisable goods quantified by assessing authority - Appeals allowed.
