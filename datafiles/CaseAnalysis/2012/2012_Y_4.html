New India Assurance Co. Ltd v Yogesh Devi & Ors

Supreme Court of India

10 February 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

J. Chelameswar, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 37; (2012) 3 SCC 613; 2012 (1) ACC 649; 2012 ACJ 702; AIR 2012 SC 945; 2012 (4) ALD(SC) 101; 2012(3) ALL MR 460; 2012 (91) ALR 202; 2012 (2) AWC 1726; JT 2012 (2) SC 265; 2012(2) M.P.H.T. 1; 2012 (166) PLR 535; 2012 (2) RCR(Civil) 536; 2012(2) SCALE 409

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Insurance - Enhancement of Compensation - Contribution - Salary - Sustainability - Respondents-legal heirs of deceased who died in accident were awarded compensation of Rs 10,00,000/- against their huge claim by Tribunal - Respondents appealed in HC for enhancement, HC enhanced compensation by calculating deceased income @ Rs 24,000/- P.M - Aggrieved instant appeal - Appellant contended that HC grossly erred in concluding that income of deceased @ Rs 24,000/- P.M - Respondents contended that HC rightly enhance compensation as there were 6 dependents of deceased - Whether HC was justified in calculating deceased income @ 24,000/- P.M - Held, appeal cannot be sustained as finding of HC that claimants lost an amount of Rs.16,000/- P.M due to death of deceases is neither based on any evidence nor logic adopted by HC for arriving at such a conclusion is right - HC opined that deceased would have contributed an amount of Rs.16,000/- per month to dependents, whereas Tribunal opined deceased would have contributed an amount of Rs.5,000/-, both Courts below proceeded to arrive at amounts on basis that as a driver of one of buses, he was getting a salary of Rs.3,900/- per month, making a reasonable conjecture that somebody to be employed for purpose of managing business of 3 mini-buses, would certainly demand a higher salary than a driver, it is reasonable to notionally fix salary of such manager at Rs.10,000/- P.M coupled with salary of one driver @ Rs.3,900/- would be loss sustained by family from income arising out of asset - Amount of compensation payable to claimants would be 13,900 x 12 x 16 = Rs.26,68,800/- - Appeal disposed of.
