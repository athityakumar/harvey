Ram Kishun and others v State of Uttar Pradesh and others

Supreme Court of India

24 May 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 409; (2012) 11 SCC 511; AIR 2012 SC 2288; 2012 (6) ALD(SC) 18; 2012 (93) ALR 238; 2012 (5) ALT 39; 2012 (4) AWC 4067; 2012 (4) BC 527; [2012] 173 Comp Cas 105; 2012 (2) DRTC 335; JT 2012 (5) SC 483; 2012 (4) RLW 3635; [2012] 6 S.C.R. 105

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: U.P. Zamindari Abolition and Land Reforms Act, 1952

Summary: Administrative - Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - U.P. Zamindari Abolition and Land Reforms Act, 1952 - Indian Contract Act, 1872, s. 128 - Recovery of Loan - Land Auction - Guarantor - Execution - Sustainability - Father of appellant had stood has guarantor to one of his friend-debtor who had taken agricultural loan, debtor died in 1985 and father of appellants died in 1986 without clearing loan amount and bank initiated recovery proceedings and sent matter to Collector for realisation of loan amount as an arrear of Land Revenue - Collector issue recovery certificate against original debtor and his land was put to auction but could not fetch total amount as such proceedings were initiate against appellants and their father - Appellants raised objections that instead of putting their property to auction, loan amount be recovered from legal heirs of debtors as he had left movable/immovable properties and live-stocks and other assets to meet recovery of bank loan - Objection of appellants was not accepted and their land was put for auction and respondent No. 4 purchased and sale was confirmed with sale certificate issued by Collector and he was put in possession - Appellant raised objection under Act before Commissioner, which was rejected on ground of delay - Appellants appeal before Board of Revenue, but same was dismissed - Aggrieved appellants filed writ petition before HC, but same was dismissed - Aggrieved instant appeal - Held, there could be no dispute to settled legal proposition of law that u/s. 128 of 1872, Act, liability of guarantor/surety was co-extensive with that of debtor, therefore, creditor had a right to obtain a decree against surety and principal debtor - Surety had no right to restrain execution of decree against him until creditor had exhausted his remedy against principal debtor - Surety does not have a right to dictate terms to creditor as how he should make recovery and pursue his remedies against principal debtor at his instance - Total amount of loan sanctioned was Rs.8,425/- Collector issued citation for recovery of Rs.10,574/- on 13-1-1986 and total amount to be recovered including principal amount, interest, collection charges etc. came to Rs.14,483.15P, property of debtor had been sold for a sum of Rs.6,000/- and total amount to be recovered remained about Rs.8,500/- - Appellants' land had been sold for Rs.25,000/i.e., three times amount which was to be recovered, instead of putting this whole land admeasuring 1 bigha and 10 biswas, sale of 1/3rd of this land could have served purpose, therefore, there had been material irregularity in putting entire property to auction - Auctioning authority had received Rs.25,000/ from respondent no.4 as a sale consideration after adjusting outstanding dues of Rs.8,500/-, balance amount of Rs.16,500/ought to have been paid to appellants, nothing on record to show that authorities had ever adopted such a course, hence auction sale stood vitiated and all consequential proceedings were liable to be quashed - Appeal dismissed.
