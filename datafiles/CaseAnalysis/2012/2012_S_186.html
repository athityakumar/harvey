Vasanti Dubey v State Of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

17 January 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Gyan Sudha Misra, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 66; (2012) 2 SCC 731; 2012 CRLJ 1309; 2012 (2) JabLJ 296; JT 2012 (1) SC 321; 2012 (2) MLJ(Crl) 402; 2012 (1) OLR 576; 2012 (1) RCR(Criminal) 588; 2013 (4) RLW 3683; 2012(1) SCALE 355; [2012] 1 S.C.R. 330

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 7 and 13(1)(d) r/w s.13(1)(2) - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 190(c) - Bribe - Closure Report - Reinvestigation - Validity - Appellant, Block Development Officer awarded contract for constructing concrete road to complainant and paid consideration towards full and final settlement of contract - Complainant lodged complaint stating that appellant had demanded bribe for payment of balance amount - Respondents - police registered a case u/ss. 7 and 13(1)(d) r/w s.13(1)(2) of the 1988 Act against appellant investigated the same and submitted closure report - Trial Court rejected closure report and directed respondents to file charge-sheet against appellant - Respondents challenged Trial Court order before HC - Single Judge quashed impugned order and directed Trial Court to take cognizance u/s. 190(c) of CrPC or to direct respondents for further investigation - Trial Court rejected closure report again and directed respondents to obtain sanction for prosecution of appellant - Appellant challenged Trial Court order before HC - HC dismissed revision holding that Trail Court order, did not suffer from any apparent error of jurisdiction - Hence instant appeal - Whether Trial Court could refuse to accept closure report and direct reinvestigation of the case for the second time in order to proceed further although it was confronted with legal impediment indicating lack of sanction for prosecution in the matter - Held, Trial Court committed error of jurisdiction by directing reinvestigation of matter practically for second time in spite of noticing that sanction for prosecution was also lacking, apart from the fact that respondents, after reinvestigation had given its report - Thus, it amounted to sheer abuse of the process of law resulting into vexatious proceeding and harassment of appellant for more than 10 years without discussing any reason why he disagreed with the report of respondents and consequently the closure report which would have emerged if Trial Court had carefully proceeded in accordance with the procedure enumerated for initiation of proceeding under CrPC - Hence, Trial Court order refusing to accept respondents' closure report and consequent HC order that upholding Trial Court order were set aside - Appeal allowed.
