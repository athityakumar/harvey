Supreme Court Bar Association & Ors v B.D. Kaushik

Supreme Court of India

07 May 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Surinder Singh Nijjar

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 502; (2012) 6 SCC 152; AIR 2012 SC (Supp) 655; 2012 (6) AWC 5805; 2012 (3) CompLJ 1; JT 2012 (5) SC 166; [2012] 4 S.C.R. 235; 2012 (2) UPLBEC 1638

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Civil Procedure; Election

Keywords: SCBA Rules

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Practice & Procedure - Societies Registration Act, 1860 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 39 r. 1 and 2, s. 151 - Election - Membership list - Proposed amendments - Implementation committee - Challenged - SC Bar Association (SCBA) was registered under 1860 Act - SCBA received a requisition signed by 343 Members seeking an amendment to r. 18 of SCBA Rules, regarding eligibility of members to contest and vote at an election - A resolution dt.18.2.2003 was passed for the same by a majority of 85% of members present and voting - Thereafter, a resolution dt.3.3.2003 was adopted to hold election of Office Bearers for next session and for constituted election committee of 3 members - In said meeting, a requisition signed by 237 Members of SCBA to recall resolution regarding eligibility of members to contest and vote at an election, but deferred on account of fact that elections was declared - However, on 10.3.2003 it was resolved to constitute an Implementation Committee to implement the Resolution of 'One Bar One Vote', which was adopted - Aggrieved respondents (Advocates) filed two suits challenging validity of resolution dt.18.2.2003 as illegal and filed applications under O. 39 r.1 and 2 r/w s. 151 of CPC, restraining appellant (defendant) form implementing resolution dt.18.2.2003, amending r. 18 of the Rules and Regulations of SCBA, which was allowed - Aggrieved appellant filed instant civil appeals - However, an interlocutory application was filed before SC, for implementation of resolution dt.10.3.2003 through implementation committee to identify members of SCBA who regularly practised in SC, which was allowed on 26.9.2011 - Hence, instant appeals - Whether purported resolutions passed by SCBA were valid - Held, SC by order dt.26.9.2011 observed that to identify regular practitioners in SC, it would be open to Office Bearers of SCBA or a small Committee appointed by SCBA, consisting of 3 senior Advocates, to collect information about those members who had contested elections in any of Court-annexed Bar Associations, such as, HCs and District Courts Bar Associations - Further it was categorically indicated Members of SCBA, whose names did not figure in final list of regular practitioners, would not be entitled to either vote at an election of Office Bearers of SCBA or to contest any of the posts for which elections would be held by SCBA - From facts it was clear that in view of interim injunction order passed in two suits filed respondents restraining SCBA from implementing its resolution dt.18.2.2003, one of major issues which was canvassed was that in connection with holding of elections to Executive Committee of SCBA, one of methods resorted to for the purpose of ensuring a candidate's success in election was to enroll a large number of members to vote for a particular candidate - Same had given rise to a lot of discussion and deliberation which ultimately resulted in amendment of r. 18 regarding eligibility of such members to contest and vote at any election - General Body Meeting was convened to consider implications of judgment dt.26.9.2011, what transpired later was a complete departure therefrom - Members of SCBA present at the meeting were bent upon their own agendas, which were directed against the 3 senior members of Bar, who were appointed as members of Implementation Committee, together with President - That was not a method which should have been resorted to said purpose - Meeting degenerated into a chaotic situation in which various things were done, which were not in accordance with provisions of Rules and Regulations of SCBA, and were against normal rules of decorum and could not be supported, despite attempts made to do so - If any member was aggrieved by actions of any other member and seeks his removal from membership of SCBA, rules provided the manner in which same was to be done and certainly not arbitrarily - It was no doubt true, that some of members were aggrieved by methodology adopted by Implementation Committee for preparing the list of eligible voters for election, but same was done pursuant to directions given by SC in its judgment dt.26.9.2011 - If members were aggrieved by questionnaire which was promulgated, nothing prevented them from approaching SC and asking for modification of contents thereof - It was unable to accept the manner in which purported General Body Meeting of SCBA was conducted on 16.1.2012 and Resolutions adopted therein, some of which the members themselves were unwilling to support - When a judgment was delivered by SC, it was the obligation of all citizens to act in aid thereof and to obey decision and directions contained therein, in view art. 141 of Constitution, until and unless the same were modified or recalled - Resolutions said to have adopted at the purported meeting of General Body of SCBA on 16.1.2012 did not muster scrutiny and should be in violation of art. 141 of Constitution and could not, thus, be countenanced - Apart from fact that agenda for meeting did not include the matters in respect whereof resolutions were adopted, resolutions themselves, being in flagrant violation of judgment delivered by SC on 26.9.2011 - It was duty of all members of SCBA to abide by and to give effect to judgments of SC and not to act in derogation thereof - Purported resolution expelling 3 senior members of Implementation Committee, appointed under directions of SC, from the primary membership of Association, speaks volumes as to the illegality thereof and deliberate and willful attempt on the part of members, who were alleged to have passed such a resolution to over-reach the orders of SC - It was sufficient ground to set aside resolutions purportedly adopted at meeting held on 16.1.2012 - Even if members of SCBA got any grievance against judgment delivered on 26.9.2011, they have to obey the same in scheme of judicial discipline - Implementation Committee should resume work of identification of members of SCBA eligible to vote on instructions that might be given by Executive Committee of SCBA - Impugned resolutions were set aside - Supreme Court Bar Association v. Union of India & Anr, 1998 Indlaw SC 688 and Vinay Balchandra Joshi v. Registrar General of Supreme Court of India, 1998 Indlaw SC 1990, relied on - Order accordingly.
