Kunjumon @ Unni v State Of Kerala

Supreme Court of India

21 November 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Madan B. Lokur, Swatanter Kumar

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 485; (2012) 13 SCC 750; 2013 ALL MR (Cri) 329; JT 2012 (11) SC 381; 2013 (1) RCR(Criminal) 182; 2012(11) SCALE 212; [2012] 9 S.C.R. 1032

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 449, 397 - Murder - Minor Witness - Test Identification Parade - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant was convicted for offence u/ss. 302, 449, 397 of IPC - Prosecution case was that appellant with an intention to commit robbery entered into house of deceased and after robbing her hit her on head due to which she died which was witnessed by grandchild of deceased - Trial court convicted accused, which was concurred by HC, in appeal - Hence instant appeal - Appellant contended that in absence of Test Identification Parade, reliance on her identification of appellant could not be considered safe - Whether conviction of appellant without TIP based on evidence of minor witness was sustainable - Held, considering delay of about 6 years in recording evidence of minor witness, SC, was of opinion that on a reading of her testimony episode did not (understandably) fade away from her memory - It was quite clear from her evidence, absence of any meaningful cross-examination, evidence given by other witnesses and recovery of gold ornaments from possession of appellant that absence of TIP made no difference to case of prosecution or identification of appellant - Hence SC, found no reason to disagree with concurrent view of Trial Court and HC that appellant was guilty of murder, but it would not be proper to convict appellant for an offence punishable u/s. 449 of IPC - Appeal allowed.
