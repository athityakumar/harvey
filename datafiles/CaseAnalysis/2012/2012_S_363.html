Geeta Mehrotra and another v State of Uttar Pradesh and another

Supreme Court of India

17 October 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Gyan Sudha Misra, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 363; (2012) 10 SCC 741; AIR 2013 SC 181; 2012 ALL MR (Cri) 4059; JT 2012 (11) SC 46; 2013 (1) OLR 372; 2012 (4) RCR(Criminal) 812; 2013 (2) RLW 1087; 2012(10) SCALE 299; [2012] 9 S.C.R. 641

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 482 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 323, 498A, 504 and 506 - Dowry Prohibition Act, 1961, ss. 3 and 4 - Dowry - Cruelty - Quashing of FIR - Denied - Challenged - Complainant (Wife) lodged FIR u/ss. 498A, 323, 504 and 506 of IPC r/w. ss. 3/4 of the Act against her husband, father-in-law, mother-in-law, brother-in-law (Appellant No. 1) and sister-in-law (Appellant No. 2) alleging cruelty - Investigating authorities investigate the case and thereafter submitted the charge sheet against the appellants and other family members of the complainant's husband - Appellants filed application u/s. 482 of CrPC for quashing of the charge sheet and the entire proceedings pending in the Trial court on the ground that FIR has been lodged with mala fide intentions to harass the appellants and that no case was made out against the appellants as well as other family members - Further contended that the incident although was alleged to have taken place at Faridabad, however the complainant with mala fide intention in connivance with the father of the complainant, got the investigating officer to record the statements by visiting Ghaziabad which was beyond his territorial jurisdiction and cannot be construed as legal and proper investigation - HC disposed of said application with an observation that the question of territorial jurisdiction cannot be properly decided by the HC for want of adequate facts - It was, therefore, left open to the appellants to move the trial court for dropping the proceedings on the ground of lack of territorial jurisdiction - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order of the HC could be upheld - Held, in so far as the plea of territorial jurisdiction was concerned, it was no doubt true that the HC was correct to the extent that the question of territorial jurisdiction could be decided by the trial court itself - But this ground was just one of the grounds to quash the proceedings initiated against the appellants wherein it was also alleged that no prima facie case was made out against the appellants for initiating the proceedings under the Act and other provisions of the IPC - HC has failed to exercise its jurisdiction as appellants were only brother and sister of the complainant's husband and were not alleged even by the complainant to have demanded dowry from her - There were no allegations against appellants except casual reference of their names who have been included in the FIR - Mere casual reference of the names of the family members in a matrimonial dispute without allegation of active involvement in the matter would not justify taking cognizance against them - FIR does not disclose any material which could be held to be constituting any offence against the appellants - Criminal proceedings initiated against the appellants were set aside - Appeal allowed.
