Ushaben v Kishorbhai Chunilal Talpada And Others

Supreme Court of India

23 March 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjana Prakash Desai, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 103; (2012) 6 SCC 353; 2012 ALL MR (Cri) 2088; 2012 CRLJ 2234; 2012 (5) CTC 354; JT 2012 (3) SC 530; 2012 (2) KLT 415; 2012 (3) MLJ(Crl) 573; 2012 (2) RCR(Criminal) 420; 2012 (2) RLW 1432; 2012(3) SCALE 594; 2012 (2) WLN(SC) 68

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 498A, 506(2), 114 - Dowry Prohibition Act, 1961, ss. 3, 7 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, s. 482 - Bigamy - Quashing - Sustainability - Appellant filed complaint against respondents u/ss. 498A, 494, 506(2) / 114 of IPC and u/ss. 3 and 7 of Act - Respondents filed application u/s. 482 of Code for quashing complaint before HC - HC quashed complaint qua respondent No. 6 to 9 against whom only allegation of bigamy was made - HC ordered deletion of offence u/s. 494 of IPC from complaint and directed that investigation of other offences should proceed - Aggrieved instant appeal - Whether HC was justified in quashing offence charged - Held, if a complaint contained allegations about commission of offence u/s. 498A of IPC which was a cognizable offence, apart from allegations about commission of offence u/s. 494 of IPC, court could take cognizance thereof even on a police report - No fetters could be put on police preventing them from investigating complaint which alleged offence u/s. 498A of IPC and also offence u/s. 494 of IPC - Appeal disposed of.
