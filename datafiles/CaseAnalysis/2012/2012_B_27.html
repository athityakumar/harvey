Bhawna Garg and another v University Of Delhi & Ors

Supreme Court of India

05 September 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Patnaik, Swatanter Kumar

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 283; (2015) 1 SCC 401; AIR 2012 SC 3299; 2012 (6) AWC 6321; JT 2012 (8) SC 618; 2012(8) SCALE 504; [2012] 10 S.C.R. 512; 2012 (3) UPLBEC 2403

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Education

Keywords: Medical Council of India Regulations on Graduate Medical Education, 1997

Summary: Education - Constitution - Medical Council of India Regulations on Graduate Medical Education, 1997, r. 5 - Admission - Reservation of seats - Constitutionality - The Delhi University issued a Bulletin of Information for admissions to the Under-Graduate Degree Courses for the academic session 2011-2012 - It was stated in the bulletin that 30 out of 150 seats in 'X' college and 6 out of 200 seats in 'Y' college in the MBBS course were reserved for Nominees of Govt. of India (NGOI) - Appellants applied as female general category candidates and also took and cleared the Delhi University Medical and Dental Entrance Test (DUMET), however, on account of their lower rank in the merit list of candidates who cleared the DUMET, the appellants could not be admitted to any of the seats in the 3 Govt. Medical colleges under the university - Appellant filed petition contending that the reservation of as many as 30 seats in the MBBS course in 'X' college was violative of art. 14 of the Constitution and that the procedure adopted by the Govt. of India in nominating the candidates for the 30 seats without holding a common entrance test for determination of their merit was contrary to the Regulations - HC held that as the nominations have already been made by the Govt. of India to the 30 seats in 'X' college in the MBBS course and the nominated students have taken admission and were undergoing the course, it may not be appropriate to disturb their admission - Hence, the instant appeals - Whether reservation of as many as 30 seats in the MBBS course in 'X' college was violative of art. 14 of the Constitution - Held, bulletin insofar as it reserves 30 seats in the MBBS course in 'X' college for NGOI was not ultra vires the Constitution and in so far it exempts candidates to be admitted to these 30 seats from taking the DUMET was not ultra vires the Regulations - Moreover, the candidates who had applied for the seats allocated to the beneficiary States/ Union Territories/ Ministries/ Agencies have not approached the Court with their grievance that their merit has been bypassed or that the selection has not been made in accordance with regn. 5 of the Regulations - Instead the appellants who had not applied for the 30 seats reserved in 'X' college for the NGOI have come before SC with their grievance that they ought to have been selected and admitted to some of those 30 seats - Appellants, who have not applied for the 30 seats reserved for the NGOI, could not challenge the selection of the candidates to the 30 seats reserved for the NGOI on the ground that merit as provided in regn. 5 of the Regulations has not been considered while making selection for nomination of these reserved seats - However, even if some of the students may have been selected for admission to the seats reserved for NGOI not on merit as determined strictly in accordance with regn. 5 of the Regulations, SC not inclined to disturb their admissions in exercise of power u/art. 142 of the Constitution - However, if there were vacant seats in the 2 Govt. medical colleges, namely, 'X' college or 'Y' College, for the academic year 2011-2012 out of the quota for NGOI, then the appellants should be given admission to these vacant seats on the basis of their merit in the DUMET 2011-2012 during the academic year 2012-2013 - Appeals disposed of.
