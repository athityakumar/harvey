Jitendra Nath Singh v The Official Liquidator & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

21 September 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Patnaik, Swatanter Kumar

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 324; (2013) 1 SCC 462; AIR 2012 SC (Supp) 956; 2012 (95) ALR 570; 2012 (4) BC 566; [2012] 175 Comp Cas 251; 2013 (1) CompLJ 101; JT 2012 (9) SC 221; 2013 (118) RD 348; 2012(9) SCALE 186; [2012] 13 S.C.R. 339

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Preferential Payments

Summary: Corporate - Practice & Procedure - Companies Act, 1956, ss. 529, 529(1)(c), 529(2), 529A - Provincial Insolvency Act, 1920, s. 2(1)(a) - Secured creditors - Charge - Differences - Entitled to - Company Judge for winding up of a company appointed respondent (Official liquidator) to conduct liquidation proceedings related to said company and to take over assets, books and documents of company - Thereafter, liquidator took over assets of company and sold some of assets of company and paid to secured creditors and workmen towards wages - Aggrieved appellant (one of workmen) filed an application before Company Judge that sold assets were not properties over which banks/financial institutions have any charge and that they could not be treated as secured creditors in respect of those properties and sale proceeds from those properties should be kept separately and be paid to workmen first before disbursing any amount to banks/financial institutions - Company Judge held that u/ss. 529 and 529A of the Act, workmen and secured creditors have pari passu charge over properties of company - Aggrieved appellant filed an appeal before DB and contended that secured creditors have pari passu charge with workmen only on the properties which have offered by company to secured creditors as security - DB by impugned order held that secured creditors have pari passu charge with workmen over all the properties of company u/ss. 529 and 529A of the Act and dismissed the appeal - Hence, instant appeal - Whether workmen and secured creditors have pari passu charge over all properties of insolvent company u/ss. 529 and 529A of the Act - Held, it was clear from s. 529(1)(c) of the Act that in winding up of an insolvent company, respective rights of secured and unsecured creditors of an insolvent company would be same as the respective rights of secured and unsecured creditors with respect to estates of persons adjudged insolvent as were in force under law of insolvency and under the 1920 Act - 1956 Act did not define 'creditor' and 'secured creditor' - In instant case, bank/financial institution, the creditors, did not hold a mortgage, charge or lien on property of company or any part thereof as a security for a debt due to it from company, it was not a secured creditor for purposes of ss. 529 and 529A of Act - U/s. 45 of 1920 Act, unsecured creditor was entitled to receive dividends equally with other creditors, whereas secured creditor had right u/s. 47 of 1920 Act to realize security and to prove for the balance due to him in case on realization of such security he was not able to recover entire amount due to him - Thus, from the provisions of cl. (c) of s. 529(1) of the Act along with the provisions of 1920 Act related to respective rights of secured and unsecured creditors, a secured creditor of an insolvent company which was wound up had only a right over particular property or asset of company offered to secured creditor as a security and unsecured creditors have rights over all other properties or assets of insolvent company - First limb of proviso to s. 529(1) of the Act did not create any pari passu charge in favour of secured creditor over property or asset of company which was not given as security by company to secured creditor - Rather, language of first limb of that proviso made crystal clear that security of every secured creditor created dehors the proviso to s. 529(1) of the Act was statutorily subjected to a pari passu charge in favour of workmen - Cl. (c) of s. 529(1) of the Act did not create a pari passu charge over properties or assets of company which were not offered to secured creditor as security, but to the extent of loss of security suffered by a particular secured creditor because of the statutory charge created in favour of workmen, secured creditor was ranked pari passu with workmen for overriding preferential payment u/s. 529A of the Act - Provision in s. 529(2) of the Act made clear that all creditors, secured and unsecured, of insolvent company were entitled to prove for and receive dividends out of assets of company but secured creditors had option either to relinquish their security in which case they like any unsecured creditor would only be entitled to prove for and receive dividends out of assets of company or to realize security instead of relinquishing the security in which case they have to pay to liquidator only expenses for preservation of security until they realize the security by appropriate proceedings other than winding up proceedings - Impugned order of DB was set aside - Appeal allowed.

Swatanter Kumar, J.

Corporate - Practice & Procedure - Companies Act, 1956, ss. 529, 529(1)(c), 529(2), 529A - Provincial Insolvency Act, 1920, s. 2(1)(a) - Secured creditors - Computation - Portions - Held, situation would be different where secured creditor did not follow the scheme of provisions of s. 47(1) of 1920 Act read in conjunction with ss. 529 and 529A of the Act but puts the sale proceeds in winding up proceedings in a common hotch-potch or even relinquishes the security for general benefit of creditors at large, then creditor would not be entitled to benefit of s. 529A of the At and would stand in line with unsecured creditors of company - Further, where secured creditor was unable to fully realize his dues owing to taking of share from his security towards workmen's portion in terms of proviso to s. 529(1) of the Act, then to the extent specified, secured creditor was entitled to a charge pari passu with workmen's dues for purposes of s. 529A of the Act - Proviso to s. 529(1) of the Act, by a deemed fiction, makes dues of workmen pari passu with that of secured creditors and creates a charge in favour of workmen upon amounts realized from enforcement of such security, to the extent of workmen's portion -Once requirements stated in proviso to s. 529(1) of the Act were satisfied, scheme contemplated u/cl. (c) of proviso to s. 529 r/w s. 529A of the Act would come into play - Court could not overlook the reality that intention of framers of law could not have been that public funds viz. money of secured creditor (like banks), should be completely ignored for the benefit of creditors in general, despite there being a definite protection in law, when the security might be sufficient for recovery of dues of such secured creditors to a limited extent, if not in entirety - Workmen's charges as well as that of secured creditors have to be paid in preference to all others, but with inter se pari passu charge on the amounts realized from the sale of security or otherwise - Secured creditor and dues of workmen in the proportion calculated in terms of s. 529A of the Act were liable to be paid in preference to all other dues but were pari passu inter se - In instant case, secured creditor has realized its security but without putting security or receipts thereof in common hotch potch of winding up proceedings for general benefit of creditors - Thus, in terms of s. 47(1) of 1920 Act, secured creditor was entitled to balance due to it, deducting net amounts realized - If secured creditor would have participated in winding up proceedings in its entirety with security being realised and/or relinquished for general benefit of creditors and not restricted to compliance of s. 529 of the Act, it would not be entitled to the benefit of s. 529A of the Act - Appeal allowed.
