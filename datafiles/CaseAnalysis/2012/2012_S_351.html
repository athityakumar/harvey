Ajay Kumar Parmar v State of Rajasthan

Supreme Court of India

27 September 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 424; (2012) 12 SCC 406; AIR 2013 SC 633; 2012 ALL MR (Cri) 3792; 2013 (1) JCC 652; 2012 (4) RCR(Criminal) 617; [2012] 8 S.C.R. 970

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 376,342 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 161,164,207 - Rape - Statement before Magistrate - Withdrawn - Discharge/Acquittal - Sustainability - Appellant was discharged of offence u/ss. 376,342 of IPC by Judicial Magistrate, which was reversed by Sessions Judge - Appellant was charged with offence u/ss. 376, 342 of IPC, FIR was registered and on application by victim statement u/s. 164 of CrPC was recorded, that FIR lodged by her was false and statement made u/s. 161 of CrPC before police was also false and that no offence was committed by appellant - Police filed chargesheet before Judicial Magistrate, who taking note of her statement u/s. 164 of CrPC, not only passes orders of acquittal but also passed strictures against investigating agency - Aggrieved respondent filed revision before Sessions Judge, who reversed order of Magistrate holding that he had no jurisdiction to discharge/acquit appellant on any ground as he was bound to commit case to Sessions Court which was competent court to deal with issue and also that statement u/s. 164 of CrPC was not worth reliance as victim was not produced before Magistrate - Appellant filed appeal before HC, which affirmed order of Sessions Court - Hence instant appeal - Whether Sessions Court was justified in holding that Magistrate had no jurisdiction to discharge/acquit appellant and also that statement u/s. 164 of CrPC was not recorded correctly - Held, revisional court as well as HC had rightly held that statement u/s. 164 CrPC had not been recorded correctly, and had rightly set aside order of Judicial Magistrate, not taking the cognizance of offence - There was no provision analogous to s. 207-A of old CrPC and Judicial Magistrate, should have committed case to Sessions court as application could be entertained only by Sessions Court, it was not permissible for court to examine weight of defence evidence at that stage, thus, order was insignificant and inconsequential being without jurisdiction - Appeal dismissed.
