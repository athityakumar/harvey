Rampal Singh v State of U.P.

Supreme Court of India

24 July 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Swatanter Kumar, Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 220; (2012) 8 SCC 289; 2012 ALL MR (Cri) 2701; 2012 CRLJ 3765; 2012 (3) JCC 2062; 2012 (3) MLJ(Crl) 665; 2013 (1) RCR(Criminal) 363; 2012(6) SCALE 574; [2012] 7 S.C.R. 160

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302 and 304 Part I - Murder - Conviction - Challenged - Appellant shot at the deceased with his rifle and ran away - Deceased succumbed to that shot and died in the hospital - Charge u/s. 302 of the IPC was framed against the appellant for which he was tried and finally convicted by the Trial Court - On appeal, HC affirmed the judgment of conviction and order of sentence passed by the Trial Court - Hence, the instant appeal - Appellant contended that the even as per the case of the prosecution, taken at its best, the only offence that the appellant could be said to have committed would be that u/s. 304 Part II of IPC and not u/s. 302 of the IPC - Whether contention of the appellant could be accepted and conviction of the appellant u/s. 302 of IPC could be set aside - Held, it was not a case of previous animosity - There was nothing on record to show that the relation between the families of the deceased and the appellant was not cordial - On the contrary, there was evidence that the relations between them were cordial - The dispute between the parties arose with a specific reference to the boundary - It was clear that the appellant had not committed the crime with any pre-meditation - There was no intention on his part to kill - The entire incident happened within a very short span of time - The deceased and the appellant had had an altercation and the appellant was thrown on the ground by the deceased - It was in that state of anger that the appellant went to his house, took out the rifle and shot at the deceased - Appellant actually fired in response to the challenge that was thrown at him by the deceased - It was true that there was knowledge on the part of the appellant that if he used the rifle and shot at the deceased, the possibility of the deceased being killed could not be ruled out - Appellant was a person from the armed forces and was fully aware of consequences of use of fire arms - But this was not necessarily conclusive of the fact that there was intention on the part of the appellant to kill his brother, the deceased - The intention probably was to merely cause bodily injury as appellant fired one shot at deceased and ran away - Hence, SC altered the offence that the appellant has been held guilty of, from that u/s. 302 of the IPC to the one u/s. 304 Part I of the IPC - Appeal disposed of.
