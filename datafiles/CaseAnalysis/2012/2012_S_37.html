Saroj Screens Pvt. Ltd. v Ghanshyam And Others

Supreme Court of India

26 March 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 110; (2012) 11 SCC 434; AIR 2012 SC 1649; 2012 (3) Bom.C.R. 565; JT 2012 (3) SC 575; 2012(4) SCALE 25; [2012] 5 S.C.R. 141

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: City of Nagpur Corporation Act, 1948

Summary: Land & Property - Municipalities & Local Governments - Constitution - City of Nagpur Corporation Act, 1948, s. 70(5) - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 116 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Public Property - Transfer by way of lease - Violation of art. 14 - - Respondent Corporation under a resolution dt. 17-3-1944 granted lease rights of a plot to 'Y' and executed a lease deed dt. 28-10-1944 for a period of 30 years and during partition among family of 'Y', lease rights of said plot was fall to share of wife of 'Y' - Wife of 'Y' assigned said lease rights in favour of one 'X' - Meanwhile, 'Y' by agreement dt. 10-9-1947 transferred all the rights, interest and renewal of lease right in favour of appellant - Appellant was provided with an option to acquire interest of 'Y' on payment of price and purchase all rights of 'Y' from Committee - Thereafter, appellant addressed and expressed to 'X' ready to pay and purchase the interest created in favour of predecessors of respondents - Thereafter, 'X' approached Corporation and sought renewal of lease and appellant filed a suit against 'Y', 'X', Corporation for specific performance agreement dt. 10-9-1947 - Suit of appellant was decreed by Trial Court and on 1st appeal preferred by heirs of 'X'(respondent Nos. 1 and 2), HC reversed Trial Court decree - Pending of said suit, Corporation passed another resolution dt. 28-8-1991 and executed a lease deed dt. 4-9-1991 in favour of appellant - Aggrieved respondent Nos. 1 and 2 filed a writ petition - Pending writ petition Corporation passed another resolution dt. 22-7-1996, stating that lease granted in favour of appellant was without previous sanction of State Govt. - Thereafter, State Govt. accorded sanction for grant of lease to appellant and communicated to Corporation - Aggrieved respondent Nos. 1 and 2 filed another writ petition stating decision of State Govt. and Corporation had violated art. 14 of Constitution, since public property was transferred to appellant without conducting auction or inviting tenders - DB held that during subsistence of resolution dt. 29-10-1975, Corporation could not have granted lease in favour of appellants and State Govt. had no right to validate such grant - (A)Whether HC erred by quashing resolution dt. 28-8-1991 passed by Corporation and sanction accorded by State Govt. u/s. 70(5) of 1948 Act - Held, resolution dt. 29-10-1975 was passed by Corporation for renewal of lease in favour of 'X' for a period of 30 years was not cancelled or rescinded and during subsistence of that resolution, neither Corporation could have renewed the lease in favour of appellant nor State Govt. could have granted sanction u/s. 70(5) of 1948 Act for such renewal - Corporation did not obtain sanction from State Govt. prior to passing resolution for renewal of lease in favour of appellant - Appellant took advantage of possession of plot in question, had induced functionaries of Corporation to enter into a clandestine compromise for forwarding a proposal to State Govt. to grant post facto sanction for renewal of lease in its favour and latter State accorded sanction without realizing that alienation of any right or interest in a public property in favour of any person without following a procedure consistent with doctrine of equality was impermissible - Thus, resolution passed by Corporation for renewal of lease in favour of appellant and the consequential action took for execution of lease deed dt. 4-9-1991 were ex facie illegal and HC did not commit any error by quashing the same - Under constitutional scheme compliance of doctrine of equality enshrined in art. 14 of Constitution was to be read as a condition precedent for exercise of power by State Govt. and Corporation, when it related to alienation of public property or any right or interest therein - Corporation holds the property as a trustee of public and any alienation of such property or any right or interest therein otherwise than by way of auction or by inviting bids would amount to breach of that trust - (B)Whether respondent Nos. 1 and 2 were entitled to claim any right or interest in the plot - Held, though lease granted to 'Y' was renewed in favour of 'X' under resolution dt. 29-10-1975, respondent Nos. 1 and 2 could not derive any benefit from said renewal merely because Corporation did not cancel or rescind resolution - Respondent Nos. 1 and 2 did not plead nor did produce any material before HC to show that 'X' took any action in furtherance of resolution dt. 29-10-1975 and fresh lease deed was executed in his favour and it was only pleaded that 'X' filed an appeal u/s. 397(3) r/w s. 411 of 1948 Act against increase in ground rent and imposition of penalty - Therefore, resolution dt. 29-10-1975 had become redundant and could no longer be relied upon by respondent Nos.1 and 2 for claiming any right or interest in plot - (C) Whether Corporation was bound to execute lease deed in favour of respondent Nos. 1 and 2 - Held, Corporation was not bound to renew lease in favour of respondent Nos. 1 and 2 u/s. 116 of 1882 Act, since, plot in question did not remain in their possession through appellant - No evidence was produced before HC to show that appellant was continuing in possession with consent of 'X' or respondent Nos. 1 and 2 - Though, initial lease was granted to 'Y' before coming into force of Constitution, while considering issue of renewal of lease, Corporation was duty bound to take action and decision strictly in consonance with constitutional principles and decision to renew lease in favour of 'X' was not taken after following a procedure consistent with equality clause - Appellant should hand over possession of plot to Corporation - Appeals dismissed.
