Central Bureau of Investigation v Amitbhai Anil Chandra Shah and another

Supreme Court of India

27 September 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Aftab Alam, Ranjana Prakash Desai

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 618; (2012) 10 SCC 545; [2012] 8 S.C.R. 945

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Cancellation of bail - Fair trial - Encounter - Transfer of case - Sustainability - CBI was directed by SC to investigate case relating to killings of Sohrabuddin and his wife - State, however, continued to maintain that killing of Tulsiram Prajapati in police encounter had no connection with killings of Sohrabuddin and his wife - Further, police did not investigate any linkages between killings of Sohrabuddin and his wife on one hand and the killing of Tulsiram Prajapati on other - CBI took up investigation into Sohrabuddin case after instituting a fresh FIR on 1-2-2010 - In charge-sheet submitted by CBI, one of accused was Amitbhai Shah, who till then was minister of State for Home in State Govt. - Amitbhai Shah was arrested and was sent to judicial custody - CBI was under obligation to investigate Prajapati case as well, as it prima facie appeared to be integrally connected with Sohrabuddin case - Police, would neither hand over records of Prajapati case to CBI nor allow it to make any independent investigation in Prajapati case - Prajapati was indeed killed in a fake encounter but there was no attempt to investigate any larger conspiracy or to try to connect it with Sohrabuddin case - Meanwhile, Amitbhai Shah was granted bail by HC - Aggrieved, CBI immediately filed instant appeal seeking cancellation of bail granted to Amitbhai Shah - CBI filed another petition for transfer of Sohrabuddin case pending in Trial Court to CBI in Mumbai or any other State and for a further direction for Constitution of a special Court - Held, SC was not inclined to cancel bail granted to Amitbhai Shah about 2 years ago - SC felt reluctant to deprive Amitbhai Shah of privilege granted to him by HC - However, apprehension expressed by CBI that Amitbhai Shah might misuse freedom and try to subvert prosecution could not be lightly brushed aside - Thus, grant of bail to Amitbhai Shah in Sohrabuddin case should have no effect in Prajapati case and in that case whether Amitbhai Shah was to be kept in judicial custody or granted bail would be decided by Court on basis of materials on record of that case and without taking into consideration grant of bail to him in Sohrabuddin case - In case Amitbhai Shah commits any breach of conditions of bail bond or undertaking given to Court, as directed above, it would be open to CBI to move Trial Court for cancellation of his bail - Decision to transfer case was not a reflection on State judiciary and it was made clear that SC reposes full trust in judiciary of State - Hence, Sohrabuddin case stands transferred to Mumbai - It was case of CBI that case of Sohrabuddin and case of Tulsiram Prajapati were closely connected and in order to avoid any miscarriage of justice, both cases could only be tried before same Court - Appeal dismissed.
