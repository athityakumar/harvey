Munna Kumar Upadhyaya @ Munna Upadhyaya v State of Andhra Pradesh through Public Prosecutor, Hyderabad, Andhra Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

08 May 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Swatanter Kumar, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 231; (2012) 6 SCC 174; AIR 2012 SC 2470; 2012 CRLJ 3068; JT 2012 (5) SC 129; 2012 (3) MLJ(Crl) 233; 2012 (2) RCR(Criminal) 962; 2012(5) SCALE 501; [2012] 6 S.C.R. 611

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 201, 302, 411 and 435 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 313 - Murder - Conviction - Challenged - Accused Nos. 1 to 4 allegedly murdered deceased persons and burnt their bodies - Trial court convicted accused nos. 1 to 4 u/ss. 302 r/w. s. 34, 201, 435 and 411 of IPC - On appeal, HC acquitted accused Nos. 3 and 4 of all offences - However, HC affirmed the conviction of accused Nos. 1 and 2 u/ss. 302 r/w. s. 34, 201, 435 and 411 of IPC - Hence, the instant appeal - Accused No. 1 contended that the case being one of circumstantial evidence, the entire evidence was of very weak nature - The prosecution has not been able to establish the chain of circumstances which undoubtedly points only towards the guilt of the accused - Whether impugned order of the HC could be upheld - Held, it was clear that the prosecution had been able to comprehensively and reliably establish the chain of circumstances - In the instant case, the evidence produced on record does not leave any major loopholes in the case of the prosecution - Most importantly, the recovery of incriminating articles, cash and jewellery belonging to the deceased, the finger prints of the accused and the false stories given by the accused No. 1 to different persons who came to the bungalow of the deceased to ensure that none of them enter the house of the deceased stand unequivocally established - Besides all this circumstantial evidence, another very significant aspect of the case was that none of the accused, particularly accused No.2, offered any explanation during the recording of their statements u/s. 313 CrPC - It was not even disputed that the material incriminating evidence was put to accused No. 2 while his statement u/s. 313 CrPC was recorded - Except for a vague denial, he stated nothing more - It was expected of the accused No. 2 to render proper explanation for his injuries and his conduct - However, he opted to deny the same and in fact even gave false replies to the questions posed to him - The Court not only draws an adverse inference, but such conduct of the accused No. 2 would also tilt the case in favour of the prosecution - No infirmity in the judgments under appeal - Appeal dismissed.
