T.N. Godavarman Thirumulpad v Union Of India & Others

Supreme Court of India

13 February 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan, Chandramauli Kumar Prasad

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 613; 2012 (4) ALD(SC) 47; JT 2012 (2) SC 379; [2012] 3 S.C.R. 460

Case Digest

Subject: Environment, Wildlife & Animal

Keywords: Endangered Species, National wildlife action plan, Centrally Sponsored Scheme, 2009

Summary: Environment, Wildlife & Animal - Constitution - Administrative - Wild Life (Protection) Act, 1972, ss. 8, 9, 2(27) - Environmental Protection Act, 1986, ss. 3 and 5 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 48A, 51A(g) - Asiatic Wild Buffalo - Wildlife Sanctuary - Protection - Directions - Instant applications were filed to directed UOI and State Govts. to prepare a rescue plan to save Wild Buffalo, an endangered species from extinction and to make funds and resources available for said purpose and that to ensure the genetic purity of wild species was maintained and to scientifically monitor wild Buffalos and its habitat be made on long term basis by involving institutes - Whether Asiatic Wild Buffalo was an endangered species and required to be protected from its extinction - Held, State Govt. stated that Centrally Sponsored Scheme of 2009 (CSS) titled 'Integrated Development of Wildlife Habitats' incorporated additional components and activities for implementing the provisions of the Act, National Wildlife Action Plan (2002-2016), recommendations of Tiger Task Force, 2005, and National Forest Commission, 2006 and the necessities felt from time to time for conservation of wildlife and biodiversity in country - However, in 2003, the Act was amended and 2 more categories of Protected Areas (PAs) i.e Conservation Reserves and Community Reserves were recognized - U/art. 48A of Constitution imposed a duty on State to protect and improve environment and to safeguard forest and wildlife of country - U/art. 51A(g) of Constitution it was duty of every citizen to protect and improve natural environment including wildlife and to have compassion for living creatures - Human-wildlife conflict was fast becoming a critical threat to survival of many endangered species, like wild buffalo, elephants, tiger, lion etc. such conflicts affect not only its population but also has broadened environmental impacts on ecosystem equilibrium and biodiversity conservation - There was likelihood of anthropocentric bias towards man, and rights of wild animals often tend to be of secondary importance but in universe man and animal were equally placed, but human rights approach to environmental protection in case of conflict, was often based on anthropocentricity - Man-animal conflict often results not because animals encroach human territories but vice-versa - Endangered not because of natural causes alone but because man failed to preserve and protect them - Often, it was said such conflicts was due human population growth, land use transformation, species habitat loss, degradation and fragmentation, increase in eco-tourism, access to natural reserves, increase in livestock population - State has to take steps to remove encroachments and, if necessary, could cancel 'patta' already granted and initiate acquisition proceedings to preserve and protect wildlife and its corridors - Areas outside PAs was reported to have maximum number of man-animal conflict, they fall prey to poachers easily, and often invite ire of cultivators when they cause damage to their crops - Those issues had to be scientifically manage so as to preserve and protect endangered species, like wild buffalo and other species included in Sch. 1 Part 1 of the Act and other species which face extinction as well - Environmental justice could be achieved only if we drift away from the principle of anthropocentric to Eco-centric - Conservation and Management of Wildlife, as per the Act was primarily vested in States/UTs who were in physical possession of area - Wild buffalo was included as Item No. 41, Part I of Sch. I of the Act - Once it was included in Sch. I, State Board for Wildlife had to advise State Govt. in selection and management of areas to be declared as protected areas, in formulation of policy for protection and conservation of wildlife etc., as per s. 8 of the Act - S. 9 of the Act stated that no person should hunt any wild animal specified in Sch. I to IV of the Act, except as provided u/ss. 11 and 12 of the Act - When wild buffalos' eco-system was not maintained, they lose their home and either forced to adopt new surroundings or human habitat - State of Chhattisgarh was directed to give effect fully Centrally Sponsored Scheme 'Integrated Development of Wildlife Habitats', to save wild buffalo from extinction - To take immediate steps to ensure that interbreeding between wild and domestic buffalos did not take place and genetic purity of wild species was maintained - Take immediate steps to undertake intensive research and monitor wild buffalo population in 'Udanti Wildlife Sanctuary and other areas, where wild buffalo, including preparing them their genetic profile for future reference - To initiate wildlife training programs for officials of State Forest Dept., especially for managing said sanctuary and other areas where wild buffalos were found - To submit Annual Plan of Operations to Central Govt. detailing the proposed course of action and effective steps should be taken by State to protect Asian wild buffalo - Applications disposed of.
