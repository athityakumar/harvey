Ex-Hav. Satbir Singh v Chief of the Army Staff, New Delhi and another

Supreme Court of India

09 November 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, Ranjan Gogoi

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 423; (2013) 1 SCC 390; 2013 (1) AWC 1; 2013 (136) FLR 131; JT 2012 (11) SC 181; 2012(11) SCALE 79; [2012] 9 S.C.R. 1001; 2013 (1) SLR 753

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces; Service

Summary: Service - Termination - Discharge - Allowance - Intervening Period - Eligibility - Sustainable - Appellant herein was enrolled in the Army on 31.08.1982. In September, 1985, he was promoted to rank of Havildar and his tenure of service was extended to 24 years - Army Headquarters, Adjutant General Branch issued a letter dated 28.12.1988, laying down the procedure for removal of undesirable and inefficient candidates by way of discharge/dismissal - Appellant in his service record had 4 Red Links Entries in his twelve and half years' service and was discharged from service - Appellant challenged before HC, praying for reinstatement of service with all consequential benefits - HC, set aside discharge and directed respondents to reinstate appellant in service with no benefit of salary and other allowances for intervening period - Aggrieved appellant filed review, which was dismissed - Aggrieved instant appeal - Whether HC, was justified in passing orders with no benefit of salary and other allowances for intervening period - Held, discharge/termination was legally unsustainable, SC, was of view that incumbent appellant, ought to have been provided relief at least to extent of counting intervening period for purpose of terminal benefits - It was true that during intervening period, appellant, did not work, DB was justified in disallowing salary for said period, however, for terminal benefits, in view of categorical conclusion of HC that discharge/termination was bad, ought to had issued a direction for counting intervening period at least for purpose of terminal benefits - DB, held conduct of appellant, securing 4 Red Ink Entries in service record as reason for not considering intervening period even for purpose of terminal benefits, which as per SC, could not be sustained in view of its own authoritative conclusion in setting aside discharge/termination order - Appeals allowed.
