Rohitash Kumar and others v Om Prakash Sharma and others

Supreme Court of India

06 November 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 414; (2013) 11 SCC 451; AIR 2013 SC 30; 2013 (1) ALD(SC) 135; 2013 (96) ALR 825; 2013 (2) AWC 1245; 2012 (4) CLT(SC) 325; 2013 (136) FLR 92; JT 2012 (11) SC 219; 2012(11) SCALE 30; [2012] 13 S.C.R. 47; 2013 (1) SLR 744; 2012 (4) UPLBEC 2920

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Border Security Force (Seniority, Promotion and Superannuation of Officers) Rules, 1978

Summary: Defence & Security Forces - Practice & Procedure - Seniority List - Selection Process - Sustainability - Appellants and contestant respondents were Assistant Commandants in Border Security Force - Union of India issued seniority list placing respondent No. 1 below all officer of batch No. 17 - Respondent No. 1 challenged seniority list on ground that officers who had joined training after him could not be ranked higher than him in seniority list before HC - SJ, allowed writ petition of respondent No. 1 and held respondent No. 1 entitled to be ranked in seniority above officers of batch No. 17 - UOI, challenged judgment of SJ, before DB, which was dismissed - Appellants who were not made party before HC, sought permission and filed SLP - Hence this appeals - Appellants contended that if training had been given in two separate batches, for administrative reasons i.e. police verification, medical examination etc., it could not be accorded different seniority by bifurcating them into two or more separate batches - Held, there was no ambiguity, validity of rule was not under challenge, it was not permissible for court to interpret rule otherwise - Proviso would have application only in a case where officers who had been selected in pursuance of same selection process were split into separate batches, interpreting rule otherwise, would amount to adding words to proviso, which law does not permit - Appellants (17th batch), joined training on 2-7-1993 and their claim was to fix their seniority from 1-2-1993 i.e. date on which, 16th batch joined training, such a course was not permissible in law, facts and circumstances of case neither require any interpretation, nor reading down of rule - Appeal dismissed.
