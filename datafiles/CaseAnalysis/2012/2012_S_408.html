Oriental Insurance Company Limited and another v Surendra Nath Loomba and another

Supreme Court of India

20 November 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 465; (2012) 13 SCC 792; 2012 (4) ACC 786; 2013 ACJ 321; AIR 2013 SC 483; 2013 (1) ALD(SC) 101; 2013 (96) ALR 199; JT 2012 (11) SC 271; 2012 (8) MLJ(SC) 694; 2013 (170) PLR 17; 2013 (1) RCR(Civil) 603; 2012(11) SCALE 134; [2012] 9 S.C.R. 1007

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Insurance - Companies Act, 1956 - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 - Accident - Injuries - Claim Compensation - Comprehensive Policy - Owner indemnity - Act Policy - Maintainability - Claimant-respondent No. 2 was travelling in car belonging to respondent No. 3 driven by respondent No. 2, when vehicle dashed against tree and smashed windscreen and its pieces got inserted into eyes of claimant due to which he lost both his eyes - Claimant was working as Senior Manager in Bank drawing salary of 18,949,86, filed claim compensation application u/s. 166 of Act for an amount of Rs. 62,00,000 with interest - Insurance resisted claim on ground that driver of vehicle did not have valid driving licence and that even if injuries were proven insurance was not liable to indemnify owner as claimant was travelling as a gratuitous passenger - Tribunal held that as insurer had issued Certificate of Insurance which was valid during period when accident occurred, and that opposite party No. 1 had a valid driving licence and accident had occurred and there was no collusion between parties, held victim was entitled to get a total sum of Rs.20,97,984 - Aggrieved insurance filed appeal before HC, which reduced compensation to Rs 16,42,656 - Hence instant appeals by insurance and claimant - Whether Tribunal was justified in awarding compensation claim amount - Held, policy had not been brought on record, appellant insurer would submit that it was an 'Act Policy' - When Certificate of Insurance was filed but policy was not brought on record it only conveys that vehicle was insured, nature of policy could not be discerned from same, thus, SC, was disposed to think that it would be appropriate to remit matter to tribunal to enable insurer to produce policy and grant liberty to parties to file additional documents and also lead further evidence as advised, hence appeals preferred by insurer, were allowed and award was set aside and matter was remitted to tribunal and appeals preferred by claimant for enhancement of compensation were dismissed - Appeals disposed of.
