Deepak Kumar Etc. v State Of Haryana And Others Etc.

Supreme Court of India

27 February 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan, Chandramauli Kumar Prasad

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 74; (2012) 4 SCC 629; AIR 2012 SC 1386; JT 2012 (3) SC 313; 2012 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1145; 2012 (2) RCR(Civil) 473; 2012(3) SCALE 96; [2012] 4 S.C.R. 819

Case Digest

Subject: Environment, Wildlife & Animal; Mines & Minerals

Keywords: Degradation, Extraction, Minor Minerals Conservation and Development Rules, 2010

Summary: Mines & Minerals - Environment, Wildlife & Animal - Mining -Clearance from Ministry of Environment and Forest (MoEF) Requirement - Department of Mines and Geology, issued an auction notice proposing to auction extraction of minor mineral boulder, gravel and sand quarries of an area not exceeding 4.5 hectares - Petitioners challenged validity of auction notices, apart from complaint of illegal mining in States - Court directed Central Empowered Committee (CEC) to make a local inspection with intimation to MoEF, State with regard to alleged illegal mining and submit report - CEC submitted report stating that auction notice referred to mining leases of less than 5 hectares and hence no environmental clearance ought to be obtained as per MoEF notification - Petitioners contended that CEC report was silent about those aspects and also whether 1 km. distance had been maintained between mining blocks of less than 5 hectares, and no Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA) had ever taken place in areas earmarked for mining especially on river beds - State contended that it had taken adequate and effective precautions to maintain 1 km. separation between mining blocks of less than 5 hectares each and that auction notice imposed strict restrictions on quarrying in the river beds - MoEF contended that grant or allotment of mining licence/lease of smaller plots of less than five hectares should not be encouraged from environmental point of view and that applicability of EIA notification of 2006, had to be seen in its letter and spirit so as to ensure environmental safeguards in place and implemented for sustainable mining - Whether auction notice issued without clearance from MoEF was sustainable - Held, it was highly necessary to have an effective framework of mining plan which would take care of all environmental issues and also evolve a long term rational and sustainable use of natural resource base and also bio-assessment protocol - Sand mining should have an adverse effect on bio-diversity as loss of habitat caused by sand mining would effect various species, flora and fauna and it would also destabilize soil structure of river banks and often leave isolated islands - Taking note of those technical, scientific and environmental matters, MoEF, Government of India, issued various recommendations which had not been implemented by State before issuing auction notice - Therefore, direction issued to States, UTs, MoEF to give effect to recommendations made by MoEF, within a period of six months and submit their compliance reports - Meanwhile, order that leases of minor mineral including their renewal for an area of less than five hectares be granted by States/Union Territories only after getting environmental clearance from MoEF - Order accordingly.
