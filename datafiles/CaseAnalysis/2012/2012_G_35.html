Gurpal Singh v High Court Of Judicature For Rajasthan

Supreme Court of India

27 November 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Surinder Singh Nijjar, H.L. Gokhale

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 487; (2012) 13 SCC 94; 2013 (136) FLR 230; JT 2012 (11) SC 557; 2012(11) SCALE 390; [2012] 11 S.C.R. 126; 2013 (1) SLR 417; 2012 (4) WLN(SC) 100

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: 1951 Rules

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Practice & Procedure - Rajasthan Service Rules, 1951, r. 54 - Judicial Service - Suspension - Departmental Proceedings - Acquittal - Full Court Resolution - Sustainability - Petitioner was selected and appointed for Judicial Service and joined as Judicial Magistrate First Class, and it was remarked that his behaviour with members of local Bar was not on best terms which led to his transfer - Dead body of local advocate was found on railway track and wife of deceased complaint that petitioner had demanded 1 lakh for exercising his influence in securing deceased as member of Board of Revenue and when after receiving money by petitioner deceased was not appointed, deceased demanded money from petitioner and petitioner agreeing to return money called deceased she suspected foul play by petitioner - FIR was booked and petitioner was formerly arrested and was placed under suspension - Trial ended in acquittal of petitioner and appeal before HC, was also dismissed and petitioner was acquitted - Petitioner submitted joining report which was kept under consideration of HC and instead of revoking suspension disciplinary proceedings was initiated against petitioner - Petition filed Writ petition before SC, u/art. 32 of Constitution, seeking immediate revocation of his suspension - HC, completed enquiry and petitioner was exonerated and was reinstated with immediate effect by order dt. 26-3-2008 - Petitioner retired and on basis of resolution passed by Full Court it was resolved that suspension of petition shall be treated as a period spent on duty, but without salary except subsistence allowances already paid to him, however, this would not effect (sic) his pensionary benefits but he would not be entitled for any promotion - Aggrieved petitioner sought for amendment to his original writ and sought for declaration to declare order dt. 24-1-2009 as void and his suspension void-ab-initio and that petitioner was entitled to all benefits for period of suspension from 20-12-1985 till 26-3-2008 - Whether petitioner was entitled for all benefits and whether suspension or petitioner was to be declared void-ab-initio - Held, it would be unjust to deny salary to petitioner with effect from date appeal against acquittal was dismissed by HC, there was no cogent reason as to why it was necessary to continue suspension of petitioner during pendency of departmental proceedings - There was no distinction between facts or evidence relied upon in criminal trial as well as department proceedings though petitioner had been acquitted of any involvement in crime of murder - Whilst exercising its jurisdiction under r. 54, it was necessary for HC to pass a detailed and reasoned order as to whether period of suspension was wholly unjustified - Upon acquittal of petitioner from criminal charges, it was no longer necessary to keep him under suspension during pendency of departmental enquiry - HC, failed to exercise its jurisdiction properly u/r. 54, as directed by this Court hence, suspension of petitioner ought to have been revoked upon acquittal by HC even during pendency of departmental enquiry - HC erred in directing in Full Court Resolution, and communication dt. 24-1-2009 that petitioner shall not be entitled for any promotion - SC, held that petitioner would be entitled to all consequential benefits, such as salary and other allowances by treating him on duty with effect from date appeal against acquittal was dismissed by HC and after fixing his last pay drawn correctly - Petition disposed of.
