Satyajit Ballulbhai Desai and others v State of Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

20 July 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Gyan Sudha Misra, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 638; (2014) 14 SCC 434; JT 2014 (1) SC 344; 2014 (4) RCR(Criminal) 548; [2013] 5 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 167, 167(1), 167(2) and 57 - Grant of police remand - Challenged - Appellants assailed the judgment and order of the HC whereby the Single Judge upheld the order passed by the Magistrate permitting police remand of the appellants for 3 days for their interrogation in complaint - Appellants contended that the order granting police remand of the appellants were not based on valid or justifiable reason on the part of the investigating agency and hence the same encroaches on the personal liberty of the appellants as the appellants have never tried to scuttle the investigation justifying police remand - It was further submitted that the grant of police remand was an exception and not the rule and therefore the investigating agency was required to make a strong case for taking police custody of the appellants in order to undertake further investigation and only in that event police custody would be justified - Whether order of the HC could be upheld -

Held, correct course for the investigating authorities seeking police remand of an accused who had been granted bail by the HC, should have been to approach the HC as power of the magistrate to grant police remand after the accused had been granted bail by the HC, would cease to exist and any direction to that effect can be permitted by the HC only in view of the fact that the HC considered it just and appropriate to enlarge the accused on bail and the magistrate could not be permitted to over-ride the order of bail even if it be for a brief period of few days - This was the only appropriate course considering the strict legal provisions in the CrPC wherein the Legislature had earmarked 24 hours minus the period of transportation of the accused from police station to the magistrate as the maximum period of police custody during the initial stage and not more than 15 days by order of the Judicial Magistrate clearly was an indication that police custody could not be permitted without adherence to strict judicial scrutiny from which it was obvious that it could not be allowed without assigning clear and cogent reason for enhancement of the period of police remand and the same would all the more be essential when police remand was sought for an accused who had been enlarged on bail by the HC - Inference was thus candid and clear that police remand of the accused - more so, who had been enlarged on bail could not be granted for an undisclosed or a flimsy reason - HC as also the Judicial Magistrate were not legally justified in permitting the police remand of the appellants even for 3 days in the wake of the existing facts and features of the matter narrated - Appeal allowed.
