Cox and Kings Limited v Indian Rly. Catering and Tourism Corporation Limited and another

Supreme Court of India

05 July 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, J. Chelameswar

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 182; (2012) 7 SCC 587; AIR 2012 SC 3391; 2012 (5) AWC 4848; 2012 (3) CLT(SC) 88; 2012 (3) CompLJ 337; 2013 (1) RCR(Civil) 710; 2012(6) SCALE 184; [2012] 7 S.C.R. 19

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Carriers & Transportation; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Solicitor

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Contract & Commercial - Carriers & Transportation - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, s. 9 - Termination of agreement - Challenged - The Ministry of Railways approved the proposal submitted by the Indian Railway Catering and Tourism Corporation Ltd. (IRCTC), for operating a Luxury Tourist Train within India - Petitioner came to be selected as the Joint Venture shareholder for the operation of the Luxury Tourist Train Project - Respondent (IRCTC) executed a Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) with the petitioner, stating that the Ministry of Railways had given the permission to the respondent to own and operate the Luxury Tourist Train for the exclusive use of the Joint Venture Company for a period of 15 years - Certain disputes arose between the shareholders, which ultimately resulted in the termination of the lease arrangement by the respondent - On account of such termination of the lease agreement, the petitioner initiated a proceeding u/s. 9 of Act, for staying the termination of the lease agreement and also to allow the arrangements to continue till the month of April, 2012 - Respondent contended that since the relationship between the Joint Venture Company and the respondent had been terminated, the petitioner was trying to create a right in its favour for operating the train, which was never in its individual possession - Single Judge found it fit to appoint a Receiver, as an interim measure, in the public interest, to prevent discontinuation of the running of the train for which bookings had already been made - The DB set aside the arrangements made by the Single Judge and allowed the appeal preferred by the respondent - Hence, the instant petitions - Whether DB rightly upheld the termination of agreement by respondent - Held, the arrangement between the respondent was with the petitioner and, although, it was the intention of the parties by virtue of the Joint Venture Agreement that the luxury train, belonging to the respondent, was to be operated by the Joint Venture Company, at least for a minimum period of 15 years, what ultimately transpired was the termination of the Agreement by the respondent of the Joint Venture Company - Petitioner was not entitled to question such termination as by itself it had no existence as far as the running of the train was concerned and it was not a party to the proceedings - No doubt that the petitioner has invested large sums of money in the project, but that cannot entitle it to pray for and obtain a mandatory order of injunction to operate the train once the lease agreement had been terminated - SC accepted the suggestions which had been made by respondent regarding the operation of the train by respondent with liberty to the parties to appoint an Arbitral Tribunal to settle their disputes - Petitions dismissed.
