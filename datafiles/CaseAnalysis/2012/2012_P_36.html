Price Waterhouse Coopers Private Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax, Kolkata-I and another

Supreme Court of India

25 September 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Madan B. Lokur, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 349; [2012] 348 ITR 306; JT 2012 (10) SC 523; 2012 (7) MLJ(SC) 883; 2012(9) SCALE 484; [2012] 8 S.C.R. 849; [2012] 211 TAXMAN 40

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 139(6)(A), 271(1)(c), 44AB - A/y 2000-2001 -Income Tax Rules, 1962, r. 6G(2) - Computation error - Penalty - Challenged - Appellant (company/assesse) engaged in multi-disciplinary management consultancy service - Assesse filed its return of income for A/y 2000-2001 u/s. 139(6) r/w s. 139(6A) of the Act - As required u/s. 139 (6A) of the Act, assesse filed its tax audit report u/s. 44AB of the Act - Thereafter, assessment was reopened and re-assessment was passed and assesse then paid tax due and interest thereon - However, AO initiated penalty proceeding u/s. 271(1)(c) of the Act and saddled the assesse with penalty at 300% on tax sought to be evaded by furnishing inaccurate particulars - Aggrieved assesse filed appeal before Commissioner, which was rejected and on appeal Tribunal upheld order of Commissioner - Aggrieved assesse filed appeal u/s. 260-A of the Act before HC, which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal filed u/s. 260(A) of the Act - Whether appellant/assesse was liable to penalty - Held, tax audit report was filed along with return and that it unequivocally stated that provision for payment was not allowable u/s. 40A(7) of the Act indicated that assessee made a computation error in its return of income - AO, who framed assessment order, made a mistake in overlooking contents of tax audit report - Contents of tax audit report suggested that there was no question of assesse concealing its income and furnished any inaccurate particulars - In instant case, a bona fide and inadvertent error was happened, while assesse submitting its return, who failed to add provision for gratuity to its total income - Said mistake could described as a human error - Absence of due care did not mean that assesse was guilty of either furnishing inaccurate particulars or attempting to conceal its income - Impugned imposition of penalty was not justified - Impugned orders were set aside - Appeal allowed.
