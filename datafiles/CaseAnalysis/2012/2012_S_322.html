State of Maharashtra and others v Saeed Sohail Sheikh

Supreme Court of India

02 November 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 392; (2012) 13 SCC 192; AIR 2013 SC 168; 2013 CRLJ 214; JT 2012 (11) SC 230; 2012 (4) MLJ(Crl) 819; 2013 (1) RCR(Criminal) 200; 2013 (2) RLW 1136; 2012(10) SCALE 660; [2012] 11 S.C.R. 916

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Bombay Land Requisition Ordinance, 1947, Maharashtra Prison (Discipline) Rules, 1963

Summary: (A)Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Administrative - Maharashtra Control of Orgnaised Crime Act, 1999 - Discipline Rules, 1963 - Prisoners Act, 1900, s. 29 - Prison Act, 1894, 26 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 167, 167(2), 309 - Transfer of under trials - Opportunity of hearing - Challenged - Appellants (Officials department of Prisons) addressed to Special Judge, under 1999 Act, requesting transfer of respondents (accused), who were under trials, from Bombay central prison to other jails in State on the ground of against capacity and proceedings in on-ongoing cases in question were stayed - In pursuant to request of appellants, Special Judge held that it was total domain of Jail authorities to transfer accused to other jails due to scarcity of premises or for security purpose and appellants were at liberty to transfer respondents to other jails - However, during transfer of respondents, appellants used force causing injuries to some of respondents - Aggrieved respondents filed writ petition before HC alleging that appellants used of excessive force and inhuman treatment and use of force was without any provocation and justification - HC by impugned order held that nature of the allegations made in petitions were found to be sufficient to call for an inquiry into violent incident and assigned inquiry Sessions Judge, to report whether use of force by appellant authorities was excessive and force used for any extraneous reasons other than for maintaining discipline in terms of 1963 Rules - HC after considering report received from Sessions Judge, found it necessary to direct Govt. to hold a departmental inquiry against officials who had used excessive force in bringing the situation in jail under control and that order transferring respondents from Bombay central jail to other jails in State was illegal and unacceptable, as the request for transfer was dealt with at an administrative level without affording an opportunity to under trials to oppose same - Hence, instant appeal - Appellants contended that s. 29 of 1900 Act empowered State Govt. or Inspector General of Prisons to transfer under trials - Whether under trials could be transferred to any prison with the permission of Court under whose orders under trial was committed to prison - Held, remand orders were invariably addressed to Superintendents of jails where the under trials were detained till their production before Court on the date fixed for that purpose - Prison where under trial was detained was thus a prison identified by competent Court either in terms of s. 167 or s. 309 of CrPC - It was axiomatic that transfer of prisoner from any such place of detention would be permissible only with the permission of Court under whose warrant the under trial was remanded to custody - In instant case, any order of transfer passed in any proceedings for transfer of prisoner, was nothing but a judicial order or at least a quasi-judicial one - Trial Court appeared to have treated the matter to be administrative and accordingly permitted the transfer without issuing notice to under-trials (respondents) or passing an appropriate order in the matter, it committed a mistake - A communication received from prison authorities was dealt with and disposed of at an administrative level by sending a communication in reply without due and proper consideration and without passing a considered judicial order which alone could justify a transfer in the case - HC was right in declaring the transfer to be void and directing the re-transfer of under trials to Bombay jail - Appeals partly allowed.

(B)Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Administrative - Maharashtra Control of Orgnaised Crime Act, 1999 - Discipline Rules, 1963 - Prisoners Act, 1900, s. 29 - Prison Act, 1894, 26 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 167, 167(2), 309 - Transfer of under trials - Use of excessive force - Disciplinary action - Whether impugned order of HC was justified - Held, directions issued by HC entirely on the basis of report submitted by Sessions Judge and report, preliminary was flawed in many respects including the fact that same did not comply with basic requirement of a fair opportunity of hearing being given to those likely to be affected - Sessions Judge had not named the officers responsible for alleged use of excessive force which was essential for any follow up or further action in the matter - Report clearly stated the officials concerned were not allowed to examine any witness although a request was made by them to do so - Some of the observation of HC that give an impression as though the misdemeanor of jail officers was proved, did not appear to be justified - It was at any rate not for HC to record a final and authoritative finding that force used by jail authorities was excessive or that it was used for any extraneous purpose - It was a matter that could be determined only after a proper inquiry was conducted and an opportunity afforded to those who were accused of using such excessive force or abusing the power vested in them - Whether any disciplinary inquiry needed against jail officials would depend upon the outcome of a proper investigation into the incident and not a preliminary enquiry in which IO, apart from statements of respondents, made use of information discreetly collected from jail inmates - Impugned directions of HC were premature - Govt. should treat the report of Sessions Judge as a preliminary inquiry and take a considered decision whether or not any further inquiry, investigation or proceedings against those allegedly responsible for using excessive force while restoring discipline in Bombay central jail needs to be conducted - Appeals partly allowed.
