Secretary, Ministry of Health and Family Welfare, Government of Maharashtra v S.C. Malte and others

Supreme Court of India

13 December 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Patnaik, Swatanter Kumar

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 567; (2012) 13 SCC 118; 2013 (4) AWC 3793; JT 2013 (1) SC 42; 2012(12) SCALE 480; [2012] 13 S.C.R. 230; 2013 (2) SLR 392

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges

Keywords: Maharashtra Retired High Court Judges (Facilities for Medical Treatment) Rules, 2006, Gujarat Minister's (Medical Attendance and Treatment) Rules 1964

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Practice & Procedure - High Court Judges (Salaries and Conditions of Service) Act, 1954, ss. 23 - Retired Judges - Medical Facilities - Central Government Employees - Benefits - Sustainability - Provisions of Act provides that every retired Judge shall be entitled for himself and his family to same facilities as respects medical treatment on same conditions as a retired officer of Central Civil Services, Class-I and his family, were entitled under any rules and orders of Central Govt. for time being in force - Retired Judges addressed a letter to CJ of HC mentioning difficulties of retired Judges in getting medical facilities under CGHS Scheme - Letter was treated as suo motu Writ Petition and an order was passed by HC directing Govt. to frame rules for medical treatment and reimbursement of retired Judges of HC - Amicus curiae appeared for suo motu writ petitioners, suggesting change in Draft Rules of 2006 entitling retired Judges to medical facilities and reimbursement provided in the Draft Rules whenever the CGHS Scheme was not availed - HC disposed of writ petition directing State Govt. to either notify Draft Rules in form suggested by amicus curiae or amend GR for medical benefits to sitting Judges and extend same benefits also to retired Judges - Govt. filed Civil Application for review of order, but HC rejected to review and directed State Govt. to comply with order - Aggrieved, instant appeal - Whether HC, was justified in directing State Govt. to adopt draft rules recommended by Amicus Curiae - Held, keeping in view doctrine of separation of powers and independence of judiciary, which were structural ethos of our Constitution, it was expected that legislative power and more particularly, subordinate legislative power, ought not to be exercised so as to obtrude these basic fundamental principles, exercise of subordinate legislative power, which by necessary implication, entrenched upon independence of judiciary, would have to be decided on touchstone of it being violative or otherwise, of basic structure of Constitution - In order to ensure absolute independence of judiciary, in interest of administration of justice and for Judges to act free of any apprehensive attitude and to provide complete certainty to service conditions of former Judges of HC, Appeal was disposed of with order-cum-directions - Appeal disposed of.
