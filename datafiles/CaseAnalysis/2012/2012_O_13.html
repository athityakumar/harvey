Chandrika Chunilal Shah v Orbit Finance Pvt. Ltd. & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

19 April 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 130; (2012) 5 SCC 416; 2012(7) ALL MR 827; 2012 (94) ALR 220; 2012 (4) Bom.C.R. 17; JT 2012 (4) SC 560; 2012 (117) RD 374; 2012(4) SCALE 512

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Maharashtra Ownership of Flats Act, 1963 - Alternate Accommodation - Leave and Licence - Third Party Interest - Sustainability - Appellant had entered into a registered agreement with respondent no. 1 for alternate accommodation - Inter-se agreement between respondent No.1 on one hand and respondent Nos. 2 and 3 regarding sale of part of accommodation in same building was served upon appellant - Appellant filed suit seeking reliefs under provisions of Act, before Civil Court - Respondents raised preliminary issue regarding pecuniary jurisdiction, plaint was returned - Appellant filed suit before HC-Trial Judge on Original side along with interim application - HC passed interim orders in favor of respondents, directing that third party interest would not be created and premises could be given on leave and licence for a period not exceeding eleven months - Appellant preferred appeal before DB - DB confirmed and further modified order of HC and directed fees received from licence to be deposited with Court - Aggrieved instant appeal - Whether relief granted by HC and confirmed and modified by DB was sustainable - Held, there was nothing on record to show that construction of building was completed in all respects and Completion Certificate had been issued by statutory authority in favour of respondents - It was nobody's case that respondent no. 1 had ever applied before statutory authority for issuance of Completion Certificate - Appellant had agreed before Trial Judge for appointment of an Architect to identify as which area ad-measuring 2680 Sq. Fts. on fourth floor would be allotted to her, architect had submitted report and appellant was at liberty to file objections to said report, if not already filed and Trial Judge would examine/consider report as well as objections of parties and pass appropriate orders - Courts below had not passed any order permitting respondent nos. 2 and 3 to create any third party rights, they had been permitted to grant leave and licence in respect of suit premises so far as area 2680 Sq. Fts. was concerned and protected interest of appellant directing that licence fee for premises would be deposited in recurring account with a nationalised bank - SC, felt that appeal did not warrant interference and lacked merit - Appeal dismissed.
