Krishnappa and another v State Of Karnataka By Babaleshwara Police Station

Supreme Court of India

31 July 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Dattu, Chandramauli Kumar Prasad

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 441; (2012) 11 SCC 237; AIR 2012 SC 2946; 2013 ALL MR (Cri) 1080; 2012 CRLJ 4347; 2012 (4) JCC 2339; 2013 (2) KarLJ 605; 2012 (3) MLJ(Crl) 820; 2012 (4) RCR(Criminal) 968; 2013 (1) RLW 224; [2012] 6 S.C.R. 1068

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 143, 147, 148, 504, 506 (Part II), 302 149 - Enmity - Common object - Unlawful assembly - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellants-Accused No. 1,15,16,21, were acquitted by Trial Court, but were convicted by appellate Court u/ss. 143, 147, 148, 504, 506 (Part II), 302 r/w. s. 149 IPC - Prosecution case was that appellants family had long standing enmity which intensified due to political rivalry with deceased and his family and due to hostile relationship, appellants and other accused killed deceased with weapons - Trial Court considering evidence on record concluded that accused No. 13,14,17 were directly responsible held them guilty and acquitted appellants on ground that acts were not solely responsible for death of deceased - Aggrieved State and accused No. 13,14,17, preferred appeal before HC, which did not accept conclusion of acquittal, convicted appellants and dismissed appeal of other accused except accused No. 17 who was acquitted by HC - Aggrieved instant appeal by appellants against reversal of acquittal - Appellants contended that reliance on PW1's evidence as an eye witness was under serious doubts because PW9 in his deposition said that PW1 and his brother came to spot after 30 minutes of occurrence of offence, hence acquittal order of Trial Court was based on plausible and cogent reasons - Whether order of conviction by HC, reversing acquittal orders passed by Trial Court was sustainable - Held, it was now well settled law that provisions of s. 149 of IPC would be attracted whenever any offence committed by any member of an unlawful assembly in prosecution of common object of that assembly, or when members of that assembly knew that offence was likely to be committed in prosecution of that object, so that every person, who, at time of committing of that offence was a member, would be also vicariously held liable and guilty of that offence, s. 149 IPC creates a constructive or vicarious liability of members of unlawful assembly for unlawful acts committed pursuant to common object by any other member of that assembly - This principle ropes in every member of assembly to be guilty of an offence where that offence was committed by any member of that assembly in prosecution of common object of that assembly, or such members or assembly knew that offence was likely to be committed in prosecution of that object - Appellants were members of same assembly which had caused murder of deceased, in terms of s. 149 IPC, as they had dragged deceased after first assault and contributed in preventing deceased from escaping assault, hence are guilty of offence with other accused - Appeals dismissed.
