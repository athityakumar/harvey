Chairman, LIC of India and Others v A. Masilamani

Supreme Court of India

23 November 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, J. S. KHEHAR

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 497; (2013) 6 SCC 530; 2013 (96) ALR 814; 2013 (1) AWC 937; 2013 (136) FLR 81; JT 2012 (11) SC 533; 2014 (3) MahLJ 524; 2014 (3) MPLJ 652; 2012(11) SCALE 413; [2012] 11 S.C.R. 35; 2013 (1) SLR 393

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Life Insurance Corporation of India (Staff) Regulations, 1960, regs. 20, 21, 27, 39(1) - Chargesheet - Disciplinary Authority - Violation of Principle of Natural Justice - Penalty - Sustainability - Respondent was working with appellant-Corporation as a Higher Grade Assistant and for purpose of construction of his house had applied to for a housing loan with appellant - Appellant had noticed that there had been irregularities and deviations in construction, and loan had been obtained upon non-disclosure of facts in entirety - Thus, charge sheet dt. 6-1-1998, was issued to respondent, for violating provisions of regs. 20, 21, 27,39(1) of Regulations - Respondent submitted reply to charges, denying all of them, but Disciplinary Authority, was not satisfied with explanation furnished and proceeded to conduct an enquiry and imposed a penalty of reduction in basic pay of respondent, to minimum amount specified in time scale applicable to him, in terms of reg. 39(1)(d) of Regulations - Respondent preferred appeal u/ reg. 40 of Regulations, which was dismissed by Appellate Authority - Aggrieved, respondent preferred writ petition before HC, for quashing of enquiry proceedings, imposition of penalty, and also for re-imbursement of amount that had been deducted from his salary, including all attendant benefits - Writ petition was allowed by HC, which observed that witnesses to case, in process of Departmental Enquiry, had been examined in violation of statutory rules applicable herein, as well as in violation of principles of natural justice - Aggrieved, appellant-Corporation filed an appeal before DB, which was also dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether HC, was justified in allowing Writ Petition, setting aside penalty imposed by Disciplinary Authority - Held, It was settled legal proposition, that once Court sets aside an order of punishment, on ground that enquiry was not properly conducted, Court could not reinstate employee, it must remit concerned case to disciplinary authority, for it to conduct enquiry from point that it stood vitiated, and conclude same - Court must examine, magnitude of misconduct alleged against delinquent employee, it was in view of this, that courts/tribunals, were not competent to quash charge-sheet and related disciplinary proceedings, before same were concluded - Court/tribunal should not generally set aside departmental enquiry, and quash charges on ground of delay in initiation of disciplinary proceedings, as such a power was de hors limitation of judicial review, in event that, court/tribunal exercises such power, it exceeds its power of judicial review at very threshold, therefore, a charge-sheet or show cause notice, issued in course of disciplinary proceedings, could not ordinarily be quashed by court, same principle was applicable, in relation to there being a delay in conclusion of disciplinary proceedings - Appeal disposed of.
