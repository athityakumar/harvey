Karuna Singh v State Of Nct Of Delhi & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

10 April 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Jagdish Singh Khehar, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 127; (2012) 5 SCC 407; AIR 2012 SC 2814; 2012 (4) Bom.C.R. 768; 2012 (5) CTC 570; 2012 (189) DLT 268; 2012 (3) JCC (NI) 150; JT 2012 (4) SC 360; 2012 (3) MLJ(Crl) 816; 2012 (3) RCR(Criminal) 76; 2012(4) SCALE 267

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Constitution - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 20, 468, 471/120B - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, ss. 138, 145(2) - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 32, 226,227, 20(3), 21 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 482, 483 - Violation of Fundamental Rights - Denial of speedy trial - Maintainability - Respondent No.2 got FIR registered against petitioner and five others u/ss. 420, 468 471 r/w s. 120B of IPC - More than three years after registration of FIR, charge-sheet was filed against petitioner - Petitioner filed complaint u/s. 138 of 1881 Act against respondent No. 2, along with evidence - Respondent No. 2 was permitted by Magistrate to cross-examine petitioner, thereafter, even after 5 years cross examination of petitioner was stated to be continuing - Further, cross examination of petitioner at behest of respondent No. 2 was mainly with reference to FIR registered which as per petitioner was gross abuse of law by respondent No. 2 violating fundamental rights of petitioner vested in him u/arts. 20(3), 21 of Constitution, and also denial of speedy and expedition trial constituted an act of harassment and violation of fundamental rights of petitioner - Hence instant petition - Whether petition filed by petitioner before SC was maintainable - Held, instant case was not a case where despite availability of an alternative remedy, petitioner had approached SC under its writ jurisdiction, but where petitioner had availed of her alternative remedies, and simultaneously on same cause of action, she had approached highest court of land u/art. 32 of Constitution, which constituted gross abuse of process of law - It was satisfied that an efficacious alternative remedy was available to a party which had initiated proceedings u/s. 138 of 1881 Act, petitioner had approached HC u/ss. 482 and 483 of CrPC, and through a separate petition u/art. 226/227 of Constitution and both petitions were pending before HC - It was not possible to accept that fundamental rights of petitioner u/arts. 20(3), 21 of Constitution had been violated in any manner whatsoever, so as to enable him to approach Court - If instant plea of petitioner was accepted, jurisdiction of SC u/art. 32 of Constitution would become available against every action of a Magistrate, not only under 1881 Act, but also, in respect of criminal proceedings conducted under other statutory provisions - Petition dismissed.
