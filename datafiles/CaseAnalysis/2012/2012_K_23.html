Delhi Administration And Others v Kaushilya Thakur And Another

Supreme Court of India

09 April 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 114; (2012) 5 SCC 412; AIR 2012 SC 2515; 2012 (92) ALR 707; 2012 (3) ALT 25; 2012 (3) AWC 3024; 2012 (2) CLT(SC) 249; JT 2012 (4) SC 84; 2012 (3) RCR(Civil) 273; 2012 (116) RD 563; 2012(4) SCALE 231

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property

Summary: Administrative - Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4(1), 18, 6, 7, 5A - Land Acquisition - Compensation - Alternate plot - Sustainability - Govt. proposed acquisition of land for planned development and declaration u/s. 6(1) was issued and award was passed - Husband of respondent No. 1 claimed compensation as his land was part of acquisition and on reference of Collector u/s. 18 of Act Additional District Judge held him entitled and awarded compensation - After 10 years, application for allotment of an alternative plot was submitted by husband of respondent No. 1, which was rejected by Special Secretary on ground that khasra was not owned by him- Second representation was also rejected by Competent Authority - Rejection was challenged before HC, which allowed writ petition - Appeal was filed by appellant administration which was dismissed on ground that reference Court had accepted his entitlement to receive compensation in lieu of acquired land which formed part of khasra - Aggrieved instant appeal - Whether HC was justified in accepting respondent's entitlement to receive compensation in lieu of acquired land - Held, payment of compensation paid in terms of award passed by Land Acquisition Collector and enhanced compensation determined by Reference Court should not lead to an inference that he was owner of land forming part of Khasra - In any case, before issuing a mandamus for allotment of 1000 square yards plot to petitioner, HC should have called upon him to produce some tangible evidence to prove his ownership of land forming part of Khasra - Unfortunately, HC and DB did not pay serious attention to stark reality that husband of respondent No. 1 was not owner of land mentioned in application filed by him for allotment of 1000 square yards land - Impugned orders passed by HC were set aside - Appeal allowed.
