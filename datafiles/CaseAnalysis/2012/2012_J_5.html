Jagan Singh (Dead) through Lrs. v Dhanwanti and another

Supreme Court of India

19 January 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Gokhale, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 602; (2012) 2 SCC 628; 2012 (2) ADJ 454; AIR 2012 SC (Supp) 629; 2012 (2) AWC 1931; JT 2012 (1) SC 369; 2012 (3) MLJ(SC) 367; 2012 (2) RCR(Civil) 576; 2012 (115) RD 391; [2012] 2 S.C.R. 303

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Hindu Succession Act, 1956, s. 14(1) - U.P. Zamindari Abolition and Land Reforms Act, 1951, ss. 169, 152 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 52 - Execution of Will - Permanent Injunction - Lis pendency - Validity - Sustainability - Appellant's brother owned certain parcels of bhumidhari lands which were covered under provisions of Act and had executed a Will bequeathing in favor of appellant - Respondent No. 1-defendant No.1-Wife of original owner i.e. brother of appellant was intending to dispose of property to respondent No. 2 - Appellant filed suit for permanent injunction restraining her from disposing of land - Respondent No. 1 had contended that she was lawfully married wife of original owner and stated that Will was forged - Respondent No. 2-prospective purchaser pleaded that he was wrongfully joined in suit and that respondent No. 1 was in possession of land - Trial Court held that Will was duly executed and was registered, but held that respondent No.1 had benefit of provision of s. 14 (1) of 1956, Act, and in view thereof property possessed by respondent No.1 would have to be held as her wholly owned property, and that she was not a restricted owner and dismissed suit - Appellant preferred appeal, but was dismissed, Thereafter, appellant filed Second Appeal before HC, which dismissed holding that no substantial question of law arose and held that u/s. 152 of Act, bhumidhar had a right to transfer his property, and such right was subject only to restrictions contained in Act as provided in s. 152 (1) itself - Judge also referred to s. 169 (2) of Act, and observed that Sub-section which restricted right of a female bhumidhar to bequeath her holding by will had now been deleted, and that right to transfer could not be restricted either by contract or by a will of a tenure holder, and that restriction contained in will that legatee would not have a right to transfer property was repugnant to incidents of a bhumidhari tenure under Act - Hence instant appeal - Appellant contended that bequest by original owner would be clearly covered u/s. 169(1) of Act, r/w s. 14 (2) of 1956 Act - Respondent contended that during pendency of litigation, respondent No. 1 had executed Sale Deed in favor of third part who also filed suit and also application for impleading, some were dismissed and others was pending - Whether appellant were eligible for permanent injunction and whether respondent No. 1 had right and title transfer property - Held, pendency of a suit or a proceeding shall be deemed to continue until suit or a proceeding was disposed of by final decree or order, and complete satisfaction or discharge of such decree or order had been obtained or had become unobtainable by reason of expiration of any period of limitation prescribed for execution thereof by any law for time being in force - In present case, it would be canvassed on behalf of respondent and applicant that sale had taken place in favour of applicant at a time when there was no stay operating against such sale, and in fact when second appeal had not been filed - Present situation under principle of lis-pendens since sale was executed at a time when second appeal had not been filed but which came to be filed afterwards within period of limitation, doctrine of lis-pendens was founded in public policy and equity, and if it had to be read meaningfully such a sale as in present case until period of limitation for second appeal was over will had to be held as covered u/s. 52 of TP Act - Appeal disposed of.
