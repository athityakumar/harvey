Narendra Champaklal Trivedi and another v State Of Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

29 May 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 135; (2012) 7 SCC 80; AIR 2012 SC 2263; 2012 ALL MR (Cri) 2426; 2012 CRLJ 3025; 2012 (3) CTC 861; 2013 (1) G.L.R. 1; JT 2012 (5) SC 496; 2012 (3) MLJ(Crl) 809; 2012 (3) RCR(Criminal) 974; 2012(5) SCALE 683; [2012] 6 S.C.R. 165

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Illegal Gratification

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 7, 13(2),20 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 313 - Prevention of Corruption - Bribery - Conviction - Sustainable - Appellants were convicted for offence punishable u/ss. 7 and 13(2) of Act - Prosecution case was that appellants demanded bribe and received money from complainant to issue property card and sketch - Complainant with help of Anti-Corruption Bureau laid trap and caught appellants taking bribe - Trial Court convinced by evidence convicted appellants - Aggrieved appellants filed appeal before HC, which upheld conviction - Hence instant appeal - Whether conviction of appellants by Trial Court was sustainable - Held, money was recovered from pockets of appellants, a presumption u/s. 20 of Act became obligatory - It was a presumption of law and casted an obligation on court to apply it in every case brought u/s. 7 of Act - Appellants in their statement u/s. 313 of CrPC had made an adroit effort to explain their stand but they miserably failed to dislodge presumption - PW2 had categorically stated that complainant took out Rs.50/- from his pocket and gave it to appellant as directed, thus analysed and understood, there remained no shadow of doubt that appellants had demanded bribe and accepted same to provide survey report - Therefore, conviction recorded by trial Judge which had been affirmed by HC, did not warrant any interference - Further it would not be appropriate to exercise jurisdiction u/art. 142 of Constitution to reduce sentence in the instant case on ground of so-called mitigating factors - Appeals dismissed.
