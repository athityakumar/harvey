Bhushan Kumar & Anr. v State (Nct Of Delhi) & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

04 April 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, J. Chelameswar

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 116; (2012) 5 SCC 424; AIR 2012 SC 1747; 2012 CRLJ 2286; 2012 (189) DLT 252; JT 2012 (4) SC 127; 2012 (2) RCR(Criminal) 794; 2012 (3) RLW 2467; 2012(4) SCALE 191; [2012] 2 S.C.R. 696

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 482, 251, 204, 239 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 420 - Chargesheet - Summoned - Quashing - Maintainability - Appellants and respondent No. 2 had entered into a handwritten note regarding distribution of assets, subsequently fresh agreement was entered into which superseded handwritten note - Dispute arose giving rise to multifarious litigation which are pending and due to non-materializing of agreement respondent No. 2 got registered FIR u/s. 420 of IPC against all signatories to agreement - Charge sheet was filed and Magistrate ordered for summoning appellants - Challenging summoning order appellants filed petition for quashing summons which was rejected by HC on ground that petition for quashing was not maintainable - Aggrieved instant petition - Whether HC was justified in dismissing petition filed by appellant for quashing order of summons by Magistrate - Held, it was inherent in s. 251 of Code that when an accused appeared before Trial Court pursuant to summons issued u/s. 204 of Code in a summons trial case, it was bounden duty of trial Court to carefully go through allegations made in charge sheet or complaint and consider evidence to come to a conclusion whether or not, commission of any offence was disclosed and if answer was in affirmative, Magistrate should explain substance of accusation to accused and ask him whether he pleaded guilty otherwise, he was bound to discharge accused as per s. 239 of Code - Thus, conclusion of HC that petition filed u/s. 482 of Code was not maintainable could not be accepted - However, impugner order passed by HC on merits was confirmed - Appeal dismissed.
