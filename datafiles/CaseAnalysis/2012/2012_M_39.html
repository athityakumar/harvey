M.P.Rural Road Development Authority & Anr. v M/S. L.G. Chaudhary Engineers & Cont

Supreme Court of India

24 January 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Ganguly, Gyan Sudha Misra

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 67; (2012) 3 SCC 495; AIR 2012 SC 1228; 2012 (2) JabLJ 329; JT 2012 (2) SC 469; 2012(1) M.P.H.T. 338; 2012(1) SCALE 688; [2012] 2 S.C.R. 162

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Madhya Pradesh Madhyasthan Adhikaran Adhiniyam, 1983, M.P. Arbitration Tribunal Act, 1983

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Contracts & Commercial - Madhya Pradesh Madhyasthan Adhikaran Adhiniyam, 1983, ss. 2 and 7 - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, s. 11 - Work Contract - Violation - Termination - Sustainability - Appellant entered into works contract with respondent for construction and maintenance of rural road package - clause 24 of contract contained 'Dispute Redress Mechanism' - In view of several breached in work contract by respondent, appellant terminated works contract and encashed bank guarantee furnished by respondent - Respondent filed writ petition challenging encashment of bank guarantee, petition was disposed with direction that bank guarantee may not be encashed till disposal of representation - Representation of respondent was rejected - Respondent submitted additional claim and requested appellant to appoint an Arbitrator for adjudicating dispute between parties - Appellant replied that clause 25 of Works contract provides for adjudication of disputes by Arbitral Tribunal under M.P. Act - Respondent filed application u/s. 11 of A.C. Act 1996 for appointment of Arbitrator before HC - HC allowed application and appointed Arbitrator relying on precedent decision - Aggrieved instant appeal - Appellant contended that DB while concluding had not noticed relevant provision of M.P. Act as well as relevant provisions of A.C. Act 1996 as such judgment was rendered 'per incur am' - Respondent contended that M.P. Act is repugnant to A.C. Act 1996 since the same is a later Act made by Parliament as per provisions of art. 254 of Constitution and as per s. 85 of A.C. Act 1996, M.P. Act stands impliedly repealed - Whether provision of M.P. Act which statutorily provides for parties to Works Contract to refer all disputes to Arbitration Tribunal constituted u/s. 7 of the Act will continue to operate in view of provisions of A.C. Act, 1996 - Held, M.P. Act 1983 was reserved for assent of President and admittedly received same and was published in Gazette Extraordinary, requirement of art. 254(2) of Constitution was satisfied - M.P. Act of 1983 prevails in State, A.C. Act 1996 was enacted by Parliament repealing earlier laws of arbitration of 1940, A.C. Act 1996 saves provisions of M.P. Act 1983 under sub-sections 2(4) and 2(5) thereof - Act made by Parliament clearly showed an intention to the effect that State Law of Arbitration should operate in State in respect of certain specified types of arbitrations which are under the M.P. Act 1983, hence there cannot be any repugnancy - Appeal allowed.
