State Of Haryana And Others v Vijay Singh And Others

Supreme Court of India

22 August 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 254; (2012) 8 SCC 633; AIR 2012 SC 2901; 2012 (135) FLR 191; 2012 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 2902; 2012 (7) MLJ(SC) 49; 2012(7) SCALE 473; 2012(7) SCALE 484; [2012] 10 S.C.R. 356; 2012 (5) SLR 691

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Maharashtra Service of Engineers (Regulation of Seniority and Preparation and Revision of Seniority Lists for Specified Period) Rules, 1982, U.P. Regularisation of Ad Hoc Appointments (On Posts Within the Purview of the Public Service Commission) Rules, 1979, Punjab Educational Service, Class III, School Cadre Rules, 1955, Reorganised Bombay State Assistant Engineers and Executive Engineers Seniority Lists Rules, 1981, Reorganised Bombay State Overseers and Deputy Engineers Seniority Lists Rules, 1978, Bombay Service of Engineers (Class I and Class II) Recruitment Rules, 1970, Bombay Service of Engineers (Class I and Class II) Recruitment Rules, 1960, Haryana State Education School Cadre (Group 'C') Service Rules, 1998, U.P. Medical Services (Men's Branch) (Amendment) Rules, 1981, U.P. Medical Service (Men's Branch) Rules, 1945

Summary: Service - Punjab Educational Service, Class III, School Cadre Rules, 1955, r. 9 - Haryana State Education School Cadre (Group 'C') Service Rules, 1998, r. 11 - Ad-hoc service - Regularization - Seniority - On being selected by the District Level Committee, respondents were appointed as teacher purely on ad hoc basis by the District Education Officers - Later, services of the respondents were regularized w.e.f. 1-10-2003 - After regularization of their services, the respondents submitted representations and claimed that the period of ad-hoc service should be counted towards seniority because they were recruited on the basis of selection made by the District Selection Committee from among the candidates sponsored by the Employment Exchanges - The department did not accept their plea and in the provisional gradation list of the Haryana Education Service Class III, their names were shown below those who were appointed on regular basis prior to 1-10-2003 - Respondents challenged the provisional gradation list vide writ petition on the ground that the same was discriminatory - DB held that the seniority of the respondents be fixed by taking into account their ad hoc service and, accordingly, they should be considered for promotion to the posts of lecturer - Hence, the instant appeal - Whether the seniority of the respondents could be fixed by taking into account their ad hoc service - Held, respondents were neither appointed by the Director on the recommendations of the Board nor they were placed on probation - Respondents were appointed on purely ad hoc basis without following the procedure prescribed for regular appointment - Therefore, the mere fact that the ad hoc appointments of the respondents were preceded by sending requisitions to the Employment Exchanges and recommendations by the District Selection Committee cannot lead to an inference that they were appointed on regular basis - Respondents were appointed on purely ad hoc basis for 6 months and they continued to serve till the regularization of their service w.e.f. 1-10-2003 - Therefore, their seniority could not be fixed either u/r. 9 of the 1955 Rules or u/r 11 of the 1998 Rules by counting their service from the date of initial appointments - The conclusion recorded by the HC that the respondents' initial appointments were regular and, therefore, ad hoc service was liable to be counted for the purpose of fixation of seniority was legally unsustainable - Impugned order of the HC was set aside - Appeal allowed.
