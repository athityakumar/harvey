M/S. Dsr Steel (P) Ltd v State Of Rajasthan & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

01 May 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, Gyan Sudha Misra

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 492; (2012) 6 SCC 782; AIR 2012 SC 1602; 2012(3) ALL MR 965; 2012 (95) ALR 128; 2012 (4) AWC 3268; JT 2012 (4) SC 547; 2012 (2) RCR(Civil) 947; [2012] 5 S.C.R. 583

Case Digest

Subject: Electricity & Energy

Summary: Administrative - Electricity & Energy - Practice & Procedure - Electricity Act, 2003, s. 125, 94 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 100 - Revised Tariff - Incentive Scheme - Substantial Question of Law - Maintainability - Electricity distribution companies had existing tariffs and had made application with Commissioner seeking tariff revision - Publication was made and several individuals and organisations appeared before commissioner raising objections - Commission eventually directed that revised tariff determined by it will become effective from 1-1- 2005 and remain in force till same was amended by Commission by a separate order passed by it - Aggrieved appellants and large number of other consumers in that category filed review petitions u/s. 94 (1)(f) of Act, seeking review and continuation of incentive scheme, contending that they were affected by withdrawal of incentive scheme as they made investments on basis of incentive scheme bona fide believing that same would continue for at least 3 years and therefore, sought continuation of said scheme by suitable review of Commission's order, review petitions were dismissed by Commission - Aggrieved appellants and others preferred appeal before Tribunal - Tribunal held that contention urged by appellants that doctrine of Promissory Estoppel was attracted in facts of case, it concurred with view taken by Commission that incentive scheme was applicable only upto 31-3-2007 or till Commission issued a tariff order whichever was earlier - Hence instant appeal - Whether Tribunal and HC, were justified in concurring with order of Commission and whether order of Commission was sustainable - Held, appeal u/s. 125 of Act, was maintainable before this Court only on grounds specified in s. 100 of CPC, s. 100 in turn permits filing of an appeal only if case involves a substantial question of law, findings of fact recorded by Courts below, which would in present case, imply Regulatory Commission as Court of first instance and Appellate Tribunal as Court hearing first appeal, could not be re-opened before this Court in an appeal u/s. 125 of Act, just as HC could not interfere with concurrent findings of fact recorded by Courts below in a second appeal u/s. 100 of CPC, so also this Court would be loathed to entertain any challenge to concurrent findings of fact recorded by Regulatory Commission and Appellate Tribunal, hence SC, dismissed appeals as no substantial question of law arose for consideration - Appeals dismissed.
