In Re : Networking of Rivers

Supreme Court of India

27 February 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Swatanter Kumar, A.K. Patnaik, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 499; (2012) 4 SCC 51; JT 2012 (3) SC 234; 2012 (3) MLJ(SC) 1165; [2012] 1 S.C.R. 1118

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Water

Keywords: Inter-State Water Disputes Act, 1956, River Boards Act, 1956, Fiscal Responsibility and Budget Management Rules

Summary: Water - Administrative - Constitution - Nationalization of rivers - Implementation of inter link of rivers program - Challenged - Petitioner instituted the petition with averment that issue appropriate order directing the respondent No. 1 to take appropriate action to nationalize all rivers in country - Petitioner alleged that issue the direction to respondent No. 1 to take appropriate action to inter link rivers in southern peninsula - However, the above directions were sought by petitioner against Central Govt. as well as against various State Govts., for effective management of water resources in the country by nationalization and inter-linking of rivers from Ganga Cauveri, Vaigai-Tambaravarmi up to Cape Kumari - Petitioner contended that due to reluctance of water-rich States, National Water Development Agency (NWDA) was not allowed to undertake detailed survey and it was argued that only by nationalization of rivers, by Govt. of India, this problem could be resolved to some extent - Held, NWDA had also, in course of framing of its policies, proposed intra-state links - Except for 6 States and 4 Union Territories, all other States and Union Territories had interest in these intra-State links - There were 8 inter-linking projects which were under review by different State authorities - However, details of divergence between State Govts. are not clearly spelt out, even as of now - An additional study was undertaken by National Council for Applied Economic Research (NCAER) and revised final report, published in April 2008, assessed economic impact of rivers interlinking program and suggested an investment roll out plan, i.e., a practical implementation schedule, for same - it was not difficult to visualize the difficulties in preparation, execution, financing and consensus building, still, it was the need of hour to carry out these projects more effectively and with greater sensitivity - NCAER was assigned the work of assessing the economic impact of river interlinking programs, which in turn, suggested an investment roll-out plan for same - Report of NCAER was prepared in April, 2008 and report considered various financial aspects and impact of various river interlinking projects in India - NCAER also suggests that after completion of linking of rivers program, different river links should be maintained by separate river basin organizations, which should all be functioning under direct control of Central Water Commission or such other appropriate central body - it was clear that primarily there was unanimity between all concerned authorities including Centre and majority of State Govts., with exception of 1 or 2, that implementation of river linking would be very beneficial - In fact, expert opinions convincingly dispel all other impressions - There should be greater growth in agricultural and allied sectors, prosperity and stimulus to economy potentially causing increase in per capita income, in addition to short and long term benefits likely to accrue by such implementation - These projects were in national interest, as was the unanimous view of all experts, most State Govts. and particularly, Central Govt. - But Court might not be very appropriate forum for planning and implementation of such program had wide national dimensions and ramifications - It would not only be desirable, but also inevitable that an appropriate body should be created to plan, construct and implement this inter linking of rivers program for benefit of nation as a whole - Direction was issued to Union of India and particularly Ministry of Water Resources, Govt. of India, to forthwith constitute Committee for Inter-linking of Rivers - Petitions disposed of.
