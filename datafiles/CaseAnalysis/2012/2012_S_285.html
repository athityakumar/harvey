Vipul Shital Prasad Agarwal v State of Gujarat and another

Supreme Court of India

06 November 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Surinder Singh Nijjar, J. Chelameswar

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 403; (2013) 1 SCC 197; AIR 2013 SC 73; 2013 ALL MR (Cri) 1084; 2013 CRLJ 336; 2013 (1) G.L.R. 913; JT 2012 (11) SC 88; 2012 (4) MLJ(Crl) 787; 2013 (1) RCR(Criminal) 5; 2013 (1) RLW 769; 2012(11) SCALE 14; [2012] 9 S.C.R. 987

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860 ss. 302, 364, 307, 333, 334, 427, 365, 368, 193, 197, 201, 120-B, 471, 34 - Arms Act, 1959, 25 - Bombay Prohibition Act, 1949 - s. 135 - Fake Encounter - Chargesheet - Statutory Bail - Eligibility - Petitioner-Superintendent of Police was accused of killing deceased in fake encounter - Police had registered a case u/ss. 307,427,34 of IPC and s. 25(1)(A) of Act, against unidentified persons and was investigating - Mother of deceased had filed writ petition praying or an inquiry into incident by CBI, during pendency police had filed chargesheet adding ss. 302, 364, 307, 333, 334, 427, 365, 368, 193, 197, 201, 120-B, 471, 34 of IPC, and ss. 25(1)(a), 27 of Act, and u/s. 135 of BP Act - HC, directed CBI to investigate matter and petitioner was arrested by CBI, meanwhile chargesheet was filed against accused persons and petitioner - CBI registered separate FIR and made application for custody of petitioner for further investigation - Petition moved application for bail before Sessions Court which was returned on ground that petitioner ought to have moved before JFCM - Petitioner moved application before JFCM, which was dismissed - Aggrieved petitioner filed application before HC, for quashing and setting aside judgment and order of rejection of bail, which was dismissed - Aggrieved instant appeal - Petitioner contended that he was entitled to benefit of statutory bail in terms of Sub-s. (2) of s. 167 CrPC, since after registering a fresh FIR and commencing of fresh investigation, as directed by this Court, CBI had failed to file charge-sheet pursuant to such FIR, within stipulated period of 90 days - Whether petitioner was eligible for bail and whether courts below were justified in dismissing application of petitioner - Held, there could not be two charge-sheets arising out of two FIRs in respect of a single incident and charge would have to be framed on basis of one of said two charge-sheets filed and, since first investigation had not been accepted, logical consequence would be that first charge-sheet also stood quashed which would give second charge-sheet due legitimacy - Charge-sheet submitted by police stood implicitly rejected was without any basis in law and misconceived, even fact that CBI purported to have registered a 'fresh FIR', did not lead to conclusion in law that earlier report or material collected by Police-CID on basis of which they filed charge-sheet ceased to exist, it only demonstrated administrative practice of CBI - SC, held that practice of CBI to register a 'fresh FIR', investigation undertaken by CBI was in nature of further investigation under S. 173 (8) of CrPC pursuant to direction of this Court - Petition dismissed.
