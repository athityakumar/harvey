A. Nawab John & Ors. v V.N. Subramaniyam

Supreme Court of India

03 July 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

J. Chelameswar, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 188; (2012) 7 SCC 738; 2012 (6) ALD(SC) 102; 2012 (5) AWC 4817; 2013 (7) Bom.C.R. 873; 2012 (3) CLT(SC) 178; 2012 (4) CTC 206; 2013 (1) MahLJ 1; 2012 (168) PLR 634; 2012 (3) RCR(Civil) 749; 2012 (117) RD 249; 2012(6) SCALE 143; [2012] 6 S.C.R. 369

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Keywords: Madras Court Fees Act, 1955

Summary: Civil Procedure - Administrative - Limitation Act, 1963, s. 5 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 149, O. 7 rr. 11, 8 - Tamil Nadu Court-Fees and Suits Valuation Act, 1955, ss. 4, 8, 12(2)(2), 12(1), 42 - Deficiency of court fees - Condonation of delay - Order of Court - Sustainability - Defendant no. 1 wanted to clear debts to two persons before property was actually conveyed to plaintiffs/appellants - Defendant no. 1 collected amount from appellants but, did not execute sale deed - Plaintiffs filed suit against defendant no. 1 - During pendency of suit, sole respondent/defendant no. 2, filed application praying that he be impleaded as party defendant to the said suit on ground that he purchased suit scheduled property for consideration - Application was allowed and respondent was impleaded as defendant no. 2 in suit - Respondent filed revision petitions challenging decision of Trial Court to condone delay in representations of plaint - During pendency of revision petitions, defendant no. 2 filed written statement and also filed IA no.3 of 2006, invoking O. 7 r. 11 of CPC to reject plaint - Plaintiffs filed IA no.1 of 2006 seeking amendment of plaint - IA No.1 of 2006 filed by plaintiffs was allowed - Respondent preferred revision to HC in revision petition no.769 of 2006, which was dismissed and revision in C.R.P. (PD) No.797 of 2006, filed challenging the same - Hence instant appeal - Whether finding of HC in 3 revision petitions disposed of by common Judgment under challenge was proper - Held, under O. 7 r. 11, plaint which was not properly valued relief claimed therein or was insufficiently stamped was liable to be rejected - If Court found that such suit was barred by limitation, once again it was required to be rejected under O. 7 r. 11(d) - Further, s. 149 CPC conferred power on Court to accept payment of deficit court fee even beyond period of limitation prescribed for filing of suit, if plaint was otherwise filed within period of limitation - Therefore, rigour of O. 7 r. 11 CPC and also s. 4 of the 1955 Act was mitigated to some extent by Parliament when it enacted s. 149 CPC - Judicial discretion was required to be exercised in accordance with settled principles of law, it should not be exercised in manner to confer unfair advantage on one of parties to litigation - In case where plaint was filed within period of limitation prescribed by law but with deficit court fee and plaintiff sought to make good deficit of court fee beyond period of limitation, Court, though had discretion u/s. 149 CPC, should scrutinise explanation offered for delayed payment of deficit court fee carefully because exercise of such discretion would certainly had some bearing on rights and obligations of defendants or persons claiming through defendants - Therefore, s. 149 CPC did not confer absolute right in favour of plaintiff to pay court fee as and when it pleased plaintiff, it only enabled plaintiff to seek indulgence of Court to permit payment of court fee at point of time later than presentation of plaint - Exercise of discretion by Court was conditional upon satisfaction of Court that plaintiff offered a legally acceptable explanation for not paying court fee within period of limitation - HC recorded its conclusion by holding that Trial Court erred in allowing IA nos.75 and 76 of 2004 by exercising discretion without analysing bona fides of plaintiffs case and without giving notice to defendant - Sufficient cause was not shown in two affidavits filed in support of application to condone delay of representation in IA no.76/2004 - Discretion u/s. 149 was not exercised by Trial Court in accordance with principles of law - Appeal dismissed.
