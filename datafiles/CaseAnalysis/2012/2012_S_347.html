Kanwar Singh Meena v State of Rajasthan and another

Supreme Court of India

16 October 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjana Prakash Desai, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 356; (2012) 12 SCC 180; AIR 2013 SC 296; 2012 ALL MR (Cri) 4074; JT 2012 (10) SC 262; 2012 (4) MLJ(Crl) 504; 2012 (4) RCR(Criminal) 955; 2012(10) SCALE 270; [2012] 10 S.C.R. 847; 2012 (4) WLN(SC) 64

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Due Course Of Justice

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 147 to 149, 364 and 302 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 164 and 439(1) - Murder - Bail - Challenged - Respondent No. 2 (accused) allegedly committed murder of brother of appellant (Complainant) and thereby allegedly committed offences u/ss. 147 to 149, 364 and 302 of IPC - By impugned order HC released accused on bail - Aggrieved appellant filed instant appeal - Appellant contended that HC without assigning any reasons, released accused on bail and that there was more than prima facie case against accused and that brother of accused was an IPS Officer was trying to exert pressure on IO - Whether impugned order of bail was liable to be quashed - Held, from complaint and statements recorded u/s. 164 of CrPC, prima facie appeared that there was illicit relationship between accused and 'R' (lady) - However, 'R' came in contact with deceased and intimate relationship developed between the two, which was not liked by accused - It appeared to be the case of investigating agency that, thus, accused eliminated the deceased with the help of his companions - Statements of 'K', watchman and 'R' recorded u/s. 164 of CrPC, appeared to be relevant as they prima facie indicate involvement of accused in the crime in question - HC ought not to have ignored those statements - HC in impugned order had referred to certain features of prosecution case, but that reference were is in the form of submissions made by accused and HC did not discussed those features - It has expressed no opinion as to why it was releasing the accused on bail - Relevant diary entry which did indicate that brother of accused tried to bring pressure on investigating agency, which was confirmed by affidavit filed by Dy. Commissioner of Police, that accused had made an effort to influence investigation - Trial Court was directed to frame the charges against accused - Impugned order of bail was quashed - Appeal disposed of.
