Mangal Amusement Park (P) Ltd. & Anr. v State Of Madhya Pradesh & Others

Supreme Court of India

28 August 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Gokhale, Surinder Singh Nijjar

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 260; (2012) 11 SCC 713; AIR 2012 SC 3325; 2012 (5) AWC 5186; 2012 (4) CLT(SC) 266; 2012 (5) CTC 329; 2012 (3) JabLJ 402; JT 2012 (8) SC 350; 2012 (4) RCR(Civil) 562; 2013 (118) RD 773; 2012(8) SCALE 19; [2012] 10 S.C.R. 388

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Indian Easements Act, 1882, Madhya Pradesh Nagar Tatha Gram Nivesh Adhiniyam, 1973

Summary: Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 105 - Change of land-use - Challenged - Respondent No. 3 floated tenders for setting up of an amusement park on a parcel of land owned by it - Under the then subsisting Development Plan the designated land-use was 'commercial' - Appellants were allotted said parcel of land for the establishment of a Children's amusement park - Accordingly, respondent No. 3 granted a license to the appellants - Later, respondent Nos. 1 (State) and 2 (Town Planning Dept.) initiated the process of modification of the Development Plan and it was proposed to change the user of this parcel of land from 'commercial' to 'regional park' - Appellants raised objection against the proposed modification which was rejected by respondent No. 1 vide letter dt. 23-9-2003 - Respondent No. 1, issued notification dt. 19-11-2003 changing the land-use from 'commercial' to 'regional park' - Respondent No. 1 also asked respondent No. 3 to invite the tenders afresh for the re-allotment of the plot - Appellants filed writ petition challenging said notification dt. 19-11-2003 and letter dt. 23-9-2003 - DB dismissed said writ petition - Hence, the instant appeal - Appellants contended that the document of allotment of the concerned parcel of land to the appellants was a document of lease and not simply a license, and that the appellants were entitled to the renewal thereof - (A) Whether the document of allotment of land was in any way a lease or a license - Held, lease was not a mere contract but envisages and transfers an interest in the demised property creating a right in favour of the lessee - As against that a license only makes an action lawful which without it would be unlawful, but does not transfer any interest in favour of the licensee in respect of the property - In the instant case, respondent No. 3 retained complete control over the concerned parcel of land - The manner in which the facilities in the amusement park were to be enjoyed was completely controlled by the respondent No. 3 - The document of allotment merely granted a permission to use the concerned parcel of land in a particular manner, and without creating any interest therein - Hence, the document of allotment was a license, and not a lease - (B) Legality of the letter dt. 23-9-2003 - Held, appellants have tried to make much ado about the stand which the respondent No. 3 took on earlier occasions in favour of the appellants - One has to recognize that where different authorities were dealing with a particular subject, it was quite possible that on some occasions, they may take a stand different from each other, though ultimately it was the decision of the competent authority which matters, and it cannot be tainted with mala fides merely on that count - Impugned judgment of the HC was upheld - Appeal dismissed.
