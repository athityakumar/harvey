Vijay Singh v State Of Delhi

Supreme Court of India

29 August 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 280; (2012) 8 SCC 763; AIR 2012 SC 3437; 2012 CRLJ 4527; 2012 (4) JCC 2523; JT 2012 (8) SC 340; 2013 (1) OLR 185; 2012 (4) RCR(Criminal) 532; 2012(8) SCALE 406; [2012] 7 S.C.R. 434

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Women & Children

Keywords: Offence Punishable, Uttar Pradesh Children Act, 1951

Summary: Criminal - Women & Children - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947, ss. 5(1)(c) and 5(2) - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120B and 409 - Corruption - Conviction - Challenged - Appellant No.1 (Assistant Divisional Manager of Company), in collusion with appellant no. 2 dishonestly and fraudulently misappropriated the Company's money, which was public money, for wrongful gain to appellant No. 2, thereby causing corresponding losses to the Company - Trial court convicted appellant Nos. 1 and 2 u/ss. 120B and 409 of IPC and u/s. 5(2) r/w s. 5(1)(c) of the 1947 Act - DB dismissed appeal filed against said order - Hence, the instant appeal - Appellants contended that FIR could not have been lodged for the offences punishable under the 1947 Act, which stood repealed by the 1988 Act - It was urged that in fact, by reason of repeal, proceedings under the 1947 Act stand obliterated - Whether order of the Trial court as affirmed by the DB could be upheld - Held, there was no substance in the contention that the appellants could not have been charged under the 1947 Act after its repeal - The offence was alleged to have been committed prior to the coming into force of the 1988 Act - When the offence was committed, the 1947 Act was in force - It was elementary that no person shall be convicted of any offence except for violation of a law in force at the time of commission of the act charged as an offence nor can he be subjected to a penalty greater than that which might have been inflicted under the law in force at the time of the commission of the offence - Appellants were, therefore, rightly charged, tried and convicted under the provisions of the 1947 Act - Provisions of the 1988 Act were more stringent than the provisions of the 1947 Act - Appellants cannot, therefore, be said to have been prejudiced - No interference in the impugned judgment of the DB which has confirmed the conviction and sentence of the appellants - However, sentence of 2 years rigorous imprisonment awarded to appellant No. 1 was reduced to 1 year rigorous imprisonment as appellant No. 1 was about 70 years' old and was suffering from various ailments - Appeals disposed of.
