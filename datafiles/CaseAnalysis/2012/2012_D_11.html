Kavita v Deepak And Others

Supreme Court of India

22 August 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 253; (2012) 8 SCC 604; 2012 (4) ACC 342; 2012 ACJ 2161; AIR 2012 SC 2893; 2012 (6) ALD(SC) 131; 2012(5) ALL MR 914; 2012 (94) ALR 691; 2012 (5) AWC 5046; 2013 (1) JabLJ 178; 2012 (6) MahLJ 748; 2013(1) M.P.H.T. 375; 2012 (4) MPLJ 485; 2013 (1) OLR 167; 2013 (169) PLR 140; 2012 (4) RLW 2900; 2012(7) SCALE 500; 2012 (3) WLN(SC) 58

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Insurance - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, s. 166 - Compensation - Enhancement - Appellant was suffered grievous injuries in an accident and disability was assessed around 90% - Appellant filed petition through her husband claiming compensation to the tune of Rs.85lakhs u/s. 166 of the 1988 Act, alleging that accident was caused due to rash and negligent driving of offending vehicle owned by respondent no. 1 and driven by respondent no. 2 -Tribunal passed award and ordained the respondents to pay total compensation of Rs.4 lakh with interest and concluded that accident took place due to rash and negligent driving of offending vehicle - Aggrieved by said order, appellant filed appeal before HC - HC allowed appeal and enhanced compensation by a sum of Rs.12,76,480/- with interest - Hence instant appeal - Appellant contended that though HC enhanced the compensation, the same could not be treated as just because as a result of the accident appellant suffered permanent disability and had virtually become a vegetable and would require treatment throughout her life - Further, compensation awarded under the head of pain and suffering was wholly inadequate and amount of compensation should be adequately enhanced keeping in view the fact that due to escalation in the cost of medical treatment, appellant would have to incur substantial expenses in future medical treatment, physiotherapy and nursing - Held, Tribunal held that appellant had suffered permanent disability however presumption could not be drawn that she suffered 75% permanent disability because she was still undergoing treatment and the doctor himself had deposed that final conclusion regarding permanent disability could not be established till the time treatment continues - HC did not record any finding on this issue but increased the amount awarded towards permanent disability and future loss of earning - Since discharge certificate was issued on 26-6-2004, the claimant had made little progress up till the time the disability certificate was issued on 23-8-2006 and even till date she continued to be in a vegetative state and require an attendant at all times and continued physiotherapy - Hence, it was not inclined to approve the approach of Tribunal and HC granting a lump sum compensation because both failed to take into consideration the loss of income during the period of treatment when the appellant was totally incapacitated - Even if the income of appellant was taken to be Rs.2,000/, the loss of income during the period of treatment, which continued till the judgment of HC i.e. from 2-5-2004 to 18-5-2010 would be Rs.1,47,000/approximately - Therefore, by applying the multiplier of 17, the future loss of earning would come to Rs.3,67,200/- - Raj Kumar v. Ajay Kumar, 2010 Indlaw SC 996 relied on, Tribunal and HC erred in failing to award compensation under the heads of loss of amenities and loss of expectation of life - Impugned judgment of HC was modified - Appeal partly allowed.
