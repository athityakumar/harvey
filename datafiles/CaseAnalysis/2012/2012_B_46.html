Brijesh Mavi v State Of Nct Of Delhi

Supreme Court of India

03 July 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjan Gogoi, Swatanter Kumar

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 192; (2012) 7 SCC 45; AIR 2012 SC 2657; 2012 CRLJ 3616; 2012 (3) JCC 1999; 2012 (4) MLJ(Crl) 278; 2012 (3) RCR(Criminal) 681; 2012(6) SCALE 132; [2012] 6 S.C.R. 803

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302 and 460 r/w. s. 34 - Arms Act, 1959, s. 25 - House trespassing and Murder - Conviction - Challenged - Appellant allegedly fired deceased consequence of which he died - Trial court convicted appellant u/ss. 302 and 460 r/w. s. 34 of IPC and u/s. 25 of Act - On appeal, HC affirmed the order of the trial court and convicted appellant u/ss. 302 and 460 r/w. s. 34 of IPC and u/s. 25 of Act - Hence, the instant appeal - Whether order of the trial court as affirmed by the HC could be upheld - Held, there was no direct evidence to connect the appellant with the firing incident involving the deceased as the only eye-witness examined by the prosecution, namely, PW 1 has categorically deposed that the appellant was not present at the place of the crime on the date of occurrence and, in fact, he had seen the appellant for the first time in court - The second person accompanying the deceased to the STD booth along with the firearm therefore remained unidentified - The prosecution, in the absence of any direct evidence, has sought to build up its case on the basis of circumstantial evidence - The prosecution has succeeded in proving a highly incriminating circumstance against the petitioner, yet, SC do not consider that it would be wholly safe to hold that the only conclusion that can follow from the aforesaid proved circumstance was that the appellant was responsible for the death of the deceased - In a situation where co-accused had died during the trial and the other co-accused had been acquitted by the HC, the culpability of the present appellant with the aid of s. 34 of IPC will be open to serious doubt - Such culpability will have to be determined on the basis of individual overt acts on the part of the appellant for which SC do not find any cogent and reliable material on record - Consequently, while the conviction of appellant u/s. 25 of Act and the sentence imposed was justified, the appellant was entitled to the benefit of doubts with regard to the offences u/ss. 302 and 460 r/w. s. 34 of IPC - Hence, order of the HC insofar as the offence u/ss. 302 and 460 r/w. s. 34 of IPC was concerned was set aside - Conviction of the appellant u/s. 25 of Act was upheld - Appeals disposed of.
