L. K. Venkat and another v Union of India and another

Supreme Court of India

01 May 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 165; (2012) 5 SCC 292; AIR 2012 SC 2503; 2012 (93) ALR 927; 2012 CRLJ 2522; JT 2012 (5) SC 438; 2012 (3) MLJ(Crl) 566; 2012 (2) RCR(Civil) 953; 2012 (2) RCR(Criminal) 883; 2012(5) SCALE 35

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 72 - Terrorist and Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Act, 1987, ss. 3, 4 and 15 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120B and 302 - Transfer of petitions - Petitioners and some others were convicted by the Special Judge for offences u/s. 302 r/w s. 120B of IPC and ss. 3, 4 and 15 of TADA and were sentenced to death - Appeals filed thereagainst were dismissed by SC - Mercy petitions filed by the petitioners were rejected by the President of India - Thereafter, they filed three writ petitions for quashing the rejection of the petitions filed by them u/art. 72 of the Constitution on the ground of violation of the principles laid down in various judgments of SC - Instant petition filed seeking transfer of the writ petitions by asserting that hearing thereof in the Madras HC may not be possible in congenial atmosphere because of the agitation launched by different political outfits, extremist groups and lawyers and also because thousands of people gathered in the HC premises and raised slogans outside and inside the Court premise - It was also submitted that question raised in the writ petitions pending before the HC was identical to the question raised in the cases of Devender Pal Singh Bhullar and Mahendra Nath Das, which were pending before SC - Whether writ petitions to be transferred to SC - Held, question which arise for consideration in the writ petitions filed was whether long delay in the decision of the mercy petitions entitled the convicts to seek commutation of death sentence was similar to the one raised in the cases filed by Devender Pal Singh Bhullar and Mahendra Nath Das - Hence, question was of substantial general importance and decision thereof was likely to affect large number of persons who have been convicted by the competent Courts and sentenced to death and whose mercy petitions have remained pending for years together - Therefore, it would be in the interest of justice to transfer the three writ petitions pending before the Madras HC to SC - Petitions allowed.
