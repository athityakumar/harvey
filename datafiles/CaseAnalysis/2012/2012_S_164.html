Syed Ahmed v State Of Karnataka

Supreme Court of India

31 July 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Madan B. Lokur, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 440; (2012) 8 SCC 527; AIR 2012 SC 3359; 2012 CRLJ 4017; 2012 (3) JCC 2266; 2013 (1) KarLJ 509; 2012 (4) MLJ(Crl) 263; 2012 (4) RCR(Criminal) 196; [2012] 7 S.C.R. 887

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Illegal Gratification

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 13(1)(d), 13(2), 7, 7(d) - Illegal Gratification - Discrepancies - Acquittal - Reversed - Sustainability - Appellant-accused was acquitted by Trial Court of offence u/ss. 7,13(1)(d) r/w s. 13(2) of Act, but in appeal HC, set aside order of acquittal and convicted appellant for same offence - Prosecution case was that complainant had a land dispute with neighbour even after entering into amicable settlement and when complainant approached appellant who was police office, he demanded illegal gratification - Complainant complaint with Lok Ayukta police and appellant was trapped taking illegal gratification - Trial Judge concluded that prosecution had failed to prove its case beyond a reasonable doubt and further held that land dispute of complainant amicably settled and so there was no occasion for complainant to demand any gratification - On appeal by State HC, set aside acquittal order and convicted appellant holding that there was no reason to disbelieve complainant - Hence instant appeal - Appellant pleaded that he was falsely framed in case due to previous enmity and further that there were discrepancies in statement of witnesses - Whether HC, was justified in reversing order of acquittal to conviction and whether there was substantial evidence against appellant - Held, discrepancy with regard to attire of appellant, and Rs.10/currency note and forensic examination of wallet were rather minor matters - This had been subject matter of discussion in Abdul Nawaz v. State of West Bengal, 2012 (5) SCALE 357 2012 Indlaw SCO 123, it was held that a discrepancy would be minor if it did not affect substratum of prosecution's case or impact on core issue, in such an event, minor discrepancy could be ignored - Evidence of witnesses that there was sufficient evidence of appellant demanding illegal gratification from complainant and receiving and accepting it when given by him, on this basis, SC, found no reason to interfere with judgment and order under appeal - Appeal dismissed.
