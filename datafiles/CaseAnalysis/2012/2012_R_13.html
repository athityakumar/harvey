Special Officer, Commerce, North Eastern Electricity Company of Orissa (NESCO) and another v Raghunath Paper Mills Private Limited and another

Supreme Court of India

09 November 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, Ranjan Gogoi

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 419; (2012) 13 SCC 479; 2012 (4) CLT(SC) 494; JT 2012 (11) SC 289; 2012(11) SCALE 82; [2012] 13 S.C.R. 71

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Electricity & Energy

Summary: Electricity - Corporate - Orissa Electricity Regulatory Commission Distribution (Conditions of Supply), Code, 2004 - Electricity dues - Liability - Challenged - Pursuant to the order of the Company Judge, Official Liquidator made an advertisement for sale of movable and immovable assets and properties of the Factory Unit of X which was in liquidation on - Sale was confirmed in favour of respondent No. 1 being the highest bidder, and the possession of the Unit was handed over - Since there was no power supply, respondent No.1 made an application to the CEO, North Eastern Electricity Supply Company of Orissa Limited (NESCO) for restoration of the same - NESCO directed respondent No. 1 to pay the arrears of electricity dues amounting to Rs. 79,02,262/- outstanding against the premises in question - Aggrieved respondent No. 1 filed petition before HC Single Judge for quashing of the demand letter by NESCO - Single Judge allowed the petition and directed NESCO to provide electricity - On further appeal DB dismissed the appeal and confirmed the order of Single Judge - Hence, instant appeal - Whether a Company, which purchased the property of another Company under liquidation through auction, was liable to pay the arrears of electricity dues outstanding against the erstwhile Company - Held, in the light of regn. 13 (10)(b) of the Code, said clause applied to a request for transfer of service connection but not to a fresh connection - Interpretation of said clause by Single Judge as well as by DB was correct being reasonable, just and fair - Haryana State Electricity Board v. Hanuman Rice Mills, Dhanauri and Others, Indlaw SC 651, relied on - Specific factual details regarding the position of respondent No. 1 which purchased the said premises under court auction sale from the Official Liquidator on 'as is where is' and 'whatever there is' basis and in the light of the regulations, particularly, regn. 13(10)(b) it was held that the request was not for the transfer from previous owner to purchaser, on the other hand, it was a request for a fresh connection for the Unit of respondent No. 1 - Impugned order passed by Single judge and DB was upheld - Appeal dismissed.
