Jile Singh v State of Uttar Pradesh and another

Supreme Court of India

12 January 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, H.L. Gokhale

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 597; (2012) 3 SCC 383; 2012 CRLJ 1603; JT 2012 (3) SC 497; 2012 (2) MLJ(Crl) 222; 2012 (1) RCR(Criminal) 583; [2012] 1 S.C.R. 201; 2012 (1) WLN(SC) 77

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 200,202,319 - Murder - No Evidence - Private Complaint - Summons - Sustainability - Complainant-respondent No. 2 was murdered and FIR was registered under unknown persons and on investigation, IO, submitted chargesheet naming 'H' as an accused and on basis of material collected by IO, no case was found against appellant, IO, concluded that appellant had falsely named appellant - Aggrieved respondent No. 2 filed complaint u/s. 200 of CrPC against appellant and another for murder of his son - Magistrate after recording statements u/s. 202 of CrPC - issued summons to appellant - Appellant filed revision before HC, which was dismissed - Hence instant appeal - Appellant, pleaded that issuance of summons by Magistrate, on a private complaint after committal of accused for murder, was without jurisdiction and any addition of a new person to array of accused in a case pending before sessions court could only be done by that court in exercise of the power u/s. 319 of CrPC and in no other way - Whether Magistrate Court was justified in issuing summons to appellant which was upheld by HC - Held, order passed by Magistrate, in issuing summons against appellant on complaint filed by respondent No. 2-complainant, which had been confirmed by HC, was allowed to stand, it would mean addition of appellant to array of accused in a pending case before Sessions Judge at a stage prior to collecting any evidence by that court - This was absolutely impermissible in view of law laid down by a 3 Judge Bench of SC, in Ranjit Singh 1998 Indlaw SC 1220 - wherein it was held that, Magistrate could not have issued process against those persons who may have been named in FIR as accused persons, but not charge-sheeted by police u/s. 173 CrPC - They could be arrayed as 'accused persons' in exercise of powers u/s. 319 CrPC, when some evidence or materials were brought on record in course of trial or they could also be arrayed as 'accused persons' only when a reference was made either by Magistrate while passing an order of commitment or by Sessions Judge to HC, and HC, on examining materials, comes to conclusion that sufficient materials exist against them even though police might not have filed charge-sheet - Appeal allowed.
