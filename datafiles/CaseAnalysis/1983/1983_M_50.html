Deputy Commissioner of Sales Tax (Law), Board of Revenue (Taxes), Ernakulam v Motor Industries Company, Ernakulam

Supreme Court of India

18 February 1983

Case Analysis

Bench

E.S. Venkataramiah, P.N. Bhagwati

Where Reported

1983 Indlaw SC 288; (1983) 2 SCC 108; AIR 1983 SC 370; 1983(1) SCALE 145; [1983] 2 S.C.R. 384; 1983 (53) STC 48; 1983 TAXLR 2857; 1983 UJ 292

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Keywords: Value Of Goods, Trade Discount, Returned Goods, Practice

Summary: Sales Tax - Indirect Tax - Kerala General Sales Tax Act, 1963 - Kerala General Sales Tax Rules, 1963 rr. 9(a), 9(b)(i) - Assessment Year (A/y) 1973-74 - Assessment - Claims of exemption - Maintainability - Respondent/assessee filed assessment for A/y 1973-74 under the Act and claimed as exemption 'service discount' u/r. 9(a) of the Rules and further amount u/r. 9(b)(i) of the Rules being 'value of goods returned' - Appellant/Assistant Commissioner of Sales Tax ( Assessment) disallowed claims - Assessee filed appeal before Deputy Commissioner, Agricultural Income-Tax and Sales Tax (Appeal), in which exemption claimed in respect of 'service discount' was allowed, but claim in respect of 'sales returns' was allowed to extent of turnover of Rs. 55270 being turnover of goods returned within period of 3 months from date of sale - Appeal filed against that order by department before Appellate Tribunal was dismissed - Against order of the Tribunal, Department filed revision petition before HC of Kerala which again was dismissed by its judgment dt. 11-6-1981 - Hence instant appeals - Whether impugned judgment of HC was justified - Held, service said to have been rendered by buyers for securing 'service discount' was integral part of transaction of sale itself which incidentally confers on assessee benefit of popularisation of assessee's goods in market - Discount so allowed was merely percentage of price of goods sold which had nothing to do with any other goods supplied or other service rendered by buyers to assessee - Fact that discount was not allowed at time of sale but on later date at end of month would not make it any-the-less trade discount - Therefore, HC had rightly upheld deduction of 'service discount' claimed in this case by assessee from total turnover - Further, deduction in respect of 'sales return' had to be allowed in assessment relating to financial year in which sales of returned goods had taken place and even where assessment for that year was completed - Department had to comply with demand for adjustment or refund by making necessary rectification in order of assessment, provided that other conditions were satisfied, as that was inevitable consequence of r. 9(b)(i) of the Rules which allowed deduction of value of goods returned within 3 months from date of their delivery from total turnover of that assessment year - But in any view of matter it was not possible to hold that such deduction in respect of returned goods could be claimed in assessment proceedings for financial year subsequent to financial year in which sales had taken place - Therefore, orders of assessment for A/Y should be modified accordingly, as consequence of this decision, order of A/Y 1972-73 should be rectified in accordance with this judgment - Appeals disposed of.
