Income Tax Officer, New Delhi, and Others v Rattan Lal and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 August 1983

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Madon, V. Balakrishna Eradi, V.D. Tulzapurkar

Where Reported

1983 Indlaw SC 202; (1986) Supp SCC 370; AIR 1984 SC 989; 1984 (38) CTR 382; [1984] 145 ITR 183; [1984] 16 TAXMAN 25

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax and Direct Taxes - Finance Act, 1965, s.24; Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 68 - Income Tax - (A) Whether the immunity enjoyable by the declarants u/s. 24 of the Finance Act of 1965 under the Voluntary Disclosure Scheme should be confined to the declarants or could be extended to the assessment of a third party (the assessee before the ITO) - Such immunity is confined to declarants alone and cannot be excluded to assessment of a third party - (B) ITO not satisfied with explanation of the assessee about the genuineness or source of amounts found credited in his books - These already subject matter of declaration by creditors - Treatment while computation - Held, ITO can include it in income of assessee , no double taxation involved - Appeal allowed
