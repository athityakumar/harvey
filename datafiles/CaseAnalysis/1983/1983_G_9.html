 Charan Lal Sahu and Others v Giani Zail Singh and Another

Supreme Court of India

13 December 1983

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.V. Chandrachud, P.N. Bhagwati, Amarendra Nath Sen, D.P. Madon, M.P. Thakkar

Where Reported

1983 Indlaw SC 250; (1984) 1 SCC 390; AIR 1984 SC 309; 1983(2) SCALE 961; [1984] 2 S.C.R. 6; 1984 UJ 873

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Election; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Election - Presidential and Vice-Presidential Elections Act, 1952, ss. 5 B(1)(a), 13(a), 14 and 14 A - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 171 C - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 71(1) - Election petitions u/s. 14 of 1952 Act to challenge election of Giani Zail Singh, as President of India - (A) Determination of effect of non-compliance with mandatory provisions of s. 5 B(1)(a) in nomination papers of petitioners - Held, claim to have been duly nominated cannot be made - (B) Provision contained in s. 5 B (1)(a) challenged on ground of its alleged unreasonableness - Further held, it has no substance in it as validity of that provision was upheld by Supreme Court in Charan Lal Sahu v. Neelam Sanjeeva Reddy - (C) Determination of locus standi - Held, they have no locus standi to file election petitions since they were neither duly nominated nor can they claim to have been duly nominated as candidates at presidential election - (D) Provisions of s. 18(1)(a) of 1952 Act - (1) Alleged exercise of undue influence over voters through his confidants (with his consent) by returned candidate - And also canvassing, use of Govt. machinery, abuse of official position and appeal to communal sentiments - Further held, election would not be invalidated - (2) Whether provision is ultravires art. 70(1) of Constitution? - Further held, s. 18(1) is not ultra vires - Petition dismissed.
