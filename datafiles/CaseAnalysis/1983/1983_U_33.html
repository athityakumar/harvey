P.K. Ramachandra Iyer and Others v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

16 December 1983

Case Analysis

Bench

D.A. Desai, V. Balakrishna Eradi

Where Reported

1983 Indlaw SC 248; (1984) 2 SCC 141; (1984) SCC (L&S) 214; AIR 1984 SC 514; 1984 LabIC 301; 1984 (1) LLJ 314; 1984 (1) LLN 433; 1983(2) SCALE 1060; [1984] 2 S.C.R. 200; 1984 (1) SLJ 474; 1983 (3) SLR 495

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Agricultural Produce Cess Act, 1940, Oil and Natural Gas Commission, University Grants Commission

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 12, 32 and 14 - (A) Whether petitioner have legal right to challenge appointment of respondent no. 6? - Held, no - Board of Governors, which is in charge of general superintendence, direction and control of affairs of Society and of its income and property is also largely controlled by nominees of State and Central Govts. - It will thus be seen that that State Govt. and by reason of provision for approval, Central Govt. also, have full control of working of Society and it would not be incorrect to say that Society is merely a projection of State - (B) Whether there is any substance in contention applying amplified essential qualification, respondent No 6 was not qualified for being selected for post? - Held, it is well-settled that experience to be of value and utility must be acquired after educational qualification is obtained and not while acquiring postgraduate qualification - Respondent No 6 appeared not to carry on research in line of Nutrition or protein Chemistry and therefore, he did not fulfil qualification - (C) Whether Committee had power to relax essential qualification pertaining to experience? - Held, by illegal selection respondent No. 6 has reached scale of Rs. 1800-2250 while Dr. Gupta is in scale of Rs. 1500-2000 - Respondent No. 6, Dr. Mehta is enjoying this utterly undeserved benefit - Respondent No. 6 was ineligible for post when selected - Selection of respondent No. 6 and appointment would be required to be quashed and set aside - (D) Whether without any fresh application would be called for interview by Second Selection Committee? - Held, petitioner had applied earlier and was entitled to be called for interview as noted in proceedings - It was obligatory upon second Selection Committee to inform Dr. Raman to appear for interview and adequate steps should have been taken to give intimation because he was attached to institute and was in active service of institute - Appeals and petitions allowed.
