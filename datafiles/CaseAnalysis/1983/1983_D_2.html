Gopalakrishna Menon And Another v D. Raja Reddy And Another

Supreme Court of India

05 September 1983

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranganath Misra, D.A. Desai

Where Reported

1983 Indlaw SC 98; (1983) 4 SCC 240; (1983) SCC (Cr) 822; AIR 1983 SC 1053; 1983 (2) Crimes 584; 1983 CRLJ 1599; 1983 CrLR(SC) 449; 1983(2) SCALE 236; [1983] 3 S.C.R. 836

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860 ss. 467, 471, 34 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 s. 195(1) (b) (ii) - Forged receipt - Complaint - Maintainability - Appellants entered into agreement with respondent no1. For carrying printing business and had to deposit Rs. 20,000 with respondent no.1 and agreed to pay Rs. 500 per month and 50 per cent of net profits - Disputes arose between them and respondent no.1 filed suit for mandatory injunction and recovery of damages and appellants filed another suit for refund of deposit amount and amount paid to respondent no.1 through cash and cheque - After production of money receipt in court, respondent no.1 filed complaint against appellants for commission of offences punishable u/ss. 467, 471 of IPC - Appellants after receiving summons from trial court, objected to maintainability of criminal action and moved HC for quashing of said proceedings, which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether in the absence of necessary complaint by Civil court where money receipt alleged to have been forged was produced, prosecution for offences punishable u/ss. 467,471 r/w s. 34 of IPC is maintainable.

Held, s. 463of IPC defines forgery and s. 467 of IPC punishes forgery of a particular category, the provision u/s. 195 (1) (b) (ii) of CrPC would immediately be attracted and on the basis that the offence punishable u/s. 467 of IPC is an offence described u/s. 463 of IPC and in the absence of a complaint by the Court the prosecution would not be maintainable. Thus, HC took a wrong view of the matter and the prosecution in the instant case on the basis of a private complaint and in the absence of a complaint from the appropriate civil court where the alleged fraudulent receipt has been produced, would not be sustainable. Hence, if the prosecution is allowed to continue, serious prejudice would be caused to the appellants and they would be called upon to face a trial which would not be sustainable, appeal is allowed and decision of the HC is set aside and quash the complaint case filed against the appellants. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - In the absence of a complaint in writing of the Civil Court where the alleged forged receipt has been produced, taking cognizance of the offence would be bad in law.
