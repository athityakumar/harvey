H. M. Kamaluddin Ansari and Company and another v Union Of India

Supreme Court of India

12 August 1983

Case Analysis

Bench

R.B. Misra, D.A. Desai, V. Balakrishna Eradi

Where Reported

1983 Indlaw SC 183; (1983) 4 SCC 417; AIR 1984 SC 29; 1983 ALJ 1104; 1983 ARBLR 293; 1983(2) SCALE 107; [1983] 3 S.C.R. 607; 1983 UJ 769

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Arbitration and ADR - Arbitration Act, 1940, s.41- Contract Act, 1872, ss. 10, 73 and 74 - Question regarding: (A) Interpretation of cl. 18 of the general conditions of contract contained in the standard from of contract entered into by the parties and (B) Ambit and scope of s. 41 of the Arbitration Act - Held, cl. 18 does not contemplate sum due; it gives wide powers to UOI to recover amount claimed by appropriating any sum then due or which at any time thereafter may become due to the contractor under other contracts - (B) Held, only for the purpose of and in relation to arbitration proceedings' before Court - Appeal dismissed.
