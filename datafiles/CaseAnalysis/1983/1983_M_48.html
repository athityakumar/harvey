Bhagwan Swaroop and Others v Mool Chand and Others

Supreme Court of India

03 February 1983

Case Analysis

Bench

D.A. Desai, Amarendra Nath Sen

Where Reported

1983 Indlaw SC 408; (1983) 2 SCC 132; AIR 1983 SC 355; 1983 (9) ALR 170; 1983(1) SCALE 204; 1983 UJ 313

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - CPC, 1908, O. 22, r. 4 and O.1, r. 10 - Impleading of LRs - Determination of effect of not bringing LRs on record within time - Preliminary decree after partition - During appeal respondent died - Application after 3 years by LRs under O. 1, r. 10, CPC, 1908 rejected by HC as appeal abate - Held, laws of procedure are devised for advancing justice and not imposing the same - But may result in miscarriage of justice - But per A. N. Sen, J. procedural laws have not been enacted merely to be brushed aside by the Court - enacted for expeditious disposal of cases in the interest of proper administration of justice - However in the peculiar facts and circumstances of the case, order of HC is set aside - Matter Remitted To High Court.
