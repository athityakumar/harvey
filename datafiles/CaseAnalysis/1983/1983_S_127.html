Babu Ram And Others v State Of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

01 February 1983

Case Analysis

Bench

R.B. Misra, E.S. Venkataramiah

Where Reported

1983 Indlaw SC 58; (1983) 2 SCC 21; (1983) SCC (Cr) 332; AIR 1983 SC 308; 1983 ALJ 826; 1983 (1) Crimes 485; 1983 CRLJ 334; 1983 CrLR(SC) 178; 1983 MLJ 478; 1983(1) SCALE 105; [1983] 2 S.C.R. 328; 1983 (318) UJ 2

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Faecal Matter

Summary: Criminal - CrPC, 1973, ss. 378 and 386 - Whether appellate Court should interfere in an appeal against acquittal when there is possibility of another view and there was reasonable finding by trial Court - Held, appellate Court should be slow in disturbing the finding of fact of the trial court and if two view are reasonably possible of the evidence on the record, it is not 'expected to interfere simple because it feels that it would have taken a different view if the case had been tried by it - Appeal allowed.
