Deena @ Deena Dayal etc. v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

23 September 1983

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.V. Chandrachud, R.S. Pathak, S. Mukharji

Where Reported

1983 Indlaw SC 264; (1983) 4 SCC 645; (1983) SCC (Cr) 879; AIR 1983 SC 1155; 1983 (2) Crimes 770; 1983 CRLJ 1602; 1983 CRLR 585; 1983(2) SCALE 340; [1984] 1 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Human Rights

Keywords: Cyanide

Summary: Constitution - Criminal - Human Rights - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Commissions of Inquiry Act, 1952 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Conviction - Death sentence - Brutality - Violation - Trial Court convicted petitioners for murder and sentenced to death - Petitioners appealed to HC was dismissed - Petitioners filed review and S.L.P were rejected - Petitioners applied for mercy petition was dismissed - Petitioners contended that mode of execution of death sentence was cruel and barbarous method and in violation of art. 21 of Constitution - Hence instant petitions - Whether mode of execution of death sentence was in violation of art.21 of constitution - Held, State had discharged burden to prove that method of hanging prescribed by s. 354(5) of 1973 Code did not violate guarantee contained in art. 21 of Constitution - Material on record make it clear that system of hanging easy to assemble as preliminaries to act of hanging were quick and simple - Chances of accident during course of hanging could safely be excluded - Method was quick and certain means of executing extreme penalty of law eliminates possibility of lingering death - System was consistent with obligation of State to ensure that process of execution was conducted with decency and decorum without involving degradation of brutality - Torture, brutality, barbarity, humiliation and degradation of kind was impermissible in execution of sentence - Hence, method prescribed by s. 354(5) of 1973 Code for executing death sentence did not violate provision in art. 21 of Constitution - Petitions disposed of.
