State of Uttar Pradesh v District Judge, Unnao and Others

Supreme Court of India

06 December 1983

Case Analysis

Bench

D.A. Desai, D.P. Madon

Where Reported

1983 Indlaw SC 299; (1984) 2 SCC 673; AIR 1984 SC 1401; 1984 ALJ 175; 1983(2) SCALE 1035

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 226, 227 - Objects of arts. 226 and 227 - Appeal against order of High Court declining to interfere with the order of the learned District Judge even though it was satisfied that the appellant had established that it was prevented by a sufficient cause from preferring the appeal in time - Learned Judge was of the view that ' if I had considered the matter as the Court of first instance I might have taken a different view, but unless it can be held that the view taken by the learned District Judge was not a plausible view that cannot be reversed in exercise of the powers u/art. 226 of the Constitution' - Held, art.227 or art. 226 were devised to advance justice and not to thwart it; rigid and inflexible view of jurisdiction u/art. 227 leading to injustice is not proper - Appeal allowed.
