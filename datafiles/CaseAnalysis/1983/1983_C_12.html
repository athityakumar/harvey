Dr. S.S. Khanna v Chief Secretary, Patna and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 April 1983

Case Analysis

Bench

E.S. Venkataramiah, Amarendra Nath Sen

Where Reported

1983 Indlaw SC 238; (1983) 3 SCC 42; (1983) SCC (Cr) 562; AIR 1983 SC 595; 1983 (1) Crimes 1049; 1983 CRLJ 1044; 1983 CrLR(SC) 329; [1983] 2 S.C.R. 724

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Socio-Economic

Keywords: Food Adulteration

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, ss. 202 and 319 - Nature of enquiry u/s. 202 - Complaint against a person dismissed after an enquiry u/s. 202 - Summoning u/s. 319 for standing trial for the same offence or connected offence along with other accused persons - Whether it is legal? - Held, an inquiry u/s. 202 of Code is not in nature of a trial and said proceedings are not strictly proceedings between complainant and accused - A person against whom a complaint is filed does not become an accused until it is decided to issue process against him and even if he participates in proceedings u/s. 202 of Code, he does so not as an accused but as a member of public - The object of inquiry u/s. 202 is ascertainment of fact whether complaint has any valid foundation calling for issue of process to person complained against or whether it is a baseless one on which no action need be taken - The section does not require any adjudication to be made about guilt or otherwise of person against whom complaint is preferred and such a person cannot even be legally called to participate in proceedings u/s. 202 of Code - If prosecution can at any stage produce evidence which satisfies court that other accused or those who have not been arrayed as accused against whom proceedings have been quashed have also committed offence Court can take cognizance against them and try them along with other accused - In view of industrial peace, no interference of order of HC - Proceedings initiated against appellant dropped - Appeal allowed.
