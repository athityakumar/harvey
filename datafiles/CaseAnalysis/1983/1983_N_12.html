Narinder Singh v Surjit Singh

Supreme Court of India

26 October 1983

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, R.B. Misra, M.P. Thakkar

Where Reported

1983 Indlaw SC 315; (1984) 2 SCC 402; AIR 1984 SC 1359; 1983(2) SCALE 894

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Constitution - Election - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 141 - High Court striking off paragraphs of election petition - SC set aside order - Scope of this case by restricted to: framing of issue in the context of allegations incorporated in paragraphs (29 and 36) of the election petition which were originally struck off by the High Court, but restores by this (Supreme)Court as that part of the High Court order was set aside by this Court's order - Held, High Court to frame issue in respect of paragraphs as they stand and then take evidence of both the parties and then give a final verdict on the issues and record its finding and submit the same to Supreme Court after - Appeal allowed
