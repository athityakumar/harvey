Haji Siddik Haji Umar and Others v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

18 January 1983

Case Analysis

Bench

E.S. Venkataramiah, A.P. Sen, R.B. Misra

Where Reported

1983 Indlaw SC 284; (1983) 1 SCC 408; AIR 1983 SC 259; 1983(1) SCALE 48; [1983] 2 S.C.R. 249; 1983 UJ 196

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Keywords: Possession of property

Summary: Land & Property - Administrative - Civil Procedure - Junagadh State Evacuee (Administration of Property) Act, 1948, s. 27 - Displaced Persons (Compensation and Rehabilition) Act, 1954, s. 12 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 80 - Possession of property - Entitlement - A notice was issued by Asst. Custodian who exercised certain specified powers of Custodian under the 1948 Act on 1-5-1948 in respect of properties of H as he had not returned to Junagadh and was away for more than 6 months - No specific reason was, however, given in reply for long delay in return of H - Since H had not returned, possession of suit properties was taken by Custodian between 23-9-1948 and 1-10-1948 as evacuee properties - By an order dt. 1-3-1949 H was declared to be a non-evacuee and ice factory, one of suit properties, was ordered to be returned to his sons - Custodian purporting to exercise his power under the 1948 Act treated H as an evacuee and directed that his ice factory should be taken possession of as an evacuee property - Ice factory was leased out by State Govt. in favour of a refugee called S - Since H had not yet returned to India, his sons filed an appeal before District Judge against order declaring him as an evacuee - Against the order passed by Custodian on appeal, a revision petition was filed before Custodian General u/s. 27 of the 1948 - Custodian General dismissed revision petition by his order dt. 9-8-1950 and order was allowed to become final - A notification u/s. 12 of the 1954 was issued in respect of properties in question on 8-6-1955 - No further effective action was taken by H against this notification except writing letters to ministers - H died on 22-5-1956 and his heirs and legal representatives/appellants filed suit after issuing required notice u/s. 80 of CPC - It was stated that Special Custodian Officer had taken possession of suit properties between 23-9-1948 and 1-10-1948 illegally acting in abuse of his power - Appellants/plaintiffs demanded return of suit properties and also damages for illegal possession from 1-10-1948 to 1-1-1959 - Trial Court recorded its finding and dismissed the suit - Appellant preferred appeal before HC, which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether HC was justified in passing the impugned order - Held, it could not be disputed that suit properties had taken over under the 1948 Act as evacuee properties and had continued to be in possession of Custodian till the 194 Act was passed - It was not possible to hold that they were not evacuee properties and they was dealt with under the 1948 Act as evacuee properties which had duly vested in Custodian under the 1948 Act - S. 46 of the 1948 Act which was worded very widely bars jurisdiction of civil or Revenue Courts in regard to matters mentioned therein - No such Court could entertain any suit or proceeding in which question whether any property was or was not evacuee property arose or in which legality of any action taken by Custodian General or Custodian under the 1948 Act was questioned - Any matter which Custodian General or Custodian was empowered to determine by or under the 1948 Act was also outside the jurisdiction of any such Court - However, it was not open to Civil Court in instant case to decide whether suit properties were evacuee properties or not - It was also not open to it to decide correctness of order of Custodian General dt. 9-8-1950 declining to interfere with order of Custodian dt. 2-6-1950 - Question whether a certificate should be issued by Central Govt. also was by implication barred as it was Custodian who had to restore property after holding an enquiry into title of evacuee when an application was made to him alongwith a certificate issued by Central Govt. and a certificate of that nature by itself should be of no use - Neither H nor after his death appellants questioned these orders before HC or SC - Thus, they became final and were beyond jurisdiction of Civil Court - High Court was justified in dismissing the appeal preferred by appellant - Appeal dismissed.
