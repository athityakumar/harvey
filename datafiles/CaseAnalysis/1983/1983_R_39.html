Biharilal Dobray v Roshan Lal Dobray

Supreme Court of India

23 November 1983

Case Analysis

Bench

E.S. Venkataramiah, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali

Where Reported

1983 Indlaw SC 175; (1984) 1 SCC 551; AIR 1984 SC 385; 1984 ALJ 203; 1983(2) SCALE 761; [1984] 1 S.C.R. 877; 1984 UJ 141

Case Digest

Subject: Election; Service

Keywords: Office Of Profit Under, Himachal Pradesh Board of School Education Act, 1968, Durgah Khawaja Saheb Act, 1955, Gujarat Panchayats Act, 1961, Uttar Pradesh Intermediate Education Act, 1921, Uttar Pradesh Basic Educational Staff rules 1973

Summary: Election - Representation of the People Act, 1951, s. 100(1) - Constitution, arts.191(1)(a), 58(2), 66(4) and 102(1)(a) - U.P. Basic Education Act, 1972, ss. 4, 6, 7 and 13 - 'Office of profit under the Government' under Constitution and 1951 Act - Whether an Assistant Teacher employed in a Basic Primary School run by the Uttar Pradesh Board of Basic Education constituted under the 1972 Act is disqualified for being chosen as a member of the State Legislative Assembly u/art.191 (1)(a) of the Constitution - Held, holding an office of profit under the State Govt. and his nomination was rightly rejected by the Returning Officer - Appeal allowed.
