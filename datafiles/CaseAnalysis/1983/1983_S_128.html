Sanjit Roy v State Of. Rajasthan

Supreme Court of India

20 January 1983

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Bhagwati, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1983 Indlaw SC 62; (1983) SCC (L&S) 217; (1983) 1 SCC 525; AIR 1983 SC 328; 1983 LabIC 312; 1983 (1) LLJ 220; 1983 (1) LLN 522; 1983(1) SCALE 38; [1983] 2 S.C.R. 271; 1983 UJ 161

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 23 and 14 - Rajasthan Famine Relief Works Employees (Exemption from Labour Laws) Act, 1964, ss. 1 and 3 - Minimum Wages Act, 1948, s. 1 - Workmen employed on famine relief work i.e preparing road - (A) Determination of constitutionality of 1964 Act as excluding applicability of 1948 Act - (B) Determination of legality of provision under 1964 Act providing for payment of less than minimum wage fixed under Minimum Wage Act - The principal grounds on which the constitutionality of the Exemption Act was challenged were based on arts. 14 and 23 of the Constitution - (C) Forced Labour - Whether payment of less than minimum wage on the basis of norms fixed by organistion itself amounts to forced labour - In the absence any such norm being fixed by notification - Held, violative of art. 23 - It is violative of art. 14 - Where a person provides labour or service to another for remuneration which is less than the minimum wage, the labour or service provided by him clearly falls within the meaning of the words 'forced labour' and attracts the condemnation of art. 23 - Service provided by workers is forced labour and less payment is violative of art. 23 of Constitution - Petition allowed.
