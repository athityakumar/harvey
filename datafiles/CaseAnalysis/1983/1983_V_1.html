S. Sivaswami v V. Malaikannan and Others

Supreme Court of India

27 September 1983

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Balakrishna Eradi, D.A. Desai, R.B. Misra

Where Reported

1983 Indlaw SC 145; (1984) 1 SCC 296; AIR 1983 SC 1293; 1983 (9) ALR 664; 1986 (1) MLJ(SC) 1; 1983(2) SCALE 418; [1984] 1 S.C.R. 104; 1983 UJ 1008

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Keywords: Assembly elections

Summary: Election - Assembly elections - Validity - Recount - Legality - Appellant contested for Assembly elections and respondent No. 1 was a rival candidate sponsored by Communist Party - Appellant was duly elected on ground that he had secured highest number of votes among contesting candidates - Validity of election was challenged by respondent No. 1 by filing election petition before HC praying for an order for scrutiny and recounting of all ballot papers cast in election to Assembly Constituency - HC directed a re-scrutiny and a recount of all rejected votes to be carried out in premises of HC - HC appointed one of Asst. Registrars of HC was Presiding Officer to supervise recounting - However, direction was also issued to respondent No. 5 Returning Officer to render all necessary assistance to enable re-scrutiny and recounting to be properly carried out by Asst. Registrar as presiding officer - Aggrieved, appellant preferred instant appeal - Whether HC was justified in passing the impugned order - Held, Election Commission had issued a 'Handbook for candidates' for election to House of People, Legislative Assemblies of States and Union Territories, etc. At page 78 of book, it was specifically stated that Returning Officer would not reject any ballot paper simply because the mark was only partially within column of one candidate and rest of mark was in blank area - Pamphlet issued by Election Commission of India in 1982 entitled A Pamphlet showing illustrative cases of valid and invalid postal and ordinary ballot papers - Illustration at page 17 of pamphlet depicts a case where mark affixed on ballot paper was partially in column of candidate No. 1 - In instant case, Returning officer was obviously misled by illustration contained in pamphlet, and that was sole reason why he was rejected as invalid ballot papers where marking was contained partly in column of respondent No. 1 and partly on demarcating line or shaded area - Returning Officer taken trouble to study instructions contained the 'Handbook for candidates' and 'Handbook for Returning Officers' it should had been apparent to him that illustration contained did not correctly reflect position laid down in rules and instructions - HC was perfectly right in holding that counting and declaration of results in instant case were vitiated by serious illegality and in directing a re-scrutiny and recounting of all rejected votes - Appeal dismissed.
