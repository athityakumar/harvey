H. C. Sharma and Others v Municipal Corporation Of Delhi And Others

Supreme Court of India

13 July 1983

Case Analysis

Bench

A. Varadarajan, V.D. Tulzapurkar

Where Reported

1983 Indlaw SC 32; (1983) SCC (L&S) 433; (1983) 3 SCC 567; AIR 1983 SC 881; 1984 (48) FLR 344; 1983 (2) LLJ 454; 1984 (1) LLN 34; 1983(2) SCALE 983; [1983] 3 S.C.R. 372; 1983 (2) SLJ 673

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments; Service

Keywords: Permissibility, Direct recruitment, Separation of category

Summary: Service - Municipalities & Local Governments - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 32, 14 - Delhi Municipal Corporation Act, 1957, ss. 487, 480(2) - Recruitment Regulations, 1970 - Direct recruitment - Separation of category - Permissibility - Instant petitions were filed u/art. 32 of Constitution by Petitioners who were Jr. Engineers (Diploma holders) and by Graduate Jr. Engineers against respondent (Corporation) seeking directions against recruiting Asst. Engineers directly and filling up posts of Asst. Engineers, quashing of seniority list and to reckon seniority of petitioners - Other petitioners sought directions against respondent to fill up 8 posts of Asst. Engineers amongst those in select panel and to direct respondent to fill up posts of Asst. Engineers in direct recruitment quota from amongst the empanelled petitioners who were Graduate Junior Engineers inter-alia other reliefs - Suggestion of petitioners to treat the Graduate Jr. Engineers as a separate category was not acceptable to respondent - Respondent contended that all appointments in Corporation carrying a minimum salary of less than Rs.700 could be made by Commissioner of Corporation since 10-1-1975 in accordance with recruitment rules which were framed in consultation with Union Public Service Commission (UPSC) though prior to that date consultation with UPSC was essential in the matter of appointment to posts carrying a minimum salary of Rs. 350 and above - Whether petitioners were entitled to the relief claimed for - Held, it was true that when Regulations were approved there were only 60 posts of Asst. Engineers (Civil) in Corporation - Number was 99 when Graduate Jr. Engineers filed instant petition and 115 when it was heard - Contention that 50:50 quota rule could applied only to 60 posts which were in existence when Regulations were approved and would not apply to posts which were in excess of that number was totally unreasonable and unsustainable and consequently rejected - There is nothing wrong in Corporation proceeding to appoint Asst. Engineers (Civil) by direct recruitment as per Regulations or in fixing 50:50 quota and working it out or in selection of Graduate Jr. Engineers as Asst. Engineers pursuant to decisions to appoint 8 Asst. Engineers (Civil) by direct recruitment in the interviews held for that purpose and that respondents should issue orders of appointment to those posts to 8 of Graduate Jr. Engineers who were in select list - Corporation was admittedly bound by Regulations made with approval of Central Govt. as required by s. 480(2) of the Act and it should fill the remaining posts of Asst. Engineers in direct recruitment quota which were kept vacant in seniority list and any further posts which might have become available thereafter or become available or otherwise in accordance with those Regulations - Names of Jr. Engineers working as Asst. Engineers on Current Duty Charge and Adhoc basis were not mentioned in seniority list as they were only Jr. Engineers who have not been regularly appointed as Asst. Engineers - They were only Jt. Engineers and consequently they could not question the correctness of seniority list relate to Asst. Engineers - As they have not yet regularly appointed or promoted as Asst. Engineers they could not have any grievance about their names not being mentioned in that seniority list - Thus, there was no need to quash seniority list - Graduate Engineers could not be declared as a separate category amongst Jt. Engineers and could not give equal quota like Diploma holders Jt. out of 50% quota for promotion as Asst. Engineers, except by carrying out two classes in same category of Jr. Engineers on the basis merely of their qualification which was not permissible in law though the creation of selection grade in same category on the basis of merit and or seniority was well known and permissible - Jr. Engineers do the same kind of work and bear the same responsibilities whatever their qualification, whether they are Degree holders or Diploma holders - Graduate Jr. Engineers were not entitled to be treated as a separate class of Jr. Engineers - Petitions disposed of.
