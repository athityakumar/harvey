State Of U.P. v Hari Ram And Others

Supreme Court of India

07 September 1983

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, M.P. Thakkar

Where Reported

1983 Indlaw SC 111; (1983) 4 SCC 453; (1983) SCC (Cr) 861; AIR 1983 SC 1081; 1983 (2) Crimes 829; 1983 CRLJ 1638; 1983 CrLR(SC) 507; 1983(2) SCALE 268; [1983] 3 S.C.R. 885

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Constitution, arts. 134 and 136 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 3 - Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Murder case - (A) Appreciation of evidence - (1) Evidence of eye-witnesses - All assailants were armed with sharp cutting weapons - Lacerated wounds and abrasions on dead body - Non-mention of this fact in FIR - (B) Interested witness - No ground to discard his testimony - (C) Appeal against order of acquittal by High Court - Ground, HC decision based on conjectures and not on sound reasonings - Held no inconsistency between ocular and medical evidence as accused armed with spears had struck deceased with lathi portion of spears and mere non-mention is immaterial - To be examined with caution - (B) Held in instant case that witness was class fellow of victim was not sufficient ground to throw out his testimony more particularly when he was common friend of both victim and one of the accused - (C) SC will interfere - Appeal Allowed.
