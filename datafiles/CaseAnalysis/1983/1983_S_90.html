State Of Haryana v Jage Ram

Supreme Court of India

12 September 1983

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.V. Chandrachud, R.S. Pathak, S. Mukharji

Where Reported

1983 Indlaw SC 186; (1983) 4 SCC 556; AIR 1983 SC 1207; 1983(2) SCALE 285; [1983] 3 S.C.R. 917; 1983 UJ 952

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Punjab Liquor Licence Rules 1956 (as amended by Notification dated 31 March 1967)

Summary: Punjab Excise Act, 1914, s. 58 - Punjab Liquor Licence Rules, 1956, rr. 36(24) and 36(3) - Excise Duty - Licence Rules - Auction of liquor vends - (A) Re-auction of vends because of default of licensee - (1) Publicity - Non-compliance of rule - (2) Regrant of license by private negotiations - (B) Charge imposed by Government - For parting with privilege of selling infoxicants - Jurisdiction - (A) Held, reauction and recovery of difference from licensee is illegal - Held, is not in conformity with Rules and has to be struck down - (B) State Government has power to charge particular amounts to licensees - Order Accordingly.
