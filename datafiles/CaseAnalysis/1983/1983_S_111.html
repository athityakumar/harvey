Machhi Singh And Others v State Of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

20 July 1983

Case Analysis

Bench

M.P. Thakkar, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, A. Varadarajan

Where Reported

1983 Indlaw SC 116; (1983) 3 SCC 470; (1983) SCC (Cr) 681; AIR 1983 SC 957; 1983 (2) Crimes 268; 1983 CRLJ 1457; 1983 CRLR 419; 1983 CrLR(SC) 419; 1983(2) SCALE 1; [1983] 3 S.C.R. 413

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Tort

Keywords: Kirpan, Criticism, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 149 and 302 - Murder - Conviction - Death sentence - Rarest-of-rare-cases - Seventeen lives were lost in the course of a series of five incidents which occurred in quick succession in five different villages - Appellant 'M' and his eleven companions, close relatives and associates were prosecuted in five sessions cases - 'M' was the common accused at each trial - Sessions Court convicted the appellants for an offence u/ss. 302 r/w s. 149 of IPC and it was held that it was established beyond reasonable doubt that the nine appellants had formed an unlawful assembly with the common object of committing murder - Sessions Court imposed death penalty on three of the appellants, viz., 'M', 'K' and 'J' - HC confirmed the order of conviction and sentence and dismissed the appeals - Instant appeal - (A) Testimony of witnesses - Held, conviction of the appellants rests on the testimony of three witnesses, PW 16, PW 21 and PW 20 - Fact that PW 20 sustained gun shot injury in the course of the transaction was satisfactorily established by the medical evidence - Medical evidence fully corroborates and lends support to her version that she was one of the inmates of the household, and was present at the scène of offence - Her evidence established that the appellants were the persons who committed the crime - Her testimony further establishes that she herself was injured by the rifle shots in the course of the incident by appellant 'K' - Her evidence that she had witnessed the murderous assault and had identified the assailants had remained unshaken and was accepted by the Sessions Court and the HC - Finding of guilt recorded by the Sessions Court and the HC confirmed - (B) Conviction of appellant 'M' - Finding of guilt recorded by the Session Court and affirmed by the HC rests on the testimony of two eye witnesses - Evidence had also been adduced to establish that one of the rifles used in the course of the murderous assault had been issued to 'M' in his capacity as an officer of Punjab Homeguards - Evidence of the ballistic expert established that the said rifle had been recently used and some of the empty cartridges found from the scene of the occurrence were fired from this rifle - This evidence was further corroborated by the evidence pertaining to the recovery of the rifle at the instance of appellant 'M' which had been accepted by the Sessions Court and the HC - Held, evidence showed that appellant 'M' had used the rifle by which shots were fired at the victims and that he was directly responsible for the killings - Order of conviction confirmed - (C) Appellant 'S' - Held, prosecution evidence did not establish that the weapon was with appellant 'S' at any point of time proximate to the point of time of the offence - In view of this lacuna in the evidence, it could not be held that the second rifle which was used in the commission of the crime was fired by appellant 'S' - Doubt was created as regards the complicity of the appellant 'S' - Benefit of reasonable doubt was accorded to appellant 'S' - Order of conviction and sentence, in so far as he was concerned set aside - (D) Death sentence - Held, death sentence imposed on appellants 'M', K, and 'J' upheld - Appeals disposed of.
