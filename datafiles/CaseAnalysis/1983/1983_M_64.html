Municipal Corporation Of Delhiand Others v Mohd. Yasin Etc.

Supreme Court of India

28 April 1983

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy, D.A. Desai

Where Reported

1983 Indlaw SC 92; (1983) SCC (Tax) 154; (1983) 3 SCC 229; AIR 1983 SC 617; 1983 (37) CTR 133; [1983] 142 ITR 737; 1983(1) SCALE 492; [1983] 2 S.C.R. 999

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Municipalities & Local Governments - Notification - Increase in fee for slaughtering of animals - Challenged - Appellant/ Corporation notified to enhance fee for slaughtering of animals in slaughter houses - Aggrieved butchers/respondents filed writ before HC contending that appellant was appellant was proposing to levy tax under guise of enhancing fee, which was allowed and notification was quashed - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, there are several other items of expenditure the whole or part of which are attributable to slaughter houses and HC was under the impression that the fees collected should be shown to be related to expenditure incurred directly and exclusively in connection with the slaughtering of animals in its slaughter houses and also, shown as such in the municipal budget. It was explained that the expenditure need not be incurred directly nor even primarily in connection with the special benefit or advantage conferred and there need not be any fastidious balancing of the cost of the services rendered with the fees collected. Thus, it appears to have been common ground before the HC that the price of meat had gone up about 10 to 12 times since the rates were originally fixed and one wonders how the respondent corporation could be expected to effectively discharge its obligations in connection with the supervision of the slaughtering of animals in the slaughter houses maintained by it by merely raising the rates two-fold and three-fold and the increase from Re. 00.25p to Rs. 2.00 per animal in the case of small animals and from Re. 1.00 to Rs. 8.00 in the case of large animals appeared to be wholly justified in the circumstances of the case. Hence, appeal is allowed with costs the judgment of the HC is set aside and the Writ filed in the HC is dismissed. Appeal allowed.
