Chandrika Jha v State of Bihar and Others

Supreme Court of India

27 October 1983

Case Analysis

Bench

A.P. Sen, E.S. Venkataramiah

Where Reported

1983 Indlaw SC 156; (1984) 2 SCC 41; AIR 1984 SC 322; 1983 (31) BLJR 670; [1984] 55 Comp Cas 347; 1983(2) SCALE 888; [1984] 1 S.C.R. 646; 1984 UJ 1

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate; Trusts & Associations

Keywords: Limited Liability

Summary: Trusts and Associations - Bihar and Orissa Co-operative Societies Act ,1935, ss. 14, 65A and 66 - Bihar Co-operative Societies Rules, 1959, r. 15 - District Central Co-operative Bank - Bye-laws empowered Registrar to nominate first Board of Directors - Orders extending their term, by Chief Minister of State by usurping powers of Registrar - Question as to legality and propriety of the action of the Chief Minister of a State in issuing certain directions, and incidentally the scope and extent of the power of a Minister to interfere with the working of a statutory functionary under his department - Orders held to be illegal in the light of art. 162 of Constitution - Appeal allowed.
