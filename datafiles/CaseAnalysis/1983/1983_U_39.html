Delhi Cloth and General Mills etc. v Union of India etc.

Supreme Court of India

21 July 1983

Case Analysis

Bench

D.A. Desai, V. Balakrishna Eradi, R.B. Misra

Where Reported

1983 Indlaw SC 270; (1983) 4 SCC 166; AIR 1983 SC 937; [1983] 54 Comp Cas 674; 1983 (2) CompLJ 281; 1983(2) SCALE 16; [1983] 3 S.C.R. 438; 1983 TAXLR 2584; 1983 UJ 699

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Constitution, arts. 19, 32 and 226, sch. 7, list I, entries 43 and 44, list 2, entry 30 - Companies Act, 1956, s. 58A - Companies (Acceptance of Deposits) Rules, 1975, r. 3A - Writ petitions - By company - The provision u/s. 58A of the Act and r. 3A of the Rules, 1975 - Complaint as to denial of freedom u/art. 19 of Constitution - Maintainibility - The validity of the provision challenged on the grounds of arbitrariness , vires to s. 58-A of the Act and delegated legislation u/art. 245 , prospective or retrospective effect - Whether the legislation is referable to Entries 43 & 44 in Union list or to entry 30 in State list - Complaint maintainable - Rule is valid as it is not ultra vires, not violate permissible limit of delegated lesislation , is prospective and not retrospective - Referable to Entries in Union List and not in State list - Petition Dismissed.
