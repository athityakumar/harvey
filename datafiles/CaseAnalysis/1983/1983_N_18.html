Pashupati Nath Sukul and Others v Nem Chandra Jain and Others

Supreme Court of India

25 November 1983

Case Analysis

Bench

E.S. Venkataramiah, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, O. Chinnappa Reddy

Where Reported

1983 Indlaw SC 253; (1984) 2 SCC 404; AIR 1984 SC 399; 1984 ALJ 215; 1983(2) SCALE 800; [1984] 1 S.C.R. 939; 1984 UJ 179

Case Digest

Subject: Election; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Election - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 21, 33 and 73 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 102, 187, 367, 188, 191 and 193 - General Clauses Act, 1897, s. 3(23) - Election Petition - (A) Whether Secretary to State Legislative Assembly (officer of Govt.) can be appointed returning officer? - (B) Election to Rajya Sabha seat - Whether person elected but has not taken oath as required by art. 188 can validly propose? - Held, can be appointed as returning officer at election for Rajya Sabha seat - Further held, can validly propose - Appeals allowed.
