Commissioner Of Wealth Tax, Madras v K. S. N. Bhatt

Supreme Court of India

21 October 1983

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Pathak, E.S. Venkataramiah

Where Reported

1983 Indlaw SC 153; (1984) 1 SCC 20; AIR 1984 SC 495; 1983 (37) CTR 273; [1984] 145 ITR 1; 1983(2) SCALE 674; [1984] 1 S.C.R. 490; 1983 TAXLR 1603; [1983] 15 TAXMAN 22; 1984 UJ 102

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Debt Laws

Summary: Income Tax and Direct Taxes - Wealth Tax Act, 1957, ss. 2(m), 2(m)(iii)(a) - (A) Computation of net wealth - Deduction of income tax, wealth tax and gift tax liabilities claimed - For 'Debt owed' liability must have crystallised on the valuation date - (B) Applicability of s. 2(m)(iii)(a) to prohibition against deduction in respect of debts owed - (A) Held, there was no tax liability and amount originally assessed is not debt owed - (B) Applicable only while proceedings are pending - Appeal partly allowed.
