Gopi Nath Ghosh v State of West Bengal

Supreme Court of India

11 November 1983

Case Analysis

Bench

D.A. Desai, Amarendra Nath Sen

Where Reported

1983 Indlaw SC 167; (1984) Supp SCC 228; (1984) SCC (Cr) 478; AIR 1984 SC 237; 1984 (21) AllCriC 121; 1983 (2) Crimes 937; 1984 CRLJ 168; 1984 CrLR(SC) 1; 1983(2) SCALE 756; [1984] 1 S.C.R. 803; 1984 UJ 166

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Women & Children

Keywords: West Bengal Children Act 1959

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302 and 34 - West Bengal Children Act, 1959, s. 24 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 39 - Murder - Appellant below 18 yrs at time of offence - Whether Trial Court had jurisdiction to convict appellant to life imprisonment who was below 18 yrs at time of offence? - Where juvenile delinquent is arrested, he/she has to be produced before juvenile court and if no Juvenile Court is established for area amongst others, Court of Session will have produces of a Juvenile Court - S. 25 forbids any trial of a juvenile delinquent and only an inquiry can be held in accordance with provisions of CrPC for trial of a summons case - In present case, appellant never questioned jurisdiction of Sessions Court which tried him for offence of murder - It was for first time that this contention was raised before Supreme Court - Held, it is unquestionably established on unassailable evidence on date of offence, appellant was aged between 16 and 17 years - Appellant was therefore, a juvenile delinquent, Magistrate could not have committed his case to Court of Session - Therefore, entire trial of appellant is without jurisdiction and is vitiated - Therefore, conviction of appellant for having committed an offence u/s. 302 IPC unsustainable and must be set aside - Case is remitted to learned Magistrate for proceeding further in accordance with law keeping in view provisions of Act - Appeal allowed.
