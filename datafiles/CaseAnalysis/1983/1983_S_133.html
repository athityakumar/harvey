Welcome Hotel and Others v State of Andhra Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

22 August 1983

Case Analysis

Bench

D.A. Desai, O. Chinnappa Reddy

Where Reported

1983 Indlaw SC 128; (1983) 4 SCC 575; (1983) SCC (Cr) 872; AIR 1983 SC 1015; 1983 CRLR 633; 1983 CrLR(SC) 633; 1983(2) SCALE 131; [1983] 3 S.C.R. 674; 1983 UJ 691

Case Digest

Subject: Health & Drug; Socio-Economic

Keywords: Andhra Pradesh Catering Establishments (Fixation and Display of Prices of Food Stuffs) Order 1978

Summary: Trade - Consumer Protection - Essential Commodities Act (10 of 1955), s. 3 - Andhara Pradesh Catering Establishments (Fixation, Display of Prices of Foodstuffs) Order (1978), cl. 3 - Food stuff - Meaning of - Foodstuff is that which is taken into system to maintain life and growth and to supply waste of tissue - Raw foodstuff undergoes a change of its condition by process of cooking, derivative is nonetheless foodstuff - (B) Fixation of maximum price - Whether arbitrary? - Held, no - Not a violation of art. 14 - Not arbitrariness - Mechanics of price fixation has necessarily to be left to judgment of executive and unless it is patent that there is hostile discrimination against a class of operators, processual basis of price fixation has to be accepted in generality of cases as valid - Petition dismissed.
