S. Kannan & Others Etc v Secretary, Karnataka Stateroad Transport Authority

Supreme Court of India

29 August 1983

Case Analysis

Bench

D.A. Desai, O. Chinnappa Reddy

Where Reported

1983 Indlaw SC 132; (1984) 1 SCC 375; AIR 1983 SC 1065; 1983(2) SCALE 212; [1983] 3 S.C.R. 740; 1983 UJ 884

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1939 s. 63 - Temporary permit - Entitled to - Central Govt. had empowered State u/s. 63(7) of the Act not exceeding 50, to grant permits valid for whole year - Several persons had applied for permit and State had granted 36 permits and applications for permits were rejected - Respondents preferred appeals before Tribunal which granted temporary all India tourist permits - Holders of regular permits filed writ before HC which was allowed and held grant of permit u/s.63(7) of the Act must be in accordance with provisions and grant of temporary permits was not permissible and quashed order of Tribunal - Hence, instant appeals - Whether grant of temporary permit in respect of tourist vehicle for all India operation was sustainable.

Held, if s.62 of the Act were to be proviso u/s. 63 (7) of the Act and therefore, one can conceive of granting a temporary all India tourist permit for meeting a particular temporary need, it is difficult to envisage a particular temporary need of an all India variant which can be noticed by respondent/ transport authority of one State and such authority can proceed side-tracking all relevant provisions to grant temporary all India tourist permit. Further, a respondent/ transport authority of one State may believe that there exists a particular temporary need but the view of the other State Transport Authority can be exactly to the contrary, and State Transport Authority of one State can open the flood-gates of temporary permits permitting tourist vehicles to invade other States and if one State cannot cater to the particular all India need, other States having their own quota can fill in the bill. Thus, the question of granting of a tourist permit to cater to a particular temporary need appears to be foreign to the very concept of all India tourist permit and opined that HC was perfectly justified in reaching the conclusion that there is no power to grant temporary all India tourist permit and the HC rightly set aside the decision of the Tribunal. Hence, all these appeals, slp's and writ petitions fail and each of which is dismissed. Appeals dismissed.
