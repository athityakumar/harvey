Sadhu Ram v Delhi Transport Corporation

Supreme Court of India

25 August 1983

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy, A.P. Sen, E.S. Venkataramiah

Where Reported

1983 Indlaw SC 245; (1983) 4 SCC 156; (1983) SCC (L&S) 507; AIR 1984 SC 1467; 1983 (47) FLR 326; 1983 LabIC 1516; 1983 (2) LLJ 383; 1983 (2) LLN 658; 1983(2) SCALE 136; [1983] 3 S.C.R. 725; 1983 (2) SLJ 372; 1983 (2) SLR 732; 1983 UJ 833

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour and Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 11 and 15 - Constitution of India,1950, art. 226 - Power of High Court - High Court interfere with the award of a Labour Court u/art. 226 on a mere technicality - Legality - Held, not entitled; the questions pertain to jurisdictional facts does not entitled the High Court to interfere with the findings on jurisdictional facts which the Tribunal is well competent to decide - Appeal allowed.
