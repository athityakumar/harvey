Ravindra Ishwardas Sethna and Another v Official Liquidator, High Court, Bombay and Another

Supreme Court of India

19 August 1983

Case Analysis

Bench

D.A. Desai, V. Balakrishna Eradi

Where Reported

1983 Indlaw SC 348; (1983) 4 SCC 269; AIR 1983 SC 1061; [1983] 54 Comp Cas 702; 1983 (3) CompLJ 23; 1983 (2) RCR(Rent) 352; 1983(2) SCALE 151; [1983] 3 S.C.R. 657; [1983] 3 S.C.R. 6577

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate

Summary: Corporate - Bombay Rents, Hotel and Lodging House Rates Control Act, 1948 - Indian Companies Act, 1956, s. 457 - Rent Act, ss. 13 and 15 - Whether liquidator was justified in executing a care taker agreement for office premises? - Held, Rent Act is no doubt enacted for protecting tenants, and indisputably its provisions must receive such interpretation as to advance protection and thwart action of landlord in rendering tenants destitute - But this does not imply that Court should lend its aid to flout provisions of Rent Act so as to earn money by unfair and impermissible use of premises - That is what Liquidator sought to do and Court extended its help to Liquidator - Appeal allowed.
