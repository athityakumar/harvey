Col. D.D. Joshi And Others v Union Of India And Others

Supreme Court of India

01 March 1983

Case Analysis

Bench

D.A. Desai, V. Balakrishna Eradi

Where Reported

1983 Indlaw SC 230; (1983) 2 SCC 235; (1983) SCC (L&S) 321; AIR 1983 SC 420; 1983 (46) FLR 263; 1983 LabIC 530; 1983 (2) LLJ 14; 1983 (2) LLN 33; 1983(1) SCALE 226; [1983] 2 S.C.R. 448; 1983 (1) SLJ 427; 1983 (1) SLR 552; 1983 UJ 324

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Defence & Security Forces; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Inter-State Migrant Workmen (Regulation Of Employment And Conditions Of Service)

Summary: Constitution - Defence and Security Forces - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14 and 16 - Discrimination - Commissioned officer belonging to Army Medical Corps , having post graduate qualifications in different specialties at the time of obtaining commission have approached Supreme Court u/art. 32 complaining of a discriminatory treatment in the matter of granting antedating benefit which is directly linked to seniority and promotional prospects - Thereby questions the legality of Army Instruction No. 78 of 78, Note - Held, not violative of art. 14 or 16 - As denial of benefit of longer period of ante-dating the commission to commissioned officers of 'AMC' having requisite qualifications when they were commissioned prior to 01-04-1978 is discriminatory and the choice of date is arbitrary - Petitions dismissed.
