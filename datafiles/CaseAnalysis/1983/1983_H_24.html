Hari Ram v Hira Singh & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

25 November 1983

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, E.S. Venkataramiah, O. Chinnappa Reddy

Where Reported

1983 Indlaw SC 455; (1984) 2 SCC 36; AIR 1984 SC 396; [1984] 1 S.C.R. 932

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Election; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Election - Indian Evidence Act, s. 165 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, O. 11 - Conduct of Election Rules, 1961, rr. 93 and 84(3), s. 152(2) - Whether proper foundation was laid for inspection and sufficient material placed before Court in order to allow prayer which would result in adversely affecting secrecy and sacrosanct nature of electoral process? - Held, an order for inspection of ballot papers cannot be granted to support vague pleas made in petition not supported by material facts or to fish out evidence to support such pleas - In a proper case where interests of justice demand it, Tribunal may call upon Returning Officer to produce ballot papers and may permit inspection by parties - Inspection of ballot papers and counterfoils should be allowed very sparingly and only when it is absolutely essential to determine issue - No details and particulars were given nor was it even mentioned whether Polling Agent of respondent had made any note of fact that votes were actually cast for dead persons and number of these votes - Appeal allowed.
