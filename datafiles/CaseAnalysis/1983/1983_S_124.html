Rameshwar Prasad Etc., Etc. v State Of Uttar Pradesh & Others

Supreme Court of India

24 February 1983

Case Analysis

Bench

E.S. Venkataramiah, A.P. Sen

Where Reported

1983 Indlaw SC 75; (1983) 2 SCC 195; AIR 1983 SC 383; 1983 ALJ 356; 1983(1) SCALE 152; [1983] 2 S.C.R. 418; 1983 UJ 549

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Carriers and Transportation - Constitution of India, 1950, art . 254 - Motor Vehicles Act, 1939 - Notifications- Validity - Question as to validity of two Notifications issued by the State Govt. u/s.43-A of the 1939 Act arises for consideration - The first notification directs that all eligible applicants shall be granted permits and that there shall be no upper limit to the number of permits to be issued for stage carriages and the second notification says that the Transport Authorities shall have regard only to matters referred to in cls. (a), (b), (d) and (f) of s. 47(1) of the Act and thereby precludes the Transport Authorities to take into consideration matters contained in the proviso to s.47(1) and in sub-ss.(1A) to (1H) of s.47 of the Act - Held, notifications are ultra vires the Act and they are liable to he struck down - Appeal allowed.
