 R. Viswan & Others v Union Of India & Others

Supreme Court of India

06 May 1983

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Bhagwati, Y.V. Chandrachud, V. Balakrishna Eradi, R.B. Misra, O. Chinnappa Reddy

Where Reported

1983 Indlaw SC 106; (1983) 3 SCC 401; (1983) SCC (L&S) 405; AIR 1983 SC 658; 1983 (2) Crimes 392; 1983 (2) LLJ 157; 1983 (2) LLN 662; 1983(1) SCALE 497; [1983] 3 S.C.R. 60; 1983 (2) SLR 193

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces; Service

Keywords: Armed Forces, Army Rules 1954, Central Civil Services (Classification, Control and Appeal) Rules 1965, Civil Services (Classification Control And Appeal) Rules

Summary: Constitution, arts. 19 and 33 - Army Act, 1950, ss. 4(1), 4(1)(4), 4(4), 21, 102(4) and 191 - (A) Constitution validity of s. 21 - (B) Meaning and scope of expression 'any force raised and maintained in India under the authority of Central Government' u/s. 4(1) of the Act - Central Government can applying certain provisions of Act and Rules to members of GREF by notifications u/s.4 - C) Notification u/s.4(1)(4) - Constitutionality of - Held, though power conferred on CG u/s.21 to impose restrictions on fundamental rights u/cls.(a) (b) and (c) of art. 19 (1) is a broad uncanalised and unrestricted power permitting violation of constitutional limitations s. 21 cannot be condemned as invalid on this ground, as it is saved by art. 33 - Members of GREF are members of Armed Forces within art. 33 - Not discriminatory and violative of art. 14 - Petition Dismissed.
