Arun Kumar Bose v Mohd. Furkan Ansari & Others

Supreme Court of India

28 September 1983

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranganath Misra, Amarendra Nath Sen

Where Reported

1983 Indlaw SC 146; (1984) 1 SCC 91; AIR 1983 SC 1311; 1983(2) SCALE 483; [1984] 1 S.C.R. 118

Case Digest

Subject: Election; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Ballot Box

Summary: Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 83(1)(a), 94, 87, 98, 116A, 100, 101, 100(1)(d)(iii), 97 - Conduct of Election Rules, 1961, rr. 56(2) Proviso, 38(1) and 36 - (A) Election petition - (1) Wrongly rejected ballot papers - Counting table number and Booth number disclosed - Ground for rejection, particulars not shown, as they were not available during counting - (2) Some of the ballots cast at particular booth and counted on particular table rejection for lack of Presiding Officer's signature - High Court allowed their inspection - Appeal against - (3) Relief claimed in terms of ss. 100 (1) (d) (iii) and 101 (a) of improper rejection of votes cast in his favour - Asserting receipt of majority of valid votes - Absence of a recrimination by returned candidate (appellant) - And s. 97 also not complied with - (B) Conduct of Election Rules - Ballot papers without signature of Presiding Officer - Issued to voters - Failure to satisfy legal requirement - (A) Held, pleadings set out material facts and no defects could be found with it - Action of HC, not open to dispute in appeal - Petitioner's claim could not be combated on such ground -(B) Proviso to r. 56 (2) attracted - Such ballot papers were not liable to be rejected -

Appeal Dismissed.
