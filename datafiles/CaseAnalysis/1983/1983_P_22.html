Glaxo Laboratories v Presiding Officer, Labour Court Meerut and Others

Supreme Court of India

06 October 1983

Case Analysis

Bench

D.A. Desai, A. Varadarajan, O. Chinnappa Reddy

Where Reported

1983 Indlaw SC 262; (1984) 1 SCC 1; (1984) SCC (L&S) 42; AIR 1984 SC 505; 1983 (47) FLR 508; 1983 LabIC 1909; 1984 (1) LLJ 16; 1984 (1) LLN 57; 1983(2) SCALE 831; [1984] 1 S.C.R. 230; 1984 (1) SLJ 229

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Labour and Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, sch.2, item 6 - Industrial Employment (Standing Orders) Act, 1946, Pre., ss. 5, 13A - Workmen's Compensation Act, 1923 - Interpretation of Statutes - Industrial Dispute - (A) Rules of Construction - Conditions of service prescribed in Certified Standing Orders - (B) Word and Phrases - Connotation of 'misconduct committed within the premises of the establishment or in the vicinity there of' - Misconduct must have causal connection with place of work and with duty hours - (C) Whether power to prescribe condition of service is unilateral - (D) Misconduct neither defined nor enumerated in Standing Orders - Employer believes it to be misconduct - If punishable - Held, should receive interpretation to advanced intendment underlying the Act - Employer has no extra-territorial jurisdiction under Standing Order to punish for misconduct - Held, not unilateral - Not punishable merely because employer believes it to be misconduct ex-post facto - Appeal dismissed.
