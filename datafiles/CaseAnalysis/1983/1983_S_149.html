Bhugdomal Gangaram and Others v State of Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

19 April 1983

Case Analysis

Bench

A. Varadarajan, P.N. Bhagwati

Where Reported

1983 Indlaw SC 370; (1984) SCC (Cr) 67; (1984) 1 SCC 319; AIR 1983 SC 906; 1983 (1) Crimes 1070; 1983 CRLJ 1276; 1983 CRLR 382; 1983(1) SCALE 411; 1983 UJ 722

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Prohibition Act

Summary: Bombay Prohibition Act, 1949, ss. 65(e) and 81 - Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 3 and 60 - (A) Conviction u/s. 65 (a) r/ w s. 81of 1949 Act - In the absence of any proof of sale or attempt to sell by accused - (B) Admissibility of evidence - (1) Testimony of witness - Being the Manager of Truck, information regarding accused's involvement was given to the witness - (2) Evidence of police officer - As to receipt of information by him that certain accused would be following in taxi, a truck carrying prohibited liquor - Informant not examined - (3) Appreciation of evidence - Suspicion if constitutes legal evidence -

(A) Held, not permissible in absence of proof - (B) Testimony of witness is not admissible - Evidence inadmissible - No amount of suspicion constitutes legal evidence for sustaining conviction -

Appeal Allowed
