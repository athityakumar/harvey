Asharam M. Jain v A. T. Gupta And Others

Supreme Court of India

25 August 1983

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy, A.P. Sen, E.S. Venkataramiah

Where Reported

1983 Indlaw SC 129; (1983) 4 SCC 125; (1983) SCC (Cr) 771; AIR 1983 SC 1151; 1984 (1) Crimes 143; 1983 CRLJ 1499; 1983 CrLR(SC) 659; 1983(2) SCALE 138; [1983] 3 S.C.R. 719; 1983 UJ 830

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Criminal; Practice & Procedure; Tort

Summary: Constitution, art. 136 - Contempt of Courts Act, 1971, s. 2(c)(iii) - A) Contempt of Courts -Serious allegations against Chief Justice of High Copourt - Contumacious disregard of all decencies - Disturbance of administration of justice - Apology - Permissibility - (B) Practice and Procedure - Filing of Special Leave Petition - Practice of filing over names of the counsel who receive papers from counsel from outside Delhi - As if drawn and settled by them - Held, SC declined to accept unqualified apology and sentenced him to S.I. for two months - Held, hardly proper and discourteous to Court - Order Accordingly.
