Hindustan Aeronautics Limited v State of Orissa

Supreme Court of India

16 December 1983

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, V.D. Tulzapurkar, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1983 Indlaw SC 57; (1984) 2 SCC 16; (1984) SCC (Tax) 113; AIR 1984 SC 753; 1984 (1) CompLJ 150; 1983(2) SCALE 1101; [1984] 2 S.C.R. 267; 1984 (55) STC 327; 1984 TAXLR 2831; 1984 UJ 818

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Keywords: Emergency Risks (Goods) Insurance Act, 1962, Central Sales Tax (Orissa) Rules, 1957

Summary: Sales Tax - Indirect Tax - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956, s. 7 (1) - Central Sales Tax (Orissa) Rules, 1957, r. 12 (3) - A/Ys 1974-75, 1975-76 and 1976-77 - Sale tax - Sustainability - Appellant/assessee was in business of aeroplanes including manufacture, assembling, buying and selling etc. of the same - In its division, manufacture of MIG engines for MIG aircrafts required for-defence and overhauling of aero engines of Indian Air Force were undertaken - Some of MIG engines manufactured by it were sent to Nasik Division and some to Indian Air Force as per instructions from Ministry of Defence - Appellant received payments from Govt. of India or Indian Air Force for manufacturing programme - In respect of payments so received, Sales Tax Officer, levied Central Sales Tax on the ground that transactions were interstate sales - This was disputed by appellant according to whom the latter was only an agent of Govt. of India - In alternative it was contended that transactions were nothing but works contract and as such not exigible to Central Sales Tax - On appeal, Tribunal confirmed order of assistant commissioner - Hence instant appeal - Whether impugned order was justified - Held, SC referred to several correspondences which indicated that property in aircrafts, in the equipments and materials was always with Govt. - Materials imported under licence or procured indigenously for manufacture were always and had always remained property of Govt. - Appellant had no property, in any part thereof, and had no right to dispose of or disposal over these materials and spares - These had to be regulated by the procedure envisaged in agreement between parties - Test by which these transactions should be judged in deciding whether this was a works contract or a contract of sale of any part of the material was emphasised in several decisions of SC - As emphasised by SC, there was no rigid or inflexible rule applicable alike to all transactions which could indicate distinction between contract for sale and contract for work and labour - Further, entire correspondence and nature of instructions from time to time issued by Govt. indicated that function of appellant division was the implementation of said entrustment - Hence, any sales tax in respect of Aero-Engines transferred to Nasik Division of appellant for installing the same in Aircrafts - It was transfer of Aircrafts to Nasik Division for the purpose of completion of the job and making of invoices was a matter of accounting and carrying out job of entrustment - Transactions were carried out in implementation of entrustment job for manufacture by appellant and all payments and actions taken in this behalf were on behalf of Govt. of India - Therefore, Tribunal was in error in concluding that there was sale involved in these transactions - Appeal allowed.
