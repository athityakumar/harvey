Manohar Nath Kaul v State of Jammu and Kashmir

Supreme Court of India

19 April 1983

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranganath Misra, D.A. Desai

Where Reported

1983 Indlaw SC 88; (1983) 3 SCC 429; (1983) SCC (Cr) 647; AIR 1983 SC 610; 1983 (1) Crimes 1165; 1983 CRLJ 988; 1983 CrLR(SC) 318; 1983(1) SCALE 399; [1983] 2 S.C.R. 791; 1983 UJ 572

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, s. 197(1) - Is sanction under s. 197 necessary for prosecution of public servant who has been accused of cheating? - Held, offence committed by him is not one while he is acting or purporting to act in discharge of his official duty as such offence has no necessary connection between it and performance of duties of a public servant - Official status furnishing only occasion or opportunity for commission of offences - Appeal dismissed.
