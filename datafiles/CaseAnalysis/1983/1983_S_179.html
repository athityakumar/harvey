 Mohd. Shakeel Wahid Ahmed v State of Maharashtra and Others

Supreme Court of India

31 March 1983

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.V. Chandrachud, Ranganath Misra, V.D. Tulzapurkar, A. Varadarajan, O. Chinnappa Reddy

Where Reported

1983 Indlaw SC 81; (1983) 2 SCC 392; (1983) SCC (Cr) 509; AIR 1983 SC 541; 1983 (1) Crimes 1013; 1983 CRLJ 967; 1983 CRLR 243; 1983 CrLR(SC) 243; 1983(1) SCALE 308; [1983] 2 S.C.R. 614

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Conservation of Foreign Exchange and Prevention of Smuggling Activities Act, 1974, ss. 3 and 8 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 32 - (A) Determination of validity of an order of detention u/s. 3 of the Act - On application by wife of detenu, High Court holding that three out of the four grounds on which the petitioner was detained were bad but remaining did not suffer from any defect and was enough to sustain the order of detention - Failure of State Govt. to place before the detaining authority, the opinion of Advisory Board - Held, on petition by detenu u/art. 32 order of detention was vitiated - (B) Whether the order of detention was passed in this case after applying the mind to the relevant facts which bear upon the detention of the petitioner? - Opinion of the Advisory Board in Shamsi's case was, at any rate, an important consideration which would and ought to have been taken into account - Detaining authority failed to apply its mind to highly relevant circumstance - Order accordingly.
