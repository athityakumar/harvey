Gulam Abbas And Others v State Of U.P. And Others

Supreme Court of India

23 September 1983

Case Analysis

Bench

V.D. Tulzapurkar, D.A. Desai, A.P. Sen

Where Reported

1983 Indlaw SC 142; (1984) SCC (Cr) 35; (1984) 1 SCC 81; AIR 1983 SC 1268; 1983 (9) ALR 659; 1983(2) SCALE 704; [1984] 1 S.C.R. 64; 1983 UJ 1016

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Constitution - Constitution, arts. 25, 26, 32 - (A) Whether the direction of the committee to shift the graves to resolve the dispute between the Shia and Sunni sects of Muslims was violative of the Fundamental Rights in art. 25 & 26 ? - Held, no - Suggestion to shift the graves made by the Chairman of such Committee could not be regarded as destructive of any fundamental rights of the Sunnis as the past experience has shown that such performance of their religious ceremonies and functions was and has been invariably accompanied by ugly incidents of violence, damage or destruction to life and property putting public order in great jeopardy - If the Court finds the implementation of the suggestion to be eminently fit in the interest of maintenance of public order consent of either party would be immaterial - For the purpose of maintaining public order which would be in the larger interest of the society - Not irreligious - (1) Jurisdiction of Supreme Court - Held, acceptance and implementation of suggestion of Committee by SC - The acceptance cannot be said to be beyond its powers or jurisdiction of SC - Petition allowed.
