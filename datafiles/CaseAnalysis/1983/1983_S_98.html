Ramji Surjya & Another v State Of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

13 May 1983

Case Analysis

Bench

E.S. Venkataramiah, V. Balakrishna Eradi

Where Reported

1983 Indlaw SC 102; (1983) 3 SCC 629; (1983) SCC (Cr) 748; AIR 1983 SC 810; 1983 (2) Crimes 237; 1983 CRLJ 1105; 1983 CrLR(SC) 388; 1983 MLJ 520; 1983(1) SCALE 763; [1983] 3 S.C.R. 268

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Supreme Court (Enlargement of Criminal Appellate Jurisdiction) Act, 1970, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, ss. 154 and 386 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 3 and 134 - (A) Conviction - Contended that there was extraordinary delay in lodging first information report and prosecution failed to explain same - Held, reason given by prosecution for inordinate delay in reporting incident to police outpost is hardly convincing - This important aspect of case has been overlooked by High Court - Sole eye witness was not found reliable and sufficient - (B) Reversal by HC whether proper ? - Further held, no - Reasons for reversing a judgment of acquittal should be cogent and if two views are reasonably possible Appellate Court should be slow in interfering with judgment of Trial Court, even if it is possible for it to take a different view after a process of laborious reasoning - Conviction is set aside - Appeal allowed.
