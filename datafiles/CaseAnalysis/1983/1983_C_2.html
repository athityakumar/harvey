Messrs Punjab Tin Supply Company, Chandigarh v Central Government and Others

Supreme Court of India

20 October 1983

Case Analysis

Bench

E.S. Venkataramiah, A.P. Sen

Where Reported

1983 Indlaw SC 259; (1984) 1 SCC 206; AIR 1984 SC 87; 1983 (2) RCJ 659; 1984 (1) RCR(Rent) 168; 1983 (2) RentLR 681; 1983(2) SCALE 503; [1984] 1 S.C.R. 428

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - East Punjab Urban Rent Restriction Act, 1949, s. 3 - Home Department Notification No. 352LD-73/602 dated January 31, 1973, as modified by Home Department Notification No. 2294-73/3474 dated September 24, 1973 and Home Department Notification No. 3205-74/3614 dated September 24, 1974 issued by Chief Commissioner of Union Territory of Chandigarh - U/s. 3 of East Punjab Urban Rent Restriction Act, 1949 - Exempting every building constructed in urban area of Chandigarh for a period of five years from respective date applicable to it from operation of Act - Whether it is constitutionally valid? - Held, preamble and provisions of a statute no doubt assist Court in finding out its object and policy but its object and policy need not always be strictly confined to its preamble and provisions contained therein - Act is passed as one of measures taken to mitigate hardship caused to tenants - Policy and object of Act generally is mitigation of hardship of tenants - Such mitigation can be attained by several measures, one of them being creation of incentive to persons with capital who are otherwise reluctant to invest in construction of new buildings in view of chilling effect of rent control laws - As a part of the said scheme in order to persuade them to invest in construction of new buildings exemption is granted to them from operation of Act for a short period of five years so that whatever may be hardship for time being to tenants of new buildings, new buildings so constructed may after expiry of period of exemption be available for pool of housing accommodation controlled by Act - Impugned notification is not ultravires s. 3 of Act as in its true effect, it advances scheme, object and purposes of Act which are articulated in preamble and substantive provisions of Act - Petitions disposed of.
