Rana Partap and Others v State of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

12 May 1983

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy, D.A. Desai

Where Reported

1983 Indlaw SC 358; (1983) 3 SCC 327; (1983) SCC (Cr) 601; AIR 1983 SC 680; 1983 (2) Crimes 342; 1983 CRLJ 1272; 1983 CRLR 377; 1983(1) SCALE 780; 1983 UJ 717

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 300 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 3 - Conviction - Murder committed in a street - Seen by three witness - Independent witnesses being described as 'chance witnesses' implying thereby that their evidence was suspicious and their presence at scene doubtful - Held, there isn't any concept of chance witness, as murder was committed in a street and only passersby will be witnesses - Their evidence cannot be brushed aside or viewed with suspicion on ground that they are mere 'chance witnesses' - Conviction confirmed - Appeal dismissed.
