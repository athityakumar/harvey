Bhalchandra alias Bapu and Another v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

11 April 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Grover, Mohammad Hidayatullah, C.A. Vaidyialingam

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 370; 1969 ACJ 87; AIR 1968 SC 1319; 1968 CRLJ 1501; [1968] 3 S.C.R. 766

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 304A and 337 - Indian Explosives Act, 1884 - Rash and Negligent act - Appellants running factory manufacturing explosives and crackers - Explosion occurred leading to death of workmen employed - Appellant convicted under Indian Explosives Act and ss. 304 - Whether the conviction of the appellants was justified? - Requirement of s. 304 A was the causing of death by doing any rash or negligent act and this meant that the death must be the direct or proximate result of the rash or negligent - Held, yes - Criminal negligence can be found on varying sets of circumstances - Appellants committed breach of conditions imposed with regard to storage and other essential conditions - Women and Children were employed - Factory situated close to residential area - Appellants displayed a high degree of negligence by allowing or causing to be used dangerous and prohibited compositions and substances which must be held to have been the efficient cause of the explosion - Direct and effective cause of explosion was gross negligence of the accused held, conviction of appellants justified - Appeal dismissed.
