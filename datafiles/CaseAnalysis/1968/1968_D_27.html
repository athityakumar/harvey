 State of Punjab v Dharam Singh

Supreme Court of India

02 February 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Bachawat, G.K. Mitter, C.A. Vaidyialingam, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 396; AIR 1968 SC 1210; 1968 (17) FLR 9; 1968 LabIC 1409; 1969 (1) SCJ 243; [1968] 3 S.C.R. 1; 1968 SLR 247

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Education

Summary: Constitution - Education - Constitution of India, 1950 - Punjab Educational Service (Provincialised Cadre) Class III Rules, 1961 - Completion of Probation - Confirmation order - Termination - Respondents were junior teachers in District Board schools - U/r. 6(3) of the Rules they were to hold posts on probation for one year - Maximum period of probation fixed by the Rules was three years - Respondents completed three years of probation but formal orders of confirmation were not passed - Later, Director terminated their services - Appellate Court allowed appeals and set aside orders - HC dismissed petitions - Respondents contended that their removal from service was in violation to art. 311 of the Constitution - Whether respondents acquired substantive rights to their posts, and that the orders of removal from service were passed in violation of art. 311 of the Constitution - Held, it was permissible to draw inference that employee allowed to continue in post even on completion of maximum period of probation was confirmed in post by implication - Constitutional protection of art. 311 of the Constitution was violated - Impugned orders were rightly set aside by HC - SC decision in Narain Singh Ahluwalia v. State, of Punjab and another, C.A. No. 492 of 1963, followed - Appeals dismissed.
