Union of India(In Both The Appeals) v Brijlal Purshottamdas(In Both The Appeals)

Supreme Court of India

30 August 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Bachawat, S.M. Sikri, K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 481; AIR 1969 SC 817; [1969] 1 S.C.R. 910

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Criminal

Keywords: Carriage By Rail

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Railways Act, ss. 74 D, 74 E and 80 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872 - High Court held that Southern Railway to which goods were delivered by consignor was liable to pay compensation for loss though loss was due to negligence or misconduct of Bengal Nagpur Railway - (A) Whether misconduct or negligence of servants of Bengal Nagpur Railway administration cannot fairly be inferred from materials on record? - Held, s. 74 D does not expressly provide that administration is bound, if necessary, to lead evidence as to how it dealt with consignment before consignor is called upon to prove misconduct or negligence but we think that this obligation is implicit in duty of disclosure imposed by section - In present case 14 packages were not delivered to respondent and such non-delivery was not due to any accident to train or to fire - Case therefore fell within s. 74D(a) - Packages were carried by Southern Railway and Bengal Nagpur Railway administrations and two administrations became bound to disclose to respondent how packages were dealt with throughout time they were in their possession or control - From disclosure made by appellant, negligence of servants of Bengal Nagpur Railway administration may fairly be inferred - (B) Whether respondent can claim compensation for loss u/s. 80 from Southern Railway to which packages were delivered by him? - Held, liability u/s. 80 is statutory - Section gives a right of suit against administration to which goods are delivered by consignor and it matters not that loss occurred while goods were being carried by another administration and was due to negligence of latter - Goods were carried at owner's risk rate over railways of two administrations and having regard to s. 74E, provisions of ss. 74C(3) and 74D apply to each administration, as if goods were carried at only one administration - Likewise s. 74E does not restrict liability imposed by s. 80 on administration to which goods were delivered by consignor - That administration is liable to be sued u/s. 80 for loss whether or not loss occurred on railway of another administration - Appeals dismissed.
