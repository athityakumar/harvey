Paokai Haokip v Rishang and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 August 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, G.K. Mitter

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 87; AIR 1969 SC 663; [1969] 1 S.C.R. 637

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Summary: Election - Representation of People Act, s. 116 A - Whether election was to contravention of Act and Rules? - Held, s. 100(1)(d)(iv) requires that election petitioner must go a little further, prove that result of election had been materially affected - What is to be seen is whether this burden has been successfully discharged by election petitioner by demonstrating to court either positively or even reasonably that poll would have gone against returned candidate if breach of rules had not occurred and proper poll had taken place at all polling stations including those at which it did not - Burden which is placed by law is very strict, even if it is strict it is for courts to apply it and it is for Legislature to consider whether it should be altered - This burden has not been discharged and election of returned candidate will stand - Appeal allowed.
