Shiv Raj Singh v Delhi Administration

Supreme Court of India

01 May 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Ramaswami, C.A. Vaidyialingam

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 116; AIR 1968 SC 1419; 1969 CRLJ 1; [1969] 1 S.C.R. 183

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Tort

Summary: Criminal - Corruption - Sanction for prosecution - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947, s. 5 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 161 - Appellant compelled complainant and his wife to execute a document in writing to pay him money or agree to go to prison - Reason behind demand of illegal gratification was concealment of birth of an illegitimate child born to complainants niece - Whether appellants conviction was justified in view of fact that there was no proper sanction for prosecution? - When a public servant is charged u/s. 161 IPC, 1860 and it is alleged that illegal gratification was taken by him for doing or procuring an official act, it is not necessary for the court to consider whether or not the accused public servant was capable of doing or intended to do such an act - Held, yes, order of sanction on face of it proved facts constituting offence and a prima facie case was made out against appellant - Sanction for prosecution was provided - Appeal dismissed.
