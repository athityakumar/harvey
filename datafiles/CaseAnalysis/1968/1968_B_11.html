Jang Bahadur Singh v Baij Nath Tiwari

Supreme Court of India

26 April 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Bachawat, S.M. Sikri

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 441; AIR 1969 SC 30; 1969 CRLJ 267; 1968 (17) FLR 300; 1969 LabIC 194; 1969 (1) LLJ 567; [1969] 1 S.C.R. 134

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Education - Constitution - Contempt of Courts Act, 1952, ss. 1 and 3 - Intermediate Education Act, 1921, s. 16G - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 311 - Interference with due course of justice in pending proceedings - Misconduct by employee - Parallel inquiries by domestic tribunal and court - Held, an enquiry by a domestic tribunal in good faith is not a contempt of court even if same matter is pending before a Civil or a Criminal Court - Employee is free to move court for an order restraining continuance of disciplinary proceedings - But, if a stay order is obtained then that would amount to contempt of court, if proceeding of tribunal still continues - Appeal allowed.
