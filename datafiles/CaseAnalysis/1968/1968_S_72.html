 Motilal Jain v State of Bihar and Others

Supreme Court of India

27 March 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, S.M. Sikri, R.S. Bachawat, G.K. Mitter, C.A. Vaidyialingam, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 378; AIR 1968 SC 1509; 1969 CRLJ 33; [1968] 3 S.C.R. 587

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Socio-Economic

Summary: Criminal - Essential Commodities Act, 1955 - Preventive Detention Act, ss. 3 and 4 - Preventive detention - Validity of - Held, detenu indulged in black marketing of controlled commodities - Appellant's representation to Advisory Board, dismissed - Habeas Corpus petition before High Court also dismissed - The ground set out in cl. (a) of the order of 27-09-1967 is not only vague but also irrelevant - The ground mentioned in cl. (d) must be held to be non existing - The defects noticed in the two grounds are sufficient to vitiate the order of detention - Individual liberty is a cherished right, one of the most valuable fundamental rights guaranteed by Indian Constitution to the citizens of this country - If that right is invaded, excepting strictly in accordance with law, the aggrieved party is entitled to appeal to the judicial power of the State for relief - Order of detention is set aside - Appeal allowed.
