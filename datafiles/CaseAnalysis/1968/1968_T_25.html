Kollipara Sriramulu v T. Aswathanarayana and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 March 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, G.K. Mitter, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 388; AIR 1968 SC 1028; 1969 (1) MLJ 6; 1969 (1) MLJ(SC) 6; [1968] 3 S.C.R. 387

Case Digest

Subject: Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Women

Summary: (A) Contract & Commercial - Partition - Specific performance - Appellant was one of the partners in a firm consisting of about thirty partners - Partnership firm owned not only a factory but also a site - Total number of shares in the partnership was 160 out of which the appellant owned 23 shares - Firm executed a lease in favour of the 1st respondent and another person of the area of the site for a period of 10 years - Lessee was permitted to construct a building - Appellant filed a suit in the Subordinate Judge's Court for dissolution of the partnership and for accounts - Before the passing of the preliminary decree a suit was filed in the District Munsiff's Court by the firm and its managing partner against respondent no. 1 and others seeking to evict respondent no. 1 - Appellant had purchased 39 shares from some of the partners in addition to the 23 shares already owned by him - He claimed partition and separate possession of 62 shares belonging to him - He amended the plaint which was allowed - Plaint was filed in the Sub ordinate Judge's court - Respondent no. 1 filed a suit claiming specific performance of the agreement to sell 39 shares owned by the said partners and contended that sale of those shares in favour of the appellant was not binding upon him - Suit was transferred to the District Court - Suit in which the appellant claimed partition and recovery of possession of his 62 shares was also finally transferred to the District Court - District Judge held that respondent no. 1 had not proved the oral agreement of sale in his favour - Suit for specific performance was dismissed and held in the suit for partition appellant was entitled to 62 shares and he granted a decree for partition and possession thereof as also damages - Respondent no. 1 appealed before the HC - By a common judgment HC allowed both the appeals - HC decreed the suit for specific performance - Decree in the partition suit was varied - Hence this appeal - Whether there was an oral agreement between the 1st respondent and all the partners of the firm except the appellant for sale of their shares on July 6, 1952 and whether respondent no. 1 was entitled to specific performance of that oral agreement - Held, the evidence adduced on behalf of respondent no. 1 does not show that the drawing up of a written agreement was a pre-requisite to the coming into effect of the oral agreement - Appeals dismissed.

(B) Contract & Commercial - Partition - Specific performance - Whether the oral agreement was ineffective because the parties contemplated the execution of a formal document or because the mode of payment of the purchase money was not actually agreed upon - Held, purchase money paid by the appellant was very nearly the same as that payable under the agreement in respondent no. 1's favour. On the basis of his evidence the HC reached the conclusion that the appellant had notice of the prior oral agreement - Appeals dismissed.
