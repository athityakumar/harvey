Kt. N. Rm. Thenappa Chettiar and Others v N. S. Kr. Karuppan Chettiar and Others

Supreme Court of India

31 January 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 397; AIR 1968 SC 915; 1968 (1) MLJ(SC) 95; [1968] 2 S.C.R. 897

Case Digest

Subject: Trusts & Associations

Summary: Trusts & Associations - Mismanagement - Appellants had filed a suit praying for settlement of a scheme in respect of a Trust and for an account of the management of the Trust - Suit was mainly contested by respondent no. 2 - Subordinate Judge dismissed the suit by holding that sole founder of the trust was Mr. X who conducted it with the aid of moneys contributed by himself and other persons and that at the time of the foundation - Other four contributors did not reserve for themselves any right in the trusteeship of the Mutt - Charges of mismanagement were unfounded and no case was made out on behalf of the plaintiffs for framing a scheme - Judgment of the Subordinate Judge was affirmed by the HC - Hence this appeal - Whether the appellants had made out any grounds for framing of a scheme or for the removal of the respondent no. 2 from the management of the trust - Held, it was alleged by the appellants in the plaints that the trust had been mismanaged by Mr. X and respondent no. 2 and both have been guilty of breach of trust - Subordinate Judge after examining the evidence held that all the charges were not proved against respondent no. 2 - HC had also reached to the same conclusion - On allegation of fabrication of accounts books by respondent no. 2 both the courts held that allegation was not true - Question of respondent no. 2 or Mr. X was guilty of breach of trust or of acts of mismanagement is a question of fact - In view of the concurrent finding of both the lower courts on this question no ground had been made out on behalf of the appellants for framing of a scheme or for removal of respondent no. 2 from the office of the managing trustee - Suit brought by the appellants has been rightly dismissed - Appeal dismissed.
