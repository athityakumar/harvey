Payment of Wages Inspector, Ujjain v Surajmal Mehta, Director, Barnagar Electric Supply and Industrial Company, Limited, and Another

Supreme Court of India

03 December 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Shelat, C.A. Vaidyialingam

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 40; AIR 1969 SC 590; 1969 (18) FLR 284; 1969 LabIC 867; 1969 (1) LLJ 762; [1969] 2 S.C.R. 1051

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: (A) Labour & Industrial Law - Practice & Procedure - Payment of Wages Act, 1936, ss. 2(vi)(d), 15(2) - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 25F, 25FF, 25FFF, 33C(2) - Retrenchment compensation - Recovery - Applicability of provision - Determination of nature of amount - Respondent no. 1 was managing director of Company, was revoked by state Govt. and company's undertaking was taken over by state Electricity Board - Respondent no.1 served notices on company's employees that their services would no longer be required - Thereupon appellant on behalf of twenty employees of company filed application u/s. 15(2) of 1936 Act to recover from respondent no.1 wages for notice month and retrenchment compensation payable to employees u/s. 25FF of 1947 Act - Board held that employees were entitled to claim compensation - Respondent no. 1 filed writ petition before DB of HC who held that s. 15 of 1936 Act did not apply and that proper forum for such application was Labour Court u/s. 33C(2) of 1947 Act - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether compensation payable u/s. 25FF of 1947 Act could fall under amended definition in s. 2(vi)(d) of 1936 Act and could be called 'wages - Held, 1947 Act was piece of legislation which mainly 'provided machinery for investigation and settlement of industrial disputes', was since then undergone frequent modifications - However, intention of legislature was that it did not wish to place transfer and closure 'on same footing as retrenchment u/s. 25F of 1947 Act - Therefore, ss. 25FF, 25FFF of 1947 Act did not contain any conditions precedent, as in case of retrenchment under s. 25F of 1947 Act, and transfer and closure could validly take place without notice or payment of month's wages in lieu thereof or payment of compensation - Thus, it was well established that words 'in accordance with provisions of s. 25F' in ss. 25FF, 25FFF of 1947 Act were used only as measure of compensation and were not used for laying down any time within which employer must pay compensation - Hence, compensation payable u/ss. 25FF, 25FFF r/w 25F of 1947 Act would be 'wages' within meaning of s. 2(vi)(d) of 1936 Act - Appeal dismissed.

(B) Labour & Industrial Law - Payment of Wages Act, 1936, ss. 4, 5, 15(2) - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss.4, 5, 25FF, 33C(2) - Compensation - Jurisdiction - Sustainability - Whether order passed by HC was justified - Held, failure to pay compensation on ground of such plea could not be said to be either deduction which was unauthorized under 1936 Act, nor could it fall under class of delayed wages as envisaged by ss. 4, 5 of 1936 Act - Claim for compensation u/s. 25FF of 1947 Act, which was denied by employer on ground that it was defeated by proviso to that section, of which all conditions were fulfilled, was one such claim which could fall within ambit of s.. 15(2) of 1936 Act - Therefore, when definition of wages was expanded to include cases of sums payable under contract, it could not have been intended that such claim for compensation which was denied on grounds which inevitably would have to be inquired into and which might entail prolonged inquiry into questions of fact as well as law was one which should be summarily determined by Authority u/s. 15 of 1936 Act - Thus, Authority could not have been intended to try as matters incidental to such claim questions arising under proviso to s. 25F of 1947 Act - Hence, it would be Labour Court in such cases which would be proper forum to determine such questions u/s. 33C(2) of 1947 Act which possessed power to appoint commissioner to take evidence where question of facts required detailed evidence - Appeal dismissed.
