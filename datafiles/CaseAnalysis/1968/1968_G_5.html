Commissioner of Sales Tax, U.P v Ganga Sugar Corporation Limited Commissioner of Sales Tax, U.P. v. Behari Lal Ram Prasad. Ganga Sugar Corporation Limited v. Commissioner of Sales Tax, U.P

Supreme Court of India

10 September 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Grover, J.C. Shah, Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 73; 1970 (25) STC 155

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Sales Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Uttar Pradesh Sales Tax Act, 1948, s. 8 A(4) - Hyderabad General Sales Tax Act, 1950 - Madras General Sales Tax Act, 1939, s. 8-B(2) - Constitution - Govt. Of India Act, 1935 - Refund - Tax liability - Inter-state sales - In assessment year 1950-51, assessee opted to submit its return on basis of turnover of previous year 1949-50 for period 01-04-1949 to 31-03-1950 - After assessing, Sales Tax Officer directed assessee to deposit a sum of Rs. 23,025-10-4 in Govt. Treasury u/s. 8-A(4) of Act, 1948 - On appeal, tax liability was reduced - Assessee claim for refund was denied by Sales Tax officer on ground that assessee had realised Rs.1,20,000 from its buyers on account of sales tax and it was liable to deposit entire balance after excluding Rs. 14, 6219-4 and was therefore not entitled to any refund - On appeal, it was held that only tax realised after 25-01-1950, was liable to be deposited u/s. 8- Appeal u/s. 8-A(4) of Act was applicable only where an assessee had realised amounts of sales tax from his customers which were unauthorised in sense that amount realised exceeded amount payable as tax or no tax was payable - Held, at any rate, it is perfectly clear that tax liability of assessee came to Rs. 14,6214 only and therefore excess which had been realised by him could not be regarded as having been realised lawfully - A(4) of Act is ultra vires State Legislature - Refund to be made as per writ petition - Order accordingly.
