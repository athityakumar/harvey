G. Narayana Raju v G. Chamaraju and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 March 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, G.K. Mitter, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 384; AIR 1968 SC 1276; [1968] 3 S.C.R. 464

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Summary: Family & Personal - Constitution of India, 1950 - Suit properties - Legal representative - Partition - Preliminary decree - Plaintiff filed a suit for partition and separate possession of suit properties - There were three defendants related to the plaintiff - Plaintiff had a joint family business - Joint status of the family was severed by the issue of a registered notice by the first defendant to the plaintiff - Ancestral house belonging to the family was acquired by the City Improvement Trust Board - Out of compensation paid for that house and supplemented by earnings of the members of joint family, item nos. 1 and 2 were purchased by a certain person - It was further alleged that the business known as 'Ambika Stores' was also the joint family business and all the properties mentioned in the Schedules except items 1 and 2 of Schedule 'A' were acquired out of the income of the members of the family including the income from the business of Ambika Stores - Plaintiff claimed that he and the first defendant would each be entitled to get 5/14ths share and the second and third defendants would each be entitled to get 2/14ths share - Suit was mainly contested by the second defendant who asserted that the properties mentioned in all the Schedules of the plaint were self- acquisitions of Mr. A and constituted his separate properties - Mr. A later on employed the plaintiff in his -shop as a salaried servant and the latter had no proprietary right in the business of Ambika Stores - District Judge granted a preliminary decree holding that the plaintiff was entitled to 2/7ths share in item No. I of Schedule 'A' - Plaintiff took the matter in appeal which was affirmed by the HC - Whether the business of Ambika Stores was really the business of the joint family and whether the plaintiff was entitled to a partition of his share in the assets of that business - Held, the business of Ambika Stores was not the business of the joint family - Plaintiff stated that he became the co-owner of the business - There was no material substance in the claim - As per the documents of the executants it was stated that there was no legal effect for making the plaintiff the co-owner of the partnership business - There was no evidence in proving that the plaintiff was the co-owner of the partnership business - Whatever the plaintiff did in connection with the business was only done with the Authority conferred by Mr. A - Thus, the Plaintiff was not entitled to a partition of his share in the assets of that business - Appeal dismissed.
