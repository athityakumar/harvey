 Hadibandhu Das v District Magistrate, Cuttack and Another

Supreme Court of India

02 May 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, Vishishtha Bhargava, C.A. Vaidyialingam, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 437; AIR 1969 SC 43; 1969 CRLJ 274; [1969] 1 S.C.R. 227

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Defense & Internal Security - Preventive Detention Act, 1950, s. 3(1)(a)(ii), 7(1) and 13(2)- Preventive detention - Public Safety - Grounds served in English - Fresh order issued on grounds on which order revoked or expired had been made - Whether detention of petitioner is valid in law? - Mere oral explanation by authorities of complicated order without supplying him, translation in script and language which he understood amounts to denial of right of being communicated grounds and of being afforded opportunity of making representation against order - Held, order made by District Magistrate, not having been followed up by service within five days, as provided by s. 7 (1) of Act of 1950, communication to him of grounds must be deemed to have become invalid - Subsequent detention to be unauthorized - Appeal allowed.
