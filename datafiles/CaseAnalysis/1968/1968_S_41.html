Ragho Prasad Gupta v Shri Krishna Poddar

Supreme Court of India

23 August 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Bachawat, S.M. Sikri, K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 474; AIR 1969 SC 316; 1969 BLJR 426; [1969] 1 S.C.R. 834

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 21, r. 97 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 144 and 155 - L obtained from R a settlement in respect a land as benamidar of appellant - Respondent claimed land on basis of another settlement from R - Respondent filed a procedings u/s. 144 of , 1973 - Respondent and L agreed to refer dispute to arbitration and arbitrator made his award - Award was filed in Munsif and an application in form of a written statement on behalf of L was filed setting forth objections to award and praying that award be set aside - L died and his heirs were substituted in his place in proceedings - Heirs of L filed a written statement adopting earlier one and stating that appellant was real owner and a necessary party - Munsif dismissed application and observed that only L and respondent were parties to arbitration and appellant had no locus standi to be added as a party - Munsif dismissed suit and declined to pass a decree mainly on ground that arbitrator was disqualified from acting as an arbitrator - Respondent filed an appeal from this decree - Addl. Sessions Judge allowed appeal, dismissed all objections and passed a decree in terms of award and respondent started execution proceedings but appellant obstructed court in giving possession of property - Respondent filed an application against appellant under O. 21, r. 97 of CPC, 1908 Munsif dismissed application on ground that as appellant's prayer for being added as a party to proceedings had been rejected and he was not bound by decree passed against his benamidar-respondent filed a revision petition u/s. 115 of CPC, 1908 and High Court allowed revision petition - Whether respondent (appellant) is bound by decree passed in previous suit against heirs of his benamidar? - Held, in Gur Narayan v. Sheo Lal Singh, (AIR 1918 PC 140) Judicial Committee held : 'The benamidar has no beneficial interest in property or business that stands in his name, he represents, in fact, real owner, and so far as their relative legal position is concerned he is a mere trustee for him' - Bulk of judicial opinion in India is in favour of proposition that in a proceeding by or against benamidar, person beneficially entitled is fully affected by rules of res judicata - In view of this decision, it is well settled that in any litigation with a third party, benamidar can sufficiently represent real owner - Decision in any proceeding brought by or against benamidar will bind real owner though he is not joined as a party unless it is shown that benamidar could not or did not in fact represent interest of real owner in that proceeding - Appeal dismissed.
