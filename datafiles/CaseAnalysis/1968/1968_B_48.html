Ningawwa v Byrappa and 3 Others

Supreme Court of India

17 January 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 465; AIR 1968 SC 956; [1968] 2 S.C.R. 797

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Limitation Act, 1908, art. 95 - Mortgage under undue influence - Wife executing mortgage of property under persuasion from husband Mortgage of wife's inherited property without knowledge and other property under influence from husband - No occasion to ascertain true character - Whether the limitation period runs from the date when the plaintiff discovered the true nature of the deed or from the date when she escaped from the influence by which she alleged that she was dominated? - Limitation period of three years runs from the date when the plaintiff discovered the true nature of the deed - Entitled to recover possession with mesne profits - For property mortgaged under influence Suit barred by limitation - Appeal allowed.
