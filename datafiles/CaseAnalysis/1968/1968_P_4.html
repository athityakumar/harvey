Institute Of Chartered Accountants Of India v P. K. Mukherji And Anr.

Supreme Court of India

26 February 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Ramaswami, J.C. Shah, G.K. Mitter

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 224; AIR 1968 SC 1104; [1968] 38 Comp Cas 628; 1968 (2) CompLJ 211; [1968] 3 S.C.R. 330

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate

Summary: Chartered Accountants Act, 1949, ss. 21, 22 and sch. , cl. (o) - Companies Act, 1956

- Chartered Accountants - Proffessional Misconduct - Company appointed CA to audit accounts of EPF - Duty towards contributors of fund - When trustees of fund advancing loan to company in contravention of Rules of fund - While company issuing cheques to fund, in order to show paper adjustment of repayment of loan in the accounting year - Cheques not cashed and ultimately returnedback to the company - And Statement of account not indicating true financial position, despite knowing all these facts , passed by CA as correct - Whether it amounts to misconduct u/cl.(o) of schedule of 1949 Act - Held, amounts to misconduct - Punishment - Considering long pendency , CA severely reprimanded for misconduct Appeal Allowed.
