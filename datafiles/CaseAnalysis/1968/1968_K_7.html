K. Venkateswara Rao and Another v Bekkam Narasimha Reddi and Others

Supreme Court of India

13 August 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

G.K. Mitter, Mohammad Hidayatullah

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 82; AIR 1969 SC 872; 1970 (1) MLJ(SC) 8; 1969 (2) SCJ 505; [1969] 1 S.C.R. 679

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Election; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Election - Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - General clauses Act, 1897, s. 9 and 10 - Limitation Act, 1963, ss. 5 and 29(2) - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 79, 81, 82, 83, 85, 86, 87, 90(4), 98, 99 and 116 A - Election of 1st Respondent challenged on grounds of corrupt practices including that of and making one V K Reddi withdraw his candidature by illegal gratification - Condonation of delay in impleading him also sought - Held, if payment amounts to an illegal gratification taint attaches to both payer and payee - Limitation Act cannot apply to proceedings like an election petition - Act is complete and self-contained which does not admit of introduction of principles or provisions of Limitation Act - Trial of an election petition is not same thing as trial of a suit - Act itself makes important provisions of CPC inapplicable to trial of an election petition - No addition of parties is possible except under provisions of s. 86(4) - Legislature has made observance of certain formalities and provisions obligatory and failure in that respect can only be visited with a dismissal of petition - Submission that, failure to implead V K Reddi did not make election petition liable to dismissal u/s. 86(1 ) for reason that having withdrawn from contest he was no longer a candidate within meaning of s. 79(b) or 82(b), cannot stand - In definition of 'candidate' in s. 79(b), expression 'any other candidate' in s. 82(b) must include a candidate who had withdrawn his candidature - S. 99 enjoins upon High Court to give an opportunity to a person sought to be held guilty of a corrupt practice if he was not a party to petition, but it does not apply to a person who is a necessary party - Appeal dismissed.
