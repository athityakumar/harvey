Bibhuti Bhusan Das Gupta and Another v State of West Bengal

Supreme Court of India

16 September 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Bachawat, S.M. Sikri, K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 215; AIR 1969 SC 381; 1969 CRLJ 654; [1969] 2 S.C.R. 104

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure 1898

Summary: Criminal - Prosecution for defamation - Personal attendance of accused - Criminal Procedure Code, 1898 , ss. 205, 342 and 540A - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 500 and 501 - Whether pleader can represent accused for purposes of s. 342 and examination of pleader is sufficient compliance when personal attendance of accused dispensed with? - Held, no - Privilege of making a statement u/s. 342 is personal to accused - Only he and nobody else can be examined - If statute gives accused a personal privilege or imposes upon him a personal duty, only he can exercise privilege or perform duty - Ss. 205 and 540A do not expressly provide that pleader cannot be examined u/s. 342, but this does not lead to conclusion that pleader can be examined - Further held, u/s. 537 the conviction and sentence are not reversible on account of any error, omission or irregularity in 'any proceedings during the trial unless the error, omission or irregularity has in fact occasioned a failure of justice - Non-examination of the accused in the present case, u/s. 342 cannot be a ground for interference since no prejudice was established - Appeal dismissed.
