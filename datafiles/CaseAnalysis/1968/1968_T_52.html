Metal Box Company of India Limited v Their Workmen

Supreme Court of India

20 August 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Shelat, C.A. Vaidyialingam

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 308; AIR 1969 SC 612; [1969] 39 Comp Cas 410; 1969 (18) FLR 336; [1969] 73 ITR 53; 1969 LabIC 995; 1969 (1) LLJ 785; [1969] 1 S.C.R. 750

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: - Labour & Industrial Law - Service - Payment Of Bonus Act, 1965, ss. 6 , 7 and 23 - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 18A - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 10(2) - Companies Act, 1956, s. 205(1) - Whether computation of bonus in respect of the accounting year ending 31-03-1965, payable to the employees/respondents is in accordance with the Payment of Bonus Ordinance? - If not, what should be the quantum of bonus for the employees? - If Parliament intended to make a departure from the rule laid down by Courts and Tribunals that the bonus amount should be calculated after provision for tax was made and not before, we would have expected an express provision to that effect either in the Act or in the Schedules - Contention urged by the appellant company that the tax liability is to be worked out by first working out the gross profits and deducting there from the prior charges but not the bonus payable to the employees is right - Held, the appellant company succeeds on the questions of development rebate and the provision for gratuity amount - Appeal to that extent is allowed - Tribunal will ascertain the amount afresh after giving the parties opportunity to lead evidence as they desire and taking that amount and the amounts of development rebate and of the provision for gratuity in the light of this judgment, the Tribunal will adjust its award and arrive at the quantum of bonus payable to the workmen - Appeal is dismissed.
