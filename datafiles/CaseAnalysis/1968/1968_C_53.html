J. K. Woollen Manufacturers v Commissioner of Income Tax, Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

02 August 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, A.N. Grover, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 262; AIR 1969 SC 609; [1969] 72 ITR 612; [1969] 1 S.C.R. 525

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Vocation

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Profits - Assessment - Expenditure - Deduction - Appellant / assessee carried on the business of manufacture and sale of blankets and other woollen cloth - For a/y 1948-49 assessee paid a sum of Rs. 75,465 as commission to general manager calculated at rate of 25% on the profits - Assessee claimed deduction of amount from assessable income - Income Tax Officer disallowed claim on the ground that it was excessive and found that no general practice of giving commission at the rate of 25% existed in assessee's line of business - Against disallowance of Rs. 75,465 paid as commission to general manager assessee preferred an appeal to Appellate Assistant Commissioner and Appellate Tribunal - Appeal dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether an amount claimed expenditure was laid out or expended wholly or exclusively, for the purpose of business u/s.10(2)(xv) of Act - Held, as per referred case employer in fixing the remuneration of his employees was entitled to consider the extent of his business, the nature of the duties to be performed and the special aptitude of the employee, future prospects of extension by the business and a host of other related circumstances - Circumstances established that entire amount of Rs.75,465 paid to the general manager, was an amount laid out or expended wholly and exclusively for the purpose of business of the assessee - Appeal allowed.
