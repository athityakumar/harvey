Moti Lal Banker v Mahraj Kumar Mahmood Hasan Khan

Supreme Court of India

09 February 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Bachawat, Vishishtha Bhargava

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 462; AIR 1968 SC 1087; 1968 (2) ALT(SC) 220; [1968] 3 S.C.R. 158

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, O. 20, rr. 3, 11, O. 21, r. 247 - Execution of decree - Rate of interest - Determined by the Compromise - Validity - Whether compromise entered in proceeding for execution of decree by which judgment debtor undertakes to pay interest at rate higher than decree rate of interest, enforceable in proceeding for execution of decree? - The jurisdiction of the executing court to enforce such a compromise is not taken away by O. 23, r. 4 of the CPC - The effect of O. 23, r. 4 is that O. 23, r. 3 does not apply to execution proceedings - Independently of O. 23, r. 3, the provisions of O. 21, r. 2 and s. 47 enable the executing Court to record and enforce such a compromise in execution proceedings - Nor does O. 20, r. 11(2) affect this power of the executing Court - O. 20, r. 11 enables the Court passing the decree to order postponement of the payment of the decreetal amount on such terms as to the payment of interest as it thinks fit on the application of the judgment-debtor and with the consent of the decree-holder - It does not affect the power of the executing Court u/s. 47 and O. 21, r. 2 - Held, reasonable rate of interest in compromise is not alteration of decree - Parties can enter in compromise with reference to their rights and obligations under decree - Appeal allowed.
