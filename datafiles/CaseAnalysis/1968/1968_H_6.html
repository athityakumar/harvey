State of Kerala v Haji K. Haji K. Kutty Naha and Others Etc

Supreme Court of India

13 August 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, A.N. Grover, V. Ramaswami

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 83; AIR 1969 SC 378; 1969 SCJ 691; [1969] 1 S.C.R. 645

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Summary: Constitution - Practice & Procedure - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Constitution of India, 1950 art. 13 - Whether Kerala Buildings Tax Act 19 of 1961 is ultra vires Legislature in that it infringes equality clause of Constitution? - In order that law may be valid, tax proposed to be levied must be within legislative competence of Legislature imposing a tax and authorising collection thereof and, secondly, tax must be subject to conditions laid down in art. 13 of Constitution - Power of Legislature to classify is, it is said, of 'widerange and flexibility' so that it can adjust its system of taxation in all proper and reasonable ways - Held, in enacting Kerala Building Tax Act, no attempt at any rational classification is made by Legislature - Charging section of Act is violative of equality clause of Constitution - Appeal dismissed.
