J. K. (Bombay) P. Limited v New Kaiser-I-Hind Spg. and Wvg. Company Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

22 November 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Shelat, Vishishtha Bhargava, C.A. Vaidyialingam

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 313; AIR 1970 SC 1041; [1970] 40 Comp Cas 689; 1970 (1) CompLJ 151; [1969] 2 S.C.R. 866

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate

Keywords: Capital Issue

Summary: Companies Act, 1956, ss. 391 and 392 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, ss. 58 and 100 - Company - Winding up - Court sanction scheme to pay off creditors - Business of the concern unprofitable it is not viable to allow shareholders to finance an run the business - Provisions ss. 391, 392, 528 and 529 of the Companies Act attracted - Scheme neither create new debts nor it become part of companies constitution - Whether Court can order winding up of company - Distinction among mortgage and charge u/ss. 58 and 100 of Transfer of Property Act - Effect of winding-up order in the light of ss. 392, 528 and 529 of the Act - Court can order winding up of company - Agreement to mortgage, is not mortgage - Properties of company to be applied in satisfaction of liabilities pari passu - Appeals dismissed.
