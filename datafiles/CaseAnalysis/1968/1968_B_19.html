Hari Chand Aggarwal v Batala Engineering Co, Limited

Supreme Court of India

24 September 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Grover, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 367; AIR 1969 SC 483; 1969 CRLJ 803; 1969 (5) DLT 250; [1969] 2 S.C.R. 201

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Defence & Security Forces; Land & Property; Service

Keywords: Defence Regulations

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 10 - Defence of India Act, 1962, s. 29 - Powers of Additional District Magistrate - Post of District Magistrate - Requisitioning order - Appellant was tenant of a shop belonging to respondent No. 1 - Appellant claimed that respondent No. 1 approached the Labour Commissioner who wrote to the Additional District Magistrate that the shop was required for setting up a cooperative consumer store and accordingly, the Additional District Magistrate passed an order u/s. 29 of the Act requisitioning the shop and directing appellant to surrender and deliver possession thereof to the manager of the store within two days of the service of the order - Appellant's petition challenging the order was dismissed by the HC - Whether the Additional District Magistrate, invested with all the powers of the District Magistrate u/s. 10(2) of the CrPC could make an order u/s. 29(1) of the 1962 Act, requisitioning a shop belonging to respondent No. 1 which was in occupation of appellant as tenant - Held, that the Additional District Magistrate who is invested with the powers of a District Magistrate did not attain the status of a District Magistrate as there could be only one person in the district who could be a District Magistrate - When the Govt. conferred powers on the District Magistrate they were conferred on the officer actually holding the office of the District Magistrate and no one else - The Additional District Magistrate was below the rank of a District Magistrate and even though he had been invested with all the powers under the CrPC and also under any other law for the time being in force he was still not the District Magistrate unless the Govt. appointed him as such u/s. 10(1) of CrPC - Appeal allowed.
