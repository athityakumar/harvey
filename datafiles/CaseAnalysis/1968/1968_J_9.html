Jambu Rao Satappa Kocheri v Neminath Appayya Hanammannaver

Supreme Court of India

10 April 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, V. Ramaswami, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 333; AIR 1968 SC 1358; 1969 (71) BomLR 62; [1968] 3 S.C.R. 706

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Bombay Tenancy and Agricultural Lands Act, 1948, ss. 5 and 67 - (A) Whether enforcement of contract would result in transgression of provisions of Bombay Tenancy and Agricultural Lands Act, 1948? - Act has imposed no restriction upon transfer of agricultural lands from one agriculturist to another - But when scheme of Act is examined, it is clear that legislature has not declared transfer or bequest invalid, for s. 84C provides that land in excess of ceiling shall be at disposal of Govt. when an order is made by Mamlatdar - Invalidity of acquisition is, therefore, only to extent to which holding exceeds ceiling prescribed by s. 5, and involves consequence that land will vest in Govt. - (B) Whether Civil Court had jurisdiction to entertain suit for specific performance? - Court, it is true, will not enforce a contract which is expressly or impliedly prohibited by statute, whatever may be intention of parties, but there is nothing to indicate, that legislature has prohibited a contract to transfer land between one agriculturist and another - Held, Act contains no general restrictions upon such transfers, and unless at date of acquisition, transferee holds land in excess of ceiling, acquisition to extent of excess over ceiling will not be invalid and Civil Court can entertain a suit for specific performance - Appeal dismissed.
