Commissioner of Income Tax, Gujarat Ii v B. M. Kharwar

Supreme Court of India

13 August 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 280; AIR 1969 SC 812; [1969] 72 ITR 603; [1969] 1 S.C.R. 651

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 10(2)(vii) - Taxation Laws (Extension to Merged States and Amendment) Act, 67 of 1949, s. 11 - Applicability of s. 10(2)(vii) - Assessee firm closed its part business of manufacturing and transferred plants and machinery of his business to a company but was carrying on purchasing and selling activities - Assessee contention that s. 10(2)(vii) not applicable as the transfer is not in the normal course of business was not accepted - Tribunal has recorded no finding that the transfer was 'a realization sale' or in the course of winding up of the business - The s. 10(2)(vii) becomes applicable only if the building, machinery or plant is sold - Held, directions to Tribunals to rehear the parties and record clear findings - Appeal allowed.
