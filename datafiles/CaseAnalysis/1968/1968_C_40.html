Commissioner of Income Tax, Kerala v K. B. Kalikutty (Decd.) and Another

Supreme Court of India

02 August 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Grover, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 261; AIR 1969 SC 869; [1969] 73 ITR 533; [1969] 1 S.C.R. 531

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 10(2)(vii) - Applicability of second proviso to s. 10(2)(vii) - All that has to be seen is whether during the entire previous year or a part of it the business has been carried on by the assessee and that the machinery has been used in the business - Both these conditions, according to the finding given by the Tribunal, exist in the present case - Tribunal had given no finding that the whole of the assessee business had been wound up during the relevant period - The business was carried on by the assessee during the previous year and the sold assets was used in the business of the assessee - Held, profits arising out of the sale of buses in question as determined by the ITO would be chargeable to tax in accordance with the second proviso to s. 10(2)(vii) - Appeal allowed.
