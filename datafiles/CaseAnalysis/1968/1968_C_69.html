Commissioner of Wealth Tax, Madras v Smt. Muthukrishna Ammal

Supreme Court of India

06 September 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, V. Ramaswami, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 202; AIR 1969 SC 740; [1969] 72 ITR 801; 1969 (2) MLJ(SC) 6; [1969] 2 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Debt Laws

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Wealth Tax Act, 1957 s. 2(e)(v) - Valuation - Amendment - Validity - Tax Officer held that value of interest of respondent in salt pans for unexpired periods of two leases was liable to be included in computation of net wealth - Valuing leasehold interest in salt pans at average rate of income received from last three years, for unexpired terms, Tax Officer brought to tax in addition to net wealth returned by respondent aggregate amount of Rs. 1,89,330 - Order was confirmed by Appellate Assistant Commissioner - Appellate Tribunal held that interest of respondent in salt pans was not 'asset' within meaning of s. 2(e)(v) - Tribunal directed that value of leasehold interest in salt pans be deleted in computation of net wealth of respondent - Tribunal referred to HC - HC held that leasehold interest of respondent in salt pans was not 'asset' within meaning of s. 2(e) (v) of the Act and its value was accordingly not liable to be included in net wealth of respondent - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order passed by HC was maintainable - Held, assuming that exception respect of interest in property which was available to assessee for period not exceeding six years from date interest vests in assessee was only to apply after date of amendment by the Act, that clause had no application and terms of section might be interpreted as they stood at appropriate valuation date which crystallized charge of wealth-tax for appropriate assessment year - It was unnecessary to refer to amendment made by same Amending Act in Explanation to s. 4 of the Act - Hence, interest in property which was available to tax-payer for period not exceeding six years from valuation date was not asset within meaning of s. 2 (e) of the Act and value could not be included in net wealth of assessee for financial year relevant to valuation date - Appeal dismissed.
