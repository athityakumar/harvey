Dhanki Mahajan v Rana Chandubha Wakhatsing and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 April 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, R.S. Bachawat

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 371; AIR 1969 SC 69; 1969 (10) G.L.R. 100; [1968] 3 S.C.R. 759

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Indian Contract Act, 1872 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882 - Bombay Agricultural Debtors' Relief Act, 1939 - Respondents executed a joint usufructuary mortgage in favour of third appellant - Liability incurred under the mortgage was a joint liability and under the terms of the deed each of the mortgagers was liable for the entire debt due under the mortgage - Respondent no. 2 had filed an application for adjustment of their debts, which was dismissed as the debts due from each of those persons were held to exceed Rs. 15,000 and that being so they could not be considered as 'debtors' under the Bombay Act - In 1955, the respondents again made applications before the Debt Adjustment Board for scaling down their debts under the provisions of the Act - Both the Board as well as the appellate court upheld the contentions of the appellants that the respondents were not 'debtors' as defined in the Act and that their present applications were barred by the principles of res judicata - HC reversed the findings of the Board and the appellate court - Hence, the present appeal - Whether the HC was incorrect in holding that each one of the respondents could not be held liable for the entire debt due under the mortgage - Held, that HC was incorrect as it could not be disputed that when a mortgage was created jointly on property in which several persons were interested each of the mortgagors was liable in the absence of a contract to the contrary to pay the entire debt, and the liability of a mortgagor was not proportionate to the extent of his interest in the mortgaged property; and that position was not altered under the provisions of the 1939 Act - Appeal allowed.
