Soli Pestonji Majoo & Ors. v Gangadhar Khemka

Supreme Court of India

06 December 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, A.N. Grover, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 348; (1969) 1 SCC 220; AIR 1969 SC 600; [1969] 3 S.C.R. 33

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Final Decree For Sale, Debt Laws

Summary: Land & Property - Code of Civil Procedure (Amendment) Act, 1956 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882 - Usurious Loans Act, 1918 - Final Decree for sale - Debt laws - Mortgage - Preliminary decree - Adjudication of rights - Appellant was the executor of the estate of the deceased referred as the mortgagor - Mortgagor was the owner of one-third share in premises during his lifetime - Mortgagor executed a deed of mortgage in respect of his one-third share and executed the third deed of mortgage in respect of his one third share of the premises in favour of the respondent - Suit of mortgage was filed impleading the puisne mortgagees as parties to the suit - Preliminary mortgage decree in Form 9 of Appendix 'D' in the First Schedule to the CPC was passed in the said suit - Since the mortgagor did not pay, a final decree was passed - Decree directed that the mortgaged property should be sold - Mortgagor without having the property put to sale paid off the decretal dues - Respondent filed the suit out of which this appeal arises - Whether a puisne mortgagee in respect of whose mortgage a decree had already been made in a prior mortgagee's suit to which he was made a party, was entitled to institute a separate suit in respect of his mortgage and ask for a decree in Form 5-A when the claim of the prior mortgagee made in the prior mortgagee's suit had been satisfied by payments made by the mortgagor-defendant and as a result thereof no sale took place in the suit - Held, the rights of puisne mortgagee-defendant in a prior mortgagee's suit are, first, the right-to redeem the prior mortgage and, secondly, the right to participate in the surplus sale proceeds - It was held that the rights of the subsequent mortgagees were contingent on the property being brought to sale for non-payment of the sum due to the plaintiff-mortgagee and a decree drawn up in Form 7 of Appendix D of the CPC shouldn't be read as a decree directing the mortgagor to, redeem each of the puisne encumbrance,; within the time limited for redeeming the first mortgagee - It was accordingly held that the puisne mortgagee was not entitled to execute the decree for the amount due to him when no sale was held for the realisation of the amount due to the prior mortgagee and the remedy of the puisne mortgagee was a suit for sale and s. 47, CPC was no bar to the suit - Appeal allowed.
