Shah Dhansukhlal Chhaganlal v Dalichand Virchand Shroff and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 March 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

G.K. Mitter, R.S. Bachawat, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 219; AIR 1968 SC 1109; 1969 RCJ 143; 1969 RCR(Rent) 116; 1968 (2) SCJ 687; [1968] 3 S.C.R. 346

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Bombay Rent, Hotel and lodging House Rates Control Act, 1947

Summary: Rent Control - Transfer of Property Act, 1882 - Bombay Rent, Hotel and Lodging House Rates Control Act, 1947, s. 12 - (A) Whether provisions of s. 12(1) of Act were applicable throughout hearing of suit and down to date of final hearing, if at that stage it was found that defendant had paid up all arrears due from him he could not be ejected? - (B) Whether even applying s. 12 (3)(b) there was no default on part of defendant which would render him liable to eviction? - S. 12(1) must be r/w Explanation and so read it means that a tenant can only be considered to be ready and willing to pay if, before expiry of period of one month after notice referred to in sub-s. (2), he makes an application to court u/s 11(3) and thereafter pays or tenders amount of rent or permitted increases specified by court - It is noticed that tenant made no payment within period of one month of notice of ejectment and although in his written statement he raised a dispute about standard rent he made no application in terms of s. 11(3) of Act - Held, tenant makes no application in terms of s. 11(3) he cannot claim protection of s. 12(1) by merely offering to pay or even paying all arrears due from him when court is about to pass a decree against him - Appeal dismissed.
