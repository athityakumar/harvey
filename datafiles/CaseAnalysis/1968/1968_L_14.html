Laxman Kalu Nikalje v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

05 April 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, A.N. Grover, C.A. Vaidyialingam

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 136; AIR 1968 SC 1390; 1968 CRLJ 1647; [1968] 3 S.C.R. 685

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Culpable homicide not amounting to murder - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 299, 300, 302 and 304 - Appellant killed deceased by stabbing him on shoulder - Convicted - Present appeal - Held, appellant's wife was eye-witness to offence and her testimony is vital to prosecution case - There is no reason that appellant's wife would falsely implicate her own husband - Powers of High Court in an appeal against acquittal are same as in an appeal against a conviction - HC in dealing with such an appeal can go into all questions of fact and law and reach its own conclusions on evidence - Injury was sufficient and caused with knowledge that it may cause death - Case fell within the third part of s. 299 IPC, 1860 and was punishable under the second part of s. 304 IPC, 1860 as culpable homicide not amounting to murder - Conviction altered from s. 302 to s. 304 - Appeal dismissed.
