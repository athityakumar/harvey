 Triloki Nath and Another v State of Jammu and Kashmir and Others

Supreme Court of India

23 April 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Mohammad Hidayatullah, S.M. Sikri, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, Vishishtha Bhargava

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 129; AIR 1969 SC 1; 1970 (1) LLJ 629; [1969] 1 S.C.R. 103; 1968 SLR 748

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 16, cls. 1, 2 and 4 - Promotion - Entitled to - Whether junior officers were promoted over senior officers on basis of community and residence in a locality? - Policy laid down by Govt. in matters of employment to State services is that 50% of vacancies are reserved for Muslims of Kashmir (for entire State) - Out of remaining 50%, 40% are reserved for Jammu Hindus and 10 per cent for Kashmiri Hindus - Nominal rule contemplated by art. 16 is equality between aspirants to public employment but in view of backwardness of certain classes it would be open to State to make a provision for reservation of appointments or posts in their favour - Provision making such reservation need not be by a statutory enactment, it may be made by an executive order or direction - Held, that State policy was a policy not of reservation of some appointments or posts but it was a scheme of distribution of all posts community wise - Distribution of appointments, post, or promotions made in implementation of that State policy is contrary to constitutional guarantee u/art. 16(1) and (2)and is not saved by cl. (4) - Promotions contrary to art. 16(1) and (4) of Constitution and therefore, void - Petition allowed.
