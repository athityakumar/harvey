Dhani Devi v Sant Bihari and Others

Supreme Court of India

18 October 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Bachawat, S.M. Sikri

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 359; AIR 1970 SC 759; 1970 (72) BomLR 431; [1969] 2 S.C.R. 507

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Civil Procedure; Family & Personal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Succession & Inheritance

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act 1939, ss. 61(2) and 64A - Permission for grant of a permanent stage carriage permit - Whether on death of an applicant for a stage carriage permit in respect of his transport vehicles Regional Transport Authority has power to allow person succeeding to possession of vehicles to prosecute application filed by deceased applicant? - Held, appellant on death of her husband made no formal application for substitution, but no objection was raised on that ground nor was any adjournment asked for by rival claimants in order to enable them to file objections - 'X' is said to have left behind other heirs also, but no objection was taken on ground of their nonjoinder - On materials on record, Regional Transport Authority found that appellant was an experienced and displaced operator - That finding was not challenged in appeal - Only point taken in appeal was that application of 'X' had abated and that Regional Transport Authority had no power to allow her to continue application - Further held, Appellate Authority accepted contention and set aside order directing grant of permit to appellant - Thus, Transport Minister rightly set aside order of Appellate Authority and held that Regional Transport Authority has power to permit her to prosecute application filed by her deceased husband - Therefore, High Court was in error in setting aside order of Transport Minister - Appeal allowed.
