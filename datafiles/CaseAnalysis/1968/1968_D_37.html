Commissioner of Sales Tax, U.P v Sukh Deo

Supreme Court of India

19 August 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, V. Ramaswami

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 78; AIR 1969 SC 499; [1969] 1 S.C.R. 710; 1969 (23) STC 385

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Uttar Pradesh Trade Tax Act, 1948 - Turnover - Taxable amount - Validity - Respondent was medical practitioner and in performance of his professional duties he examined patients, advised them and prescribed medicines which were issued from his dispensary - Sales Tax Officer being of view that dispensing of medicines, according to prescriptions issued by respondent, amounted to manufacture of medicines within meaning of notification no. S.T. 3504/X dt. 10-5- 1956, assessed respondent to pay tax on turnover of Rs. 12,943 for year 1956-57 - HC held that respondent was not manufacturer of medicines and pharmaceutical preparations within meaning of notification - Against answer recorded by HC Commissioner of Sales Tax had appealed to SC - Hence, instant appeal - Whether by virtue of notification respondent was exempt from liability to pay tax - Held, scheme was to levy sales-tax at one point only viz., at point of sale by importer in respect of medicines imported by him into State and at point of sale by manufacturer of medicines manufactured by him within State - If preparation of mixture of drugs as prescribed by medical practitioner in his own dispensary was not manufacture of medicines or pharmaceutical preparation, exception clause of notification would have no application - Appeal dismissed.
