Central Bank of India Limited, New Delhi v Shri Prakash Chand Jain

Supreme Court of India

20 August 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

Vishishtha Bhargava, C.A. Vaidyialingam

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 209; AIR 1969 SC 983; 1969 (39) FLR 393; 1969 LabIC 1880; 1969 (2) LLJ 377; [1969] 1 S.C.R. 735

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1938, s. 33 - Whether when Tribunal found that enquiry was fair, Tribunal had no jurisdiction to go into question whether findings of fact recorded by Enquiry Officer were correct? - It is not for Tribunal to sit in judgment over view taken by Enquiry Officer about value to be attached to evidence of these witnesses - When an Industrial Tribunal is asked to give its approval to an order of dismissal u/s. 33(2)(b) of Act, it can disregard findings given by Enquiry Officer only if findings are perverse - Test of perversity that is indicated in these cases is that findings may not be supported by any legal evidence at all or that Principles of Natural Justice are not complied with - Held, it is clear that Tribunal was fully justified in holding that findings recorded by Enquiry Officer on both charges were perverse in sense of not being supported by any legal evidence - Appeal dismissed.
