Commissioner of Income Tax, West Bengal v Durga Prasad More

Supreme Court of India

22 August 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 291; [1969] 72 ITR 807

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922 - Whether there was any evidence to support the finding the remitted amount constituted income from undisclosed sources? - Explanation offered by the assessee to the effect that the amount was not income was rejected and it resulted into that amount represents income from undisclosed sources - It was never argued that the source of the income affected its eligibility to tax - Enquiry for determining whether the income was from business activity or from other source was not relevant - Held, principle laid down by Supreme Court that 'once it is found that' a receipt by the assessee was income of the assessee it was not necessary for the revenue to locate its exact source applies alike to cases in which an entry is found in the books of account of the assessee, and to cases in which no such entry is found - Further Held, answered the question in affirmative, and order of the High Court was discharged - Appeal allowed.
