Virji Ram Sutaria v Nathalal Premji Bhanvadia And Ors

Supreme Court of India

04 November 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

G.K. Mitter, K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 158; (1969) 1 SCC 77; AIR 1970 SC 765; 1970 ALJ 579; 1970 (2) SCJ 157; [1969] 2 S.C.R. 627

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Sovereignty And Integrity Of India

Summary: Election - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 173, sch. III - Representation of People Act, 1951, s. 15(2) - Oath or affirmation - Election petition - Ground, improper subscribing to oath/affirmation at time of filing of nomination paper, as prescribed u/art. 173 - Form VII in English of oath or affirmation - Nomination paper of returned candidate contained a form of oath or affirmation which was both in Gujarati as well as English - Difference between two forms as relevant for appeal, was that forms lay in this that words 'Legislative Assembly' in form in English were translated in Gujarati form as 'Rajya Sabha' and contention raised that use of this word show that oath that was taken was for purpose of filling a seat not in legislative assembly of State but in legislative council of State - Contention whether acceptable? - Held, meaning of 'Rajya Sabha' in common parlance - In common parlance in many of States in Northern India expression 'Rajya Sabha' has come to mean Legislative Council of a State while State Legislative Assembly is generally known as Rajya Vidhan Sabha - Absence of any authoritative translation of expression 'State Legislative Assembly' in Gujarati no clue can be taken by popular rendering of expression - Further, fact noted that State of Gujarat does not have a Legislative Council - Thus, there could not be any misapprehension either in person taking oath or in Returning Officer when he was accepting nomination paper - Essential requirement u/art. 173 r/w Form VII-A is that person taking oath or making affirmation would bear true faith and allegiance to Constitution of India, 1950 and uphold sovereignty and integrity of India - Words which precede this portion are merely descriptive of person and of his nomination as a candidate - It is reasonable to think that a mere misprint in form of oath or a mere inaccuracy in rendering expression 'Legislative Assembly' in Gujarati would not be fatal to election of candidate, if otherwise valid - Appeal dismissed.
