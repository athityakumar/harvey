R.M. Seshadri v G. Vasantha Pai

Supreme Court of India

29 November 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, G.K. Mitter

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 178; (1969) 1 SCC 27; AIR 1969 SC 692; 1969 (2) MLJ(SC) 50; [1969] 2 S.C.R. 1019

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Election; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Election - Code of Civil Procedure, O. 16, r. 14 - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 98 and 123(5) - Corrupt practice - Determination - Held, Representation of People Act enjoins that all powers under CPC can be exercised and all procedure as far as may be applicable to trial of civil suits may be followed in trial of election petitions - Court would be acting within its jurisdiction in using O. 16 r. 14 to summon witnesses who can throw light upon matter - On merits - Upon considering circumstantial evidence it was established that corrupt practice was indeed involved - Appeal dismissed.
