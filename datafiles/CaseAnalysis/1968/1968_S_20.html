Satish Kumar and Others v Surinder Kumar and Others

Supreme Court of India

27 September 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri, R.S. Bachawat, K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 417; AIR 1970 SC 833; 1970 (1) SCJ 522; [1969] 2 S.C.R. 244

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Practice & Procedure - Indian Arbitration Act, 1940, ss. 14, 16, 17, 30, 32, 33 and 35 - Indian Registration Act, 1908, s. 17(1)(b) - Registration of award - Whether an award given under Act on a private reference requires registration u/s. 17(1)(b) of Indian Registration Act, if award effects partition of immovable property exceeding value of Rs. 100/-? - Held, filing of an unregistered award u/s. 49 of Registration Act, 1908 is not prohibited, what is prohibited is that it cannot be taken into evidence so as to affect immovable property falling u/s. 17 of Act, therefore award required registration and if award affects immovable property over value of Rs. 100/-, its registration does get rid of disability created by s. 49 of Registration Act, 1908 - Appeal dismissed.
