Income Tax Officer, Distt. Ii(ii), Kanpur And others v Shri Mani Ram

Supreme Court of India

20 August 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Ramaswami, J.C. Shah, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 210; AIR 1969 SC 543; [1969] 72 ITR 203; [1969] 1 S.C.R. 724

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Tax - Income Tax Act, 1945, s.17 (1) (a) - Joint Hindu Family - Middle of A/y - Regular Assessment - Provisional Assessment - Respondents were members of joint Hindu family carrying on business until they became divided in middle of assessment year 1953-54 and thereafter they were carrying on business in partnership - For year 1953-54 firm submitted a return showing loss and in next succeeding year 1954-55 it disclosed a profit and submitted a return - All partners filed returns individually and they were provisionally assessed on their returns - Income Tax Officer while assessing them u/s.23 of Act found that they were liable to pay interest u/s.18A(8) and imposed penalty for each year of assessment by virtue of s.28 and s.18A(9) (b) of Act -Respondents preferred appeals to Appellate Assistant Commissioner on ground that levy of interest and penalty was unauthorized and appeals were dismissed - HC allowed and s.18A(3) could not apply to case as there had been a provisional assessment u/s. 23B in year 1954 - Appellants preferred appeals before Division Bench and appeals were dismissed by a common judgment of HC - Hence instant appeal - Whether any person who had not been assessed in s.18A(3) of Income Tax Act, 1922 after Income Tax Amendment Act (Act 67 of 1949) should be interpreted to include a person who had been provisionally assessed u/s. 23B of income tax act - Held, a partner of a firm may be provisionally assessed his share in firm's income, profits and gains if its return had been received although return of partner himself may not had been received - After a regular assessment had been made u/s.23 any amount paid or deemed to had been paid towards a provisional assessment made u/sub-s. (1), shall be deemed to had been paid towards regular assessment and where amount paid or deemed to had been paid towards provisional assessment exceeds amount payable under regular assessment and excess shall be refunded to assessee - Income Tax Officer had to assess provisionally total income of assessee and he has to determine tax payable on basis of provisionally assessed income - If Parliament had contemplated s.18A (3) should apply only in case of a regular assessment there was no reason why it did not put some qualifying words or expressions before or after word assessed in s.18A(1) - s.18A (1) will be applicable whenever a person had been assessed whatever be nature of assessment -There was no such qualification of word assessed in s.18A(3) and it could be read in its ordinary sense as including every kind of assessment including a provisional assessment u/s. 23B of income tax act - Appeals dismissed.
