Bengal Chemical and Pharmaceutical Works Limited v Its Workmen

Supreme Court of India

16 September 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

C.A. Vaidyialingam, Vishishtha Bhargava, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 183; AIR 1969 SC 360; 1969 (18) FLR 172; 1969 LabIC 599; 1969 (1) LLJ 751; [1969] 2 S.C.R. 113

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Age Of Superannuation, Cost Of Living, Neutralisation

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Reference to industrial tribunal - Award - Legality - Govt. by its order dt. 5-11-1963 referred for adjudication of issues of workmen to Industrial Tribunal - Tribunal in its orders directed that dearness allowance should stand revised from november 1963, certain modifications be made to existing scheme of gratuity and Tribunal also increased existing age of superannuation from 55 years to 58 years - Hence, instant appeals - Whether impugned orders of Tribunal were justified - Held, Tribunal had made substantially correct approach in considering claim for revision of dearness allowance - Tribunal had referred to rise in cost of living index after date of settlement of 6-1-1962, and it had also quite rightly held that cl. 10 of settlement was no bar for entertaining claim, therefore, its decision that revision of dearness allowance should be made was perfectly correct - It was, after having regard to all circumstances that Tribunal felt that workmen should get dearness allowance commensurate with cost of living index, at least from month of reference november 1963 - Therefore, on aspect of dearness allowance, excepting for direction that rate of increase or decrease awarded by Tribunal should be related to cost of living index of 421 and not 402 in all other respects decision of Tribunal on instant point would stand - This closed discussion on appeal of company and appeal of Union, in so far as they related to dearness allowance - Tribunal had effected certain modifications to r. 3 which were quite consistent, Management of Wenger & Co. v. Workmen 1962 Indlaw SC 94, relied on - Therefore Union could not have any grievance regarding Tribunal's directions, in this regard, no case was made out by Union for interfering with directions given by Tribunal and SC was also satisfied that there was no improper exercise of discretion by Tribunal in this regard, it had effected certain modifications in favour of workmen and obviously it did not think it necessary to make any further modifications as pressed by Union - Therefore, objections to modifications, raised on behalf of Union were rejected - Further, SC was satisfied that Tribunal's fixing of age of retirement at 58 years was justified - Tribunal had taken note of trend in particular area, when it increased age of superannuation from 55 to 58 years and Union's claim that it should be further increased to 60 years could not be sustained - Thus, excepting for modification indicated by SC with regard to cost of living index in respect of dearness allowance, in all other respects SC confirmed award - Appeal dismissed.
