Estate of The Late A. M.K.M. Karuppan Chettiar v Commissioner of Income Tax, Madras

Supreme Court of India

22 August 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 293; [1969] 72 ITR 403

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 34 - Income Assessment - Income Tax Returns - Notice for Assessment - Appellant never assessed to tax before filing returns - Returns filed by appellant were not disposed of - Notice u/s. 34 of the Act issued by respondent and assessment was made there under - HC affirmed action of respondent - Hence, instant appeal - Whether notice u/s. 34 of the Act issued by Income-tax Officer against appellant valid - Held, no - Notice u/s. 34 of the Act could not be issued against appellant in his individual capacity unless returns which had already been filed by him were disposed of - Appellant in respect of his individual income had never been assessed to tax before he filed returns and unless returns filed by him were disposed of no notice u/s. 34 of the Act was competent - Hence, notice u/s. 34 of the Act issued by Income-tax Officer was liable to be dismissed as incompetent and no assessments could be made in pursuance thereof - Appeals allowed.
