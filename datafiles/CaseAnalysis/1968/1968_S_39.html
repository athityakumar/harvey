Kaushalya Devi and Others v Shri K.L. Bansal

Supreme Court of India

03 December 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri, R.S. Bachawat, K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 157; (1969) 1 SCC 59; AIR 1970 SC 838; 1969 (5) DLT 481; 1969 (71) PLR 289; 1969 RCJ 152; [1969] 2 S.C.R. 1048

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - Delhi and Ajmer Rent Control Act, 1952, s. 13 - Compromise - Decree - Whether decree passed on basis of compromise was a nullity, as order did not indicate any grounds mentioned in s. 13 of Act existed? - Held, in Bahadur Singh's case([1969] 2 SCR 4321969 (2) SCR 432), this Court held that decree passed on basis of an award was in contravention of s. 13(1) of Act because Court had passed decree in terms of award without satisfying itself that ground of eviction existed - Decree in so far as it directs delivery of possession of premises to landlord is a nullity and cannot be executed - Appeal must fail, in view of judgment of Supreme Court in Bahadur Singh's case - Appeal dismissed.
