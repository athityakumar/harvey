Manager, Messrs Pyarchand Kesarimal Ponwal Bidi Factory v Omkar Laxman Thange and Others

Supreme Court of India

27 September 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Shelat, Vishishtha Bhargava

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 416; AIR 1970 SC 823; 1970 (20) FLR 340; 1970 LabIC 737; 1970 (1) LLJ 492; [1969] 2 S.C.R. 272; 1973 (1) SLR 946

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Central Provinces and Berar Shops and Establishment Act, 1947 - Factories Act - Respondent 1 was originally employed in the factory at Kamptee - Services of employee lent to third person - Original employment does not cease - Dismissal - Challenged - Held, contract of service being incapable of transfer unilaterally a transfer of service from one employer to another can only be effected by a tripartite agreement between 'employer', 'employee' and 'third party', 'effect of which would be to terminate' original contract of service and to make a new contract between employee and third party - So long as contract of service is not terminated, a new contract is not made and employee continues to be in employment of 'employer' - When an employer orders him to do a certain work for another person, employee still continues to be in his employment - Employee has right to claim his wages from employer and not from third party - Such third party-hirer may pay his wages but that is because of his agreement with employer - Hirer may also exercise control and direction in doing of thing for which he is hired or even manner in which it is to be done - But hirer third party cannot dismiss him - Right of dismissal vests in employer - Held, High Court was, right in setting aside order of Asst. Commr. and Industrial Court on ground that unless a finding was reached on facts of case that contract of service with said factory came to an end and a fresh contract with head office came into being respondent 1 continued to be in employment of factory and head office, therefore, was not competent to dismiss him - Appeal dismissed.
