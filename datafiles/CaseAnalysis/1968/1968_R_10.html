Amolak Chand v Raghuveer Singh

Supreme Court of India

22 February 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Ramaswami, G.K. Mitter, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 338; AIR 1968 SC 1203; [1968] 3 S.C.R. 246

Case Digest

Subject: Election; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Election - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 116-A, 33(2) and 36, Rule 4 - Parliamentary Election Rules, r. 8(2) - Appellant was a candidate at election of Legislative Assembly - Appellant was declared elected to Assembly - Besides appellant and Respondent two other candidates proposed by one elector - Their nomination papers rejected - Respondent filed an Election Petition u/s. 80 of RP Act in High Court against Appellant on ground that election of appellant was void under s. 100(c) of RP Act on ground that nomination papers of other two candidates were improperly rejected - HC accepted contention of Respondent - Appeal - (A) Whether an elector can propose only one, candidate for a single seat Constituency and if more than one nomination is made for a single seat Constituency, all nominations should be taken to be null and void? - Held, there is no express ban or prohibition u/s. 33 or s. 36 of RP Act against an elector proposing more than one candidate for a single seat constituency - Amendment to s. 33 of RP Act indicates that it was intention of Parliament that there should be no ban on number of nomination papers or number of candidates to be proposed by an elector for a single seat constituency - (B) Whether nomination papers of Nathu and Sita Ram were rightly rejected by returning officer? - Held, printed form 2-A is meant both for General and Reserved Constituencies but while it is obligatory for candidates in reserved constituency to make a declaration in proper column that he is a member of a particular caste or tribe, there is no such rule with regard to a General Constituency - S. 33(2) of RP Act imposes an obligation on candidate in reserved constituency to make a declaration in proper column, but there is no such direction in statute regard to General Constituency - There is nothing either in section or in rules forbidding candidate from mentioning his caste - There is no violation of provisions of s. 33 of Act or breach of general directions contained in Rule 4 and nomination papers cannot be held to be invalid on this account - Appeal dismissed.
