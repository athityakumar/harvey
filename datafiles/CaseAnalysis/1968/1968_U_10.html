Raj Kumar v Union of India and Another

Supreme Court of India

18 April 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Vishishtha Bhargava, G.K. Mitter

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 44; AIR 1969 SC 180; 1969 (18) FLR 125; 1969 LabIC 310; 1970 (1) LLJ 13; [1968] 3 S.C.R. 857; 1968 SLR 730

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service

Summary: Service - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art.311 - Resignation from service - Dismissal from employment - Appellant belonged to Indian Administrative Service who addressed letter to Chief Minister as well as to Chief Secretary to Govt. for resignation from service and requested that it may be forwarded to Government of India with remarks of State Govt. - After some time appellant by letter, requested Chief Secretary to Govt. to recommend 'acceptance of withdrawal' of his resignation from service - Order accepting resignation of appellant from Indian Administrative Service was issued and appellant was directed to hand over charge to Additional Collector - Appellant moved petition in HC - HC rejected petition holding that resignation became effective on date on which it was accepted by Govt. of India, and subsequent withdrawal of resignation was ineffective, even if acceptance of resignation was not intimated to appellant - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether acceptance of resignation amounted to dismissal from employment and failure to comply with requirements of art. 311 of the Constitution - Held, when public servant was invited by his letter of resignation determination of his employment, his services normally stood terminated from date on which letter of resignation was accepted by appropriate authority - Further, in absence of any law or rule governing conditions of his service to contrary, it should not be open to public servant to withdraw his resignation after it was accepted by appropriate authority - Though, resignation was accepted within short time after it was received by Govt. of India, State did not immediately implement order and relieve, appellant of his duties, but appellant could not profit by delay in intimating acceptance or in relieving him of his duties - Thus, acceptance of resignation amounted to dismissal from employment and failure to comply with requirements of art. 311 of the Constitution vitiates order accepting resignation was no force - Appeal dismissed.
