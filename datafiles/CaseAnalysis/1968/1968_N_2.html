 State of Madras v N. K. Nataraja Mudaliar

Supreme Court of India

18 April 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, R.S. Bachawat, G.K. Mitter, K.S. Hegde, C.A. Vaidyialingam

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 121; AIR 1969 SC 147; 1969 (1) MLJ(SC) 28; [1968] 3 S.C.R. 829; 1968 (22) STC 376

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Sales Tax

Keywords: Declared Goods, Tax On The Sale Or Purchase Of Goods, Collected, Different Rates Of Tax, Transfer Of Documents Of Title, Andhra Pradesh Sugarcane (Regulation Of Supply And Purchase) Act, 1961, Sales Tax Laws Validation Act, 1956

Summary: Constitution - Sales Tax - Indirect Tax - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 301, 302 and 303(1) - Central Sales-tax Act, 1956, ss. 8(2), (2-A) and (5) - Whether enactment is ultra vires constitutional safeguards for protecting interstate trade? - Held, restrictions or impediments which directly and immediately impede or hamper free flow of trade, commerce and intercourse fall within prohibition imposed by art. 301 and subject to other provisions of Constitution they may be regarded as void sub-s. (5) of s. 8 which authorises State Govt, notwithstanding anything contained in s. 8 in public interest to waive tax or impose tax on sales at a lower rate on inter-state trade or commerce - It is dear that legislature has contemplated that elasticity of rates consistent with economic forces is clearly intended to be maintained tax though levied for and collected in name of Central Govt. is a part of sales-tax levy imposed for benefit of States - By leaving it to States to levy sales tax in respect of a commodity on intra-State transactions no discrimination is practised and by authorising State from which movement of goods commences to levy on transactions of sale Central sales-tax, at rates prevailing in State, subject to limitation already set out, no discrimination can be deemed to be practised - Appeal allowed.
