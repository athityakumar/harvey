Caltex (India) Limited v Bhagwan Devi Marodia

Supreme Court of India

26 September 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Bachawat, S.M. Sikri

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 365; AIR 1969 SC 405; 1969 RCR(Rent) 552; [1969] 2 S.C.R. 238

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Rent Control - Calcutta Thika Tenancy Act, 1949 - Power of authorities to renew the lease under the provisions of Thika Tenancy Act - High Court has stayed execution of order of eviction for a month - On the ground that authority has no power - Whether the tenant is entitled to renew the lease? - Held, no time is fixed for renewal of lease - An application for renewal for lease may be made within a reasonable time before expiry of term - In present case, lease fixes a time within which application for renewal is to be made - Time so fixed is of essence of bargain - Tenant loses his right unless he makes application within stipulated time - Equity will not relieve tenant from consequences of his own neglect which could well be avoided with reasonable diligence - Stipulation as to time in cl. 3(c) of indenture of lease D/- 17-02-1954 should be regarded as of essence of contract - Appellant not having exercised option of renewal within time limited by clause, is not entitled to a renewal - Appeal dismissed.
