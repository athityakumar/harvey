Official Trustee, W.B. and Others v Sachindra Nath Chatterjee and Another

Supreme Court of India

03 December 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri, R.S. Bachawat, K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 482; AIR 1969 SC 823; [1969] 3 S.C.R. 92

Case Digest

Subject: Trusts & Associations

Keywords: Official Trustees Act, 1913, Trustees and Mortgagee's Powers Act, 1866, Calcutta High Court (Original Side) Rules (1914), Chap.13

Summary: Trusts & Associations - Indian Trusts Act, 1882, s. 34 - Official Trustees Act, 1913, s. 10(1) - Trustees and Mortgagee's Powers Act, 1866, s. 43(1) - Calcutta High Court (Original Side) Rules, 1914, chap. 13, rr. 1 and 9 - (A) Whether the settlor was entitled to execute the second trust deed in pursuance of the power reserved by him under the original trust deed? - Held, the settlor had the power to make the variation in question - (B) Whether in any event the validity of the second trust deed is not open to challenge made by Ramfry, J.? - Held,the said order was outside the jurisdiction of the Learned Judge, he had no competence to make such an order, therefore, a void order - As per order of High Court, the official trustee must have been disbursing the trust income to the various beneficiaries - Accounting ordered, from the date of institution of present suit - Appeal dismissed.
