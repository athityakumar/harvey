Balammal and Others Etc v State of Madras and Ors.Etc

Supreme Court of India

23 April 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 128; AIR 1968 SC 1425; 1969 (1) MLJ(SC) 21; 1969 (1) SCJ 209; [1969] 1 S.C.R. 90

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition (Madras Amendment) Act, 1961 - Land Acquisition Act, 1894,s.21 - Madras City Improvement Trust Act, 1945 - (A) Whether owners are entitled to get 15% solatium in addition to market value for compulsory acquisition of lands? - Held, an owner of land is ordinarily entitled to receive solatium in addition to market-value, for compulsory acquisition of his land, if it is acquired under Land Acquisition Act but not if it is acquired under Madras City Improvement Trust Act - Clear case of discrimination thus arises - Owners of land in present case were entitled to receive statutory solatium u/s. 23(2) of Land Acquisition Act - (B) Compensation - Held, HC committed no error of principle in fixing value of the, lands in question, nor had it failed to apply its mind to any important piece of evidence - Appeals dismissed.
