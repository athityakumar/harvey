Commissioner of Sales Tax, M.P., Indore v Allwyn Cooper

Supreme Court of India

21 August 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 64; 1970 (25) STC 26

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Sales Tax

Keywords: Railway Freight, Madhya Pradesh General Sales Tax Act, 1958

Summary: Sales Tax - Tax liability - Inter-state trade - Madhya Pradesh General Sales Tax Act, 1958, s. 18(3) - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956, s. 3(a) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 286 - Constitution (Sixth Amendment) Act, 1956 - Sales Tax Officer assessed sales of respondent under Act, 1958 - Respondent contended that entire turnover was of sales in course of export of manganese ore out of India and alternatively, sales were inter-State sales, and, therefore, not liable to be taxed under Act, 1958 - Board of Revenue accepted respondent's contention - On appeal, High Court held that disputed sales were in course of inter-State trade of manganese ore - Whether disputed sales of manganese were sales in course of inter-State trade and commerce or were intra-State sales ? - Held, movement of manganese ore from State of Madhya Pradesh to Vishakhapatnam outside State was a direct result of covenant of contracts - Sales were inter-State sales u/s. 3(a) of Central Act and were not liable to be taxed under Act, 1958 - Appeal dismissed.
