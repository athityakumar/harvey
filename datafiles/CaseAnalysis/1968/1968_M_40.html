 Messrs Tarapore and Company, Madras v Messrs V/O. Tractors Export, Moscow and Another

Supreme Court of India

15 November 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, G.K. Mitter, K.S. Hegde, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 471; AIR 1970 SC 1168; 1971 (73) BomLR 31; 1970 (2) MLJ(SC) 1; [1969] 2 S.C.R. 699

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 133(1)(a) and 133(1)(b) - Suit filed by plaintiffs is a civil proceeding, and suit involves some claim or question respecting property of not less than twenty thousand rupees - Plaintiffs applied in suit for an interim injunction restraining Bank of India Ltd.-first defendant from taking any steps in pursuance of a letter of credit opened in favour of second defendant - Granted - High Court set aside that order - Plaintiffs applied for certificate u/arts. 133(1)(a) and 133(1)(b) of Constitution - HC observed that an order granting interim injunction is a final order and they were competent to grant certificate - Whether correct? - Expression 'judgment' in art. 133(1) in context in which it occurs means a final adjudication by court of rights of parties, and that an interlocutory judgment even if it decides an issue or issues without finally determining rights and liabilities of parties is not a judgment, however, cardinal issue may be - Held, in present case not even an issue has been decided, and it is not contended that order of HC amounts to a judgment or a decree - Order passed by HC in appeal which does not finally dispose of a suit or proceeding and leaves rights and obligations of parties for determination in suit or proceeding from which appeal has arisen, is not final within meaning of arts. 133(1)(a) and (b) - Order refusing to grant an interim injunction did not determine rights and obligations of parties in relation to matter in dispute in suit - Order accordingly.
