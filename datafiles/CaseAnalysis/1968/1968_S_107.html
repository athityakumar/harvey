Mulamchand v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

20 February 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, G.K. Mitter, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 393; AIR 1968 SC 1218; 1968 ALJ 745; 1968 JabLJ 1025; [1968] 3 S.C.R. 214

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Defence & Security Forces

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 299 - Government of India Act, 1935, s. 175 - Indian Contract Act,s. 70 - Madhya Pradesh Abolition of Proprietary Rights (Estates, Mahals, Alienated Lands) Act, 1950, ss. 3, 4, 5, 7, 47 and 63 - Appellant purchased right to pluck, collect and remove forest produce from proprietors of different Malguzari jungles for years 1951, 1952 and 1953 - Right acquired by appellant came to be vest in State of Madhya Pradesh under Act, 1950 - Case of appellant was that he was entitled to refund of amount as right to collect tendu leaves for year 1951 had already been purchased by him - Refund was claimed on the basis that there was no valid contract between appellant and State of Madhya Pradesh as the provisions of art. 299 of Constitution were not complied with and contract was void - Whether appellant entitled to refund of amount as right to collect tendu leaves for year 1951 had already been purchased by him? - Contract between Dominion of India and a private individual is not in form required by s. 175(3) of Act, 1935, it was void and could not be enforced and therefore Dominion of India cannot be sued by private individual breach of such contract - Principle is that the provisions of s. 175(3) of Act, 1935 or corresponding provisions of art. 299(1) of Constitution are mandatory in character and contravention of these provisions nullifies contracts and makes them void - No question of estoppel or ratification in such a case - Further held, where a claim for compensation is made by one person against another u/s. 70, it is not on the basis of any subsisting contract between parties but a different kind of obligation - Appellant would have been entitled to compensation u/s. 70 of Indian Contract Act if he had adduced evidence to support of his claim - Appellant did not produce sufficient evidence to show to what extent he worked contract and what was profit made by him in year 1951 and succeeding year - Held, in absence of reliable evidence appellant was not entitled to restitution or refund of deposit he had made - Appeal dismissed.
