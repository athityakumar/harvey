State of Kerala v P. P. Hassan Koya

Supreme Court of India

19 March 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, G.K. Mitter

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 385; AIR 1968 SC 1201; [1968] 3 S.C.R. 459

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: (A) Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - Receiver - Securities - Land Acquisition Officer - Whether the rate of capitalization was unduly high - Held, multiple based on a return from the gilt-gedged securities was the appropriate multiple for determining the value of the probe under acquisition, and no ground has been suggested for not lepting the basis and the rate of capitalization adopted by them - It was relevant to note that the same multiple which had been adopted in other cases relating to lands and buildings acquired under the same notification under which the land of the respondent was acquired had not been challenged by the State - Appeal dismissed.

(B) Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - Receiver - Securities - Land Acquisition Officer - Govt. of Madras issued a notification u/s. 4 of the Act for acquisition for a public purpose i.e widening of a road - There were seven units of lands with buildings - One of the unit with a building was used for business purposes - Notification u/s. 6 was issued for possession of land - Buildings constructed on the land belonged to the respondent and were let out to tenants at an aggregate monthly rent - Land Acquisition Officer determined the compensation payable to the persons interested - In a reference at the instance of the respondent u/s. 18 of the Act the Subordinate Judge was of the view that the method adopted by the Land Acquisition Officer for compensation by separately valuing the lands as garden lands and the break-up value of the houses was 'manifestly unjust and improper' - Each unit had to be valued as a composite property - Land Acquisition Officer then adopted the method of determining the market value by capitalizing the net rent received from the unit and taking into consideration the return from the gilt-edged securities at the relevant date, the learned Judge awarded compensation for the unit in which the respondent was interested at 35 times the net annual rental - HC determined compensation by multiplying the net rent 33-1/3 times that being in their view the true, multiple derived from the return based on the current return from gilt-edged securities - Whether the Receiver having accepted the award of the Land Acquisition. Officer, the respondent could claim compensation only for the right which he had in the land and the buildings and the method adopted by the Land Acquisition Officer was in the circumstances the only appropriate method - Held, the method adopted by the Land Acquisition Officer for determining compensation payable for extinction of the interest of the holder of the land and the buildings separately was unwarranted - In determining compensation payable in respect of land with buildings, compensation shouldn't be determined' by ascertaining the value of the land and the "break-up value" of the building separately - U/s. 23 of the Act compensation had to be determined by taking into consideration the market value of the land at the date of the publication of the notification u/s. 4(1) and the damage, if any, sustained by the persons interested under any of the heads mentioned in secondly to sixthly in s. 23(1) of the Act - Respondent was the holder of Kanam rights in the land and buildings - Respondent being Kanamdar had interest in the disputed land and he was entitled to apportionment of compensation even in respect of land - Appeal dismissed.
