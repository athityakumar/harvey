 Anglo-American Direct Tea Trading Company Limited v Commissioner of Agricultural Income Tax, Kerala

Supreme Court of India

10 January 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Bachawat, G.K. Mitter, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, C.A. Vaidyialingam, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 258; AIR 1968 SC 1213; 1968 (2) ITJ 408; [1968] 69 ITR 667; 1968 KerLJ 565; [1968] 2 S.C.R. 745

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Tax - Income-tax Rules,1962, rr.8,24 - Agricultural income-tax - Non agricultural - Taxable territories - Deductions allowable - Appellants carried business of cultivation, manufacture and sale of tea and own tea plantations in State of Kerala and some of them own tea plantations both within and outside state and they were assessed to non-agricultural as well as agricultural income-tax - Central income tax authorities computed total tea income of appellants and 40 per cent thereof representing non-agricultural income was assessed to non-agricultural income-tax and balance 60 percent was left un assessed as agricultural income - Appellants produced before Agricultural Income-tax Assistant Commissioner, Kerala and requested him to take 60 per cent of tea income computed by central income-tax authorities as gross income derived from agriculture - HC assumed that agricultural Income-tax Officer was not obliged to accept computation of tea income made by Income-tax Officer - Whether Tribunal was justified in holding surcharge on agricultural income-tax - Held, agricultural income derived from land by cultivation of tea means that portion of income derived from cultivation, manufacture and sale of tea was defined to be agricultural income for purposes of enactments relating to Indian Income-tax - Power of State Legislature to make a law in respect of taxes on agricultural income arising from tea plantation was limited to legislating with respect to agricultural income determined and legislature cannot add to amount of agricultural income determined by disallowing any item of deductions allowable - Agricultural Income-tax Officer in making an assessment of agricultural income was bound to accept computation of tea income already made by central income-tax authorities and to assess only 60 per cent of income so computed less allowable deductions as agricultural income taxable - No surcharge on agricultural income can be levied under Kerala Surcharge on Taxes Act - SC stated HC judgment should be set aside - Appeals disposed of.
