Hira Singh Pal v Madan Lal

Supreme Court of India

15 January 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, Mohammad Hidayatullah

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 242; AIR 1968 SC 1179; [1968] 2 S.C.R. 778

Case Digest

Subject: Election; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Election - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 36, 100 - Whether grounds on which Returning Officer rejected nomination papers of respondent were substantial grounds as contemplated by s. 36? - Returning officer rejected nomination of respondent on ground that he was a dummy candidate but that was not a matter for him to decide - If he was a dummy candidate there was occasion for him to withdraw his candidature after scrutiny of nomination papers - Therefore, it is quite clear that respondent's nomination papers were improperly rejected - Such a rejection was impermissible u/s. 36 and same is a ground for setting aside election u/s. 100 of Act - Returning officer ignored clerical errors in nomination papers - Appeal dismissed.
