 Hardeodas Jagannath v State Of Assam & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

27 September 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Ramaswami, G.K. Mitter, K.S. Hegde, A.N. Grover, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 336; AIR 1970 SC 724; [1969] 2 S.C.R. 261; 1970 (26) STC 10

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure; Sales Tax

Summary: Extra Provincial Jurisdiction Act, 1947, ss. 3, 4 and 6(2) - Assam Sales Tax Act, 1947, Pre., s. 30 - (A) Question whether Dominion of India was entitled to exercise extra provincial jurisdiction over the Shillong Administered Areas on 15-04- 1948 - Question of law or of fact - (B) Local Sales Tax - Provision of Assam Sales Tax - (1) Act extended to Shillong Administered Areas - If Union Govt. is entitled to exercise extra provincial jurisdiction - By Central Govt. notification D/- 15-04-1948 - Answers of the Central Govt. to the questions forwarded by the Supreme Court - Conclusiveness - (2) Amendment requiring deposit of assessed tax and penalty as condition for filing appeal - Amendment coming into force on 01-04-1958 - Assessment for period ending on dates prior to 01-04-1958 completed after this date - (3) 'Otherwise directed' u/s. 30 - Interpretation - Held, is not a pure question of fact but relating to a fact of State which is peculiarly within cognizance of Central Govt. - Act was validly extended and reply of Central Govt. is conclusive u/s. 6(2), Extra Provincial Jurisdiction Act and, after considering the answers of Central Govt. to the questions referred by the SC that the Union Govt. was entitled to exercise extra provincial jurisdiction - Amended section applies - Section does not give power to appellate authority to permit assessee to furnish security in lieu of cash amount of tax - Appeal Dismissed.
