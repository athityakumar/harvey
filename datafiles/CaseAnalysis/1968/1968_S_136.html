State of Uttar Pradesh v Maharaj Narain and Others

Supreme Court of India

30 January 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, Mohammad Hidayatullah

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 401; AIR 1968 SC 960; 1968 CRLJ 1132; [1968] 2 S.C.R. 842

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Indian Limitation Act, 1908, s. 12(2) - Scope of expression 'time requisite for obtaining a copy of the decree, sentence or order appealed from' - Whether appeal made in High Court by State against order of Asst. Sessions Judge, acquitting respondents, was barred by limitation? - Expression 'time requisite' in s. 12(2) of Limitation Act cannot be understood as time absolutely necessary for obtaining copy of order - What is deductible u/s. 12(2) is not minimum time within which a copy of order appealed against could have been obtained - It must be remembered that s. 12(2) enlarges period of limitation prescribed under entry 157 of Sch. 1 - Section lays no obligation on appellant to be prompt in his application for a copy of order - Plain reading of s. 12(2) shows that in computing period of limitation prescribed for an appeal, day on which judgment or order complained of was pronounced and time taken by court to make available copy applied for have to be excluded - In s. 12 words 'time requisite for obtaining a copy of decree' mean time beyond party's control occupied in obtaining copy which is filed with memorandum of appeal and not an ideal lesser period which might have been occupied if application for copy had been filed on some other date - Held, decision under appeal does not lay down law correctly - Yet it is not a fit case to interfere with order of HC dismissing appeal - Appeal dismissed.
