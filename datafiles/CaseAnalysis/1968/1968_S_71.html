Chhatu Ram Horil Ram Limited v State of Bihar and Another

Supreme Court of India

31 January 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 463; AIR 1969 SC 177; [1968] 2 S.C.R. 881

Case Digest

Subject: Mines & Minerals

Summary: Mines & Minerals - Bihar Land Reforms Act, 1950, ss. 3, 4, 4(1), 10 and 10(1) - Mineral Concession Rules, 1949, r. 40 - Whether appellant company had any interest in land and was it entitled to a renewal of lease? - Held, a clause for renewal of lease at a future date was a limitation imposed upon lessor - Lessor's freedom as an absolute owner was sought to be curtailed by such agreement - It was thus an encumbrance and all encumbrances were wiped out by s. 4 of Act - Taking all these provisions into consideration, an agreement for renewal of a lease in future cannot be binding upon State Govt. after vesting of estate' - Even assuming that r. 40 of Rules, 1949 applies to such a statutory lease, duration of 'original lease' may be deemed to be no longer than period between date of vesting and 30-09-1955 - That period for which renewal may have been claimed has expired many years ago, and recognition of rights of appellant - Company will be of no practical significance in this appeal - Appeal dismissed.
