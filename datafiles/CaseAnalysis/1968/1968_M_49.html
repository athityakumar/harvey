Mohan Raj v Surendra Kumar Taparia and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 August 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, G.K. Mitter

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 86; AIR 1969 SC 677; [1969] 1 S.C.R. 630

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Election - Practice & Procedure - Civil Procedure Code, 1908 - Representation of People Act, 1950, ss. 82(b) and 86(1) - Returned candidate - (A) Whether election petition can be dismissed for non joinder of party against whom charges of corrupt practice made? - Held, duly nominated candidate (who withdrew later) had to be compulsorily joined if allegations of corrupt practice made against him - Relied on Har Swarup and Anr v. Brij Bhushan Saran and Ors, Indlaw SC 301 - Candidate who is duly nominated continues to be a candidate for purposes of s. 82(b) in spite of withdrawal - (B) Applicability of CPC, 1908 - Held, CPC, 1908 applies subject to provisions of Representation of People Act, 1950 and any rules made thereunder - When Act enjoins penalty of dismissal of petition for non-joinder of a party provisions of CPC, 1908 cannot be used as curative means to save petition - Appeal dismissed.
