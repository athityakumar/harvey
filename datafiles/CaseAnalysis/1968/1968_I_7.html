Sahu Rajeshwar Nath v Income Tax Officer, C-Ward, Meerut, and Another

Supreme Court of India

04 September 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, A.N. Grover, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 270; AIR 1969 SC 667; 1969 (1) ITJ 851; [1969] 72 ITR 617; [1969] 1 S.C.R. 999

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Assessment Year

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, ss.29, 46(2) - Code of Civil Procedure,1908,O. 21, r.50(2) - Arrear of tax - Recovery of tax - Appellant was a partner of a firm and assumed 12/53 share - Partnership firm was assessed to Income Tax for the assessment year 1945-46, by Income Tax Officer on a total income of Rs.64,622 - Income Tax Officer forwarded a certificate to the Collector u/s.46(2) of Act for recovery of the tax due - Appellant moved petition before HC for restraining respondents from proceeding with recovery of Income Tax from him - Petition dismissed on ground that liability of every partner in respect of the tax due from firm was joint and several and it was open to Income Tax Officer to proceed against any one of the partners alone - Whether tax assessed against firm sought to be recovered from partner of firm - Held, it was manifest that provisions of O.21 r.50(2) of CPC apply and since appellant does not dispute that he was a partner of the unregistered firm for relevant accounting year, Collector could lawfully proceed to execute the certificate u/s.46(2) of Act against appellant and recover the Income Tax arrears from him - Proceedings taken for recovery of tax by respondents against appellant were legally valid and appellant was not entitled to grant of a writ u/art.226 of Constitution - Appeal dismissed.
