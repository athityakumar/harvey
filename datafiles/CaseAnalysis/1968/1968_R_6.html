Mussamiya Imam Halder Bax Razvi v Rabari Govindhai Ratnabhai and Others

Supreme Court of India

21 August 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, A.N. Grover, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 211; AIR 1969 SC 439; 1969 (71) BomLR 681; 1969 (10) G.L.R. 421; 1969 MahLJ 513; [1969] 1 S.C.R. 785

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Keywords: Maharashtra Agricultural Land (Celling on holdings) Act, 1961

Summary: Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 -

Guardians and Wards Act, 1890 - Maharashtra Agricultural Land Act, 1961 - Tenancy - Possession - Lease - Statutory ownership - Estate was held by plaintiff/minor and Collector of was appointed as guardian and manager - Plaintiff initiated suit that defendants fraudulently entered for getting possession of lands - Defendant stated that they required lands for carrying on agriculture and it was granted by collector - Defendant contended that valid lease had created and Trial Court had no jurisdiction to suit - Plaintiff claim for recovery of possession was allowed by Trial Court - HC u/s. 85A of 1905 Act referred issue to Mamlatdar - Hence instant appeal - Whether defendants was statutory owners of land and possession of tenancy was valid and Whether Trial Court was barred in its Jurisdiction - Held, no independent plea of tenancy set up by defendants as subsisting on date of suit and there was no issue to referred for Mamlatdar u/s. 85A of 1905 Act - HC was error in referring fresh issue to Mamlatdar - Tenancy was based upon alleged lease of substantive claim of statutory ownership - Jurisdiction of Trial Court was not barred in considering provisions of 1905 Act - Defendants had not substantiated with valid lease of lands - Hence, defendants were not tenants - Appeals disposed of.
