R. H. Bhutani v Miss Mani J. Desai and Others

Supreme Court of India

23 April 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Shelat, Vishishtha Bhargava, S.M. Sikri

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 443; AIR 1968 SC 1444; 1968 Crimes 223; 1969 CRLJ 13; [1969] 1 S.C.R. 80

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 145 - Eviction - Restoration of possession - Entitlement of - Respondent no.1 had her office premises which given to appellant by agreement with permitting him to occupy cabins on leave and licence - Respondent no.1 demanded higher compensation which appellant refused to pay and thereupon respondent no.1 refused to execute renewal and threatened to eject him forcibly if he did not vacate - Respondent no.1 then handed over possession of cabin to respondent nos.2, 3 - Appellant approached higher authorities in consequence of which police at last recorded case of assault against respondent no.1 and she was arrested but released her on bail - Appellant filed application before Additional Chief Presidency Magistrate where Magistrate directed restoration of possession to appellant till he would be evicted - Respondent no.1 filed suit in City Civil Court and Court dismissed notice of motion - Revision was made before HC, and HC set aside order of Magistrate - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether order passed by HC was liable to be quashed and set aside - Held, party who was forcibly and wrongfully dispossessed did not in retaliation take law into his hands, be should be at disadvantage and could not have benefit of s. 145 of CrPC - HC erred in holding that merely as dispossession of appellant was completed, there was no dispute existing on that day which was likely to lead to breach of peace or that Magistrate was prevented from passing his preliminary order and proceeding thence to continue enquiry and pass his final order - Therefore, reading of s.145 of CrPC as whole, it was clear that even though respondent no.1 had taken over possession of said cabin, since that incident took place within prescribed period of two months next before date of preliminary order, appellant was deemed to be in possession on date of that order and Magistrate was competent to pass final order directing restoration of possession and restraining respondent no.1 from interfering with that possession until appellant's eviction in due course of law - Hence, order passed by HC was set aside and order passed by trial Magistrate was restored - Appeal allowed.
