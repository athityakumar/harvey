State of Madras v Messrs K.C.P. Limited

Supreme Court of India

20 August 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Grover, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 207; AIR 1969 SC 348; 1969 (1) MLJ(SC) 47; [1969] 1 S.C.R. 778; 1969 (23) STC 173

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Bombay Sales Act, 1953

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956, s. 2 (b) - Turnover - Fixed assets - Sustainability - Respondent main business was manufacture and sale of machinery and parts of machinery and accessories - For manufacturing parts of machinery respondent maintained foundry and purchased two arc furnaces - In account books and balance sheet these furnaces were shown under the heading 'workshop equipment' - According to respondent furnaces were found to be unsuitable and were disposed of - For a/y 1958-59 assessing authorities sought to include amount in turnover of company although it was maintained by respondent that sale represented isolated sale of its fixed capital assets - Appeal before Tribunal failed - Revision petition was presented to HC - HC rejected petition - Hence, instant appeal - Whether respondent was liable to pay sales tax on an amount being sale-price of two arc furnaces which 'had been purchased in 1952 and sold in 1958 - Held, assessee decided to sell furnaces because it was discovered that they were too big to be installed in manufacturing plant - Case of assessee throughout was and no evidence or material to contrary existed that furnaces had been shown in books of assessee under classification 'workshop equipment' - Same entries existed in balance sheet - Although assessee was dealing in sale of heavy machinery and machinery part it was nowhere proved that furnaces were ever manufactured or sold by it or were part of its business or ingrained therein - Furnaces were either fixed assets or discarded goods which had been found to be unserviceable or unsuitable - Assessee could therefore hardly be said to be dealer within definition given in s. 2(b) of the Act - Appeal dismissed.
