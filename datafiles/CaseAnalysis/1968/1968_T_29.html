T. S. Pl. P. Chidambaram Chettiar v T. K. B. Santhanaramaswami Odayar and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 January 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, Vishishtha Bhargava, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 405; AIR 1968 SC 1005; 1968 (2) ALT(SC) 375; 1968 (2) MLJ(SC) 83; [1968] 2 S.C.R. 754

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Madras Estates Land Act, 1908 - Restoration - Appellant instituted several suits for recovery of possession from respondents of the disputed lands and for payment of damages - Subordinate Judge dismissed the suits holding that the lands were situated in an estate and were 'ryoti lands' in which the defendants were entitled to occupancy rights - Appellant appealed to the HC which affirmed the decision of the trial court and dismissed all the appeals - Hence these appeals - Whether the suit lands were located in an estate within the meaning of s. 3 (2) (d) of the Act - Held, appellant had not adduced sufficient evidence to rebut the presumption u/s. 18 of the Act that the lands in the village were not private land - Judgment of the HC was correct - Appeals dismissed.
