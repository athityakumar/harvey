Dewaji v Ganpatlal

Supreme Court of India

06 August 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri, R.S. Bachawat, K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 101; AIR 1969 SC 560; 1969 (71) BomLR 693; 1969 MahLJ 495; [1969] 1 S.C.R. 573

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Central Provinces and Berar Regulation of Agricultural Leases Act,1951, ss. 16,16 A and 16 B - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 9 and 105(2) - Letters Patent (Bom), Cl. 15 - Application of s. 16,16 A and 16 B of Central Provinces and Berar Regulation of Agricultural Leases Act,1951 to appeals pending when Amendment Act of 1953 came into force - Jurisdiction of Civil Court - Exclusion - Letter Patent Appeal against an interlocutory order of Single Judge - (A)Whether Act of 1953 was to apply to pending appeals? - There are No words in s. 16, 16 A and 16 B which can lead to necessary inference that these provisions were intended to apply to appeals pending when 1953 Act came into force - It is true that word 'whenever' is wide but s. 16 A uses words 'suit or proceeding and these words do not ordinarily indicate appellate proceedings. Further, s. 16 B uses word 'entertain' and not words 'entertain or try any suit' as contained in s. 15(2) of 1951 Act - If intention was to affect pending proceedings, word 'try' along with word 'entertain' would have been used in s. 16 B of 1953 Act - Held, intention was not to apply 1953 Act to pending appeals -(B) Whether under Letters Patent to obtain a separate leave to appeal against an interlocutory order of Single Judge id to be obtained? - Held,it is not necessary under Letters Patent to obtain a separate leave to appeal against an interlocutory order of Single Judge and in appeal filed against judgment of Single Judge Letters Patent Bench can decide all points decided by Single Judge in his interlocutory order s. 105(2) CPC does not apply to such a case - Appeal dismissed.
