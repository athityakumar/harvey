Purtabpore Company Limited v Cane Commissioner of Bihar and Others

Supreme Court of India

21 November 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, S.M. Sikri

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 353; (1969) 1 SCC 308; AIR 1970 SC 1896; [1969] 2 S.C.R. 807

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative

Summary: Administrative - Constitution of India, 1950 art. 226 - Appellant Co. was established in 1903 - Though its sugar mill is in Uttar Pradesh it used to draw its sugarcane requirement mainly from neighboring areas in Bihar State - Mill in question is within about 100 yards of Bihar border - Appellant's case is that for last over 30 years the 208 villages of Bihar, which had been principal source of its supply of sugarcane and that Bihar authorities used to reserve those villages for it - Validity of order made by Cane Commr. was challenged before High Court on six different grounds - HC rejected all contentions - It Concluded that Cane Commr. who had power to make reservations u/cl. 6 of 'order' had also power to modify or cancel those reservations in view of s. 21 of General Clauses Act - (A) Whether conclusion of HC that the proceeding before the Cane Commr. was not a quasi-judicial proceeding is justified? - Held, there is hardly any doubt that modification of reservation made in favour of appellant would have had serious repercussions on the working of the appellant's mill - It was bound to affect its interests adversely - Hence it is not possible to accept the conclusion of HC that proceeding before Cane Commr. was not a quasi-judicial proceeding - (B) Whether there was a lis between the appellant and the 5th respondent at an earlier stage or not? - Held, Court opined that as soon as 5th respondent moved Govt. for altering or modifying reservation made in favour of appellant, a lis commenced - Dispute that arose between appellant and 5th respondent had to be decided on the basis of objective criteria, prescribed by cl. 6 of the 'order' i.e., (1) the crushing capacity of the appellant mill; (2) the availability of the sugarcane in the reserved area and (3) the need for the production of sugar - Appeal allowed.
