 P. V. Jagannath Rao and Others v State of Orissa and Others

Supreme Court of India

30 April 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, J.C. Shah, Vishishtha Bhargava, G.K. Mitter, C.A. Vaidyialingam

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 473; AIR 1969 SC 215; [1968] 3 S.C.R. 789

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Commissions of Inquiry Act, 1952, s. 3 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Govt. notification - Validity- Appeals were brought against common judgment of HC - By these applications petitioners prayed for appropriate direction or order u/art. 226 of Constitution for quashing and setting aside notification No. 813-EC, dt. 26-10-1967, issued by Govt. in exercise of powers conferred on it by s. 3 of the Act and for other reliefs - Schedule to Notification gave names of 15 persons against whom inquiry was to be made - Petitioners were originally impleaded as opposite parties Nos. 5 to 9. - BP filed application to be impleaded as opposite party - As other parties had no objection he was also impleaded as opposite party No. 10 - Rules were issued and except opposite parties Nos. 3 and 6 other opposite parties showed cause - HC dismissed applications, holding that notification of State Govt. dated 26-10- 1967 appointing Commission of Inquiry was legal and valid - Hence, instant appeals - Whether order passed by HC was sustainable - Held, object to set up Commission of Inquiry in instant case was to take appropriate legislative or administrative measures for maintaining high standards of public conduct and purity of political administration in State - It followed that notification of Sate Govt falls within ambit of s. 3 of the Act and should be held to be legally valid and intra vires - Court further opined that dominant purpose of setting up Commission of Inquiry was to promote measures for maintaining purity and integrity of administration in political life of State and not 'character assassination' of BP and BM and their group - Therefore, impugned notification of Sate Govt. dt. 26-10-1967 was legally valid - There was no factual enquiry into allegations - It was also not possible to accept argument that present inquiry was in relation to very matters which were subject-matter of civil suits and of first appeal - Appeals dismissed.
