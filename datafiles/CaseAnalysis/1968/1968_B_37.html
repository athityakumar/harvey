 Municipal Corporation Of Delhi v Birla Cotton, Spinning And Weaving Mills, Delhiand Another

Supreme Court of India

23 February 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, S.M. Sikri, C.A. Vaidyialingam, J.C. Shah, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 391; AIR 1968 SC 1232; [1968] 3 S.C.R. 251

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Municipalities & Local Governments; Service; Socio-Economic

Keywords: Abuse Of Power

Summary: Municipalities & Local Governments - Delhi Municipal Corporation Act, 1957, ss. 109, 113(2), 150 and 150(1) - Essential Supplies (Temporary Powers) Act, 1946 - Municipal Corporation (Validation of Electricity Tax) Act, 1966 - Slum Areas (Improvement And Clearance) Act, 1956 - Delhi Laws Act, 1951 - Questions relating to constitutionality of delegation of 'taxing powers to municipal corporations and effect of Validation Act, passed by Parliament, in connection with tax on consumption or sale of electricity levied by Municipal Corporation of Delhi - Whether s. 150 of Act suffers from vice of excessive delegation? - Essential legislative function consists of determination of legislative policy and its formulation as a binding rule of conduct and cannot be delegated by legislature - Nor is there any unlimited right of delegation inherent in legislative power itself - Where legislative policy is enunciated with sufficient clearness or a standard is laid down, courts should not interfere - What guidance should be given and to what extent and whether guidance has been given in a particular case at all depends on a consideration of provisions of particular Act with which Court has to deal including its preamble - Nature of body to which delegation is made is also a factor to be taken into consideration in determining whether there is sufficient guidance in matter of delegation - S. 150 itself says that maximum rates fixed by Corporation at its meeting by a resolution have to be submitted to Govt. for its sanction and without such sanction there can be no imposition of tax - Legislature has made Govt. watch-dog to control actions of Corporation in matter of fixing rates and other incidents of taxes and that is also a check to see that reasonable rates are fixed by Corporation when it proceeds to impose taxes u/s. 150 - Legislature cannot in circumstances be said to have abdicated its power for it has indicated taxes which Corporation can impose - Considering constitution and set up of municipal corporation, its need for finance to carry out functions entrusted to it, its elective character, its responsibility to electors, safe guards and controls provided in Act, procedural and otherwise, it is difficult to hold that power of taxation conferred on it is either uncanalised, arbitrary or without guidance or policy - Further held, Municipal Corporation was legally invested with power to levy electricity tax - There is no flaw in conferral of power on Municipal Corporation by ss. 113 and 150 of Act and resolutions passed thereunder - Tax was validly imposed and appeal in relation to it must be allowed - Appeals allowed.
