 Statesman Private Limited v H. R. Deb and Others

Supreme Court of India

02 April 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, K.S. Hegde, A.N. Grover, C.A. Vaidyialingam, Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 141; AIR 1968 SC 1495; 1969 (18) FLR 101; 1968 LabIC 1526; [1968] 3 S.C.R. 614

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Service

Keywords: Industrial Disputes (Appellate Tribunal) Act, 1950

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Industrial Disputes (Appellate Tribunal) Act, 1950 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 7(3) (d), 7A, 7B, 8 and 9 - Whether appointment can be question in quo warranto proceedings? - Held, persons employed on multifarious duties and in addition performing some judicial functions, may not truly answer requirement of s. 7 and it may be open in a quo warranto proceeding to question their appointment on ground that they do not hold essentially a judicial office because they primarily perform other functions - For it cannot be denied that expression 'holding a judicial office' signifies more than discharge of judicial functions while holding some other office phrase postulates that there is an office and that that office is primarily judicial office means a fixed position for performance of duties - In this case distinction was unsubstantial because Magistrate was holding a fixed position for nineteen years and performing functions primarily of a judicial character - Appeal dismissed.
