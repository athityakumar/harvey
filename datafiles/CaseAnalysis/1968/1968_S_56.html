P. D. Sharma v State Bank of India

Supreme Court of India

07 February 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, G.K. Mitter

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 233; AIR 1968 SC 985; 1968 (17) FLR 153; 1968 LabIC 1223; 1969 (1) LLJ 513; 1968 (2) MLJ(SC) 134; [1968] 3 S.C.R. 91

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: National Tribunal

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Bombay Industrial Disputes Act, 1938 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 13, 132, 133, 136 and 226 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Uttar Pradesh Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 33 and 33B - Whether order of High Court not having been appealed against same, has become final and therefore it would be inappropriate to appeal u/art. 136? - Held, in an appeal u/art. 136, Court can go into questions of facts as well as law whereas HC in writ petition could have only considered questions which would have been strictly relevant in an application for a writ - (B) Whether Labour Court was right in its conclusion that it was not competent to deal with an application u/s. 33(3) of Industrial Disputes Act, 1947? - Held, limitation placed on respondent's rights by s. 33(3) disappeared moment industrial dispute was decided, therefore Tribunal had no competence to consider application made by respondent after industrial dispute was decided - Appeal dismissed.
