Sailendranath Bose v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

26 March 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, G.K. Mitter

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 41; AIR 1968 SC 1292; 1968 CRLJ 1484; 1968 LabIC 1412; 1969 (1) LLJ 549; [1968] 3 S.C.R. 563

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 161 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947, ss. 5(2), 5(1)(d), 6 and 6(1) - (A) Investigation - Permission - Held, an investigation is one and indivisible 'Investigation' includes all proceedings under CrPC, 1973 for collection of evidence conducted by a police officer or any person (other than a Magistrate) who is authorised by a Magistrate in this behalf - Permission u/s. 5-A is a permission to investigate case - Laying trap is a part of investigation - Illegality of investigation does not vitiate jurisdiction of Court for trial - Prejudice has to be shown - (B) Onus u/s. 4 of Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947 - Presumption - Held, no - Word - 'Gratification' - Meaning - It is not necessary that before presumption arises prosecution must prove that money was paid as a bribe - U/s. 4(1), court is bound to draw presumption mentioned therein - Presumption in question will hold good unless accused proves contrary - (C) Sanction granted u/s. 6 of Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947 - Held, court can validly take cognizance of any of offences mentioned in s. 6(1) of Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947 without previous sanction of authority competent to remove from office accused - Without a valid sanction, court had no jurisdiction to try case - Appeal allowed.
