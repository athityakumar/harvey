 Virudhunagar Steel Rolling Mills Limited v Government of Madras

Supreme Court of India

10 January 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, C.A. Vaidyialingam, R.S. Bachawat, G.K. Mitter, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 310; AIR 1968 SC 1196; [1968] 38 Comp Cas 928; [1968] 70 ITR 726; 1968 (2) MLJ(SC) 92; [1968] 2 S.C.R. 740

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: CPC, 1908, s. 11 - Constitution, arts. 14. 32 and 226 - Madras Electricity (Taxation on Consumption) Act, 1962, s. 2 - Practice and Procedure - Res judicata - Writ petition - Dismissal u/art. 226 on merits by a speaking order - Whether a petition u/art. 32 can be entertatined - Whether the provisions of s. 2 of 1962 Act are discriminatory in nature -Held, bars a petition u/art. 32 even if no notice was issued to other side before such dismissal - Provision not discrininatory in the light of art. 14 - Petition dismissed.
