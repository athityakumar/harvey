Arbind Kumar Singh v Nand Kishore Prasad and Others

Supreme Court of India

26 February 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, G.K. Mitter

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 223; AIR 1968 SC 1227; [1968] 3 S.C.R. 322

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Constitution - Motor Vehicles Act 1939, ss. 47(1) and 64A - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 133 and 226 - (A) Whether High Court is competent to grant certificate u/art. 133? - Held, yes - Art. 133 not only discards distinction between appellate and original jurisdiction but deliberately uses words which are as wide as language can make them - Intention is not only to include all judgments, decrees and orders passed in exercise of appellate and ordinary original civil jurisdiction but also to make language wide enough to cover other jurisdictions under which civil rights would come for decision - (B) Whether conditions under which Regional Transport Authority refused to grant a stage carriage permit is justified? - Held, HC on a consideration of evidence had come to conclusion that claim made by appellant that document was not disclosed at hearing before Minister of Transport, and Minister acted upon that document without informing appellant cannot be accepted - There is no bar against an appellate or revising authority taking into consideration additional evidence brought on record, if authority requires additional evidence to be brought on record or allows it to be brought on record to do complete justice between parties - Appeal dismissed.
