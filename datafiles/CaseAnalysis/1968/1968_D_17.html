Delhi Cloth and General Mills Company, Limited, and Others v Its Workmen and Others

Supreme Court of India

27 September 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 17; AIR 1970 SC 919; 1970 (20) FLR 176; 1969 (2) LLJ 755; [1969] 2 S.C.R. 307

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Fixation of gratuity scheme - Award - Error - Two workmen Unions in Delhi region pressed for unit-wise scheme of gratuity - Tribunal entered upon reference in respect of fixation of gratuity scheme - Tribunal by its award framed two schemes relating to payment of gratuity to workmen employed in four textile units in Delhi region - Employers and workmen were dissatisfied with schemes and filed appeals challenging certain provisions of schemes - Court assumed average basic wage of workmen and uniform basic wage levels prevalence - While broadly supporting award of Tribunal workmen claimed certain modifications and that award was to be made operative from date of reference to Tribunal - Petitioners urged that there was settlement between respondents and their management - Parties had submitted application before Tribunal, admitted that 'overall settlement', but changed their mind subsequently - Hence, instant appeal - Whether Tribunal was incompetent to make award that framing scheme for payment of gratuity at rates of gratuity unit-wise - Held, unit-wise approach in framing gratuity scheme for four units was appropriate, and on terms of reference plea of employers to fix age of superannuation was beyond scope of reference - Financial condition of petitioners justified imposition of gratuity schemes from date on which award became operative - Settlement between parties had not barred jurisdiction of Tribunal to make scheme of gratuity payable to any respondent's - Tribunal erred in relating gratuity awardable to respondents to consolidated wage - Minimum period for qualifying for voluntary retirement was reduced to 10 years and one months basic wage in case of two appellants and 21 days' basic wage in case others for each completed year of service should be paid but not exceeding 20 months wages in aggregate - Respondents dismissed or discharged from service for misconduct were not entitled to gratuity if guilty conduct involved acts of violence against management or other employees, or riotous or disorderly behaviour in or near place of employment - No modification was made with regard to Badli respondents - Award was not modified with regard to date of operation of award - Respondents of two appellants who commenced service and continued to serve till 1-1-1964, and thereafter were entitled to elect time when gratuity became due to claim gratuity either on scheme in force under Employees Benefit Fund Trust of employers or under this award - Some incidental changes were made to streamline scheme - Remaining clauses stood unaffected except that for words 'within six months from date of publication of this Award' words 'within six months from date of this judgment" were substituted in relation to two petitioners, and modified - There was no order as to costs in appeals - Order accordingly.
