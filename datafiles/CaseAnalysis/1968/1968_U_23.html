J.K. Steel Limited v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

18 October 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri, R.S. Bachawat, K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 360; AIR 1970 SC 1173; 1978 (2) E.L.T. 355; [1969] 2 S.C.R. 481

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - Indirect Tax - Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Finance (No. 2) Act 1962 - Indian Tariff (Amendment) Act, 1963 - Tariff Act, 1934 - Central Excise Rules, 1944 - Assessee manufactured iron and steel products, and manufactured wires out of steel rods, which had been imported by it prior to 24-4-1962 - Excise duty was being levied on the wires - Hence, the present appeal - Whether the wires were liable to be paid duty for - Held, that the wires which were the subject matter of the levy impugned in this case were not liable to pay the duty in dispute in this case - For the purpose of implementing the scheme under the 1962 Act, that excise duty was leviable at the rate mentioned in column 3 of Item 26AA of the 1962 Act, on pig iron or steel ingot used in the production of the articles on which duty under entry 26AA, of the 1962 Act, was sought to be levied but to the extent, any excise duty or countervailing custom duty had been paid on any of the material used in the manufacture of any of that article, the same was exempt - Appeal dismissed.
