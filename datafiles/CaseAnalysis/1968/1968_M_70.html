Municipal Corporation, Indore v Shri K.N. Palshikar, Indore

Supreme Court of India

06 September 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri, R.S. Bachawat, K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 206; AIR 1969 SC 579; [1969] 2 S.C.R. 13

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 23(2), 24 and 25 - Madhya Bharat Municipal Corporation Act 1956, ss. 387(3) and 392 - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, s. 115 - (A) Withdrawal from acquisition proceedings - Held, no provision in the Act for enabling Corporation to withdraw from acquisition proceedings - There is automatic vesting of land in Corporation u/s. 305 once requisite conditions are satisfied - (B) Payment of compensation and solatium - Held, ss. 23, 24 and 25 of Land Acquisition Act, 1894 constitute a code laying down principles on which District Court is to act in arriving at compensation to be paid, and it is quite impossible to leave out of that code sub-s. (2) of s. 23 - Truth is that sections determine basis on which value of land is to be ascertained on compulsory purchase and allowance of fifteen per cent must be set off against matters disallowed u/s. 24 - Those provisions in Land Acquisition Act, 1894 are contained in a chapter entitled 'Reference to Court and Procedure thereon' they must be treated as applicable to proceedings in District Court u/s. 198 of Bombay Municipal Boroughs Act, 1925 - Held, Addl. District Judge was right in awarding 15 per cent solatium - (C) Whether amount of compensation is excessive? - Held, High Court could not, in a revision u/s. 392, go into questions of fact and determine amount of compensation, and HC was right in declining to deal with this question - It is not necessary to determine whether powers of revision u/s. 392 are same as u/s. 115, CPC, 1908 because even if powers u/s. 392 of Act are wider than that u/s. 115, CPC, 1908 they do not extend to determining questions fact - Appeals dismissed.
