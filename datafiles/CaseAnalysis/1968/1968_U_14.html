 Som Datt Datta v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

20 September 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, G.K. Mitter, A.N. Grover, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 187; AIR 1969 SC 414; 1969 CRLJ 663; [1969] 2 S.C.R. 177

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Defence & Security Forces; Indirect Tax; Service

Keywords: Defence Service, Court Martial, Attorney-General

Summary: Criminal - Court Martial - Army Act, 1950, ss. 50, 121, 125, 126, 164 and 165 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 32 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 149 and 304 - Scuffle between army jawans - Petitioner and other co-accused during a scuffle committed murder of deceased by stabbing him - Petitioner was tried and convicted by Court Martial - Present petition u/art. 32 of Constitution of India, 1950 that Court Martial has no jurisdiction to convict - Whether Court Martial had jurisdiction to try and convict of offences u/s. 304 and 149, IPC, 1860? - Held, yes - Legal position is that when an offence is for first time created by Army Act, it would be exclusively triable by a Court-martial, but where a civil offence is also an offence, both an ordinary criminal court as well as a Court-martial would have jurisdiction to try person committing offence - No case made out for issue of writ - (B) Whether Chief of Army Staff confirming the proceedings of Court-Martial u/s. 164 of the Army Act, 1950 was illegal since no reason has been given in support of the order by the Chief of the Army Staff? - Held, there is no express obligation imposed by s. 164 or by s. 165 of the Army Act, 1950 on the confirming authority or upon Central Govt. to give reasons in support of its decision to confirm the proceedings of the Court Martial - It cannot be said that there is a general principle or any rule of natural justice that a statutory tribunal should always and in every case give reasons in support of its decision - Such orders cannot, therefore, be held to be illegal for not giving any reasons for confirming the orders of Court Martial - Petition dismissed.
