Assistant Controller of Estate-Duty, Hyderabad v Nawab Sir Mir Osman Ali Khan Bahadur, H. E. H. The Nizam of Hyderabad, and Others

Supreme Court of India

23 August 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Grover, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 295; [1969] 72 ITR 376

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Estate, Estate Duty, Estate Duty Act, 1953

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Estate Duty Act, 1953, ss. 59, 34(1)(b), 33B, 35 - Valuation of estate - Correctness - Applicability - Stepsister of Nizam(Begum), to whom annuity had to be given was died and property passing on her death became, liable to estate duty under the Act - Assistant Controller of Estate Duty after making necessary calculations, net market value of estate of deceased was assessed on which demand was created and Assistant Controller issued notice - Trustees made appeal before Central Board of Revenue disputing correctness of valuation of made by Assistant Controller where valuation made by Assistant Controller was conformed - Respondents filed petition in HC challenging validity and legality of notice issued u/s. 59 of the Act was allowed and held that s. 59 of the Act was introduced by Amendment Act and it could not be made applicable to assessments which had become final before date on which amendment Act came into force - Appellant made appeal which was disposed of by Division Bench - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether order passed by HC regarding s.59 of the Act was justified - Held, it was contended that ss. 33B, 35 of the Act conferred ample powers on specified authorities to revise Income-tax Officer's orders and to rectify mistakes respectively and thus it would be legitimate to construe word 'information' in s. 34(1)(b) of the Act strictly and to confine it to information in regard to facts or particulars - However, if word ' information ' in its plain grammatical meaning included information as to facts as well as information as to state of law, it would be unreasonable to limit to information as to facts on extraneous consideration that some cases of assessment which needed to be revised or rectified on ground of mistake of law should conceivably be covered by ss.33B, 35 of the Act - Therefore, matter would have to go back for decision of that question - Hence, order passed by HC was set aside - Appeal allowed.
