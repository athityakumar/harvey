 Sampat Prakash v State of Jammu and Kashmir and Another

Supreme Court of India

10 October 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

Vishishtha Bhargava, G.K. Mitter, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.M. Shelat, C.A. Vaidyialingam

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 192; AIR 1970 SC 1118; 1969 CRLJ 1555; 1970 LabIC 873; [1969] 2 S.C.R. 365

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 13, 22, 32, 35, 77, 367, 370, 372 and 394 - General Clauses Act, 1897, s. 21 - Jammu and Kashmir Preventive Detention Act, 1964, s. 3 - Constitution (Application to Jammu and Kashmir) Amendment Order, 1964 - Whether art. 35(c) of Constitution of India, 1950, as initially introduced by Constitution (Application to Jammu and Kashmir) Order, 1964, had given protection to any law relating to preventive detention in Jammu and Kashmir against invalidity on ground of infringement of any of fundamental rights guaranteed by Part III of Constitution of India, 1950 for a limited period of five years only? - Held, as a result of art. 35(c), applicability of provisions of Part III for purpose of judging validity of a law relating to preventive detention made by State Legislature was postponed for a period of five years, during which law could not be declared void and validity of Act cannot be challenged on ground that any of provisions of Act are inconsistent with art. 22 of Constitution of India, 1950 - Petition disposed of.
