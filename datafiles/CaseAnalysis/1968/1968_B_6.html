Shashibhushan Prasad Misra (Dead) and Another v Babuaji Rai (Dead) By His Legal Representatives and Others

Supreme Court of India

27 November 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Bachawat, S.M. Sikri, K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 470; AIR 1970 SC 809; [1969] 2 S.C.R. 971

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Plea of adverse possession - Whether plea of adverse possession can be raised in appellate stage? - Held, no - Plea for acquisition of title by adverse possession cannot be raised for first time at appellate stage - Plaintiffs not able to prove possession of disputed land in favour of deity as alleged in plaint - Relied on, Munni Bibi v. Trilokinath, (AIR 1931 PC 114) - No question of abatement of appeal as no party to appeal has died - Appeal dismissed.
