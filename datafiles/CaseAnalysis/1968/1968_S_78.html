 Nishi Kant Jha v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

02 December 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

G.K. Mitter, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, A.N. Grover, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 453; (1969) 1 SCC 347; AIR 1969 SC 422; 1969 ALJ 638; 1969 CRLJ 671; [1969] 2 S.C.R. 1033

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Evidence Act - Appreciation of Evidence - Extra judicial confession - Whether statement of appellant recorded by a village Mukhiya before he was handed over to police is admissible in evidence?; and if so, whether court could reject a part thereof and rely on remainder along with other evidence adduced to hold him guilty of an offence he was charged with? - Held, statement of accused made before Mukhiiya was acceptable; it had to be corroborated with other evidence to come to conclusion - Further held, where there is other evidence, a portion of confession may in light of that evidence be rejected while acting upon remainder with other evidence and where there is no other evidence and exculpatory element is not inherently incredible, element - Conviction upheld - Appeal dismissed.
