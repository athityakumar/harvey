 Tilokchand Motichand and Others v H. B. Munshi and Another

Supreme Court of India

22 November 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, S.M. Sikri, R.S. Bachawat, G.K. Mitter, K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 75; (1969) 1 SCC 110; AIR 1970 SC 898; [1969] 2 S.C.R. 824; 1970 (25) STC 289

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Limitation Act, 1939, Bombay City Land Revenue Act, 1876

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Bombay Sales Tax Act, 1953, s. 21(4) - Limitation Act, 1963, ss. 30(a) and 113 - Limitation Act, 1908, ss. 96, 17(1)(c) and 120, art. 62 - Indian Contract Act, 1872, s. 72 - Bombay Sales Tax Act, 1946, s. 12(A)(4) - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 226, 14, 19 and 32 - (A) Whether claim be barred by limitation if it were subject-matter of a suit in February, 1968? - Held, a suit for recovery of money instituted in February, 1968, would be barred by limitation If petitioners could claim relief on ground of mistake suit would be governed by art. 96 of Indian Limitation Act, 1908, and time would begin to run from date when mistake becomes known to plaintiff - Art. 96 applied to a suit for recovery of money paid under a mistake of law - (B) Whether in circumstances court should give relief in a writ petition u/art. 32 of Constitution? - Held, no period of limitation is prescribed for such a petition - Right to move Supreme Court for enforcement of fundamental right is guaranteed by art. 32 - Writ u/art. 32 issues, as a matter of course, if a breach of a fundamental right is established - In giving relief u/art. 32 court must ignore and trample on foot all laws of procedure, evidence, limitation, res judicata and like - U/art. 145(1)(c) rules may be framed for regulating practice and procedure in proceedings u/art. 32 - Petition dismissed.
