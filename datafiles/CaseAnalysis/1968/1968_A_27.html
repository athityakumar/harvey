 Assistant Collector of Customs v L. R. Malwani

Supreme Court of India

16 October 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, G.K. Mitter, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, J.C. Shah, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 1; AIR 1970 SC 962; 1970 (72) BomLR 782; 1970 CRLJ 885; 1999 (110) E.L.T. 317; [1969] 2 S.C.R. 438

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 403 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 20(2) - Smuggling of foreign goods - Summoning documents - Jurisdiction of HC - Appellant's case was that respondent along with others had conspired at Bombay and other places in beginning of October 1959 and thereby smuggled several items of foreign goods in years 1959 and 1960 - Enquiry was held by Customs authorities, smuggled goods were seized and confiscation was ordered - Penalty was imposed - Thereafter after obtaining required sanction of Govt., appellant filed complaint against five persons including respondent - Before commencement of enquiry in complaint, respondent filed application - Respondent and others wanted to invoke from art. 20(2) of Constitution, rule of autrefois acquit embodied in s. 403 of CrPC - Respondent's application before Magistrate lacked substance in pleas advanced in that application and accordingly was dismissed - In revision, HC agreed with trial Magistrate, negatived all but one of respondent's contentions - Division Bench had not agreed that there was no need, at that stage to summon statements of witnesses recorded, it directed Magistrate to summon statements, and further directed to see that copies of statements were made available to respondent before commencement of enquiry in case - Appellant filed appeal against correctness of decision of Bombay HC as it went against them and respondents challenged decision in other respects - Hence, instant appeals - Whether decision of Bombay HC that that went against appellants was correct and respondents contentions - Held, art. 20(2) of Constitution had no direct bearing on question at issue - It was not possible to hold that proceeding before Collector of Customs was prosecution for offence - To get benefit of s. 403 of CrPC or art. 20(2) of Constitution, it was necessary for respondent to establish that he was tried by 'court of competent jurisdiction' for offence and he was convicted or acquitted of that offence and said conviction or acquittal was in force - Proceeding before Collector of Customs was not criminal trial, thus decision of Collector was not verdict of acquittal - Contention that prosecution should be quashed on ground that there was delay in instituting complaint lacked substance - HC was not justified in interfering with discretion of Magistrate as concerned summoning of documents - Magistrate's reasons for his order were good reasons - HC had not concluded that documents in question, if not produced in court were likely to be destroyed or tampered with or were not likely to be made available when required, therefore it has proceeded on erroneous basis that accused was deprived of fair trial unless they were supplied with copies of statements even before enquiry commenced - HC was not empowered to interfere with discretion conferred on trial courts in matter of summoning documents - Unnecessary impeded progress of eases and resulted in waste of public money and time as had happened in instant case - Order of Magistrate was restored - Order accordingly.
