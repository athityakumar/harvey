Commissioner of Sales Tax, Madhya Pradesh, Indore v Madhya Pradesh Electricity Board, Jabalpur

Supreme Court of India

26 November 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Grover, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 351; (1969) 1 SCC 200; AIR 1970 SC 732; [1969] 2 S.C.R. 939; 1970 (25) STC 188

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Summary: M.P. General Sales Tax Act, 1959, ss. 2(d) and 2(g) - Indian Electricity Act, 1910

Sales Tax - Whether electricity is 'Goods' and Electricity Board is 'dealer' u/ss. 2(d) and 2(g) - New plea for the first time u/art. 136 raising objection as to proper execution of contract and its admissibility in evidence - Sale of goods and works contract distinguished in the light of s. 2(n) of 1959 Act - Held, electric energy is goods for purposes of sales tax and statutory body like Electricity Board is 'dealer' in respect of its principal activity of sale and supply of electricity - Objection not allowed by SC - Appeal Partly Allowed.
