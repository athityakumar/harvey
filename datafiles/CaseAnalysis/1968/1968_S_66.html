State of Andhra Pradesh and Another v P. Sagar

Supreme Court of India

27 March 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, G.K. Mitter

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 149; AIR 1968 SC 1379; 1968 CRLJ 1412; 1968 (2) MLJ(SC) 114; [1968] 3 S.C.R. 595

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Backward Classes

Summary: Constitution - Education - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 15(4), 16, 25, 29, 32, 46 and 226 - Whether order passed by High Court declaring invalid reservation for backward classes u/rr. 4A and 5A of Telangana and Andhra Rules was legaly correct? - When a dispute is raised before a Court that a particular law which is inconsistent with Guarantee against discrimination is valid on plea that it is permitted u/cl. (4) of art. 15, assertion by State that officers of State had taken into consideration criteria which had been adopted by Courts for determining who socially and educationally backward classes of Society are, or that authorities had acted in good faith in, determining socially and educationally, backward classes of citizens, would not be sufficient to sustain validity of claim - Courts are invested with power to determine validity of law which infringes fundamental rights of citizens and others and when a question arises whether a law which prima facie infringes a guaranteed fundamental right is within an exception, validity of that law has to be determined by Courts on materials placed before them - Held, yes - No materials at all were placed on record to enable HC to decide whether criteria laid down by Court for determining that list prepared by Govt. conformed to requirements of cl.(4) of art. 15 were followed - Further held, test of validity of a law alleged to infringe fundamental rights of a citizen or any act done in execution of that law lies not in belief of maker of law or of person executing law, but in demonstration by evidence and argument before Courts that guaranteed right is not infringed - Appeal dismissed.
