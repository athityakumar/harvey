Nair Service Society Limited v Rev. Father K. C. Alexander and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 February 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, S.M. Sikri, K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 395; AIR 1968 SC 1165; 1968 KerLJ 176; 1968 KLT 182; [1968] 3 S.C.R. 163

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 9 - Specific Relief Act, 1877, ss. 8 and 9 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 110 - High Court reversed decree of Trial Judge and decreed it against Society and its Manager ordering possession of entire suit lands with mesne profits past and future, and compensation for any waste - Whether after expiry of 6 months a regular suit based on prior possession without proof of title was maintainable? - Held, if s. 9 of Specific Relief Act, 1877 is utilised plaintiff need not prove title and title of defendant does not avail him, when period of 6 months has passed questions of title can be raised by defendant and if he does so plaintiff must establish a better title or fail - Appeal partly allowed.
