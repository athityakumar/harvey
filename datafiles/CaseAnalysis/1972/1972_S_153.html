 Kunjukutty Sahib Etc. Etc. v State Of Kerala & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

26 April 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

I.D. Dua, Hans Raj Khanna, S.M. Sikri, J.M. Shelat, G.K. Mitter

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 375; (1972) 2 SCC 364; AIR 1972 SC 2097; 1972 KLT 353; [1973] 1 S.C.R. 326

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Kerala Land Reforms Act, 1964, ss. 73, 45 A and 85(1), Explanation 85(1), 72 and 80 A to 80 G - Kerala Land Reforms (Amendment) Act, 1969 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 19(1)(f), 31 A, 31 A(1) and second proviso - (A) Amendment of Kerala Land Reforms Act, 1964 by 1969 Act - Constitutionality of following provisions : (1) Of s. 73 providing for discharge of arrears of rent - (2) Of s. 45 A providing that rent appropriated towards arrears of rent for period prior to 01-05-1966 shall be adjusted towards rent accrued due for period after 01-05-1966 (3) Of s. 85(1), Explanation (4) of s. 85(1) - Amended provision reducing ceiling limit and requiting surrender of excess land without payment of compensation at market value - (5) Of ss. 80 A to 80 G - Provision enabling Kudikidappukaran to purchase land mainly for agricultural purposes - Use of part of land for erecting homestead etc. would not deviate from general agricultural purpose - (6) Of s. 72 - Extinguishment or modification of landlord's rights if it is in relation to agrarian reforms is protected by art. 31 A of Constitution - (B) Precedents - Where a number of cases involving same point are pending consideration - Duty of High Court - (A) Held, it is not entitled to protection of art. 31 A of Constitution and is violative of art. 19(1)(f) - Is violative of art. 19 (1)(f) and is not entitled to protection of art. 31 A - Provision is void as offending second proviso to art. 31 A - It is not violative of constitutional inhibition in second proviso to art. 31 A(1) - It is protected by art. 31 A - Section is not liable to be struck down on this ground - (B) HC should refrain from giving opinion which is not necessary for giving relief to parties approaching it especially where a number of cases involving same point are pending consideration - Appeals dismissed.
