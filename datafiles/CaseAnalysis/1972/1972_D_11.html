Commissioner of Income Tax (Central) Calcutta v Daulat Ram Rawatmull

Supreme Court of India

12 September 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

Hans Raj Khanna

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 230; (1973) 3 SCC 133; 1972 CTR 411; 1973 (2) ITJ 1; [1973] 87 ITR 349; [1973] 2 S.C.R. 184; 1972 TAXLR 1174

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Practice & Procedure - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922 - Whether Tribunal had sufficient material to hold that sum involved was a concealed income of respondent firm? - Sum involved was in form of fixed deposit held in some other name - Held, a finding of fact by tribunal can be reviewed only on ground that there is no evidence to support it or that it is perverse - Facts available to support conclusion of tribunal was not relevant to issue - Appeal dismissed.
