Shivaji Genu Mohite v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

20 September 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Shelat, S.N. Dwivedi, Y.V. Chandrachud

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 480; (1973) SCC (Cr) 214; (1973) 3 SCC 219; AIR 1973 SC 55; 1973 CRLJ 159; 1973 CRLR 268; 1973 UJ 163

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Criminal Procedure Code, 1898, s. 423 - Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 5 and 118 - Constitution of India, 1950 art. 134 - (A) Appeal against acquittal - Powers of High Court - (B) Appreciation of evidence in murder case - (1) Motive - Adequacy - (2) Absence of motive - (3) Single witness - Evidence of - Witness not only a teenagar but also only eye-witness - (4) Ground that witness being village Ramoshi would be under police influence - (C) Constitution - Order of acquittal - Setting aside of, by HighCourt in appeal - Interference by Supreme Court - (A) Held, under CrPC, 1898, HC has full power to review at large evidence upon which an order of acquittal has been passed by Sessions Court and to reach conclusion that upon that evidence, order of acquittal should be reversed - (B) Refusal to transfer land and possibility of land going out of reach of accused could not be regarded as an inadequate motive - Evidence as to motive would, no doubt, go a long way in cases wholly dependent on circumstantial evidence, but does not mean that if motive is not established, evidence of an eye-witness is rendered untrustworthy - Held, her evidence had to be scrutinised with care and caution - Held this could hardly be a justifiable ground for discarding his testimony - (C) Only such examination of evidence would ordinarily be necessary as is needed to see that HC approached question properly and applied principles correctly - Appeal dismissed.
