Rai Bahadur Mohan Singh Oberoi v Commissioner of Income Tax, West Bengal

Supreme Court of India

29 November 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

Hans Raj Khanna, K.S. Hegde, P. Jaganmohan Reddy

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 289; (1973) 3 SCC 491; AIR 1973 SC 651; 1973 (2) CTR 112; [1973] 88 ITR 53; [1973] 1 S.C.R. 1057

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Finding of fact, Benami Transaction, Fiduciary Relations

Summary: Income Tax and Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 16 - Grossing up of dividends - Benami Transaction - Finding that the assessee is the real owner of the shares and they have been purchased benami in name of his wife and two sons - Held, it will be presumed that the ownership of the shares continues to remain vested in the assessee, unless it is shown that because of some subsequent event, he has ceased to be the owner of the shares - Appeals dismissed.
