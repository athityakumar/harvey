State of Assam v Jilkadar Ali

Supreme Court of India

18 July 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Shelat, Hans Raj Khanna

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 159; (1972) 2 SCC 320; (1972) SCC (Cr) 662; AIR 1972 SC 2166; 1972 CRLJ 1441; [1973] 1 S.C.R. 685

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Foreigners; International

Summary: Criminal - Foreigners - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 7 - Citizenship Act, 1955 - Foreigners Act, 1946, ss. 2 and 14 - At time of partition, respondent a Constable, opted for service in Pakistan - He overstayed in India - He was prosecuted u/s. 14 of Foreigners Act, 1946 and cl. 7 of Foreigner Order, 1948 - Whether prosecution of appellant was justified? - Held, yes - Person who had opted for and gone to Pakistan in 1947, coming to India on short visit on Pakistan passport and visa is a 'foreigner' - Every foreigner who enters into India on authority of visa has to indicate period during which he is authorised to remain in India - In case he wants to stay beyond that period, requisite permission has to be obtained and in absence of such extension he is liable to be deported from India - Respondent was foreigner even under definition of foreigner and by overstaying he violated Foreigners Order, 1948 - Appeal allowed.
