Workmen of Messrs Firestone Tyre and Rubber Company of India Private Limited and Others v Management and Others

Supreme Court of India

06 March 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

C.A. Vaidyialingam, I.D. Dua

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 315; (1973) 1 SCC 813; AIR 1973 SC 2; 1973 (1) LLJ 278

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Legislations Referred - Industrial Employment (Standing Orders) Act, 1946 Uttar Pradesh Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - (A) Whether s. 11A applied to industrial disputes which have already been referred to for adjudication and were pending an on 15-12-71? - Held that s. 11A was added by amendment act - S. 1(2) provides for its coming into operation on 8-12-1971 - S. 11A applies to such proceedings - S. 11A does not apply to disputes which had been referred prior to 15-12-1971 - (B) Jurisdiction of Labour Court in directing reinstatement? - Held that it has jurisdiction when (i) when there is want of good faith; (ii) when there is victimisation or unfair labour practice; (iii) when the management has been guilty of a basic error or violation of a principle of natural justice and; (iv) when on the materials the finding is completely baseless or perverse - Employer is bound to conduct inquiry before inquiry which begin with the supply of a specific chargesheet to the employee - Opportunity be given to the employer to prove his case before tribunal - (C) Matter on which tribunal may act? - Held that tribunal will take into account the entire evidence taken under domestic proceedings - It will also consider additional evidences submitted by the management - U/S. 11A, tribunal can modify the punishment - (D) Effect of an employer not holding an enquiry? - Held that tribunal would have to consider prima facie evidence of dismissal and find that whether the misconduct alleged is proved - The point about the exercise of managerial functions does not arise at all - (E) Tribunal cannot interfere if the inquiry proves misconduct - Tribunal can interfere in cases where the punishment is so harsh as to suggest victimisation - (F) Scope of s.11A of the IDA, 1947 act? - To invoke s. 11A, industrial dispute should have been referred to an Industrial Tribunal - Tribunal has to be satisfied that the order of discharge or dismissal was not justified - Tribunal will set aside the order in case it is not justified - The power to interfere with the punishment and alter the same has been now conferred on the tribunal by s. 11A (G) Industrial Employment (Standing Orders) Act, applies only to those industrial establishments which are covered by s. 1(3) - But the field of operation of the Act is much wider and it applies to employers who may have no standing orders at all - Appeal allowed in part.
