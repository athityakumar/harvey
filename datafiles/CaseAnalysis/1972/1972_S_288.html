Seshammal and Others v State of Tamil Nadu

Supreme Court of India

14 March 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

D.G. Palekar

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 607; (1972) 2 SCC 11; AIR 1972 SC 1586; 1973 (1) MLJ 58; 1973 (1) MLJ(SC) 58; [1972] 3 S.C.R. 815

Case Digest

Subject: Trusts & Associations

Summary: Trust & Associations - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 32, 25 and 26 - Tamil Nadu Hindu Religious and Charitable Endowments Act, 1959, ss. 28, 55 and 56 - Tamil Nadu Hindu Religious and Charitable Endowments (Amendment) Act, 1970 - Validity of - Secular usage and religious stage - Held, If it is a secular usage, legislation would be permissible u/art. 25(1)(a) and if it is a religious usage it would be permissible if it falls squarely under sub-section 25(1)(b) - If any rule is framed by Govt. which purports to interfere with rituals and ceremonies of temples same will be liable to be challenged by those who are interested in temple worship - Apprehensions now expressed by petitioners are groundless and premature - Petition dismissed.
