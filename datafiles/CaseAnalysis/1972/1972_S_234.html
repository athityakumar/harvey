Harbansingh Sardar Lenasingh and Others v State of Maharashtra and Others

Supreme Court of India

24 February 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

Hans Raj Khanna, P. Jaganmohan Reddy, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 622; (1972) 3 SCC 775; (1972) SCC (Cr) 843; AIR 1972 SC 1224; 1972 CRLJ 759; 2004 (177) E.L.T. 13

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Fema

Summary: Customs Act, 1962, ss. 107, 108 and 135 - Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 3 and 25 - Constitution of India,1950, art. 20(3) - Foreign Exchange Regulation Act, 1947, s. 23 - (A) Admissibility of statement recorded by Custom Officer - (B) Conviction u/s 135 of 1872 Act - Proof of guilt - Incriminating circumstances - Held, confessional statements recorded by an officer of Customs under Customs Act are admissible in evidence and are not hit by s.25 of 1872 Act or art. 20(3) of Constitution - Further held, that all these incriminating circumstances clearly pointed to guilt of appellant - Appeal dismissed.
