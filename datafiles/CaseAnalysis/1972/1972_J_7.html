Virendra Kumar Saklecha v Jagjiwan and Others

Supreme Court of India

22 March 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Ray, M. Hameedullah Beg, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 383; (1972) 1 SCC 826; AIR 1974 SC 1957; [1972] 3 S.C.R. 955

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Civil Procedure; Election; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Election - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, ss. 19 and 102 - Representation of the People Act, 1951, ss. 83, 87 and 123(4) - Corrupt practice - Appellant delivered speech at different places as part of election campaigning - Appellant held guilty by High Court u/s. 123(4) on ground of religion defined in s. 123(3) and considered speech of appellant very serious - HC accepted oral evidence of respondent but oral evidence of appellant was not accepted - Whether HC right to held appellant guilty of corrupt practices? - Held, respondent filed affidavit which does not disclose source of information in respect of speeches - Non-disclosure of information makes case of respondent weak - No single piece of documentary evidence to support case of respondent - Respondent gave an undertaking to this court to produce witness for cross-examination but failed to produce - Election petition is dismissed and order of HC set aside - Appeal allowed.
