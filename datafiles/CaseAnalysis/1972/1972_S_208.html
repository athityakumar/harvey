Chahat Khan v State of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

09 March 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Grover, M. Hameedullah Beg

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 613; (1972) 3 SCC 408; (1972) SCC (Cr) 558; AIR 1972 SC 2574; 1973 CRLJ 36; 1972 UJ 773

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 300, 302 and 325 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Murder - Whether appellant rightly held guilty of murder? - When a person causes an injury on a vital part of body, intention to kill can be attributed to him - Held, injury was on a region of head which was a vital part and according to medical evidence this injury proved fatal - No infirmity in conclusion of High Court - Appeal dismissed.
