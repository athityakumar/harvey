 Bennett Coleman and Company and Others v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

30 October 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Ray, S.M. Sikri, P. Jaganmohan Reddy, Kuttyil Kurien Mathew, M. Hameedullah Beg

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 337; (1972) 2 SCC 788; AIR 1973 SC 106; [1973] 2 S.C.R. 757

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Freedom Of Speech And Expression, Working Journalists (Conditions of Service) and Miscellaneous Provisions Act, 1955, Kerala Education Bill, 1957, Newsprint Control Order 1962, Daily Newspaper (Price and Page) Order, 1960, Press Council Act,1965

Summary: Constitution of India Act, 1950 - Daily Newspaper (Price and Page) Order, 1960 - Essential Commodities Act, 1955 - Essential Supplies (Temporary Powers) Act, 1946 - Govt. of India Act, 1935 - Newspaper (Price And Page) Act, 1956 - Arts. 14 and 19(1)(a) - Whether they are reasonable restrictions warranted by either art. 19(2) or art. 19(6) of Constitution? - Sub-clauses 3 and 3 A of 1962 - Newsprint Order are challenged in these petitions, infringement of art. 19(1)(a) of Constitution - Clauses 3 and 3 A of 1962 Newsprint Order are offending to art. 14 of Constitution, 1950 - Additional Solicitor General raised two pleas - First, was that petitioners were companies and therefore they could not invoke fundamental rights - Secondly, art. 358 of Constitution is a bar to any challenge by petitioners of violation of fundamental rights - This court in State Trading Corporation of India Ltd v. Commercial Tax Officer, Visakhapatnam and Tata Engineering and Locomotive Co. v. State of Bihar expressed that a corporation was not a citizen within meaning of art. 19, and, could not invoke that article - Art. 358 of Constitution was invoked to raise bar to maintainability of petition - Held - It must first have authority of law to support whether there could be reasonable restrictions on fundamental rights - Appeal allowed.
