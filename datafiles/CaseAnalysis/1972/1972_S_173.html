State of Assam v Mohan Chandra Kalita and Another

Supreme Court of India

08 August 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Jaganmohan Reddy, S.M. Sikri, A.N. Ray, Kuttyil Kurien Mathew

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 753; (1972) 4 SCC 11; AIR 1972 SC 2535

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Service - Respondent was a Sub Deputy Collector in employment of State Govt. of Assam and in that capacity was directed to distribute sums of money to agriculturists of Missamari as compensation for occupation of their lands by army authorities - Respondent had to travel from Dhekiajuli to Missamari to disburse payments and found it difficult to obtain conveyance to reach Missamari - One day villagers of Missamari who were to be paid compensation were waiting for respondent who however arrived in a school bus at about 12 noon - Though respondent made payments to some of them he could not pay all of them - It is in evidence that there was someone collecting arrears of land revenue, some other person was collecting pooja contribution and yet others were collecting amounts allegedly for Garibhara (carriage of Sub Divisional Officer) - There is also evidence to show that a person was collecting some fee allegedly on behalf of respondent - While these collections were being made Asstt District Magistrate visited Missamari and on complaint of villagers on their complaint that they were being made to pay Garibhara from out of compensation amounts paid to them, he asked two of them to get into his jeep and with them arrived at Missamari - Addl District Magistrate then made a report to Govt. on which a preliminary enquiry was held and a prima facie case being found against respondent, an enquiry was ordered - High Court quashed an order removing respondent from service on ground that there was no evidence to support charge against him - Held, it appears that because compensation amounts were being paid everyone was trying to collect whatever he could from those recipients - There was no need for respondent to collect any money for payment of taxi charges because he could recover those from Govt. and in fact he had recovered that amount from Govt. - There is no dispute that he did express his difficulty in finding a conveyance to come to Missamari as indeed he had to come there on that day in a school bus - He was not sure whether he could get a conveyance to come there next day and naturally he informed those concerned that if he cannot get any conveyance and come there they should go to Dhekiajuli - It is at that stage that they suggested that he should come in a taxi and they will pay for it, but this did not imply that S.D.C had consented to anyone collecting moneys for hire or much less permit them to pay for hire on his behalf - All this merely shows that suggestion that they should come to Dhekiajuli would seriously inconvenience them and they were even prepared to pay his taxi fare if he came to Missamari - Their earnest entreaties must have persuaded S.D.C to come there in a taxi - Beyond this, there is no evidence to show that he had wanted them to pay for his taxi or authorised them to collect money for it - Further held, HC was therefore right in quashing order of A D M Tezpur on ground that there was no evidence to sustain charge - Appeal dismissed.
