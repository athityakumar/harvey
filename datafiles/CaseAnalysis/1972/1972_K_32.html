Kishori Mohan Bera v State of West Bengal

Supreme Court of India

04 May 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Shelat, Hans Raj Khanna

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 551; (1972) 3 SCC 845; (1973) SCC (Cr) 30; AIR 1972 SC 1749; 1973 UJ 98

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Defence & Security Forces

Keywords: Preventive Detention Act, 1950

Summary: Criminal - Detention - Constitution of India, 1950 - Maintenance of Internal Security Act, 1971, s. 3 - Commission of acts prejudicial to maintenance of public order or security of state - Involvement in criminal acts - Assault on an individual - Detention - Challenged - S. 3 of Act empowers authorities specified therein to detain person on specific grounds laid down therein, namely, preventing person concerned from acting in a manner prejudicial to (i) Defence of India, relations of India with foreign powers or security of India, or (ii) Security of state or maintenance of public order, or (iii) Maintenance of supplies and services essential to community - Held, District Magistrate was not certain regarding grounds - Ground relates to an assault on an individual, undoubtedly with lethal and dangerous weapons, by petitioner and his associates - Such an act, as is expressed in ground cannot, however, be said to place public order in jeopardy, actual or likely, much less security of state - It is clearly an act which infringes law and order, but cannot be said, being in relation to a particular individual only, to endanger public order - This ground clearly was extraneous to any of heads, endangering which gives ground for invoking power under Act - That being so, in view of well settled position that an extraneous ground vitiates order since it is impossible to predicate whether without it requisite satisfaction could have been reached, impugned order cannot be upheld - Detention set aside - Petition allowed.
