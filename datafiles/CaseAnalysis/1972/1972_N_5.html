Union of India and Others v N.K. Private Limited and Another

Supreme Court of India

11 February 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Jaganmohan Reddy, K.S. Hegde, Kuttyil Kurien Mathew

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 213; (1973) 3 SCC 388; AIR 1972 SC 915; [1972] 3 S.C.R. 437

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Constitution

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Constitution of India, 1950 art. 299 - Arbitration Act, 1940, ss. 2(a)and 20 - (A) Effect of Non-execution of contract by proper authority - (B) Application by Co. u/s 20 of Arbitration Act - For reference of a dispute on basis of such a contract - Maintainability of application - Held, though words 'expressed' and 'executed' in art. 299 (1) might suggest that it should be by a deed or by a formal written contract, a binding contract by tender and acceptance can also come into existence; if acceptance is by a person duly authorised on this behalf by President of India, and a contract whether by a formal deed or otherwise by persons not authorised by President cannot be binding and is absolutely void - Further held, application is not maintainable - Appeal allowed.
