Nika Ram v State of Himachal Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

28 April 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

Hans Raj Khanna, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 150; (1972) 2 SCC 80; (1972) SCC (Cr) 635; AIR 1972 SC 2077; 1972 CRLJ 1317; [1973] 1 S.C.R. 428; 1972 UJ 932

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Specially Empowered, Code of Criminal Procedure 1898

Summary: Constitution - Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Code of Criminal Procedure,1898, s. 164 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872,s. 74 -(A) Whether a record of confession made u/s 164, CrPC,1898, by Magistrate second class not specially empowered in that behalf during investigation is admissible? - Held, as recording Magistrate was a second class magistrate not specially empowered in that behalf - Record was inadmissible in evidence at trial - Conviction by lower courts based on evidence inter alia on inadmissible record of confession - (B) Appeal by special leave against conviction for murder based on inadmissible record of confession - Whether liable to be interfered - Further held, Supreme Court will not interfere if other evidence on record is sufficient to sustain conviction after excluding inadmissible confession - (C) Circumstantial evidence - Sentence u/s 302, IPC, 1860 - Sentence of life imprisonment in view of extenuating circumstances - Appeal dismissed.
