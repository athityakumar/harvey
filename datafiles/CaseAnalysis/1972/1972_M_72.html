Minerals & Metals Trading Corporation Ofindia Ltd v Union Of India & Others

Supreme Court of India

24 August 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Grover, K.S. Hegde, D.G. Palekar

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 306; (1972) 2 SCC 620; AIR 1972 SC 2551; 1989 (25) ECR 278; 1983 (13) E.L.T. 1542; [1973] 1 S.C.R. 997; 1973 TAXLR 20

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax; Trade

Summary: Customs - Import Duty - Refund claimed - Denied - Challenged - Appellant had imported Wolfram Concentrate and entered into contract with 'X' for purchase of 200 metric tons - Customs authorities levied duty under item 87 of sch.I amounting to Rs.4,13,796-24 and appellant had paid said amount under protest - Later on appellant had claimed refund under item 28 or 70(7) of said sch. before Assistant Collector of Customs(Refund Section) which had passed orders on three consignments - Appeal was filed against said orders before Appellate Collector of Customs which was disposed of by holding that goods were not classifiable as ores and was not entitled to claim exemption - Aggrieved appellant filed revision before Govt. which refused to refund duty charged - Hence, instant appeals - Whether Wolfram Ore falls within item 26 which covers metallic ores of all sorts.

Held, wolfram ore which was imported by the appellants was never subjected to any process of roasting or treatment with chemicals to remove the impurities. Thus, wolfram ore concentrate containing 65% W03 which was of the merchantable quality was known commercially imported as ore. Therefore, goods imported by the appellants fell within item 26 of the Import Tariff and no duty was leviable on them. Hence, appellants were entitled to refund of the amounts which were paid by them by way of duty and impugned order passed by Govt. is set aside. Appeals allowed.

Ratio- In interpreting items in Taxing Statutes resort should be had not to the scientific or technical meaning but to the meaning attached to them by those dealing in them in their commercial sense.
