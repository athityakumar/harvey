Raza Textiles Limited v Income Tax Officer, Rampur

Supreme Court of India

22 September 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, I.D. Dua, P. Jaganmohan Reddy, Hans Raj Khanna

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 275; (1973) SCC (Tax) 327; (1973) 1 SCC 633; AIR 1973 SC 1362; 1973 (2) CTR 238; [1973] 87 ITR 539; 1973 TAXLR 684

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 30 (1 A) - (A) Jurisdictional fact - Interference by High Court - (B) Who can file appeal u/s 30 (1 A) of Act - Held, question whether jurisdictional fact has been rightly decided or not is a question that is open for examination by HC in an application for writ of certiorari - Person must have first deducted tax due from non-resident assessee and must have paid same to Govt. except a person who contends that firm to whom he made payment is not a non-resident firm - Appeal allowed.
