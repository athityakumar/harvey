Commissioner of Sales Tax, U.P. v Bhagwan Industries Private Limited, Lucknow

Supreme Court of India

10 October 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

Hans Raj Khanna, K.S. Hegde, P. Jaganmohan Reddy

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 469; (1973) 3 SCC 265; AIR 1973 SC 370; 1973 (2) CTR 37; [1973] 2 S.C.R. 625; 1973 (31) STC 293; 1973 TAXLR 1703

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Sales Tax - Uttar Pradesh Sales Tax Act, 1948 - Uttar Pradesh SaleTax Rule, r. 41(5) - Provision of s. 21 of Act - (A) Import of words 'reason to believe' - Difference in turnover of preceding and succeeding years - Initiation of proceedings u/s 21 - Whether proper? - (B) Sufficiency of grounds which induced assessing authority to act u/s 21- (C) Assessment or reassessment - Procedure - (D) Operation to assessments other than those which have been made ex-parte under that rule - (E) Notice of reassessment - Notice and a memorandum - Only stated, to call upon an assessee to produce account books for relevant year and it was also mentioned that in case of non-compliance proceedings would be taken u/s 21 - Construction - Held, proper - Is not justiciable? - Question as to whether that material is sufficient for making assessment or reassessment u/s 21 would be gone into after notice is issued to dealer and he has been heard in matter or given an opportunity for that purpose - Proceedings u/s 21 can be initiated in respect of ex-parte assessment made u/r. 41(5) of Rules - Notice and memorandum could not be construed as notices u/s 21 as they were of a preliminary nature and did not constitute notices - Appeal allowed.
