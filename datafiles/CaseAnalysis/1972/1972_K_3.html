Union of India v K. P. Joseph and Others

Supreme Court of India

27 October 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

Kuttyil Kurien Mathew, A.N. Grover, A.K. Mukherjea

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 211; (1973) SCC (L&S) 133; (1973) 1 SCC 194; AIR 1973 SC 303; 1973 LabIC 191; [1973] 2 S.C.R. 752; 1973 SLJ 1; 1973 (1) SLR 910

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950 - Respondent was a combatant Clerk in Indian Army - Discharged from that post - Re-employed on 2-7-1953 - Ministry of Defence, issued a general order providing for certain benefits to ex-military personnel on re-employment applying to all cases occurring on or after 25-11-1958 - (A) Whether respondent was entitled to benefit of order? - Held, no doubt true that past cases of persons re-employed prior to 25-11-1958 will not be reopened - That is general rule - But effect of cl. (3) of para (3) is to create an exception to general rule in case of persons re- Employed before 25-11-1958 for an unspecified period or for a period which extends beyond date of order and who have exercised their option in writing to be brought under Order - There is no dispute that first respondent has exercised option to be brought under provisions of Order - (B) Whether respondent could have enforced his right in a Court by a writ or order in nature of mandamus for order being an administrative direction? - Held, order in question conferred upon first respondent right to have his pay fixed in manner specified in order and that was part of conditions of his service - No reason why Court should not enforce that right - Appeal dismissed.
