Thulia Kali v State of Tamil Nadu

Supreme Court of India

25 February 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

Hans Raj Khanna, G.K. Mitter

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 114; (1972) 3 SCC 393; (1972) SCC (Cr) 543; AIR 1973 SC 501; 1972 CRLJ 1296; 1974 (1) MLJ(SC) 32; [1972] 3 S.C.R. 622

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Constitution - Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1898, s. 154 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 114 - Appeal against conviction u/art. 136 of Constitution - Whether there can be reappraisal of evidence in Appeal u/art. 136 ? - Held, Supreme Court though does not normally reappraise evidence in appeal by special leave but same can be, when court on consideration of vital prosecution evidence in case finds it to be afflicted with ex facie infirmity - (B) First Information Report - Consequence upon delay in giving information - Further held, First Information Report in a criminal case is an extremely vital and valuable piece of evidence for purpose of corroborating oral evidence adduced at trial - Object of insisting upon prompt lodging of report to police in respect of commission of an offence is to obtain early information regarding circumstances in which crime was committed, names of actual culprits and part played by them as well as names of eye witnesses present at scene of occurrence - Delay in lodging first information report quite often results in embellishment which is a creature of afterthought - Delay in lodging of first information report should be satisfactorily explained -(C) Inference of non-production of material witness by prosecution - Further held, failure in non-production of material witness by prosecution would make Court draw an inference against prosecution - Appeal allowed.
