Sheveroy Estates Limited v Government of Madras

Supreme Court of India

26 September 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, P. Jaganmohan Reddy, I.D. Dua

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 278; (1972) 4 SCC 574; [1975] 100 ITR 14

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Madras Agricultural Income-tax Act, 1955

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Assessment - Mercantile system - Assessee was maintaining his accounts according to the mercantile system - In assessing the income-tax of the assessee for very first A/Y very first A/Y 1954 but sold later during the financial year 1954-55 - Assessee contended that only the crop gathered in the financial year 1954-55 and sold during that period could be taken into consideration for purposes of the assessment - Contention was rejected by the authorities as well as by the HC - Hence this appeal - Whether the HC was right in rejecting the appellant's contention - Held, from a reading of the order of assessment made by the assessing authority and the order of the Tribunal it was clear that in computing the assessee's income for the A/Y 1955-56 the authorities not only took into consideration the opening stock on April 1954, but also the closing stock on March 1955 - It was not necessary to decide to decide this question in this court - Order accordingly.
