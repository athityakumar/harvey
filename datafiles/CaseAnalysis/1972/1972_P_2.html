 P. B. Roy v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

11 February 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Hameedullah Beg, S.M. Sikri, A.N. Grover, A.N. Ray, D.G. Palekar

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 215; (1972) 3 SCC 432; AIR 1972 SC 908; 1972 LabIC 437; [1972] 3 S.C.R. 449; 1972 SLR 343

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Editor

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 16 and 311 - Central Information Service Rules, 1959, r. 2 - (A) Violation of right to equality granted under Constitution - (B) Constitutionality of r. 5 of 1959 Rules - Order terminating an officiating appointment of a person but giving him a fresh appointment with a different designation but permanent tenure and prospects cannot be said to violate arts. 14 and 16 merely because process which resulted in such an order does not have a similar effect upon position or rights of any other servant in Department - Rule does not violate arts. 14, 16 and 311 of Constitution - Appeal dismissed.
