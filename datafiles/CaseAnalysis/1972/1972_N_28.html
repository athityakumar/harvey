Neel & Nirenjan Majumdar v The State Of West Bengal

Supreme Court of India

23 May 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Shelat, Hans Raj Khanna

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 153; (1972) 2 SCC 668; (1973) SCC (Cr) 64; AIR 1972 SC 2066; [1973] 1 S.C.R. 675; 1973 UJ 119

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Criminal - West Bengal (Prevention of Violent Activities) Act, 1970, s. 3 - Arms Act, 1959, ss. 4 and 25(1)(b) - General clauses Act, 1897, ss. 6 and 24 - Possession of and carrying a sword without licence - Detention u/s 3 of 1970 Act - (A) Grounds of detention - Such possessing is punishable u/s 25(1)(b) Arms Act - (B) Determination of effect of notification u/s 15 of repealed Act - Held, ground is not extraneous to cl.(d) of s. 3(2) and detention is valid - Notification would be deemed to be in force after repeal of Act as if enacted u/s 4 of 1959 Act - Appeal dismissed.
