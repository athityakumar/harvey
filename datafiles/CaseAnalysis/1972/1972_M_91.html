Municipal Corporation, Bhopal, M.P v Misbahul Hasan and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 February 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Hameedullah Beg, S.M. Sikri, A.N. Ray

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 227; (1972) 1 SCC 696; AIR 1972 SC 892; [1972] 3 S.C.R. 353

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments; Service

Keywords: Madhya Pradesh General Clauses Act, 1957, Madhya Pradesh Municipal Corporation Act 1956

Summary: Service - Municipalities & Local Governments - Madhya Pradesh General Clauses Act, 1957, s. 24 - (A) Whether procedure laid down by Act for amending a bye-law was mandatory to be followed? - Held, this is a case not governed by procedure laid down in s. 432 of Act at all - That procedure is only applicable where there is an existing bye-law which appears to Govt. to stand in need of modification or repeal wholly or in part - It is only then that Govt. had to cause its reasons for entertaining opinion that bye-law in question should be modified or repealed, to be communicated to Corporation - It has not been shown to us, by references to relevant records and provisions, that this Ailan could be deemed to be a bye-law as contemplated by Act - (B) Whether alteration in a rule governing age of retirement of an employee of Corporation without following procedure prescribed u/s. 24 valid? - Whether rule making powers of Govt. u/s. 433 of Act are very wide so that State could make rule 'for purpose of carrying into effect provisions of Act? - Held, no - Legislative procedure envisaged by s. 24 is in consonance with notions of justice and fair play as it would enable persons likely to be affected to be informed so that they may take such steps as may be open to them to have wisdom of a proposal duly debated and considered before it becomes law - This mandatory procedure was not shown to have been complied with here - Valid alteration in age of retirement of employee respondent was not made in accordance with law - Appeal dismissed.
