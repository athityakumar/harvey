Nanhku Singh and Another v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

21 April 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Jaganmohan Reddy, Kuttyil Kurien Mathew

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 570; (1972) 3 SCC 590; AIR 1973 SC 491; 1972 CRLJ 1204; 1973 UJ 14

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 145 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1898, s. 154 - (A) Appreciation of evidence - Inimical witness - (B) How to use First Information Report? - (C) Supreme Court - Appreciation of evidence - Interference if - Held, evidence must be scrutinised carefully - First Information Report is not a substantive piece of evidence, it is an information of a cognizable offence given u/s 154 CrPC, 1898 and any statement made therein can only be used for purposes of contradicting and discrediting a witness u/s 145 of 1872 Act - Supreme Court does not ordinarily interfere with findings arrived at, on an appreciation of evidence by High Court particularly when it affirms finding of Trial Court unless conclusions arrived at cannot be supported by evidence or that it is perverse - Appeal dismissed.
