Niranjan Singh v State Of Madhya-Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

18 July 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Jaganmohan Reddy, Kuttyil Kurien Mathew

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 371; (1972) 2 SCC 542; (1972) SCC (Cr) 880; AIR 1972 SC 2215; [1973] 1 S.C.R. 691

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Constitution, arts. 22(5), 32 and 226 - M.P. Public Security (Amendment) Act, 1970, s. 2A - CrPC, 1898, s. 491 - CPC, 1908, s. 11 - (A) Dismissal of earlier petition u/art. 226 by High Court - Whether operate as res judicata so as to bar subsequent petition u/art. 32 to Supreme Court - (B) Obligatory on the State Govt. - To consider detenu's representation - (C) Interpretation of Statutes - Penal Acts - Preventive detention - Nature of - (D) Writ of habeas corpus - Issue of rule nisi - Duty of State - Held, does not operate as resjudicata - Held, u/art. 22 (5) it is obligatory on the State Govt. to consider detenu's representation as expeditiously as possible, even before the constitution of an Advisory Board and a reference to it - As the law relating to preventive detention, which has to conform to the limits imposed in art. 22 of Constitution is a restriction on the fundamental right of freedom of a citizen, it has necessary to be construed in a manner which will not restrict that right to any extent greater than is necessary to effectuate the object of that provision - It is incumbent upon State to satisfy Court that the detention was legal and in conformity not only with the mandatory provision of the Act but also in accord with requirement implicit in art. 22 (5) - Petition allowed .
