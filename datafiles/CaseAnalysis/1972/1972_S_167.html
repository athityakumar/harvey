State of Andhra Pradesh and Others v U.S.V. Balram

Supreme Court of India

28 January 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

C.A. Vaidyialingam, Kuttyil Kurien Mathew

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 392; (1972) 1 SCC 660; AIR 1972 SC 1375; [1972] 3 S.C.R. 247

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Education - Constitution - Reservation - Integrated MBBS Course in State of Andhra Pradesh - G O No. 1648/ Health D/- 23-07-1970, rr. 8 and 9 - G O No. 1793, /Education D/- 23-09-1970 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 15(1)(4), 16, 29, 46 and 341 - Selection and admission to Integrated MBBS Course in Govt. Medical Colleges of State - Rules therefore provided in GO 1648 - Reservation to candidates who belonged to socially and educationally backward class (SEBC) provided for vide GO 1793 - Eligibility condition for admission, passing of qualifying examination of Pre-University Course(PUC) or passing of Higher Secondary Course (Multipurpose) (HSC) - Entrance test - Minimum passing percentage required was not less than 50% of marks in Physical and Biological Sciences put together - Admissions were to be made on basis of results of entrance test - Seats were to be filled on basis of merit in entrance test - Vide rr. 8 and 9 further provisions were made for providing a pattern of earmarking of seats for students according to qualifying examinations taken by them - R. 8 stated that 40% each is to be allotted on basis of qualifying examination to HSC and PUC students and r. 9 refers to distribution in same proportion to two sets of candidates on basis of result of entrance test - (A) Whether reservation of 40% of seats for HSC candidates under r. 9 is valid? - Held, considering scheme of rules and object sought to be achieved, namely, of getting best students for Medical Colleges, provision is discriminatory and has no reasonable relation to object sought to be achieved, thus is invalid - (B) Reservation to tune of 25% was provided by State for SEBC - High Court struck down reservation on ground that it violates art. 15(1) and falls outside art. 15(4) of Constitution - HC's judgment whether proper? - Criteria adopted by Commission was foursome, which was adopted by State Govt., viz, firstly, general poverty of class or community as a whole; secondly, occupations pursued by classes of citizens, nature of which must be inferior or unclean or undignified and unremunerative or one which does not carry influence or power; thirdly, caste in relation to Hindus; and fourthly, educational backwardness - There was sufficient material to enable Commission to be satisfied that persons included in list are really socially and educationally backward - If an entire caste, is as a fact found to be socially and educationally backward, their inclusion in list of Backward Classes by their caste name is not violative of art. 15(4) - Though prima facie list of Backward Classes in Commissions report may be considered to be on basis of caste, a closer examination clearly shows that it is only a description of group following particular occupations or professions, exhaustively referred to by Commission - List of BCs found to be satisfying various tests propounded by Court in its various tests - Held, reservation to be within scope of art. 15(4) - (C) Quantum of reservation, also challenged - Challenge rejected - Total of reservation for BCs, SCs and STs should not ordinarily exceed 50% of available seats as held in M R Balaji's case - In instant case total reservation is only 43% - Quantum of reservation thus held to be within limits - Appeal partly allowed.
