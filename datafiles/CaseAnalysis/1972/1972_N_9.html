Nageshwar Sh. Krishna Ghobe v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

19 September 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

I.D. Dua, Hans Raj Khanna, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 684; (1973) 4 SCC 23; (1973) SCC (Cr) 664; 1973 ACJ 108; AIR 1973 SC 165; 1973 BomLR 483; 1973 CRLJ 235; 1973 CRLR 92; [1973] 2 S.C.R. 377

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Constitution Of India, 1950,art. 136 - Culpable homicide - Appellant accused of killing one accidentally - Jurisdiction of Supreme Court to examine evidence in an appeal arising from criminal case - Whether Supreme Court is competent to examine evidence in an appeal in a criminal case? - Held, when in criminal case judgments of trial Court and of High Court proceed principally on assumptions not fully supportable on material on record and there is a serious lacuna in case wholly due to inefficient and perfunctory investigation by investigating agency in appeal u/art. 136 of Constitution arising from that case, SC itself examine evidence -Appeal allowed.
