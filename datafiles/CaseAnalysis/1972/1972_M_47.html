State of Gujarat v Manshankar Prabhasankar Dwivedi

Supreme Court of India

26 April 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Grover, M. Hameedullah Beg

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 148; (1972) 2 SCC 392; (1972) SCC (Cr) 728; AIR 1973 SC 330; 1972 CRLJ 1247; [1973] 1 S.C.R. 313

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Socio-Economic

Keywords: Gujarat University Act, 1949

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 21, cl. 9 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947, s. 5(1)(d) - Education - Lecturer of a Govt. College appointed as examiner by University commits an act of corruption in his capacity as examiner - (B) Applicability of s. 5(1)(d) of 1947 Act - Where a lecturer of a Govt. College appointed as examiner by University commits an act of corruption in his capacity as examiner - Held, is not an officer and therefore not a public servant within s. 21, cl. 9 - Further held, s. 5(1)(d) will not apply because he cannot be said to have abused his position as a public servant as he was not a public servant when he was acting as an examiner - Appeal dismissed.
