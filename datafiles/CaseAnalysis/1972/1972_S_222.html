Bhakla and Ramadhin and Others v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

30 August 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

D.G. Palekar, S.N. Dwivedi, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 496; (1973) SCC (Cr) 478; (1973) 3 SCC 672; AIR 1972 SC 2576; 1973 CRLJ 39

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Murder, Conviction

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302 r/w s. 149 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellants (accused) allegedly murdered the deceased and Trial Court by its judgment acquitted appellant - On appeal HC were found guilty of murder u/s. 302 r/w s. 149 IPC and were sentenced to imprisonment for life - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, there is nothing in the evidence to suggest that PW1, 2 and 3 witnesses had any hostility towards any of the accused. The evidence also establishes that they were working in the field when deceased was assaulted close to them on the bund of the field and they had an opportunity to see the assault themselves. The HC has accepted their evidence and in the circumstances of the case the reliability of these witnesses cannot be questioned. The Trial Court showed a degree of hesitancy in appreciating their evidence which, was not justified. No corroboration was necessary for their evidence. It shows that when the assault was going on they all fled from the place because of the threat given to them by the accused. They first went naturally to the house of the deceased where they met the head servant PW11. They told him the names of all the accused as the assailants and PW11 corroborates that fact. The sequence of events is also important to show that even before the FIR was lodged at the Police Station at about 1.00 a.m. the names of the accused were known as assailants and they had been taken into custody. It cannot be said that there was undue delay in filing the FIR. It was suggested that PW1 must not have left till after PW18 the Circle Inspector reached the village. There is anything suspicious either in the filing of the FIR or in the activities of the Circle Inspector after he reached the village. There was some irregularity in the alleged discovery. Injury might not have been caused by the particular sickle and though it was produced by accused no. 2, it may not have been the sickle which he had actually used at the time. The eye-witnesses who had been the scratching with a sickle at the neck from a distance could not possibly identify the particular sickle used. All they could say was that a sickle had been used. Court do not find anything in the medical evidence which goes to contradict the evidence of the eye-witnesses. Court do not think that in fairness to the Court it can be contended that the HC placed the onus on the defence to prove that they were not guilty. Appeal dismissed.

Legislation Cited

Indian Penal Code, 1860
