C. L. Subramaniam v Collector Of Customs, Cochin

Supreme Court of India

15 February 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, Kuttyil Kurien Mathew

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 109; (1972) 3 SCC 542; AIR 1972 SC 2178; 1973 (26) FLR 170; 1972 KLT 274; 1972 LabIC 1049; 1972 (1) LLJ 465; [1972] 3 S.C.R. 485; 1973 (2) SLR 415

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Constitution, art. 311(2) - Central Civil Services (Classification, Control and Appeal) Rules, 1965, r. 15(5) - (A) Guarantee of reasonable opportunity of being heard - Breach of guarantee - Effect upon enquiry - (B) Provisions of r. 15(5) of 1965 Rules - Opportunity to engage a legal practitioner - Refusal of, effect - Govt. appointing a trained prosecutor to present its case against the govt. servant - But permission to engage, denied to him - Breach of guarantee vitiates the enquiry - Refusal to permit govt. servant to engage a legal practitioner vitiates the enquiry - Appeal Allowed.
