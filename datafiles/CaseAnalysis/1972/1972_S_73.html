Shri Amiya Kumar Karmakar v State of West Bengal

Supreme Court of India

31 July 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Shelat, I.D. Dua, Hans Raj Khanna

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 526; (1972) 2 SCC 672; (1973) SCC (Cr) 68; AIR 1972 SC 2259

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Maintenance of Internal Security Act, 1971, s. 3 - Detention - (A) Concepts of Public Order and law and order - Distinction - (B) Representation by detenu - Time for disposal - No hard and fast rule - Lapse of 21 days due to declaration of emergency and abrupt increase in detention cases causing delayed regular movements - Held, potentiality of detenu's act stated in the grounds of detention, or degree of its impact on the members of the community in the locality in which the act is committed, is the criterion to distinguish whether detention pertains to the field of public order or of merely law and order - Delay held not inordinate so as to invalidate the detention order - Petition Dismissed.
