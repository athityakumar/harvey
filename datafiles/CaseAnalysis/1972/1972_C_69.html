Chowringhee Sales Bureau Private Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax, West Bengal

Supreme Court of India

10 October 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

Hans Raj Khanna, P. Jaganmohan Reddy, K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 282; (1973) 1 SCC 46; (1973) SCC (Tax) 163; AIR 1973 SC 376; 1973 (2) CTR 44; [1973] 87 ITR 542; [1973] 2 S.C.R. 618; 1973 (31) STC 254; 1973 TAXLR 155

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 66 (1) - A/y 1960-61 - Assessment - Appellant-assessee is a private limited company dealing in furniture and also act as an auctioneer - In respect of sales effected by appellant as auctioneer, it realised in addition to commission, Rs.32, 986 as sales tax - This amount was credited separately in books under sales tax collection account - Income-tax Officer held that the said sum was in reality a portion of the sale price itself because the sales tax was not the liability of the purchasers of the goods but was the liability of the sellers of the goods only - Said sum was accordingly added to the appellant's income - On appeal, Appellant Asst. Commissioner excluded the amount of Rs. 32,986/- for the assessee's income - Decision of the Appellate Assistant Commissioner was affirmed by the Income-tax Appellate Tribunal - HC allowed appeal filed against said order - Hence, present appeal - Whether the sum of Rs. 32,986 had been validly excluded from the assessee's business income for the relevant a/y? - Held, it is apparent from order of Appellate Asst. Commissioner and has not been disputed in present case that in cash memos issued by appellant to purchasers in auction sale it was appellant who was shown as seller - Amount realised by appellant from purchasers included sales tax - Appellant, however, did not pay amount of sales tax to actual owner of goods auctioned because statutory liability for payment of that sales tax was that of appellant - Appellant did not also deposit amount realised by it as sales tax in state exchequer because it took position that statutory provision creating that liability upon it was not valid - As amount of sales tax was received by appellant in its character as an auctioneer, amount, should be held to form part of its trading or business receipt - Thus, appellant would, be entitled to claim deduction of amount as and when it pays it to State Govt. - Impugned order of the HC upheld - Appeal dismissed.
