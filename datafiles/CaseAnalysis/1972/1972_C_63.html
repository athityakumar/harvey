Rameshwar Prasad Bagla v Commissioner of Income Tax, U.P., Lucknow

Supreme Court of India

27 September 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

Hans Raj Khanna, K.S. Hegde, P. Jaganmohan Reddy, I.D. Dua

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 201; (1973) 3 SCC 575; AIR 1973 SC 182; 1972 (1) CTR 459; 1973 CTR 459; 1973 (2) ITJ 159; [1973] 87 ITR 421; [1973] 2 S.C.R. 452; 1973 TAXLR 129

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Question Of Fact And Law

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 12B, 66 and 66A - Practice & Procedure - (A) Finding of fact by Tribunal - Jurisdiction of High Court and Supreme Court to interfere with - (B) Capital gain - Shares are not stock-in-trade of assessee - Taxability of profits made on their sale of shares - Held, these Courts only exercise advisory jurisdiction in such references - Even if shares in question are not stock-in-trade of assessee, profits made on sale of those shares will constitute capital gain chargeable to income Tax u/s. 12-B - Appeal allowed.
