S. K. Kedar v State of West Bengal

Supreme Court of India

02 May 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

Kuttyil Kurien Mathew, P. Jaganmohan Reddy, G.K. Mitter

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 101; (1972) 3 SCC 816; (1973) SCC (Cr) 1; AIR 1972 SC 1647; 1972 CRLJ 997; [1973] 1 S.C.R. 488; 1973 UJ 62

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Defence & Security Forces

Summary: Prevention of Violent Activities Act 1970, ss. 3(2)(b), 3(2)(d)and 11- Constitution of India 1950, arts. 22 and 32 - Which acts are prejudicial to law and order and to public order? - Held, whether an act is a breach only of law and order or has caused disturbance of public order is one of degree of disturbance and its effect upon life of community in a locality and whether acts committed are of such a nature, which affect not only immediate victims but general or local public also and are prejudicial to maintenance of public order, depends on facts of each case.
