Ishar Das v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

31 January 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

Hans Raj Khanna, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 97; (1973) 2 SCC 65; (1973) SCC (Cr) 708; AIR 1972 SC 1295; 1972 CRLJ 874; 1972 (8) DLT 146; 1972 (74) PLR 475; [1972] 3 S.C.R. 312

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Probation of Offenders Act 1958

Summary: Criminal -Probation of Offenders Act, 1958, s. 1 - Prevention of Food Adulteration Act, 1954, s.7, 16 -Accused selling adulterated ice-cream - Pleaded guilty of same - 20 years of age - Acquitted by Trial court but convicted by High Court -(A)Whether accused being aged twenty years is entitled to advantage of Probation of Offenders Act in spite of being convicted under Prevention of Food Adulteration Act?- Held, as regards persons under 21 years of age, however, policy of law appears to be that such a person in spite of his conviction under Prevention of Food Adulteration Act, should not be deprived of advantage of Probation of Offenders Act which is a beneficent measure and reflects and incorporates modern approach and latest trend in penology -(B)Whether in instant case sentence of fine can be imposed? - Further held, sentence of fine cannot also be imposed in such a case as it carried with it consequences of imprisonment in case accused fails to pay fine - As object of Probation of Offenders Act is to avoid imprisonment of person covered by provisions of that Act, said object cannot be set at naught by imposing a sentence of fine which would necessarily entail imprisonment in case there is a default in payment of fine -(C) Whether trial was justified in releasing accused? - Further held, that accused was in a repentant mood and was less than 20 years, releasing him after furnishing bond u/s 4 of Act of 1958, by trial court was proper - Appeal allowed.
