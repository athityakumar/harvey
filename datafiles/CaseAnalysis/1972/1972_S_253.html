Yeshwant and Others v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

20 April 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Hameedullah Beg, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 147; (1972) 3 SCC 639; (1972) SCC (Cr) 684; AIR 1973 SC 337; 1972 CRLJ 1254; [1973] 1 S.C.R. 291; 1972 UJ 923

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Tort

Keywords: Waylaid, Employee's Provident Funds and Miscellaneous Provisions Act 1952

Summary: Evidence Act 1872, s. 9 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34 and 302 - Was suspect seen by identifying witnesses before a test identification parade - Held, identification parade was a farce - No reliable corroborative evidence regarding identification - Accused entitled to be a benefit of doubt - Discovery of true motive not imperative in every case - Identity of one of participants doubtful - Acquittal of remaining accused depends upon facts and circumstances of each case.
