Vidya Ram Mishra v Managing Committee, Shri Jai Narain College

Supreme Court of India

31 January 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

Kuttyil Kurien Mathew, K.S. Hegde, P. Jaganmohan Reddy

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 96; (1972) 1 SCC 623; AIR 1972 SC 1450; 1972 (26) FLR 182; 1972 LabIC 829; 1972 (1) LLJ 442; 1972 (2) SCJ 770; [1972] 3 S.C.R. 320

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Special Relief Act, 1963

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Constitution of India, 1953, art. 226 - Writ for enforcement of contractual rights - Service contract - Office of public employment - Whether Order of non-statuary body terminating contract can be questioned in writ proceeding - Held, a lecturer appointed by Managing Committee of a College affiliated to Lucknow University does not hold any office of public employment, or status, which is capable of protection - He has a remedy only by way of a suit for damages and not by way of application u/art.' 226 - Appeal dismissed.
