Kanungo and Company v Collector of Customs, Calcutta and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 February 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 5; (1973) 2 SCC 438; (1973) SCC (Cr) 846; AIR 1972 SC 2136; 1983 (13) E.L.T. 1486

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Sea Customs Act, 1878, s. 167(8) - Proceedings u/s.167(8) - (A) Natural Justice - All the material on which the Customs Authorities have relied set out in show cause notice issued to person concerned - (B) Confiscation of watches - For illegal import of - Customs Authorities falsifying in many particulars the story put forward by the concerned person - Burden of proof on Custom Authorities is discharged - (C) Illegal import - Evidence - Held, principles of natural justice do not require that the persons who have given information should be examined in the presence of the person concerned or should be allowed to be cross-examined by him on the statements made before the Customs Authorities - Onus of proof that the watches were lawfully imported shifts on the person concerned after the Customs Authorities inform the person concerned the results of the enquiries and investigation - Held, in absence of anything to show that the invoice and bill of entry produced by the accused has nothing to do with a particular piece of watch then mere fact that the watch was not shown in the stock register would not be material for showing that it had been illegally imported - Appeal Partly Allowed.
