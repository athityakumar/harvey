Ramagya Prasad Gupta and others v Murli Prasad

Supreme Court of India

03 April 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

D.G. Palekar, C.A. Vaidyialingam, Kuttyil Kurien Mathew

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 744; (1973) 2 SCC 9; AIR 1972 SC 1181; [1973] 1 S.C.R. 63

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 41, r. 4, O. 41, r. 33 - Abatement of Appeal - Failure to bring into record the LRs of deceased partner in a partnership - Whether the appeal would be abated on the reason of failure to bring into record the LRs of deceased partner who has share in the assets of a partnership in dispute? - Held, If under O. 41, r. 4 of the CPC, 1908 it was open to the appellants to appeal to Supreme Court from the whole decree, for the reason that the decree proceeded on a ground common to all the respondents before the High Court, namely, that the partnership was illegal and, therefore, no. suit for dissolution of it lay, and, for Supreme Court to reverse or vary the decree in favour of a non-appealing respondent and therefore set aside the decree against the deceased partner passed by the HC - Relied on Mahabir Prasad v Jage Ram, (1971 Indlaw SC 529) - Therefore the present appeal has not been abated - Appeal dismissed.
