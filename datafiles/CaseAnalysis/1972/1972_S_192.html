Ramlagan Singh and Others v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

17 August 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

Hans Raj Khanna, I.D. Dua, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 511; (1973) SCC (Cr) 563; (1973) 3 SCC 881; AIR 1972 SC 2593; 1973 CRLJ 44; 1973 UJ 332

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1908, ss. 302, 109, 147 and 148 - Murder - Interference of evidence by Supreme Court - Propriety of - Held, no question, however, appears to have been put to any of prosecution witnesses regarding injuries caused to accused persons - As such, there arose no occasion for prosecution witnesses to explain injuries on persons of two of accused - A cross case was started against prosecution witnesses in this case regarding injuries in the persons of both the accused - In cross case, accused were acquitted on appeal by High Court - It appears that witnesses refrained from referring to injuries on persons of accused persons because of fear of being implicated in cross case - The non-mention of the injuries on the persons of the two accused in the prosecution evidence would not justify interference with the appraisement of the evidence by the Trial Court and the HC - Appeals dismissed.
