Tribhuvan Nath v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

28 March 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Shelat, Hans Raj Khanna

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 590; (1972) 3 SCC 511; (1972) SCC (Cr) 604; AIR 1973 SC 450; 1972 CRLJ 1277; 1972 UJ 826

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Prohibition Act

Summary: Criminal - Criminal Procedure Code, 1898, s. 342 A - Prohibition Act - Evidence Act, 1872 - (A) Criminal Procedure - Accused along with others, voluntarily steps in witness box as a witness in defence - He is subject to cross-examination by prosecution counsel and evidence brought out in such cross-examination can be used against his co-accused - (B) Evidence - (1) Conspiracy to cheat banks by using forged stolen negotiable instruments and obtaining proceeds thereunder - Evidence of one accused as witness in defence - Proof of existence of conspiracy - (2) Evidence of an accused is held as an accomplice evidence - Duty of court - Held, if such a witness incriminates his co-accused, other accused, jointly tried with him, has right to cross-examine him if he wants so to do - Statements by one accused to another and evidence as to acts done by him disclosing participation by other accused in conspiracy are also relevant - Court has to see reliablility and corroboration of evidence with independent direct or circumstantial evidence - Appeal dismissed.
