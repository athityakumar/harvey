Messrs Jethmull Bhojraj v State of Bihar and Others

Supreme Court of India

25 January 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, P. Jaganmohan Reddy, D.G. Palekar

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 92; (1972) 1 SCC 714; AIR 1972 SC 1363; [1972] 3 S.C.R. 193; 1972 UJ 644

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, s. 17(1) - Bihar Private Forest Act 1947, ss.14 and 21 - Indian Forest Act, s. 29 - (A) Whether Govt. took possession of lands in question in 1954 by notifying them u/s 29 of Indian Forest Act? - (B) Whether delivery of lands notified for acquisition was taken u/s 17(1)? - Held, (A) in absence of satisfactory evidence to show that Govt. had taken possession of these lands in 1953 or 1954, issue cannot be finally decided as rightly held by High Court - (B) U/s 17, Collector may, in cases of urgency, if Govt. so directs, though no award has been made u/s 11, on expiration of 15 days from publication of notice mentioned in s. 9(1), take possession of land which shall thereupon vest absolutely with Govt. free from all encumbrances - In absence of any such direction by Govt. u/s 17(1), it cannot be said that acquisition was taken u/s 17(1) - Appeals dismissed.
