Pandharinath Shridhar Rangnekar v Dy. Commissioner of Police, State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

11 December 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.V. Chandrachud, Hans Raj Khanna

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 680; (1973) SCC (Cr) 342; (1973) 1 SCC 372; AIR 1973 SC 630; 1973 CRLJ 612; 1973 MLJ 355; [1973] 3 S.C.R. 63

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Defence & Security Forces - Bombay Police Act, 1951, ss. 56 and 59 - (A) Applicability of s. 56 - If all witness are required to be unwilling to give evidence - (B) Proposed externee - Entitlement - To know specific particulars of material allegations - (C) Period and area of externment - Depends upon, what - Held, to attract s. 56 all witness need not be found unwilling to give evidence - Proposed externee is not entitled to know specific particulars of material allegations - Period and area of externment depends on nature of data collected in each case - Appeal dismissed.
