Commissioner of Income Tax, Madras v Ashok Leyland Limited

Supreme Court of India

03 October 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, P. Jaganmohan Reddy, I.D. Dua

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 243; (1973) 3 SCC 201; AIR 1973 SC 420; 1973 (2) CTR 9; [1972] 86 ITR 549; 1973 (2) MLJ(SC) 27; [1973] 2 S.C.R. 516; 1973 TAXLR 358

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922 - Whether payment made for termination of managing agency is an allowable deduction in computing total income of assessee-Co.? - Assessee Co. claimed deduction of same in its assessment as revenue expenditure laid out wholly and exclusively for purpose of business in relevant previous year - Held, from facts found, it is clear that managing agency was terminated on business considerations and as a matter of commercial expediency - Assessee Co. cannot be said to have acquired enduring benefits or acquired any income yielding asset - Expenditure in question was a revenue expenditure and allowable deduction - Appeal dismissed.
