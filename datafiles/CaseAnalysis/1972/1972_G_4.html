 S. Narayanaswami v G. Pannerselvam & Ors

Supreme Court of India

12 April 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Hameedullah Beg, S.M. Sikri, A.N. Grover, A.N. Ray, D.G. Palekar

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 229; (1972) 3 SCC 717; AIR 1972 SC 2284; 1973 (2) MLJ(SC) 1; [1973] 1 S.C.R. 172; 1972 UJ 885

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces

Summary: Constitution, art. 173, 171(3)(a), 171(3)(b) and 171(3)(c) - Govt. of India Act, 1935 - Representation of the People Act, 1951, ss. 2(1)(e) and 6 - Provisions of art.171- (A) Omission to prescribe graduation as a qualification of the candidate for the graduates' constituency - Presumption - Test - (B) Whether article confer a right of functional representation upon persons possessing special types of knowledge and experience - (C) 'Electorate' - Meaning - Held, High Court invaded the legislative sphere by setting aside election of a candidate to Legislative Council from the Graduates' Constituency on the ground of his not being graduate - Article does not confer a right of functional representation upon persons - Held, plain and ordinary meaning of 'electorate' is confined to body of persons who elect and not the extended notion of a body of persons electing representatives 'from amongst themselves' - Appeal Dismissed.
