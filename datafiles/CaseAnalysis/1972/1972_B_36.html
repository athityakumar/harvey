Bhavan Vaja and Others v Solanki Hanuji Khodaji Mansang and Another

Supreme Court of India

03 February 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, P. Jaganmohan Reddy, Kuttyil Kurien Mathew

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 745; (1973) 2 SCC 40; AIR 1972 SC 1371

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 115 - Construction of decree - Duty of the Execution Court - Whether omission to construe decree is omission to exercise jurisdiction vested with court? - Held, it is true that an executing court cannot go behind the decree under execution - But that does not mean that it has no duty to find out the true effect of that decree - For construing a decree it can and in appropriate cases, it ought to take into consideration the pleadings as well as the proceedings leading upto the decree - In order to find out the meaning of the words employed in a decree the Court, often has to ascertain the circumstances under which those words came to be used - That is the plain duty of the execution Court and if that Court fails to discharge that duty it has plainly failed to exercise the jurisdiction vested in it - Evidently the execution court in this case thought that its jurisdiction began and ended with merely looking at the decree as it was finally drafted - Despite the fact that the pleadings as well as the earlier judgment rendered by the Board as well as by the appellate Court had been placed before it, the execution Court does not appear to have considered those documents - If one reads the order of that Court, it is clear that it failed to construe the decree though it purported to have construed the decree - The executing Court and the appellate Court had not construed the decree at all - They had not even referred to the relevant documents - They had merely gone by the words used in the decree under execution - It is clear that they had failed to construe the decree - Their omission to construe the decree is really an omission to exercise the jurisdiction vested in them - Appeal partly allowed.
