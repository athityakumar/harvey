Raj Narain Pandey and Others v Sant Prasad Tewari and Others

Supreme Court of India

31 October 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

Hans Raj Khanna, Y.V. Chandrachud, C.A. Vaidyialingam

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 336; (1973) 2 SCC 35; AIR 1973 SC 291; [1973] 2 S.C.R. 835

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 91(a) - Uttar Pradesh Tenancy Act,1939 - (A) Precedents - Interpretation of local statute - Consideration of view taken by High Court - (B) Mortgage by occupancy tenant - (1) Right of persons recognised by landlord as tenants to redeem - (2) Maintainability of suit - Fact that suit has been filed not by occupancy tenant who mortgaged property but others in whom occupancy rights were subsequently vested - Held, in interpreting a local statute, view taken by HC over a number of years should normally be adhered to and not disturbed - They had sufficient interest in land within s. 91 (a) of Act to redeem land from mortgagee - Would make no difference and would be no bar to maintainability of suit - Appeal dismissed.
