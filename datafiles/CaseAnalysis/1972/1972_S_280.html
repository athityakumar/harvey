Bhagat Ram v State of Rajasthan

Supreme Court of India

31 January 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

Hans Raj Khanna, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 98; (1972) 2 SCC 466; (1972) SCC (Cr) 751; 1972 (9) ACC 67; AIR 1972 SC 1502; 1972 CRLJ 909; [1972] 3 S.C.R. 303

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Tort

Summary: Criminal - Corruption - Prevention of Corruption Act, s. 5(1) - Indian Penal Code,1860, ss. 120-B and 161 - Appellant, who was circle inspector and co-accused demanded illegal gratification from complainant - Whether appellant entitled to be acquitted in view of fact that his co-accused has been acquitted? - Held, yes - It is not prosecution case that appellant had made demand directly to complainant for payment of illegal gratification - Appellant attempted to obtain illegal gratification through instrumentality of co-accused - In view of acquittal of co-accused, it is not possible to maintain conviction of appellant - Acquittal of co-accused would necessarily lead to conclusion that prosecution allegation of demand of illegal gratification from has not been proved - Conviction set aside - Order accordingly.
