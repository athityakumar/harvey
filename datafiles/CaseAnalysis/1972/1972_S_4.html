S. G. Mercantile Corporation Private Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax, Calcutta

Supreme Court of India

04 January 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

Hans Raj Khanna, I.D. Dua, J.M. Shelat, G.K. Mitter

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 250; (1972) 1 SCC 465; AIR 1972 SC 732; 1972 CTR 8; [1972] 83 ITR 700; [1972] 2 S.C.R. 980

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 2 (4), 9, 10 and 12 - Whether income from sub-letting stalls was assessable u/s 10 or s. 12? - Taking of property on lease and subletting portions of same was part of business and trading activity of appellant - S. 9 does not apply to present case because appellant is not owner of property - S. 12 can only be invoked when all preceding heads of income including business income as given in s. 10 are ruled out - Held, income shall be assessable u/s 10 - Appeal allowed.
