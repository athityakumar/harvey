Union Of India And Another v Gajendra Singh, Etc., Etc

Supreme Court of India

07 March 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

Kuttyil Kurien Mathew, K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 156; (1973) SCC (L&S) 269; (1973) 3 SCC 797; AIR 1972 SC 1329; 1973 (26) FLR 241; 1972 LabIC 665; [1972] 3 S.C.R. 660; 1977 (1) ShimLC 167; 1972 SLR 537; 1972 UJ 764

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Administrative - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950 art. 311(2) - Promoted - Standing Order - Reverted - Challenged - Respondent was working as Kanungo in Revenue Department and promoted in pursuance of para 37(ii) of Standing order passed by appellant no.2 - Later on he was reverted to his substantive post because he had not passed departmental examination within prescribed period - Aggrieved respondent filed writ petition contending that he was entitled to continue until qualified person became available and could not be reverted u/s. 37(ii) of Standing order - Judicial Commissioner quashed order of reversion which contravened provision u/art. 311(2) of Constitution - Hence, instant appeals.

Held, respondent, no doubt, was appointed to officiate in the post of Naib Tehsildar in pursuance of para 37(ii) of the Standing Order and that did not provide that he could be reverted for his not passing the departmental examination within any specified time but that would not in any way vitiate the order of reversion as the respondent had no right to hold the post and the appellant no.2 had the undoubted power to revert him. Thus, the fact that appellant no.2 give wrong reason for reverting the respondent would not in any way affect the power which he undoubtedly possessed to revert him. Therefore, contention of the respondent that the retention of his juniors, to officiate in the post of Naib Tehsildar violated his fundamental right u/arts. 14 and 16 of the Constitution and has no substance for his juniors were retained either because they had passed the departmental examination or because they had been exempted from passing that examination. Thus, respondent was not, therefore, similarly situated with his juniors and there was no discrimination to attract the Articles and order of Judicial commissioner was set aside. Appeals allowed.
