Modi Ram Lala v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

11 August 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

I.D. Dua, Hans Raj Khanna, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 514; (1972) 2 SCC 630; (1973) SCC (Cr) 45; AIR 1972 SC 2438; 1972 CRLJ 1521; 1972 MPLJ 1019; 1973 UJ 326

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: CrPC, 1898, s. 32 - Sentence - Adequacy of - General principles - Accused and another cut off a person 's male organ and the nose, as he had seduced and married the accused 's wife about 25-26 days ago - Accused, about 21-22 years of age was married to her a year ago - High Court enhanced conviction to 8 years R. I. - A appeal by special leave to Supreme Court - Held, character and antecedents of the offender did not justify the abnormally harsh sentence imposed on him by the HC - The ends of justice would be amply met if both the accused are sentenced to three years' rigorous imprisonment - Appeal Allowed.
