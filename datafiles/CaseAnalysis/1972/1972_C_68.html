Aluminium Corporation of India Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax, West Bengal

Supreme Court of India

29 August 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, Hans Raj Khanna, P. Jaganmohan Reddy

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 263; (1972) 4 SCC 37; AIR 1973 SC 520; 1972 CTR 336; [1972] 86 ITR 11; [1973] 1 S.C.R. 1097

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax and Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 10(2)(xv) - Income Tax - Commission paid to Seeling Agent - Whether deductible expenditure - Applicability of an illustrative case - Held, payment of commission made to the directors was not because of any commercial expediency but for collateral reasons in that case - Hence the rule laid down in that decision is inapplicable to the facts of the present case - Appeal allowed.
