Sri Amar Chand Inani v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

13 October 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

Kuttyil Kurien Mathew, A.N. Grover, A.K. Mukherjea

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 464; (1973) 1 SCC 115; AIR 1973 SC 313; [1973] 2 S.C.R. 684; 1973 UJ 463

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, ss. 80 and 105, O. 7, r. 10, O. 43 r. 1 - Limitation Act, 1908, arts. 22, 4, 14 and 15(2) - Accident between midnight of December, 1957 and January, 1958 - Trial Court dismissed suit claiming damages as barred by limitation - High Court dismissed appeal - Whether suit was filed within period of limitation? - Held, no - S. 4 of Limitation Act provides that where period of limitation prescribed for any suit expires on a day when court is closed, suit may be instituted on day court reopens but, if Karnal Court was not proper court in which suit should have been filed, plaintiff would not be entitled to benefit of s. 4 of Act - Appeal dismissed.
