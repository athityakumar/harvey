 Azimunissa and Others v Deputy Custodian, Evacuee Properties, District Deoria

Supreme Court of India

26 October 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

J.L. Kapur, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, K. Subba Rao, Kailas Nath Wanchoo

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 253; AIR 1961 SC 365; 1962 (1) SCJ 324; [1961] 2 S.C.R. 91

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Evacuee Property & Displaced Persons

Summary: Constitution - Land & Property - Administration of Evacuee Property Act, 1950, s. 8(2 A) - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 19(1)(f) and 31 - Evacuee Interest (Separation) Act, 1951, s. 10(a) - (A) Whether s. 8(2-A) of Administration of Evacuee Property Act, 1950, is having retrospective effect on s. 8(2) of Administration of Evacuee Property Ordinance XXVII of 1949 ? - Meaning of word 'Purport' - Held, retrospective effect is given to Act to validate - What purports to be vested - Removes all defects or invalidity in vesting or fictional vesting u/s. 8(2) of ordinance XXVII of 1949 of s. 8(2) of Act which repealed ordinance - Makes decrees and judgments to contrary of any court in regard to vesting ineffective - Makes property evacuee property by its deeming effect - Validates all orders passed by custodian in regard to property - Purporting is indicative of what appears on face of it or is apparent even though in law it may not be so - This means that at time when Act purported to vest property in dispute in custodian even though power was not exercisable, s. 8(2 A) by giving a retrospective effect to s. 8(2) of Act makes vesting as if it was vesting u/s. 8(2) of Act, therefore attack on ground of invalidity cannot be sustained - (B) Whether order of sale of composite property comprising sugar mill contravenes art. 19(1)(f) and 31 of Constitution? - Held, composite property comprising of a sugar mill could not be partitioned - Sale of property and division of sale proceeds was only mode of separation available - Order of sale was not violative of arts. 19(1)(f) and 31 of Constitution - Petition dismissed.
