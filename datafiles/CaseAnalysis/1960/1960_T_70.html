KHANDESH SPG. and WVG. MILLS Company Limited v RASHTRIYA GIRNI KAMGAR SANGH, JALGAON

Supreme Court of India

02 January 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, K.C. Das Gupta

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 404; AIR 1960 SC 571; 1960 (1) LLJ 541; [1960] 2 S.C.R. 841

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Bombay Industrial Relation Act, 1946, ss. 42(2), 73A - Bonus claimed - Validity - Respondent issued notice to appellant/company u/s. 42(2) of the Act, demanding payment of reasonable bonus for period from 1-1-1955 to 31-12-1955, however, negotiations was failed - Aggrieved respondent made reference to Industrial Court u/s. 73A of the Act, who held appellant would not be entitled to any allocation as prior charge for rehabilitation after excluding said item of rehabilitation, it and fixed surplus of Rs. 2.20 lakhs and awarded four months basic wages as bonus to employees - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order of Industrial Court was sustainable - Held, respondent not accepted position that reserve funds were utilized as working capital - Strong reliance was placed upon evidence of General Superintendent of appellant but perusal of that evidence disclosed that said witness had not deposed that appellant used reserves as working capital, nor does said witness seek to proved either balance sheet or any extract taken there from - In circumstances, respondent had no opportunity to cross-examine him in respect of alleged user of reserves - For aforesaid reasons, SC had no option but to hold that Rs. 51 lakhs represented reserves were not used as working capital and, therefore, said amount was rightly deducted by Industrial Court from Rs. 60 lakhs fixed by it towards rehabilitation - As balance of Rs. 9 lakhs spread over 15 years come to only Rs. 60,000 during bonus year and as statutory depreciation was Rs. 83,639, Industrial Court rightly excluded entire rehabilitation amount from its calculations in arriving at surplus - Appeal dismissed.
