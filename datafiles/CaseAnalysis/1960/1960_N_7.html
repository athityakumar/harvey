Nanduri Yoganandalakshminarasim Achari and Others v Sri Agastheswaraswami Varu Ofkolakalur

Supreme Court of India

15 January 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

J.L. Kapur, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, K.C. Das Gupta

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 135; AIR 1960 SC 622; 1960 (2) MLJ(SC) 61; 1960 SCJ 954; [1960] 2 S.C.R. 768

Case Digest

Subject: Trusts & Associations

Summary: Trusts & Associations - Endowment - Grant of - Whether courts below is correct in holding that nature of grant are specific endowment for Kalyanotsavam? - Held, High Court held that grant was a specific endowment for Kalyanotsavam of deity and therefore a specific trust and not a grant to appellant with added obligation of spending on service must be accepted to be correct - In considering question of nature of a grant, inam registers have always been treated as evidence of utmost importance particularly where Sanad and Inam title deeds are not produced, and when two inferences are possible from reading of documents there is no reason why this court will interfere with view taken by courts below - When grant was made specific charitable payments exhausted income of property and it is a fair inference to draw therefrom that intention was to devote whole income to charity and any subsequent increase in value of property accrues to charity - Further held, HC was therefore justified in holding that whole of income was to go to deity - Appeal dismissed.
