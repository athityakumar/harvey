State of Bombay and Others v Hospital Mazdoor Sabha and Others

Supreme Court of India

29 January 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, K. Subba Rao, K.C. Das Gupta

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 47; AIR 1960 SC 610; 1960 (62) BomLR 553; 1960 (1) LLJ 251; 1960 SCJ 679; [1960] 2 S.C.R. 866

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Uttar Pradesh Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss.25F and 25H - Was Appellate Court justified in holding that contravention of material provisions of s. 25 F of Act rendered impugned order invalid, and do relevant provisions of Act apply to group of hospitals run by appellant, are they an industry within meaning of Act ? - S. 25F(b) provides that no workman shall be retrenched until condition in question has been satisfied - Noscuntur a sociis is merely a rule of construction and it cannot prevail in cases where it is clear that wider words have been deliberately used in order to make scope of defined word correspondingly wider and it is only where intention of legislature in associating wider words with words of narrower significance is doubtful or otherwise not clear, that present rule of construction can be usefully applied - It can also be applied where meaning of words of wider import is doubtful, but where object of legislature in using wider words is clear and free of ambiguity, rule of construction in question cannot be pressed into service - Activities undertaken by Govt. in interests if socio-economic progress of country as beneficial measures should be exempted from operation of Act - Manner in which activity in question is organized or arranged, condition of co-operation between employer and employee necessary for its success and its object to render material service to community are some of features which are distinctive of activities to which s. 2(j) applies - In deciding question as to whether any activity in question is an undertaking u/s. 2(j) doctrine of quid pro quo has no application - Held, High Court was right in coming to conclusion that conduct and running of group of hospitals by appellant amounted to an undertaking u/s. 2(j) and relevant provisions of Act were applicable and dispute in question was industrial dispute - Appeal dismissed.
