 A. S. T. Arunachalam Pillai v Messrs Southern Roadways Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

29 April 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

Syed Jaffer Imam, A.K. Sarkar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, K. Subba Rao, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 468; AIR 1960 SC 1191; 1961 (1) LLJ 502; 1961 (1) MLJ(SC) 104; 1961 (1) SCJ 421; [1960] 3 S.C.R. 764

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Land & Property

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1939, ss. 44 A, 48 A and 133 A - Board of Revenue Act, 1913 - Whether Regional Transport Officer had power to vary conditions of a permit to ply a stage carriage? - Held, s. 44 A authorises State Govt. to appoint a State Transport Commr. - It further provides that notwithstanding anything contained in Act State Govt. may by notification authorise State Transport Commr. or any officer subordinate to him to exercise and discharge in lieu of any other authority prescribed by or under Act such powers and functions as may be specified in notification - By this section, although u/s 48 A it is State Transport Authority which can vary conditions of a stage carriage permit, State Govt. could, notwithstanding provisions of that section, authorise State Transport Commr. to exercise such powers in lieu of State Transport Authority - It could also confer such power on an officer subordinate to State Transport Commr. - Since, however no rules have been framed u/s. 133 A, so far as s. 44 A is concerned, any officer who is subordinate to Transport Commr. in any way must include Regional Transport Officers in Transport Department - Appeal allowed.
