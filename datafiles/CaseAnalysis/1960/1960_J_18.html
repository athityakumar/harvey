Jhagrakhand Collieries Private, Limited, and Another v Central Government Industrial Tribunal, Dhanbad, and Others

Supreme Court of India

15 February 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 50; 1960 (19) FLR 128; 1960 (2) LLJ 71

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act 1947, s.10 - (A) Whether Tribunal had jurisdiction to deal with merits of case? - Held, order of reference by which dispute has been sent to tribunal for adjudication gave jurisdiction - (B) Whether Tribunal was justified in allowing benefit of award to employees entering subsequent to 01-11-1947 ? - Further held, appellant benefited by increasing prices of coal so wages of employees be incremented on principles of social justice - (C) Whether entitlement of increments should have retrospective effect? - Further held, original increase was in operation only for a short time - No evidence to show that this demand had been specifically and clearly made earlier - Benefit should not be given prior to date of demand - Tribunal award of retrospective effect set aside - Appeal partly allowed.
