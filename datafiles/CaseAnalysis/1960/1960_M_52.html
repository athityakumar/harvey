 State of Vindhya Pradesh (Now Madhya Pradesh) v Moradhwaj Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

24 February 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Syed Jaffer Imam, A.K. Sarkar, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 476; AIR 1960 SC 796; 1960 JabLJ 793; 1960 MPLJ 1118; 1960 SCJ 754; [1960] 3 S.C.R. 106

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Jagirdar, Board Of Revenue Act, 1913

Summary: Land & Property - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 9 - Board of Revenue Act, 1913 - Vindhya Pradesh (now Madhya Pradesh) Abolition of Jagirs and Land Reforms Act, 1952, ss. 22 (1) and 37 - Appeals nos 40 to 109 filed challenging constitutionality of Vindhya Pradesh Abolition of Jagirs and Land Reforms Act - Whether three provisions namely, ss. 22(1) and 37 and cl. (4)(e) of Schedule to Act were a colourable piece of legislation? - Held, scheme of s. 22 is to give effect to s. 7(a) by which certain lands were allowed to remain in possession of jagirdar - S. 22(1) provides that in first instance jagirdar will get all his sir and khudkasht land which he had been cultivating for three years continuously before date of resumption - Even if it could be held that jagirdars and other occupants of land stood in same class and there was discrimination u/s. 22(1) as compared to s. 28(1), such discrimination could not be a ground for striking down s. 22(1) in view of specific constitutional provision in art. 31-A - Provisions of s. 22 as a whole provide a scheme for carrying out intention of legislature expressed in s. 7(a) of Act and perfectly constitutional - As regards s. 37, learned Judicial Commissioner has held this section invalid on ground that it is repugnant to s. 9 of CPC, 1908 - S. 37 provides is that civil courts shall have no jurisdiction to hear certain matters of a civil nature; and s. 9 expressly recognizes that if such a provision is made by any law, jurisdiction of civil courts will disappear - There is thus no question of any repugnancy between s. 9 of CPC and s. 37 of Act - Learned Judicial Commissioner thinks that arithmetical result of cl. (4) (e) is that so far as these lands are concerned, landlord has lost his proprietary interest and has to pay rent to Govt., but at same time gets no compensation - It cannot be said that he has been deprived of proprietary interest without any compensation, for he is relieved of charge of paying land revenue which has also been taken into account in arriving at net assets for that purpose, and that is all that he can except considering that land remains in his possession for all other purposes - Appeals allowed.
