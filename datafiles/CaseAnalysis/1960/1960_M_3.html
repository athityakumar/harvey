 State of Madras and Another v M. A. Noor Mohammed and Company and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 August 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

J.L. Kapur, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, K. Subba Rao, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 125; AIR 1960 SC 1254; 1961 (1) MLJ(SC) 51; 1961 (1) SCJ 301; [1961] 1 S.C.R. 148; 1960 (11) STC 570

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Sales Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Indirect Tax - Madras General Sales Tax Act, 1939, ss. 5(vi)and 3(1) - Constitution of India, 1950 arts. 14 and 226 - Madras General Sales Tax (Turnover and Assessment) Rules,1939, rr. 16(5), 4, 15 and 16 - Madras General Sales Tax Rules, 1939, r. 5 - Whether there is any repugnancy between s. 5(vi) of Madras General Sales Tax Act, 1939 and r. 16(5) of Madras General Sales Tax (Turnover and Assessment) Rules? - Held, no repugnancy - R. 16(5) only emphasises on consequences of non observance of conditions prescribed u/ss. 5(vi) and 6 A - Appeal allowed.
