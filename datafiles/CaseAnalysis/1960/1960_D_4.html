 Dalip Singh v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

28 July 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

K.C. Das Gupta, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 208; AIR 1960 SC 1305; 1961 (2) SCJ 58; [1961] 1 S.C.R. 88

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service

Keywords: Pepsu Service Regulations, Patiala State Regulations

Summary: Service - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 311(2) - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 80 - Pepsu Service Regulations - Patiala State Regulations, r. 278 - Whether appellants removal from service was in compliance with art. 311 of Constitution? - Order does not purport to be passed on any charge of misconduct or inefficiency - All it states is that compulsory retirement is for 'administrative reasons' - There is therefore no basis for saying that order of retirement contained any imputation or charge against officer - If provision had been for retirement without pension in accordance with rules there might have been some reason to hold that retirement was by way of punishment - As however retirement can only be on pension in accordance with rules in present case full pension has been granted to officer, order of retirement is clearly not by way of punishment - Held, order of compulsory retirement made against appellant was not removal from service so as to attract provisions of art. 311 of Constitution - Appeal dismissed.
