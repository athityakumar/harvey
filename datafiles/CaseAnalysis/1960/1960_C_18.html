 State of Madhya Pradesh v Chintaman Sadashiva Waishampayan

Supreme Court of India

01 November 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, A.K. Sarkar, K. Subba Rao, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 503; AIR 1961 SC 1623; 1961 JabLJ 702

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Service - Reasonable opportunity - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 311(2) - Respondent was appointed as a Sub-Inspector of Police - Charge-sheeted - Disciplinary proceedings - Respondent request for supply of certain documents was granted in respect of some documents but not with regard to all - Request for documents which he wanted to inspect before leading evidence in defence and giving his own statement rejected - Found guilty - Dismissed from service - High Court set aside impugned order of dismissal - Whether enquiry held by Hyderabad authorities was contrary to all principles of natural justice, and at said enquiry respondent had not been given a reasonable opportunity to meet charges framed against him? - U/art. 311(2) respondent is entitled to have such a reasonable opportunity - If it appears that effective exercise of this right has been prevented by Enquiry Officer by not giving to officer relevant documents to which he is entitled, that inevitably would be that enquiry had not been held in accordance with rules of natural justice - Proper opportunity must be afforded to him at stage of enquiry after charge is supplied to him as well as at second stage when punishment is about to be imposed on him - Held, failure to supply said copies to respondent made it almost impossible for respondent to submit said two witnesses to an effective cross-examination and that in substance deprived respondent of a reasonable opportunity to meet charge - Appeal dismissed.
