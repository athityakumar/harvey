 Pratap Singh v State of Vindhya Pradesh (Now Madhya Pradesh)

Supreme Court of India

18 November 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

Syed Jaffer Imam, K.C. Das Gupta, Raghubar Dayal, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 268; AIR 1961 SC 586; 1961 CRLJ 733; 1962 (2) SCJ 628; [1961] 2 S.C.R. 509

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Defence & Security Forces

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Constitution - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 14 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898, ss. 420, 421 and 430 - (A) Whether subsequent appeal filed through pleader is maintainable, when appeal by convicted person through jail authorities is dismissed summarily? - Held, previous order of High Court dismissing appeal is final u/s. 430 - Order of dismissal is lawful - Appeal by convicted person through pleader is not maintainable - (B) Whether there is a violation of art. 14 in denial of opportunity of being heard before summary dismissal ? - Held, code intended in case of a convicted person who presents his petition of appeal while in jail that his petition and judgment of court which convicted him must be considered by Appellate Court before it is summarily dismissed, otherwise right of appeal conferred on such a person u/s. 410 would be meaningless - No question could arise of his being heard in person because he has not presented appeal in person nor could there be any question of his pleader being heard because no pleader had been engaged by him to present appeal - Different considerations arise in case of a convicted person who presents his petition of appeal in person or through a pleader in which case he or his pleader must be heard before appeal is summarily dismissed - There is a rational basis for making classification into three categories which has a reasonable connection with object of code - Appeal dismissed.
