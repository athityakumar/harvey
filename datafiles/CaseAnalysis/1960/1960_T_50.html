Shoorji Vallabhdas and Company, Bombay v Commissioner of Income Tax/EXCESSPROFITS Tax, Bombay

Supreme Court of India

19 April 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

S.K. Das, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 174; AIR 1960 SC 1162; [1960] 39 ITR 775; 1961 (1) SCJ 146; [1960] 3 S.C.R. 557

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Excess Profits Tax Act, 1940, ss. 5,21 - Indian Income Tax Act,1922 - Agreement - Commission - Assessment - Remuneration - Exemption - Assessee/appellant was a firm registered under 1922 Act - Firm assumed managing agency of three companies - Appellant as three managed companies were resident in taxable territories within meaning of 1922 Act - Appellant became managing agent of 'M' and firm consisted of appellant and his two sons - Formerly, appellant was managing agent of 'M' and a managing agency agreement was executed between managing agent and managed company and that agreement as varied by two subsequent deeds constituted contract of managing agency between appellant and managed company - Managing agency yielded the commission - Appellant was assessed to Income Tax for three assessment years - Appellant was assessed to excess profits tax under 1940 Act - Hence instant appeal - Whether commission earned by appellant was exempted from tax - Held, appellant practically performed all services and commission which it earned though computed on percentage of freight and passage money in respect of two of managed companies, accrued or arose - As to third managed company whose business was stevedoring and trading and remuneration was payable at 25 per cent of net profits, there could be no doubt that he remuneration accrued - HC correctly answered question against appellant - Appeal dismissed.
