East India Housing and Land Development Trust Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax, West Bengal

Supreme Court of India

02 November 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, S.K. Das, Mohammad Hidayatullah

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 384; [1961] 42 ITR 49

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Companies Act, 1956 - Indian Income-tax Act, 1922, ss. 2(4), 9, 10 - Derived income - Profits - Assessment - Liability to tax - Appellant private Company registered under 1956 Act, incorporated with the object, to buy and develop landed properties and to promote and develop markets - In 1946 appellant purchased ten bighas of land and set up market - Appellant constructed shops and stalls on platforms on that land - For the assessment year 1953-54 appellant received Rs.53,145 as income from tenants of shops and Rs.29,721 from tenants or occupants of stalls - Income Tax Officer assessed the income derived from shops and stalls u/s. 9 of 1922 Act - Order of assessment was confirmed in appeal by Appellate Assistant Commissioner and by Tribunal - Whether income derived from shops and stalls was liable to be taxed u/s. 10 of 1922 Act - Held, appellant was assessable u/s.9 of 1922 Act and not u/s.10 of 1922 Act - Profits or gains of business and that income was not liable to be taxed as income from property - Income from stalls was occupation of stalls and occupation which was source of income was temporary - Income received by appellant from shops was indisputably income from property - Income Tax authorities were right in holding that income received by appellant was assessable u/s. 9 of 1922 Act - Appeal dismissed.
