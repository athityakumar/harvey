Moti Ram v Suraj Bhan and Others

Supreme Court of India

03 February 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, K.C. Das Gupta, K. Subba Rao

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 426; AIR 1960 SC 655; 1960 SCJ 699; [1960] 2 S.C.R. 896

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Keywords: East Punjab Urban Rent Restrictions Act, 1949, East Punjab Urban Rent Restriction (Amendment) Act, 1956

Summary: Rent Control - East Punjab Urban Rent Restriction (Amendment) Act, 1956, ss. 2 and 3 - East Punjab Urban Rent Restrictions Act, 1949, ss. 15(4), 13(3)(A)(iii)and 15(5) - Ejectment Suit - Whether subsequent amendment of statute, which permits ejectment for reconstruction of building more stringent, can be operated in retrospective effect? - An amendment which affected vested rights operated prospectively unless it was made retrospective expressly or by necessary implication - Held, provisions of amended s. 13(3)(a)(iii) were not retrospective and did not apply to present case - Amendment was in regard to a matter of substantive law as it affected substantive rights of landlord - Appeal dismissed.
