Messrs Zoraster and Company v Commissioner of Income Tax, Delhi, Ajmer, Rajasthan and

Supreme Court of India

17 April 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, S.K. Das, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 171; AIR 1961 SC 107; [1960] 40 ITR 552; 1961 (1) SCJ 295; [1961] 1 S.C.R. 210

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act,1922,ss.66(1),66(2),66(4) - Partnership - Endorsement - Assessment - Jurisdiction - Assessee consisted of three partners and formed that partnership for manufacture and sale of blankets, felts and other woollen articles - Deed of partnership was executed - Assessee entered into contracts with Govt. for supply of goods - Supplies to Govt. were made by assessee and payment was by cheques which were received and were endorsed in favour of joint Hindu family, which acted as assessee's bankers - For assessment year 1942-43, Rs. 10,80,658-0-0 and in assessment year 1943-44, Rs.17,45,336-0-0 were assessed as its income by Income-tax Officer -Assessee contended that income was received outside taxable territories - Contention was not accepted by Tribunal - Assessee applied for a reference to HC u/s.66(1) of Act - HC called for a supplemental statement of case from the Income Tax Appellate Tribunal - Hence instant appeal - Whether HC had jurisdiction to call for supplemental statement - Held, HC exceeded its jurisdiction under s. 66(4) of Act - If question actually referred did not bring out clearly real issue between parties, HC may reframe the question so that matter actually agitated before Tribunal might be raised before HC - But s.66(4)of Act did not enable HC to raise a new question of law which did not arouse out of Tribunal's order and direct Tribunal to investigate new or further facts necessary to determine that new question which had not been referred to it u/ss.66(1), 66(2)of Act and direct Tribunal to submit a supplementary statement of case - Appeal dismissed.
