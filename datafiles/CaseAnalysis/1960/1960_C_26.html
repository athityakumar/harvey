R. G. S. Naidu And Co. v Commissioner Of Income-Tax And Excess Profits Tax, Madras

Supreme Court of India

14 December 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, J.L. Kapur, Mohammad Hidayatullah

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 510; AIR 1961 SC 1007; 1961 (1) MLJ(SC) 191; [1961] 3 S.C.R. 271

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Excess Profits Tax Act, 1940

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 34 - Excess Profits Tax Act, 1940 (XV of 1940), s. 15, r. 9, Sch. 1 - A/y 1944-1946 - Excess Profits Tax - Liability - Appellants were appointed managing agents of the company for 20 years - Remuneration was provided to them including 10% commission - Prior to 1-10-1944, the appellants were the Managing Agents of the Agency Company who were the Managing Agents of the company - - For the a/y 1945-46, appellants submitted a return of their income which was accepted by Additional Income-tax Officer - Excess Profits Tax was also worked out on the same basis for the chargeable accounting period ending 31-03-1945 - Appellants submitted a return of their income for the a/y 1946-47 which included commission for the period 1-4-45 to 30-6-45 on purchases of cotton and stores and on capital expenditure - Tax Officer directed that commission on purchases and capital expenditure be taken into account for the year 1-04-1945 to 31-03-1946, and that the receipts be computed accordingly - Assessment for 1945-46 was then reopened u/s. 34 of IT Act and commission on the profits of the company was apportioned between the period 1-10-1944 to 31-03-1945 and 1-04-1945 to 30-06-1945, by the application of r. 9 of Sch. 1 of the Excess Profits Tax Act - Liability of the appellants was revised and fresh assessments were made - Appellate authority confirmed said orders of assessments - On appeal, Tribunal drew up a statement of the case and submitted question of reopening of assessment to HC - HC answered in favour of department - Hence, present appeal - Whether it was open to the Taxing Officer to re-open the assessment for 1945-46 and whether the commission received by the appellants was liable to be apportioned u/r. 9 of Sch. 1 of the Excess Profits Tax Act? - Held, chargeable accounting period for the assessment of Excess Profits Tax and the year of account of the company did not tally - By the assessment of income made on the assumption that the chargeable accounting period and the accounting period of the company tallied, there resulted under assessment in the computation of tax liability for Excess Profits Tax, and it was open to the Tax Officer to take action under s. 15 of the Excess Profits Tax Act- Hence, HC was right in holding that the assessment made by the Excess Profits Tax Officer by apportionment of the commission income between the chargeable accounting periods was correct - Appeals dismissed.
