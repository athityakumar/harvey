Godavari Sugar Mills, Limited v Kopargaon Taluka Sakhar Kamgar Sabha, Sakarwadi

Supreme Court of India

16 December 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, K.C. Das Gupta, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 482; AIR 1961 SC 1016; 1961 (2) FLR 180; 1961 (1) LLJ 313; 1961 (2) SCJ 323; [1961] 3 S.C.R. 342

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Constitution - Labour & Industrial Law - Bombay Industrial Relations Act, 1947, s. 73 A, Sch. II item 2, item 9, item 10, Sch. III item 6 - Constitution of India, 1950 arts. 19(6), 19(1)(g) and 19(1)(b) - (A) Whether idustrial court has jurisdiction to decide dispute relating to abolition of contract labour u/s. 73A of Bombay Industrial Relations Act, 1947? - Held, if any matter is covered by Sch. II it can only be referred to industrial court u/s. 73A - A question relating to abolition of contract labour (assuming that employer does not accept contract labour as part of its labour force) is so inextricably mixed up with question of permanent increase in number of persons employed, their wages, hours of work and rest intervals, that any dispute relating to contract labour would inevitably raise questions covered by Sch. II - A dispute relating to contract labour can be referred u/ s. 73A - (B) Whether s. 3(18) of Bombay Industrial Relations Act 1947 is an unreasonable restriction on fundamental right of employer to carry on trade? - Further held, no - Matter being entrusted to a quasi-judicial tribunal, tribunal would always go into reasonableness of matter and if it comes to conclusion that mode of employment desired by labour is not reasonable it will not allow it - It does not contravene art. 19(1)(g) - Appeal dismissed.
