Ram Prasad Vishwakarma v Chairman, Industrial Tribunal

Supreme Court of India

12 December 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

K.C. Das Gupta, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 289; AIR 1961 SC 857; 1961 (2) FLR 540; 1961 (1) LLJ 504; 1961 (1) SCJ 708; [1961] 3 S.C.R. 196

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 36 - (A) Whether an individual workman can be represented by representatives of his own choice when there is an Industrial dispute pending before tribunal? - Held, as a matter affecting workmen in general - Workman is a party not independent of Union which has espoused his cause - Ordinary rule - That such representation by an officer of trade union should continue throughout proceedings - Appeal dismissed.
