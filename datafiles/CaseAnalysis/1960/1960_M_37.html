Messrs New India Motors Private Limited New Delhi v K. T. Morris

Supreme Court of India

22 March 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, K.C. Das Gupta, Kailas Nath Wanchoo

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 150; AIR 1960 SC 875; 1960 (1) FLR 186; 1960 (1) LLJ 551; [1960] 3 S.C.R. 350

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 33A, 33(1)(a), 18 - Employment -Termination - Justifiability - Respondent was employed by appellant's firm and assigned as field service representative - Respondent's services were terminated on ground that appellant had decided to abolish the post of field service representative - Aggrieved respondent filed complaint u/s. 33A of the Act with reference to existing dispute between appellant and seven employees - Tribunal had directed appellant to reinstate respondent to his original post with back wages - Hence instant appeal - Whether order of Tribunal was sustainable - Held, that by enacting s. 33 of the Act, Legislature wanted to ensure fair and satisfactory enquiry of industrial dispute undisturbed by any action on part of employer or employee which would create fresh cause for disharmony between them - However, workmen concerned in industrial dispute was essential condition for raising of industrial dispute itself, and if industrial dispute was raised by a group of workmen acting on their own or through their union then it would be difficult to resist conclusion that all those who sponsored dispute were concerned in it - Therefore, construction was harmonious with definition prescribed u/s. 2(s) of the Act and with provisions contained u/s. 18 of the Act and expression workmen concerned in such dispute'could be limited only to such workmen who were directly concerned with dispute in question and that expression includes all workmen on whose behalf dispute had been raised as well as those who would be bound by the award which would be made in said dispute - Hence, construction of relevant clause had given rise to divergence of opinion in industrial courts, but consensus of opinion appeared to be in favour of construction which was adopted in said clause - Appeal dismissed.
