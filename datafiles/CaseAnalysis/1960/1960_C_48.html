Pingle Industries Limited, Secunderabad v Commissioner of Income Tax, Hyderabad

Supreme Court of India

26 April 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

S.K. Das, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 178; AIR 1960 SC 1034; [1960] 40 ITR 67; 1963 (2) MLJ(SC) 3; [1960] 3 S.C.R. 681

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: misa, Hyderabad Income Tax Act, 1357

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Hyderabad Income Tax Act, 1357,s.12(2) (XV) - Indian Income Tax Act,1922, ss. 10(2)(xv) - Capital expenditure - Books of accounts - Stock in trade - Assessment - Appellant was a private limited company carrying on business - Appellant took on contract right to excavate stones from certain quarries for a period of twelve years under a Quolnama - Appellant/contractor had no right to manufacture cement from stones and had only right to excavate stones from quarries till end of contract period - According to appellant's books of account, it paid each year of account Rs. 37,000 as lease or contract money to extract stones under two contracts and it claimed an allowance u/s.12(2)(xv) of 1357 Act, corresponding to s.10(2)(xv) of 1922 Act - Appellate Assistant Commissioner found that expenditure was capital expenditure - Tribunal allowed claim of appellant that payment of two sums of Rs. 27,054 and Rs. 28,158 for assessment years respectively was in its true nature a revenue expenditure rather than capital expenditure - Hence instant appeal - Whether lease money paid by assessee company was capital expenditure or revenue expenditure - Held, assessee acquired by his long-term lease a right to win stones and leases conveyed to him a part of land - Stones in situ were not his stock-in-trade in a business sense but a capital asset from which after extraction he converted the stones into his stock-in-trade - Payment, though periodic in fact was neither rent nor royalty but a lump payment in instalments for acquiring a capital asset of enduring benefit to his trade - HC was right in treating outgoings as on capital account - Order accordingly.
