 Rajputana Mining Agencies Limited v Union of India and Another

Supreme Court of India

31 August 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, S.K. Das, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, K.C. Das Gupta, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 226; AIR 1961 SC 56; [1960] 40 ITR 490; 1961 (1) SCJ 611; [1961] 1 S.C.R. 453

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Finance Act, 1950 - Income Tax (Amendment) Act, 1953 - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922 - A/y 1950-51 - Income tax exemption - Validity - Appellant/Company was incorporated in 1945 in former Kotah State - Income-tax authorities sought to tax its profits and income for A/y corresponding to previous year, 1949-50 - Appellant claimed exemption u/s. 14(2)(c) of the 1922 Act, as it stood before amendment, claim was rejected by HC - Hence instant appeal - Whether impugned order of the HC was justified - Held, exemption was in respect of liability to tax in any year of assessment, and exemption in A/y was in regard to income in previous year - Therefore, withdrawal of exemption in A/y conversely affected income of previous year, 1949-50 which was subject matter of tax in instant case - Further, application of Indian Income-tax Act made Rajasthan taxable territory subject to Indian Income-tax law, and Parliament was competent to enact new law for the area, just as it did for whole of rest of India - Thus, fiction in amendment made exemption to disappear as if it had never been granted, and unless there was saving, amendment should operate to obliterate exemption - Therefore whole purpose and intent of amendment was to reach this result from A/y onwards, and there could be no saving - Moreover, the 1950 Act was concerned with application of the 1922 Act to this area, which it did by amending definition of 'taxable territory' in the 1953 Act and by applying that Act to territory, thereafter, Indian Parliament could amend Income-tax Act retrospectively, and amendment would apply also to new taxable territory - Appeal dismissed.
