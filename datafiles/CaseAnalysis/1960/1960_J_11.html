Jaikrishnadas Manohardasdesai and Another v State of Bombay

Supreme Court of India

16 March 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 147; AIR 1960 SC 889; 1960 CRLJ 1250; 1961 (1) SCJ 42; [1960] 3 S.C.R. 319

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: misa

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 409/34 - Dishonest misappropriation - Conviction of two appellants for offence u/ss. 409/34 - Challenged - Whether conviction u/ss. 409/34 maintainable when charge framed only u/s. 409? - Held, s. 34 does not create an offence; it merely enunciates a principle of joint liability for criminal acts done in furtherance of common intention of offenders - Conviction of an accused person recorded, relying upon principle of joint liability, is therefore for offence committed in furtherance of common intention and if reasons for conviction establish that accused was convicted for an offence committed in furtherance of common intention of himself and others, a reference in order recording conviction to s. 34 may appear to be a surplusage - Order of HC recording conviction of appellants for offence u/ss. 409/34 is therefore not illegal - Appeal dismissed.
