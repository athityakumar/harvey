 Indian Copper Corporation Limited v State of Bihar and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 November 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, S.K. Das, Mohammad Hidayatullah, K.C. Das Gupta, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 199; AIR 1961 SC 347; 1961 (1) SCJ 457; [1961] 2 S.C.R. 276; 1961 (12) STC 56

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Defence & Security Forces; Municipalities & Local Governments; Sales Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Constitution (Sixth Amendment) Act, 1956 - Bihar Sales Tax Act, 1959 - Bihar Sales Tax Act, 1947, s. 12 (2) - Constitution Of India , 1950, art. 286(1)(a) - Govt. of India Act, 1935 - Whether sales under which goods were delivered outside State of Bihar for purpose of consumption but not within state of first delivery or first destination, are exempt from levy of sales-tax by Bihar state by virtue of art. 286(1)(a) of Constitution as it stood before recent amendment? - Where property in goods passes within a State as a direct result of sale, sale transaction is not outside State for purpose of art. 286(1)(a), unless Explanation operates - Explanation to art. 286 was satisfied if purpose of delivery tinder sale was for consumption in that state - Held, order of High Court will be modified by making it clear that if goods were as a direct result of sale delivered outside State of Bihar for purpose of consumption in State of first delivery assessee would be entitled to exemption of sales-tax imposed and that it would not be necessary for assessee to prove further that goods so delivered were actually consumed in State of first destination - Appeals dismissed.
