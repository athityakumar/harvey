Champalal v Mst. Samarath Bai

Supreme Court of India

21 January 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

J.L. Kapur, Syed Jaffer Imam, K. Subba Rao

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 136; AIR 1960 SC 629; 1960 SCJ 969; [1960] 2 S.C.R. 810

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Arbitration & ADR; Family & Personal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Family & Personal - Limitation Act, art. 178 - Registration Act, 1908, ss. 17, 49, 21 and 22 - Indian Succession Act, 1925, s. 192 - Arbitration Act, 1940, s. 14(2)- (A) Whether filing of award was within time allowed by Limitation Act? - Held, after award was made, arbitrators filed it in court and they also gave notice to parties by registered post informing them of making of award - It has not been shown as to how filing of award is barred by limitation - Art. 178 of Limitation Act applies to application, made by parties and not to filing of award by arbitrators - (B) Whether award was illegal as it was not registered in accordance with law? - Further held, filing of an unregistered award u/s 49 of Registration Act is not prohibited, what is prohibited is that it cannot be taken into evidence so as to affect immoveable property falling u/s 17 of that Act - (C) Whether court had jurisdiction to grant extension of time to arbitrators for making award? - Further held, court had jurisdiction to entertain application for filing award and to extend time for filing it - Appellant was not constituted an executor or nominiee but was to be an executor if he was adopted - Similarly question whether appellant after accepting office of an executor had renounced it or a discharge was necessary u/s. 301 of Succession Act does not arise - (D) Whether arbitration award was invalid as it was in excess of powers given to arbitrators and was contrary to law on the face of it? - Further held, as it was specifically stated in arbitration agreement that consequences of adoption or non-adoption were to be decided by arbitrators, they rightly laid down what was to happen if adoption did not take place and also provided that if it was due to default of appellant one consequence will follow and if it was default of respondent another consequence would follow - Award could not be treated as having perverted line of succession by merely stating that if adoption did not take place respondent would receive share of testator's property and it would form her stridhan - Appeal dismissed.
