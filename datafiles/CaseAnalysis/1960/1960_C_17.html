Charandas Haridas and Another v Commissioner of Income Tax, Bombay North, Kutch and Saurashtra, Ahmedabad, and Another

Supreme Court of India

15 March 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.L. Kapur, S.K. Das

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 344; AIR 1960 SC 910; [1960] 39 ITR 202; 1960 SCJ 929; [1960] 3 S.C.R. 296

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Capital Receipt, Question Of Fact And Law

Summary: Income tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, s. 66(2) - Karta - Partnership - Capital Receipt - A Karta of Hindu undivided family was a partner in six managing agency firms in six mills - In previous years income received by him as partner was assessed as income of Hindu undivided family - A acting for his three minor sons and himself and his wife entered into oral agreement for a partial partition and by that agreement A gave one pie share to his daughter B in managing agency commission from two of six managing agencies held by family - Balance together with shares in other managing agencies was divided into five equal shares between A, his wife and sons - A executed a memorandum of partial partition and claimed that income should no longer be treated as income of Hindu undivided family but as separate income of divided members - Income-tax Officer declined - Appeal was unsuccessful - Appellate Tribunal found that, division if any was of income and not of assets from which income was derived inasmuch as agreements of managing agency with managed Companies did not undergo any change whatever as a result of alleged partition - HC opined though income might had been purported to be divided source of income still remained united as belonging to Hindu undivided family and answered question in affirmative - Leave to appeal to SC refused - Hence instant appeal - Whether Tribunal was justified in finding that income in share of the commission agency of Mills was income of Hindu undivided family - Held, as per document no doubt mentioned a commission in respect of each of six managing agencies which commission was divided by document - Fact of partition in Hindu law might not effect upon position of partner so far as law of Partnership was concerned but it had full effect upon family in so far as Hindu law was concerned - Income tax law before partition took note factually of position of Karta and assesses not him qua partner but as representing Hindu undivided family - Document was fully effective between members of family and there was factually no Hindu undivided family in respect of those particular assets and assets at all times stood in name of A and assets were in name of A - Document was a genuine document and not a sham and it effectually divided income and there were no materials to justify finding income in share of the commission agency of Mills was income of Hindu undivided family - Appeal allowed.
