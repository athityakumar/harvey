Homi Jehangir Gheesta v Commissioner of Income Tax, Bombay

Supreme Court of India

22 September 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

S.K. Das, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 243; AIR 1961 SC 1135; [1961] 41 ITR 135; [1961] 1 S.C.R. 770

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income tax Act, 1922, ss. 23(3), 66 - Denomination - Assessment - Refusal - Rejection of explanation - Income from undisclosed source - Summary refusal by HC to direct a reference - Appellant encashed high denomination currency notes of value and was called upon by Income tax Officer to submit a return for relevant year - Appellant made three statements, discrepant in material particulars, at different stages as to how he received the amount - Income-tax Officer found that true nature of receipt had not been disclosed, treated it as income from an undisclosed source and assessed him - Assistant Commissioner of Income-tax upheld that order on appeal - On a further appeal, Appellate Tribunal reviewed facts, considered discrepancies in appellant's and affirmed order of assessment - An application a reference to HC have been made u/s. 66 of 1922 Act, Tribunal found that no question of law arose from its order and dismissed the same - HC summarily dismissed the application made by the appellant under s. 66(2) of the Act - Whether order of Tribunal disclosed any question of law and HC was right in summarily dismissing application u/s. 66(2) of Act - Held, that no question of law arose from the order of Tribunal - Rejection of explanation of assessee, character of particular receipt as income can be said to have been established - Proper inference was that receipt must be treated as income in the bands of the assessee, there was no reason why the assessing authorities should not draw such an inference - Such an inference was an inference of fact and not of law - For the reasons given above we are of the view that no question of law arose from the order of Tribunal and no grounds for interference with the judgment and order of HC - Appeal dismissed.
