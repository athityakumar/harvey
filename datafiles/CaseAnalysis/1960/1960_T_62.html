C. A. Abraham, Uppoottil v Income Tax Officer

Supreme Court of India

29 November 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 280; AIR 1961 SC 609; [1961] 41 ITR 425; 1961 KerLJ 165; 1961 (1) MLJ(SC) 183; 1961 (1) SCJ 673; [1961] 2 S.C.R. 765

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Travancore Income Tax Act

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Constitution - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 226 - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 28 and 28(1) (c) - Travancore Income Tax Act, M E 1124, s. 41 - (A ) Whether jurisdiction u/art. 226 of Constitution can be invoked by a person aggrieved by an order of appellate Asst. Commr. imposing a penalty? - Held, adequate remedy is open by way of appeal to tribunal - Jurisdiction u/art. 226 of Constitution cannot be invoked - (B) What is meaning of expressions 'assessment' and 'so far as may be' in s. 44 of Income Tax Act, 1922 - Held, liability declared by s. 44 is undoubtedly to assessment under Chap. IV, but expression 'assessment' used therein does not merely mean computation of income u/s. 23 - Word 'assessment' has been used in Chap.IV in its widest connotation and includes procedure for declaration and imposition of tax liability and machinery for enforcement thereof including a proceeding for imposition of penalty u/s. 28 - Use of expression 'so far as may be' in last clauses of s. 44 also does not restrict application of provisions of Chap. IV only to those which provide for computation of income - Appeal dismissed.
