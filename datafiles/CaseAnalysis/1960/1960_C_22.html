 Burmah Shell Oil Storage and Distributing Company, of India Limited, and Another v Commercial Tax Officer and Others

Supreme Court of India

27 September 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, S.K. Das, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, K.C. Das Gupta, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 114; AIR 1961 SC 315; 1962 (2) SCJ 251; [1961] 1 S.C.R. 902; 1960 (11) STC 764

Case Digest

Subject: Air Law; Constitution; Sales Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 286 - Bengal Motor Spirit Sales Taxation Act, 1941, ss. 3(4) and 22 - Bengal Motor Spirit Sales Taxation (Second Amendment) Act, 1954, s. 2 - Indian Aircraft Act, 1934, s. 16 - Sea Customs Act, ss. 42, 51 and 52 - Bengal Motor Spirit Sales Taxation (Amendment) Act, 1955 - Bengal Motor Spirit Sales Taxation (Amendment) Act, 1957 - Indian Aircraft Rules, 1920, r. 63 - Whether the assessment to sales tax on sale of motor spirit for aviation purposes supplied by the appellants, under the Bengal Motor Spirit Taxation Act, valid in law? - No single element of the contract of sale is by itself a decisive factor in determining which State is to tax the sale where there are more States than one involved, except the test of actual delivery of the goods in a State as a direct result of the sale for purposes of consumption in that State, and it is that State and that State only which has the right to tax the sale and none other - The test is that the goods must have a foreign destination where they can be said to be imported - Held, assessment of sales tax was valid - Appeal dismissed.
