Delhi Stock Exchange Association Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax, Delhi

Supreme Court of India

30 November 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

J.L. Kapur, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 282; AIR 1961 SC 1144; [1961] 41 ITR 495; 1961 (2) SCJ 152; [1961] 2 S.C.R. 798

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income tax & Direct Taxes - Admission fees - Shares - Taxable income - Memorandum - Articles of Association - Appellant/Company running a Stock Exchange and dealing in shares - Admission fees of Members and Authorised Assistants - Its capital was divided into shares on which dividend could be earned - During assessment year in company's receipts consisted of certain amounts received as admission fee from Members - Authorised Amount received as membership fee was shown as capital in the books of the company - There was no periodicity, it should be treated as capital receipt exempt from assessment - Whether Members' admission fee was taxable income was to determined by nature of business of company - Held, Profits and distribution as disclosed by its Memorandum and Articles of Association and rules made for conduct of business - They showed that the income of company was distributable amongst its shareholders - Body of trading members who paid the entrance fees and share-holders were not identical - Appeals dismissed.
