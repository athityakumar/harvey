 State of Orissa v Dhirendranath Das

Supreme Court of India

18 August 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, S.K. Das, Mohammad Hidayatullah, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, K.C. Das Gupta

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 504; AIR 1961 SC 1715

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Service - Constitution - Disciplinary Proceedings (Administrative Tribunal) Rules, 1951, r. 4(1)(a)(b) - Bihar and Orissa Subordinate Services Discipline and Appeal Rules, 1935 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Respondent initially appointed as a lower Division Asst. in Secretariat of Govt. of Orissa - Later promoted as an Upper Division Grade I Asst. in Commerce Department - Thus a non-gazetted servant of State - Respondent placed under suspension pending enquiry - Order of dismissal - Writ before High Court - HC held that in directing enquiry under Tribunal Rules, 1951 instead of an enquiry under Service Rules, 1937 discrimination was practised against respondent, and accordingly declared that cls. (a) and (b) of sub-r. (1) of r. 4 of Tribunal Rules, 1951 in their application to non-gazetted Govt. servants infringed guarantee u/art. 14 of Constitution, 1950 and directed that entire departmental proceedings against respondent held by Tribunal and orders of punishment passed by Govt. be declared inoperative, and further directed that disciplinary proceedings against respondent be restored to stage which they had reached on respective date before case was referred to Tribunal - HC's finding and judgment, whether proper? - Held, yes - Under Tribunal Rules case is referred to by Governor, and findings of Tribunal are merely in nature of recommendations, which Govt. is not bound to accept, but Govt. has to consult Public Service Commission before passing final Order - Service Rules, 1935 applies to non-gazetted Govt. Servants, an enquiry is held by departmental head of public servant concerned and there is a right of appeal to authority immediately superior to authority who imposes a penalty upon public servant - HC gave finding that both Rules were simultaneously in operation at time of enquiry - HC judgment upheld - Appeal dismissed.
