 R. P. Kapur and Others v Sardar Pratap Sing Kairon and Others

Supreme Court of India

28 October 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

S.K. Das, Mohammad Hidayatullah, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, K.C. Das Gupta, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 501; AIR 1961 SC 1117; 1961 CRLJ 161; [1961] 2 S.C.R. 143

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 21 and 32 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1898, ss. 154, 156, 157 and 551 - Fundamental rights u/arts. 14 and 21 claimed to be violated by petitioner No. 1 and his wife and mother-in-law, who all are petitioners - Respondents were Punjab State Chief Minister and authorities under State - Petitioners' case was that petitioner No. 1 had misfortune to incur wrath of CM of State, as petitioner No. 1 has shown disfavors in two of cases one relating to property of Chief Minister's son and other related to criminal case against certain police officials of State - It is further stated that as a result of displeasure which petitioner No. 1 had incurred, a special procedure was adopted in investigation of criminal cases instituted against petitioners, and some new cases were started through instrumentality of CID Police with a view to subject petitioners to harassment and persecution - Whether in matter of institution and investigation of these cases a special procedure unknown to law has been adopted and whether petitioners have been singled out for unequal treatment in administering law relating to institution and investigation of criminal cases in State? - Two cases in which petitioner No. 1 has disfavoured cause of Chief Minister, noted and perused - Four cases in which special procedure was alleged to have been followed to single out petitioners, also noted in detail as to procedure adopted - No force found in allegation of special procedure - Petitioners have not established what they have alleged, namely, that petitioner No. 1, had incurred displeasure of Chief Minister by reason of what happened in first two mentioned cases - Held, petitioners have failed to establish either of their two contentions, that procedure adopted was illegal, or that petitioners were unfairly discriminated against - Petitions dismissed.
