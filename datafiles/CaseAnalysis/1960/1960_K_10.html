Commissioner of v Karamchand Premchand

Supreme Court of India

28 April 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

S.K. Das, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 182; AIR 1960 SC 1175; 1961 (63) BomLR 534; [1960] 40 ITR 106; 1960 SCJ 1289; [1960] 3 S.C.R. 727

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, Business Profits Tax Act, 1947, s. 5 - Business profits - Assessment - Exemption - Assessee's/respondent's business in Baroda showed loss for four accounting periods for which assessee claimed reduction of loss against its assessable income in India - Appellant/ITO rejected claim of assessee - Aggrieved assessee appealed before Assistant Commissioner which was allowed- Appellant filed appeal before Tribunal which upheld order of ITO - Tribunal referred matter to HC which was answered in favour of assessee - Appellant moved HC and obtained certificate of fitness - Hence, instant appeal - Whether HC was justified in applying true scope and effect of third proviso u/s. 5 of the Act - Held, language of proviso as it stands, it does not exclude Baroda business of assessee but exempts only income, profits or gains thereof unless they were received or deemed to be received in or brought into India - Hence, HC had rightly held that if there was any ambiguity of language, benefit of that ambiguity should be given to assessee - Appeal dismissed.
