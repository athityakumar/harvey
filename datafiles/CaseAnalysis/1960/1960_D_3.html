 DIRECTOR of RATIONING and DISTRIBUTION v CORPORATION of CALCUTTA and OTHERS

Supreme Court of India

16 August 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Syed Jaffer Imam, A.K. Sarkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 411; AIR 1960 SC 1355; 1960 CRLJ 1684; 1961 (1) MLJ(SC) 88; 1961 (1) SCJ 406; [1961] 1 S.C.R. 158

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Defence & Security Forces; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Attorney-General

Summary: Municipalities & Local Govts - Practice & Procedure - Constitution - Calcutta Municipal Act, 1923, ss. 386, cl. 1(a) and 488 -Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 13, cls. 3, arts. 300, 366 and 372 - Calcutta Municipal Act, 1951, s. 437 - Whether state can be punished by penal statute's or law? - Held, proper rule of construction which should now be applied, after January 26, 1950, is that State in India, whether in Centre or in States, is bound by law unless there is an express exemption in favour of State or an exemption can be inferred by necessary implication - State is exempt, by necessary implication, from provisions contained in s. 488 - Provision is penal and state as such is exempt from all penal statutes and provisions, including those imposing fines, like instant case, which when realised goes to State - Fines when inflicted by courts are realised by state and go to coffers of State - In effect if state as such is to be prosecuted under a penal statute imposing fine result is that court will sentence state to fine which will go to state itself - It is obvious that if such is result of a prosecution, namely that accused gets fine, intention could never be that such a prosecution should be launched - Where penalty is fine and fine goes to state - It must be held that by necessary implication law does exempt state to be prosecuted for such an offence - Appeal allowed.
