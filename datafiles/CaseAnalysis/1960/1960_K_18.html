 Kishan Chand Arora v Commissioner of Police, Calcutta

Supreme Court of India

09 December 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, K. Subba Rao, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 234; AIR 1961 SC 705; 1961 (2) SCJ 309; [1961] 3 S.C.R. 135

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Motor Vehicles Act, Indian Bar Councils Act, 1926, Police Act, Criminal Law Amendment Act, 1908, Bihar Mica Act, 1948, Iron and Steel (Control of Production and Distribution) Order, 1941, Ajmer Government Wards Regulation, 1888, Ajmer Tenancy and Land Records Act, Criminal Law Amendment Act, 1958, Bihar Mica Act, Indian Criminal Law Amendment Act, 1908

Summary: Administrative - Constitution - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 19(1)(g) - Calcutta Police Act, 1866, s. 39 - Granting license - Provision - Constitutionality - Petitioner applied to Commissioner of Police for license to carry on business of eating house, which was rejected by order dt. 30-5-1959 - Hence, instant Writ Petition - Whether in s. 39 of the Act no criteria was laid down anywhere to guide discretion of Commissioner in granting license - Held, s. 39 of the Act clearly provided that Commissioner would use his discretion in deciding whether person applying for license was in actual and effective control and possession of place where eating house was to be kept and was thus keeper thereof - Commissioner would also satisfy himself that keeper was person of good behaviour and further that he was able to prevent drunkenness and disorder in eating house, if he was satisfied on these 3 matters - Discretion that was given to him was to satisfy himself on these 3 points and if he was satisfied about them he had to grant license - On other hand if he was not satisfied on any one or more of these points he would exercise discretion by refusing license - Further, SC could not agree with petitioner that 2 parts of s. 39 of the Act should be read separately, as if one has no effect on other, reading them together, it was fair and reasonable to come to conclusion that discretion of Commissioner in this matter was guided by 2 objects mentioned in section and by necessary implication contained in it that person applying should be in actual and effective control and possession of place where he was going to keep eating house - Therefore, argument that s. 39 of the Act conferred arbitrary and uncanalised power without any criteria for guiding discretion of licensing authority should fail and section could not be held to be unreasonable restriction on right to carry on trade on this ground - Petition dismissed.
