All India Reporter Limited, Nagpur v Ramachandra Dhondo Datar

Supreme Court of India

29 November 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 281; AIR 1961 SC 943; [1961] 41 ITR 446; 1961 (1) MLJ(SC) 124; 1961 (1) SCJ 468; [1961] 2 S.C.R. 773

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Debt Laws, Cross- Decree

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922 r. 21, ss. 18(2), 46 - Execution of decree - Tax deduction at source - Maintainability - Respondent/employee filed civil suit for compensation for wrongful termination of employment, which was decree - Further, respondent applied for execution of decree and claimed less amount decreed against him in cross suit filed by appellant/company - Appellant made application before Trial Court to declare that appellant company was entitled and in law bound to deduct tax due on amount - Trial Court directed appellant company to pay to Income Tax Department on account of income-tax and super-tax on amount due to respondent and directed it to pay balance in the Court after filing receipt for payment of tax from Income Tax department - On appeal, HC reversed order passed by Trial Court and directed execution as claimed by respondent - Hence instant appeal - Whether impugned order of HC was justified - Held, there was decree passed in favour of respondent under scheme of the CPC, that decree had to be executed as it stood, subject to such deductions or adjustments as were permissible under the Code - There was no tax liability which respondent was assessed to pay in respect of this amount till date on which appellant sought to satisfy alleged tax liability of respondent - As between appellant and respondent, amount did not represent salary, it represented judgment-debt and for payment of income-tax thereon, no provision was made in decree - Further, defence to execution if any should be raised in execution proceeding and not by separate action - Thus, amount payable by appellant company to respondent was not salary but judgment-debt, and before paying that debt appellant company could not claim to deduct at source tax payable by respondent, nor could appellant company seek to justify its plea on ground that judgment-creditor was indebted to 3rd person - Appeal dismissed.
