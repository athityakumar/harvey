 State of Madhya Pradesh and Another v Baldeo Prasad

Supreme Court of India

03 October 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, K. Subba Rao, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 144; AIR 1961 SC 293; 1961 CRLJ 442; 1961 (2) SCJ 484; [1961] 1 S.C.R. 970

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Police Act, City of Bombay Police Act, 1902

Summary: Constitution - Criminal - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 13, 132(1) and 226 - Bombay Police Act, 1951, ss. 56, 57 and 59 - City Of Bombay Police Act, 1902, s. 27 - Central Provinces and Berar Goondas Act,1946, ss. 3, 4, 4-A and 6 - Validity of 1946 Act as amended by Madhya Pradesh Act, 1950 - Against respondent, appellant no.1 passed an order u/art. 4-A of 1946 Act - Respondent challenged validity of said orders before High Court, on the ground that the Act under which said orders passed was itself ultra vires - Appellants disputed respondent's contention about vires of Act - HC, however, has upheld respondent's plea and has held that ss. 4 and 4-A of Act are invalid, and since two sections contain main operative provisions of Act, whole Act became invalid - Whether conclusion drawn by HC is correct or not? - It is duty of state legislature to ensure that public peace and tranquillity is not disturbed by prejudicial activities of criminals and undesirable characters in society - As court have indicated, infirmities in operative sections of Act are so serious that it would be impossible to hold that Act is saved u/art. 19(5) - Held, there is no doubt that if operative sections are invalid whole Act must fall - Thus, order passed by HC is confirmed - Appeal dismissed.
