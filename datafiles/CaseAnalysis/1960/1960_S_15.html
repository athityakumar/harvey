 Sarat Chandra Rabha and Others v Khagendranath Nath and Others

Supreme Court of India

27 October 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, J.L. Kapur, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, K. Subba Rao

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 434; AIR 1961 SC 334; 1963 (1) SCJ 796; [1961] 2 S.C.R. 133

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Summary: Election - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 7(b), 100(1)(b) and 123(5) - Whether nomination paper of appellant was wrongly rejected and whether corrupt practice was committed with consent of candidate? - Held, what s. 7(b) lays down is that there should be a conviction by a court in India for any offence and a sentence of imprisonment for not less than two years in order that a person may be disqualified for being chosen as a member of either House of Parliament or of legislative assembly or of legislative council of a state - Effect of an order of remission is to wipe out that part of sentence of imprisonment which has not been served out and thus in practice to reduce sentence to period already undergone, in law order of remission merely means that rest of sentence need not be undergone, leaving order of conviction by court and sentence passed by it untouched - Question whether such consent was given in circumstances of this case is a question of fact and not a mixed question of law and fact - Terms of s. 7(b) would be satisfied in present case and appellant being a person convicted and sentenced to three years' rigorous imprisonment would be disqualified, as five years had not passed since his release and as Election Commission had not removed his disqualification - Appeal dismissed.
