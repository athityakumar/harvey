 Vasantlal Maganbhai Sanjanwala v State of Bombay and Others(And Connected Appeal)

Supreme Court of India

25 August 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, J.L. Kapur, K. Subba Rao, Kailas Nath Wanchoo

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 437; AIR 1961 SC 4; 1961 (63) BomLR 138; 1961 (1) SCJ 394; [1961] 1 S.C.R. 341

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Rent Control

Keywords: Bombay General Act, 1904

Summary: Land & Property - Rent Control - Constitution - Bombay Tenancy and Agricultural Lands Act, 1948, s. 6(2) - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 31(b) and 254 - (A) Whether Bombay Tenancy and Agricultural Lands Act, 1948, s. 6(2) carries excessive delegation of power?- Held, no - In dealing with challenge to vires of any statute on ground of excessive delegation, it is necessary to enquire whether impugned delegation involves delegation of an essential legislative function or power and whether Legislature has enunciated its policy and principle and given guidance to delegatee - Having regard to legislative policy laid down by Bombay Tenancy and Agricultural Lands Act, 1948, in its Preamble and in other relevant sections and having regard to guidance which has been provided for fixing a reasonable rent u/s. 12 (3), it would not be possible to hold that power delegated to Provincial Govt. by s. 6(2) suffers from infirmity of excessive delegation - Fact that no minimum has been prescribed would not materially affect this position - Other guidelines are provided but minimum prescribed limit is not provided - It is not excessive delegation and not ultra vires constitution - Appeal dismissed.
