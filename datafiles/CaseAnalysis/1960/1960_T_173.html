 Lt. Col. Khajoor Singh v Union of India and Another

Supreme Court of India

01 December 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, K. Subba Rao, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, J.L. Kapur, K.C. Das Gupta, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 449; AIR 1961 SC 532; 1961 (2) MLJ(SC) 46; 1961 (2) SCJ 235; [1961] 2 S.C.R. 828

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Prohibition Act, Taxation on Income (Investigation Commission) Act, 1947, Board Of Revenue Act, 1913

Summary: Service - Constitution - Constitution of India , 1950, art. 226 and 300 - Code of Civil Procedure , 1908, O. 27, r. 3 - (A) Whether words of art. 226 mean that person or authority to whom a writ is to be issued has to be resident in or located within territories of High Court issuing writ ? - Held, what art. 226 requires is residence or location as a fact and if therefore there is a seat from which Govt. functions as a fact even though that seat is not mentioned in Constitution, HC within whose territories that seat is located will be HC having jurisdiction u/art. 226 so far as orders of Govt. as such are concerned - (B) Whether it is possible to introduce concept of cause of action in art. 226? - Further held, there is no place for introduction of concept of place where impugned order has effect or of concept of functioning of a Govt., apart from location of its office concerned with case, or even of concept of place where cause of action arises in art. 226 and that language of that article is plain - If any inconvenience is felt on account of this interpretation of art. 226, remedy seems to be a constitutional amendment - There is no scope for avoiding inconvenience by an interpretation which is not reasonable, on language of article, adopt and which language of article does not bear - Appeal dismissed.
