 Jagdish Prasad Saxena v State of Madhya Bharat

Supreme Court of India

28 October 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, A.K. Sarkar, K. Subba Rao, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 22; AIR 1961 SC 1070; 1961 JabLJ 414; 1963 (1) LLJ 325

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Departmental enquiry - Appellant was permanently employed in department of Customs and Excise - Whether decision of High Court to remove appellant from service, justified? - Departmental is a serious proceeding intended to give officer concerned a chance to meet charge and to prove his innocence - In taking disciplinary action against a public servant a proper departmental enquiry must be held against him after supplying him with chargesheet - Authorities failed to realise this - Held, HC was in error in assuming that no prejudice had been caused to appellant as a result of respondent's failure to hold an enquiry - Appeal allowed.
