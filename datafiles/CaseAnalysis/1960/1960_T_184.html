Alembic Chemical Works Company, Limited v Workmen

Supreme Court of India

15 December 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, K.C. Das Gupta

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 299; AIR 1961 SC 647; 1961 (2) FLR 191; 1961 (1) LLJ 328; [1961] 3 S.C.R. 297

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Practice & Procedure - Constitution - Factories Act, 1948, ss. 79(1), 78 and 84 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 10(1)(d) - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 136 - (A) Whether award of Industrial Tribunal providing for privilege leave in excess of that sanctioned by s. 79 (1) is without jurisdiction? - Held, s. 79(1) does not purport to standardise annual leave with wages - S. 78 recognises exceptions to leave prescribed by s. 79 (1) - Existing benefits would be equally true about benefits which may be granted by an employer to employees in future - Award is not without jurisdiction u/s. 79(1) - (B) Whether interference by Supreme Court in award of Industrial Tribunal regarding provision for leave is justifiable? - Held, Tribunal should not ignore consideration that unduly generous or liberal leave - Provisions would affect production - It appears that impugned provisions cannot be sustained on any reasonable ground or that they mark a violent departure from prevailing practice or trend - Supreme Court justified in interfering in award of Industrial Tribunal regarding leave provisions - Appeal dismissed.
