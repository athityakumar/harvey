P.C. Joshi and Another v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

25 October 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, S.K. Das

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 499; AIR 1961 SC 387; 1961 CRLJ 566; [1961] 2 S.C.R. 63

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Media & Entertainment; Practice & Procedure; Tort

Keywords: Defamatory Material, Defamatory Articles

Summary: Criminal - Media & Entertainment - Practice & Procedure - Tort - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898, ss. 198 and 198 B(3)(b) - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 500 - Complaint by Public Prosecutor for offence u/s 500, against appellants in respect of news item published - Complaint filed under signature of Home Secretary to Govt. of Uttar Pradesh sanctioning u/s. 198 B(3)(b) - Charges framed against appellants for offence of defamation - Challenged - If facts which constitute charge do not appear on face of sanction, it must be established by extraneous evidence that those facts were placed before authority competent to grant sanction and that authority applied his mind to those facts before giving sanction - Provisions of s. 198B does not provide that before taking cognizance of complaint, complaint shall be signed by any person other than Public Prosecutor - Held, Home Secy. was authorised to sanction a complaint for defamation of a Minister of Govt. of Uttar Pradesh - Complaint by Public Prosecutor u/s. 198B would undoubtedly lie in court of Session and complaint u/s. 198 would lie in court of a Magistrate because it is a Magistrate who alone can take cognizance of offence of defamation u/s. 198 - Contentions raised by appellants rejected - Appeal dismissed.
