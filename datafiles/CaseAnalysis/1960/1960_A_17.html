 J. V. Gokal and Company Private Limited v Assistant Collector of Sales Tax (Inspection)

Supreme Court of India

25 January 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, K.C. Das Gupta, J.C. Shah, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 3; AIR 1960 SC 595; 1999 (110) E.L.T. 106; 1960 SCJ 671; [1960] 2 S.C.R. 852; 1960 (11) STC 186

Case Digest

Subject: Customs

Summary: Customs - Constitution of India, 1950 - Companies Act, 1956 - Bill of lading - Customs barrier - Time - Petitioner was a private limited company under the Act - Petitioner entered into two contracts with the Govt. of India for selling to the Govt. of India two consignments of sugar - To fulfill the terms of contracts petitioner placed order with dealers in foreign countries - Petitioner delivered to its bankers along with the invoice the Documents of Title together with other papers and instructed the said Bankers to present the same to the Govt. of India from the Deputy Accountant General - Whether the sales by the petitioner to the Govt. of India are sales in the course of import - Held, the property in the goods passed to the Govt. of India when the shipping documents were delivered to them against payment - Sale of the goods by the petitioner to the Govt. of India took place when the goods were on the high seas - sales in question have taken place in the course of the import into India - They would be exempted from sales tax under Art. 286(1)(b) of the Constitution - Petition allowed.
