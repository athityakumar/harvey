 Mineral Development Limited, Calcutta v Union of India and Another

Supreme Court of India

31 August 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, J.L. Kapur, K. Subba Rao

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 225; AIR 1960 SC 1373; 1961 (1) SCJ 230; [1961] 1 S.C.R. 445

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property

Keywords: Mineral Concession Rules, Mines Concession Rules 1949, Mines and Minerals (Regulation and Development) Act

Summary: Land & Property - Mines and Minerals (Regulation and Development) Act, 1957 - Mineral Concession Rules, 1949 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882 - Constitution Of India, 1950 - Mines Concession Rules, 1949 - Whether term 'mining lease' as defined in s. 3(d) of Act includes a sub-lease? - Held, there is no specific mention of a sub-lease in s. 3(d) - Definition does not require that lessor must be proprietor; and so on a fair reading it would include a lease executed by proprietor as much as a lease executed by lessee from such a proprietor - Therefore, document may be termed a sub-lease in view of fact that transferor is not owner of property transferred but is itself a lessee, transaction between appellant and Bhagat Singh is nothing but a mining lease - Wordings of s. 3(d) and object and purpose for which Act was passed, it is clear that a mining sub-lease is included within definition of term 'mining lease' and there is nothing in Act which militates against this - Appeal dismissed.
