Printers (Mysore) Private Limited v Pothan Joseph

Supreme Court of India

27 April 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, K.C. Das Gupta, Kailas Nath Wanchoo

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 400; AIR 1960 SC 1156; 1962 (1) MLJ(SC) 127; 1962 (1) SCJ 274; [1960] 1 S.C.R. 713

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Constitution

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration Act, 1940 - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 133(1) - Whether it is an error in refusing to stay a suit filed if requisite conditions specified in arbitration agreement are met? - Section clearly contemplates that, even though there is an arbitration agreement and requisite conditions specified by it are satisfied, court may nevertheless refuse to grant stay if it is satisfied that there are sufficient reasons why matter should not be referred in accordance with arbitration agreement - Held, power to stay legal proceedings is discretionary, and so a party to an arbitration agreement against whom legal proceedings have been commenced cannot by relying on arbitration agreement claim stay of legal proceedings instituted in a court as a matter of right - Appeal dismissed.
