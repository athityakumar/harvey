BAYYANA BHIMAYYA v GOVERNMENT of ANDHRA PRADESH

Supreme Court of India

14 December 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.L. Kapur, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 405; AIR 1961 SC 1065; 1961 (1) MLJ(SC) 196; 1961 (1) SCJ 636; [1961] 3 S.C.R. 267; 1961 (12) STC 147

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Sales Tax; Water; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Madras General Sales Tax Act, 1939

Summary: Sales Tax - Indirect Tax - Madras General Sales Tax Act, 1939 - Sale of Goods Act - Indian Sale of Goods Act, 1930 - Whether tax authorities had erred in treating transaction between appellants and third parties as a fresh sale? - These two deliveries might synchronise in point of time, but were separate, in point of fact and in eye of law - There being two separate transactions of sale, tax was payable at both points - Held, tax authorities had not erred in treating transaction between appellants and third parties as fresh sale - Appeal dismissed.
