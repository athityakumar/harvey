 State of Orissa and Another v Messrs Chakobhai Ghelabhai and Company

Supreme Court of India

20 September 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

S.K. Das, Mohammad Hidayatullah, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, K.C. Das Gupta, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 238; AIR 1961 SC 284; 1961 (2) SCJ 95; [1961] 1 S.C.R. 719; 1960 (11) STC 716

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Keywords: Orissa Sales Tax Rules, 1947, Government of India Act 1935, Orissa Sales Tax Rules 1947

Summary: Indirect Tax - Sales Tax - Practice & Procedure - Orissa Sales Tax Act, 1947, ss. 2(g) and 12(5) - (A) Whether admission by a lawyer that sale was completed in a State has any binding affect on assessee? - Held, admission of assessee's pleader that sale was completed in State of Orissa brings sale within s. 2(g) of Act - Liable to tax - Binding on assessee - (B) Whether notice issued in form VI u/s. 12(5) of Orissa Sales Tax Act, 1947 is invalid if unnecessary words are not scored out? - Further held, failure on part of assessing authority to score out unnecessary words in notice issued in form No VI under s. 12(5) does not make notice bad in law - Appeal allowed.
