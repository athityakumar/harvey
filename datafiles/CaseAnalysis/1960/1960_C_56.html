Provat Kumar Mitter v Commissioner of Income Tax, West Bengal

Supreme Court of India

08 December 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

S.K. Das, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 218; AIR 1961 SC 1019; [1961] 41 ITR 624; 1962 (2) MLJ(SC) 119; 1962 (2) SCJ 496; [1961] 3 S.C.R. 37

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income-tax Act, 1922, ss. 16(1)(c), 16(3) - Dividend Liability - Deed - Term of natural life - Settlement -Transfer - Assignment by shareholder of right to dividend Liability to tax of such share-holder - Appellant who was registered holder of 500 shares of company executed a deed by which he assigned to his wife right, title and interest to all dividends and sums of money which might be declared or might become due on account or in respect of those shares for term of her natural life - During the accounting year which ended, dividend declared on shares amounted and assessing appellant for assessment year 1953-54 Income-tax Officer included said sum in his income under s. 16(i)(c) and s. 16(3) of Act, 1922 - Whether appellant settlement was for lifetime of his wife, third proviso to s. 16(i)(c) applied and dividend which his wife received could not be deemed to be his income under s. 16(i)(c), and that s. 16(3) of Act was not applicable - Held, deed was not a transfer of any existing property of appellant namely, shares by him, but only a contract to transfer or make over in future every dividend and sum of money which may be declared or become due and payable on account or in respect of shares, to his wife during her lifetime - Since company could pay dividend only to registered shareholder or under his orders, income continued to accrue to appellant though applied subsequently towards payment to wife under terms of contract - Income was assessable in hands of appellant - Appeal allowed.
