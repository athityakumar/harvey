 H. C. Narayanappa and Others v State of Mysore and Others

Supreme Court of India

28 April 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Syed Jaffer Imam, A.K. Sarkar, K. Subba Rao

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 180; AIR 1960 SC 1073; 1961 (1) MLJ(SC) 5; 1961 (1) SCJ 7; [1960] 3 S.C.R. 742

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Constitution; Defence & Security Forces; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1939 - Constitution (Seventh Amendment) Act, 1956 - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 19 (6), 68 C and 298 - Govt. of India Act, 1935 - On D\- 13-01-1959, 2nd respondent published a scheme in exercise of powers conferred by s. 68C of Motor Vehicles Act, 1939, for exclusion of private operators on certain routes and reservation of those routes for State transport undertaking in Anekal area - (A) Whether scheme violates equal protection clause of Constitution? - Held, column 1 of approved scheme undoubtedly describes area in relation to which scheme is approved, but by designation of area, in scheme, an intention to exclude either wholly or partially operators of stage carriages from that area is not evinced either expressly or by implication - (B) Whether in absence of legislation expressly undertaken by State of Mysore in that behalf, that State was incompetent to enter into arena of motor transport business to exclusion of private operators? - Held, right of State to carry on trade or business is recognised by art. 298; authority to exclude competitors in field of such trade or business is conferred on State by entrusting power to enact laws under entry 21 of List III of Seventh Schedule, and exercise of that power in context of fundamental rights is secured from attack by art. 19(6), and scheme framed u/s 68 C may properly be regarded as within meaning of art. 19(6) made by State - Petition dismissed.
