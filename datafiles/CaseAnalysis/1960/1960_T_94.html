 K.M. Nanavati v State of Bombay

Supreme Court of India

05 September 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, K. Subba Rao, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 186; AIR 1961 SC 112; 1961 CRLJ 173; 1961 (2) SCJ 100; [1961] 1 S.C.R. 497

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Keywords: Power Of The Court, Navy Act, 1957, Code of Criminal Procedure 1898, Supreme Court Rules 1950

Summary: Constitution - Criminal - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 72, 129, 141, 142, 144, 145, 154, 161 and 245 - Code of Criminal Procedure,1973 ss. 307, 337, 338, 358, 401, 402, 411 and 426 - Govt. Of India Act 1935, s. 295 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Navy Act,ss. 3(12), 89(2), (3) - Supreme Court Rules, O. XXI, r.5, O.XLV, rr.2 and 5 - Whether order of suspension passed by Governor u/art. 161 could operate when Supreme Court had been moved for granting special leave to appeal from judgment and order of High Court? - Power given to Governor in regard to pardons is a specific power specially conferred as was vested in colonial and British Governors in Indian provinces during British days - Power given to court u/art. 142(1) is a general power exercisable for doing complete justice in any cause or matter - If arts. 161 and 142(1) deal with same subject matter then art. 161 must prevail over art. 142(1) which is in accord with constitutional position - Held, (majority view) order of Governor granting suspension of sentence could only operate until matter became sub judice in Supreme Court on filing of petition for special leave to appeal - Petition dismissed.
