Seth Mathuradas v Commissioner of Income Tax, M. P. and Bhopal

Supreme Court of India

22 November 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

J.L. Kapur, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 319

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Smallness of profit

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Companies Act, 1956 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 25(4), 25A, 66(2) - Commercial profit - Smallness of profit - Computation - M/s. Bipinchandra Maganlal & Co. Ltd/company purchased certain machinery for Rs. 89, 000 and sold it sometime in March, 1947, for price for which it was originally purchased - Trading profits of company as disclosed by its books of account for year 1946-47 were Rs. 33,245/- - Income-tax Officer computed assessable income of company for year of account 1946-47 at Rs. 48,761/- after adding back to profit of Rs. 33,245 returned by company, Rs. 15,608/- realized in excess of written down value of machinery sold in March, 1947 and passed an order - Company preferred appeal before Appellate Assistant Commissioner and same was allowed - Aggrieved officer appealed before Appellate Tribunal - Appellate Tribunal at instance of company referred to HC at Bombay u/ss. 66(1), 23A(1) of the Act - HC held that amount of Rs. 15, 608 was not liable to be taken into account in considering whether having regard to smallness of profit made by company, it would be unreasonable to declare larger dividend and passed order in favor of Income tax officer - Hence instant appeal - Whether sum of Rs. 15, 608 should have been included in assessee company's 'profit' for purpose of determining whether payment of a larger dividend than that declared by it would be unreasonable - Held, Legislature had deliberately used expression 'smallness of profit' and not 'smallness of assessable income' and there was nothing in context in which expression 'smallness of profit' occurs which justifies equation of expression 'profit' with assessable income Smallness of profit in s. 23A had to be adjudged in light of commercial principles and not in light of total receipts, actual or fictional - View appeared to have been taken by HC's in India without any dissentient opinion Sir Kasturchand Ltd. v. Commissioner of Income-tax, Ezra Proprietary Estates Ltd. v. Commissioner of Income-tax and Commissioner of Income-tax v. F. L. Smidth & Co. (Bombay) Ltd, by fiction in s. 10(2)(vii), second proviso, r/w. s. 2(6C), what was really not income was, for purpose of computation of assessable income, made taxable income, but on that account, it does not become commercial profit, and if it was not commercial profit, it was not liable to be taken into account in assessing whether in view of smallness of profits larger dividend would be unreasonable - HC was right in holding that amount of Rs. 15, 608 was not liable to be taken into account in considering whether having regard to smallness of profit made by company, it would be unreasonable to declare larger dividend - Appeal Dismissed.
