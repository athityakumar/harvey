Commissioner of Income Tax, Bombay City, Bombay v Nandlal Gandalal

Supreme Court of India

21 April 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

S.K. Das, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 176; AIR 1960 SC 1147; [1960] 40 ITR 1; 1961 (1) SCJ 342; [1960] 3 S.C.R. 620

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Excess Profits Tax

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 4 A(b) - (A) Whether joint family was a resident in taxable territories, by virtue of cl. (b) of s. 4 A? - Held, that residence u/s. 4 A(b) of a Hindu joint family is determined by seat of control and management of its affairs and that undivided family as such exercised no control or management, even partly, over partnership business in taxable territories and it could not be said that this business was affair of joint family - Undivided family represented by co -parceners in partnership was not a resident in taxable territories - Appeal dismissed.
