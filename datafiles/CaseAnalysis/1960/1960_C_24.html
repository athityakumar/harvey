Hoshiarpur Electric Supply Compnay v Commisoner of income tax

Supreme Court of India

06 December 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 193; AIR 1961 SC 892; [1961] 41 ITR 608; 1961 (2) SCJ 297; [1961] 2 S.C.R. 956

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Land Acquisition

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Service line - Trading receipt - Taxable income - Capital - Assessee/ Electric Supply Company is a licensee of an electricity undertaking - In the year of account April 1, 1947 March 31, 1948, assessee received Rs. 12,530 for new service connections granted to its customers - Out of this amount Rs. 5,929 were spent for laying the service lines and Rs. 1,338 were spent for laying certain mains - Income Tax Officer treated the entire amount of Rs. 12,530 as trading receipt - On Appeal to the Appellate Assistant Commissioner cost incurred for laying service lines and mains was excluded and the balance was treated as taxable income - Whether assessee's receipts from consumers for laying service lines were trading receipts and whether the profit element of service connection receipts minus service connection cost was taxable income in the assessee's hands- Held assessee has installed machinery for producing electrical energy and has also laid mains and distributing lines for supplying it to its customers - Assessee only spends a part of the amount received by it from the consumers - By the installation of the service lines a capital asset is brought into existence - Receipts though related to the business of the assessee as distributors of electricity were not incident nor in the course of the carrying on the assessee's business they were receipts for bringing into existence capital of lasting value - Total receipts being capital receipts the fact that in the installation of capital only a certain amount was immediately expended the balance remaining in hand could not be regarded as profit in the nature of a trading receipt - Appeal allowed.
