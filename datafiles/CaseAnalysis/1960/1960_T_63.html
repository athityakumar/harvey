Lord Krishna Textile Mills v Its Workmen

Supreme Court of India

12 December 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 290; AIR 1961 SC 860; 1961 (2) FLR 112; 1961 (1) LLJ 211; 1961 (2) SCJ 300; [1961] 3 S.C.R. 204

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Trade Union Act, 1926, misa, United Provinces Industrial Disputes Act 1947 (UP 28 of 1947) (ENG)

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 33(2)(b) - (A) Whether Industrial Tribunal can consider application to approve order of dismissal passed against workmen? - Held, authority can enquire whether conditions prescribed by s. 33(2)(b) and proviso are satisfied or not - Approving authority has to consider whether standing orders justify order of dismissal - Whether an enquiry has been held as provided by standing order? - Whether wages for month have been paid as required by proviso? - Whether an application has been made as prescribed by proviso? - When considerations are fulfilled tribunal has to give approval tribunals order is erroneous if it is without jurisdiction - Appeal allowed.
