 Shri Balwan Singh v Shri Lakshmi Narain and Others

Supreme Court of India

23 February 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, A.K. Sarkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Syed Jaffer Imam, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 415; AIR 1960 SC 770; 1960 (2) MLJ(SC) 27; 1960 SCJ 716; [1960] 3 S.C.R. 91

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Election; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Election - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 227 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 123(5) and 116A - Whether appellant and/or his election agent and/or any other persons with his consent, had committed corrupt practices and result of election was materially affected by such corrupt practices committed in his interest? - A corrupt practice is committed not by conveying voter but by act of hiring or procuring conveyance - Insistence upon full particulars of corrupt practices is undoubtedly of paramount importance in trial of an election petition, but if parties go to trial despite absence of full particulars of corrupt practice alleged, and evidence of contesting parties is led on plea raised by petition, petition cannot thereafter be dismissed for want of particulars, because defect is one of procedure and not one of jurisdiction of Tribunal to adjudicate upon plea in absence of particulars - Appellate court may be justified in setting aside judgment of Tribunal if it is satisfied that by reason of absence of full particulars, material prejudice has resulted; and in considering whether material prejudice has resulted failure to raise and press objection about absence of particulars before going to trial must be given due weight - Held, appellant was entitled to particulars want of which he now complains - Appeal dismissed.
