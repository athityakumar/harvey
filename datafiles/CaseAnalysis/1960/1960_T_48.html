Commissioner of Income Tax, West Bengal v Royal Calcutta Turf Club

Supreme Court of India

28 November 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

J.L. Kapur, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 276; AIR 1961 SC 1028; [1961] 41 ITR 414; 1961 (1) SCJ 632; [1961] 2 S.C.R. 729

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Business Profits-tax Act, 1947

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 10(2)(xv) - Whether in facts and circumstances of this case, appellate tribunal was right in holding that Rs. 61,818 spent by assessee to train Indian boys as jockeys, did not constitute expenses of business of assessee allowable u/s. 10(2)(xv)? - Held, that amount in dispute was laid out wholly and exclusively for purpose of assessee's business because if supply of jockeys with efficiency and skill failed, business of assessee would no longer be possible - Money was spent for preservation of assessee's business, and was allowable u/s. 10(2)(xv) - Appeal dismissed.
