 Babu Barkya Thakur v State of Bombay and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 August 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Syed Jaffer Imam, A.K. Sarkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 461; AIR 1960 SC 1203; 1961 (2) SCJ 392; [1961] 1 S.C.R. 128

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property -Constitution - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4 and 40 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 19 and 31 - By a notification D/-03-04-1959, first respondent, State of Bombay (now Maharashtra) u/s. 4 of Land Acquisition Act of 1894, stated that lands specified in schedule attached to said notification were likely to be needed for purposes of third respondent, Messrs Mukund Iron and Steel Works Ltd,for its factory buildings etc - (A) Whether notification u/s. 4 of Act is illegal? - Held, it is argued that in terms notification does not state that land sought to be acquired was needed for a public purpose - It is not absolutely necessary to validity of land acquisition proceedings that that statement should find a place in notification actually issued - It is not correct to say that any defect in notification u/s. 4 is fatal to validity of proceedings, particularly when acquisition is for a Company and purpose has to be investigated u/s. 5A or s. 40 necessarily after notification u/s. 4 of Act - (B) Whether land acquisition proceedings are in violation of arts. 14, 19 and 31 of Constitution and that acquisition is not for a public purpose and is mala fide? - Further held, even if Act contemplated acquisition for a company which may or may not be for a public purpose, it would be saved by art. 31(5)(a) as an existing law - It is not necessary for purposes of this case to go into question whether acquisition for a Company, even apart from provisions of s. 40, will be for a public purpose, or justifiable under provisions of Act, even on assumption that it will not serve a public purpose - Facts of present case have not been investigated, as Court was moved when only a notification u/s. 4 of Act had been issued and purpose of acquisition in question was still at enquiry stage - Petition dismissed.
