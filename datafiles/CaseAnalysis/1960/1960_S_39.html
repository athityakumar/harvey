Shri Ram and Others v State of Bombay

Supreme Court of India

05 December 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, Syed Jaffer Imam, Raghubar Dayal

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 188; AIR 1961 SC 674; 1961 (63) BomLR 434; 1961 CRLJ 760; 1961 (1) MLJ(SC) 221; 1961 (1) SCJ 677; [1961] 2 S.C.R. 890

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Committal Proceedings, Code of Criminal Procedure 1898

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898, s. 207 A(4) - (A) Can Magistrate discharge or commit accused merely on basis of documents referred to in s. 173 of CrPC, 1898?- Held, u/s. 207 A, the Magistrate is bound to take evidence of only such eye-witnesses as are actually produced by prosecution in court - If prosecution has not examined any eye-witnesses, Magistrate has jurisdiction to discharge or commit accused to sessions merely on basis of documents referred to in s. 173 - Appeal dismissed.
