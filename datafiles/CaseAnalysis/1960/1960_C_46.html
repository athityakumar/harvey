 Sardar Baldev Singh v Commissioner of Income Tax, Delhi and Ajmer

Supreme Court of India

02 September 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sarkar, K. Subba Rao, Syed Jaffer Imam, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 336; AIR 1961 SC 736; [1960] 40 ITR 605; 1961 (1) SCJ 512; [1961] 1 S.C.R. 482

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Tax - Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 22(2), 23A, 34 - Fresh assessment - Agricultural income - Evasion of Tax - Shareholder - Dividend - Appellant assessed to income-tax by Income-tax Officer for A/y 1944-45 an income of Rs. 49,047/- - Appellant held shares in a company, other shares held by A and B - In annual general meeting , accounts passed but no dividend declared though accounts disclosed large profits - Income-tax Officer passed an order u/s. 23A of the Act that Rs. 14,23,110/- being undistributed portion of assessable income of company for year ending after deductions to had been distributed as dividend among three shareholders - As a result of order a sum of appellants share directed to be distributed to be included in income of appellant for A/y - Income-tax Officer, issued notice to appellant requiring him to file within thirty-five days a revised return as a part of his income for that year had escaped assessment - Appellant submitted a revised return and earlier assessment was re-opened, made fresh assessment order - Appeal dismissed - Further appeal to unsuccessful - Whether proceedings taken against appellant u/s. 34 of the Act was valid - Held, appellant did applied to Tribunal to refer to HC any question of law that arose out of former's decision and act itself gave no right of appeal at all from that decision nor any other remedy against it - Appellant had to raise objection that proceedings u/s. 34 of the Act could not be taken at 'D' within 35 days mentioned in notice - Contention of appellant was without foundation and s. 34 provided income might be assessed or reassessed and provisions of the Act shall so far as may be apply accordingly as if notice issued u/s. 22(2) of Act and where an assessment was to be made pursuant to a notice u/s. 22(2) of the Act had to be determined u/s. 64 of the Act - Indeed was only provision in the Act for deciding proper place for any assessment - Income should be deemed to had been in existence on date mentioned for purpose of assessment to tax and if assessment for relevant year did not include that income it has escaped assessment - Appeal dismissed.
