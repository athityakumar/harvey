 State of Punjab v Sodhi Sukhdev Singh

Supreme Court of India

15 November 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, K. Subba Rao, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 262; AIR 1961 SC 493; 1961 (2) MLJ 203; 1961 (2) MLJ(SC) 203; 1961 (2) SCJ 691; [1961] 2 S.C.R. 371

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 123 and 162 - Whether Court can examine documents purported to be affairs of State u/s. 123 keeping in view s. 162 of Act 1872? - A fair and reasonable construction of two provisions must be had - 'Maxim silus populi est supreme les' which means that regard for public welfare is highest law is basis of provisions contained in s. 123 - Care must be taken to see that other interests do not masquerade as public interest and take undue advantage - Preliminary enquiry as contained in s. 162(1) has to be held by court, and it is after court has found in favour of character of document pleaded by state that occasion arises for head of department to exercise his discretion conferred by s. 123 - Held, dealing with question of privilege it is not a part of court's jurisdiction to decide whether disclosure of given document would lead to any public injury that is a matter for head of department to consider and decide - Also held that preliminary enquiry where character of documents falls to be considered is within jurisdiction and competence of court within purview of s. 162- Appeal allowed.
