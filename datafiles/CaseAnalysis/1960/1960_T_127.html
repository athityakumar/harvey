 T. Cajee v U. Jormanik Siem and Another

Supreme Court of India

20 September 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, J.L. Kapur, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, K. Subba Rao

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 240; AIR 1961 SC 276; 1961 (2) FLR 304; 1961 (1) LLJ 652; [1961] 1 S.C.R. 750

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Parliament & Legislature

Keywords: Assam (Alteration Of Boundaries) Act, 1951

Summary: Administrative - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950 - Appointment - Removal - Justifiability - Respondent was Siem of Mylliem siemship and elected by Myntries and people according to custom - After constitution of District Council, siemship was brought under it and respondent continued to discharge administrative and judicial functions - Siem once appointed could not be removed from office except through referendum of people, according to custom until such custom was changed by legislation passed by district council - Chief Executive Member alleged charges against respondent and asked to appear before Durbar, consequently respondent was suspended from his office - Aggrieved respondent filed writ petition before High court which was allowed and directed that order of suspension from office was not given effect - Hence, instant appeal by appellant. Whether respondent being an officer appointed to carry on the administration by the District Council could be removed by it in accordance with the terms and conditions of his appointment.

Held, in instant case terms and conditions communicated to respondent do not indicate an express term giving power to the District Council to make an order of interim suspension while inquiring into the conduct of the respondent with a view to his ultimate removal. No statute or rules framed there under have been brought to our notice which authorised interim suspension having the effect of withholding remuneration in whole or in part. Therefore though an order of interim suspension could be made against the respondent while inquiry into his conduct with a view to his ultimate removal is going on, his remuneration according to the terms and conditions communicated to him cannot be withheld unless there is some statute or rules framed thereunder which would justify the withholding of the whole or part of the remuneration. Further, there is no statute or rule there under the remuneration cannot be withheld from the respondent even though an order of interim suspension, in the sense he is told not to do the work of his office, may be made against him. The order of interim suspension therefore passed would be valid subject of course to the respondent being paid the full remuneration unless the District Council can legitimately withhold the whole or part of it under some statute or rules framed thereunder, there being undoubtedly DO express contract to that effect in this case. Hence, there is now a valid law empowering the District Council to remove a Siem; and, as the enquiry in question is only at its initial stage, it can hereafter be validly conducted under the provisions of the said Act.

Appeal allowed.
