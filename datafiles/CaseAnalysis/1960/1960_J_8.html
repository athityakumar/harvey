Sahebzada Mohammad Kamgar Shah v Jagdish Chandra Deo Dhabal Deoand Others

Supreme Court of India

21 April 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

K.C. Das Gupta, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 439; AIR 1960 SC 953; 1962 (1) SCJ 260; [1960] 3 S.C.R. 604

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Mines & Minerals

Summary: Land & Property -Mines & Minerals - Limitation Act, s. 19 - Receiver who was only contesting defendant contended that lessor had dispossessed him from part of leasehold property and so there ought to be total suspension of rents and royalties - Though written statement itself did not in terms mention nature of lessee's dispossession from leasehold property, definite case at trial was that this dispossession was in respect of minerals which had been specifically excluded from earlier lease of 1900 but according to defendant included in later lease - (A) Whether defendant was entitled to suspension of rents and royalties as claimed? - Held, on a proper construction of document as a whole, intention of parties has been clearly established to be that minerals excluded by cl. 16 of principal lease will remain excluded from later lease also, there is no scope of any benefit accruing to lessee by reason of rule that all deeds are to be construed strictly against grantor and in favour of grantee - (B) Whether plaintiff's claim is barred by limitation as regards period of claim prior to 12-08-1935? - Further held, suit is based on second lease of 1919 which was executed in favour of then Receiver - Acknowledgments by which limitation is claimed to have been saved is by a previous Receiver of Estate through whom appellant who is present Receiver has derived his liability to pay debt - S. 19 is therefore in terms applicable as acknowledgments have been signed personally by those previous Receivers and no recourse is needed by plaintiff to second part of Explanation II - There can be no doubt that acknowledgments on which plaintiff relies are acknowledgments within meaning of s. 19 of Limitation Act and save limitation in respect of period prior to 12-08-1935 - Appeal dismissed.
