Sanwat Singh and Others v State of Rajasthan

Supreme Court of India

09 December 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, Syed Jaffer Imam, Raghubar Dayal

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 446; AIR 1961 SC 715; 1961 CRLJ 766; 1961 (2) SCJ 179; [1961] 3 S.C.R. 120

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 148, 149 and 304 -Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Appeal against conviction - (A) Whether High Court correctly reappreciated evidence and convicted persons accused - Held, yes - Sessions Court did not consider voluminous evidence in support of case and rejected evidence of three main witnesses - Further held, Appellate court has full power to review evidence upon which order of acquittal is founded giving due weightage and consideration to following factors - (1) views of trial Judge as to the credibility of witnesses, (2) presumption of innocence in favour of accused, which is not weakened by fact that he has been acquitted at his trial, (3) right of accused to benefit of any doubt, and (4) slowness of an appellate court in disturbing a finding of fact arrived at by a Judge who had advantage of seeing witnesses - Power of an Appellate court in an appeal against acquittal is not different from that it has in an appeal against conviction - HC on substantial and compelling reasons rejected finding of Sessions Court - (B) Whether appellants have made out any case for interference with judgment of HC u/art 136 of Constitution - Further held, no - Art. 136 is couched in widest terms, Supreme Court should not to interfere on questions of fact except in exceptional cases when finding is such that it shocks conscience of court - HC has rghtly set aside acquittal and has also given reasons for same - Appeal dismissed.
