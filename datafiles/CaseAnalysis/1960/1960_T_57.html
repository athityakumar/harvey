 Jadab Singh and Others v Himachal Pradesh Administrationand Another

Supreme Court of India

28 April 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, K. Subba Rao, K.C. Das Gupta

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 181; AIR 1960 SC 1008; 1961 (1) SCJ 185; [1960] 3 S.C.R. 755

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Election; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Election - Constitution (Fifth Amendment) Act, 1955 - Constitution (Fourth Amendment) Act, 1955 - Govt. of Part C States Act, 1951, s. 4 - Himachal Pradesh and Bilaspur (New State) Act, 1954 - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 14, 19, 31, 32 and 240 - Constitution (Seventh Amendment) Act, 1956 - Representation of People Act, 1951, s. 74 - Whether Abolition Act could be recognised as a piece of legislation validly enacted? - Held, legislative competence of Parliament to enact Act is not open to challenge - There is no absolute bar against authority of Parliament to enact legislation which takes away vested rights provided legislation falls within any of legislative lists within competence of Parliament and it does not infringe any of fundamental rights of citizens - A statute object of which is to bring about agrarian reform by transferring interest of land-owners to tenants falls within class of statutes contemplated by art. 31-A(a) and is protected from attack that it violates fundamental rights enshrined in arts. 14, 19 and 31 of Constitution - Validity of provisions of act were not liable to be challenged - Petition dismissed.
