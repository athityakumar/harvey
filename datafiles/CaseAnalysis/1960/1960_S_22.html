Setabganj Sugar Mills Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax, Central, Calcutta

Supreme Court of India

17 November 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.L. Kapur, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 317; AIR 1961 SC 360; [1961] 41 ITR 272; 1961 (1) MLJ(SC) 136; 1961 (1) SCJ 520; [1961] 2 S.C.R. 488

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 24(2) and 66(2) - Whether the High Court can decline to call for a statement of the case on the proved facts? - Held, it's a mixed question of law & fact - Certain principles applied to determine whether on the facts found a legal inference can be drawn that the different ventures constitute separate businesses or not - Held, when the true facts have been determined, the ultimate conclusion is a legal inference from proved facts - It is one of mixed law and fact, on which depends the application of s. 24 (2), and the HC cannot u/s. 66 (2), decline to call for a statement of the case on the proved facts - HC cannot, u/s. 66 (2), decline to call for a statement of the case on the proved facts - Appeal dismissed.
