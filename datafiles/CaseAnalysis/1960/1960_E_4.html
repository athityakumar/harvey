Commissioner of Income Tax, Bombay City v Elphinstone Spinning and Weaving Mills Company Limited

Supreme Court of India

04 May 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, S.K. Das, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 350; AIR 1960 SC 1016; [1960] 40 ITR 142; 1961 (1) SCJ 158; [1960] 3 S.C.R. 953

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922 - Indian Finance Act, 1951, para B of Part I of the sch. 1 - Assessee company declared dividend when after allowance of depreciation profits earned during the relevant previous year was converted to loss for income tax purpose - Issue - Whether assessee company was liable to pay additional income tax? - Contention of the appellant was that even a loss may be a total income - Respondent contended that there was no income which was taxable - It was observed that 'dividends payable out of such profits' can only apply when there are profits and not when there are no profits - Legislature did not contemplate the imposition of tax in the circumstances - Imposition of the additional income tax is conditioned by the existence of income and profits - Held, assessee company was not liable to pay additional tax - Appeal dismissed.
