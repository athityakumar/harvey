State of Uttar Pradesh and Others v Raja Syed Mohammad Saadat Ali Khan and Others

Supreme Court of India

28 July 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

S.K. Das, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 306; AIR 1960 SC 1283; 1961 ALJ 79; [1961] 41 ITR 737; 1961 (2) SCJ 35; [1961] 1 S.C.R. 82

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - United Provinces Agricultural Income-tax Act, Act III of 1949, ss. 3 and 14 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Tax assessment - Imposition of Tax - Sustainability - Legislature of the United Provinces enacted Act, authorising imposition of tax on agricultural income within appellant - By s. 3 of the Act, the liability to pay agricultural income-tax and super-tax at rates specified in the schedule therein was charged on the total agricultural income of the previous year of every person - By s. 14 of Act, Collector and the Assistant Collector were for the purposes of the Act declared to be the assessing authorities within their respective revenue jurisdictions - Claiming to exercise the authority of the Collector tinder s. 14 of Act, Additional Collector assessed the respondent's/assessee's net agricultural income at Rs. 2,81,110-10-3 and ordered him to pay Rs. 1,36,390-2-0 as agricultural income-tax and super-tax - Validity of such order was challenged by the assessee by an application u/art. 226 of the Constitution before the HC - Contention of respondent that the Additional Collector was not authority competent bylaw to assess the agricultural income-tax under Act was upheld by HC - HC issued writ of certiorari quashing the order of the Additional Collector, because in its opinion, where property of assessee was situate in several districts, Collector as the assessing authority under Act exercised "extra-territorial" jurisdiction, but as Additional Collector was not invested with that extraterrestrial jurisdiction, impugned proceeding assessing agricultural income-tax was unauthorized - Whether HC rightly awarded judgement in favour of respondent - Held, SC was seized of an appeal from the order of the HC quashing the assessment on the ground that the Additional Collector had no extra-territorial authority to assess agricultural income-tax - It was true that Act was amended after the HC delivered its judgment but in dealing with this appeal, SC was bound to consider the amended law as it stood and to give effect to it, having regard to the clearly expressed intention of the legislature in the amended provisions - Accordingly SC held that the Additional Collector was competent to assess the liability of the assessee to pay agricultural income-tax and super-tax under Act - Appeal allowed.
