Associated Cement Company Limited v Shri P. D. Vyas and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 February 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, K.C. Das Gupta

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 424; AIR 1960 SC 665; 1960 (62) BomLR 563; 1961 (2) FLR 569; 1960 (1) LLJ 563; 1960 (2) MLJ(SC) 107; 1960 SCJ 1269; [1960] 2 S.C.R. 974

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Industrial Employment (Standing Order ) Act, 1946, Industrial Employment (Standing Orders) Act

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Standing Order - Modification - Industrial Employment (Standing Orders) Act, ss. 2 and 3 - Whether respondents were competent enough to make modifications in draft standing orders submitted by appellants? - It is practicable to insist upon conformity with model standing order in regard to matters in dispute by making suitable modifications - Held, standing Orders can be modified in matters covered by model standing orders if it satisfies that conformity with standing order is practicable and reasonable - True effect of provisions of Act - Appeal dismissed.
