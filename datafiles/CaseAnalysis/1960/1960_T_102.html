K. R. C. S. Balakrishna Chetty and Sons and Company v State of Madras

Supreme Court of India

29 November 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

J.L. Kapur, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 277; AIR 1961 SC 1152; 1961 (1) MLJ(SC) 207; 1961 (1) SCJ 712; [1961] 2 S.C.R. 736; 1961 (12) STC 114

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Keywords: Madras General Sales Tax Act, 1939

Summary: Sales Tax - Madras General Sales Tax Act, 1939, s. 5 - License exempted appellants from assessment to sales tax on sale of cotton yarn 'subject to such restrictions and conditions' prescribed including conditions as to license and license fees - Whether observance of conditions of license was necessary for availability of exemption u/s. 5 or having a licence was sufficient for purpose of exemption? - Exemption under licence is conditional upon observance of conditions prescribed and upon restrictions which are imposed by and under Act and mere holding of licence is not sufficient - Held, appellants have been found to have contravened provisions of Act as well as rules and therefore it cannot be said that they have observed conditions upon which exemption under licence is available - Appellants were held as not exempt from assessment under Act - Appeal dismissed.
