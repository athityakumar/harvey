 M. Pentiah and Others v Muddala Veeramallappa and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 November 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, A.K. Sarkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 433; AIR 1961 SC 1107; 1963 (1) SCJ 768; [1961] 2 S.C.R. 295

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Hyderabad District Municipalities Act, 1956

Summary: Constitution - Municipalities & Local Governments - Constitution of India, 1950 - Hyderabad District Municipalities Act, 1956 - Initially appellants filed the W.P. contending that the respondents ceased to be members of the Municipal Committee on the expiry of three years from the date the 1956 Act came into force and that, therefore, they had no right to sell the land, and that, in any view, the sale made by the respondents, of the property acquired for the purpose of constructing a market, was ultra vires the provisions of the 1956 Act - The said petition was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal against the order of the dismissal by HC - Whether, the contention of the appellant was sustainable and HC erred while dismissing the petition of the appellant - Held, under the provisions of s. 35 of the 1956 Act the respondents were continue to hold office since, the first meeting of the Committee was not called u/s. 35 of 1956 Act - S. 77 of the 1956 Act confers an express power on the Municipal Committee to sell property subject to the conditions mentioned therein, hence, the impugned sales were not ultra vires the powers of the Committee and in view of the said express power, no prohibition could be implied from the provisions of s. 76 of the 1956 Act - And HC had rightly dismissed the petition of appellant - Appeal dismissed.
