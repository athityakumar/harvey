Messrs Piyare Lal Adishwar Lal v Commissioner of Income Tax, Delhi

Supreme Court of India

26 April 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

J.L. Kapur, S.K. Das, Mohammad Hidayatullah

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 177; AIR 1960 SC 997; [1960] 40 ITR 17; 1960 (2) MLJ(SC) 111; 1960 SCJ 1282; [1960] 3 S.C.R. 669

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Indirect Tax; Service

Keywords: Master And Servant Relationship

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Excess Profits Tax Act, 1940, s.2(5) - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922,ss.7,10 - Appointment - Salary - Assessment - Expenditure - Appellant/assessee a Hindu undivided family with S as its Karta - Appellant up to his death was Treasurer of several branches of bank - During his father's lifetime S was employed as an Overseer in Bank on a salary of Rs.400 a month - S was appointed Treasurer of Bank and 16 other branches of Bank - Hindu undivided family owns considerable property - As Treasurer S received in year of account from Bank a sum of Rs. 23,286 - Authorities held this sum to be the latter and taxed it - Hence instant appeal - Whether salary received by S was assessable as part of income of Hindu undivided family - Held, there was nothing to show that S had received any particular training at expense of family funds or his appointment was result of any outlay or expenditure of or detriment to family property - Emoluments received by S were in nature of salary and assessable u/s.7 of 1922 Act and not u/s.10 of Act as profits and gains of business and salary was income of individual S and not income of Hindu undivided family - Appeal allowed.
