 Shri Manna Lal and v Collector of Jhalawar

Supreme Court of India

07 December 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sarkar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, S.K. Das, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 200; AIR 1961 SC 828; 1961 CRLJ 1; 1961 (3) FLR 241; 1961 (2) LLJ 146; 1961 (1) SCJ 665; [1961] 2 S.C.R. 962

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Constitution; Agriculture & Agri. Products

Keywords: Debt Laws

Summary: Banking & Finance - Rajasthan Public Demands Recovery Act, 1952, ss. 6 and 8 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Notice u/s. 6 of Rajasthan Public Demands Recovery Act, 1952 - Appellants filed petition before High Court for issue of a writ quashing proceedings under Public Demands Recovery Act, 1952 - Which dismissed - Whether money due to Jhalawar State Bank, is money payable to Govt. of Rajasthan? - Held, yes - Jhalawar State Bank was one of assets of Jhalawar State and is now vested in State of Rajasthan also debts due from appellants are now due to Bank of Rajasthan Ltd. in its own right and it would follow that such debts remained debts due to Govt. of State of Rajasthan - Appeal dismissed.
