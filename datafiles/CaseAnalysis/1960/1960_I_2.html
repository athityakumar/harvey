Imperial Chemical Industries (India) Private Limited v Workmen(And Connected Appeal)

Supreme Court of India

14 November 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, A.K. Sarkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 261; AIR 1961 SC 1175; 1961 (2) FLR 68; 1960 (2) LLJ 716; 1961 (1) SCJ 535; [1961] 2 S.C.R. 349

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Festival

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 10(1) - Industrial demands - Award - Maintainability - Appellant/workmen were employed in respondent/company and they made industrial demands against company, which were referred for industrial adjudication u/s. 10 of the Act - Tribunal had partly allowed claim of workmen and had directed company to give to employees concerned, for work done on Sundays and holidays, half of a day's total salary and dearness allowance and the Tribunal also revised age of retirement fixed by company at 58 instead of 55 years - Hence, instant appeals - Whether impugned award of the Tribunal was justified - Held, it was not realised by Tribunal that in making distinction between work done on sundays on one hand and work done on festival holidays on other, and in making 2 different directions in respect of said 2 categories of work, the Tribunal's order in regard to latter category of work would have effect of placing workmen in worse position after award than before - Thus, relevant direction deprived workmen of their right to compensatory weekly off or substituted holiday and inevitable consequence of this direction would be ultimately to deprive workmen of part of benefits to which they were entitled under pre-existing arrangement - Therefore, SC upheld plea raised on behalf of workmen and directed that in respect of work done on festival holidays practice prevailing before instant dispute arose should continue - Further, Tribunal failed to take into account evidence supplied by workmen in their document filed along with their claim, this evidence strongly suggested almost uniform tendency to fix age of retirement at 60 and not 55 years - Since award did not refer to this document and gave no reasons why trend disclosed by document should not be adopted in instant case it had become necessary for SC to consider that question for itself - Therefore, age of retirement in case of workmen concerned in instant appeal was raised to 60 from 55 - Appeals disposed of.
