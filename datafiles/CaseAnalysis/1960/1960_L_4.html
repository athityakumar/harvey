Lalit Hari Ayurvedic Collage Pharmacy, Pilibhit v Lalit Hari Ayurvedic College Pharmacy Workers' Union

Supreme Court of India

29 January 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 48; AIR 1960 SC 1261; 1960 (1) LLJ 250

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 2(j) - (A)Whether pharmacy and hospital were run for benefit of students or not? - Held, in hospital run by appellant about 30 per cent of medicines manufactured in pharmacy and consumed and about 70 per cent are sold in market - Primary object of pharmacy was to manufacture ayurvedic medicines for sale in market and not for students benefit who were not taught in pharmacy -(B) Whether dispute in question was an industrial dispute or not? - Further held, activity of appellant in running pharmacy and hospital was an undertaking under s. 2 (j) and was an industry - Appeal dismissed.
