S. N. Namasivayam Chettiar v Commissioner of Income Tax, Madras

Supreme Court of India

03 February 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

J.L. Kapur, Mohammad Hidayatullah

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 427; AIR 1960 SC 729; [1960] 38 ITR 579; 1961 (1) MLJ(SC) 20; 1961 (1) SCJ 170; [1960] 2 S.C.R. 885

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Excess Profits Tax Act, 1940

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s.13 - Excess Profits Tax Act, 1940 - Account books - Gross profit - Income - Assessment - Appellant carried on extensive trade, showed gross profit about 3.5 per cent for assessment year 1943-1944 - Income Tax Officer estimated gross profit of 15 per cent - Assistant Commissioner confirmed order of Income tax officer - Income Tax Appellate assumed that correct profit could not be deduced from the books produced by assesses and s.13 of 1922 Act applied and computed profits at 15 per cent - Whether Tribunal was justified in applying s.13 of 1922 Act - Held, correct profit could not be deduced from books produced by assesses and s.13 of the Act applies in this case - No error in order of Tribunal - It cannot be said that observation of Tribunal was in any manner erroneous - Other appeals under 1940 Act, for various chargeable accounting periods depend upon result of Income Tax assessment appeals - Appeals disposed of.
