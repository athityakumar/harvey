Commissioner of Income Tax/Excess Profits Tax, Bombay City v Shamsher Printing Press

Supreme Court of India

08 March 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sarkar, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 368; AIR 1961 SC 98; [1960] 39 ITR 90

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income-tax Act, 1961 - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 3, 4(3)(Vii) - Compensation - Tax exemption - Capital receipt - Revenue receipts - Respondent was a firm carrying on a business of stationery and other things and had a printing press for the purpose of its business - Business was carried on and the press housed in a building belonging to its partners where the latter also resided - This building was requisitioned by the Govt. for the duration of the war - Respondent had to shift its business to another place where it was subsequently restarted - Respondent claimed compensation for the requisition on various accounts and was paid various sums - Department contended that there was no claim for injury to any such goodwill and that such a goodwill would be a capital asset - Hence, the present appeal - Whether a certain sum received by respondent was a capital receipt or a revenue receipt - Held, the sum of amount had not been received by respondent for any injury to any of its capital assets, but the sum was received as compensation for loss of profits for the period during which, it was imagined, respondent's business would remain stopped before it could be re-started at a new premises - That being so, it was clearly a revenue receipt - As it was a trading receipt, it could not be held exempt from tax u/s. 4(3)(vii) of the 1961 Act - Appeal allowed.
