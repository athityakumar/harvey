Commissioner of Income Tax, Bombay City I v Khatau Makanji Spinning and Weaving Company Limited

Supreme Court of India

04 May 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, S.K. Das, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 348; AIR 1960 SC 1022; [1960] 40 ITR 189; 1960 SCJ 1226; [1960] 3 S.C.R. 873

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Undistributed Profits

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income-tax Act, 1922, ss. 3,66(1) - Super-tax - Dividends - Rebate - Undistributed Profits - Whether additional income tax was legally charged under Indian Finance Act - Held, Finance Act in providing additional income tax should be paid upon accumulated profits of previous year goes beyond purpose for which Central Act was passed every year and cannot stand by itself without support of s.3 of Indian Income-tax Act - Income-tax Act puts tax on income or something which it deems to be income - Tax deals with income and income only tax shall be collected at a particular rate on total income for which provision shall be made in an yearly Central Act and Finance Act also follows same scheme and lays down rate at which tax was to be collected - Excess dividend shall be deemed to be out of whole or such portion of undistributed profits of one or more years immediately preceding previous year as would be just sufficient to cover amount of excess dividend and as have not likewise been taken into account to cover an excess dividend of a preceding year - Income-tax was a tax on income of previous year and it would not cover something which was not the income of previous year or made fictionally - Appeal dismissed.
