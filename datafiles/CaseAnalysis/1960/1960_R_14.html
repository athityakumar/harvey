 Raja Narayanlal Bansilal v Maneck Phiroz Mistry and Another

Supreme Court of India

31 August 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, K.C. Das Gupta, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 224; AIR 1961 SC 29; [1960] 30 Comp Cas 644; 1961 MLJ 208; 1961 (1) MLJ(SC) 73; 1961 (1) SCJ 353; [1961] 1 S.C.R. 417

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate

Keywords: General Clauses Act, Indian Evidence Act, Code of Criminal Procedure 1898, Prevention of Corruption Act, Indian Companies Act 1913, misa, Public Servants Inquiries Act, 1850

Summary: Corporate - Companies Act, 1956, ss. 240, 235, 645, 646 and 658 - Companies Act, 1913 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898, s.161(1) and (2) - Foreign Exchange Regulation Act, 1947, s.23 - General Clauses Act, s.6 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 161 and 165 - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 14, 20(2) and (3) 32, 226 and 311(2) - Persons in charge of management of Companies denied protection u/s. 132 of Evidence Act and u/s. 161 (1) and (2) of CrPC, 1898 - Inspector appointed to investigate u/s. 138 of Repealed Act,of 1913 - Issued notices u/s. 240 of new Act, 1956 - (A) Effect on notice - (B) Constitutionality of s. 239 and ss. 239 and 240, Companies Act, 1956 - By legal fiction he is authorised by s.645, be deemed to have been appointed u/s. 235 of new act, and hence has authority and power to issue notices - If relevant provisions of Act dealing with enquiries and investigations of affairs of companies are considered there would be no difficulty in holding that art.14 is not violated either by s. 239 or s. 240 of new Act and s. 240 does not offend against art. 20(3) - Appeal dismissed.
