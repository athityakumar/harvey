Commissioner of Income Tax, Bombay City II v Sitaldas Tirathdas

Supreme Court of India

24 November 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.L. Kapur, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 323; AIR 1961 SC 728; [1961] 41 ITR 367; 1961 (1) MLJ(SC) 128; 1961 (1) SCJ 472; [1961] 2 S.C.R. 634

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 3 and 9(1)(iv) - Whether maintenance paid to wife and children in pursuance of decree can be deducted when amount is not charged under Income Tax ? - Held, true test - Whether the amount sought to be deducted, in truth, reached the assessee as his income - When the obligation income is diverted before it reaches the assessee, it is deductible - If income is required to be applied to discharge an obligation after such income reaches the assessee - The same consequence, in law, does not follow - Maintenance paid to wife and children cannot be deducted - Appeal allowed.
