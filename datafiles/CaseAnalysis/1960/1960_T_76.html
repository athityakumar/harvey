 Samarth Transport Company Private Limited v Regional Transport Authority, Nagpur and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 September 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, J.L. Kapur, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 211; AIR 1961 SC 93; 1961 (1) SCJ 368; [1961] 1 S.C.R. 631

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Constitution; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Motor Vehicles Act, 1939 - Constitution Of India, 1950 - (A) Whether Regional Transport Authority was actuated by mala fides in disposal of applications for renewal of permits? - Held, as provisions of Act do not prescribe any time limit for disposal of an application for renewal of permits, court cannot hold that Regional Transport Authority acted without jurisdiction in rejecting applications some months after date of expiry of terms of permits - (B) Whether Regional Transport Authority exceeded its power in rejecting applications? - Held, Regional Transport Authority is authorized for purpose of giving effect to an approved scheme to refuse to entertain an application for renewal of any other permit - By time application for renewal came to be disposed of, admittedly scheme had been approved by Govt. of Bombay and routes in question were included in said scheme - Therefore, Authority was within its rights not to entertain applications filed by petitioner - Petition dismissed.
