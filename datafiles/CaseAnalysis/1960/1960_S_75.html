 Madhaorao Phalke v State of Madhya Bharat

Supreme Court of India

03 October 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, K. Subba Rao, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 455; AIR 1961 SC 298; 1961 (2) SCJ 477; [1961] 1 S.C.R. 957

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 372 - Kalambandis - Whether Kalambandis constitute an existing law or not and can be extinguished by the issue of an executive order? - Kalambandis under which appellant's right to receive by way of Bachat (balance) is guaranteed constitute an existing law within meaning of art. 372 - It was urged that since appellant's right to receive specified amount had been statutorily recognised by State of Gwalior it was not open to respondent 1 to extinguish that right merely by an executive order - Right to receive said amount from month to month was property to which appellant was entitled, and he could not be divested of that property without payment of compensation u/art. 31 - It is not disputed that if Kalambandis on which appellant's right is based, are rules or regulations having force of law impugned executive order issued by respondent 1 would be invalid - Held, thus Kalambandis constitute an existing law - Further held, right guaranteed to appellant by an existing law cannot be extinguished by issue of an executive order - Appeal allowed.
