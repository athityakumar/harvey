State of Bombay v Parshottam Kanaiyalal

Supreme Court of India

31 August 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, S.K. Das, Mohammad Hidayatullah

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 227; AIR 1961 SC 1; 1961 CRLJ 170; 1961 (1) MLJ(SC) 59; 1961 (1) SCJ 226; [1961] 1 S.C.R. 458

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Food Adulteration

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Prevention of Food Adulteration Act, 1954 ss. 20, 20(1) - Competency of complainant - Proceedings - Maintainability - Complaint was filed against respondent/accused for selling adulterated food - Trial Court convicted and sentenced accused - Appellate Court set aside order of conviction and sentence on ground that complainant/Food Inspector was not competent to institute prosecution u/s. 20(1) of the Act - HC affirmed order passed by Appellate Court - Hence, instant appeal - Whether impugned order of HC was justified - Held, on terms of s. 20(1) of the Act prosecution could be instituted with written consent not merely of State Govt. but 'of a local authority' or 'a person authorized in this behalf by the State Govt. or a local authority' - Further, analyst's report was before Chief Officer of Municipality and it was after considering that report and connected documents that written consent or sanction was given - In second place, subsection itself contained indication that written consent was for launching of specified prosecution, and not one 'in favour' of complainant authorising him to file complaint - Further, 2 classes were distinct and employment of different phraseology to designate 2 types of devolution of authority, constituted indication that in 2nd class of cases, where prosecutions were filed on basis of written consents granted by competent person or authority, specification of name of complainant was not statutory requirement, consent being to specified prosecution - Therefore, prosecution in instant case was instituted on complaint which fulfilled requirements of s. 20(1) of the Act - Appeal allowed.
