Jagat Dhish Bhargava v Jawahar Lal Bhargava and Others

Supreme Court of India

05 December 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, K.C. Das Gupta

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 448; AIR 1961 SC 832; 1961 (1) KerLR 437; 1963 (2) SCJ 11; [1961] 2 S.C.R. 918

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, O. 20, r.3, O. 41, r. 11 - Limitation Act 1908, s. 12(2) - Whether appeal preferred against appellant and respondents 8 and 9 in High Court of Punjab by respondents 2 to 7 was competent in law or not? - Contended O. 41, r. 1 was mandatory so appeal when filed with memorandum of appeal accompanied with certified copy of judgment appeal should be dismissed as being incompetent - High Court failed to notice that defect and to take necessary step at time of admission under O. 41, r. 1 - Litigant deserves to be protected against default committed or negligence shown by Court or its officers in discharge of their duties - HC realised it would be unfair to penalize party for mistake committed by trial court - Held, no hard and fast rule of general applicability can be laid down for dealing with appeals defectively filed under O. 41, r. 1 - Appellant is not justified in challenging propriety or validity of order passed by HC - Matter remanded back to HC.
