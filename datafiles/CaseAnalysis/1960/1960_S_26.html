Shew Bux Mohata and Others v Bengal Breweries Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

15 September 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sarkar, Syed Jaffer Imam, Raghubar Dayal

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 237; AIR 1961 SC 137; 1961 (1) SCJ 322; [1961] 1 S.C.R. 680

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 21 r. 35 - Execution of decree - Further execution - Maintainability - Will was executed regarding scheduled plots of land and respondents/defendants nos. 1, 2, 6 were appointed as executors, who granted leases to defendants nos. 3 to 5 and put them in possession - Mohatas whose interests were represented by appellants/plaintiffs claimed that executant had only life interest in lands and on death of executant schedule plots of land had vested in them - Appellants filed suit against executors and tenants for khas possession by evicting defendants from lands - Trial Court passed decree for khas possession in favour of Mohatas and gave defendants time to remove structures put up on land - In scheduled plots defendant no. 4 was carrying on business and defendant no. 5 was in possession on which stood some temples - Appellants filed execution of decree which was granted and fully executed against defendant no. 4 - Further, defendant no. 4 had executed 3 successive mortgages of premises No. 27 with all structures and appurtenances and gave possession to additional respondents - Therefore, appellants filed fresh execution petition to dispossess additional respondents, which was granted by Trial Court but was reversed in appeal by the HC - Hence, instant appeal - Whether impugned order of HC rejecting further execution petition was justified - Held, it was not in dispute that if decree was once executed against defendant no. 4 in full, then it could not be executed over again regarding premises No. 27 - In other words, if possession had been fully delivered to decree-holders in execution of decree on 1-10-1948, decree should have been wholly satisfied and nothing remains of it for enforcement by further execution - Further, decree was for khas possession and u/O. 21 r. 35, CPC in execution of it possession of the property concerned had to be delivered to decree-holders, if necessary, by removing any person bound by decree who refused to vacate property, records of proceedings show that such possession was delivered - Defendant no. 4 was party in possession and bound by decree - Thus, decree-holders in instant case, of their own accepted delivery of possession with defendant no. 4 remaining on premises with their permission, they granted receipt acknowledging full delivery of possession and permitted execution case to be dismissed on 8-9-1949, on basis that full possession had been delivered to them by defendant no. 4 - Further, fact that appellants put their guards on premises as mentioned in Nazir's return would also show that they had obtained full possession, it was open to decree-holders to accept such possession - Therefore, having once done so, they were bound to position that decree had been fully executed, from which it follows that it could not be executed any more - Appeal dismissed.
