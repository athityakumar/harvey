Pipraich Sugar Mills Limited v Pipraich Sugar Mills Mazdoor Union

Supreme Court of India

23 October 1956

Case Analysis

Bench

T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar

Where Reported

1956 Indlaw SC 101; AIR 1957 SC 95; 1957 (1) LLJ 235; 1957 SCJ 38; [1956] 1 S.C.R. 872

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Closure

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 2, 3 and 7 - Appellant is a limited company, which had been carrying on business in crushing sugarcane - It decided to expand its business - Sold its old machinery which had a crushing capacity of 160 tons per day, and purchased a new one with 650 tons capacity -The new plant was installed - Owing to shortage of sugarcane large losses were incurred by the sugarcane industry and the Govt. assumed control of its production and supply -The quota which was allotted to the appellate's mill proved too small to its being worked profitably - On application, permission granted to the appellant for the sale of the mills - The workers decided to prevent the transaction going through - They moved the Govt. to cancel the permission granted to the appellant for the sale of the mills -They also passed a resolution on to go on strike and communicated the same to the appellant - This led to correspondence between the parties regarding the compensation to be granted to the workers - (A) Whether respondent, apart from said agreement, must be awarded compensation? - Though, there is discharge of workmen both when there is retrenchment and closure of business, the compensation is to be awarded under the law, not for discharge as such but for discharge on retrenchment - Held, apart from agreement, workmen should not be awarded compensation - (B) Whether the award by the Industrial Tribunal is void? - A true construction of s. 3, the power of the State to make a reference, under that section must be determined with reference not to the date on which it is made but to the date on which the right which is the subject-matter of the dispute arises - Held, the notification D/- 16-11-1951, referring the dispute to the adjudication of the Industrial Tribunal is not ultra vires, and the reference and the award therein are not in consequence void - Appeal allowed - (C) Whether on this correspondence there was a concluded and binding agreement that the appellant should pay 25 per cent of the profits on the sale transaction to the workmen? - Held,the promise of the management as contained in the letters of 03-01-1951 and 10-01-1951, is a binding agreement under which the workmen are entitled to compensation for termination of their services on the closure of the mills - Appeal allowed.
