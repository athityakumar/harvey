Deoki Nandan v Murlidhar

Supreme Court of India

04 October 1956

Case Analysis

Bench

T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, S.K. Das, B. Jagannadhadas

Where Reported

1956 Indlaw SC 67; AIR 1957 SC 133; 1957 (1) MLJ(SC) 28; 1957 SCJ 75; [1956] 1 S.C.R. 756

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Trusts & Association - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, ss. 92 and 109(c) -(A) Distinction between Private and Public Trusts? - Held, whereas in former beneficiaries are specific individuals, in latter they are general public or a class thereof - While in former beneficiaries are persons who are ascertained or capable of being ascertained, in latter they constitute a body which is incapable of ascertainment - (B) Whether a Thakurdwara of Sri Radhakrishnaji in village of Bhadesia in District of Sitapur is a private temple or a public one in which all Hindus are entitled to worship? - At trial, while witnesses for plaintiff deposed that temple was built with object of providing a place of worship for all Hindus, witnesses examined by defendants merely deposed that Sheo Ghulam built Thakurdwara for his own use and 'for his puja only' - Held, view of lower court that temple must be taken to have been dedicated to members of family goes beyond pleading, and is not supported by evidence in case - Having considered all aspects, Courts are of opinion that Thakurdwara of Sri Radhakrishnaji in Bhadesia is a public temple - Appeal allowed.
