 Lawrence Joachim Joseph D'Souza v State of Bombay

Supreme Court of India

24 April 1956

Case Analysis

Bench

B. Jagannadhadas, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Syed Jaffer Imam, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar

Where Reported

1956 Indlaw SC 71; AIR 1956 SC 531; 1956 (58) BomLR 989; 1956 CRLJ 935; 1956 SCJ 559; [1956] 1 S.C.R. 382

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure; Socio-Economic

Keywords: Freedom Of Speech And Expression

Summary: Criminal - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Preventive Detention Act, 1950, ss. 3 and 7 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Appellant detained for preventing him from acting prejudical to the interest of state - Petitioner contended that detention invalid because the grounds not furnished, rest of grounds vague and detention violative of freedom of speech and expression - Held, the grounds not vague and effective representation could have been made but not made by petitioner - And all grounds not disclosed in public interest, valid - Detention order not mala-fide - Petition dismissed.
