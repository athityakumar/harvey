Brij Bhukhan and Others v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

09 November 1956

Case Analysis

Bench

Syed Jaffer Imam, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha

Where Reported

1956 Indlaw SC 131; AIR 1957 SC 474; 1957 CRLJ 591

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Constitution of India, 1950 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 149, 449, 147, 323 and 300 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1898, s. 342 - Appellants convicted of murder - (A) Whether right of private defence arises? - Appellants and his companions were aggressors and in course of occurrence neither appellant nor his companions were assaulted - Held, question of exercise of right of private defence does not arise - (B) Whether in view of medical evidence offence committed by assailants was not murder because none of them have been proved to be sufficient in ordinary course of nature to cause death? - However, nature of injuries found on body of deceased can only indicate that assailants intended to cause his death - Held, even if none of injuries by themselves was sufficient in ordinary course of nature to cause death, cumulatively they were certainly sufficient in ordinary course of nature to cause his death, which in fact took place soon after assault - (C) Whether since evidence consists of interested persons only corroboration by independent persons or by circumstantial evidence was necessary? - Ample corroboration by way of circumstantial evidence as to place of occurrence - Held, corroborative evidence in support of prosecution witnesses - (D) Whether even if conviction of appellants under ss. 302/149 is upheld, this was not a case in which sentence of death should be maintained on appellant when a similar sentence passed on other appellants was reduced by High Court to transportation for life? - Evidence clearly indicated that appellant did not actually commit any assault - At best he instigated assault - Held, merely because leniency had been shown to others in matter of sentence it is no ground for reducing sentence passed on appellant - Appeal dismissed.
