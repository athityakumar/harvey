Nagubai Ammal and Others v B. Shama Rao and Others

Supreme Court of India

26 April 1956

Case Analysis

Bench

T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Syed Jaffer Imam

Where Reported

1956 Indlaw SC 18; AIR 1956 SC 593; 1956 ALT(SC) 1029; 1957 BLJR 264; 1956 SCJ 655; [1956] S.C.R. 451

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 52 - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882 - (A) Whether as question of lis pendens was not raised in pleadings, it is not open to plaintiff? - Held, question of lis pendens was raised by plaintiff at very commencement of trial - Defendants went to trial with full knowledge that question of lis pendens was in issue and had ample opportunity to adduce their evidence thereon, and fully availed themselves of same, and that, in circumstances, absence of a specific pleading on question was a mere irregularity, which resulted in no prejudice to them - (B) Whether suit for maintenance, O S No. 100 of 1919-20 and sale in execution of decree passed therein are all collusive, and s. 52 has accordingly no application? - Held, there is no question of estoppel, as title of Dr Nanjunda Rao arose under a purchase which was long prior to admissions made in 1932 and in subsequent years - There cannot be any doubt, therefore, that suits were bona fide - Plaintiff obtained no advantage against appellants by pleading in 0 S No. 92 of 1938-39 that proceedings in 0 S No. 100 of 1919-20 were collusive and nor did their acting on those pleadings acquire rights to suit properties - Nor is there any question of election, because only relief which plaintiff claimed in 0 S No. 92 of 1938-39 and which he now claims is that he is entitled to suit properties - Only, ground on which that relief is claimed is different and, it is true, inconsistent - But principle of election does not forbid it, and there being no question of estoppel, plea that proceedings in 0 S No. 100 of 1919-20 are not collusive is open to plaintiff - (C) Whether purchase by Devamma in execution of decree in O S No 160 of 1919-20 on 2-8-1928 is void and inoperative, as Official Receiver in whom estate of Keshavananda had vested on 19-02-1926 was not a party to sale proceedings? - Held, u/s 28(2) of Insolvency Act, what vests in Official Receiver is only property of insolvent and as suit properties had ceased to be his properties by reason of sale deed dated 30-01-1920, they did not vest in Official Receiver - Sale held on 02-08-1928 is not liable to be attacked on ground that he bad not been impleaded as a party thereto - Appeal dismissed.
