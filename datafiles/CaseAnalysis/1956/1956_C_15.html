Rao Bahadur Ravulu Subba Rao and Others v Commissioner of Income Tax, Madras

Supreme Court of India

09 May 1956

Case Analysis

Bench

T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati

Where Reported

1956 Indlaw SC 105; AIR 1956 SC 604; [1956] 30 ITR 163; 1956 (2) MLJ(SC) 43; 1956 SCJ 591; [1956] 1 S.C.R. 577

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Contract Act, 1872 - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 26a, 59, 66 (1) - Application for renewal - Powers-Of-Attorney Act, 1882 - Income-tax Rules, 1962 , rr.2,6 - Partnership - Power of attorney - Registration - Ultra vires - Appellant / partnership firm registered u/s. 26-A of Act - One of partners, S was stated to have left on a long pilgrimage, and affairs of partnership managed by H as his agent under a general power-of-attorney - Those rules provide that an application for registration of a firm and for renewal of registration certificate should be signed personally by all partners - Income-tax Officer rejected application for renewal on ground that it was not personally signed by one of partners - Reference u/s. 66 (1) of Act to HC, which held that word personally in r. 6 required that partner should himself sign application, and that principles of agency under general law were excluded - Hence instant appeal - Whether rr. 2, 6 were ultra vires to the rule making authority - Held, Indian Legislature immediately followed suit and enacted 1882 Act incorporating in s. 2 therein word for word, s. 46 of English Act - Object of s.46 was to effectuate instruments executed by an agent but not in accordance with rule of common law and enactment was more procedural than substantive - It did not confer on a person a right to act through agents -S. 26-A said that a partner could not delegate exercise of his rights under that section to an agent - S. 2 of 1882 Act says that if there could be and in fact there was delegation, it could be exercised in manner provided - To sum up, Act was a self contained code exhaustive of matters dealt with and its provisions showed an intention to depart from common rule - And as those rules require application to be signed by partner in person, signature by an agent was invalid - rr. 2, 6 were intravires powers of rule-making authority - Appeal dismissed.
