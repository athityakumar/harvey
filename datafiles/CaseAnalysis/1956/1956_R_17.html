 Raman and Raman Limited v State of Madras and Another

Supreme Court of India

15 March 1956

Case Analysis

Bench

Syed Jaffer Imam, Vivian Bose, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, B. Jagannadhadas, N. Chandrasekhara Aiyar

Where Reported

1956 Indlaw SC 77; AIR 1956 SC 463; 1956 (1) MLJ(SC) 169; 1956 SCJ 368; [1956] 1 S.C.R. 256

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Constitution - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Motor Vehicles Act,1939, ss. 64A, 47 and 64 - Question of jurisdiction - The powers of Govt. u/s. 64-A of Motor Vehicles Act, 1939, as amended by Motor Vehicles (Madras Amendment) Act, 1948 for State of Madras, to interfere with order of subordinate - Transport Authorities on ground of propriety limits judicial review which courts have u/art. 226 of Constitution - (A) Whether superior court has a power to disturb decision of inferior court or tribunal on a question of jurisdiction u/art. 226 of Constitution? - Held, in some cases where jurisdiction of an inferior tribunal may depend upon fulfillment of some condition precedent or upon existence of some particular fact and that fact is collateral to actual matter which inferior tribunal has to try and determination of whether it exists or not is logically and in sequence prior to determination of actual question which inferior tribunal has to try then by a certioraris proceedings a court can enquire into correctness of decision of inferior tribunal as to collateral fact and may reverse that decision if it appears to it, on materials before it to be erroneous but if lower courts decision is wrong on facts or law, it cannot be corrected by a writ of certioraris - (B) Whether State Govt. has power to interfere with order of subordinate transport authority in matter of granting permit for stage carriage? - Held, by enacting s. 64-A of Motor Vehicles Act,1939, Legislature clearly intended that State Govt. should have powers to intervene, if it was satisfied that order in question was either illegal, irregular or improper - State Govt. can decide issue as to whether any order in question was illegal, irregular or improper - It would not be open to a court exercising power of certiorari to intervene merely because it might be of opinion that view taken by State Govt. was erroneous - (B1) What is condition precedent for exercise of powers u/s. 64-A of Motor Vehicles Act (4 of 1939)? - Held, that condition precedent for exercise of powers u/s. 64-A is that, there should be in fact an order passed by subordinate transport authority in respect of which powers u/s. 64-A can be exercised - In order to satisfy itself whether order of an authority subordinate to it was legal, regular or proper, State Govt. is not deciding existence of a collateral fact but issue itself as to legality, regularity or propriety of order - (C) Whether s. 64-A inserted by Motor Vehicles (Madras Amendment) Act (20 of 1948) is valid or not? - Held, it was within competence of State Legislature to insert s. 64-A into Act - It is a reasonable provision in keeping with entire scheme of Act concerning transport vehicles and control of road transport - (D) What is nature of function performed by Regional Transport Authority, Board and State Govt. under Motor Vehicles Act,1939, ss. 47, 64 and 64 A? - Held, In proceedings u/ss. 47, 64 and 64-A of Act, there is no determination of any individual's rights and from that point of view functions of these authorities may be regarded as executive or administrative - Appeal dismissed.
