 Abdul Jabar Butt v State of Jammu and Kashmir

Supreme Court of India

13 November 1956

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhi Ranjan Das, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, S.K. Das

Where Reported

1956 Indlaw SC 98; AIR 1957 SC 281; 1957 CRLJ 404; 1957 SCJ 184; [1957] 1 S.C.R. 51

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Constitution - Jammu and Kashmir Preventive Detention Act, 1964, s. 8(1) - Interpretation of s. 8 of Jammu and Kashmir Preventive Detention Act, 1964 - Notifying grounds for detention - Both petitions have been filed under art. 32 of Constitution, complaining that petitioners have been and are being wrongfully detained under Act and praying for their immediate release - (A) Whether phrase 'as soon as may' points to reasonable shortest possible time with regard to particular circumstances of case u/s. 8(1) Jammu and Kashmir Preventive Detention Act, 1964? - Held, that period of time predicated by phrase 'as soon as may be' begins to run from time detention in pursuance of detention order begins - To communicate grounds 'as soon as may be' may well be said to mean to do so within a reasonable time with an understanding to do it within shortest possible time with regards to particular circumstances of case in which question arises for decision -(B) Whether proviso implies that time for making declaration should be co-terminous with time fixed for communicating grounds u/s 8(1)? - Held, that proviso implies that time for making declaration should be co-terminous with time fixed for communicating grounds u/s. 8(1)- Authority vested in Govt. to make a declaration contemplated by proviso must be exercised before expiry of span of time predicated by expression 'as soon as may be' occurring in sub-s. 8(1) - Petition allowed.
