 A. S. Krishna v State Of, Madras

Supreme Court of India

28 November 1956

Case Analysis

Bench

T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, S.K. Das

Where Reported

1956 Indlaw SC 87; AIR 1957 SC 297; 1957 CRLJ 409; 1957 (1) MLJ(SC) 59; 1957 SCJ 216; [1957] 1 S.C.R. 399

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces

Keywords: Attorney-General

Summary: Constitution - Madras Prohibition Act, ss. 4(2), 28 to 32 - Government of India Act, 1935, s. 107 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14 and 13(1) - (A) Whether impugned provisions of Madras Prohibition Act, ss. 4(2) and 28 to 32 are in contravention to s. 107 of Government of India Act, 1935? - Two conditions required: (1) provisions of Provincial law and those of Central legislation must both be in respect of a matter which is enumerated' in Concurrent List, and (2) they must be repugnant to each other - Only when both conditions are satisfied provincial law will, to extent of repugnancy, become void - Presumptions in s. 4(2) are not presumptions which are to be raised in trial of all criminal cases, as are those enacted in Evidence Act, 1872 - They are to be raised only in trial of offences u/s. 4(1) of Act - They are therefore purely ancillary to exercise of legislative power in respect of Entry 31 in List 11 - Also, provisions relating to search, seizure and arrest in ss. 28 to 32 are only with reference to offences committed or suspected to have been committed under said Act - They have no operation generally or to offences which fall outside Act - Held, no conflict, said provision have no operation apart from offences created by Act, and are wholly ancillary to legislation under Entry 31 in List II - (B) Whether s. 4(2) of Madras Prohibition Act is repugnant to art. 14 of Constitution, 1950? - Presumptions of equality, enacted therein have to be raised against all persons against whom facts mentioned therein are established and if presumption of equality exist no violation - Held, no violation, s. 4(2) do not offends requirement as to equality before law or equal protection of laws - Appeals dismissed.
