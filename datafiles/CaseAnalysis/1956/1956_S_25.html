Chikkarange Gowda and Others v State of Mysore

Supreme Court of India

09 May 1956

Case Analysis

Bench

S.K. Das, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati

Where Reported

1956 Indlaw SC 139; AIR 1956 SC 731; 1956 CRLJ 1365; 1957 KerLJ 25

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 148, 302, 34 and 149 - Criminal Proceure Code, 1898, s. 342 - Appellants guilty of murder - Convicted by Trial Court and High Court - (A) Whether findings of High Court do not justify convictions of appellants? - First essential elements of s. 149 is commission of an offence by any member of an unlawful assembly and second is that offence must be committed in prosecution of common object of unlawful assembly, or must be such as members of that assembly knew to be likely to be committed in prosecution of common object - Charge framed against Appellants mixed up ss. 32 and 149 - Charge in judgement of High Court gave no notice to appellants that three of them had a separate common intention from that of other members of unlawful assembly - None of members of unlawful assembly had intention to kill nor did any of them know that deceased was likely to be killed in prosecution of common object of chastisement - S. 34 embodies a principle of joint liability if doing a criminal act - Two appellants who have been held to be liable u/s. 34 did not cause any fatal injury to deceased - Further way in which charge u/s. 302 r/w ss.149 and 34 was framed gave appellants no effective notice that they had to meet case of a separate common intention of three persons, as distinct from object of other members of unlawful assembly - Therefore, conviction of appellant for an offence u/s. 302 r/w s.34 cannot be sustained - Appellants will be held responsible for such of their individual acts as went beyond common object of unlawful assembly - Held, that conviction of appellants for offence u/s. 148, is correct but not their conviction u/s. 302 r/w s.149 or s. 302 r/w s.34 - (B) Whether there was failure to comply with s.342, CrPC? - Compliance with provisions of s. 342, is not a mere idle formality - In this case examination of appellants to enable them to explain circumstances appearing against them was neither full nor very satisfactory - Held, that no serious prejudice was caused such as to vitiate whole trial - Further held, that though there are no reasons for interference with convictions and sentences of Appellants u/s. 148, their convictions and sentences u/s. 302 r/w s.149, or s. 302, r/w s. 34.
