Baroda Borough Municipality v Its Workmen

Supreme Court of India

13 November 1956

Case Analysis

Bench

S.K. Das, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, P. Govinda Menon

Where Reported

1956 Indlaw SC 36; AIR 1957 SC 110; 1957 (1) LLJ 8; 1957 SCJ 95; [1957] 1 S.C.R. 33

Case Digest

Subject: Electricity & Energy; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: misa, Electricity Act, 1934

Summary: Electricity & Energy - Labour & Industrial Law - Bombay Municipal Boroughs Act, 1925 - Electricity (Supply) Act,1948 - Electricity Act,1934 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 2(j) - Municipal service - Surplus earnings - Payment of bonus - Not maintainable - Appellant/ Baroda Borough Municipality and respondents/workmen employed in electricity department of Municipality represented by respondent Union - Baroda Municipality got from State Govt. of Baroda merged in and became municipal property under provisions of Municipal Act and was not capital on which a return had to be earned in accordance with principles - Municipal Act were relevant only for purpose of determining quality those provisions cannot be stretched beyond that limited purpose for defeating a claim of bonus - State Govt. of Baroda decided to hand over concern as a gift to Baroda Municipality/ appellant - Whether respondents/workmen were entitled for payment of bonus claimed out of surplus earnings of department - Held, that Baroda Municipality constituted one integrated whole and the activities of different departments of Municipality were not distinct as to permit isolation of one department from another earning department - Respondents not entitled to bonus claimed because municipality was not a profit-making concern - Amenities appertaining to municipal service were not entitled to claim such bonus was granted to them during regime of former State owned company - Respondent's claim of bonus was not maintainable -There were no profits of one single department of municipality out of respondents could claim a bonus - Payment of bonus to their employees by Electric Supply Companies, not run as a State or municipal undertaking was considered with reference to provisions of 1948 Act - Appeal allowed.
