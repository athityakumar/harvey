Surendra Nath Khosla v Dalip Singh

Supreme Court of India

29 November 1956

Case Analysis

Bench

Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha

Where Reported

1956 Indlaw SC 95; AIR 1957 SC 242; 1957 ALT(SC) 174; 1957 MPLJ 249; [1957] 1 S.C.R. 179

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Keywords: Representation Of People (Second Amendment) Act, 1951

Summary: Election - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 90(3) and 100(1)(c) - (A) Whether respondent was duly proposed and seconded and thumb impressions of proposer and seconder on his nomination paper were attested in accordance with law? - Held, yes, persons whose thumb impressions nomination paper purported to bear had really proposed and seconded candidate - Thumb impressions were attested by a magistrate who was authorised in that behalf - Returning Officer was not justified in rejecting nomination paper in question - (B) Whether rejection of nomination paper had materially affected result of election? - Held, where a nomination paper had been improperly rejected there is a strong presumption that result of election has been materially affected - If it had to be declared void, tribunal was justified in setting aside election as a whole - Appeal dismissed.
