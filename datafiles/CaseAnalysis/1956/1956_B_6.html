 Banaras Ice Factory Limited v Its Workmen

Supreme Court of India

28 November 1956

Case Analysis

Bench

S.K. Das, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha

Where Reported

1956 Indlaw SC 40; AIR 1957 SC 168; 1957 (1) LLJ 253; 1957 (1) MLJ 36; 1957 (1) MLJ(SC) 36; 1957 SCJ 139; [1957] 1 S.C.R. 143

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Closure, Labour Appellate Tribunal, Industrial Disputes (Appellate Tribunal) Act, 1950, misa, Industrial Disputes (Appellate Tribunal) Act

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes (Appellate Tribunal) Act, 1950, ss. 22 and 23 - Industrial Disputes Act,1947, s. 2(S) - 'Discharge of workmen' - 'Closure of business' - Bona fide - (A) Whether s. 22(a) and (b) of Industrial Disputes Act, or any other provisions of Act relates only to running or existing industry? - Alteration referred to in s. 22(a) must be an alteration in conditions of service to prejudice of workman concerned, in an existing or running industry - Similarly, second part of cl.(b) relating to punishment can have application to a running or existing industry only - Unqualified though word 'discharge' is, it must be interpreted in harmony with general scheme and scope of Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Held, provisions of Industrial Disputes Act 1947, applies to an existing industry and not a dead industry- (A1) Whether word discharge u/s 2(s) of Industrial Dispute Act means discharge of all workmen? - Held, that word 'Discharge' in definition of a workman in s. 2(s) of that Act also means discharge of a person in a running or continuing business not discharge of all workmen when industry itself ceases to exist on a bona fide closure of business - (B) Whether word 'Discharge' in s. 22(b) of Act of 1950 includes termination of workmen? -Held, word 'Discharge' occurring in cl(b) of s. 22 does not include termination of services of all workmen on a real and bona fide closure of his business by employer - (C) Whether word 'discharge' as per Act of 1950 means Discharge of all workmen when industry itself ceases to exist ? - Alteration in s. 22(a) of Act of 1950 must be an alteration in conditions of service to prejudice of workman concerned, in an existing or running industry - Similarly, s. 22(b) of Act of 1950 relating to punishment can have application to a running or existing industry only - When industry itself ceases to exist, there can be no question of punishment of a workman or dismissal by or otherwise - Held, word 'Discharge' in definition of a workman in s. 2(s) of that Act also means discharge of a person in a running or continuing business not discharge of all workmen when industry itself ceases to exist on a bona fide closure of business - Appeal allowed.
