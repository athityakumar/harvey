 Raja Ram Chandra Reddy and Another v Rani Shankaramma and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 February 1956

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhi Ranjan Das, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Syed Jaffer Imam, B. Jagannadhadas

Where Reported

1956 Indlaw SC 132; AIR 1956 SC 319

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Family & Personal - Land & Property - Hyderabad Court of Wards Act, 1350 F, s. 53 - Hyderabad (Abolition of Jagirs) Regulation, 1358 F - Atiyat Enquiries Act, 1952, s. 13(2) - Hindu Law - Atiyat property and non-Atiyat property - Jagir, main Atiyat property - One Raja Durga Reddy (Raja) died in year 1900, who was in possession of properties including a Jagir Samashtanam(estate) Surviving member, his widow (appellant No. 2) than aged about 16 years and a daughter (respondent No. 1) then aged about one year - On Raja's death management of his entire estate was taken over by Court of Wards under orders of Govt. - Succession thereafter was sanctioned in favour of respondent No. 1 - Six years later respondent No. 1 was married to brother of appellant No. 2 - Estate continued to be under supervision of - Permission to appellant No. 2/widow given for adoption - Appellant No. 1 (A1/adopted son) was selected to be adopted - Hyderabad (Abolition of Jagirs) Regulation enforced in year 1949 - Original Jagir tenure as such was abolished and under this Regulation a hereditary but inalienable personal right to receive a portion of net income thereof by way of interim maintenance was substituted - Applications moved by A1/adopted son inter alia for compensation for taking over of Jagirs - Applications on other hand also moved by respondent No. 1 to Revenue Member of State - Whether Revenue Minister's order as confirmed by Chief Minister u/s. 13(2) of Atiyat Enquiries Act, 1952 is valid and final in so far as it relates to Atiyat and Jagir property, thus validly and finally determining respective rights therein of A1/adopted son, appellant No. 2/widow and respondent No. 1/daughter? - Held, Chief Minister's order must be taken to be a competent, valid and final order in so far as it relates to Atiyat and Jagir property and that, in events that have happened and by virtue of s. 13(2) of Atiyat Act, 1952 there has emerged a valid and final determination of respective rights of all three parties - Impugned order of High Court quashed - Appeal allowed.
