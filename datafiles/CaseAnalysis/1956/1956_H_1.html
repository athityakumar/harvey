H H Raja Harinder Singh v S Karnail Singh

Supreme Court of India

20 December 1956

Case Analysis

Bench

T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, S.K. Das

Where Reported

1956 Indlaw SC 42; AIR 1957 SC 271; 1957 SCJ 261; [1957] 1 S.C.R. 208

Case Digest

Subject: Election; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Election Expenses, General Clauses Act

Summary: Election - General Clauses Act, 1897, s. 10 - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 81, 123(7), 100(2)(b) and 140 - Representation of People (Conduct of Elections and Election Petitions) Rules, 1951, rr. 2(6), 113, 118 and 119 - (A) Whether election petition was presented beyond time prescribed by r. 119(a), and should have been dismissed u/s 90(4)? - Held, no - Petition which respondent filed is entitled to protection afforded by that section and is in time - (B) Whether conclusion that r. 118 had been contravened does not follow and is erroneous ? - Held, it cannot be said that r. 118 had been infringed - Requirements of r. 118 are not satisfied by proving merely that person does work in connection with election, that work must be done under a contract of employment - Appeal allowed.
