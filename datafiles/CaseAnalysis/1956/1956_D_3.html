Fruit and Vegetable Merchants Union v Delhi Improvement Trust

Supreme Court of India

06 November 1956

Case Analysis

Bench

Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Syed Jaffer Imam, B. Jagannadhadas

Where Reported

1956 Indlaw SC 21; AIR 1957 SC 344; 1957 SCJ 167; [1957] 1 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Keywords: Trade Union Act, 1926, Requisitioning and Acquisition of Immovable Property Act, Trade Unions Act

Summary: Rent Control - Delhi and Ajmer Rent Control Act, 1952, s. 3(a) - Delhi Improvement Trust - Nazul Estate, cl. (e) of sub-s. (1) of s. 72 - Issue - (A) Whether the Delhi and Ajmer Rent Control Act, XXXVIII of 1952 is applicable to the premises in question? - Held, operative provisions of the Control Act, 1952 do not apply to the premises in question- (B) Whether the property in dispute belongs to the Govt. within the meaning of s. 3(a) of the Rent Control Act, 1952? - Held, market, as also the land on which it stands, is the property of Govt. - There are no provisions either in the Improvement Trust or in the indenture, Ex. D-5, to the effect that the title to the Nazul Estate vested in the Trust - Trust only holds the market and realizes the income therefrom which is disbursed in accordance with the terms of the agreement and the rules framed by the Chief Commr. in exercise of the powers conferred on him by cl. (e) of sub-s. (1) of s. 72 - Appeal dismissed.
