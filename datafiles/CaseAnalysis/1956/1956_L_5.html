Laxmi Devi Sugar Mills v Nand Kishore Singh

Supreme Court of India

04 October 1956

Case Analysis

Bench

Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, P. Govinda Menon, S.K. Das, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar

Where Reported

1956 Indlaw SC 68; AIR 1957 SC 7; 1956 (2) LLJ 439; 1956 SCJ 735; [1956] 1 S.C.R. 746

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Freedom Of Speech And Expression

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Practice & Procedure - Industrial Employment (Standing Orders) Act, 1946, s. 5 - (A) Whether general manager can be removed based on a resolution by union? - Held, a general manager cannot be removed simply on a resolution by union, however, resolution can be subject to proper enquiry by management, which has authenticity to form conclusions regarding removal - (1) Whether insubordination and breach of discipline by an employee attracts punishment of dismissal? - Held, conduct of employee is subversive of discipline and amounts to misconduct, insubordination and breach of discipline and it would entitle employer to dismiss him from his employment - (2) Whether enquiry is conducted solely on charges specified in charge sheet? - Held, an enquiry shall be conducted on basis of charges provided in charge sheet and omission of a charge shall not be enquired into - Appeal dismissed.
