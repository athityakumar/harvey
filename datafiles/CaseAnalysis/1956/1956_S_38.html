Niranjan Singh v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

03 October 1956

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Govinda Menon, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, Syed Jaffer Imam, S.K. Das

Where Reported

1956 Indlaw SC 102; AIR 1957 SC 142; 1957 CRLJ 294; 1957 SCJ 69; [1956] 1 S.C.R. 734

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Criminal - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136(1) - Uttar Pradesh Police Regulations, Chap XI, r. 109 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1898, s. 537 - Compliance with rules relating to submission of police case diary - Factors vitiating trial - (A) Whether finding of guilt of accused by Session Judge and High Court in concurrence can be reopened by Supreme Court? - Sessions Judge accepts testimony of prosecution witnesses and disbelieves story put forward by accused and in this he has concurrence of High Court on appeal - Held, Supreme Court would not be justified in re-opening finding about guilt of accused appellants if no question of law is involved or if conclusion is not perverse or opposed to principles of natural justice or revolting to judicial conscience - (B) Whether under Uttar Pradesh Police Regulations, Chap XI, r. 109 has any statutory foundation? - Held, that r. 109 has no statutory foundation but is only an injunction by executive Govt. to police officers as to how they must regulate their work and conduct themselves during course of investigation - (C) Whether non compliance of r. 109, Uttar Pradesh Police Regulations during course of investigation can be said to be an illegality? - CrPC in laying down omissions or irregularities which would vitiate proceedings has not anywhere specifically said that a mistake committed by a police officer during course of investigation can be said to be an illegality or irregularity - Investigation is certainly not an inquiry or trial before court and fact is that there is no specific provision in Chapter XLV with respect to omissions or mistakes committed during course of investigations except with regard to holding of an inquest is a sufficient indication - Held, failure to comply with r. 109, Uttar Pradesh Police Regulations, relating to submission of police case diary cannot in any way interfere with legality of a trial -Appeal dismissed.
