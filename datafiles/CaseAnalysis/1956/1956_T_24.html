Pandit Ram Narain v State of Uttar Pradesh And Others

Supreme Court of India

20 September 1956

Case Analysis

Bench

S.K. Das, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, Syed Jaffer Imam, P. Govinda Menon

Where Reported

1956 Indlaw SC 32; AIR 1957 SC 18; 1956 SCJ 725; [1956] 1 S.C.R. 664

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Land & Property

Keywords: Uttar Pradesh Town Areas Act, 1914, Professions Tax Limitation Act, 1941, Uttar Pradesh District Board Act, 1922

Summary: Land & Property - Municipalities & Local Governments - Practice & Procedure - Service - Income Tax & Direct Tax - Uttar Pradesh Town Areas Act, 1914, ss. 14(1)(f) and 15 - (A) Whether imposition of tax is legal under s. 14 cl.(1)(f) of Uttar Pradesh Town Areas Act? - Held, imposition of tax on assessee is legal - It must be considered with reference to clause under which assessment is actually made - A different clause under which assessment might have fallen cannot be called in aid of assessment - List prepared under s. 15 must show an assessee as assessed to a certain amount of tax under cl(f) of sub s. (1) of s. 14 and it must have been confirmed on that basis by District Magistrate - (1) Whether residence in Town Area is a necessary condition for imposition of tax? - Held, residence within Town Area is not a necessary condition for imposition of tax - S. 14 of Uttar Pradesh Town Areas Act does not say in express terms that residence within Town Area is a necessary condition for imposition of tax - (B) Whether person assessed u/cls. (a) to (e) of s. 14 can be assessed again u/cl. (f) of sub s. (1) of s. 14? - Further held, proviso to cl.(f) makes it clear that a person who is already assessed under cls. (a) to (e) cannot be assessed again under cl. (f) - Proviso is intended to avoid multiple taxation, but it is manifest from proviso that there may be overlapping of different clauses in sub s. (1) of s. 14 - Therefore, proviso is necessary to prevent same person from being taxed more than once under different clauses of sub s. (1) of s. 14 - (C)Whether tax on 'circumstances and propert' is a composite tax or not? - Held, a tax on 'circumstances and property' is a composite tax - Word 'circumstances' means financial position, his status as a whole depending, among other things, on his income from trade or business -(D) Whether it is a sound principle to interpret expressions used in one Act with reference to their use in another? - Held, it is not a sound principle of construction to interpret expressions used in one Act with reference, to their use in another Act - Meanings of words and expressions used in an Act must take their colour from context in which they appear - Appeal dismissed.
