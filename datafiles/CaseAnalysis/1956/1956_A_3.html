 Hariprasad Shivshankar Shukla v A.D. Divikar

Supreme Court of India

27 November 1956

Case Analysis

Bench

S.K. Das, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, P. Govinda Menon

Where Reported

1956 Indlaw SC 39; AIR 1957 SC 121; 1957 (1) LLJ 243; 1957 SCJ 83; [1957] 1 S.C.R. 121

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Closure, Industrial Disputes (Amendment And Miscellaneous Provisions) Act, 1956, Industrial Disputes (Amendment) Act, 1953, Industrial Disputes (Amendment) Act, 1956

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Bombay Industrial Disputes Act,1938 - Industrial Disputes (Amendment and Miscellaneous Provisions) Act, 1956 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s.25F(b) - Payment of Wages Act,1936, s.15 - Closure of business - Retrenchment - Compensation - Termination - Respondent made application to relevant authority under 1936 Act claiming retrenchment compensation for workmen of appellant u/s.25F(b) of 1947 Act - HC detained that workmen entitled to claim compensation u/s.25F(b) of 1947 Act - Hence, instant appeal - Whether authority under 1936 Act had adjudicate claim of retrenchment compensation u/s.25F(b) of 1947 Act - Held, workman service had terminated by reason of change of employers on account of change of ownership or management would be entitled to retrenchment compensation u/s.25F of 1947 Act - Any transfer or closure of business was judicially alleged to claim for retrenchment compensation with consequences in complete industrial deadlock - Standardization of retrenchment compensation and perplexing variety of factors for granting retrenchment compensation though basic consideration granting of unemployment relief - No compensation paid by appellants - Appellants in two appeals were not liable to pay any compensation u/s.25F of 1947 Act to their erstwhile workmen who were not retrenched - Appeal disposed of.
