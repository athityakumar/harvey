Mohammad Shah v Fasihuddin Ansari and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 May 1956

Case Analysis

Bench

Vivian Bose, Syed Jaffer Imam

Where Reported

1956 Indlaw SC 137; AIR 1956 SC 713

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Family & Personal - Civil Procedure Code, O. 1, r. 8 - Whether property is wakf property and does not belong to defendants? - Held, before it was built, compound in which mosque stands was takiya of one of defendants' collateral ancestors - There was no proof of dedication either by defandant's great grand uncle or anyone else and so held that property belonged to three defendants in that suit to were heirs of Miskin Shah - (B) Whether there is evidence of user and if so, user of what? - Held, there is no evidence of any demarcation of compound to mosque at that date, so it must be taken that all that was admitted to be public property was mosque property and area on which it was then built, defendant and his family continued to claim rest of property as theirs - There is no evidence at all of public user and still less of public user for a religious purpose - But though there is no direct evidence about this it will be fair to assume that public did use a part of extensions, namely courtyard in front of mosque and area covered by Chabutra to east of mosque when they used mosque proper - (C) Whether Imambara was made and accretion to old mosque or any of its later extension? - Held, only evidence is support of that is an inspection note dates 30-10-1938 made by Trial Judge - Masjid and adjoining places appear to be inter connected with each other - That observation is justified because extensions that have been made to various items of property in this area have brought them all so close together that they now adjoin each other - Appeal allowed.
