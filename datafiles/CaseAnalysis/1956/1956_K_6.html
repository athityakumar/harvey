Jai Narain Ram Lundia v Kedar Nath Khetan and Others

Supreme Court of India

31 January 1956

Case Analysis

Bench

Vivian Bose, Syed Jaffer Imam, N. Chandrasekhara Aiyar

Where Reported

1956 Indlaw SC 11; AIR 1956 SC 359; 1956 ALJ 345; 1956 (1) MLJ(SC) 151; 1956 SCJ 301; [1956] 1 S.C.R. 62

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, ss. 47, 38, 39, O. 21 r. 32 (1) - Execution - Power of transferee Court - Defendant/respondent sought to execute a decree for specific performance of a contract - Unable to perform contract because subject matter ceased to exist - An executing Court cannot go behind a decree so as to vary its terms - When obligations contract imposes on parties are reciprocal and inseverable, rendering partial execution impossible, decree must be executed wholly as it stands or not at all - Held, respondent was not entitled to execute decree - Appeal dismissed.
