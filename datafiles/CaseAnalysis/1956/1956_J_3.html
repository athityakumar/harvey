Jaswantrai Manilal Akhaney v State of Bombay

Supreme Court of India

04 May 1956

Case Analysis

Bench

Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Vivian Bose, B. Jagannadhadas

Where Reported

1956 Indlaw SC 16; AIR 1956 SC 575; [1956] 26 Comp Cas 340; 1956 CRLJ 1116; 1956 (2) MLJ(SC) 49; 1956 SCJ 613; [1956] 1 S.C.R. 483

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Indian Contract Act, Code of Criminal Procedure 1898, misa

Summary: Corporate - Indain Contract Act, 1872, ss. 176 and 179 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 409 and 405 - Companies Act, 1956, s. 457 - Companies Act, 1913, s. 179 - Criminal Penal Code, 1898, ss. 202, 221, 222, 223 and 225 - Appeal against conviction u/s 409 of IPC, 1860 for criminal breach of trust in respect of property (Govt. Promissory Loan Note) entrusted to him in his capacity as Managing Director of Exchange Bank of India and Africa Ltd., belonging to Bank - Question raised as to determination of rights of pledgee over property pledged - Civil liability or criminal prosecution - Held, right arises only on happening of certain events - Liability depends upon facts and ingredients of case - It is not a case in which it can be said that omission in charge has materially affected trial of case or prejudiced appellant in his defence or has occasioned a failure of justice - Appeal dismissed.
