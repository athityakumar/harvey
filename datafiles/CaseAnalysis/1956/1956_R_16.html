Ram Krishan and Another v State of Delhi and others

Supreme Court of India

09 March 1956

Case Analysis

Bench

Vivian Bose, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Syed Jaffer Imam

Where Reported

1956 Indlaw SC 134; AIR 1956 SC 476; 1956 CRLJ 837; 1957 (1) LLJ 4; 1956 (2) MLJ(SC) 4; [1956] S.C.R. 182

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Criminal; Socio-Economic

Summary: Criminal - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947, s. 5 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 161, 162, 163 and 120 B - Railways Act, s. 137 - Appellants allegedly exporting potatoes at concessional rates on false declaration that they were seed potatoes - Whether act of appellant falls under bribery? - If a man obtains a pecuniary advantage by abuse of his position, he will be guilty under sub-cl.(d),s. 161 - It is enough if by abusing his position as a public servant a man obtains for himself any pecuniary advantage, entirely irrespective of motive or reward for showing favour or disfavour - Held, every servant of railways is a public servant and fact of bribery proved against appellants - Appeals dismissed.
