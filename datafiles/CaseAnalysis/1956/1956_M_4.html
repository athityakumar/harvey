Mehta Parikh and Company v Commissioner of Income Tax, Bombay

Supreme Court of India

10 May 1956

Case Analysis

Bench

Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, Sudhi Ranjan Das, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar

Where Reported

1956 Indlaw SC 57; AIR 1956 SC 554; [1956] 30 ITR 181; 1956 SCJ 678; [1956] 1 S.C.R. 626

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 23 (3) and 26 A - High Denomination Bank Notes (Demonetisation) Ordinance, 1946 - Whether Tribunal had material to justify part assessment of sum representing value of high denomination notes which were encashed subsequent to promulgation of ordinance? - Cash book of appellants assessee were accepted and entries therein were not challenged - Tribunal could not negate the possibility of appellant being in possession of a substantial number of high denomination currency notes - Tribunal's order had no linking to any material based upon which it fixed part of sum involved to be treated as secret profit or profits from undisclosed sources - Held, High Court's decision was in error and there were no material with Tribunal to justify assessment of sum involved partly - Appeal allowed.
