Videsh Sanchar Nigam Ltd. v M.V. Kapitan Kud And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

10 November 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, B.N. Kirpal

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 803; (1996) 7 SCC 127; AIR 1996 SC 516; 1996 (2) CLT 44; JT 1995 (8) SC 166; 1995(6) SCALE 339; [1995] Supp5 S.C.R. 76

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Civil Procedure

Keywords: Assessors, Foreign Ship

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 140 - Admiralty suit - Release of arrested foreign ship - Making out of prima facie case by plaintiff - Suit on ground of breakage of cable by certain vessel - Surveyors appointed by Govt. to assist Court, concluding that vessel in question, was one highly probable for breakage - They relying on affidavit of captain of ship and helicopter's report, Coast guard authorities report - Reasons given by them not partial or one-sided - Plaintiff must be considered to have made out prima facie case - Release of ship on undertaking given by captain of ship that they would honour decree - (A) Whether proper - (B) Whether ship can be released without imposing conditions -Held, (A) Not proper - (B) Cannot be released - Ship being a foreign ship, may not return to jurisdiction of Indian Courts, if it leaves Indian territorial waters - Appeal allowed.
