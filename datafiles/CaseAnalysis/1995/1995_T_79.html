Syed Azam Hussaini v The Andhra Bank Ltd.

Supreme Court of India

31 January 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Agrawal, Sujata V. Manohar

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 161; (1995) Supp1 SCC 557; (1995) SCC (L&S) 573; (1996) 1 SCC 557; AIR 1995 SC 1352; 1995 (1) CLR 435; 1995 (70) FLR 827; JT 1995 (2) SC 37; 1995 (2) LLJ 126; 1995 (2) LLN 250; 1995(1) SCALE 380; 1995 (1) SLR 706; 1995 (1) UJ 361

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Andhara Pradesh Shops and Commercial Establishments Act, 1966 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 2(ss), 2(j), 2(oo) and 25 F - Probationer appointment in clerical grade of Bank which is an 'industry', completed 240 days of service - Terminated from service before expiry of extended probation period without showing any reasonable cause - One month's wages in lieu of notice not paid at time of retrenchment but subsequently - Held, probationer is a workman, therefore, termination of his services amounts to retrenchment - Non-compliance of s. 25 F - Court directed payment of Rs. 75,000 by way of compensation - Appeal allowed.
