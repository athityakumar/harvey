Smt. Kamalabai Jageshwar Joshi And Ors. v State Of Maharashtra And

Supreme Court of India

30 November 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, S.B. Majmudar

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 2149; (1996) 1 SCC 669; 1996 (1) AD(SC) 139; AIR 1996 SC 981; 1995 (4) CCC 323; JT 1995 (9) SC 127; 1995(6) SCALE 764; 1996 (1) SCJ 209; [1995] Supp6 S.C.R. 8

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Administrative - Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - Compensation - Enhancement - Entitlement of - Notification was issued for acquisition of appellant's land - Award was made determining the compensation @ Rs.250/- per acre - On reference u/s.18 of the Act by award and decree the Court had enhanced the compensation to Rs. 2,000/- per acre - On further appeal DB further enhanced the compensation to Rs.7,000/- per acre - Hence instant appeal.

Held, so far as the claim @ Rs.4/- per sq. yd. is concerned, it was only an offer made by the appellant and there was no concluded agreement nor at least acceptance by the Department to purchase the lands at that rate. The concerned engineer had written to the appellant to give his offer for acquisition of the land whereat he had quoted at Rs.4/- per sq. yd. There was no acceptance thereof. It is true that sanction was obtained for converting the lands into non-agricultural lands. It would be obvious that having become aware of the proposal for acquisition, the permission for conversion was obtained by the appellant with a view to inflate the market value. All the sale deeds relate to small extents of agricultural lands purchased on square feet basis. They would offer no reasonable basis to further enhance the compensation though they fetched higher market value worked out at Rs.33,000/- per acre. No reasonable and prudent purchaser would offer to purchase this vast extent of land at that rate. Except obtaining sanction for conversion no further action to develop the lands was taken. Considered from these angles the HC having had the advantage of considering the entire evidence, determined the compensation at Rs.7,000/- per acre. SC does not think that we would be justified to further enhance the compensation. Appeal dismissed.
