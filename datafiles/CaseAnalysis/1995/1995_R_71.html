The Executive Engineer,Bihar State Housing Board v Ramesh Kumar Singh And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

22 November 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Paripoornan, A.S. Anand

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1954; (1996) 1 SCC 327; AIR 1996 SC 691; AIR 1995 SCW 4710; JT 1995 (8) SC 331; 1995(6) SCALE 625; [1995] Supp5 S.C.R. 543

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Bihar State Housing Board Act, 1982, s .59 - Maintainability of writ against notice of competent authority - Whether the Writ Petition in the instant case should be entertained against a show cause notice issued by a competent statutory authority? No question of infringement of any fundamental right - No infringement of statutory provision governing the matter -first the party should avail alternate remedy - Held, 1st respondent was unjustified in invoking the extraordinary jurisdiction of the High Court u/art. 226 of the Constitution of India, without first showing cause against the notice - Appeal Allowed
