Mettur Chemical and Industrial Corporation Limited v Commissioner of Income-Tax, Madras-1

Supreme Court of India

16 November 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Kirpal, B.P. Jeevan Reddy

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 657; (1995) Supp4 SCC 732; 1996 (132) CTR 389; [1996] 217 ITR 768; 1995(6) SCALE 468; [1996] 86 TAXMAN 157

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: (A) Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1922 - Income-tax Act, 1961 - Development rebate - Assessee was manufacturing coustic soda utilising billiter cells - Assessee in order to expand its capacity obtained a licence which permitted it to manufacture 20 tons of caustic soda per day and gradually replaced billiter cells with hooker cells - Instalation of the hooker cells required a change over in the power system and the installation of a rectifier was completed only in the year ended 1959 - Hooker cells were utilised for the production as and when they were installed by a suitable adjustment in the power system even before the rectifier was installed - Remaining sixty hooker cells were installed by the end of 1958 and the installation of the unit together with the rectifier was also completed in the year 1959 - It was clear that the capacity of the unit gradually increased from the stage of the installation of the hooker cells, though full capacity was reached only with the installation of all the hooker cells - Claim was made by the assessee before Income Tax Officer to the effect that it was entitled to the relief u/s. 84 of the 1961 Act in respect of A/Y 1962-63 on the ground that such relief was admissible for five A/Ys starting from the year in which the industrial undertaking began to manufacture or produce articles - Whether the assessee was entitled to relief in respect of the A/Y 1962-63 u/s. 84 of the 1961 Act - Held, the industrial unit ultimately consisted of sixty hooker cells and the rectifier but it had commenced commercial production in the year ended 1957 and, therefore, the five years had to be reckoned from the year 1957-58 and not 1958-59 - As 1957-58 was the first A/Y in which the relief u/s. 15(C) of the 1922 Act, corresponding to s. 84 of the 1961 Act could have been claimed, the four succeeding years would end by 1961-62 with the result that the appellant would not be entitled to any relief in the year 1962-63 which was rightly regarded as the sixth A/Y - Reference answered in favour of Revenue.

(B) Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income-tax Act,1961 - Finance Act,1967 - Development Rebate - Whether the assessee was entitled to get relief u/s.84 of the 1961 Act with respect to deduction of development rebate which was allowed to new industrial undertaking in computing the profits and gains - Held, that there was no income from the new industrial undertaking on which relief was admissible u/s.84(1) of the 1961 Act - The relief under the said provision was allowable only in case where there was a positive income of the industrial undertaking after the allowance of development rebate - Reference answered in favour of Revenue.
