Union Of India And Ors. v Pratibha Bonnerjea And Anr.

Supreme Court of India

21 November 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, K.S. Paripoornan

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 845; (1995) 6 SCC 765; (1996) SCC (L&S) 92; AIR 1996 SC 693; 1996 (32) ATC 58; 1996 (72) FLR 93; JT 1995 (8) SC 357; 1996 (1) LLN 29; 1995(6) SCALE 753; 1996 (1) SLR 20; [1995] Supp5 S.C.R. 511; 1996 (1) Supreme 20; 1996 (1) UPLBEC 122

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Constitution

Keywords: High Court Judges (Conditions of Service) Act, 1954

Summary: Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 217 and 50 - High Court Judge - Retired and appointed as vice chairman of Administration Tribunal - Benefit of pension - Held, HC Judge is neither Govt. servant nor holds post under Union or a State - Hence, not entitled to - Although, after retirement from post of Vice-Chairman of Administrative Tribunal would be entitled to pension.
