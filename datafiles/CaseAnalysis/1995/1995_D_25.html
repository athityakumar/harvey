Deepti @ Arati Rai v Akhil Rai And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

14 September 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

G.T. Nanavati, A.S. Anand

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1238; (1995) 5 SCC 751; (1995) SCC (Cr) 1020; 1995 (4) CCR 49; 1995 (3) Crimes 818; JT 1995 (7) SC 175; 1995(5) SCALE 328; 1995 (3) SCJ 659

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Tort

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 498 A - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 482 - Complaint alleging demand of dowry, harassment and cruelty by husband, father-in-law and mother-in-law - Framing of charge - Petition for quashing charge and proceedings - Allowed - Whether order of quashing of charge and proceedings against in-laws of complainant is justified? - There was sufficient material for Magistrate for framing a charge u/s. 498-A even against respondents 2 and 3 - High Court also should have taken care to verify record before accepting concession made by Govt. advocate - Second revision application, after dismissal of first one by Sessions Court is not maintainable and that inherent power u/s. 482 cannot be utilised for exercising powers which are expressly barred - Appeal allowed.
