Union of India and Others v Bhagwan Singh

Supreme Court of India

30 August 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Paripoornan, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1199; (1995) 6 SCC 476; (1996) SCC (L&S) 33; 1995 (31) ATC 736; 1996 (72) FLR 782; 1996 (1) LLJ 1127; 1996 (1) LLN 577; 1996 (1) SLJ 100; [1995] Supp3 S.C.R. 155

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Service - Appointment - On compassionate grounds - Entitled to - Whether Tribunal by impugned order is correct in quashing orders assailed before it and directed respondents to reconsider application of respondent/applicant for appointment on compassionate grounds and provide him with an appointment, if he is otherwise found suitable within three months? - Held, as a rule, appointments in public services should be made strictly on basis of open invitation of applications and merit - No other mode of appointment nor any other consideration is permissible - To this general rule which is to be followed strictly in every case, there are some exceptions carved out in interests of justice and to meet certain contingencies - One such exception is in favour of dependants of an employee dying in harness and leaving his family in penury and without any means of livelihood - Mere death of an employee in harness does not entitle his family to such source of livelihood - It is settled law, that even if Court reaches conclusion that applicant has made out a case, all that High Court or Administrative Tribunal can do, is only to direct authority concerned to consider claim of applicant in accordance with relevant law or rules, if any - At time Ram Singh died on 12-9-1972 there were two major sons and mother of children who were apparently capable of meeting needs in family and so they did not apply for any job on compassionate grounds and for nearly 20 years, family has pulled on, apparently without any difficulty, in this background, Central Administrative Tribunal acted illegally and wholly without jurisdiction in directing authorities to consider case of respondent for appointment on compassionate grounds and to provide him with an appointment, if he is found suitable - Order of Tribunal D/-22-1993 is set aside - Appeal allowed.
