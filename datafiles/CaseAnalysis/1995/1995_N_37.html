Naresh J. Sukhawani v Union Of India

Supreme Court of India

06 November 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, B.N. Kirpal

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 116; (1995) Supp4 SCC 663; (1996) SCC (Cr) 76; AIR 1996 SC 522; AIR 1996 SCW 4422; 1996 (64) ECR 346; 1996 (83) E.L.T. 258; JT 1995 (8) SC 160; [1995] Supp4 S.C.R. 778

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Customs Act, 1962, s. 108 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 30 - Code Of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 161 - Statement made before customs officials - Evidentiary value - Statement of Co-accused u/s. 108 incriminating himself and accused in passing off foreign currency out of India - Whether can be used as substantive evidence connecting accused with contravention of provisions of Act - If it is a statement recorded u/s. 161 of CrPC - Held, Can be used as substantive evidence -Not a statement u/s. 161 of CrPC - Petition dismissed.
