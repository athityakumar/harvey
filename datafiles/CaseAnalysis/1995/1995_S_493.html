M. O. Shamsudhin v State of Kerala

Supreme Court of India

21 March 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Jayachandra Reddy, M.M. Punchhi

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1607; (1995) 3 SCC 351; (1995) SCC (Cr) 509; AIR 1995 SCW 2717; 1995 (2) ALT(SC) 114; 1995 (2) CCR 37; 1995 (2) Crimes 282; JT 1995 (3) SC 367; 1995(2) SCALE 298; [1995] 2 S.C.R. 900; 1995 (2) UJ 79

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Land & Property; Tort

Summary: Criminal - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 5(1)(d) and 5(2) - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120-B and 161 - Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 114 and 133 - Whether inconsistencies found in the evidence of PWs 1 and 2, do, in any manner, affect the prosecution case? - Held, no doubt PW 2 has been treated hostile but there is no reason to reject the evidence of PW 1 who is the main witness regarding the demand of bribe and the acceptance of the same by A-2 on behalf of A-1 as directed by A-1 - (B) Whether corroboration of evidence of PW 1 who is in the nature of an accomplice regarding the demand is necessary for conviction of the accused? - A distinction could as well be drawn between cases where a person offers a bribe to achieve his own purpose and where one is forced to offer bribe under coercion, the latter who becomes a party for laying a trap stands on a different footing because he is only a victim of threat or coercion; where such witnesses fall under the category of 'accomplices' by reason of their being bribe-givers, the Court has to consider the degree of complicity and then look for corroboration if necessary as a rule of prudence; while in the case of evidence of an accomplice, no conviction can be based on his evidence unless it is corroborated in material particulars but as regards the evidence of a partisan witness it is open to a Court to convict an accused person solely on the basis of that evidence if it is satisfied that that the evidence is reliable, but it may in appropriate case look for corroboration which is in a general way and not material corroboration as in the case of evidence of accomplices - Held, the utmost that can be said against the Dy. SP, Erulkar and Santramji at that they are partisan witnesses as they were interested in the success of the trap laid by them but it cannot be said that they were accomplices, therefore, the law does not require that their evidence should be corroborated before being accepted as sufficient to found a conviction - Appeals dismissed.
