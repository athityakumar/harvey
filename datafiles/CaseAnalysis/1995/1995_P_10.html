P.Periasami (Dead) By LRs v P. Periathambi and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 October 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

M.M. Punchhi, Sujata V. Manohar

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1857; (1995) 6 SCC 523; 1995 (2) HLR 709; 1995(6) SCALE 50; [1995] Supp4 S.C.R. 230

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Women & Children

Keywords: Succession & Inheritance

Summary: Family & Personal - Hindu Succession Act, 1956, s. 19 - Sons got self-acquired property of their father by inheritance - Suit for partition filed by plaintiff - Nature of property is not joint family property - Whether under Hindu law self-acquired property of a father goes on his death to his sons or does it descend by inheritance to them in well defined shares as tenants-in-common? - Income derived therefrom does not cloak new acquisition with character of joint Hindu family property - Properties coming from elders in absence of grandsons cannot be held as joint hindu family properties but was joint properties capable of partition - Held, property in possession of two branches of joint family was not joint hindu family property but property obtained by inheritance and their status was that of tenants-common - Appeals dismissed.
