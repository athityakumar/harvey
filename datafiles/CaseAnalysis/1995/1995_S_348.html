Sukhwant Singh v State Of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

28 March 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

A.S. Anand, Sujata V. Manohar, S.C. Agrawal, B.L. Hansaria

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 961; (1995) 3 SCC 367; (1995) SCC (Cr) 524; AIR 1995 SC 1601; AIR 1995 SCW 2521; 1995 (2) ALT(SC) 201; 1995 (2) Crimes 148; JT 1995 (3) SC 495; 1995(2) SCALE 482; 1995 (2) SCJ 181; [1995] 2 S.C.R. 1190; 1995 (2) UJ 29

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Ballistics

Summary: Criminal - Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 45 and 138 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 300 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1898 - Evidence of murder - (A) Effect of tendering witness for cross-examination without his being examined-in-chief - (B) Firearm and cartridges not sent to ballistic expert - Effect on case - (C) Appeciation of evidence - Validity of conviction - No explanation about delay in receipt of special report by Magistrate - Prosecution tendering witnesses for cross-examination only and testimony of sole eye-witness suffered infirmities - Held, it affects credibility of prosecution case - Affects creditworthiness of prosecution case to a great extent - Conviction, not proper - Appeal allowed.
