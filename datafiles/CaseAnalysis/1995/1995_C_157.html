Modi Industries Limited, Modinagar v Commissioner of Income Tax, Delhi and Another

Supreme Court of India

15 September 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, S.C. Sen, S.B. Majmudar

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1529; (1995) 6 SCC 396; 1995 (128) CTR 361; [1995] 216 ITR 759; JT 1995 (6) SC 549B; 1995(5) SCALE 362; [1995] Supp3 S.C.R. 642; [1995] 82 TAXMAN 377

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Advance Payment, Tax Demand, Finance Act, 1964, Taxation Laws (Amendment) Act, 1975, Collection And Recovery, Taxation Laws (Amendment) Act, 1984, Summary Assessment, Income Tax (Amendment) Act, 1953

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 214, 215, 237 - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 143 - Advance tax - Payment of interest - Perfected debt - Assessment order - Assessee had paid advance tax according to his estimate of income and filed his returns and his assessment was made u/s. 143 of the 1961 Act - Assessee had paid more tax by way of advance tax and was to be refunded the extra amount with interest calculated from the first day of April of that A/Y to the date of assessment - Assessee filed appeal and assessment order was revised - Due to such revised tax order, the tax refundable to the assessee was larger than the original assessment and where assessee was entitled for refund of Rs. 10,000/- he became entitled for refund of Rs. 15,000/- Respondent disallowed the assessee's claim for interest u/ss. 214, 244(1A) of the 1933 Act - Assessee filed W.P. and order passed favouring the respondents - Assessee aggrieved - Hence, instant appeal - Whether, order of the HC disallowing the payment of interest to the assessee u/s. 214 of the 1922 Act was justified - Held, that the right to get refund arose because of the advance payment of tax is in excess of tax liability of that A/Y - Interest became payable u/s. 214 of the 1922 Act, as soon as the liability to refund the excess amount arose - If income tax liability on the first day of the A/Y was larger than the amount of advance tax standing to the credit of the assessee, then interest was to be paid on seventy five percent of the deficient amount of tax by the assessee from first day of the A/Y to the date of the assessment order - Interest was payable from first day of April of the relevant year, because on that date a perfected debt had come into existence which was in excess of the amount of advance tax paid by the assessee - Once the tax paid by the assessee was adjusted against the income tax demand in the assessment order, the assessee ceased to be liable to pay interest on the outstanding amount - Interest was payable only upto the date of assessment order - No interest was payable by the Central Govt. u/s. 214 and by the assessee u/s. 215 of the 1922 Act beyond the date of assessment order - Excess realisation of advance tax upon assessment and adjustment became refundable u/s. 237 of the 1922 Act - Interest would not run beyond period of assessment order - Interest u/s. 214 of the 1922 Act was payable from first day of April of relevant A/Y to date of the first assessment order upto 31-3-1975 - Appeal allowed.
