Commissioner Of Sales Tax, Orissa And Anr. v Jagannath Cotton Company And Anr.

Supreme Court of India

28 July 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, S.C. Sen

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 479; (1995) 5 SCC 527; JT 1995 (5) SC 569; 1995(4) SCALE 584; 1995 (3) SCJ 449; 1995 (99) STC 83; [1995] Supp2 S.C.R. 390

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Sales Tax

Keywords: Industrial Policy Resolution

Summary: Sales Tax - Practice & Procedure - Industrial Policy Resolution - Waste cotton - Incentives - Industrial policy resolution (IPR) passed to encourage industrialisation, provided several incentives for new establishment of industries as well as certain concessions in matters of sales tax - HC allowed W.P.s of the respondent - Department aggrieved - Hence, instant appeals - Whether, the HC erred in passing an order in favour of the respondent - Held, that several concessions at substantial cost to public exchequer were provided only with a view to accelerate the pace of industrialisation - Incentives were meant only for those units engaged in the manufacture or production of goods - Though the respondent asserted that he obtained cotton from the waste cotton by employing machinery, the exact process employed by him was not clearly set out or clarified in counter affidavits nor mentioned in the judgement - HC proceeded on the assumption that the IPR by itself was enough to provide the exemption from the sales tax - Order of the Sales Tax Officer was also not clear - Appeals allowed.
