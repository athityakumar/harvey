G.D. Zalani and Another v Union Of India And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

02 February 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, S.C. Sen

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 361; (1995) Supp2 SCC 512; AIR 1995 SC 1178; [1995] 84 Comp Cas 40; 1995 (2) CompLJ 1; JT 1995 (2) SC 420; 1995(1) SCALE 437; [1995] 1 S.C.R. 794

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Efficiency

Summary: Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 14 and 77 - (A) Public undertaking in collaboration with foreign company - Subject to Govt. approval - Govt. obtained expert opinion and directed Managing Director to enter into MoU - MoU challenged for acceptance of lower rental than stipulated - Whether necessary to grant opportunity of being heard before to companies whose proposal was rejected - (B) Powers of President - art. 77 - Whether Deputy Secy. to Govt. competent to exercise President's power by issuance of directive and authentication - Held, not necessary to grant hearing and power need not be exercised by President personally - Deputy Secy. is equally competent - Appeals dismissed.
