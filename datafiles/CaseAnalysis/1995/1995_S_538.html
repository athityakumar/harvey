M. W. Mohiuddin v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

21 March 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Jayachandra Reddy, M.M. Punchhi

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1606; (1995) SCC (Cr) 546; (1995) 3 SCC 567; 1995 (2) CCR 18; [1995] 2 S.C.R. 864

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Socio-Economic

Summary: Criminal - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947, ss. 12, 5 and 7 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 161 - Illegal gratification - Appellant convicted by Trial Court and High Court - Appellant, Special auditor, demanded a sum of Rs. 500 to remove objections in audit - Whether conviction of appellant just and proper? - Held, appellant found in possession of currency notes and had every intention to receive amount making an offence u/s. 13 of Act - No prior sanction required to prosecute appellant - Offence took place long back so sentence reduced to already undergone - Appeal dismissed.
