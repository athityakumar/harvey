State of Himachal Pradesh and Others v Dharam Das

Supreme Court of India

25 August 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

B.L. Hansaria, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1181; (1995) 5 SCC 683; AIR 1996 SC 127; AIR 1995 SCW 3817; 1995 (26) ALR 438; 1995 (3) CCC 411; JT 1995 (6) SC 519; 1996 (1) MLJ(SC) 35; 1995(5) SCALE 220; 1995 (3) SCJ 642; [1995] Supp3 S.C.R. 74; 1995 (2) UJ 746

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1994, ss. 17(4), 23(1-A), 28 and 31 - Compensation - Interest thereon - Award of - Whether Court is empowered to award 12% interest, in addition to benefits u/s. 23(1-A) of Act as amended under Amendment Act 68 of 1984? - Held, when statute deals with payment of interest to claimants either u/s. 31 or s. 28 of Act, Court has no power to award interest in a manner other than one in which statute prescribes payment - It is seen that in a case where decision has been taken exercising urgency power u/s. 17(4) of Act and award was made subsequent to taking over possession, obviously claimant would be entitled to payment of interest u/s. 31 from date of taking possession till amount is deposited pursuant to award of Collector u/s. 11 - On reference, if compensation is enhanced u/s. 28 of Act and proviso thereto claimants would be entitled to rates of interest specified therein - Apart from these two provisions there is no other provision under Act empowering Court to award interest on equitable grounds, in addition to statutory rates of interest prescribed under Act - Equitable consideration has no role to play in determination of compensation and manner of awarding interest as enjoined under Act - Act is to be administered in manner laid in Act and in no other way - As a concomitance, equity jurisdiction of Court is taken out and Act enjoins Court to grant interest as per statutory rates specified in Act - Order of High Court to extent of granting 12% interest from date of taking possession, namely, October 1972 till date of deposit, namely, March 1987, is illegal and is accordingly set aside - Appeal allowed.
