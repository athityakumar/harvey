Shri Bodhisattwa Gautam v Miss Subhra Chakraborty

Supreme Court of India

15 December 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Saghir Ahmad, Kuldip Singh

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1920; (1996) SCC (Cr) 133; (1996) 1 SCC 490; 1996 (1) AD(SC) 1; AIR 1996 SC 922; 1996 (1) ALT(SC) 252; 1996 (1) CCR 81; 1995 (4) Crimes 722; JT 1995 (9) SC 509; 1995(7) SCALE 228; 1996 (1) SCJ 338; [1995] Supp6 S.C.R. 731

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Competence Of The Legislature

Summary: Constitution - Criminal - Rape - Compensation - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 32 - Right of victim for compensation for serious allegation of rape made against accused - Held, Apex Court, as the highest Court of the country, has a variety of jurisdiction - U/art. 32, it has the jurisdiction to enforce the Fundamental Rights guaranteed by the Constitution by issuing writs in the nature of Habeas Corpus, Mandamus, Prohibition, Quo-Warranto and Certiorari - Fundamental Rights can be enforced even against private bodies and individuals - Even the right to approach Supreme Court for the enforcement of the Fundamental Rights u/art. 32 itself is a Fundamental Right - Rudul Sah v. State of Bihar: 1983 Indlaw SC 231; Peoples' Union for Democratic Rights (through its Secretary &Anr ) v. Police Commissioner, Delhi Police HQs &Anr : 1989 Indlaw SC 386 relied on - Further Court held, for the exercise of this jurisdiction, it is not necessary that the person who is the victim of violation of his fundamental right should personally approach the Court as the Court can itself take cognizance of the matter and proceed suo motu or on a petition of any public spirited individual - Further observation of Court is that whatever has been said in this Judgment shall not, in any way, affect or prejudice the Magistrate from deciding the complaint on merits on the basis of the evidence as may be tendered before it and in accordance with law - Order accordingly.
