Mahendra Singh and Another Gayatribai v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

07 February 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

M.M. Punchhi, Sujata V. Manohar

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1675; (1995) Supp3 SCC 731; (1995) SCC (Cr) 1157; AIR 1995 SCW 4570; 1996 CRLJ 894

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 306 and 498 A - Abetment to suicide - Appellants convicted u/s 306 - Appeal - Held, neither of the ingredients of abetment are attracted on the statement of the deceased and conviction of the appellants u/s 306 IPC, 1860 merely on the allegation of harassment to the deceased is not sustainable - Appellant deserve to be acquitted of the charge - As regards the charge u/s 498 A is concerned it would be imprudent to substitute the charge u/s 498 A and cull out incidences of cruelty inflicted on the deceased by the husband or relatives of the husband - As appellants have already undergone the sentence imposed on them appeal disposed as infructuous - Appeal of other accused who has gone through some sentence is allowed and is acquitted of the charge - Order accordingly.
