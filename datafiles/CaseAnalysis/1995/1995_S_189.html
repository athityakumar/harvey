Dr. Mahesh Chand Sharma v Smt Raj Kumari Sharma And Ors

Supreme Court of India

01 December 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, S.B. Majmudar

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 740; (1996) 8 SCC 128; AIR 1996 SC 869; AIR 1996 SCW 253; 1995 (4) CCC 330; 1996 (1) CLT 165; JT 1995 (8) SC 466; 1995(6) SCALE 809; [1995] Supp6 S.C.R. 41

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Keywords: Remainder, Hindu Adoptions And Maintenance Act, 1956., Hindu Succession Act,1956

Summary: Indian Limitation Act, 1908, s. 65 - Succession Act, 1925, ss. 119 and 111 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 3 - Hindu Succession Act, 1956, s. 14 - Date of vesting of legacy - Transfer - Enlargement of widow's estate - Bequeathing house for life to wife and then to son - Wife prohibited to transfer house - Dispute between mother and son - Validity of will in dispute in suit - Compromise - Mother acknowledged and accepted son's title to house in lieu of right of residence in first floor and cash maintenance - Settlement not challenged as not bona fide - Held, wife would become absolute owner only of first floor of house and not of whole house - Compromise does not amount to transfer as de hors their claims under will - Legacy will vest in heirs of testator on date of death of testator despite stipulation that devolution on heirs would be after death of wife - Order accordingly.
