U. P. State Sugar Corporation Limited v U. P. State Sugar Corporation Karamchari Assn. and Others

Supreme Court of India

02 May 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Agrawal, Sujata V. Manohar

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 922; (1995) 4 SCC 276; AIR 1995 SC 1484; 1995 ALJ 1771; [1995] 84 Comp Cas 139; JT 1995 (5) SC 676; 1995(3) SCALE 118; [1995] 3 S.C.R. 1004

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Sick Industrial Companies, Act, 1986, ss. 15, 22A and 23 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 162 and 226 - Disposal of assets by potentially sick industrial Co. - Report made to (BIFR) by Co. - (A) Whether Co. can be restricted from disposing of its assets after it had sent letter by way of report to BIFR u/s. 23 - (B) Whether slight error in filing of counter affidavit? - Lead to inference that a false plea was manufactured by Corporation on whose behalf affidavit was filed - (C) Whether blame on civil servants without having full appreciation of reasons for failure to implement particular state policy justified? - Held, such a Co., in order to revive itself, should be able to dispose of its assets - Complaint set aside - Remarks against civil servants - Unjustified.
