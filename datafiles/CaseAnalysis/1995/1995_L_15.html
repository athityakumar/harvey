P. Ram Reddy and Others v Land Acquisition Officer, Hyderabad Urban Development Authority, Hyderabad and Others

Supreme Court of India

27 January 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Venkatachala, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1502; (1995) 2 SCC 305; AIR 1995 SCW 871; 1995 (1) CCC 372; JT 1995 (1) SC 593; 1995 (1) RentLR 462; 1995(1) SCALE 332B; [1995] 1 S.C.R. 584

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - Enhanced compensation - In the instant case appellant-claimant's land was acquired by Hyderabad Urban Development Authority (HUDA) for formation of inner ring road required to connect Old Karnool Road with Hyderabad-Bangalore National Highway No. 7 - Notice was issued to the appellant-claimant calling upon him to make his claim for compensation of the acquired land before the Land Acquisition Officer of the HUDA - Respondent had revised the claim of the appellant, and reduced the compensation claimed - HC in the appeal revised the claim and improved the claim but not to the extent claimed by the appellant - Hence, instant appeals - In the one of the appeal appellant-claimant was appellant and in the other one appellant was the respondent - Whether, appellant-claimant's claim should be enhanced - Held, after considering the market price of the land, instant court, revised and enhanced the rate of compensation of the acquired land of the appellant-claimant - And, it was decided by the instant court that appellant-claimant should get interest on the enhanced compensation at the rate of 9 per cent per annum from the date on which he gave the possession of the land to the date of payment of such excess amount before the expiry of the period of one year from the date when possession was given, failing which compensation would be payable at the rate of 15 per cent per annum from the date of the expiry of the period of one year till the excess amount was paid - Appeals disposed of.
