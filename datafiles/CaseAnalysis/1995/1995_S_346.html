Mukhtiar Singh v State Of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

04 January 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

A.S. Anand, M.K. Mukherjee

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 409; (1995) SCC (Cr) 296; (1995) 1 SCC 760; 1995 (32) ACC 341; AIR 1995 SC 686; 1995 (1) CCR 1; 1995 (1) Crimes 293; JT 1995 (1) SC 531; 1995(1) SCALE 56; [1995] 1 S.C.R. 38; 1995 (1) UJ 338

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Arms Act, 1959 - Terrorists Affected Areas (Special Court) Act, 1984 - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Judgment of Trial Court - Sustainability - Deceased was assaulted and injured and later succumbed to injuries - 11 accused persons were put on trial before Special Court, for offences, inter alia, u/s. 25 of 1959 Act, of which 4 accused were convicted for offences under IPC and 1959 Act and others were acquitted - Hence, instant appeals, u/s. 14 of 1984 Act, one by accused-Mukhtiar Singh and accused-Jasbir Singh and another by complainant against acquittal of other co-accused - Whether impugned judgment of Trial Court is sustainable.

Held, both order of acquittal as well as order of conviction were passed by Trial Court in most perfunctory manner without considering and discussing evidence led by prosecution or arguments raised at the bar. The Trail Court apparently failed in discharge of its essential duties. The judgment passed by Trial Court is not a judgment in eye of law and the judgment contained only conclusions but not reasons which formed basis for arriving at conclusions. Hence, judgment of Trial Court could not be sustained and so, the same is set aside and case is remanded to Trial Court for fresh disposal by writing fresh judgment in accordance with law. Appeals disposed of.
