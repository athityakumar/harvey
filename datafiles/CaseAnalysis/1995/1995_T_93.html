The Chairman, Thiruvalluvar Transport Corporation v The Consumer Protection Council

Supreme Court of India

09 February 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, S. Mohan

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1728; (1995) 2 SCC 479; AIR 1995 SC 1384; 1995 (1) CCC 181; 1995 (1) CLR 249; [1995] 83 Comp Cas 82; 1995 (1) CPJ 3; 1995 (1) CPR 371; 1995 (3) CTJ 193; JT 1995 (2) SC 441; 1995(1) SCALE 525; [1995] 2 S.C.R. 1; 1995 (1) UJ 463

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection; Insurance

Keywords: Trader

Summary: Consumer Protection Act, 1986, ss. 2(o), 2(c) and 21 - Claim for compensation arising out of motor accident - Claim arising out of accident cannot be said to be in relation to any service hired or availed by consumer - Deceased passenger - Whether National Commission under Consumer protection Act - Can adjudicate - Held, Motor Vehicle Act which is special law would prevail over Consumer Protection Act - Claim cannot be adjudicated by National Commission.
