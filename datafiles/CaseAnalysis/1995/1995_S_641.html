Chameli Singh and Others v State of Uttar Pradesh and Another

Supreme Court of India

15 December 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, B.N. Kirpal, Faizan Uddin

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 888; (1996) 2 SCC 549; 1996 (1) AD(SC) 609; AIR 1996 SC 1051; AIR 1996 SCW 542; 1996 ALJ 413; 1996 (1) CLT 242; JT 1995 (9) SC 380; 1996 (1) RentLR 255; 1996(1) SCALE 101; [1995] Supp6 S.C.R. 827

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Notification, Urgency, Universal Declaration of Human Rights

Summary: Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 17(1A), 17(4) and 4(1) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 21 - Acquisition - Dispensing with enquiry - Govt. making opinion as to necessity of taking immediate possession of land for building houses for dalits - Held, Govt. is entitled to - No deprivation of right to livelihood in view of provisions for payment of solatium, compensation and interest.
