K. S. Shivadevamma and Others v Assistant Commissioner and Land Acquisition Officer and Another

Supreme Court of India

08 December 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, B.L. Hansaria

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 2238; (1996) 2 SCC 62; AIR 1996 SC 2886; JT 1995 (9) SC 501; 1996(1) SCALE 118; [1995] Supp6 S.C.R. 364

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, s. 23 - Determination of market value and compensation thereby - (A) Whether Commissioner's assessment can form basis to determine market value - (B) Determination of compensation when sale deed of small piece of land relied on by claimants - And deduction of development charges as land possessing potential value, but no development had taken place on date of notification u/s. 4 - Held, Commissioner's assessment is his 'best judgment assessment' and cannot form basis to determine market value - Further held, sale deed of small land cannot form basis and charges can be deducted - Appeal allowed.
