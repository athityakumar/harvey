Government of T.N. and Others v S. Balasubramanian and Others

Supreme Court of India

31 October 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Agrawal, B.P. Jeevan Reddy

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1343; (1995) 6 SCC 642; (1996) SCC (L&S) 72; 1995 (31) ATC 805; JT 1995 (8) SC 110; 1996 LabIC 458; 1995(6) SCALE 170; 1996 (1) SLR 99; [1995] Supp4 S.C.R. 567

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Administrative Tribunals Act, 1985 - Special Rules for the Tamil Nadu Revenue Subordinate Service, r. 6 - Tamil Nadu State and Subordinate Services Rules, r. 22 - (A) Whether as per decision in Indra Sawhney case, reservation as per existing rules cannot be questioned? - Held, since in Indra Sawhney case Supreme Court has held that existing Rules providing for reservation in matter of promotion can be continued for a period of five years, appointments that have been made on post of Deputy Tahsildar by applying principle of reservation cannot be questioned on basis of law laid down in Indian Sawhney case that principle of reservation cannot be applied at state of promotion - (B) Whether Tribunal was right in holding that amendment introduced in r. 6 by GOMs No. 660 D/-19-04-1988, gives retrospective effect to said amendment? - Held, it was not necessary to make an amendment in r. 6 of Special Rules to incorporate amendment that was introduced in r. 22 of General Rules in 1967 - Moreover, principle that where a subsequent enactment incorporates provisions of a previous enactment, then borrowed provisions become an integral and independent part of subsequent enactment and are totally unaffected by any repeal or amendment in previous enactment is subject to certain exceptions - One such exception excluding applicability of this principle is where subsequent Act and previous Act are supplemental to each other - Instant case would fall under this exception because r. 6 of Special Rules and r. 22 of General Rules are supplemental to each other - Tribunal was not right in holding that amendment introduced in r. 6 by GOMs No. 660 D/-19-04-1988, insofar as it gives retrospective effect to said amendment, is invalid - Appeals allowed.
